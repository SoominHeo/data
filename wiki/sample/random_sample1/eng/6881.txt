<p><b>The Black Eyed Peas</b> is an American group, consisting of rappers <a href="/wiki/Will.i.am" title="Will.i.am">will.i.am</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apl.de.ap" title="Apl.de.ap">apl.de.ap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taboo_(rapper)" title="Taboo (rapper)">Taboo</a>, and singer <a href="/wiki/Fergie_(singer)" title="Fergie (singer)">Fergie</a>. Originally an <a href="/wiki/Alternative_hip_hop" title="Alternative hip hop">alternative hip hop</a> group, they subsequently changed their musical sound to <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dance-pop" title="Dance-pop">dance-pop</a> music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboard.com_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-billboard.com-2">[2]</a></sup> Although the group was founded in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> in 1995, it was not until the release of their third album <i><a href="/wiki/Elephunk" title="Elephunk">Elephunk</a></i> in 2003 that they achieved high record sales. Since that time, the group has sold an estimated 76 million records (35 million albums and 41 million singles), making them one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_best-selling_music_artists" title="List of best-selling music artists">world's best-selling groups of all time</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> According to <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_SoundScan" title="Nielsen SoundScan">Nielsen SoundScan</a>, the Black Eyed Peas were the second-best-selling artist/group of all time for <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">downloaded tracks</a>, with over 42 million sales as of the end of 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Their first major hit was the 2003 single "<a href="/wiki/Where_Is_the_Love%3F" title="Where Is the Love?">Where Is the Love?</a>" from <i>Elephunk</i>, which topped the charts in 13 countries, including the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, where it spent seven weeks at number one and went on to become Britain's biggest selling single of 2003. Another European hit single from the album was "<a href="/wiki/Shut_Up_(The_Black_Eyed_Peas_song)" title="Shut Up (The Black Eyed Peas song)">Shut Up</a>". Their fourth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Monkey_Business_(album)" title="Monkey Business (album)">Monkey Business</a></i>, was an even bigger worldwide success, certified 4× <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Platinum_certification" title="Platinum certification">Platinum</a> in the U.S., and spawning four singles, "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Phunk_with_My_Heart" title="Don't Phunk with My Heart">Don't Phunk with My Heart</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Lie" title="Don't Lie">Don't Lie</a>", "<a href="/wiki/My_Humps" title="My Humps">My Humps</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Pump_It" title="Pump It">Pump It</a>". In 2009, the group became one of only 11 artists to have simultaneously held the No. 1 and No. 2 spots on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, with their singles "<a href="/wiki/Boom_Boom_Pow" title="Boom Boom Pow">Boom Boom Pow</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/I_Gotta_Feeling" title="I Gotta Feeling">I Gotta Feeling</a>", which topped the chart for an unprecedented 26 consecutive weeks. This album <i><a href="/wiki/The_E.N.D" title="The E.N.D">The E.N.D</a></i> later produced a third Hot 100 number-one placement with "<a href="/wiki/Imma_Be" title="Imma Be">Imma Be</a>", making the group one of few to ever place three number one singles on the chart from the same album, before being followed with "<a href="/wiki/Rock_That_Body" title="Rock That Body">Rock That Body</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Meet_Me_Halfway" title="Meet Me Halfway">Meet Me Halfway</a>", which peaked in the Top 10 of the Hot 100. "I Gotta Feeling" became the first single to sell more than one million downloads in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Black Eyed Peas were ranked 12th on <i>Billboard</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s Decade-End Chart Artist of the Decade, and 7th in the Hot 100 Artists of the Decade. At the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/52nd_Grammy_Awards" title="52nd Grammy Awards">52nd Grammy Awards</a> ceremony, held in January 2010, they won three awards out of six nominations. In November 2010, they released the album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Beginning_(The_Black_Eyed_Peas_album)" title="The Beginning (The Black Eyed Peas album)">The Beginning</a></i>. In February 2011, the group performed at the <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLV" title="Super Bowl XLV">Super Bowl XLV</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_Super_Bowl_halftime_shows" title="List of Super Bowl halftime shows">halftime show</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1988.E2.80.932000:_Atban_Klann.2C_Behind_the_Front.2C_and_Bridging_the_Gap">1988–2000: Atban Klann, <i>Behind the Front</i>, and <i>Bridging the Gap</i></span></h3>
<p>The Black Eyed Peas date back to 1988, when eighth-graders William Adams (<a href="/wiki/Will.i.am" title="Will.i.am">will.i.am</a>) and Allan Pineda (<a href="/wiki/Apl.de.ap" title="Apl.de.ap">apl.de.ap</a>) met and began rapping and performing together around Los Angeles. The pair signed to <a href="/wiki/Ruthless_Records" title="Ruthless Records">Ruthless Records</a> (run by <a href="/wiki/Eazy-E" title="Eazy-E">Eazy-E</a>) in 1992, catching the attention of Eazy-E manager <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Heller" title="Jerry Heller">Jerry Heller</a>'s nephew. Along with another friend of theirs, Dante Santiago, they called their trio <a href="/wiki/Atban_Klann" title="Atban Klann">Atban Klann</a> (ATBAN: A Tribe Beyond a Nation).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_page_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic_page-6">[6]</a></sup> Will 1X (aka will.i.am), apl.de.ap, Mookie Mook, DJ Motiv8 (aka Monroe Walker), and Dante Santiago formed Atban Klann. Their debut album, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grass_Roots_(Atban_Klann_album)" title="Grass Roots (Atban Klann album)">Grass Roots</a></i>, was never released due to Ruthless records founder Eazy-E's death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_page_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic_page-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>After Eazy-E died in 1995, Atban Klann reformed and changed their name to Black Eyed Pods, and then Black Eyed Peas. Dante Santiago was replaced with Jaime Gomez (<a href="/wiki/Taboo_(rapper)" title="Taboo (rapper)">Taboo</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Kim_Hill_(soul_musician)" title="Kim Hill (soul musician)">Kim Hill</a> became a steady background singer. Unlike the "<a href="/wiki/Gangsta_rap" title="Gangsta rap">gangsta rap</a>" sounds of Los Angeles-based <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> acts at the time, the trio performed with a live band and adopted a conscious musical and appearance style. After being signed to <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> and releasing their debut, <i><a href="/wiki/Behind_the_Front" title="Behind the Front">Behind the Front</a></i> (1998) the group (and their accompanying live band) earned critical acclaim. One of the singles from the album was "<a href="/wiki/Joints_%26_Jam" title="Joints &amp; Jam">Joints &amp; Jam</a>", and was featured on the <i><a href="/wiki/Bulworth" title="Bulworth">Bulworth</a></i> soundtrack. Singer Sierra Swan joined in 1998, appearing on the 1998 song "Fallin' Up", before she left in 2000. Their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Bridging_the_Gap_(Black_Eyed_Peas_album)" title="Bridging the Gap (Black Eyed Peas album)">Bridging the Gap</a></i> (2000), produced the singles "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weekends_(Black_Eyed_Peas_song)" title="Weekends (Black Eyed Peas song)">Weekends</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Esthero" title="Esthero">Esthero</a> and "<a href="/wiki/Request_%2B_Line" title="Request + Line">Request + Line</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Macy_Gray" title="Macy Gray">Macy Gray</a>. Hill left the band while producing the album, but was still featured on the album tracks for "Hot" and "Tell Your Mama Come" as well as in the video for "Weekends".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000.E2.80.9304:_Addition_of_Fergie_and_Elephunk">2000–04: Addition of Fergie and <i>Elephunk</i></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Black_Eyed_Peas_2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Black_Eyed_Peas_2.jpg/220px-Black_Eyed_Peas_2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Black_Eyed_Peas_2.jpg/330px-Black_Eyed_Peas_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Black_Eyed_Peas_2.jpg/440px-Black_Eyed_Peas_2.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Black_Eyed_Peas_2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Black Eyed Peas signing autographs in 2004.</div>
</div>

<p>Their third album <i><a href="/wiki/Elephunk" title="Elephunk">Elephunk</a></i> began development on November 2, 2001, but was not released until 2003. It was the first album to feature the vocals of current member <a href="/wiki/Fergie_(singer)" title="Fergie (singer)">Fergie</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Scherzinger" title="Nicole Scherzinger">Nicole Scherzinger</a> was originally approached to join the Peas, but was forced to decline because she was a member of <a href="/wiki/Eden%27s_Crush" title="Eden's Crush">Eden's Crush</a> and was under contract. Fergie joined the group in 2002, introduced to will.i.am by Dante Santiago. <i>Elephunk</i> is also the first album that indicated and demonstrated the new, polished pop sound designed to attract mass audiences. In a positive review of The Black Eyed Peas' new-found style, <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> noted that since 2002, when the group "hired a blond bombshell named Stacy 'Fergie' Ferguson and gave up their pursuit of backpack-rapper cred, they have made a kind of spiritual practice of recording futuristic songs – a total aesthetic commitment that extends from their garish wardrobes to their <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Group" title="Benetton Group">United Colors of Benetton</a> worldview."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>From <i><a href="/wiki/Elephunk" title="Elephunk">Elephunk</a></i> came "<a href="/wiki/Where_Is_the_Love%3F" title="Where Is the Love?">Where Is the Love?</a>", which became The Black Eyed Peas' first major hit, peaking at No.8 on the U.S. Hot 100, but topping the charts in several other countries, including seven weeks at No.1 in the United Kingdom, where it became the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_best-selling_singles_by_year_(UK)" title="List of best-selling singles by year (UK)">biggest-selling single</a> of 2003. The single had similar results in Australia, staying at No.1 for six weeks. In an interview with TalkofFame.com, Taboo shared that <a href="/wiki/Justin_Timberlake" title="Justin Timberlake">Justin Timberlake</a>'s split with <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a> impacted the recording of "Where Is The Love?".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The album subsequently spawned "<a href="/wiki/Shut_Up_(The_Black_Eyed_Peas_song)" title="Shut Up (The Black Eyed Peas song)">Shut Up</a>", which peaked at No.2 in the UK and topped the charts in many success and went <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">Gold</a> and <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">Platinum</a> in the U.S., UK, Germany, and other European markets. The third single from the album, although significantly restyled from the original <i>Elephunk</i> version, "<a href="/wiki/Hey_Mama_(The_Black_Eyed_Peas_song)" title="Hey Mama (The Black Eyed Peas song)">Hey Mama</a>" hit the top 5 in Australia and the top 10 in the UK, Germany and other European countries and reached No.23 in the U.S. The song received even more exposure in 2003 when it was featured in the first "silhouette" television commercial for the <a href="/wiki/IPod" title="IPod">iPod</a>. Their fourth single from the album was titled "<a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Get_It_Started" title="Let's Get It Started">Let's Get It Started</a>". Let's Get It Started" won a 2005 Grammy for Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group and also received two nominations for Record of the Year and Best Rap Song. In 2004, The Black Eyed Peas embarked on the <i>Elephunk Tour</i>, hitting many countries in Europe, Africa, and Asia.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004.E2.80.9309:_Monkey_Business_and_first_hiatus">2004–09: <i>Monkey Business</i> and first hiatus</span></h3>

<p>Their fourth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Monkey_Business_(album)" title="Monkey Business (album)">Monkey Business</a></i>, was recorded through 2004 and was released on June 7, 2005. Much of the pre-production writing was performed on the <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon_Educational_Tour_Bus" title="John Lennon Educational Tour Bus">John Lennon Educational Tour Bus</a> while on The Black Eyed Peas tour of 2004. The album's first single, "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Phunk_with_My_Heart" title="Don't Phunk with My Heart">Don't Phunk with My Heart</a>", was a hit in the U.S., reaching number three on the U.S. Hot 100. The spot the song reached was the highest peak yet of their career in the U.S. (this was later broken when "<a href="/wiki/Boom_Boom_Pow" title="Boom Boom Pow">Boom Boom Pow</a>" peaked at the top spot of the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100) and earned them another Grammy for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Performance_by_a_Duo_or_Group" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group">Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group</a>. The song reached three in the UK, and five in Canada, and stayed at number one for three weeks in Australia. Some radio stations, concerned about complaints of obscenity, played an alternative version, "Don't Mess with My Heart". "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Lie" title="Don't Lie">Don't Lie</a>", the second single from the album, saw success on the U.S. Hot 100, reaching No. 14, although becoming somewhat more successful in the UK and Australia, reaching a peak of No.6 in both countries. "<a href="/wiki/My_Humps" title="My Humps">My Humps</a>", another song from the album, immediately achieved commercial success in the U.S. and fairly substantial radio play despite the sexually suggestive lyrics, reaching number three on the U.S. Hot 100 and number one in Australia, making it their fourth Australian number one single. However, many mocked the song for its poor lyrical content; John Bush, writing for <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a>, described it as "one of the most embarrassing rap performances of the new millennium".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Despite this, the album <i>Monkey Business</i> debuted at number two on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">U.S. <i>Billboard</i> 200</a> albums chart, selling over 295,000 copies in its first week and was later <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">certified</a> triple platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>. Their next and last commercially released single from the album was "<a href="/wiki/Pump_It" title="Pump It">Pump It</a>", which borrows much of its sound from "<a href="/wiki/Misirlou" title="Misirlou">Misirlou</a>", specifically <a href="/wiki/Dick_Dale" title="Dick Dale">Dick Dale</a>'s version; it peaked at number 8 in Australia but 18 in America.</p>

<p>In September 2005, The Black Eyed Peas released an <i><a href="/wiki/ITunes_Originals" title="ITunes Originals">iTunes Originals</a></i> playlist of their greatest hits, as well as some that were re-recorded especially for purchase through iTunes. The playlist includes popular songs such as "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Lie" title="Don't Lie">Don't Lie</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Shut_Up_(The_Black_Eyed_Peas_song)" title="Shut Up (The Black Eyed Peas song)">Shut Up</a>", and a new version of "<a href="/wiki/Where_Is_the_Love%3F" title="Where Is the Love?">Where Is the Love?</a>" It has small stories containing info and commentary about the songs and how the group first met. In autumn 2005, The Black Eyed Peas set off to tour with Gwen Stefani, as supporting act. In December 2005, they embarked on the "European Tour", which toured multiple countries in Europe. The European leg which opened in <a href="/wiki/Tel_Aviv" title="Tel Aviv">Tel Aviv</a>, Israel, continued to Ireland, the UK, France and Germany. After heading to Europe and Asia, they toured the U.S. again, and South America. The Black Eyed Peas have starred in a series of web shorts for <a href="/wiki/Snickers" title="Snickers">Snickers</a> called Instant Def, appearing as a group of hip-hop superheroes. On March 21, 2006, The Black Eyed Peas released a <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remix</a> album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Renegotiations:_The_Remixes" title="Renegotiations: The Remixes">Renegotiations: The Remixes</a></i> to <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>. It features remixed versions of "Ba Bump", "My Style", "Feel It", "Disco Club", "They Don't Want Music", "Audio Delite at Low Fidelity", and the standard version and video of "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Like_That_(The_Black_Eyed_Peas_song)" title="Like That (The Black Eyed Peas song)">Like That</a>". The following week, it was released on CD without the music video. Participants on the <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> included <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pete_Rock" title="Pete Rock">Pete Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Erick_Sermon" title="Erick Sermon">Erick Sermon</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Jazzy_Jeff" title="DJ Jazzy Jeff">DJ Jazzy Jeff</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Large_Professor" title="Large Professor">Large Professor</a>. In March 2006, The Black Eyed Peas hit the road again as the featured headliner for the 6th Annual <a href="/wiki/Honda_Civic_Tour" title="Honda Civic Tour">Honda Civic Tour</a> with supporting bands <a href="/wiki/Flipsyde" title="Flipsyde">Flipsyde</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Pussycat_Dolls" title="The Pussycat Dolls">The Pussycat Dolls</a>. They again brought the John Lennon Educational Tour Bus on the tour with them to craft new songs for <a href="/wiki/Fergie_(singer)" title="Fergie (singer)">Fergie</a>'s solo debut album. In 2006, they went on tour with <a href="/wiki/The_Pussycat_Dolls" title="The Pussycat Dolls">The Pussycat Dolls</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2007, The Black Eyed Peas embarked on the <a href="/wiki/Black_Blue_%26_You_Tour" title="Black Blue &amp; You Tour">Black Blue &amp; You World Tour</a>, visiting more than 20 countries, including Macau, Sweden, Poland, Romania, Hungary, South Africa, South Korea, Malaysia, India, Indonesia, Israel, Singapore, China, Russia, Kazakhstan, Australia, Nigeria, Mexico, Thailand, Venezuela, Guatemala, Nicaragua, Costa Rica, Argentina, Philippines, El Salvador and Brazil. This tour was presented by <a href="/wiki/Pepsi" title="Pepsi">Pepsi</a>, in conjunction with the new "Pepsi More" advertising campaign, featuring The Black Eyed Peas. They recorded the song "More" for the new Pepsi spot. On December 31, 2006, The Black Eyed Peas performed at the last stop on the Monkey Business tour, at <a href="/wiki/Ipanema" title="Ipanema">Ipanema</a> Beach in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>, Brazil for more than 1 million people, being their biggest concert ever. The Black Eyed Peas performed at the <a href="/wiki/Live_Earth_concert,_London" title="Live Earth concert, London">UK leg</a> of <a href="/wiki/Live_Earth" title="Live Earth">Live Earth</a> on July 7, 2007 at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a>, London. will.i.am performed a new song, "Help Us Out", at the event, which is now on his album, <i><a href="/wiki/Songs_About_Girls" title="Songs About Girls">Songs About Girls</a></i>, as "S.O.S. (Mother Nature)". They headlined the main Ocean Stage at the <a href="/wiki/Summer_Sonic_Festival" title="Summer Sonic Festival">Summer Sonic Festival</a> in Tokyo, Japan on August 11, and Osaka, Japan on August 12, 2007. The act consisted of the group performing their hits, as well as Fergie singing solo tracks from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dutchess" title="The Dutchess">The Dutchess</a></i>. will.i.am again sang songs from his <a href="/wiki/Songs_About_Girls" title="Songs About Girls">upcoming album</a>. Already having produced the bulk of the Peas' albums, will.i.am has begun his solo career in earnest by producing and collaborating with a variety of musicians, most notably <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a> and also including <a href="/wiki/U2" title="U2">U2</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9rgio_Mendes" title="Sérgio Mendes">Sérgio Mendes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Santana" title="Carlos Santana">Carlos Santana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Too_Short" title="Too Short">Too Short</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kelis" title="Kelis">Kelis</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cheryl_Cole" title="Cheryl Cole">Cheryl Cole</a>, <a href="/wiki/SMAP" title="SMAP">SMAP</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Legend" title="John Legend">John Legend</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nelly_Furtado" title="Nelly Furtado">Nelly Furtado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Timberlake" title="Justin Timberlake">Justin Timberlake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bone_Thugs_and_Harmony" title="Bone Thugs and Harmony">Bone Thugs and Harmony</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.9314:_The_E.N.D.2C_The_Beginning_and_second_hiatus">2009–14: <i>The E.N.D</i>, <i>The Beginning</i> and second hiatus</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Black_Eyed_Peas_Performing_at_END.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="861" data-file-width="1280" height="148" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Black_Eyed_Peas_Performing_at_END.jpg/220px-Black_Eyed_Peas_Performing_at_END.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Black_Eyed_Peas_Performing_at_END.jpg/330px-Black_Eyed_Peas_Performing_at_END.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Black_Eyed_Peas_Performing_at_END.jpg/440px-Black_Eyed_Peas_Performing_at_END.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>In early 2009, both Fergie and the group left <a href="/wiki/A%26M_Records" title="A&amp;M Records">A&amp;M Records</a> for unknown reasons, both Fergie and the group are still with <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The group's fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_E.N.D" title="The E.N.D">The E.N.D</a></i> ("The <i>E</i>nergy <i>N</i>ever <i>D</i>ies"), was released on June 9, 2009. The overall sound of the album has a more electro hop beat rather than the usual hip pop/R&amp;B feeling of their previous albums. Following its release, will.i.am remarked that the album had been inspired by a trip to Australia, specifically the sound of <a href="/wiki/The_Presets" title="The Presets">The Presets</a>' "<a href="/wiki/My_People_(The_Presets_song)" title="My People (The Presets song)">My People</a>". "The energy on the Presets' small little stage was crazy energy. That song My People – that shit is wild," will.i.am said, "That's the reason why this record sounds the way it does – my three months in Australia."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In its first week, the album sold 304,000 copies and debuted at number 1 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. In the United States, the album became the ninth album to top the one million mark in sales in 2009. The album spent 38 weeks within the top 10 of the <i>Billboard</i> 200. <i>The E.N.D</i> was the 7th best-selling album of 2009 in the U.S. It also debuted at number one in Australia, number two in New Zealand and three in the United Kingdom. Three additional singles, "<a href="/wiki/Imma_Be" title="Imma Be">Imma Be</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Alive_(The_Black_Eyed_Peas_song)" title="Alive (The Black Eyed Peas song)">Alive</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Meet_Me_Halfway" title="Meet Me Halfway">Meet Me Halfway</a>", were released through the <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a> in the three weeks running up to the album's release.</p>
<p>The first single "<a href="/wiki/Boom_Boom_Pow" title="Boom Boom Pow">Boom Boom Pow</a>" was released on March 30, 2009 in the U.S. on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>. The single sold 465,000 downloads in its first week of digital release, the third-largest number of download sales in a single week overall, and the largest single-week and debut-download totals by a group in the history of digital-download sales, reaching No. 1 on the U.S. The single also reached the top spot in <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 and Pop 100. being the group's first U.S. No. 1, holding the spot for twelve consecutive weeks. It also reached number 1 in Australia, Canada, and the UK. After the release of <i>The E.N.D</i>, the Black Eyed Peas released "<a href="/wiki/I_Gotta_Feeling" title="I Gotta Feeling">I Gotta Feeling</a>" (produced by <a href="/wiki/David_Guetta" title="David Guetta">David Guetta</a>) as the official second single from the album. The single charted at number three and then went to number one on the UK Singles Chart. It debuted at number 2 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> behind "Boom Boom Pow" and later surpassed it, taking the number 1 spot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The Black Eyed Peas joined a group of artists who have held the No.1 and 2 Spot on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> simultaneously. From April 18, 2009, when "Boom Boom Pow" reached No. 1, through October 10, 2009, the last week "I Gotta Feeling" was at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Hot_100_number-one_singles_of_2009_(U.S.)" title="List of Hot 100 number-one singles of 2009 (U.S.)">No. 1</a>, the group was on top of the chart for 26 weeks, more consecutive weeks than any other artist.</p>
<p>On July 30, 2009, <i>Billboard</i> announced that the Black Eyed Peas set a record for the longest successive No. 1 chart run by a duo or group in the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100's history. "I Gotta Feeling" hit its fifth consecutive week at No. 1, following 12 weeks at the top by the Peas' "Boom Boom Pow". <a href="/wiki/Boyz_II_Men" title="Boyz II Men">Boyz II Men</a> reigned on the chart for two 16-week runs in the mid-'90s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> "Meet Me Halfway" was released as the third single from the album in September 2009. The single reached number one in the UK and Australia, making it their third chart topper from <i>The E.N.D</i> in both countries. It also peaked at seven on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100, making it the group's third top-10 single from <i>The E.N.D</i> "Imma Be" was released as the fourth single in the U.S. on December 15, 2009, reaching number 1 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 for two weeks, becoming the group's third number one single on that chart. Heavy airplay in Canada caused "Imma Be" to reach number 5 on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a>, their fourth consecutive top 5 hit from the album. "Rock That Body" was then released as the fifth single, and has so far reached number 9 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100.</p>
<p>In September 2009, the group embarked on <a href="/wiki/The_E.N.D_World_Tour" title="The E.N.D World Tour">The E.N.D World Tour</a>, visiting Japan, Thailand, Malaysia, Australia and New Zealand. In October 2009, they also were the opening acts for 5 concerts of the <a href="/wiki/U2_360%C2%B0_Tour" title="U2 360° Tour">U2 360° Tour</a> North America leg. The group performed at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010_Grammy_Awards" title="2010 Grammy Awards">Grammys</a> on January 31, 2010. They performed a mash-up of "Imma Be"/"I Gotta Feeling". They won 3 out of the 6 awards they were nominated for including Best Pop Vocal Album for <i>The E.N.D.</i>, Best Pop VocalPerformance by a Group for "I Gotta Feeling" and Best Short Form Video for "Boom Boom Pow".</p>
<p>On July 27, 2010, The Black Eyed Peas released a remix album: <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_E.N.D._Summer_2010_Canadian_Invasion_Tour:_Remix_Collection" title="The E.N.D. Summer 2010 Canadian Invasion Tour: Remix Collection">The E.N.D. Summer 2010 Canadian Invasion Tour: Remix Collection</a></i>. It was released on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> in Canada only, during the Canadian leg of The E.N.D World Tour. It mostly features remixes of the singles taken from <i>The E.N.D.</i> It also features a remix of "Let's Get It Started" taken from <i><a href="/wiki/Elephunk" title="Elephunk">Elephunk</a></i>; the remix was also a bonus track on the deluxe edition of <i>The E.N.D.</i></p>
<p>Their sixth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Beginning_(The_Black_Eyed_Peas_album)" title="The Beginning (The Black Eyed Peas album)">The Beginning</a></i>, was released on November 30, 2010, and received mixed reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The album's first single release was called "<a href="/wiki/The_Time_(Dirty_Bit)" title="The Time (Dirty Bit)">The Time (Dirty Bit)</a>",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and was revealed on October 20, 2010, through will.i.am's Twitter account. The music video was directed by Rich Lee, who had previously directed the video for "Imma Be Rocking That Body".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Just_Can%27t_Get_Enough_(The_Black_Eyed_Peas_song)" title="Just Can't Get Enough (The Black Eyed Peas song)">Just Can't Get Enough</a>", the album's second single, was released in February 18, 2011. The music video was released on March 16, 2011, and it was filmed in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, one week before the <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">earthquake and the tsunami</a>. The video was directed by Ben Mor.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The group's third single was "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Stop_the_Party_(The_Black_Eyed_Peas_song)" title="Don't Stop the Party (The Black Eyed Peas song)">Don't Stop the Party</a>" and it was released on May 10, 2011. On the same day, a music video for the song was released on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>, along with the single. The video, which is directed by Ben Mor, features on stage and backstage footage of the group during <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_E.N.D._World_Tour" title="The E.N.D. World Tour">The E.N.D. World Tour</a> in 2009–10. The video premiered on <a href="/wiki/Vevo" title="Vevo">Vevo</a> on May 12, 2011. On May 22, the group appeared on the <a href="/wiki/2011_Billboard_Music_Awards" title="2011 Billboard Music Awards">2011 Billboard Music Awards</a> and won 1 of their 4 nominations, for "Top Duo/Group".</p>
<p>On the July 6, 2011 during a concert at <a href="/wiki/Alton_Towers" title="Alton Towers">Alton Towers</a> in <a href="/wiki/Staffordshire" title="Staffordshire">Staffordshire</a>, The Black Eyed Peas announced they are taking an indefinite hiatus following the completion of their current tour, as they did between 2005 and 2009. will.i.am later confirmed the news on Twitter, adding that the break does not mean they will "stop creating".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Breakup_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Breakup-19">[19]</a></sup> On November 23, 2011, the group made their last performance for <a href="/wiki/The_Beginning_(concerts)" title="The Beginning (concerts)">The Beginning Tour</a> in <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a> with <a href="/wiki/Opening_act" title="Opening act">opening acts</a> including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cee_Lo_Green" title="Cee Lo Green">Cee Lo Green</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen_Latifah" title="Queen Latifah">Queen Latifah</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.E2.80.93present:_20th_anniversary_and_new_projects">2015–present: 20th anniversary and new projects</span></h3>
<p>During an interview with NRJ, will.i.am in talking about his solo album also confirmed that The Black Eyed Peas would start recording sessions for their seventh studio album in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In an interview on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capital_Breakfast_(London)" title="Capital Breakfast (London)">Capital Breakfast</a></i>, will.i.am said that the Peas will reform in 2015 for their 20th anniversary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The Peas premiered a new song titled "Awesome" that was included in commercials for the 2015 NBA playoffs. The song did not include Fergie, with people questioning if she would be a part of the upcoming album. On the 20th anniversary of their debut, the Peas premiered a new song titled "Yesterday" through <a href="/wiki/Apple_Music" title="Apple Music">Apple Music</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> though the song also does not feature Fergie.</p>
<p>On August 31, 2016, The Black Eyed Peas released a new version of their song <a href="/wiki/Where_Is_the_Love%3F#2016_version" title="Where Is the Love?">Where Is the Love?</a>. The song is titled #WHERESTHELOVE and is credited to "The Black Eyed Peas featuring The World". The song as well as the accompanying video feature many other popular artists and all four members of the group, will.i.am, Fergie, apl.de.ap &amp; Taboo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearances">Appearances</span></h2>
<p>On September 8, 2009, the group performed live for Oprah Winfrey's 24th Season Kickoff Party, on Michigan Avenue in Chicago. An estimated 21,000 dancers in the streets performed a <a href="/wiki/Flash_mob" title="Flash mob">flash mob</a> to the live performance of "I Gotta Feeling".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The group performed at the <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Awards_of_2009" title="American Music Awards of 2009">American Music Awards of 2009</a> with the songs "Meet Me Halfway" and "Boom Boom Pow". They also won 2 out of 3 categories in which they were nominated; Favorite Rock/Pop Group &amp; Favorite R&amp;B/Soul Group.</p>
<p>On March 30, 2010, The Black Eyed Peas were the first group to broadcast a concert live in 3D.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In June 2010, The Black Eyed Peas performed "Where is the Love?", "Pump It", "Meet me Halfway", "Boom Boom Pow", and "I Gotta Feeling" at the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> Kick-Off Celebration concert in South Africa. The concert had over 700 million viewers worldwide, making it the biggest event the group performed in. They also performed at <a href="/wiki/T_in_the_Park_2010" title="T in the Park 2010">T in the Park 2010</a> on the NME Stage on the Friday July 9 and Oxegen in Kildare, Ireland on Saturday July 10 on the Vodafone stage. In July 2010 The Black Eyed Peas performed "Boom Boom Pow", "Rock That Body" and "I Gotta Feeling" in Central Park as part of Good Morning America's free summer concert series. In December 2010 they appeared on the <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_series_7)" title="The X Factor (UK series 7)">seventh series</a> of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK)" title="The X Factor (UK)">The X Factor</a></i> in the UK performing "The Time (Dirty Bit)". On April 2, the group appeared on the 24th Annual <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon_Kids%27_Choice_Awards" title="Nickelodeon Kids' Choice Awards">Nickelodeon Kids' Choice Awards</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Charity_events">Charity events</span></h2>
<p>On December 10, 2005, the Peas cover of the <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon" title="John Lennon">John Lennon</a> classic "<a href="/wiki/Power_to_the_People_(song)" title="Power to the People (song)">Power to the People</a>", which was mostly recorded on the John Lennon Educational Tour Bus, was released by <a href="/wiki/Amnesty_International" title="Amnesty International">Amnesty International</a> as part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Make_Some_Noise,_Amnesty_International" title="Make Some Noise, Amnesty International">Make Some Noise</a> campaign to celebrate human rights. The song was later released on the 2007 John Lennon tribute album, <i><a href="/wiki/Instant_Karma:_The_Amnesty_International_Campaign_to_Save_Darfur" title="Instant Karma: The Amnesty International Campaign to Save Darfur">Instant Karma: The Amnesty International Campaign to Save Darfur</a></i>. .</p>
<p>At the release of video-clip for the single "<a href="/wiki/Just_Can%27t_Get_Enough_(The_Black_Eyed_Peas_song)" title="Just Can't Get Enough (The Black Eyed Peas song)">Just Can't Get Enough</a>" in early 2011, the group spent a short film to the whole country of Japan as that country had been recently hit by an earthquake and the group filmed the video a week before the disaster in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> In July 2011, The Black Eyed Peas founded a school for New York teenagers where students of 13–19 years can learn video production and music using professional equipment.The Peapod Foundation, in collaboration with the Adobe Foundation, opened the music academy and media Peapod Adobe Youth Voices in Manhattan on July 19. The school will have its facilities in a building operated by the Urban Arts Partnership, which provides art-school programs for adolescents studying in areas of very low income. Young people will be admitted according to the recommendations of their teachers and their expressions of interest in the subjects taught, such as camera work, editing and graphic design.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> On September 3, 2011, the group performed a concert in <a href="/wiki/Minot,_North_Dakota" title="Minot, North Dakota">Minot, North Dakota</a> to benefit the victims of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Souris_River_flood" title="2011 Souris River flood">2011 Souris River flood</a> that damaged over 4,000 homes and displaced over 12,000 people in Minot and along the Souris River.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Fergie is married to Minot native, <a href="/wiki/Josh_Duhamel" title="Josh Duhamel">Josh Duhamel</a>. On September 30, 2011 the group performed a free concert, "Chase Presents The Black Eyed Peas and Friends", for over 50,000 people in New York City's Central Park.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The event raised over $4 million for the charity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 31, 2016, The Black Eyed Peas' remake of their song, <a href="/wiki/Where_Is_the_Love%3F#2016_version" title="Where Is the Love?">Where Is the Love?</a> , #WHERESTHELOVE was released and will.i.am told in an interview that all proceeds raised from the song will go to his "i.am.angel foundation", which funds educational programs and college scholarships in the United States. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_media">Other media</span></h2>
<p>In June 2011 <a href="/wiki/Ubisoft" title="Ubisoft">Ubisoft</a> announced they are developing <i><a href="/wiki/The_Black_Eyed_Peas_Experience" title="The Black Eyed Peas Experience">The Black Eyed Peas Experience</a></i>, a dance video game for <a href="/wiki/Kinect" title="Kinect">Kinect</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wii" title="Wii">Wii</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> On November 11, 2011, the video game was released internationally in association with <a href="/wiki/Ubisoft" title="Ubisoft">Ubisoft</a>.</p>
