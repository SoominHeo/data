<p>The <b>Black Cat</b> (<b>Felicia Hardy</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Character_(arts)" title="Character (arts)">fictional character</a> appearing in <a href="/wiki/American_comic_book" title="American comic book">American comic books</a> published by <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a>. Created by writer <a href="/wiki/Marv_Wolfman" title="Marv Wolfman">Marv Wolfman</a> and artist <a href="/wiki/Keith_Pollard" title="Keith Pollard">Keith Pollard</a>, she <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_appeared" title="First appeared">first appeared</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Amazing_Spider-Man" title="The Amazing Spider-Man">The Amazing Spider-Man</a></i> #194 (July 1979).</p>
<p>Felicia Hardy is the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Walter_Hardy" title="Walter Hardy">a world-renowned cat burglar</a>. After suffering a traumatic event as a college freshman, she trained herself in various fighting styles and acrobatics and, after deciding to follow in her father's footsteps, adopted her costumed identity as the Black Cat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Felicia Hardy made her cinematic debut in the 2014 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Amazing_Spider-Man_2" title="The Amazing Spider-Man 2">The Amazing Spider-Man 2</a></i>, portrayed by actress <a href="/wiki/Felicity_Jones" title="Felicity Jones">Felicity Jones</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publication_history">Publication history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Cat_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Publication history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1979, creator <a href="/wiki/Marv_Wolfman" title="Marv Wolfman">Marv Wolfman</a> was looking for a female foil for <a href="/wiki/Spider-Woman_(Jessica_Drew)" title="Spider-Woman (Jessica Drew)">Spider-Woman</a>. He decided to base a character on a <a href="/wiki/Tex_Avery" title="Tex Avery">Tex Avery</a> cartoon "<a href="/wiki/Bad_Luck_Blackie" title="Bad Luck Blackie">Bad Luck Blackie</a>", in which a black cat brought misfortune to anyone in close proximity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbulr22_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-cbulr22-4">[4]</a></sup> The Black Cat's costume and appearance were designed by <a href="/wiki/Dave_Cockrum" title="Dave Cockrum">Dave Cockrum</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>When Wolfman changed writing assignments within <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a> to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Amazing_Spider-Man" title="The Amazing Spider-Man">The Amazing Spider-Man</a></i>, he brought his character with him. He made considerable changes to the character and her appearance over this time; the Black Cat intended to debut in <i>Spider-Woman</i> had only her name and powers in common with the one who finally appeared in <i>The Amazing Spider-Man</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On the bottom of the <a href="/wiki/Letter_to_the_editor" title="Letter to the editor">letters page</a> of <i>The Amazing Spider-Man</i> #194, a <a href="/wiki/Thumbnail" title="Thumbnail">thumbnail</a> of the intended cover for <i>Spider-Woman</i> #9 appears along with a rejected cover for <i>The Amazing Spider-Man</i> #194. Wolfman said in an interview, "I didn't plan Black Cat to be in Spidey. I created her for Spider-Woman (look at the letter column of the first B.C. story and you’ll see). I then decided to leave Spider-Woman and moved her over. So, I never even thought of <a href="/wiki/Catwoman" title="Catwoman">Catwoman</a> when I did her. I got the idea for her from a Tex Avery cartoon, Bad Luck Blackie."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbulr22_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-cbulr22-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Writer/director <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Smith" title="Kevin Smith">Kevin Smith</a> began writing the <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man/Black_Cat:_The_Evil_that_Men_Do" title="Spider-Man/Black Cat: The Evil that Men Do">Spider-Man/Black Cat: The Evil that Men Do</a></i> miniseries in 2002. After the third issue the series went on a hiatus until 2005, when Smith revealed he had finally finished writing the scripts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Smith has stated, "While I have zero defense for my lateness (particularly when folks like <a href="/wiki/Brian_Michael_Bendis" title="Brian Michael Bendis">Bendis</a> turn out great stories in multiple books on a monthly basis), I will say this: it's a much better story now than it would've been had I completed it back in '02."</p>
<p>In the mid-2000s, she starred alongside <a href="/wiki/Wolverine_(character)" title="Wolverine (character)">Wolverine</a> in a limited comic book miniseries titled <i>Claws</i>. A sequel to this miniseries, entitled <i>Claws II</i>, began publication in July 2011. Black Cat was a lead character in the 2006-2007 <i>Heroes for Hire</i> series.</p>

<p>Felicia Hardy was born in <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. Her father <a href="/wiki/Walter_Hardy" title="Walter Hardy">Walter</a> pretended to be a travelling salesman, but was a world-renowned cat burglar who, before his arrest, encouraged her to never settle for second best. If she loved basketball, she should work to become a basketball player and not just a cheerleader.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man/Black_Cat:_The_Evil_that_Men_Do" title="Spider-Man/Black Cat: The Evil that Men Do">Spider-Man/Black Cat: The Evil that Men Do</a></i> reveals that, as a freshman at Empire State University, Felicia was <a href="/wiki/Rape" title="Rape">raped</a> by her boyfriend Ryan. Hating the idea of being a victim, she trained herself in various fighting styles and acrobatics, intent on killing her rapist. Finally, after months of preparing, she set out for revenge, but before she could find him, Ryan was killed in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drunk_driving" title="Drunk driving">drunk driving</a> accident. Furious that she was denied the chance for revenge, Hardy decided to utilize her new skills to follow in her father's footsteps. After amassing a fortune in stolen items, Felicia adopted her costumed identity.</p>
<p>She first donned the Black Cat costume in order to break her father out of prison. On the same night, she met Spider-Man. Unfortunately for Felicia, her father died, and she then faked her own death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Despite her antipathy towards men, Felicia felt a kinship with this lone hero; Spider-Man was the first man she felt she could trust and she grew to believe herself in love with him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Felicia looked for a way to earn his trust and continued with the Black Cat persona as a misguided attempt to attract his affection. Seeing the good in Felicia, Spider-Man made every attempt to have her criminal record expunged.</p>
<p>Felicia had been placed in a mental institution, but escaped. She joined forces with Spider-Man against the <a href="/wiki/Maggia_(comics)" title="Maggia (comics)">Maggia</a>. She was granted conditional amnesty, and again convinced Spider-Man that she had died.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Black Cat finally found the opportunity to prove herself after learning the <a href="/wiki/Kingpin_(comics)" title="Kingpin (comics)">Kingpin</a> controlled an incredibly powerful detonator. The <a href="/wiki/Owl_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Owl (Marvel Comics)">Owl</a> planned to use the weapon to hold New York City hostage. Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Octopus" title="Doctor Octopus">Doctor Octopus</a> planned to use the weapon to destroy the city altogether. However, the Black Cat was able to use her abilities to steal the item first and protect it from all parties. She gave the detonator to Spider-Man and became the target of Doctor Octopus' revenge. Although Spider-Man was able to tear off his mechanical appendages, Octopus was still able to mentally control them and hold the Black Cat still while his men opened fire. Spider-Man barely got her to the hospital in time and as they operated on the dozens of bullet and knife wounds, Peter realized just how much he cared for Felicia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>After she recovered, they began a relationship and soon Peter revealed his identity to her. Felicia had great difficulty accepting the fact that Peter was just a man beneath the mask and could not understand his need for a civilian life. Peter was hurt, but continued the relationship since it was the first time he did not need to hide his life as Spider-Man from someone.</p>
<p>Initially, the "accidents" which seemed to befall those who crossed the Black Cat's path were merely well-planned <a href="/wiki/Stunt" title="Stunt">stunts</a> and <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/trap" title="wikt:trap">traps</a>. After her near-death experience,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Felicia feared her lack of superpowers made her a liability to Spider-Man. She was terrified that his overwhelming need to protect her would eventually get him killed. So, Felicia sought a way to make herself Spider-Man's equal. After learning <a href="/wiki/Iron_Man" title="Iron Man">Tony Stark</a> was missing, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henry_Pym" title="Henry Pym">Henry Pym</a> would not return her calls, and being rejected by the <a href="/wiki/Avengers_(comics)" title="Avengers (comics)">Avengers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Four" title="Fantastic Four">Fantastic Four</a>, Felicia was offered an opportunity to undergo the same process that was used to create the <a href="/wiki/Mac_Gargan" title="Mac Gargan">Scorpion</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Human_Fly_(comics)" title="Human Fly (comics)">Fly</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The Kingpin chose to use it on the Black Cat as payback for a theft she committed. Scared and ashamed of being empowered by the Kingpin of crime, she decided to keep her new abilities a secret from Peter. While Spider-Man was gone during the "<a href="/wiki/Secret_Wars" title="Secret Wars">Secret Wars</a>", Felicia searched for him at <a href="/wiki/Avengers_Mansion" title="Avengers Mansion">Avengers Mansion</a>; <a href="/wiki/Vision_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Vision (Marvel Comics)">Vision</a> offered her the chance to join the Avengers, which she declined, being interested solely in Spider-Man. Upon his return, her 'bad luck' power turned out to be infectious, and began to jinx Spider-Man, which was exactly the Kingpin's intent. Feeling a wall of secrets growing between them, Spider-Man broke up with Felicia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Felicia then began a "Robin Hood crusade," stealing from the rich to give to the poor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Peter soon realized something was amiss with his own luck and enlisted the aid of <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Strange" title="Doctor Strange">Doctor Strange</a> to remove the "hex" on him. By doing so, he altered the hex's source and changed the Black Cat's powers in the process. She soon found she had heightened <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Superhuman_strength" title="Superhuman strength">strength</a>, <a href="/wiki/Agility" title="Agility">agility</a>, <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stability" title="wikt:stability">balance</a>, <a href="/wiki/Visual_perception" title="Visual perception">vision</a>, and retractable <a href="/wiki/Claw" title="Claw">claws</a>. While burglarizing the mercenary known as the <a href="/wiki/Foreigner_(comics)" title="Foreigner (comics)">Foreigner</a>, Black Cat was attacked by <a href="/wiki/Sabretooth_(comics)" title="Sabretooth (comics)">Sabretooth</a>, the Foreigner's hitman; Spider-Man saved her life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Black Cat updated her look and her attitude and rekindled her relationship with Spider-Man. She made peace with his need for a normal life as Peter Parker and stood by him while he was being accused of murder as Spider-Man. Together, they tracked down the source of the elaborate scheme to frame him and fought the Foreigner. Eventually, just as his life seemed to be coming together, Spider-Man came home to discover Black Cat discussing her plans to ruin his life. He followed her to find she had never forgiven him for breaking up with her and in revenge became the Foreigner's lover.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> She then battled and defeated Sabretooth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Her apartment was fire-bombed by the Foreigner's hitman Blaze, and she began living with Peter Parker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> During her ruse against Peter and despite her anger, Felicia began to fall back onto her desire to love him. She soon donned another new costume. Spider-Man then discovered her association with the Foreigner. She cleared Spider-Man of a murder charge. In the end, the Black Cat doublecrossed the Foreigner and left for Paris to find a new life,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> which unexpectedly pushed Peter to find support and a new relationship with <a href="/wiki/Mary_Jane_Watson" title="Mary Jane Watson">Mary Jane Watson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Years later, the Black Cat returned to America, and went "shopping" with <a href="/wiki/Cloak_and_Dagger_(comics)" title="Cloak and Dagger (comics)">Dagger</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> She returned to her original costume, went to seek out Peter Parker, and in a chance confrontation with <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Brock" title="Eddie Brock">Venom</a> learned that Peter had married Mary Jane Watson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Angry and jealous, Felicia began harassing the couple, taunting Peter as she dated his friend <a href="/wiki/Flash_Thompson" title="Flash Thompson">Flash Thompson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> She began physically threatening Mary Jane, confronting her and swearing to ruin their marriage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> She saved a powerless Spider-Man from the <a href="/wiki/Mac_Gargan" title="Mac Gargan">Scorpion</a>, but then all her powers were rendered inert by one of the <a href="/wiki/Chameleon_(comics)" title="Chameleon (comics)">Chameleon</a>'s devices. She began to believe that she sincerely cared for Thompson,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> but he broke up with her when he found out her secret. The Black Cat later made up with both Spider-Man and Mary Jane, becoming close friends with them.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (July 2009)">volume &amp; issue needed</span></a></i>]</sup> She was one of the few to stand with Spider-Man and Venom against <a href="/wiki/Carnage_(comics)" title="Carnage (comics)">Carnage</a> and his "brood" in the <a href="/wiki/Maximum_Carnage" title="Maximum Carnage">Maximum Carnage</a> arc. Originally she sided with Venom's belief that the only way to defeat Carnage was to kill him, but at the end, she fought alongside Spider-Man in his effort to battle the chaos with compassion.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (July 2009)">volume &amp; issue needed</span></a></i>]</sup> After Spider-Man used a device to remove his superhuman abilities, the Black Cat aided him in finding the device again in order to restore them. In the process, the Black Cat's cat-like abilities were completely erased.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (July 2009)">volume &amp; issue needed</span></a></i>]</sup> She subsequently purchased equipment from the <a href="/wiki/Tinkerer" title="Tinkerer">Tinkerer</a> to incorporate into her costume in order to compensate for her lost abilities, and occasionally teamed up with Spider-Man.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (July 2009)">volume &amp; issue needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>After Spider-Man unmasked himself, the Black Cat was enraged, because she had felt that they shared a bond over knowing his identity, which she described as "sexy".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (July 2009)">volume &amp; issue needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Though she is dating Thomas Fireheart (a.k.a. <a href="/wiki/Puma_(comics)" title="Puma (comics)">Puma</a>), her new sexual interest notes that Felicia may still have some romantic inclinations toward Peter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_War.2FThe_Initiative">Civil War/The Initiative</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Cat_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Civil War/The Initiative">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Black Cat became a member of the new <a href="/wiki/Heroes_for_Hire" title="Heroes for Hire">Heroes for Hire</a> during the ongoing <a href="/wiki/Civil_War_(comics)" title="Civil War (comics)">Civil War</a> although <a href="/wiki/Misty_Knight" title="Misty Knight">Misty Knight</a> believed that Felicia was just there for the money.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marvel_Divas">Marvel Divas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Cat_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Marvel Divas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Black Cat returns in the limited series, <i><a href="/wiki/Marvel_Divas" title="Marvel Divas">Marvel Divas</a></i> (a parody of <i><a href="/wiki/Sex_and_the_City" title="Sex and the City">Sex and the City</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marveldivas_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-marveldivas-29">[29]</a></sup> She is one of the main characters, alongside <a href="/wiki/Firestar" title="Firestar">Firestar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patsy_Walker" title="Patsy Walker">Hellcat</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Monica_Rambeau" title="Monica Rambeau">Photon</a>. The writers are Roberto Aguirre-Sacasa and Tonci Zonjic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marveldivas_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-marveldivas-29">[29]</a></sup> Summoned by her friends to cope with Firestar's <a href="/wiki/Breast_cancer" title="Breast cancer">breast cancer</a>, Felicia is revealed financially broken: unable to restart her investigation firm, she's too proud to accept Puma's monetary assistance, and her attempts to get a <a href="/wiki/Loan" title="Loan">loan</a> are met with open hostility from the banks of New York, often targets of her villainous activities as the Black Cat. Thus, feeling rejected and angry, Felicia begins to consider returning to villainy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following <i>Marvel Divas</i>, Black Cat returns as a recurring character in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Amazing_Spider-Man" title="The Amazing Spider-Man">The Amazing Spider-Man</a></i>, following a storyline in #606-607 entitled "Back in Black Cat".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-backinblackcat_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-backinblackcat-31">[31]</a></sup> Although she remembers her previous encounters with Spider-Man, the events of "<a href="/wiki/Spider-Man:_One_More_Day" title="Spider-Man: One More Day">One More Day</a>" have erased her memory of his identity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-backinblackcat_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-backinblackcat-31">[31]</a></sup> Also, she has regained her "bad luck" powers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Amazing_Spider-Man_.23606_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Amazing_Spider-Man_.23606-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Spider-Man ran into the Black Cat as she was breaking into the penthouse apartment of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dexter_Bennett" title="Dexter Bennett">Dexter Bennett</a>. Spider-Man caught up to her, reminding her that she was the one that dumped him. She and <a href="/wiki/Puma_(comics)" title="Puma (comics)">Puma</a> had ended their relationship, so she and Spider-Man share a kiss,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Amazing_Spider-Man_.23606_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_Amazing_Spider-Man_.23606-32">[32]</a></sup> and later they slept together with the lights off, so Felicia could not see Peter's face.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Following this encounter Peter and Felicia begin a sexual relationship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Peter later enlists the Black Cat's help in getting back his <a href="/wiki/The_Gauntlet_and_Grim_Hunt#Out_for_Blood" title="The Gauntlet and Grim Hunt">vial of blood</a> from <a href="/wiki/Mister_Negative" title="Mister Negative">Mister Negative</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following this, Black Cat received her own four issue mini-series, which was written by <a href="/wiki/Jen_Van_Meter" title="Jen Van Meter">Jen Van Meter</a>, drawn by Javier Pullido and covered by <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Conner" title="Amanda Conner">Amanda Conner</a>. In the series, Felicia became caught up in the events of <a href="/wiki/The_Gauntlet_and_Grim_Hunt" title="The Gauntlet and Grim Hunt">"The Gauntlet" and "Grim Hunt"</a> storyline after her mother, Lydia, was kidnapped by the members of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ana_Tatiana_Kravinoff" title="Ana Tatiana Kravinoff">Kravinoff family</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Felicia agreed to turn herself over to the Kravinoffs in exchange for her mother's freedom, but instead sent a decoy while she tracked down her mother and attempted to free her herself. After removing the duct tape from her mother's mouth, Felicia discovered that the Kravinoffs had strapped an explosive device to her wrists in order to kill both of them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Felicia is ultimately able to rescue her mother and keep her safe from the clutches of the Kravinoff family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mary Jane enlisted the Black Cat's help after Spider-Man was captured by Doc Tramma, who turned out to be the one who restored Felicia's bad luck powers. Together they managed to defeat Tramma and save Spider-Man, and agreed to become friends afterwards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Black Cat is next seen in the "<a href="/wiki/Spider-Man:_Big_Time" title="Spider-Man: Big Time">Big Time</a>" storyline helping Spider-Man retrieve some experimental <a href="/wiki/Vibranium" title="Vibranium">vibranium</a> the new <a href="/wiki/Phil_Urich" title="Phil Urich">Hobgoblin</a> stole for the <a href="/wiki/Kingpin_(comics)" title="Kingpin (comics)">Kingpin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spider-Island">Spider-Island</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Cat_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Spider-Island">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Black Cat appears in the story arc of the spider island, fighting against the half-spider creatures, when the Heroes for Hire are called in to help with the quarantine of Manhattan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>Afterwards, Black Cat was blamed for the theft of a Holographic Projector from Horizon Labs. Since Spider-Man was with her at the time of the robbery he knew that she was innocent, and he recruited Matt Murdock (a.k.a. <a href="/wiki/Daredevil_(Marvel_Comics_character)" title="Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)">Daredevil</a>) to help prove her innocence. It was later revealed that the criminal organization Black Spectre framed her to test her as part of their plot to recruit the Black Cat to their side and offer her a huge cash reward for retrieving a device that Matt Murdock has in his possession. Afterwards she goes to Matt's apartment and they start a relationship which is part of her plan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> However she betrays Black Spectre and alerts Daredevil, leaving him a note saying the organization is monitoring his actions, before going into hiding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marvel_NOW.21">Marvel NOW!</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Cat_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Marvel NOW!">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As part of the <i><a href="/wiki/Marvel_NOW!" title="Marvel NOW!">Marvel NOW!</a></i> event, Felicia was later contacted by her friend, Misty Knight, in order to help the <a href="/wiki/Fearless_Defenders" title="Fearless Defenders">Fearless Defenders</a> fight the so-called Doom Maidens along with other female heroines such as Storm, Tigra, Hellcat and Valkyrie.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>Superior Spider-Man (<a href="/wiki/Doctor_Octopus" title="Doctor Octopus">Doctor Octopus</a>' mind in Peter Parker's body) swings in his patrol heading for another date with his girlfriend Anna Maria Marconi only to bump into the Black Cat, who was committing a robbery. Felicia attempts to flirt, but suddenly Superior Spider-Man attacks her punching her straight into her face taking out a tooth in the process. Superior Spider-Man continues the assault reminiscing about how he once tried to kill her, but now he was just apprehending her as a criminal. This leaves Felicia totally shocked and angry while swearing revenge after Superior Spider-Man wraps her in webbing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the conclusion of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Superior_Spider-Man" title="The Superior Spider-Man">The Superior Spider-Man</a></i> storyline returning the true Spider-Man, Black Cat later escapes at the beginning of the new <i>Amazing Spider-Man</i> series when Electro stages a breakout at the prison where she was being held.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Determined to regain her standing by defeating Spider-Man, she attacks him while he is trying to evacuate a burning building, forcing Spider-Man to pretend to still be Doctor Octopus to drive her away as he cannot afford to lose time rescuing civilians. Felicia then makes contact with Electro, suggesting that they collaborate in seeking revenge on Spider-Man.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Black Cat is in Parker Industries, making a record of all the tech they are currently producing. However she does not understand them, and opts to 'steal' someone who does. Sajani has had enough of Peter's constant disappearances and vows to make Peter see how he deals with everything without her help. Black Cat then pulls her away, commenting on her perfect timing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Black Cat and Electro later raid <a href="/wiki/Eel_(comics)" title="Eel (comics)">Eel</a>'s hideout where she has Electro defeat him. Black Cat and Electro later show up at a meeting between <a href="/wiki/Mister_Negative" title="Mister Negative">Mister Negative</a> and <a href="/wiki/Phil_Urich" title="Phil Urich">Phil Urich</a> (who is now leading the remnants of the Goblin Underground as the self-proclaimed Goblin King) where they throw Eel's body in between them. Black Cat states to Mister Negative and Phil Urich that she has heard about how they were outed by Spider-Man and wants in on their plan. During Peter's interview on the Fact Channel, Electro attacks the security team on the channel and Black Cat arrives announcing her demand that if Spider-Man does not show up in fifteen minutes, Peter will be dead. Electro complains about the plan but Black Cat stays determined that Spider-Man will show up, but then <a href="/wiki/Silk_(comics)" title="Silk (comics)">Silk</a> arrives to confront the both, giving Peter the time to change into Spider-Man and jump into action, naming Silk. As she battles against Electro and Spider-Man deals with Black Cat, J. Jonah Jameson remains on the channel forcing the cameraman to film the action. Spider-Man tries to convince Black Cat to stop even while she does not believe his story of being mind-swapped. Black Cat deviate one of Electro's bolts hitting Spider-Man, knocking him down in the process. Black Cat attempts to unmask Spider-Man as Jameson aims the camera,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> Jameson's angle prevents anyone seeing Spider-Man's face long enough for Silk to knock Black Cat back and Spider-Man to put his mask back on. Although Electro accepts being depowered when his uncontrollable abilities prove dangerous even to himself, Black Cat continues her vendetta against Spider-Man regardless of who is behind the mask. Following Electro's depowerment, the criminals at the Bar with No Name want Black Cat to lead them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Powers_and_abilities">Powers and abilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Cat_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Powers and abilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Initially, the Black Cat had no <a href="/wiki/Superhuman" title="Superhuman">superhuman</a> abilities. Later, a test induced by the Kingpin gave her the psionic ability to affect <a href="/wiki/Probability" title="Probability">probability</a> fields; essentially, she could produce "bad luck" for her enemies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> The "bad luck" power entails that under stress she is subconsciously able to cause anyone in her immediate vicinity that she perceives as a threat to be susceptible to freak accidents, like guns jamming and exploding, or tripping on objects. This ability also had the <a href="/wiki/Side_effect" title="Side effect">side effect</a> of eventually causing problems for anyone spending long periods of time around her.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Strange" title="Doctor Strange">Doctor Strange</a> eventually tampered with her powers that briefly removed her bad luck abilities, along with the unwanted side effect. However, this magical tampering temporarily endowed her with cat-like abilities, giving her <a href="/wiki/Night_vision" title="Night vision">night vision</a>, retractable talons in her fingertips, superhuman speed, strength, agility and endurance, proportionate to a cat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> Her "bad luck" powers were eventually restored by Doc Tramma through the use of <a href="/wiki/Cybernetics" title="Cybernetics">cybernetics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Black Cat has reflexes, agility, and stamina of an Olympic level acrobat. She is physically very strong and athletic and has great physical endurance. She is an excellent street fighter capable of taking on several armed assailants and incapacitating them without being injured herself. She is trained in several <a href="/wiki/Martial_arts" title="Martial arts">martial arts</a> styles.</p>
<p>The Black Cat has also acquired several devices from the <a href="/wiki/Tinkerer" title="Tinkerer">Tinkerer</a> that increase her agility and heighten her strength. She wears earrings that interact with the balance centers of her brain to grant her enhanced agility. She has contact lenses that let her see in various ranges of the electromagnetic spectrum, such as <a href="/wiki/Infrared" title="Infrared">infrared</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ultraviolet" title="Ultraviolet">ultraviolet</a>. Her costume contains micro-<a href="/wiki/Servomechanism" title="Servomechanism">servos</a> that enhance her strength above normal human levels. The gloves of her costume contain steel micro-filaments, which form retractable claws at the fingertips when she flexes her fingers (triggering a magnetic surge which condenses the filaments into polarized talons) which enable her to tear through most surfaces and easily scale walls. Using this equipment, the Black Cat has been able to beat enemies who have superhuman abilities.</p>
<p>The Black Cat has a miniature grappling hook device hidden in the "fur" of each glove, designed by her father <a href="/wiki/Walter_Hardy" title="Walter Hardy">Walter Hardy</a>, which enables her to swing from buildings in a manner similar to Spider-Man, though not quite as fast. She can also use the cable from this device as a tightrope, wall scaling device, swing line, or as a weapon in combat.</p>

She was originally a simple thief, who stole a cursed magical amulet for its monetary value, but this act attracted the attention of <a href="/wiki/Alternative_versions_of_Daredevil#Marvel_Mangaverse" title="Alternative versions of Daredevil">Matt Murdock, the Devil Hunter</a>, and she was cut in half. <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Marvel" title="Ultimate Marvel">Ultimate Marvel</a> universe, the Black Cat is Felicia Hardy, a young woman who blames her father’s death on <a href="/wiki/Kingpin_(comics)#Ultimate_Marvel" title="Kingpin (comics)">Wilson Fisk, the Kingpin</a>. Seeking revenge, she stole a priceless tablet that he was going to use on his comatose wife. Kingpin hired the assassin <a href="/wiki/Elektra_(comics)#Ultimate_Marvel" title="Elektra (comics)">Elektra</a> to retrieve it. When Black Cat threw it into the harbor, Elektra threw one of her <a href="/wiki/Sai_(weapon)" title="Sai (weapon)">sai</a> into the Black Cat's chest, sending the thief over the edge. She was presumed dead, though no body was found.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup></p>
<p>She later returned during a gang war, stopping <a href="/wiki/Hammerhead_(comics)#Ultimate_Hammerhead" title="Hammerhead (comics)">Hammerhead</a> from shooting <a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Spider-Man" title="Ultimate Spider-Man">Spider-Man</a> in the right temple.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> She later expressed interest in working for Hammerhead if it would mean getting rid of the Kingpin although Spider-Man opposed this.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> She later assisted Hammerhead and Elektra in a confrontation against Moon Knight, Shang-Chi, Iron Fist and Spider-Man.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p>
<p>Later, after the crisis was resolved, she passionately kissed Spider-Man through his mask, her previous interest in him having gotten stronger. She had apparently no idea of the age disparity between her and Peter, and mistakenly assumed, after he left in a rush, that he was a married man. When she managed to unmask him and realized how young he truly was, she was repulsed. The shock and embarrassment of such a discovery caused her to vomit on his costume.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> She is next seen trying to assassinate the Kingpin. However, <a href="/wiki/Mysterio" title="Mysterio">Mysterio</a> beats her to it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> She then tries to steal the Zodiac Key from the Kingpin's vault. Mysterio, knowing that it contained great power, tried to steal it from her. Felicia tried to wish Mysterio to go away, but instead she accidentally caused the key to create a massive explosion. Shocked by what she did, she handed it over to Mysterio. Spider-Man came to the scene with <a href="/wiki/Iron_Man" title="Iron Man">Iron Man</a>. Mysterio subdued Iron Man and then Spider-Man, Mysterio, and Felicia then fought over the key. Spider-Man won and then both he and Felicia decided to help the people who were caught in the middle of Felicia's mistake.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup></p>
<p>In this version, the Black Cat actually has short brunette hair, but wears a white-haired wig as part of her costume.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spider-Gwen">Spider-Gwen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Cat_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Spider-Gwen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On <a href="/wiki/Spider-Gwen" title="Spider-Gwen">Earth-65</a>, Felicia and her father are French and professional burglars. When her father stole Wilson Fisk's first dollar, Fisk sent Matt Murdock to kill him. Felicia went on to become a famous musician after singing in the streets and has a band called the Black Cats. She had a concert in New York with Mary Jane as the opening act to which she invited Murdock with the intention to kill him. While there, Murdock unleashes the Hand where she is defended by <a href="/wiki/Spider-Woman_(Gwen_Stacy)" title="Spider-Woman (Gwen Stacy)">Spider-Woman</a>. When she attempts to kill Matt, Spider-Woman knocks her out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="What_If.3F">What If?</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Cat_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: What If?">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <i><a href="/wiki/What_If_(comics)" title="What If (comics)">What If?</a></i> storyline <i>What if the Alien Costume had Possessed Spider-Man?</i>, after the <a href="/wiki/Symbiote_(comics)" title="Symbiote (comics)">symbiote</a> forms a permanent bond with Spider-Man due to <a href="/wiki/Mister_Fantastic" title="Mister Fantastic">Mister Fantastic</a> being unable to examine the web-slinger until it is too late to break them apart, Felicia goes on a brief crime spree until she learns that the symbiote—which here lives on adrenaline from superhumans—has abandoned the now-deceased Spider-Man in favor of the <a href="/wiki/Hulk_(comics)" title="Hulk (comics)">Hulk</a>; she favors killing the Hulk over the objections of the other heroes. After Spider-Man's funeral, she is contacted by the Kingpin, who helps her develop a weapon that will kill the symbiote in exchange for a lifetime of service from her; despite the heroes having already defeated the symbiote thanks to the actions of <a href="/wiki/Thor_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Thor (Marvel Comics)">Thor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Black_Bolt" title="Black Bolt">Black Bolt</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Strange" title="Doctor Strange">Doctor Strange</a> being about to banish it, Felicia kills the symbiote anyway, subsequently berating the heroes for not knowing the price she had to pay to ensure that justice was done.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup></p>
<p>In another storyline, Spider-Man marries the Black Cat rather than Mary Jane - <a href="/wiki/Alistair_Smythe" title="Alistair Smythe">Alistair Smythe</a>'s attack having left Mary Jane injured in this reality, leaving Peter doubting his ability to look after her, with he and Felicia rekindling their romance while fighting supervillains together, but their relationship is complicated by such factors as Peter and Felicia being unable to live together in order to preserve his secret identity. After Felicia's carelessness results in Spider-Man's identity being discovered by an unidentified informant, combined with her own jealousy over Peter's continued friendship with Mary Jane, she is killed by <a href="/wiki/Paladin_(comics)" title="Paladin (comics)">Paladin</a> after he mistakenly assumes she attempted to kill Mary Jane, resulting in Peter forming a romantic relationship with <a href="/wiki/Silver_Sable" title="Silver Sable">Silver Sable</a> as the two bond over their shared grief at the loss of their loved ones.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></p>

<li>Felicia Hardy/Black Cat appeared in the 1990s <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_(1994_TV_series)" title="Spider-Man (1994 TV series)">Spider-Man: The Animated Series</a></i>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vagueness" title="Wikipedia:Vagueness"><span title="This information is too vague. It's revealed her father The Cat was fooled into sneaking into an American lab during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, to memorize the super soldier formula that created <a href="/wiki/Captain_America" title="Captain America">Captain America</a> and report back to the <a href="/wiki/Red_Skull" title="Red Skull">Red Skull</a>. In the present, the Cat is in a <a href="/wiki/S.H.I.E.L.D." title="S.H.I.E.L.D.">S.H.I.E.L.D.</a> prison but is then broken out by the <a href="/wiki/Kingpin_(comics)" title="Kingpin (comics)">Kingpin</a>. <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Octopus" title="Doctor Octopus">Doctor Octopus</a> (working for the Kingpin) kidnaps Felicia as leverage and to be used as a guinea pig for an improved super soldier formula. The formula proves a success, making her overall physical prowess comparable to Spider-Man and also gives her the ability to switch between her normal blond haired form and her empowered well-muscled white haired form at will; she then recruited <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man" title="Spider-Man">Spider-Man</a> to assist in a rescue attempt for her father. After <a href="/wiki/Kraven_the_Hunter" title="Kraven the Hunter">Kraven the Hunter</a> was believed to be creating havoc in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, Black Cat helped make Spider-Man realize that Kraven was trying to help <a href="/wiki/Calypso_(comics)" title="Calypso (comics)">Dr. Crawford</a>. She was also featured in a smaller story arc where she was again kidnapped this time by <a href="/wiki/Alistair_Smythe" title="Alistair Smythe">Alistair Smythe</a> to be used as leverage to get Spider-Man to kidnap either the <a href="/wiki/Mac_Gargan" title="Mac Gargan">Scorpion</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Vulture_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Vulture (Marvel Comics)">Vulture</a>. It was later revealed that Smythe had developed a machine reversed engineered from the Time Tablet and Vulture's talons and that data extracted from the genetic and molecular structure of Scorpion (both Vulture and Scorpion suffered heavy exposure to Vulture's earlier prototypes) was needed. The three components were used in conjunction in an attempt to swap age and abilities of the baby <a href="/wiki/Silvermane" title="Silvermane">Silvermane</a> and Spider-Man but a rescue attempt by Black Cat, Vulture, and the freed Scorpion destroyed the machine and returned Silvermane to his proper age. After Morbius awakens as a vampire, she struggles at figuring out how to stop Morbius because of her personal feelings. She then finds herself teaming up with <a href="/wiki/Blade_(comics)" title="Blade (comics)">Blade</a>, Spider-Man, and Morbius to take down Blade's vampire mother who was using <a href="/wiki/Lizard_(comics)" title="Lizard (comics)">Dr. Connors</a>'s genetic recombinator to create an army of vampires. She then decides to leave Spider-Man to work for Blade along with Morbius, but not before she admits her love for the web-slinger. She does come back however to help Spider-Man defeat <a href="/wiki/Hydro-Man" title="Hydro-Man">Hydro-Man</a> and also appears at Peter's and Mary Jane's wedding. Black Cat allies herself with Spider-Man and Kingpin fighting droids created by Smythe that are <a href="/wiki/Wedding_crashing" title="Wedding crashing">crashing</a> the wedding. After Blade, Morbius, and Black Cat nearly succeeded in capturing Blade's mother, Black Cat was brought into the Secret War due to an effort by <a href="/wiki/Storm_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Storm (Marvel Comics)">Storm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iron_Man" title="Iron Man">Iron Man</a>, and Spider-Man. She is initially angry and wants to return to Earth. She nearly fell into a river of lava, but Spider-Man saved her by webbing Black Cat's ankle. When Red Skull's stronghold is attacked, she goes after Red Skull while the others are distracted trying to destroy the Cyber-Skulls. However she is seized by Doctor Octopus, covering her mouth. The other heroes are unable to attack with her hostage, but she is freed, though the two villains escape. She is later seen fighting them with other heroes before <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Doom" title="Doctor Doom">Doctor Doom</a> sends them back to Earth. After the Secret War was over she was sent back to the exact time and place she was taken from with no memory of the war. Despite her aristocratic background she flirts, kisses, or dates other characters in her age range. She does so more than once with Spider-Man while dating another person. It is alluded to that her promiscuous nature is rooted in her relationship with her father. With him having left her when she was young, she constantly tries to fill that void and if a man disappoints her, leaves, dies, etc.; she turns to Spider-Man because he tends to "always be there" unlike her father.</li>

In season two, she tries to break her father <a href="/wiki/Walter_Hardy" title="Walter Hardy">Walter Hardy</a> out of prison. <li>Black Cat made her video game debut as a playable character in the 1991 arcade game <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man:_The_Video_Game" title="Spider-Man: The Video Game">Spider-Man: The Video Game</a></i>, a side-scrolling fighting game. Black Cat can scratch enemies, pounce on them with leaping kicks, or use her grappling hook like a whip to strike from a distance.</li>
<li>Black Cat is a summon in the <a href="/wiki/Super_Nintendo_Entertainment_System" title="Super Nintendo Entertainment System">Super NES</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sega_Genesis" title="Sega Genesis">Genesis</a> game <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_and_Venom:_Maximum_Carnage" title="Spider-Man and Venom: Maximum Carnage">Spider-Man and Venom: Maximum Carnage</a></i>, where her attacks vary depending on whether the player is Spider-Man or <a href="/wiki/Venom_(comics)" title="Venom (comics)">Venom</a>. She also appears in many of the pages from the <i><a href="/wiki/Maximum_Carnage" title="Maximum Carnage">Maximum Carnage</a></i> comic book that were shown between levels.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_(console)" title="PlayStation (console)">PlayStation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dreamcast" title="Dreamcast">Dreamcast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">PC</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_64" title="Nintendo 64">Nintendo 64</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_(2000_video_game)" title="Spider-Man (2000 video game)">Spider-Man</a></i> video game, Black Cat appears in the first stage giving gaming tips every time the player walks into a question mark, and reappears later in the game. She is knocked out by the <a href="/wiki/Rhino_(comics)" title="Rhino (comics)">Rhino</a> and kidnapped by <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Octopus" title="Doctor Octopus">Doctor Octopus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carnage_(comics)" title="Carnage (comics)">Carnage</a> who have thugs pose as paramedics. Spider-Man eventually finds her and rescues her from the symbiote base. <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Hale" title="Jennifer Hale">Jennifer Hale</a> reprises her role from <i>Spider-Man: The Animated Series</i>.</li>
<li>Black Cat appears in the <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_2_(video_game)" title="Spider-Man 2 (video game)">Spider-Man 2</a></i> video game, voiced by <a href="/wiki/Holly_Fields" title="Holly Fields">Holly Fields</a>. She is not a playable character. During the game, Black Cat appears as a thief who develops an interest in Spider-Man. Throughout certain levels of the game Spider-Man has to meet with Black Cat in certain places on top of buildings; once met, Black Cat will usually race Spider-Man to another region which she claims to be trouble. During cutscenes in the game, Black Cat will continuously persuade Spider-Man to 'ditch' his normal life as Peter Parker for a permanent life as Spider-Man. Black Cat makes her first appearance during a theft at an art museum. She later reveals her identity as Black Cat before diving off a building and vanishing into the city below. She later leads Spider-Man to <a href="/wiki/Shocker_(comics)" title="Shocker (comics)">Shocker</a>'s location where she helps defeat the foe. Black Cat makes her final appearance in the game after Spider-Man helps her defeat a fleet of armed mechas, after which he tells her the world needs both Spider-Man and Peter Parker. She departs, telling Spider-Man "once I cross your path, I'm not so easy to get rid of." Two of the hint markers found in and around the city of the game also reveal tidbits of information about her.</li>
<li>Black Cat is a playable character in <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man:_Friend_or_Foe" title="Spider-Man: Friend or Foe">Spider-Man: Friend or Foe</a></i>, voiced by <a href="/wiki/Audrey_Wasilewski" title="Audrey Wasilewski">Audrey Wasilewski</a>. Spider-Man encounters her outside of Doctor Octopus' secret lab in a Japanese industrial plant. During the game Black Cat's skills are best suited to the art of infiltration, but she is no pushover in a fight. Her grappling hook, usually employed to climb walls, is a quick and efficient weapon in her capable hands. After meeting Black Cat, she will assist Spider-Man throughout the chapter. During fights Black Cat wields her grappling hook and has two power moves called: Heaven's Claw and Tornado Claw.</li>
<li>Black Cat appears in <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man:_Web_of_Shadows" title="Spider-Man: Web of Shadows">Spider-Man: Web of Shadows</a></i>, with <a href="/wiki/Tricia_Helfer" title="Tricia Helfer">Tricia Helfer</a> reprising her role. Near the beginning of the game, she serves as the first boss having gone to work for the Kingpin, though after the fight she revealed in utter distress that it was a complex attempt to impress Spider-Man and comes on to him. If the player chooses the Red Suit path, Spider-Man rejects her advances stating that he both does not want her and cannot be with her as he leaves to go meet up with Moon Knight. If the player chooses the Black Suit path, Spider-Man accepts Black Cat's advances and stays the night with her. If the player choose her, she will also give Spider-Man missions to track down who was manufacturing weapons for Kingpin instead of <a href="/wiki/Moon_Knight" title="Moon Knight">Moon Knight</a>. Sometime after helping Spider-Man, <a href="/wiki/S.H.I.E.L.D." title="S.H.I.E.L.D.">S.H.I.E.L.D.</a>, and Kingpin's forces clear a rooftop full of symbiotes, Black Cat becomes possessed by a symbiote and fights Spider-Man. After the fight, Black Cat is gravely injured, freeing her of the symbiote. The player is then given two choices again. If the player takes the Red Suit path, she is brought on to a S.H.I.E.L.D medivac uttering her last conscious words to Mary-Jane "He really does love you". If the player chooses the Black Suit path, Spider-Man reinfects her with his more powerful symbiote to heal her leaving her in more control of her symbiote. Doing this will cause Mary-Jane to leave in tears. In one Black Suit ending, Spider-Man rules with the symbiote-controlled Black Cat by his side telling Spider-Man that she had offered him the entire city.</li>
<li>The character is alluded in <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man:_Shattered_Dimensions" title="Spider-Man: Shattered Dimensions">Spider-Man: Shattered Dimensions</a></i>; Felicia Hardy's Noir version is referenced as <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_Noir" title="Spider-Man Noir">Spider-Man Noir</a>'s tip about <a href="/wiki/Hammerhead_(comics)" title="Hammerhead (comics)">Hammerhead</a>, and one of the background billboards in the <a href="/wiki/Marvel_2099" title="Marvel 2099">2099</a> era depicts Black Cat seemingly being used to advertise a shampoo brand.</li>
<li>An exclusive 2099 incarnation of Black Cat appears in <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man:_Edge_of_Time" title="Spider-Man: Edge of Time">Spider-Man: Edge of Time</a></i>, voiced by <a href="/wiki/Katee_Sackhoff" title="Katee Sackhoff">Katee Sackhoff</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-89"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup> According to her in-game profile, Alchemax acquired the original Black Cat's DNA when she came to the company looking for a way to halt or reverse the aging process. But she escaped when she realised the extent of Alchemax's corruption, clones of the woman were created by Alchemax as a security force. One clone was given the <a href="/wiki/Vanisher" title="Vanisher">Vanisher</a>'s powers, instilled with a powerful <a href="/wiki/Agoraphobia" title="Agoraphobia">agoraphobia</a> so that she would not be tempted to escape the building, upgraded with cybernetic weaponry, and has been seemingly force-fed anti-aging pills.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup> After fighting and defeating the other Black Cat clones, <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man" title="Spider-Man">Spider-Man</a> (Peter Parker) fights the special clone. After being defeated, she implied that she still has feelings for her opponent. Later when <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_2099" title="Spider-Man 2099">Spider-Man 2099</a> sets off to confront Alchemax's CEO, Spider-Man 2099 has to fight the other Black Cat clones along other Alchemax guards.</li>
<li>Black Cat is a playable character in <i><a href="/wiki/Marvel_Super_Hero_Squad_Online" title="Marvel Super Hero Squad Online">Marvel Super Hero Squad Online</a></i>, voiced by <a href="/wiki/Tara_Strong" title="Tara Strong">Tara Strong</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-voice_91-0"><a href="#cite_note-voice-91">[91]</a></sup></li>
<li>Black Cat is a playable character in the <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> game <i><a href="/wiki/Marvel:_Avengers_Alliance" title="Marvel: Avengers Alliance">Marvel: Avengers Alliance</a></i>.</li>
<li>Felicia Hardy serves as a villain in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Amazing_Spider-Man_(2012_video_game)" title="The Amazing Spider-Man (2012 video game)">The Amazing Spider-Man</a></i> video game, voiced by <a href="/wiki/Ali_Hillis" title="Ali Hillis">Ali Hillis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-92"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup> She is seen as a patient at Beloit Psychiatric Hospital until she escapes during the breakout. Felicia and her hired thugs, mercenaries, and urban commandos later invade St. Gabriel's Bank to rob it. She takes the bank director hostage in order to get into the vaults. When Spider-Man catches up to her in the vaults, she uses a subway car to escape into the subway. Upon catching up to Felicia in an underground parking lot, Spider-Man defeats her and hands her over to the police. In one of the photo missions, it is revealed Felicia escaped, gained cat like abilities, and has taken on her Black Cat persona. She even leaves a note with a paw mark and a kiss saying "You missed me Spider-Man."</li>
<li>Black Cat is available as downloadable content for <i><a href="/wiki/LittleBigPlanet" title="LittleBigPlanet">LittleBigPlanet</a></i>, as part of "Marvel Costume Kit 6".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LittleBigPlanetMCK6_93-0"><a href="#cite_note-LittleBigPlanetMCK6-93">[93]</a></sup></li>
<li>Black Cat appears in <i><a href="/wiki/Marvel_Heroes_(video_game)" title="Marvel Heroes (video game)">Marvel Heroes</a></i>.</li>
<li>Black Cat appears as a playable character in <i><a href="/wiki/Lego_Marvel_Super_Heroes" title="Lego Marvel Super Heroes">Lego Marvel Super Heroes</a></i>, voiced by <a href="/wiki/Laura_Bailey_(voice_actress)" title="Laura Bailey (voice actress)">Laura Bailey</a>.</li>
<li>Black Cat appears in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Amazing_Spider-Man_2_(2014_video_game)" title="The Amazing Spider-Man 2 (2014 video game)">The Amazing Spider-Man 2</a></i> video game, voiced again by Ali Hillis. Felicia Hardy was sprung from Ravencroft by the Kingpin who has doctors do a cross-species experiment on Felicia, causing her to become Black Cat.</li>
<li>A teenage version of Felicia Hardy/Black Cat appears as a sidekick character in <i><a href="/wiki/Disney_Infinity:_Marvel_Super_Heroes" title="Disney Infinity: Marvel Super Heroes">Disney Infinity: Marvel Super Heroes</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup></li>
<li>Black Cat appears as a <a href="/wiki/Player_character" title="Player character">player character</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_Unlimited_(video_game)" title="Spider-Man Unlimited (video game)">Spider-Man Unlimited</a></i>, again voiced by <a href="/wiki/Tara_Strong" title="Tara Strong">Tara Strong</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-95"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup></li>
<li>Black Cat appears as a playable character in the mobile game <i><a href="/wiki/Marvel:_Future_Fight" title="Marvel: Future Fight">Marvel: Future Fight</a></i>.</li>
</ul>
