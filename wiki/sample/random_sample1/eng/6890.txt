<p><i><b>Blackhat</b></i> is a 2015 American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Action_thriller" title="Action thriller">action thriller</a> <a href="/wiki/Mystery_film" title="Mystery film">mystery film</a> co-written, co-produced and directed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michael_Mann_(director)" title="Michael Mann (director)">Michael Mann</a>. The film stars <a href="/wiki/Chris_Hemsworth" title="Chris Hemsworth">Chris Hemsworth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tang_Wei" title="Tang Wei">Tang Wei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Viola_Davis" title="Viola Davis">Viola Davis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holt_McCallany" title="Holt McCallany">Holt McCallany</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wang_Leehom" title="Wang Leehom">Wang Leehom</a>. The film premiered at the TCL Chinese Theatre in Los Angeles on January 8, 2015, and was released in theaters on January 16.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <i>Blackhat</i> was a <a href="/wiki/Box_office_bomb" title="Box office bomb">box office bomb</a>, earning only $19.7 million at the box office against a budget of $70 million. While the movie received generally mixed-to-negative reviews, with criticisms focused on casting and pace, some critics found brilliance in the film, enough to place the film in some critics' year-end lists.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blackhat_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At a nuclear plant in <a href="/wiki/Chai_Wan" title="Chai Wan">Chai Wan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, a hacker causes the coolant pumps to overheat and explode. Not long after in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Mercantile_Exchange" title="Chicago Mercantile Exchange">Mercantile Trade Exchange</a> gets hacked, causing soy futures to rise. The <a href="/wiki/Government_of_China" title="Government of China">Chinese government</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FBI" title="FBI">FBI</a> determine that the hack was caused by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Remote_Access_Tool" title="Remote Access Tool">Remote Access Tool</a> (RAT). A military officer in China's cyber warfare unit, Captain Chen Dawai (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leehom_Wang" title="Leehom Wang">Leehom Wang</a>), is tasked to find the people responsible for the attacks, and enlists the aid of his sister Chen Lien (<a href="/wiki/Tang_Wei" title="Tang Wei">Tang Wei</a>), a hacking engineer. He meets with FBI Agent Carol Barrett (<a href="/wiki/Viola_Davis" title="Viola Davis">Viola Davis</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> and reveals the code in the RAT was written by himself and Nicholas Hathaway (<a href="/wiki/Chris_Hemsworth" title="Chris Hemsworth">Chris Hemsworth</a>), his college roommate, in their school days, years before Hathaway was sent to prison for an unrelated hack. Dawai asks that the FBI arrange for Hathaway to be released from prison, where he is serving a sentence for computer crimes. Hathaway is offered temporary release in exchange for his services.</p>
<p>A dead colleague's computer yields a clue to a connection, so Hathaway and Lien go to the rendezvous but it is a trap set by the hacker to alert him to pursuit. Hathaway fights three men.</p>
<p>In Hong Kong, the team works with Hong Kong inspector Alex Trang (<a href="/wiki/Andy_On" title="Andy On">Andy On</a>) and traces the stock trade money to a known paramilitary operative named Elias Kassar. A stakeout team is murdered while Hathaway, Jessup, Chen, Trang along with a Special Duties Unit team raid Kassar's hideout but once again it is a trap. A shootout ensues and Trang as well as a number of SDU officers are killed. Kassar and his men escape.</p>
<p>The nuclear plant has stabilized enough to retrieve a data drive from the control room but it is corrupted. Agent Barrett turns a blind eye while Hathaway hacks into the NSA to use a fossiled data tool called Black Widow to reconstruct the corrupted data. He learns that the hacker's server is based in <a href="/wiki/Jakarta" title="Jakarta">Jakarta</a>. Lien finds out the hacker has been buying high-resolution satellite photos of a site near <a href="/wiki/Seri_Manjung" title="Seri Manjung">Seri Manjung</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>.</p>
<p>Hathaway's hack into the NSA does not go unnoticed and the US government demands his return to prison. Dawai's superiors agree as they do not want to antagonize the US, while Barrett and her partner Agent Jessup receive orders to detain Hathaway. Dawai, Lien and Hathaway manage to elude their pursuers and make plans to leave Hong Kong and continue their investigation when they are attacked by Kassar and his men, who have been following them. Dawai is killed; shortly after, Barrett and Jessup, who have been tracking Hathaway using an ankle bracelet, arrive at the scene. While both Barrett and Jessup are killed in a firefight, they buy enough time for Lien and Hathaway to escape into the subway.</p>
<p>Hathaway and Lien travel to the location of the satellite photos in Malaysia to try and figure out the hacker's goal. Hathaway realizes that the hacker's attack at the nuclear plant was merely a test for a later plan to destroy several major tin mines in Malaysia, allowing the hacker to make a fortune trading tin futures, financed using the funds from the Mercantile Exchange hack. The two then travel to Jakarta and using a distraction manage to physically gain access to the hacker's server. They manage to transfer the hacker's money from his Hong Kong bank and force the hacker to contact them. The hacker (<a href="/wiki/Yorick_van_Wageningen" title="Yorick van Wageningen">Yorick van Wageningen</a>) and Hathaway agree to meet to negotiate the return of the money, supposedly in exchange for Hathaway's involvement in the larger scheme.</p>
<p>Hathaway insists the red hat and Kassar come alone but they bring their gang along. The meeting place is a crowded parade and Hathaway trails the hacker and Kassar from behind. Kassar pulls a gun on Hathaway but Hathaway is prepared and manages to stab him with a hidden screwdriver. Two of the hacker's men catch up and wound Hathaway, who manages to kill them. Hathaway finally faces off with the hacker and manages to kill him despite getting stabbed. He escapes the chaos of the parade with Lien's help, and then gets broke up. The film ends with Lien and Hathaway leaving <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, with the hacker's money still in their bank account.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blackhat_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tmulti tright">

<p>In an interview done at the LMU Film school, Michael Mann said he was inspired to make <i>Blackhat</i> after reading about the events surrounding <a href="/wiki/Stuxnet" title="Stuxnet">Stuxnet</a>, which was a computer worm that targeted and reportedly ruined almost one fifth of Iran's nuclear centrifuges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In keeping with his high standard for authenticity, Mann brought in several technical advisors and consultants like former hackers Christopher McKinley and <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Poulsen" title="Kevin Poulsen">Kevin Poulsen</a> (senior editor for <i>Wired News</i>), to make the film as authentic as possible.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> McKinley was famous for hacking the online dating site, OkCupid, in order to make his profile the most attractive to women.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Director Mann also met with <a href="/wiki/Mike_Rogers_(Michigan_politician)" title="Mike Rogers (Michigan politician)">Mike Rogers</a>, who was Chairman of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Permanent_Select_Committee_on_Intelligence" title="Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence">Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence</a> until 2015. <a href="/wiki/Parisa_Tabriz" title="Parisa Tabriz">Parisa Tabriz</a>, who manages Google's information security engineering team, said that "It's the most accurate information security film I've seen."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film was tentatively titled <i>Cyber</i>, however the final title was revealed on July 26, 2014 during a panel at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego_Comic-Con_International" title="San Diego Comic-Con International">San Diego Comic-Con International</a>, and it was being estimated that it might qualify for the Oscars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The first official trailer for the film was released on September 25, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-firstshowing.net_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-firstshowing.net-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Principal_photography" title="Principal photography">Filming</a> began on May 17, 2013, in Los Angeles, California; Hong Kong; Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia; and at Lapangan Banteng in Jakarta, Indonesia.</p>
<p>The film would be Mann's first feature to be shot entirely using digital cameras. Although <i><a href="/wiki/Collateral_(film)" title="Collateral (film)">Collateral</a>,</i> <i><a href="/wiki/Miami_Vice_(film)" title="Miami Vice (film)">Miami Vice</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Public_Enemies_(2009_film)" title="Public Enemies (2009 film)">Public Enemies</a></i> were predominantly digital features, Mann employed 35mm sparingly.</p>
<p>Director Mann donated HK$300,000 (US$38,500) to <a href="/wiki/The_Community_Chest_of_Hong_Kong" title="The Community Chest of Hong Kong">The Community Chest of Hong Kong</a> in the name of <a href="/wiki/Hang_Seng_Bank" title="Hang Seng Bank">Hang Seng Bank</a>, to thank the bank for allowing him to film <i>Blackhat</i> for five evenings in the bank's lobby area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2013, Universal set the North American release date for January 16, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Joko_Widodo" title="Joko Widodo">Joko Widodo</a>, President of Indonesia, visited the filming site in Jakarta.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Soundtrack">Soundtrack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blackhat_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Soundtrack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Film_score" title="Film score">film score</a> was composed by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Gregson-Williams" title="Harry Gregson-Williams">Harry Gregson-Williams</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-contentviewer.adobe.com_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-contentviewer.adobe.com-1">[1]</a></sup> with <a href="/wiki/Atticus_Ross" title="Atticus Ross">Atticus Ross</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-contentviewer.adobe.com_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-contentviewer.adobe.com-1">[1]</a></sup> Upon viewing the film, however, Gregson-Williams posted a message on Facebook stating that his score went almost unused in the final edit, which included synthesized music not prepared by him or by Ross.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He went on to say that, "I therefore reluctantly join the long list of composers who have had their scores either sliced and diced mercilessly or ignored completely by Michael Mann."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He stated that although he is credited for the score, the final film "contains almost none of my compositions".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Deadlinescore_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Deadlinescore-15">[15]</a></sup> He would later delete the status update containing this information.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Deadlinescore_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Deadlinescore-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mann later explained that he often prefers to use more than one composer "to rotate among different emotional perspectives", stating, "If a composer wants to have his music stand alone, he should be a recording artist and let his work contest itself in that arena."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blackhat_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Blackhat</i> opened on January 16, 2015, against the <a href="/wiki/Wide_release" title="Wide release">wide release</a> of <i>American Sniper</i>, an "unexpected juggernaut"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> which set records for the largest January opening weekend in history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> <i>Blackhat</i> was a <a href="/wiki/Box_office_bomb" title="Box office bomb">box office bomb</a>, opening at #11 and earning only $1.7 million on its opening day. It made just $4.4 million for the weekend against its $70 million budget.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deadline.com_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-deadline.com-19">[19]</a></sup> This made the movie one of the worst debuts ever for a movie playing in over 2,500 locations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>An in-depth analysis by industry trade publication <a href="/wiki/Deadline.com" title="Deadline.com"><i>Deadline</i></a> of why <i>Blackhat</i> did not perform primarily examined the marketing strategy as “the major challenge they were unable to overcome” <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-21">[21]</a></sup> with independent tracking services supporting this conclusion: “total awareness for <i>Blackhat</i> was in the 40-50% range on January 4 and grew to 50-60% on January 15 (versus <i>American Sniper</i>’s 80-90%).” <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-21">[21]</a></sup> Additionally, “the film wasn’t helped by a marketing campaign that failed to convey a sophisticated plot and a romance… <i>Blackhat</i> instead chased a young audience with action footage that did not seem fresh.” <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>After only two weeks, Universal decided to withdraw the film from all but 236 theaters. It had been in 2,568 theaters, making it the sixth-biggest drop in history for a third-week film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Internationally, the film grossed $2.33 million in 19 territories in its opening weekend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-23">[23]</a></sup> It played below expectations in markets including Denmark, Greece, Poland, Taiwan, Turkey and Vietnam.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Deadline credited <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lee_Hom_Wang" title="Lee Hom Wang">Lee Hom Wang</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tang_Wei" title="Tang Wei">Tang Wei</a>’s inclusion with increased success in other nations including Indonesia, Malaysia, Singapore, and Thailand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-23">[23]</a></sup> In its third weekend, the film grossed $1.8 million with openings of $595,000 and $446,000 in Russia and Spain, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In its fourth weekend, the film grossed $1.2 million for a total of $8.4 million, with its top opener in Germany at $526,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Due to the less-than-stellar numbers at the American and Asian box-office, Universal Pictures International opted not to release <i>Blackhat</i> theatrically in Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The film was also scrapped for a theatrical release in Belgium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the aftermath, Legendary took a $90 million write-down on the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 20, 2015, <i>Blackhat</i> debuted in the UK.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blackhat_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Blackhat</i> was released on <a href="/wiki/Blu-ray" title="Blu-ray">Blu-ray</a> and <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> on May 12, 2015 in North America. The Blu-ray edition includes both a DVD copy of the film and a voucher for an <a href="/wiki/UltraViolet_(system)" title="UltraViolet (system)">UltraViolet</a>/<a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> digital copy, as well as three featurettes: "The Cyber Threat", "On Location Around the World", and "Creating Reality".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The DVD edition contains only one featurette: "Creating Reality".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Australia, the film was originally slated to be released theatrically on February 25, 2015, but due to its poor performance at the US box office, it was instead released straight to home video on May 14, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> In the UK, the film was also released on Blu-ray and DVD on June 22, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Director.27s_Cut">Director's Cut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blackhat_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Director's Cut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Michael Mann premiered a re-edited Director's Cut of the film at the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Academy_of_Music" title="Brooklyn Academy of Music">Brooklyn Academy of Music</a> on February 20, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> The re-edited version played once, as part of a retrospective series of Mann's films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> The primary change in this cut was the movement of the film's nuclear reactor attack sequence from the opening to the middle of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Mann originally intended to place the reactor sequence in the middle, but moved it to the beginning of the theatrical cut just before its release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blackhat_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> the film holds a rating of 34%, based on 161 reviews, and an average rating of 4.9/10. The site's critical consensus reads, "Thematically timely but dramatically inert, <i>Blackhat</i> strands Chris Hemsworth in a muddled misfire from director Michael Mann."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, the film has a score of 51 out of 100, based on 37 critics, with 16 positive reviews, 12 mixed reviews, and 9 negative reviews, indicating "mixed or average reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> According to <a href="/wiki/CinemaScore" title="CinemaScore">CinemaScore</a>, audiences gave the film a grade of "C−" on an A+ to F scale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>For many critics, a significant issue of the film is the casting of <a href="/wiki/Chris_Hemsworth" title="Chris Hemsworth">Chris Hemsworth</a> as a hacker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Christy_Lemire" title="Christy Lemire">Christy Lemire</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Sun-Times" title="Chicago Sun-Times">Chicago Sun-Times</a></i> stated in her review, "Anyone who makes his or her way in the world sitting in front of a computer screen all day is not going to look as hunky as Hemsworth."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> However, <a href="/wiki/Manohla_Dargis" title="Manohla Dargis">Manohla Dargis</a> from <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> gave the film a largely positive review stating, “Michael Mann’s thriller ‘Blackhat,’ a story about the intersection of bodies and machines, is a spectacular work of unhinged moviemaking.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Turan" title="Kenneth Turan">Kenneth Turan</a> from <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Los_Angeles_Times" title="The Los Angeles Times">The Los Angeles Times</a></i> also gave it a positive review, writing, “It lures us in with the promise of up-to-the-minute villainy, but the satisfactions of ‘Blackhat’ are surprisingly old school.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i>’s Sheri Linden noted, “The essential problem of cyber-thrillers is one that even so gifted a director hasn’t quite solved, particularly in the film’s first half: Characters looking at computer screens and explaining the significance of what they see doesn’t make for the most riveting viewing.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Matt_Zoller_Seitz" title="Matt Zoller Seitz">Matt Zoller Seitz</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Editor-in-Chief" title="Editor-in-Chief">Editor-in-Chief</a> of <i>RogerEbert.com</i>, gave <i>Blackhat</i> three and a half out of four stars, stating in his review, "‘Blackhat’ is mainly about what happens when the real world is annexed by the virtual: what it does to geography and relationships; how it signal-jams our species' sense of time as a series of self-contained moments, and substitutes an existence that can feel like an endless, intrusive buzz."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Year-end_lists">Year-end lists</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blackhat_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Year-end lists">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
