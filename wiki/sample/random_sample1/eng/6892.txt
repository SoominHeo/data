<p><i><b>Bloodwork</b></i> – aka <i>Bloodworx</i> (US), and <i>The Last Experiment</i> (UK) – is a 2012 Canadian-American thriller directed by Eric Wostenberg and written by David Nahmod. The film stars <a href="/wiki/Travis_Van_Winkle" title="Travis Van Winkle">Travis Van Winkle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tricia_Helfer" title="Tricia Helfer">Tricia Helfer</a>. The production was filmed in Canada under the <a href="/wiki/Working_title" title="Working title">working title</a> <i>Phase One</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sud_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-sud-1">[1]</a></sup> in November and December 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloodwork_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Two friends have hopes of traveling around <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. As they need to fund the trip, they enlist as patients in allergy drug testing, a sure way to make easy money. However it turns out that rather than allergy medicine testing, they have enlisted in a government sponsored program on human regeneration. They are overwhelmed by the reality and seek to escape the medical unit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sud_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-sud-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloodwork_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
