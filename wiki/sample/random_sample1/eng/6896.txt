<p><b>David Blair Redford</b> (born July 27, 1983) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>. He is known for his television roles such as <a href="/wiki/Scotty_Grainger" title="Scotty Grainger">Scotty Grainger</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_and_the_Restless" title="The Young and the Restless">The Young and the Restless</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Lopez-Fitzgerald" title="Miguel Lopez-Fitzgerald">Miguel Lopez-Fitzgerald</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Passions" title="Passions">Passions</a></i>, Tyler "Ty" Mendoza on <i><a href="/wiki/Switched_at_Birth_(TV_series)" title="Switched at Birth (TV series)">Switched at Birth</a></i> and Ethan Whitehorse on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lying_Game" title="The Lying Game">The Lying Game</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blair_Redford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Redford grew up in the suburb of <a href="/wiki/Canton,_Georgia" title="Canton, Georgia">Canton, Georgia</a> and attended <a href="/wiki/Sequoyah_High_School_(Georgia)" title="Sequoyah High School (Georgia)">Sequoyah High School</a>. He is of <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_people" title="French people">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a> descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jns10_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-jns10-1">[1]</a></sup> He worked for several summers in Georgia's Renaissance Festival performing and doing stunts, creating a pirate character named "Rusty Compass." He is an avid tennis player, and turned down scholarships upon graduating high school in order to act.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blair_Redford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Redford got his start with a win in a <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Bros.</a> open call that granted him a seat as a team member of the WB Road Crew. After about a year and a half on the Road Crew, he moved to Los Angeles. After a short time there, he then landed the role of Scotty Grainger on the <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap opera</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_and_the_Restless" title="The Young and the Restless">The Young and the Restless</a></i> in July 2005, which he left in February 2006.</p>
<p>Redford later replaced <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Bellani" title="Adrian Bellani">Adrian Bellani</a> in the role of the Latino character Miguel Lopez-Fitzgerald in the soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/Passions" title="Passions">Passions</a></i> from 2007 to 2008 when the show moved from <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> exclusively to DirectTV for its final season. Redford also landed the starring role of Nash Rambler in <a href="/wiki/Gregg_Bishop" title="Gregg Bishop">Gregg Bishop</a>'s horror-comedy film <i>Dance of the Dead</i> which was released by <a href="/wiki/Sam_Raimi" title="Sam Raimi">Sam Raimi</a>'s <i>Ghost House Pictures</i> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lions_Gate_Entertainment" title="Lions Gate Entertainment">Lions Gate Entertainment</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Redford starred in <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">The CW</a> drama pilot <i>Betwixt</i>, which wasn't picked up to series, and also appeared in the third season of The CW's <i><a href="/wiki/90210_(TV_series)" title="90210 (TV series)">90210</a></i> as a recurring character named Oscar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He played James, a Bumper Band member in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Burlesque_(2010_American_film)" title="Burlesque (2010 American film)">Burlesque</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Cher" title="Cher">Cher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christina_Aguilera" title="Christina Aguilera">Christina Aguilera</a>. In 2011, he appeared in five episodes of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABC_Family" title="ABC Family">ABC Family</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Switched_at_Birth_(TV_series)" title="Switched at Birth (TV series)">Switched at Birth</a></i> as Tyler "Ty" Mendoza, then left the show to start filming for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABC_Family" title="ABC Family">ABC Family</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lying_Game" title="The Lying Game">The Lying Game</a></i>. He was in the series for two seasons before it was cancelled by the network. <a href="/wiki/BuddyTV" title="BuddyTV">BuddyTV</a> ranked him #17 on its list of "TV's Sexiest Men of 2011". He reprised his role in the second half of <i><a href="/wiki/Switched_at_Birth_(TV_series)" title="Switched at Birth (TV series)">Switched at Birth</a>'</i>s second season in summer 2013 and left to pursue other shows.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2013, it was announced that Redford would guest star as Zach in the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/Beauty_%26_the_Beast_(2012_TV_series)" title="Beauty &amp; the Beast (2012 TV series)">Beauty &amp; the Beast</a></i> on The CW.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-threat_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-threat-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 19, 2014, it was reported that Redford had been cast in <i><a href="/wiki/Satisfaction_(2014_TV_series)" title="Satisfaction (2014 TV series)">Satisfaction</a></i> as Simon. The male escort drama series aired for two seasons on the <a href="/wiki/USA_Network" title="USA Network">USA Network</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
