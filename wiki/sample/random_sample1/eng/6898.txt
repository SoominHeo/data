<p><i><b>Blade &amp; Soul</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">블레이드 앤 소울</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Beulleideu aen soul</span></i>) is a Korean fantasy martial-arts <a href="/wiki/Massively_multiplayer_online_role-playing_game" title="Massively multiplayer online role-playing game">massively multiplayer online role-playing game</a> developed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NCSOFT" title="NCSOFT">NCSOFT</a> (Team Bloodlust). On September 13, 2012, NCSOFT announced that <i>Blade &amp; Soul</i> would release in Western territories, which eventually happened on January 19, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">Japanese animated</a> television adaptation began airing on April 3, 2014 on <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Broadcasting_System" title="Tokyo Broadcasting System">TBS</a> and other stations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blade_%26_Soul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Blade &amp; Soul features a combination of martial arts inspired combat and <a href="/wiki/Qinggong" title="Qinggong">qinggong</a> in an open-world environment. Players create playable characters that explore around the world by completing quests assigned by various NPCs. The game uses a real-time battle system in the third person camera view and requires players to "combo" a series of attacks, much like that of fighting games. According to NCSOFT, the game also features an innovative “Downed” mechanic, allowing players to recover from the brink of death. Players begin with “player-versus environment” (PvE) but may participate in “player-versus-player” (PvP) combat later in the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Character_Customization">Character Customization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blade_%26_Soul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Character Customization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the developers, Blade &amp; Soul will provide a highly customizable system and it has been stated that NPCs in the game were made with the character customization system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Customizations a player has access to include hair styles, facial structures, facial markings and makeup, eye color, height, and body sliders.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Races">Races</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blade_%26_Soul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Races">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Characters may be one of four playable races based on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Four_benevolent_animals" title="Four benevolent animals">Four benevolent animals</a>: The Gon, the Jin, the Kun(Yun), or the Lyn.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blade_%26_Soul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Weeks before NCmedia day, NCSOFT announced a new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unreal_Engine_3" title="Unreal Engine 3">Unreal Engine 3</a> based <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MMORPG" title="MMORPG">MMORPG</a> only known for its codename Project [M]. For the next two weeks, a teaser website was updated revealing more information about the project.</p>
<p>During <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/E3_2007" title="E3 2007">E3 2007</a>, Sony revealed that they had entered into an exclusive deal with <a href="/wiki/NCsoft" title="NCsoft">NCsoft</a> to produce new titles exclusively for the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a>. This contract included titles from both existing intellectual properties owned by NCsoft.</p>
<p>On February 2010 it was announced that Blade &amp; Soul alongside <a href="/wiki/Guild_Wars_2" title="Guild Wars 2">Guild Wars 2</a> is being developed for release on the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a> and <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a>. However, since then, there have been no updates on the development of the titles for the consoles.</p>
<p>The first closed beta test was scheduled for 27 April 2011 while the open beta test and commercial release were to be determined within the year.</p>
<p>In July 2011, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tencent_Holdings" title="Tencent Holdings">Tencent</a> gained publishing rights of Blade &amp; Soul in China, and made its first appearance at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ChinaJoy" title="ChinaJoy">ChinaJoy</a> 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Blade &amp; Soul launched in China on November 23, 2013 with an open beta which seamlessly moved into official launch.</p>
<p>The second closed beta test was scheduled from August 29 to September 10, 2011. During this event the Assassin class was playable for the first time.</p>
<p>After an open beta test on June 21, 2012 with a record number of 150,000 players, NCSOFT confirmed that the official Korean Blade &amp; Soul launch would commence on June 30, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hyung-tae_Kim" title="Hyung-tae Kim">HTK</a> has stated that an English version of the game would indeed be released sometime in 2014 after China commercial launch, the new Blade &amp; Soul anime in Japan alongside Taiwan and Russia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 20, 2015 Blade &amp; Soul has been announced for NA and EU region and is set to release this winter. Closed beta officially began on October 29, 2015.</p>
<p>NA and EU beta testing occurred over the course of five weekends beginning on October 30, 2015, and concluding on December 21, 2015.</p>
<p>The official launches of NA and EU servers occurred on January 19, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blade_%26_Soul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blade_%26_Soul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>An anime adaptation of the game has been produced and began airing in April 2014, in which Hiroshi Hamasaki and Hiroshi Takeuchi directed the anime at studio <a href="/wiki/Gonzo_(company)" title="Gonzo (company)">Gonzo</a> from scripts by Atsuhiro Tomioka. Eri Nagata adapts <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kim_Hyung-tae" title="Kim Hyung-tae">Hyung-Tae Kim</a>'s game character designs for animation and serves as chief animation director.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The original storyline revolves around a female swordsman named Alka who travels to seek revenge for her murdered teacher.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ann0403_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ann0403-10">[10]</a></sup> The opening theme of the anime is "Sayonara Usotsuki" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">サヨナラ嘘ツキ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> by MimimemeMIMI, and the ending theme is "RAINBOW" by LEGO BIG MORL. The series was streamed by <a href="/wiki/Crunchyroll" title="Crunchyroll">Crunchyroll</a> in the West.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ann0403_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-ann0403-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
