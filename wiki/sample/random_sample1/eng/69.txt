<p><b>132524 APL</b>—previously known by its provisional designation, <b><span class="nowrap">2002 JF<sub>56</sub></span></b>—is an <a href="/wiki/Asteroid" title="Asteroid">asteroid</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Asteroid_belt" title="Asteroid belt">asteroid belt</a> approximately 2.3 kilometers across.</p>
<p>132524 APL was discovered on 9 May 2002 by the <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Near-Earth_Asteroid_Research" title="Lincoln Near-Earth Asteroid Research">Lincoln Near-Earth Asteroid Research</a> (LINEAR) Team at Socorro, NM.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jpldata_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-jpldata-1">[1]</a></sup> The <i><a href="/wiki/New_Horizons" title="New Horizons">New Horizons</a></i> probe flew by it at a distance of approximately 102,000 kilometers on 13 June 2006. The spectra obtained by <i>New Horizons</i> show that APL is a stony <a href="/wiki/S-type_asteroid" title="S-type asteroid">S-type asteroid</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBET-547_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-CBET-547-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The asteroid orbits the Sun in a somewhat eccentric orbit at a distance of 1.9–3.3 <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_unit" title="Astronomical unit">AU</a> once every 4.2 years. Its orbit is <a href="/wiki/Orbital_inclination" title="Orbital inclination">tilted</a> off the ecliptic by 4 degrees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jpldata_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-jpldata-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
