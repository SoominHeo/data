<p><b>July</b> is the seventh <a href="/wiki/Month" title="Month">month</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Year" title="Year">year</a> (between <a href="/wiki/June" title="June">June</a> and <a href="/wiki/August" title="August">August</a>) in the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gregorian_Calendar" title="Gregorian Calendar">Gregorian Calendars</a> and the fourth month to have the length of 31 days. It was named by the Roman Senate in honor of the Roman general, <a href="/wiki/Julius_Caesar" title="Julius Caesar">Julius Caesar</a>, it being the month of his birth. Prior to that, it was called <a href="/wiki/Quintilis" title="Quintilis">Quintilis</a>.</p>
<p>It is on average the warmest month in most of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_hemisphere" title="Northern hemisphere">Northern hemisphere</a> (where it is the second month of summer) and the coldest month in much of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_hemisphere" title="Southern hemisphere">Southern hemisphere</a> (where it is the second month of winter). The second half of the year commences in July. In the Southern hemisphere, July is the seasonal equivalent of January in the Northern hemisphere.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Dog_days" title="Dog days">Dog days</a>" are considered to begin in early July, when the hot sultry weather of summer usually starts. <a href="/wiki/Lamb_and_mutton#Classifications" title="Lamb and mutton">Spring lambs</a>, born in late winter or early spring, are usually sold before July 1.</p>
<p>July is the traditional period known as "fence month" (the closed season for <a href="/wiki/Deer_hunting#United_Kingdom" title="Deer hunting">deer</a> in England), the end <a href="/wiki/Trinity_term" title="Trinity term">Trinity term</a> (sitting of the High Court of Justice of England), taking place on July 31, and also the time in which the elections of Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_of_Councillors" title="House of Councillors">House of Councillors</a>, replacing half of its seats, held every three years (the latest one in <a href="/wiki/Japanese_House_of_Councillors_election,_2016" title="Japanese House of Councillors election, 2016">2016</a>).</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Ancient Rome</a> the festival of <a href="/wiki/Poplifugia" title="Poplifugia">Poplifugia</a> was celebrated on July 5, <a href="/wiki/Ludi_Apollinares" title="Ludi Apollinares">Ludi Apollinares</a> on July 13 and for several days afterwards, but these dates does not correspond to the modern <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian</a> one.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="July_symbols">July symbols</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=July&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: July symbols">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>July's <a href="/wiki/Birthstone" title="Birthstone">birthstone</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Ruby" title="Ruby">ruby</a>, which symbolizes contentment.</li>
<li>Its birth flowers are the <a href="/wiki/Delphinium" title="Delphinium">Larkspur</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Nymphaeaceae" title="Nymphaeaceae">Water Lily</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Zodiac" title="Zodiac">zodiac</a> signs for the month of July are <a href="/wiki/Cancer_(astrology)" title="Cancer (astrology)">Cancer</a> (until July 21) and <a href="/wiki/Leo_(astrology)" title="Leo (astrology)">Leo</a> (July 22 onwards).</li>
</ul>
