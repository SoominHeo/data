<p><b>Blake Adam Ritson</b> (born 14 January 1978<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>) is an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> actor and director.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blake_Ritson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Blake was born on 14 January 1978 in London and attended the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dolphin_School_(Berkshire)" title="Dolphin School (Berkshire)">Dolphin School</a> in Reading, Berkshire until 1993, before going to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Paul%27s_School_(London)" title="St Paul's School (London)">St Paul's School</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_(London_sub_region)" title="West (London sub region)">West London</a> on an academic scholarship. He then attended <a href="/wiki/Jesus_College,_Cambridge" title="Jesus College, Cambridge">Jesus College, Cambridge</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> where he studied English and Medieval Italian, graduating in 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officiallondontheatre.co.uk_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-officiallondontheatre.co.uk-3">[3]</a></sup> While a student he acted on both stage and screen,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dolphinschool.com_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-dolphinschool.com-4">[4]</a></sup> playing Paul Etheridge in <i>White Chameleon</i>, <a href="/wiki/Fleance" title="Fleance">Fleance</a> in <i>Macbeth</i>, and Augustus in <a href="/wiki/Tom_Stoppard" title="Tom Stoppard">Tom Stoppard</a>’s <i>Arcadia</i> (1996) at the National Theatre in productions directed by Richard Eyre and Trevor Nunn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officiallondontheatre.co.uk_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-officiallondontheatre.co.uk-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blake_Ritson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ritson is best known in recent years for playing King Edward III in the TV miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/World_Without_End_(miniseries)" title="World Without End (miniseries)">World Without End</a></i> (2012), the Duke of Kent in <a href="/wiki/Upstairs_Downstairs_(2010_TV_series)" title="Upstairs Downstairs (2010 TV series)"><i>Upstairs Downstairs</i></a> (2010-2012), Mr Elton in the <a href="/wiki/Emma_(2009_TV_serial)" title="Emma (2009 TV serial)">2009 BBC adaptation of <i>Emma</i></a>, Edmund Bertram in the <a href="/wiki/Mansfield_Park_(2007_film)" title="Mansfield Park (2007 film)">2007 ITV adaptation of <i>Mansfield Park</i></a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Giles Vicary in the BBC series <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Cap_(TV_series)" title="Red Cap (TV series)">Red Cap</a></i> and for portraying sidekick Robert Presley in <i><a href="/wiki/A_Touch_of_Frost" title="A Touch of Frost">A Touch of Frost</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He also played the part of Justin in <i><a href="/wiki/The_League_of_Gentlemen" title="The League of Gentlemen">The League of Gentlemen</a></i>, Idek in <i><a href="/wiki/God_on_Trial" title="God on Trial">God on Trial</a></i>, and the comic lead in <i><a href="/wiki/For_Elsie_(film)" title="For Elsie (film)">For Elsie</a></i>, an Oscar-winning short film, which he also co-produced. He also portrayed the main antagonist in David Goyer’s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Da_Vinci%E2%80%99s_Demons" title="Da Vinci’s Demons">Da Vinci’s Demons</a></i> (2013).</p>
<p>Ritson writes and directs with his brother, Dylan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The brothers first directed the short film <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Out_of_Time_(2004_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Out of Time (2004 film) (page does not exist)">Out of Time</a></i>. Starring <a href="/wiki/Mark_Heap" title="Mark Heap">Mark Heap</a> and <a href="/wiki/Raquel_Cassidy" title="Raquel Cassidy">Raquel Cassidy</a>, the film won the Global Audience Award at the first <a href="/wiki/CON-CAN_Movie_Festival" title="CON-CAN Movie Festival">CON-CAN Movie Festival</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and was runner up at Minimalen and the Berlin Film Festival. It also screened at the London, São Paulo and Dresden film festivals. Their second short film, <i>More More More</i>, starring The League of Gentlemen's <a href="/wiki/Mark_Gatiss" title="Mark Gatiss">Mark Gatiss</a>, was a runner-up in the Turner Classic Movies Competition. Their third film, shot in 2009, was entitled <i>Love Hate</i>, and starred <a href="/wiki/Ben_Whishaw" title="Ben Whishaw">Ben Whishaw</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hayley_Atwell" title="Hayley Atwell">Hayley Atwell</a>. It was chosen for the <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_International_Film_Festival" title="Edinburgh International Film Festival">Edinburgh International Film Festival</a>, Palm Springs Film Festival, London BFI Film Festival, Edinburgh International Film Festival, Foyle, Omaha, Encounters, <a href="/wiki/Berkshire_International_Film_Festival" title="Berkshire International Film Festival">Berkshire International Film Festival</a>, Sofia Film Fest (Bulgaria), <a href="/wiki/Tofifest" title="Tofifest">Tofifest</a> International Film Festival (Poland), West Hollywood International, LA Shorts Fest and Short Shorts Film Festival (Japan). It won the Jury Award at Palm Springs International Shortsfest and won the Best International Short award at Cinema St. Louis Film Festival.</p>
<p>The Ritson brothers have also shot a fourth short film, <i>Good Boy</i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Hynes" title="Jessica Hynes">Jessica Hynes</a> (née Stevenson), <a href="/wiki/Reece_Shearsmith" title="Reece Shearsmith">Reece Shearsmith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Burns_(actor)" title="Nicholas Burns (actor)">Nicholas Burns</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joanna_Page" title="Joanna Page">Joanna Page</a> and Blake Ritson's fiancee <a href="/wiki/Hattie_Morahan" title="Hattie Morahan">Hattie Morahan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Ritson Brothers were selected for the front cover of <a href="/wiki/Screen_International" title="Screen International">Screen International</a>'s <i>Stars Of Tomorrow 2009</i>.</p>
<p>Ritson played the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Banjolele" title="Banjolele">banjolele</a> on <a href="/wiki/Douglas_Hodge" title="Douglas Hodge">Douglas Hodge</a>'s 2006 album <i>Cowley Road Songs</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> which was recorded in four days at the Blue Moon Studios in Banbury. He describes the banjolele as "a hybrid instrument between a ukulele and a banjo; teamed up with the gazoo it’s a winning combination."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officiallondontheatre.co.uk_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-officiallondontheatre.co.uk-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, Ritson appeared as Colonel Brandon in <a href="/wiki/Helen_Edmundson" title="Helen Edmundson">Helen Edmundson</a>'s <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_4" title="BBC Radio 4">BBC Radio 4</a> adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Sense_and_Sensibility" title="Sense and Sensibility">Sense and Sensibility</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blake_Ritson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ritson is engaged to actress Hattie Morahan, whom he met at Cambridge University. They have a daughter, Amity, born in August 2016 and are living in North London. Ritson is the brother of Dylan Ritson, with whom he directs and writes.</p>
