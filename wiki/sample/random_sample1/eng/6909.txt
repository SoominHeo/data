<p><i><b>Tera</b></i> is a 3D fantasy themed <a href="/wiki/Massively_multiplayer_online_role-playing_game" title="Massively multiplayer online role-playing game">massively multiplayer online role-playing game</a> (MMORPG) developed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bluehole_Inc." title="Bluehole Inc.">Bluehole Inc.</a>. The game was released in South Korea on January 25, 2011, in Japan on August 18, 2011, in North America on May 1, 2012, in Europe on May 3, 2012, and in Russia on February 26, 2015, with closed and open beta testings taking place before the launch dates. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NHN_Corporation" title="NHN Corporation">NHN Corporation</a>, NHN Japan Corporation, <a href="/wiki/En_Masse_Entertainment" title="En Masse Entertainment">En Masse Entertainment</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gameforge" title="Gameforge">Gameforge</a> publishes the game in these regions, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gameforge_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gameforge-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nadate_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-nadate-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-localization_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-localization-5">[5]</a></sup> In February 2013 the game was renamed to <i>Tera: Rising</i> concurrently with the game's launch to the <a href="/wiki/Free-to-play" title="Free-to-play">free-to-play</a> model.</p>
<p>In September 2014 the game was renamed to "Tera: Fate of Arun" in the same patch that added a new level cap and expanded Tera's horizons with the new continent in Northern Arun: Val Oriyn "cut off from the rest of the world for centuries, Northern Arun is a land of savage jungles, colossal ruins, and the undiscovered homeland of the Barakas" <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Patch29.03.02_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Patch29.03.02-6">[6]</a></sup> The patch became effective on December 2014 in America and Europe .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Patch29.03.02_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Patch29.03.02-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Patchnotes29.03_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Patchnotes29.03-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tera_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Tera</i> has typical MMORPG features such as questing, crafting, and <a href="/wiki/Player_versus_player" title="Player versus player">player versus player</a> action. The game's combat uses a real-time battle system that incorporates <a href="/wiki/Virtual_camera_system#Third-person_view" title="Virtual camera system">third-person camera view</a>. The player targets an enemy with a cross-hair cursor rather than clicking or tabbing an individual opponent (which is called the "Non-Target battle system" by the developer). The Players need to actively dodge enemy attacks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ign_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ign-8">[8]</a></sup> A keyboard and mouse or a <a href="/wiki/Gamepad" title="Gamepad">control pad</a> can be used to control the character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamespot_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-gamespot-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Characters may be one of seven races.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamespot_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-gamespot-9">[9]</a></sup> Each race has a set of unique "racial skills" that gives them minor advantages. Races also have their own unique animations for many class-specific skills. <i>Tera</i> also has 12 classes (as of August, 2016),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mmobyte_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-mmobyte-10">[10]</a></sup> each with their own unique abilities and attributes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-teraclasses_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-teraclasses-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The developers collaborated with <a href="/wiki/CCP_Games" title="CCP Games">CCP Games</a> and their successful use of "PLEX" for <i><a href="/wiki/Eve_Online" title="Eve Online">Eve Online</a></i> as a way of deterring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gold_farmers" title="Gold farmers">gold farmers</a>. As a result, <i>Tera</i> released a currency called "Chronoscrolls" that works similarly, as it allows game time to be purchased with real money and sold for in-game gold. The use of Chronoscrolls is only available to users who have purchased the game either digitally or physically. Those with the "Discovery Edition" cannot use Chronoscrolls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enmasse_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-enmasse-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p>In February 2013, Tera in North America and Europe transitioned to "Tera Rising", which changed the subscription model to a "<a href="/wiki/Freemium" title="Freemium">freemium</a>" design (free play plus purchasable premium status and customization options). The developer has promised no time, level, or content restrictions for free players after this update.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-risingpromo_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-risingpromo-13">[13]</a></sup> Players who purchase a 30-day "Elite" status will receive increased dungeon rewards, in-game discounts and other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metagame" title="Metagame">metagame</a> advantages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-risingarticle_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-risingarticle-14">[14]</a></sup> Due to the new model, the "Chronoscrolls" mentioned above are being phased out, and are no longer sold by the developer, nor usable to extend game time. Existing chronoscrolls can be sold to merchants for 2,000 gold.</p>
<p>The Japanese and Korean versions of Tera also became free to play in December 2012 and January 2013, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-f2pAsia_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-f2pAsia-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<p><i>Tera</i> has been met with generally favorable reviews,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> citing the action-based gameplay, huge and widely varied seamless world, the exciting and recurring Big Ass Monster (BAM)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> fights, the ease of using the game's auction house (called the Trade Broker) and lush, detailed graphics as the game's high points.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pcgamer1_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-pcgamer1-25">[25]</a></sup> The option of using a console controller also provided much satisfaction for players that wanted to play an MMORPG without relying on a keyboard and mouse.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pcgamer1_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-pcgamer1-25">[25]</a></sup> The game's musical score received praise for its varied and emotionally charged tracks ranging from epic orchestral pieces to more mellow tunes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Criticism is directed at the game's generic collect/kill/rendezvous quest grind to level up, with some quests backtracking simply to talk to the same NPC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-27">[27]</a></sup> Player killing is commonly done in the Open World and has been met with a lot of praise due to the freedom and constant action it can bring to daily questing and traveling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-27">[27]</a></sup> Critics have targeted the Nexus Wars feature as a blatant rip-off of <a href="/wiki/Rift_(video_game)" title="Rift (video game)">Rift</a>'s system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2013, after switching to a free-to-play business model, the game passed 1.4 million registered accounts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 5, 2015 Tera's new patch <b>31.04</b> was released in which the game became available on Steam. Along with being available on Steam, the gunner class was released and new dungeons were added.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Bluehole also "release[d] inactive character names, making them available to new characters."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trade_secret_civil_actions">Trade secret civil actions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tera_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Trade secret civil actions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2007, <a href="/wiki/NCsoft" title="NCsoft">NCsoft</a> filed a complaint to South Korean gosu gamer authorities and brought a civil action for damages and an injunction to Bluehole. The Bluehole founders and employees, formerly employed under NCsoft and working on the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lineage_III" title="Lineage III">Lineage III</a></i> development team, were convicted by a Korean criminal court for the theft of valuable trade secrets from NCsoft in 2009. In 2010, a Korean civil court held these individuals, along with Bluehole, liable for misappropriation of trade secrets, awarding NCsoft about $2 million in damages and issuing an injunction against utilizing trade secrets for monetary gain. The damages were later reversed by an appellate court, but the injunction remained in place. Despite the injunction, Bluehole developed and released <i>Tera</i> in South Korea.</p>
<p>On January 9, 2012, NCsoft filed a civil action in the United States against Bluehole and its U.S. subsidiary En Masse. NCsoft was seeking a preliminary and permanent injunction prohibiting the launch of <i>Tera</i> in the United States, or damages for the substantial harm that the launch would cause NCsoft. They asserted claims for copyright infringement, trade secret misappropriation, breach of confidence, unfair competition, and unjust enrichment under the laws of the state of New York.</p>
<p>On April 18, 2012, En Masse Entertainment announced they were found not guilty in the Korean civil actions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theft2_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-theft2-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-notguilty_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-notguilty-34">[34]</a></sup> However, three employees were found guilty.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
