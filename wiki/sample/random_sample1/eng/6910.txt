<p><b>731 Lexington Avenue</b> is a 1,345,489 sq ft (125,000.0 m<sup>2</sup>) glass <a href="/wiki/Skyscraper" title="Skyscraper">skyscraper</a> on the East Side of <a href="/wiki/Midtown_Manhattan" title="Midtown Manhattan">Midtown Manhattan</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It houses the headquarters of <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_L.P." title="Bloomberg L.P.">Bloomberg L.P.</a> and as a result, is sometimes referred to informally as <b>Bloomberg Tower</b>. The building also houses retail outlets, restaurants and 105 luxury <a href="/wiki/Condominium" title="Condominium">condominiums</a>. The residences are known as One Beacon Court and are served by a separate entrance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_Background_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_Background-1">[1]</a></sup> The tower is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_tallest_buildings_in_New_York_City" title="List of tallest buildings in New York City">15th tallest building</a> in New York City and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_tallest_buildings_in_the_United_States" title="List of tallest buildings in the United States">46th tallest</a> in the United States. It stands at 55 stories tall, reaching 806 ft (246 m).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_55_Stories_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_55_Stories-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Located at 731 <a href="/wiki/Lexington_Avenue" title="Lexington Avenue">Lexington Avenue</a>, the building occupies the same block where <a href="/wiki/Alexander%27s" title="Alexander's">Alexander's</a> department store once stood. It opened in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_Background_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_Background-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Construction_and_design">Construction and design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=731_Lexington_Avenue&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Construction and design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Construction on 731 Lexington Avenue began in 2001, three years after the vacant <a href="/wiki/Alexander%27s" title="Alexander's">Alexander's</a> department store was demolished.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_Background_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_Background-1">[1]</a></sup> The building was developed by <a href="/wiki/Vornado_Realty_Trust" title="Vornado Realty Trust">Vornado Realty Trust</a> and <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A9sar_Pelli" title="César Pelli">César Pelli &amp; Associates</a> served as the design architect.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Construction_Details_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Construction_Details-2">[2]</a></sup> The building comprises a full city block between Lexington and Third Avenues and 58th and 59th Streets. The complex features two towers constructed above a steel office and retail section, separated by a seven-story atrium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Construction_Details_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Construction_Details-2">[2]</a></sup> The residences are known as One Beacon Court and are served by a separate entrance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The design of the courtyard at One Beacon Court, which connects 58th and 59th Streets, was inspired by other historic New York enclosures such as <a href="/wiki/Grand_Central_Terminal" title="Grand Central Terminal">Grand Central Terminal</a>, the reading room of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Public_Library_Main_Branch" title="New York Public Library Main Branch">New York Public Library Main Branch</a>, and the skating rink at <a href="/wiki/Rockefeller_Center" title="Rockefeller Center">Rockefeller Center</a>; glass walls curve around the perimeter of the courtyard, tilting slightly inward as they rise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Building_Architecture_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Building_Architecture-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tenants">Tenants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=731_Lexington_Avenue&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tenants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_L.P." title="Bloomberg L.P.">Bloomberg L.P.</a>, seeking to consolidate its operations into one space, negotiated a deal with Vornado Realty Trust to lease 700,000 sq ft (65,000 m<sup>2</sup>) of office space at the base of the tower, even though One Beacon Court was originally intended to be a residential building. As part of the deal, Bloomberg with STUDIOS Architecture as their design firm, was able to design the office to suit the company's needs, which included space for rows of uniform desks for approximately 4,000 employees and a large central atrium known as the Link that overlooks the courtyard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bloomberg_office_space_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bloomberg_office_space-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Paul_Goldberger" title="Paul Goldberger">Paul Goldberger</a>, in the Aug. 6, 2007, issue of <i>New Yorker</i> magazine, lauded the Bloomberg offices as "a newsroom truly designed for the electronic age ... a dazzling work environment tucked inside a refined but conventional skyscraper."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Yorker_Background_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_Yorker_Background-10">[10]</a></sup> By 2011, Bloomberg L.P. added another 200,000 sq ft (19,000 m<sup>2</sup>) of office space at 731 Lexington Avenue and announced plans to expand to an additional 400,000 sq ft (37,000 m<sup>2</sup>) in the former Philip Morris building at 120 Park Avenue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Additional_expansion_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Additional_expansion-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The nationally syndicated talk show <i><a href="/wiki/Charlie_Rose_(TV_series)" title="Charlie Rose (TV series)">Charlie Rose</a></i> is produced and filmed at 731 Lexington Avenue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Bloomberg L.P.'s own <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Television" title="Bloomberg Television">Bloomberg Television</a> also has its flagship studio housed there.</p>
<p>Primary retail tenants include the first large national chains to open stores in the neighborhood, namely <a href="/wiki/The_Home_Depot" title="The Home Depot">The Home Depot</a>, <a href="/wiki/H%26M" title="H&amp;M">H&amp;M</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Container_Store" title="The Container Store">The Container Store</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_Background_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_Background-1">[1]</a></sup> The tower is also home to <a href="/wiki/Sirio_Maccioni" title="Sirio Maccioni">Sirio Maccioni's</a> <a href="/wiki/Le_Cirque" title="Le Cirque">Le Cirque</a> restaurant which moved from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_New_York_Palace_Hotel" title="The New York Palace Hotel">The New York Palace Hotel</a> in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Le_Cirque_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Le_Cirque-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of 2014<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=731_Lexington_Avenue&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, residential tenants included <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>, former <a href="/wiki/NBC_Nightly_News" title="NBC Nightly News">NBC Nightly News</a> anchor <a href="/wiki/Brian_Williams" title="Brian Williams">Brian Williams</a>, and baseball players <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Damon" title="Johnny Damon">Johnny Damon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Abreu" title="Bobby Abreu">Bobby Abreu</a>. <a href="/wiki/Steven_A._Cohen" title="Steven A. Cohen">Steven A. Cohen</a> cut the price on his duplex-penthouse from $115 million to $79 million as of June 2015, and a year later (June 2016) to $72 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-duplex_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-duplex-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Amenities">Amenities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=731_Lexington_Avenue&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Amenities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Residents have access to concierge service by the London-based Quintessentially, as well as use of the Beacon Club, the 29th-floor amenity space that includes fitness center, entertainment suite, children's playroom and business center. Other amenities include 24-hour doorman, garage and valet parking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
