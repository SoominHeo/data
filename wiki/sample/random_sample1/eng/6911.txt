<p><b>Bloomsbury Publishing plc</b> (formerly <b>M.B.N.1 Limited</b> and <b>Bloomsbury Publishing Company Limited</b>) is a British independent, worldwide publishing house of fiction and non-fiction. It is a constituent of the <a href="/wiki/FTSE_SmallCap_Index" title="FTSE SmallCap Index">FTSE SmallCap Index</a>. Bloomsbury's head office is located in <a href="/wiki/Bloomsbury" title="Bloomsbury">Bloomsbury</a>, an area of the <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Camden" title="London Borough of Camden">London Borough of Camden</a> and it has a US publishing office located in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, an India publishing office in <a href="/wiki/New_Delhi" title="New Delhi">New Delhi</a> and an Australia sales office in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sydney_CBD" title="Sydney CBD">Sydney CBD</a>. The company's growth over the past two decades is primarily attributable to the <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a></i> series by <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> and, from 2008, to the development of its academic and professional publishing division. The Bloomsbury Academic &amp; Professional division won the Bookseller Industry Award for Academic, Educational &amp; Professional Publisher of the Year in both 2013 and 2014.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Divisions">Divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloomsbury_Publishing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bloomsbury Publishing group has four separate publishing divisions — Academic &amp; Professional; Adult; Children's and Educational; and Information and Business Development — supported by group functions, namely Sales and Marketing, Book Production, Finance and Technology. Imprints and book lists of publishing businesses acquired by Bloomsbury are assigned to the most relevant publishing division.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloomsbury_Publishing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The company was founded in 1986 by <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Newton" title="Nigel Newton">Nigel Newton</a>, who had previously been employed by other publishing companies. It was floated as a public registered company in 1994, raising £5.5million, which was used to fund expansion of the company into paperback and children's books. A rights issue of shares in 1998 raised a further £6.1m, which was used to expand the company, in particular to found a U.S. branch. In 1998, Bloomsbury USA was established. Bloomsbury USA Books for Young Readers was established in 2002, and in 2005, Bloomsbury acquired Walker &amp; Co, a small company dedicated to publishing nonfiction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2008, Bloomsbury opened a branch in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doha,_Qatar" title="Doha, Qatar">Doha, Qatar</a>, under joint-partnership with <a href="/wiki/Qatar_Foundation" title="Qatar Foundation">Qatar Foundation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The publishing house created, called <a href="/wiki/Bloomsbury_Qatar_Foundation_Publishing" title="Bloomsbury Qatar Foundation Publishing">Bloomsbury Qatar Foundation Publishing</a>, worked mainly with English and Arabic literature.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bloomsbury_Qatar_Foundation_Journals" title="Bloomsbury Qatar Foundation Journals">Bloomsbury Qatar Foundation Journals</a> (BQFJ), an <a href="/wiki/Open_access" title="Open access">open access</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peer_review" title="Peer review">peer review</a> academic publisher, was created in December 2010 as a joint venture with Qatar Foundation. Journal research articles were published through BQFJ's website <i>Qscience.com</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2012, Bloomsbury established a publishing office in India.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The company's partnership with Qatar Foundation ended in December 2015 and all of Bloomsbury Qatar Foundation Publishing works were incorporated in Qatari-owned HBKU Press. At the time of BQFP's dissolution it had published over 200 books. BQFJ's works were also incorporated in HBKU Press.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
