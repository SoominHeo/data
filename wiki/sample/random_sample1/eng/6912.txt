<p><b>Bloemfontein</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'b' in 'buy'">b</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/uː/ long 'oo' in 'food'">uː</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="'f' in 'find'">f</span><span title="/ɒ/ short 'o' in 'body'">ɒ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span><span title="/ˌ/ secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span></span>/</a></span></span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <small>Afrikaans pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Afrikaans" title="Help:IPA for Afrikaans">[ˈblumfɔntəi̯n]</a></span>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Afrikaans_language" title="Afrikaans language">Afrikaans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a> previously "fountain of flowers" or "blooming fountain;" also known as <b>Bloem</b>) is the capital city of the province of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Free_State_(South_African_province)" title="Free State (South African province)">Free State</a> of <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>; and, as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Judicial" title="Judicial">judicial</a> capital of the nation, one of South Africa's three national capitals; the other two capitals are <a href="/wiki/Cape_Town" title="Cape Town">Cape Town</a>, the legislative capital, and <a href="/wiki/Pretoria" title="Pretoria">Pretoria</a>, the administrative capital. Bloemfontein is the sixth largest city in South Africa.</p>
<p>Bloemfontein was popularly and poetically known as "the city of <a href="/wiki/Rose" title="Rose">roses</a>", owing to the abundance of these flowers and the annual rose festival held there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The city's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sesotho" title="Sesotho">Sesotho</a> name is <i>Mangaung</i>, meaning "place of <a href="/wiki/Cheetah" title="Cheetah">cheetahs</a>".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Bloemfontein has since 2011 formed part of the Mangaung Metropolitan Municipality, having been in the Mangaung Local Municipality beforehand.</p>
<p>Bloemfontein is situated grassland at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Bloemfontein&amp;params=29_06_S_26_13_E_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">29°06′S</span> <span class="longitude">26°13′E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">29.100°S 26.217°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">-29.100; 26.217</span></span></span></a></span>, at an altitude of 1,395 m (4,577 ft) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Above_mean_sea_level" title="Above mean sea level">above sea level</a>. The city is home to 369,568 residents, while the <a href="/wiki/Mangaung_Metropolitan_Municipality" title="Mangaung Metropolitan Municipality">Mangaung Metropolitan Municipality</a> has a population of 645,455.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bloemfontein,_from_the_South_-_c1900.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="450" data-file-width="665" height="149" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Bloemfontein%2C_from_the_South_-_c1900.JPG/220px-Bloemfontein%2C_from_the_South_-_c1900.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Bloemfontein%2C_from_the_South_-_c1900.JPG/330px-Bloemfontein%2C_from_the_South_-_c1900.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Bloemfontein%2C_from_the_South_-_c1900.JPG/440px-Bloemfontein%2C_from_the_South_-_c1900.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bloemfontein,_from_the_South_-_c1900.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Bloemfontein, circa 1900.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Though historically a predominantly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Afrikaner" title="Afrikaner">Afrikaner</a> settlement, Bloemfontein was officially founded in 1846 as a fort by British army major <a href="/wiki/Henry_Douglas_Warden" title="Henry Douglas Warden">Henry Douglas Warden</a> as a British outpost in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Transoranje&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Transoranje (page does not exist)">Transoranje</a> region, at that stage occupied by various groups of peoples including <a href="/wiki/Cape_Colony" title="Cape Colony">Cape Colony</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trek_Boers" title="Trek Boers">Trek Boers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Griqua_people" title="Griqua people">Griqua</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basotho" title="Basotho">Basotho</a>. Although modern day Bloemfontein has a reputation for its flowers in an otherwise arid region, the origin of the city's name is unclear. Popular legends include an ox named "Bloem" owned by one of the pioneer farmers that was taken by a lion near a fountain on his property, while another story names Jan Bloem (1775–1858), a Korana <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KhoiKhoi" title="KhoiKhoi">KhoiKhoi</a> leader who inhabited the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> <i>Bloemfontein</i> literally means <i>fountain of flowers</i> or <i>flower spring</i> in <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>. With colonial policy shifts, the region changed into the <a href="/wiki/Orange_River_Sovereignty" title="Orange River Sovereignty">Orange River Sovereignty</a> (1848–54) and eventually the <a href="/wiki/Orange_Free_State" title="Orange Free State">Orange Free State</a> Republic (1854–1902). From 1902–10 it served as the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Orange_River_Colony" title="Orange River Colony">Orange River Colony</a> and since that time as the provincial capital of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Free_State_(South_African_province)" title="Free State (South African province)">Free State</a>. In 1910 it became the Judicial capital of the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_South_Africa" title="Union of South Africa">Union of South Africa</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Founding_and_early_days">Founding and early days</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Founding and early days">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Warden originally chose the site largely because of its proximity to the main route to Winburg, the spacious open country, and the absence of <a href="/wiki/African_horse_sickness" title="African horse sickness">horse sickness</a>. Bloemfontein was the original farm of Johannes Nicolaas Brits born 21 February 1790, owner and first inhabitant of Bloemfontein. Johann – as he was known – sold the farm to Maj Warden.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1854.E2.80.9398">1854–98</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1854–98">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As the capital of the Orange Free State Republic the growth and maturing of the Republic resulted in the growth of the town. Numerous public buildings that remain in use today were constructed. This was largely facilitated by the excellent governance of the Republic (which acquired the term model republic) and the compensation from the British for the loss of the diamond rich <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Griqualand" title="Griqualand">Griqualand</a> area.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. The old <a href="/wiki/Orange_Free_State" title="Orange Free State">Orange Free State</a>'s presidential residence the <a href="/wiki/Old_Presidency" title="Old Presidency">Old Presidency</a> is currently a museum and cultural space in the city.</p>
<p>A railway line was built in 1890 connecting Bloemfontein to <a href="/wiki/Cape_Town" title="Cape Town">Cape Town</a>.</p>
<p>The writer <a href="/wiki/J._R._R._Tolkien" title="J. R. R. Tolkien">J. R. R. Tolkien</a> was born in the city on 3 January 1892, though his family left South Africa following the death of his father, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arthur_Tolkien" title="Arthur Tolkien">Arthur Tolkien</a>, while Tolkien was only three. He recorded that his earliest memories were of "a hot country."</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_Anglo-Boer_War.2FSouth_African_War">Second Anglo-Boer War/South African War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Second Anglo-Boer War/South African War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1899 the city was the site of the <a href="/wiki/Bloemfontein_Conference" title="Bloemfontein Conference">Bloemfontein Conference</a>, which failed to prevent the outbreak of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Boer_War" title="Second Boer War">Second Boer War</a>. The conference was a final attempt to avert a war between <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">Britain</a> and the <a href="/wiki/South_African_Republic" title="South African Republic">South African Republic</a>. With its failure the stage was set for war, which broke out on 11 October 1899.</p>
<p>The rail line from <a href="/wiki/Cape_Town" title="Cape Town">Cape Town</a> provided a centrally located <a href="/wiki/Bloemfontein_railway_station" title="Bloemfontein railway station">railway station</a>, and proved critical to the British in occupying the city later.</p>
<p>On 13 March 1900, following the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Paardeberg" title="Battle of Paardeberg">Battle of Paardeberg</a>, British forces captured the city and built a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Concentration_camp_(South_Africa)" title="Concentration camp (South Africa)">concentration camp</a> nearby to house <a href="/wiki/Boer" title="Boer">Boer</a> women and children. The <a href="/wiki/National_Women%27s_Monument" title="National Women's Monument">National Women's Monument</a>, on the outskirts of the city, pays homage to the 26,370 women and children as well as 1,421 old men<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> (also 14,154 black people, though some sources feel that the records are unsatisfactory, and that this number could be as high as 20,000<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>) who died in these camps in various parts of the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The hill in town was named <b>Naval Hill</b> after the naval guns brought in by the British in order to fortify the position against attack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_history">Recent history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Recent history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Until 1994 the city was the sole judicial capital of South Africa. It remains the seat for the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Appeal_of_South_Africa" title="Supreme Court of Appeal of South Africa">Supreme Court Of Appeal</a> (formerly the Appellate Division of the Supreme Court) and is therefore generally regarded<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (August 2008)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> as the judicial capital. It is also an administrative center with many private hospitals and educational institutions.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bloemfontein forms part of the <a href="/wiki/Mangaung_Metropolitan_Municipality" title="Mangaung Metropolitan Municipality">Mangaung Metropolitan Municipality</a>, which was upgraded from a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Local_Municipality_(South_Africa)" title="Local Municipality (South Africa)">Local Municipality</a> in 2011.</p>

<p>Bloemfontein is located in central South Africa on the southern edge of the <a href="/wiki/Highveld" title="Highveld">Highveld</a> at an elevation of 1,400 metres (4,600 ft), bordering on the semi-arid region of the <a href="/wiki/Karoo" title="Karoo">Karoo</a>. The area is generally flat with occasional hills (<i>koppies</i> in <a href="/wiki/Afrikaans" title="Afrikaans">Afrikaans</a>) and the general vegetation is <a href="/wiki/Highveld" title="Highveld">Highveld</a> grassland. Bloemfontein experiences a <a href="/wiki/Semi-arid_climate" title="Semi-arid climate">semi-arid climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen</a>: <i>BSk </i>), with hot summer days [Jan: max:32 °C (90 °F) min:19 °C (66 °F); frequent afternoon <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">thunderstorms</a>] and cooler, dry winters, often with frosts [July: max:14 °C (57 °F) min:−3 °C (27 °F)]. Snow is uncommon but as recently as August 2006 it snowed in the city, with snowfalls occurring again at the airport on 26 July 2007.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stadia">Stadia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Stadia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Free_State_Stadium" title="Free State Stadium">Free State Stadium</a> and the surrounding sports complex is the main sports venue in the city and province. The venue was the hosting stadium of the 2010 FIFA World Cup South Africa matches played in Bloemfontein. There are several other sports venues in the city, however, including facilities belonging to the university, schools and sports clubs.other stadium's in the city is mangaung oval, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dr_molemela_stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dr molemela stadium (page does not exist)">Dr molemela stadium</a> and Clive Solomon's stadium</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soccer">Soccer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Soccer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bloemfontein is joint home (together with nearby <a href="/wiki/Botshabelo" title="Botshabelo">Botshabelo</a>) to <a href="/wiki/Premier_Soccer_League" title="Premier Soccer League">Premier Soccer League</a> team <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bloemfontein_Celtic" title="Bloemfontein Celtic">Bloemfontein Celtic</a>. Some of the matches of the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> were played at the Free State Stadium, including the historic 4–1 defeat of England by Germany in the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup#Round_of_16" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">round of 16</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rugby">Rugby</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Rugby">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bloemfontein's <a href="/wiki/Free_State_Stadium" title="Free State Stadium">Free State Stadium</a> is home to two <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a> teams; the <a href="/wiki/Cheetahs_(rugby_union)" title="Cheetahs (rugby union)">Cheetahs</a> who compete in <a href="/wiki/Super_Rugby" title="Super Rugby">Super Rugby</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Free_State_Cheetahs" title="Free State Cheetahs">Free State Cheetahs</a> who play in the domestic <a href="/wiki/Currie_Cup" title="Currie Cup">Currie Cup</a>. The Free State Cheetahs won the Currie Cup in 2005 against the <a href="/wiki/Blue_Bulls" title="Blue Bulls">Blue Bulls</a>, they drew the final with the Blue Bulls in 2006 and retained the Currie Cup title in 2007 by beating the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Lions" title="Golden Lions">Golden Lions</a> resulting in the Cheetahs remaining Currie Cup champions until 2008 when the failed to make the final for the first time since 2004. 2009 saw the Cheetahs return to the Currie Cup final but they were unable to beat the Blue Bulls at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loftus_Versfeld" title="Loftus Versfeld">Loftus Versfeld</a>. In 2016 the Cheetahs won the Currie Cup after a perfect season, beating the Blue Bulls at home in Bloemfontein.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cricket">Cricket</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Cricket">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Knights_(cricket_team)" title="Knights (cricket team)">Knights cricket team</a> representing the Free State and Northern Cape in various series is located at <a href="/wiki/Mangaung_Oval" title="Mangaung Oval">Mangaung Oval</a>, part of the Free State Stadium complex. Bloemfontein features as a regular venue for touring international and local cricket teams.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Motor_sports">Motor sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Motor sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bloemfontein has a <a href="/wiki/Motocross" title="Motocross">motocross</a> track (tempe) run by the Bloemfontein Off Road Club, as well as a <a href="/wiki/Go-kart" title="Go-kart">go-kart</a> circuit (M&amp;F Raceway), which was closed down in early 2015.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shooting_sports">Shooting sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Shooting sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bloemfontein has a comprehensive shooting centre 20 km (12 mi) south of the city, offering most forms of shooting including various clay target, pistol and rifle disciplines. Bloemfontein's shooting sport community has produced many provincial and national representatives over the years.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tertiary_education">Tertiary education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Tertiary education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_tertiary_institutions">Public tertiary institutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Public tertiary institutions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The largest tertiary institutions in Bloemfontein are the <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Free_State" title="University of the Free State">University of the Free State</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Central_University_of_Technology" title="Central University of Technology">Central University of Technology</a>.</p>
<p>There is a Further Education and Training College called Motheo FET College which comprises three main campuses(Thaba N'chu, Hillside view and Bloemfontein) and the satellite campuses in Zastron, Philippolis and Botshabelo.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Private_tertiary_institutions">Private tertiary institutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Private tertiary institutions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some major national private educational brands maintain a presence in Bloemfontein, namely:</p>
<ul>
<li>Boston City Campus focuses on video based, self study. There are a variety of academic divisions.</li>
<li><a class="external text" href="http://www.cti.co.za" rel="nofollow">CTI/MGI</a> forming part of the international group <a class="external text" href="http://www.pearson.com" rel="nofollow">Pearson</a>, offering degrees in IT, Commerce, Creative Arts and Communication, Law and Social Sciences, Psychology and Counselling</li>
<li>CTU Training Solutions focuses on IT and multi-media.</li>
<li>Damelin focuses on budget education offered for full-time, part-time and via correspondence. There are a variety of academic divisions.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Qualitas_Career_Academy" title="Qualitas Career Academy">Qualitas Career Academy</a>, caters for full-time and part-time studies for students as well as corporate training and consulting services for businesses and government departments. There are a variety of academic divisions.</li>
<li><a class="external text" href="http://www.attibloemfontein.co.za" rel="nofollow">ATTI</a> (Advanced Technology Training Institute) offers short <a class="external text" href="http://www.attibloemfontein.co.za/index.php?page=short_courses" rel="nofollow">short courses</a> and <a class="external text" href="http://www.attibloemfontein.co.za/index.php?page=skills_courses" rel="nofollow">skills programs</a> with a strong focus on IT subjects and also provides training for corporate clients in various IT programs.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Road">Road</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Road">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bloemfontein's national and regional roads are as follows: The <a href="/wiki/N1_road_(South_Africa)" title="N1 road (South Africa)">N1</a>, a major highway running roughly SE to NW from <a href="/wiki/Cape_Town" title="Cape Town">Cape Town</a> to <a href="/wiki/Johannesburg" title="Johannesburg">Johannesburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a> largely bypasses this city to the west. The <a href="/wiki/N8_road_(South_Africa)" title="N8 road (South Africa)">N8</a> runs east/west connecting Bloemfontein to <a href="/wiki/Kimberley,_Northern_Cape" title="Kimberley, Northern Cape">Kimberley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maseru" title="Maseru">Maseru</a>, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Lesotho" title="Lesotho">Lesotho</a>. Bloemfontein is also the northern end of the <a href="/wiki/N6_road_(South_Africa)" title="N6 road (South Africa)">N6</a> road heading roughly southwards to the port of <a href="/wiki/East_London,_Eastern_Cape" title="East London, Eastern Cape">East London</a>. At a T-junction just before the city, the N6 becomes the M30, and the intersecting road is designated the N6. This road ends in an interchange with the N1.</p>
<p>There are also two two-digit R routes: the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R64_road_(South_Africa)" title="R64 road (South Africa)">R64</a>, which is the old road to Kimberley, via <a href="/wiki/Dealesville" title="Dealesville">Dealesville</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boshof" title="Boshof">Boshof</a>. It ends at the N1. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R30_road_(South_Africa)" title="R30 road (South Africa)">R30</a> ends at the N1 north of the town. It is the road to <a href="/wiki/Welkom" title="Welkom">Welkom</a>.</p>
<p>Three other three-digit R routes have their origin in Bloemfontein. The <a href="/wiki/R706_road_(South_Africa)" title="R706 road (South Africa)">R706</a> takes origin from the N8 in the city centre, and heads south-east towards <a href="/wiki/Jagersfontein" title="Jagersfontein">Jagersfontein</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fauresmith" title="Fauresmith">Fauresmith</a>. The <a href="/wiki/R702_road_(South_Africa)" title="R702 road (South Africa)">R702</a> also originates from the N8 in the city centre, but heads south-west towards the towns of <a href="/wiki/Dewetsdorp" title="Dewetsdorp">Dewetsdorp</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wepener" title="Wepener">Wepener</a>. The third road, the <a href="/wiki/R700_road_(South_Africa)" title="R700 road (South Africa)">R700</a>, starts south of the city centre from the M30 and heads north crossing the N8 and N1 towards <a href="/wiki/Bultfontein" title="Bultfontein">Bultfontein</a></p>
<p>Below that level, Bloemfontein has a number of metropolitan or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metropolitan_routes_in_Bloemfontein" title="Metropolitan routes in Bloemfontein">M roads</a>. These roads are numbered independently of m roads in other South African cities.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">Rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bloemfontein is located on the important rail junction between <a href="/wiki/Johannesburg" title="Johannesburg">Johannesburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cape_Town" title="Cape Town">Cape Town</a>, with daily trains to <a href="/wiki/Port_Elizabeth" title="Port Elizabeth">Port Elizabeth</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_London" title="East London">East London</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johannesburg" title="Johannesburg">Johannesburg</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Air">Air</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: Air">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bloemfontein is served by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bram_Fischer_International_Airport" title="Bram Fischer International Airport">Bram Fischer International Airport</a>, with flights to all major cities in <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=40" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bloemfontein has a large and diverse Christian population. The city houses several churches and denominations:</p>
<ul>
<li>It is the seat of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglican_Diocese_of_the_Free_State" title="Anglican Diocese of the Free State">Anglican Diocese of the Free State</a></li>
<li>Afrikaans <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baptist_Church" title="Baptist Church">Baptist Church</a> (Afrikaans: <i>Afrikaanse Baptiste Kerk</i>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dutch_Reformed_Church" title="Dutch Reformed Church">Dutch Reformed Church</a> (Afrikaans: <i>Nederduitse Gereformeerde Kerk</i>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sacred_Heart" title="Sacred Heart">Sacred Heart</a> Cathedral in Bloemfontein is the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Bloemfontein" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Bloemfontein">Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Bloemfontein</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Seventh-day_Adventist_Church" title="Seventh-day Adventist Church">Seventh-day Adventist Church</a>, Southern African headquarters.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Christian_Revival_Church" title="Christian Revival Church">Christian Revival Church</a>, is the largest church in Bloemfontein with over 30,000 members. CRC's net is widely cast and is established in Bloemfontein, Pretoria and Johannesburg along with 90 national and international CRC Churches worldwide, executing the mission of "One Church, Many Locations" throughout South Africa, Europe, America &amp; Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The church is led by Pastor <a href="/wiki/At_Boshoff" title="At Boshoff">At Boshoff</a>, together with his wife Nyretta.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/New_Covenant_Ministries_International" title="New Covenant Ministries International">New Covenant Ministries International</a> led by Chris Gerber. It is a New Testament Church known by the name Fountainhead Church International.</li>
</ul>
<p>The city also has a large Jewish population, which was established during the mid-19th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>There are two main cemeteries in Bloemfontein:</p>
<ul>
<li>The Old Cemetery: Over 1,000 names from the three cemeteries. Old: The oldest of the three cemeteries, dating from 1871, is simply a small fenced off area of a public cemetery near the city center. It includes a few graves of several Jewish pioneers involved in the early days of the city who died serving on either side in the Boer War, 1899–1902. Each of the thirty or so tombstones of these pioneer families has been completely transcribed.</li>
<li>South Park: This cemetery was consecrated in 1978 and now includes more than 10 000 graves and is the biggest cemetery in the Free State.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>On 7 October 2010, Several tombstones in the Jewish cemetery in Bloemfontein were defaced with swastikas and antisemitic graffiti.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On 10 April 2012, Bloemfontein's historic Memorium cemetery was vandalized, with 35 tombstones toppled and obscene graffiti daubed on the walls of the adjoining Ohel. The graffiti included images of money bags and diamonds, as well as of a crudely drawn <a href="/wiki/Star_of_David" title="Star of David">Magen David</a>, allegedly as part of an anti-Semitic act.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> There is also the old Phahameng cemetery which dates back to the 1960s and was specifically reserved for Africans during the apartheid era and has a Heroes Acre, where freedom fighters are laid to rest. The Heide Heights cemetery in Heidedal was reserved for coloured people during the apartheid era but all races could bury their dead after 1994. This cemetery has been closed due to it being full.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Community_service_organizations">Community service organizations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloemfontein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=41" title="Edit section: Community service organizations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20080123182124/http://www.roundtable158.co.za:80/" rel="nofollow">Round Table 158 Bloemfontein</a> The first <a href="/wiki/Round_Table_(club)" title="Round Table (club)">Round Table</a> was formed in <a href="/wiki/Norwich" title="Norwich">Norwich</a>, England in 1927. The founder, <a href="/wiki/Louis_Marchesi" title="Louis Marchesi">Louis Marchesi</a>, was a young member of Norwich Rotary Club who felt a need existed for a club where the young business men of the town could gather on a regular basis. At their meetings they could exchange ideas, learn from the experiences of their colleagues and play a collective part in the civic life of Norwich. From a very early stage it was agreed that Round Table would be a non-religious, non-political club and this has continued to this day.</li>
<li><a class="external text" href="http://www.childwelfarebfn.org.za" rel="nofollow">Child Welfare Bloemfontein</a> Child Welfare Bloemfontein &amp; Childline Free State is a non-profit organisation that was founded in 1914 by a group of volunteers who identified a need for welfare services among the community. In 2004, Child Welfare Bloemfontein celebrated its 90th birthday. Over the past 90 years, many community programmes have been successfully implemented.</li>
<li><a class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131113044451/http://www.masoniclodges.co.za/index.php" rel="nofollow">FreeMasons</a> The Masonic Centre, Bloemfontein, was built during the 1970s when all the Masonic Lodges in Bloemfontein, sold their individual properties. The Centre was erected to facilitate all Masonic activities in Bloemfontein and surrounding areas. All four Constitutions that is active in South Africa, gather at the Centre. The Lodges that has ownership of the Centre is Lodge Unie (est. 1864); the Rising Star Lodge (est. 1865); Thistle Lodge (est.1903); Emerald Lodge (est. 1905); Lodge Dagbreek (est. 1932) &amp; Lodge Oranje (est. 1964). Various side degrees are catered for at the Centre which includes the Mark, Ark, the Royal Arch &amp; Rose Croix. Bloemfontein has a rich Masonic history especially in the Anglo Boer War (1899–1902), with members such as Lord Kitchener, Rudyard Kipling and Sir Arthur Conan Doyle, visiting Lodges in Bloemfontein.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
</ul>
