<p>The <b>nasal cavity</b> (or <b>nasal fossa</b>) is a large air filled space above and behind the <a href="/wiki/Human_nose" title="Human nose">nose</a> in the middle of the face. Each cavity is the continuation of one of the two <a href="/wiki/Nostril" title="Nostril">nostrils</a>.</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Function">Function</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasal_cavity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Function">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The term "nasal cavity" can refer to each of the two sides of the nose or to the two sides combined. The two nasal cavities condition the air to be received by the other areas of the <a href="/wiki/Respiratory_tract" title="Respiratory tract">respiratory tract</a>. Owing to the large surface area provided by the <a href="/wiki/Nasal_concha" title="Nasal concha">nasal conchae</a> (also known as turbinates), the air passing through the nasal cavity is warmed or cooled to within 1 degree of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Body_temperature" title="Body temperature">body temperature</a>. In addition, the air is humidified, and dust and other particulate matter is removed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vibrissae" title="Vibrissae">vibrissae</a>, short, thick hairs, present in the <a href="/wiki/Nasal_vestibule" title="Nasal vestibule">vestibule</a>. The entire mucosa of the nasal fossae is covered by a blanket of mucus, which lies superficial to the microscopic cilia and also filters inspired air. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cilia" title="Cilia">cilia</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Respiratory_epithelium" title="Respiratory epithelium">respiratory epithelium</a> move the secreted mucus and particulate matter posteriorly towards the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_pharynx" title="Human pharynx">pharynx</a> where it passes into the <a href="/wiki/Esophagus" title="Esophagus">esophagus</a> and is digested in the stomach. The nasal cavity also houses our sense of smell and contributes greatly to taste sensation through its posterior communication with the mouth via the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Choanae" title="Choanae">choanae</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Walls">Walls</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasal_cavity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Walls">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The lateral wall of each nasal cavity mainly consists of the <a href="/wiki/Maxilla" title="Maxilla">maxilla</a>. However, there is a deficiency that is compensated by the perpendicular plate of the <a href="/wiki/Palatine_bone" title="Palatine bone">palatine bone</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Medial_pterygoid_plate" title="Medial pterygoid plate">medial pterygoid plate</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Labyrinth_of_ethmoid" title="Labyrinth of ethmoid">labyrinth of ethmoid</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inferior_concha" title="Inferior concha">inferior concha</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paranasal_sinus" title="Paranasal sinus">paranasal sinuses</a> are connected to the nasal cavity through small orifices called <a href="/wiki/Sinus_ostium" title="Sinus ostium">ostia</a>. Most of these ostia communicate with the nose through the lateral nasal wall, via a semi-lunar depression in it known as the infundibulum. The infundibulum is bound laterally by a projection known as the uncinate process.</p>
<p>The roof of each nasal cavity is formed in its upper third to one half by the <a href="/wiki/Nasal_bone" title="Nasal bone">nasal bone</a> and more inferiorly by the junctions of the upper lateral cartilage and nasal septum. Connective tissue and skin cover the bony and cartilaginous components of the dorsum of the nose.</p>
<p>The floor of the nasal cavities, which also form the roof of the mouth, is made up by the bones of the hard palate: the horizontal plate of the palatine bone posteriorly and the palatine process of the maxilla anteriorly. To the front of the nasal cavity is the nasal vestibule and external opening, while the back blends, via the <a href="/wiki/Posterior_nasal_apertures" title="Posterior nasal apertures">choanae</a>, into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nasopharynx" title="Nasopharynx">nasopharynx</a>.</p>
<p>The nasal cavity is divided in two by a vertical fin called the <a href="/wiki/Nasal_septum" title="Nasal septum">nasal septum</a>. On the sides of the nasal cavity are three horizontal outgrowths called <a href="/wiki/Nasal_concha" title="Nasal concha">nasal conchae</a> (singular "concha") or turbinates. These turbinates disrupt the airflow, directing air toward the <a href="/wiki/Olfactory_epithelium" title="Olfactory epithelium">olfactory epithelium</a> on the surface of the turbinates and the septum. The <a href="/wiki/Vomeronasal_organ" title="Vomeronasal organ">vomeronasal organ</a> is located at the back of the <a href="/wiki/Septum" title="Septum">septum</a> and has a role in <a href="/wiki/Pheromone" title="Pheromone">pheromone</a> detection.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cilia_and_mucus">Cilia and mucus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasal_cavity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cilia and mucus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cilia" title="Cilia">Cilia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mucus" title="Mucus">mucus</a> along the inside wall of the nasal cavity traps and removes dust and pathogens from the air as it flows through the nasal cavity. The cilia move the mucus down the nasal cavity to the <a href="/wiki/Pharynx" title="Pharynx">pharynx</a>, where it can be swallowed.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Segments">Segments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasal_cavity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Segments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The nasal cavity is divided into two segments: the respiratory segment and the olfactory segment.</p>
<ul>
<li>The respiratory segment comprises most of each nasal fossa and is lined with ciliated pseudostratified columnar epithelium (also called respiratory epithelium). The conchae, or turbinates, are located in this region. The turbinates have a very vascularized lamina propria (erectile tissue) allowing the venous plexuses of their mucosa to engorge with blood, restricting airflow and causing air to be directed to the other side of the nose, which acts in concert by shunting blood out of its turbinates. This cycle occurs approximately every two and a half hours.</li>
<li>The olfactory segment is lined with a specialized type of pseudostratified columnar epithelium, known as <a href="/wiki/Olfactory_epithelium" title="Olfactory epithelium">olfactory epithelium</a>, which contains receptors for the sense of the smell. This segment is located in and beneath the mucosa of the roof of each nasal cavity and the medial side of each middle turbinate. Histological sections appear yellowish-brown due to the presence of lipofuscin pigments. Olfactory mucosal cell types include bipolar neurons, supporting (sustentacular) cells, basal cells, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bowman%27s_glands" title="Bowman's glands">Bowman's glands</a>. The axons of the bipolar neurons form the <a href="/wiki/Olfactory_nerve" title="Olfactory nerve">olfactory nerve</a> (cranial nerve I) which enters the brain through the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cribiform_plate" title="Cribiform plate">cribiform plate</a>. Bowman's glands are serous glands in the lamina propria, whose secretions trap and dissolve odoriferous substances.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Blood_supply">Blood supply</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasal_cavity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Blood supply">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There is a rich blood supply to the nasal cavity. In some animals, such as <a href="/wiki/Dog" title="Dog">dogs</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Capillary" title="Capillary">capillary</a> beds flowing through the nasal cavity help cool the blood flow to the <a href="/wiki/Brain" title="Brain">brain</a>.</p>
<p>Blood supply comes from branches of both the <a href="/wiki/Internal_carotid_artery" title="Internal carotid artery">internal</a> and <a href="/wiki/External_carotid_artery" title="External carotid artery">external carotid artery</a>, including branches of the <a href="/wiki/Facial_artery" title="Facial artery">facial artery</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maxillary_artery" title="Maxillary artery">maxillary artery</a>. The named arteries of the nose are:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Sphenopalatine and Greater palatine arteries, branches of the maxillary artery.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Anterior_ethmoidal_artery" title="Anterior ethmoidal artery">Anterior ethmoidal artery</a> and <a href="/wiki/Posterior_ethmoidal_artery" title="Posterior ethmoidal artery">posterior ethmoidal artery</a>, branches of the <a href="/wiki/Ophthalmic_artery" title="Ophthalmic artery">ophthalmic artery</a></li>
<li>Septal Branches of the <a href="/wiki/Superior_labial_artery" title="Superior labial artery">superior labial artery</a>, a branch of the <a href="/wiki/Facial_artery" title="Facial artery">facial artery</a>, which supplies the <i>vestibule</i> of the nasal cavity.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Innervation">Innervation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasal_cavity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Innervation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Innervation" title="Innervation">Innervation</a> of the nasal cavity responsible for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sense_of_smell" title="Sense of smell">sense of smell</a> is via the <a href="/wiki/Olfactory_nerve" title="Olfactory nerve">olfactory nerve</a>, which sends microscopic fibers from the <a href="/wiki/Olfactory_bulb" title="Olfactory bulb">olfactory bulb</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Cribriform_plate" title="Cribriform plate">cribriform plate</a> to reach the top of the nasal cavity.</p>
<p>General sensory innervation is by branches of the <a href="/wiki/Trigeminal_nerve" title="Trigeminal nerve">trigeminal nerve</a> (V<sub>1</sub> &amp; V<sub>2</sub>):</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nasociliary_nerve" title="Nasociliary nerve">Nasociliary nerve</a> (V<sub>1</sub>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nasopalatine_nerve" title="Nasopalatine nerve">Nasopalatine nerve</a> (V<sub>2</sub>)</li>
<li>Posterior nasal branches of <a href="/wiki/Maxillary_nerve" title="Maxillary nerve">Maxillary nerve</a> (V<sub>2</sub>)</li>
</ul>
<p>There are two passages in the nasal cavity, not to be confused with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nostrils" title="Nostrils">nostrils</a>.The entire nasal cavity is innervated by autonomic fibers. <a href="/wiki/Sympathetic_nervous_system" title="Sympathetic nervous system">Sympathetic</a> innervation to the blood vessels of the mucosa causes them to <a href="/wiki/Vasoconstriction" title="Vasoconstriction">constrict</a>, while the control of secretion by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mucous_glands" title="Mucous glands">mucous glands</a> is carried on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Postganglionic" title="Postganglionic">postganglionic</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parasympathetic" title="Parasympathetic">parasympathetic</a> nerve fibers originating from the <a href="/wiki/Facial_nerve" title="Facial nerve">facial nerve</a>.</p>

<p>Diseases of the nasal cavity include <a href="/wiki/Virus" title="Virus">viral</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bacteria" title="Bacteria">bacterial</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fungus" title="Fungus">fungal</a> infections, nasal cavity <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tumors" title="Tumors">tumors</a>, both benign and much more often malignant, as well as inflammations of the <a href="/wiki/Mucous_membrane_of_nose" title="Mucous membrane of nose">nasal mucosa</a>. Many problems can affect the nose, including:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deviated_septum" title="Deviated septum">Deviated septum</a> - a shifting of the wall that divides the nasal cavity into halves</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nasal_polyps" title="Nasal polyps">Nasal polyps</a> - soft growths that develop on the lining of your nose or sinuses</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nosebleeds" title="Nosebleeds">Nosebleeds</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rhinitis" title="Rhinitis">Rhinitis</a> - inflammation of the nose and sinuses sometimes caused by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allergies" title="Allergies">allergies</a>. The main symptom is a runny nose.</li>
<li>Nasal fractures, also known as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broken_nose" title="Broken nose">broken nose</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Common_cold" title="Common cold">Common cold</a></li>
</ul>
