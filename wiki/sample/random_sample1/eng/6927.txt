<p><b>Viðoy</b> (<a href="/wiki/Danish_language" title="Danish language">Danish</a>: <span lang="da" xml:lang="da"><i>Viderø</i></span>) is the northern-most island in the <a href="/wiki/Faroe_Islands" title="Faroe Islands">Faroe Islands</a>, located east of <a href="/wiki/Bor%C3%B0oy" title="Borðoy">Borðoy</a> to which it is linked via a causeway. The name means <i>wood island,</i> despite the fact that no trees grow on the island: the name relates to the <a href="/wiki/Driftwood" title="Driftwood">driftwood</a> that floats in from <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vi%C3%B0oy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The island has two settlements: <a href="/wiki/Hvannasund" title="Hvannasund">Hvannasund</a> on the south-west coast and <a href="/wiki/Vi%C3%B0arei%C3%B0i" title="Viðareiði">Viðareiði</a> on the north-west coast, the northernmost settlement in the Faroes. A road along the west coast of the island connects the two. The island is connected by a road causeway to <a href="/wiki/Nor%C3%B0depil" title="Norðdepil">Norðdepil</a> on Borðoy and a bus service from <a href="/wiki/Klaksv%C3%ADk" title="Klaksvík">Klaksvík</a> runs across the causeway to the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-loneplan_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-loneplan-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Important_bird_area">Important bird area</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vi%C3%B0oy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Important bird area">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The island’s northern and eastern coast has been identified as an <a href="/wiki/Important_Bird_Area" title="Important Bird Area">Important Bird Area</a> by <a href="/wiki/BirdLife_International" title="BirdLife International">BirdLife International</a> because of its significance as a breeding site for <a href="/wiki/Seabird" title="Seabird">seabirds</a>, especially <a href="/wiki/European_storm_petrel" title="European storm petrel">European storm petrels</a> (500 pairs), <a href="/wiki/Black-legged_kittiwake" title="Black-legged kittiwake">black-legged kittiwakes</a> (5300 pairs), <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_puffin" title="Atlantic puffin">Atlantic puffins</a> (25,000 pairs), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Common_guillemot" title="Common guillemot">common guillemots</a> (6700 individuals) and <a href="/wiki/Black_guillemot" title="Black guillemot">black guillemots</a> (200 pairs).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>Viðoy has eleven mountains of which <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Villingadalsfjall&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Villingadalsfjall (page does not exist)">Villingadalsfjall</a> is the northern-most peak in the Faroes. The north coast also has the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enniberg" title="Enniberg">Enniberg</a> cliff, which at 750 m is the second highest sea-cliff in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> (after <a href="/wiki/Hornelen" title="Hornelen">Hornelen</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>). The mountains are shown with their overall rank in the Faroe Islands.</p>
