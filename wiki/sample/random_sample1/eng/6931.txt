<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Video blog</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>A <b>video blog</b> or <b>video log</b>, usually shortened to <b>vlog</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'v' in 'vie'">v</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ɒ/ short 'o' in 'body'">ɒ</span><span title="'g' in 'guy'">ɡ</span></span>/</a></span></span>, is a form of <a href="/wiki/Blog" title="Blog">blog</a> for which the medium is <a href="/wiki/Video" title="Video">video</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and is a form of <a href="/wiki/Web_television" title="Web television">web television</a>. Vlog entries often combine embedded video (or a video link) with supporting text, images, and other <a href="/wiki/Metadata" title="Metadata">metadata</a>. Entries can be recorded in one take or cut into multiple parts. The vlog category is popular on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>.</p>
<p>Video logs (vlogs) also often take advantage of <a href="/wiki/Web_syndication" title="Web syndication">web syndication</a> to allow for the distribution of video over the Internet using either the <a href="/wiki/RSS" title="RSS">RSS</a> or <a href="/wiki/Atom_(standard)" title="Atom (standard)">Atom</a> syndication formats, for automatic aggregation and playback on mobile devices and personal computers (See <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Video_podcast" title="Video podcast">video podcast</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/History_of_blogging" title="History of blogging">History of blogging</a></div>
<p>On January 2, 2000, Adam Kontras posted a video alongside a blog entry aimed at informing his friends and family of his cross-country move to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> in pursuit of <a href="/wiki/Show_business" title="Show business">show business</a>, marking the first post on what would later become the longest-running video blog in history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kontraspost_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-kontraspost-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nakedlens_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-nakedlens-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kapusomo_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-kapusomo-5">[5]</a></sup> In November of that year, Adrian Miles posted a video of changing text on a still image, coining the term <i>vog</i> to refer to his video blog.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-milesvideo1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-milesvideo1-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-milesvideo2_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-milesvideo2-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2004, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Garfield" title="Steve Garfield">Steve Garfield</a> launched his own video blog and declared that year "the year of the video blog".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-garfieldyearoftheblog_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-garfieldyearoftheblog-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-garfieldyearoftheblog2_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-garfieldyearoftheblog2-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<p>Vlogging saw a strong increase in popularity beginning in 2005. The <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo!</a> Videoblogging Group saw its membership increase dramatically in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-businessweek_pioneers_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-businessweek_pioneers-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wired_vlogging_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-wired_vlogging-11">[11]</a></sup> The most popular video sharing site to date, <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, was founded in February 2005. By July 2006, it had become the 5th most popular web destination, with 100 million videos viewed daily and 65,000 new uploads per day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday100million_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday100million-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Many <a href="/wiki/Open-source_software" title="Open-source software">open source</a> <a href="/wiki/Content_management_system" title="Content management system">content management systems</a> have enabled the inclusion of video content, allowing bloggers to host and administer their own video blogging sites. In addition, the convergence of mobile phones with digital cameras allows publishing of video content to the Web almost as it is recorded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-journalism_mobile_blogging_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-journalism_mobile_blogging-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Radio_station" title="Radio station">Radio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Television_station" title="Television station">television stations</a> may use video blogging as a way to help interact more with listeners and viewers.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guinness_World_Record">Guinness World Record</span></h3>
<p>Charles Trippy, under the <i><a href="/wiki/Internet_Killed_Television" title="Internet Killed Television">Internet Killed Television</a></i> YouTube channel, currently holds the <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">Guinness World Record</a> for the “Most Consecutive Daily Personal Video Blogs Posted On YouTube,” with 2,200 consecutive videos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="VidCon">VidCon</span></h3>
<p>Hosted in Los Angeles, California, <a href="/wiki/VidCon" title="VidCon">VidCon</a> is an annual convention that allows YouTube content creators and viewers to come together in order to share content ideas and business contacts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vidcon_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-vidcon-15">[15]</a></sup> The first VidCon event was held on July 10 and 11, 2010, and has now become the largest in-person gathering of internet creators, viewers, and representatives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> This convention realizes that the ways in which society entertains, educates, shares, and communicates are being revolutionized, and chooses to highlight this fact via panels, meet and greets, and talks given to audiences at the convention.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vidcon_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-vidcon-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="YouTube_presence">YouTube presence</span></h2>
<p>YouTube currently ranks among the top three most-visited sites on the web.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> As a high traffic area for video bloggers, or <i>vloggers</i>, YouTube has created a platform for these participants to present their personal videos, which oftentimes are filmed using hand held point and shoot cameras.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-onlinelibrary.wiley.com_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-onlinelibrary.wiley.com-18">[18]</a></sup> The popularity of vlogs in the YouTube community has risen exponentially in the past few years<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>; out of the top 100 most subscribed YouTube channels, 17 provide vlogs as their primary style of footage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-onlinelibrary.wiley.com_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-onlinelibrary.wiley.com-18">[18]</a></sup> Many of these vloggers are a part of the YouTube Partner Program, which professionalizes the industry and allows for monetary gain from video production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> This professionalization additionally helps increase exposure to various channels as well as creates a sense of stability within the field. Additionally, this professionalization allows content creators to be deemed a credible source by their viewers. Furthermore, many vloggers have been able to turn their channels into sustainable careers; in 2013, the highest paid vlogger brought in a minimum of $720,000 for the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Hollywood is taking notice of this rising medium, and has placed its value ranked over other entertainment companies such as Marvel, which was recently bought out by Disney as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Vlogumentary">Vlogumentary</span></h2>
<p><i>I’m Vlogging Here</i> is a 90-minute “vlogumentary” that focuses on documenting the world of video blogging and centers on YouTube vloggers that have found success in using this medium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Starring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/YouTube_personality" title="YouTube personality">YouTube personality</a> <a href="/wiki/Shay_Carl" title="Shay Carl">Shay Carl</a> and his family of ShayTards, this film, to be released in late 2016, follows a family whose lives have been drastically altered by vlogging, as their day-to-day lives are documented and uploaded for the world to see. Shay Carl is a co-founder of Maker Studios, a YouTube based video supplier bought out by The Walt Disney Company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The involvement of larger corporations outside of the Internet industries is a primary example of the ever increasing need for a strong front on the digital side of one’s company. This documentary is being created by a group with links to the YouTube community in hopes that it will spark interest and raise awareness of the impact that vlogging and the digital community are having on the entertainment industry.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other">Other</span></h2>
<p>A video log created while riding a <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle" title="Motorcycle">motorcycle</a> is known as a <b><a href="/wiki/Motovlog" title="Motovlog">motovlog</a></b> (abbreviation of <b>motorcycle video log</b>).</p>
