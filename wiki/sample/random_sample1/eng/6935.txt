<p><b>Birol Ünel</b> (born 18 August 1961) is a <a href="/wiki/Turks_in_Germany" title="Turks in Germany">German actor of Turkish descent</a>. He has acted in a number of <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Germany" title="Cinema of Germany">German</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Turkey" title="Cinema of Turkey">Turkish</a> films, TV series and theatrical productions. His name, "Birol", means "unique" in Turkish.</p>
<p>Ünel was born in <a href="/wiki/Mersin" title="Mersin">Mersin</a> in southern Turkey. In 1968, his family moved to <a href="/wiki/Brinkum" title="Brinkum">Brinkum</a> near <a href="/wiki/Bremen" title="Bremen">Bremen</a> in Germany. He studied acting at Hanover Conservatory.</p>
<p>Ünel began as a theatre actor at the Berliner Tacheles where he played the lead and also directed the play "Caligula". Ünel made his film debut in 1988's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Passenger_-_Welcome_to_Germany" title="The Passenger - Welcome to Germany">The Passenger</a></i>. He played a private detective in the film <i>Dealer</i> which he followed up with a role in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fatih_Ak%C4%B1n" title="Fatih Akın">Fatih Akın</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Im_Juli" title="Im Juli">Im Juli</a></i>. Birol then acted in the award winning <i><a href="/wiki/Head-On_(film)" title="Head-On (film)">Gegen die Wand</a></i> as Cahit Tomruk. He won the German film prize for best actor for this role.</p>
<p>His latest films have been <i><a href="/wiki/Transylvania_(film)" title="Transylvania (film)">Transylvania</a></i>, <i>Seven Heroes</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Soul_Kitchen_(film)" title="Soul Kitchen (film)">Soul Kitchen</a></i>. Ünel also acted in the hit Turkish film <i>Hırsız var!</i> in 2005.</p>
