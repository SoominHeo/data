<p><b>Birgitte Hjort Sørensen</b> (born 16 January 1982) is a Danish actress who made her debut on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Eagle_(TV_series)" title="The Eagle (TV series)">The Eagle</a></i> in 2005. She followed this by playing <a href="/wiki/Roxie_Hart" title="Roxie Hart">Roxie Hart</a> in the musical <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_(musical)" title="Chicago (musical)">Chicago</a></i> in Copenhagen and on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_End_(theatre)" title="West End (theatre)">West End</a>. Sørensen's breakthrough role was as journalist Katrine Fønsmark in the television political drama <i><a href="/wiki/Borgen_(TV_series)" title="Borgen (TV series)">Borgen</a></i> (2010–13).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Birgitte_Hjort_S%C3%B8rensen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sørensen was born in Copenhagen, Denmark, on 16 January 1982.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-supremacy_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-supremacy-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Both her parents are doctors and she has two elder sisters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-doctor_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-doctor-3">[3]</a></sup> While on a school trip to London, she watched the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_End_(theatre)" title="West End (theatre)">West End</a> production of the musical <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_(musical)" title="Chicago (musical)">Chicago</a></i> which motivated her to become an actress. Sørensen spoke of the experience in an interview for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i>, "It was so amazing and sexy, and I sat there thinking, 'I want to be on that stage one day.'".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-power_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-power-4">[4]</a></sup> She continued her studies at the <a href="/wiki/Danish_National_School_of_Theatre_and_Contemporary_Dance" title="Danish National School of Theatre and Contemporary Dance">Danish National School of Theatre</a>, during which time Sørensen made her acting debut in 2005 as a receptionist on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Eagle_(TV_series)" title="The Eagle (TV series)">The Eagle</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ekstra_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-ekstra-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afton_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-afton-6">[6]</a></sup> Two years later she played the lead role of <a href="/wiki/Roxie_Hart" title="Roxie Hart">Roxie Hart</a> in the 2007 production of <i>Chicago</i> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Det_Ny_Theater" title="Det Ny Theater">Det Ny Theater</a> in Copenhagen. Sørensen then reprised the role in the West End production in 2008 at the <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_Theatre" title="Cambridge Theatre">Cambridge Theatre</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-supremacy_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-supremacy-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Birgitte_Hjort_S%C3%B8rensen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2010, she made her breakthrough by playing the idealistic journalist, Katrine Fønsmark, in the <a href="/wiki/DR_(broadcaster)" title="DR (broadcaster)">DR</a> political drama <i><a href="/wiki/Borgen_(TV_series)" title="Borgen (TV series)">Borgen</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Sørensen received critical acclaim for playing a strong female protagonist on the show, and international recognition especially in the United Kingdom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> She also appeared in the action film <i><a href="/wiki/Ved_verdens_ende" title="Ved verdens ende">Ved verdens ende</a></i> (2010) opposite <a href="/wiki/Nikolaj_Lie_Kaas" title="Nikolaj Lie Kaas">Nikolaj Lie Kaas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nikolaj_Coster-Waldau" title="Nikolaj Coster-Waldau">Nikolaj Coster-Waldau</a> which garnered her first nomination for the <a href="/wiki/Robert_Award_for_Best_Actress_in_a_Leading_Role" title="Robert Award for Best Actress in a Leading Role">Robert Award for Best Actress in a Leading Role</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In 2012, Sørensen portrayed painter <a href="/wiki/Marie_Kr%C3%B8yer" title="Marie Krøyer">Marie Krøyer</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Passion_of_Marie" title="The Passion of Marie">The Passion of Marie</a></i> which earned her second Best Actress nomination at the <a href="/wiki/Robert_Awards" title="Robert Awards">Robert Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marie_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-marie-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, Sørensen appeared in the "<a href="/wiki/Hardhome" title="Hardhome">Hardhome</a>" episode of the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> fantasy drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones">Game of Thrones</a></i> playing Karsi, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wildling_(character)" title="Wildling (character)">wildling</a> chieftain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-karsi_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-karsi-18">[18]</a></sup> In the same year, she starred opposite <a href="/wiki/Anna_Kendrick" title="Anna Kendrick">Anna Kendrick</a> in the musical comedy sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Pitch_Perfect_2" title="Pitch Perfect 2">Pitch Perfect 2</a></i> as Kommissar, the leader of a rival German <a href="/wiki/A_cappella" title="A cappella">a cappella</a> group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pitch_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-pitch-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, Sørensen played <a href="/wiki/Andy_Warhol" title="Andy Warhol">Andy Warhol</a>'s muse <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ingrid_Superstar" title="Ingrid Superstar">Ingrid</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Mick_Jagger" title="Mick Jagger">Mick Jagger</a>-produced <a href="/wiki/Martin_Scorsese" title="Martin Scorsese">Martin Scorsese</a> <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> series, <i><a href="/wiki/Vinyl_(TV_series)" title="Vinyl (TV series)">Vinyl</a>.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Politiken-Vinyl-2015_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Politiken-Vinyl-2015-20">[20]</a></sup> She is also set to star in thriller <i>3 Things</i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Nikolaj_Coster-Waldau" title="Nikolaj Coster-Waldau">Nikolaj Coster-Waldau</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety.com_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-variety.com-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
