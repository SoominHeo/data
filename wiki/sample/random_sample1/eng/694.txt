<p>The <b>800 nanometer</b> (<b>800 nm</b>) process refers to the level of semiconductor process technology that was reached in the 1989–1990 timeframe, by most leading semiconductor companies, like <a href="/wiki/Intel" title="Intel">Intel</a> and <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a>.</p>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Intel_80486" title="Intel 80486">Intel 80486</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CPU" title="CPU">CPU</a> launched in 1989 was manufactured using this process.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/MicroSPARC" title="MicroSPARC">microSPARC I</a> launched in 1992</li>
<li>First Intel <a href="/wiki/P5_(microarchitecture)#P5" title="P5 (microarchitecture)">P5</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pentium_(brand)" title="Pentium (brand)">Pentium</a> CPUs at 60 MHz and 66 MHz launched in 1993</li>
</ul>
