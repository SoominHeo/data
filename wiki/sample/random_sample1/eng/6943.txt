<p><b>Vivendi Games</b>, formerly known as <b>Vivendi Universal Games</b> or <b>VU Games</b>, was a wholly owned subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Vivendi" title="Vivendi">Vivendi</a> responsible for <a href="/wiki/Video_game_developer" title="Video game developer">video game developers</a> inherited after acquiring <a href="/wiki/Havas" title="Havas">Havas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios" title="Universal Studios">Universal Studios</a>.</p>
<p>Headed by Bruce Hack, it was headquartered in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a> and employed over 3,400 people at four separate development divisions. Vivendi Games owned the rights to franchises such as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warcraft_(series)" title="Warcraft (series)">Warcraft</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/StarCraft" title="StarCraft">StarCraft</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Diablo_(video_game)" title="Diablo (video game)">Diablo</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/World_of_Warcraft" title="World of Warcraft">World of Warcraft</a></i> (all games developed by <a href="/wiki/Blizzard_Entertainment" title="Blizzard Entertainment">Blizzard Entertainment</a>), as well as others like <i><a href="/wiki/Empire_Earth_(series)" title="Empire Earth (series)">Empire Earth</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Ground_Control_(video_game)" title="Ground Control (video game)">Ground Control</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Tribes_(series)" title="Tribes (series)">Tribes</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crash_Bandicoot_(series)" title="Crash Bandicoot (series)">Crash Bandicoot</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Spyro_(series)" title="Spyro (series)">Spyro the Dragon</a></i> owned by <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Entertainment" title="Sierra Entertainment">Sierra Entertainment</a>. It later merged with <a href="/wiki/Activision" title="Activision">Activision</a> and is now known as <a href="/wiki/Activision_Blizzard" title="Activision Blizzard">Activision Blizzard</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivendi_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Universal_Interactive">Universal Interactive</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivendi_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Universal Interactive">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Universal_Interactive" title="Universal Interactive">Universal Interactive</a></div>
<p>The direct predecessor of Vivendi Games was Universal Interactive, the game publishing division of <a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios" title="Universal Studios">Universal Studios</a>. Founded in 1993, it was best known for publishing the <i>Crash Bandicoot</i> and <i>Spyro the Dragon</i> series, along with games based on Universal properties.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="CUC_Software">CUC Software</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivendi_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: CUC Software">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>CUC Software</b> was founded in February 1996, when <a href="/wiki/CUC_International" title="CUC International">CUC International</a>, a large mail order and subscription company, offered to acquire entertainment software developer <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Entertainment" title="Sierra Entertainment">Sierra Entertainment</a>. Headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Bellevue,_Washington" title="Bellevue, Washington">Bellevue, Washington</a>, Sierra published adventure game series such as <i><a href="/wiki/King%27s_Quest" title="King's Quest">King's Quest</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Knight" title="Gabriel Knight">Gabriel Knight</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Space_Quest" title="Space Quest">Space Quest</a></i>. Sierra was a public company, which employed roughly 1,000 employees at 12 different acquired studios. CUC offered to absorb Sierra's stock shares at a price roughly 90% higher than what Sierra was trading, and on July 24, 1996, Sierra became a wholly owned subsidiary of CUC.</p>
<p>At the same time, CUC also approached <a href="/wiki/Davidson_%26_Associates" title="Davidson &amp; Associates">Davidson &amp; Associates</a>, a leading publisher and distributor of educational software, with an offer of a similar stock swap. Headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Torrance,_California" title="Torrance, California">Torrance, California</a>, Davidson published the <i><a href="/wiki/Blaster_Learning_System" title="Blaster Learning System">Math Blaster</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warcraft_(series)" title="Warcraft (series)">Warcraft</a></i>, and <i>Diablo</i> series. While mainly a game publisher, Davidson also had a major in-house developer in the form of <a href="/wiki/Blizzard_Entertainment" title="Blizzard Entertainment">Blizzard Entertainment</a>, which Davidson had acquired in 1994, and employed over 800 employees as of February 1996. Davidson was also a public company, founded and headed by Bob Davidson, who acted as CEO, and also by Jan Davidson, who acted as President.</p>
<p>After acquiring these companies, CUC quickly integrated these two new divisions into the main CUC organization by announcing in September 1996 the creation of CUC Software, a holding company which would consolidate the finance, distribution, manufacturing, accounting, sales, R&amp;D and overall management of CUC's software companies.</p>
<p>Soon after its creation, CUC Software acquired in January 1997 <a href="/wiki/Knowledge_Adventure" title="Knowledge Adventure">Knowledge Adventure</a>, a leading developer of educational software, which published the <i><a href="/wiki/JumpStart" title="JumpStart">JumpStart</a></i> series of child oriented programs. CUC also acquired Gryphon Software, another educational software company. Davidson &amp; Associates and Gryphon were then absorbed into Knowledge Adventure, and <a href="/wiki/Blizzard_Entertainment" title="Blizzard Entertainment">Blizzard Entertainment</a> was made a separate division of CUC Software. In 1997, CUC Software also acquired <a href="/wiki/Berkeley_Systems" title="Berkeley Systems">Berkeley Systems</a> (a California-based developer that published the <i><a href="/wiki/You_Don%27t_Know_Jack_(video_game_series)" title="You Don't Know Jack (video game series)">You Don't Know Jack</a></i> series) and integrated Berkeley Systems into Sierra On-Line.</p>
<p>CUC then merged with a hotel, real-estate, and car-rental franchiser called HFS Corporation to form <a href="/wiki/Cendant" title="Cendant">Cendant</a> in 1997. In 1998, it became apparent that CUC had engaged in accounting fraud for years before the merger; Cendant's stock lost 80% of its value over the next six months in the ensuing widely discussed accounting scandal. The company sold its consumer software operations, which included Sierra and Blizzard, to French publisher <a href="/wiki/Havas" title="Havas">Havas</a> in 1998, the same year Havas was purchased by <a href="/wiki/Vivendi" title="Vivendi">Vivendi</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vivendi_Universal_Games">Vivendi Universal Games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivendi_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Vivendi Universal Games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2000, <a href="/wiki/Vivendi" title="Vivendi">Vivendi</a> acquired <a href="/wiki/Seagram" title="Seagram">Seagram</a> (owner of <a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios" title="Universal Studios">Universal Studios</a>) to become Vivendi Universal, and Universal Interactive joined Vivendi's acquired studios under the new division <b>Vivendi Universal Games</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2004, Vivendi Universal Games sold one of its divisions, <a href="/wiki/Knowledge_Adventure" title="Knowledge Adventure">Knowledge Adventure</a> to a group of private investors.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:VivendiUniversalGamesLogo.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="168" data-file-width="399" height="63" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/82/VivendiUniversalGamesLogo.png/150px-VivendiUniversalGamesLogo.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/82/VivendiUniversalGamesLogo.png/225px-VivendiUniversalGamesLogo.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/82/VivendiUniversalGamesLogo.png/300px-VivendiUniversalGamesLogo.png 2x" width="150"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:VivendiUniversalGamesLogo.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Vivendi Universal Games Logo used from 2002 until May 1, 2006.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>When parent company Vivendi Universal dropped the "Universal" in its name in 2006 to simply become Vivendi SA, Vivendi Universal Games followed suit and became Vivendi Games. It ceased to publish under its own name and instead assigned those duties to its divisions. <i>Spyro</i> and <i>Crash Bandicoot</i> were assigned to Sierra Entertainment in this arrangement.</p>
<p>In 2006, Vivendi Games created a new mobile division Vivendi Games Mobile, which was promised to begin publishing and distributing games in 2006 through mobile carriers and portals.</p>
<p>In December 2007, it was announced that Vivendi Games would merge with games publisher <a href="/wiki/Activision" title="Activision">Activision</a>, forming <a href="/wiki/Activision_Blizzard" title="Activision Blizzard">Activision Blizzard</a>. In July 2008, the merger went active. Activision Blizzard operated the games division of Vivendi SA, and became an independent company on July 25, 2013 (including Vivendi Games).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-actblizmerger_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-actblizmerger-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Divisions">Divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivendi_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Blizzard_Entertainment">Blizzard Entertainment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivendi_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Blizzard Entertainment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Blizzard_Entertainment" title="Blizzard Entertainment">Blizzard Entertainment</a></div>
<p>Blizzard Entertainment is a development studio best known for creating the series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warcraft_(series)" title="Warcraft (series)">Warcraft</a></i> (including <i><a href="/wiki/World_of_Warcraft" title="World of Warcraft">World of Warcraft</a></i>), <i><a href="/wiki/Diablo_(series)" title="Diablo (series)">Diablo</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/StarCraft_(series)" title="StarCraft (series)">StarCraft</a></i>. <i>World of Warcraft</i>, one of the most popular MMORPG games, has currently over 3.5 million subscribers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Blizzard Entertainment later became independent, as compared to a subsidiary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inmultibillion_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-inmultibillion-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sierra_Entertainment">Sierra Entertainment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivendi_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sierra Entertainment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Entertainment" title="Sierra Entertainment">Sierra Entertainment</a></div>
<p>Sierra Entertainment creates and publishes software for consoles, handheld gaming devices and personal computers. Sierra Entertainment features a portfolio of titles, including <i><a href="/wiki/F.E.A.R." title="F.E.A.R.">F.E.A.R.</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Scarface:_The_World_Is_Yours" title="Scarface: The World Is Yours">Scarface</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Ice_Age_(franchise)" title="Ice Age (franchise)">Ice Age</a></i>.</p>
<p>Operating out of Los Angeles, California, Sierra employed over 700 people in development and had four wholly owned studios providing creative talents and development capabilities across multiple gaming genres: <a href="/wiki/High_Moon_Studios" title="High Moon Studios">High Moon Studios</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radical_Entertainment" title="Radical Entertainment">Radical Entertainment</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swordfish_Studios" title="Swordfish Studios">Swordfish Studios</a> and <a href="/wiki/Massive_Entertainment" title="Massive Entertainment">Massive Entertainment</a>. After the Activision Blizzard merger, Sierra was closed down and both <a href="/wiki/Swordfish_Studios" title="Swordfish Studios">Swordfish Studios</a> and <a href="/wiki/Massive_Entertainment" title="Massive Entertainment">Massive Entertainment</a> were sold off.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sierra_Online">Sierra Online</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivendi_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sierra Online">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sierra Online is the division that focuses on developing and publishing short and mid-session casual online games for <a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">PC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xbox_Live_Arcade" title="Xbox Live Arcade">Xbox Live Arcade</a> and a range of other platforms.</p>
<p>Sierra Online’s titles include <i>Assault Heroes</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/FreeStyle_Street_Basketball" title="FreeStyle Street Basketball">FreeStyle Street Basketball</a></i>, an online PC game from Korean developer JC Entertainment, Inc.</p>
<p>The division is also developing a variety of other Xbox Live Arcade and PC online games targeting the mass market.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vivendi_Games_Mobile">Vivendi Games Mobile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivendi_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Vivendi Games Mobile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Vivendi Games Mobile, a newly formed division, creates and publishes games for the worldwide mobile market. The division publishes games based on original intellectual property, popular entertainment licenses and classic Sierra Entertainment intellectual property games which are distributed by more than 90 operators and dozens of Web portals in more than 60 countries.</p>
<p>Vivendi Games Mobile has launched a number of award-winning titles, including <i>SWAT Force</i> which was named “Best Wireless Game of the Year” by Spike TV in December 2006.</p>
<p>Vivendi Games Mobile ceased operations in early 2009.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fox_Interactive">Fox Interactive</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivendi_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Fox Interactive">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Fox_Interactive" title="Fox Interactive">Fox Interactive</a></div>
<p>In March 2003, Fox Interactive was acquired by Vivendi Universal Games and closed down in 2006.</p>
