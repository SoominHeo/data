<p><b>Vivian Hsu</b> (<a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">徐若瑄</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Xú Ruòxuān</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Wade%E2%80%93Giles" title="Wade–Giles">Wade–Giles</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-wadegile" xml:lang="zh-Latn-wadegile">Hsu Jo-hsüan</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Pe%CC%8Dh-%C5%8De-j%C4%AB" title="Pe̍h-ōe-jī">Pe̍h-ōe-jī</a>: <i><span lang="nan" xml:lang="nan">Chhî Jio̍k-soan</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ビビアン・スー <i>Bibian Sū</i></span>), born March 19, 1975 in <a href="/wiki/Taichung" title="Taichung">Taichung</a>, is a <a href="/wiki/Taiwanese_people" title="Taiwanese people">Taiwanese</a> singer, actress, and <a href="/wiki/Model_(person)" title="Model (person)">model</a> who has gained remarkable popularity in East Asia, mainly in Taiwan and Japan.</p>
<p>Hsu rose to prominence in Japan, where she made her first appearance in 1995 and has become a highly recognized celebrity with her countless appearances in media during the late 1990s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>She was also the main vocalist for <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Biscuits&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Black Biscuits (page does not exist)">Black Biscuits</a>, a Japanese dance band which released four singles, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Stamina_(Black_Biscuits_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Stamina (Black Biscuits song) (page does not exist)">Stamina</a></i>, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Timing_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Timing (song) (page does not exist)">Timing</a></i>, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Relax_(Black_Biscuits_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Relax (Black Biscuits song) (page does not exist)">Relax</a></i>, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bye-Bye_(Black_Biscuits_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bye-Bye (Black Biscuits song) (page does not exist)">Bye-Bye</a></i>, and one album, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Life_(Black_Biscuits_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Life (Black Biscuits album) (page does not exist)">Life</a></i>. All of these four singles reached the top 5 spots of the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon singles chart</a>, and <i>Life</i> reached #6 in the album chart.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivian_Hsu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivian_Hsu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The second of three children, Hsu was born to a <a href="/wiki/Hoklo_Taiwanese" title="Hoklo Taiwanese">Taiwanese</a> father and <a href="/wiki/Atayal_people" title="Atayal people">Tayal</a> Taiwanese Aborigine mother<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> as <b>Hsu Su-chuan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">徐淑娟</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Xú Shújuān</span></i>), and used this name until she began her modeling career. Her parents divorced when she was a young child. She attended <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei</a> Jianxing Elementary School and Taipei Shulinguo Junior High School.</p>
<p>Hsu's career in entertainment industry started after winning first place in a "Talented Beautiful Girl" contest held by Taiwan's <a href="/wiki/China_Television" title="China Television">CTS</a> in 1990. At that point, she was working by delivering food on her bicycle, and her customers began to recognise her from her television appearances. The same year she joined a musical trio named "Girls' Team" (少女隊). They released two albums, in 1991 and 1992, then broke up. Following this, Hsu started modeling.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modelling">Modelling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivian_Hsu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Modelling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As a model, she posed in two photobooks titled <i>Angel</i> (天使心) and <i>Venus</i>. <i>Angel</i>, published in 1995, was shot in <a href="/wiki/Capri" title="Capri">Capri</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>. <i>Venus</i>, published on July 7, 1996, was shot in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> and had separate runs in Taiwan and Japan.</p>
<p>Hsu's success in modeling achieved its zenith in July 1996 when she was featured on the cover of two biography (the Japanese edition, and the comparatively low-distribution Hong Kong edition). Like the photobooks, both these items have become very sought after by collectors and fans.</p>
<p>Hsu has continued to model for designers such as <a href="/wiki/Hang_Ten" title="Hang Ten">Hang Ten</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gucci" title="Gucci">Gucci</a> and Mode Marie, and has modelled in all three of her brief, autobiographical books: <i>Earth Onigiri</i> (2001), <i>Privacy</i> (2001, <a class="internal mw-magiclink-isbn" href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/4887450605">ISBN 4-88745-060-5</a>), and <i>Wo Ai Taiwan</i> (2002). In addition, most of her musical albums contain substantial inserts featuring new modelling work.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">Acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivian_Hsu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Vivian_Hsu_filmography" title="Vivian Hsu filmography">Vivian Hsu filmography</a></div>
<p>Hsu has appeared in a number of films and television programs both in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and Japan. She appeared in the ambitious 2006 mainland China-Taiwan-Hong Kong collaboration <i><a href="/wiki/The_Knot" title="The Knot">The Knot</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/Romance_film" title="Romance film">romance film</a> that takes place between the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">CCP</a>'s takeover of mainland China and the present. Her debut film was <i><a href="/wiki/Shaolin_Popey" title="Shaolin Popey">Shaolin Popey</a></i> (1994), a <a href="/wiki/Comedy_film" title="Comedy film">comedy film</a> which became a box office hit in Taiwan and Hong Kong. She also has some experience as a <a href="/wiki/Voice_acting_in_Japan" title="Voice acting in Japan">voice actress</a>, having voiced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aisha_(Gundam_SEED)" title="Aisha (Gundam SEED)">Aisha</a> in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gundam_SEED" title="Gundam SEED">Gundam SEED</a></i> for three episodes.</p>
<p>Hsu also starred in three Hong Kong motion pictures, <i>Hunting List</i>, <i>Devil Angel</i> and <i>Angel Heart</i>. She played the role of Chao Jiale in <i>Love Storm</i> (2003), alongside <a href="/wiki/Vic_Chou" title="Vic Chou">Vic Chou</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ken_Chu" title="Ken Chu">Ken Chu</a>. She also composed and sang the opening theme for this series, <i>Decide to Love You</i> (決定愛你), which was released as a single in 2003. As with many actors and actresses, she appears from time to time in commercials and as spokesperson for various causes. In 2006, Hsu played a role in the film <i>The Shoe Fairy of Do Do</i>, a modern <a href="/wiki/Fairy_tale" title="Fairy tale">fairy tale</a>.</p>
<p>Also appeared in the 2001 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Accidental_Spy" title="The Accidental Spy">The Accidental Spy</a></i>, with Jackie Chan.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivian_Hsu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Vivian_Hsu_discography" title="Vivian Hsu discography">Vivian Hsu discography</a></div>
<p>Hsu released her first solo single in 1995. She released her first full-length album, <i><a href="/wiki/Tianshi_Xiang" title="Tianshi Xiang">Tianshi Xiang</a></i> (天使想) in 1996. Shortly thereafter, she took a speed course in Korean, and released a Korean version of the album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Cheonsa_Misonyeo" title="Cheonsa Misonyeo">Cheonsa Misonyeo</a></i> (天使美少女). Unusually, neither of Hsu's first two albums was sung in her native language.</p>
<p>In 1997, with two Japanese comedians—<a href="/wiki/Kiyotaka_Nanbara" title="Kiyotaka Nanbara">Kyoya Nanami</a> and Amazan—and later with a second female vocalist known as 'Keddy', she was included in a musical act known as the Black Biscuits. The group was formed primarily as a "rival band" for the Japanese music group Pocket Biscuits, which was the creation of the Japanese variety show <i>Utchan Nanchan no UriNari</i>. Between 1997 and 1999, they released four hit singles and a full-length album, <i>Life</i>. Their single CD "<i>Timing</i>" recorded 1.5 million sales in 1998. And, they participated in <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dhaku_Uta_Gassen" title="Kōhaku Uta Gassen">Kōhaku Uta Gassen</a> in 1998.</p>
<p>Towards the end of the group's run, they failed to accomplish one of the challenges set forth by the variety show and the resulting punishment was the disbanding of Black Biscuits.</p>
<p>During this period, Hsu also released two more solo albums: <i><a href="/wiki/Da_Mafan" title="Da Mafan">Da Mafan</a></i> (大麻煩) in 1998, and <i><a href="/wiki/Bubai_de_Lianren" title="Bubai de Lianren">Bubai de Lianren</a></i> (不敗の戀人) in 1999. She followed <i>Bubai de Lianren</i> with a Japanese translation, <i>Fuhai no Koibito</i>, in 2000.</p>
<p>Later that same year she released another new album, <i><a href="/wiki/Jiaban_de_Tianshi" title="Jiaban de Tianshi">Jiaban de Tianshi</a></i> (假扮的天使).</p>
<p>In 2001, she formed The d.e.p with Masahide Sakuma, Gota Yashiki, Masami Tsuchiya and Mick Karn. The band was short-lived: by the end of the year they had released all the music they ever would. This amounted to two singles and a full-length album, <i>地球的病気 -We Are the d.e.p-</i>.</p>
<p>Over the next two years, she released a few more singles, including, alongside Kazuma Endo of <a href="/wiki/Siam_Shade" title="Siam Shade">Siam Shade</a>, <i>Moment</i>, the second opening theme to <i><a href="/wiki/Mobile_Suit_Gundam_SEED" title="Mobile Suit Gundam SEED">Mobile Suit Gundam SEED</a></i>, followed in 2003 by her next solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Secret_to_Happiness_Is_Love" title="The Secret to Happiness Is Love">The Secret to Happiness Is Love</a></i>. Her last album is <i><a href="/wiki/Hen_Hen_Ai" title="Hen Hen Ai">Hen Hen Ai</a></i> (狠狠愛), released in April 2005. Her most recent album is <i><a href="/wiki/Vivi_and..." title="Vivi and...">Vivi and...</a></i>, released in September 2006. For this album, Hsu collaborated with many artists and people including Jay Chou, Wang Leehom, Gary Cao, Nicky Lee and even her little brother and uncle.</p>
<p>Hsu's music, especially in her later work, covers a great range of genres. Included are slow love songs, R&amp;B, hard rock, and of course J-pop. She has even dabbled in rap. This, combined with the fact that she is fluent in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Standard_Mandarin" title="Standard Mandarin">Mandarin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cantonese" title="Cantonese">Cantonese</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a> makes her a very versatile artist.</p>
<p>Hsu is also an accomplished lyricist. Her lyric-writing talent first gained serious notice when she completed several songs for Taiwan's R&amp;B singer/songwriter <a href="/wiki/Jay_Chou" title="Jay Chou">Jay Chou</a>. By proving that she is not just another pretty idol, but a very capable artist, Hsu surprised both her critics and adorned fans. She continued to collaborate with Jay on his second and third albums after his triumphant self-titled debut, and co-wrote songs for other pop performers, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leehom_Wang" title="Leehom Wang">Leehom Wang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vanness_Wu" title="Vanness Wu">Vanness Wu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Show_Luo" title="Show Luo">Show Luo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gigi_Leung" title="Gigi Leung">Gigi Leung</a>. Her lyric work is much praised for its clever and straightforward simplicity.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Japan">Return to Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivian_Hsu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Return to Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hsu returned to Japan after a seven-year absence to release her new single <i>Beautiful Day</i> on March 3, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Since 2010 she has appeared in two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nippon_Television" title="Nippon Television">NTV</a> television shows, and two television commercials for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Aesthetic_Salons" title="Japanese Aesthetic Salons">aesthetic salon</a> Slim Beauty House, and <a href="/wiki/Kao_Corporation" title="Kao Corporation">Kao</a>'s <i>Essential</i> <a href="/wiki/Hair_care" title="Hair care">hair care</a> brand line as one of its three newfaces along with <a href="/wiki/Yuriko_Yoshitaka" title="Yuriko Yoshitaka">Yuriko Yoshitaka</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nozomi_Sasaki_(model)" title="Nozomi Sasaki (model)">Nozomi Sasaki</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivian_Hsu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January 2014, Vivian got engaged to 36-year-old Singapore businessman Sean Lee of Marco Polo Marine Limited.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> They held a wedding ceremony in Bali, Indonesia on June 26.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Vivian announced her pregnancy in March, 2015 via Facebook calling the pregnancy a "surprise" and "heaven sent" and said it came at a time when the self-professed workaholic promised to clear her work schedule in 2015 to focus on starting a family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On the 13th of August, she gave birth to a boy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
