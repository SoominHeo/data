<p><b>AD 80</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">LXXX</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Saturday" title="Leap year starting on Saturday">leap year starting on Saturday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_consul" title="Roman consul">Consulship</a> of <a href="/wiki/Titus" title="Titus">Augustus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Domitian" title="Domitian">Domitianus</a></b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 833 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination AD 80 for this year has been used since the early <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medieval_period" title="Medieval period">medieval period</a>, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AD_80&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AD_80&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Empire"><a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AD_80&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roman Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Roman_emperor" title="Roman emperor">Emperor</a> <a href="/wiki/Titus" title="Titus">Titus</a> completes and inaugurates the <a href="/wiki/Colosseum" title="Colosseum">Colosseum</a> with <a href="/wiki/Inaugural_games_of_the_Flavian_Amphitheatre" title="Inaugural games of the Flavian Amphitheatre">100 days of games</a>.</li>
<li>The earliest stage of <a href="/wiki/Lullingstone" title="Lullingstone">Lullingstone</a> <a href="/wiki/Roman_villa" title="Roman villa">Roman villa</a> is built.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Roman_conquest_of_Britain" title="Roman conquest of Britain">Roman occupation of Britain</a> reaches the <a href="/wiki/River_Tyne" title="River Tyne">River Tyne</a>–<a href="/wiki/Solway_Firth" title="Solway Firth">Solway Firth</a> frontier area. <a href="/wiki/Gnaeus_Julius_Agricola" title="Gnaeus Julius Agricola">Gnaeus Julius Agricola</a> creates a fleet for the conquest of <a href="/wiki/Caledonia" title="Caledonia">Caledonia</a>; he finally proves that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Britannia_(Roman_province)" title="Britannia (Roman province)">Britannia</a> is an island.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Legio_II_Adiutrix" title="Legio II Adiutrix">Legio II Adiutrix</a> is stationed at <a href="/wiki/Lindum_Colonia" title="Lindum Colonia">Lindum Colonia</a> (modern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lincoln,_Lincolnshire" title="Lincoln, Lincolnshire">Lincoln</a>). The city is an important settlement for retired <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_legionary" title="Roman legionary">Roman legionaries</a>.</li>
<li>The original Roman <a href="/wiki/Pantheon,_Rome" title="Pantheon, Rome">Pantheon</a> is destroyed in a fire, together with many other buildings.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Eifel_Aqueduct" title="Eifel Aqueduct">Eifel Aqueduct</a> is constructed to bring water 95 km (59 mi) from the <a href="/wiki/Eifel" title="Eifel">Eifel</a> region to Colonia Claudia Ara Agrippinensum (modern <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gnaeus_Julius_Agricola" title="Gnaeus Julius Agricola">Gnaeus Julius Agricola</a> begins his invasion of <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AD_80&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Some 30,000 <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asian</a> tribesmen <a href="/wiki/Human_migration" title="Human migration">migrate</a> from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steppes" title="Steppes">steppes</a> to the west with 40,000 <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horses</a> and 100,000 <a href="/wiki/Cattle" title="Cattle">cattle</a>, joining with <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iranian</a> tribesmen and with <a href="/wiki/Mongols" title="Mongols">Mongols</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberian</a> forests to form a group that will be known in Europe as the <a href="/wiki/Huns" title="Huns">Huns</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_topic">By topic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AD_80&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: By topic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Arts_and_sciences">Arts and sciences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AD_80&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Arts and sciences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Aeolipile" title="Aeolipile">aeolipile</a>, the first <a href="/wiki/Steam_engine" title="Steam engine">steam engine</a>, is described by <a href="/wiki/Hero_of_Alexandria" title="Hero of Alexandria">Hero of Alexandria</a>.</li>
</ul>
