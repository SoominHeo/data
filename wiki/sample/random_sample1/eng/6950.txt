<p><b>Visegrád</b> (<small>Hungarian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Hungarian" title="Help:IPA for Hungarian">[ˈviʃɛɡraːd]</a></span>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Castle_town" title="Castle town">castle town</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pest_(county)" title="Pest (county)">Pest County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>. It is north of <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a> on the right bank of the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Danube_Bend" title="Danube Bend">Danube Bend</a>. It had a population of 1,864 in 2010. Visegrád is famous for the remains of the Early Renaissance summer <a href="/wiki/Palace" title="Palace">palace</a> of King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matthias_Corvinus_of_Hungary" title="Matthias Corvinus of Hungary">Matthias Corvinus of Hungary</a> and the medieval <a href="/wiki/Citadel" title="Citadel">citadel</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Visegr%C3%A1d&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name <i>Visegrád</i> (<i>Vyšehrad</i>) is of <a href="/wiki/Slavic_languages" title="Slavic languages">Slavic</a> origin, meaning acropolis, literary "the upper castle" (the castle with a privileged position) or "the upper settlement". In modern <a href="/wiki/Slovak_language" title="Slovak language">Slovak</a> and <a href="/wiki/Czech_language" title="Czech language">Czech</a> the form is <i>Vyšehrad</i>. In <a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a> language, it is known as <i>Višegrad</i> and when pronounced, it sounds the same as it does in <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>.</p>
<p>There is a town in <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a>/<a href="/wiki/Republika_Srpska" title="Republika Srpska">Republika Srpska</a> with the same name, <a href="/wiki/Vi%C5%A1egrad" title="Višegrad">Višegrad</a>; a city in <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a> right next to <a href="/wiki/Kiev" title="Kiev">Kiev</a> named <a href="/wiki/Vyshhorod" title="Vyshhorod">Vyshhorod</a>; one in <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a> with the name <a href="/wiki/Wyszogr%C3%B3d" title="Wyszogród">Wyszogród</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Vy%C5%A1ehrad" title="Vyšehrad">Vyšehrad</a> castle in <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a> (<a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a>.</p>
<p>The castle of Visegrád is called <i>Fellegvár</i> in <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>, with the literal meaning "cloud castle".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Papp_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Papp-1">[1]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>, the town is called <i>Plintenburg</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Visegr%C3%A1d&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Visegrád was first mentioned in 1009 as a county town and the chief town of an <a href="/wiki/Archdeacon" title="Archdeacon">archdeaconry</a>. After the destructive <a href="/wiki/Mongol_invasion_of_Europe" title="Mongol invasion of Europe">Mongol invasion of Europe</a> in 1242, the town was rebuilt in a slightly different location to the south. King <a href="/wiki/Charles_I_of_Hungary" title="Charles I of Hungary">Charles I of Hungary</a> made Visegrád, his hometown, the royal seat of Hungary in 1325.</p>
<p>In 1335, Charles hosted at Visegrád a <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Visegr%C3%A1d_(1335)" title="Congress of Visegrád (1335)">two-month congress</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Bohemia" title="Bohemia">Bohemian</a> king, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_of_Luxembourg" title="John of Luxembourg">John of Luxembourg</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Poland_(1320%E2%80%931385)" title="Kingdom of Poland (1320–1385)">Polish</a> king, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Casimir_III_of_Poland" title="Casimir III of Poland">Casimir III</a>. It was crucial in creating a peace between the three kingdoms and securing an alliance between Poland and Hungary against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Habsburg" title="Habsburg">Habsburg</a> <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>. <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Visegr%C3%A1d_(1339)" title="Congress of Visegrád (1339)">Another congress</a> followed in 1338.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sigismund,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Sigismund, Holy Roman Emperor">Sigismund</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Holy Roman Emperor">Holy Roman Emperor</a> and King of Hungary and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Croatia_in_personal_union_with_Hungary" title="Croatia in personal union with Hungary">Croatia</a>, moved the royal seat to <a href="/wiki/Buda" title="Buda">Buda</a> between 1405 and 1408. King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matthias_Corvinus_of_Hungary" title="Matthias Corvinus of Hungary">Matthias Corvinus of Hungary</a> used Visegrád as a country residence.</p>
<p>Visegrád lost importance after the partition of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary" title="Kingdom of Hungary">Kingdom of Hungary</a> following the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Moh%C3%A1cs" title="Battle of Mohács">Battle of Mohács</a> in 1526.</p>
<p>In 1991, the leading politicians of <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a> met here to form a periodical forum, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Visegr%C3%A1d_group" title="Visegrád group">Visegrád group</a>, with an intentional allusion to the meeting centuries earlier in 1335.</p>
<p>Visegrád was granted <a href="/wiki/Town_privileges" title="Town privileges">town privileges</a> again in 2000.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Upper_Castle">Upper Castle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Visegr%C3%A1d&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Upper Castle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the Mongol invasion, King <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A9la_IV_of_Hungary" title="Béla IV of Hungary">Béla IV of Hungary</a> and his wife had a new fortification system constructed in the 1240-50s near the one destroyed earlier. The first part of the new system was the Upper Castle on top of a high hill. The castle was laid out on a triangular ground plan and had three towers at its corners. In the 14th century, at the time of the <a href="/wiki/Capetian_House_of_Anjou" title="Capetian House of Anjou">Angevin</a> kings of Hungary, the castle became a royal residence and was enlarged with a new curtain wall and palace buildings.</p>
<p>Around 1400 King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sigismund_of_Luxembourg" title="Sigismund of Luxembourg">Sigismund</a> had a third curtain wall constructed and enlarged the palace buildings. At the end of the 15th century, King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matthias_Corvinus_of_Hungary" title="Matthias Corvinus of Hungary">Matthias Corvinus</a> had the interior renovated. The Upper Castle also served for the safekeeping of the Hungarian <a href="/wiki/Regalia" title="Regalia">royal insignia</a> between the 14th century and 1526. In 1544 Visegrád was occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>, and, apart from a short period in 1595-1605, it remained in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ottoman_Turks" title="Ottoman Turks">Turkish</a> hands until 1685. The castle was seriously damaged by the Turks and was never used afterwards.</p>
<p>The castle is now open to the public to visit.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lower_Castle">Lower Castle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Visegr%C3%A1d&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Lower Castle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Lower Castle is the part of the fortification system that connects the Upper Castle with the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a>. In its centre rises the Solomon Tower, a large, hexagonal residential tower dating from the 13th century. In the 14th century, new curtain walls were built around the tower. During a Turkish raid in 1544, the southern part of the tower collapsed. Its renovation began only in the 1870s and was finished in the 1960s.</p>
<p>At present, the Tower houses exhibitions installed by the King Matthias Museum (Mátyás Király Múzeum) of Visegrád. The exhibitions present the reconstructed Gothic fountains from the Royal Palace, Renaissance sculpture in Visegrád, and the history of Visegrád.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Royal_Palace">Royal Palace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Visegr%C3%A1d&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Royal Palace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first royal house on this site was built by King <a href="/wiki/Charles_I_of_Hungary" title="Charles I of Hungary">Charles I of Hungary</a> after 1325. In the second half of the 14th century, this was enlarged into a palace by his son, King <a href="/wiki/Louis_I_of_Hungary" title="Louis I of Hungary">Louis I of Hungary</a>.</p>
<p>In the last third of the 14th century, King Louis and his successor Sigismund of Luxembourg had the majority of the earlier buildings dismantled and created a new, sumptuous palace complex, the extensive ruins of which are still visible today. The palace complex was laid out on a square ground plan measuring 123 x 123 m. A garden adjoined to it from the north and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Franciscan" title="Franciscan">Franciscan</a> friary, founded by King Sigismund in 1424, from the south. In the time of Louis I and Sigismund, the palace was the official residence of the kings of Hungary until about 1405-08.</p>
<p>Between 1477 and 1484 Matthias Corvinus had the palace complex reconstructed in late <a href="/wiki/Gothic_architecture" title="Gothic architecture">Gothic style</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Italian_Renaissance" title="Italian Renaissance">Italian Renaissance</a> <a href="/wiki/Renaissance_architecture" title="Renaissance architecture">architectural style</a> was used for decoration, the first time the style appeared in Europe outside <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. After the Ottoman Turks' siege in 1544, the palace fell into ruins. By the 18th century it was completely covered by earth. Its excavation began in 1934 and continues today.</p>
<p>The reconstructed royal residence building is open to the public and houses exhibitions on the history of the palace and reconstructed historical interiors.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sibrik_Hill">Sibrik Hill</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Visegr%C3%A1d&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sibrik Hill">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The ruins of this military camp can be seen outside Visegrád, to the north, on a hill that overlooks the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a>. The camp has a triangular ground plan. It was built in the first half of the 4th century as one of the important fortifications along the <a href="/wiki/Limes" title="Limes">limes</a>, the frontier of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Praetorium" title="Praetorium">praetorium</a> (the commander's building) was constructed at the end of the 4th century. In the early 5th century, the Roman army abandoned the military camp.</p>
<p>In the 10th and 11th centuries, the fortification, rebuilt as a <a href="/wiki/Castle" title="Castle">castle</a>, became a regional centre of the recently formed Hungarian state. "Visegrád" appears for the first time as the name of this regional centre (1009). The fortification was finally destroyed in 1242 by the <a href="/wiki/Mongol_invasion_of_Europe" title="Mongol invasion of Europe">Mongol invasion of Europe</a>.</p>
