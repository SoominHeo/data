<p><b>Bjarne Stroustrup</b> (<small>Danish:Â </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Danish" title="Help:IPA for Danish">[ËbjÉËnÉ ËsdÊÊÊÌ¯ËsdÊÉb]</a></span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> born 30 December 1950) is a Danish <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a>, most notable for the creation and development of the widely used <a href="/wiki/C%2B%2B" title="C++">C++</a> programming language.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He is a visiting professor at <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a>, and works at <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Stanley" title="Morgan Stanley">Morgan Stanley</a> as a Managing Director in New York.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bjarne_Stroustrup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stroustrup has a <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master's degree">master's degree</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> (1975) from <a href="/wiki/Aarhus_University" title="Aarhus University">Aarhus University</a>, Denmark, and a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Ph.D.</a> in computer science (1979) from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">University of Cambridge</a>, England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wong_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-wong-8">[8]</a></sup> His thesis advisor at Cambridge was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/David_Wheeler_(computer_scientist)" title="David Wheeler (computer scientist)">David Wheeler</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stroustrupphd_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-stroustrupphd-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bjarne_Stroustrup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stroustrup began developing C++ in 1978 (then called "<a href="/wiki/C_(programming_language)" title="C (programming language)">C</a> with Classes"), and, in his own words, "invented C++, wrote its early definitions, and produced its first implementation... chose and formulated the design criteria for C++, designed all its major facilities, and was responsible for the processing of extension proposals in the C++ standards committee."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Stroustrup also wrote a textbook for the language, <i><a href="/wiki/The_C%2B%2B_Programming_Language" title="The C++ Programming Language">The C++ Programming Language</a></i>.</p>
<p>Stroustrup was the head of <a href="/wiki/Bell_Labs" title="Bell Labs">AT&amp;T Bell Labs'</a> Large-scale Programming Research department, from its creation until late 2002. Stroustrup was elected member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Academy_of_Engineering" title="National Academy of Engineering">National Academy of Engineering</a> in 2004. He is a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">ACM</a> (1994) and an <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Electrical_and_Electronics_Engineers" title="Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers">IEEE</a> Fellow. From 2002 to 2014, Stroustrup was the College of Engineering Chair in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computer_Science" title="Computer Science">Computer Science</a> Professor at <a href="/wiki/Texas_A%26M_University" title="Texas A&amp;M University">Texas A&amp;M University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> As of January 2014, Stroustrup is a Managing Director in the technology division of <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Stanley" title="Morgan Stanley">Morgan Stanley</a> in New York City and a Visiting Professor in Computer Science at Columbia University.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/ITMO_University" title="ITMO University">ITMO University</a> noble doctor since 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, he was made a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Computer_History_Museum" title="Computer History Museum">Computer History Museum</a> for his invention of the C++ programming language.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Together with his wife and children, Stroustrup has been a resident of <a href="/wiki/Watchung,_New_Jersey" title="Watchung, New Jersey">Watchung, New Jersey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
