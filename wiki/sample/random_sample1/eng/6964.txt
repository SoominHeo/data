<p><span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><b>Villarreal</b></span> (<small>Spanish: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[ˈbiʎareˈal]</a></span>), officially <span lang="ca" xml:lang="ca"><b>Vila-real</b></span> (<small>Valencian: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Catalan" title="Help:IPA for Catalan">[ˈvila reˈaɫ]</a></span>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-racocatala2006_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-racocatala2006-1">[1]</a></sup> is a city in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Castell%C3%B3n" title="Province of Castellón">province of Castellón</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Valencian_Community" title="Valencian Community">Valencian Community</a>, Spain.</p>
<p>The city is located at 42 m above sea level, 7 km to the south of the province's capital (<a href="/wiki/Castell%C3%B3n_de_la_Plana" title="Castellón de la Plana">Castellón de la Plana</a>), which it is separate from by the <a href="/wiki/Mijares_(river)" title="Mijares (river)">Mijares</a> (or Millars) River. it has 51,367 inhabitants (2010 data), most of them living in the urban area that covers about 10.72% of its <i>comarca's</i> 55.4 km<sup>2</sup> surface. Ranked by population, it is the second-largest city in the province (after the capital), and fifteenth in the Valencian Community.</p>
<p>The city was founded with royal status by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_of_Aragon" title="King of Aragon">King</a> <a href="/wiki/James_I_of_Aragon" title="James I of Aragon">James I of Aragon</a> in 1274 during his campaign to regain Muslim territory in present-day Valencia during the <a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">Reconquista</a>. It later became an agricultural centre for orange cultivation, and more recently a centre for the ceramics industry.</p>
<p>The city is the birthplace of <b>Francisco de Asís Tárrega y Eixea</b> (21 November 1852 – 15 December 1909) who was a Spanish <a href="/wiki/Composer" title="Composer">composer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Classical_guitar" title="Classical guitar">classical guitarist</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Romantic_music" title="Romantic music">Romantic</a> period. He is also the composer of <i>Gran Vals</i>, an excerpt of which was used in the iconic <a href="/wiki/Nokia_tune" title="Nokia tune">Nokia ringtone</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Villarreal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Both the <a href="/wiki/Castilian_Spanish" title="Castilian Spanish">Castilian Spanish</a> <i>Villarreal</i> and <a href="/wiki/Valencian" title="Valencian">Valencian Catalan</a> <i>Vila-real</i> are <a href="/wiki/Cognate" title="Cognate">cognates</a> meaning 'Royal Village', due to the city's foundation by King <a href="/wiki/James_I_of_Aragon" title="James I of Aragon">James I</a> of <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Aragon" title="Crown of Aragon">Aragon</a>. Throughout the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>, as were most European settlements, it was officially known by its Latinised name <i>Villae Regalis</i>. A 1592 tapestry of the <a href="/wiki/Corts_Valencianes" title="Corts Valencianes">Valencian Parliament</a> shows the city's representative with the old Valencian name <i>Vilareal</i>. It was in the late 18th century, as Spain became more centralised, that the Castilian name took over. The city was renamed in 1939, after the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War" title="Spanish Civil War">Spanish Civil War</a>, as <i>Villarreal de los Infantes</i> (Royal Town of the <a href="/wiki/Infante" title="Infante">Infantes</a>), to avoid confusion with other Spanish localities with the same name. It is sometimes still referred to under this extended version.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-top1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-top1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 27 February 2006, after a campaign which started in the 1980s, the Valencian form became the only official name for the city, after a unanimous vote by the city council.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-racocatala2006_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-racocatala2006-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Inhabitants of the city are known as <i>vila-realencs</i> (male) or <i>vila-realenques</i> (female) in Valencian, and <i>villarrealenses</i> in Spanish.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Villarreal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>Villarreal was founded on 20 February 1274 by King James I of Aragon (hence its royal status), to strengthen his reconquest of Eastern Spain from the Moors. It was placed strategically on the ancient <a href="/wiki/Via_Augusta" title="Via Augusta">Via Augusta</a> 65 km north from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valencia,_Spain" title="Valencia, Spain">Valencia</a>, and in the outskirts of the then-Muslim stronghold of <a href="/wiki/Borriana,_Castell%C3%B3n" title="Borriana, Castellón">Borriana</a>. It was founded with royal status, with representation in the Valencian Parliament and Delegation of the Kingdom, and had the privilege of using the royal standard as its ensign. Villarreal was part of the royal sector of the Valencian Parliament and had an active say in its affairs.</p>
<p>The original town was surrounded by wall, but expanded outside these limits beginning in the 14th century due to an increase in population. The demographic and economic imbalances which characterise the 15th century carried on into the beginning of the 16th with constant conflicts between the Christian population and the <a href="/wiki/Mud%C3%A9jar" title="Mudéjar">Mudéjars</a> (Muslims who still lived in Christian majority Spain) who lived in the area.</p>
<p>During the 16th century, productive farm land was extended and enhanced by drilling wells to obtain water and convert dry land into irrigated fields, making it possible to begin commercial agriculture in the 17th century. After the city's disastrous participation in the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Spanish_Succession" title="War of the Spanish Succession">War of the Spanish Succession</a>, in the second half of the 18th century, the first industries were established, mainly for textiles. Also during that time, the city became involved in various revolts and wars which changed Spain's political make-up.</p>
<p>In the 19th century, the cultivation and trade of oranges was introduced, which gave the town great economic progress, led both by businessmen and by farmers' associations or "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agricultural_marketing_cooperative#Marketing_cooperatives" title="Agricultural marketing cooperative">cooperatives</a>". After the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War" title="Spanish Civil War">Spanish Civil War</a> (1936-1939), the earnings obtained from the orange trade allowed several entrepreneurs to build ceramic tile factories as a way to diversify their industries. Ceramics has continued to expand up to the present day, and is now Villareal's main economic activity.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Villarreal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Villarreal's economy has evolved since the days of cultivation and trade of <a href="/wiki/Orange_(fruit)" title="Orange (fruit)">oranges</a> in the last century to the manufacturing of <a href="/wiki/Ceramic" title="Ceramic">ceramic</a> <a href="/wiki/Tile" title="Tile">tiles</a>, which today is its most important source of income.</p>
<p>It hosts 4 of Spain's 500 largest businesses, all of which trade in the ceramic tile industry. Other industries in the city include financial services, bricks and other clay products, and basic metals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-top1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-top1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Villarreal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:El_Madrigal_Stadium_02.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1354" data-file-width="2032" height="133" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/El_Madrigal_Stadium_02.jpg/200px-El_Madrigal_Stadium_02.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/El_Madrigal_Stadium_02.jpg/300px-El_Madrigal_Stadium_02.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/El_Madrigal_Stadium_02.jpg/400px-El_Madrigal_Stadium_02.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:El_Madrigal_Stadium_02.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Villarreal_CF" title="Villarreal CF">Villarreal CF</a> playing at the <a href="/wiki/Estadio_de_la_Cer%C3%A1mica" title="Estadio de la Cerámica">Estadio de la Cerámica</a></div>

<p>The town is home to <a href="/wiki/Villarreal_CF" title="Villarreal CF">Villarreal CF</a>, a football club in <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a>, Spain's highest tier of football. Founded in 1923, the club reached the national divisions in 1956 and the top-tier in 1998. During a successful spell in La Liga, the side were League runners-up in the 2007-08 season and a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> semi-finalist in <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2005–06 UEFA Champions League">2006</a>. The team play at the <a href="/wiki/Estadio_de_la_Cer%C3%A1mica" title="Estadio de la Cerámica">Estadio de la Cerámica</a> which, with a 25,000-seat capacity, could fit half of the population of the town.</p>
<p>Villarreal CF full-back <a href="/wiki/Joan_Capdevila" title="Joan Capdevila">Joan Capdevila</a> played in the Spain national squad that won the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>.</p>
