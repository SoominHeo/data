<p><i><b>Puente Viejo</b></i> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[ˈpwente ˈβjexo]</a></span>, "Old Bridge") is the oldest and smallest of three <a href="/wiki/Bridge" title="Bridge">bridges</a> that span the 120-metre (390 ft) deep <a href="/wiki/Canyon" title="Canyon">chasm</a> that carries the <a href="/wiki/Guadalev%C3%ADn" title="Guadalevín">Guadalevín</a> River and divides the city of <a href="/wiki/Ronda" title="Ronda">Ronda</a> in southern <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. It was built in 1616, and currently only carries <a href="/wiki/Pedestrian" title="Pedestrian">pedestrian</a> traffic.</p>
