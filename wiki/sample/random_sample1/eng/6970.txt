<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Before_and_during_the_Nguyen_Dynasty">Before and during the Nguyen Dynasty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bi%C3%AAn_H%C3%B2a&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Before and during the Nguyen Dynasty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The area around Biên Hòa was part of small kingdom prior to being annexed by <a href="/wiki/Chenla" title="Chenla">Chenla</a>. It was an agricultural and fishing region.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Capture_of_Bi%C3%AAn_H%C3%B2a" title="Capture of Biên Hòa">capture of Biên Hòa</a> on December 16, 1861 was an important allied victory in the <a href="/wiki/Cochinchina_Campaign" title="Cochinchina Campaign">Cochinchina Campaign</a> (1858–62). This campaign, fought between the French and the Spanish on the one side and the Vietnamese (under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nguyen_Dynasty" title="Nguyen Dynasty">Nguyen Dynasty</a>) on the other, began as a limited <a href="/wiki/Punitive_expedition" title="Punitive expedition">punitive expedition</a> and ended as a French war of conquest. The war concluded with the establishment of the French colony of Cochinchina, a development that inaugurated nearly a century of French colonial dominance in Vietnam.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Republic_of_Vietnam">Republic of Vietnam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bi%C3%AAn_H%C3%B2a&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Republic of Vietnam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Biên Hòa grew into a major suburb of Saigon as the capital city of the <a href="/wiki/South_Vietnam" title="South Vietnam">Republic of (South) Vietnam</a> grew. Following the <a href="/wiki/First_Indochina_War" title="First Indochina War">First Indochina War</a>, tens of thousands of refugees from the northern and central regions of Vietnam—a large portion of whom were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" title="Roman Catholicism">Roman Catholics</a> — resettled in Biên Hòa as part of <a href="/wiki/Operation_Passage_to_Freedom" title="Operation Passage to Freedom">Operation Passage to Freedom</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">United States Air Force</a> operated <a href="/wiki/Bien_Hoa_Air_Base" title="Bien Hoa Air Base">Bien Hoa Air Base</a> near the city. <a href="/wiki/Mortar_(weapon)" title="Mortar (weapon)">Mortar</a> attacks on U.S. and <a href="/wiki/Army_of_the_Republic_of_Vietnam" title="Army of the Republic of Vietnam">ARVN</a> targets were frequently staged from residential districts in Biên Hòa. Two of the better-known attacks took place during Tet of 1968 as well as 1969.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Socialist_Republic">Socialist Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bi%C3%AAn_H%C3%B2a&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Socialist Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Like most other areas of Vietnam, post-war Biên Hòa suffered a period of severe economic decline between 1975 and the second half of the 1980s. In part, because of its high concentration of former refugees and their descendants who had fled the <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">Communist</a> government of <a href="/wiki/North_Vietnam" title="North Vietnam">North Vietnam</a> in the mid-1950s, Biên Hòa was the site of small-scale resistance to the Communist government in the months immediately following <a href="/wiki/Fall_of_Saigon" title="Fall of Saigon">the fall</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Vietnam" title="Republic of Vietnam">Republic of Vietnam</a>.</p>
<p>In the 1980s, the government of the Socialist Republic of Vietnam initiated the economic reform policy of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doi_Moi" title="Doi Moi">Đổi Mới</a> <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (December 2011)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and Biên Hòa experienced an economic resurgence. Biên Hòa and the surrounding areas received large amounts of foreign investment capital, and the area rapidly industrialized.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>As of 2005<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Bi%C3%AAn_H%C3%B2a&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, Biên Hòa is now an industrial center of southern Vietnam, and many factories and warehouses (often funded in collaboration with Japanese, Singaporean, American, Swiss and other foreign investors) operate in the area surrounding the city. <a href="/wiki/Bien_Hoa_Sugar" title="Bien Hoa Sugar">Bien Hoa Sugar</a> is located near the city.</p>
<p>With regard to entertainment, the city includes several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amusement_parks" title="Amusement parks">amusement parks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nightclub" title="Nightclub">nightclubs</a> and restaurants lining the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BB%93ng_Nai_River" title="Đồng Nai River">Đồng Nai River</a>. Construction has increased rapidly (with many Western-style houses and villas under development), and the real estate market has experienced a series of boom cycles since the mid-1990s.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Biên Hòa also is the location of the Biên Hòa Military Cemetery, a large national cemetery for fallen soldiers and military officials of the former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Vietnam" title="Republic of Vietnam">Republic of Vietnam</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ARVN" title="ARVN">ARVN</a>). The cemetery today is now neglected by the current communist regime, and many sections of the cemetery are either vandalized, or demolished for the construction of various building projects. Most of the time there was no proper reburial for the skeletal remains, and this caused an outcry by <a href="/wiki/Overseas_Vietnamese" title="Overseas Vietnamese">Overseas Vietnamese</a>, most of whom came from the South.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The Vietnamese America Foundation, and its program called "The Returning Casualty" are attempting to restore the cemetery and excavate a nearby mass grave.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-martin_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-martin-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
