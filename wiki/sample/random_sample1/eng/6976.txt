<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acteonoidea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Taxonomy_of_the_Gastropoda_(Bouchet_%26_Rocroi,_2005)" title="Taxonomy of the Gastropoda (Bouchet &amp; Rocroi, 2005)">taxonomy of Bouchet &amp; Rocroi</a> (2005), the superfamily Acteonoidea has been included into the informal group "<a href="/wiki/Lower_Heterobranchia" title="Lower Heterobranchia">Lower Heterobranchia</a>" (Heterostropha <i>sensu</i> Ponder &amp; Warén, 1988), also known as the Allogastropoda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bouchet_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bouchet-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Only one of analyses by Jörger et al. (2010)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-3">[3]</a></sup> indicates the Acteonoidea sister to <a href="/wiki/Nudipleura" title="Nudipleura">Nudipleura</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-3">[3]</a></sup> This clade that had resulted repeatedly in molecular studies with still limited "lower heterobranch" taxon sampling, either in a derived position or as a basal offshoot within Euthyneura.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-3">[3]</a></sup> A recent molecular phylogeny on Acteonoidea suggest a common origin with lower heterobranch <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rissoelloidea" title="Rissoelloidea">Rissoelloidea</a> and a sister group relationship to Nudipleura.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gbbeler_2010_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gbbeler_2010-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-3">[3]</a></sup> While the basal position of Acteonoidea was commonly accepted, some authors doubted the basal position of Nudipleura, which was originally considered as a highly derived taxon, and suspect rate heterogeneity and deviant base composition as causing this unnatural grouping.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-3">[3]</a></sup> Based on potential <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Synapomorphies" title="Synapomorphies">synapomorphies</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Reproductive_system_of_gastropods" title="Reproductive system of gastropods">reproductive system</a> (presence of a ciliary stripe within the ampulla, androdiaulic or triaulic pallial gonoduct), Ghiselin already suggested a relationship between Acteonoidea and Nudipleura.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-3">[3]</a></sup> However, Acteonoidea form a well-supported "lower heterobranch" clade with Rissoelloidea, confirming results by Aktipis et al. and Dinapoli and Klussmann-Kolb.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-3">[3]</a></sup> The latter authors also recovered Nudipleura as the first offshoot of Euthyneura, which is confirmed by our<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (October 2012)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> study. Salvini-Plawen and Steiner grouped Umbraculoidea with Nudipleura, but none of the recent molecular or morphological studies support such a relationship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Families">Families</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acteonoidea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Families">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The following five <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">families</a> have been recognized in <a href="/wiki/Taxonomy_of_the_Gastropoda_(Bouchet_%26_Rocroi,_2005)" title="Taxonomy of the Gastropoda (Bouchet &amp; Rocroi, 2005)">the taxonomy of Bouchet &amp; Rocroi (2005)</a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bouchet_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bouchet-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>family <a href="/wiki/Acteonidae" title="Acteonidae">Acteonidae</a> d' Orbigny, 1843</li>
<li>† family <a href="/wiki/Acteonellidae" title="Acteonellidae">Acteonellidae</a> Gill, 1871</li>
<li>family <a href="/wiki/Aplustridae" title="Aplustridae">Aplustridae</a> Gray, 1847 - former name Hydatinidae has been declared invalid</li>
<li>family <a href="/wiki/Bullinidae" title="Bullinidae">Bullinidae</a> Gray, 1850</li>
<li>† family <a href="/wiki/Zardinellidae" title="Zardinellidae">Zardinellidae</a> Bandel, 1994</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acteonoidea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All acteonoids have a <a href="/wiki/Gastropod_shell" title="Gastropod shell">shell</a> that resembles that of many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prosobranch" title="Prosobranch">prosobranchs</a>. Some of the members are able to withdraw completely into the shell and to close the shell with an <a href="/wiki/Operculum_(gastropod)" title="Operculum (gastropod)">operculum</a>, e.g. <i><a href="/wiki/Acteon_tornatilis" title="Acteon tornatilis">Acteon tornatilis</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-W.C3.A4gele_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-W.C3.A4gele-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Anatomy">Anatomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acteonoidea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Anatomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>No defensive strategies are known from these animals although histological investigations show a highly glandular area in the <a href="/wiki/Mantle_(mollusc)" title="Mantle (mollusc)">mantle</a> cavity and the mantle rim. The mantle rim glands, for example, are very conspicuous. These comprise large <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Epithelial_cell" title="Epithelial cell">epithelial cells</a> that are filled with a non-staining <a href="/wiki/Vacuole" title="Vacuole">vacuole</a>. The glandular area is highly folded. The cells appear to lie subepithelially due to their size. They alternate with small <a href="/wiki/Cilium" title="Cilium">ciliated cells</a>. The hypobranchial gland in the roof of the mantle cavity is small and consists of violet-staining epithelial cells indicating acid <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mucopolysaccharide" title="Mucopolysaccharide">mucopolysaccharides</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-W.C3.A4gele_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-W.C3.A4gele-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Feeding_habits">Feeding habits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acteonoidea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Feeding habits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Acteonidae and Aplustridae are carnivorous and mainly feed on <a href="/wiki/Polychaete" title="Polychaete">polychaetes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-W.C3.A4gele_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-W.C3.A4gele-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
