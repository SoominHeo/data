<p><b>Nasal septum deviation</b> or <b>deviated nasal septum</b> (<b>DNS</b>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a physical disorder of the <a href="/wiki/Human_nose" title="Human nose">nose</a>, involving a displacement of the <a href="/wiki/Nasal_septum" title="Nasal septum">nasal septum</a>. Some displacement is common, affecting 80% of people, most unknowingly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-webmd_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-webmd-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Signs_and_symptoms">Signs and symptoms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasal_septum_deviation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Signs and symptoms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Only more severe cases of a deviated septum will cause symptoms of difficulty breathing and require treatment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-webmd_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-webmd-2">[2]</a></sup> Symptoms of a deviated septum include <a href="/wiki/Infection" title="Infection">infections</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Paranasal_sinuses" title="Paranasal sinuses">sinus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sleep_apnea" title="Sleep apnea">sleep apnea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoring" title="Snoring">snoring</a>, repetitive <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sneezing" title="Sneezing">sneezing</a>, facial pain, <a href="/wiki/Nosebleed" title="Nosebleed">nosebleeds</a>, difficulty with <a href="/wiki/Breathing" title="Breathing">breathing</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and mild to severe loss of the ability to <a href="/wiki/Olfaction" title="Olfaction">smell</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Nasal_septum" title="Nasal septum">nasal septum</a> is the bone and cartilage in the nose that separates the <a href="/wiki/Nasal_cavity" title="Nasal cavity">nasal cavity</a> into the two <a href="/wiki/Nostril" title="Nostril">nostrils</a>. The cartilage is called the quadrangular cartilage and the bones comprising the septum include the maxillary crest, <a href="/wiki/Vomer" title="Vomer">vomer</a> and the perpendicular plate of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ethmoid" title="Ethmoid">ethmoid</a>. Normally, the septum lies centrally, and thus the nasal passages are symmetrical.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aao_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-aao-5">[5]</a></sup> A deviated septum is an abnormal condition in which the top of the cartilaginous ridge leans to the left or the right, causing obstruction of the affected nasal passage. The condition can result in poor drainage of the sinuses. People can also complain of difficulty <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Breath" title="Breath">breathing</a>, headaches, bloody noses, or of sleeping disorders such as snoring or sleep apnea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aao_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-aao-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>It is common for nasal septa to depart from the exact centerline; the septum is only considered deviated if the shift is substantial or is adversely affecting the patient.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metson_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-metson-6">[6]</a></sup> Many people with a deviation are unaware they have it until some <a href="/wiki/Pain" title="Pain">pain</a> is produced. By itself, a deviated septum can go undetected for years and thus be without any need for correction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metson_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-metson-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Causes">Causes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasal_septum_deviation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Causes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is most frequently caused by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Physical_trauma" title="Physical trauma">impact trauma</a>, such as by a blow to the <a href="/wiki/Face" title="Face">face</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metson_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-metson-6">[6]</a></sup> It can also be a <a href="/wiki/Congenital_disorder" title="Congenital disorder">congenital disorder</a>, caused by compression of the nose during childbirth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metson_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-metson-6">[6]</a></sup> Deviated septum is associated with genetic connective tissue disorders such as <a href="/wiki/Marfan_syndrome" title="Marfan syndrome">Marfan syndrome</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Homocystinuria" title="Homocystinuria">Homocystinuria</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and <a href="/wiki/Ehlers%E2%80%93Danlos_syndrome" title="Ehlers–Danlos syndrome">Ehlers–Danlos syndrome</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Treatment">Treatment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasal_septum_deviation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Treatment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In mild cases, symptoms can simply be treated with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medications" title="Medications">medications</a> such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Decongestants" title="Decongestants">decongestants</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antihistamines" title="Antihistamines">antihistamines</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nasal_spray" title="Nasal spray">nasal spray</a>. Medication temporarily relieves symptoms, but does not correct the underlying condition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Non-medical relief can also be obtained using <a href="/wiki/Nasal_strip" title="Nasal strip">nasal strips</a>. A cure to symptoms related to septal deviations is available in the form of a minor surgical procedure known as a <a href="/wiki/Septoplasty" title="Septoplasty">septoplasty</a>. The surgery is performed quickly (lasts roughly 1 hour) and does not result in any cosmetic alteration or external scars. Recovery from the procedure may take anywhere from 2 days to 4 weeks to heal completely.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Septal bones never regrow. If symptoms reappear they are not related to deviations. Reappearance of symptoms may be due to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mucosal" title="Mucosal">mucosal</a> <a href="/wiki/Metaplasia" title="Metaplasia">metaplasia</a> of the nose.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Complications_of_septoplasty">Complications of septoplasty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasal_septum_deviation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Complications of septoplasty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nasal_septum_perforation" title="Nasal septum perforation">Nasal septum perforation</a> due to bilateral trauma of the mucoperichondrial flaps opposite each other.</li>
<li>Septal <a href="/wiki/Hematoma" title="Hematoma">hematoma</a> and septal <a href="/wiki/Abscess" title="Abscess">abscess</a>.</li>
<li>Adhesions and synachiae between septal mucosa and lateral nasal wall.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Saddle_nose" title="Saddle nose">Saddle nose</a> due to over-resection of the dorsal wall of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Septal_cartilage" title="Septal cartilage">septal cartilage</a>.</li>
<li>Dropped nasal tip due to resection of the caudal margin.</li>
</ul>
