<p><b>Marcel Theo Hall</b> (born April 8, 1964),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AllMusic_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-AllMusic-1">[1]</a></sup> better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Biz Markie</b>, is an American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper">rapper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beatboxing" title="Beatboxing">beatboxer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a>, <a href="/wiki/Comedian" title="Comedian">comedian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reality_television" title="Reality television">reality television</a> personality, <a href="/wiki/Grunge" title="Grunge">grartist</a>, and commercial spokesperson. He is best known for his single "<a href="/wiki/Just_a_Friend" title="Just a Friend">Just a Friend</a>", an American <a href="/wiki/Top_40" title="Top 40">Top 10</a> hit in 1989. In 2008, "Just a Friend" made #100 on <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>'s list of the 100 greatest hip hop songs of all time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Biz Markie currently lives in <a href="/wiki/Howard_County,_Maryland" title="Howard County, Maryland">Howard County, Maryland</a>, and has helped support County Executive <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ken_Ulman" title="Ken Ulman">Ken Ulman</a> during his fundraisers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Markie has been called "the <a href="/wiki/Honorific_nicknames_in_popular_music" title="Honorific nicknames in popular music">Clown Prince of Hip Hop</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biz_Markie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Markie's career began on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_Islip" title="Central Islip">Long Island, New York</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He attended and graduated from <a href="/wiki/Longwood_Central_School_District" title="Longwood Central School District">Longwood High School</a> in 1982.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biz_Markie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1980s">1980s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biz_Markie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1980s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Before he got famous Biz Markie was interviewed in the 1986 cult documentary <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Big_Fun_In_The_Big_Town" title="Big Fun In The Big Town">Big Fun In The Big Town</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Markie released his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Goin%27_Off" title="Goin' Off">Goin' Off</a></i> in 1988, which attracted a fair amount of attention, largely due to the lead single, "Make the Music With Your Mouth, Biz". The album also featured the underground hit singles "Nobody Beats The Biz", "<a href="/wiki/Vapors_(song)" title="Vapors (song)">Vapors</a>", and "Pickin' Boogers".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 10, 1989 <i><a href="/wiki/The_Biz_Never_Sleeps" title="The Biz Never Sleeps">The Biz Never Sleeps</a></i>, Biz Markie's second studio album, was released on Cold Chillin'/Warner Bros. Records, produced by Biz, his cousin Cool V and Paul C. The single "Just a Friend", in which he alternates between rap and singing, became Markie's most successful single, reaching #9 on the Billboard charts.</p>
<p>The song interpolates the 1968 song "You Got What I Need" by singer/songwriter <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Scott" title="Freddie Scott">Freddie Scott</a>, whose basic chord and melody provided the base for the song's chorus. "Just A Friend" was ranked 81st on VH1's 100 Greatest One-Hit Wonders in 2000, and later as number 100 on VH1's 100 Greatest Songs of Hip Hop in 2008.</p>
<p>The music video, directed by Lionel C. Martin, chronicles the rapper's woman problems. At the climax of the video, Biz Markie's character stumbles upon a girl he was trying to date kissing another man she had previously referred to as "just a friend". It also includes a memorable scene of Biz singing the chorus dressed as Mozart in 18th-century clothing with a powdered wig in a candlelit room while playing the piano. This scene was likely inspired by the video for the 1986 hit "Rock Me Amadeus", which was in turn inspired by the 1984 film <i>Amadeus</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s">1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biz_Markie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1990s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Markie's third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/I_Need_a_Haircut" title="I Need a Haircut">I Need a Haircut</a></i> was released on August 27, 1991, on Cold Chillin'/Warner Bros. Records and was produced by Biz Markie and his cousin Cool V. Sales of the album were already low when Markie was served a lawsuit by <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_O%27Sullivan" title="Gilbert O'Sullivan">Gilbert O'Sullivan</a>, who claimed that the album's "Alone Again" featured an unauthorized sample from his hit "<a href="/wiki/Alone_Again_(Naturally)" title="Alone Again (Naturally)">Alone Again (Naturally)</a>". O'Sullivan's claim was upheld in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landmark_ruling" title="Landmark ruling">landmark ruling</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Upright_Music,_Ltd._v._Warner_Bros._Records_Inc." title="Grand Upright Music, Ltd. v. Warner Bros. Records Inc.">Grand Upright Music, Ltd. v. Warner Bros. Records Inc.</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> that altered the landscape of hip-hop, finding that all samples must be cleared with the original artist before being used. In accordance with the ruling, Warner Bros., the parent company of Cold Chillin', had to pull <i>I Need a Haircut</i> from circulation, and all companies had to clear samples with the samples' creators before releasing the records. This development reflected the increasing popularity of hip-hop and the financial stakes over which releases were set. Biz responded in 1993 with the mischievously titled <i>All Samples Cleared!</i>, but his career had been hurt by the publicity emanating from the lawsuit, and the record suffered accordingly. Additional bad news came when the video for the track 'Toilet Stool Rap' was labeled Worst Video of the Year on the <i><a href="/wiki/Fromage" title="Fromage">Fromage</a></i> show from Canada's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MuchMusic" title="MuchMusic">MuchMusic</a>.</p>
<p>For the remainder of the decade, Markie occasionally made television appearances, including guest appearances on <i><a href="/wiki/In_Living_Color" title="In Living Color">In Living Color</a></i> (including as contestant Damian "Foosball" Franklin in the recurring game show sketch "The Dirty Dozens" and as Marlon Cain in "Ed Bacon: Guidance Counselor") and in a 1996 <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle rap</a> commercial on <a href="/wiki/MTV2" title="MTV2">MTV2</a>. He also made numerous guest appearances with the <a href="/wiki/Beastie_Boys" title="Beastie Boys">Beastie Boys</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Check_Your_Head" title="Check Your Head">Check Your Head</a></i> (1992), <i><a href="/wiki/Ill_Communication" title="Ill Communication">Ill Communication</a></i> (1994), <i><a href="/wiki/Hello_Nasty" title="Hello Nasty">Hello Nasty</a></i> (1998), and their anthology <i><a href="/wiki/Beastie_Boys_Anthology:_The_Sounds_of_Science" title="Beastie Boys Anthology: The Sounds of Science">The Sounds of Science</a></i> (1999). He also rapped on the song "Schizo Jam", on Don Byron's 1998 release, <i>Nu Blaxploitation</i> (Blue Note/Capitol) and worked with <a href="/wiki/Canibus" title="Canibus">Canibus</a> on the first track on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Office_Space_soundtrack" title="Office Space soundtrack">Office Space soundtrack</a> (1999). He also rapped on the track "So Fresh" alongside <a href="/wiki/Slick_Rick" title="Slick Rick">Slick Rick</a> on <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">Will Smith</a>'s 1999 album <i><a href="/wiki/Willennium" title="Willennium">Willennium</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 1996, Markie appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Organization" title="Red Hot Organization">Red Hot Organization</a>'s compilation CD, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/America_is_Dying_Slowly" title="America is Dying Slowly">America is Dying Slowly</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coolio" title="Coolio">Coolio</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a>, among others. The CD, meant to raise awareness of the AIDS epidemic among African American men, was heralded as a masterpiece by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Source_(magazine)" title="The Source (magazine)">The Source</a> magazine.</p>
<p>In 1997, Markie appeared on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rolling_Stones" title="Rolling Stones">Rolling Stones</a>' song "<a href="/wiki/Anybody_Seen_My_Baby%3F" title="Anybody Seen My Baby?">Anybody Seen My Baby?</a>" on their album <i><a href="/wiki/Bridges_to_Babylon" title="Bridges to Babylon">Bridges to Babylon</a></i>. His part was shortened on some radio versions. Biz also teamed up with <a href="/wiki/Frankie_Cutlass" title="Frankie Cutlass">Frankie Cutlass</a> on his third single and music video titled "The Cypher Part 3" with some of <a href="/wiki/Marley_Marl" title="Marley Marl">Marley Marl</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Juice_Crew" title="Juice Crew">Juice Crew</a> veterans.</p>
<p>In 1999, Markie appeared on <a href="/wiki/Len_(band)" title="Len (band)">Len's</a> song "Beautiful Day" on their album <i><a href="/wiki/You_Can%27t_Stop_the_Bum_Rush" title="You Can't Stop the Bum Rush">You Can't Stop the Bum Rush</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s">2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biz_Markie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2002, Markie appeared as an alien in <i><a href="/wiki/Men_in_Black_II" title="Men in Black II">Men in Black II</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">Will Smith</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Lee_Jones" title="Tommy Lee Jones">Tommy Lee Jones</a>, essentially playing an alien parody of himself, whose native language sounded exactly like <a href="/wiki/Beatboxing" title="Beatboxing">beatboxing</a>. Between 2002 and 2003 he appeared in episode 5 of the TV series <a href="/wiki/Fastlane_(TV_series)" title="Fastlane (TV series)">Fastlane</a> playing himself as a nightclub DJ. In 2003 he appeared in the international television series titled <a href="/wiki/Kung_Faux" title="Kung Faux">Kung Faux</a> performing a series of voice over characters featured in a variety of episodes. In 2004, his song <i>Vapors</i> appeared on the soundtrack of <a href="/wiki/Rockstar_Games" title="Rockstar Games">Rockstar</a>'s popular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Videogame" title="Videogame">videogame</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto:_San_Andreas" title="Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas">Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas</a></i> which featured an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_school_hip_hop" title="Old school hip hop">old school hip hop</a> radio station, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Playback_FM" title="Playback FM">Playback FM</a>. In 2005, Biz detoured from his recording duties to appear on the first season of the television show <i><a href="/wiki/Celebrity_Fit_Club_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Celebrity Fit Club (U.S. TV series)">Celebrity Fit Club</a></i> which challenged celebrities to lose weight by a combination of diet and exercise. Biz Markie lost more weight than anybody else in the competition. That year, he was also in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Andy_Milonakis_Show" title="The Andy Milonakis Show">The Andy Milonakis Show</a></i>.</p>
<p>Biz Markie was a cast member on <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cannon" title="Nick Cannon">Nick Cannon</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_%27n_Out" title="Wild 'n Out">Wild 'n Out</a></i>, seasons 1 and 3. Biz also does the beatboxing segment, <i>Biz's Beat of the Day</i> on the <a href="/wiki/Nick_Jr." title="Nick Jr.">Nick Jr.</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/Yo_Gabba_Gabba!" title="Yo Gabba Gabba!">Yo Gabba Gabba!</a></i>.</p>
<p>Biz Markie began 2008 opening for <a href="/wiki/Chris_Rock" title="Chris Rock">Chris Rock</a>'s "No Apologies" tour. Biz Markie's act includes spinning records ranging from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_school_hip_hop" title="Old school hip hop">old school hip hop</a> to <a href="/wiki/Lynyrd_Skynyrd" title="Lynyrd Skynyrd">Lynyrd Skynyrd</a> and then performing "Just a Friend".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Biz Markie's playlist includes the following: "<a href="/wiki/Children%27s_Story" title="Children's Story">Children's Story</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Slick_Rick" title="Slick Rick">Slick Rick</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Rapper%27s_Delight" title="Rapper's Delight">Rapper's Delight</a>" by <a href="/wiki/The_Sugarhill_Gang" title="The Sugarhill Gang">The Sugarhill Gang</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Billie_Jean" title="Billie Jean">Billie Jean</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Holiday_(Madonna_song)" title="Holiday (Madonna song)">Holiday</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Wake_Me_Up_Before_You_Go-Go" title="Wake Me Up Before You Go-Go">Wake Me Up Before You Go-Go</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Wham!" title="Wham!">Wham!</a>, "<a href="/wiki/It_Takes_Two_(Rob_Base_%26_DJ_E-Z_Rock_song)" title="It Takes Two (Rob Base &amp; DJ E-Z Rock song)">It Takes Two</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Rob_Base_and_DJ_E-Z_Rock" title="Rob Base and DJ E-Z Rock">Rob Base and DJ E-Z Rock</a>, "<a href="/wiki/The_Breaks" title="The Breaks">The Breaks</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Kurtis_Blow" title="Kurtis Blow">Kurtis Blow</a> and "<a href="/wiki/Robot_Rock_(song)" title="Robot Rock (song)">Robot Rock</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Daft_Punk" title="Daft Punk">Daft Punk</a>.</p>
<p>In December 2009, Biz Markie appeared in a <a href="/wiki/RadioShack" title="RadioShack">RadioShack</a> commercial, repeating the line: "Oh Snap! Guess what I saw!" from his song "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Just_A_Friend" title="Just A Friend">Just A Friend</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> That same year saw his debut with <a href="/wiki/Andy_Milonakis" title="Andy Milonakis">Andy Milonakis</a> in television commercials for the commercial Internet service, Tune Up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s">2010s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biz_Markie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, Biz Markie appeared on VH1's <i>100 Greatest Artists of All Time</i>, providing commentary throughout the series. Biz Markie himself was not included on the list. On November 9, 2010, Biz appeared on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Aquabats!" title="The Aquabats!">The Aquabats!</a> new EP, <i><a href="/wiki/Radio_Down!" title="Radio Down!">Radio Down!</a></i> in the title track. On November 11, 2010, Biz sat in with <a href="/wiki/The_Roots" title="The Roots">The Roots</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_with_Jimmy_Fallon" title="Late Night with Jimmy Fallon">Late Night with Jimmy Fallon</a></i>, and performed "Just a Friend" with actor <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Goldblum" title="Jeff Goldblum">Jeff Goldblum</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, Markie appeared several times on MTV2's new game show <i>Hip Hop Squares</i>, a spin-off of the popular game show <i><a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Squares" title="Hollywood Squares">Hollywood Squares</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2012 Biz Markie made an appearance in the track "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2012_(You_Must_Be_Upgraded)" title="2012 (You Must Be Upgraded)">2012 (You Must Be Upgraded)</a>" by Oklahoma band The Flaming Lips alongside Ke$ha.</p>
<p>In 2013 Markie toured with the <i><a href="/wiki/Yo_Gabba_Gabba!" title="Yo Gabba Gabba!">Yo Gabba Gabba!</a></i> live show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, his song, "Just a Friend" was featured in <a href="/wiki/Saints_Row_IV" title="Saints Row IV">Saints Row IV</a>, which included a Pop station 107.77 The Mix FM.</p>
<p>He appeared in the CN show <i><a href="/wiki/Mad_(TV_series)" title="Mad (TV series)">MAD</a></i>, as the <a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit:_An_Unexpected_Journey" title="The Hobbit: An Unexpected Journey">Hip Hop Hobbit</a>.</p>
<p>He voiced rapper Rhymez and his DJ, Tiny Timmy Scratch It, on <i><a href="/wiki/Randy_Cunningham:_9th_Grade_Ninja" title="Randy Cunningham: 9th Grade Ninja">Randy Cunningham: 9th Grade Ninja</a></i> episode "Hip Hopocalypse Now".</p>
<p>He guest starred in the <i><a href="/wiki/SpongeBob_SquarePants" title="SpongeBob SquarePants">SpongeBob SquarePants</a></i> episode "Kenny the Cat," in the episode's title role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> His voice acting work also includes the voice of Snorlock the Beatboxing Slug in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Adventure_Time" title="Adventure Time">Adventure Time</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, he appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Syfy" title="Syfy">Syfy</a> network movie <i><a href="/wiki/Sharknado_2:_The_Second_One" title="Sharknado 2: The Second One">Sharknado 2: The Second One</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> as Vinnie the pizza shop owner.</p>
<p>Also in 2014, he threw a ceremonial first pitch for an <a href="/wiki/Oakland_Athletics" title="Oakland Athletics">Oakland Athletics</a> <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> game.</p>
<p>In 2016, his song, "Just a Friend" was featured in the <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> Series <i><a href="/wiki/Love_(TV_series)" title="Love (TV series)">Love</a></i> as an ending theme for episode 4. He also makes an appearance in a song titled "The Noisy Eater" off the critically acclaimed album <i>Wildflower</i> by The Avalanches.</p>
