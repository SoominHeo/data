<p><b>Bryon Anthony McCane II</b> (born September 12, 1976),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whitburn_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Whitburn-1">[1]</a></sup> better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Bizzy Bone</b>, a.k.a. <b>Li'l Ripsta</b>, is an American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper">rapper</a> and the youngest member of the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland" title="Cleveland">Cleveland</a> rap group <a href="/wiki/Bone_Thugs-n-Harmony" title="Bone Thugs-n-Harmony">Bone Thugs-n-Harmony</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bizzy_Bone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bryon Anthony McCane II was born in <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">Columbus, Ohio</a>. In 1980, 4-year-old Bryon and his two sisters were abducted and held captive for over two years by his stepfather. Both his sisters were natural children of Bryon's stepfather. The children were put into the backseat of a car and driven off, while their mother was at work. He says that at first, he did not know he had been kidnapped, and was made to believe that his mother and grandmother were dead. During his abduction period, he lived in many homes, apartments, cars and motels, all in Northern Oklahoma. He was often beaten, tortured, and sexually assaulted. In 1983, the "family" had been living on the <a href="/wiki/Kaw_people" title="Kaw people">Kaw Indian Reservation</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kaw_City,_Oklahoma" title="Kaw City, Oklahoma">Kaw, Oklahoma</a> for about a year-and-a-half. A babysitter saw his photo at the end of the made-for-TV movie "Adam" about the producer and creator of America's Most Wanted, John Walsh's son abduction, and called the police, resulting in his return to his family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvprofile_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtvprofile-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>When he was thirteen, Bryon went to live with his oldest sister, at her home near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cleveland,_Ohio" title="Cleveland, Ohio">Cleveland, Ohio</a>. During this period he met <a href="/wiki/Layzie_Bone" title="Layzie Bone">Layzie Bone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Krayzie_Bone" title="Krayzie Bone">Krayzie Bone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wish_Bone" title="Wish Bone">Wish Bone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flesh-n-Bone" title="Flesh-n-Bone">Flesh-n-Bone</a>. Bizzy Bone started in hip hop as a founding member of the group Bone Enterprise, which was formed in 1992. This Cleveland-based crew first got the ear of <a href="/wiki/N.W.A" title="N.W.A">N.W.A</a> founder <a href="/wiki/Eazy-E" title="Eazy-E">Eazy-E</a> who bestowed the moniker Thugs-n-Harmony on the group. In 2002, he appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">FOX</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Most_Wanted" title="America's Most Wanted">America's Most Wanted</a></i> (hosted by <a href="/wiki/John_Walsh_(television_host)" title="John Walsh (television host)">John Walsh</a>, Adam's father) <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-new.music.yahoo.com_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-new.music.yahoo.com-3">[3]</a></sup> and he revealed his abusive childhood and molestation. Bryon also wrote and performed a song on the show entitled "A.M.W." in which he thanks Walsh and encourages abused children to come forward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2003, he returned to Kaw City to see his former neighbors, and meet the man who helped save him. He says that although he has several bad memories here, he still considers Kaw City his hometown.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bizzy_Bone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bizzy Bone started his solo career in 1998. He is best known for his style of combining rapid fire raps, double harmony choruses, with a soft-spoken and high-pitched delivery. His solo debut, <i><a href="/wiki/Heaven%27z_Movie" title="Heaven'z Movie">Heaven'z Movie</a>,</i> was critically acclaimed and certified Platinum by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA">RIAA</a>.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Gift_(Bizzy_Bone_album)" title="The Gift (Bizzy Bone album)">The Gift</a></i>, released on April 7, 2001, peaked at #2 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">US Billboard Independent Albums</a> chart. Many of the songs on the album dealt with his personal life and childhood.</p>
<p>In 2004, Bizzy released <i><a href="/wiki/Alpha_and_Omega_(Bizzy_Bone_album)" title="Alpha and Omega (Bizzy Bone album)">Alpha and Omega</a></i> which showed a change in his musical and lyrical content. Fans responded positively to the album. Bizzy followed up with the limited-edition internet album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Beginning_and_the_End_(album)" title="The Beginning and the End (album)">The Beginning and the End (album)</a></i>.</p>
