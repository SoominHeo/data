<p><b>Vichada Department</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>Departamento del Vichada</i></span>, <small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[biˈ tʃaða]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_Colombia" title="Departments of Colombia">department</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Colombia" title="Republic of Colombia">Republic of Colombia</a> in South America. Vichada is located in the eastern plains of Colombia, in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orinoqu%C3%ADa_Region,_Colombia" title="Orinoquía Region, Colombia">Orinoquía Region</a> within the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orinoco_River" title="Orinoco River">Orinoco river basin</a> bordering the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolivarian_Republic_of_Venezuela" title="Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela">Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela</a> to the north and east. To the north the department also borders with <a href="/wiki/Arauca_Department" title="Arauca Department">Arauca Department</a>, to the northwest with <a href="/wiki/Casanare_Department" title="Casanare Department">Casanare Department</a>, to the west with <a href="/wiki/Meta_Department" title="Meta Department">Meta Department</a>, to the southwest narrowly bordering with <a href="/wiki/Guaviare_Department" title="Guaviare Department">Guaviare Department</a> and to the south with <a href="/wiki/Guain%C3%ADa_Department" title="Guainía Department">Guainía Department</a>. The department is the second largest in Colombia and scarcely populated in comparison to other departments.</p>

<p>The department was previously a commissary established in 1913.</p>
<p>The largest town and capital of the department is <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Carre%C3%B1o" title="Puerto Carreño">Puerto Carreño</a> located in extreme northeastern part of the department and bordering Venezuela. the department is subdivided into four <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Colombia" title="Municipalities of Colombia">municipalities</a>; Puerto Carreño, <a href="/wiki/La_Primavera,_Vichada" title="La Primavera, Vichada">La Primavera</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santa_Rosalia,_Vichada" title="Santa Rosalia, Vichada">Santa Rosalía</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cumaribo" title="Cumaribo">Cumaribo</a>. It also contains 46 indigenous reserves and 6 communities.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vichada_Department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The department is located on the eastern plains of Colombia known colloquially as the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Llanos,_Venezuela" title="Los Llanos, Venezuela">Llanos</a></i>. The department limits to the north with the <a href="/wiki/Arauca_Department" title="Arauca Department">Arauca Department</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolivarian_Republic_of_Venezuela" title="Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela">Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela</a>; to the east with Venezuela; to the south with the <a href="/wiki/Guain%C3%ADa_Department" title="Guainía Department">Guainía Department</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guaviare_Department" title="Guaviare Department">Guaviare Department</a>; and to the west with <a href="/wiki/Meta_Department" title="Meta Department">Meta Department</a> and <a href="/wiki/Casanare_Department" title="Casanare Department">Casanare Department</a>.</p>
<p>The department is located to the left margin of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orinoco_River" title="Orinoco River">Orinoco River</a> and the right margin of the <a href="/wiki/Meta_River" title="Meta River">Meta River</a> within the plains of los Llanos. Part of the department lies on the <a href="/wiki/Guiana_Shield" title="Guiana Shield">Guiana Shield</a>. Soil lacks sediments due to the lack of alluvions from rivers coming from the <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a> mountains.</p>
<p>Climate in the department is predominantly hot and humid with an average temperature of 28 °C (82 °F) throughout the year.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vichada_Department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1913 the government of Colombia created the commissary of Vichada and the capital was established in a locality known as <i>El Picacho</i> between the <a href="/wiki/Meta_River" title="Meta River">Meta</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orinoco_River" title="Orinoco River">Orinoco</a> rivers. The town was named <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Carre%C3%B1o" title="Puerto Carreño">Puerto Carreño</a> in honor of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pedro_Mar%C3%ADa_Carre%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pedro María Carreño (page does not exist)">Pedro María Carreño</a>, then acting Minister of Government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 1974, Puerto Carreño was elevated to the category of <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Colombia" title="Municipalities of Colombia">municipality</a> by decree 1594 of that same year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 5, 1991 Vichada was elevated to the category of <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_Colombia" title="Departments of Colombia">department</a> and Puerto Carreño as its capital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
