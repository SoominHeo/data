<p><i>For the sport called "beach paddleball", see <a href="/wiki/Matkot" title="Matkot">Matkot</a>. For other sports called "paddleball", see <a href="/wiki/Paddle_ball" title="Paddle ball">Paddleball (sport)</a>.</i></p>
<p><b>Beach tennis</b> is a game combining elements of <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volleyball" title="Volleyball">volleyball</a> and played on a beach.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Forms">Forms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beach_tennis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Forms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Beach_Tennis_USA">Beach Tennis USA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beach_tennis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Beach Tennis USA">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Beach tennis was formalized in 2005 in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Altheim&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Marc Altheim (page does not exist)">Marc Altheim</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ITF_Partners_with_Beach_Tennis_USA_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ITF_Partners_with_Beach_Tennis_USA-1">[1]</a></sup> He discovered beach tennis on a trip to <a href="/wiki/Aruba" title="Aruba">Aruba</a> in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ITF_Partners_with_Beach_Tennis_USA_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ITF_Partners_with_Beach_Tennis_USA-1">[1]</a></sup> The sport had been played there since 2000,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ITF_Partners_with_Beach_Tennis_USA_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-ITF_Partners_with_Beach_Tennis_USA-1">[1]</a></sup> having been introduced by a Dutchman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Start_a_racket_in_the_sand_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Start_a_racket_in_the_sand-2">[2]</a></sup> As of 2007, beach tennis had made progress toward acceptance as a mainstream sport with an official standards organization known as Beach Tennis USA (BT USA). In 2007, BT USA signed two television deals: one with SNY in New York City and one with the <a href="/wiki/Tennis_Channel" title="Tennis Channel">Tennis Channel</a>. The Tennis Channel agreed to televise three major BTUSA or National Beach Tennis / Beach Paddle Ball Association tournaments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Start_a_racket_in_the_sand_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Start_a_racket_in_the_sand-2">[2]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miami,_Florida" title="Miami, Florida">Miami</a> BT USA open featured 40 teams, including several formerly highly ranked pro tennis players, including <a href="/wiki/Jay_Berger" title="Jay Berger">Jay Berger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pablo_Arraya" title="Pablo Arraya">Pablo Arraya</a>. In 2007, <a href="/wiki/Andre_Agassi" title="Andre Agassi">Andre Agassi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steffi_Graf" title="Steffi Graf">Steffi Graf</a> played the sport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Start_a_racket_in_the_sand_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Start_a_racket_in_the_sand-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Beach tennis merges the world of beach volleyball and tennis. It is related to <a href="/wiki/Beach_volleyball" title="Beach volleyball">beach volleyball</a> but played with a tennis ball and paddle or racket. The court is a standard beach volleyball court that is 30 feet wide and 60 feet long. There is a center line that splits the court lengthwise. At the center of the court is a 5-foot-10-inch-high (1.78 m) net. A standard <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tennis_racquet#Tennis" title="Tennis racquet">tennis racquet</a> or a paddle and a slightly depressurized <a href="/wiki/Tennis_ball" title="Tennis ball">tennis ball</a> (6 oz instead of 12 oz) are used.</p>
<p>Sanctioned Beach Tennis USA events are different depending upon the number of entrants, number of courts available and time. Typically, the preliminary rounds are round-robin, or pool-play. This means that there is a pool of four, and one would play all the other teams in the pool (three matches). The top one or two teams from each pool advance to the elimination rounds (single-elimination).</p>
<p>The rules are a mix of tennis and volleyball rules. Ball that hit the sand result in a point. Scoring is similar to tennis with scores of 0 - 15 - 30 - 40 and no-ad at deuce. There is only one contact per side. Balls that hit the net remain in play. At deuce, the receiving team chooses which player will receive.</p>
<p>In BT USA-sanctioned events, each match consists of one eight-game set. The match must be won by two games. If the match score is tied at 8–8, a 12-point tennis tiebreaker is played to determine the winner.</p>
<p>Beach tennis was one of the attractions at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Family_Circle_Cup" title="Family Circle Cup">Family Circle Cup</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Charleston,_South_Carolina" title="Charleston, South Carolina">Charleston</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a>, on April 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Serve.27s_Up.21_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Serve.27s_Up.21-3">[3]</a></sup> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Beach_Tennis_USA&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Beach Tennis USA (page does not exist)">Beach Tennis USA</a> organized its first professional tournament there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Serve.27s_Up.21_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Serve.27s_Up.21-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The BT USA 2008 tour commenced in Key Biscayne, Florida on the grounds of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Ericcson_Open" title="Sony Ericcson Open">Sony Ericcson Open</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Islander_News_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Islander_News-4">[4]</a></sup> The 2008 season saw tournaments held nationwide, and a network of events under the BT USA banner will help foster the growth of the sport, which has increased ever since.</p>
<p>In 2008 the exposure to the sport of beach tennis increased, with new broadcast agreements with Fox Sports Net (FSN) in California and Florida and from coverage on TV shows like NBC's <i>Today Show</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Start_a_racket_in_the_sand_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Start_a_racket_in_the_sand-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Beach_tennis_with_paddle_racquet">Beach tennis with paddle racquet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beach_tennis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Beach tennis with paddle racquet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In other countries beach tennis is called <i>beach paddle ball</i> or nation-specific names like <i><a href="/wiki/Matkot" title="Matkot">matkot</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> and <i>eacchettoni</i> in Italy. It was first played in <a href="/wiki/Romagna" title="Romagna">Romagna</a> near <a href="/wiki/Ravenna" title="Ravenna">Ravenna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rimini" title="Rimini">Rimini</a> in 1978.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Islander_News_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_Islander_News-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1996, it was formalized by Italian Gianni Bellettini,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beach_tennis.2C_anyone.3F_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Beach_tennis.2C_anyone.3F-5">[5]</a></sup> president of International Federation Beach Tennis-IFBT.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beach_tennis.2C_anyone.3F_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Beach_tennis.2C_anyone.3F-5">[5]</a></sup> The best players in the world are from Ravenna Italy: Alex Mingozzi and Matteo Marighella, two-time USA National Champions and four-time world champions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beach_tennis.2C_anyone.3F_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Beach_tennis.2C_anyone.3F-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On a trip to the USA by Paul Mapley in 2008, he and Marc Altheim metin a restaurant in SOHO, New York for lunch. Paul urged Marc to put a US team together and travel to Ravenna and contest the World Championships of IFBT Beach Tennis.</p>
<p>Marc was at first reluctant at first as he had already spent so much time on the development of the racquet game in the US that he had imported from Aruba. Jim Lorenzo, Alex Querna and a bunch of young American sporting pioneers boarded a plane for Italy to have a crack at the Italians, who were undoubtedly were the world leaders in the paddle style of the sport.</p>
<p>Jim's boys and girls also packed their tennis rackets as they considered themselves the world champs of the string game; however they were sadly mistaken as they met an awesome contingent of Italians, who were also most skilled at that form. They too had met their match in both disciplines of the game, but like everyone before them had a great respect for the Italians and a great love of this form of beach tennis. At this time Alex Mingozzi, Matteo Marighella, Alan Maldini and Nicola Gambi were considered the pinnacle of the sport, along with an exciting bunch of female players who were amazing to watch. These players did not necessarily have a tennis background, but their skill with the paddle was second to none.</p>
