<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Area">Area</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bicolanos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Area">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Peoples_of_the_Philippines_en.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2863" data-file-width="1846" height="341" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Peoples_of_the_Philippines_en.svg/220px-Peoples_of_the_Philippines_en.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Peoples_of_the_Philippines_en.svg/330px-Peoples_of_the_Philippines_en.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Peoples_of_the_Philippines_en.svg/440px-Peoples_of_the_Philippines_en.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Peoples_of_the_Philippines_en.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The provinces where Bicolanos are predominant are coloured violet.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Bicolanos live in the southeastern peninsula of <a href="/wiki/Luzon" title="Luzon">Luzon</a>, now containing the provinces of <a href="/wiki/Albay" title="Albay">Albay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Camarines_Sur" title="Camarines Sur">Camarines Sur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Camarines_Norte" title="Camarines Norte">Camarines Norte</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catanduanes" title="Catanduanes">Catanduanes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sorsogon" title="Sorsogon">Sorsogon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Masbate" title="Masbate">Masbate</a> (although the majority of Masbate's population are Visayans). Many Bicolanos also live in the province of <a href="/wiki/Quezon" title="Quezon">Quezon</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bicolanos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Bicolanos number about 5,907,000.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> They are descended from the Austronesian-speaking immigrants who came from Southern China during the <a href="/wiki/Iron_Age" title="Iron Age">Iron Age</a>. Many of Bicolanos also have <a href="/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Chinese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arabs" title="Arabs">Arab</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_people" title="Spanish people">Spanish</a> admixtures. Most of the townsfolk have Spanish Mixtures and their language is referred to as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bicol_languages" title="Bicol languages">Bicol</a> or Bicolano. The Bicolano language is very fragmented, and its dialects are mutually incomprehensible to speakers of other Bicolano dialects. The majority of the Bicolano people are devout Roman Catholics due to the Spanish conquering the country and converting them to their religion. Catholic Mass is celebrated daily in many churches in the Bicol Region.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_.26_Traits">Culture &amp; Traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bicolanos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Culture &amp; Traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Bicolano cuisine is primarily noted for the prominent use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chili_peppers" title="Chili peppers">chili peppers</a> and <i>gata</i> (<a href="/wiki/Coconut_milk" title="Coconut milk">coconut milk</a>) in its food. A classic example is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gulay_na_lada" title="Gulay na lada">gulay na lada</a>, known outside the region as <a href="/wiki/Bicol_Express" title="Bicol Express">Bicol Express</a>, a well-loved dish using <i>siling labuyo</i> (native small chillies) and the aforementioned <i>gata</i>.</p>
<p>Like their other neighboring regions, Bicolanas are also expected to lend a hand in household work. They are even anticipated to offer assistance after being married. On the other hand, Bicolano men are expected to assume the role of becoming the primary source of income and financial support of his family.</p>
