<p><b>Vicky Lee McClure</b> (born 8 May 1983) is an English actress best known for her work in the films of director <a href="/wiki/Shane_Meadows" title="Shane Meadows">Shane Meadows</a>. She played the title character's sister Ladine in <i><a href="/wiki/A_Room_for_Romeo_Brass" title="A Room for Romeo Brass">A Room for Romeo Brass</a></i> (1999), and featured in Meadows's successful project and spin offs, <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_England" title="This Is England">This Is England</a></i> (2006), <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/This_is_England_%2786" title="This is England '86">This is England '86</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_England_%2788" title="This Is England '88">This Is England '88</a></i> (2011) and <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_England_%2790" title="This Is England '90">This Is England '90</a></i> (2015).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph-ThisIsEngland-2015_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-ThisIsEngland-2015-1">[1]</a></sup> She is also known for roles in the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Line_of_Duty" title="Line of Duty">Line of Duty</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Broadchurch" title="Broadchurch">Broadchurch</a></i>. Her other film roles include <i><a href="/wiki/Filth_and_Wisdom" title="Filth and Wisdom">Filth and Wisdom</a></i> (2008), <i><a href="/wiki/Hummingbird_(film)" title="Hummingbird (film)">Hummingbird</a></i> (2013), and <i><a href="/wiki/Svengali_(2013_film)" title="Svengali (2013 film)">Svengali</a></i> (2013).</p>
<p>McClure won the <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_Television_Award_for_Best_Actress" title="British Academy Television Award for Best Actress">BAFTA Television Award for Best Actress</a> for <i>This is England '86</i> in 2011.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vicky_McClure&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McClure was born in <a href="/wiki/Wollaton" title="Wollaton">Wollaton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nottingham" title="Nottingham">Nottingham</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph-ThisIsEngland-2015_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-ThisIsEngland-2015-1">[1]</a></sup> and educated at the Fernwood Comprehensive School. She also attended the <a href="/wiki/Central_Junior_Television_Workshop" title="Central Junior Television Workshop">Central Junior Television Workshop</a> where she was spotted by fellow Nottingham-born actress <a href="/wiki/Samantha_Morton" title="Samantha Morton">Samantha Morton</a> who helped launch her career.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vicky_McClure&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McClure has worked on the <i>This is England</i> films for a period of over 9 years, portraying the role of Frances Lorraine "Lol" Jenkins. In an April 2007 interview with <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Time_Out_(company)" title="Time Out (company)">Time Out</a></i>, McClure spoke of Meadows approaching her for the role while she was in a pub with <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Shim" title="Andrew Shim">Andrew Shim</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TO260407_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-TO260407-2">[2]</a></sup> She described the making of the film, which was highly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Improvised" title="Improvised">improvised</a>, as "constant laughing and jokes". In 2011, her performance as Frances Lorraine "Lol" Jenkins in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/This_is_England_%2786" title="This is England '86">This is England '86</a></i> won her the <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_Television_Award_for_Best_Actress" title="British Academy Television Award for Best Actress">British Academy Television Award for Best Actress</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RTS_Awards" title="RTS Awards">Royal Television Society Award for Best Actress</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph-ThisIsEngland-2015_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-ThisIsEngland-2015-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>McClure co-starred in the London-based comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Filth_and_Wisdom" title="Filth and Wisdom">Filth and Wisdom</a></i>, the first feature film directed by pop singer <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TimesOL140208_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-TimesOL140208-3">[3]</a></sup> She admitted to being slightly star-struck upon first meeting Madonna, "I tried to act as cool as possible but inside I'm like, "Oh my God; there's Madonna!" You can't help it".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The film premiered at the <a href="/wiki/Berlin_International_Film_Festival" title="Berlin International Film Festival">Berlin International Film Festival</a> on 13 February 2008.</p>
<p>She also joined the cast of the British <a href="/wiki/Police_procedural" title="Police procedural">police procedural</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a>, <a href="/wiki/Line_of_Duty" title="Line of Duty">Line of Duty</a>, in 2012 as DC Kate Fleming, continuing in the role in Series 2 (2014) and Series 3 (2016). In 2013, she appeared in the British action-thriller film <i><a href="/wiki/Hummingbird_(film)" title="Hummingbird (film)">Hummingbird</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Jason_Statham" title="Jason Statham">Jason Statham</a>.</p>
<p>In July 2016, she appeared as Winnie Verloc in <i>The Secret Agent</i>, based on the <a href="/wiki/The_Secret_Agent" title="The Secret Agent">eponymous novel</a> by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Conrad" title="Joseph Conrad">Joseph Conrad</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_and_other_work">Video and other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vicky_McClure&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Video and other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>She was also featured alongside <a href="/wiki/Kaya_Scodelario" title="Kaya Scodelario">Kaya Scodelario</a>, Abbey Butler, <a href="/wiki/Andy_Crane" title="Andy Crane">Andy Crane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Young" title="Paul Young">Paul Young</a> in <a href="/wiki/Plan_B_(musician)" title="Plan B (musician)">Plan B</a>'s music video "<a href="/wiki/She_Said" title="She Said">She Said</a>". In 2010, McClure appeared in a number of promotional short films for the English cosmetics brand Illamasqua.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Illamasqua_promotional_short_films_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Illamasqua_promotional_short_films-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2012, McClure appeared in the video for <a href="/wiki/Jake_Bugg" title="Jake Bugg">Jake Bugg</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/Two_Fingers_(song)" title="Two Fingers (song)">Two Fingers</a>" along with <i>Line of Duty</i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Craig_Parkinson" title="Craig Parkinson">Craig Parkinson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On 18 March 2014, McClure appeared on BBC Radio 1's Innuendo Bingo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
