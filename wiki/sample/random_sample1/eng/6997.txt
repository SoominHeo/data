<p><b>Vitanje</b> (<small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Slovene" title="Help:IPA for Slovene">[ˈʋiːtanjɛ]</a></span>) is a small town and a <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipality</a> in northeastern <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>. It lies on the <a href="/wiki/Hudinja_(river)" title="Hudinja (river)">Hudinja River</a> north of <a href="/wiki/Celje" title="Celje">Celje</a>. Traditionally the area was part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lower_Styria" title="Lower Styria">Styria</a> region. The municipality is now included in the <a href="/wiki/Savinja_Statistical_Region" title="Savinja Statistical Region">Savinja Statistical Region</a>. From the 11th century onwards it became a regional centre and was granted <a href="/wiki/Market_town" title="Market town">market rights</a> in 1306.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Parish_church" title="Parish church">parish church</a> in the settlement is dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Feast_of_Saints_Peter_and_Paul" title="Feast of Saints Peter and Paul">Saints Peter and Paul</a> and belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Celje" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Celje">Roman Catholic Diocese of Celje</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Nave" title="Nave">nave</a> dates to the early 13th century with 14th-century <a href="/wiki/Fresco" title="Fresco">frescos</a>, a 15th-century <a href="/wiki/Sanctuary" title="Sanctuary">sanctuary</a>, and 18th-century side <a href="/wiki/Chapel" title="Chapel">chapels</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> A second church in the settlement is dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Mary_Help_of_Christians" title="Mary Help of Christians">Mary Help of Christians</a>. It was built between 1747 and 1754 and its <a href="/wiki/Spire" title="Spire">spire</a> heightened in 1812.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, the <a href="/wiki/Cultural_Centre_of_European_Space_Technologies" title="Cultural Centre of European Space Technologies">Cultural Centre of European Space Technologies (KSEVT)</a> was built in Vitanje. The building is on the one hand the main cultural venue of Vitanje and on the other hand an institute for composite research of art and science in space. KSEVT Institute organizes research activities, exhibitions, conferences, symposia and educational workshops.</p>
