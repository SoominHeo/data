<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Flag of Indiana</h1>

<p>The <b>flag of Indiana</b> was designed by Paul Hadley and officially adopted by the state of <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a> on May 31, 1917. It was the state's first official <a href="/wiki/Flag" title="Flag">flag</a> and has remained unchanged since then except for the creation of a statute to standardize the production of the flag.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Indiana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Indiana_Flag_(1903).png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="719" data-file-width="465" height="340" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Indiana_Flag_%281903%29.png/220px-Indiana_Flag_%281903%29.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Indiana_Flag_%281903%29.png/330px-Indiana_Flag_%281903%29.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Indiana_Flag_%281903%29.png/440px-Indiana_Flag_%281903%29.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Indiana_Flag_(1903).png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Previous State Flag, utilizing the <a href="/wiki/Seal_of_Indiana" title="Seal of Indiana">Seal of Indiana</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>To commemorate the stateâs 1916 <a href="/wiki/Centennial" title="Centennial">centennial anniversary</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_General_Assembly" title="Indiana General Assembly">Indiana General Assembly</a> issued a resolution to adopt a state flag. At the request of the General Assembly, a contest was sponsored by the Indiana Society of the <a href="/wiki/Daughters_of_the_American_Revolution" title="Daughters of the American Revolution">Daughters of the American Revolution</a> to design a flag to serve as the official state banner. As an incentive to increase the number of submissions, the contest offered the winner a one hundred dollar cash prize. More than two hundred submissions were received and examined by the Society before a winner was selected. The entry created by Paul Hadley of <a href="/wiki/Mooresville,_Indiana" title="Mooresville, Indiana">Mooresville, Indiana</a>, was ultimately chosen as the winner of the contest and the cash prize.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ihb_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ihb-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 31, 1917, the flag was chosen as the state's official banner. The General Assembly made only one change to Hadley's original design: they added the word <i>Indiana</i>, in a crescent shape, over the top of the torch. The state <i>banner</i> was later renamed the state's <i>flag</i> in a new statute passed in 1955 that also standardized the dimensions of the flag.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ihb_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ihb-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Iconography">Iconography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Indiana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Iconography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The flag consists of a gold <a href="/wiki/Torch" title="Torch">torch</a> that represents <a href="/wiki/Liberty" title="Liberty">liberty</a> and <a href="/wiki/Enlightenment_in_Western_secular_tradition" title="Enlightenment in Western secular tradition">enlightenment</a>; the rays around the torch represent their far-reaching influence. The nineteen stars represent Indiana's place as the nineteenth state to join the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The thirteen stars in the outer loop symbolize the original <a href="/wiki/Thirteen_Colonies" title="Thirteen Colonies">Thirteen Colonies</a>, the five inner stars represent the next five states added to the Union, and the one large star above the torch represents Indiana.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ihb_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-ihb-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ic_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ic-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2001, a survey conducted by the <a href="/wiki/North_American_Vexillological_Association" title="North American Vexillological Association">North American Vexillological Association</a> (NAVA) placed Indiana's flag 32nd in design quality out of the 72 Canadian provincial, U.S. state and U.S. territorial flags ranked.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Statute">Statute</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Indiana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Statute">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:IndianaWorldWarMemorial_04.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="225" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/IndianaWorldWarMemorial_04.JPG/300px-IndianaWorldWarMemorial_04.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/IndianaWorldWarMemorial_04.JPG/450px-IndianaWorldWarMemorial_04.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/IndianaWorldWarMemorial_04.JPG/600px-IndianaWorldWarMemorial_04.JPG 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:IndianaWorldWarMemorial_04.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Indiana State Flag flying at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indiana_World_War_Memorial" title="Indiana World War Memorial">Indiana World War Memorial</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The current statute that governs the design of the state flag states:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>The flag's dimensions shall be three feet fly by two feet hoist; or five feet fly by three feet hoist; or any size proportionate to either of those dimensions. The field of the flag shall be blue with nineteen stars and a flaming torch in gold or buff. Thirteen stars shall be arranged in an outer circle, representing the original thirteen states; five stars shall be arranged in a half circle below the torch and inside the outer circle of stars, representing the states admitted prior to Indiana; and the nineteenth star, appreciably larger than the others and representing Indiana shall be placed above the flame of the torch. The outer circle of stars shall be so arranged that one star shall appear directly in the middle at the top of the circle, and the word "Indiana" shall be placed in a half circle over and above the star representing Indiana and midway between it and the star in the center above it. Rays shall be shown radiating from the torch to the three stars on each side of the star in the upper center of the circle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ic_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-ic-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage">Usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Indiana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Several other laws govern the use of the state flag. The flag is required to be flown by all <a href="/wiki/Indiana_Guard_Reserve" title="Indiana Guard Reserve">state militias</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_National_Guard" title="Indiana National Guard">Indiana National Guard</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It is to be on display at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indiana_Statehouse" title="Indiana Statehouse">Indiana Statehouse</a> at all times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The flag must also be displayed at any agency that is funded in part or in full by the state government, including public schools, state universities, and state parks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In all other respects the flag should be treated with the same care and respect as the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_United_States" title="Flag of the United States">flag of the United States</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late 2008, <a href="/wiki/Tribune_Media" title="Tribune Media">Tribune Media</a> station <a href="/wiki/WTTV" title="WTTV">WTTV</a> in <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis" title="Indianapolis">Indianapolis</a> began to use the flag's design as part of their station's logo until acquiring <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> affiliation in January 2015.</p>
<p>A variation of the Indiana state flag was used as the Gotham flag in the 1989 movie <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_(1989_film)" title="Batman (1989 film)">Batman</a></i>. The flag can be seen in the mayor's office.</p>
<p>The logo of the <a href="/wiki/BP" title="BP">BP</a>-acquired <a href="/wiki/Amoco" title="Amoco">Amoco Corporation</a> (formerly Standard Oil of Indiana) prominently features a torch to commemorate the company's Indiana origins; it remains in use at the few BP stations using Amoco and Standard <a href="/wiki/Trade_dress" title="Trade dress">trade dress</a> to maintain trademark protection.</p>
