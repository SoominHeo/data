<p><b>Vitali Vladimirovich Kutuzov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Belarusian_language" title="Belarusian language">Belarusian</a>: <span lang="be">Віталь Кутузаў</span>, born 20 March 1980 in <a href="/wiki/Pinsk" title="Pinsk">Pinsk</a>) is a retired <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarusian</a> <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">football striker</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vitali_Kutuzov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2001 he joined <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Italian</a> side <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">A.C. Milan</a> from <a href="/wiki/FC_BATE_Borisov" title="FC BATE Borisov">BATE Borisov</a>, Belarus. During the half of the season he spent with the team he failed to establish himself in the first team, playing only two games.</p>
<p>For 2002/03 football season he was loaned to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portuguese_Liga" title="Portuguese Liga">Portuguese</a> side <a href="/wiki/Sporting_Clube_de_Portugal" title="Sporting Clube de Portugal">Sporting Lisbon</a>. He played 30 games for them and scored 7 goals, including games in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> and the domestic cup.</p>
<p>For the following, 2003/04 season he was again loaned, this time to the Italian <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> side <a href="/wiki/U.S._Avellino_1912" title="U.S. Avellino 1912">Avellino</a>. Kutuzov established himself as one of the most important players of the side and a fan-favourite. He played 43 games, scoring 15 goals. Despite his efforts, the team was relegated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C1" title="Serie C1">Serie C1</a>.</p>
<p>After his successful season in Serie B, he was noticed by the Serie A side <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">U.C. Sampdoria</a>. The club bought half of the rights for the footballer from <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">A.C. Milan</a> in 2004 for €1.5 million<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and signed him until 2008. In the 2004/05 season he played in 32 games (14 times in starting line-up, totaling 1,604 minutes) and scored 4 goals.</p>
<p>On 20 June 2006, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Parma" title="F.C. Parma">F.C. Parma</a> bought the full rights on Kutuzov from Sampdoria (and in earlier Sampdoria bought Milan's half for €1 million). He signed a three-year contract with them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He was loaned to newly promoted Serie B side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pisa_Calcio" title="Pisa Calcio">Pisa</a> on July 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He returned to Parma during the summer of 2008.</p>
<p>In January 2009, he left for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.S._Bari" title="A.S. Bari">A.S. Bari</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vitali_Kutuzov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kutuzov has over 50 caps for the <a href="/wiki/Belarus_national_football_team" title="Belarus national football team">Belarus national football team</a>. On 17 April 2002, he made his debut for the national side, contributing 2 goals in the 5:2 away win over <a href="/wiki/Hungary_national_football_team" title="Hungary national football team">Hungary</a> in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friendly_match" title="Friendly match">friendly match</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
