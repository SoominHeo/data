<p><b>Vítor Manuel de Oliveira Lopes Pereira</b> (born 26 July 1968) is a Portuguese retired <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> and current manager of German club <a href="/wiki/TSV_1860_Munich" title="TSV 1860 Munich">1860 Munich</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Football_career">Football career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=V%C3%ADtor_Pereira_(football_manager)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Football career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Espinho,_Portugal" title="Espinho, Portugal">Espinho</a>, Pereira only played amateur football, and retired at the age of 28. He started managing on junior level, and his first head coach experience arrived midway through the 2004–05 season, when he was appointed at <a href="/wiki/A.D._Sanjoanense" title="A.D. Sanjoanense">A.D. Sanjoanense</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Second_Division" title="Portuguese Second Division">third division</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Subsequently, Pereira worked with <a href="/wiki/S.C._Espinho" title="S.C. Espinho">S.C. Espinho</a> in the same level,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> being fired with ten games left in his second campaign and returning to <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">FC Porto</a>'s juniors for a further season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2008 he returned to head coaching again, with <a href="/wiki/C.D._Santa_Clara" title="C.D. Santa Clara">C.D. Santa Clara</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Segunda_Liga" title="Segunda Liga">division two</a>, leading the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a> side to the third position in <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Liga_de_Honra" title="2008–09 Liga de Honra">his first year</a> and the fourth in <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Liga_de_Honra" title="2009–10 Liga de Honra">the second</a>, on both occasions narrowly missing out on <a href="/wiki/Primeira_Liga" title="Primeira Liga">Primeira Liga</a> promotion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the summer of 2010, Pereira left Santa Clara to become assistant manager to <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Villas-Boas" title="André Villas-Boas">André Villas-Boas</a> at Porto.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On 21 June 2011, following the head coach's departure to <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a>, he was promoted to first-team manager,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> winning his first official match – and title – against <a href="/wiki/Vit%C3%B3ria_S.C." title="Vitória S.C.">Vitória de Guimarães</a>, for <a href="/wiki/2011_Superta%C3%A7a_C%C3%A2ndido_de_Oliveira" title="2011 Supertaça Cândido de Oliveira">the season</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Superta%C3%A7a_C%C3%A2ndido_de_Oliveira" title="Supertaça Cândido de Oliveira">Portuguese Supercup</a>.</p>
<p>Despite a less than stellar performance both in <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">Europe</a> – with Porto being knocked out of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2011–12 UEFA Champions League">group stage</a> and in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2011–12 UEFA Europa League">round-of-32</a> – and in the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Ta%C3%A7a_de_Portugal" title="2011–12 Taça de Portugal">Portuguese Cup</a>, Pereira lead the club to the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Primeira_Liga" title="2011–12 Primeira Liga">league title</a> in his first season in charge.</p>
<p>In March 2013, following the team's <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2012–13 UEFA Champions League">elimination from Champions League contention</a> (1–2 on aggregate against <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1laga_CF" title="Málaga CF">Málaga CF</a>), Pereira came under heavy criticism, notably due to his decision of benching <a href="/wiki/James_Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="James Rodríguez">James Rodríguez</a> during the first half on both games. The <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombian</a> stated his disappointment in the coach's decision, but said that he respected him and trusted his reasons, adding that the situation was possibly created because of his questionable fitness, even though he claimed to be 100% fit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In early May, as Porto ranked second in the league, Pereira deemed the Portuguese league as a "dirty competition".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Only a few days later, after his team defeated <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">S.L. Benfica</a> at home to surpass its opponents – eventually winning <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Primeira_Liga" title="2012–13 Primeira Liga">the league title</a>, conceding six draws in 30 games –<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> he considered it to be a "highly competitive and prestigious league".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late May 2013, Pereira was interviewed for the vacant job at <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> but eventually signed a two-year deal with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al-Ahli_SC_(Jeddah)" title="Al-Ahli SC (Jeddah)">Al-Ahli SC (Jeddah)</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Professional_League" title="Saudi Professional League">Saudi Professional League</a>. On 7 January 2015 he moved clubs and countries again, replacing fired <a href="/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer)" title="Míchel (footballer)">Míchel</a> at the helm of <a href="/wiki/Olympiacos_F.C." title="Olympiacos F.C.">Olympiacos F.C.</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and eventually winning <a href="/wiki/Double_(association_football)" title="Double (association football)">the double</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IND_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-IND-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 10 June 2015, Olympiacos announced a mutual contract termination with Pereira.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The following day, he was appointed at <a href="/wiki/Fenerbah%C3%A7e_S.K._(football)" title="Fenerbahçe S.K. (football)">Fenerbahçe SK</a> for two years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Turkish club cut ties unilaterally with Pereira on 15 August 2016, with the case being subsequently taken to the <a href="/wiki/Court_of_Arbitration_for_Sport" title="Court of Arbitration for Sport">Court of Arbitration for Sport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On 18 December he was named head coach of <a href="/wiki/TSV_1860_Munich" title="TSV 1860 Munich">TSV 1860 Munich</a> in the German <a href="/wiki/2._Bundesliga" title="2. Bundesliga">2. Bundesliga</a>, with the one-and-a-half-year deal being made effective the following 1 January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
