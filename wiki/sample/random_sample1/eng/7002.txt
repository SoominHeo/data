<p><b>Vittorio Miele</b> (November 24, 1926, <a href="/wiki/Cassino" title="Cassino">Cassino</a> – November 18, 1999, <a href="/wiki/Cassino" title="Cassino">Cassino</a>) was an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Painting" title="Painting">painter</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vittorio_Miele&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Vittorio_Miele,_Carabinieri.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="720" data-file-width="537" height="295" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Vittorio_Miele%2C_Carabinieri.jpg/220px-Vittorio_Miele%2C_Carabinieri.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Vittorio_Miele%2C_Carabinieri.jpg/330px-Vittorio_Miele%2C_Carabinieri.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Vittorio_Miele%2C_Carabinieri.jpg/440px-Vittorio_Miele%2C_Carabinieri.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Vittorio_Miele,_Carabinieri.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Vittorio Miele, Carabinieri</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Vittorio Miele was born in <a href="/wiki/Cassino" title="Cassino">Cassino</a> in 1926 and he lived the tragedy of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Monte_Cassino" title="Battle of Monte Cassino">Battle of Monte Cassino</a> where his father and his little sister died. His mother later died of her injuries.</p>
<p>Vittorio Miele survived and he went to Northern Italy.</p>
<p>In 1958 he participated in the Mantova City National Art Exhibition and in 1966 he had his first one-man exhibition in <a href="/wiki/Frosinone" title="Frosinone">Frosinone</a>.</p>
<p>In 1969 with the painting "Il Dolore" he was awarded the second prize at the International Painting Exhibition in Piervert, in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> and with the painting "Case di Ciociaria" he was awarded the first prize at the Exhibition "Figurative Arts Province" in Rome.</p>
<p>In 1971 the Administration of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_San_Marino" title="Republic of San Marino">Republic of San Marino</a> invited him to hold a one-man exhibition at the Ridotto of the Titano Theatre. In July 1971, thousands of people visited this exhibition.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Vittorio_Miele_-_Olio.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="671" data-file-width="720" height="280" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Vittorio_Miele_-_Olio.jpg/300px-Vittorio_Miele_-_Olio.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Vittorio_Miele_-_Olio.jpg/450px-Vittorio_Miele_-_Olio.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Vittorio_Miele_-_Olio.jpg/600px-Vittorio_Miele_-_Olio.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Vittorio_Miele_-_Olio.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Vittorio Miele, oil painting</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1972 some of his paintings were chosen, together with those of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/De_Chirico" title="De Chirico">De Chirico</a>, Campigli, Gentilini, Cantore and <a href="/wiki/El_Greco" title="El Greco">Greco</a> for a collective exhibition in the "2000" Gallery in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, the only gallery specialized in the presentation and popularisation of contemporary art in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. His international experience enriches after only one year. In June 1973, with "L'ultimo Gradino" and "Passaggio Ciociaro" he is present in the Arts Pavillon in <a href="/wiki/Sarajevo" title="Sarajevo">Sarajevo</a>.</p>
<p>In October, the Provincaial Tourist Organization in Frosinone awarded him the gold medal for "having contributed to the revival of Art in <a href="/wiki/Ciociaria" title="Ciociaria">Ciociaria</a>". In Autumn 1974 was ready for his first trip to <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>. The Canadian press and television present the exhibitions Miele holds in the major Canadian cities. In October he exhibited, with great success, over one hundred paintings in Place Bonaventure in Montreal. In November he held a one-man exhibition in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> at the Hilton Hotel and also participated with other important contemporary artists at the Fine Arts Gallery exhibition.</p>
<p>Vittorio Miele works hard during those two months, even outdoors, trying to physically capture the harsh climate in his paintings. Paintings of particular interest remain from that Canadian period, linked to that fathal symbiosis established between the master's "provincial luggage" and the temerarious clash wit immeasurable perfectionism. The Nordic houses become Ciociaria countryside cottages.</p>
<p>In '70s he was invited to <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>, guest of the State Artist Colony in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Po%C4%8Ditelj" title="Počitelj">Počitelj</a>. There he discovered the freshness of the oriental archaic culture. He began to paint outdoors again, the style become less "harsh", the Bosnian mountains become "rounded" and the shadows are softly superimposed. He discovered a luminosity lost in his years of travelling.</p>
<p>After a lot of other expositions in Italy and in the USA, in 1991 he exposed at the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">European Parliament</a>.</p>
<p>He died in his home in <a href="/wiki/Cassino" title="Cassino">Cassino</a> on 18 November 1999, after many months of <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/lucid" title="wikt:lucid">lucid</a> <a href="/wiki/Pain" title="Pain">agony</a>.</p>
