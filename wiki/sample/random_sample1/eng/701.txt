<p>Year <b>871</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">DCCCLXXI</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Monday" title="Common year starting on Monday">common year starting on Monday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=871&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=871&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=871&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The English retreat onto the <a href="/wiki/Berkshire_Downs" title="Berkshire Downs">Berkshire Downs</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Great_Heathen_Army" title="Great Heathen Army">Great Heathen Army</a>, led by the Danish Viking 'kings' <a href="/wiki/Halfdan_Ragnarsson" title="Halfdan Ragnarsson">Halfdan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bagsecg" title="Bagsecg">Bagsecg</a>, march out after the Saxons. Six pitched battles are fought between the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Demark" title="Demark">Danes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wessex" title="Wessex">Wessex</a>. Of two of them the place and date are not recorded, the others are:
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_4" title="January 4">January 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Reading_(871)" title="Battle of Reading (871)">Battle of Reading</a>: A West Saxon force, under the command of King <a href="/wiki/%C3%86thelred_of_Wessex" title="Æthelred of Wessex">Æthelred I</a> and his brother <a href="/wiki/Alfred_the_Great" title="Alfred the Great">Alfred</a>, is defeated by the Danes at <a href="/wiki/Reading,_Berkshire" title="Reading, Berkshire">Reading</a>. Among the many dead on both sides is <a href="/wiki/%C3%86thelwulf_of_Berkshire" title="Æthelwulf of Berkshire">Æthelwulf</a>. The Saxon troops are forced to retreat, allowing the <a href="/wiki/Vikings" title="Vikings">Vikings</a> to continue their advance into Wessex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_8" title="January 8">January 8</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ashdown" title="Battle of Ashdown">Battle of Ashdown</a>: The West Saxons, led by Æthelred I and Alfred, gather at the Berkshire Downs. The Danes under the command of Halfdan and Bagsecg occupy the high ground, but are successfully attacked by Alfred's men. During the battle Alfred breaches the <a href="/wiki/Shield_wall" title="Shield wall">shield wall</a> formation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_22" title="January 22">January 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Basing" title="Battle of Basing">Battle of Basing</a>: The West Saxon army, under the command of Æthelred I, is defeated at <a href="/wiki/Old_Basing" title="Old Basing">Basing</a>. The Danes, led by Halfdan, are victorious; Æthelred is forced to flee and regroup, leaving behind precious <a href="/wiki/Winter" title="Winter">winter</a> supplies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_22" title="March 22">March 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Marton" title="Battle of Marton">Battle of Meretum</a>: The West Saxons, led by Æthelred I and Alfred, are defeated by the Danes. Among the many dead is <a href="/wiki/Heahmund" title="Heahmund">Heahmund</a>, bishop of <a href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Salisbury" title="Diocese of Salisbury">Salisbury</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>May – <a href="/wiki/Wilton,_Wiltshire#History" title="Wilton, Wiltshire">Battle of Wilton</a>: Alfred the Great is defeated by the Danes at Wilton (along the southern side of the <a href="/wiki/River_Wylye" title="River Wylye">River Wylye</a>), leaving him in retreat for several years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wch_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-wch-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_2" title="February 2">February 2</a> – <a href="/wiki/Francia" title="Francia">Franco</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lombards" title="Lombards">Lombard</a> forces, aided by a <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatian</a> fleet (of <i><a href="/wiki/Sclaveni" title="Sclaveni">Sclaveni</a></i>), led by Emperor <a href="/wiki/Louis_II_of_Italy" title="Louis II of Italy">Louis II</a>, capture <a href="/wiki/Bari" title="Bari">Bari</a>, capital of the <a href="/wiki/Emirate_of_Bari" title="Emirate of Bari">Emirate of Bari</a> in <a href="/wiki/Southern_Italy" title="Southern Italy">Southern Italy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_23" title="April 23">April 23</a> – Alfred succeeds as king of Wessex after Æthelred's death. He makes peace with the Danes, and pays them <a href="/wiki/Danegeld" title="Danegeld">Danegeld</a>, each ruling parts of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.</li>
<li>Alfred makes <a href="/wiki/Winchester" title="Winchester">Winchester</a> his <a href="/wiki/Home" title="Home">residence</a>. The Danish armies colonize areas of north, central and eastern England, which become known as the <a href="/wiki/Danelaw" title="Danelaw">Danelaw</a>.</li>
<li>The Danes sail down the <a href="/wiki/River_Thames" title="River Thames">River Thames</a>, to raid the <a href="/wiki/Mercia" title="Mercia">Mercian</a> port of <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxon_London#Lundenwic" title="Anglo-Saxon London">Lundenwic</a> (in the <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> area). Here, over the winter, they divide their spoils.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Rhodri_the_Great" title="Rhodri the Great">Rhodri <i>Mawr</i></a> ("the Great") of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Gwynedd" title="Kingdom of Gwynedd">Gwynedd</a> annexes <a href="/wiki/Seisyllwg" title="Seisyllwg">Seisyllwg</a>, uniting most of <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> under his rule (approximate date).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/T%C3%B8nsberg" title="Tønsberg">Tønsberg</a>, the oldest surviving town in the <a href="/wiki/Nordic_countries" title="Nordic countries">Nordic countries</a>, is founded.</li>
</ul>
