<p>A <b>squadron</b> in <a href="/wiki/Air_force" title="Air force">air force</a>, <a href="/wiki/Army_aviation" title="Army aviation">army aviation</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Naval_aviation" title="Naval aviation">naval aviation</a> is mainly a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_unit" title="Military unit">unit</a> comprising a number of <a href="/wiki/Military_aircraft" title="Military aircraft">military aircraft</a> and their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aircrews" title="Aircrews">aircrews</a>, usually of the same type, typically with 12 to 24 aircraft, sometimes divided into three or four <a href="/wiki/Flight_(military_unit)" title="Flight (military unit)">flights</a>, depending on aircraft type and air force. Land based squadrons equipped with heavier type aircraft such as long-range bombers, or cargo aircraft, or air refueling tankers have around 12 aircraft as a typical authorization, while most fighter equipped units have an authorized number of 18 to 24.</p>
<p>In most armed forces, two or more squadrons will form a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Group_(air_force_unit)" title="Group (air force unit)">group</a> or a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wing_(air_force_unit)" title="Wing (air force unit)">wing</a>. Some air forces (including the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a>, <a href="/wiki/Royal_Netherlands_Air_Force" title="Royal Netherlands Air Force">Royal Netherlands Air Force</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Air_Component" title="Belgian Air Component">Belgian Air Component</a>, <a href="/wiki/German_Air_Force" title="German Air Force">German Air Force</a>, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Singapore_Air_Force" title="Republic of Singapore Air Force">Republic of Singapore Air Force</a>, and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">United States Air Force</a>) also use the term squadrons for non-flying ground units (e.g., radar squadrons, missile squadrons, aircraft maintenance squadrons, security forces squadrons, civil engineering squadrons, operations management squadron, medical squadrons, etc.).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States_military_air_services">United States military air services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Squadron_(aviation)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: United States military air services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">For more details on U.S. Navy squadrons, see <a href="/wiki/Dictionary_of_American_Naval_Aviation_Squadrons" title="Dictionary of American Naval Aviation Squadrons">Dictionary of American Naval Aviation Squadrons</a>.</div>
<p>In contrast to <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">United States Air Force</a> units where flying squadrons are separate from supporting administrative and aircraft maintenance squadrons, flying squadrons in U.S. Naval Aviation (<a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">United States Marine Corps</a>) typically contain both embedded administrative support functions and organizational level aircraft maintenance functions, plus all their associated personnel as part of the total squadron manning.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps_Aviation" title="United States Marine Corps Aviation">United States Marine Corps Aviation</a> the nomenclature "squadron" is also used to designate all battalion-equivalent, aviation support organizations. These squadrons include: wing headquarters, tactical air command, air control, air support, aviation logistics, wing support, and wing communications squadrons.</p>
<p>Also in contrast to USAF flying squadrons, most tactical sea-based and land-based U.S. Naval Aviation squadrons (USN and USMC), vice training squadrons and test and evaluation squadrons, usually do not have more than 12 aircraft authorized/assigned at any one time. Exceptions are USN helicopter mine countrmeasures squadrons (17 MH-53), USMC composite medium tilt-rotor squadrons (12 MV-22s, 6 AH-1s, 4 CH-53s, 3 UH-1s, and 6 AV-8s), heavy helicopter (16 CH-53s) and light/attack helicopter (18 AH-1s and 9 UH-1s) squadrons, and Marine attack squadrons (16 AV-8s).</p>
<p>Although part of U.S. naval aviation, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a> aviation units are centered on an air station versus a squadron or group/wing organizational structure. The one exception to this is the Coast Guard's Helicopter Interdiction Squadron (HITRON), which is engaged primarily in counter-narcotics (CN) interdiction operations.</p>
<p>In U.S. Army Aviation, flying units may be organized in battalions or squadrons (air cavalry only) reporting to an aviation brigade.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Others">Others</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Squadron_(aviation)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Others">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An <i>escadron</i> is the equivalent unit in France's <a href="/wiki/French_Air_Force" title="French Air Force">Armée de l'Air</a>. It is normally subdivided into <i><a href="/wiki/Escadrille" title="Escadrille">escadrilles</a></i> of eight aircraft.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Air_Training_Corps" title="Air Training Corps">Air Training Corps</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and many <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a> nations, a Squadron is a group of <a href="/wiki/Cadet" title="Cadet">cadets</a> who parade regularly.</p>
<p>In the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Civil_Air_Patrol" title="Civil Air Patrol">Civil Air Patrol</a>, a squadron is the basic administrative unit.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Air_Force" title="Swedish Air Force">Swedish Air Force</a> a helicopter squadron (<i>helikopterskvadron</i>) is a detachment from the "Helicopter Wing" (<i>Helikopterflottiljen</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
