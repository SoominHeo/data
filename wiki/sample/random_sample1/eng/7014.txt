<p><b>Marvic Valentin Castelo Sotto</b> (born April 28, 1954), known as <b>Vic Sotto</b>, is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filipino_people" title="Filipino people">Filipino</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Television_presenter" title="Television presenter">television presenter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Comedian" title="Comedian">comedian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singer-songwriter" title="Singer-songwriter">singer-songwriter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Film_producer" title="Film producer">film producer</a> from the Philippines. Sotto is well-known for his various television and film projects on the major Philippine television networks, <a href="/wiki/GMA_Network" title="GMA Network">GMA Network</a>, <a href="/wiki/TV5_(Philippines)" title="TV5 (Philippines)">TV5</a>, <a href="/wiki/ABS-CBN" title="ABS-CBN">ABS-CBN</a> and <a href="/wiki/Radio_Philippines_Network" title="Radio Philippines Network">RPN</a>. Sotto is one of the three pioneer hosts of <i><a href="/wiki/Eat_Bulaga!" title="Eat Bulaga!">Eat Bulaga!</a></i>, which is the longest running noon-time variety show in the Philippines; Sotto also owns a film and TV production outfit named <a href="/wiki/M-Zet_Productions" title="M-Zet Productions">M-Zet Productions</a>. Sotto won four consecutive titles for Philippine Box Office King from 2004 through 2007 in the Guillermo Mendoza Memorial Scholarship Foundation Awards and received three nominations for Best Actor in the Metro Manila Film Festival Awards.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vic_Sotto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_background">Family background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vic_Sotto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Family background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Marvic Valentin Castelo Sotto was born on April 28, 1954 to Marcelino Ojeda Sotto, Sr. and Dr. Herminia Castelo. He studied at <a href="/wiki/Colegio_de_San_Juan_de_Letran" title="Colegio de San Juan de Letran">Colegio de San Juan de Letran</a>. He is the brother of Marcelino "Maru" Sotto, Jr., <a href="/wiki/Tito_Sotto" title="Tito Sotto">Vicente "Tito" Sotto III</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Val_Sotto" title="Val Sotto">Valmar "Val" Sotto</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vic_Sotto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vic_Sotto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He started his career as a folk singer and guitarist. During these years, Sotto was also the lead vocalist of the disco funk band <a href="/wiki/VST_%26_Co." title="VST &amp; Co.">VST &amp; Company</a>. He produced such songs as "Awitin Mo at Isasayaw Ko", "Rock, Baby, Rock", and "Kung Sakali".</p>
<p>He joined his brothers <a href="/wiki/Tito_Sotto" title="Tito Sotto">Tito</a> and Val in the early 1970s gag show <i>OK Lang</i> under <a href="/wiki/Intercontinental_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Intercontinental Broadcasting Corporation">IBC 13</a>. This is where he met another comedian, <a href="/wiki/Joey_de_Leon" title="Joey de Leon">Joey de Leon</a>, who invited the brothers to join him. The trio of Tito, Vic and Joey (TVJ) was thus formed, casting in <i><a href="/wiki/Iskul_Bukol" title="Iskul Bukol">Iskul Bukol</a></i>, <i>TVJ (Television's Jesters)</i>, <i>Rock and Roll 2000</i> and hosting <i><a href="/wiki/Eat_Bulaga!" title="Eat Bulaga!">Eat Bulaga!</a></i></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_career">Current career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vic_Sotto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Current career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the last three decades, he has done sitcoms and television shows for 3 networks, <a href="/wiki/ABS-CBN" title="ABS-CBN">ABS-CBN</a>, <a href="/wiki/GMA_Network" title="GMA Network">GMA Network</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Associated_Broadcasting_Company" title="Associated Broadcasting Company">TV5</a> (most of which were for GMA).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vic_Sotto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sotto has four children from previous relationships:</p>
<ul>
<li>Danica Sotto-Pingris (with actress <a href="/wiki/Dina_Bonnevie" title="Dina Bonnevie">Dina Bonnevie</a>; married to <a href="/wiki/Marc_Pingris" title="Marc Pingris">Marc Pingris</a> and their children: Anielle Micaela and Jean Michael Pingris)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Oyo_Boy_Sotto" title="Oyo Boy Sotto">Oyo Sotto</a> (with actress <a href="/wiki/Dina_Bonnevie" title="Dina Bonnevie">Dina Bonnevie</a>; married to <a href="/wiki/Kristine_Hermosa" title="Kristine Hermosa">Kristine Hermosa</a> and their children: Kristian Daniel, Ondrea Bliss, Kaleb Hanns &amp; Marvic Valentin "Vin" Sotto II)</li>
<li>Vico Sotto (with actress <a href="/wiki/Coney_Reyes" title="Coney Reyes">Coney Reyes</a>)</li>
<li>Paulina Sotto (with former commercial model/TV actress Angela Luz; married to her fiance Jed Salgado-Llanes).</li>
</ul>
<p>Sotto confirmed his engagement to <i><a href="/wiki/Eat_Bulaga!" title="Eat Bulaga!">Eat Bulaga!</a></i> co-host <a href="/wiki/Pauleen_Luna" title="Pauleen Luna">Pauleen Luna</a> in September 2015. The couple married on January 30, 2016 at <a href="/wiki/Ayala_Alabang" title="Ayala Alabang">Ayala Alabang</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muntinlupa_City" title="Muntinlupa City">Muntinlupa City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
