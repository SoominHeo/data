<p><b>Sean Michael Leonard Anderson</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (born March 25, 1988), known professionally as <b>Big Sean</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_bio_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic_bio-2">[2]</a></sup> is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>. Sean signed with <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s <a href="/wiki/GOOD_Music" title="GOOD Music">GOOD Music</a> in 2007, <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a> in 2008 and <a href="/wiki/Roc_Nation" title="Roc Nation">Roc Nation</a> in 2014. After releasing a number of <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtapes</a>, Sean released his debut <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Finally_Famous_(Big_Sean_album)" title="Finally Famous (Big Sean album)">Finally Famous</a>,</i> in 2011. He released his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Hall_of_Fame_(Big_Sean_album)" title="Hall of Fame (Big Sean album)">Hall of Fame</a>,</i> on August 27, 2013. Sean's third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_Sky_Paradise" title="Dark Sky Paradise">Dark Sky Paradise</a></i> was released in 2015 and which earned him his first No. 1 studio album.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1988.E2.80.932003:_Early_life_and_career_beginnings">1988–2003: Early life and career beginnings</span></h3>
<p>Sean Michael Leonard Anderson was born on March 25, 1988 in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Monica,_California" title="Santa Monica, California">Santa Monica, California</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> to Myra and James Anderson. When he was 3 months old, he moved to Detroit, Michigan, where he was raised by his mother, a school teacher, and grandmother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_bio_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic_bio-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Def_Jam_bio_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Def_Jam_bio-4">[4]</a></sup> He attended the <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Waldorf_School" title="Detroit Waldorf School">Detroit Waldorf School</a> and graduated from <a href="/wiki/Cass_Technical_High_School" title="Cass Technical High School">Cass Technical High School</a> with a 3.7 GPA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MIDIGI_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-MIDIGI-5">[5]</a></sup> Big Sean is often heard saying "west side" in his songs; he is referring to the west side of his hometown Detroit, Michigan. In his later years in high school, Sean gained a valuable relationship with Detroit hip-hop station <a href="/wiki/WPZR" title="WPZR">WHTD</a>; he would show his rhyming skills on a weekly basis as part of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rap_battle" title="Rap battle">rap battle</a> contest held by the station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metro_Times_2008_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Metro_Times_2008-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2005, <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a> was doing a radio interview at <a href="/wiki/WPZR" title="WPZR">102.7 FM</a>. Hearing about this, Sean headed over to the station to meet West and perform some freestyle. Initially West was reluctant to hear him, however he gave Sean 16 bars to rap for him. According to Big Sean, West enjoyed his freestyle: "As we get to the entrance of the radio station ... we stopped in the middle of the doorway. He starts looking at me and bobbing his head,".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> After the freestyle, Sean left West his demo tape. Two years later, West signed Big Sean to <a href="/wiki/GOOD_Music" title="GOOD Music">GOOD Music</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.932010:_Mixtapes_and_internet_following">2007–2010: Mixtapes and internet following</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>On September 30, 2007, Big Sean released his first official mixtape <i>Finally Famous: The Mixtape</i>. His hit single, "Get'cha Some", produced by WrighTrax, attained media attention and led to articles in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Source_(magazine)" title="The Source (magazine)">The Source</a></i> and the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Detroit_Metro_Times" title="Detroit Metro Times">Detroit Metro Times</a></i>. He also recorded a music video for "Get'cha Some", which was directed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hype_Williams" title="Hype Williams">Hype Williams</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Sean released a second mixtape hosted by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mick_Boogie" title="Mick Boogie">Mick Boogie</a> on April 16, 2009, called <i>UKNOWBIGSEAN</i>. It featured the songs "Million Dollars", "Get'cha Some" and "Supa Dupa". This mixtape includes 30 tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Sean released a third mixtape hosted by <a href="/wiki/Don_Cannon" title="Don Cannon">Don Cannon</a> on August 31, 2010 called <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Finally_Famous_Vol._3:_BIG" title="Finally Famous Vol. 3: BIG">Finally Famous Vol. 3: BIG</a></i>, which features include major artists like <a href="/wiki/Bun_B" title="Bun B">Bun B</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chip_tha_Ripper" title="Chip tha Ripper">Chip tha Ripper</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Curren$y" title="Curren$y">Curren$y</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tyga" title="Tyga">Tyga</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aubrey_Graham" title="Aubrey Graham">Drake</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mike_Posner_(musician)" title="Mike Posner (musician)">Mike Posner</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Cool_Kids" title="The Cool Kids">Chuck Inglish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asher_Roth" title="Asher Roth">Asher Roth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dom_Kennedy" title="Dom Kennedy">Dom Kennedy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chiddy_Bang" title="Chiddy Bang">Chiddy Bang</a>. The mixtape includes 20 tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>An unauthorized leak of his collaboration with Drake, called "Made" made its way onto the internet on April 30, 2010. In an interview with TheHipHopUpdate.com on May 1, Big Sean expressed his disappointment over the leak, calling it an unfinished version both musically and lyrically. Big Sean's official Facebook page confirmed that his debut album <i>Finally Famous</i> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Consequence_(rapper)" title="Consequence (rapper)">Consequence</a>'s <i>Cons TV</i> would be released on September 14, 2010. On August 31, Big Sean tweeted that the album was not coming out on that day, but it would be coming out sometime in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.932012:_Finally_Famous_and_Detroit">2011–2012: <i>Finally Famous</i> and <i>Detroit</i></span></h3>
<p>In an interview on Conspiracy Worldwide Radio, Sean discussed the role Kanye West and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/No_I.D._production_discography" title="No I.D. production discography">No I.D.</a> have had in the development of his first studio album's sonic direction as well as the challenges of being inside the studio with West.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The album's <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a>, "<a href="/wiki/My_Last" title="My Last">My Last</a>", features vocals from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chris_Brown_(American_entertainer)" title="Chris Brown (American entertainer)">Chris Brown</a> and was produced by No I.D.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> According to Amazon.com, Big Sean's <i>Finally Famous</i> was delayed by a week, with a tentative release date set for June 28. Big Sean has revealed the cover art for his G.O.O.D. Music debut and explained that the one-week pushback was to be blamed on sample and feature clearance issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The official track list was revealed on June 7.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<p><i><a href="/wiki/Finally_Famous_(Big_Sean_album)" title="Finally Famous (Big Sean album)">Finally Famous</a></i>, Sean's debut <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a>, was released June 28, 2011, and spawned three <a href="/wiki/Hit_single" title="Hit single">hit singles</a>; "<a href="/wiki/My_Last" title="My Last">My Last</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Marvin_%26_Chardonnay" title="Marvin &amp; Chardonnay">Marvin &amp; Chardonnay</a>" and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dance_(A$$)" title="Dance (A$$)">Dance (A$$)</a>". The album featured <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> from <a href="/wiki/Lupe_Fiasco" title="Lupe Fiasco">Lupe Fiasco</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Legend" title="John Legend">John Legend</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roscoe_Dash" title="Roscoe Dash">Roscoe Dash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chiddy_Bang" title="Chiddy Bang">Chiddy Bang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a>, and included <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_production" title="Hip hop production">production</a> from <a href="/wiki/No_I.D." title="No I.D.">No I.D.</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Legendary_Traxster" title="The Legendary Traxster">The Legendary Traxster</a>, Andrew "Pop" Wansel, Xaphoon Jones and <a href="/wiki/The_Neptunes" title="The Neptunes">The Neptunes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> When the songs "O.T.T.R." and "Flowers" were <a href="/wiki/Internet_leak" title="Internet leak">leaked</a> in July 2011, speculation began of a new mixtape. Sean confirmed in an interview June 28, 2011 that a collaborative mixtape between him and "two other guys in hip-hop that are just killing it right now" will be released "in a couple of weeks". <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Curren$y" title="Curren$y">Curren$y</a>, were the suspected featured rappers on the mixtape.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_DOTR_2011_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_DOTR_2011-19">[19]</a></sup> However, Wiz Khalifa later confirmed that there would be no mixtape, claiming that the songs were created, "just for fun".</p>
<p>In September 2011, Big Sean confirmed in an interview with the <i>Daily Tribune</i> that he'll be working on his second album during the <i>I Am Finally Famous Tour</i> and plans to release the album sometime in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On October 19, 2011, Kanye West announced on his Twitter plans for a Spring 2012 <a href="/wiki/GOOD_Music" title="GOOD Music">GOOD Music</a> album release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tweet_126770388121292800_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tweet_126770388121292800-21">[21]</a></sup> On April 6, 2012, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mercy_(Kanye_West_song)" title="Mercy (Kanye West song)">Mercy</a>", the <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> from the GOOD Music <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">compilation album</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cruel_Summer_(GOOD_Music_album)" title="Cruel Summer (GOOD Music album)">Cruel Summer</a></i>, was released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The song, produced by newly signed in-house producer Lifted, features Big Sean along with Kanye West, <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a> and southern rapper <a href="/wiki/2_Chainz" title="2 Chainz">2 Chainz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Big Sean then announced his fourth mixtape would be titled "Detroit" and would serve as a lead-in to his second studio album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tweet_126770388121292800_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tweet_126770388121292800-21">[21]</a></sup> He then began promoting the mixtape, releasing short versions of the songs on his <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> page. On September 5, 2012, Big Sean released the mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Detroit_(mixtape)" title="Detroit (mixtape)">Detroit</a></i> which features guest appearances from fellow rappers <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juicy_J" title="Juicy J">Juicy J</a>, <a href="/wiki/King_Chip" title="King Chip">King Chip</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Royce_da_5%279%22" title="Royce da 5'9&quot;">Royce da 5'9"</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tyga" title="Tyga">Tyga</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.9316:_Hall_of_Fame.2C_Dark_Sky_Paradise.2C_and_TWENTY88">2013–16: <i>Hall of Fame,</i> <i>Dark Sky Paradise, and TWENTY88</i></span></h3>
<p>Big Sean pushed back the release date of his second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Hall_of_Fame_(Big_Sean_album)" title="Hall of Fame (Big Sean album)">Hall of Fame</a></i>, and was eventually released on August 27, 2013. The album has skits to give it a "classic feel", and includes features from multiple artists, including <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miguel_(singer)" title="Miguel (singer)">Miguel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>. The album's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Record_production" title="Record production">production</a> was primarily handled by <a href="/wiki/No_I.D." title="No I.D.">No I.D.</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Key_Wane" title="Key Wane">Key Wane</a> along with additional production from <a href="/wiki/Hit-Boy" title="Hit-Boy">Hit-Boy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Da_Internz" title="Da Internz">Da Internz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Dean_(record_producer)" title="Mike Dean (record producer)">Mike Dean</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Travis_Scott_(musician)" title="Travis Scott (musician)">Travis Scott</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chiddy_Bang" title="Chiddy Bang">Xaphoon Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Young_Chop" title="Young Chop">Young Chop</a> among others. Big Sean also stated in an interview that he was in the studio with fellow Detroit native <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>. Sean went on to say they made a "Detroit classic" but he is unsure if it will be on <i>Hall of Fame</i> because of "timing issues".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> <i>Hall of Fame</i> had spawned five singles, "<a href="/wiki/Guap" title="Guap">Guap</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Switch_Up" title="Switch Up">Switch Up</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Common_(rapper)" title="Common (rapper)">Common</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Beware_(Big_Sean_song)" title="Beware (Big Sean song)">Beware</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Jhen%C3%A9_Aiko" title="Jhené Aiko">Jhené Aiko</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Fire_(Big_Sean_song)" title="Fire (Big Sean song)">Fire</a>", and "Ashley" featuring <a href="/wiki/Miguel_(singer)" title="Miguel (singer)">Miguel</a>.</p>
<p>A month prior to the release of <i>Hall of Fame</i>, Big Sean told <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a></i> that he had already begun work on his third album due to the inspiration from his new relationship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-complex1_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-25">[25]</a></sup> On September 12, 2014 Big Sean announced that he had signed with <a href="/wiki/Roc_Nation" title="Roc Nation">Roc Nation</a>. Later that same day he released four new songs titled, "<a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Fuck_with_You" title="I Don't Fuck with You">I Don't Fuck with You</a>", "Paradise", "4th Quarter" and "Jit/Juke". Producers for these songs include, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Will_Made_It" title="Mike Will Made It">Mike Will Made It</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Mustard" title="DJ Mustard">DJ Mustard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Dahi" title="DJ Dahi">DJ Dahi</a>, Nate Fox, <a href="/wiki/Da_Internz" title="Da Internz">Da Internz</a>, L&amp;F, and <a href="/wiki/Key_Wane" title="Key Wane">Key Wane</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> "I Don't Fuck with You" was released to iTunes on September 19, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In an interview with Sway Calloway, Sean confirmed that Lil Wayne will be featured on the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 25, 2015 various artists who worked on Big Sean's upcoming third studio album such as <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Mustard" title="DJ Mustard">DJ Mustard</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Travis_Scott_(musician)" title="Travis Scott (musician)">Travis Scott</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ariana_Grande" title="Ariana Grande">Ariana Grande</a> posted the album cover to <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> or <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The next day, Big Sean posted a trailer to his new album which revealed the album's release date as February 24, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The album, <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_Sky_Paradise" title="Dark Sky Paradise">Dark Sky Paradise</a></i> debuted atop the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> making it his first No. 1 album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> He would lead with Video singles for songs entitled "IDFWU", "Blessings", "I Know", "One Man Can Change The World", and "Play No Games." As of February 1, 2016, <i>Dark Sky Paradise</i> was certified platinum. On December 31, 2015, Big Sean released a track recapping 2015, "What A Year (Ft. Pharrell Williams &amp; Detail)" and On March 25, 2016, Big Sean released a track to celebrate his 28th birthday, "Get My Shit Together". Two days later he announced a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twenty88_(album)" title="Twenty88 (album)">self-titled</a> album with <a href="/wiki/Jhen%C3%A9_Aiko" title="Jhené Aiko">Jhené Aiko</a>, as TWENTY88, scheduled to be released on April 1, 2016, exclusively on Tidal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> On April 5, 2016, the Album became available on Spotify and Apple Music. A week after the exclusive release of TWENT88 on Tidal, Big Sean and <a href="/wiki/Jhen%C3%A9_Aiko" title="Jhené Aiko">Jhené Aiko</a> released a 15-minute short film called "Out of Love", which is composed of several recordings from the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Around the same month, upon the wake of the <a href="/wiki/Flint_water_crisis" title="Flint water crisis">Flint water crisis</a>, Big Sean tweeted to the city's official Twitter account, asking if he could do anything to help, and later on donated $10,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016.E2.80.93present:_I_Decided.">2016–present: <i>I Decided.</i></span></h3>
<p>On October 31, 2016, Big Sean released "<a href="/wiki/Bounce_Back_(Big_Sean_song)" title="Bounce Back (Big Sean song)">Bounce Back</a>" as the lead single from his upcoming fourth studio album, <i>I Decided</i>, which is due to be released February 3, 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> "Moves" was released as an instant-grat on December 16, 2016. Sean has consulted the advice of <a href="/wiki/Jay_Z" title="Jay Z">Jay Z</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rick_Rubin" title="Rick Rubin">Rick Rubin</a> while working on the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>Big Sean dated his high school sweetheart, Ashley Marie, whom he met when he was sixteen and started dating at nineteen until their break-up in early 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> He subsequently began dating actress <a href="/wiki/Naya_Rivera" title="Naya Rivera">Naya Rivera</a>, whom he first met over <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>; their relationship became public in April 2013, though Big Sean told <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Complex_Magazine" title="Complex Magazine">Complex</a></i> that they had been dating for a while beforehand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-complex1_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-25">[25]</a></sup> Big Sean and Rivera announced their engagement in October 2013, but ended the engagement in April 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-e2013_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-e2013-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> In October 2014, singer <a href="/wiki/Ariana_Grande" title="Ariana Grande">Ariana Grande</a> confirmed she and Big Sean were dating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Sean and Grande made their red carpet debut at the <a href="/wiki/57th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="57th Annual Grammy Awards">2015 Grammys</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> In April 2015, the couple ended their relationship after 8 months of dating, due to a text message.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late October 2008, Big Sean was featured in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Source_(magazine)" title="The Source (magazine)">The Source</a></i> and headlined the "Style" section of the magazine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> In the article Sean talks about his own personal style and states that his favorite clothing brands are 10 Deep, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billionaire_Boys_Club_(clothing)" title="Billionaire Boys Club (clothing)">Billionaire Boys Club</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bape" title="Bape">Bape</a>. Big Sean has posed in the Winter 2008 <a href="/wiki/Billionaire_Boys_Club_(clothing_retailer)" title="Billionaire Boys Club (clothing retailer)">Billionaire Boys Club</a> lookbook,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> and is a consistent representative of Ti$A<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> clothing and hats, along with Chris Brown and Tyga. He also has an endorsement deal with <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a> through which he has released his own "Detroit Player" line of sneakers <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mlive_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mlive-49">[49]</a></sup> and is a follower of the Rosewood clothing style.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> In 2013, Big Sean launched his own clothing company, Aura Gold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_troubles">Legal troubles</span></h3>
<p>On August 4, 2011, Big Sean was arrested for third degree <a href="/wiki/Sexual_assault" title="Sexual assault">sexual assault</a> at a concert in <a href="/wiki/Lewiston,_New_York" title="Lewiston, New York">Lewiston, New York</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> On October 26, he pleaded guilty to second-degree unlawful imprisonment, and was fined $750.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pfeiffer_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pfeiffer-54">[54]</a></sup> The charges of third-degree sexual abuse were dropped. Big Sean's attorney, Scott Leemon, stated that he "...did not engage in any type of sexual misconduct."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pfeiffer_54-1"><a href="#cite_note-Pfeiffer-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
