<p><b>The Big Cartoon DataBase</b> (or <b>BCDB</b> for short) is an online <a href="/wiki/Database" title="Database">database</a> of information about <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Animated_cartoons" title="Animated cartoons">animated cartoons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Feature_film" title="Feature film">animated feature films</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Animated_television_series" title="Animated television series">animated television shows</a>, and cartoon <a href="/wiki/Short_film" title="Short film">shorts</a>.</p>
<p>The BCDB project began in 1996 as a list of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Disney_animated_features" title="List of Disney animated features">Disney animated features</a> on creator Dave Koch's local computer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABOUT_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ABOUT-1">[1]</a></sup> In response to increasing interest in the material, the database went online in 1998 as a searchable resource dedicated to compiling information about <a href="/wiki/Cartoon" title="Cartoon">cartoons</a>, including production details such as voice actors, producers, and directors, as well as plot summaries and user reviews of cartoons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RUSQ1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-RUSQ1-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2003, BCDB became a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/501(c)" title="501(c)">501(c)</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-profit_corporation" title="Non-profit corporation">non-profit corporation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABOUT_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ABOUT-1">[1]</a></sup> On June 24, 2009, it was announced by creator Dave Koch on his BCDB forums that the site had 100,000 titles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Big_Cartoon_DataBase&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>One feature of the BCDB is its "Top Rated" page which provides a listing of the top 25 animated films as voted by the registered users of the website. Ratings are shown based on top score as well as the cartoons that receive the highest number of votes. Users are given the option of rating a movie from "1" (lowest) to "10" (highest). To safeguard against attempts to skew the data, the DataBase employs data filters and a vote quota in an attempt to give an accurate <a href="/wiki/Bayesian_probability" title="Bayesian probability">Bayesian estimate</a>. The BCDB also has a lowest-20 rated cartoons feature which, based on the same data, shows the least liked cartoons in the database.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>BCDB also includes a linked, online forum, where users express their opinions about cartoons, and/or ask questions about them. The forum is available to all registered users, and is actively moderated by a team of moderators and administrators.</p>
<p>Other features include news items related to the animation industry and the recently added image gallery, which allows users to see images of various <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cartoon_characters" title="Cartoon characters">cartoon characters</a> taken from popular films.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recognition">Recognition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Big_Cartoon_DataBase&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Recognition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2002, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_San_Diego_Union_Tribune" title="The San Diego Union Tribune">The San Diego Union Tribune</a></i> listed BCDB as a "top site" and wrote "with more than 42,000 cartoons, 2,000 series[,] and 1,300 cartoon reviews, this may be one of the Internet's largest searchable databases of cartoons".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SDUT1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-SDUT1-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2005, <i><a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple Hot News</a></i> wrote "The Big Cartoon Database is the place to find in depth information about any cartoon ever made".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AHN1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-AHN1-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2006, it was reported by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reference_and_User_Services_Association" title="Reference and User Services Association">Reference and User Services Association</a>, in their Eighth Annual List of Best Free Reference Web Sites, that "The Big Cartoon Database is the definitive Web compendium for anyone interested in the history of animation."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RUSQ1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-RUSQ1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The BCDB is used as a reference by such sources as <i><a href="/wiki/Hartford_Courant" title="Hartford Courant">Hartford Courant</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HC1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-HC1-7">[7]</a></sup> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_San_Diego_Union_Tribune" title="The San Diego Union Tribune">The San Diego Union Tribune</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SDUT1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-SDUT1-5">[5]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Oakland_Tribune" title="Oakland Tribune">Oakland Tribune</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OT1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-OT1-8">[8]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Pacific_Publishing_Company" title="Pacific Publishing Company">Beacon News</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BN1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-BN1-9">[9]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USAT1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-USAT1-10">[10]</a></sup> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Animation_World_Network" title="Animation World Network">Animation World Network</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AWN1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-AWN1-11">[11]</a></sup> among others. <i>Que's Official Internet Yellow Pages</i> rates the site as 5 (out of 5) stars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
