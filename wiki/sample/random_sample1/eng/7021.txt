<p><b>Viktor Gustaf Balck</b> <a href="/wiki/Order_of_St_Michael_and_St_George" title="Order of St Michael and St George">KCMG</a> (25 April 1844 – 31 May 1928) was a <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Army" title="Swedish Army">Swedish Army</a> <a href="/wiki/Officer_(armed_forces)" title="Officer (armed forces)">officer</a> and sports personality who was one of the original members of the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a> and who is often called "the father of Swedish sports".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_career">Military career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Viktor_Balck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Military career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:No-nb_bldsa_1c036.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="5631" data-file-width="3925" height="316" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/No-nb_bldsa_1c036.jpg/220px-No-nb_bldsa_1c036.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/No-nb_bldsa_1c036.jpg/330px-No-nb_bldsa_1c036.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/No-nb_bldsa_1c036.jpg/440px-No-nb_bldsa_1c036.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:No-nb_bldsa_1c036.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Balck in uniform.</div>

<p>Balck was born in <a href="/wiki/Karlskrona" title="Karlskrona">Karlskrona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, and was a <a href="/wiki/Sailor" title="Sailor">sailor</a> in his youth. In 1861, he joined the Swedish War Academy at <a href="/wiki/Karlberg_Palace" title="Karlberg Palace">Karlberg</a> in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> as an <a href="/wiki/Cadet" title="Cadet">officer cadet</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Navy" title="Swedish Navy">Swedish Navy</a>. After a while, he switched track to become a cadet for the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Army" title="Swedish Army">Swedish Army</a>, and was active in <a href="/wiki/Fencing" title="Fencing">fencing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gymnastics" title="Gymnastics">gymnastics</a>. He stayed on as assistant gymnastics instructor at Karlberg for a while, and in 1866 was commissioned as a <a href="/wiki/Second_lieutenant" title="Second lieutenant">second lieutenant</a> in the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%A4rke" title="Närke">Närke</a> <a href="/wiki/Regiment" title="Regiment">regiment</a>. He was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant" title="Lieutenant">lieutenant</a> in 1875, in the same regiment, and to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Captain_(land_and_air)" title="Captain (land and air)">captain</a> in 1884.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NFB_1904_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NFB_1904-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>However, Balck's military career came to be devoted almost entirely to gymnastics and sports. He was assistant instructor at Karlberg from 1868 to 1870, then gymnastics teacher at the Swedish <a href="/wiki/Cavalry" title="Cavalry">cavalry</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Riding_school" title="Riding school">riding school</a> at <a href="/wiki/Str%C3%B6msholm" title="Strömsholm">Strömsholm</a>, 1870-1872. He became an instructor of military gymnastics and fencing at the <a href="/wiki/Gymnastik-_och_idrottsh%C3%B6gskolan" title="Gymnastik- och idrottshögskolan">Swedish Central Institute of Gymnastics</a> in 1885, was chief instructor in the same subject, 1887-1909, and the Institute's director 1907-1909.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NFB_1922_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NFB_1922-2">[2]</a></sup> He was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Major" title="Major">major</a> in the Swedish Army in 1894, to <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_colonel" title="Lieutenant colonel">lieutenant colonel</a> in 1900, and to <a href="/wiki/Colonel" title="Colonel">colonel</a> in 1904.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NFB_1904_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-NFB_1904-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1909 he transferred to the reserve list and in 1914 received an honorary promotion to <a href="/wiki/Major_general" title="Major general">major general</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NFB_1922_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NFB_1922-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_as_sports_leader">Career as sports leader</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Viktor_Balck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career as sports leader">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Directly after completing his officer's training, Black studied the pedagogical, military and medical course at the Swedish Central Institute of Gymnastics 1866-1868, and stayed on as assistant teacher at the Institute 1868-1870, while also being an assistant teacher at Karlberg. From 1872, his main activities - both in military and civilian gymnastics and sports - had the Institute as a base.</p>
<p>As a young officer and gymnastics teacher, Balck was of the impression that voluntary gymnastics and sports activities in Sweden, i.e., outside the army and the schools, were undeveloped in comparison to the contemporary situation in many other countries. Determined to change this, from the 1870s he participated in the formation of several sporting clubs and organisations, and the founding of several related journals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NFB_1904_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-NFB_1904-1">[1]</a></sup> During this time, Swedish organised sports took shape, and Balck became one of its leading figures.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_sports_career">International sports career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Viktor_Balck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International sports career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Balck also became involved in the emerging international sports movement at the end of the 19th century. In 1894 he became one of the original members of International Olympic Committee (IOC), and was one of two vice presidents of the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Olympic_Committee" title="Swedish Olympic Committee">Swedish Olympic Committee</a> from 1913 to his death in 1928. He was also one of the leading figures behind the <a href="/wiki/Nordic_Games" title="Nordic Games">Nordic Games</a> which were arranged from 1901.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SOC_1912_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-SOC_1912-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Already in 1894 in IOC, Balck had proposed Stockholm as a venue for the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a>. The official application to arrange the games came in 1908, and despite strong competition from <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, Stockholm were chosen to host the <a href="/wiki/1912_Summer_Olympics" title="1912 Summer Olympics">1912 Summer Olympics</a> with Balck a prominent member of the national organising committee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SOC_1912_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-SOC_1912-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was also a president of the <a href="/wiki/International_Skating_Union" title="International Skating Union">International Skating Union</a> from 1894 and 1924. His <a href="/wiki/Ice_skating" title="Ice skating">ice skating</a> career also included the construction of the "Balck skate".</p>
<p>In recognition of his international sports career, Balck was appointed as an honorary Knight Commander of the British <a href="/wiki/Order_of_St_Michael_and_St_George" title="Order of St Michael and St George">Order of St Michael and St George</a>, so in Britain he was styled as "Viktor Balck, KCMG".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NFB_1922_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-NFB_1922-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
