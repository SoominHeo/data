<p><b>Viktor Pavičić</b> (15 October 1898 – 20 January 1943) was a Croatian military commander who led the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/369th_Reinforced_Croatian_Infantry_Regiment" title="369th Reinforced Croatian Infantry Regiment">369th Reinforced Croatian Infantry Regiment</a>, which fought on the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Front_(World_War_II)" title="Eastern Front (World War II)">Eastern Front</a> and was involved in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Stalingrad" title="Battle of Stalingrad">Battle of Stalingrad</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Viktor_Pavi%C4%8Di%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Viktor Pavičić was born on 15 October 1898. A colonel, he was the commander of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Croatian_Home_Guard_(Independent_State_of_Croatia)" title="Croatian Home Guard (Independent State of Croatia)">Croatian Home Guard</a> military academy from July to August 1942. In August, he was named commander of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/369th_Reinforced_Croatian_Infantry_Regiment" title="369th Reinforced Croatian Infantry Regiment">369th Reinforced Croatian Infantry Regiment</a> that was to fight alongside other <a href="/wiki/Axis_powers" title="Axis powers">Axis</a> forces on the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Front_(World_War_II)" title="Eastern Front (World War II)">Eastern Front</a>. In late September, Croatian leader <a href="/wiki/Ante_Paveli%C4%87" title="Ante Pavelić">Ante Pavelić</a> met with Pavičić and decorated him. Immediately afterwards, Pavičić and his regiment left the <a href="/wiki/Independent_State_of_Croatia" title="Independent State of Croatia">Independent State of Croatia</a> to participate in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Stalingrad" title="Battle of Stalingrad">Battle of Stalingrad</a>. They were devastated in the ensuing battle, and Pavičić elected to leave the surviving Croatian soldiers and return to Croatia. He was killed on 20 January 1943. There are several conflicting accounts of how he died. One account holds that the airplane in which he was travelling was shot down by the Soviets, while another states that he was executed by the Germans for wishing to leave the battlefield.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDizdarGr.C4.8Di.C4.87Ravli.C4.87Stupari.C4.871997312_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDizdarGr.C4.8Di.C4.87Ravli.C4.87Stupari.C4.871997312-1">[1]</a></sup> He was replaced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lieutenant_Colonel" title="Lieutenant Colonel">Lieutenant Colonel</a> <a href="/wiki/Marko_Mesi%C4%87_(soldier)" title="Marko Mesić (soldier)">Marko Mesić</a> on 21 January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEde_BruyneRikmenspoel2004272_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEde_BruyneRikmenspoel2004272-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
