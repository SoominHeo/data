<p><b>Victoria Ainsle Pratt</b> (born December 18, 1970) is a Canadian actress, author and <a href="/wiki/Physical_fitness" title="Physical fitness">fitness</a> model.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Victoria_Pratt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pratt grew up in <a href="/wiki/Chesley,_Ontario" title="Chesley, Ontario">Chesley, Ontario</a>, a self described "tomboy at heart."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pratt_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-pratt-1">[1]</a></sup> She attend the <a href="/wiki/Kinesiology" title="Kinesiology">Kinesiology</a> program at <a href="/wiki/York_University" title="York University">York University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> and graduated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Summa_cum_laude" title="Summa cum laude">summa cum laude</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Before starting her acting career, Pratt made a notable mark in the fitness world as a performance tester, working at the York University campus with various <a href="/wiki/Athlete" title="Athlete">athletes</a> including players from the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Maple_Leafs" title="Toronto Maple Leafs">Toronto Maple Leafs</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Jose_Sharks" title="San Jose Sharks">San Jose Sharks</a>, among others.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Victoria_Pratt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After graduating from York, Pratt met <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kennedy_(publisher)" title="Robert Kennedy (publisher)">Robert Kennedy</a> (publisher of <i><a href="/wiki/MuscleMag_International" title="MuscleMag International">MuscleMag</a></i>) who convinced Pratt to try her hand at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Modelling_career" title="Modelling career">modelling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Acting" title="Acting">acting</a>. Pratt quickly found success as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fitness_model_(person)" title="Fitness model (person)">fitness model</a>, gracing several magazine covers (including Muscle Mag and Oxygen) and working alongside now-famous <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a> stars <a href="/wiki/Trish_Stratus" title="Trish Stratus">Trish Stratus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Torrie_Wilson" title="Torrie Wilson">Torrie Wilson</a>.</p>
<p>Pratt eventually began to land high-profile acting roles in TV throughout the late 90s, including <i><a href="/wiki/Xena:_Warrior_Princess" title="Xena: Warrior Princess">Xena: Warrior Princess</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Once_a_Thief_(TV_series)" title="Once a Thief (TV series)">Once a Thief</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cleopatra_2525" title="Cleopatra 2525">Cleopatra 2525</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Mutant_X_(TV_series)" title="Mutant X (TV series)">Mutant X</a></i> (which she starred on for 3 seasons).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pratt made her film debut in the 1998 film titled <i><a href="/wiki/Legacy_(1998_film)" title="Legacy (1998 film)">Legacy</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/David_Hasselhoff" title="David Hasselhoff">David Hasselhoff</a>, directed by her eventual husband, <a href="/wiki/T.J._Scott" title="T.J. Scott">T.J. Scott</a>. Pratt also starred in several <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">independent</a> or small <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Made-for-TV_movie" title="Made-for-TV movie">made-for-TV</a> movies during the 90s and early 2000s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pratt has since garnered a small <a href="/wiki/Cult_following" title="Cult following">cult following</a> who recognize her large body of work as an actress in cancelled, but popular <a href="/wiki/Low-budget_film" title="Low-budget film">low-budget</a> <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science-fiction</a> productions. There are several <a href="/wiki/Tumblr" title="Tumblr">Tumblr</a> fan pages and websites dedicated to Pratt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/AskMen" title="AskMen">AskMen</a> called her a "butt-kicking sci-fi queen."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since the mid 2000s, Pratt has guest-starred on several hit TV programs including <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI: Crime Scene Investigation</a></i> (2009), <i><a href="/wiki/NCIS_(TV_series)" title="NCIS (TV series)">NCIS</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/Lie_to_Me" title="Lie to Me">Lie to Me</a></i> (2011) and <i><a href="/wiki/Castle_(TV_series)" title="Castle (TV series)">Castle</a></i> (2013). In 2006, Pratt co-starred with model <a href="/wiki/Josie_Maran" title="Josie Maran">Josie Moran</a> in the film <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Mallory_Effect_film&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Mallory Effect film (page does not exist)">The Mallory Effect</a></i> (2006) which premiered at the <a href="/wiki/Slamdance_Film_Festival" title="Slamdance Film Festival">Slamdance Film Festival</a>. The same year Pratt starred with <a href="/wiki/Taye_Diggs" title="Taye Diggs">Taye Diggs</a> on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Day_Break" title="Day Break">Day Break</a></i> (2006),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> which premiered on November 15, 2006.</p>
<p>Pratt has continued modelling throughout her career, recently being named <i>Oxygen Magazine</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s 'Cover Girl' in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OxMag_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-OxMag-12">[12]</a></sup> As of October 2015, she is a published author of adult fiction, her first book being "Double Down".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Victoria_Pratt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pratt has been married to award-winning director and photographer T.J. Scott since 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pratt_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-pratt-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>She divides her time among <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>. Pratt has a blue belt in <a href="/wiki/Shotokan" title="Shotokan">Shotokan</a> karate and is a keen <a href="/wiki/Kickboxing" title="Kickboxing">kickboxer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In her interview with <i>Oxygen Magazine</i>, Pratt said she enjoys "salsa dancing, hiking in the Hollywood hills and volleyball."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OxMag_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-OxMag-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
