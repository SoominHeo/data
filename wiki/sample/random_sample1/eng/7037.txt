<p><b>Weltausstellung 1873 Wien</b> (English: World Exposition 1873 Vienna) was the large <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_exposition" title="World exposition">world exposition</a> that was held in 1873 in the <a href="/wiki/Austria-Hungary" title="Austria-Hungary">Austria-Hungarian</a> capital of <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>. Its motto was <i>Kultur und Erziehung</i> (English: Culture and Education).</p>
<p>There were almost 26,000 exhibitors<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> housed in different buildings that were erected for this exposition, including the <i><a href="/wiki/Rotunde" title="Rotunde">Rotunde</a></i> (English: <a href="/wiki/Rotunda_(architecture)" title="Rotunda (architecture)">Rotunda</a>), a large circular building in the great park of <a href="/wiki/Prater" title="Prater">Prater</a> designed by the Scottish engineer <a href="/wiki/John_Scott_Russell" title="John Scott Russell">John Scott Russell</a>. The Rotunde was destroyed by fire on September 17, 1937.</p>
<p>The Russian pavilion had a naval section designed by <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Hartmann" title="Viktor Hartmann">Viktor Hartmann</a>. Exhibits included models of the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Rijeka" title="Port of Rijeka">Port of Rijeka</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Ill%C3%A9s_Relief" title="Illés Relief">Illés Relief</a> model of Jerusalem.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
