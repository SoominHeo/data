<p>Year <b>897</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">DCCCXCVII</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Saturday" title="Common year starting on Saturday">common year starting on Saturday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=897&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=897&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=897&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Spring – King <a href="/wiki/Lambert_of_Italy" title="Lambert of Italy">Lambert II</a> travels to <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> with his mother, Queen <a href="/wiki/Ageltrude" title="Ageltrude">Ageltrude</a> and brother <a href="/wiki/Guy_IV_of_Spoleto" title="Guy IV of Spoleto">Guy IV</a>, Lombard <a href="/wiki/Duke" title="Duke">duke</a> of <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Spoleto" title="Duchy of Spoleto">Spoleto</a>, to meet Pope <a href="/wiki/Pope_Stephen_VI" title="Pope Stephen VI">Stephen VI</a> to receive reconfirmation of his imperial title. Guy is murdered on the <a href="/wiki/Tiber" title="Tiber">Tiber</a> by agents of <a href="/wiki/Alberic_I_of_Spoleto" title="Alberic I of Spoleto">Alberic I</a>, a Frankish <a href="/wiki/Nobility" title="Nobility">nobleman</a> with political interests. He seizes Spoleto (possibly at the instigation of King <a href="/wiki/Berengar_I_of_Italy" title="Berengar I of Italy">Berengar I</a>) and sets himself up as duke.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Britain">Britain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=897&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Britain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>English warships (nine vessels from Alfred's new fleet) intercept six Viking <a href="/wiki/Longship" title="Longship">longships</a> in the mouth of an unknown <a href="/wiki/Estuary" title="Estuary">estuary</a> on the south coast (possibly at <a href="/wiki/Poole_Harbour" title="Poole Harbour">Poole Harbour</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Danes" title="Danes">Danes</a> are blockaded, and three ships attempt to break through the English lines. Lashing the <a href="/wiki/Vikings" title="Vikings">Viking</a> boats to their own, the English crew board the enemy's vessels and kill everyone on board. Some ships manage to escape, two of the other three boats where driven against the <a href="/wiki/Sussex" title="Sussex">Sussex</a> coast. The <a href="/wiki/Shipwreck" title="Shipwreck">shipwrecked</a> sailors are brought before King <a href="/wiki/Alfred_the_Great" title="Alfred the Great">Alfred the Great</a> at <a href="/wiki/Winchester" title="Winchester">Winchester</a> and hanged. Just one Viking ship returns to <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_East_Anglia" title="Kingdom of East Anglia">East Anglia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Arabian_Empire">Arabian Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=897&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Arabian Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Caliph <a href="/wiki/Al-Mu%27tadid" title="Al-Mu'tadid">Al-Mu'tadid</a> recovers control of the <a href="/wiki/Cilicia" title="Cilicia">Cilician</a> <a href="/wiki/Al-%27Awasim" title="Al-'Awasim">Thughur</a> (Southern <a href="/wiki/Anatolia" title="Anatolia">Anatolia</a>) and of northern <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>, during the turmoil in the <a href="/wiki/Tulunids" title="Tulunids">Tulunid</a> government (approximate date).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Al-Hadi_ila%27l-Haqq_Yahya" title="Al-Hadi ila'l-Haqq Yahya">Al-Hadi ila'l-Haqq Yahya</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Arabs" title="Arabs">Arab</a> religious and political leader, becomes the first <a href="/wiki/Zaidiyyah" title="Zaidiyyah">Zaidiyyah</a> imam who rules over portions of <a href="/wiki/Yemen" title="Yemen">Yemen</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Japan">Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=897&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Emperor <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Uda" title="Emperor Uda">Uda</a> abdicates the <a href="/wiki/Throne" title="Throne">throne</a> after a 10-year reign. He is succeeded by his 12-year-old son <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Daigo" title="Emperor Daigo">Daigo</a>, as the 60th <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Japan" title="Emperor of Japan">emperor of Japan</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_topic">By topic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=897&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: By topic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=897&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>January – The <a href="/wiki/Cadaver_Synod" title="Cadaver Synod">Cadaver Synod</a>: Lambert II orders Stephen VI to exhume the nine-month-old cadaver of former pope <a href="/wiki/Pope_Formosus" title="Pope Formosus">Formosus</a>, to redress in papal robes and placed in a chair on trail at <a href="/wiki/Old_St._Peter%27s_Basilica" title="Old St. Peter's Basilica">St. Peter's</a>. Formosus is 'convicted' of several crimes, his fingers of <a href="/wiki/Consecration" title="Consecration">consecration</a> are cut off, and the body is stripped of his <a href="/wiki/Pontifical_vestments" title="Pontifical vestments">vestments</a>.</li>
<li>August – Stephen VI is removed from office, imprisoned and <a href="/wiki/Strangling" title="Strangling">strangled</a> in his cell. He is succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Romanus" title="Pope Romanus">Romanus</a> as the 114th <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">pope</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a>.</li>
<li>December – Romanus is deposed and succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Theodore_II" title="Pope Theodore II">Theodore II</a> as the 115th pope of Rome, but dies twenty days later.</li>
</ul>
