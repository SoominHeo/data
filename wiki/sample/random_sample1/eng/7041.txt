<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Congress of Vienna</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>

<p>The <b>Congress of Vienna</b> (German: <i>Wiener Kongress</i>) was a conference of ambassadors of European states chaired by <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Empire" title="Austrian Empire">Austrian</a> statesman <a href="/wiki/Klemens_von_Metternich" title="Klemens von Metternich">Klemens von Metternich</a>, and held in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> from November 1814 to June 1815, though the delegates had arrived and were already negotiating by late September 1814. The objective of the Congress was to provide a long-term peace plan for Europe by settling critical issues arising from the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolutionary_Wars" title="French Revolutionary Wars">French Revolutionary Wars</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars" title="Napoleonic Wars">Napoleonic Wars</a>. The goal was not simply to restore old boundaries but to resize the main powers so they could balance each other off and remain at peace. The leaders were conservatives with little use for republicanism or revolution, both of which threatened to upset the status quo in Europe. France lost all its recent conquests, while Prussia, Austria and Russia made major territorial gains. Prussia added smaller German states in the west, <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Pomerania" title="Swedish Pomerania">Swedish Pomerania</a> and 60% of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Saxony" title="Kingdom of Saxony">Kingdom of Saxony</a>; Austria gained <a href="/wiki/Venice" title="Venice">Venice</a> and much of northern Italy. Russia gained <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Warsaw" title="Duchy of Warsaw">parts of Poland</a>. The new <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Kingdom of the Netherlands">Kingdom of the Netherlands</a> had been created just months before, and included formerly Austrian territory that in 1830 became <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>.</p>

<p>The immediate background was <a href="/wiki/First_French_Empire" title="First French Empire">Napoleonic France's</a> defeat and <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1814)" title="Treaty of Paris (1814)">surrender in May 1814</a>, which brought an end to twenty-five years of nearly continuous war. Negotiations continued despite the outbreak of fighting triggered by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_I_of_France" title="Napoleon I of France">Napoleon's</a> dramatic return from exile and resumption of power in France during the <a href="/wiki/Hundred_Days" title="Hundred Days">Hundred Days</a> of March–July 1815. The Congress's "Final Act" was signed nine days before his final defeat at Waterloo on 18 June 1815.</p>
<p>The Congress has often been criticized for causing the subsequent suppression of the emerging national and liberal movements,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Olson-Shadle_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Olson-Shadle-1">[1]</a></sup> and it has been seen as a reactionary movement for the benefit of traditional monarchs. However, others praise it for having created relatively long-term stable and peaceful conditions in most of Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jarrett_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jarrett-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schroeder_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Schroeder-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a technical sense, the "Congress of Vienna" was not properly a Congress: it never met in <a href="/wiki/Plenary_session" title="Plenary session">plenary session</a>, and most of the discussions occurred in informal, face-to-face, sessions among the <a href="/wiki/Great_power" title="Great power">Great Powers</a> of Austria, Britain, France, Russia, and sometimes Prussia, with limited or no participation by other delegates. On the other hand, the Congress was the first occasion in history where, on a continental scale, national representatives came together to formulate treaties, instead of relying mostly on messages between the several capitals. The Congress of Vienna settlement, despite later changes, formed the framework for European international politics until the outbreak of the <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">First World War</a> in 1914.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preliminaries">Preliminaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Congress_of_Vienna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Preliminaries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Chaumont" title="Treaty of Chaumont">Treaty of Chaumont</a> in 1814 had reaffirmed decisions that had been made already and which would be ratified by the more important Congress of Vienna of 1814–15. They included the establishment of a confederated Germany, the division of Italy into independent states, the restoration of the Bourbon kings of Spain, and the enlargement of the Netherlands to include what in 1830 became modern Belgium. The Treaty of Chaumont became the cornerstone of the European Alliance which formed the balance of power for decades.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Other partial settlements had already occurred at the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1814)" title="Treaty of Paris (1814)">Treaty of Paris</a> between France and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sixth_Coalition" title="Sixth Coalition">Sixth Coalition</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Kiel" title="Treaty of Kiel">Treaty of Kiel</a> which covered issues raised regarding <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a>. The Treaty of Paris had determined that a "general congress" should be held in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>, and that invitations would be issued to "all the Powers engaged on either side in the present war."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The opening was scheduled for July 1814.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>The Congress functioned through formal meetings such as working groups and official diplomatic functions; however, a large portion of the Congress was conducted informally at salons, banquets, and balls.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Four_Great_Powers_and_Bourbon_France">Four Great Powers and Bourbon France</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Congress_of_Vienna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Four Great Powers and Bourbon France">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Four Great Powers had previously formed the core of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sixth_Coalition" title="Sixth Coalition">Sixth Coalition</a>. On the verge of Napoleon's defeat they had outlined their common position in the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Chaumont" title="Treaty of Chaumont">Treaty of Chaumont</a> (March 1814), and negotiated the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1814)" title="Treaty of Paris (1814)">Treaty of Paris (1814)</a> with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bourbon_dynasty" title="Bourbon dynasty">Bourbons</a> during <a href="/wiki/Bourbon_Restoration" title="Bourbon Restoration">their restoration</a>:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Austrian_Empire" title="Austrian Empire">Austria</a> was represented by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince_Klemens_Wenzel_von_Metternich" title="Prince Klemens Wenzel von Metternich">Prince Metternich</a>, the Foreign Minister, and by his deputy, <a href="/wiki/Baron_Johann_von_Wessenberg-Ampringen" title="Baron Johann von Wessenberg-Ampringen">Baron Johann von Wessenberg</a>. As the Congress's sessions were in Vienna, Emperor <a href="/wiki/Francis_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Francis II, Holy Roman Emperor">Francis</a> was kept closely informed.</li>
<li>Great Britain was represented first by its <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Foreign_and_Commonwealth_Affairs" title="Secretary of State for Foreign and Commonwealth Affairs">Foreign Secretary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Stewart,_Viscount_Castlereagh" title="Robert Stewart, Viscount Castlereagh">Viscount Castlereagh</a>; then by the <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Wellesley,_1st_Duke_of_Wellington" title="Arthur Wellesley, 1st Duke of Wellington">Duke of Wellington</a>, after Castlereagh's return to England in February 1815. In the last weeks it was headed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Richard_Le_Poer_Trench,_2nd_Earl_of_Clancarty" title="Richard Le Poer Trench, 2nd Earl of Clancarty">Earl of Clancarty</a>, after Wellington left to face Napoleon during the <a href="/wiki/Hundred_Days" title="Hundred Days">Hundred Days</a>.</li>
<li>Tsar <a href="/wiki/Alexander_I_of_Russia" title="Alexander I of Russia">Alexander I</a> controlled the Russian delegation which was formally led by the foreign minister, Count <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karl_Robert_Nesselrode" title="Karl Robert Nesselrode">Karl Robert Nesselrode</a>. The tsar had two main goals, to gain control of Poland and to promote the peaceful coexistence of European nations. He succeeded in forming the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Alliance" title="Holy Alliance">Holy Alliance</a> (1815), based on monarchism and anti-secularism, and formed to combat any threat of revolution or republicanism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>Prussia was represented by Prince <a href="/wiki/Karl_August_von_Hardenberg" title="Karl August von Hardenberg">Karl August von Hardenberg</a>, the Chancellor, and the diplomat and scholar <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_von_Humboldt" title="Wilhelm von Humboldt">Wilhelm von Humboldt</a>. King <a href="/wiki/Frederick_William_III_of_Prussia" title="Frederick William III of Prussia">Frederick William III of Prussia</a> was also in Vienna, playing his role behind the scenes.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bourbon_Restoration" title="Bourbon Restoration">France</a>, the "fifth" power, was represented by its foreign minister, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Maurice_de_Talleyrand-P%C3%A9rigord" title="Charles Maurice de Talleyrand-Périgord">Talleyrand</a> as well as the Minister Plenipotentiary the Duke of Dalberg. Talleyrand had already negotiated the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1814)" title="Treaty of Paris (1814)">Treaty of Paris (1814)</a> for <a href="/wiki/Louis_XVIII_of_France" title="Louis XVIII of France">Louis XVIII of France</a>; the king, however, distrusted him and was also secretly negotiating with Metternich, by mail.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Malettke66_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Malettke66-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_signatories_of_the_Treaty_of_Paris.2C_1814">Other signatories of the Treaty of Paris, 1814</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Congress_of_Vienna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other signatories of the Treaty of Paris, 1814">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>These parties had not been part of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Chaumont" title="Treaty of Chaumont">Chaumont agreement</a>, but had joined the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1814)" title="Treaty of Paris (1814)">Treaty of Paris (1814)</a>:</p>

<li>Spain – Marquis <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pedro_G%C3%B3mez_Labrador,_Marquis_of_Labrador" title="Pedro Gómez Labrador, Marquis of Labrador">Pedro Gómez de Labrador</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Portugal" title="Kingdom of Portugal">Kingdom of Portugal and the Algarves</a> – Plenipotentiaries: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pedro_de_Sousa_Holstein" title="Pedro de Sousa Holstein">Pedro de Sousa Holstein, Count of Palmela</a>; <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_de_Saldanha_da_Gama,_Count_of_Porto_Santo" title="António de Saldanha da Gama, Count of Porto Santo">António de Saldanha da Gama, Count of Porto Santo</a>; Joaquim Lobo da Silveira.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>

<ul>

<li>The Netherlands – <a href="/wiki/Richard_Trench,_2nd_Earl_of_Clancarty" title="Richard Trench, 2nd Earl of Clancarty">Earl of Clancarty</a>, the British Ambassador at the Dutch court,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and Baron <a href="/wiki/Hans_Christoph_Ernst_von_Gagern" title="Hans Christoph Ernst von Gagern">Hans von Gagern</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> – Every <a href="/wiki/Cantons_of_Switzerland" title="Cantons of Switzerland">canton</a> had its own delegation. <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pictet_de_Rochemont" title="Charles Pictet de Rochemont">Charles Pictet de Rochemont</a> from <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a> played a prominent role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Papal_States" title="Papal States">Papal States</a> – Cardinal <a href="/wiki/Ercole_Consalvi" title="Ercole Consalvi">Ercole Consalvi</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></li>

<li>On German issues,
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bavaria" title="Kingdom of Bavaria">Bavaria</a> – <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_von_Montgelas" title="Maximilian von Montgelas">Maximilian Graf von Montgelas</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Kingdom of Württemberg">Württemberg</a> – <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Georg_Ernst_Levin_von_Wintzingerode&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Georg Ernst Levin von Wintzingerode (page does not exist)">Georg Ernst Levin von Wintzingerode</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georg_Ernst_Levin_von_Wintzingerode" title="de:Georg Ernst Levin von Wintzingerode">de</a>)</span></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Brunswick-L%C3%BCneburg" title="Duchy of Brunswick-Lüneburg">Hanover</a>, then in a personal union with the British crown – <a href="/wiki/George_III_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="George III of the United Kingdom">Georg Graf zu Münster</a>. (King <a href="/wiki/George_III_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="George III of the United Kingdom">George III</a> had refused to recognize the dissolution of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a> in 1806 and maintained a separate diplomatic staff as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elector_of_Hanover" title="Elector of Hanover">Elector of Hanover</a> to conduct the affairs of the family estate, the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Brunswick-L%C3%BCneburg" title="Duchy of Brunswick-Lüneburg">Duchy of Brunswick-Lüneburg</a>, until the results of the Congress were concluded establishing the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hanover" title="Kingdom of Hanover">Kingdom of Hanover</a>.)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Mecklenburg-Schwerin" title="Grand Duchy of Mecklenburg-Schwerin">Mecklenburg-Schwerin</a> – <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Leopold_von_Plessen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Leopold von Plessen (page does not exist)">Leopold von Plessen</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leopold_von_Plessen_(Minister)" title="de:Leopold von Plessen (Minister)">de</a>)</span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Virtually every state in Europe had a delegation in Vienna – more than 200 states and princely houses were represented at the Congress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In addition, there were representatives of cities, corporations, religious organizations (for instance, abbeys) and special interest groups – e.g., a delegation representing German publishers, demanding a copyright law and freedom of the press.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The Congress was noted for its lavish entertainment: according to a famous joke it did not move, but danced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Talleyrand.27s_role">Talleyrand's role</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Congress_of_Vienna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Talleyrand's role">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Talleyrand_02.jpg"><img alt="oil painting of Tallyrand, the French ambassador" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="417" data-file-width="320" height="287" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Talleyrand_02.jpg/220px-Talleyrand_02.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/40/Talleyrand_02.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Talleyrand_02.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Charles_Maurice_de_Talleyrand-P%C3%A9rigord" title="Charles Maurice de Talleyrand-Périgord">Talleyrand</a> proved an able negotiator for the defeated French.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Initially, the representatives of the four victorious powers hoped to exclude the French from serious participation in the negotiations, but Talleyrand skillfully managed to insert himself into "her inner councils" in the first weeks of negotiations. He allied himself to a Committee of Eight lesser powers (including Spain, Sweden, and Portugal) to control the negotiations. Once Talleyrand was able to use this committee to make himself a part of the inner negotiations, he then left it,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> once again abandoning his allies.</p>
<p>The major Allies' indecision on how to conduct their affairs without provoking a united protest from the lesser powers led to the calling of a preliminary conference on protocol, to which Talleyrand and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pedro_G%C3%B3mez_Labrador,_Marquis_of_Labrador" title="Pedro Gómez Labrador, Marquis of Labrador">Marquis of Labrador</a>, Spain's representative, were invited on 30 September 1814.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nicolson_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nicolson-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Congress Secretary <a href="/wiki/Friedrich_von_Gentz" title="Friedrich von Gentz">Friedrich von Gentz</a> reported, "The intervention of Talleyrand and Labrador has hopelessly upset all our plans. Talleyrand protested against the procedure we have adopted and soundly [be]rated us for two hours. It was a scene I shall never forget."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The embarrassed representatives of the Allies replied that the document concerning the protocol they had arranged actually meant nothing. "If it means so little, why did you sign it?" snapped Labrador.</p>
<p>Talleyrand's policy, directed as much by national as personal ambitions, demanded the close but by no means amicable relationship he had with Labrador, whom Talleyrand regarded with disdain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Labrador later remarked of Talleyrand: "that cripple, unfortunately, is going to Vienna."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Talleyrand skirted additional articles suggested by Labrador: he had no intention of handing over the 12,000 <i>afrancesados</i> – Spanish fugitives, sympathetic to France, who had sworn fealty to <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Bonaparte" title="Joseph Bonaparte">Joseph Bonaparte</a>, nor the bulk of the documents, paintings, pieces of fine art, and books that had been looted from the archives, palaces, churches and cathedrals of Spain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Polish-Saxon_crisis">Polish-Saxon crisis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Congress_of_Vienna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Polish-Saxon crisis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The most dangerous topic at the Congress was the so-called Polish-Saxon Crisis. Russia wanted most of Poland, and Prussia wanted all of Saxony, whose king had allied with Napoleon. The tsar would become king of Poland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Austria was fearful this would make Russia much too powerful, a view which was supported by Britain. The result was deadlock, for which Talleyrand proposed a solution: Admit France to the inner circle, and France would support Austria and Britain. The three nations signed a secret treaty on 3 January 1815, agreeing to go to war against Russia and Prussia, if necessary, to prevent the Russo-Prussian plan from coming to fruition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nicolson_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nicolson-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>When the tsar heard of the secret treaty he agreed to a compromise that satisfied all parties on 24 October 1815. Russia received most of the Napoleonic <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Warsaw" title="Duchy of Warsaw">Duchy of Warsaw</a> as a "Kingdom of Poland" – called <a href="/wiki/Congress_Poland" title="Congress Poland">Congress Poland</a>, with the tsar as king ruling it independently of Russia. Russia, however, did not receive the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Posen" title="Province of Posen">province of Posen</a> (Poznań), which was given to Prussia as the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Posen" title="Grand Duchy of Posen">Grand Duchy of Posen</a>, nor <a href="/wiki/Krak%C3%B3w" title="Kraków">Kraków</a>, which became a <a href="/wiki/Free_City_of_Krak%C3%B3w" title="Free City of Kraków">free city</a>. Furthermore, the tsar was unable to unite the new domain with the parts of Poland that had been incorporated into Russia in the 1790s. Prussia received 40 percent of Saxony-later known as the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Saxony" title="Province of Saxony">Province of Saxony</a>, with the remainder returned to King <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Augustus_I_of_Saxony" title="Frederick Augustus I of Saxony">Frederick Augustus I</a> as his <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Saxony" title="Kingdom of Saxony">Kingdom of Saxony</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Final_Act">Final Act</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Congress_of_Vienna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Final Act">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Gains_territoriaux_de_la_France_en_1814.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1898" data-file-width="2000" height="209" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Gains_territoriaux_de_la_France_en_1814.svg/220px-Gains_territoriaux_de_la_France_en_1814.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Gains_territoriaux_de_la_France_en_1814.svg/330px-Gains_territoriaux_de_la_France_en_1814.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Gains_territoriaux_de_la_France_en_1814.svg/440px-Gains_territoriaux_de_la_France_en_1814.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Gains_territoriaux_de_la_France_en_1814.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
In pink, territories left to France in 1814 but removed after the <a href="/wiki/Hundred_Days" title="Hundred Days">Hundred Days</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Final Act, embodying all the separate treaties, was signed on 9 June 1815 (a few days before the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Waterloo" title="Battle of Waterloo">Battle of Waterloo</a>). Its provisions included:</p>
<ul>
<li>Russia was given most of the Duchy of Warsaw (Poland) and was allowed to keep <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Finland" title="Grand Duchy of Finland">Finland</a> (which it had annexed from Sweden in 1809 and held until 1917).</li>
<li>Prussia was given three fifths of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Saxony" title="Kingdom of Saxony">Saxony</a>, parts of the Duchy of Warsaw (the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Posen" title="Grand Duchy of Posen">Grand Duchy of Posen</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Danzig" title="Danzig">Danzig</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Rhineland" title="Rhineland">Rhineland</a>/<a href="/wiki/Westphalia" title="Westphalia">Westphalia</a>.</li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/German_Confederation" title="German Confederation">German Confederation</a> of 38 states was created from the previous 360 of the Holy Roman Empire, under the presidency of the Austrian Emperor. Only portions of the territory of Austria and Prussia were included in the Confederation.</li>
<li>The Netherlands and the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Netherlands" title="Southern Netherlands">Southern Netherlands</a> (approx. modern-day Belgium) were united in a monarchy, the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="United Kingdom of the Netherlands">United Kingdom of the Netherlands</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Orange-Nassau" title="House of Orange-Nassau">House of Orange-Nassau</a> providing the king (the <a href="/wiki/Eight_Articles_of_London" title="Eight Articles of London">Eight Articles of London</a>).</li>
<li>To compensate for the Orange-Nassau's loss of the Nassau lands to Prussia, the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="United Kingdom of the Netherlands">United Kingdom of the Netherlands</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Luxembourg" title="Grand Duchy of Luxembourg">Grand Duchy of Luxembourg</a> were to form a <a href="/wiki/Personal_union" title="Personal union">personal union</a> under the House of Orange-Nassau, with Luxembourg (but not the Netherlands) inside the <a href="/wiki/German_Confederation" title="German Confederation">German Confederation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Swedish_Pomerania" title="Swedish Pomerania">Swedish Pomerania</a>, given to Denmark a year earlier in return for <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, was ceded by Denmark to Prussia. <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> received back <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</a> from Sweden in return for <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe_Fund" title="Guadeloupe Fund">yearly installments</a> to the Swedish king.</li>
<li>The neutrality of <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> was guaranteed.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hanover" title="Kingdom of Hanover">Hanover</a> gave up the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Lauenburg" title="Duchy of Lauenburg">Duchy of Lauenburg</a> to Denmark, but was enlarged by the addition of former territories of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bishop_of_M%C3%BCnster" title="Bishop of Münster">Bishop of Münster</a> and by the formerly Prussian <a href="/wiki/East_Frisia" title="East Frisia">East Frisia</a>, and made a kingdom.</li>
<li>Most of the territorial gains of <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Württemberg">Württemberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baden" title="Baden">Baden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Hesse" title="Grand Duchy of Hesse">Hesse-Darmstadt</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Nassau" title="Duchy of Nassau">Nassau</a> under the <a href="/wiki/German_mediatization" title="German mediatization">mediatizations</a> of 1801–1806 were recognized. Bavaria also gained control of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palatinate_region" title="Palatinate region">Rhenish Palatinate</a> and parts of the Napoleonic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duchy_of_W%C3%BCrzburg" title="Duchy of Würzburg">Duchy of Würzburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Frankfurt" title="Grand Duchy of Frankfurt">Grand Duchy of Frankfurt</a>. Hesse-Darmstadt, in exchange for giving up the Duchy of Westphalia to Prussia, received <a href="/wiki/Rhenish_Hesse" title="Rhenish Hesse">Rhenish Hesse</a> with its capital at <a href="/wiki/Mainz" title="Mainz">Mainz</a>.</li>
<li>Austria regained control of the <a href="/wiki/County_of_Tyrol" title="County of Tyrol">Tyrol</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salzburg" title="Salzburg">Salzburg</a>; of the former <a href="/wiki/Illyrian_Provinces" title="Illyrian Provinces">Illyrian Provinces</a>; of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tarnopol" title="Tarnopol">Tarnopol</a> district (from Russia); received <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Lombardy-Venetia" title="Kingdom of Lombardy-Venetia">Lombardy-Venetia</a> in Italy and <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ragusa" title="Republic of Ragusa">Ragusa</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dalmatia" title="Dalmatia">Dalmatia</a>. Former Austrian territory in Southwest Germany remained under the control of Württemberg and Baden, and the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Netherlands" title="Southern Netherlands">Austrian Netherlands</a> were also not recovered.</li>
<li>Habsburg princes were returned to control of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Tuscany" title="Grand Duchy of Tuscany">Grand Duchy of Tuscany</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Modena" title="Duchy of Modena">Duchy of Modena</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Papal_States" title="Papal States">Papal States</a> were under the rule of the pope and restored to their former extent, with the exception of <a href="/wiki/Avignon" title="Avignon">Avignon</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Comtat_Venaissin" title="Comtat Venaissin">Comtat Venaissin</a>, which remained part of France.</li>
<li>Britain was confirmed in control of the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Colony" title="Cape Colony">Cape Colony</a> in Southern Africa; <a href="/wiki/Tobago" title="Tobago">Tobago</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ceylon" title="Ceylon">Ceylon</a>; and various other colonies in Africa and Asia. Other colonies, most notably the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Dutch East Indies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a>, were restored to their previous owners.</li>
<li>The King of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sardinia" title="Kingdom of Sardinia">Sardinia</a> was restored in <a href="/wiki/Piedmont" title="Piedmont">Piedmont</a>, Nice, and <a href="/wiki/Savoy" title="Savoy">Savoy</a>, and was given control of <a href="/wiki/Genoa" title="Genoa">Genoa</a> (putting an end to the brief proclamation of a restored <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Genoa" title="Republic of Genoa">Republic</a>).</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Parma" title="Duchy of Parma">Duchies of Parma, Piacenza and Guastalla</a> were given to <a href="/wiki/Marie_Louise,_Duchess_of_Parma" title="Marie Louise, Duchess of Parma">Marie Louise</a>, Napoleon's wife.</li>
<li>The Duchy of Lucca was created for the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Bourbon-Parma" title="House of Bourbon-Parma">House of Bourbon-Parma</a>, which would have reversionary rights to Parma after the death of <a href="/wiki/Marie_Louise,_Duchess_of_Parma" title="Marie Louise, Duchess of Parma">Marie Louise</a>.</li>
<li>The Bourbon <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_I_of_the_Two_Sicilies" title="Ferdinand I of the Two Sicilies">Ferdinand IV</a>, King of Sicily was restored to control of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Naples" title="Kingdom of Naples">Kingdom of Naples</a> after <a href="/wiki/Joachim_Murat" title="Joachim Murat">Joachim Murat</a>, the king installed by Bonaparte, supported Napoleon in the <a href="/wiki/Hundred_Days" title="Hundred Days">Hundred Days</a> and started the <a href="/wiki/Neapolitan_War" title="Neapolitan War">Neapolitan War</a> by attacking Austria.</li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slave_trade" title="Slave trade">slave trade</a> was condemned.</li>
<li>Freedom of navigation was guaranteed for many rivers, notably <a href="/wiki/Central_Commission_for_Navigation_on_the_Rhine" title="Central Commission for Navigation on the Rhine">the Rhine</a> and the Danube.</li>
</ul>
<p>The Final Act was signed by representatives of Austria, France, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Portugal" title="Kingdom of Portugal">Portugal</a>, Prussia, Russia, <a href="/wiki/Union_between_Sweden_and_Norway" title="Union between Sweden and Norway">Sweden-Norway</a>, and Britain. Spain did not sign the treaty but ratified it in 1817.</p>

<p>The Congress's principal results, apart from its confirmation of France's loss of the territories annexed between 1795–1810, which had already been settled by the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1814)" title="Treaty of Paris (1814)">Treaty of Paris</a>, were the enlargement of Russia, (which gained most of the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Warsaw" title="Duchy of Warsaw">Duchy of Warsaw</a>) and Prussia, which acquired the district of Poznań, Swedish Pomerania, Westphalia and the northern Rhineland. The consolidation of Germany from the nearly 300 states of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a> (dissolved in 1806) into a much less complex system of thirty-nine states (4 of which were free cities) was confirmed. These states formed a loose <a href="/wiki/German_Confederation" title="German Confederation">German Confederation</a> under the leadership of Austria and Prussia.</p>
<p>Representatives at the Congress agreed to numerous other territorial changes. By the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Kiel" title="Treaty of Kiel">Treaty of Kiel</a>, Norway had been ceded by the king of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Denmark-Norway" title="Denmark-Norway">Denmark-Norway</a> to the king of <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>. This sparked the nationalist movement which led to the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Norway_(1814)" title="Kingdom of Norway (1814)">Kingdom of Norway</a> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norway_in_1814" title="Norway in 1814">May 17, 1814</a> and the subsequent personal <a href="/wiki/Union_between_Sweden_and_Norway" title="Union between Sweden and Norway">Union</a> with <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>. Austria gained <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Lombardy-Venetia" title="Kingdom of Lombardy-Venetia">Lombardy-Venetia</a> in Northern Italy, while much of the rest of North-Central Italy went to Habsburg dynasties (the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Tuscany" title="Grand Duchy of Tuscany">Grand Duchy of Tuscany</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Modena" title="Duchy of Modena">Duchy of Modena</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Parma" title="Duchy of Parma">Duchy of Parma</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stearns-Langer_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stearns-Langer-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Papal_States" title="Papal States">Papal States</a> were restored to the Pope. The Kingdom of Piedmont-Sardinia was restored to its mainland possessions, and also gained control of the Republic of <a href="/wiki/Genoa" title="Genoa">Genoa</a>. In Southern Italy, Napoleon's brother-in-law, <a href="/wiki/Joachim_Murat" title="Joachim Murat">Joachim Murat</a>, was originally allowed to retain his <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Naples" title="Kingdom of Naples">Kingdom of Naples</a>, but his support of Napoleon in the <a href="/wiki/Hundred_Days" title="Hundred Days">Hundred Days</a> led to the restoration of the Bourbon <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ferdinand_IV_of_Naples" title="Ferdinand IV of Naples">Ferdinand IV</a> to the throne.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stearns-Langer_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stearns-Langer-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>A large <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="United Kingdom of the Netherlands">United Kingdom of the Netherlands</a> was formed for the <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Orange" title="Prince of Orange">Prince of Orange</a>, including both the old <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">United Provinces</a> and the formerly Austrian-ruled territories in the Southern Netherlands. Other, less important, territorial adjustments included significant territorial gains for the German Kingdoms of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hanover" title="Kingdom of Hanover">Hanover</a> (which gained <a href="/wiki/East_Frisia" title="East Frisia">East Frisia</a> from Prussia and various other territories in Northwest Germany) and <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bavaria" title="Kingdom of Bavaria">Bavaria</a> (which gained the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palatinate_region" title="Palatinate region">Rhenish Palatinate</a> and territories in <a href="/wiki/Franconia" title="Franconia">Franconia</a>). The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Lauenburg" title="Duchy of Lauenburg">Duchy of Lauenburg</a> was transferred from Hanover to Denmark, and Prussia annexed <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Pomerania" title="Swedish Pomerania">Swedish Pomerania</a>. <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> was enlarged, and Swiss neutrality was established. Swiss mercenaries had played a significant role in European wars for a couple of hundred years: the Congress intended to put a stop to these activities permanently.</p>
<p>During the wars, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> had lost its town of <a href="/wiki/Olivenza" title="Olivenza">Olivença</a> to Spain and moved to have it restored. Portugal is historically Britain's oldest ally, and with British support succeeded in having the re-incorporation of <a href="/wiki/Olivenza" title="Olivenza">Olivença</a> decreed in Article 105 of the Final Act, which stated that the Congress "understood the occupation of Olivença to be illegal and recognized Portugal's rights". Portugal ratified the Final Act in 1815 but Spain would not sign, and this became the most important hold-out against the Congress of Vienna. Deciding in the end that it was better to become part of Europe than to stand alone, Spain finally accepted the Treaty on 7 May 1817; however, <a href="/wiki/Olivenza" title="Olivenza">Olivença</a> and its surroundings were never returned to Portuguese control and this question remains unresolved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="Quotation needed from source to verify. (January 2016)">need quotation to verify</span></a></i>]</sup> Great Britain received parts of the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a> at the expense of the Netherlands and Spain and kept the former Dutch colonies of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ceylon" title="Ceylon">Ceylon</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Colony" title="Cape Colony">Cape Colony</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heligoland" title="Heligoland">Heligoland</a>. Under the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1815)" title="Treaty of Paris (1815)">Treaty of Paris</a>, Britain obtained a protectorate over the <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_the_Ionian_Islands" title="United States of the Ionian Islands">United States of the Ionian Islands</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Seychelles" title="Seychelles">Seychelles</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_criticism">Later criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Congress_of_Vienna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Later criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Congress of Vienna was frequently criticized by nineteenth-century and more recent historians for ignoring national and liberal impulses, and for imposing a stifling <a href="/wiki/Reactionary" title="Reactionary">reaction</a> on the Continent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Olson-Shadle_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Olson-Shadle-1">[1]</a></sup> It was an integral part in what became known as the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Order" title="Conservative Order">Conservative Order</a>, in which the liberties and civil rights associated with the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolutions</a> were de-emphasized, so that a fair balance of power, peace and stability, might be achieved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Olson-Shadle_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Olson-Shadle-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 20th century, however, many historians came to admire the statesmen at the Congress, whose work prevented another widespread European war for nearly a hundred years (1815–1914). Among these is <a href="/wiki/Henry_Kissinger" title="Henry Kissinger">Henry Kissinger</a>, who in 1954 wrote his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctoral_dissertation" title="Doctoral dissertation">doctoral dissertation</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/A_World_Restored" title="A World Restored">A World Restored</a></i>, on it. Historian Mark Jarrett argues that the Congress of Vienna and the Congress System marked "the true beginning of our modern era." He says the Congress System was deliberate conflict management, and was the first genuine attempt to create an international order based upon consensus rather than conflict. "Europe was ready," Jarrett states, "to accept an unprecedented degree of international cooperation in response to the French Revolution."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jarrett_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Jarrett-2">[2]</a></sup> Historian Paul Schroeder argues that the old formulae for "<a href="/wiki/Balance_of_power_(international_relations)" title="Balance of power (international relations)">balance of power</a>" were in fact highly destabilizing and predatory. He says the Congress of Vienna avoided them and instead set up rules that produced a stable and benign equilibrium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schroeder_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Schroeder-3">[3]</a></sup> The Congress of Vienna was the first of a series of international meetings that came to be known as the <a href="/wiki/Concert_of_Europe" title="Concert of Europe">Concert of Europe</a>, which was an attempt to forge a peaceful <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Balance_of_power_in_international_relations" title="Balance of power in international relations">balance of power</a> in Europe. It served as a model for later organizations such as the <a href="/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a> in 1919 and the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> in 1945.</p>
<p>Prior to the opening of the Paris peace conference of 1918, the British Foreign Office commissioned a history of the Congress of Vienna to serve as an example to its own delegates of how to achieve an equally successful peace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Besides, the main decisions of the Congress were made by the Four Great Powers and not all the countries of Europe could extend their rights at the Congress. The Italian peninsula became a mere "geographical expression" as divided into seven parts: <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Lombardy%E2%80%93Venetia" title="Kingdom of Lombardy–Venetia">Lombardy–Venetia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Modena_and_Reggio" title="Duchy of Modena and Reggio">Modena</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Two_Sicilies" title="Kingdom of Two Sicilies">Naples–Sicily</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Parma" title="Duchy of Parma">Parma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sardinia" title="Kingdom of Sardinia">Piedmont–Sardinia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Tuscany" title="Grand Duchy of Tuscany">Tuscany</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Papal_States" title="Papal States">Papal States</a> under the control of different powers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a> remained partitioned between Russia, Prussia and Austria, with the largest part, the newly created <a href="/wiki/Congress_Poland" title="Congress Poland">Kingdom of Poland</a>, remaining under Russian control.</p>
<p>The arrangements made by the Four Great Powers sought to ensure future disputes would be settled in a manner that would avoid the terrible wars of the previous twenty years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Although the Congress of Vienna preserved the balance of power in Europe, it could not check the spread of revolutionary movements across the continent <a href="/wiki/Revolutions_of_1848" title="Revolutions of 1848">some 30 years later</a>.</p>
