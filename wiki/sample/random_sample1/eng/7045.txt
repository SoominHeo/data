<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Go_(board_game)" title="Go (board game)">game of Go</a>, the <b>empty triangle</b> is the most fundamental example of the concept of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bad_shape" title="Bad shape">bad shape</a>.</p>
<p>Three stones of one color form an empty triangle when they are placed in a triangle arrangement that fits in a 2×2 square, and when one intersection is left empty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> If the triangle is <i>filled</i> by a stone of the opponent's at the fourth point of the 2×2, the shape is neutral – not necessarily good or bad.</p>
<p>The deficiencies of the empty triangle are twofold. Three stones in a straight line have eight <a href="/wiki/List_of_Go_terms#Liberty" title="List of Go terms">liberties</a>, while in an empty triangle they have only seven. This can mean the difference between success and failure in a life-and-death struggle. Also the formation lacks efficiency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OH1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-OH1-2">[2]</a></sup> In the case cited, the diagonally adjacent stones are tactically connected without the third stone, since the opponent can't prevent them from connecting unless he is ignored for a turn.</p>
<p>However even though the empty triangle is a prime example of bad shape, creating one could make sense, or even qualify as brilliant, in certain situations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>The Empty Triangle</i> is also the name of a popular series of comic panels about the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Go_(board_game)" title="Go (board game)">game of Go</a> that features personalities from the <a href="/wiki/KGS_Go_Server" title="KGS Go Server">KGS Go Server</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
