<p><b>Vincenzo Montella</b> (<small>Italian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Italian" title="Help:IPA for Italian">[vinˈtʃɛntso monˈtɛlla]</a></span>; born 18 June 1974) is a retired Italian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who is currently the manager of <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a>.</p>
<p>Montella's nickname during his playing career was <i>L'Aeroplanino</i> ("The Little Airplane"), in reference to his small stature and trademark goal celebration, in which he spread his arms like wings. He was known as a quick, hard-working, and opportunistic <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>, with an eye for goal, and has also been described as a "fine all round player, with excellent passing and <a href="/wiki/Dribbling#Association_football" title="Dribbling">dribbling</a> skills."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Montella is remembered for his performances with <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Roma</a> (1999–2007), winning the <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> title and the <a href="/wiki/2001_Supercoppa_Italiana" title="2001 Supercoppa Italiana">Supercoppa Italiana</a> with the club during the <a href="/wiki/2000%E2%80%9301_Serie_A" title="2000–01 Serie A">2000–01 season</a>, also later reaching the <a href="/wiki/2003_Coppa_Italia_Final" title="2003 Coppa Italia Final">2003 Coppa Italia Final</a>. In 2013, Montella was inducted into the A.S. Roma Hall of Fame.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> At international level, he was notably a member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_national_football_team" title="Italian national football team">Italian team</a> that reached the final of <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2000" title="UEFA Euro 2000">UEFA Euro 2000</a>, and he also represented his country at the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 FIFA World Cup</a>.</p>
<p>Montella began his managerial career as Roma's caretaker manager in 2011, later coaching <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catania_calcio" title="Catania calcio">Catania</a> the following season. In 2012, he moved to Fiorentina, where he spent three seasons, leading the club to three consecutive fourth-place league finishes, the <a href="/wiki/2014_Coppa_Italia_Final" title="2014 Coppa Italia Final">2014 Coppa Italia Final</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> semi-finals in <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2014–15 UEFA Europa League">2015</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vincenzo_Montella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vincenzo_Montella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Montella began his club career in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C1" title="Serie C1">Serie C1</a> at <a href="/wiki/Empoli_F.C." title="Empoli F.C.">Empoli</a> in 1990 before moving to <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> club <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a> in 1995, where he scored 21 goals in his only season with the club, at the end of which he lifted the <a href="/wiki/1995%E2%80%9396_Anglo-Italian_Cup" title="1995–96 Anglo-Italian Cup">Anglo-Italian Cup</a>. He then moved up to <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> to <a href="/wiki/Derby_della_Lanterna" title="Derby della Lanterna">city rivals</a> <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">U.C. Sampdoria</a>, where he remained three years, until 1999, when he moved to Roma in a 50 billion <a href="/wiki/Italian_lira" title="Italian lira">lire</a> (about €25.823 million) transfer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Montella made his Serie A debut on 8 September 1996, against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perugia_Calcio" title="Perugia Calcio">Perugia</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roma">Roma</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vincenzo_Montella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roma">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It was Roma coach <a href="/wiki/Zden%C4%9Bk_Zeman" title="Zdeněk Zeman">Zdeněk Zeman</a> that wanted him to spearhead the Roma attack, but that year Roma also signed a new coach, <a href="/wiki/Fabio_Capello" title="Fabio Capello">Fabio Capello</a>, who disliked short forwards. Despite this, during the <a href="/wiki/1999%E2%80%932000_Serie_A" title="1999–2000 Serie A">1999–2000 season</a>, Montella scored 18 goals, being the topscorer of the <i>giallorossi</i>, playing alongside <a href="/wiki/Marco_Delvecchio" title="Marco Delvecchio">Marco Delvecchio</a>, in front of advanced playmaker <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Totti" title="Francesco Totti">Francesco Totti</a>. The following year, Roma signed the Argentinian forward <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Batistuta" title="Gabriel Batistuta">Gabriel Batistuta</a> from <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">ACF Fiorentina</a>, the tall striker wanted by Capello.</p>
<p>There was a slight controversy between the two forwards concerning the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Squad_number#Football" title="Squad number">jersey #9</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> that was the number of both players; in the end Batistuta chose #18, although "Batigol" was often the coach's first choice, while Montella was usually deployed as a substitute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Despite his lack of starts he was one of the main protagonists of Roma's third <a href="/wiki/Scudetto" title="Scudetto">Scudetto</a> in <a href="/wiki/2000%E2%80%9301_Serie_A" title="2000–01 Serie A">2000–01</a> and scored many important goals for the team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The controversies between Capello and Montella continued in the following years as Montella was unhappy at his lack of first team opportunities, and in March 2002 he made his sadness clear stating "<i>I would have better expectations with another coach</i>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Despite his struggle to gain a starting spot during his time with Roma under Capello, he became a club idol to the supporters, as he had a knack for performing well in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rome_derby" title="Rome derby">Rome derby</a> against <a href="/wiki/S.S._Lazio" title="S.S. Lazio">S.S. Lazio</a>, scoring a brace in his first derby in 1999, and four goals in a 5–1 win on 10 March 2002;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> His four-goal haul is still the record of goals scored in a derby match by a single player.</p>
<p>2003 was a difficult year for Montella, as he divorced from his former wife, Rita,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and was plagued with many injuries. During the <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_Serie_A" title="2003–04 Serie A">2003–04 season</a>, he played only 12 games but still managed a good scoring record, scoring six goals, despite his limited playing time.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_Serie_A" title="2004–05 Serie A">2004–05 season</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fabio_Capello" title="Fabio Capello">Fabio Capello</a> finally left Roma for <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> and, while the <i>giallorossi</i> had a disastrous season, Montella scored 23 goals and earned a new contract lasting until 30 June 2010, also helping Roma to the <a href="/wiki/2005_Coppa_Italia_Final" title="2005 Coppa Italia Final">Coppa Italia final</a> that season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Serie_A" title="2005–06 Serie A">2005–06 season</a>, he was again plagued with injuries. He underwent surgery on both his back and shoulder, limiting him to just twelve games. In January 2007, during the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Serie_A" title="2006–07 Serie A">2006–07 season</a> he was loaned to <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a> because Francesco Totti was the lone forward in <a href="/wiki/Luciano_Spalletti" title="Luciano Spalletti">Luciano Spalletti</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#4.E2.80.932.E2.80.933.E2.80.931" title="Formation (association football)">4–2–3–1</a>, while Montella wanted to have more chances to play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the yellow and red Roma jersey, he played a total of 215 matches in eight years, scoring 94 goals, becoming the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AS_Roma_statistics#Top_15_all-time_goalscorers" title="AS Roma statistics">fifth best topscorer of Roma's history</a>.</p>
<p>He played his last game for Roma on 23 December 2006 before leaving on loan to England.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_to_Fulham">Loan to Fulham</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vincenzo_Montella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Loan to Fulham">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Montella joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fulham_FC" title="Fulham FC">Fulham</a> on a six-month loan on 4 January 2007 and was given the number 11 shirt. He scored twice against <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a> on his home debut in the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He scored again on 27 January in the same competition against <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 13 January 2007, he made his <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> debut against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_Ham_United" title="West Ham United">West Ham United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tactical_Formation_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tactical_Formation-15">[15]</a></sup> Against Tottenham at <a href="/wiki/Craven_Cottage" title="Craven Cottage">Craven Cottage</a> seven days later, he scored his first Premier League goal with a penalty kick.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He also scored a goal against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers" title="Blackburn Rovers">Blackburn Rovers</a> in a 1–1 draw which gave Fulham the boost to avoid relegation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Montella quickly became popular with the Fulham fans and showed his gratitude for making him feel at home at Craven Cottage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> However, he hardly started under manager Chris Coleman, despite several public pleas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> After Coleman's sacking, Montella only started twice under replacement manager <a href="/wiki/Lawrie_Sanchez" title="Lawrie Sanchez">Lawrie Sanchez</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tactical_Formation_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tactical_Formation-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Montella hinted that he would like to return to Roma after the end of the 2006–07 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The loan was terminated by Sanchez on 8 May 2007, a week before its natural expiration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_to_Sampdoria_and_return">Loan to Sampdoria and return</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vincenzo_Montella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Loan to Sampdoria and return">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>He was loaned to <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">Sampdoria</a> for the 2007–08 season. <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Conti" title="Bruno Conti">Bruno Conti</a> brought Montella back to Roma in 2008–09, after <a href="/wiki/Mancini_(Brazilian_footballer)" title="Mancini (Brazilian footballer)">Amantino Mancini</a> had left Roma. Montella took number 23 when he came back; <a href="/wiki/Mirko_Vu%C4%8Dini%C4%87" title="Mirko Vučinić">Mirko Vučinić</a> held on the number 9 shirt which was formerly Montella's, and Montella took the number 23 shirt which was formerly for Vučinić.</p>
<p>Montella was only able to make substitute appearances for Roma in the 2008–09 season. He played his last game on 16 May 2009.</p>
<p>On 2 July 2009, Montella announced his retirement from professional football as a player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vincenzo_Montella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Montella received his first international <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">cap</a> under <a href="/wiki/Dino_Zoff" title="Dino Zoff">Dino Zoff</a> in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2000_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2000 qualifying">UEFA Euro 2000 qualifying</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_football_team" title="Wales national football team">Wales</a> on 5 June 1999, which Italy won 4–0, and he was part of the final 22-man <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italian squad</a> that took part at <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2000" title="UEFA Euro 2000">Euro 2000</a>, where they reached the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Although Montella did not score during the competition, he recorded an assist in Italy's final group match against <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_football_team" title="Sweden national football team">Sweden</a>, setting up <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Del_Piero" title="Alessandro Del Piero">Del Piero</a>'s match-winning goal, which allowed Italy to top their group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> He would also make one more appearance during the tournament, in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2000_Final" title="UEFA Euro 2000 Final">final</a> defeat against <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> in extra-time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> He scored his first goal for Italy in an international friendly match against <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_football_team" title="South Africa national football team">South Africa</a> in Perugia, on the 25 April 2001, which ended in a 1–0 home win for Italy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> In March 2002, he scored a notable double against <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> in an international friendly match in Leeds, giving Italy a 2–1 away victory after they had been trailing 1–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> After appearing for Italy under <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Trapattoni" title="Giovanni Trapattoni">Giovanni Trapattoni</a> during their <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2002 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">2002 FIFA World Cup qualifying</a> campaign, he was chosen as a member of the Italy team that would be competing at the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 World Cup</a>, where the Italians were eliminated controversially in the round of 16 to co-hosts <a href="/wiki/South_Korea_national_football_team" title="South Korea national football team">South Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-La_storia_dei_Mondiali_-_2002_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-La_storia_dei_Mondiali_-_2002-31">[31]</a></sup> In his only World Cup appearance, during Italy's final group stage match against <a href="/wiki/Mexico_national_football_team" title="Mexico national football team">Mexico</a> in the 2002 World Cup, he had a goal wrongly disallowed. He later set up <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Del_Piero" title="Alessandro Del Piero">Del Piero</a>'s equaliser, which helped Italy to progress to the second round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-La_storia_dei_Mondiali_-_2002_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-La_storia_dei_Mondiali_-_2002-31">[31]</a></sup> He was set to come in the round of 16 match, but South Korea scored the golden goal moments before he was set to come on.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-La_storia_dei_Mondiali_-_2002_31-2"><a href="#cite_note-La_storia_dei_Mondiali_-_2002-31">[31]</a></sup> He also appeared for Italy in 3 <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2004_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2004 qualifying">UEFA Euro 2004 qualifying</a> matches, and he made his final appearance for Italy in an international friendly match against <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_football_team" title="Russia national football team">Russia</a> on the 9 February 2005, which Italy won 2–0. In total, Montella holds 20 caps and 3 goals for Italy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roma_2">Roma</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vincenzo_Montella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Roma">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Montella signed a three-year contract with Roma as youth team coach for the <i>Giovanissimi Nazionali</i> (Under-15 level).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 21 February 2011, he was appointed as interim head coach of the first team for the remainder of the season, taking over the role from resigning boss <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Ranieri" title="Claudio Ranieri">Claudio Ranieri</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-montella_coach_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-montella_coach-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 23 February 2011, in his first game in <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a>, <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Roma</a> won away beating <a href="/wiki/Bologna_F.C._1909" title="Bologna F.C. 1909">Bologna</a> 1–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 8 March 2011, in his debut in <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a>, <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Roma</a> lost away beaten by <a href="/wiki/FC_Shakhtar_Donetsk" title="FC Shakhtar Donetsk">Shakhtar Donetsk</a> 3–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> He completed an eventful season for AS Roma leading the <i>Giallorossi</i> to sixth place in the league table, and he was subsequently released after the new club owners led by U.S. businessman <a href="/wiki/Thomas_R._DiBenedetto" title="Thomas R. DiBenedetto">Thomas R. DiBenedetto</a> decided to appoint <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luis_Enrique_Mart%C3%ADnez_Garc%C3%ADa" title="Luis Enrique Martínez García">Luis Enrique</a> as permanent head coach for the 2011–12 season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Catania">Catania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vincenzo_Montella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Catania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 9 June 2011, and only a few days after being released by Roma, Montella was announced as new boss of <a href="/wiki/Calcio_Catania" title="Calcio Catania">Catania</a>, signing a two-year contract with the club from Eastern Sicily.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-montella_catania_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-montella_catania-38">[38]</a></sup> He led Catania to a mid-table placement, and ahead of Sicilian rivals Palermo for the first time in eight years; by the end of season, he was heavily linked with a comeback at Roma and the vacant job at Fiorentina. Montella left Catania by mutual consent on 4 June 2012, after only one season in charge of the team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>

<p>On 11 June 2012, <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a> announced on their official website that Montella signed a two-year-deal (later prolonged until June 2017) with the Tuscan outfit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Montella's arrival saw a massive upheaval of the squad which saw 17 (including <a href="/wiki/Borja_Valero" title="Borja Valero">Borja Valero</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Aquilani" title="Alberto Aquilani">Alberto Aquilani</a>) out of the 26 senior players being new to the club. Fiorentina finished the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Serie_A" title="2012–13 Serie A">2012–13 Serie A</a> in 4th place, missing out on a Champions League spot to <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a> on the final day of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>In his second season in charge, Montella led Fiorentina to the <a href="/wiki/2014_Coppa_Italia_Final" title="2014 Coppa Italia Final">2014 Coppa Italia Final</a> but they were beaten 3–1 by <a href="/wiki/S.S.C._Napoli" title="S.S.C. Napoli">Napoli</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> whilst the team finished the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Serie_A" title="2013–14 Serie A">league season</a> in fourth place once again. In his third season, Montella helped Fiorentina reach the semi-final of the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2014–15 UEFA Europa League">2014–15 UEFA Europa League</a>, where they were eliminated by defending champions and eventual winners <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla</a>. Fiorentina finished the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Serie_A" title="2014–15 Serie A">league season</a> in fourth place for the third consecutive season. On 8 June, he was sacked as Fiorentina head coach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sampdoria">Sampdoria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vincenzo_Montella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sampdoria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 15 November 2015, <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">Sampdoria</a> announced Montella was appointed new head coach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 28 June 2016, Montella left <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">Sampdoria</a> to join <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Milan">Milan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vincenzo_Montella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Milan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 28 June 2016, <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a> announced Montella as their new head coach with a contract starting from 1 July. He signed a two-year deal, reportedly worth €2.3 million per year, while Sampdoria was paid €500,000 as a compensation to release him from his former contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 23 December 2016, Montella led the "rossoneri" to their first title since 2011, the <a href="/wiki/2016_Supercoppa_Italiana" title="2016 Supercoppa Italiana">Supercoppa Italiana</a>, defeating <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> 4–3 in a penalty shootout after a 1–1 draw following extra time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
