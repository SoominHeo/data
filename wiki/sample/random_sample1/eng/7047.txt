<p><b>Eros Airport</b> or <b>Windhoek Eros Airport</b> (<a href="/wiki/International_Air_Transport_Association_airport_code" title="International Air Transport Association airport code">IATA</a>: <b>ERS</b>, <a href="/wiki/International_Civil_Aviation_Organization_airport_code" title="International Civil Aviation Organization airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>FYWE</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/Airport" title="Airport">airport</a> serving <a href="/wiki/Windhoek" title="Windhoek">Windhoek</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NAC_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NAC-2">[2]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital</a> and largest <a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_and_towns_in_Namibia" title="List of cities and towns in Namibia">city</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Republic of Namibia</a>. It is located in the <a href="/wiki/Khomas_Region" title="Khomas Region">Khomas Region</a>, about 5 km (3 mi) south of Windhoek's <a href="/wiki/Central_business_district" title="Central business district">central business district</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NAC_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-NAC-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eros_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Eros is a busy hub of general aviation and one of the busiest<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> airports in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_African_Development_Community" title="South African Development Community">SADC</a> region. It is the host to commercial, private, and scheduled traffic ranging from high performance <a href="/wiki/Jet_aircraft" title="Jet aircraft">jet aircraft</a> to <a href="/wiki/Cessna_152" title="Cessna 152">Cessna 152</a> trainers. The majority of traffic comes from the general aviation charter market, consisting mainly of the <a href="/wiki/Cessna_210" title="Cessna 210">Cessna 210</a> aircraft, which is the most commonly used aircraft for charter and fly-in safaris in Namibia. The airport handles approximately 150 to 200 movements per day (around 50,000 per year). In 2004, the airport served 141,605 passengers.</p>
<p>Eros is an alternate for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Windhoek_International_Airport" title="Windhoek International Airport">Windhoek International Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Visual_meteorological_conditions" title="Visual meteorological conditions">VMC</a> conditions<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. The largest passenger aircraft to land at Eros was a Boeing 737 on a diversion from Windhoek International due to strong crosswinds and windshear<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accident">Accident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eros_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Accident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January 2008, a Cessna 210 crashed after take off killing the pilot and all 6 tourists on board. The accident was ruled to be caused by pilot error, according to the Directorate of Aircraft Accident Investigations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eros_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The airport resides at an <a href="/wiki/Elevation" title="Elevation">elevation</a> of 5,575 feet (1,699 m) above <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mean_sea_level" title="Mean sea level">mean sea level</a>. It has two <a href="/wiki/Asphalt" title="Asphalt">asphalt</a> paved <a href="/wiki/Runway" title="Runway">runways</a>: 01/19 is the primary runway measuring 2,229 by 30 metres (7,313 ft × 98 ft)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NAC_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-NAC-2">[2]</a></sup> and 09/27 is the secondary runway measuring 1,005 m × 30 m (3,297 ft × 98 ft).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WAD_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-WAD-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
