<p><b>Adelino William ("Billy") Gonsalves</b> (sometimes spelt in the Portuguese form, <i>Gonçalves</i>) (August 10, 1908 in <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth,_Rhode_Island" title="Portsmouth, Rhode Island">Portsmouth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a> – July 17, 1977 in <a href="/wiki/Kearny,_New_Jersey" title="Kearny, New Jersey">Kearny</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>) was an American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soccer" title="Soccer">soccer</a> player, sometimes described as the "<a href="/wiki/Babe_Ruth" title="Babe Ruth">Babe Ruth</a> of American Soccer".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He spent over 25 years playing in various American professional leagues and was a member of the U.S. squad at the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/1930_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1930 FIFA World Cup">1930</a> and <a href="/wiki/1934_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1934 FIFA World Cup">1934</a>. He is a member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Soccer_Hall_of_Fame" title="National Soccer Hall of Fame">National Soccer Hall of Fame</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth">Youth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Billy_Gonsalves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Youth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gonsalves was the seventh of nine children born to Augustine and Rose Gonsalves. His parents had immigrated from <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> two years before his birth. Although born in <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth,_Rhode_Island" title="Portsmouth, Rhode Island">Portsmouth, Rhode Island</a>, Gonsalves grew up in <a href="/wiki/Fall_River,_Massachusetts" title="Fall River, Massachusetts">Fall River, Massachusetts</a>. An excellent athlete, Gonsalves boxed and played both <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> and soccer as a boy. When he was fourteen, Gonsalves began playing for Pioneer, a local amateur team. He then went on to play for Charlton Mill and Liberal. Gonsalves grew in prominence in the local leagues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1926, he signed with Lusitania Recreation Club of <a href="/wiki/East_Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="East Cambridge, Massachusetts">East Cambridge, Massachusetts</a>. In 1927, Lusitania won both the Boston city and District League titles.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Billy_Gonsalves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1927, <a href="/wiki/Boston_Soccer_Club" title="Boston Soccer Club">Boston Soccer Club</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Soccer_League_(1921-1933)" title="American Soccer League (1921-1933)">American Soccer League</a> (ASL) signed Gonsalves. Nineteen years old at the time, Gonsalves joined a team which had taken second in the ASL the previous season and was stocked with talented international players. Finding it difficult to break into the starting lineup, Gonsalves did not play a game with Boston until Christmas Eve. However, given the chance, he grabbed it and scored a goal two minutes later. He went on to find the net six times in his next twenty games. That season the Wonder Workers also took the league title.</p>
<p>Gonsalves spent one more season with the Wonder Workers before moving to the <a href="/wiki/Fall_River_Marksmen" title="Fall River Marksmen">Fall River Marksmen</a> of his hometown in 1929. From the time he joined the Marksmen until the team merged with the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Soccer_Club" title="New York Soccer Club">New York Soccer Club</a> in the summer of 1930 to form the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees_(soccer)" title="New York Yankees (soccer)">New York Yankees</a>, Gonsalves played seventy-five games and scored forty-nine goals. Additionally, he was the team’s assists leader.</p>
<p>While playing with the Marksmen, Gonsalves formed a lethal striking partnership with another local player, <a href="/wiki/Bert_Patenaude" title="Bert Patenaude">Bert Patenaude</a>. The two led Fall River to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Challenge_Cup" title="National Challenge Cup">National Challenge Cup</a> title in 1930 and 1931. Overall, Gonslaves won the tournament a record eight times.</p>
<p>The onset of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a> and the <i>Soccer Wars</i> had taken a toll on the ASL and teams began to merge or fold. The Marksmen, one of the strongest ASL teams, had merged with the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Soccer_Club" title="New York Soccer Club">New York Soccer Club</a> to form the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees_(soccer)" title="New York Yankees (soccer)">New York Yankees</a> in 1930. Then in 1931, the Yankees moved to <a href="/wiki/New_Bedford,_Massachusetts" title="New Bedford, Massachusetts">New Bedford, Massachusetts</a> where the team took up the name of the defunct <a href="/wiki/New_Bedford_Whalers" title="New Bedford Whalers">New Bedford Whalers</a>. In 1932, the team moved, this time back to Fall River where it was known as <a href="/wiki/Fall_River_F.C." title="Fall River F.C.">Fall River F.C.</a>.</p>
<p>By this time the American Soccer League was collapsing and Gonsalves began looking for other playing opportunities. In 1933 <a href="/wiki/Alex_McNab" title="Alex McNab">Alex McNab</a>, a former team-mate who was coaching <a href="/wiki/Stix,_Baer_and_Fuller_F.C." title="Stix, Baer and Fuller F.C.">Stix, Baer and Fuller F.C.</a> of the <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Soccer_League" title="St. Louis Soccer League">St. Louis Soccer League</a> contacted Gonsalves and offered him a contract. Gonsalves took the opportunity and moved west. That year, Gonsalves added another National Cup title to his resume, this time with his new team. Gonsalves spent the 1933-1934 season with Stix, Baer and Fuller, winning the league title. At the end of the season, he joined the U.S. national team for the <a href="/wiki/1934_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1934 FIFA World Cup">1934 FIFA World Cup</a> in Italy.</p>
<p>On his return to St. Louis, Gonsalves found that McNab had moved to a new team, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Louis_Central_Breweries_F.C." title="St. Louis Central Breweries F.C.">St. Louis Central Breweries F.C.</a>. Gonsalves made the move as well and spent the 1934-1935 season with Central Breweries, winning the league title and the 1935 National Cup. In 1935, Gonsalves moved, this time to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Louis_Shamrocks_(1935-1938)" title="St. Louis Shamrocks (1935-1938)">St. Louis Shamrocks</a>. The Shamrocks went to both the 1936 and 1937 National Cup championships, but lost both times. In October 1937, Gonsalves quit the Shamrocks and signed with St. Patrick's. However, Shamrocks sued St. Patrick's and St. Patrick's settled out of court by agreeing to a player trade. Despite the agreement, Gonsalves decided not to play for either team and instead signed with Beltmars in the semi-professional second division St. Louis Municipal League. In February 1938, Gonsalves was back in the SLSL with <a href="/wiki/South_Side_Radio" title="South Side Radio">South Side Radio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He finished the season with the South Siders before moving to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chicago_Manhattan_Beer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chicago Manhattan Beer (page does not exist)">Chicago Manhattan Beer</a>. Then in 1940, he moved back east, joining <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Healy_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Healy F.C. (page does not exist)">Healy F.C.</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Soccer_League_of_New_York" title="National Soccer League of New York">National Soccer League of New York</a>, winning the 1941 league title. In 1941, he moved, this time to the <a href="/wiki/Kearny_Scots" title="Kearny Scots">Kearny Scots</a> of the second <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Soccer_League_(1933-1983)" title="American Soccer League (1933-1983)">American Soccer League</a> (ASL) which was created in 1933 following the collapse of the first ASL. Gonsalves spent only one season with the Scots before moving to <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Hispano" title="Brooklyn Hispano">Brooklyn Hispano</a>. Uncharacteristically for him, Gonsalves spent the next five seasons with Brooklyn. In his first season with Brooklyn, he scored eight goals in sixteen games. Brooklyn also won the National Challenge Cup in 1943 and 1944.</p>
<p>In 1947, Gonsalves left Hispano to play for the <a href="/wiki/Newark_Germans" title="Newark Germans">Newark Germans</a> of the lower division <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_American_Soccer_League" title="German American Soccer League">German American Soccer League</a>. In 1948, the team became known simply as Newark F.C. Gonsalves remained with the team until his retirement from playing professionally in 1952.</p>
<p>According to Steve Holroyd, in a professional career spanning twenty-five years, “Gonsalves was also the consummate gentleman on the pitch: legend has it he was never cautioned or ejected from any match for rough play or ungentlemanly conduct.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_team">National team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Billy_Gonsalves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: National team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gonsalves played six times for the <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States men's national soccer team">United States</a>, including the first two <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cups</a> in <a href="/wiki/1930_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1930 FIFA World Cup">1930</a> and <a href="/wiki/1934_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1934 FIFA World Cup">1934</a>. He scored one goal for the US.</p>
<p>Gonsalves was part of the inaugural induction class into the United States <a href="/wiki/National_Soccer_Hall_of_Fame" title="National Soccer Hall of Fame">National Soccer Hall of Fame</a> in 1950.</p>
