<p><b>William Eugene</b> "<b>Billy</b>" <b>Burrows, Jr.</b> (born September 18, 1949), better known by his stage name <b>Billy Drago</b>, is an American actor known for his roles as <a href="/wiki/Villain" title="Villain">villains</a> in television and motion pictures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Among his more well known films are <a href="/wiki/Clint_Eastwood" title="Clint Eastwood">Clint Eastwood</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_film" title="Western film">western</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Pale_Rider" title="Pale Rider">Pale Rider</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Brian_De_Palma" title="Brian De Palma">Brian De Palma</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Untouchables_(film)" title="The Untouchables (film)">The Untouchables</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Billy_Drago&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born William Eugene Burrows, Jr. in <a href="/wiki/Hugoton,_Kansas" title="Hugoton, Kansas">Hugoton, Kansas</a>, The son of actor/director William Eugene Burrows, Sr. (1914–1999) and Gladys Burrows (1917–1993), William,Jr. took his grandmother's maiden name as his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> to keep from being confused with another actor. His father was of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chiricahua_Apache" title="Chiricahua Apache">Chiricahua Apache</a> ancestry and his mother <a href="/wiki/Romani_people" title="Romani people">Romany</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> After high school he worked as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stuntman" title="Stuntman">stuntman</a> at <a href="/wiki/Boot_Hill" title="Boot Hill">Boot Hill</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dodge_City,_Kansas" title="Dodge City, Kansas">Dodge City, Kansas</a>. From there he went on to the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Kansas" title="University of Kansas">University of Kansas</a>. After finishing college he was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio_host" title="Radio host">radio host</a> for a time before joining an acting company that took him first to <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> then <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VOID_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-VOID-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Drago is the father of actor <a href="/wiki/Darren_E._Burrows" title="Darren E. Burrows">Darren E. Burrows</a>. He was married to actress <a href="/wiki/Silvana_Gallardo" title="Silvana Gallardo">Silvana Gallardo</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Billy_Drago&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Drago began his acting career in 1979. His early films include <i>No Other Love</i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Windwalker_(1981_film)" title="Windwalker (1981 film)">Windwalker</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Bridges" title="Jeff Bridges">Jeff Bridges</a> vehicle <i><a href="/wiki/Cutter%27s_Way" title="Cutter's Way">Cutter's Way</a></i>. He went on to guest star in numerous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a> including: <i><a href="/wiki/Hill_Street_Blues" title="Hill Street Blues">Hill Street Blues</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Moonlighting_(TV_series)" title="Moonlighting (TV series)">Moonlighting</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walker_Texas_Ranger" title="Walker Texas Ranger">Walker Texas Ranger</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Trapper_John,_M.D." title="Trapper John, M.D.">Trapper John, M.D.</a></i>.</p>
<p>Drago is perhaps best known for the role of <a href="/wiki/Al_Capone" title="Al Capone">Al Capone</a>'s henchman <a href="/wiki/Frank_Nitti" title="Frank Nitti">Frank Nitti</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brian_De_Palma" title="Brian De Palma">Brian De Palma</a>'s 1987 film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Untouchables_(1987_film)" title="The Untouchables (1987 film)">The Untouchables</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup> Since that point he has appeared in numerous films and <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> roles; the latter list includes <i><a href="/wiki/The_Adventures_of_Brisco_County,_Jr." title="The Adventures of Brisco County, Jr.">The Adventures of Brisco County, Jr.</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_X-Files" title="The X-Files">The X-Files</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 1999 he played the demon <a href="/wiki/Barbas_(Charmed)" title="Barbas (Charmed)">Barbas</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_WB_Television_Network" title="The WB Television Network">WB</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Charmed" title="Charmed">Charmed</a></i>. His character was supposed to appear in only one episode, but due to popularity with fans he was brought back for six additional episodes, appearing in five of the show's eight seasons.</p>
<p>His ongoing film resume includes <i><a href="/wiki/Delta_Force_2:_The_Colombian_Connection" title="Delta Force 2: The Colombian Connection">Delta Force 2</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Tremors_4:_The_Legend_Begins" title="Tremors 4: The Legend Begins">Tremors 4: The Legend Begins</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Pale_Rider" title="Pale Rider">Pale Rider</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup> He also was featured in <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>'s 2001 music video "<a href="/wiki/You_Rock_My_World" title="You Rock My World">You Rock My World</a>". Drago also played a mysterious stranger who gave a boy a special key in the <a href="/wiki/Mike_%2B_The_Mechanics" title="Mike + The Mechanics">Mike + The Mechanics</a> music video for "<a href="/wiki/Silent_Running_(On_Dangerous_Ground)" title="Silent Running (On Dangerous Ground)">Silent Running (On Dangerous Ground)</a>" in 1985.</p>
<p>Other film roles include <a href="/wiki/Gregg_Araki" title="Gregg Araki">Gregg Araki</a>'s 2004 film <i><a href="/wiki/Mysterious_Skin" title="Mysterious Skin">Mysterious Skin</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup> the remake of horror film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hills_Have_Eyes_(2006_film)" title="The Hills Have Eyes (2006 film)">The Hills Have Eyes</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Children_of_the_Corn:_Genesis" title="Children of the Corn: Genesis">Children of the Corn: Genesis</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Takashi_Miike" title="Takashi Miike">Takashi Miike</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Masters_of_Horror" title="Masters of Horror">Masters of Horror</a></i> episode <i><a href="/wiki/Imprint_(Masters_of_Horror)" title="Imprint (Masters of Horror)">Imprint</a></i>, which <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a> pulled from the air due to "disturbing content."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VOID_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-VOID-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
