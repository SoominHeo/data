<p>In the 1582 <b>siege of Takamatsu</b> (備中高松城の戦い), <a href="/wiki/Toyotomi_Hideyoshi" title="Toyotomi Hideyoshi">Toyotomi Hideyoshi</a> laid siege to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Takamatsu_Castle_(Bitchu)" title="Takamatsu Castle (Bitchu)">Takamatsu Castle</a>, which was controlled by the <a href="/wiki/M%C5%8Dri_clan" title="Mōri clan">Mōri clan</a>. He diverted a nearby river with dikes to surround and flood the castle, leading to a relatively speedy surrender. He also constructed towers on barges from which his arquebusiers could keep up a constant rate of fire and be unhindered themselves by the flooding. As the battle grew more intense, the garrison received reinforcements from the Mōri, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kikkawa_family" title="Kikkawa family">Kikkawa</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kobayakawa_family" title="Kobayakawa family">Kobayakawa</a> clans, and Hideyoshi sent for aid from his lord <a href="/wiki/Oda_Nobunaga" title="Oda Nobunaga">Oda Nobunaga</a>. In response, Nobunaga sent a contingent of men west, to make their way to Takamatsu, while he himself stopped at the <a href="/wiki/Honn%C5%8D-ji" title="Honnō-ji">Honnō-ji</a> for a time; during this stay, he would be betrayed and killed by <a href="/wiki/Akechi_Mitsuhide" title="Akechi Mitsuhide">Akechi Mitsuhide</a>.</p>
<p>Hideyoshi soon learned of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Incident_at_Honn%C5%8Dji" title="Incident at Honnōji">death</a> of his lord, which encouraged him to hurry the arrangement of surrender terms. <a href="/wiki/Shimizu_Muneharu" title="Shimizu Muneharu">Shimizu Muneharu</a>, the castle's commander, was forced to commit suicide in a boat on the artificial lake created by the flooding, in full view.</p>
