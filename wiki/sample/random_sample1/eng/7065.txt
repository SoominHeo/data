<p><b>Frozen dessert</b> is the generic name for <a href="/wiki/Dessert" title="Dessert">desserts</a> made by freezing liquids, semi-solids, and sometimes even solids. They may be based on flavored water (<a href="/wiki/Shave_ice" title="Shave ice">shave ice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ice_pop" title="Ice pop">ice pops</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sorbet" title="Sorbet">sorbet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snow_cone" title="Snow cone">snow cones</a>, <i>etc.</i>), on fruit purées (such as <a href="/wiki/Sorbet" title="Sorbet">sorbet</a>), on <a href="/wiki/Milk" title="Milk">milk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cream" title="Cream">cream</a> (most <a href="/wiki/Ice_cream" title="Ice cream">ice creams</a>), on <a href="/wiki/Custard" title="Custard">custard</a> (<a href="/wiki/Frozen_custard" title="Frozen custard">frozen custard</a> and some ice creams), on <a href="/wiki/Mousse" title="Mousse">mousse</a> (<a href="/wiki/Semifreddo" title="Semifreddo">semifreddo</a>), and others.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and elsewhere, the term is often used on imitations of ice cream which do not satisfy its legal definition (<i>e.g.</i>, <a href="/wiki/Mellorine" title="Mellorine">mellorine</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
