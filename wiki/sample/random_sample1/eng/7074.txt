<p><b>Pattani</b> (<a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>: <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">ปัตตานี</span>, <small>pronounced</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Thai_and_Lao" title="Help:IPA for Thai and Lao">[pàt.tāː.nīː]</a></span>) is one of the southern <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Thailand" title="Provinces of Thailand">provinces</a> (<i>changwat</i>) of <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>. Neighboring provinces are (from southeast clockwise) <a href="/wiki/Narathiwat_Province" title="Narathiwat Province">Narathiwat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yala_Province" title="Yala Province">Yala</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Songkhla_Province" title="Songkhla Province">Songkhla</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pattani_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name <i>Pattani</i> is the <a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a> adaptation of the Malay name <b>Patani</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jawi_script" title="Jawi script">Jawi</a>: ڤتاني), which can mean "this beach" in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pattani_Malay" title="Pattani Malay">Patani Malay</a> language. (In standard Malay, this would be <i>pantai ini</i>.) Another suggestion is that it derives from a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sanskrit_language" title="Sanskrit language">Sanskrit</a> word <i>pathini</i>, meaning "virgin nymph"; <i>Pathini</i> was the name of a daughter of <a href="/wiki/Merong_Mahawangsa" title="Merong Mahawangsa">Merong Mahawangsa</a>, founder of the preceding <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Langkasuka_Empire" title="Langkasuka Empire">Langkasuka Empire</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Historically, Pattani Province was the centre of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malay_people" title="Malay people">Malay</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patani_kingdom" title="Patani kingdom">Sultanate of Patani Darul Makrif</a>. For centuries a tributary state of Siam, Pattani has been governed by Siam since its conquest in 1785. Siamese rule was officially acknowledged by the <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Siamese_Treaty_of_1909" title="Anglo-Siamese Treaty of 1909">Anglo-Siamese Treaty of 1909</a> negotiated with the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a>. Both Yala (Jala) and Narathiwat (Menara) were originally part of Patani, but were made provinces of their own during the territorial administrative reform and the creation of a united centralized Siam state.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pattani_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pattani is one of the four provinces of Thailand where the majority of the population are <a href="/wiki/Malays_(ethnic_group)" title="Malays (ethnic group)">Malay</a> <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a>. They make up about 88 percent of its population. The people speak the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yawi_language" title="Yawi language">Patani Malay</a> language, although most also speak Thai. The Pattani Malays are similar in ethnicity and culture to the Malays of <a href="/wiki/Kelantan" title="Kelantan">Kelantan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pattani_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pattani is on the Malay Peninsula, with the coast of the Gulf of Thailand to the north. The south is dominated by the Sankalakhiri mountain range, which includes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Budo-Su-ngai_Padi_National_Park" title="Budo-Su-ngai Padi National Park">Budo-Su-ngai Padi National Park</a>, on the border with <a href="/wiki/Yala_Province" title="Yala Province">Yala</a> and <a href="/wiki/Narathiwat_Province" title="Narathiwat Province">Narathiwat</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Symbols">Symbols</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pattani_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Symbols">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seals_of_The_Provinces_of_Thailand" title="Seals of The Provinces of Thailand">seal of the province</a> shows the cannon called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phraya_Tani" title="Phraya Tani">Phraya Tani</a>, known as <i>Sri Pattani</i> in Malay, which was cast in Pattani Province. It was brought to Bangkok in 1785, and is now on display in front of the Ministry of Defence in Bangkok.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The provincial flower is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_hibiscus" title="Chinese hibiscus">Chinese hibiscus</a> (<i>Hibiscus rosa-sinensis</i>), and the provincial tree the <a href="/wiki/Ironwood" title="Ironwood">Ironwood</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Hopea_odorata" title="Hopea odorata">Hopea odorata</a></i>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pattani_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Administrative divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pattani is divided into 12 districts (<i><a href="/wiki/Amphoe" title="Amphoe">amphoe</a></i>), which are further subdivided into 115 communes (<i><a href="/wiki/Tambon" title="Tambon">tambon</a></i>) and 629 villages (<i><a href="/wiki/Muban" title="Muban">muban</a></i>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The districts of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amphoe_Chana" title="Amphoe Chana">Chana</a> (Malay: <i>Chenok</i>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amphoe_Thepha" title="Amphoe Thepha">Thepa</a> (Malay:<i>Tiba</i>) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amphoe_Saba_Yoi" title="Amphoe Saba Yoi">Saba Yoi</a> (Malay:<i>Sebayu</i>) were detached from Pattani and transferred to <a href="/wiki/Songkhla_Province" title="Songkhla Province">Songkhla</a> in recent times by the Thai government.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
