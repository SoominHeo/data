<p>The <b>Type 92 Heavy Machine Gun</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">九二式重機関銃</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kyūni-shiki jū-kikanjū</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> entered service in 1932 and was the standard <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Heavy_machine_gun" title="Heavy machine gun">heavy machine gun</a> used during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. It was used extensively by the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Army" title="Imperial Japanese Army">Imperial Japanese Army</a> and <a href="/wiki/Collaborationist_Chinese_Army" title="Collaborationist Chinese Army">Collaborationist Chinese forces</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Korea_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Korea-1">[1]</a></sup> Captured weapons were also used extensively by Chinese <a href="/wiki/National_Revolutionary_Army" title="National Revolutionary Army">National Revolutionary Army</a> troops against the Japanese during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, and the North Korean army against the UN forces during the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Korea_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Korea-1">[1]</a></sup> The Type 92 refers to the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_era_name#Imperial_year" title="Japanese era name">Japanese Imperial year</a> 2592 – 1932 in the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a> – in which the gun entered service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nambu_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nambu-2">[2]</a></sup> Designed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kijiro_Nambu" title="Kijiro Nambu">Kijiro Nambu</a> and built by <a href="/wiki/Hino_Motors" title="Hino Motors">Hino Motors</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hitachi" title="Hitachi">Hitachi</a>, total production was about 45,000 guns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Type_92_heavy_machine_gun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Type 92 was essentially a scaled-up version of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Type_3_Heavy_Machine_Gun" title="Type 3 Heavy Machine Gun">Type 3 Heavy Machine Gun</a>, with its <a href="/wiki/Caliber" title="Caliber">calibre</a> increased to 7.7 mm, and like the Type 3 was air cooled, ammo strip fed, and based on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hotchkiss_M1914" title="Hotchkiss M1914">Hotchkiss M1914</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-weapons_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-weapons-4">[4]</a></sup> It could use both a rimless and semi-rimmed 7.7x58mm Shiki round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Korea_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Korea-1">[1]</a></sup> A 7.7 mm round could be used if needed or if other ammunition supplies dwindled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nambu_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nambu-2">[2]</a></sup> Rounds fired from the gun traveled at about 730 m/s (2,400 ft/s), and the rate of fire was about 450 rpm. It was sometimes used as a light <a href="/wiki/Anti-aircraft_warfare" title="Anti-aircraft warfare">anti-aircraft</a> gun during the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_War" title="Pacific War">Pacific War</a>. It was nicknamed "<a href="/wiki/Woodpecker" title="Woodpecker">woodpecker</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Western Allied</a> soldiers because of the characteristic sound it made when fired, and the "chicken neck" (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">雞脖子</span>) by Chinese soldiers due to its appearance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Korea_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Korea-1">[1]</a></sup> The Type 92 had a maximum range of 4,500 meters, but a practical range of 800 meters.</p>
<p>The gun was intended to be fired on a tripod with a team of 3 men. The unusual tripod was designed with removable carry poles, so that the weapon could be transported fully assembled for quicker deployment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nambu_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Nambu-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>An unusual characteristic of this gun was the placement of its iron sights – canted slightly to the right instead of center. A number of different sights were produced for the weapon, the Type 93 and Type 94 <a href="/wiki/Periscope" title="Periscope">periscopic sights</a> as well as the Type 96 <a href="/wiki/Telescopic_sight" title="Telescopic sight">telescopic sight</a>. A ring-type anti-aircraft sight was also produced.</p>
<p>A major problem with this weapon was the short strip. This led to a lowering in the rate of fire, and could cause jams in the loading mechanism. To alleviate this the Japanese oiled the rounds in the factories producing the ammunition. However, this caused the problem to escalate, as the grease picked up dirt during operations. This grime was then deposited directly into the breech mechanism, worsening the problem. This was a problem common among Japanese machine guns.</p>
