<p><i><b>Pan de Peace!</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">パンでPeace!</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Pan de Pīsu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. "Peace Through Bread!")</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Yonkoma" title="Yonkoma">four-panel</a> <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series by Emily. It is serialized in <a href="/wiki/Kadokawa_Corporation" title="Kadokawa Corporation">Kadokawa Corporation</a> / <a href="/wiki/Media_Factory" title="Media Factory">Media Factory</a>'s manga magazine <i>Comic Cune</i>. An <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> television series adaptation by <a href="/wiki/Asahi_Production" title="Asahi Production">Asahi Production</a> aired in Japan between April and June 2016.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pan_de_Peace!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The "cute and soft bread four-panel manga" centers around Minami, an airheaded girl who is starting high school and who loves eating bread for breakfast. Baked goods bring happiness everyday to her and her classmates — the reliable Yū, the pastry-baking Fuyumi, and the independent Noa.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pan_de_Peace!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<dd>The main protagonist of the story. She is fond of egg sandwiches.</dd>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pan_de_Peace!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pan_de_Peace!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Pan de Peace!</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Yonkoma" title="Yonkoma">four-panel</a> <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series by Emily (styled as "emily"), a Japanese manga artist who mainly draws <a href="/wiki/Adult_comics" title="Adult comics">adult comics</a>. It began serialization in <i>Comic Cune'</i>s October 2014 issue released on August 27, 2014; At first, <i>Comic Cune</i> was a "magazine in magazine" placed in <i><a href="/wiki/Monthly_Comic_Alive" title="Monthly Comic Alive">Monthly Comic Alive</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> later it became independent of <i>Comic Alive</i> and changed to a formal magazine on August 27, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <i>Pan de Peace!</i> is also available on <a href="/wiki/Kadokawa_Corporation" title="Kadokawa Corporation">Kadokawa Corporation</a>'s <i>ComicWalker</i> website. Two <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> volumes of the manga were released between August 27, 2015 and March 26, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pan_de_Peace!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> television series produced by <a href="/wiki/Asahi_Production" title="Asahi Production">Asahi Production</a> aired in Japan between April 4, 2016 and June 27, 2016 and was simulcast by <a href="/wiki/Crunchyroll" title="Crunchyroll">Crunchyroll</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ANN1_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-ANN1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The opening theme is "Seishun wa Tabemono desu" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">青春は食べ物です</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Youth is Food</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Petit_Milady&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Petit Milady (page does not exist)">Petit Milady</a>.</p>
