<p>In <a href="/wiki/Fashion" title="Fashion">fashion</a>, <b>falsies</b> are <a href="/wiki/Padding" title="Padding">paddings</a> for use in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brassiere" title="Brassiere">brassiere</a> to create the appearance of larger <a href="/wiki/Breast" title="Breast">breasts</a>. The term has also, more rarely, been used for pads which create the appearance of larger <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buttock" title="Buttock">buttocks</a>. In both cases, there is a note of (more or less) amusement conveyed by the term. In a specifically <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Humor" title="Humor">humorous</a> context, the term refers to moulded <a href="/wiki/Plastic" title="Plastic">plastic</a> <a href="/wiki/Replica" title="Replica">replicas</a> of female breasts that may be worn (covered or uncovered) by <a href="/wiki/Male" title="Male">males</a> for <a href="/wiki/Comedy" title="Comedy">comedic</a> effect. Typically held onto the chest by <a href="/wiki/Elastomer" title="Elastomer">elastic</a> straps or forming the top part of an <a href="/wiki/Apron" title="Apron">apron</a>, they are a perennial favourite amongst <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a> students and at "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buck%27s_night" title="Buck's night">buck's nights</a>".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bosom_pads">Bosom pads</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Falsies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Bosom pads">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:USpatent152341_1874.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1109" data-file-width="1175" height="208" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/USpatent152341_1874.png/220px-USpatent152341_1874.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/USpatent152341_1874.png/330px-USpatent152341_1874.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/USpatent152341_1874.png/440px-USpatent152341_1874.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:USpatent152341_1874.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Corset" title="Corset">Corset</a> by Bosom pad 1873</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Victorian_Era" title="Victorian Era">Victorian Era</a>, girls were considered grown-up upon reaching the age of fifteen. However, many girls had not developed large enough breasts to fit into adult clothes, therefore <b>bosom pads</b> were used.</p>
<p>When small, rounded breasts were in <a href="/wiki/Fashion" title="Fashion">fashion</a>, the problem was reversed. Adult women solved the problem with tight corsets and <a href="/wiki/Breast_binding" title="Breast binding">breast binding</a>.</p>
<p>When used after <a href="/wiki/Mastectomy" title="Mastectomy">breast removal surgery</a>, as for <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">cancer</a>, the usual term used is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Breast_prosthesis" title="Breast prosthesis">breast prosthesis</a>.</p>
