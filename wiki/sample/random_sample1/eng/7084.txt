<p><i><b>Rhizobium</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gram-negative" title="Gram-negative">Gram-negative</a> <a href="/wiki/Soil" title="Soil">soil</a> <a href="/wiki/Bacteria" title="Bacteria">bacteria</a> that <a href="/wiki/Nitrogen_fixation" title="Nitrogen fixation">fix nitrogen</a>. <i>Rhizobium</i> species form an <a href="/wiki/Endosymbiont" title="Endosymbiont">endosymbiotic</a> <a href="/wiki/Nitrogen_fixation" title="Nitrogen fixation">nitrogen-fixing</a> association with roots of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legumes" title="Legumes">legumes</a> and <i><a class="extiw" href="https://species.wikimedia.org/wiki/Parasponia" title="species:Parasponia">Parasponia</a></i>.</p>
<p>The bacteria colonize plant cells within <a href="/wiki/Root_nodule" title="Root nodule">root nodules</a>, where they convert atmospheric nitrogen into <a href="/wiki/Ammonia" title="Ammonia">ammonia</a> and then provide organic nitrogenous compounds such as <a href="/wiki/Glutamine" title="Glutamine">glutamine</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ureide" title="Ureide">ureides</a> to the plant. The plant, in turn, provides the bacteria with <a href="/wiki/Organic_compound" title="Organic compound">organic compounds</a> made by <a href="/wiki/Photosynthesis" title="Photosynthesis">photosynthesis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> This mutually beneficial relationship is true of all of the <a href="/wiki/Rhizobia" title="Rhizobia">rhizobia</a>, of which the <i>Rhizobium</i> genus is a typical example.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rhizobium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beijerinck in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> was the first to isolate and cultivate a <a href="/wiki/Microorganism" title="Microorganism">microorganism</a> from the nodules of legumes in 1888. He named it <i>Bacillus radicicola</i>, which is now placed in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bergey%27s_Manual_of_Determinative_Bacteriology" title="Bergey's Manual of Determinative Bacteriology">Bergey's Manual of Determinative Bacteriology</a></i> under the genus <i>Rhizobium</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rhizobium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Rhizobium</i> forms a symbiotic relationship with certain plants such as legumes, fixing nitrogen from the air into <a href="/wiki/Ammonia" title="Ammonia">ammonia</a>, which acts as a natural fertilizer for the plants. Current research is being conducted by Agricultural Research Service microbiologists to discover a way to use <i>Rhizobium</i>’s biological nitrogen fixation. This research involves the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genetic_mapping" title="Genetic mapping">genetic mapping</a> of various rhizobial species with their respective symbiotic plant species, like alfalfa or soybean. The goal of this research is to increase the plants’ productivity without using fertilizers. <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.ars.usda.gov/is/AR/archive/jan10/microbe0110.htm" rel="nofollow">[1]</a></p>
<p>In molecular biology, <i>Rhizobium</i> has also been identified as a contaminant of DNA extraction kit reagents and ultrapure water systems, which may lead to its erroneous appearance in microbiota or metagenomic datasets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The presence of nitrogen fixing bacteria as contaminants may be due to the use of nitrogen gas in ultra-pure water production to inhibit microbial growth in storage tanks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
