<p><b>Piyapong Pue-on</b> (<a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>: <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">ปิยะพงษ์ ผิวอ่อน</span>; <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_General_System_of_Transcription" title="Royal Thai General System of Transcription"><span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant:small-caps;">rtgs</span></a>: <span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Thai transliteration"><i>Piyaphong Phiu-on</i></span>) born: <b>Padej Khanklue</b> (<a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>: <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">เผด็จ ขันเครือ</span>; <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_General_System_of_Transcription" title="Royal Thai General System of Transcription"><span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant:small-caps;">rtgs</span></a>: <span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Thai transliteration"><i>Phadet Khankhluea</i></span>; November 14, 1959) is a retired <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thai</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> player who played as a striker. He was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Thailand_national_football_team" title="Thailand national football team">Thailand national football team</a> and also played for <a href="/wiki/FC_Seoul" title="FC Seoul">FC Seoul</a> of the <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/K_League" title="K League">K League</a>.</p>
<p>He also appeared in the 2004 film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kerd_ma_lui" title="Kerd ma lui">Kerd ma lui</a></i> (<i><a href="/wiki/Born_to_Fight_(2004_film)" title="Born to Fight (2004 film)">Born to Fight</a></i> in English).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>An active <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_Air_Force" title="Royal Thai Air Force">Thai Air Force</a> officer, Piyapong coached the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air_Force_United_F.C." title="Air Force United F.C.">Royal Thai Air Force</a> football club between 1997 and 2008.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Piyapong_Pue-on&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="FC_Seoul">FC Seoul</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Piyapong_Pue-on&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: FC Seoul">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He joined <a href="/wiki/FC_Seoul" title="FC Seoul">FC Seoul</a>, then known as the Lucky-Goldstar FC, in August 1984. At the time, the Lucky-Goldstar worked fiercely to bring him from October 1983.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/1984_K_League" title="1984 K League">1984 K League season</a>, he scored on his debut on 8 September against <a href="/wiki/Pohang_Steelers" title="Pohang Steelers">POSCO Dolphins</a>. He played one of the leading roles to crown the Lucky-Goldstar as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_K_League_Classic_champions" title="List of K League Classic champions">K League champions</a> in 1985, scoring 12 goals and providing 6 assists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Piyapong_Pue-on&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He played for <a href="/wiki/Thailand_national_football_team" title="Thailand national football team">Thailand national football team</a> for 16 years (1981–1997), scoring 70 goals in 100 appearances in full international matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
