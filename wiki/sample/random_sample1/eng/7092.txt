<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sagrada_Familia,_Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the 2002 <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Statistics_Institute_(Chile)" title="National Statistics Institute (Chile)">National Statistics Institute</a>, Sagrada Familia spans an area of 548.8¬†km<sup>2</sup> (212¬†sq¬†mi) and has 17,519 inhabitants (9,108 men and 8,411 women). Of these, 5,080 (29%) lived in <a href="/wiki/Urban_area" title="Urban area">urban areas</a> and 12,439 (71%) in <a href="/wiki/Rural_area" title="Rural area">rural areas</a>. The population grew by 3.7% (625 persons) between the 1992 and 2002 censuses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-INE_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-INE-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sagrada_Familia,_Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a commune, Sagrada Familia is a third-level <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_division_of_Chile" title="Administrative division of Chile">administrative division of Chile</a> administered by a <a href="/wiki/Municipal_council" title="Municipal council">municipal council</a>, headed by an <a href="/wiki/Alcalde" title="Alcalde">alcalde</a> who is directly elected every four years. The 2008-2012 alcalde is Francisco Mel√©ndez Rojas (<a href="/wiki/Socialist_Party_of_Chile" title="Socialist Party of Chile">PS</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Official_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Official-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Electoral_divisions_of_Chile" title="Electoral divisions of Chile">electoral divisions of Chile</a>, Sagrada Familia is represented in the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_Chile" title="Chamber of Deputies of Chile">Chamber of Deputies</a> by Roberto Le√≥n (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_Democratic_Party" title="Christian Democratic Party">PDC</a>) and Celso Morales (<a href="/wiki/Independent_Democratic_Union" title="Independent Democratic Union">UDI</a>) as part of the 36th electoral district, (together with <a href="/wiki/Curic%C3%B3" title="Curic√≥">Curic√≥</a>, <a href="/wiki/Teno" title="Teno">Teno</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romeral" title="Romeral">Romeral</a>, <a href="/wiki/Molina,_Chile" title="Molina, Chile">Molina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huala%C3%B1%C3%A9" title="Huala√±√©">Huala√±√©</a>, <a href="/wiki/Licant%C3%A9n" title="Licant√©n">Licant√©n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vichuqu%C3%A9n" title="Vichuqu√©n">Vichuqu√©n</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rauco" title="Rauco">Rauco</a>). The commune is represented in the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Chile" title="Senate of Chile">Senate</a> by Juan Antonio Coloma Correa (UDI) and Andr√©s Zald√≠var Larra√≠n (PDC) as part of the 10th senatorial constituency (Maule-North).</p>
