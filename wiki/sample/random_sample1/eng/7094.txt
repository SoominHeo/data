<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Alpha² Canum Venaticorum variable</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>An <b>Alpha<sup>2</sup> Canum Venaticorum variable</b> (or <b>α<sup>2</sup> CVn variable</b>) is a type of <a href="/wiki/Variable_star" title="Variable star">variable star</a>. These stars are <a href="/wiki/Chemically_peculiar_star" title="Chemically peculiar star">chemically peculiar</a> <a href="/wiki/Main_sequence" title="Main sequence">main sequence</a> <a href="/wiki/Star" title="Star">stars</a> of <a href="/wiki/Stellar_classification" title="Stellar classification">spectral class</a> B8p to A7p. They have strong <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magnetic" title="Magnetic">magnetic</a> fields and strong <a href="/wiki/Silicon" title="Silicon">silicon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Strontium" title="Strontium">strontium</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Chromium" title="Chromium">chromium</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spectral_lines" title="Spectral lines">spectral lines</a>. Their brightness typically varies by 0.01 to 0.1 <a href="/wiki/Apparent_magnitude" title="Apparent magnitude">magnitudes</a> over the course of 0.5 to 160 days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In addition to their intensities, the intensities and profiles of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spectral_lines" title="Spectral lines">spectral lines</a> of α<sup>2</sup> CVn variables also vary, as do their magnetic fields. The periods of these variations are all equal and are believed to equal the period of <a href="/wiki/Rotation" title="Rotation">rotation</a> of the star. It is thought that they are caused by an inhomogeneous distribution of metals in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atmospheres" title="Atmospheres">atmospheres</a> of these stars, so that the surface of the star varies in brightness from point to point.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The type-star which this class is named after is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%CE%91%C2%B2_Canum_Venaticorum" title="Α² Canum Venaticorum">α² Canum Venaticorum</a>, a star in the binary system of Cor Caroli, which is located in the northern <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Canes_Venatici" title="Canes Venatici">Canes Venatici</a>. Its brightness fluctuates by 0.14 magnitudes with a period of 5.47 days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
