<p><b>Sarawak Self-government Day</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Self-governance" title="Self-governance">self-government day</a> celebrated on 22 July every year by the <a href="/wiki/States_and_federal_territories_of_Malaysia" title="States and federal territories of Malaysia">state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sarawak" title="Sarawak">Sarawak</a> in <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-discovery.nationalarchives.gov.uk_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-discovery.nationalarchives.gov.uk-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The holiday has been received widely by the Sarawak state government and citizens only since 2012, after public discontent about <a href="/wiki/Hari_Merdeka" title="Hari Merdeka">Hari Merdeka</a> being too Malaya-centric.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>While Sarawak Self-government Day is often referred to as 'Independence Day', this is strictly speaking incorrect, since British legislation on Sarawak's self-government did not provide for its independence prior to it joining the federation of Malaysia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sarawak_Self-government_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sarawak_anti-cession_demonstration.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="589" data-file-width="673" height="175" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/Sarawak_anti-cession_demonstration.JPG/200px-Sarawak_anti-cession_demonstration.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/Sarawak_anti-cession_demonstration.JPG/300px-Sarawak_anti-cession_demonstration.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/Sarawak_anti-cession_demonstration.JPG/400px-Sarawak_anti-cession_demonstration.JPG 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sarawak_anti-cession_demonstration.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Sarawakian citizens protested against the transforming of the Kingdom of Sarawak into a <a href="/wiki/Crown_Colony_of_Sarawak" title="Crown Colony of Sarawak">Crown colony</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Originally, the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sarawak" title="Kingdom of Sarawak">Kingdom of Sarawak</a> was granted independence by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultanate_of_Brunei" title="Sultanate of Brunei">Sultanate of Brunei</a> in 1841, but came under British protection from 1888 onwards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> However, at this time, Sarawak was not "fully" granted independence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> After the end of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the territory was administered by the <a href="/wiki/British_Military_Administration_(Borneo)" title="British Military Administration (Borneo)">British Military Administration</a>, then became a <a href="/wiki/Crown_Colony_of_Sarawak" title="Crown Colony of Sarawak">Crown Colony</a> in 1946.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The transferring of the territory to colonial administration has led to the <a href="/wiki/Anti-cession_movement_of_Sarawak" title="Anti-cession movement of Sarawak">major protest</a> by Sarawakian citizens who wanted the independence of Sarawak to be restored. This led to the assassination of <a href="/wiki/Duncan_Stewart_(British_diplomat)" title="Duncan Stewart (British diplomat)">Duncan Stewart</a>, the second governor of the Colony, by <a href="/wiki/Rosli_Dhobi" title="Rosli Dhobi">Rosli Dhobi</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> who was captured and subsequently hanged for murder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The position of the Governor was succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Abell" title="Anthony Abell">Anthony Abell</a>, who also became one of the members for the <a href="/wiki/Cobbold_Commission" title="Cobbold Commission">Cobbold Commission</a> which brought Sarawak and <a href="/wiki/Crown_Colony_of_North_Borneo" title="Crown Colony of North Borneo">North Borneo</a> into the Federation of Malaysia.</p>
<p>Sarawak was granted self-government on 22 July 1963,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-discovery.nationalarchives.gov.uk_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-discovery.nationalarchives.gov.uk-1">[1]</a></sup> on the condition that it would join to form the Federation of Malaysia on 16 September the same year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Before the self-government ceremony on 22 July 1963, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Alexander_Waddell&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Alexander Waddell (page does not exist)">Alexander Waddell</a>, the last Governor of the Colony, left the <a href="/wiki/The_Astana,_Sarawak" title="The Astana, Sarawak">Astana</a> and boarded a white <a href="/wiki/Sampan" title="Sampan">sampan</a> to cross the <a href="/wiki/Sarawak_River" title="Sarawak River">Sarawak River</a>, then handed the administration of Sarawak to the Sarawakian citizens, with the Colonial flag lowered and the Sarawak flag raised.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-swk_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-swk-17">[17]</a></sup> Before he left, the Governor appointed <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Kalong_Ningkan" title="Stephen Kalong Ningkan">Stephen Kalong Ningkan</a> as the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chief_Minister_of_Sarawak" title="Chief Minister of Sarawak">Chief Minister of Sarawak</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-swk_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-swk-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, Chief Minister <a href="/wiki/Adenan_Satem" title="Adenan Satem">Adenan Satem</a> announced 22 July a public holiday in Sarawak starting from 2016 in view of Sarawak Day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
