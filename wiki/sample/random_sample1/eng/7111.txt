<p><i><b>The Yellow Handkerchief</b></i> is a 2008 American <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">independent</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_film" title="Drama film">drama film</a>. The film is a remake of the 1977 Japanese classic of the same name <i><a href="/wiki/The_Yellow_Handkerchief_(1977_film)" title="The Yellow Handkerchief (1977 film)">The Yellow Handkerchief</a></i> (幸福の黄色いハンカチ <i>Shiawase no kiiroi hankachi</i>, lit. <i>The yellow handkerchief of happiness</i>) directed by <a href="/wiki/Yoji_Yamada" title="Yoji Yamada">Yoji Yamada</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Set in present-day <a href="/wiki/Southern_United_States" title="Southern United States">southern United States</a>, <i>The Yellow Handkerchief</i> stars <a href="/wiki/William_Hurt" title="William Hurt">William Hurt</a> as Brett Hanson, an ex-convict who embarks on a road trip straight out of prison. Hanson hitches a ride with two troubled teens, Martine (<a href="/wiki/Kristen_Stewart" title="Kristen Stewart">Kristen Stewart</a>) and Gordy (<a href="/wiki/Eddie_Redmayne" title="Eddie Redmayne">Eddie Redmayne</a>) traversing post-<a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">Hurricane Katrina</a> <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a> in an attempt to reach his ex-wife and long-lost love, May (<a href="/wiki/Maria_Bello" title="Maria Bello">Maria Bello</a>). Along the way, the three reflect on their existence, struggle for acceptance, and find their way not only through Louisiana, but through life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FA01_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FA01-3">[3]</a></sup> Directed by <a href="/wiki/Udayan_Prasad" title="Udayan Prasad">Udayan Prasad</a> and produced by <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Cohn" title="Arthur Cohn">Arthur Cohn</a>, the film was shown at <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance</a> in 2008 and given a limited release on February 26, 2010, by <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Goldwyn_Films" title="Samuel Goldwyn Films">Samuel Goldwyn Films</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Yellow_Handkerchief_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After being released from prison after six years, ex-<a href="/wiki/Convict" title="Convict">convict</a> Brett Hanson becomes lost in a new and unfamiliar world of freedoms and responsibilities. Struggling to reconcile himself with his disastrous past, he embarks on a journey to his home of Southern <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a> to reunite with the ex-wife he left behind, May (<a href="/wiki/Maria_Bello" title="Maria Bello">Maria Bello</a>). Along this journey, he meets two teenagers: Martine (<a href="/wiki/Kristen_Stewart" title="Kristen Stewart">Kristen Stewart</a>), a troubled 15-year-old who has just escaped her family, and Gordy (<a href="/wiki/Eddie_Redmayne" title="Eddie Redmayne">Eddie Redmayne</a>), a geeky outcast desperately seeking acceptance. Martine and Gordy offer to give Brett a lift home, and on the ensuing road trip the three reflect on their own personal misfit status while discovering in themselves and each other the acceptance each so deeply desires. Brett weighs whether to start a new life or rekindle his love with May – he's not sure she'll take him back<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FA01_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-FA01-3">[3]</a></sup> – while Martine reevaluates her relationships with boys and her family and Gordy struggles with his affection for Martine.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Yellow_Handkerchief_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2003, Cohn obtained remake rights for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Yellow_Handkerchief" title="The Yellow Handkerchief">The Yellow Handkerchief</a></i> from <a href="/wiki/Shochiku" title="Shochiku">Shochiku</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ScreenDaily_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ScreenDaily-4">[4]</a></sup> Financing came from private investments rather than through pre-sales. Cohn commented saying "The money came from four Europeans — three women from <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>: Annette Grisard, Jeannine Eckenstein, Esther Grether and a gentleman by the name of Samuel Falk from <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a> — all of whom were anxious to support a film they hoped would be memorable without any scenes of violence, brutality or sex. These investors were not concerned that the film would be considered "small." They felt, as we do, that there are no small films or big films, only films which are good and emotional and others which are not."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Filming took place in February 2007 and was shot in 43 different locations.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Hurt prepared for his role by spending four days at <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_State_Penitentiary" title="Louisiana State Penitentiary">Louisiana State Penitentiary</a> at Angola, including a rare overnight for a volunteer in a maximum-security cell. In an interview, he said he'd also done "[y]ou'd call it charitable work... periodically visit[ing] the prisons in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rockland_County" title="Rockland County">Rockland County</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_State" title="New York State">New York State</a> to take a program of hope and self-rehabilitation to" the prisoners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FA01_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-FA01-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Yellow_Handkerchief_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Yellow_Handkerchief_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>The Yellow Handkerchief</i> received generally positive reviews. It holds a 67% favorable rating on <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> based on 49 reviews,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RT_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-RT-6">[6]</a></sup> as well as a weighted average score of 64 out of 100 on <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metacritic_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Metacritic-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Yellow_Handkerchief_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
