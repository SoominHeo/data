<p>A <b>command</b> in <a href="/wiki/Military_terminology" title="Military terminology">military terminology</a> is an organisational unit for which a military commander is responsible. A commander is normally specifically appointed to the role in order to provide a legal framework for the authority bestowed. Naval and military officers have legal authority by virtue of their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commissioned_officer" title="Commissioned officer">officer's commission</a>, but the specific responsibilities and privileges of command are derived from the publication of appointment.</p>
<p>The relevant definition of "command" according to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Department_of_Defense" title="US Department of Defense">US Department of Defense</a> is as follows:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>(DOD) 3. A unit or units, an organization, or an area under the command of one individual. Also called CMD. See also area command; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Combatant_command" title="Combatant command">combatant command</a>; combatant command (command authority).</p>
</blockquote>
