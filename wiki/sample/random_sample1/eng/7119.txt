<p><b>Sarufutsu</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">猿払村</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sarufutsu-mura</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_villages_in_Japan" title="List of villages in Japan">village</a> located in <a href="/wiki/S%C5%8Dya_District,_Hokkaido" title="Sōya District, Hokkaido">Sōya District</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C5%8Dya_Subprefecture" title="Sōya Subprefecture">Sōya Subprefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hokkaido" title="Hokkaido">Hokkaido</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>As of September 2014, the village has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 2,884 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">density</a> of 4.91 persons per km². The total area is 590.00 km².</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sarufutsu,_Hokkaido&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sarufutsu is the northernmost village in Japan and is the largest village in Hokkaido in area. Located on the Tonbetsu Plain, it faces the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Okhotsk" title="Sea of Okhotsk">Sea of Okhotsk</a> to the east where, during the winter, there is <a href="/wiki/Drift_ice" title="Drift ice">drift ice</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> 80% of the village's total area is covered by forests.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sarufutsu,_Hokkaido&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sarufutsu is famous for its large and particularly excellent scallops, among other seafood. Likewise, a relatively large part of the village is utilized for dairy farming. Sarufutsu has a fledgling tourist industry, as it is a highlight for motor bikers in the summer, who stop overnight at one of the biker camps. The tourist center is located a few kilometers south of Hamaonishibetsu.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sarufutsu,_Hokkaido&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="War">War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sarufutsu,_Hokkaido&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sarufutsu was the site for many Korean prisoners of war from World War II to be sent as labor force for the building of an Air Field in Asajino. Approximately 80 Korean prisoners of war died over the course of construction from abuse or malnutrition. The air field was never completed, and has since lost nearly all signs of its existence. In recent year, the village made attempts to construct a monument in memory of the Korean prisoners who died there. However, construction was forced to be cancelled after Japanese nationalists orchestrated by the Internet began overwhelming the village office with threatening phone calls, calling them traitors, and threatening the village with a boycott of its scallop industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Settlements">Settlements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sarufutsu,_Hokkaido&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Settlements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Koishi">Koishi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sarufutsu,_Hokkaido&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Koishi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the 1960s to early-1970s, Koishi was the largest settlement in Sarufutsu with several thousand people working in the coal industry. It had its own hospital, as well as a movie theater. With the decline of the coal industry across Japan, the population also drastically dropped in Koishi, and its current population is less than 50 people.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Asajino">Asajino</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sarufutsu,_Hokkaido&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Asajino">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>As previously mentioned, Asajino was the potential site for an air field during World War II.</p>
