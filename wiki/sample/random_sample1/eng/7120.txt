<p><b>Sardar Azmoun</b> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="fa">سردار آزمون</span>‎‎; born 1 January 1995) is an Iranian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">forward</a> for Russian side <a href="/wiki/FC_Rostov" title="FC Rostov">FC Rostov</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Iran_national_football_team" title="Iran national football team">Iran national team</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardar_Azmoun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardar_Azmoun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Azmoun started to play club football when he was 9.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Navad_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Navad-3">[3]</a></sup> He began his career at Oghab Gonbad of <a href="/wiki/Gonbad-e_Kavus" title="Gonbad-e Kavus">Gonbad-e Kavus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Radio_Farda_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Radio_Farda-4">[4]</a></sup> He also played <a href="/wiki/Volleyball" title="Volleyball">volleyball</a> and was invited to <a href="/wiki/Iran_men%27s_national_volleyball_team" title="Iran men's national volleyball team">Iran's national under-15 volleyball team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Navad_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Navad-3">[3]</a></sup> After some years, he joined Shamoushak Gorgan, before joining <a href="/wiki/Etka_Gorgan_F.C." title="Etka Gorgan F.C.">Etka Gorgan</a>, who were playing in Division 1 in Iran at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sepahan">Sepahan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardar_Azmoun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sepahan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At age 15, Azmoun joined <a href="/wiki/Sepahan_F.C." title="Sepahan F.C.">Sepahan</a>'s youth ranks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Radio_Farda_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Radio_Farda-4">[4]</a></sup> While he appeared for the club during their preseason friendlies abroad in Turkey, he did not make a professional appearance for the club as Sepahan won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Iran_Pro_League" title="2011–12 Iran Pro League">2011–12 Iran Pro League</a> and lifted the trophy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Navad_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Navad-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Sepahan_F.C._season" title="2012–13 Sepahan F.C. season">2012–13 season</a>, thanks to Azmoun's great display in Iran's youth team, Sardar became sought after by a couple of European clubs, namely <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rubin_Kazan" title="Rubin Kazan">Rubin Kazan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Azmoun became Iran's youngest Legionnaire when later that year he transferred to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rubin_Kazan" title="Rubin Kazan">Rubin Kazan</a> at the age of 17.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Radio_Farda_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Radio_Farda-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rubin_Kazan">Rubin Kazan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardar_Azmoun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Rubin Kazan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.9313_season">2012–13 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardar_Azmoun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2012–13 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In January 2013, he Azmoun an offer from Russian team <a href="/wiki/FC_Rubin_Kazan" title="FC Rubin Kazan">Rubin Kazan</a> to join them; meanwhile he had interest from two Iranian teams, <a href="/wiki/Persepolis_F.C." title="Persepolis F.C.">Persepolis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Esteghlal_F.C." title="Esteghlal F.C.">Esteghlal</a>, as well as an offer for first team contract in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sepahan" title="Sepahan">Sepahan</a>, he chose to work with Turkmen speaking <a href="/wiki/Kurban_Berdyev" title="Kurban Berdyev">Kurban Berdyev</a> and became Iran's first football player in the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Premier_League" title="Russian Premier League">Russian Premier League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Radio_Farda_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Radio_Farda-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In his first season at the club, he made 8 appearances for his club's reserve team, scoring two goals and receiving one yellow card. He was also picked for the 18-man match day squad on a number of occasions, but failed to make his senior debut.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.9314_season">2013–14 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardar_Azmoun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2013–14 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>On 25 July 2013, Azmoun made his debut for the senior team in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> match against <a href="/wiki/FK_Jagodina" title="FK Jagodina">Jagodina</a>, coming on as a 73rd-minute substitute as Rubin won 1–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On 29 August, he scored his first goal in just his second senior appearance for Rubin in a 3–0 <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_UEFA_Europa_League_qualifying_phase_and_play-off_round#Rubin_Kazan_v_Molde" title="2013–14 UEFA Europa League qualifying phase and play-off round">Europa League</a> win against <a href="/wiki/Molde_FK" title="Molde FK">Molde</a>, coming on as a substitute in the 64th minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He made his league debut with a goal and an assist on 6 October 2013, coming on as a substitute in the 72nd minute in a 5–1 win over <a href="/wiki/FC_Anzhi_Makhachkala" title="FC Anzhi Makhachkala">Anzhi Makhachkala</a>.</p>
<p>On 27 March 2014, English <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> reportedly made a £2 million offer to Kazan for the transfer of 19-year-old Azmoun, sparking interest from <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">A.C. Milan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham</a>, and <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a> as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Three days later, he scored his second league goal for Rubin Kazan in a 2–1 defeat to <a href="/wiki/FC_Rostov" title="FC Rostov">Rostov</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On 6 April, he scored a goal in Rubin Kazan's defeat to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zenit_St._Petersburg" title="Zenit St. Petersburg">Zenit St. Petersburg</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In a match against <a href="/wiki/FC_Krylia_Sovetov_Samara" title="FC Krylia Sovetov Samara">Kyrlia Sovetov</a> on 10 May, Azmoun came on as a substitute in the 55th minute, scoring a goal and providing an assist in a 4–0 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.9315_season">2014–15 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardar_Azmoun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2014–15 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 4 July 2014, Rubin Kazan announced through their official website that Azmoun would remain at the club despite transfer offers from <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> and <a href="/wiki/FC_Zenit_Saint_Petersburg" title="FC Zenit Saint Petersburg">Zenit St. Petersburg</a>. He scored Rubin Kazan's goal in a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Hellas_Verona_F.C." title="Hellas Verona F.C.">Hellas Verona</a> in a pre-season friendly match on 27 July 2014. He scored his first goal of the season in a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/FC_Terek_Grozny" title="FC Terek Grozny">Terek Grozny</a> in the 87th minute. On 30 October, he scored a goal in their victory over <a href="/wiki/FC_Spartak_Moscow" title="FC Spartak Moscow">Spartak Moscow</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Russian_Cup" title="2014–15 Russian Cup">2014–15 Russian Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rostov">Rostov</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardar_Azmoun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Rostov">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 26 February 2015, Azmoun signed a three-and-a-half-month loan deal with relegation-battling <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Football_Premier_League" title="Russian Football Premier League">Russian Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/FC_Rostov" title="FC Rostov">FC Rostov</a> until the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ArtyomDzyuba_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-ArtyomDzyuba-19">[19]</a></sup> On 16 March, Azmoun came on as a substitute in the 73rd minute and scored the match winner in the 88th minute against <a href="/wiki/FC_Kuban_Krasnodar" title="FC Kuban Krasnodar">Kuban Krasnodar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Azmoun again found the back of the net on 26 April 2015 in Rostov's 2–2 draw against <a href="/wiki/Dynamo_Moscow" title="Dynamo Moscow">Dynamo Moscow</a>. His good performance earned him a place on the Russian Premier League Team of the Week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-football.kulichki.net_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-football.kulichki.net-21">[21]</a></sup> He scored Rostov's winning goal in the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Russian_Premier_League#Relegation_play-offs" title="2014–15 Russian Premier League">relegation play-offs</a> on 7 June.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.E2.80.9316_season">2015–16 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardar_Azmoun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2015–16 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>At the end of the 2014–15 season, Azmoun extended his loan deal with Rostov until the end of the 2015–16 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> He scored his first goal of the season on 22 August 2015 in a 2–1 loss to <a href="/wiki/CSKA_Moscow" title="CSKA Moscow">CSKA Moscow</a>. After his good performances in the first half of the season, he was linked with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_Premier_League" title="English Premier League">English Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Later in March 2016 he was also linked with a move to English side <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metro.co.uk_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-metro.co.uk-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>After a long goalless drought, Azmoun scored in Rostov's 3–0 win against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zenit_Saint_Petersburg" title="Zenit Saint Petersburg">Zenit Saint Petersburg</a> on 24 April 2016. he scored a brace in a 3–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/Dynamo_Moscow" title="Dynamo Moscow">Dynamo Moscow</a> on 12 May 2016, which kept Rostov in second place and in contention for the Russian Premier League championship. On 16 May 2016 with his goal, Rostov defeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FK_Ural_Sverdlovskaya_Oblast" title="FK Ural Sverdlovskaya Oblast">Ural</a> 1–0 and clinched at least an <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> play–off spot for the 2016–17 season. His goal against Ural was his fifth consecutive game with a goal which included 6 goals in 5 games.</p>
<p>On the last day of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Premier_League" title="Russian Premier League">Russian Premier League</a> on 21 May 2016 needing a win and <a href="/wiki/CSKA_Moscow" title="CSKA Moscow">CSKA Moscow</a> to drop points to become champions, Azmoun assisted Rostov's first goal in their 2–0 win against <a href="/wiki/FC_Terek_Grozny" title="FC Terek Grozny">Terek Grozny</a>. However, CSKA won their match and Rostov finished as runners up, thus securing a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> playoff spot.</p>
<p>Azmoun finished seventh in league scoring with nine goals, despite getting half as much playing time as some other top scorers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> He was also voted as the fifth most valuable player of the league, narrowly behind players such as <a href="/wiki/Fyodor_Smolov" title="Fyodor Smolov">Fyodor Smolov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moussa_Doumbia_(footballer,_born_1994)" title="Moussa Doumbia (footballer, born 1994)">Moussa Doumbia</a>, and Brazilian superstar <a href="/wiki/Hulk_(footballer)" title="Hulk (footballer)">Hulk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2016.E2.80.9317_season">2016–17 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardar_Azmoun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2016–17 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Initially it was announced that Azmoun would return to <a href="/wiki/FC_Rubin_Kazan" title="FC Rubin Kazan">Rubin Kazan</a>. However, Rostov triggered the buy out clause in Azmoun's contract. Rubin denied this clause and claimed Azmoun was their player. Rubin lodged a complaint with <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a>. FC Rostov filed a counter-complaint with the <a href="/wiki/Court_of_Arbitration_for_Sport" title="Court of Arbitration for Sport">Court of Arbitration for Sport</a>, and on 22 July 2016 CAS temporarily ruled in favour of Azmoun and he was included in Rostov's <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> squad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CAS22July_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-CAS22July-30">[30]</a></sup> According to Rostov's lawyer Yuri Zaytsev, the final CAS decision on his status might not be reached until late 2016 or even the summer of 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Azmoun made his Champions League debut on 26 July 2016 in the first leg of a third-round qualifying round match against <a href="/wiki/R.S.C._Anderlecht" title="R.S.C. Anderlecht">Anderlecht</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olimp_%E2%80%93_2" title="Olimp – 2">Olimp – 2</a>, coming on for <a href="/wiki/Aleksandr_Bukharov" title="Aleksandr Bukharov">Aleksandr Bukharov</a> at the 56th minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> He started in the return leg on 3 August 2016 and scored his first Champions League goal in the 47th minute, helping Rostov advance 4–2 on aggregate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> He scored his second Champions League goal on 24 August 2016 in the return leg of the playoff round match against <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a>, scoring the opening goal in a 4–0 victory which resulted in Rostov advancing to the group stage of the Champions League for the first time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Azmoun scored on 1 November 2016 in a Rostov's 2–1 Champions League group stage loss against <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a>. In the process, he became the first Iranian to score in the Champions League group stage since <a href="/wiki/Ali_Karimi" title="Ali Karimi">Ali Karimi</a> in 2005. On 23 November 2016, Azmoun scored his second Champions League goal in the group stage against <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a> at home, leading Rostov to a 3–2 victory and their first ever official Champions League group stage win.</p>
<p>After his good performances and goals in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>, Azmoun drew interest from European giants such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympique_Marseille" title="Olympique Marseille">Marseille</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bayer_04_Leverkusen" title="Bayer 04 Leverkusen">Bayer Leverkusen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 6 November 2016 Azmoun scored his first <a href="/wiki/Russian_Premier_League" title="Russian Premier League">Russian Premier League</a> goal of the 2016–17 season in a 4–1 victory. He scored his second goal of the season on 28 November against <a href="/wiki/FC_Anzhi_Makhachkala" title="FC Anzhi Makhachkala">Anzhi Makhachkala</a>.</p>
<p>On 26 January 2017, FIFA rejected appeals by <a href="/wiki/FC_Rubin_Kazan" title="FC Rubin Kazan">FC Rubin Kazan</a> related to Azmoun's transfer to Rostov and ruled that there were no obligations outstanding towards Rubin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardar_Azmoun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth">Youth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardar_Azmoun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Youth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Azmoun broke out while representing the <a href="/wiki/Iran_national_under-20_football_team" title="Iran national under-20 football team">Iran U-20</a> side, becoming top scorer of the <a href="/wiki/2012_Commonwealth_of_Independent_States_Cup" title="2012 Commonwealth of Independent States Cup">2012 Commonwealth of Independent States Cup</a>, scoring seven goals in six games. On 8 October 2013, he played for the U-20 team during the <a href="/wiki/2014_AFC_U-19_Championship_qualification" title="2014 AFC U-19 Championship qualification">2014 AFC U-19 Championship qualification</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kerman" title="Kerman">Kerman</a>. In a match against <a href="/wiki/Lebanon_national_under-20_football_team" title="Lebanon national under-20 football team">Lebanon</a>, he captained the team by scoring two goals, assisting one and drawing a penalty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>Azmoun was called into the <a href="/wiki/Iran_national_under-23_football_team" title="Iran national under-23 football team">U-23's</a> team for <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 2016 Summer Olympics">Olympic</a> <a href="/wiki/2016_AFC_U-23_Championship_qualification" title="2016 AFC U-23 Championship qualification">qualifiers</a> in March 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> He was the <a href="/wiki/2016_AFC_U-23_Championship_qualification" title="2016 AFC U-23 Championship qualification">tournament</a>'s leading goalscorer going into the final matchday with 4 goals in 3 matches. As the <a href="/wiki/2016_AFC_U-23_Championship" title="2016 AFC U-23 Championship">2016 AFC U-23 Championship</a> was not held during the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_International_Match_Calendar" title="FIFA International Match Calendar">FIFA International Match Calendar</a>, he was not released by Rostov.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Senior">Senior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardar_Azmoun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Senior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 5 October 2013, Azmoun was called up to the <a href="/wiki/Iran_national_football_team" title="Iran national football team">Iranian national football team</a> against <a href="/wiki/Thailand_national_football_team" title="Thailand national football team">Thailand</a> by <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Queiroz" title="Carlos Queiroz">Carlos Queiroz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> He was selected in Iran's 28-man provisional squad for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> by Queiroz but was one of five players being dropped from the final squad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>Azmoun made his senior debut on 26 May 2014 in a friendly match against <a href="/wiki/Montenegro_national_football_team" title="Montenegro national football team">Montenegro</a>, coming on as a substitute in the 60th minute for <a href="/wiki/Reza_Ghoochannejhad" title="Reza Ghoochannejhad">Reza Ghoochannejhad</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> However, he was not included in Iran's squad for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>, announced on 1 June 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2014wcsquad_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-2014wcsquad-46">[46]</a></sup> He scored his first goal on 18 November 2014 in a 1–0 friendly win over <a href="/wiki/South_Korea_national_football_team" title="South Korea national football team">South Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p>Azmoun was called into Iran's <a href="/wiki/2015_AFC_Asian_Cup" title="2015 AFC Asian Cup">2015 AFC Asian Cup</a> squad on 30 December 2014 by Carlos Queiroz.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2015acsquad_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-2015acsquad-48">[48]</a></sup> He scored a goal in the next match, a 1–0 friendly win over <a href="/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93Iraq_football_rivalry" title="Iran–Iraq football rivalry">rivals</a> <a href="/wiki/Iraq_national_football_team" title="Iraq national football team">Iraq</a>. He then scored the winner in the second game of the Asian Cup with a great piece of individual skill against <a href="/wiki/Qatar_national_football_team" title="Qatar national football team">Qatar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> He also opened the scoring against <a href="/wiki/Iraq_national_football_team" title="Iraq national football team">Iraq</a> in the quarter-finals with a header.</p>
<p>On 16 June 2015, Azmoun scored a goal against <a href="/wiki/Turkmenistan_national_football_team" title="Turkmenistan national football team">Turkmenistan</a> in a <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_%E2%80%93_AFC_Second_Round" title="2018 FIFA World Cup qualification – AFC Second Round">2018 FIFA World Cup qualifier</a>. On 3 September 2015, he scored his first senior brace in a 6–0 defeat of <a href="/wiki/Guam_national_football_team" title="Guam national football team">Guam</a> in the World Cup qualifiers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> He scored his first <a href="/wiki/Hat-trick" title="Hat-trick">hat-trick</a> for Iranian national team in 3–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Macedonia_national_football_team" title="Macedonia national football team">Macedonia</a> at <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_Arena" title="Philip II Arena">Philip II Arena</a> on 2 June 2016.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardar_Azmoun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Azmoun has been praised for his aerial ability, creativity, and bursts of acceleration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> He has been referred to as a young <a href="/wiki/Zlatan_Ibrahimovi%C4%87" title="Zlatan Ibrahimović">Zlatan Ibrahimović</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> and has also been called the <i>Iranian Messi</i> by mainly British media.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metro.co.uk_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-metro.co.uk-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardar_Azmoun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Azmoun is of <a href="/wiki/Iranian_Turkmen" title="Iranian Turkmen">Iranian Turkmen</a> origin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> He speaks <a href="/wiki/Turkmen_language" title="Turkmen language">Turkmen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a> fluently, and is fond of riding on <a href="/wiki/Equestrianism" title="Equestrianism">horseback</a>. He communicates with <a href="/wiki/Kurban_Berdyev" title="Kurban Berdyev">Kurban Berdyev</a>, his Turkmenistani coach at <a href="/wiki/FC_Rubin_Kazan" title="FC Rubin Kazan">FC Rubin Kazan</a> and <a href="/wiki/FC_Rostov" title="FC Rostov">FC Rostov</a>, in Turkmen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> He decided to wear number 69 at Kazan as a tribute to his hometown's <a href="/wiki/Vehicle_registration_plates_of_Iran" title="Vehicle registration plates of Iran">vehicle registration plate</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Navad_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Navad-3">[3]</a></sup> Azmoun has stated he is a supporter of Spanish club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF" title="Real Madrid CF">Real Madrid</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> in the future he has expressed interest in playing for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tractor_Sazi" title="Tractor Sazi">Tractor Sazi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf_Pro_League" title="Persian Gulf Pro League">Persian Gulf Pro League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>He is the son of Khalil Azmoun, a former <a href="/wiki/Iran_men%27s_national_volleyball_team" title="Iran men's national volleyball team">Iranian national volleyball team</a> player who has coached several volleyball teams, such as Golgohar Sirjan and <a href="/wiki/Javaheri_Gonbad_VC" title="Javaheri Gonbad VC">Javaheri Gonbad VC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
