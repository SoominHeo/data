<p><b>Sar-e Pol</b>, also spelled <b>Sari Pul</b> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="fa" xml:lang="fa">سرپل</span>‎‎; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pashto_language" title="Pashto language">Pashto</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ps" xml:lang="ps">سرپل</span>‎), is one of the thirty-four <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Afghanistan" title="Provinces of Afghanistan">provinces</a> of <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, located in the north of the country. It borders <a href="/wiki/Jowzjan_Province" title="Jowzjan Province">Jowzjan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Balkh_Province" title="Balkh Province">Balkh</a> to the west and north, Ghor Province to the south, and <a href="/wiki/Samangan_Province" title="Samangan Province">Samangan</a> to the east. The province is divided into 7 <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Afghanistan" title="Districts of Afghanistan">districts</a> and contains 896 villages. It has a population of about 532,000,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cso_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-cso-1">[1]</a></sup> which is multi-ethnic and mostly a tribal society. The province was created in 1988, with the support of northern Afghan politician <a href="/wiki/Sayed_Nasim_Mihanparast" title="Sayed Nasim Mihanparast">Sayed Nasim Mihanparast</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nojumi2002_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nojumi2002-2">[2]</a></sup> The city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sar-e_Pol_(city)" title="Sar-e Pol (city)">Sar-e Pol</a> serves as the provincial capital.</p>

<p>Between the early 16th century and the mid-18th century, the territory was ruled by the <a href="/wiki/Khanate_of_Bukhara" title="Khanate of Bukhara">Khanate of Bukhara</a>. It was given to <a href="/wiki/Ahmad_Shah_Durrani" title="Ahmad Shah Durrani">Ahmad Shah Durrani</a> by <a href="/wiki/Mohammad_Murad_Beg" title="Mohammad Murad Beg">Murad Beg</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bukhara" title="Bukhara">Bukhara</a> after a treaty was signed in or about 1750, and became part of the <a href="/wiki/Durrani_Empire" title="Durrani Empire">Durrani Empire</a>. It was ruled by the <a href="/wiki/Durrani_dynasty" title="Durrani dynasty">Durranis</a> followed by the <a href="/wiki/Barakzai_dynasty" title="Barakzai dynasty">Barakzai dynasty</a>. The area was untouched by the <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">British</a> during the three <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Afghan_War" title="Anglo-Afghan War">Anglo-Afghan wars</a> that were fought in the 19th and 20th centuries. It remained peaceful for about one hundred years until the 1980s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviet_war_in_Afghanistan" title="Soviet war in Afghanistan">Soviet war in Afghanistan</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_history">Recent history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sar-e_Pol_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Recent history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_war_in_Afghanistan_(1996%E2%80%932001)" title="Civil war in Afghanistan (1996–2001)">Afghan Civil War</a>, the area was controlled by forces loyal to <a href="/wiki/Abdul_Rashid_Dostum" title="Abdul Rashid Dostum">Abdul Rashid Dostum</a>. It was captured by the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_Emirate_of_Afghanistan" title="Islamic Emirate of Afghanistan">Taliban</a> in 1998. <a href="/wiki/Aminullah_Amin" title="Aminullah Amin">Aminullah Amin</a>, the first senior member of the Taliban to be captured, was the former governor of the province.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bbc20011220_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bbc20011220-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a>-led <a href="/wiki/Provincial_Reconstruction_Team" title="Provincial Reconstruction Team">Provincial Reconstruction Team</a> (PRT), which has been based in Mazar-e Sharif since about 2005 and responsible for four provinces including Sar-e Pol, established an office and some troops in the province. The <a href="/wiki/Afghan_National_Security_Forces" title="Afghan National Security Forces">Afghan National Security Forces</a> (ANFS) began expanding in the last decade and slowly took over security from <a href="/wiki/International_Security_Assistance_Force" title="International Security Assistance Force">International Security Assistance Force</a> (ISAF). The Afghanistan-Turkmenistan border is maintained by the <a href="/wiki/Afghan_Border_Police" title="Afghan Border Police">Afghan Border Police</a> (ABP) while law and order for the rest of the province is provided by the <a href="/wiki/NATO_Training_Mission-Afghanistan" title="NATO Training Mission-Afghanistan">NATO-trained</a> <a href="/wiki/Afghan_National_Police" title="Afghan National Police">Afghan National Police</a> (ANP).</p>
<p>In 2009, the provincial Police Chief stated that weapons had been collected from many people and three districts, namely, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sangcharak" title="Sangcharak">Sangcharak</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gosfandi_District" title="Gosfandi District">Gosfandi</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sozama_Qala" title="Sozama Qala">Sozama Qala</a> areas termed as the peaceful districts of the province. In operations against militants, the police chief said they had arrested a prominent Taliban commander Mullah Nader along with 11 other people during the recent operations. He said scores of kilograms of <a href="/wiki/Hashish" title="Hashish">hashish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Opium" title="Opium">opium</a> had also been seized from people during the operations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The biggest threat to travelers in Sar-i-Pul remains highway bandits and thieves, corrupt militiamen and police, and road hazards. The Taliban have small cadres operating throughout the province but rely on larger support networks in neighboring provinces.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics_and_governance">Politics and governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sar-e_Pol_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Politics and governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_Afghanistan" title="Politics of Afghanistan">Politics of Afghanistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_governors_of_Sar-e_Pol" title="List of governors of Sar-e Pol">List of governors of Sar-e Pol</a></div>
<p>The current <a href="/wiki/List_of_governors_of_Sar-e_Pol" title="List of governors of Sar-e Pol">governor</a> of the province is <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mohammad_Zahir_Wahdat&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mohammad Zahir Wahdat (page does not exist)">Mohammad Zahir Wahdat</a>. The city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sar-e_Pol_(city)" title="Sar-e Pol (city)">Sar-e Pol</a> serves as the capital of the province. All <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement_in_Afghanistan" title="Law enforcement in Afghanistan">law enforcement</a> activities throughout the province are managed by the <a href="/wiki/Afghan_National_Police" title="Afghan National Police">Afghan National Police</a> (ANP). The provincial police chief represents the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_the_Interior_(Afghanistan)" title="Ministry of the Interior (Afghanistan)">Ministry of the Interior</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kabul" title="Kabul">Kabul</a>. The ANP is backed by other <a href="/wiki/Afghan_National_Security_Forces" title="Afghan National Security Forces">Afghan National Security Forces</a> (ANSF), including the <a href="/wiki/International_Security_Assistance_Force" title="International Security Assistance Force">NATO-led forces</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sayed_Anwar_Rahmati" title="Sayed Anwar Rahmati">Sayed Anwar Rahmati</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Aziza_Jalis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Aziza Jalis (page does not exist)">Aziza Jalis</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sayed_Anwar_Sadat&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sayed Anwar Sadat (page does not exist)">Sayed Anwar Sadat</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mohammad_Hossein_Fahimi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mohammad Hossein Fahimi (page does not exist)">Mohammad Hossein Fahimi</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sayed_Mohammad_Hossein_Sharifi_Balkhabi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sayed Mohammad Hossein Sharifi Balkhabi (page does not exist)">Sayed Mohammad Hossein Sharifi Balkhabi</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Haji_Khair_Mohammad_Imaq&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Haji Khair Mohammad Imaq (page does not exist)">Haji Khair Mohammad Imaq</a> are the current <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wolesi_Jirga" title="Wolesi Jirga">Wolesi Jirga</a> members. They represent Sar-e Pol province in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Afghan_National_Assembly" title="Afghan National Assembly">Afghan National Assembly</a> (Afghan Parliament) in Kabul.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iec_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-iec-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Healthcare">Healthcare</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sar-e_Pol_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Healthcare">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Health_in_Afghanistan" title="Health in Afghanistan">Health in Afghanistan</a></div>
<p>The percentage of households with clean drinking water increased from 8% in 2005 to 15% in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cimicweb.org_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-cimicweb.org-6">[6]</a></sup> The percentage of births attended to by a skilled birth attendant increased from 0% in 2005 to 20% in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cimicweb.org_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-cimicweb.org-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sar-e_Pol_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Education_in_Afghanistan" title="Education in Afghanistan">Education in Afghanistan</a></div>
<p>The overall literacy rate (6+ years of age) increased from 12% in 2005 to 23% in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cimicweb.org_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-cimicweb.org-6">[6]</a></sup> The overall net enrolment rate (6–13 years of age) increased from 22% in 2005 to 46% in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cimicweb.org_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-cimicweb.org-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sar-e_Pol_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_Afghanistan" title="Geography of Afghanistan">Geography of Afghanistan</a></div>
<p>Sar-e Pol is a mountainous province, especially in its southern part. It covers an area of 16,360 km. Three quarters (75%) of the province is mountainous or semi mountainous terrain while one-seventh (14%) of the area is made up of flat land. The province is divided into 7 <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Afghanistan" title="Districts of Afghanistan">districts</a>, containing 896 of villages.</p>

<p>The total population of the province is about 532,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cso_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-cso-1">[1]</a></sup> According to the <a href="/wiki/Naval_Postgraduate_School" title="Naval Postgraduate School">Naval Postgraduate School</a>, the ethnic groups of the province are as follows: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tajik_people" title="Tajik people">Tajik</a>(+<a href="/wiki/Sayyid" title="Sayyid">Sayyeds</a>) (majority), <a href="/wiki/Uzbeks" title="Uzbeks">Uzbeks</a>, followed by small <a href="/wiki/History_of_Arabs_in_Afghanistan" title="History of Arabs in Afghanistan">Arabs</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pashtun_people" title="Pashtun people">Pashtun</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hazara_people" title="Hazara people">Hazara</a> communities.</p>
<p>The languages spoken in and around the province are <a href="/wiki/Dari_language" title="Dari language">Persian language</a> (main language of the province), <a href="/wiki/Uzbek_language" title="Uzbek language">Uzbeki</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pashto_language" title="Pashto language">Pashto</a>. All the inhabitants practice <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>, majority of which are <a href="/wiki/Sunni_Islam" title="Sunni Islam">Sunnis</a>, while the <a href="/wiki/Shia_Islam_in_Afghanistan" title="Shia Islam in Afghanistan">Shias</a> make up the minority.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sar-e_Pol_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Economy_of_Afghanistan" title="Economy of Afghanistan">Economy of Afghanistan</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mining_in_Afghanistan" title="Mining in Afghanistan">Mining</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agriculture_in_Afghanistan" title="Agriculture in Afghanistan">agriculture</a> are the main industries of the province. The Government of Afghanistan signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/China_National_Petroleum_Corporation" title="China National Petroleum Corporation">China National Petroleum Corporation</a> (CNPC) for the development of oil blocks in the <a href="/wiki/Amu_Darya" title="Amu Darya">Amu Darya</a> basin, a project expected to earn billions of dollars over two decades; the deal covers drilling and a refinery in the northern provinces of Sar-e Pol and <a href="/wiki/Faryab_Province" title="Faryab Province">Faryab</a> and is the first international oil production agreement entered into by the Afghan government for several decades.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Production of the Afghan oil began in October 2012, which is expected to increase to 1 million barrels per year in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shalizi_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shalizi-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Communications in and around the province are provided by <a href="/wiki/Afghan_Wireless" title="Afghan Wireless">Afghan Wireless</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roshan_(telco)" title="Roshan (telco)">Roshan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emirates_Telecommunications_Corporation" title="Emirates Telecommunications Corporation">Etisalat</a>, and <a href="/wiki/MTN_Group" title="MTN Group">MTN Group</a>.</p>
