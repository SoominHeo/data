<p><b>Sarpsborg 08 Fotballforening</b> is a Norwegian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Sarpsborg" title="Sarpsborg">Sarpsborg</a>, currently playing in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tippeligaen" title="Tippeligaen">Tippeligaen</a>. Sarpsborg 08 played in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adeccoligaen" title="Adeccoligaen">Adeccoligaen</a> from 2005 to 2010. In 2010, the club was promoted to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tippeligaen" title="Tippeligaen">Tippeligaen</a>, the top league in <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, but finished last and was relegated back to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adeccoligaen" title="Adeccoligaen">Adeccoligaen</a> in 2011. In 2012, they were promoted again. They play their home games at <a href="/wiki/Sarpsborg_Stadion" title="Sarpsborg Stadion">Sarpsborg Stadion</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sarpsborg_08_FF&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Football in <a href="/wiki/Sarpsborg" title="Sarpsborg">Sarpsborg</a> has traditionally been dominated by <a href="/wiki/Sarpsborg_FK" title="Sarpsborg FK">Sarpsborg FK</a> (SFK), which won the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Football_Cup" title="Norwegian Football Cup">Norwegian Cup</a> six times and played 20 seasons in the top division, and to a lesser extent <a href="/wiki/IL_Sparta" title="IL Sparta">IL Sparta</a>, which won the Norwegian Cup in <a href="/wiki/1952_Norwegian_Football_Cup" title="1952 Norwegian Football Cup">1952</a> and played nine seasons in the top division. However, after SFK's relegation from the <a href="/wiki/1974_Norwegian_First_Division" title="1974 Norwegian First Division">1974</a>, no teams from Sarpsborg played in the top division for the next two decades. In 1999, 16 teams in the Sarpsborg-area joined forces to create a team that could compete in the top divisions of Norwegian football, called <b>Sarpsborg Fotball</b>, which overtook SFK's spot in the league-system. In its first season the team was relegated from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2000_Norwegian_Second_Division" title="2000 Norwegian Second Division">Second Division</a>, and several clubs including both SFK and Sparta, withdrew from the collaboration.</p>
<p>The remaining clubs in the "Sarpsborg Fotball"-project formed a new club called <b>Borg Fotball</b>, which won promotion to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norwegian_Second_Division" title="Norwegian Second Division">Second Division</a> in 2002, but was relegated again in 2003. Meanwhile, Sparta was promoted to the Second Division the same season, and re-entered the collaboration club which now was renamed <b>FK Sparta Sarpsborg</b>. The club earned promotion to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adeccoligaen" title="Adeccoligaen">Adeccoligaen</a> (the second tier of Norwegian football) in 2005.</p>
<p>At the end of the 2007 season, the last major club in the district, Sarpsborg FK, joined the collaboration of teams and ended a rather long and hefty rivalry between the two clubs. At the same time, in an effort to unite the different fractions within the collaboration, the jersey was changed and the club renamed itself <b>Sarpsborg Sparta FK</b>. This name remained a source of contention throughout the 2008 season, however, and a new name, <b>Sarpsborg 08 FF</b>, was adopted in 2009.</p>
<p>The 2009 season started badly for Sarpsborg, and it appeared they would be fighting against relegation to the Second Division. The situation worsened when the <a href="/wiki/Football_Association_of_Norway" title="Football Association of Norway">Football Association of Norway</a> determined that Sarpsborg 08 had submitted an unrealistic budget when they obtained their license to play in the First Division, and were penalized three points in June, leaving Sarpsborg in a relegation spot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> However, after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roar_Johansen_(football_coach)" title="Roar Johansen (football coach)">Roar Johansen</a> became coach, the second part of the season went well for the team, which not only climbed out of the relegation spots, but finished fifth, allowing them to participate in the qualification matches for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010_Norwegian_Premier_League" title="2010 Norwegian Premier League">2010 Norwegian Premier League</a>. In the semi-final match of the qualification, Sarpsborg defeated local rivals <a href="/wiki/Fredrikstad_FK" title="Fredrikstad FK">Fredrikstad FK</a> 2–0 (who were therefore relegated from the Premier League).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In the home match of the qualification final, Sarpsborg defeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KIL_Toppfotball" title="KIL Toppfotball">Kongsvinger</a> 3–2,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> but lost the second leg 3–1 and remained in the First Division.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2010 season ended with a 4–0 home-win against <a href="/wiki/Alta_IF" title="Alta IF">Alta</a>. This meant that the team ended in second place, after league-winner <a href="/wiki/Sogndal_Fotball" title="Sogndal Fotball">Sogndal</a>, and won the right to play in the 2011 edition of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tippeligaen" title="Tippeligaen">Tippeligaen</a>.</p>
<p>The 2011 season started with a surprising 3–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Molde_FK" title="Molde FK">Molde</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ole_Gunnar_Solskj%C3%A6r" title="Ole Gunnar Solskjær">Ole Gunnar Solskjær</a> first match as manager of Molde. But after a good start, Sarpsborg was relegated back to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adeccoligaen" title="Adeccoligaen">Adeccoligaen</a> on 23 October 2011, when they lost 3–0 away at <a href="/wiki/SK_Brann" title="SK Brann">Brann</a> with three rounds left to play.</p>
<p>On 3 January 2013, former Sheffield United striker <a href="/wiki/Brian_Deane" title="Brian Deane">Brian Deane</a> was appointed as coach.</p>
