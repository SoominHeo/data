<p><b>Anderson's crocodile newt</b>, <b>Anderson's newt</b>, <b>Ryukyu spiny newt</b>, or <b>Japanese warty newt</b> (<i><b>Echinotriton andersoni</b></i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Salamander" title="Salamander">salamander</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Salamandridae" title="Salamandridae">Salamandridae</a> family found in the <a href="/wiki/Ryukyu_Islands" title="Ryukyu Islands">Ryukyu Islands</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, and, at least formerly, Guanyinshan (=Mount Guanyin) in northern <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, where it is now believed to be extinct.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-frost_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-frost-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SoC_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-SoC-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anderson%27s_crocodile_newt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Echinotriton andersoni</i> is a stout, flat salamander. Head is broad and triangular in shape. There are 12–15 conspicuous knob-like lateral glands. Colouration is uniformly dark brown or black, only the underside of the tail, cloacal region, and the soles of the feet are yellow-orange. The maximum size is at least 80 mm (3.1 in) in snout–vent length and 169 mm (6.7 in) in total length.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SoC_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-SoC-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat_and_conservation">Habitat and conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anderson%27s_crocodile_newt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Habitat and conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Its natural <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitats</a> are broad-leaved evergreen forests, secondary forests, grasslands and swamps. It has also been found in and near sugar cane fields. It breeds in standing water such as ponds and temporary pools;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">[1]</a></sup> outside breeding season it is difficult to observe as adult salamanders live in leaf litter, in rocky crevices, and under rocks and logs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SoC_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-SoC-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Echinotriton andersoni</i> is uncommon, and it is threatened by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Habitat_loss" title="Habitat loss">habitat loss</a> and by collection for illegal pet trade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
