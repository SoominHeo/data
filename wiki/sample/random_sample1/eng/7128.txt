<div class="hatnote" role="note">This is a <a href="/wiki/Chinese_name" title="Chinese name">Chinese name</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_surname" title="Chinese surname">family name</a> is <a href="/wiki/Sima_(Chinese_surname)" title="Sima (Chinese surname)">Sima</a>.</div>
<p><b>Sima Hui</b> (birth and death dates unknown), <a href="/wiki/Courtesy_name" title="Courtesy name">courtesy name</a> <b>Decao</b> (德操) and <a href="/wiki/Art_name" title="Art name">pseudonym</a> <b>Shuijing</b> (水鏡; lit. "Water Mirror"), was a hermit who lived in the late <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Han_Dynasty" title="Eastern Han Dynasty">Eastern Han Dynasty</a>. He was from Yingchuan (present-day <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuzhou_City" title="Yuzhou City">Yuzhou City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a>) and lived in northern <a href="/wiki/Jingzhou_(ancient_China)" title="Jingzhou (ancient China)">Jing Province</a> (covering present-day <a href="/wiki/Hubei" title="Hubei">Hubei</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hunan" title="Hunan">Hunan</a>). He was popularly remembered as the one who recommend <a href="/wiki/Zhuge_Liang" title="Zhuge Liang">Zhuge Liang</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pang_Tong" title="Pang Tong">Pang Tong</a> to Liu Bei in the <i><a href="/wiki/Romance_of_the_Three_Kingdoms" title="Romance of the Three Kingdoms">Romance of the Three Kingdoms</a></i>, while it was actually his friend <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pang_Degong&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pang Degong (page does not exist)">Pang Degong</a> who did it in history.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Haohao_Xiansheng">Haohao Xiansheng</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sima_Hui&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Haohao Xiansheng">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sima Hui was known to never mention others' shortcomings or flaws. His reply to both good and bad news would always be "<i>hao</i>" (好; lit. "good" or "yes"). When someone asked him, "How are you?", he replied, "Good." When somebody told him that their son had died, he said, "Very good." His wife chided him for that, saying, "Everyone sees you as a person of good moral conduct so they are willing to share their problems with you. Why do you say 'very good' when someone tells you that his son had died?" Sima Hui replied, "It's also good to hear what you've just said." This gave rise to the <a href="/wiki/Chengyu" title="Chengyu">Chinese idiom</a> <i>haohao xiansheng</i> (好好先生; lit. "Mr. Yes" or "Mr. Good").</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_fiction">In fiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sima_Hui&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In fiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In chapter 37 of <a href="/wiki/Luo_Guanzhong" title="Luo Guanzhong">Luo Guanzhong</a>'s historical novel <i><a href="/wiki/Romance_of_the_Three_Kingdoms" title="Romance of the Three Kingdoms">Romance of the Three Kingdoms</a></i>, Sima is described to be a carefree, wandering man who is well-versed in many arts. He has a close relationship with <a href="/wiki/Pang_Tong" title="Pang Tong">Pang Tong</a>, and refers to Pang as a younger brother.</p>
<p>Sima is also featured in <a href="/wiki/Chan_Mou" title="Chan Mou">Chan Mou</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Manhua" title="Manhua">manhua</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ravages_of_Time" title="The Ravages of Time">The Ravages of Time</a></i> as the teacher of the "Eight Enigmas", a group of elite strategists and advisors.</p>
