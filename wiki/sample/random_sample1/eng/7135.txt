<p><b>Samuel Umtiti</b> (<small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[sa.mɥɛl ym.ti.ti]</a></span>; born 14 November 1993) is a French professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">centre-back</a> for Spanish club <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a> and the <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France national team</a>.</p>
<p>He began his professional career with <a href="/wiki/Olympique_Lyonnais" title="Olympique Lyonnais">Lyon</a> in 2012, winning the <a href="/wiki/Coupe_de_France" title="Coupe de France">Coupe de France</a> and <a href="/wiki/Troph%C3%A9e_des_Champions" title="Trophée des Champions">Trophée des Champions</a> that year. He totalled 170 games and three goals before a €25 million transfer to Barcelona in 2016.</p>
<p>After winning 47 caps and scoring three goals at youth level, including winning the <a href="/wiki/2013_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup">2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup</a>, Umtiti represented <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> at <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">UEFA Euro 2016</a>, where his nation reached the final.</p>

<p>Umtiti was included in a <a href="/wiki/Olympique_Lyonnais" title="Olympique Lyonnais">Lyon</a> squad for the first time on 16 August 2011, remaining an unused substitute as they defeated <a href="/wiki/FC_Rubin_Kazan" title="FC Rubin Kazan">Rubin Kazan</a> 3–1 in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Champions_League_qualifying_phase_and_play-off_round" title="2011–12 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round">play-off round first leg</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He made his professional debut on 8 January 2012, playing the entire match in a 3–1 victory over local rivals <a href="/wiki/AS_Lyon-Duch%C3%A8re" title="AS Lyon-Duchère">Lyon-Duchère</a> in a <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Coupe_de_France#Round_of_64" title="2011–12 Coupe de France">Coupe de France</a> game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Six days later, he made his <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> debut in a 1–0 away defeat against <a href="/wiki/Montpellier_HSC" title="Montpellier HSC">Montpellier</a>, playing the full game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He made a total of 18 appearances in all competitions in his <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Olympique_Lyonnais_season" title="2011–12 Olympique Lyonnais season">first season</a>.</p>
<p>In the 2012–13 season, he became a more regular member of the Lyon first team. On 12 January 2013, against <a href="/wiki/Troyes_AC" title="Troyes AC">Troyes</a> in a 2–1 away win, he scored his first career goal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He finished the season with 32 appearances in all competitions and two goals.</p>
<p>At the end of the 2015-16 season he was named as the "best player of the season" by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympique_lyonnais" title="Olympique lyonnais">Lyon</a> fans, in front of <a href="/wiki/Alexandre_Lacazette" title="Alexandre Lacazette">Alexandre Lacazette</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Barcelona">Barcelona</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Umtiti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Barcelona">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 30 June 2016, Umtiti signed for Spanish <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> side <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a> for a fee of €25 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On 17 August, Umtiti made his first appearance for Barcelona in the second leg of the <a href="/wiki/2016_Supercopa_de_Espa%C3%B1a" title="2016 Supercopa de España">2016 Supercopa de España</a> which Barcelona won 3–0 versus <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla</a> and lifted the trophy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Umtiti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth">Youth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Umtiti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Youth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Umtiti is a <a href="/wiki/France_national_youth_football_team" title="France national youth football team">France youth international</a>, having represented his nation from <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-17_football_team" title="France national under-17 football team">under-17</a> to <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-21_football_team" title="France national under-21 football team">under-21</a> levels. He was an integral member of the team that won France's first ever <a href="/wiki/2013_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup">FIFA U-20 World Cup</a>. He played in almost every game but missed the final against <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_under-20_football_team" title="Uruguay national under-20 football team">Uruguay</a> due to a red card received in the semi-final against <a href="/wiki/Ghana_national_under-20_football_team" title="Ghana national under-20 football team">Ghana</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Senior">Senior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Umtiti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Senior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Umtiti was born and spent his first two years in the Cameroonian city of <a href="/wiki/Yaound%C3%A9" title="Yaoundé">Yaoundé</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Camerounaise_de_Football" title="Fédération Camerounaise de Football">Fédération Camerounaise de Football</a> and former international <a href="/wiki/Roger_Milla" title="Roger Milla">Roger Milla</a> met his advisors in an unsuccessful attempt to get him to represent <a href="/wiki/Cameroon_national_football_team" title="Cameroon national football team">their team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Umtiti, who was uncapped at senior level, was selected by France national team manager <a href="/wiki/Didier_Deschamps" title="Didier Deschamps">Didier Deschamps</a> to be part of his Euro 2016 <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016_squads#France" title="UEFA Euro 2016 squads">squad</a> following an injury to fellow centre-back <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%A9my_Mathieu" title="Jérémy Mathieu">Jérémy Mathieu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Euro_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Euro-9">[9]</a></sup> Umtiti made his senior debut on 3 July by playing every minute of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016_knockout_phase" title="UEFA Euro 2016 knockout phase">quarter-final</a> against <a href="/wiki/Iceland_national_football_team" title="Iceland national football team">Iceland</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Stade_de_France" title="Stade de France">Stade de France</a>, replacing the suspended <a href="/wiki/Adil_Rami" title="Adil Rami">Adil Rami</a>. He became the first outfield player since <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_De_Mich%C3%A8le" title="Gabriel De Michèle">Gabriel De Michèle</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1966_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1966 FIFA World Cup">1966 FIFA World Cup</a> to win his first cap for France by appearing in the finals of a major tournament and was accurate with all 77 of his passes during the match, which France won 5–2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
