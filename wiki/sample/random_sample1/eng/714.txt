<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Type 97 torpedo</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>Type 97</b> was a 17.7 inches (45 cm) diameter torpedo used by the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Imperial Japanese Navy</a> during World War II. Intended for use with Japan's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ko-hyoteki_class_submarine" title="Ko-hyoteki class submarine"><i>Ko-hyoteki</i> class midget submarines</a>, the torpedo was based on the 24-inch diameter <a href="/wiki/Type_93_torpedo" title="Type 93 torpedo">Type 93 "Long Lance"</a> used by Japanese surface vessels, but redesigned to meet the smaller 18-inch physical dimensions of the midgets' two torpedo tubes. Larger Japanese submarines were armed with the 21-inch <a href="/wiki/Type_95_torpedo" title="Type 95 torpedo">Type 95 torpedo</a>.</p>
<p>It was not a great success. Its first operational use was in the <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">attack on Pearl Harbor</a>, after which it was modified as the Type 97 Special, sometimes known as the Type 98. It had a 772 pounds (350 kg) warhead and a range of 3.4 miles (5.5 km) at 44 knots (81 km/h).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Type_97_Special">Type 97 Special</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Type_97_torpedo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Type 97 Special">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Four Type 97 Special torpedoes were fired during the Japanese midget submarine <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Sydney_Harbour" title="Attack on Sydney Harbour">raid on Sydney Harbour</a> in the early hours of June 1, 1942. Two were fired by the midget <i>M-24</i> and aimed at the American heavy cruiser <a href="/wiki/USS_Chicago_(CA-29)" title="USS Chicago (CA-29)">USS <i>Chicago</i></a>, then tied to the No 2. buoy at Garden Island in Sydney Harbour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grose123-139_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Grose123-139-1">[1]</a></sup> Both missed. One struck the harbour wall beneath the depot ship <a href="/wiki/HMAS_Kuttabul_(ship)" title="HMAS Kuttabul (ship)">HMAS <i>Kuttabul</i></a>, sinking the converted ferry and killing 19 Australian and 2 British sailors aboard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Elbourne_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Elbourne-2">[2]</a></sup> The second failed to arm, and ran harmlessly aground at Garden Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grose143_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Grose143-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The remaining two were fired in Sydney Harbour by the midget <i>M-21</i>. The timing of their firing and their target, if any, are unknown. <i>M-21</i> had been rammed and depth charged by <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HMAS_Yandra" title="HMAS Yandra">HMAS Yandra</a></i> at the entrance to Sydney Harbour around 2300 on May 31, but managed to recover sufficiently to enter the harbour at 03:01 on June 1. M-21 was eventually cornered and sunk at 05:15 on June 1 in Taylors Bay inside Sydney Harbour. When the wreck was raised on June 4, both torpedoes had been fired. However the bow caps covering the torpedo tubes failed to drop clear, instead remaining caught in the buckled bow cage of the submarine. The No. 1 torpedo travelled approximately three feet clear of the tube before jamming. The No. 2 torpedo travelled 18 inches before it too jammed.</p>
