<p><b>Saban of Baekje</b> (?–?, r. 234) was the <a href="/wiki/List_of_monarchs_of_Korea" title="List of monarchs of Korea">seventh king</a> of <a href="/wiki/Baekje" title="Baekje">Baekje</a>, one of the <a href="/wiki/Three_Kingdoms_of_Korea" title="Three Kingdoms of Korea">Three Kingdoms of Korea</a>. He was the eldest son of the 6th king <a href="/wiki/Gusu_of_Baekje" title="Gusu of Baekje">Gusu</a>, of the Hae clan.</p>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Samguk_Sagi" title="Samguk Sagi">Samguk Sagi</a>, Saban was found too young to rule and was quickly succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Goi_of_Baekje" title="Goi of Baekje">King Goi</a>, the younger brother of the 5th king <a href="/wiki/Chogo_of_Baekje" title="Chogo of Baekje">Chogo</a>. This is interpreted by scholars as a power struggle within the court, and the rise of the Jin clan over the Hae clan; the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samguk_Yusa" title="Samguk Yusa">Samguk Yusa</a> says that he was deposed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nothing is known of the birth, later life, or death of Saban. However, he is recorded in some <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> chronicles, including <i><a href="/wiki/Shinsen_Sh%C5%8Djiroku" title="Shinsen Shōjiroku">Shinsen Shōjiroku</a></i>, as the progenitor of certain clans of <a href="/wiki/Yamato_period" title="Yamato period">Yamato period</a> Japan. This may indicate that he spent the later part of his life in that country.</p>
