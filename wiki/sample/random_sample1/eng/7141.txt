<p><b>Savonia</b> (<a href="/wiki/Finnish_language" title="Finnish language">Finnish</a>: <span lang="fi" xml:lang="fi"><i>Savo</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a>: <span lang="sv" xml:lang="sv"><i>Savolax</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Historical_provinces_of_Finland" title="Historical provinces of Finland">historical province</a> in the east of <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>. It borders <a href="/wiki/Tavastia_(historical_province)" title="Tavastia (historical province)">Tavastia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ostrobothnia_(historical_province)" title="Ostrobothnia (historical province)">Ostrobothnia</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Finnish_Karelia" title="Finnish Karelia">Karelia</a>. The largest cities in Savonia by population are <a href="/wiki/Kuopio" title="Kuopio">Kuopio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mikkeli" title="Mikkeli">Mikkeli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Savonlinna" title="Savonlinna">Savonlinna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Varkaus" title="Varkaus">Varkaus</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Savonia_(historical_province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Historical_province_of_Savonia_in_Finland.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3429" data-file-width="1957" height="298" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Historical_province_of_Savonia_in_Finland.png/170px-Historical_province_of_Savonia_in_Finland.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Historical_province_of_Savonia_in_Finland.png/255px-Historical_province_of_Savonia_in_Finland.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Historical_province_of_Savonia_in_Finland.png/340px-Historical_province_of_Savonia_in_Finland.png 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Historical_province_of_Savonia_in_Finland.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Historical province of Savonia in bright yellow (modern regions bordered in pink)</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In the 19th century, Savonia was divided between <a href="/wiki/Kuopio_Province" title="Kuopio Province">Kuopio Province</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mikkeli_Province" title="Mikkeli Province">Mikkeli Province</a>. From 1997 to 2010, it lay within the administrative province of eastern Finland. (The provinces have no administrative function today, but live on as a historical legacy.) Since 2010, Savonia has been divided between the regions of <a href="/wiki/Northern_Savonia" title="Northern Savonia">northern Savonia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southern_Savonia" title="Southern Savonia">southern Savonia</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Savonia_(historical_province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The province of Savo represents the original homeland of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Savonians" title="Savonians">Savonians</a>, one of the subgroups that later became assimilated to form the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Finnish_people" title="Finnish people">Finns</a>. It was the heartland of the east Finnish or <a href="/wiki/Savonian_dialects" title="Savonian dialects">Savonian dialects</a>.</p>
<p>The people of Savonia traditionally pursued <a href="/wiki/Slash-and-burn" title="Slash-and-burn">slash-and-burn</a> agriculture, which settlers successfully imported into <a href="/wiki/Ostrobothnia_(region)" title="Ostrobothnia (region)">Ostrobothnia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kainuu" title="Kainuu">Kainuu</a>, <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4rmland" title="Värmland">Värmland</a> in southwestern Sweden, and eastern Norway. Savonian settlers also migrated to Finnish Karelia, <a href="/wiki/Ingria" title="Ingria">Ingria</a> (see: <a href="/wiki/Ingrian_Finns" title="Ingrian Finns">Ingrian Finns</a>) and to southern <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> (see: <a href="/wiki/Forest_Finns" title="Forest Finns">Forest Finns</a>).</p>
<p>Savonia, which had been a part of <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> from the late-13th century, was separated from Sweden when Finland was ceded to <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> in 1809.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Savonia_(historical_province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Traditionally, the Savonian people have often been considered as "sneaky". Recent research has shown that this fame is largely due to misunderstandings caused by the traditional Savonian social indirectness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Heraldry">Heraldry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Savonia_(historical_province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Heraldry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The arms of Savonia are crowned by a count's coronet, though by Finnish tradition this more resembles a Swedish baronal coronet. Blazon: "Sable, a drawn hand bow in and arrow aimed toward dexter chief, or; bow string, arrowhead and feathered tail, argent." The traditional colors of the province are black and gold.</p>
