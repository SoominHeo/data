<p><b>Savusavu</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_Fiji" title="Local government in Fiji">town</a> in the Fijian Province of <a href="/wiki/Cakaudrove_Province" title="Cakaudrove Province">Cakaudrove</a>. The town is located on the south coast of <a href="/wiki/Vanua_Levu" title="Vanua Levu">Vanua Levu Island</a> and had a <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Fiji" title="Demographics of Fiji">population</a> of 3,372 in the 2007 census.</p>
<p>Savusavu is known as "the hidden paradise of Fiji."</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Savusavu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Savusavu is located on Fiji's northern island of Vanua Levu. It can be reached by plane from Nadi (one hour) or by ferry from Suva or Lautoka (approx 12-hour trip).</p>
<p>It's famous for its hot springs, located mostly opposite the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hot_Springs_Hotel_(Savusavu)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hot Springs Hotel (Savusavu) (page does not exist)">Hot Springs Hotel</a> – although at low tide you can see the steam from numerous smaller outlets all along the foreshore. In late 19th century these hot springs for a period of two months turned into 12 – 18 m tall <a href="/wiki/Geyser" title="Geyser">geysers</a>.</p>
<p>The old <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Copra_Shed_Marina&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Copra Shed Marina (page does not exist)">Copra Shed Marina</a>, build in the 19th century, is a major landmark in Savusavu. Renovated in recent years by geologist and Savusavu resident <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Geoff_Taylor_(geologist)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Geoff Taylor (geologist) (page does not exist)">Geoff Taylor</a>, it now houses the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Savusavu_Yacht_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Savusavu Yacht Club (page does not exist)">Savusavu Yacht Club</a>. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fiji_Air&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fiji Air (page does not exist)">Fiji Air</a> offices, Pacific Sun reservations (Formerly Sun Air) offices, a chandlery shop, a geologist's office, a gift shop, travel agency and two rental apartments. There are also moorings for yachts, along with shower and toilet facilities.</p>
<p>Another significant landmark is the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Savarekareka_Mission&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Savarekareka Mission (page does not exist)">Savarekareka Mission</a>, 10 kilometres north of Savusavu. The chapel, the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic</a> mission on Vanua Levu, was built around 1870 and is still functioning.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_activities">Economic activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Savusavu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economic activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Savusavu was originally established as a trading centre for <a href="/wiki/Sandalwood" title="Sandalwood">sandalwood</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beche-de-mer" title="Beche-de-mer">beche-de-mer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Copra" title="Copra">copra</a>, and is the site of a major copra mill. Tourism is growing in importance, owing to its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SCUBA_diving" title="SCUBA diving">SCUBA diving</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yachting" title="Yachting">yachting</a> facilities.</p>
<p>Savusavu hosts a number of resorts, some of which have interesting specialities. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Namale_Resort&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Namale Resort (page does not exist)">Namale Resort</a> hosts regular seminars in the <a href="/wiki/Tony_Robbins" title="Tony Robbins">Tony Robbins</a> Life Mastery programme; the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jean_Michel_Cousteau_Resort&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jean Michel Cousteau Resort (page does not exist)">Jean Michel Cousteau Resort</a> is one of the few 5 star resorts to offer a children's camp; <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Daku_Resort&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Daku Resort (page does not exist)">Daku Resort</a> runs a programme of learning holidays in art, yoga, singing snorkelling and birdwatching throughout the year. The birdwatching tour visits the rare silktail which is only found in the island of Vanua Levu, in a habitat about an hour and a half from Savusavu. The silktail is one of the species listed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Birdlife_International" title="Birdlife International">Birdlife International</a> as being under threat.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Geothermal_power" title="Geothermal power">Geothermal</a> energy is a resource waiting to be tapped. A geological survey has found that Savusavu's hot springs could generate enough electricity to power the entire island of Vanua Levu.</p>
<p>Most land in Fiji is owned by native land owners – the mataqali (extended family unit). Savusavu and surrounding areas has a large amount of freehold land, much of it once used as coconut plantations. Increasingly this land has been subdivided and sold, often to expats seeking a retirement or holiday home. As a result, Savusavu is now home to a small but significant community of Americans, Australians, New Zealanders and Europeans which has helped fuel its economic development.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Savusavu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Incorporated as a Town in 1969, Savusavu is governed by a 9-member <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Savusavu_Town_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Savusavu Town Council (page does not exist)">Town Council</a>, elected for a three-year term. The Councillors elect a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mayor_of_Savusavu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mayor of Savusavu (page does not exist)">Mayor</a> from among themselves for a one-year term, which may be renewed any number of times. Following the victory of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Savusavu_Ratepayers_and_Citizens_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Savusavu Ratepayers and Citizens Party (page does not exist)">Savusavu Ratepayers and Citizens Party</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Fijian_municipal_election,_2005" title="Fijian municipal election, 2005">municipal elections</a> held on 22 October 2005, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ram_Pillay&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ram Pillay (page does not exist)">Ram Pillay</a> was chosen as Mayor, succeeding <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Peni_Naulu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Peni Naulu (page does not exist)">Peni Naulu</a> of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lighthouse_(Savusavu)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lighthouse (Savusavu) (page does not exist)">Lighthouse Party</a>.</p>
<p>In 2009, the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Fiji_Military_Forces" title="Republic of Fiji Military Forces">Military</a>-backed interim government dismissed all municipal governments throughout Fiji and appointed special administrators to run the urban areas. As of 2015, elected municipal government has not been restored. The special administrator of Savusavu, along with nearby <a href="/wiki/Labasa" title="Labasa">Labasa</a>, is <a href="/wiki/Vijay_Chand" title="Vijay Chand">Vijay Chand</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
