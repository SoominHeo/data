<p><b>U.S. Fleet Activities Sasebo</b> is a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> base, in <a href="/wiki/Sasebo,_Nagasaki" title="Sasebo, Nagasaki">Sasebo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, on the island of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB" title="Kyūshū">Kyūshū</a>. It provides facilities for the logistic support of forward-deployed units and visiting operating forces of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Pacific_Fleet" title="United States Pacific Fleet">United States Pacific Fleet</a> and designated tenant activities.</p>

<p>Sasebo has been a naval base since 1883, when Lieutenant Commander <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dg%C5%8D_Heihachir%C5%8D" title="Tōgō Heihachirō">Tōgō Heihachirō</a> nominated the small fishing village to form the nucleus of a base for the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Imperial Japanese Navy</a>. In 1905, ships of the Japanese Navy under Admiral Togo sailed from Sasebo to combat the <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Baltic_Fleet" title="Russian Baltic Fleet">Baltic Fleet</a>, leading to victory for Togo at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Tsushima" title="Battle of Tsushima">Battle of Tsushima</a>.</p>

<p>The Imperial Japanese Navy had approximately 60,000 people working in the dock yard and associated naval stations at the peak of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, outfitting ships, <a href="/wiki/Submarine" title="Submarine">submarines</a> and aircraft. Sasebo was a popular <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Liberty_port&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Liberty port (page does not exist)">liberty port</a> for navy personnel.</p>
<p>In September 1945, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">U.S. Marine Corps</a>' <a href="/wiki/5th_Marine_Division_(United_States)" title="5th Marine Division (United States)">Fifth Division</a> landed at Sasebo, and in June 1946, U.S. Fleet Activities Sasebo was established.</p>
<p>When war broke out in <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a> three years later, Sasebo became the main launching point for the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> and the U.S. Forces. Millions of tons of ammunition, fuel, tanks, trucks and supplies flowed through Sasebo on their way to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Forces" title="United Nations Forces">U.N. Forces</a> in Korea. The number of Americans in Sasebo grew to about 20,000; and some 100 warships and freighters per day swelled the foreign populations still more.</p>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a> ended, the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Self-Defense_Forces" title="Japan Self-Defense Forces">Japan Self-Defense Forces</a> were formed, and <a href="/wiki/Japan_Maritime_Self-Defense_Force#District_Forces" title="Japan Maritime Self-Defense Force">Japan Maritime Self-Defense Force</a> ships began to homeport in Sasebo (<b>Sasebo District Force</b>). The U.S. Fleet Activities continued to support ships of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Seventh_Fleet" title="United States Seventh Fleet">U.S. Seventh Fleet</a>. Service Force ships as well as minecraft made Sasebo their homeport.</p>

<p>The U.S. Fleet Activities Sasebo provided heavy support to the expanded Seventh Fleet during the years of war in Southeast Asia. In the mid-seventies, the U.S. Fleet Activities Sasebo became the <b>Naval Ordnance Facility Sasebo</b>, and fleet visits dwindled to a low level.</p>
<p>On 4 July 1980, this trend was reversed when U.S. Fleet Activities Sasebo regained its name, and Seventh Fleet ships were once again forward-deployed to Sasebo.</p>
<p>U.S. Fleet Activities Sasebo played a vital logistics role in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Operation_Desert_Shield" title="Operation Desert Shield">Operation Desert Shield</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Operation_Desert_Storm" title="Operation Desert Storm">Storm</a> during 1990–91, by serving as a supply point for ordnance and fuel for ships and Marines operating in the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a> theater.</p>
<p>Sasebo was expanded as a result of the <a href="/wiki/East_Asian_foreign_policy_of_the_Barack_Obama_administration" title="East Asian foreign policy of the Barack Obama administration">East Asian foreign policy of the Barack Obama administration</a>, with a doubling of the number of LCACs stationed there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
