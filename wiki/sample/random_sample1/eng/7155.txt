<p><b>Sashiōgi Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">指扇駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sashiōgi-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station on the <a href="/wiki/Kawagoe_Line" title="Kawagoe Line">Kawagoe Line</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nishi-ku,_Saitama" title="Nishi-ku, Saitama">Nishi-ku, Saitama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Prefecture" title="Saitama Prefecture">Saitama Prefecture</a>, Japan, operated by <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">East Japan Railway Company</a> (JR East).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jreast_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-jreast-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sashi%C5%8Dgi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sashiōgi Station is served by the <a href="/wiki/Kawagoe_Line" title="Kawagoe Line">Kawagoe Line</a> between <a href="/wiki/Kawagoe_Station" title="Kawagoe Station">Kawagoe</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cmiya_Station_(Saitama)" title="Ōmiya Station (Saitama)">Ōmiya</a>, and is located 7.7 km from Ōmiya.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kawashima2011_Vol.11_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-kawashima2011_Vol.11-2">[2]</a></sup> Most trains continue beyond Ōmiya on the <a href="/wiki/Saiky%C5%8D_Line" title="Saikyō Line">Saikyō Line</a> to <a href="/wiki/Shinjuku_Station" title="Shinjuku Station">Shinjuku</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shin-Kiba_Station" title="Shin-Kiba Station">Shin-Kiba</a>. Services operate every 20 minutes during the daytime.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jrtt201012_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-jrtt201012-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sashi%C5%8Dgi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station has two <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platforms</a> serving two tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kawashima2011_Vol.11_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-kawashima2011_Vol.11-2">[2]</a></sup> The station entrance was previously located on the south side only, with the platforms connected by a footbridge, but a new north entrance was added in March 2014 as part of a scheme to completely rebuild the station with the ticket barriers located on the second floor, above the platforms, and allowing free passage between the north and south sides of the tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-saitama201403_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-saitama201403-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sashi%C5%8Dgi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station opened on 22 July 1940 in what was then the village of Sashiōgi in <a href="/wiki/Kita-Adachi_District,_Saitama" title="Kita-Adachi District, Saitama">Kita-Adachi District, Saitama</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-teishajo1998_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-teishajo1998-5">[5]</a></sup> The line was electrified on 30 September 1985, from which date through-running began to and from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saikyo_Line" title="Saikyo Line">Saikyo Line</a>. With the privatization of <a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a> (JNR) on 1 April 1987, the station came under the control of JR East.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-teishajo1998_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-teishajo1998-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Work to rebuild the station started in 2012, and the new north entrance to the station was opened on 9 March 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-saitama201403_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-saitama201403-4">[4]</a></sup> Station rebuilding was completed during fiscal 2014, including a new station forecourt on the north side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-saitama201403_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-saitama201403-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
