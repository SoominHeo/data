<p><b>Sayamashi Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">狭山市駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sayamashi-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station on the <a href="/wiki/Seibu_Shinjuku_Line" title="Seibu Shinjuku Line">Seibu Shinjuku Line</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sayama,_Saitama" title="Sayama, Saitama">Sayama, Saitama</a>, Japan, operated by the private railway operator <a href="/wiki/Seibu_Railway" title="Seibu Railway">Seibu Railway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-seibu_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-seibu-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sayamashi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sayamashi Station is served by the <a href="/wiki/Seibu_Shinjuku_Line" title="Seibu Shinjuku Line">Seibu Shinjuku Line</a> between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seibu_Shinjuku_Station" title="Seibu Shinjuku Station">Seibu Shinjuku Station</a> in Tokyo and <a href="/wiki/Hon-Kawagoe_Station" title="Hon-Kawagoe Station">Hon-Kawagoe Station</a> in Kawagoe, and is located 38.6 km from the Seibu Shinjuku terminus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-terada2002_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-terada2002-2">[2]</a></sup> All services (<i>Koedo</i>, Commuter Express, Express, Semi Express, and Local) stop at this station.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sayamashi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station consists of two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Side_platforms" title="Side platforms">side platforms</a> serving two tracks,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-seibu_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-seibu-1">[1]</a></sup> with an elevated station building located above the platforms.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sayamashi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station opened on 21 March 1895.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-terada2002_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-terada2002-2">[2]</a></sup> Station numbering was introduced on all Seibu Railway lines during fiscal 2012, with Sayamashi Station becoming "SS26".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-seibu20120223_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-seibu20120223-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Passenger_statistics">Passenger statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sayamashi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Passenger statistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
