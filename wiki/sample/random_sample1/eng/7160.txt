<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saeko_(actress)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saeko_(actress)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 11, 2007, she married <a href="/wiki/Yu_Darvish" title="Yu Darvish">Yu Darvish</a>. The two chose the 11/11 date, because 11 was husband Yu’s number.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> They had their formal wedding in Hawaii in January 2008. They have two sons, but were divorced in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010, a late push from Japanese voters gave Saeko Darvish (she was number 5) the title of Baseball's Hottest Wife for 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saeko_(actress)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Saeko was born on November 16, 1986 in <a href="/wiki/Miyazaki,_Miyazaki" title="Miyazaki, Miyazaki">Miyazaki, Miyazaki</a>. She is a graduate of <a href="/wiki/Horikoshi_High_School" title="Horikoshi High School">Horikoshi High School</a> a private high school in Nakano, Tokyo, famous for being attended by many Japanese celebrities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tokyograph20080218_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-tokyograph20080218-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>She co-hosted and performed a cover song at the annual Girl Pop Factory show in the summer of 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
