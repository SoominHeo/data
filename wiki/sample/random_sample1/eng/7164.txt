<p><b>Shaoyang</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">邵阳</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">邵陽</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Shàoyáng</span></i>) is a prefecture-level city in southwestern <a href="/wiki/Hunan" title="Hunan">Hunan</a> province, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>, bordering <a href="/wiki/Guangxi" title="Guangxi">Guangxi</a> to the south. Shaoyang has a history of 2500 years and remains an important commercial and transportation city in Hunan. It has a city area and 8 suburban counties, with a population of 7.6 million, the largest in Hunan province.</p>
<p>One of the major forest areas in Hunan, Shaoyang has a forest coverage of 42.7%. The 23,000,000 acres (93,000 km<sup>2</sup>) Nan Shan Pastures is one of the biggest in Southern China which provide dairy products and meat for Hunanese.</p>
<p>Shaoyang is home to <a href="/wiki/Shaoyang_University" title="Shaoyang University">Shaoyang University</a>. The school is composed of former Shaoyang Normal College and Shaoyang College.</p>
<p>Shaoyang dialect of Lou Shao group of dialects of <a href="/wiki/Xiang_Chinese" title="Xiang Chinese">Xiang</a> is spoken here.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shaoyang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shaoyang has a <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoon</a>-influenced, four-season <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen</a> <i>Cfa</i>), with cool, damp winters, and hot, humid summers. Winter begins relatively dry but not sunny and becomes progressively damper and cloudier; spring brings both the most frequent and heaviest rain of the year. Summer is comparatively sunny, while autumn is somewhat dry. The monthly 24-hour average temperatures ranges from 5.2 °C (41.4 °F) in January to 28.2 °C (82.8 °F) in July, while the annual mean is 17.05 °C (62.7 °F). The annual precipitation is about 1,340 mm (53 in).</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shaoyang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Sixth_National_Population_Census_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Sixth National Population Census of the People's Republic of China">Sixth National Census in 2010</a>, Shaoyang's permanent residence population of 7,071,741 ranked it 2nd out of 14 prefecture-level divisions of Hunan; the male-female ratio was 107.95 to 100. Educational attainment levels were as follows: 4.49% bachelor's or higher, 60.01% middle school (<span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">初中</span>) or higher, and the illiteracy rate was 3.24%. There were 1,950,605 households in which 6,913,913 resided, forming 97.77% of the provincial population and resulting in an average of 3.54 persons/household. Age distribution was as follows: 1,512,664 (21.39%) ≤14 years, 4,862,468 (68.76%) 15−64 years, 696,609 (9.85%) 65+ years.</p>

<p>Although it is, by 2010 Census permanent resident population, the second-largest prefecture-level division of Hunan, its GDP (4.62% of provincial output) places it ninth within the province, making it an archetypal example of a large-population, relatively backwards-economy city. The GDP per capita of 12,797 CNY is the lowest in the province, and is only 42.9% of the provincial average, 36.5% of the national average, and 78% of even <a href="/wiki/Guizhou" title="Guizhou">Guizhou</a>'s corresponding figure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.E9.82.B5.E9.98.B32011.E7.BB.8F.E6.B5.8E.E6.8C.87.E6.A0.87_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-.E9.82.B5.E9.98.B32011.E7.BB.8F.E6.B5.8E.E6.8C.87.E6.A0.87-5">[5]</a></sup> Primary, secondary, and tertiary sectors respectively accounted for 23.9%, 38.2%, and 37.9% of the economy.</p>
