<p><i><b>Sawako Decides</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">川の底からこんにちは</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i><b>Kawa no Soko kara Konnichiwa</b></i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, <i>"Hello from the bottom of the river"</i>)</span> is a 2009 Japanese comedy-drama film directed by <a href="/wiki/Yuya_Ishii_(director)" title="Yuya Ishii (director)">Yuya Ishii</a>. The film stars <a href="/wiki/Hikari_Mitsushima" title="Hikari Mitsushima">Hikari Mitsushima</a> as Sawako.</p>
<p><i>Sawako Decides</i> premiered at Tokyo at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=PIA_Film_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="PIA Film Festival (page does not exist)">PIA Film Festival</a> in 2009. At the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fantasia_Film_Festival" title="Fantasia Film Festival">Fantasia Film Festival</a>, the film won the award for Best Feature Film and Best Actress for <a href="/wiki/Hikari_Mitsushima" title="Hikari Mitsushima">Hikari Mitsushima</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sawako_Decides&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In present-day Tokyo, 23-year-old Sawako Kimura (<a href="/wiki/Hikari_Mitsushima" title="Hikari Mitsushima">Hikari Mitsushima</a>) is in her fifth part-time job and with her fifth boyfriend since leaving high school. Kenichi Arai (<a href="/wiki/Masashi_End%C5%8D" title="Masashi Endō">Masashi Endō</a>) is a second-rate designer at the toy company where Sawako works, and has a four-year-old daughter, Kayoko (Kira Aihara). Kenichi keeps pressing Sawako to marry him, but she considers him "only average" (as indeed she considers herself "nothing special"), and on top of that she dislikes children. At the same time, Sawako's uncle, Nobuo (Ryo Iwamatsu) keeps phoning, asking her to return to her hometown, as her father Tadao (Kotaro Shiga) is dying from cirrhosis of the liver and wants her to take over the failing freshwater clam packing company he owns. When Kenichi is fired from his toy-designer job, he persuades the reluctant Sawako to accept the offer to return home, and to take him and Kayoko along into the bargain. The trio arrive in the Pacific coast town of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kawaminami" title="Kawaminami">Kawaminami</a> in southern Japan, where Sawako finds herself scorned by the womenfolk for having run away from home five years earlier with her high school tennis club captain, Yoshio (Ryu Morioka). At this point Sawako is forced to decide what she wants to do with the company, Kenichi, Kayoko, and her whole life.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sawako_Decides&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Actress <a href="/wiki/Hikari_Mitsushima" title="Hikari Mitsushima">Hikari Mitsushima</a> found the story of the film amusing and personally negotiated with director Yuya Ishii, saying that he would "regret it if he didn't cast me."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sawako_Decides&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Sawako Decides</i> premiered at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=PIA_Film_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="PIA Film Festival (page does not exist)">PIA Film Festival</a> in Tokyo on July 30, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmbizasia_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-filmbizasia-2">[2]</a></sup> It premiered theatrically in Japan on May 1, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmbizasia_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-filmbizasia-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Sawako Decides</i> had its North American premiere at the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Asian_Film_Festival" title="New York Asian Film Festival">New York Asian Film Festival</a> on July 1, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It was shown in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London_Film_Festival" title="London Film Festival">London Film Festival</a> in October 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sawako_Decides&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fantasia_Film_Festival" title="Fantasia Film Festival">Fantasia Film Festival</a>, the film won the award for Best Feature Film and Best Actress for <a href="/wiki/Hikari_Mitsushima" title="Hikari Mitsushima">Hikari Mitsushima</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Total_Film" title="Total Film">Total Film</a></i> gave the film three stars out of five, noting that the film's "jokes occasionally get lost in translation".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> gave the film two stars out of five, stating that the film is "somewhere between funny and irritating".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hollywood_Reporter" title="Hollywood Reporter">Hollywood Reporter</a></i> gave the film a mixed review by praising Mitsushima's acting, while stating that the film "does not challenge the status quo that makes life so miserable for non-achievers."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
