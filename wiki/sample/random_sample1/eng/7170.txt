<p>The <b>Savu Sea</b> (or the <b>Sawu Sea</b>) is a small sea within <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> named for the island of <a href="/wiki/Savu" title="Savu">Savu</a> (Sawu) on its southern boundary. It is bounded by Savu and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rai_Jua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rai Jua (page does not exist)">Rai Jua</a> to the south, the islands of <a href="/wiki/Rote_Island" title="Rote Island">Rote</a> and <a href="/wiki/Timor" title="Timor">Timor</a> (split between <a href="/wiki/East_Timor" title="East Timor">East Timor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>) to the east, <a href="/wiki/Flores" title="Flores">Flores</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alor_archipelago" title="Alor archipelago">Alor archipelago</a> to the north/northwest, and the island of <a href="/wiki/Sumba" title="Sumba">Sumba</a> to the west/northwest. Between these islands, it flows into the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> to the south and west, the <a href="/wiki/Flores_Sea" title="Flores Sea">Flores Sea</a> to the north, and the <a href="/wiki/Banda_Sea" title="Banda Sea">Banda Sea</a> to the northeast.</p>
<p>The Savu Sea reaches about 3500 m in depth. It spans about 600 km from west to east, and 200 km from north to south. The largest city on the sea is <a href="/wiki/Kupang" title="Kupang">Kupang</a>, the capital of <a href="/wiki/East_Nusa_Tenggara" title="East Nusa Tenggara">East Nusa Tenggara</a> <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Indonesia" title="Provinces of Indonesia">province</a> on the island of <a href="/wiki/Timor" title="Timor">Timor</a>, with about 450,000 inhabitants.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Extent">Extent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Savu_Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Extent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/International_Hydrographic_Organization" title="International Hydrographic Organization">International Hydrographic Organization</a> (IHO) defines the Savu Sea as being one of the waters of the <a href="/wiki/East_Indian_Archipelago" title="East Indian Archipelago">East Indian Archipelago</a>. The IHO defines its limits as follows:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote>
<p><i>On the North.</i> By the Southern limits of <a href="/wiki/Flores_Sea" title="Flores Sea">Flores Sea</a> [The North coasts of <a href="/wiki/Flores" title="Flores">Flores</a>, <a href="/wiki/Komodo_(island)" title="Komodo (island)">Komodo</a>, Banta and a line to Tanjong Naroe the Northeast point of <a href="/wiki/Sumbawa" title="Sumbawa">Soembawa</a>, thence along its North coast to Tanjong Sarokaja (<span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Savu_Sea&amp;params=8_22_S_117_10_E_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">8°22′S</span> <span class="longitude">117°10′E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">8.367°S 117.167°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">-8.367; 117.167</span></span></span></a></span>)] and <a href="/wiki/Banda_Sea" title="Banda Sea">Banda Sea</a> [<a href="/wiki/125th_meridian_east" title="125th meridian east">125° East</a> ... [on] <a href="/wiki/Alor_Island" title="Alor Island">Alor Island</a>, thence round the East point and along the North coasts of the Alor, <a href="/wiki/Pantar" title="Pantar">Pantar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lembata" title="Lembata">Lomblen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adonara" title="Adonara">Adoenara</a> Islands and thence across the Northern end of Flores Stait to Tanjong Serbete the Eastern extreme of Flores].</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p><i>On the East.</i> By the <a href="/wiki/125th_meridian_east" title="125th meridian east">meridian of 125° East</a> between <a href="/wiki/Alor_Island" title="Alor Island">Alor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Timor" title="Timor">Timor</a>.</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p><i>On the South.</i> By a line from the Southwest point of Timor to the Northeast point of <a href="/wiki/Rote_Island" title="Rote Island">Roti</a>, through this island to its Southwest point, thence a line to Poeloe Dana (<span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Savu_Sea&amp;params=10_49_S_121_17_E_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">10°49′S</span> <span class="longitude">121°17′E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">10.817°S 121.283°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">-10.817; 121.283</span></span></span></a></span>) and to Tanjong Ngoendjoe, the Southern extreme of <a href="/wiki/Sumba" title="Sumba">Soemba</a> and through this island to Tanjong Karosso, its Western point.</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p><i>On the West.</i> A line from Tanjong Karosso (Soemba) to Toro Doro (<span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Savu_Sea&amp;params=8_53_S_118_30_E_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">8°53′S</span> <span class="longitude">118°30′E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">8.883°S 118.500°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">-8.883; 118.500</span></span></span></a></span>) on the South coast of Soembawa.</p>
</blockquote>
