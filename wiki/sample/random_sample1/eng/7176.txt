<p><b>South Ribble</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">non-metropolitan district</a> and <a href="/wiki/Borough" title="Borough">borough</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. Its council is based in <a href="/wiki/Leyland,_Lancashire" title="Leyland, Lancashire">Leyland</a>. The population of the Non Metropolitan District at the 2011 Census was 109,057.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In May 2007, the council was officially declared "Excellent" by the <a href="/wiki/Audit_Commission_(United_Kingdom)" title="Audit Commission (United Kingdom)">Audit Commission</a>, gaining its place among the best five district councils in the country. It is the only non-metropolitan district not to be a member of the <a href="/wiki/District_Councils%27_Network" title="District Councils' Network">District Councils' Network</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Ribble&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 4 October 2007, a team from South Ribble won the LGC Challenge, defeating over 40 other authorities, including larger metropolitan and unitary councils.</p>
<p>South Ribble Borough Council is working in partnership with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chorley_Borough_Council" title="Chorley Borough Council">Chorley Borough Council</a> (a neighbouring authority) on Finance, Procurement, Audit and other centralised functions.</p>
<p>Other notable population areas within South Ribble are <a href="/wiki/Penwortham" title="Penwortham">Penwortham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Longton,_Lancashire" title="Longton, Lancashire">Longton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hutton,_Lancashire" title="Hutton, Lancashire">Hutton</a> (headquarters of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lancashire_Police" title="Lancashire Police">Lancashire Police</a>), <a href="/wiki/Walton-le-Dale" title="Walton-le-Dale">Walton-le-Dale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bamber_Bridge" title="Bamber Bridge">Bamber Bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lostock_Hall" title="Lostock Hall">Lostock Hall</a>, a significant part of Buckshaw Village and <a href="/wiki/Moss_Side,_South_Ribble" title="Moss Side, South Ribble">Moss Side</a>.</p>
<p>The district was formed on 1 April 1974 under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Leyland,_Lancashire" title="Leyland, Lancashire">Leyland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Walton-le-Dale" title="Walton-le-Dale">Walton-le-Dale</a> <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban districts</a>, along with part of <a href="/wiki/Preston_Rural_District" title="Preston Rural District">Preston Rural District</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/South_Ribble_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="South Ribble (UK Parliament constituency)">Parliamentary Constituency of South Ribble</a> includes the <a href="/wiki/West_Lancashire" title="West Lancashire">West Lancashire</a> communities of <a href="/wiki/Tarleton" title="Tarleton">Tarleton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hesketh_Bank" title="Hesketh Bank">Hesketh Bank</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Meols" title="North Meols">Meols</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Council_affiliation">Council affiliation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Ribble&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Council affiliation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_local_elections,_2007" title="United Kingdom local elections, 2007">2007 local elections</a>, South Ribble witnessed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Ribble_Council_election,_2007" title="South Ribble Council election, 2007">landslide Conservative victory</a>, gaining 24 seats to hold 44 of 55 on the borough council.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of April 2011, the council stood as a <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> majority run borough.,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and the Conservatives, despite losing a handful of seats, were relatively comfortably re-elected in May of that year.</p>
