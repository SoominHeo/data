<p><b>Southend-on-Sea</b> (<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:En-uk-SouthendOnSea.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a> </span><a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/bd/En-uk-SouthendOnSea.ogg" title="En-uk-SouthendOnSea.ogg">pronunciation</a></span> <small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:En-uk-SouthendOnSea.ogg" title="File:En-uk-SouthendOnSea.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span>), commonly referred to as simply <b>Southend</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Seaside_resort" title="Seaside resort">seaside resort</a> town and wider <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authorities_of_England" title="Unitary authorities of England">unitary authority area</a> with borough status, in <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, on the north side of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thames_estuary" title="Thames estuary">Thames estuary</a> 40 miles (64 km) east of central London. It is bordered to the north by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rochford_(district)" title="Rochford (district)">Rochford</a> and to the west by <a href="/wiki/Castle_Point" title="Castle Point">Castle Point</a>. It is home to the longest leisure pier in the world, <a href="/wiki/Southend_Pier" title="Southend Pier">Southend Pier</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lonely_planet_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-lonely_planet-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/London_Southend_Airport" title="London Southend Airport">London Southend Airport</a> is located 1.5 <a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">NM</a> (2.8 km; 1.7 mi) north of the town centre.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Originally the "south end" of the village of <a href="/wiki/Prittlewell" title="Prittlewell">Prittlewell</a>, Southend was home to a few poor fisherman huts and farms that lay at the southern extremity of <a href="/wiki/Prittlewell_Priory" title="Prittlewell Priory">Prittlewell Priory</a> land. In the 1790s landowner Daniel Scratton sold off land either side of what was to become the High Street, and the Grand Hotel (now Royal Hotel) and Grove Terrace (now Royal Terrace) were completed by 1794, and stagecoaches from London made it accessible.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Due to the bad transportation links between Southend and London, there was not rapid development during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Georgian_Era" title="Georgian Era">Georgian Era</a> like <a href="/wiki/Brighton" title="Brighton">Brighton</a>. It was the coming of the railways in the 19th Century and the visit of Princess <a href="/wiki/Caroline_of_Brunswick" title="Caroline of Brunswick">Caroline</a> that Southend's status of a seaside resort grew. During the 19th century Southend's pier was first constructed and the Clifftown development built,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> attracting many tourists in the summer months to its seven miles of beaches and bathing in the sea. Good rail connections and proximity to London mean that much of the economy has been based on tourism, and that Southend has been a dormitory town for city workers ever since. <a href="/wiki/Southend_Pier" title="Southend Pier">Southend Pier</a> is the world's longest pleasure <a href="/wiki/Pier" title="Pier">pier</a> at 1.34 mi (2.16 km).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lonely_planet_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-lonely_planet-3">[3]</a></sup> It has suffered fires and ship collisions, most recently in October 2005,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> but the basic pier structure has been repaired each time.</p>
<p>As a holiday destination, Southend declined from the 1960s onwards, as holidaying abroad became more affordable. Southend reinvented itself as the Home of the <a href="/wiki/Access_(credit_card)" title="Access (credit card)">Access (credit card)</a> due to it having one of the UK's first electronic telephone exchanges (it is still home to RBS Card Services – one of the former members of Access), with offices based in the former <a href="/wiki/EKCO" title="EKCO">EKCO</a> factory, Maitland House (<a href="/wiki/Keddies" title="Keddies">Keddies</a>), Victoria Circus and Southchurch Road. Since then, much of the town centre has been developed for commerce and retail, and during the 1960s many original structures were lost to redevelopment – such as the Talza Arcade &amp; Victoria Market (replaced by what is now known as The Victoria Shopping Centre) and Southend Technical College (now a campus of South Essex College,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> on the site of the ODEON Cinema). However, about 6.4 million tourists still visit Southend per year, generating estimated revenues of £200 million a year. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Revenue_and_Customs" title="Her Majesty's Revenue and Customs">H.M. Revenue &amp; Customs</a> (HMRC), (formerly H.M. Customs and Excise), are major employers in the town, and the central offices for the collection of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VAT" title="VAT">VAT</a> are located at Alexander House on Victoria Avenue.</p>
<p>An annual seafront airshow, started in 1986 when it featured a flypast by <a href="/wiki/Concorde" title="Concorde">Concorde</a> whilst on a passenger charter flight, used to take place each May and became one of Europe's largest free airshows. The aircraft flew parallel to the seafront, offset over the sea. The <a href="/wiki/RAF_Falcons" title="RAF Falcons">RAF Falcons</a> parachute display team and RAF <a href="/wiki/Red_Arrows" title="Red Arrows">Red Arrows</a> aerobatics team were regular visitors to the show. The show has not been held since 2012 due to a lack of sponsorship. An attempt to revive the show for September 2015, as the Southend Airshow and Military Festival, failed. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_government_district">Local government district</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Local government district">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Southend-on-Sea was formed as a <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal borough</a> in 1892 with the functions of local government shared with <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex County Council</a>. In 1913 the borough was enlarged by the former area of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leigh_on_Sea_Urban_District" title="Leigh on Sea Urban District">Leigh on Sea Urban District</a>. In 1914 the enlarged Southend gained the status of <a href="/wiki/County_borough" title="County borough">county borough</a>, exempt from <a href="/wiki/County_council" title="County council">county council</a> control and a single-tier of local government. The county borough was enlarged in 1933 by the former area of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shoeburyness_Urban_District" title="Shoeburyness Urban District">Shoeburyness Urban District</a> and part of <a href="/wiki/Rochford_Rural_District" title="Rochford Rural District">Rochford Rural District</a>.</p>
<p>In 1974, under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, Southend became a district of Essex with borough status. However, in 1998 it again became the single tier of local government when it became a <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authority</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Southend-on-Sea_Borough_Council" title="Southend-on-Sea Borough Council">Southend-on-Sea Borough Council</a> was controlled by the Conservative Party after they gained control in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southend-on-Sea_Council_election,_2000" title="Southend-on-Sea Council election, 2000">2000 election</a>. They maintained a minority administration after the 2012 local elections, however the council has been run by a coalition of Lib Dems, Labour and Independents since June 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Most day-to-day decisions are by an eight-member executive headed by the council leader.</p>
<p>The Latin motto, 'Per Mare Per Ecclesiam', emblazoned on the municipal coat of arms, translates as 'By [the] Sea, By [the] Church', reflecting Southend's position between the church at <a href="/wiki/Prittlewell" title="Prittlewell">Prittlewell</a> and the sea as in the <a href="/wiki/River_Thames" title="River Thames">Thames</a> estuary. The town has been twinned with the resort of <a href="/wiki/Sopot" title="Sopot">Sopot</a> in Poland since 1999<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sopot_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-sopot-2">[2]</a></sup> and has been developing three-way associations with <a href="/wiki/Lake_Worth,_Florida" title="Lake Worth, Florida">Lake Worth, Florida</a>.</p>
<p>Southend Borough Council was criticised as one of the worst financially managed local authorities in England by the <a href="/wiki/Audit_Commission_(United_Kingdom)" title="Audit Commission (United Kingdom)">Audit Commission</a> report for 2006–07 one of three to gain only one of four stars, the others being <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>. Areas of criticism were the use of consultants and the spending of <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>3.5 million on taxis during the 2006–07 financial year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>However, in March 2012, Southend Borough Council was awarded the title of 'Council of the Year 2012' by the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Chronicle" title="Local Government Chronicle">Local Government Chronicle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the 2015 general and local election there was a turnout of 62.17%, equating to 80,899 votes cast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-southend.gov.uk_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-southend.gov.uk-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Members_of_Parliament">Members of Parliament</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Members of Parliament">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Rochford_and_Southend_East_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Rochford and Southend East (UK Parliament constituency)">Rochford and Southend East (UK Parliament constituency)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southend_West_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Southend West (UK Parliament constituency)">Southend West (UK Parliament constituency)</a></div>
<p>Southend is represented by two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">MPs</a> at Westminster.</p>
<p>The MP for <a href="/wiki/Southend_West_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Southend West (UK Parliament constituency)">Southend West</a> since 1997 has been <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_David_Amess" title="Sir David Amess">Sir David Amess</a> (<a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a>) who replaced <a href="/wiki/Paul_Channon" title="Paul Channon">Paul Channon</a>.</p>
<p>Since 2005 the MP for <a href="/wiki/Rochford_and_Southend_East_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Rochford and Southend East (UK Parliament constituency)">Rochford and Southend East</a> has been <a href="/wiki/James_Duddridge" title="James Duddridge">James Duddridge</a> (<a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a>), who replaced Sir Teddy Taylor. Despite its name the majority of the constituency is in Southend, Rochford makes up only a small part and the majority of Rochford District Council is represented in <a href="/wiki/Rayleigh_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Rayleigh (UK Parliament constituency)">the Rayleigh constituency</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demography">Demography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Demography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Southend is the seventh most densely populated area in the United Kingdom outside of the London Boroughs, with 38.8 people per hectare compared to a national average of 3.77. By 2006, the majority, or 52% of the Southend population were between the ages of 16–54, 18% were below age 15, 18% were above age 65 and the middle age populace between 55–64 accounted for the remaining 12%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Communities_and_Local_Government" title="Department for Communities and Local Government">Department for Communities and Local Government</a>'s 2010 Indices of Multiple Deprivation <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.imd.communities.gov.uk/" rel="nofollow">[3]</a> data showed that Southend is one of Essex's most deprived areas. Out of 32,482 Lower Super Output Areas in England, area 014D in the Kursaal ward is 99th, area 015B in Milton ward is 108th, area 010A in Victoria ward is 542nd, and area 009D in Southchurch ward is 995th, as well as an additional 5 areas all within the top 10% most deprived areas in England (with the most deprived area having a rank of 1 and the least deprived a rank of 32,482). <a class="external autonumber" href="https://www.theguardian.com/news/datablog/2011/mar/31/deprivation-map-indices-multiple" rel="nofollow">[4]</a> Victoria and Milton wards have the highest percentage of ethnic minority population. Southend has the highest percentage of residents receiving housing benefit (19%) and the third highest percentage of residents receiving council tax benefit in Essex.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Save_the_Children" title="Save the Children">Save the Children</a>'s research data shows that for 2008–09, Southend had 4,000 children living in poverty, a rate of 12%, the same as <a href="/wiki/Thurrock" title="Thurrock">Thurrock</a>, but above the 11% child poverty rate of the rest of Essex. <a class="external autonumber" href="https://www.theguardian.com/news/datablog/2011/feb/23/child-poverty-britain-map" rel="nofollow">[5]</a></p>

<p>In 2006, travel insurance company <a href="/wiki/InsureandGo" title="InsureandGo">InsureandGo</a> relocated its offices from <a href="/wiki/Braintree,_Essex" title="Braintree, Essex">Braintree</a> to Maitland House in Southend-on-Sea. The company brought 120 existing jobs from Braintree and announced the intention to create more in the future.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> However the business announced the plan to relocate to Bristol in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Southend has industrial parks located at Progress Road, Comet and Aviation Ways in Eastwood and Stock Road in Sutton. Firms located in Southend include <a href="/wiki/Olympus_Corporation" title="Olympus Corporation">Olympus Keymed</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hi-Tec_Sports" title="Hi-Tec Sports">Hi-Tec Sports</a> and <a href="/wiki/MK_Electric" title="MK Electric">MK Electric</a>. Southend has declined as a centre for credit card management with only <a href="/wiki/Royal_Bank_of_Scotland" title="Royal Bank of Scotland">Royal Bank of Scotland</a> card services still operating in the town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>A fifth of the working population commutes to London daily. Wages for jobs based in Southend were the second lowest among UK cities in 2015. It also has the fourth highest proportion of people aged over 65. This creates considerable pressures on the housing market. It is the 11th most expensive place to live in Britain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>

<p>Southend is served by two National Rail lines. Running from <a href="/wiki/Southend_Victoria_railway_station" title="Southend Victoria railway station">Southend Victoria</a> north out of the town is the Liverpool Street line, a branch of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Eastern_Main_Line" title="Great Eastern Main Line">Great Eastern Main Line</a> operated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abellio_Greater_Anglia" title="Abellio Greater Anglia">Abellio Greater Anglia</a>. The services operate to <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Street_station" title="Liverpool Street station">London Liverpool Street</a> via <a href="/wiki/Prittlewell_railway_station" title="Prittlewell railway station">Prittlewell</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/London,_Tilbury_and_Southend_Railway" title="London, Tilbury and Southend Railway">London, Tilbury and Southend Railway</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Fenchurch_Street" title="Fenchurch Street">Fenchurch Street</a> line, is operated by <a href="/wiki/C2c" title="C2c">c2c</a> and runs from <a href="/wiki/Shoeburyness_railway_station" title="Shoeburyness railway station">Shoeburyness</a> in the east of the borough, west through <a href="/wiki/Thorpe_Bay_railway_station" title="Thorpe Bay railway station">Thorpe Bay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southend_East_railway_station" title="Southend East railway station">Southend East</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southend_Central_railway_station" title="Southend Central railway station">Southend Central</a> to <a href="/wiki/Fenchurch_Street_railway_station" title="Fenchurch Street railway station">Fenchurch Street</a> in London via <a href="/wiki/Benfleet_railway_station" title="Benfleet railway station">Benfleet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Basildon_railway_station" title="Basildon railway station">Basildon</a> or <a href="/wiki/Tilbury_Town_railway_station" title="Tilbury Town railway station">Tilbury</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barking_station" title="Barking station">Barking</a>. Additionally, one service from Southend Central each weekday evening terminates at Liverpool Street.</p>
<p>From 1910 to 1939 the <a href="/wiki/London_Underground" title="London Underground">London Underground</a>'s <a href="/wiki/District_line" title="District line">District line</a>'s eastbound service ran as far as Southend and Shoeburyness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Besides its main line railway connections, Southend is also the home of two smaller railways. The <a href="/wiki/Southend_Pier_Railway" title="Southend Pier Railway">Southend Pier Railway</a> provides transport along the length of <a href="/wiki/Southend_Pier" title="Southend Pier">Southend Pier</a>, whilst the nearby <a href="/wiki/Southend_Cliff_Railway" title="Southend Cliff Railway">Southend Cliff Railway</a> provides a connection from the promenade to the cliff top above.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tht_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-tht-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Road">Road</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Road">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two A-roads connect Southend with London and the rest of the country, the <a href="/wiki/A127_road" title="A127 road">A127</a> (<i>The Southend Arterial Road</i>), via Basildon and <a href="/wiki/Romford" title="Romford">Romford</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A13_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A13 road (Great Britain)">A13</a>, via Tilbury and <a href="/wiki/London_Docklands" title="London Docklands">London Docklands</a>. Both are major routes, however, within the borough, the A13 is a single carriageway local route, whereas the A127 is entirely dual carriageway. Both connect to the M25 and eventually London.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/No_To_Mob" title="No To Mob">No To Mob</a></i> patrols warn motorists of the presence of active council parking enforcement CCTV vans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Buses">Buses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Buses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Local public transport is provided by two main bus companies, <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Southend" title="Arriva Southend">Arriva Southend</a> (formerly the council-owned Southend Corporation Transport) and <a href="/wiki/First_Essex" title="First Essex">First Essex Buses</a> (formerly NBC/<a href="/wiki/Eastern_National" title="Eastern National">Eastern National</a>/Thamesway). Minor companies include <a href="/wiki/Stephensons_of_Essex" title="Stephensons of Essex">Stephensons of Essex</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Regal_Busways" title="Regal Busways">Regal Busways</a>. Southend has a bus station in Chichester Road which was developed from a temporary facility added in the 1970s. Southend Bus station had sat on the London Road and was run by Eastern National, but it was demolished in the 1980s to make way for a Sainsbury's Supermarket.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Arriva Southend is the only bus company based in Southend, with their depot being located in Short Street (previously on the corner of London Road and Queensway and also a small facility in Tickfield Road).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> First Essex buses are based out of the depot in Hadleigh, but prior to the 1980s Eastern National had depots on the London Road (at the Bus Station) and Fairfax Drive.</p>

<p>London Southend Airport was developed from the military airfield at <a href="/wiki/Rochford" title="Rochford">Rochford</a>, opened as a civil airport in 1935, and now offers scheduled flights to destinations across Europe, corporate and recreational flights, aircraft maintenance and training for pilots and engineers.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Southend-on-Sea has a typical British <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_climate" title="Oceanic climate">marine climate</a> with summer highs of around 22 °C (72 °F) and winters highs being around 7.5 °C (45.5 °F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Southend-on-Sea_climate_averages_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Southend-on-Sea_climate_averages-30">[30]</a></sup> Summer temperatures are generally slightly cooler than those in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>. Frosts are occasional. During the 1981–2010 period there was an average of 31.5 days of air frost. Rainfall was low by British standards and averaged 515 millimetres (20.3 in). Weather station data is available from <a href="/wiki/Shoeburyness" title="Shoeburyness">Shoeburyness</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Southend-on-Sea_climate_averages_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-Southend-on-Sea_climate_averages-30">[30]</a></sup> which is adjacent to Southend in the eastern part of the urban area.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_schools_in_Southend-on-Sea" title="List of schools in Southend-on-Sea">List of schools in Southend-on-Sea</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Secondary_schools">Secondary schools</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Secondary schools">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>All mainstream secondary schools are mixed-sex comprehensives, including <a href="/wiki/Belfairs_Academy" title="Belfairs Academy">Belfairs Academy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cecil_Jones_Academy" title="Cecil Jones Academy">Cecil Jones Academy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chase_High_School" title="Chase High School">Chase High School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southchurch_High_School" title="Southchurch High School">Southchurch High School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shoeburyness_High_School" title="Shoeburyness High School">Shoeburyness High School</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Eastwood_Academy" title="The Eastwood Academy">The Eastwood Academy</a>.</p>
<p>In 2004, Southend retained the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammar_schools_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Grammar schools in the United Kingdom">grammar school system</a> and has four such schools: <a href="/wiki/Southend_High_School_for_Boys" title="Southend High School for Boys">Southend High School for Boys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southend_High_School_for_Girls" title="Southend High School for Girls">Southend High School for Girls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Westcliff_High_School_for_Boys" title="Westcliff High School for Boys">Westcliff High School for Boys</a> and <a href="/wiki/Westcliff_High_School_for_Girls" title="Westcliff High School for Girls">Westcliff High School for Girls</a>.</p>
<p>Additionally there are two single-sex schools assisted by the Roman Catholic Church: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Bernard%27s_High_School_for_Girls" title="St Bernard's High School for Girls">St Bernard's High School for Girls</a> and <a href="/wiki/St_Thomas_More_High_School_for_Boys" title="St Thomas More High School for Boys">St Thomas More High School for Boys</a>. Both, while not grammar schools, contain a grammar stream; entrance is by the same exam as grammar schools.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Further_and_higher_education">Further and higher education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Further and higher education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The main higher education provider in Southend is the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Essex" title="University of Essex">University of Essex</a>. The University established a campus in Southend.</p>
<p>In addition, a number of secondary schools offer further education, but the largest provider is <a href="/wiki/South_Essex_College" title="South Essex College">South Essex College</a> in a new building in the centre of town. Formerly known as South East Essex College, (and previously Southend Municipal College) the college changed name in January 2010 following a merger with Thurrock and Basildon College.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> South Essex College offers 30-degree courses matriculated by the University of Essex, as well as a number of weekend and evening courses. The university's centre in the town is a single building on the High Street. The university has built its own centre in Southend, next to the college building on the site of the Odeon cinema. These buildings form the first two phases of the Southend Campus. Additionally, there is <a href="/wiki/SEEVIC_College" title="SEEVIC College">SEEVIC College</a>. The <a href="/wiki/East_15_Acting_School" title="East 15 Acting School">East 15 Acting School</a>, a drama school, has its second campus there. Also available is the Southend Adult Community College.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Southend has two football teams, one of league stature, <a href="/wiki/Southend_United_F.C." title="Southend United F.C.">Southend United</a>. The other, <a href="/wiki/Southend_Manor_F.C." title="Southend Manor F.C.">Southend Manor</a>, play in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Essex_Senior_League" title="Essex Senior League">Essex Senior League</a>. United will compete in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_One" title="Football League One">Football League One</a> in 2015-16 season after winning the playoff final at Wembley.</p>
<p>There are two <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a> clubs <a href="/wiki/Southend_RFC" title="Southend RFC">Southend RFC</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Westcliff_R.F.C." title="Westcliff R.F.C.">Westcliff R.F.C.</a>, with Southend having the higher ranking team playing in <a href="/wiki/National_League_2_South" title="National League 2 South">National League 2 South</a>. Southend was formerly home to the Essex Eels rugby league team. Southend was home to the <a href="/wiki/Essex_Pirates" title="Essex Pirates">Essex Pirates</a> basketball team that played in the <a href="/wiki/British_Basketball_League" title="British Basketball League">British Basketball League</a> between 2009 and 2011.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Essex_County_Cricket_Club" title="Essex County Cricket Club">Essex County Cricket Club</a> play in Southend one week a season. Previously the festival was held at <a href="/wiki/Chalkwell_Park" title="Chalkwell Park">Chalkwell Park</a> and most recently <a href="/wiki/Southchurch_Park" title="Southchurch Park">Southchurch Park</a>, but it has now moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Garons_Park" title="Garons Park">Garons Park</a> next to the Southend Leisure &amp; Tennis Centre. The only other cricket is local.</p>
<p>The Old Southendians Hockey Club is based at Warner's Bridge in Southend.</p>
<p>The eight-lane, floodlit, synthetic athletics track at Southend Leisure and Tennis Centre is home to <a href="/wiki/Southend-on-Sea_Athletic_Club" title="Southend-on-Sea Athletic Club">Southend-on-Sea Athletic Club</a>. The facilities cover all track and field events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The centre has a 25m swimming pool and a world championship level diving pool with 1, 3, 5, 7 and 10m boards, plus springboards with the only 1.3m in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Entertainment_and_culture">Entertainment and culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Entertainment and culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Southend_Pleasure_Pier">Southend Pleasure Pier</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Southend Pleasure Pier">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Southend-on-Sea is home to the world's longest pleasure pier, built in 1830 and stretching some 1.33 miles (2.14 km) from shore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-test_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-test-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Kursaal">The Kursaal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: The Kursaal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kursaal_(amusement_park)" title="Kursaal (amusement park)">Kursaal (amusement park)</a></div>
<p>The Kursaal was one of the earliest theme parks, built at the start of the 20th century. It closed in the 1970s and much of the land was developed as housing. The entrance hall, a listed building, is a bowling alley arcade operated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Megabowl" title="Megabowl">Megabowl</a> and casino.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Cliff_Lift">The Cliff Lift</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: The Cliff Lift">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Southend_Cliff_Railway" title="Southend Cliff Railway">Southend Cliff Railway</a></div>
<p>A short funicular railway, constructed in 1912, links the seafront to the High Street level of the town. The lift re-opened to the public in 2010, following a period of refurbishment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_seafront_attractions">Other seafront attractions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Other seafront attractions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>An amusement park Adventure Island, formerly known as Peter Pan's Playground, straddles the pier entrance. The seafront houses the "Sea-Life Adventure" <a href="/wiki/Aquarium" title="Aquarium">aquarium</a>.</p>
<p>The cliff gardens, which included <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Never_Never_Land" title="Never Never Land">Never Never Land</a> and a Victorian bandstand were an attraction until slippage in 2003 made parts of the cliffs unstable. The bandstand has been removed and re-erected in Priory Park. Beaches include Three Shells and Jubilee Beach.</p>
<p>A modern vertical lift links the base of the High Street with the seafront and the new pier entrance. The older <a href="/wiki/Southend_Cliff_Railway" title="Southend Cliff Railway">Southend Cliff Railway</a>, a short <a href="/wiki/Funicular" title="Funicular">funicular</a>, is a few hundred metres away.</p>
<p>The London to Southend Classic Car run takes place each summer. It is run by the South Eastern Vintage and Classic Vehicle Club and features classic cars which line the seafront.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Southend Shakedown, organised by Ace Cafe, is an annual event featuring motorbikes and scooters. There are other scooter runs throughout the year, including the Great London Rideout, which arrives at Southend seafront each year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Festival_events">Festival events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Festival events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Southend-on-Sea Film Festival is an annual event that began in 2009 and is run by the White Bus film and theatrical company based at The Old Waterworks Arts Center located inside a Victorian era Old Water Works plant. <a href="/wiki/Ray_Winstone" title="Ray Winstone">Ray Winstone</a> attended the opening night gala in both 2010 and 2011, and has become the Festival Patron.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since 2008 Chalkwell Park becomes home to the Village Green Art &amp; Music Festival for a weekend every July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shopping">Shopping</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Shopping">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are regular vintage fairs and markets in Southend, held at a variety of locations including the Leigh Community Centre, the Kursaal and the Railway Hotel. A record fair is frequently held at West Leigh Schools, Ronald Hill Grove, Leigh on Sea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>Southend High Street runs from the top of Pier Hill in the South, to Victoria Circus in the north. It currently has two shopping centres - the Victoria (built during the 1960s and a replacement for the old Talza Arcade, Victoria Arcade and Broadway Market)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> and The Royals (built late 1980s replacing bottom part of High Street, Grove Road, Ritz Cinema, Grand Pier Hotel). Southend High Street has many chain stores, with Debenhams, Primark and Boots in the Royals, and Next anchoring the Victoria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>This was not always the case with many independent stores closing in the 1970s and 1980s - <a href="/wiki/Keddies" title="Keddies">Keddies</a> (department store), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J_L_Dixons_(department_store)" title="J L Dixons (department store)">J L Dixons (department store)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brightwells" title="Brightwells">Brightwells</a> (department store), Garons (grocers, caterers and cinema),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Owen Wallis (ironmongers and toys),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Bermans (sports and toys),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> J Patience (photographic retailers) &amp; <a href="/wiki/R._A._Jones" title="R. A. Jones">R. A. Jones</a> (jewellers) being the most notable. One of Southend's most notable business, Schofield and Martin, was purchased by <a href="/wiki/Waitrose" title="Waitrose">Waitrose</a> in 1944 with the name being used until the 1960s. The Alexandra Street branch was the first Waitrose store in 1951 to be made self-service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Southend is home to the largest store in the Waitrose portfolio.</p>
<p>A Southend family business started in 1937 that was still going in 2014 was <a href="/wiki/Dixons_Retail" title="Dixons Retail">Dixons Retail</a>. The oldest surviving independent retail business in Southend is Ravens which opened in 1897.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>The borough of Southend has shopping in other areas too. Leigh Broadway and Leigh Road in <a href="/wiki/Leigh-on-Sea" title="Leigh-on-Sea">Leigh-on-Sea</a>, Hamlet Court Road in <a href="/wiki/Westcliff-on-Sea" title="Westcliff-on-Sea">Westcliff-on-Sea</a>, Southchurch Road and London Road are where many of Southend's independent business now reside.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Hamlet Court Road is still home to one of Southend's oldest business - <a href="/wiki/Havens_(department_store)" title="Havens (department store)">Havens</a> opened in 1901.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Parks">Parks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Parks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Southend is home to many recreation grounds. Its first formal park to open was <a href="/wiki/Prittlewell_Square" title="Prittlewell Square">Prittlewell Square</a> in the 19th century. Since then <a href="/wiki/Prittlewell_Priory" title="Prittlewell Priory">Priory Park</a> and Victory Sports Grounds were donated by the town benefactor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R_A_Jones" title="R A Jones">R A Jones</a>, who also has the sports ground Jones Corner Recreation Ground named after his wife. Other formal parks that have opened since are <a href="/wiki/Chalkwell_Park" title="Chalkwell Park">Chalkwell Park</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southchurch_Hall" title="Southchurch Hall">Southchurch Hall</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Southchurch_Park" title="Southchurch Park">Southchurch Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Garon_Park" title="Garon Park">Garon Park</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gunners_Park_and_Shoebury_Ranges" title="Gunners Park and Shoebury Ranges">Gunner Park</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Art.2C_galleries.2C_museums_and_libraries">Art, galleries, museums and libraries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Art, galleries, museums and libraries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Focal Point Gallery is South Essex's gallery for contemporary visual art, promoting and commissioning major solo exhibitions, group and thematic shows, a programme of events including performances, film screenings and talks, as well as offsite projects and temporary public artworks. The organisation is funded by Southend-on-Sea Borough Council and Arts Council England.</p>
<p>Southend Museums Service, part of Southend on Sea Borough Council, operates a number of historic attractions, an art gallery and a museum in the town. These include: <a href="/wiki/Beecroft_Art_Gallery" title="Beecroft Art Gallery">The Beecroft Art Gallery</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southchurch_Hall" title="Southchurch Hall">Southchurch Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prittlewell_Priory" title="Prittlewell Priory">Prittlewell Priory</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Southend_Central_Museum" title="Southend Central Museum">Central Museum on Victoria Avenue</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Old Waterworks Arts Center operates in North Road, Westcliff in the former Victorian water works building. It holds art exhibitions, talks and workshops.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p>Southend has several small libraries located in Leigh, Westcliff, Kent Elms and Southchurch. The central library has moved from its traditional location on Victoria Avenue to the Forum in Elmer Approach, a new facility paid for by Southend Council, South Essex College and The University of Essex. It replaced the former Farringdon Multi Story Car Park. The old Central Library building has become home to the Beecroft Gallery.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Theatres">Theatres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Theatres">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are three theatres. The <a href="/wiki/Cliffs_Pavilion" title="Cliffs Pavilion">Cliffs Pavilion</a> is a large building to host concerts and performances on ice, opening in 1964. The Edwardian <a href="/wiki/Palace_Theatre,_Westcliff-on-Sea" title="Palace Theatre, Westcliff-on-Sea">Palace Theatre</a> is a grade II building built in 1912. It shows plays by professional troupes and repertory groups, as well as comedy acts. The theatre has two circles and the steepest rake in Britain. Part of the theatre was a smaller venue called The Dixon Studio. They are both owned by Southend Council and run by Southend Theatres Ltd.</p>
<p>The most recent theatre was the <a href="/wiki/New_Empire_Theatre" title="New Empire Theatre">New Empire Theatre</a>. It was, unlike the other two, privately owned. It was used more by amateur groups. The theatre was converted from the old ABC Cinema, which had been the Empire Theatre built in 1896. The New Empire theatre closed in 2009 after a dispute between the trust that run the theatre and its owners. The building was badly damaged by fire on Saturday 1 August 2015<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p>Musical shows are also shown at the Plaza, a Christian community centre and concert hall based on Southchurch Road, which was formerly a cinema.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cinema">Cinema</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Cinema">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Southend has one cinema – the Odeon Multiplex at Victoria Circus which has eight screens. The borough of Southend had at one time a total of 18 cinema theatres,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> with the most famous being the Odeon (formerly the Astoria Theatre), which as well as showing films hosted live entertainers including the Beatles and Laurel and Hardy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> This building no longer stands having been replaced by the Southend Campus of the University of Essex. There are plans to build a new 10 screen cinema and entertainment facility on the site of the Seaway Car Park, which was earmarked to start in 2015 and be completed by 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> However Cineworld pulled out in 2015 and planned to open a new multiscreen site at the planned <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fossets_Farm_Stadium" title="Fossets Farm Stadium">Fossets Farm</a> development, next to the new <a href="/wiki/Southend_United_F.C." title="Southend United F.C.">Southend United F.C.</a> ground.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>Southend has appeared in films over the years, with the New York New York arcade on Marine Parade being used in the British gangsta flick <a href="/wiki/Essex_Boys" title="Essex Boys">Essex Boys</a>, and its airport being used for the filming of James Bond's <a href="/wiki/Goldfinger_(film)" title="Goldfinger (film)">Goldfinger</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Junk Club, at one time a centre of Southend's music scene, was predominantly held in the basement at the Royal Hotel during the period of 2001–06. Co-run by Oliver "Blitz" Abbott &amp; Rhys Webb, of The Horrors, the underground club night played and eclectic mix from Post Punk to Acid House, 1960s Psychedelia to Electro. It was noted as spearheading what became known as the <i>Southend Scene</i> and was featured in the NME, Dazed &amp; Confused, ID, Rolling Stone, Guardian and Vogue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> Bands associated with the scene included; <a href="/wiki/The_Horrors" title="The Horrors">The Horrors</a>, <a href="/wiki/These_New_Puritans" title="These New Puritans">These New Puritans</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Violets" title="The Violets">The Violets</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ipso_Facto_(band)" title="Ipso Facto (band)">Ipso Facto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neils_Children" title="Neils Children">Neils Children</a> and The Errorplains.</p>
<p>Southend has three major venues; Chinnerys, the Riga Club (formerly at the Cricketers Pub London Road) at O'Neill's, and the <a href="/wiki/Cliffs_Pavilion" title="Cliffs Pavilion">Cliffs Pavilion</a>. <a href="/wiki/The_Railway_Hotel,_Southend" title="The Railway Hotel, Southend">The Railway Hotel</a> is a live music pub, which features a variety of acts, and curates the Southend Pier Festival.</p>
<p>There have also been a number of popular music videos filmed in Southend,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> by such bands as <a href="/wiki/Oasis_(band)" title="Oasis (band)">Oasis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morrissey" title="Morrissey">Morrissey</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Michael" title="George Michael">George Michael</a>.</p>
<p>Other bands and musicians originating from Southend include; <a href="/wiki/Danielle_Dax" title="Danielle Dax">Danielle Dax</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Kursaal_Flyers" title="The Kursaal Flyers">The Kursaal Flyers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scroobius_Pip" title="Scroobius Pip">Scroobius Pip</a>, <a href="/wiki/Procol_Harum" title="Procol Harum">Procol Harum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_and_the_Hot_Rods" title="Eddie and the Hot Rods">Eddie and the Hot Rods</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busted_(band)" title="Busted (band)">Busted</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nothing_But_Thieves" title="Nothing But Thieves">Nothing But Thieves</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio">Radio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Radio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1981, Southend became the home of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Essex_FM" title="Essex FM">Essex Radio</a>, which broadcast from studios below Clifftown Road. The station was formed by several local companies including Keddies, Garons &amp; TOTS nightclub with David Keddie, owner of the Keddies department store in Southend becoming its chairman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> In 2004, the renamed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Essex_FM" title="Essex FM">Essex FM</a>, now <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heart_Essex" title="Heart Essex">Heart Essex</a> moved to studios in Chelmsford.</p>
<p>On 28 March 2008, Southend got its own radio station for the first time which is also shared with Chelmsford Radio formerly known as Dream 107.7 FM and Chelmer FM before that, <a href="/wiki/Southend_Radio" title="Southend Radio">Southend Radio</a> started broadcasting on 105.1FM from purpose built studios adjacent to the Adventure Island theme park.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> The station merged with Chelmsford Radio in 2015 and became <a href="/wiki/Radio_Essex" title="Radio Essex">Radio Essex</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Southend has appeared in several television shows and advertisements.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> It has been used on numerous occasions by the soap <a href="/wiki/EastEnders" title="EastEnders">EastEnders</a> with its most recent visit in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> Southend Pier was used by <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> show <a href="/wiki/Minder_(TV_series)" title="Minder (TV series)">Minder</a> for its end credits in season 8, 9 and 10,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> and since 2014 has been home to <a href="/wiki/Jamie_%26_Jimmy%27s_Friday_Night_Feast" title="Jamie &amp; Jimmy's Friday Night Feast">Jamie &amp; Jimmy's Friday Night Feast</a>. Advertisements have included Abbey National, CGU Pensions, National Lottery and most recently the 2015 <a href="/wiki/Vauxhall" title="Vauxhall">Vauxhall</a> Corsa adverts featuring Electric Avenue, a seafront arcade<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> and for the promo for <a href="/wiki/David_Hasselhoff" title="David Hasselhoff">David Hasselhoff</a>'s new <a href="/wiki/Dave_(TV_channel)" title="Dave (TV channel)">Dave</a> programme Hoff the Record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_fiction">In fiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: In fiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Southend is the seaside vacation place chosen by the John Knightley family in <a href="/wiki/Emma_(novel)" title="Emma (novel)">Emma</a> by Jane Austen, published 1816.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> The family arrived by stage coach, and strongly preferred it to the choice of the Perry family, <a href="/wiki/Cromer" title="Cromer">Cromer</a>, which was 100 miles from London, compared to the easier distance of 40 miles from the London home of the John and Isabella Knightley, as discussed at length with Mr Woodhouse in the novel in Chapter XII of volume one.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Places_of_worship">Places of worship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: Places of worship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are churches in the borough catering to different Christian denominations, such as <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_Help_of_Christians_and_St_Helen%27s_Church,_Westcliff-on-Sea" title="Our Lady Help of Christians and St Helen's Church, Westcliff-on-Sea">Our Lady Help of Christians and St Helen's Church</a> for the Roman Catholic community. There are two synagogues, one for orthodox Jews, in Westcliff, and a reform synagogue in Chalkwell. Three mosques provide for the Muslim population; one run by the Bangladeshi community, The others are run by the Pakistani community,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="run by (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="York_Road_Market">York Road Market</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southend-on-Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: York Road Market">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Demolition of the historic market began on 23 April 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> The site became a car park. A temporary market was held there every Friday until 2012 after the closure of the former Southend market at the rear of the Odeon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> As of 2013, a market is now held in the High Street every Thursday with over 30 stalls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
