<p><b>South Yorkshire</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_county" title="Metropolitan county">metropolitan county</a> in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It is the southernmost county in the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_and_the_Humber" title="Yorkshire and the Humber">Yorkshire and the Humber</a> region and had a population of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_ceremonial_counties_of_England_by_population" title="List of ceremonial counties of England by population">1.34 million</a> in 2011. It has an area of 1,552 square kilometres (599 sq mi)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and consists of four <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan boroughs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough_of_Barnsley" title="Metropolitan Borough of Barnsley">Barnsley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough_of_Doncaster" title="Metropolitan Borough of Doncaster">Doncaster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough_of_Rotherham" title="Metropolitan Borough of Rotherham">Rotherham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>. South Yorkshire was created on 1 April 1974 as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LGFSE16_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-LGFSE16-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> with Barnsley as its county town.</p>
<p>Lying on the east side of the <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennines</a>, South Yorkshire is landlocked, and borders <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">Derbyshire</a> to the west and south-west, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a> to the north-west, <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a> to the north, the <a href="/wiki/East_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="East Riding of Yorkshire">East Riding of Yorkshire</a> to the north-east, <a href="/wiki/Lincolnshire" title="Lincolnshire">Lincolnshire</a> to the east and <a href="/wiki/Nottinghamshire" title="Nottinghamshire">Nottinghamshire</a> to the south-east. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sheffield_Urban_Area" title="Sheffield Urban Area">Sheffield Urban Area</a> is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_conurbations_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="List of conurbations in the United Kingdom">tenth most populous conurbation in the UK</a>, and dominates the western half of South Yorkshire with over half of the county's population living within it. South Yorkshire lies within the <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_City_Region" title="Sheffield City Region">Sheffield City Region</a> with Barnsley also being within the Leeds City Region, reflecting its geographical position midway between Yorkshire's two largest cities.</p>
<p>South Yorkshire County Council was abolished in 1986 and its metropolitan boroughs are now effectively <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authorities</a>, although the metropolitan county continues to exist in law.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-geographic_frame_of_reference_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-geographic_frame_of_reference-4">[4]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Ceremonial_counties_of_England" title="Ceremonial counties of England">ceremonial county</a>, South Yorkshire has a <a href="/wiki/Lord_Lieutenant_of_South_Yorkshire" title="Lord Lieutenant of South Yorkshire">Lord Lieutenant</a> and a <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff_of_South_Yorkshire" title="High Sheriff of South Yorkshire">High Sheriff</a>.</p>
<p>South Yorkshire was created from 32 local government districts of the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a> (the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_counties_of_England" title="Administrative counties of England">administrative county</a> and four independent <a href="/wiki/County_borough" title="County borough">county boroughs</a>), with small areas from Derbyshire and Nottinghamshire.</p>

<p>Although the modern county of South Yorkshire was not created until 1974, the history of its constituent settlements and parts goes back centuries. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prehistoric" title="Prehistoric">Prehistoric</a> remains include a <a href="/wiki/Mesolithic" title="Mesolithic">Mesolithic</a> "house" (a circle of stones in the shape of a hut-base) dating to around 8000 BC, found at <a href="/wiki/Deepcar" title="Deepcar">Deepcar</a>, in the northern part of Sheffield.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Evidence of even earlier inhabitation in the wider region exists about 3 miles (4.8 km) over the county boundary at <a href="/wiki/Creswell_Crags" title="Creswell Crags">Creswell Crags</a> in <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">Derbyshire</a>, where artefacts and rock art found in caves have been dated by archaeologists to the late <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Upper_Palaeolithic" title="Upper Palaeolithic">Upper Palaeolithic</a> period, at least 12,800 years ago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The region was on the frontier of the Roman Empire during the Roman period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The main settlements of South Yorkshire grew up around the industries of <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mining</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steel" title="Steel">steel</a> manufacturing. The main mining industry was coal which was concentrated to the north and east of the county. There were also iron deposits which were mined in the area. The rivers running off the <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennines</a> to the west of the county supported the steel industry that is concentrated in the city of Sheffield. The proximity of the iron and coal also made this an ideal place for steel manufacture.</p>
<p>Although Christian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nonconformism" title="Nonconformism">nonconformism</a> was never as strong in South Yorkshire as in the <a href="/wiki/Mill_town" title="Mill town">mill towns</a> of West Yorkshire, there are still many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Methodist" title="Methodist">Methodist</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baptist" title="Baptist">Baptist</a> churches in the area. Also, South Yorkshire has a relatively high number of followers of <a href="/wiki/Spiritualism" title="Spiritualism">spiritualism</a>. It is the only county that counts as a full region in the <a href="/wiki/Spiritualists%27_National_Union" title="Spiritualists' National Union">Spiritualists' National Union</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Redcliffe-Maud_Report">Redcliffe-Maud Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Redcliffe-Maud Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Redcliffe-Maud_Report" title="Redcliffe-Maud Report">Redcliffe-Maud Report</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Commission_for_England_(1958%E2%80%931967)" title="Local Government Commission for England (1958–1967)">Local Government Commission for England</a> presented draft recommendations, in December 1965, proposing a new county—York and North Midlands—roughly centred on the southern part of the West Riding of Yorkshire and northern parts of Derbyshire and Nottinghamshire. The review was abolished in favour of the <a href="/wiki/Redcliffe-Maud_Report" title="Redcliffe-Maud Report">Royal Commission on Local Government</a> before it was able to issue a final report.</p>
<p>The Royal Commission's 1969 report, known as the Redcliffe-Maud Report, proposed the removal of much of the then existing system of local government. The commission described the system of administering <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rural_district" title="Rural district">rural districts</a> separately as outdated, noting that urban areas provided employment and services for rural dwellers, and open countryside was used by town dwellers for recreation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Redcliffe_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Redcliffe-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Redcliffe-Maud's recommendations were accepted by the Labour government in February 1970.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Redcliffe2_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Redcliffe2-10">[10]</a></sup> Although the Redcliffe-Maud Report was rejected by the Conservative government after the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_general_election,_1970" title="United Kingdom general election, 1970">1970 general election</a>, there was a commitment to local government reform, and the need for a metropolitan county of South Yorkshire.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="After_1974">After 1974</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: After 1974">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a> reformed <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_England" title="Local government in England">local government in England</a> by creating a system of two-tier <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_and_non-metropolitan_counties_of_England" title="Metropolitan and non-metropolitan counties of England">metropolitan and non-metropolitan counties</a> and <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_England" title="Districts of England">districts</a> throughout the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lga1972_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-lga1972-11">[11]</a></sup> The act formally established South Yorkshire on 1 April 1974, although <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire_County_Council" title="South Yorkshire County Council">South Yorkshire County Council</a> (SYCC) had been running since <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_local_elections,_1973" title="United Kingdom local elections, 1973">elections in 1973</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1973_Elections_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-1973_Elections-12">[12]</a></sup> The leading article in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i> on the day the Local Government Act came into effect noted that the "new arrangement is a compromise which seeks to reconcile familiar geography which commands a certain amount of affection and loyalty, with the scale of operations on which modern planning methods can work effectively".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>South Yorkshire initially had a two tier structure of local government with a strategic-level county council and four districts providing most services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1974, as part of the <i>South Yorkshire Structure Plan</i> of the environment, conservation and land use, South Yorkshire County Council commissioned a public attitudes survey covering job opportunities, educational facilities, leisure opportunities, health and medical services, shopping centres and transport in the county.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1986, throughout England the metropolitan county councils were abolished. The functions of the county council were devolved to the boroughs; joint-boards covering fire, police and public transport; and to other special joint arrangements.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The joint boards continue to function and include the <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire_Passenger_Transport_Executive" title="South Yorkshire Passenger Transport Executive">South Yorkshire Passenger Transport Executive</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire_Police_and_Crime_Commissioner" title="South Yorkshire Police and Crime Commissioner">South Yorkshire Police and Crime Commissioner</a> also oversees <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire_Police" title="South Yorkshire Police">South Yorkshire Police</a>.</p>
<p>Although the county council was abolished, South Yorkshire remains a <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_and_non-metropolitan_counties_of_England" title="Metropolitan and non-metropolitan counties of England">metropolitan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ceremonial_counties_of_England" title="Ceremonial counties of England">ceremonial</a> county with a <a href="/wiki/Lord_Lieutenant_of_South_Yorkshire" title="Lord Lieutenant of South Yorkshire">Lord Lieutenant of South Yorkshire</a> and a <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff_of_South_Yorkshire" title="High Sheriff of South Yorkshire">High Sheriff</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The county borders <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">Derbyshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a>, the <a href="/wiki/East_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="East Riding of Yorkshire">East Riding of Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lincolnshire" title="Lincolnshire">Lincolnshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nottinghamshire" title="Nottinghamshire">Nottinghamshire</a>. The metropolitan county lies largely on the carboniferous rocks of the <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire_Coalfield" title="South Yorkshire Coalfield">Yorkshire coalfield</a> which have produced a rolling landscape with hills, escarpments and broad valleys. In this landscape there is widespread evidence of both current and former industrial activity. There are numerous mine buildings, former spoil heaps and iron and steel plants. The scenery is a mixture of built up areas, industrial land with some dereliction, and farmed open country. Ribbon developments along transport routes including canal, road and rail are prominent features of the area although some remnants of the pre industrial landscape and semi-natural vegetation still survive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The west of the county is fringed by the Pennines and its foothills, most of which lie inside the <a href="/wiki/Peak_District" title="Peak District">Peak District National Park</a> and also contain carboniferous rocks, with the underlying geology primarily being millstone grit sandstones rising from the Yorkshire coalfield. The Pennine range within the county is distinguished by the moorlands and plateaus of the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Peak" title="Dark Peak">Dark Peak</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> while the Pennine fringes are distinguished by many steep valleys, and a transition from uplands and rural landscape to lowlands and urban landscape towards the east of the county.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Major rivers which cross the area are the <a href="/wiki/River_Dearne" title="River Dearne">Dearne</a>, <a href="/wiki/River_Rother,_South_Yorkshire" title="River Rother, South Yorkshire">Rother</a> and <a href="/wiki/River_Don,_South_Yorkshire" title="River Don, South Yorkshire">Don</a>. To the east, in the Doncaster area the landscape becomes flatter as the eastward dipping carboniferous rocks of the coalfield are overlain by the lacustrine deposits of the <a href="/wiki/Humberhead_Levels" title="Humberhead Levels">Humberhead Levels</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> There is very little evidence of glaciation in the area as it lies largely beyond the limit of the last glaciation.</p>

<p>Of these settlements above, South Yorkshire has three main urban areas: the <a href="/wiki/Dearne_Valley" title="Dearne Valley">Dearne Valley</a> which covers Barnsley and surrounding area; the <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_urban_area" title="Sheffield urban area">Sheffield urban area</a> which covers Sheffield, Rotherham and surrounding area; and the Doncaster urban area which covers Doncaster and surrounding area.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_civil_parishes_in_South_Yorkshire" title="List of civil parishes in South Yorkshire">List of civil parishes in South Yorkshire</a></div>

<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire_County_Council" title="South Yorkshire County Council">South Yorkshire County Council</a> was abolished and its districts became unitary authorities; they are the <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">City of Sheffield</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough_of_Doncaster" title="Metropolitan Borough of Doncaster">Metropolitan Borough of Doncaster</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough_of_Barnsley" title="Metropolitan Borough of Barnsley">Metropolitan Borough of Barnsley</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough_of_Rotherham" title="Metropolitan Borough of Rotherham">Metropolitan Borough of Rotherham</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1986, throughout England the metropolitan county councils were abolished. The functions of the county council were devolved to the boroughs. In practice many functions are jointly administered by joint authorities containing representatives of the four councils. The joint authorities cover fire, police and public transport.</p>
<p>In the case of South Yorkshire, these authorities are:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire_Fire_and_Rescue_Authority" title="South Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Authority">South Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Authority</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire_Passenger_Transport_Executive" title="South Yorkshire Passenger Transport Executive">South Yorkshire Passenger Transport Executive</a> (the Sheffield City Region Combined Authority is the transport authority)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire_Pensions_Authority" title="South Yorkshire Pensions Authority">South Yorkshire Pensions Authority</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire_Police_and_Crime_Panel" title="South Yorkshire Police and Crime Panel">South Yorkshire Police and Crime Panel</a></li>
</ul>
<p>These authorities are supported by the <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire_Joint_Secretariat" title="South Yorkshire Joint Secretariat">South Yorkshire Joint Secretariat</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Barnsley" title="Barnsley">Barnsley</a>. South Yorkshire is the only metropolitan county in the UK that has established a formal joint secretariat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_City_Region_Combined_Authority" title="Sheffield City Region Combined Authority">Sheffield City Region Combined Authority</a> was established in 2014 to bring the leaders of the four councils that make up South Yorkshire together on a statutory basis.</p>
<p>Although the county council was abolished, South Yorkshire remains a <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_and_non-metropolitan_counties_of_England" title="Metropolitan and non-metropolitan counties of England">metropolitan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ceremonial_counties_of_England" title="Ceremonial counties of England">ceremonial</a> county with a <a href="/wiki/Lord_Lieutenant_of_South_Yorkshire" title="Lord Lieutenant of South Yorkshire">Lord Lieutenant of South Yorkshire</a> and a <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff_of_South_Yorkshire" title="High Sheriff of South Yorkshire">High Sheriff</a>.</p>
