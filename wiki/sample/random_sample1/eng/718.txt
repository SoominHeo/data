<p>The <b>9K720 Iskander</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">«Искандер»</span>; <a href="/wiki/NATO_reporting_name" title="NATO reporting name">NATO reporting name</a> <b>SS-26 Stone</b>) is a mobile <a href="/wiki/Short-range_ballistic_missile" title="Short-range ballistic missile">short-range ballistic missile</a> system produced and deployed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Federation" title="Russian Federation">Russian Federation</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=9K720_Iskander&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The road-mobile Iskander was the second attempt to replace the <a href="/wiki/Scud" title="Scud">Scud</a> missile. The first attempt, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SS-23" title="SS-23">Oka</a>, was eliminated under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/INF_Treaty" title="INF Treaty">INF Treaty</a>. The Iskander appears to have several different conventional warheads, including a cluster munitions warhead, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fuel-air_explosive" title="Fuel-air explosive">fuel-air explosive</a> enhanced-blast warhead, a high explosive-fragmentation warhead, an earth penetrator for <a href="/wiki/Bunker_buster" title="Bunker buster">bunker busting</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Electromagnetic_pulse" title="Electromagnetic pulse">electromagnetic pulse</a> device for anti-radar missions. The missile can also carry nuclear warheads.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IskHist_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-IskHist-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Isk_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Isk-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The first successful launch occurred in 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-expert.ru_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-expert.ru-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2004, at a meeting with senior defense officials reporting to <a href="/wiki/President_of_Russia" title="President of Russia">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a> on the drafting of a defense budget for 2005, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Ministry_of_Defence" title="Russian Ministry of Defence">Russian Defence Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Ivanov" title="Sergei Ivanov">Sergei Ivanov</a> spoke about the completion of static tests of a new tactical missile system called the Iskander. He said that the system would go into quantity production in 2005 and toward the end of that year, Russia would have a brigade armed with it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IskHist_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-IskHist-1">[1]</a></sup> In March 2005, a source in the Russian defence industry told Interfax-AVN the development of new missiles with a range of 500–600 km, based on existing Iskander-E tactical missile systems, was a possibility. He said, however, that it "may take up to five or six years".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IskHist_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-IskHist-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006, serial production of the Iskander-M Tactical Ballistic Missile System launched, and the system was adopted by the Russian army.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IskHist_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-IskHist-1">[1]</a></sup> The cost of the rocket decreased by 30% in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> A number of countries were attempting to secure arms deals for the Iskander system, beginning in 2011, with the most likely contenders being China, India and South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-expert.ru_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-expert.ru-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=9K720_Iskander&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Iskander ballistic missile is superior to its predecessor, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SS-23" title="SS-23">Oka</a>. The Iskander-M system is equipped with two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solid-propellant" title="Solid-propellant">solid-propellant</a> single-stage <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guided_missile" title="Guided missile">guided missiles</a>, model <b>9M723K1</b>. Each one is controlled throughout the entire flight path and fitted with an inseparable <a href="/wiki/Warhead" title="Warhead">warhead</a>. Each missile in the launch carrier vehicle can be independently targeted in a matter of seconds. The mobility of the Iskander launch platform makes a launch difficult to prevent.</p>
<p>Targets can be located not only by satellite and aircraft but also by a conventional intelligence center, by an <a href="/wiki/Artillery_observer" title="Artillery observer">artillery observer</a> or from aerial photos scanned into a computer. The missiles can be re-targeted during flight in the case of engaging mobile targets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Isk_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Isk-9">[9]</a></sup> Another unique feature of Iskander-M <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> is the optically guided warhead, which can also be controlled by encrypted radio transmission, including such as those from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Airborne_Early_Warning_and_Control" title="Airborne Early Warning and Control">AWACS</a> or <a href="/wiki/Unmanned_aerial_vehicle" title="Unmanned aerial vehicle">UAV</a>. The electro-optical guidance system provides a self-homing capability. The missile's on-board computer receives images of the target, then locks onto the target with its sight and descends towards it at supersonic speed.</p>
<p>Boost phase thrust vector control (TVC) is accomplished by graphite vanes similar in layout to the V-2 and Scud series tactical ballistic missiles. In flight, the missile follows a <a href="/wiki/Ballistic_missile#Quasi_ballistic_missiles" title="Ballistic missile">quasi-ballistic</a> path, performing evasive maneuvers in the terminal phase of flight and releasing <a href="/wiki/Decoy" title="Decoy">decoys</a> in order to penetrate missile defense systems. The missile never leaves the atmosphere as it follows a relatively flat trajectory. The missile is controlled during the whole flight with <a href="/wiki/Thrust_vectoring" title="Thrust vectoring">gas-dynamic</a> and aerodynamic control surfaces. It uses a small scattering surface, special coatings and small size projections to reduce its radar signature.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rbase.new-factoria.ru_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-rbase.new-factoria.ru-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Russian Iskander-M travels at <a href="/wiki/Hypersonic_speed" title="Hypersonic speed">hypersonic speed</a> of 2100–2600 m/s (Mach 6–7) at a height of 50 km. The Iskander-M weighs 4615 kg, carries a warhead of 710–800 kg, has a range of 500 km and achieves a <a href="/wiki/Circular_error_probable" title="Circular error probable">circular error probable</a> (CEP) of 5–7 meters. During flight it can maneuver at different altitudes and trajectories and can turn at up to 20 to 30 G to evade anti-ballistic missiles. For example, in one of the trajectory modes it can dive at the target at 90 degrees at the rate of 700–800 m/s performing anti-ABM maneuvers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tomsk1_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-tomsk1-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The missile is controlled in all phases.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Iskander has achieved sufficient accuracy, range and reliability (ability to penetrate defenses) to function as an alternative to precision bombing for air forces that cannot expect to launch bombing or cruise missile fire missions reliably in the face of superior enemy fighters and air defenses. Training and competence requirements are much lower than for normal air force assets such as a fighter bomber squadron utilizing guided bombs.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Iskander is a tactical missile system designed to be used in theater level conflicts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> It is intended to use <a href="/wiki/Conventional_weapon" title="Conventional weapon">conventional</a> or nuclear <a href="/wiki/Warhead" title="Warhead">warheads</a> for the engagement of small and area targets (both moving and stationary), such as hostile fire weapons, air and antimissile defense weapons, command posts and communications nodes and troops in concentration areas, among others. The system can therefore destroy both active military units and targets to degrade the enemy's capability to wage war.</p>
<p>In 2007, a new missile for the system (and launcher), the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=R-500&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="R-500 (page does not exist)">R-500</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ru.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D0%A0-500_(%D1%80%D0%B0%D0%BA%D0%B5%D1%82%D0%B0)" title="ru:Р-500 (ракета)">ru</a>)</span> (range of applications up to 2000 km and more<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rnns.ru_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-rnns.ru-18">[18]</a></sup>) cruise missile, was test fired.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Now complex "Iskander-M" is transmitted to the troops complete with cruise and ballistic missiles. In 2013, army missile brigades first received missiles equipped with a new control system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>When approaching the target false targets and small jammers separate from the rocket.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The missile uses stealth technology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rnns.ru_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-rnns.ru-18">[18]</a></sup> Iskander-M also carries a complex of <a href="/wiki/Electronic_warfare" title="Electronic warfare">electronic warfare</a> jamming devices, both passive and active, for the suppression of the enemy's radar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The system can be transported by any means of transport, not excluding airplanes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dokwar.ru_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-dokwar.ru-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>The maximum power for the nuclear warhead is 50 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kiloton" title="Kiloton">kiloton</a> TNT (Iskander-M).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vpk.name_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-vpk.name-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Deployment_and_combat_history">Deployment and combat history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=9K720_Iskander&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Deployment and combat history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the Moscow reports, the <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Defense_Brief" title="Moscow Defense Brief">Moscow Defense Brief</a> stated that an Iskander missile was used for a high precision strike on the Georgian Separate Tank Battalion base in <a href="/wiki/Gori,_Georgia" title="Gori, Georgia">Gori</a>, destroying 28 tanks. Russian officials have denied using of the Iskander missile against Georgia but official reports<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (February 2010)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> testify to the high effectiveness of the Iskander missiles, as one of the most devastating and accurate weapons in the Russian arsenal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mdb_iskander_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-mdb_iskander-26">[26]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dutch_government" title="Dutch government">Dutch government</a>'s investigation claims that a single, 5 mm fragment from an anti-personnel sub-munition, that was propelled by an Iskander missile, killed Dutch journalist <a href="/wiki/Stan_Storimans" title="Stan Storimans">Stan Storimans</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gori,_Georgia" title="Gori, Georgia">Gori</a>, which was home to various military targets and had been almost completely evacuated before the bombardment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2008, the Russian President <a href="/wiki/Dmitry_Medvedev" title="Dmitry Medvedev">Dmitry Medvedev</a> stated in his first state of the nation speech, that Russia would deploy Iskander missiles to Russia's western district of <a href="/wiki/Kaliningrad" title="Kaliningrad">Kaliningrad</a> "to neutralize, if necessary, a <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_missile_defense_complex_in_Poland" title="US missile defense complex in Poland">missile defense system</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> On 17 September 2009, <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">US president</a> <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> announced the cancellation of the US missile defense project in Poland and the Czech Republic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Following the announcement, on 26 September, Medvedev stated in a press conference that he would in turn cancel the plans to deploy Iskander missiles to Kaliningrad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> According to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stratfor.com" title="Stratfor.com">Stratfor.com</a> e-mails from 2009 there are a number of Iskander brigades operational.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2013, it was revealed that Russia had deployed several Iskander-M ballistic missile systems in <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a> at undisclosed locations throughout the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harutyunyan_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Harutyunyan-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2016, at least one Iskander system was deployed to the Russian airbase Hmeimim in Syria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kaliningrad">Kaliningrad</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=9K720_Iskander&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Kaliningrad">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 29 September 2009, the Russian military announced plans to set up an extensive network of Iskander missiles throughout the country as part of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Ground_Forces#Reforms_under_Putin" title="Russian Ground Forces">broader military reforms</a> underway. According to General <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Boldyrev" title="Vladimir Boldyrev">Vladimir Boldyrev</a> (rtd.), Iskander systems would be stationed in every <a href="/wiki/Military_district#Russian_Federation" title="Military district">military district</a> in Russia but not in Kaliningrad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> On 23 November 2011, Medvedev again said that Russia may deploy Iskander tactical missiles in the Kaliningrad region as part of Russia’s reaction to the United States' reformulated missile shield plans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2013, Russia disclosed that the Iskander missile system had been deployed in the <a href="/wiki/Western_Military_District" title="Western Military District">Western Military District</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> In March 2015, there were reports in Russian media that Russia had deployed Iskander missiles to Kaliningrad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> In October 2016, there were again reports that the missile system had been deployed in Kaliningrad. <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a> called the situation "very alarming".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to Russian Chairman of the Defense Committee of the State Duma <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Shamanov" title="Vladimir Shamanov">Vladimir Shamanov</a> the transfer of missile systems "Iskander-M" in the Kaliningrad region was made to counter a possible threat from the appearance in Europe of US missile defense facilities: "These missiles are primarily directed against the objects that disturb the balance, harmony, and who delivered or will be put in the foreseeable future in the framework of the so-called missile defense."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Variants">Variants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=9K720_Iskander&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Variants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Iskander-M – 9M723 version for Russian armed forces. Range: published 415 km, rumoured 500 km. Flight altitude up to 6–50 km, stealth missile, controlled at all stages, not ballistic flight path. The intense maneuvering on takeoff and descent complicates prediction of target.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-42">[42]</a></sup> Missile constantly maneuvers during the flight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dokwar.ru_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-dokwar.ru-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></li>
<li>Iskander-K (K stands for <i>krylataya</i> or "winged") - 9M728 cruise missiles, flight altitude up to 6 km, automatic adjustment in the way, <a href="/wiki/TERCOM" title="TERCOM">follow of terrain relief</a> in flight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-42">[42]</a></sup> Range: 1500 km.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup>
<ul>
<li>R-500 is a <a href="/wiki/Cruise_missile" title="Cruise missile">cruise missile</a>, based and evolved from <a href="/wiki/RK-55" title="RK-55">3M10</a> and <a href="/wiki/3M-54_Klub" title="3M-54 Klub">3M54 3M14</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kh-55" title="Kh-55">Kh-101 102</a> ALCMs.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Currently the system includes 5 ballistic and 1 cruise missiles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Intended_targets">Intended targets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=9K720_Iskander&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Intended targets">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The system is intended to use <a href="/wiki/Conventional_weapon" title="Conventional weapon">conventional</a> <a href="/wiki/Warhead" title="Warhead">warheads</a> for the engagement of point and area targets, including:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>hostile fire weapons (missile systems, multiple launch rocket systems, long-range artillery pieces)</li>
<li>air and missile defense weapons, aerodrome</li>
<li>fixed- and rotary-wing aircraft at airfields</li>
<li>command posts and communications nodes</li>
<li>troops in concentration areas</li>
<li>critical civilian infrastructure facilities</li>
</ul>
<p>Can hit strongly protected targets (bunkers)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rbase.new-factoria.ru_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-rbase.new-factoria.ru-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
