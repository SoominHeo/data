<p><b>Saigata Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">犀潟駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Saigata-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station in <a href="/wiki/J%C5%8Detsu,_Niigata" title="Jōetsu, Niigata">Jōetsu, Niigata</a>, Japan.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saigata_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Saigata Station is served by the <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">East Japan Railway Company</a> (JR East) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shinetsu_Main_Line" title="Shinetsu Main Line">Shinetsu Main Line</a> and is 7.1 kilometers from the terminus of the line at <a href="/wiki/Naoetsu_Station" title="Naoetsu Station">Naoetsu Station</a>. It is also served by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hokuetsu_Express_Hokuhoku_Line" title="Hokuetsu Express Hokuhoku Line">Hokuetsu Express Hokuhoku Line</a> and is 59.7 kilometers from the terminus of the line at <a href="/wiki/Muikamachi_Station" title="Muikamachi Station">Muikamachi</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saigata_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station has one <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platform</a> and one <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platform</a>, connected by a footbridge. The station has a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Midori_no_Madoguchi" title="Midori no Madoguchi">Midori no Madoguchi</a></i> staffed ticket office.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saigata_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station opened on 13 March 1897. With the privatization of <a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a> (JNR) on 1 April 1987, the station came under the control of JR East.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Passenger_statistics">Passenger statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saigata_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Passenger statistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In fiscal 2015, the station was used by an average of 704 passengers daily (boarding passengers only).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jreast2015stats_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-jreast2015stats-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
