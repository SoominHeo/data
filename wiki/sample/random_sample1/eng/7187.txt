<p><b>Saigon Port</b> is a network of <a href="/wiki/Port" title="Port">ports</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh_City" title="Ho Chi Minh City">Ho Chi Minh City</a>. The port name is derived from the former name of the city. By 2013, it has become the 24th busiest container port in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saigon_Port&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Saigon Port played an important role in the foundation and development of the city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saigon" title="Saigon">Saigon</a>. During the era of <a href="/wiki/French_Indochina" title="French Indochina">French Indochina</a>, the port played a significant role in the import and export of materials from the colony. Today, this port network is the hub for export-import of goods in south Vietnam - the economic hub of the nation, which accounts for more than two-thirds of Vietnam's economy.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saigon_Port&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2006, Saigon Port handled more than 35 metric tonnes of cargo and 1.5 million <a href="/wiki/Twenty-foot_equivalent_unit" title="Twenty-foot equivalent unit">TEU</a> of containers. By the end of 2012, Saigon Port now handled 3.5 million <a href="/wiki/Twenty-foot_equivalent_unit" title="Twenty-foot equivalent unit">TEU</a> of containers, an increase of 14% from 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relocation">Relocation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saigon_Port&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Relocation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Due to urban planning, the network of Saigon Port has been relocated to the outskirts of <a href="/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh_City" title="Ho Chi Minh City">Ho Chi Minh City</a>, specifically to the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hiep_Phuoc_New_Urban_and_Port_Area&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hiep Phuoc New Urban and Port Area (page does not exist)">Hiep Phuoc New Urban and Port Area</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cat_Lai_New_Port&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cat Lai New Port (page does not exist)">Cat Lai New Port</a> area and especially to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Thi_Vai_Port&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Thi Vai Port (page does not exist)">Thi Vai Port</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cai_Mep_Port&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cai Mep Port (page does not exist)">Cai Mep Port</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B%C3%A0_R%E1%BB%8Ba%E2%80%93V%C5%A9ng_T%C3%A0u_Province" title="Bà Rịa–Vũng Tàu Province">Bà Rịa–Vũng Tàu Province</a>, 60 km South-East of Ho Chi Minh City, 30 km northwest of <a href="/wiki/V%C5%A9ng_T%C3%A0u" title="Vũng Tàu">Vũng Tàu</a>. The Thi Vai Port with the capacity of handling ships up to 50,000 tonnes will be the leading deep-water port of this region.</p>
