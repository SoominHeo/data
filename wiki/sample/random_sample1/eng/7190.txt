<p><b>Simon Wright</b> (born 19 June 1963) is an English drummer best known for his work with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock_n%27_roll" title="Rock n' roll">rock n' roll</a> bands <a href="/wiki/AC/DC" title="AC/DC">AC/DC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dio_(band)" title="Dio (band)">Dio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Music_Legends_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Music_Legends-1">[1]</a></sup> He started playing drums at the age of 13<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and cites <a href="/wiki/Cozy_Powell" title="Cozy Powell">Cozy Powell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Aldridge" title="Tommy Aldridge">Tommy Aldridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Bonham" title="John Bonham">John Bonham</a> as his greatest influences. He was also the drummer for <a href="/wiki/Rhino_Bucket" title="Rhino Bucket">Rhino Bucket</a>. He currently plays in the band <a href="/wiki/Operation:_Mindcrime_(band)" title="Operation: Mindcrime (band)">Operation: Mindcrime</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Wright_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wright began his career with local band Tora Tora (not to be confused with the American glam metal group), before joining <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> group <a href="/wiki/A_II_Z" title="A II Z">A II Z</a>, a <a href="/wiki/New_wave_of_British_heavy_metal" title="New wave of British heavy metal">new wave of British heavy metal</a> band founded in 1979 in Manchester, England by guitarist Gary Owens. The full line-up consisted of David Owens (vocals), Gary Owens (guitar), Gam Campbell (bass), Karl Reti (drums). They acquired a local following in Manchester, and were signed by Polydor Records, eager to cash in on the exploding NWOBHM boom. A single live album, <i>The Witch of Berkley</i>, followed in 1980. Reti was subsequently replaced by Wright. The band disintegrated and Wright went on to perform in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tytan_(band)" title="Tytan (band)">Tytan</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and recorded on their full-length debut album <i>Rough Justice</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> however; that band also dissolved shortly before the release of the debut.</p>
<p>Wright, just a month shy of turning twenty years old, joined AC/DC after their drummer, <a href="/wiki/Phil_Rudd" title="Phil Rudd">Phil Rudd</a>, left in May 1983. AC/DC recorded three albums with Wright in the mid-late 80s; <i><a href="/wiki/Fly_on_the_Wall_(AC/DC_album)" title="Fly on the Wall (AC/DC album)">Fly on the Wall</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Who_Made_Who" title="Who Made Who">Who Made Who</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Blow_Up_Your_Video" title="Blow Up Your Video">Blow Up Your Video</a></i>. Wright left the group in November 1989 to join <a href="/wiki/Dio_(band)" title="Dio (band)">Dio</a>, and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Slade" title="Chris Slade">Chris Slade</a>. Simon Wright joined <a href="/wiki/Rhino_Bucket" title="Rhino Bucket">Rhino Bucket</a> when he wasn't working with Dio to replace the departed Liam Jason for their third album, 1994's independently released <i>Pain</i>. In 2007, he reunited with the band and is featured on their latest album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hardest_Town" title="The Hardest Town">The Hardest Town</a></i>.</p>
<p>Wright has had two stints with <a href="/wiki/Dio_(band)" title="Dio (band)">Dio</a>, 1990–91 and 1998–2010. With the band he has recorded four studio albums (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lock_up_the_Wolves" title="Lock up the Wolves">Lock up the Wolves</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Magica_(album)" title="Magica (album)">Magica</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Killing_the_Dragon" title="Killing the Dragon">Killing the Dragon</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Master_of_the_Moon" title="Master of the Moon">Master of the Moon</a></i>) and two live albums (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evil_or_Divine_-_Live_In_New_York_City" title="Evil or Divine - Live In New York City">Evil or Divine - Live In New York City</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holy_Diver_-_Live" title="Holy Diver - Live">Holy Diver - Live</a></i>).</p>
<p>His drumming can be heard on albums by <a href="/wiki/UFO_(band)" title="UFO (band)">UFO</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schenker" title="Michael Schenker">Michael Schenker Group</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Norum" title="John Norum">John Norum</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2005, Wright participated on a <a href="/wiki/Tribute" title="Tribute">tribute</a> album to <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">Heavy metal</a> icons <a href="/wiki/Iron_Maiden" title="Iron Maiden">Iron Maiden</a>. It was his second of three times doing a tribute disc; the first experience being in 1998 on an AC/DC tribute album titled <i>Thunderbolt</i>, while in 2013 he took part in the DIO Tribute album "This Is Your Life".</p>
<p>In 2006 Wright is credited in the song "Lucy in the Sky with Diamonds" on the album <i>Butchering the Beatles</i>, a heavy metal tribute.</p>
<p>In August 2009, Wright teamed up with <a href="/wiki/Joe_Lynn_Turner" title="Joe Lynn Turner">Joe Lynn Turner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phil_Soussan" title="Phil Soussan">Phil Soussan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Cavazo" title="Carlos Cavazo">Carlos Cavazo</a> as part of the Big Noize project, playing shows in Iraq and Kuwait.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 25 January 2013, it was announced that Wright had joined <a href="/wiki/Geoff_Tate" title="Geoff Tate">Geoff Tate</a>'s band Operation:Mindcrime after his dismissal from <a href="/wiki/Queensr%C3%BFche" title="Queensrÿche">Queensrÿche</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
