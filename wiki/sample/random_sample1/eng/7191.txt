<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Simon Biwott</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Simon Biwott</b> (born March 3, 1970 in <a href="/wiki/Eldoret" title="Eldoret">Eldoret</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uasin_Gishu_District" title="Uasin Gishu District">Uasin Gishu District</a>) is a former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long-distance_runner" title="Long-distance runner">long-distance runner</a> from <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a> who won the <a href="/wiki/Silver_medal" title="Silver medal">silver medal</a> in the men's <a href="/wiki/Marathon" title="Marathon">marathon</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2001_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2001 World Championships in Athletics">2001 World Championships in Athletics</a>. The race in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edmonton,_Canada" title="Edmonton, Canada">Edmonton, Canada</a> was won by <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>'s reigning Olympic champion <a href="/wiki/Gezahegne_Abera" title="Gezahegne Abera">Gezahegne Abera</a>. A year later, Biwott won the <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam_Marathon" title="Rotterdam Marathon">Rotterdam Marathon</a>. He also won marathons in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Canc%C3%BAn_Marathon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cancún Marathon (page does not exist)">Cancún</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City_Marathon" title="Mexico City Marathon">Mexico City</a> (1998), <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Marathon" title="Berlin Marathon">Berlin</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Milan_Marathon" title="Milan Marathon">Milan</a> (2000), and <a href="/wiki/Paris_Marathon" title="Paris Marathon">Paris</a> (2001). After his career in athletics, he started a private school in <a href="/wiki/Eldoret" title="Eldoret">Eldoret</a>. His personal best of 2:06:49 was run at the 2002 edition of the Berlin Marathon.</p>
