<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reading">Reading</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reading">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Amersham" title="Amersham">Amersham</a>, Buckinghamshire,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hugman2010-11_83_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Hugman2010-11_83-1">[1]</a></sup> Church was spotted as a youngster while playing at a youth tournament and joined hometown club <a href="/wiki/Wycombe_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wycombe Wanderers F.C.">Wycombe Wanderers</a> Centre of Excellence as a nine-year-old upon recommendation from the club scout.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Five years later, he joined Reading's Youth Academy at the age of 14, as part of the Nathan Tyson sale to Wycombe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He was a regular in Reading's reserve side whilst still in the academy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-professional_contract_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-professional_contract-4">[4]</a></sup> On 6 July 2007, he signed a one-year professional contract with Reading,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-professional_contract_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-professional_contract-4">[4]</a></sup> but did not make a first team appearance that season due to stiff competition from the likes of <a href="/wiki/Dave_Kitson" title="Dave Kitson">Dave Kitson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Irish</a> internationals <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Doyle" title="Kevin Doyle">Kevin Doyle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shane_Long" title="Shane Long">Shane Long</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_moves">Loan moves</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Loan moves">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Church joined <a href="/wiki/Crewe_Alexandra_F.C." title="Crewe Alexandra F.C.">Crewe Alexandra</a> on loan for one month on 19 October 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> which was subsequently extended to 19 January 2008 following rave reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He was put into the starting XI for Crewe on 20 October 2007 against <a href="/wiki/Luton_Town_F.C." title="Luton Town F.C.">Luton Town</a>, where he wore the number 31 shirt and assisted <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Lowe" title="Ryan Lowe">Ryan Lowe</a> for Crewe Alexandra's second goal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He scored his first goal for Crewe in a 2–2 draw with Cheltenham on 27 October 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Church joined <a href="/wiki/Yeovil_Town_F.C." title="Yeovil Town F.C.">Yeovil Town</a> on loan for the remainder of the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_in_English_football" title="2007–08 in English football">2007–08</a> season on 29 January 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He followed this with a loan move to his hometown club <a href="/wiki/Wycombe_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wycombe Wanderers F.C.">Wycombe Wanderers</a> at the start of the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_in_English_football" title="2008–09 in English football">2008–09</a> season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> His original month at the club was cut short due to a sending off and subsequent three match ban, but he later rejoined the club for a further two months on 20 October 2008.</p>
<p>On 17 February 2009 Church joined <a href="/wiki/Leyton_Orient_F.C." title="Leyton Orient F.C.">Leyton Orient</a>, initially on a month's loan, but later extended to the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="First-team_breakthrough">First-team breakthrough</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: First-team breakthrough">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Church made his full first team debut for Reading in the play-off semi-final second leg defeat to <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a> on 12 May 2009. With the departures of Doyle and Kitson, he became more of a regular in the first team and soon claimed a starting place at the expense of Long. On 19 September, he scored his first league goal in only his fourth start in a 3–2 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Peterborough_United_F.C." title="Peterborough United F.C.">Peterborough United</a> and dedicated it to his father who died earlier that month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On 2 January 2010, he scored against Premiership side Liverpool in the FA Cup third round as the Royals took a shock lead and held the Merseyside club to a 1–1 draw at the Madejski Stadium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The Royals went on to win 2–1 in the replay. Four days later, he was offered a new deal and extended his contract until 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In February, he scored a brace against <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a> in a 3–1 win, which lifted Reading out of the relegation zone. Midway through the 2011–12 season, Church hit form with four goals in four matches, opening the scoring against Peterborough, getting a brace against promotion contenders West Ham and earning a 0–1 win away at Leeds United with a well taken lob over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andrew_Lonergan" title="Andrew Lonergan">Andrew Lonergan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> At the end of the 2012–13 season, Church was released by the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Huddersfield_Town">Huddersfield Town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Huddersfield Town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 8 November 2012, Church joined <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a> on a one-month loan deal and made his Town debut two days later in Huddersfield's 1–0 win away at <a href="/wiki/Oakwell" title="Oakwell">Oakwell</a>, over <a href="/wiki/Barnsley" title="Barnsley">Barnsley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Church scored his first goal for Huddersfield Town in the 90th minute of his second game for them, a 1–2 defeat at home to <a href="/wiki/Brighton_%26_Hove_Albion_F.C." title="Brighton &amp; Hove Albion F.C.">Brighton &amp; Hove Albion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Church returned to Reading after their game with <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a> game on 29 December, having made a total of seven appearances for Huddersfield.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Charlton_Athletic">Charlton Athletic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Charlton Athletic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 1 August 2013 Church joined <a href="/wiki/Charlton_Athletic_F.C." title="Charlton Athletic F.C.">Charlton Athletic</a> on a two-year contract and was handed the number 9 shirt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> He scored his first goals for the club in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> against <a href="/wiki/Oxford_United_F.C." title="Oxford United F.C.">Oxford United</a> five days later in a 4–0 win home win for Charlton.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 12 May 2015, Church was released at the end of his contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Milton_Keynes_Dons">Milton Keynes Dons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Milton Keynes Dons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 30 June 2015, Church signed for newly promoted Championship side <a href="/wiki/Milton_Keynes_Dons_F.C." title="Milton Keynes Dons F.C.">Milton Keynes Dons</a> on a two-year contract. Church scored his first goal for the MK Dons in a 2–1 defeat to Leeds United<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Aberdeen_.28loan.29">Aberdeen (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Aberdeen (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 1 February 2016, after finding limited first team opportunities, Church signed for <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Premiership" title="Scottish Premiership">Scottish Premiership</a> side <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen_F.C." title="Aberdeen F.C.">Aberdeen</a> on loan for the remainder of the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Aberdeen_F.C._season" title="2015–16 Aberdeen F.C. season">2015–16 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> He scored on his debut in Aberdeen's 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Celtic_F.C." title="Celtic F.C.">Celtic</a> on 3 February 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roda_JC_Kerkrade">Roda JC Kerkrade</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Roda JC Kerkrade">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 24 August 2016, Church joined Dutch <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> side <a href="/wiki/Roda_JC_Kerkrade" title="Roda JC Kerkrade">Roda JC Kerkrade</a> on a one-year deal for an undisclosed fee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born and brought up in England, Church qualifies to play for Wales through grandparents from <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Wales" title="Newport, Wales">Newport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> He made his début for the <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_under-21_football_team" title="Wales national under-21 football team">Wales U21</a> team as a substitute in their 4–3 win over <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_under-21_football_team" title="Sweden national under-21 football team">Sweden U21</a> on 21 August 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> On 10 October 2007, he scored twice as Wales U-21 lost 3–2 to England in the first leg of their European Championship qualifying match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Church scored a brace for Wales under 21s against <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England under 21s</a> as they lost 3–2 on 10 October 2008, in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship_qualification_play-offs" title="2009 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship qualification play-offs">2009 European Championship play-offs</a>. He also scored Wales's second goal in the first half of the second leg of the play-off against England, neatly lifting the ball over the on rushing <a href="/wiki/Joe_Hart" title="Joe Hart">Joe Hart</a> but they lost 5–4 on aggregate to England, thus missing out on a spot in the final tournament. In March 2009, he was named captain of the U-21s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 29 May 2009 Church made his debut for the senior Wales international team in the friendly against Estonia. He received call-ups for the match against Italy U21 and a 2010 World Cup qualifier against Russia on 9 September but did not participate due to the death of his father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> He scored his first senior goal on 14 November in friendly against Scotland that ended 3–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-First_international_goal_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-First_international_goal-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
