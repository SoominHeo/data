<p><b>Simon Fuller</b> (born 17 May 1960) is an English <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Entrepreneur" title="Entrepreneur">entrepreneur</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artist_manager" title="Artist manager">artist manager</a> and <a href="/wiki/Television_producer" title="Television producer">television producer</a>. He is best known for being the creator of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Idol_series" title="Idol series"><i>Idol</i> franchise</a>, which was first seen in the UK under the name <i><a href="/wiki/Pop_Idol" title="Pop Idol">Pop Idol</a></i>, and includes <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> in the U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMail2012_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-DMail2012-1">[1]</a></sup> Fuller is the executive producer of many other hit shows including the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> TV reality shows <i><a href="/wiki/So_You_Think_You_Can_Dance_(U.S._TV_series)" title="So You Think You Can Dance (U.S. TV series)">So You Think You Can Dance</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Q%27Viva!_The_Chosen" title="Q'Viva! The Chosen">Q'Viva</a></i>.</p>
<p>One of the most influential figures in <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Culture of the United Kingdom">British popular culture</a>, Fuller came to prominence through managing pop group the <a href="/wiki/Spice_Girls" title="Spice Girls">Spice Girls</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He has managed a wide range of talent that includes <a href="/wiki/David_Beckham" title="David Beckham">David</a> and <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Beckham" title="Victoria Beckham">Victoria Beckham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Annie_Lennox" title="Annie Lennox">Annie Lennox</a>, <a href="/wiki/S_Club_7" title="S Club 7">S Club 7</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_Bradley_Wiggins" title="Sir Bradley Wiggins">Sir Bradley Wiggins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steven_Tyler" title="Steven Tyler">Steven Tyler</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andy_Murray" title="Andy Murray">Andy Murray</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amy_Winehouse" title="Amy Winehouse">Amy Winehouse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Clarkson" title="Kelly Clarkson">Kelly Clarkson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Will_Young" title="Will Young">Will Young</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emma_Bunton" title="Emma Bunton">Emma Bunton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Marie_Presley" title="Lisa Marie Presley">Lisa Marie Presley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gary_Barlow" title="Gary Barlow">Gary Barlow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cathy_Dennis" title="Cathy Dennis">Cathy Dennis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aloe_Blacc" title="Aloe Blacc">Aloe Blacc</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_McManaman" title="Steve McManaman">Steve McManaman</a> and others. He is in partnership with the duo <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marc_Anthony" title="Marc Anthony">Marc Anthony</a>.</p>
<p>In 2007, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Time_magazine" title="Time magazine">Time</a></i> magazine named Fuller one of the 100 most influential people in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2008, Fuller was certified as the most successful British music manager of all time by <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> magazine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard08_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard08-4">[4]</a></sup> Fuller received the 2,441st star on the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Walk_of_Fame" title="Hollywood Walk of Fame">Hollywood Walk of Fame</a> on 23 May 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The 2012 <i><a href="/wiki/Sunday_Times_Rich_List" title="Sunday Times Rich List">Sunday Times Rich List</a></i> values Fuller at <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>375m; the sixth richest music millionaire in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Mail" title="Daily Mail">Daily Mail</a></i> music promoter <a href="/wiki/Harvey_Goldsmith" title="Harvey Goldsmith">Harvey Goldsmith</a> ranked Fuller at No.1 in a list of the greatest British entrepreneurs, commenting "he is a man of real vision".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMail2012_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-DMail2012-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_background">Business background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fuller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Business background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fuller began his career in 1981 at <a href="/wiki/Chrysalis_Records" title="Chrysalis Records">Chrysalis</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A%26R" title="A&amp;R">A&amp;R</a> (Chrysalis Records) in the UK,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> where he signed <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>'s first hit <a href="/wiki/Holiday_(Madonna_song)" title="Holiday (Madonna song)">'Holiday'</a> to the company. He started his own management company in London in 1985, <a href="/wiki/19_Entertainment" title="19 Entertainment">19 Entertainment</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> eventually selling the business to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CKX,_Inc." title="CKX, Inc.">CKX, Inc.</a> in a cash and stock deal worth over $200 million in March 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2008 the company delivered a profit of $92.5 million (£66m) into the parent company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Fuller became a director of CKX, a position which gave him creative control over all of CKX's assets including the <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley_Enterprises" title="Elvis Presley Enterprises">Elvis Presley Estate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_Ali" title="Muhammad Ali">Muhammad Ali</a>’s business interests, and other iconic rights properties, until January 2010 when he started a new firm, <a href="/wiki/XIX_Entertainment" title="XIX Entertainment">XIX Entertainment</a>, while continuing to work with CKX/19 as a consultant and executive producer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> XIX Entertainment was given a valuation of $100 million. It is run from offices in London, Los Angeles and Nashville.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FT2_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-FT2-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIUK_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-CIUK-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2008 Fuller was presented with the PGA Award by the <a href="/wiki/Producers_Guild_of_America" title="Producers Guild of America">Producers Guild of America</a> in Hollywood. At the event he was praised for his creativity, entrepreneurship, and charity work by <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">British Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Brown" title="Gordon Brown">Gordon Brown</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HRH_The_Prince_of_Wales" title="HRH The Prince of Wales">Prince Charles</a> who also thanked Fuller for his support of the Prince's charity <a href="/wiki/The_Prince%27s_Trust" title="The Prince's Trust">The Prince's Trust</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Charles_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Charles-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="TV_production_and_formats">TV production and formats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fuller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: TV production and formats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fuller has created several hit TV formats including <i><a href="/wiki/S_Club_7" title="S Club 7">S Club 7</a></i>, a pop group and <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>/<a href="/wiki/Fox_Kids" title="Fox Kids">Fox Kids</a> television show, which was broadcast in over 100 territories around the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reach_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Reach-15">[15]</a></sup> S Club has been named as the inspiration for the wave of teen-oriented musical television shows that followed most notably Glee and High School Musical.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RT1_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-RT1-16">[16]</a></sup> The S Club project produced several stars including <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Stevens" title="Rachel Stevens">Rachel Stevens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jon_Lee_(singer)" title="Jon Lee (singer)">Jon Lee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holly_Willoughby" title="Holly Willoughby">Holly Willoughby</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Barnes_(actor)" title="Ben Barnes (actor)">Ben Barnes</a>, as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frankie_Sandford" title="Frankie Sandford">Frankie Sandford</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rochelle_Wiseman" title="Rochelle Wiseman">Rochelle Wiseman</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Saturdays" title="The Saturdays">The Saturdays</a>.</p>
<p>In 2001, Fuller created the British television show <i><a href="/wiki/Pop_Idol" title="Pop Idol">Pop Idol</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Idol_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Idol-17">[17]</a></sup> The show broke TV records when it was broadcast over two series in the UK, as well as uncovering <a href="/wiki/Will_Young" title="Will Young">Will Young</a> whose single Evergreen became the ‘biggest selling single of the century’ in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Fuller sold the show to the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> network in the US, and <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> quickly became a No.1 rated show. The show has made stars of its presenter <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Seacrest" title="Ryan Seacrest">Ryan Seacrest</a> as well as judges <a href="/wiki/Randy_Jackson" title="Randy Jackson">Randy Jackson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paula_Abdul" title="Paula Abdul">Paula Abdul</a> and <a href="/wiki/Simon_Cowell" title="Simon Cowell">Simon Cowell</a>, as well as many of its performers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> And later the show has featured many more high profiled judges including <a href="/wiki/Ellen_DeGeneres" title="Ellen DeGeneres">Ellen DeGeneres</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kara_DioGuardi" title="Kara DioGuardi">Kara DioGuardi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steven_Tyler" title="Steven Tyler">Steven Tyler</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Keith_Urban" title="Keith Urban">Keith Urban</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>American Idol</i> has since won many awards and has been credited with originating a new form of reality TV. It is claimed to be the most valuable TV format in the world with a value of over $8 billion made up of revenue from sponsors, merchandising, telephone, music sales, broadcast and advertising sources. Over 30 million viewers saw <a href="/wiki/David_Cook_(singer)" title="David Cook (singer)">David Cook</a> win the seventh season of American Idol in May 2008 and when Fuller re-launched the show in 2011 for its 10th season ratings climbed 20% for the finale event. 122.4 million votes were cast, a record for a reality TV show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In 2005 Fuller, along with co-creator <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Lythgoe" title="Nigel Lythgoe">Nigel Lythgoe</a>, launched <i><a href="/wiki/So_You_Think_You_Can_Dance_(U.S._TV_series)" title="So You Think You Can Dance (U.S. TV series)">So You Think You Can Dance</a></i> on the Fox network in the USA. The show grew over four seasons to become a regular summer hit. In 2012 Simon Fuller's XIX Entertainment created a new international format with <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marc_Anthony" title="Marc Anthony">Marc Anthony</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jamie_King" title="Jamie King">Jamie King</a> named Q'Viva! The Chosen, and described as a celebration of Latin culture that screened in 21 territories reaching over 30 million Spanish speaking TV viewers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_in_music_culture">Impact in music culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fuller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact in music culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Simon Fuller has been certified as the most successful British music manager of all time by <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> magazine,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard08_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard08-4">[4]</a></sup> having produced over 500 No.1 hit singles globally and over 240 No.1 albums; his reputation for supporting new music talent is well documented.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> He has managed the career of <a href="/wiki/Annie_Lennox" title="Annie Lennox">Annie Lennox</a> since the release of her multi platinum album <i>Diva</i> in 1991. He has managed hit songwriters and producers, most notably <a href="/wiki/Cathy_Dennis" title="Cathy Dennis">Cathy Dennis</a>, who first met Fuller as the singer of <a href="/wiki/D_Mob" title="D Mob">D Mob</a> in the 1980s. Under Fuller's guidance <a href="/wiki/Cathy_Dennis" title="Cathy Dennis">Cathy Dennis</a> has become the UK's No. 1 female songwriter with a string of global hits for artists including <a href="/wiki/Kylie_Minogue" title="Kylie Minogue">Kylie Minogue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a>, <a href="/wiki/Katy_Perry" title="Katy Perry">Katy Perry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pink_(singer)" title="Pink (singer)">Pink</a>, <a href="/wiki/Will_Young" title="Will Young">Will Young</a> and others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In 2002 Fuller's company discovered 19-year-old <a href="/wiki/Amy_Winehouse" title="Amy Winehouse">Amy Winehouse</a>, produced her first award winning album and signed her to Universal Music.</p>
<p>Fuller managed the <a href="/wiki/Spice_Girls" title="Spice Girls">Spice Girls</a> through the peak of their success.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Under Fuller, the Spice Girls rose to chart topper status and went on to sell over 37.5 million records in less than three years.</p>
<p>In 2012 Simon Fuller announced a partnership with <a href="/wiki/Chris_Blackwell" title="Chris Blackwell">Chris Blackwell</a>, founder of Island Records and owner of Bob Marley catalogue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In 2005, 2006 and 2007 the biggest selling music acts in North America (Kelly Clarkson, Carrie Underwood and Daughtry) were all managed by Simon Fuller.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion_into_fashion">Expansion into fashion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fuller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Expansion into fashion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Simon Fuller owns a significant stake in several British fashion businesses including Victoria Beckham Ltd and the Roland Mouret brand. In 2006, he launched <a class="external text" href="http://www.19rm.tv/" rel="nofollow">19RM</a> with acclaimed designer <a href="/wiki/Roland_Mouret" title="Roland Mouret">Roland Mouret</a>. In 2011, Mouret and Fuller opened their first retail venture, a six-floor headquarters in Carlos Place, London comprising retail, design and Atelier in one building and in 2009, Fuller brokered a deal for 19 Entertainment to purchase a majority share in <a href="/wiki/Storm_Model_Management" title="Storm Model Management">Storm Model Management</a>.</p>
<p>In 2008 Fuller launched the <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Beckham" title="Victoria Beckham">Victoria Beckham</a> dress collection at <a href="/wiki/New_York_Fashion_Week" title="New York Fashion Week">New York Fashion Week</a>, which has since won many plaudits including Brand of The Year at the 2012 and 2014 <a href="/wiki/British_Fashion_Awards" title="British Fashion Awards">British Fashion Awards</a>. In 2012 Fuller accompanied <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Beckham" title="Victoria Beckham">Victoria Beckham</a> as she launched the business in China,.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 February 2012, XIX Entertainment launched a <a href="/wiki/David_Beckham" title="David Beckham">David Beckham</a> bodywear range in London with <a href="/wiki/H%26M" title="H&amp;M">H&amp;M</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> In 2014, Fuller and <a href="/wiki/David_Beckham" title="David Beckham">David Beckham</a> announced a joint venture with Hong Kong-based Global Brands Group Holding Ltd (787) to develop David Beckham and other icon branded consumer products.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_activity">Sports activity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fuller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sports activity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fuller first ventured into sports management in the mid-1990s, when he negotiated the historic first post <a href="/wiki/Bosman" title="Bosman">Bosman</a> transfer of <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> footballer <a href="/wiki/Steve_McManaman" title="Steve McManaman">Steve McManaman</a> to <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In 2003 Fuller formed a joint venture partnership with <a href="/wiki/David_Beckham" title="David Beckham">David Beckham</a>, and later masterminded Beckham's $250 million move to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Galaxy" title="Los Angeles Galaxy">LA Galaxy</a>. He remains Beckham's closest advisor.</p>
<p>In 2009, Fuller took on personal representation of tennis player <a href="/wiki/Andy_Murray" title="Andy Murray">Andy Murray</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> guiding him to Grand Slam and Wimbledon wins and later helping the player to establish his own business. In March 2011, Simon Fuller was announced as the manager of British F1 driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> and surprised many when he moved Hamilton from McLaren and signed him to a long term contract with the MercedesAMG team. Hamilton won the 2014 F1 World Championship and publicly thanked Fuller,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> although Hamilton did not renew his deal with Fuller's <a href="/wiki/XIX_Entertainment" title="XIX Entertainment">XIX Entertainment</a> company once it expired at the end of that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> In 2013 it was announced that Fuller was managing 2012 Tour De France winner, <a href="/wiki/Bradley_Wiggins" title="Bradley Wiggins">Bradley Wiggins</a>.</p>
<p>In 2014, Fuller and Beckham revealed their plan to buy an MLS football team in Miami <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> and later the same year Fuller was revealed as the mastermind behind Beckham's involvement in a new global whiskey launch with leading British drinks company Diageo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Charity_work">Charity work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fuller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Charity work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Simon Fuller has supported many good causes throughout his working life, most notably through his TV format Idol Gives Back, which over five years raised over $185 million for charities including Malaria No More, Save The Children and others. In December 2010 Simon Fuller was honored by UN Secretary General <a href="/wiki/Ban_Ki-moon" title="Ban Ki-moon">Ban Ki-moon</a>: "Simon Fuller is an extraordinary leader" he said. "Idol Gives Back is a tremendous initiative in giving Americans a way to save lives around the world".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2008, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HRH_The_Prince_of_Wales" title="HRH The Prince of Wales">HRH The Prince of Wales</a> thanked Fuller for his support of <a href="/wiki/The_Prince%27s_Trust" title="The Prince's Trust">The Prince's Trust</a>, stating; "He has been one of the most effective and generous ambassadors for my Trust, which I set up 32 years ago to help the most disadvantaged young people in the United Kingdom. There are many young people in the United Kingdom whose lives have been transformed and, in some cases, saved – literally – thanks to the contribution which Simon has made.".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Charles_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Charles-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, Simon Fuller was a co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Malaria_No_More_UK" title="Malaria No More UK">Malaria No More UK</a> donating resources and funds to allow the charity to set up and operate in London. Fuller remains a passionate ambassador for the cause and has shared <a class="external text" href="http://malarianomore.org.uk/real-life-stories/uk/malaria/simon-fuller" rel="nofollow">his own experience of malaria</a> with the charity.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fuller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fuller was born on 17 May 1960 in Cyprus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> He spent some of his childhood in a <a href="/wiki/Akrotiri_and_Dhekelia" title="Akrotiri and Dhekelia">British Overseas Territory on the island of Cyprus</a>, where his father, a <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a> pilot, had established a school, though later the family moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Accra,_Ghana" title="Accra, Ghana">Accra, Ghana</a> where his father was the headmaster of the British Military School, Burma Camp. The family moved back to <a href="/wiki/Hastings" title="Hastings">Hastings</a>, East Sussex, where Simon's father became headmaster of the school he attended.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guar_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-guar-41">[41]</a></sup> He has two brothers, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Fuller" title="Kim Fuller">Kim</a> and Mark. He married his long-term girlfriend Natalie Swanston in May 2008, and they have three children.</p>
<p>He has a portfolio of real estate including properties in Europe, North America and South America. In 2014, Fuller was awarded an honorary degree by the University of Sussex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
