<p><i><b>Cyborg 009</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">サイボーグゼロゼロナイン</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">Hepburn</a>: <i>Saibōgu Zero-Zero-Nain</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> created by <a href="/wiki/Shotaro_Ishinomori" title="Shotaro Ishinomori">Shotaro Ishinomori</a>. It was serialized in many different <a href="/wiki/List_of_manga_magazines" title="List of manga magazines">magazines</a>, including <i><a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Dnen_Gah%C5%8Dsha" title="Shōnen Gahōsha">Monthly Shōnen King</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Weekly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Magazine" title="Weekly Shōnen Magazine">Weekly Shōnen Magazine</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weekly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Big_Comic" title="Weekly Shōnen Big Comic">Shōnen Big Comic</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/COM_(manga_magazine)" title="COM (manga magazine)">COM</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Djo_Comic" title="Shōjo Comic">Shōjo Comic</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Weekly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Sunday" title="Weekly Shōnen Sunday">Weekly Shōnen Sunday</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Monthly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Jump" title="Monthly Shōnen Jump">Monthly Shōnen Jump</a></i> and <i>Monthly Comic Nora</i> in Japan. The first two arcs of the manga were published in English by <a href="/wiki/Tokyopop" title="Tokyopop">Tokyopop</a> with all the sound effects left out untranslated; as of 2006 it is out of print. However, in 2012, <a href="/wiki/ComiXology" title="ComiXology">comiXology</a> acquired the digital distribution rights to Shotaro Ishinomori's catalogue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-comiX_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-comiX-1">[1]</a></sup> Only the first 10 volumes are available, though.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>Nine regular humans are kidnapped by the evil Black Ghost organization to undergo human experiments which resulted in nine cyborgs with each one having super human powers. The nine cyborgs band together to fight for their freedom and to stop Black Ghost. The evil organization's goal is to start the next <a href="/wiki/World_war" title="World war">world war</a> by supplying rich buyers with countless weapons of mass destruction.</p>
<p>After the destruction of Black Ghost, the nine cyborgs also fight a variety of threats, from mad scientists, to supernatural beings and ancient civilizations.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyborg_009&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>The <b>first arc</b> was serialized in <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Weekly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_King&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Weekly Shōnen King (page does not exist)">Weekly Shōnen King</a></i> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Dnen_Gahosha" title="Shōnen Gahosha">Shōnen Gahosha</a>). It depicts the 009 origin story, the escape from Black Ghost, and running from cyborg assassins. It was briefly ended with the battle with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mythos" title="Mythos">Mythos</a> Cyborgs story.</li>
<li>The <b>second arc</b> called <i>The Underground Empire Yomi Arc.</i> Appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Weekly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Magazine" title="Weekly Shōnen Magazine">Weekly Shōnen Magazine</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Kodansha" title="Kodansha">Kodansha</a>) alongside the release of the film version. The story is highly influenced by <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Rice_Burroughs" title="Edgar Rice Burroughs">Edgar Rice Burroughs</a>' <a href="/wiki/At_the_Earth%27s_Core_(novel)" title="At the Earth's Core (novel)">Earth's Core series</a>, including an expedition to the center of the Earth with a drill tank and a reptile race who can use telepathy and grow wings. The story ends with the final battle against Black Ghost. The final scene has 009 and 002 falling into the Earth's atmosphere and being seen as a shooting star by two small children, one who wishes for a toy gun the other for world peace (a scene reminiscent of <a href="/wiki/Ray_Bradbury" title="Ray Bradbury">Ray Bradbury</a>'s <a href="/wiki/The_Illustrated_Man" title="The Illustrated Man"><i>Kaleidoscope</i></a>). This point was the intended finish for the series; however popular demand necessitated the two heroes be rescued. As such, 001 was able at the last minute of using his telekinetic power to retrieve 002 and 009 from their plummet before death.</li>
<li>Thus began the <b>third arc</b> Serialized in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Monthly_Bouken-oh&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Monthly Bouken-oh (page does not exist)"><i>Monthly Bouken-oh</i> (Adventure King)</a> (<a href="/wiki/Akita_Shoten" title="Akita Shoten">Akita Shoten</a>). It contained 6 story arcs, including the Monster Island Arc, the Middle East Arc, and the Angels Arc. The series abruptly ended during the Angels Arc.</li>
<li>The <b>fourth arc</b> called <i>The Battle of the Gods Arc</i> was serialized in <a href="/wiki/COM_(manga_magazine)" title="COM (manga magazine)"><i>COM</i></a> (<a href="/wiki/Mushi_Production" title="Mushi Production">Mushi Production</a>). Ishinomori resumed and retold the interrupted Angels Arc with a new plot, but the series once again ended abruptly. Ishinomori would not resume the series for a few years after this.</li>
<li>The <b>fifth arc</b> was serialized in <i><a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Djo_Comic" title="Shōjo Comic">Shōjo Comic</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Shogakukan" title="Shogakukan">Shogakukan</a>). Included the <i>Wind City Arc</i>, the <i>Snow Carnival Arc</i>, and the <i>Edda Arc</i>. The story deals with legendary and mythical like characters challenging the 00 Number Cyborgs.</li>
<li>The <b>sixth arc</b> connected to the 5th work. Arcs such as the <i>Deinonychus Arc</i> (appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Monthly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Jump" title="Monthly Shōnen Jump">Monthly Shōnen Jump</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Shueisha" title="Shueisha">Shueisha</a>)) and <i>Green Hole Arc</i> (appeared in <i>Play Comic</i> (<a href="/wiki/Akita_Shoten" title="Akita Shoten">Akita Shoten</a>)) were depicted, then after a long time the <i>Underwater Pyramid Arc</i> was serialized in <i>Monthly Manga Shōnen</i> (<a href="/wiki/Asahi_Sonorama" title="Asahi Sonorama">Asahi Sonorama</a>).</li>
<li>The <b>seventh arc</b> was serialized in <i><a href="/wiki/Weekly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Sunday" title="Weekly Shōnen Sunday">Weekly Shōnen Sunday</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Shogakukan" title="Shogakukan">Shogakukan</a>) to go with the revival anime. A long arc consisting of many short arcs, this series dealt with the battle against Neo Black Ghost as well as the emotional trauma of the 00 Number Cyborgs. The story is set approximately 20 years after the <i>Yomi Arc</i>, and the personalities and conduct of the cyborgs are depicted as more adult.</li>
<li>The <b>eighth arc</b> was serialized in <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Monthly_Comic_Nora&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Monthly Comic Nora (page does not exist)">Monthly Comic Nora</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Gakken" title="Gakken">Gakken</a>). A long arc called <i>People Drifting Through Time and Space Arc</i>. A sequel to the <i>Immigration Arc</i>. The <a href="/wiki/Count_of_St._Germain" title="Count of St. Germain">Count of St. Germain</a> from the Underwater Pyramid Arc appears, but his design is different.</li>
<li>Ishinomori's death made this the last work of the series, although it is not the final chapter.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga_publication_history">Manga publication history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyborg_009&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Manga publication history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Written and illustrated by <a href="/wiki/Shotaro_Ishinomori" title="Shotaro Ishinomori">Shotaro Ishinomori</a>; serialized in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Monthly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_King&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Monthly Shōnen King (page does not exist)">Monthly Shōnen King</a>; published in Japan by <a href="/wiki/Akita_Shoten" title="Akita Shoten">Akita Shoten</a> and other companies through its history; published in North America by <a href="/wiki/Tokyopop" title="Tokyopop">Tokyopop</a>.</p>
<p>The Tokyopop release took multiple liberties with the dialogue and translation, and was sourced from the Media Factory "MF Comics" release that had been published in 2003. The MF Comics release had comprised 36 volumes, of which Tokyopop had only covered 10. Ishinomori's initial intent was to have the Yomi arc be the end of the series, but he wound up continuing due to the fan letters that urged him to resurrect 009 and 002.</p>
<p>Digital comics distributor <a href="/wiki/ComiXology" title="ComiXology">comiXology</a> licensed the entire catalogue from <a href="/wiki/Ishimori_Productions" title="Ishimori Productions">Ishimori Productions</a> in 2012<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-comiX_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-comiX-1">[1]</a></sup> and has since made the first 10 volumes of <i>Cyborg 009</i> available. It is not known if comiXology intends to translate the entire series, as the volumes available had recycled the original Tokyopop adaptation by Mike Wellman and Bryan Matsumoto.</p>
<p>In April 2012, Shogakukan announced that the <i>Cyborg 009</i> manga would be given a proper conclusion in <i>Weekly Shōnen Sunday</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Entitled <b>Cyborg 009 Conclusion: God's War</b>, the manga is to be illustrated by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Masato_Hayase&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Masato Hayase (page does not exist)">Masato Hayase</a> and based on Ishinomori's original concept notes, sketches, and a novel draft, all of which had been gathered by his son, Jo Onodera. <i>Conclusion</i> is scheduled to debut on April 13, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Graphic_novel">Graphic novel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyborg_009&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Graphic novel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A full-color <a href="/wiki/Graphic_novel" title="Graphic novel">graphic novel</a> based off the franchise was released at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego_Comic-Con_International" title="San Diego Comic-Con International">San Diego Comic-Con International</a> on July 21, 2013 to align with the anniversary of Ishinomori's original manga. The book is a condensed retelling of the 00 Cyborgs' battle against Black Ghost, led by Sekar (Skull). The book also cites Foundation X, the recurring evil organization from the <a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Series" title="Kamen Rider Series">Kamen Rider Series</a> since <a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_W" title="Kamen Rider W">Kamen Rider W</a>, as being the financial backers of Black Ghost, as they are for the various Evil Organizations in the recent Kamen Rider series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The full release date is scheduled for September 11, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The graphic novel is written by F. J. DeSanto and Bradley Cramp, penciled and inked by <a href="/wiki/Marcus_To" title="Marcus To">Marcus To</a> and published by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archaia_Studios_Press" title="Archaia Studios Press">Archaia Comics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_Movie_009_Re:Cyborg">2012 Movie <a href="/wiki/009_Re:Cyborg" title="009 Re:Cyborg">009 Re:Cyborg</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyborg_009&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2012 Movie 009 Re:Cyborg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>A 3DCG movie produced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Production_I.G." title="Production I.G.">Production I.G.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sanzigen" title="Sanzigen">Sanzigen</a> and Ishimori Productions, was released on October 27, 2012. <a href="/wiki/Kenji_Kamiyama" title="Kenji Kamiyama">Kenji Kamiyama</a> is the director and writer. <a href="/wiki/Kenji_Kawai" title="Kenji Kawai">Kenji Kawai</a>, who has worked before with Kamiyama on <i><a href="/wiki/Moribito:_Guardian_of_the_Spirit" title="Moribito: Guardian of the Spirit">Moribito: Guardian of the Spirit</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Eden_of_the_East" title="Eden of the East">Eden of the East</a></i>, is composing the music. The film was released in Japan on October 27, 2012. It will also open simultaneously in more than five Asian regions, including Hong Kong, Taiwan, Singapore, Malaysia, and South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> A manga adaptation by Gatou Asou, character designer for <i>Moribito</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Occult_Academy" title="Occult Academy">Occult Academy</a></i>, is currently being serialized in <a href="/wiki/Square_Enix" title="Square Enix">Square Enix</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Gangan_Comics" title="Gangan Comics">Monthly Big Gangan</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The UK anime distributor Anime Limited has announced that they have acquired the movie and will produce an English dub at <a href="/wiki/NYAV_Post" title="NYAV Post">NYAV Post</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Madman_Entertainment" title="Madman Entertainment">Madman Entertainment</a> also has rights to release the film in Australia and New Zealand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Anime_Expo" title="Anime Expo">Anime Expo</a> 2013, <a href="/wiki/Funimation" title="Funimation">Funimation</a> had announced that they acquired the film for North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The English voice cast was announced on April 16, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-animenewsnetwork.com_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-animenewsnetwork.com-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<p>A 2016 3DCG movie produced by Production I.G and Signal M.D and distributed by Toho, was released on November 25, 2016. The movie itself will be divided into 3 parts, with Part 2 being released December 2, 2016 and Part 3 on December 9, 2016. Kenji Kamiyama is credited as chief director of the project, and Koudai Kakimoto is directing the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Netflix has acquired digital distribution rights to the movie, where the movie will be shown first on Netflix Japan in Spring 2017, with other territories to follow later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_games">Video games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyborg_009&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Video games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Three video games based on the series were released only in Japan. One of them was an <a href="/wiki/Action_game" title="Action game">action</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Platformer" title="Platformer">platformer</a> released for the <a href="/wiki/Super_Nintendo_Entertainment_System" title="Super Nintendo Entertainment System">Super Famicom</a> by BEC in 1994, in which each level one of the eight adult cyborgs (001 is not playable) is selected as the leader of a strike force for a particular mission and the player is allowed to choose two others to accompany them. The other game (released by <a href="/wiki/Telenet_Japan" title="Telenet Japan">Telenet Japan</a>'s subsidiary Riot) in 1993 was for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mega_CD" title="Mega CD">Mega CD</a> and is also a side scroller.</p>
<p>In 2002, <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_Simple_series_video_games#Simple_Character_2000_Series" title="List of Simple series video games">Simple Characters 2000 Series Vol. 15: Cyborg 009: The Block Kuzushi</a></i> was released for the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation" title="PlayStation">PlayStation</a> by <a href="/wiki/Bandai" title="Bandai">Bandai</a>.</p>

<p>The 1979 version aired in Italy and became popular with Italian viewers.</p>
<p>The 2001 version aired on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MBC_3_(Middle_East)" title="MBC 3 (Middle East)">MBC 3</a> several times starting from 2005 and became extremely popular with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arab" title="Arab">Arab</a> viewers.</p>
<p>The 1967 movie was aired in Mexico, and years later, the 2001 Television series aired on Toonami in 2003, and was later replayed on Cadena Tres in 2007, and was quite popular with Mexican viewers.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="North_American_releases">North American releases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyborg_009&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: North American releases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 1979 series was broadcast with English subtitles on Japanese-language television in Hawaii, California, and the New York City area. The English subtitles were produced by San Francisco-based, Fuji Television, which did not broadcast the series as part of its Japanese programming on <a href="/wiki/KEMO-TV" title="KEMO-TV">KEMO-TV</a>.</p>
<p>The 1980 film was released stateside in 1988 by Celebrity Home Entertainment as <i>Defenders of the Vortex</i> with an edited version of a poorly received English dub that was commissioned through the Tokyo, Japan-based Frontier Enterprises. It later received an unedited direct-to-video English release in 1995 by Best Film and Video Corporation with the full version of the same dub.</p>
<p>The 2001 TV series was licensed by Avex Inc. (the North American branch of Avex Mode, the 2001 series' original distributor in Japan) and dubbed into English by <a href="/wiki/Animaze" title="Animaze">Animaze</a> and ZRO Limit Productions. The entire series was dubbed, with the first 26 episodes shown on the <a href="/wiki/Toonami" title="Toonami">Toonami</a> programming block on <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a>, while episodes 27 to 47 were shown in a late-night block before the show was dropped from the lineup. The first 8 episodes are currently available on DVD from <a href="/wiki/Sony_Pictures_Home_Entertainment" title="Sony Pictures Home Entertainment">Columbia TriStar Home Entertainment</a> in both an uncut bilingual and a dub-only "cut" broadcast version, though as of 2017, none of the other episodes have become available on home video. Japanese and Hong Kong releases remain the only way to see the entire series on DVD, while <a href="/wiki/Madman_Entertainment" title="Madman Entertainment">Madman Entertainment</a> released the first 26 dubbed episodes to DVD in Australia.</p>
<p>The 2012 movie is licensed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FUNimation_Entertainment" title="FUNimation Entertainment">FUNimation Entertainment</a> (North America), <a href="/wiki/Anime_Limited" title="Anime Limited">Anime Limited</a> (UK), and <a href="/wiki/Madman_Entertainment" title="Madman Entertainment">Madman Entertainment</a> (Australia/New Zealand), with an English dub produced by <a href="/wiki/NYAV_Post" title="NYAV Post">NYAV Post</a>, and a theatrical release in all three territories.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-animenewsnetwork.com_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-animenewsnetwork.com-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<p>Joe/009 makes an appearance in Chapter 36 of the <i>The <a href="/wiki/Skull_Man" title="Skull Man">Skull Man</a></i> manga, in a chance meeting with Ryuusei Chisato, the Skull Man. Both discuss the nature of evil and humanity over coffee, and meet again by chance when the Skull Man stops a dirty deal by politicians going on at the docks. The 2007 <i><a href="/wiki/Skull_Man" title="Skull Man">Skull Man</a></i> anime was set up as a prequel of <i>Cyborg 009</i>, with many of the events in the series finale setting up <i>Cyborg 009</i>. The 1997 anime <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_of_Braves_GaoGaiGar" title="King of Braves GaoGaiGar">King of Braves GaoGaiGar</a></i>, the character Soldato J is a tribute to Jet Link/002, sharing his love of flight, acceleration mode, and characteristic nose.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="The material near this tag possibly contains original research. Dengekitai">J.A.K.Q. Dengekitai</a></i>, the latter series being especially similar to <i>Cyborg 009</i> in terms of characters and premise.
