<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mathematics">Mathematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cycle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Mathematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cyclic_(mathematics)" title="Cyclic (mathematics)">Cyclic (mathematics)</a>, a list of mathematics articles with "cyclic" in the title.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cycle_(algebraic_topology)" title="Cycle (algebraic topology)">Cycle (algebraic topology)</a>, a simplicial chain with 0 boundary</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cycle_(graph_theory)" title="Cycle (graph theory)">Cycle (graph theory)</a>, a nontrivial path in a graph from a node to itself
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cycle_graph" title="Cycle graph">Cycle graph</a>, a graph that is itself a cycle</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cycle_matroid" title="Cycle matroid">Cycle matroid</a>, a matroid derived from the cycle structure of a graph</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cyclic_group" title="Cyclic group">Cyclic group</a>, a group generated by a single element</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cycle_(sequence)" title="Cycle (sequence)">Cycle (sequence)</a>, a sequence with repeating values
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cycle_detection" title="Cycle detection">Cycle detection</a>, the algorithmic problem of detecting eventual repetitions in sequences generated by iterated functions</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Cycle, a set equipped with a <a href="/wiki/Cyclic_order" title="Cyclic order">cyclic order</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Necklace_(combinatorics)" title="Necklace (combinatorics)">Necklace (combinatorics)</a>, equivalence classes of cyclically ordered sequences of symbols modulo certain symmetries</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cyclic_permutation" title="Cyclic permutation">Cyclic permutation</a>, a basic permutation (all permutations are products of cycles)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cycle_per_second" title="Cycle per second">Cycles per second</a>, a unit of frequency, the modern equivalent being <a href="/wiki/Hertz" title="Hertz">hertz</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Turn_(geometry)" title="Turn (geometry)">Turn (geometry)</a> or cycle, a unit of plane angle equal to 360 degrees</li>
</ul>
