<p>A <b>cycloalkene</b> or <b>cycloolefin</b> is a type of <a href="/wiki/Alkene" title="Alkene">alkene</a> <a href="/wiki/Hydrocarbon" title="Hydrocarbon">hydrocarbon</a> which contains a closed ring of <a href="/wiki/Carbon" title="Carbon">carbon</a> <a href="/wiki/Atom" title="Atom">atoms</a>, but has no <a href="/wiki/Aromaticity" title="Aromaticity">aromatic</a> character. Some cycloalkenes, such as <a href="/wiki/Cyclobutene" title="Cyclobutene">cyclobutene</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cyclopentene" title="Cyclopentene">cyclopentene</a>, can be used as <a href="/wiki/Monomer" title="Monomer">monomers</a> to produce <a href="/wiki/Polymer" title="Polymer">polymer</a> chains. Due to geometrical considerations, smaller cycloalkenes are almost always the <a href="/wiki/Cis%E2%80%93trans_isomerism" title="Cis–trans isomerism"><i>cis</i> isomers</a>, and the term <i>cis</i> tends to be omitted from the names. In larger rings (from around 8 atoms), <a href="/wiki/Cis%E2%80%93trans_isomerism" title="Cis–trans isomerism"><i>cis</i>–<i>trans</i> isomerism</a> of the double bond may occur.</p>
