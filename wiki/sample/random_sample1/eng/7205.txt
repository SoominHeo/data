<p>The <b>New Shuttle</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ニューシャトル</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nyū Shatoru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a manually driven rubber-tyred <a href="/wiki/People_mover" title="People mover">people mover</a> system in <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Prefecture" title="Saitama Prefecture">Saitama Prefecture</a>, Japan, operated by Saitama New Urban Transit Co., Ltd. <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">埼玉新都市交通株式会社</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Saitama Shin Toshi Kōtsū Kabushiki-gaisha</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.</p>
<p>The 12.7-kilometre <b>Ina Line</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">伊奈線</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ina-sen</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> that runs north from <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cmiya_Station_(Saitama)" title="Ōmiya Station (Saitama)">Ōmiya Station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Saitama,_Saitama" title="Saitama, Saitama">Saitama, Saitama</a>, alongside the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tohoku_Shinkansen" title="Tohoku Shinkansen">Tohoku Shinkansen</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joetsu_Shinkansen" title="Joetsu Shinkansen">Joetsu Shinkansen</a> elevated high-speed lines through <a href="/wiki/Ageo,_Saitama" title="Ageo, Saitama">Ageo</a> to <a href="/wiki/Uchijuku_Station" title="Uchijuku Station">Uchijuku Station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ina,_Saitama" title="Ina, Saitama">Ina</a> in <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Prefecture" title="Saitama Prefecture">Saitama Prefecture</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Tokyo_Area" title="Greater Tokyo Area">Greater Tokyo Area</a> is the only route that is run on the system. The line is double tracked from <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cmiya_Station_(Saitama)" title="Ōmiya Station (Saitama)">Ōmiya Station</a> to <a href="/wiki/Maruyama_Station_(Saitama)" title="Maruyama Station (Saitama)">Maruyama Station</a> and single tracked from Maruyama to <a href="/wiki/Uchijuku_Station" title="Uchijuku Station">Uchijuku Station</a>.</p>
<p>Saitama New Urban Transit is a <a href="/wiki/Kabushiki_gaisha" title="Kabushiki gaisha">kabushiki gaisha</a> whose major shareholders include the <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">East Japan Railway Company</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tobu_Railway" title="Tobu Railway">Tobu Railway</a>, banks, Saitama prefectural government, and the cities and the town served.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Shuttle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The people of Ina town, on the branch point of the <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dhoku_Shinkansen" title="Tōhoku Shinkansen">Tohoku</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joetsu_Shinkansen" title="Joetsu Shinkansen">Joetsu Shinkansen</a> high-speed railway lines, opposed the latter being routed through their area, complaining that the town would be divided by the new tracks and beset with <a href="/wiki/Noise_pollution" title="Noise pollution">noise pollution</a>. To placate the residents, new railway lines were planned. The AGT Ina Line was the solution reached for the area north of Ōmiya Station, where the potential demand was not large enough to run <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heavy_rail" title="Heavy rail">heavy rail</a> lines economically. (Currently, the line generates an operating profit.) A heavy rail line (the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saikyo_Line" title="Saikyo Line">Saikyo Line</a>) was the solution reached for the south of Ōmiya Station.</p>
<ul>
<li>1 April 1980: Operating company Saitama New Urban Transit Co., Ltd. <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">埼玉新都市交通株式会社</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Saitama Shin Toshi Kōtsū Kabushiki-gaisha</i></span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>) is incorporated.</li>
<li>22 December 1983: The section between Ōmiya and Hanuki stations opens.</li>
<li>2 August 1990: The remaining section between Hanuki and Uchijuku stations opens.</li>
<li>14 October 2007: Ōnari Station is renamed Tetsudō-Hakubutsukan Station when the <a href="/wiki/Railway_Museum_(Saitama)" title="Railway Museum (Saitama)">Railway Museum</a> (<i>Tetsudō-Hakubutsukan</i>) opens.</li>
