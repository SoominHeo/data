<p><a href="/wiki/Viscount" title="Viscount">Viscount</a> <b>Saitō Makoto</b>, <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Bath" title="Order of the Bath">GCB</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">斎藤 実</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, October 27, 1858 – February 26, 1936)</span> was a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> naval officer and politician.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nussbaum809_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nussbaum809-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Saitō was an <a href="/wiki/Admiral" title="Admiral">admiral</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Imperial Japanese Navy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He was two-time <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Governor-General_of_Korea" title="Japanese Governor-General of Korea">Governor-General of Korea</a> from 1919 to 1927 and from 1929 to 1931, and the 30th <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Japan" title="Prime Minister of Japan">Prime Minister of Japan</a> from May 26, 1932 to July 8, 1934.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sait%C5%8D_Makoto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sait%C5%8D_Makoto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Saitō was born in <a href="/wiki/Mizusawa,_Iwate" title="Mizusawa, Iwate">Mizusawa Domain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mutsu_Province" title="Mutsu Province">Mutsu Province</a> (part of present-day <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Csh%C5%AB,_Iwate" title="Ōshū, Iwate">Ōshū City</a> <a href="/wiki/Iwate_Prefecture" title="Iwate Prefecture">Iwate Prefecture</a>), as the son of a <i><a href="/wiki/Samurai" title="Samurai">samurai</a></i> of the Mizusawa Clan. In 1879, he graduated from the 6th class <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Naval_Academy" title="Imperial Japanese Naval Academy">Imperial Japanese Naval Academy</a>, ranking third out of a class of 17 cadets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He was commissioned an <a href="/wiki/Ensign_(rank)" title="Ensign (rank)">ensign</a> on September 8, 1882, and promoted to <a href="/wiki/Sub-lieutenant" title="Sub-lieutenant">sub-lieutenant</a> on February 25, 1884.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_career">Military career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sait%C5%8D_Makoto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Military career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1884, Saitō went to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> for four years to study as a <a href="/wiki/Military_attach%C3%A9" title="Military attaché">military attaché</a>. Promoted to <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant" title="Lieutenant">lieutenant</a> on July 14, 1886; in 1888, after returning to Japan, he served as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy_General_Staff" title="Imperial Japanese Navy General Staff">Imperial Japanese Navy General Staff</a>.</p>
<p>After his promotion to <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_commander" title="Lieutenant commander">lieutenant commander</a> on December 20, 1893, he served as <a href="/wiki/Executive_officer" title="Executive officer">executive officer</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Cruiser" title="Cruiser">cruiser</a> <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Izumi" title="Japanese cruiser Izumi"><i>Izumi</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/Battleship" title="Battleship">battleship</a> <a href="/wiki/Japanese_battleship_Fuji" title="Japanese battleship Fuji"><i>Fuji</i></a>.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/First_Sino-Japanese_War" title="First Sino-Japanese War">First Sino-Japanese War</a>, Saitō served as captain of the <a href="/wiki/Cruiser" title="Cruiser">cruisers</a> <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Akitsushima" title="Japanese cruiser Akitsushima"><i>Akitsushima</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Itsukushima" title="Japanese cruiser Itsukushima"><i>Itsukushima</i></a>. He received rapid promotions to <a href="/wiki/Commander" title="Commander">commander</a> on December 1, 1897 and to <a href="/wiki/Captain_(naval)" title="Captain (naval)">captain</a> on December 27. On November 10, 1898, he became Vice Minister of the Navy, and was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Rear_admiral" title="Rear admiral">rear admiral</a> on May 20, 1900<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sait%C5%8D_Makoto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Saitō was again Vice Navy Minister at the start of the <a href="/wiki/Russo-Japanese_War" title="Russo-Japanese War">Russo-Japanese War</a> of 1904–1905. He was promoted to Vice Admiral on June 6, 1904. He was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Rising_Sun" title="Order of the Rising Sun">Order of the Rising Sun</a> (1st class) in 1906. After the end of the war, he served as <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_the_Navy_of_Japan" title="Ministry of the Navy of Japan">Navy Minister</a> for 6 years, from 1906 to 1914, during which time he continually strove for expansion of the navy.</p>
<p>On September 21, 1907, Saitō was ennobled with the title of <i>danshaku</i> (<a href="/wiki/Baron" title="Baron">baron</a>) under the <i><a href="/wiki/Kazoku" title="Kazoku">kazoku</a></i> peerage system. On October 16, 1912, he was promoted to full <a href="/wiki/Admiral" title="Admiral">admiral</a>. However, on April 16, 1914, Saitō was forced to resign from his post as Navy Minister due implications of his involvement in the <a href="/wiki/Siemens_scandal" title="Siemens scandal">Siemens scandal</a>, and officially entered the reserves.</p>
<p>In September 1919, Saitō was appointed as the third <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Governor-General_of_Korea" title="Japanese Governor-General of Korea">Japanese Governor-General of Korea</a>. Rising to the post right after the <a href="/wiki/March_1st_Movement" title="March 1st Movement">culmination</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_independence_movement" title="Korean independence movement">Korean independence movement</a>, he was subject to an immediate assassination attempt by radical <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_nationalist" title="Korean nationalist">Korean nationalists</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He served as governor-general of Korea twice—in 1919–1927,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nussbaum809_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nussbaum809-1">[1]</a></sup> and again in 1929–1931,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> implementing a series of measures to moderate Japan's policies on Koreans. He was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Paulownia_Flowers" title="Order of the Paulownia Flowers">Order of the Paulownia Flowers</a> in 1924. On April 29, 1925, his title was elevated to that of <i>shishaku</i> (<a href="/wiki/Viscount" title="Viscount">viscount</a>).</p>
<p>In 1927, Saitō was a member of the Japanese delegation at the <a href="/wiki/Geneva_Naval_Conference" title="Geneva Naval Conference">Geneva Naval Conference</a> on Disarmament, and he later became a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Privy_councillor" title="Privy councillor">privy councillor</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Prime_Minister">Prime Minister</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sait%C5%8D_Makoto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Prime Minister">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the assassination of <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Japan" title="Prime Minister of Japan">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Inukai_Tsuyoshi" title="Inukai Tsuyoshi">Inukai Tsuyoshi</a> on May 15, 1932 by fanatical navy officers who thought Inukai far too conciliatory (the <a href="/wiki/May_15_Incident" title="May 15 Incident">May 15 Incident</a>), Prince <a href="/wiki/Saionji_Kinmochi" title="Saionji Kinmochi">Saionji Kinmochi</a>, one of the Emperor's closest and strongest advisors, attempted to stop the slide towards a military take-over of the government. In a compromise move, Saitō was chosen to be Inukai's successor. <a href="/wiki/Sadao_Araki" title="Sadao Araki">Sadao Araki</a> remained as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/War_Minister" title="War Minister">War Minister</a> and immediately began making demands on the new government. During Saitō's tenure, Japan recognized the independence of <a href="/wiki/Manchukuo" title="Manchukuo">Manchukuo</a>, and withdrew from the <a href="/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a>.</p>
<p>Saitō's administration was one of the longer-serving ones of the inter-war period, and it continued until July 8, 1934, when the cabinet resigned en masse because of the <a href="/wiki/Teijin_Incident" title="Teijin Incident">Teijin Incident</a> bribery scandal. <a href="/wiki/Keisuke_Okada" title="Keisuke Okada">Keisuke Okada</a> succeeded as prime minister.</p>
<p>Saitō continued to be an important figure in politics as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keeper_of_the_Privy_Seal_of_Japan" title="Keeper of the Privy Seal of Japan">Lord Keeper of the Privy Seal</a> from December 26, 1935, but was assassinated during the <a href="/wiki/February_26_Incident" title="February 26 Incident">February 26 Incident</a> of 1936 at his home in <a href="/wiki/Yotsuya" title="Yotsuya">Yotsuya</a>, Tokyo. Takahashi, his predecessor, was shot dead the same day, along with several other top-rank politicians targeted by the rebels.</p>
<p>Saitō was posthumously awarded the Supreme <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Chrysanthemum" title="Order of the Chrysanthemum">Order of the Chrysanthemum</a>.</p>
