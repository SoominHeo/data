<p><b>September 17</b> is the 260th day of the year (261st in <a href="/wiki/Leap_year" title="Leap year">leap years</a>) in the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>. There are 105 days remaining until the end of the year. This date is slightly more likely to fall on a Monday, Thursday or Saturday (58 in 400 years each) than on Tuesday or Wednesday (57), and slightly less likely to occur on a Friday or Sunday (56).</p>

<ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/1176" title="1176">1176</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Myriokephalon" title="Battle of Myriokephalon">Battle of Myriokephalon</a> is fought between the Byzantine Empire and the Seljuk Turks.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1382" title="1382">1382</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis_the_Great" title="Louis the Great">Louis the Great</a>'s daughter, <a href="/wiki/Mary,_Queen_of_Hungary" title="Mary, Queen of Hungary">Mary</a>, is crowned <a href="/wiki/King_of_Hungary" title="King of Hungary">"king" of Hungary</a>.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/1577" title="1577">1577</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Bergerac" title="Treaty of Bergerac">Treaty of Bergerac</a> is signed between <a href="/wiki/Henry_III_of_France" title="Henry III of France">Henry III of France</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Huguenot" title="Huguenot">Huguenots</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1620" title="1620">1620</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Cecora_(1620)" title="Battle of Cecora (1620)">Battle of Cecora (1620)</a> is fought between the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Lithuanian_Commonwealth" title="Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth">Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Ottoman_War_(1620%E2%80%9321)" title="Polish–Ottoman War (1620–21)">Polish–Ottoman War (1620–21)</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1630" title="1630">1630</a> – The city of <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> is founded.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1631" title="1631">1631</a> – Sweden wins a major victory at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Breitenfeld_(1631)" title="Battle of Breitenfeld (1631)">Battle of Breitenfeld</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War" title="Thirty Years' War">Thirty Years' War</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1658" title="1658">1658</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Vilanova" title="Battle of Vilanova">Battle of Vilanova</a> is fought between the Portugal and Spain during the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Restoration_War" title="Portuguese Restoration War">Portuguese Restoration War</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1683" title="1683">1683</a> – <a href="/wiki/Antonie_van_Leeuwenhoek" title="Antonie van Leeuwenhoek">Antonie van Leeuwenhoek</a> writes a letter to the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">Royal Society</a> describing "<a href="/wiki/Animalcule" title="Animalcule">animalcules</a>": the first known description of <a href="/wiki/Protozoa" title="Protozoa">protozoa</a>.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/1775" title="1775">1775</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>: The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Canada_(1775)" title="Invasion of Canada (1775)">Invasion of Canada</a> begins with the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Fort_St._Jean" title="Siege of Fort St. Jean">Siege of Fort St. Jean</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1776" title="1776">1776</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Presidio_of_San_Francisco" title="Presidio of San Francisco">Presidio of San Francisco</a> is founded in <a href="/wiki/New_Spain" title="New Spain">New Spain</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1778" title="1778">1778</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Fort_Pitt" title="Treaty of Fort Pitt">Treaty of Fort Pitt</a> is signed. It is the first formal treaty between the United States and a <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a> tribe (the <a href="/wiki/Lenape" title="Lenape">Lenape</a> or Delaware Indians).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1787" title="1787">1787</a> – The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Constitution" title="United States Constitution">United States Constitution</a> is signed in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1793" title="1793">1793</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Peyrestortes" title="Battle of Peyrestortes">Battle of Peyrestortes</a> is fought.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1794" title="1794">1794</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Sprimont" title="Battle of Sprimont">Battle of Sprimont</a> is fought.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1809" title="1809">1809</a> – Peace between Sweden and Russia in the <a href="/wiki/Finnish_War" title="Finnish War">Finnish War</a>; the territory that will become <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a> is ceded to Russia by the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Fredrikshamn" title="Treaty of Fredrikshamn">Treaty of Fredrikshamn</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1814" title="1814">1814</a> – <a href="/wiki/Francis_Scott_Key" title="Francis Scott Key">Francis Scott Key</a> finishes his poem "Defence of Fort McHenry", later to be the lyrics of "<a href="/wiki/The_Star-Spangled_Banner" title="The Star-Spangled Banner">The Star-Spangled Banner</a>".</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1849" title="1849">1849</a> – American <a href="/wiki/Abolitionism_in_the_United_States" title="Abolitionism in the United States">abolitionist</a> <a href="/wiki/Harriet_Tubman" title="Harriet Tubman">Harriet Tubman</a> escapes from <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slavery</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1859" title="1859">1859</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joshua_A._Norton" title="Joshua A. Norton">Joshua A. Norton</a> declares himself "Norton I, Emperor of the United States."</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1861" title="1861">1861</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Pav%C3%B3n" title="Battle of Pavón">Battle of Pavón</a> is fought.</li>
