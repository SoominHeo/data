<p><b>Nicholas Tse Ting-fung</b> (<a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E8%AC%9D" title="wikt:謝">謝</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E9%9C%86" title="wikt:霆">霆</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E9%8B%92" title="wikt:鋒">鋒</a></span>; born 29 August 1980) is a <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_people" title="Hong Kong people">Hong Kong</a> actor, singer, songwriter, musician, entrepreneur, and martial artist. He is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Entertainment_Group" title="Emperor Entertainment Group">Emperor Entertainment Group</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-profile1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tse initially entered the entertainment industry in 1996 as a singer. He originally learned martial arts from <a href="/wiki/Philip_Ng" title="Philip Ng">Philip Ng</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andy_On" title="Andy On">Andy On</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sammo_Hung" title="Sammo Hung">Sammo Hung</a> for the screen and television, which Tse continues to practice. Tse made his film debut in 1998 with the crime film <i><a href="/wiki/Young_and_Dangerous:_The_Prequel" title="Young and Dangerous: The Prequel">Young and Dangerous: The Prequel</a></i>, for which he received the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Film_Award" title="Hong Kong Film Award">Hong Kong Film Award</a> for his performance in the Best New Performer category. In 2003, Tse founded <b>Post Production Office Limited</b>, a special effects companies in Hong Kong which provides services for movies, video games, and advertisements. The company grosses over one billion <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_dollar" title="Hong Kong dollar">Hong Kong dollars</a>, and the company has since been sold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, he won the award for <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Film_Award_for_Best_Actor" title="Hong Kong Film Award for Best Actor">Hong Kong Film Award for Best Actor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In November 2012, Tse attended a <a href="/wiki/Avenue_of_Stars,_Hong_Kong" title="Avenue of Stars, Hong Kong">Hong Kong Avenue of Stars</a> Hand Imprint Ceremony.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Tse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tse was born in Hong Kong on 29 August 1980, to Hong Kong actor <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Tse" title="Patrick Tse">Patrick Tse</a> (<span lang="zh">謝賢</span>) and actress Deborah Lee (<span lang="zh"><a class="extiw" href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%8B%84%E6%B3%A2%E6%8B%89" title="zh:狄波拉">狄波拉</a></span>). Tse moved to <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, Canada at a young age.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kungfu1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kungfu1-5">[5]</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/St._George%27s_School_(Vancouver)" title="St. George's School (Vancouver)">St. George's School, Vancouver</a>, until his roommate and himself were both expelled for getting into a fight with an older student. Nicholas later attended <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_International_School" title="Hong Kong International School">Hong Kong International School</a> for one year before dropping out at grade 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kungfu1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Kungfu1-5">[5]</a></sup> For a year he lived in <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix, Arizona</a> before going back to Vancouver. He also studied music in Japan before returning to Hong Kong. He holds dual Canadian and Hong Kong citizenship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kungfu1_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Kungfu1-5">[5]</a></sup> Tse has a younger sister, <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Tse" title="Jennifer Tse">Jennifer Tse</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Tse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Tse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tse was singing at a party for a family friend when he was discovered by <a href="/wiki/Albert_Yeung" title="Albert Yeung">Albert Yeung</a>, head of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Entertainment_Group" title="Emperor Entertainment Group">Emperor Entertainment Group</a>. In 1997, Tse signed a record deal with EEG under the Fitto label and released his first album, <i>My Attitude</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kungfu1_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-Kungfu1-5">[5]</a></sup> The album was ranked 3rd on the top selling CD chart of IFPI.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-profile2-6">[6]</a></sup> It was that year where he won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1997_Jade_Solid_Gold_Top_10_Awards" title="1997 Jade Solid Gold Top 10 Awards">most popular new artist award</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tse received mainstream success in 1998 with songs including "早知" (Knew it would be like that) and "如果只得一星期" (If there's only one week) and became a prominent heartthrob in the entertainment industry. He received a musical award during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1999_Jade_Solid_Gold_Top_10_Awards" title="1999 Jade Solid Gold Top 10 Awards">1999 Jade Solid Gold Top 10 Awards</a> with his popular song "非走不可" (No choice but to leave).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Recorded on 27 September 1993.</p>
<p>In 2000, Tse released the album with the main hit "活著VIVA", which was regarded as his most popular song ever, and furthered his career significantly. It was after this song which he became an A-list singer at the time. In 2001, he released the album "玉蝴蝶"(Jade butterfly). In which two hits "玉蝴蝶" and "潛龍勿用" are collected.</p>
<p>In 2003, Tse received a <a href="/wiki/World_Music_Awards" title="World Music Awards">World Music Award</a> for "World's Best Chinese Artist" at the <a href="/wiki/2003_World_Music_Awards" title="2003 World Music Awards">2003 World Music Awards</a> for the success of album "玉蝴蝶".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Tse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tse's most notable films have included <i><a href="/wiki/Young_and_Dangerous:_The_Prequel" title="Young and Dangerous: The Prequel">Young and Dangerous: The Prequel</a></i> (1998), <i><a href="/wiki/Gen-X_Cops" title="Gen-X Cops">Gen-X Cops</a></i> (1999), <i><a href="/wiki/Time_and_Tide_(2000_film)" title="Time and Tide (2000 film)">Time and Tide</a></i> (2000), and <i><a href="/wiki/Invisible_Target" title="Invisible Target">Invisible Target</a></i> (2007). He also collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Chan" title="Jackie Chan">Jackie Chan</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/New_Police_Story" title="New Police Story">New Police Story</a></i> (2004).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Tse has also acted in romance films including <i><a href="/wiki/Tiramisu_(film)" title="Tiramisu (film)">Tiramisu</a></i> (2002) and <i><a href="/wiki/Jade_Goddess_of_Mercy" title="Jade Goddess of Mercy">Jade Goddess of Mercy</a></i> (2003). Only later in his career did he participate in comedy roles including <i><a href="/wiki/Enter_the_Phoenix" title="Enter the Phoenix">Enter the Phoenix</a></i> (2004), <i><a href="/wiki/A_Chinese_Tall_Story" title="A Chinese Tall Story">A Chinese Tall Story</a></i> (2005), and <i><a href="/wiki/McDull,_the_Alumni" title="McDull, the Alumni">McDull, the Alumni</a></i> (2006). In the Hong Kong translated version of <i><a href="/wiki/A_Bug%27s_Life" title="A Bug's Life">A Bug's Life</a></i> (<span lang="zh">蟲蟲特工隊</span>), the character <a href="/wiki/A_Bug%27s_Life#Flik" title="A Bug's Life">Flik</a> was dubbed by Tse. He also lent his voice to the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_animation" title="Chinese animation">Chinese animation</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Lotus_Lantern" title="Lotus Lantern">Lotus Lantern</a></i> (1999). Tse's performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Bodyguards_and_Assassins" title="Bodyguards and Assassins">Bodyguards and Assassins</a></i> (2009), earned him an Asian Film Award at the <a href="/wiki/4th_Asian_Film_Awards" title="4th Asian Film Awards">4th Asian Film Awards</a> for Best Supporting Actor, and in 2011, Tse received a <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Film_Award" title="Hong Kong Film Award">Hong Kong Film Award</a> for Best Actor at the <a href="/wiki/30th_Hong_Kong_Film_Awards" title="30th Hong Kong Film Awards">30th Hong Kong Film Awards</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Stool_Pigeon_(2010_film)" title="The Stool Pigeon (2010 film)">The Stool Pigeon</a></i> (2010), beating out fellow actors <a href="/wiki/Chow_Yun-fat" title="Chow Yun-fat">Chow Yun-fat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Cheung" title="Jacky Cheung">Jacky Cheung</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Leung_Ka-fai" title="Tony Leung Ka-fai">Tony Leung Ka-fai</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cheung" title="Nick Cheung">Nick Cheung</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="TV_series">TV series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Tse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: TV series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tse has starred in two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_Broadcasts_Limited" title="Television Broadcasts Limited">TVB</a> Hong Kong local dramas, <i>Aiming High</i> (<span lang="zh">撻出愛火花</span> and <a href="/wiki/The_Monkey_King:_Quest_for_the_Sutra" title="The Monkey King: Quest for the Sutra">The Monkey King: Quest for the Sutra</a> (<span lang="zh">齊天大聖孫悟空</span>). He also participated in a documentary produced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_Broadcasts_Limited" title="Television Broadcasts Limited">Television Broadcasts Limited</a><i><a href="/wiki/On_the_Road_(Hong_Kong_TV_series)" title="On the Road (Hong Kong TV series)">On the Road II</a></i> (<span lang="zh">向世界出發II</span>), made in <a href="/wiki/Cambodia" title="Cambodia">Cambodia</a> in 2007. The rest of his drama series were filmed and produced by companies in <a href="/wiki/Mainland_China" title="Mainland China">Mainland China</a> . Most of Tse's drama are <a href="/wiki/Wuxia" title="Wuxia">wuxia</a> series, which are based in ancient Chinese societies.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Tse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tse once had a relationship with musician <a href="/wiki/Faye_Wong" title="Faye Wong">Faye Wong</a>, who is 11 years his senior, but they broke up in 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 31 July 2006, Tse officially admitted dating <a href="/wiki/Cecilia_Cheung" title="Cecilia Cheung">Cecilia Cheung</a> in an interview with Hong Kong commercial radio station 881/903. On 29 September 2006, Tse showed off his wedding ring at the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_International_Airport" title="Hong Kong International Airport">Hong Kong International Airport</a>, announcing he and Cecilia Cheung were married in a secret wedding ceremony in <a href="/wiki/Pamalican" title="Pamalican">Pamalican</a>, Philippines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kungfu1_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-Kungfu1-5">[5]</a></sup> The couple's eldest son, Lucas Tse, was born on 2 August 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stareastasia1_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stareastasia1-12">[12]</a></sup> Cheung gave birth to their second son, Quintus Tse, on 12 May 2010. However, their deemed romantic relationship ended on 14 June 2011 when Tse reportedly filed for divorce with Cheung.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On 21 September 2014 it was reported that Tse is back in a relationship with Faye Wong, who had divorced her latest spouse in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Incidents">Incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Tse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2002_motoring_incident">2002 motoring incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Tse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2002 motoring incident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the early morning of 23 March 2002, Tse crashed his <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_360" title="Ferrari 360">Ferrari 360 Modena</a> in Hong Kong <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_(Hong_Kong)" title="Central (Hong Kong)">Central</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Initially it was claimed that his driver was responsible for the crash, but further investigation by the <a href="/wiki/Independent_Commission_Against_Corruption_(Hong_Kong)" title="Independent Commission Against Corruption (Hong Kong)">ICAC</a> revealed the story was fabricated, involving three other men and a police officer. Tse spent two weeks in prison<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and was sentenced to 240 hours of <a href="/wiki/Community_service" title="Community service">community service</a> for obstruction of justice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kungfu1_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-Kungfu1-5">[5]</a></sup> Officer Lau Chi-Wai was sentenced to six months in prison. Although some viewed this charge as lenient, the 240 hours was the maximum punishment for this offense, given that Tse was a young offender at the time (21 years and 7 months). Nonetheless, some claimed that the sentence would send out a wrong message to Hong Kong youths, but it could be argued handing out a harsher sentence because he was a celebrity is just as inappropriate as handing out a lighter one. This incident had caused him to have a downfall in popularity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kungfu1_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-Kungfu1-5">[5]</a></sup> Tse have said that money to him has gone up by 40% since the accident.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time1_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Time1-17">[17]</a></sup> The car was originally a gift from the company EEG, as Tse himself accounted for 56% of the revenue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time1_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-Time1-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2002_assault_allegations">2002 assault allegations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Tse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2002 assault allegations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tse has had his share of brush-ups with "<a href="/wiki/Paparazzi" title="Paparazzi">paparazzi</a>". A photographer of <i><a href="/wiki/Sudden_Weekly" title="Sudden Weekly">Sudden Weekly</a></i> magazine accused Tse of hitting him outside a restaurant in Taiwan, bruising his nose and elbow. Although Tse denied the assault charge, he settled in July 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2003_motoring_incident">2003 motoring incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Tse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2003 motoring incident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tse had another accident in 2003, where he crashed his <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Camry" title="Toyota Camry">Toyota Camry</a> into <a href="/wiki/Wan_Chai" title="Wan Chai">Wan Chai</a> train station. He was banned from driving for six months and fined <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_dollar" title="Hong Kong dollar">HK$</a>4000 for reckless driving. Prior to this, he was fined HK$7000 and had his license suspended for a year for two separate charges of speeding.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005_motoring_incident">2005 motoring incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Tse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2005 motoring incident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He later had another accident in November 2005, where he collided with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_Security_Bureau" title="Public Security Bureau">Public Security Bureau</a> Bus in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Audi_RS4" title="Audi RS4">Audi RS4</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lantau" title="Lantau">Lantau</a>. He was banned from driving for another six months and fined HK$4,000, again for reckless driving. After the incident, Tse has resorted to traveling on a motorbike or scooter instead of an automobile.</p>
<p>After the police charge, Tse spent several months to "discover himself", then releasing the album <i>Reborn.</i> He has stated that he will be taking a break from singing and focusing on his acting for a year.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008_Edison_Chen_photo_scandal">2008 Edison Chen photo scandal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Tse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2008 Edison Chen photo scandal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2008, Tse's wife, Cecilia Cheung, was implicated in the <a href="/wiki/Edison_Chen_photo_scandal" title="Edison Chen photo scandal">Edison Chen photo scandal</a>, when intimate photographs of her were widely distributed on the internet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The couple and their relationship were under intense media spotlight, particularly from the gossip magazines, as a result.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Tse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">Musical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Tse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Musical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Most of Tse's songs were based on the <a href="/wiki/Cantopop" title="Cantopop">cantopop</a> <a href="/wiki/Chinese_rock" title="Chinese rock">rock</a> genre such as some of his top hits "<i>front, back, left, right</i>" (前前後後左左右右), "<i>Exposure</i>" (曝光) and "<i>Lonely Base</i>" (寂寞堂口). Some of his songs were produced in Tse's own 'Nic Production' which was revealed in TVB interviews several times. "<i>Live Viva</i>" (活著 Viva), one of Tse's most classical pop rock song, in whose MV Tse was featured with his flame guitar and a flamethrower, is widely known in Hong Kong.</p>
<p>He has sung a number of <a href="/wiki/Duet" title="Duet">duets</a> with fellow cantopop singers, namely "<i>Love</i>" (愛) with <a href="/wiki/Charlene_Choi" title="Charlene Choi">Charlene Choi</a>, "<i>Second Life</i>" (第二世), "<i>Amen</i>" (阿門) both with <a href="/wiki/Joey_Yung" title="Joey Yung">Joey Yung</a>, "<i>Kid</i>" (細路) with <a href="/wiki/Eason_Chan" title="Eason Chan">Eason Chan</a>, "<i>You Can't Stop Me</i>" with <a href="/wiki/Sam_Lee_(actor)" title="Sam Lee (actor)">Sam Lee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Fung" title="Stephen Fung">Stephen Fung</a> as well as "<i><a href="/wiki/Beauty_and_the_Beast" title="Beauty and the Beast">Beauty and the Beast</a></i>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> with <a href="/wiki/Meilin_Gray" title="Meilin Gray">Meilin</a>.</p>
<p>Besides being a singer, Tse also showed his interest and talent in song-writing. Plenty of his songs were produced by himself, either of the lyrics or the melody or both. Take his most renowned Mandarin songs "<i>I love you because I love you</i>"(因為愛所以愛） and "<i>Huang Zhong Ren</i>"(黃種人） for example, Tse composed and arranged both of these songs' melodies and also participated in the creation of lyrics in "<i>Huang Zhong Ren</i>".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_endorsements">Commercial endorsements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Tse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Commercial endorsements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tse has been in many advertisements since he joined the industry, from big names like <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pepsi" title="Pepsi">Pepsi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a> to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Biolyn&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Biolyn (page does not exist)">Biolyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_World_Mobility" title="New World Mobility">New World Mobility</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oris_(watches)" title="Oris (watches)">Oris</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Matsunichi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Matsunichi (page does not exist)">Matsunichi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daphne" title="Daphne">Daphne</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile3_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-profile3-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>He has also appeared in various magazines, including: <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Men%27s_Uno&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Men's Uno (page does not exist)">Men's Uno</a></i>, <i>Amoeba</i>, <i>Milk</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Ming_Pao" title="Ming Pao">Ming Pao</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/East_Week" title="East Week">East Week</a></i>in various forms including television commercials, posters and magazine advertisements.</p>
<p>On 12 January 2006, <a href="/wiki/Wax_sculpture" title="Wax sculpture">wax statues</a> of Tse was first exhibited along with seven other well known Chinese figures including basketball star <a href="/wiki/Yao_Ming" title="Yao Ming">Yao Ming</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Chan" title="Jackie Chan">Jackie Chan</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Madame_Tussauds_Hong_Kong" title="Madame Tussauds Hong Kong">Madame Tussauds</a> in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> On 19 June 2006, wax statues of both Tse and Cheung were revealed in Shanghai exhibition in Shanghai.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
