<p>The <i><b>Condor Trilogy</b></i> (射鵰三部曲) is a series of three <a href="/wiki/Wuxia" title="Wuxia">wuxia</a> novels written by <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>-based Chinese writer <a href="/wiki/Jin_Yong" title="Jin Yong">Jin Yong</a> (Louis Cha). The series is amongst the most popular of Jin Yong's works.</p>
<p>The novels in the trilogy are:</p>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_the_Condor_Heroes" title="The Legend of the Condor Heroes">The Legend of the Condor Heroes</a></i> (射鵰英雄傳), published in 1957.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/The_Return_of_the_Condor_Heroes" title="The Return of the Condor Heroes">The Return of the Condor Heroes</a></i> (神鵰俠侶), published in 1959.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/The_Heaven_Sword_and_Dragon_Saber" title="The Heaven Sword and Dragon Saber">The Heaven Sword and Dragon Saber</a></i> (倚天屠龍記), published in 1961.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_background">Historical background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Condor_Trilogy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Historical background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first novel, <i>The Legend of the Condor Heroes</i>, is set against a backdrop of <a href="/wiki/Jin%E2%80%93Song_Wars" title="Jin–Song Wars">a series of wars</a> fought between the <a href="/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Han Chinese</a>-dominated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_Song_dynasty" title="Southern Song dynasty">Southern Song dynasty</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Jurchen_people" title="Jurchen people">Jurchen</a>-led <a href="/wiki/Jin_dynasty_(1115%E2%80%931234)" title="Jin dynasty (1115–1234)">Jin dynasty</a>. Concurrently, <a href="/wiki/Mongols" title="Mongols">Mongol</a> tribes led by <a href="/wiki/Genghis_Khan" title="Genghis Khan">Temüjin (Genghis Khan)</a> emerge as a rising power in the north. The second novel, <i>The Return of the Condor Heroes</i>, is set in the period when the Mongols attack the Southern Song dynasty after having conquered the Jin dynasty. The historical <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Xiangyang" title="Battle of Xiangyang">Battle of Xiangyang</a> is also featured as one of the key events in the novel. The third and last novel, <i>The Heaven Sword and Dragon Saber</i>, is set in the late <a href="/wiki/Yuan_dynasty" title="Yuan dynasty">Yuan dynasty</a> (founded by the Mongols). Towards the end of the novel, the Yuan dynasty is overthrown by rebel forces and the <a href="/wiki/Ming_dynasty" title="Ming dynasty">Ming dynasty</a> is established.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Book_of_Wumu">Book of Wumu</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Condor_Trilogy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Book of Wumu">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <i>Book of Wumu</i> is a fictional military treatise written by the Song general <a href="/wiki/Yue_Fei" title="Yue Fei">Yue Fei</a>. The book is highly sought after by the Jurchens of the Jin dynasty. Guo Jing uses the knowledge he acquired from the book to defend the Song dynasty. Before his death, he hid the book in the blade of the Dragon Slaying Saber. Nearly a century after the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Xiangyang" title="Battle of Xiangyang">Battle of Xiangyang</a>, the book comes into <a href="/wiki/Zhang_Wuji" title="Zhang Wuji">Zhang Wuji</a>'s possession. Zhang uses one of the strategies in the book to defeat Yuan forces at the siege of <a href="/wiki/Mount_Song" title="Mount Song">Mount Song</a>. He passes the book to <a href="/wiki/Xu_Da" title="Xu Da">Xu Da</a>, who studies it and becomes a brilliant military leader. Xu Da leads rebel forces to overthrow the Yuan dynasty and becomes one of the founding pioneers of the Ming dynasty.</p>
