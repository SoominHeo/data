<p><b>September 2</b> is the 245th day of the year (246th in <a href="/wiki/Leap_year" title="Leap year">leap years</a>) in the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>. There are 120 days remaining until the end of the year. This date is slightly more likely to fall on a Wednesday, Friday or Sunday (58 in 400 years each) than on Monday or Tuesday (57), and slightly less likely to occur on a Thursday or Saturday (56).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=September_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/44_BC" title="44 BC">44 BC</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pharaoh" title="Pharaoh">Pharaoh</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cleopatra_VII" title="Cleopatra VII">Cleopatra VII</a> of <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> declares her son co-ruler as Ptolemy XV <a href="/wiki/Caesarion" title="Caesarion">Caesarion</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/44_BC" title="44 BC">44 BC</a> – <a href="/wiki/Cicero" title="Cicero">Cicero</a> launches the first of his <i><a href="/wiki/Philippic" title="Philippic">Philippics</a></i> (oratorical attacks) on <a href="/wiki/Mark_Antony" title="Mark Antony">Mark Antony</a>. He will make 14 of them over the following months.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/31_BC" title="31 BC">31 BC</a> – <a href="/wiki/Final_War_of_the_Roman_Republic" title="Final War of the Roman Republic">Final War of the Roman Republic</a>: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Actium" title="Battle of Actium">Battle of Actium</a>: Off the western coast of Greece, forces of <a href="/wiki/Augustus" title="Augustus">Octavian</a> defeat troops under <a href="/wiki/Mark_Antony" title="Mark Antony">Mark Antony</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cleopatra" title="Cleopatra">Cleopatra</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1192" title="1192">1192</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Jaffa" title="Treaty of Jaffa">Treaty of Jaffa</a> is signed between <a href="/wiki/Richard_I_of_England" title="Richard I of England">Richard I of England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saladin" title="Saladin">Saladin</a>, leading to the end of the <a href="/wiki/Third_Crusade" title="Third Crusade">Third Crusade</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1649" title="1649">1649</a> – The Italian city of <a href="/wiki/Castro,_Lazio" title="Castro, Lazio">Castro</a> is completely destroyed by the forces of <a href="/wiki/Pope_Innocent_X" title="Pope Innocent X">Pope Innocent X</a>, ending the <a href="/wiki/Wars_of_Castro" title="Wars of Castro">Wars of Castro</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1666" title="1666">1666</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Great_Fire_of_London" title="Great Fire of London">Great Fire of London</a> breaks out and burns for three days, destroying 10,000 buildings including <a href="/wiki/St_Paul%27s_Cathedral" title="St Paul's Cathedral">St Paul's Cathedral</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1752" title="1752">1752</a> – Great Britain adopts the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>, nearly two centuries later than most of <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">Western Europe</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1789" title="1789">1789</a> – The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_the_Treasury" title="United States Department of the Treasury">United States Department of the Treasury</a> is founded.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1792" title="1792">1792</a> – During what became known as the <a href="/wiki/September_Massacres" title="September Massacres">September Massacres</a> of the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>, rampaging mobs slaughter three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic Church</a> <a href="/wiki/Bishop" title="Bishop">bishops</a>, more than two hundred <a href="/wiki/Priest" title="Priest">priests</a>, and prisoners believed to be royalist sympathizers.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1806" title="1806">1806</a> – A massive <a href="/wiki/Landslide" title="Landslide">landslide</a> destroys the town of <a href="/wiki/Goldau" title="Goldau">Goldau</a>, Switzerland, killing 457.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1807" title="1807">1807</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Copenhagen_(1807)" title="Battle of Copenhagen (1807)">bombards Copenhagen</a> with fire bombs and phosphorus rockets to prevent Denmark from surrendering its fleet to <a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1811" title="1811">1811</a> – The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oslo" title="University of Oslo">University of Oslo</a> is founded as The Royal Fredericks University, after <a href="/wiki/Frederick_VI_of_Denmark" title="Frederick VI of Denmark">Frederick VI of Denmark</a> and Norway.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1833" title="1833">1833</a> – <a href="/wiki/Oberlin_College" title="Oberlin College">Oberlin College</a> is founded by <a href="/wiki/John_Jay_Shipherd" title="John Jay Shipherd">John Jay Shipherd</a> and Philo P. Stewart in <a href="/wiki/Oberlin,_Ohio" title="Oberlin, Ohio">Oberlin, Ohio</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1856" title="1856">1856</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Tianjing_incident" title="Tianjing incident">Tianjing incident</a> takes place in <a href="/wiki/Nanjing" title="Nanjing">Nanjing</a>, China.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1862" title="1862">1862</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>: United States President <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a> reluctantly restores <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">Union</a> <a href="/wiki/General_officer" title="General officer">General</a> <a href="/wiki/George_B._McClellan" title="George B. McClellan">George B. McClellan</a> to full command after General <a href="/wiki/John_Pope_(military_officer)" title="John Pope (military officer)">John Pope</a>'s disastrous defeat at the <a href="/wiki/Second_Battle_of_Bull_Run" title="Second Battle of Bull Run">Second Battle of Bull Run</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1864" title="1864">1864</a> – American Civil War: Union forces enter <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, a day after the <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America" title="Confederate States of America">Confederate</a> defenders flee the city, ending the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Campaign" title="Atlanta Campaign">Atlanta Campaign</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1867" title="1867">1867</a> – Mutsuhito, <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Meiji" title="Emperor Meiji">Emperor Meiji</a> of Japan, marries Masako Ichijō. The <a href="/wiki/Queen_consort" title="Queen consort">Empress consort</a> is thereafter known as <i>Lady Haruko</i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1870" title="1870">1870</a> – <a href="/wiki/Franco-Prussian_War" title="Franco-Prussian War">Franco-Prussian War</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Sedan_(1870)" title="Battle of Sedan (1870)">Battle of Sedan</a>: <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussian</a> forces take <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_III_of_France" title="Napoleon III of France">Napoleon III of France</a> and 100,000 of his soldiers prisoner.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1885" title="1885">1885</a> – <a href="/wiki/Rock_Springs_massacre" title="Rock Springs massacre">Rock Springs massacre</a>: In <a href="/wiki/Rock_Springs,_Wyoming" title="Rock Springs, Wyoming">Rock Springs, Wyoming</a>, 150 white miners, who are struggling to unionize so they could strike for better wages and work conditions, attack their <a href="/wiki/Overseas_Chinese" title="Overseas Chinese">Chinese</a> fellow workers killing 28, wounding 15 and forcing several hundred more out of town.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1898" title="1898">1898</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Omdurman" title="Battle of Omdurman">Battle of Omdurman</a>: British and Egyptian troops defeat <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudanese</a> tribesmen and establish British dominance in Sudan.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/1912" title="1912">1912</a> – <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Rose_Eldred" title="Arthur Rose Eldred">Arthur Rose Eldred</a> is awarded the first <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Scout_(Boy_Scouts_of_America)" title="Eagle Scout (Boy Scouts of America)">Eagle Scout</a> award of the <a href="/wiki/Boy_Scouts_of_America" title="Boy Scouts of America">Boy Scouts of America</a>.</li>
