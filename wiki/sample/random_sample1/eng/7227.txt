<p><b>Zacatecoluca</b> is the capital <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipality</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/La_Paz_Department,_El_Salvador" title="La Paz Department, El Salvador">La Paz</a> <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_El_Salvador" title="Departments of El Salvador">Department</a> of <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a>.</p>
<p>Located in Southern <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a>, in the Rio Lempa valley, at the foot of <a href="/wiki/San_Vicente_(volcano)" title="San Vicente (volcano)">San Vicente</a> (Chinchotepec) Volcano. A commercial center for the surrounding agricultural area, it also trades in cotton goods, baskets, salt, and lumber.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zacatecoluca,_La_Paz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/Earthquake" title="Earthquake">earthquake</a> in 1932 caused severe damage.</p>
<p>Zacatecoluca was the birthplace of Jose Simeon Canas, who fought for and secured the emancipation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slaves" title="Slaves">slaves</a> in <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a> by Spaniards in 1825.</p>
<p>Zacatecoluca houses many traditional Spanish buildings including the Santa Lucia Catholic Church and used to hold the house where <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_Sime%C3%B3n_Ca%C3%B1as&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="José Simeón Cañas (page does not exist)">José Simeón Cañas</a> lived, until poor economy due to the many years of war caused the local government to sell the house to a supermarket branch, the house was destroyed and a poorly marked stone on the left of the supermarket entrance is all that is left of Cañas' housing legacy.</p>
<p>Santa Lucia Catholic Church holds a dear history to the people of Zacatecoluca, as an image of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Virgin_Mary" title="Virgin Mary">Virgin Mary</a> placed on top is said to have stopped an eruption of <a href="/wiki/Lava" title="Lava">lava</a> and ash from completely destroying the city and its local population. On May 11th, 1844, Zacatecoluca received the title of Generous and Loyal City becoming the Departmental Head of La Paz on February 21st, 1852.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zacatecoluca,_La_Paz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The local professional football club is named <a href="/wiki/C.D._Platense_Municipal_Zacatecoluca" title="C.D. Platense Municipal Zacatecoluca">Platense</a> and they are former national league champions. They currently play in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_de_F%C3%BAtbol_Salvadore%C3%B1o" title="Segunda División de Fútbol Salvadoreño">Salvadoran Second Division</a>.</p>
