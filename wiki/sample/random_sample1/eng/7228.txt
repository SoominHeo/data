<p><b>Sacatepéquez</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[sakateˈpekes]</a></span>) is one of the 22 <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_Guatemala" title="Departments of Guatemala">departments</a> of <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>. Population estimate 265,500 in 2000. The name comes from <a href="/wiki/Sacatep%C3%A9quez" title="Sacatepéquez">Sacatepéquez</a>, a city from November 21, 1542 until July 29, 1773 when it was destroyed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santa_Marta_earthquake" title="Santa Marta earthquake">Santa Marta earthquake</a>. Sacatepéquez means <i>grasshill</i> in the Pipil Maya dialect. The capital of Sacatepéquez is <a href="/wiki/Antigua_Guatemala" title="Antigua Guatemala">Antigua Guatemala</a>. Other important cities include <a href="/wiki/Ciudad_Vieja" title="Ciudad Vieja">Ciudad Vieja</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sacatep%C3%A9quez_Department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sacatepéquez has also been called <b>Zacatepeques</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baily1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Baily1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sacatep%C3%A9quez_Department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 1850, the area had a population of an estimated 56,000. The southern area has the largest population, which is closer to Guatemala City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baily2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Baily2-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sacatep%C3%A9quez_Department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area is <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountainous</a>, with what the <a href="/wiki/British_people" title="British people">British</a> described, in 1850, as a "mild climate."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baily1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Baily1-3">[3]</a></sup> Rivers include: Guacalate, Los Encuentros, Las Cañas, Pensativo y Sumpango.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sacatep%C3%A9quez_Department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The more populated areas produce <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">fruit</a> and various crops, including <a href="/wiki/Maize" title="Maize">maize</a>. <a href="/wiki/Livestock" title="Livestock">Livestock</a> is also raised, with trade going towards Guatemala City. The southern area of the department produces <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sugar" title="Sugar">sugar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">tobacco</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a>. As of 1850, products were being shipped out of <a href="/wiki/Iztapa" title="Iztapa">Iztapa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baily2_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Baily2-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
