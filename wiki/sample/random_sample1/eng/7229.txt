<p><b>Sakura-ku</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">桜区</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sakura-ku</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is one of ten <a href="/wiki/Wards_of_Japan" title="Wards of Japan">wards</a> of the city of <a href="/wiki/Saitama,_Saitama" title="Saitama, Saitama">Saitama</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Prefecture" title="Saitama Prefecture">Saitama Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, and is located in the southwestern part of the city. As of 1 February 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Sakura-ku,_Saitama&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the ward had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 98,029 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 5,260 persons per km². Its total area was 18.64 square kilometres (7.20 sq mi).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sakura-ku,_Saitama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sakura Ward is located in the far southwestern corner of Saitama City on the floodplain of the <a href="/wiki/Arakawa_River_(Kanto)" title="Arakawa River (Kanto)">Arakawa River</a> and the Kamo River.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sakura-ku,_Saitama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of modern Sakura Ward has been inhabited since prehistoric times, and there are many <a href="/wiki/Kofun" title="Kofun">kofun</a> burial mounds in the area. The villages of Okubo and Tsuchiai and Miyamoto were created within <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitaadachi_District,_Saitama" title="Kitaadachi District, Saitama">Kitaadachi District, Saitama</a> with the establishment of the municipalities system on April 1, 1889. On January 1, 1955 these villages were annexed by Urawa City. On May 1, 2001 the cities of Urawa, Yono and Ōmiya merged to form the new city of Saitama. When Saitama was proclaimed a <a href="/wiki/Cities_designated_by_government_ordinance_of_Japan" title="Cities designated by government ordinance of Japan">designated city</a> in 2003, the area corresponding to former Okubo and most of former became Sakura Ward. The name of Sakura-ku means "Cherry-ward" literally, though it refers to <i>Sakura-sō</i> (<a href="/wiki/Primula_japonica" title="Primula japonica">Japanese primrose</a>), as this area has been well known for wild primrose flowers.</p>
