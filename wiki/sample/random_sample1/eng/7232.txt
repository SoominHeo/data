<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Sakurai Prize</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>J. J. Sakurai Prize for Theoretical Particle Physics</b>, is presented by the <a href="/wiki/American_Physical_Society" title="American Physical Society">American Physical Society</a> at its annual "April Meeting", and honors outstanding achievement in <a href="/wiki/Particle_physics#Theory" title="Particle physics">particle physics theory</a>. The prize, considered one of the most prestigious in physics, consists of a monetary award ($10,000 USD), a certificate citing the contributions recognized by the award, and a travel allowance for the recipient to attend the presentation. The award is endowed by the family and friends of particle physicist <a href="/wiki/J._J._Sakurai" title="J. J. Sakurai">J. J. Sakurai</a>. The prize has been awarded annually since 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PrizeCite_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-PrizeCite-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
