<p><b>Sark</b> (French: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Sercq</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/Sercquiais" title="Sercquiais">Sercquiais</a>: <i>Sèr</i> or <i>Cerq</i>) is an island in the <a href="/wiki/Channel_Islands" title="Channel Islands">Channel Islands</a> in the southwestern <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">English Channel</a>, off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Normandy" title="Normandy">Normandy</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. It is a royal <a href="/wiki/Fief" title="Fief">fief</a>, which forms part of the <a href="/wiki/Bailiwick_of_Guernsey" title="Bailiwick of Guernsey">Bailiwick of Guernsey</a>, with its own set of laws based on Norman law and its own parliament. It has a population of about 600. Sark (including the nearby island of <a href="/wiki/Brecqhou" title="Brecqhou">Brecqhou</a>) has an area of 2.10 square miles (5.44 km<sup>2</sup>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sark is one of the few remaining places in the world where <a href="/wiki/List_of_car-free_places" title="List of car-free places">cars are banned from roads</a> and only tractors and horse-drawn vehicles are allowed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2011, Sark was designated as a <a href="/wiki/Dark-sky_preserve" title="Dark-sky preserve">Dark Sky Community</a> and the first Dark Sky Island in the world.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_geology">Geography and geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography and geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:292px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sark_Coupee.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2592" data-file-width="3872" height="194" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Sark_Coupee.jpg/290px-Sark_Coupee.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Sark_Coupee.jpg/435px-Sark_Coupee.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Sark_Coupee.jpg/580px-Sark_Coupee.jpg 2x" width="290"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sark_Coupee.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
La Coupée</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Sark consists of two main parts, Greater Sark, located at about <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Sark&amp;params=49_25_N_2_22_W_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">49°25′N</span> <span class="longitude">2°22′W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">49.417°N 2.367°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">49.417; -2.367</span></span></span></a></span>, and <a href="/wiki/Little_Sark" title="Little Sark">Little Sark</a> to the south. They are connected by a narrow <a href="/wiki/Isthmus" title="Isthmus">isthmus</a> called La Coupée which is 300 feet (91 m) long and has a drop of 330 feet (100 m) on each side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-islandlife.org_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-islandlife.org-3">[3]</a></sup> Protective <a href="/wiki/Guard_rail" title="Guard rail">railings</a> were erected in 1900; before then, children would crawl across on their hands and knees to avoid being blown over the edge. There is a narrow concrete road covering the entirety of the isthmus that was built in 1945 by German <a href="/wiki/Prisoner_of_war" title="Prisoner of war">prisoners of war</a> under the direction of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Engineers" title="Royal Engineers">Royal Engineers</a>. Due to its isolation, the inhabitants of Little Sark had their own distinct form of <a href="/wiki/Sercquiais" title="Sercquiais">Sercquiais</a>, the native <a href="/wiki/Norman_language" title="Norman language">Norman dialect</a> of the island.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sark_windmill_working.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="947" data-file-width="1529" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Sark_windmill_working.jpg/220px-Sark_windmill_working.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Sark_windmill_working.jpg/330px-Sark_windmill_working.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Sark_windmill_working.jpg/440px-Sark_windmill_working.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sark_windmill_working.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
"Le Moulin" windmill, <a href="/wiki/Circa" title="Circa">c.</a> 1905</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The highest point on Sark is 374 feet (114 m) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Above_sea_level" title="Above sea level">above sea level</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-islandlife.org_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-islandlife.org-3">[3]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Windmills_in_the_Channel_Islands" title="Windmills in the Channel Islands">windmill</a>, dated 1571, is found there, the sails of which were removed during World War I. This high point is named <i><a href="/wiki/Le_Moulin" title="Le Moulin">Le Moulin</a></i>, after the windmill. The location is also the highest point in the Bailiwick of Guernsey. Little Sark had a number of mines accessing a source of <a href="/wiki/Galena" title="Galena">galena</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> At Port Gorey, the ruins of silver mines<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> may be seen. Off the south end of Little Sark are the Venus Pool and the Adonis Pool, both natural swimming pools whose waters are refreshed at high tide.</p>
<p>The whole island is extensively penetrated at sea level by natural cave formations that provide unique habitats for many marine creatures, notably sea anemones, some of which are only safely accessible at low tide.</p>
<p>Sark is made up mainly of <a href="/wiki/Amphibolite" title="Amphibolite">amphibolite</a> and <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">granite</a> <a href="/wiki/Gneiss" title="Gneiss">gneiss</a> rocks, intruded by igneous magma sheets called <a href="/wiki/Quartz_diorite" title="Quartz diorite">quartz diorite</a>. Recent (1990–2000)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> geological studies and rock age dating by geologists from <a href="/wiki/Oxford_Brookes_University" title="Oxford Brookes University">Oxford Brookes University</a> shows that the gneisses probably formed around 620–600 million years ago during the Late Pre-Cambrian Age <a href="/wiki/Cadomian_Orogeny" title="Cadomian Orogeny">Cadomian Orogeny</a>. The quartz diorite sheets were intruded during this Cadomian deformation and metamorphic event.</p>
<p>All the Sark rocks (and those of the nearby Channel Islands of <a href="/wiki/Guernsey" title="Guernsey">Guernsey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alderney" title="Alderney">Alderney</a>) formed during geological activity in the continental crust above an ancient <a href="/wiki/Subduction" title="Subduction">subduction</a> zone. This geological setting would have been analogous to the modern-day subduction zone of the Pacific Ocean plate colliding and subducting beneath the North and South American continental plate.</p>
<p>Sark also exercises jurisdiction over the island of <a href="/wiki/Brecqhou" title="Brecqhou">Brecqhou</a>, only a few hundred feet west of Greater Sark. It is a <a href="/wiki/Private_island" title="Private island">private island</a>, but it has recently been opened to some visitors. Since 1993, Brecqhou has been owned by Sir David Barclay, one of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barclay_brothers" title="Barclay brothers">Barclay brothers</a> who are co-owners of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Daily Telegraph</a></i>. They contest Sark's control over the island. However, the candidates endorsed by their various business interests on the Island failed to win any seats in the elections held in 2008<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The etymology of Sark is unknown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> However, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Coates" title="Richard Coates">Richard Coates</a> has suggested that in the absence of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proto-Indo-European" title="Proto-Indo-European">Proto-Indo-European</a> etymology it may be worthwhile looking for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proto-Semitic" title="Proto-Semitic">Proto-Semitic</a> source for the name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> This is because the <a href="/wiki/British_Isles" title="British Isles">British Isles</a> were likely repopulated from the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a> following the last <a href="/wiki/Last_glacial_period" title="Last glacial period">Ice Age</a>. He proposes a comparison between the probable root of Sark, <i>*Sarg-</i>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proto-Semitic" title="Proto-Semitic">Proto-Semitic</a> <i>*śrq</i> "redden; rise (as of the sun); east", noting Sark's position as the easternmost island of the <a href="/wiki/Guernsey" title="Guernsey">Guernsey</a> group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Early history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In ancient times, Sark was almost certainly occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Veneti_(Gaul)" title="Veneti (Gaul)">Veneti</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute#Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (April 2015)">dubious</span></a> <span class="metadata">– <a href="/wiki/Talk:Sark#Dubious" title="Talk:Sark">discuss</a></span></i>]</sup> These people were subdued by the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a> about 56 BC and the island annexed. After the Roman retreat during the fifth century AD, Sark was probably an outpost of one or other <a href="/wiki/Breton_language" title="Breton language">Breton-speaking</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute#Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (April 2015)">dubious</span></a> <span class="metadata">– <a href="/wiki/Talk:Sark#Dubious" title="Talk:Sark">discuss</a></span></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/History_of_Brittany#Middle_Ages" title="History of Brittany">kingdoms</a> until 933, when it became part of the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Normandy" title="Duchy of Normandy">Duchy of Normandy</a>. Following the <a href="/wiki/Norman_conquest_of_England" title="Norman conquest of England">Norman conquest of England</a> in 1066, the island was united with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crown_of_England" title="Crown of England">Crown of England</a>. In the thirteenth century, the French pirate <a href="/wiki/Eustace_the_Monk" title="Eustace the Monk">Eustace the Monk</a>, having served <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_of_England" title="John of England">King John</a>, used Sark as a base of operations.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>, the island was populated by <a href="/wiki/Monasticism" title="Monasticism">monastic</a> communities. By the 16th century, however, the island was uninhabited and used by pirates as a refuge and base. In 1565, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Helier_de_Carteret" title="Helier de Carteret">Helier de Carteret</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seigneur" title="Seigneur">Seigneur</a> of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Ouen,_Jersey" title="Saint Ouen, Jersey">St. Ouen</a> in <a href="/wiki/Jersey" title="Jersey">Jersey</a>, received <a href="/wiki/Letters_patent" title="Letters patent">letters patent</a> from <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_I_of_England" title="Elizabeth I of England">Queen Elizabeth I</a> granting him Sark as a <a href="/wiki/Fief" title="Fief">fief</a> in perpetuity on condition that he kept the island free of pirates and occupied by at least forty men who were of her <a href="/wiki/Commoner" title="Commoner">English subjects</a> or swore allegiance to the Crown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> This he duly did, leasing 40 parcels of land (known as "Tenements") at a low rent to forty families, mostly from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Ouen" title="St. Ouen">St. Ouen</a>, on condition that a house was built and maintained on each parcel and that "the Tenant" provided one man, armed with a musket, for the defence of the island. The 40 tenements survive to this day, albeit, with minor boundary changes. A subsequent attempt by the families to endow a <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a> under a <a href="/wiki/Bailiff_(Channel_Islands)" title="Bailiff (Channel Islands)">bailiff</a>, as in Jersey, was stopped by the Guernsey authorities who resented any attempt to wrest Sark from their bailiwick.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_history">Recent history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Recent history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Sark_during_the_German_occupation_of_the_Channel_Islands" title="Sark during the German occupation of the Channel Islands">Sark during the German occupation of the Channel Islands</a></div>
<p>In 1844, desperate for funds to continue the operation of the silver mine on the island, the incumbent Seigneur, <a href="/wiki/Ernest_le_Pelley" title="Ernest le Pelley">Ernest le Pelley</a>, obtained <a href="/wiki/The_Crown#Crown_dependencies" title="The Crown">Crown permission</a> to mortgage Sark's fief to local <a href="/wiki/Privateer" title="Privateer">privateer</a> John Allaire. After the company running the mine went bankrupt, le Pelley was unable to keep up the mortgage payments and, in 1849, his son <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Carey_le_Pelley" title="Pierre Carey le Pelley">Pierre Carey le Pelley</a>, the new Seigneur, was forced to sell the fief to <a href="/wiki/Marie_Collings" title="Marie Collings">Marie Collings</a> for a total of £1,383<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marr_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Marr-13">[13]</a></sup> (£6,000 less the sum borrowed and an accumulated interest of £616.13s).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ewen_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ewen-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the island, along with the other Channel Islands, was <a href="/wiki/German_occupation_of_the_Channel_Islands" title="German occupation of the Channel Islands">occupied by German forces</a> between 1940 and 1945. <a href="/wiki/Sark_during_the_German_occupation_of_the_Channel_Islands" title="Sark during the German occupation of the Channel Islands">German military rule on Sark</a> began on 4 July 1940, the day after the Guernsey <i><a href="/wiki/Commandant" title="Commandant">Kommandant</a></i> Major Albrecht Lanz and his interpreter and chief of staff Major Maas visited the island to inform the Dame and Seigneur (<a href="/wiki/Sibyl_Hathaway" title="Sibyl Hathaway">Sibyl</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Hathaway" title="Robert Hathaway">Robert Hathaway</a>) of the new regime. <a href="/wiki/British_Commandos" title="British Commandos">British Commandos</a> raided the island several times, <a href="/wiki/Operation_Basalt" title="Operation Basalt">Operation Basalt</a> during the night of 3–4 October 1942, captured a prisoner and <a href="/wiki/Operation_Hardtack_(commando_raid)" title="Operation Hardtack (commando raid)">Hardtack 7</a>, was a failed British landing in December 1943. Sark was finally liberated on 10 May 1945, a full day after Guernsey.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Invasion_attempt">Invasion attempt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Invasion attempt">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In August 1990, an unemployed French <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuclear_physicist" title="Nuclear physicist">nuclear physicist</a> named André Gardes armed with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Semi-automatic_weapon" title="Semi-automatic weapon">semi-automatic weapon</a> attempted an invasion of Sark. The night Gardes arrived, he put up two posters declaring his intention to take over the island the following day at noon. The following day he started a solo foot patrol in front of the manor in battle-dress with weapon in hand. While Gardes was sitting on a bench waiting for noon to arrive, the island's volunteer <a href="/wiki/Constable" title="Constable"><i>connétable</i></a> approached the Frenchman and complimented him on the quality of his weapon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Liberation_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Liberation-15">[15]</a></sup> Gardes then proceeded to change the gun's magazine, at which point he was tackled to the ground, arrested, and given a seven-day sentence which he served in Guernsey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Liberation_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Liberation-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Caesar_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Caesar-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Gardes attempted a comeback the following year, but was intercepted in Guernsey.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Transition_to_new_system_of_government">Transition to new system of government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Transition to new system of government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sark_general_election,_2008" title="Sark general election, 2008">Sark general election, 2008</a></div>
<p>Until 2008, Sark's parliament (Chief Pleas) was a single chamber consisting of 54 members, comprising the Seigneur, the Seneschal, 40 owners of the Tenements and 12 elected deputies. A change to the system was advocated largely by the <a href="/wiki/David_and_Frederick_Barclay#Sark_politics" title="David and Frederick Barclay">Barclay brothers</a>, who had purchased an island within Sark's territorial waters in 1993<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Liberation_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-Liberation-15">[15]</a></sup> along with the hotels on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_Harrell_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Time_Harrell-19">[19]</a></sup> Their premise was that a change was necessary to comply with the <a href="/wiki/European_Convention_on_Human_Rights" title="European Convention on Human Rights">European Convention on Human Rights</a> though it was suggested that their objection was more likely at odds with certain property tax requirements and primogeniture laws affecting their holdings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_Harrell_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-Time_Harrell-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The old system was described as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feudal" title="Feudal">feudal</a> and undemocratic because the Tenants were entitled to sit in Chief Pleas as of right.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 16 January 2008 and 21 February 2008, the Chief Pleas approved a law to reform Chief Pleas as a 30-member chamber, with 28 members elected in island-wide elections, one hereditary member (the Seigneur) and one member (the Seneschal) appointed for life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Privy_Council_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Privy Council of the United Kingdom">Privy Council of the United Kingdom</a> approved the Sark law reforms on 9 April 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The first elections under the new law were held in December 2008 and the new chamber first convened in January 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some Sark residents have complained that the new system is not democratic and have described the powers the new law granted to the Seneschal, an unelected member whose term the new law extended to the duration of his natural life, as imperial or dictatorial. The Court of Appeal has indeed ruled his powers to be in breach of the European Convention on Human Rights and his powers are subject to further legal challenges on these grounds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012 the BBC <i><a href="/wiki/Today_(BBC_Radio_4)" title="Today (BBC Radio 4)">Today</a></i> programme reported on local disquiet about the influence on the island of <a href="/wiki/David_and_Frederick_Barclay" title="David and Frederick Barclay">David and Frederick Barclay</a>, the billionaire brothers who own <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Daily Telegraph</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Yorker" title="The New Yorker">The New Yorker</a></i> magazine further illustrated the ongoing and escalating tensions between the Barclays and some of the longer-term residents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In 2017 <i><a href="/wiki/Private_Eye" title="Private Eye">Private Eye</a></i> also reported on the situation, following the Barclays' decision to close their vineyard and a number of hotels and shops they own on Sark.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Dark_Sky_Community_status">Dark Sky Community status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Dark Sky Community status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In January 2011, the <a href="/wiki/International_Dark-Sky_Association" title="International Dark-Sky Association">International Dark-Sky Association</a> designated Sark as Europe's first Dark Sky Community<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IDA_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-IDA-30">[30]</a></sup> and the first Dark Sky Island in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> This designation recognises that Sark is sufficiently clear of <a href="/wiki/Light_pollution" title="Light pollution">light pollution</a> to allow naked-eye <a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">astronomy</a>. Although Sark was aided in its achievement by its location, its historic ban on cars and the fact that there is no public lighting, it was also necessary for local residents to make adjustments, such as re-siting lights, to cut the light pollution.</p>
<p>Following an audit in 2010 by the IDA the designation was made in January 2011. The award is significant in that Sark is the first island community to have achieved this; other Dark-Sky Places have, up to now, been mainly uninhabited areas, and IDA chairman Martin Morgan-Taylor commended Sark residents for their effort.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> After the designation in 2011, Sark Astronomy Society worked to secure funds for an astronomical observatory on the island. In October 2015 Sark's observatory was officially opened by Dr Marek Kukula, public astronomer from the Royal Observatory Greenwich.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sark was considered the last <a href="/wiki/Feudalism" title="Feudalism">feudal state</a> in Europe. Together with the other <a href="/wiki/Channel_Islands" title="Channel Islands">Channel Islands</a>, it is the last remnant of the former <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Normandy" title="Duchy of Normandy">Duchy of Normandy</a> still belonging to <a href="/wiki/The_Crown" title="The Crown">the Crown</a>. Sark belongs to the Crown in its own right and has an independent relationship with the Crown through the Lieutenant Governor in Guernsey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hansard_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-hansard-34">[34]</a></sup> Formally, the Seigneur holds it as a <a href="/wiki/Fief" title="Fief">fief</a> from the Crown, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enfeoffment" title="Enfeoffment">reenfeoffing</a> the landowners on the island with their respective parcels. The political consequences of this construction were abolished in recent years, particularly in the reform of the legislative body, Chief Pleas, which took place in 2008.</p>
<p>Although geographically located within the <a href="/wiki/Guernsey" title="Guernsey">Bailiwick of Guernsey</a>, Sark is fiscally entirely separate from it and has been granted its own UN country code (680) to assist in identifying this fact to the world at large. Together with the islands of Alderney and Guernsey, Sark from time to time approves Bailiwick of Guernsey legislation, which, subject to the approval of all three legislatures, applies in the entire Bailiwick.</p>
<p>Legislation cannot be made which applies on Sark without the approval of the Chief Pleas, although recently Chief Pleas has been delegating a number of Ordinance making powers to the States of Guernsey. Such powers are, however, in each case subject to dis-application, or repeal, by the Chief Pleas. By long standing custom, Sark's criminal law has been made by the States of Guernsey, and this custom was put on a statutory basis in Section 4 of the Reform (Sark) Law, 2008, by which Sark delegates criminal law making power to the States of Guernsey.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Seigneur">Seigneur</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Seigneur">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Seigneurs_of_Sark" title="List of Seigneurs of Sark">List of Seigneurs of Sark</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Christopher_Beaumont,_23rd_Seigneur_of_Sark" title="Christopher Beaumont, 23rd Seigneur of Sark">Christopher Beaumont</a> is the current and twenty-third Seigneur of Sark, inheriting the seigneury in 2016.</p>
<p>The Seigneur of Sark was, before the constitutional reforms of 2008, the head of the feudal government of the Isle of Sark (in the case of a woman, the title was <i>Dame</i>). Many of the laws, particularly those related to inheritance and the rule of the Seigneur, had changed little since they were enacted in 1565 under Queen <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_I_of_England" title="Elizabeth I of England">Elizabeth I</a>. The Seigneur retained the sole right on the island to keep pigeons and was until 2008 the only person allowed to keep an <a href="/wiki/Neutering" title="Neutering">unspayed</a> dog. In 2008, the latter privilege was abolished (on the proposal of political opponents of the Barclay brothers) because it did not comply with the <a href="/wiki/European_Convention_on_Human_Rights" title="European Convention on Human Rights">European Convention on Human Rights</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Caesar_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-Caesar-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sarklaw_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-sarklaw-35">[35]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tenants">Tenants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Tenants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Pursuant to the royal letters patent, the Seigneur was to keep the island inhabited by at least 40 armed men.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Therefore, from his lands, 39 parcels or <a href="/wiki/Tenement_(law)" title="Tenement (law)">tenements</a>, each sufficient for one family, were subdivided and granted to settlers, the <i>Tenants</i>. Later, some of these parcels were dismembered, and parts of the Seigneurial land were sold, creating more parcels.</p>
<p>Originally each head of a parcel-holding family had the right to vote in Chief Pleas, but in 1604 this right was restricted to the 39 original tenements required by the Letters Patent, the so-called <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">Quarantaine</span> Tenements (<i>quarantaine</i>: French for a group of forty). The newer parcels mostly did not have the obligation to bear arms. In 1611 the dismemberment of tenements was forbidden, but the order was not immediately followed.</p>
<p>In Sark, the word <i>tenant</i> is used (and often pronounced as in French) in the sense of <i>feudal landholder</i> rather than the common English meaning of <i>lessee</i>. Originally, the word referred to any landowner, but today it is mostly used for a holder of one of the <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">Quarantaine</span> Tenements.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chief_Pleas">Chief Pleas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Chief Pleas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sark_July_2011_50.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3340" data-file-width="2639" height="278" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Sark_July_2011_50.jpg/220px-Sark_July_2011_50.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Sark_July_2011_50.jpg/330px-Sark_July_2011_50.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Sark_July_2011_50.jpg/440px-Sark_July_2011_50.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sark_July_2011_50.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Meeting place for Chief Pleas and the Court of the Seneschal</div>

<p>Chief Pleas (French: <i>Chefs Plaids</i>; Sercquiais: <i>Cheurs Pliaids</i>) is the parliament of Sark. Until 2008, it consisted of the tenants, and 12 deputies of the people as the only representation of the majority, an office introduced in 1922. The Seigneur and the <a href="/wiki/Seneschal" title="Seneschal">Seneschal</a> (who presides) are also members of Chief Pleas. The <a href="/wiki/Pr%C3%A9v%C3%B4t" title="Prévôt">Prévôt</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Greffier" title="Greffier">Greffier</a>, and the Treasurer also attend but are not members; the Treasurer may address Chief Pleas on matters of taxation and finance.</p>
<p>The executive officers on the island are:</p>
<ul>
<li>The <b>Seneschal</b> (President of Chief Pleas and Chief Judge) and Deputy</li>
<li>The <b>Prevôt</b> (Sheriff of the Court and of Chief Pleas) and Deputy</li>
<li>The <b>Greffier</b> (Clerk) and Deputy</li>
<li>The <b>Treasurer</b> (Finances)</li>
<li>The <b>Connétable</b> (or <a href="/wiki/Constable" title="Constable">Constable</a>) is the senior of two police officers and police administrator and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vingtenier" title="Vingtenier">Vingtenier</a> is the junior police officer.</li>
</ul>
<p>The Seneschal, Prevôt, Greffier and Treasurer are chosen by the Seigneur, while the Constable and Vingtenier are elected by Chief Pleas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since 2000, Chief Pleas has been working on its own reform, responding to internal and international pressures. On 8 March 2006 by a vote of 25–15 Chief Pleas voted for a new legislature of the Seigneur, the Seneschal, 14 elected landowners and 14 elected non-landowners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> But it was made plain<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Cleanup" title="Wikipedia:Cleanup"><span title="Wikipedia:Cleanup">by whom?</span></a></i>]</sup> that this option was not on the table. Offered two options for reform involving an elected legislature, one fully elected, one with a number of seats reserved for elected Tenants, 56% of the inhabitants expressed a preference in a totally elected legislature.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sark_Website_Opinion_Polls_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sark_Website_Opinion_Polls-42">[42]</a></sup> Following the poll, Chief Pleas voted on 4 October 2006 to replace the 12 Deputies and 40 Tenants in Chief Pleas by 28 <i>Conseillers</i> elected by universal adult suffrage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> This decision was suspended in January 2007 when it was pointed out to Chief Pleas that the 56% versus 44% majority achieved in the opinion poll did not achieve the 60% majority required for the constitutional change. The decision was replaced by the proposal that Chief Pleas should consist of 16 Tenants and 12 Conseillers both elected by universal adult suffrage from 2008 to 2012 and that a binding referendum should then decide whether this composition should be kept or replaced by 28 Conseillers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> This proposal was rejected by the Privy Council and the 28 Conseiller option was reinstated in February 2008 and accepted by Privy Council in April 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2003, Chief Pleas voted to vary the long-standing <a href="/wiki/Ban_(law)" title="Ban (law)">ban</a> on divorce in the island by extending to the Royal Court of Guernsey power to grant divorces.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bailiwick of Guernsey Laws and United Kingdom Acts of Parliament can (the latter as also in the case of all the other Channel Islands) be extended to Sark with the consent of Chief Pleas. In practice, Sark does not make its own criminal laws; the responsibility for making criminal law is formally delegated to the States of Guernsey by Section 4(3) of The Reform (Sark) Law, 2008.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Clameur_de_Haro">Clameur de Haro</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Clameur de Haro">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Among the old laws of the Channel Islands is the old <a href="/wiki/Normans" title="Normans">Norman</a> custom of the <a href="/wiki/Clameur_de_haro" title="Clameur de haro">Clameur de haro</a>. Using this legal device, a person can obtain immediate cessation of any action he considers to be an infringement of his rights. At the scene, he must, in front of witnesses, recite the <a href="/wiki/Lord%27s_Prayer" title="Lord's Prayer">Lord's Prayer</a> in French and cry out <i>"Haro, Haro, Haro! À mon aide mon Prince, on me fait tort!"</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sarklaw_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-sarklaw-35">[35]</a></sup> (<i>"Haro, Haro, Haro! To my aid, my Prince! I am being wronged!"</i>). It should then be registered with the Greffe Office within 24 hours. All actions against the person must then cease until the matter is heard by the Court. The last <i>Clameur</i> recorded on Sark was raised in June 1970 to prevent the construction of a garden wall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Caesar_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-Caesar-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Periodicals">Periodicals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Periodicals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A local resident on Sark has set up an online newspaper called <i>The Sark Newspaper</i>, which voices his concerns about the way the island is governed and the impact of its policies, although contributions or comments from others are not published.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> A group of islanders has set up a quarterly magazine called <i>Sark Life</i>, which promotes a positive view of the island and welcomes contributions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sercquiais">Sercquiais</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Sercquiais">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sercquiais" title="Sercquiais">Sercquiais</a></div>
<p>Sercquiais (<i>Sarkese</i>, or sometimes called <i>Sark-French</i>) is a dialect of the <a href="/wiki/Norman_language" title="Norman language">Norman language</a> still spoken by older inhabitants of the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Its use has declined in recent years due to a large influx of people who have moved to Sark.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sark's economy depends primarily on tourism and financial services. Sark has no company registry and relies on <a href="/wiki/Guernsey_Financial_Services_Commission" title="Guernsey Financial Services Commission">Guernsey's financial services commission</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EconomicPolicy2013_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-EconomicPolicy2013-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxation">Taxation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Taxation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sark is fiscally autonomous from <a href="/wiki/Guernsey" title="Guernsey">Guernsey</a>, and consequently has control over how it raises taxes. There are no taxes on income, capital gains or inheritances. There is also no <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VAT" title="VAT">VAT</a> charged on goods and services, but import duties (Impôts) are charged on some goods brought onto the island at around 70–75% of Guernsey rates. However, the island does levy a Personal Capital Tax, a Property Tax, a Poll Tax ("Landing Tax") on visitors coming to the island, and a Property Transfer Tax (PTT) on residential properties when they are sold.</p>
<p>The island has its own tax assessor (in 2016, this remained Simon de Carteret),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> who collects the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Property_Tax" title="Property Tax">Property Tax</a>, PTT, and the Personal Capital Tax (direct tax).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> Currently, the Personal Capital Tax ranges from a minimum of £300, to a maximum of £6,400 or 0,3% per annum (whichever is the lower). In 2014, there were 5 taxpayers who paid the maximum amount of £6,400 (PCT and Property Tax combined), and 6 who paid zero tax. Residents over the age of 69 do not pay the PCT. If a resident chooses not to declare the value of their personal assets, they can elect to pay a flat-rate under the <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Forfait" title="Forfait">Forfait</a><sup class="plainlinks noprint Inline-Template" style="vertical-align:text-top;white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="external text" href="http://dispenser.homenet.org/~dispenser/cgi-bin/dab_solver.py?page=Sark&amp;editintro=Template:Disambiguation_needed/editintro&amp;client=Template:Dn" rel="nofollow"><span title="Link needs disambiguation. (December 2016)">disambiguation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> method.</p>
<p>In 2006, Property Transfer Tax replaced the feudal <i>Treizième</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> This used to be calculated by dividing the purchase price of any of the 30 tenements or 40 freehold properties on Sark by 13. The proceeds from doing this were then paid directly to the <a href="/wiki/Seigneur" title="Seigneur">Seigneur</a>. When the Treizième was abolished, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chief_Pleas" title="Chief Pleas">Chief Pleas</a> introduced an indexed-linked pension of £28,000 per year, payable to the Seigneur.</p>
<p>An individual is considered to be a resident for tax purposes if they have remained on the island for at least 91 days in any tax year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sark generally follows the <a href="/wiki/Education_in_England" title="Education in England">education system of England</a> though this is not strictly adhered to.</p>
<p>Sark has one school, the Sark School, which takes residents from the ages of 4 to 15. School is divided into 4 classes. Class 1 takes children from the ages of 4 to 7 (reception to year 2), class 2 caters for 7- to 10-year-olds (year 3 to year 5), class 3 has 10- to 14-year-olds (year 6 to year 9) and the older children attend class 4 (years 10 and 11).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> Pupils wishing to obtain a <a href="/wiki/General_Certificate_of_Secondary_Education" title="General Certificate of Secondary Education">GCSE</a> or A-level qualification often finish their education in Guernsey or in England. Since 2006, however, a limited number of GCSEs have been offered to pupils at Sark School.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sark_ferry_arrival.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2193" data-file-width="3222" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Sark_ferry_arrival.jpg/220px-Sark_ferry_arrival.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Sark_ferry_arrival.jpg/330px-Sark_ferry_arrival.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Sark_ferry_arrival.jpg/440px-Sark_ferry_arrival.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sark_ferry_arrival.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The high-speed ferry service from Jersey arriving at Sark</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Isle of Sark Shipping Company operates small ferries from Sark to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Peter_Port" title="St Peter Port">St Peter Port</a>, Guernsey. The service takes 45 minutes for the 9 miles (14 km) crossing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> A high-speed passenger ferry is operated in summer by the French company Manche Iles Express to <a href="/wiki/Jersey" title="Jersey">Jersey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> A 12-passenger boat, the <i>Lady Maris II</i>, operates regular services to <a href="/wiki/Alderney" title="Alderney">Alderney</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup></p>
<p>The island is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Car-free_zone" title="Car-free zone">car-free zone</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> where the only vehicles allowed are <a href="/wiki/Carriage" title="Carriage">horse-drawn vehicles</a>, bicycles, <a href="/wiki/Tractor" title="Tractor">tractors</a>, and battery-powered buggies or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motorised_bicycle" title="Motorised bicycle">motorised bicycles</a> for elderly or disabled people. Passengers and goods arriving by ferry from Guernsey are transported from the wharf by tractor-pulled vehicles. To the dismay of residents, large tractors, which produce even more noise and dust than cars of the same size, have proliferated in recent years.</p>
<p>There is no airport on Sark, and flight over Sark below 2400 ft is prohibited by the <i>Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Guernsey) Regulations 1985</i> (Guernsey 1985/21). The closest airports are <a href="/wiki/Guernsey_Airport" title="Guernsey Airport">Guernsey Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jersey_Airport" title="Jersey Airport">Jersey Airport</a>. Sark lies directly in line of approach to the runway of Guernsey airport, however, and low-flying<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> aircraft regularly fly over the island.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Church_-_Sark.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2262" data-file-width="1782" height="279" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Church_-_Sark.jpg/220px-Church_-_Sark.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Church_-_Sark.jpg/330px-Church_-_Sark.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Church_-_Sark.jpg/440px-Church_-_Sark.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Church_-_Sark.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Peter" title="St. Peter">St. Peter</a>'s Church (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglican" title="Anglican">Anglican</a>)</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In common with the other Channel Islands, Sark is attached to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglican" title="Anglican">Anglican</a> <a href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Winchester" title="Diocese of Winchester">diocese of Winchester</a>.</p>
<p>Sark has an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglican" title="Anglican">Anglican</a> church (St. Peter's, built 1820) and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Methodist" title="Methodist">Methodist</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> church. <a href="/wiki/John_Wesley" title="John Wesley">John Wesley</a> first proposed a mission to Sark in 1787. Jean de Quetteville of Jersey subsequently began preaching there, initially in a cottage at Le Clos à Geon and then at various houses around Sark. Preachers from Guernsey visited regularly, and in 1796, land was donated by Jean Vaudin, leader of the Methodist community in Sark, for the construction of a chapel, which Jean de Quetteville dedicated in 1797.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> In the mid-1800s there was a small <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Brethren" title="Plymouth Brethren">Plymouth Brethren</a> assembly. Its most notable member was the classicist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Kelly_(Guernsey_and_Blackheath)" title="William Kelly (Guernsey and Blackheath)">William Kelly</a> (1821–1906). Kelly was then the tutor to the Seigneur's children.</p>
<p>Supported by the evidence of the names of the <i>tenements</i> of La Moinerie and La Moinerie de Haut, it is believed<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> that the Seigneurie was constructed on the site of the <a href="/wiki/Monastery" title="Monastery">monastery</a> of Saint <a href="/wiki/Magloire" title="Magloire">Magloire</a>. Magloire had been <a href="/wiki/Samson_of_Dol" title="Samson of Dol">Samson of Dol</a>'s successor as bishop of <a href="/wiki/Dol-de-Bretagne" title="Dol-de-Bretagne">Dol</a>, but retired and founded a monastery in Sark where he died in the late sixth century. According to the <i>vita</i> of Magloire, the monastery housed 62 <a href="/wiki/Monk" title="Monk">monks</a> and a school for the instruction of the sons of noble families from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cotentin" title="Cotentin">Cotentin</a>. Magloire's <a href="/wiki/Relic" title="Relic">relics</a> were venerated at the monastery until the mid-ninth century when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viking" title="Viking">Viking</a> raids rendered Sark unsafe, and the monks departed for Jersey, taking the relics with them.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Law_enforcement">Law enforcement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Law enforcement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Despite having its own legislative assembly, Sark voluntarily submits to Guernsey in matters of criminal law. For matters of routine law enforcement and policing the island relies upon the <a href="/wiki/States_of_Guernsey_Police_Service" title="States of Guernsey Police Service">States of Guernsey Police Service</a>. Sark has a small police station and jail, with two (rarely used) cells available.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Caesar_16-3"><a href="#cite_note-Caesar-16">[16]</a></sup> The island has no full-time police officers permanently stationed on it, but has access to police services in three principal ways: firstly through the activity of a volunteer <a href="/wiki/Special_constable" title="Special constable">special constable</a> on the island (there has been a resident volunteer constable since before the formal policing agreement with Guernsey first began); secondly through the designation of a member of the Guernsey Neighbourhood Policing Team as a dedicated point of contact for Sark authorities;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> thirdly by means of regular visits and patrols by Guernsey-based officers who cross to Sark on the passenger ferry service.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Emergency_services">Emergency services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Emergency services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sark_Ambulance.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="715" data-file-width="1024" height="154" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Sark_Ambulance.jpg/220px-Sark_Ambulance.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Sark_Ambulance.jpg/330px-Sark_Ambulance.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Sark_Ambulance.jpg/440px-Sark_Ambulance.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>A resident doctor provides healthcare on Sark, and is available to attend accidents and emergencies. The <b>Sark Ambulance Service</b> operates two tractor-drawn ambulances,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> and is able to treat casualties and transport them to the harbour for transfer onto the Guernsey marine ambulance launch, <i>Flying Christine III</i>, operated by <a href="/wiki/Guernsey_Ambulance_and_Rescue_Service" title="Guernsey Ambulance and Rescue Service">Guernsey Ambulance and Rescue Service</a>. A small ambulance station houses the two ambulances.</p>
<p>Fire and rescue services are provided by an independent and volunteer service established in 1958. Originally named 'Sark Fire Brigade', it is now known as the <b>Sark Fire and Rescue Service</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> The services operates two pump tenders and an all-purpose trailer; all three appliances are drawn by tractors owing to the ban on other motor vehicles on Sark. The original fire station was a large garage. Today the service operates from a large purpose-built fire station on La Chasse Marette.</p>
<p>Lifeboat services are provided by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Lifeboat_Institution" title="Royal National Lifeboat Institution">Royal National Lifeboat Institution</a> from the Guernsey lifeboat station, supported by the RNLI stations on Jersey and Alderney.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union_in_the_Bailiwick_of_Guernsey" title="Rugby union in the Bailiwick of Guernsey">Rugby union in the Bailiwick of Guernsey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sark_football_team" title="Sark football team">Sark football team</a></div>
<p>Participation in sport tends towards individual sports rather than team sports, but the population supports a cricket team, a rugby union team and a <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> Sark competes in the biannual <a href="/wiki/International_Island_Games_Association" title="International Island Games Association">Island Games</a> in which the <a href="/wiki/Sark_football_team" title="Sark football team">Sark football team</a> has participated. The annual Sark to Jersey Rowing Race is contested by teams from both bailiwicks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Carl_Hester" title="Carl Hester">Carl Hester</a>, who was born in Sark, won a gold medal at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Equestrian_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="Equestrian at the 2012 Summer Olympics">Individual and Team Dressage events</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> A Sark <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics_and_Paralympics_gold_post_boxes" title="2012 Summer Olympics and Paralympics gold post boxes">post box was painted gold</a> to celebrate the event.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sark_in_media">Sark in media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Sark in media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are many examples of media taking Sark as an inspiration or setting.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Norman_literature">Norman literature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Norman literature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although there is no record of literature about Sark in Sercquiais, <a href="/wiki/Guern%C3%A9siais" title="Guernésiais">Guernésiais</a> and <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A8rriais_literature" title="Jèrriais literature">Jèrriais literature</a> has included writing about Sark; for example by such authors as <a href="/wiki/Edwin_John_Luce" title="Edwin John Luce">Edwin John Luce</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Alfred_Grut" title="Thomas Alfred Grut">Thomas Grut</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/George_F._Le_Feuvre" title="George F. Le Feuvre">George F. Le Feuvre</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Denys_Corbet" title="Denys Corbet">Denys Corbet</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="English_literature">English literature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: English literature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Algernon_Charles_Swinburne" title="Algernon Charles Swinburne">Algernon Swinburne</a> wrote a poem, <i>In Sark</i>, which appears in the collection <i>A Century of Roundels</i>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Oxenham" title="John Oxenham">John Oxenham</a> wrote <i>Carette of Sark</i> (1907) and his 1910 novel <i>A Maid of the Silver Sea</i> uses the mines of Little Sark as its setting.</li>
<li>The novel <i><a href="/wiki/Mr_Pye" title="Mr Pye">Mr Pye</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Mervyn_Peake" title="Mervyn Peake">Mervyn Peake</a>, best known for the <i><a href="/wiki/Gormenghast_(series)" title="Gormenghast (series)">Gormenghast</a></i> series, is set on Sark. The book has been adapted for radio and television. The TV series, filmed on Sark, starred <a href="/wiki/Derek_Jacobi" title="Derek Jacobi">Derek Jacobi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Judy_Parfitt" title="Judy Parfitt">Judy Parfitt</a>, and featured a number of islanders. Sark may also have been a crucial inspiration for Peake while writing <i>Gormenghast</i> (he lived on the island at some point in his life).</li>
<li><i>Dame of Sark</i>, the memoirs of the 21st Seigneur <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sibyl_Mary_Hathaway" title="Sibyl Mary Hathaway">Sibyl Mary Hathaway</a>, who was present during the German occupation, were made into a play and television drama of the same name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> Dame Sibyl also wrote <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Maid_of_Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Maid of Sark (page does not exist)">Maid of Sark</a></i>, an historical romance published in 1939; set in the sixteenth Century, it incorporates events related to the defence of the island against the Bretons.</li>
<li>The novel <i><a href="/wiki/Appointment_with_Venus" title="Appointment with Venus">Appointment with Venus</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Jerrard_Tickell" title="Jerrard Tickell">Jerrard Tickell</a> is set on the fictional island of Armorel, which is presumed to be based on Sark. The 1951 <a href="/wiki/Appointment_with_Venus_(film)" title="Appointment with Venus (film)">film</a> of the book used Sark as a principal location.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sarah_Caudwell" title="Sarah Caudwell">Sarah Caudwell</a>'s <i>The Sirens Sang of Murder</i> (1989) is partly set in Sark.</li>
<li>The 2016 novel <i>Iron Chamber of Memory</i> by <a href="/wiki/John_C._Wright_(author)" title="John C. Wright (author)">John C. Wright</a> is set on Sark.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="French_literature">French literature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: French literature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Maurice_Leblanc" title="Maurice Leblanc">Maurice Leblanc</a>'s novel <i>L'Île aux Trente Cercueils</i> (translated in English as <i>The Secret of Sarek</i>) features an island called Sarek, off the coast of Brittany, and bears obvious similarities to Sark. In the story, gentleman-thief <a href="/wiki/Ars%C3%A8ne_Lupin" title="Arsène Lupin">Arsène Lupin</a> rescues Véronique d'Hergemont from a local superstition requiring the death of thirty women to appease vengeful spirits.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_music">In music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: In music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Irish musician, composer and singer <a href="/wiki/Enya" title="Enya">Enya</a>'s 2015 album <a href="/wiki/Dark_Sky_Island" title="Dark Sky Island">Dark Sky Island</a> was inspired by Sark's designation as the first 'dark sky island'. Certain songs on the album, the title track especially, explore the stars, skies and nature.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=35" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 1986 television adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Mr_Pye" title="Mr Pye">Mr Pye</a> by <a href="/wiki/Mervyn_Peake" title="Mervyn Peake">Mervyn Peake</a> and starring <a href="/wiki/Derek_Jacobi" title="Derek Jacobi">Derek Jacobi</a> was filmed on the island. The original short story is also set on Sark.</p>
<p>Sark featured in the 6th episode of the fourth series of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Statesman" title="The New Statesman">The New Statesman</a></i>, <i>The Irresistible Rise of Alan B'Stard</i> and also in the Jersey-based television detective series <i><a href="/wiki/Bergerac_(TV_series)" title="Bergerac (TV series)">Bergerac</a></i>.</p>
<p>Part of the seventh episode of the second series of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> television drama <i><a href="/wiki/Enemy_at_the_Door" title="Enemy at the Door">Enemy at the Door</a></i> takes place in Sark. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/La_Coup%C3%A9e" title="La Coupée">La Coupée</a> features in a number of scenes.</p>
<p>Sark, and in particular the Gouliot Caves, features in episode 8 of series 3 of the BBC television series <i><a href="/wiki/Coast_(TV_series)" title="Coast (TV series)">Coast</a></i>.</p>
<p>Sark was featured in Episode 3 of the 2009 <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Islands_of_Britain_(TV_series)" title="Islands of Britain (TV series)">Islands of Britain</a></i>, presented by <a href="/wiki/Martin_Clunes" title="Martin Clunes">Martin Clunes</a>; islanders involved in the programme included Alan Blythe (Constable) and Rossford de Carteret.</p>
<p>Sark was featured on the <a href="/wiki/ITV2" title="ITV2">ITV2</a> programme <i><a href="/wiki/Holiday_Showdown" title="Holiday Showdown">Holiday Showdown</a></i> where one family chose Sark as their holiday destination.</p>
<p>In episode 2 of the 2009 ITV mini-series <i><a href="/wiki/Collision_(TV_series)" title="Collision (TV series)">Collision</a></i>, Guy Pearson (played by <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Farrell" title="Nicholas Farrell">Nicholas Farrell</a>) says, "I'm moving to the <a href="/wiki/Channel_Islands" title="Channel Islands">Channel Islands</a>: Sark. No cars on Sark, it'll be heaven."</p>
<p>In John Shuttleworth's <i>Southern Softies</i> (2009), John and his crew cannot find anywhere to stay on the island.</p>
<p>Series 7 and 8 of the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/An_Island_Parish" title="An Island Parish">An Island Parish</a></i> follows the Anglican priest and Methodist minister on Sark.</p>
