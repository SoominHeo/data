<p><b>Satake Yoshitaka</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">佐竹義隆</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, 18 February 1609 – 4 January 1672)</span> was the 20th head of the <a href="/wiki/Satake_clan" title="Satake clan">Satake clan</a> and 2nd <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daimyo" title="Daimyo">daimyo</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kubota_Domain" title="Kubota Domain">Kubota Domain</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dewa_Province" title="Dewa Province">Dewa Province</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Satake_Yoshitaka_(1609-1672)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yoshitaka was born in 1609 as the eldest son of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Iwaki_Sadataka&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Iwaki Sadataka (page does not exist)">Iwaki Sadataka</a>, the 3rd son of <a href="/wiki/Satake_Yoshishige" title="Satake Yoshishige">Satake Yoshishige</a>. His mother was the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Soma_Yoshitane" title="Soma Yoshitane">Soma Yoshitane</a>.</p>
<p>On the death of his father in 1620, he became daimyo of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shinano-Nakamura_Domain&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shinano-Nakamura Domain (page does not exist)">Shinano-Nakamura Domain</a> (10,000 <i><a href="/wiki/Koku" title="Koku">koku</a></i>), taking the name of Iwaki Yoshitaka. In 1622, his revenues were increased by 10,000 <i>koku</i> in Yuri District of Dewa Province, and in November 1623, he moved his seat from Shinano-Nakamura to <a href="/wiki/Kameda_Domain" title="Kameda Domain">Kameda Village</a> in Yuri District, where he built a <i><a href="/wiki/Jin%27ya" title="Jin'ya">jin'ya</a>.</i> He was awarded lower 5th court rank and the courtesy title of <i>Shuri-Daiyu</i> in December 1624.</p>
<p>He was adopted as heir by his uncle, <a href="/wiki/Satake_Yoshinobu" title="Satake Yoshinobu">Satake Yoshinobu</a> in April 1626, following the disinheritance of Yoshinobu’s son <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Satake_Yoshinao&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Satake Yoshinao (page does not exist)">Satake Yoshinao</a> for incompetence, and was given the name Satake Yoshitaka. On 27 April 1626, he was presented in formal audience to <a href="/wiki/Shogun" title="Shogun">Shogun</a> <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Hidetada" title="Tokugawa Hidetada">Tokugawa Hidetada</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Iemitsu" title="Tokugawa Iemitsu">Tokugawa Iemitsu</a>, and was awarded lower 4th court rank and the courtesy title of <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/chamberlain" title="wikt:chamberlain"><i>Jijū</i></a> (侍従).</p>
<p>Yoshitaka was awarded an estate of 50,000 <i>koku</i> in November 1630 by his adoptive father, who exhibited no signs of intending to retire or relax his control over Kubota Domain. However, on Yoshinobu’s death on 26 February 1633 Yoshitaka finally succeeded to the leadership of the clan. He was permitted to formally enter the domain for the first time on 8 May 1633. On 28 December 1666, his court title was promoted to “General of the Left Imperial Guard” (左近衛少将). He died at <a href="/wiki/Kubota_Castle" title="Kubota Castle">Kubota Castle</a> on 5 December 1671.</p>
<p>His official wife was the daughter of Satake Yoshiaki, and he is known to have had at least two concubines. His eldest son, Yoshioki predeceased him at the age of 22, and Yoshioki’s son became daimyo of Kubota-Shinden domain. Yoshitaka’s second son, <a href="/wiki/Satake_Yoshizumi" title="Satake Yoshizumi">Satake Yoshizumi</a> inherited Kubota.</p>
