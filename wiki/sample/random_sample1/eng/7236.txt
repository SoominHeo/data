<p><i><b>Child's Play 3</b></i> is a 1991 American supernatural <a href="/wiki/Slasher_film" title="Slasher film">slasher film</a>. It is the third installment in the <i><a href="/wiki/Child%27s_Play_(franchise)" title="Child's Play (franchise)">Child's Play</a></i> series, with <a href="/wiki/Brad_Dourif" title="Brad Dourif">Brad Dourif</a> returning as the voice of <a href="/wiki/Chucky_(Child%27s_Play)" title="Chucky (Child's Play)">Chucky</a>. Although released only one year later, the story takes place eight years following the events of 1990's <i><a href="/wiki/Child%27s_Play_2" title="Child's Play 2">Child's Play 2</a></i>. It was executive produced by <a href="/wiki/David_Kirschner" title="David Kirschner">David Kirschner</a>.</p>
<p>The film became notorious in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> when it was suggested it might have inspired the real-life murder of British child <a href="/wiki/Murder_of_James_Bulger" title="Murder of James Bulger">James Bulger</a>, a suggestion rejected by officers investigating the case,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Murder_of_Suzanne_Capper" title="Murder of Suzanne Capper">murder of Suzanne Capper</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The film was followed by <i><a href="/wiki/Bride_of_Chucky" title="Bride of Chucky">Bride of Chucky</a>.</i></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Child%27s_Play_3&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1998, eight years after <a href="/wiki/Child%27s_Play_2" title="Child's Play 2">the previous film</a>, The Play Pals (Good Guys) has recovered from bad publicity brought along by Chucky's (voiced by <a href="/wiki/Brad_Dourif" title="Brad Dourif">Brad Dourif</a>) murder spree. The company releases a new line of Good Guy dolls and recycles Chucky's remains. However, the soul of serial killer Charles Lee Ray still inhabits the remains, and Chucky is soon revived. Chucky is unwittingly given to Play Pals' <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">CEO</a> Mr. Sullivan, whom Chucky kills. He then uses computer records to relocate Andy Barclay (<a href="/wiki/Alex_Vincent_(actor)" title="Alex Vincent (actor)">Alex Vincent</a>).</p>
<p>Still troubled by his past encounters with Chucky, 16-year-old Andy Barclay (<a href="/wiki/Justin_Whalin" title="Justin Whalin">Justin Whalin</a>) has been sent to Kent Military Academy after having failed to cope in several foster homes (due to Andy's mother still being in a mental hospital). Colonel Cochran (<a href="/wiki/Dakin_Matthews" title="Dakin Matthews">Dakin Matthews</a>), the school's <a href="/wiki/Commandant" title="Commandant">commandant</a>, begrudgingly enrolls Andy, but advises him to forget his "fantasies" about the doll. Andy befriends cadets Harold Aubrey Whitehurst (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dean_Jacobson&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dean Jacobson (page does not exist)">Dean Jacobson</a>), Ronald Tyler (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jeremy_Sylvers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jeremy Sylvers (page does not exist)">Jeremy Sylvers</a>), and Kristin DeSilva (<a href="/wiki/Perrey_Reeves" title="Perrey Reeves">Perrey Reeves</a>), for whom he develops romantic feelings. He also meets Brett C. Shelton (<a href="/wiki/Travis_Fine" title="Travis Fine">Travis Fine</a>), a <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_colonel" title="Lieutenant colonel">lieutenant colonel</a> who routinely bullies the cadets, especially Andy.</p>
<p>Shortly after Andy arrives, Tyler is asked to deliver a package to his room. Tyler realizes that the package contains a Good Guy doll and, excited, takes it to the cellar to open it, only to have Chucky burst free from the package. Remembering the rule that he can possess the first person who learns his true nature (and that with a new body, it need not be Andy) he tells Tyler his secret, but just as Chucky is about to possess him, they are interrupted by Cochran who takes the doll away. Cochran throws Chucky into a garbage truck, but Chucky escapes by luring the driver into the truck's <a href="/wiki/Compactor" title="Compactor">compactor</a> and crushing him. That night, Chucky attacks Andy and tells him his plans for taking over Tyler's soul. But before Andy can attack Chucky, Shelton comes in and takes the doll from him. Andy tries to get the doll back by sneaking into Shelton's room, but Shelton catches him in the act. Upon realizing the doll has vanished, Shelton suspects it stolen and forces all the cadets to do exercises in the courtyard as punishment.</p>
<p>Andy unsuccessfully tries to warn Tyler about Chucky. At one point, Chucky lures Tyler into playing <a href="/wiki/Hide-and-seek" title="Hide-and-seek">hide-and-seek</a> in Cochran's office, where he attempts to possess the boy again. However, they are interrupted by De Silva and, moments later, Cochran himself. When the cadets leave, Cochran is suddenly confronted by a knife-wielding Chucky. The resulting shock causes Cochran to suffer a fatal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heart_attack" title="Heart attack">heart attack</a>.</p>
<p>Despite Cochran's death, Sgt. Clark declares that the school's annual <a href="/wiki/Military_simulation" title="Military simulation">war games</a> will proceed as planned, with Andy and Shelton on the same team. However, Chucky secretly replaces the paint bullets of the Red team with live ammunition. When the simulation begins, Chucky accosts Tyler, then threatens the boy when he refuses to take part. Tyler stabs Chucky with a pocket knife and flees, trying to find Andy. Chucky then attacks Kristen and holds her hostage, attempting to lure the teams into fighting each other to save her. Chucky forces Andy to exchange Kristen for Tyler.</p>
<p>Suddenly, the Red team descends upon the area and obliviously opens fire with their live rounds. Shelton is killed in the crossfire. Amidst the chaos, Tyler makes a quick getaway, but before giving chase, Chucky tosses a live grenade at the quarreling cadets. Recognizing the danger, Whitehurst bravely leaps on top of the grenade and sacrifices himself to save the others. With no time to mourn his friend, Andy heads off in pursuit of Chucky, with Kristen close behind.</p>
<p>Eventually the chase leads the group into a fake <a href="/wiki/Haunted_house" title="Haunted house">haunted house</a> at a nearby <a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">carnival</a>. Tyler tries to get a security guard to help him, but Chucky kills the guard offscreen and kidnaps Tyler. In the ensuing melee, Chucky shoots Kristen in the leg, leaving Andy to fight Chucky alone. When Tyler is inadvertently knocked out, Chucky seizes the opportunity to possess him, but Andy intervenes, shooting him several times. Enraged, Chucky attempts to strangle Andy, but Andy uses Tyler's knife to cut off Chucky's hand, dropping him into a giant fan which slices him to pieces. Afterwards, Andy is taken away by the police for questioning, while Kristen is rushed to the nearby hospital. Tyler's fate is left unknown.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Novelization">Novelization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Child%27s_Play_3&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Novelization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A tie-in novel was later written by Matthew J. Costello. Just like <i>Child's Play 2</i>, this novel had some of the author's own parts. In the beginning (unlike the film's), in the Play Pals factory, a rat scours for food and chews on Chucky's remains. Blood then leaks out of the remains and somehow leaks into another doll. Chucky's death in this book is also different. In the novel, Andy shoots Chucky in the chest and causes his body to fall to the floor, and watches his head shatter to blood, metal and plastic.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Child%27s_Play_3&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Child's Play 3</i> opened in second place behind <a href="/wiki/Dead_Again" title="Dead Again">Dead Again</a> to $5.7 million over the 4-day 1991 Labor Day weekend, which the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> called "slow numbers".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It finished its theatrical run with $15 million in the US, and a total of $20.5 million worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Child%27s_Play_3&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">review aggregator</a>, reports that 23% of 13 surveyed critics gave the film a positive review; the average rating is 3.9/10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Chris Hicks of the <i>Deseret News</i> called it "perverse" and criticized the film's plot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Caryn James of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> called the Chucky doll "an impressive technological achievement" but said the film "misses the sharpness and dark humor" of the original film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> called it a "noisy, mindless sequel" with good acting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Richard Harrington of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Washington_Post" title="The Washington Post">The Washington Post</a></i> wrote, "Chucky himself is an animatronic delight, but one suspects the film's energies and budget have all been devoted to what is essentially a one-trick pony."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Stephen Wigle of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Baltimore_Sun" title="The Baltimore Sun">The Baltimore Sun</a></i> called it "fun for any fan of the slasher genre".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Series creator <a href="/wiki/Don_Mancini" title="Don Mancini">Don Mancini</a> said that this was his least favorite entry in the series, adding that he ran out of ideas after the second film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Mancini would not make another entry in the <i>Chucky</i> series until seven years later, with <i><a href="/wiki/Bride_of_Chucky" title="Bride of Chucky">Bride of Chucky</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_video_releases">Home video releases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Child%27s_Play_3&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Home video releases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Child's Play 3</i> was originally released on home video in North America on March 12, 1992,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and on DVD in 2003. <i>Child's Play 3</i> was also released in the <i>Chucky Collection</i> (which also featured <i><a href="/wiki/Child%27s_Play_2" title="Child's Play 2">Child's Play 2</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Bride_of_Chucky" title="Bride of Chucky">Bride of Chucky</a></i>) on October 7, 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On September 19, 2006, it was released as part of <i>Chucky - The Killer DVD Collection</i> (alongside <i>Child's Play 2</i>, <i>Bride of Chucky</i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Seed_of_Chucky" title="Seed of Chucky">Seed of Chucky</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> On October 8, 2013, <i>Child's Play 3</i> was re-released on DVD and Blu-ray as part of <i>Chucky: The Complete Collection</i> (alongside the original <i><a href="/wiki/Child%27s_Play_(1988_film)" title="Child's Play (1988 film)">Child's Play</a></i>, <i>Child's Play 2</i>, <i>Bride of Chucky</i>, <i>Seed of Chucky</i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Curse_of_Chucky" title="Curse of Chucky">Curse of Chucky</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="James_Bulger_controversy">James Bulger controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Child%27s_Play_3&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: James Bulger controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film also had some controversy because it has been indirectly linked to the brutal United Kingdom <a href="/wiki/Murder_of_James_Bulger" title="Murder of James Bulger">murder of James Bulger</a>. The killers, who were 10 years old at the time, were said to have imitated a scene in which one of Chucky's victims is splashed with blue paint. Although these allegations against the film have never been proven, the case has led to some new legislation for video films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Psychologist Guy Cumberbatch has stated, "The link with a video was that the father of one of the boys – Jon Venables – had rented <i>Child's Play 3: Look Who's Stalking</i> some months earlier."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> However, the police officer who directed the investigation, Albert Kirby, found that the son, Jon, was not living with his father at the time and was unlikely to have seen the film. Moreover, the boy disliked horror films—a point later confirmed by psychiatric reports. Thus the police investigation, which had specifically looked for a video link, concluded there was none.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequel">Sequel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Child%27s_Play_3&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Sequel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mancini originally intended for this film to be the final installment in the series, however following the success of <i><a href="/wiki/Scream_(1996_film)" title="Scream (1996 film)">Scream</a></i>, five years later; interest in the slasher subgenre was revived and a sequel was then planned and released seven years after the third film in 1998 with the title "<i><a href="/wiki/Bride_of_Chucky" title="Bride of Chucky">Bride of Chucky</a></i>". Unlike its predecessors, <i>Bride of Chucky</i> was a horror-comedy and made Chucky the main character and gave him a girlfriend and changed the plot from trying to take a child's soul to him and his girlfriend going after his amulet which was buried with his corpse. Unlike in the previous three films, the protagonist Andy Barclay made no appearance but was briefly mentioned at the beginning of the film in a newspaper article about Chucky. <i>Bride of Chucky</i> was then followed by two sequels; <i><a href="/wiki/Seed_of_Chucky" title="Seed of Chucky">Seed of Chucky</a></i> in 2004 and <i><a href="/wiki/Curse_of_Chucky" title="Curse of Chucky">Curse of Chucky</a></i> in 2013, the latter of which returned to the straightforward horror elements found in the first three films and also brought back Andy Barclay, the protagonist from the first three films for a quick post-credits cameo role. A seventh installment is also planned and scheduled for release sometime in 2017.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Halloween_Horror_Nights">Halloween Horror Nights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Child%27s_Play_3&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Halloween Horror Nights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2009, the climax of <i>Child's Play 3</i> received its own maze at <a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios" title="Universal Studios">Universal Studios</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Halloween_Horror_Nights" title="Halloween Horror Nights">Halloween Horror Nights</a></i>, entitled <i>Chucky's Fun House</i>.</p>
<p>This is not the first time <a href="/wiki/Chucky_(Child%27s_Play)" title="Chucky (Child's Play)">Chucky</a> has been featured in <i>Halloween Horror Nights</i>. <i><a href="/wiki/Curse_of_Chucky" title="Curse of Chucky">Curse of Chucky</a></i> has been slated to receive its own scarezone in the 2013 lineup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Since 1992, Chucky has starred in his own shows, <i>Chucky's In-Your-Face Insults</i> and <i>Chucky's Insult Emporium</i>.</p>
