<p><b>Sha Tin</b>, also spelt <b>Shatin</b>, is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_areas_of_Hong_Kong" title="List of areas of Hong Kong">area</a> around the <a href="/wiki/Shing_Mun_River" title="Shing Mun River">Shing Mun River</a> in the <a href="/wiki/New_Territories" title="New Territories">New Territories</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>. Administratively, it is part of the <a href="/wiki/Sha_Tin_District" title="Sha Tin District">Sha Tin District</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sha_Tin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sha Tin is located in a valley, on both sides of the <a href="/wiki/Shing_Mun_River" title="Shing Mun River">Shing Mun River</a>, running from the southwest to the northeast. It is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Tai_Wai" title="Tai Wai">Tai Wai</a> in the southwest and by <a href="/wiki/Fo_Tan" title="Fo Tan">Fo Tan</a> (left bank) and <a href="/wiki/Shek_Mun" title="Shek Mun">Shek Mun</a> (right bank) in the northeast.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sha_Tin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ShaTin-ShingMunRiver-EarlyStageOfDevelopment.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="612" data-file-width="793" height="170" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/ShaTin-ShingMunRiver-EarlyStageOfDevelopment.jpg/220px-ShaTin-ShingMunRiver-EarlyStageOfDevelopment.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/ShaTin-ShingMunRiver-EarlyStageOfDevelopment.jpg/330px-ShaTin-ShingMunRiver-EarlyStageOfDevelopment.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/ShaTin-ShingMunRiver-EarlyStageOfDevelopment.jpg/440px-ShaTin-ShingMunRiver-EarlyStageOfDevelopment.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ShaTin-ShingMunRiver-EarlyStageOfDevelopment.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Sha_Tin_New_Town" title="Sha Tin New Town">Sha Tin New Town</a> under development in the late 1970s.</div>
</div>

<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tai_Wai_Village" title="Tai Wai Village">Tai Wai Village</a>, located in <a href="/wiki/Tai_Wai" title="Tai Wai">Tai Wai</a>, next to Sha Tin, and the oldest and largest <a href="/wiki/Walled_villages_of_Hong_Kong" title="Walled villages of Hong Kong">walled village</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sha_Tin_District" title="Sha Tin District">Sha Tin District</a>, was built in 1574, during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ming_Dynasty" title="Ming Dynasty">Ming Dynasty</a>.</p>
<p>Before <a href="/wiki/British_Hong_Kong" title="British Hong Kong">British rule in Hong Kong</a>, the area of Sha Tin and its vicinity was referred to as <b>Lek Yuen</b> (lit. "source of trickling" or "source of clear water"). Colonial officials allegedly mistook the name of the <a href="/wiki/Sha_Tin_Wai" title="Sha Tin Wai">Sha Tin Wai</a> village as the name of the area and it has been used ever since. Nowadays, the original name is used to refer to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lek_Yuen_Estate" title="Lek Yuen Estate">Lek Yuen Estate</a>.</p>
<p>Sha Tin was formerly a <a href="/wiki/Market_town" title="Market town">market town</a> at the present location of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sha_Tin_Centre_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sha Tin Centre Street (page does not exist)">Sha Tin Centre Street</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Town_Plaza" title="New Town Plaza">New Town Plaza</a> shopping centre, near the <a href="/wiki/Sha_Tin_Station" title="Sha Tin Station">Sha Tin Station</a> of the <a href="/wiki/MTR" title="MTR">MTR</a> <a href="/wiki/East_Rail_Line" title="East Rail Line">East Rail Line</a>.</p>
<p>Sha Tin was the location of <a href="/wiki/Aviation_history_of_Hong_Kong" title="Aviation history of Hong Kong">the first flight</a> of a powered aircraft in Hong Kong in 1911. The aeroplane was named as the <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Spirit_of_Sha_Tin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Spirit of Sha Tin (page does not exist)">Spirit of Sha Tin</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">沙田精神號</span>). A full size replica of this plane now hangs in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_International_Airport" title="Hong Kong International Airport">Hong Kong International Airport</a>.</p>
<p>The area was formerly agricultural farmland. Before Sha Tin's development into a <a href="/wiki/New_towns_of_Hong_Kong" title="New towns of Hong Kong">new town</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hung_Mui_Kuk&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hung Mui Kuk (page does not exist)">Hung Mui Kuk</a> (紅梅谷), southwest of Sha Tin, was perennially the main site for school <a href="/wiki/Picnic" title="Picnic">picnics</a>. The valley remains a popular barbecue site.</p>
<p>Starting in the 1970s, the area became part of the <a href="/wiki/Sha_Tin_New_Town" title="Sha Tin New Town">Sha Tin New Town</a> development. Since then, the economy in the area has greatly improved and living standards have also increased. Sha Tin Town Centre was developed during the mid-1980s to help "link the town's currently dispersed residents into one cohesive community."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The 18-hectare site, adjacent to the railway station, was built up in stages to house an array of uses including the New Town Plaza, numerous smaller shopping centres, Sha Tin Park, magistracy, library, town hall, marriage registry, hotel, New Town Tower, a town square, and residential towers.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cross-border_activities">Cross-border activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sha_Tin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Cross-border activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Due to their proximity to the Shenzhen border, towns in the northern parts of Hong Kong, notably Sheung Shui and Yuen Long, have become hubs for parallel traders who have been buying up large quantities of goods, forcing up local prices and disrupting the daily lives of local citizens.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Since 2012, there has been a vertiginous increase in mainland <a href="/wiki/Parallel_trading_in_Hong_Kong" title="Parallel trading in Hong Kong">parallel traders</a> arriving in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_District,_Hong_Kong" title="North District, Hong Kong">North District</a> of Hong Kong to re-export infant formula and household products – goods popular with mainlanders – across the border to Shenzhen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The volume of smuggling activity spilled over into Tuen Mun and Shatin in 2014.</p>
<p>The first anti-parallel trading protest was started at Sheung Shui in September 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> As government efforts to limit the adverse impact of mainland trafficking were widely seen as inadequate, so there have been further <a href="/wiki/Anti-parallel_trading_protests" title="Anti-parallel trading protests">subsequent protests</a> in towns in the North District including Shatin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20150309bloomberg_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-20150309bloomberg-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Health">Health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sha_Tin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Health">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Wales_Hospital" title="Prince of Wales Hospital">Prince of Wales Hospital</a> was officially opened in 1982. It provides about 1,400 hospital beds and 24 hours Accident &amp; Emergency service to the eastern New Territories. Other institutions which provide hospital services include the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sha_Tin_Hospital" title="Sha Tin Hospital">Sha Tin Hospital</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cheshire_Home" title="Cheshire Home">Cheshire Home</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Union_Hospital_(Hong_Kong)" title="Union Hospital (Hong Kong)">Union Hospital</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture.2C_sports_and_recreational_facilities">Culture, sports and recreational facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sha_Tin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Culture, sports and recreational facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Shatin_026.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Shatin_026.jpg/220px-Shatin_026.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Shatin_026.jpg/330px-Shatin_026.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Shatin_026.jpg/440px-Shatin_026.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Shatin_026.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A stall selling snacks at <a href="/wiki/Pai_Tau_Village" title="Pai Tau Village">Pai Tau Village</a>, located near <a href="/wiki/New_Town_Plaza" title="New Town Plaza">New Town Plaza</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>There are numerous cultural, recreational and sport facilities in Sha Tin including the <a href="/wiki/Sha_Tin_Town_Hall" title="Sha Tin Town Hall">Town Hall</a>, swimming pools, football pitches, indoor recreation centres and various track and field facilities for the use of Sha Tin residents.</p>
<p>The 8-hectare <a href="/wiki/Sha_Tin_Park" title="Sha Tin Park">Sha Tin Park</a> was opened to public in 1988. Apart from its horticultural gardens and impressive water features, it also includes a large open plaza and a bandstand. The Ma On Shan Park, which is adjacent to Ma On Shan Swimming Pool, occupies 5.5 hectare of land.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Sha_Tin_Racecourse" title="Sha Tin Racecourse">Sha Tin Racecourse</a>, occupying approximately 70 hectares, rests on reclaimed flatland. At the centre of the racecourse is the Penfold Garden which opens to the public on non-racing days.</p>
<p>Located in <a href="/wiki/Tai_Wai" title="Tai Wai">Tai Wai</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Heritage_Museum" title="Hong Kong Heritage Museum">Hong Kong Heritage Museum</a> was opened at the end of 2000. Apart from introducing the art, culture and history of the New Territories, the museum also exhibits a variety of cultural artifacts for public appreciation. The museum, which can accommodate 6,000 visitors, is the largest in the territory.</p>
<p>Cycling has been a distinctive feature in Sha Tin and is very popular among both local people and visitors. The first cycle track in Sha Tin was opened to public in 1981, running along <a href="/wiki/Tolo_Highway" title="Tolo Highway">Tolo Highway</a> to <a href="/wiki/Tai_Po" title="Tai Po">Tai Po</a>, and this remains the territory's most popular cycling venue, drawing many occasional riders at the weekends, as well as dedicated cyclists. To tie in with the development of Ma On Shan, the cycle track was extended to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ma_On_Shan_(area)" title="Ma On Shan (area)">Ma On Shan</a>.</p>
<p>Hiking is also a wonderful activity you could do in your leisure time living in Sha Tin. There are several starting points including Hin Tin Village, Sha Tin Tau Village and hung mui kuk barbecue area leading to the track of Lion Rock Mountain hiking route. It would take you 1 hour to 4 hours to complete the track depends on the starting point and ending point you choose.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_delicacies">Local delicacies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sha_Tin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Local delicacies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sha Tin is famous for certain <a href="/wiki/Northern_and_southern_China" title="Northern and southern China">southern Chinese</a> <a href="/wiki/Chinese_cuisine" title="Chinese cuisine">food</a> such as <i>shanshui <a href="/wiki/Tofu" title="Tofu">doufu</a></i> (Chinese: 山水豆腐; lit. mountain-water beancurd), barbecued pigeon and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chicken_congee" title="Chicken congee">chicken congee</a>. The cooked food stalls in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wo_Che_Estate" title="Wo Che Estate">Wo Che Estate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fo_Tan" title="Fo Tan">Fo Tan</a> are hotspots for food.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sha_Tin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are numerous transportation links both within the Sha Tin District and connecting it to other places in Hong Kong.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roads">Roads</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sha_Tin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Roads">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The road network in Sha Tin is well developed to provide efficient cross-town and local access traffic. Connection between Sha Tin and Kowloon mainly relies on the <a href="/wiki/Lion_Rock_Tunnel" title="Lion Rock Tunnel">Lion Rock Tunnel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tate%27s_Cairn_Tunnel" title="Tate's Cairn Tunnel">Tate's Cairn Tunnel</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shing_Mun_Tunnel" title="Shing Mun Tunnel">Shing Mun Tunnel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tai_Po_Road" title="Tai Po Road">Tai Po Road</a> which makes it easy to reach from many areas of Kowloon as well as from Tsuen Wan.</p>
<ul>
<li>Tai Po Road spans from <a href="/wiki/Sham_Shui_Po" title="Sham Shui Po">Sham Shui Po</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kowloon" title="Kowloon">Kowloon</a> to <a href="/wiki/Tai_Po" title="Tai Po">Tai Po</a> in the <a href="/wiki/New_Territories" title="New Territories">New Territories</a>, connecting Sha Tin en route. It was once the only road connecting Kowloon and the eastern part of the New Territories.</li>
<li>Opened in the 1960s, the Lion Rock Tunnel offers access to Central Kowloon.</li>
<li>The Tate's Cairn Highway was completed in 1991, connecting East Kowloon (<a href="/wiki/Wong_Tai_Sin,_Hong_Kong" title="Wong Tai Sin, Hong Kong">Wong Tai Sin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diamond_Hill" title="Diamond Hill">Diamond Hill</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Choi_Hung" title="Choi Hung">Choi Hung</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kowloon_Bay" title="Kowloon Bay">Kowloon Bay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kwun_Tong" title="Kwun Tong">Kwun Tong</a>) and Hong Kong Island via the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Harbour_Crossing" title="Eastern Harbour Crossing">Eastern Harbour Crossing</a> with the North East New Territories (Sha Tin, Tai Po and <a href="/wiki/Fanling" title="Fanling">Fanling</a>) via the Tolo Highway and through Tate's Cairn Tunnel.</li>
<li>Route connects Sha Tin to <a href="/wiki/Tsuen_Wan" title="Tsuen Wan">Tsuen Wan</a> via the <a href="/wiki/Shing_Mun_Tunnels" title="Shing Mun Tunnels">Shing Mun Tunnels</a> and to <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Island" title="Hong Kong Island">Hong Kong Island</a> via the <a href="/wiki/Western_Harbour_Crossing" title="Western Harbour Crossing">Western Harbour Crossing</a>. The travelling time is only about 30 minutes.</li>
<li>Sai Sha Road was opened in 1988, connecting Sha Tin to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sai_Kung" title="Sai Kung">Sai Kung</a> via <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ma_On_Shan_(area)" title="Ma On Shan (area)">Ma On Shan</a>. Route T7, opened in August 2004, allows traffic to bypass Ma On Shan Town Centre when going from north of Ma On Shan to Sai Kung.</li>
<li>An expressway (via Eagle's Nest Tunnel) connecting <a href="/wiki/Cheung_Sha_Wan" title="Cheung Sha Wan">Cheung Sha Wan</a> in Kowloon to Sha Tin was opened on March 21, 2008. It aims at distributing traffic from Sha Tin and the area to its north, to <a href="/wiki/Lai_Chi_Kok" title="Lai Chi Kok">Lai Chi Kok</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kwai_Chung" title="Kwai Chung">Kwai Chung</a>, and the airport at <a href="/wiki/Chek_Lap_Kok" title="Chek Lap Kok">Chek Lap Kok</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mong_Kok" title="Mong Kok">Mong Kok</a> It has shorten the trip from Sha Tin to the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_International_Airport" title="Hong Kong International Airport">Hong Kong International Airport</a> to around 35 minutes.</li>
</ul>
<p>At present, there are over 110 routes of public bus serving Sha Tin.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Railway">Railway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sha_Tin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Railway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/MTR" title="MTR">MTR</a> (<a href="/wiki/East_Rail_Line" title="East Rail Line">East Rail Line</a>) is a major means of transportation between <a href="/wiki/Kowloon" title="Kowloon">Kowloon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lo_Wu" title="Lo Wu">Lo Wu</a> via Sha Tin. After the electrification of the Line between 1979 and 1983, the East Rail now carries over 730,000 passengers daily.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Ma_On_Shan_Line" title="Ma On Shan Line">Ma On Shan Line</a> opened on 21 December 2004. The 11.4 km long railway has 9 stations linking <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ma_On_Shan_(area)" title="Ma On Shan (area)">Ma On Shan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tai_Wai" title="Tai Wai">Tai Wai</a>, where it connects with the main East Rail line (terminus). The MTR Maintenance Centre is located in Tai Wai.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sha_Tin_to_Central_Link" title="Sha Tin to Central Link">Sha Tin to Central Link</a> (Traditional Chinese: 沙田至中環線) is a railway project under construction which incorporates an extension to the Ma On Shan Line to Hung Hom via <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kai_Tak" title="Kai Tak">Kai Tak</a>, as well as an extension of the East Rail Line to <a href="/wiki/Central,_Hong_Kong" title="Central, Hong Kong">Central</a> via a new tunnel under the <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Harbour" title="Victoria Harbour">harbour</a>.</li>
</ul>
