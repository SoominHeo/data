<p><b>Satō Tetsutarō</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">佐藤 鉄太郎</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, 22 August 1866 – 4 March 1942)</span> was a Japanese military theorist and an admiral in the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Imperial Japanese Navy</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sat%C5%8D_Tetsutar%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sat%C5%8D_Tetsutar%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in the Tsuruoka domain, <a href="/wiki/Dewa_Province" title="Dewa Province">Dewa Province</a> (present day <a href="/wiki/Tsuruoka,_Yamagata" title="Tsuruoka, Yamagata">Tsuruoka city</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yamagata_prefecture" title="Yamagata prefecture">Yamagata prefecture</a>), Satō graduated from the 14th class of the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Naval_Academy" title="Imperial Japanese Naval Academy">Imperial Japanese Naval Academy</a> in 1887, ranked 5th of 45 cadets. He served as <a href="/wiki/Midshipman" title="Midshipman">midshipman</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Corvette" title="Corvette">corvette</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_corvette_Tsukuba" title="Japanese corvette Tsukuba"><i>Tsukuba</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/Cruiser" title="Cruiser">cruiser</a> <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Naniwa" title="Japanese cruiser Naniwa"><i>Naniwa</i></a> and corvette <a href="/wiki/Japanese_corvette_Yamato" title="Japanese corvette Yamato"><i>Yamato</i></a>. On being commissioned an <a href="/wiki/Ensign_(rank)" title="Ensign (rank)">ensign</a>, he was assigned to the <a href="/wiki/Gunboat" title="Gunboat">gunboat</a> <a href="/wiki/Japanese_gunboat_Ch%C5%8Dkai" title="Japanese gunboat Chōkai"><i>Chōkai</i></a>, becoming chief navigator by 1890. After his promotion to <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant" title="Lieutenant">lieutenant</a> in 1892, he served as chief navigator aboard the <a href="/wiki/Gunboat" title="Gunboat">gunboat</a> <a href="/wiki/Japanese_gunboat_Akagi" title="Japanese gunboat Akagi"><i>Akagi</i></a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_Sino-Japanese_War">First Sino-Japanese War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sat%C5%8D_Tetsutar%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: First Sino-Japanese War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the time of the <a href="/wiki/First_Sino-Japanese_War" title="First Sino-Japanese War">First Sino-Japanese War</a>, Sato was still assigned to <i>Akagi</i>. He took command of the vessel when its captain was killed during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Yalu" title="Battle of the Yalu">Battle of the Yalu</a> on 17 September 1894. Satō himself was wounded in the battle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kowner_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kowner-2">[2]</a></sup> Afterwards, he served as chief navigator on <i>Naniwa</i>.</p>
<p>On the conclusion of the war, Satō's talents and heroism were awarded with an assignment to the staff of Admiral <a href="/wiki/Yamamoto_Gonnohy%C5%8De" title="Yamamoto Gonnohyōe">Yamamoto Gonnohyōe</a>'s staff within the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_the_Navy_of_Japan" title="Ministry of the Navy of Japan">Navy Ministry</a>'s Naval Affairs Department.</p>
<p>Satō was sent to study naval strategy in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> from 1899–1901 and in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> from 1901 - 1902. Upon his return to Japan, he published <i>On the Defense of the Empire</i> while an instructor at the <a href="/wiki/Naval_War_College_(Japan)" title="Naval War College (Japan)">Naval War College</a>, which advocated the Navy as the main force of national security and military strength. He was also appointed <a href="/wiki/Executive_officer" title="Executive officer">executive officer</a> of the cruiser <i>Miyako</i>. After his promotion to <a href="/wiki/Commander" title="Commander">commander</a> in 1902, he was <a href="/wiki/Executive_officer" title="Executive officer">executive officer</a> on <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Itsukushima" title="Japanese cruiser Itsukushima"><i>Itsukushima</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Izumo" title="Japanese cruiser Izumo"><i>Izumo</i></a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Russo-Japanese_War">Russo-Japanese War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sat%C5%8D_Tetsutar%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Russo-Japanese War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Russo-Japanese_War" title="Russo-Japanese War">Russo-Japanese War</a>, Satō served as a staff officer of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IJN_2nd_Fleet" title="IJN 2nd Fleet">IJN 2nd Fleet</a> under Vice Admiral <a href="/wiki/Kamimura_Hikonoj%C5%8D" title="Kamimura Hikonojō">Kamimura Hikonojō</a>, partly to protect him from political retaliation by the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Army" title="Imperial Japanese Army">Imperial Japanese Army</a>, who were incensed by his thesis that the navy was of more importance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kowner_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Kowner-2">[2]</a></sup> While aboard the 2nd Fleet <a href="/wiki/Flagship" title="Flagship">flagship</a> <i>Izumo</i>, Sato took part in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_off_Ulsan" title="Battle off Ulsan">Battle off Ulsan</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Tsushima" title="Battle of Tsushima">Battle of Tsushima</a>. Following the war, Satō was <a href="/wiki/Executive_officer" title="Executive officer">executive officer</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Battleship" title="Battleship">battleship</a> <a href="/wiki/Japanese_battleship_Asahi" title="Japanese battleship Asahi"><i>Asahi</i></a>, and was then given his first command: the gunboat <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_gunboat_Tatsuta&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Japanese gunboat Tatsuta (page does not exist)"><i>Tatsuta</i></a>; however, he returned within a year to the Naval War College as an advanced student, and later served as an instructor from 1906 to 1908.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kowner_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Kowner-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Naval_theorist">Naval theorist</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sat%C5%8D_Tetsutar%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Naval theorist">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After winning promotion to <a href="/wiki/Captain_(naval)" title="Captain (naval)">captain</a> in 1907, Satō published several revised editions and expanded on his initial 1903 thesis including <i>History of Naval Defense</i> (1907), <i>History of the Empire's Defense</i> (1908), and the <i>Revised History of the Empire's Defense</i> (1912). Satō has been called "the <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Thayer_Mahan" title="Alfred Thayer Mahan">Mahan</a> of Japan", as his writings emphasized that the key to Japan's safety was denial of <a href="/wiki/Power_projection" title="Power projection">power projection</a> by hypothetical enemies (such as the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>), into waters adjacent to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_home_islands" title="Japanese home islands">Japanese home islands</a>.</p>
<p>Satō's works, along with documents of the Navy Ministry regarding policy, would form the basis of the Japanese naval expansion into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Netherlands_East_Indies" title="Netherlands East Indies">Netherlands East Indies</a>, using elements from naval plans developed by <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> Admiral <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alfred_T._Mahan" title="Alfred T. Mahan">Alfred T. Mahan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> Vice Admiral <a href="/wiki/Philip_Howard_Colomb" title="Philip Howard Colomb">Philip Howard Colomb</a>. He also began urging the Japanese government to maintain at least a 70% capital ship level over the United States, Japan's hypothetical rival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kowner_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Kowner-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Satō was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Rear_admiral" title="Rear admiral">rear admiral</a> in 1912, while at the Navy War College, as well as serving at sea between 1908 until 1914 as captain of the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Soya" title="Japanese cruiser Soya"><i>Soya</i></a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Aso" title="Japanese cruiser Aso"><i>Aso</i></a>.</p>
<p>The following year after Japan's entry into <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, Satō was appointed <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy_General_Staff" title="Imperial Japanese Navy General Staff">Vice Chief of the Navy General Staff</a> in 1915, and promoted to <a href="/wiki/Vice_admiral" title="Vice admiral">vice admiral</a> the next year while serving as president of the Navy War College.</p>
<p>Leaving the Navy War College in 1920, Satō commanded the <a href="/wiki/Maizuru_Naval_District" title="Maizuru Naval District">Maizuru Naval District</a> before being placed on the inactive list in 1922. In November 1928, he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Sacred_Treasure" title="Order of the Sacred Treasure">Order of the Sacred Treasure</a> (1st class).</p>
<p>Named to the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Peers_(Japan)" title="House of Peers (Japan)">House of Peers</a> in 1934, Satō was a major opponent to Japan's participation in the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Naval_Treaty" title="Washington Naval Treaty">Washington Naval Treaty</a> (and thus a member of the <a href="/wiki/Fleet_Faction" title="Fleet Faction">Fleet Faction</a> following World War I until his death in 1942.</p>
