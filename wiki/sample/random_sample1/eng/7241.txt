<p><b>Satu Mare County</b> (<a href="/wiki/Romanian_language" title="Romanian language">Romanian</a>: <span lang="ro" xml:lang="ro"><i>Județul Satu Mare</i></span>, <small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Romanian" title="Help:IPA for Romanian">[ˌsatu ˈmare]</a></span>) is a county (<a href="/wiki/Jude%C8%9B" title="Județ">județ</a>) of <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a> on the border with <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>. The capital city is <a href="/wiki/Satu_Mare" title="Satu Mare">Satu Mare</a>. Besides Romanians (58.8% of the population), Satu Mare features a significant <a href="/wiki/Minority_group" title="Minority group">ethnic minority</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hungarians_in_Romania" title="Hungarians in Romania">Hungarians</a> (35.2%).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Satu_Mare_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>, it is known as <i>Szatmár megye</i>, in <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> as <i>Kreis Sathmar</i>, in <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a> as Сату-Маре, and in <a href="/wiki/Slovak_language" title="Slovak language">Slovak</a> as <i>Satmárska župa</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Satu_Mare_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Satu Mare is a multicultural city, with a population mix of Romanian, Hungarian, Roma, German and other ethnicities.</p>
<p>In 2002, Satu Mare County had a population of 367,281 and the <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 83/km².<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Popula.C8.9Bia_dup.C4.83_etnie_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Popula.C8.9Bia_dup.C4.83_etnie-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Romanians" title="Romanians">Romanians</a> – 58.8%</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hungarians_in_Romania" title="Hungarians in Romania">Hungarians</a> – 35.2%</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Romani_people" title="Romani people">Roma</a> – 3.7%</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">Germans</a> – 1.7%</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ukrainians" title="Ukrainians">Ukrainians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovaks" title="Slovaks">Slovaks</a>, other</li>
</ul>
<p>In 2011, its population was 329,079 and <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 74.48/km².<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Popula.C8.9Bia_dup.C4.83_etnie_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Popula.C8.9Bia_dup.C4.83_etnie-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Romanians" title="Romanians">Romanians</a> – 57.73%</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hungarians_in_Romania" title="Hungarians in Romania">Hungarians</a> – 34.5%</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Romani_people" title="Romani people">Roma</a> – 5.32%</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">Germans</a> – 1.51%</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rusyns" title="Rusyns">Rusyns</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ukrainians" title="Ukrainians">Ukrainians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovaks" title="Slovaks">Slovaks</a>, other</li>
</ul>
<p>Hungarians mostly reside along the border with Hungary, but some are also scattered throughout the whole county.</p>
<p>Historically, Hungarians were concentrated in the cities, where administration resides, while the Romanian population was larger in the villages throughout the county. In 1930 the Hungarians were representing 41,9% of the urban population in Satu Mare County and only 20,0% of the population in the villages according to census data.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1930census_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-1930census-6">[6]</a></sup> The proportion of different ethnic groups varied throughout the history, due to regime and political changes. After the <a href="/wiki/Austro-Hungarian_Compromise_of_1867" title="Austro-Hungarian Compromise of 1867">Austro-Hungarian Compromise of 1867</a> the Hungarian population increased its proportion greatly, in 1880 representing 44.4% and in 1910 reaching 55.1% of the county population, according to Árpád E. Varga.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-varga_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-varga-7">[7]</a></sup> After World War I the Hungarian and German population declined.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Satu_Mare_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This county has a total area of 4,418 square kilometres (1,706 sq mi).</p>
<p>In the north are the <a href="/wiki/Oa%C8%99_Mountains" title="Oaș Mountains">Oaș Mountains</a>, part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Carpathians" title="Eastern Carpathians">Eastern Carpathians</a>. This makes up around 17% of the area. The remainder is hills, forming 20% of the area, and plains. The western county takes up the Eastern part of the <a href="/wiki/Pannonian_Basin" title="Pannonian Basin">Pannonian Plain</a>.</p>
<p>The county is crossed by the <a href="/wiki/Some%C8%99_(river)" title="Someș (river)">Someș River</a> and also <a href="/wiki/Tur_(river)" title="Tur (river)">Tur River</a> and <a href="/wiki/Crasna_(Tisza)" title="Crasna (Tisza)">Crasna River</a>.</p>
<p>The county lies partly in the <a href="/wiki/Maramure%C8%99" title="Maramureș">historical region of Maramureș</a> and partly in the historical region of <a href="/wiki/Cri%C8%99ana" title="Crișana">Crișana</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Satu_Mare_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Satu Mare County benefits from its position, close to the border of <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a> with <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, and it is one of the places which attracts foreign investment in industry and agriculture.</p>
<p>The predominant industries in the county are:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Textile" title="Textile">Textiles</a> industry.</li>
<li>Machine and automotive components.</li>
<li>Food industry.</li>
<li>Wood and furniture industry.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Satu_Mare_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The main tourist attractions in the county are:</p>
<ul>
<li>The "<a href="/wiki/Oa%C8%99_Country" title="Oaș Country">Oaș Country</a>", with its strong Romanian folk traditions, on the North Eastern side of the county.</li>
<li>The Oaș Mountains.</li>
<li>The cities of <a href="/wiki/Satu_Mare" title="Satu Mare">Satu Mare</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carei" title="Carei">Carei</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/T%C4%83%C8%99nad" title="Tășnad">Tășnad</a> Resort.</li>
<li>The fortifications from <a href="/wiki/Ardud" title="Ardud">Ardud</a> and <a href="/wiki/Medie%C8%99u_Aurit" title="Medieșu Aurit">Medieșu Aurit</a></li>
</ul>
