<p><b>Satya Narayana Nadella</b> (born August 19, 1967) is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_American" title="Indian American">Indian American</a> business executive. He is the current <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">chief executive officer</a> (CEO) of <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-9">[9]</a></sup> He was appointed as CEO on 4 February 2014, succeeding <a href="/wiki/Steve_Ballmer" title="Steve Ballmer">Steve Ballmer</a>. Before becoming CEO of Microsoft, he was Executive Vice President of Microsoft's cloud and enterprise group, responsible for building and running the company's computing platforms, developer tools and cloud computing services.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Nadella was born into a <a href="/wiki/Telugu_people" title="Telugu people">Telugu</a>-speaking family<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sg_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-sg-10">[10]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Hyderabad" title="Hyderabad">Hyderabad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andhra_Pradesh" title="Andhra Pradesh">Andhra Pradesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> (now in the state of <a href="/wiki/Telangana" title="Telangana">Telangana</a>). His father, Bukkapuram Nadella Yugandher, was a civil servant of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Administrative_Service" title="Indian Administrative Service">Indian Administrative Service</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dnaindia_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-dnaindia-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Satya_Nadella_Eenadu_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Satya_Nadella_Eenadu-12">[12]</a></sup> Nadella attended the <a href="/wiki/Hyderabad_Public_School#Notable_alumni" title="Hyderabad Public School">Hyderabad Public School, Begumpet</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> before attaining a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> from the <a href="/wiki/Manipal_Institute_of_Technology" title="Manipal Institute of Technology">Manipal Institute of Technology</a> in 1988 (then affiliated with <a href="/wiki/Mangalore_University" title="Mangalore University">Mangalore University</a>), <a href="/wiki/Manipal" title="Manipal">Manipal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karnataka" title="Karnataka">Karnataka</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Official_Bio_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Official_Bio-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nadella subsequently traveled to the U.S. to study for an M.S. degree in <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Wisconsin%E2%80%93Milwaukee" title="University of Wisconsin–Milwaukee">University of Wisconsin–Milwaukee</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> receiving his degree in 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Later he received an MBA from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Chicago" title="University of Chicago">University of Chicago</a> <a href="/wiki/Booth_School_of_Business" title="Booth School of Business">Booth School of Business</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Time-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nadella said he "always wanted to build things".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He knew that computer science was what he wanted to pursue,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> but that passion was at precocious stage while studying at Manipal University. "And so it [electrical engineering] was a great way for me to go discover what turned out to become a passion," he says.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msftceo_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-msftceo-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sun_Microsystems">Sun Microsystems</span></h3>
<p>Nadella worked with <a href="/wiki/Sun_Microsystems" title="Sun Microsystems">Sun Microsystems</a> as a member of its technology staff prior to joining Microsoft in 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TOI_01FEB14_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-TOI_01FEB14-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Microsoft">Microsoft</span></h3>
<p>At Microsoft, Nadella has led major projects including the company's move to <a href="/wiki/Cloud_computing" title="Cloud computing">cloud computing</a> and the development of one of the largest cloud infrastructures in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nadella worked as the senior vice-president of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Research_and_Development" title="Research and Development">Research and Development</a> (R&amp;D) for the Online Services Division and vice-president of the Microsoft Business Division. Later, he was made the president of Microsoft's $19 billion Server and Tools Business and led a transformation of the company's business and technology culture from <a href="/wiki/Client_(computing)" title="Client (computing)">client services</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cloud_infrastructure" title="Cloud infrastructure">cloud infrastructure</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cloud_services" title="Cloud services">services</a>. He has been credited for helping bring <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_SQL_Server" title="Microsoft SQL Server">Microsoft's database</a>, <a href="/wiki/Windows_Server" title="Windows Server">Windows Server</a> and <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Visual_Studio" title="Microsoft Visual Studio">developer tools</a> to its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Windows_Azure" title="Windows Azure">Azure</a> cloud.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-Time-19">[19]</a></sup> The revenue from Cloud Services grew to $20.3 billion in June 2013 from $16.6 billion when he took over in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nadella's 2013 base salary was nearly $700,000, for a total compensation, with stock bonuses, of $7.6 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>

<p>Previous positions held by Nadella include:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>President of the Server &amp; Tools Division (9 February 2011 – February 2014)</li>
<li>Senior Vice-President of Research and Development for the Online Services Division (March 2007 – February 2011)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></li>
<li>Vice-President of the Business Division</li>
<li>Corporate Vice-President of Business Solutions and Search &amp; Advertising Platform Group</li>
<li>Executive Vice-President of Cloud and Enterprise group<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Official_Bio_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Official_Bio-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>On 4 February 2014, Nadella was announced as the new CEO of Microsoft,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-9">[9]</a></sup> the third chief executive in the company's history, following Bill Gates and Steve Ballmer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2014, Nadella courted controversy when he made a statement that women should not ask for a raise and should trust the system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The statement was made while he was attending an event on Women in Computing in Phoenix, AZ. Nadella was roundly criticised for the statement and he apologised later on twitter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> He later sent an email to Microsoft Employees admitting he was "Completely wrong"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the two years that have since passed, Nadella is viewed as having done well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> With the Microsoft stock having risen more than 60% since he took over.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>In 1992, Nadella married Anupama, daughter of his father's <a href="/wiki/Indian_Administrative_Service" title="Indian Administrative Service">Indian Administrative Service</a> (IAS) batchmate, K.R. Venugopal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> The families had known each other for thirty years and the marriage was arranged by their parents. The couple has three children, a son and two daughters, and live in <a href="/wiki/Bellevue,_Washington" title="Bellevue, Washington">Bellevue, Washington</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-POLITICO_04FEB14_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-POLITICO_04FEB14-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nadella is an avid reader of <a href="/wiki/American_poetry" title="American poetry">American</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indian_poetry" title="Indian poetry">Indian poetry</a>. He also has an interest in <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a> (his passion growing up), having played on his school team. He has mentioned having learned something about <a href="/wiki/Leadership" title="Leadership">leadership</a> and teamwork from cricket.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-POLITICO_04FEB14_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-POLITICO_04FEB14-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msftceo_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-msftceo-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
