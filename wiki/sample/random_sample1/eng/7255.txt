<p>A <b>presenter</b> <b>host</b> or <b>hostess</b> is a person or organization responsible for the running of a public event. A <a href="/wiki/Museum" title="Museum">museum</a> or <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a>, for example, may be the presenter or host of an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Collection_(museum)" title="Collection (museum)">exhibit</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">films</a>, a presenter (but not a host) is a usually a well-known <a href="/wiki/Executive_producer" title="Executive producer">executive producer</a> credited with introducing a film or filmmaker to a larger audience. For example: "Presented by <a href="/wiki/Cecil_B._DeMille" title="Cecil B. DeMille">Cecil B. DeMille</a>".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcast_media">Broadcast media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presenter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Broadcast media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the broadcast media, a presenter is the person who hosts, narrates, or otherwise takes the main role in narrating or hosting a <a href="/wiki/Radio_program" title="Radio program">radio program</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Television_program" title="Television program">television program</a>. The phrases "television presenter" or "radio presenter" are never used in <a href="/wiki/American_English" title="American English">American English</a>, and rarely in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.</p>
<p>A person who hosts other kinds of public entertainments may also be called a <a href="/wiki/Master_of_ceremonies" title="Master of ceremonies">master of ceremonies</a> (often abbreviated "M.C." or "emcee").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_presenter">Television presenter<span id="TV"></span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presenter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Television presenter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Television_presenter" title="Television presenter">television presenter</a> is a person who introduces or hosts <a href="/wiki/Television_program" title="Television program">television programmes</a>, including factual documentaries, live events, and sports. Nowadays, it is common for minor celebrities in other fields to take on this role, but there are also a number of people who have made their name solely within the field of presenting, particularly within <a href="/wiki/Children%27s_television_series" title="Children's television series">children's television series</a> to become a television personality.</p>
<p>Some presenters may double as an <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Model_(person)" title="Model (person)">model</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Comedian" title="Comedian">comedian</a>, etc. Others may be subject matter experts, such as scientists or politicians, serving as presenters for a programme about their field of expertise (for example, Sir <a href="/wiki/David_Attenborough" title="David Attenborough">David Attenborough</a>). Some are celebrities who have made their name in one area, and then move on to get involved in other areas. Examples of this latter group include the British comedian <a href="/wiki/Michael_Palin" title="Michael Palin">Michael Palin</a> who now presents programmes about <a href="/wiki/Travel" title="Travel">travel</a> (such as <i><a href="/wiki/Michael_Palin:_Around_the_World_in_80_Days" title="Michael Palin: Around the World in 80 Days">Around the World in 80 Days</a></i>), and <a href="/wiki/Alan_Alda" title="Alan Alda">Alan Alda</a>, who has been the host of the <i><a href="/wiki/Scientific_American_Frontiers" title="Scientific American Frontiers">Scientific American Frontiers</a></i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_program" title="TV program">TV program</a> for about a decade.</p>
<p>The term is also used in other countries, such as <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, such a person is always called a host, a hostess (females), or an M.C. (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Master_of_Ceremonies" title="Master of Ceremonies">Master of Ceremonies</a> or "emcee").</p>
<p>The term does not apply to reading the news however. This role is known in American English as an anchor, and in British and Commonwealth English as a newsreader. A quiz show host is sometimes also described as a presenter.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio_presenter">Radio presenter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presenter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Radio presenter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Radio_personality" title="Radio personality">Radio personality</a></div>
<p>Broadly speaking, a radio presenter (often, especially in <a href="/wiki/American_English" title="American English">American English</a>, radio personality) is the same as a television presenter, except that they present <a href="/wiki/Radio" title="Radio">radio</a> programs instead of television programs. Big name presenters in the UK include <a href="/wiki/Terry_Wogan" title="Terry Wogan">Terry Wogan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ken_Bruce" title="Ken Bruce">Ken Bruce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Blackburn" title="Tony Blackburn">Tony Blackburn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Simon_Mayo" title="Simon Mayo">Simon Mayo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Wright_(DJ)" title="Steve Wright (DJ)">Steve Wright (DJ)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scottie_McClue" title="Scottie McClue">Scottie McClue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Radcliffe" title="Mark Radcliffe">Mark Radcliffe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Evans_(presenter)" title="Chris Evans (presenter)">Chris Evans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clint_Boon" title="Clint Boon">Clint Boon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greg_James" title="Greg James">Greg James</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="News_presenter">News presenter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presenter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: News presenter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/News_presenter" title="News presenter">News presenter</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_presenter">Sports presenter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presenter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sports presenter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Sports_commentator" title="Sports commentator">sports commentator</a> (also sports announcer, sports broadcaster, sportscaster, or sports presenter) is a type of <a href="/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalist</a> on radio and/or <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> who specializes in reporting or commentating on sporting events. Sports casting is often done <a href="/wiki/Live_television" title="Live television">live</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Weather_presenter">Weather presenter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Presenter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Weather presenter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A weather presenter is a type of <a href="/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalist</a> on <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> who specializes in presenting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weather_forecast" title="Weather forecast">weather forecasts</a>.</p>
<p>In the UK, this includes separate presenters for national and regional/local weather forecasts.</p>
