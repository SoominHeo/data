<p><b>Social philosophy</b> is the study of questions about <a href="/wiki/Social_behavior" title="Social behavior">social behavior</a> and interpretations of <a href="/wiki/Society" title="Society">society</a> and social <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Institutions" title="Institutions">institutions</a> in terms of <a href="/wiki/Value_(ethics)" title="Value (ethics)">ethical values</a> rather than empirical relations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Social philosophers place new emphasis on understanding the social contexts for political, legal, moral, and cultural questions, and to the development of novel theoretical frameworks, from social ontology to care ethics to cosmopolitan theories of democracy, human rights, gender equity and global justice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subdisciplines">Subdisciplines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Social_philosophy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Subdisciplines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There is often a considerable overlap between the questions addressed by social philosophy and <a href="/wiki/Ethics" title="Ethics">ethics</a> or <a href="/wiki/Value_theory" title="Value theory">value theory</a>. Other forms of social philosophy include <a href="/wiki/Political_philosophy" title="Political philosophy">political philosophy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jurisprudence" title="Jurisprudence">jurisprudence</a>, which are largely concerned with the societies of <a href="/wiki/State_(polity)" title="State (polity)">state</a> and <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a> and their functioning.</p>
<p>Social philosophy, ethics, and political philosophy all share intimate connections with other disciplines in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_sciences" title="Social sciences">social sciences</a>. In turn, the social sciences themselves are of focal interest to the <a href="/wiki/Philosophy_of_social_science" title="Philosophy of social science">philosophy of social science</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Philosophy_of_language" title="Philosophy of language">philosophy of language</a> and <a href="/wiki/Social_epistemology" title="Social epistemology">social epistemology</a> are subfields which overlap in significant ways with social philosophy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
