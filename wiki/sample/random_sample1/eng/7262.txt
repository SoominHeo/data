<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">European Amalfi Prize for Sociology and Social Sciences</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>European Amalfi Prize for Sociology and Social Sciences</b> <i>(Premio Europeo Amalfi per la Sociologia e le Scienze Sociali)</i> is a prestigious <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> award in the social sciences. Established in 1987 on the initiative of the Section for Sociological Theories and Social Transformations of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Italian_Association_of_Sociology&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Italian Association of Sociology (page does not exist)">Italian Association of Sociology</a>, it is conferred annually in <a href="/wiki/Amalfi" title="Amalfi">Amalfi</a> to the author of a book or an article which was published within the previous two years and has made an important contribution to sociology.</p>
<p>The award ceremony is accompanied by an international sociological conference. The awardees are selected by a jury currently consisting of <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Archer" title="Margaret Archer">Margaret Archer</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Allessandro_Cavalli&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Allessandro Cavalli (page does not exist)">Allessandro Cavalli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salvador_Giner" title="Salvador Giner">Salvador Giner</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Joachim_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Joachim Israel (page does not exist)">Joachim Israel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michel_Maffesoli" title="Michel Maffesoli">Michel Maffesoli</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Carlo_Mongardini&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Carlo Mongardini (page does not exist)">Carlo Mongardini</a> (chair), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Birgitta_Nedelmann&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Birgitta Nedelmann (page does not exist)">Birgitta Nedelmann</a>, <a href="/wiki/Helga_Nowotny" title="Helga Nowotny">Helga Nowotny</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Piotr_Sztompka" title="Piotr Sztompka">Piotr Sztompka</a>. Previous members include <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Giddens" title="Anthony Giddens">Anthony Giddens</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Peter_Gerlich&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Peter Gerlich (page does not exist)">Peter Gerlich</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Friedrich_H._Tenbruck&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Friedrich H. Tenbruck (page does not exist)">Friedrich H. Tenbruck</a>.</p>
<p>The award was suspended in 1993 and 1996, 1992 and 2011-2015 the jury decided not to assign the award.</p>
<p>In 1999, a biennial special award for an outstanding first book was established and named after Norbert Elias, the first recipient of the Amalfi Award. There are also other auxiliary awards in various categories; the main award can also be split.</p>
