<p>The <b>"<i>San Marco</i>" Marine Brigade</b> (<a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <i>Brigata Marina "San Marco"</i>) is an amphibious formation of the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Navy" title="Italian Navy">Italian Navy</a> established on 1 March 2013, reorganizing the Navy Landing Force.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marina.difesa_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-marina.difesa-1">[1]</a></sup> It has its command in <a href="/wiki/Brindisi" title="Brindisi">Brindisi</a>.</p>
<p>It is a joint formation, framed within the amphibious projection force, whose Commander is the same Admiral who serves as Commander of the San Marco Brigade.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Marco_Marine_Brigade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A joint Italian-Spanish landing force (<i>SILF</i> - <i>Spanish Italian Landing Force</i>) was established on 23 November 1996, in order to operate in international combat theaters on behalf of <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a>.</p>
<p>The SILF was structured in the form of an amphibious brigade, consisting of two manoeuvre units, one being "<i>San Marco</i>" Regiment, of marine infantry, artillery, anti-armour and air defense weapons, reconnaissance and demolition, engineers, helicopters and air support, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IFV" title="IFV">IFVs</a> and assault vehicles.</p>

<p>On 1 October 1999, the Landing Force of the Navy was created, under the command of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rear_Admiral" title="Rear Admiral">Rear Admiral</a>, stationed in Brindisi Naval Base and formed in two regiments: the "San Marco" Regiment and the "Carlotto" Regiment, both controlled by a <a href="/wiki/Captain_(naval)" title="Captain (naval)">Captain</a>, and the Landing Craft Group, commanded by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commander_(naval)" title="Commander (naval)">Commander</a>.</p>
<p>The Landing Force was renamed from 1 March 2013 as "<i>San Marco</i>" Marine Brigade, still being based in Brindisi, but consisting of three regiments, totalling about 3,800 marines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marina.difesa_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-marina.difesa-1">[1]</a></sup> It is commanded by an <a href="/wiki/Admiral" title="Admiral">Admiral</a>, reporting directly to the Commander in Chief of the Italian Navy Fleet Command (CINCNAV).</p>
<p>With the <a href="/wiki/Lagunari" title="Lagunari">Lagunari regiment</a> of <a href="/wiki/Italian_Army" title="Italian Army">Italian Army</a> form the <i>Forza di proiezione dal mare</i> (Landing projection force), reporting to the <a href="/wiki/Chief_of_the_Defence_Staff_(Italy)" title="Chief of the Defence Staff (Italy)">Chief of the Defence Staff</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Equipment">Equipment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Marco_Marine_Brigade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Equipment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The brigade can now rely on three <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landing_Platform_Dock" title="Landing Platform Dock">LPD</a></i> amphibious assault ships, built specifically for the <i>San Marco</i> by <a href="/wiki/Fincantieri" title="Fincantieri">Fincantieri</a>: the two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Giorgio-class_amphibious_assault_ship" title="San Giorgio-class amphibious assault ship">San Giorgio-class</a> ships: the <i>San Giorgio</i> and the <i>San Marco</i> and the improved and enhanced <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_ship_San_Giusto_(L9894)" title="Italian ship San Giusto (L9894)">San Giusto (L 9894)</a></i>.</p>
<p>Air support for the Brigade employs <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EH-101" title="EH-101">Agusta-Westland AW-101</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agusta-Bell_AB_212" title="Agusta-Bell AB 212">Agusta-Bell AB 212</a>, grouped in the "Reparto Eliassalto", formerly Amphibious Fight Team of the 4th Helicopters Group, located in Station Aircraft <a href="/wiki/Grottaglie" title="Grottaglie">Grottaglie</a>.</p>
<p>The mechanized support is provided by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AAV-7A1" title="AAV-7A1">AAV-7A1</a> armoured amphibious vehicles and fighting vehicles, as well as other light vehicles.</p>
