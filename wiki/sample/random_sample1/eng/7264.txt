<p><b>San Martino</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> church located on Via del Porrione, in the Terzo San Martino (named after this church) in central in <a href="/wiki/Siena" title="Siena">Siena</a>, region of Tuscany, Italy. Adjacent to the church is the <a href="/wiki/Renaissance_architecture" title="Renaissance architecture">Renaissance</a> style <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Logge_del_Papa,_Siena" title="Logge del Papa, Siena">Logge del Papa</a> erected in 1462 by commission by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Pius_II" title="Pope Pius II">Pope Pius II</a> Piccolomini.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Martino_(Siena)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A church at the site was present by the 12th century, but it was rebuilt and enlarged in the 16th century. The <a href="/wiki/Baroque_architecture" title="Baroque architecture">Baroque</a> façade was built in 1613 and the bell tower completed in 1738.</p>
<p>The interior, in the counterfaçade, has a canvas of the <i>Immaculate Conception Protecting Siena during the 1526 Battle of Camollia</i> by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Giovanni_di_Lorenzo_(painter)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Giovanni di Lorenzo (painter) (page does not exist)">Giovanni di Lorenzo</a>, commissioned by the city's commune in 1528. In the chapels are, among the others: the <i><a href="/wiki/Circumcision_of_Jesus" title="Circumcision of Jesus">Circumcision of Jesus</a></i>, one of <a href="/wiki/Guido_Reni" title="Guido Reni">Guido Reni</a>'s masterworks, the <i>Martyrdom of San Bartolomeo</i> (1637) by <a href="/wiki/Guercino" title="Guercino">Guercino</a> and a <i>Nativity</i> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Domenico_Beccafumi" title="Domenico Beccafumi">Domenico Beccafumi</a>.</p>
<p>The presbytery's restoration in the 17th century was patronized by the de' Vecchi family, who commissioned the decorations to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mazzuoli_family" title="Mazzuoli family">Mazzuoli family</a>. <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Mazzuoli_(1644%E2%80%931725)" title="Giuseppe Mazzuoli (1644–1725)">Giuseppe Mazzuoli</a> completed the statue of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Thomas_of_Villanova" title="St. Thomas of Villanova">St. Thomas of Villanova</a></i>, and he helped his brother Giovanni complete the main altar.</p>
