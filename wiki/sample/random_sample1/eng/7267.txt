<p>The <b><i>San Giorgio</i>-class <a href="/wiki/Amphibious_transport_dock" title="Amphibious transport dock">amphibious transport dock</a></b> (LPD)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> were built by <a href="/wiki/Fincantieri" title="Fincantieri">Fincantieri</a> for the Italian Navy. These ships can carry a battalion of troops, and up to 36 armored vehicles. The stern floodable dock can accommodate three landing craft. The ships are based at the <a href="/wiki/Brindisi" title="Brindisi">Brindisi</a> naval base on the Adriatic coast.</p>
<p><i>San Giorgio</i> and <i>San Marco</i> have been extensively modified to accommodate a full-length flight deck with four landing spots. <i>San Giusto</i>, the third vessel, has not been modified since construction, however featured an improved design, and is normally employed as a training ship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Replacement_class">Replacement class</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Giorgio-class_amphibious_transport_dock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Replacement class">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The two oldest vessels of this class are scheduled for replacement. The Italian Navy has received the go-ahead to procure two 21,000/24,000-ton 190-metre (620 ft) long amphibious assault ships (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landing_Helicopter_Dock" title="Landing Helicopter Dock">Landing Helicopter Dock</a>), with the possibility of a third ship, configured with extensive aviation facilities (<a href="/wiki/Landing_Helicopter_Assault" title="Landing Helicopter Assault">Landing Helicopter Assault</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Improved_San_Giorgio_or_B.C3.A2timent_de_D.C3.A9barquement_et_de_Soutien_Logistique">Improved <i>San Giorgio</i> or Bâtiment de Débarquement et de Soutien Logistique</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Giorgio-class_amphibious_transport_dock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Improved San Giorgio or Bâtiment de Débarquement et de Soutien Logistique">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On July 2011 the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Algerian_Navy" title="Algerian Navy">Algerian Navy</a> placed an order with <a href="/wiki/Fincantieri" title="Fincantieri">Fincantieri</a> for an improved version of the <i>San Giorgio</i> class amphibious transport ships classified as <i>Bâtiment de Débarquement et de Soutien Logistique</i> (BDSL).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On 8 January 2014 the BDSL was launched on a barge at the Fincantieri shipyard in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Riva_Trigoso" title="Riva Trigoso">Sestri Levante</a>.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Algerian_amphibious_transport_dock_Kalaat_B%C3%A9ni_Abb%C3%A8s" title="Algerian amphibious transport dock Kalaat Béni Abbès">Kalaat Béni-Abbès</a></i> is fitted with <a href="/wiki/Aster_(missile_family)" title="Aster (missile family)">Aster 15 and Aster 30</a> behind the island superstructure, with one <a href="/wiki/OTO_Melara_76_mm" title="OTO Melara 76 mm">OTO Melara 76 mm Super Rapid</a> at the bow and with two 25mm remote weapon stations. The BDSL can accommodate three <a href="/wiki/Landing_Craft_Mechanized" title="Landing Craft Mechanized">Landing Craft Mechanized</a>, three small <a href="/wiki/LCPL" title="LCPL">Landing Craft Vehicle Personnel</a>, one large Landing Craft Personnel and two semi-rigid boats. The hangar can accommodate up to 15 armoured vehicles. The crew will consist of 150 sailors while the ship may accommodate a landing force of 440 soldiers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
