<p><i><b>The A-Team</b></i> is a 2010 American <a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action</a> <a href="/wiki/Comedy_film" title="Comedy film">comedy film</a> based on the <a href="/wiki/The_A-Team" title="The A-Team">television series of the same name</a> created by <a href="/wiki/Frank_Lupo" title="Frank Lupo">Frank Lupo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stephen_J._Cannell" title="Stephen J. Cannell">Stephen J. Cannell</a>. Co-written (with Brian Bloom and <a href="/wiki/Skip_Woods" title="Skip Woods">Skip Woods</a>) and directed by <a href="/wiki/Joe_Carnahan" title="Joe Carnahan">Joe Carnahan</a>, the film stars <a href="/wiki/Liam_Neeson" title="Liam Neeson">Liam Neeson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bradley_Cooper" title="Bradley Cooper">Bradley Cooper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quinton_Jackson" title="Quinton Jackson">Quinton Jackson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sharlto_Copley" title="Sharlto Copley">Sharlto Copley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Biel" title="Jessica Biel">Jessica Biel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Wilson_(American_actor)" title="Patrick Wilson (American actor)">Patrick Wilson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brian_Bloom" title="Brian Bloom">Brian Bloom</a>. The film tells the story of "The A-Team", a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Special_Forces" title="Special Forces">Special Forces</a> team imprisoned for a crime they did not commit, who escape and set out to clear their names. The film was produced by Stephen J. Cannell,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ridley_Scott" title="Ridley Scott">Ridley Scott</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Scott" title="Tony Scott">Tony Scott</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The film was theatrically released on June 11, 2010 by <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox" title="20th Century Fox">20th Century Fox</a>.</p>
<p>The film had been in development since the mid-1990s having gone through a number of writers and story ideas and being put on hold a number of times. Upon its release, the film received mixed reviews from critics<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metacritic_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-metacritic-7">[7]</a></sup> and underperformed at the box office making $177 million on a $110 million budget.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_A-Team_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/John_%22Hannibal%22_Smith" title='John "Hannibal" Smith'>John "Hannibal" Smith</a> (<a href="/wiki/Liam_Neeson" title="Liam Neeson">Liam Neeson</a>) is held captive in Mexico by two <a href="/wiki/Federal_Police_(Mexico)" title="Federal Police (Mexico)">Federal Police</a> officers working for renegade General Javier Tuco (<a href="/wiki/Yul_Vazquez" title="Yul Vazquez">Yul Vazquez</a>). Hannibal escapes and sets out to rescue <a href="/wiki/Templeton_Peck" title="Templeton Peck">Templeton "Faceman" Peck</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bradley_Cooper" title="Bradley Cooper">Bradley Cooper</a>), who is held captive at Tuco's ranch. Hannibal saves Face after enlisting fellow <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/75th_Ranger_Regiment" title="75th Ranger Regiment">Ranger</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B.A._Baracus" title="B.A. Baracus">B.A. Baracus</a> (<a href="/wiki/Quinton_Jackson" title="Quinton Jackson">Quinton Jackson</a>), driving to the rescue in BA's modified <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GMC_Vandura" title="GMC Vandura">GMC Vandura</a> van.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-van1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-van1-9">[9]</a></sup> Pursued by Tuco, they stop at a nearby <a href="/wiki/Army_Medical_Department_(United_States)" title="Army Medical Department (United States)">Army Hospital</a> to recruit the services of eccentric pilot <a href="/wiki/Howling_Mad_Murdock" title="Howling Mad Murdock">Howling Mad Murdock</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sharlto_Copley" title="Sharlto Copley">Sharlto Copley</a>). They flee in a medical helicopter, chased by Tuco, in a <a href="/wiki/Dogfight" title="Dogfight">dogfight</a> that leaves BA with a fear of flying. The battle ends when they lure Tuco's helicopter into American airspace, where it is shot down by a USAF F-22 Raptor for trespassing.</p>
<p>Eight years later in Iraq, Hannibal is contacted by CIA <a href="/wiki/Special_Activities_Division" title="Special Activities Division">Special Activities Division</a> operative <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agent_Lynch" title="Agent Lynch">Lynch</a> (<a href="/wiki/Patrick_Wilson_(American_actor)" title="Patrick Wilson (American actor)">Patrick Wilson</a>), who assigns them a <a href="/wiki/Black_operation" title="Black operation">black ops</a> mission to recover <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Treasury" title="United States Treasury">U.S. Treasury</a> plates and over $1 billion in cash from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iraqi_insurgency_(Iraq_War)" title="Iraqi insurgency (Iraq War)">Iraqi insurgents</a> slated to move it out of Baghdad in an armored convoy. Hannibal's commanding officer, General Morrison (<a href="/wiki/Gerald_McRaney" title="Gerald McRaney">Gerald McRaney</a>), consents to the operation but Face's former girlfriend, <a href="/wiki/Defense_Criminal_Investigative_Service" title="Defense Criminal Investigative Service">Defense Criminal Investigative Service</a> <a href="/wiki/Captain_(armed_forces)" title="Captain (armed forces)">Capt.</a> Charissa Sosa (<a href="/wiki/Jessica_Biel" title="Jessica Biel">Jessica Biel</a>), tries to discourage the team against getting the plates. The mission is successful; when the team returns to base, however, the money and Morrison's vehicle are destroyed by <a href="/wiki/List_of_The_A-Team_characters#Brock_Pike" title="List of The A-Team characters">Brock Pike</a> (<a href="/wiki/Brian_Bloom" title="Brian Bloom">Brian Bloom</a>) and his men from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Private_military_firm" title="Private military firm">private security firm</a> Black Forest. Without Morrison (the only proof that they were authorized to act), Hannibal, Face, Murdock, and BA are <a href="/wiki/Court-martial" title="Court-martial">court-martialed</a> and they are sentenced to ten years in separate prisons and <a href="/wiki/Military_discharge" title="Military discharge">dishonorably discharged</a>. Sosa also ended up court-martialed and is demoted to lieutenant.</p>
<p>Six months later, Lynch visits Hannibal in prison and tells him that Pike may be trying to sell the plates with the help of an Arab backer. Hannibal, who has been tracking Pike on his own, makes a deal with Lynch: full reinstatement and clean records for his team in return for the plates. Lynch agrees and Hannibal escapes, breaking out Face, BA, and Murdock in the process. Sosa is hot on the team's trail. The team hijacks a USAF Lockheed C-130 Hercules aircraft, which is later shot down by <a href="/wiki/General_Atomics_MQ-9_Reaper" title="General Atomics MQ-9 Reaper">Reaper</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UCAV" title="UCAV">UCAVs</a>, but not before the team parachute away in a tank stashed aboard and make it to the ground safely. The team moves to reclaim the plates and kidnap Pike's backer. It is revealed that the backer is actually General Morrison, who plotted with Lynch and Pike to steal the plates but teamed up with Pike to double-cross Lynch and fake his death. Lynch orders an airstrike to kill the team and Morrison, but the team manages to escape.</p>
<p>Hannibal arranges to meet Sosa on board a <a href="/wiki/Container_ship" title="Container ship">container ship</a> at the Los Angeles docks, saying he will hand over Morrison and the plates. Face then calls Sosa on a drop phone he planted on her at the train station, and conspires a different plan with her. It all unfolds according to plan until Pike, who is now working with Lynch, blows up the container ship and chases Face to near death. BA (having converted to Buddhism while in prison) finally gives up his pacifist ways and kills Pike, saving Face. Hannibal leads Lynch into a container with Murdock, who, wearing a covered bullet-proof helmet, is portraying Morrison. Lynch shoots at Murdock’s head, believing that he is killing Morrison, and is later tricked into admitting that he stole the plates, and is subsequently arrested by Sosa.</p>
<p>The CIA agents led by a man named "Lynch" (<a href="/wiki/Jon_Hamm" title="Jon Hamm">Jon Hamm</a>) comes and claims custody of the other Lynch. Despite their success and proving themselves innocent, the military still arrests the team for escaping from prison, also a crime; they and Sosa are angered by this, since it's only being done so Sosa's boss doesn't have to fill out paperwork. Sosa is reinstated to captain, but she promises to do all she can to set the team free and kisses Face as everybody is led into a prison van. In the van, everyone starts saying that the system has burned us again, but Hannibal tells them that there is always a way out of any situation, and turns towards Face, who smiles and says "I don't want to steal your line, boss, but... I love it when a plan comes together" and opens his mouth and reveals a handcuff key, given to him by Sosa through the kiss. The final scene includes a narration (spoken by <a href="/wiki/Corey_Burton" title="Corey Burton">Corey Burton</a>) similar to the show's opening narration.</p>
<p>In a post-credits scene, Murdock and Face of A-Team's original cast are seen.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_A-Team_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Locations_and_filming">Locations and filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_A-Team_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Locations and filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The entire film was shot at various locations in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> including <a href="/wiki/Kamloops" title="Kamloops">Kamloops</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, Cache Creek and Ashcroft,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, with much of the studio works being done at <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_North#Vancouver" title="Hollywood North">Mammoth Studios</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hollywoodinsider_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-hollywoodinsider-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vancouver-set_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-vancouver-set-16">[16]</a></sup> Other footage was included as well, such as aerial shots of <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a> (though referred to as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frankfurt_am_Main" title="Frankfurt am Main">Frankfurt</a> in the movie).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_Forces_Base_Cold_Lake" title="Canadian Forces Base Cold Lake">Canadian Forces Base Cold Lake</a> is also featured in the German escape scene where a number of base buildings and landmarks are clearly visible, as is the false canopy painted under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CF-188" title="CF-188">CF-18s</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Royal_Canadian_Air_Force" title="Royal Canadian Air Force">Royal Canadian Air Force</a> along with some USMC squadrons are the only Hornet users to have the false canopy painted on the bottom. American markings were digitally added later. The Hawaii Mars <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martin_Mars" title="Martin Mars">Martin Mars</a> water bomber, based at Sproat Lake, British Columbia, is also used in one scene of the movie to cross the Atlantic.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_A-Team_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The film had been in development since the mid-1990s, going through a number of writers and story ideas, and being put on hold a number of times. Producer Stephen J. Cannell hoped to update the setting, perhaps using the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_War" title="Gulf War">Gulf War</a> as part of the backstory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/John_Singleton" title="John Singleton">John Singleton</a> was initially assigned to direct, but in October 2008 he pulled out of the project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> When Singleton was still attached to the project as director, <a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a> was approached for the role of B.A. Baracus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>The production budget for the film was $110 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mojo_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-the-numbers_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-the-numbers-22">[22]</a></sup> but the cost came in at $100 million after tax credits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casting">Casting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_A-Team_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Casting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2009, <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> revealed that <a href="/wiki/Liam_Neeson" title="Liam Neeson">Liam Neeson</a> was in negotiations with <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox" title="20th Century Fox">20th Century Fox</a> to star as Hannibal Smith,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Bradley_Cooper" title="Bradley Cooper">Bradley Cooper</a> announced to <a href="/wiki/MTV_News" title="MTV News">MTV News</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> that he would be playing the role of Templeton Peck after he first denied the rumors saying that he was not involved and insisted that he had not seen any script.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 26, 2009, MMAjunkie.com reported that <a href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_arts" title="Mixed martial arts">mixed martial arts</a> fighter <a href="/wiki/Quinton_Jackson" title="Quinton Jackson">Quinton Jackson</a> would play the role of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B.A._Baracus" title="B.A. Baracus">B.A. Baracus</a> in the upcoming film,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> but this was later denied by a representative for Jackson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In September 2009, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Vancouver_Sun" title="The Vancouver Sun">The Vancouver Sun</a></i> suggested that Jackson has been attached to the role and was postponing his fight at <a href="/wiki/UFC_107" title="UFC 107">UFC 107</a> with <a href="/wiki/Rashad_Evans" title="Rashad Evans">Rashad Evans</a> due to filming for <i>The A-Team</i>. Filming started in Vancouver in late 2009, and Jackson's involvement was then confirmed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 15, 2009, <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> confirmed the casting of Neeson, Cooper and Jackson. They additionally reported that <a href="/wiki/Sharlto_Copley" title="Sharlto Copley">Sharlto Copley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Biel" title="Jessica Biel">Jessica Biel</a> were in final negotiations to join the cast. Copley would be playing the role of H.M. Murdock and Biel would be playing the ex-lover of Face who is a disillusioned and ruthless Army officer in charge of pursuing the team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> 20th Century Fox later confirmed that Copley and Biel were cast in the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hollywoodinsider_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-hollywoodinsider-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 30, 2009, Liam Neeson and the rest of the cast were seen filming scenes in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, as shooting got under way.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The first official pictures of Neeson, Cooper, Copley and Jackson in character were soon released, including one which features the <a href="/wiki/B._A._Baracus#GMC_van" title="B. A. Baracus">iconic van</a> in the background.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vancouver-set_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-vancouver-set-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 30, 2009, <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Schultz" title="Dwight Schultz">Dwight Schultz</a> confirmed that he had filmed a cameo scene for the movie.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> This news was followed on the November 23, 2009, that <a href="/wiki/Dirk_Benedict" title="Dirk Benedict">Dirk Benedict</a> would also make a cameo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Schultz and Benedict played Howling Mad Murdock and Templeton Peck respectively in the original series. <a href="/wiki/Mr._T" title="Mr. T">Mr. T</a>, the original BA Baracus, did not appear in the film. In an interview with <a href="/wiki/Wendy_Williams" title="Wendy Williams">Wendy Williams</a>, he said he did not like doing a cameo appearance in a film based on the original series he once did.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_A-Team_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Comics">Comics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_A-Team_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Comics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2010, it was announced a series of <a href="/wiki/Comic_book" title="Comic book">comics</a> for the movie would be released beginning in March. Written by Carnahan and <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Dixon" title="Chuck Dixon">Chuck Dixon</a>, the series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_A-Team_(comics)#The_A-Team:_War_Stories" title="The A-Team (comics)">The A-Team: War Stories</a></i> is a prequel to the film, featuring one-shots focusing each on Hannibal, Face, BA, and Murdock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> A second series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_A-Team_(comics)#The_A-Team:_Shotgun_Wedding" title="The A-Team (comics)">The A-Team: Shotgun Wedding</a></i>, is a tie-in to the film by showing an all-new adventure set after the quartet escaped. Film director Joe Carnahan and Tom Waltz collaborated to pen the series.</p>
<p>Jazwares released a line of action figures featuring the four main characters, plus the GMC Vandura.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_game">Video game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_A-Team_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Video game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>An application for the <a href="/wiki/IPhone" title="IPhone">iPhone</a> was released as part of the marketing blitz for the film. The A-Team application is a side-scrolling, third person, action shooter game. Produced by <a href="/wiki/RealNetworks" title="RealNetworks">RealNetworks</a> the game includes voice-overs from B.A. Baracus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_A-Team_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film's first trailer was released January 8, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The film's second trailer was released April 1, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> The film premiered in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> on Thursday June 3, 2010, at <a href="/wiki/Grauman%27s_Chinese_Theatre" title="Grauman's Chinese Theatre">Grauman's Chinese Theatre</a> on <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Boulevard" title="Hollywood Boulevard">Hollywood Boulevard</a>. Liam Neeson arrived in The A-Team custom <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chevrolet_G20" title="Chevrolet G20">Chevrolet G20</a> van; Bradley Cooper and Sharlto Copley rode in on a real U.S. Army tank.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> The film opened nationwide on June 11, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film premiered in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> on July 27, before going on general release the next day. The event was attended by the four team members along with Jessica Biel, and the A-Team van.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_A-Team_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film was released on December 14, 2010 on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> It was released on DVD and Blu-ray on October 27 in Australia and on November 29, 2010 in the UK. An extended cut was also released, pushing the running time to 133 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Two of the most noteworthy additions in the extended cut were the two cameo scenes of the original Face and Murdock, which were pushed back after the end credits in the original cut due to pacing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MC_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-MC-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_A-Team_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_A-Team_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film received mixed reviews from critics, who praised the action, humor and acting, mostly from Sharlto Copley, but criticized the story for being generic and predictable.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metacritic_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-metacritic-7">[7]</a></sup> Review aggregation website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> gives the film a score of 47%, based on reviews from 203 critics, with an average rating of 5.4/10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tomatoes_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-tomatoes-45">[45]</a></sup> The site's critical consensus is: "For better and for worse, Joe Carnahan's big-screen version of <i>The A-Team</i> captures the superficial, noisy spirit of the TV series."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tomatoes_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-tomatoes-45">[45]</a></sup> Another aggregator <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> calculated a weighted average score of 47 out of 100, based on 37 critics, indicating "mixed or average reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metacritic_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-metacritic-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Owen_Gleiberman" title="Owen Gleiberman">Owen Gleiberman</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> says of the film: "It's trash so compacted it glows".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Richard_Corliss" title="Richard Corliss">Richard Corliss</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">Time</a></i> magazine calls the film "the best in a mediocre line-up of summer-action flicks". He goes on to say the film lacks "a coherent plot and complex characterization", though he does note that these qualities "are irrelevant to the genre".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Peter_Travers" title="Peter Travers">Peter Travers</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> magazine calls the film, "big, loud, ludicrous and edited into visual incomprehension", but "pity the fool who lets that stand in the way of enjoying <i>The A-Team</i>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> In contrast, <a href="/wiki/Lou_Lumenick" title="Lou Lumenick">Lou Lumenick</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Post" title="New York Post">New York Post</a></i>, who titled his piece "Pity the fool who sees 'The A-Team'", is among the most critical, calling the film "overlong, overblown and utterly forgettable."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> <i>The Hollywood Reporter</i> criticizes the film's story, character development and logic, calling it "nearly writer-free",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> while The <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Petersburg_Times" title="St. Petersburg Times">St. Petersburg Times</a></i> was far more positive, calling the film "literally a blast" from start to finish, and praises it for "containing more thrills than the average shoot-em-up".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>Film critic <a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a> of The <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Sun-Times" title="Chicago Sun-Times">Chicago Sun-Times</a></i> said <i>The A-Team</i> is an incomprehensible mess, criticizing the film for being as shallow as the television series, which he describes as "punishment" when drawn out to a two-hour-long film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> Stephen Whitty of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Star-Ledger" title="The Star-Ledger">The Star-Ledger</a></i> complains the film makers remembered little more from the television series than a <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dirty_Dozen" title="The Dirty Dozen">Dirty Dozen</a></i> gimmick and compares the film to the "awful" <i><a href="/wiki/Smokin%27_Aces" title="Smokin' Aces">Smokin' Aces</a></i> by the same director.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Comments_by_original_cast">Comments by original cast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_A-Team_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Comments by original cast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Dirk_Benedict" title="Dirk Benedict">Dirk Benedict</a>, who played Templeton Peck in the TV series, spoke of regretting his cameo, stating "You'll miss me if you blink. I kind of regret doing it because it's a non-part. They wanted to be able to say, 'Oh yeah, the original cast are in it,' but we're not. It is three seconds. It's kind of insulting."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digitalspy-remake_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-digitalspy-remake-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mr._T" title="Mr. T">Mr. T</a>, the original B. A. Baracus, was offered a cameo, but turned it down.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> In a 2010 interview with <i>Script</i> magazine director Joe Carnahan claimed that Mr. T, after viewing scenes from the film, thought the final product was "the greatest thing in the world".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube-script_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-youtube-script-56">[56]</a></sup> After the premiere of the film Mr. T allegedly stated that he had become disillusioned and felt the story emphasized sex and violence, and that it was unfaithful to the original series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> An attorney for Mr. T later stated that the actor had not yet seen the film and could not comment on it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dwight Schultz, who played the TV series' "Howling Mad" Murdock, issued a statement to his official fansite that the film "pays homage to the series while it eschews its essential working premise: a band of capable military brothers for hire determined to save underdog and usually poor civilians from scum. ... The team characters are sufficiently different and, with so many roles reversed from the original, one could say they are not really derivative, save for their names." He also noted that Sharlto Copley's Murdock "is faithful to the original, but at the same time is big screen twisted and right at home with the new team."</p>
<p>In the psychiatric hospital scene, <a href="/wiki/Reginald_Barclay" title="Reginald Barclay">Reginald Barclay</a>, Schultz's character from <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_The_Next_Generation" title="Star Trek: The Next Generation">Star Trek: The Next Generation</a></i>, is credited during the opening title of a film, as is G.F. Starbuck, referencing <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_Starbuck" title="Lieutenant Starbuck">Lieutenant Starbuck</a>, Benedict's character from the original <i><a href="/wiki/Battlestar_Galactica_(1978_TV_series)" title="Battlestar Galactica (1978 TV series)">Battlestar Galactica</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_A-Team_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film fell slightly short of expectations for its opening weekend, earning $26 million, as opposed to the initially predicted $30–35 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-2">[2]</a></sup> The film opened behind <i><a href="/wiki/The_Karate_Kid_(2010_film)" title="The Karate Kid (2010 film)">The Karate Kid</a></i>, which took in $56 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mojo-weekend_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-weekend-61">[61]</a></sup> The film opened in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK" title="UK">UK</a>/<a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> on July 28, 2010, and came at No. 3 in at the box office with a first weekend haul of $5.6 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mojo_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-3">[3]</a></sup> As of August 26, 2010<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=The_A-Team_(film)&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, <i>The A-Team</i> has taken over $77 million at the U.S. box office, and $99 million internationally, for a worldwide total of over $176 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mojo_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Canceled_sequel">Canceled sequel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_A-Team_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Canceled sequel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Neeson, Cooper, Copley and Jackson originally expressed interest in doing a <a href="/wiki/Sequel" title="Sequel">sequel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> Joe Carnahan has expressed interest in directing a sequel and said it will depend on DVD and Blu-ray sales and rentals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> On March 10, 2011, Cooper stated that the film had not generated enough revenue for there to be a sequel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> This was confirmed by Liam Neeson in a webchat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> Neeson later commented in early 2012 that he understood why the film was not successful: "I watched it about two months ago and I found it a little confusing and I was in the thing. I just couldn’t figure out who was who and what’s been done to him and why, a little bit."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> Later in 2013 Carnahan said on his Twitter account "For the record guys and as much as I appreciate all the A-TEAM love. There will NOT be a sequel. It didn't make enough $$$ and that's that."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup></p>
