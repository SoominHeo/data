<p><b>San Marzano sul Sarno</b> is a town and <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Salerno" title="Province of Salerno">province of Salerno</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Campania" title="Campania">Campania</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Southern_Italy" title="Southern Italy">southern Italy</a>, situated about halfway between <i>Autostrade</i> <a href="/wiki/Autostrada_A3_(Italy)" title="Autostrada A3 (Italy)">A3</a> and <a href="/wiki/Autostrada_A30_(Italy)" title="Autostrada A30 (Italy)">A30</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Marzano_sul_Sarno&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sarno is located in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sarno_River" title="Sarno River">Sarno River</a> valley, in which traces of human presence have been found that date back to as early as the 9th-6th centuries BC. San Marzano is first mentioned in 601 AD in a letter by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Gregory_I" title="Pope Gregory I">Pope Gregory I</a>. In 1220 <a href="/wiki/Frederick_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Frederick II, Holy Roman Emperor">Frederick II of Sicily</a> gave it to the abbey of <a href="/wiki/Montevergine" title="Montevergine">Montevergine</a>, and in 1234 it was acquired by the <a href="/wiki/Filangieri" title="Filangieri">Filangieri</a> family, who held it until the <a href="/wiki/Capetian_House_of_Anjou" title="Capetian House of Anjou">Angevine</a> conquest of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Naples" title="Kingdom of Naples">Kingdom of Naples</a>. Later families who held the title of San Marzano include the Del Tufo, the Mastrili, and the Albertini.</p>
<p>The town suffered damage during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.</p>
