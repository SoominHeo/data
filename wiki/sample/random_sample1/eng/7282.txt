<p><b>San Miguel de Tucumán</b> (usually called simply <b>Tucumán</b>) is the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Tucum%C3%A1n_Province" title="Tucumán Province">Tucumán Province</a>, located in northern <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> 1,311 kilometres (815 mi) from <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>. It is the fifth-largest city of Argentina after Buenos Aires, <a href="/wiki/C%C3%B3rdoba,_Argentina" title="Córdoba, Argentina">Córdoba</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rosario" title="Rosario">Rosario</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mendoza,_Argentina" title="Mendoza, Argentina">Mendoza</a> and the most important of the northern region. The Spanish <a href="/wiki/Conquistador" title="Conquistador">Conquistador</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Diego_de_Villarroel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Diego de Villarroel (page does not exist)">Diego de Villarroel</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diego_de_Villarroel" title="es:Diego de Villarroel">es</a>)</span> founded the city in 1565 in the course of an expedition from present-day <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>. Tucumán moved to its present site in 1685.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Miguel_de_Tucum%C3%A1n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city is bordered on the north by Las Talitas (<a href="/wiki/Taf%C3%AD_Viejo,_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="Tafí Viejo, Tucumán">Tafí Viejo</a>), on the east by <a href="/wiki/Banda_del_R%C3%ADo_Sal%C3%AD" title="Banda del Río Salí">Banda del Río Salí</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alderetes_(Tucum%C3%A1n)" title="Alderetes (Tucumán)">Alderetes</a> (Cruz Alta), on the west by the city of <a href="/wiki/Yerba_Buena,_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="Yerba Buena, Tucumán">Yerba Buena</a>, and on the south by Lules.</p>
<p>The city is located on the slopes of the Aconquija mountains, the easternmost mountain range before the large <a href="/wiki/Gran_Chaco" title="Gran Chaco">Chaco</a>-<a href="/wiki/Pampas" title="Pampas">Pampean</a> flats. It is the commercial center of an irrigated area that produces large quantities of <a href="/wiki/Sugarcane" title="Sugarcane">sugarcane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">tobacco</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">fruit</a>, giving the province its nickname, the Garden of the Republic. The <a href="/wiki/National_University_of_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="National University of Tucumán">National University of Tucumán</a> (1914) and the <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas_Aquinas_University_of_the_North" title="Saint Thomas Aquinas University of the North">Saint Thomas Aquinas University of the North</a> (1965) are in the city.</p>
<p>On July 9, 1816, a <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="Congress of Tucumán">congress gathered in Tucumán</a> declared independence from <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, which did not officially recognize it until 1862. The meeting place of the congress, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_of_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="House of Tucumán">House of Tucumán</a>, has been reconstructed as a national monument. After the national government broke down in 1820, the town was capital of the short-lived <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="Republic of Tucumán">Republic of Tucumán</a>.</p>
<p>Its telephone code is 0381, and its postal codes are T4000 (Center), T4001 (North), T4002 (South) and T4003 (East).</p>

<p>The first foundation of "San Miguel de Tucumán y Nueva Tierra de Promisión" was on May 31, 1565 by Diego de Villarroel in the Campos de Ibatín, distant 60 km to the south west from the current city is located nowadays. The city was moved to "La Toma" (where the old town or <i>casco histórico</i> is placed today) in 1685, due to the low quality of Ibatín water.</p>
<p>On September 24, 1812, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="Battle of Tucumán">Battle of Tucumán</a> took place near the city, when the Spanish army coming from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alto_Per%C3%BA" title="Alto Perú">Alto Perú</a> were defeated by the army led by <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Belgrano" title="Manuel Belgrano">Manuel Belgrano</a>. Belgrano had been committed to step back to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C%C3%B3rdoba_(Argentina)" title="Córdoba (Argentina)">Córdoba</a> by the government of Buenos Aires, but the Tucumán inhabitants requested him to resist another Spanish invasion.</p>
<p>With his troops almost unarmed and tired but reinforced with local <a href="/wiki/Gaucho" title="Gaucho">gauchos</a> (self named <i>Los decididos de Tucumán</i>), Belgrano attacked the Spanish army by their backs, defeating them and ensuring the Independence of Argentina so after the battle of Tucumán, the same army led by Belgrano would achieve another victory in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salta,_Argentina" title="Salta, Argentina">Salta</a>.</p>
<p>After those battles, Belgrano established in a circle-shaped fortress known as "La Ciudadela", located 1-km from the current Plaza de la Independencia (former Plaza Mayor). Because of being a patriot barracks and located on an intermediate point between the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_de_la_Plata" title="Río de la Plata">Río de la Plata</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alto_Per%C3%BA" title="Alto Perú">Alto Perú</a> and <a href="/wiki/Santa_Cruz_de_la_Sierra" title="Santa Cruz de la Sierra">Santa Cruz de la Sierra</a>, San Miguel de Tucumán was designated as city venue for the Congress of the Independence. On July 9, 1816, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Independence_of_Argentina" title="Independence of Argentina">Independence of Argentina</a> was declared, not only of Spain but any other foreign domination. The act of the Independence was signed at the <a href="/wiki/Casa_de_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="Casa de Tucumán">Casa de Tucumán</a>, also named "Casa Histórica" or "Casa de la Independencia".</p>
<p>By 1850 the city has been increased its population considerably, overpassing the estimated registers. Because of that, in 1870 it was proposed the city was expanded, setting new limits. During those years, the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway" title="Railway">railway</a> line reached the city, built by <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a>-owned <a href="/wiki/C%C3%B3rdoba_Central_Railway" title="Córdoba Central Railway">Córdoba Central Railway</a>. The immigrants arrived to the region (most of them were Spanish, Arabian, Jews and Italians) influenced the architectural style that adapted to those new cultures, leaving the original <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Colonial_architecture" title="Spanish Colonial architecture">colonial style</a> behind. Therefore, new buildings in the city were made in <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">Neoclassical</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eclecticism_in_architecture" title="Eclecticism in architecture">eclectic</a> and pinturesque styles.</p>
<p>During the first years of 20th Century the city added 400 ha for recreational uses, therefore the first great park (similar to the existing in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> and <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>) was built. Until 1930 the city doubled its population.</p>

<p>The House of Government of Tucumán was built in <a href="/wiki/Art_Nouveau" title="Art Nouveau">Art Nouveau</a> style at the end of 19th. Century. The White Room is commonly used to receive notable people that visit the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Casa_de_Gobierno_de_Tucum.C3.A1n_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Casa_de_Gobierno_de_Tucum.C3.A1n-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the city downtown, the San Miguel de Tucumán Cathedral still preserves some colonial elements and other elements from the Italian architecture. The Basílica de San Francisco (also declared Historical Heritage), the Parroquia San roque, Basílica del Santísimo Sacramento (known as "Iglesia de Santo Domingo"), Basílica de Nuestra Señora de la Merced and the Iglesia Nuestra Señora de Lourdes are some of the most important <a href="/wiki/Church_(building)" title="Church (building)">churches</a> of the city.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Casa_de_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="Casa de Tucumán">Casa de Tucumán</a> (or "Casa de la Independencia") is the most significative building of the city so the Independence of Argentina was declared there. After the Congress of Tucumán, the house was inhabited by diverse people and the deterioration became visible durint all those years, which is clearly evident in the famous photo taken by Angel Paganelli in 1869. The <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Argentina" title="Government of Argentina">Government of Argentina</a> acquired the historic house in 1874, being destinated to serve as a post office. In that building were held some celebrations to commemorate the Independence, starting in the 1880s.</p>
<p>Nevertheless, the government did not remodel the house until 1903, when it was demolished almost completely due to its very poor condition. The only room that was preserved from demolition was the room where the Independence was declared by the congressists. In 1942 the house was completely rebuilt, based on the original plans and the picture took by Paganelli in 1869. For that purpose, the same kind of bricks, tajas and baldoas were used</p>
<p>Other notable buildings of San Miguel are the Teatro San Martín (with some elements in neoclassical style), and the Correo Central, made in a mix of styles and a tower inspired in the palaces of <a href="/wiki/Florence" title="Florence">Florence</a> (specially <a href="/wiki/Palazzo_Vecchio" title="Palazzo Vecchio">Palazzo Vecchio</a>), the old Legislature, the Palace of Justice, the Casino (former Savoy Hotel, built in 1912), the birthplace of <a href="/wiki/Nicol%C3%A1s_Avellaneda" title="Nicolás Avellaneda">Nicolás Avellaneda</a>, the Colegio Nacional Bartolomé Mitre and the Campo de las Carreras, where the battle of Tucumán took place and now an historical park.</p>

<p>For decades, San Miguel de Tucumán has been one of the cultural spots in the country, in part due to the influence of the <a href="/wiki/National_University_of_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="National University of Tucumán">National University of Tucumán</a>. It has been the birthplace and/or home of well-known personalities such as folk singer <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_Sosa" title="Mercedes Sosa">Mercedes Sosa</a>, author <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tomas_Eloy_Mart%C3%ADnez" title="Tomas Eloy Martínez">Tomas Eloy Martínez</a>, a professor at Rutgers University in the United States; musician <a href="/wiki/Miguel_%C3%81ngel_Estrella" title="Miguel Ángel Estrella">Miguel Ángel Estrella</a>, artist/architect <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tom%C3%A1s_Saraceno&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tomás Saraceno (page does not exist)">Tomás Saraceno</a>, painter <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Luis_Lobo_de_la_Vega&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Luis Lobo de la Vega (page does not exist)">Luis Lobo de la Vega</a>, and many others.</p>
<p>Two large theatres (San Martín and Alberdi) and several smaller and independent theaters offer a wide array of events, including plays, concerts, operas, and ballet, all year round. The <i>Septiembre Musical</i> is by far the most important cultural event during the year. This music festival, generally held at Independence Square, brings together several local and national artists who perform different musical styles ranging from folk music to rock.</p>
<p>Universities in the city include the public <a href="/wiki/National_University_of_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="National University of Tucumán">National University of Tucumán</a> and the <a href="/wiki/National_Technological_University" title="National Technological University">National Technological University</a>, and the private (and Catholic) <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas_Aquinas_University_of_the_North" title="Saint Thomas Aquinas University of the North">Saint Thomas Aquinas University of the North</a> and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Paul_T_University&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Saint Paul T University (page does not exist)">Saint Paul T University</a>.</p>
<p>Since August 2008, the city has been the location of trials of high-ranking former military officers charged with war crimes from the 1976–83 <a href="/wiki/National_Reorganization_Process" title="National Reorganization Process">dictatorship</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luciano_Men%C3%A9ndez" title="Luciano Menéndez">Luciano Menéndez</a>, a former colonel, was convicted for crimes against humanity, including the kidnapping and disappearance of senator (Guillermo Vargas Aignasse) on the night of the <i>golpe</i> (coup) in 1976. Many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abuelas_de_la_Plaza_de_Mayo" title="Abuelas de la Plaza de Mayo">Abuelas de la Plaza de Mayo</a> (Grandmothers of the Plaza de Mayo) have been seen in and around the Tucumán trials. The convictions of Menéndez and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ricardo_Bussi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ricardo Bussi (page does not exist)">Ricardo Bussi</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ricardo_Bussi" title="es:Ricardo Bussi">es</a>)</span> were the first of this round of prosecution of military leaders of the Jorge Rafael Videla dictatorship. Their sentencings were seen as symbolic victories for the mothers and grandmothers whose children or husbands were "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forcibly_disappeared" title="Forcibly disappeared">disappeared</a>" by the military during that dark period of Argentine history.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Miguel_de_Tucum%C3%A1n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">Association football</a> is the favorite sport in the city. San Miguel's main football clubs are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_San_Mart%C3%ADn_de_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="Club Atlético San Martín de Tucumán">Club Atlético San Martín de Tucumán</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="Club Atlético Tucumán">Club Atlético Tucumán</a>. Club Atletico San Martin is based out of San Miguel de Tucuman and was founded in 1909. This team has won four total national titles (1944, 1988, 2005, and 2007-08) and several regional titles. The longtime rival, Club Atletico Tucuman, is also based in the city of San Miguel de Tucuman but was founded in 1901. This team is the oldest football club in the Tucuman Province and the team has five national titles (1959, 2004, 2005, 2007-08, and 2008-09) and also many regional titles.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">Basketball</a> is also a popular sport, some clubs are Juan Bautista Alberdi Club, Central Córdoba Club, Belgrano Club, Villa Luján Club, Tucumán BB Club and others. Tucumán was one of two co-hosts of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1995_FIBA_Americas_Championship" title="1995 FIBA Americas Championship">1995 FIBA Americas Championship</a>.</p>
<p>The city is also a <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a> hotbed and hosts the <a href="/wiki/Uni%C3%B3n_de_Rugby_de_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="Unión de Rugby de Tucumán">Unión de Rugby de Tucumán</a>, as well as the province's two most successful clubs: <a href="/wiki/Tucum%C3%A1n_Rugby_Club" title="Tucumán Rugby Club">Tucumán Rugby Club</a> and <a href="/wiki/Universitario_Rugby_Club_de_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="Universitario Rugby Club de Tucumán">Universitario</a>. The rugby of Tucumán is the second most powerful in the Argentine, behind the Rugby of the Buenos Aires Union. For eight times, the <i>Naranjas</i> (Oranges) won the <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Argentino" title="Campeonato Argentino">Argentine Championship of Unions</a>; this is the greatest number won by a hinterland union. Other important rugby clubs of the city are <a href="/wiki/Club_Nataci%C3%B3n_y_Gimnasia" title="Club Natación y Gimnasia">Natación y Gimnasia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cardenales_Rugby_Club" title="Cardenales Rugby Club">Cardenales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tucum%C3%A1n_Lawn_Tennis_Club" title="Tucumán Lawn Tennis Club">Tucumán Lawn Tennis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Tarcos_Rugby_Club" title="Los Tarcos Rugby Club">Los Tarcos</a>, amongst others. The fans of the rugby of Tucumán are the most passionate among the Argentines.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Miguel_de_Tucum%C3%A1n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city is served by several bus lines that have routes within the city limits, and some others that connect it to the neighbouring cities of Yerba Buena, El Manantial, Tafí Viejo, Las Talitas, Banda del Río Salí, and Alderetes. San Miguel de Tucumán enjoys one of the largest bus stations in Argentina. The 30,000 m² estación central de ómnibus (opened in 1994) is the point from where hundreds of bus services arrive from and depart to almost all of the largest and mid-size cities throughout the country.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Teniente_General_Benjam%C3%ADn_Matienzo_International_Airport" title="Teniente General Benjamín Matienzo International Airport">Teniente General Benjamín Matienzo International Airport</a> (TUC/SANT) is the city's airport (though located 12 kilometres (7.5 mi) east of the city, in the neighboring department of Cruz Alta) serving over 290,000 passengers a year. There are daily flights to <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jujuy" title="Jujuy">Jujuy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santiago_del_Estero" title="Santiago del Estero">Santiago del Estero</a>, Campo Arenal, the <a href="/wiki/Campo_Arenal_Aerodrome" title="Campo Arenal Aerodrome">Minera Alumbrera</a> Gold Mine, as well as international flights to <a href="/wiki/Santa_Cruz_de_la_Sierra" title="Santa Cruz de la Sierra">Santa Cruz de la Sierra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Mauricio_Gilli_Aerodrome" title="Mauricio Gilli Aerodrome">Mauricio Gilli Aerodrome</a> is a Private Airport, located 16 kilometres (9.9 mi) west from the city, for Private Aviation. It is locally known as Aeroclub.</p>
<p>The city has also four <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_station" title="Railway station">railway stations</a>, with only the <a href="/wiki/Tucum%C3%A1n_Mitre_railway_station" title="Tucumán Mitre railway station">Mitre Railway terminus</a> operating passenger trains to <a href="/wiki/Retiro_Mitre_railway_station" title="Retiro Mitre railway station">Retiro</a> in Buenos Aires, with intermediate stops in Santiago del Estero and Santa Fe provinces amongst other stations. The other train station active is <a href="/wiki/Tucum%C3%A1n_Belgrano_railway_station" title="Tucumán Belgrano railway station">Belgrano Railway station</a>, originally built by the <a href="/wiki/C%C3%B3rdoba_Central_Railway" title="Córdoba Central Railway">Córdoba Central Railway</a> and currently operated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freight_rail_transport" title="Freight rail transport">freight</a> company <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trenes_Argentinos_Cargas_y_Log%C3%ADstica" title="Trenes Argentinos Cargas y Logística">Trenes Argentinos Cargas y Logística</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Miguel_de_Tucum%C3%A1n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>San Miguel de Tucuman lies in a transition zone between temperate climates to the south, and subtropical climates to the north. It has a <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a> (<i>Cwa</i>) under the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a>, with vastly more precipitation in the summer than in the winter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sesma1998_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sesma1998-7">[7]</a></sup> The average annual temperature is 19.3 °C (66.7 °F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IRNtucuman_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-IRNtucuman-8">[8]</a></sup> The precipitation pattern is monsoonal: out of the 966 mm (38.0 in) that fall annually, most of it falls in the summer months, while the winter months tend to be drier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IRNtucuman_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-IRNtucuman-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SMN_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-SMN-9">[9]</a></sup> The average temperature in winter is 13.6 °C (56.5 °F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sesma1998_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sesma1998-7">[7]</a></sup> July is the coldest month with a mean temperature of 12.1 °C (53.8 °F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SMN_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-SMN-9">[9]</a></sup> Frosts are uncommon, with some years recording no frosts at all.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heladas_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-heladas-10">[10]</a></sup> Usually, when frosts occur, they are light with temperatures rarely falling below −2 °C (28.4 °F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heladas_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-heladas-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ORA_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ORA-11">[11]</a></sup> Winters are sunny, averaging 9–12 clear days and 9–12 overcast days per month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SMN_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-SMN-9">[9]</a></sup> Snow is extremely rare, but in 2007, it reached the city center. There have been other episodes of sleet and snow in the mountains around the city, and in 2010, sleet was reported downtown again, a very rare event.</p>
<p>Spring and fall are transition seasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SMN_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-SMN-9">[9]</a></sup> Springs are very short, and by October, summer weather settles in the city, with highs beyond 30 °C (86.0 °F) very common. This is due to the dryness of the season: daytime highs are close to those in the summer, when rainfall and clouds are persistent, whereas spring is often sunny and arid. April marks the beginning of the fall, but temperatures remain near summer levels: 21 to 27 °C (69.8 to 80.6 °F) during the day, and 12 to 18 °C (53.6 to 64.4 °F) during the night. Rainfall decreases as fall progresses.</p>
<p>Summers are the hottest and most humid time of year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IRNtucuman_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-IRNtucuman-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SMN_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-SMN-9">[9]</a></sup> The average temperature during the summer ranges from 24 to 26 °C (75.2 to 78.8 °F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sesma1998_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Sesma1998-7">[7]</a></sup> In the summer, one can expect daytime highs ranging from 30 to 31 °C (86.0 to 87.8 °F); at night, 19 to 20 °C (66.2 to 68.0 °F) are the norm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SMN_9-5"><a href="#cite_note-SMN-9">[9]</a></sup> Much of the rainfall that the city receives occurs during the summer months and cloudy weather tends to be more common, averaging 11–13 overcast days and only 2–4 clear days per month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SMN_9-6"><a href="#cite_note-SMN-9">[9]</a></sup> Heat waves can push temperatures up to 40 to 45 °C (104.0 to 113.0 °F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IRNtucuman_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-IRNtucuman-8">[8]</a></sup> However, some relief is possible after cold fronts from the south caused by <a href="/wiki/Pampero_(wind)" title="Pampero (wind)">Pampero</a> winds which brings in cooler air.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IRNtucuman_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-IRNtucuman-8">[8]</a></sup> These winds can be strong following a hot day in advance of the cold fronts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IRNtucuman_8-5"><a href="#cite_note-IRNtucuman-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The highest temperature recorded was 45.0 °C (113.0 °F) on October 31, 2009 while the lowest temperature recorded was −2.5 °C (27.5 °F) on July 11, 2007 and on August 16, 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ORA_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-ORA-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Healthcare">Healthcare</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Miguel_de_Tucum%C3%A1n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Healthcare">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>San Miguel de Tucuman is part of the Provincial Health System (Sistema Provincial de Salud or SIPROSA). This system divided the Tucuman province into four systematic areas with San Miguel being in its own area. Each area has its own public hospitals and Health Primary Attention Centers for the people. This divisions purpose was to help regulate health care assistance across the population and make it more accessible. Since then, SIPROSA has been a part of a modernizing program with the government in trying to make a successful medical records system. This would collect data from all the different resources and compile it into one database making it a lot easier and quicker for patients to pull up medical history, medical records, or anything of that nature. This is a big step for the Tucuman region.</p>
