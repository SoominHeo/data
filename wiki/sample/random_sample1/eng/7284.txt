<p><b>San Benedetto dei Marsi</b> (<a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>: <b>Marruvium</b> or <b>Marrubium</b>; <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="grc" xml:lang="grc">Μαρούϊον</span>) is a <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Town" title="Town">town</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_L%27Aquila" title="Province of L'Aquila">province of L'Aquila</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Abruzzo" title="Abruzzo">Abruzzo</a> region of central <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. It is on the eastern shore of the dried <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lake_Fucino" title="Lake Fucino">Lake Fucino</a>, 20 kilometres (12 mi) from the remains of another ancient site, <a href="/wiki/Alba_Fucens" title="Alba Fucens">Alba Fucens</a>.</p>
<p>Near the town is the stream <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Giovenco&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Giovenco (page does not exist)">Giovenco</a>, identified as the ancient stream known as <i>Pitonius</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Benedetto_dei_Marsi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The ancient <i>Marruvium</i> was the chief city of the Italic tribe of the <a href="/wiki/Marsi" title="Marsi">Marsi</a>; <i>Marruvii</i> or <i>Marrubii</i> is another form of the name of the Marsi, and was used by <a href="/wiki/Virgil" title="Virgil">Virgil</a> as an ethnic appellation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In accordance with this, <a href="/wiki/Silius_Italicus" title="Silius Italicus">Silius Italicus</a> also describes Marruvium as deriving its name from a certain Marrus, who is evidently only an eponymous hero of the Marsi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> There is no account of Marruvium, however, previous to the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> conquest of the Marsic territory; but under the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a> it was a flourishing municipal town; it is noticed as such both by <a href="/wiki/Strabo" title="Strabo">Strabo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pliny_the_Elder" title="Pliny the Elder">Pliny</a>, and in inscriptions we find it called "splendidissima civitas Marsorum Marruvium".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was also called <b>Civitas Marsorum</b>, and, in the Middle Ages, <b>Civitas Marsicana</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It is noticed in the <a href="/wiki/Tabula_Peutingeriana" title="Tabula Peutingeriana">Tabula Peutingeriana</a>, which places it 13 Roman miles from Alba; but it was not situated on the <a href="/wiki/Via_Valeria" title="Via Valeria">Via Valeria</a>, and must have communicated with that high-road by a branch from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cerfennia" title="Cerfennia">Cerfennia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The town was an episcopal see in the Middle Ages, being destroyed in 1340 during the <a href="/wiki/Capetian_House_of_Anjou" title="Capetian House of Anjou">Angevine</a> wars for the conquest of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Naples" title="Kingdom of Naples">Kingdom of Naples</a>. In 1580 the see was moved to the neighboring town of <a href="/wiki/Pescina" title="Pescina">Pescina</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_sights">Main sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Benedetto_dei_Marsi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Main sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Considerable ruins of the ancient city still remain, including portions of its walls and the remains of an <a href="/wiki/Amphitheatre" title="Amphitheatre">amphitheatre</a>, and numerous inscriptions, as well as statues, have been discovered on the site. These ruins are situated close to the margin of the lake, about 3 kilometres (2 mi) below Pescina.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
