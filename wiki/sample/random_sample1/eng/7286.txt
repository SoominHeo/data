<p><b>San Blas-Canillejas</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Madrid" title="Districts of Madrid">district</a> at the east of <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>'s city centre. The population of the district is estimated in 149,909.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Madrid_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Madrid-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Blas-Canillejas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The municipality of <a href="/wiki/Canillejas" title="Canillejas">Canillejas</a> was one of the oldest towns in the Community of Madrid, annexed to the city in 1949. When Madrid was divided into different districts, it did not manage to recover the name of Canillejas as the name of the district, something different from what happened with other municipalities that conserved their names, such as Barajas, Vicálvaro, Carabanchel or Villaverde.</p>
