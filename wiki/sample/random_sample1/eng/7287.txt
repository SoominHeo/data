<p><b>San Vicente de Tagua Tagua</b>, or just <b>San Vicente</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chilean</a> <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_Chile" title="Communes of Chile">commune</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_in_Chile" title="List of cities in Chile">city</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cachapoal_Province" title="Cachapoal Province">Cachapoal Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/O%27Higgins_Region" title="O'Higgins Region">O'Higgins Region</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Vicente_de_Tagua_Tagua&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the 2002 <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Statistics_Institute_(Chile)" title="National Statistics Institute (Chile)">National Statistics Institute</a>, San Vicente spans an area of 475.8 km<sup>2</sup> (184 sq mi) and had 40,253 inhabitants (20,095 men and 20,158 women). Of these, 21,965 (54.6%) lived in <a href="/wiki/Urban_area" title="Urban area">urban areas</a> and 18,288 (45.4%) in <a href="/wiki/Rural_area" title="Rural area">rural areas</a>. The population grew by 14.5% (5,086 persons) between the 1992 and 2002 censuses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-INE_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-INE-2">[2]</a></sup> The 2012 census reported 44,046 inhabitants, an increase of 9.4% from 2002 to 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Censo-2012_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Censo-2012-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Vicente_de_Tagua_Tagua&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a commune, San Vicente is a third-level <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Chile" title="Administrative divisions of Chile">administrative division</a> administered by a <a href="/wiki/Municipal_council" title="Municipal council">municipal council</a>, headed by an <a href="/wiki/Alcalde" title="Alcalde">alcalde</a> who is directly elected every four years.</p>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Electoral_divisions_of_Chile" title="Electoral divisions of Chile">electoral divisions of Chile</a>, San Vicente is represented in the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_Chile" title="Chamber of Deputies of Chile">Chamber of Deputies</a> by Mrs. Alejandra Sepúlveda (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regionalist_Party_of_the_Independents" title="Regionalist Party of the Independents">PRI</a>) and Mr. Javier Macaya (<a href="/wiki/Independent_Democratic_Union" title="Independent Democratic Union">UDI</a>) as part of the 34th electoral district, (together with <a href="/wiki/San_Fernando,_Chile" title="San Fernando, Chile">San Fernando</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chimbarongo" title="Chimbarongo">Chimbarongo</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Vicente,_Chile" title="San Vicente, Chile">San Vicente</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peumo" title="Peumo">Peumo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pichidegua" title="Pichidegua">Pichidegua</a> and <a href="/wiki/Las_Cabras,_Chile" title="Las Cabras, Chile">Las Cabras</a>). The commune is represented in the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Chile" title="Senate of Chile">Senate</a> by Andrés Chadwick Piñera (UDI) and Juan Pablo Letelier Morel (<a href="/wiki/Socialist_Party_of_Chile" title="Socialist Party of Chile">PS</a>) as part of the 9th senatorial constituency (O'Higgins Region).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Archaeology">Archaeology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Vicente_de_Tagua_Tagua&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Archaeology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tagua-Tagua represents a very early <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paleo-Indian" title="Paleo-Indian">Paleo-Indian</a> archaeological site, and it is dated to 11,380 ±380 <sup>14</sup>C yr BP (before present).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>This is an ancient <a href="/wiki/Pleistocene" title="Pleistocene">pleistocene</a> site where humans butchered large animals that they hunted. The site was discovered in the 1860s.</p>
<p>An upper, younger stratum is about 1 m below the surface. The older stratum is about 2.4 m below the surface, and contains chipped stone tools. Horse and mastodon remains are represented, as well as smaller animals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
