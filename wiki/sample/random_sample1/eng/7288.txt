<p><b>San Sebastiano al Vesuvio</b> is a <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> in the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_City_of_Naples" title="Metropolitan City of Naples">Metropolitan City of Naples</a>, located on the western slopes of <a href="/wiki/Mount_Vesuvius" title="Mount Vesuvius">Mount Vesuvius</a>. Its elevation means that it is often a few degrees cooler than the neighbouring metropolis of <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a>.</p>
<p>In 1944 it was struck by the eruption of Mount Vesuvius during the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Campaign_(World_War_II)" title="Italian Campaign (World War II)">Allies' occupation</a> of southern <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, but has since been rebuilt. The main attraction is the church of <i>San Sebastiano Martire</i> from the 16th century.</p>
