<p><b>Shaanxi</b>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Postal_romanization" title="Postal romanization">formerly</a> <a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Chinese" title="Romanization of Chinese">romanized</a> as <b>Shensi</b>, is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Provinces_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Provinces of the People's Republic of China">province</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>. Officially part of the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_China" title="Northwest China">Northwest China</a> region, it lies in central China, bordering the provinces of <a href="/wiki/Shanxi" title="Shanxi">Shanxi</a> (NE, E), <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a> (E), <a href="/wiki/Hubei" title="Hubei">Hubei</a> (SE), <a href="/wiki/Chongqing" title="Chongqing">Chongqing</a> (S), <a href="/wiki/Sichuan" title="Sichuan">Sichuan</a> (SW), <a href="/wiki/Gansu" title="Gansu">Gansu</a> (W), <a href="/wiki/Ningxia" title="Ningxia">Ningxia</a> (NW), and <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia" title="Inner Mongolia">Inner Mongolia</a> (N). It covers an area of over 205,000 km<sup>2</sup> (79,151 sq mi) with about 37 million people. <a href="/wiki/Xi%27an" title="Xi'an">Xi'an</a>—which includes the sites of the former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capitals_of_China" title="Capitals of China">Chinese capitals</a> <a href="/wiki/Fenghao" title="Fenghao">Fenghao</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chang%27an" title="Chang'an">Chang'an</a>—is the provincial capital. <a href="/wiki/Xianyang" title="Xianyang">Xianyang</a>, which served as the <a href="/wiki/Qin_dynasty" title="Qin dynasty">Qin</a> capital, is located nearby. The other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prefecture_(China)" title="Prefecture (China)">prefecture</a>-level <a href="/wiki/Prefecture-level_city" title="Prefecture-level city">cities</a> into which the province is divided are <a href="/wiki/Ankang" title="Ankang">Ankang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baoji" title="Baoji">Baoji</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hanzhong" title="Hanzhong">Hanzhong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shangluo" title="Shangluo">Shangluo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tongchuan" title="Tongchuan">Tongchuan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Weinan" title="Weinan">Weinan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yan%27an" title="Yan'an">Yan'an</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yulin,_Shaanxi" title="Yulin, Shaanxi">Yulin</a>.</p>
<p>Shaanxi comprises the <a href="/wiki/Wei_River" title="Wei River">Wei Valley</a> and much of the surrounding fertile <a href="/wiki/Loess_Plateau" title="Loess Plateau">Loess Plateau</a>, stretching from the <a href="/wiki/Qin_Mountains" title="Qin Mountains">Qin Mountains</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shannan_(region)" title="Shannan (region)">Shannan</a> in the south to the <a href="/wiki/Ordos_Desert" title="Ordos Desert">Ordos Desert</a> in the north. Along with areas of adjacent <a href="/wiki/Shanxi" title="Shanxi">Shanxi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a>, it formed the cradle of Chinese civilization, with its <a href="/wiki/Guanzhong" title="Guanzhong">Guanzhong</a> region sheltering the capitals of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Zhou_dynasty" title="Western Zhou dynasty">Zhou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Qin_dynasty" title="Qin dynasty">Qin</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Han_dynasty" title="Western Han dynasty">Han</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sima_Jin" title="Sima Jin">Jin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sui_dynasty" title="Sui dynasty">Sui</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tang_dynasty" title="Tang dynasty">Tang</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Chinese_dynasties" title="List of Chinese dynasties">dynasties</a>. It does not include the full territory of the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_River" title="Yellow River">Yellow River</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Ordos_Loop" title="Ordos Loop">Ordos Loop</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Great_Wall_of_China" title="Great Wall of China">Great Wall of China</a> separating it from the grasslands and deserts of <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia" title="Inner Mongolia">Inner Mongolia</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shaanxi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Shaanxi" is an irregular <a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Chinese" title="Romanization of Chinese">romanization</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Standard_Mandarin" title="Standard Mandarin">Mandarin</a> pronunciation of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a> name <span lang="zh"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E9%99%95%E8%A5%BF" title="wikt:陕西">陕西</a></span>, meaning "[Land] West of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shan_Pass" title="Shan Pass">Shan Pass</a>". This pass in <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a>, now part of <a href="/wiki/Sanmenxia" title="Sanmenxia">Sanmenxia</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Shanzhou_District" title="Shanzhou District">Shanzhou District</a>, was considered to be the place where the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_River" title="Yellow River">Yellow River</a> left the <a href="/wiki/Loess_Plateau" title="Loess Plateau">Loess Plateau</a> and entered the <a href="/wiki/North_China_Plain" title="North China Plain">North China Plain</a>, although its exposed nature meant that the <a href="/wiki/Hangu_Pass" title="Hangu Pass">Hangu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tongguan_County" title="Tongguan County">Tong Passes</a> to its west were more strategically important.</p>
<p>Because the Mandarin pronunciation of Shaanxi and its eastern neighbor <a href="/wiki/Shanxi" title="Shanxi">Shanxi</a> differ only in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_tones" title="Chinese tones">tone</a>, their spelling in <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a> romanization differ only by tone marks (<i>Shǎnxī</i> and <i>Shānxī</i>, respectively). The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a> therefore adopted the special official spelling "Shaanxi" for occasions when such marks are omitted. The first syllable is derived from <a href="/wiki/Gwoyeu_Romatzyh" title="Gwoyeu Romatzyh">Gwoyeu Romatzyh</a> romanization, which reflects the tones of the words' vowels in their spelling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The second syllable—which would be <i>shi</i> in Gwoyeu Romatzyh<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>—is instead given its usual pinyin spelling <i>xi</i>. When tone marks are noted, it is spelled <i>Shǎnxī</i> rather than <i>Shǎanxī</i> or <i>Shaǎnxī</i>. Similarly, although syllables starting with a vowel are often noted with an apostrophe in pinyin, the form "Sha'anxi" is a <a href="/wiki/Hypercorrection" title="Hypercorrection">hypercorrection</a> which misinterprets the name as three syllables ("Sha·an·xi") rather than its actual two syllables ("Shaan·xi").</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shaanxi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Chang%27an" title="Chang'an">Chang'an</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zhou_Dynasty" title="Zhou Dynasty">Zhou Dynasty</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qin_Dynasty" title="Qin Dynasty">Qin Dynasty</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Han_Dynasty" title="Han Dynasty">Han Dynasty</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sui_Dynasty" title="Sui Dynasty">Sui Dynasty</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tang_Dynasty" title="Tang Dynasty">Tang Dynasty</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xia_dynasty" title="Xia dynasty">Xia dynasty</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lantian_Man" title="Lantian Man">Lantian Man</a></div>
<p>Shaanxi is considered one of the cradles of Chinese civilization. Thirteen feudal dynasties established their capitals in the province during a span of more than 1,100 years, from the Zhou Dynasty to the Tang Dynasty.</p>
<p>The province's principal city and current capital, <a href="/wiki/Xi%27an" title="Xi'an">Xi'an</a>, is one of the <a href="/wiki/Historical_capitals_of_China" title="Historical capitals of China">four great ancient capitals of China</a> and is the eastern terminus of the <a href="/wiki/Silk_Road" title="Silk Road">Silk Road</a>, which leads to Europe, the Arabian Peninsula and Africa.</p>
<p>Under the Han Dynasty, the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Silk_Road" title="Northern Silk Road">Northern Silk Road</a> was expanded to advance exploration and military purposes to the west. This Northern Silk Road is the northernmost of the <a href="/wiki/Silk_Road" title="Silk Road">Silk Roads</a> and is about 2,600 kilometres (1,600 mi) in length. It connected the ancient Chinese capital of Xi'an to the west over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wushao_Ling_Pass" title="Wushao Ling Pass">Wushao Ling Pass</a> to <a href="/wiki/Wuwei,_Gansu" title="Wuwei, Gansu">Wuwei</a> and emerging in <a href="/wiki/Kashgar" title="Kashgar">Kashgar</a> before linking to ancient <a href="/wiki/Parthia" title="Parthia">Parthia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Under the <a href="/wiki/Ming_dynasty" title="Ming dynasty">Ming dynasty</a>, Shaanxi was incorporated into <a href="/wiki/Gansu" title="Gansu">Gansu</a> but was again separated in the <a href="/wiki/Qing_dynasty" title="Qing dynasty">Qing dynasty</a>.</p>
<p>One of the most devastating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earthquakes" title="Earthquakes">earthquakes</a> in history occurred near <a href="/wiki/Mount_Hua" title="Mount Hua">Hua Shan</a>, in south-eastern part of Shaanxi Province on January 23, 1556, killing an estimated 830,000 people (see <a href="/wiki/1556_Shaanxi_earthquake" title="1556 Shaanxi earthquake">1556 Shaanxi earthquake</a>).</p>
<p>The end of the short-lived <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jiangxi_Soviet" title="Jiangxi Soviet">Jiangxi Soviet</a> signalled the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Long_March" title="Long March">Long March</a> by <a href="/wiki/Mao_Zedong" title="Mao Zedong">Mao Zedong</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_Communists" title="Chinese Communists">Chinese Communists</a> to the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shaanxi_Soviet&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shaanxi Soviet (page does not exist)">Shaanxi Soviet</a> at Yan'an.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-historic_site">Pre-historic site</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shaanxi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pre-historic site">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Lantian_Man" title="Lantian Man">Lantian Man</a> site, with hominin fossils of one million years ago, was found in Lantian County in northwestern Shaanxi province, near the city of Xi'an. Scientists classify Lantian Man as a subspecies of Homo erectus. The fossils are displayed at the <a href="/wiki/Shaanxi_History_Museum" title="Shaanxi History Museum">Shaanxi History Museum</a>, Xi'an, China.</p>

<p>The geography of the area is described as being part of the <a href="/wiki/Ordos_Desert" title="Ordos Desert">Ordos Desert</a> in the north along the border with <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia" title="Inner Mongolia">Inner Mongolia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Loess_Plateau" title="Loess Plateau">Loess Plateau</a> in the central part of the province, the <a href="/wiki/Qin_Mountains" title="Qin Mountains">Qin Mountains</a> (Qinling) running east to west in the south central part, and subtropical climate south of the Qinling. In between the Loess Plateau and the Qinling lies the <a href="/wiki/Wei_River" title="Wei River">Wei River</a> Valley, or <a href="/wiki/Guanzhong" title="Guanzhong">Guanzhong</a>, a cradle of early Chinese civilization.</p>
<p>Going clockwise, Shaanxi borders <a href="/wiki/Shanxi" title="Shanxi">Shanxi</a> (E, NE), <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a> (E), <a href="/wiki/Hubei" title="Hubei">Hubei</a> (SE), <a href="/wiki/Chongqing" title="Chongqing">Chongqing</a> (S), <a href="/wiki/Sichuan" title="Sichuan">Sichuan</a> (SW), <a href="/wiki/Gansu" title="Gansu">Gansu</a> (W), <a href="/wiki/Ningxia" title="Ningxia">Ningxia</a> (NW), and <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia" title="Inner Mongolia">Inner Mongolia</a> (N). In terms of number of bordering provincial-level divisions, Shaanxi ties Inner Mongolia.</p>
<p>Due to its large span in latitude, Shaanxi has a variety of climates. Under the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shanbei" title="Shanbei">northern parts</a>, including the Loess Plateau, have either a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cold_desert_climate" title="Cold desert climate">cold arid</a> (Köppen <i>BWk</i>) or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cold_semi-arid_climate" title="Cold semi-arid climate">cold semi-arid</a> (Köppen <i>BSk</i>), with cold and very dry winters, dry springs and autumns, and hot summers. The area known as Guanzhong is mostly semi-arid, though there are a few areas with a <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a> (Köppen <i>Cwa</i>), with cool to cold winters, and hot, humid summers that often see early-season heatwaves. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shannan_(region)" title="Shannan (region)">southern portion</a> is much more humid and lies in the humid subtropical zone, with more temperate winters and long, hot, humid summers. Annual mean temperature is roughly between 8 to 16 °C (46 to 61 °F), with January temperatures ranging from −11 to 3.5 °C (12.2 to 38.3 °F) and July temperatures ranging from 21 to 28 °C (70 to 82 °F).</p>
<p>Besides the provincial capital of Xi'an, other cities include: <a href="/wiki/Baoji" title="Baoji">Baoji</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hanzhong" title="Hanzhong">Hanzhong</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lintong" title="Lintong">Lintong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tongchuan" title="Tongchuan">Tongchuan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xianyang" title="Xianyang">Xianyang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yan%27an" title="Yan'an">Yan'an</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ankang" title="Ankang">Ankang</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shaanxi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_Shaanxi" title="Politics of Shaanxi">Politics of Shaanxi</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_provincial_leaders_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="List of provincial leaders of the People's Republic of China">List of provincial leaders of the People's Republic of China</a></div>
<p>The politics of Shaanxi is structured in a triple party-government system like all other governing institutions in mainland China.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Governor_of_Shaanxi" title="Governor of Shaanxi">Governor of Shaanxi</a> is the highest-ranking official in the People's Government of Shaanxi. However, in the province's dual party-government governing system, the Governor is considered to have less power than the Shaanxi <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party of China</a> Provincial Committee Secretary (中共陕西省委书记), colloquially termed the "Shaanxi <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CPC_Party_Chief" title="CPC Party Chief">CPC Party Chief</a>"; since the Governor is always ranked as the First-Deputy Secretary in the Shaanxi Communist Party of China Provincial Committee.</p>
<p>Shaanxi was established as a provincial government since <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" title="Qing Dynasty">Qing Dynasty</a>. On 10 January 1950, the People's Government of Shaanxi was established in <a href="/wiki/Xi%27an" title="Xi'an">Xi'an</a>. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ma_Minfang&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ma Minfang (page does not exist)">Ma Minfang</a> was then appointed as the first Governor of Shaanxi.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shaanxi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of the mid-19th century, Shaanxi exported animal skins, wine, liquor, and <a href="/wiki/Musk" title="Musk">musk</a>. Money loans were also common, with Shaanxi business people involved in the Guangzhou loan business. Shaanxi commonly imported European animal skins, watches, Chinese language books, and cloth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Roberts1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Roberts1-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The fossil fuel and high technology sectors compose the two largest industries in Shaanxi province. During 2009, the province ranked third in China for production of coal, natural gas and crude oil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thechinaperspective.com_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-thechinaperspective.com-12">[12]</a></sup> As the home of several of the leading universities and research institutes in Western China, Shaanxi province also plays a major role in China's burgeoning aircraft and aerospace industries, producing more than 50% of the R&amp;D and manufacturing equipment for the country's domestic commercial air industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thechinaperspective.com_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-thechinaperspective.com-12">[12]</a></sup> Nominal GDP for 2011 was 1,239 billion RMB (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Dollar" title="US Dollar">US$</a>196.7 billion) and GDP per capita was 21,729 RMB (US$3,179), ranking 17th in the PRC.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_and_technological_development_zones">Economic and technological development zones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shaanxi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Economic and technological development zones">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Baoji" title="Baoji">Baoji</a> Hi-Tech Industrial Development Zone</li>
</ul>
<p>Established in 1992, Baoji Hi-Tech Industrial Development Zone was approved as a national hi-tech zone by State Council. It has a long-term planned area of 40 km<sup>2</sup> (15 sq mi). The transportation system around the zone includes Xi'an-Xianyang International Airport and National Highway 310. Its encouraged industries are auto parts, electronics, IT, pharmaceuticals and bioengineering industries and new materials.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Shaanxi <a href="/wiki/Xi%27an" title="Xi'an">Xi'an</a> Export Processing Zone</li>
</ul>
<p>Shaanxi Xi'an Export Processing Zone (XEPZ) was approved on 21June 2002 by the State Council for its establishment and has been put into operation since 5 April 2004. As the first state-level export processing zone in northwest China, XEPZ has become one of the 7 pioneer EPZs with the function of bonded logistics in China. XEPZ is under the leadership of the Administrative Committee of Xi'an Economic and Technological Development Zone (XETDZ), which is designated by Xi'an municipal government to exercise economic and administrative power within the zone. XEPZ is a special economic zone. By now, there are more than 40 enterprises home and abroad settled in XEPZ, and the pillar industries featuring aviation, machinery, electronics and new energy have taken shape.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/West_Triangle_Economic_Zone" title="West Triangle Economic Zone">Western Triangle</a></li>
</ul>
<p>The Western Triangle is a new economic zone composing the three major city-level economies of Western China: <a href="/wiki/Xi%27an" title="Xi'an">Xi'an</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chongqing" title="Chongqing">Chongqing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chengdu" title="Chengdu">Chengdu</a>. It is believed that the addition of Xi'an to the Triangle will spur economic growth in the region and also allow the city an opportunity to capitalize on the commercial potential of its high-technology industries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thechinaperspective.com_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-thechinaperspective.com-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Xi'an Economic &amp; Technological Development Zone</li>
</ul>
<p>Established in 1993, Xi'an Economic and Technology Development Zone was approved as a national zone in 2000. The zone is only 20 minutes away from Xi'an Xianyang International Airport, and several National Highways pass through here. It has formed four pillar industries, including automotive, electronics, food, and new materials industries. So far, the zone has attracted more than 1,700 enterprises.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Xi'an High-tech Industrial Development Zone</li>
</ul>
<p>Xi'an HTDZ opened its gates in 1991. It was established as a "pivotal location" for investment by high-tech industry companies in central and northwest China. Established in 1991, Xi‘an Hi-Tech Industrial Development Zone is a national high-tech zone. The zone is surrounded by several National Highways and it is within 30 minutes of Xi'an International Airport. Furthermore, it is ranked in the top three high-tech zones in China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Xi'an Software Park</li>
</ul>
<p>Xi'an Software Park, established in December 1998, is the professional park for Xi'an to develop scale software and service outsourcing industries. The park has been appraised as a software industry base under the National Torch Program, national software industry base, national software export base, city demonstrational area of national service outsourcing base and it is one of the four parks with "double bases" of software in China currently. Xi'an Software Park assembles 90% of enterprises engaging in software and service outsourcing in Xi'an. There are nearly 780 companies, of which foreign-funded enterprises account for 170, and over 71,000 jobholders in the park by the end of 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yangling" title="Yangling">Yangling</a> Agriculture Hi-Tech Industrial Zone</li>
</ul>
<p>Yangling Agriculture Hi-Tech Industrial Zone was approved as a national-level hi-tech development zone by State Council in 1997. It is only 82 km (51 mi) from Xi'an to the east and 70 km (43 mi) from Xi'an Xianyang International Airport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<p>Nearly all the people in Shaanxi are ethnic <a href="/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Han Chinese</a>, with pockets of <a href="/wiki/Hui_people" title="Hui people">Hui</a> population in the northwestern region (adjacent to Ningxia). Shaanxi province is one of the centers of ancient Chinese civilization. The southern part of Shaanxi, known as <a href="/wiki/Guanzhong" title="Guanzhong">Guanzhong</a>, where the provincial capital Xi'an is located, is more populated compared to the northern part.</p>

<div class="legend"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color:FireBrick; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;"> </span> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_ancestral_religion" title="Chinese ancestral religion">Chinese ancestral religion</a> (7.58%)</div>
<div class="legend"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color:DodgerBlue; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;"> </span> <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> (1.57%)</div>
<div class="legend"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color:Honeydew; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;"> </span> Other religions or not religious people<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[note 2]</a></sup> (90.85%)</div>

<p>The predominant religions in Shaanxi are <a href="/wiki/Chinese_folk_religion" title="Chinese folk religion">Chinese folk religions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taoism" title="Taoism">Taoist traditions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Buddhism" title="Chinese Buddhism">Chinese Buddhism</a>. According to surveys conducted in 2007 and 2009, 7.58% of the population believes and is involved in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_ancestral_religion" title="Chinese ancestral religion">ancestor veneration</a>, while 1.57% of the population identifies as Christian.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wang2015_29-2"><a href="#cite_note-Wang2015-29">[29]</a></sup> The reports didn't give figures for other types of religion; 90.85% of the population may be either irreligious or involved in <a href="/wiki/Chinese_folk_religion" title="Chinese folk religion">worship of nature deities</a>, Buddhism, <a href="/wiki/Confucianism" title="Confucianism">Confucianism</a>, Taoism, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_salvationist_religions" title="Chinese salvationist religions">folk religious sects</a>, and small minorities of <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslims</a>.</p>
