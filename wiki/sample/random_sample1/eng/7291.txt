<p><b>Archipelago of San Andrés, Providencia and Santa Catalina</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>Archipiélago de San Andrés, Providencia y Santa Catalina</i></span>), or colloquially <b>San Andrés y Providencia</b>, is one of the <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_Colombia" title="Departments of Colombia">departments</a> of <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>. It consists of two island groups about 775 km (482 mi) northwest of mainland Colombia, and eight outlying banks and reefs. The largest island of the archipelago is called <a href="/wiki/San_Andr%C3%A9s_(island)" title="San Andrés (island)">San Andrés</a> and its capital is <a href="/wiki/San_Andr%C3%A9s,_San_Andr%C3%A9s_y_Providencia" title="San Andrés, San Andrés y Providencia">San Andrés</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s,_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name is sometimes abbreviated to "Archip. de San Andres".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The official website abbreviates it as San Andrés ("Gobernación de San Andrés").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/ISO_3166-2:CO" title="ISO 3166-2:CO">ISO 3166-2:CO</a> lists it as "San Andrés, Providencia y Santa Catalina".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Statoids lists it as "San Andrés y Providencia".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s,_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Spain formally claimed the archipelago of <a href="/wiki/San_Andr%C3%A9s_(island)" title="San Andrés (island)">San Andres</a> and <a href="/wiki/Isla_de_Providencia" title="Isla de Providencia">Providencia</a> in 1510, a few years after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Discovery_of_America" title="Discovery of America">Discovery of America</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christopher_Colombus" title="Christopher Colombus">Christopher Colombus</a>. In 1544 the territory was placed under the administration of the <a href="/wiki/Captaincy_General_of_Guatemala" title="Captaincy General of Guatemala">Captaincy General of Guatemala</a>. During the early years, Spain concentrated on exploring and colonizing the mainland, and the islands were hardly settled.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1630, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puritan" title="Puritan">Puritans</a> arrived in Providence Island, under the aegis of the <a href="/wiki/Providence_Island_Company" title="Providence Island Company">Providence Island Company</a>. These Puritans decided to settle this promising tropical island rather than cold, rocky <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a>, but the <a href="/wiki/Providence_Island_colony" title="Providence Island colony">Providence Island colony</a> did not succeed in the same way as the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Bay_Colony" title="Massachusetts Bay Colony">Massachusetts Bay Colony</a>. They established <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slave" title="Slave">slave</a>-worked plantations and engaged in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Privateering" title="Privateering">privateering</a>, which led to the capture of the colony by <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spain</a> in 1641, who had claimed it a century earlier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In the 1640s, the Puritan-controlled Commonwealth government of England tried to regain the island, but without success. In 1670, English <a href="/wiki/Buccaneer" title="Buccaneer">buccaneers</a> led by <a href="/wiki/Henry_Morgan" title="Henry Morgan">Henry Morgan</a> took over the islands, which they are connected to the British <a href="/wiki/Mosquito_Coast" title="Mosquito Coast">Mosquito Coast</a> in present-day Nicaragua. The buccaneers and other pirates controlled the islands until 1689. Later Spain regained control of the archipelago, which it held until the independence of Colombia in 1822.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1775, a Spanish lieutenant of Irish descent called Tomás O'Neil, was given military command of the islands and in 1790 named governor. He requested transferring the islands to the jurisdiction of the <a href="/wiki/Viceroyalty_of_New_Granada" title="Viceroyalty of New Granada">Viceroyalty of New Granada</a> which was granted in 1803. That year Spain assigned the islands together with the province of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Veraguas" title="Veraguas">Veraguas</a> (western <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a> and the east coast of <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a>) to the <a href="/wiki/Viceroyalty_of_New_Granada" title="Viceroyalty of New Granada">Viceroyalty of New Granada</a>. The territory was administered from the province of <a href="/wiki/Cartagena,_Colombia" title="Cartagena, Colombia">Cartagena</a>. Soon, trade links with Cartagena were greater than those with Guatemala.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On July 4, 1818, French Corsair <a href="/wiki/Louis-Michel_Aury" title="Louis-Michel Aury">Louis-Michel Aury</a>, flying the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argentine_flag" title="Argentine flag">Argentine flag</a>, captured Old Providence and St. Catherine islands with the help of 400 men and 14 ships. The island was populated by white English-speaking Protestants and their slaves. Aury and his team used the islands as their new base from which to pursue Central American independence. However, his efforts to also support Bolivar in his fight for Venezuelan and Colombian independence were repeatedly turned down.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>After the Spanish colonies became independent, the inhabitants of San Andrés, Providence and St. Catherine voluntarily adhered to the Republic of <a href="/wiki/Gran_Colombia" title="Gran Colombia">Gran Colombia</a> in 1822, who placed them under the administration of the <a href="/wiki/Magdalena_Department" title="Magdalena Department">Magdalena Department</a>. The <a href="/wiki/First_Mexican_Empire" title="First Mexican Empire">First Mexican Empire</a>, which was succeeded by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Provinces_of_Central_America" title="United Provinces of Central America">United Provinces of Central America</a> (UPCA), also claimed the islands. Gran Colombia in turn protested the UPCA's occupation of the eastern coast of Nicaragua. The UPCA broke up in 1838–1840, but Nicaragua carried on the dispute, as did Gran Colombia's successors, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_New_Granada" title="Republic of New Granada">New Granada</a> and Colombia. Colombia established a local administration (<i>intendencia</i>) in the islands in 1912.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1928, Colombia and Nicaragua signed the <a href="/wiki/Esguerra-B%C3%A1rcenas_Treaty" title="Esguerra-Bárcenas Treaty">Esguerra-Bárcenas Treaty</a>, which gave control of the islands to Colombia. However, when the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sandinista" title="Sandinista">Sandinista</a> government assumed power in the 1980s, Nicaragua repudiated the treaty. Colombia argues that the treaty's final ratification in 1930 (when U.S. forces were already on their way out) confirms its validity. Colombia and <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a> signed a <a href="/wiki/Maritime_boundary" title="Maritime boundary">maritime boundary</a> treaty in 1999 which implicitly accepts Colombian sovereignty over the islands.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2001 Nicaragua filed claims with the <a href="/wiki/International_Court_of_Justice" title="International Court of Justice">International Court of Justice</a> (ICJ) over the disputed maritime boundary, claiming 50,000 km<sup>2</sup> (19,305 sq mi) in the Caribbean, including the San Andrés and Providencia archipelagoes. Colombia responded that the ICJ has no jurisdiction over the matter, and increased its naval and police presence in the islands. Colombia also defended its claim in the ICJ. On December 13, 2007 the ICJ ruled that the islands were Colombian territory, but left the maritime border dispute unresolved. Colombia and Nicaragua will go through another trial to resolve these claims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On November 19, 2012, the International Court of Justice decided that Colombia had sovereignty over the islands.</p>
<p>The island of Providencia was hit by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Beta" title="Hurricane Beta">Hurricane Beta</a> on October 29, 2005, inflicting minor to moderate damage.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U.S._claims">U.S. claims</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s,_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: U.S. claims">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 19th century, the U.S. claimed several uninhabited locations in the area under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guano_Island_Act" title="Guano Island Act">Guano Island Act</a>, including several now claimed by Colombia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 1981, the U.S. ceded its claims to <a href="/wiki/Serrana_Bank" title="Serrana Bank">Serrana Bank</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roncador_Bank" title="Roncador Bank">Roncador Bank</a> to Colombia and abandoned its claim to <a href="/wiki/Quita_Sue%C3%B1o_Bank" title="Quita Sueño Bank">Quita Sueño Bank</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The United States still maintains claims over <a href="/wiki/Serranilla_Bank" title="Serranilla Bank">Serranilla Bank</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bajo_Nuevo_Bank" title="Bajo Nuevo Bank">Bajo Nuevo Bank</a> and considers them both to be unincorporated territories of the United States.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Declaration_of_Self_Determination">Declaration of Self Determination</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s,_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Declaration of Self Determination">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Raizal" title="Raizal">Raizal</a></div>
<p>In 1903 the local Raizal population rejected an offer from the USA to separate from Colombia in the wake of <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a>'s secession from Colombia. However, the native population of the Island soon changed their minds when the policies of successive Colombian governments tried consistently to modify the majority Raizal and British ethnic composition of the Islands through extensive migration of Spanish-speaking mainland Colombians. To add fuel to the fire, the assimilation and immigration efforts were led largely by Catholic missionaries, which did not go well, considering that the native population was Protestant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_government_and_representation">Local government and representation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s,_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Local government and representation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007_elections">2007 elections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s,_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2007 elections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A member of the departmental assembly for 15 years, Pedro Gallardo Forbes, of the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Integration_Movement" title="Regional Integration Movement">Regional Integration Movement</a> (MIR), won the governor election at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colombian_regional_election,_2007" title="Colombian regional election, 2007">October 28, 2007 election</a>, with support from the <a href="/wiki/Colombian_Conservative_Party" title="Colombian Conservative Party">Colombian Conservative Party</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Radical_Change" title="Radical Change">Radical Change</a> party. He succeeds a governor from the <a href="/wiki/Colombian_Liberal_Party" title="Colombian Liberal Party">Colombian Liberal Party</a>. He got 8,187 votes (38.93%), Aury Guerrero Bowie (Liberal Party, with support from the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Colombia_Party" title="Democratic Colombia Party">Democratic Colombia Party</a>) 8,160 votes (38.8%) and Jack Housni Jaller (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_National_Unity_Party" title="Social National Unity Party">Social National Unity Party</a>) 4,063 votes (19.3%). Only 21,991 out of 41,197 potential electors voted in the gubernatorial election.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tiempo071026_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-tiempo071026-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-archipelago071028_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-archipelago071028-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the departmental assembly, elected the same day, the 9 seats were distributed among 6 parties: 3 Liberals (Arlington Howard, Qwincy Bowie Gordon and Leroy Carol Bent Archbold), 2 MIR (Jorge Méndez and Freddy Herazo) 2 Democratic Colombia Party (former MP María Teresa Uribe Bent and former Interior Secretary Rafael Gómez Redondo), 2 SNUP (Fernando Cañon Florez and María Said Darwich), 1 Radical Change (Heber Esquivel Benitez) and 1 Conservative (Julio César Gallardo Martínez).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-archipelago071028_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-archipelago071028-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The new mayor of Providence, Janeth Archbold (<a href="/wiki/Team_Colombia" title="Team Colombia">Team Colombia</a> party), a political ally of the new governor, was elected with 1,013 votes against Liberal Mark Taylor (515 votes), SNUP Arturo Robinson (514 votes) and Conservative Peter Bent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-archipelago071028_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-archipelago071028-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s,_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Besides the San Andrés and <a href="/wiki/Isla_de_Providencia" title="Isla de Providencia">Providencia</a> island groups, there are eight <a href="/wiki/Atoll" title="Atoll">atolls</a> that belong to the Department, including submerged <a href="/wiki/Alice_Shoal" title="Alice Shoal">Alice Shoal</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Island_of_San_Andr.C3.A9s">Island of San Andrés</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s,_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Island of San Andrés">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="San_Andr.C3.A9s_Island">San Andrés Island</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s,_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: San Andrés Island">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/San_Andr%C3%A9s_(island)" title="San Andrés (island)">San Andrés (island)</a></div>
<p>This is the main island of the San Andrés group, and the largest of the Departament. It is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s%2C_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina&amp;params=12_33_N_81_43_W_&amp;title=San+Andr%C3%A9s+Island"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">12°33′N</span> <span class="longitude">81°43′W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="vcard"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">12.550°N 81.717°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">12.550; -81.717</span></span><span style="display:none">﻿ (<span class="fn org">San Andrés Island</span>)</span></span></span></a></span>. It measures 12 km in length with a width of 3 km and covers an area of 26 km². There is a tiny <a href="/wiki/Lagoon" title="Lagoon">lagoon</a> in the centre of the island called Big Pond. The principal town is San Andrés in the north of the island. Another town is San Luis on the east coast. <i>Cayo Johnny</i> (Johnny Caye) lies 1.5 km ENE of German Point (Punta Norte), the island's northern tip, and Haynes Cay about the same distance east of the island. Cotton Cay is less than 1 km south of San Andrés town, on the northeastern coast.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Cayos_de_Albuquerque_.28Cayos_de_S.W..2C_Southwest_Cays.29">Cayos de Albuquerque (Cayos de S.W., Southwest Cays)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s,_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Cayos de Albuquerque (Cayos de S.W., Southwest Cays)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This atoll is southwest of San Andrés at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s%2C_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina&amp;params=12_10_N_81_51_W_&amp;title=Cayos+de+Albuquerque"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">12°10′N</span> <span class="longitude">81°51′W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="vcard"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">12.167°N 81.850°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">12.167; -81.850</span></span><span style="display:none">﻿ (<span class="fn org">Cayos de Albuquerque</span>)</span></span></span></a></span>. It is the westernmost point of Colombia. The reef is about 7 km across. In the southern part are Cayo del Norte and Cayo del Sur. Cayo del Norte, the larger of the two, is up to 2 m high and overgrown with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palm_tree" title="Palm tree">palm trees</a> and bushes. Cayo del Sur, a few hundred metres further South, reaches a height of a little more than 1 m and is vegetated with a few bushes, and in the South with mangroves. There is a <a href="/wiki/Lighthouse" title="Lighthouse">lighthouse</a> on Cayo del Norte, at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s%2C_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina&amp;params=12_10_N_81_50_W_&amp;title=Cayo+del+Norte"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">12°10′N</span> <span class="longitude">81°50′W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="vcard"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">12.167°N 81.833°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">12.167; -81.833</span></span><span style="display:none">﻿ (<span class="fn org">Cayo del Norte</span>)</span></span></span></a></span>, operating since 1980. It is maintained by the Colombian Navy.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p>This atoll is 22 km east-south-east of San Andrés Island and 35 km northeast of Cayos de Alburquerque, at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s%2C_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina&amp;params=12_24_N_81_28_W_&amp;title=Cayos+del+Este+Sudeste"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">12°24′N</span> <span class="longitude">81°28′W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="vcard"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">12.400°N 81.467°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">12.400; -81.467</span></span><span style="display:none">﻿ (<span class="fn org">Cayos del Este Sudeste</span>)</span></span></span></a></span>. It is 14 km long and 4 km wide. There are a few sand <a href="/wiki/Cay" title="Cay">cays</a> in the southeast. The largest ones are Cayo del Este, Cayo Bolivar, West Cay, and Cayo Arena, none of which are higher than 2 metres. All cays are overgrown with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palm_tree" title="Palm tree">palm trees</a> and bushes, and surrounded by <a href="/wiki/Mangrove" title="Mangrove">mangroves</a>. There is a <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombian</a> Navy lighthouse on Cayo Bolivar. The cays are regularly visited by fishermen from the Colombian mainland and San Andrés. There are two concrete buildings on Cayo Bolivar, and a few wooden huts on the other cays.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Banks_and_Shoals">Banks and Shoals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s,_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Banks and Shoals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Colombia claims sovereignty over six additional outlying banks and shoals: <a href="/wiki/Alice_Shoal" title="Alice Shoal">Alice Shoal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bajo_Nuevo_Bank" title="Bajo Nuevo Bank">Bajo Nuevo Bank</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serranilla_Bank" title="Serranilla Bank">Serranilla Bank</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quita_Sue%C3%B1o_Bank" title="Quita Sueño Bank">Quita Sueño Bank</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serrana_Bank" title="Serrana Bank">Serrana Bank</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Roncador_Bank" title="Roncador Bank">Roncador Bank</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s,_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Departamento de San Andrés, Providencia y Santa Catalina covers a land area of 44 km<sup>2</sup> and had a Census population of 59,573. The latest official estimate for 2007 is 72,923.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Raizal" title="Raizal">Raizal</a></div>
<p>Before 1960, the population of the islands was almost entirely <a href="/wiki/Raizal" title="Raizal">Raizals</a>, who are an Afro-Caribbean group, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Protestant" title="Protestant">Protestant</a> in religion, speaking <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Andr%C3%A9s-Providencia_Creole" title="San Andrés-Providencia Creole">San Andrés-Providencia Creole</a>. There is a minority of white English-speaking Protestants of British descent. Colombia has promoted the migration of Spanish-speaking mainlanders, with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Catholic</a> missions participating since 1947. This policy seems to be an answer to growing discontent within the Raizal community that could strengthen separatist movements; a raizal majority would in this case win a pro-independence referendum but this could be neutralized by outnumbering them with mainland Colombians.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>By 2005, Raizals were only 30% of the 60,000 or more inhabitants of the islands, with the rest being mainland <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colombian_people" title="Colombian people">Colombians</a> and English-speaking whites of British descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Raizals can speak both <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s,_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Airports">Airports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s,_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Airports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The airport serves the towns of San Andrés and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=San_Luis,_San_Andr%C3%A9s&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="San Luis, San Andrés (page does not exist)">San Luis</a>, but also commercially serves the nearby island of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Providencia_Island" title="Providencia Island">Providencia Island</a>, all being major tourist and vacation spots for South and Central American tourists. <a href="/wiki/Gustavo_Rojas_Pinilla_International_Airport" title="Gustavo Rojas Pinilla International Airport">Gustavo Rojas Pinilla International Airport</a> is also known as Sesquicentenario International Airport. The airport is the 6th busiest airport in Colombia in terms of passengers, with 836,234 in 2006. Most of these passengers come from the continental part of the country, due to poor international direct service to the island. Many international tourists have to fly to one of Colombia's or Panama's largest airports (Bogotá, Medellín, Cali, Cartagena, Panama City) to be able to reach the islands. Although in recent years San Andrés has started to receive seasonal charter flights, mainly from Canada and a few Central American countries.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The airport is one of Colombia's fastest growing airports with a 13.4% increase in the number of passengers between 2005 and 2006.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
