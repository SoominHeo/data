<p><b>Sanjay Balraj Dutt</b> (born 29 July 1959) is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_film" title="Indian film">Indian film</a> actor and producer known for his work in Hindi cinema (<a href="/wiki/Bollywood" title="Bollywood">Bollywood</a>). The son of veteran Hindi film actors <a href="/wiki/Sunil_Dutt" title="Sunil Dutt">Sunil Dutt</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nargis_Dutt" title="Nargis Dutt">Nargis Dutt</a>, he made his acting debut in 1981 and has since appeared in more than 100 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hindi_film" title="Hindi film">Hindi films</a>. Although Dutt has enjoyed great success as a lead actor in genres ranging from romance to comedy, it has been the roles of gangsters, thugs and police officers in the drama and action genres that have won him much appreciation. The Indian media and audiences alike popularly refer to him as <i>Deadly Dutt</i> for his larger-than-life portrayals of such characters.</p>
<p>In a film career spanning more than 35 years, Dutt has won two <a href="/wiki/Filmfare_Awards" title="Filmfare Awards">Filmfare Awards</a>, two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IIFA_Awards" title="IIFA Awards">IIFA Awards</a>, two <a href="/wiki/Bollywood_Movie_Awards" title="Bollywood Movie Awards">Bollywood Movie Awards</a>, three <a href="/wiki/Screen_Awards" title="Screen Awards">Screen Awards</a>, three <a href="/wiki/Stardust_Awards" title="Stardust Awards">Stardust Awards</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Global_Indian_Film_Awards" title="Global Indian Film Awards">Global Indian Film Award</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Bengal_Film_Journalists%27_Association_Awards" title="Bengal Film Journalists' Association Awards">Bengal Film Journalist's Association Award</a>. He has played the lead in four films that have won national awards.</p>
<p>Dutt was arrested under the <a href="/wiki/Terrorist_and_Disruptive_Activities_(Prevention)_Act" title="Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act">Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act</a> in April 1993. Charges of terrorism were dropped but he was convicted of illegal possession of armaments. After serving his sentence with good behaviour and conduct, he was released on 25 February 2016.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanjay_Dutt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Dutt was born to Hindi cinema actors <a href="/wiki/Sunil_Dutt" title="Sunil Dutt">Sunil Dutt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nargis" title="Nargis">Nargis</a>. His mother died in 1981, shortly before his debut film's premiere; her death has been cited as the cause of his drug addictions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> As a child actor, Dutt appeared in the 1972 film <i><a href="/wiki/Reshma_Aur_Shera" title="Reshma Aur Shera">Reshma Aur Shera</a></i>, which starred his father; he appears briefly as a qawali singer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dutt married actress <a href="/wiki/Richa_Sharma_(actress)" title="Richa Sharma (actress)">Richa Sharma</a> in 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trib1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-trib1-4">[4]</a></sup> Sharma died of brain tumour in 1996. The couple have a daughter, Trishala, born in 1988, who lives in the United States with her grandparents, following a custody battle with Dutt after the death of his wife.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Dutt's second marriage was to model <a href="/wiki/Rhea_Pillai" title="Rhea Pillai">Rhea Pillai</a> in 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> They divorced in 2005. Dutt married <a href="/wiki/Manyata_Dutt" title="Manyata Dutt">Manyata</a> (born Dilnawaz Sheikh)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> in 2008 in a private ceremony in <a href="/wiki/Goa" title="Goa">Goa</a>, after two years of dating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On 21 October 2010, he became a father to twins, a boy named Shahraan and a girl named Iqra.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_career">Film career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanjay_Dutt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Film career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1981.E2.80.931997">1981–1997</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanjay_Dutt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1981–1997">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sanjay Dutt made his <a href="/wiki/Bollywood" title="Bollywood">Bollywood</a> movie debut with the box office hit <i><a href="/wiki/Rocky_(1981_film)" title="Rocky (1981 film)">Rocky</a></i> in 1981. He went on to star in <i><a href="/wiki/Vidhaata" title="Vidhaata">Vidhaata</a></i>, the highest grossing Hindi film of 1982 along with the super hit <i><a href="/wiki/Main_Awara_Hoon" title="Main Awara Hoon">Main Awara Hoon</a></i> (1983). In 1985 he shot for his first film in three years, <i>Jaan ki Baazi</i> (1985). This was Sanjay's first film post what is now referred to as his drug phase years, when personal problems and a number of films that had been completed before his departure to the USA flopped at the box office, after which he had contemplated not returning to films. <i>Jaan ki Baazi</i> marked a comeback for Dutt and he appeared in successful films throughout the 80s such as <i>Imaandaar</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Inaam_Dus_Hazaar" title="Inaam Dus Hazaar">Inaam Dus Hazaar</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Jeete_Hain_Shaan_Se" title="Jeete Hain Shaan Se">Jeete Hain Shaan Se</a></i> (1988), <i><a href="/wiki/Mardon_Wali_Baat" title="Mardon Wali Baat">Mardon Wali Baat</a></i> (1988), <i><a href="/wiki/Ilaaka" title="Ilaaka">Ilaaka</a></i> (1989), <i><a href="/wiki/Hum_Bhi_Insaan_Hain_(1989_film)" title="Hum Bhi Insaan Hain (1989 film)">Hum Bhi Insaan Hain</a></i> (1989), <i><a href="/wiki/Kanoon_Apna_Apna" title="Kanoon Apna Apna">Kanoon Apna Apna</a></i> (1989), and <i><a href="/wiki/Taaqatwar" title="Taaqatwar">Taaqatwar</a></i> (1989).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The 1986 blockbuster <i><a href="/wiki/Naam_(1986_film)" title="Naam (1986 film)">Naam (1986 film)</a></i> was Dutt's first major critical success and a turning point in his career, the film firmly cemented him amongst India's finest young mainstream leading actors as he earned praise for his portrayal of an illegal immigrant in Dubai who spirals into a life of crime. His performances in both <i><a href="/wiki/Kabzaa" title="Kabzaa">Kabzaa</a></i> (1988) and <a href="/wiki/J._P._Dutta" title="J. P. Dutta">J. P. Dutta</a>'s 1989 <i><a href="/wiki/Hathyar_(1989_film)" title="Hathyar (1989 film)">Hathyar</a></i>. were also both well received by critics although both films only managed average collections at the box office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stargold.in_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-stargold.in-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stargold.in_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-stargold.in-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In the late 1980s he was seen in a number of multi-starrers alongside actors like <a href="/wiki/Govinda" title="Govinda">Govinda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mithun_Chakraborty" title="Mithun Chakraborty">Mithun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dharmendra" title="Dharmendra">Dharmendra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Shroff" title="Jackie Shroff">Jackie Shroff</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sunny_Deol" title="Sunny Deol">Sunny Deol</a>.</p>
<p>His successes continued in the 1990s, with films that include <i><a href="/wiki/Tejaa" title="Tejaa">Tejaa</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Khatarnaak" title="Khatarnaak">Khatarnaak</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Zahreelay" title="Zahreelay">Zahreelay</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Thanedaar" title="Thanedaar">Thanedaar</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Khoon_Ka_Karz" title="Khoon Ka Karz">Khoon Ka Karz</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yalgaar_(India_film)" title="Yalgaar (India film)">Yalgaar</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Gumrah_(1993_film)" title="Gumrah (1993 film)">Gumrah</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sahibaan" title="Sahibaan">Sahibaan</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Aatish:_Feel_the_Fire" title="Aatish: Feel the Fire">Aatish: Feel the Fire</a></i>. He went on to star in some of the most era-defining Indian films of the early 1990s such as <i><a href="/wiki/Sadak" title="Sadak">Sadak</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Saajan" title="Saajan">Saajan</a></i> (for which he was nominated for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filmfare_Best_Actor_Award" title="Filmfare Best Actor Award">Filmfare Best Actor Award</a>) and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khal_Nayak" title="Khal Nayak">Khal Nayak</a></i>, for which he earned his second Filmfare Best Actor Award nomination. Three weeks before its release, however, in April 1993, he was arrested, charged with involvement in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1993_Mumbai_bomb_blasts_case" title="1993 Mumbai bomb blasts case">1993 Mumbai bombings</a>. Dutt was unable to act for the next four years due to his subsequent arrests and bails although many incomplete films released featuring Dutt during this time that all sank without a trace, the 1994 hit <i>Aatish</i> with <a href="/wiki/Aditya_Pancholi" title="Aditya Pancholi">Aditya Pancholi</a>, being the only exception, which was completed before his 1993 arrest.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1997.E2.80.93present">1997–present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanjay_Dutt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1997–present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dutt's first film after his 1993 arrest 1997's <i><a href="/wiki/Daud_(film)" title="Daud (film)">Daud</a></i> which did not manage to sustain its phenomenal opening at the box office and was declared a box office flop.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He followed this up with the 1998 <i><a href="/wiki/Dushman_(1998_film)" title="Dushman (1998 film)">Dushman</a></i> but all credit for the film's success was given to lead actress <a href="/wiki/Kajol" title="Kajol">Kajol</a>. 1999 was an excellent year for Dutt and one that is truly regarded as his comeback year, with all his five releases being amongst the highest-grossing films of that year. He began the year by starring in the Mahesh Bhatt-directed hit <i><a href="/wiki/Kartoos" title="Kartoos">Kartoos</a></i>, followed by <i><a href="/wiki/Khoobsurat_(1999_film)" title="Khoobsurat (1999 film)">Khoobsurat</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daag:_The_Fire" title="Daag: The Fire">Daag: The Fire</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Haseena_Maan_Jayegi" title="Haseena Maan Jayegi">Haseena Maan Jayegi</a></i> and the award-winning <i><a href="/wiki/Vaastav:_The_Reality" title="Vaastav: The Reality">Vaastav: The Reality</a></i>, for which he won his first Filmfare Best Actor Award.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> His role in 2000's <i><a href="/wiki/Mission_Kashmir" title="Mission Kashmir">Mission Kashmir</a></i> won him critical acclaim and a number of awards and nominations, as did his subsequent performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Kurukshetra_(2000_film)" title="Kurukshetra (2000 film)">Kurukshetra</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> As the decade went on, he continued to play lead roles in popular and critical successes such as <i><a href="/wiki/Jodi_No.1" title="Jodi No.1">Jodi No.1</a></i> (2001), <i><a href="/wiki/Pitaah" title="Pitaah">Pitaah</a></i> (2002), <i><a href="/wiki/Kaante" title="Kaante">Kaante</a></i> (2002) and the National Award-winning blockbuster <i><a href="/wiki/Munna_Bhai_M.B.B.S." title="Munna Bhai M.B.B.S.">Munna Bhai M.B.B.S.</a></i> (2003), which garnered him several awards.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Later successes came with <i><a href="/wiki/Musafir_(2004_film)" title="Musafir (2004 film)">Musafir</a></i> (2004), <i><a href="/wiki/Plan" title="Plan">Plan</a></i> (2004), <i><a href="/wiki/Parineeta_(2005_film)" title="Parineeta (2005 film)">Parineeta</a></i> (2005) and <i><a href="/wiki/Dus" title="Dus">Dus</a></i> (2005). He won critical acclaim for his performances in <i><a href="/wiki/Shabd_(film)" title="Shabd (film)">Shabd</a></i> (2005) and <i><a href="/wiki/Zinda_(film)" title="Zinda (film)">Zinda</a></i> although both films only fared averagely at the box office.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The blockbuster sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Lage_Raho_Munna_Bhai" title="Lage Raho Munna Bhai">Lage Raho Munna Bhai</a></i> was released in late 2006. He received a number of awards for his performance in the film along with an award from the Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Manmohan_Singh" title="Manmohan Singh">Manmohan Singh</a> for his work in the <i>Munna bhai</i> series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In the same year news channel <a href="/wiki/NDTV" title="NDTV">NDTV</a> named him Indian of the Year. He also sang and danced in a music video with Asha Bhosle called <i>Asha and Friends</i>, directed by S Ramachandran. Although he was going through a successful time professionally and receiving great acclaim for his performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Lage_Raho_Munna_Bhai" title="Lage Raho Munna Bhai">Lage Raho Munna Bhai</a></i>, this coincided with the start of the Mumbai bombing's trial. The nation became transfixed with Dutt's court ongoings which saw him found not guilty of terrorism related acts but jailed for short periods on two occasions between 2006–2007, as he was found guilty under the Possession of Arms Act. He was granted bail, enabling him to star in successes like <i><a href="/wiki/Dhamaal" title="Dhamaal">Dhamaal</a></i> (2007), <i><a href="/wiki/Shootout_at_Lokhandwala" title="Shootout at Lokhandwala">Shootout at Lokhandwala</a></i> (2007), <i><a href="/wiki/All_the_Best:_Fun_Begins" title="All the Best: Fun Begins">All the Best</a></i> (2009), <i><a href="/wiki/Double_Dhamaal" title="Double Dhamaal">Double Dhamaal</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/Son_of_Sardaar" title="Son of Sardaar">Son of Sardaar</a></i> (2012) and <i><a href="/wiki/Agneepath_(2012_film)" title="Agneepath (2012 film)">Agneepath</a></i> (2012) for which he won a number of awards as the psychotic villain Kaancha. His latest role was as a supporting character in the Bollywood film <i><a href="/wiki/PK_(film)" title="PK (film)">PK</a></i>, which he completed before being sentenced to imprisonment in early 2013, for the possession of illegal arms verdict handed out to him in 2006.</p>
<p>In January 2008, the Indian film Institute Filmfare listed 12 films featuring Dutt in its top 100 highest-grossing movies of all-time list. In its May 2013 edition 100 years of Indian cinema Filmfare listed three films featuring Dutt in its top 20 list of highest-grossing Hindi films of all time, adjusted for inflation these films were <i>Lage Raho Munna Bhai</i>, <i>Khalnayak</i> and <i>Saajan</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Vidhu Vinod Chopra announced on 29 September 2016 that production on the third Munna Bhai film starring Dutt in the title role will begin in 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_activities">Other activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanjay_Dutt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bigg_Boss_5">Bigg Boss 5</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanjay_Dutt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Bigg Boss 5">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dutt co-hosted the <a href="/wiki/Bigg_Boss_5" title="Bigg Boss 5">fifth session</a> of the Indian reality show <i><a href="/wiki/Bigg_Boss" title="Bigg Boss">Bigg Boss</a></i> along with <a href="/wiki/Salman_Khan" title="Salman Khan">Salman Khan</a>. The show aired on <i>Colors</i> television from 2 October 2011 to 7 January 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sanjay_Dutt.2C_Salman_to_host_Bigg_Boss_5_together_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sanjay_Dutt.2C_Salman_to_host_Bigg_Boss_5_together-15">[15]</a></sup> Dutt later said it was Khan who persuaded him to co-host the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Salman_convinced_me_for_Bigg_Boss:_Sanjay_Dutt_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Salman_convinced_me_for_Bigg_Boss:_Sanjay_Dutt-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Super_Fight_League">Super Fight League</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanjay_Dutt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Super Fight League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dutt and entrepreneur <a href="/wiki/Indian_Premier_League" title="Indian Premier League">Indian Premier League</a>-cricket team owner <a href="/wiki/Raj_Kundra" title="Raj Kundra">Raj Kundra</a> together launched India's first professionally- organised <a href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_arts" title="Mixed martial arts">mixed martial arts</a> league — the <a href="/wiki/Super_Fight_League" title="Super Fight League">Super Fight League</a> — on 16 January 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TOI01172012_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-TOI01172012-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Illegal_possession_of_arms">Illegal possession of arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanjay_Dutt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Illegal possession of arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1993.E2.80.932006">1993–2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanjay_Dutt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 1993–2006">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bombay suffered a series of serial bombings in 1993. Dutt was among several people associated with Bollywood who were accused of involvement. Dutt was accused of accepting a delivery of weapons at his house from <a href="/wiki/Abu_Salem" title="Abu Salem">Abu Salem</a> and co-accused Riyaz Siddiqui, who had also been implicated in relation to the Mumbai blasts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> It was claimed that the weapons formed a part of a large consignment of arms connected to the terrorists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibn_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-ibn-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 1993, he was arrested under the provisions of the Terrorist and Disruptive Activities Act (TADA).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibn_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-ibn-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Dutt was granted bail by the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_India" title="Supreme Court of India">Supreme Court of India</a> in October 1995 but was re-arrested in December 1995. He was again released on bail in April 1997.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The case came to court in 2006 and during the period of 2006–2007, Dutt spent seven months in <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Road_Jail" title="Arthur Road Jail">Arthur Road Jail</a> and Pune prison on three occasions for the offences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.932009">2007–2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanjay_Dutt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2007–2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Abdul Qayyum Abdul Karim Shaikh, who was thought to be a close aide of the terrorists' ringleader, <a href="/wiki/Dawood_Ibrahim" title="Dawood Ibrahim">Dawood Ibrahim</a>, was arrested.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Dutt had given Qayuum's name to the police when confessing to arms possession, saying that in September 1992 he had bought a <a href="/wiki/Pistol" title="Pistol">pistol</a> from Qayuum in <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 31 July 2007, the TADA court sentenced Dutt to six years' <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rigorous_imprisonment" title="Rigorous imprisonment">rigorous imprisonment</a> for illegal possession of weapons and cleared him of charges relating to the Mumbai blasts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibn_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-ibn-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-star_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-star-26">[26]</a></sup> According to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i>, "The actor claimed he feared for his life after the notorious "Black Friday" bombings, which were allegedly staged by Mumbai's Muslim-dominated mafia in retaliation for deadly Hindu-Muslim clashes a few months earlier. But the judge rejected this defence and also refused bail."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-star_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-star-26">[26]</a></sup> Dutt was returned to jail at Arthur Road and soon after moved to the <a href="/wiki/Yerwada_Central_Jail" title="Yerwada Central Jail">Yerwada Central Jail</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pune" title="Pune">Pune</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibn_19-3"><a href="#cite_note-ibn-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dutt appealed against the sentence<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> and was granted interim bail on 20 August 2007 until such time as the TADA court provided him with a copy of its judgement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> On 22 October 2007 Dutt was back in jail but again applied for bail. On 27 November 2007, Dutt was granted bail by the Supreme Court.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> On 21 March 2013 the Supreme Court upheld the verdict of the TADA court but shortened the sentence to five years' imprisonment. Dutt was given a month to surrender before the authorities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-As_it_happened:_I.E2.80.99ve_been_a_good_human.2C_says_Sanjay_Dutt_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-As_it_happened:_I.E2.80.99ve_been_a_good_human.2C_says_Sanjay_Dutt-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dutt has said that "I am not a politician but I belong to a political family."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> He was persuaded by a close friend to contest the 2009 <a href="/wiki/Indian_general_election,_2009" title="Indian general election, 2009">Lok Sabha elections</a> as a candidate for the <a href="/wiki/Samajwadi_Party" title="Samajwadi Party">Samajwadi Party</a> but withdrew when the court refused to suspend his conviction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> He was then appointed General Secretary of the party, leaving that post in December 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Re-imprisonment_and_release">Re-imprisonment and release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanjay_Dutt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Re-imprisonment and release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 2013 the Supreme Court upheld Dutt's five-year sentence, 18 months of which he already spent in jail during the trial.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> He was given four weeks to surrender to the authorities, the court having refused to release him on probation due to the severity of the offence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>With analysts believing that his imprisonment would have a significant impact on the fortunes of Bollywood, people from the movie business made statements of support following the ruling and some said that they would be making appeals for him to be pardoned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> On 10 May, the Supreme Court rejected Dutt's <a href="/wiki/Review_petition" title="Review petition">review petition</a> for the reconsideration of his conviction and asked him to surrender on the stipulated date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> On 13 May, the Supreme Court rejected appeals filed by two film producers who were seeking more time for the actor to surrender so that he could complete two of his under-production films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> On 14 May, Dutt withdrew the mercy plea and surrendered to the Mumbai Police on 16 May 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sanjay_Dutt_withdraws_plea.2C_set_to_surrender_before_TADA_court_tomorrow_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sanjay_Dutt_withdraws_plea.2C_set_to_surrender_before_TADA_court_tomorrow-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sanjay_Dutt_reaches_Tada_court.2C_will_surrender_shortly_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sanjay_Dutt_reaches_Tada_court.2C_will_surrender_shortly-43">[43]</a></sup> Just before the surrender, the Mumbai jail authority got an anonymous letter threatening Dutt's life. Dutt filed an appeal to allow him to surrender before Yerwada Central Jail. Later, Dutt withdrew this request too.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sanjay_Dutt_to_surrender_today.2C_jail_gets_threat_letter_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sanjay_Dutt_to_surrender_today.2C_jail_gets_threat_letter-44">[44]</a></sup> Dutt subsequently surrendered in court as ordered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sanjay_Dutt_surrenders_before_TADA_court_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sanjay_Dutt_surrenders_before_TADA_court-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HT_Sanjay_Dutt_surrenders_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-HT_Sanjay_Dutt_surrenders-46">[46]</a></sup> He was paroled from 21 December 2013. The parole was extended three times until March 2014, raising concern in Mumbai High Court and a proposal from the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Maharashtra" title="Government of Maharashtra">Government of Maharashtra</a> to amend the law of parole. He returned to Yerwada prison after his parole ended.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Dutt was out on a two weeks' furlough granted by the Yerwada Central Jail authorities on 24 December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> He said "I have lost 18kg. Now if I lose any more weight, I will vanish."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Sanjay Dutt was subsequently incarcerated in Yerwada Central Jail, to complete his jail term.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> After serving his sentence, Dutt was released from Yerwada Central Jail on 25 February 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
