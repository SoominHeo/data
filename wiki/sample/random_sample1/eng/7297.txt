<p><b>Sanjō</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">三条市</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sanjō-shi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> located in central <a href="/wiki/Niigata_Prefecture" title="Niigata Prefecture">Niigata Prefecture</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Hokuriku_region" title="Hokuriku region">Hokuriku region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of 1 June 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Sanj%C5%8D,_Niigata&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the city had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 98,492 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 228 persons per km². Its total area was 431.97 square kilometres (166.78 sq mi).</p>

<p>Sanjō is located in an inland region of north-central Niigata Prefecture. It is about 2 hours from <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> via the <a href="/wiki/J%C5%8Detsu_Shinkansen" title="Jōetsu Shinkansen">Jōetsu Shinkansen</a> or 4 hours on the <a href="/wiki/Kan-Etsu_Expressway" title="Kan-Etsu Expressway">Kan-Etsu Expressway</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hokuriku_Expressway" title="Hokuriku Expressway">Hokuriku Expressway</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Shinano_River" title="Shinano River">Shinano River</a> flows through the west of it from south to north and the Igarashi River flows through the centre of the urbanised area.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanj%C5%8D,_Niigata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of present-day Sanjō was part of ancient <a href="/wiki/Echigo_Province" title="Echigo Province">Echigo Province</a>. The modern town of Sanjō was created with the establishment of the municipalities system on April 1, 1889. It was raised to city status on January 1, 1934. Heavy rain causes extensive flooding in 1961 and in 2004. On May 1, 2005 the town of <a href="/wiki/Sakae,_Niigata" title="Sakae, Niigata">Sakae</a>, and the village of <a href="/wiki/Shitada,_Niigata" title="Shitada, Niigata">Shitada</a> (both from <a href="/wiki/Minamikanbara_District,_Niigata" title="Minamikanbara District, Niigata">Minamikanbara District</a>) were merged into Sanjō.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanj%C5%8D,_Niigata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
