<p><b>San Giovanni in Galdo</b> is a <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> (municipality) in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Campobasso" title="Province of Campobasso">Province of Campobasso</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Molise" title="Molise">Molise</a>, located about 7 kilometres (4 mi) east of <a href="/wiki/Campobasso" title="Campobasso">Campobasso</a>.</p>
<p>San Giovanni in Galdo borders the following municipalities: <a href="/wiki/Campobasso" title="Campobasso">Campobasso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Campodipietra" title="Campodipietra">Campodipietra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Campolieto" title="Campolieto">Campolieto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Matrice" title="Matrice">Matrice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monacilioni" title="Monacilioni">Monacilioni</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toro,_Italy" title="Toro, Italy">Toro</a>. San Giovanni in Galdo is home to many families that emigrated to America in the early 1900s. Families with the surnames of Trotta, DeMaioribus, Marino, Santone and Di'Orio.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Italic_sanctuary">Italic sanctuary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Giovanni_in_Galdo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Italic sanctuary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>San Giovanni in Galdo is the site of a <a href="/wiki/Samnites" title="Samnites">Samnitic</a> sanctuary atop Colle Rimontato (709 meters a.s.l.).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Excavated between 1974 and 1976, the sanctuary took the form of a frontal Italic podium temple surrounded by a portico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The ritual activity at the sanctuary was confine to a limited area, likely indicating that the temple served a discrete, local population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stek2009_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stek2009-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
