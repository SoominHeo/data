<p>The <b>A1</b> (<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt"><i>Auto-estrada do Norte</i></span>) is the principal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motorway" title="Motorway">motorway</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freeway" title="Freeway">freeway</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>. It connects <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capital_(political)" title="Capital (political)">capital</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Porto" title="Porto">Porto</a>, the country's second-largest city, and serves other important cities like <a href="/wiki/Santar%C3%A9m,_Portugal" title="Santarém, Portugal">Santarém</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leiria" title="Leiria">Leiria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coimbra" title="Coimbra">Coimbra</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aveiro,_Portugal" title="Aveiro, Portugal">Aveiro</a>. A1 is operated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brisa_-_Auto-estradas_de_Portugal" title="Brisa - Auto-estradas de Portugal">Brisa - Auto-estradas de Portugal</a>, S.A.</p>
<p>Construction on A1 began in 1961 and was completed in 1991. It extends 303 km, comprising 26 <a href="/wiki/Interchange_(road)" title="Interchange (road)">interchanges</a>, 7 <a href="/wiki/Rest_area" title="Rest area">service areas</a> and 2 leisure rest areas (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F%C3%A1tima" title="Fátima">Fátima</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Oi%C3%A3&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Oiã (page does not exist)">Oiã</a>).</p>
