<p><b>Santa Luċija</b> is a village in the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Region,_Malta" title="Southern Region, Malta">Southern Region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, with a population of 2997 as of March 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is one of the modern towns developed in Malta during the 20th century. By virtue of an article which appeared in Government Gazette of 7 July 1961 the area between <a href="/wiki/Tal-Barrani" title="Tal-Barrani">Tal-Barrani</a> (<a href="/wiki/Tarxien" title="Tarxien">Tarxien</a> By-Pass) and <a href="/wiki/Luqa" title="Luqa">Luqa</a> By-Pass. Santa Luċija was named after a 16th-century chapel located in the vicinity. The Parish Church is dedicated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pius_X" title="Pius X">Pius X</a>. It is home of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Garden_of_Serenity" title="Chinese Garden of Serenity">Chinese Garden of Serenity</a> (a Chinese-built public garden). Located there is a <a href="/wiki/Hypogeum" title="Hypogeum">hypogeum</a> which was discovered in 1973.</p>
