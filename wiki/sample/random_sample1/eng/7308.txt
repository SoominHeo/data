<p><b>Province of Santa Cruz de Tenerife</b> also <b>Province of Santa Cruz</b> (Spanish: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><b>Provincia de Santa Cruz de Tenerife</b></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Spain" title="Provinces of Spain">province of Spain</a>, consisting of the western part of the <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_communities_of_Spain" title="Autonomous communities of Spain">autonomous community</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>. It consists of about half of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a> archipelago: the islands of <a href="/wiki/Tenerife" title="Tenerife">Tenerife</a>, <a href="/wiki/La_Gomera" title="La Gomera">La Gomera</a>, <a href="/wiki/El_Hierro" title="El Hierro">El Hierro</a>, and <a href="/wiki/La_Palma" title="La Palma">La Palma</a>. It occupies an area of 3,381 km².</p>
<p>Its capital is the city of <a href="/wiki/Santa_Cruz_de_Tenerife" title="Santa Cruz de Tenerife">Santa Cruz de Tenerife</a> (commonly known as <i>Santa Cruz</i>), on the island of Tenerife (Spain's most populous island). In 2008 the province had 1,005,936 inhabitants and a density of 313.57 /km², making it the province of Spain with the sixth highest population density, higher than that of the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Las_Palmas" title="Province of Las Palmas">province of Las Palmas</a> (the eastern half of the Canary Islands). 24% live in the capital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Santa Cruz de Tenerife is the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Autonomous Community of the Canary Islands</a>. There are 54 municipalities in the province; see <a href="/wiki/List_of_municipalities_in_Santa_Cruz_de_Tenerife" title="List of municipalities in Santa Cruz de Tenerife">List of municipalities in Santa Cruz de Tenerife</a>. Tenerife is the most populated island in the province of the Canary Islands and most populous island of Spain. The island of Tenerife has the highest altitude of Spain (<a href="/wiki/Teide" title="Teide">Teide</a> 3718 m).</p>
<p>Earlier issued vehicle license plates in this province bear the first two letters "TF" (named after Tenerife). Nowadays the plates share the same numbering <a href="/wiki/Vehicle_registration_plates_of_Spain" title="Vehicle registration plates of Spain">system</a> as in mainland Spain.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Province_of_Santa_Cruz_de_Tenerife&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This province was established in 1927, when the Canarias province (with <a href="/wiki/Santa_Cruz_de_Tenerife" title="Santa Cruz de Tenerife">Santa Cruz de Tenerife</a> as the capital city) was divided into two provinces: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Las_Palmas_(province)" title="Las Palmas (province)">Las Palmas</a> and the province of Santa Cruz de Tenerife.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_parks">National parks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Province_of_Santa_Cruz_de_Tenerife&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: National parks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This province contains three of <a href="/wiki/List_of_national_parks_of_Spain" title="List of national parks of Spain">Spain's national parks</a>, more than any other province: the <a href="/wiki/Caldera_de_Taburiente_National_Park" title="Caldera de Taburiente National Park">Caldera de Taburiente National Park</a> on La Palma, the <a href="/wiki/Garajonay_National_Park" title="Garajonay National Park">Garajonay National Park</a> on La Gomera, and the <a href="/wiki/Teide_National_Park" title="Teide National Park">Teide National Park</a> on Tenerife, encompassing <a href="/wiki/Teide" title="Teide">Teide</a>, Spain's highest mountain and also an inactive <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcano</a>.</p>

<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mount_Teide" title="Mount Teide">Mount Teide</a>: is a volcano on Tenerife in the Canary Islands. Its 3,718-metre (12,198-foot)-high summit is the highest point in Spain, the highest point above sea level in the islands of the Atlantic, and it is the third highest volcano in the world measured from its base on the ocean floor, after <a href="/wiki/Mauna_Loa" title="Mauna Loa">Mauna Loa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mauna_Kea" title="Mauna Kea">Mauna Kea</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The volcano and its surroundings comprise the <a href="/wiki/Teide_National_Park" title="Teide National Park">Teide National Park</a>, is also one of the most visited National Parks in the world, with a total of 2.8 million visitors, according to the Instituto Canario de Estadística (ISTAC). The park has an area of 18,900 hectares (73 sq mi) and was named a <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a> by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> on 29 June 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unesco_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-unesco-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Auditorio_de_Tenerife" title="Auditorio de Tenerife">Auditorio de Tenerife</a>: Was designed by architect <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santiago_Calatrava_Valls" title="Santiago Calatrava Valls">Santiago Calatrava Valls</a>, construction began in 1997 and was completed in 2003. The auditorium was inaugurated on 26 September of that year with the presence of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Felipe_de_Borb%C3%B3n" title="Felipe de Borbón">Felipe de Borbón</a>, Prince of Asturias, and was later visited by former U.S. President <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a>. The building is framed within the tenets of late-modern <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">architecture</a> of the late 20th century. The majestic profile of the <a href="/wiki/Auditorium" title="Auditorium">auditorium</a> has become an architectural symbol of the city of <a href="/wiki/Santa_Cruz_de_Tenerife" title="Santa Cruz de Tenerife">Santa Cruz de Tenerife</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Auditorio_de_Tenerife_in_Spanish_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Auditorio_de_Tenerife_in_Spanish-5">[5]</a></sup> It is also regarded as the finest modern building in the Canary Islands<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and one of the most emblematic buildings of <a href="/wiki/Spanish_architecture" title="Spanish architecture">Spanish architecture</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Basilica_of_Candelaria" title="Basilica of Candelaria">Basilica of Our Lady of Candelaria</a> (in <a href="/wiki/Candelaria,_Tenerife" title="Candelaria, Tenerife">Candelaria</a>): The place where the image of the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_of_Candelaria" title="Virgin of Candelaria">Virgin of Candelaria</a> (saint patron of the Canary Islands) can be found, this sanctuary is built in neoclassical style, and is visited daily by the parishioners, who visit the Villa Mariana out of devotion to the Virgin. Is the first Marian shrine of the Canary Islands, the principal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catholic" title="Catholic">catholic</a> center of peregrination of the Canary Islands<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and one of the principal ones of Spain, the basilica hosts more than 2,5 million visitors annually.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/San_Crist%C3%B3bal_de_La_Laguna" title="San Cristóbal de La Laguna">San Cristóbal de La Laguna</a>: Is third-most populous city of the archipelago and second-most populous city of the island. Its economy is business-oriented while agriculture dominates the northeastern portion of the city. The urban area dominates the central and the southern parts. Tourism covers the northern coast. The main industry includes some manufacturing. La Laguna historical center was declared <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a> by the <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> in 1999. In 2010 after a survey, La Laguna was listed as the city with the best reputation in the Canary Islands and the third no provincial capital city of Spain with the best reputation, but behind <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gijon" title="Gijon">Gijon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marbella" title="Marbella">Marbella</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Museo_de_la_Naturaleza_y_el_Hombre" title="Museo de la Naturaleza y el Hombre">Museo de la Naturaleza y el Hombre</a>: Is a <a href="/wiki/Museum" title="Museum">museum</a> based in Santa Cruz de Tenerife, it contains many significant archaeological finds and is considered the best repository of objects from the Prehispanic Canary Islands. The museum also houses significant <a href="/wiki/Paleontology" title="Paleontology">paleontological</a>, <a href="/wiki/Botany" title="Botany">botanical</a>, <a href="/wiki/Entomology" title="Entomology">entomological</a> and marine and terrestrial vertebrate collections, in an excellent state of preservation, and is considered the best Natural Library of the Canary Islands. The museum also integrates the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archaeological" title="Archaeological">Archaeological</a> Museum of Tenerife, the Bioantropología's Canary Institute and the Museum of Natural Sciences of Tenerife. The museum is located in the downtown area of Santa Cruz, in the former Civil Hospital, a <a href="/wiki/Building" title="Building">building</a> that constitutes an example of the <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">neoclassical architecture</a> of Canary Islands. The archaeological section was founded in 1958. The museum holds the largest collection on the culture of the <a href="/wiki/Guanches" title="Guanches">Guanche</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and also has one of the most modern methods of presentation of mummies, (announced in 2006 by the <a href="/wiki/Cabildo_de_Tenerife" title="Cabildo de Tenerife">Cabildo de Tenerife</a> through a communique).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> It is also an internationally renowned museum and has participated in international meetings on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archeology" title="Archeology">archeology</a>, but its fame is mainly due to its formidable collection of <a href="/wiki/Guanche_mummies" title="Guanche mummies">Guanche mummies</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Los_Cristianos" title="Los Cristianos">Los Cristianos</a>: Is a town situated on the south coast of Tenerife. Located in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Arona,_Tenerife" title="Arona, Tenerife">Arona</a> between the cone of the mountain Chayofita and the greater mountain Guaza. The town centre is around the Los Cristianos bay, but is rapidly expanding inland with modern development. The town is a popular tourist resort and includes a ferry port.</li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cathedral_of_La_Laguna" title="Cathedral of La Laguna">Cathedral of San Cristóbal de La Laguna</a>: Is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catholic" title="Catholic">Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Cathedral" title="Cathedral">cathedral</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tenerife" title="Tenerife">Tenerife</a>, Spain. Begun in 1904 and completed in 1915, it is dedicated to the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_of_Los_Remedios" title="Virgin of Los Remedios">Virgin of Los Remedios</a>. This is one of the most important temples of the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Located in the city of <a href="/wiki/San_Crist%C3%B3bal_de_La_Laguna" title="San Cristóbal de La Laguna">San Cristóbal de La Laguna</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Puerto_de_la_Cruz" title="Puerto de la Cruz">Puerto de la Cruz</a>: Is a city and municipality located on the north coast of Tenerife, in the <a href="/wiki/Orotava_Valley" title="Orotava Valley">Orotava Valley</a>. Prior to the development of hotels and buildings, much of the area consisted of agricultural land. Considerable fiscal pressure led to the land being developed and the population shifted from rural to urban and tourism. The population is mainly urban today.</li>
</ul>
