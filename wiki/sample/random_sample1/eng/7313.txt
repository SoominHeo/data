<p><b>Santo Amaro da Imperatriz</b>, or simply Santo Amaro, is a <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Brazil" title="Municipalities of Brazil">municipality</a> in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Catarina_(state)" title="Santa Catarina (state)">Santa Catarina</a> located close to the state capital <a href="/wiki/Florian%C3%B3polis" title="Florianópolis">Florianópolis</a> and is connected to <a href="/wiki/Palho%C3%A7a" title="Palhoça">Palhoça</a> by <a href="/wiki/BR-282" title="BR-282">BR-282</a> in the northeast.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santo_Amaro_da_Imperatriz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The municipality contains part of the 84,130 hectares (207,900 acres) <a href="/wiki/Serra_do_Tabuleiro_State_Park" title="Serra do Tabuleiro State Park">Serra do Tabuleiro State Park</a>, a protected area created in 1975. The lushly-forested park protects the sources of the <a href="/wiki/Vargem_do_Bra%C3%A7o_River" title="Vargem do Braço River">Vargem do Braço</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cubat%C3%A3o_River_(south_Santa_Catarina)" title="Cubatão River (south Santa Catarina)">Cubatão</a> and <a href="/wiki/D%27Una_River" title="D'Una River">D'Una</a> rivers, which supply most of the drinking water for greater Florianópolis and the south coast region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> There are several farms in the municipality. The only urban zone is situated in <a href="/wiki/Downtown" title="Downtown">Downtown</a> Santo Amaro.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Parties_and_festivals">Parties and festivals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santo_Amaro_da_Imperatriz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Parties and festivals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <b>Festa do Divino Espírito Santo</b> (Divine Holy Spirit Party) is the main religious celebration of the Florianópolis Region. <b>Festa do Milho</b> (Corn Festival) is another important festival of Santo Amaro.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santo_Amaro_da_Imperatriz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Even though Santo Amaro is a small city, important enterprises were founded in the city. <b>Imperatriz Supermarkets</b> was founded in 1974 and is the most profitable supermarket of Santa Catarina. <b>Rosa Supermarkets</b> is also another important supermarkets network.</p>
<p><b>Auto Viação Imperatriz</b> is the bus enterprise that drives people to <a href="/wiki/Palho%C3%A7a" title="Palhoça">Palhoça</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Jos%C3%A9,_Santa_Catarina" title="São José, Santa Catarina">São José</a> and <a href="/wiki/Florian%C3%B3polis" title="Florianópolis">Florianópolis</a>. <b>Imperatriz</b> is also another important enterprise that manufactures <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ice-cream" title="Ice-cream">ice-cream</a>.</p>
<p>As Santo Amaro is mainly a rural city, their economy is based on it.</p>
