<p><b>Santo André</b> (<small>Portuguese pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">[sɐ̃twɐ̃ˈdɾɛ]</a></span>, <i>Saint <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Andrew" title="Saint Andrew">Andrew</a></i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Brazil" title="Municipalities of Brazil">municipality</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_area" title="Metropolitan area">Metropolitan</a> Region of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>. It is part of a group of municipalities known as <a href="/wiki/ABC_Region" title="ABC Region">Greater ABC Region</a>. The population is 710,210 (2015 est.) in an area of 175.8 km².<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibge_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ibge-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santo_Andr%C3%A9,_S%C3%A3o_Paulo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The settlement, which became a town in 1553, with the name of Santo André da Borda do Campo, experienced rapid growth beginning in the 1930s. It was originally named São Bernardo because the municipality district headquarters were in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Bernardo_do_Campo" title="São Bernardo do Campo">São Bernardo do Campo</a>, now a city nearby. In 1938, the name was changed to Santo André, as the district government was transferred to Santo André. Industries include chemical engineering, textiles, oil, metal products, metallurgy and printed matter. It is an industrial city, but more than 60% of Santo André's total area are protected by environmental water laws.</p>
<p>In 1867, a railway named the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Railway_Co.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="São Paulo Railway Co. (page does not exist)">São Paulo Railway Co.</a> or the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Estrada_de_Ferro_Santos_Jundia%C3%AD" title="Estrada de Ferro Santos Jundiaí">Estrada de Ferro Santos Jundiaí</a>, made it easier to be reached. In 1954, it became the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Santo_Andr%C3%A9" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Santo André">Roman Catholic Diocese of Santo André</a>.</p>
<p>In 2002 the city shot to national prominence with the assassination of serving mayor <a href="/wiki/Celso_Daniel" title="Celso Daniel">Celso Daniel</a>, whose murder remains unsolved.</p>
