<p><b>Santiago Rodríguez</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[sanˈtjaɣo ɾoˈðɾiɣes]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_the_Dominican_Republic" title="Provinces of the Dominican Republic">province</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>. It was split from <a href="/wiki/Monte_Cristi_Province" title="Monte Cristi Province">Monte Cristi</a> in 1948.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santiago_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Santiago Rodríguez province has the <a href="/wiki/Monte_Cristi_Province" title="Monte Cristi Province">Monte Cristi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Valverde_Province" title="Valverde Province">Valverde</a> provinces to the north, the <a href="/wiki/Santiago_Province_(Dominican_Republic)" title="Santiago Province (Dominican Republic)">Santiago</a> province to the east, the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Province_(Dominican_Republic)" title="San Juan Province (Dominican Republic)">San Juan</a> and <a href="/wiki/El%C3%ADas_Pi%C3%B1a_Province" title="Elías Piña Province">Elías Piña</a> provinces to the south and the <a href="/wiki/Dajab%C3%B3n_Province" title="Dajabón Province">Dajabón</a> province to the west.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin_of_name">Origin of name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santiago_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origin of name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Santiago_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Masag%C3%B3" title="Santiago Rodríguez Masagó">Santiago Rodríguez</a> was an officer of the Dominican army in the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_War_of_Independence" title="Dominican War of Independence">Dominican War of Independence</a>. He was one of the founders of the city of Sabaneta and an important military leader during the initial stages of the <i>Restoration War</i> (1863–1865)</p>

<p>For comparison with the municipalities and municipal districts of other provinces see the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_municipalities_and_municipal_districts_of_the_Dominican_Republic" title="List of municipalities and municipal districts of the Dominican Republic">list of municipalities and municipal districts of the Dominican Republic</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santiago_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cordillera_Central,_Hispaniola&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cordillera Central, Hispaniola (page does not exist)">Cordillera Central</a> ("Central mountain chain") is found in the southern part of the province, and the Sierra Samba (a chain of low <a href="/wiki/Hill" title="Hill">hills</a>) runs across the northern half.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santiago_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The climate of the province is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical" title="Tropical">tropical</a> climate, hot most of the year, but it is cooler on the mountains.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rivers">Rivers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santiago_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Rivers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The main rivers are Guayubín and Mao, both <a href="/wiki/Tributary" title="Tributary">tributaries</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Yaque_del_Norte_River" title="Yaque del Norte River">Yaque del Norte</a> river.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santiago_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The only economic activity of importance is <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a>.</p>
<p>Tourist Attractions</p>
<p>Santiago Rodriguez is a province of the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a> that has interesting tourist attraction, located mainly in the mountains. San Ignacio de Sabaneta, the main town is a beautiful and clean city, one of the nicest places of the Dominican Republic. The town has several parks, boulevards and a quiet atmosphere.</p>
<p>Just a few miles away,the visitor is going to find hills,mountains and one of the wildest places of the caribbean nature,that is <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Armando_Bermudez&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Armando Bermudez (page does not exist)">Armando Bermudez</a> National Park. This national park has rivers,mountain streams,and great variety of plants, birds and wildlife. One of the biggest attractions are the interminable pine trees, the largest stronghold of primary forest in the country. Next to this park is Moncion Damn or Presa de Moncion, an artificial lake surrounded by hills and mountains. Its deep blue waters are rich in fish.</p>
<p>One of the most exciting places is La Peonía, a coffee community located a few miles to the south of Villa Los Almacigos. La peonia is the camping to those to want to explore <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nalga_de_Maco&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nalga de Maco (page does not exist)">Nalga de Maco</a> National Park, a 6,000 mountain peak with a foggy summit that has the only Elfin Forest in the island. This misty place is draped with bromeliads, mosses and orchids.</p>
