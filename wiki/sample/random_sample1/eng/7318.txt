<p><b>San Pablo</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chilean</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_towns_in_Chile" title="List of towns in Chile">town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_Chile" title="Communes of Chile">commune</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Osorno_Province" title="Osorno Province">Osorno Province</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Los_Lagos_Region" title="Los Lagos Region">Los Lagos Region</a>. It is located 27 km from the city and provincial capital of <a href="/wiki/Osorno,_Chile" title="Osorno, Chile">Osorno</a>. The commune is mostly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rural" title="Rural">rural</a>. Its activity is almost entirely agricultural and ranching. In the commune also performs <a href="/wiki/Agritourism" title="Agritourism">Agritourism</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Pablo,_Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>San Pablo was in the <a href="/wiki/Osorno_Province" title="Osorno Province">Department of Osorno</a> and administratively dependent of the <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">Municipality</a> of Osorno. On 22 December 1891, the Municipal Building decree establishing the <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">City</a> of San Pablo, based in San Pablo, whose territory corresponded to the subdelegations 8a, 9a, 10a, 11a and 12a of the Department of Osorno. The first <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">mayor</a> of San Pablo was Guillermo Heufemann.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Pablo,_Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the 2002 <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Statistics_Institute_(Chile)" title="National Statistics Institute (Chile)">National Statistics Institute</a>, San Pablo spans an area of 637.3 km<sup>2</sup> (246 sq mi) and has 10,162 inhabitants (5,210 men and 4,952 women). Of these, 3,478 (34.2%) lived in <a href="/wiki/Urban_area" title="Urban area">urban areas</a> and 6,684 (65.8%) in <a href="/wiki/Rural_area" title="Rural area">rural areas</a>. The population fell by 9.1% (1016 persons) between the 1992 and 2002 censuses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-INE_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-INE-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Pablo,_Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a commune, San Pablo is a third-level <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_division_of_Chile" title="Administrative division of Chile">administrative division of Chile</a> administered by a <a href="/wiki/Municipal_council" title="Municipal council">municipal council</a>, headed by an <a href="/wiki/Alcalde" title="Alcalde">alcalde</a> who is directly elected every four years. The 2008-2012 alcalde is Omar Alvarado.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Official_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Official-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Electoral_divisions_of_Chile" title="Electoral divisions of Chile">electoral divisions of Chile</a>, San Pablo is represented in the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_Chile" title="Chamber of Deputies of Chile">Chamber of Deputies</a> by Mr. Javier Hernández (<a href="/wiki/Independent_Democratic_Union" title="Independent Democratic Union">UDI</a>) and Mr. Sergio Ojeda (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_Democratic_Party" title="Christian Democratic Party">PDC</a>) as part of the 55th electoral district, (together with <a href="/wiki/Osorno,_Chile" title="Osorno, Chile">Osorno</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_de_la_Costa" title="San Juan de la Costa">San Juan de la Costa</a>). The commune is represented in the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Chile" title="Senate of Chile">Senate</a> by Camilo Escalona Medina (<a href="/wiki/Socialist_Party_of_Chile" title="Socialist Party of Chile">PS</a>) and Carlos Kuschel Silva (<a href="/wiki/National_Renewal_(Chile)" title="National Renewal (Chile)">RN</a>) as part of the 17th senatorial constituency (Los Lagos Region).</p>
