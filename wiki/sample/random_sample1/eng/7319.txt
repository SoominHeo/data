<p>The <b>mountain paca</b> (<i>Cuniculus taczanowskii</i>) is a small burrow-dwelling <a href="/wiki/Rodent" title="Rodent">rodent</a> whose habitats are high altitude <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South American</a> forests. <a href="/wiki/Paca" title="Paca">Pacas</a> are nocturnal, sedentary, and solitary animals with territorial tendencies. It eats mostly fruits and seeds. The mountain paca primarily inhabits higher <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andean</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Montane_forest" title="Montane forest">Montane forest</a> regions in <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a>.</p>
<p>This paca has coarse fur without underfur, dark brown to black on the upper body and white or yellowish on the underbelly. It usually has three to five rows of white spots along its sides, against a dark grey background. It has thick strong legs, with four digits in the forefeet and five in the hind feet (the first and fifth are reduced); the nails function as hooves. The tail is short and hairless. The <a href="/wiki/Zygomatic_arch" title="Zygomatic arch">zygomatic arch</a> is expanded laterally and dorsally and is used as a resonating chamber - a unique feature among mammals.</p>
<p>The mountain paca is <a href="/wiki/Hunting" title="Hunting">hunted</a> for its meat, which is high in calories and is considered a delicacy particularly in rural communities. Due to its quick growth, it may be bred in <a href="/wiki/Captivity_(animal)" title="Captivity (animal)">captivity</a> for commercial use. However, it has a low reproductive capacity and its numbers have been significantly reduced in recent years due to hunting and <a href="/wiki/Habitat_destruction" title="Habitat destruction">habitat destruction</a>. It is fairly abundant in protected areas.</p>
