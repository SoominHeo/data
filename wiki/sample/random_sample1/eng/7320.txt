<p><b>San Fernando del Valle de Catamarca</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[sam feɾˈnando ðel ˈβaʝe ðe kataˈmaɾka]</a></span>) is a city in <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Northwest" title="Argentine Northwest">northwestern</a> <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> and capital of <a href="/wiki/Catamarca_Province" title="Catamarca Province">Catamarca Province</a>, on the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%ADo_Valle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Río Valle (page does not exist)">Río Valle</a> River, at the feet of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cerro_Ambato&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cerro Ambato (page does not exist)">Cerro Ambato</a>. The city name is normally shortened as <b>Catamarca</b>.</p>
<p>The city of 684 km<sup>2</sup> (264 sq mi), located 500 m (1,640 ft) above the sea level, has 141,000 inhabitants (2001 <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> <small>[<a href="/wiki/National_Institute_of_Statistics_and_Census_of_Argentina" title="National Institute of Statistics and Census of Argentina">INDEC</a>]</small>), with more than 200,000 counting the suburbia, what represents around 70% of the population of the province.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Fernando_del_Valle_de_Catamarca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city is located 1,130 km (702 mi) from <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>. The closest provincial capitals are <a href="/wiki/La_Rioja,_Argentina" title="La Rioja, Argentina">La Rioja</a> (154 km (96 mi)), <a href="/wiki/San_Miguel_de_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="San Miguel de Tucumán">Tucumán</a> (230 km (143 mi)) and <a href="/wiki/Santiago_del_Estero" title="Santiago del Estero">Santiago del Estero</a> (209 km (130 mi)).</p>
<p>Many <a href="/wiki/Pilgrim" title="Pilgrim">pilgrims</a> come to San Fernando del Valle de Catamarca to visit the Church of the Virgin of the Valley (1694), which contains a statue of <i>Nuestra Señora</i> del Valle (Our Lady of the Valley).</p>
<p>Catamarca is also the touristic centre of the province, with its <a href="/wiki/Colonial_architecture" title="Colonial architecture">colonial architecture</a>, and serves as a hub to many touristic points and excursions, hiking, mountain-bike tours, horse riding, and wine tasting.</p>
<p>San Fernando del Valle is served by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coronel_Felipe_Varela_International_Airport)" title="Coronel Felipe Varela International Airport)">Coronel Felipe Varela International Airport)</a> (CTC/SANC), with flights to IRJ, TUC and AEP.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_politics">History and politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Fernando_del_Valle_de_Catamarca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History and politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An initial settlement, called Londres, was established here by <a href="/wiki/Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Spanish colonization of the Americas">Spanish colonists</a> in 1558; a permanent settlement was not founded until 1683, by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fernando_de_Mendoza_y_Mate_de_Luna&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fernando de Mendoza y Mate de Luna (page does not exist)">Fernando de Mendoza y Mate de Luna</a>. <i>Catamarca</i> comes from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quechua_language" title="Quechua language">Quechua</a> meaning "fortress on the slope".</p>
<p>The city, which had only 8,000 inhabitants in 1882 and was reached by a <a href="/wiki/Rail_transport_in_Argentina" title="Rail transport in Argentina">railway line</a> in 1888, grew very slowly, and the region remained poor even in the middle of the 20th century. This situation was exacerbated by <a href="/wiki/Nepotism" title="Nepotism">nepotism</a> on the part of the ruling Saadi family, before they lost political power in the 1990s. In spite of this, the province remains still politically unstable, and a large part of its population lives in precarious conditions.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Fernando_del_Valle_de_Catamarca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The warm <a href="/wiki/Semi-arid_climate" title="Semi-arid climate">semi-arid climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen</a> <i>BSh</i>) of Catamarca has an annual average of 20 °C with an average maximum temperature of 34 °C (93 °F) in summer with several days typically exceeding 43 °C (109 °F), and an average minimum of 5 °C (41 °F) in winter. Temperatures in the high mountains can drop down to −30 °C (−22 °F). The Valle (valley) receives relatively little rainfall from summer thunderstorms; most of the precipitation is received in the form of snow on the high mountain tops surrounding the city.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Fernando_del_Valle_de_Catamarca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">Agriculture</a> is San Fernando del Valle de Catamarca's chief economic activity; however, low rainfall makes <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">irrigation</a> necessary. Fruit and grapes are grown in the oasis areas near the city, and there is a considerable production of wine. Other local production includes preserves and handwoven <a href="/wiki/Poncho" title="Poncho">ponchos</a>.</p>
<p>Cotton and <a href="/wiki/Cattle" title="Cattle">cattle</a> are important economically, as well as mining at the <i>Farallón Negro</i>, <i>Bajo de la Alumbrera</i> and <i>Capillitas</i> mines where <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a>, <a href="/wiki/Silver" title="Silver">silver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">copper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tin" title="Tin">tin</a>.</p>
