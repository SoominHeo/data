<p><b>San Pietro Avellana</b> is a <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> (municipality) in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Isernia" title="Province of Isernia">Province of Isernia</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Molise" title="Molise">Molise</a>, about 50 kilometres (31 mi) northwest of <a href="/wiki/Campobasso" title="Campobasso">Campobasso</a> and some 20 kilometres (12 mi) north of <a href="/wiki/Isernia" title="Isernia">Isernia</a>. As of 31 December 2004, it had a population of 630 and an area of 45.0 square kilometres (17.4 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-istat_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-istat-1">[1]</a></sup> In local dialect, or <i>dialetto sampietrese,</i> Saint Amico is called <i>Sand'Amig</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Pietro_Avellana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town was originally a religious settlement, Volana, named in honor of Saint Peter.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The patron saint of San Pietro Avellana is <a class="external text" href="http://www.amico.mysite.com/" rel="nofollow">Sant' Amico</a> (Saint Amico/Saint Friend), a saint whose remains rest on hillside in the woods, overlooking the village where he worked. The shrine of Sant'Amico is referred to as Il Bosco di Sant'Amico. He was an Italian monk who worked in the <a href="/wiki/Apennine_Mountains" title="Apennine Mountains">Apennine</a> region of Southcentral <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> (al confine coll'<a href="/wiki/Abruzzo" title="Abruzzo">Abruzzo</a> e <a href="/wiki/Molise" title="Molise">Molise</a>), known for the story of a wolf who slayed a donkey and was cursed, forced to serve Sant'Amico as a <i>ciuccio</i> for the rest of his life, carrying chopped wood from the church to the monastery.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The municipality of San Pietro Avellana contains the <i><a href="/wiki/Frazione" title="Frazione">frazione</a></i> (subdivision) Masserie di Cristo. San Pietro Avellana borders municipalities <a href="/wiki/Ateleta" title="Ateleta">Ateleta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Capracotta" title="Capracotta">Capracotta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Castel_del_Giudice" title="Castel del Giudice">Castel del Giudice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Castel_di_Sangro" title="Castel di Sangro">Castel di Sangro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roccaraso" title="Roccaraso">Roccaraso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vastogirardi" title="Vastogirardi">Vastogirardi</a>.</p>
<p>Many descendants of the original San Pietro Avellana, a village with a population well over 2,000, live in the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland" title="Cleveland">Cleveland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Youngstown,_Ohio" title="Youngstown, Ohio">Youngstown</a> areas in the United States. They have spread out all across the globe, from <a href="/wiki/Rock_Springs,_Wyoming" title="Rock Springs, Wyoming">Rock Springs, Wyoming</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, to northern <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>, and back to northern <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>'s Industrial Triangle.</p>
