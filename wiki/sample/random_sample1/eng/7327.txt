<p><b>San Juan de los Morros</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[saŋ xwan de loz ˈmoros]</a></span>) is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capital_(political)" title="Capital (political)">capital</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Gu%C3%A1rico" title="Guárico">Guárico</a> state and capital of the <a href="/wiki/Juan_Germ%C3%A1n_Roscio" title="Juan Germán Roscio">Juan Germán Roscio</a> municipality, and is a town located in central <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Juan_de_los_Morros&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:122px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:San_Juan_Bautista_Guarico.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1024" data-file-width="728" height="169" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/San_Juan_Bautista_Guarico.jpg/120px-San_Juan_Bautista_Guarico.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/San_Juan_Bautista_Guarico.jpg/180px-San_Juan_Bautista_Guarico.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/San_Juan_Bautista_Guarico.jpg/240px-San_Juan_Bautista_Guarico.jpg 2x" width="120"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:San_Juan_Bautista_Guarico.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/John_the_Baptist_Monument" title="John the Baptist Monument">Statue of San Juan Bautista</a>, erected 1935</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Guarico is the fourth largest state in the country in terms of area, but not in population, as it has a low population density. San Juan de los Morros used to be part of Aragua state, but it was exchanged during the 1930s to Guarico state, and the town was later elevated to become Capital of the state, replacing the former capital <a href="/wiki/Calabozo" title="Calabozo">Calabozo</a>.</p>
<p>Among the major features of the city are the <a href="/wiki/John_the_Baptist_Monument" title="John the Baptist Monument">Statue of San Juan Bautista</a> (<a href="/wiki/John_the_Baptist" title="John the Baptist">John the Baptist</a>) which is 19.8 meters (62,3 ft) high, the Bandera Monument, Bolivar Plaza, the 'Los Morros de San Juan' monument, the historic place and monument known as "La Puerta" (The Gate), and the hot springs. The medicinal sulfuric content of the springs makes them a significant tourist attraction.</p>
<p>San Juan de los Morros has a climate of 24 to 32 degrees Celsius and is dominated by the surrounding <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountains</a>, which contrasts with the plains of the rest of the state. Typical dishes that originate from the Guarico area are: la <a href="/wiki/Cachapa" title="Cachapa">cachapa</a>, el <a href="/wiki/Pabell%C3%B3n_criollo" title="Pabellón criollo">pabellón criollo</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arepas" title="Arepas">arepas</a>.</p>
<p>The city is connected to the South with <a href="/wiki/Parapara,_Gu%C3%A1rico" title="Parapara, Guárico">Parapara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ortiz,_Gu%C3%A1rico" title="Ortiz, Guárico">Ortiz</a> by a national road. It is served by the <a href="/wiki/Guarico_Municipal_Airport" title="Guarico Municipal Airport">Guarico Municipal Airport</a>.</p>
