<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">AASHTO Soil Classification System</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>AASHTO Soil Classification System</b> was developed by the <a href="/wiki/American_Association_of_State_Highway_and_Transportation_Officials" title="American Association of State Highway and Transportation Officials">American Association of State Highway and Transportation Officials</a>, and is used as a guide for the <a href="/wiki/Soil_classification" title="Soil classification">classification of soils</a> and soil-aggregate mixtures for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Highway_construction" title="Highway construction">highway construction</a> purposes. The classification system was first developed by Hogentogler and <a href="/wiki/Karl_von_Terzaghi" title="Karl von Terzaghi">Terzaghi</a> in 1929,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> but has been revised several times since.</p>
