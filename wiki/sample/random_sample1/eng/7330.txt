<p><b>Salah Hissou</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">صلاح حيسو</span>‎‎) (born January 16, 1972 in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ait_Taghia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ait Taghia (page does not exist)">Ait Taghia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kasba_Tadla" title="Kasba Tadla">Kasba Tadla</a>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long-distance_runner" title="Long-distance runner">long-distance runner</a> from <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>, who won the gold medal over <a href="/wiki/5000_metres" title="5000 metres">5000 metres</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1999_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1999 World Championships in Athletics">1999 World Championships in Athletics</a> in Seville. With 26:38.08 he also set a <a href="/wiki/10,000_metres_world_record_progression" title="10,000 metres world record progression">world record</a> over <a href="/wiki/10,000_metres" title="10,000 metres">10,000 metres</a> in Brussels in 1996 and won a bronze medal over 10,000 m at the <a href="/wiki/1996_Summer_Olympics" title="1996 Summer Olympics">1996 Summer Olympics</a> in Atlanta.</p>
