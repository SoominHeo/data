<p><b>Salamanca</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[salaˈmaŋka]</a></span>) is an ancient Celtic city in northwestern Spain that is the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Salamanca" title="Province of Salamanca">Province of Salamanca</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_communities_of_Spain" title="Autonomous communities of Spain">community</a> of <a href="/wiki/Castile_and_Le%C3%B3n" title="Castile and León">Castile and León</a>. The city lies on several hills by the <a href="/wiki/Tormes" title="Tormes">Tormes</a> River. Its Old City was declared a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UNESCO_World_Heritage_Site" title="UNESCO World Heritage Site">UNESCO World Heritage Site</a> in 1988. With a metropolitan population of 228,881 in 2012 according to the National Institute of Statistics (INE), Salamanca is the second most populated urban area in Castile and León, after <a href="/wiki/Valladolid" title="Valladolid">Valladolid</a> (414,000), and ahead of <a href="/wiki/Le%C3%B3n,_Spain" title="León, Spain">Leon</a> (187,000) and <a href="/wiki/Burgos" title="Burgos">Burgos</a> (176,000).</p>
<p>It is one of the most important university cities in Spain and supplies 16% of Spain's market for the teaching of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish language</a>, partially due to the usage of a neutral Castilian Spanish variant which is greatly appreciated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Salamanca attracts thousands of international students,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> generating a diverse environment.</p>
<p>It is situated approximately 200 kilometres (120 miles) west of the Spanish capital <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> and 80 km (50 mi) east of the Portuguese border. The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Salamanca" title="University of Salamanca">University of Salamanca</a>, which was founded in 1218, is the oldest university in Spain and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_oldest_universities_in_continuous_operation" title="List of oldest universities in continuous operation">fourth</a> oldest western university, but the first to be given its status by the <a href="/wiki/Pope_Alexander_IV" title="Pope Alexander IV">Pope Alexander IV</a> who gave universal validity to its degrees. With its 30,000 students, the university is, together with tourism, a primary source of income in Salamanca.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salamanca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Timeline_of_Salamanca" title="Timeline of Salamanca">Timeline of Salamanca</a></div>
<p>The city was founded in the pre-<a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Ancient Rome</a> period by the <a href="/wiki/Vaccaei" title="Vaccaei">Vaccaei</a>, a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Celtic_tribes" title="List of Celtic tribes">Celtic</a> tribe, or the <a href="/wiki/Vettones" title="Vettones">Vettones</a>, a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Celtic_tribes" title="List of Celtic tribes">Celtic</a> or pre-Celtic indo-European tribe, as one of a pair of forts to defend their territory near the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duero" title="Duero">Duero</a> river. In the 3rd century BC, <a href="/wiki/Hannibal" title="Hannibal">Hannibal</a> laid siege to the city. With the fall of the <a href="/wiki/Carthage" title="Carthage">Carthaginians</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Romans</a>, the city of Helmantica, as it was known, began to take more importance as a commercial hub in the Roman <a href="/wiki/Hispania" title="Hispania">Hispania</a> due to its favorable location. Salamanca lay on a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_road" title="Roman road">Roman road</a>, known as the <a href="/wiki/V%C3%ADa_de_la_Plata" title="Vía de la Plata">Vía de la Plata</a>, which connected it with <a href="/wiki/Emerita_Augusta" title="Emerita Augusta">Emerita Augusta</a> (present day <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9rida,_Spain" title="Mérida, Spain">Mérida</a>) to the south and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asturica_Augusta" title="Asturica Augusta">Asturica Augusta</a> (present-day <a href="/wiki/Astorga,_Spain" title="Astorga, Spain">Astorga</a>) to the north. Its <a href="/wiki/Roman_bridge" title="Roman bridge">Roman bridge</a> dates from the 1st century, and was a part of this road.</p>
<p>With the fall of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Alans" title="Alans">Alans</a> established in Lusitania, and Salamanca was part of this region. Later the city was conquered by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Visigoth" title="Visigoth">Visigoths</a> and included in their territory. The city was already an episcopal <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Salamanca" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Salamanca">see</a>, and signatures of bishops of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bishopric_of_Salamanca" title="Bishopric of Salamanca">Salamanca</a> are found in the <a href="/wiki/Councils_of_Toledo" title="Councils of Toledo">Councils of Toledo</a>.</p>
<p>Salamanca surrendered to the <a href="/wiki/Moors" title="Moors">Moors</a>, led by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Musa_bin_Nusair" title="Musa bin Nusair">Musa bin Nusair</a>, in the year 712 AD. For years, this area between the south of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duero_River" title="Duero River">Duero River</a> and the north of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tormes_River" title="Tormes River">Tormes River</a>, became the main battlefield between the Christian kingdoms and the Muslim Al-Andalus rulers. The constant fighting of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="Kingdom of León">Kingdom of León</a> first, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile_and_Le%C3%B3n" title="Kingdom of Castile and León">Kingdom of Castile and León</a> later against the <a href="/wiki/Caliphate_of_C%C3%B3rdoba" title="Caliphate of Córdoba">Caliphate</a> depopulated Salamanca and reduced it to an unimportant settlement. After the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Simancas" title="Battle of Simancas">battle of Simancas</a> (939) the Christians resettled this area. After the capture of <a href="/wiki/Toledo,_Spain" title="Toledo, Spain">Toledo</a> by <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_VI_of_Le%C3%B3n_and_Castile" title="Alfonso VI of León and Castile">Alfonso VI of León and Castile</a> in 1085, the definitive resettlement of the city took place. <a href="/wiki/Raymond_of_Burgundy" title="Raymond of Burgundy">Raymond of Burgundy</a>, instructed by his father-in-law <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alfonso_VI_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="Alfonso VI of León">Alfonso VI of León</a>, led a group of settlers of various origins in 1102.</p>
<p>One of the most important moments in Salamanca's history was the year 1218, when <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_IX_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="Alfonso IX of León">Alfonso IX of León</a> granted a royal charter to the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Salamanca" title="University of Salamanca">University of Salamanca</a>, although formal teaching had existed at least since 1130. Soon it became one of the most significant and prestigious academic centres in Europe.</p>
<p>During the 16th century, the city reached its height of splendour (around 6,500 students and a total population of 24,000). During that period, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Salamanca" title="University of Salamanca">University of Salamanca</a> hosted the most important intellectuals of the time; these groups of mostly-<a href="/wiki/Dominican_Order" title="Dominican Order">Dominican</a> scholars were designated the <a href="/wiki/School_of_Salamanca" title="School of Salamanca">School of Salamanca</a>. The juridical doctrine of the School of Salamanca represented the end of medieval concepts of law, and founded the fundamental body of the ulterior European law and morality concepts, including rights as a corporeal being (right to life), economic rights (right to own property) and spiritual rights (rights to freedom of thought and rights related to intrinsic human dignity).</p>
<p>In 1551, the Holy Roman Emperor <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles V</a> ordered an inquiry to find out if the science of Andreas <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vesalius" title="Vesalius">Vesalius</a>, physician and anatomist, was in line with Catholic doctrine. Vesalius came to Salamanca that same year to appear before the board and was acquitted.</p>
<p>Salamanca suffered the general downturns of the Kingdom of Castile during the 17th century, but in the 18th century it experienced a rebirth. In this period, the new baroque Cathedral and main square (Plaza Mayor) were finished.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_War" title="Peninsular War">Peninsular War</a> of the Napoleonic campaigns, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Salamanca" title="Battle of Salamanca">Battle of Salamanca</a>, fought on July 22, 1812, was a serious setback for the French, and a mighty setback for Salamanca, whose western quarter was seriously damaged. The battle which raged that day is famous as a defining moment in military history; many thousands of men were slaughtered by cannon fire in the space of only a few short hours.</p>

<p>During the devastating Spanish Civil War (1936–39) the city quickly went over to the Nationalist side and was used as the <i>de facto</i> capital. Franco was named <i>Generalissimo</i> on 21 September 1937 while at the city, and in the same year was formed, by a decree signed in the city, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Falange_Espa%C3%B1ola_Tradicionalista_y_de_las_Juntas_de_Ofensiva_Nacional-Sindicalista" title="Falange Española Tradicionalista y de las Juntas de Ofensiva Nacional-Sindicalista">official fascist party that ruled Spain until the end of the Francoist regime</a>, officially suppressing any other political party. The Nationalists soon moved most of the administrative departments to <a href="/wiki/Burgos" title="Burgos">Burgos</a>, which being more central was better suited for this purpose. However, some administrative departments, Franco's headquarters (located at the Palacio Episcopal, next to the Old Cathedral) and the military commands stayed in Salamanca, along with the German and Italian fascist delegations, making it the <i>de facto</i> Nationalist capital and centre of power during the entire civil war.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ht01_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ht01-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ht02_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-ht02-5">[5]</a></sup> Like much of fervently Catholic and largely rural <a href="/wiki/Le%C3%B3n_(historical_region)" title="León (historical region)">Leon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Old_Castile" title="Old Castile">Old Castile</a> regions, Salamanca was a staunch supporter of the Nationalist side and <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Franco" title="Francisco Franco">Francisco Franco</a>'s regime for its long duration.</p>
<p>In 1988, the old city was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site. In 1998, it was declared a European Capital of Culture for year 2002 (shared with Bruges). During 14 and 15 October 2005, it hosted the XV the Ibero-American Summits of Heads of State and Governments.</p>
<p>Since 1996, Salamanca has been the designated site of the <a href="/wiki/General_Archive_of_the_Spanish_Civil_War" title="General Archive of the Spanish Civil War">archives of the Spanish Civil War</a> (<i>Archivo General de la Guerra Civil Española</i>). The original documents were assembled by the <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Franco" title="Francisco Franco">Francoist</a> regime, selectively obtained from the administrative departments of various institutions and organizations during the Spanish Civil War as a repressive instrument used against opposition groups and individuals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The socialist government moved the Catalan part of the archive to Barcelona in 2006 despite opposition from the local authorities and popular protests.</p>

<p>The Old City of Salamanca was declared a <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a> by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> in 1988. Sightseeing in the city, many of them within the old quarter,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CYL_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-CYL-7">[7]</a></sup> are:</p>

<ul>
<li>La <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plaza_Mayor_de_Salamanca" title="Plaza Mayor de Salamanca">Plaza Mayor</a></b>: of <a href="/wiki/Baroque" title="Baroque">Baroque</a> style, designed by architects Alberto and Nicolás <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Churriguera_family" title="Churriguera family">Churriguera</a> is the most important of public spaces and the heart of the city.</li>
<li><b>Campo de San Francisco (Salamanca)</b>: First public garden in the city on grounds of the former convent of San Francisco Real.</li>
<li><b>Huerto de Calisto y Melibea (Salamanca)</b>: Garden near the cathedrals where, some say, lies the plot of the novel <i><a href="/wiki/La_Celestina" title="La Celestina">La Celestina</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Fernando_de_Rojas" title="Fernando de Rojas">Fernando de Rojas</a>. Beside it are remains of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman</a> Walls.</li>
<li><b>Plaza del Corrillo (Salamanca)</b>: Small square adjacent to the Plaza Mayor. On the left is the <a href="/wiki/Romanesque_architecture" title="Romanesque architecture">Romanesque</a> church of San Martín and the right a series of houses with porches formed by columns of stone completed in pads representing the days of the week (a moon for the Monday, a Mars for Tuesday, etc.).</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Capilla_de_la_Vera_Cruz_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Capilla de la Vera Cruz (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">Capilla de la Vera Cruz</a></b>: Baroque church with Renaissance façade, headquarters of the five-hundred-year-old <a href="/wiki/Illustrious_Brotherhood_of_the_Holy_Cross_of_the_Redeemer_and_the_Immaculate_Conception,_his_Mother_(Salamanca)" title="Illustrious Brotherhood of the Holy Cross of the Redeemer and the Immaculate Conception, his Mother (Salamanca)">Brotherhood of the Vera Cruz of Salamanca</a>. It houses countless works of art.</li>
<li>Cathedrals: Salamanca has two cathedrals, the <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Cathedral,_Salamanca" title="Old Cathedral, Salamanca">Old Cathedral</a></b>, of the 12th century and of <a href="/wiki/Romanesque_architecture" title="Romanesque architecture">Romanesque</a> style, and the <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Cathedral,_Salamanca" title="New Cathedral, Salamanca">New Cathedral</a></b>, much larger, built in the 16th century of <a href="/wiki/Gothic_architecture" title="Gothic architecture">Gothic</a> style and completed in the 18th century. The place where they both join is known as Patio Chico and is one of the most charming corners of the city.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=La_Clerec%C3%ADa_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="La Clerecía (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">La Clerecía</a></b>: currently houses the <a href="/wiki/Pontifical_University_of_Salamanca" title="Pontifical University of Salamanca">Pontifical University</a>. Building started in 1617 and was completed 150 years later as the Colegio Real del Espíritu Santo, of the <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Jesus" title="Society of Jesus">Society of Jesus</a>. The style is <a href="/wiki/Baroque" title="Baroque">Baroque</a>. It difference the school, with an interesting cloister and the church, with an impressive façade of three bodies, two twin towers of 50 meters high and a huge dome. The Clerecía name is because it belonged to the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Real_Clerec%C3%ADa_de_San_Marcos&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Real Clerecía de San Marcos (page does not exist)">Real Clerecía de San Marcos</a> after the <a href="/wiki/Suppression_of_the_Society_of_Jesus" title="Suppression of the Society of Jesus">expulsion of the Jesuits</a>.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Colegio_de_Calatrava_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Colegio de Calatrava (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">Colegio de Calatrava</a></b>: Built in the 18th century, by the initiative of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Calatrava" title="Order of Calatrava">Order of Calatrava</a>, now houses the Casa de la Iglesia.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Convento_de_las_Agustinas_e_Iglesia_de_la_Pur%C3%ADsima_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Convento de las Agustinas e Iglesia de la Purísima (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">Convento de las Agustinas e Iglesia de la Purísima</a></b>: In the church is a painting of the Immaculate Conception painted by <a href="/wiki/Jusepe_de_Ribera" title="Jusepe de Ribera">Jusepe de Ribera</a>. It is the only construction of totally Italian space and decor in Spain.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Convento_de_las_Due%C3%B1as" title="Convento de las Dueñas">Convento de las Dueñas</a></b> (15th century): Highlights the irregular Renaissance cloister.</li>
<li><b>Convento de las Isabeles (Salamanca)</b></li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Convento_de_San_Antonio_el_Real_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Convento de San Antonio el Real (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">Convento de San Antonio el Real</a></b> (1736): of Baroque style, its remains were divided between the Lyceum Theatre and a store where it can visit.</li>
<li><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convent_of_St._Stephen,_Salamanca" title="Convent of St. Stephen, Salamanca">Convento de San Esteban</a></b>, of the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Order" title="Dominican Order">Dominican</a> <a href="/wiki/Friar" title="Friar">friars</a> (16th century): the <a href="/wiki/Plateresque" title="Plateresque">plateresque</a> façade, with its shape of an arc of triumph, is a jewel of the Salamancan <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a>. Impressive Baroque altarpiece by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Benito_Churriguera" title="José Benito Churriguera">José Benito Churriguera</a>. Also noteworthy is the Cloister of the Kings, Renaissance.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Convento_de_la_Anunciaci%C3%B3n_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Convento de la Anunciación (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">Convento de la Anunciación</a></b> (Calle de las Úrsulas): Founded by the Archbishop Alonso II de Fonseca in 1512. Stresses the exterior apse of <a href="/wiki/Gothic_architecture" title="Gothic architecture">Gothic</a> style. In the inside, the <a href="/wiki/Baroque" title="Baroque">Baroque</a> altarpiece and the tomb of the founder, Renaissance, work by <a href="/wiki/Beto" title="Beto">Beto</a>.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Convento_de_la_Trinidad_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Convento de la Trinidad (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">Convento de la Trinidad</a></b>: Former Palacio de Montellano adapted in the 16th century to host a <a href="/wiki/Trinitarian_Order" title="Trinitarian Order">Trinitarian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friary" title="Friary">friary</a>.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Monasterio_de_Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_la_Victoria_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Monasterio de Nuestra Señora de la Victoria (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">Monasterio de Nuestra Señora de la Victoria</a></b>, of the <a href="/wiki/Monk" title="Monk">monks</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Hieronymites" title="Hieronymites">Order of St. Jerome</a>, completed in 1513, almost destroyed by the French in the early 19th century, the <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_War" title="Peninsular War">Peninsular War</a>, is now integrated into the manufacturing facilities of the 19th century, of the <a href="/wiki/Mirat" title="Mirat">Grupo Mirat</a>.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ermita_de_Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_la_Misericordia_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ermita de Nuestra Señora de la Misericordia (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">Ermita de Nuestra Señora de la Misericordia</a></b> (16th-17th centuries): small Baroque chapel which was begun in 1389 in the Plaza de San Cristobal. Currently very damaged, is a printing, while its <a href="/wiki/Bell-gable" title="Bell-gable">bell-gable</a> decorates the church of the Pizarrales neighborhood.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Antigua_Iglesia_de_las_Bernardas_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Antigua Iglesia de las Bernardas (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">Antigua Iglesia de las Bernardas</a></b> work by <a href="/wiki/Rodrigo_Gil_de_Honta%C3%B1%C3%B3n" title="Rodrigo Gil de Hontañón">Rodrigo Gil de Hontañón</a>. Prototype of the Salamancan churches of the 16th century. Stresses the shell-shaped head. Today it is within the Colegio de San José de Calasanz.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Iglesia_del_Carmen_de_Abajo_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Iglesia del Carmen de Abajo (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">Iglesia del Carmen de Abajo</a></b>: Chapel of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Order" title="Third Order">Third Order</a> <a href="/wiki/Carmelites" title="Carmelites">Carmelites</a>, incorporated in the Friary of San Andrés. It is the only remnant of that friary, which disappeared in the 19th century.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Iglesia_de_San_Benito_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Iglesia de San Benito (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">Iglesia de San Benito</a></b>: Gothic church built under the patronage of Alonso II de Fonseca, pantheon of the Maldonado family.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Iglesia_de_San_Juli%C3%A1n_y_Santa_Basilisa_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Iglesia de San Julián y Santa Basilisa (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">Iglesia de San Julián</a></b>: Romanesque church subsequently restored.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Iglesia_de_San_Marcos_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Iglesia de San Marcos (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">Iglesia de San Marcos</a></b>: <a href="/wiki/Romanesque_architecture" title="Romanesque architecture">Romanesque</a> church near the path which ran the North walls of the city. Outside circular plant has three naves and apses inside.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Iglesia_de_San_Mart%C3%ADn_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Iglesia de San Martín (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">Iglesia de San Martín</a></b>: Romanesque church with Gothic reforms, Renaissance and Baroque, attached to the Plaza Mayor.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Iglesia_de_San_Pablo_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Iglesia de San Pablo (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">Iglesia de San Pablo</a></b>: Baroque church belonging to the former convent of the Trinitarians, houses the image of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Congregation_of_Rescued_Jesus_and_Our_Lady_of_Sorrows&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Congregation of Rescued Jesus and Our Lady of Sorrows (page does not exist)">Jesus Rescued</a>, much venerated in the city. Parish hosts, governed by the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Diocesan_Laborer_Priests_of_the_Sacred_Heart_of_Jesus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Diocesan Laborer Priests of the Sacred Heart of Jesus (page does not exist)">Diocesan Laborer Priests</a>.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Iglesia_de_Santo_Tom%C3%A1s_Cantuariense_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Iglesia de Santo Tomás Cantuariense (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">Iglesia de Santo Tomás Cantuariense</a></b>: <a href="/wiki/Romanesque_architecture" title="Romanesque architecture">Romanesque</a> church founded in honor of <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Becket" title="Thomas Becket">St. Thomas</a>, Archbishop of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archbishopric_of_Canterbury" title="Archbishopric of Canterbury">Canterbury</a> in 1175, just five years after his death and two after his canonization. It has three apses and a nave with a wooden roof. Form Parish along with St. Paul, governed by the Diocesan Laborer Priests.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="University_buildings">University buildings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salamanca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: University buildings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<a href="/wiki/Colegio_Mayor_de_Santiago_el_Zebedeo" title="Colegio Mayor de Santiago el Zebedeo">Colegio Mayor de Santiago el Zebedeo</a> (old Irish College).</div>

<ul>
<li>University: Set of buildings that made up the former <a href="/wiki/University_of_Salamanca" title="University of Salamanca">University of Salamanca</a>, including the <b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Escuelas_Mayores&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Escuelas Mayores (page does not exist)">Escuelas Mayores</a></b>, the <b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Escuelas_Menores&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Escuelas Menores (page does not exist)">Escuelas Menores</a></b> and the <b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hospital_de_Estudio&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hospital de Estudio (page does not exist)">Hospital de Estudio</a></b> (current rectorate). These buildings are situated around the square known as <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Patio_de_Escuelas_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Patio de Escuelas (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">Patio de Escuelas</a>. In this same square is the home of Dr. Álvarez Abarca or of the Doctors of the Queen (15th century), whose façade is Gothic with Renaissance details and is now the Museum of Salamanca.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Casa-museo_de_Unamuno&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Casa-museo de Unamuno (page does not exist)">Casa-museo de Unamuno</a></b> (18th century): former home of the rectors of the university. It preserved as in its time it had <a href="/wiki/Miguel_de_Unamuno" title="Miguel de Unamuno">Miguel de Unamuno</a> when he took this position.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Colegio_Mayor_de_Santiago_el_Zebedeo" title="Colegio Mayor de Santiago el Zebedeo">Colegio Mayor de Santiago el Zebedeo</a></b>, also called "of the Archbishop Fonseca" or "of the Irish" (16th century).</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Colegio_de_San_Ambrosio&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Colegio de San Ambrosio (page does not exist)">Colegio de San Ambrosio</a></b> (1719): Is currently <a href="/wiki/General_Archive_of_the_Spanish_Civil_War" title="General Archive of the Spanish Civil War">General Archive of the Spanish Civil War</a>. Houses documents and items seized by the national troops and their allies during and at the end of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War" title="Spanish Civil War">Spanish Civil War</a>. While over the entire postwar its basic objective was to preserve the information related to organizations and peoples potentially opposing the Franco regime, and therefore use this information for repressive, since the return of democracy this building would become one of the most important archives that existed in Spain to investigate the historical period of the Second Republic. Many of the documents and objects that still remain in the archive are related to the <a href="/wiki/Freemasonry" title="Freemasonry">Freemasonry</a>, including several furniture that has been rebuilt a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Masonic_Lodge" title="Masonic Lodge">Masonic Lodge</a>.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Colegio_Triling%C3%BCe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Colegio Trilingüe (page does not exist)">Colegio Trilingüe</a></b>: founded in 1554 to the teaching of Latin, Greek and Hebrew. It also preserves part of the original courtyard, remade in 1829, in the Faculty of Physics.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Palacio_de_Anaya_(Salamanca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Palacio de Anaya (Salamanca) (page does not exist)">Palacio de Anaya</a></b> was the last headquarters of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Colegio_Mayor_de_San_Bartolom%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Colegio Mayor de San Bartolomé (page does not exist)">Colegio Mayor de San Bartolomé</a> or <b>Colegio de Anaya</b> founded in the 15th century by Don <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Diego_de_Anaya_Maldonado&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Diego de Anaya Maldonado (page does not exist)">Diego de Anaya</a>, abolished in the early 19th century. Today is the faculty of philology. Next to the building is the Iglesia of San Sebastian, former chapel of the college and the Inn, work by Joaquín de Churriguera.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Colegio_Santa_Cruz_de_Ca%C3%B1izares&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Colegio Santa Cruz de Cañizares (page does not exist)">Colegio Santa Cruz de Cañizares</a></b> (16th century): Music Conservatory. Of it only remains the old chapel, now incorporated into the assembly hall of the conservatory, and the main façade, of <a href="/wiki/Plateresque" title="Plateresque">plateresque</a> style.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Colegio_de_San_Pelayo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Colegio de San Pelayo (page does not exist)">Colegio de San Pelayo</a></b>: founded in the mid-16th century. Since 1990 home to the <a class="external text" href="http://fgh.usal.es" rel="nofollow">Faculty of Geography and History</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Palaces_and_palatial_houses">Palaces and palatial houses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salamanca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Palaces and palatial houses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Patio_Palacio_Salinas_(Salamanca).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2592" data-file-width="3888" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Patio_Palacio_Salinas_%28Salamanca%29.jpg/220px-Patio_Palacio_Salinas_%28Salamanca%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Patio_Palacio_Salinas_%28Salamanca%29.jpg/330px-Patio_Palacio_Salinas_%28Salamanca%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Patio_Palacio_Salinas_%28Salamanca%29.jpg/440px-Patio_Palacio_Salinas_%28Salamanca%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Patio_Palacio_Salinas_(Salamanca).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
La Salina Palace</div>

<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Casa_de_las_Conchas" title="Casa de las Conchas">Casa de las Conchas</a></b>: built in the late 15th century. of <a href="/wiki/Gothic_architecture" title="Gothic architecture">Gothic</a> civil style, its façade is decorated with about 350 shells of scallops, distinctive of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Santiago" title="Order of Santiago">Order of Santiago</a>. Also important are the bars Gothic windows. It currently houses a public library.</li>
<li><b>Casa de Don Diego Maldonado</b>: 16th-century Plateresque palace. It houses the Hispanic-Brazilian Cultural Foundation and the Centre for Brazilian Studies at the University of Salamanca.</li>
<li><b>Casa de Doña María la Brava</b>: 15th-century Gothic building, prototype of the noble mansions of the time. Its owner, María Rodríguez de Monroy was the head of one of the two sides in that split the city in the 15th century. Beheaded the murderers of her children. It is located in the Plaza de los Bandos.</li>
<li><b>Casa Lis</b>: <a href="/wiki/Art_Nouveau" title="Art Nouveau">Art Nouveau</a> palace of 1905 with iron façade. Built on the walls. It houses the Collections of Art Nouveau and Art Deco donated by Manuel Ramos Andrade.</li>
<li><b>Casa de las Muertes</b> (early 16th century), built by Juan de Álava and named such for the skulls that decorate the façade.</li>
<li><b>Casa del Regidor Ovalle</b> (18th century): The Spanish writer <a href="/wiki/Miguel_de_Unamuno" title="Miguel de Unamuno">Miguel de Unamuno</a> died here.</li>
<li><b>Casa de Santa Teresa</b> (16th century): The saint <a href="/wiki/Teresa_of_%C3%81vila" title="Teresa of Ávila">Teresa of Ávila</a> stayed here when she visited Salamanca in 1570 to found a convent and here she wrote the poem <i>Vivo sin vivir en mí</i>.</li>
<li><b>Casa de la Tierra</b> (15th century): doorway with arched, Gothic window tracery. Headquarters of the Chamber of Commerce and Industry of Salamanca.</li>
<li><b>Casa de las Viejas</b> (17th century): old workhouse for poor, now the headquarters of the Regional Film Archive of Castile and León. Permanent exhibition of equipment related to cinema and its history, owned by Salamancan filmmaker <a href="/wiki/Basilio_Mart%C3%ADn_Patino" title="Basilio Martín Patino">Basilio Martín Patino</a>.</li>
<li><b>Fonda Veracruz</b> : courtyard with wooden galleries in form of dead-end street. Currently catering school.</li>
<li><b>Arias Corvelle Palace</b> (15th century): <a href="/wiki/Sgraffito" title="Sgraffito">sgraffito</a> façade very similar to that of San Boal. It houses the School of Fine and Performing Arts of San Eloy.</li>
<li><b>Castellanos Palace</b> (15th-16th centuries): The Palace of the Marquises of Castellanos construction began in the late 15th century, although the façade dates from the late 19th century which combines Gothic and Neoclassical styles. With a powerful Gothic interior courtyard, this building now serves as a hotel.</li>
<li><b>Garci Grande Palace</b> (16th century): Renaissance doorway and chamfered corner windows unique in the city. Head Office of the Savings Bank (<a href="/wiki/List_of_banks_in_Europe#Cajas.28Savings_banks.29" title="List of banks in Europe">Caja Duero</a>).</li>
<li><b>Monterrey Palace</b>: was built in the 16th century and is of <a href="/wiki/Plateresque" title="Plateresque">plateresque</a> style. Belongs to the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Alba" title="House of Alba">House of Alba</a> and highlight its towers and chimneys. Only it built one of the four parts that composed all designed initially.</li>
<li><b>Orellana Palace</b> (16th century): building of <a href="/wiki/Classicism" title="Classicism">classical</a> architecture with <a href="/wiki/Mannerism" title="Mannerism">Mannerist</a> influence. The courtyard in <i>L</i> shape and the ladder.</li>
<li><b>Rodríguez de Figueroa Palace</b> (1545): has interesting façades at the streets Concejo and Zamora and interior courtyard. Today the Salamanca Casino.</li>
<li><b>La Salina Palace</b> (1546): Renaissance, work by <a href="/wiki/Rodrigo_Gil_de_Honta%C3%B1%C3%B3n" title="Rodrigo Gil de Hontañón">Rodrigo Gil de Hontañón</a>. Since 1884 is the headquarters of the Provincial Diputation.</li>
<li><b>San Boal Palace</b> (15th century): façade decorated with <a href="/wiki/Sgraffito" title="Sgraffito">sgraffitos</a>. Was School of Commerce and later Faculty of Business. Since 1999 is Hispanic-Japanese Cultural Center of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Salamanca" title="University of Salamanca">University of Salamanca</a>. In the same square is the Iglesia de San Boal (17th century).</li>
<li><b>Solís Palace</b> (15th century): In this palace were married <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Spain" title="Philip II of Spain">Philip II of Spain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maria_Manuela,_Princess_of_Portugal" title="Maria Manuela, Princess of Portugal">Maria Manuela of Portugal</a> in 1543. Today it houses the <a href="/wiki/Telef%C3%B3nica" title="Telefónica">Telefónica</a>.</li>
<li><b>Tower del Aire</b>: is all that remains of the Palace of the dukes of Fermoselle, built in the 15th century. It has beautiful Gothic windows. It is currently a student residence.</li>
<li><b>Tower del Clavero</b> (15th century): remains of a palace, apparently built by Francisco de Sotomayor, Clavero Staff of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Alc%C3%A1ntara" title="Order of Alcántara">Order of Alcántara</a>, about 1470 . The lower part is quadrangular, while the upper is octagonal adorned with eight cylindrical turrets.</li>
<li><b>Torreón de los Anaya</b> (15th century): old manor house of <a href="/wiki/Gothic_architecture" title="Gothic architecture">Gothic</a> civil style which highlights the mullioned window and the Patio de Tres Lados. For years it was the seat of <i>Institute of Studies of Latin America and Portugal</i> of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Salamanca" title="University of Salamanca">University of Salamanca</a>, also known as Palacio de Abrantes.</li>
</ul>

<li><b>Permanent Exhibition IERONIMUS</b>. The name of the exhibition: IERONIMUS alludes to Don Jerónimo de Périgueux, famed French-born Spanish bishop by the Diocese of Salamanca in 1102, who commissioned the construction of the Iglesia de Santa María. This was the event that marked the origin of the 900 Years of Art and History of the Cathedrals of Salamanca. In this tour one can admire amazing places like the one offered by the gazebo next to the Tower del Gallo, the Patio Chico or the Terraza de Anaya. The circuit of the exhibition begins in the Board of Warden, continuing on the Board of the Tower Mocha, the Platform of the Superior Room and the Board of Vault.</li>
</ul>
<p>It is accessed from the last tower, next to the gate of Santa Lucía (giving access to the old cathedral). In three of the rooms one can find exposed drawings, documents and religious objects related to the cathedrals; especially with its construction, one can see, both inside and outside the two cathedrals. From the "Sala del Alcaide" enjoy a splendid view of the nave and the altar of the old cathedral, and from the upper platform located thereupon, one may accurately observe the Tower del Gallo, as well as views of the Tormes and Transtormes neighborhoods. One may also enjoy the vaults of the new cathedral, and again on the outside with views of the plaza Anaya, the Tower del Reloj, the Rua Mayor and all of the historical centre.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Others">Others</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salamanca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Others">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><b>Theatre Bretón</b>. Now destroyed.</li>
<li><b>Cave of Salamanca</b>. Located on the Carvajal slope, where it is said that the devil taught black magic.</li>
<li><b>Central Market</b> (1899–1909). Located in the old Plaza de la Verdura. Made of iron.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Roman_bridge_of_Salamanca" title="Roman bridge of Salamanca">Roman bridge of Salamanca</a></b>. Crossing the Tormes River, it is 150 m long and built on 26 arches, of which fifteen are Roman of the 1st century BC, while the remainder date from a 16th-century reconstruction after a flood.</li>
</ul>
<p>Nearby are the Mudéjar Romanesque church of Santiago (modern reconstruction) and the stone bull quoted in <i><a href="/wiki/Lazarillo_de_Tormes" title="Lazarillo de Tormes">Lazarillo de Tormes</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="University">University</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salamanca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: University">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:University_of_Salamanca_Fray_Luis_de_Leon.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1153" data-file-width="1537" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/University_of_Salamanca_Fray_Luis_de_Leon.jpg/220px-University_of_Salamanca_Fray_Luis_de_Leon.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/University_of_Salamanca_Fray_Luis_de_Leon.jpg/330px-University_of_Salamanca_Fray_Luis_de_Leon.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/University_of_Salamanca_Fray_Luis_de_Leon.jpg/440px-University_of_Salamanca_Fray_Luis_de_Leon.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:University_of_Salamanca_Fray_Luis_de_Leon.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Plateresque façade of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Salamanca" title="University of Salamanca">University of Salamanca</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Salamanca" title="University of Salamanca">University of Salamanca</a> was founded in 1134 and in 1218 it was given the royal charter of foundation ("Estudio General") by <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_IX_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="Alfonso IX of León">Alfonso IX of León</a>. It was the first university to receive the title of "University" in 1254. Under the patronage of the learned <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_X_of_Castile" title="Alfonso X of Castile">Alfonso X</a>, its wealth and reputation greatly increased (1252–1282), and its schools of <a href="/wiki/Canon_law" title="Canon law">canon law</a> and <a href="/wiki/Civil_law_(legal_system)" title="Civil law (legal system)">civil law</a> attracted students even from the Universities of Paris and Bologna.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (November 2010)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> In the 16th century, the city's fortunes depended on those of the University. About the time <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> was lecturing there on his discoveries, <a href="/wiki/Hern%C3%A1n_Cort%C3%A9s" title="Hernán Cortés">Hernán Cortés</a> took classes at Salamanca, but returned home in 1501 at age 17, without completing his course of study. (About ten years later the <i><a href="/wiki/Conquistador" title="Conquistador">conquistador</a></i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Francisco_V%C3%A1squez_de_Coronado" title="Francisco Vásquez de Coronado">Francisco Vásquez de Coronado</a> was born in Salamanca.)</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salamanca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Salamanca has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cold_semi-arid_climate" title="Cold semi-arid climate">cold semi-arid climate</a> (<i>BSk</i>), bordering on a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warm-summer_Mediterranean_climate" title="Warm-summer Mediterranean climate">warm-summer Mediterranean climate</a> (<i>Csb</i>) according to the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a>, with cool winters, and warm dry summers due to the altitude.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salamanca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Instalaciones_MIRAT_S.A._Salamanca.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2160" data-file-width="3840" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Instalaciones_MIRAT_S.A._Salamanca.JPG/220px-Instalaciones_MIRAT_S.A._Salamanca.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Instalaciones_MIRAT_S.A._Salamanca.JPG/330px-Instalaciones_MIRAT_S.A._Salamanca.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Instalaciones_MIRAT_S.A._Salamanca.JPG/440px-Instalaciones_MIRAT_S.A._Salamanca.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The city's economy is dominated by the university and tourism, but other sectors including agriculture and livestock rearing along with construction and manufacturing are also significant. Not surprisingly, in December 2007 83% of the working population, equivalent to 55,838, were employed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tertiary_sector" title="Tertiary sector">the service sector</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CE_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-CE-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry">Industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salamanca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Industrial activity accounted for 5% of the working population, or 3,340 workers employed over 360 businesses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CE_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-CE-9">[9]</a></sup> Two of the largest businesses, both of them numbered among the largest 100 enterprises in the region, are the veterinary <a href="/wiki/Vaccine" title="Vaccine">vaccine</a> manufacturer "Laboratorios Intervet", and the fertilizer specialist manufacturers <a class="extiw" href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mirat" title="es:Mirat">S.A. Mirat</a>, which is the city's oldest industrial company, having been established originally as a starch factory in 1812.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MIR_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-MIR-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_and_sports">Culture and sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salamanca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Culture and sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Old City of Salamanca was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1988.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 2002 Salamanca shared the title of <a href="/wiki/European_Capital_of_Culture" title="European Capital of Culture">European Capital of Culture</a> with <a href="/wiki/Bruges" title="Bruges">Bruges</a>. In 2005, Salamanca celebrated the 250th anniversary of the construction of the <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Mayor,_Salamanca" title="Plaza Mayor, Salamanca">Plaza Mayor</a> with a number of European events (<i>Plaza Mayor de Europa</i>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Holy_Week">Holy Week</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salamanca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Holy Week">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Holy_Week_in_Salamanca" title="Holy Week in Salamanca">Holy Week in Salamanca</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Holy_Week_in_Salamanca" title="Holy Week in Salamanca">Holy Week in Salamanca</a> (Semana Santa) is the most well-known of Salamanca's festivals. Salamanca is renowned for the solemn and sober processions celebrated during Holy Week. 16 confraternities, 10.000 brothers or "cofrades", 50 floats or "<a href="/wiki/Paso" title="Paso">pasos</a>" celebrate the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Passion_of_Christ" title="Passion of Christ">Passion of Christ</a> with 20 processions and thousands of followers, tourist and visitors.</p>
<p>Some of the celebrations have been performed for centuries. The confraternities carry artistic pasos created by important Spanish artists such as <a href="/wiki/Luis_Salvador_Carmona" title="Luis Salvador Carmona">Luis Salvador Carmona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alejandro_Carnicero" title="Alejandro Carnicero">Alejandro Carnicero</a> or <a href="/wiki/Mariano_Benlliure" title="Mariano Benlliure">Mariano Benlliure</a>. in 2003 the Semana Santa of Salamanca obtained the official declaration of International Touristic Interest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Other">Other</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salamanca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Other">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Salamanca is also famous throughout Spain and the rest of Europe for its celebrations of "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nochevieja" title="Nochevieja">Nochevieja</a> Universitaria," loosely translated as "University New Year".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="This claim needs references to better sources. (January 2016)">better source needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It is usually held on the Thursday of the last week of school in December and two weeks before the real New Year's Eve. On this day, students congregate in the <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Mayor,_Salamanca" title="Plaza Mayor, Salamanca">Plaza Mayor, Salamanca</a> to watch free performances and take part in the countdown to midnight.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salamanca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Salamanca offers the amenities of a larger city while retaining an intimate small town atmosphere. Since 1923, "Los Charros", formally the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_Deportiva_Salamanca" title="Union Deportiva Salamanca">Union Deportiva Salamanca</a>, have been the Salamanca football team. In 2013, the club went bankrupt and its activities were abandoned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cinema">Cinema</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salamanca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Cinema">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With its old streets being ideal for historical film settings Salamanca has been used several times in cinematic productions, including <i><a href="/wiki/1492:_Conquest_of_Paradise" title="1492: Conquest of Paradise">1492: Conquest of Paradise</a></i> by Ridley Scott and <i><a href="/wiki/Goya%27s_Ghosts" title="Goya's Ghosts">Goya's Ghosts</a></i> by Miloš Forman. Salamanca was also the setting for the 2008 political thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Vantage_Point_(film)" title="Vantage Point (film)">Vantage Point</a></i>, although the movie was almost exclusively filmed in Mexico.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gastronomy">Gastronomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salamanca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Gastronomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Salamanca has a very interesting and distinctive gastronomy. Among many dishes, <b>Chanfaina</b> (steamed rice with pork) is very popular. Another classic dish of the Salamancan, known as <b>Charreria</b> ("peasant lands"), is a <i>cocido</i>, a slow-cooked casserole including <a href="/wiki/Chickpea" title="Chickpea">chickpeas</a>. However, <b><a href="/wiki/Hornazo" title="Hornazo">Hornazo</a></b>, a meat pie, is the most popular dish of all.</p>
