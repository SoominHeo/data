<p><b>Salah</b> ("Muslim prayer", <span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%D8%B5%D9%84%D8%A7%D8%A9" title="wikt:صلاة">صلاة</a></span> or <a href="/wiki/Genitive_case#Arabic" title="Genitive case"><span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant:small-caps;">Gen</span></a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="DIN 31635 Arabic">ṣalāt</span></i>; pl. <span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">صلوات</span> <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="DIN 31635 Arabic">ṣalawāt</span></i>), called in some languages by the <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>-derived term <i>namāz</i> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="fa" xml:lang="fa">نَماز</span>‎‎) is one of the <a href="/wiki/Five_Pillars_of_Islam" title="Five Pillars of Islam">Five Pillars</a> in the faith of Islam and an obligatory religious duty for every Muslim. It is a physical, mental, and spiritual act of worship that is observed five times every day at prescribed times. In this ritual, the worshiper starts standing, bows, prostrates, and concludes while sitting on the ground.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> During each posture, the worshiper recites or reads certain verses, phrases and prayers. The word salah is commonly translated to prayer but this definition might be confusing. Muslims use the words "Dua" or "Supplication" when referring to the common definition of prayers which is "reverent petitions made to God."</p>
<p>Salat is preceded by <a href="/wiki/Wudu" title="Wudu">ritual ablution</a>. Salat consists of the repetition of a unit called a <i><a href="/wiki/Rakat" title="Rakat">rakʿah</a></i> (pl. <i>rakaʿāt</i>) consisting of prescribed actions and words. The number of obligatory (<i><a href="/wiki/Fard" title="Fard">fard</a></i>) <i>rakaʿāt</i> varies from two to four according to the time of day or other circumstances (such as Friday congregational worship, which has two rakats). Prayer is obligatory for all Muslims except those who are <a href="/wiki/Preadolescence" title="Preadolescence">prepubescent</a>, are <a href="/wiki/Menstruation" title="Menstruation">menstruating</a>, or are experiencing bleeding in the 40 days after childbirth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Every movement in the salat is accompanied by the <a href="/wiki/Takbir" title="Takbir">takbir</a> except the <a href="/wiki/Standing" title="Standing">standing</a> between the ruku and sujud, and the ending which has a derivation of the Muslim greeting <a href="/wiki/As-salamu_alaykum" title="As-salamu alaykum">As-salamu alaykum</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminology">Terminology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Terminology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Salat <i>(ṣalāt)</i> is an Arabic word whose basic meaning is "bowing, homage, worship, prayer." In its English usage, the reference of the word is almost always confined to the Muslim formal, obligatory worship described in this article.</p>
<p>Translating <i>salat</i> as "prayer" is not usually considered precise enough, as "prayer" can indicate several different ways of relating to God; personal prayer or supplication is called duʿāʾ (Arabic: دُعَاء, literally "invocation") in Islamic usage.</p>
<p>Muslims themselves use several terms to refer to <i>salah</i> depending on their language or culture. In many parts of the world, including many non-Arab countries such as <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, the Arabic term <i>salat</i> or <i>salah</i> is used. The other major term is the Persian word <i>namāz</i> (<span lang="fa" xml:lang="fa"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%D9%86%D9%85%D8%A7%D8%B2" title="wikt:نماز">نماز</a></span>), used by speakers of the <a href="/wiki/Indo-Iranian_languages" title="Indo-Iranian languages">Indo-Iranian languages</a> (e.g., <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurdish_language" title="Kurdish language">Kurdish</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urdu_language" title="Urdu language">Urdu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Balochi_language" title="Balochi language">Balochi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hindi" title="Hindi">Hindi</a>), as well as Turkish, Russian, Chinese, Bosnian and Albanian. In <a href="/wiki/North_Caucasian_languages" title="North Caucasian languages">North Caucasian languages</a>, the term is <i>lamaz</i> (<span lang="ce" xml:lang="ce">ламаз</span>) in <a href="/wiki/Chechen_language" title="Chechen language">Chechen</a>, <i>chak</i> (<span lang="lbe" xml:lang="lbe">чак</span>) in <a href="/wiki/Lak_language" title="Lak language">Lak</a> and <i>kak</i> in <a href="/wiki/Avar_language" title="Avar language">Avar</a> (<span lang="av" xml:lang="av">как</span>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Purpose_and_importance">Purpose and importance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Purpose and importance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The chief purpose of salah is to act as a person's communication with and remembrance of God. By reciting <a href="/wiki/Al-Fatiha" title="Al-Fatiha">"The Opening"</a>, the first <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sura" title="Sura">sura</a></i> (chapter) of the <a href="/wiki/Quran" title="Quran">Quran</a>, as required in daily worship, the worshiper can stand before God, thank and praise Him, and ask for guidance along the "<a href="/wiki/Sirat_al-Mustaqim" title="Sirat al-Mustaqim">Straight Path</a>".</p>
<p>Under the <a href="/wiki/Hanbali" title="Hanbali">Hanbali School</a> of thought, a person who doesn't pray five times a day is an unbeliever. The other <a href="/wiki/Fiqh#Sunni_jurisprudence" title="Fiqh">three Sunni schools of thought</a> say that the person who doesn't pray five times a day is an unholy sinner. Those who prescribe to the Hanbali view cite a <i><a href="/wiki/Hadith" title="Hadith">hadith</a></i> from <a href="/wiki/Sahih_Muslim" title="Sahih Muslim">Sahih Muslim</a> that states that prayer is a dividing line between a believer and a non-believer.</p>
<p>In addition, daily worship reminds Muslims to give thanks for God's blessings and that submission to God takes precedence over all other concerns, thereby revolving their life around God and submitting to His will. Worship also serves as a formal method of <i><a href="/wiki/Dhikr" title="Dhikr">dhikr</a></i> or remembering <a href="/wiki/Allah" title="Allah">Allah</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslims</a> believe that all <a href="/wiki/Prophets_and_messengers_in_Islam" title="Prophets and messengers in Islam">prophets</a> of <a href="/wiki/Allah" title="Allah">Allah</a> offered daily prayers and were humble in submission to the Oneness of Allah. Muslims also believe that the main duty of the prophets of Allah is to teach mankind to humbly submit themselves to Oneness of Allah, the God.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Quran, it is written that: "For, Believers are those who, when Allah is mentioned, feel a tremor in their hearts, and when they hear His signs rehearsed, find their faith strengthened, and put (all) their trust in their Lord;"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>"To those whose hearts, when God is mentioned, are filled with fear, who show patient perseverance over their afflictions, keep up regular prayer, and spend (in charity) out of what we have bestowed upon them."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Salah is also cited as a means of restraining a believer from social wrongs and moral deviancy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to a <i>hadith</i> in the collection <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sahih_Bukhari" title="Sahih Bukhari">Sahih Bukhari</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Muhammad" title="Muhammad">Muhammad</a> considered <i>salah</i> "the best deed".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The importance of the Salah was further demonstrated by <a href="/wiki/Muhammad" title="Muhammad">Muhammad</a> who on his deathbed and in the pangs of death would announce: <span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">الصَّلاةَ ، الصَّلاةَ وَمَا مَلَكَت أَيْمَانُكُم.</span> "The Salah, [I remind you of] the Salah; and [to treat well] those who are in your power."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conditions">Conditions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Conditions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Yemeni_fighter_praying.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

<p>At the bottom of the central nave of the prayer hall there is a niche (the <i>mihrab</i>) indicating the <i><a href="/wiki/Qibla" title="Qibla">qibla</a>.</i> This compulsory act of worship is obligatory for those who meet these conditions:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>are Muslim</li>
<li>are of sound mind</li>
<li>have reached the age of puberty (beginning at age seven is recommended).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>Elements that make salah valid:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Confidence of the time of worship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
<li>Facing the <i>qibla</i>, with the chest facing the direction of the <a href="/wiki/Kaaba" title="Kaaba">Kaaba</a>. The ill and the old are allowed leniency with posture.</li>
<li>Covering the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Awrah" title="Awrah">awrah</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
<li>Clean clothes, body, place of prostration.</li>
<li>Ritual purity (<i><a href="/wiki/Wudu" title="Wudu">wudu</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Tayammum" title="Tayammum">tayammum</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Ghusl" title="Ghusl">ghusl</a></i>)</li>
<li>Praying in front of a <i><a href="/wiki/Sutrah" title="Sutrah">sutrah</a></i> is recommended.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
</ul>
<p>The place of worship should be clean. In a few cases where blood is leaving the body, salah is forbidden until a later time. Women are not allowed to pray during their menses and for a period after childbirth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparation">Preparation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Preparation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cleanliness_and_dress">Cleanliness and dress</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Cleanliness and dress">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Islam advises that salah be performed in a ritually clean environment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cite_quran.7C5.7C6.7Cs.3Dns_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-cite_quran.7C5.7C6.7Cs.3Dns-18">[18]</a></sup> When worshipping, the clothes that are worn and the place of prayer must be clean. Both men and women are required to cover their bodies (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Awrah" title="Awrah">awrah</a></i>) in reasonably loose-fitting garments. The well-known adage or <i>hadith</i> by <a href="/wiki/Al-Nawawi" title="Al-Nawawi">al-Nawawi</a> that "purity is half the faith"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> illustrates how Islam has incorporated and modified existing rules of purity in its religious system.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ritual_ablution">Ritual ablution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Ritual ablution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wudu_Istiqlal_Mosque.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2136" data-file-width="2848" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Wudu_Istiqlal_Mosque.JPG/220px-Wudu_Istiqlal_Mosque.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Wudu_Istiqlal_Mosque.JPG/330px-Wudu_Istiqlal_Mosque.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Wudu_Istiqlal_Mosque.JPG/440px-Wudu_Istiqlal_Mosque.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Wudu_Istiqlal_Mosque.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Women at Istiqlal Mosque, <a href="/wiki/Jakarta" title="Jakarta">Jakarta</a> washing, <a href="/wiki/Wudu" title="Wudu">wudu</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Wudu" title="Wudu">Wudu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tayammum" title="Tayammum">Tayammum</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ghusl" title="Ghusl">Ghusl</a></div>
<p>Before conducting salah, a Muslim has to perform a ritual ablution. The minor ablution is performed using water (<i>wudu</i>), or sand (<i>tayammum</i>) when water is unavailable or not advisable to use for reasons such as illness. <i>Wudu</i> is performed by <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslims</a> according to the instructions of <a href="/wiki/God_in_Islam" title="God in Islam">God</a> given in the Quran:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>"O you who believe! when you rise up to prayer, wash your faces and your hands as far as the elbows, and wipe your heads and your feet to the ankles; and if you are under an obligation to perform a total ablution, then wash (yourselves) and if you are sick or on a journey, or one of you come from the privy, or you have touched the women, and you cannot find water, betake yourselves to pure earth and wipe your faces and your hands therewith, Allah does not desire to put on you any difficulty, but He wishes to purify you and that He may complete His favor on you, so that you may be grateful."</p>
<div class="templatequotecite"><cite>— Qur'an, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sura" title="Sura">sura</a> 5 (<a href="/wiki/Al-Ma%27ida" title="Al-Ma'ida">Al-Ma'ida</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ayat" title="Ayat">ayat</a> 6<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cite_quran.7C5.7C6.7Cs.3Dns_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-cite_quran.7C5.7C6.7Cs.3Dns-18">[18]</a></sup></cite></div>
</blockquote>
<p>More specifically, <i>wudu</i> is performed by Muslims by washing the hands, mouth, nose, arms, face, hair (often washing the hair is merely drawing the already wet hands from the fringe to the nape of the neck), ears, and feet three times each in that order. (It is not obligatory to wash the hair three times, once is sufficient, and men must also wash their beard and mustache when washing the face).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Call_to_prayer">Call to prayer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Call to prayer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>"Adhan" is the Islamic call to prayer, recited by the one known as the <a href="/wiki/Muezzin" title="Muezzin">muezzin</a> for the five obligatory prayers, traditionally from a tower called a <a href="/wiki/Minaret" title="Minaret">minaret</a>. A second call immediately before praying, known as the <a href="/wiki/Iqama" title="Iqama">Iqama</a>, is for the assembled Muslims in the prayer area to line up for the beginning of the prayers.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Intention">Intention</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Intention">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Niyyah" title="Niyyah">Niyyah</a></div>
<p>The person should be conscious and aware of the particular salah that is being offered, whether it is obligatory, if it is a missed (<i>qadha</i>) worship, performed individually or among the congregation, a shortened traveller's worship etc. The explicit verbalization of this intention is not required. The person should think his worship to be the Last Worship so that he may perform the best he can.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Components">Components</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Components">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Rakat" title="Rakat">Rakat</a></div>
<p>Each salah is made up of repeating units or cycles called <i><a href="/wiki/Rakat" title="Rakat">rakat</a></i>s. The number of <i>rakats</i> for the five daily worship can be found <a href="/wiki/Salah#The_five_daily_prayers" title="Salah">below</a>.</p>
<p>People who find it physically difficult can perform Salah in a way suitable for them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[note 1]</a></sup> A basic <i>rakat</i> is made up of these parts.</p>

<ul>
<li>If this is the first <i>rakah</i> then prayer is commenced by the saying of the <i><a href="/wiki/Takbir" title="Takbir">takbir</a></i>, which is <span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">اَللهُ أَكْبَرْ</span> (transliteration "allāhu 'akbar", meaning God is Great). The hands are raised touched with ears, fingers spread not apart nor together. This is done before, with or after saying the <i>takbir</i>. Both arms are placed on either the stomach or the chest or are left on the sides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haddad_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Haddad-21">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li>If this is the first <i>rakat</i>, a supplication praising God is said such as</li>
</ul>
<p><span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">سُبْحَاْنَكَ اَلْلّٰھُمَّ وَ بِحَمدِکَ وَ تَبَارَکَ اسْمُکَ وَ تَعَاْلٰی جَدُّکَ وَ لَا اِلٰہَ غَیْرُكَ</span></p>
<p><i>Subhaanak-Allaahumma, wa bihamdik, wa tabaarakasmuk, wa ta'aalaa jadduk, wa laa ilaaha ghayruk</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haddad_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-Haddad-21">[20]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>The recitation of the Quran begins by asking refuge with God from the accursed devil by reciting</li>
</ul>
<p><span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">أَعُوْذُ بِاللهِ مِنَ الشَّـيْطٰنِ الرَّجِيْمِ</span></p>
<p><i>a<sup>c</sup>ūdhu bi-llāhi min ash-shayṭāni r-rajīm</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haddad_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-Haddad-21">[20]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fatiha" title="Fatiha">Surah Al-Fatiha</a> is recited.</li>
<li>For the first or second rakat only, the recitation of Al-Fatiha is followed with a recitation from any other section from the Quran of choice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haddad_21-3"><a href="#cite_note-Haddad-21">[20]</a></sup> The customary practice is reciting the ayahs in order found in the Qur'an.</li>
<li>The <i>takbir</i> is said again and the hands are raised as previously described and the next position, bowing or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ruku%27" title="Ruku'">ruku'</a>, begins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haddad_21-4"><a href="#cite_note-Haddad-21">[20]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bowing">Bowing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Bowing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ruku%27" title="Ruku'">Ruku'</a></div>
<ul>
<li>The palms are placed on the knees. Ideally, fingers are spaced out and the back is erected at an angle at which poured water may not fall from it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haddad_21-5"><a href="#cite_note-Haddad-21">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li>Some of many types of remembrances of God are recited for this situation such as <span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">سبحان ربى العظيم</span> (transliteration <i>subḥāna rabbī al-<sup>c</sup>aẓīm</i>, meaning "Glory to my Lord, the Most Magnificent") three times or more.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haddad_21-6"><a href="#cite_note-Haddad-21">[20]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_standing">Second standing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Second standing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><i>I'tidal</i> is the standing again after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ruku%27" title="Ruku'">ruku'</a>. The back is straightened and the hands are raised as in takbir as mentioned before but saying <span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">سمع الله لمن حمده</span> (transliteration <i>sami<sup>c</sup>a allāhu li-man ḥamidah</i>, meaning "Allah listens and responds to the one who praises him.")<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haddad_21-7"><a href="#cite_note-Haddad-21">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li>Some of many praises to God are said for this situation such as <span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">ربنا لك الحمد</span> (transliteration <i>rabbanā wa-laka al-ḥamd</i>, meaning "O our Lord! And all praise is for You.")<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haddad_21-8"><a href="#cite_note-Haddad-21">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li>The takbir is said and the hands can be raised as mentioned before as the next position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haddad_21-9"><a href="#cite_note-Haddad-21">[20]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sujud" title="Sujud">Sujud</a></div>
<p>Each cycle of prayer contains 2 prostrations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haddad_21-10"><a href="#cite_note-Haddad-21">[20]</a></sup> During the prostration:</p>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Nose" title="Nose">nose</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Forehead" title="Forehead">forehead</a> are placed on the ground along with the knees and palms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haddad_21-11"><a href="#cite_note-Haddad-21">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li>Some of many types of remembrances of God are recited for this situation such as <span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">سبحان ربى الأعلى وبحمده</span> (transliteration <i>subḥāna rabbī al-'a<sup>c</sup>lā wa-bi-ḥamdih</i> meaning "Glory to my Lord, the Most High Most Praiseworthy") three times or more. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haddad_21-12"><a href="#cite_note-Haddad-21">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li>The takbir is said again and the hands can be raised as mentioned before while the next position, kneeling, begins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haddad_21-13"><a href="#cite_note-Haddad-21">[20]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_prostration">Second prostration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Second prostration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Second prostrations are done exactly as the first time.</li>
<li>The head is raised and the takbir is said again and the hands can be raised as mentioned before. For every second and last rakat only you would go to the sitting position again. Otherwise, the standing position begins again for the start of a new rakat.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_sitting">Second sitting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Second sitting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sitting_in_salah" title="Sitting in salah">Sitting in salah</a></div>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Tashahhud" title="Tashahhud">Tashahhud</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taslim" title="Taslim">Taslim</a></div>
<ul>
<li>If this is the second raka'ah, sitting is done as before. If this is not part of the second raka'ah, then the left thigh is leans against the ground and both feet are protruding from the right side with the right foot either upright or along the ground. The left palm leans on the left knee.</li>
<li>The right index finger is pointed towards the qibla.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haddad_21-14"><a href="#cite_note-Haddad-21">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Tashahhud" title="Tashahhud">Tashahhud</a> is recited.</li>
<li>Greetings on Muhammad, called the <a href="/wiki/Salawat" title="Salawat">aṣ-ṣalātu ʿala -n-nabī</a> are recited.</li>
<li>If this is not the last raka'ah, a new raka'ah begins by standing up again with the takbir and the hands can be raised as mentioned before.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haddad_21-15"><a href="#cite_note-Haddad-21">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li>If this is the last raka'ah, the greetings of <a href="/wiki/Taslim" title="Taslim">taslim</a> are said to the right such as <span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">السلام عليكم ورحمة الله</span> (transliteration "As-Salaamu 'alaykum wa rahmatullaah," meaning "May Allaah grant you peace and security, and may His Mercy be upon you") and then similarly to the left.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haddad_21-16"><a href="#cite_note-Haddad-21">[20]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Differences_in_practice">Differences in practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Differences in practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:November13,2013_-_Muharram_9,1435_-_Grand_Mosque_of_Nishapur_22.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2304" data-file-width="3072" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/November13%2C2013_-_Muharram_9%2C1435_-_Grand_Mosque_of_Nishapur_22.JPG/220px-November13%2C2013_-_Muharram_9%2C1435_-_Grand_Mosque_of_Nishapur_22.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/November13%2C2013_-_Muharram_9%2C1435_-_Grand_Mosque_of_Nishapur_22.JPG/330px-November13%2C2013_-_Muharram_9%2C1435_-_Grand_Mosque_of_Nishapur_22.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/November13%2C2013_-_Muharram_9%2C1435_-_Grand_Mosque_of_Nishapur_22.JPG/440px-November13%2C2013_-_Muharram_9%2C1435_-_Grand_Mosque_of_Nishapur_22.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>

</div>
</div>
<p>The Islamic worship practiced by one Muslim may differ from another's in minor details, which can affect the precise actions and words involved. Differences arise because of different interpretations of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Usul_al-fiqh" title="Usul al-fiqh">Islamic legal sources</a> by the different schools of law (<i><a href="/wiki/Madhhab" title="Madhhab">madhhabs</a></i>) in <a href="/wiki/Sunni_Islam" title="Sunni Islam">Sunni Islam</a>, and by different legal traditions within <a href="/wiki/Shia_Islam" title="Shia Islam">Shia Islam</a>. In the case of ritual worship these differences are generally minor, and should rarely cause dispute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[21]</a></sup> Muslims believe that Muhammad practiced, taught, and disseminated the worship ritual in the whole community of Muslims and made it part of their life. The practice has, therefore, been concurrently and perpetually practiced by the community in each of the generations. The authority for the basic forms of the Salah is neither the hadiths nor the Qur'an, but rather the consensus of Muslims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[22]</a></sup> Differences also occur due to optional (recommended rather than obligatory) articles of prayer procedure, for example, which verses of the Quran to recite. A 2015 Pew Research Center study found that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Women" title="Women">women</a> are two percent more likely than <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Men" title="Men">men</a> to pray on a daily basis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shia Muslims, after the end of the prayer, raise their hands three times, reciting <i>Allah hu akbar</i> and Sunnis just look at the left and right shoulder saying <i>salams</i>. Also Shias in the second Rakat often read "Qunoot", which for Sunnis is often done after salah.</p>
<p>In each of the positions, the Muslim usually needs to consider these, which may vary between schools and gender :</p>
<ul>
<li>Position of legs and feet.</li>
<li>Position of hands, including fingers</li>
<li>Place where eyes should focus</li>
<li>The minimum amount of adhkar/recitation, depending upon whether the worshipper is Imam or following the Imam or praying alone.</li>
<li>Loudness of recitation : audible, or moving of lips, or just listening</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types_of_prayers">Types of prayers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Types of prayers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prayers in Islam are classified into four categories based on degrees of obligation: <a href="/wiki/Fard" title="Fard">fard</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wajib" title="Wajib">wajib</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sunnah" title="Sunnah">sunnah</a>, and nafl.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fard_Salah">Fard Salah</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Fard Salah">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <i>Fard Salah</i> are all of the compulsory <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> prayers - the five daily prayers, as well as the Friday prayer (<a href="/wiki/Jumu%27ah" title="Jumu'ah">Salat al-Jumu'ah</a>) and the Eid prayers (<a href="/wiki/Eid_prayers" title="Eid prayers">Eid prayers</a>). Non-performance of any these prayers renders one a non-Muslim according to the stricter Hanbali <a href="/wiki/Madhhab" title="Madhhab">madhhab</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sunni_Islam" title="Sunni Islam">Sunni Islam</a>, while the other Sunni <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Madhhabs" title="Madhhabs">madhhabs</a> simply consider doing so a major sin. However, all four <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Madhhabs" title="Madhhabs">madhhabs</a> agree by consensus that denial of the compulsory status of these prayers invalidates the faith of those who do so, rendering them <a href="/wiki/Kafir" title="Kafir">non-Muslim</a>. Fardh prayers (as with all <a href="/wiki/Fard" title="Fard">fard</a> actions) are further classed as <i><a href="/wiki/Fard" title="Fard">fard al-ayn</a></i> (obligation of the self) and <i><a href="/wiki/Fard" title="Fard">fard al-kifayah</a></i> (obligation of sufficiency). Fard al-Ayn are actions considered obligatory on individuals, for which the individual will be held to account if the actions are neglected. Fard al-Kifayah are actions considered obligatory on the Muslim community at large, so that if some people within the community carry it out no Muslim is considered blameworthy, but if no one carries it out, all incur a collective punishment.</p>
<p>Men are required to perform the fard salat in <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/congregation" title="wiktionary:congregation">congregation</a> (<i>jama'ah</i>), behind an <a href="/wiki/Imam" title="Imam">imam</a> when they are able. According to most Islamic scholars, performing prayers in congregation is <a href="/wiki/Mustahabb" title="Mustahabb">mustahabb</a> (recommended) for men,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[35]</a></sup> when they are able, but is neither required nor forbidden for women.</p>

<p>Muslims are commanded to perform prayers five times a day. These prayers are obligatory on every Muslim who has reached the age of puberty, with the exception being those who are mentally ill, too physically ill for it to be possible, menstruating, or experiencing postnatal bleeding. Those who are ill or otherwise physically unable to offer their prayers in the traditional form are permitted to offer their prayers while sitting or lying, as they are able. The five prayers are each assigned to certain <a href="/wiki/Salah_times" title="Salah times">prescribed times</a> (<i>al waqt</i>) at which they must be performed, unless there is a compelling reason for not being able to perform them on time. These times are measured according to the movement of the sun. These are: near dawn (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fajr" title="Fajr">fajr</a></i>), after midday has passed and the sun starts to tilt downwards / Noon (<i><a href="/wiki/Zuhr_prayer" title="Zuhr prayer">zuhr</a></i>), in the afternoon (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asr" title="Asr">asr</a></i>), just after sunset (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maghrib" title="Maghrib">maghrib</a></i>) and around nightfall (<i><a href="/wiki/Isha_prayer" title="Isha prayer">Isha</a></i>). Under some circumstances ritual worship can be shortened or combined (according to prescribed procedures). In case a ritual worship is not performed at the right time, it must be performed later.</p>
<p>Some Muslims offer voluntary prayers (<i>sunna rawatib</i>) immediately before and after the prescribed fard prayers. Sunni Muslims classify these prayers as sunnah, while Shi'ah consider them nafil. <p>Salat al-Jumu'ah is a congregational prayer on Friday, which replaces the Zuhr prayer. It is compulsory upon men to perform it in congregation, while women may perform it so or may perform Zuhr salat instead. Salat al-Jumu'ah consists of a sermon (<i>khutba</i>) given by the speaker (<i>khatib</i>) after which two rakats are performed. There is no Salat al-Jumu'ah without a khutba.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wajib_salat">Wajib salat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Wajib salat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Wajib As-salat</i> are compulsory, non-performance of which renders one a sinner. However, the evidence of the obligation is open to interpretation, with some of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Madhab" title="Madhab">madhab</a> saying it is obligatory while others saying it is optional. To deny that a fard salah is obligatory is an act of disbelief while denying the obligation of a wajib salat is not disbelief. There are some who believe that as the 5 prayers are obligatory, it automatically renders all other prayers optional.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sunnah_salah">Sunnah salah</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Sunnah salah">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sunnah_salah" title="Sunnah salah">Sunnah salah</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">

Though not a mandatory part of the course, most <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muslims" title="Muslims">Muslims</a> supplicate after completing salah.</div>

<p><i>Sun'nah sal'ah</i> are optional and were additional voluntary prayers performed by <a href="/wiki/Muhammad" title="Muhammad">Muhammad</a> — they are of two types<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>— the <i>Sunnah Mu'akkaddah</i>, those practiced on a regular basis, which if abandoned cause the abandoner to be regarded as sinful by the Hanafi School and the <i>Sunnah Ghair Mu'akkaddah</i>, those practiced on a semi-regular practice by Muhammad about which all are agreed that their abandonment doesn't render one sinful.</p>
<p>Certain sunnah prayers have prescribed <i>waqts</i> associated with them. Those ordained for before each of the <i>fard</i> prayers must be performed between the first call to prayer (<i><a href="/wiki/Adhan" title="Adhan">adhan</a></i>) and the second call (<i><a href="/wiki/Iqama" title="Iqama">iqama</a></i>), which signifies the start of the <i>fard</i> prayer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-islamqa.info_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-islamqa.info-53">[44]</a></sup> Those sunnah ordained for after the fard prayers can be performed any time between the end of the <i>fard</i> prayers and the end of the current prayer's <i>waqt</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-islamqa.info_53-1"><a href="#cite_note-islamqa.info-53">[44]</a></sup> Any amount of extra <a href="/wiki/Rakat" title="Rakat">rakats</a> may be offered, but most <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Madh%27hab" title="Madh'hab">madha'ib</a></i> prescribe a certain number of rakats for each sunnah salah.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nafl_salah">Nafl salah</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Nafl salah">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Nafl_prayer" title="Nafl prayer">Nafl salah</a></div>
<p>Nafl salah (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Supererogatory" title="Supererogatory">supererogatory</a> prayers) are voluntary, and one may offer as many as he or she likes almost any time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[45]</a></sup> There are many specific conditions or situations when one may wish to offer nafl prayers. They cannot be offered at <a href="/wiki/Sunrise" title="Sunrise">sunrise</a>, <a href="/wiki/Noon" title="Noon">true noon</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Sunset" title="Sunset">sunset</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[46]</a></sup> The prohibition against salah at these times is to prevent the practice of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sun_worship" title="Sun worship">sun worship</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Salat-al-Witr">Salat-al-Witr</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Salat-al-Witr">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Witr" title="Witr">Witr</a></div>
<p>Witr is performed after the salah of Isha (dusk). Some Muslims consider witr wajib while others consider it optional. It may contain any odd number of rakats from one to eleven according to the different <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Madhab" title="Madhab">schools of jurisprudence</a>. However, Witr is most commonly offered with three rakats.</p>
<p>To end prayers for the night after Isha, the odd numbered rakats must have the <a href="/wiki/Niyyah" title="Niyyah">niyyah</a> of "wajib-ul-Lail", which is mandatory to "close" one's salah for that day.</p>
<p>Shi'ahs offer this as a one rakat salah at the end of salatul layl (the night prayer), which is an optional prayer according to some shi'ah scholars, and a wajib (obligatory) prayer according to others. This is to be prayed any time after Isha, up until fajr. The best time to pray it is the last third of the night (the night being divided into three, between maghrib and fajr of that night). It is considered highly meritorious by all shi'ah Muslims, and is said to bring numerous benefits to the believer, mainly gaining proximity to Allah. There are various methods of salatul-layl's performance, including shorter and longer versions, in the longer version the believer must perform 8 nawafil salah, in sets of 2 rakats each, then they must pray a 2 rakats salah called 'salatul shafa'ah' this is to include surah nas after surah fatihah in the first rakat and surah falaq after surah fatihah in the secound rakat, and unusually no <a href="/wiki/Qunut" title="Qunut">qunut</a> (a du'ah recited before going into ruku' of the second rakat of most prayers performed by shi'ahs) It is after this that the believer performs salatul witr, it's long method being - Starting with takbiratul ehram, then surah fatihah, then surah ikhlas, then surah falaq, then surah nas, then the hands are raised to recite qunut, upon which the believer can recite any du'a, however there are many recommended du'as for this purpose. Within qunut, the believer must pray for the forgiveness of 40 believers, then further prayers are read where the believer asks for forgiveness for himself a certain number of times using specified phrases and amounts of times to repeat those phrases. The believer then completes the salah in the usual way, by completing his qunut, reciting takbir whilst rasing his hands, going into ruku' and reciting the usual phrase for that, then returning up right and reciting takbir whilst doing so and upon being upright recites 'sami allahu liman hamida' (verily Allah has heard the one who has praised him) thereupon the believer recites takbir whilst raising his hands and goes into sajda. He recites the proscribed phrase in sajda rises, recites takbir whilst rising and then again whilst returnin to sajdah, then rises with takbir again and recites tashahud and salam, thus ending this prayer. It is then optional to recite certain other du'as and dhikr (remembrance of Allah through certain phrases and some of his names being repeated) It is then recommended to perform and sajdah ash-shukr (prostration of thanks) and to then recite ayatul kursi (verse of the throne) and then perform another sajdah ash-shukr.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Eid">Eid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Eid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Eid_prayers" title="Eid prayers">Salat al Eid</a></div>

<p>Eid salah is performed on the morning of <a href="/wiki/Eid_al-Fitr" title="Eid al-Fitr">Eid al-Fitr</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eid_al-Adha" title="Eid al-Adha">Eid al-Adha</a>. The Eid prayer is most likely an individual obligation (<i><a href="/wiki/Fard" title="Fard">fard al-ayn</a></i>) and Niyyah for both Eid salah is made as Wajib, though some Islamic scholars argue it is only a collective of the obligation(<i>fard al-kifayah</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[47]</a></sup> It consists of two rakats, with seven (or three for the followers Imam Hanafi) <i>takbirs</i> offered before the start of the first rakat and five (or three for the followers of Imam Hanafi) before the second. After the salah is completed, a sermon (<i><a href="/wiki/Khutbah" title="Khutbah">khutbah</a></i>) is offered. However, the khutbah is not an integral part of the Eid salah.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[48]</a></sup> The Eid salah must be offered between sunrise and true noon i.e. between the time periods for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fajr" title="Fajr">Fajr</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Zuhr_prayer" title="Zuhr prayer">Zuhr</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Istikhaarah">Istikhaarah</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Istikhaarah">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salaat-ul-Istikhaarah" title="Salaat-ul-Istikhaarah">Salaat-ul-Istikhaarah</a></div>
<p>Salat al-Istikhaarah is a prayer performed when a Muslim needs guidance on a particular matter, such as whether they should marry a certain person. In order to perform this <i>salah</i> one should pray a normal two rakats <i>salah</i> to completion. After completion one should say a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Du%27a" title="Du'a">du'a</a></i> called the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salaat-ul-Istikhaarah" title="Salaat-ul-Istikhaarah">Istikhaarah du'a</a>. The intention for the <i>salah</i> should be in one's heart to pray two rakats of <i>salah</i> followed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salaat-ul-Istikhaarah" title="Salaat-ul-Istikhaarah">Istikhaarah</a>. The <i>salah</i> can be performed at any of the times where <i>salah</i> is not forbidden.</p>
<p>The <i>salah</i> must be performed in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic language</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qada">Qada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Qada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In certain circumstances one may be unable to perform one's prayer within the prescribed <a href="/wiki/Time" title="Time">time</a> period (<i>waqt</i>). In this case, the prayer must be performed as soon as one is able to do so. Several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ahadith" title="Ahadith">Ahadith</a> narrate that <a href="/wiki/Muhammad" title="Muhammad">Muhammad</a> stated that permissible reasons to perform <a href="/wiki/Qada_(Islamic_term)" title="Qada (Islamic term)">Qada</a> Salah are forgetfulness and accidentally sleeping through the prescribed time. However, knowingly sleeping through the prescribed time for Salah is deemed impermissible.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qasr_and_Jam.27_bayn_as-Salaatayn">Qasr and Jam' bayn as-Salaatayn</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Qasr and Jam' bayn as-Salaatayn">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When travelling over long distances, one may shorten some prayers, a practice known as <i>qasr</i>. Furthermore, several prayer times may be joined, which is referred to as <i>Jam' bayn as-Salaatayn</i>. <i>Qasr</i> involves shortening the obligatory components of the <a href="/wiki/Zuhr_prayer" title="Zuhr prayer">Zuhr</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asr" title="Asr">Asr</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Isha_prayer" title="Isha prayer">Isha</a> prayers to two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rakats" title="Rakats">rakats</a>. <i>Jam' bayn as-Salaatayn</i> combines the <i>Zuhr</i> and <i>Asr</i> prayers into one prayer offered between <a href="/wiki/Noon" title="Noon">noon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sunset" title="Sunset">sunset</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maghrib" title="Maghrib">Maghrib</a> and <i>Isha</i> prayers into one between sunset and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fajr" title="Fajr">Fajr</a>. Neither <i>Qasr</i> nor <i>Jam' bayn as-Salaatayn</i> can be applied to the <i>Fajr</i> prayer.</p>
<p>There is no reference to <i>Qasr</i> during travel within the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qur%27an" title="Qur'an">Qur'an</a> itself; the Qur'an allows for <i>Qasr</i> when there is fear of attack, but does not forbid it for travel in non-hostile circumstances.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sajdah_of_forgetfulness">Sajdah of forgetfulness</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Sajdah of forgetfulness">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sujud_Sahwi" title="Sujud Sahwi">Sujud Sahwi</a></div>
<p>During the ritual <i>salat</i> prayer, if a person forgets to do one of the actions of prayer he can make up for certain actions by performing two sujud at the end of the prayer. This can only be done if specific types of actions are forgotten by the person praying.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tahiyyatul_masjid">Tahiyyatul masjid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Tahiyyatul masjid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Upon entering the mosque, "<i>Tahiyyatul masjid</i>" may be performed; this is to pay respects to the mosque. Every Muslim entering the mosque is encouraged to perform these two rakats.</p>

<p>Prayer in <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/congregation" title="wiktionary:congregation">congregation</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Jamia" title="Jamia">jama'ah</a></i>) is considered to have more social and spiritual benefit than praying by oneself. When praying in congregation, the people stand in straight parallel rows behind one person who conduct the prayer, called <a href="/wiki/Imam" title="Imam">imam</a>, and face the <a href="/wiki/Qibla" title="Qibla">qibla</a>. The imam is usually chosen to be a scholar or the one who has the best knowledge of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qur%27an" title="Qur'an">Qur'an</a>, preferably someone who has memorised it (a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hafiz_(Qur%27an)" title="Hafiz (Qur'an)"><i>hafiz</i></a>). In the first row behind the imam, if available, would be another <i>hafiz</i> to correct the imam in case a mistake is made during the performance of the salah. The prayer is performed as normal, with the congregation following the actions and movements of the imam as he performs the salah.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Standing_arrangement">Standing arrangement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: Standing arrangement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For two people of the same gender, the imam would stand on the left and the other person is on the right. For more than two people, the imam stands one row ahead of the rest.</p>
<p>When the worshippers consist of men and women combined, a man is chosen as the imam. In this situation, women are typically forbidden from performing this role. This point, though unanimously agreed on by the major schools of Islam, is <a href="/wiki/Women_as_imams" title="Women as imams">disputed by some groups</a>, based partly on a <i><a href="/wiki/Hadith" title="Hadith">hadith</a></i> whose interpretation is controversial. When the congregation consists entirely of women and pre-pubescent children, one woman is chosen as imam. When men, women, and children are praying, the children's rows are usually between the men's and women's rows, with the men at the front and women at the back. Another configuration is where the men's and women's rows are side by side, separated by a curtain or other barrier, with the primary intention being for there to be no direct line of sight between male and female worshippers, following a Qur'anic injunction toward men and women each lowering their gazes (Qur'an 24:30–31).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Conducting">Conducting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: Conducting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The prayer commences with the imam saying “Allahu akbar” out loud. For the five daily prayers, the imam would read the surah fatiha and a following section of the quran out loud only for the first two rakats of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fajr" title="Fajr">Fajr</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maghrib" title="Maghrib">Maghrib</a> and <a href="/wiki/Isha_prayer" title="Isha prayer">Isha</a>. To move between positions, the imam would say “Allahu akbar” out loud, such as when going to the bowing position, except when standing up again from the bowing position, when the imam would say “SamiAllahu liman hamidah” out loud. The prayer is concluded with the imam saying the <a href="/wiki/Taslim" title="Taslim">taslim</a> out loud.</p>
<p>A worshiper who has joined a congregation prayer late after missing rakats is called a <a href="/wiki/Masbuq" title="Masbuq">masbuq</a>. The masbuq would join with the rest of the group in prayer, however, when the taslim is said at the end, he or she would not say the taslim but would instead stand up and continue for the number of rakats missed. If he or she joined after the bowing stage of a rakat, then he or she would have considered to have missed that rakat.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Quranist_Salah">Quranist Salah</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=35" title="Edit section: Quranist Salah">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The concept of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quran_alone" title="Quran alone">Quranist</a> Salah Timings has been discussed in Hujjat Allah Al-Baligha (Arabic/Urdu) by Shah Waliullah. He said that there are 3 Salah timings (prayers) instead of the 5 Salahs (prayers).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>The number of regular Salah mentioned by their respective names in Arabic in the Qur'an are three as follows:</p>
<ol>
<li>Ṣalāt Fajr (<i>Dawn Prayer</i>) <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cite_quran.7C24.7C58.7Cs.3Dns_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-cite_quran.7C24.7C58.7Cs.3Dns-59">[50]</a></sup></li>
<li>Aṣ-Ṣalāt al-Wusṭā ( <i>The Middle Prayer</i>) <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cite_quran.7C17.7C78.7Cs.3Dns_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-cite_quran.7C17.7C78.7Cs.3Dns-61">[52]</a></sup></li>
<li>Ṣalāt <sup>c</sup>Ishā' (<i>Night Prayer</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cite_quran.7C24.7C58.7Cs.3Dns_59-1"><a href="#cite_note-cite_quran.7C24.7C58.7Cs.3Dns-59">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cite_quran.7C11.7C114.7Cs.3Dns_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-cite_quran.7C11.7C114.7Cs.3Dns-62">[53]</a></sup></li>
</ol>
<p>The other Salah normally prayed by most Muslims are not explicitly mentioned in Qur'an by specific Arabic terms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cite_quran.7C24.7C58.7Cs.3Dns_59-2"><a href="#cite_note-cite_quran.7C24.7C58.7Cs.3Dns-59">[50]</a></sup> Most Muslims pray them by giving reference to the Hadith of Muhammad, and the consensus of Muslims, whereas Quranists may only pray the 3 times mentioned.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Salah_timings_according_to_Quranists_and_other_minorities">Salah timings according to Quranists and other minorities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=36" title="Edit section: Salah timings according to Quranists and other minorities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Salah timings of Qur'an are mentioned, in particular three salah times are described <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cite_quran.7C11.7C114.7Cs.3Dns_62-1"><a href="#cite_note-cite_quran.7C11.7C114.7Cs.3Dns-62">[53]</a></sup> and that they are recorded in a written document.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[54]</a></sup> The Qur'an states that you should interrupt any activity you were previously doing to pray, as this betters the individual.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[55]</a></sup> Also noted is the volume at which the salah should be uttered, somewhere in between spoken aloud and spoken in a low tone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>The time for performing Fajr (Dawn) Prayer starts when the first thin ray of light is observed in the sky<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[57]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[58]</a></sup> and ends at the first "taraf" (terminal) of the day, or sunrise <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cite_quran.7C11.7C114.7Cs.3Dns_62-2"><a href="#cite_note-cite_quran.7C11.7C114.7Cs.3Dns-62">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p>The time for performing middle or Salat Al-Wusta can be observed from the moment the sun begins its descend from its highest point in the sky (duluk al shams) until sunset but before the darkness of the night (ghasaq al-layl) starts to set in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cite_quran.7C17.7C78.7Cs.3Dns_61-1"><a href="#cite_note-cite_quran.7C17.7C78.7Cs.3Dns-61">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>'Duluk ash-shams' can also mean 'sunset.' It literally means 'the rubbing of the sun.' The most accepted meaning is that this means the apparent rubbing of the sun with the horizon at sunset. Although, the meaning of a declining noon sun can also be found in Classical Arabic sources. Literally, it can imply a meaning of both sunset and dawn in its meaning of a sun making apparent contact i.e. 'rubbing' with the horizon.</p>
<p>The Qur'an, if we take the understanding of 'a declining noon sun' implies that the time of the Middle prayer ends with sunset.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some Quranists however believe that there are only two Salah, dawn and dusk including the times of night close to these two periods; which is Based on the Qur'an (24:58). Some groups like Ahl Al-Quran and The Submitters believe that the 5 Salah as they are practiced by Muslims today were passed down from Abraham generationally through the Arabs and the Children of Israel, to then be inherited by those who adopted the Qur'an (and rejected by most Jews and Christians), as a ritual of the religion of Abraham.The emphasize on constantly mentioning of God in holy books, is believed to be about the believers' righteous endeavors and not just performing Salah. For instance, in (51:56) when God states: "<i>I did not create the jinn and the humans except to worship Me;</i>" worshiping God could not mean just performing the prayer ritual all day long.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Salah_in_Babi_Faith">Salah in Babi Faith</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=37" title="Edit section: Salah in Babi Faith">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>It was written in Siyyid 'Ali Muhammad-i-Shirazi al-Bab's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_Bayan" title="Arabic Bayan">Arabic Bayan</a> to split each day into five parts starting at 'nightfall'. The <a href="/wiki/Adhan" title="Adhan">Adhan</a> is to be recited in an elevated place so others can hear it. Also, He has prescribed a total of 19 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rakats" title="Rakats">rakats</a> each day. One is to seat oneself after each rakat and the hands are to be held like a book once.</p>
<ul>
<li>1st prayer: repeat 19x: 'La ilaha illa-Allah' (There is no god but God) and 19x 'Allah'u'ighma' (God is in no need of nothing/fainting);</li>
<li>2nd prayer: 19x 'La illa-Allah' (the only God) and 19x 'Allah'u'A'slem' (God is most wise/origin);</li>
<li>3rd prayer: 19x 'La illaha illa-Allah' and 19x 'Allah'u'APaken' (God is highest ordainers/eraser);</li>
<li>4th prayer: 19x 'La ilaha illa-Allah' and 19x 'Allah'u'Amlek (God possesses the most);</li>
<li>5th prayer: 19x 'La illaha illa-Allah' and 19x 'Allah'u'Aslet' (God is most victorious/continued).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[60]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
