<p><b>Salah Aboud Mahmoud</b> (born 1950; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: صلاح عبود محمود) is a former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_of_Iraq_under_Ba%27athist_rule" title="History of Iraq under Ba'athist rule">Iraqi</a> <a href="/wiki/Iraqi_Army" title="Iraqi Army">Army</a> general, best known for his role in <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Khafji" title="Battle of Khafji">Battle of Khafji</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_73_Easting" title="Battle of 73 Easting">73 Easting</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_War" title="Gulf War">Persian Gulf War</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah_Aboud_Mahmoud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On January 29, 1991, Salah took part in battle with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coalition_of_Gulf_War" title="Coalition of Gulf War">coalition forces</a> to take control of the <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabian</a> city of <a href="/wiki/Khafji" title="Khafji">Khafji</a>. Mahmoud also took part in the <a href="/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93Iraq_War" title="Iran–Iraq War">Iran–Iraq War</a> of 1980–1988, along with the tank battle of <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_73_Easting" title="Battle of 73 Easting">73 Easting</a>.</p>
<p>Salah was appointed commander of the Iraqi Third Corps in the aftermath of the Iran–Iraq War, a regular process in the Iraqi military to ensure that former high-ranking officers did not pose a threat to the Ba'athist Iraqi government. He was later governor of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dhi_Qar_Province" title="Dhi Qar Province">Dhi Qar Province</a>, a Shia province which had briefly been taken by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1991_Iraqi_uprising" title="1991 Iraqi uprising">1991 Iraqi insurgency</a> before it was brutally suppressed.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s">1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah_Aboud_Mahmoud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1990s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In December 1994, Major-General <a href="/wiki/Wafiq_Al-Samarrai" title="Wafiq Al-Samarrai">Wafiq Al-Samarrai</a> defected to Jordan and called on officers to revolt against Saddam Hussein's regime. Salah was one of them he called on. He did not, and despite his connections to many of the purged officers he was never executed. Rather, he was gradually forced out of his government roles. President Hussein divided Iraq into four administrative regions in 1998. Many expected Salah would be recalled to the military and appointed to the Central Euphrates governorship as governor <a href="/wiki/Mizban_Khadr_al-Hadi" title="Mizban Khadr al-Hadi">Mizban</a> had been dismissed. However this did not come to pass and Mizban was reinstated.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Invasion_of_Iraq">Invasion of Iraq</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salah_Aboud_Mahmoud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Invasion of Iraq">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Iraq" title="Invasion of Iraq">Invasion of Iraq</a> in March 2003, Salah disappeared and his current whereabouts are unknown.</p>
