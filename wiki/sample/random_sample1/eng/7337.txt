<p><b>Salem</b> (<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:Salem.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a> </span><a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/0a/Salem.ogg" title="Salem.ogg">pronunciation</a></span> <small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:Salem.ogg" title="File:Salem.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span>) is a city in <a href="/wiki/Salem_district" title="Salem district">Salem district</a> in the Indian state of <a href="/wiki/Tamil_Nadu" title="Tamil Nadu">Tamil Nadu</a>. It is located about 160 kilometres (100 mi) northeast of <a href="/wiki/Coimbatore" title="Coimbatore">Coimbatore</a>, 186 kilometres (116 mi) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Bangalore" title="Bangalore">Bangalore</a> and about 340 kilometres (210 mi) southwest of the state capital, <a href="/wiki/Chennai" title="Chennai">Chennai</a>. Salem is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_in_India_by_population" title="List of cities in India by population">Fifth</a> largest city in <a href="/wiki/Tamil_Nadu" title="Tamil Nadu">Tamil Nadu</a> by population and covers 91.34 km<sup>2</sup> (35.27 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>Cheralam, Shalya, Kurukshetra, Sayilam and Sailam are found on inscriptions referring to the country around the hills: Nagarmalai in the north, Jeragamalai in the south, Kanjamalai in the west and Godumalai in the east. Salem is claimed as the birthplace of the Tamil <a href="/wiki/Avvaiyar" title="Avvaiyar">Avvaiyar</a> poets. It and the surrounding hilly regions were part of the <a href="/wiki/Chera_dynasty" title="Chera dynasty">Chera dynasty</a>, and the city was ruled by kings known as the Kurunila Mannargal. Salem was later ruled by Gatti Mudalis, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poligars" title="Poligars">Poligars</a> who built temples and forts in and around the city.</p>
<p>During the early 18th century, after the <a href="/wiki/Mangammal#War_with_Mysore" title="Mangammal">Mysore-Madurai war</a>, the region was ruled by <a href="/wiki/Hyder_Ali" title="Hyder Ali">Hyder Ali</a>. Salem was taken from Hyder Ali by a Colonel Wood at the beginning of 1768, and recaptured by Ali in late 1772. Under <a href="/wiki/Robert_Clive" title="Robert Clive">Robert Clive</a> it was occupied by a detachment of a regiment stationed at Sankagiri Durg, remaining a military station until 1861. Salem and Sankagiri were the sites of battles between <a href="/wiki/Kongu_Nadu" title="Kongu Nadu">Kongu Nadu</a> soldiers led by <a href="/wiki/Dheeran_Chinnamalai" title="Dheeran Chinnamalai">Dheeran Chinnamalai</a> and the British. Chinnamalai was hanged in the Sankagiri fort, which became the British army headquarters. According to volume five of <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Thurston" title="Edgar Thurston">Edgar Thurston</a>'s <i>Castes and Tribes of Southern India</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Edgar_Thurston_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Edgar_Thurston-4">[4]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Chola_Nadu" title="Chola Nadu">Chola Nadu</a> region was ruled by a series of twenty-eight kings before being conquered by the <a href="/wiki/Chola_dynasty" title="Chola dynasty">Cholas</a> of <a href="/wiki/Thanjavur" title="Thanjavur">Tanjore</a>.</p>

<p>Salem is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Salem%2C_Tamil_Nadu&amp;params=11.669437_N_78.140865_E_"><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">11°40′10″N</span> <span class="longitude">78°08′27″E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">11.669437°N 78.140865°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">11.669437; 78.140865</span></span></span></a></span>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> at an average elevation of 278 m (912 ft). The city is surrounded by hills: Nagaramalai on the north, Jarugumalai on the south, <a href="/wiki/Kanjamalai" title="Kanjamalai">Kanjamalai</a> on the west, <a href="/wiki/Godumalai" title="Godumalai">Godumalai</a> on the east and the <a href="/wiki/Shevaroy_Hills" title="Shevaroy Hills">Shevaroy Hills</a> on the northeast. Kariyaperumal Hill is in southwestern Salem. The <a href="/wiki/Thirumanimutharu_River" title="Thirumanimutharu River">Thirumanimutharu River</a> flows through the city, dividing it in two. The fort area is the oldest part of Salem.</p>
<p>The city's climate is dry, except during the monsoon season. January and February are generally pleasant; the dry summer begins in March, with the year's highest temperatures during May. The weather becomes more temperate in June and July, and August is cloudy but still dry. The <a href="/wiki/Monsoon#Northeast_monsoon" title="Monsoon">northeast monsoon</a> occurs from September to November, and December is clear and pleasant.</p>

<p>Salem is the headquarters of <a href="/wiki/Salem_district" title="Salem district">Salem district</a>. The town was constituted as a municipality in 1867, and was upgraded to a special-grade municipality in 1979 and to a municipal corporation on 1 April 1994. The Salem municipal corporation has 60 wards, each with an elected councillor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The functions of the municipal corporation are divided into six departments: general administration and personnel, engineering, revenue, public health, city planning and information technology (IT). All six departments are governed by a municipal commissioner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Legislative power is vested in the 60-member council,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> headed by an elected chairperson and assisted by a deputy chairperson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Law and order is maintained by the Salem city subdivision of the <a href="/wiki/Tamil_Nadu_Police" title="Tamil Nadu Police">Tamil Nadu Police</a>, headed by a <a href="/wiki/Deputy_superintendent" title="Deputy superintendent">Deputy superintendent</a>. Special units include prohibition enforcement, district crime, social justice and human rights, district crime records and a district-level special branch headed by a superintendent of police.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Salem is a part of the <a href="/wiki/Salem_North_(State_Assembly_Constituency)" title="Salem North (State Assembly Constituency)">Salem North</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salem_West_(State_Assembly_Constituency)" title="Salem West (State Assembly Constituency)">Salem West</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salem_South_(State_Assembly_Constituency)" title="Salem South (State Assembly Constituency)">Salem South</a> assembly constituencies delineated in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The city elects the three members to the <a href="/wiki/Tamil_Nadu_Legislative_Assembly" title="Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly">Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly</a> once every five years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Present MLAs are <a href="/wiki/R._Mohan_Raj" title="R. Mohan Raj">R. Mohan Raj</a> (Salem North) from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Desiya_Murpokku_Dravidar_Kazhagam" title="Desiya Murpokku Dravidar Kazhagam">Desiya Murpokku Dravidar Kazhagam</a>, <a href="/wiki/M._K._Selvaraju" title="M. K. Selvaraju">M. K. Selvaraju</a> (Salem West) and <a href="/wiki/G._Venkatachalam" title="G. Venkatachalam">G. Venkatachalam</a> (Salem South) from <a href="/wiki/All_India_Anna_Dravida_Munnetra_Kazhagam" title="All India Anna Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam">All India Anna Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLA1_of_Salem_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-MLA1_of_Salem-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLA2_of_Salem_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-MLA2_of_Salem-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLA3_of_Salem_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-MLA3_of_Salem-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Until 2008, the city was part of the <a href="/wiki/Salem_%E2%80%93_I_(State_Assembly_Constituency)" title="Salem – I (State Assembly Constituency)">Salem I</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salem_%E2%80%93_II_(State_Assembly_Constituency)" title="Salem – II (State Assembly Constituency)">Salem II</a> assembly constituencies. Since 1977, the ADMK party won the Salem I assembly seat five times (1977, 1980, 1984, 2001 and 2006); <a href="/wiki/Dravida_Munnetra_Kazhagam" title="Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam">Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam</a> won twice (1989 and 1996), and the <a href="/wiki/Indian_National_Congress" title="Indian National Congress">Indian National Congress</a> (INC) won in 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Since 1977, the ADMK won the Salem II assembly seat three times (1980, 1984 and 1991) and the DMK also won three times (1989, 1996 and 2006); the <a href="/wiki/Janata_Party" title="Janata Party">Janata Party</a> (JP) won in 1977 and <a href="/wiki/Pattali_Makkal_Katchi" title="Pattali Makkal Katchi">Pattali Makkal Katchi</a> (PMK) won in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>The city is part of the <a href="/wiki/Salem_(Lok_Sabha_constituency)" title="Salem (Lok Sabha constituency)">Salem</a> <a href="/wiki/Lok_Sabha" title="Lok Sabha">Lok Sabha</a> constituency consisting of six <a href="/wiki/Electoral_district" title="Electoral district">assembly constituencies</a>: <a href="/wiki/Omalur_(State_Assembly_Constituency)" title="Omalur (State Assembly (State Assembly Constituency)">Salem West</a>, <a href="/wiki/Veerapandi_(State_Assembly_Constituency)" title="Veerapandi (State Assembly Constituency)">Veerapandi</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" 1952, the Salem parliament seat was held by the Indian National Congress eight times (1952, 1957, 1962, 1984, 1989, 1991 and 2004), by the ADMK four times (1977, 1991, 2009 and 2014), by DMK three times (1967, 1971 and 1980), and once each by an independent (1998) and the <a href="/wiki/Tamil_Maanila_Congress" title="Tamil Maanila Congress">Tamil Maanila Congress</a> (1996).<sup <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salem,_Tamil_Nadu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Salem is a major textile centre in Tamil Nadu, with more than 125 spinning mills, weaving units and garment units. Until the 1960s, it had less few spinning mills. Private handloom weaving began to increase in the region after the 1960s and during the 1980s, the textile industry expanded with major spinning mills and dying units established supporting the industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>The area also houses a number of <a href="/wiki/Sago" title="Sago">sago</a> factories for the production starch. In Salem district, 34,000 hectares (130 sq mi) of land are devoted to <a href="/wiki/Cassava" title="Cassava">cassava</a> and 650 industrial units are engaged in tapioca processing. In 1981, the Salem Starch and Sago Manufacturers Service Industrial Co-operative Society (SAGOSERVE) was established to promote the sago industry and nearly 80 percent of the national demand for sago and starch is met by SAGOSERVE.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> In and around Salem cassava yields are 25–30 tons per hectare, one of the highest in the world; the national average is 19 tons per hectare, and the world average is 10 tons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Salem Steel Plant, a unit of the <a href="/wiki/Steel_Authority_of_India" title="Steel Authority of India">Steel Authority of India</a>, produces cold-rolled <a href="/wiki/Stainless_steel" title="Stainless steel">stainless steel</a> and a hot-rolled stainless-carbon steel alloy. The plant is being expanded and modernised, with plans for steel-melting and continuous-casting facilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> The Southern Iron and Steel Company (part of <a href="/wiki/JSW_Steel_Ltd#Salem_Works" title="JSW Steel Ltd">JSW Steel</a>) have their first integrated steel plant in Salem for the production of <a href="/wiki/Thermomechanical_processing" title="Thermomechanical processing">TMT</a> corrosion-resistant bars and <a href="/wiki/Alloy_steel" title="Alloy steel">alloy steels</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> The Salem region is rich in mineral ores, with some of the largest <a href="/wiki/Magnesite" title="Magnesite">magnesite</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bauxite" title="Bauxite">bauxite</a> deposits in India. Public and private magnesite factories include Burn Standard and Company, Dalmia Magnesites and Tata Refractories.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Leigh Bazaar is the region's largest market for agricultural products. The Tamil Nadu government and the <a href="/wiki/Electronics_Corporation_of_Tamil_Nadu" title="Electronics Corporation of Tamil Nadu">Electronics Corporation of Tamil Nadu</a> are planning a 160-acre (65 ha) IT park in the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> The Steel Authority of India is planning a 250-acre (100 ha) steel <a href="/wiki/Special_economic_zone" title="Special economic zone">special economic zone</a> in its Salem plant,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> and an electrical and electronics <a href="/wiki/Industrial_park" title="Industrial park">industrial estate</a> is in the Suramangalam area of the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>

<p>Salem had a population of 826,267 in the <a href="/wiki/2011_Census_of_India" title="2011 Census of India">2011 census</a>. There were 987 females for every 1,000 males, significantly higher than the national average of 929.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dashboard_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-dashboard-48">[48]</a></sup> A total of 79,067 were under age six: 40,570 males and 38,497 females. The city's literacy rate was 76.37 percent, higher than the national average of 72.99 percent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dashboard_48-1"><a href="#cite_note-dashboard-48">[48]</a></sup> Salem had 215,747 households and a total of 332,147 workers: 1,599 farmers, 3,040 agricultural labourers, 32,597 in household industries, 278,892 other workers, 16,019 part-time workers, 165 part-time farmers, 544 part-time agricultural workers, 1,937 part-time workers in household industries and 13,373 other part-time workers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011census_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-2011census-1">[1]</a></sup> As per the religious census of 2011, Salem had 89.79% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hindus" title="Hindus">Hindus</a>, 7.48% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muslims" title="Muslims">Muslims</a>, 2.36% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christians" title="Christians">Christians</a>, 0.02% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sikhs" title="Sikhs">Sikhs</a>, 0.01% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buddhists" title="Buddhists">Buddhists</a>, 0.11% <a href="/wiki/Jainism" title="Jainism">Jains</a>, 0.2% following other religions and 0.02% following no religion or did not indicate any religious preference.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-religion2011_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-religion2011-49">[49]</a></sup></p>

<p>Salem has a number of <a href="/wiki/Hindu" title="Hindu">Hindu</a> temples. The Kottai Mariamman Temple, dedicated to the goddess <a href="/wiki/Mariamman" title="Mariamman">Mariamman</a>, has an annual five-day festival during the <a href="/wiki/Tamil_calendar#Months" title="Tamil calendar">Tamizh month</a> of Aadi (mid-July to mid-August). The temple's <a href="/wiki/Gopuram" title="Gopuram">gopuram</a> is made up of seven tiers. Sugavaneshwarar Temple is dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Shiva" title="Shiva">Shiva</a>, and according to mythology the sage Sugha Brahmarishi worshiped here. Arunagirinadhar sang a song about <a href="/wiki/Kartikeya" title="Kartikeya">Muruga</a> in the temple, which was built during the 13th century by Mamannan Sundara Pandiyan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> Kottai Perumal Temple, Skandhashram, Kothandaramar Temple and Sithar Koil are also in Salem. Oothumali Murugan Temple, Pandurangan Hill Temple, Kumaragiri Hill Murugan Temple and Kariperumal Karadu Hill Temple are in the hills in Salem. The <a href="/wiki/International_Society_for_Krishna_Consciousness" title="International Society for Krishna Consciousness">International Society for Krishna Consciousness</a> (ISKCON) has built a temple in Karuppur, near the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>Jama Masjid, on the southern bank of the Thirumanimutharu River in the heart of the city, is Salem's oldest mosque. It was reportedly built by the Mysore ruler Tippu Sulthan, who is said to have prayed here. The <i>masjid inam</i> granted to the mosque in 1862 was confirmed by the British with a special resolution in 1880. <a href="/wiki/Christ_Church,_Salem" title="Christ Church, Salem">Christ Church</a> is located on Fort Road and the church was consecrated in 1875.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hindu-2014_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hindu-2014-52">[52]</a></sup> The other popular churches in and around the city includes Lechler Memorial Church, the oldest church in Salem, was built in 1856 by a German missionary in Fort Salem. Infant Jesus Church near Four Roads, the Calvary Assembly of God Church on the Bangalore highway in Mamangam and Reformed Baptist Church in the Yercaud foothills are other churches in the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shopping_and_recreation">Shopping and recreation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salem,_Tamil_Nadu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Shopping and recreation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Salem's traditional shopping areas are in the Town area, with major retailers in Bazaar Street, Car Street, First Agraharam and Chinna Kadai Street. Shevapet and the Fort area are noted for hardware and furniture, and Leigh Bazaar in Shevapet is the main wholesale market. Reliance Shopping Mall, the city's largest shopping complex is situated near Five Roads.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> Kurumbapatti Zoological Park and Anna Park are government-run parks. Paravasa Ulagam and Dream Land are amusement parks in the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (July 2015)">not in citation given</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p>Salem has six <a href="/wiki/Arterial_road" title="Arterial road">arterial roads</a>: Omalur Road, Cherry Road, Saradha College Road, Junction Main Road, Gugai Main Road and Attur Road.</p>
<pre>
Three <a href="/wiki/National_Highway_(India)" title="National Highway (India)">National Highways</a> originate in or pass through: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Highway_7_(India)(old_numbering)" title="National Highway 7 (India)(old numbering)">NH 7</a> (<a href="/wiki/Varanasi" title="Varanasi">Varanasi</a> – <a href="/wiki/Kanyakumari" title="Kanyakumari">Kanyakumari</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Highway_47_(India)(old_numbering)" title="National Highway 47 (India)(old numbering)">NH 47</a> (Salem – <a href="/wiki/Kanyakumari" title="Kanyakumari">Kanyakumari</a> via <a href="/wiki/Coimbatore" title="Coimbatore">Coimbatore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kochi" title="Kochi">Kochi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kollam" title="Kollam">Kollam</a>) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Highway_68_(India)(old_numbering)" title="National Highway 68 (India)(old numbering)">NH 68</a> (Salem – <a href="/wiki/Ulundurpet" title="Ulundurpet">Ulundurpet</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup>
</pre>
<p>Salem is the headquarters of the Salem division of <a href="/wiki/Tamil_Nadu_State_Transport_Corporation" title="Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation">TNSTC</a>. The city has two major bus stations: the MGR Integrated Bus Terminus in Meyyanoor and the Town Bus Station (Old Bus Stand) in the town area. Intercity and interstate routes and private buses originate at the Central Bus Stand, and local buses originate at the Old Bus Stand.</p>
<pre>
The Anna Flyover is the oldest in the city, and the Trumpet Interchange was built in the realignment of NH 47 to ease traffic towards <a href="/wiki/Coimbatore" title="Coimbatore">Coimbatore</a>.
</pre>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">Rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salem,_Tamil_Nadu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Salem_Junction_railway_station" title="Salem Junction railway station">Salem Junction</a> is located in Suramangalam, 5 kilometres (3.1 mi) west of the city. In 2005, the <a href="/wiki/Railway_Board" title="Railway Board">Railway Board</a> approved the creation of a <a href="/wiki/Salem_railway_division" title="Salem railway division">Salem railway division</a> from <a href="/wiki/Palakkad_railway_division" title="Palakkad railway division">Palakkad</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tiruchirappalli_railway_division" title="Tiruchirappalli railway division">Tiruchirapalli divisions</a>.</p>
<pre>
It is the fourth-largest of the six <a href="/wiki/Southern_Railway_zone" title="Southern Railway zone">Southern Railway zone</a> divisions.
</pre>
<p>It Has Six Platforms and well connected with big cities like <a href="/wiki/Chennai" title="Chennai">Chennai</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bnagalore&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bnagalore (page does not exist)">Bnagalore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coimbatore" title="Coimbatore">Coimbatore</a>.Nearly 50,000-75,000 Passengers will travel daily by train.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Air">Air</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salem,_Tamil_Nadu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Air">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Salem_Airport_(India)" title="Salem Airport (India)">Salem Airport</a> (<a href="/wiki/International_Air_Transport_Association" title="International Air Transport Association">IATA</a> <i>SXV</i>, <a href="/wiki/International_Civil_Aviation_Organization" title="International Civil Aviation Organization">ICAO</a> <i>VOSM</i>) is located on the Salem-Bangalore Highway (NH 7) in Kaamalapuram about 15 kilometres (9.3 mi) from the city . <a href="/wiki/Airports_Authority_of_India" title="Airports Authority of India">Airports Authority of India</a> (AAI) opened the airport in 1993 for commercial operations.</p>
<pre>
<a href="/wiki/Kingfisher_Airlines" title="Kingfisher Airlines">Kingfisher Airlines</a> flew from <a href="/wiki/Chennai_International_Airport" title="Chennai International Airport">Chennai</a> but ended its service in 2012 due to low occupancy.
The nearest major airports are <a href="/wiki/Tiruchirappalli_International_Airport" title="Tiruchirappalli International Airport">Tiruchirappalli</a> (152 km) and <a href="/wiki/Coimbatore_International_Airport" title="Coimbatore International Airport">Coimbatore</a> (159 km).
</pre>
