<p><b>Shallum of Israel</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="he" xml:lang="he">שלום בן יבש</span>‎‎), name meaning "retribution", was the king of the ancient <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Israel_(Samaria)" title="Kingdom of Israel (Samaria)">Kingdom of Israel</a>, and the son of <a href="/wiki/Jabesh" title="Jabesh">Jabesh</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_Bible">In the Bible</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shallum_of_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In the Bible">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Originally a <a href="/wiki/Captain_(armed_forces)" title="Captain (armed forces)">captain</a> in the army of King <a href="/wiki/Zechariah_of_Israel" title="Zechariah of Israel">Zechariah</a>, Shallum "conspired against Zechariah, and smote him before the people; and slew him, and reigned in his stead" (<a class="external text" href="http://www.mechon-mamre.org/p/pt/pt09b15.htm#10" rel="nofollow">2 Kings 15:10</a>). He reigned only "a month of days in <a href="/wiki/Samaria" title="Samaria">Samaria</a>" (<a class="external text" href="http://www.mechon-mamre.org/p/pt/pt09b15.htm#13" rel="nofollow">2 Kings 15:13</a>) before <a href="/wiki/Menahem" title="Menahem">Menahem</a>—another captain from Zechariah's army—rose up and put Shallum to death (<a class="external text" href="http://www.mechon-mamre.org/p/pt/pt09b15.htm#14" rel="nofollow">2 Kings 15:14–17</a>). Menahem then became king in Shallum's stead.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shallum_of_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/William_F._Albright" title="William F. Albright">William F. Albright</a> has dated his reign to 745 BC, while Hooker says 747.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=E.R._Thiele&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="E.R. Thiele (page does not exist)">E.R. Thiele</a> offers the date 752 BC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
