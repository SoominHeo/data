<p><b>Salvador Cabañas Ortega</b> (born 5 August 1980) is a retired <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguayan</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Striker_(association_football)" title="Striker (association football)">striker</a>.</p>
<p>Cabañas played for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_12_de_Octubre" title="Club 12 de Octubre">Club 12 de Octubre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Club_Guaran%C3%AD" title="Club Guaraní">Guaraní</a>, <a href="/wiki/Audax_Italiano" title="Audax Italiano">Audax Italiano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chiapas_F.C." title="Chiapas F.C.">Chiapas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Club_Am%C3%A9rica" title="Club América">Club América</a>, <a href="/wiki/General_Caballero_Sport_Club" title="General Caballero Sport Club">General Caballero</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tanabi_Esporte_Clube" title="Tanabi Esporte Clube">Tanabi Esporte Clube</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Independiente_F%C3%BAtbol_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Independiente Fútbol Club (page does not exist)">Independiente PJC</a>. His football career saw him play in <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>.</p>
<p>Cabañas was also a member of the <a href="/wiki/Paraguay_national_football_team" title="Paraguay national football team">Paraguay national football team</a> from 2004 to 2010, scoring 10 goals in 44 appearances and participating at the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2007_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2007 Copa América">2007 Copa América</a>. Cabañas was leading goal scorer of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Copa_Libertadores" title="2007 Copa Libertadores">2007</a> and <a href="/wiki/2008_Copa_Libertadores" title="2008 Copa Libertadores">2008</a> <a href="/wiki/Copa_Libertadores" title="Copa Libertadores">Copa Libertadores</a> competitions. In 2007, he was awarded as both <a href="/wiki/Paraguayan_Footballer_of_the_Year" title="Paraguayan Footballer of the Year">Paraguayan Footballer of the Year</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_American_Footballer_of_the_Year" title="South American Footballer of the Year">South American Footballer of the Year</a>. Cabañas had also been top-scorer in <a href="/wiki/Chilean_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Chilean Primera División">Chile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liga_MX" title="Liga MX">Mexico</a>. He was known for his array of skills on the field such as excellent heading, accurate shooting with either foot, receptions in tight spaces, and a combination of technique, power and positional sense.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Cabañas was shot in the head in January 2010, but survived the attack. Sixteen months after the assault he returned to football and participated in a tribute match for him between Paraguay's national team and Club América.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salvador_Caba%C3%B1as&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salvador_Caba%C3%B1as&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cabañas started his career at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_12_de_Octubre" title="Club 12 de Octubre">Club 12 de Octubre</a>, where he was promoted to the first division by then coach <a href="/wiki/Alicio_Solalinde" title="Alicio Solalinde">Alicio Solalinde</a>, scoring a 17 goals in his first season. He moved to a different team, <a href="/wiki/Club_Guaran%C3%AD" title="Club Guaraní">Guaraní</a>, where he had a brief stint before moving to <a href="/wiki/Audax_Italiano" title="Audax Italiano">Audax Italiano</a> of <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>. In the 2003 Apertura tournament of the <a href="/wiki/Chilean_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Chilean Primera División">Chilean Primera División</a> Cabañas netted 18 goals and was the top goalscorer.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Years_in_Mexico">Years in Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salvador_Caba%C3%B1as&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Years in Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Afterwards, he joined <a href="/wiki/Chiapas_F.C." title="Chiapas F.C.">Chiapas</a> of Mexico from Audax Italiano before the Mexican 2003 Apertura tournament. That season, Cabañas went on to play in 18 games for the team, scoring five goals. He came into his own in the 2004 Clausura however, as he scored 15 goals in 20 games for the club. He would lead the club in goals again in the 2004 Apertura, with eight in 16 matches.</p>
<p>Following the Clausura 2006 season in which Cabañas won his first goalscoring crown in the Mexican League, he attracted the attention of <a href="/wiki/Club_Am%C3%A9rica" title="Club América">Club América</a>, one of the most important clubs in the country, and was signed by the <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a> squad prior to the Apertura 2006 season.</p>
<p>Cabañas became Club América's most prolific goalscorer for 2007, finishing up the first half of the year with an impressive 19 goals between <a href="/wiki/2007_Copa_Libertadores" title="2007 Copa Libertadores">2007 Copa Libertadores</a> (in which he became the top-scorer with 10 goals) and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Mexican Primera División">Mexican Primera División</a>. He started the second half of the year with a single goal in the <a href="/wiki/North_American_SuperLiga" title="North American SuperLiga">North American SuperLiga</a>, then scored 4 more goals in the <a href="/wiki/2007_Copa_Sudamericana" title="2007 Copa Sudamericana">2007 Copa Sudamericana</a>, and has scored 9 more in the Mexican Primera División, bringing his year's total with the club to 33 goals. His top form in 2007 earned him the <a href="/wiki/Paraguayan_Footballer_of_the_Year" title="Paraguayan Footballer of the Year">Paraguayan Footballer of the Year</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_American_Footballer_of_the_Year" title="South American Footballer of the Year">South American Footballer of the Year</a> awards.</p>
<p>For 2008, Cabañas has started where he left off in 2007. He has scored 3 goals in the <a href="/wiki/2008_InterLiga" title="2008 InterLiga">2008 InterLiga</a>, scored 8 in the <a href="/wiki/2008_Copa_Libertadores" title="2008 Copa Libertadores">2008 Copa Libertadores</a> (in which, for a consecutive second time, he became the top-scorer), and has scored 6 more goals in the Mexican Primera División. He started the second half of the year with 6 goals in the Mexican Primera División, bringing his year's total to 23 goals with the club.</p>
<p>Cabañas started 2009 by scoring his first goal in the <a href="/wiki/2009_InterLiga" title="2009 InterLiga">2009 InterLiga</a> and scoring 13 more goals in the Mexican Primera División. He then finished the second half of the year with 12 goals in the Mexican Primera División. Taking his tally to 26 goals for the year. On January 18, against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Santos_Laguna" title="Club Santos Laguna">Santos Laguna</a>, he scored two goals, taking his personal tally to 100 goals in the Mexican Primera División.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010, Cabañas again started the year scoring goals in the <a href="/wiki/2010_InterLiga" title="2010 InterLiga">2010 InterLiga</a>, scoring four. In the two games he played in the 2010 Bicentenario, he scored two goals. His last game before being assaulted was on 24 January 2010 in a 2–0 loss against Morelia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Assault">Assault</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salvador_Caba%C3%B1as&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Assault">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cabañas was shot in the head inside the "Bar Bar" night club, in Mexico City, on January 25, 2010 at 5:00 a.m.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> According to the official police reports the assault was the result of a taunting incident in the bar's bathroom<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> between Cabañas and a mobster of the <a href="/wiki/Beltr%C3%A1n-Leyva_Cartel" title="Beltrán-Leyva Cartel">Beltrán-Leyva Drug-Cartel</a> called <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_Balderas_Garza&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="José Balderas Garza (page does not exist)">José Balderas Garza</a>, alias "JJ", who shot him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to Cabañas ex-wife, the altercation was a result of Cabañas reacting against a robbery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Cabañas received medical attention during the early hours of 25 January 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Later on the day he was submitted to a <a href="/wiki/Craniotomy" title="Craniotomy">craniotomy</a> but doctors decided that it was too risky to proceed and the bullet was not extracted. Cabañas left <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intensive_Care_Unit" title="Intensive Care Unit">intensive care</a> in late February and was hoping to fully recover in time for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> but was not selected for the final Paraguay squad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Doctors dealing with Cabañas have described his recovery so far as "tremendous".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> However, it is announced he does have short-term memory loss and may not recover for another one to three years. He gave his first interview on 12 March 2010 in appreciation to all the people who prayed for him. In 2013, Cabañas gave his version of the incident which stated that in his opinion the attack occurred so that he would not play at the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> and that <a href="/wiki/Paraguay_national_football_team" title="Paraguay national football team">Paraguay</a> would not be crowned champions of the competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mediotiempo1_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-mediotiempo1-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>At that moment, Cabañas challenged the man, asking him 'what are you waiting for', noting to him that he had held the gun towards Cabañas for almost 10 minutes, and after a little bit, the man pulled the trigger on Cabañas shot his forehead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-14">[14]</a></sup> He also stated that right after being shot, he went directly to heaven and spoke with <a href="/wiki/God" title="God">God</a>, who touched him on his forehead, where Cabañas had been shot, and told him that a long time remained until he was due to come to heaven and that he enjoy life and help those in need.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mediotiempo1_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-mediotiempo1-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_football">Return to football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salvador_Caba%C3%B1as&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Return to football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cabañas signed a contract with Paraguayan third-division club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_12_de_Octubre" title="Club 12 de Octubre">12 de Octubre</a>, on January 20, 2012, two years after the assault.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> He then made his competitive return to football on 14 April, starting and playing forty minutes, in the club's 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Club_Mart%C3%ADn_Ledesma" title="Club Martín Ledesma">Martín Ledesma</a> in the opening round of the <a href="/wiki/Paraguayan_Tercera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Paraguayan Tercera División">Primera B</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Salvador's side gained promotion into the <a href="/wiki/Paraguayan_Divisi%C3%B3n_Intermedia" title="Paraguayan División Intermedia">Second Division of Paraguay</a> where he will now compete on a regular basis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Cabañas was invited to partake in a half time memorial appearance for <a href="/wiki/Chiapas_F.C." title="Chiapas F.C.">Chiapas</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Liga_MX" title="Liga MX">Liga MX</a> match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pumas_UNAM" title="Pumas UNAM">Pumas</a>. He received a standing ovation from the crowd at half time. Jaguares later went on to win the match 3–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_retirement_and_return_to_football">First retirement and return to football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salvador_Caba%C3%B1as&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: First retirement and return to football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 26 January 2014, Cabañas announced his retirement from football, after having received an offer from Mexican second division side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Murci%C3%A9lagos_FC" title="Murciélagos FC">Murciélagos FC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> However, on 1 February 2014, it was announced that Cabañas had signed with Club 12 de Octubre, who had been promoted to the Paraguayan Primera División for the 2014 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> On 16 February 2014, the day that Cabañas was meant to debut against Cerro Porteño in the Round 1 of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Paraguayan_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n_season" title="2014 Paraguayan Primera División season">2014 Paraguayan Primera División season</a>, it was announced by club spokes man, Miguel Soloaga, that Cabañas would not participate in the competition due to his low form. Sologa stated that Cabañas was out of physical form and discarded from the Torneo Apertura but would continue to participate in the club's training sessions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tanabi_Esporte_Clube">Tanabi Esporte Clube</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salvador_Caba%C3%B1as&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Tanabi Esporte Clube">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 17 March 2014, it was announced that Cabañas had signed a three-month contract with <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_(state)" title="São Paulo (state)">São Paulo</a> <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Paulista_Segunda_Divis%C3%A3o" title="Campeonato Paulista Segunda Divisão">Série B</a> (equivalent to the state fourth division) outfit <a href="/wiki/Tanabi_Esporte_Clube" title="Tanabi Esporte Clube">Tanabi Esporte Clube</a>. The club's president, Irineu Alves, travelled by car from Brazil to Paraguay in order to finalize the contract, stating that Cabañas was a big financial signing for the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> On 18 May 2014, Cabañas had debuted for the club in a friendly against Gremio Barueri, which ended in a 2-2 draw, where Cabañas missed a penalty in the last minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_retirement">Second retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salvador_Caba%C3%B1as&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Second retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 30 May 2014, Cabañas officially announced that he had retired due to not being able to maintain rhythm to play since being shot in the head in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Independiente_F.C3.BAtbol_Club_PJC">Independiente Fútbol Club PJC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salvador_Caba%C3%B1as&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Independiente Fútbol Club PJC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 27 June 2014, Cabañas came out of retirement and signed with Independiente Fútbol Club of Pedro Juan Caballero.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> On 29 June 2014, Cabañas debuted for Independiente against former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n_Paraguaya" title="Primera División Paraguaya">Primera División Paraguaya</a> club <a href="/wiki/Club_2_de_Mayo" title="Club 2 de Mayo">Sportivo 2 de Mayo</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Liga_Deportiva_del_Amambay" title="Liga Deportiva del Amambay">Liga Deportiva Amamby</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Paraguayan_Cuarta_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Paraguayan Cuarta División">Paraguayan Cuarta División</a>, entering the field in the 35th minute of the second half.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salvador_Caba%C3%B1as&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1997, Cabañas represented Paraguay's U17 team at the <a href="/wiki/1997_South_American_Under-17_Football_Championship" title="1997 South American Under-17 Football Championship">1997 South American Under-17 Football Championship</a> held in Paraguay, scoring 3 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Two years later, Cabañas was selected in Paraguay's U20 squad to participate at the <a href="/wiki/1999_South_American_U-20_Championship" title="1999 South American U-20 Championship">1999 South American U-20 Championship</a>, where he scored two goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Paraguay qualified for the <a href="/wiki/1999_FIFA_World_Youth_Championship" title="1999 FIFA World Youth Championship">1999 FIFA World Youth Championship</a> as Cabañas was selected in the squad which featured <a href="/wiki/Roque_Santa_Cruz" title="Roque Santa Cruz">Roque Santa Cruz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Cuevas" title="Nelson Cuevas">Nelson Cuevas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>Cabañas debuted for the <a href="/wiki/Paraguay_national_football_team" title="Paraguay national football team">Paraguay national team</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(CONMEBOL)" title="2006 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONMEBOL)">2006 World Cup qualification campaign</a>. He was selected in the squad for the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">tournament finals</a> but did not play.</p>
<p>He scored three goals in four matches in the <a href="/wiki/2007_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2007 Copa América">2007 Copa América</a> as Paraguay reached the quarter-finals. He was the team's top scorer in the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(CONMEBOL)" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONMEBOL)">2010 World Cup qualifiers</a> with six goals, including one in a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a> in <a href="/wiki/Asunci%C3%B3n" title="Asunción">Asunción</a>.</p>
