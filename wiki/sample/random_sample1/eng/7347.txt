<p><i><b>Puncture Wounds</b></i> (produced and released internationally as <i><b>A Certain Justice</b></i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ACJone_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ACJone-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ACJtwo_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ACJtwo-2">[2]</a></sup> is a 2014 American <a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action</a> film directed by Giorgio Serafini and James Coyne, and starring <a href="/wiki/Cung_Le" title="Cung Le">Cung Le</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dolph_Lundgren" title="Dolph Lundgren">Dolph Lundgren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vinnie_Jones" title="Vinnie Jones">Vinnie Jones</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Briana_Evigan" title="Briana Evigan">Briana Evigan</a>. The film follows John Nguyen, a war veteran suffering from <a href="/wiki/Posttraumatic_stress_disorder" title="Posttraumatic stress disorder">posttraumatic stress disorder</a>. After he rescues a prostitute, Hollis, a local crime lord, marks them for death. It was released <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Direct_to_video" title="Direct to video">direct to video</a> in the United States on March 11, 2014, followed by a limited release in other countries.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span></h2>
<p>John Nguyen is a veteran suffering from posttraumatic stress disorder. Despite his status as a war hero, he's had difficulty adjusting to civilian life. When he hears a woman scream, he investigates and gets into a fight with several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aryan_Nation" title="Aryan Nation">Aryan Nation</a> thugs who are hassling Tanya, a prostitute in the service of local crime boss Hollis. Tanya is horrified when John kills all but one of the thugs; although thankful for his help, she says that John has doomed them both to die at Hollis' hands. Planning to flee the city, Tanya buys a bus ticket, but Hollis convinces her to stay by promising her that she can come off the streets and live with him again. Hollis directs his lieutenant Vin to murder John's family. Although reluctant, Vin complies. Enraged, John tracks down Bennett, a drug dealer, and strikes back at Hollis' meth business, brutally killing several of the men responsible for his family's death.</p>
<p>As Hollis and John war with each other, two cops try to solve the case. Sgt. Mitchell believes John to be honorable, while his partner, a dirty cop in the employ of Hollis, attempts to derail the investigation. John recruits his disabled war buddy J. P. to help him in the final confrontation with Hollis. As Hollis is in the midst of violently raping Tanya, Vin intercedes. Before Vin and Hollis can come to blows, John assaults Hollis' house. John kills most of Hollis' henchmen and leaves Vin to die after a knife fight. Although John captures Hollis, reinforcements arrive and turn the tide against him. Both John and J. P. are captured, and Hollis murders J. P. John challenges Hollis to a one-on-one fight, and, when John appears to be winning, Tanya shoots and kills Hollis' remaining henchmen before they can interfere. After John kills Hollis, Mitchell arrives and tells him to leave the scene of the crime, as he will clean it up.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span></h2>
<p><i>Puncture Wounds</i> was released <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Direct_to_video" title="Direct to video">direct to video</a> in the United States on March 11, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-release_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-release-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span></h3>
<p>Eoin Friel, of The Action Elite, praised <i>Puncture Wounds</i>, saying despite the film not having a "huge budget" or "spectacular action", it does have "plenty of hand-to-hand" combat and "well-developed characters". According to Friel, none of characters are "one dimensional or over the top", and Dolph Lundgren is "very restrained" and "genuinely scary" in his role. He went on to say that the script didn't have any "clunky dialogue", and to him, the "whole film" had a "real and gritty approach".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EoinFrielReview_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-EoinFrielReview-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nav Qateel, of Influx Magazine, rated it B and said "there is no shortage of action". In his opinion, the "cinematography was good", and the editing was "mostly decent", but he did notice a "few shaky cuts". Apart from there being "too much" plot, the only issue he had was with the fight choreography, which he felt "could have been made to look better". Despite the issues, Qateel summed up his review by saying: "for those of you who enjoy a decent bit of B-movie action, then this will keep you entertained".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-QateelInfluxMag_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-QateelInfluxMag-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ian Jane, of <a href="/wiki/DVD_Talk" title="DVD Talk">DVD Talk</a>, awarded the film 3 out of 5 stars. He called it "fast paced and enjoyable if not particularly original", writing that "elements" were "borrowed" from <i><a href="/wiki/First_Blood" title="First Blood">First Blood</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Thunder_(film)" title="Rolling Thunder (film)">Rolling Thunder</a></i>. Jane criticized the film's cinematography, saying that it relies "too heavily on shaky cam tactics". On the other hand, he complimented the film for being "fast paced, reasonably well acted, [very] violent and generally [a] solid [piece of] entertainment" for the "less demanding action movie aficionado". He summed up his review by calling <i>Puncture Wounds</i> a "satisfying low budget action movie".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Matthew Tilt, of <a href="/wiki/Stafford_FM" title="Stafford FM">Stafford FM</a>, gave the film a negative review, claiming that in "many ways", James Coyne made the "same mistake in his script as <a href="/wiki/Nick_Love" title="Nick Love">Nick Love</a> did with the British thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Outlaw_(2007_film)" title="Outlaw (2007 film)">Outlaw</a></i>. He takes a very real issue; the treatment of ex-soldiers, which is alluded to in a brief scene early on, and then goes on to portray them as mindless psychopaths". Describing the directing as "poor", Tilt said the only positive aspect of the film "would be some well-choreographed actions scenes", but even then it's "too much".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MatthewTiltreview_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-MatthewTiltreview-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Geek Syndicate heavily criticized the film, awarding 1 out of 5 stars. According to the website, the film is "clearly" one of those straight-to-video productions that Lundgren "hams up" for a pay check in between making sequels to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Expendables_(film_series)" title="The Expendables (film series)">The Expendables</a></i>. They went on to say the film is "pretty brutal" and "cruel" in places, and that it adds nothing to the story except "shock value". The review was summed up by advising any readers to "save" themselves the effort and "give the film a miss".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GeekSynd_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-GeekSynd-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
