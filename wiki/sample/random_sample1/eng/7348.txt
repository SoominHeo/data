<p><i><b>Thylamys</b></i> is a genus of <a href="/wiki/Opossum" title="Opossum">opossum</a> in the family <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Didelphidae" title="Didelphidae">Didelphidae</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Premaxilla" title="Premaxilla">premaxillae</a> are rounded rather than pointed. The females lack a <a href="/wiki/Pouch_(marsupial)" title="Pouch (marsupial)">pouch</a>. The females' <a href="/wiki/Nipple" title="Nipple">nipples</a> are arranged in two symmetrical rows on the abdomen<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eisenberg_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Eisenberg-2">[2]</a></sup> All species but <i>T. macrurus</i> store fat in their tails.,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MSA_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-MSA-3">[3]</a></sup> although this is not necessarily true for all species in the genus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Voss2003_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Voss2003-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The genus contains the following species:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cinderella_fat-tailed_mouse_opossum" title="Cinderella fat-tailed mouse opossum">Cinderella fat-tailed mouse opossum</a> (<i>Thylamys cinderella</i>)</li>
<li>(<i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Thylamys_citellus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Thylamys citellus (page does not exist)">Thylamys citellus</a></i>) <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Elegant_fat-tailed_mouse_opossum" title="Elegant fat-tailed mouse opossum">Elegant fat-tailed mouse opossum</a> (<i>Thylamys elegans</i>)</li>
<li>(<i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Thylamys_fenestrae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Thylamys fenestrae (page does not exist)">Thylamys fenestrae</a></i>) <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Karimi%27s_fat-tailed_mouse_opossum" title="Karimi's fat-tailed mouse opossum">Karimi's fat-tailed mouse opossum</a> (<i>Thylamys karimii</i>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Paraguayan_fat-tailed_mouse_opossum" title="Paraguayan fat-tailed mouse opossum">Paraguayan fat-tailed mouse opossum</a> (<i>Thylamys macrurus</i>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/White-bellied_fat-tailed_mouse_opossum" title="White-bellied fat-tailed mouse opossum">White-bellied fat-tailed mouse opossum</a> (<i>Thylamys pallidior</i>)</li>
<li>(<i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Thylamys_pulchellus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Thylamys pulchellus (page does not exist)">Thylamys pulchellus</a></i>) <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Common_fat-tailed_mouse_opossum" title="Common fat-tailed mouse opossum">Common fat-tailed mouse opossum</a> (<i>Thylamys pusillus</i>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Argentine_fat-tailed_mouse_opossum" title="Argentine fat-tailed mouse opossum">Argentine fat-tailed mouse opossum</a> (<i>Thylamys sponsorius</i>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tate%27s_fat-tailed_mouse_opossum" title="Tate's fat-tailed mouse opossum">Tate's fat-tailed mouse opossum</a> (<i>Thylamys tatei</i>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dwarf_fat-tailed_mouse_opossum" title="Dwarf fat-tailed mouse opossum">Dwarf fat-tailed mouse opossum</a> (<i>Thylamys velutinus</i>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Buff-bellied_fat-tailed_mouse_opossum" title="Buff-bellied fat-tailed mouse opossum">Buff-bellied fat-tailed mouse opossum</a> (<i>Thylamys venustus</i>)</li>
</ul>
<p>Fossils belonging to the genus date back to the <a href="/wiki/Miocene" title="Miocene">Miocene</a>, with the oldest specimens being found in <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Braun2005_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Braun2005-8">[8]</a></sup> Genetic studies indicate that the genus may have originated around 14 million years ago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Steiner2005_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Steiner2005-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
