<p><i><b>Three Kingdoms</b></i> is a Chinese television series based on the events in the <a href="/wiki/End_of_the_Han_dynasty" title="End of the Han dynasty">late Eastern Han dynasty</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Three_Kingdoms" title="Three Kingdoms">Three Kingdoms</a> period. The plot is adapted from the 14th century historical novel <i><a href="/wiki/Romance_of_the_Three_Kingdoms" title="Romance of the Three Kingdoms">Romance of the Three Kingdoms</a></i> and other stories about the Three Kingdoms period. Directed by <a href="/wiki/Gao_Xixi" title="Gao Xixi">Gao Xixi</a>, the series had a budget of over 180 million RMB (30 million USD)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and was released in China in May 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The series was well received both domestically and internationally, earning an estimated 800 million RMB (133.3 million USD) in total as of May 2012.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jiang_Wen" title="Jiang Wen">Jiang Wen</a> was originally cast as <a href="/wiki/Cao_Cao" title="Cao Cao">Cao Cao</a>, but decided to abandon the role after two directors quit the project. Soon afterwards, <a href="/wiki/Tang_Guoqiang" title="Tang Guoqiang">Tang Guoqiang</a>, who played <a href="/wiki/Zhuge_Liang" title="Zhuge Liang">Zhuge Liang</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Romance_of_the_Three_Kingdoms_(TV_series)" title="Romance of the Three Kingdoms (TV series)">Romance of the Three Kingdoms</a></i> (1994), asked for the role, but it had already been reassigned to <a href="/wiki/Chen_Jianbin" title="Chen Jianbin">Chen Jianbin</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ken_Watanabe" title="Ken Watanabe">Ken Watanabe</a> asked director <a href="/wiki/Gao_Xixi" title="Gao Xixi">Gao Xixi</a> for the role of <a href="/wiki/Guan_Yu" title="Guan Yu">Guan Yu</a>. Gao reportedly was unable to meet Watanabe's request for a salary of 30-40 million yuan, and was forced to turn him away. The role eventually went to <a href="/wiki/Yu_Rongguang" title="Yu Rongguang">Yu Rongguang</a> (who previously appeared as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Han_De" title="Han De">Han De</a> in the 2008 film <i><a href="/wiki/Three_Kingdoms:_Resurrection_of_the_Dragon" title="Three Kingdoms: Resurrection of the Dragon">Three Kingdoms: Resurrection of the Dragon</a></i>).</li>
</ul>
