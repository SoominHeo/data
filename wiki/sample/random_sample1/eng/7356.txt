<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Triplicity</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>In <a href="/wiki/Astrology" title="Astrology">astrology</a>, a <b>triplicity</b> is a group of three signs belonging to the same <a href="/wiki/Classical_element" title="Classical element">element</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trines">Trines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Triplicity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Trines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Western astrology assumes that each sign of the same triplicity is 120 degrees apart, forming <a href="/wiki/Angle_(astrology)" title="Angle (astrology)">angles</a> to one another called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trine_(astrological_aspect)" title="Trine (astrological aspect)">trines</a>, which are each equivalent to the 360 degrees of the circle divided by three. Trines are considered to be very powerful and yet very comfortable, free-flowing connections. This suggests that the signs in each element are very comfortable and compatible with one another and tend to have many of the same qualities in common.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Method_of_organization">Method of organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Triplicity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Method of organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In traditional astrology, the concept of triplicity embodied several factors concerning the four classical elements and were considered of particular importance. Besides the four classical elements, two other ways triplicity could be organized were by <i>rulership</i> type and by <i>season</i>. However, neither are given much attention by modern astrologers. However, astrology by season, in particular has been adopted by astrologers who practice in modern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neopaganism" title="Neopaganism">Neopaganism</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Druidism" title="Druidism">Druidism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wicca" title="Wicca">Wicca</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Classical_elements">Classical elements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Triplicity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Classical elements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="floatright"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Four_elements_representation.svg" title="Four Classical Elements; this classic diagram has two squares on top of each other, with the corners of one being the classical elements, and the corners of the other being the properties"><img alt="Four Classical Elements; this classic diagram has two squares on top of each other, with the corners of one being the classical elements, and the corners of the other being the properties" data-file-height="278" data-file-width="333" height="278" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Four_elements_representation.svg/333px-Four_elements_representation.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Four_elements_representation.svg/500px-Four_elements_representation.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Four_elements_representation.svg/666px-Four_elements_representation.svg.png 2x" width="333"/></a></div>
<p>In traditional <a href="/wiki/Western_astrology" title="Western astrology">Western astrology</a> there are four triplicities based on the <a href="/wiki/Astrology_and_the_classical_elements" title="Astrology and the classical elements">classical elements</a>. Beginning with the first sign Aries which is a Fire sign, the next in line Taurus is Earth, then to Gemini which is Air, and finally to Cancer which is Water -- in Western astrology the sequence is always Fire, Earth, Air, &amp; Water in that exact order. This <a href="/wiki/Rotation" title="Rotation">cycle</a> continues on twice more and ends with the twelfth and final <a href="/wiki/Astrological_sign" title="Astrological sign">astrological sign</a>, Pisces. The elemental rulerships for the twelve astrological signs of the <a href="/wiki/Zodiac" title="Zodiac">zodiac</a> (according to Marcus Manilius) are summarized as follows:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Fire</b> — <a href="/wiki/Aries_(astrology)" title="Aries (astrology)">Aries</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leo_(astrology)" title="Leo (astrology)">Leo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sagittarius_(astrology)" title="Sagittarius (astrology)">Sagittarius</a> - hot, dry</li>
<li><b>Earth</b> — <a href="/wiki/Taurus_(astrology)" title="Taurus (astrology)">Taurus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virgo_(astrology)" title="Virgo (astrology)">Virgo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Capricorn_(astrology)" title="Capricorn (astrology)">Capricorn</a> - cold, dry</li>
<li><b>Air</b> — <a href="/wiki/Gemini_(astrology)" title="Gemini (astrology)">Gemini</a>, <a href="/wiki/Libra_(astrology)" title="Libra (astrology)">Libra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aquarius_(astrology)" title="Aquarius (astrology)">Aquarius</a> - hot, wet</li>
<li><b>Water</b> — <a href="/wiki/Cancer_(astrology)" title="Cancer (astrology)">Cancer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scorpio_(astrology)" title="Scorpio (astrology)">Scorpio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pisces_(astrology)" title="Pisces (astrology)">Pisces</a> - cold, wet</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rulerships">Rulerships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Triplicity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Rulerships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In traditional astrology, each triplicity has several planetary <i>rulers,</i> which change with conditions of <a href="/wiki/Astrology_of_sect" title="Astrology of sect">sect</a>--that is, whether the chart is a day chart or a night chart.</p>
<p>Triplicity rulerships are a very important <a href="/wiki/Essential_dignity" title="Essential dignity">essential dignity</a>--one of the several factors used by traditional astrologers to weigh the strength, effectiveness and integrity of each planet in a chart. Many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hellenistic" title="Hellenistic">Hellenistic</a> astrologers (for example, <a href="/wiki/Dorotheus_of_Sidon" title="Dorotheus of Sidon">Dorotheus of Sidon</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>) considered triplicity rulership the most powerful and demonstrable of the several essential dignities of a planet.</p>
<p>Triplicty rulerships (using the "Dorothean system"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>) are as follows:</p>

<div style="font-size:90%">* <i>(<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Claudius_Ptolemy" title="Claudius Ptolemy">Ptolemy</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> later modified the rulerships of Water triplicity, making <b>Mars</b> the ruler of the water triplicity for both day and night charts--and <a href="/wiki/William_Lilly" title="William Lilly">William Lilly</a> concurred.)</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></div>
<p>One way in which triplicty rulerships were used by earlier astrologers was to divide a person's life into three periods: early, middle, and late. The condition of the triplicity ruler that is <i>in sect</i> in the chart is evaluated when considering the tenor of the early part of life; the ruler out of sect is examined for the middle of life; and the last third of life is evaluated by looking at the condition of the participating triplicity ruler.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> "Participating" rulers were not used after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hellenistic" title="Hellenistic">Hellenistic</a> period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medieval" title="Medieval">medieval</a> systems of astrology, each <a href="/wiki/Essential_dignity" title="Essential dignity">essential dignity</a> was given a different weight. <a href="/wiki/Domicile_(astrology)" title="Domicile (astrology)">Domicile rulers</a> were given 5 points of weight; <a href="/wiki/Exaltation_(astrology)" title="Exaltation (astrology)">exaltation</a> rulers were given 4 points; and triplicity rulers were assigned 3 points of weight. This gives some idea of how much power medieval astrologers accorded to each essential dignity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Actual_seasons">Actual seasons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Triplicity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Actual seasons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Traditional astrology also organizes triplicities according to the actual season in which the zodiac rose.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> For example, Aries, Taurus, and Gemini appear during the spring season.</p>
<p>The triplicities of seasonal elements in ancient astrology were the following:</p>
<ul>
<li>Spring - Aries - Taurus - Gemini</li>
<li>Summer - Cancer - Leo - Virgo</li>
<li>Autumn - Libra - Scorpio - Sagittarius</li>
<li>Winter - Capricorn - Aquarius - Pisces</li>
</ul>
