<p>The <b>Sambyeolcho Rebellion</b> (1270–1273) was a <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korean</a> rebellion against the <a href="/wiki/Goryeo" title="Goryeo">Goryeo</a> dynasty that happened at the last stage of the <a href="/wiki/Mongol_invasions_of_Korea" title="Mongol invasions of Korea">Mongol invasions of Korea</a>. It was suppressed by the Goryeo and the Mongol <a href="/wiki/Yuan_dynasty" title="Yuan dynasty">Yuan dynasty</a>. After the rebellion Goryeo became a <a href="/wiki/Korea_under_Yuan_rule" title="Korea under Yuan rule">vassal</a> of the Yuan dynasty, division of the <a href="/wiki/Mongol_Empire" title="Mongol Empire">Mongol Empire</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sambyeolcho_Rebellion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 1231, Goryeo was intermittently invaded by the <a href="/wiki/Mongol_Empire" title="Mongol Empire">Mongol Empire</a>. During this time, Goryeo was controlled by <a href="/wiki/Goryeo_military_regime" title="Goryeo military regime">a military regime</a> led by the Choe family. In 1232 the government under the nominal king fled to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ganghwa_Island" title="Ganghwa Island">Ganghwa Island</a>, which Mongol horse riders were unable to land on, and resisted the Mongol invasion. Unfortunately because of its fragile foundation, Goryeo faced frequent rebellions. The 1258 rebellion resulted in the establishment of Ssangseong (쌍성총관부, 雙城摠管府) and Dongnyeong Prefectures (동녕부, 東寧府) by the Mongols.</p>
<p>Unlike these rebels, the <a href="/wiki/Sambyeolcho" title="Sambyeolcho">Sambyeolcho</a> (Three Elite Patrols) were an organ of the military government. They were organized by the Choe family to maintain security. However, unlike the Choe private guards unit (which was to personally protect the family), the Sambyeolcho assumed public functions performed by police and combat forces, effectively replacing the Six Divisions of the military.</p>
<p>In 1258, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Choe_Ui&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Choe Ui (page does not exist)">Choe Ui</a>, the fourth of the Choe family, was overthrown by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Jun&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kim Jun (page does not exist)">Kim Jun</a> (also known as Kim Injun) using the Sambyeolcho. Kim Jun took a pro-Mongol policy and sent Crown Prince <a href="/wiki/Wonjong_of_Goryeo" title="Wonjong of Goryeo">Wang Jeon</a> to the Mongol Empire. At the same time, King Gojong and the crown prince approached the Mongols to restore power from Kim Jun.</p>
<p>In 1268, however, Kim Jun was annihilated by the Sambyeolcho under the order of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Im_Yeon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Im Yeon (page does not exist)">Im Yeon</a>. The next year, Im Yeon's attempt to replace King Wonjong was reversed by the crown prince (<a href="/wiki/Chungnyeol_of_Goryeo" title="Chungnyeol of Goryeo">Chungnyeol</a>) with the help from the Mongol force. In 1270, Im Yeon's successor <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Im_Yumu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Im Yumu (page does not exist)">Im Yumu</a> was killed by the pro-Mongol faction using the Sambyeolcho. It marked the end of the military regime.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Anti-Mongol_Struggle">Anti-Mongol Struggle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sambyeolcho_Rebellion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Anti-Mongol Struggle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>By the order of the Mongol Court, Wonjong moved the capital from Ganghwa Island to Kaesŏng. Regaining power from military officials with the support of the Mongols, the king decided to abolish the <a href="/wiki/Sambyeolcho" title="Sambyeolcho">Sambyeolcho</a> (삼별초, 三別抄). Resentful of the peace terms worked out with the Mongols, the Sambyeolcho, led by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bae_Jungson&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bae Jungson (page does not exist)">Bae Jungson</a> (배중손, 裴仲孫), revolted against the government. Systematically blocking passage between Gangwha and the mainland, they brought nearby islands and coastal regions under their domain. Wang On, a royal kinsman was proclaimed king of the maritime kingdom. They gave up Ganghwa Island and fled to <a href="/wiki/Jindo_County" title="Jindo County">Jindo Island</a> in the southwest.</p>
<p>Although the Sambyeolcho raided the coastlines of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeolla" title="Jeolla">Jeolla</a> Province, the southwestern province, Jin Island started to face food shortages in January 1271. In February the court of <a href="/wiki/Kublai_Khan" title="Kublai Khan">Kublai Khan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Yuan_dynasty" title="Yuan dynasty">Yuan dynasty</a> called for the Sambyeolcho's surrender. In response, its leader, Bae Jungson, asked Kublai Khan to secure Jeolla Province and put it under the direct rule of the empire, just as preceding rebels had. But his request was never fulfilled.</p>
<p>In April, the Yuan court decided to crush the rebels. It only took a month until Jin Island fell to a combined Goryeo and Mongol army. The puppet king was killed and the survivors, led by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kim_Tongjeong" title="Kim Tongjeong">Kim Tongjeong</a> (김통정, 金通精), fled to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeju_Island" title="Jeju Island">Jeju Island</a>. The rebels captured the island and banished the king of <a href="/wiki/Tamna" title="Tamna">Tamna</a> in November 1270.</p>
<p>The Sambyeolcho laid low until the end of 1271. During that time, they sought help from <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kamakura_Shogunate" title="Kamakura Shogunate">Kamakura Shogunate</a>. They regained their strength to some degree the following year. They repeatedly looted the Korean coast. A combined Goryeo-Mongol assault began in February 1272, and crushed the rebels in April. Thereafter, the Mongols directly controlled Tamna until 1294.</p>
