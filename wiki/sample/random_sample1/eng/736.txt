<p><b>Associazioni Calcio Riunite Messina 1947</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Football_in_Italy" title="Football in Italy">Italian football</a> club based in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Messina,_Italy" title="Messina, Italy">Messina</a>, Sicily. It currently plays in <a href="/wiki/Lega_Pro" title="Lega Pro">Lega Pro</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A.C.R._Messina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_football_in_Messina">The football in Messina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A.C.R._Messina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The football in Messina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_A.C.R._Messina" title="History of A.C.R. Messina">History of A.C.R. Messina</a></div>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Brief_history">Brief history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A.C.R._Messina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Brief history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The origins of the team go back to 1900 when <b>Messina F.C</b> was founded in the city. The club has spent most of its history in the lower <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_football" title="Italian football">Italian football</a> leagues. They last competed in <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">43 Specimen</a> in 2007–08, this following three consecutive seasons in <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a>. On July 2008 Messina were excluded from professional football due to financial issues, being later registered into amateur <a href="/wiki/Serie_D" title="Serie D">Serie D</a>.</p>
<p>The farthest Messina has reached in the <a href="/wiki/Coppa_Italia" title="Coppa Italia">Coppa Italia</a> is the last 16, this was achieved in the 2000s decade. In the past, they have also reached the semi-finals in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coppa_Italia_Serie_C" title="Coppa Italia Serie C">Coppa Italia Serie C</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Messina have appeared in the Italy's top league Serie A, for a total of five seasons during their history. The club's first spell in the league was in the 1960s, the second began in the 2000s decade. The highest ever position they have finished is 7th,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> which happened during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_A_2004-05" title="Serie A 2004-05">2004–05</a> season.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="From_Messina_F.C._to_Giostra_Messina">From Messina F.C. to Giostra Messina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A.C.R._Messina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: From Messina F.C. to Giostra Messina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="From_1900_to_1939">From 1900 to 1939</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A.C.R._Messina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: From 1900 to 1939">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:122px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:FCMessinaBadge.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="180" data-file-width="133" height="162" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/ce/FCMessinaBadge.png/120px-FCMessinaBadge.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/c/ce/FCMessinaBadge.png 1.5x" width="120"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

This logo was used until the early 2000s.</div>

<p>The history of Messina Football Club began when Alfredo Marangolo returned to Sicily in August 1900 from studying in London, England. In Great Britain the game of <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> was fast gathering popularity with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Football_League" title="The Football League">The Football League</a> in its early stages.</p>
<p>Messina Football Club were officially founded on 1 December 1900<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> by Marangolo with the help of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglican" title="Anglican">Anglican</a> reverend "<a href="/wiki/Charles_Bousfield_Huleatt" title="Charles Bousfield Huleatt">Caulifield</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the college where Marangolo visited he had also made the acquaintance of Ignazio Majo Pagano who formed <a href="/wiki/U.S._Citt%C3%A0_di_Palermo" title="U.S. Città di Palermo">Anglo Palermitan</a> (Palermo) on his return, only a month before Messina. Indeed, the first Sicilian derby was held between Messina and Palermo on 18 April 1901, 1000 fans turned out to Via Notarbartolo in support their respective club for the match. The game ended 3–2 to the Palermitan side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>A strong bond, and healthy rivalry had built up between the two Sicilian clubs and a competition named the <i>Whitaker Challenge Cup</i> was arranged to be played between them. The first was held in 1905; Messina won another game (the result was 3–2) and captured their first trophy. Messina repeated the feat the following year at San Ranieri; capturing the trophy in a 2–1 victory.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Messina1910.jpeg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="230" data-file-width="297" height="155" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Messina1910.jpeg/200px-Messina1910.jpeg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f8/Messina1910.jpeg 1.5x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Messina1910.jpeg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Messina team photograph from 1910.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The earthquake of 1908, which killed 60,000 people in Messina, later affected the club in a large manner; deaths included Charles Bousfield Huleatt, players Frank John Carter, Walter Oates and financial backer George H. Peirce. Football resumed in Messina during the following year, thanks largely to Arthur Barret Lascelles who used money from his own pocket to ensure football activity in the city would continue. By 1910 the funds of Barret had dried up and the club was folded, <b>Società Ginnastica Garibaldi Messina</b> (<i>Gymnastic Society Garibaldi Messina</i>) briefly took its place until it too was dissolved due to the First World War.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>After World War I, a club under the name <b>US Messinese</b> was founded and entered to play in the <a href="/wiki/Coppa_Federale_Siciliana" title="Coppa Federale Siciliana">Coppa Federale Siciliana</a> the following year; an all Sicilian championship disputed in <a href="/wiki/Messina" title="Messina">Messina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catania" title="Catania">Catania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Palermo" title="Palermo">Palermo</a>. Messina finished as runners-up.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:122px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:FC_Messina_logo.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="439" data-file-width="322" height="164" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/33/FC_Messina_logo.svg/120px-FC_Messina_logo.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/33/FC_Messina_logo.svg/180px-FC_Messina_logo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/33/FC_Messina_logo.svg/240px-FC_Messina_logo.svg.png 2x" width="120"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:FC_Messina_logo.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Logo used from the early 2000s to 2009.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The club participated in the Italian Football Championship of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_Football_Championship_1921-22_(C.C.I.)" title="Italian Football Championship 1921-22 (C.C.I.)">1921–22</a> organised by the C.C.I. finishing third in the Sicilian group section; this was the first Championship in which clubs from the Island were entered. The following season the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIGC" title="FIGC">FIGC</a> and CCI were unified. This coincided with mergers in Messina, as another side, <b>Umberto I Messina</b>, was incorporated into US Messinese, and thus the club changed its name to <b>US Messinese Umberto I</b> in October 1922. The following month this new side was fused again, this time with Messina Sporting Club; creating the <b>Messina Football Club</b>. Only two years later in December 1924 FC Messina was melted, and the players became part of the reformed <b>US Messinese</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Finally, Messinese qualified for the semi-finals of the International league, after beating Palermo 3–0 in the Sicilian championship of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_Football_Championship_1924-25#Sicily" title="Italian Football Championship 1924-25">1924–25</a>. Here Messinese played against <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Alba Roma</a>, <a href="/wiki/S.S._Cavese_1919" title="S.S. Cavese 1919">Cavese</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.S._Bari" title="A.S. Bari">Liberty Bari</a>, but failed to win a single match, scoring only two goals in six games. Messina would be promoted to <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_B_1932-33" title="Serie B 1932-33">1932–33</a> campaign under the presidency of Francesco Lombardo and Koenig's coaching, and remained in the league for six seasons. The spell in <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> was also notable for the local rivalry between them and <a href="/wiki/Calcio_Catania" title="Calcio Catania">Calcio Catania</a>.</p>

<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="From_1940_to_1947">From 1940 to 1947</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A.C.R._Messina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: From 1940 to 1947">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>Down in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C" title="Serie C">Serie C</a>, AC Messina were withdrawn and folded during 1940–41. The following season in 1941–42 a club named <b>US Peloro 1906</b> changed its name and became <b>US Mario Passamonte</b> (named after a fallen hero of the war in Africa). The idea was to enter the club into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C" title="Serie C">Serie C</a> in place of Messina, however this was unsuccessful, until the following season.</p>
<p>It would not be long before all activity was halted in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_football" title="Italian football">Italian football</a> for World War II. After several mergers in 1945, including one between US Passamonte and AP Messina, the club <b>AS Messina</b> subsequently emerged as a post-war representative of <a href="/wiki/Messina" title="Messina">Messina</a>. This was not a clean cut merger however, some players and officials formed the rival club <b>Giostra Messina</b>; both Giostra and AS Messina reached the finals of the Southern League but eventually finished fourth and fifth respectively.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="A.C._Riunite_Messina">A.C. Riunite Messina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A.C.R._Messina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: A.C. Riunite Messina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ACR_Messina.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="201" data-file-width="200" height="151" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/ff/ACR_Messina.png/150px-ACR_Messina.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/f/ff/ACR_Messina.png 1.5x" width="150"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ACR_Messina.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Logo used until 1993, as A.C. Riunite Messina.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1947, the two teams <i>AS Messina</i> and <i>Giostra Messina</i> were united as one merged club <b>Associazioni Calcio Riunite Messina</b>, abbreviated as AC Riunite Messina.</p>
<p>The 1950s for Messina began in glorious fashion, they were crowned champions of Serie C under the management of <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslav</a> manager Mihalj Balačić. Messina did not falter in <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a>, during their first season in the league they avoided relegation. Giuseppe Melazzo and the Comitato Reggenza owned the club during this new period of relative success. During the following season, Messina finished in third place.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Messina1938.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="310" data-file-width="530" height="129" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/5e/Messina1938.JPG/220px-Messina1938.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/5e/Messina1938.JPG/330px-Messina1938.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/5e/Messina1938.JPG/440px-Messina1938.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Messina1938.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Messina's last squad of the 1930s, while Lombardo was president.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Throughout the rest of the 1950s, Messina remained in the division as a whole finishing in a respectful position. Goffredo Muglia took over as president in 1958 to begin a brand new era for the club. For the first time in their history, Messina were crowned champions of <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> during the 1962–63 season. The race for the championship was a very close one which went down to the last day of the season, with Messina finishing above <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.S._Bari" title="A.S. Bari">Bari</a> and <a href="/wiki/S.S._Lazio" title="S.S. Lazio">Lazio</a>.</p>
<p>For their first ever season in <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a>, the football squad for Messina included; Morelli, Brambilla, Stucchi, Pagani, Dotti, <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peruvian</a> Benitez, Ghelfi, Fascetti, Morbello, Canuti and Clerici. Messina's first game in Serie A took place on 15 September 1963, it ended in a 3–1 defeat against <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">Sampdoria</a> at <a href="/wiki/Stadio_Luigi_Ferraris" title="Stadio Luigi Ferraris">Stadio Luigi Ferraris</a>. As a whole the first part of the season was not a success, they won only 2 games, but they managed to turn it around in the second half of the season, with 7 wins; beating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juventus" title="Juventus">Juventus</a> (1–0), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fiorentina" title="Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a> (1–0) and Sampdoria (4–3). The surge of wins in the latter part of the season helped them stay up, finishing 14th.</p>
<p>The next season for the club in Serie A would not be so fortunate, they were relegated in 17th place. Some notable high points of the season included a 1–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Roma</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Stadio_Olimpico" title="Stadio Olimpico">Stadio Olimpico</a>. A team from Rome would also be the opposition for Messina's other most impressive result of the season, they stunned <a href="/wiki/S.S._Lazio" title="S.S. Lazio">Lazio</a> by beating them 4–0 on the last day of the season.</p>
<p>The Sicilian side were not able to bounce straight back up into Serie A, and in fact during their third season back down in Serie B were relegated. After several seasons finishing in and around the top 10 positions of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C" title="Serie C">Serie C</a>, Messina were relegated down to <a href="/wiki/Serie_D" title="Serie D">Serie D</a> in the 1972–73 season. The club managed to bounce straight back however, winning the Serie D championship and achieving promotion back into C. After a few decent seasons in the upper parts of the table, relegation struck Messina again. In the 1979 season <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C2" title="Serie C2">Serie C2</a> was formed and Messina were placed into it.</p>
<p>By 1983 Messina were champions of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C2" title="Serie C2">Serie C2</a> and had a future star amongst their ranks in <a href="/wiki/Salvatore_Schillaci" title="Salvatore Schillaci">Salvatore Schillaci</a>. The club, now back on the right tracks came close to promotion to Serie B in 1985 with a third-place finish just behind <a href="/wiki/U.S._Citt%C3%A0_di_Palermo" title="U.S. Città di Palermo">Palermo</a>, and won <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C1" title="Serie C1">Serie C1</a> and earned themselves promotion back to <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> the following season.</p>
<p>Time in Serie B during the 1980s was a pleasant one for the Sicilian side, they notched up seventh and eighth-place finishes. In 1989 Schillaci was sold to Turin giants <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> and, just three seasons after Schillaci's departure, Messina lost their position in Serie B and were relegated down to C1 and then spiraled into further trouble. The club finished 12th in Serie C1 in the season 1992–93, but due to financial difficulties the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIGC" title="FIGC">FIGC</a> cancelled all professional football activity for Messina.</p>
<p>In 1993 it was included to <a href="/wiki/Promozione" title="Promozione">Sicilian Promozione</a> and in 1994 it was admitted in <a href="/wiki/Eccellenza_Sicily" title="Eccellenza Sicily">Sicilian Eccellenza</a>. In this league the club has played until 1998 when following the relegation in <a href="/wiki/Promozione" title="Promozione">Sicilian Promozione</a> was dissolved.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="A.S._Messina">A.S. Messina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A.C.R._Messina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: A.S. Messina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The decision was thought to be unjust by the club and fans, with Messina been thrown into a footballing abyss never known before. In the summer 1993 <b>A.S. Messina</b> was founded with the president Pietro La Malfa, beginning in the amateurs national championship (C.N.D.) with the objective to bring back the <i>giallorossi</i> to professional football.</p>
<p>They played in the Campionato Nazionale Dilettanti for four seasons, finishing high up for the first three of them, but in the season 1996–97 it was ranked last and relegated to the <a href="/wiki/Eccellenza_Sicily" title="Eccellenza Sicily">Sicilian Eccellenza</a> league.</p>
<p>In the season 1998–99 it was relegated to <a href="/wiki/Promozione" title="Promozione">Sicilian Promozione</a> and was dissolved.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="From_US_Peloro_to_F.C._Messina_Peloro">From US Peloro to F.C. Messina Peloro</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A.C.R._Messina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: From US Peloro to F.C. Messina Peloro">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the summer 1994 after the merger of <i>Villafranca</i> and <i>Tremestieri</i> <b>US Peloro</b> was founded. It plays two season in <a href="/wiki/Eccellenza_Sicily" title="Eccellenza Sicily">Sicilian Eccellenza</a> and in 1996 it was promoted to Campionato Nazionale Dilettanti. Then in the 1996 season the club has played in the same league of the town's other team <i>A.S. Messina</i>, where it was ranked 6th.</p>
<p>In July 1997 the club changed its name to <b>Football Club Messina Peloro</b>. In a few short seasons, the club ramped up the Italian league system, from <i>Campionato Nazionale Dilettanti</i> in the 1997–98 season, Messina were promoted into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C2" title="Serie C2">Serie C2</a> as champions. Promoted to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C1" title="Serie C1">Serie C1</a> in 2000, they immediately managed to fight hard for a promotion spot back to <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a>, winning promotion to <a href="/wiki/Calcio_Catania" title="Calcio Catania">Calcio Catania</a> after play-offs. In less than a decade, under the presidency of Aliotta, Messina had climbed back up from the abyss and were back in the upper part of the Italian league system.</p>
<p>After two seasons in Serie B, local entrepreneur Pietro Franza took over, and in 2003–04 the Sicilians gained promotion back into <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> under coach <a href="/wiki/Bortolo_Mutti" title="Bortolo Mutti">Bortolo Mutti</a>. The club had not appeared in the top Italian league since 1965, a total of forty years.</p>
<p>After being tipped as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Underdog_(competition)" title="Underdog (competition)">underdogs</a> in <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> for the 2004–05 season, Messina surprised doubters by producing several good results including defeating both of the Milanese clubs, beating <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">AC Milan</a> first at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Siro_(stadium)" title="San Siro (stadium)">San Siro</a> 2–1,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and then later in the season also with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internazionale_Milano_F.C." title="Internazionale Milano F.C.">Internazionale</a>, this time at home, for the first time in their history; the winning goal was struck by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rafael_Pereira_da_Silva_(footballer_born_1980)" title="Rafael Pereira da Silva (footballer born 1980)">Rafael</a> in the third minute of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Injury_time" title="Injury time">injury time</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Messina managed to stay clear of relegation throughout the whole season, and eventually finished in seventh place in the table, just a single place away from securing a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> place. Also during this period, for the first time in the club's history<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Messina players were called up to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_national_football_team" title="Italian national football team">Italian national football team</a>: first was Alessandro Parisi in 2004, then <a href="/wiki/Carmine_Coppola_(footballer)" title="Carmine Coppola (footballer)">Carmine Coppola</a> in 2005 who was called up twice by the <i>azzurri</i>.</p>
<p>Despite this impressive form, Messina still were in danger of being relegated from Serie A at the end of the season due to a possibility of not having enough finances available to compete in the league. Eventually, though, they successfully managed to stay in the Serie A league. In the 2005–06 season Messina were however unable to repeat their previous impressive season, leading this to the sacking of Mutti, who was replaced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Giampiero_Ventura" title="Giampiero Ventura">Giampiero Ventura</a>; despite all, they looked to be mathematically relegated from the top division after Day 36 losing the derby against Reggina 3–0. However, due to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_A_scandal_of_2006" title="Serie A scandal of 2006">Serie A scandal of 2006</a>, Messina avoided relegation to Serie B despite finishing 18th.</p>
<p>Messina started the 2006–07 season with <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giordano" title="Bruno Giordano">Bruno Giordano</a> as head coach; however, he was replaced on 30 January 2007, by <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Cavasin" title="Alberto Cavasin">Alberto Cavasin</a> because of poor results. On 2 April, following a 2–0 away defeat to <a href="/wiki/Cagliari_Calcio" title="Cagliari Calcio">Cagliari</a>, another team involved in the battle to avoid relegation, Cavasin was sacked too, and Giordano was recalled to fill the coaching position. Giordano made even worse in his second time at Messina, with four defeats in four matches; with Messina second-last placed in the table five matchdays prior to the end of the season, Giordano was sacked was sacked again on 23 April and replaced by <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Bolchi" title="Bruno Bolchi">Bruno Bolchi</a>. Messina were relegated at the end of the season.</p>
<p>After a quiet Serie B season in 2007–08, on July 2008 Messina chairman Pietro Franza announced he did not find any investor ready to take over the club, and that he was consequently giving up the club's <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> membership, declaring also he would look forward to enter the club into an amateur league. On 1 August, it was confirmed that Messina was admitted to <a href="/wiki/Serie_D" title="Serie D">Serie D</a>. On November 2008, the Court of Messina declared the club to be bankrupt and appointed a trustee to start a search for potential investors.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="A.C._Rinascita_Messina">A.C. Rinascita Messina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A.C.R._Messina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: A.C. Rinascita Messina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On March 2009, Rome-based entrepreneur Alfredo Di Lullo acquired <i>Football Club Messina Peloro</i> in a blind auction held by the Court of Messina and in April 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The club was renamed <b>A.C. Rinascita Messina</b> (the term <i>rinascita</i> means "revived").</p>
<p>On 4 January 2011 the club is transferred to the group Martorano which 14 August 2011 sold it to Raffaele "Lello" Manfredi.</p>
<p>In June 2012 the club was acquired by a consortium led by prominent football manager <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pietro_Lo_Monaco&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pietro Lo Monaco (page does not exist)">Pietro Lo Monaco</a>, former managing director at Serie A club Catania and one of the masterminds behind the club's rise into top flight in the 2000s. Messina finished 4th in Group I and qualified to promotion playoffs in 2011–12 season. It eliminated <a href="/wiki/A.S.D._Battipagliese" title="A.S.D. Battipagliese">Battipagliese</a> by a score of 3–1 in 1st round but was eliminated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuova_Cosenza_Calcio" title="Nuova Cosenza Calcio">Cosenza</a> by a score of 3–0 in 2nd round. Messina finally became champions of I Group of Serie D and promoted to <a href="/wiki/Lega_Pro_Seconda_Divisione" title="Lega Pro Seconda Divisione">Lega Pro Seconda Divisione</a>, 4th level of Italian league next season. After winning the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Lega_Pro_Seconda_Divisione" title="2013–14 Lega Pro Seconda Divisione">Lega Pro Seconda Divisione/B title</a>, the club was then admitted to play in the unified <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Lega_Pro" title="2014–15 Lega Pro">2014–15 Lega Pro</a> season.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="A.C._Riunite_Messina_1947">A.C. Riunite Messina 1947</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A.C.R._Messina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: A.C. Riunite Messina 1947">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Since the summer 2014 the new name of the club is "Associazioni Calcio Riunite Messina 1947".</p>
<p>In the 2014–15 season it was relegated to Serie D, but it was readmitted to Lega Pro for involvement in the sporting fraud.</p>
<p>Since summer 2015 the new owner and president is Natale Stracuzzi.</p>

<p>The former club <b>Football Club Messina Peloro</b> have played their home matches in the new <a class="external text" href="http://www.acrmessinasrl.it/infostadio.aspx" rel="nofollow">Stadio Comunale San Filippo</a> since the 2004–05 to 2008–09 season. Since the season 2009–10 plays here the current team of <b>A.C. Riunite Messina 1947</b>.</p>
<p>The capacity of the stadium is 37,895 seats. It is named after the part of the city in which it is located, but a couple of petitions aim to rename it after the former Messina manager <a href="/wiki/Franco_Scoglio" title="Franco Scoglio">Franco Scoglio</a> or the Messina Saint <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hannibal_Mary_Di_Francia" title="Hannibal Mary Di Francia">Hannibal Mary Di Francia</a>.</p>
<p>The old stadium, the 11,000 seater <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stadio_Comunale_Giovanni_Celeste" title="Stadio Comunale Giovanni Celeste">Stadio Comunale Giovanni Celeste</a>, is now used by <b>S.S.D. Città di Messina</b>, the second team of the city.</p>
