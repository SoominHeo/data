<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Launch">Launch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_R&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Launch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Galaxy R was launched in September 2011 in <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Discontinuation">Discontinuation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_R&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Discontinuation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This mobile set suffered some problems and its production was stopped due to hardware problems. Many people reported that the phone had gone dead, and was not able to boot even after the re-setting the mobile phone and many other issues. Samsung started replacing the Galaxy R Handset with Galaxy S. This problem was specifically noticed in the phones released in India. There has been no official stance taken by Samsung. Limited stock release remains the reason stated by many of its dealers and customer service centers.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hardware">Hardware</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_R&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hardware">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Processor">Processor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_R&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Processor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Nvidia" title="Nvidia">Nvidia</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tegra_2#Tegra_2_series" title="Tegra 2">Tegra 2</a> <a href="/wiki/System_on_a_chip" title="System on a chip">System on a chip</a> (SoC) uses Nvidia's own GeForce ultra-low power (ULP) for its GPU.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tegra_2_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tegra_2-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nvidia's Tegra 2 supports the <i>Tegra Zone</i> application and service<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tegra_Zone_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tegra_Zone-6">[6]</a></sup> which is intended to give Android users an enhanced gaming experience by allowing users to download games that have been well optimized for Tegra 2 powered devices. This has been marketed as "console-quality gaming" by Nvidia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Tegra 2 also features support for hardware acceleration for Flash and Javascript within websites, and is one of the first SoCs to be natively supported by Android 3.0 (a.k.a. <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)#Version_history" title="Android (operating system)">Honeycomb</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Memory">Memory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_R&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Memory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Galaxy R features 1 GB of dedicated RAM and 8 GB of internal mass storage. Within the battery compartment there is an external microSD card slot.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Display">Display</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_R&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Display">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Samsung Galaxy R uses a 108.5-millimetre (4.27 in) WVGA Super CLEAR LCD (SC-LCD) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capacitive_touchscreen" title="Capacitive touchscreen">capacitive touchscreen</a>. It has a 4.2" 16 million colours screen, without using the Pentile matrix.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Camera">Camera</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_R&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Camera">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the back of the device is an 5-megapixel camera with single LED flash that can record videos in high-definition <a href="/wiki/720p" title="720p">720p</a>. There is also a fixed-focus front-facing 1.3-megapixel camera for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Video_call#Current_usage" title="Video call">video calling</a>, taking photos as well as general video recording. The video recording has been criticized for the lack of focus.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Connectivity">Connectivity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_R&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Connectivity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>GPRS:</b> Class 33, <b>EDGE:</b> Class 33, <b>HSDPA:</b> 21 Mbit/s; HSUPA, 5.76 Mbit/s, <b>WLAN:</b> Wi-Fi 802.11 b/g/n, Wi-Fi Direct, Wi-Fi hotspot, DLNA, <b>Bluetooth:</b> Yes, v3.0 with A2DP, EDR, <b>USB:</b> Yes, microUSB v2.0</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Software">Software</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_R&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Software">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Android_2.3_and_4.0">Android 2.3 and 4.0</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_R&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Android 2.3 and 4.0">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Galaxy R ships with <a href="/wiki/Android_version_history#2.3" title="Android version history">Android 2.3.4 (Gingerbread)</a> installed. An Android 4.0 Ice Cream Sandwich (ICS) update for the Galaxy R was released in August 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ics_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-ics-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="After-market_development">After-market development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_R&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: After-market development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt>Android Jelly Bean 4.1, 4.2 and 4.3</dt>
</dl>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="User_interface">User interface</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_R&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: User interface">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The phone employs the latest proprietary Samsung <a href="/wiki/TouchWiz" title="TouchWiz">TouchWiz</a> 4.0 user interface.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bundled_applications">Bundled applications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_R&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Bundled applications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Four new Samsung 'Hub' applications were revealed at the 2011 <a href="/wiki/Mobile_World_Congress" title="Mobile World Congress">Mobile World Congress</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Galaxy_S_II" title="Samsung Galaxy S II">Galaxy S II</a> and the Galaxy R includes the same:</p>
<dl>
<dt>Social Hub</dt>
<dd>Which integrates popular social networking services like <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> and <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> into one place rather than in separate applications.</dd>
<dt>Readers Hub</dt>
<dd>This hub provides the ability to access, read and download online newspapers, ebooks and magazines from a worldwide selection.</dd>
<dt>Music Hub</dt>
<dd>An application store for downloading and purchasing music tracks on the device. Samsung has teamed up with <a href="/wiki/7digital" title="7digital">7digital</a> to offer this service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-techradar_mwc_2011_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-techradar_mwc_2011-16">[16]</a></sup></dd>
<dt>Game Hub</dt>
<dd>An application store for downloading and purchasing games. Samsung has teamed up with partners including <a href="/wiki/Gameloft" title="Gameloft">Gameloft</a> to offer this service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-techradar_mwc_2011_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-techradar_mwc_2011-16">[16]</a></sup></dd>
<dt>Other applications</dt>
<dd>More applications include <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Kies" title="Samsung Kies">Kies 2.0</a>, Kies Air,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> AllShare (for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DLNA" title="DLNA">DLNA</a>), Voice Recognition, Google Voice Translation,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Google_Maps" title="Google Maps">Google Maps</a> with Latitude, Places, Navigation (beta) and Lost Phone Management, <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Flash" title="Adobe Flash">Adobe Flash</a> 10.2, <a href="/wiki/Polaris_Office" title="Polaris Office">Polaris Office</a> application and 'QuickType' by SWYPE.</dd>
</dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Media_support">Media support</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_R&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Media support">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Galaxy R supports various audio formats including MP3, OGG, AAC, AAC+, eAAC+, AMR-NB, AMR-WB, WMA, WAV, MID, AC3, IMY, FLAC, XMF audio formats and video formats support for MPEG4, H.264, H.263, WMV, DivX, Xvid, VC-1. Recording &amp; Playback in up to <a href="/wiki/720p" title="720p">720p</a> HD video files.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-galaxy-r-Tegra-2_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-galaxy-r-Tegra-2-19">[19]</a></sup> Unlike the Exynos 4210 found in the Galaxy S II, the Tegra 2 has been found to not support 'high profile' encoded h.264 video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-no_high_profile_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-no_high_profile-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-no_high_profile_2_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-no_high_profile_2-21">[21]</a></sup> A recent kernel for ICS (developed by a xda developer) also enabled the mobile to support HDMI output support via micro USB to MHL cable..</p>
