<p>The <b>Samsung Galaxy Mini</b> (GT-S5570[B/L/i]) is a <a href="/wiki/Smartphone" title="Smartphone">smartphone</a> manufactured by <a href="/wiki/Samsung" title="Samsung">Samsung</a> that runs the <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">Android</a> operating system. It was announced and released by Samsung in early 2011. In some markets it is known as <b>Samsung Galaxy Next/Pop</b>. It is currently available in four different colors; steel grey, white, lime and orange. The same device is sold in the United States as the <b>Samsung Dart</b> exclusively for <a href="/wiki/T-Mobile" title="T-Mobile">T-Mobile</a>. The face buttons were changed to capacitive matching the rest of the Galaxy lineup. The FM radio functionality was removed.</p>
<p>It was succeeded by the <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Galaxy_Mini_2" title="Samsung Galaxy Mini 2">Samsung Galaxy Mini 2</a>, featuring major processor improvements from the original.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_Mini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Galaxy Mini is a 3.5G smartphone that offers quad-band <a href="/wiki/GSM" title="GSM">GSM</a> and was announced with two-band <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HSDPA" title="HSDPA">HSDPA</a> (900/2100 MHz) at 7.2 Mbit/s. The display is a 3.14 in (80 mm)-diagonal TFT LCD with a 240×320 pixels QVGA resolution supporting up to 256,000 colors.</p>
<p>The Galaxy Mini is presented as an entry-level smartphone, and is (as of 13 May 2011) one of the cheapest Android phones on the market.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The Galaxy Mini originally ran on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Android_2.2" title="Android 2.2">Android 2.2 Froyo</a>, but in May 2011, Samsung announced that the Galaxy Mini (along with other Galaxy models) will get an official upgrade to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Android_2.3" title="Android 2.3">Android 2.3 Gingerbread</a>. An official upgrade to Android 2.3.6 (Gingerbread) was released via <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Kies" title="Samsung Kies">Samsung Kies</a> on December 9, 2011 for some mobile operators. The Galaxy Mini can also be flashed with custom ROMs such as <a href="/wiki/CyanogenMod" title="CyanogenMod">CyanogenMod</a> releases (although not officially supported by Samsung) where it is codenamed <i>tass</i>. The officially supported version of CyanogenMod on the Galaxy Mini as of August 2012 is CyanogenMod 7.2. It will run CyanogenMod 10 but it is unofficially supported so there may be stability issues. It can be overclocked to 800 MHz with SetCPU.</p>
