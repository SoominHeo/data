<p><b><a href="/wiki/Samsung_Galaxy" title="Samsung Galaxy">Samsung Galaxy</a> Gio</b> (GT-S5660) is a <a href="/wiki/Smartphone" title="Smartphone">smartphone</a> manufactured by <a href="/wiki/Samsung" title="Samsung">Samsung</a> and running the <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">Android operating system</a>.</p>
<p>It was announced at the 2011 <a href="/wiki/Mobile_World_Congress" title="Mobile World Congress">Mobile World Congress</a> as one of four low-end Samsung smartphones, along with <i><a href="/wiki/Samsung_Galaxy_Ace" title="Samsung Galaxy Ace">Galaxy Ace</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Samsung_Galaxy_Fit" title="Samsung Galaxy Fit">Galaxy Fit</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Samsung_Galaxy_Mini" title="Samsung Galaxy Mini">Galaxy Mini</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-engadget_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-engadget-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <i>Galaxy Gio</i> phone made its Canadian debut in August 2011. Initially available to <a href="/wiki/Bell_Canada" title="Bell Canada">Bell Canada</a>, the <i>Gio</i> was soon made available at <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Mobile_Canada" title="Virgin Mobile Canada">Virgin Mobile Canada</a> and <a href="/wiki/Solo_Mobile" title="Solo Mobile">Solo Mobile</a>. Starting in December 2011, <i>Galaxy Gio</i> was also made available in the United States as <i>Samsung Repp</i> on U.S. Cellular.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_Gio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hardware">Hardware</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_Gio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Hardware">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Galaxy Gio</i> has a 3.2 in (8.1 cm) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HVGA" title="HVGA">HVGA</a> multi-touch, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capacitive_touchscreen" title="Capacitive touchscreen">capacitive touchscreen</a>, a 3.2-megapixel camera with auto focus, and an 800 MHz Qualcomm MSM7227 Turbo (ARMv6), 278 MB RAM, 158 Mb internal storage, 3G HSPA+, an FM radio with <a href="/wiki/Radio_Data_System" title="Radio Data System">RDS</a> support, GPS, Wi-Fi 802.11 b/g/n, and a 1350 mAh battery. In addition to the phone's internal memory, the device has a MicroSD card slot, and comes with 2 Gb MicroSD card (as new).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GSMArena_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-GSMArena-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-engadget_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-engadget-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> With that phone, the bundled MicroSD card is the main location for storage of apps and user-generated data, such as photos and multimedia.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Software">Software</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_Gio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Software">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Galaxy Gio</i> originally came with Android 2.2 "<a href="/wiki/Android_version_history" title="Android version history">Froyo</a>", with Samsung's <a href="/wiki/TouchWiz" title="TouchWiz">TouchWiz</a> user interface.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-engadget_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-engadget-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In early August 2011, Samsung officially rolled out the 2.3 "Gingerbread" update via <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Kies" title="Samsung Kies">Kies</a>. In September 2011, Samsung rolled out an update in the Netherlands marked "PDA:KPS PHONE:KPA CSC:KP1 (XEN)".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tweakers.net_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-tweakers.net-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Users can also upgrade to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Android_2.3#Android_2.3.x_Gingerbread" title="Android 2.3">Android 2.3.6</a> via <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Kies" title="Samsung Kies">Samsung Kies</a>, and as of October 2012<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Galaxy_Gio&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, Android 2.3.4 and 2.3.6 had come preinstalled on many <i>Gio</i>s sold throughout the world.</p>
<p>Changes associated with the 2.3.6 update include a new lockscreen, a new phone icon, the blue-glow scrolling effect, and a few other cosmetic changes related to the system UI.</p>
<p>Samsung has not made Android 4.1 available for Gio<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> due to its less powerful hardware, though users can upgrade via Root and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ClockworkMod_Recovery" title="ClockworkMod Recovery">ClockworkMod Recovery</a>. MaclawStudio has also made available a bugless and stable port of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Android_Open_Source_Project" title="Android Open Source Project">AOSP</a>-ROM — both 4.0.x Ice Cream Sandwich and 4.1 <abbr title="Jelly Bean also includes 4.3, but not necessarily as a ROM for Gio">Jelly Bean</abbr>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Because the native Android browser is outdated, modern websites can be visited with <a href="/wiki/Firefox_for_Android" title="Firefox for Android">Firefox for Android</a> by Mozilla. As the <i>Galaxy Gio</i> phone contains a central processor based on ARMv6 architecture, then the most recent Firefox version for ARMv6 devices is <a class="external text" href="https://archive.mozilla.org/pub/mobile/releases/31.3.0esr/android-armv6/" rel="nofollow">31.3.0esr</a>, released on 17 October 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Mozilla have since ceased development for this CPU architecture. The primary method to reduce Firefox resource usage is to install the <a href="/wiki/NoScript" title="NoScript">NoScript Anywhere</a> addon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
