<p>The <b>Samsung ATIV S</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Touchscreen" title="Touchscreen">touchscreen</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slate_phone" title="Slate phone">slate</a> <a href="/wiki/Smartphone" title="Smartphone">smartphone</a> manufactured by <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Electronics" title="Samsung Electronics">Samsung Electronics</a> running the <a href="/wiki/Windows_Phone_8" title="Windows Phone 8">Windows Phone 8</a> <a href="/wiki/Mobile_operating_system" title="Mobile operating system">operating system</a>, upgradeable to <a href="/wiki/Windows_Phone_8.1" title="Windows Phone 8.1">Windows Phone 8.1</a>. The ATIV S is Samsung's first Windows Phone 8 device, and one of the first devices under its <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Ativ" title="Samsung Ativ">ATIV</a> series of <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Microsoft Windows</a>-based products. It was shown at the <a href="/wiki/Internationale_Funkausstellung_Berlin" title="Internationale Funkausstellung Berlin">IFA 2012</a> in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> and was the first Windows Phone 8 device to be officially unveiled,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnet-ativifa_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-cnet-ativifa-2">[2]</a></sup> ahead of the <a href="/wiki/Nokia_Lumia_920" title="Nokia Lumia 920">Nokia Lumia 920</a>.</p>
<p>There are three variants of the ATIV S. All feature a 4.8″ 1280 by 720 HD <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Super_AMOLED" title="Super AMOLED">Super AMOLED</a> display with a pixel density of 306ppi, a 1.5 GHz dual-core processor, 1 GB of RAM, an 8-megapixel rear-facing camera, <a href="/wiki/Near_field_communication" title="Near field communication">near field communications</a>, and support for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HSPA%2B" title="HSPA+">HSPA+</a> networks. Specific variants include:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AtivSReview_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-AtivSReview-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>GT-I8370 - the globalized version of the ATIV S, with a 1.9MP front-facing camera</li>
<li>GT-I8750 - a version specific to the UK and Singapore but with a 1.2MP front camera instead of 1.9MP</li>
<li>SGH-T899M - sold in Canada, equipped with 700 MHz <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long_Term_Evolution" title="Long Term Evolution">LTE</a> and Bluetooth 2.1 (downgraded from 3.0)</li>
</ul>
<p>The ATIV S is available with 16 or 32 GB of internal storage with micro SDXC expansion<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnet-ativifa_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-cnet-ativifa-2">[2]</a></sup> for up to an additional 128 GB or more. It further features a 2300mAh removable battery and a brushed-metal look of the battery door. Overall, the hardware is very similar to the Android-based <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galaxy_S_III" title="Galaxy S III">Galaxy S III</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>A variant of the Samsung ATIV S, called the <b>Samsung ATIV S Neo</b>, was announced by <a href="/wiki/Sprint_Corporation" title="Sprint Corporation">Sprint</a> on June 26, 2013 for a summer 2013 release, alongside the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HTC_8X" title="HTC 8X">HTC 8XT</a>. This phone's appearance is similar to the ATIV S, though has notable differences spec-wise, having 1.4 GHz processor speed, a 2000mAh battery, TFT LCD screen, and LTE connectivity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
