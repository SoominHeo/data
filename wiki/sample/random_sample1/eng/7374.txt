<p><b>Samsung Electro-Mechanics</b> (<b>SEM</b>, <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: 삼성전기) is a <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> <a href="/wiki/Electronic_component" title="Electronic component">electronic component</a> company headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Suwon" title="Suwon">Suwon</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gyeonggi-do" title="Gyeonggi-do">Gyeonggi-do</a>, South Korea. It is the flagship subsidiary of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samsung_Group" title="Samsung Group">Samsung Group</a>.</p>
<p>Samsung Electro-Mechanics, established in 1973 as a manufacturer of key electronic components, has become the leading electronic parts maker in Korea and a major global player, with US$6.12 billion in revenue (2011).</p>
<p>Samsung Electro-Mechanics consists of four divisions. The LCR (Inductance Capacitance Resistance) division includes multilayer ceramic chip capacitors and tantalum capacitors; the ACI (Advanced Circuit Interconnection) division includes high density interconnections and IC (integrated circuit) substrates; the CDS (circuit drive solution) division includes digital tuners, network modules, power modules, and ubiquitous modules; and the OMS (Opto and Mechatronics Solution) division includes image sensor modules and precision motors.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Printed_circuit_boards_.28PCBs.29">Printed circuit boards (PCBs)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Electro-Mechanics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Printed circuit boards (PCBs)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The purpose of printed circuit boards (PCBs) is to transmit electrical signals among various electronic parts and components. Microcircuits and build-up technologies are applied to produce the class of PCB known as the high-density interconnection (HDI) board. These value-added parts enable engineers to make their finished products lighter, thinner and smaller. HDI boards are mainly found in top-end handheld devices, and Samsung Electro-Mechanics enjoys a major share of the world market for HDI boards used in mobile phones.</p>
<p><b>Package substrates</b> The package substrate is responsible for conveying electrical signals between semiconductors and the motherboard on which they are attached. This substrate consists of much smaller circuitry than that found on conventional types. <b>Ball grid array (BGA)</b> is a technology that eliminates the need for wire bonding on chip packages. <b>The flip-chip chip scale package (FC CSP)</b> is a substrate that uses bumps to electrically connect the chip with the motherboard. The flip-chip ball grid array is a substrate for high-end semiconductor packages. The semiconductor chip and substrate are connected by flip-chip bumps, improving the electrical and thermal properties.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="MLCC_.28multilayer_ceramic_capacitor.29">MLCC (multilayer ceramic capacitor)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Electro-Mechanics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: MLCC (multilayer ceramic capacitor)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The multilayer ceramic capacitor (MLCC) is a commonly used part in electronic products. For example, a smart phone has more than 400 MLCCs, while a tablet PC contains about 500, a laptop computer contains least 800 and an LCD TV uses over 1,300. These devices serve as a kind of dam to adjust electric current to the specification of the product in question.</p>
<p>The global trend toward smaller and lighter electronics products is driving demand for MLCCs of smaller sizes with higher capacities.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Camera_modules">Camera modules</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Electro-Mechanics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Camera modules">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The camera module has an image sensor that converts the light that enters through the lens into digital signals, which are then used to produce still pictures or video.</p>
<p>Samsung Electro-Mechanics is developing exceptional mobile phone camera modules with high pixel resolution, ultra-slim profile, automatic focus, zoom lens and other attractive features.</p>
<p>The company is also expanding its camera module business into new applications such as automobiles and home networks.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Greenhouse_gas">Greenhouse gas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Electro-Mechanics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Greenhouse gas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the results of the evaluation of CDP(Carbon Disclosure Project) Korea Committee in 2016, Samsung Electro-Mechanics has been selected in CDP Korea Hall of Fame for three consecutive years in 2014. CDP Korea Hall of Fame is retained only if the company achieves Leadership A- or higher.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> CDP is the assessment initiative showing high reliability of the entire world's sustainability assessment indicators.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It is used as investment guidelines related to climate change of financial investment institute by collecting and opening greenhouse gas information of the world's major listed companies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since 2008, Samsung Electro-Mechanics has built low-carbon management system such as carbon management system, low-carbon product development. It also participated in Green Partnership promoted by Korea government for making low-carbon supply of suppliers by supporting for reduction of carbon dioxide and development of low-carbon product to the suppliers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2013, it published carbon management report in order to open performance carbon management and future plans of carbon management and it opened 'Low-Carbon mid-term Vision 2020' vision with goal of 15% reduction of GHG intensity compared to 2014 by the year of 2020. It reduced by about 250,000 tons of greenhouse gases over the last five years based on the low-carbon medium term strategy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Energy">Energy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Electro-Mechanics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Energy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 34th Energy-Saving Promotion conference awarding a prize to person who contributed to the improvement of energy efficiency and overcoming the power supply crisis, Chi-joon Choi, CEO of Samsung Electro-Mechanics was awarded the Siver Tower Industry medal with best honor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Since the organization dedicated to energy was established in 2008, it has established energy management policy and has built global energy management system basis. With these system, it had made about 2,000 energy reduction projects for 5 years until 2012 and it accomplished investment of 35.6 billion won and reduction effect of 42.4 billion one. It is removing energy waste factors by installing standards of equipment energy efficiency rating system, building efficiency rating system, supply equipment efficiency rating system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was awarded the energy management system certificate (ISO 50001) by Korea Energy Management Corporation and it is participating to "Energy Management Accomplishment Evaluation Demo Business".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_Contribution">Social Contribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Electro-Mechanics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Social Contribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Samsung Electro-Mechanics is contributing to society in many ways by providing cultural activities, physical activities and medical support. SEMCO runs a handicapped/youth orchestra program, hosts a national badminton tournament for handicapped players and provides free artificial joint surgery for low income families. In addition, each Division of SEMCO has established a sisterhood relationship with farming/fishing villages, in order to provide support for the local communities.</p>
<p><br/>
Since 2002, Samsung Electro-Mechanics started to set up a sisterhood relationship with farming or fishing villages around the country, and as of 2016, Samsung Electro-Mechanics is now twinned with 17 small towns in Jeolla-do and Gangwon-do.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2005, Samsung Electro-Mechanics started to provide free artificial joint surgery for low income families as part of the company's medical support policy, and as of 2016, 514 beneficiaries from Gyeonggi-do, Chungcheong-do and Gangwon-do regions underwent the procedure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since 2006, a badminton tournament for disabled is regularly held annually, and about 3,000 people including the players, supporters and volunteer service teams participate from 16 cities around the country.</p>
<p>Since 2008, the national music competition for handicapped students, in which individuals or groups of students with developmental disabilities, visual impairment or physical disabilities can participate and compete, is being hosted along with TJB Daejeon TV.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, Samsung Electro-Mechanics founded an orchestra, hello!SEMCO, composed of 35 children and teenage members with disabilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> They recently held their first regular concert.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Win-Win_Growth">Win-Win Growth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Electro-Mechanics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Win-Win Growth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2016, Samsung Electro-Mechanics obtained an excellent grade in win-win growth index evaluated by the National Commission for Corporate Partnership since 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Samsung Electro-Mechanics developed a win-win fund of 77.5 billion KRW from October 2010 to support facilities and operation of its partner companies (suppliers), and still strengthening support for its suppliers by expanding performance sharing policy, providing funds and supports for 2nd vendors and providing consultation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> For 6 years from 2006 until 2012, SEMCO's sales improved by 2.4 times, while the average sales of partner companies (suppliers) improved by 3.1 times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2004, Samsung Electro-Mechanics created its own win-win cooperation department and provided a variety of supports in many different areas since.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2005, "Win-Win Plaza" that can accommodate employees from partner companies and co-develop many projects, was established in Suwon Plant. Every year, about 10 suppliers move in and closely work with Samsung Electro-Mechanics from the initial stage of product development. For instance, "Digitaloptics", a partner company who moved into the Win-Win Plaza in 2011, co-participated in "Sharing Performance Policy" with Samsung Electro-Mechanics, and as a result, they were able to improve their productivity, reduced the cost of lens to 50% and reduced molding cost.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, Samsung Electro-Mechanics established an educational-industrial consortium and a win-win academy to expand learning opportunities for employees of partner companies and provide training &amp; education that centers around the work-site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Since 2011, Samsung Electro-Mechanics hosted an annual "Win-Win Growth Technology Development Exhibition" where SEMCO and partner companies can co-develop new products that require immediate securing of original technologies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2013, Samsung Electro-Mechanics held a relay open communication forum with 320 partner companies and 2nd vendors for 1 month and discussed about new technologies, new product development R&amp;D and how to improve competitiveness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> From the open communication forum, 3 key topics for Win-Win Growth ("Improving supplier competitiveness, Expanding performance sharing policy and creating green management ecosystem) was suggested.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, Samsung Electro-Mechanics set direction of win-win growth as TRUST and run a variety of programs; Technology, Responsibility, Upgrade, Share/Security, Together. Since 2004, Samsung Electro-Mechanics has been holding a Win-win Growth Festival in which Samsung Electro-Mechanics shares the direction of win-win growth and award top suppliers every year in order to strengthen strategic partnership with suppliers and promote win-win relationship. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
