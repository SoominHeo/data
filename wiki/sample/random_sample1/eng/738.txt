<p><b>Associazione Calcio Cesena</b> (<small>Italian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Italian" title="Help:IPA for Italian">[tʃɛˈzeːna]</a></span>) is an <a href="/wiki/Football_in_Italy" title="Football in Italy">Italian football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Cesena" title="Cesena">Cesena</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romagna" title="Romagna">Romagna</a>.</p>
<p>The club was formed in 1940 and won the first promotion to <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> in <a href="/wiki/1973%E2%80%9374_Serie_A" title="1973–74 Serie A">1973</a>. Since then, the club have been in the Serie A for a total of 13 seasons, reaching the best achievement in <a href="/wiki/1975%E2%80%9376_Serie_A" title="1975–76 Serie A">1976</a> with a sixth-place finish and a short run in the following season's <a href="/wiki/1976%E2%80%9377_UEFA_Cup" title="1976–77 UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a>. The other three promotions in Serie A have been achieved in <a href="/wiki/1981%E2%80%9382_Serie_A" title="1981–82 Serie A">1981</a>, <a href="/wiki/1987%E2%80%9388_Serie_A" title="1987–88 Serie A">1987</a>, and <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Serie_A" title="2010–11 Serie A">2010</a>, the last one after two consecutive promotions — from the third league (<a href="/wiki/Lega_Pro" title="Lega Pro">Lega Pro</a>) in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lega_Pro_Prima_Divisione_2008%E2%80%9309" title="Lega Pro Prima Divisione 2008–09">2009</a> and from <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> in <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Serie_B" title="2009–10 Serie B">2010</a>, both won in the final game of the season. The club currently plays in the Serie B, relegated after one season in the Serie A at the end of the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Serie_A" title="2014–15 Serie A">2014–15 season</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A.C._Cesena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Founded in 1940, Cesena reached Serie B in 1968 and were promoted to Serie A for the first time in 1973. With players such as Pierluigi Cera and <a href="/wiki/Gianluigi_Savoldi" title="Gianluigi Savoldi">Gianluigi Savoldi</a>, the club held its own and finished a respectable 11th place in their debut season, repeating that finish the following year. In the 1975–76 season, Cesena surprised Italy by finishing sixth and subsequently qualifying for the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a>. The glory was short-lived and they would be relegated the next year.</p>
<p>A second promotion to Serie A followed in 1981 and finished a respectable 10th before being relegated once more in 1983, staying in Serie B for four years. After winning a play-off, they were back in Serie A for 1987–88 and enjoyed a four-year stay, being obdurate enough to just avoid relegation in this time.</p>
<p>After relegation in 1991, Cesena had another chance to return to Serie A in 1994. With players such as Alessandro Teodorani, <a href="/wiki/Emiliano_Salvetti" title="Emiliano Salvetti">Emiliano Salvetti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Piangerelli" title="Luigi Piangerelli">Luigi Piangerelli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aldo_Dolcetti" title="Aldo Dolcetti">Aldo Dolcetti</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dario_H%C3%BCbner" title="Dario Hübner">Dario Hübner</a>, this was a team of considerable ability. They finished level on points with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Padova_Calcio" title="Padova Calcio">Padova</a>, however, and lost a promotion play-off which would be a bitter blow for the club, who would suffer relegation to Serie C1 in 1997. Whilst promotion followed, a relegation play-off against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Pistoiese" title="A.C. Pistoiese">Pistoiese</a> in 2000 would see them condemned to a four-year stay in the third division.</p>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_B_2005-06" title="Serie B 2005-06">2005–06 Serie B</a>, Cesena surprisingly emerged as contenders for promotion to Serie A, ending in sixth place and being therefore qualified for the promotion play-offs. They escaped relegation the following season, but not in 2007–08.</p>
<p>Cesena's first campaign in Serie C1, now rebranded <a href="/wiki/Lega_Pro_Prima_Divisione" title="Lega Pro Prima Divisione">Lega Pro Prima Divisione</a>, started with former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foligno_Calcio" title="Foligno Calcio">Foligno</a> boss <a href="/wiki/Pierpaolo_Bisoli" title="Pierpaolo Bisoli">Pierpaolo Bisoli</a> as new head coach. Throughout the season, Cesena quickly emerged as major contenders for direct promotion, and managed to take first place in the league on Week 33, with only one game remaining and a two-point advantage to challengers <a href="/wiki/Aurora_Pro_Patria_1919" title="Aurora Pro Patria 1919">Pro Patria</a>. On the final week of the season, Cesena's 0–0 draw with <a href="/wiki/Hellas_Verona_F.C." title="Hellas Verona F.C.">Verona</a>, coupled with Pro Patria's 0–0 draw with Padova, gave the <i>bianconeri</i> the league title and direct promotion back to Serie B, after only one season in the Italian third tier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Cesena went to as 3rd in Round 33, one point behind <a href="/wiki/Brescia_Calcio" title="Brescia Calcio">Brescia</a> in the 2009–10 season. Cesena earned their second consecutive promotion after a 1–0 victory at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lupa_Piacenza_S.S.D." title="Lupa Piacenza S.S.D.">Piacenza</a> and Brescia's 2–1 loss in <a href="/wiki/Padua" title="Padua">Padua</a> against Padova on 30 May 2010, finishing 19 years of absence from the Serie A.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Yo-yoing_between_Serie_A_and_B">Yo-yoing between Serie A and B</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A.C._Cesena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Yo-yoing between Serie A and B">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cesena returned to Serie A after 19 years of absence in 2010. After the 2011–12 season, Cesena were relegated from the top tier to the <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a>.</p>
<p>Since relegation Cesena also restructured their finance, which the company revered merger with intermediate holding company <b>Cesena 1940 Srl</b> in December 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The company held 98.23% shares of <b>AC Cesena SpA</b>. After the merger, another intermediate holding company, <b>Opera Cesena Calcio Srl</b> represented by Igor Campedelli (from unknown investor through <b>Romagna Sport Srl</b>), which previously own 65.03% shares on Cesena 1940 Srl,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tuttob_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-tuttob-3">[3]</a></sup> sold 27.6% shares of Cesena to <b>GMG Srl</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> a company of Giorgio Lugaresi, who already owned 30.06% shares of Cesena 1940 Srl before the merger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tuttob_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-tuttob-3">[3]</a></sup> On 24 April 2013 Giorgio Lugaresi was re-elected as the president of A.C. Cesena SpA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> After the transactions the club also recapitalized €9.5 million during 2013–14 season in order to avoid bankruptcy, which GMG Srl through subsidiary <b>Cesena &amp; Co. Scarl</b>, held 9,499,000 out of 9,500,000 shares (99.9895%) of Cesena as of 30 June 2014, with a nominal value of €1 per shares.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cesena2014bilancio_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cesena2014bilancio-6">[6]</a></sup> In February 2014 Cesena was under criminal investigation for the fraud in Campedelli era.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Campedelli was banned 6 months in March 2013 by FIGC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite the financial difficulties, Cesena won promotion back to Serie A on 18 June 2014, winning <a href="/wiki/U.S._Latina_Calcio" title="U.S. Latina Calcio">Latina</a> in playoffs in 4–2 aggregate. In that match, most of the players (12 out of 20) were on loan from other clubs, with only 4 players were under Cesena contract in starting lineup (<a href="/wiki/Francesco_Renzetti" title="Francesco Renzetti">Renzetti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_De_Feudis" title="Giuseppe De Feudis">De Feudis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luca_Garritano" title="Luca Garritano">Garritano</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gr%C3%A9goire_Defrel" title="Grégoire Defrel">Defrel</a>) and 4 players on the bench (Alberto Iglio, <a href="/wiki/Nicol%C3%B2_Consolini" title="Nicolò Consolini">Consolini</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alejandro_Rodr%C3%ADguez_de_Miguel" title="Alejandro Rodríguez de Miguel">Rodríguez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Davide_Succi" title="Davide Succi">Succi</a>). Financially, Cesena had another year of negative <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EBITDA" title="EBITDA">EBITDA</a> in 2013–14 season, for about €11 million, if excluding windfall profit from player trading from the calculation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
