<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Samcheonggak</h1>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Samcheonggak</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>:삼청각 <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>:三淸閣) is a small <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_Korea" title="Culture of Korea">Korean-style</a> mountainside resort in <a href="/wiki/Seongbuk-dong" title="Seongbuk-dong">Seongbuk-dong</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seongbuk-gu" title="Seongbuk-gu">Seongbuk-gu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>. Samcheonggak is near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cheong_Wa_Dae" title="Cheong Wa Dae">Cheong Wa Dae</a>, the official residence of the <a href="/wiki/President_of_South_Korea" title="President of South Korea">President of South Korea</a>, so the surrounding forest remained undeveloped during Korea's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miracle_on_the_Han" title="Miracle on the Han">miraculous economic growth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samcheonggak&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Samcheonggak" comes from <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Chinese characters</a> meaning "the pavilion of the three purities." The "three purities" (三淸) are pure water (淸水), pure mountains (淸山), and pure humanity (淸人). The name evokes a dwelling place for <i>shinseon</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Taoism" title="Taoism">Taoist</a> hermit with supernatural powers (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>:신선 <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>:神仙).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samcheonggak&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Samcheonggak was first established in 1972 by the <a href="/wiki/Authoritarianism" title="Authoritarianism">authoritarian</a> <a href="/wiki/Park_Chung-hee" title="Park Chung-hee">Park Chung-hee</a> government as a traditional Korean entertainment venue (<a href="/wiki/Kisaeng" title="Kisaeng">Kisaeng</a> house) for high-ranking government officials, especially the meeting between the respective <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a> groups of <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea_National_Red_Cross" title="Republic of Korea National Red Cross">South</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-seoul.go.kr_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-seoul.go.kr-2">[2]</a></sup> After the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Park_Chung-hee_assassination" title="Park Chung-hee assassination">assassination of Park Chung-hee</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kim_Jae-kyu" title="Kim Jae-kyu">Kim Jae-kyu</a> in 1979, Samcheonggak operated as a private restaurant. Samcheonggak was closed due to financial problems in December 1999 and purchased by the <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Metropolitan_Government" title="Seoul Metropolitan Government">Seoul Metropolitan Government</a> in May 2000. The site was opened to the public on October 29, 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-seoul.go.kr_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-seoul.go.kr-2">[2]</a></sup> In August 2005, Samcheonggak was entrusted to Paradise Co., Ltd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-koreaconvention_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-koreaconvention-3">[3]</a></sup> The site contains a performance hall, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_restaurant" title="Korean restaurant">Korean restaurant</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Tea_house" title="Tea house">tea house</a>, and guest accommodations. There are six <a href="/wiki/Hanok" title="Hanok">hanok</a>. Samcheonggak is one of the most famous places in Seoul for traditional performances and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fine_dining" title="Fine dining">fine dining</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-koreaconvention_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-koreaconvention-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
