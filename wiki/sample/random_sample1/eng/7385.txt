<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Sampan</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="hatnote" role="note">For the Chinese/English bilingual newspaper, see <a href="/wiki/Sampan_(newspaper)" title="Sampan (newspaper)">Sampan (newspaper)</a>.</div>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Not to be confused with the commune in France <a href="/wiki/Sampans,_Jura" title="Sampans, Jura">Sampans, Jura</a>.</div>

<p>A <b>sampan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">舢舨</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">shānbǎn</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Pe%CC%8Dh-%C5%8De-j%C4%AB" title="Pe̍h-ōe-jī">Pe̍h-ōe-jī</a>: <i><span lang="nan" xml:lang="nan">sam-pán</span></i>) is a relatively flat bottomed <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> wooden <a href="/wiki/Boat" title="Boat">boat</a>. Some sampans include a small shelter on board, and may be <a href="/wiki/Houseboat" title="Houseboat">used as a permanent habitation</a> on inland waters. Sampans are generally used for transportation in coastal areas or rivers, and are often used as <a href="/wiki/Traditional_fishing_boat" title="Traditional fishing boat">traditional fishing boats</a>. It is unusual for a sampan to sail far from land as they do not have the means to survive rough weather.</p>
<p>The word "sampan" comes from the original <a href="/wiki/Cantonese" title="Cantonese">Cantonese</a> term for the boats, 三板 (<i>sam pan</i>), literally meaning "three planks".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The name referred to the hull design, which consists of a flat bottom (made from one plank) joined to two sides (the other two planks). The design closely resembles Western hard <a href="/wiki/Chine_(boating)" title="Chine (boating)">chine</a> boats like the <a href="/wiki/Scow" title="Scow">scow</a> or <a href="/wiki/Punt_(boat)" title="Punt (boat)">punt</a>.</p>
<p>Sampans may be propelled by poles, oars (particularly a single, long <a href="/wiki/Sculling" title="Sculling">sculling</a> oar called a <i>yuloh</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>) or may be fitted with outboard motors.</p>
<p>Sampans are still in use by rural residents of <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>, particularly <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burma" title="Burma">Burma</a> (<a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">Myanmar</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>.</p>
