<p><b>Samhita</b> (<a href="/wiki/Sanskrit" title="Sanskrit">Sanskrit</a>: संहिता, <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="International Alphabet of Sanskrit Transliteration">saṁhitā</span></i>) literally means "put together, joined, union" and "a methodically, rule-based combination of text or verses".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-monier_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-monier-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <i>Samhita</i> also refers to the most ancient layer of text in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Veda" title="Veda">Vedas</a>, consisting of <a href="/wiki/Mantra" title="Mantra">mantras</a>, hymns, prayers, <a href="/wiki/Litany" title="Litany">litanies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Benediction" title="Benediction">benedictions</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jl_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-jl-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Parts of <i>Vedic Samhitas</i> constitute the oldest living part of <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hindu</a> tradition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jl_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-jl-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samhita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Samhita</i> is a Sanskrit word from the roots, sam (सं) and hita (हित), which mean "correct, proper" and "wholesome, arranged" respectively. The combination word thus means "put together , joined, compose, arrangement, place together, union" and "combination of letters according to euphonic rules, any methodically arranged collection of texts or verses".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-monier_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-monier-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Discussion">Discussion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samhita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Discussion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the most generic context, a <i>Samhita</i> may refer to any methodical collection of text or verses. Any <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sastra" title="Sastra">sastra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sutra" title="Sutra">sutra</a> or Sanskrit Epic, along with Vedic texts, can be called a <i>Samhita</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-monier_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-monier-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Samhita</i>, however, in contemporary literature typically implies the earliest, archaic part of the Vedas. These contain <a href="/wiki/Mantra" title="Mantra">mantras</a> – sacred sounds with or without literal meaning, as well as panegyrics, prayers, <a href="/wiki/Litany" title="Litany">litanies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Benediction" title="Benediction">benedictions</a> petitioning nature or Vedic deities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jl_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-jl-3">[3]</a></sup> Vedic Samhita refer to mathematically precise metrical archaic text of each of the <i><a href="/wiki/Vedas" title="Vedas">Vedas</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Rigveda" title="Rigveda">Rigveda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yajurveda" title="Yajurveda">Yajurveda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Samaveda" title="Samaveda">Samaveda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atharvaveda" title="Atharvaveda">Atharvaveda</a>).</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Vedas" title="Vedas">Vedas</a> have been divided into four styles of texts – the Samhitas (mantras and benedictions), the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aranyakas" title="Aranyakas">Aranyakas</a> (text on rituals, ceremonies, sacrifices and symbolic-sacrifices), the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brahmanas" title="Brahmanas">Brahmanas</a> (commentaries on rituals, ceremonies and sacrifices), and the <a href="/wiki/Upanishads" title="Upanishads">Upanishads</a> (text discussing meditation, philosophy and spiritual knowledge).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The Samhitas are sometimes identified as <i>karma-kanda</i> (कर्म खण्ड, action/ritual-related section), while the Upanishads are identified as <i>jnana-kanda</i> (ज्ञान खण्ड, knowledge/spirituality-related section).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ab_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-ab-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Aranyaka" title="Aranyaka">Aranyakas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brahmana" title="Brahmana">Brahmanas</a> are variously classified, sometimes as the ceremonial <i>karma-kanda</i>, other times (or parts of them) as the <i>jnana-kanda</i>.</p>
<p>The <i>Vedic Samhitas</i> were chanted during ceremonies and rituals, and parts of it remain the oldest living part of <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hindu</a> tradition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jl_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-jl-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>A collective study of Vedas and later text suggests that the compendium of <i>Samhitas</i> and associated Vedic texts were far larger than currently available. However, most have been lost at some point or over a period of Indian history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rig_veda">Rig veda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samhita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Rig veda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gayatri_mantra" title="Gayatri mantra">Gayatri mantra</a> is among the famous Hindu mantras. It is found in Rig Veda Samhita.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mmwpage17_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-mmwpage17-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dd>
<dl>
<dd>ॐ भूर्भुवस्वः। तत्सवितुर्वरेण्यम्। भर्गो देवस्य धीमहि। धियो यो नः प्रचोदयात्</dd>
<dd><i>Oṁ Bhūr Bhuva~Swah', Tat savitur varenyam, Bhargo devasya dhīmahi, Dhiyo yo nah prachodayāt</i></dd>
</dl>
</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>
<dl>
<dd>Let us meditate on that excellent glory of the divine Light (Sun). May he inspire our thoughts, stimulate our understandings.</dd>
<dd>– <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rig_Veda" title="Rig Veda">Rig Veda</a> 3.62.10<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mmwpage17_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-mmwpage17-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
</dd>
</dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sama_veda">Sama veda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samhita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sama veda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Albrecht_Weber" title="Albrecht Weber">Weber</a> noted that the <i>Samhita</i> of <a href="/wiki/Samaveda" title="Samaveda">Samaveda</a> is an anthology taken from the Rigveda-Samhita.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The difference is in the refinement and application of arts such as melody, meters of music and literary composition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fstaal_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-fstaal-13">[13]</a></sup> Thus, the root hymn that later became the <i>Rathantara</i> (Excellent Chariot) mantra chant is found in both Rigveda and Samaveda Samhitas, as follows,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fstaal_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-fstaal-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dd>
<dl>
<dd>Rigveda form: <i>Abhi tva sura nonumo 'dugdha iva dhenavah | isanam asya jagatah svardrsam isanam indra tasthusah</i></dd>
<dd>Samaveda form: <i>obhitvasuranonumova | adugdha iva dhenava isanamasya jagatassuvardrsam | isanama indra | ta sthu sa o va ha u va | as ||</i></dd>
</dl>
</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>
<dl>
<dd>Translation (same for both):<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fstaal_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-fstaal-13">[13]</a></sup></dd>
<dd>We cry out for you, hero, like unmilked cows to the lord of the living world !</dd>
<dd>To the lord of the unmoving world who eye is the sun, O <a href="/wiki/Indra" title="Indra">Indra</a> !</dd>
</dl>
</dd>
</dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Yajur_veda">Yajur veda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samhita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Yajur veda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The hymns in Section 4.1.5 of the Yajurveda <i>Samhita</i>, dedicated to several ancient deities, state:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpa_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-hpa-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edward_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-edward-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>May the <a href="/wiki/Vasu" title="Vasu">Vasus</a> prepare you, with the <a href="/wiki/Gayatri" title="Gayatri">gayatri</a> meter, you are the earth,<br/>
May the <a href="/wiki/Rudra" title="Rudra">Rudras</a> prepare you, with the <a href="/wiki/Tristubh" title="Tristubh">tristubh</a> meter, you are the sky.<br/>
May the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aditya" title="Aditya">Adityas</a> prepare you, with the jagati meter, you are the heaven.<br/>
May the <a href="/wiki/Visvedevas" title="Visvedevas">Visvedevas</a>, common to all men, prepare you, with the <a href="/wiki/Anu%E1%B9%A3%E1%B9%ADubh" title="Anuṣṭubh">anustubh</a> meter, you are the directions.<br/>
You are the unchanging direction, make unchanging in me children, abundance of wealth, abundance of cattle, abundance of heroism.</p>
<div class="templatequotecite"><cite>— <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taittiriya" title="Taittiriya">Taittiriya</a> Samhita, 4.1.5<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpa_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-hpa-14">[14]</a></sup></cite></div>
</blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atharva_veda">Atharva veda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samhita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Atharva veda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A hymn in the Atharva Veda Samhita, for example, is a woman's petition to deity <a href="/wiki/Agni" title="Agni">Agni</a>, to attract suitors and a good husband.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rp_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-rp-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>May O Agni!, a suitor after this girl's heart come to her,<br/>
May he come to this maiden with fortune!<br/>
May she be agreeable to suitors, charming at festivals, promptly obtain happiness through a husband!</p>
<div class="templatequotecite"><cite>— <a href="/wiki/Atharvaveda" title="Atharvaveda">Atharva Veda</a>, 2.36<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rp_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-rp-17">[17]</a></sup></cite></div>
</blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-Vedic_Samhitas">Post-Vedic Samhitas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samhita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Post-Vedic Samhitas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are many well known books written in the post-vedic period, also known as Samhitas or Sanhitas, because the word “Samhita” also means “systematic compilation of knowledge”. Vedic samhitas should not be confused with these samhitas of post-vedic period.</p>
<p>Some post-vedic Samhitas are: <a href="/wiki/Ashtavakra_Gita" title="Ashtavakra Gita">Ashtavakra Gita</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bhrigu_Samhita" title="Bhrigu Samhita">Bhrigu Samhita</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brahma_Samhita" title="Brahma Samhita">Brahma Samhita</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charaka_Samhita" title="Charaka Samhita">Charaka Samhita</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deva_Samhita" title="Deva Samhita">Deva Samhita</a>, <a href="/wiki/Garga" title="Garga">Garga Samhita</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gheranda_Samhita" title="Gheranda Samhita">Gheranda Samhita</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kashyap_Samhita" title="Kashyap Samhita">Kashyap Samhita</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shiva_Samhita" title="Shiva Samhita">Shiva Samhita</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sushruta_Samhita" title="Sushruta Samhita">Sushruta Samhita</a> (a treatise on food and medicine), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yogayajnavalkya_Samhita" title="Yogayajnavalkya Samhita">Yogayajnavalkya Samhita</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
