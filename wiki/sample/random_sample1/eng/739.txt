<p><b><a href="/wiki/Aitken_Double_Star_Catalogue" title="Aitken Double Star Catalogue">ADS</a> 16402</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Binary_star" title="Binary star">binary</a> <a href="/wiki/Star_system" title="Star system">star system</a>, composed of two sun-like <a href="/wiki/Star" title="Star">stars</a> located 450 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light-years" title="Light-years">light-years</a> away in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lacerta_(constellation)" title="Lacerta (constellation)">Lacerta</a>. It was first identified by <a href="/wiki/John_Herschel" title="John Herschel">John Herschel</a> in 1831.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bakos2007_4-13"><a href="#cite_note-Bakos2007-4">[4]</a></sup> The two stars are separated by 1500 <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_unit" title="Astronomical unit">AUs</a>. The star system is estimated to be 1.9 ± 0.6 billion years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bonfanti2015_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Bonfanti2015-6">[6]</a></sup> The secondary star ADS 16402 B is also designated HAT-P-1.</p>

<p>On September 14, 2006 the <a href="/wiki/HATNet_Project" title="HATNet Project">HATNet Project</a> announced their first extrasolar planet discovery <a href="/wiki/HAT-P-1b" title="HAT-P-1b">HAT-P-1b</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_jupiter" title="Hot jupiter">hot jupiter</a> type <a href="/wiki/Gas_giant" title="Gas giant">gas giant</a> in orbit around the secondary star ADS 16402B. Following the designation scheme used by the HATNet Project, the secondary star is known as HAT-P-1, and the planet itself designated HAT-P-1b.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bakos2007_4-14"><a href="#cite_note-Bakos2007-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cfa_press_release_2006-24_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-cfa_press_release_2006-24-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
