<p>The <b>Tōhō Line</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">東豊線</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tōhō-sen</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Rubber-tyred_metro" title="Rubber-tyred metro">rubber-tyred metro</a> line in <a href="/wiki/Sapporo" title="Sapporo">Sapporo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hokkaido" title="Hokkaido">Hokkaido</a>, Japan, operated by <a href="/wiki/Sapporo_City_Transportation_Bureau" title="Sapporo City Transportation Bureau">Sapporo City Transportation Bureau</a>. It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Sapporo_Municipal_Subway" title="Sapporo Municipal Subway">Sapporo Municipal Subway</a> system. It runs from <a href="/wiki/Sakaemachi_Station_(Hokkaido)" title="Sakaemachi Station (Hokkaido)">Sakaemachi Station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Higashi-ku,_Sapporo" title="Higashi-ku, Sapporo">Higashi-ku</a> to <a href="/wiki/Fukuzumi_Station" title="Fukuzumi Station">Fukuzumi Station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Toyohira-ku,_Sapporo" title="Toyohira-ku, Sapporo">Toyohira-ku</a>. The Tōhō Line color on maps is sky blue. Its stations carry the letter "H" followed by a number.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Former">Former</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=T%C5%8Dh%C5%8D_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Former">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>7000 series 4-car EMUs (from 1988 until 2016)</li>
</ul>
<p>The line is operated using a fleet of 20 four-car 7000 series EMUs (sets 7101 to 7120) <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jrrprivate2014_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-jrrprivate2014-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first of a fleet of 20 new 9000 series four-car EMUs was introduced on the Tōhō Line in May 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railfan20150509_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-railfan20150509-2">[2]</a></sup> Built by <a href="/wiki/Kawasaki_Heavy_Industries" title="Kawasaki Heavy Industries">Kawasaki Heavy Industries</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kobe" title="Kobe">Kobe</a>, the first trainset was unveiled to the media in November 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mynavi20141104_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-mynavi20141104-3">[3]</a></sup> All 20 trains are scheduled to be in service by fiscal 2016, replacing the fleet of 7000 series trains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railfan20150509_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-railfan20150509-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
