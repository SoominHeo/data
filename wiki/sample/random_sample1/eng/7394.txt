<p><b>Sanguié</b> is one of the 45 <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Burkina_Faso" title="Provinces of Burkina Faso">provinces</a> of <a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina Faso">Burkina Faso</a>, located in its <a href="/wiki/Centre-Ouest_Region" title="Centre-Ouest Region">Centre-Ouest Region</a>. In 2006 the population was 297,230.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-c2006_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-c2006-1">[1]</a></sup> Its capital is <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9o" title="Réo">Réo</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ly%C3%A9l%C3%A9_language" title="Lyélé language">Lyele</a> is a major first language in this province.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sangui%C3%A9_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2011 the province had 218 primary schools and 27 secondary schools.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Healthcare">Healthcare</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sangui%C3%A9_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Healthcare">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2011 the province had 34 health and social promotion centers (<i>Centres de santé et de promotion sociale</i>), 3 doctors and 66 nurses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sangui%C3%A9_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of June 2014 <a href="/wiki/Rail_transport_in_Burkina_Faso" title="Rail transport in Burkina Faso"><i>Sitarail</i></a> operates a passenger train three times a week along the route from Ouagadougou to Abidjan which passes through the province and stops at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Zamo,_Burkina_Faso&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Zamo, Burkina Faso (page does not exist)">Zamo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
