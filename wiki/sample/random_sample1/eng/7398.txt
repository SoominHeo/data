<p>An <b>amphibious readiness group</b> (<b>ARG</b>) of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> consists of a naval element—a group of <a href="/wiki/Warship" title="Warship">warships</a> known as an <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Amphibious_task_force&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Amphibious task force (page does not exist)">amphibious task force</a> (ATF)—and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landing_force" title="Landing force">landing force</a> (LF) of <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">U.S. Marines</a> (and occasionally <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">U.S. Army</a> soldiers), in total about 5,000 people. Together, these elements and supporting units are trained, organized, and equipped to perform <a href="/wiki/Amphibious_warfare" title="Amphibious warfare">amphibious operations</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amphibious_ready_group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A typical U.S. Amphibious Readiness Group consists of:</p>
<ul>
<li>One <a href="/wiki/Amphibious_assault_ship" title="Amphibious assault ship">amphibious assault ship</a> (<a href="/wiki/Landing_Helicopter_Assault" title="Landing Helicopter Assault">LHA</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landing_Helicopter_Dock" title="Landing Helicopter Dock">LHD</a>): the primary landing ship, resembling a small <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_carrier" title="Aircraft carrier">aircraft carrier</a>, designed to transport troops into the war zone by air using transport helicopters. In a secondary role, these ships perform sea control and limited <a href="/wiki/Power_projection" title="Power projection">power projection</a> missions using <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AV-8B_Harrier_II" title="AV-8B Harrier II">AV-8B Harrier II</a> Marine aircraft and Navy airborne assets including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MH-60S_Seahawk" title="MH-60S Seahawk">MH-60S Seahawk</a>.</li>
<li>One <a href="/wiki/Amphibious_transport_dock" title="Amphibious transport dock">amphibious transport dock</a> ship (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landing_Platform_Dock" title="Landing Platform Dock">LPD</a>): a warship that transports troops into the war zone by sea, primarily using conventional <a href="/wiki/Landing_craft" title="Landing craft">landing craft</a> and <a href="/wiki/Landing_Craft_Air_Cushion" title="Landing Craft Air Cushion">Landing Craft Air Cushion</a> <a href="/wiki/Hovercraft" title="Hovercraft">hovercraft</a> (LCAC), although they also have the capability of operating helicopters from their flight decks as well.</li>
<li>One <a href="/wiki/Dock_landing_ship" title="Dock landing ship">dock landing ship</a> (LSD): a warship supporting amphibious operations including landings onto hostile shores via <a href="/wiki/Landing_Craft_Air_Cushion" title="Landing Craft Air Cushion">Landing Craft Air Cushion</a> (LCAC), conventional landing craft, and helicopters. There are currently two classes of LSDs in service: the <a href="/wiki/Harpers_Ferry-class_dock_landing_ship" title="Harpers Ferry-class dock landing ship"><i>Harpers Ferry</i> class</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Whidbey_Island-class_dock_landing_ship" title="Whidbey Island-class dock landing ship"><i>Whidbey Island</i> class</a>.</li>
<li>A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marine_Expeditionary_Unit" title="Marine Expeditionary Unit">Marine Expeditionary Unit</a> (MEU): the smallest configuration of a <a href="/wiki/Marine_Air-Ground_Task_Force" title="Marine Air-Ground Task Force">Marine Air-Ground Task Force</a> that is deployed from an <a href="/wiki/Amphibious_assault_ship" title="Amphibious assault ship">amphibious assault ship</a>. Each MEU includes: a <a href="/wiki/Ground_combat_element" title="Ground combat element">ground combat element</a> of a Marine <a href="/wiki/Infantry" title="Infantry">infantry</a> <a href="/wiki/Battalion" title="Battalion">battalion</a> reinforced with <a href="/wiki/M1_Abrams" title="M1 Abrams">M1 Abrams</a> <a href="/wiki/Tank" title="Tank">tanks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Artillery" title="Artillery">artillery</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Combat_engineers" title="Combat engineers">combat engineers</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amphibious_vehicles" title="Amphibious vehicles">amphibious vehicles</a>, <a href="/wiki/LAV-25" title="LAV-25">light armored vehicles</a>, and other ground combat assets; an <a href="/wiki/Aviation_combat_element" title="Aviation combat element">aviation combat element</a> composed of a composite squadron of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rotary-wing_aircraft" title="Rotary-wing aircraft">rotary-wing aircraft</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AV-8B_Harrier_II" title="AV-8B Harrier II">AV-8B Harrier II</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ground_attack" title="Ground attack">ground attack</a> and <a href="/wiki/Close_air_support" title="Close air support">close air support</a> <a href="/wiki/Jet_aircraft" title="Jet aircraft">jets</a>, and an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air_Traffic_Control" title="Air Traffic Control">Air Traffic Control</a> and command and control detachment; a battalion-sized <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Logistics_combat_element" title="Logistics combat element">logistics combat element</a>, and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Command_element" title="Command element">command element</a>. Each Marine Expeditionary Unit (MEU) typically consists of about 2,200 Marines and is usually commanded by a <a href="/wiki/Colonel_(United_States)" title="Colonel (United States)">colonel</a>. The makeup of the MEU can be customized as situations require; additional artillery, armor, or air units can be attached, including squadrons of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F/A-18_Hornet" title="F/A-18 Hornet">F/A-18 Hornet</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Multirole_jet_fighter" title="Multirole jet fighter">multirole jet fighters</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AV-8B_Harrier_II" title="AV-8B Harrier II">AV-8B Harrier IIs</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ground-attack_aircraft" title="Ground-attack aircraft">ground-attack aircraft</a> designed to attack and destroy surface targets.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CH-53E_Super_Stallion" title="CH-53E Super Stallion">CH-53E Super Stallions</a>: <a href="/wiki/Military_transport_aircraft#Transport_helicopters" title="Military transport aircraft">heavy-lift helicopters</a> designed to transport personnel, supplies and equipment in support of amphibious and shore operations.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AH-1_SuperCobra" title="AH-1 SuperCobra">AH-1W SuperCobras</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AH-1Z_Viper" title="AH-1Z Viper">AH-1Z Vipers</a>: <a href="/wiki/Attack_helicopter" title="Attack helicopter">attack helicopters</a> providing <a href="/wiki/Fire_support" title="Fire support">fire support</a> and fire support coordination to the landing force during amphibious assaults and subsequent operations ashore. The newer, more capable AH-1Z is replacing the older AH-1Ws.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UH-1Y_Venom" title="UH-1Y Venom">UH-1Y Venom</a>: Provides command and control during heliborne operations as well a light attack and assault capabilities.</li>
<li>USMC MV-22B squadrons are designated as <a href="/wiki/List_of_active_United_States_Marine_Corps_aircraft_squadrons#Marine_Medium_Tiltrotor_Squadrons" title="List of active United States Marine Corps aircraft squadrons">VMM</a>, or Marine Medium Tiltrotor Squadron, and CH-53E squadrons as <a href="/wiki/List_of_active_United_States_Marine_Corps_aircraft_squadrons#Marine_Heavy_Helicopter_Squadrons" title="List of active United States Marine Corps aircraft squadrons">HMM</a>, or Marine Heavy Helicopter Squadron. When assigned to a MEU, the detachments of the various other squadrons are combined with either the MV-22 or CH-53 squadron to create a reinforced, composite squadron. The reinforced squadron is designated as VMM-XXX(REIN) for MV-22s or HMH-XXX(REIN) for CH-53s, where the Xs are the squadron's number. As such, the various aircraft will don the tailcodes and markers of the VMM or HMH squadron, though will usually keep their own squadron tail art.</li>
</ul>
<p>The resulting forces may range from a single Amphibious Ready Group/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marine_Expeditionary_Unit" title="Marine Expeditionary Unit">Marine Expeditionary Unit</a> (<a href="/wiki/Special_Operations_Capable" title="Special Operations Capable">Special Operations Capable</a>) [ARG/MEU (SOC)], to a larger organization capable of employing a <a href="/wiki/Marine_Expeditionary_Brigade" title="Marine Expeditionary Brigade">Marine Expeditionary Brigade</a> (MEB) or even a <a href="/wiki/Marine_Expeditionary_Force" title="Marine Expeditionary Force">Marine Expeditionary Force</a> (MEF).</p>
<p>Amphibious forces must be capable of performing missions ranging from humanitarian assistance and disaster relief to major theater war (MTW). Additionally, they can be configured and deployed to operate at various levels of conflict and in multiple theaters simultaneously. They can provide a presence that may preclude adventurous actions by a potential belligerent.</p>
<p>Because they are sea-based and because the decision to position and engage amphibious forces will always be easily reversible, amphibious forces greatly expand the repertoire of available response options. Among other national resources, they are particularly well placed to provide a demonstration of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>'s commitment and resolve to friends and allies as well as adversaries.</p>
<p>Normally two to three ARGs are forward deployed: one in the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>/<a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a>–<a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> area, and one or two in the western <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> area. The other ships of the ARG are either working up to deploy, in transit, or in overhaul. One ARG/MEU, known as <a href="/wiki/Task_Force_76" title="Task Force 76">Task Force 76</a>/Expeditionary Strike Group 7, is forward based in <a href="/wiki/United_States_Fleet_Activities_Sasebo" title="United States Fleet Activities Sasebo">Sasebo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marine_Corps_Base_Camp_Smedley_D._Butler" title="Marine Corps Base Camp Smedley D. Butler">Okinawa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>In most cases, the ATF will be deployed under the protective umbrella of a <a href="/wiki/Carrier_battle_group#The_U.S._Navy_carrier_strike_group" title="Carrier battle group">carrier strike group</a> (CSG), which provides cover for the ATF and combat support to operations ashore. Ships of the ATF are capable of embarking and supporting other forces when the mission requires, including the United States Army, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Special_Operations_Command" title="United States Special Operations Command">Special Operations Forces</a> (SOF), or other joint and combined forces.</p>
