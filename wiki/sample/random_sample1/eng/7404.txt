<p><b>Sangwŏn County</b> is a county of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Hwanghae" title="North Hwanghae">North Hwanghae</a>, formerly one of the four suburban counties located in east <a href="/wiki/Pyongyang" title="Pyongyang">P'yŏngyang</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>. Prior to 1952, Sangwŏn was merely a township of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chunghwa,_North_Korea" title="Chunghwa, North Korea">Chunghwa</a> County. In 1952 it was separated as a separate county, and in 1963 it was added as a county of Pyongyang. It is north of North Hwanghae's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hwangju" title="Hwangju">Hwangju</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yontan" title="Yontan">Yŏntan</a>, and Suan counties, south of the Nam River and Kangdong county west of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yonsan" title="Yonsan">Yŏnsan</a> county, and east of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chunghwa_county&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chunghwa county (page does not exist)">Chunghwa county</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 2010, it was administratively reassigned from P'yŏngyang to North Hwanghae; foreign media attributed the change as an attempt to relieve shortages in P'yŏngyang's food distribution system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_Divisions">Administrative Divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sangwon_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Administrative Divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The county is divided into one town (ŭp), one worker's district (rodongjagu) and 20 'ri' (villages).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
