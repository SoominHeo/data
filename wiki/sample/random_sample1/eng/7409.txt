<p><b>Saint Petersburg Governorate</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Санкт-Петербу́ргская губе́рния</span>, <i>Sankt-Peterburgskaya guberniya</i>), or <b>Government of Saint Petersburg</b>, was an administrative division (a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guberniya" title="Guberniya">guberniya</a></i>) of the <a href="/wiki/Tsardom_of_Russia" title="Tsardom of Russia">Tsardom of Russia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_SFSR" title="Russian SFSR">Russian SFSR</a>, which existed in 1708–1927.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Establishment">Establishment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Petersburg_Governorate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Establishment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:377px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sw_BalticProv_en.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="829" data-file-width="922" height="337" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Sw_BalticProv_en.png/375px-Sw_BalticProv_en.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Sw_BalticProv_en.png/563px-Sw_BalticProv_en.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Sw_BalticProv_en.png/750px-Sw_BalticProv_en.png 2x" width="375"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sw_BalticProv_en.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Territory of the Swedish realms as well as Ingria in 17th century</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The governorate, together with seven other governorates, was established by <a href="/wiki/Tsar" title="Tsar">Tsar</a> <a href="/wiki/Peter_the_Great" title="Peter the Great">Peter the Great</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Ukase" title="Ukase">edict</a> as <b>Ingermanland Governorate</b> (<span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Ингерманла́ндская губе́рния</span>, <i>Ingermanlandskaya guberniya</i>) on December 29 [<a href="/wiki/Adoption_of_the_Gregorian_calendar" title="Adoption of the Gregorian calendar">O.S.</a> December 18], 1708<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1708List_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-1708List-1">[1]</a></sup> on the territories conquered from the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Empire" title="Swedish Empire">Swedish Empire</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Northern_War" title="Great Northern War">Great Northern War</a>. As with the rest of the governorates, neither the borders nor internal subdivisions of Ingermanland Governorate were defined; instead, the territory was defined as a set of cities and the lands adjacent to those cities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tarkhov_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tarkhov-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>By another edict on June 3, 1710, the governorate was renamed St. Petersburg Governorate after the newly founded city of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Saint Petersburg</a>, and in 1721 the former <a href="/wiki/Dominions_of_Sweden" title="Dominions of Sweden">Swedish</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Ingria" title="Duchy of Ingria">Duchy of Ingria</a>, and parts of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/County_of_Kexholm" title="County of Kexholm">County of Kexholm</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/County_of_Viborg_and_Nyslott" title="County of Viborg and Nyslott">County of Viborg and Nyslott</a> were formally ceded to Russia in the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Nystad" title="Treaty of Nystad">Treaty of Nystad</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_%C3%85bo" title="Treaty of Åbo">Treaty of Åbo</a> in 1743, the parts of Kexholm and Viborg were joined with new territorial gains from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sweden_(1721%E2%80%931809)" title="Kingdom of Sweden (1721–1809)">Sweden</a> into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Governorate_of_Vyborg" title="Governorate of Vyborg">Governorate of Vyborg</a> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Выборгская губерния</span>).</p>
<p>From August 18, 1914 to January 26, 1924 it was named <b>Petrograd Governorate</b>, and in 1924-1927 — <b>Leningrad Governorate</b>. It was abolished on August 1, 1927 when modern <a href="/wiki/Leningrad_Oblast" title="Leningrad Oblast">Leningrad Oblast</a> was created.</p>
