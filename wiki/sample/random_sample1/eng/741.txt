<p>The <b>AEK-971</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> / <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> selective fire <a href="/wiki/Assault_rifle" title="Assault rifle">assault rifle</a> that was developed at the <a href="/wiki/Kovrov" title="Kovrov">Kovrov</a> Mechanical Plant (KMZ) by chief designer <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Koksharov" title="Sergey Koksharov">Sergey Koksharov</a> in the late 1970s and 1980s. The primary model, the AEK-971, uses the <a href="/wiki/5.45%C3%9739mm" title="5.45×39mm">5.45×39mm</a> round fed from standard 30-round or larger box magazines used by the <a href="/wiki/AK-74" title="AK-74">AK-74</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AK-74M" title="AK-74M">AK-74M</a>, and similar weapons.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AEK-971&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The AEK-971 was developed to participate in a competition announced by the Ministry of Defense of the USSR, during which preference was given to the <a href="/wiki/AN-94" title="AN-94">AN-94</a>. The initial AEK version differs from modern samples, as many innovations were perceived as unnecessary by the Ministry of Defence, which led to a simplification of the early model. The AEK-971 is approximately 0.5 kg (1.1 lb) lighter than the AN-94, simpler in design and cheaper to manufacture.</p>
<p>The AEK-971 assault rifle has been combat proven in Chechnya tested by <a href="/wiki/Naval_Infantry_(Russia)" title="Naval Infantry (Russia)">naval infantry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Internal_Troops_of_Russia" title="Internal Troops of Russia">interior forces</a>, and prepared for mass production. Only a few small batches of this assault rifle have been produced so far and adopted for service with units of the Ministry of Justice of the Russian Federation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gunsru.ru_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-gunsru.ru-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-all4shooters.com_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-all4shooters.com-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Though losing an initial contract for production against the AN-94 as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Project_Abakan" title="Project Abakan">Project Abakan</a> assault rifle selection trials held from 1980 to 1994 in Russia, the Russian Army begun field trials of this weapon. The AN-94 assault rifle, which was officially adopted by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_army" title="Russian army">Russian army</a>, has a slight accuracy edge over the AEK-971 in 2-round-burst mode. In full automatic mode or during longer bursts (3–10 rounds per burst) the AEK-971 is more accurate. In late 2014, an updated variant of the rifle passed state trials and will be tested operationally with Russian forces in early 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thefirearmblog23dec14_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-thefirearmblog23dec14-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design_details">Design details</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AEK-971&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design details">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:AEK971IRL.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="124" data-file-width="400" height="68" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/AEK971IRL.jpg/220px-AEK971IRL.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/AEK971IRL.jpg/330px-AEK971IRL.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e2/AEK971IRL.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:AEK971IRL.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
An early model of the AEK-971.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The AEK-971 is based on previous AK rifles in internal design and layout, but features a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Balanced_Automatics_Recoil_System" title="Balanced Automatics Recoil System">Balanced Automatics Recoil System</a> (BARS) that enhances the traditional <a href="/wiki/AK-47" title="AK-47">Kalashnikov</a> <a href="/wiki/Gas-operated_reloading#Long-stroke_piston" title="Gas-operated reloading">long stroke gas piston</a> operating system by reducing the negative effects of <a href="/wiki/Free_recoil" title="Free recoil">recoil</a>. Balanced recoil systems were previously applied in the <a href="/wiki/AO-38_assault_rifle" title="AO-38 assault rifle">AO-38</a> and <a href="/wiki/AL-7" title="AL-7">AL-7</a> experimental assault rifles and in the <a href="/wiki/AK-107" title="AK-107">AK-107</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AK-108" title="AK-108">AK-108</a>. BARS works by shifting mass toward the <a href="/wiki/Muzzle_(firearms)" title="Muzzle (firearms)">muzzle</a> of the rifle as the bolt and bolt carrier recoil rearward by way of a counter-weight that negates the impulse of the gas piston and bolt carrier, resulting in more controllable automatic fire. For the AEK-971 automatic firing accuracy is improved by 15-20% in comparison with the AK-74M.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.D0.AD.D0.BD.D1.86.D0.B8.D0.BA.D0.BB.D0.BE.D0.BF.D0.B5.D0.B4.D0.B8.D1.8F_.D0.9E.D1.80.D1.83.D0.B6.D0.B8.D1.8F_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-.D0.AD.D0.BD.D1.86.D0.B8.D0.BA.D0.BB.D0.BE.D0.BF.D0.B5.D0.B4.D0.B8.D1.8F_.D0.9E.D1.80.D1.83.D0.B6.D0.B8.D1.8F-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The travel distance of the AEK-971 reciprocating parts is less than in non balanced recoil AK-pattern designs increasing the cyclic rate of fire significantly. The original automatic fire rate of the early model was 1,500 rounds/min and was decreased to 900 rounds/min for the AEK-971.</p>
<p>The AEK-971 uses an adjustable notched rear tangent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iron_sight" title="Iron sight">iron sight</a> calibrated in 100 m (109 yd) increments from 100 to 1,000 m (109 to 1,094 yd). The front sight is a hooded front post. Each AEK rifle is fitted with a Warsaw Pact side-rail bracket for mounting optics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gunsru.ru_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-gunsru.ru-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Models_and_variants">Models and variants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AEK-971&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Models and variants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are three different models: the <b>AEK-971</b>, <b>AEK-972</b> and <b>AEK-973</b>. The different variants are most easily recognized by their respective magazine curvatures. The AEK-972 is chambered in <a href="/wiki/5.56%C3%9745mm_NATO" title="5.56×45mm NATO">5.56×45mm NATO</a> and the AEK-973 in <a href="/wiki/7.62%C3%9739mm" title="7.62×39mm">7.62×39mm</a>. The AEK-972 uses the magazines of the 5.56×45mm NATO chambered arms of the <a href="/wiki/AK-100_(Rifle_family)" title="AK-100 (Rifle family)">AK-10x series</a> and the AEK-973 all of the standard 7.62×39mm AK/<a href="/wiki/RPK" title="RPK">RPK</a> magazines.</p>
<p>The AEK-971 and the AEK-973 have one variant each: <b>AEK-971S</b> and <b>AEK-973S</b>. These are improved variants with a new trigger mechanism with the location of the thumb safety-selector lever on the right side, receiving additionally a three-shot burst fire mode, and an extended padded retractable lightweight stock. When the stock is retracted, the shoulder rest connects with the pistol grip forming a streamlined structure which allows the weapon to be used without hampering fire capabilities. Burst fire accuracy of the 5.45×39mm AEK-971S and 7.62×39mm AEK-973S is two times higher than that of the 5.45×39mm AK-74M and 7.62×39mm <a href="/wiki/AKM" title="AKM">AKM</a> assault rifles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gunsru.ru_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-gunsru.ru-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="A-545">A-545</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AEK-971&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: A-545">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>On 23 December 2014, the Russian Army announced that a successor variant of the AEK-971, line designated the <b>A-545</b> (official <a href="/wiki/GRAU#Designation_scheme" title="GRAU">GRAU designation</a> <b>6P67</b>), as well as the <a href="/wiki/AK-12" title="AK-12">AK-12</a>, had passed state <a href="/wiki/Ratnik_(program)" title="Ratnik (program)">Ratnik</a> trials and would be accepted into service with operational units for evaluation. It is expected that both weapons will begin being trialed operationally with the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Armed_Forces" title="Russian Armed Forces">Russian Armed Forces</a> by March 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thefirearmblog23dec14_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-thefirearmblog23dec14-4">[4]</a></sup> The 5.45×39mm A-545 features iron sights that consist of a rotary rear drum aperture and a hooded front post creating a significantly longer sight line and ambidextrous fire mode selection controls. On the A-545, the Warsaw Pact side-rail bracket is omitted and replaced by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Picatinny_Rail" title="Picatinny Rail">Picatinny Rail</a> on a redesigned receiver top for mounting auxiliary equipment, such as aiming optics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-all4shooters.com_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-all4shooters.com-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="A-762">A-762</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AEK-971&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: A-762">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A successor variant of the AEK-973 line designated the <b>A-762</b> (official <a href="/wiki/GRAU#Designation_scheme" title="GRAU">GRAU designation</a> <b>6P68</b>) which beside its 7.62×39mm chambering is technically similar to the A-545 was also developed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
