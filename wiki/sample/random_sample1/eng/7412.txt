<p>The <b>São Paulo Metrô</b> (<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt"><i>Metropolitano de São Paulo</i></span>), commonly called the <i><b>Metrô</b></i>, is the main <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">rapid transit</a> system in the city of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a> and the largest in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. It is also the second largest system in South America and the third largest in Latin America, behind the <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City_Metro" title="Mexico City Metro">Mexico City Metro</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Santiago_Metro" title="Santiago Metro">Santiago Metro</a>. The five main lines in the metro system (Lines 1, 2, 3, 4 &amp; 5) operate on 78.4 kilometres (48.7 mi) of route, serving 65 stations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spmetro_about_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-spmetro_about-1">[1]</a></sup> A sixth line, Line 15, is a monorail line that partially opened for service in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spmetro_about_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-spmetro_about-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2014, the four lines operated by CMSP (Lines 1, 2, 3 &amp; 5) achieved an average weekday ridership of 3.09 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2014ridership_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-2014ridership-2">[2]</a></sup> and provided 895.6 million rides over the course of 2014;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2014ridership_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-2014ridership-2">[2]</a></sup> the entire Metro system served 1,098 million passengers when Line 4 is included with the other four lines.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The Companhia do Metropolitano de São Paulo (Metrô) was founded on April 24, 1968.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spmetro_about_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-spmetro_about-1">[1]</a></sup> Eight months later, work on North-South line was initiated. In 1972, the first test train trip occurred between <a href="/wiki/Jabaquara_(S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro)" title="Jabaquara (São Paulo Metro)">Jabaquara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sa%C3%BAde_(S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro)" title="Saúde (São Paulo Metro)">Saúde</a> stations. In 1974, the segment between Jabaquara and <a href="/wiki/Vila_Mariana_(S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro)" title="Vila Mariana (São Paulo Metro)">Vila Mariana</a> entered into commercial operation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spmetro_about_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-spmetro_about-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The system is interlinked with CPTM (<a href="/wiki/Companhia_Paulista_de_Trens_Metropolitanos" title="Companhia Paulista de Trens Metropolitanos">São Paulo Metropolitan Trains Company</a>) at <a href="/wiki/Br%C3%A1s_(S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro)" title="Brás (São Paulo Metro)">Brás</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palmeiras-Barra_Funda_(S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro)" title="Palmeiras-Barra Funda (São Paulo Metro)">Palmeiras-Barra Funda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tatuap%C3%A9_(S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro)" title="Tatuapé (São Paulo Metro)">Tatuapé</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corinthians-Itaquera_(S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro)" title="Corinthians-Itaquera (São Paulo Metro)">Corinthians-Itaquera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tamanduate%C3%AD_(S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro)" title="Tamanduateí (São Paulo Metro)">Tamanduateí</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pinheiros_(S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro)" title="Pinheiros (São Paulo Metro)">Pinheiros</a> and <a href="/wiki/Santo_Amaro_(S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro)" title="Santo Amaro (São Paulo Metro)">Santo Amaro</a> stations, and at other modal transportation terminals in the city of São Paulo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The São Paulo Metro was voted Best Metro Americas at the MetroRail 2010 industry conference.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<p>The metro system consists of five color-coded lines: <a href="/wiki/Line_1_(S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro)" title="Line 1 (São Paulo Metro)">Line 1 (Blue)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Line_2_(S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro)" title="Line 2 (São Paulo Metro)">Line 2 (Green)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Line_3_(S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro)" title="Line 3 (São Paulo Metro)">Line 3 (Red)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Line_4_(S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro)" title="Line 4 (São Paulo Metro)">Line 4 (Yellow)</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Line_5_(S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro)" title="Line 5 (São Paulo Metro)">Line 5 (Lilac)</a>, all of them operating from Sunday to Saturday, from 4:40 AM to midnight (1:00 AM on Saturdays). The metro system carries 4,500,000 passengers a day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spmetro_about_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-spmetro_about-1">[1]</a></sup> A sixth line, <a href="/wiki/Line_15_(S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro)" title="Line 15 (São Paulo Metro)">Line 15 (Silver)</a>, is a monorail, a section of which (the rest being currently under construction) is presently open for service.</p>
<p>Metro itself is far from covering the entire urban area in the city of São Paulo and only runs within the city limits. Another company, <a href="/wiki/Companhia_Paulista_de_Trens_Metropolitanos" title="Companhia Paulista de Trens Metropolitanos">Companhia Paulista de Trens Metropolitanos</a> (CPTM), serves 22 municipalities that make up the <a href="/wiki/Greater_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="Greater São Paulo">São Paulo Metropolitan Region</a> with commuter lines, which total six lines (7, 8, 9, 10, 11 and 12), are a total of 258.4-kilometre (160.6 mi) in length, serve 92 stations, and carry 2,900,000 passengers a day. Metro and CPTM are integrated through various stations. Metro and CPTM both operate as State-owned companies, and have received awards in the recent past as one of the cleanest systems in the world by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ISO9001" title="ISO9001">ISO9001</a>. The times between the trains both in Metro and CPTM are about one-two minutes in the high traffic times, and three-five minutes in the low traffic periods. The CPTM differs from Metro because it serves other municipalities around São Paulo and also cargo trains, and because of the considerably larger distance between stations (except for the Line 9, which has almost no differences to the Metro lines).</p>
<p>The first line, Norte/Sul (North/South), later renamed "Blue Line" or Line 1 - Blue, was opened on September 18, 1972, with an experimental operation between Saúde and Jabaquara stations. Commercial operations started on September 14, 1974, after an eight-year "gestation" period that began in 1966, under Mayor <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Vicente_Faria_Lima" title="José Vicente Faria Lima">Faria Lima</a>'s administration. Expansion of the metro system includes new lines. As of late 2004, construction began on a US$1 billion, 12.8 km (7.9 mi) all-underground line (Line 4 - Yellow), with eleven stations, aimed at transporting almost one million people per day. By 2004, Line 2 was also being expanded, with two new stations open in 2006 and another one in 2007.</p>
<p>An 10.5-kilometre (6.5 mi) expansion of Line 5 is currently under construction. Plans also include updating the CPTM suburban rail system, which will add several million passengers capacity into the system. It is expected that the São Paulo Metro and CPTM systems will carry about 9.3 million people on average week days by 2018, as opposed to today's 7.5 million (Metro: 4.6 million; CPTM: 2.9 million as of 2014). Metro stations operate from 4:40 AM to around 12:00 AM. As of January 2015<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, tickets cost R$3.50. In 2006, the São Paulo Metro system has started to use a <a href="/wiki/Smart_card" title="Smart card">smart card</a>, called "<a href="/wiki/Bilhete_%C3%9Anico" title="Bilhete Único">Bilhete Único</a>" (or "Single Ticket" in English).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_operational_data">Current operational data</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Current operational data">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Its current extension does not cover all the areas in the city, however, the subway network, with five lines (three of which are undergoing construction for extensions), is complemented by a network of metropolitan trains of 258.4 km (162 mi), divided into six lines operated by CPTM, which serve the capital and other cities in the <a href="/wiki/Greater_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="Greater São Paulo">Greater São Paulo</a>, extending up to <a href="/wiki/Jundia%C3%AD" title="Jundiaí">Jundiaí</a>, <a href="/wiki/Osasco" title="Osasco">Osasco</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santo_Andr%C3%A9" title="Santo André">Santo André</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mogi_das_Cruzes" title="Mogi das Cruzes">Mogi das Cruzes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ferraz_de_Vasconcelos" title="Ferraz de Vasconcelos">Ferraz de Vasconcelos</a>, and others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Metro is funded by the São Paulo State government and is run by a special self-administered organisation. In terms of service the lines are generally average compared to international standards although certain more prominent lines and stations such as the Yellow Line and the downtown stations feature much more capable infrastructure due to their expected high use and run much more quickly and efficiently while less used lines are historically given less attention and usually run a lesser number of cars. The São Paulo State government has begun to address this issue and is currently building more lines and stations in farther out areas and ordering more cars to run on lines already in service with increase in funding that the state government has been receiving in recent years due to the growth of the Brazilian economy.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conversion_of_metropolitan_lines_to_surface_metro">Conversion of metropolitan lines to surface metro</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Conversion of metropolitan lines to surface metro">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This project of conversion of lines to metro arose due to the high demand of passengers who use the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_area" title="Metropolitan area">metropolitan</a> lines of CPTM and the need to recover the old stations.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hyundai_Rotem_-_Interna_-_Linha_4_Amarela.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2112" data-file-width="2816" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Hyundai_Rotem_-_Interna_-_Linha_4_Amarela.jpg/220px-Hyundai_Rotem_-_Interna_-_Linha_4_Amarela.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Hyundai_Rotem_-_Interna_-_Linha_4_Amarela.jpg/330px-Hyundai_Rotem_-_Interna_-_Linha_4_Amarela.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Hyundai_Rotem_-_Interna_-_Linha_4_Amarela.jpg/440px-Hyundai_Rotem_-_Interna_-_Linha_4_Amarela.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hyundai_Rotem_-_Interna_-_Linha_4_Amarela.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Interior of one of Line 4 - Yellow <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hyundai-Rotem" title="Hyundai-Rotem">Rotem</a> train.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Currently, there are old 19th century stations that were never modernised. With the extreme need to recover stations, it appeared that the plan of conversion, which is nothing but the modernisation of the stations, purchase of new trains and reduction of headway to less than three minutes, as international standards.</p>
<p>Between the end of the 1990s and the early 2000s, with this project of refurbishing the CPTM lines, inherited from the <a href="/wiki/RFFSA" title="RFFSA">RFFSA</a> (Federal Railway Network) and Fepasa (former São Paulo State Railways), the conversion of some metropolitan lines to Metro standard began. This experience started in Line E, in the stretch known as "East Express", serving the east end of São Paulo City and running parallel to Line 3 - Red. The stretch completed (to Guaianazes station) today has new and modern trains and stations with a new route in the final part. The next stretch to be built, between Guaianazes station, in São Paulo City, and Estudantes in <a href="/wiki/Mogi_das_Cruzes" title="Mogi das Cruzes">Mogi das Cruzes</a>, also covering the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Ferraz_de_Vasconcelos" title="Ferraz de Vasconcelos">Ferraz de Vasconcelos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suzano" title="Suzano">Suzano</a> and <a href="/wiki/Po%C3%A1" title="Poá">Poá</a>, is estimated to have its works resumed in 2007, but this was again postponed, now to 2008. Currently, all CPTM lines, with exception of Line 7 and Line 8, are working as full Metro system.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus_terminals">Bus terminals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bus terminals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In May 1977, Metro assumed the administration and commercial utilization of the Inter-City <a href="/wiki/Jabaquara_Intermunicipal_Terminal" title="Jabaquara Intermunicipal Terminal">Jabaquara Intermunicipal Terminal</a>, and inaugurated, in May 1982, the modern Inter-city <a href="/wiki/Tiet%C3%AA_Bus_Terminal" title="Tietê Bus Terminal">Tietê Bus Terminal</a>, replacing the former Júlio Prestes Terminal.</p>
<p>This agreement established that Metro would be in charge of the studies for the planning, implementation, and operation of passenger transportation in the municipal district of São Paulo, either directly or through third parties.</p>
<p>Later, the other inter-city bus terminals were integrated into the system, such as Bresser, in January 1988, and Palmeiras-Barra Funda, in December 1989. In January 1990 the inter-city bus terminals were outsourced by Metrô, which through public bidding, contracted Consortium Prima for the administration and commercial utilization of the 4 inter-city bus terminals of the city of São Paulo. This contract included the responsibility for maintenance and conservation of the existing installations, as well as of the expansion and modernisation of the terminals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rolling_stock">Rolling stock</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Rolling stock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Budd_Mafersa_C144.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1584" data-file-width="2908" height="120" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Budd_Mafersa_C144.jpg/220px-Budd_Mafersa_C144.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Budd_Mafersa_C144.jpg/330px-Budd_Mafersa_C144.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Budd_Mafersa_C144.jpg/440px-Budd_Mafersa_C144.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The first cars started operating in 1974, the same year the company's commercial activities were initiated. They were originally made in USA by The <a href="/wiki/Budd_Company" title="Budd Company">Budd Company</a>, and the national building company <a href="/wiki/Mafersa" title="Mafersa">Mafersa</a> took the prototype to manufacture the model in large scale, so the rest of the 50 cars are all the same. The model was designed to be used along the North-South line, known as <a href="/wiki/Line_1_(S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro)" title="Line 1 (São Paulo Metro)">Line 1 (São Paulo Metro)</a>.</p>
<p>The initial idea was using two cars during a low-demand operation, then attaching those to others as the demand increased, up to a maximum of six. All of them have a pair of electric engines and a cabin. This model was named Series 100, whose cars received the numbers of 1001 to 1306 (51 trains of 6 cars each).</p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Trem_Linha_3.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Trem_Linha_3.jpg/220px-Trem_Linha_3.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Trem_Linha_3.jpg/330px-Trem_Linha_3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Trem_Linha_3.jpg/440px-Trem_Linha_3.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>In the end, the six-car formation train got standardized. Currently, this fleet is known by "A Fleet", and was planned to be entirely phased out by the beginning of 2015, as the recent modernization processes saw them being converted into two different fleets: I and J. However, this conversion, in January 2016, was still unfinished.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>To reduce the manufacturing costs, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cobrasma&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cobrasma (page does not exist)">Cobrasma</a> company decided to provide, for the East-West Line <a href="/wiki/Line_3_(S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro)" title="Line 3 (São Paulo Metro)">Line 3 (São Paulo Metro)</a>, trains with two cabins only and making use of more advanced ventilation and maintenance systems. This fleet is known by the name of "C". The batch of trains designed for this line were produced by two different national companies, Cobrasma and Mafersa (whose trains got named as "D").</p>
<p>The only difference between the two is the front mask and some structural framework. Their technical nomenclature is 300. According to it,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (July 2016)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> the C fleet has trains with numbers from 301 (C01) to 325 (C25), and the D fleet has trains numbered 326 (D26) to 347 (D47). A large part of C fleet trains were already refurbished as K fleet (or L fleet, for the D cars).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Security">Security</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Security">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Metro's security agents have <a href="/wiki/Police" title="Police">police</a> powers and in case of need they will provide assistance. All police matters that occur within the system are directed to the police station of the subway system, DELPOM (Delegacia de Polícia do Metropolitano de São Paulo), located at Palmeiras-Barra Funda station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
