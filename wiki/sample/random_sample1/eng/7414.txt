<p><b>Shanghai Jiao Tong University</b> (abbreviated <b>SJTU</b>; Chinese: 上海交通大学, pinyin: Shànghǎi Jiāotōng Dàxué) is a public research university located in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. Established in 1896 as Nan Yang College by an imperial edict issued by the <a href="/wiki/Guangxu_Emperor" title="Guangxu Emperor">Guangxu Emperor</a>, the university is one of the first national institutions of higher learning in China, and renowned as one of the oldest, most prestigious and selective universities in China. It is one of the nine members in the <a href="/wiki/C9_League" title="C9 League">C9 League</a> of universities.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shanghai_Jiao_Tong_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The word <i>jiaotong</i> (交通) means "transportation". The university was administered by the state telegraph and communications agency — the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Posts_and_Communications" title="Ministry of Posts and Communications">Ministry of Posts and Communications</a> — during its early period. Today, the university does not translate "jiao tong" in its English name, as it is a comprehensive university that provides instruction and research in many fields, including those unrelated to communications.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shanghai_Jiao_Tong_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1896, the <b>Nanyang Public School</b> (<span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">南洋公學</span>) was founded in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> by an imperial edict issued by the <a href="/wiki/Guangxu_Emperor" title="Guangxu Emperor">Guangxu Emperor</a>, under the Business and Telegraphs Office of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" title="Qing Dynasty">imperial government</a>. Four schools were established: a <a href="/wiki/Normal_school" title="Normal school">normal school</a>, a school of foreign studies, a middle school, and a <a href="/wiki/Nanyang_Model_High_School" title="Nanyang Model High School">high school</a>. <a href="/wiki/Sheng_Xuanhuai" title="Sheng Xuanhuai">Sheng Xuanhuai</a>, the mandarin responsible for proposing the idea to the Guangxu Emperor, became the first president and is regarded as the founder of the university, with the assistance of <a href="/wiki/John_Calvin_Ferguson" title="John Calvin Ferguson">John Calvin Ferguson</a>, a missionary educator.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:NYGX001.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="345" data-file-width="550" height="125" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/NYGX001.jpg/200px-NYGX001.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/NYGX001.jpg/300px-NYGX001.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/NYGX001.jpg/400px-NYGX001.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:NYGX001.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Nanyang Public School</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1904, the Ministry of Commerce took over the school, and in 1905 changed its name to <b>Imperial Polytechnic College of the Commerce Ministry</b>.</p>
<p>In 1906, the college was placed under the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Posts_and_Communications" title="Ministry of Posts and Communications">Ministry of Posts and Telegraphs</a>, and its name was changed to <b>Shanghai Industrial College of the Ministry of Posts and Telegraphs</b>.</p>
<p>When the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_China" title="Republic of China">Republic of China</a> was founded, the college was placed under the Ministry of Communications and its name was once again changed, this time to <b>Government Institute of Technology of the Communications Ministry</b>.</p>
<p>In 1918, the republic government founded the School of Management. In 1920, the Government Institute of Technology of the Communications Ministry merged with two other colleges and changed its name to <b>Nan Yang College of Chiao Tung</b>.</p>
<p>In the 1930s, it was achieved renown for nurturing top engineers and scientists and was referred to as the "Eastern <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">MIT</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-easternmit_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-easternmit-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1938, the Ministry of Education took over the university and renamed it to <b>National Chiao Tung University</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">國立交通大學</span>) (the separate institution of <a href="/wiki/National_Chiao_Tung_University" title="National Chiao Tung University">National Chiao Tung University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, is still known by this name). In 1943, the graduate school was founded.</p>
<p>When the Japanese surrendered in 1945, neither the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party of China</a> (CPC) nor the <a href="/wiki/Kuomintang" title="Kuomintang">Kuomintang</a> KMT trusted each other or were actively cooperating. After American-sponsored attempts to negotiate a coalition government failed in 1946, the Chinese Civil War resumed. The CPC defeated the Nationalists in 1949, forcing Chiang's government to retreat to Taiwan. During the evacuation, a part of faculty and alumni was taken to Taiwan by <a href="/wiki/Chiang_Kai-shek" title="Chiang Kai-shek">Chiang Kai-shek</a>, founding <a href="/wiki/National_Chiao_Tung_University" title="National Chiao Tung University">National Chiao Tung University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> in 1958.</p>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War" title="Chinese Civil War">Chinese Civil War</a>, the People's Republic of China was founded in 1949. Chiao Tung lost its "National" appellation and became Chiao Tung University to reflect the fact that all universities under the new socialist state would be public.</p>
<p>In the 1950s, the <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a> romanization system was developed in Mainland China and Chiao Tung University changed its English name to <b>Jiao Tong University</b>.</p>
<p>From 1952, the Communist government adopted a policy of creating Soviet-style <a href="/wiki/Specialized_school" title="Specialized school">specialized schools</a>, reshuffling nearly all universities and colleges to follow the Soviet-style higher education model. Under this policy, some faculties of the university were shifted to other universities, and some engineering faculties from outside were absorbed into Jiao Tong University to form a specialized engineering university. A massive rearrangement came in 1956 for the school when the central government ordered the university move to Xi'an, in the western Chinese province of Shaanxi. Afterwards, about 1959, the remaining portion was officially renamed Shanghai Jiao Tong University, SJTU.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shanghai_Second_Medical_University" title="Shanghai Second Medical University">Shanghai Second Medical University</a> was merged into Shanghai Jiao Tong University on July 18, 2005, under the name <b>Medical School of Shanghai Jiao Tong University</b>.</p>
<p>Since the reform and opening up policy in China, SJTU has grown substantially. It is composed of five campuses, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xuhui" title="Xuhui">Xuhui</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minhang" title="Minhang">Minhang</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luwan" title="Luwan">Luwan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Qibao" title="Qibao">Qibao</a>, and Fahua (法华), taking up an area of about 3,225,833 square meters. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-statisticsinsum_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-statisticsinsum-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Hollande" title="François Hollande">François Hollande</a> inaugurated the SJTU-ParisTech Elite Institute of Technology (SPEIT), an institution based on the <a href="/wiki/Grandes_%C3%A9coles" title="Grandes écoles">French engineering education system</a>. The four founding member universities are <a href="/wiki/%C3%89cole_Polytechnique" title="École Polytechnique">École Polytechnique</a>, <a href="/wiki/ENSTA_ParisTech" title="ENSTA ParisTech">ENSTA ParisTech</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mines_ParisTech" title="Mines ParisTech">Mines ParisTech</a> and <a href="/wiki/T%C3%A9l%C3%A9com_ParisTech" title="Télécom ParisTech">Télécom ParisTech</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The university was also producer of the <a href="/wiki/Academic_Ranking_of_World_Universities" title="Academic Ranking of World Universities">Academic Ranking of World Universities</a> until 2008.</p>

<p>Today SJTU has 31 schools (departments), 63 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Undergraduate" title="Undergraduate">undergraduate</a> programs, 250 masters-degree programs, 203 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ph.D." title="Ph.D.">Ph.D.</a> programs, 28 post-doctorate programs, and 11 state key laboratories and national engineering research centers.</p>
<p>Its total enrollment of students amounts to 42,881, of which 1,598 are international students. There are 17,766 undergraduates, and 24,017 masters and Ph.D. candidates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-statisticsinsum_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-statisticsinsum-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The university has more than 1,900 professors and associate professors, including 15 academicians of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Academy_of_Sciences" title="Chinese Academy of Sciences">Chinese Academy of Sciences</a>, 20 academicians of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Academy_of_Engineering" title="Chinese Academy of Engineering">Chinese Academy of Engineering</a>, 92 accredited professors and chair professors of the "Cheung Kong Scholars Program".</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_Ranking_of_World_Universities">Academic Ranking of World Universities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shanghai_Jiao_Tong_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Academic Ranking of World Universities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since 2003, Shanghai Jiao Tong University has produced the <i><a href="/wiki/Academic_Ranking_of_World_Universities" title="Academic Ranking of World Universities">Academic Ranking of World Universities</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> which analyzes the top universities in the world on quality of faculty (40%), research output (40%), quality of education (10%) and performance vs. size (10%).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Its ranking is exclusively of research universities, mainly in the empirical sciences.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campuses">Campuses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shanghai_Jiao_Tong_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Campuses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Xuhui_Campus">Xuhui Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shanghai_Jiao_Tong_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Xuhui Campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Xuhui_Liberary_Scene_01.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="426" height="282" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Xuhui_Liberary_Scene_01.jpg/200px-Xuhui_Liberary_Scene_01.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Xuhui_Liberary_Scene_01.jpg/300px-Xuhui_Liberary_Scene_01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Xuhui_Liberary_Scene_01.jpg/400px-Xuhui_Liberary_Scene_01.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Xuhui_Liberary_Scene_01.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A scene of the original library, Xuhui campus</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The previous main campus was located in <a href="/wiki/Xujiahui" title="Xujiahui">Xujiahui</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Xuhui_District" title="Xuhui District">Xuhui District</a> of Shanghai, formerly a largely <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic</a> area and a site of several educational institutions.</p>
<p>Most buildings on campus were influenced by American architecture, while the main gate, which was built in 1935, is of traditional Chinese style reflecting the University's earlier status as the "Imperial Polytechnic College". The approaches to the main gate were via a series of marble bridges, similar to, although smaller than, those built in front of <a href="/wiki/Tiananmen" title="Tiananmen">Tiananmen</a>. However, recent road expansions have demolished the river as well as most of bridges. A copy of this famous gate scene have been built on the east of Minhang campus.</p>
<p>The construction of the campus never came to an end in the past century. Existing buildings: the Middle College building (built in 1898), the original library (built in 1919), the gymnasium (built in 1925), the administrative building (built in 1933), the Xinjian building (built in 1953), the new Upper College building (built in 1955), the second teaching building (built in 1960), Bao Zhaolong library (built in 1985) and Haoran high-tech building (built in 1996) were constructed one after another.</p>
<p>The university's School of International Education is located here, where a large number of international students take classes throughout the academic year (however, most international students that take degree courses are in Minhang campus). The campus still houses a variety of scientific research institutions, <a class="external text" href="http://oe.sjtu.edu.cn/_ENGLISH.HTM" rel="nofollow">State Key Laboratory of Ocean Engineering</a> for instance, although most moved to the new campus since 1987.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minhang_Campus">Minhang Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shanghai_Jiao_Tong_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Minhang Campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:SJTU_Siyuan_Lake.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="394" data-file-width="1232" height="90" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/e1/SJTU_Siyuan_Lake.jpg/280px-SJTU_Siyuan_Lake.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/e1/SJTU_Siyuan_Lake.jpg/420px-SJTU_Siyuan_Lake.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/e1/SJTU_Siyuan_Lake.jpg/560px-SJTU_Siyuan_Lake.jpg 2x" width="280"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:SJTU_Siyuan_Lake.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Siyuan Lake, Minhang Campus</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Minhang campus is located in the <a href="/wiki/Minhang_District" title="Minhang District">Minhang District</a> on the outskirts of metropolitan <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>. This young campus, which was founded in 1987, covers 2,822,903m<sup>2</sup> once farmland. Zizhu Science Park, where <a href="/wiki/Intel" title="Intel">Intel</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ST_Microelectronics" title="ST Microelectronics">ST Microelectronics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sandisk" title="Sandisk">Sandisk</a> and other high-tech companies placed their <a href="/wiki/Research_and_development" title="Research and development">research and development</a> centers, to the south of this campus.</p>
<p>Currently, most administrative and academic framework of the university and the majority of undergraduate and graduate students are situated at this campus, which is now accompanied by the newly built Minhang Campus of <a href="/wiki/East_China_Normal_University" title="East China Normal University">East China Normal University</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Chongqing_Road_Campus">South Chongqing Road Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shanghai_Jiao_Tong_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: South Chongqing Road Campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It used to be the campus of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shanghai_Second_Medical_University" title="Shanghai Second Medical University">Shanghai Second Medical University</a>, which is located in the <a href="/wiki/Luwan_District" title="Luwan District">Luwan District</a> of Shanghai. The <a href="/wiki/Aurora_University_(Shanghai)" title="Aurora University (Shanghai)">Aurora University (Shanghai)</a>, a pre-eminent Catholic university in Shanghai, was located there before 1952.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qibao_Campus">Qibao Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shanghai_Jiao_Tong_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Qibao Campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_alumni">Notable alumni</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shanghai_Jiao_Tong_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Notable alumni">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After more than a century of operation, Jiao Tong University has inherited the old tradition of "high starting points, solid foundation, strict requirements and extensive practice." Today, associations organized by Jiao Tong's alumni can be found in the United States, Japan, European Union countries, Canada, Southeast Asia, Taiwan, Hong Kong and Mainland China. They call themselves Jiao Tong (Jiaoda) people.</p>
<p>The routine services of these alumni associations include: updating contact lists, holding conferences, conducting Sino-foreign cultural exchanges, and keeping contact with other alumni associations throughout the world.</p>
<p>Students from SJTU have won top prizes in various competitions, including ACM International Collegiate Programming Contest, International Mathematical Contest in Modeling and Electronics Design Contests. Famous alumni of SJTU or its predecessors include <a href="/wiki/Jiang_Zemin" title="Jiang Zemin">Jiang Zemin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lu_Dingyi" title="Lu Dingyi">Lu Dingyi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ding_Guangen" title="Ding Guangen">Ding Guangen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wang_Daohan" title="Wang Daohan">Wang Daohan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Qian_Xuesen" title="Qian Xuesen">Qian Xuesen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wu_Wenjun" title="Wu Wenjun">Wu Wenjun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zou_Taofen" title="Zou Taofen">Zou Taofen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mao_Yisheng" title="Mao Yisheng">Mao Yisheng</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cai_Er" title="Cai Er">Cai Er</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huang_Yanpei" title="Huang Yanpei">Huang Yanpei</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shao_Lizi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shao Lizi (page does not exist)">Shao Lizi</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wang_An" title="Wang An">Wang An</a>, and many more. More than 200 of the academicians of the Chinese Academy of Sciences and Chinese Academy of Engineering are alumni of Jiao Tong University.</p>
