<p><b>Shanghai International Finance Centre</b>, usually abbreviated as <b>Shanghai IFC</b>, is a commercial building complex and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shopping_centre" title="Shopping centre">shopping centre</a> (branded <b>Shanghai IFC mall</b>) in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>. It incorporates two tower blocks at 249.9 metres (south tower) and 259.9 metres (north tower) housing offices and a hotel, and an 85-metre tall multi-storey building behind and between the two towers.</p>
<p>Shanghai IFC is located in <a href="/wiki/Lujiazui" title="Lujiazui">Lujiazui</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Pudong" title="Pudong">Pudong</a>, Shanghai. It occupies a prominent position southeast of the Lujiazui roundabout, diagonally across from the <a href="/wiki/Oriental_Pearl_Tower" title="Oriental Pearl Tower">Oriental Pearl Tower</a> and across the road from <a href="/wiki/Super_Brand_Mall" title="Super Brand Mall">Super Brand Mall</a>. It is adjacent to <a href="/wiki/Lujiazui_Station" title="Lujiazui Station">Lujiazui Station</a> on <a href="/wiki/Line_2,_Shanghai_Metro" title="Line 2, Shanghai Metro">Metro Line 2</a>, and can be accessed directly from the underground station via a tunnel.</p>
<p>The south tower of Shanghai IFC and part of the multistorey building was completed in 2009, while the north tower and the rest of the complex was completed in 2010. Work continued for several years afterwards on peripheral aspects of the development, including landscaping and <a href="/wiki/Footbridge" title="Footbridge">footbridge</a> connections to nearby buildings and Lujiazui Central Park.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ritz-Carlton" title="Ritz-Carlton">Ritz-Carlton</a> Hotel occupies the south tower, while the north tower houses the current Shanghai headquarters of <a href="/wiki/HSBC" title="HSBC">HSBC</a> in China. Other prominent tenants of the complex include an <a href="/wiki/Apple_Store" title="Apple Store">Apple Store</a> under the sunken forecourt of the building (topped by a cylindrical glass skylight rising from the forecourt), a multi-screen cinema, and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C!ty%27super" title="C!ty'super">Citysuper</a> supermarket. The remainder of the retail area is largely taken up by upscale chain restaurants and international luxury fashion brands.</p>
<p>Shanghai IFC, like its sister project the <a href="/wiki/International_Finance_Centre_(Hong_Kong)" title="International Finance Centre (Hong Kong)">International Finance Centre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, was developed by <a href="/wiki/Sun_Hung_Kai_Properties" title="Sun Hung Kai Properties">Sun Hung Kai Properties</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="HSBC_lions">HSBC lions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shanghai_IFC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: HSBC lions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/HSBC_lions" title="HSBC lions">HSBC lions</a></div>
<p>Maintaining a tradition begun with the historic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HSBC_Building,_Shanghai" title="HSBC Building, Shanghai">HSBC Building</a> across the <a href="/wiki/Huangpu_River" title="Huangpu River">Huangpu River</a> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Bund_(Shanghai)" title="The Bund (Shanghai)">the Bund</a>, the front of the north tower features a pair of bronze lions, the fourth pair of copies of the original which once graced the bank's old Shanghai headquarters.</p>
<p>The original lions are now held by the Shanghai Historic Museum (which currently has no permanent home) and are separately on display at the Museum's display room under the <a href="/wiki/Oriental_Pearl_Tower" title="Oriental Pearl Tower">Oriental Pearl Tower</a> and the Shanghai Banking Museum, both nearby in Lujiazui. The Shanghai IFC copies were cast from the copies outside <a href="/wiki/HSBC_Building_(Hong_Kong)" title="HSBC Building (Hong Kong)">HSBC's Hong Kong headquarters</a>; the other two copies (outside <a href="/wiki/8_Canada_Square" title="8 Canada Square">HSBC's global headquarters in London</a> and the historic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HSBC_Building,_Shanghai" title="HSBC Building, Shanghai">HSBC Building in Shanghai</a>) were cast from the Hong Kong copies and Shanghai originals respectively.</p>
