<p><i><b>Tinea cruris</b></i>, also known as <b>crotch itch</b>, <b>crotch rot</b>, <b>Dhobi itch</b>, <i><b>eczema marginatum</b></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bolognia_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bolognia-1">[1]</a></sup> <b>gym itch</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bolognia_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bolognia-1">[1]</a></sup> <b>jock itch</b>, <b>jock rot</b>, <b>scrot rot</b> and <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ringworm" title="Ringworm">ringworm</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Groin" title="Groin">groin</a></b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bolognia_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Bolognia-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Andrews_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Andrews-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:303</sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Dermatophyte" title="Dermatophyte">dermatophyte</a> <a href="/wiki/Mycosis" title="Mycosis">fungal infection</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Groin" title="Groin">groin</a> region in any sex,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> though more often seen in <a href="/wiki/Male" title="Male">males</a>.</p>
<p>Tinea cruris is similar to, but different from <a href="/wiki/Candidal_intertrigo" title="Candidal intertrigo">Candidal intertrigo</a>, which is an infection of the skin by <i><a href="/wiki/Candida_albicans" title="Candida albicans">Candida albicans</a></i>. The latter is more specifically located between <a href="/wiki/Intertriginous" title="Intertriginous">intertriginous</a> folds of adjacent skin, which can be present in the groin or <a href="/wiki/Scrotum" title="Scrotum">scrotum</a>, and be indistinguishable from fungal infections caused by <i><a href="/wiki/Tinea" title="Tinea">tinea</a></i>. However, candidal infections tend to both appear and with treatment disappear more quickly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Andrews_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Andrews-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:309</sup> It may also affect the scrotum.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Signs_and_symptoms">Signs and symptoms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tinea_cruris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Signs and symptoms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As the common name for this condition implies, it causes <a href="/wiki/Itch" title="Itch">itching</a> or a burning sensation in the groin area, thigh <a href="/wiki/Skin_fold" title="Skin fold">skin folds</a> or <a href="/wiki/Anus" title="Anus">anus</a>. It may involve the inner thighs and genital areas, as well as extending back to the <a href="/wiki/Perineum" title="Perineum">perineum</a> and perianal areas.</p>
<p>Tinea is often painful or itchy, but not in every case. There are visual clues to help identify a fungal infection. These include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Inflammation" title="Inflammation">Inflammation</a> of the groin, anal area and upper thigh - <i>not</i> including the genitals themselves</li>
<li>slightly raised patch</li>
<li>sharp borders</li>
<li>expanding or spreading, with clearing in the center</li>
<li>dry or scaly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blisters" title="Blisters">blisters</a> (occasionally oozing or crusting)</li>
<li>abnormally dark or light skin</li>
<li>skin redness or inflammation</li>
</ul>
<p>Fungal infections often spread out in a circle, leaving normal-looking skin in the middle. At the leading edge of the infection the skin is raised, red and scaly.</p>
<p>Affected areas may appear red, tan, or brown, with flaking, rippling, peeling or cracking skin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The acute infection begins with an area in the groin fold about a half-inch across, usually on both sides. The area may enlarge, and other sores may develop. The rash has sharply defined borders that may blister and ooze.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tinea cruris does have similar symptoms to <a href="/wiki/Inverse_psoriasis" title="Inverse psoriasis">Inverse psoriasis</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Causes">Causes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tinea_cruris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Causes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p><a href="/wiki/Opportunistic_infection" title="Opportunistic infection">Opportunistic infections</a> (infections that are caused by a diminished <a href="/wiki/Immune_system" title="Immune system">immune system</a>) are frequent. <a href="/wiki/Fungus" title="Fungus">Fungus</a> from an <a href="/wiki/Athlete%27s_foot" title="Athlete's foot">athlete's foot</a> infection can spread to the groin through clothing. Tight, restrictive clothing, such as <a href="/wiki/Jockstrap" title="Jockstrap">jockstraps</a>, traps heat and moisture, providing an ideal environment for the fungus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The type of fungus involved is usually <i><a href="/wiki/Trichophyton_rubrum" title="Trichophyton rubrum">Trichophyton rubrum</a></i>. Some other contributing fungi are <i><a href="/wiki/Candida_albicans" title="Candida albicans">Candida albicans</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trichophyton_mentagrophytes" title="Trichophyton mentagrophytes">Trichophyton mentagrophytes</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Epidermophyton_floccosum" title="Epidermophyton floccosum">Epidermophyton floccosum</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prevention">Prevention</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tinea_cruris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Prevention">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Medical professionals recommend a preventative based approach of stopping fungus before it occurs. Prevention is preferable over a reactive treatment approach. The preventative based approach involves removing heat and moisture to the groin area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Dry off with a clean towel immediately after showering, swimming or perspiring.</li>
<li>Shower after physical activities.</li>
<li>Wear underwear with high air and moisture permeability.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Treatment">Treatment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tinea_cruris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Treatment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Tinea" title="Tinea">Tinea</a> cruris is best treated with topical <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antifungal_medication" title="Antifungal medication">antifungal medications</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antifungal_medication#Allylamines" title="Antifungal medication">allylamine</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antifungal_medication#Imidazole.2C_triazole.2C_and_thiazole_antifungals" title="Antifungal medication">azole</a> type.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The evidence is best for <a href="/wiki/Terbinafine" title="Terbinafine">terbinafine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Naftifine" title="Naftifine">naftifine</a> but other agents may also work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EL2014_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-EL2014-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The benefits of the use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Topical_steroids" title="Topical steroids">topical steroids</a> in addition to an antifungal is unclear.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EL2014_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-EL2014-9">[9]</a></sup> There might be a greater cure rate but no guidelines currently recommend its addition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EL2014_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-EL2014-9">[9]</a></sup> The effect of <a href="/wiki/Whitfield%27s_ointment" title="Whitfield's ointment">Whitfield's ointment</a> is also unclear.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EL2014_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-EL2014-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
