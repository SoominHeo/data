<p>The <b>AFC U-16 Women's Championship</b> is a biennial <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_association_football" title="Women's association football">women's football</a> tournament for youth teams organised by the <a href="/wiki/Asian_Football_Confederation" title="Asian Football Confederation">Asian Football Confederation</a>. It further serves as the qualifying competition for the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_U-17_Women%27s_World_Cup" title="FIFA U-17 Women's World Cup">FIFA U-17 Women's World Cup</a>.</p>
<p>The tournament was first held in 2005 as an Under-17 edition. With only eleven teams entering in the inaugural year, there was no qualification held.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2007 the tournament switched to the Under-16 modus, again eight teams entered the competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2009 twelve teams entered and thus for a first time a qualifying round was held.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The 2011 edition featured two qualification rounds. Here five seeded teams for the finals were joined by an additional 13 teams fighting for a final sixth spot.</p>
