<p><b>Samuel Stewart "Sam" Witwer</b> (born October 20, 1977) is an American actor and musician. He has portrayed <a href="/wiki/Alex_Quartararo" title="Alex Quartararo">Crashdown</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Battlestar_Galactica_(2004_TV_series)" title="Battlestar Galactica (2004 TV series)">Battlestar Galactica</a></i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Davis_Bloome" title="Davis Bloome">Davis Bloome</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Smallville" title="Smallville">Smallville</a></i>, and vampire Aidan Waite of the US/Canadian remake of BBC's supernatural drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Being_Human_(North_American_TV_series)" title="Being Human (North American TV series)">Being Human</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Syfy" title="Syfy">Syfy</a> in the US and <a href="/wiki/Space_(TV_channel)" title="Space (TV channel)">Space</a> in Canada. He also <a href="/wiki/Voice_acting" title="Voice acting">voiced</a> protagonist <a href="/wiki/Starkiller" title="Starkiller">Galen Marek/Starkiller</a> in the successful multimedia project <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Force_Unleashed_(project)" title="Star Wars: The Force Unleashed (project)">Star Wars: The Force Unleashed</a></i>, as well as The Son and <a href="/wiki/Darth_Maul" title="Darth Maul">Darth Maul</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars_(2008_TV_series)" title="Star Wars: The Clone Wars (2008 TV series)">Star Wars: The Clone Wars</a></i> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emperor_Palpatine" title="Emperor Palpatine">Emperor Palpatine</a> as well as Maul in <a href="/wiki/Disney_XD" title="Disney XD">Disney XD</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Rebels" title="Star Wars Rebels">Star Wars Rebels</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Witwer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Witwer grew up in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glenview,_Cook_County,_Illinois" title="Glenview, Cook County, Illinois">Glenview</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>, a small suburb outside of Chicago. He attended <a href="/wiki/Glenbrook_South_High_School" title="Glenbrook South High School">Glenbrook South High School</a>, during which time he was involved in drama and theater classes, as well as being the lead singer of a high school band called "Love Plumber". He attended the <a href="/wiki/Juilliard_School" title="Juilliard School">Juilliard School</a> for a time before moving to Los Angeles.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Witwer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Samuel_Witwer,_2012.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1996" data-file-width="1641" height="268" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Samuel_Witwer%2C_2012.jpg/220px-Samuel_Witwer%2C_2012.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Samuel_Witwer%2C_2012.jpg/330px-Samuel_Witwer%2C_2012.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Samuel_Witwer%2C_2012.jpg/440px-Samuel_Witwer%2C_2012.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Witwer's first on-screen credit was that of a <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Bulls" title="Chicago Bulls">Chicago Bulls</a> commercial. He soon found himself in speaking roles on hit television series, such as <i><a href="/wiki/ER_(TV_series)" title="ER (TV series)">ER</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Witwer's first major recurring role came in the form of portraying <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lt._Crashdown" title="Lt. Crashdown">Lt. Crashdown</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Battlestar_Galactica_(2004_TV_series)" title="Battlestar Galactica (2004 TV series)">Battlestar Galactica</a></i>, although he has credited much of his current success to his role as Neil Perry on the <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Dexter_(TV_series)" title="Dexter (TV series)">Dexter</a></i>. Witwer also appeared as Private Wayne Jessup in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mist_(film)" title="The Mist (film)">The Mist</a></i>.</p>
<p>From 2008 to 2009, Witwer was cast <a href="/wiki/Smallville_(season_8)" title="Smallville (season 8)">Season 8</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Smallville" title="Smallville">Smallville</a></i> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Davis_Bloome" title="Davis Bloome">Davis Bloome</a>, a charming paramedic who struggles with a darkness within as he is the human camouflage of <a href="/wiki/Doomsday_(comics)" title="Doomsday (comics)">Doomsday</a>, a character in the comics best known for being the only villain to have killed <a href="/wiki/Superman" title="Superman">Superman</a>. While Witwer played Davis, Doomsday was played by stuntman Dario Delacio. Witwer's performance in <i>Smallville</i> was incredibly well-received, and he has stated that his experience on the show had opened more opportunities for him in the future of acting.</p>
<p>Witwer made a cameo appearance in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Walking_Dead_(TV_series)" title="The Walking Dead (TV series)">The Walking Dead</a></i> as a dead (zombie) soldier in a tank in the <a href="/wiki/The_Walking_Dead_(season_1)" title="The Walking Dead (season 1)">Season 1</a> episode "<a href="/wiki/Days_Gone_Bye_(The_Walking_Dead)" title="Days Gone Bye (The Walking Dead)">Days Gone Bye</a>" which reunited him with director <a href="/wiki/Frank_Darabont" title="Frank Darabont">Frank Darabont</a> from <i>The Mist</i>.</p>
<p>Witwer starred in the North American <a href="/wiki/Being_Human_(North_American_TV_series)" title="Being Human (North American TV series)">remake</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Being_Human_(UK_TV_series)" title="Being Human (UK TV series)">Being Human</a></i>. He played the role of vampire Aidan Waite.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>He voiced <a href="/wiki/Ocean_Master" title="Ocean Master">Ocean Master</a> in the animated film <i><a href="/wiki/Justice_League:_Throne_of_Atlantis" title="Justice League: Throne of Atlantis">Justice League: Throne of Atlantis</a></i> for the <i><a href="/wiki/DC_Universe_Animated_Original_Movies" title="DC Universe Animated Original Movies">DC Universe Animated Original Movies</a></i> series, replacing fellow Star Wars actor <a href="/wiki/Steven_Blum" title="Steven Blum">Steven Blum</a> from <i><a href="/wiki/Justice_League:_War" title="Justice League: War">Justice League: War</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2016 Witwer played a recurring role of Mr Hyde on <i><a href="/wiki/Once_Upon_a_Time_(TV_series)" title="Once Upon a Time (TV series)">Once Upon a Time</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roleplaying_games">Roleplaying games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Witwer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roleplaying games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sam is an avid player of both electronic games and pen-and-paper roleplaying games. This is a hobby that he has engaged in for years. He has been a frequent guest on the Podcast <i><a href="/wiki/Order_66_(podcast)" title="Order 66 (podcast)">Order 66</a></i> and has expressed a love for all iterations of <i>Star Wars</i> roleplaying games <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Roleplaying_Game" title="Star Wars: The Roleplaying Game">Star Wars: The Roleplaying Game</a></i> (by <a href="/wiki/West_End_Games" title="West End Games">West End Games</a>) to <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Roleplaying_Game_(Wizards_of_the_Coast)" title="Star Wars Roleplaying Game (Wizards of the Coast)">Star Wars Roleplaying Game</a></i> (by <a href="/wiki/Wizards_of_the_Coast" title="Wizards of the Coast">Wizards of the Coast</a>) and to the current series of <a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Roleplaying_Game_(Fantasy_Flight_Games)" title="Star Wars Roleplaying Game (Fantasy Flight Games)">cross-compatible <i>Star Wars</i> roleplaying games</a> by <a href="/wiki/Fantasy_Flight_Games" title="Fantasy Flight Games">Fantasy Flight Games</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sam appeared in episode 19 of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geek_and_Sundry" title="Geek and Sundry">Geek and Sundry</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tabletop_(web_series)" title="Tabletop (web series)">Tabletop</a></i> playing the <i><a href="/wiki/Dragon_Age_(role-playing_game)" title="Dragon Age (role-playing game)">Dragon Age</a></i> roleplaying game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Star_Wars_career"><i>Star Wars</i> career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Witwer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Star Wars career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A lifelong <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars" title="Star Wars">Star Wars</a></i> fan, Witwer provided both his voice and likeness for lead character <a href="/wiki/Starkiller" title="Starkiller">Galen Marek/Starkiller</a> (<a href="/wiki/Darth_Vader" title="Darth Vader">Darth Vader</a>'s secret apprentice) in the 2008 video game <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Force_Unleashed" title="Star Wars: The Force Unleashed">Star Wars: The Force Unleashed</a></i> and 2010 sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Force_Unleashed_II" title="Star Wars: The Force Unleashed II">Star Wars: The Force Unleashed II</a></i>. He has since received much praise for his contribution to the role. He also voiced <a href="/wiki/Palpatine" title="Palpatine">Darth Sidious (Emperor Palpatine)</a> in both games, and reprised it for the games <i><a href="/wiki/Disney_Infinity_3.0" title="Disney Infinity 3.0">Disney Infinity 3.0</a></i> and <i>Battlefront</i> and for season 2 of <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Rebels" title="Star Wars Rebels">Star Wars Rebels</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-IGN-5">[5]</a></sup> where he also plays Darth Maul.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>For <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars_(2008_TV_series)" title="Star Wars: The Clone Wars (2008 TV series)">Star Wars: The Clone Wars</a></i>, Witwer had also vocally performed the Son in a three episode story arc (known as the Mortis trilogy) in Season 3 as well as the iconic Sith Lord <a href="/wiki/Darth_Maul" title="Darth Maul">Darth Maul</a> in the Season 4 finale episodes and in four episodes of Season 5. He also lent his voice for Darth Maul in other projects such as the <i>Star Wars: Episode I Brisk</i> commercial as well as the Cartoon Network special <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lego_Star_Wars:_The_Empire_Strikes_Out" title="Lego Star Wars: The Empire Strikes Out">Lego Star Wars: The Empire Strikes Out</a></i>, and is playing Maul again for <i>Star Wars Rebels</i>.</p>
<p>Sam attended <i>The Clone Wars</i> Season 5 Red Carpet Premier in Orlando, Florida on August 24, 2012 during Celebration VI along with some of his co-stars <a href="/wiki/Matt_Lanter" title="Matt Lanter">Matt Lanter</a>, <a href="/wiki/James_Arnold_Taylor" title="James Arnold Taylor">James Arnold Taylor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dee_Bradley_Baker" title="Dee Bradley Baker">Dee Bradley Baker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Kane" title="Tom Kane">Tom Kane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Logan" title="Daniel Logan">Daniel Logan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Stanton" title="Stephen Stanton">Stephen Stanton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Eckstein" title="Ashley Eckstein">Ashley Eckstein</a>, as well as Supervising Director <a href="/wiki/Dave_Filoni" title="Dave Filoni">Dave Filoni</a>. During Celebration VI, he also had his own panel called Sam Witwer "The Maul Within" on August 26, 2012. In 2013, he attended for the first time the annual event, Star Wars Weekends at Disney's Hollywood Studios in Orlando, Florida for a three-day weekend and fans praised him for his roles in <i>The Force Unleashed</i> games and on <i>The Clone Wars</i>. During the live show "Behind the Force", Sam showed his acting skills by saying one or two lines of the characters that he's played in the <i>Star Wars</i> universe.</p>
<p>Sam also guest voiced an alien as well as a stormtrooper during the siege of Maz Kanata's palace in <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Force_Awakens" title="Star Wars: The Force Awakens">Star Wars: The Force Awakens</a></i>.</p>
