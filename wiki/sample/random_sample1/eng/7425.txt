<p><i><b>Yoake Mae yori Ruriiro na</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">夜明け前より瑠璃色な</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. <i>A Brighter Blue Than That Before the Dawn</i>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Eroge" title="Eroge">adult</a> <a href="/wiki/Visual_novel" title="Visual novel">visual novel</a> developed by <a href="/wiki/August_(company)" title="August (company)">August</a> and first released on September 22, 2005 for <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Windows</a>. It was later ported to the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a> and <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Portable" title="PlayStation Portable">PlayStation Portable</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> illustrated by Hoehoe Nōmiso was serialized in <i><a href="/wiki/Dengeki_Daioh" title="Dengeki Daioh">Dengeki Daioh</a></i> between the November 2005 and May 2007 issues. A 12-episode <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> adaptation titled <i>Yoake Mae yori Ruriiro na: Crescent Love</i> aired in Japan between October and December 2006. The anime is licensed by Sentai Filmworks in North America and was released on June 1, 2010.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yoake_Mae_yori_Ruriiro_na&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Yoake Mae yori Ruriiro na</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Romance_(love)" title="Romance (love)">romance</a> <a href="/wiki/Visual_novel" title="Visual novel">visual novel</a> in which the player assumes the role of Tatsuya Asagiri. Its <a href="/wiki/Gameplay" title="Gameplay">gameplay</a> requires little player interaction as much of the game's duration is spent on reading the text that appears on the screen, which represents the story's <a href="/wiki/Narrative" title="Narrative">narrative</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dialogue" title="Dialogue">dialogue</a>. The text is accompanied by character <a href="/wiki/Sprite_(computer_graphics)" title="Sprite (computer graphics)">sprites</a>, which represent who Tatsuya is talking to, over background art. Throughout the game, the player encounters <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CG_artwork" title="CG artwork">CG artwork</a> at certain points in the story, which take the place of the background art and character sprites. <i>Yoake Mae yori Ruriiro na</i> follows a <a href="/wiki/Nonlinear_gameplay#Branching_storylines" title="Nonlinear gameplay">branching plot line</a> with multiple endings, and depending on the decisions that the player makes during the game, the plot will progress in a specific direction.</p>
<p>There are seven main plot lines that the player will have the chance to experience, one for each of the heroines in the story. Every so often, the player will come to a point where he or she is given the chance to choose from multiple options. Text progression pauses at these points until a choice is made. To view all plot lines in their entirety, the player will have to replay the game multiple times and choose different choices to further the plot to an alternate direction. After the completion of the six heroine routes, the final scenario called Yoake Mae yori Ruriiro na is made available. Throughout gameplay, there are scenes with sexual CGs depicting Tatsuya and a given heroine having sex.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yoake_Mae_yori_Ruriiro_na&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Yoake Mae yori Ruriiro na</i> centers on Princess Feena Fam Earthlight from the Kingdom of Sphere on Earth's moon. It was decided that she was to live on Earth in a homestay with the family of the primary secretary to the president of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> so as to get more knowledgeable of Earth in order to better prepare herself for her succession as the Kingdom of Sphere's next queen.</p>
<p>The background of the story explains that humans landed on the moon long ago intending to create a new home for themselves called the Kingdom of Sphere. However, the relations between Earth and the Kingdom have worsened things and a war called the Oedipus war started. Once the war was over, it was decided that the city of Mitsurugasaki in Shizuoka Prefecture of Japan, where the story takes place, under the rule of the United Nations, be developed as the center of diplomacy with the Lunar Kingdom, which, currently, has closed its doors to the world. This central lunar junction port city, which has the only spaceport within the United Nations with ships going to and coming from the Lunar Embassy, is, as its name suggests, the gateway which connects the Earth and the moon. Although tensions have temporarily eased now, relations between the two worlds is still in a situation when the story begins where caution must still be taken.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_characters">Main characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yoake_Mae_yori_Ruriiro_na&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Main characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt>Tatsuya Asagiri <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">朝霧 達哉</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Asagiri Tatsuya</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: Kouichi Tsukasa</span> (Drama CD) / <a href="/wiki/Susumu_Chiba" title="Susumu Chiba">Susumu Chiba</a> (anime)</dd>
<dd>Tatsuya is the main male protagonist in the story. He is very knowledgeable about the moon and the Kingdom of Sphere because he has been interested in it since he was very young. He has this odd unconscious habit of pinching girls on the nose as a sign of affection. Tatsuya constantly finds enjoyment in teasing Natsuki about random things. He is quite loyal toward Feena and her aim to fulfill her mother's dream to reunite the Earth and Moon. Though somewhat apprehensive about the homestay, Tatsuya eventually falls in love with the princess and their union cements the alliance between the two worlds.</dd>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_and_release">Development and release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yoake_Mae_yori_Ruriiro_na&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Development and release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Yoake Mae yori Ruriiro na</i> is the fifth title developed by the visual novel developer August. The project was overseen by game director Rune, and the scenario was written by four people. Taku Sakakibara, Hiroyuki Uchida, Hideaki Anzai and Runa Okada. Character design and art direction for the game was provided by Bekkankō, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CG_artwork" title="CG artwork">CG</a> supervision was handled by Fujihisa Satomi. The game's <a href="/wiki/Background_music" title="Background music">background music</a> was produced by members of Active Planets.</p>
<p><i>Yoake Mae yori Ruriiro na</i> was released as a limited edition on September 22, 2005 for <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Windows</a>; the regular edition was released on December 22, 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Aria and <a href="/wiki/HuneX" title="HuneX">HuneX</a> released a <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a> (PS2) port titled <i>Yoake Mae yori Ruriiro na: Brighter than dawning blue</i> on December 7, 2006, which removed the adult elements of the game and introduced two additional heroines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The PS2 version was later ported back to Windows titled <i>Yoake Mae yori Ruriiro na: Brighter than dawning blue for PC</i> on September 18, 2009. A sequel to the story established in the PS2 version titled <i>Yoake Mae yori Ruriiro na: Moonlight Cradle</i> was released as a limited edition on February 27, 2009 for Windows; the regular edition was released on September 18, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Premium packs were later released containing both <i>Brighter than dawning blue</i> and <i>Moonlight Cradle</i>. Aria released a <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Portable" title="PlayStation Portable">PlayStation Portable</a> port titled <i>Yoake Mae yori Ruriiro na: Portable</i> on February 25, 2010. A version compatible with <a href="/wiki/Windows_7" title="Windows 7">Windows 7</a> was released on July 29, 2011.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Drama_CDs">Drama CDs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yoake_Mae_yori_Ruriiro_na&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Drama CDs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A set of six <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_CD" title="Drama CD">drama CDs</a> were created based on the video game <i>Yoake Mae yori Ruriiro na -Brighter than dawning blue-</i>, entitled <i>Yoake Mae Yori Ruriiro na ~Fairy tale of Luna~ #1-6</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">夜明け前より瑠璃色な〜Fairy tale of Luna〜 #1-6</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> released between April 21, 2006 and September 22, 2006 at roughly one-month intervals by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geneon_Entertainment" title="Geneon Entertainment">Marine Entertainment</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Two additional drama CDs based on the anime adaptation were released on October 25, 2006 and December 22, 2006 respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Print_media">Print media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yoake_Mae_yori_Ruriiro_na&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Print media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A seven volume <a href="/wiki/Light_novel" title="Light novel">light novel</a> series, written by Tasuku Saika and illustrated by Bekkankō, was published between May 1 and November 1, 2006. Three volumes of a collection of short stories serialized in <i><a href="/wiki/Dengeki_G%27s_Magazine" title="Dengeki G's Magazine">Dengeki G's Magazine</a></i> titled <i>Official Heroine Story: Yoake Mae yori Ruriiro na Koisuru Shunkan</i>, written by Runa Okada and illustrated by Bekkankō, Mika Takeda and Keitarō Kawagashi, were released between September 30, 2006 and March 30, 2007. A light novel titled <i>Yoake Mae yori Ruriiro: Lavender Eyes</i>, written by Saya Amō and illustrated by Bekkankō, was published on December 20, 2006.</p>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> illustrated by Hoehoe Nōmiso was serialized in <a href="/wiki/MediaWorks_(publisher)" title="MediaWorks (publisher)">MediaWorks'</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Dengeki_Daioh" title="Dengeki Daioh">Dengeki Daioh</a></i> magazine between the November 2005 and May 2007 issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Two <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> volumes were released: the first on September 27, 2006 and the second on May 26, 2007.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yoake_Mae_yori_Ruriiro_na&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Directed by Masahiko Ohta and written by Aoshima Takashi, a 12-episode anime adaptation of <i>Yoake Mae yori Ruriiro na</i> was animated by <a href="/wiki/Daume" title="Daume">Daume</a> titled <i>Yoake Mae yori Ruriiro na: Crescent Love</i>. It aired between October 4 and December 20, 2006 on <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Broadcasting_System" title="Tokyo Broadcasting System">TBS Japan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_List_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_List-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sentai_Filmworks" title="Sentai Filmworks">Sentai Filmworks</a> distributed the anime as <i>Brighter than the Dawning Blue</i>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Original_soundtrack" title="Original soundtrack">original soundtrack</a> for the anime was released on January 26, 2007 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geneon_Entertainment" title="Geneon Entertainment">Geneon Entertainment</a> with 28 tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>A badly animated cabbage in the third episode generated an <a href="/wiki/Internet_meme" title="Internet meme">Internet meme</a> in Japan where people on <a href="/wiki/Futaba_Channel" title="Futaba Channel">2chan</a> posted <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fanart" title="Fanart">fanart</a> of Feena with the spherical cabbage with concentric leaves and, changing two <a href="/wiki/Kanji" title="Kanji">kanji</a> symbols, referred to the series as Brighter than Dawning Green -Cabbage Love- <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">夜明け前より黄緑色な -Cabbage Love-</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yoake mae yori kimidori iro na -Cabbage Love-</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Eventually, the DVD version of the anime was given a smoother look and visibly better animation (albeit without a change in storyline). The aforementioned badly-drawn cabbage was also redrawn correctly.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
