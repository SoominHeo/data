<p><b>Sandy Mölling</b> (born 27 April 1981) is a German singer-songwriter, actress and television personality. Born in <a href="/wiki/Wuppertal" title="Wuppertal">Wuppertal</a> and raised in <a href="/wiki/Koblenz" title="Koblenz">Koblenz</a>, she performed in various singing competitions and became a studio singer in her teenage years. Möllig later rose to fame as one of the members of the girl group <a href="/wiki/No_Angels" title="No Angels">No Angels</a>, which were formed in 2000 on the German television talent show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Popstars_(Germany)" title="Popstars (Germany)">Popstars</a></i>, becoming one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_best-selling_girl_groups" title="List of best-selling girl groups">best-selling girl groups</a> of European origin of all time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-presseportal_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-presseportal-1">[1]</a></sup> During their hiatus, Mölling released a solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Unexpected_(Sandy_M%C3%B6lling_album)" title="Unexpected (Sandy Mölling album)">Unexpected</a></i> (2004), which produced the top ten singles "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unnatural_Blonde" title="Unnatural Blonde">Unnatural Blonde</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Tell_Me_(Sandy_M%C3%B6lling_song)" title="Tell Me (Sandy Mölling song)">Tell Me</a>", and became as a judge on the <a href="/wiki/Popstars_%E2%80%93_Jetzt_oder_Nie!" title="Popstars – Jetzt oder Nie!">fourth season</a> of <i>Popstars</i>.</p>
<p>In 2006, she released her second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Frame_of_Mind_(album)" title="Frame of Mind (album)">Frame of Mind</a></i> to moderate success and finished third on the <a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Dance_(German_season_1)" title="Let's Dance (German season 1)">debut season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Dance_(German_TV_series)" title="Let's Dance (German TV series)">Let's Dance</a></i>. The following year, she became a presenter and actress in several short-living television shows on <a href="/wiki/Sat.1" title="Sat.1">Sat.1</a>, <a href="/wiki/Das_Vierte" title="Das Vierte">Das Vierte</a>, and <a href="/wiki/VIVA_Germany" title="VIVA Germany">VIVA Germany</a> before serving as a judge on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Singing_Bee_(U.S._game_show)" title="The Singing Bee (U.S. game show)">Die singende Firma</a></i>. In 2010, Mölling made her stage debut in the musical <i><a href="/wiki/A_Christmas_Carol" title="A Christmas Carol">Vom Geist der Weihnacht</a></i>, which led her to parts in productions such as <i><a href="/wiki/Joseph_and_the_Amazing_Technicolor_Dreamcoat" title="Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat">Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Cabaret_(musical)" title="Cabaret (musical)">Cabaret</a></i>. For starring as <a href="/wiki/Eliza_Doolittle" title="Eliza Doolittle">Eliza Doolittle</a> in the 2016 <a href="/wiki/Bad_Hersfelder_Festspiele" title="Bad Hersfelder Festspiele">Bad Hersfeld</a> revival of the musical <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/My_Fair_Lady_(musical)" title="My Fair Lady (musical)">My Fair Lady</a></i>, she won the <a href="/wiki/Hersfeld-Preis" title="Hersfeld-Preis">Audience Award for Favorite Actress</a>.</p>
<p>Throughout her career, Mölling has scored four number-one hits and sold over 5 million albums and singles worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-presseportal_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-presseportal-1">[1]</a></sup> Outside her work in the entertainment industry, she is involved in many charitable activities, promoting various causes, including education, children's care and animal welfare. In 2015, she co-authored the children's book <i>Pumpel, der Baumzwerg</i> along with her mother. The same year, Mölling relocated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a>, where she gave birth to her second son, her first child with Canadian music producer and <a href="/wiki/Magic!" title="Magic!">Magic!</a> lead singer <a href="/wiki/Nasri_(musician)" title="Nasri (musician)">Nasri Atweh</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sandy_M%C3%B6lling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mölling was born in <a href="/wiki/Wuppertal" title="Wuppertal">Wuppertal</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a>, to Lothar Mölling and his first wife, Dagmar Sprenger-Fuchs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shz_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-shz-2">[2]</a></sup> She has three brothers, Brian, Kevin and Dustin, and a younger half sister named Melina.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ks_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ks-3">[3]</a></sup> After her parents divorce Mölling, her mother, and her siblings moved from <a href="/wiki/Remscheid" title="Remscheid">Remscheid</a> to <a href="/wiki/Koblenz" title="Koblenz">Koblenz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhineland-Palatinate" title="Rhineland-Palatinate">Rhineland-Palatinate</a>, where she finished <a href="/wiki/Secondary_school" title="Secondary school">secondary school</a> and later quit the <a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_(school)" title="Gymnasium (school)">gymnasium</a> to start an apprenticeship as a retail saleswoman at a <a href="/wiki/Jeans" title="Jeans">jeans</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Retailer" title="Retailer">retailer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ks_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-ks-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sandy_M%C3%B6lling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000.E2.80.942003:_Breakthrough_with_the_No_Angels">2000—2003: Breakthrough with the No Angels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sandy_M%C3%B6lling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2000—2003: Breakthrough with the No Angels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/No_Angels" title="No Angels">No Angels</a></div>

<p>In 2000, Mölling auditioned for the debut installment of the German reality television program <i><a href="/wiki/Popstars" title="Popstars">Popstars</a></i>, entering the competition with thousands of other women. She earned a position in the top thirty finalists and immediately travelled to <a href="/wiki/Majorca" title="Majorca">Majorca</a>, Spain to join her competitors for a workshop, where she made it to the final ten on the show despite of her struggle with dancing choreographies. During a special episode in November 2000, jury member Moslener eventually disclosed that Sandy was chosen to become part of the final five-member girl group <a href="/wiki/No_Angels" title="No Angels">No Angels</a>.</p>
<p>With the final five members of the band in place, <i>Popstars</i> continued tracking the development and struggles of the group who left homes to move into a shared flat near <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>. However, it took another four months until the band released their debut single "<a href="/wiki/Daylight_in_Your_Eyes" title="Daylight in Your Eyes">Daylight in Your Eyes</a>," which would subsequently appear on the band's debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Elle%27ments" title="Elle'ments">Elle'ments</a></i> (2001). Both the single and the album became an unexpected but record-breaking success, when both instantly entered the top position on the Austrian, German and Swiss <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Media_Control_Charts" title="Media Control Charts">Media Control</a></i> singles, albums and airplay charts, making the No Angels one of the most successful debuts in years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the following years, the No Angels released another two number-one studio albums, <i><a href="/wiki/Now_..._Us!" title="Now ... Us!">Now ... Us!</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Pure_(No_Angels_album)" title="Pure (No Angels album)">Pure</a></i>, a live album and a successful <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swing_(genre)" title="Swing (genre)">swing</a> album branded <i><a href="/wiki/When_the_Angels_Swing" title="When the Angels Swing">When the Angels Swing</a></i>, totalling twelve singles altogether - including four-number one singles. Eventually selling more than five million singles and albums worldwide, the No Angels became the best-selling <a href="/wiki/Girl_group" title="Girl group">German girl band</a> to date<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-presseportal_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-presseportal-1">[1]</a></sup> and the second most successful girl group of <a href="/wiki/Continental_Europe" title="Continental Europe">Continental Europe</a> between the years of 2001 and 2003 after <a href="/wiki/Atomic_Kitten" title="Atomic Kitten">Atomic Kitten</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-presseportal_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-presseportal-1">[1]</a></sup> On 5 September 2003 the four remaining members of the band (<a href="/wiki/Jessica_Wahls" title="Jessica Wahls">Jessica Wahls</a> had left the band following the birth of her first child in March 2003) announced that they would no longer be performing together after three years of continual touring and increasing cases of illness. The release of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Best_of_No_Angels" title="The Best of No Angels">The Best of No Angels</a></i> in November of the same year marked the end of the band, with each member going their separate ways in early 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-presseportal_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-presseportal-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004.E2.80.942006:_Career_development">2004—2006: Career development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sandy_M%C3%B6lling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2004—2006: Career development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mölling spend much of early 2004 with the recording of her solo debut album. After months of intensive recording sessions in Sweden and Great Britain Sandy released her first solo single "Unnatural Blonde," which became a Top 10 success and the highest charting single of all solo releases by all former band members. The summer-laid follow-up "Tell Me", a ballad, saw similar success in August of the same year, becoming another Top 10 hit.</p>

<p>Following the successful release of the singles, Mölling's full-length debut album <i>Unexpected</i> was released on 13 September 2004. It debuted at number 13 on the German albums chart. A third single, "Unexpected" (featuring German rapper Manuellsen), was released when Sandy was chosen to represent <a href="/wiki/Rhineland-Palatinate" title="Rhineland-Palatinate">Rhineland-Palatinate</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bundesvision_Song_Contest_2005" title="Bundesvision Song Contest 2005">Bundesvision Song Contest 2005</a>. The singer eventually finished last with 10 points, while the single entered a moderate number 29 on the German singles chart. Around the same time Sandy received an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Echo_(music_award)" title="Echo (music award)">ECHO</a> nomination for "Female Artist National (2005)".</p>
<p>After a few hosting jobs and a sudden label change Sandy released her minor succeeded second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Frame_of_Mind_(album)" title="Frame of Mind (album)">Frame of Mind</a></i> (#53), in May 2006 via <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">Starwatch Music</a>. The album's first single, "<a href="/wiki/Crash_(The_Primitives_song)" title="Crash (The Primitives song)">Crash</a>", was a cover version of the same-titled 1988 UK hit (released by <a href="/wiki/The_Primitives" title="The Primitives">The Primitives</a>) and peaked at number 24 on the German singles chart. Additionally Sandy was seen as a competitor in TV contest <i>Let's Dance</i>, eventually ending third. The album's second single, "Living without You" was released in November 2006 and entered the German single chart at number 36.The song was lyrically dedicated to her step father who died in early 2005.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.94present:_Reformation_of_the_band">2007—present: Reformation of the band</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sandy_M%C3%B6lling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2007—present: Reformation of the band">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In mid-2006, Mölling agreed upon former bandmate <a href="/wiki/Lucy_Diakovska" title="Lucy Diakovska">Lucy Diakovska</a>'s request to reunite for a musical comeback. However, all band members have affirmed that they will continue to pursue individual careers as solo artists at suitable opportunities. Sandy has also presented three <a href="/wiki/Television_program" title="Television program">television programes</a> on two different German networks, including <i>Autsch TV</i> on <a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Das_Vierte" title="de:Das Vierte">Das Vierte</a> and both <i>Du bist...</i> and <i>Damenwahl</i> on the music channel <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viva_(TV_station)" title="Viva (TV station)">VIVA</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sandy_M%C3%B6lling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sandy Molling has one son, Jayden Mölling (born 27 March 2009), by her long-time friend and manager Renick Bernadina, whom she split from in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Since 2009, she has been romantically involved with Canadian singer/songwriter <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nasri_Tony_Atweh" title="Nasri Tony Atweh">Nasri Tony Atweh</a>. The two met while working on the No Angels album Welcome to the Dance. In October 2015, she had her second son Noah.</p>
