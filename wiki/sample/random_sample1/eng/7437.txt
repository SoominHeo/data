<p>The <b>San Diego Gulls</b> were a professional <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> team in the <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_Hockey_League" title="West Coast Hockey League">West Coast Hockey League</a> (WCHL) and later in the <a href="/wiki/ECHL" title="ECHL">ECHL</a>. The team, the third to use the <i>Gulls</i> nickname, was founded in 1995 immediately upon the departure of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego_Gulls_(1990%E2%80%931995)" title="San Diego Gulls (1990–1995)">IHL team of the same name</a>. The Gulls played at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego_Sports_Arena" title="San Diego Sports Arena">San Diego Sports Arena</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Diego_Gulls_(1995%E2%80%932006)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Gulls were the dominant team throughout the WCHL's eight-year existence, winning the regular season championship five times. The Gulls never finished worse than second overall in WCHL league play, attaining the 100-point mark five times. The Gulls also won five of the league's Taylor Cup championships. No other WCHL team won the Taylor Cup more than once.</p>
<p>In 2003, the WCHL was absorbed by the <a href="/wiki/ECHL" title="ECHL">ECHL</a>, formerly known as the East Coast Hockey League. In 2004, the Gulls became the ECHL affiliate of the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Avalanche" title="Colorado Avalanche">Colorado Avalanche</a>. The Gulls won the ECHL regular season title in <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_ECHL_season" title="2003–04 ECHL season">2003–04</a> but missed the playoffs for the first and only time in their history the following year. In stark contrast to their WCHL playoff success, the Gulls failed to win a postseason series in three ECHL seasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Throughout their WCHL stint and through their first year of ECHL play, the Gulls were coached by <a href="/wiki/St._Cloud_State_University" title="St. Cloud State University">St. Cloud State University</a> (Minnesota) alumnus <a href="/wiki/Steve_Martinson" title="Steve Martinson">Steve Martinson</a>. Martinson left the Gulls and went on to coach the <a href="/wiki/Rockford_IceHogs" title="Rockford IceHogs">Rockford IceHogs</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Hockey_League" title="United Hockey League">UHL</a>. <a href="/wiki/Martin_St._Amour" title="Martin St. Amour">Martin St. Amour</a>, who was a star for the Gulls in their WCHL years replaced Martinson as head coach prior to the 2004–05 season. St. Amour stepped down in the middle of the 2005–06 season and was replaced by former Gulls players Jamie Black and B.J. MacPherson as co-coaches. The final starting goalie was Kevin Lentz. He went 20-1-1 in the final stretch of the regular season but retired due to a knee injury.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2006_folding">2006 folding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Diego_Gulls_(1995%E2%80%932006)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2006 folding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On of June 30, 2006, the Gulls folded and released their players as <a href="/wiki/Free_agent" title="Free agent">free agents</a> in 2006 following years of unprofitable seasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote>
<p>GULLS TO FORMALLY CEASE OPERATIONS FRIDAY – [ 06-29-2006] SAN DIEGO — The San Diego Gulls announced Thursday that they will formally cease operations as of Friday (June 30). Ongoing negotiations to sell the club did not materialize in a timeframe sufficient to operate for the 2006–07 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
