<p><b>San Clemente Island</b> (<b>SCI</b>) is the southernmost of the <a href="/wiki/Channel_Islands_of_California" title="Channel Islands of California">Channel Islands of California</a>. It is owned and operated by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and is a part of <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_County,_California" title="Los Angeles County, California">Los Angeles County</a>. Defined by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a> as <a href="/wiki/Census_block_group" title="Census block group">Block Group</a> 2 of <a href="/wiki/Census_tract" title="Census tract">Census Tract</a> 5991 of <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_County,_California" title="Los Angeles County, California">Los Angeles County, California</a>, it is 21 nautical miles (39 km) long and contains 147.13 km<sup>2</sup> (56.81 sq mi) of land. The island is officially <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uninhabited_island" title="Uninhabited island">uninhabited</a> as of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2000_U.S._Census" title="2000 U.S. Census">2000 U.S. Census</a>. It is estimated, however, that the number of military and civilian personnel present on the island at any given time is at least 300.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The city of <a href="/wiki/San_Clemente,_California" title="San Clemente, California">San Clemente</a> in <a href="/wiki/Orange_County,_California" title="Orange County, California">Orange County, California</a> is named after the island.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Clemente_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Clemente_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This region experiences warm (but not hot) and dry summers, with no average monthly temperatures above 71.6 °F. According to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_Climate_Classification" title="Köppen Climate Classification">Köppen Climate Classification</a> system, San Clemente Island has a <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_climate#Warm-summer_Mediterranean_climate" title="Mediterranean climate">warm-summer Mediterranean climate</a>, abbreviated "Csb" on climate maps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Clemente_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fauna_and_flora">Fauna and flora</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Clemente_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Fauna and flora">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Clemente_Island_loggerhead_shrike" title="San Clemente Island loggerhead shrike">San Clemente Island loggerhead shrike</a> is an <a href="/wiki/Endangered_species" title="Endangered species">endangered species</a> that the Navy is taking steps to protect. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Clemente_Island_fox" title="San Clemente Island fox">San Clemente Island fox</a> is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Endemic_(ecology)" title="Endemic (ecology)">indigenous</a> species. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feral" title="Feral">Feral</a> goats roamed the island for centuries, reaching a population of 11,000 in 1972, when their effect on indigenous species was realized. By 1980 the population had been reduced to 4,000. A plan for shooting remaining goats was blocked in court by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fund_for_Animals" title="Fund for Animals">Fund for Animals</a>, so the goats were removed with nets and helicopters. The <a href="/wiki/San_Clemente_Island_goat" title="San Clemente Island goat">San Clemente Island goat</a> is a recognized breed of domestic goat. The coves around the island are visited by snorkelers attracted by the abundant sea life, including <a href="/wiki/Sea_lion" title="Sea lion">sea lions</a>, <a href="/wiki/California_spiny_lobster" title="California spiny lobster">spiny lobsters</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydrocoral" title="Hydrocoral">hydrocoral</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kelp_forest" title="Kelp forest">kelp forests</a>. The Island is also home to the endangered <a class="external text" href="http://ecos.fws.gov/docs/five_year_review/doc2568.pdf" rel="nofollow"><i>San Clemente Island Sage Sparrow</i></a></p>
<p>The flora of the island includes some plant species found nowhere else in the world. These <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> species include the wildflowers <a href="/wiki/Brodiaea_kinkiensis" title="Brodiaea kinkiensis">San Clemente Island brodiaea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Triteleia_clementina" title="Triteleia clementina">San Clemente Island triteleia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lithophragma_maximum" title="Lithophragma maximum">San Clemente Island woodland star</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Castilleja_grisea" title="Castilleja grisea">San Clemente Island Indian paintbrush</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Shrub" title="Shrub">shrubs</a> <a href="/wiki/Malacothamnus_clementinus" title="Malacothamnus clementinus">San Clemente Island bushmallow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Munzothamnus" title="Munzothamnus">Blair's wirelettuce</a>. A unique subspecies of <a href="/wiki/Heteromeles" title="Heteromeles">toyon</a>, ssp. <i>macrocarpa</i>, also grows here,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> as do two rare subspecies of the <a href="/wiki/Delphinium_variegatum" title="Delphinium variegatum">royal larkspur</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earthworms" title="Earthworms">Earthworms</a> appear to have been introduced in 2008 in soil from the mainland used in a road construction project. In this earthworm-free region, the worms alter the soil and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Microbial_communities" title="Microbial communities">microbial communities</a> which allows nonnative plants to alter the island’s unique ecosystem and threaten <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a> that exists there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Clemente_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Archaeologists have found traces of human occupation on San Clemente Island dating back 10,000 years.</p>
<p>Later inhabitants left trade materials from the northern islands and from the mainland, including <a href="/wiki/Coso_Volcanic_Field" title="Coso Volcanic Field">Coso</a> <a href="/wiki/Obsidian" title="Obsidian">obsidian</a> from the California desert. It has not been established what tribe the recent inhabitants belonged to, although the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tongva_people" title="Tongva people">Tongva</a>, who are well attested from <a href="/wiki/Santa_Catalina_Island,_California" title="Santa Catalina Island, California">Santa Catalina Island</a>, are the most likely candidates. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chumash_(tribe)" title="Chumash (tribe)">Chumash</a>, who occupied the northern Channel Islands, may have influenced the inhabitants. Evidence of battles; 'the skeletons of dozens of men piled, one upon another' were also noted on San Clemente and <a href="/wiki/San_Nicolas_Island" title="San Nicolas Island">San Nicolas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first European to sight the island was <a href="/wiki/Juan_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Cabrillo" title="Juan Rodríguez Cabrillo">Juan Rodríguez Cabrillo</a> in 1542, who named it <i>Victoria</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kelsey_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kelsey-8">[8]</a></sup> It was renamed by Spanish explorer <a href="/wiki/Sebasti%C3%A1n_Vizca%C3%ADno" title="Sebastián Vizcaíno">Sebastián Vizcaíno</a>, who spotted it on November 23, 1602, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clement_I" title="Clement I">Saint Clement</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feast_day" title="Feast day">feast day</a>. It was used by ranchers, fishermen, and smugglers during the 19th century and into the 20th century.</p>
<p>In 1835, the <a href="/wiki/Whaler" title="Whaler">whaleship</a> <i>Elbe</i> of <a href="/wiki/Poughkeepsie,_New_York" title="Poughkeepsie, New York">Poughkeepsie, New York</a>, under Captain Josiah B. Whippey (or Whipple), hunted <a href="/wiki/Sperm_whale" title="Sperm whale">sperm whales</a> as far north as "St. Clements Island" (San Clemente Island).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The American steamship <i>Lansing</i>, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Schooner" title="Schooner">steam-schooner</a> <i>California</i>, both anchored in Pyramid Cove, on the south side of San Clemente Island, to process <a href="/wiki/Blue_whale" title="Blue whale">blue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fin_whale" title="Fin whale">fin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Humpback_whale" title="Humpback whale">humpback whales</a> caught by their "killer boats" (steam-driven whale catchers)—the former between 1926<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and 1930,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and the latter between 1933<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and 1937.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In 1935, the Norwegian factory ship <i>Esperanza</i> caught blue whales as far north as San Clemente Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Navy_base">Navy base</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Clemente_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Navy base">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The US Navy acquired the island in 1934. It is the Navy's only remaining ship-to-shore live firing range, and is the center of the integrated air/land/sea San Clemente Island Range Complex covering 2,620 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Square_nautical_mile" title="Square nautical mile">nm<sup>2</sup></a> (8,990 km<sup>2</sup>).</p>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the island was used as a training ground for amphibious landing craft. These small to mid-sized ships were crucial to the island hopping that would be required to attack the islands occupied by the Japanese.</p>
<p>It is an active <a href="/wiki/Sonar" title="Sonar">sonar</a> base and has a $21 million simulated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Embassy" title="Embassy">embassy</a> for <a href="/wiki/Commando" title="Commando">commando</a> training.</p>
<p>There is a US Navy rocket-test facility on San Clemente. Some <a href="/wiki/UGM-27_Polaris" title="UGM-27 Polaris">Polaris</a>-program test rockets were launched from San Clemente between 1957 and 1960. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SEALAB_(United_States_Navy)" title="SEALAB (United States Navy)">SEALAB III</a> project took place off San Clemente in February 1969.</p>
<p>The US Navy uses the island as an auxiliary naval airfield, <a href="/wiki/Naval_Auxiliary_Landing_Field_San_Clemente_Island" title="Naval Auxiliary Landing Field San Clemente Island">Naval Auxiliary Landing Field San Clemente Island</a>. The main runway 23/05 is used for carrier training by the Navy. Other branches also use this airfield, including the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a>. Pilots that use this airfield find it to be one of the most demanding airbases in the US, known for its high winds and dangerous terrain surrounding the runway.</p>
<p>The airfield is home to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy_SEALs" title="United States Navy SEALs">United States Navy SEALs</a> training facilities located north of the runways.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_and_infrastructure">Government and infrastructure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Clemente_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Government and infrastructure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_County_Sheriff%27s_Department" title="Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department">Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department</a> (LASD) operates the Avalon Station in <a href="/wiki/Avalon,_California" title="Avalon, California">Avalon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santa_Catalina_Island,_California" title="Santa Catalina Island, California">Santa Catalina Island</a>, serving San Clemente Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
