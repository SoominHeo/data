<p>The <b>Sanford Pentagon</b> (colloquially known as <b>The Pentagon</b>) is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indoor_arena" title="Indoor arena">indoor arena</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Sioux_Falls,_South_Dakota" title="Sioux Falls, South Dakota">Sioux Falls</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Dakota" title="South Dakota">South Dakota</a>. The Pentagon opened in September 2013 and has a seating capacity of 3,250 spectators. It hosts the <a href="/wiki/Sioux_Falls_Skyforce" title="Sioux Falls Skyforce">Sioux Falls Skyforce</a> of the <a href="/wiki/NBA_Development_League" title="NBA Development League">NBA Development League</a>. The facility is known for hosting a wide range of sporting events including: regional youth basketball tournaments, <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_I" title="NCAA Division I">NCAA Division I</a> basketball games, <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_II" title="NCAA Division II">NCAA Division II</a> basketball games and tournaments, <a href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_arts" title="Mixed martial arts">Mixed martial arts</a> events, and occasional concerts.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_events">Major events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanford_Pentagon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Major events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013">2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanford_Pentagon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Pentagon's first major event was hosted on October 10, 2013. The <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Timberwolves" title="Minnesota Timberwolves">Minnesota Timberwolves</a> played the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Bucks" title="Milwaukee Bucks">Milwaukee Bucks</a> in the main court as a preseason exhibition game, and television coverage was provided by <a href="/wiki/Fox_Sports_North" title="Fox Sports North">Fox Sports North</a>. The Wolves defeated the Bucks by a score of 98-89 in front of an announced crowd of 3,250.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On November 8, 2013, the Pentagon hosted its first <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_I" title="NCAA Division I">NCAA Division I</a> men's basketball game between the then 20th ranked <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin_Badgers" title="Wisconsin Badgers">Wisconsin Badgers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s_Red_Storm" title="St. John's Red Storm">St. John's Red Storm</a>. The game was televised on the <a href="/wiki/Big_Ten_Network" title="Big Ten Network">Big Ten Network</a>. Wisconsin won the game 86-75 in front of a sellout crowd of 3,523.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014">2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanford_Pentagon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2014">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 18, 2014, the Pentagon hosted an early season non-conference <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_I" title="NCAA Division I">NCAA Division I</a> basketball game between the then 11th ranked <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Wichita_State_Shockers_men%27s_basketball_team" title="2014–15 Wichita State Shockers men's basketball team">Wichita State Shockers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Memphis_Tigers_men%27s_basketball_team" title="2014–15 Memphis Tigers men's basketball team">Memphis Tigers</a>. The game was televised by <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a> as part of their Tip-Off Marathon programming. The Shockers won that game 71-56 in front of a sellout crowd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On November 30, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_South_Dakota_State_Jackrabbits_men%27s_basketball_team" title="2014–15 South Dakota State Jackrabbits men's basketball team">South Dakota State Jackrabbits</a> took on the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Florida_Gulf_Coast_Eagles_men%27s_basketball_team" title="2014–15 Florida Gulf Coast Eagles men's basketball team">Florida Gulf Coast Eagles</a>, winning 71-58. The announced attendance was 3,250 and coverage was provided by <a href="/wiki/Fox_Sports_North" title="Fox Sports North">Fox Sports North</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the third and final <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_I" title="NCAA Division I">NCAA Division I</a> basketball game of the 2014-15 season at the Sanford Pentagon, the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_UNLV_Runnin%27_Rebels_basketball_team" title="2014–15 UNLV Runnin' Rebels basketball team">UNLV Runnin' Rebels</a> played the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_South_Dakota_Coyotes_men%27s_basketball_team" title="2014–15 South Dakota Coyotes men's basketball team">South Dakota Coyotes</a> on December 13, 2014. UNLV won the game 75-61 and the game was televised by Midco Sports Network and <a href="/wiki/ESPN3" title="ESPN3">ESPN3</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015">2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanford_Pentagon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2015">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 16th, 2015, it was announced that the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Iowa_State_Cyclones_men%27s_basketball_team" title="2015–16 Iowa State Cyclones men's basketball team">Iowa State Cyclones</a> men's basketball team would play the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Colorado_Buffaloes_men%27s_basketball_team" title="2015–16 Colorado Buffaloes men's basketball team">Colorado Buffaloes</a> men's basketball team on November 13th, 2015, in an early season non-conference match-up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Annual.2FRecurring">Annual/Recurring</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanford_Pentagon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Annual/Recurring">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On December 11, 2013, it was announced that the Sanford Pentagon would be home to multiple <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_II" title="NCAA Division II">NCAA Division II</a> championships. These include the 2017 and 2018 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NCAA_Men%27s_Division_II_Basketball_Championship" title="NCAA Men's Division II Basketball Championship">NCAA Men's Division II Basketball Championships</a>, the 2015, 2016, and 2018 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NCAA_Women%27s_Division_II_Basketball_Championship" title="NCAA Women's Division II Basketball Championship">NCAA Women's Division II Basketball Championships</a> (except for the championship final in 2016, which will be held at <a href="/wiki/Bankers_Life_Fieldhouse" title="Bankers Life Fieldhouse">Bankers Life Fieldhouse</a> in <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis" title="Indianapolis">Indianapolis</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>), and the 2016 <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_II_Women%27s_Volleyball_Championship" title="NCAA Division II Women's Volleyball Championship">NCAA Division II Women's Volleyball Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Beginning in 2014, it is also home to the NSIC (<a href="/wiki/Northern_Sun_Intercollegiate_Conference" title="Northern Sun Intercollegiate Conference">Northern Sun Intercollegiate Conference</a>) conference basketball tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-register1_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-register1-1">[1]</a></sup> The NSIC is a <a href="/wiki/National_Collegiate_Athletic_Association" title="National Collegiate Athletic Association">NCAA</a> <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_II" title="NCAA Division II">Division II</a> conference with schools located mainly in Minnesota, South Dakota, and North Dakota.</p>
