<p><b>Salli Elise Richardson</b> (born November 23, 1967) is an <a href="/wiki/Americans" title="Americans">American</a> television and film actress. Richardson is known for her role as Angela on the 1994 hit comedy/action film <i><a href="/wiki/A_Low_Down_Dirty_Shame" title="A Low Down Dirty Shame">A Low Down Dirty Shame</a></i> and for her role as Dr. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allison_Blake" title="Allison Blake">Allison Blake</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Syfy" title="Syfy">Syfy</a> comedy-drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Eureka_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Eureka (U.S. TV series)">Eureka</a></i> (2006–2012).</p>
<p>She is also known for her voice acting as <a href="/wiki/Elisa_Maza" title="Elisa Maza">Elisa Maza</a> on the Disney animated series <i><a href="/wiki/Gargoyles_(TV_series)" title="Gargoyles (TV series)">Gargoyles</a></i> (1994–1996),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-1">[1]</a></sup> and as Viveca Foster on the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Family_Law_(TV_series)" title="Family Law (TV series)">Family Law</a></i> (1999-2002). Richardson also has appeared in a number of films such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Great_White_Hype" title="The Great White Hype">The Great White Hype</a></i> (1996), <i><a href="/wiki/Antwone_Fisher_(film)" title="Antwone Fisher (film)">Antwone Fisher</a></i> (2002), <i><a href="/wiki/Anacondas:_The_Hunt_for_the_Blood_Orchid" title="Anacondas: The Hunt for the Blood Orchid">Anacondas: The Hunt for the Blood Orchid</a></i> (2004) and <i><a href="/wiki/I_Am_Legend_(film)" title="I Am Legend (film)">I Am Legend</a></i> (2007). She had leading roles in the independent films <i><a href="/wiki/Pastor_Brown" title="Pastor Brown">Pastor Brown</a></i> (2009), <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Dynamite" title="Black Dynamite">Black Dynamite</a></i> (2009) and <i><a href="/wiki/I_Will_Follow_(film)" title="I Will Follow (film)">I Will Follow</a></i> (2010).</p>
<p>In August-September 2016, she made appearances as one of the co-hosts of the <i>Hollywood Today Live</i> daily show seating next to <a href="/wiki/Ross_Mathews" title="Ross Mathews">Ross Mathews</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salli_Richardson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Richardson-Whitfield was born in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>, the daughter of an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American" title="African American">African American</a>-<a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a> mother and a White father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> She has three brothers: Josh Richardson, Chad Richardson, and Nalin. Richardson played tennis in high school, and launched her acting career in the Kuumba Workshop theater there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-3">[3]</a></sup> She graduated from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Chicago_Laboratory_Schools" title="University of Chicago Laboratory Schools">University of Chicago Laboratory Schools</a> in 1985.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salli_Richardson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Richardson began her acting career in the theater before roles in television and film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-3">[3]</a></sup> In film, she played small roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Prelude_to_a_Kiss_(film)" title="Prelude to a Kiss (film)">Prelude to a Kiss</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mo%27_Money" title="Mo' Money">Mo' Money</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Posse_(1993_film)" title="Posse (1993 film)">Posse</a></i>, and later had major roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Sioux_City_(film)" title="Sioux City (film)">Sioux City</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/A_Low_Down_Dirty_Shame" title="A Low Down Dirty Shame">A Low Down Dirty Shame</a></i>. From 1994 to 1996 she had voice role of <a href="/wiki/Elisa_Maza" title="Elisa Maza">Elisa Maza</a> on the animated series <i><a href="/wiki/Gargoyles_(TV_series)" title="Gargoyles (TV series)">Gargoyles</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-1">[1]</a></sup> She had many guest-starring roles in numerous television shows, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Deep_Space_Nine" title="Star Trek: Deep Space Nine">Star Trek: Deep Space Nine</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Undercover" title="New York Undercover">New York Undercover</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pretender_(TV_series)" title="The Pretender (TV series)">The Pretender</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Stargate_SG-1" title="Stargate SG-1">Stargate SG-1</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/NYPD_Blue" title="NYPD Blue">NYPD Blue</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/House_(TV_series)" title="House (TV series)">House</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Bones_(TV_series)" title="Bones (TV series)">Bones</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Criminal_Minds" title="Criminal Minds">Criminal Minds</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/NCIS_(TV_series)" title="NCIS (TV series)">NCIS</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Castle_(TV_series)" title="Castle (TV series)">Castle</a></i>.</p>

<p>Richardson starred opposite <a href="/wiki/Dixie_Carter" title="Dixie Carter">Dixie Carter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kathleen_Quinlan" title="Kathleen Quinlan">Kathleen Quinlan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> legal drama series, <i><a href="/wiki/Family_Law_(TV_series)" title="Family Law (TV series)">Family Law</a></i>, from 1999 to 2002. She had recurring roles as Nancy Adams on <i><a href="/wiki/Rude_Awakening_(TV_series)" title="Rude Awakening (TV series)">Rude Awakening</a></i>, and as Laura on <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Miami" title="CSI: Miami">CSI: Miami</a></i>. She starred opposite <a href="/wiki/Denzel_Washington" title="Denzel Washington">Denzel Washington</a> in the 2002 drama film <i><a href="/wiki/Antwone_Fisher_(film)" title="Antwone Fisher (film)">Antwone Fisher</a></i>. She also appeared in the 2004 horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Anacondas:_The_Hunt_for_the_Blood_Orchid" title="Anacondas: The Hunt for the Blood Orchid">Anacondas: The Hunt for the Blood Orchid</a></i> and starred opposite <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">Will Smith</a> in 2007's post-apocalyptic film <i><a href="/wiki/I_Am_Legend_(film)" title="I Am Legend (film)">I Am Legend</a></i>. She later had a leading role in the independent dramas <i><a href="/wiki/Pastor_Brown" title="Pastor Brown">Pastor Brown</a></i> (2009) and <i><a href="/wiki/I_Will_Follow_(film)" title="I Will Follow (film)">I Will Follow</a></i> (2010). She also starred in the <i>The Sin Seer</i> with <a href="/wiki/Isaiah_Washington" title="Isaiah Washington">Isaiah Washington</a>, set for 2015 release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 2006 to 2012, Richardson starred as <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Defense" title="United States Department of Defense">Department of Defense</a> agent (and later head of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Global_Dynamics" title="Global Dynamics">Global Dynamics</a>) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allison_Blake" title="Allison Blake">Allison Blake</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Syfy" title="Syfy">Syfy</a> comedy-drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Eureka_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Eureka (U.S. TV series)">Eureka</a></i>. Her second pregnancy was written into the storyline of the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-3">[3]</a></sup> After the series' cancellation, she was cast as the lead in the <a href="/wiki/Lifetime_(TV_network)" title="Lifetime (TV network)">Lifetime</a> drama pilot <i>The Secret Lives of Wives</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2014, Richardson was cast in a recurring role on Lifetime's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lottery_(TV_series)" title="The Lottery (TV series)">The Lottery</a></i> as the first lady of the United States,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> but she lost it to Shelley Conn;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She also has a role on <a href="/wiki/BET" title="BET">BET</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Being_Mary_Jane" title="Being Mary Jane">Being Mary Jane</a></i> as an old friend of the lead character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2015, Richardson was cast in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABC_Family" title="ABC Family">ABC Family</a> series, <i><a href="/wiki/Stitchers" title="Stitchers">Stitchers</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salli_Richardson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On September 8, 2002, she married long-time boyfriend and fellow actor <a href="/wiki/Dondre_Whitfield" title="Dondre Whitfield">Dondre Whitfield</a>. She and Whitfield have one daughter, Parker Richardson-Whitfield, and one son, Dondre Terrell Whitfield Jr. (born January 24, 2009).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Whitfield has appeared along with her husband Dondre Whitfield in a late night infomercial as part of an advertising campaign to promote the <a href="/wiki/Time_Life" title="Time Life">Time Life</a> Zestify Midnight Soul collection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
