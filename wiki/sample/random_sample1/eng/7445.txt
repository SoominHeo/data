<p><b>Sally Blane</b> (born <b>Elizabeth Jane Young</b>; July 11, 1910 – August 27, 1997)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-highbeam_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-highbeam-1">[1]</a></sup> was an American actress. She appeared in over 70 movies.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sally_Blane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Blane was born in <a href="/wiki/Salida,_Colorado" title="Salida, Colorado">Salida, Colorado</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-highbeam_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-highbeam-1">[1]</a></sup> She was the sister of actresses <a href="/wiki/Polly_Ann_Young" title="Polly Ann Young">Polly Ann</a> and <a href="/wiki/Loretta_Young" title="Loretta Young">Loretta Young</a>, and half-sister of actress <a href="/wiki/Georgiana_Young" title="Georgiana Young">Georgiana Young</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sally_Blane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Blane had her film debut at the age of seven when she appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Sirens_of_the_Sea_(film)" title="Sirens of the Sea (film)">Sirens of the Sea</a></i> in 1917. She returned to the film business as an adult in the 1920s, playing small parts in a number of <a href="/wiki/Silent_film" title="Silent film">silent films</a>.</p>
<p>Her career continued into the 1930s when Blane appeared in a number of low-budget films, among them <i><a href="/wiki/Once_a_Sinner_(1931_film)" title="Once a Sinner (1931 film)">Once a Sinner</a></i> (1930), <i>A Dangerous Affair</i> (1930), <i>Arabian Knights</i> (1931), <i><a href="/wiki/Annabelle%27s_Affairs" title="Annabelle's Affairs">Annabelle's Affairs</a></i> (1931), <i>Hello Everybody!</i> (1933),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/City_Limits_(1934_film)" title="City Limits (1934 film)">City Limits</a></i> (1934), <i>Against the Law</i> (1934), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Silver_Streak" title="The Silver Streak">The Silver Streak</a></i> (1934), and <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_the_Life_(1935_film)" title="This Is the Life (1935 film)">This is the Life</a></i> (1935). Some of her scenes, including one in <i>Annabelle's Affairs</i>, in which she appeared in skimpy lingerie with <a href="/wiki/Jeanette_MacDonald" title="Jeanette MacDonald">Jeanette MacDonald</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joyce_Compton" title="Joyce Compton">Joyce Compton</a>, were quite risqué for their day, pre-dating the industry's <a href="/wiki/Motion_Picture_Production_Code" title="Motion Picture Production Code">Hays Code</a> that largely forbade such shots after 1934. The footage from <i>Annabelle's Affairs</i> is considered lost.</p>
<p>Although her appearances tapered off toward the late 1930s, Blane eventually appeared in over 100 films. She appeared onscreen at one time or another with all her sisters, for example with all three in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Story_of_Alexander_Graham_Bell" title="The Story of Alexander Graham Bell">The Story of Alexander Graham Bell</a></i> (1939). After that, Blane appeared in only four more movies in small supporting roles – <i>Fighting Mad</i> (1939), <i><a href="/wiki/Charlie_Chan_at_Treasure_Island" title="Charlie Chan at Treasure Island">Charlie Chan at Treasure Island</a></i> (1939), <i>La Fuga</i> (1944) and <i><a href="/wiki/A_Bullet_for_Joey" title="A Bullet for Joey">A Bullet for Joey</a></i> (1955).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sally_Blane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Blane married actor and director <a href="/wiki/Norman_Foster_(director)" title="Norman Foster (director)">Norman Foster</a> in October 1935. In June 1936, they had their first child, Gretchen, named after her sister <a href="/wiki/Loretta_Young" title="Loretta Young">Loretta Young</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> They also had a son named Robert.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sally_Blane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Blane died in <a href="/wiki/Palm_Springs,_California" title="Palm Springs, California">Palm Springs, California</a> on August 27, 1997, of <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">cancer</a> (as did her sisters Polly and Loretta) at the age of 87. Blane is interred in Culver City's <a href="/wiki/Holy_Cross_Cemetery,_Culver_City" title="Holy Cross Cemetery, Culver City">Holy Cross Cemetery</a>.</p>
