<p><b>Samuel Michael "Sam" Vokes</b> (born 21 October 1989) is a professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Striker_(association_football)" title="Striker (association football)">striker</a> for English club <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_football_team" title="Wales national football team">Wales national team</a>.</p>
<p>Vokes began his professional career at then League One <a href="/wiki/A.F.C._Bournemouth" title="A.F.C. Bournemouth">Bournemouth</a>, making his debut in 2006. His form there earned a move to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> side <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a>, where he helped them win promotion to the Premier League in his first season.</p>
<p>He has become an established member of the <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_football_team" title="Wales national football team">Welsh national team</a>, who he has also represented at <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_under-21_football_team" title="Wales national under-21 football team">under-21 level</a>. Born and raised in England, Vokes is eligible to represent Wales through a grandfather born there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bournemouth_Echo_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bournemouth_Echo-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Southampton" title="Southampton">Southampton</a> and raised in <a href="/wiki/Lymington" title="Lymington">Lymington</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Vokes stepped up into <a href="/wiki/A.F.C._Bournemouth" title="A.F.C. Bournemouth">Bournemouth's</a> first team on 5 December 2006 in a 2–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/Nottingham_Forest_F.C." title="Nottingham Forest F.C.">Nottingham Forest</a>. His first senior goal came in a 1–1 draw at <a href="/wiki/Gillingham_F.C." title="Gillingham F.C.">Gillingham</a> on 16 December. This form saw the club give him a three-and-a-half-year professional contract during the following month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The striker remained at Bournemouth during the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_in_English_football" title="2007–08 in English football">2007–08 season</a>, despite being linked to several other clubs, including <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Celtic_F.C." title="Celtic F.C.">Celtic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The youngster was a bright spark in an otherwise tumultuous season for the <i>Cherries</i> as they were embroiled in a club ownership saga. His 12-goal tally could not prevent Bournemouth from being relegated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Two" title="Football League Two">League Two</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wolves">Wolves</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sam_Vokes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Wolves">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He eventually signed a four-year contract with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> side <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a> on 23 May 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Vokes marked his debut for the club by coming off the bench to equalise in a 2–2 draw away to <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Argyle_F.C." title="Plymouth Argyle F.C.">Plymouth Argyle</a> on the opening day of the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_in_English_football" title="2008–09 in English football">2008–09 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>With the goalscoring form of <a href="/wiki/Chris_Iwelumo" title="Chris Iwelumo">Chris Iwelumo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sylvan_Ebanks-Blake" title="Sylvan Ebanks-Blake">Sylvan Ebanks-Blake</a> firing the club to the top of the table – that they led for almost the entire season – Vokes was mostly only employed as a substitute. Nonetheless he contributed a number of vital goals to help the team win promotion to the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> as champions.</p>
<p>To gain playing time, Vokes moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_One" title="Football League One">League One</a> side <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a> in October 2009 on loan until the New Year,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> making his Leeds debut in a 2–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City</a> on 19 October. He scored once for the club against <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Rovers_F.C." title="Bristol Rovers F.C.">Bristol Rovers</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> but competition between Leeds strikers meant Vokes returned to Wolves when the deal ended.</p>
<p>Vokes went out on loan once more in August 2010 when he moved to Championship side <a href="/wiki/Bristol_City_F.C." title="Bristol City F.C.">Bristol City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He endured a miserable stay, tearing his hamstring within minutes of making his debut in a game against <a href="/wiki/Millwall_F.C." title="Millwall F.C.">Millwall</a> and re-aggravating the injury in a reserve match in December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He returned to Wolves when his loan expired in January having played just 13 minutes of first team football.</p>
<p>Once again fit, he moved on a month-long loan to another Championship side, <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_United_F.C." title="Sheffield United F.C.">Sheffield United</a>, in February 2011<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and made his début the same evening in a 1–1 draw with <a href="/wiki/Reading_F.C." title="Reading F.C.">Reading</a>. With their other attacking options regaining fitness, the <i>Blades</i> elected not to extend Vokes' loan period,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> meaning he was instead signed up by promotion-chasing <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City</a> on 23 March on an emergency loan for the rest of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> However, an injury to Wolves' leading goalscorer <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Doyle" title="Kevin Doyle">Kevin Doyle</a> saw the club recall him after just four appearances for the <i>Canaries</i>, during which he scored once.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Back at <a href="/wiki/Molineux_Stadium" title="Molineux Stadium">Molineux</a>, he made two substitute appearances for the team during the run-in as they narrowly avoided relegation on the final day.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Premier_League" title="2011–12 Premier League">2011–12 season</a>, he made four Premier League appearances for Wolves, but did not start a league game. In November 2011 he once more went out on loan to a Championship side, this time joining Burnley until 15 January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 30 January 2012, Vokes joined Championship side <a href="/wiki/Brighton_%26_Hove_Albion_F.C." title="Brighton &amp; Hove Albion F.C.">Brighton &amp; Hove Albion</a> on loan until the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> He scored on his full home debut in a 2–2 draw with Millwall on 14 February 2012. He scored three goals in total during his 14 appearances for the Seagulls.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Burnley">Burnley</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sam_Vokes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Burnley">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 31 July 2012, Vokes joined Championship side <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a> permanently signing a three-year contract for an undisclosed fee, believed to be £350k.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On 2 December, Vokes scored an equalising goal for Burnley in the 89th-minute against fierce rivals <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> a game which ended 1–1. Despite this goal, Vokes struggled to establish himself in the first eleven and was used predominantly as a substitute. This was mainly down to the form of top scorer <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Austin" title="Charlie Austin">Charlie Austin</a>, with manager <a href="/wiki/Sean_Dyche" title="Sean Dyche">Sean Dyche</a> often opting to partner Austin with fellow strikers <a href="/wiki/Martin_Paterson" title="Martin Paterson">Martin Paterson</a> or <a href="/wiki/Danny_Ings" title="Danny Ings">Danny Ings</a>, both being quicker and more mobile than Vokes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the eve of the 2013–2014 Championship season, Burnley agreed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a> for the transfer of <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Austin" title="Charlie Austin">Charlie Austin</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> who had attracted lots of attention from Premier League teams throughout the summer. With Austin moving out of the club, this gave Vokes his long-awaited chance in the starting eleven, and he finished the season with 21 goals in 44 games.</p>
<p>On 8 August 2015, on the opening day of the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Football_League_Championship" title="2015–16 Football League Championship">2015–16 Championship</a> season, Vokes scored his first league goal since returning from a knee injury for Burnley against former club Leeds United in a 1–1 draw. He scored his first Premier League goal on 20 August 2016 at <a href="/wiki/Turf_Moor" title="Turf Moor">Turf Moor</a> in a 2–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sam_Vokes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Born and brought up in England, Vokes is eligible to represent Wales through a grandfather born there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bournemouth_Echo_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bournemouth_Echo-4">[4]</a></sup> Upon discovering Vokes' eligibility, Wales U21 coach <a href="/wiki/Brian_Flynn" title="Brian Flynn">Brian Flynn</a> called him up to the <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_under-21_football_team" title="Wales national under-21 football team">Welsh under-21 team</a>, and he made his debut against <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_national_under-21_football_team" title="Northern Ireland national under-21 football team">Northern Ireland</a> on 6 February 2007. The seventeen-year-old made an instant impression by scoring after 36 seconds, a team record, in a 4–0 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-debut_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-debut-25">[25]</a></sup> He remained in the team and featured in their <a href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship_qualification_Group_10" title="2009 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification Group 10">European U-21 Championship qualifying campaign</a>.</p>
<p>He was soon called up to the senior squad and made his debut in a 1–0 friendly win over <a href="/wiki/Iceland_national_football_team" title="Iceland national football team">Iceland</a> on 28 May 2008. Vokes scored his first senior international goal when he netted the winner in a 1–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan_national_football_team" title="Azerbaijan national football team">Azerbaijan</a> in their opening <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_%E2%80%93_UEFA_Group_4" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group 4">2010 World Cup qualifier</a> on 6 September 2008; his second international goal came in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/Montenegro_national_football_team" title="Montenegro national football team">Montenegro</a> on 12 August 2009. In November 2011 Vokes came on as an 85th-minute substitute and scored two goals in a 4–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_football_team" title="Norway national football team">Norway</a>. In February 2013 he scored one goal against <a href="/wiki/Austria_national_football_team" title="Austria national football team">Austria.</a> Just over a year later he scored again in March 2014, against Iceland.</p>
<p>In May 2016, Vokes was listed for the 29-man squad for a pre-<a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">Euro 2016</a> training camp,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> and was retained for the tournament final squad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> On 1 July 2016, Vokes came on as a substitute and scored for Wales in a 3–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_football_team" title="Belgium national football team">Belgium</a> at the finals, sealing the victory to take Wales to their first ever semi-final appearance at a major tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wales_3.E2.80.931_Belgium_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wales_3.E2.80.931_Belgium-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
