<p><b>Saint-Dizier</b> (<small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[sɛ̃.di.zje]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_France" title="Communes of France">commune</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Haute-Marne" title="Haute-Marne">Haute-Marne</a> <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_France" title="Departments of France">department</a> in north-eastern <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.</p>
<p>It has a population of 31,000 (2003 figure) and is a <a href="/wiki/Subprefectures_in_France" title="Subprefectures in France">subprefecture</a> of the department. Although Saint-Dizier is marginally the most populous commune in Haute-Marne, the <i>préfecture</i> (capital) resides in the somewhat smaller commune of <a href="/wiki/Chaumont,_Haute-Marne" title="Chaumont, Haute-Marne">Chaumont</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint-Dizier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Named after an unknown saint (possibly <a href="/wiki/Desiderius_of_Fontenelle" title="Desiderius of Fontenelle">Desiderius of Fontenelle</a>), the town originated as a fortified settlement around a thirteenth-century <a href="/wiki/Ch%C3%A2teau" title="Château">château</a>, eventually becoming a royal fortress to guard the French kingdom's eastern approaches. The town was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siege_of_St._Dizier_(1544)" title="Siege of St. Dizier (1544)">besieged</a> and captured by <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor</a>, in the summer of 1544. A fire in 1775 destroyed two-thirds of the town centre. The château was owned by the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Orl%C3%A9ans" title="House of Orléans">Orléans family</a> until the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>, was a base for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wehrmacht_Heer" title="Wehrmacht Heer">German troops</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, and currently houses the Municipal Museum.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people">Notable people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint-Dizier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notable people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Saint-Dizier is the birthplace of</p>
<ul>
<li>Baroque-era musicologist <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Pirro" title="André Pirro">André Pirro</a></li>
<li>Organist <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Isoir" title="André Isoir">André Isoir</a></li>
<li>Conductor <a href="/wiki/Jean-Paul_Penin" title="Jean-Paul Penin">Jean-Paul Penin</a></li>
<li>Former world middleweight boxing champion <a href="/wiki/Marcel_Thil" title="Marcel Thil">Marcel Thil</a>. A street is named in his honor.</li>
</ul>
