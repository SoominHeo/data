<p>The <b>Treaty of Saint-Germain-en-Laye</b> was signed on 10 September 1919 by the victorious <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_I" title="Allies of World War I">Allies of World War I</a> on the one hand and by the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_German-Austria" title="Republic of German-Austria">Republic of German-Austria</a> on the other. Like the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Trianon" title="Treaty of Trianon">Treaty of Trianon</a> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary_(1920%E2%80%931946)" title="Kingdom of Hungary (1920–1946)">Hungary</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Versailles" title="Treaty of Versailles">Treaty of Versailles</a> with <a href="/wiki/Weimar_Republic" title="Weimar Republic">Germany</a>, it contained the Covenant of the <a href="/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a> and as a result was not ratified by the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> but was followed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US%E2%80%93Austrian_Peace_Treaty_(1921)" title="US–Austrian Peace Treaty (1921)">US–Austrian Peace Treaty</a> of 1921.</p>
<p>The treaty signing ceremony took place at the <a href="/wiki/Ch%C3%A2teau_de_Saint-Germain-en-Laye" title="Château de Saint-Germain-en-Laye">Château de Saint-Germain-en-Laye</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treaty_of_Saint-Germain-en-Laye_(1919)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Already on 21 October 1918, 208 German-speaking delegates of the Austrian <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Council_(Austria)" title="Imperial Council (Austria)">Imperial Council</a> had convened in a "provisional national assembly of German-Austria " at the <a href="/wiki/Palais_Nieder%C3%B6sterreich" title="Palais Niederösterreich">Lower Austrian Landtag</a>. While the collapse of the <a href="/wiki/Austro-Hungarian_Army" title="Austro-Hungarian Army">Austro-Hungarian Army</a> culminated at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Vittorio_Veneto" title="Battle of Vittorio Veneto">Battle of Vittorio Veneto</a>, the Social Democrat <a href="/wiki/Karl_Renner" title="Karl Renner">Karl Renner</a> was elected German-Austrian State Chancellor on 30 October. In the course of the <a href="/wiki/Aster_Revolution" title="Aster Revolution">Aster Revolution</a> on 31 October, the newly established <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_Hungary" title="Democratic Republic of Hungary">Democratic Republic of Hungary</a> under Minister President <a href="/wiki/Mih%C3%A1ly_K%C3%A1rolyi" title="Mihály Károlyi">Mihály Károlyi</a> declared the <a href="/wiki/Real_union" title="Real union">real union</a> with Austria terminated.</p>
<p>With the <a href="/wiki/Armistice_of_Villa_Giusti" title="Armistice of Villa Giusti">Armistice of Villa Giusti</a> on 3 November 1918, the fate of the <a href="/wiki/Austria-Hungary" title="Austria-Hungary">Austro-Hungarian Monarchy</a> was sealed. On 11 November 1918 Emperor <a href="/wiki/Charles_I_of_Austria" title="Charles I of Austria">Charles I of Austria</a> officially declared to "relinquish every participation in the administration", one day later the provisional assembly declared German-Austria a democratic republic and part of the German Republic. However, on the territory of the <a href="/wiki/Cisleithania" title="Cisleithania">Cisleithanian</a> ("Austrian") half of the former empire, the newly established states of <a href="/wiki/First_Czechoslovak_Republic" title="First Czechoslovak Republic">Czechoslovakia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Second_Polish_Republic" title="Second Polish Republic">Poland</a>, and the Yugoslav <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Yugoslavia" title="Kingdom of Yugoslavia">Kingdom of Serbs, Croats and Slovenes</a> (the "successor states") had been proclaimed. Moreover, <a href="/wiki/South_Tyrol" title="South Tyrol">South Tyrol</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trentino" title="Trentino">Trentino</a> were occupied by <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy" title="Kingdom of Italy">Italian</a> forces and Yugoslav troops entered the former <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Carinthia" title="Duchy of Carinthia">Duchy of Carinthia</a>, leading to violent fights.</p>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Constitutional_Assembly_election,_1919" title="Austrian Constitutional Assembly election, 1919">Austrian Constitutional Assembly election</a> was held on 16 February 1919. The Assembly re-elected Karl Renner state chancellor and enacted the <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Law" title="Habsburg Law">Habsburg Law</a> concerning the banishment of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Lorraine" title="House of Lorraine">House of Lorraine</a>. When Chancellor Renner arrived at Saint-Germain in May 1919, he and the Austrian delegation found themselves excluded from the negotiations led by French Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Georges_Clemenceau" title="Georges Clemenceau">Georges Clemenceau</a>. Upon an Allied ultimatum, Renner signed the treaty on 10 September. The Treaty of Trianon in June 1920 between Hungary and the Allies completed the disposition of the former Dual Monarchy.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Provisions">Provisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treaty_of_Saint-Germain-en-Laye_(1919)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Provisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The treaty declared that the Austro-Hungarian Empire was to be dissolved. According to article 177, the Austrian side accepted responsibility for causing the war along with the <a href="/wiki/Central_Powers" title="Central Powers">Central Powers</a>. The new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_Republic_of_Austria" title="First Republic of Austria">Republic of Austria</a>, consisting of most of the German-speaking Danubian and Alpine provinces in former <a href="/wiki/Cisleithania" title="Cisleithania">Cisleithania</a>, recognized the independence of Hungary, Czechoslovakia, Poland, and the Kingdom of Serbs, Croats and Slovenes. The treaty included 'war reparations' of large sums of money, directed towards the Allies.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Territory">Territory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treaty_of_Saint-Germain-en-Laye_(1919)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Territory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cisleithanian Austria had to face significant territorial losses, amounting to over 60 percent of the prewar Austrian Empire's territory:</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dissolution_of_Austria-Hungary.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="970" data-file-width="1568" height="173" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Dissolution_of_Austria-Hungary.png/280px-Dissolution_of_Austria-Hungary.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Dissolution_of_Austria-Hungary.png/420px-Dissolution_of_Austria-Hungary.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Dissolution_of_Austria-Hungary.png/560px-Dissolution_of_Austria-Hungary.png 2x" width="280"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dissolution_of_Austria-Hungary.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Dissolution of Austria-Hungary</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Lands_of_the_Bohemian_Crown" title="Lands of the Bohemian Crown">Lands of the Bohemian Crown</a>, i.e. the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bohemia" title="Kingdom of Bohemia">Bohemia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Moravia" title="Moravia">Moravia</a> crownlands (including small adjacent <a href="/wiki/Lower_Austria" title="Lower Austria">Lower Austrian</a> territories around <a href="/wiki/Valtice" title="Valtice">Feldsberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C4%8Cesk%C3%A9_Velenice" title="České Velenice">Gmünd</a>) formed the core of the newly created state of Czechoslovakia. The <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Silesia" title="Austrian Silesia">Austrian Silesia</a> province upon the <a href="/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Czechoslovak_War" title="Polish–Czechoslovak War">Polish–Czechoslovak War</a> of January 1919 was split between <a href="/wiki/Czech_Silesia" title="Czech Silesia">Czech Silesia</a> and Polish <a href="/wiki/Cieszyn_Silesia" title="Cieszyn Silesia">Cieszyn Silesia</a> incorporated into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silesian_Voivodeship_(1920%E2%80%931939)" title="Silesian Voivodeship (1920–1939)">Silesian Voivodeship</a>. These cessions concerned a large <a href="/wiki/Sudeten_Germans" title="Sudeten Germans">German</a>-speaking population in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_Bohemia" title="German Bohemia">German Bohemia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sudetenland" title="Sudetenland">Sudetenland</a>.</li>
<li>The former <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Galicia_and_Lodomeria" title="Kingdom of Galicia and Lodomeria">Kingdom of Galicia and Lodomeria</a>, made up of the territory the <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Monarchy" title="Habsburg Monarchy">Habsburg Monarchy</a> had annexed in the 1772 <a href="/wiki/First_Partition_of_Poland" title="First Partition of Poland">First Partition of Poland</a>, fell back to the re-established Polish Republic.</li>
<li>The adjacent <a href="/wiki/Bukovina" title="Bukovina">Bukovina</a> in the east passed to the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Romania" title="Kingdom of Romania">Kingdom of Romania</a>.</li>
<li>The southern half of the former <a href="/wiki/County_of_Tyrol" title="County of Tyrol">Tyrolean</a> crownland up to the <a href="/wiki/Brenner_Pass" title="Brenner Pass">Brenner Pass</a>, including predominantly German-speaking South Tyrol and the present-day Trentino province, together with the Carinthian Canal Valley around <a href="/wiki/Tarvisio" title="Tarvisio">Tarvisio</a> fell to Italy, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Littoral" title="Austrian Littoral">Austrian Littoral</a> (<a href="/wiki/Gorizia_and_Gradisca" title="Gorizia and Gradisca">Gorizia and Gradisca</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Free_City_of_Trieste" title="Imperial Free City of Trieste">Imperial Free City of Trieste</a> and the <a href="/wiki/March_of_Istria" title="March of Istria">March of Istria</a>) and several <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Dalmatia" title="Kingdom of Dalmatia">Dalmatian</a> islands, as stipulated by the 1915 <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_London_(1915)" title="Treaty of London (1915)">London Pact</a>.</li>
<li>The main part of the former <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Dalmatia" title="Kingdom of Dalmatia">Kingdom of Dalmatia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Carniola" title="Duchy of Carniola">Duchy of Carniola</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lower_Styria" title="Lower Styria">Lower Styria</a> with the Carinthian <a href="/wiki/Me%C5%BEa" title="Meža">Meža</a> Valley and <a href="/wiki/Municipality_of_Jezersko" title="Municipality of Jezersko">Jezersko</a> was ceded to the Yugoslav Kingdom of Serbs, Croats and Slovenes, contrary to what was stipulated by the 1915 London Pact.</li>
<li>Austria-Hungary's only overseas possession, its <a href="/wiki/Concessions_in_Tianjin#Austro-Hungarian_concession_.281901.E2.80.931917.29" title="Concessions in Tianjin">concession</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tianjin" title="Tianjin">Tianjin</a>, was turned over to <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_(1912%E2%80%9349)" title="Republic of China (1912–49)">China</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p><a href="/wiki/Burgenland" title="Burgenland">Burgenland</a>, i.e. the predominantly German-speaking western parts of the Hungarian <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Hungary" title="Counties of Hungary">counties</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mosonmagyar%C3%B3v%C3%A1r" title="Mosonmagyaróvár">Moson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sopron" title="Sopron">Sopron</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vas_County" title="Vas County">Vas</a>, were awarded to Austria. The affiliation of the Southern Carinthian territory with its <a href="/wiki/Carinthian_Slovenes" title="Carinthian Slovenes">Slovene</a>-speaking share of population was to be decided in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carinthian_Plebiscite" title="Carinthian Plebiscite">Carinthian Plebiscite</a>.</p>
<p>In nearly all of these cases, the Allies not only assumed without question that the minority peoples wanted to leave Austria, but allowed the successor states to absorb significant blocks of German-speaking territory.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics_and_military">Politics and military</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treaty_of_Saint-Germain-en-Laye_(1919)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Politics and military">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Article 88 of the treaty required Austria to refrain from directly or indirectly compromising its independence, which meant that Austria could not enter into political or economic union with the <a href="/wiki/German_Reich" title="German Reich">German Reich</a> without the agreement of the council of the <a href="/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a>. Accordingly, the new republic's initial self-chosen name of German-Austria (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Deutschösterreich</i></span>) had to be changed to Austria. Many Austrians would come to find this term harsh (especially among the Austrian Germans who were <a href="/wiki/Pan-Germanism" title="Pan-Germanism">German nationalists</a> of the former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Austro-Hungarian_Empire" title="Austro-Hungarian Empire">Austro-Hungarian Empire</a>), due to Austria's later economic weakness, which was caused by loss of land. The economic weakness of Austria would later lead to support for the idea of <i><a href="/wiki/Anschluss" title="Anschluss">Anschluss</a></i> (political union) with Nazi Germany.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Conscription" title="Conscription">Conscription</a> was abolished and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Austrian_Army" title="Austrian Army">Austrian Army</a> was limited to a force of 30,000 volunteers. There were numerous provisions dealing with <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danubian</a> navigation, the transfer of railways, and other details involved in the breakup of a great empire into several small independent states.</p>
<p>The vast reduction of population, territory and resources of the new Austria relative to the old empire wreaked havoc on the economy of the old nation, most notably in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>, an imperial capital now without an empire to support it. For a time, the country's very unity was called into question. Unlike its former Hungarian partner, Austria had never been a nation in the true sense of the word. While the Austrian state had existed in one form or another for over 700 years, it had no unifying force other than loyalty to the Habsburgs.</p>
