<p>The <b>AK-101</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Assault_rifle" title="Assault rifle">assault rifle</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Kalashnikov" title="Mikhail Kalashnikov">Kalashnikov</a> series. The AK-101 is designed for the world export market, using <a href="/wiki/5.56%C3%9745mm_NATO" title="5.56×45mm NATO">5.56×45mm NATO</a> cartridges, which is the standard of most <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> armies. The AK-101 is marketed at those looking for a weapon that combines the logistical compatibility and familiarity of the 5.56×45mm NATO round with the reliability of a Kalashnikov. It is designed with modern and composite materials, including <a href="/wiki/Plastic" title="Plastic">plastics</a> that reduce weight and improve accuracy. Many of the improvements found in the AK-101 are also present in the <a href="/wiki/AK-103" title="AK-103">AK-103</a> and the rest of the AK-10x series of rifles.</p>
<p>A common misconception is that the AK-101 has entered service as the main assault rifle of the <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian Federation</a>, but this is not true; the <a href="/wiki/AK-74" title="AK-74">AK-74M</a> is still the main assault rifle with the AK-105 being introduced alongside, both chambered for <a href="/wiki/5.45%C3%9739mm" title="5.45×39mm">5.45×39mm</a>. The <a href="/wiki/AKM" title="AKM">AKM</a> firing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/7.62_mm_Soviet" title="7.62 mm Soviet">7.62 mm Soviet</a> rounds is in limited service with selected units in the Russian army, and the <a href="/wiki/AK-103" title="AK-103">AK-103</a> is in service with some Russian civil police agencies and spec ops units. The advanced <a href="/wiki/AN-94" title="AN-94">AN-94</a> is entering limited service in the elite forces of the Russian military, some Russian police forces, and the Ministry of Internal Affairs.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AK-101&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The AK-101 is a <a href="/wiki/Selective_fire" title="Selective fire">selective fire</a> weapon that can be fired in either semi-automatic or fully automatic mode. The disassembly procedure for the AK-101 is identical to that of the <a href="/wiki/AK-74" title="AK-74">AK-74</a>. The AK-101 has an attachment rail installed on the side of the receiver for mounting scopes and other optical sights, which will accept most types of Russian and European AK optics. The rifle accepts most synthetic and metal AK-74-style magazines with 30-round capacity. The AK-101 has a 415 mm (16.3 in) barrel with an AK-74 style <a href="/wiki/Muzzle_brake" title="Muzzle brake">muzzle brake</a> attached to the barrel to control muzzle climb.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AK-102_assault_rifle" title="AK-102 assault rifle">AK-102</a>, <a href="/wiki/AK-104" title="AK-104">AK-104</a> and <a href="/wiki/AK-105" title="AK-105">AK-105</a> are the designations given to the more compact <a href="/wiki/Carbine" title="Carbine">carbine</a> variants of the AK-10x rifle series, firing the <a href="/wiki/5.56%C3%9745mm_NATO" title="5.56×45mm NATO">5.56×45mm NATO</a>, <a href="/wiki/7.62%C3%9739mm" title="7.62×39mm">7.62×39mm</a> M43 and <a href="/wiki/5.45%C3%9739mm" title="5.45×39mm">5.45×39mm</a> M74 rifle rounds respectively. These carbines differ from the normal rifles of the series in that they have much shorter barrels, only 314 mm (12.4 in) in length. These AK-10x carbines, much like their rifle counterparts, were made primarily for export.</p>
<p>The AK-101 is chambered in 5.56mm NATO and features a black glass-filled <a href="/wiki/Polyamide" title="Polyamide">polyamide</a> side folding <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stock_(firearm)" title="Stock (firearm)">stock</a>. The side folding stock looks similar to a normal fixed stock, but folds and locks securely to the left side of the <a href="/wiki/Receiver_(firearms)" title="Receiver (firearms)">receiver</a>. It has a cutout to compensate for the side rail.</p>
