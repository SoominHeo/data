<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Treaty of Saint-Clair-sur-Epte</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>Treaty of Saint-Clair-sur-Epte</b>, between <a href="/wiki/Charles_the_Simple" title="Charles the Simple">Charles the Simple</a> (King Charles III of France) and <a href="/wiki/Rollo" title="Rollo">Rollo</a>, the leader of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viking" title="Viking">Vikings</a>, was signed<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> in autumn 911. The treaty permitted the <a href="/wiki/Normans" title="Normans">Normans</a> to settle in <a href="/wiki/Neustria" title="Neustria">Neustria</a> in return for their protection of Charles' kingdom from any new invasion by the "northmen". No written records survive concerning the creation of the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Normandy" title="Duchy of Normandy">Duchy of Normandy</a>.</p>
<p>In 911, a group of Vikings led by <a href="/wiki/Rollo" title="Rollo">Rollo</a> attacked <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> before laying <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Chartres_(911)" title="Siege of Chartres (911)">siege to Chartres</a>. Appeals for help from the Bishop of Chartres, Joseaume, were answered by Robert, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marquis" title="Marquis">Marquis</a> of Neustria, <a href="/wiki/Richard,_Duke_of_Burgundy" title="Richard, Duke of Burgundy">Richard, Duke of Burgundy</a> and Manasses, Count of <a href="/wiki/Dijon" title="Dijon">Dijon</a>. On 20 July 911, at the Battle of Chartres, they defeated Rollo despite the absence of many French barons and of Charles the Simple.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> After the Frankish victory near Chartres on 26 August, Charles decided to negotiate with Rollo. The talks, led by Herv√©, the Archbishop of Reims, resulted in the Treaty of Saint-Clair-sur-Epte. The treaty granted Rollo and his soldiers all the land between the river <a href="/wiki/Epte" title="Epte">Epte</a> and the sea "in freehold and good money". In addition, it granted him <a href="/wiki/Brittany" title="Brittany">Brittany</a> "for his livelihood"<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (April 2014)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. At the time, Brittany was an independent country which France had unsuccessfully tried to conquer. In exchange, Rollo guaranteed the king his loyalty, which involved military assistance for the protection of the kingdom. As a token of his goodwill, Rollo also agreed to be <a href="/wiki/Baptism" title="Baptism">baptised</a> and to marry <a href="/wiki/Gisela_of_France" title="Gisela of France">Gisela</a>, a presumed legitimate daughter of Charles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The territory covered by the treaty corresponds to the northern part of today's <a href="/wiki/Upper_Normandy" title="Upper Normandy">Upper Normandy</a> down to the <a href="/wiki/Seine" title="Seine">Seine</a>, but the territory of the Vikings would eventually extend west beyond the Seine to form the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Normandy" title="Duchy of Normandy">Duchy of Normandy</a>, so named because of the <a href="/wiki/Norsemen" title="Norsemen">Norsemen</a> who ruled it.</p>
<p>The treaty was entered into<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (April 2014)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> after the death of <a href="/wiki/Alan_I,_King_of_Brittany" title="Alan I, King of Brittany">Alan I, King of Brittany</a> and while another group of Vikings occupied Brittany. Around 937, Alan I's son <a href="/wiki/Alan_II,_Duke_of_Brittany" title="Alan II, Duke of Brittany">Alan II</a> returned from England to expel those Vikings from Brittany in a war that was concluded in 939. During this period the <a href="/wiki/Cotentin_Peninsula" title="Cotentin Peninsula">Cotentin Peninsula</a> was lost by Brittany and gained by Normandy.</p>
