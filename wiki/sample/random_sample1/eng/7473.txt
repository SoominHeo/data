<p><b>Shania Twain</b>, <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Canada" title="Order of Canada">OC</a> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ʃ/ 'sh' in 'shy'">ʃ</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span><span title="/aɪ/ long 'i' in 'tide'">aɪ</span><span title="/./ syllable break">.</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span> <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="'w' in 'wind'">w</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born <b>Eilleen Regina Edwards</b>; August 28, 1965) is a Canadian singer and songwriter. With nearly 25 years in the music industry, Twain has sold over 85 million records, making her the best-selling female artist in the history of country music and one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_best-selling_music_artists" title="List of best-selling music artists">best-selling artists of all time</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Her success garnered her several honorific titles including the "<a href="/wiki/Honorific_nicknames_in_popular_music" title="Honorific nicknames in popular music">Queen of Country Pop</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Twain's second album, 1995's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Woman_in_Me_(Shania_Twain_album)" title="The Woman in Me (Shania Twain album)">The Woman in Me</a></i>, brought her widespread success selling 20 million copies worldwide, spawning hits such as "<a href="/wiki/Any_Man_of_Mine" title="Any Man of Mine">Any Man of Mine</a>" and earning her a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a>. Twain's third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Come_On_Over" title="Come On Over">Come On Over</a></i>, became the <a href="/wiki/List_of_best-selling_albums" title="List of best-selling albums">best-selling studio album</a> of all time by a female act in any genre and the best-selling country album, selling around 40 million copies worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-News124_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-News124-5">[5]</a></sup> <i>Come On Over</i> produced several singles, including "<a href="/wiki/You%27re_Still_the_One" title="You're Still the One">You're Still the One</a>", "<a href="/wiki/From_This_Moment_On_(Shania_Twain_song)" title="From This Moment On (Shania Twain song)">From This Moment On</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Man!_I_Feel_Like_a_Woman!" title="Man! I Feel Like a Woman!">Man! I Feel Like a Woman!</a>", and earned Twain four Grammy Awards. Her fourth and latest studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Up!_(album)" title="Up! (album)">Up!</a></i>, was released in 2002 and, like her previous two albums, was also <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">certified Diamond</a> in the U.S., spawning hits like "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_Gonna_Getcha_Good!" title="I'm Gonna Getcha Good!">I'm Gonna Getcha Good!</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Forever_and_for_Always" title="Forever and for Always">Forever and for Always</a>".</p>
<p>Twain has received five <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Awards</a>, 27 <a href="/wiki/Broadcast_Music,_Inc." title="Broadcast Music, Inc.">BMI</a> Songwriter awards, stars on <a href="/wiki/Canada%27s_Walk_of_Fame" title="Canada's Walk of Fame">Canada's Walk of Fame</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Walk_of_Fame" title="Hollywood Walk of Fame">Hollywood Walk of Fame</a>, and an induction into the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Music_Hall_of_Fame" title="Canadian Music Hall of Fame">Canadian Music Hall of Fame</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> She is the only female artist in history to have three consecutive albums <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">certified Diamond</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>. Altogether, Twain is ranked as the 10th best-selling artist of the <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_SoundScan" title="Nielsen SoundScan">Nielsen SoundScan</a> era.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_b_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_b-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2004, Twain retired from performing and retreated to her home in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In her 2011 autobiography, she cited a weakening singing voice as the reason for not performing publicly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> When both her singing and speaking were affected, Twain consulted the <a href="/wiki/Vanderbilt_University_Medical_Center" title="Vanderbilt University Medical Center">Vanderbilt Dayani Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville</a>. Specialists discovered lesions on her vocal cords and diagnosed her with <a href="/wiki/Dysphonia" title="Dysphonia">dysphonia</a>, all treatable with careful rehabilitation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2012, Twain returned to the concert stage in her critically acclaimed show <i><a href="/wiki/Shania:_Still_the_One" title="Shania: Still the One">Still the One</a></i>, exclusively at <a href="/wiki/The_Colosseum_at_Caesars_Palace" title="The Colosseum at Caesars Palace">The Colosseum at Caesars Palace</a>. In 2015, Twain returned to the road for what she has billed as her farewell tour. The <i><a href="/wiki/Rock_This_Country_Tour" title="Rock This Country Tour">Rock This Country</a></i> tour kicked off on June 5, 2015 in Seattle, Washington and originally concluded in Fresno, California on August 23<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> but was extended to include additional dates and ended October 27, 2015 in <a href="/wiki/Kelowna" title="Kelowna">Kelowna</a>, British Columbia, Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shania_Twain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Twain was born Eilleen Regina Edwards in <a href="/wiki/Windsor,_Ontario" title="Windsor, Ontario">Windsor, Ontario</a> on August 28, 1965,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> to Sharon (<i>née</i> Morrison) and Clarence Edwards. Her parents divorced when she was two and her mother then moved to <a href="/wiki/Timmins" title="Timmins">Timmins</a>, Ontario with Eilleen and her sisters Jill and Carrie Ann. Sharon married Jerry Twain, an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ojibwa" title="Ojibwa">Ojibwa</a> from the nearby <a href="/wiki/Mattagami_First_Nation" title="Mattagami First Nation">Mattagami First Nation</a>, and they had a son together, Mark. Jerry adopted the girls, legally changing their last name to Twain. When Mark was still a toddler, the Twains adopted Jerry's baby nephew, Darryl, after Darryl's mother died. Because of her connection to her adoptive father, in the past it has been reported that Twain's ancestry was Ojibwe,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> but she stated in an interview that her biological father was part <a href="/wiki/Cree" title="Cree">Cree</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-youtube-15">[15]</a></sup> She also has <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_people" title="French people">French</a>, and <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> ancestry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Through a maternal great-grandmother, she is a descendant of <a href="/wiki/Zacharie_Cloutier" title="Zacharie Cloutier">Zacharie Cloutier</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_d_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_d-18">[18]</a></sup> Her maternal grandmother, Eileen Pearce, emigrated from <a href="/wiki/Newbridge,_County_Kildare" title="Newbridge, County Kildare">Newbridge, County Kildare</a>, Ireland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Twain had a difficult childhood in Timmins. Her parents earned little money and food was often scarce in their household. Twain did not confide her situation to school authorities, fearing they might break up the family. In the remote, rugged community, she learned to hunt and to chop wood. Sharon and Jerry's marriage was at times stormy, and from a young age, Twain witnessed violence between them. Sharon struggled with bouts of depression. In the summer of 1979, while Jerry was at work, at Twain's insistence, her mother drove the rest of the family 680 kilometres (420 mi) south to a <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> homeless shelter for assistance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_f_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_f-20">[20]</a></sup> Sharon returned to Jerry with the children in 1981. In Timmins, Twain started singing at bars at the age of 8 to try to help pay her family's bills; she often earned 20 <a href="/wiki/Canadian_dollar" title="Canadian dollar">dollars</a> between midnight and one in the morning performing for remaining customers after the bar had finished serving. Although she expressed a dislike for singing in those bars, Twain believes that this was her own kind of performing arts school on the road.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_h_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_h-21">[21]</a></sup> She has said of the ordeal, "My deepest passion was music and it helped. There were moments when I thought, 'I hate this.' I hated going into bars and being with drunks. But I loved the music and so I survived".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_i_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_i-22">[22]</a></sup> Twain wrote her first songs at the age of 10, <i>Is Love a Rose</i> and <i>Just Like the Storybooks</i> which were fairy tales in rhyme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eggar_2005_p.3D51_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Eggar_2005_p.3D51-23">[23]</a></sup> She states that the art of creating, of actually writing songs, "was very different from performing them and became progressively important".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eggar_2005_p.3D51_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-Eggar_2005_p.3D51-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the age of 13, Twain was invited to perform on <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Canadian Broadcasting Corporation">CBC</a> television's the <i><a href="/wiki/Tommy_Hunter" title="Tommy Hunter">Tommy Hunter</a> Show</i>. While attending <a href="/wiki/Timmins_High_and_Vocational_School" title="Timmins High and Vocational School">Timmins High and Vocational School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Timmins" title="Timmins">Timmins</a>, she was also the singer for a local band called "Longshot" which covered Top 40 music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the early 1980s, Twain spent some time working with her father's <a href="/wiki/Reforestation" title="Reforestation">reforestation</a> business in northern Ontario, a business that employed some 75 Ojibwe and Cree workers. Although the work was demanding and the pay low, Twain said, "I loved the feeling of being stranded. I'm not afraid of being in my own environment, being physical, working hard. I was very strong, I walked miles and miles every day and carried heavy loads of trees. You can't shampoo, use soap or deodorant, or makeup, nothing with any scent; you have to bathe and rinse your clothes in the lake. It was a very rugged existence, but I was very creative and I would sit alone in the forest with my dog and a guitar and would just write songs".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_j_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_j-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shania_Twain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1983.E2.80.931992:_Beginnings">1983–1992: Beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shania_Twain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1983–1992: Beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After graduating from Timmins High in June 1983, Twain was eager to expand her musical horizons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eggy_2005_p.3D88_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Eggy_2005_p.3D88-26">[26]</a></sup> After the demise of the band Longshot, Twain was approached by a cover band led by <a href="/wiki/Diane_Chase" title="Diane Chase">Diane Chase</a> called "Flirt" and they toured all over Ontario.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eggy_2005_p.3D88_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-Eggy_2005_p.3D88-26">[26]</a></sup> Twain also began taking singing lessons from Toronto-based coach Ian Garrett and would often clean his house in payment for her lessons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eggar_2005_p.3D89_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Eggar_2005_p.3D89-27">[27]</a></sup> In the autumn of 1984, Twain's talents were noticed by Toronto DJ Stan Campbell who wrote about her in a <i>Country Music News</i> article: "Eilleen possesses a powerful voice with an impressive range. She has the necessary drive, ambition and positive attitude to achieve her goals".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eggar_2005_p.3D89_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-Eggar_2005_p.3D89-27">[27]</a></sup> Campbell happened to be making an album by Canadian musician (and present-day <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CKTB_(AM)" title="CKTB (AM)">CKTB</a> radio personality) Tim Denis at the time and Twain was featured on the backing vocals of the song <i>Heavy on the Sunshine</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_k_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_k-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eggar_2005_p.3D89_27-2"><a href="#cite_note-Eggar_2005_p.3D89-27">[27]</a></sup> Campbell later took Twain to Nashville to record some demos, which Twain found particularly difficult to finance. She became acquainted with regional country singer Mary Bailey who had had some country chart success in 1976. Bailey had seen Twain perform in <a href="/wiki/Greater_Sudbury" title="Greater Sudbury">Sudbury</a>, Ontario, saying "I saw this little girl up on stage with a guitar and it absolutely blew me away. She performed <a href="/wiki/Willie_Nelson" title="Willie Nelson">Willie Nelson</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Blue_Eyes_Crying_in_the_Rain" title="Blue Eyes Crying in the Rain">Blue Eyes Crying in the Rain</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Hank_Williams" title="Hank Williams">Hank Williams</a>' "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_So_Lonesome_I_Could_Cry" title="I'm So Lonesome I Could Cry">I'm So Lonesome I Could Cry</a>". Her voice reminded me of <a href="/wiki/Tanya_Tucker" title="Tanya Tucker">Tanya Tucker</a>, it had strength and character, a lot of feeling. She's a star, she deserves an opportunity".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_l_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_l-29">[29]</a></sup> Bailey later said "She sang a few songs that she had written, and I thought to myself, this kid is like nineteen years old, where does she get this? This is from a person who's lived sixty years".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_m_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_m-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kenogami_Lake_ON.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="757" data-file-width="1156" height="144" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Kenogami_Lake_ON.JPG/220px-Kenogami_Lake_ON.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Kenogami_Lake_ON.JPG/330px-Kenogami_Lake_ON.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Kenogami_Lake_ON.JPG/440px-Kenogami_Lake_ON.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kenogami_Lake_ON.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Kenogami_Lake,_Ontario" title="Kenogami Lake, Ontario">Kenogami Lake, Ontario</a> where Twain spent much time practicing in 1985</div>

<p>Bailey acquired the contract from Stan Campbell and Twain moved into Bailey's home on <a href="/wiki/Kenogami_Lake,_Ontario" title="Kenogami Lake, Ontario">Kenogami Lake</a> where she practised her music every day for hours. In the fall of 1985, Bailey took Twain down to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nashville" title="Nashville">Nashville</a> to stay with a friend, record producer Tony Migliore, who at the time was producing an album for fellow Canadian singer Kelita Haverland and Twain was featured on the backing vocals to the song <i>Too Hot to Handle</i>. She also demo-ed songs with Cyril Rawson but the demos were without success, partly due to Twain's wish to become a rock singer, not a country artist. After five months she returned to Canada and moved in with Bailey in a flat in downtown <a href="/wiki/Kirkland_Lake" title="Kirkland Lake">Kirkland Lake</a>, not far from Kenogami.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eggar_2005_p.3D92_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-Eggar_2005_p.3D92-31">[31]</a></sup> There she met rock keyboardist Eric Lambier and drummer Randy Yurko and formed a new band, moving three months later to <a href="/wiki/Bowmanville" title="Bowmanville">Bowmanville</a>, near Toronto. In late summer 1986, Mary Bailey arranged for Twain to meet John Kim Bell, a half <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mohawk_nation" title="Mohawk nation">Mohawk</a>, half American conductor who had close contacts with the directors of the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Country_Music_Association" title="Canadian Country Music Association">Canadian Country Music Association</a>. Bell recognized Twain's ability as well as her looks and the two began secretly dating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eggar_2005_p.3D92_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-Eggar_2005_p.3D92-31">[31]</a></sup> In the fall of 1986 Twain continued to express her desire to be a pop or rock singer rather than country, which led to her falling out with Mary Bailey for two years. Twain's first break finally came on February 8, 1987, when Bell staged a fundraiser for the <a href="/wiki/Indspire" title="Indspire">National Aboriginal Achievement Foundation</a>, now Indspire, at the <a href="/wiki/Roy_Thomson_Hall" title="Roy Thomson Hall">Roy Thomson Hall</a> in Toronto where Twain performed with Broadway star <a href="/wiki/Bernadette_Peters" title="Bernadette Peters">Bernadette Peters</a>, jazz guitarist <a href="/wiki/Don_Ross_(guitarist)" title="Don Ross (guitarist)">Don Ross</a>, and the Toronto Symphony Orchestra. Her performance received little acclaim, but it convinced Bell, who hated pop music, that Twain should stay well away from it and concentrate on country music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_n_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_n-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>On November 1, 1987, Twain's mother and stepfather died in a car accident approximately 50 kilometres north of <a href="/wiki/Wawa,_Ontario" title="Wawa, Ontario">Wawa, Ontario</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_o_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_o-33">[33]</a></sup> As a result, Twain moved back to Timmins to take care of her younger siblings and then took them all to <a href="/wiki/Huntsville,_Ontario" title="Huntsville, Ontario">Huntsville, Ontario</a>. There, she supported them by earning money performing at the nearby <a href="/wiki/Deerhurst_Resort" title="Deerhurst Resort">Deerhurst Resort</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eggar2005_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-Eggar2005-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1993.E2.80.931994:_Shania_Twain">1993–1994: <i>Shania Twain</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shania_Twain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1993–1994: Shania Twain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A couple of years passed and Twain's siblings got older and moved out on their own. Twain assembled a demo tape of her songs and her Huntsville manager set up a showcase for Twain to present her material to record executives. She caught the attention of a few labels, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mercury_Nashville_Records" title="Mercury Nashville Records">Mercury Nashville Records</a>, who signed her within a few months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-35">[35]</a></sup> During this time, she changed her name to Shania, which was said to be an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ojibwa" title="Ojibwa">Ojibwa</a> word which means "on my way". However, Twain's biographer, Robin Eggar, writes: "There is a continuing confusion about what 'Shania' means and if indeed it is an Ojibwe word or phrase at all. [...] There is no mispronounced or misheard phrase in either Ojibwe or Cree that comes close to meaning 'on my way.' Yet the legend of her name continues to be repeated in the media to this day."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eggar_2005_p.3D145_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-Eggar_2005_p.3D145-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>Twain's <a href="/wiki/Shania_Twain_(album)" title="Shania Twain (album)">self-titled debut album</a> was released in 1993 in North America and garnered her audiences outside Canada. Shortly before its release, she sang backing vocals for other Mercury artists, including <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Chance" title="Jeff Chance">Jeff Chance</a>'s 1992 album <i>Walk Softly on the Bridges</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Sammy_Kershaw" title="Sammy Kershaw">Sammy Kershaw</a>'s 1993 album <i><a href="/wiki/Haunted_Heart_(Sammy_Kershaw_album)" title="Haunted Heart (Sammy Kershaw album)">Haunted Heart</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> <i>Shania Twain</i> only reached No.67 on the US Country Albums Chart, but it gained positive reviews from critics. The album failed to sell significant copies initially, although Twain's future success generated enough interest for the album to be certified <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">platinum</a> six years later by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA">RIAA</a>, denoting sales of over a million. The album yielded two minor hit singles in the United States with "<a href="/wiki/What_Made_You_Say_That" title="What Made You Say That">What Made You Say That</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Dance_with_the_One_That_Brought_You" title="Dance with the One That Brought You">Dance with the One That Brought You</a>". The album was more successful in Europe, where Twain won Country Music Television Europe's "Rising Video Star of the Year" award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1995.E2.80.931996:_The_Woman_in_Me_and_commercial_success">1995–1996: <i>The Woman in Me</i> and commercial success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shania_Twain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1995–1996: The Woman in Me and commercial success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When rock producer <a href="/wiki/Robert_John_%22Mutt%22_Lange" title='Robert John "Mutt" Lange'>Robert John "Mutt" Lange</a> heard Twain's original songs and singing from her debut album, he offered to produce and write songs with her. After many telephone conversations, they met at <a href="/wiki/CMA_Music_Festival" title="CMA Music Festival">Nashville's Fan Fair</a> in June 1993. Twain and Lange became very close within just weeks, culminating in their wedding on December 28, 1993. Lange and Twain either wrote or co-wrote the songs that would form her second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Woman_in_Me_(Shania_Twain_album)" title="The Woman in Me (Shania Twain album)">The Woman in Me</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_35-2"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Record executives were stunned when they listened to the album. It was unlike anything else in country music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>The Woman in Me</i> was released in the spring of 1995. The album's first single, "<a href="/wiki/Whose_Bed_Have_Your_Boots_Been_Under%3F" title="Whose Bed Have Your Boots Been Under?">Whose Bed Have Your Boots Been Under?</a>" went to No.11 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs"><i>Billboard</i> Country Chart</a>. This was followed by her first Country Top 10 and No.1 hit single, "<a href="/wiki/Any_Man_of_Mine" title="Any Man of Mine">Any Man of Mine</a>", which also cracked the Top 40 on the Billboard Hot 100. Twain had further hits from the album, including the title track which peaked at No.14 and three additional No.1 hits: "<a href="/wiki/(If_You%27re_Not_in_It_for_Love)_I%27m_Outta_Here!" title="(If You're Not in It for Love) I'm Outta Here!">(If You're Not in It for Love) I'm Outta Here!</a>", "<a href="/wiki/You_Win_My_Love" title="You Win My Love">You Win My Love</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/No_One_Needs_to_Know" title="No One Needs to Know">No One Needs to Know</a>", which was selected for the original soundtrack for the 1996 film <i><a href="/wiki/Twister_(1996_film)" title="Twister (1996 film)">Twister</a></i>, a first for Twain, plus minor country hit "<a href="/wiki/Home_Ain%27t_where_His_Heart_Is_(Anymore)" title="Home Ain't where His Heart Is (Anymore)">Home Ain't where His Heart Is (Anymore)</a>" and a re-recorded gospel version of the album track "<a href="/wiki/God_Bless_the_Child_(Shania_Twain_song)" title="God Bless the Child (Shania Twain song)">God Bless The Child</a>" with new lyrics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_35-3"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-35">[35]</a></sup> Meanwhile, in Australia, five of these singles: "Any Man of Mine", "The Woman in Me", "I'm Outta Here!", "You Win My Love" and "God Bless The Child", were remixed for the Australian pop market, and "I'm Outta Here!" became Twain's breakthrough hit there, reaching No.5 on the ARIA charts.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>As of 2007, the album had sold more than 12 million copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RIAA_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-41">[41]</a></sup> The album was a quick breakthrough and because of this Twain performed selected international venues and television shows including two CMA Fan Fair performances with Nashville guitarists <a href="/wiki/Randy_Thomas_(musician)" title="Randy Thomas (musician)">Randy Thomas</a> (co-writer of the song "<a href="/wiki/Butterfly_Kisses_(song)" title="Butterfly Kisses (song)">Butterfly Kisses</a>"), <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Joseph_Schafer" title="Daniel Joseph Schafer">Dan Schafer</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Chris_Rodriguez" title="Chris Rodriguez">Chris Rodriguez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russ_Taff" title="Russ Taff">Russ Taff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hugh_McDonald_(American_musician)" title="Hugh McDonald (American musician)">Hugh McDonald</a> bass player of <a href="/wiki/Bon_Jovi" title="Bon Jovi">Bon Jovi</a>, Dave Malachowski and Stanley T., formerly with <a href="/wiki/The_Beach_Boys" title="The Beach Boys">The Beach Boys</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Mercury Nashville's promotion of the album was based largely upon a series of music videos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-book544_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-book544-43">[43]</a></sup> During this period, Twain made major television appearances on shows such as two performances on <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Show_with_David_Letterman" title="Late Show with David Letterman">Late Show with David Letterman</a></i>, <i>Blockbuster Music Awards</i>, <i>Billboard Music Awards</i> and the <i>American Music Awards</i>. <i>The Woman in Me</i> won the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Country_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Country Album">Grammy Award for Best Country Album</a> as well as the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music" title="Academy of Country Music">Academy of Country Music</a> award for Album of the Year; the latter group also awarded Twain as Best New Female Vocalist.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1997.E2.80.932001:_Come_On_Over_and_international_pop_breakthrough">1997–2001: <i>Come On Over</i> and international pop breakthrough</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shania_Twain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 1997–2001: Come On Over and international pop breakthrough">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Shania_Twain_-_Come_on_Over_Tour_-_Boston.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1035" data-file-width="740" height="308" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Shania_Twain_-_Come_on_Over_Tour_-_Boston.jpg/220px-Shania_Twain_-_Come_on_Over_Tour_-_Boston.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Shania_Twain_-_Come_on_Over_Tour_-_Boston.jpg/330px-Shania_Twain_-_Come_on_Over_Tour_-_Boston.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Shania_Twain_-_Come_on_Over_Tour_-_Boston.jpg/440px-Shania_Twain_-_Come_on_Over_Tour_-_Boston.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>In 1997, Twain released her follow-up album, <i><a href="/wiki/Come_On_Over" title="Come On Over">Come On Over</a></i>. This was the album that would establish her as a successful crossover singer. Slowly, following the release of lead singles "<a href="/wiki/Love_Gets_Me_Every_Time" title="Love Gets Me Every Time">Love Gets Me Every Time</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Be_Stupid_(You_Know_I_Love_You)" title="Don't Be Stupid (You Know I Love You)">Don't Be Stupid (You Know I Love You)</a>", which allowed Twain to make more appearances in the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100, the album started racking up sales. It never hit the top spot, but with the multi-chart hit single "<a href="/wiki/You%27re_Still_the_One" title="You're Still the One">You're Still the One</a>", sales skyrocketed. Other songs like "<a href="/wiki/When_(Shania_Twain_song)" title="When (Shania Twain song)">When</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Honey,_I%27m_Home" title="Honey, I'm Home">Honey, I'm Home</a>", "<a href="/wiki/You%27ve_Got_a_Way" title="You've Got a Way">You've Got a Way</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Man!_I_Feel_Like_a_Woman!" title="Man! I Feel Like a Woman!">Man! I Feel Like a Woman!</a>", "<a href="/wiki/That_Don%27t_Impress_Me_Much" title="That Don't Impress Me Much">That Don't Impress Me Much</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/From_This_Moment_On_(Shania_Twain_song)" title="From This Moment On (Shania Twain song)">From This Moment On</a>" joined the 12 songs that eventually saw release as singles. "From This Moment On" is a duet with singer <a href="/wiki/Bryan_White" title="Bryan White">Bryan White</a> and there was a re-recorded solo pop version as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>The album stayed on the charts for the next two years, going on to sell 40 million copies worldwide, making it the biggest-selling album of all time by a female musician.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harris2005_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-Harris2005-45">[45]</a></sup> She continued to break international boundaries for country music and female crossover artists. It is also the eighth biggest-selling album by any type of artist in the US<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RIAA_41-1"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-41">[41]</a></sup> and the top selling country album in history. Songs from the album won four Grammy Awards during this time, including <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Country_Song" title="Grammy Award for Best Country Song">Best Country Song</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Female_Country_Vocal_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Female Country Vocal Performance">Best Female Country Performance</a> (for "You're Still the One" and "Man! I Feel Like a Woman!") for Twain. Lange won Grammys for "You're Still the One" and "<a href="/wiki/Come_On_Over_(Shania_Twain_song)" title="Come On Over (Shania Twain song)">Come on Over</a>".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Despite the album's record sales it wasn't able to top the <i>Billboard</i> 200, peaking at No. 2. In 1998, following the pop release of "You're Still the One", the <i>Come on Over</i> album was released in a remixed format for the European market as a pop album with less country instrumentation and actually gave her the big breakthrough in Europe she and her producer husband (Robert John "Mutt" Lange) were looking for. <i>Come on Over</i> went to number 1 on the UK album charts for 11 weeks. It became the biggest selling album of the year in Great Britain and a bestseller in other big European markets as well, selling more than one million copies in Germany and nearly 4 million in the UK alone. Although "You're Still The One" and the pop version of "From This Moment On" cracked the Top 10 of the UK charts and "When" had minor success in the Top 20, the songs that had finally drawn European attention to the album were the pop remixed singles "That Don't Impress Me Much", a No.3 in the UK and Top 10 hit in Germany in the summer of 1999, and "Man! I Feel Like a Woman!" which peaked at No.3 in both the UK and France in autumn of that year. Additionally, "You've Got a Way" was remixed specifically for inclusion on the <i><a href="/wiki/Notting_Hill_(film)" title="Notting Hill (film)">Notting Hill</a></i> soundtrack. Subsequently, a reissue of the international version of the album was released worldwide, including the US and Europe, containing three of these new remixes. Additionally, the album set the record for the longest ever stay in the Top 20 of the <i>Billboard</i> 200, remaining in the Top 20 for 99 weeks.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1998, Twain launched her first major concert tour, aided by her manager <a href="/wiki/Jon_Landau" title="Jon Landau">Jon Landau</a>, a veteran of many large-scale tours with <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen" title="Bruce Springsteen">Bruce Springsteen</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Come_on_Over_Tour" title="Come on Over Tour">Come on Over Tour</a> shows was a success and won the "Country Tour of the Year" in 1998 and 1999 by Pollstar Concert Industry Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2000, Twain was initially scheduled to release a Christmas album, but plans to release one were cancelled later in the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brunner2000_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-Brunner2000-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the success of <i>Come On Over</i>, independent label Limelight Records released <i><a href="/wiki/The_Complete_Limelight_Sessions" title="The Complete Limelight Sessions">The Complete Limelight Sessions</a></i> in October 2001. The album includes 16 tracks recorded in the late 1980s before Twain signed her record deal with Mercury.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2002.E2.80.932004:_Up.21">2002–2004: <i>Up!</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shania_Twain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2002–2004: Up!">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After a change in management – QPrime replaced Landau — and a two-year break, along with the birth of their son, Eja (pronounced "Asia") D'Angelo, who was born on August 12, 2001,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Twain and Lange returned to the studio. <i><a href="/wiki/Up!_(album)" title="Up! (album)">Up!</a></i> was released on November 19, 2002. About a year later, Twain kicked off the <a href="/wiki/Up!_Tour" title="Up! Tour">Up! Tour</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_Ontario" title="Hamilton, Ontario">Hamilton</a>, Ontario, Canada on September 25, 2003. <i>Up!</i> was released with three different discs – country/acoustic (green CD), pop/rock (red CD), and world/dance (blue CD). <i>Up!</i> was given four out of five stars by <i>Rolling Stone</i> magazine, and debuted at No.1 on the <i>Billboard</i> albums chart, selling 874,000 in the first week alone. It remained at the top of the charts for five weeks. Twain's crossover appeal in country, pop, and dance music, led the album <i>Up!</i> to sell over 11 million copies by 2004. <i>Up!</i> reached No.1 in Germany, No.2 in Australia and the Top Five in the UK and France. In Germany, <i>Up!</i> was certified 4x platinum and stayed in the Top 100 for one and a half years. The international music disc was remixed with <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a>-style orchestral and percussion parts recorded in <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a>, India. The new versions were produced by Simon and <a href="/wiki/Diamond_Duggal" title="Diamond Duggal">Diamond Duggal</a>, brothers from <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">Birmingham</a>, England. They were originally invited to contribute parts to the pop version of "I'm Gonna Getcha Good!" which retained the Indian influence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_p_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_p-49">[49]</a></sup></p>

<p>Twain's popularity in UK was reflected by numerous appearances on the long-running music show <i><a href="/wiki/Top_of_the_Pops" title="Top of the Pops">Top of the Pops</a></i>, performing singles from <i>Come on Over</i> from 1999. In 2002 an entire special show was dedicated to her on sister show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TOTP2" title="TOTP2">TOTP2</a></i>, in which Twain herself introduced some past performances of her greatest hits and new singles from <i>Up!</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-totp2_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-totp2-50">[50]</a></sup> In November 2004, she appeared on the annual BBC charity telethon <i><a href="/wiki/Children_in_Need" title="Children in Need">Children in Need</a></i>. During the show, she performed "<a href="/wiki/Up!_(Shania_Twain_song)" title="Up! (Shania Twain song)">Up!</a>", and then took part in an all-star magic act in which she was sawed in half by magician Scott Penrose in an illusion called <a href="/wiki/Clearly_Impossible" title="Clearly Impossible">Clearly Impossible</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-walkoffame_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-walkoffame-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first single from the album, "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_Gonna_Getcha_Good!" title="I'm Gonna Getcha Good!">I'm Gonna Getcha Good!</a>" became a top 10 country hit in the US, after debuting at No. 24 after only five days of airplay; but only made the Top 40 on the pop charts. It was a much bigger hit on the other side of the Atlantic, released in a pop version, the single hit No. 4 in the UK. In Australia, Germany and France the song reached the Top 15 in each case. The follow-up single, "<a href="/wiki/Up!_(Shania_Twain_song)" title="Up! (Shania Twain song)">Up!</a>", reached the Top 15 in the US country charts but failed to reach the pop Top 40. The second European single became the mid-tempo song "<a href="/wiki/Ka-Ching!" title="Ka-Ching!">Ka-Ching!</a>" (which was never released as a single in North America) with lyrics where Twain was criticizing unchecked consumerism. The song eventually became another smash hit in the important European markets, reaching No.1 in Germany and Austria and other European countries, the UK Top 10 and the Top 15 in France. The third single from the album would be the most successful in the US. The romantic ballad "<a href="/wiki/Forever_and_for_Always" title="Forever and for Always">Forever and for Always</a>" was released as a single in April 2003 and peaked at No.4 on the country chart and No.1 on the Adult Contemporary chart, and made as well the <i>Billboard</i> Top 20. Again success was even bigger on the other side of the Atlantic with "Forever and For Always" again reaching the Top 10 in both, the UK and Germany. Further singles were "<a href="/wiki/She%27s_Not_Just_a_Pretty_Face" title="She's Not Just a Pretty Face">She's Not Just a Pretty Face</a>", which was a country Top 10 hit, while the last US single, "<a href="/wiki/It_Only_Hurts_When_I%27m_Breathing" title="It Only Hurts When I'm Breathing">It Only Hurts When I'm Breathing</a>", made the Top 20 on both Country and AC. Due to the enormous European success of <i>Up!</i> and its first three singles, two more singles were released in the second half of 2003 with up-tempo "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thank_You_Baby" title="Thank You Baby">Thank You Baby</a>" (#11 in the UK, Top 20 in Germany) and just before Christmas the romantic, acoustic ballad "When You Kiss Me", at least a minor hit in both territories. The title track "Up!" also saw a single release in a limited edition of European countries, such as Germany, in early 2004. In January 2008, <i>Up!</i> had sold 5.5 million copies in the U.S. and was certified by the RIAA as 11x platinum (the organization counts double albums as two units).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RIAA_41-2"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004.E2.80.932010:_Greatest_Hits_and_delay_of_new_album">2004–2010: <i>Greatest Hits</i> and delay of new album</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shania_Twain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2004–2010: Greatest Hits and delay of new album">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, she released the <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits_(Shania_Twain_album)" title="Greatest Hits (Shania Twain album)">Greatest Hits</a></i> album, with three new tracks. As of 2012, it had sold over 4.15 million copies in the U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RIAA_41-3"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-41">[41]</a></sup> The first single, the multi-format duet "<a href="/wiki/Party_for_Two" title="Party for Two">Party for Two</a>", made the country top ten with <a href="/wiki/Billy_Currington" title="Billy Currington">Billy Currington</a>, while the pop version with <a href="/wiki/Sugar_Ray" title="Sugar Ray">Sugar Ray</a> lead singer <a href="/wiki/Mark_McGrath" title="Mark McGrath">Mark McGrath</a> made top ten in the United Kingdom and Germany. The follow-up singles, "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t!" title="Don't!">Don't!</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/I_Ain%27t_No_Quitter" title="I Ain't No Quitter">I Ain't No Quitter</a>" did not fare as well. The former made Top 20 on Adult Contemporary, while the latter did not gain enough airplay to crack the Country Top 40.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_1965_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_1965-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2005, she released the single "<a href="/wiki/Shoes_(Shania_Twain_song)" title="Shoes (Shania Twain song)">Shoes</a>" from the <i><a href="/wiki/Desperate_Housewives" title="Desperate Housewives">Desperate Housewives</a></i> soundtrack. In 2008, Twain and <a href="/wiki/Anne_Murray" title="Anne Murray">Anne Murray</a> recorded a duet version of Murray's hit "<a href="/wiki/You_Needed_Me" title="You Needed Me">You Needed Me</a>" for her 2007 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Anne_Murray_Duets:_Friends_%26_Legends" title="Anne Murray Duets: Friends &amp; Legends">Anne Murray Duets: Friends &amp; Legends</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2009, Twain released a letter to her fans explaining the delays in the release of her next album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_r_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_r-54">[54]</a></sup> In August 2009, at a conference in Timmins, Ontario, a spokesman for Twain's label said a new record from the singer is still "nowhere in sight".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_s_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_s-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.93present:_Return_to_music.2C_Residency_Show.2C_and_Rock_This_Country_Tour">2011–present: Return to music, Residency Show, and Rock This Country Tour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shania_Twain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2011–present: Return to music, Residency Show, and Rock This Country Tour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In May 2011, Twain confirmed in an interview that she will release her first new single in six years, "<a href="/wiki/Today_Is_Your_Day" title="Today Is Your Day">Today Is Your Day</a>", after the finale of <i><a href="/wiki/Why_Not%3F_with_Shania_Twain" title="Why Not? with Shania Twain">Why Not? with Shania Twain</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-today_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-today-56">[56]</a></sup> Twain previewed the song in the first episode of the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-today_56-1"><a href="#cite_note-today-56">[56]</a></sup> Twain worked with music producers <a href="/wiki/David_Foster" title="David Foster">David Foster</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Chapman_(record_producer)" title="Nathan Chapman (record producer)">Nathan Chapman</a> on the new song. The last episode of "Why Not?" features Twain and <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Richie" title="Lionel Richie">Lionel Richie</a> recording "<a href="/wiki/Endless_Love_(song)" title="Endless Love (song)">Endless Love</a>" which would be the first single from his 2012 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Tuskegee_(album)" title="Tuskegee (album)">Tuskegee</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cgm_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-cgm-57">[57]</a></sup> "Today Is Your Day" was officially released to iTunes and country radio on June 12, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcp_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcp-58">[58]</a></sup> In addition to "Today is Your Day", Shania also collaborated with Michael Buble on his 2011 album <i>Michael Buble Christmas</i> (also produced by David Foster). Shania recorded "White Christmas" with Buble which was the first single from the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> On June 8, 2011, at a press conference at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Country_Music_Hall_of_Fame" title="Country Music Hall of Fame">Country Music Hall of Fame</a> in Nashville, Twain announced that she will headline <a href="/wiki/Caesars_Palace" title="Caesars Palace">Caesars Palace</a> in Las Vegas for two years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcp_58-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcp-58">[58]</a></sup> Her show, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Shania:_Still_the_One" title="Shania: Still the One">Still the One</a></i>, began on December 1, 2012 with shows expected to run in 2013 and 2014.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In July 2013, Twain announced on <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> that she is working on her album over the summer during a break from <i>Still the One</i>. This will be her fifth studio album and first in 13 years.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In October 2013, Twain sat down with Robin Roberts from <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a></i> as a featured artist on the Countdown to the CMA Awards. In the interview, Twain said that a new album was coming, but she said that she was still in the process of finding the right producer.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Outside of her show at Caesars Palace, Twain performed two concerts at the <a href="/wiki/Calgary_Stampede" title="Calgary Stampede">Calgary Stampede</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Calgary,_Alberta" title="Calgary, Alberta">Calgary, Alberta</a>, on July 9 and 10, 2014. In a series of interviews leading up to her Calgary Stampede shows, Twain said she hoped to tour in 2015 and that it would lead to the release of a new album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> Alongside her Calgary Stampede shows, Shania also headlined a show on Labour Day weekend at Charlottetown, Prince Edward Island, Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 4, 2015, Twain announced on <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a></i> she would be going on tour for the first time in 11 years, and would begin June 5 in <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>, Washington and end on October 11 in Toronto, Ontario. Twain also announced this will be her last tour before her fifth studio album, which she intends to release while she is 50.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> In an interview on Global Television Network's <i>The Morning Show</i> on March 6, Twain confirmed that she is not retiring from her music career after the tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> In an interview with Radio.com published on March 5, she stated that she has found several producers for her upcoming album, describing it as "soul music".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 24, 2015, Twain stated: "First, I have to finish my new album this winter. Six tracks are already completed. I've written 38 songs in total, and now the process is underway to narrow that down to another six or eight to finish recording". That same month, it was announced by several sources, that even though her current <a href="/wiki/Rock_This_Country_Tour" title="Rock This Country Tour">Rock This Country Tour</a> is her final time touring, she is possibly planning on extending the tour overseas because the Rock This Country tour was only based in the United States and Canada. Shania also mentioned, possibly returning to Las Vegas with a new residency show for possibly late 2016 or 2017. The new show would end up featuring music from her long-awaited new album as well as her hits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> In October 2016, Twain confirmed to <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> that she had new music coming "really soon".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2016 in an interview with <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_magazine" title="Billboard magazine">Billboard</a></i>, she spoke about her forthcoming album, describing the finished product as "kind of schizophrenic musically" maintaining "She's the glue".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="TV_career">TV career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shania_Twain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: TV career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Twain's mainstream pop acceptance was further helped by her appearance in the 1998 first edition of the <i><a href="/wiki/VH1_Divas" title="VH1 Divas">VH1 Divas</a></i> concert where she sang alongside <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Celine_Dion" title="Celine Dion">Celine Dion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gloria_Estefan" title="Gloria Estefan">Gloria Estefan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carole_King" title="Carole King">Carole King</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aretha_Franklin" title="Aretha Franklin">Aretha Franklin</a>, and also by <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>'s 1999 heavily aired <i><a href="/wiki/Behind_the_Music" title="Behind the Music">Behind the Music</a></i>, which concentrated on the tragic aspects of her early life as well as her physical attractiveness and Nashville's early resistance to her bare-midriff music videos. After Divas, Shania Twain wrote and/or sang background vocals with her then husband Robert John "Mutt" Lange the songs, "<a href="/wiki/If_Walls_Could_Talk" title="If Walls Could Talk">If Walls Could Talk</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Goodbye%27s_(The_Saddest_Word)" title="Goodbye's (The Saddest Word)">Goodbye's (The Saddest Word)</a>" for Celine Dion.</p>
<p>On November 12, 2008, Twain made her first television appearance since her split from ex-husband Robert "Mutt" Lange, where she appeared as a surprise presenter at the 42nd CMA Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_2008_68-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_2008-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 17, 2009, <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> announced that Twain would be a guest judge on <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> in Chicago, for the show's August 30 and 31 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AI_69-0"><a href="#cite_note-AI-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2010, Twain announced plans for her own TV show, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Why_Not%3F_with_Shania_Twain" title="Why Not? with Shania Twain">Why Not? with Shania Twain</a></i>. The show debuted on May 8, 2011 on <a href="/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey_Network_(U.S._TV_channel)" title="Oprah Winfrey Network (U.S. TV channel)">OWN</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_u_70-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_u-70">[70]</a></sup> Twain returned to <i>American Idol</i> as a guest mentor for a week where the top 6 contestants showcased her songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_2010_71-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_2010-71">[71]</a></sup> After the conclusion of the ninth season Twain was very close to becoming a judge but ultimately it was <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a> who got the job.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Endorsements">Endorsements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shania_Twain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Endorsements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January 2005, Twain joined Scentstories by <a href="/wiki/Febreze" title="Febreze">Febreze</a> to create a limited edition scent disc with the proceeds going to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/America%27s_Second_Harvest" title="America's Second Harvest">America's Second Harvest</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_w_73-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_w-73">[73]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late 2005, Twain partnered with <a href="/wiki/Coty,_Inc." title="Coty, Inc.">Coty</a> to produce her namesake fragrance "Shania" by Stetson. A second fragrance was released in September 2007, called "Shania Starlight".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_x_74-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_x-74">[74]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 1, 2010, Twain carried the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympic_Flame" title="Olympic Flame">Olympic Torch</a> through her hometown as part of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Winter_Olympics_torch_relay" title="2010 Winter Olympics torch relay">2010 Winter Olympics torch relay</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_t_75-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_t-75">[75]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shania_Twain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Twain met producer <a href="/wiki/Robert_John_%22Mutt%22_Lange" title='Robert John "Mutt" Lange'>Robert John "Mutt" Lange</a> after he heard her original songs and singing from her debut album; he then offered to produce and write songs with her. They first met at <a href="/wiki/CMA_Music_Festival" title="CMA Music Festival">Nashville's Fan Fair</a> in June 1993 and quickly became close. They were married on December 28, 1993 and Twain gave birth to their son Eja (pronounced Asia) in August 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-peopleceleb_76-0"><a href="#cite_note-peopleceleb-76">[76]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 15, 2008, a spokesperson for Mercury Nashville announced that Twain and Lange were separating after Lange allegedly had an affair with Twain's best friend, Marie-Anne Thiébaud.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_y_77-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_y-77">[77]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_z_78-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_z-78">[78]</a></sup> Their divorce was finalized on June 9, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Julie2010_79-0"><a href="#cite_note-Julie2010-79">[79]</a></sup> On December 20, 2010, Twain's manager said that Twain was engaged to Frédéric Thiébaud (the Swiss former husband of her former best friend Marie-Anne Thiébaud), an executive at <a href="/wiki/Nestl%C3%A9" title="Nestlé">Nestlé</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> They married on January 1, 2011 in <a href="/wiki/Rinc%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico" title="Rincón, Puerto Rico">Rincón</a>, Puerto Rico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, Twain was reported as having the Perfect Face. The BBC reported on a study, led by the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Toronto" title="University of Toronto">University of Toronto</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_San_Diego" title="University of California, San Diego">University of California, San Diego</a>, which appeared in the journal <i><a href="/wiki/Vision_Research" title="Vision Research">Vision Research</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2010, it was confirmed that Twain was to release her autobiography, <i>From This Moment On</i>, on May 3, 2011,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_v_84-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_v-84">[84]</a></sup> and the cover work for the book was released on March 2, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup> Twain is a vegetarian<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> and is also a devotee of <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_Sant_Mat_movements" title="Contemporary Sant Mat movements">Sant Mat</a>, an Eastern spiritual philosophy which calls for daily meditation as well as abstinence from alcohol, drugs and premarital sex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_honors">Awards and honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shania_Twain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Awards and honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>In addition to her various awards for her singles and albums, Twain has received a number of personal honours:</p>
<ul>
<li>She was named the 1999 Entertainer of the Year by both the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music" title="Academy of Country Music">Academy of Country Music</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association" title="Country Music Association">Country Music Association</a>; Twain was the first non-US citizen to win the CMA award.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>Twain was ranked No.7 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Country_Music_Television" title="Country Music Television">Country Music Television</a>'s <i>40 Greatest Women of Country Music</i> in 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_ad_89-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_ad-89">[89]</a></sup></li>
<li>In 2003, Twain was inducted into <a href="/wiki/Canada%27s_Walk_of_Fame" title="Canada's Walk of Fame">Canada's Walk of Fame</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-walkoffame_51-1"><a href="#cite_note-walkoffame-51">[51]</a></sup></li>
<li>The city of Timmins <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, renamed a street for her, gave her the key to the city, and built the <a href="/wiki/Shania_Twain_Centre" title="Shania Twain Centre">Shania Twain Centre</a> in her honour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_af_90-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_af-90">[90]</a></sup></li>
<li>On November 18, 2005, Twain was invested as an Officer in the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Canada" title="Order of Canada">Order of Canada</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_2005_91-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_2005-91">[91]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ottawa2008_92-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ottawa2008-92">[92]</a></sup></li>
<li>Twain was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Music_Hall_of_Fame" title="Canadian Music Hall of Fame">Canadian Music Hall of Fame</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juno_Awards" title="Juno Awards">Juno Awards</a> on March 27, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-93"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup></li>
<li>On June 2, 2011, Twain received a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame. Her star is the 2,442nd Star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame in the Category of Recording.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref_ae_94-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref_ae-94">[94]</a></sup></li>
<li>In 2016, Twain was declared the "Artist of a Lifetime" by <a href="/wiki/CMT_(U.S._TV_channel)" title="CMT (U.S. TV channel)">CMT</a> and was given a special award during the 2016 Artist's of the Year ceremony.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-95"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup></li>
<li>In January 2017, it was announced that Twain will have her own exhibit at the Country Music Hall of Fame, which will open on June 26 and run through 2018.</li>
</ul>
