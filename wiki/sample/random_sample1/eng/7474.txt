<p><b>Sade</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ʃ/ 'sh' in 'shy'">ʃ</span><span title="/ɑː/ 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span></span>/</a></span></span> <span title="English pronunciation respelling"><a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i>shah-<b><span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">DAY</span></span></b></i></a></span>) are an English band formed in London in 1982. Three of their members were originally from <a href="/wiki/Kingston_upon_Hull" title="Kingston upon Hull">Kingston upon Hull</a> in the <a href="/wiki/East_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="East Riding of Yorkshire">East Riding of Yorkshire</a>. <a href="/wiki/Sade_(singer)" title="Sade (singer)">Sade Adu</a> is the lead singer of the eponymous band. Their music features elements of <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhythm_and_blues" title="Rhythm and blues">R&amp;B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soft_rock" title="Soft rock">soft rock</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a>.</p>
<p>Sade's debut <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Diamond_Life" title="Diamond Life">Diamond Life</a></i>, was released in 1984, reaching No. 2 in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK_Album_Chart" title="UK Album Chart">UK Album Chart</a>, selling over 1.2 million copies in the UK, and won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brit_Award" title="Brit Award">Brit Award</a> for Best British Album in 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brits_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Brits-5">[5]</a></sup> The album was also a hit internationally, reaching No. 1 in several countries and the Top Ten in the US where it has sold in excess of 4 million copies. In late 1985, Sade released their second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Promise_(Sade_album)" title="Promise (Sade album)">Promise</a></i>, which peaked at No. 1 in both the UK and the US.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BritChart_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-BritChart-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-7">[7]</a></sup> It was certified <a href="/wiki/British_Phonographic_Industry" title="British Phonographic Industry">double platinum</a> in the UK, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA">quadruple platinum</a> in the US. In 1986 Sade won a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_New_Artist" title="Best New Artist">Best New Artist</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grammy_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Grammy-8">[8]</a></sup> Their 2000 fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Lovers_Rock_(album)" title="Lovers Rock (album)">Lovers Rock</a></i>, won the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Pop_Vocal_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Pop Vocal Album">Grammy Award for Best Pop Vocal Album</a>. Sade's sixth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Soldier_of_Love_(album)" title="Soldier of Love (album)">Soldier of Love</a></i>, was released on 8 February 2010, and peaked at No. 4 in the UK, and No. 1 in the US.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Soldier_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Soldier-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2011, the band won their fourth Grammy Award for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_R%26B_Performance_by_a_Duo_or_Group_with_Vocals" title="Grammy Award for Best R&amp;B Performance by a Duo or Group with Vocals">Best R&amp;B Performance by a Duo or Group with Vocals</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grammy_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Grammy-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sade's US certified sales so far stand at 23.5 million units according to <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Top_Selling_Artists_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Top_Selling_Artists-10">[10]</a></sup> and have sold more than 75 million records worldwide to date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The band were ranked at No. 50 on <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>'s list of the "100 greatest artists of all time."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sade_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1980s">1980s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sade_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1980s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The musical group Sade was formed in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> in 1982, by members of a <a href="/wiki/Latin_soul" title="Latin soul">Latin soul</a> band, Pride. <a href="/wiki/Sade_(singer)" title="Sade (singer)">Sade Adu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Matthewman" title="Stuart Matthewman">Stuart Matthewman</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Denman" title="Paul Denman">Paul Denman</a>, together with Paul Anthony Cook, formed the break-away group and began to write their own material. They named the band after lead singer Sade Adu, and made their debut performance in December 1982 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ronnie_Scott%27s_Club" title="Ronnie Scott's Club">Ronnie Scott's Club</a> in London in support of Pride. In May 1983 the band performed their first US show at <a href="/wiki/Danceteria" title="Danceteria">Danceteria</a> Club in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. Andrew Hale joined the band in 1983; Cook left in 1984. Sade received more attention from the media and record companies than Pride had, and eventually separated from that group altogether. On 18 October 1983, the band signed with <a href="/wiki/Portrait_Records" title="Portrait Records">Portrait Records</a> (which was absorbed by its parent label, <a href="/wiki/Epic_Records" title="Epic Records">Epic Records</a>, in 1986). When singer Sade and her band of the same name were establishing themselves, their record company, Epic, printed "Pronounced Shar-day" on the record labels of their releases<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> (which led to <i>mis</i>pronunciation in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhotic_and_non-rhotic_accents" title="Rhotic and non-rhotic accents">rhotic</a> North America).</p>
<p>In February 1984, Sade released their first single, "<a href="/wiki/Your_Love_Is_King" title="Your Love Is King">Your Love Is King</a>", which became a Top Ten hit. A second single, "<a href="/wiki/When_Am_I_Going_to_Make_a_Living" title="When Am I Going to Make a Living">When Am I Going to Make a Living</a>" barely made the Top 40, but the band's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Diamond_Life" title="Diamond Life">Diamond Life</a></i>, was released in July 1984 and peaked at No. 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BritChart_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-BritChart-6">[6]</a></sup> It spent over six months in the UK Top Ten and was later certified 4x Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/British_Phonographic_Industry" title="British Phonographic Industry">BPI</a>. <i>Diamond Life</i> won the 1985 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brit_Award" title="Brit Award">Brit Award</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_BRIT_Awards_ceremonies#1985" title="List of BRIT Awards ceremonies">Best British Album</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brits_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Brits-5">[5]</a></sup> The band embarked on their first major UK tour, augmented by <a href="/wiki/Dave_Early" title="Dave Early">Dave Early</a> (drums), Martin Ditcham (percussion), Terry Bailey (trumpet) and Gordon Matthewman (trombone). A third single, "<a href="/wiki/Smooth_Operator" title="Smooth Operator">Smooth Operator</a>", was released from the album with a video directed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Julian_Temple" title="Julian Temple">Julian Temple</a>. The single became their first US hit in Spring 1985, propelling the album in the US Top Ten. Also in 1985, the band were nominated for two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="MTV Video Music Awards">MTV Video Music Awards</a>—"<a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Award_for_Best_Female_Video" title="MTV Video Music Award for Best Female Video">Best Female Video</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Award_for_Best_New_Artist" title="MTV Video Music Award for Best New Artist">Best New Artist</a>". On 13 July 1985, Sade performed at the <a href="/wiki/Live_Aid" title="Live Aid">Live Aid</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium_(1924)" title="Wembley Stadium (1924)">Wembley Stadium</a> in London. Sade Adu became the only African-born artist to appear in front of the live audience of 75,000, and an estimated worldwide television audience of 1.4 billion in 170 countries.</p>
<p>In late 1985, Sade released their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Promise_(Sade_album)" title="Promise (Sade album)">Promise</a></i>, which peaked at No. 1 in both the UK and the US.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BritChart_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-BritChart-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-7">[7]</a></sup> It was certified double platinum by the <a href="/wiki/British_Phonographic_Industry" title="British Phonographic Industry">BPI</a> in the UK, and quadruple platinum in the US. In 1986, Adu was nominated for an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Music_Awards" title="American Music Awards">American Music Awards</a> for Favorite <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">Soul</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%26B" title="R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> Female Video Artist, and the band won a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_New_Artist" title="Best New Artist">Best New Artist</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grammy_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Grammy-8">[8]</a></sup> On 28 June 1986, after touring for the album, the band performed at the Artists Against Apartheid Concert in the Freedom Festival on <a href="/wiki/Clapham_Common" title="Clapham Common">Clapham Common</a> in London. In 1987 the band was nominated for a Grammy Award for Best R&amp;B Vocal Performance by a Duo or Group for <i>Promise</i>.</p>
<p>Sade's third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Stronger_Than_Pride" title="Stronger Than Pride">Stronger Than Pride</a></i>, was released in May 1988. The album peaked at No. 3 in the UK and has been certified platinum by the <a href="/wiki/British_Phonographic_Industry" title="British Phonographic Industry">BPI</a>. It was preceded by the single "Paradise", which made the UK Top 30 and US Top 20.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BritChart_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-BritChart-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-7">[7]</a></sup> The band toured across the world again, augmented by Blair Cunningham (drums), Martin Ditcham (percussion), Leroy Osbourne (vocals), Gordon Hunte (guitar), James McMillan (trumpet) and Jake Jacas (trombone &amp; vocals). In 1989, Sade Adu was nominated for an American Music Award for Favorite Soul/R&amp;B Female Artist.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s">1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sade_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1990s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sade's fourth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Deluxe" title="Love Deluxe">Love Deluxe</a></i>, was released in November 1992. The album peaked at No. 3 on the US Album charts and was certified quadruple-Platinum,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and peaked at No. 10 in the UK and was certified Gold by the BPI.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1993 the band recorded a cover of the <a href="/wiki/Percy_Mayfield" title="Percy Mayfield">Percy Mayfield</a> song, "<a href="/wiki/Please_Send_Me_Someone_to_Love" title="Please Send Me Someone to Love">Please Send Me Someone to Love</a>", for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a>-winning film <i><a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_(film)" title="Philadelphia (film)">Philadelphia</a></i>, before launching the <i>Love Deluxe</i> world tour. Joining the band were Leroy Osbourne (vocals), Gordon Hunte (guitar), Trevor Murrell (drums), Karl Vanden Bossche (percussion), and <a href="/wiki/Rick_Braun" title="Rick Braun">Rick Braun</a> (trumpet).</p>
<p>The 1994 <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy</a> for Best R&amp;B Performance by a Duo or Group was awarded to Sade for "<a href="/wiki/No_Ordinary_Love" title="No Ordinary Love">No Ordinary Love</a>", featured in the 1993 film <i><a href="/wiki/Indecent_Proposal" title="Indecent Proposal">Indecent Proposal</a></i>. In November the group released their first compilation album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Best_of_Sade" title="The Best of Sade">The Best of Sade</a></i>. The album was another Top Ten hit in both the UK and US<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AllMusic-BestOf_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-AllMusic-BestOf-17">[17]</a></sup> and was certified Platinum and Quadruple-Platinum respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In 1996 Hale, Denman, and Matthewman formed their own band as a side project, <a href="/wiki/Sweetback_(band)" title="Sweetback (band)">Sweetback</a>, and released a self-titled album.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s">2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sade_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2000 Sade Adu came out of retirement to perform at the prestigious <a href="/wiki/MOBO_Awards" title="MOBO Awards">MOBO Awards</a>, her first live performance in several years. The following month, Sade released their fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Lovers_Rock_(album)" title="Lovers Rock (album)">Lovers Rock</a></i>, their first album in eight years. The album peaked at number 18 in the UK (their only studio album not to make the top 10) though was certified Gold by the <a href="/wiki/British_Phonographic_Industry" title="British Phonographic Industry">BPI</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BritChart_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-BritChart-6">[6]</a></sup> It fared better in the US, peaking at number 3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-7">[7]</a></sup> It also won the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Pop_Vocal_Album" title="Best Pop Vocal Album">Best Pop Vocal Album</a> in 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grammy_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-Grammy-8">[8]</a></sup> The band toured the US throughout 2001. The tour resulted in a live album, <i><a href="/wiki/Lovers_Live" title="Lovers Live">Lovers Live</a></i>, which was released in the UK and US in February 2002. In 2005 the band contributed the track "Mum" to the <i>Voices for Darfur</i> DVD.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s">2010s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sade_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sade's sixth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Soldier_of_Love_(album)" title="Soldier of Love (album)">Soldier of Love</a></i> was released worldwide on 8 February 2010, the band's first album of new material in ten years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Following the release of the "<a href="/wiki/Soldier_of_Love_(Sade_song)" title="Soldier of Love (Sade song)">Soldier of Love</a>" single on 8 December 2009, the track debuted at number 11 on the Urban Hot AC chart, making it the highest debut of the decade and the third highest all-time on the Urban Hot AC chart. "Soldier of Love" debuted at number 5 on the Smooth Jazz airplay chart and became the first ever vocal to hit number 1 on the Smooth Jazz Top 20 Countdown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>The album peaked at No. 4 in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BritChart_6-5"><a href="#cite_note-BritChart-6">[6]</a></sup> In the US the album sold 502,000 copies in its first week and topped the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Soldier_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Soldier-9">[9]</a></sup> The album stayed at No. 1 in the US for three weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The group released the second single from the album, "<a href="/wiki/Babyfather_(song)" title="Babyfather (song)">Babyfather</a>", in April 2010, followed by a video in May. On 13 April 2010, the band performed "Babyfather" and "<a href="/wiki/The_Sweetest_Taboo" title="The Sweetest Taboo">The Sweetest Taboo</a>" on the US TV show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dancing_With_The_Stars" title="Dancing With The Stars">Dancing With The Stars</a></i>. In September 2010 the group announced the first dates of their global tour, <a href="/wiki/Sade_Live" title="Sade Live">Sade Live</a>, to begin in April 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011 Sade received their fourth Grammy Award (<a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_R%26B_Performance_by_a_Duo_or_Group_with_Vocals" title="Grammy Award for Best R&amp;B Performance by a Duo or Group with Vocals">Best R&amp;B Performance by a Duo or Group with Vocals</a>) for <i><a href="/wiki/Soldier_of_Love_(Sade_song)" title="Soldier of Love (Sade song)">Soldier of Love</a></i>, and released a second <a href="/wiki/Greatest_hits_album" title="Greatest hits album">greatest hits album</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ultimate_Collection_(Sade_album)" title="The Ultimate Collection (Sade album)">The Ultimate Collection</a></i>, which made the UK Top Ten.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grammy_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-Grammy-8">[8]</a></sup> A new video for the track "Love Is Found" premiered in July 2011.</p>
