<p><b>Stéphane Charbonnier</b> (<small>French: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[ʃaʁbɔnje]</a></span>; 21 August 1967 – 7 January 2015), known as <b>Charb</b> (<span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[ʃaʁb]</a></span>), was a French <a href="/wiki/Satire" title="Satire">satirical</a> <a href="/wiki/Caricature" title="Caricature">caricaturist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalist</a>. He was assassinated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamist" title="Islamist">Islamist</a> terrorists during the <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Hebdo_shooting" title="Charlie Hebdo shooting"><i>Charlie Hebdo</i> shooting</a> in January 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-econ_Char_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-econ_Char-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He worked for several newspapers and magazines, joining <i><a href="/wiki/Charlie_Hebdo" title="Charlie Hebdo">Charlie Hebdo</a></i> in 1992 and becoming the director of publication in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Due to the publication of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muhammad_cartoons" title="Muhammad cartoons">Muhammad cartoons</a> in <i>Charlie Hebdo</i>, Charb became subject to death threats from Islamists. From the time the magazine was firebombed in 2011, he lived under police protection until his assassination.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stéphane Charbonnier was born in <a href="/wiki/Conflans-Sainte-Honorine" title="Conflans-Sainte-Honorine">Conflans-Sainte-Honorine</a> on 21 August 1967<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and raised in <a href="/wiki/Pontoise" title="Pontoise">Pontoise</a>. His mother worked as a secretary and his father worked as a technician for <a href="/wiki/Postes,_t%C3%A9l%C3%A9graphes_et_t%C3%A9l%C3%A9phones_(France)" title="Postes, télégraphes et téléphones (France)">Postes, télégraphes et téléphones</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-luc_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-luc-5">[5]</a></sup> His grandparents owned a grocery store in Pontoise. Stéphane's talent for drawing was discovered in school and he published his first drawings in <i>Echo des collégiens</i> at the age of fourteen. He continued to draw while studying at Lycée Camille Pissarro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marie_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-marie-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>In the late 1980s he started working as a cartoonist. His work included creating cartoons for the newspaper <i>Les Nouvelles du Val-d’Oise</i> and a magazine for the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Utopia_(cin%C3%A9ma)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Utopia (cinéma) (page does not exist)">Utopia (cinéma)</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Utopia_(cin%C3%A9ma)" title="fr:Utopia (cinéma)">fr</a>)</span> in <a href="/wiki/Saint-Ouen-l%27Aum%C3%B4ne" title="Saint-Ouen-l'Aumône">Saint-Ouen-l'Aumône</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marie_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-marie-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Later freelance work by Charb included cartoons for <i><a href="/wiki/L%27%C3%89cho_des_savanes" title="L'Écho des savanes">L'Écho des savanes</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/T%C3%A9l%C3%A9rama" title="Télérama">Télérama</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/L%27Humanit%C3%A9" title="L'Humanité">L'Humanité</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He joined <i>Charlie Hebdo</i> in 1992 and was its <a href="/wiki/Editor-in-chief" title="Editor-in-chief">editor-in-chief</a> from 2009 until his death on 7 January 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Charb's comic strip, <i>Maurice et Patapon</i> featured Maurice, a dog described by the newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Lib%C3%A9ration" title="Libération">Libération</a></i> as leftist, pacifist, outgoing, and omnisexual, and a cat, <i>Patapon</i>, who is conservative, violent, asexual, and perverse. Libération described the series as philosophical and <a href="/wiki/Scatology#Literature" title="Scatology">scatalogical</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-luc_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-luc-5">[5]</a></sup> Charb also drew the character "Marcel Keuf, le flic" ("Marcel Pig, the cop") in <i><a href="/wiki/Fluide_Glacial" title="Fluide Glacial">Fluide Glacial</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Charb's column in <i>Charlie Hebdo</i> was titled "Charb n'aime pas les gens" ("Charb does not like people"). One of his regular pieces was the monthly <i>La fatwa de l'Ayatollah Charb</i> (<i>The Fatwa of the Ayatollah Charb</i>) in <i>Fluide Glacial</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-obit-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2007 and 2008, he was a set cartoonist on the talk show <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=T%27emp%C3%AAches_tout_le_monde_de_dormir_(%C3%A9mission)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="T'empêches tout le monde de dormir (émission) (page does not exist)">T'empêches tout le monde de dormir</a></i> on television channel <a href="/wiki/M6_(TV_channel)" title="M6 (TV channel)">M6</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He was a long-time supporter of the <a href="/wiki/French_Communist_Party" title="French Communist Party">French Communist Party</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>He also drew cartoons for anti-racism organizations such as <a href="/wiki/MRAP_(NGO)" title="MRAP (NGO)">MRAP</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2011_bomb_attack_and_subsequent_threats">2011 bomb attack and subsequent threats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2011 bomb attack and subsequent threats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 2 November 2011, <i>Charlie Hebdo</i> <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Hebdo#2011.E2.80.93present" title="Charlie Hebdo">was firebombed</a> just before its 3 November issue was due to be published; the issue was entitled <i>Charia Hebdo</i> and satirically featured the Islamic prophet, <a href="/wiki/Muhammad" title="Muhammad">Muhammad</a> as guest-editor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Charb and two co-workers at <i>Charlie Hebdo</i> subsequently received police protection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2012, a man was arrested in <a href="/wiki/La_Rochelle" title="La Rochelle">La Rochelle</a>, allegedly for having called for the beheading of Charb on a <a href="/wiki/Jihadism" title="Jihadism">Jihadist</a> website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a 2012 interview Charb was quoted as saying, "I am not afraid of reprisals, I have no children, no wife, no car, no debt. It might sound a bit pompous, but I'd prefer to die on my feet than to live on my knees."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Al-Qaeda" title="Al-Qaeda">Al-Qaeda</a> put Charb on their "most-wanted list" in 2013,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-time-12dead_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-time-12dead-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tele-wanted_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-tele-wanted-20">[20]</a></sup> after he edited an edition of <i>Charlie Hebdo</i> that satirised radical <a href="/wiki/Mohammedan" title="Mohammedan">Mohammedans</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Also on that list was <a href="/wiki/Lars_Vilks" title="Lars Vilks">Lars Vilks</a>, as well as three <i>Jyllands-Posten</i> staff members: <a href="/wiki/Kurt_Westergaard" title="Kurt Westergaard">Kurt Westergaard</a> (whose cartoons <i>Charlie Hebdo</i> had published), <a href="/wiki/Carsten_Juste" title="Carsten Juste">Carsten Juste</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Flemming_Rose" title="Flemming Rose">Flemming Rose</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-time-12dead_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-time-12dead-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tele-wanted_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-tele-wanted-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thewire2013_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-thewire2013-22">[22]</a></sup> Being a sport shooter, Charb applied for permit to be able to <a href="/wiki/Concealed_carry" title="Concealed carry">carry a firearm for self-defence</a>. The application was, however, not approved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telerama_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-telerama-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the week of the <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Hebdo_shooting" title="Charlie Hebdo shooting"><i>Charlie Hebdo</i> shooting</a>, a Charb illustration in issue for that week observed that there had not been any terrorist attacks in France, with a caricatured armed jihadist fighter turning a customary French phrase to malicious use: "Wait! ... we still have until the end of January to present our wishes" — a reference to the French tradition of offering New Year's greetings until the end of January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-charb-illustration-la-libre_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-charb-illustration-la-libre-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Beliefs">Beliefs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Beliefs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Charbonnier was an <a href="/wiki/Atheism" title="Atheism">atheist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pacifism" title="Pacifism">pacifist</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Two days prior to his death, Charb had completed an essay on Islamophobia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cavna_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cavna-28">[28]</a></sup> One year after the massacre, it was published in English translation, with a foreword by <a href="/wiki/Adam_Gopnik" title="Adam Gopnik">Adam Gopnik</a>, under the title <i>Open Letter: On Blasphemy, Islamophobia, and the True Enemies of Free Expression</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Charb was killed, with seven of his colleagues, two police officers, and two other people on 7 January 2015 <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Hebdo_shooting" title="Charlie Hebdo shooting">when a pair of jihadist gunmen stormed</a> the <i>Charlie Hebdo</i> newspaper offices in Paris.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lessentiel-cabu-mort_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-lessentiel-cabu-mort-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lepoint-fusillade_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-lepoint-fusillade-30">[30]</a></sup> One of the police officers killed, Franck Brinsolaro, was Charb's bodyguard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tend-policiers_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-tend-policiers-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>The funeral was held in Pontoise and included speeches by <a href="/wiki/Luz_(cartoonist)" title="Luz (cartoonist)">Luz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Pelloux" title="Patrick Pelloux">Patrick Pelloux</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean-Luc_M%C3%A9lenchon" title="Jean-Luc Mélenchon">Jean-Luc Mélenchon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Laurent_(politician)" title="Pierre Laurent (politician)">Pierre Laurent</a>. The government ministers <a href="/wiki/Christiane_Taubira" title="Christiane Taubira">Christiane Taubira</a>, <a href="/wiki/Najat_Vallaud-Belkacem" title="Najat Vallaud-Belkacem">Najat Vallaud-Belkacem</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Pellerin" title="Fleur Pellerin">Fleur Pellerin</a> also attended the funeral.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jeannette_Bougrab" title="Jeannette Bougrab">Jeannette Bougrab</a>, a human rights attorney and former Minister for Youth and Community Life, indicated after his death that she had been Charb's <a href="/wiki/Life_partner" title="Life partner">life partner</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> His family disputed this and, in a statement issued by his brother on 10 January 2015, denied the existence of any "interpersonal commitment" between Charb and Jeannette Bougrab<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> while some of his colleagues confirmed it. Police investigations suggest that she was not Charb's life partner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
