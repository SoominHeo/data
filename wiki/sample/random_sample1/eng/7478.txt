<p>The <b>Emirate of Sharjah</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/ʃ/ 'sh' in 'shy'">ʃ</span><span title="/ɑːr/ 'ar' in 'far'">ɑːr</span><span title="/dʒ/ 'j' in 'jam'">dʒ</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">الشارقة</span>‎‎ <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Arabic transliteration">Aš Šāriqah</span></i>) is one of the <a href="/wiki/Emirates_of_the_United_Arab_Emirates" title="Emirates of the United Arab Emirates">states</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>. The emirate covers 2,590 square kilometres (1,000 sq mi) and has a population of over 800,000 (2008).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abu_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-abu-2">[2]</a></sup> The emirate of Sharjah comprises the capital city of <a href="/wiki/Sharjah" title="Sharjah">Sharjah</a>, after which it is named, and other minor towns and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exclave" title="Exclave">exclaves</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Kalba" title="Kalba">Kalba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dibba_Al-Hisn" title="Dibba Al-Hisn">Dibba Al-Hisn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Khor_Fakkan" title="Khor Fakkan">Khor Fakkan</a>.</p>
<p>The emirate is a <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy" title="Constitutional monarchy">constitutional monarchy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It has been ruled by <a href="/wiki/Sultan_bin_Muhammad_Al-Qasimi" title="Sultan bin Muhammad Al-Qasimi">Sultan bin Muhammad Al-Qasimi</a> since 1972.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emirate_of_Sharjah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sharjah,_UAE.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2736" data-file-width="3648" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Sharjah%2C_UAE.jpg/220px-Sharjah%2C_UAE.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Sharjah%2C_UAE.jpg/330px-Sharjah%2C_UAE.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Sharjah%2C_UAE.jpg/440px-Sharjah%2C_UAE.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

Older residential area of Sharjah, displaying the local architecture</div>

<p>Human settlement in the area covered by the emirate has existed for over 120,000 years, with significant finds made of early axes and stone tools as well as Copper and Iron Age implements in Al Dhaid, Madam and Faya.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Archaeological finds in the Mleiha area point to human habitation consistent with the spread of humanity from Africa to the wider world, evidenced by finds displayed at the <a href="/wiki/Mleiha_Archaeological_Centre" title="Mleiha Archaeological Centre">Mleiha Archaeological Centre</a>.</p>
<p>Historically the emirate was one of the wealthiest towns in the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Around 1727, the <a href="/wiki/Al_Qasimi" title="Al Qasimi">Al Qasimi</a> clan took control of Sharjah and declared the polity independent.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The first in a long series of maritime skirmishes between the <a href="/wiki/Al_Qasimi" title="Al Qasimi">Al Qasimi</a> and British vessels took place in 1797, when the British-flagged <i>Bassein Snow</i> was seized and released two days later. The cruiser <i>Viper</i> was subsequently attacked off <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bushehr" title="Bushehr">Bushire</a>. A period of great instability followed along the coast, with a number of actions between British and <a href="/wiki/Al_Qasimi" title="Al Qasimi">Al Qasimi</a> vessels alongside various changes of leadership and allegiances between the Rulers of <a href="/wiki/Ras_al-Khaimah" title="Ras al-Khaimah">Ras Al Khaimah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ajman" title="Ajman">Ajman</a> and Sharjah with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultan_Bin_Saqr_Al_Qasimi" title="Sultan Bin Saqr Al Qasimi">Sheikh Sultan bin Saqr Al Qasimi</a> claiming sovereignty over 'all the Joasmee ports' in 1823, a claim recognised by the British at the time.</p>
<p>On 8 January 1820, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultan_Bin_Saqr_Al_Qasimi" title="Sultan Bin Saqr Al Qasimi">Sheikh Sultan bin Saqr Al Qasimi</a> signed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_General_Maritime_Treaty_with_Britain" title="The General Maritime Treaty with Britain">the General Maritime Treaty with Britain</a>, accepting protectorate status in order to resist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ottoman_Turks" title="Ottoman Turks">Ottoman</a> domination. Following the expiration of a further, ten year, treaty in 1843, on 4 May 1853 Sharjah, along with other Sheikhdoms on what was then known as the 'Arabian Coast' signed the Perpetual Treaty of Peace, which gave rise to the collective name <a href="/wiki/Trucial_States" title="Trucial States">Trucial States</a> being given to the emirates of the coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Like four of its neighbours, <a href="/wiki/Ajman" title="Ajman">Ajman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ras_al-Khaimah" title="Ras al-Khaimah">Ras al-Khaimah</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Umm_al-Qaiwain" title="Umm al-Qaiwain">Umm al-Qaiwain</a>, its position along trade routes to <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> made it important enough to be recognized as a <a href="/wiki/Salute_state" title="Salute state">salute state</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Throughout the 19th and early 20th centuries, Sharjah was an important pearl fishing port. A British marine survey of 1830 found 'three to four hundred boats' fishing in the season, earning the ruler 100,000 <a href="/wiki/Maria_Theresa_thaler" title="Maria Theresa thaler">Maria Theresa Dollars</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1932, <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Airways" title="Imperial Airways">Imperial Airways</a> established a regular air service through Sharjah, which was an overnight stop on the Eastern British Empire route. <a href="/wiki/Al_Mahatta_Fort" title="Al Mahatta Fort">Al Mahatta Fort</a> was built to house the airline's guests.</p>
<p>On 2 December 1971, <a href="/wiki/Khalid_bin_Mohammed_Al_Qasimi" title="Khalid bin Mohammed Al Qasimi">Sheikh Khalid bin Muhammad Al Qasimi</a> (Sheikh Khalid) joined Sharjah to the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-8">[8]</a></sup> On 24 January 1972 the former ruler Sheikh Saqr staged a leftist coup. Having previously deposed Saqr, Sheikh Khalid had ordered the demolition of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sharjah_Fort_(Al_Hisn_Sharjah)" title="Sharjah Fort (Al Hisn Sharjah)">Sharjah Fort</a> (Al Hisn Sharjah) to expiate Saqr's memory. Saqr took over Khalid's palace, holding him inside and in the ensuing confusion Sheikh Khalid was killed. Saqr was arrested and Khalid's brother, <a href="/wiki/Sultan_bin_Muhammad_Al-Qasimi" title="Sultan bin Muhammad Al-Qasimi">Sultan bin Muhammad Al-Qasimi</a>, an author and historian, came to power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1987 Sultan's brother Abdulaziz staged a coup while Sultan was abroad. Huge state debt was stated as the reason. UAE President Zayed vehemently denounced the coup, and a deal was reached for Sultan to be restored, while Abdulaziz would become the Deputy Ruler. Sultan, however, dismissed Abdulaziz quite quickly.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1999, the Crown Prince (Sultan's eldest son) died while on vacation in their palace in England. Sultan made the decision to testify in front of a UK court.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The new Crown Prince was appointed from a remote branch of the family.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emirate_of_Sharjah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tmulti tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:388px;max-width:388px">
<div class="tsingle" style="float:left;margin:1px;width:192px;max-width:192px">

<div class="thumbcaption" style="clear:left;text-align:left;background-color:transparent">The city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sharjah_(city)" title="Sharjah (city)">Sharjah</a>, the largest population center in the emirate</div>

<p>Sharjah is the third largest emirate in the United Arab Emirates, and is the only one to have land on both the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Oman" title="Gulf of Oman">Gulf of Oman</a>. The emirate covers 2,590 square kilometres (1,000 sq mi) which is equivalent to 3.3 per cent of the UAE's total area, excluding the islands. It has a population of over 800,000 (2008).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abu_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-abu-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The emirate of Sharjah comprises the city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sharjah_(city)" title="Sharjah (city)">Sharjah</a> (the seat of the emirate), and other minor towns and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enclave" title="Enclave">enclaves</a>. The <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> of Sharjah, which overlooks the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a>, has a population of 519,000 (2003 census estimate).</p>
<p>Sharjah City borders <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a> to the south and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ajm%C3%A2n" title="Ajmân">Ajmân</a> to the north and the three form a conurbation.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Khorfakkan_beach.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="575" data-file-width="1024" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Khorfakkan_beach.jpg/220px-Khorfakkan_beach.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Khorfakkan_beach.jpg/330px-Khorfakkan_beach.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Khorfakkan_beach.jpg/440px-Khorfakkan_beach.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Khorfakkan_beach.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Khor_Fakkan" title="Khor Fakkan">Khor Fakkan</a> beach</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The city lies some 170 kilometers away from the UAE capital city <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a>.</p>
<p>Sharjah also owns three enclaves on the east coast, bordering the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Oman" title="Gulf of Oman">Gulf of Oman</a>. These are <a href="/wiki/Kalba" title="Kalba">Kalba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dibba_Al-Hisn" title="Dibba Al-Hisn">Dibba Al-Hisn</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Khor_Fakkan" title="Khor Fakkan">Khor Fakkan</a>, which provides Sharjah with a major east coast port. In the Persian Gulf, the island of <a href="/wiki/Sir_Abu_Nu%E2%80%99ayr" title="Sir Abu Nu’ayr">Sir Abu Nu’ayr</a> belongs to Sharjah,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abu_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-abu-2">[2]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Abu_Musa" title="Abu Musa">Abu Musa</a> is claimed by UAE, but controlled by <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>. Sharjah has an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exclave" title="Exclave">exclave</a> containing the village of <a href="/wiki/Nahwa" title="Nahwa">Nahwa</a>, located inside the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Omani" title="Omani">Omani</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enclave" title="Enclave">enclave</a> of <a href="/wiki/Madha" title="Madha">Madha</a> which borders <a href="/wiki/Fujairah" title="Fujairah">Fujairah</a> and Sharjah.</p>
<p>Sharjah also encompasses some important oasis areas, the most famous of which is the fertile <a href="/wiki/Dhaid" title="Dhaid">Dhaid</a> region, where a range of vegetables and fruits are cultivated.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emirate_of_Sharjah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Superior_Court_of_Sharjah.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2736" data-file-width="3648" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Superior_Court_of_Sharjah.jpg/220px-Superior_Court_of_Sharjah.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Superior_Court_of_Sharjah.jpg/330px-Superior_Court_of_Sharjah.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Superior_Court_of_Sharjah.jpg/440px-Superior_Court_of_Sharjah.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Superior_Court_of_Sharjah.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Superior Court of Sharjah</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Sharjah Municipality was founded in 1927. A decree issued in 1971 defined the role of the contemporary municipality, which encompasses public health, agricultural policy and management and infrastructural and engineering support. Water and electricity services are provided by <a class="external text" href="https://www.sewa.gov.ae/en/" rel="nofollow">Sharjah Electricity and Water Authority</a> (SEWA) and waste management by recycling and waste management company <a class="external text" href="http://www.beeah-uae.com/" rel="nofollow">Bea'ah</a>. Company registration and other aspects of economic planning and activity are undertaken by the <a class="external text" href="http://www.sedd.ae/" rel="nofollow">Economic Development Department</a>.</p>
<p>A Sharjah <a class="external text" href="http://www.sharjah.ae/" rel="nofollow">e-government portal</a> offers access to a number of services online.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Commerce_and_Tourism_Development_Authority">Commerce and Tourism Development Authority</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emirate_of_Sharjah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Commerce and Tourism Development Authority">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Sharjah Commerce and Tourism Development Authority was established by decree in 1996 to "promote commercial and tourism activities" in the emirate. The state-backed authority is responsible for, among other things, creating policies for local and international exhibitions and marketing Sharjah to attract foreign investment and tourists.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Investment_and_Development_Authority">Investment and Development Authority</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emirate_of_Sharjah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Investment and Development Authority">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sharjah_eyes1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1024" data-file-width="1009" height="223" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Sharjah_eyes1.jpg/220px-Sharjah_eyes1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Sharjah_eyes1.jpg/330px-Sharjah_eyes1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Sharjah_eyes1.jpg/440px-Sharjah_eyes1.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sharjah_eyes1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
"The Eye," the observation tower at Al Qasba</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Sharjah Investment and Development Authority (Shurooq) was established in 2009 as an independent government body to "oversee the social, cultural, environmental and economic development of Sharjah in line with its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamic" title="Islamic">Islamic</a> identity." It focuses on three areas for the emirate; investment, development, and asset and property management. Shurooq's developments include:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Al Qasba</b> – 10,0000-acre project including a 1 km-long man-made <a href="/wiki/Canal" title="Canal">canal</a> and 60 m high observation wheel, it was made open for the public in 2005. It includes Masrah Al Qasba, a theatre, the Maraya Art Centre, the Multaqa Al Qasba meetings and conference centre and a children’s Fun Zone. The project, managed by the Al Qasba Development Authority (an affiliate of Shurooq) has an architectural design including pedestrian bridges, boats, and lagoons. It attracted two million visitors in 2011.</li>
<li><b>Al Majaz Waterfront</b> – Shurooq opened the Al Majaz Waterfront in December 2011 after redeveloping it. The new park, located between Jamal Abdul Nasser Street and Khalid Lagoon Corniche, covers an area of three km<sup>2</sup> for recreation, including six new buildings with restaurants and a 100 m tall water fountain.</li>
<li><b>Heart of Sharjah</b> – The Heart of Sharjah, slated for completion in January 2015, has been proposed by Shurooq as a tourist and trade destination with commercial, cultural and residential projects as well as hotel, archaeological sites, museums and commercial space.</li>
<li><b>The Chedi Khorfakkan Resort</b> – The Chedi Khorfakkan, scheduled to open in 2015, is under construction an hour outside of Sharjah city centre. Shurooq has stated that once complete, the project will include a tourist resort and a fort on the Al Suwifa Mountain.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Decency_Laws">Decency Laws</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emirate_of_Sharjah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Decency Laws">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Conservative Sharjah is the only Emirate in the UAE in which the sale of <a href="/wiki/Alcohol" title="Alcohol">alcohol</a> is prohibited, although its consumption in one's own home is permissible if you are in possession of a valid Alcohol Licence (as is the transportation of alcohol between the place of sale and the home). The only place this prohibition is relaxed is the members-only sporting club, the <i><a href="/wiki/Sharjah_Wanderers" title="Sharjah Wanderers">Sharjah Wanderers</a></i>. Sharjah also maintains the strictest decency laws in the UAE, introduced in 2001, with a conservative dress code required for both men and women. Mixing between unmarried men and women is illegal: "A man and a woman who are not in a legally acceptable relationship should not be alone in public places, or in suspicious times or circumstances." According to a booklet published by the municipality in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-six_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-six-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_Trade_Zones">Free Trade Zones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emirate_of_Sharjah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Free Trade Zones">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sharjah has two main free trade zones: the seaport and free trade zone of Hamriyah and the Sharjah Airport International Free Trade Zone (SAIF Zone).</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Hamriyah_Free_Zone" title="Hamriyah Free Zone">Hamriyah Free Zone</a> Authority was established by an Emiri decree issued on November 12, 1995 and consists of a port and associated logistics and trade facilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Also established in 1995, the SAIF Zone is adjacent to Sharjah International Airport and is currently home to some 2,500 companies.</p>

<p>Sharjah has long invested considerable resources in building a strong cultural identity and heritage and was named in 1998 as <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>'s '<a href="/wiki/Arab_Capital_of_Culture" title="Arab Capital of Culture">Arab Capital of Culture</a>', an event commemorated by a large pillar erected opposite the Sharjah Desert Park and Arabian Wildlife Centre between Sharjah and Dhaid cities.</p>
<p>Sharjah was also named as <a class="external text" href="http://sicc.ae/en/" rel="nofollow">Islamic Culture Capital</a> 2014 by the Islamic Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (<a href="/wiki/Islamic_Educational,_Scientific_and_Cultural_Organization" title="Islamic Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization">ISESCO</a>), a specialized institution of the Organization of Islamic Cooperation (OIC) in the fields of Education, Science and Culture. This is commemorated by a (smaller) pillar mounted to the front of Sharjah's University City.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sharjah_Art_Foundation">Sharjah Art Foundation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emirate_of_Sharjah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Sharjah Art Foundation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Sharjah_Art_Foundation" title="Sharjah Art Foundation">Sharjah Art Foundation</a> works on a broad range of contemporary art and cultural programmes to the communities of Sharjah, the UAE and the region, including the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sharjah_Biennale" title="Sharjah Biennale">Sharjah Biennial</a> which first took place in 1993. Founded in 2009, SAF also organises the annual March Meeting, residencies, production grants, commissions, exhibitions, research, publications and a growing art collection as well as a number of education and public programmes. Sharjah Art Foundation is funded by the Department of Culture and Information.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sharjah_Light_Festival">Sharjah Light Festival</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emirate_of_Sharjah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Sharjah Light Festival">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a class="external text" href="http://www.sharjahlightfestival.ae/" rel="nofollow">Sharjah Light Festival</a> (SCTDA) is a nightly art exhibit with local and international artists that takes place in public places. The festival, which is the first event of its kind in the region, runs for nine nights in the month of February using the latest graphics and lighting techniques and 3-D technology. The festival takes place in 14 locations across the emirate offering a unique combination of light and music to create innovative three-dimensional designs on mosques, key landmarks and heritage buildings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Postage_stamps_and_postal_history_of_Sharjah" title="Postage stamps and postal history of Sharjah">Postage stamps and postal history of Sharjah</a></div>
<p>In 1963, Britain ceded responsibility for the Trucial States' postal systems. An American philatelic entrepreneur by the name of Finbar Kenny saw the opportunity to create a number of editions of stamps aimed at the lucrative collector's market and in 1964 concluded a deal with a number of Trucial States to take the franchise for the production of stamps for their respective governments. Kenny had made something of a specialty out of signing these deals, also signing with the Rulers of Ajman and Fujairah in 1964—and getting involved in a bribery case in the USA over his dealings with the government of the Cook Islands. The issue of stamps from <a href="/wiki/Manama,_Ajman" title="Manama, Ajman">Ajman's dependency of Manama</a>—a tiny agrarian village in the remote plains at which a 'post office' was opened—is a perfect example.</p>
<p>These stamps, luridly illustrated and irrelevant to the actual emirates they purported to come from (editions included 'Space Research' and 'Tokyo Olympic Games') became known as 'dunes'. Their proliferation quickly devalued them.</p>
<p>Sharjah is therefore known by many <a href="/wiki/Stamp_collecting" title="Stamp collecting">stamp collectors</a> for these issues by the <a href="/wiki/Postage_stamps_and_postal_history_of_Sharjah" title="Postage stamps and postal history of Sharjah">Sharjah Post Office</a> shortly before the formation of the United Arab Emirates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Many of these items are not listed by many popular catalogues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cricket">Cricket</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emirate_of_Sharjah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Cricket">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sharjah_Cricket_Association_Stadium" title="Sharjah Cricket Association Stadium">Sharjah Cricket Association Stadium</a> has hosted almost 200 <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a> <a href="/wiki/One_Day_International" title="One Day International">One Day Internationals</a>, more than any other ground in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UAE" title="UAE">UAE</a>, and 4 <a href="/wiki/Test_cricket" title="Test cricket">Test matches</a>. Since 2003, the increasingly crowded cricket calendar has precluded the holding of any major international matches at Sharjah although the stadium has been the venue for certain other matches, such as the 2004 ICC Intercontinental Cup. The use of the venue has declined as the new 20,000 seat Sheikh Zayed Stadium in Abu Dhabi has become the preferred venue for cricket in the UAE.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Airports">Airports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emirate_of_Sharjah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Airports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>International airports in the city of Sharjah include the <a href="/wiki/Sharjah_International_Airport" title="Sharjah International Airport">Sharjah International Airport</a> and Port Khalid.</p>
<p>Sharjah International Airport (<a href="/wiki/International_Air_Transport_Association_airport_code" title="International Air Transport Association airport code">IATA</a>: SHJ) has connections to all major international locations. It is 10 kilometres from Sharjah City Centre and about 15 kilometres away from Dubai. It is a major cargo airport and the main base of <a href="/wiki/Air_Arabia" title="Air Arabia">Air Arabia</a>. The airport served total 4,324,313 passengers and 51,314 flights in 2007. It also handled 570,363 tonnes of cargo in the same year. Over 60,000 aircraft movements were recorded in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eight_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-eight-20">[20]</a></sup> It handled 6,634,570 passengers in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nine_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-nine-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sharjah International Airport is located 10 kilometres from Sharjah city centre and 15 km from Dubai. As of 2012 it has 34 international carriers and 13 cargo airlines. In 2008, Sharjah International Airport announced it would invest AED662m (US$180m) to renovate the airport’s buildings, build a new terminal and increase car parking. The project is slated for completion in 2015, by which time the airport aims to be handling eight million passengers annually.</p>
<dl>
<dt>Air Arabia</dt>
</dl>
<p>Sharjah International Airport is the main base of <a href="/wiki/Air_Arabia" title="Air Arabia">Air Arabia</a>, the Middle East’s largest low cost carrier. It was the Middle East’s first budget airline when launched by the Ruler of Sharjah in 2003. The number of passengers flying to its near 70 destinations grew six percent in 2011 to 4.7 million. Net profit for 2011 was AED274m (US$74.6m), down 13 percent on the previous year. The airline, which also has hubs in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> (<a href="/wiki/Borg_El_Arab_Airport" title="Borg El Arab Airport">Borg El Arab Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexandria" title="Alexandria">Alexandria</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a> (<a href="/wiki/Mohammed_V_International_Airport" title="Mohammed V International Airport">Mohammed V International Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Casablanca" title="Casablanca">Casablanca</a>), delayed plans to establish a fourth hub in <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a> in 2012.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_transportation">Public transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emirate_of_Sharjah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Public transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Sharjah Public Transport Corporation (SPTC) has started the public transport system in Sharjah from May 23, 2008 with 11 buses running on the first route, Route 14 from Sharjah International Airport to Al-Sharq terminal. By November 2008, 142 buses are expected to operate on 18 routes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ten_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-ten-22">[22]</a></sup> In February 2010, there were 115 Sharjah intercity buses, which make 250 trips daily. The tariff for these intercity buses ranges from AED 5 to AED 30.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eleven_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-eleven-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxis">Taxis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emirate_of_Sharjah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Taxis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Metered Taxis are available in Sharjah for the intra-city as well as the inter-city travel. The base fare is AED 11.50 with AED 1 charged for every 650 meters of travel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twelve_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-twelve-24">[24]</a></sup> For intra-city travel, the minimum fare is AED 11.50 and for Sharjah to <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a> travel, base fare is AED 20.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="City_of_Sharjah">City of Sharjah</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emirate_of_Sharjah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: City of Sharjah">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sharjah_(city)" title="Sharjah (city)">Sharjah (city)</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> of Sharjah contains the main administrative and commercial centers, as well as a number of cultural institutions including several museums. Distinctive landmarks are the two major covered <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Souk" title="Souk">souks</a>, reflecting Islamic design; a number of recreational areas and public parks such as Al Jazeirah Fun Park and Al Buheirah Corniche. The city is also notable for numerous <a href="/wiki/Mosque" title="Mosque">mosques</a>.</p>
