<p><b>Charles IV, Duke of Anjou</b>, also <b>Charles of Maine, Count of Le Maine and Guise</b> (1446 – 10 December 1481) was the son of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Valois-Anjou" title="House of Valois-Anjou">Angevin</a> prince <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charles_of_Le_Maine" title="Charles of Le Maine">Charles of Le Maine</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Count_of_Maine" title="Count of Maine">Count of Maine</a>, who was the youngest son of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis_II_of_Anjou" title="Louis II of Anjou">Louis II of Anjou</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yolande_of_Aragon" title="Yolande of Aragon">Yolande of Aragon</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_of_Four_Kingdoms" title="Queen of Four Kingdoms">Queen of Four Kingdoms</a>.</p>
<p>He succeeded his father as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Count_of_Maine" title="Count of Maine">Count of Maine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guise" title="Guise">Guise</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mortain" title="Mortain">Mortain</a> and Gien in 1472. He succeeded his uncle <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_I_of_Naples" title="René I of Naples">René I of Naples</a> in 1480 as fifth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duke_of_Anjou" title="Duke of Anjou">Duke of Anjou</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Count_of_Provence" title="Count of Provence">Count of Provence</a>, according to the will of René who had no surviving son.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGriersonTravaini1998338_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGriersonTravaini1998338-1">[1]</a></sup> René's surviving daughter Yolande received <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/County_of_Bar" title="County of Bar">Bar</a> and was already <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duchess_of_Lorraine" title="Duchess of Lorraine">Duchess of Lorraine</a>.</p>
<p>He also used the title of <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Calabria" title="Duke of Calabria">Duke of Calabria</a>, in token of the claims to <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a> he inherited from René.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGriersonTravaini1998338_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGriersonTravaini1998338-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1474 he married Joan of Lorraine (1458 – 25 January 1480), daughter of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frederick_II_of_Vaud%C3%A9mont" title="Frederick II of Vaudémont">Frederick II of Vaudémont</a>, but they had no children. He died on 10 December 1481.</p>
<p>He willed his inheritance to his cousin <a href="/wiki/Louis_XI_of_France" title="Louis XI of France">Louis XI of France</a>, whose heirs thus obtained a claim to the affairs of <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, pursued in the next decades.</p>
