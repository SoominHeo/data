<p><b>Chamalières</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Auvergnat_language" title="Auvergnat language">Auvergnat</a>: <i>Chamalèira</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_France" title="Communes of France">commune</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Puy-de-D%C3%B4me" title="Puy-de-Dôme">Puy-de-Dôme</a> <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_France" title="Departments of France">department</a> in <a href="/wiki/Auvergne-Rh%C3%B4ne-Alpes" title="Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes">Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes</a> in central <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.</p>
<p>Chamalières is the third-largest town in the department and lies about 150 miles (241 km) from <a href="/wiki/Lyon" title="Lyon">Lyon</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chamali%C3%A8res&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Several thousand wooden <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gallo-Roman" title="Gallo-Roman">Gallo-Roman</a> <a href="/wiki/Ex-voto" title="Ex-voto">ex-votos</a>, most of them anthropomorphic standing figures, also including images of limbs and internal organs, dated by associated coins to the first century, were recovered from the shrine at the mineral springs known as the <i>Source des Roches</i> ("Rock Spring"). An inscribed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chamali%C3%A8res_Tablet" title="Chamalières Tablet">lead tablet</a> found at the spring is a major source of information on the <a href="/wiki/Gaulish_language" title="Gaulish language">Gaulish language</a>. A comparable cache of Gaulish <i>ex-voto</i> were recovered from a sanctuary at the sources of the <a href="/wiki/Seine" title="Seine">Seine</a>, sacred to <a href="/wiki/Sequana" title="Sequana">Sequana</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_places">Notable places</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chamali%C3%A8res&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Notable places">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chamalières is the place where the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Banque_de_France" title="Banque de France">Banque de France</a> located its printing works in 1923, which printed former <a href="/wiki/French_franc" title="French franc">French franc</a> banknotes, and now prints <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">Euro</a> banknotes.</p>
