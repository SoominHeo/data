<p><b>Xiamen Gaoqi International Airport</b> (<a href="/wiki/International_Air_Transport_Association_airport_code" title="International Air Transport Association airport code">IATA</a>: <b>XMN</b>, <a href="/wiki/International_Civil_Aviation_Organization_airport_code" title="International Civil Aviation Organization airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>ZSAM</b>) is the airport serving the city of <a href="/wiki/Xiamen" title="Xiamen">Xiamen</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fujian_Province" title="Fujian Province">Fujian Province</a>, China. It is the main base of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xiamen_Airlines" title="Xiamen Airlines">Xiamen Airlines</a> and TAECO, an aircraft maintenance provider. The airport is located on the north side of <a href="/wiki/Xiamen_Island" title="Xiamen Island">Xiamen Island</a>. Construction of a new terminal started in October 2011 and was completed in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, Xiamen airport was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/China%27s_busiest_airports_by_cargo_traffic" title="China's busiest airports by cargo traffic">8th busiest</a> airport in China in terms of cargo traffic, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/China%27s_busiest_airports_by_passenger_traffic" title="China's busiest airports by passenger traffic">11th busiest</a> in terms of passenger traffic with 17,354,076 passengers and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/China%27s_busiest_airports_by_traffic_movements" title="China's busiest airports by traffic movements">10th busiest</a> airport by traffic movements.</p>
