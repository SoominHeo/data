<p><b>Shamo Quaye</b> (22 October 1971 – 30 November 1997) was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> player from <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a>, who was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Ghana_national_football_team" title="Ghana national football team">Men's National Team</a> that won the bronze medal at the <a href="/wiki/1992_Summer_Olympics" title="1992 Summer Olympics">1992 Summer Olympics</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barcelona,_Spain" title="Barcelona, Spain">Barcelona, Spain</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shamo_Quaye&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He played for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Accra_Hearts_of_Oak_SC" title="Accra Hearts of Oak SC">Hearts of Oak</a> in his native country. At the time of death he was playing for Umeå FC in Sweden, but he was in his home country with the national team. During practice Quaye was hit in the face. He died two days later from sudden complications.</p>
