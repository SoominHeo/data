<p><b>Lower Shebelle</b> (<a href="/wiki/Somali_language" title="Somali language">Somali</a>: <span lang="so" xml:lang="so"><i>Shabeellaha Hoose</i></span>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">شبيلي السفلى</span>‎‎) is an administrative region (<i><a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Somalia" title="Administrative divisions of Somalia">gobol</a></i>) in southern <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cwfsom_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cwfsom-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lower_Shebelle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is bordered by the Somali regions of <a href="/wiki/Banaadir" title="Banaadir">Banaadir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Middle_Shebelle" title="Middle Shebelle">Middle Shebelle</a> (Shabeellaha Dhexe), <a href="/wiki/Hiran,_Somalia" title="Hiran, Somalia">Hiran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bay,_Somalia" title="Bay, Somalia">Bay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Middle_Juba" title="Middle Juba">Middle Juba</a> (Jubbada Dhexe), and the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>. Lower Shebelle is named after the <a href="/wiki/Shebelle_River" title="Shebelle River">Shebelle River</a>, which passes through it.</p>
<p>Until 1984, when the regions were reassigned, it was part of the larger <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benadir" title="Benadir">Benadir</a> region and its capital was <a href="/wiki/Mogadishu" title="Mogadishu">Mogadishu</a>. However, the provincial capital is now <a href="/wiki/Merca" title="Merca">Merca</a>.</p>
