<p><b>Xiaogang District</b> (<a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">小港區</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Xiǎogāng Qū</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Wade%E2%80%93Giles" title="Wade–Giles">Wade–Giles</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-wadegile" xml:lang="zh-Latn-wadegile">Hsiao<sup>3</sup>-kang<sup>1</sup> Ch'ü<sup>1</sup></span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Pe%CC%8Dh-%C5%8De-j%C4%AB" title="Pe̍h-ōe-jī">Pe̍h-ōe-jī</a>: <i><span lang="nan" xml:lang="nan">Sió-káng-khu</span></i>) is the southernmost district of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kaohsiung_City" title="Kaohsiung City">Kaohsiung City</a> in southern <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>. The second largest airport in Taiwan, <a href="/wiki/Kaohsiung_International_Airport" title="Kaohsiung International Airport">Kaohsiung International Airport</a>, is located here.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Siaogang_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Xiaogang was developed during <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Formosa" title="Dutch Formosa">Dutch era</a>. The Dutch built <a href="/wiki/Ang_mo" title="Ang mo">Ang mo</a> Harbor as an addition to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anping,_Tainan" title="Anping, Tainan">Anping</a> Harbor in <a href="/wiki/Tainan" title="Tainan">Tainan</a>. After losing the war against <a href="/wiki/Koxinga" title="Koxinga">Koxinga</a>, the Dutch left Taiwan. Koxinga's government developed Taiwan as the base to revive the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ming_Dynasty" title="Ming Dynasty">Ming Dynasty</a> and prepare his troops for fighting the <a href="/wiki/Qing_dynasty" title="Qing dynasty">Qing dynasty</a>. He sent one of his men, Wu Yanshang, to expand Xiaogang. After Koxinga, his son, and his grandson died, the Qing quickly defeated the Ming remnants and annexed Taiwan.</p>
<p>In 1895, the Qing dynasty ceded Taiwan to <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Shimonoseki" title="Treaty of Shimonoseki">Treaty of Shimonoseki</a> after losing the <a href="/wiki/First_Sino-Japanese_War" title="First Sino-Japanese War">First Sino-Japanese War</a>, and Xiaogang was used to produce <a href="/wiki/Sugar" title="Sugar">sugar</a> for Japanese. In 1920, the original name of <i>Kang-a-ki</i> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">港仔墘</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pe%CC%8Dh-%C5%8De-j%C4%AB" title="Pe̍h-ōe-jī">Pe̍h-ōe-jī</a>: <i><span lang="nan" xml:lang="nan">Káng-á-kîⁿ</span></i>) was changed, and the incorporated areas were administered as Kominato Village <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">小港庄</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> under Hōzan District, <a href="/wiki/Takao_Prefecture" title="Takao Prefecture">Takao Prefecture</a>.</p>
<p>As a heavy industry base in <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, Xiaogang was more heavily bombed by the <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allied</a> force than other parts of the city. After the war, Xiaogang was administrated as <b>Xiaogang Township</b> of <a href="/wiki/Kaohsiung_County" title="Kaohsiung County">Kaohsiung County</a>, a third-level government of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_China" title="Republic of China">Republic of China</a>, under <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Province" title="Taiwan Province">Taiwan Province</a>. Xiaogang was annexed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kaohsiung_City" title="Kaohsiung City">Kaohsiung City</a> and become <b>Xiaogang District</b> on 1 July 1979, when Kaohsiung City became a <a href="/wiki/Special_municipality_(Taiwan)" title="Special municipality (Taiwan)">special municipality</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Siaogang_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Xiaogang is located in the southern part of Kaohsiung. It borders <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qianzhen_District" title="Qianzhen District">Qianzhen District</a> to the north, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daliao" title="Daliao">Daliao</a> Township to the east, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Linyuan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Linyuan (page does not exist)">Linyuan</a> Township to the south, and the <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Strait" title="Taiwan Strait">Taiwan Strait</a> to the west.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Siaogang_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Linhai_Industrial_Park" title="Linhai Industrial Park">Linhai Industrial Park</a></div>
<p>As far as the industry of <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> is concerned, Xiaogang is one of the most important regions for the shipbuilding and steel industries. During the 1970s, the government spent 5 years carrying out <a href="/wiki/Ten_Major_Construction_Projects" title="Ten Major Construction Projects">Ten Major Construction Projects</a>. Two companies resulting from the projects are based here: <a href="/wiki/China_Steel" title="China Steel">China Steel</a> and <a href="/wiki/CSBC_Corporation,_Taiwan" title="CSBC Corporation, Taiwan">CSBC Corporation, Taiwan</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CPC_Corporation,_Taiwan" title="CPC Corporation, Taiwan">CPC Corporation, Taiwan</a> also a part of the ten projects, has a major oil refinery facility located here. With the proximity of Kaohsiung Harbor, the products of these companies are exported worldwide. These products are also transported throughout the island via <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Highway_No._1_(Taiwan)" title="National Highway No. 1 (Taiwan)">National Highway No. 1 (Taiwan)</a>, which terminates on the northern border of the district.</p>

<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kao_Tower&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kao Tower (page does not exist)">Kao Tower</a>. The name comes from its shape: it is built in the shape of Chinese character "<a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E9%AB%98" title="wikt:高">高</a>" (pronounced "Gao", meaning high, tall). It was once a lighthouse. After the building of second harbor of Kaohsiung, it was renewed and became a tourist attraction. The shape of the building is not only a Chinese character but also represent the spirit of Kaohsiung.</li>
</ul>
