<p><b>Šiauliai County</b> (Lithuanian: <b>Šiaulių apskritis</b>) is one of ten <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Lithuania" title="Counties of Lithuania">counties</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a>. It is in the north of the country, and its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capital_(political)" title="Capital (political)">capital</a> is <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0iauliai" title="Šiauliai">Šiauliai</a>. On 1 July 2010, the county administration was abolished,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and since that date, Šiauliai County remains as the territorial and statistical unit.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0iauliai_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Formation of administrative regions in Lithuania started in the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Lithuania" title="Grand Duchy of Lithuania">Grand Duchy of Lithuania</a> in the 18th century.</p>
<p>In October 1795, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catherine_II_of_Russia" title="Catherine II of Russia">Catherine II of Russia</a> granted Šiauliai the city rights and the privilege to become the capital town of the region. Administrative division of <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> remained unchanged up to the end of <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. When the war came to its end, in 1918 Lithuania was restored as an independent state.</p>
<p>On December 17, 1918, a circular No.1 was issued "On Municipalities in Lithuania" that declared that the entire area of Lithuania would be divided into the regions - apskritys (county sometimes translated into English as a provinces or counties). There were 10 apskritys in Lithuania.</p>
<p>In 1937 Siauliai county's overall territory was 6042 km² and 210 thousands people lived there. It was the largest apskritis in Lithuania.</p>
<p>In 1940 the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> occupied Lithuania and reformed the administrative system. The county system remained until 1950 when the counties were abolished and entire area was divided into smaller units - districts, or <a href="/wiki/Raion" title="Raion">rajonas</a> in Lithuanian.</p>
<p>Only in 1994 - four years after the restoration of Lithuania's independence in 1990 - the apskritys were created again. However, the interwar apskritys should not be mixed with current apskritys, as their purpose, sizes, and number are all different, see <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_division_of_Lithuania" title="Administrative division of Lithuania">administrative division of Lithuania</a>. The entire area of Lithuania is currently divided into 10 larger units - apskritys and smaller districts, consisting of urban and rural <a href="/wiki/Elderships_of_Lithuania" title="Elderships of Lithuania">elderships</a>.</p>
