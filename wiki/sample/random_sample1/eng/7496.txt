<p><b>Schaumburg-Lippe</b> was created as a county in 1647, became a principality in 1807, a free state in 1918, and was until 1946 a small state in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, located in the present day state of <a href="/wiki/Lower_Saxony" title="Lower Saxony">Lower Saxony</a>, with its capital at <a href="/wiki/B%C3%BCckeburg" title="Bückeburg">Bückeburg</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Principality_of_Schaumburg-Lippe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Schaumburg-Lippe was formed as a county in 1647 through the division of the <a href="/wiki/County_of_Schaumburg" title="County of Schaumburg">County of Schaumburg</a> by treaties between the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duke_of_Brunswick-L%C3%BCneburg" title="Duke of Brunswick-Lüneburg">Duke of Brunswick-Lüneburg</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landgrave_of_Hesse-Kassel" title="Landgrave of Hesse-Kassel">Landgrave of Hesse-Kassel</a> and the Count of <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Lippe" title="Principality of Lippe">Lippe</a>. The division occurred because Count Otto V of Holstein-Schaumburg had died in 1640 leaving no male heir. Initially Schaumburg-Lippe's position was somewhat precarious: it had to share a wide variety of institutions and facilities with the County of Schaumburg (which belonged to Hesse-Kassel), including the representative assembly and the highly productive <a href="/wiki/B%C3%BCckeberg" title="Bückeberg">Bückeberg</a> mines, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landgrave_of_Hesse-Kassel" title="Landgrave of Hesse-Kassel">Landgrave of Hesse-Kassel</a> retained some feudal rights over it. It was further threatened by the headstrong policies of ruling <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friedrich_Christian,_Count_of_Schaumburg-Lippe" title="Friedrich Christian, Count of Schaumburg-Lippe">Count Friedrich Christian</a>. To counter these threats, Friedrich's grandson <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wilhelm,_Count_of_Schaumburg-Lippe" title="Wilhelm, Count of Schaumburg-Lippe">Count Wilhelm</a> (who reigned 1748–1777) retained a standing army of up to 1000 troops - quite a lot for such a small territory.</p>
<p>With Wilhelm's death in 1777 the junior <a href="/wiki/Lippe-Alverdissen" title="Lippe-Alverdissen">Schaumburg-Lippe-Alverdissen</a> inherited the County thereby reuniting Schaumburg-Lippe with Lippe-Alverdissen.</p>
<p>Schaumburg-Lippe was a county until 1807 when it became a principality; from 1871 it was a state within the <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German Empire</a>. In 1913, it was the smallest state in the German Empire in terms of population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The capital was <a href="/wiki/B%C3%BCckeburg" title="Bückeburg">Bückeburg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Stadthagen" title="Stadthagen">Stadthagen</a> was the only other town. Under the constitution of 1868, there was a legislative diet of 15 members, 10 elected by the towns and rural districts and 1 each by the nobility, clergy and educated classes, the remaining 2 nominated by the prince. Schaumburg-Lippe sent one member to the <a href="/wiki/Bundesrat_of_Germany" title="Bundesrat of Germany">Bundesrat</a> (federal council) and one deputy to the <a href="/wiki/Reichstag_(German_Empire)" title="Reichstag (German Empire)">Reichstag</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It lasted until <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_Revolution" title="German Revolution">the end of the German monarchies</a> in 1918, when it became a <a href="/wiki/Free_state_(government)#Germany" title="Free state (government)">free state</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Free_State_of_Schaumburg-Lippe" title="Free State of Schaumburg-Lippe">Free State of Schaumburg-Lippe</a>. In November 1918, Prince Adolf was the second last reigning German monarch to abdicate.</p>
