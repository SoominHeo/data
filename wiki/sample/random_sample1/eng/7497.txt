<p><b>Kenneth</b> "<b>Chi</b>" <b>McBride</b> (born September 23, 1961) is an American actor. He starred as high school principal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steven_Harper_(Boston_Public)" title="Steven Harper (Boston Public)">Steven Harper</a> on the series <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Public" title="Boston Public">Boston Public</a></i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emerson_Cod" title="Emerson Cod">Emerson Cod</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Pushing_Daisies" title="Pushing Daisies">Pushing Daisies</a></i>, Detective Laverne Winston on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fox_(TV_network)" title="Fox (TV network)">Fox</a> drama <i><a href="/wiki/Human_Target_(2010_TV_series)" title="Human Target (2010 TV series)">Human Target</a></i>, and more recently Detective Don Owen in the short-lived <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> crime drama <i><a href="/wiki/Golden_Boy_(TV_series)" title="Golden Boy (TV series)">Golden Boy</a></i>. He currently plays in a main role as Captain Lou Grover of the Five-0 taskforce in the CBS drama <i><a href="/wiki/Hawaii_Five-0_(2010_TV_series)" title="Hawaii Five-0 (2010 TV series)">Hawaii Five-0</a></i>. He has also appeared in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Gone_in_60_Seconds_(2000_film)" title="Gone in 60 Seconds (2000 film)">Gone in 60 Seconds</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Terminal" title="The Terminal">The Terminal</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/I,_Robot_(film)" title="I, Robot (film)">I, Robot</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Roll_Bounce" title="Roll Bounce">Roll Bounce</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Draft_Day" title="Draft Day">Draft Day</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chi_McBride&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McBride was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chicago,_Illinois" title="Chicago, Illinois">Chicago, Illinois</a>, from which his nickname, Chi, derives. He was raised in the <a href="/wiki/Seventh-day_Adventist_Church" title="Seventh-day Adventist Church">Seventh-day Adventist</a> religion<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and attended Shiloh Academy, now known as Chicago SDA Academy, a Seventh-day Adventist school. He graduated from high school at the age of 16. McBride originally planned to pursue a career in music. After studying several instruments and singing with gospel choirs in his native Chicago, he relocated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" title="Atlanta, Georgia">Atlanta, Georgia</a> in 1986 to work for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Telephone_%26_Telegraph" title="American Telephone &amp; Telegraph">AT&amp;T</a> as a billing clerk.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p>His first success in show business came with the hit song "He's the Champ", which parodied the marriage of boxer <a href="/wiki/Mike_Tyson" title="Mike Tyson">Mike Tyson</a> and actress <a href="/wiki/Robin_Givens" title="Robin Givens">Robin Givens</a>. Based on the tune's success, McBride was signed by Esquire Records and he joined the rhythm and blues band Covert. Convinced he should try his hand in front of the camera, the singer moved to Los Angeles and, billed as "Chi", landed guest spots on Fox's <i><a href="/wiki/In_Living_Color" title="In Living Color">In Living Color</a></i> and NBC's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fresh_Prince_of_Bel-Air" title="The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air">The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air</a></i> as well as a featured role in the TV movie <i><a href="/wiki/Revenge_of_the_Nerds_III:_The_Next_Generation" title="Revenge of the Nerds III: The Next Generation">Revenge of the Nerds III: The Next Generation</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 1998, he was a co-star of <i><a href="/wiki/Mercury_Rising" title="Mercury Rising">Mercury Rising</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Willis" title="Bruce Willis">Bruce Willis</a>. He was later given the role of Principal Steven Harper on the series <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Public" title="Boston Public">Boston Public</a></i>. Much of McBride's work sees him playing right-hand man to the hero, as in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Mercury_Rising" title="Mercury Rising">Mercury Rising</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Terminal" title="The Terminal">The Terminal</a></i>. He is notable for his television roles on <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Public" title="Boston Public">Boston Public</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_John_Larroquette_Show" title="The John Larroquette Show">The John Larroquette Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/House_(TV_series)" title="House (TV series)">House</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Secret_Diary_of_Desmond_Pfeiffer" title="The Secret Diary of Desmond Pfeiffer">The Secret Diary of Desmond Pfeiffer</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Killer_Instinct_(TV_series)" title="Killer Instinct (TV series)">Killer Instinct</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Pushing_Daisies" title="Pushing Daisies">Pushing Daisies</a></i>.</p>
<p>McBride's film credits include <i><a href="/wiki/Cradle_2_the_Grave" title="Cradle 2 the Grave">Cradle 2 the Grave</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Distinguished_Gentleman" title="The Distinguished Gentleman">The Distinguished Gentleman</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Gone_in_60_Seconds_(2000_film)" title="Gone in 60 Seconds (2000 film)">Gone in 60 Seconds</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Frighteners" title="The Frighteners">The Frighteners</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Narc_(film)" title="Narc (film)">Narc</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Terminal" title="The Terminal">The Terminal</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/I,_Robot_(film)" title="I, Robot (film)">I, Robot</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Roll_Bounce" title="Roll Bounce">Roll Bounce</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Annapolis_(film)" title="Annapolis (film)">Annapolis</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hoodlum_(film)" title="Hoodlum (film)">Hoodlum</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Undercover_Brother" title="Undercover Brother">Undercover Brother</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Go_to_Prison" title="Let's Go to Prison">Let's Go to Prison</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Brothers_Solomon" title="The Brothers Solomon">The Brothers Solomon</a></i>. McBride portrayed eight different characters in the play <i>Nagataki Sake</i>, directed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Downey,_Sr." title="Robert Downey, Sr.">Robert Downey, Sr.</a></p>
<p>He starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Human_Target_(2010_TV_series)" title="Human Target (2010 TV series)">Human Target</a></i> as Winston, business partner of Christopher Chance (the protagonist). The show premiered on January 17, 2010, on the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox Broadcasting Company</a>. He appeared on an episode of "Celebrity Ghost Stories", depicting an incident during 1988 before his acting career in which he was subconsciously led to the Lorraine Motel while stopped over in <a href="/wiki/Memphis,_Tennessee" title="Memphis, Tennessee">Memphis</a>, and experienced psychic visions of Martin Luther King, Jr.'s assassination on the 20th anniversary of the event.</p>
