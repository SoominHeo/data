<p><b>Shiny Toy Guns</b> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band that formed in 2002 in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles, California</a>. They released their first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a> <i><a href="/wiki/We_Are_Pilots" title="We Are Pilots">We Are Pilots</a></i> in 2006,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> after recording it two previous times. It featured three singles that peaked inside the top 30 in the <a href="/wiki/Alternative_Songs" title="Alternative Songs">Alternative Songs Chart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <i>We Are Pilots</i> was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allmusic_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusic-4">[4]</a></sup> Their second album <i><a href="/wiki/Season_of_Poison" title="Season of Poison">Season of Poison</a></i> was released in 2008<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and featured two singles that peaked inside the top 30 in the <a href="/wiki/Alternative_Songs" title="Alternative Songs">Alternative Songs Chart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ghost_Town_-_Shiny_Toy_Guns_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ghost_Town_-_Shiny_Toy_Guns-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shiny_Toy_Guns&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2002.E2.80.932006:_Formation_and_We_Are_Pilots">2002–2006: Formation and <i>We Are Pilots</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shiny_Toy_Guns&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2002–2006: Formation and We Are Pilots">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/We_Are_Pilots" title="We Are Pilots">We Are Pilots</a></div>
<p>The band was founded in 2002 by <a href="/wiki/Bassist" title="Bassist">bassist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Keyboardist" title="Keyboardist">keyboardist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Dawson" title="Jeremy Dawson">Jeremy Dawson</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vocalist" title="Vocalist">vocalist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guitarist" title="Guitarist">guitarist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gregori_Chad_Petree" title="Gregori Chad Petree">Chad Petree</a>. Both had known each other for years growing up in <a href="/wiki/Shawnee,_Oklahoma" title="Shawnee, Oklahoma">Shawnee, Oklahoma</a> and worked together on previous music projects such as <a href="/wiki/Cloud2Ground" title="Cloud2Ground">Cloud2Ground</a> and Slyder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> After these projects, they went underground for a brief time and continued making music together, eventually enlisting vocalist <a href="/wiki/Carah_Faye_Charnow" title="Carah Faye Charnow">Carah Faye Charnow</a> to be their new female vocalist (replacing Ursula Vari), as well as <a href="/wiki/Drummer" title="Drummer">drummer</a> Mikey Martin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allmusic_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusic-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shiny Toy Guns gained popularity in California and on the internet through networking on their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MySpace" title="MySpace">MySpace</a> page. In January 2005, they released their first album <i><a href="/wiki/We_Are_Pilots" title="We Are Pilots">We Are Pilots</a></i> through the record company Stormwest International.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> That summer they toured the United States in support of their album. <i>We Are Pilots</i> was re-recorded and re-released with a revised track list through record company <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SideCho" title="SideCho">SideCho</a> in November 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The band signed with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Records" title="Universal Records">Universal Records</a> in June 2006 and released a third, final version of <i>We Are Pilots</i> on October 17, 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Le_Disko" title="Le Disko">Le Disko</a>" was the first single, "<a href="/wiki/You_Are_the_One_(Shiny_Toy_Guns_song)" title="You Are the One (Shiny Toy Guns song)">You Are the One</a>" was the second single, and "<a href="/wiki/Rainy_Monday" title="Rainy Monday">Rainy Monday</a>" was the third. However, "Don't Cry Out" was originally announced to be the third single.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.932008:_Season_of_Poison">2007–2008: <i>Season of Poison</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shiny_Toy_Guns&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2007–2008: Season of Poison">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Season_of_Poison" title="Season of Poison">Season of Poison</a></div>
<p>On October 29, 2007, the band appeared as "The Shinys" on the children's television series <i><a href="/wiki/Yo_Gabba_Gabba!" title="Yo Gabba Gabba!">Yo Gabba Gabba!</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> They performed a sequence called <i>The House of Spooks</i>. <i>We Are Pilots</i> received a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> nomination on December 6, 2007 in the category of Best Electronic/Dance Album, but lost to <a href="/wiki/The_Chemical_Brothers" title="The Chemical Brothers">The Chemical Brothers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2008, it was revealed that <a href="/wiki/Carah_Faye_Charnow" title="Carah Faye Charnow">Carah Faye Charnow</a> was no longer part of the band and had been replaced by <a href="/wiki/Sisely_Treasure" title="Sisely Treasure">Sisely Treasure</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> whose vocals are featured on the band's second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Season_of_Poison" title="Season of Poison">Season of Poison</a></i>. Carah Faye Charnow went on to pursue a new band, <a href="/wiki/Versant_(band)" title="Versant (band)">Versant</a>, in Sweden.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Season_of_Poison" title="Season of Poison">Season of Poison</a></i> was released through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Records" title="Universal Records">Universal Records</a> on November 4, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 2, 2008, "<a href="/wiki/Ricochet!" title="Ricochet!">Ricochet!</a>" was released as the first single. It hit No. 17 on the <a href="/wiki/Alternative_Songs" title="Alternative Songs">Alternative Songs Chart</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> which is the highest the band has ever reached with a single on the Alternative Songs chart. Shiny Toy Guns released their second <i>Season of Poison</i> single, "<a href="/wiki/Ghost_Town_(Shiny_Toy_Guns_song)" title="Ghost Town (Shiny Toy Guns song)">Ghost Town</a>", on January 30, 2009, which peaked at No. 26 on the <a href="/wiki/Alternative_Songs" title="Alternative Songs">Alternative Songs Chart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ghost_Town_-_Shiny_Toy_Guns_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ghost_Town_-_Shiny_Toy_Guns-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.932010:_Girls_Le_Disko">2009–2010: <i>Girls Le Disko</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shiny_Toy_Guns&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2009–2010: Girls Le Disko">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Girls_Le_Disko" title="Girls Le Disko">Girls Le Disko</a></div>
<p>In February 2009, the band introduced their Fancorps Street Team as a way for their fans to become more involved with the band.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In March, the band released their first animated video for their single, "<a href="/wiki/Ghost_Town_(Shiny_Toy_Guns_song)" title="Ghost Town (Shiny Toy Guns song)">Ghost Town</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The animation was completed by well known illustrator, Glen Hanson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the end of March 2009, the band performed a cover of <a href="/wiki/Peter_Schilling" title="Peter Schilling">Peter Schilling</a>'s "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Major_Tom_(Coming_Home)(Shiny_Toy_Guns_song)" title="Major Tom (Coming Home)(Shiny Toy Guns song)">Major Tom (Coming Home)</a>". Initially used in a television commercial for the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_MKZ" title="Lincoln MKZ">Lincoln MKZ</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> the song was released as a single on May 5, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In September, the band covered <a href="/wiki/Blue_%C3%96yster_Cult" title="Blue Öyster Cult">Blue Öyster Cult</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Burnin%27_for_You" title="Burnin' for You">Burnin' for You</a>" for another Lincoln commercial advertising the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_MKS" title="Lincoln MKS">Lincoln MKS</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the spring, at a show in Milwaukee, Shiny Toy Guns said their next album would drop "sometime in late 2010".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 2009, <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Dawson" title="Jeremy Dawson">Jeremy Dawson</a> admitted via Shiny Toy Guns' official website that while he believes <i>Season of Poison</i> is a great album, it was "too different, too soon," and promised a return to their electronic roots for the band's third album.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On December 15, 2009, the remix album <i><a href="/wiki/Girls_Le_Disko" title="Girls Le Disko">Girls Le Disko</a></i> was released on Ultra Records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Near the end of January 2010, "<a href="/wiki/Rocketship_2010" title="Rocketship 2010">Rocketship 2010</a>" was released as a Myspace download featured on Coca Cola's Formula for Happiness free weekly download.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Also, at end of January, Shiny Toy Guns teamed up with Lincoln again to produce a music video for "Major Tom".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In March, a music video was released through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ultra_Records" title="Ultra Records">Ultra Records</a> for "Rocketship 2010".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> "Rocketship 2010" was released as a single on iTunes on April 20, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late fall 2010, Shiny Toy Guns announced a planned December 2010<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> release of an LP<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> tentatively named "III".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> According to Jeremy, fans "will fall in love with what we [Shiny Toy Guns] are just now starting to work with on this record." On their Facebook,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Shiny Toy Guns announced that "Speaking Japanese" would be a single on the next album. December passed without an album drop.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, Chad and Jeremy worked on a side project called Mirror Machines,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> which has since been postponed in favor of Shiny Toy Guns once again.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.93present:_III">2011–present: <i>III</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shiny_Toy_Guns&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2011–present: III">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/III_(Shiny_Toy_Guns_album)" title="III (Shiny Toy Guns album)">III (Shiny Toy Guns album)</a></div>
<p>Early January 2011, Shiny Toy Guns' official website featured a countdown which ended at midnight between February 10 and 11.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Below the countdown read the following text: "you know what you hope is at the end of this timer, but you can't be sure. and it doesn't matter.. because we'll be together." The quote is a modified version of a quote from <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Nolan" title="Christopher Nolan">Christopher Nolan</a>'s film, <i><a href="/wiki/Inception" title="Inception">Inception</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In addition, the piano riff that accompanies the countdown mirrors a scale from "Time", a song composed by <a href="/wiki/Hans_Zimmer" title="Hans Zimmer">Hans Zimmer</a> for the <i>Inception</i> soundtrack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the end of January, Shiny Toy Guns relaunched their fan-incorporated street team, the Satellites,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> stating, "our closest allies and the most important people in our world are the 2011 SATELLITE team. Join us, and become a part of the momentum of Shiny Toy Guns on a first hand basis. Together we will reach the eyes and the ears of the world."</p>
<p>In early February, <a href="/wiki/Sisely_Treasure" title="Sisely Treasure">Sisely Treasure</a> released a single,"That You Like," on her website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> On February 7, Shiny Toy Guns announced on Facebook<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> that Sisely was no longer part of the band.</p>
<p>On February 11, 2011 2:00:00 AM <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Time_Zone" title="Eastern Time Zone">EST</a> a video posted on the band's website announced that former band member, Carah Faye Charnow, would be rejoining the band along with her husband, Daniel Johansson, for the upcoming album <i>III</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube.com_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-39">[39]</a></sup> The video contained the song, "If I Lost You (Orchestral Mix)".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube.com_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-39">[39]</a></sup> Shortly after the end of the countdown, Versant posted to Facebook, "Boys and Girls, Carah Faye and Daniel Johansson, of your beloved Versant, are joining Shiny Toy Guns for album number three. Please enjoy this piece of music history! Here is another announcement: Versant isn't going ANYWHERE!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 3, 2011, "The Sun", the first single from <i>III</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> was released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> During a webchat, Jeremy explained that they hope <i>III</i> will be ready for a May or June 2011 release, but that it may come later this summer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> During an interview at SXSW, Jeremy confirmed that the band still hopes for a mid-June release, but that mid-August would be more realistic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> However, when asked in an interview with LIVE 105, Jeremy said "probably [say] November." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Also, during the Phoenix Pride Festival in April 2011 another new song, "Stay Down", was sung. On March 1, 2012, in an interview with speedcast.com, Jeremy and Carah announced that the third album was set to release in July, with the first single to be released in May, along with the beginning of a tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> On April 25, 2012, it was announced, via Instagram, that III will finish being mixed May 6. The album will feature 11 songs.</p>
<p>On May 10, 2012, the band announced, via Facebook, that Daniel Johansson would be leaving. The band cited the end of Daniel and Carah Faye's long-term relationship as the primary reason, but that they would remain friends.</p>
<p>An issue of <i>fljtokyo</i> stated that the album was planned to be released fall of 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p>On June 26, Shiny Toy Guns released a single titled "Waiting Alone" and announced <i>III</i> will be released on October 23, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 26, 2012 the band released their second single from their upcoming album <i>III</i>, titled "Fading Listening".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 6, Shiny Toy Guns released the official track listing for the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> The album was officially released on iTunes on October 26, 2012.</p>
<p>In September 2013, Shiny Toy Guns were announced for the maiden voyage of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=PARAHOY&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="PARAHOY (page does not exist)">PARAHOY</a>, a 4-day <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Festival_Cruise&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Festival Cruise (page does not exist)">Festival Cruise</a> with <a href="/wiki/Paramore" title="Paramore">Paramore</a> which was expected to set sail March 7, 2014.</p>
