<p><b>The Shining Stars</b> are a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestling</a> <a href="/wiki/Tag_team" title="Tag team">tag team</a> performing in <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a>, under the <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw</a></i> brand, the team is composed of real-life cousins <a href="/wiki/Primo_(wrestler)" title="Primo (wrestler)">Primo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Epico" title="Epico">Epico</a>. The cousins are former <a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw_Tag_Team_Championship" title="WWE Raw Tag Team Championship">WWE Tag Team Champions</a>, with the title won during their first run as <b>Primo &amp; Epico</b>, from 2011 to 2013. They also formerly performed as Diego and Fernando of <b>Los Matadores</b>, from 2013 to 2015 in WWE.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Primo_%26_Epico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Primo_.26_Epico_.282011.E2.80.932013.29">Primo &amp; Epico (2011–2013)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Primo_%26_Epico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Primo &amp; Epico (2011–2013)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the November 4, 2011, episode of <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown" title="WWE SmackDown">SmackDown</a></i>, Epico debuted on the main roster as a <a href="/wiki/Heel_(professional_wrestling)" title="Heel (professional wrestling)">heel</a> and realigned with <a href="/wiki/Hunico" title="Hunico">Hunico</a>, his former partner from when both were masked in <a href="/wiki/Florida_Championship_Wrestling" title="Florida Championship Wrestling">FCW</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On the November 11 episode of <i>Smackdown</i>, Primo, Epico's real-life cousin,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> was in the corner of Epico when he teamed with Hunico in a winning effort against <a href="/wiki/The_Usos" title="The Usos">The Usos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-All_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-All-3">[3]</a></sup> On the November 17 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Superstars" title="WWE Superstars">Superstars</a></i>, Primo &amp; Epico beat the Usos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On the December 1 episode of <i>Superstars</i>, <a href="/wiki/Rosa_Mendes" title="Rosa Mendes">Rosa Mendes</a> aligned herself with the group as their valet, as they were quietly disassociated from Hunico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jiggly_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jiggly-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Primo &amp; Epico then began a feud with WWE Tag Team Champions Air Boom, beating both <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evan_Bourne" title="Evan Bourne">Evan Bourne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kofi_Kingston" title="Kofi Kingston">Kofi Kingston</a> in singles matches,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> followed by two victories for Primo &amp; Epico against Air Boom during non-title tag team matches on December 13 at <a href="/wiki/WWE_Tribute_to_the_Troops#2011" title="WWE Tribute to the Troops">WWE Tribute to the Troops</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and the December 15 <i>Superstars</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> This earned Primo &amp; Epico a shot at the titles at <a href="/wiki/TLC:_Tables,_Ladders_%26_Chairs_(2011)" title="TLC: Tables, Ladders &amp; Chairs (2011)">TLC: Tables, Ladders &amp; Chairs</a> pay-per-view, but were unsuccessful.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Primo &amp; Epico's feud with Air Boom continued with Air Boom triumphant in singles matches,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> while Primo &amp; Epico obtained yet another victory in a non-title tag match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<p>At a <i>Raw</i> <a href="/wiki/House_show" title="House show">live event</a> on January 15, 2012, Primo &amp; Epico defeated Air Boom to win the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw_Tag_Team_Championship" title="WWE Raw Tag Team Championship">WWE Tag Team Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The following night on <i>Raw</i>, Primo &amp; Epico successfully defended their titles in a rematch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Primo &amp; Epico would go on to earn victories in non-title matches, defeating various teams such as <a href="/wiki/Jim_Duggan" title="Jim Duggan">Jim Duggan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Santino_Marella" title="Santino Marella">Santino Marella</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mason_Ryan" title="Mason Ryan">Mason Ryan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alex_Riley" title="Alex Riley">Alex Riley</a> and The Usos; however, Primo &amp; Epico would suffer non-title losses against the team of Kofi Kingston and <a href="/wiki/Ron_Killings" title="Ron Killings">R-Truth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On the February 27 edition of <i>Raw</i>, Primo &amp; Epico successfully defended their titles in a Triple Threat tag team match against the teams of Kingston and R-Truth &amp; <a href="/wiki/Dolph_Ziggler" title="Dolph Ziggler">Dolph Ziggler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jack_Swagger" title="Jack Swagger">Jack Swagger</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In the pre-show of <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_XXVIII" title="WrestleMania XXVIII">WrestleMania XXVIII</a>, Primo &amp; Epico successfully defended their titles in a triple threat tag team match against the Usos and <a href="/wiki/Justin_Gabriel" title="Justin Gabriel">Justin Gabriel</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Tyson_Kidd" title="Tyson Kidd">Tyson Kidd</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> After WrestleMania, Primo &amp; Epico suffered non-title losses to the teams of <a href="/wiki/Big_Show" title="Big Show">Big Show</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/The_Great_Khali" title="The Great Khali">The Great Khali</a> and <a href="/wiki/Santino_Marella" title="Santino Marella">Santino Marella</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Zack_Ryder" title="Zack Ryder">Zack Ryder</a>. Also, <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Washington" title="Abraham Washington">A.W.</a> began offering his managerial services to the team, pointing out that the tag champions had been treated like jokes, being left off WrestleMania and Extreme Rules.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> At the April 30 <i>Raw</i>, Primo &amp; Epico lost the tag titles to Kingston &amp; R-Truth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>After losing the titles, Primo, Epico and Mendes joined A.W.'s talent agency in May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AllWorld_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-AllWorld-23">[23]</a></sup> However, after joining "All World Promotions", Primo &amp; Epico would not wrestle on television until the <a href="/wiki/No_Way_Out_(2012)" title="No Way Out (2012)">No Way Out</a> pay-per-view on 17 June. At No Way Out, it was revealed that Primo &amp; Epico's 30-day <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_terms#Rematch_.28or_Return.29_clause" title="Professional wrestling terms">rematch clause</a> for the tag titles had expired, and that the duo had to compete in a four-way match against the teams of Justin Gabriel &amp; Tyson Kidd, the Usos and <a href="/wiki/The_Prime_Time_Players" title="The Prime Time Players">The Prime Time Players</a> (<a href="/wiki/Darren_Young" title="Darren Young">Darren Young</a> and <a href="/wiki/Titus_O%27Neil" title="Titus O'Neil">Titus O'Neil</a>) to earn another title shot. During the match, A. W. betrayed Primo &amp; Epico, directly costing them the match and aligning with the match winners, the Prime Time Players.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> At the following <i>Raw</i>, Primo &amp; Epico defeated the Players by count-out after O'Neil and Young walked out of the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Primo &amp; Epico would wrestle as <a href="/wiki/Face_(professional_wrestling)" title="Face (professional wrestling)">babyfaces</a> for their feud against the Prime Time Players.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The tag teams would continue their feud on <i>Superstars</i> with Epico losing to Young and Primo beating both O'Neil and Young in singles matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Money_in_the_Bank_(2012)" title="Money in the Bank (2012)">Money in the Bank</a>, Primo &amp; Epico defeated the Prime Time Players.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> On the August 10 <i>SmackDown</i>, Primo &amp; Epico faced the Players in a #1 contender match and were disqualified when champions Kingston &amp; Truth attacked the Players; A.W. was released from WWE <a href="/wiki/Broadcast_delay" title="Broadcast delay">between that episode's taping and airing</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>After Primo &amp; Epico ended their feud with the Prime Time Players in August, they reverted to being <a href="/wiki/Heel_(professional_wrestling)" title="Heel (professional wrestling)">heels</a> during a victory over the Usos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Primo &amp; Epico then went on to lose every televised match for the rest of 2012, with losses to Kingston &amp; Truth, the Players, <a href="/wiki/Rey_Mysterio" title="Rey Mysterio">Rey Mysterio</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/M%C3%ADstico" title="Místico">Sin Cara</a>, Team Co-Bro (Santino Marella &amp; Zack Ryder), Team Rhodes Scholars, The Usos and to both <a href="/wiki/Ryback" title="Ryback">Ryback</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Great_Khali" title="The Great Khali">The Great Khali</a> in handicap matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/Survivor_Series_(2012)" title="Survivor Series (2012)">Survivor Series</a> pay-per-view, Primo &amp; Epico took part in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_tag_team_match_types#Elimination_tag_team_matches" title="Professional wrestling tag team match types">10 man elimination tag team match</a>, but were eliminated by Rey Mysterio and <a href="/wiki/Tyson_Kidd" title="Tyson Kidd">Tyson Kidd</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-33">[33]</a></sup> By the first <i>SmackDown</i> of 2013, Primo was on a 20 match losing streak, and Epico was at 17.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> On the January 9 <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_NXT_(TV_series)" title="WWE NXT (TV series)">NXT</a></i> episode, Primo &amp; Epico broke their losing streaks with a win over <a href="/wiki/Bo_Dallas" title="Bo Dallas">Bo Dallas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Axel" title="Curtis Axel">Michael McGillicutty</a>, their first win in months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-backstabba_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-backstabba-35">[35]</a></sup> However, Dallas and McGillicutty gained revenge by defeating Primo &amp; Epico in the first round of the <a href="/wiki/NXT_Tag_Team_Championship#Inaugural_tournament" title="NXT Tag Team Championship">NXT Tag Team Championship tournament</a> to crown the inaugural champions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Los_Matadores_.282013.E2.80.932015.29">Los Matadores (2013–2015)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Primo_%26_Epico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Los Matadores (2013–2015)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Los_Matadores_%26_El_Torito.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1506" data-file-width="2678" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Los_Matadores_%26_El_Torito.jpg/220px-Los_Matadores_%26_El_Torito.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Los_Matadores_%26_El_Torito.jpg/330px-Los_Matadores_%26_El_Torito.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Los_Matadores_%26_El_Torito.jpg/440px-Los_Matadores_%26_El_Torito.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>In August 2013, WWE hyped the debut of "Los Matadores",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> and on the September 30 episode of <i>Raw</i>, Primo &amp; Epico debuted new characters, as Diego and Fernando of Los Matadores, a <a href="/wiki/Face_(professional_wrestling)" title="Face (professional wrestling)">face</a> team of two masked Spanish <a href="/wiki/Torero" title="Torero">bullfighters</a> with <a href="/wiki/Mascarita_Dorada" title="Mascarita Dorada">El Torito</a> as mascot as they defeated 3MB.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Primo and Epico were named after the real life sons of Christopher Columbus (In Spanish, Cristobal Colón, Los Matadores' real last name).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> It was the start of a winning streak that featured nine wins over 3MB (<a href="/wiki/Heath_Slater" title="Heath Slater">Heath Slater</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drew_Galloway" title="Drew Galloway">Drew McIntyre</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jinder_Mahal" title="Jinder Mahal">Jinder Mahal</a>, with El Torito wrestling in some matches), and also over the The Real Americans (<a href="/wiki/Cesaro_(wrestler)" title="Cesaro (wrestler)">Antonio Cesaro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jack_Swagger" title="Jack Swagger">Jack Swagger</a>) at <a href="/wiki/Hell_in_a_Cell_(2013)" title="Hell in a Cell (2013)">Hell in a Cell</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twoohone_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-twoohone-41">[41]</a></sup> Also, El Torito was able to interfere in matches without incurring a disqualification for Los Matadores.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Los Matadores' winning streak was finally snapped on the January 24 episode of <i>SmackDown</i> in 2014, courtesy of The RybAxel (<a href="/wiki/Ryback" title="Ryback">Ryback</a> and <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Axel" title="Curtis Axel">Curtis Axel</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twoohone_41-1"><a href="#cite_note-twoohone-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the March 5 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Main_Event" title="WWE Main Event">Main Event</a></i>, Los Matadores received a tag team championship match against The Usos in which they were defeated. They also made an appearance as Primo and Epico alongside <a href="/wiki/Carly_Col%C3%B3n" title="Carly Colón">Carlito</a> to induct <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Col%C3%B3n_Sr." title="Carlos Colón Sr.">Carlos Colón Sr.</a> into the WWE Hall of Fame class of 2014. At the <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_XXX" title="WrestleMania XXX">WrestleMania XXX</a> pre-show, they received a WWE Tag Team Championship shot against The Usos, RybAxel and The Real Americans, in a Fatal Four-Way Elimination Tag Team match, but lost after being the first team eliminated when Fernando submitted to Jack Swagger's Patriot Lock. On the June 23, WWE.com reported that Fernando was injured at a live event that past weekend. Despite this, Diego and El Torito would then start a short feud with <a href="/wiki/Fandango_(wrestler)" title="Fandango (wrestler)">Fandango</a>.</p>
<p>After 3MB broke up, their former mascot <a href="/wiki/Hornswoggle" title="Hornswoggle">Hornswoggle</a> joined Los Matadores as "La Vaquita", a midget cow mascot accompanying Torito's bullness on the August 19 edition of <i>Main Event</i>. Fernando made his return as well in a losing effort to Slater-Gator (Heath Slater and Titus O'Neil).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_31" title="WrestleMania 31">WrestleMania 31</a> pre-show, Los Matadores competed in the Fatal Four Way tag team match, but they failed to win the titles. At <a href="/wiki/Elimination_Chamber_(2015)" title="Elimination Chamber (2015)">Elimination Chamber</a>, Los Matadores participated in the first-ever <a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw_Tag_Team_Championship" title="WWE Raw Tag Team Championship">WWE Tag Team Championship</a> Elimination Chamber, but failed and got eliminated first by <a href="/wiki/The_Ascension_(professional_wrestling)" title="The Ascension (professional wrestling)">The Ascension</a>. At <a href="/wiki/SummerSlam_(2015)" title="SummerSlam (2015)">SummerSlam</a>, Los Matadores competed in a Fatal 4-Way Tag Team Match for the WWE Tag Team Championship in a losing effort when they were pinned by <a href="/wiki/The_New_Day_(wrestling)" title="The New Day (wrestling)">The New Day</a>. On September 7, 2015 episode of <i>Raw</i> after losing to <a href="/wiki/The_Dudley_Boyz" title="The Dudley Boyz">The Dudley Boyz</a>, Los Matadores turned <a href="/wiki/Heel_(professional_wrestling)" title="Heel (professional wrestling)">heel</a> when Diego attacked El Torito after the match, however he was then put through a table by the Dudley Boyz. They were last seen on <i>Main Event</i> that week, minus El Torito, where they were defeated by The Prime Time Players.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Shining_Stars_.282016.E2.80.93present.29">The Shining Stars (2016–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Primo_%26_Epico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Shining Stars (2016–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From April to May 2016, several videos aired on <i>Raw</i> promoting the return of Primo and Epico, who revived their Puerto Rican gimmick under the new name <b>The Shining Stars</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> They were presented as arrogant heels, urging viewers to come to Puerto Rico, calling it the "Shining Star of the Caribbean."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-primoepico_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-primoepico-46">[46]</a></sup> On the May 16 episode of <i>Raw</i>, The Shining Stars debuted by defeating a local tag team in a squash match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> On the July 25 episode of <i>Raw</i>, the team returned to television where they were defeated by <a href="/wiki/Enzo_and_Cass" title="Enzo and Cass">Enzo and Cass</a>. On the September 5 episode of <i>Raw</i>, The Shining Stars defeated Enzo and Cass. The week after, Epico, accompanied by Primo defeated <a href="/wiki/Enzo_Amore" title="Enzo Amore">Enzo Amore</a>, who was accompanied by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colin_Cassady" title="Colin Cassady">Big Cass</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> They then began showing tendencies of <a href="/wiki/Confidence_trick" title="Confidence trick">con men</a>, trying to persuade various superstars to purchase <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Timeshares" title="Timeshares">timeshares</a> to their Puerto Rican resort. This led to an alliance with Titus O'Neil, who was looking to cross-promote with them for his new "Titus Brand." The trio would enter a feud with The Golden Truth (<a href="/wiki/Goldust" title="Goldust">Goldust</a> and R-Truth) and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Henry" title="Mark Henry">Mark Henry</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> On the November 7 edition of <i>Raw</i>, after R-Truth sold The Golden Truth's <a href="/wiki/Survivor_Series_(2016)" title="Survivor Series (2016)">Survivor Series</a> spot for a stay at a <a href="/wiki/Timeshare" title="Timeshare">timeshare</a> to The Shining Stars, both teams competed to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_tag_team_match_types#Elimination_tag_team_matches" title="Professional wrestling tag team match types">10–on–10 Survivor Series Tag Team Elimination match</a>, which Primo and Epico won.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
