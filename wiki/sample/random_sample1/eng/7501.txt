<p><b>Cheyenne Tozzi</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a> <a href="/wiki/Model_(person)" title="Model (person)">model</a> and singer. She has appeared on the covers of Mexican <i><a href="/wiki/Vogue_(magazine)" title="Vogue (magazine)">Vogue</a></i>, German <i><a href="/wiki/Cosmopolitan_(magazine)" title="Cosmopolitan (magazine)">Cosmopolitan</a></i>, and French <i><a href="/wiki/Madame_Figaro" title="Madame Figaro">Madame Figaro</a></i> magazine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chiccelebrity1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-chiccelebrity1-1">[1]</a></sup> Tozzi has also worked with the likes of ACP’s ‘30 Days Of Fashion’.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-2">[2]</a></sup> Tozzi is the lead singer of rock and blues band <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Van_Hoorn" title="Van Hoorn">Van Hoorn</a>. She is the younger sister of model, actress and singer <a href="/wiki/Tahyna_Tozzi" title="Tahyna Tozzi">Tahyna Tozzi</a> and the daughter of Yvonee Tozzi, a retired model.</p>
<p>She is represented by Chic Talent Management (Australia),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chiccelebrity1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-chiccelebrity1-1">[1]</a></sup> and Storm Model Management (London),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Elite (Barcelona) <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and Bornmodels (Denmark).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cheyenne_Tozzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tozzi grew up in the Sydney beach-side suburb of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cronulla" title="Cronulla">Cronulla</a> with her Italian father, Dutch mother and older sister, <a href="/wiki/Tahyna_Tozzi" title="Tahyna Tozzi">Tahyna Tozzi</a>. Tozzi began her modelling at 8yrs of age. At the age of 13, Tozzi became one of the youngest models to grace the cover of prestigious ‘Harper’s Bazaar’, and was immediately dubbed "the next Elle Macpherson".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag needs to be fact-checked with the cited source(s). (March 2014)">verification needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cheyenne_Tozzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tozzi appeared on <i>Australia’s Next Top Model</i> in 2010, as well as a various daytime TV appearances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chiccelebrity1_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-chiccelebrity1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of March 2014 she was working with David Jones and Tush Magazine in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> while also writing and recording tracks for her debut solo record. Cheyenne is also an Ambassador for the Ovarian Cancer Research Foundation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, Tozzi was a mentor on <i>The Face AU</i> with models <a href="/wiki/Naomi_Campbell" title="Naomi Campbell">Naomi Campbell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Trunfio" title="Nicole Trunfio">Nicole Trunfio</a>. The show takes aspiring models through the passes with the help of their supermodel mentors.</p>
<p>Also in 2014, Tozzi collaborated with KISSKILL's designer and director Jane Carrodus to create Cheyenne for KISSKILL lingerie line. The pair met over social media site Instagram where Carrodus originally wanted to label the line after Cheyenne, but it quickly evolved into a collaboration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since 2014, Tozzi has been the face of the Australian international skincare brand <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jergens" title="Jergens">Jergens</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Van_Hoorn">Van Hoorn</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cheyenne_Tozzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Van Hoorn">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tozzi is the lead singer of the band <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Van_Hoorn" title="Van Hoorn">Van Hoorn</a> who have made appearances on Channel 7 as well as travelling to the Middle East to perform to the Australian Troops.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2013 Van Hoorn was the supporting act for the Kooks Australian tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The band also includes guitarist Joshua (Izzy) Mullane, and is managed by Tozzi's partner Tyson Mullane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Boom_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Boom-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In December 2012 they performed for Australian troops in Afghanistan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
