<p><b>Tamara Marthe</b> (born 28 November 1985), better known as <b>Shy'm</b> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/ʃ/ 'sh' in 'shy'">ʃ</span><span title="/aɪ/ long 'i' in 'tide'">aɪ</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span></span>/</a></span></span>, is a <a href="/wiki/Music_of_France" title="Music of France">French singer</a>. She released her first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Mes_Fantaisies" title="Mes Fantaisies">Mes fantaisies</a></i>, in 2006 and has since released four more albums. She is one of the most successful French singers of the 21st century, with three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Platinum_album" title="Platinum album">Platinum</a> albums, including her number-one 2012 album, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cam%C3%A9l%C3%A9on" title="Caméléon">Caméléon</a></i>. Both in 2012 and 2013 she received the <a href="/wiki/NRJ_Music_Award" title="NRJ Music Award">NRJ Music Award</a> for Francophone Female Artist of the Year.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shy%27m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tamara Marthe was born on 28 November 1985 in <a href="/wiki/Trappes" title="Trappes">Trappes</a> (a <a href="/wiki/Banlieue" title="Banlieue">suburb</a> west of <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>), France to a <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_France" title="Metropolitan France">French metropolitan</a> mother and a <a href="/wiki/French_West_Indies" title="French West Indies">French Caribbean</a> father from <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a>, in a family oriented to music and creative arts. Fan of <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers" title="Red Hot Chili Peppers">Red Hot Chili Peppers</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9_Knowles" title="Beyoncé Knowles">Beyoncé</a>, she nevertheless grew up to the sound of <a href="/wiki/Zouk" title="Zouk">zouk</a>—a French Caribbean music genre—and <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a>. From an early age, she became interested in dancing and singing. She started taking lessons in dancing and she earned the nickname "Spicy Tam" as a result of the effort she put in it.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In academics, she obtained a baccalaureat<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> at the age of seventeen. Along with her studies, she had been making demo recordings of herself while avoiding public scrutiny.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Later, she went to Paris, where she sent the recordings to record companies. It was at this time that Lebanese-Canadian rapper <a href="/wiki/K.Maro" title="K.Maro">K.Maro</a> discovered her. Marthe acquired the stage name <b>Shy'm</b> and began her journey to stardom. Her pseudonym is a <a href="/wiki/Portmanteau" title="Portmanteau">portmanteau</a>, derived from "shy" and "Martinique".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> She says she is shy, but much less so than before when the mere idea of being on stage paralysed her with fright. But her passion has allowed her to overcome her shyness and she now takes "pleasure in putting herself in the crowd".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shy%27m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rapper K.Maro agreed to feature her in the song "Histoire de luv" from his album. The song was recorded in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Montr%C3%A9al" title="Montréal">Montréal</a>. After that, she released her own album, <i><a href="/wiki/Mes_Fantaisies" title="Mes Fantaisies">Mes fantaisies</a></i>. It was written, composed and produced by K.Maro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Shy%27m_2011_cropped.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="494" data-file-width="220" height="494" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/bd/Shy%27m_2011_cropped.jpg" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Shy%27m_2011_cropped.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Shy'm in 2011 .</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Shy'm explored various themes for the album, such as recurring love and <a href="/wiki/Interracial_marriage" title="Interracial marriage">intermarriage</a> and its success. The first single, "Femme de couleur", peaked at number 5 on the French singles chart. In 2007, she made a guest appearance on the popular French game show, <i><a href="/wiki/Fort_Boyard_(TV_series)" title="Fort Boyard (TV series)">Fort Boyard</a></i>.</p>
<p>Shy'm's musical style is a mix of <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a>. Her first album, <i>Mes fantaisies</i>, has a prominent R&amp;B sound, with pop, <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> influences. Her second album, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reflets" title="Reflets">Reflets</a></i>, has a pop and R&amp;B sound, though it has <a href="/wiki/Dance-pop" title="Dance-pop">dance-pop</a> influences. Her third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Prendre_l%27air" title="Prendre l'air">Prendre l'air</a></i>, deviates towards a more <a href="/wiki/Synthpop" title="Synthpop">synthpop</a>-inspired sound, with her fourth album, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cam%C3%A9l%C3%A9on" title="Caméléon">Caméléon</a></i>, having a deeper exploration of this sound through <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance</a>.</p>
<p>In 2011, Shy'm was one of the nine contestants in the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/Danse_avec_les_stars_(France_season_2)" title="Danse avec les stars (France season 2)">Danse avec les stars</a></i>—the French version of <i><a href="/wiki/Dancing_with_the_Stars" title="Dancing with the Stars">Dancing with the Stars</a></i>. She was partnered with professional dancer Maxime Dereymez. On 19 November 2011 they won the competition. In 2012, she joined the <a href="/wiki/Les_Enfoir%C3%A9s" title="Les Enfoirés">Les Enfoirés</a> charity ensemble.</p>
