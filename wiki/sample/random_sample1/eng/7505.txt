<p><i><b>Šatrovački</b></i> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serbian_Cyrillic_language" title="Serbian Cyrillic language">Serbian Cyrillic</a>: <span lang="sr" xml:lang="sr"><span class="Unicode">Шатровачки</span></span>) or <i><b>šatra</b></i> is a feature of permuting syllables of words used in <a href="/wiki/Serbo-Croatian" title="Serbo-Croatian">Serbo-Croatian</a>. It is similar to <i><a href="/wiki/Verlan" title="Verlan">verlan</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Louch%C3%A9bem" title="Louchébem">louchébem</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Vesre" title="Vesre">vesre</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Rioplatense_Spanish" title="Rioplatense Spanish">Rioplatense Spanish</a>. The term is sometimes used to describe other slang in which words are deformed, as well. Šatrovački was initially developed by criminals in <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>, as it was incomprehensible to police. Today, it is spread among youth in informal speech. It is more widespread in urban areas, such as capitals <a href="/wiki/Belgrade" title="Belgrade">Belgrade</a> (Serbia), <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a> (Croatia) and <a href="/wiki/Sarajevo" title="Sarajevo">Sarajevo</a> (Bosnia and Herzegovina).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Standard_.C5.A1atrova.C4.8Dki">Standard šatrovački</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0atrova%C4%8Dki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Standard šatrovački">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Words are formed by replacing the syllable order. For example: <i><b>pi</b>vo</i> (beer), becomes <i>vo<b>pi</b></i>. The new word has the same meaning as the stem. Since the spelling is nearly phonetic it does not change. However, sometimes one of the vowels is changed to make the new word easier to pronounce, avoid ambiguity, or if the stem word is not in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nominative" title="Nominative">nominative</a>. For example, <i>tra<b>va</b></i> ("grass", marijuana) would become <i>vutra</i> instead of <i>vatra</i> ("fire").</p>
<p>Some words are more commonly spoken in Šatrovački than others, but there is no specific rule. Examples of transformed sentences (although, most of the words in a single sentence are rarely transformed) are:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>Brate, neću da igram fudbal. — Tebra, ćune da grami dbalfu.</i> ("Bro, I don't want to play football.")</li>
<li><i>Zemun zakon brate. — Munze konza tebra.</i> (literally: "<a href="/wiki/Zemun" title="Zemun">Zemun</a> is the law, bro" - meaning: "Zemun rules dude")</li>
<li><i>Pazi brate, murija! — Zipa tebra, rijamu!</i> ("Watch out mate - cops!", <i>murija</i> slang for police)</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Utrova.C4.8Dki">Utrovački</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0atrova%C4%8Dki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Utrovački">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Utrovački</b> (Утровачки) is a more complex form of <i>šatrovački</i>. Words are formed using: U + last part + ZA + first part + NJE. E.g. <b>vikipedija</b> (Wikipedia) becomes u<b>kipedija</b>za<b>vi</b>nje. Today, utrovački is not widely used.</p>
<p>Alternative Utrovački is same as above, but without "ZA", e.g. <b>pivo</b> (beer) becomes u<b>vopi</b>nje, or <b>cigare</b> (tobacco) becomes u<b>gareci</b>nje.</p>

<ul>
<li><b>udragunje</b> - droga (drug)</li>
<li><b>utravunje</b> - trava (weed)</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Leaving_out_syllables">Leaving out syllables</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0atrova%C4%8Dki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Leaving out syllables">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A more simplified version of Šatrovački is using only parts of the word, while excluding the first syllable, and is most commonly used among young people in Serbia. For example, kon<b>cert</b> (concert) would be shorthened to <b>cert</b>. The rules of creating a new word that can be used in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nominative" title="Nominative">nominative</a> while the stem is not apply similarly to standard šatrovački. An example of a full sentence would be:</p>
<dl>
<dd>
<dl>
<dd><i>Drugar i ja idemo na koncert na Tvrđavi.</i> (My friend and I are going to a concert on the Fortress)</dd>
<dd><i>Gari i ja idemo na cert na Đavi.</i></dd>
</dl>
</dd>
</dl>
<p>This is particularly characteristic of <a href="/wiki/Novi_Sad" title="Novi Sad">Novi Sad</a> youth subculture, and is very rarely spoken outside of <a href="/wiki/Vojvodina" title="Vojvodina">Vojvodina</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Adding_syllables">Adding syllables</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0atrova%C4%8Dki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Adding syllables">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A very rare but present form of expression found in the Belgrade projects (blokovi). Words are reconstructed by adding various suffixes so that the original word remains relatively intact. Usually, the basis is šatrovački. The resulting words have a generally diminutive meaning.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">Examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0atrova%C4%8Dki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><b>kajblo or kićblo</b> - blok (Blok of suburb <a href="/wiki/Blokovi" title="Blokovi">Blokovi</a> or <a href="/wiki/New_Belgrade" title="New Belgrade">New Belgrade</a>)</li>
<li><b>kićso or kajso</b> - sok (juice)</li>
<li><b>pajdo</b> - dop (heroin)</li>
</ul>
<p>These diminutives can later be combined using the <b>Šatrovački</b> method, resulting in words like <i>kajblo</i>, or <i>kićblo</i>. However, this is a rare usage, confined to the area of <a href="/wiki/Zemun" title="Zemun">Zemun</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Belgrade" title="New Belgrade">New Belgrade</a>.</p>
<p>Also there is another type of šatrovački, where the words are reconstructed by addition of letter P after each syllable:</p>
