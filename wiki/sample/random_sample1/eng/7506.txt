<p><b>Chapecó</b> is a major city in the western area of the state of <a href="/wiki/Santa_Catarina_(state)" title="Santa Catarina (state)">Santa Catarina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, located 630 km west of <a href="/wiki/Florian%C3%B3polis" title="Florianópolis">Florianópolis</a>, the state capital.</p>
<p>As of 2016, it has a <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of around 209,553 inhabitants, who are known as chapecoense.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibge-2016_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ibge-2016-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chapec%C3%B3&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Until the <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a>, the area around Chapecó land was under <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_in_Brazil" title="Indigenous peoples in Brazil">indigenous</a> (mainly <a href="/wiki/Kaingang" title="Kaingang">Kaingang</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> control, mostly unoccupied by <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_groups_in_Europe" title="Ethnic groups in Europe">European</a> people and subject to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argentina-Brazil_border" title="Argentina-Brazil border">border issues</a> and armed conflicts. Brazilian control was in fact established as a <a class="extiw" href="https://pt.wikipedia.org/wiki/Col%C3%B4nia_Militar_de_Chapec%C3%B3" title="pt:Colônia Militar de Chapecó">military colony</a> in 1882. Its official status as a <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Brazil" title="Municipalities of Brazil">municipality</a> was established by state government decree after the <a href="/wiki/Contestado_War" title="Contestado War">Contestado War</a> on August 25, 1917. Its area was then given to a private colonization company<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> which began selling land to people coming mainly from the neighboring state of <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_do_Sul" title="Rio Grande do Sul">Rio Grande do Sul</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Most of its inhabitants are descended from <a href="/wiki/Italian_Brazilians" title="Italian Brazilians">Italian</a>, <a href="/wiki/German_Brazilians" title="German Brazilians">German</a> and <a href="/wiki/Polish_Brazilians" title="Polish Brazilians">Polish</a> immigrants.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demography">Demography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chapec%C3%B3&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chapecó itself has a population of over 200 thousand inhabitants and its metropolitan area, approximately 400 thousand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibge-2016_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ibge-2016-1">[1]</a></sup> It is the hub city of <a href="/wiki/Oeste_Catarinense" title="Oeste Catarinense">Western Santa Catarina</a>, where there are about two hundred municipalities, summing together more than 2 million inhabitants.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chapec%C3%B3&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This region of the state is home of some of the largest meat processing and exporting industrial enterprises such as <a href="/wiki/Sadia" title="Sadia">Sadia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seara_Foods" title="Seara Foods">Seara Foods</a>; the farmers are organized in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agricultural_cooperatives" title="Agricultural cooperatives">agricultural cooperatives</a>. Chapecó is known as a Brazilian agro-industrial capital, specialized in <a href="/wiki/Pork" title="Pork">pork</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poultry" title="Poultry">poultry</a> and technology involved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The city is headquarters of <a class="external text" href="http://www.auroraalimentos.com.br/export/aurora.php" rel="nofollow">Aurora Central Cooperative</a> and has a plant of <a href="/wiki/BRF_S.A." title="BRF S.A.">BRF S.A.</a> since 1973.</p>
<p>Other major economic sectors are metal mechanics specialized in equipment for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slaughterhouses" title="Slaughterhouses">slaughterhouses</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transportation" title="Transportation">transportation</a>, plastics and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Packaging" title="Packaging">packaging</a>, <a href="/wiki/Furniture" title="Furniture">furniture</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beverages" title="Beverages">beverages</a>, <a href="/wiki/Software_development" title="Software development">software development</a> and <a href="/wiki/Biotechnology" title="Biotechnology">biotechnology</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_construction" title="Civil construction">Civil construction</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">trade</a> are also important source of income.</p>
<p>Chapecó is served by <a href="/wiki/Chapec%C3%B3_Airport" title="Chapecó Airport">Serafin Enoss Bertaso Airport</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chapec%C3%B3&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chapec%C3%B3&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Chapecó is home to <a href="/wiki/Associa%C3%A7%C3%A3o_Chapecoense_de_Futebol" title="Associação Chapecoense de Futebol">Associação Chapecoense de Futebol</a> (commonly known as Chapecoense), a club <a href="/wiki/Football" title="Football">football</a> team that plays at <a href="/wiki/Arena_Cond%C3%A1" title="Arena Condá">Arena Condá</a>. The club was founded in 1973 and advanced to its most recent spell in <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Brasileiro_S%C3%A9rie_A" title="Campeonato Brasileiro Série A">Série A</a> in 2014. The club went on to reach the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copa_Sudamericana_2016" title="Copa Sudamericana 2016">Copa Sudamericana 2016</a> finals, but most of the team's players and staff were killed in the crash of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LaMia_Airlines_Flight_2933" title="LaMia Airlines Flight 2933">LaMia Airlines Flight 2933</a> on 28 November 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
