<p>The <b>Char Leclerc</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Main_battle_tank" title="Main battle tank">main battle tank</a> (MBT) built by GIAT, now <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nexter" title="Nexter">Nexter</a> of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. It was named in honour of General <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Leclerc_de_Hauteclocque" title="Philippe Leclerc de Hauteclocque">Philippe Leclerc de Hauteclocque</a> who led the French element of the <a href="/wiki/Liberation_of_Paris" title="Liberation of Paris">drive towards Paris</a> while in command of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_Armored_Division_(France)" title="2nd Armored Division (France)">Free French 2nd Armoured Division</a> (<i>2e DB</i>) in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.</p>
<p>The Leclerc is in service with the <a href="/wiki/French_Army" title="French Army">French Army</a> and the army of the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>. In production since 1991,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TGelbart_3-9"><a href="#cite_note-TGelbart-3">[3]</a></sup> the Leclerc entered French service in 1992,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TGelbart_3-10"><a href="#cite_note-TGelbart-3">[3]</a></sup> replacing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AMX_30" title="AMX 30">AMX 30</a> as the country's main armoured platform. With production now complete, the French Army has a total of 406 Leclerc and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_of_the_United_Arab_Emirates" title="Military of the United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates Army</a> has 388. The price in 2011 was €9.3 million, which made it the most expensive tank in history at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-defensenews.com_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-defensenews.com-6">[6]</a></sup> Following the devaluation of the <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">Euro</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (February 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> its price fell dramatically, and in 2014 the <a href="/wiki/K2_Black_Panther" title="K2 Black Panther">K2 Black Panther</a> surpassed the Leclerc's price record.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AMX_Leclerc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1964 studies were initiated about a possible replacement vehicle for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AMX_30" title="AMX 30">AMX 30</a>: the <i>Engin Principal Prospectif</i>. In 1971, in view of the inferiority of the AMX 30 in comparison to the new generation of Soviet tanks about to be introduced, the <i>Direction des Armements Terrestres</i> ordered the beginning of the <i>Char Futur</i> project. In 1975 a working committee was created that in 1977 agreed on a list of specifications. In February 1980 however, a Memorandum of Understanding was signed with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_Republic_of_Germany" title="Federal Republic of Germany">Federal Republic of Germany</a> involving the joint development of a MBT, called the <i>Napoléon I</i> in France and <i>Kampfpanzer III</i> in Germany. Fundamental disagreements about its desired configuration led to a failure of this cooperation in December 1982. It was announced that a purely French battle tank would be developed, called "EPC" (<i>Engin Principal de Combat</i>). Importation of foreign equipment, like the <a href="/wiki/M1_Abrams" title="M1 Abrams">M1 Abrams</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Leopard_2" title="Leopard 2">Leopard 2</a>, or the <a href="/wiki/Merkava" title="Merkava">Merkava</a> had been studied and rejected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In contrast to most Western programmes of the time, much consideration was given to active, besides passive protection, to limit the overall mass of the vehicle. Mobility for evading enemy fire and fire control systems were given particular attention. Nevertheless, it was a stated design goal to achieve at least double the protection against KE-penetrators in comparison to the level attained in then current MBTs of the fifty ton weight class, the latter indicated at about 400 mm RHA equivalency, the higher level at the same time protecting against shaped charges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Leclerc-IMG_1720.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2048" data-file-width="3072" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Leclerc-IMG_1720.jpg/220px-Leclerc-IMG_1720.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Leclerc-IMG_1720.jpg/330px-Leclerc-IMG_1720.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Leclerc-IMG_1720.jpg/440px-Leclerc-IMG_1720.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Partnership with a foreign state was sought to limit the cost per unit, and this was found when the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a> ordered 436 vehicles, adding to the 426 units already planned for the French Army.</p>
<p>In 1986, the project was started under the name of "Leclerc", six prototypes being built swiftly. Mass production started in 1990 with the four-unit Batch 1, used mainly for comparative tests in foreign countries. The 17 units of Batch 2 were shipped, with improvements in the turret and in the hull armour. These units were diagnosed with problems in the engine and suspension, and were quickly retired. Batch 3 followed with some improvements and have been used to define the doctrine of use, and instruction.</p>
<p>Batches 4 and 5 were better built, eliminating the recurrent problems in the powerplant, and are still in service, after having been refitted at the end of the 1990s. The second series started with Batch 6, with an added climate control system in the right rear of the turret. Batch 7 introduced a transmission system to the command vehicle, and a data system giving instantaneous vision of the state of all battle tanks and acquired targets. It also incorporated minor improvements in the visor. Batch 8 was a modernisation of the electronic system, and Batch 9 replaced the thermal imaging ATHOS by a <a href="/wiki/SAGEM" title="SAGEM">SAGEM</a> Iris with better resolution.</p>
<p>All previous batches will be modernised up to the standards of Batch 9 from 2005. In 2004, Batch 10 was presented, incorporating new information systems which could share the disposition of enemy and friendly units to all vehicles on the battlefield, and a new armor package. This was the beginning of the 96-unit third series. By 2007, 355 tanks should have been operational, 320 of them incorporated in four regiments, each of 80 Leclerc vehicles.</p>
<p>As of 2010<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=AMX_Leclerc&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, after a French defence review, each of the four regiments operated 60 Leclerc tanks for a total of 240 in operational units; with a further 100 Leclerc in combat ready reserve. Due to finance cuts, only 254 tanks were fully operational in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-defensenews.com_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-defensenews.com-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The four regiments are:</p>
<ul>
<li>1er Régiment de Chasseurs stationed near <a href="/wiki/Verdun" title="Verdun">Verdun</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/7th_Armoured_Brigade_(France)" title="7th Armoured Brigade (France)">7th Armoured Brigade</a></li>
<li>4e Régiment de Dragons stationed in <a href="/wiki/Carnoux-en-Provence" title="Carnoux-en-Provence">Carnoux-en-Provence</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/7th_Armoured_Brigade_(France)" title="7th Armoured Brigade (France)">7th Armoured Brigade</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/12th_Cuirassier_Regiment_(France)" title="12th Cuirassier Regiment (France)">12e Régiment de Cuirassiers</a> stationed in <a href="/wiki/Olivet,_Loiret" title="Olivet, Loiret">Olivet</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_Armoured_Brigade_(France)" title="2nd Armoured Brigade (France)">2nd Armoured Brigade</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/501st-503rd_Tank_Regiment" title="501st-503rd Tank Regiment">501e Régiment de Chars de Combat</a> stationed in <a href="/wiki/Mourmelon-le-Grand" title="Mourmelon-le-Grand">Mourmelon-le-Grand</a>, <a href="/wiki/2nd_Armoured_Brigade_(France)" title="2nd Armoured Brigade (France)">2nd Armoured Brigade</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AMX_Leclerc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Armament">Armament</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AMX_Leclerc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Armament">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Leclerc-IMG_1763.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1889" data-file-width="1889" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Leclerc-IMG_1763.jpg/220px-Leclerc-IMG_1763.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Leclerc-IMG_1763.jpg/330px-Leclerc-IMG_1763.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Leclerc-IMG_1763.jpg/440px-Leclerc-IMG_1763.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Leclerc-IMG_1763.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The commander's sight is on the right and gunner's sight on the left side of the picture.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <i>Leclerc</i> is equipped with a GIAT (Nexter) CN120-26 120mm <a href="/wiki/Smoothbore" title="Smoothbore">smoothbore</a> cannon. This cannon is capable of firing the same <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> standard 120mm rounds as the German <a href="/wiki/Leopard_2" title="Leopard 2">Leopard 2</a> and US <a href="/wiki/M1_Abrams" title="M1 Abrams">M1 Abrams</a>, but in practice only French-produced ammunition is issued. The gun is insulated with a <a href="/wiki/Thermal_sleeve" title="Thermal sleeve">thermal sleeve</a> and has an automatic compressed-air fume extraction system instead of the usual <a href="/wiki/Bore_evacuator" title="Bore evacuator">bore evacuator</a>. The Leclerc has a unique autoloading system which was specifically designed for it, and reduces the crew to three by eliminating the human loader. The turret of the Leclerc was designed around the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Auto-loading_system" title="Auto-loading system">auto-loading system</a> in order to avoid the problems common to other tanks with an autoloader. The <i>Leclerc</i> autoloader allows a rate of fire of 12 shots per minute and holds 22 rounds of ready ammunition; it can accommodate up to six different types of ammunition at once, although like most autoloader systems it cannot change ammunition types once a round has been loaded. The most common types are the armour piercing fin-stabilised discarding sabot (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KE-penetrator" title="KE-penetrator">APFSDS</a>) with a tungsten core and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_explosive_anti-tank" title="High explosive anti-tank">high explosive anti-tank</a> (HEAT) round. There are 18 other rounds available for reload. A Leclerc tank can fire while traveling at a speed of 50 km/h on a target 4,000 metres away.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The gun is 52 <a href="/wiki/Caliber_(artillery)" title="Caliber (artillery)">calibres</a> long instead of the 44 calibres common on most tanks of the Leclerc's generation,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TGelbart_3-11"><a href="#cite_note-TGelbart-3">[3]</a></sup> giving the rounds a higher muzzle velocity.</p>
<p>The <i>Leclerc</i> is also equipped with a 12.7 mm coaxial machine gun and a remote-controlled 7.62 mm machine gun, whereas most other NATO tanks use 7.62 mm weapons for both their coaxial and top machine gun mounts; the major exception is the American M1 Abrams, which has a 7.62 mm coaxial machine gun and two top-mounted machine guns, one 7.62 mm and one 12.7 mm.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Protection">Protection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AMX_Leclerc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Protection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <i>Leclerc</i> has the <b>GALIX</b> combat vehicle protection system from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GIAT" title="GIAT">GIAT</a>, which fires a variety of <a href="/wiki/Smoke_grenade" title="Smoke grenade">smoke grenades</a> and infra red screening rounds, as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anti-personnel" title="Anti-personnel">anti-personnel</a> grenades.</p>
<p>The hull and the turret are made of welded steel fitted with modular armour, which can be replaced easily for repair or upgraded over the years. The French army in the late 1970s rejected <a href="/wiki/Chobham_armour" title="Chobham armour">Chobham armour</a> as being overly specialised in its optimisation to defeat <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hollow_charge" title="Hollow charge">hollow charge</a>-weapons; it, therefore, opted to develop their own composite arrangement to defeat both hollow charge and sabot round. Due to the introduction of modernised threats, the batch 3 has a new armour package, including composite armour, titanium insert and on the sides of the turret, ERA blocks.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fire_control_and_observation">Fire control and observation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AMX_Leclerc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Fire control and observation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Leclerc_mg_7754.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2912" data-file-width="4368" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Leclerc_mg_7754.jpg/220px-Leclerc_mg_7754.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Leclerc_mg_7754.jpg/330px-Leclerc_mg_7754.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Leclerc_mg_7754.jpg/440px-Leclerc_mg_7754.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Leclerc_mg_7754.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Closeup of the front of the Leclerc's turret; the 12.7mm coaxial machine gun can be seen below and to the side of the 120mm main gun</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The last 96 MBT XL <i>Leclerc</i> have the <i>ICONE TIS</i> battle management system with digital communication system which integrates data from other tanks and upper levels of command. Since 2009, All Leclerc in service (S2 and SXXI) have ICONE BMS.</p>
<p>The Leclerc tank features the HL-60 gunner's primary sight from SAGEM. The sight's day channel offers X3.3 and X10 magnification (x14 in case of the UAE version). The night channel thermal intensifier offers X3, X6, X10 and X20 magnifications. No emergency or auxiliary sights are mounted. The laser range finder is of the Nd:YAG type. The tank commander can observe the surroundings through 7 periscopes and the HL-70 Panoramic sight. The HL-70 is equipped with a day channel at X2.5 and X10 magnification and a night channel with X2.5 magnification. On the Series XXI and UAE variant the HL-120 and HL-80 commander's panoramic sight are mounted respectively. The sight offers X2.5 and X10 magnification (and an electronic zoom X2) for the thermal intensifier and a laser range finder of the Nd:YAG type. Both sights are also equipped with a semi auto tracker for target acquisition. The combination of the gunner's primary sight and commander's panoramic sight allows the Leclerc to operate in a hunter-killer mode.</p>
<p>The <i>Leclerc's</i> digital fire control system can be operated independently by the gunner or the commander, and it offers real time integrated imaging from all of the tank's sensors and sights, including the gunner's SAVAN 20 stabilised sight, developed by SAGEM.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Propulsion">Propulsion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AMX_Leclerc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Propulsion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <i>Leclerc</i> has an eight-cylinder SACM (now <a href="/wiki/W%C3%A4rtsil%C3%A4" title="Wärtsilä">Wärtsilä</a>) V8X-1500 1,500 hp Hyperbar <a href="/wiki/Diesel_engine" title="Diesel engine">diesel engine</a> and a Renk AG <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Automatic_transmission" title="Automatic transmission">automatic transmission</a>, with five forward and two reverse gears. The official maximum speed by road is 72 km/h and 55 km/h cross country (speeds in excess of 80 km/h were reported on road). The maximum range is given as 550 km, and can be extended to 650 km with removable external tanks. The "hyperbar" system integrates a <a href="/wiki/Turbomeca" title="Turbomeca">Turbomeca</a> TM 307B <a href="/wiki/Gas_turbine" title="Gas turbine">gas turbine</a> in the engine, acting both as a <a href="/wiki/Turbocharger" title="Turbocharger">turbocharger</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Auxiliary_power_unit" title="Auxiliary power unit">APU</a> giving auxiliary power to all systems when the main engine is shut down. The Hyperbar name comes from the unusually high <a href="/wiki/Forced_induction" title="Forced induction">boost pressure</a> of 7.5 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">bar</a> and the resulting <a href="/wiki/Mean_effective_pressure" title="Mean effective pressure">mean effective pressure</a> of 32.1 bar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> To compare, the largest diesel engine in the world (the <a href="/wiki/W%C3%A4rtsil%C3%A4-Sulzer_RTA96-C" title="Wärtsilä-Sulzer RTA96-C">Wärtsila RTA96</a>) has a mean effective pressure of 18.6 bar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In addition, with boost available even at idle, this arrangement also works as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anti-lag_system" title="Anti-lag system">anti-lag system</a>.</p>
<p>At a combat weight of just 56 tons, the Leclerc is one of the lightest main battle tanks in the world; this gives it one of the best power-to-weight ratios among the Western tanks (27 hp per ton) and makes it one of the fastest MBTs of its generation (0 to 32 km/h in 5 seconds).</p>
<p>The engine exhaust, exiting at the rear left, is cooled to reduce the thermal signature of the Leclerc. <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">Transmission</a> is a hydromechanical type with five forward and two reverse gears. Fuel tanks carry 1,300 litres and act as extra protection for the tank; two 200-litre external tanks can be fitted on the rear of the turret, but have to be jettisoned before entering combat since they limit turret rotation.</p>
<p>The gear box is equipped with a hydrokinetic <a href="/wiki/Retarder_(mechanical_engineering)" title="Retarder (mechanical engineering)">retarder</a> which can slow the Leclerc down at a deceleration rate of 7 m/s² (0.7 g) which is automatically used over 30 km/h.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Combat_history">Combat history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AMX_Leclerc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Combat history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In service since 1992 (after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf_War" title="Persian Gulf War">Persian Gulf War</a>), the Leclerc has mostly seen deployment on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low-intensity_conflict" title="Low-intensity conflict">low-intensity conflicts</a>, including 15 <i>Leclerc</i> stationed in <a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Kosovo</a> (<a href="/wiki/Kosovo_Force" title="Kosovo Force">KFOR</a>) and others in <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UNIFIL" title="UNIFIL">UNIFIL</a>) within UN peace-keeping operations, where their performance was judged satisfactory by French officials.</p>
<p>Until 2010, 13 Leclerc were deployed in the south Lebanon for a <a href="/wiki/Peacekeeping" title="Peacekeeping">peacekeeping mission</a> with UNIFIL.</p>
<p>As of August 2015, Leclerc tanks of the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a> were deployed in combat operations in Yemen (near Aden) as part of the <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabian-led_intervention_in_Yemen" title="Saudi Arabian-led intervention in Yemen">Saudi-led coalition</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube.com_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> It is estimated 70 Leclerc MBTs were deployed by the UAE in Yemen, 15 of them equipped with the AZUR package. 3 tanks were damaged, 2 by antitank mines and 1 by a RPG which damaged the grid without piercing the hull. In a single incident; one Leclerc tank was hit and penetrated in the front hull by an ATGM, possibly of Konkurs or Konkurs-M type, resulting in the death of the driver and injuries to the legs of the commander. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> None of these tanks were lost.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
