<p><b>Charlotte Independence</b> are an American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soccer" title="Soccer">soccer</a> team based in <a href="/wiki/Charlotte,_North_Carolina" title="Charlotte, North Carolina">Charlotte, North Carolina</a>. They play in the <a href="/wiki/United_Soccer_League" title="United Soccer League">United Soccer League</a>, the second tier of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_soccer_pyramid" title="American soccer pyramid">American soccer pyramid</a>. They are affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">Major League Soccer</a> (MLS) team the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rapids" title="Colorado Rapids">Colorado Rapids</a>. They play their home games at Ramblewood Soccer Complex.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charlotte_Independence&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Charlotte Independence were founded in 2014. They took over the USL Pro franchise of the <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Eagles" title="Charlotte Eagles">Charlotte Eagles</a>, who moved into the amateur <a href="/wiki/Premier_Development_League" title="Premier Development League">Premier Development League</a> (PDL).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Independence take their name from <a href="/wiki/Mecklenburg_Declaration_of_Independence" title="Mecklenburg Declaration of Independence">Mecklenburg Declaration of Independence</a>. Their crest features the year 1775 (the year in which it was alleged to have been signed) and a horse-mounted Captain James Jack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Supporters were asked to choose one of twelve designs, all 12 featured the year and Jack.</p>
<p>Former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dallas_Burn" title="Dallas Burn">Dallas Burn</a> head coach <a href="/wiki/Mike_Jeffries_(soccer)" title="Mike Jeffries (soccer)">Mike Jeffries</a> was hired as the Independence head coach on December 5, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Independence lost their first game to <a href="/wiki/Charleston_Battery" title="Charleston Battery">Charleston Battery</a> 3-2 on March 28, 2015 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transamerica_Field" title="Transamerica Field">Transamerica Field</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jack_Thompson_(soccer)" title="Jack Thompson (soccer)">Jack Thompson</a> scored the team's first ever goal in the 13th minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stadium">Stadium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charlotte_Independence&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Stadium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On February 25, 2015, the team announced they would play most of their 2015 season in a temporary soccer stadium being assembled at <a href="/wiki/Ramblewood_Soccer_Complex" title="Ramblewood Soccer Complex">Ramblewood Soccer Complex</a>, near the <a href="/wiki/Interstate_77" title="Interstate 77">I-77</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interstate_485_(North_Carolina)" title="Interstate 485 (North Carolina)">I-485</a> interchange in the southwest corner of the city. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> After splitting its first five home games between venues on <a href="/wiki/University_of_North_Carolina_at_Charlotte" title="University of North Carolina at Charlotte">University of North Carolina at Charlotte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Winthrop_University" title="Winthrop University">Winthrop University</a>, the Independence won their Ramblewood debut on June 20, 2015. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
