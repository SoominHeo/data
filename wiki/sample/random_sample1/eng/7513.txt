<p><b>Charlotte Maria Church</b> (born 21 February 1986) is a <a href="/wiki/Welsh_people" title="Welsh people">Welsh</a> singer-songwriter, actress and television presenter.</p>
<p>She rose to fame in childhood as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_classical_music" title="European classical music">classical singer</a> before branching into pop music in 2005. By 2007, she had sold more than 10 million records worldwide<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> including over 5 million in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2010 she was reported to be worth as much as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C2%A3" title="Â£">Â£</a>11m<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> (though one 2003 report quoted her worth at Â£25m<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>). She hosted a <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> chat show titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Charlotte_Church_Show" title="The Charlotte Church Show">The Charlotte Church Show</a></i>. Church released her first album in five years, titled <i>Back to Scratch</i>, on 17 October 2010. Church is a <a href="/wiki/Soprano" title="Soprano">soprano</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charlotte_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charlotte_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Charlotte Church was born <b>Charlotte Maria Reed</b> in <a href="/wiki/Llandaff" title="Llandaff">Llandaff</a>, a district of <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a>, Wales. She was brought up as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> by her mother, Maria, who was separated from Church's biological father, Stephen Reed. Church was adopted by her mother's second husband, James Church, in 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Her musical break came at age 11 when she sang <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Lloyd_Webber" title="Andrew Lloyd Webber">Andrew Lloyd Webber</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Pie_Jesu" title="Pie Jesu">Pie Jesu</a>" over the telephone on the television show <i><a href="/wiki/This_Morning_(TV_programme)" title="This Morning (TV programme)">This Morning</a></i> in 1997, followed by her performance on <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a>'s <i>Big, Big Talent Show</i> in 1997.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>This led to concerts at <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_Arms_Park" title="Cardiff Arms Park">Cardiff Arms Park</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Albert_Hall" title="Royal Albert Hall">Royal Albert Hall</a> and opening for Dame <a href="/wiki/Shirley_Bassey" title="Shirley Bassey">Shirley Bassey</a> in Antwerp. She also received a vocal scholarship to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Howell%27s_School_Llandaff" title="Howell's School Llandaff">Howell's School Llandaff</a> in Cardiff where she started in 1998 after leaving <a href="/wiki/The_Cathedral_School,_Llandaff" title="The Cathedral School, Llandaff">The Cathedral School, Llandaff</a>. She balanced performing and school with help from tutors for when she was on the road and said in many interviews that she was "just like every other girl her age". Charlotte left school at age 16.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1998.E2.80.932003:_Classical_career">1998â2003: Classical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charlotte_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1998â2003: Classical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As a classical music singer, Church used to sing in English, <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>, Italian and French. She was then introduced to the Cardiff impresario Jonathan Shalit, who became her manager and negotiated a contract with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment" title="Sony Music Entertainment">Sony Music</a>. Her first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Voice_of_an_Angel" title="Voice of an Angel">Voice of an Angel</a></i>, was a collection of <a href="/wiki/Aria" title="Aria">arias</a>, sacred songs, and traditional pieces that sold millions worldwide and made her the youngest artist with a No. 1 album on the British classical crossover charts.</p>
<p>Church appeared on US <a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">Public Broadcasting Service (PBS)</a> specials. Her <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Church_(album)" title="Charlotte Church (album)">self-titled second album</a> also included operatic, religious and traditional tracks. One, the soaring and inspirational <i>Just Wave Hello</i>, was the centrepiece of a millennium-themed ad campaign for the <a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford Motor Company</a>. The song's full-length video, featuring Church, won acclaim at the <a href="/wiki/North_American_International_Auto_Show" title="North American International Auto Show">Detroit Auto Show</a> and introduced her to new fans. The track reached No. 31 in Britain.</p>
<p>In 2000, she released <i><a href="/wiki/Dream_a_Dream" title="Dream a Dream">Dream a Dream</a></i>, an album of <a href="/wiki/Christmas_carol" title="Christmas carol">Christmas carols</a>. It included Church's first foray into a more non classical, pop-influenced style in the title track <i>Dream a Dream</i>, borrowing the melody from <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Faur%C3%A9" title="Gabriel FaurÃ©">FaurÃ©'s</a> <a href="/wiki/Pavane_(Faur%C3%A9)" title="Pavane (FaurÃ©)">Pavane</a> and featuring child American country singer <a href="/wiki/Billy_Gilman" title="Billy Gilman">Billy Gilman</a>.</p>
<p>In 2001, Church added more pop, swing, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broadway_theater" title="Broadway theater">Broadway</a> with her album <i><a href="/wiki/Enchantment_(Charlotte_Church_album)" title="Enchantment (Charlotte Church album)">Enchantment</a></i>. That year, Church made her first film appearance in the 2001 <a href="/wiki/Ron_Howard" title="Ron Howard">Ron Howard</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/A_Beautiful_Mind_(film)" title="A Beautiful Mind (film)">A Beautiful Mind</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Celine_Dion" title="Celine Dion">Celine Dion</a> was beginning a concert engagement in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Las_Vegas,_Nevada" title="Las Vegas, Nevada">Las Vegas</a> and was not available to perform the film's end title song, "All Love Can Be", so composer <a href="/wiki/James_Horner" title="James Horner">James Horner</a> enlisted Church and the song was rewritten for her <a href="/wiki/Vocal_range" title="Vocal range">vocal range</a>. Church also handled other vocal passages throughout the score.</p>
<p>In 2002, at 16, she released a "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_of" title="Best of">best of</a>" album called <i><a href="/wiki/Prelude:_The_Best_of_Charlotte_Church" title="Prelude: The Best of Charlotte Church">Prelude</a></i>, and took part in the Royal Christmas tour alongside Dame <a href="/wiki/Julie_Andrews" title="Julie Andrews">Julie Andrews</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Plummer" title="Christopher Plummer">Christopher Plummer</a>, concluding the end of her classical music career.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2003.E2.80.9309:_Pursuing_pop_career.3B_Tissues_and_Issues">2003â09: Pursuing pop career; <i>Tissues and Issues</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charlotte_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2003â09: Pursuing pop career; Tissues and Issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In 2003 Church teamed up with <a href="/wiki/Trance_music" title="Trance music">trance music</a> producer <a href="/wiki/Darren_Tate" title="Darren Tate">Jurgen Vries</a> to sing vocals on his track <i><a href="/wiki/The_Opera_Song_(Brave_New_World)" title="The Opera Song (Brave New World)">The Opera Song (Brave New World)</a></i>. She was credited on the records as CMC (her initials) as it was her first foray into pop music. The song reached number 3 in the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>, Church's second highest charting single and Vries' highest.</p>
<p>In 2005, Church issued her first pop album <i><a href="/wiki/Tissues_and_Issues" title="Tissues and Issues">Tissues and Issues</a>.</i> Four singles were moderately successful in the UK with "<a href="/wiki/Crazy_Chick" title="Crazy Chick">Crazy Chick</a>" reaching no. 2, "<a href="/wiki/Call_My_Name_(Charlotte_Church_song)" title="Call My Name (Charlotte Church song)">Call My Name</a>" number 10, "<a href="/wiki/Even_God_Can%27t_Change_the_Past" title="Even God Can't Change the Past">Even God Can't Change the Past</a>" number 17, and "<a href="/wiki/Moodswings_(to_Come_at_Me_like_That)" title="Moodswings (to Come at Me like That)">Moodswings</a>" number 14. Although these were released in Australia as well, they failed to reach the same level of success there. Church's pop album was released in the US through Amazon.com MP3 shop, and iTunes in 2009.</p>
<p>In April 2006, she performed three concerts in <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a>, London, and Cardiff, in venues holding between 2,000 and 3,000 people; the dates at London and Cardiff were sold out. Supported by Irish band the New Druids, Church performed a mix of tracks from her debut pop album and a number of pop covers including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince_(artist)" title="Prince (artist)">Prince's</a> "<a href="/wiki/Kiss_(Prince_song)" title="Kiss (Prince song)">Kiss</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Gloria_Estefan" title="Gloria Estefan">Gloria Estefan</a>'s "Rhythm is Gonna Get You". Though Church hinted at the possibility of a full tour in the future, none took place.</p>
<p>In November 2006, it was announced that she and Sony had parted ways. According to her publicist, this was a mutual decision reached after a series of meetings throughout the year, which were held since her 6-album deal had ended. There was speculation that Church had decided to take a break from her singing career to focus on her television show. Others suggested that her pop releases' chart performance contributed to the decision.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Yet another factor was her pregnancy with her daughter, Ruby Megan Henson.</p>

<p>In 2007, Church became Patron of the charity The Topsy Foundation UK,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> helping to raise awareness and funds for its work to support rural communities in South Africa, empowering people infected with and affected by HIV and AIDS, through medical care, social support and skills development.</p>
<p>Articles emerged in the UK press in March and April 2008 stating that she was still training classically, considering a return to classical crossover. Church has sung in religious services in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taiz%C3%A9" title="TaizÃ©">TaizÃ©</a>. She has also performed before <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_Paul_II" title="Pope John Paul II">Pope John Paul II</a>, Queen <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Elizabeth II</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Charles,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Charles, Prince of Wales">Prince of Wales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a>.</p>
<p>In June 2008, she became pregnant with her son, Dexter Lloyd Henson. In Church's latest interview, she mentioned she would be ready to work on more music in a few months, although she was not sure whether she would further develop her career in classical, pop, or both, as she loves both genres for different reasons. Church also mentioned that she has been working with a vocal instructor to keep her voice in check, and while she has never put a focus on her body image, she would like to get back into pre-pregnancy shape before resuming work.</p>
<p>In June 2009, Church was interviewed for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hello!" title="Hello!">Hello!</a></i> magazine,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hellomagazine.com_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-hellomagazine.com-12">[12]</a></sup> and discussed her life since having her second child. She said that she was currently in the studio, resuming work on a new album and that her partner, <a href="/wiki/Gavin_Henson" title="Gavin Henson">Gavin Henson</a>, had been strongly encouraging her to get back to work pursuing the career that she has greatly missed since settling down.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.9311:_Return_to_music_and_Back_to_Scratch">2010â11: Return to music and <i>Back to Scratch</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charlotte_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010â11: Return to music and Back to Scratch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Church appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Friday_Night_with_Jonathan_Ross" title="Friday Night with Jonathan Ross">Friday Night with Jonathan Ross</a></i> on 13 March 2010, where she confirmed that she had already begun writing and recording her sixth studio album. Church also stated that the album was of a different sound to previous ones, more mature with a "kooky" vibe.</p>
<p>On 4 August 2010, Church made a new track, "Cold California", available to download from her website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-charlottechurch.co_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-charlottechurch.co-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Church's new album, <i><a href="/wiki/Back_to_Scratch" title="Back to Scratch">Back to Scratch</a></i>, was released in the UK on 25 October 2010. The 14-track album was produced by Martin Terefe. <i>Back to Scratch</i> was preceded by a singleÂ - the LP's title trackÂ - on 24 October. "Back to Scratch" was originally inspired "by problems facing a family member", but Church admitted in a press release that the song now has resonance to her own personal life following her split from Gavin Henson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digitalspy.co.uk_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-digitalspy.co.uk-14">[14]</a></sup> <i>Back to Scratch</i> also featured the song "The Actors", which Church performed on BBC One talent contest <i><a href="/wiki/Over_the_Rainbow_(2010_TV_series)" title="Over the Rainbow (2010 TV series)">Over The Rainbow</a></i>, and a cover of <a href="/wiki/Joni_Mitchell" title="Joni Mitchell">Joni Mitchell</a>'s "River".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digitalspy.co.uk_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-digitalspy.co.uk-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was announced on 13 March 2011 that Church had terminated her US$3 million deal with Power Amp Music over promotional disputes. Her spokesperson released the following statement: "All I can really say, because of the confidentiality issues, is that it was in Charlotte's financial interests to do so before the agreement entered the second year of its term. This is typical for these type of deals, which are investment deals rather than record deals. I can also say that the decision to terminate the term early, which suited both parties, was made well before the commercial release of the album". A spokesperson from the record label also released a statement saying "It didn't work out with Charlotte and that's fair enough. There was no falling out. It was a mutual decision. They decided to exit the deal".</p>
<p>The third single to be released from the album, "Snow" was released on 11 April 2011.</p>

<p>On 26 May 2012, Church premiered three new songs "The Rise", "How Not to be Surprised When You're a Ghost", and "Say It's True" on BBC Radio with Bethan Elfyn. Church released her first EP <i><a href="/wiki/One_(Charlotte_Church_EP)" title="One (Charlotte Church EP)">ONE</a></i> on 4 September 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 19 December 2012, Church released the lead single from her second EP, "Glitterbombed" on The Line of Best Fit, an online music magazine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Her second EP <i><a href="/wiki/Two_(Charlotte_Church_EP)" title="Two (Charlotte Church EP)">TWO</a></i> was released on 4 March 2013.</p>

<p><i>ONE</i> and <i>TWO</i> were combined for a US release on 12 March 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Church promoted <i>ONE</i> and <i>TWO</i> in the US with her first North American performances in almost a decade. She appeared in New York, Los Angeles, Toronto, and at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_by_Southwest_Festival" title="South by Southwest Festival">South by Southwest Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Her costumes for her North American concerts were designed by Zoe Howerska.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_and_television_career">Acting and television career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charlotte_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Acting and television career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Church has made a number of cameo appearances on television. She appeared in the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Touched_by_an_Angel" title="Touched by an Angel">Touched by an Angel</a></i>, starred in the 1999 Christmas special of <i><a href="/wiki/Heartbeat_(UK_TV_series)" title="Heartbeat (UK TV series)">Heartbeat</a></i>, and in 2002, 2003 and 2012 she appeared on episodes of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Have_I_Got_News_For_You" title="Have I Got News For You">Have I Got News For You</a></i> (the first time as the show's youngest-ever panellist; the second time as host). In 2005 she played herself in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Catherine_Tate_Show" title="The Catherine Tate Show">The Catherine Tate Show</a></i>, in a sketch with the fictional character <a href="/wiki/Joannie_Taylor" title="Joannie Taylor">Joannie Taylor</a>. In 2008 she appeared briefly in a sketch in <i><a href="/wiki/Katy_Brand%27s_Big_Ass_Show" title="Katy Brand's Big Ass Show">Katy Brand's Big Ass Show</a></i>.</p>
<p>In December 2005, for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Paul_O%27Grady_Show" title="The Paul O'Grady Show">The Paul O'Grady Show</a></i> Christmas <a href="/wiki/Pantomime" title="Pantomime">pantomime</a>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Wizard_of_Oz_(adaptations)" title="The Wizard of Oz (adaptations)">The Wizard of Oz</a></i>, Church played <a href="/wiki/Dorothy_Gale" title="Dorothy Gale">Dorothy Gale</a>.</p>
<p>In the summer of 2006, Church began work on her own entertainment TV show, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Charlotte_Church_Show" title="The Charlotte Church Show">The Charlotte Church Show</a></i>. After a pilot episode which caused some controversy and which was never released to the public,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> the series began on 1 September 2006 on <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a>. Church won a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Comedy_Award" title="British Comedy Award">British Comedy Award</a> for "Best Female Comedy Newcomer" in 2006,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> and the 'Funniest TV Personality' award at the 2006 <i><a href="/wiki/Loaded_(magazine)" title="Loaded (magazine)">Loaded Magazine's</a></i> 'LAFTA' awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In 2008 she was nominated for the <i><a href="/wiki/Rose_d%27Or" title="Rose d'Or">Rose d'Or</a></i> Special Award for Best Entertainer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late June 2008, <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> began showing trials for the series. It has since concluded its eight-show run. A Christmas special aired on 21 December 2008. The show ended after its third series.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In January 2010 for <i>Hospital 24/7</i>, Church made an appearance on the programme finale, where she visits the Children's Hospital for Wales to launch the Noah's Ark Appeals campaign to fund the equipment in the new Critical Care Unit, which will help children needing high dependency, or critical &amp; intensive care.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charlotte_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Church released an autobiography titled <i>Voice of an Angel (My Life So Far)</i> at 14.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> She released a second autobiography titled <i>Keep Smiling</i> in late 2007.</p>
<p>Church's personal life has often been portrayed in UK <a href="/wiki/Tabloid_(newspaper_format)" title="Tabloid (newspaper format)">tabloid</a> newspapers, inspiring the song "Let's Be Alone" on her album <i><a href="/wiki/Tissues_and_Issues" title="Tissues and Issues">Tissues and Issues</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> At age 15 she was criticised for remarks she made about the behaviour of people in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">11 September 2001 attacks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/Leveson_Inquiry" title="Leveson Inquiry">Leveson Inquiry</a> she stated that her comments were taken vastly out of context.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> In a 2006 interview with <a href="/wiki/Davina_McCall" title="Davina McCall">Davina McCall</a>, she agreed being diplomatic was "not in her nature".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In November 2011, Church testified before the <a href="/wiki/Leveson_Inquiry" title="Leveson Inquiry">Leveson Inquiry</a> about the media intrusion into her personal life stating "I've been made a caricature for so long, and this person portrayed in the tabloids really isn't me. It's not the person I am, and it's had a massive impact on my career. As an artist, I find it hard to be taken seriously because my credibility has been blown to bits."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> On 27 February 2012 Church accepted Â£600,000 in damages and costs in settlement of a lawsuit arising out of the <a href="/wiki/News_International_phone_hacking_scandal" title="News International phone hacking scandal">News International phone hacking scandal</a>. She had claimed that 33 stories about her that appeared in the <i><a href="/wiki/News_of_the_World" title="News of the World">News of the World</a></i> were the product of illegal hacking into her family's voicemail. After the settlement was announced in open court she made a lengthy statement<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> in which she said, "I have also discovered that despite the apology which the newspaper has just given in court, these people were prepared to go to any lengths to prevent me exposing their behaviour. They are not truly sorry. They are just sorry they got caught."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>

At the end of 2005, she bought a property in her native <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a>âfor a reported Â£500,000âwhich she later sold for Â£900,000. The couple mentioned marriage on talk shows and in the press<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hello_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-hello-36">[36]</a></sup> but put off marriage while Church was pregnant.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 2007, Church made another appearance on a British young people's <a href="/wiki/Sunday_Times_Rich_List" title="Sunday Times Rich List">rich list</a> with Henson. They were ranked 49th-richest young people in Britain with an estimated joint wealth of Â£12 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> The couple bought a <a href="/wiki/Princess_Yachts" title="Princess Yachts">Princess yacht</a> in spring 2008 for Â£800,000, named <i>Sketchy</i>, which is berthed at <a href="/wiki/Swansea_docks" title="Swansea docks">Swansea marina</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <p>On 20 September 2007, Church gave birth to a daughter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> On 11 January 2009, Church gave birth to a son.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> The <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Mail" title="Daily Mail">Daily Mail</a></i> reported on 31 May 2010 that Church and Henson had split up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
