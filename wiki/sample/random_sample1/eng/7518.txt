<p>The <b>Type 093</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">093型核潜艇</span>; Chinese designation: <b>09-III</b>; <a href="/wiki/NATO_reporting_name" title="NATO reporting name">NATO reporting name</a>: <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shang_Dynasty" title="Shang Dynasty"><i>Shang</i></a>-class</b>) is a class of <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_submarine" title="Nuclear submarine">nuclear-powered</a> <a href="/wiki/Attack_submarine" title="Attack submarine">attack submarines</a> deployed by the Chinese <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army_Navy_Submarine_Force" title="People's Liberation Army Navy Submarine Force">People's Liberation Army Navy Submarine Force</a>. The improved <b>Type 093G</b> (reported as <b>Type 093A</b> by Western analysts<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-O.27Rourke_2014:_14_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-O.27Rourke_2014:_14-4">[4]</a></sup>) is longer than the Type 093. The 093G is now confirmed having a <a href="/wiki/Vertical_launching_system" title="Vertical launching system">vertical launching system</a> (VLS) for <a href="/wiki/YJ-18" title="YJ-18">YJ-18</a> supersonic <a href="/wiki/Anti-ship_missile" title="Anti-ship missile">anti-ship missiles</a>, and anti-ship variants of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CJ-10" title="CJ-10">CJ-10</a> cruise missile.</p>
<p>They are constructed at the Bohai Shipyard in <a href="/wiki/Huludao" title="Huludao">Huludao</a>. These boats are expected to replace the older <a href="/wiki/Type_091_submarine" title="Type 091 submarine">Type 091 submarines</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Type_093_submarine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Global Security.org reports that development of the Type 093 began in the early 1980s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globalsecurity_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-globalsecurity-1">[1]</a></sup> However, Admiral <a href="/wiki/Liu_Huaqing" title="Liu Huaqing">Liu Huaqing</a> wrote in his memoirs that development began in 1994; this followed President <a href="/wiki/Jiang_Zemin" title="Jiang Zemin">Jiang Zemin</a>'s continued support for nuclear submarine development after the launch of the final Type 091 in 1990. Erickson and Goldstein suggest that the <a href="/wiki/Yinhe_incident" title="Yinhe incident">Yinhe incident</a> in 1993,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and continued tensions with Taiwan,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> also drove approval of the program.</p>
<p>Two Type 093s were launched in 2002 and 2003 respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-O.27Rourke_2014:_91_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-O.27Rourke_2014:_91-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, it was understood that four improved Type 093s were at various stages of construction,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> including one which had already been launched in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-O.27Rourke_2014:_91_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-O.27Rourke_2014:_91-7">[7]</a></sup> In December 2014, Chinese media reported that two Type 093Gs may have been completed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chinatimes_2015-02-15_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-chinatimes_2015-02-15-9">[9]</a></sup> In May 2015, Chinese media reported three Type 093Gs were completed and awaiting delivery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chinadaily_2015-04-03_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-chinadaily_2015-04-03-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Analysts speculate that no further Type 093s will be built due to the development of the <a href="/wiki/Type_095_submarine" title="Type 095 submarine">Type 095 submarine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globalsecurity_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-globalsecurity-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Type_093_submarine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Type 093 is estimated to be roughly 7,000 <a href="/wiki/Ton" title="Ton">t</a> displacement when submerged, 110 metres (360 ft) long with a beam of 11 metres (36 ft). Commercial imagery suggests the Type 093G is longer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-O.27Rourke_2014:_14_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-O.27Rourke_2014:_14-4">[4]</a></sup> The sonar suite includes H/SQC-207 flank-mounted sonar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globalsecurity_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-globalsecurity-1">[1]</a></sup> The boat may also use a seven-blade <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skewback_propeller" title="Skewback propeller">asymmetric propeller</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globalsecurity_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-globalsecurity-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Erickson_and_Goldstein_2007:_67_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Erickson_and_Goldstein_2007:_67-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The powerplant is speculated to be two <a href="/wiki/Pressurized_water_reactor" title="Pressurized water reactor">pressurized water reactors</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globalsecurity_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-globalsecurity-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2002, Chinese sources said the Type 093 was powered by a high-temperature gas-cooled reactor, but the immaturity of the technology makes this unlikely.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Type 093 is armed with six torpedo tubes; reports conflict on whether these are 553 mm<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globalsecurity_1-11"><a href="#cite_note-globalsecurity-1">[1]</a></sup> or 650 mm tubes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Erickson_and_Goldstein_2007:_68_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Erickson_and_Goldstein_2007:_68-12">[12]</a></sup> The Type 093 has been said to be armed with the <a href="/wiki/YJ-12" title="YJ-12">YJ-12</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Erickson_and_Goldstein_2007:_68_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Erickson_and_Goldstein_2007:_68-12">[12]</a></sup> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/YJ-82" title="YJ-82">YJ-82</a> anti-ship missiles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globalsecurity_1-12"><a href="#cite_note-globalsecurity-1">[1]</a></sup> Anti-ship cruise missiles may also be future options.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Chinese sources say the Type 093G includes a <a href="/wiki/Vertical_launching_system" title="Vertical launching system">vertical launching system</a> for <a href="/wiki/YJ-18" title="YJ-18">YJ-18</a> anti-ship missiles, and a longer teardrop hull with a "wing-shaped cross-section" for improved speed and stealth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chinadaily_2015-04-03_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-chinadaily_2015-04-03-3">[3]</a></sup> Rear Admiral <a href="/wiki/Yin_Zhuo" title="Yin Zhuo">Yin Zhuo</a> has said the Type 093G may carry 12 anti-ship variants of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CJ-10" title="CJ-10">CJ-10</a> cruise missile.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wct_2015-04-07_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-wct_2015-04-07-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Noise">Noise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Type_093_submarine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Noise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2002, Chinese sources said the Type 093's noise level was on par with the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles-class_submarine" title="Los Angeles-class submarine">improved Los Angeles-class</a>. In 2004, Chinese sources said the Type 093 was on par with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Project_971" title="Project 971">Project 971</a> (<a href="/wiki/NATO_reporting_name" title="NATO reporting name">NATO reporting name</a> <i>Akula</i>), at 110 <a href="/wiki/Decibel" title="Decibel">decibels</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Erickson_and_Goldstein_2007:_67_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Erickson_and_Goldstein_2007:_67-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2009, USN ONI listed the Type 093 as being noisier than <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Project_671" title="Project 671">Project 671RTM</a> (NATO reporting name <i>Victor III</i>) which entered service in 1979.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Chinese media say the Type 093G is quieter than the shorter Type 093 due to an altered hull form.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chinadaily_2015-04-03_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-chinadaily_2015-04-03-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Variants">Variants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Type_093_submarine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Variants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are two suspected variants of the Type 093. One, as noted above is the Type 093G, the <a href="/wiki/Cruise_missile_submarine" title="Cruise missile submarine">cruise missile</a> variant. This and the fast attack variant, might be able to launch the YJ-18 anti-ship cruise missile and a naval version of the DF-10 long-range land-attack cruise missile. The second variant is the Type 093A, which will be or are the third and subsequent three boats. These are stretched versions of the two original Shang-class boats: possibly in an attempt to accommodate a Dry Dock Shelter for special forces.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IHSJanes_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-IHSJanes-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
