<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chantal_Kreviazuk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Albums">Albums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chantal_Kreviazuk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Albums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kreviazuk's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Under_These_Rocks_and_Stones" title="Under These Rocks and Stones">Under These Rocks and Stones</a></i>, was first issued in Canada in August 1996 before being released in the US June 1997 to critical praise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The album sold over 150,000 copies in Canada according to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SoundScan" title="SoundScan">SoundScan</a>, fueled mostly by the singles "<a href="/wiki/Surrounded_(Chantal_Kreviazuk_song)" title="Surrounded (Chantal Kreviazuk song)">Surrounded</a>" and "God Made Me". <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>Three videos from the album received modest play on the video channel MuchMusic and radio ("God Made Me", "Believer" and "Wayne"), but it was a fourth, "Surrounded", that became her first major Canadian airplay hit in 1997. That year, Kreviazuk received her first <a href="/wiki/Juno_Award" title="Juno Award">Juno Award</a> nomination as Best New Artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> She also took part in the 1998 <a href="/wiki/Lilith_Fair" title="Lilith Fair">Lilith Fair</a> music festival; "Surrounded" was included in the live compilation album from that year.</p>
<p>In 1999, Kreviazuk released her second album. Titled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colour_Moving_and_Still" title="Colour Moving and Still">Colour Moving and Still</a></i>, it featured tracks written with her new husband, Raine Maida, lead singer of <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_Peace" title="Our Lady Peace">Our Lady Peace</a>. The lead single from the album "<a href="/wiki/Before_You" title="Before You">Before You</a>" became a huge radio hit in Canada and she performed the single on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2000_Juno_Awards" title="2000 Juno Awards">2000 Juno Awards</a>, where she won two awards for <i>Best Adult/Pop Album</i> and <i>Best Female Artist</i>. Kreviazuk released two more videos from the album, "Dear Life" and "Far Away", as well as an additional radio release, "Souls", which was also remixed and was released as a promo vinyl for the M1 &amp; Steve Fernandez Remix.</p>
<p>Her third album, <i><a href="/wiki/What_If_It_All_Means_Something" title="What If It All Means Something">What If It All Means Something</a></i>, was released in 2002. This album also featured multiple collaborations with Maida. The first single, "In This Life", was a hit in Canada. Kreviazuk performed the song live on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_with_Jay_Leno" title="The Tonight Show with Jay Leno">The Tonight Show with Jay Leno</a></i>.</p>
<p>Kreviazuk announced via her blog in August 2005 that she was writing and recording her fourth album in her home studio, with Maida producing. The first single off the album, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_I_Can_Do_(Chantal_Kreviazuk)" title="All I Can Do (Chantal Kreviazuk)">All I Can Do</a>", was made available on her official website and MySpace page prior to the release of the album. On July 24 the album premiered on the Canadian music channel Much More Music along with a behind the scenes special. The album, <i><a href="/wiki/Ghost_Stories_(Chantal_Kreviazuk_album)" title="Ghost Stories (Chantal Kreviazuk album)">Ghost Stories</a></i>, was released August 29, 2006.</p>
<p>On October 28, 2008, she released her first compilation album, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Since_We_Met:_The_Best_of_1996-2006" title="Since We Met: The Best of 1996-2006">Since We Met: The Best of 1996-2006</a></i>.</p>
<p>On October 13, 2009, she released her fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Plain_Jane_(album)" title="Plain Jane (album)">Plain Jane</a></i>.</p>
<p>She is featured on a song called "Over My Dead Body" by fellow Canadian recording artist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drake_(entertainer)" title="Drake (entertainer)">Drake</a> from his 2011 album <i><a href="/wiki/Take_Care_(album)" title="Take Care (album)">Take Care</a></i>.</p>
<p>Her first live album, entitled <i>In This Life</i>, was released on July 3, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>She featured on rapper <a href="/wiki/Jay_Rock" title="Jay Rock">Jay Rock</a>'s track <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pay_for_It_(Jay_Rock_song)" title="Pay for It (Jay Rock song)">Pay for It</a></i>, which she performed alongside Jay Rock and <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a> at <a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a> on November 15, 2014.</p>
<p>In November 2014, Kreviazuk released a new single called "I Will Be", speaking to her desire to want to help those who feel alone and isolated. She sang the song live on December 12 on the Toronto radio station <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/104.5_CHUM_FM" title="104.5 CHUM FM">104.5 CHUM FM</a>.</p>

<p>Kreviazuk's work has appeared on many soundtracks for films and television shows. In 1998, Kreviazuk scored her only international hit to date with a cover of "<a href="/wiki/Leaving_on_a_Jet_Plane" title="Leaving on a Jet Plane">Leaving on a Jet Plane</a>", a song written by <a href="/wiki/John_Denver" title="John Denver">John Denver</a> and popularized by <a href="/wiki/Peter,_Paul_and_Mary" title="Peter, Paul and Mary">Peter, Paul and Mary</a>. The song appeared on the soundtrack to the blockbuster film <i><a href="/wiki/Armageddon_(1998_film)" title="Armageddon (1998 film)">Armageddon</a></i> and was that album's follow-up single to <a href="/wiki/Aerosmith" title="Aerosmith">Aerosmith</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Want_to_Miss_a_Thing" title="I Don't Want to Miss a Thing">I Don't Want to Miss a Thing</a>". The soundtrack album also featured a remix of <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_Peace" title="Our Lady Peace">Our Lady Peace</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/Starseed_(song)" title="Starseed (song)">Starseed</a>".</p>
<p>In 1999, Kreviazuk recorded a cover of the <a href="/wiki/Randy_Newman" title="Randy Newman">Randy Newman</a> ballad "<a href="/wiki/Feels_Like_Home_(Chantal_Kreviazuk_song)" title="Feels Like Home (Chantal Kreviazuk song)">Feels Like Home</a>" for the <i><a href="/wiki/Dawson%27s_Creek" title="Dawson's Creek">Dawson's Creek</a></i> soundtrack, as well as <i><a href="/wiki/How_to_Lose_a_Guy_in_10_Days" title="How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days">How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days</a></i>. She also performed a cover version of <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">The Beatles</a>' "<a href="/wiki/In_My_Life" title="In My Life">In My Life</a>" as the theme song for the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> television drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Providence_(TV_series)" title="Providence (TV series)">Providence</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2002, Kreviazuk recorded <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Another_Small_Adventure" title="Another Small Adventure">Another Small Adventure</a> which was played in <i><a href="/wiki/Stuart_Little_2" title="Stuart Little 2">Stuart Little 2</a></i> and was also on the soundtrack.</p>
<p><i>In this Life</i> was featured in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Saved!" title="Saved!">Saved!</a></i> and also in a trailer for the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Door_in_the_Floor" title="The Door in the Floor">The Door in the Floor</a></i> starring Jeff Bridges, as well as in an episode of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=CW_Television_Network%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="CW Television Network's (page does not exist)">CW Television Network's</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Smallville" title="Smallville">Smallville</a></i>. In May 2003 was the soundtrack of the "Episode 20" from "<a href="/wiki/Everwood" title="Everwood">Everwood</a>" in WB. Kreviazuk's "Time" was played in the credits of the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Uptown_Girls" title="Uptown Girls">Uptown Girls</a></i>, and featured in an episode of the <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> reality show, <i><a href="/wiki/Laguna_Beach:_The_Real_Orange_County" title="Laguna Beach: The Real Orange County">Laguna Beach: The Real Orange County</a></i>, the pilot episode of the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romantic_comedy" title="Romantic comedy">romantic comedy</a>/drama <i><a href="/wiki/Men_in_Trees" title="Men in Trees">Men in Trees</a></i>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Columbia_Broadcasting_System" title="Columbia Broadcasting System">CBS</a> drama <i><a href="/wiki/Joan_of_Arcadia" title="Joan of Arcadia">Joan of Arcadia</a></i>, and the ABC family movie <i><a href="/wiki/Lucky_7_(film)" title="Lucky 7 (film)">Lucky 7</a></i>. Her song "This Year" was featured on the <i><a href="/wiki/Serendipity_(film)" title="Serendipity (film)">Serendipity</a></i> soundtrack. In 2005, two new songs written and performed by Kreviazuk were also featured on the soundtrack of the movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sisterhood_of_the_Traveling_Pants_(film)" title="The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants (film)">The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants</a></i>. In 2006, "It's All About a Kiss" played during the film <i><a href="/wiki/Just_My_Luck_(2006_film)" title="Just My Luck (2006 film)">Just My Luck</a></i>. The song "Weight of the World" is also featured during the credits of the 2003 film <i><a href="/wiki/How_to_Lose_a_Guy_in_10_Days" title="How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days">How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days</a></i> and in the us television series <i><a href="/wiki/Wildfire" title="Wildfire">Wildfire</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Songwriting_for_others">Songwriting for others</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chantal_Kreviazuk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Songwriting for others">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Chantal_Kreviazuk_production_discography" title="Chantal Kreviazuk production discography">Chantal Kreviazuk production discography</a></div>
<p>Since 2003, Kreviazuk has co-written many songs for other artists, often collaborating with her husband <a href="/wiki/Raine_Maida" title="Raine Maida">Raine Maida</a>. In 2003, Kreviazuk and Maida collaborated on a number of tracks with <a href="/wiki/Avril_Lavigne" title="Avril Lavigne">Avril Lavigne</a> for Lavigne's second album <i><a href="/wiki/Under_My_Skin_(Avril_Lavigne_album)" title="Under My Skin (Avril Lavigne album)">Under My Skin</a></i>. Kreviazuk and Maida also contributed songs to <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Clarkson" title="Kelly Clarkson">Kelly Clarkson</a>'s 2004 album <i><a href="/wiki/Breakaway_(Kelly_Clarkson_album)" title="Breakaway (Kelly Clarkson album)">Breakaway</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/The_Veronicas" title="The Veronicas">The Veronicas</a>' <i>The Secret Life of the Veronicas</i>, <a href="/wiki/Marion_Raven" title="Marion Raven">Marion Raven</a>'s 2005 and 2007 albums <i>Here I Am</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Set_Me_Free_(Marion_Raven_album)" title="Set Me Free (Marion Raven album)">Set Me Free</a></i> and recently <a href="/wiki/Cheyenne_Kimball" title="Cheyenne Kimball">Cheyenne Kimball</a>'s debut <i>Hanging On</i> in 2006. Their most recent collaboration came on the song "Permanent" for <a href="/wiki/David_Cook_(singer)" title="David Cook (singer)">David Cook</a>'s self-titled album <a href="/wiki/David_Cook_(album)" title="David Cook (album)">David Cook</a>. She also helped write a song called "Gardenia" which appears on <a href="/wiki/Mandy_Moore" title="Mandy Moore">Mandy Moore</a>'s 2007 album <a href="/wiki/Wild_Hope" title="Wild Hope">Wild Hope</a>. In 2011, she appeared on the rapper <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drake_(entertainer)" title="Drake (entertainer)">Drake</a>'s album<a href="/wiki/Take_Care_(album)" title="Take Care (album)">Take Care</a>; she wrote part of the song "Over My Dead Body" and sang the chorus. In 2012 Chantal wrote the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pitbull_(entertainer)" title="Pitbull (entertainer)">Pitbull</a> ft. <a href="/wiki/Christina_Aguilera" title="Christina Aguilera">Christina Aguilera</a> single <i><a href="/wiki/Feel_This_Moment" title="Feel This Moment">Feel This Moment</a></i> with The Messengers. She also wrote Josh Groban's single "<a href="/wiki/Brave_(Josh_Groban_song)" title="Brave (Josh Groban song)">Brave</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Josh_Groban" title="Josh Groban">Josh Groban</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thomas_%22Tawgs%22_Salter" title='Thomas "Tawgs" Salter'>Thomas "Tawgs" Salter</a>. In 2014 she wrote a song with <a href="/wiki/Shakira" title="Shakira">Shakira</a>, released on her self-titled album. The song is called "You Don't Care About Me". She also worked with Jennifer Lopez on her album "A.K.A." and the song "Emotions".</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chantal_Kreviazuk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2001, Kreviazuk made her big-screen debut as a main character in David Weaver's indie film <i>Century Hotel</i> (co-starring alongside <a href="/wiki/Sandrine_Holt" title="Sandrine Holt">Sandrine Holt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mia_Kirshner" title="Mia Kirshner">Mia Kirshner</a>). She also co-wrote the movie's theme song, "Can't Make it Good", with Maida.</p>
<p>Kreviazuk sang background vocals on a Nightmare of You song called "I Want to Be Buried in Your Backyard", which was released on their 2005 self-titled debut album. She has also contributed to Maida's solo EP, <i>Love Hope Hero</i>.</p>
<p>Kreviazuk also provided additional vocals on a <a href="/wiki/Men,_Women_%26_Children_(band)" title="Men, Women &amp; Children (band)">Men, Women &amp; Children</a> song titled "Monkey Monkee Men", from their 2006 self-titled debut album.</p>
<p>In 2007, Kreviazuk starred in <i>Pretty Broken</i>, a short film about a woman dealing with mental illness. Kreviazuk also co-produced the movie.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kreviazuk performed vocals for Chris Botti on the song "The Look of Love".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>She also writes a MySpace page, where she posts about her daily life, her thoughts on world politics, as well as updating fans with the latest on how writing/recording for her album is going.</p>
<p>On March 27, 2010, Kreviazuk headlined a concert held at <a href="/wiki/Yonge-Dundas_Square" title="Yonge-Dundas Square">Yonge-Dundas Square</a> in Toronto to celebrate <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Fund_for_Nature" title="World Wide Fund for Nature">WWF</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Earth_Hour" title="Earth Hour">Earth Hour</a>.</p>
<p>In 2010, she contributed the song "Na Miso" to the <a href="/wiki/Enough_Project" title="Enough Project">Enough Project</a> and <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Records" title="Downtown Records">Downtown Records</a>' <a class="external text" href="http://raisehopeforcongomusic.org/" rel="nofollow">Raise Hope for Congo</a> compilation with Bibiana Mpoyo. Proceeds from the compilation fund efforts to make the protection and empowerment of Congo's women a priority, as well as inspire individuals around the world to raise their voice for peace in Congo.</p>
<p>Kreviazuk also appears on Canadian singer-songwriter <a class="external text" href="http://www.ryanmacgrath.com" rel="nofollow">Ryan MacGrath</a>'s 2010 record, "Cooper Hatch Paris". She performs a duet with the artist entitled "Bird &amp; Cage".</p>
<p>In December 2014 Kreviazuk was appointed a member of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Canada" title="Order of Canada">Order of Canada</a> along with her husband <a href="/wiki/Raine_Maida" title="Raine Maida">Raine Maida</a> by <a href="/wiki/Governor_General_of_Canada" title="Governor General of Canada">Governor General</a> <a href="/wiki/David_Johnston" title="David Johnston">David Johnston</a> for their charitable and humanitarian work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chantal_Kreviazuk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kreviazuk was born in <a href="/wiki/Winnipeg" title="Winnipeg">Winnipeg</a>, Manitoba<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and attended <a href="/wiki/Balmoral_Hall_School" title="Balmoral Hall School">Balmoral Hall School</a> School for Girls. In 1994, she received multiple jaw and leg fractures in a serious motorcycle accident in Italy and spent several months recuperating.</p>
<p>Kreviazuk met <a href="/wiki/Raine_Maida" title="Raine Maida">Raine Maida</a>, the lead singer of <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_Peace" title="Our Lady Peace">Our Lady Peace</a>, at a <a href="/wiki/Pearl_Jam" title="Pearl Jam">Pearl Jam</a> concert in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> in 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> They married in December 1999,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and have three sons: Rowan Michael (born January 2004), Lucca Jon (born June 2005), and Salvador 'Sal' Daniel (born June 2008).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-third_son_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-third_son-15">[15]</a></sup> In honour of their tenth wedding anniversary, Kreviazuk and Maida renewed their wedding vows in <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a> in November 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The family lives in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> and also has a home in the USA.</p>
<p>Kreviazuk's parents are Carol and John Kreviazuk. John Kreviazuk is a successful businessman, the founder and principal owner of Krevco Pools in Winnipeg. Kreviazuk is of <a href="/wiki/Ukrainians" title="Ukrainians">Ukrainian</a> descent from her father and maternal grandfather.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Chantal is the first cousin of businessman and philanthropist Josh Kreviazuk<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> of Winnipeg, and is also the second cousin of curler and <a href="/wiki/2013_Scotties_Tournament_of_Hearts" title="2013 Scotties Tournament of Hearts">2013 Scotties Tournament of Hearts</a> champion <a href="/wiki/Alison_Kreviazuk" title="Alison Kreviazuk">Alison Kreviazuk</a>.</p>
