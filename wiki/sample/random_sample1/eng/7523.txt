<p><b>Shannon Rutherford</b> was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_character" title="Fictional character">fictional character</a> played by <a href="/wiki/Maggie_Grace" title="Maggie Grace">Maggie Grace</a> on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> drama <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_(TV_series)" title="Lost (TV series)">Lost</a></i>, which chronicled the lives of the survivors of a plane crash in the <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">South Pacific</a>. Shannon was introduced in the <a href="/wiki/Pilot_(Lost)" title="Pilot (Lost)">pilot episode</a> as the stepsister of fellow crash survivor <a href="/wiki/Boone_Carlyle" title="Boone Carlyle">Boone Carlyle</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ian_Somerhalder" title="Ian Somerhalder">Ian Somerhalder</a>). She was a series regular until her funeral in "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/What_Kate_Did_(Lost)" title="What Kate Did (Lost)">What Kate Did</a>". For most of her time on the Island, she was unhelpful and spent much of her time sunbathing. She formed a relationship with another survivor from the plane crash, <a href="/wiki/Sayid_Jarrah" title="Sayid Jarrah">Sayid Jarrah</a> (<a href="/wiki/Naveen_Andrews" title="Naveen Andrews">Naveen Andrews</a>). Shannon was accidentally shot by <a href="/wiki/Ana_Lucia_Cortez" title="Ana Lucia Cortez">Ana Lucia Cortez</a> who mistakes her for an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Others_(Lost)" title="Others (Lost)">Other</a>.</p>
<p>During the casting process, she was compared to <a href="/wiki/Paris_Hilton" title="Paris Hilton">Paris Hilton</a>. <a href="/wiki/Naveen_Andrews" title="Naveen Andrews">Naveen Andrews</a>, who played the character Sayid on the show, had the idea of encouraging the writers to write a romantic relationship between his character and Shannon into the story. Critics found her to be a largely unsympathetic character until only shortly before her death. Nonetheless, Grace received a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teen_Choice_Award" title="Teen Choice Award">Teen Choice Award</a> nomination for her role as Shannon.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Arc">Arc</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shannon_Rutherford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Arc">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Prior_to_the_crash">Prior to the crash</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shannon_Rutherford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Prior to the crash">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When Shannon is eight, her father marries Sabrina Carlyle (<a href="/wiki/Lindsay_Frost" title="Lindsay Frost">Lindsay Frost</a>), and she spends much of her adolescence with her stepbrother Boone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hearts_and_Minds_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hearts_and_Minds-1">[1]</a></sup> She studies ballet and, as she gets older, teaches dance classes to young girls. When she is eighteen, Shannon's father dies in a car crash. He did not have a will prepared, so all of his money and property goes to his wife. Sabrina refuses to help Shannon financially, despite Shannon winning a prestigious yet non-paying internship to the <a href="/wiki/Martha_Graham" title="Martha Graham">Martha Graham</a> Dance Company in New York. Unable to come up with the money herself, Shannon moves to France for a short time to work as an <a href="/wiki/Au_pair" title="Au pair">au pair</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Abandoned_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Abandoned-2">[2]</a></sup> Shannon later forms a plan to con her stepbrother, <a href="/wiki/Boone_Carlyle" title="Boone Carlyle">Boone Carlyle</a>, into giving her some of the inheritance she rightly deserved. Playing on her knowledge of Boone's love for her, she makes it appear her boyfriend Brian abuses her. Boone pays Brian to leave her, and Shannon plans to secretly take half the money. During one con in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sydney,_Australia" title="Sydney, Australia">Sydney</a>, her "abusive boyfriend" ruins Shannon's ruse after Boone cuts him a check. Boone storms off to his hotel room and plans to return to the States. When Shannon's boyfriend takes off with all of the money, Shannon goes to Boone's hotel room in Sydney, drunk, and Boone allows her to seduce him. After they have sex, Shannon tells Boone things will go back to the way they had been before.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hearts_and_Minds_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hearts_and_Minds-1">[1]</a></sup> He expresses anger because, as always, they both know she is in control of their situation/ relationship. The following morning at the Sydney airport, <a href="/wiki/Sayid_Jarrah" title="Sayid Jarrah">Sayid Jarrah</a> approaches Shannon, asking her to watch his bag for him. She deftly obliges, but soon leaves it unattended.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Exodus:_Part_1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Exodus:_Part_1-3">[3]</a></sup> Before the doomed Flight 815 takes off, Shannon rummages through her hand-luggage for her <a href="/wiki/Asthma" title="Asthma">asthma</a> inhaler, which Boone casually hands to her. She smiles, the platonic love she has for Boone briefly relieving her of her misery. Six hours into the flight, only moments before the crash, Shannon and Boone are momentarily seen as <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Pace" title="Charlie Pace">Charlie Pace</a> stumbles through their row.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Exodus:_Part_2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Exodus:_Part_2-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="After_the_crash">After the crash</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shannon_Rutherford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: After the crash">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Upon landing on the Island, she decides to join Sayid on a hike to transmit a distress signal, after she and Boone have an argument about her selfishness. When they discover a <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> looped signal already being transmitted, Shannon's knowledge of the language is used to translate it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pilot:_Part_2_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pilot:_Part_2-5">[5]</a></sup> Later, Shannon has an asthma attack, but her inhalers are nowhere to be found. <a href="/wiki/Sun-Hwa_Kwon" title="Sun-Hwa Kwon">Sun</a> (<a href="/wiki/Yunjin_Kim" title="Yunjin Kim">Yunjin Kim</a>) cures her, by using eucalyptus plants to help her breathing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Confidence_Man_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Confidence_Man-6">[6]</a></sup> Because of her selfish and <a href="/wiki/Psychological_manipulation" title="Psychological manipulation">manipulative</a> behavior, Boone brands Shannon useless. Sayid enlists her to help translate the maps belonging to the French woman who made the radio transmission, <a href="/wiki/Danielle_Rousseau" title="Danielle Rousseau">Danielle Rousseau</a> (<a href="/wiki/Mira_Furlan" title="Mira Furlan">Mira Furlan</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whatever_the_Case_May_Be_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Whatever_the_Case_May_Be-7">[7]</a></sup> Shannon and Sayid soon form a romantic relationship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.E2.80.A6In_Translation_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-.E2.80.A6In_Translation-8">[8]</a></sup> After returning from a romantic night along the beach with Sayid, she learns Boone has died after falling from a great height.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Do_No_Harm_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Do_No_Harm-9">[9]</a></sup> At his funeral, Shannon does not speak, but allows Sayid to talk instead. Shannon holds <a href="/wiki/John_Locke_(Lost)" title="John Locke (Lost)">Locke</a> (<a href="/wiki/Terry_O%27Quinn" title="Terry O'Quinn">Terry O'Quinn</a>) responsible for Boone's death, and asks Sayid to take action. When he refuses, she steals the key to the gun case from <a href="/wiki/Jack_Shephard" title="Jack Shephard">Jack Shephard</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matthew_Fox_(actor)" title="Matthew Fox (actor)">Matthew Fox</a>) and holds Locke at gunpoint in the jungle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Greater_Good_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Greater_Good-10">[10]</a></sup> When Rousseau arrives on the beach one morning to warn the camp of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Others_(Lost)" title="Others (Lost)">Others</a>' imminent arrival, Shannon prepares to migrate to the caves with the rest of the group. Before leaving on the raft the survivors have built, <a href="/wiki/Walt_Lloyd" title="Walt Lloyd">Walt Lloyd</a> (<a href="/wiki/Malcolm_David_Kelley" title="Malcolm David Kelley">Malcolm David Kelley</a>) gives Shannon guardianship of his dog, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Characters_of_Lost#Minor_Oceanic_815_crash_survivors" title="Characters of Lost">Vincent</a>, as a means to help her recover from her loss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Exodus:_Part_1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Exodus:_Part_1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the start of <a href="/wiki/Lost_(season_2)" title="Lost (season 2)">season two</a>, Shannon loses Vincent in the jungle. While searching for him, she is shocked to find Walt standing before her, dripping wet. However, when Sayid finds her, Walt disappears.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Man_of_Science.2C_Man_of_Faith_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Man_of_Science.2C_Man_of_Faith-11">[11]</a></sup> Over the next few days, Sayid builds Shannon a shelter on the beach. He brings her to it and they soon decide to sleep together for the first time. Sayid leaves to get a bottle of water for Shannon and in his absence, Shannon again sees a vision of Walt, standing in the shelter with her. Sayid brushes off her experience as a dream. She uses Vincent to try to find Walt, only for him to lead her to Boone's grave. After a while, she takes Vincent into the jungle again, with Sayid in pursuit. As it starts to rain, Sayid and Shannon admit their feelings for each other before both seeing Walt. Shannon runs after him, only to be accidentally shot by <a href="/wiki/Ana_Lucia_Cortez" title="Ana Lucia Cortez">Ana Lucia</a> (<a href="/wiki/Michelle_Rodriguez" title="Michelle Rodriguez">Michelle Rodriguez</a>), who mistakes her for an Other.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Abandoned_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Abandoned-2">[2]</a></sup> She dies moments later in Sayid's arms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Collision_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Collision-12">[12]</a></sup> Shannon's death not only left Sayid heartbroken, but also caused a rift between the survivors and the Tailies. Throughout the rest of the season, Ana Lucia is shown to be guilt-ridden over killing Shannon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Whole_Truth_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Whole_Truth-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>During season six the afterlife experienced by the survivors is shown as an alternate timeline where the plane never crashed. Boone is seen on the plane traveling alone and explains he went to save Shannon from a bad relationship, but she did not want to come with him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LA_X.2C_Part_1_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-LA_X.2C_Part_1-14">[14]</a></sup> In the series finale "<a href="/wiki/The_End_(Lost)" title="The End (Lost)">The End</a>" Boone is being attacked by someone outside a local bar, with Hurley and Sayid watching from afar. Shannon tries to defend him but is overpowered. Sayid gets out of the car and knocks the attacker unconscious. He gets down to help Shannon, and they remember their lives on the island and realize they are in the afterlife. Later that night she is reunited with the other passengers of Oceanic 815 and they all "move on" together.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_End_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_End-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality">Personality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shannon_Rutherford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the casting process, Shannon was compared to Paris Hilton,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Before_They_Were_Lost_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Before_They_Were_Lost-16">[16]</a></sup> and <i>Lost</i> producer <a href="/wiki/Damon_Lindelof" title="Damon Lindelof">Damon Lindelof</a> described her as a "bitch. She is like a typical spoiled daddy's girl who gets whatever she wants and is not responsible to the slightest bit."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PodcastNov05_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-PodcastNov05-17">[17]</a></sup> Eirik Knutzen of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canton_Repository" title="Canton Repository">The Repository</a></i> found Shannon to be "a self-centered twit,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and she was also called a "spoiled daddy's girl."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Boone describes her as a "self-centered little bitch."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hearts_and_Minds_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hearts_and_Minds-1">[1]</a></sup> On her first night on the Island, Shannon is confident they will be rescued, refusing to help the others organize the luggage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pilot:_Part_1_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pilot:_Part_1-20">[20]</a></sup> She does not care <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rose_Henderson" title="Rose Henderson">Rose</a> (<a href="/wiki/L._Scott_Caldwell" title="L. Scott Caldwell">L. Scott Caldwell</a>) has lost her husband,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Walkabout_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Walkabout-21">[21]</a></sup> and spends much of her time sunbathing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Moth_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Moth-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Solitary_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Solitary-23">[23]</a></sup> She manipulates <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Pace" title="Charlie Pace">Charlie</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominic_Monaghan" title="Dominic Monaghan">Dominic Monaghan</a>) into catching a fish for her, after which Boone apologizes on behalf of Shannon for "using this poor guy like [she] use[s] everyone else."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Walkabout_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-Walkabout-21">[21]</a></sup> In "<a href="/wiki/Whatever_the_Case_May_Be" title="Whatever the Case May Be">Whatever the Case May Be</a>," Boone says to Shannon, "Don't you see the way they look at us around here? They don't take us seriously. We're a joke. I'm trying to contribute something. You're just – you're useless."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whatever_the_Case_May_Be_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Whatever_the_Case_May_Be-7">[7]</a></sup> This motivates her to help Sayid translate maps he obtained from Rousseau.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whatever_the_Case_May_Be_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Whatever_the_Case_May_Be-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shannon_Rutherford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The producers were looking for someone who had a "Paris Hilton quality" to play Shannon, but she could not just be shallow, as the storyline would require more than that.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Before_They_Were_Lost_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-Before_They_Were_Lost-16">[16]</a></sup> A lot of women auditioned before the producers finally settled on Maggie Grace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Before_They_Were_Lost_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-Before_They_Were_Lost-16">[16]</a></sup> She was written to be a bitch in the first season as the producers needed a character they could use to create opposition and conflict.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PodcastNov05_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-PodcastNov05-17">[17]</a></sup> The producers thought it would be interesting for there to be more to Shannon and Boone's relationship than just Boone's possessive brotherly interest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DVDHearts_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-DVDHearts-24">[24]</a></sup> They came up with the idea for them to be unrelated stepsiblings, who have a romantic encounter, which became the twist for their backstory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DVDHearts_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-DVDHearts-24">[24]</a></sup> When Boone is killed at the end of season one, it forces Shannon into a more adult existence on the Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PodcastNov05_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-PodcastNov05-17">[17]</a></sup> Grace noted Boone is "the only person that ever really knew and loved Shannon, in spite of herself. Of course, losing him, especially with so much left unsaid and unresolved, will change her irrevocably."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-today_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-today-25">[25]</a></sup> This freed her up to develop her relationship with Sayid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PodcastNov05_17-3"><a href="#cite_note-PodcastNov05-17">[17]</a></sup> Naveen Andrews, who plays Sayid, came up with the idea for Shannon and Sayid relationship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DVDAbandoned_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-DVDAbandoned-26">[26]</a></sup> He thought, "What would really shock Middle-America? What if Sayid was to have a relationship with a woman that looked like <a href="/wiki/Miss_America" title="Miss America">Miss America</a>? The most unlikely pairing… to come completely from left field."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DVDAbandoned_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-DVDAbandoned-26">[26]</a></sup> This pairing made Shannon "less flighty... more of a person to be reckoned with."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DVDAbandoned_26-2"><a href="#cite_note-DVDAbandoned-26">[26]</a></sup> According to <i>Lost</i> producer <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Cuse" title="Carlton Cuse">Carlton Cuse</a>, "The relationships [the audience] expect on the show are not necessarily the ones that we [the producers] necessarily are going to give you, but we want to try to find ways in which characters that you don't expect to kind of connect to one another to connect in ways that are kind of surprising."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DVDAbandoned_26-3"><a href="#cite_note-DVDAbandoned-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shannon's flashbacks in "Abandoned" were used to allow the audience to get to a "really emotional level with the character, [to] finally understand ... why she was the way she was."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PodcastNov05_17-4"><a href="#cite_note-PodcastNov05-17">[17]</a></sup> Her death was planned before the start of season two, as the producers wanted the survivors in the tail section of the plane and the survivors of the fuselage to be forced to come together under difficult circumstances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DVDAbandoned_26-4"><a href="#cite_note-DVDAbandoned-26">[26]</a></sup> Her death was also used to "spin Sayid off in another direction."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PodcastNov05_17-5"><a href="#cite_note-PodcastNov05-17">[17]</a></sup> Damon Lindelof commented "It's sort of understood on <i>Lost</i> that that's what you sign up for. There's going to be constant character turnover, because the stakes on the Island are life-or-death."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DVDAbandoned_26-5"><a href="#cite_note-DVDAbandoned-26">[26]</a></sup> Lindelof felt an "intense, emotional sense of loss" when watching her death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PodcastNov05_17-6"><a href="#cite_note-PodcastNov05-17">[17]</a></sup> In her post-death appearance in "Collision," Maggie Grace was credited as "special guest star."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Collision_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Collision-12">[12]</a></sup> She received the same billing when she returned in <a href="/wiki/Lost_(season_3)" title="Lost (season 3)">season three</a>, appearing in <a href="/wiki/Nikki_and_Paulo" title="Nikki and Paulo">Nikki and Paulo</a>'s flashbacks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Expos.C3.A9_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Expos.C3.A9-27">[27]</a></sup> Carlton Cuse noted "it was really fun for us to actually have Boone and Shannon reemerge in the stories."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PodcastMar07_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-PodcastMar07-28">[28]</a></sup> Shannon was originally in "<a href="/wiki/LA_X" title="LA X">LA X</a>", the season premiere for the <a href="/wiki/Lost_(season_6)" title="Lost (season 6)">sixth and final season</a>, but had to be written out as Grace was unavailable.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The producers eventually arranged for Grace to appear in "The End".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shannon_Rutherford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chris Carabott from <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a> was critical of both Shannon and her relationship with Boone before the plane crash, commenting "Shannon's ability to 'sort-of' understand French and Boone's lifeguard training have had little impact and as characters they bring very little to the table. There are also too many more intriguing storylines and characters around them that deserve attention." On their relationship he added, "except for the final act, when they finally succumb to their lust for one another ... There's nothing exciting here or anything that will ultimately be built upon in the <i>Lost</i> universe."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> In a later review, for the episode following Boone's death, he commented "[Boone and Shannon] were both left with very little to do this season. Especially Shannon, whose high point of usefulness was deciphering Rousseau's documents for Sayid. Well, Maggie Grace is given an opportunity to shine and she does an acceptable job as the distraught sibling who is now looking for someone to blame for Boone's death. We finally get to see some real depth to Shannon's character and her quest for vengeance is believable."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> According to Melanie McFarland of the <i><a href="/wiki/Seattle_Post-Intelligencer" title="Seattle Post-Intelligencer">Seattle Post-Intelligencer</a></i>, Shannon is "the least deserving of sympathy of all the previously known survivors."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Seattle_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Seattle-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>C. K. Sample, III, from <a href="/wiki/AOL" title="AOL">AOL</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_Squad" title="TV Squad">TV Squad</a>, joked, "Boone's mother is the devil. How else to make us sympathize with Shannon right before killing her off of the show."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Virginia Rohan of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Seattle_Times" title="The Seattle Times">The Seattle Times</a></i> thought the "death of troubled Shannon, just as she was becoming more likable and had found love with Sayid, was sadder than the first-season death of her stepbrother, Boone. Still, it would grieve me far more to lose Jack, Kate, Sawyer, Locke, Sun or Jin."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Maureen Ryan of the <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Tribune" title="Chicago Tribune">Chicago Tribune</a></i> felt fans echoed this view, describing their reaction as "muted," as they were more angry at Ana Lucia for shooting Shannon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Maggie Grace co-won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Awards_2005" title="Screen Actors Guild Awards 2005">2005</a> <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award" title="Screen Actors Guild Award">Screen Actors Guild Award</a> for "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award_for_Best_Ensemble_-_Drama_Series" title="Screen Actors Guild Award for Best Ensemble - Drama Series">Best Ensemble - Drama Series</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> She was also nominated for the Teen Choice Award for "Choice TV Breakout Performance - Female," but lost out to <i><a href="/wiki/Desperate_Housewives" title="Desperate Housewives">Desperate Housewives</a></i>' <a href="/wiki/Eva_Longoria" title="Eva Longoria">Eva Longoria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
