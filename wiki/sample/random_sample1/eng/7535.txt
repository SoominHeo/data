<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">West–Eastern Divan Orchestra</h1>

<p>The <b>West–Eastern Divan Orchestra</b> is a youth orchestra based in <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a>, Spain, consisting of musicians from countries in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, of <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egyptian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iranian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israeli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordanian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palestinian_territories" title="Palestinian territories">Palestinian</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Syrian" title="Syrian">Syrian</a> and Spanish background.</p>
<p>It was founded in 1999 by the conductor <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Barenboim" title="Daniel Barenboim">Daniel Barenboim</a> and academic <a href="/wiki/Edward_Said" title="Edward Said">Edward Said</a>, and named after <a href="/wiki/West%E2%80%93%C3%B6stlicher_Divan" title="West–östlicher Divan">an anthology of poems</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goethe" title="Goethe">Goethe</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West%E2%80%93Eastern_Divan_Orchestra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Argentine-Israeli conductor <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Barenboim" title="Daniel Barenboim">Daniel Barenboim</a> and the late Palestinian-American academic <a href="/wiki/Edward_Said" title="Edward Said">Edward Said</a> founded the orchestra in 1999, and named the ensemble after an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West-%C3%B6stlicher_Diwan" title="West-östlicher Diwan">anthology of poems</a> by <a href="/wiki/Johann_Wolfgang_von_Goethe" title="Johann Wolfgang von Goethe">Johann Wolfgang von Goethe</a>. The first orchestra workshop was in <a href="/wiki/Weimar" title="Weimar">Weimar</a>, Germany, in 1999,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tommasini_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tommasini-1">[1]</a></sup> after the organisation had received over 200 applications from Arab music students.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Barenboim_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Barenboim-2">[2]</a></sup> Barenboim has also expressed interest in musicians from <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> (a non-Arab country but in conflict with Israel) and allocating three chairs for Iranian musicians to play in the orchestra each year.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Performances">Performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West%E2%80%93Eastern_Divan_Orchestra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The orchestra under Barenboim, in the presence of Italian President and Mrs. <a href="/wiki/Giorgio_Napolitano" title="Giorgio Napolitano">Giorgio Napolitano</a>, performed for <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_XVI" title="Pope Benedict XVI">Pope Benedict XVI</a> at the courtyard of the <a href="/wiki/Apostolic_Palace" title="Apostolic Palace">Apostolic Palace</a> of <a href="/wiki/Castel_Gandolfo" title="Castel Gandolfo">Castel Gandolfo</a> 11 July 2012, the <a href="/wiki/Abbot" title="Abbot">abbot</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Benedict_of_Nursia" title="Saint Benedict of Nursia">Saint Benedict of Nursia</a>'s (the founder of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benedictines" title="Benedictines">Benedictines</a>) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feast_day" title="Feast day">feast day</a>, and thus the name day of the Pope.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kerr_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kerr-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The West–Eastern Divan participated in the 9th <a href="/wiki/Gwangju_Biennale" title="Gwangju Biennale">Gwangju Biennale</a> in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gwangju_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gwangju-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The orchestra, conducted by Barenboim, performed the complete Beethoven symphonic cycle at <a href="/wiki/The_Proms" title="The Proms">The Proms</a> in July 2012 – the first time all nine symphonies were performed under a single conductor in a single Prom season since <a href="/wiki/Henry_Wood" title="Henry Wood">Henry Wood</a> did so in 1942.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Comer_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Comer-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aims">Aims</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West%E2%80%93Eastern_Divan_Orchestra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Aims">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The aim of the West–Eastern Divan Orchestra is to promote understanding between Israelis and Palestinians and pave the way for a peaceful and fair solution of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arab-Israeli_conflict" title="Arab-Israeli conflict">Arab-Israeli conflict</a>. Barenboim himself has spoken of the ensemble as follows:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"The Divan is not a love story, and it is not a peace story. It has very flatteringly been described as a project for peace. It isn't. It's not going to bring peace, whether you play well or not so well. The Divan was conceived as a project against ignorance. A project against the fact that it is absolutely essential for people to get to know the other, to understand what the other thinks and feels, without necessarily agreeing with it. I'm not trying to convert the Arab members of the Divan to the Israeli point of view, and [I'm] not trying to convince the Israelis to the Arab point of view. But I want to – and unfortunately I am alone in this now that Edward died a few years ago – ...create a platform where the two sides can disagree and not resort to knives."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vulliamy2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vulliamy2-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>One of the young musicians of the orchestra reinforced this point:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"Barenboim is always saying his project is not political. But one of the really great things is that this is a political statement by both sides. It is more important not for people like myself, but for people to see that it is possible to sit down with Arab people and play. The orchestra is a human laboratory that can express to the whole world how to cope with the other."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vulliamy1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vulliamy1-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Andalucia">Andalucia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West%E2%80%93Eastern_Divan_Orchestra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Andalucia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The orchestra has performed around the world. It has an annual summer school in <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a>. Since 2002, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Junta_de_Andaluc%C3%ADa" title="Junta de Andalucía">Junta de Andalucía</a> (Regional Government of Andalusia) and a private foundation have provided a base for the ensemble in Seville, Spain. Young musicians from Spain now also take part in the orchestra.</p>
<p>The West–Eastern Divan Workshop takes place during several weeks each summer in Andalucia. Once the working period is over, the concert tour of the West–Eastern Divan Orchestra starts. The orchestra has been awarded several prizes since its creation, among them the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pr%C3%ADncipe_de_Asturias_Prize" title="Príncipe de Asturias Prize">Príncipe de Asturias</a> concord award in 2002 for Said and Barenboim, and the Premium Imperiale awarded by the Japan Arts Association.</p>
<p>In 2004, the Barenboim–Said Foundation, based in Seville and financed by the Junta de Andalucía was established with the purpose of developing several education through music projects based on the principles of coexistence and dialogue promoted by Said and Barenboim. In addition to managing the orchestra, the Barenboim–Said Foundation assists with other projects such as the Academy of Orchestral Studies, the Musical Education in Palestine project and the Early Childhood Musical Education Project in Seville.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West%E2%80%93Eastern_Divan_Orchestra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A film by Paul Smaczny about the orchestra, <i>Knowledge is the Beginning</i>, won the Emmy Award for best documentary related to arts of 2006. In 2007, the orchestra received the <a href="/wiki/Praemium_Imperiale" title="Praemium Imperiale">Praemium Imperiale Grant for Young Artists</a>. It has recorded for the Teldec label.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ashley_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ashley-8">[8]</a></sup> The current concertmaster is Michael Barenboim, the son of Daniel Barenboim.</p>
