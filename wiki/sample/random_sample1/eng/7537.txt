<p><i><b>Sudden Attack</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Korean</a>: 서든어택) is a <a href="/wiki/Free-to-play" title="Free-to-play">free-to-play</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Multiplayer" title="Multiplayer">multiplayer</a> <a href="/wiki/First-person_shooter" title="First-person shooter">first-person shooter</a> online game developed by the South Korean company GameHi (currently be reformed into NEXON GT) and taken over by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nexon_Co._Ltd." title="Nexon Co. Ltd.">Nexon</a>. On December 2, 2013, Nexon America C.E.O. Min Kim announced plans to discontinue Sudden Attack North America service. Nexon America terminated <i>Sudden Attack</i> on January 29, 2014.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sudden_Attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are two teams in the game, red and blue. The red team is the Tanzirilo Independence Force, and the blue team is the United Great Force. There are various types of game play matches in different maps. Deathmatch, Destruction, capture the flag, or custom modes (sniper only, pistol only, or knife only). Both teams must satisfy certain conditions, such as defusing a bomb, successful planting and detonation, or achieving a certain amount of kills.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>There are two types of currencies: Cash and Points. Cash is based on real money and can be charged in exchange for a monetary payment, cell phone billing, or home phone billing and by bank transfer. Points can be earned from playing games. Points and cash can be spent at the in-game shop. Points are used for buying weapons. Cash enables the purchase of in-game boosts (i.e. +50% points) and special bonus items, along with special team-specific outfits. Cash and points can also be earned through participation in promotions.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sudden_Attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On September 30, 2011, <i>Sudden Attack'</i>s <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwanese</a> publisher, Gamon, announced it would not renew its contract with <a href="/wiki/Nexon" title="Nexon">Nexon</a> to operate the game in <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>. A Few months later, <a href="/wiki/Gamania" title="Gamania">Gamania</a>, which had published many of <a href="/wiki/Nexon" title="Nexon">Nexon</a>'s games including <i><a href="/wiki/Counter-Strike_Online" title="Counter-Strike Online">Counter-Strike Online</a></i>, obtained the rights. The game was renamed "<i>S.A. STORM</i>" (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese" title="Traditional Chinese">traditional Chinese</a>：<span lang="zh-hant" xml:lang="zh-hant">突擊風暴</span>). The game's closed beta started on February 22, 2012 and became fully operational on April 18, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
