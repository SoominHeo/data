<p><b>Samantha Mathis</b> (born May 12, 1970) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a>. The daughter of actress <a href="/wiki/Bibi_Besch" title="Bibi Besch">Bibi Besch</a>, Mathis made her film debut in <i><a href="/wiki/Pump_Up_the_Volume_(film)" title="Pump Up the Volume (film)">Pump Up the Volume</a></i> (1990), and later co-starred or appeared in such films as <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Women_(1994_film)" title="Little Women (1994 film)">Little Women</a></i> (1994), <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_American_President_(film)" title="The American President (film)">The American President</a></i> (1995), <i><a href="/wiki/Broken_Arrow_(1996_film)" title="Broken Arrow (1996 film)">Broken Arrow</a></i> (1996), <i><a href="/wiki/American_Psycho_(film)" title="American Psycho (film)">American Psycho</a></i> (2000), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Punisher_(2004_film)" title="The Punisher (2004 film)">The Punisher</a></i> (2004), and <i><a href="/wiki/Atlas_Shrugged:_Part_II" title="Atlas Shrugged: Part II">Atlas Shrugged: Part II</a></i> (2012).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samantha_Mathis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mathis was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York" title="Brooklyn, New York">Brooklyn, New York</a>, the daughter of Donald Mathis and Austrian-born actress <a href="/wiki/Bibi_Besch" title="Bibi Besch">Bibi Besch</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bikini_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bikini-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Her parents divorced when she was two years old, and Mathis was raised by her mother. She relocated with her mother to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles</a> at the age of five.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Detour_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Detour-3">[3]</a></sup> Mathis's mother discouraged her from pursuing acting, but growing up on locations, in theaters, and in acting classes, Mathis knew she wanted to act.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bikini_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bikini-1">[1]</a></sup> She decided to become an actress at the age of twelve.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes1990_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes1990-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samantha_Mathis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mathis began acting professionally at the age of 16.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Seventeen_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Seventeen-5">[5]</a></sup> Her first job was a commercial for "Always Slender Pads – Just For Teens."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bikini_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Bikini-1">[1]</a></sup> She costarred in the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Aaron%27s_Way" title="Aaron's Way">Aaron's Way</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Knightwatch" title="Knightwatch">Knightwatch</a></i> from 1988 to 1989. Her first starring role in a <a href="/wiki/Feature_film" title="Feature film">feature film</a> was that of Nora in <i><a href="/wiki/Pump_Up_the_Volume_(film)" title="Pump Up the Volume (film)">Pump Up the Volume</a></i> (1990), opposite <a href="/wiki/Christian_Slater" title="Christian Slater">Christian Slater</a>, whom she briefly dated at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Detour_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Detour-3">[3]</a></sup> Mathis dyed her natural blonde hair black for the role in an effort to change her image from sweet and innocent to strong-willed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes1990_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes1990-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mathis appeared in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_movie" title="Television movie">television movies</a> <i>Extreme Close-up,</i> <i>83 Hours 'til Dawn,</i> and <i>To My Daughter</i> in 1990. Mathis and Slater had voice roles in the animated film <i><a href="/wiki/FernGully:_The_Last_Rainforest" title="FernGully: The Last Rainforest">FernGully: The Last Rainforest</a></i> (1992). She next appeared in the comedy <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_My_Life_(1992_film)" title="This Is My Life (1992 film)">This Is My Life</a></i> (1992), written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Nora_Ephron" title="Nora Ephron">Nora Ephron</a>, playing an insecure teenager.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Seventeen_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Seventeen-5">[5]</a></sup> Mathis appeared in the play <i><a href="/wiki/Fortinbras_(play)" title="Fortinbras (play)">Fortinbras</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> in October 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Mario_Bros._(film)" title="Super Mario Bros. (film)">Super Mario Bros.</a></i> (1993), in which she played <a href="/wiki/Princess_Daisy" title="Princess Daisy">Princess Daisy</a> from the popular <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">Nintendo</a> video game, was a <a href="/wiki/Box_office_bomb" title="Box office bomb">box-office bomb</a>.</p>
<p>Mathis met actor <a href="/wiki/River_Phoenix" title="River Phoenix">River Phoenix</a> on the set of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Thing_Called_Love" title="The Thing Called Love">The Thing Called Love</a></i> (1993) and the two started a relationship. She was with Phoenix on October 31, 1993, the night he died at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cedars-Sinai_Hospital" title="Cedars-Sinai Hospital">Cedars-Sinai Hospital</a> of a <a href="/wiki/Drug_overdose" title="Drug overdose">drug overdose</a> after collapsing outside <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_Hollywood" title="West Hollywood">West Hollywood's</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viper_Room" title="Viper Room">Viper Room</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OK.21_Weekly_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-OK.21_Weekly-7">[7]</a></sup> She made the film <i><a href="/wiki/Jack_and_Sarah" title="Jack and Sarah">Jack and Sarah</a></i> (1995), which was shot in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, in order to get out of the country after his death because of the plentiful press coverage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Detour_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Detour-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mathis appeared in the 1994 film adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Women_(1994_film)" title="Little Women (1994 film)">Little Women</a></i>, and in <i><a href="/wiki/How_to_Make_an_American_Quilt" title="How to Make an American Quilt">How to Make an American Quilt</a></i> (1995), both starring <a href="/wiki/Winona_Ryder" title="Winona Ryder">Winona Ryder</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> She then appeared in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_American_President_(film)" title="The American President (film)">The American President</a></i> (1995), playing the assistant to the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a>. Mathis costarred with Christian Slater again, along with <a href="/wiki/John_Travolta" title="John Travolta">John Travolta</a>, in <a href="/wiki/John_Woo" title="John Woo">John Woo</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Broken_Arrow_(1996_film)" title="Broken Arrow (1996 film)">Broken Arrow</a></i> (1996). She took a little over a year off from acting after her mother died in 1996 from breast cancer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mathis later appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/American_Psycho_(film)" title="American Psycho (film)">American Psycho</a></i> (2000), a film adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Bret_Easton_Ellis" title="Bret Easton Ellis">Bret Easton Ellis</a>'s 1991 novel <a href="/wiki/American_Psycho" title="American Psycho">of the same name</a>. She starred in <i>Attraction</i> (2000), and in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Simian_Line" title="The Simian Line">The Simian Line</a></i> (2001). She starred in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turner_Network_Television" title="Turner Network Television">TNT</a> television <a href="/wiki/Miniseries" title="Miniseries">miniseries</a> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Mists_of_Avalon_(TV_miniseries)" title="The Mists of Avalon (TV miniseries)">The Mists of Avalon</a></i> (2001). Mathis starred with <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Jane" title="Thomas Jane">Thomas Jane</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Punisher_(2004_film)" title="The Punisher (2004 film)">The Punisher</a></i> (2004). She had a guest role on the ABC television show <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_(TV_series)" title="Lost (TV series)">Lost</a></i> as Olivia Goodspeed. Her indie film <i>Lebanon, PA</i> had its world premiere at the 2010 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SXSW" title="SXSW">SXSW</a> Film Festival. She appeared in an <a href="/wiki/Off-Broadway" title="Off-Broadway">off-Broadway</a> production of the play, <i><a href="/wiki/Love,_Loss,_and_What_I_Wore" title="Love, Loss, and What I Wore">Love, Loss, and What I Wore</a></i>, at <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Westside_Theatre" title="Westside Theatre">Westside Theatre</a> in October 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, Mathis played psychiatrist Alice Calvert on the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Under_the_Dome_(TV_series)" title="Under the Dome (TV series)">Under the Dome</a></i>, based on a novel by <a href="/wiki/Stephen_King" title="Stephen King">Stephen King</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> She joined the cast of the <a href="/wiki/FX_(TV_channel)" title="FX (TV channel)">FX</a> horror drama series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Strain_(TV_series)" title="The Strain (TV series)">The Strain</a></i> in 2014, playing Justine Feraldo, a <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> councilwoman for <a href="/wiki/Staten_Island" title="Staten Island">Staten Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samantha_Mathis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mathis was the last girlfriend of actor <a href="/wiki/River_Phoenix" title="River Phoenix">River Phoenix</a> and was with Phoenix the night he died as a result of a drug overdose while attending <a href="/wiki/The_Viper_Room" title="The Viper Room">The Viper Room</a> in Los Angeles, California. In the autopsy report the Los Angeles Police detailed that Mathis refused several times to give more details surrounding the death of Phoenix and had told deputies at the time of Phoenix's death that she had no knowledge of his drug use.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_nominations">Awards and nominations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samantha_Mathis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards and nominations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mathis was nominated in 1995 for a Young Artist Award at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Young_Artist_Awards" title="Young Artist Awards">Young Artist Awards</a> for Best Young Actress Starring in a Motion Picture for <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_My_Life_(1992_film)" title="This Is My Life (1992 film)">This Is My Life</a></i> (1992) and in 2005 for a <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Award" title="Saturn Award">Saturn Award</a> by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_of_Science_Fiction,_Fantasy_%26_Horror_Films" title="Academy of Science Fiction, Fantasy &amp; Horror Films">Academy of Science Fiction, Fantasy &amp; Horror Films</a> for Best Supporting Actress on Television for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salem%27s_Lot_(2004_TV_miniseries)" title="Salem's Lot (2004 TV miniseries)">Salem's Lot</a></i> (2004) (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_movie" title="Television movie">TV</a>).</p>
