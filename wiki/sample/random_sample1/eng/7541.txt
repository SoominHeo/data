<p><b>Samantha Karen Fox</b> (born 15 April 1966) is an English <a href="/wiki/Dance-pop" title="Dance-pop">dance-pop</a> singer, songwriter, actress, and former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glamour_model" title="Glamour model">glamour model</a>. In 1983, at age 16, she began appearing as a topless model on <a href="/wiki/Page_3" title="Page 3">Page 3</a> of British tabloid <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sun_(United_Kingdom)" title="The Sun (United Kingdom)">The Sun</a></i>, and continued as a Page 3 girl until 1986. During this time, she became the most popular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pin-up_girl" title="Pin-up girl">pin-up girl</a> of her era, as well as one of the most photographed British women of the 1980s. She later came out as a <a href="/wiki/Lesbian" title="Lesbian">lesbian</a>.</p>
<p>In 1986, she launched her pop-music career with her debut single "<a href="/wiki/Touch_Me_(I_Want_Your_Body)" title="Touch Me (I Want Your Body)">Touch Me (I Want Your Body)</a>", which hit Number 1 in 17 countries. In 1988, Fox received a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brit_Award" title="Brit Award">Brit Award</a> nomination for Best British Female Artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> She has also appeared in a number of films and reality television shows, and has occasionally worked as a television presenter.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samantha_Fox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fox was born in <a href="/wiki/Mile_End" title="Mile End">Mile End</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_London" title="East London">East London</a>, the eldest daughter of actress Carole Ann Wilken and John Patrick Fox. She has a younger sister, Vanessa, and two half-siblings from her father's second marriage, Frederica and Frankie.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Fox comes from a family of market traders.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samantha_Fox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fox attended <a href="/wiki/St_Thomas_More_Catholic_School,_Wood_Green" title="St Thomas More Catholic School, Wood Green">St Thomas More Catholic School, Wood Green</a> and took an interest in the theatre from an early age. She first appeared on a theatre stage at age 3, and was enrolled in the <a href="/wiki/Anna_Scher_Theatre" title="Anna Scher Theatre">Anna Scher Theatre</a> School from age 15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BioOnBio_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-BioOnBio-4">[4]</a></sup> The next year she got her first record deal,with Lamborghini Records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1983, when Fox was 16, her mother submitted several photographs that she had taken of her daughter in <a href="/wiki/Lingerie" title="Lingerie">lingerie</a> to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sunday_People" title="The Sunday People">The Sunday People</a></i> newspaper's Girl of the Year amateur modelling contest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BioOnBio_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-BioOnBio-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OrangeCoast_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-OrangeCoast-6">[6]</a></sup> She came in second out of 20,000 entrants<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ericson2012_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ericson2012-7">[7]</a></sup> and the photographs drew her to the attention of the newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sun_(United_Kingdom)" title="The Sun (United Kingdom)">The Sun</a></i>, which invited her to pose for Page 3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BioOnBio_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-BioOnBio-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OrangeCoast_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-OrangeCoast-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her parents gave their consent for her to pose topless,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ericson2012_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ericson2012-7">[7]</a></sup> and her first Page 3 photograph appeared in the <i>Sun</i> on 22 February 1983.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> She signed a four-year Page 3 modelling contract with the <i>Sun</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ericson2012_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ericson2012-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and won its "Page 3 Girl of the Year" award for three consecutive years between 1984 and 1986.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> She is recognized today as the most popular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pin-up_girl" title="Pin-up girl">pin-up girl</a> of her era, as well as one of the most-photographed British women of the 1980s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1986, Fox retired from Page 3 modelling, at the age of 20, and transitioned into a career in pop music. In 1995, aged 29, she made a one-off appearance in <i>The Sun</i> to promote Page 3's 25th anniversary. After receiving an overwhelmingly positive reader response, she appeared in the slot every day of that week, with Friday's final topless picture given away as an <a href="/wiki/Paper_size" title="Paper size">A3</a>-sized poster. The following year she appeared in the October issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Playboy" title="Playboy">Playboy</a></i> magazine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2008, Fox was voted the top Page 3 girl of all time.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In December 2009, her latest compilation was issued, <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits_(2009_Samantha_Fox_album)" title="Greatest Hits (2009 Samantha Fox album)">Greatest Hits</a></i>, both in single CD and double CD formats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In 2012 her first 4 albums were-re-issued as double deluxe CDs by Cherry Red.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_and_television">Film and television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samantha_Fox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film and television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the late 1980s, she appeared in television adverts for <a href="/wiki/Leicestershire" title="Leicestershire">Leicestershire</a>-based car dealership network with the slogan "Follow the Fox to Swithland Motors". Around the same time, she also appeared in television adverts for bingo in <i>The Sun</i> newspaper.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1989, she co-presented the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BRIT_Awards" title="BRIT Awards">BRIT Awards</a> with <a href="/wiki/Mick_Fleetwood" title="Mick Fleetwood">Mick Fleetwood</a>, which became notorious for turning into a shambles; Fox has asserted in interviews since that the <a href="/wiki/Autocue" title="Autocue">autocue</a> did not work properly that night. She spent a year in New York presenting pop promo videos for MTV, and she made other attempts at TV presenting, including an interview with <a href="/wiki/Rolf_Harris" title="Rolf Harris">Rolf Harris</a>, which was ill-fated even before it started as Fox referred to her interviewee as "Ralph" on several occasions, annoying Harris.</p>
<p>In 1990 she appeared on the sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Charles_in_Charge" title="Charles in Charge">Charles in Charge</a></i> as Samantha Steele, a fictional rock star whose agent pushes her to romance Charles (<a href="/wiki/Scott_Baio" title="Scott Baio">Scott Baio</a>) so the paparazzi will print it in the tabloids. She featured in the <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> programme <i>An Audience with... <a href="/wiki/Ken_Dodd" title="Ken Dodd">Ken Dodd</a></i> (1994). She also featured in 2 movies: <i>It's Been Real</i>, written and directed by Steve Varnom and starring <a href="/wiki/John_Altman_(actor)" title="John Altman (actor)">John Altman</a>; and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Match_(film)" title="The Match (film)">The Match</a></i>, written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Davis_(director)" title="Michael Davis (director)">Mick Davis</a> and starring <a href="/wiki/Pierce_Brosnan" title="Pierce Brosnan">Pierce Brosnan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ian_Holm" title="Ian Holm">Ian Holm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Sizemore" title="Tom Sizemore">Tom Sizemore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neil_Morrissey" title="Neil Morrissey">Neil Morrissey</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Hayman" title="David Hayman">David Hayman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Isla_Blair" title="Isla Blair">Isla Blair</a>.</p>
<p>In 1995, under the group name 'Sox', Fox took part in <i><a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest_1995" title="United Kingdom in the Eurovision Song Contest 1995">A Song For Europe</a></i>, the UK heat for the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Eurovision Song Contest">Eurovision Song Contest 1995</a>. Her song <i>Go For The Heart</i> finished 4th of the 8 shortlisted entries with 65,436 telephone votes and the single went on to reach no.47 in the UK Singles Chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2008, Fox and her partner Myra took part in <i><a href="/wiki/Wife_Swap_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Wife Swap (U.S. TV series)">Celebrity Wife Swap</a></i>, exchanging with <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Starr" title="Freddie Starr">Freddie Starr</a> and his wife Donna. In November 2009, she took part in ITV's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I%27m_a_Celebrity%E2%80%A6Get_Me_out_of_Here!_(UK_series_9)" title="I'm a Celebrityâ¦Get Me out of Here! (UK series 9)">I'm a Celebrityâ¦Get Me Out of Here</a></i>; she was voted out on Day 16. In July 2010, she appeared in a celebrity episode of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Come_Dine_With_Me" title="Come Dine With Me">Come Dine With Me</a> with <a href="/wiki/Calum_Best" title="Calum Best">Calum Best</a>, <a href="/wiki/Janice_Dickinson" title="Janice Dickinson">Janice Dickinson</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Brazier" title="Jeff Brazier">Jeff Brazier</a>. In July 2016 Fox became a participant in <i><a href="/wiki/Celebrity_Big_Brother_18" title="Celebrity Big Brother 18">Celebrity Big Brother 18</a></i> where she just missed out on reaching the final and finished in 7th place.</p>

<p>Fox's father Patrick, a former carpenter, managed her career until 1991, when she hired accountants to trace over Â£1Â million (Â£1.9 million today) that she believed he had embezzled from her accounts. She sued her father, who by then had divorced and remarried, and in May 1995 she was awarded a Â£363,000 (Â£0.6 million today) court settlement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Patrick Fox died in 2000, at which time they had not spoken for almost a decade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1994, it was reported that Fox had become a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Born_again_(Christianity)" title="Born again (Christianity)">born again Christian</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> that year she played at the Christian arts festival <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenbelt_festival" title="Greenbelt festival">Greenbelt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the late 1980s Fox became romantically linked with Australian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Con_man" title="Con man">con man</a> <a href="/wiki/Peter_Foster" title="Peter Foster">Peter Foster</a>. They began dating but she turned down his marriage proposal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> She also had a relationship with <a href="/wiki/Paul_Stanley" title="Paul Stanley">Paul Stanley</a>, guitarist and singer of the rock band <a href="/wiki/Kiss_(band)" title="Kiss (band)">Kiss</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Rumours regarding Fox's <a href="/wiki/Sexual_orientation" title="Sexual orientation">sexual orientation</a> began to surface in 1999 when she judged a lesbian beauty pageant, and rumours circulated that the woman she resided withâ<a href="/wiki/Cris_Bonacci" title="Cris Bonacci">Cris Bonacci</a>, the Australian former guitarist for the rock band <a href="/wiki/Girlschool" title="Girlschool">Girlschool</a>âwas her lover.</p>
<p>Though the two women did live in the same residence together, neither stated publicly that they were lovers. The relationship was confirmed later by Bonacci in an interview.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Prior to Bonacci, Fox shared her home with Debbie Connor, but again she herself never stated that they were romantically linked, only that they were "best mates".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2003, Fox made a statement about her personal life:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> "I have slept with other women but I've not been in love before Myra Stratton. People say I'm gay....I don't know what I am. All I know is that I'm in love with Myra [Stratton, my manager]. I love her completely and want to spend the rest of my life with her." Fox said that she had been reluctant to come out because, having already dealt with obsessed fans and <a href="/wiki/Stalking" title="Stalking">stalkers</a>, she feared fans' possible reactions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian-harriet-lane-interviewed_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-harriet-lane-interviewed-27">[27]</a></sup> In August 2009, she announced her plans to form a <a href="/wiki/Civil_partnership_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Civil partnership in the United Kingdom">civil partnership</a> with Stratton.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 2 August 2015, Fox's long-term partner, Stratton, died following a two-year battle with cancer. She was 60 years old. Fox announced the news via Twitter and Facebook: "It is with great sadness we announce the passing of Myra Stratton. She was Samantha Fox's partner and manager for the past 16 years."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
