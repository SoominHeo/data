<p>The <b>Western Hemisphere</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Geography" title="Geography">geographical</a> term for the half of the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">earth</a> that lies <a href="/wiki/West" title="West">west</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Prime_meridian" title="Prime meridian">Prime meridian</a> (which crosses <a href="/wiki/Greenwich" title="Greenwich">Greenwich</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK" title="UK">UK</a>) and <a href="/wiki/East" title="East">east</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antimeridian" title="Antimeridian">antimeridian</a>, the other half being called the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Hemisphere" title="Eastern Hemisphere">Eastern Hemisphere</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In this sense, the Western Hemisphere consists of the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>, the western portions of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, the extreme eastern tip of <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, numerous territories in <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">Oceania</a>, and a portion of <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a>, while excluding some of the <a href="/wiki/Aleutian_Islands" title="Aleutian Islands">Aleutian Islands</a> to the southwest of the <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaskan</a> mainland.</p>
<p>In an effort to define the Western Hemisphere as the parts of the world which are not part of the <a href="/wiki/Old_World" title="Old World">Old World</a>, there also exist projections which use the <a href="/wiki/20th_meridian_west" title="20th meridian west">20th meridian west</a> and the diametrically opposed <a href="/wiki/160th_meridian_east" title="160th meridian east">160th meridian east</a> to define the hemisphere.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> This projection excludes the European and African mainlands and a small portion of northeast <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a>, but includes more of eastern Russia and Oceania.</p>
<p>The center of the Western Hemisphere is located in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> at the intersection of the <a href="/wiki/90th_meridian_west" title="90th meridian west">90th meridian west</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a> very close to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gal%C3%A1pagos" title="Galápagos">Galápagos</a>.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_highest_mountains" title="List of highest mountains">highest mountain</a> in the Western Hemisphere is <a href="/wiki/Aconcagua" title="Aconcagua">Aconcagua</a> in the Andes of <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> at 6,960.8 metres (22,837 ft). <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UNC-Sigma_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-UNC-Sigma-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
