<p><i><b>Servamp</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">SERVAMP -サーヴァンプ-</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">Hepburn</a>: <i>Sāvanpu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series by Strike Tanaka, serialized in <a href="/wiki/Media_Factory" title="Media Factory">Media Factory</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Djo_manga" title="Shōjo manga"><i>shōjo</i> manga</a> magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Monthly_Comic_Gene" title="Monthly Comic Gene">Monthly Comic Gene</a></i> since 2011. It has been collected into ten <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> volumes. The series is licensed in North America by <a href="/wiki/Seven_Seas_Entertainment" title="Seven Seas Entertainment">Seven Seas Entertainment</a>. A television <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> adaptation began airing on July 5, 2016 and concluded on September 20, 2016 with 12 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-anime_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-anime-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AirDateAndStaff_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-AirDateAndStaff-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Servamp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mahiru Shirota is a normal high-school student who likes something simple. One day, on his way back home, he found a black cat and decided to take care of him, naming him Kuro. However, the next day, he is surprised to find out that the black cat is in fact, one of the seven <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vampires" title="Vampires">Vampire</a> that represents <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seven_Deadly_Sins" title="Seven Deadly Sins">Seven Deadly Sins</a>, Sleepy Ash that represents Sloth and he will turn into cat when exposed to sunlight. From the moment Mahiru calls his given name in his human form and gives him a neck collar, Mahiru has formed a temporary contract with Sleepy Ash. Their contract is finalized once Sleepy Ash drinks his blood, making Sleepy Ash a vampire servant known as "Servamp" and Mahiru as his master called "Eve". Following this, Mahiru and Sleepy Ash encounters Tsubaki, a vampire who claims to be the servamp of melancholy and the unknown eighth brother of Sleepy Ash and the other servamps. Tsubaki intends to wage an all out war against the seven servamps. Mahiru decides to gather the seven servamps to fight together against Tsubaki and his group.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eves">Eves</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Servamp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Eves">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt>Mahiru Shirota <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">城田真昼</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shirota Mahiru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Terashima" title="Takuma Terashima">Takuma Terashima</a> (drama CD, anime)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cast_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-cast-3">[3]</a></sup> (Japanese); <a href="/wiki/Clifford_Chapin" title="Clifford Chapin">Clifford Chapin</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ENcast_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ENcast-4">[4]</a></sup> (English)</span></dd>
<dd>A Freshman High School student who lost his mother to an accident at a young age. Having no father to take care of him, he was then left in the care of his ever-busy uncle. Thanks to this, Mahiru grew up mostly alone and has developed many talents for household chores because of his time alone. Mahiru's talent for housework has also extended into his school life where he often uses them for class projects and the like. After discovering Sleepy Ash as a cat and eventually becoming his Eve, he obtains a Lead (essentially a conjured tool for Eves) in the form of a Broomstick. Kuro later notes in irony that it's what may represent him the most due to Mahiru's personality, and that it was the first thing that he used to hit Kuro with when they met. However, his Lead later changes form into a Spear, giving Mahiru more physical prowess. Mahiru's catchphrase is "Thinking simply, it's got to be me!"</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Misono Alicein <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">有栖院 御園</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Arisuin Misono</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Hiro_Shimono" title="Hiro Shimono">Hiro Shimono</a> (drama CD, anime)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cast_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-cast-3">[3]</a></sup> (Japanese); <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Bevins" title="Christopher Bevins">Christopher Bevins</a> (English)</span></dd>
<dd>Lily's Eve, a high school student who is in the same year as Mahiru. Misono comes from a very wealthy family and unfortunately possesses a very weak physical composition, which causes him to be easily exhausted. He has a tendency to look down on others such as when he commanded Mahiru to work for him in stopping Tsubaki, but it's possible that this may be due to lack of social skills. He is a child born from the affair between his father and a servant. The affair caused his father's wife to kill his real mother out with Doubt Doubt's help. However, she spared Misono and decided to raise him as her own son. Unfortunately, she eventually tried to kill Misono, leading Mikuni to kill her to save Misono's life. Misono was initially unaware of this and hated his brother, but eventually learns the truth. Misono's Lead is a chair which he often sits in to rest, and has so far shown creative uses for said chair in combat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TV_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-TV-5">[5]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Mikuni Alicein <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">有栖院 御国</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Arisuin Mikuni</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Tetsuya_Kakihara" title="Tetsuya Kakihara">Tetsuya Kakihara</a> (anime)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-additional_cast_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-additional_cast-6">[6]</a></sup> (Japanese); Christopher Wehkamp (English)</span></dd>
<dd>Jeje's Eve, Misono's older brother and an antique dealer. Misono bears resentment towards Mikuni for killing their mother and then took Doubt with him. It's later revealed that Mikuni and Misono are actually half-brothers, Misono born as the result of their father's affair with a servant, which led to Mikuni's mother to kill Misono's real mother out of jealousy. Mikuni accepted what happened, but was disgusted of his father as the result. Unfortunately, Doubt's power eventually took the toll on his mother and she tried to kill Misono. To protect his brother, Mikuni killed his mother and took Doubt Doubt with him since then.</dd>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Servamp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Strike Tanaka began publishing <i>Sevamp</i> in the first issue of <a href="/wiki/Media_Factory" title="Media Factory">Media Factory</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Monthly_Comic_Gene" title="Monthly Comic Gene">Monthly Comic Gene</a></i> magazine in April 2011, where it was first tentatively titled <i>Eve and Servamps</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Seven_Seas_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Seven_Seas-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The chapters from the magazine have been collected into eleven <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> that have been released by <a href="/wiki/Kadokawa_Corporation" title="Kadokawa Corporation">Kadokawa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cast_3-11"><a href="#cite_note-cast-3">[3]</a></sup> In July, 2014 <a href="/wiki/Seven_Seas_Entertainment" title="Seven Seas Entertainment">Seven Seas Entertainment</a> announced it had licensed the series for publication in North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Seven_Seas_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Seven_Seas-8">[8]</a></sup> Seven adapted English language manga volumes have been released, with more announced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SSE_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-SSE-10">[10]</a></sup> In addition to print material, the series also inspired six <a href="/wiki/Radio_drama" title="Radio drama">drama CDs</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-anime_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-anime-1">[1]</a></sup> the first of which was released with volume six of the manga in July 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Seven_Seas_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Seven_Seas-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Servamp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In addition to drama CDs the series has also produced several <a href="/wiki/Image_song" title="Image song">character song</a> CDs, as well as an <a href="/wiki/Soundtrack" title="Soundtrack">original soundtrack</a> CD. In total there have been five character pair CDs, released in 2015, as well as a character song mini-album and an original soundtrack, both to be released in October 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-character_cds_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-character_cds-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-character_mini-album_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-character_mini-album-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-original_soundtrack_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-original_soundtrack-24">[24]</a></sup> The anime series also had an opening song, "Deal With" by <a href="/wiki/Oldcodex" title="Oldcodex">Oldcodex</a>, and an ending song, "Sunlight Avenue" by <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Terashima" title="Takuma Terashima">Takuma Terashima</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deal_with_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-deal_with-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sunlight_avenue_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-sunlight_avenue-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Servamp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
