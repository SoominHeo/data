<p><b>Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg</b> (<small>Hungarian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Hungarian" title="Help:IPA for Hungarian">[ˈsɒbolt͡ʃ ˈsɒtmaːr ˈbɛrɛɡ]</a></span>) is an <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Hungary" title="Counties of Hungary">administrative county</a> (Hungarian: <i>megye</i>) in north-eastern <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>, bordering <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>. It shares borders with the Hungarian counties <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hajd%C3%BA-Bihar" title="Hajdú-Bihar">Hajdú-Bihar</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Borsod-Aba%C3%BAj-Zempl%C3%A9n" title="Borsod-Abaúj-Zemplén">Borsod-Abaúj-Zemplén</a>. The capital of Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg county is <a href="/wiki/Ny%C3%ADregyh%C3%A1za" title="Nyíregyháza">Nyíregyháza</a>.</p>
<p>Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg county was organised after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> from the previous counties <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Szatm%C3%A1r" title="Szatmár">Szatmár-Ugocsa-Bereg</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Szabolcs_(county)" title="Szabolcs (county)">Szabolcs</a>. Before 1991, it was called Szabolcs-Szatmár county.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Szabolcs-Szatm%C3%A1r-Bereg_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many settlements founded after the conquest of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hungarian_people" title="Hungarian people">Magyars</a> were largely devastated by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mongol" title="Mongol">Mongol</a> invasion in 1241. Recovery then took decades. The history of <a href="/wiki/Ny%C3%ADregyh%C3%A1za" title="Nyíregyháza">Nyíregyháza</a> provides an illuminating example of this process. The Turks hardly succeeded in invading the northern parts of the region: therefore, many buildings from Árpád's age and the Medieval Era were preserved in relatively good state. In the <a href="/wiki/Romanesque_architecture" title="Romanesque architecture">Romanesque</a> church of <a href="/wiki/Csaroda" title="Csaroda">Csaroda</a>, there are wall paintings dating back to the 13th century, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medieval" title="Medieval">Medieval</a> <a href="/wiki/Church_(building)" title="Church (building)">churches</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ny%C3%ADrb%C3%A1tor" title="Nyírbátor">Nyírbátor</a> have been almost completely preserved. In the 16th century, the spread of <a href="/wiki/Protestantism" title="Protestantism">Protestantism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Calvinism" title="Calvinism">Calvinism</a> brought about changes in town life.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Szabolcs-Szatm%C3%A1r-Bereg_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg is located in the north-eastern tip of <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>. It borders <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>, and has good connections both by <a href="/wiki/Road" title="Road">road</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rail_transport" title="Rail transport">rail</a>. Within Hungary, the county is bordered by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Borsod-Aba%C3%BAj-Zempl%C3%A9n" title="Borsod-Abaúj-Zemplén">Borsod-Abaúj-Zemplén</a> county and the River Tisza to the north west and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hajd%C3%BA-Bihar" title="Hajdú-Bihar">Hajdú-Bihar</a> county to the south west.</p>
<p>The early Hungarians transformed this region significantly by clearing large areas of forest to make way for <a href="/wiki/Pasture" title="Pasture">pastures</a> and farmland. Approximately 5 to 6 square kilometres of <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forest</a> were cleared for the construction of the <a href="/wiki/Szabolcs_(village)" title="Szabolcs (village)">Szabolcs</a> earthwork in the ninth and tenth centuries, and its ruins are still present. The area was the gateway for the Mongol invasion of Hungary in the 1240s, and suffered considerable destruction and population decrease during the raids. With the subsequent development of the country, the region became even more marginalized in the 15th century. Ongoing <a href="/wiki/Civil_war" title="Civil war">civil war</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rebellion" title="Rebellion">rebellion</a>, and <a href="/wiki/War" title="War">war</a> exacted a heavy price and further hindered the region's development.</p>
<p>The county's borders have been altered frequently over the years, its current territory being established in 1950 with the amalgamation of the counties of Szabolcs-Ung and Szatmár-Bereg-Ugocsa.</p>
<p>There are many <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forests</a>, <a href="/wiki/Field_(agriculture)" title="Field (agriculture)">fields</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pasture" title="Pasture">pastures</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meadow" title="Meadow">meadows</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Moorland" title="Moorland">moorland</a> forests in the county. The <a href="/wiki/Bog" title="Bog">bog</a> <a href="/wiki/Moss" title="Moss">moss</a> moors at <a href="/wiki/Csaroda" title="Csaroda">Csaroda</a>, the Nyíres lake, and the Bábtava lake are especially valuable, as they contain many rare species of fauna and flora.</p>
<p>Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg is Hungary's sixth biggest county with a total land area of 5,936 square kilometres. From a geographical aspect, it is possible to divide the county into two main regions: The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Upper_Tisza_Valley&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Upper Tisza Valley (page does not exist)">Upper Tisza Valley</a> and the Nyírség. <a href="/wiki/Tisza" title="Tisza">Tisza</a> is one of the most important <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">rivers</a> of the county, entering Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg — and Hungary — at <a href="/wiki/Tiszabecs" title="Tiszabecs">Tiszabecs</a>, and leaving at <a href="/wiki/Tiszadob" title="Tiszadob">Tiszadob</a>. Its segment in Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg county is 235 kilometres long, out of which 208 kilometres belongs to the Upper Tisza Valley, reaching the area of <a href="/wiki/Tokaj" title="Tokaj">Tokaj</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rakamaz" title="Rakamaz">Rakamaz</a>. The larger area named the Nyírség is derived from the word nyír meaning <a href="/wiki/Birch" title="Birch">birch</a>, as the region is dominated by birch woodlands. The northern part of Nyírség is covered with sandy forest soils, the southern areas have loose wind-blown sand. Alluvial and meadow soils are found in the Upper Tisza region.</p>
<p>The county has a <a href="/wiki/Continental_climate" title="Continental climate">continental climate</a>; it is cooler than the Great Plain because it is further north. Summers are cooler than in other parts of the Plains. Annual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">precipitation</a> is 550-600 millimetres. The higher than average number of days of sunshine make ideal conditions for the growing of <a href="/wiki/Tomato" title="Tomato">tomatoes</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sunflower" title="Sunflower">sunflower</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">tobacco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apple" title="Apple">apples</a>, and other <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">fruits</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Plum" title="Plum">plums</a>—for which the county is famous, being eaten fresh, dried into prunes (some made into <a href="/wiki/Lekvar" title="Lekvar">lekvar</a>) and fermented into well-known brandies.</p>
<p>The county has 229 settlements, of which 20 are towns. The county capital and largest city is <b><a href="/wiki/Ny%C3%ADregyh%C3%A1za" title="Nyíregyháza">Nyíregyháza</a></b> with a <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 116,900 in 2003. The other cities have relatively small populations, only those of <a href="/wiki/Kisv%C3%A1rda" title="Kisvárda">Kisvárda</a> and <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1t%C3%A9szalka" title="Mátészalka">Mátészalka</a> having around 18,000 inhabitants. The eastern part of the county is lightly populated and is dotted with small villages which often have very poor economic conditions.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="River_Tisza">River Tisza</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Szabolcs-Szatm%C3%A1r-Bereg_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: River Tisza">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Upper Tisza region has many <a href="/wiki/Stream" title="Stream">streams</a> and <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">rivers</a>, but the Nyírség region has little surface water. The most important of River Tisza's tributaries is the River <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Szamos" title="Szamos">Szamos</a>, which is also characterised by great variations in water volume. There are <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">irrigation</a> systems, a water <a href="/wiki/Barrage_(dam)" title="Barrage (dam)">barrage</a>, and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydroelectric_power_station" title="Hydroelectric power station">hydroelectric power station</a> on the Tisza at <a href="/wiki/Tiszal%C3%B6k" title="Tiszalök">Tiszalök</a>.</p>
<p>Lakes of various sizes have evolved in sandy areas such as the basin of the Sóstó (Salty lake) of <a href="/wiki/Ny%C3%ADregyh%C3%A1za" title="Nyíregyháza">Nyíregyháza</a>, whose <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alkaline" title="Alkaline">alkaline</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydrogen_carbonate" title="Hydrogen carbonate">hydrogen-carbonated</a> waters have medicinal qualities. Many water <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reservoirs" title="Reservoirs">reservoirs</a> have been built according to local demand. Thermal waters of 55-65 °C can be brought to the surface from wells as shallow as 1,000 metres. The most important thermal water reserves are in Nyíregyháza, Kisvárda, Mátészalka, and Tiszavasvári. The county's <a href="/wiki/Geothermal_energy" title="Geothermal energy">geothermal energy</a> still awaits exploitation.</p>
<p>The county has relatively few <a href="/wiki/Mineral" title="Mineral">mineral</a> reserves. Almost all of the large energy source transport systems cross the county.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ethnicity">Ethnicity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Szabolcs-Szatm%C3%A1r-Bereg_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ethnicity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Besides the Hungarian majority, the main minorities are the Roma (approx. 44,000), Germans (2,000) and Ukrainians (1,000).</p>
<p>Total population (2011 census): 559,272<br/>
Ethnic groups (2011 census):<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Identified themselves: 525 958 persons:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hungarians" title="Hungarians">Hungarians</a>: 476 256 (90,55%)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Romani_people_in_Hungary" title="Romani people in Hungary">Gypsies</a>: 44 133 (8,40%)</li>
<li>Others and indefinable: 5 569 (1,06%)</li>
</ul>
<p>Approx. 66,000 persons in Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County did not declare their ethnic group at the 2011 census.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Szabolcs-Szatm%C3%A1r-Bereg_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The county borders three countries, and it is the only Hungarian county bordering Ukraine. The railway border crossing towards <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a> is well developed; its high capacity is able to meet the requirements of transit and bilateral trade. Following the reconstruction of the <a href="/wiki/Road" title="Road">road</a> border crossing, the county is also able to cope with increased road transportation.</p>
<p>Several regions in the county have <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism</a> potential, mostly unexploited. Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg has several agricultural products of excellent quality, with capacity for higher production. There is an abundance of low-cost, semiskilled labour.</p>
<p>The county's biggest problem is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Economic_crisis" title="Economic crisis">economic crisis</a>. There is a shortage of local capital and inward <a href="/wiki/Investment" title="Investment">investment</a>, which restrains the creation of new jobs, thus the unemployment rate remains the second highest in Hungary. Manufacturing lags the rest of the country, most notably lacking high quality, high value-added products. The marginal soil quality limits the scope of agricultural production to a few products which suffer from shrinking export markets to the east.</p>
<p>It is home to the <a href="/wiki/Szakoly_Power_Plant" title="Szakoly Power Plant">Szakoly Power Plant</a>.</p>
