<p>The <b>western lesser bamboo lemur</b> (<i>Hapalemur occidentalis</i>), also known as the <b>northern bamboo lemur</b> or <b>western gentle lemur</b>, is species of <a href="/wiki/Bamboo_lemur" title="Bamboo lemur">bamboo lemur</a> <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> to <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_lesser_bamboo_lemur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The total length of this <a href="/wiki/Primate" title="Primate">primate</a> is 55–67 cm (22–26 in), more than half of which is tail, and average weight is just under 1 kilogram (2.2 lb).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mitt_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-mitt-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_lesser_bamboo_lemur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It lives in several discontinuous areas in northern and western Madagascar including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ankarana" title="Ankarana">Ankarana</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Analamerana&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Analamerana (page does not exist)">Analamerana</a> in the north, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sambirano&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sambirano (page does not exist)">Sambirano</a> and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ampasindava_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ampasindava Peninsula (page does not exist)">Ampasindava Peninsula</a> in the north-west, and various areas in the west between the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mahavany_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mahavany River (page does not exist)">Mahavany</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tsiribihina_River" title="Tsiribihina River">Tsiribihina Rivers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mitt_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-mitt-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> This lemur has been reported from eight national parks (<a href="/wiki/Ankarafantsika_National_Park" title="Ankarafantsika National Park">Ankarana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baie_de_Baly_National_Park" title="Baie de Baly National Park">Baie de Baly</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mananara_Nord_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mananara Nord National Park (page does not exist)">Mananara-Nord</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marojejy_National_Park" title="Marojejy National Park">Marojejy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Masoala_National_Park" title="Masoala National Park">Masoala</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sahamalaza_National_Park" title="Sahamalaza National Park">Sahamalaza-Iles Radama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tsingy_de_Namoroka_Strict_Nature_Reserve" title="Tsingy de Namoroka Strict Nature Reserve">Tsingy de Namoroka</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zahamena_National_Park" title="Zahamena National Park">Zahamena</a>), two strict nature reserves (<a href="/wiki/Tsaratanana_Reserve" title="Tsaratanana Reserve">Tsaratanana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zahamena_Reserve" title="Zahamena Reserve">Zahamena</a>), and eight special reserves (<a href="/wiki/Ambatovaky_Reserve" title="Ambatovaky Reserve">Ambatovaky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Analamerana_Reserve" title="Analamerana Reserve">Analamerana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anjanaharibe-Sud_Reserve" title="Anjanaharibe-Sud Reserve">Anjanaharibe-Sud</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bemarivo_Reserve" title="Bemarivo Reserve">Bemarivo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kasijy_Reserve" title="Kasijy Reserve">Kasijy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maningoza_Reserve" title="Maningoza Reserve">Maningoza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manongarivo_Reserve" title="Manongarivo Reserve">Manongarivo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Manongarivo_Reserve" title="Manongarivo Reserve">Marotandrano</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_lesser_bamboo_lemur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Preferred habitat is dry deciduous forest and humid forest which have areas of <a href="/wiki/Bamboo" title="Bamboo">bamboo</a> and bamboo vines; but they can exist in stands of bamboo surrounded by rice fields and other agricultural land. Lives in groups of six individuals and tends to be active at night. Females have a gestation period of 137 to 140 days and give birth, usually to one infant, from October through to January. Food includes fruit, <a href="/wiki/Liana" title="Liana">liana</a> flowers and bamboo. Bamboo species include <i><a href="/wiki/Dendrocalamus_giganteus" title="Dendrocalamus giganteus">Dendrocalamus giganteus</a></i>, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ochlandra_capitata&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ochlandra capitata (page does not exist)">Ochlandra capitata</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Phyllostachys_aurea" title="Phyllostachys aurea">Phyllostachys aurea</a></i>. It co-exists with other species of lemurs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Status">Status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_lesser_bamboo_lemur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This species is listed by the <a href="/wiki/CITES" title="CITES">Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species</a> of Wild Fauna and Flora (CITES) on <a href="/wiki/CITES#Appendix_I" title="CITES">Appendix 1</a> and the <a href="/wiki/International_Union_for_Conservation_of_Nature" title="International Union for Conservation of Nature">International Union for Conservation of Nature</a> (IUCN) categorise this species as <a href="/wiki/Vulnerable_species" title="Vulnerable species">vulnerable</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">[1]</a></sup> As long as there is some bamboo, the western lesser bamboo lemur is able survive in a degraded habitat. Burning the forest for livestock pasture is the major concern for this species as well as charcoal production and mining in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ankarana" title="Ankarana">Ankarana</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Makira" title="Makira">Makira</a> hunting with firearms, machetes and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slingshots" title="Slingshots">slingshots</a> occurs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">[1]</a></sup> There are eighteen individuals in European zoos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
