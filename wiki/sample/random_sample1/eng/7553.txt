<p>The <b>western sucker-footed bat</b> (<i>Myzopoda schliemanni</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Malagasy</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chiroptera" title="Chiroptera">bat</a>. Little is known about its habits, but they are assumed to be similar to those of the <a href="/wiki/Madagascar_sucker-footed_bat" title="Madagascar sucker-footed bat">Madagascar sucker-footed bat</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Garbutt_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Garbutt-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_characteristics">Physical characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_sucker-footed_bat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Physical characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The western sucker-footed bat is 92–107 cm (3.02–3.51 ft) long. It has large <a href="/wiki/Ear" title="Ear">ears</a>, and prominent suckers on its feet and thumbs. It has buff-brown upper parts, and mouse-gray underparts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Garbutt_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Garbutt-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Range_and_habitat">Range and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_sucker-footed_bat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Range and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The western sucker-footed bat has been found on three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lowland" title="Lowland">lowland</a> sites in north-western <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>. They were found in broad-leaf <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rain_forest" title="Rain forest">rain forest</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Garbutt_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Garbutt-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
