<p><b>Western Asia</b>, <b>West Asia</b>, <b>Southwestern Asia</b> or <b>Southwest Asia</b> is the westernmost subregion of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. The concept is in limited use, as it significantly overlaps with the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> (or <a href="/wiki/Near_East" title="Near East">Near East</a>), the main difference being the exclusion of <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> (which would be counted as part of <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>). The term is sometimes used for the purposes of grouping countries in <a href="/wiki/Statistics" title="Statistics">statistics</a>.</p>
<p>The total population of Western Asia is estimated at about 300 million as of 2015.</p>
<p>In an unrelated context, the term is also used in <a href="/wiki/Ancient_history" title="Ancient history">ancient history</a> and <a href="/wiki/Archaeology" title="Archaeology">archaeology</a> to divide the <a href="/wiki/Fertile_Crescent" title="Fertile Crescent">Fertile Crescent</a> into the "Asiatic" or "Western Asian" cultures as opposed to <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">ancient Egypt</a>.</p>
<p>As a <a href="/wiki/Boundaries_between_continents" title="Boundaries between continents">geographic</a> concept, "Western Asia" includes the <a href="/wiki/Levant" title="Levant">Levant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabian_peninsula" title="Arabian peninsula">Arabian peninsula</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anatolia" title="Anatolia">Anatolia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Highlands" title="Armenian Highlands">Armenian Highlands</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Caucasus" title="South Caucasus">South Caucasus</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Sinai_Peninsula" title="Sinai Peninsula">Sinai Peninsula</a> belongs to Western Asia, making <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transcontinental_country" title="Transcontinental country">transcontinental country</a>.</p>
<p>However, the term "Western Asia" is mostly used as a convenient division of <a href="/wiki/List_of_sovereign_states" title="List of sovereign states">contemporary sovereign states</a> into a manageable number of <a href="/wiki/Region" title="Region">world regions</a> for statistical purposes; it is sometimes used instead of the more geopolitical term "<a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>", for example by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Statistics_Division" title="United Nations Statistics Division">United Nations Statistics Division</a> (UNSD) statistics office or <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Canada" title="Government of Canada">Canada's government</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StatisticsCanada_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-StatisticsCanada-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The term is used pragmatically and has no "correct" or generally agreed-upon definition. The government of Canada and also, for example, the <a href="/wiki/National_Geographic_Society" title="National Geographic Society">National Geographic</a> Style Manual only includes the nations of Turkey, Iran, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Georgia, Lebanon, Syria, Qatar, Kuwait, Jordan, Iraq, UAE, Saudi Arabia, Cyprus, Oman, and Yemen as part of West Asia, excluding Israel and West Bank/Gaza.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ngs_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ngs-2">[2]</a></sup> As a further example, Maddison, <i>The World Economy: Historical Statistics</i> (2003) includes Bahrain, Iran, Iraq, Israel, Jordan, Kuwait, Lebanon, Oman, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Syria, Turkey, UAE, Yemen, and West Bank/Gaza.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The United Nations Industrial Organization includes Azerbaijan, Iran, Armenia, Oman, Yemen, Lebanon, Syria, Israel, Iraq, United Arab Emirates, Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait, Saudi Arabia, and Jordan when listing the nations of the West Asia region, and leaves Turkey and Georgia out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The UNSD leaves out Iran but includes all other commonly West Asian listed nations.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Middle_East" title="History of the Middle East">History of the Middle East</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Near_East" title="Ancient Near East">Ancient Near East</a>, <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Ottoman_Empire" title="History of the Ottoman Empire">History of the Ottoman Empire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Gulistan" title="Treaty of Gulistan">Treaty of Gulistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Turkmenchay" title="Treaty of Turkmenchay">Treaty of Turkmenchay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Partitioning_of_the_Ottoman_Empire" title="Partitioning of the Ottoman Empire">Partitioning of the Ottoman Empire</a></div>
<p>"Western Asia" was in use as a geographical term in the early 19th century, even before "<a href="/wiki/Near_East" title="Near East">Near East</a>" became current as a geopolitical concept.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In the context of the history of <a href="/wiki/Classical_antiquity" title="Classical antiquity">classical antiquity</a>, "Western Asia" could mean the part of Asia known in classical antiquity, as opposed to the reaches of "interior Asia", i.e. <a href="/wiki/Scythia" title="Scythia">Scythia</a>, and "Eastern Asia" the easternmost reaches of geographical knowledge in classical authors, i.e. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transoxania" title="Transoxania">Transoxania</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indus_valley" title="Indus valley">India</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In the 20th century, "Western Asia" was used to denote a rough geographical era in the fields of <a href="/wiki/Archaeology" title="Archaeology">archaeology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ancient_history" title="Ancient history">ancient history</a>, especially as a shorthand for "the <a href="/wiki/Fertile_Crescent" title="Fertile Crescent">Fertile Crescent</a> excluding <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Ancient Egypt</a>" for the purposes of comparing the early civilizations of Egypt and the former.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Use of the term in the context of contemporary <a href="/wiki/Geopolitics" title="Geopolitics">geopolitics</a> or <a href="/wiki/World_economy" title="World economy">world economy</a> appears to date from the 1960s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_Asia" title="Geography of Asia">Geography of Asia</a></div>
<p>Western Asia is located directly south of <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Europe" title="Eastern Europe">Eastern Europe</a>. The region is surrounded by seven major seas; the <a href="/wiki/Aegean_Sea" title="Aegean Sea">Aegean Sea</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian Sea</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Sea" title="Arabian Sea">Arabian Sea</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>.</p>
<p>To the north, the region is delimited from <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Caucasus" title="Greater Caucasus">Caucasus Mountains</a>, to the southwest, it is delimited from <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Isthmus_of_Suez" title="Isthmus of Suez">Isthmus of Suez</a>, while to the east, the region adjoins <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Dasht-e_Kavir" title="Dasht-e Kavir">Dasht-e Kavir</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dasht-e_Lut" title="Dasht-e Lut">Dasht-e Lut</a> deserts in eastern <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> naturally delimit the region somewhat from <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> itself.</p>
<p>European <a href="/wiki/Geography" title="Geography">geographers</a> historically viewed the <a href="/wiki/North_Caucasus" title="North Caucasus">North Caucasus</a> as part of Western Asia, as well as much of what is today <a href="/wiki/European_Russia" title="European Russia">European Russia</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Climate_of_Asia" title="Climate of Asia">Climate of Asia</a></div>
<p>Western Asia is primarily <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arid_climate" title="Arid climate">arid</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Semi-arid" title="Semi-arid">semi-arid</a>, and can be subject to <a href="/wiki/Drought" title="Drought">drought</a>, but it also contains vast expanses of forest and fertile valleys. The region consists of <a href="/wiki/Grassland" title="Grassland">grasslands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rangeland" title="Rangeland">rangelands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">deserts</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mountains" title="Mountains">mountains</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Water_shortage" title="Water shortage">Water shortages</a> are a problem in many parts of West Asia, with rapidly growing <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">populations</a> increasing demands for water, while <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salinization" title="Salinization">salinization</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">pollution</a> threaten water supplies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ipcc-1997_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ipcc-1997-11">[11]</a></sup> Major rivers, including the <a href="/wiki/Tigris" title="Tigris">Tigris</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euphrates_River" title="Euphrates River">Euphrates</a>, provide sources for <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">irrigation</a> water to support <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a>.</p>
<p>There are two wind phenomena in Western Asia: the <i>sharqi</i> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Shamal_(wind)" title="Shamal (wind)">shamal</a></i>. The <i>sharqi</i> (or <i>sharki</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Wind" title="Wind">wind</a> that comes from the south and southeast. It is seasonal, lasting from April to early June, and comes again between late September and November. The winds are dry and dusty, with occasional gusts up to 80 kilometers per hour (50 miles per hour) and often kick up violent sand and dust storms that can carry sand a few thousand meters high, and can close down airports for short periods of time. These winds can last for a full day at the beginning and end of the season, and for several days during the middle of the season. The <i>shamal</i> is a summer northwesterly wind blowing over Iraq and the Persian Gulf states (including Saudi Arabia and Kuwait), often strong during the day, but decreasing at night. This weather effect occurs anywhere from once to several times a year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Topography">Topography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Topography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Western Asia contains large areas of mountainous terrain. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anatolian_Plateau" title="Anatolian Plateau">Anatolian Plateau</a> is sandwiched between the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pontus_Mountains" title="Pontus Mountains">Pontus Mountains</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taurus_Mountains" title="Taurus Mountains">Taurus Mountains</a> in <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mount_Ararat" title="Mount Ararat">Mount Ararat</a> in Turkey rises to 5,137 meters. The <a href="/wiki/Zagros_Mountains" title="Zagros Mountains">Zagros Mountains</a> are located in Iran, in areas along its border with Iraq. The Central Plateau of Iran is divided into two <a href="/wiki/Drainage_basin" title="Drainage basin">drainage basins</a>. The northern basin is <a href="/wiki/Dasht-e_Kavir" title="Dasht-e Kavir">Dasht-e Kavir</a> (Great Salt Desert), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dasht-e-Lut" title="Dasht-e-Lut">Dasht-e-Lut</a> is the southern basin.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Yemen" title="Yemen">Yemen</a>, elevations exceed 3,700 meters in many areas, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Highland_(geography)" title="Highland (geography)">highland</a> areas extend north along the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a> coast and north into <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a>. A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geologic_fault" title="Geologic fault">fault</a>-zone also exists along the Red Sea, with continental <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rifting" title="Rifting">rifting</a> creating <a href="/wiki/Trough_(geology)" title="Trough (geology)">trough</a>-like <a href="/wiki/Topography" title="Topography">topography</a> with areas located well-below <a href="/wiki/Sea_level" title="Sea level">sea level</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Dead_Sea" title="Dead Sea">Dead Sea</a>, located on the border between the <a href="/wiki/West_Bank" title="West Bank">West Bank</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a>, is situated at 418 m (1371 ft) below sea level, making it the <a href="/wiki/Extremes_on_Earth" title="Extremes on Earth">lowest point</a> on the surface of the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Rub%27_al_Khali" title="Rub' al Khali">Rub' al Khali</a>, one of the world's largest sand deserts, spans the southern third of the Arabian Peninsula in Saudi Arabia, parts of Oman, the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a> and Yemen. <a href="/wiki/Jebel_Akhdar_(Oman)" title="Jebel Akhdar (Oman)">Jebel al Akhdar</a> is a small range of mountains located in northeastern Oman, bordering the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Oman" title="Gulf of Oman">Gulf of Oman</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Plate_tectonics">Plate tectonics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Plate tectonics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Three major <a href="/wiki/Plate_tectonics" title="Plate tectonics">tectonic plates</a> converge on Western Asia, including the <a href="/wiki/African_Plate" title="African Plate">African</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_Plate" title="Eurasian Plate">Eurasian</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Plate" title="Arabian Plate">Arabian</a> plates. The boundaries between the tectonic plates make up the <a href="/wiki/Azores%E2%80%93Gibraltar_Transform_Fault" title="Azores–Gibraltar Transform Fault">Azores-Gibraltar Ridge</a>, extending across <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a>, and into Iran.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beaumont-pg22_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-beaumont-pg22-15">[15]</a></sup> The Arabian Plate is moving northward into the <a href="/wiki/Anatolian_Plate" title="Anatolian Plate">Anatolian plate</a> (Turkey) at the <a href="/wiki/East_Anatolian_Fault" title="East Anatolian Fault">East Anatolian Fault</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and the boundary between the <a href="/wiki/Aegean_Sea_Plate" title="Aegean Sea Plate">Aegean</a> and Anatolian plate in eastern Turkey is also <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seismic" title="Seismic">seismically</a> active.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beaumont-pg22_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-beaumont-pg22-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Water_resources">Water resources</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Water resources">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Several major <a href="/wiki/Aquifer" title="Aquifer">aquifers</a> provide <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a> to large portions of Western Asia. In Saudi Arabia, two large aquifers of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palaeozoic" title="Palaeozoic">Palaeozoic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Triassic" title="Triassic">Triassic</a> origins are located beneath the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jabal_Tuwayq" title="Jabal Tuwayq">Jabal Tuwayq</a> mountains and areas west to the Red Sea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beaumont-86_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-beaumont-86-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Cretaceous" title="Cretaceous">Cretaceous</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eocene" title="Eocene">Eocene</a>-origin aquifers are located beneath large portions of central and eastern Saudi Arabia, including Wasia and Biyadh which contain amounts of both <a href="/wiki/Fresh_water" title="Fresh water">fresh water</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saline_water" title="Saline water">saline water</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beaumont-86_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-beaumont-86-17">[17]</a></sup> Flood or furrow irrigation, as well as sprinkler methods, are extensively used for <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">irrigation</a>, covering nearly 90,000 km² across Western Asia for agriculture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The population of Western Asia was estimated at 272 million as of 2008, projected to reach 370 million by 2030 by Maddison (2007; the estimate excludes the Caucasus and Cyprus). This corresponds to an annual growth rate of 1.4% (or a <a href="/wiki/Doubling_time" title="Doubling time">doubling time</a> of 50 years), well above the <a href="/wiki/World_population_estimates" title="World population estimates">world average</a> of 0.9% (doubling time 75 years). The population of Western Asia is estimated at about 4% of <a href="/wiki/World_population" title="World population">world population</a>, up from about 39 million at the beginning of the 20th century, or about 2% of world population at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>The most populous countries in the region are <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> and, each with around 75 million people, followed by <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a> with around 32 million people each.</p>
<p>Culturally, Western Asia is predominantly <a href="/wiki/Arab_world" title="Arab world">Arab</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greater_Persia" title="Greater Persia">Persian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Turkish</a>, and the dominating languages are correspondingly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>, each with of the order of 70 million speakers, followed by smaller communities of <a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iranian_languages" title="Iranian languages">Iranian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkic_languages" title="Turkic languages">Turkic</a> speakers, the largest of which are the <a href="/wiki/Kurds" title="Kurds">Kurds</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijanis" title="Azerbaijanis">Azerbaijanis</a>. The dominance of Arabic and Turkish is the result of the medieval <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamic_conquests" title="Islamic conquests">Arab</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turkic_expansion" title="Turkic expansion">Turkic</a> invasions which displaced the formerly dominant <a href="/wiki/Aramaic_language" title="Aramaic language">Aramaic</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hebrew" title="Hebrew">Hebrew</a> in the Levant, and <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> in Anatolia, although <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hebrew" title="Hebrew">Hebrew</a> is once again the dominant language in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aramaic" title="Aramaic">Aramaic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> both remain present in their respective territories as small minority languages.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Economy_of_Western_Asia" title="Economy of Western Asia">Economy of Western Asia</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economy</a> of Western Asia is diverse and the region experiences high economic growth. Turkey has the largest economy in the region, followed by <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a> and Iran. <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">Petroleum</a> is the major <a href="/wiki/Petroleum_industry" title="Petroleum industry">industry</a> in the regional economy, as more than half of the world's <a href="/wiki/Oil_reserves" title="Oil reserves">oil reserves</a> and around 40 percent of the world's <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_natural_gas_proven_reserves" title="List of countries by natural gas proven reserves">reserves</a> are located in the region.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/West_Asian_Tennis_Federation" title="West Asian Tennis Federation">West Asian Tennis Federation</a> regulates the championships and leagues in the region.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/West_Asian_Billiards_%26_Snooker_Federation" title="West Asian Billiards &amp; Snooker Federation">West Asian Billiards &amp; Snooker Federation</a> regulates the championships related to billiards and snooker, amongst which an annual tournament.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/West_Asian_Games" title="West Asian Games">West Asian Games</a> have been held in 1997, 2002 and 2005.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/West_Asian_Football_Federation" title="West Asian Football Federation">West Asian Football Federation</a> was founded in 2001 and is one of the regional federations of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_Football_Federation" title="Asian Football Federation">Asian Football Federation</a>. They organize the <a href="/wiki/WAFF_Championship" title="WAFF Championship">WAFF Championship</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/West_Asia_Basketball_Association" title="West Asia Basketball Association">West Asia Basketball Association</a> organizes the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WABA_Championship" title="WABA Championship">WABA Championship</a> since 1999.</p>
