<p>The <b>Central Bank of West African States</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Banque Centrale des États de l'Afrique de l'Ouest</i></span>, <b>BCEAO</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Central_bank" title="Central bank">central bank</a> serving the eight <a href="/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">west African</a> countries which share the common West African <a href="/wiki/CFA_franc" title="CFA franc">CFA franc</a> currency and comprise the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_African_Economic_and_Monetary_Union" title="West African Economic and Monetary Union">West African Economic and Monetary Union</a> (UEMOA):</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Benin" title="Benin">Benin</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina Faso">Burkina Faso</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Guinea-Bissau" title="Guinea-Bissau">Guinea-Bissau</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ivory_Coast" title="Ivory Coast">Ivory Coast (Côte d'Ivoire)</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Togo" title="Togo">Togo</a></li>
</ul>
<p>The Bank is active in developing <a href="/wiki/Financial_inclusion" title="Financial inclusion">financial inclusion</a> policy and is a member of the Alliance for Financial Inclusion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_Bank_of_West_African_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Its predecessor, the <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">Institut d’Emission de l’Afrique Occidentale Française et du Togo</span>, was created in 1955 and was transformed into BCEAO in 1959.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dates_cl.C3.A9s_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dates_cl.C3.A9s-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The treaty establishing the West African Monetary Union (UMOA), signed on May 12, 1962, gave BCEAO the exclusive right to issue the currency as the common <a href="/wiki/Central_bank" title="Central bank">central bank</a> for the then seven member countries:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dates_cl.C3.A9s_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Dates_cl.C3.A9s-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
