<p><b>Seoul United FC</b> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: 서울 유나이티드 FC) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> amateur football club based in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>. It currently competes in the fourth tier of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korea_Republic_football_league_system" title="Korea Republic football league system">South Korean football league system</a>, the <a href="/wiki/K3_League" title="K3 League">K3 League</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seoul_United_FC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Founding">Founding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seoul_United_FC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Founding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The movement to create Seoul United FC began in 2001, with Michael Atkinson and a group of supporters setting about bringing professional football back to the city of Seoul, following the <a href="/wiki/Decentralization_policy_in_K_League" title="Decentralization policy in K League">K League's decision</a> to relocate the then-capital clubs to other cities.</p>
<p>The movement suffered a setback at the end of 2003 when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LG_Group" title="LG Group">LG</a> moved their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anyang_LG_Cheetahs" title="Anyang LG Cheetahs">Anyang LG Cheetahs</a> club from <a href="/wiki/Anyang,_Gyeonggi" title="Anyang, Gyeonggi">Anyang</a> to Seoul, becoming <a href="/wiki/FC_Seoul" title="FC Seoul">FC Seoul</a> and taking up residence at the <a href="/wiki/Seoul_World_Cup_Stadium" title="Seoul World Cup Stadium">Seoul World Cup Stadium</a>.</p>
<p>Despite this, the fan push continued and the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/K3_League" title="K3 League">K3 League</a> in 2007 gave a realistic opportunity for involvement in the league set-up. Two amateur sides, <b>Good Bu&amp;Bu (Good Friend)</b> and <b>Youngseo FC (Jin Seoul)</b>, were merged to create the new club and the home venue of <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Olympic_Stadium" title="Seoul Olympic Stadium">Seoul Olympic Stadium</a> was chosen. For the 2010 season the team moved to <a href="/wiki/Hyochang_Stadium" title="Hyochang Stadium">Hyochang Stadium</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yongsan-gu" title="Yongsan-gu">Yongsan-gu</a>, Seoul. In 2011 the team relocated again to the smaller <a href="/wiki/Madeul_Stadium" title="Madeul Stadium">Madeul Stadium</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nowon-gu" title="Nowon-gu">Nowon-gu</a>, Seoul.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entry_into_the_league_system">Entry into the league system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seoul_United_FC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Entry into the league system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The club's first ever match was played on April 21, 2007, a 2–2 home draw with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Changwon_Dudae_FC" title="Changwon Dudae FC">Changwon Dudae FC</a>.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K3_League_2007" title="K3 League 2007">inaugural K3-League season</a> ended with the club clinching the first ever <a href="/wiki/K3_League" title="K3 League">K3 League</a> title after a 3–0 aggregate victory over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hwasung_Shinwoo_Electronics_FC" title="Hwasung Shinwoo Electronics FC">Hwasung Sinwoo Electronics</a> in the championship playoffs.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Partnerships">Partnerships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seoul_United_FC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Partnerships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Seoul United currently has agreements with Dr.Son Football Academy to support the players in professional development,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and with the social development company, CrazyFootball, to support the team's youth academy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the beginning of 2015, the club announced a partnership with <a href="/wiki/FC_Ryukyu" title="FC Ryukyu">FC Ryukyu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> A friendly match between the clubs at Madeul Stadium was arranged for the 1st of March 2015. This agreement marked the first partnership between clubs of the lowest league in Korea and Japan.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Managerial_Changes">Managerial Changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seoul_United_FC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Managerial Changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the closing weeks of 2014 it was announced that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Choi_Sang-Kook" title="Choi Sang-Kook">Choi Sang-Kook</a> would take over as the 9th manager of the club. He is a former K-League striker with the interesting destination of having a Doctorate in Football.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Team_Colours">Team Colours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seoul_United_FC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Team Colours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The team plays in black and white thick striped shirt, black shorts and white socks.</p>
