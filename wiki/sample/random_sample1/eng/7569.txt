<p><b>Seoul E-Land FC</b> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">서울 이랜드 FC</span>) is <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a> which plays in the <a href="/wiki/K_League_Challenge" title="K League Challenge">K League Challenge</a> (second division). Seoul E-Land was announced as the league's second football club based in Seoul on April 14, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>This club is owned by <a href="/wiki/E-Land_Group" title="E-Land Group">E-Land Group</a> and plays at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympic_Stadium_(Seoul)" title="Olympic Stadium (Seoul)">Seoul Olympic Stadium</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seoul_E-Land_FC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 14 April 2014, <a href="/wiki/E-Land_Group" title="E-Land Group">E-Land Group</a> officially announced foundation of professional football club based in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>.</p>
<p>On 17 July 2014, the club appointed former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vancouver_Whitecaps" title="Vancouver Whitecaps">Vancouver Whitecaps</a> manager <a href="/wiki/Martin_Rennie" title="Martin Rennie">Martin Rennie</a> as head coach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He was sacked on 15 June 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 24 June 2016, the club appointed <a href="/wiki/Park_Kun-ha" title="Park Kun-ha">Park Kun-ha</a> as head coach. He resigned on 9 January 2017 and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Kim_Byung-soo" title="Kim Byung-soo">Kim Byung-soo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
