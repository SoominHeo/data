<p><b>Seoul Science High School</b> is a public school for gifted students (ages 15–18) interested in science. The school was established in 1989, and is located at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jongno-gu" title="Jongno-gu">Jongno-gu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>. It is one of the nineteen science high schools in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea" title="Republic of Korea">Republic of Korea</a>. In 2009, it has become an 'academy for gifted students' selected by the ministry of education of Korea. Now it is one of the four such academies in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea" title="Republic of Korea">Republic of Korea</a>.</p>
<p>The school accepts its intake from the top 0.01% of middle school graduates.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Before the transition, there were about 180 students in each grade, but the number decreased to 156. The general program was a three-year course, but an early graduation program existed. About one-third of sophomore students graduated one year earlier than typical high school students.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>However, after the transition to 'academy for gifted students', students in each grade have decreased to 120, and the curriculum has set again to fulfill the need and curiosity of students. The new curriculum includes of the following :</p>
<ul>
<li>Non-graded, credit-based graduation system</li>
<li>Strengthening research activities to develop creativity</li>
<li>Interdisciplinary curriculum</li>
<li>Focusing on advanced courses</li>
<li>Geared to the level and aptitude of each individual</li>
<li>Various programs for whole-person education</li>
</ul>
<p>In order to achieve graduation, a student must fulfill 170 credits in overall course work, which consists of 140 credits on academic course and 30 credits on research. Also, the student must fulfill at least 240 hours on special activities: 120 hours on group activity, and 120 hours on volunteer work. The university-level courses are accepted as Advanced Placement credit at <a href="/wiki/KAIST" title="KAIST">KAIST</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/POSTECH" title="POSTECH">POSTECH</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UNIST" title="UNIST">UNIST</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gwangju_Institute_of_Science_and_Technology" title="Gwangju Institute of Science and Technology">GIST</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DGIST" title="DGIST">DGIST</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The school has many national and international <a href="/wiki/Science_Olympiad" title="Science Olympiad">Science Olympiad</a> participants and medal winners. Usually a quarter to a half of the Korean participants in the <a href="/wiki/International_Science_Olympiad" title="International Science Olympiad">International Science Olympiads</a> are from Seoul Science High School.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Graduates from this school typically enter <a href="/wiki/Seoul_National_University" title="Seoul National University">Seoul National University</a>, <a href="/wiki/KAIST" title="KAIST">KAIST</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/POSTECH" title="POSTECH">POSTECH</a>. Some students enroll to colleges abroad such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MIT" title="MIT">MIT</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caltech" title="Caltech">Caltech</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UC_Berkeley" title="UC Berkeley">UC Berkeley</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stanford" title="Stanford">Stanford</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harvard" title="Harvard">Harvard</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cornell" title="Cornell">Cornell</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Many alumni have undertaken graduate studies at top institutions in Korea, U.S., and Europe after earning their bachelor's degrees.</p>
<p>The school motto is "Intelligence and Righteous Action" (예지의행, 叡智義行).</p>
