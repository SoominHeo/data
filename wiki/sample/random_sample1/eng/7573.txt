<ul>
<li>1995 Prof. Jong-Sang Lee proposes the establishment of the MoA and the Samsung Cultural foundation promises to fund the creation of the museum. The Seoul National University Campus Planning Committee selects the location for the future museum.</li>
<li>1996 Officials from the Samsung Foundation of Culture and Rem Koolhaas conduct a field investigation of the building’s site.</li>
<li>1997 Schematic Design Completed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>2004 Construction of the structure begins.</li>
<li>2005 The construction of the structure is completed.</li>
<li>2006 The Museum of Art opens on June 8.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture">Architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seoul_National_University_Museum_of_Art&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Architecture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The SNUMoA was designed by <a href="/wiki/Rem_Koolhaas" title="Rem Koolhaas">Rem Koolhaas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> The structure's exterior is made of U-glass which reveals the underlying structural steel framed trusses. The building is located on sloping site which the architect utilized in the final design. The museum is cantilevered on a concrete core, thus giving the appearance of lightness and floating in space. The museum is divided into four basic program areas: Operations, Educational, Exhibition, and Library. The slope of the building benefits the auditorium which has ramped seating. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Directions">Directions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seoul_National_University_Museum_of_Art&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Directions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Once you arrive, the MoA is located to the left of the main gate of the Seoul National University Gwanak Campus. Take the subway or a bus to the main gate and walk up the staircase of the amphi-plaza which is to the left of the gate.</p>
<p>Transportation</p>
<ul>
<li>By Bus: 5511, 5512 (previously 413), 5513, 5516 and 5518.</li>
<li>By Subway: Seoul National University Station, Line 2, Exit No. 3. School shuttle buses are available from this exit.</li>
</ul>
