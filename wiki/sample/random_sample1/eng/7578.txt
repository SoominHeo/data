<p><b>Western European Summer Time</b> (<b>WEST</b>) is a summer <a href="/wiki/Daylight_saving_time" title="Daylight saving time">daylight saving time</a> scheme, 1 hour ahead of <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">Coordinated Universal Time</a>. It is used in:</p>
<ul>
<li>the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> (including <a href="/wiki/Madeira" title="Madeira">Madeira</a> but not the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a></li>
<li>the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a></li>
<li>the British <a href="/wiki/Crown_dependencies" title="Crown dependencies">Crown dependencies</a></li>
<li>the <a href="/wiki/Faroe_Islands" title="Faroe Islands">Faroe Islands</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a> in North Africa</li>
</ul>
<p>Western European Summer Time is known in the countries concerned as:</p>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/British_Summer_Time" title="British Summer Time">British Summer Time</a></b> (<b>BST</b>) in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.</li>
<li><b>Irish Standard Time</b> (<b>IST</b>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STANDARD_TIME_ACT.2C_1968_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-STANDARD_TIME_ACT.2C_1968-1">[1]</a></sup> (<span lang="ga" xml:lang="ga"><i>Am Caighdeánach na hÉireann</i></span> (ACÉ)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>) in <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a>. Also sometimes erroneously referred to as "Irish Summer Time" (<span lang="ga" xml:lang="ga"><i>Am Samhraidh na hÉireann</i></span>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>The scheme runs from the last Sunday in March to the last Sunday in October each year. At both the start and end of the schemes, clock changes take place at 01:00 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC" title="UTC">UTC</a>. During the winter, <a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Mean_Time" title="Greenwich Mean Time">Greenwich Mean Time</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC%2B0" title="UTC+0">UTC+0</a>) is used.</p>
<p>The start and end dates of the scheme are asymmetrical in terms of daylight hours: the <a href="/wiki/Spring_(season)" title="Spring (season)">vernal</a> time of year with a similar amount of daylight to late October is mid-February, well before the start of summer time. The asymmetry reflects temperature more than the length of daylight.</p>
<p>Ireland observes Standard Time during the summer months and changes to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC%2B0" title="UTC+0">UTC+0</a> in winter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STANDARD_TIME_ACT.2C_1968_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-STANDARD_TIME_ACT.2C_1968-1">[1]</a></sup> As Ireland's winter time period begins on the last Sunday in October and finishes on the last Sunday in March, the result is the same as if it observed summer time.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage">Usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_European_Summer_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The following countries and territories use <b>Western European Summer Time</b> during the summer, between 1:00 UTC on the last Sunday of March and 1:00 UTC on the last Sunday of October.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>, regularly since 1980 (rest of Spain is <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">CEST</a>, i.e. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC%2B2" title="UTC+2">UTC+2</a>)</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Denmark" title="Kingdom of Denmark">Kingdom of Denmark</a>

</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a>
<ul>
<li>1916–1939 summers IST</li>
<li>1940–1946 all year IST</li>
<li>1947–1968 summers IST</li>
<li>1968–1971 all year IST</li>
<li>1972– summers IST</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>
<ul>

<ul>

<li>1924 summer WEST</li>
<li>1926–1929 summers WEST</li>
<li>1931–1932 summers WEST</li>
<li>1934–1941 summers WEST</li>
<li>1942–1945 summers WEST (1942–1945 midsummers <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Western_European_Midsummer_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Western European Midsummer Time (page does not exist)">WEMT</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>=WEST+1)</li>
<li>1946–1966 summers WEST</li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ireland">Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_European_Summer_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Time_in_the_Republic_of_Ireland" title="Time in the Republic of Ireland">Time in the Republic of Ireland</a></div>
<p>In Ireland, since the Standard Time (Amendment) Act, 1971, Ireland has used <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC%2B1" title="UTC+1">UTC+1</a> in summer (officially "standard time",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a>: <span lang="ga" xml:lang="ga"><i>am caighdeánach</i></span>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> though usually called "summer time") and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC%2B0" title="UTC+0">UTC+0</a> in winter (officially "winter time").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Portugal">Portugal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_European_Summer_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Portugal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Time_in_Portugal" title="Time in Portugal">Time in Portugal</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> moved to Central European Time and Central European Summer Time in 1992, but reverted to Western European Time in 1996 after concluding that energy savings were small, it had a disturbing effect on children's sleeping habits as it would not get dark until 22:00 or 22:30 in summer evenings, during winter mornings the sun was still rising at 9:00, with repercussions on standards of learning and school performance, and insurance companies reported a rise in the number of accidents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_European_Summer_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/British_Summer_Time" title="British Summer Time">British Summer Time</a></div>
<p>Starting in 1916, the dates for the beginning and end of BST each year were mandated by the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Parliament of the United Kingdom</a>. In February 2002, the Summer Time Order 2002<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SummerTime2002_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-SummerTime2002-13">[13]</a></sup> changed the dates and times to match European rules for moving to and from daylight saving time.</p>
