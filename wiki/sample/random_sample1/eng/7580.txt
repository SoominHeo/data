<p>The <b>207 series</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">207系</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nihyaku-shichi-kei</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a commuter <a href="/wiki/Electric_multiple_unit" title="Electric multiple unit">electric multiple unit</a> (EMU) train type operated by <a href="/wiki/West_Japan_Railway_Company" title="West Japan Railway Company">West Japan Railway Company</a> (JR-West) in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kansai_Region" title="Kansai Region">Kansai Region</a> of Japan since 1991.</p>
<p>This EMU was introduced in 1991 to replace ageing <a href="/wiki/103_series" title="103 series">103 series</a> EMUs operating on the <a href="/wiki/Fukuchiyama_Line" title="Fukuchiyama Line">Fukuchiyama Line</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Katamachi_Line" title="Katamachi Line">Katamachi Line</a>.</p>
<p>This train bears no relation to the <a href="/wiki/207_series_(JR_East)" title="207 series (JR East)">207 series</a> built by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/JNR" title="JNR">JNR</a> and operated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/JR_East" title="JR East">JR East</a> until 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prototype2005_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-prototype2005-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<li><a href="/wiki/Osaka_Higashi_Line" title="Osaka Higashi Line">Osaka Higashi Line</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kansai_Main_Line" title="Kansai Main Line">Kansai Main Line</a> (<a href="/wiki/Yamatoji_Line" title="Yamatoji Line">Yamatoji Line</a>): Kizu – <a href="/wiki/Nara_Station" title="Nara Station">Nara</a>, Nara – <a href="/wiki/Ky%C5%ABh%C5%8Dji_Station" title="Kyūhōji Station">Kyūhōji</a> – <a href="/wiki/Hanaten_Station" title="Hanaten Station">Hanaten</a></li>
