<p><b>West Japan Railway Company</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">西日本旅客鉄道株式会社</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nishi-Nihon Ryokaku Tetsudō Kabushiki-gaisha</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, also referred to as <b>JR-West</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">JR西日本</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Jeiāru Nishi-Nihon</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, is one of the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Railways_Group" title="Japan Railways Group">Japan Railways Group</a> (JR Group) companies and operates in western <a href="/wiki/Honshu" title="Honshu">Honshu</a>. It has its headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Kita-ku,_Osaka" title="Kita-ku, Osaka">Kita-ku, Osaka</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-annual_report_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-annual_report-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Japan_Railway_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shinkansen">Shinkansen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Japan_Railway_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Shinkansen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hokuriku_Shinkansen" title="Hokuriku Shinkansen">Hokuriku Shinkansen</a> (<a href="/wiki/J%C5%8Detsumy%C5%8Dk%C5%8D_Station" title="Jōetsumyōkō Station">Jōetsumyōkō</a> - <a href="/wiki/Kanazawa_Station" title="Kanazawa Station">Kanazawa</a>)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sanyo_Shinkansen" title="Sanyo Shinkansen">Sanyo Shinkansen</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hakata_Minami_Line" title="Hakata Minami Line">Hakata Minami Line</a></li>
</ul>
<p>JR-West's highest-grossing line is the Sanyo Shinkansen <a href="/wiki/High-speed_rail" title="High-speed rail">high-speed rail</a> line between <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fukuoka,_Fukuoka" title="Fukuoka, Fukuoka">Fukuoka</a>. The Sanyo Shinkansen alone accounts for about 40% of JR-West's passenger revenues. The company also operates <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hakata_Minami_Line" title="Hakata Minami Line">Hakata Minami Line</a>, a short commuter line with Shinkansen trains in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fukuoka,_Fukuoka" title="Fukuoka, Fukuoka">Fukuoka</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Urban_Network">Urban Network</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Japan_Railway_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Urban Network">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The "Urban Network" is JR-West's name for its <a href="/wiki/Commuter_rail" title="Commuter rail">commuter rail</a> lines in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Osaka-Kobe-Kyoto" title="Osaka-Kobe-Kyoto">Osaka-Kobe-Kyoto</a> metropolitan area. These lines together comprise 610 km of track, have 245 stations and account for about 40% of JR West's passenger revenues. Urban Network stations are equipped to handle <a href="/wiki/ICOCA" title="ICOCA">ICOCA</a> fare cards. Train control on these lines is highly automated, and during peak hours trains run as often as every two minutes.</p>
<p>JR-West's Urban Network competes with a number of private commuter rail operators around Osaka, the "Big 4" being <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hankyu_Railway" title="Hankyu Railway">Hankyu Railway</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hanshin_Railway" title="Hanshin Railway">Hanshin Railway</a> (Hankyu bought Hanshin in 2005), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keihan_Railway" title="Keihan Railway">Keihan Railway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kintetsu_Railway" title="Kintetsu Railway">Kintetsu</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nankai_Railway" title="Nankai Railway">Nankai Railway</a>. JR-West's market share in the region is roughly equal to that of the Big 4 put together, largely due to its comprehensive network and high-speed commuter trains (Special Rapid Service trains on the Kobe and Kyoto lines operate at up to 130 km/h).</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Intercity_and_regional_lines">Intercity and regional lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Japan_Railway_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Intercity and regional lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Japan_Railway_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>JR-West was incorporated as a business corporation (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kabushiki_kaisha" title="Kabushiki kaisha">kabushiki kaisha</a>) on April 1, 1987 as part of the breakup of government-owned <a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a> (JNR). Initially, it was a wholly owned subsidiary of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/JNR_Settlement_Corporation" title="JNR Settlement Corporation">JNR Settlement Corporation</a> (JNRSC), a special company created to hold the assets of the former JNR while they were shuffled among the new JR companies.</p>
<p>For the first four years of its existence, JR-West leased its highest-revenue line, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sany%C5%8D_Shinkansen" title="Sanyō Shinkansen">Sanyō Shinkansen</a>, from the separate Shinkansen Holding Corporation. JR-West purchased the line in October 1991 at a cost of 974.1 billion <a href="/wiki/Japanese_yen" title="Japanese yen">JPY</a> (about US$7.2 billion) in long-term payable debt.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>JNRSC sold 68.3% of JR-West in an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Stock_Exchange" title="Tokyo Stock Exchange">Tokyo Stock Exchange</a> in October 1996. After JNRSC was dissolved in October 1998, its shares of JR-West were transferred to the government-owned Japan Railway Construction Public Corporation (JRCC), which merged into the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Railway_Construction,_Transport_and_Technology_Agency" title="Japan Railway Construction, Transport and Technology Agency">Japan Railway Construction, Transport and Technology Agency</a> (JRTT) as part of a bureaucratic reform package in October 2003. JRTT offered all of its shares in JR-West to the public in an international IPO in 2004, ending the era of government ownership of JR-West. JR-West is now listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange, <a href="/wiki/Nagoya_Stock_Exchange" title="Nagoya Stock Exchange">Nagoya Stock Exchange</a>, <a href="/wiki/Osaka_Securities_Exchange" title="Osaka Securities Exchange">Osaka Securities Exchange</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka_Stock_Exchange" title="Fukuoka Stock Exchange">Fukuoka Stock Exchange</a>.</p>
