<p><b>King Li of Zhou</b> (died in 828 BC) (<a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">周厲王</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Zhōu Lì Wáng</span></i>) was the tenth king of the <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zhou_Dynasty" title="Zhou Dynasty">Zhou Dynasty</a>. Estimated dates of his <a href="/wiki/Reign" title="Reign">reign</a> are 877–841 BC or 857–842 BC (<i>Cambridge History of Ancient China</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>King Li was a corrupt and decadent king. To pay for his pleasures and vices, King Li raised taxes and caused misery among his subjects. It is said that he barred the commoners from profiting from the communal forests and lakes. He enstated a new law which allowed him to punish anyone, by death, who dared to speak against him. King Li’s bad rule soon forced many peasants and soldiers into revolt, and Li was sent into exile at a place called Zhi near <a href="/wiki/Linfen" title="Linfen">Linfen</a> (842 BC). His son was taken by one of his ministers and hidden.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> When Li died in exile in 828 BC, power was passed to his son.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
