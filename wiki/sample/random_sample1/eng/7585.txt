<p>The <b>IAI Searcher</b> (also known by the Hebrew name מרומית <i>Meyromit</i> - "<a href="/wiki/Marsh_tern" title="Marsh tern">Marsh tern</a>",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> or officially in Israel as the חוגלה <i>Hugla</i> - "<a href="/wiki/Alectoris" title="Alectoris">Alectoris</a>") is a reconnaissance <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UAV" title="UAV">UAV</a> developed in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> in the 1980s. In the following decade, it replaced the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IMI_Mastiff" title="IMI Mastiff">IMI Mastiff</a> and <a href="/wiki/IAI_Scout" title="IAI Scout">IAI Scout</a> UAVs then in service with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Israeli_Army" title="Israeli Army">Israeli Army</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IAI_Searcher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Searcher looks almost identical to the <a href="/wiki/IAI_Scout" title="IAI Scout">Scout</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IAI_Pioneer" title="IAI Pioneer">Pioneer</a>, but is in fact scaled up and is well over twice the size of the Scout. The Searcher is powered by a 35 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kilowatt" title="Kilowatt">kW</a> (47 <a href="/wiki/Horsepower" title="Horsepower">hp</a>) piston engine. The new design features updated avionics and sensor systems with greater flight endurance as well as increased redundancy for improved survivability. In addition to Israel, the system had been exported and is currently in use by <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operational_deployment">Operational deployment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IAI_Searcher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operational deployment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>More than 100 Searcher IIs are being operated by the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Air_Force" title="Indian Air Force">Indian Air Force</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Navy" title="Indian Navy">Indian Navy</a></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Crashes">Crashes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IAI_Searcher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Crashes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>May 21, 2015 Russian UAV <i>"Forpost"</i> (Searcher II, tail number 923) crashed near <a href="/wiki/Avdiivka" title="Avdiivka">Avdiivka</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a> during the <a href="/wiki/War_in_Donbass" title="War in Donbass">War in Donbass</a>. The aircraft was shot down by Ukrainian volunteer battalion <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dnipro_Battalion" title="Dnipro Battalion">Dnipro-1</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:IAI-Searcher2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2592" data-file-width="3888" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/IAI-Searcher2.jpg/220px-IAI-Searcher2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/IAI-Searcher2.jpg/330px-IAI-Searcher2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/IAI-Searcher2.jpg/440px-IAI-Searcher2.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

The IAI Searcher II</div>

<p><span style="font-size: 120%;"><b>General characteristics</b></span></p>
<ul>
<li><b>Crew:</b> None</li>
<li><b>Capacity:</b> 68 kg (150 lb) payload</li>
<li><b>Length:</b> 5.85 m (19 ft 2 in)</li>
<li><b>Wingspan:</b> 8.54 m (28 ft 0 in)</li>
<li><b>Height:</b> 1.25 m (4 ft 1 in)</li>
<li><b>Gross weight:</b> 500 kg (1,100 lb)</li>
<li><b>Powerplant:</b> 1 × <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Limbach_L_550&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Limbach L 550 (page does not exist)">Limbach L 550</a>, 35 kW (47 hp) each</li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-size: 120%;"><b>Performance</b></span></p>
<ul>
<li><b>Maximum speed:</b> 200 km/h (125 mph)</li>
<li><b>Endurance:</b> 18 hours</li>
<li><b>Service ceiling:</b> 6,100 m (20,000 ft)</li>
</ul>
