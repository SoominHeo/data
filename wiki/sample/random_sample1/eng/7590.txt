<p>The <b>Tangut script</b> (Chinese: 西夏文 xī xià wén) was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Logographic" title="Logographic">logographic</a> writing system, used for writing the extinct <a href="/wiki/Tangut_language" title="Tangut language">Tangut language</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Western_Xia" title="Western Xia">Western Xia</a> Dynasty. According to the latest count, 5863 Tangut characters are known, excluding variants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The Tangut characters are similar in appearance to <a href="/wiki/Chinese_characters" title="Chinese characters">Chinese characters</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mote2003_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mote2003-2">[2]</a></sup> with the same type of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stroke_(CJK_character)" title="Stroke (CJK character)">strokes</a>, but the methods of forming characters in the Tangut writing system are significantly different from <a href="/wiki/Chinese_character_classification" title="Chinese character classification">those of forming Chinese characters</a>. As in <a href="/wiki/Chinese_calligraphy" title="Chinese calligraphy">Chinese calligraphy</a>, regular, running, cursive and seal scripts were used in Tangut writing.</p>
<p>According to the <i><a href="/wiki/History_of_Song" title="History of Song">History of Song</a></i> (1346), the script was designed by the high-ranking official <a href="/wiki/Yeli_Renrong" title="Yeli Renrong">Yeli Renrong</a> under Western Xia Emperor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Li_Yuanhao" title="Li Yuanhao">Li Yuanhao</a>'s supervision in 1036.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YongPeng2008_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-YongPeng2008-4">[4]</a></sup> The script was invented in a short period of time, and was put into use quickly. Government schools were founded to teach the script. Official documents were written in the script (with diplomatic ones written bilingually). A great number of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buddhist_scripture" title="Buddhist scripture">Buddhist scriptures</a> were translated from Tibetan and Chinese, and <a href="/wiki/Woodblock_printing" title="Woodblock printing">block printed</a> in the script.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Although the dynasty collapsed in 1227, the script continued to be used for another few centuries. The last example of the script occurs on a pair of <a href="/wiki/Tangut_dharani_pillars" title="Tangut dharani pillars">Tangut dharani pillars</a> found at <a href="/wiki/Baoding" title="Baoding">Baoding</a> (130 km southwest of <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>), which were erected in 1502.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>6,125 characters of the Tangut script were included in Unicode version 9.0 in June 2016 in the <a href="/wiki/Tangut_(Unicode_block)" title="Tangut (Unicode block)">Tangut block</a>. 755 Radicals and components used in the modern study of Tangut were added to the <a href="/wiki/Tangut_Components" title="Tangut Components">Tangut Components</a> block, and the Tangut iteration mark was included in the <a href="/wiki/Ideographic_Symbols_and_Punctuation" title="Ideographic Symbols and Punctuation">Ideographic Symbols and Punctuation</a> block.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>The [Tangut] language is remarkable for being written in one of the most inconvenient of all scripts, a collection of nearly 5,800 characters of the same kind as Chinese characters but rather more complicated; very few are made up of as few as four strokes and most are made up of a good many more, in some cases nearly twenty. It is extremely difficult to remember them, since there are few recognizable indications of sound and meaning in the constituent parts of a character, and in some cases characters which differ from one another only in minor details of shape or by one or two strokes have completely different sounds and meanings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<div class="templatequotecite"><cite>— <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Clauson" title="Gerard Clauson">Gerard Clauson</a></cite></div>
</blockquote>
<p>Tangut characters can be divided into two classes: simple and composite. The latter are more numerous. The simple characters can be either semantic or phonetic. None of the Tangut characters are pictographic, while some of the Chinese characters were at the time of their creation; this is one of the major differences between Tangut and Chinese characters.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tangut_script_2.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="400" data-file-width="1746" height="57" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Tangut_script_2.png/250px-Tangut_script_2.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Tangut_script_2.png/375px-Tangut_script_2.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Tangut_script_2.png/500px-Tangut_script_2.png 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tangut_script_2.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Tangut character "mud" is made with part of the character "water" (far left) and the whole of the character "soil"</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Most composite characters comprise two components. A few comprise three or four. A component can be a simple character, or part of a composite character. The composite characters include semantic-semantic ones and semantic-phonetic ones. A few special composite characters were made for transliterating Chinese and <a href="/wiki/Sanskrit" title="Sanskrit">Sanskrit</a>.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:132px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tangut_script_3.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="400" data-file-width="848" height="61" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Tangut_script_3.png/130px-Tangut_script_3.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Tangut_script_3.png/195px-Tangut_script_3.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Tangut_script_3.png/260px-Tangut_script_3.png 2x" width="130"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tangut_script_3.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Tangut characters for "toe" (left) and "finger" (right), both characters having the same components</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>There are a number of pairs of special composite characters worth noting. The members of such a pair have the same components, only the location of the components in them is different (e.g. AB vs. BA, ABC vs. ACB). The members of such a pair have very similar meanings.</p>
