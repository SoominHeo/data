<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Emperor Huizong of Western Xia</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Huizong</b> (Li Bingchang, Li ping chang<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ireland1883_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ireland1883-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>)(1060–1086) was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Western_Xia" title="Emperor of Western Xia">Emperor of Western Xia</a> (1067–1086).</p>
<p>After his father's sudden death, Huizong assumed the throne at the young age of six. His mother became the regent for the rest of Huizong's reign. In 1076, Huizong turned sixteen, and was supposed to assume direct control of the throne, but was stopped by the enormous influence of the Dowager. He was depressed by the control of his mother, and Huizong later died at the young age of 26.</p>
<p>Huizong was succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Chongzong_of_Western_Xia" title="Emperor Chongzong of Western Xia">Emperor Chongzong of Western Xia</a>.</p>
