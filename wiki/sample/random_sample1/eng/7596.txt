<p><b>Molluginaceae</b> are a <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flowering_plants" title="Flowering plants">flowering plants</a> recognized by several taxonomists. It was previously included in the larger family <a href="/wiki/Aizoaceae" title="Aizoaceae">Aizoaceae</a>. The <a href="/wiki/APG_III_system" title="APG III system">APG III system</a> of 2009 made no change in the status of the family as compared to the <a href="/wiki/APG_II_system" title="APG II system">APG II system</a> of 2003 and the <a href="/wiki/APG_system" title="APG system">APG system</a> of 1998, apart from a reassignment of several genera, such as the placement of <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Corrigiola&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Corrigiola (page does not exist)">Corrigiola</a></i> and <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Telephium&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Telephium (page does not exist)">Telephium</a></i> into Caryophyllaceae, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Corbichonia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Corbichonia (page does not exist)">Corbichonia</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Lophiocarpaceae" title="Lophiocarpaceae">Lophiocarpaceae</a>, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Microtea&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Microtea (page does not exist)">Microtea</a></i> into <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Microteaceae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Microteaceae (page does not exist)">Microteaceae</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Limeum" title="Limeum">Limeum</a></i> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Limeaceae" title="Limeaceae">Limeaceae</a>, because the family was found to be widely polyphyletic in Caryophyllales. In addition <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Macarthuria&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Macarthuria (page does not exist)">Macarthuria</a></i> was found not to be related to <i><a href="/wiki/Limeum" title="Limeum">Limeum</a></i> as previously thought and thus it was placed in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Macarthuriaceae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Macarthuriaceae (page does not exist)">Macarthuriaceae</a>, and similarly species formerly placed in <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hypertelis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hypertelis (page does not exist)">Hypertelis</a></i>, apart from type species <i>Hypertelis spergulacea</i>, a true Molluginaceae, were found to belong elsewhere and were described as <i><a href="/wiki/Kewa_(plant)" title="Kewa (plant)">Kewa</a></i> in the family <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kewaceae" title="Kewaceae">Kewaceae</a>, named for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Botanic_Gardens_Kew" title="Royal Botanic Gardens Kew">Royal Botanic Gardens Kew</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kewaceae_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kewaceae-2">[2]</a></sup> Molluginaceae is still assigned to the order <a href="/wiki/Caryophyllales" title="Caryophyllales">Caryophyllales</a> in the clade <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Core_eudicots" title="Core eudicots">core eudicots</a>, although the generic circumscription is difficult because <i><a href="/wiki/Mollugo" title="Mollugo">Mollugo</a></i> is not monophyletic.</p>
