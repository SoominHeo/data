<p>The <b>Sun National Bank Center</b>, formerly known as <b>Sovereign Bank Arena</b>, is a $53-million, 10,500-<a href="/wiki/Seating_capacity" title="Seating capacity">seat</a> arena in <a href="/wiki/Trenton,_New_Jersey" title="Trenton, New Jersey">Trenton</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>; was home to the <a href="/wiki/Trenton_Freedom" title="Trenton Freedom">Trenton Freedom</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Professional_Indoor_Football_League_(2012)" title="Professional Indoor Football League (2012)">PIFL</a> and hosts other special events including family shows, sporting events and concerts. On November 13, 2009, <a href="/wiki/Sun_National_Bank" title="Sun National Bank">Sun National Bank</a> signed a naming-rights deal for seven years for $2.1 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_.26_events">History &amp; events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sun_National_Bank_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History &amp; events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The arena opened as Sovereign Bank Arena on October 6, 1999 with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Wrestling_Entertainment" title="World Wrestling Entertainment">World Wrestling Entertainment</a> event. Since then the arena has hosted over 1200 events with over 4 million guests attending and has sold out shows by <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen" title="Bruce Springsteen">Bruce Springsteen</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Wrestling_Entertainment" title="World Wrestling Entertainment">World Wrestling Entertainment</a> (WWE), <a href="/wiki/Shania_Twain" title="Shania Twain">Shania Twain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keith_Urban" title="Keith Urban">Keith Urban</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cher" title="Cher">Cher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ringling_Bros._and_Barnum_%26_Bailey_Circus" title="Ringling Bros. and Barnum &amp; Bailey Circus">Ringling Bros. and Barnum &amp; Bailey Circus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a> and <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The arena held the first and second rounds of the <a href="/wiki/2006_NCAA_Women%27s_Division_I_Basketball_Tournament" title="2006 NCAA Women's Division I Basketball Tournament">2006 NCAA Women's Division I Basketball Tournament</a> The 2000 and 2001 <a href="/wiki/Northeast_Conference" title="Northeast Conference">Northeast Conference</a> men's basketball tournaments were held there as was the 2003 <a href="/wiki/Metro_Atlantic_Athletic_Conference" title="Metro Atlantic Athletic Conference">Metro Atlantic Athletic Conference</a> men's basketball tournament. The 2009 edition of the MAAC men's basketball tournament was scheduled to be played at the arena until administrators at the facility asked MAAC tournament officials to consider an alternate location for the games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The finals of the Trenton Regional in the <a href="/wiki/2009_NCAA_Women%27s_Division_I_Basketball_Tournament" title="2009 NCAA Women's Division I Basketball Tournament">2009 NCAA women's tournament</a>, were held there as well.</p>
<p>The arena seats 7,605 for hockey and other ice events, 8,600 for basketball and up to 10,500 for concerts, family shows, and other events which makes it the largest arena in Southern <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>. The arena is located next to, and served by, the <a href="/wiki/Hamilton_Avenue_(River_Line_station)" title="Hamilton Avenue (River Line station)">Hamilton Avenue station</a> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Jersey_Transit" title="New Jersey Transit">NJ Transit</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/River_Line_(New_Jersey_Transit)" title="River Line (New Jersey Transit)">River Line</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey_Route_129" title="New Jersey Route 129">New Jersey Route 129</a>.</p>
<p>The arena is managed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philadelphia,_Pennsylvania" title="Philadelphia, Pennsylvania">Philadelphia</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Comcast_Spectacor#Global_Spectrum" title="Comcast Spectacor">Global Spectrum</a>, a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Comcast_Spectacor" title="Comcast Spectacor">Comcast Spectacor</a>.</p>
<p>The arena hosted the <a href="/wiki/Total_Nonstop_Action_Wrestling" title="Total Nonstop Action Wrestling">Total Nonstop Action Wrestling</a> Pay-Per-View <a href="/wiki/Hard_Justice_(2008)" title="Hard Justice (2008)">Hard Justice 2008</a> on August 10, 2008.</p>
<p>The Sun National Bank Center also plays host to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSBA" title="USSBA">USSBA</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zildjian" title="Zildjian">Zildjian</a> Indoor Percussion Championships every April as the culmination of the Winter Percussion season and the National Championships for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSBA" title="USSBA">USSBA</a> Indoor<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
