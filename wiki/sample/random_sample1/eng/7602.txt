<p>The <i><b>Thunderbird</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">サンダーバード</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sandābādo</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Limited_express" title="Limited express">limited express</a> train service operated by the <a href="/wiki/West_Japan_Railway_Company" title="West Japan Railway Company">West Japan Railway Company</a> (JR West) between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Osaka_Station" title="Osaka Station">Osaka</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wakuraonsen_Station" title="Wakuraonsen Station">Wakuraonsen</a> in Japan, using portions of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokaido_Main_Line" title="Tokaido Main Line">Tokaido Main Line</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kosei_Line" title="Kosei Line">Kosei Line</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hokuriku_Main_Line" title="Hokuriku Main Line">Hokuriku Main Line</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nanao_Line" title="Nanao Line">Nanao Line</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thunderbird_(train)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From the start of the 20 April 1995 timetable revision, new <a href="/wiki/681_series" title="681 series">681 series</a> EMUs were introduced on Osaka to Toyama services, named <i>Super Raichō (Thunderbird)</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meikan1995_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-meikan1995-1">[1]</a></sup> These became simply <i>Thunderbird</i> from March 1997.</p>
<p>The last remaining <i><a href="/wiki/Raich%C5%8D" title="Raichō">Raichō</a></i> service was discontinued from the start of the 12 March 2011 timetable revision, with all trains subsequently using the <i>Thunderbird</i> name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>With the opening of the <a href="/wiki/Hokuriku_Shinkansen" title="Hokuriku Shinkansen">Hokuriku Shinkansen</a> in March 2015, the <i>Thunderbird</i> timetable was revised, and services between Kanazawa and Toyama/Uozu stations was discontinued.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2006_rape_incident">2006 rape incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thunderbird_(train)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2006 rape incident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 3 August 2006, a woman was raped by a 36-year-old man in a train toilet while travelling on the <i>Thunderbird</i> 50 service (just after Fukui Station), despite the car being occupied by approximately 40 other passengers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-japantoday_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-japantoday-4">[4]</a></sup> As a result, JR West introduced a "women-only" section in the reserved-seating cars of <i>Raichō</i> and <i>Thunderbird</i> trains from October 2007, and JR East added prominent "SOS" stickers inside all of its trains in June 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-japantimes20070524_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-japantimes20070524-5">[5]</a></sup> On 17 January 2008, district court in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C5%8Ctsu,_Shiga" title="Ōtsu, Shiga">Otsu</a> sentenced the man to 18 years in prison.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-japantimes20080118_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-japantimes20080118-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
