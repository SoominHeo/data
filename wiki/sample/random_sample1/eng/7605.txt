<p>The <b>National High School Baseball Invitational Tournament</b> (選抜高等学校野球大会 <i>senbatsu kōtō gakkō yakyū taikai</i>) of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, commonly known as "<b>Spring Kōshien</b>" (春の甲子園 <i>haru no kōshien</i>) or "Senbatsu" (センバツ), is an annual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_school" title="High school">high school</a> <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> tournament.</p>
<p>The tournament, organized by the <a href="/wiki/Japan_High_School_Baseball_Federation" title="Japan High School Baseball Federation">Japan High School Baseball Federation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mainichi_Shimbun" title="Mainichi Shimbun">Mainichi Shimbun</a>, takes place each year in March at <a href="/wiki/Koshien_Stadium" title="Koshien Stadium">Hanshin Koshien Stadium</a> in the Koshien district of <a href="/wiki/Nishinomiya" title="Nishinomiya">Nishinomiya City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hy%C5%8Dgo_Prefecture" title="Hyōgo Prefecture">Hyōgo</a>, Japan.</p>
<p>Teams qualify for the tournament by participating in the regional fall tournaments held throughout the country. While finishing in the top teams generally guarantees an invitation, it is up to the <a href="/wiki/Japan_High_School_Baseball_Federation" title="Japan High School Baseball Federation">Japan High School Baseball Federation</a> to determine invitees. For instance, in the 2008 Fall Tournament in the Tōhoku region, Ichinoseki Gakuin was the runner-up. However, they were passed over for 3rd place Hanamaki Higashi for the 2009 invitation tournament.</p>
<p>Of the 32 bids, 26 are automatically awarded as follows to the following regions:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hokkaido" title="Hokkaido">Hokkaido</a> - 1</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dhoku" title="Tōhoku">Tōhoku</a> - 2</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kant%C5%8D" title="Kantō">Kantō</a> - 4</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> - 1</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dkai_region" title="Tōkai region">Tōkai</a> - 2</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Shin%27etsu_region" title="Shin'etsu region">Shin'etsu</a> - 2</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kinki" title="Kinki">Kinki</a> - 6</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ch%C5%ABgoku" title="Chūgoku">Chūgoku</a> - 2</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Shikoku" title="Shikoku">Shikoku</a> - 2</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kyushu" title="Kyushu">Kyushu</a> + <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Okinawa" title="Okinawa">Okinawa</a> - 4</li>
</ul>
<p>In addition, one additional bid is awarded to a team in the Kanto/Tokyo region, and another to a team in the Chugoku/Shikoku region.</p>
<p>The region of the winning team in the Meiji Jingu Fall Tournament (which consists of all fall regional champions) also receives a bid.</p>
<p>The final 3 bids are what are considered "21st Century Teams".</p>
<p>21st century teams are a way to give teams who either may not get close to qualifying or who have served as a model school in some way a chance to compete. A team from each prefecture is nominated around November–December. The teams must have advanced to the round of 16 play, but not gotten past the quarterfinals of their prefecture's tournament. Then each region nominates one of those nominees to the selection committee by December 15. Finally, the committee selects the three 21st century teams.</p>
