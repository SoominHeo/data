<p><a href="/wiki/Counting" title="Counting">Counting</a> in <a href="/wiki/Prehistory" title="Prehistory">prehistory</a> was first assisted by using body parts, primarily the <a href="/wiki/Finger-counting" title="Finger-counting">fingers</a>. This is reflected in the etymology of certain <a href="/wiki/Numeral_(linguistics)" title="Numeral (linguistics)">number names</a>, such as in the names of <i>ten</i> and <i>hundred</i> in the <a href="/wiki/Proto-Indo-European_numerals" title="Proto-Indo-European numerals">Proto-Indo-European numerals</a>, both containing the root *<i>dḱ</i> also seen in the word for "finger" (Latin <i>digitus</i>, cognate to English <i>toe</i>).</p>
<p>Early systems of counting using <a href="/wiki/Tally_marks" title="Tally marks">tally marks</a> appear in the <a href="/wiki/Upper_Paleolithic" title="Upper Paleolithic">Upper Paleolithic</a>. The first more complex systems develop in the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Near_East" title="Ancient Near East">Ancient Near East</a> together with the development of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bronze_Age_writing" title="Bronze Age writing">early writing</a> out of <a href="/wiki/Proto-writing" title="Proto-writing">proto-writing</a> systems.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prehistoric_numerals&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Tally_marks" title="Tally marks">Tally marks</a></div>
<p>Numerals originally developed from the use of <a href="/wiki/Tally_marks" title="Tally marks">tally marks</a> as a counting aid, with the oldest examples being about 35,000 to 25,000 years old.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prehistoric_numerals&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Counting aids like tally marks become more sophisticated in the Near Eastern <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">Neolithic</a>, developing into various types of <a href="/wiki/Proto-writing" title="Proto-writing">proto-writing</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Cuneiform_script" title="Cuneiform script">Cuneiform script</a> develops out of proto-writing associated with keeping track of goods during the <a href="/wiki/Chalcolithic" title="Chalcolithic">Chalcolithic</a>.</p>

Old Mokshan numerals<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag needs to be fact-checked with the cited source(s). <p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moksha_people" title="Moksha people">Moksha people</a>, whose existence dates to about the beginning of the 1st millennium BC, had a numeral system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag needs to be fact-checked with the cited source(s). (April 2016)">verification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The numerals were <a href="/wiki/Tally_marks" title="Tally marks">tally marks</a> carved on wood, drawn on clay or birch bark. In some places they were preserved until the beginning of 20th century mostly among small traders, bee-keepers, and village elders. These numerals still can be found on old shepherd and tax-gatherer staffs, apiaries, and pottery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag needs to be fact-checked with the cited source(s). (April 2016)">verification needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
