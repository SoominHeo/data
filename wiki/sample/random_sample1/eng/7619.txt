<p>The <b>Emmelichthyidae</b> are a <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> of small to medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Marine_(ocean)" title="Marine (ocean)">marine</a> fish known commonly as <b>rovers</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HeemstraRandall1977_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-HeemstraRandall1977-2">[2]</a></sup> The family was once much larger, including a wide range of <a href="/wiki/Plankton" title="Plankton">plankton</a>-eating fish, but most of the genera were discovered to be unrelated examples of <a href="/wiki/Parallel_evolution" title="Parallel evolution">parallel evolution</a>, and were moved to other families.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EoF_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-EoF-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The rovers are distributed in tropical and warmer temperate waters in the <a href="/wiki/Indo-Pacific" title="Indo-Pacific">Indo-Pacific</a>, southern <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific</a>, eastern <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worms_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-worms-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>These fish have protrusible, toothless or nearly toothless jaws, long dorsal fins, and forked tail fins with lobes that fold in like <a href="/wiki/Scissors" title="Scissors">scissors</a>. The largest species reach about 50 centimeters in length.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worms_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-worms-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
