<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Xue Rengui</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="hatnote" role="note">This is a <a href="/wiki/Chinese_name" title="Chinese name">Chinese name</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_surname" title="Chinese surname">family name</a> is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xue_(surname)" title="Xue (surname)">Xue</a>.</div>
<p><b>Xue Rengui</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">薛仁贵</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">薛仁貴</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Xuē Rénguì</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Wade%E2%80%93Giles" title="Wade–Giles">Wade–Giles</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-wadegile" xml:lang="zh-Latn-wadegile">Hsüeh<sup>1</sup> Jen<sup>2</sup>-kuei<sup>4</sup></span></i>; 614–683), formal name <b>Xue Li</b> (薛禮) but went by the <a href="/wiki/Courtesy_name" title="Courtesy name">courtesy name</a> of Rengui, was one of the most famous Chinese generals during the early <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tang_Dynasty" title="Tang Dynasty">Tang Dynasty</a>, due to references to him in popular literature. During his career, he participated in successful campaigns against remnants of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Tujue" title="Western Tujue">Western Tujue</a> and against <a href="/wiki/Goguryeo" title="Goguryeo">Goguryeo</a>, with only one major flaw on his record which was an unsuccessful campaign against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tufan" title="Tufan">Tufan</a> in 670, where another general in his army refused to listen to Xue's advice and charged ahead and caused the majority of the army to be lost.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="During_Emperor_Taizong.27s_reign">During Emperor Taizong's reign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xue_Rengui&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: During Emperor Taizong's reign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Xue Rengui was born in 614, during the reign of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Yang_of_Sui" title="Emperor Yang of Sui">Emperor Yang of Sui</a>, but his early activities were not recorded, other than that his wife had the surname Liu (柳). It was said that he was poor and was a farmer. Around the time that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tang_Dynasty" title="Tang Dynasty">Tang Dynasty</a>'s second emperor <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Taizong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Taizong of Tang">Emperor Taizong</a> was set to launch a major campaign against <a href="/wiki/Goguryeo" title="Goguryeo">Goguryeo</a> in 644, Xue was planning to rebury his ancestors, when Lady Liu told him:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>You have abilities higher than most people, and you need to know when to use them. Now, the <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_China" title="Emperor of China">Son of Heaven</a> is ready to attack <a href="/wiki/Liaodong_Peninsula" title="Liaodong Peninsula">Liaodong</a> and he is seeking for fierce warriors. These times do not come often. Is it not the case that you want to have achievements to show yourself? Once you received great honors, it will not be too late to rebury your ancestors.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Xue thus went to meet the general Zhang Shigui (張士貴) to volunteer for the army. Once he reached the front, on an occasion when the general Liu Jun'ang (劉君卬) was being surrounded by Goguryeo forces, Xue went to rescue Liu, and he was able to kill the Goguryeo commander and hang the Goguryeo commander's head on his saddle; after this incident, he began to gain fame. When Emperor Taizong was ready to attack the Goguryeo city of Ansi (安市, in modern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anshan,_China" title="Anshan, China">Anshan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liaoning" title="Liaoning">Liaoning</a>) in 645 and was faced with a major relief army sent by Goguryeo's <i>Dae Mangniji</i> (regent) <a href="/wiki/Yeon_Gaesomun" title="Yeon Gaesomun">Yeon Gaesomun</a>, commanded by the generals Go Yeonsu (高延壽) and Go Hyejin (高惠真), Emperor Taizong had his officers try to repel the Goguryeo forces. Xue, believing himself to be powerful and wanting to show his ferocity, put on a white armor and armed himself with a trident and a two bows. As he fought his way through the frontlines, he inflicted much casualties on the Gogureyo soldiers. Emperor Taizong spotted him from a distance and asked his attendants, "Who is that man in white armor?" and was told it was Xue. Emperor Taizong summoned Xue to his presence and awarded him with gold and silk and also gave him a general title. After Emperor Taizong retreated later in the year, he told Xue:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>My generals are all old, and I am trying to find new generals to entrust the military matters to. I cannot find one better than you. I am less happy about gaining Liaodong than about gaining you.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Emperor Taizong made Xue one of the commanding generals for the imperial guards.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="During_Emperor_Gaozong.27s_reign">During Emperor Gaozong's reign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xue_Rengui&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: During Emperor Gaozong's reign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After Emperor Taizong's death in 649, his son <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Gaozong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Gaozong of Tang">Li Zhi</a> succeeded him (as Emperor Gaozong), and for the first few years of Emperor Gaozong's reign, Xue Rengui appeared to remain a commanding general for the imperial guards. In 654, when Emperor Gaozong was visiting the vacation palace Wannian Palace (萬年宮, in modern <a href="/wiki/Baoji" title="Baoji">Baoji</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shaanxi" title="Shaanxi">Shaanxi</a>), there was a major storm. On the night of June 22,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> it rained particularly hard, and a flash flood descended on the Xuanwu Gate of Wannian Palace. The imperial guards all fled, but Xue did not, and instead climbed up the gate and yelled loudly inside the palace to warn the emperor. Emperor Gaozong quickly got out of his bed and climbed as high as he could, and in brief time, the water flooded into his bedchambers, and this flood killed some 3,000 residents of Linyou County, where the palace was located, and imperial guards. Afterwards, Emperor Gaozong made the comment to Xue, "It is only by your call that I avoided drowning, and from this I know that you are a faithful subject." He awarded Xue a horse.</p>
<p>In 657, when the general <a href="/wiki/Su_Dingfang" title="Su Dingfang">Su Dingfang</a> attacked <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Tujue" title="Western Tujue">Western Tujue</a>'s <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shaboluo_Khan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shaboluo Khan (page does not exist)">Shaboluo Khan</a> Ashina Helu (阿史那賀魯), Xue submitted a suggestion that if the wife and children of the chief of one of Western Tujue's constituent tribes, Nishu Tribe (泥孰), who did not particularly support Ashina Helu but was forced to comply after Ashina Helu took his wife and children hostage, were to be captured by Tang forces, that they be immediately released so that the chief of Nishu would submit to Tang. When Emperor Gaozong approved this suggestion, the chief of Nishu did, indeed, join Tang's army. Su was subsequently able to defeat and capture Ashina Helu.</p>
<p>Later that year, Xue was made deputy to the general Cheng Mingzhen (程名振) in an operation against <a href="/wiki/Goguryeo" title="Goguryeo">Goguryeo</a>, and they captured Goguryeo's city Chifeng (赤烽, in modern <a href="/wiki/Fushun" title="Fushun">Fushun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liaoning" title="Liaoning">Liaoning</a>) and defeated the Goguryeo general Du Bangnu (豆方婁). In 659, Xue further defeated the Goguryeo general Wen Shamen (溫沙門). He also engaged <a href="/wiki/Khitan_people" title="Khitan people">Qidan</a> forces, capturing their chief Abugu (阿卜固) and taking him back to the eastern capital <a href="/wiki/Luoyang" title="Luoyang">Luoyang</a>. For this achievement, he was created the Baron of Hedong.</p>
<p>In 661, after the chief of Tang's vassal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Huige" title="Huige">Huige</a>, Yaoluoge Porun (藥羅葛婆閏) died and was succeeded by his nephew Yaoluoge Bisudu (藥羅葛比粟毒), Yaoluoge Bisudu broke away from Tang and allied with two other tribes, Tongluo (同羅) and Pugu (僕固) in attacking Tang's northern boundary. Emperor Gaozong commissioned the general Zheng Rentai (鄭仁泰) to be in charge of an operation against Huige, while making the Xue and Liu Shenli (劉審理) Zheng's deputies. When they encountered Huige's coalition forces—which by this point appeared to include all nine major tribes of <a href="/wiki/Tiele_people" title="Tiele people">Tiele</a>—the coalition challenged Tang forces to a small battle, with Tiele sending out their 10 fiercest warriors. Xue personally engaged them and killed three of them with three arrows, intimidating the Tiele coalition and causing them to submit—but after their surrender, he ordered to kill all the ten and several thousands people. From this incident, a military song was written to praise Xue's ferocity, including the words: "The general is able to pacify <a href="/wiki/Tian_Shan" title="Tian Shan">Tian Shan</a> with three arrows, and the warriors sing their long songs as they enter the boundaries of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Han_Dynasty" title="Han Dynasty">Han</a>." After the army returned to Tang territory, however, Xue was charged with killing those who had already surrendered and seizing the spoils of war and arrested for a time, but was eventually released after Emperor Gaozong ruled that the achievements outweighed the crimes.</p>
<p>In 666, Yeon Gaesomun died, and infighting developed between Yeon's oldest son <a href="/wiki/Yeon_Namsaeng" title="Yeon Namsaeng">Yeon Namsaeng</a>, who succeeded Yeon Gaesomun as <i>Dae Mangniji</i>, and two younger sons <a href="/wiki/Yeon_Namgeon" title="Yeon Namgeon">Yeon Namgeon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yeon_Namsan" title="Yeon Namsan">Yeon Namsan</a>, over suspicions that they had for each other. Eventually, Yeon Namgeon, while Yeon Namsaeng was away from the capital <a href="/wiki/Pyongyang" title="Pyongyang">Pyongyang</a>, claimed the <i>Dae Mangniji</i> title himself. Yeon Namsaeng sent his son (later known as Cheon Heonseong/Quan Xiancheng (泉獻誠), as Yeon (淵) was the same character as Emperor Gaozong's grandfather <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Gaozu_of_Tang" title="Emperor Gaozu of Tang">Emperor Gaozu</a>'s name Yuan and therefore could not be used as part of <a href="/wiki/Naming_taboo" title="Naming taboo">naming taboo</a>) to seek aid from Tang. When Emperor Gaozong sent the generals Pang Tongshan (龐同善) and Gao Kan (高侃) to aid Yeon Namsaeng, Yeon Namgeon tried to intercept them, and Xue, who trailed them, marched forward to aid them, allowing them to defeat Goguryeo forces together. After they captured the cities of Namso (南蘇, in modern <a href="/wiki/Tieling" title="Tieling">Tieling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liaoning" title="Liaoning">Liaoning</a>), Mokjeo (木底, in modern Fushun), and Changam (蒼巖, in modern <a href="/wiki/Benxi" title="Benxi">Benxi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liaoning" title="Liaoning">Liaoning</a>), they were able to rendezvous with Yeon Namsaeng. In spring 668, they further marched east and captured Goguryeo's major northeastern city Buyeo (扶餘, in modern <a href="/wiki/Siping,_Jilin" title="Siping, Jilin">Siping</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jilin" title="Jilin">Jilin</a>), and Xue was described to have marched to the sea (probably <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Japan" title="Sea of Japan">Sea of Japan</a>) and taking some 40 cities in Goguryeo's northeastern territories, before marching southwest to rendezvous with the supreme commander of the entire operation, <a href="/wiki/Li_Shiji" title="Li Shiji">Li Ji</a>, at Pyongyang. After Pyongyang fell later in 668, thus ending Goguryeo, Emperor Gaozong ordered that Goguryeo territory be annexed into Tang territory and that a protector general (known as the <a href="/wiki/Protectorate_General_to_Pacify_the_East" title="Protectorate General to Pacify the East">Protectorate General to Pacify the East</a>) by established at Pyongyang, with Xue appointed as the protector general to defend Pyongyang, along with the general <a href="/wiki/Liu_Rengui" title="Liu Rengui">Liu Rengui</a>. Emperor Gaozong created him the Duke of Pingyang. It was said that Xue was a capable administrator and was, for a while, able to receive allegiance from the people of Goguryeo.</p>
<p>In 670, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tufan" title="Tufan">Tufan</a> launched a major attack on Tang's <a href="/wiki/Western_Regions" title="Western Regions">Western Regions</a> (<i>Xiyu</i>, i.e., formerly Western Tujue) holdings, capturing 18 prefectures. Emperor Gaozong commissioned Xue to command a major counterattack, with Ashina Daozhen (阿史那道真) and Guo Daifeng (郭待封) as his deputies. However, as Guo had been of the same rank as Xue, he viewed serving as Xue's deputy as a dishonor and often disobeyed Xue's orders. Xue's initial strategy was to take some troops ahead toward, with Guo remaining behind at <a href="/wiki/Qinghai_Lake" title="Qinghai Lake">Qinghai Lake</a> with the military supplies—and once Xue had cleared the pass, he would then signal for Guo to proceed. Guo, however, disobeyed the order and did not wait for Xue's signal, but proceeded after Xue departed, and he was intercepted and defeated by a 200,000-men Tufan army. After Guo's army collapsed, Xue was himself attacked by the Tufan prime minister <a href="/wiki/Gar_Trinring_Tsendro" title="Gar Trinring Tsendro">Gar Trinring Tsendro</a> ("Lun Qinling" (論欽陵) in Chinese) and <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Dafei_River" title="Battle of Dafei River">defeated</a>. Nearly the entire army was lost, and Xue was forced to sue for peace under unfavorable terms. Emperor Gaozong sent the official <a href="/wiki/Le_Yanwei" title="Le Yanwei">Le Yanwei</a> to the front to put Xue, Guo, and Ashina under arrest, but released them once they were brought back to the capital <a href="/wiki/Chang%27an" title="Chang'an">Chang'an</a>. However, they were relieved of their posts.</p>
<p>At later time, when the people of Goguryeo were rising in resistance to Tang occupation, Xue was put in charge of pacifying the region, but yet later, probably in 675, Xue was deposed for reasons not clearly stated in historical records and exiled to Xiang Prefecture (象州, roughly modern <a href="/wiki/Laibin" title="Laibin">Laibin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guangxi" title="Guangxi">Guangxi</a>), only allowed to return from exile when a general pardon was declared. In 681, Emperor Gaozong, remembering Xue's contributions, summoned him and again made him a general. In 682, when remnants of the Eastern Tujue, rising under the chiefs <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ashina_Gudulu" title="Ashina Gudulu">Ashina Gudulu</a> and Ashide Yuanzhen (阿史德元珍), declared independence from Tang, Xue was commissioned to attack Ashide Yuanzhen. His presence intimidated the Eastern Tujue soldiers, who had thought that he was long dead, and he scored a major victory over Ashide Yuanzhen.</p>
<p>Xue died in 683. His sons <a href="/wiki/Xue_Ne" title="Xue Ne">Xue Ne</a> and Xue Chuyu (薛楚玉), and several later descendants would serve as generals as well.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_fiction">In fiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xue_Rengui&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In fiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Xue Rengui's life was dramatized in a number of fictional works. The most prominent of these works were the play <i>Xue Rengui's Glorious Return Home</i> (薛仁貴衣錦歸鄉), by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuan_Dynasty" title="Yuan Dynasty">Yuan Dynasty</a> playwright Zhang Guobin (張國賓) and an anonymous novel from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" title="Qing Dynasty">Qing Dynasty</a>, <i>Xue Rengui's Campaign to the East</i> (薛仁貴征東).</p>
<p>His tenure as General of Andong Protectorate following the fall of <a href="/wiki/Goguryeo" title="Goguryeo">Goguryeo</a> has been dramatized in a popular Korean television series called <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dae_Joyeong_(drama)" title="Dae Joyeong (drama)">Dae Joyeong</a></i>, and portrays Xue Rengui as a Tang general who is constantly frustrated by the insurgency of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dongmyeongchun_League&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dongmyeongchun League (page does not exist)">Dongmyeongchun League</a>; remnants of the Goguryeo underground resistance against the Tang.</p>
<p>According to this television action-drama, broadcast worldwide on <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">KBS-1</a>, Xue Rengui could finally claim victory over the Goguryeo insurgency when he had <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dae_Joyoung" title="Dae Joyoung">Dae Joyoung</a> and his legion of escaped <a href="/wiki/Baekje" title="Baekje">Baekje</a>, Goguryeo, <a href="/wiki/Khitan_people" title="Khitan people">Khitan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Silla" title="Silla">Silla</a> prisoners cornered, and had Dae Joyoung vow loyalty to the Tang Empire, and become a military officer of high-rank in the Tang army. However, Xue Rengui is to lose Dae Joyoung again following a stratagem in which Dae Joyoung volunteers to lead a punitive force of Tang soldiers against nomadic bandits in Mount Madu to recover stolen Tang money.</p>
<p>While Dae Joyoung successfully recovers the gold and silver taels and convinces the bandits to fight another group of the Tang punitive force led by the evil Tang general Li Wen, he dispatches the stolen property back to <a href="/wiki/Chang%27an" title="Chang'an">Chang'an</a> alongside his close Dongmyeongchun League confidant <a href="/wiki/Mimosa" title="Mimosa">Mimosa</a> and a small group of Tang soldiers, Dae Joyoung took the opportunity escape the Tang to return to his Goguryeo land on the true-pretext that general Li Wen was going to attack kill him and his Goguryeo subordinates.</p>
<p>This television series also portrays Xue Rengui travelling from Chang'an with only his friend and bodyguard <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hongpei&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hongpei (page does not exist)">Hongpei</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Khitan_people" title="Khitan people">Khitan</a>-held Yingzhou in order meet his old war protege the Khitan Khan <a href="/wiki/Li_Jinzhong" title="Li Jinzhong">Li Jinzhong</a> to personally warn him about general Li Wen's desire to destroy the Khitans in the year 695. But this is apparently a symbolic gesture made by the show's producers, since General Xue Rengui died in 683, to indicate his good and friendly nature and his disdain for political matters.</p>
<p>A "ballad-narrative" (說晿詞話) known as "The story of Xue Rengui crossing the sea and Pacifying Liao" (薛仁貴跨海征遼故事)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> is to written in the <a href="/wiki/Suzhou_dialect" title="Suzhou dialect">Suzhou dialect</a> of <a href="/wiki/Wu_Chinese" title="Wu Chinese">Wu Chinese</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Popular_culture">Popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xue_Rengui&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He also appears famously as a hero in Chinese <a href="/wiki/Folklore" title="Folklore">folklore</a> in which he is the father of the fictional general <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Xue_Dingshan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Xue Dingshan (page does not exist)">Xue Dingshan</a> and the father-in-law of the <a href="/wiki/Turkic_peoples" title="Turkic peoples">Turkic</a> princess <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fan_Lihua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fan Lihua (page does not exist)">Fan Lihua</a>. The story of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Xue_Dingshan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Xue Dingshan (page does not exist)">Xue Dingshan</a> and his wife <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fan_Lihua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fan Lihua (page does not exist)">Fan Lihua</a> is often used as a subject for <a href="/wiki/Chinese_opera" title="Chinese opera">Chinese opera</a>. There is a drama film named <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=How_Xue_Dingshan_Thrice_Angered_Fan_Lihua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="How Xue Dingshan Thrice Angered Fan Lihua (page does not exist)">Xue Dingshan San Qi Fan Lihua</a> about them. Because of his campaigns in Korea against both <a href="/wiki/Goguryeo" title="Goguryeo">Goguryeo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Silla" title="Silla">Silla</a>, he was referred as the "The General who Pacified the East" for the Tang. It is ironic, since Xue's fictional son is referred as the "The General who Pacified the West" for the Tang because of his fictional campaigns against the Turks, however, the campaigns against the Turks should also be attributed to the latter Xue. In the folklore, Xue Rengui was known to have a massive appetite in which he was thought to be possess by the "hungry god." The legend of Xue Dingshan and Fan Lihua takes place between the Tang and the fictional Turkic kingdom of <a href="/wiki/Western_Liang_(Sixteen_Kingdoms)" title="Western Liang (Sixteen Kingdoms)">Western Liang</a>, ironically there was a real <a href="/wiki/Western_Liang_(Sixteen_Kingdoms)" title="Western Liang (Sixteen Kingdoms)">Western Liang</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Sixteen_Kingdoms" title="Sixteen Kingdoms">Sixteen Kingdoms</a> period that was thought to be the ancestors of the Tang.</p>
<p>Xue Rengui also sometimes appears as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Door_god" title="Door god">door god</a> in <a href="/wiki/Chinese_temple" title="Chinese temple">Chinese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taoist_temple" title="Taoist temple">Taoist temples</a> along with the Korean general <a href="/wiki/Yeon_Gaesomun" title="Yeon Gaesomun">Yeon Gaesomun</a>.</p>
