<p>The <b>Church of Saint Simeon Stylites</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">كنيسة مار سمعان العمودي</span>‎‎ <i>Kanīsat Mār Simʿān al-ʿAmūdī</i>) is a historical building located about 30 km (19 mi) northwest of <a href="/wiki/Aleppo" title="Aleppo">Aleppo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>. It is one of the oldest surviving <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine</a> churches, dating back to the 5th century. Built on the site of the pillar of St. <a href="/wiki/Simeon_Stylites" title="Simeon Stylites">Simeon Stylites</a>, a famed <a href="/wiki/Hermit" title="Hermit">hermit</a> monk, the church is popularly known as either Qalaat <a href="/wiki/Semaan" title="Semaan">Semaan</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">قلعة سمعان</span>‎‎ <i>Qalʿat Simʿān</i>), the 'Fortress of Simeon', or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deir_Semaan" title="Deir Semaan">Deir Semaan</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">دير سمعان</span>‎‎ <i>Dayr Simʿān</i>), the 'Monastery of Simeon' .</p>
<p>As of June 2011, the Church and surrounding village was designated by United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (<a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>) as part of the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient_Villages_of_Northern_Syria" title="Ancient Villages of Northern Syria">Ancient Villages of Northern Syria</a>", a World Heritage Site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Church was held by <a href="/wiki/Islamic_extremism" title="Islamic extremism">Islamist extremist</a> groups (including <a href="/wiki/Islamic_State_of_Iraq_and_the_Levant" title="Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant">Islamic State</a>) who are opposed to reverence for and existence of shrines and holy sites, both Muslim and Christian raising concerns for its continued existence. On the 28th of May 2015 the church was captured by the Kurdish <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Protection_Units" title="People's Protection Units">YPG</a>/<a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Protection_Units" title="Women's Protection Units">YPJ</a> with apparently little damage. On 12 May 2016, the church was heavily damaged in an air strike.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
