<p><b>Seonggeon-dong</b> is an administrative <i><a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_South_Korea#Dong_.28Neighborhood.29" title="Administrative divisions of South Korea">dong</a></i> or a <a href="/wiki/Neighbourhood" title="Neighbourhood">neighbourhood</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Gyeongju" title="Subdivisions of Gyeongju">administrative subdivisions</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gyeongju_City" title="Gyeongju City">Gyeongju City</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Gyeongsang" title="North Gyeongsang">North Gyeongsang</a> province, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. It consists of four <i>legal dongs</i> including Seonggeon-dong and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Seokjang-dong&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Seokjang-dong (page does not exist)">Seokjang-dong</a>. It is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Dongcheon-dong,_Gyeongju" title="Dongcheon-dong, Gyeongju">Dongcheon-dong</a> on the east, <a href="/wiki/Geoncheon-eup" title="Geoncheon-eup">Geoncheon-eup</a> on the west and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jungbu-dong" title="Jungbu-dong">Jungbu-dong</a> on the south and <a href="/wiki/Hyeongok-myeon" title="Hyeongok-myeon">Hyeongok-myeon</a> on the north. Its 6.42 square kilometers are home to about 18,219 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Doosan_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Doosan-1">[1]</a></sup> The Gyeongju branch campus of <a href="/wiki/Dongguk_University" title="Dongguk University">Dongguk University</a> is situated in the district and it has an elementary school and a high school<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
