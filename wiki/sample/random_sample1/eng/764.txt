<p><b><i>Cheonghaejin</i> Class ASR</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">청해진급 잠수함 구조함</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: <span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">淸海鎭級潛水艦救助艦</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Submarine_rescue_ship" title="Submarine rescue ship">Submarine rescue ship</a> class of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea_Navy" title="Republic of Korea Navy">Republic of Korea Navy</a>. Only one ship has been built in her class, the ROKS <i>Cheonghaejin</i> (ASR 21), in 1995. Its operations include rescuing trapped sailors in submarines, naval operation support for submarines, underwater research and mapping support, and recovery of sunk vessels. It is equipped with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deep_Submergence_Rescue_Vehicle" title="Deep Submergence Rescue Vehicle">Deep Submergence Rescue Vehicle</a> (DSRV) that operates up to 500 meters, and a rescue chamber that holds up to 9 people.</p>
<p>Once all 9 <a href="/wiki/Type_214_submarine" title="Type 214 submarine"><i>Son Wonil</i> class</a> submarines are delivered to the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea_Navy" title="Republic of Korea Navy">Republic of Korea Navy</a>, another ASR ship of the same class is planned to be built.</p>
<ul>
<li>Notable Operations
<ul>
<li>salvaged a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yugo_class_submarine" title="Yugo class submarine">Yugo class</a> North Korean submarine in 1998</li>
<li>salvaged <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chamsuri_class_patrol_boat" title="Chamsuri class patrol boat">Chamsuri class patrol boat</a> #357 that was sunk during the <a href="/wiki/Second_Battle_of_Yeonpyeong" title="Second Battle of Yeonpyeong">naval clash</a> in 2002 with North Korea</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
