<p><b>Sung Min</b> (also <i>Seong Min</i>, <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">성 민</span>; born October 15, 1982) is a South Korean former swimmer, who specialized in freestyle and backstroke events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He represented his nation <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> in three editions of the Olympic Games (2000 to 2008), and held multiple national championship titles and swimming records in the relay freestyle and backstroke events (50, 100, and 200 m). Sung had also won a total of five bronze medals, including one from the 50 m backstroke, at the <a href="/wiki/2002_Asian_Games" title="2002 Asian Games">2002 Asian Games</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Busan,_South_Korea" title="Busan, South Korea">Busan, South Korea</a>, and at the <a href="/wiki/2006_Asian_Games" title="2006 Asian Games">2006 Asian Games</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doha,_Qatar" title="Doha, Qatar">Doha, Qatar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sm-busan_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-sm-busan-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sung_Min_(swimmer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sung's Olympic debut came as a seventeen-year-old teen at the <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">2000 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>. There, he posted a lifetime best of 57.12 to lead the second heat of <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_100_metre_backstroke" title="Swimming at the 2000 Summer Olympics – Men's 100 metre backstroke">men's 100 m backstroke</a> by exactly one second ahead of Uruguay's <a href="/wiki/Diego_Gallo" title="Diego Gallo">Diego Gallo</a>, but finished only in thirty-first place from the prelims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Two years later, Sung won two bronze medals, as a member of the South Korean swimming team, in the men's 400 m freestyle and medley relay at the <a href="/wiki/2002_Asian_Games" title="2002 Asian Games">2002 Asian Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">Busan</a>, with a total time of 3:23.58 and 3:46.44, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sm-busan_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-sm-busan-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On his second Olympic appearance in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athens_2004" title="Athens 2004">Athens 2004</a>, Sung failed to reach the top 16 in any of his individual events, finishing thirtieth in the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_100_metre_backstroke" title="Swimming at the 2004 Summer Olympics – Men's 100 metre backstroke">100 m backstroke</a> (56.78), and thirty-second in the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_200_metre_backstroke" title="Swimming at the 2004 Summer Olympics – Men's 200 metre backstroke">200 m backstroke</a> (2:04.86).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2005_Summer_Universiade" title="2005 Summer Universiade">2005 Summer Universiade</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Izmir,_Turkey" title="Izmir, Turkey">Izmir, Turkey</a>, Sung defeated Japan's Masafumi Yamaguchi and United States' <a href="/wiki/Matt_Grevers" title="Matt Grevers">Matt Grevers</a> to earn a silver medal by two hundredths of a second (0.02) in the 50 m backstroke, posting a lifetime best of 25.59.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The following year, he snared the bronze medal in the same event at the <a href="/wiki/2006_Asian_Games" title="2006 Asian Games">2006 Asian Games</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doha,_Qatar" title="Doha, Qatar">Doha, Qatar</a>, lowering his time at 25.57 seconds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Eight years after competing in his first Olympics, Sung qualified for his third South Korean team, as a 25-year-old, at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>. He broke a new South Korean record on a bodysuit and cleared a FINA B-cut time of 55.43 (100 m backstroke) from the Good Luck Beijing China Open.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_100_metre_backstroke" title="Swimming at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's 100 metre backstroke">100 m backstroke</a>, Sung challenged seven other swimmers on the third heat, including five-time Olympian <a href="/wiki/Derya_B%C3%BCy%C3%BCkuncu" title="Derya Büyükuncu">Derya Büyükuncu</a> of Turkey. He finished ahead of France's <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Stasiulis" title="Benjamin Stasiulis">Benjamin Stasiulis</a> in fourth place by a nine hundredth (0.09) margin, lowering his Olympic time to 54.99 seconds. Sung failed to advance into the semifinals, as he placed twenty-third out of 45 swimmers in the evening preliminaries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sung_Min_(swimmer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Cerritos,_California" title="Cerritos, California">Cerritos, California</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, Sung holds a dual citizenship to train and compete internationally for <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> in swimming. He is now a founder and coach for age group swimmers at the Rapids Swim Club in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sung-min-us_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-sung-min-us-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
