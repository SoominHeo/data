<p><b>Associazione Sportiva Livorno Calcio</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Football_in_Italy" title="Football in Italy">Italian football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Livorno" title="Livorno">Livorno</a>, Tuscany. The club was formed in 1915 and currently plays in <a href="/wiki/Lega_Pro" title="Lega Pro">Lega Pro</a>. The team's colours are dark red or maroon (<i>amaranto</i> in Italian, from which the team nickname derives). The best placement in Italian Serie A was second place in 1942–43 season, during which the <i>amaranto</i> gave life to a head-to-head competition with <a href="/wiki/Torino_F.C." title="Torino F.C.">Torino</a>. Livorno Calcio play their home matches at the 19,238 seater <a href="/wiki/Stadio_Armando_Picchi" title="Stadio Armando Picchi">Stadio Armando Picchi</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A.S._Livorno_Calcio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Founded February 15, 1915 as <b>US Livorno</b>, the club ended the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_Football_Championship_1919%E2%80%9320" title="Italian Football Championship 1919–20">Italian Football Championship 1919–20</a> in second place, losing the final to <a href="/wiki/Inter_Milan" title="Inter Milan">Internazionale</a>. One year later, they were defeated in the semi-final by arch-rivals <a href="/wiki/A.C._Pisa_1909" title="A.C. Pisa 1909">Pisa</a>. In 1933, the club moved to the current stadium, originally named after <a href="/wiki/Edda_Mussolini" title="Edda Mussolini">Edda Ciano Mussolini</a>, daughter of fascist dictator <a href="/wiki/Benito_Mussolini" title="Benito Mussolini">Benito Mussolini</a>. Livorno was one of original Serie A teams. She played in top level during periods of 1929–31, 1933–35 and 1937–39. Successively, Livorno ended as Serie A runners-up in the <a href="/wiki/1942%E2%80%9343_Serie_A" title="1942–43 Serie A">1942–43 season</a>. Livorno left Serie A in 1949 after seven consecutive seasons. They relegated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C" title="Serie C">Serie C</a> soon after (1951–52 season), making a return to Serie B in 1955 for a single season and again from 1964 to 1972. They relegated to Serie C2 in 1982–83 and played again in third level between 1984 and 1989. The club was then cancelled in 1991, being forced to start from <a href="/wiki/Eccellenza" title="Eccellenza">Eccellenza</a>; two consecutive promotions led the team back to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C2" title="Serie C2">Serie C2</a>. The club was promoted to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C1" title="Serie C1">Serie C1</a> in 1997 and was acquired by Aldo Spinelli two years later. Under the new property, Livorno returned to Serie B in 2001.</p>
<p>Livorno were promoted to Serie A after finishing third in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_B_2003%E2%80%9304" title="Serie B 2003–04">Serie B 2003–04</a>, one of six clubs to be promoted that season. It had been 55 years since Livorno's last season in the top flight, and as a result of this, most were predicting an instant return to Serie B for the club. The first match in the major league was attended by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_President" title="Italian President">Italian President</a> <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Azeglio_Ciampi" title="Carlo Azeglio Ciampi">Carlo Azeglio Ciampi</a>, a Livorno's citizen and team supporter in his childhood. There were spells of struggle during the season, but there were many more good performances shown, and Livorno finished a surprise and creditable ninth place in the league for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_A_2004%E2%80%9305" title="Serie A 2004–05">Serie A 2004–05</a>, also thanks to goals by striker <a href="/wiki/Cristiano_Lucarelli" title="Cristiano Lucarelli">Cristiano Lucarelli</a>, who won the Serie A top scorer award that season, outscoring even the likes of <a href="/wiki/Andriy_Shevchenko" title="Andriy Shevchenko">Andriy Shevchenko</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adriano_Leite_Ribeiro" title="Adriano Leite Ribeiro">Adriano</a>.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_A_2005%E2%80%9306" title="Serie A 2005–06">Serie A 2005–06</a> saw Livorno in sixth place after the first half of the season the team, being involved for qualification to the next <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a>. However, shortly after <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Donadoni" title="Roberto Donadoni">Roberto Donadoni</a> announced his resignations after having been criticised by club's chairman Aldo Spinelli. Donadoni was replaced by veteran coach <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Mazzone" title="Carlo Mazzone">Carlo Mazzone</a>, who was only able to save a UEFA Cup place due to the expulsion of three teams from Europe in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_A_scandal_of_2006" title="Serie A scandal of 2006">2006 Serie A match fixing scandal</a>. Mazzone then saw his team suffer a run of seven straight defeats. In May 2006, <a href="/wiki/Daniele_Arrigoni" title="Daniele Arrigoni">Daniele Arrigoni</a> was appointed new coach for the next season.</p>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_A_2006%E2%80%9307" title="Serie A 2006–07">Serie A 2006–07</a> season, Livorno took part to the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_UEFA_Cup" title="2006–07 UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> for their first time ever. The Tuscan side were drawn to face Austrian team <a href="/wiki/ASK%C3%96_Pasching" title="ASKÖ Pasching">SV Pasching</a> in the first round, beating them comfortably 3–0 on aggregate. They thus qualified for the group stages being drawn in Group A, along with <a href="/wiki/Rangers_F.C." title="Rangers F.C.">Rangers</a>, <a href="/wiki/AJ_Auxerre" title="AJ Auxerre">Auxerre</a>, <a href="/wiki/FK_Partizan" title="FK Partizan">FK Partizan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Maccabi_Haifa_F.C." title="Maccabi Haifa F.C.">Maccabi Haifa</a>. After a home loss to Rangers (2–3) and two 1–1 draws against Partizan in <a href="/wiki/Belgrade" title="Belgrade">Belgrade</a> (where <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">goalkeeper</a> <a href="/wiki/Marco_Amelia" title="Marco Amelia">Marco Amelia</a> scored in the 87th minute) and Maccabi (in Livorno), the Tuscan side gained a 1–0 victory over Auxerre in the last game played in France, thus earning a spot in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Round_of_32" title="Round of 32">Round of 32</a> of the competition. However, Spanish team <a href="/wiki/RCD_Espanyol" title="RCD Espanyol">Espanyol</a> knocked out Livorno from the UEFA Cup by winning 4–1 on aggregate.</p>
<p>After day 19 of the Italian Serie A, Arrigoni was sacked by chairman Spinelli, but his position was kept due to the strong opposition by the team. His dismissal was, however, only delayed, as Arrigoni was eventually fired on 21 March 2007, and replaced by <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Orsi" title="Fernando Orsi">Fernando Orsi</a>, who managed to keep the team away from the relegation battle. For the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_A_2007-08" title="Serie A 2007-08">2007–08 campaign</a>, Orsi was confirmed as head coach and a number of notable signings such as <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Tavano" title="Francesco Tavano">Francesco Tavano</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diego_Tristan" title="Diego Tristan">Diego Tristan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vikash_Dhorasoo" title="Vikash Dhorasoo">Vikash Dhorasoo</a> were finalised, but also the transfer of Lucarelli to <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukrainian</a> club <a href="/wiki/FC_Shakhtar_Donetsk" title="FC Shakhtar Donetsk">Shakhtar Donetsk</a>. The club, however, did not start well, making a mere two points in the first seven matches, and Orsi was sacked on 9 October and replaced by <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Camolese" title="Giancarlo Camolese">Giancarlo Camolese</a>. Despite showing some positive signals at the beginning, Livorno found himself again at the bottom of league table. On 28 April 2008 Camolese was fired as Orsi was re-appointed, but in the penultimate day of the season, the team could not avoid relegation, due to a 1–0 home defeat against <a href="/wiki/Torino_F.C." title="Torino F.C.">Torino</a>. They finished last in the Serie A standings of the 2007–08 season. Thus, being relegated to Serie B. They finished Serie B as the third place team in 2008–09 season and returned to Serie A after winning promotion play-offs after defeating successively <a href="/wiki/F.C._Grosseto_S.S.D." title="F.C. Grosseto S.S.D.">Grosseto</a> with a 4–3 aggregate score and <a href="/wiki/Brescia_Calcio" title="Brescia Calcio">Brescia</a> with a 5–2 aggregate score. However, this return was short-lived and one season later they relegated again to Serie B after finishing last. Livorno were promoted again after they beat <a href="/wiki/Empoli_F.C." title="Empoli F.C.">Empoli</a> 2–1 on aggregate to get the Serie A promotion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014 Livorno relegated back to Serie B again. The club also sold flagship striker <a href="/wiki/Paulinho_(footballer,_born_January_1986)" title="Paulinho (footballer, born January 1986)">Paulinho</a> for €8 million fee,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> in order to cover the net loss. In 2016 Livorno relegated again to Lega Pro.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Supporters">Supporters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A.S._Livorno_Calcio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Supporters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:20%; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>No Serie A club's supporters wear their political allegiance more boldly than Livorno's, whose leanings are strongly to the left, the city of Livorno being the birthplace of Italy's Communist party.</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;"> <i><a href="/wiki/The_National_(Abu_Dhabi)" title="The National (Abu Dhabi)">The National</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></cite></div>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Stadio_Armando_Picchi_3.jpg"><img alt="Stadio Armando Picchi, Livorno, Italy – (Livorno – Udinese)" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Stadio_Armando_Picchi_3.jpg/220px-Stadio_Armando_Picchi_3.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Stadio_Armando_Picchi_3.jpg/330px-Stadio_Armando_Picchi_3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Stadio_Armando_Picchi_3.jpg/440px-Stadio_Armando_Picchi_3.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Stadio_Armando_Picchi_3.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AS_Livorno" title="AS Livorno">AS Livorno</a> supporters during a match against <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Livorno's supporters are well known for their <a href="/wiki/Left-wing_politics" title="Left-wing politics">left-wing politics</a> which often spark fiercely violent clashes with opposing right-wing supporter groups, especially those of <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Roma</a>, <a href="/wiki/S.S._Lazio" title="S.S. Lazio">Lazio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inter_Milan" title="Inter Milan">Inter Milan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hellas_Verona_F.C." title="Hellas Verona F.C.">Verona</a>. The notorious right-wing Lazio striker <a href="/wiki/Paolo_Di_Canio" title="Paolo Di Canio">Paolo Di Canio</a> once made a fascist salute to his own fans during a match against Livorno, when tensions were running high between the two clubs' <i><a href="/wiki/Ultras" title="Ultras">ultra</a></i> groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Times_Online_2005-04-03_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Times_Online_2005-04-03-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since 2005, a group of migrant Livorno supporters resident in northern Europe have styled themselves <i>Partigiani Livornesi Scandinavia</i> (Livornian partisans of Scandinavia). A so-called "triangle of brotherhood" has developed between the most heavily supported left-wing fan clubs of <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Marseille</a>, Livorno, and <a href="/wiki/AEK_Athens_F.C." title="AEK Athens F.C.">AEK Athens</a>, namely between Commando Ultras 84, Brigate Autonome Livornesi 99, and Original 21. Their connection is mostly an ideological one. They also have a connection with <a href="/wiki/Adana_Demirspor" title="Adana Demirspor">Adana Demirspor</a> (Şimşekler), <a href="/wiki/Celtic_F.C." title="Celtic F.C.">Celtic</a> and <a href="/wiki/AC_Omonia" title="AC Omonia">Omonoia</a> of Nicosia. The team's friendly game in September 2009 with Adana Demirspor created a leftist rally in the city of Adana, Turkey.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
