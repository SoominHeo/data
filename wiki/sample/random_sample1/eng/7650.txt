<p><b>Holy Hour</b> is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> devotional tradition of spending an hour in <a href="/wiki/Eucharistic_adoration" title="Eucharistic adoration">Eucharistic adoration</a> in the presence of the <a href="/wiki/Blessed_Sacrament" title="Blessed Sacrament">Blessed Sacrament</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> A plenary <a href="/wiki/Indulgence" title="Indulgence">indulgence</a> is granted for this practice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The practice is also observed in many Anglican churches.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holy_Hour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1673 Saint <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Mary_Alacoque" title="Margaret Mary Alacoque">Margaret Mary Alacoque</a> stated that she had a <a href="/wiki/Visions_of_Jesus_and_Mary" title="Visions of Jesus and Mary">vision of Jesus</a> in which she was instructed to spend an hour every Thursday night to meditate on the sufferings of Jesus in the <a href="/wiki/Agony_in_the_Garden" title="Agony in the Garden">Garden of Gethsemane</a>. This practice later became widespread among Roman Catholics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The inspiration for the Holy Hour is <a class="external text" href="http://www.biblica.com/en-us/bible/online-bible/?osis=niv:Matthew.26:40–26:40" rel="nofollow">Matthew 26:40</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stravinskas_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stravinskas-6">[6]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Gospel_of_Matthew" title="Gospel of Matthew">Gospel of Matthew</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/Agony_in_the_Garden" title="Agony in the Garden">agony in the Garden of Gethsemane</a> the night before his crucifixion, Jesus spoke to his disciples, saying "My soul is sorrowful even to death. Remain here and keep watch with me." (Matthew 26:38) Returning to the disciples after prayer, he found them asleep and in Matthew 26:40 he asked Peter:</p>
<dl>
<dd>"So, could you men not keep watch with me for an hour?".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stravinskas_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stravinskas-6">[6]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<p>In 1829, the <i>Archconfraternity of the Holy Hour</i> was established by Père Robert Debrosse at <a href="/wiki/Paray-le-Monial" title="Paray-le-Monial">Paray-le-Monial</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burgundy" title="Burgundy">Burgundy</a>, France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 1911 it received the right of aggregation for the entire world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> A similar society called "The Holy Perpetual Hour of Gethsemani" was formed in <a href="/wiki/Toulouse" title="Toulouse">Toulouse</a> in 1885 and was canonically erected in 1907. In 1909 it received indulgences from Pope <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pius_X" title="Pius X">Pius X</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The practice of a "daily Holy Hour" has been encouraged in the Catholic tradition, for instance <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mother_Teresa_of_Calcutta" title="Mother Teresa of Calcutta">Mother Teresa of Calcutta</a> had a Holy Hour each day and all members of her <a href="/wiki/Missionaries_of_Charity" title="Missionaries of Charity">Missionaries of Charity</a> followed her example.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The Holy Hour has been practiced by saints such as <a href="/wiki/Gemma_Galgani" title="Gemma Galgani">Gemma Galgani</a> and <a href="/wiki/Faustina_Kowalska" title="Faustina Kowalska">Faustina Kowalska</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
