<p><b>Sungshin Women’s University</b> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: 성신여자대학교) is a private women’s university located in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. It was founded in 1936 by Dr. Sook-Chong Lee. During the 1960s and 70s, Sungshin was well known as a prestigious Teachers College in South Korea. Then, in the 1980s, the college was promoted to the status of a comprehensive university. Today, the university comprises ten colleges and five graduate schools with total enrollment of about 12,000 students. In 2006, the university rebuilt the <i>Sungshin Hall</i> to mark the university's 70th anniversary. Also, Sungshin Women's University has succeeded to annex the nursing college of a national hospital, the first event of its kind to happen in South Korea.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Admissions">Admissions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sungshin_Women%27s_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Admissions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sungshin_image_05.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="533" data-file-width="800" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Sungshin_image_05.jpg/220px-Sungshin_image_05.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Sungshin_image_05.jpg/330px-Sungshin_image_05.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Sungshin_image_05.jpg/440px-Sungshin_image_05.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

Sungshin Women's University</div>

<p>Admission to Sungshin Women's University is very competitive, with an acceptance rate of less than 5%. Most of the students who enroll at Sungshin are ranked in the top 10% of their graduating class in high school. Also, students are admitted by their specified major instead of into a general freshman pool. Some of the most popular majors offered at Sungshin are psychology, nursing and consumer science &amp; living culture.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campus">Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sungshin_Women%27s_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sungshin Women’s University features newly renovated lecture halls and internal facilities. The campus is equipped with diverse student facilities: bookstore, stationery store, bank, post office, and cafeteria. Also, students are offered convenient one-stop services.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_Campus_.28Woonjung_Green_Campus.29">Second Campus (Woonjung Green Campus)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sungshin_Women%27s_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Second Campus (Woonjung Green Campus)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sungshin has accomplished something all other universities hope to accomplish: the establishment of a second campus in Seoul. The 54,200㎡ Woonjung Green Campus is within 5 kilometers of the Soojung Campus and makes Sungshin the only university with two campuses in Seoul.</p>
<p>The Woonjung Green Campus, with three college buildings and one shared facilities building being surrounded by a vast green area, is designed as an eco-friendly campus with global and futuristic vision. It opened in March 2010 and houses four colleges: College of Natural Sciences, College of Human Ecology, College of Nursing, and College of Convergence Culture and Arts. Woonjung Green Campus, with its organic design to facilitate research experiments and researcher-learner interactions, will brighten Sungshin's future as the training grounds for expert talents.</p>
<p>Woonjung Green campus is a new leap forward for Sungshin Women's University. It will be used as a place of nurturing women with talents demanded in the information society, as a forum for creative education and research, as driving force to build competitive edge for the new Specialization Program, and as foundation for a leap toward becoming one of the world's best private universities. In 2010, Woonjung Campus succeeded the dreams and hopes of the Sungshin community that began in 1936 at the Donam (Soojung) Campus and expands them.</p>
<p>Furthermore, on the lower slopes of Mt. Dobong, the school has built a compound of alpine-style cottages offering all the facilities and amenities where anyone in the Sungshin family can enjoy the beauty of nature as they gather for school and departmental events. The entire setting provides stunning natural views and comfortable lodging and complete kitchen facilities. This is to ensure that the students can enjoy their outdoor activities in a safe and relaxing environment.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sungshin_Global_Leaders_Program">Sungshin Global Leaders Program</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sungshin_Women%27s_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Sungshin Global Leaders Program">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sungshin Women’s University operates a Global Leaders Program to develop their unique abilities and personal qualities. Sponsored by the school's Korean Women's Institute, this program enables the participants to cultivate the knowledge fundamental to attaining the role of being a leader. This program has been held at the Nan-hyang Cottage in an overnight session twice a year since 2011.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_Elite_Training_Course">International Elite Training Course</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sungshin_Women%27s_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: International Elite Training Course">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sungshin Women’s University seeks to develop women with expert foreign language capabilities through its International Elite Training Course. This program was inaugurated in 1994 and selects 120 students from the incoming class and other undergraduates providing them with English courses. The group is organized into classes of ten students, who study for two years in small classes to improve their language ability.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_and_Welfare_Specialization">Health and Welfare Specialization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sungshin_Women%27s_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Health and Welfare Specialization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The university plans to produce experts in the areas of elderly care, health management, and stress management at the undergraduate level. It will actively support research on various therapeutic methods, such as art therapy, consultation treatment, and rehabilitation, at the graduate level. Both concentration areas have been selected with consideration to today's aging society and the resulting importance of family welfare.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_Specialization">Culture Specialization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sungshin_Women%27s_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Culture Specialization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The university will establish various research and educational programs with an objective of educating “Sungshin Intellectuals” for the future. All Sungshin students must take general education courses in the Creative Intellectuals Training Curriculum and can participate in programs like Culture Supporters and Culture Ambassadors.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sungshin_2015_Strategic_Development_Plan">Sungshin 2015 Strategic Development Plan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sungshin_Women%27s_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Sungshin 2015 Strategic Development Plan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>The university has established a strategic development plan with help from Samsung Economic Research Institute (SERI) to strengthen its competitiveness under rapidly changing internal and external conditions in the age of intense competition. With supplying the best liberal arts education that brings out the best from the students as our mission, the university will run short- and mid-term programs through 2015 that will help educate sophisticated Sungshin intellectuals with expertise, creativity and courage. For this Sungshin identified seven areas of improvement, including the four core areas of specializations, department and enrollment restructuring, academic affairs, and general education curriculum, and is working on planning and implementation.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Integration_of_Departments_and_Enrollment_Restructuring">Integration of Departments and Enrollment Restructuring</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sungshin_Women%27s_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Integration of Departments and Enrollment Restructuring">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The university integrated redundant departments that are similar to one another to improve academic competitiveness and to maximize investment efficiency. The Department of Athletics and the Department of Leisure Sports became the Department of Sports and Leisure. The School of Computer Information and the School of Media Information were integrated into the School of Information Technology. Evening departments and their classes will be discontinued and the enrollment for the departments will be adjusted after evaluation. The Department of Law becomes the College of Law with increased enrollment.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="College_and_Department_Realignment">College and Department Realignment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sungshin_Women%27s_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: College and Department Realignment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>To go with the health and welfare specialization project, the university decided to concentrate related major programs and departments in one college. As a result, the College of Human Ecology welcomed the new departments of Sports and Leisure and Welfare to its roster.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="English_Competency_and_Graduation_Requirements">English Competency and Graduation Requirements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sungshin_Women%27s_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: English Competency and Graduation Requirements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Each department or major program will establish its own mandatory list of major courses to improve the competency level of its graduates. The number of major courses required for graduation will be increased and the same standard will be applied for the double major program. In addition, certification programs will be introduced for graduation and for English competency.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Comprehensive_Academic_Advisory_System">Comprehensive Academic Advisory System</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sungshin_Women%27s_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Comprehensive Academic Advisory System">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The university has employed a new, comprehensive academic advisory system to help in academic and career advising. One faculty advisor will be assigned to about 15 first-year students. Also, 15 best faculty advisors will be assigned to 10 Open Major Division students each.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bachelor.E2.80.99s.2FMaster.E2.80.99s_Degree_Program">Bachelor’s/Master’s Degree Program</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sungshin_Women%27s_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Bachelor’s/Master’s Degree Program">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The university established a combined bachelor’s/master’s degree program so that students can receive both the bachelor's and the master's degree in just five years: seven semesters for Bachelor’s and three for Master’s.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="General_Education_Curriculum">General Education Curriculum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sungshin_Women%27s_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: General Education Curriculum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Training_of_.E2.80.9CSungshin_Intellectuals.E2.80.9D">Training of “Sungshin Intellectuals”</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sungshin_Women%27s_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Training of “Sungshin Intellectuals”">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The university overhauled its General Education Curriculum to properly educate “Sungshin Intellectuals” who will become the faces of the university. The Office of General Education was established to oversee the General Education program and the students in the Open Major Division.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Strengthening_of_General_Education_Curriculum">Strengthening of General Education Curriculum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sungshin_Women%27s_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Strengthening of General Education Curriculum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Office of General Education administers the General Education Curriculum with mandatory and foundation courses. Among the mandatory General Education courses are First-Year Seminar, Foundation of Thinking, English, Sungshin Leadership and Sungshin Culture. These courses are specifically designed to enhance analytic ability, critical thinking, expressivity, and global citizenship qualities. The foundation courses are offered in four domains of Humanities, Social Science, Natural Science, and Art, and they are often interdisciplinary.</p>
