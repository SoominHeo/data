<p><b>Sung Yu-ri</b> (born March 3, 1981) is a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a>-born <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a>. She made her entertainment debut in 1998 as a member of the now-defunct <a href="/wiki/K-pop" title="K-pop">K-pop</a> group <a href="/wiki/Fin.K.L" title="Fin.K.L">Fin.K.L</a>. Sung turned to acting in 2002, starring in <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">television dramas</a> such as <i><a href="/wiki/Thousand_Years_of_Love" title="Thousand Years of Love">Thousand Years of Love</a></i> (2003), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Snow_Queen_(TV_series)" title="The Snow Queen (TV series)">The Snow Queen</a></i> (2006), <i><a href="/wiki/Hong_Gil-dong_(TV_series)" title="Hong Gil-dong (TV series)">Hong Gil-dong</a></i> (2008), and <i><a href="/wiki/Feast_of_the_Gods_(TV_series)" title="Feast of the Gods (TV series)">Feast of the Gods</a></i> (2010).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sung_Yu-ri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sung Yu-ri was born in 1981 in <a href="/wiki/T%C3%BCbingen" title="Tübingen">Tübingen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Württemberg</a> (in then-<a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a>). Her father Sung Jong-hyun, a prominent professor of theology at the Presbyterian College and Theological Seminary, was studying at the time in West Germany. Sung's family returned to <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> when she was four years old and she grew up in <a href="/wiki/Gangdong_District" title="Gangdong District">Gangdong District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>. She attended Myung Elementary and Middle School, Kwang Nam High School and graduated from Kyung Hee University with Post Modern Music Major in 2005.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sung_Yu-ri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When Sung was a high school student, she began her entertainment career in 1998 as the youngest member of the four-member <a href="/wiki/K-pop" title="K-pop">K-pop</a> <a href="/wiki/Girl_group" title="Girl group">girl group</a> <a href="/wiki/Fin.K.L" title="Fin.K.L">Fin.K.L</a> (which stood for "Fine Killing Liberty"), one of the very first <a href="/wiki/Korean_pop_idol" title="Korean pop idol">Korean idol</a> groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Fin.K.L quickly rose in popularity, releasing four albums (<i>Blue Rain</i> (1998), <i>White</i> (1999), <i>Now</i> (2001), <i>Eternity</i> (2002)), and various <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">singles</a>, live concert albums and <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">compilation albums</a>. But after the release of their fourth album, Sung and fellow band members <a href="/wiki/Ock_Joo-hyun" title="Ock Joo-hyun">Ock Joo-hyun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Hyori" title="Lee Hyori">Lee Hyori</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lee_Jin" title="Lee Jin">Lee Jin</a> began doing solo activities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sung made her acting debut in 2002 with <i>Bad Girls</i>, and a year later was cast in her first leading role in <i><a href="/wiki/Thousand_Years_of_Love" title="Thousand Years of Love">Thousand Years of Love</a></i> (2003). Sung learned horseback riding and martial arts in her role as a <a href="/wiki/Baekje" title="Baekje">Baekje</a> princess who <a href="/wiki/Time_travel" title="Time travel">time travels</a> to modern-day Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> This was followed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romantic_comedy" title="Romantic comedy">romantic comedy</a> <i><a href="/wiki/First_Love_of_a_Royal_Prince" title="First Love of a Royal Prince">First Love of a Royal Prince</a></i> (2004), partly shot in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bali" title="Bali">Bali</a>, in which Sung's fun-loving sandwich shop delivery girl was a departure from her previous feminine characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> During this time, she was harshly criticized for her acting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rabbit_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-rabbit-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-true_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-true-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sung graduated from <a href="/wiki/Kyung_Hee_University" title="Kyung Hee University">Kyung Hee University</a> in February 2005 with a degree in Theater and Film; she also received an Achievement Award for promoting her <a href="/wiki/Alma_mater" title="Alma mater">alma mater</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> After a brief return to music via Fin.K.L's <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital</a> single <i>Corealism</i> in late 2005, she decided to focus solely on her acting career. In line with this, all the Fin.K.L members left their agency <a href="/wiki/DSP_Media" title="DSP Media">DSP Entertainment</a>, and Sung joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SidusHQ" title="SidusHQ">SidusHQ</a> in June 2005. In 2006, she starred in <i><a href="/wiki/One_Fine_Day_(TV_series)" title="One Fine Day (TV series)">One Fine Day</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Snow_Queen_(TV_series)" title="The Snow Queen (TV series)">The Snow Queen</a></i>, playing, respectively, an orphan adopted by a wealthy but suffocating family, and a cold-hearted heiress with an incurable disease.</p>
<p>But it was fusion-<a href="/wiki/Historical_period_drama" title="Historical period drama">period</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dramedy" title="Dramedy">dramedy</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Hong_Gil-dong_(TV_series)" title="Hong Gil-dong (TV series)">Hong Gil-dong</a></i> in 2008 that changed how the industry perceived Sung as an actress. The series' screenwriters, the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Sisters" title="Hong Sisters">Hong Sisters</a> were initially criticized for casting her, but they defended Sung, and her portrayal of a tomboyish, vanity-free outlaw drew a positive reception from audiences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sung then played an aspiring show director who joins the <a href="/wiki/Cirque_du_Soleil" title="Cirque du Soleil">Cirque du Soleil</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Swallow_the_Sun_(TV_series)" title="Swallow the Sun (TV series)">Swallow the Sun</a></i>, a 2009 big-budget <a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action</a>-romance series with overseas location shoots in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> That same year, she also appeared in her first big-screen starring role as a Korean-American adoptee who returns to her native country to search for her biological mother in <i>Maybe</i> (titled <i>Rabbit and Lizard</i> in <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rabbit_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-rabbit-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Sung left SidusHQ and transferred to <a href="/wiki/King_Kong_Entertainment" title="King Kong Entertainment">King Kong Entertainment</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> She then starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Romance_Town" title="Romance Town">Romance Town</a></i>, in the role of a maid who wins ₩14 billion in the lottery and keeps it a secret from her boss and fellow household help.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> A more traditional <a href="/wiki/Melodrama" title="Melodrama">melodrama</a> followed with <i><a href="/wiki/Feast_of_the_Gods_(TV_series)" title="Feast of the Gods (TV series)">Feast of the Gods</a></i> (2012), about two rival female chefs of <a href="/wiki/Korean_royal_court_cuisine" title="Korean royal court cuisine">Korean royal court cuisine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-true_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-true-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Afterwards, Sung reunited onscreen with previous <i>Hong Gil-dong</i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Kang_Ji-hwan" title="Kang Ji-hwan">Kang Ji-hwan</a> in the action-comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Runway_Cop" title="Runway Cop">Runway Cop</a></i>, which revolves around a detective who goes undercover as a fashion model for a drug case.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, she played a genius with <a href="/wiki/Psychogenic_amnesia" title="Psychogenic amnesia">psychogenic amnesia</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Secret_of_Birth" title="The Secret of Birth">The Secret of Birth</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The low-budget <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">indie</a> <i>A Boy's Sister</i> was then released, in which Sung played the titular character who's grieving after her brother's death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Later that year, she replaced <a href="/wiki/Han_Hye-jin" title="Han Hye-jin">Han Hye-jin</a> as one of the hosts of talk show <i><a href="/wiki/Healing_Camp,_Aren%27t_You_Happy" title="Healing Camp, Aren't You Happy">Healing Camp, Aren't You Happy</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>When Sung's contract with King Kong Entertainment expired in January 2014, she signed with <a href="/wiki/Fantagio" title="Fantagio">Fantagio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> She then appeared for free in the <a href="/wiki/Short_film" title="Short film">short film</a> <i>Chorogi and the Stalker Guy</i> for the E-Cut for the Directors project of the 6th Seoul International Extreme-Short Image &amp; Film Festival (SESIFF).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Sung next played a diva actress who unexpectedly falls for her loyal manager in the 2015 <a href="/wiki/Anthology_film" title="Anthology film">omnibus</a> <i>Summer Snow</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>Actress Sung Yu-ri on Monday signed an exclusive contract with SL Entertainment.(April 20, 2015)</p>
