<p><b>Sung Ji-hyun</b> (born July 29, 1991) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> badminton player from <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gz2010_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-gz2010-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-profile-2">[2]</a></sup> She competed at the 2010 and 2014 Asian Games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sung_Ji-hyun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sung went into <a href="/wiki/Badminton" title="Badminton">badminton</a> following the path of her parents <a href="/wiki/Sung_Han-kook" title="Sung Han-kook">Sung Han-kook</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kim_Yun-ja" title="Kim Yun-ja">Kim Yun-ja</a> who both competed internationally in the 1980s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sung_Ji-hyun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She won the Korea Grand Prix Gold title in 2011. Unlike most South Korean badminton players Sung has focused on singles and in December 2011 she received a career high ranking of number seven in the world in that discipline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-profile-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, Sung's performance at Super Series was progressingly better, with semi final performance in <a href="/wiki/Indonesia_Open_(badminton)" title="Indonesia Open (badminton)">Indonesia Open</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Open_(badminton)" title="Singapore Open (badminton)">Singapore Open</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan_Open_(badminton)" title="Japan Open (badminton)">Japan Open</a>. She was seeded 8th at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympics_2012" title="Olympics 2012">Olympics 2012</a>. However, her lackluster performance in the Olympics 2012 saw her failing to make past the group match, losing to <a href="/wiki/Yip_Pui_Yin" title="Yip Pui Yin">Yip Pui Yin</a>. She defended her title Korea Grand Prix Gold title at the end of the year.</p>
<p>Sung won her first Super Series Premier title early in 2013 at her hometown, the <a href="/wiki/Korea_Open_(badminton)" title="Korea Open (badminton)">Korea Open</a>. This propelled her ranking up to world no.5, the highest of her career. Sung reached semi final at the All England, losing to the eventual winner <a href="/wiki/Tine_Baun" title="Tine Baun">Tine Baun</a> in an exciting match lasted for 76 minutes, 22-24, 21-19, 19-21. She later won the <a href="/wiki/2013_Chinese_Taipei_Open_Grand_Prix_Gold" title="2013 Chinese Taipei Open Grand Prix Gold">2013 Chinese Taipei Open Grand Prix Gold</a> against <a href="/wiki/Tai_Tzu-ying" title="Tai Tzu-ying">Tai Tzu-ying</a> 21–16, 21–9. Then, she reached the finals of <a href="/wiki/2013_Denmark_Super_Series_Premier" title="2013 Denmark Super Series Premier">2013 Denmark Super Series Premier</a> losing to Wang Yihan in 21–16, 18–21, 20–22. In <a href="/wiki/2013_Korea_Open_Grand_Prix_Gold" title="2013 Korea Open Grand Prix Gold">2013 Korea Open Grand Prix Gold</a>, she went up against compatriot <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bae_Youn-joo" title="Bae Youn-joo">Bae Youn-joo</a> and she was beaten in 3 sets 21–19, 15–21, 21–9.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/2014_German_Open_Grand_Prix_Gold" title="2014 German Open Grand Prix Gold">2014 German Open Grand Prix Gold</a>, She went on to the finals. <a href="/wiki/Sayaka_Takahashi" title="Sayaka Takahashi">Sayaka Takahashi</a> won 21–17, 8–21, 21–12. In <a href="/wiki/2014_Badminton_Asia_Championships" title="2014 Badminton Asia Championships">2014 Badminton Asia Championships</a>, she became the first South Korean in 10 years to win the title, beating 1st seeded <a href="/wiki/Wang_Shixian" title="Wang Shixian">Wang Shixian</a> 21-19, 21-15. In <a href="/wiki/2014_Chinese_Taipei_Open_Grand_Prix_Gold" title="2014 Chinese Taipei Open Grand Prix Gold">2014 Chinese Taipei Open Grand Prix Gold</a>, she won 21–13, 21–18 against <a href="/wiki/Liu_Xin" title="Liu Xin">Liu Xin</a>. She qualified for the Dubai <a href="/wiki/2014_BWF_Super_Series_Masters_Finals" title="2014 BWF Super Series Masters Finals">2014 BWF Super Series Masters Finals</a> and lost to <a href="/wiki/Tai_Tzu-ying" title="Tai Tzu-ying">Tai Tzu-ying</a> in the final, 17-21, 12-21</p>
<p>She won the <a href="/wiki/2015_German_Open_Grand_Prix_Gold" title="2015 German Open Grand Prix Gold">2015 German Open Grand Prix Gold</a> beating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carolina_Marin" title="Carolina Marin">Carolina Marin</a> 21-15, 14-21, 21-6</p>
