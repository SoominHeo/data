<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">World International Doubles Squash Championships</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>World International Doubles Squash Championships</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Squash_(sport)" title="Squash (sport)">squash</a> tournament which includes Men's Doubles, Women's Doubles and Mixed Doubles events. Under the rules of the championships, double pairs must be made up of two players from the same country. The event has been held three times – in 1997, 2004, and 2006.Australia will be holding the 4th edition of the tournament in August 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The championships are played on courts measuring 32 feet (9.75 metres) by 21 feet – a court size approved by the <a href="/wiki/World_Squash_Federation" title="World Squash Federation">World Squash Federation</a> in 1992 for the international doubles game. This court size is the same depth as courts for the singles game, but appears to be slightly wider (singles courts are also 21 feet wide).</p>
<p>A separate world championship event, known as the World Hardball Doubles Squash Championships, is also held once every two years for players of the <a href="/wiki/Hardball_squash" title="Hardball squash">hardball</a> version of doubles squash (which is played with a different type of ball, on courts measuring 45 feet by 25 feet).</p>
