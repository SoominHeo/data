<p><b>Confédération Mondiale des Activités Subaquatiques</b> (CMAS) is an international federation that represents underwater activities in <a href="/wiki/Underwater_sports" title="Underwater sports">underwater sport</a> and underwater sciences, and oversees an international system of recreational <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Snorkelling" title="Snorkelling">snorkel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuba_diving" title="Scuba diving">scuba diver</a> training and recognition. It is also known by its English name, the <i>World Underwater Federation</i>, and its Spanish name, <i>Confederacion Mundial De Actividades Subacuaticas</i>. Its foundation in <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a> during January 1959 makes it one of the world's oldest <a href="/wiki/Underwater_diving" title="Underwater diving">underwater diving</a> organisations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An international congress of diving federations representing all underwater disciplines met in <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a> on 28 September 1958. National Delegates attended from following countries: <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, Federal Republic of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">United States of America</a> and the former <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>. Following a decision at that congress, a meeting was held in <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a> on 9–11 January 1959, which officially established the <i>World Underwater Federation</i>, with an <a href="/wiki/Acronym" title="Acronym">acronym</a> based on its French title as <i>CMAS</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aboutcmas_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-aboutcmas-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>A founding member and key proponent of <i>CMAS</i> was the French underwater explorer and diving pioneer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jacques-Yves_Cousteau" title="Jacques-Yves Cousteau">Jacques-Yves Cousteau</a> who was chosen to be the inaugural President with Luigi Ferraro, Italian underwater pioneer, appointed as Vice-President.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Luigi_Ferraro.3B_C.M.A.S._3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Luigi_Ferraro.3B_C.M.A.S.-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>CMAS succeeded the <i>Comité des Sports Sous-Marins</i> (Underwater Sports Committee) of the <a href="/wiki/Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_la_P%C3%AAche_Sportive" title="Confédération Internationale de la Pêche Sportive">Confédération Internationale de la Pêche Sportive</a> (CIPS) (International Confederation of Sport Fishing), which was founded on 22 February 1952.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aboutcmas_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-aboutcmas-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Luigi_Ferraro.3B_C.M.A.S._3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Luigi_Ferraro.3B_C.M.A.S.-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation">Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>CMAS consists of 3 major committees - Sport, Technical and Scientific.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-technical_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-technical-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sport_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-sport-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scientific_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-scientific-6">[6]</a></sup> These committees are overseen by a Board of Directors (BoD) elected periodically at the annually convened General Assembly. The BoD, the Sport Committee and the Scientific Committee oversee sub-committees known as commissions. Day-to-day operation is overseen by a steering committee appointed from the BoD. Its headquarters is currently located in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Steering_Committee">The Steering Committee</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Steering Committee">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The steering committee consists of seven members. As of 2013, the members were:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-steering_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-steering-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport_Committee">Sport Committee</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sport Committee">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The sports committee consists of commissions representing the following underwater sports - <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Free-diving#Competitive_freediving" title="Free-diving">Apnoea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aquathlon_(underwater_wrestling)" title="Aquathlon (underwater wrestling)">Aquathlon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finswimming" title="Finswimming">Finswimming</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spearfishing" title="Spearfishing">Spearfishing</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sport_Diving_(competitive_scuba)" title="Sport Diving (competitive scuba)">Sport diving</a>, <a href="/wiki/Underwater_hockey" title="Underwater hockey">Underwater hockey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Underwater_orienteering" title="Underwater orienteering">Underwater orienteering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Underwater_rugby" title="Underwater rugby">Underwater rugby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Underwater_target_shooting" title="Underwater target shooting">Underwater target shooting</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aquathon_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-aquathon-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-orienteering_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-orienteering-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sportdiving_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-sportdiving-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> An additional commission known as <i>Visual</i> represents the sports of <a href="/wiki/Underwater_photography_(sport)" title="Underwater photography (sport)">Underwater photography</a> and Underwater video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> All of those sports are governed at the international level uniquely by CMAS with exception of apnoea in which competition and governance is also provided by a rival organisation, <a href="/wiki/AIDA_International" title="AIDA International">AIDA International</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Technical_Committee">Technical Committee</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Technical Committee">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Role">Role</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Role">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The role of the Technical Committee is the provision of ‘safe diving for CMAS members’ and seeks to achieve this by ‘promoting world class standards for all aspects of Scuba Diving and ensuring adherence of them by member federations and dive providers’.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-About-CMAS-TC_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-About-CMAS-TC-14">[14]</a></sup> Its officers who are elected from persons nominated at the CMAS General Assembly by affiliated national diving federations include the following positions - President, Secretary, Standards Director, Education Director, Technical Director, Diving Security Director, Special Tasks Director and a number of general members. It oversees the two following systems - a diver training standards system known as the <i>CMAS International Diver Training Standards</i> and a certification system known as <i>CMAS International Diver Certificates</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-About-CMAS-TC_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-About-CMAS-TC-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Learn_To_Dive_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Learn_To_Dive-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since CMAS effectively started as a volunteer organisation for hobbyists, its courses tend to reflect the full range of European and world diving standards. Compared to other diving organisations which may be more geared towards holiday and tropical water diving. While organisations like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PADI" title="PADI">PADI</a> or <a href="/wiki/Scuba_Schools_International" title="Scuba Schools International">SSI</a> tend to bring divers into the water immediately, CMAS entry-level training is more extensive, featuring more "classroom" delivered theory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dtp_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-dtp-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifications">Qualifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Qualifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Standards.2C_certification_and_training_delivery">Standards, certification and training delivery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Standards, certification and training delivery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>The CMAS Technical Committee has developed a qualification system currently known as the <i>CMAS International Diver Training Standards</i> which consists of published universal standards for <a href="/wiki/Recreational_diving" title="Recreational diving">recreational diving</a>, <a href="/wiki/Technical_diving" title="Technical diving">technical diving</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diving_instructor" title="Diving instructor">leadership diver grades</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CMAS-S.2BP_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-CMAS-S.2BP-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>The CMAS Technical Committee has also developed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diving_certification" title="Diving certification">diving certification system</a> called the <i>CMAS International Diver Training Certification System</i> for most of its diver training standards and which permits divers that have been trained in accordance with the CMAS International Diver Training Standards, to have their training recognised worldwide particularly in countries where CMAS affiliated federations exist. The system includes a double sided certification card format where one side depicts the achieved CMAS standard while the other side has details of the issuing organisation and the diver.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Learn_To_Dive_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Learn_To_Dive-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Certificate_format_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Certificate_format-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>CMAS itself does not provide training or conduct the issuing of certifications - this is available from two sources. Firstly, from national diving federations affiliated to the CMAS Technical Committee using their member diving clubs, their member instructors where the federation is exclusively an instructor organisation or by agreement with independent underwater diving training organizations operating in the countries where those federations are based.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CMAS_Technical_Committee_Affiliates_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-CMAS_Technical_Committee_Affiliates-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CMAS-ISA_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-CMAS-ISA-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SEI_Organizational_Standards_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-SEI_Organizational_Standards-21">[21]</a></sup> Secondly, from specially accredited dive centres known as <i>CMAS Dive Centers</i> (CDC) who use dedicated CMAS training materials.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CMAS_Diving_Centers_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-CMAS_Diving_Centers-22">[22]</a></sup></p>

<p>Standards are offered for recreational diver training for the following grades of snorkel and scuba divers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CMAS-S.2BP_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-CMAS-S.2BP-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<div class="div-col columns column-count column-count-2" style="-moz-column-count: 2; -webkit-column-count: 2; column-count: 2;">
<ul>
<li><b>One Star Snorkel Diver</b> - “a snorkel diver who is competent in the safe and correct use of relevant snorkel diving equipment used in a swimming pool or sheltered water. The snorkel diver is familiar with relevant personal equipment and its use in a sheltered open water area. The snorkel diver is ready to gain further open water training.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Snorkel_Diver_Standards_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Snorkel_Diver_Standards-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Two Star Snorkel Diver</b> - “a snorkel diver who has gained some open water diving experience. The snorkel diver is considered ready to take part in dives with other snorkel divers, under supervision if a minor. The CMAS 2 star snorkel diver is considered trained.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Snorkel_Diver_Standards_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-Snorkel_Diver_Standards-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Three Star Snorkel Diver</b> - “A fully trained snorkel diver who has gained considerable experience in open water snorkel diving under various conditions. The 3 star snorkel diver has acquired life saving skills and can lead snorkel divers in open water dives.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Snorkel_Diver_Standards_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-Snorkel_Diver_Standards-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Introductory SCUBA Experience</b> - “this training programme aims at providing interested persons with an introductory SCUBA diving experience, to a maximum depth of ten (10) metres under the direct supervision of a CMAS Instructor, whilst using air as a breathing gas, in a safe manner.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CMAS-ISE_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-CMAS-ISE-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/CMAS*_SCUBA_Diver" title="CMAS* SCUBA Diver">One Star Diver</a></b> - “a diver who is competent in the safe and correct use of all appropriate open water scuba diving equipment in a sheltered water training area and is ready to gain open water diving experience in the company of an experienced diver.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-diver_definitions_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-diver_definitions-25">[25]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Two Star Diver</b> - “a diver who has gained some open water diving experience and is considered ready to take part in dives partnered by a diver of at least the same or a higher grade. The two star diver may dive with a One Star Diver in sheltered shallow water.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-diver_definitions_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-diver_definitions-25">[25]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Three Star Diver</b> - “a fully trained, experienced, senior diver who is considered competent to supervise other divers of any grade in open water and support an instructor in pool and open water training.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-diver_definitions_25-2"><a href="#cite_note-diver_definitions-25">[25]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Four Star Diver</b> - “a three star diver who has attained a higher than average level of knowledge and ability supported by broad diving experience. He be able to assist in the training of One Star Divers and be competent to lead divers in order to accomplish major diving tasks or project objectives.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-diver_definitions_25-3"><a href="#cite_note-diver_definitions-25">[25]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
</div>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Leadership_Diver_Training_Programmes">Leadership Diver Training Programmes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Leadership Diver Training Programmes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Standards are provided for the training of the following grades of recreational snorkel and scuba instructors:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CMAS-S.2BP_17-4"><a href="#cite_note-CMAS-S.2BP-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<div class="div-col columns column-count column-count-2" style="-moz-column-count: 2; -webkit-column-count: 2; column-count: 2;">
<ul>
<li><b>One Star Snorkel Diver Instructor</b> - “a CMAS 2 star snorkel diver who is interested in and has knowledge of practical snorkel diver instruction. The instructor is qualified to conduct practical lessons in a swimming pool and to teach the CMAS 1 star snorkel diver certificate. The instructor can be used as an assistant instructor in open water, but cannot plan an open water dive and lead a group of divers on his own.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Snorkel_Diver_Standards_23-3"><a href="#cite_note-Snorkel_Diver_Standards-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Two Star Snorkel Diver Instructor</b> - “an experienced one star snorkel diver instructor who has the knowledge, skills, and experience required to lead and instruct 1, 2 and 3 Star snorkel diver students in the classroom, swimming pool, and open water. The experienced CMAS 2 Star snorkel diver instructor may assist in the training and education of CMAS 1 Star snorkel diver instructors.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Snorkel_Diver_Standards_23-4"><a href="#cite_note-Snorkel_Diver_Standards-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>One Star Instructor</b> - “a three star or four star diver who has demonstrated a knowledge of the techniques of diving instruction and has proven, under evaluation, to be competent in practical instructional skills and diving safety procedures: he or she is qualified to train and certify novice diving students in a full CMAS One Star Diver scuba program.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-diver_definitions_25-4"><a href="#cite_note-diver_definitions-25">[25]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Two Star Instructor</b> - “an experienced one star instructor who has the knowledge, skills, and experience required to teach groups of divers in the classroom, pool, and in open water, and to train qualified dive supervisors, assistant instructors and assist in the training &amp; evaluation of One Star Instructors. He is qualified to teach and certify all CMAS diver levels including snorkel Instructor levels.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-diver_definitions_25-5"><a href="#cite_note-diver_definitions-25">[25]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Three Star Instructor</b> - “a highly experienced two star instructor who is competent to train all grades of divers and instructors and able to take responsibility for instructor certification programs and the conduct of diving schools/centres and specialised training courses or events.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-diver_definitions_25-6"><a href="#cite_note-diver_definitions-25">[25]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
</div>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scientific_Committee">Scientific Committee</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Scientific Committee">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Role_2">Role</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Role">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Committee considers its main task is to bring to the attention of the world underwater diving community, the important issues concerning the marine environment and how divers can play a major role in protecting it by serving as frontline observers of its overall health, particularly in respect to invasive species, coastal ecosystems and biodiversity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_scientific_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-about_scientific-26">[26]</a></sup> Its officers who are elected from persons nominated at the CMAS General Assembly by affiliated national diving federations include the following positions - President, Secretary, a number of general members and Presidents of the following commissions - marine biology, marine archaeology, geology and professional relationships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scientific_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-scientific-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Scientific_diving_codes">Scientific diving codes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Scientific diving codes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Over a 10-year period from 1977, it was responsible for the development of the "Code of Practice for Scientific Diving" for <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flemming_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Flemming-27">[27]</a></sup> in cooperation with <a href="/wiki/National_Sea_Grant_College_Program" title="National Sea Grant College Program">Sea Grant</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifications_2">Qualifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Qualifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The CMAS Scientific Committee oversees a system of diving standards and certification that operates in parallel to the CMAS International Diver Training Certification System. The system was developed to which recognize the status of a diver who is qualified to dive in the course of research whilst employed. This internationally recognized standard of competence is a distinct advantage for working scientists who wish to travel between laboratories and institutes in different countries. Known as the CMAS Scientific Diver Standard, the system consists of the following diver and instructor grades:</p>
<ul>
<li>CMAS Scientific Diver (CSD) (pre-requisite of CMAS 2 Star Diver plus scientific diver training)</li>
<li>CMAS Advanced Scientific Diver (CASD) (pre-requisite of CMAS 3 Star Diver plus advanced scientific diver training)</li>
<li>CMAS Scientific Diving Instructor (pre-requisite: CMAS 2 Star Instructor)</li>
<li>CMAS Confirmed Scientific Diving Instructor (pre-requisite: CMAS 3 Star Instructor)</li>
</ul>
<p>A programme of specialist courses both at entry and advanced levels in <a href="/wiki/Underwater_archaeology" title="Underwater archaeology">underwater archaeology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freshwater_biology" title="Freshwater biology">freshwater biology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marine_biology" title="Marine biology">marine biology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marine_geology" title="Marine geology">marine geology</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oceanology" title="Oceanology">oceanology</a> is also offered. Training and certification (also known as brevets) for the above qualifications is available from organisations known as CMAS Scientific Centres (CSC).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scientific_diver_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Scientific_diver-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
