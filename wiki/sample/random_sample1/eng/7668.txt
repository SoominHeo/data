<p><b>World Suicide Prevention Day</b> (<b>WSPD</b>) is an awareness day observed on 10 September every year, in order to provide worldwide commitment and action to prevent suicides, with various activities around the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sitnews_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-sitnews-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/International_Association_for_Suicide_Prevention" title="International Association for Suicide Prevention">International Association for Suicide Prevention</a> (IASP), collaborates with the <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">World Health Organization</a> (WHO) and the <a href="/wiki/World_Federation_for_Mental_Health" title="World Federation for Mental Health">World Federation for Mental Health</a>, to host World Suicide Prevention Day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-suicidology_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-suicidology-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2011 an estimated 40 countries held awareness events to mark the occasion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lfp_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-lfp-3">[3]</a></sup> The United Nations issued 'National Policy for Suicide Prevention' in the 1990s which some countries use as a basis for their suicide policies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-united_nations_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-united_nations-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>Suicide has a number of complex and interrelated and underlying contributing factors ... that can contribute to the feelings of pain and hopelessness. Having access to means to kill oneself – most typically firearms, medicines and poisons – is also a risk factor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sitnews_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-sitnews-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<div class="templatequotecite"><cite>— Campaign release</cite></div>
</blockquote>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Suicide_Prevention_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2011<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=World_Suicide_Prevention_Day&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, an estimated one million people per year die by suicide or "a death every 40 seconds or about 3,000 every day."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lfp_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-lfp-3">[3]</a></sup> According to WHO there are twenty people who have a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Failed_suicide_attempt" title="Failed suicide attempt">failed suicide attempt</a> for every one that is successful, at a rate approximately one every three seconds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-time_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-time-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dailymail_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-dailymail-6">[6]</a></sup> Suicide is the "most common cause of death for people aged 15 – 24."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beausejour_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Beausejour-7">[7]</a></sup> More people die from suicide than from murder and war; it is the 13th leading cause of death worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sitnews_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-sitnews-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rtenews_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-rtenews-8">[8]</a></sup> According to WHO, suicide accounts for nearly half of all violent deaths in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dailymail_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-dailymail-6">[6]</a></sup> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Brian_Mishara&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Brian Mishara (page does not exist)">Brian Mishara</a>, IASP president, noted that, "more people kill themselves than die in all wars, terrorist acts and interpersonal violence combined."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scoop_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-scoop-9">[9]</a></sup> The number of people who die by suicide is expected to reach 1.5 million per year by 2020.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rtenews_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-rtenews-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The UN noted that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suicide_bombers" title="Suicide bombers">suicide bombers</a>' deaths are seen as secondary to their goal of killing other people or specific targets and the bombers are not otherwise typical of people committing suicide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-united_nations_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-united_nations-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pesticides">Pesticides</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Suicide_Prevention_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pesticides">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to a WHO press release, one third of worldwide suicides were committed with <a href="/wiki/Pesticide" title="Pesticide">pesticides</a>, "some of which were forbidden by United Nations (UN) conventions."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-united_nations_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-united_nations-4">[4]</a></sup> WHO urges Asian countries to restrict pesticides that are commonly used in failed attempts, especially <a href="/wiki/Organophosphate" title="Organophosphate">organophosphate</a>-based pesticides that are banned by international conventions but still made in and exported by some Asian countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBCNews_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBCNews-10">[10]</a></sup> From 1996–2006 pesticide ingestion accounted for an estimated 60–90 percent of suicides in China, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, Sri Lanka, and <a href="/wiki/Trinidad" title="Trinidad">Trinidad</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBCNews_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-CBCNews-10">[10]</a></sup> WHO reports an increase in pesticide suicides in other Asian countries as well as <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central</a> and South America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBCNews_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-CBCNews-10">[10]</a></sup> It is estimated that such painful failed attempts could be reduced by legalizing controlled voluntary euthanasia options, as implemented in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Country_information">Country information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Suicide_Prevention_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Country information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Of the 34 member countries of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Organization_for_Economic_Cooperation_and_Development" title="Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development">Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development</a>, a group of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Developed_countries" title="Developed countries">developed countries</a> that uses <a href="/wiki/Market_economy" title="Market economy">market economy</a> to improve the <a href="/wiki/Human_Development_Index" title="Human Development Index">Human Development Index</a>, South Korea had the highest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suicide_rate" title="Suicide rate">suicide rate</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes-11">[11]</a></sup> In 2011 South Korea's Ministry of Health and Welfare enacted legislation coinciding with WSPD to address the high rate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In some countries, such as China, young people 15–34 years old are more likely to die by suicide than by any other means.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBCNewsBeijing_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBCNewsBeijing-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to WHO, in 2009 the four countries with the highest rates of suicide were all in Eastern Europe; <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a> had the fourth highest rate preceded by <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, Latvia, and <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> This stays within findings from the start of the WSPD event in 2003 when the highest rates were also found in Eastern European countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IASPpr_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-IASPpr-14">[14]</a></sup> The countries with the lowest rates tend to be in Latin America, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muslim_countries" title="Muslim countries">Muslim countries</a> and a few Asian countries."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IASPpr_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-IASPpr-14">[14]</a></sup> There is a lack of information from most African countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IASPpr_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-IASPpr-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gender">Gender</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Suicide_Prevention_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Gender">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With the exception of China, men commit suicide more often than women. In the Western world, males die three to four times more often by means of suicide than females do.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
