<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">World Autism Awareness Day</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><b>World Autism Awareness Day</b> is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_observance" title="International observance">internationally recognised day</a> on the 2nd of April every year, encouraging <a href="/wiki/Member_states_of_the_United_Nations" title="Member states of the United Nations">Member States</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> to take measures to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Raising_awareness" title="Raising awareness">raise awareness</a> about children with <a href="/wiki/Autism" title="Autism">autism</a> throughout the world. It was designated by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a> resolution "62/139. World Autism Awareness Day", passed in council on November 1, 2007, and adopted on December 18, 2007. It was proposed by the United Nations representative from <a href="/wiki/Qatar" title="Qatar">Qatar</a>, Her Highness <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sheikha" title="Sheikha">Sheikha</a> <a href="/wiki/Moza_bint_Nasser" title="Moza bint Nasser">Mozah Bint Nasser Al-Missned</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen_consort" title="Queen consort">Consort</a> of His Highness <a href="/wiki/Sheikh" title="Sheikh">Sheikh</a> <a href="/wiki/Hamad_bin_Khalifa_Al_Thani" title="Hamad bin Khalifa Al Thani">Hamad Bin Khalifa Al-Thani</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Emir" title="Emir">Emir</a> of the State of Qatar, and supported by all member states.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>This resolution was passed and adopted without a vote in the UN General Assembly, mainly as a supplement to previous UN initiatives to improve <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">human rights</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> Since its inception autism awareness and research around the world has increased as a result<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<p>World Autism Day is also one of only four official health-specific UN Days. The day itself brings individual autism organizations together all around the world to aid in things like research, diagnoses, treatment, and overall awareness for those with the disorder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Components">Components</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Autism_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Components">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The original resolution had four main components:</p>
<ul>
<li>the establishment of the 2nd day of April as World Autism Awareness Day, beginning in 2008</li>
<li>invitation to Member States and other relevant organizations to the UN or the international <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Societal_system" title="Societal system">societal system</a>, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-governmental_organizations" title="Non-governmental organizations">non-governmental organizations</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Private_sector" title="Private sector">private sector</a>, to create initiatives to raise public <a href="/wiki/Awareness" title="Awareness">awareness</a> of autism</li>
<li>encourages Member States to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Awareness_raising" title="Awareness raising">raise awareness</a> of <a href="/wiki/Autism" title="Autism">autism</a> on all levels in society</li>
<li>asks the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UN_Secretary-General" title="UN Secretary-General">UN Secretary-General</a> to deliver this message to member states and all other UN organizations<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_Initiatives">Notable Initiatives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Autism_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Notable Initiatives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="April_2.2C_2008">April 2, 2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Autism_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: April 2, 2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first Autism Awareness Day was highly mandated and hosted by the UN branches around the world. The highlighted events were held at the United Nations Headquarters in New York. The events hosted by the UN includes a panel discussion sponsored by Qatar and the UN representative responsible for the resolution, along with the <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">World Health Organization</a> and the NGO <a href="/wiki/Autism_Speaks" title="Autism Speaks">Autism Speaks</a>. In addition, there was a briefing held for NGO's on topics relating to autism awareness. Both of these events focused on personal efforts for awareness raising and eliminating negative stigma associated with autism. In addition, there were highlights on the struggles of people with autism and the importance of better understanding the disorder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><br/></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Autism_Speaks">Autism Speaks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Autism_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Autism Speaks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Autism_Speaks" title="Autism Speaks">Autism Speaks</a> is considered one of the largest non-governmental organizations in the field of efforts to raise awareness to autism, further research, and act as an advocate to those with the disorder. This group is considered by many to lead the field in science and advocacy, and is a leader in research dealing with causes, prevention, treatments and cures. They also act as a large support system for families of an autistic person.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> However, they are also seen by many as a controvesial group, especially amongst autistic communities, for their perceived support of abusive treatments and demonisation of autistic people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Light_It_Up_Blue">Light It Up Blue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Autism_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Light It Up Blue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Light_It_Up_Blue" title="Light It Up Blue">Light It Up Blue</a> is one of the largest initiatives posed by Autism Speaks. It is observed on WAAD, and is a dedication to autism awareness.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Power_of_One_March">The Power of One March</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Autism_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The Power of One March">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="external text" href="https://www.thepowerofonemarch.org/" rel="nofollow">The Power of One March</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[A]</a></sup> is The National Autism Awareness March, and is a unique night-time march in Washington, D.C., on World Autism Awareness Day, which is April 2, 2016. The march aims to unify the autism awareness community and provide a time of healing and peace around the globe. Attendees may choose which organization they want to march for with proceeds of LED bracelet sales going to the organization of their choosing.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ontario">Ontario</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Autism_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Ontario">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="external text" href="http://www.autismontario.com/" rel="nofollow">Autism Ontario</a> celebrates World Autism Awareness Day by "Raising a Flag" for autism. Municipalities around Ontario raise a flag to raise awareness for autism in their region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Onesie_Wednesday">Onesie Wednesday</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Autism_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Onesie Wednesday">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2014 WAAD coincided with Onesie Wednesday, a day created by the <a href="/wiki/National_Autistic_Society" title="National Autistic Society">National Autistic Society</a> to encourage people in England, Wales and Northern Ireland to show their support for anyone on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autistic_spectrum" title="Autistic spectrum">autistic spectrum</a>. By wearing a <a href="/wiki/Onesie_(jumpsuit)" title="Onesie (jumpsuit)">onesie</a> or pyjamas, participants are saying, "it’s all right to be different".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Outcomes">Outcomes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Autism_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Outcomes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Autism_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In a 2015 Presidential Proclamation, President Obama highlighted some of the initiatives that the US government was taking to bring rights to those with autism and to bring awareness to the disorder. He highlighted things like The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Affordable_Care_Act" title="Affordable Care Act">Affordable Care Act</a>, which prohibits health insurance companies from denying coverage based on a pre-existing condition such as autism. He also pointed out the recent <a href="/wiki/Autism_CARES_Act_of_2014" title="Autism CARES Act of 2014">Autism CARES Act of 2014</a>, which provides higher level training for those serving citizens on the autism spectrum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[10]</a></sup></p>
