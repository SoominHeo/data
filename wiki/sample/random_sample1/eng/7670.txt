<p>The <b>World Professional Billiards and Snooker Association</b> (<b>WPBSA</b>), founded in 1968 and based in <a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">Bristol</a>, the United Kingdom, is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sport_governing_body" title="Sport governing body">governing body</a> of men's professional <a href="/wiki/Snooker" title="Snooker">snooker</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_billiards" title="English billiards">English billiards</a>. It owns and sets the <a class="external text" href="http://www.wpbsa.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/03/official-rules-of-the-game.pdf" rel="nofollow">official rules</a> of the two sports and engages in promotional activities on behalf of the sports.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Professional_Billiards_and_Snooker_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The WPBSA operates as both the professional governing body of the two <a href="/wiki/Cue_sports" title="Cue sports">cue sports</a>, alongside its amateur counterpart in the <a href="/wiki/International_Billiards_and_Snooker_Federation" title="International Billiards and Snooker Federation">International Billiards and Snooker Federation</a> (IBSF), as well as a <a href="/wiki/Trade_association" title="Trade association">trade association</a> promoting snooker and billiards more generally.</p>
<p>The WPBSA has four subsidiaries: World Snooker, <a href="/wiki/World_Billiards" title="World Billiards">World Billiards</a>, World Ladies Billiards &amp; Snooker (formerly the World Ladies Billiards &amp; Snooker Association (WLBSA)) and World Disability Billiards &amp; Snooker.</p>
<p>The <b>World Snooker Association</b>, is responsible for running and administering snooker's main <a href="/wiki/Snooker_world_rankings" title="Snooker world rankings">ranking circuit events</a>. These include the <a href="/wiki/Shanghai_Masters_(snooker)" title="Shanghai Masters (snooker)">Shanghai Masters</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Open_(snooker)" title="World Open (snooker)">World Open</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK_Championship_(snooker)" title="UK Championship (snooker)">UK Championship</a>, <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Open_(snooker)" title="Welsh Open (snooker)">Welsh Open</a>, <a href="/wiki/China_Open_(snooker)" title="China Open (snooker)">China Open</a> and the <a href="/wiki/World_Snooker_Championship" title="World Snooker Championship">World Snooker Championship</a>. The latter is the most famous and popular event on the snooker season, with a remarkable global audience.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="A claim like that requires a source citations. (January 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Staged at the <a href="/wiki/Crucible_Theatre" title="Crucible Theatre">Crucible Theatre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a> since 1977, it is the richest, most prestigious and most important tournament in snooker. The subsidiary is often referred to informally as simply <b>World Snooker</b>, although this is more properly the title of its website.</p>
<p>The WPBSA head office is based in <a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">Bristol</a>, England. <a href="/wiki/Jason_Ferguson_(snooker_player)" title="Jason Ferguson (snooker player)">Jason Ferguson</a> is WPBSA chairman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The organisation also determines the rules and regulations of snooker and English billiards, including disciplinary matters.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Professional_Billiards_and_Snooker_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The organisation was founded in 1968.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Prior to the formation of the WPBSA, the world governing body of both snooker and English billiards was the Billiards Association and Control Council (BACC).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotional_activities">Promotional activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Professional_Billiards_and_Snooker_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Promotional activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In recent years, WPBSA has been successful in promoting the sport in China, snooker's biggest growth area. Both the Shanghai Masters and China Open (in Beijing) are fully funded by promoters in China; likewise the <a href="/wiki/Jiangsu" title="Jiangsu">Jiangsu</a> Classic, an invitation-only event. Further tournaments are expected to be established as the sport continues to thrive in the Far East. In 2008 WPBSA made inroads into the Middle East by staging the first ever ranking event in the Kingdom of <a href="/wiki/Bahrain" title="Bahrain">Bahrain</a>.</p>
<p>WPBSA owns and runs a snooker training academy in <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>, which is a coaching centre for talented players from the UK and overseas. Top professionals such as Ding Junhui and Peter Ebdon use it as a base, and it also houses the Paul Hunter Scholarship, which is awarded annually to a promising junior player.</p>
<p>In 2008, WPBSA launched the HotShots campaign, which is aimed at boosting the popularity of the sport among a younger audience, by raising the profiles of emerging talents such as Mark Selby, Mark Allen, and Jamie Cope.</p>
<p>World Snooker also has a charitable arm that helps players who have fallen on hard times. In 2008 this was investigated for accounting irregularities and the apparent involvement in the decision-making process of WPBSA officials.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The decision to decline an application for a grant from <a href="/wiki/Chris_Small" title="Chris Small">Chris Small</a>, a former player who retired due to <a href="/wiki/Ankylosing_spondylitis" title="Ankylosing spondylitis">Ankylosing spondylitis</a>, was also criticised by several of the game's leading figures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> WPBSA's commercial interests are managed by <a href="/wiki/IMG_(company)" title="IMG (company)">IMG</a>, which also own <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trans_World_International" title="Trans World International">Trans World International</a>, who produce the game's coverage for the BBC.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="President">President</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Professional_Billiards_and_Snooker_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: President">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The current President is <a href="/wiki/Jason_Ferguson_(snooker_player)" title="Jason Ferguson (snooker player)">Jason Ferguson</a> who was elected in 2015. Previous Presidents include <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Archer" title="Jeffrey Archer">Jeffrey Archer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism_and_controversy">Criticism and controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Professional_Billiards_and_Snooker_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Criticism and controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The body has received criticism in the late 2000s. <a href="/wiki/John_Higgins_(snooker_player)" title="John Higgins (snooker player)">John Higgins</a> has been particularly vocal in his opinion that World Snooker has not done enough to promote the game in new territories, particularly in Eastern Europe. The rival <a href="/wiki/World_Series_of_Snooker" title="World Series of Snooker">World Series of Snooker</a> was launched by a consortium including Higgins in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>When World Snooker scheduled the <a href="/wiki/2008_Bahrain_Championship" title="2008 Bahrain Championship">2008 Bahrain Championship</a> on dates which clashed with <a href="/wiki/Premier_League_Snooker" title="Premier League Snooker">Premier League Snooker</a> matches scheduled five months earlier with World Snooker approval, this caused four leading players (coincidentally including Higgins) to miss the Bahrain event and consequently lose ranking points—Higgins called the clash "laughable".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Premier League organiser <a href="/wiki/Barry_Hearn" title="Barry Hearn">Barry Hearn</a> commented that "I am very disappointed and I can't understand why World Snooker hasn't discussed dates with us", while Higgins and his manager Pat Mooney threatened legal action over the ranking points situation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In response, World Snooker referred to the Premier League being a "third-party promoter", noted that they run events on 11 out of 13 weeks between September and November, and (ignoring the threat of legal action if the players involved broke their Premier League contract) declared that "our members have the freedom of choice to pick which tournaments to participate in". The idea of scheduling the event in free time in January or March, or arranging it in advance of Premier League scheduling, was not mentioned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010, Barry Hearn acquired a controlling interest in snooker's commercial arm World Snooker Limited.</p>
