<p><b>Cédric Gervais</b> (born <b>Cedric DePasquale</b>; 7 June 1979) is a <a href="/wiki/French_people" title="French people">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a> and <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">producer</a> of <a href="/wiki/House_music" title="House music">house music</a> living in <a href="/wiki/Miami_Beach,_Florida" title="Miami Beach, Florida">Miami Beach, Florida</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2013, he produced a remix of <a href="/wiki/Lana_Del_Rey" title="Lana Del Rey">Lana Del Rey</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Summertime_Sadness" title="Summertime Sadness">Summertime Sadness</a>", for which he won a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cedric_Gervais&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gervais was born in <a href="/wiki/Marseille" title="Marseille">Marseille</a>. He was a resident DJ at Paris' <a href="/wiki/Le_Queen" title="Le Queen">Le Queen</a> Club. In 1998, he moved to <a href="/wiki/South_Beach" title="South Beach">South Beach</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miami_Beach" title="Miami Beach">Miami Beach</a>. He was 19 years old when he left for Miami, and gained residencies at Crobar (now Cameo) and Nikki Beach, before taking over as DJ at the <a href="/wiki/Club_Space" title="Club Space">Club Space</a> Terrace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> His first single, "Burning", was released with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ultra_Records" title="Ultra Records">Ultra Records</a> through their sublabel You. In 2006, his debut album <i>The Experiment</i>, featured the single "Spirit in My Life", also on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ultra_Records" title="Ultra Records">Ultra Records</a>. His production credits include working with <a href="/wiki/Lenny_Kravitz" title="Lenny Kravitz">Lenny Kravitz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Deep_Dish_(band)" title="Deep Dish (band)">Deep Dish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Lawler" title="Steve Lawler">Steve Lawler</a>, and Rachel Star.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>His single "Mauri’s Dream", from 2009, was <a href="/wiki/Pete_Tong" title="Pete Tong">Pete Tong</a>’s Essential Tune of the Week three weeks in a row.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2011, Gervais released his second album, titled <i>Miamication</i>.</p>
<p>Gervais also has had two record labels of his own, Sleaze (for digital releases) and Sleaze Industries (for physical releases). He operated the labels during the 2000s releasing <a href="/wiki/Progressive_house" title="Progressive house">progressive</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tribal_house" title="Tribal house">tribal</a> house music.</p>
<p>In April 2012 Gervais released the single "Molly",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> which was accompanied by a very racy music video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The single was another <a href="/wiki/Pete_Tong" title="Pete Tong">Pete Tong</a> Essential Tune of the Week and was the highest new dance entry in the official UK charts reaching no. 26,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> whilst the video racked up over 2.5 million views on YouTube.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> "Molly" reached no. 1 in the Buzz and Cool Cuts charts,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and was day time play listed on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">Radio One</a> and Sirius.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The track also caused controversy between <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Deadmau5" title="Deadmau5">Deadmau5</a>, when Madonna made comments about the track while on stage at Ultra music festival in Miami.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013 he produced a number of remixes: <a href="/wiki/Lana_Del_Rey" title="Lana Del Rey">Lana Del Rey</a>'s "Summertime Sadness", <a href="/wiki/Willy_Moon" title="Willy Moon">Willy Moon</a>'s "Yeah Yeah", <a href="/wiki/Katy_Perry" title="Katy Perry">Katy Perry</a>'s "Wide Awake" and <a href="/wiki/Borgore" title="Borgore">Borgore</a>'s "Decisions".</p>
<p>The "<a href="/wiki/Summertime_Sadness" title="Summertime Sadness">Summertime Sadness</a>" remix reached number one on Beatport and remained there for two weeks in February.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The track was the No. 1 added track to US radio with 35 adds throughout summer 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The remix was then added to <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a>'s A-List, reaching number one in the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> making it his most successful single to date. The track reached number six on the American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_100" title="Billboard 100"><i>Billboard</i> 100</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> UK iTunes Chart to No. 2 and US iTunes Chart to No. 17. The remix was reviewed in <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daily_Star_(United_Kingdom)" title="Daily Star (United Kingdom)">The Daily Star</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The track also achieved 2x Platinum status in the US.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He won a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for Best Remixed Recording, Non Classical for the song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Michael_Bay" title="Michael Bay">Michael Bay</a>'s 2013 film <i><a href="/wiki/Pain_%26_Gain" title="Pain &amp; Gain">Pain &amp; Gain</a></i> featured two of Gervais' tracks, including his hit 'Molly'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In November 2013, Gervais launched his weekly 'Miamication' Radio Show, which is to be broadcast every Saturday night on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SiriusXM" title="SiriusXM">SiriusXM</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> His most recent single, a remix of <a href="/wiki/Miley_Cyrus" title="Miley Cyrus">Miley Cyrus</a>' "<a href="/wiki/Adore_You" title="Adore You">Adore You</a>" was released in May 2014 on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spinnin_Records" title="Spinnin Records">Spinnin Records</a>. There was also a free give away of the track 'Hashtag' as a thank you to his fans on Facebook.</p>
<p>In February 2014, Gervais partnered with EDM lifestyle brand <a class="external text" href="http://www.electricfamily.com" rel="nofollow">Electric Family</a> to produce a <a class="external text" href="http://electricfamily.com/shop/artist-series/cedric-gervais/" rel="nofollow">collaboration bracelet</a> for which 100% of the proceeds are donated to Miami Children's Health.</p>
<p>In October 2014, Gervais signed to <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> with its related label <a href="/wiki/Polydor_Records" title="Polydor Records">Polydor Records</a>, in the following month Interscope released his new single "<a href="/wiki/Love_Again_(Cedric_Gervais_song)" title="Love Again (Cedric Gervais song)">Love Again</a>" on 14 October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
