<p><b>Sarah Ann Darling</b> (born October 4, 1982) is an American <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country music</a> singer and songwriter.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sarah_Darling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2003, Darling made her name first known to the public when she was a top-three finalist on <a href="/wiki/E!" title="E!">E! Entertainment Television</a>'s reality show <i>The Entertainer</i>, hosted by <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Newton" title="Wayne Newton">Wayne Newton</a>. She missed out on winning the show as Newton believed she was not right for <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Las Vegas</a> and he advised her to head to <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville</a> to focus on country music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sarah Darling went into the studio in the summer of 2008 to record her debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Every_Monday_Morning" title="Every Monday Morning">Every Monday Morning</a></i>, which was released on June 16, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Her single "Jack of Hearts" successfully made its debut on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Country_Music_Television" title="Country Music Television">Country Music Television</a>'s top 20 countdown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> A second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Angels_%26_Devils_(Sarah_Darling_album)" title="Angels &amp; Devils (Sarah Darling album)">Angels &amp; Devils</a></i>, was released on February 15, 2011, following its lead single "Something to Do With Your Hands" and a digital single release of the <a href="/wiki/U2" title="U2">U2</a> cover "<a href="/wiki/With_or_Without_You" title="With or Without You">With or Without You</a>". To promote the single, Black River Music Group arranged for Darling to sing the national anthem for a <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_Sabres" title="Buffalo Sabres">Buffalo Sabres</a> game in place of usual singer Doug Allen; the Sabres and Black River are both owned by <a href="/wiki/Terrence_Pegula" title="Terrence Pegula">Terrence Pegula</a>.</p>
<p>In late 2012, a new single titled "Home to Me," was released to digital retailers on August 21, 2012 after being previously featured on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sirius_XM_Radio" title="Sirius XM Radio">Sirius XM</a>'s <a href="/wiki/The_Highway_(Sirius_XM)" title="The Highway (Sirius XM)">The Highway</a> country-music station. It sold over 8,000 copies in the first week of release becoming the top selling digital single from a new female artist since May 2011 when <a href="/wiki/Lauren_Alaina" title="Lauren Alaina">Lauren Alaina</a> released "<a href="/wiki/Like_My_Mother_Does" title="Like My Mother Does">Like My Mother Does</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> "Home to Me" became Darling's first single to chart on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart, reaching a peak of number 34. "Home To Me" was issued to radio, in multiple versions, customized for different states.</p>
<p>However, after releasing a digital EP, <i>Home to Me</i>, on January 29, 2013, Darling and Black River Entertainment parted ways.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Darling released "Little Umbrellas," independently on June 4, 2013 to digital retailers. It was also issued to country radio on June 24, 2013, though it failed to chart.</p>
<p>Darling has been featured on <i>TBS' CONAN, FOX &amp; Friends, HLN Morning Express with Robin Meade, ABC's The Bachelor, Better TV</i> and many additional news outlets. She wrote the song "Knowing What I Know About Heaven," which was recorded and released by Guy Penrod, and has a passion for baking.</p>
<p>Darling was a contestant on <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Rising_Star_(TV_series)" title="Rising Star (TV series)">Rising Star</a></i> first season in 2014, and was eliminated in the fourth week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> However, after her elimination <a href="/wiki/Brad_Paisley" title="Brad Paisley">Brad Paisley</a>, one of the show's judges, invited Darling to perform with him at the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Ole_Opry" title="Grand Ole Opry">Grand Ole Opry</a>.</p>
