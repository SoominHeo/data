<p><b>Sarah Butler</b> (born February 11, 1985) is an American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a>. She starred in the 2010 film <i><a href="/wiki/I_Spit_on_Your_Grave_(2010_film)" title="I Spit on Your Grave (2010 film)">I Spit on Your Grave</a></i>, a remake of the controversial 1978 <a href="/wiki/I_Spit_on_Your_Grave" title="I Spit on Your Grave">film of the same title</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-truitt_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-truitt-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sarah_Butler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A native of <a href="/wiki/Puyallup,_Washington" title="Puyallup, Washington">Puyallup, Washington</a>, Butler was interested in the arts while growing up; she sang in choirs, entered singing competitions, and performed in high school and <a href="/wiki/Community_theatre" title="Community theatre">community theatre</a>. She graduated from <a href="/wiki/Governor_John_R._Rogers_High_School" title="Governor John R. Rogers High School">Rogers High School</a> in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jasmin_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-jasmin-2">[2]</a></sup> Butler relocated to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> to study theatre at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">University of Southern California</a>, and then played <a href="/wiki/Belle_(Disney)" title="Belle (Disney)">Belle</a>, the princess in <i><a href="/wiki/Beauty_and_the_Beast_(musical)" title="Beauty and the Beast (musical)">Beauty and the Beast</a></i>, for a year and a half at <a href="/wiki/Disneyland" title="Disneyland">Disneyland</a>. She dropped out of college, focused on finding a <a href="/wiki/Talent_agent" title="Talent agent">talent agent</a>, and began auditioning for work in television and film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Butler guest-starred on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Miami" title="CSI: Miami">CSI: Miami</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_NY" title="CSI: NY">CSI: NY</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-truitt_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-truitt-1">[1]</a></sup> She was cast in the film <i>A Couple of White Chicks at the Hair Dresser</i> and the 2008 <a href="/wiki/Syfy" title="Syfy">Syfy</a> TV horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Flu_Bird_Horror" title="Flu Bird Horror">Flu Bird Horror</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She had a role that year on the <a href="/wiki/Web_series" title="Web series">web series</a> <i>Luke 11:17</i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Don_Stark" title="Don Stark">Don Stark</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She also had a recurring role on the web series <i><a href="/wiki/I_Heart_Vampires" title="I Heart Vampires">I &lt;3 Vampires</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Butler won the lead in the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Rape_and_revenge_film" title="Rape and revenge film">rape and revenge</a> thriller, <i><a href="/wiki/I_Spit_on_Your_Grave_(2010_film)" title="I Spit on Your Grave (2010 film)">I Spit on Your Grave</a></i>, a remake of the 1978 cult <a href="/wiki/I_Spit_on_Your_Grave" title="I Spit on Your Grave">film of the same title</a>. Although initially put off by the film's nudity and violence, the film's strong <a href="/wiki/Character_arc" title="Character arc">character arc</a> and "<a href="/wiki/Feminism" title="Feminism">feminist</a> edge" helped convince her to take the role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Butler starred as Jennifer Hills, a novelist who is brutally <a href="/wiki/Gang_rape" title="Gang rape">gang raped</a> while staying at a cabin in the woods; her character then seeks revenge on her rapists. Directed by <a href="/wiki/Steven_R._Monroe" title="Steven R. Monroe">Steven R. Monroe</a>, the film was released on October 8, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-truitt_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-truitt-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Butler began filming the <a href="/wiki/Psychological_thriller" title="Psychological thriller">psychological thriller</a>, <i>The Stranger Within</i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Estella_Warren" title="Estella Warren">Estella Warren</a> and <a href="/wiki/William_Baldwin" title="William Baldwin">William Baldwin</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mallorca,_Spain" title="Mallorca, Spain">Mallorca, Spain</a> in November 2011. The film was also scheduled to shoot in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copenhagen,_Denmark" title="Copenhagen, Denmark">Copenhagen, Denmark</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Butler then joined the cast of the independent thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Treachery_(film)" title="Treachery (film)">Treachery</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Michael_Biehn" title="Michael Biehn">Michael Biehn</a>, which began filming in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> in June 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2013, Butler starred in the horror film, <i>The Demented</i>, with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Welch_(actor)" title="Michael Welch (actor)">Michael Welch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kayla_Ewell" title="Kayla Ewell">Kayla Ewell</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Anthology_film" title="Anthology film">anthology film</a>, <i>Tom Holland's Twisted Tales</i>, playing a bomb disposal expert in the segment, "Boom".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In 2014, she played a murder victim in the episode "<a href="/wiki/List_of_Castle_episodes#Season_7_.282014.29" title="List of Castle episodes">Once Upon a Time in the West</a>" of the ABC television drama <i><a href="/wiki/Castle_(TV_series)" title="Castle (TV series)">Castle</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, Butler reprised her role as Jennifer Hills in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I_Spit_on_Your_Grave_III:_Vengeance_is_Mine" title="I Spit on Your Grave III: Vengeance is Mine">I Spit on Your Grave III: Vengeance is Mine</a></i>, a sequel to 2010's <i><a href="/wiki/I_Spit_on_Your_Grave_(2010_film)" title="I Spit on Your Grave (2010 film)">I Spit on Your Grave</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Butler co-starred with <a href="/wiki/Lindsay_Hartley" title="Lindsay Hartley">Lindsay Hartley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Traci_Lords" title="Traci Lords">Traci Lords</a> in the 2016 <a href="/wiki/Lifetime_(TV_network)" title="Lifetime (TV network)">Lifetime</a> TV film, <i><a href="/wiki/Nightmare_Nurse" title="Nightmare Nurse">Nightmare Nurse</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> She also played a comedian in <a href="/wiki/Debra_Eisenstadt" title="Debra Eisenstadt">Debra Eisenstadt</a>'s film, <i>Before the Sun Explodes</i>, which premiered at the 2016 <a href="/wiki/South_by_Southwest" title="South by Southwest">South by Southwest</a> film festival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Butler guest starred on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Grey%27s_Anatomy" title="Grey's Anatomy">Grey's Anatomy</a></i>, playing Danielle in the episode, "<a href="/wiki/Grey%27s_Anatomy_(season_13)#Episodes" title="Grey's Anatomy (season 13)">You Haven't Done Nothin'</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jasmin_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-jasmin-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2017, she starred with <a href="/wiki/Charles_Shaughnessy" title="Charles Shaughnessy">Charles Shaughnessy</a> in <i>Moontrap Target Earth</i>, produced by the makers of the 1989 sci-fi film <i><a href="/wiki/Moontrap" title="Moontrap">Moontrap</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Butler will also appear in the film, <i>Doubting Thomas</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
