<p><b>Sarah Catharine Paulson</b> (born December 17, 1974) is an American actress. After beginning her acting career on stage, she starred in the 1990s television series <i><a href="/wiki/American_Gothic_(1995_TV_series)" title="American Gothic (1995 TV series)">American Gothic</a></i> (1995–96) and <i><a href="/wiki/Jack_%26_Jill_(TV_series)" title="Jack &amp; Jill (TV series)">Jack &amp; Jill</a></i> (1999–2001). Paulson later appeared in comedy films such as <i><a href="/wiki/What_Women_Want" title="What Women Want">What Women Want</a></i> (2000) and <i><a href="/wiki/Down_with_Love" title="Down with Love">Down with Love</a></i> (2003), and had dramatic roles in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Path_to_War" title="Path to War">Path to War</a></i> (2002) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_Bettie_Page" title="The Notorious Bettie Page">The Notorious Bettie Page</a></i> (2005). From 2006 to 2007, Paulson played the role of Harriet Hayes in the NBC comedy-drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Studio_60_on_the_Sunset_Strip" title="Studio 60 on the Sunset Strip">Studio 60 on the Sunset Strip</a></i>, for which she received her first <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe Award</a> nomination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2008, she starred as <a href="/wiki/Ellen_Dolan_(comics)" title="Ellen Dolan (comics)">Ellen Dolan</a> in the superhero noir film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Spirit_(film)" title="The Spirit (film)">The Spirit</a></i>.</p>
<p>Paulson has appeared on Broadway in the plays <i><a href="/wiki/The_Glass_Menagerie" title="The Glass Menagerie">The Glass Menagerie</a></i> (2005) and <i><a href="/wiki/Collected_Stories_(play)" title="Collected Stories (play)">Collected Stories</a></i> (2010).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She also starred in a number of independent films, and had a leading role on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Cupid_(2009_TV_series)" title="Cupid (2009 TV series)">Cupid</a></i> in 2009. She later starred in the independent drama <i><a href="/wiki/Martha_Marcy_May_Marlene" title="Martha Marcy May Marlene">Martha Marcy May Marlene</a></i> (2011), and received <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award" title="Primetime Emmy Award">Primetime Emmy Award</a> and <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe Award</a> nominations for her performance as <a href="/wiki/Nicolle_Wallace" title="Nicolle Wallace">Nicolle Wallace</a> in the <a href="/wiki/HBO_Films" title="HBO Films">HBO film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Game_Change_(film)" title="Game Change (film)">Game Change</a></i> (2012).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-1">[1]</a></sup> Paulson starred as Mary Epps in the 2013 historical drama film <i><a href="/wiki/12_Years_a_Slave_(film)" title="12 Years a Slave (film)">12 Years a Slave</a></i> and as Abby Gerhard in the 2015 drama film <i><a href="/wiki/Carol_(film)" title="Carol (film)">Carol</a></i>. Both films received widespread critical acclaim and were nominated for multiple <a href="/wiki/Academy_Awards" title="Academy Awards">Academy Awards</a>.</p>
<p>In 2011, Paulson began starring in the <a href="/wiki/FX_(TV_channel)" title="FX (TV channel)">FX</a> <a href="/wiki/Anthology_series" title="Anthology series">anthology series</a> <i><a href="/wiki/American_Horror_Story" title="American Horror Story">American Horror Story</a></i>, playing different characters in each of the show's six seasons. For her work throughout the series, she has been nominated for four <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award" title="Primetime Emmy Award">Primetime Emmy Awards</a> (2013–2016) and won two <a href="/wiki/Critics%27_Choice_Television_Award" title="Critics' Choice Television Award">Critics' Choice Television Awards</a> for <a href="/wiki/Critics%27_Choice_Television_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress_in_a_Movie/Miniseries" title="Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Supporting Actress in a Movie/Miniseries">Best Supporting Actress in a Movie/Miniseries</a> (2013 and 2015). In 2016, Paulson portrayed real life prosecutor <a href="/wiki/Marcia_Clark" title="Marcia Clark">Marcia Clark</a> in the first season of the anthology series <i><a href="/wiki/American_Crime_Story" title="American Crime Story">American Crime Story</a></i>, subtitled <i><a href="/wiki/The_People_v._O._J._Simpson:_American_Crime_Story" title="The People v. O. J. Simpson: American Crime Story">The People v. O. J. Simpson</a></i>. For her work in the series, she has been awarded the <a href="/wiki/Critics%27_Choice_Television_Award_for_Best_Actress_in_a_Movie/Miniseries" title="Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Actress in a Movie/Miniseries">Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Actress in a Movie/Miniseries</a>, <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Lead_Actress_in_a_Limited_Series_or_Movie" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Limited Series or Movie">Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Limited Series or Movie</a>, <a href="/wiki/TCA_Award_for_Individual_Achievement_in_Drama" title="TCA Award for Individual Achievement in Drama">TCA Award for Individual Achievement in Drama</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Actress_%E2%80%93_Miniseries_or_Television_Film" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Miniseries or Television Film">Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Miniseries or Television Film</a> as well as the <a href="/wiki/23rd_Screen_Actors_Guild_Awards" title="23rd Screen Actors Guild Awards">Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Female Actor in a Miniseries or Television Movie.</a></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sarah_Paulson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Paulson was born on December 17, 1974 in <a href="/wiki/Tampa,_Florida" title="Tampa, Florida">Tampa, Florida</a>, the daughter of Catharine Gordon (née Dolcater) and Douglas Lyle Paulson II.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She lived in <a href="/wiki/South_Tampa" title="South Tampa">South Tampa</a> until she was 5, when her parents divorced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tampa_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-tampa-5">[5]</a></sup> She then spent time in <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>, before moving to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> at the age of 5 with her mother. She lived in <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gramercy_Park" title="Gramercy Park">Gramercy Park</a>, before settling in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Park_Slope,_Brooklyn" title="Park Slope, Brooklyn">Park Slope, Brooklyn</a>. She spent her summers in Florida visiting her father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tampa_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-tampa-5">[5]</a></sup> Paulson attended <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fiorello_H._LaGuardia_High_School" title="Fiorello H. LaGuardia High School">Fiorello H. LaGuardia High School</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bw_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-bw-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/American_Academy_of_Dramatic_Arts" title="American Academy of Dramatic Arts">American Academy of Dramatic Arts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tampa_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-tampa-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sarah_Paulson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Paulson began working as an actress right out of high school. She appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Horton_Foote" title="Horton Foote">Horton Foote</a> play <i>Talking Pictures</i> at the <a href="/wiki/Signature_Theatre_Company_(New_York_City)" title="Signature Theatre Company (New York City)">Signature Theatre</a>, and in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order" title="Law &amp; Order">Law &amp; Order</a></i> in 1994. Paulson then appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Hallmark" title="Hallmark">Hallmark</a> film <i>Friends at Last</i> (1995) opposite <a href="/wiki/Kathleen_Turner" title="Kathleen Turner">Kathleen Turner</a>. She went on to star in the short-lived television series <i><a href="/wiki/American_Gothic_(1995_TV_series)" title="American Gothic (1995 TV series)">American Gothic</a></i>. Paulson then played Elisa Cronkite in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_WB_Television_Network" title="The WB Television Network">The WB</a> comedy-drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Jack_%26_Jill_(TV_series)" title="Jack &amp; Jill (TV series)">Jack &amp; Jill</a></i> (1999).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Paulson had a minor role in the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Deadwood_(TV_series)" title="Deadwood (TV series)">Deadwood</a></i> and was a focal character in an episode of the critically acclaimed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FX_Networks" title="FX Networks">FX</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Nip/Tuck" title="Nip/Tuck">Nip/Tuck</a></i>. She appeared in the short-lived <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Leap_of_Faith_(TV_series)" title="Leap of Faith (TV series)">Leap of Faith</a></i> as the main character. In 2004, she had a supporting role in the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> series <i>The D.A.</i>, which ended after only a few episodes. Paulson starred in a revival of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Glass_Menagerie" title="The Glass Menagerie">The Glass Menagerie</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> and has appeared <a href="/wiki/Off-Broadway" title="Off-Broadway">Off-Broadway</a> in <i>Killer Joe</i>, and <i>Colder Than Here</i>. Paulson's film credits include <i><a href="/wiki/Down_with_Love" title="Down with Love">Down with Love</a>,</i> <i><a href="/wiki/What_Women_Want" title="What Women Want">What Women Want</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Other_Sister" title="The Other Sister">The Other Sister</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Levitation_(film)" title="Levitation (film)">Levitation</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Serenity_(film)" title="Serenity (film)">Serenity</a></i>.</p>
<p>In the 2006–07 television season, Paulson co-starred in NBC's <i><a href="/wiki/Studio_60_on_the_Sunset_Strip" title="Studio 60 on the Sunset Strip">Studio 60 on the Sunset Strip</a></i> playing <a href="/wiki/Harriet_Hayes" title="Harriet Hayes">Harriet Hayes</a>, one of the stars of the show-within-a-show. This role earned her a nomination for <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress_%E2%80%93_Series,_Miniseries_or_Television_Film" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress – Series, Miniseries or Television Film">Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress – Series, Miniseries or Television Film</a>. In December 2008, Paulson appeared in the <a href="/wiki/The_Spirit_(film)" title="The Spirit (film)">screen adaptation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Will_Eisner" title="Will Eisner">Will Eisner</a>'s comic book <i><a href="/wiki/Spirit_(comics)" title="Spirit (comics)">The Spirit</a></i>, playing an updated version of the character <a href="/wiki/Ellen_Dolan_(comics)" title="Ellen Dolan (comics)">Ellen Dolan</a>.</p>

<p>On August 25, 2008, <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> ordered her pilot <i><a href="/wiki/Cupid_(2009_TV_series)" title="Cupid (2009 TV series)">Cupid</a></i> to series. It was a remake of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cupid_(TV_series)" title="Cupid (TV series)">1998 series</a> starring <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Piven" title="Jeremy Piven">Jeremy Piven</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paula_Marshall" title="Paula Marshall">Paula Marshall</a>. In the new version, Paulson starred opposite <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Cannavale" title="Bobby Cannavale">Bobby Cannavale</a>. It debuted in late March 2009 on ABC but was cancelled on May 19, 2009, after only six episodes. In February 2010, Paulson portrayed the <a href="/wiki/Circa" title="Circa">circa</a> 1982 mother of main character <a href="/wiki/Meredith_Grey" title="Meredith Grey">Meredith Grey</a>, on the ABC drama <i><a href="/wiki/Grey%27s_Anatomy" title="Grey's Anatomy">Grey's Anatomy</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> She played <a href="/wiki/Nicolle_Wallace" title="Nicolle Wallace">Nicolle Wallace</a> in the <a href="/wiki/HBO_Films" title="HBO Films">HBO film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Game_Change_(film)" title="Game Change (film)">Game Change</a></i> (2012), based on events of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_2008" title="United States presidential election, 2008">2008 U.S. presidential election campaign</a>.</p>
<p>In 2011, Paulson guest starred in three episodes of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FX_(channel)" title="FX (channel)">FX</a> <a href="/wiki/Anthology_series" title="Anthology series">anthology series</a> <i><a href="/wiki/American_Horror_Story" title="American Horror Story">American Horror Story</a></i>, playing medium Billie Dean Howard. Paulson returned for season two, <i><a href="/wiki/American_Horror_Story:_Asylum" title="American Horror Story: Asylum">American Horror Story: Asylum</a></i>, in which she played a new character, Lana Winters, a writer who is committed to an asylum for being gay. She appeared in the third season of the series, titled <i><a href="/wiki/American_Horror_Story:_Coven" title="American Horror Story: Coven">American Horror Story: Coven</a></i> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cordelia_Foxx" title="Cordelia Foxx">Cordelia Foxx</a>, a witch who runs an academy for other young witches. She appeared in the fourth season of the show, titled <i><a href="/wiki/American_Horror_Story:_Freak_Show" title="American Horror Story: Freak Show">American Horror Story: Freak Show</a></i>, portraying conjoined twin sisters Bette and Dot Tattler. She returned for the fifth season, subtitled <i><a href="/wiki/American_Horror_Story:_Hotel" title="American Horror Story: Hotel">Hotel</a></i>, portraying the role of Hypodermic Sally.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> She also portrayed Billie Dean Howard again for the last episode of the season. Paulson went on to star in the sixth iteration of the series, subtitled <i><a href="/wiki/American_Horror_Story:_Roanoke" title="American Horror Story: Roanoke">Roanoke</a></i>. She portrayed British actress Audrey Tindall, who appeared in the <i>My Roanoke Nightmare</i> documentary as tortured wife and yoga instructor Shelby Miller.</p>
<p>Paulson has starred in numerous critically acclaimed films. She played the role of Mary Lee in the 2012 film <i><a href="/wiki/Mud_(2012_film)" title="Mud (2012 film)">Mud</a></i>. In 2013, she starred as Mary Epps in the historical drama film <i><a href="/wiki/12_Years_a_Slave_(film)" title="12 Years a Slave (film)">12 Years a Slave</a></i>. She then portrayed Abby Gerhard in the 2015 romantic drama film <i><a href="/wiki/Carol_(film)" title="Carol (film)">Carol</a></i>.</p>
<p>Paulson has also had a successful theatre career. In 2005, she starred as Laura Wingfield in a revival of <a href="/wiki/Tennessee_Williams" title="Tennessee Williams">Tennessee Williams</a>' play <i><a href="/wiki/The_Glass_Menagerie" title="The Glass Menagerie">The Glass Menagerie</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Lange" title="Jessica Lange">Jessica Lange</a>, <a href="/wiki/Josh_Lucas" title="Josh Lucas">Josh Lucas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Christian_Slater" title="Christian Slater">Christian Slater</a>. In 2010, Paulson starred in <a href="/wiki/Donald_Margulies" title="Donald Margulies">Donald Margulies</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Collected_Stories_(play)" title="Collected Stories (play)">Collected Stories</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Linda_Lavin" title="Linda Lavin">Linda Lavin</a>. Paulson starred in a revival of <a href="/wiki/Lanford_Wilson" title="Lanford Wilson">Lanford Wilson</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Talley%27s_Folly" title="Talley's Folly">Talley's Folly</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Danny_Burstein" title="Danny Burstein">Danny Burstein</a> in 2013.</p>
<p>In June 2016, the <a href="/wiki/Human_Rights_Campaign" title="Human Rights Campaign">Human Rights Campaign</a> released a video in tribute to the victims of the <a href="/wiki/2016_Orlando_nightclub_shooting" title="2016 Orlando nightclub shooting">2016 Orlando nightclub shooting</a>; in the video, Paulson and others told the stories of the people killed there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, Paulson starred in the true crime <a href="/wiki/Anthology_series" title="Anthology series">anthology series</a> <i><a href="/wiki/American_Crime_Story" title="American Crime Story">American Crime Story</a></i>. She portrayed prosecutor <a href="/wiki/Marcia_Clark" title="Marcia Clark">Marcia Clark</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_People_v._O._J._Simpson:_American_Crime_Story" title="The People v. O. J. Simpson: American Crime Story">The People v. O.J. Simpson</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> She received widespread praise for her performance and won multiple awards, including the <a href="/wiki/TCA_Award_for_Individual_Achievement_in_Drama" title="TCA Award for Individual Achievement in Drama">TCA Award for Individual Achievement in Drama</a> and her first <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Lead_Actress_in_a_Limited_Series_or_Movie" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Limited Series or Movie">Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Limited Series or Movie</a> for her role in the series. In 2016, it was announced that Paulson would be part of the ensemble cast of the upcoming heist film <i><a href="/wiki/Ocean%27s_Eight" title="Ocean's Eight">Ocean's Eight</a></i>. She will also be starring as <a href="/wiki/Geraldine_Page" title="Geraldine Page">Geraldine Page</a> in the upcoming drama anthology series <i><a href="/wiki/Feud_(TV_series)" title="Feud (TV series)">Feud</a></i>.</p>
<p>In October 2016, <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Murphy_(writer)" title="Ryan Murphy (writer)">Ryan Murphy</a> announced that Paulson will direct an episode of one of his television shows, as part of his Half project. Murphy's initiative involves at least half of one of his four projects being directed by women, people of LGBT status, and different ethnicities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sarah_Paulson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Paulson dated actress <a href="/wiki/Cherry_Jones" title="Cherry Jones">Cherry Jones</a> from 2004 to 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In an interview with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broadway.com" title="Broadway.com">Broadway.com</a> in 2013, referring to her sexuality, she said "the situation is fluid for me."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Prior to her relationship with Jones, she had only dated men, including playwright <a href="/wiki/Tracy_Letts" title="Tracy Letts">Tracy Letts</a> to whom she was engaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Paulson is currently dating actress <a href="/wiki/Holland_Taylor" title="Holland Taylor">Holland Taylor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> At age 25, Paulson developed a <a href="/wiki/Melanoma" title="Melanoma">melanoma</a> on her back. The mole was later removed before the cancer could spread.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
