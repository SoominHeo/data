<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Admiral_of_the_Fleet_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Admiral of the Fleet of the Soviet Union">Admiral of the Fleet of the Soviet Union</a> <b>Sergey Georgiyevich Gorshkov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Серге́й Георгиевич Горшков</span>) (February 26, 1910 – May 13, 1988) was a <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> naval officer during the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a> who oversaw the expansion of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Navy" title="Soviet Navy">Soviet Navy</a> into a global force.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergey_Gorshkov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Kamianets-Podilskyi" title="Kamianets-Podilskyi">Kamianets-Podilskyi</a>, Gorshkov grew up in <a href="/wiki/Kolomna" title="Kolomna">Kolomna</a>. Gorshkov joined the Soviet Navy in 1927. He graduated from the Leningrad M.V. Frunze Higher Naval School in 1931, and gained command of surface boats in the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a> in 1932. During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> he distinguished himself in landings on the <a href="/wiki/Kerch" title="Kerch">Kerch</a> Peninsula and commanded a destroyer squadron at the end of the war. In 1948, he became Chief of Staff of the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea_Fleet" title="Black Sea Fleet">Black Sea Fleet</a>, soon he assumed its full command. He was appointed Commander-in-Chief of the Soviet Navy by <a href="/wiki/Nikita_Khrushchev" title="Nikita Khrushchev">Nikita Khrushchev</a> in 1956, and under <a href="/wiki/Leonid_Brezhnev" title="Leonid Brezhnev">Leonid Brezhnev</a> oversaw a massive naval build-up of surface and submarine forces, creating a force capable of challenging Western naval power by the late 1970s.</p>
<p>Gorshkov is often associated with the phrase "'Better' is the enemy of 'Good Enough'" ("Лучшее - враг хорошего") which is reputed to have hung on the wall of his office as a motto. Similar sentiments have been attributed to <a href="/wiki/Carl_von_Clausewitz" title="Carl von Clausewitz">Clausewitz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Voltaire" title="Voltaire">Voltaire</a>. The motto appears in the <a href="/wiki/Tom_Clancy" title="Tom Clancy">Tom Clancy</a> novel, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunt_for_Red_October" title="The Hunt for Red October">The Hunt for Red October</a></i>. The phrase is also attributed to Admiral Gorshkov in Norman Polmar's <i>Guide to the Soviet Navy</i> (1983, 3rd edition).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> That is one year prior to Clancy's first published date for "Hunt" by the Naval Institute Press.</p>
