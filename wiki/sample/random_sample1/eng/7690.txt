<p><b>Sergey Mikhaylovich Darkin</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Серге́й Миха́йлович Да́рькин</span>) (b. December 9, 1963 in <a href="/wiki/Bolshoy_Kamen" title="Bolshoy Kamen">Bolshoy Kamen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Primorsky_Krai" title="Primorsky Krai">Primorsky Krai</a>) is the former Governor of Primorsky Krai, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergey_Mikhaylovich_Darkin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Darkin attended school in <a href="/wiki/Artyom,_Russia" title="Artyom, Russia">Artyom</a> and later in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vesyoly_Yar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vesyoly Yar (page does not exist)">Vesyoly Yar</a> settlement. After graduating from school he entered the Far Eastern Naval College and simultaneously started work as a docker in the port of <a href="/wiki/Vladivostok" title="Vladivostok">Vladivostok</a>. He graduated from the college with high honors in 1985 and chose to continue with graduate studies. Having completed graduate studies he worked as a <a href="/wiki/Stevedore" title="Stevedore">stevedore</a> in Vladivostok port.</p>
<p>In 1989 he became deputy director of the <i>Dallizing</i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joint_stock_company" title="Joint stock company">joint stock company</a>. In 1991 he founded the <i>Roliz</i> company. The company's activities included shipbuilding and later fishing. In 1998 Darkin also became president of the <i>Primorye Bank</i>. He remained president of <i>Roliz</i> until his election as Governor of Primorsky Krai in 2001.</p>
<p>On May 27, 2001, in the first round of the governorship elections, Darkin received the largest number of votes cast (24%); under the electoral law this was insufficient, however, for him to be elected without a second round of voting. In the run-off election held on June 17, 2001 Darkin obtained a share of the vote of 40.17%. He was sworn in as Governor of Primorsky Krai on June 25, 2001 in succession to Governor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yevgeny_Ivanovich_Nazdratenko" title="Yevgeny Ivanovich Nazdratenko">Yevgeny Ivanovich Nazdratenko</a>, who resigned on February 5, 2001 after imperative request of the President Putin and having a <a href="/wiki/Myocardial_infarction" title="Myocardial infarction">heart attack</a>.</p>
<p>In January 2005, according to the new law of appointing the governors of the Russian <a href="/wiki/Federal_subjects_of_Russia" title="Federal subjects of Russia">federal subjects</a>, Darkin became the first governor in Russia appointed to his post by the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Russia" title="President of Russia">President of Russia</a>. He held this position until 2010, when a new governor, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vladimir_Miklushevsky&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vladimir Miklushevsky (page does not exist)">Vladimir Miklushevsky</a> was appointed.</p>
<p>The governor has three daughters (one of whom was adopted). His youngest daughter, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yaroslava_Darkina&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yaroslava Darkina (page does not exist)">Yaroslava Darkina</a>, was born on April 8, 2001, the day on which Darkin submitted his request to be registered as a candidate in the governorship elections.</p>
<p>Sergey Darkin's hobbies include <a href="/wiki/Volleyball" title="Volleyball">volleyball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuba_diving" title="Scuba diving">scuba diving</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">fishing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hunting" title="Hunting">hunting</a>.</p>
