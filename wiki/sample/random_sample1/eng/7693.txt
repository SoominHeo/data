<p><b>Sergei Vadimovich Stepashin</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Серге́й Вади́мович Степа́шин</span>; born 2 March 1952) is a Russian politician who briefly served as <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Russia" title="Prime Minister of Russia">Prime Minister of Russia</a> in 1999. Currently he is Chairman of the <a href="/wiki/Accounts_Chamber_of_Russia" title="Accounts Chamber of Russia">Accounts Chamber of Russia</a>. He was appointed federal security minister by President <a href="/wiki/Boris_Yeltsin" title="Boris Yeltsin">Boris Yeltsin</a> in 1994. He resigned his position in 1995 as a consequence of the <a href="/wiki/Budyonnovsk_hospital_hostage_crisis" title="Budyonnovsk hospital hostage crisis">Budyonnovsk hospital hostage crisis</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergei_Stepashin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stepashin was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L%C3%BCshunkou" title="Lüshunkou">Lüshunkou, China</a> on 2 March 1952. He graduated from the Higher Political School of the USSR Ministry of the Interior (1973), in 1981 from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lenin_Military-Political_Academy" title="Lenin Military-Political Academy">Lenin Military-Political Academy</a>, and in 2002 from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Finance_University_under_the_Government_of_the_Russian_Federation" title="Finance University under the Government of the Russian Federation">Finance Academy</a>. He is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Law" title="Doctor of Law">Doctor of Law</a>, <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a>, and has a rank of the State Advisor on Justice of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Federation" title="Russian Federation">Russian Federation</a>. His <a href="/wiki/Military_rank" title="Military rank">Military rank</a> is Colonel-General.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergei_Stepashin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stepashin served as the Head of the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Counterintelligence_Service" title="Federal Counterintelligence Service">FSK</a> (the predecessor of the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Security_Service" title="Federal Security Service">FSB</a>) from February 1994 until June 1995. He then became <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Justice_(Russia)" title="Ministry of Justice (Russia)">justice minister</a>, serving from 1997 to March 1998, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interior_minister" title="Interior minister">interior minister</a>, holding that office from March 1998 to May 1999, when he was appointed and confirmed by parliament as <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Russia" title="Prime Minister of Russia">prime minister</a>. Yeltsin made it fairly clear when he appointed him Prime Minister that Stepashin would only hold the position temporarily<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, and he was replaced in August 1999 by future president <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a>.</p>
<p>Stepashin's attitude towards the Chechen conflict was markedly different from that of Vladimir Putin. Stepashin had, for example, presented leaders of the separatist regime in Chechnya with monogrammed pistols, praised the activities of the religious extremists who had taken over several Dagestani villages, and had proclaimed publicly: "We can afford to lose Dagestan!".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tishkov_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-tishkov-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>After having been fired from the position of Prime Minister, Stepashin joined the political party <a href="/wiki/Yabloko" title="Yabloko">Yabloko</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Russian_legislative_election,_1999" title="Russian legislative election, 1999">Russian parliamentary elections of 1999</a> and was elected to the <a href="/wiki/State_Duma" title="State Duma">State Duma</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Lower_house" title="Lower house">lower house</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_Assembly_of_Russia" title="Federal Assembly of Russia">Russian parliament</a>. Later on he resigned his parliamentary seat and became head of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Account_Chamber_of_the_Russian_Federation" title="Account Chamber of the Russian Federation">Account Chamber of the Russian Federation</a>, the federal audit agency. He holds his job to date.</p>
<p>Most recently, he has been asked by lawyers for <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hermitage_Capital&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hermitage Capital (page does not exist)">Hermitage Capital</a>, once among Russia's top foreign investors, to investigate what it says was a series of fake <a href="/wiki/Tax_refund" title="Tax refund">tax refunds</a> which defrauded Russian taxpayers of 11.2 billion roubles ($382 million), according to lawyers Brown Rudnick in a letter to Stepashin.</p>
<p>Since 2007 Stepashin is the head of the revived <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Orthodox_Palestine_Society" title="Imperial Orthodox Palestine Society">Imperial Orthodox Palestine Society</a>.</p>
