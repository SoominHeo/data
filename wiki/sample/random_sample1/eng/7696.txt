<p><b>Districts</b> (<a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a>: <span lang="sr" xml:lang="sr">Окрузи, <i>Okruzi</i></span>), officially called <b>administrative districts</b> (управни окрузи, <i>upravni okruzi</i>) are the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_unit" title="Administrative unit">administrative units</a> of <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>, comprising several <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_and_cities_of_Serbia" title="Municipalities and cities of Serbia">municipalities and/or cities</a> each. They are defined by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Serbia" title="Government of Serbia">Government of Serbia</a>'s <i>Enactment</i> of 29 January 1992. Districts are regional centers of state authority and they do not have any form of self-government. They run affairs in the name of the Government.</p>
<p>There are 29 districts in Serbia (7 in <a href="/wiki/Vojvodina" title="Vojvodina">Vojvodina</a>, 8 in <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0umadija_and_Western_Serbia" title="Šumadija and Western Serbia">Šumadija and Western Serbia</a>, 9 in <a href="/wiki/Southern_and_Eastern_Serbia" title="Southern and Eastern Serbia">Southern and Eastern Serbia</a> and 5 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kosovo_and_Metohija" title="Kosovo and Metohija">Kosovo and Metohija</a><sup class="reference" id="ref_status"><a href="#endnote_status">[a]</a></sup>). The only part of Serbia that is not part of any district is the territory of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_of_Belgrade" title="City of Belgrade">City of Belgrade</a> which has a special status, very similar to that of a district. Every districts has its seat in the largest city of the district.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Term">Term</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_districts_of_Serbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Term">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Slavic_languages" title="Slavic languages">Slavic</a> word <i><a href="/wiki/Okrug" title="Okrug">okrug</a></i> (округ) denotes administrative subdivision in some states. Its etymology is similar to the <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> <i>Kreis</i>, circle (in the meaning of administrative division) (although translated in German as <i>Bezirk</i>): <i>okrug</i> is literally something "encircling". In the subdivisions of <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>, the term is translated as <i>district</i>, sometimes as <i>county</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definition">Definition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_districts_of_Serbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Definition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The territorial organisation of <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a> is regulated by the <i>Law on Territorial Organization</i>, adopted by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Serbia" title="National Assembly of Serbia">National Assembly</a> on 29 December 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-law_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-law-1">[1]</a></sup> According to the Law, the territorial organization of the republic comprises municipalities and cities, the City of Belgrade with special status, and autonomous provinces. Districts are not mentioned in this law but are defined by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Serbia" title="Government of Serbia">Government of Serbia</a>'s <i>Enactment</i> of 29 January 1992. They are defined as the districts as "regional centers of state authority", enacting affairs run by the relevant Ministries.</p>
<p>Serbia is divided into 29 districts (8 in <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0umadija_and_Western_Serbia" title="Šumadija and Western Serbia">Šumadija and Western Serbia</a>, 9 in <a href="/wiki/Southern_and_Eastern_Serbia" title="Southern and Eastern Serbia">Southern and Eastern Serbia</a>, 7 in <a href="/wiki/Vojvodina" title="Vojvodina">Vojvodina</a> and 5 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kosovo_and_Metohija" title="Kosovo and Metohija">Kosovo and Metohija</a>), plus the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_of_Belgrade" title="City of Belgrade">City of Belgrade</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-facts_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-facts-2">[2]</a></sup> The City of Belgrade is not part of any district, but has a special status very similar to that of a district.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Districts_of_Kosovo">Districts of Kosovo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_districts_of_Serbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Districts of Kosovo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Serbian laws treat <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kosovo_and_Metohija" title="Kosovo and Metohija">Kosovo and Metohija</a> as integral part of Serbia (<i>Autonomous Province of Kosovo and Metohija</i>). The <i>Enactment</i> defines five districts on the territory of Kosovo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-law_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-law-1">[1]</a></sup> But, since 1999, Kosovo is under United Nations' administration of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UNMIK" title="UNMIK">UNMIK</a>. In 2000, the UNMIK administration changed territorial organisation on the territory of Kosovo. All five districts were abolished, and seven <i>new</i> districts were created. Serbian government does not recognize this move, and accepts only five pre-2000 districts.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Districts_in_Kosovo_and_Metohija">Districts in Kosovo and Metohija</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_districts_of_Serbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Districts in Kosovo and Metohija">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:M_kosovo02.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="950" data-file-width="835" height="284" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/M_kosovo02.png/250px-M_kosovo02.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/M_kosovo02.png/375px-M_kosovo02.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/M_kosovo02.png/500px-M_kosovo02.png 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:M_kosovo02.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Districts in Kosovo and Metohia.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Five of Serbian Districts are on the territory of <a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Kosovo</a>, comprising 28 municipalities and 1 city. In 2000, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UNMIK" title="UNMIK">UNMIK</a> created 7 new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Districts_of_Kosovo" title="Districts of Kosovo">districts</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and 30 <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Kosovo" title="Municipalities of Kosovo">municipalities</a>. Serbia does not exercise sovereignty over this polity. For the UNMIK districts and the districts of Kosovo, see <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Districts_of_Kosovo" title="Districts of Kosovo">Districts of Kosovo</a>. Because the Serbian government has no control over Kosovo since it declared independence, it was not included in Serbia's 2011 census. For current demographic information about Kosovo, see <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Kosovo#2011_Census" title="Demographics of Kosovo">Kosovo's 2011 census</a>.</p>

<td><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_status"><b><a href="#ref_status">^</a></b></span> <a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Kosovo</a> is the subject of a territorial dispute between the <a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Republic of Kosovo</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Republic of Serbia</a>. The Republic of Kosovo <a href="/wiki/2008_Kosovo_declaration_of_independence" title="2008 Kosovo declaration of independence">unilaterally declared independence</a> on 17 February 2008, but <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serbia%27s_reaction_to_the_2008_Kosovo_declaration_of_independence" title="Serbia's reaction to the 2008 Kosovo declaration of independence">Serbia continues to claim</a> it as part of its <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_Province_of_Kosovo_and_Metohija" title="Autonomous Province of Kosovo and Metohija">own sovereign territory</a>. Kosovo has received recognition as an independent state from <a href="/wiki/International_recognition_of_Kosovo" title="International recognition of Kosovo">110</a> out of 193 <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> <a href="/wiki/Member_states_of_the_United_Nations" title="Member states of the United Nations">member states</a>.</td>
