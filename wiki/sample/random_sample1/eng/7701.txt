<p><b>Sergio Daniel "Checho" Batista</b> (born 9 November 1962) is an <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentine</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> manager and former international player.</p>
<p>As a footballer he played as a <a href="/wiki/Midfield" title="Midfield">midfield</a> for <a href="/wiki/Argentinos_Juniors" title="Argentinos Juniors">Argentinos Juniors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_River_Plate" title="Club Atlético River Plate">River Plate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Nueva_Chicago" title="Club Atlético Nueva Chicago">Nueva Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tosu_Futures" title="Tosu Futures">Tosu Futures</a> and <a href="/wiki/All_Boys" title="All Boys">All Boys</a>. He represented <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">his national team</a> 39 times between 1985 and 1990 where he won the <a href="/wiki/1986_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1986 FIFA World Cup">1986 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> After retiring he moved into coaching with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Bella_Vista" title="Club Atlético Bella Vista">Bella Vista</a> before returning to his former club Argentinos Juniors, as well as spells at <a href="/wiki/Talleres_de_C%C3%B3rdoba" title="Talleres de Córdoba">Talleres</a>, <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Nueva_Chicago" title="Club Atlético Nueva Chicago">Nueva Chicago</a> and <a href="/wiki/Godoy_Cruz_Antonio_Tomba" title="Godoy Cruz Antonio Tomba">Godoy Cruz</a>. With the <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_under-23_football_team" title="Argentina national under-23 football team">Argentine U23 team</a> ,he won the gold medal in the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament">2008 Olympics</a>, which led to him being head coach of the <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina national football team</a> from July 2010 to July 2011.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_career">Playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergio_Batista&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Club">Club</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergio_Batista&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Batista played youth football in <a href="/wiki/Argentinos_Juniors" title="Argentinos Juniors">Argentinos Juniors</a>, and debuted with the first team in 1981. With Argentinos, he won the <a href="/wiki/1984_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n#Campeonato_Metropolitano" title="1984 Argentine Primera División">1984 Metropolitano</a> and <a href="/wiki/1985_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n#Campeonato_Nacional" title="1985 Argentine Primera División">1985 Nacional</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Argentine Primera División">Argentine Primera División</a>, as well as the <a href="/wiki/1985_Copa_Libertadores" title="1985 Copa Libertadores">1985 Copa Libertadores</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1988, Batista moved to <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_River_Plate" title="Club Atlético River Plate">River Plate</a>, helping the team to win the <a href="/wiki/1989%E2%80%9390_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="1989–90 Argentine Primera División">1989–90 league title</a>. Before retiring as a player, Batista played for <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Nueva_Chicago" title="Club Atlético Nueva Chicago">Nueva Chicago</a> and <a href="/wiki/All_Boys" title="All Boys">All Boys</a> in Argentina, as well as <a href="/wiki/Tosu_Futures" title="Tosu Futures">Tosu Futures</a> in Japan.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="National_team">National team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergio_Batista&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: National team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After his impressive displays for Argentinos Juniors in the 1985 Copa Libertadores, the Argentine coach <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Bilardo" title="Carlos Bilardo">Carlos Bilardo</a> called Batista up for a friendly against <a href="/wiki/Mexico_national_football_team" title="Mexico national football team">Mexico</a> on 14 November 1985 in a game that ended in a 1-1 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> With the <a href="/wiki/1986_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1986 FIFA World Cup">1986 FIFA World Cup</a> soon looming, Batista quickly went on to establish himself as a first choice player within the <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina national football team</a>. Despite his limited international experience, he played in every game throughout the tournament, which Argentina eventually won.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> An established regular within the squad, Batista played in the <a href="/wiki/1987_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="1987 Copa América">1987 Copa América</a>, <a href="/wiki/1989_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="1989 Copa América">1989 Copa América</a> and <a href="/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1990 FIFA World Cup">1990 FIFA World Cup</a>, which Argentina came runner-up in.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Managerial_career">Managerial career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergio_Batista&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Managerial career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Batista started his managerial career with <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguayan</a> club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Bella_Vista" title="Club Atlético Bella Vista">Bella Vista</a> in 2000. He then had two spells with <a href="/wiki/Argentinos_Juniors" title="Argentinos Juniors">Argentinos Juniors</a>, one with <a href="/wiki/Talleres_de_C%C3%B3rdoba" title="Talleres de Córdoba">Talleres de Córdoba</a> and one with <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Nueva_Chicago" title="Club Atlético Nueva Chicago">Nueva Chicago</a>. Between 2005 and 2006, he was assistant to <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Ruggeri" title="Oscar Ruggeri">Oscar Ruggeri</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_San_Lorenzo_de_Almagro" title="Club Atlético San Lorenzo de Almagro">San Lorenzo</a>.</p>
<p>In October 2007, the former midfielder was appointed as the head coach of the <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_under-20_football_team" title="Argentina national under-20 football team">Argentine under-20 national team</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Tocalli" title="Hugo Tocalli">Hugo Tocalli</a>. He also managed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argentina_Olympic_football_team" title="Argentina Olympic football team">Argentine Olympic football team</a> that won the gold medal at the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament">2008 Summer Olympics</a>.</p>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 World Cup</a>, Argentine national team manager <a href="/wiki/Diego_Maradona" title="Diego Maradona">Diego Maradona</a> did not renew his contract, and Batista was appointed as <a href="/wiki/Caretaker_manager" title="Caretaker manager">caretaker manager</a> on 27 July 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In his role, Batista led Argentina to two wins (1–0 over <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Ireland</a> and 4–1 over World Cup holders <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a>) and suffered a defeat against <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_football_team" title="Japan national football team">Japan</a> 0–1. Three months later, he was named the official head coach of the Argentine national team. In his first match after being officialized as Argentina's coach, his team defeated <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a> 1–0, with a 90th-minute goal by <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Messi" title="Lionel Messi">Lionel Messi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On 25 July 2011, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argentina_Football_Association" title="Argentina Football Association">AFA</a> announced that Batista had stepped down as manager of the Argentine national team after poor results in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011_Copa_America" title="2011 Copa America">Copa America</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
