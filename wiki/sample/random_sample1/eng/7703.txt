<p><i><b>Trichomonas</b></i> is a genus of anaerobic <a href="/wiki/Excavata" title="Excavata">excavates</a> that are parasites of vertebrates. They are included with the <a href="/wiki/Parabasalid" title="Parabasalid">Parabasalids</a>.</p>
<p>Species of <i>Trichomonas</i> include:</p>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Trichomonas_vaginalis" title="Trichomonas vaginalis">Trichomonas vaginalis</a></i>, an organism generally living inside the human urinogenital tract.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Trichomonas_gallinae" title="Trichomonas gallinae">Trichomonas gallinae</a></i>, which infects birds, causing <b>canker</b> in pigeons and doves, and <b>frounce</b> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Raptor_(bird)" title="Raptor (bird)">raptors</a>. Frounce-like lesions found in the fossil jaws of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tyrannosaurid" title="Tyrannosaurid">tyrannosaurid</a> dinosaurs such as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tyrannosaurus_rex" title="Tyrannosaurus rex">Tyrannosaurus rex</a></i> were probably caused by a parasite similar to <i>Trichomonas gallinae</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wolff_et_al_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wolff_et_al-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Trichomonas_tenax" title="Trichomonas tenax">Trichomonas tenax</a></i>, an organism found in the oral cavity of humans, cats, and dogs</li>
<li><i>Trichomonas termopsidis</i>, found in termite guts<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
