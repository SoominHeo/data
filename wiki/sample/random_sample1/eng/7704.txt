<p><b>Semion Andreyevich Elistratov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Семён Андреевич Елистратов</span>; born 3 May 1990) is a Russian short-track speed-skater. He is the 2014 Olympic champion in the 5000m relay.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Semion_Elistratov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Semion_Elistratov_Moscow_2015.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3456" data-file-width="4608" height="225" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Semion_Elistratov_Moscow_2015.JPG/300px-Semion_Elistratov_Moscow_2015.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Semion_Elistratov_Moscow_2015.JPG/450px-Semion_Elistratov_Moscow_2015.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Semion_Elistratov_Moscow_2015.JPG/600px-Semion_Elistratov_Moscow_2015.JPG 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

Elistratov at the <a href="/wiki/2015_World_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_Championships" title="2015 World Short Track Speed Skating Championships">2015 World Short Track Speed Skating Championships</a></div>

<p>Elistratov competed at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Winter_Olympics" title="2010 Winter Olympics">2010 Winter Olympics</a> for Russia. In the <a href="/wiki/Short_track_speed_skating_at_the_2010_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_500_metres" title="Short track speed skating at the 2010 Winter Olympics – Men's 500 metres">500 metres</a> and <a href="/wiki/Short_track_speed_skating_at_the_2010_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_1000_metres" title="Short track speed skating at the 2010 Winter Olympics – Men's 1000 metres">1000 metres</a>, he placed third in his round one heat, failing to advance, and in the <a href="/wiki/Short_track_speed_skating_at_the_2010_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_1500_metres" title="Short track speed skating at the 2010 Winter Olympics – Men's 1500 metres">1500 metres</a>, he placed fourth, also failing to advance. In all three events he ended up 24th overall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sportsrefprofile_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-sportsrefprofile-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of 2013, Elistratov's best performance at the <a href="/wiki/World_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_Championships" title="World Short Track Speed Skating Championships">World Championships</a> came in <a href="/wiki/2015_World_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_Championships" title="2015 World Short Track Speed Skating Championships">2015</a>, when he won a gold medal in 1500 m individual race. Elistratov won gold medals as a member of the Russian relay team at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=2013_European_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_Championships&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="2013 European Short Track Speed Skating Championships (page does not exist)">2013</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=2014_European_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_Championships&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="2014 European Short Track Speed Skating Championships (page does not exist)">2014 European Championships</a>, and was runner-up in the overall competition at the 2014 European Championships as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-isuprofile_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-isuprofile-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of 2013, Elistratov has two <a href="/wiki/ISU_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_World_Cup" title="ISU Short Track Speed Skating World Cup">ISU Short Track Speed Skating World Cup</a> victories, as part of the relay team in <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_ISU_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_World_Cup" title="2012–13 ISU Short Track Speed Skating World Cup">2012–13</a> at Sochi, and in the 500 metres at <a href="/wiki/Changchun" title="Changchun">Changchun</a> in <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_ISU_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_World_Cup" title="2010–11 ISU Short Track Speed Skating World Cup">2010–11</a>. He also has nine other podium finishes at World Cup events, three as an individual and six in relay races. His top World Cup ranking is 4th, in the 1500 metres in 2012–13.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-isuprofile_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-isuprofile-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 8 March 2016, it was known Elistratov failed a drug test for <a href="/wiki/Meldonium" title="Meldonium">meldonium</a> and was withdrawn from entry list for Russia's Team to compete at the <a href="/wiki/2016_World_Short_Track_Speed_Skating_Championships" title="2016 World Short Track Speed Skating Championships">2016 World Short Track Speed Skating Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, South Korea. He was temporarily suspended from the Russian team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> On April 13, the <a href="/wiki/World_Anti-Doping_Agency" title="World Anti-Doping Agency">World Anti-Doping Agency</a> gave amnesty to athletes with the presence of less than 1 microgram of meldonium in doping samples in tests conducted on athletes before March 1, 2016 is acceptable, WADA cites due to uncertainties and lack of studies for how long <a href="/wiki/Meldonium" title="Meldonium">meldonium</a> stays in the body.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> On 21 April 2016, the <a href="/wiki/International_Skating_Union" title="International Skating Union">International Skating Union</a> lifted its temporary ban on Elistratov and he was reinstated in the team, since the concentration of meldonium was below the threshold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>the International Skating Union's Official Statement was quoted: "In the case of Meldonium, the WADA recognized that there is currently a lack of clear scientific information on excretion time and considers that in certain circumstances there may be grounds for no fault or negligence on the part of the athlete. All mentioned above skaters credibly assured the ISU that they have discontinued the use of Meldonium prior to January 1, 2016, when the substance was included in the prohibited list. In light of the given information, the ISU has decided to lift the provisional suspension imposed on the four skaters, with immediate effect, to stay the results management process and consequently not to disqualify any results at the present stage."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISU16_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ISU16-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
