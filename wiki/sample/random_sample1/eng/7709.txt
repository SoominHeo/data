<p><b>Sebastián Eduardo Leto</b> (born 30 August 1986) is an <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentine</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/Panathinaikos_F.C." title="Panathinaikos F.C.">Panathinaikos</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Winger" title="Midfielder">winger</a> or <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">forward</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sebasti%C3%A1n_Leto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lan.C3.BAs">Lanús</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sebasti%C3%A1n_Leto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Lanús">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Alejandro_Korn,_Buenos_Aires" title="Alejandro Korn, Buenos Aires">Alejandro Korn</a>, Leto began his career with Buenos Aires club <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Lan%C3%BAs" title="Club Atlético Lanús">Lanús</a>, making his first team debut on 25 June 2005. He spent two seasons at the club, making 52 appearances and scoring 8 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Liverpool">Liverpool</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sebasti%C3%A1n_Leto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Liverpool">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 10 August 2007, Leto signed for <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> for £1,850,000 ($3.6million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> His debut was against <a href="/wiki/Toulouse_FC" title="Toulouse FC">Toulouse</a>, in a 4–0 victory for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> qualifying round fixture on 28 August 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following this he featured in another <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> game and two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">Football League Cup</a> matches. He never made his <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> debut due to problems with his passport,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> forcing him to play in the <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C._Reserves_and_Academy" title="Liverpool F.C. Reserves and Academy">Liverpool reserve team</a> instead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Leto was granted an Italian passport in mid-2007 but this was later revoked, making the Argentine need to apply for a work permit; a request which was later refused. Liverpool manager <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Ben%C3%ADtez" title="Rafael Benítez">Rafael Benítez</a> criticised the decision given the player's track record in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> and national selection for the <a href="/wiki/Summer_Olympic_Games" title="Summer Olympic Games">Olympics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Olympiacos">Olympiacos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sebasti%C3%A1n_Leto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Olympiacos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Leto joined the Greek team <a href="/wiki/Olympiacos_F.C." title="Olympiacos F.C.">Olympiacos</a> in June 2008 on a one-year loan deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leto_signs_Olympiakos_loan_deal_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Leto_signs_Olympiakos_loan_deal-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 16 September 2008, Leto scored the second goal in the 2–0 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> first round, first leg victory away against <a href="/wiki/FC_Nordsj%C3%A6lland" title="FC Nordsjælland">FC Nordsjælland</a>. He scored his first and only league goal in a 2–1 home win against Thrassyvoulos in December 2008.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Panathinaikos">Panathinaikos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sebasti%C3%A1n_Leto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Panathinaikos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 23 June 2009, <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> accepted an offer from <a href="/wiki/Panathinaikos_F.C." title="Panathinaikos F.C.">Panathinaikos</a> believed to be in the region of £3,000,000. On 1 July 2009, the player signed a 5-year contract for about 1.2 million (with bonus fees).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On 28 July, Leto made his debut appearance for the greens on the away <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> qualifying stages game against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sparta_Prague" title="Sparta Prague">Sparta Prague</a>, coming from the bench on the second half. On 8 August in Athens, Panathinaikos needed a two-goal margin victory and Leto was one of the starting eleven for the Greek side who dominated the game 3–0 and qualified to the next round. On 19 August, Leto came from the bench to score in Panathinaikos 2–3 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a> in the UEFA Champions League play-off first leg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On 22 August Leto scored two goals against the Cretan side Ergotelis away in a 3–0 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 13 September, Leto scored the only goal against the Thracians Panthrakikos in an important 1–0 away victory. On 24 April 2010 he scored the victorious goal of Panathinaikos at the Greek Cup final against Aris Thessaloniki. The previous week he also won the Greek championship, thus achieving the double (second consequent double of his career, after the first one with Olympiacos in 2009). After the departure of captain <a href="/wiki/Djibril_Ciss%C3%A9" title="Djibril Cissé">Djibril Cissé</a> and vice-captain <a href="/wiki/Gilberto_Silva" title="Gilberto Silva">Gilberto Silva</a> he was chosen after two seasons serving the club to be the 3rd Captain.</p>
<p>Leto started the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Superleague_Greece" title="2011–12 Superleague Greece">2011–12 Superleague Greece</a> in commanding form, netting a brace in a 3–2 victory over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xanthi_FC" title="Xanthi FC">Xanthi</a> on 16 October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He followed this up by notching another brace against <a href="/wiki/Ergotelis_F.C." title="Ergotelis F.C.">Ergotelis</a> on 22 October, in which Panathinaikos ran out 4–0 winners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Leto's good performances were enough for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011-12_Serie_B" title="2011-12 Serie B">Serie B</a> table-toppers <a href="/wiki/Torino_F.C." title="Torino F.C.">Torino</a> to report interest in the Argentine, as they look to regain status in the <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Italian top flight</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 30 October, Panathinaikos faced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/P.A.O.K._F.C." title="P.A.O.K. F.C.">PAOK</a>, Leto grabbed a goal as his side won 3–1 and maintained their lead at the top of the Greek first division.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In the following game against <a href="/wiki/Ergotelis_F.C." title="Ergotelis F.C.">Ergotelis</a>, Leto added two more goals as his side won 4–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Leto's inspired start to the season resulted with ten goals and one assist from the first seven games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> His performances were enough to guide Panathinaikos to the top of the table on 19 points, having played fewer games than most of their title challengers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Leto continued in brilliant form scoring 15 goals in 13 matches as well as 5 assists, keeping Panathinaikos still in the fight for the title.</p>
<p>Through the first 17 league fixtures, Leto lead the goal-scorers chart with 15 league goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On 25 January 2012, Leto suffered a knee injury and flew to Bologna to be checked by a specialist who said that he would be forced out at least until March. It was revealed in March though that he would not be available until at least early April with continued knee problems. After a few days, it was revealed that Leto will not be available for the whole season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On 22 January 2013, Leto terminated his contract with Panathinaikos.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Catania">Catania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sebasti%C3%A1n_Leto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Catania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 16 April 2013, <a href="/wiki/Calcio_Catania" title="Calcio Catania">Catania</a> confirmed that they had signed the Argentine on a deal over a few seasons, the exact details of the length and payment were not released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Sebastian Leto was brought in during the summer 2013, on a free transfer as a replacement for the departed <a href="/wiki/Alejandro_Dar%C3%ADo_G%C3%B3mez" title="Alejandro Darío Gómez">Alejandro Gomez</a>. The former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panathinaikos" title="Panathinaikos">Panathinaikos</a> winger had a history of knee problems, which ultimately led to his release from the Greek club. Catania took a gamble, but he failed to live up to expectations. Leto suffered another knee injury and was being unfairly compared to Gomez, who was arguably the Rossazzuri’s best player, but the Argentinean was sloppy with the ball, wasteful with possession and often very slow. He did not succeed to make an impact in the club, therefore in January 2015, he signed a long year loan with his former Argentinian club <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Lan%C3%BAs" title="Club Atlético Lanús">Lanús</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> On 11 February 2015, Leto has been taken to hospital for emergency treatment after a barbell fell on his head during training. At Italian side <a href="/wiki/Calcio_Catania" title="Calcio Catania">Catania</a>, Leto suffered the injury after a squat exercise in the gym which saw a barbell drop on his head. The 28-year-old, who has been on loan at Argentinian club Lanus since February 3 with a loan fee of €500.000, was initially sent home by the medical staff with a deep wound in his head. But he later went to the hospital after suffering headaches and is now in intensive care. Doctors are not ruling out surgery for the 28-year-old and it is feared Leto may have internal hemorrhaging.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the end of the loan, Cypriot club <a href="/wiki/Anorthosis_Famagusta_FC" title="Anorthosis Famagusta FC">Anorthosis</a> have made an offer for Sebastian Leto, but the Argentine left winger asked for a 48 hours deadline because he prefers to join Greek side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panathinaikos" title="Panathinaikos">Panathinaikos</a>. The 29-year-old playerwas recently released by <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Lan%C3%BAs" title="Club Atlético Lanús">Lanús</a> and wants to return to the Greens after three years, asking for a low-salary contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_spell_at_Panathinaikos">Second spell at Panathinaikos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sebasti%C3%A1n_Leto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Second spell at Panathinaikos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On early February 2015, according to latest news, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panathinaikos" title="Panathinaikos">Panathinaikos</a> will sign Sebastian Leto after being released from <a href="/wiki/Calcio_Catania" title="Calcio Catania">Catania</a>. The manager of <a href="/wiki/U.S._Citt%C3%A0_di_Palermo" title="U.S. Città di Palermo">Palermo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guillermo_Barros_Schelotto" title="Guillermo Barros Schelotto">Guillermo Barros Schelotto</a> spoke about his compatriot and former player at <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Lan%C3%BAs" title="Club Atlético Lanús">Lanús</a>, Sebastian Leto, and his desire to return in Greece for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panathinaikos" title="Panathinaikos">Panathinaikos</a>.<i>"Leto was enjoying being in an argentine club and having his family along, but he always wanted to return in Greece for Panathinaikos. He had a serious accident, but he is strong and despite not playing match in Lanus, in a good fitness condition. Sebastian is a talented and clever player, with quality and still a future ahead of him, he will help Panathinaikos very much and maybe the next season decrease the difference from champions Olympiakos", said the 43-year-old Argentine at</i> SDNA<i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></i></p>
<p>On 5 February 2016, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panathinaikos" title="Panathinaikos">Panathinaikos</a> officially announced the signing of Sebastian Leto until the end of next season and the Argentinian left winger returned to the club after three years. At first, it was said that Leto would wear the team's only retired number, no. 13. Eventually, the player said that he picked no. 5. On 3 April 2016, he helped his club by scoring a brace to win a game against <a href="/wiki/Veria_F.C." title="Veria F.C.">Veria</a> for the Greek <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> At the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_Superleague_Greece" title="2016–17 Superleague Greece">2016–17 Superleague Greece</a> he eventually picked up his favorite number, no.11. On 11 September 2016, he scored his first goal for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2016-17_Superleague_Greece" title="2016-17 Superleague Greece">2016-17 season</a> in a glorious 3-0 away win against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Levadiakos" title="Levadiakos">Levadiakos</a>.</p>
