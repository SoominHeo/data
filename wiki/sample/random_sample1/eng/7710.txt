<p><b>Severobaykalsk</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Северобайка́льск</span>; <a href="/wiki/Buryat_language" title="Buryat language">Buryat</a>: <span lang="bxr" xml:lang="bxr">Хойто-Байгал</span>, <i>Khojto-Bajgal</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Buryatia" title="Republic of Buryatia">Republic of Buryatia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located on the northern end of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Baikal" title="Lake Baikal">Lake Baikal</a> at the mouth of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tyya_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tyya River (page does not exist)">Tyya River</a>, 440 kilometers (270 mi) northwest of <a href="/wiki/Ulan-Ude" title="Ulan-Ude">Ulan-Ude</a> and 490 kilometers (300 mi) northeast of <a href="/wiki/Irkutsk" title="Irkutsk">Irkutsk</a>. Population: <span class="nowrap">24,929 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-3">[3]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">25,434 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopCensus_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-PopCensus-7">[7]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">28,336 (<a href="/wiki/Soviet_Census_(1989)" title="Soviet Census (1989)">1989 Census</a>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census1989_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census1989-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Severobaykalsk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Severobaykalsk is located on a plateau at the northern end of Lake Baikal at the mouth of the Tyya River. To the west the town is surrounded by the <a href="/wiki/Baikal_Mountains" title="Baikal Mountains">Baikal Mountains</a>, to the northeast by the <a href="/wiki/Stanovoy_Range" title="Stanovoy Range">Stanovoy Range</a>. Severobaykalsk is geographically isolated, the closest town is <a href="/wiki/Ust-Kut" title="Ust-Kut">Ust-Kut</a>, more than 260 kilometers (160 mi) away. The closest large cities are <a href="/wiki/Ulan-Ude" title="Ulan-Ude">Ulan-Ude</a>, 440 kilometers (270 mi) to the southeast and <a href="/wiki/Irkutsk" title="Irkutsk">Irkutsk</a>, more than 490 kilometers (300 mi) to the southwest.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Severobaykalsk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The history of Severobaykalsk is closely related to the history of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baikal-Amur_Mainline" title="Baikal-Amur Mainline">Baikal-Amur Mainline</a> (BAM). It was founded in 1974 as a <a href="/wiki/Urban-type_settlement" title="Urban-type settlement">work settlement</a> for workers constructing the BAM, serving as a logistics center and a central starting point for the railway project.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> To the west the railway line was developed to <a href="/wiki/Bratsk" title="Bratsk">Bratsk</a> and to the east to <a href="/wiki/Tynda" title="Tynda">Tynda</a>. At this time the only settlement in the area was the village <a href="/wiki/Nizhneangarsk" title="Nizhneangarsk">Nizhneangarsk</a>, located at the edge of a swampy plain at the northern tip of the lake. It was decided to develop the new town Severobaykalsk 20 kilometers (12 mi) southwest of Nizhneangarsk on a plateau above the lake, thus allowing further extensions and development in the future.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bmerkel_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-bmerkel-9">[9]</a></sup> Originally it was planned to increase the population to 140,000 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first volunteers of the <a href="/wiki/Komsomol" title="Komsomol">Komsomol</a> and workers arrived in 1974 and founded a work camp that would later become the town of Severobaykalsk. The camp was initially named Novogodny (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Новогодний</span>, lit. <i>New Year</i>) and consisted of tents, wooden shacks and railway cars. The camp grew rapidly with the development of the railway, and Severobaykalsk was eventually granted town status in 1980.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sbaikal_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-sbaikal-5">[5]</a></sup> During this time until official completion of the railway line in 1984 the town had a partnership with <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Leningrad</a>. Since completion of the Baikal Amur Mainline the town has been in decline, with many projects cancelled during <a href="/wiki/Perestroika" title="Perestroika">Perestroika</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bmerkel_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-bmerkel-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Population reached a high with an estimated 35,000 inhabitants in the 1980s and subsequently declined. As the town was founded mostly by volunteers of the Komsomol the population is relatively young. Ninety percent of the population is <a href="/wiki/Russian_Orthodox_Church" title="Russian Orthodox Church">Russian Orthodox</a> and ten percent are <a href="/wiki/Buryats" title="Buryats">Buryats</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bmerkel_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-bmerkel-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Severobaykalsk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, it is incorporated as the <b><a href="/wiki/City_of_federal_subject_significance" title="City of federal subject significance">town of republic significance</a> of Severobaykalsk</b>—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_the_Republic_of_Buryatia" title="Administrative divisions of the Republic of Buryatia">districts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref28_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ref28-1">[1]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the town of republic significance of Severobaykalsk is incorporated as <b>Severobaykalsk Urban Okrug</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref405_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ref405-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Severobaykalsk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a Siberian town, Severobaykalsk experiences a <a href="/wiki/Subarctic_climate" title="Subarctic climate">subarctic climate</a>, characterized by extreme variation of temperatures between seasons. Temperatures can be very warm in the summer, and brutally cold in the winter. The warmest month of the year in Severobaykalsk is July, when the mean temperature is +16 °C (61 °F). The coldest month of the year is January, when the mean temperature is only −23 °C (−9 °F).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cityscape">Cityscape</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Severobaykalsk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Cityscape">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Like most Soviet-planned cities, the town center is dominated by five to six floor <a href="/wiki/Tower_block" title="Tower block">high rise buildings</a> made of prefabricated concrete panels. Because the northern region of Lake Baikal is in a seismically active region the standard design of the high rise buildings has been adapted to ensure greater resistance to earthquakes. The suburbs are dominated by shacks that trace back to the early foundation period when Severobaykalsk was a work camp. Some of these shacks are made of old railway cars.</p>
<p>The main street of Severobaykalsk is Leningradsky Avenue, which starts at the railway station and runs through the central area of the town. The railway station is shaped similar to a sail and was designed by architects from Leningrad. In front of the railway station is a monument to volunteers and workers from Leningrad who built the town. North of the railway station, along Leningradsky Avenue is the main square, where the town administration and the <a href="/wiki/Palace_of_Culture" title="Palace of Culture">Palace of Culture</a> of the railway workers are located. Severobaykalsk also has a church and a museum dedicated to the history of the Baikal Amur Mainline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sbaikal_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-sbaikal-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Severobaykalsk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Severobaykalsk is connected by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baikal-Amur_Mainline" title="Baikal-Amur Mainline">Baikal-Amur Mainline</a> to Bratsk and <a href="/wiki/Tayshet" title="Tayshet">Tayshet</a> in the west and Tynda and <a href="/wiki/Komsomolsk-on-Amur" title="Komsomolsk-on-Amur">Komsomolsk-on-Amur</a> and in the east. In the summer <a href="/wiki/Voskhod_(hydrofoil)" title="Voskhod (hydrofoil)">Voskhod hydrofoils</a> connect Severobaykalsk with <a href="/wiki/Irkutsk" title="Irkutsk">Irkutsk</a> via <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Port_Baikal" title="Port Baikal">Port Baikal</a>. By air Severobaykalsk is connected with Irkutsk and Ulan-Ude via <a href="/wiki/Nizhneangarsk_Airport" title="Nizhneangarsk Airport">Nizhneangarsk Airport</a> located 23 kilometers (14 mi) north of the town. There are no road connections to the outside world.</p>
