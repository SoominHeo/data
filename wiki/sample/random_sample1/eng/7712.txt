<p><b>Sebezh</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Се́беж</span>, <a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>: <span lang="pl"><i>Siebież</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sebezhsky_District" title="Sebezhsky District">Sebezhsky District</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pskov_Oblast" title="Pskov Oblast">Pskov Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located in a picturesque setting between Lakes <a href="/wiki/Lake_Sebezhskoye" title="Lake Sebezhskoye">Sebezhskoye</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Orono&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lake Orono (page does not exist)">Orono</a> 189 kilometers (117 mi) south of <a href="/wiki/Pskov" title="Pskov">Pskov</a>, the administrative center of the <a href="/wiki/Oblast" title="Oblast">oblast</a>. Population: <span class="nowrap">6,375 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-5">[5]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">7,138 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopCensus_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-PopCensus-9">[9]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">9,497 (<a href="/wiki/Soviet_Census_(1989)" title="Soviet Census (1989)">1989 Census</a>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census1989_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census1989-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<p>It was first mentioned in 1414 as a fortress protecting <a href="/wiki/Pskov" title="Pskov">Pskov</a> from the south, when <a href="/wiki/Vytautas" title="Vytautas">Vytautas</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a> sacked it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gr_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-gr-7">[7]</a></sup> Prince Ivan <a href="/wiki/Shuysky" title="Shuysky">Shuysky</a> built a wooden fortress there in 1535. In the 16th century, the fortress defended the Western approaches to the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Moscow" title="Grand Duchy of Moscow">Grand Duchy of Moscow</a>. The Poles captured it during the <a href="/wiki/Livonian_War" title="Livonian War">Livonian War</a> and held until the <a href="/wiki/First_Partition_of_Poland" title="First Partition of Poland">First Partition of Poland</a>. The fortifications of Sebezh, now dismantled, were reinforced at the behest of <a href="/wiki/Peter_the_Great" title="Peter the Great">Peter the Great</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Great_Northern_War" title="Great Northern War">Great Northern War</a>. The castle hill is still dominated by the Roman Catholic Church, built in 1625-1648 and reconsecrated as a <a href="/wiki/Russian_Orthodox_Church" title="Russian Orthodox Church">Russian Orthodox Church</a> in 1989.</p>
<p>Until 1927, Sebezh was the seat of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sebezhskly_Uyezd&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sebezhskly Uyezd (page does not exist)">Sebezhskly Uyezd</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pskov_Governorate" title="Pskov Governorate">Pskov Governorate</a> (of <a href="/wiki/Vitebsk_Governorate" title="Vitebsk Governorate">Vitebsk Governorate</a> before 1924).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On August 1, 1927, the uyezds and governorates were abolished and Sebezhsky District, with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> in Sebezh, was established as a part of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Velikiye_Luki_Okrug&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Velikiye Luki Okrug (page does not exist)">Velikiye Luki Okrug</a> of <a href="/wiki/Leningrad_Oblast" title="Leningrad Oblast">Leningrad Oblast</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PskovO1988_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-PskovO1988-11">[11]</a></sup> It included parts of former Sebezhsky Uyezd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SebezhskyDHist_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-SebezhskyDHist-12">[12]</a></sup> On June 3, 1929, Sebezhsky District was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Western_Oblast" title="Western Oblast">Western Oblast</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SebezhskyDHist_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-SebezhskyDHist-12">[12]</a></sup> On July 23, 1930, the okrugs were also abolished and the districts were directly subordinated to the oblast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PskovO1988_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-PskovO1988-11">[11]</a></sup> On January 29, 1935, Western Oblast was abolished and the district was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Tver_Oblast" title="Tver Oblast">Kalinin Oblast</a>, and on February 5 of the same year, Sebezhsky District became a part of Velikiye Luki Okrug of Kalinin Oblast,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PskovO1988_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-PskovO1988-11">[11]</a></sup> one of the okrugs abutting the state boundaries of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>. On May 4, 1938, the district was transferred to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Opochka_Okrug&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Opochka Okrug (page does not exist)">Opochka Okrug</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PskovO1988_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-PskovO1988-11">[11]</a></sup> On February 5, 1941, the okrug was abolished.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PskovO1988_11-4"><a href="#cite_note-PskovO1988-11">[11]</a></sup> Between July 9, 1941 and July 17, 1944, Sebezh was occupied by <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">German</a> troops.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gr_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-gr-7">[7]</a></sup> On August 22, 1944, the district was transferred to newly established <a href="/wiki/Velikiye_Luki_Oblast" title="Velikiye Luki Oblast">Velikiye Luki Oblast</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PskovO1988_11-5"><a href="#cite_note-PskovO1988-11">[11]</a></sup> On October 2, 1957, Velikiye Luki Oblast was abolished and Sebezhsky District was transferred to Pskov Oblast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PskovO1988_11-6"><a href="#cite_note-PskovO1988-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to the 1939 population census, there were 845 Jews living in Sebezh. The SS Division “<a href="/wiki/Totenkopf" title="Totenkopf">Totenkopf</a>” arrived in the town on July 7, 1941. Lots of Jews managed to escape during this period. During the entire occupation, which lasted from July 1941 to July 1944, there was an Ortskommandantur in the town. There was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ordnungsdienst" title="Ordnungsdienst">Ordnungsdienst</a> too. A <a href="/wiki/Ghetto" title="Ghetto">ghetto</a> was created in September 1941 and existed until March 1942. After that, it was liquidated. During the liquidation, more than 100 Jews were shot in pits. The perpetrators of the shooting were local Russian policemen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sebezh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, Sebezh serves as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sebezhsky_District" title="Sebezhsky District">Sebezhsky District</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OKATO_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-OKATO-2">[2]</a></sup> to which it is directly subordinated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Incorp_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Incorp-1">[1]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> of Sebezh, together with sixty-eight rural localities, is incorporated within Sebezhsky Municipal District as <b>Sebezh Urban Settlement</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PskovO_mun_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-PskovO_mun-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sebezh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sebezh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/M9_highway_(Russia)" title="M9 highway (Russia)">M9 Highway</a>, which connects <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Riga" title="Riga">Riga</a>, passes Sebezh. Another road connects Sebezh with <a href="/wiki/Opochka" title="Opochka">Opochka</a>. There are also local roads.</p>
<p>The railway connecting Moscow and Riga also passes Sebezh.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_and_recreation">Culture and recreation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sebezh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Culture and recreation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sebezh contains one cultural heritage monument of federal significance and additionally fifteen objects classified as cultural and historical heritage of local significance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rkn_base_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-rkn_base-14">[14]</a></sup> The federally protected monument is the Trinity Church (formerly a Catholic church), consecrated in 1648 and probably the oldest <a href="/wiki/Baroque" title="Baroque">baroque</a> structure in Russia.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> As of 2013, the church is closed to the public because of reconstruction.</p>
