<p><b>Light of the World</b> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el" xml:lang="el">φώς τοῦ κόσμου</span> <i>Phṓs tou <a href="/wiki/Cosmos" title="Cosmos">kósmou</a></i>) is a phrase <a href="/wiki/Jesus" title="Jesus">Jesus</a> used to describe himself and his <a href="/wiki/Disciple_(Christianity)" title="Disciple (Christianity)">disciples</a> in the <a href="/wiki/New_Testament" title="New Testament">New Testament</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spear226_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spear226-1">[1]</a></sup> The phrase is recorded in the <a href="/wiki/Gospel_of_John" title="Gospel of John">Gospel of John</a> and again in the <a href="/wiki/Gospel_of_Matthew" title="Gospel of Matthew">Gospel of Matthew</a>. It is closely related to the parables of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salt_and_Light" title="Salt and Light">Salt and Light</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lamp_under_a_bushel" title="Lamp under a bushel">Lamp under a bushel</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gospel_accounts">Gospel accounts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Light_of_the_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gospel accounts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Referring_to_himself">Referring to himself</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Light_of_the_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Referring to himself">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a class="external text" href="http://bible.oremus.org/?passage=John+8:12–8:12&amp;version=nrsv" rel="nofollow">John 8:12</a> Jesus applies the title to himself while debating with the Jews and states:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spear226_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Spear226-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>I am the light of the world. Whoever follows me will never walk in darkness, but will have the light of life.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Jesus again claims to be Light of the World in <a class="external text" href="http://bible.oremus.org/?passage=John+9:5–9:5&amp;version=nrsv" rel="nofollow">John 9:5</a>, during the miracle of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Healing_the_blind_at_birth" title="Healing the blind at birth">healing the blind at birth</a>, saying: <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Matera235_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Matera235-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dd>When I am in the world, I am the Light of the World.</dd>
</dl>
<p>This episode leads into <a class="external text" href="http://bible.oremus.org/?passage=John+9:39–9:39&amp;version=nrsv" rel="nofollow">John 9:39</a> where Jesus metaphorically explains that he came to this world, so that the blind may see.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Matera235_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Matera235-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Christology" title="Christology">Christological</a> context, the use of the title Light of the World is similar to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bread_of_Life" title="Bread of Life">Bread of Life</a> title in <a class="external text" href="http://bible.oremus.org/?passage=John+6:35–6:35&amp;version=nrsv" rel="nofollow">John 6:35</a> where Jesus states: “I am the bread of life: he who comes to me shall not hunger.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jonge_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jonge-3">[3]</a></sup> These assertions build on the Christological theme of <a class="external text" href="http://bible.oremus.org/?passage=John+5:26–5:26&amp;version=nrsv" rel="nofollow">John 5:26</a> where Jesus claims to possess life just as the Father does and provide it to those who follow him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jonge_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Jonge-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The term “Life of the World” is applied in the same sense by Jesus to himself in <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Bible_(American_Standard)/John#6:51" title="wikisource:Bible (American Standard)/John">John 6:51</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Matera235_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Matera235-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Referring_to_his_disciples">Referring to his disciples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Light_of_the_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Referring to his disciples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jesus also used that term to refer to his disciples in <a class="external text" href="http://bible.oremus.org/?passage=Matthew+5:14–5:14&amp;version=nrsv" rel="nofollow">Matthew 5:14</a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spear226_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Spear226-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>You are the light of the world. A city on a hill cannot be hidden. Neither do people light a lamp and put it under a bowl. Instead they put it on its stand, and it gives light to everyone in the house. In the same way, let your light shine before men, that they may see your good deeds and praise your Father in heaven.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>This application of "light compared with darkness" also appears in <a class="external text" href="http://bible.oremus.org/?passage=1+John+1:5–1:5&amp;version=nrsv" rel="nofollow">1 John 1:5</a> which applies it to God and states: "God is light, and in him is no darkness at all".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<li><b>Light &amp; Darkness</b></li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>
<dl>
<dd>Light and darkness in John’s gospel is an antithesis that has symbolic meaning and is essential to understanding the author of John. The fourth ::gospel expresses certain ideas using the antithesis more frequently than any other writings in the New Testament. The Johannine community may ::have borrowed the symbolic use of the antithesis Light-Darkness from Essene literature, “which considered History as a permanent conflict between ::Good and Evil, using Light as a symbol of Truth and Righteousness and Darkness as that of Falsehood and Evil”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
</dd>
</dl>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Darkness_in_the_Fourth_Gospel">Darkness in the Fourth Gospel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Light_of_the_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Darkness in the Fourth Gospel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"In John's writings, walking in darkness does not mean committing acts of sin but rather, rejecting God's message of eternal life through Christ"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Those who define darkness as sin also define light as good and righteous. However, in the Gospel of John light is never defined as righteous. Light is defined as life, as seen in John 1:4, “In Him was life; and the life was the light of men”. Those have faith through Him will have eternal life. This “life” is a gift Jesus brought from God into a dying world. In John, “darkness is present in the absence of light; the absence of eternal life,” and darkness referring to death, spiritually.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Those who walk in darkness do not have eternal life because they are unknown to God. The only way to receive "life" is to be known of the light through God and his promises.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Extra-biblical_sources">Extra-biblical sources</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Light_of_the_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Extra-biblical sources">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the non-canonical <a href="/wiki/Gospel_of_Thomas" title="Gospel of Thomas">Gospel of Thomas</a>, a used similar phrase appears, "There is light within a man of light, and he lights up the whole world. If he does not shine, he is darkness".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
