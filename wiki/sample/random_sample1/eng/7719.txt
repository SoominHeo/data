<p><b>Seth Gabel</b> (born October 3, 1981) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> actor. Gabel is known for his roles of agent <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Lee" title="Lincoln Lee">Lincoln Lee</a> on <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a>'s television series <i><a href="/wiki/Fringe_(TV_series)" title="Fringe (TV series)">Fringe</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Cotton_Mather" title="Cotton Mather">Cotton Mather</a> on <a href="/wiki/WGN_America" title="WGN America">WGN America</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Salem_(TV_series)" title="Salem (TV series)">Salem</a></i>. He is a great-nephew of actor <a href="/wiki/Martin_Gabel" title="Martin Gabel">Martin Gabel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-anthem_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-anthem-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seth_Gabel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gabel drew attention for his portrayal of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adrian_Moore_(Nip/Tuck)" title="Adrian Moore (Nip/Tuck)">Adrian Moore</a>, the sexually confused adopted son of <a href="/wiki/Ava_Moore" title="Ava Moore">Ava Moore</a> (portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Famke_Janssen" title="Famke Janssen">Famke Janssen</a>) on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FX_Network" title="FX Network">FX</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Nip/Tuck" title="Nip/Tuck">Nip/Tuck</a></i>. The story arc garnered much attention for their incestuous and emotionally abusive relationship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newyorkmag_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-newyorkmag-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the big screen, Gabel just completed production on <i>The Free World</i>, directed by Jason Lew and Forever, directed by Tatia Pilieva. He starred in the Universal Pictures coming-of-age drama, <i><a href="/wiki/Take_Me_Home_Tonight_(film)" title="Take Me Home Tonight (film)">Take Me Home Tonight</a></i> and appeared in the 2006 film version of Dan Brown’s novel, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)" title="The Da Vinci Code (film)">The Da Vinci Code</a></i>, directed by his father-in-law <a href="/wiki/Ron_Howard" title="Ron Howard">Ron Howard</a>. Television credits include recurring and guest starring roles on <i><a href="/wiki/United_States_of_Tara" title="United States of Tara">United States of Tara</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Closer" title="The Closer">The Closer</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI: Crime Scene Investigation</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sex_and_the_City" title="Sex and the City">Sex and the City</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/100_Centre_Street" title="100 Centre Street">100 Centre Street</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Sidney_Lumet" title="Sidney Lumet">Sidney Lumet</a>. He starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Dirty_Sexy_Money" title="Dirty Sexy Money">Dirty Sexy Money</a></i> as Jeremy Darling, the charming, rebellious and exceedingly idiosyncratic son of the privileged and powerful Darling family, and the fraternal twin of socialite Juliet Darling, played by <a href="/wiki/Samaire_Armstrong" title="Samaire Armstrong">Samaire Armstrong</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newyorkmag_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-newyorkmag-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_United_States_network_television_schedule" title="2010–11 United States network television schedule">2010–11 TV season</a>, Gabel appeared on the FOX series <i><a href="/wiki/Fringe_(TV_series)" title="Fringe (TV series)">Fringe</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Lee" title="Lincoln Lee">Lincoln Lee</a>, a supporting character who is a member of the Fringe Division on the alternate universe. During the third season of the show, he began appearing in a supporting role as the two Lincoln Lees from both universes. Gabel was promoted to the main cast for the <a href="/wiki/Fringe_(season_4)" title="Fringe (season 4)">fourth season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-returning_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-returning-3">[3]</a></sup> His character was written off at the end of that season before returning briefly for the series finale.</p>
<p>On January 30, 2013, Gabel was cast on the episode "Vertigo" of the series <i><a href="/wiki/Arrow_(TV_series)" title="Arrow (TV series)">Arrow</a></i> as a character based on <a href="/wiki/Count_Vertigo" title="Count Vertigo">Count Vertigo</a>, known as the Count.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He plays <a href="/wiki/Cotton_Mather" title="Cotton Mather">Cotton Mather</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Salem_(TV_series)" title="Salem (TV series)">Salem</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2016, Gabel was cast as <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Dahmer" title="Jeffrey Dahmer">Jeffrey Dahmer</a> in the series <i><a href="/wiki/American_Horror_Story" title="American Horror Story">American Horror Story</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seth_Gabel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gabel was born in <a href="/wiki/Hollywood,_Florida" title="Hollywood, Florida">Hollywood, Florida</a>, and is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jewish" title="Jewish">Jewish</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He was raised under the surname of his stepfather, who adopted him, <i>Cosentino</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-anthem_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-anthem-1">[1]</a></sup> He graduated from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_School_of_Nova_Southeastern_University" title="University School of Nova Southeastern University">University School of Nova Southeastern University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Davie,_Florida" title="Davie, Florida">Davie, Florida</a> in 1999, and from <a href="/wiki/New_York_University" title="New York University">New York University</a>'s Tisch School of the Arts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-anthem_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-anthem-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gabel met actress <a href="/wiki/Bryce_Dallas_Howard" title="Bryce Dallas Howard">Bryce Dallas Howard</a> (daughter of actor/director <a href="/wiki/Ron_Howard" title="Ron Howard">Ron Howard</a>) at <a href="/wiki/New_York_University" title="New York University">New York University</a>, and they dated for five years before marrying on June 17, 2006, in <a href="/wiki/Greenwich,_Connecticut" title="Greenwich, Connecticut">Greenwich, Connecticut</a>. They have two children: son Theodore Norman Howard-Gabel (born February 16, 2007), and daughter Beatrice Jean Howard-Gabel (born January 19, 2012).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
