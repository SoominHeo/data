<p><b>Cécile Kashetu Kyenge</b> (<small>IPA: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA" title="Help:IPA">[seˈsil ˈkjɛŋɡe]</a></span>; born <b>Kashetu Kyenge</b> 28 August 1964) is an Italian politician and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ophthalmologist" title="Ophthalmologist">ophthalmologist</a>. She was the Minister for <a href="/wiki/Social_integration" title="Social integration">Integration</a> in the 2013–14 <a href="/wiki/Letta_Cabinet" title="Letta Cabinet">Letta Cabinet</a>. On 25 May 2014 she was elected as a <a href="/wiki/Member_of_the_European_Parliament" title="Member of the European Parliament">Member of the European Parliament</a> (MEP).</p>
<p>After moving to Italy in 1983, she became a qualified ophthalmologist in <a href="/wiki/Modena" title="Modena">Modena</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emilia-Romagna" title="Emilia-Romagna">Emilia-Romagna</a>. She has founded an intercultural Association (DAWA) to promote mutual awareness, integration and cooperation between Italy and Africa, particularly in her country of birth, the Democratic Republic of Congo. She is also the spokesperson of the association "March First", which works to promote the rights of <a href="/wiki/Immigration" title="Immigration">migrants</a> in Italy.</p>
<p>In February 2013 she was elected member of the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies" title="Chamber of Deputies">Chamber of Deputies</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(Italy)" title="Democratic Party (Italy)">Democratic Party</a> in Emilia-Romagna. Two months later she was appointed Minister for Integration in the <a href="/wiki/Grand_coalition" title="Grand coalition">grand coalition</a> government formed by <a href="/wiki/Enrico_Letta" title="Enrico Letta">Enrico Letta</a>, becoming Italy's first <a href="/wiki/Black_people" title="Black people">black</a> cabinet minister.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bacchi2013_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bacchi2013-1">[1]</a></sup> She supports the introduction of a <i><a href="/wiki/Jus_soli" title="Jus soli">Jus soli</a></i> law to grant <a href="/wiki/Citizenship" title="Citizenship">citizenship</a> to children of immigrants born on Italian soil.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=C%C3%A9cile_Kyenge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kyenge was born in Kambove (<a href="/wiki/Haut-Katanga_District" title="Haut-Katanga District">Haut-Katanga District</a>) in the Democratic Republic of Congo on 28 August 1964. She arrived in Italy with a student visa in 1983.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-www.ilmessaggero.it_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-www.ilmessaggero.it-2">[2]</a></sup> She has been married since 1994 with an Italian engineer, Domenico Grisino, and has two daughters named Giulia and Maisha.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gazzettadimodena.gelocal.it_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-gazzettadimodena.gelocal.it-3">[3]</a></sup> She lives in <a href="/wiki/Castelfranco_Emilia" title="Castelfranco Emilia">Castelfranco Emilia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She has a degree in medicine and surgery from <a href="/wiki/Universit%C3%A0_Cattolica_del_Sacro_Cuore" title="Università Cattolica del Sacro Cuore">the Università Cattolica del Sacro Cuore</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-faris_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-faris-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (July 2013)">not in citation given</span></a></i>]</sup> She is a specialist in ophthalmology at <a href="/wiki/University_of_Modena_and_Reggio_Emilia" title="University of Modena and Reggio Emilia">the University of Modena and Reggio Emilia</a>.</p>
<p>In 2002 she founded the Association for Intercultural DAWA ("dawa" is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kiswahili" title="Kiswahili">Kiswahili</a> word meaning "medicine"), with the aim of promoting mutual understanding of different cultures and develop processes of awareness, integration and cooperation between Italy and Africa, particularly in the Democratic Republic of Congo where Kyenge focuses mostly her efforts. Since September 2010 she has been the national spokesperson of the Italian association "March First" that works to promote the rights of migrants. She collaborates with various organizations and associations in national campaigns on the rights of citizenship. She collaborates with many Italian magazines, including <i>Combonifem</i> and <i>Corriere Immigrazione</i>, an online newspaper and a weekly journal on the culture of Italy of the present and future.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_activity">Political activity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=C%C3%A9cile_Kyenge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political activity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2004 Kyenge was elected in a district of the town of <a href="/wiki/Modena" title="Modena">Modena</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Democrats_of_the_Left" title="Democrats of the Left">Democrats of the Left</a> and later became the provincial head of the Forum of International Cooperation and Immigration.</p>
<p>On 7 June 2009 she was elected provincial councilor in Modena for the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(Italy)" title="Democratic Party (Italy)">Democratic Party</a> (PD) and joined the committee Welfare and social policies. She is also responsible for immigration policies in <a href="/wiki/Emilia-Romagna" title="Emilia-Romagna">Emilia-Romagna</a> for the Democratic Party.</p>
<p>On 25 February 2013 she was elected member of the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies" title="Chamber of Deputies">Chamber of Deputies</a> for the PD in Emilia-Romagna.<br/>
Immediately after her election to the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Parliament" title="Italian Parliament">Italian Parliament</a>, with the support of other signatories (<a href="/wiki/Pier_Luigi_Bersani" title="Pier Luigi Bersani">Pier Luigi Bersani</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Khalid_Chaouki&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Khalid Chaouki (page does not exist)">Khalid Chaouki</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Roberto_Speranza&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Roberto Speranza (page does not exist)">Roberto Speranza</a>), she began to promote a plan for a law granting citizenship to children of immigrants born on Italian soil (a so-called <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ius_soli" title="Ius soli">Ius soli</a></i> law).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minister_for_Integration.2C_2013.E2.80.9314">Minister for Integration, 2013–14</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=C%C3%A9cile_Kyenge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Minister for Integration, 2013–14">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2013 Kyenge was appointed Minister for Integration in the <a href="/wiki/Grand_coalition" title="Grand coalition">grand coalition</a> government formed by <a href="/wiki/Enrico_Letta" title="Enrico Letta">Enrico Letta</a>, becoming Italy's first <a href="/wiki/Black_people" title="Black people">black</a> cabinet minister.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bacchi2013_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bacchi2013-1">[1]</a></sup> Her ministerial nomination was repudiated in some circles on cultural and/or racial grounds, being met by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Racist" title="Racist">racist</a> insults from individual politicians belonging to the <a href="/wiki/Lega_Nord" title="Lega Nord">Northern League</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Calderoli" title="Roberto Calderoli">Roberto Calderoli</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vicepresident" title="Vicepresident">VP</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_Senate" title="Italian Senate">Italian Senate</a>, who called her an <a href="/wiki/Orangutan" title="Orangutan">orangutan</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> in addition to racist campaigns orchestrated by the <a href="/wiki/New_Force_(Italy)" title="New Force (Italy)">New Force</a> party and other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Far-right" title="Far-right">far-right</a> groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-adnkronos2013_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-adnkronos2013-9">[9]</a></sup> Her treatment as Italy's first black minister has been described by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Economist" title="The Economist">The Economist</a></i> as "shameful" with "limited condemnation".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Writer and University of Tokyo Professor Flavio Rizzo further analysed the climate around Minister Kyenge in the context of Italian inability to relate to diversity and "the emergence of institutional racism".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Member_of_the_European_Parliament.2C_2014.E2.80.93present">Member of the European Parliament, 2014–present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=C%C3%A9cile_Kyenge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Member of the European Parliament, 2014–present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kyenge was elected to the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">European Parliament</a> in the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament_election,_2014_(Italy)" title="European Parliament election, 2014 (Italy)">elections of 2014</a> as a candidate for the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(Italy)" title="Democratic Party (Italy)">Democratic Party</a> from the constituency of <a href="/wiki/North-East_Italy_(European_Parliament_constituency)" title="North-East Italy (European Parliament constituency)">North-East Italy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> She has since been serving on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Committee_on_Civil_Liberties,_Justice_and_Home_Affairs" title="Committee on Civil Liberties, Justice and Home Affairs">Committee on Civil Liberties, Justice and Home Affairs</a>. In addition to her committee assignments, she currently serves as vice-chairwoman of the parliament's delegation to the <a href="/wiki/ACP%E2%80%93EU_Joint_Parliamentary_Assembly" title="ACP–EU Joint Parliamentary Assembly">ACP–EU Joint Parliamentary Assembly</a>. She is also a member of the European Parliament Intergroup on Integrity (Transparency, Anti-Corruption and Organized Crime).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kyenge led the EU-Election Observer Mission for the <a href="/wiki/Burkinab%C3%A9_general_election,_2015" title="Burkinabé general election, 2015">2015 general election</a> in <a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina Faso">Burkina Faso</a>.</p>
