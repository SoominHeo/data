<p><b>Sead Kolašinac</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_German" title="Help:IPA for German">[ˈsɛ.ad ˈkɔ.la.ʃɪ.nats]</a></span>, <small>Bosnian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Serbo-Croatian" title="Help:IPA for Serbo-Croatian">[ˈse.ad ko.laˈʃi.nat͡s]</a></span>; born 20 June 1993) is a Bosnian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">left back</a> for German club <a href="/wiki/FC_Schalke_04" title="FC Schalke 04">Schalke 04</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina_national_football_team" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina national football team">Bosnia and Herzegovina national team</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sead_Kola%C5%A1inac&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kolašinac began his career in his hometown club, <a href="/wiki/Karlsruher_SC" title="Karlsruher SC">Karlsruher SC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3_Sead_Kola.C5.A1inac_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-3_Sead_Kola.C5.A1inac-2">[2]</a></sup> After eight years of playing for Kalsruhe, Kolašinac joined <a href="/wiki/TSG_1899_Hoffenheim" title="TSG 1899 Hoffenheim">TSG 1899 Hoffenheim</a>, where he played a season, afterwards being bought by <a href="/wiki/VfB_Stuttgart" title="VfB Stuttgart">VfB Stuttgart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3_Sead_Kola.C5.A1inac_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-3_Sead_Kola.C5.A1inac-2">[2]</a></sup> Kolašinac's stay at Stuttgart was short-lived, and a season later, he joined <a href="/wiki/FC_Schalke_04" title="FC Schalke 04">Schalke 04</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3_Sead_Kola.C5.A1inac_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-3_Sead_Kola.C5.A1inac-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Schalke_04">Schalke 04</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sead_Kola%C5%A1inac&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Schalke 04">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After spending a season with the youth team, Kolašinac was promoted to the second team, <a href="/wiki/FC_Schalke_04_II" title="FC Schalke 04 II">Schalke 04 II</a>, in one of the German lower leagues, <a href="/wiki/Regionalliga_West" title="Regionalliga West">Regionalliga West</a>. After playing seven matches and scoring two goals for <a href="/wiki/FC_Schalke_04_II" title="FC Schalke 04 II">Schalke 04 II</a>, he was promoted to the first team. On 15 September 2012, he made his <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> debut in a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/SpVgg_Greuther_F%C3%BCrth" title="SpVgg Greuther Fürth">Greuther Fürth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In December 2012, he played in his first game in European competitions, which was a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Montpellier_HSC" title="Montpellier HSC">Montpellier</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>.</p>
<p>In June 2013 Kolašinac extended his contract with Schalke to June 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> At the beginning of the following season Kolašinac tore the anterior cruciate ligament of his right knee and was out for approximately six months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sead_Kola%C5%A1inac&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kolašinac has a <a href="/wiki/German_passport" title="German passport">German passport</a> and after playing his youth career for <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a>, Kolašinac switched allegiance to his parents' homeland <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina_national_football_team" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina national football team">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a> and received a call-up for the country's senior national team in 2013. His father stated in an interview that was always the player's first choice to play for the Bosnian national team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On 18 November 2013, Kolašinac made his debut for Bosnia in the 2–0 friendly loss against <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He was named in Bosnia's squad for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In the third minute of the team's opening group match, Kolašinac scored an unfortunate own goal, the fastest in World Cup history, as Argentina ran out 2–1 winners at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Est%C3%A1dio_do_Maracan%C3%A3" title="Estádio do Maracanã">Estádio do Maracanã</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sead_Kola%C5%A1inac&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kolašinac was born in <a href="/wiki/Karlsruhe" title="Karlsruhe">Karlsruhe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Württemberg</a>, to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bosniak" title="Bosniak">Bosniak</a> parents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mosque_school_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-mosque_school-10">[10]</a></sup> His father, Faik, works at a <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz" title="Mercedes-Benz">Mercedes</a> factory. Faik was born in <a href="/wiki/Nik%C5%A1i%C4%87" title="Nikšić">Nikšić</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SR_Montenegro" title="SR Montenegro">SR Montenegro</a>, to parents from <a href="/wiki/Plav,_Montenegro" title="Plav, Montenegro">Plav</a>, SR Montenegro; the family moved to <a href="/wiki/%C4%8Capljina" title="Čapljina">Čapljina</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SR_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="SR Bosnia and Herzegovina">SR Bosnia and Herzegovina</a> in 1969, where Faik met his wife.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mosque_school_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-mosque_school-10">[10]</a></sup> The family, including Sead, are practising <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslims</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mosque_school_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-mosque_school-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>As well as having a <a href="/wiki/German_passport" title="German passport">German passport</a> Kolašinac was given a <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina_passport" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina passport">Bosnian passport</a> in 2013 and decided to play for the Bosnian national team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mosque_school_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-mosque_school-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sead_Kola%C5%A1inac&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kolašinac is regarded as a defensive all-rounder which means that he can be deployed in multiple defensive positions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1_Sead_Kola.C5.A1inac_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-1_Sead_Kola.C5.A1inac-11">[11]</a></sup> In Kolašinac's <a href="/wiki/FC_Schalke_04_II" title="FC Schalke 04 II">Schalke 04 II</a> career, he was deployed mostly as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Centre_back" title="Centre back">centre back</a> or <a href="/wiki/Association_football_positions#Defensive_midfield" title="Association football positions">defensive midfielder</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1_Sead_Kola.C5.A1inac_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-1_Sead_Kola.C5.A1inac-11">[11]</a></sup> Schalke head coach <a href="/wiki/Jens_Keller" title="Jens Keller">Jens Keller</a> has primarily deployed Kolašinac as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">left full-back</a>; a playing position in which Kolašinac has received praise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1_Sead_Kola.C5.A1inac_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-1_Sead_Kola.C5.A1inac-11">[11]</a></sup> Kolašinac is deemed to be physically strong and tough in his physique, clever defensively on the pitch and defensively consistent in winning one-on-one duels against his opponents.</p>
