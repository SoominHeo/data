<p>The <b>flag of <a href="/wiki/Seychelles" title="Seychelles">Seychelles</a></b> was adopted on January 8, 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The current flag is the third used by the country since its independence from <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">Britain</a> on June 29, 1976.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The colours used in the current flag are the official colours of two of the nation's major political parties–<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seychelles_People%27s_United_Party" title="Seychelles People's United Party">Seychelles People's United Party</a> and the Seychelles Democratic Party.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Seychelles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The flag consists of five different coloured bands (green, white, red, yellow and blue) starting from one end and diverging towards the other end.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mfa_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-mfa-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MairBeckley2012_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-MairBeckley2012-4">[4]</a></sup> The oblique bands symbolize a dynamic new country moving into the future. The colour blue depicts the sky and the sea that surrounds the Seychelles. Yellow is for the sun which gives light and life, red symbolizes the people and their determination to work for the future in unity and love, while the white band represents social justice and harmony. The green depicts the land and natural environment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mfa_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-mfa-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brittanica_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Brittanica-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Seychelles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The original flag was adopted after independence on June 29, 1976. It had alternating blue and red triangles. Coincidentally the flag was almost identical to the <a href="/wiki/Australian_United_Steam_Navigation_Company" title="Australian United Steam Navigation Company">Australasia's United Steam Navigation Company</a>'s flag.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brittanica_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Brittanica-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1977, when president <a href="/wiki/James_Mancham" title="James Mancham">James Mancham</a> was overthrown by <a href="/wiki/France-Albert_Ren%C3%A9" title="France-Albert René">France-Albert René</a>, the old flag was abolished and the red, white and green flag of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seychelles_People%27s_United_Party" title="Seychelles People's United Party">Seychelles People's United Party</a> came into use and it had a district wavy white stripe. The only significant difference between the national flag and SPUP's flag was the depiction of sun in the party's flag which was not used in the country's flag. When the party lost the majority in the elections, other parties demanded a change in the flag which led to a parliamentary approval of a new proposed design.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brittanica_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Brittanica-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
