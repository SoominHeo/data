<p><b>Saint Martin</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Saint-Martin</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <span lang="nl" xml:lang="nl"><i>Sint Maarten</i></span>) is an <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a> in the northeast <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>, approximately 300Â km (190Â mi) east of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The 87-square-kilometre (34Â sqÂ mi) island is divided roughly 60/40 between the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French Republic</a> (53Â km<sup>2</sup>, 20Â sqÂ mi)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saint_Martin_land_area_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Saint_Martin_land_area-1">[1]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Kingdom of the Netherlands">Kingdom of the Netherlands</a> (34Â km<sup>2</sup>, 13Â sqÂ mi);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sint_Maarten_land_area_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sint_Maarten_land_area-2">[2]</a></sup> but the two parts are roughly equal in population, with slightly more people living on the Dutch side. It is the smallest inhabited <a href="/wiki/List_of_divided_islands" title="List of divided islands">island divided between two nations</a>. The division dates to 1648. The southern Dutch part comprises <a href="/wiki/Sint_Maarten" title="Sint Maarten">Sint Maarten</a> and is one of four constituent countries that form the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Kingdom of the Netherlands">Kingdom of the Netherlands</a>. The northern French part comprises the <a href="/wiki/Collectivity_of_Saint_Martin" title="Collectivity of Saint Martin">CollectivitÃ© de Saint-Martin</a> (Collectivity of St Martin) and is an <a href="/wiki/Overseas_collectivity" title="Overseas collectivity">overseas collectivity</a> of France.</p>
<p>On 1 January 2009, the population of the whole island was 77,741 inhabitants, with 40,917 living on the Dutch side,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sint_Maarten_population_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sint_Maarten_population-3">[3]</a></sup> and 36,824 on the French side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saint_Martin_population_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Saint_Martin_population-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Collectively, the two territories are known as "St-Martin / St Maarten". Sometimes "SXM", the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IATA" title="IATA">IATA</a> identifier for <a href="/wiki/Princess_Juliana_International_Airport" title="Princess Juliana International Airport">Princess Juliana International Airport</a> (the island's main airport), is used to refer to the island. St. Martin (the French portion) received the <a href="/wiki/ISO_3166-1" title="ISO 3166-1">ISO 3166-1</a> code MF in October 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The Dutch part changed in status to a country within the Kingdom of the Netherlands in 2010 and was given the code SX.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>Saint Martin has a land area of 87Â km<sup>2</sup> (34Â sqÂ mi), 53Â km<sup>2</sup> (20Â sqÂ mi) of which is under the sovereignty of France,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saint_Martin_land_area_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Saint_Martin_land_area-1">[1]</a></sup> and 34Â km<sup>2</sup> (13Â sqÂ mi) under the sovereignty of the Kingdom of the Netherlands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sint_Maarten_land_area_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sint_Maarten_land_area-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template"><span style="white-space: nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Link_rot" title="Wikipedia:Link rot"><span title="Â Dead link since August 2016">dead link</span></a></i>]</span></sup> This is the only land border shared by France and the Kingdom of the Netherlands anywhere on Earth, the country Sint Maarten being the sharing <i>part</i> of the Kingdom of the Netherlands.</p>
<p>The main cities are <a href="/wiki/Philipsburg,_Sint_Maarten" title="Philipsburg, Sint Maarten">Philipsburg</a> (Dutch side) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marigot,_St._Martin" title="Marigot, St. Martin">Marigot</a> (French side). The Dutch side is more heavily populated. The largest settlement on the entire island is <a href="/wiki/Lower_Prince%27s_Quarter" title="Lower Prince's Quarter">Lower Prince's Quarter</a>, on the Dutch side.</p>
<p>The highest hilltop is the <a href="/wiki/Pic_Paradis" title="Pic Paradis">Pic Paradis</a> (424 metres (1,391Â ft)) in the center of a hill chain on the French side. Both sides are hilly with large mountain peaks. This forms a valley where many houses are located. There are no rivers on the island, but many dry guts. Hiking trails give access to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dry_forest" title="Dry forest">dry forest</a> covering tops and slopes.</p>
<p>The island is located south of <a href="/wiki/Anguilla" title="Anguilla">Anguilla</a>, separated from the British territory by the <a href="/wiki/Anguilla_Channel" title="Anguilla Channel">Anguilla Channel</a>. Saint Martin is northwest of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Barth%C3%A9lemy" title="Saint BarthÃ©lemy">Saint BarthÃ©lemy</a>, separated from the French territory by the <a href="/wiki/Saint-Barth%C3%A9lemy_Channel" title="Saint-BarthÃ©lemy Channel">Saint-BarthÃ©lemy Channel</a>. It is one of the <a href="/wiki/Renaissance_Islands" title="Renaissance Islands">Renaissance Islands</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Under the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="KÃ¶ppen climate classification">KÃ¶ppen climate classification</a>, the island has a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_monsoon_climate" title="Tropical monsoon climate">tropical monsoon climate</a> with a dry season from January to April and a rainy season from August to December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meteoan_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-meteoan-7">[7]</a></sup> The precipitation patterns are due to the movement of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Azores_high" title="Azores high">Azores high</a> during the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meteoan_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-meteoan-7">[7]</a></sup> With the wind direction predominantly from the east or the northeast, temperatures remain stable throughout the year and temperatures rarely exceed 34Â Â°C (93Â Â°F) or fall below 20Â Â°C (68Â Â°F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meteoan_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-meteoan-7">[7]</a></sup> Temperatures remain steady throughout the year with an average mean temperature of 27.2Â Â°C (81.0Â Â°F). The average sea temperature is 27.2Â Â°C (81.0Â Â°F) ranging from a low of 25.9Â Â°C (78.6Â Â°F) in February to a high of 28.4Â Â°C (83.1Â Â°F) in October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-climate_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-climate-8">[8]</a></sup> The total average yearly rainfall is 1,047Â mm (41.2Â in), with 142 days of measurable rainfall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-climate_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-climate-8">[8]</a></sup> Thunderstorms can occasionally occur, with 18 days with thunder per year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-climate_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-climate-8">[8]</a></sup> Precipitation totals are quite variable from year to year, depending on the number of passing tropical storms and hurricanes.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Saint_Martin" title="History of Saint Martin">History of Saint Martin</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Marigot_flags_St_Martin.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="300" data-file-width="500" height="132" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Marigot_flags_St_Martin.jpg/220px-Marigot_flags_St_Martin.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Marigot_flags_St_Martin.jpg/330px-Marigot_flags_St_Martin.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Marigot_flags_St_Martin.jpg/440px-Marigot_flags_St_Martin.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>In 1493, <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> embarked on his second voyage to the New World. According to legend, Columbus sighted and perhaps anchored at the island of Saint Martin on November 11, 1493, the feast day of Saint <a href="/wiki/Martin_of_Tours" title="Martin of Tours">Martin of Tours</a>. In his honor, Columbus named the island "San Martin".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> This name was translated to "Sint Maarten" (Dutch), "Saint-Martin" (French) and "Saint Martin" in English.</p>
<p>At Columbus' time, St. Martin was populated, if at all, by <a href="/wiki/Island_Caribs" title="Island Caribs">Carib</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amerindian" title="Amerindian">amerindians</a>. The former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arawaks" title="Arawaks">Arawaks</a> had been chased by the Caribs coming from the North coast of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> a short time before the arrival of the <a href="/wiki/Spaniards" title="Spaniards">Spaniards</a> who followed in Columbus' wake.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The Arawaks were agricultural people who fashioned pottery and whose social organization was headed by hereditary chieftains who derived their power from personal deities called <i>zemis</i>. The Caribs' territory was not completely conquered until the mid-17th century when most of them perished in the struggle between the French, English, <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Danes</a> and Spanish for control of the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The Dutch first began to ply the island's ponds for salt in the 1620s.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Despite the Dutch presence on the island, the Spaniards recaptured St. Martin in 1633. One year later, they built a fort (now Ft. Amsterdam) and another <a href="/wiki/Artillery_battery" title="Artillery battery">artillery battery</a> at Pointe Blanche to assert their claim and control access to Great Bay <a href="/wiki/Tropical_salt_pond_ecosystem" title="Tropical salt pond ecosystem">salt pond</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> A massive influx of African slaves<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> took place in the 18th century with the development of <a href="/wiki/Sugarcane" title="Sugarcane">sugarcane</a> plantations by the French <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Protestant" title="Protestant">Protestants</a> and Dutch. Slavery was <a href="/wiki/Abolitionism" title="Abolitionism">abolished</a> in the first half of the 19th century. On some of their territories the British imported <a href="/wiki/Chinese_people" title="Chinese people">Chinese</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ethnic_groups_of_India" title="Ethnic groups of India">East Indians</a> to take the place of slaves. Thus, St. Martin and the other islands are populated by a mixture of Amerindian, European, African, Indian and Asian peoples.</p>

<p>On March 23, 1648, <a href="/wiki/History_of_France" title="History of France">France</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Dutch Republic</a> agreed to divide the island between their two territories, with the signing of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Concordia" title="Treaty of Concordia">Treaty of Concordia</a>. Folklore surrounds the history of the once ever-changing border division between St. Martin and Sint Maarten, and a popular story among locals narrates that "to divide the island into two sections, [in 1648] the inhabitants were told to choose two walkers, one chosen by the French-dominated community and the other one by the Dutch-dominated community, who were put back to back in one extreme of the island, making them walk in opposite directions while stuck to the littoral line, and not allowing them to run. The point where they eventually met was set as the other extreme of the island, and the subsequently created line was chosen as the frontier, dividing Saint-Martin from Sint Maarten. Seemingly, the French walker had walked more than his Dutch counterpart (each one earned his land, respectively, 54Â kmÂ² and 32Â kmÂ²). As the first man chose wine as his stimulant prior to the race, while the latter chose <a href="/wiki/Jenever" title="Jenever">Jenever</a> (Dutch Gin), the difference between such beverages' lightness was said to be the cause of the territorial differences by French locals, while Dutch locals tended to blame the French walker for running."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NewUniversalGeography_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-NewUniversalGeography-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Economy_of_Saint_Martin" title="Economy of Saint Martin">Economy of Saint Martin</a></div>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/INSEE" title="INSEE">INSEE</a> estimated in 2005 the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GDP_per_capita" title="GDP per capita">GDP per capita</a> of Saint Martin for 1999 to be â¬14,500 (about USD15,400).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The main industry of the island is tourism. In 2000, the island had about one million visitors annually. About 85% of the workforce was engaged in the tourist industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On January 1, 2009 the population of the entire island of Saint Martin was 77,741 inhabitants, 40,917 of whom lived on the Dutch side of the island,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sint_Maarten_population_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sint_Maarten_population-3">[3]</a></sup> and 36,824 on the French side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saint_Martin_population_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Saint_Martin_population-4">[4]</a></sup> A local dialect is spoken informally on both sides of the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_and_tourism">Culture and tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Culture and tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_Saint_Martin" title="Culture of Saint Martin">Culture of Saint Martin</a></div>
<p>St. Martin's <a href="/wiki/Sint_Maarten" title="Sint Maarten">Dutch side</a> is known for its festive nightlife, <a href="/wiki/Beach" title="Beach">beaches</a>, jewellery, <a href="/wiki/Drink" title="Drink">drinks</a> made with native <a href="/wiki/Rum" title="Rum">rum</a>-based <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guavaberry" title="Guavaberry">guavaberry</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liquor" title="Liquor">liquors</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Casino" title="Casino">casinos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The island's <a href="/wiki/Collectivity_of_Saint_Martin" title="Collectivity of Saint Martin">French side</a> is known for its <a href="/wiki/Nude_beach" title="Nude beach">nude beaches</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clothes" title="Clothes">clothes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shopping" title="Shopping">shopping</a> (including outdoor markets), and French and Indian Caribbean cuisine. <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> is the most commonly spoken language along with a local <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Netherlands_Antilles_Creole" title="Netherlands Antilles Creole">dialect</a>. The official languages are <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> for Saint-Martin, and both <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a> and English for Sint Maarten. Other common languages include various <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antillean_Creole" title="Antillean Creole">French-based creoles</a> (spoken by immigrants from other French Caribbean islands), <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> (spoken by immigrants from the Dominican Republic and various South American countries), and <a href="/wiki/Papiamento" title="Papiamento">Papiamento</a> (spoken by immigrants from Aruba, Bonaire and CuraÃ§ao).</p>
<p>Among the leading cultural artists of the island are Youth Waves, music band; Isidore "Mighty Dow" York, <a href="/wiki/Kaiso" title="Kaiso">kaisonian</a>, panman; Roland Richardson, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Impressionist" title="Impressionist">Impressionist</a> painter; Ruby Bute, painter; Nicole de Weever, dancer and Broadway star; <a href="/wiki/Lasana_M._Sekou" title="Lasana M. Sekou">Lasana M. Sekou</a>, poet, author, independence advocate; Clara Reyes, choreographer; and Tanny and The Boys, string band music group.</p>
<p>Public buses are the primary mode of transportation for visitors staying on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Traffic on the island has become a major problem. Long traffic jams between Marigot, Philipsburg and the airport are common.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In recent years <a href="/wiki/Sargassum" title="Sargassum">sargassum</a> weed has arrived on the south ("Dutch") shoreline, but the northern French coast is generally free of the weed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Because the island is located along the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intertropical_convergence_zone" title="Intertropical convergence zone">intertropical convergence zone</a>, it is occasionally threatened by tropical storm activity in the late summer and early fall.</p>
<p>Neighbouring islands include <a href="/wiki/Saint_Barth%C3%A9lemy" title="Saint BarthÃ©lemy">Saint BarthÃ©lemy</a> (French), <a href="/wiki/Anguilla" title="Anguilla">Anguilla</a> (British), <a href="/wiki/Saba" title="Saba">Saba</a> (Dutch), <a href="/wiki/Sint_Eustatius" title="Sint Eustatius">Sint Eustatius</a> "Statia" (Dutch), <a href="/wiki/Saint_Kitts" title="Saint Kitts">Saint Kitts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nevis" title="Nevis">Nevis</a> (independent, formerly British). With the exception of Nevis, all of these islands are easily visible on a clear day from St. Martin.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Shopping">Shopping</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Shopping">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shopping on St Maarten and Saint Martin offers duty-free goods in numerous boutiques. Popular goods include local crafts &amp; arts, exotic foods, jewelry, liquor, tobacco, leather goods, as well as most designer goods. Most often the designer goods are offered at significant discounts, often up to 40% lower than EU retail prices.</p>

<p>Saint Martin uses the <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">Euro</a> as its currency, while Sint Maarten uses the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_Antillean_guilder" title="Netherlands Antillean guilder">Netherlands Antillean guilder</a>, pegged at 2,00 per Euro. As a consequence of the dissolution of the Netherlands Antilles, the Netherlands Antillean guilder will cease to be legal tender and be replaced by the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_guilder" title="Caribbean guilder">Caribbean guilder</a> in the coming years. Almost every store on the island also accepts the <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">United States dollar</a>, although sometimes a more expensive exchange rate is used.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Border_checks">Border checks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Border checks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Neither side of the island is part of the <a href="/wiki/Schengen_Area" title="Schengen Area">Schengen Area</a>; full border checks are performed when travelling between the island and Europe. Passport controls are also exercised when taking the ferry from Marigot or Princess Juliana International Airport to <a href="/wiki/Anguilla" title="Anguilla">Anguilla</a>. There are rarely checks at the border between the two sides of the island. The Franco-Dutch treaty on Saint Martin border controls requires a working group that has never been set up, to harmonize external checks at the two main airports. The Dutch side has expressed concern that new and tighter French visa requirements would harm their tourism income.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1994, the Kingdom of the Netherlands and France signed the <a href="/wiki/Franco-Dutch_treaty_on_Saint_Martin_border_controls" title="Franco-Dutch treaty on Saint Martin border controls">Franco-Dutch treaty on Saint Martin border controls</a>, which allows for joint Franco-Dutch border controls on so-called "risk flights". After some delay, the treaty was ratified in November 2006 in the Netherlands, and subsequently entered into force on 1 August 2007. Though the treaty is now in force, its provisions are not yet implemented as the working group specified in the treaty is not yet installed.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
