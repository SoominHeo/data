<p><b>St. Elizabeth</b>, one of <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>'s largest <a href="/wiki/Parishes_of_Jamaica" title="Parishes of Jamaica">parishes</a>, is located in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ordinal_directions" title="Ordinal directions">southwest</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/County" title="County">county</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cornwall,_Jamaica" title="Cornwall, Jamaica">Cornwall</a>. Its capital, <a href="/wiki/Black_River,_Jamaica" title="Black River, Jamaica">Black River</a>, is located at the <a href="/wiki/River_delta" title="River delta">mouth</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Black_River_(Jamaica)" title="Black River (Jamaica)">Black River</a>, the longest on the island.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Elizabeth_Parish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Saint Elizabeth originally included most of the south-west part of the island, but in 1703 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Westmoreland_Parish,_Jamaica" title="Westmoreland Parish, Jamaica">Westmoreland</a> was taken from it and in 1814 a part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchester_Parish,_Jamaica" title="Manchester Parish, Jamaica">Manchester</a>. The resulting areas were named after the wife of Sir Thomas Modyford, the first English <a href="/wiki/Governor" title="Governor">Governor</a> of Jamaica.</p>
<p>There are archeological traces of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_Jamaica" title="Indigenous peoples of Jamaica">Taíno/Arawak</a> existence in the parish, as well as of 17th-century colonial Spanish settlements. After 1655, when the English settled on the island, they concentrated on developing large sugar cane plantations with enslaved African workers. Today, buildings with 'Spanish wall' construction (masonry of limestone sand and stone between wooden frames) can still be seen in some areas.</p>
<p>St Elizabeth became a prosperous parish and Black River an important seaport. In addition to shipping sugar and molasses, Black River became the centre of the <a href="/wiki/Logging" title="Logging">logging</a> trade. Large quantities of logwood were exported to Europe to make a Prussian-blue dye, which was very popular in the 18th and 19th centuries.</p>
<p>St Elizabeth was the first parish to have electric power, where it was first introduced in a house called Waterloo in Black River in 1893.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Elizabeth_Parish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The parish is located <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">latitude</a> 18°15'N, and <a href="/wiki/Longitude" title="Longitude">longitude</a> 77°56'W; to the west of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchester_Parish,_Jamaica" title="Manchester Parish, Jamaica">Manchester</a>, the east of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Westmoreland_Parish,_Jamaica" title="Westmoreland Parish, Jamaica">Westmoreland</a>, and to the south of <a href="/wiki/Saint_James_Parish,_Jamaica" title="Saint James Parish, Jamaica">St. James</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trelawny_Parish,_Jamaica" title="Trelawny Parish, Jamaica">Trelawny</a>. It covers an area of 1212.4 km², making it Jamaica's second-largest parish, behind <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Ann_Parish,_Jamaica" title="Saint Ann Parish, Jamaica">Saint Ann's</a> 1212.6 km². The parish is divided into four electoral districts (constituencies), that is North-East, North-West, South-East and South-West.</p>

<p>The northern and northeastern parts of the parish are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mountainous" title="Mountainous">mountainous</a>. There are three mountain ranges —the Nassau Mountains to the north-east, the Lacovia Mountains to the west of the Nassau Mountains, and the Santa Cruz Mountains which, running south, divide the wide plain to end in a precipitous drop of 1,600 feet (490 m) at Lovers' Leap. The central and southern sections form an extensive <a href="/wiki/Plain" title="Plain">plain</a> divided by the Santa Cruz Mountains. A large part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lowland" title="Lowland">lowlands</a> is covered by morass, but it still provides <a href="/wiki/Grazing" title="Grazing">grazing</a> land for <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horses</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mule" title="Mule">mules</a>.</p>
<p>The main river in the parish is the <a href="/wiki/Black_River_(Jamaica)" title="Black River (Jamaica)">Black River</a>, and measuring 53.4 kilometres (33.2 mi), it is one of the longest rivers in Jamaica. It is navigable for about 40 kilometres (25 mi), and is supported by many <a href="/wiki/Tributary" title="Tributary">tributaries</a> including Y.S., Broad, Grass and Horse Savannah. The river has its source in the mountains of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchester_Parish,_Jamaica" title="Manchester Parish, Jamaica">Manchester</a> where it rises and flows west as the border between Manchester and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trelawny_Parish,_Jamaica" title="Trelawny Parish, Jamaica">Trelawny</a> then goes underground. It reappears briefly in several surrounding towns, but reemerges near Balaclava and tumbles down gorges to the plain known as the Savannah, through the Great Morass and to the sea at Black River, the capital of the parish.</p>
<p>The geology of the parish is primarily alluvial plains to the south, and karstic <a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">limestone</a> to the north. The karstic zones are known to contain over 130 caves (Jamaica Cave Register as of 2007 - from Fincham and JCO). These include Mexico Cave and Wallingford River Cave, near Balaclava, which are two associated sections of a major underground river that has its source in south Trelawny, as well as Yardley Chase Caves near the foot of Lovers' Leap, and Peru Cave, near Goshen, which has <a href="/wiki/Stalactite" title="Stalactite">stalactites</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stalagmite" title="Stalagmite">stalagmites</a>. Mineral deposits include <a href="/wiki/Bauxite" title="Bauxite">bauxite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antimony" title="Antimony">antimony</a>, white limestone, <a href="/wiki/Clay" title="Clay">clay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peat" title="Peat">peat</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silica" title="Silica">silica</a> <a href="/wiki/Sand" title="Sand">sand</a> which is used to manufacture glass.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Demography">Demography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Elizabeth_Parish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The parish had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 148,000 in 2001, 4000 of whom live in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capital_(political)" title="Capital (political)">capital</a> town. The distinct feature of this parish is that numerous <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_group" title="Ethnic group">ethnic groups</a> can be found here; St Elizabeth probably has the greatest ethnic mixture in Jamaica. St. Elizabeth provides the best testimony of the Jamaican motto – "Out of many, one people". The Meskito (corrupted to ‘Mosquito’) Indians brought to Jamaica to help capture the <a href="/wiki/Maroon_(people)" title="Maroon (people)">Maroons</a>, were allowed to settle in southern St. Elizabeth in return for their assistance and given land grants in this parish. This parish has also attracted Dutch, Spanish, Indian, <a href="/wiki/Maroon" title="Maroon">Maroon</a>, mulatto, English, and European inhabitants from the 17th century onwards, with the result that many observers feel that it has more people of mixed-race ancestry than can be found in any other part of the island.</p>
<p>In the 19th century <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Irish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scottish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germans</a>, Chinese, and East Indians migrated to Saint Elizabeth. There are pockets of ethnic concentrations in the parish, including <a href="/wiki/Mulatto" title="Mulatto">Mulatto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Creole_peoples" title="Creole peoples">Creole</a>, notably found in the southeast.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Elizabeth_Parish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mining">Mining</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Elizabeth_Parish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mining">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The parish has been a major producer of <a href="/wiki/Bauxite" title="Bauxite">bauxite</a> since the 1960s. Port Kaiser, near a town called <a href="/wiki/Alligator_Pond" title="Alligator Pond">Alligator Pond</a>, has a leading deep-water <a href="/wiki/Pier" title="Pier">pier</a> for bauxite <a href="/wiki/Export" title="Export">export</a>. The Alpart alumina refinery was constructed in the 1960s at Nain and produces nearly 2 million tonnes of alumina annually for export. The replacement cost of building the refinery is approximately $2 billion.</p>
<p>There are other alumina refineries close to the nearby town of Mandeville.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Agriculture">Agriculture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Elizabeth_Parish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Agriculture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Apart from bauxite mining, the parish also produces a large quantity of Jamaica's <a href="/wiki/Sugar" title="Sugar">sugar</a>; there are two sugar factories in the parish. <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">Fishing</a> is a major <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">industry</a> in the parish, as is tomato canning; a plant is at Bull Savannah. The parish also cultivates <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">crops</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Cassava" title="Cassava">cassava</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cereal" title="Cereal">corn</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peas" title="Peas">peas</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beans" title="Beans">beans</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pimento" title="Pimento">pimento</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ginger" title="Ginger">ginger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">tobacco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tomato" title="Tomato">tomato</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a> sweet potatoes and <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a>. As a result of the fertile soil that provide for grazing fields, <a href="/wiki/Pastoralism" title="Pastoralism">pastoralism</a> is possible. <a href="/wiki/Livestock" title="Livestock">Livestock</a> include <a href="/wiki/Goat" title="Goat">goats</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheep" title="Sheep">sheep</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hog_(swine)" title="Hog (swine)">hogs</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cattle" title="Cattle">cattle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horses</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Elizabeth_Parish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since the 1990s, the parish has become a significant <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourist</a> destination, with most visitors going to the <a href="/wiki/Treasure_Beach" title="Treasure Beach">Treasure Beach</a> area. The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Appleton_Estate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Appleton Estate (page does not exist)">Appleton</a> <a href="/wiki/Rum" title="Rum">rum</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Distillery" title="Distillery">distillery</a>, near the rough <a href="/wiki/Cockpit_Country" title="Cockpit Country">Cockpit Country</a> in the north of the parish, is also a tourist destination. The Cockpit area was the site of Maroon settlements through much of the 18th century. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecological" title="Ecological">Ecological</a> tourism along the Black and YS rivers, and in the Great Morass, has been developed in recent years.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_Places_of_Interest">Other Places of Interest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Elizabeth_Parish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Other Places of Interest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lover%27s_Leap" title="Lover's Leap">Lover's Leap</a> is a cliff plunging several hundred metres into the sea, with an attached romantic legend of two young slaves jumping to their death rather than live apart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> There is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lover%E2%80%99s_Leap_Lighthouse" title="Lover’s Leap Lighthouse">lighthouse</a> here too.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Y.S._Falls&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Y.S. Falls (page does not exist)">Y.S. Falls</a> is a famous falls in <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>, similar to <a href="/wiki/Dunn%27s_River_Falls" title="Dunn's River Falls">Dunn's River Falls</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ocho_Rios" title="Ocho Rios">Ocho Rios</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Ann,_Jamaica" title="Saint Ann, Jamaica">St. Ann</a>.</li>
<li>Bamboo Avenue - was developed in the 17th century when local landowners planted bamboo on both sides of the road to provide shade during their travels.</li>
</ul>
