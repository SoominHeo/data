<p>The <b>flag of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Helena" title="Saint Helena">Saint Helena</a></b> was adopted on October 4, 1984. It is a <a href="/wiki/Defacement_(flag)" title="Defacement (flag)">defaced</a> <a href="/wiki/Blue_Ensign" title="Blue Ensign">Blue Ensign</a>, i.e., a blue field with the <a href="/wiki/Union_Jack" title="Union Jack">Union Jack</a> in the upper hoist-side quadrant and the shield from the <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms_of_Saint_Helena" title="Coat of arms of Saint Helena">coat of arms of Saint Helena</a> centred on the outer half of the flag. The shield features a rocky coastline and three-masted sailing ship, with a <a href="/wiki/Saint_Helena_plover" title="Saint Helena plover">Saint Helena plover</a>, also known as a wirebird, atop. Prior to the adoption of the current coat of arms and flag in 1984, the flag and shield showed the ship and coastal scene taken from the colonial seal of the colony.</p>
<p>Ships registered in Saint Helena fly the <a href="/wiki/Red_Ensign" title="Red Ensign">Red Ensign</a>; there is no defaced variant for the territory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
