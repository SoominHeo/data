<p><i><b>The Salesman</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="fa">فروشنده</span>‎‎ <i>Forushande</i>) is a 2016 Iranian-French <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_film" title="Drama film">drama film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Asghar_Farhadi" title="Asghar Farhadi">Asghar Farhadi</a>. It was included in the In Competition section at the <a href="/wiki/2016_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="2016 Cannes Film Festival">2016 Cannes Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CFF2016_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-CFF2016-1">[1]</a></sup> At Cannes, <a href="/wiki/Shahab_Hosseini" title="Shahab Hosseini">Shahab Hosseini</a> won the award for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Actor_Award_(Cannes_Film_Festival)" title="Best Actor Award (Cannes Film Festival)">Best Actor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asghar_Farhadi" title="Asghar Farhadi">Asghar Farhadi</a> won the award for <a href="/wiki/Best_Screenplay_Award_(Cannes_Film_Festival)" title="Best Screenplay Award (Cannes Film Festival)">Best Screenplay</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Deadline2016_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Deadline2016-3">[3]</a></sup> It was the Iranian submission for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Foreign_Language_Film" title="Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film">Best Foreign Language Film</a> for the <a href="/wiki/89th_Academy_Awards" title="89th Academy Awards">89th Academy Awards</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THR_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-THR-4">[4]</a></sup> and was nominated in that category.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oscars2017_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Oscars2017-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OscarsVar_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-OscarsVar-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Salesman_(2016_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>The Salesman</i> tells the story of a young couple Emad and Rana who play the lead roles in a local rendition of <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Miller" title="Arthur Miller">Arthur Miller</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Death_of_a_Salesman" title="Death of a Salesman">Death of a Salesman</a></i>. Meanwhile, their personal relationship takes a hit after moving into a house that was previously inhabited by a woman who allegedly pursued a career in prostitution.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Salesman_(2016_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film received critical acclaim. It holds a 98% "fresh" rating on <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">review aggregator</a> website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, based on 42 reviews with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weighted_average" title="Weighted average">weighted average</a> score of 7.7/10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, it holds an 84/100.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Owen_Gleiberman" title="Owen Gleiberman">Owen Gleiberman</a> wrote in <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> that "The new movie from Asghar Farhadi, the masterly Iranian director of <i>A Separation</i> and <i>The Past</i>, is another finely cut gem of neorealist suspense."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Donald Clarke's review from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Irish_Times" title="The Irish Times">The Irish Times</a></i> stated that "The latest from Asghar Farhadi sees another family put under stress after a violent attack."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Jo-Ann Titmarsh on <i>HeyUGuys</i> wrote "Asghar Farhadi wowed audiences and critics with <i>A Separation</i> in 2011. Although his next film, French-set <i>The Past</i>, was less successful, the Iranian director’s latest offering was highly anticipated. While lacking the intensity of <i>A Separation</i>, this is an intelligent and interesting film."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Deborah Young in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i> summarized the film as "Masterful particularly in the finale, but not so readily engaging."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
