<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Washing</h1>

<p><b>Washing</b> is a method of cleaning, usually with <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a> and often some kind of <a href="/wiki/Soap" title="Soap">soap</a> or <a href="/wiki/Detergent" title="Detergent">detergent</a>. Washing both body and clothing is an essential part of good <a href="/wiki/Hygiene" title="Hygiene">hygiene</a> and health.</p>
<p>Often people use soaps and detergents to assist in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emulsification" title="Emulsification">emulsification</a> of oils and dirt particles so they can be washed away. The soap can be applied directly, or with the aid of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washcloth" title="Washcloth">washcloth</a>.</p>
<p>People wash themselves, or <a href="/wiki/Bathing" title="Bathing">bathe</a> periodically. Infants, the sick, and people with disabilities are bathed by a <a href="/wiki/Caregiver" title="Caregiver">caregiver</a>, but those that can wash themselves often do so. Often a <a href="/wiki/Shower" title="Shower">shower</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Bathtub" title="Bathtub">bathtub</a> is used for washing. People bathe <a href="/wiki/Nudity" title="Nudity">naked</a> under most circumstances, and commonly do so in the privacy of their home.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, some people use a <a href="/wiki/Bidet" title="Bidet">bidet</a> to wash their external genitalia and the anal region after using the <a href="/wiki/Toilet" title="Toilet">toilet</a>, in addition to using <a href="/wiki/Toilet_paper" title="Toilet paper">toilet paper</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>More frequent is washing of just the <a href="/wiki/Hand" title="Hand">hands</a>, e.g. before and after preparing food and eating, after using the toilet, after handling something dirty, etc. <a href="/wiki/Hand_washing" title="Hand washing">Hand washing</a> is important in reducing the spread of <a href="/wiki/Pathogen" title="Pathogen">germs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> also common is washing the face, which is done after waking up, or to keep oneself cool during the day. <a href="/wiki/Tooth_brushing" title="Tooth brushing">Brushing one's teeth</a> is also essential for hygiene and is a part of washing.</p>
<p>'Washing' can also refer to the <a href="/wiki/Laundry" title="Laundry">washing of clothing</a> or other cloth items, like bed sheets, whether by hand or with a <a href="/wiki/Washing_machine" title="Washing machine">washing machine</a>. It can also refer to <a href="/wiki/Car_wash" title="Car wash">washing one's car</a>, by lathering the exterior with car soap, then rinsing it off with a hose, or <a href="/wiki/Dishwashing" title="Dishwashing">washing cookware</a>.</p>
