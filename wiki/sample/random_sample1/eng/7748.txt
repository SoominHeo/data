<p><b>Sekondi-Takoradi</b>, a city comprising the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twin_cities_(geographical_proximity)" title="Twin cities (geographical proximity)">twin cities</a> of Sekondi and Takoradi, is the capital of <a href="/wiki/Sekondi_Takoradi_Metropolitan_Assembly" title="Sekondi Takoradi Metropolitan Assembly">Sekondi – Takoradi Metropolitan District</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Western_Region_(Ghana)" title="Western Region (Ghana)">Western Region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a>. Sekondi-Takoradi is the region's largest city and an industrial and commercial centre, with a population of 445,205 people (2012).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-World_Gazetteer_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-World_Gazetteer-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<p>The chief industries in Sekondi-Takoradi are Timber, Cocoa processing, Plywood, <a href="/wiki/Shipbuilding" title="Shipbuilding">shipbuilding</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harbor" title="Harbor">Harbor</a> and railway repair, and recently, <a href="/wiki/Sweet_crude_oil" title="Sweet crude oil">sweet crude oil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">crude oil</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-info_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-info-2">[2]</a></sup> Sekondi-Takoradi lies on the main <a href="/wiki/Railway_stations_in_Ghana" title="Railway stations in Ghana">railway</a> lines to <a href="/wiki/Kumasi" title="Kumasi">Kumasi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Accra" title="Accra">Accra</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sekondi-Takoradi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:The_Desert_Air_Force,_1943_TR824.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="583" data-file-width="800" height="182" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/The_Desert_Air_Force%2C_1943_TR824.jpg/250px-The_Desert_Air_Force%2C_1943_TR824.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/The_Desert_Air_Force%2C_1943_TR824.jpg/375px-The_Desert_Air_Force%2C_1943_TR824.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/The_Desert_Air_Force%2C_1943_TR824.jpg/500px-The_Desert_Air_Force%2C_1943_TR824.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

The <a href="/wiki/Desert_Air_Force" title="Desert Air Force">Desert Air Force</a>, 1943; <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a> (RAF) airmen in tropical dress work on the <a href="/wiki/Allison_V-1710" title="Allison V-1710">Allison V-1710</a> <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_engine" title="Aircraft engine">Aircraft engine</a> of a <a href="/wiki/Curtiss_P-40_Warhawk" title="Curtiss P-40 Warhawk">Tomahawk</a> aircraft in a makeshift hangar. <p>Sekondi, older and larger, was the site of Dutch <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fort_Orange_(Ghana)" title="Fort Orange (Ghana)">Fort Orange</a> (1642) and English <a href="/wiki/Fort_Sekondi" title="Fort Sekondi">Fort Sekondi</a> (1682). It prospered from a railroad built in 1903 to hinterland <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mineral</a> and timber resources. Takoradi was the site of Dutch <a href="/wiki/Fort_Witsen" title="Fort Witsen">Fort Witsen</a> (1665) and has an important <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_deep_water_ports&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="List of deep water ports (page does not exist)">deepwater</a> <a href="/wiki/Port" title="Port">seaport</a>, Ghana's first, built in 1928.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-info_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-info-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Port_of_Takoradi_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Port_of_Takoradi-3">[3]</a></sup> During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=RAF_Takoradi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="RAF Takoradi (page does not exist)">RAF Takoradi</a> was an important staging point for British aircraft destined for Egypt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/26_Squadron_SAAF" title="26 Squadron SAAF">26 Squadron SAAF</a> was also based in Takoradi during World War II flying anti-submarine and convoy protection patrols over the Atlantic. A number of South African airmen are buried in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Takoradi_European_Public_cemetery" title="Takoradi European Public cemetery">Takoradi European Public cemetery</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The cities combined in 1946. On 20 November 1969, the city became the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Sekondi%E2%80%93Takoradi" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Sekondi–Takoradi">Roman Catholic Diocese of Sekondi–Takoradi</a>.</p>
<p>The city is currently named (although not officially) as the Oil City of Ghana due to the massive discovery of oil in the western region and has attracted massive migration from people all around the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> For example, of the 248,680 people in the Sekondi-Takoradi metro area in 2010, only 92,000, or well under half, were born in the Western Region of Ghana. 60,000 came from the central region of Ghana, centered on Cape Coast and just to the west of Sekondi-Takoradi. 20,000 from the Ashanti region centered on Kumasi, and 20,000 were born in the Greater Accra Region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sekondi-Takoradi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The population of Sekondi-Takoradi is overwhelmingly Christian. The largest specific group is Pentocastal/Charismatic which makes up 35% of the population, followed by 26% Protestant and 14% Roman Catholics and 11.8% of the population are other Christians. 9% of the population is Muslim. 3.5% of the population has no religion and 0.2% identify as practiontners of traditional religions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sekondi-Takoradi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Takoradi_Harbour" title="Takoradi Harbour">Takoradi Harbour</a></div>
<p>Takoradi is also a port city and has timber, energy, and technology industries. Over the years it has attracted a good number of investors, including <a href="/wiki/Miner" title="Miner">miners</a>, as the city is close to the <a href="/wiki/Mining_community" title="Mining community">mining towns</a> in the western part of Ghana.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Port_of_Takoradi_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Port_of_Takoradi-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourist_attractions">Tourist attractions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sekondi-Takoradi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tourist attractions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sekondi-Takoradi has plenty of beaches, however they are not a major tourist attraction.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Many beaches are also found to the west of Takoradi, with small resorts such as Fanta's Folly, The Hideout, and Green Turtle Lodge with larger ones such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Busua_Beach" title="Busua Beach">Busua Beach</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-info_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-info-2">[2]</a></sup> Lou Moon Lodge and Axim Beach Resort. It also has an annual <a href="/wiki/Street_Carnival_In_Takoradi" title="Street Carnival In Takoradi">street carnival</a>, which is popular with tourists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<p>Sekondi-Takoradi city has several secondary schools, colleges, and special schools, ranging from single-sex to coeducational institutions. Among the tertiary institutions are: Takoradi Polytechnic (a tertiary institution), Nurses and Midwifery Training College, Holy Child Teachers Training College (now a College of Education).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schools_in_Ghana_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Schools_in_Ghana-10">[10]</a></sup> The Sekondi-Takoradi city currently has several secondary schools and Special schools, ranging from single-sex to coeducational institutions. <p>Takoradi Technical Institute houses a <a href="/wiki/Fab_lab" title="Fab lab">Fab lab</a>, equipped by the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">Massachusetts Institute of Technology</a> (MIT), which is the first of its kind in Africa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schools_in_Ghana_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-Schools_in_Ghana-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Takoradi has a well-equipped technical training centre (Takoradi Technical Institute, TTI, 1,400 students in two shifts) assisted by the German government through GTZ/GOPA. Apart from TTI, Takoradi has a polytechnic and other renowned secondary schools including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ghana_Secondary_Technical_School" title="Ghana Secondary Technical School">Ghana Secondary Technical School</a>, St. Mary Secondary School, Bompeh Secondary School, Takoradi Secondary School and many others. Sekondi-Takoradi has many <a href="/wiki/Internet_caf%C3%A9" title="Internet café">internet cafés</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computer_training" title="Computer training">computer training</a> centres to encourage familiarity with computers and acquire computer skills.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/List_of_libraries_in_Ghana" title="List of libraries in Ghana">Western Regional Library</a> was established in Sekondi in 1955.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-saur_2011_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-saur_2011-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
