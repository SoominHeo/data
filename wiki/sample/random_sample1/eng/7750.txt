<p><b>Cepheus</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> in the northern sky. It is named after <a href="/wiki/Cepheus,_King_of_Aethiopia" title="Cepheus, King of Aethiopia">Cepheus, King of Aethiopia</a> in <a href="/wiki/Greek_mythology" title="Greek mythology">Greek mythology</a>. It was one of the 48 constellations listed by the 2nd century astronomer <a href="/wiki/Ptolemy" title="Ptolemy">Ptolemy</a>, and remains one of the 88 modern constellations. Its brightest star is <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Cephei" title="Alpha Cephei">Alpha Cephei</a> with an apparent magnitude of 3.5. <a href="/wiki/Delta_Cephei" title="Delta Cephei">Delta Cephei</a> is the prototype of an important class of star known as a Cepheid variable. <a href="/wiki/RW_Cephei" title="RW Cephei">RW Cephei</a>, an orange hypergiant, together with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_supergiant" title="Red supergiant">red supergiants</a> <a href="/wiki/Mu_Cephei" title="Mu Cephei">Mu Cephei</a>, <a href="/wiki/VV_Cephei" title="VV Cephei">VV Cephei</a> and <a href="/wiki/V354_Cephei" title="V354 Cephei">V354 Cephei</a> are among the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_largest_known_stars" title="List of largest known stars">largest stars known</a>. In addition, Cepheus also has the hyperluminous <a href="/wiki/Quasar" title="Quasar">quasar</a> <a href="/wiki/S5_0014%2B81" title="S5 0014+81">S5 0014+81</a>, hosting an ultramassive <a href="/wiki/Black_hole" title="Black hole">black hole</a> in its core at 40 billion solar masses, about 10,000 times more massive than the <a href="/wiki/Sagittarius_A*" title="Sagittarius A*">central black hole of the Milky Way</a>, making it the <a href="/wiki/List_of_most_massive_black_holes" title="List of most massive black holes">most massive black hole</a> known in the universe.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_mythology">History and mythology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cepheus_(constellation)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and mythology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cepheus was the <a href="/wiki/Cepheus,_King_of_Aethiopia" title="Cepheus, King of Aethiopia">King</a> of <a href="/wiki/Aethiopia" title="Aethiopia">Aethiopia</a>. He was married to <a href="/wiki/Cassiopeia_(mythology)" title="Cassiopeia (mythology)">Cassiopeia</a> and was the father of <a href="/wiki/Andromeda_(mythology)" title="Andromeda (mythology)">Andromeda</a>, both of whom are immortalized as modern day constellations along with Cepheus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-staal_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-staal-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cepheus_(constellation)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:CepheusCC.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="512" data-file-width="343" height="299" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/CepheusCC.jpg/200px-CepheusCC.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/CepheusCC.jpg/300px-CepheusCC.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/37/CepheusCC.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>

The constellation Cepheus as it can be seen by the naked eye.</div>

<p><a href="/wiki/Delta_Cephei" title="Delta Cephei">Delta Cephei</a> is the prototype <a href="/wiki/Cepheid_variable" title="Cepheid variable">Cepheid variable</a>, a yellow-hued supergiant star 980 light-years from Earth. It was discovered to be <a href="/wiki/Variable_star" title="Variable star">variable</a> by <a href="/wiki/John_Goodricke" title="John Goodricke">John Goodricke</a> in 1784. It varies between 3.5<sup><a href="/wiki/Apparent_magnitude" title="Apparent magnitude">m</a></sup> and 4.4<sup>m</sup> over a period of 5 days and 9 hours. The Cepheids are a class of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pulsating_variable_stars&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pulsating variable stars (page does not exist)">pulsating variable stars</a>; Delta Cephei has a minimum size of 40 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_diameter" title="Solar diameter">solar diameters</a> and a maximum size of 46 solar diameters. It is also a <a href="/wiki/Double_star" title="Double star">double star</a>; the yellow star also has a wide-set blue-hued companion of magnitude 6.3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERidpathTirion2001112-113-114.2C115_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERidpathTirion2001112-113-114.2C115-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>There are several other prominent variable stars in Cepheus. One, <a href="/wiki/Mu_Cephei" title="Mu Cephei">μ Cephei</a>, is also known as Herschel's Garnet Star due to its deep red colour. It is a <a href="/wiki/Semiregular_variable_star" title="Semiregular variable star">semiregular variable star</a> with a minimum magnitude of 5.1 and a maximum magnitude of 3.4. Its period is approximately 2 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERidpathTirion2001112-113_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERidpathTirion2001112-113-4">[4]</a></sup> The star is around 11.8 <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_unit" title="Astronomical unit">AU</a> in radius. If it were placed at the center of the <a href="/wiki/Solar_System" title="Solar System">Solar System</a>, it would extend to the orbit of <a href="/wiki/Saturn" title="Saturn">Saturn</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Another, <a href="/wiki/VV_Cephei" title="VV Cephei">VV Cephei</a> A, like Mu Cephei, is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_supergiant" title="Red supergiant">red supergiant</a> and a semiregular variable star, located at least 5,000 light-years from Earth. It has a minimum magnitude of 5.4 and a maximum magnitude of 4.8. One of the largest stars in the galaxy, it has a diameter of 1,300 solar diameters. VV Cephei is also an unusually long-period <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eclipsing_binary" title="Eclipsing binary">eclipsing binary</a>, but the eclipses, which occur every 20.3 years, are too faint to be observed with the unaided eye. <a href="/wiki/T_Cephei" title="T Cephei">T Cephei</a>, also a red giant, is a <a href="/wiki/Mira_variable" title="Mira variable">Mira variable</a> with a minimum magnitude of 11.3 and a maximum magnitude of 5.2, 685 light-years from Earth. It has a period of 13 months and a diameter of 500 solar diameters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERidpathTirion2001112-113_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERidpathTirion2001112-113-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>There are several prominent <a href="/wiki/Double_star" title="Double star">double stars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Binary_star" title="Binary star">binary stars</a> in Cepheus. <a href="/wiki/Omicron_Cephei" title="Omicron Cephei">Omicron Cephei</a> is a binary star with a period of 800 years. The system, 211 light-years from Earth, consists of an orange-hued giant primary of magnitude 4.9 and a secondary of magnitude 7.1. <a href="/wiki/Xi_Cephei" title="Xi Cephei">Xi Cephei</a> is another binary star, 102 light-years from Earth, with a period of 4000 years. It has a blue-white primary of magnitude 4.4 and a yellow secondary of magnitude 6.5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERidpathTirion2001112-113_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERidpathTirion2001112-113-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kruger_60" title="Kruger 60">Kruger 60</a> is an 11th magnitude binary star consisting of two red dwarfs. The star system is one of the nearest, being only 13 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_year" title="Light year">light years</a> away from Earth.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Deep-sky_objects">Deep-sky objects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cepheus_(constellation)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Deep-sky objects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Smoky_Shells.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="682" data-file-width="672" height="223" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Smoky_Shells.jpg/220px-Smoky_Shells.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Smoky_Shells.jpg/330px-Smoky_Shells.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Smoky_Shells.jpg/440px-Smoky_Shells.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Smoky_Shells.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/NGC_7354" title="NGC 7354">NGC 7354</a> is a planetary nebula located in the constellation Cepheus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/NGC_188" title="NGC 188">NGC 188</a> is an <a href="/wiki/Open_cluster" title="Open cluster">open cluster</a> that has the distinction of being the closest open cluster to the north celestial pole, as well as one of the oldest known open clusters.</li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fireworks_Galaxy" title="Fireworks Galaxy">Fireworks Galaxy</a> (<a href="/wiki/New_General_Catalogue" title="New General Catalogue">NGC</a> 6946) is a <a href="/wiki/Spiral_galaxy" title="Spiral galaxy">spiral galaxy</a> in which nine <a href="/wiki/Supernova" title="Supernova">supernovae</a> have been observed, more than in any other <a href="/wiki/Galaxy" title="Galaxy">galaxy</a>.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=IC_469&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="IC 469 (page does not exist)">IC 469</a> is another spiral galaxy, characterized by a compact nucleus, of oval shape, with perceptible side arms.</li>
<li>The nebula <a href="/wiki/NGC_7538" title="NGC 7538">NGC 7538</a> is home to the largest yet discovered <a href="/wiki/Protostar" title="Protostar">protostar</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NGC_7023" title="NGC 7023">NGC 7023</a> is a reflection nebula with an associated star cluster (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Collinder_429&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Collinder 429 (page does not exist)">Collinder 429</a>); it has an overall magnitude of 7.7 and is 1400 light-years from Earth. The nebula and cluster are located near <a href="/wiki/Beta_Cephei" title="Beta Cephei">Beta Cephei</a> and <a href="/wiki/T_Cephei" title="T Cephei">T Cephei</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELevy2005107_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELevy2005107-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/S_155" title="S 155">S 155</a>, also known as the Cave Nebula is a dim and very diffuse bright nebula within a larger nebula complex containing emission, reflection, and dark nebulosity.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Quasar" title="Quasar">quasar</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/6C_B0014%2B8120" title="6C B0014+8120">6C B0014+8120</a> is one of the most powerful objects in the universe, powered by a <a href="/wiki/Supermassive_black_hole" title="Supermassive black hole">supermassive black hole</a> equivalent to 40 billion Suns.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Visualizations">Visualizations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cepheus_(constellation)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Visualizations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sidney_Hall_-_Urania%27s_Mirror_-_Cepheus.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3568" data-file-width="2493" height="243" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Sidney_Hall_-_Urania%27s_Mirror_-_Cepheus.jpg/170px-Sidney_Hall_-_Urania%27s_Mirror_-_Cepheus.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Sidney_Hall_-_Urania%27s_Mirror_-_Cepheus.jpg/255px-Sidney_Hall_-_Urania%27s_Mirror_-_Cepheus.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Sidney_Hall_-_Urania%27s_Mirror_-_Cepheus.jpg/340px-Sidney_Hall_-_Urania%27s_Mirror_-_Cepheus.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>

Cepheus as depicted in <i><a href="/wiki/Urania%27s_Mirror" title="Urania's Mirror">Urania's Mirror</a></i>, a set of constellation cards published in London c. 1825.</div>

<p>Cepheus is most commonly depicted as holding his arms aloft, praying for the gods to spare the life of Andromeda. He is also depicted as a more regal monarch sitting on his throne.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-staal_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-staal-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Equivalents">Equivalents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cepheus_(constellation)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Equivalents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Chinese_astronomy" title="Chinese astronomy">Chinese astronomy</a>, the stars of the constellation <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cepheus_(Chinese_astronomy)" title="Cepheus (Chinese astronomy)">Cepheus</a> are found in two areas: the <a href="/wiki/Purple_Forbidden_enclosure" title="Purple Forbidden enclosure">Purple Forbidden enclosure</a> (紫微垣, <i>Zǐ Wēi Yuán</i>) and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Tortoise_(Chinese_constellation)" title="Black Tortoise (Chinese constellation)">Black Tortoise of the North</a> (北方玄武, <i>Běi Fāng Xuán Wǔ</i>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cepheus_(constellation)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>In the TV sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/3rd_Rock_from_the_Sun" title="3rd Rock from the Sun">3rd Rock from the Sun</a></i>, the aliens' home planet is stated to be located in a <a href="/wiki/Barred_spiral_galaxy" title="Barred spiral galaxy">barred spiral galaxy</a> on the Cepheus-<a href="/wiki/Draco_(constellation)" title="Draco (constellation)">Draco</a> border.</li>
<li>The performer <a href="/wiki/Deadmau5" title="Deadmau5">Deadmau5</a> named his song, "<a href="/wiki/HR_8938_Cephei" title="HR 8938 Cephei">HR 8938 Cephei</a>" after a star in the constellation.</li>
</ul>
