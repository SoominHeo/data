<p><b>Shen Duo</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">沈铎</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Shēn Duō</span></i>; born 9 June 1997) is a Chinese competitive <a href="/wiki/Swimming_(sport)" title="Swimming (sport)">swimmer</a> who specializes in sprint freestyle events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-incheon2014_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-incheon2014-1">[1]</a></sup> Considered one of the most promising swimmers in the international scene, she currently holds three world junior records each in the 100 m freestyle, the 400 m medley relay, and the mixed 400 m freestyle relay, and also pocketed a total of nine gold medals to her career hardware in two major meets at the peak of 2014 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shen_Duo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When China hosted the <a href="/wiki/2014_Summer_Youth_Olympics" title="2014 Summer Youth Olympics">2014 Summer Youth Olympics</a> in her home city <a href="/wiki/Nanjing" title="Nanjing">Nanjing</a>, Shen won five gold medals and broke three world junior records in swimming. Shen helped the Chinese foursome scorch the final field of swimmers with two astonishing world junior records each in the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2014_Summer_Youth_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Mixed_4_%C3%97_100_metre_freestyle_relay" title="Swimming at the 2014 Summer Youth Olympics – Mixed 4 × 100 metre freestyle relay">mixed 4×100 m freestyle</a> (3:27.02) and in the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2014_Summer_Youth_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Girls%27_4_%C3%97_100_metre_medley_relay" title="Swimming at the 2014 Summer Youth Olympics – Girls' 4 × 100 metre medley relay">girls' 4×100 m medley</a> (4:03.58).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Two days later, she continued to smash another world record with a time of 53.84 in the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2014_Summer_Youth_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Girls%27_100_metre_freestyle" title="Swimming at the 2014 Summer Youth Olympics – Girls' 100 metre freestyle">100 m freestyle</a>, vaulting her up to thirteenth in the world rankings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On August 20, Shen picked up her fourth gold of the meet in the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2014_Summer_Youth_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Girls%27_200_metre_freestyle" title="Swimming at the 2014 Summer Youth Olympics – Girls' 200 metre freestyle">200 m freestyle</a>, finishing a personal best in 1:56.12.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2014_Summer_Youth_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Girls%27_4_%C3%97_100_metre_freestyle_relay" title="Swimming at the 2014 Summer Youth Olympics – Girls' 4 × 100 metre freestyle relay">girls' 4×100 m freestyle</a> relay, Shen demolished the field with a phenomenal 53.59 split to run away with another relay victory for her host nation's foursome in 3:41.19.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>One month later, at the <a href="/wiki/2014_Asian_Games" title="2014 Asian Games">2014 Asian Games</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Incheon,_South_Korea" title="Incheon, South Korea">Incheon, South Korea</a>, Shen added four more golds to her career tally in her respective events, most notably in both freestyle relay and sprint. On the first day of the competition, Shen posted a 53.58 split on the second leg to put the Chinese squad ahead to a marvelous victory in the women's 4×100 m freestyle relay, with a time of 3:37.25.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In the women's 100 m freestyle, Shen held off her hard-charging teammate and 2012 Olympic bronze medalist <a href="/wiki/Tang_Yi" title="Tang Yi">Tang Yi</a> by 0.08 of a second to take the gold medal in 54.37.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Shen continued to dominate the medal tally on her third night, as she threw down an anchor-leg split in 2:00.60 to deliver the Chinese foursome another relay victory in 7:55.17.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In the women's 200 m freestyle, Shen pulled ahead from the rest of the field to pick up her fourth straight gold of the meet in 1:57.66.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>For her outstanding successes at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Summer_Youth_Olympics" title="Summer Youth Olympics">Summer Youth Olympics</a> and a six-gold medal streak in swimming, Shen has been nominated for the Breakthrough of the Year Award in the upcoming 2015 <a href="/wiki/Laureus_World_Sports_Awards" title="Laureus World Sports Awards">Laureus World Sports Awards</a> in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2015_World_Aquatics_Championships" title="Swimming at the 2015 World Aquatics Championships">2015 FINA World Championships</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kazan,_Russia" title="Kazan, Russia">Kazan, Russia</a>, Shen helped the Chinese squad secured a bronze medal in the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2015_World_Aquatics_Championships_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_4_%C3%97_200_metre_freestyle_relay" title="Swimming at the 2015 World Aquatics Championships – Women's 4 × 200 metre freestyle relay">4×200 m freestyle</a> with a combined time of 7:49.10, and then pulled off a lead on the freestyle anchor leg (53.00) to deliver the solid foursome of <a href="/wiki/Fu_Yuanhui" title="Fu Yuanhui">Fu Yuanhui</a>, Shi Jinglin, and <a href="/wiki/Lu_Ying" title="Lu Ying">Lu Ying</a> a blazing fast finish in 3:54.41 to claim the gold in the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2015_World_Aquatics_Championships_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_4_%C3%97_100_metre_medley_relay" title="Swimming at the 2015 World Aquatics Championships – Women's 4 × 100 metre medley relay">4×100 m medley</a> relay, just over two seconds away of the current meet record set by her team in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
