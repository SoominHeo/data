<p><b>CentralWorld</b> (<a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>: <span lang="th">เซ็นทรัลเวิลด์</span>) is a shopping plaza and complex in <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>, Thailand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AsiaWebCentralWorld_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-AsiaWebCentralWorld-5">[5]</a></sup> It is the tenth largest shopping complex in the world. The complex, which includes a hotel and office tower, is owned by <a href="/wiki/Central_Pattana" title="Central Pattana">Central Pattana</a>. In 2006, after three years of design and renovation, CentralWorld was expanded to 550,000 square metres of shopping mall and 1,024,000 square metres of complex, topping nearby rival <a href="/wiki/Siam_Paragon" title="Siam Paragon">Siam Paragon</a> in terms of size. 29 December 2013</p>
<p>On 19 May 2010, CentralWorld was one of the many properties set on fire when the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_United_Front_of_Democracy_Against_Dictatorship" title="National United Front of Democracy Against Dictatorship">Red Shirts</a> anti-government protesters were forcibly removed after months of demonstrations which included complete blockage of the Rachaprasong shopping district. The parts of the complex most affected were the Zen department store and its adjacent annex.</p>
<p>A portion of the shopping complex reopened on 28 September 2010 after months of repair work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CentralWorld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Trade_Center">World Trade Center</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CentralWorld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: World Trade Center">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Originally called the World Trade Center, the eight-story mall was opened in 1990. <a href="/wiki/Central_Group" title="Central Group">Central Group</a> acquired the property in 2002 from the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Wang_Petchaboon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Wang Petchaboon (page does not exist)">Wang Petchaboon</a> group and changed the name to Central World Plaza. In 2005 it was renamed CentralWorld. It is on leased <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bureau_of_the_Crown_Property" title="Bureau of the Crown Property">Crown Property Bureau</a> land. In its acquisition, the Central Group's property development arm, <a href="/wiki/Central_Pattana" title="Central Pattana">Central Pattana</a> (<a href="/wiki/Stock_Exchange_of_Thailand" title="Stock Exchange of Thailand">SET</a>: CPN), secured a new 30-year lease on the site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mall_renovations">Mall renovations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CentralWorld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mall renovations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Anticipating the opening of the luxury <a href="/wiki/Siam_Paragon" title="Siam Paragon">Siam Paragon</a>, CPN started massive renovations and expansion on the site in 2003. The original mall structure was 300,000 square meters. The expansion plans boosted that to 550,000 square meters. Though work was not yet complete, CPN held an official opening of the renovated complex on July 21, 2006; it was expected to be fully operational by October 2006. By May 2007 the mall was fully opened, but some parts of the upper floors remained vacant. Major exterior construction was ongoing on the hotel in the northwest corner of the mall and the extension floors to the <a href="/wiki/ZEN_(department_store)" title="ZEN (department store)">ZEN</a> department store in southeast corner.</p>
<p>The renovated complex aimed to attract 150,000 customers per day, spending more than 7 billion <a href="/wiki/Thai_baht" title="Thai baht">baht</a> annually. It marketed itself as a middle class shopping center, opposed to the upper class-market Siam Paragon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>CentralWorld did not achieve its goal in term of daily visitors because of several reasons, including political turmoil and an ongoing economic downturn. However, shopper numbers soon increased, though reliable figures are lacking. Bangkok's New Year countdown celebrations were held in front of CentralWorld, which quickly became a tradition, with the number of people increasing every year.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Offices_at_CentralWorld">The Offices at CentralWorld</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CentralWorld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Offices at CentralWorld">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Renovations included completing an unfinished office tower, work on which had been halted in 1999 with only 39 of the planned 63 floors completed. Construction resumed in early 2003, expanding the tower to a 45-story, 204 meter, design, with the completed tower opening in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Centara_Grand_at_CentralWorld">Centara Grand at CentralWorld</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CentralWorld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Centara Grand at CentralWorld">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Centara_Grand_and_Bangkok_Convention_Centre" title="Centara Grand and Bangkok Convention Centre">Centara Grand and Bangkok Convention Centre</a></div>
<p>Centara Grand is a chain hotel built on land leased from the Crown Properties Bureau. The flagship hotel, the Centara Grand at CentralWorld, is attached to both the CentralWorld mall and the Offices at CentralWorld.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fire_damage">Fire damage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CentralWorld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Fire damage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>CentralWorld was temporarily closed on 19 May 2010 due to severe arson damage, which occurred as troops dislodged protesters from the area during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010_Thailand_political_protests" title="2010 Thailand political protests">2010 Thailand political protests</a>. The adjoining 'Zen' section suffered massive damage from the rioters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Isetan portion was reopened soon after. Following repairs, a large part of CentralWorld was reopened on 28 Sep 2010. The 'Zen' section reopened on 6 Jan 2012.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CentralWorld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>CentralWorld is located in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pathum_Wan" title="Pathum Wan">Pathum Wan</a> district at the <a href="/wiki/Ratchaprasong" title="Ratchaprasong">Ratchaprasong</a> Intersection in one of Bangkok's busiest shopping and tourism districts.</p>

<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_tenants">Former tenants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CentralWorld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Former tenants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/King_Power" title="King Power">King Power</a></b> – A local duty-free store which used to operate on the sixth floor of Zone A. It eventually closed down in June 2006 to make way for the expansion of the <a href="/wiki/ZEN_(department_store)" title="ZEN (department store)">ZEN</a> Department Store.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Major_Cineplex" title="Major Cineplex">Major Cineplex</a></b> – A former cineplex boasting six screens and twenty-four bowling lanes. The cineplex eventually closed down due to poor business, after <a href="/wiki/SF_Group" title="SF Group">SF World Cinema</a> opened in January 2008. Japanese town now occupies this space.</li>
<li><b><a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Centerpoint" title="Centerpoint">Centerpoint</a> at CentralWorld</b> – A complex of shops, restaurants, game zones and entertainment venues located on level 7. The complex was developed and operated by Pornpailin Development Co Ltd, who has previously operated the Centerpoint at Siam Square. It closed down in August 2012 to make Endutainment Zone.</li>
</ul>
