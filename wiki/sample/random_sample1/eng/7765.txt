<p><b>Celia Weston</b> (born December 14, 1951) is an American character actress. Weston received an <a href="/wiki/Independent_Spirit_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Female" title="Independent Spirit Award for Best Supporting Female">Independent Spirit Award</a> nomination for her performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Man_Walking_(film)" title="Dead Man Walking (film)">Dead Man Walking</a></i> (1995), and also had supporting roles in the more than 40 movies, including <i><a href="/wiki/The_Talented_Mr._Ripley_(film)" title="The Talented Mr. Ripley (film)">The Talented Mr. Ripley</a></i> (1999), <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Bedroom" title="In the Bedroom">In the Bedroom</a></i> (2001), <i><a href="/wiki/Hulk_(film)" title="Hulk (film)">Hulk</a></i> (2003) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Village_(2004_film)" title="The Village (2004 film)">The Village</a></i> (2004). On television, she is best known for her role as <a href="/wiki/Jolene_Hunnicutt" title="Jolene Hunnicutt">Jolene Hunnicutt</a> in the CBS sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Alice_(TV_series)" title="Alice (TV series)">Alice</a></i> (1981–85).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celia_Weston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Weston began her career on stage, making her Broadway debut in 1979.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brd_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-brd-1">[1]</a></sup> After recurring role on the ABC daytime soap opera, <i><a href="/wiki/Ryan%27s_Hope" title="Ryan's Hope">Ryan's Hope</a></i>, she joined the cast of the CBS sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Alice_(TV_series)" title="Alice (TV series)">Alice</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/Jolene_Hunnicutt" title="Jolene Hunnicutt">Jolene Hunnicutt</a>. The series ended in 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Weston later said that her role in <i>Alice</i> hindered her film career. Although she had initially rejected the role, she admitted that "the money became so phenomenal that I just had to do it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-3">[3]</a></sup> In later years, Weston acted in the independent films and stage productions. She was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Independent_Spirit_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Female" title="Independent Spirit Award for Best Supporting Female">Independent Spirit Award for Best Supporting Female</a> for her role as Mary Beth Percy in 1995 crime drama film, <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Man_Walking_(film)" title="Dead Man Walking (film)">Dead Man Walking</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Susan_Sarandon" title="Susan Sarandon">Susan Sarandon</a>. She also received her <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Award</a> nomination for her performance in comedy play <i><a href="/wiki/The_Last_Night_of_Ballyhoo" title="The Last Night of Ballyhoo">The Last Night of Ballyhoo</a></i> (1997).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brd_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-brd-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Weston had many supporting roles in films during her career. In 2000s, she had guest starring roles on <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Frasier" title="Frasier">Frasier</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Desperate_Housewives" title="Desperate Housewives">Desperate Housewives</a></i>. From 2010 to 2011 she co-starred alongside <a href="/wiki/Jason_Lee_(actor)" title="Jason Lee (actor)">Jason Lee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alfre_Woodard" title="Alfre Woodard">Alfre Woodard</a> in the TNT comedy-drama, <i><a href="/wiki/Memphis_Beat" title="Memphis Beat">Memphis Beat</a></i>. She later had recurring roles on <i><a href="/wiki/Modern_Family" title="Modern Family">Modern Family</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/American_Horror_Story:_Freak_Show" title="American Horror Story: Freak Show">American Horror Story: Freak Show</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celia_Weston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Weston was born in <a href="/wiki/Spartanburg,_South_Carolina" title="Spartanburg, South Carolina">Spartanburg, South Carolina</a>. She is a graduate of <a href="/wiki/Salem_College" title="Salem College">Salem College</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winston-Salem" title="Winston-Salem">Winston-Salem</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_North_Carolina_School_of_the_Arts" title="University of North Carolina School of the Arts">University of North Carolina School of the Arts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
