<p><i><b>Sherlock Holmes</b></i> is a 2009 British-American <a href="/wiki/Neo-noir" title="Neo-noir">neo-noir</a> <a href="/wiki/Mystery_film" title="Mystery film">mystery</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Period_piece" title="Period piece">period</a> <a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action film</a> based on the <a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes" title="Sherlock Holmes">character of the same name</a> created by <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Conan_Doyle" title="Arthur Conan Doyle">Sir Arthur Conan Doyle</a>. The film was directed by <a href="/wiki/Guy_Ritchie" title="Guy Ritchie">Guy Ritchie</a> and produced by <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Kosove" title="Andrew Kosove">Andrew Kosove</a>, <a href="/wiki/Broderick_Johnson" title="Broderick Johnson">Broderick Johnson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Susan_Downey" title="Susan Downey">Susan Downey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dan_Lin" title="Dan Lin">Dan Lin</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Clooney" title="George Clooney">George Clooney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marc_Abraham" title="Marc Abraham">Marc Abraham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Bliss" title="Thomas Bliss">Thomas Bliss</a>, and <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Eric_Newman" title="Eric Newman">Eric Newman</a>. The screenplay, by Michael Robert Johnson, <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Peckham" title="Anthony Peckham">Anthony Peckham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Simon_Kinberg" title="Simon Kinberg">Simon Kinberg</a>, was developed from a story by Wigram and Johnson. <a href="/wiki/Robert_Downey_Jr." title="Robert Downey Jr.">Robert Downey Jr.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jude_Law" title="Jude Law">Jude Law</a> portray Sherlock Holmes and <a href="/wiki/Dr._Watson" title="Dr. Watson">Dr. John Watson</a> respectively. In the film, set in 1890, eccentric detective Holmes and his companion Watson are hired by a secret society to foil a mysticist's plot to expand the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a> by seemingly supernatural means. <a href="/wiki/Rachel_McAdams" title="Rachel McAdams">Rachel McAdams</a> stars as their former adversary <a href="/wiki/Irene_Adler" title="Irene Adler">Irene Adler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Strong" title="Mark Strong">Mark Strong</a> portrays villain Lord Henry Blackwood.</p>
<p>The film went on general release in the United States on December 25, 2009, and on December 26, 2009 in the UK, Ireland, the Pacific and the Atlantic.</p>
<p><i>Sherlock Holmes</i> received mostly positive critical reaction,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-comingsoon_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-comingsoon-3">[3]</a></sup> praising the story, action sequences, set pieces, costume design, Hans Zimmer’s musical score, and Downey's performance as the main character, winning Downey the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Actor_%E2%80%93_Motion_Picture_Musical_or_Comedy" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Motion Picture Musical or Comedy">Best Actor in a Musical or Comedy</a>. The film was also nominated for two <a href="/wiki/Academy_Awards" title="Academy Awards">Academy Awards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Original_Score" title="Academy Award for Best Original Score">Best Original Score</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Art_Direction" title="Academy Award for Best Art Direction">Best Art Direction</a>, which it lost to <i><a href="/wiki/Up_(2009_film)" title="Up (2009 film)">Up</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Avatar_(2009_film)" title="Avatar (2009 film)">Avatar</a></i>, respectively.</p>
<p>A sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes:_A_Game_of_Shadows" title="Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows">Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows</a></i>, was released on December 16, 2011.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherlock_Holmes_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1890, private detective <a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes" title="Sherlock Holmes">Sherlock Holmes</a> (<a href="/wiki/Robert_Downey_Jr." title="Robert Downey Jr.">Robert Downey Jr.</a>) and his partner <a href="/wiki/Dr._Watson" title="Dr. Watson">Dr. John Watson</a> (<a href="/wiki/Jude_Law" title="Jude Law">Jude Law</a>) prevent the ritual murder of a woman by Lord Henry Blackwood (<a href="/wiki/Mark_Strong" title="Mark Strong">Mark Strong</a>), who has killed five other young women similarly. <a href="/wiki/Inspector_Lestrade" title="Inspector Lestrade">Inspector Lestrade</a> (<a href="/wiki/Eddie_Marsan" title="Eddie Marsan">Eddie Marsan</a>) and the police arrest Blackwood. Three months later, Watson is engaged to <a href="/wiki/Minor_Sherlock_Holmes_characters#Mary_Morstan_.28later_Watson.29" title="Minor Sherlock Holmes characters">Mary Morstan</a> (<a href="/wiki/Kelly_Reilly" title="Kelly Reilly">Kelly Reilly</a>) and moving out of <a href="/wiki/221B_Baker_Street" title="221B Baker Street">221B Baker Street</a>; while he enjoys their adventures together, Watson looks forward to not having to deal with Holmes' eccentricities. Meanwhile, Blackwood has been sentenced to death and requests to see Holmes, who he warns of three more unstoppable deaths that will cause great changes to the world. Blackwood is subsequently hanged.</p>
<p>Holmes is visited by <a href="/wiki/Irene_Adler" title="Irene Adler">Irene Adler</a> (<a href="/wiki/Rachel_McAdams" title="Rachel McAdams">Rachel McAdams</a>), a <a href="/wiki/A_Scandal_in_Bohemia" title="A Scandal in Bohemia">former adversary</a> who asks him to find a missing man named Luke Reordan. After her departure, Holmes follows her as she meets with her secret employer, and only learns that the man is a professor and that he intimidates Adler. Meanwhile, sightings of a living Blackwood and his tomb destroyed lead to belief that Blackwood has risen from the grave; Reordan is found dead inside Blackwood's coffin. Following a series of clues from the body, Holmes and Watson find Reordan's home and discover experiments attempting to merge <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a> with <a href="/wiki/Magic_(paranormal)" title="Magic (paranormal)">magic</a>. After they survive a battle with Blackwood's men that attempt to destroy the lab, Holmes is taken to the Temple of the Four Orders, a secret magical fraternity with considerable political influence. The leaders – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lord_Chief_Justice_of_England" title="Lord Chief Justice of England">Lord Chief Justice</a> Sir Thomas Rotheram (<a href="/wiki/James_Fox" title="James Fox">James Fox</a>), <a href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador_to_the_United_Kingdom" title="United States Ambassador to the United Kingdom">U.S. Ambassador</a> Standish (<a href="/wiki/William_Hope_(actor)" title="William Hope (actor)">William Hope</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Home_Secretary" title="Home Secretary">Home Secretary</a> Lord Coward (<a href="/wiki/Hans_Matheson" title="Hans Matheson">Hans Matheson</a>) – ask Holmes to stop Blackwood, a former member of the society and Sir Thomas's son.</p>
<p>That night Sir Thomas drowns in his bath as Blackwood watches, and the next night Lord Coward calls a meeting of the Order. He nominates Blackwood to take command in place of Sir Thomas and Blackwood reveals himself to the group. Standish attempts to shoot Blackwood but is set on fire when he pulls the trigger of his gun, and runs out a window to his death. Lord Coward issues an arrest warrant for Holmes, causing him to go into hiding. Holmes studies the rituals of the Order and recognizes their symbols in Blackwood's murders that were staged at specific locations; from this he deduces the targets of the final murder are the members of <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Parliament</a>. With the aid of Lestrade, Holmes fakes arrest and is taken to see Coward, where he uses evidence on Coward's clothes to deduce Blackwood has conducted a ceremony in the sewers beneath the <a href="/wiki/Palace_of_Westminster" title="Palace of Westminster">Palace of Westminster</a>.</p>
<p>Holmes, Watson, and Adler, find Blackwood's men in the sewers guarding a device based on Reordan's experiments, designed to release cyanide gas into the Parliament chambers and kill all but Blackwood's supporters, whom he has secretly given an antidote. Blackwood comes before Parliament and announces their impending deaths, then attempts to activate the cyanide device by remote control, but it is disabled by Adler. Blackwood flees Parliament and sees Adler and Holmes in the sewers, and pursues them to the top of the incomplete <a href="/wiki/Tower_Bridge" title="Tower Bridge">Tower Bridge</a>. Blackwood fights Holmes, as the latter deduces how all of Blackwood's supposed supernatural feats were the work of science and trickery. Blackwood falls from the bridge and entangled, is hung in a noose of chains. Adler explains to Holmes that her employer is <a href="/wiki/Professor_Moriarty" title="Professor Moriarty">Professor Moriarty</a>, and she warns that Moriarty is as intelligent as Holmes and far more duplicitous.</p>
<p>As Watson moves out of 221B, the police report to him and Holmes that a dead officer was found near Blackwood's device. Holmes recognizes the wounds as coming from a weapon he saw Moriarty use, and realizes Adler was a distraction to allow Moriarty access to Blackwood's device so he could steal key components based on the <a href="/wiki/History_of_radio" title="History of radio">new science of radio</a>. Holmes smiles as he think of the potential in such technology, and tells the police he'll take the case.</p>

Holmes is such a weirdo".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-weirdo_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-weirdo-6">[6]</a></sup> Downey also revealed what his wife had to say: "that when you read the description of the guy  — quirky and kind of nuts — it could be a description of me".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-settle_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-settle-7">[7]</a></sup> Downey intended to focus more on Holmes' patriotic side and his <a href="/wiki/Bohemianism" title="Bohemianism">bohemianism</a>, and felt that his work on <i><a href="/wiki/Chaplin_(film)" title="Chaplin (film)">Chaplin</a></i> had prepared him for an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_accent" title="English accent">English accent</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bohemian_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-bohemian-8">[8]</a></sup> Ritchie feels his accent is "flawless".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brilliance_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-brilliance-9">[9]</a></sup> Both Downey and Ritchie are martial arts enthusiasts, and have been inspired by the <a href="/wiki/Baritsu" title="Baritsu">Baritsu</a> mentioned in the 1901 story "<a href="/wiki/The_Adventure_of_the_Empty_House" title="The Adventure of the Empty House">The Adventure of the Empty House</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Downey lost weight for the part, because during a chat he had with <a href="/wiki/Chris_Martin" title="Chris Martin">Chris Martin</a>, Martin recommended that Holmes look "gaunt" and "skinny".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>

<a href="/wiki/Jude_Law" title="Jude Law">Jude Law</a> as <a href="/wiki/Dr._Watson" title="Dr. Watson">Dr. John Watson</a>, Holmes' companion and close friend who is also a <a href="/wiki/Surgeon" title="Surgeon">surgeon</a> and a veteran of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_Afghan_War" title="Second Afghan War">Second Afghan War</a>. Downey believed by emphasizing Watson's qualities as a former soldier, a doctor, a womanizer and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gambler" title="Gambler">gambler</a>, it would make for a more interesting <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/foil" title="wikt:foil">foil</a> for Holmes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bromance_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-bromance-14">[14]</a></sup> Law made a notebook of phrases from the stories to improvise into his dialogue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Ritchie originally envisioned <a href="/wiki/Russell_Crowe" title="Russell Crowe">Russell Crowe</a> in the role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-elementary_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-elementary-16">[16]</a></sup></li>

Having returned after his execution, Blackwood unveils plans to control the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a>. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherlock_Holmes_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherlock_Holmes_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:30%; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>"A lot of the action that Conan Doyle refers to was actually made manifest in our film. Very often, Sherlock Holmes will say things like, 'If I hadn't been such an expert short [single] stick person, I would have died in that' or he would refer to a fight off screen. We're putting those fights on screen."</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;">—Producer/co-writer Lionel Wigram<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-csonlocation_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-csonlocation-23">[23]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p><span id="Writing"></span> Producer <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Wigram_(film_producer)" title="Lionel Wigram (film producer)">Lionel Wigram</a> remarked that for around ten years, he had been thinking of new ways to depict Sherlock Holmes. "I realized the images I was seeing in my head [when reading the stories] were different to the images I'd seen in previous films." He imagined "a much more modern, more bohemian character, who dresses more like an artist or a poet", namely <a href="/wiki/Henri_de_Toulouse-Lautrec" title="Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec">Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec</a>. After leaving his position as executive for <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Bros.</a> in 2006,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newyorktimes_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-newyorktimes-5">[5]</a></sup> Wigram sought a larger scope to the story so it could attract a large audience, and amalgamated various Holmes stories to flesh it out further.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-settle_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-settle-7">[7]</a></sup> Some sequences in the movie were more than suggested by uncredited incidents found in a 1979 novel <i>Enter the Lion: A Posthumous Memoir of <a href="/wiki/Mycroft_Holmes" title="Mycroft Holmes">Mycroft Holmes</a></i>. Lord Blackwood's character was developed as a nod to Victorian interests in spiritualism and the later influence of <a href="/wiki/Aleister_Crowley" title="Aleister Crowley">Aleister Crowley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-csonlocation_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-csonlocation-23">[23]</a></sup> The producer felt he was "almost clever" pitting Holmes, who has an almost supernatural ability to solve crimes, against a supposedly supernatural villain. The plot point, moreover, nods to the Holmesian tale of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hound_of_the_Baskervilles" title="The Hound of the Baskervilles">The Hound of the Baskervilles</a></i>, where a string of seemingly supernatural events is finally explained through intuitive reasoning and scientific savvy. Wigram wrote and <a href="/wiki/John_Watkiss" title="John Watkiss">John Watkiss</a> drew a 25-page comic book about Holmes in place of a <a href="/wiki/Spec_script" title="Spec script">spec script</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-csonlocation_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-csonlocation-23">[23]</a></sup> Professor Moriarty was included in the script to set up the sequels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-moriarty_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-moriarty-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2007, Warner Bros. chose to produce, seeing similarities in the concept with <i>Batman Begins</i>. Arthur Conan Doyle's estate had some involvement in sorting out legal issues, although the stories are in the <a href="/wiki/Public_domain" title="Public domain">public domain</a> in the United States.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/Neil_Marshall" title="Neil Marshall">Neil Marshall</a> was set to direct,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> but Guy Ritchie signed on to direct in June 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> When a child at <a href="/wiki/Boarding_school" title="Boarding school">boarding school</a>, Ritchie and other pupils listened to the Holmes stories through dormitory loudspeakers. "Holmes used to talk me to sleep every night when I was seven years old," he said.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Therefore, his image of Holmes differed from the films. He wanted to make his film more "authentic" to Doyle,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bohemian_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-bohemian-8">[8]</a></sup> explaining, "There's quite a lot of intense action sequences in the stories, [and] sometimes that hasn't been reflected in the movies."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-embrace_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-embrace-28">[28]</a></sup> Holmes' "brilliance will <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/percolate" title="wiktionary:percolate">percolate</a> into the action", and the film will show that his "intellect was as much of a curse as it was a blessing".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brilliance_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-brilliance-9">[9]</a></sup> Ritchie sought to make <i>Sherlock Holmes</i> a "very contemporary film as far as the tone and texture", because it has been "a relatively long time since there's been a film version that people embraced".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-embrace_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-embrace-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherlock_Holmes_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Filming began in October 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-start_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-start-29">[29]</a></sup> The crew shot at <a href="/wiki/Freemasons%27_Hall,_London" title="Freemasons' Hall, London">Freemasons' Hall</a> and <a href="/wiki/St_Paul%27s_Cathedral" title="St Paul's Cathedral">St Paul's Cathedral</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-moriarty_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-moriarty-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-locations_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-locations-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>Filming was conducted in <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Northern_Quarter_(Manchester)" title="Northern Quarter (Manchester)">Northern Quarter</a>. <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Town_Hall" title="Manchester Town Hall">Manchester Town Hall</a> was extensively used for numerous scenes with some minor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computer_generated_imagery" title="Computer generated imagery">CGI</a> modifications. The interior courtyard was used for a fight scene, the <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Town_Hall#Great_Hall" title="Manchester Town Hall">Great Hall</a> doubled as the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Lords" title="House of Lords">House of Lords</a> and numerous areas such as the landing were used as a backdrop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>They shot the opening scene for three days at <a href="/wiki/St_Bartholomew-the-Great" title="St Bartholomew-the-Great">St Bartholomew-the-Great</a> church in London,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-csonlocation_23-3"><a href="#cite_note-csonlocation-23">[23]</a></sup> and shot on the river Thames at <a href="/wiki/Wapping" title="Wapping">Wapping</a> for a scene involving a <a href="/wiki/Steamboat" title="Steamboat">steamboat</a> on 7 November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Filming continued at <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Dock" title="Stanley Dock">Stanley Dock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clarence_Dock_(Liverpool)" title="Clarence Dock (Liverpool)">Clarence Dock</a> in Liverpool <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Chatham_Dockyard" title="Chatham Dockyard">The Historic Dockyard, Chatham</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Street scenes were filmed in cobbled alleyways in <a href="/wiki/Chatham,_Kent" title="Chatham, Kent">Chatham</a> and Manchester. <a href="/wiki/Brompton_Cemetery" title="Brompton Cemetery">Brompton Cemetery</a> in London was used for a key scene, and the palatial 19th-century interior of the <a href="/wiki/Reform_Club" title="Reform Club">Reform Club</a> stood in for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caf%C3%A9_Royal" title="Café Royal">Café Royal</a>. Scenes from the interior of 221B Baker Street were shot on a sound stage at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leavesden_Studios" title="Leavesden Studios">Leavesden Studios</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trail_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-trail-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late November 2008, actor Robert Maillet, who played Dredger, was filming a fight scene at <a href="/wiki/Chatham_Dockyard" title="Chatham Dockyard">Chatham Dockyard</a> in Kent, and accidentally punched Robert Downey Jr. in the face, causing Downey to be bloodied and knocked down, but not knocked unconscious as originally reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maillet_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-Maillet-21">[21]</a></sup> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Sun_(newspaper)" title="The Sun (newspaper)">The Sun</a></i> reported that on 28 November, a <a href="/wiki/Tank_truck" title="Tank truck">tank truck</a> caught fire, forcing filming to stop for two hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> When filming at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_John_Street,_London" title="St John Street, London">St John's Street</a> in December, the schedule had to be shortened from 13 to nine days because locals complained about how they would always have to park cars elsewhere during the shoot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> In January 2009, filming moved to <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p><span id="Costumes"></span><span id="Costume"></span> Ritchie wanted his Holmes' costume to play against the popular image of the character, joking "there is only one person in history who ever wore a <a href="/wiki/Deerstalker" title="Deerstalker">deerstalker</a>". Downey selected the character's hat, a beat-up <a href="/wiki/Fedora" title="Fedora">fedora</a>. The director kept to the tradition of making Holmes and Watson's apartment quite messy, and had it decorated with artifacts and scientific objects from the continents they would have visited.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-elementary_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-elementary-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherlock_Holmes_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes_(soundtrack)" title="Sherlock Holmes (soundtrack)">Sherlock Holmes (soundtrack)</a></div>
<p>The soundtrack for the film was composed by <a href="/wiki/Hans_Zimmer" title="Hans Zimmer">Hans Zimmer</a>. It was released on 12 January 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zimmer2010_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-Zimmer2010-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherlock_Holmes_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film had its world premiere on 14 December 2009, in London and was subsequently released worldwide on 25 December 2009 (26 December, boxing day, in the UK and Ireland), after being pushed from a November release date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-comingsoon_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-comingsoon-3">[3]</a></sup> An advance charity screening was held in select locations in Belgium on 10 December 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherlock_Holmes_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><span id="DVD"></span><span id="Video"></span> <i>Sherlock Holmes</i> was released on DVD and Blu-ray/DVD/digital on 30 March 2010 in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> The film has since grossed $44,908,336 in DVD sales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherlock_Holmes_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><span id="Reception"></span></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherlock_Holmes_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film opened to an estimated $62.4 million in its first weekend in America alone, placing in second at the US box office to <i><a href="/wiki/Avatar_(2009_film)" title="Avatar (2009 film)">Avatar</a></i>, which grossed $75.6 million. The film earned a strong per-theater average of $18,031 from its 3,626 theaters. Its one-day Christmas sales broke records. <i>Sherlock Holmes</i> had grossed $523,000,000 worldwide<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mojo_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-2">[2]</a></sup> making it Guy Ritchie's biggest box-office success yet,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> and the 8th highest grossing <a href="/wiki/2009_in_film" title="2009 in film">film of 2009</a> worldwide, and domestically. On the domestic charts, it is the fourth highest-grossing film to never hit No. 1 in the weekend box office, behind <i><a href="/wiki/My_Big_Fat_Greek_Wedding" title="My Big Fat Greek Wedding">My Big Fat Greek Wedding</a></i>, fellow Christmas opener <i><a href="/wiki/Alvin_and_the_Chipmunks:_The_Squeakquel" title="Alvin and the Chipmunks: The Squeakquel">Alvin and the Chipmunks: The Squeakquel</a></i>, and said film's <a href="/wiki/Alvin_and_the_Chipmunks_(film)" title="Alvin and the Chipmunks (film)">predecessor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Worldwide, it is the fourth highest in this category, behind <i><a href="/wiki/Ice_Age:_Dawn_of_the_Dinosaurs" title="Ice Age: Dawn of the Dinosaurs">Ice Age: Dawn of the Dinosaurs</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Casino_Royale_(2006_film)" title="Casino Royale (2006 film)">Casino Royale</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Day_After_Tomorrow" title="The Day After Tomorrow">The Day After Tomorrow</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherlock_Holmes_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><span id="Critics"></span></p>
<p>The film received generally positive reviews. Review aggregate <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> reports that 70% of critics have given the film a positive review based on 224 reviews, with a <a href="/wiki/Average" title="Average">rating average</a> of 6.2 out of 10, with the consensus being that "Guy Ritchie's directorial style might not be quite the best fit for an update on the legendary detective, but Sherlock Holmes benefits from the elementary appeal of a strong performance by Robert Downey, Jr."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, which assigns a <a href="/wiki/Weighted_arithmetic_mean" title="Weighted arithmetic mean">weighted average</a> score out of 1–100 reviews from film critics, has a rating score of 57 based on 34 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metacritic_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-metacritic-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Sun-Times" title="Chicago Sun-Times">Chicago Sun-Times</a></i> gave the film three out of four stars and highlighted the film's strong characters, visuals and action-packed plot;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ebert_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-ebert-47">[47]</a></sup> the characters were also praised by Jake Tomlinson of <i>Shave Magazine</i>, who believed that Downey Jr. and Law were "perfect together" and that Strong was "a convincing and creepy villain".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shavemagazine_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-shavemagazine-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/A._O._Scott" title="A. O. Scott">A. O. Scott</a> of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Times" title="New York Times">New York Times</a></i> noted that the director's approach to films was "to make cool movies about cool guys with cool stuff" and that <i>Sherlock Holmes</i> was essentially "a series of poses and stunts" which was "intermittently diverting" at best.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/David_Stratton" title="David Stratton">David Stratton</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Australian" title="The Australian">The Australian</a></i> disliked the film's interpretation of the original <i>Holmes</i> stories and concluded, "The makers of this film are mainly interested in action; that, they believe, is all that gets young audiences into cinemas today. They may be right, but they have ridden roughshod over one of literature's greatest creations in the process." He praised the production design and score.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DSAUS_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-DSAUS-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherlock_Holmes_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><span id="Awards"></span> On 17 January 2010, the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Foreign_Press_Association" title="Hollywood Foreign Press Association">Hollywood Foreign Press Association</a> announced the winners of the <a href="/wiki/67th_Golden_Globe_Awards" title="67th Golden Globe Awards">67th Golden Globe Awards</a> with Robert Downey Jr. winning <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Actor_%E2%80%93_Motion_Picture_Musical_or_Comedy" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Motion Picture Musical or Comedy">Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Motion Picture Musical or Comedy</a> for the portrayal of Sherlock Holmes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Broadcast_Film_Critics_Association" title="Broadcast Film Critics Association">Broadcast Film Critics Association</a> nominated Hans Zimmer for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broadcast_Film_Critics_Association_Award_for_Best_Composer" title="Broadcast Film Critics Association Award for Best Composer">Best Score</a> but lost to <i><a href="/wiki/Up_(2009_film)" title="Up (2009 film)">Up</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Giacchino" title="Michael Giacchino">Michael Giacchino</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> The film was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Original_Score" title="Academy Award for Best Original Score">Best Original Score</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Art_Direction" title="Academy Award for Best Art Direction">Best Art Direction</a> at the <a href="/wiki/82nd_Academy_Awards" title="82nd Academy Awards">82nd Academy Awards</a>. The film won Best Thriller at the <a href="/wiki/Empire_Awards" title="Empire Awards">Empire Awards</a>. The film was nominated for the Vits Awards for "Best Music" and "Best Effects".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequel">Sequel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherlock_Holmes_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Sequel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><span id="Sequel"></span><span id="Sequels"></span> The sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes:_A_Game_of_Shadows" title="Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows">Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows</a></i>, with Downey and Law reprising their roles, was released on 16 December 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
