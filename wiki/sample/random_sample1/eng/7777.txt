<p><b>Shirley Henderson</b> (born 24 November 1965) is a Scottish actress. She is perhaps best known for her film roles as Gail in <i><a href="/wiki/Trainspotting_(film)" title="Trainspotting (film)">Trainspotting</a></i> (1996), Jude in <i><a href="/wiki/Bridget_Jones%27s_Diary_(film)" title="Bridget Jones's Diary (film)">Bridget Jones's Diary</a></i> (2001) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minor_Harry_Potter_characters#Moaning_Myrtle" title="Minor Harry Potter characters">Moaning Myrtle</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i> (2002) and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i> (2005). Her other films include <i><a href="/wiki/Topsy-Turvy" title="Topsy-Turvy">Topsy-Turvy</a></i> (1999), <i><a href="/wiki/Wilbur_Wants_to_Kill_Himself" title="Wilbur Wants to Kill Himself">Wilbur Wants to Kill Himself</a></i> (2002), <i><a href="/wiki/Bridget_Jones:_The_Edge_of_Reason_(film)" title="Bridget Jones: The Edge of Reason (film)">Bridget Jones: The Edge of Reason</a></i> (2004), <i><a href="/wiki/Frozen_(2005_film)" title="Frozen (2005 film)">Frozen</a></i> (2005), <i><a href="/wiki/Miss_Pettigrew_Lives_for_a_Day" title="Miss Pettigrew Lives for a Day">Miss Pettigrew Lives for a Day</a></i> (2008) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anna_Karenina_(2012_film)" title="Anna Karenina (2012 film)">Anna Karenina</a></i> (2012). On television from 1995-1997, she starred opposite <a href="/wiki/Robert_Carlyle" title="Robert Carlyle">Robert Carlyle</a> in the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Hamish_Macbeth_(TV_series)" title="Hamish Macbeth (TV series)">Hamish Macbeth</a></i>. In 2014, she received a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAFTA_TV_Award" title="BAFTA TV Award">BAFTA TV Award</a> nomination for the <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/Southcliffe" title="Southcliffe">Southcliffe</a></i> (2013). She has also featured most recently as Frances Drummond in the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> drama <i><a href="/wiki/Happy_Valley_(TV_series)" title="Happy Valley (TV series)">Happy Valley</a></i> (2016).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shirley_Henderson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Henderson was born in <a href="/wiki/Forres" title="Forres">Forres</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moray" title="Moray">Moray</a>, but grew up in <a href="/wiki/Kincardine" title="Kincardine">Kincardine</a>, Fife.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ind051203_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ind051203-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> As a child, she began singing in local clubs, at charity events, holiday camps and even a boxing contest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ind051203_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ind051203-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ind131002_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ind131002-3">[3]</a></sup> Having joined an after-school drama club,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SMW150397_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-SMW150397-4">[4]</a></sup> Henderson attended <a href="/wiki/Adam_Smith_College" title="Adam Smith College">Fife College</a> at the age of 16, where she completed a one-year course resulting in a National Certificate in Theatre Arts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-G130104_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-G130104-5">[5]</a></sup> She moved to London at 17 where she spent three years at the <a href="/wiki/Guildhall_School_of_Music_and_Drama" title="Guildhall School of Music and Drama">Guildhall School of Music and Drama</a>, graduating in 1986.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-G061104_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-G061104-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shirley_Henderson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Henderson got her first big break when <a href="/wiki/Leonard_White_(producer)" title="Leonard White (producer)">Leonard White</a> cast her as the lead in the children's television drama <i><a href="/wiki/Shadow_of_the_Stone" title="Shadow of the Stone">Shadow of the Stone</a></i> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ITV_Network" title="ITV Network">ITV</a>. After this, she spent the majority of her twenties concentrating on performing a wide variety of roles in the theatre.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Early 1990 saw her return to television screens when she appeared in the third series of the wartime drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Wish_Me_Luck" title="Wish Me Luck">Wish Me Luck</a></i> and in <i><a href="/wiki/Clarissa" title="Clarissa">Clarissa</a></i> (1991). More stage work followed<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> before she landed the key role of Isobel in the popular BBC series <i>Hamish Macbeth</i> in 1995.</p>
<p>Henderson then moved into films, playing Morag in <i><a href="/wiki/Rob_Roy_(1995_film)" title="Rob Roy (1995 film)">Rob Roy</a></i> (1995) and Spud's girlfriend Gail in Danny Boyle's <i>Trainspotting</i> (1996). She continued her work in the theatre, including many productions at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Theatre" title="Royal National Theatre">National Theatre</a> in London.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> 1999 saw critically acclaimed performances<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> in <a href="/wiki/Mike_Leigh" title="Mike Leigh">Mike Leigh's</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Topsy-Turvy" title="Topsy-Turvy">Topsy-Turvy</a></i> — which also provided an opportunity for her to show off her singing skills – and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Winterbottom" title="Michael Winterbottom">Michael Winterbottom</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Wonderland_(1999_film)" title="Wonderland (1999 film)">Wonderland</a></i>.</p>
<p>She played Jude in both <i>Bridget Jones</i> films and Moaning Myrtle in <i>Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</i> (2002) and <i>Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</i> (2005). She was the oldest actress to portray a child's character in the films. She co-starred in the British film "Close Your Eyes" (2002) along with <a href="/wiki/Goran_Vi%C5%A1nji%C4%87" title="Goran Višnjić">Goran Višnjić</a> and <a href="/wiki/Miranda_Otto" title="Miranda Otto">Miranda Otto</a> and also played French princess <a href="/wiki/Princess_Sophie_of_France" title="Princess Sophie of France">Sophie-Philippine</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sofia_Coppola" title="Sofia Coppola">Sofia Coppola</a>'s <i>Marie Antoinette</i> (2006).</p>

<p>She played the school matron in <a href="/wiki/Nick_Moore_(film_director)" title="Nick Moore (film director)">Nick Moore's</a> 2008 film <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_Child_(film)" title="Wild Child (film)">Wild Child</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Small-screen appearances have included playing Marie Melmotte in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Way_We_Live_Now_(2001_TV_serial)" title="The Way We Live Now (2001 TV serial)">The Way We Live Now</a></i> (2001); <a href="/wiki/Catherine_of_Braganza" title="Catherine of Braganza">Catherine of Braganza</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Charles_II:_The_Power_and_The_Passion" title="Charles II: The Power and The Passion">Charles II: The Power and The Passion</a></i> (2003); Charlotte in <i><a href="/wiki/Dirty_Filthy_Love" title="Dirty Filthy Love">Dirty Filthy Love</a></i> (2004); Ursula Blake in the <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">Doctor Who</a></i> episode "<a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Monsters" title="Love &amp; Monsters">Love &amp; Monsters</a>" (2006); Emmeline Fox in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Crimson_Petal_and_the_White" title="The Crimson Petal and the White">The Crimson Petal and the White</a></i> (2011); DS Angela Young in <i><a href="/wiki/Death_in_Paradise_(TV_series)" title="Death in Paradise (TV series)">Death in Paradise</a></i> (2011); and Meg Hawkins in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Treasure_Island_(2012_TV_miniseries)" title="Treasure Island (2012 TV miniseries)">Treasure Island</a></i> (2012). She played Karen, the lead role, opposite <a href="/wiki/John_Simm" title="John Simm">John Simm</a> in <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4's</a> <i>Everyday</i> and Meme Kartosov in <a href="/wiki/Anna_Karenina_(2012_film)" title="Anna Karenina (2012 film)"><i>Anna Karenina</i></a>.</p>
