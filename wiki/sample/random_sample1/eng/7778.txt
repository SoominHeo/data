<p><b>Sherry Lea Stringfield</b> (born June 24, 1967) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a>. She is best known for playing the role of Dr. <a href="/wiki/Susan_Lewis" title="Susan Lewis">Susan Lewis</a> on the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> medical drama <i><a href="/wiki/ER_(TV_series)" title="ER (TV series)">ER</a></i>, a role for which she received three <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> nominations. Stringfield was a member of <i>ER's</i> original cast, but she quit the show during its third season, despite being contractually tied to appear in five. She returned to the role in 2001, and quit once again in 2005. She is also known for her regular roles on <i><a href="/wiki/NYPD_Blue" title="NYPD Blue">NYPD Blue</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Guiding_Light" title="Guiding Light">Guiding Light</a></i>.</p>
<p>She has acted mainly on television, but she has also played various parts in films.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherry_Stringfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The eldest of three children, Stringfield was born in <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Springs,_Colorado" title="Colorado Springs, Colorado">Colorado Springs, Colorado</a>. Her family briefly relocated to <a href="/wiki/Albuquerque,_New_Mexico" title="Albuquerque, New Mexico">Albuquerque</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a>, before settling in <a href="/wiki/Spring,_Texas" title="Spring, Texas">Spring</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Suburb" title="Suburb">suburb</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" title="Houston, Texas">Houston</a>, where Stringfield grew up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pro_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-pro-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>She developed a penchant for acting while attending <a href="/wiki/Klein_High_School" title="Klein High School">Klein High School</a>, where she starred in various musicals and plays including <i>Oklahoma</i>, <i>Mother Courage</i>, and <i>Fiddler on the Roof</i>. At 18 she attended the Acting Conservatory of <a href="/wiki/State_University_of_New_York_at_Purchase" title="State University of New York at Purchase">SUNY at Purchase</a> — rooming with fellow actress and student <a href="/wiki/Parker_Posey" title="Parker Posey">Parker Posey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> During that time, she appeared in numerous off-Broadway productions and learned to control and alter her Texan accent with the help of a college speech teacher.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Stringfield graduated in 1989 with a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">Bachelor's degree</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Fine_Arts" title="Bachelor of Fine Arts">Fine Arts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pro_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-pro-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherry_Stringfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherry_Stringfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>She got her first role on the <a href="/wiki/CBS_Daytime" title="CBS Daytime">CBS Daytime</a> <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap opera</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Guiding_Light" title="Guiding Light">Guiding Light</a></i>, playing <a href="/wiki/Blake_Marler" title="Blake Marler">Christina "Blake" Thorpe</a> from 1989 until 1992. After three years she left the show to spend a year traveling <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, but soon returned to television on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> drama, <i><a href="/wiki/NYPD_Blue" title="NYPD Blue">NYPD Blue</a></i>. She played <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> Assistant District Attorney Laura Kelly, the ex-wife of officer <a href="/wiki/John_Kelly_(NYPD_Blue)" title="John Kelly (NYPD Blue)">John Kelly</a> (<a href="/wiki/David_Caruso" title="David Caruso">David Caruso</a>) during the first season (1993–94) of the series. Unsatisfied, she was released early from her contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-why_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-why-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="ER"><i>ER</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherry_Stringfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: ER">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Stringfield became one of the original cast members of <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>'s medical drama, <i><a href="/wiki/ER_(TV_series)" title="ER (TV series)">ER</a></i>. Her personal success mirrored the show's success — she was nominated for an <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> for lead actress during each of her first three seasons of the show. Stringfield was contractually tied to appear in five seasons of <i>ER</i>; however, a desire for a "normal life" and to escape <i>ER'</i>s grueling filming schedule prompted her to leave the drama early in its third season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-why_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-why-4">[4]</a></sup> In order to be released from her contract, Stringfield was forced to sign a no-work agreement, which blocked her from working on television for the remainder of her original contract. Stringfield's decision to quit reportedly angered the show's executive producer, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Wells_(TV_producer)" title="John Wells (TV producer)">John Wells</a>, because she left just as Dr. Lewis got embroiled in a budding romance with <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Edwards" title="Anthony Edwards">Anthony Edwards</a>' <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dr._Mark_Greene" title="Dr. Mark Greene">Dr. Mark Greene</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-why_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-why-4">[4]</a></sup> Stringfield has commented: "I wouldn't describe the situation as pleasant. The producers were in shock. They tried to talk me out of it. It took a long time to get out of my contract."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Stringfield's much publicised departure reportedly "sent a small shock wave through Hollywood"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and her exit episode in November 1996 attracted 37 million viewers, the biggest night of the season for the NBC network.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Stringfield quit just as television executive Dick Robertson was closing a deal that guaranteed the <i>ER</i> cast millions in future earnings from sales to cable and <a href="/wiki/Broadcast_syndication" title="Broadcast syndication">syndication</a>, meaning she effectively "walked away from a fortune."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Stringfield claims she was asked to reprise the role of Susan Lewis in <i>ER</i> several times, but turned each offer down.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-back_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-back-9">[9]</a></sup> However, following the birth of her daughter, she approached the producers of <i>ER</i> and then rejoined the cast in 2001, the show's eighth season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-back_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-back-9">[9]</a></sup> "I changed my mind," Stringfield commented, "I really want to work now. It just made sense for me to go back to <i>ER</i>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> 26.7 million people watched Stringfield's return, placing the show at first place in the ratings. During her second stint in <i>ER</i>, Stringfield's character was featured in a special '<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_crossover" title="Fictional crossover">crossover</a>' with NBC's <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> based police drama, <i><a href="/wiki/Third_Watch" title="Third Watch">Third Watch</a></i>, which aired in 2002. She remained in the role for four additional seasons, however in August 2005, Stringfield announced that she would be leaving the show again as the 12th season came underway. "I am extremely grateful for the time I spent on <i>ER</i>," Stringfield explained. "It is a wonderful show, and there are so many people I will miss. But I'm ready for new roles and new challenges."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In 2009, Stringfield returned to <i>ER</i> for the <a href="/wiki/Series_finale" title="Series finale">series finale</a> "<a href="/wiki/And_in_the_End..." title="And in the End...">And in the End...</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherry_Stringfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After leaving <i>ER</i> in 1996, Stringfield taught a script analysis class and directed several plays at her alma mater. She took roles in films such as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/54_(movie)" title="54 (movie)">54</a></i> (1998) and <i><a href="/wiki/Autumn_in_New_York_(film)" title="Autumn in New York (film)">Autumn in New York</a></i> (2000) and appeared in the television movies <i>Border Line</i> (1999) — produced by her <i>ER</i> co-star Anthony Edwards — and <i>Going Home</i> (2000), in which she starred opposite <a href="/wiki/Jason_Robards" title="Jason Robards">Jason Robards</a>. She also guest-starred in the CBS drama <i><a href="/wiki/Touched_by_an_Angel" title="Touched by an Angel">Touched by an Angel</a></i> in 1999.</p>
<p>In 2005, Stringfield was cast alongside <a href="/wiki/Michael_Michele" title="Michael Michele">Michael Michele</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blair_Underwood" title="Blair Underwood">Blair Underwood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Esai_Morales" title="Esai Morales">Esai Morales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gary_Cole" title="Gary Cole">Gary Cole</a> and <a href="/wiki/Catherine_Bell_(actress)" title="Catherine Bell (actress)">Catherine Bell</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Television_pilot" title="Television pilot">pilot episode</a> of the CBS drama <i>Company Town</i> (created by <a href="/wiki/Elwood_Reid" title="Elwood Reid">Elwood Reid</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> directed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thomas_Carter_(film_director)" title="Thomas Carter (film director)">Thomas Carter</a>), playing Angie Amberson, a mother of teenagers and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Whistle-blower" title="Whistle-blower">whistle-blower</a> on the investment firm where she works. <i>Company Town</i> failed to get picked up for the 2006 fall season. The series was produced by <a href="/wiki/Jack_Clements" title="Jack Clements">Jack Clements</a> and Larry Sanitsky. In 2007 Stringfield starred in the Andrew Shea film <i>Forfeit</i>. She played the role of Karen, the ex-girlfriend of a clinical sociopath who schemes and plots to stage a massive robbery and sets Karen up to take the rap.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2007, Stringfield guest-starred as attorney Nora March in the CBS drama <i><a href="/wiki/Shark_(TV_series)" title="Shark (TV series)">Shark</a></i>. She also played a recurring role in the HBO drama <i><a href="/wiki/Tell_Me_You_Love_Me" title="Tell Me You Love Me">Tell Me You Love Me</a></i> (previously known as <i>Sexlife</i>), which also aired in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In 2009, she appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Stepfather_(2009_film)" title="The Stepfather (2009 film)">The Stepfather</a></i>, a remake of <a href="/wiki/The_Stepfather_(1987_film)" title="The Stepfather (1987 film)">the 1987 American thriller film of the same name</a>.</p>
<p>She appeared in an episode of the <a href="/wiki/USA_Network" title="USA Network">USA Network</a> drama <i><a href="/wiki/In_Plain_Sight" title="In Plain Sight">In Plain Sight</a></i>. Stringfield also guest-starred on <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order" title="Law &amp; Order">Law &amp; Order</a></i> in late 2008 as a ruthless court clerk and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Cutter" title="Michael Cutter">Michael Cutter</a>'s love interest. She most recently played Mary Jane Porter, an old girlfriend of <a href="/wiki/Larry_David" title="Larry David">Larry David</a>'s who runs into and then goes on a date with him in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Curb_Your_Enthusiasm" title="Curb Your Enthusiasm">Curb Your Enthusiasm</a></i> that aired on October 11, 2009.</p>
<p>In 2010, Stringfield appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Who_Is_Clark_Rockefeller%3F" title="Who Is Clark Rockefeller?">Who Is Clark Rockefeller?</a></i> playing the role of Sandra Boss.</p>
<p>In addition to acting, Stringfield has done <a href="/wiki/Voice-over" title="Voice-over">voice-over</a> work, including the voice of Eyeleen in the children's television show <i><a href="/wiki/Blue%27s_Clues" title="Blue's Clues">Blue's Clues</a></i> and voicing her <i>ER</i> character for the <a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">PC</a> game <i>ER: The Game</i>. Stringfield was also featured in the U.S. <i><a href="/wiki/Got_Milk%3F" title="Got Milk?">Got Milk?</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Advertising_campaign" title="Advertising campaign">advertising campaign</a>, despite being <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lactose_intolerant" title="Lactose intolerant">lactose intolerant</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2012, Stringfield starred in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Confession_(2012_film)" title="The Confession (2012 film)">The Confession</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/Hallmark_Channel" title="Hallmark Channel">Hallmark Channel</a> movie and a sequel to <i>The Shunning</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2013, Stringfield guest-starred in the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> police drama <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI: Crime Scene Investigation</a></i> in season 14: episode 8 titled "Helpless", playing swing shift CSI Dawn Banks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In 2014, Stringfield played a recurring role on the CBS mystery <a href="/wiki/Under_the_Dome_(TV_series)" title="Under the Dome (TV series)">Under the Dome</a> during its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Under_the_Dome_(Season_2)" title="Under the Dome (Season 2)">second season</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards.2Fnominations">Awards/nominations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherry_Stringfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Awards/nominations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1991 and 1993, Stringfield was nominated in the "Outstanding Villain/Villainess" category at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soap_Opera_Digest_Award" title="Soap Opera Digest Award">Soap Opera Digest Awards</a> for her role in <i><a href="/wiki/Guiding_Light" title="Guiding Light">Guiding Light</a></i>. She has also received multiple awards and award-nominations for her role as <a href="/wiki/Susan_Lewis" title="Susan Lewis">Susan Lewis</a> in <i>ER</i>, including three separate Emmy Award nominations in the category "Outstanding Lead Actress in a Drama Series" in 1995, 1996 and 1997. In 1995 she was nominated in the category "Favorite Female Performer" at the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Choice_Awards" title="People's Choice Awards">People's Choice Awards</a> and in 1995 and 1996 she was nominated in the "Best Performance by an Actress in a TV-Series -Drama" category at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Awards" title="Golden Globe Awards">Golden Globe Awards</a>.</p>
<p>In 1996, she won a Q Award for "Best Actress in a Quality Drama Series" and in 1997 she, along with several of her <i>ER</i> cast-mates, won a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Awards" title="Screen Actors Guild Awards">Screen Actors Guild Award</a> for "Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Drama Series". In addition Stringfield was voted one of the "100 Sexiest Women in the World" by readers of <a href="/wiki/FHM" title="FHM">FHM</a> Magazine in 1997.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherry_Stringfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the early 1990s Stringfield dated <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> businessman Paul Goldstein (chairman of Nevica skiwear) for nearly three years, but the pressures of a long-distance romance eventually ended the relationship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Stringfield married <a href="/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalist</a> Larry E. Joseph in October 1998. They have two children: their daughter, Phoebe (born March 2001) and son Milo (born April 2004). Stringfield and Joseph divorced in January 2006 after seven years of marriage. She currently resides in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>.</p>
