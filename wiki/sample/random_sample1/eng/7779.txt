<p><b>Sheridan Smith</b>, <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Order of the British Empire">OBE</a> (born 25 June 1981)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GuardianMar2013_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-GuardianMar2013-1">[1]</a></sup> is an English actress, singer, and dancer.</p>
<p>Smith came to prominence on television for her roles in comedy shows <i><a href="/wiki/Two_Pints_of_Lager_and_a_Packet_of_Crisps" title="Two Pints of Lager and a Packet of Crisps">Two Pints of Lager and a Packet of Crisps</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Soup" title="Love Soup">Love Soup</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Gavin_%26_Stacey" title="Gavin &amp; Stacey">Gavin &amp; Stacey</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Grownups_(2006_TV_series)" title="Grownups (2006 TV series)">Grownups</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Benidorm_(TV_series)" title="Benidorm (TV series)">Benidorm</a></i>. She went on to star in television dramas like <i><a href="/wiki/Mrs_Biggs" title="Mrs Biggs">Mrs Biggs</a></i> (for which she won the <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_Television_Award_for_Best_Actress" title="British Academy Television Award for Best Actress">BAFTA TV Award for Best Actress</a> for playing <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Biggs" title="Ronnie Biggs">Ronnie Biggs</a>' wife), <i><a href="/wiki/The_7.39" title="The 7.39">The 7.39</a></i>, and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cilla_(TV_series)" title="Cilla (TV series)">Cilla</a></i> (in which she played <a href="/wiki/Cilla_Black" title="Cilla Black">Cilla Black</a>). Her film credits include <i><a href="/wiki/Tower_Block_(film)" title="Tower Block (film)">Tower Block</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Quartet_(2012_film)" title="Quartet (2012 film)">Quartet</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Powder_Room_(film)" title="Powder Room (film)">Powder Room</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Harry_Hill_Movie" title="The Harry Hill Movie">The Harry Hill Movie</a></i>.</p>
<p>Smith made her <a href="/wiki/West_End_theatre" title="West End theatre">West End</a> debut in a National Youth Music Theatre production of <i><a href="/wiki/Bugsy_Malone" title="Bugsy Malone">Bugsy Malone</a></i> and has performed in musicals <i><a href="/wiki/Into_the_Woods" title="Into the Woods">Into the Woods</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Shop_of_Horrors_(musical)" title="Little Shop of Horrors (musical)">Little Shop of Horrors</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Legally_Blonde_(musical)" title="Legally Blonde (musical)">Legally Blonde</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Funny_Girl_(musical)" title="Funny Girl (musical)">Funny Girl</a></i>. She won two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laurence_Olivier_Awards" title="Laurence Olivier Awards">Laurence Olivier Awards</a> in consecutive years,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thestage.co.uk_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-thestage.co.uk-2">[2]</a></sup> for <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Olivier_Award_for_Best_Actress_in_a_Musical" title="Laurence Olivier Award for Best Actress in a Musical">Best Actress in a Musical</a> as <a href="/wiki/Elle_Woods" title="Elle Woods">Elle Woods</a> in <i>Legally Blonde</i> in 2011 and <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Olivier_Award_for_Best_Performance_in_a_Supporting_Role" title="Laurence Olivier Award for Best Performance in a Supporting Role">Best Performance in a Supporting Role</a> as Doris in a revival of the play <i><a href="/wiki/Flare_Path" title="Flare Path">Flare Path</a></i> in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Olivier2011_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Olivier2011-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Doris_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Doris-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Smith was appointed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Officer_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Officer of the Order of the British Empire">Officer of the Order of the British Empire</a> (OBE) in the <a href="/wiki/2015_New_Year_Honours" title="2015 New Year Honours">2015 New Year Honours</a> for services to drama.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheridan_Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Smith was born in <a href="/wiki/Epworth,_Lincolnshire" title="Epworth, Lincolnshire">Epworth, Lincolnshire</a>, the daughter of Colin and Marilyn Smith, who performed as a <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country music</a> duo called The Daltons. She studied dance at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joyce_Mason_School_of_Dancing" title="Joyce Mason School of Dancing">Joyce Mason School of Dancing</a> from an early age.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GuardianMar2013_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-GuardianMar2013-1">[1]</a></sup> She later attended South Axholme Community School,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ST_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ST-8">[8]</a></sup> where she received the highest possible grade in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GCSE" title="GCSE">GCSE</a> performing arts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Though she never trained at a drama school,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Smith was a member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Youth_Music_Theatre" title="National Youth Music Theatre">National Youth Music Theatre</a> from 1995–2001, performing a number of lead roles in productions such as <i><a href="/wiki/Bugsy_Malone" title="Bugsy Malone">Bugsy Malone</a>,</i> <i>Pendragon</i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Into_the_Woods" title="Into the Woods">Into the Woods</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> She completed further education at John Leggott College in <a href="/wiki/Scunthorpe" title="Scunthorpe">Scunthorpe, Lincolnshire</a>. Her great-great-grandfather, Benjamin Doubleday, was a respected <a href="/wiki/Banjo" title="Banjo">banjo</a> player in <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> and later played as a trio with his daughters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheridan_Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_and_television">Film and television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheridan_Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film and television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 1999 to 2000, Smith played Emma in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Royle_Family" title="The Royle Family">The Royle Family</a></i>. She played <a href="/wiki/List_of_Two_Pints_of_Lager_and_a_Packet_of_Crisps_characters#Janet_Keogh_Nee_Smith" title="List of Two Pints of Lager and a Packet of Crisps characters">Janet</a> in the long-running series <i><a href="/wiki/Two_Pints_of_Lager_and_a_Packet_of_Crisps" title="Two Pints of Lager and a Packet of Crisps">Two Pints of Lager and a Packet of Crisps</a></i> from 2001 until her departure from the show in 2009. In February 2003, she appeared in "Immediate Care", an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Royal" title="The Royal">The Royal</a></i>, where she played the part of Francesca Wheeler, a teenage girl who was suffering from leukemia. In 2004, she made a one-episode appearance in <i><a href="/wiki/Mile_High" title="Mile High">Mile High</a></i> as Suzy, an airport cleaner who was so desperate to become cabin crew, she impersonated a flight attendant. She also featured in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Comic_Strip" title="The Comic Strip">The Comic Strip</a>'</i>s 2005 episode "Sex Actually" as Angie.</p>
<p>Smith played Cleo Martin in <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Soup" title="Love Soup">Love Soup</a></i> (2005) and starred as Michelle, a hypocritical nutritionist, in three series of the sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Grownups_(2006_TV_series)" title="Grownups (2006 TV series)">Grownups</a></i> (2006–2009). She has also appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lenny_Henry_Show" title="The Lenny Henry Show">The Lenny Henry Show</a></i> as M.E. Westmocott, a role spoofing the various medical examiners on <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI</a></i>. In the 2009 <i>Two Pints of Lager</i> <a href="/wiki/Comic_Relief" title="Comic Relief">Comic Relief</a> special, which crossed over with <i>Grownups</i>, she played her characters from both series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Smith has also appeared in a number of other shows including the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Eyes_Down" title="Eyes Down">Eyes Down</a></i>, in which she played Sandy, and smaller parts in shows such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bill" title="The Bill">The Bill</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Fat_Friends" title="Fat Friends">Fat Friends</a></i>. She featured as Rudi in the second and third series of the BBC Three sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Gavin_%26_Stacey" title="Gavin &amp; Stacey">Gavin &amp; Stacey</a></i>. She guest starred in the Christmas 2008 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Lark_Rise_to_Candleford_(TV_series)" title="Lark Rise to Candleford (TV series)">Lark Rise to Candleford</a></i>. Since 2009, she has played Joey Ross in special seasonal episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Creek" title="Jonathan Creek">Jonathan Creek</a></i>, namely "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Grinning_Man_(Jonathan_Creek)" title="The Grinning Man (Jonathan Creek)">The Grinning Man</a>" (2009), "<a href="/wiki/List_of_Jonathan_Creek_episodes#Easter_Special_.282010.29" title="List of Jonathan Creek episodes">The Judas Tree</a>" (2010), and "The Clue of the Savant's Thumb" (2013). She played Brandy in the third series of <i><a href="/wiki/Benidorm_(TV_series)" title="Benidorm (TV series)">Benidorm</a></i>.</p>
<p>During 2010, Smith acted as a mentor to the contestants of the BBC show <i><a href="/wiki/Over_the_Rainbow_(2010_TV_series)" title="Over the Rainbow (2010 TV series)">Over the Rainbow</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Sky_Arts" title="Sky Arts">Sky Arts</a>' Chekhov Comedy Shorts also featured Smith in 2010 when she starred as Natasha in <i>The Proposal</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> She appeared in the 2011 film <i><a href="/wiki/Hysteria_(2011_film)" title="Hysteria (2011 film)">Hysteria</a></i>, about the invention of the vibrator.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> For the factual hospital series <i><a href="/wiki/Bizarre_ER" title="Bizarre ER">Bizarre ER</a></i>, she took over narration duties from <a href="/wiki/Freema_Agyeman" title="Freema Agyeman">Freema Agyeman</a>. She appeared in the 2012 <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> <a href="/wiki/The_Scapegoat_(2012_film)" title="The Scapegoat (2012 film)">film adaptation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Daphne_du_Maurier" title="Daphne du Maurier">Daphne du Maurier</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Scapegoat_(novel)" title="The Scapegoat (novel)">The Scapegoat</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2012 Smith played the title role in <i><a href="/wiki/Mrs_Biggs" title="Mrs Biggs">Mrs Biggs</a></i>, a five-part series on <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a>. In 2013, she appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Dates_(TV_series)" title="Dates (TV series)">Dates</a></i>. In January 2014, she starred in the two-part BBC drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_7.39" title="The 7.39">The 7.39</a></i>. In March 2014, she appeared in the ITV crime drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Widower_(miniseries)" title="The Widower (miniseries)">The Widower</a></i> as the first wife of serial killer <a href="/wiki/Malcolm_Webster_(murderer)" title="Malcolm Webster (murderer)">Malcolm Webster</a>. Later that year, she featured in the series <i><a href="/wiki/Who_Do_You_Think_You_Are%3F_(UK_TV_series)" title="Who Do You Think You Are? (UK TV series)">Who Do You Think You Are?</a></i> and played <a href="/wiki/Cilla_Black" title="Cilla Black">Cilla Black</a> in the three-part ITV drama series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cilla_(TV_series)" title="Cilla (TV series)">Cilla</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2015 she starred as cancer patient <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Lynch" title="Lisa Lynch">Lisa Lynch</a> in the one-off BBC drama <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_C_Word_(TV_series)" title="The C Word (TV series)">The C Word</a></i> and as policewoman Jo Gillespie in the three-part <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> drama <i>Black Work</i>, which was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/National_Television_Awards" title="National Television Awards">National Television Award</a> in 2016.</p>
<p>Smith has narrated the reality series <i><a href="/wiki/Bear_Grylls:_Mission_Survive" title="Bear Grylls: Mission Survive">Bear Grylls: Mission Survive</a></i> since 2015. The show was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/National_Television_Awards" title="National Television Awards">National Television Award</a> in 2016 but was cancelled after two series.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stage">Stage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheridan_Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Stage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Smith's theatre credits include the musical <i><a href="/wiki/Into_the_Woods" title="Into the Woods">Into the Woods</a></i> at London's <a href="/wiki/Donmar_Warehouse" title="Donmar Warehouse">Donmar Warehouse</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">Shakespeare</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Taming_of_the_Shrew" title="The Taming of the Shrew">The Taming of the Shrew</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream" title="A Midsummer Night's Dream">A Midsummer Night's Dream</a></i> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Open_Air_Theatre,_Regent%27s_Park" title="Open Air Theatre, Regent's Park">Open Air Theatre, Regent's Park</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> She also played Audrey in the stage production of <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Shop_of_Horrors_(musical)" title="Little Shop of Horrors (musical)">Little Shop of Horrors</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Menier_Chocolate_Factory" title="Menier Chocolate Factory">Menier Chocolate Factory</a>, London, from November 2006, with the production then transferring to the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_York%27s_Theatre" title="Duke of York's Theatre">Duke of York's Theatre</a> in March 2007. Following a successful run the production moved again at the end of June 2007 to the <a href="/wiki/Ambassadors_Theatre_(London)" title="Ambassadors Theatre (London)">New Ambassadors Theatre</a>, where its played until September 2007. Smith was nominated for the 2008 <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Olivier_Award_for_Best_Actress_in_a_Musical" title="Laurence Olivier Award for Best Actress in a Musical">Laurence Olivier Award for Best Actress in a Musical</a> for her performance in the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Smith appeared as Vanessa in <i>Tinderbox: a Revenge Comedy</i> by <a href="/wiki/Lucy_Kirkwood" title="Lucy Kirkwood">Lucy Kirkwood</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Bush_Theatre" title="Bush Theatre">Bush Theatre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shepherd%27s_Bush" title="Shepherd's Bush">Shepherd's Bush</a>, in April 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Smith originated the role of <a href="/wiki/Elle_Woods" title="Elle Woods">Elle Woods</a> in the musical version of <i><a href="/wiki/Legally_Blonde_(musical)" title="Legally Blonde (musical)">Legally Blonde</a></i> in its transfer from Broadway to London's <a href="/wiki/West_End_theatre" title="West End theatre">West End</a> in December 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Ex-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blue_(boy_band)" title="Blue (boy band)">Blue boyband</a> member <a href="/wiki/Duncan_James" title="Duncan James">Duncan James</a> played opposite Smith until June 2010, when he was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Richard_Fleeshman" title="Richard Fleeshman">Richard Fleeshman</a>. Other cast members Smith has performed alongside include <a href="/wiki/Aoife_Mulholland" title="Aoife Mulholland">Aoife Mulholland</a> as Brooke, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Denise_Van_Outen" title="Denise Van Outen">Denise Van Outen</a> as Paulette, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Davison" title="Peter Davison">Peter Davison</a> as Professor Callahan and <a href="/wiki/Alex_Gaumond" title="Alex Gaumond">Alex Gaumond</a> as Emmett Forrest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Her performance was well received, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Daily Telegraph</a></i> describing her as: "blessed with vitality, warmth, great comic timing and sudden moments of touching vulnerability. She is infinitely more likeable than <a href="/wiki/Reese_Witherspoon" title="Reese Witherspoon">Reese Witherspoon</a> in <a href="/wiki/Legally_Blonde" title="Legally Blonde">the film</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Smith was originally supposed to leave <i>Legally Blonde</i> on 23 October 2010, but she extended her run to 8 January 2011, when <a href="/wiki/Susan_McFadden" title="Susan McFadden">Susan McFadden</a> took over the role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> For her role in <i>Legally Blonde</i>, Smith won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theatregoers%27_Choice_Award" title="Theatregoers' Choice Award">WhatsOnStage.com Theatregoers' Choice Award for Best Actress in a Musical</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> She was also nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Evening_Standard_Theatre_Awards" title="Evening Standard Theatre Awards">Natasha Richardson Award for Best Actress</a> and was the winner of the <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Olivier_Award_for_Best_Actress_in_a_Musical" title="Laurence Olivier Award for Best Actress in a Musical">Laurence Olivier Award for Best Actress in a Musical</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Between March and June 2011, she appeared in <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Nunn" title="Trevor Nunn">Trevor Nunn</a>'s production of <i><a href="/wiki/Flare_Path" title="Flare Path">Flare Path</a></i> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theatre_Royal,_Haymarket" title="Theatre Royal, Haymarket">Theatre Royal, Haymarket</a> as Doris, a former barmaid married to a Polish count in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RAF" title="RAF">RAF</a>. The production was part of the playwright <a href="/wiki/Terence_Rattigan" title="Terence Rattigan">Terence Rattigan</a>'s centenary year celebrations and also starred <a href="/wiki/Sienna_Miller" title="Sienna Miller">Sienna Miller</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Purefoy" title="James Purefoy">James Purefoy</a>. For this role she won the 2012 <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Olivier_Award_for_Best_Performance_in_a_Supporting_Role" title="Laurence Olivier Award for Best Performance in a Supporting Role">Laurence Olivier Award for Best Performance in a Supporting Role</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thestage.co.uk_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-thestage.co.uk-2">[2]</a></sup> the 2011 <a href="/wiki/Evening_Standard_Theatre_Awards" title="Evening Standard Theatre Awards">Natasha Richardson Award for Best Actress</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Doris_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Doris-4">[4]</a></sup> and the BroadwayWorld UK Award for Best Featured Actress in a Play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>As well as her role in <i>Legally Blonde</i>, Smith also performed the title role in the workshop productions of a new <a href="/wiki/Bridget_Jones%27_Diary_(musical)" title="Bridget Jones' Diary (musical)">musical adaptation of <i>Bridget Jones' Diary</i></a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> On 5 April 2012, it was announced that Smith had decided to pull out of the musical, and would no longer be playing the title role, delaying the production of the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> From September to November 2012, Smith played the title role in <i><a href="/wiki/Hedda_Gabler" title="Hedda Gabler">Hedda Gabler</a></i> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Vic_Theatre" title="Old Vic Theatre">Old Vic</a>, winning the 2013 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theatregoers%27_Choice_Award" title="Theatregoers' Choice Award">Theatregoers' Choice Award</a> for Best Actress in a Play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Shortly before collecting her award at the ceremony on 17 February 2013, Smith performed the original song "Stagey And Proud", which was written by Chris Passey and Amy Carroll.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>From September to November 2013, she starred alongside <a href="/wiki/David_Walliams" title="David Walliams">David Walliams</a> in a <a href="/wiki/West_End_theatre" title="West End theatre">West End</a> production of <i><a href="/wiki/A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream" title="A Midsummer Night's Dream">A Midsummer Night's Dream</a></i>, as part of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Grandage" title="Michael Grandage">Michael Grandage</a>'s season of plays at the <a href="/wiki/No%C3%ABl_Coward_Theatre" title="Noël Coward Theatre">Noël Coward Theatre</a>.</p>
<p>Smith starred in a new London production of the musical <i><a href="/wiki/Funny_Girl_(musical)" title="Funny Girl (musical)">Funny Girl</a></i>. It previewed at the <a href="/wiki/Menier_Chocolate_Factory" title="Menier Chocolate Factory">Menier Chocolate Factory</a> from 20 November 2015, opened on 2 December and ran until 6 March 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> The show then transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Savoy_Theatre" title="Savoy Theatre">Savoy Theatre</a> in the West End for a 12-week run, but extended through 8 October 2016.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Sheridan Smith states in <i>The Stage</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> that she is married to the job..</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio">Radio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheridan_Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Radio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Smith played companion <a href="/wiki/Lucie_Miller" title="Lucie Miller">Lucie Miller</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Paul_McGann" title="Paul McGann">Paul McGann</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Eighth_Doctor" title="Eighth Doctor">Eighth Doctor</a> in a radio series of <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">Doctor Who</a></i>, produced by <a href="/wiki/Big_Finish_Productions" title="Big Finish Productions">Big Finish</a> and transmitted on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBC_7" title="BBC 7">BBC 7</a> from New Year's Eve 2006; the plays returned in a second series released on CD and scheduled for BBC 7 broadcast. A third series of adventures for Lucie and the Doctor aired in March 2009, as weekly half-hour downloads followed by a Christmas Special. Lucie Miller made her final appearances in the fourth series, ending in 2011. Smith also appeared as Ruby Ruggles in the 2008 radio serial <i><a href="/wiki/The_Way_We_Live_Right_Now" title="The Way We Live Right Now">The Way We Live Right Now</a></i>, and took over the role of Tamsin Trelawny in series 2 of <i><a href="/wiki/Elephants_to_Catch_Eels" title="Elephants to Catch Eels">Elephants to Catch Eels</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_4" title="BBC Radio 4">BBC Radio 4</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheridan_Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Smith was in a relationship with <a href="/wiki/James_Corden" title="James Corden">James Corden</a>, which ended in June 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> In an interview in March 2010, she revealed that she and Scottish actor <a href="/wiki/Ross_McCall" title="Ross McCall">Ross McCall</a> had begun dating after a 10-year friendship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> However, in August the same year, she stated that they had separated due to the pressure of a long-distance relationship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
