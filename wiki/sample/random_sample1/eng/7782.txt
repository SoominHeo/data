<p><b>Skeid Fotball</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norwegian</a> <a href="/wiki/Football" title="Football">football</a> club and is located in the capital <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a>. Its current home field is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nordre_%C3%85sen" title="Nordre Åsen">Nordre Åsen</a>, after the club stopped playing at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bislett_stadion#Football" title="Bislett stadion">Bislett</a> after the 2012 season. In past decades it has gained a reputation as a talent factory for the larger clubs in Norwegian football, and it has produced several players for the <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_football_team" title="Norway national football team">national team</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Braaten" title="Daniel Braaten">Daniel Braaten</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Fredheim_Holm" title="Daniel Fredheim Holm">Daniel Fredheim Holm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Omar_Elabdellaoui" title="Omar Elabdellaoui">Omar Elabdellaoui</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mohammed_Abdellaoue" title="Mohammed Abdellaoue">Mohammed Abdellaoue</a>. Other notable players include <a href="/wiki/Paul_Miller_(footballer,_born_1959)" title="Paul Miller (footballer, born 1959)">Paul Miller</a>. Skeid played in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adeccoligaen" title="Adeccoligaen">Adeccoligaen</a> in 2009 after a short stint in the second division. They finished champions of the Second Group of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norwegian_Second_Division" title="Norwegian Second Division">Norwegian Second Division</a> in 2008. However, Skeid relegated again to Second Division at end of 2009 season in 16th and last position despite a good start.</p>
<p>They played in Norwegian Premier League between 1938-1970 (Norwegian League didn't play between 1940–1947), 1972-1975 (4 seasons), 1978-1980 (3 seasons), 1996-1997 (2 seasons). Their last season in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tippeligaen" title="Tippeligaen">Norwegian Premier League</a> was in 1999.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skeid_Fotball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norwegian_Cup" title="Norwegian Cup">Norwegian Cup</a>: 1947, 1954, 1955, 1956, 1958, 1963, 1965, 1974</p>
<p>Won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tippeligaen" title="Tippeligaen">Norwegian Premier League</a>: 1966</p>
<p>Runner-up in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norwegian_Cup" title="Norwegian Cup">Norwegian Cup</a>: 1939, 1940, 1949</p>
<p>Runner-up in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tippeligaen" title="Tippeligaen">Norwegian Premier League</a>: 1938-39, 1952-53, 1953-54, 1957-58, 1967</p>
<p>Third-place in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tippeligaen" title="Tippeligaen">Norwegian Premier League</a>: 1963,</p>
<p>Other honours:</p>
<p>1929: Won the regional championships after beating <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A5lerenga_Fotball" title="Vålerenga Fotball">Vålerenga</a> 2-1. The red and blue colors are used for the first time.</p>
<p>1940, 1945: Won the Oslo championships.</p>
<p>1962, 1969, 1998, 1999: Won the Norwegian junior championships.</p>
