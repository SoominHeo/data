<p><b>Sheikh Hasina Wazed</b> (<a href="/wiki/Bengali_language" title="Bengali language">Bengali</a>: <span lang="bn" xml:lang="bn">শেখ হাসিনা ওয়াজেদ</span>; <span class="nowrap"><small>English</small> <span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/ʃ/ 'sh' in 'shy'">ʃ</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span></span> <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'h' in 'hi'">h</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/iː/ long 'e' in 'seed'">iː</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>/</a></span></span>, <span title="English pronunciation respelling"><a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i><b><span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">SHAYK</span></span></b></i></a></span> <span title="English pronunciation respelling"><a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i>hə-<b><span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">SEE</span></span></b>-nə</i></a></span>; born 28 September 1947) is the current <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Bangladesh" title="Prime Minister of Bangladesh">Prime Minister of Bangladesh</a>, in office since January 2009. She previously served as Prime Minister from 1996 to 2001, and has led the <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh_Awami_League" title="Bangladesh Awami League">Bangladesh Awami League</a> since 1981.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hasina is one of the most powerful women in the world, ranking 36th on Forbes' list of The World's 100 Most Powerful Women in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Her political career has spanned more than four decades during which she has been both Prime Minister and opposition leader. As opposition leader, she was the target of an assassination attempt in 2004. In 2007, she was arrested for corruption and charged with murder by the military-backed <a href="/wiki/Caretaker_government_of_Bangladesh" title="Caretaker government of Bangladesh">Caretaker Government</a> during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%932008_Bangladeshi_political_crisis" title="2006–2008 Bangladeshi political crisis">2006–2008 Bangladeshi political crisis</a>, when the generals imposed a <a href="/wiki/State_of_emergency" title="State of emergency">state of emergency</a>. She returned as Prime Minister after a landslide victory for the Awami League-led Grand Alliance in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bangladeshi_parliamentary_election,_2008" title="Bangladeshi parliamentary election, 2008">2008</a>, when they took two-thirds of the seats in parliament. In January 2014 she became the prime minister for the third time after winning the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bangladeshi_parliamentary_election,_2014" title="Bangladeshi parliamentary election, 2014">2014 parliamentary election</a>, which was boycotted by the main opposition BNP-led alliance.</p>
<p>For the better part of the last two decades, Hasina's chief rival has been BNP leader <a href="/wiki/Khaleda_Zia" title="Khaleda Zia">Khaleda Zia</a>. The two women have alternated as non-interim Prime Ministers since 1991.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheikh_Hasina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sheikh Hasina was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tungipara" title="Tungipara">Tungipara</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Pakistan" title="East Pakistan">East Pakistan</a> on 28 September 1947. She is the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Sheikh_Mujibur_Rahman" title="Sheikh Mujibur Rahman">Sheikh Mujibur Rahman</a>, first president of Bangladesh, and <a href="/wiki/Sheikh_Fazilatunnesa_Mujib" title="Sheikh Fazilatunnesa Mujib">Sheikh Fazilatunnesa Mujib</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">[6]</a></sup> As she said in many interviews that she had grown up in fear due to her father's political works. During the peak of violence of the 1970 Elections in Pakistan as well as her father's arrest she had lived in refuge with her grandmother. Saying "I was not allowed to go to the school. Because I had to cross the canal by a wooden bridge, she was very much afraid that if I fall from this wooden bridge I will fall in the river".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:4_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-:4-7">[7]</a></sup> She was active in the student politics of Dhaka University.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Hasina was not in Bangladesh when her father was <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_Sheikh_Mujibur_Rahman" title="Assassination of Sheikh Mujibur Rahman">assassinated on 15 August 1975</a>. She was not allowed to return to the country until after she was elected to lead the Awami League Party in 16 February 1981 and arrived on 17 May 1981.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:4_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-:4-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_political_career">Early political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheikh_Hasina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Movement_against_General_Ershad.27s_presidency">Movement against General Ershad's presidency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheikh_Hasina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Movement against General Ershad's presidency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>While living in self-exile in India after her father and family's assassination in 1975 (only she and a sister survived as they were in <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a>), Hasina was elected President of the Bangladesh Awami League in 1981.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-9">[9]</a></sup> The Awami League has been described as a "left-of-center" party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> After she returned to Bangladesh, President <a href="/wiki/Ziaur_Rahman" title="Ziaur Rahman">Ziaur Rahman</a> was assassinated on 30 May 1981 in an attempted military coup. General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hossain_Mohammad_Ershad" title="Hossain Mohammad Ershad">Hossain Mohammad Ershad</a> and most of the army remained loyal to the government. The following year, Ershad captured power through a bloodless coup and declared <a href="/wiki/Martial_law" title="Martial law">martial law</a>, suppressing political party activity and suspending the constitution. The constitution was later restored in 1986 after the general election won by General Ershad's <a href="/wiki/Jatiya_Party_(Ershad)" title="Jatiya Party (Ershad)">Jatiya Party</a> won, and martial law was withdrawn.</p>
<p>Hasina was in and out of detention throughout the 1980s. In 1984, Hasina was put under <a href="/wiki/House_arrest" title="House arrest">house arrest</a> in February and again in November. In March 1985, she was put under house arrest for three months. Her party, along with the <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh_Nationalist_Party" title="Bangladesh Nationalist Party">Bangladesh Nationalist Party</a>, led by Ziaur Rahman's widow Khaleda Zia, continued to work to restore democratically elected government, which they achieved by the democratic election in 1991, won by the BNP.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leader_of_the_opposition.2C_1986-87">Leader of the opposition, 1986-87</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheikh_Hasina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Leader of the opposition, 1986-87">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hasina and the Awami League participated in the 1986 parliamentary elections held under President Ershad. She served as the leader of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parliamentary_opposition" title="Parliamentary opposition">opposition</a> in 1986–1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">[6]</a></sup> Hasina's decision to take part in the election had been criticised by her opponents, since the election was held under the martial law, and the other main opposition group, led by Khaleda Zia, boycotted the poll. However, her supporters maintained that she used the platform effectively to challenge Ershad's rule. Ershad dissolved the parliament in December 1987 when Hasina and her Awami League resigned from the parliament in an attempt to call for a fresh general election to be held under a neutral government. During November and December in 1987, mass uprising happened in Dhaka, several people were killed including <a href="/wiki/Noor_Hossain" title="Noor Hossain">Noor Hossain</a>, a Hasina supporter.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1991_election">1991 election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheikh_Hasina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1991 election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After several years of autocratic rule, widespread protests and strikes created so much unrest that the economy was not functioning. A huge mass protest in December 1990 ousted General Ershad from the power, who resigned in favour of his Vice President Justice Shahabuddin. The <a href="/wiki/Caretaker_government" title="Caretaker government">caretaker government</a>, headed by <a href="/wiki/Shahabuddin_Ahmed" title="Shahabuddin Ahmed">Shahabuddin Ahmed</a>, the Chief Justice of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bangladesh_Supreme_Court" title="Bangladesh Supreme Court">Bangladesh Supreme Court</a>, administered a general election for the parliament. The Bangladesh Nationalist Party (BNP) led by Khaleda Zia won a general majority, and Hasina's Awami League emerged as the largest opposition party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Among 3 constituencies Hasina fought, she lost in two and won in one. Accepting election defeat, Hasina offered resignation as the party president but stayed on at the request of party leaders.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_1991.E2.80.931996_period">The 1991–1996 period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheikh_Hasina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The 1991–1996 period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Politics in Bangladesh took a decisive turn in 1994, after <a href="/wiki/Magura_District" title="Magura District">Magura</a> <a href="/wiki/By-election" title="By-election">by-elections</a>. This election was held after the death of the MP for that constituency, a member of Hasina's party. The Awami League expected to win back the seat. But the BNP candidate won through rigging and manipulation, as per the neutral observer who came to witness the election.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1996_elections">1996 elections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheikh_Hasina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 1996 elections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Awami_League" title="Awami League">Awami League</a>, with other opposition parties, demanded that the next general elections be held under a neutral caretaker government, and that provision for caretaker governments to manage elections be incorporated in the constitution. The ruling BNP refused to act on these demands.</p>
<p>Opposition parties launched an unprecedented campaign, calling strikes for weeks on end. The government accused them of destroying the economy while the opposition countered that BNP could solve this problem by acceding to their demands. In late 1995, the MPs of the Awami League and other parties resigned from the parliament. Parliament completed its term and a general election was held on 15 February 1996. The election was boycotted by all major parties except the ruling BNP. Hasina described the election as a farce.</p>
<p>The new parliament, composed mostly of BNP members, amended the constitution to create provisions for a caretaker government (CTG). The next parliamentary elections on 30 June 1996 were held under a neutral caretaker government headed by retired chief Justice <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_Habibur_Rahman" title="Muhammad Habibur Rahman">Muhammad Habibur Rahman</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="First_term_as_Prime_Minister.2C_1996-2001">First term as Prime Minister, 1996-2001</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheikh_Hasina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: First term as Prime Minister, 1996-2001">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Sheik Hasina Wazed served her first term as prime minister of Bangladesh from 1996-2001.She became the first Bangladeshi Prime Minister since its independence to complete the entire term.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">[6]</a></sup> She signed the 30 year water sharing treaty of the Ganges with India. Her administration repealed the Indemnity Act, which protected the killers of Sheikh Muijib, her father and the first president of Bangladesh. Her government opened the telecom industry to the private sector which till then was limited to government owned companies. In 1999 the government started the New Industrial Policy (NIP) which aimed to strengthen the private industry and encourage growth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-:3-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bangladesh joined two multilateral bodies, <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal_Initiative_for_Multi-Sectoral_Technical_and_Economic_Cooperation" title="Bay of Bengal Initiative for Multi-Sectoral Technical and Economic Cooperation">BIMSTEC</a> (Bay of Bengal Initiative for Multi-Sectoral Technical and Economic Cooperation) and <a href="/wiki/D-8_Organization_for_Economic_Cooperation" title="D-8 Organization for Economic Cooperation">D-8</a> (D-8 Organization for Economic Cooperation). The NIP allowed foreign companies to open 100 percent owned subsidiaries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-:3-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2001_election">2001 election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheikh_Hasina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2001 election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 2001 election, although winning 40% of the popular vote (slightly less than the BNP's 41%), the Awami League won just 62 seats in the Parliament, while the 'Four Party Alliance' led by the Bangladesh Nationalist Party won 234 seats, giving them a two-thirds majority in Parliament. Hasina herself ran in three constituencies<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, and was defeated in a constituency in <a href="/wiki/Rangpur_District" title="Rangpur District">Rangpur</a>, which included her husband's home town, but won in two other seats. Hasina and the Awami League rejected the results, claiming that the election was rigged with the help of the President and the caretaker government. The international community was largely satisfied with the elections, and the 'Four Party Alliance' went on to form the government.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Opposition_period.2C_2001-2008">Opposition period, 2001-2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheikh_Hasina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Opposition period, 2001-2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Awami League MPs were irregular in attending the Parliament during the following period.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In late 2003, the Awami League started its first major anti-government movement, culminating in the declaration by party general secretary <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abdul_Jolil_(Bangladesh_politician)" title="Abdul Jolil (Bangladesh politician)">Abdul Jolil</a> that the government would fall before 30 April 2004. This failed to happen and was seen as a blow to the party and Hasina, who had implicitly supported Jalil.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004_assassination_attempt">2004 assassination attempt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheikh_Hasina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2004 assassination attempt">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/2004_Dhaka_grenade_attack" title="2004 Dhaka grenade attack">2004 Dhaka grenade attack</a></div>
<p>During her second term as leader of the opposition, political unrest and violence increased. Ahsanullah Master, an MP, was killed in 2004. This was followed by a <a href="/wiki/2004_Dhaka_grenade_attack" title="2004 Dhaka grenade attack">grenade attack</a> on <a href="/wiki/2004_Dhaka_grenade_attack" title="2004 Dhaka grenade attack">21 August</a> on an Awami League gathering in Dhaka, resulting in the death of 21 party supporters, including party women's secretary <a href="/wiki/Ivy_Rahman" title="Ivy Rahman">Ivy Rahman</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shah_M_S_Kibria" title="Shah M S Kibria">Shah M S Kibria</a>, Hasina's former finance minister, was also killed that year, in a grenade attack in <a href="/wiki/Sylhet" title="Sylhet">Sylhet</a> that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2005, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.B.M._Mohiuddin_Chowdhury" title="A.B.M. Mohiuddin Chowdhury">A.B.M. Mohiuddin Chowdhury</a>, an incumbent of the Awami League, won the important mayoral election in <a href="/wiki/Chittagong" title="Chittagong">Chittagong</a>, the port city and second-largest city in Bangladesh. This election was seen as a showdown between the opposition and the ruling party.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Caretaker_government_and_military_intervention.2C_October_2006.E2.80.932008">Caretaker government and military intervention, October 2006–2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheikh_Hasina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Caretaker government and military intervention, October 2006–2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The months preceding the planned <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bangladeshi_parliamentary_elections,_2007" title="Bangladeshi parliamentary elections, 2007">22 January 2007 elections</a> were filled with political unrest and controversy. Following the end of Khaleda Zia's government in late October 2006, there were protests and strikes, during which 40 people were killed in the following month, over uncertainty about who would head the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bangladeshi_caretaker_government" title="Bangladeshi caretaker government">caretaker government</a>. The caretaker government had difficulty bringing the all parties to the table. Awami League and its allies protested and alleged that the caretaker government favored the BNP.</p>
<p>The interim period was marked by violence and strikes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Presidential Advisor <a href="/wiki/Mukhlesur_Rahman_Chowdhury" title="Mukhlesur Rahman Chowdhury">Mukhlesur Rahman Chowdhury</a> negotiated with Hasina and Khaleda Zia and brought all the parties to the planned 22 January 2007 parliamentary elections. Later <a href="/wiki/Hussain_Muhammad_Ershad" title="Hussain Muhammad Ershad">Hussain Muhammad Ershad</a>'s nomination was cancelled<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="(January 2014)">why?</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (January 2014)">by whom?</span></a></i>]</sup>; as a result, the Grand Alliance withdrew its candidates en masse on the last day possible.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> They demanded to have voters' lists published.</p>
<p>Later in the month, the president <a href="/wiki/Iajuddin_Ahmed" title="Iajuddin Ahmed">Iajuddin Ahmed</a> was compelled to impose a state of emergency and thus Lt General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moeen_Uddin_Ahmed" title="Moeen Uddin Ahmed">Moeen Uddin Ahmed</a> took over the government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Political activity was prohibited. <a href="/wiki/Fakhruddin_Ahmed" title="Fakhruddin Ahmed">Fakhruddin Ahmed</a> became the chief advisor with the support of Bangladesh army.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="July_2007_arrest">July 2007 arrest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheikh_Hasina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: July 2007 arrest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 16 July 2007, Hasina was arrested by state police at her home and taken before a local court in Dhaka.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> She was accused of extortion and denied bail, and was held in a building converted into a jail on the premises of the National Parliament. The Awami League said the arrest was politically motivated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Security_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Security-24">[24]</a></sup> On 17 July 2007, the Anti-Corruption Commission sent notices to both Hasina and Khaleda Zia, instructing them to provide details of their assets to the Commission within one week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Hasina's son <a href="/wiki/Sajeeb_Wazed" title="Sajeeb Wazed">Sajeeb Wazed Joy</a> was out of the country, and said he would try to organise worldwide protest. These arrests of the political leaders were widely seen as a move by the military-backed interim government to force Hasina and Zia out of the country and into political exile.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> United Kingdom MPs condemned the arrest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 30 July 2007, the Dhaka High Court suspended Hasina's extortion trial and ordered her release on bail.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> On 2 September 2007, an additional case was filed against Hasina by the Anti-Corruption Commission regarding the awarding of a contract for the construction of a power plant in 1997, for which she allegedly took a bribe of 30 million takas and kept the contract from going to the lowest bidder. Six others were also accused of involvement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sued_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sued-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> A graft case was filed against Zia on the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sued_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sued-30">[30]</a></sup> On 13 January 2008, Hasina was indicted on extortion charges by a special court along with two of her relatives, her sister Sheikh Rehana and her cousin Sheikh Selim.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> On 6 February, the High Court stopped the trial, ruling that she could not be prosecuted under emergency laws for crimes alleged to have been committed prior to the imposition of the state of emergency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 11 June 2008, Hasina was released on <a href="/wiki/Parole" title="Parole">parole</a> for medical reasons. The next day she flew to the United States to be treated for hearing impairment, eye problems and high blood pressure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Prof. <a href="/wiki/Syed_Modasser_Ali" title="Syed Modasser Ali">Syed Modasser Ali</a>, her personal <a href="/wiki/Physician" title="Physician">physician</a>, threatened to sue the caretaker government over negligence regarding Hasina's treatment during her detention.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The caretaker government held mayoral elections in which Sheikh Hasina's Awami League won 12 out of 13 mayoral elections. The caretaker government extended her two month medical parole by one more month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008_election_and_return_to_power">2008 election and return to power</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheikh_Hasina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2008 election and return to power">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 6 November 2008, Hasina returned to Bangladesh to attend the <a href="/wiki/Bangladeshi_general_election,_2008" title="Bangladeshi general election, 2008">Ninth National Parliamentary Elections 2008</a> scheduled for 29 December 2008<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> and decided to participate in the parliamentary election under the name of "Grand Alliance" with the Jatiya Party led by <a href="/wiki/Hussain_Muhammad_Ershad" title="Hussain Muhammad Ershad">Hussain Muhammad Ershad</a> as its main partner. On 11 December 2008, Hasina formally announced her party's election manifesto during a news conference &amp; vowed to build a "<a href="/wiki/Vision_2021#Digital_Bangladesh" title="Vision 2021">Digital Bangladesh</a>" by 2021.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her Bangladesh Awami League and its Grand Alliance (a total of 14 parties) won the <a href="/wiki/Bangladeshi_general_election,_2008" title="Bangladeshi general election, 2008">general election</a> held on 29 December 2008 with a two-thirds majority numerically the party controls 230 seats out of 299.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> But Khaleda Zia leader of BNP-led coalition (4-Party Alliance) rejected the results of the election by questioning the Chief Election Commissioner "for stage-managing the parliamentary election".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Finally Hasina was sworn into office as the Prime Minister for the second time on 6 January 2009.</p>
<p>After being <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a>, Hasina reneged on her agreement with Jatiya Party to make <a href="/wiki/Hussain_Muhammad_Ershad" title="Hussain Muhammad Ershad">Hussain Muhammad Ershad</a> as <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">President</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>

<p>Hasina removed many of her party politicians who supported reforms forced by the previous caretaker government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> She had to confront a major national crisis in the form of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_Bangladesh_Rifles_revolt" title="2009 Bangladesh Rifles revolt">2009 Bangladesh Rifles revolt</a>. In 2012, she maintained a hardline stance and refused to allow entry to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rohingya" title="Rohingya">Rohingya</a> refugees fleeing <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">Myanmar</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2012_Rakhine_State_riots" title="2012 Rakhine State riots">2012 Rakhine State riots</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> On 27 June a case against Hasina and 24 other Bangladeshi ministers and security personnel was lodged at the <a href="/wiki/International_Criminal_Court" title="International Criminal Court">International Criminal Court</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> She has been "credited internationally" for the achievement of some of the United Nations <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Development_Goals" title="Millennium Development Goals">Millennium Development Goals</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-:2-46">[46]</a></sup> In 2012 a coup attempt against her by mid ranking army officers was stopped, with Bangladesh army being tipped off by Indian intelligence agency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheikh_Hasina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Criminal_charges">Criminal charges</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheikh_Hasina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Criminal charges">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2007, Hasina was charged with graft and extortion by military backed government. She was accused of having forced Bangladeshi businessman Tajul Islam Farooq to pay bribes in 1998 before his company could build a power plant. Farooq said that he paid Hasina BDT 30 million (US$441,000, or €383,211) to get his project approved by the government <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup>.</p>
<p>On 11 April 2007, the police filed murder charges against Hasina, alleging that she masterminded the killing in October 2006 of four supporters of a rival political party. The four alleged victims were beaten to death during clashes between the Awami League and rival party activists. Deputy police commissioner, Shahidul Haq Bhuiyan, said "detective branch police submitted the charge-sheet of the case to a Dhaka court today after carrying out investigations and taking evidence".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Hasina was visiting the United States at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 18 April 2007, the government barred Hasina from returning, saying that she had made provocative statements and that her return could cause disorder. This was described as a temporary measure. The CTG had also been trying to get Khaleda Zia to leave the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Hasina vowed to return home, and on 22 April 2007, a warrant was issued for her arrest for murder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> Describing the case against her as "totally false and fake", Hasina said that she wanted to defend herself against the charges in court. On 23 April 2007, the arrest warrant was suspended,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> and on 25 April 2007, the ban on Hasina's entry into the country was dropped.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> After spending 51 days in the United States and the UK, on 7 May 2007 Hasina returned to Dhaka, where she was greeted by a crowd of several thousand. She told reporters that the government should not have delayed her return.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheikh_Hasina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hasina got married to Dr. <a href="/wiki/M._A._Wazed_Miah" title="M. A. Wazed Miah">M. A. Wazed Miah</a> in 1968 who died on 9 May 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brittanica_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-brittanica-57">[57]</a></sup> She has one son <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sajeeb_Wazed_Joy" title="Sajeeb Wazed Joy">Sajeeb Wazed Joy</a> and one daughter <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saima_Wazed_Putul" title="Saima Wazed Putul">Saima Wazed Hossain Putul</a>. Joy lives in the United States, and Putul lives in Canada<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arabnews_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-arabnews-58">[58]</a></sup>. Hasina's only sibling alive is <a href="/wiki/Sheikh_Rehana" title="Sheikh Rehana">Sheikh Rehana</a> who lives in the UK. Former minister of Expatriates' Welfare and Overseas Employment and present minister of LGRD, <a href="/wiki/Khandaker_Mosharraf_Hossain" title="Khandaker Mosharraf Hossain">Khandaker Mosharraf Hossain</a> is the kin of Sheikh Hasina in that he is the father-in-law of Putul<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup>.</p>
