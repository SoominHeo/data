<p><b>Sheppard–Yonge</b> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Toronto_subway_stations" title="List of Toronto subway stations">station</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Line_1_Yonge%E2%80%93University" title="Line 1 Yonge–University">Line 1 Yonge–University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Line_4_Sheppard" title="Line 4 Sheppard">Line 4 Sheppard</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_subway" title="Toronto subway">Toronto subway</a>. It is the third-busiest station in the system, after <a href="/wiki/Bloor%E2%80%93Yonge_(TTC)" title="Bloor–Yonge (TTC)">Bloor–Yonge</a> and <a href="/wiki/St._George_(TTC)" title="St. George (TTC)">St. George</a>, serving a combined total of approximately 121,410 people per day in <span class="nowrap">2014<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></span>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheppard%E2%80%93Yonge_(TTC)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sheppard-Yonge first opened as Sheppard in 1974, when the Yonge-University subway line was extended from <a href="/wiki/Eglinton_(TTC)" title="Eglinton (TTC)">Eglinton</a> to <a href="/wiki/Finch_(TTC)" title="Finch (TTC)">Finch</a>. The extension was planned to open in two stages with Sheppard as the temporary terminus, but construction north of <a href="/wiki/York_Mills_(TTC)" title="York Mills (TTC)">York Mills</a> was delayed by various problems and in 1973, York Mills was opened as the temporary terminus instead; Sheppard and Finch station opened in 1974. The H-2 class subway cars delivered in 1971 included destination signs for "Sheppard via downtown" on the expectation that it would be a terminal station.</p>
<p>The station was expanded and renamed "Sheppard-Yonge" in 2002 with the opening of the Sheppard subway line, for which this station became the western terminus. The renaming was similar to that of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bloor-Yonge_(TTC)" title="Bloor-Yonge (TTC)">Bloor-Yonge Station</a>. Unlike Bloor-Yonge, where the signs on Line 1 platforms still read "Bloor" and those on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Line_2_Bloor-Danforth" title="Line 2 Bloor-Danforth">Line 2 Bloor-Danforth</a> read "Yonge", Sheppard-Yonge is given its full name on both sets of platforms; all existing signs within the station were changed to give the new name. At that time, this station became accessible with elevators. When the automated announcements were installed on Toronto's subway trains, Line 1 trains referred to the station as "Sheppard" while Line 4 trains referred to the station as "Sheppard-Yonge", the new <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Rocket" title="Toronto Rocket">Toronto Rocket</a> subway trains refer to the station on both lines as "Sheppard-Yonge".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_description">Station description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheppard%E2%80%93Yonge_(TTC)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station is located under <a href="/wiki/Yonge_Street" title="Yonge Street">Yonge Street</a> at <a href="/wiki/Sheppard_Avenue" title="Sheppard Avenue">Sheppard Avenue</a>, and is built on five levels. All seven entrances are located at street level,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ttc_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ttc-3">[3]</a></sup> as well as the bus platform. The three levels below are concourse levels, which provide access to the bus platform and the two subway lines. The subway platforms are on the two lower levels, with the Yonge–University line on the bottom and the newer Sheppard line crossing above.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ttc_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-ttc-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>There are two accessible entrances, one on the east side of Yonge Street beside the Sheppard Centre and the other by a private elevator from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nestle_Canada_Building" title="Nestle Canada Building">Nestle Canada Building</a> at 25 Sheppard Avenue West. Other entrances with automatic turnstiles are from Sheppard Avenue West, Beecroft Road, and Harlandale Avenue. There is also an unmanned entrance to the south end of the Yonge–University platform accessible from the official entrance located on the northeast corner of Yonge Street and Anndale Drive, with alternative access through the <a href="/wiki/Procter_%26_Gamble" title="Procter &amp; Gamble">Procter &amp; Gamble</a> building (Monday to Friday, 6am to 7pm excluding holidays) or connecting through the underground parking lot of Whole Foods Market. Access by Emerald Park condominium on the northwest corner of Yonge Street at Poyntz Road is under construction and is not available.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture_and_art">Architecture and art</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheppard%E2%80%93Yonge_(TTC)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Architecture and art">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sheppard-Yonge_TTC_3828611528.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="768" data-file-width="1024" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Sheppard-Yonge_TTC_3828611528.jpg/220px-Sheppard-Yonge_TTC_3828611528.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Sheppard-Yonge_TTC_3828611528.jpg/330px-Sheppard-Yonge_TTC_3828611528.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Sheppard-Yonge_TTC_3828611528.jpg/440px-Sheppard-Yonge_TTC_3828611528.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sheppard-Yonge_TTC_3828611528.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Spiegel's <i>Immersion Land</i></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The station on the Sheppard line was designed by architectural firm NORR Limited. The construction of the Sheppard line included the integration of the bus terminal at street level into the fare-paid zone.</p>
<p>The artwork in the station, entitled <i>Immersion Land</i> and created by the artist <a href="/wiki/Stacey_Spiegel" title="Stacey Spiegel">Stacey Spiegel</a>, consists of panoramic <a href="/wiki/Posterization" title="Posterization">posterized</a> murals created from 150 digital photos rendered onto single-colour <a href="/wiki/Mosaic" title="Mosaic">mosaic</a> tiles. The artwork depicts rural scenery along Yonge Street or <a href="/wiki/Ontario_Highway_11" title="Ontario Highway 11">Highway 11</a> somewhere between <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ontario" title="Lake Ontario">Lake Ontario</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Bay,_Ontario" title="North Bay, Ontario">North Bay</a>, and is located on the upper (Sheppard line) platform level.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subway_infrastructure_in_the_vicinity">Subway infrastructure in the vicinity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheppard%E2%80%93Yonge_(TTC)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Subway infrastructure in the vicinity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sheppard_Yonge_Platform_02.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2000" data-file-width="3000" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Sheppard_Yonge_Platform_02.jpg/220px-Sheppard_Yonge_Platform_02.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Sheppard_Yonge_Platform_02.jpg/330px-Sheppard_Yonge_Platform_02.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Sheppard_Yonge_Platform_02.jpg/440px-Sheppard_Yonge_Platform_02.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sheppard_Yonge_Platform_02.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The roughed-in centre platform to be potentially used as a <a href="/wiki/Spanish_solution" title="Spanish solution">Spanish solution</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>A connecting track from the southbound Yonge-University line, used only if cars or work equipment need to be transferred between the two lines, curves around to a point 500 metres west of Yonge, where the Sheppard line tunnel actually begins. This provides an area where trains can be stored clear of the line.</p>
<p>In the station, the Sheppard line tracks cross above the Yonge line. The Sheppard line station has platforms on the outer sides of the tracks, but there is also a roughed-in centre platform. Should the station become a busy transport hub, this platform will be opened and trains will open all their doors, allowing riders <a href="/wiki/Spanish_solution" title="Spanish solution">to enter on one side and exit on the other</a> to improve efficiency.</p>
<p>Trains normally pull into the southern platform to load and discharge passengers, before returning in the direction from which they came; the northern platform is used only by trains which are going out of service and so must discharge their passengers without allowing more aboard.</p>
<p>Just east of the station, the Sheppard line converges with a second junction track from the northbound Yonge-University line.</p>
