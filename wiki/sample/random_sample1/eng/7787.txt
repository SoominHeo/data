<p><b>Sheffield Football Club</b> is an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club from <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire" title="South Yorkshire">South Yorkshire</a>. They play in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Premier_League" title="Northern Premier League">Northern Premier League</a> Division One South, at level 8 of the <a href="/wiki/English_football_league_system" title="English football league system">English football league system</a>.</p>
<p>Founded in <a href="/wiki/1850s_in_association_football" title="1850s in association football">1857</a>, the club is the <a href="/wiki/Oldest_football_clubs" title="Oldest football clubs">oldest club now playing association football</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Sheffield F.C. initially played games under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sheffield_rules" title="Sheffield rules">Sheffield rules</a> and did not officially adopt the FA rules until 1878.</p>
<p>The club competes in the <a href="/wiki/Rules_derby" title="Rules derby">Rules derby</a> with near neighbours <a href="/wiki/Hallam_F.C." title="Hallam F.C.">Hallam</a>. In 2004 they were given the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_Order_of_Merit" title="FIFA Order of Merit">FIFA Order of Merit</a> - an award given to only one other club: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_Madrid" title="Real Madrid">Real Madrid</a> - and in 2007 they were inducted into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_football_hall_of_fame" title="English football hall of fame">English football hall of fame</a>, to commemorate their 150th anniversary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the pitch, the club's finest hour came in 1904 when they won the <a href="/wiki/FA_Amateur_Cup" title="FA Amateur Cup">FA Amateur Cup</a>, a competition conceived after a suggestion by Sheffield. They also finished as runners up of the <a href="/wiki/FA_Vase" title="FA Vase">FA Vase</a> in 1977.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheffield_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1855, members of a Sheffield <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a> club organised informal kick-abouts without any official rules.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Football_in_Sheffield_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Football_in_Sheffield-4">[4]</a></sup> – subsequently two members, <a href="/wiki/Nathaniel_Creswick" title="Nathaniel Creswick">Nathaniel Creswick</a> and <a href="/wiki/William_Prest" title="William Prest">William Prest</a>, formed the Sheffield Football Club.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sheffield_FC_rules_and_regulations.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2112" data-file-width="2251" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Sheffield_FC_rules_and_regulations.jpg/200px-Sheffield_FC_rules_and_regulations.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Sheffield_FC_rules_and_regulations.jpg/300px-Sheffield_FC_rules_and_regulations.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Sheffield_FC_rules_and_regulations.jpg/400px-Sheffield_FC_rules_and_regulations.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sheffield_FC_rules_and_regulations.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Rules" title="Sheffield Rules">Sheffield Rules</a> were devised by <a href="/wiki/Nathaniel_Creswick" title="Nathaniel Creswick">Nathaniel Creswick</a> and <a href="/wiki/William_Prest" title="William Prest">William Prest</a> for use by the newly founded Sheffield F.C.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The inaugural meeting of the club took place on 24 October 1857 at Parkfield House in the suburb of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Highfield,_Sheffield" title="Highfield, Sheffield">Highfield</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The original headquarters was a greenhouse on East Bank Road lent by Thomas Asline Ward, father of the first club president Frederick Ward, and the adjacent field was used as their first playing ground.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Initially, Sheffield FC games were played among club members themselves and took the format of "Married v Singles" or "Professionals v the Rest".</p>
<p>Creswick and Prest were responsible for drawing up the club's rules of play, which were decided upon at the club's AGM on 21 October 1858.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-From_Sheffield_With_Love_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-From_Sheffield_With_Love-7">[7]</a></sup> They were referred to as the <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Rules" title="Sheffield Rules">Sheffield Rules</a>, and were the first official set of rules and laws for the game of football. At the time, before the formation of <a href="/wiki/The_Football_Association" title="The Football Association">the Football Association</a> (FA), many different kinds of football were popular in England. For example, each of the various <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_school_(England)" title="Public school (England)">public schools</a> played football according to their own individual rules, and these varied widely.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The Sheffield Rules were later adopted by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sheffield_and_Hallamshire_Football_Association" title="Sheffield and Hallamshire Football Association">Sheffield Football Association</a> when it was formed in 1867.</p>
<p>Sheffield's near neighbour, <a href="/wiki/Hallam_F.C." title="Hallam F.C.">Hallam</a>, was formed in 1860 and in the same year the two clubs first met each other in a <a href="/wiki/Rules_derby" title="Rules derby">local derby</a> which is still contested today. By 1862 there were 15 clubs in the Sheffield area.</p>
<p>They became members of <a href="/wiki/The_Football_Association" title="The Football Association">The Football Association</a> on 30 November 1863 but continued to use their own set of rules.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Football_in_Sheffield_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Football_in_Sheffield-4">[4]</a></sup> On 2 January 1865, the club played its first fixture outside Sheffield against <a href="/wiki/Nottingham" title="Nottingham">Nottingham</a>, playing eighteen-a-side under Nottingham Rules.</p>
<p>By this time the club had decided only to play teams outside Sheffield in order to seek a bigger challenge. On 31 March 1866, there was a match between a team representing the city of Sheffield and one representing <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, at <a href="/wiki/Battersea_Park" title="Battersea Park">Battersea Park</a>. Rules that differed only slightly from the FA rules were used. The game, played as an eleven aside, was won by London by 2 goals and four touchdowns to nil. However the matter of rules remained a problem with Sheffield clubs continuing to play by their own rules. A number of rule proposals by the club were rejected by the FA in February 1867 and the London Committee were reluctant to commit to further fixtures over Sheffield's refusal to play strictly to FA rules. Sheffield clubs finally adopted the FA rules in 1878.</p>
<p>In 1873 the club entered the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> for the first time, their first ever tie in the competition, against <a href="/wiki/Shropshire_Wanderers_F.C." title="Shropshire Wanderers F.C.">Shropshire Wanderers</a>, being decided after a replay by a coin toss; the only time in the history of the competition that a tie has been decided in this way.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> They would reach the 4th Round of the competition in 1877–78 and 1879–80.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wildstat_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-wildstat-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

The Sheffield squad pictured in 1890.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Their reluctance to play against local clubs led to the formation of Thursday Wanderers in 1876, a team of players registered to Sheffield who wished to play in the <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_%26_Hallamshire_Senior_Cup" title="Sheffield &amp; Hallamshire Senior Cup">Sheffield Challenge Cup</a>. The Wanderers operated from 1876 to 1879, winning the cup in their final year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sheffield's decline from the top echelon of football began with the introduction of professionalism in July 1885, with the amateurs of Sheffield failing to compete with professional teams, losing heavily that year to <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nottingham_Forest_F.C." title="Nottingham Forest F.C.">Nottingham Forest</a> and <a href="/wiki/Notts_County_F.C." title="Notts County F.C.">Notts County</a>. After the legalisation of professionalism, the staunchly amateur Sheffield suggested to the FA the creation of a cup exclusively for amateur clubs. The <a href="/wiki/FA_Amateur_Cup" title="FA Amateur Cup">FA Amateur Cup</a> was inaugurated in 1893 and Sheffield themselves won the competition in 1904.</p>
<p>They joined their first league competition in 1889 when entering the <a href="/wiki/Midland_Football_League#Midland_League_1889.E2.80.931960" title="Midland Football League">Midland League</a>, but left after just one season when they finished bottom of the table.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fchd_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-fchd-12">[12]</a></sup> They were also founder members of the original <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Football_League#Original_League" title="Yorkshire Football League">Yorkshire League</a> in 1898, but again they spent just a single season in the competition.</p>
<p>After the turn of the century, Sheffield competed mainly in local leagues. By 1925–26 they were competing in the <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Association_League" title="Sheffield Association League">Sheffield Association League</a>.</p>

<p>Fifty years after leaving the competition, Club rejoined the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Football_League" title="Yorkshire Football League">Yorkshire League</a>, in 1949. Three years later they won promotion to Division One, but were relegated back to Division Two in 1954. They returned to the top flight at the first time of asking before entering their centenary year in 1957. Celebrations included games against the <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_B_team" title="England national football B team">England B team</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hillsborough_Stadium" title="Hillsborough Stadium">Hillsborough</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and fellow amateur side <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_Park_F.C." title="Queen's Park F.C.">Queen's Park</a> at <a href="/wiki/Bramall_Lane" title="Bramall Lane">Bramall Lane</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1961, Sheffield were relegated to Division Two again, only returning to the top flight again in 1967, and then only for one more season before another relegation. Three years later, in 1970, they were relegated again, to the newly formed Division Three.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fchd_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-fchd-12">[12]</a></sup> Club would spend six seasons in the Yorkshire League's basement division, finishing as low as 10th in 1974. They finally started to turn their fortunes around in 1976 by returning to Division Two, and a year later they were crowned as Division Two champions to return to Division One. In the same season, Sheffield reached the final of the newly formed <a href="/wiki/FA_Vase" title="FA Vase">FA Vase</a>. At <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium_(1923)" title="Wembley Stadium (1923)">Wembley stadium</a>, they drew 1–1 with <a href="/wiki/Billericay_Town_F.C." title="Billericay Town F.C.">Billericay Town</a>, before being beaten 1–2 in the replay at the <a href="/wiki/City_Ground" title="City Ground">City Ground</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nottingham" title="Nottingham">Nottingham</a>.</p>
<p>When the Yorkshire League merged with the <a href="/wiki/Midland_Football_League#Midland_Counties_League_1961.E2.80.931982" title="Midland Football League">Midland League</a> to form the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Counties_East_League" title="Northern Counties East League">Northern Counties East League</a> (NCEL) in 1981, Sheffield were placed in Division One South of the new competition. They stayed in this division for three seasons before the league was restructured, with the club being reassigned to the newly formed Division One. In 1989 they won the Division One title, but they were relegated back again a year later because of their lack of floodlights.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sheffield_FC_v_Inter_Milan.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2000" data-file-width="3008" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Sheffield_FC_v_Inter_Milan.jpg/220px-Sheffield_FC_v_Inter_Milan.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Sheffield_FC_v_Inter_Milan.jpg/330px-Sheffield_FC_v_Inter_Milan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Sheffield_FC_v_Inter_Milan.jpg/440px-Sheffield_FC_v_Inter_Milan.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

Sheffield's 150th anniversary celebration match against Inter at Bramall Lane in 2007</div>

<p>At the first time of asking Sheffield again won the Division One title, this time remaining in the NCEL Premier Division for 15 years. In 1994 the club won the <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_%26_Hallamshire_Senior_Cup" title="Sheffield &amp; Hallamshire Senior Cup">Sheffield &amp; Hallamshire Senior Cup</a> for the first time, beating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Worksop_Town" title="Worksop Town">Worksop Town</a> on penalties at Hillsborough. They would later win the trophy on a further four occasions throughout the 2010s.</p>
<p>2007 was a momentous year for Sheffield F.C. as they entered their 150th year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-celebration_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-celebration-13">[13]</a></sup> They finished as runners-up in the league to secure promotion to the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Premier_League" title="Northern Premier League">Northern Premier League</a> (NPL) for the first time. In October 2007, <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a> president <a href="/wiki/Sepp_Blatter" title="Sepp Blatter">Sepp Blatter</a> attended the club's anniversary dinner, and the following month the club played anniversary celebration matches against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internazionale" title="Internazionale">Internazionale</a> and <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a> at Bramall Lane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-celebration_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-celebration-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Football legend <a href="/wiki/Pel%C3%A9" title="Pelé">Pelé</a> was guest of honour at the first game and was introduced to the teams and the fans before the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The match ended 5–2 to Inter, with 18,741 supporters attending the match. Inter's side included <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cup</a> winner <a href="/wiki/Marco_Materazzi" title="Marco Materazzi">Marco Materazzi</a> and a young <a href="/wiki/Mario_Balotelli" title="Mario Balotelli">Mario Balotelli</a>. As part of his visit, Pelé opened an exhibition which included the first public showing in 40 years of the original hand-written rules of football.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-celebration_13-3"><a href="#cite_note-celebration-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sheffield have reached the play-offs of the NPL Division One South on three occasions, but have so far failed to win promotion. In their first Division One South campaign in 2008 they reached the final, losing on penalties to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nantwich_Town" title="Nantwich Town">Nantwich Town</a>, before being knocked out in the semi-finals in 2010 and 2012. The club first played in the <a href="/wiki/FA_Trophy" title="FA Trophy">FA Trophy</a> in 2007 after winning promotion to the NPL, but have so far failed to advance past the qualifying rounds.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Grounds">Grounds</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheffield_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Grounds">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sheffield have played at a number of grounds around Sheffield. Initially they played at Strawberry Hall Lane Park.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-From_Sheffield_With_Love_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-From_Sheffield_With_Love-7">[7]</a></sup> However, like all of the early grounds they played at, it was not owned by the club. In the following years they would play at Newhall Athletic ground, Old Forge ground and a ground near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hunters_Bar" title="Hunters Bar">Hunters Bar</a> on <a href="/wiki/Ecclesall_Road" title="Ecclesall Road">Ecclesall Road</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_History_of_Sheffield_Football_Club_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_History_of_Sheffield_Football_Club-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>There was much reluctance from the owners of <a href="/wiki/Bramall_Lane" title="Bramall Lane">Bramall Lane</a> to see the pitch used for football. They did not relent until a charity match between Sheffield and Hallam was suggested in late 1862. The ground was used by Sheffield for its more important fixtures but relations with the owners remained strained. They collapsed altogether in 1875 when the club vowed never to play at the ground again.</p>
<p>In 1921, Sheffield settled at the new <a href="/wiki/Abbeydale_Park" title="Abbeydale Park">Abbeydale Park</a> ground. They moved to <a href="/wiki/Hillsborough_Park" title="Hillsborough Park">Hillsborough Park</a> in 1988,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> then to <a href="/wiki/Owlerton_Stadium" title="Owlerton Stadium">Owlerton Stadium</a> and <a href="/wiki/Don_Valley_Stadium" title="Don Valley Stadium">Don Valley Stadium</a>.</p>
<p>The club bought the Coach &amp; Horses ground in <a href="/wiki/Dronfield" title="Dronfield">Dronfield</a> in 2001, which was previously the home of <a href="/wiki/Norton_Woodseats_F.C." title="Norton Woodseats F.C.">Norton Woodseats F.C.</a>, a notable football team who reached the semi-finals of the <a href="/wiki/FA_Amateur_Cup" title="FA Amateur Cup">FA Amateur Cup</a> in 1939. It was the first time the club had owned its own ground.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The ground has a capacity of just over 2,000 with 250 seats in the stand behind the southern goal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
