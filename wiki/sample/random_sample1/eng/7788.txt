<p><b>Xianning</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans">咸宁</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant">咸寧</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Xiánníng</span></i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Prefecture-level_city" title="Prefecture-level city">prefecture-level city</a> in southeastern <a href="/wiki/Hubei" title="Hubei">Hubei</a> province, People's Republic of China, bordering <a href="/wiki/Jiangxi" title="Jiangxi">Jiangxi</a> to the southeast and <a href="/wiki/Hunan" title="Hunan">Hunan</a> to the southwest. It is known as the "City of <a href="/wiki/Osmanthus" title="Osmanthus">Osmanthus</a>".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_climate">Geography and climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xianning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography and climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Xianning is located in southeastern Hubei province, just south of <a href="/wiki/Wuhan" title="Wuhan">Wuhan</a>, between the southern bank of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yangtze_River" title="Yangtze River">Yangtze River</a> in the north and the <a href="/wiki/Mufu_Mountains" title="Mufu Mountains">Mufu Mountains</a> in the south. It borders <a href="/wiki/Jiangxi" title="Jiangxi">Jiangxi</a> to the southeast and <a href="/wiki/Hunan" title="Hunan">Hunan</a> to the southwest. It is called Hubei's southern gateway. Xianning is hilly and mountainous (especially in its southern part), with some flatlands (mostly in the north) and lakes. Its area is 1,501 square kilometres (580 sq mi), 56% of which is forested.</p>
<p>Xianning's climate is <a href="/wiki/Temperate_climate" title="Temperate climate">temperate</a>/continental. Average annual temperature is 17 °C and precipitation is 1,400 millimetres (55 in).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xianning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Xianning was under the jurisdiction of Nanjun County during the <a href="/wiki/Qin_dynasty" title="Qin dynasty">Qin dynasty</a>. At the end of the <a href="/wiki/Han_dynasty" title="Han dynasty">Han dynasty</a>, Xianning became a strategic point where conflict occurred. During the <a href="/wiki/Cultural_Revolution" title="Cultural Revolution">Cultural Revolution</a>, it was an important area for the Hunan, Hubei and Jiangxi Revolutionary Bases.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xianning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Xianning has a population of 2,772,000 people. Most of the inhabitants of Xianning are <a href="/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Han</a>; only 4,785 are from other ethnic groups including <a href="/wiki/Hui_people" title="Hui people">Hui</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tujia_people" title="Tujia people">Tujia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zhuang_people" title="Zhuang people">Zhuang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hmong_people" title="Hmong people">Miao</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchu" title="Manchu">Manchu</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dong_people" title="Dong people">Dong</a>. The place with the largest ethnic minority population is in Chibi City.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xianning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Xianning-fields-9733.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Xianning-fields-9733.jpg/220px-Xianning-fields-9733.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Xianning-fields-9733.jpg/330px-Xianning-fields-9733.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Xianning-fields-9733.jpg/440px-Xianning-fields-9733.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Xianning-fields-9733.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Rice fields south of Xianning center city</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Agriculture and forestry are two of Xianning's biggest industries. It is known by the three names, "City of Osmanthus", "City of Nan Bamboo", and "City of Tea". There are more than 1 million <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mu_(unit_of_area)" title="Mu (unit of area)">mu</a> (~151,822 English acres) of "Nan" Bamboo, 30,000 Chinese acres of osmanthus, and more than 150,000 Chinese acres of tea.</p>
<p>Mineral resources are also an important part of Xianning's economy. <a href="/wiki/Niobium" title="Niobium">Niobium</a>, gold, <a href="/wiki/Magnesium" title="Magnesium">magnesium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antimony" title="Antimony">antimony</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monazite" title="Monazite">monazite</a>, coal, <a href="/wiki/Manganese" title="Manganese">manganese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vanadium" title="Vanadium">vanadium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mica" title="Mica">mica</a>, and marble are all mined or quarried in Xianning.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Xianning_Nuclear_Power_Plant" title="Xianning Nuclear Power Plant">Xianning Nuclear Power Plant</a> is under construction near Dafan Town, Tongshan County. On 17 August 2010, the Shaw Group announced an agreement with State Nuclear Power Engineering Corp. Ltd., a subsidiary of China’s State Nuclear Power Technology Corp. Ltd. (SNPTC), to add two new AP1000 units at the Xianning Nuclear Power Plant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xianning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Xianning has major rail lines, including the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jingguang_Railway" title="Jingguang Railway">Beijing-Guangzhou line</a>, many national highways, and a large stretch of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yangtze_River" title="Yangtze River">Yangtze River</a>, making it an important transportation and shipping center.</p>
<p>As of 2015, four different train stations located in or near Xianning's main urban area have "Xianning" in their name; several other stations are located elsewhere within the prefecture-level city.</p>
<p>There are two passenger train stations on the "conventional" <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jingguang_Railway" title="Jingguang Railway">Beijing-Guangzhou line</a> within the Prefecture-level city of Xianning - the Xianning Railway Station in urban area itself (80 kilometres (50 mi) from Wuhan's <a href="/wiki/Wuchang_District" title="Wuchang District">Wuchang</a> train station), and the one in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chibi,_Hubei" title="Chibi, Hubei">Chibi</a> (118 kilometres (73 mi) from Wuchang) - are usually the first and the second stops for the slower trains leaving Wuchang toward <a href="/wiki/Changsha" title="Changsha">Changsha</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou" title="Guangzhou">Guangzhou</a>. Typical travel times from Wuchang is 40-50 min and 1 hour 20 min, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The new <a href="/wiki/Wuhan%E2%80%93Guangzhou_High-Speed_Railway" title="Wuhan–Guangzhou High-Speed Railway">Wuhan–Guangzhou High-Speed Railway</a> has <a href="/wiki/Xianning_North_Railway_Station" title="Xianning North Railway Station">Xianning North Railway Station</a>, which is located on the northern outskirts of the city's main urban area, about 2 km (1.2 mi) northeast of Xianning Railway Station.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Wuhan%E2%80%93Xianning_Intercity_Railway" title="Wuhan–Xianning Intercity Railway">Wuhan–Xianning Intercity Railway</a> (part of the future <a href="/wiki/Wuhan_Metropolitan_Area_Intercity_Railway" title="Wuhan Metropolitan Area Intercity Railway">Wuhan Metropolitan Area Intercity Railway</a> system), opened in December 2013, provides direct rail service from Wuhan's <a href="/wiki/Wuchang_Railway_Station" title="Wuchang Railway Station">Wuchang Railway Station</a> to Xianning city center. It has two stations in the city's main urban area: <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Xianning_East_Railway_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Xianning East Railway Station (page does not exist)">Xianning East Railway Station</a> in the downtown Xianning, and Xianning South, the end-of-line (dead-end) station in the industrial area south of downtown. When the system was under construction, the expected travel time from Wuchang to Xianning was stated to be 28 min,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> but as of 2015, the typical scheduled time from Wuchang to Xianning South (91 km) is more like 70 to 100 min.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xianning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Flussufer_Xianning.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1712" data-file-width="2560" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Flussufer_Xianning.JPG/220px-Flussufer_Xianning.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Flussufer_Xianning.JPG/330px-Flussufer_Xianning.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Flussufer_Xianning.JPG/440px-Flussufer_Xianning.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Flussufer_Xianning.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A park in Xianning</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Xianning's biggest tourist draw is its natural scenery. There are many sites including Taiyi Cave (<span lang="zh">太乙洞</span>), Star Bamboo Sea (<span lang="zh">星星竹海</span>), Mount Jiugong (<span lang="zh-hans">九宫山</span>, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tongshan_County,_Hubei" title="Tongshan County, Hubei">Tongshan County</a>), Lushui Lake (<span lang="zh-hans">陆水湖</span>), as well as the historical site the Chibi Ancient Ruins (<span lang="zh-hans">赤壁遗址</span>).</p>
<p>At the "<a href="/wiki/Underground_Project_131" title="Underground Project 131">Underground Project 131</a>" site, some 15 kilometres (9.3 mi) southeast of downtown Xianning (within <a href="/wiki/Xian%27an_District" title="Xian'an District">Xian'an District</a>, tourists can visit a system of underground tunnels that were built in 1969-71 to accommodate national military headquarters in the case of a war, but never used.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
