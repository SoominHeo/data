<p><b>Skellefteå</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cities_in_Sweden" title="Cities in Sweden">city</a> and the seat of <a href="/wiki/Skellefte%C3%A5_Municipality" title="Skellefteå Municipality">Skellefteå Municipality</a> in <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4sterbotten_County" title="Västerbotten County">Västerbotten County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, with 32,775 inhabitants in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scb_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-scb-2">[2]</a></sup> The municipality had approximately 72,000 inhabitants at the end of 2013 .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scb_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-scb-2">[2]</a></sup> In the local accent the "å" is omitted and the city is pronounced locally as <i>Skellefte</i>.</p>
<p>The city is a historically industrial city with mining being a large industry, especially for gold – hence the nickname "Gold Town". Politically it is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_Democrats_(Sweden)" title="Social Democrats (Sweden)">Social Democratic</a> stronghold. The city is a well-known <a href="/wiki/Hockeytown" title="Hockeytown">ice hockey town</a>, with its main team <a href="/wiki/Skellefte%C3%A5_AIK" title="Skellefteå AIK">Skellefteå AIK</a> playing in the Swedish top division, the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Hockey_League" title="Swedish Hockey League">SHL</a> and being Swedish champions on multiple occasions.</p>
<p>The city was incorporated in 1845 and grew to its current population size mostly in the 1950s and 1960s, growing only slowly since. It is the second largest city in Västerbotten after <a href="/wiki/Ume%C3%A5" title="Umeå">Umeå</a> and is located roughly halfway between it and <a href="/wiki/Lule%C3%A5" title="Luleå">Luleå</a>. The river <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skellefte_%C3%A4lv" title="Skellefte älv">Skellefte älv</a> passes through the city and it is located around 15 kilometres (9.3 mi) from the <a href="/wiki/Bothnian_Bay" title="Bothnian Bay">Bothnian Bay</a> open sea. Skellefteå is served by <a href="/wiki/Skellefte%C3%A5_Airport" title="Skellefteå Airport">Skellefteå Airport</a>, locally known as <i>Falmark</i>, also around 15 kilometres (9.3 mi) from the city centre to the south.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skellefte%C3%A5&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name Skellefteå is recorded to having been spelled as <i>Skelepht</i> in 1327.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The origin of the name remains unknown.</p>
<p>From the 14th century on, attempts were made to Christianize Skellefteå. However, – for the most part – the entire large Northern Swedish territory of <a href="/wiki/Norrland" title="Norrland">Norrland</a> was not Christianized until several hundred years after the rest of Sweden, and many northern areas such as Skellefteå remained unexplored well past the Middle Ages.</p>
<p>Not before the very end of the 17th century did the indigenous <a href="/wiki/Sami_people" title="Sami people">Sami people</a> of Northern Sweden begin turning into Christianity, much due to the efforts by the Northern Swedish superintendent <a href="/wiki/Mathias_Steuchius" title="Mathias Steuchius">Mathias Steuchius</a>, who worked hard to accomplish this.</p>
<p>Eventually, the reason for the sudden awakened interest towards Skellefteälven and the surrounding areas were the great northern fishing grounds of <a href="/wiki/Salmon" title="Salmon">salmon</a>. The increased demand for fish was sparked by a stricter enforcement of the annual month-long <a href="/wiki/Fasting" title="Fasting">fasting</a> by the Catholic Church, whereby meat was substituted by fish.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The actual city of Skellefteå is one of the youngest cities of Norrland. It was founded in 1845 by the vicar Nils Nordlander.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Today">Today</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skellefte%C3%A5&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Today">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the 20th century, Skellefteå evolved to an industrial and mining city and many wooden houses were demolished to give room for brick buildings.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The largest private employer in Skellefteå is the mining company <a href="/wiki/Boliden_AB" title="Boliden AB">Boliden AB</a>, with about 1'200 employees. The mining is primarily done for copper, but with the ore particles of gold, silver and platinum also follow. However, Skellefteå is still referred to as the <i>"Goldtown"</i>. During the 1990s, the computer industry flourished, causing subsidiaries of <a href="/wiki/Ericsson" title="Ericsson">Ericsson</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tieto_Enator" title="Tieto Enator">Tieto Enator</a> to become important employers.</p>
<p>If things go according to plan, Skellefteå will be the site of the hind legs of the world's largest moose – <a href="/wiki/Stoorn" title="Stoorn">Stoorn</a> –, a tourist attraction planned to be built, spanning from Skellefteå into <a href="/wiki/Arvidsjaur" title="Arvidsjaur">Arvidsjaur</a> in <a href="/wiki/Norrbotten" title="Norrbotten">Norrbotten</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skellefte%C3%A5&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Skellefteå has <a href="/wiki/Subarctic_climate" title="Subarctic climate">subarctic climate</a> that is borderline <a href="/wiki/Humid_continental_climate" title="Humid continental climate">continental</a> with mild summers and cold and snowy winters. The climate is somewhat moderated by the Bothnia Bay, although marine effects are limited, ensuring very mild summer temperatures for a coastal area so far north. The surrounding municipality has an all-time high of 33.9 °C (93.0 °F)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> during the <a href="/wiki/2014_Swedish_heat_wave" title="2014 Swedish heat wave">2014 Swedish heat wave</a>, which is very hot given the latitude and proximity to a major body of water. Although summer highs traditionally averaged just below 20 °C (68 °F) it has been warmer in recent decades.</p>
