<p><b>Sonali Bendre</b> (born 1 January 1975<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>) is an Indian film actress and model who predominantly worked in <a href="/wiki/Bollywood" title="Bollywood">Hindi</a> films. She has also appeared in <a href="/wiki/Telugu_cinema" title="Telugu cinema">Telugu</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Tamil_Nadu" title="Cinema of Tamil Nadu">Tamil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marathi_cinema" title="Marathi cinema">Marathi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kannada_cinema" title="Kannada cinema">Kannada</a> films. She is considered one of the most beautiful actresses of her time and a Top Heroine of 90s.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life_and_education">Personal life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonali_Bendre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sonali was born in <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> She studied in <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">dubai</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kendriya_Vidyalaya_Malleswaram" title="Kendriya Vidyalaya Malleswaram">Kendriya Vidyalaya Malleswaram</a> and <a href="/wiki/Holy_Cross_Convent_High_School,_Thane" title="Holy Cross Convent High School, Thane">Holy Cross Convent High School, Thane</a>. Thereafter she went to Welham Girls School, Dehradun. She married film director <a href="/wiki/Goldie_Behl" title="Goldie Behl">Goldie Behl</a> on 12 November 2002. On 11 August 2005, she gave birth to a son, Ranveer, at the <a href="/wiki/Breach_Candy_Hospital" title="Breach Candy Hospital">Breach Candy Hospital</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>She gave birth to a daughter on 9 November 2007. In an interview she said that her favourite language was <a href="/wiki/Tamil_language" title="Tamil language">Tamil</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonali_Bendre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bendre started out as a model before being selected for the "Star Dust Talent Search". She was invited to Mumbai and received training from a variety of top actors and performers of the Indian film industry. Her first role was in <i><a href="/wiki/Aag_(1994_film)" title="Aag (1994 film)">Aag</a></i> (1994) opposite <a href="/wiki/Govinda_(actor)" title="Govinda (actor)">Govinda</a>.The movie flopped,but she got noticed. And she got two awards for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filmfare_Award_for_Lux_New_Face_of_the_Year" title="Filmfare Award for Lux New Face of the Year">Filmfare Award for Lux New Face of the Year</a> and Star Screen Awards Most Promising Newcomer Though, she initially struggled to be a successful actress, she was eventually given critical acclaim for her performances in <i><a href="/wiki/Diljale" title="Diljale">Diljale</a></i> in 1996, followed by <i><a href="/wiki/Bhai_(1997_film)" title="Bhai (1997 film)">Bhai</a></i> (1997), <i><a href="/wiki/Sarfarosh" title="Sarfarosh">Sarfarosh</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Zakhm" title="Zakhm">Zakhm</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Duplicate_(1998_film)" title="Duplicate (1998 film)">Duplicate</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Kadhalar_Dhinam" title="Kadhalar Dhinam">Kadhalar Dhinam</a></i> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tamil_film" title="Tamil film">Tamil film</a>), <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hum_Saath-Saath_Hain:_We_Stand_United" title="Hum Saath-Saath Hain: We Stand United">Hum Saath-Saath Hain: We Stand United</a></i> (1999), <i><a href="/wiki/Tera_Mera_Saath_Rahen" title="Tera Mera Saath Rahen">Tera Mera Saath Rahen</a></i> (2001) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anahat_(film)" title="Anahat (film)">Anahat</a></i> (2003) among others. She is also known for being one of the few actresses who acted opposite all top four Khans (Salman, Aamir, Shahrukh and Saif). She has also starred opposite Bollywood actors such as <a href="/wiki/Akshay_Kumar" title="Akshay Kumar">Akshay Kumar</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sunil_Shetty" title="Sunil Shetty">Sunil Shetty</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ajay_Devgn" title="Ajay Devgn">Ajay Devgn</a>. She also starred opposite Telugu matinee idol <a href="/wiki/Chiranjeevi" title="Chiranjeevi">Chiranjeevi</a> in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Indra_(2002_film)" title="Indra (2002 film)">Indra</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Shankar_Dada_M.B.B.S." title="Shankar Dada M.B.B.S.">Shankar Dada M.B.B.S.</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In addition to her acting performances, she was also recognized as a graceful dancer through films such as <i>Gaddar</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sapoot" title="Sapoot">Sapoot</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Bombay_(film)" title="Bombay (film)">Bombay</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lajja_(film)" title="Lajja (film)">Lajja</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Major_Saab" title="Major Saab">Major Saab</a></i>. In 2003, she made a special appearance as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shahrukh_Khan" title="Shahrukh Khan">Shahrukh Khan</a>'s doctor named Dr. Priya in <i><a href="/wiki/Kal_Ho_Naa_Ho" title="Kal Ho Naa Ho">Kal Ho Naa Ho</a></i> (produced by <a href="/wiki/Karan_Johar" title="Karan Johar">Karan Johar</a>), which also starred <a href="/wiki/Saif_Ali_Khan" title="Saif Ali Khan">Saif Ali Khan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Preity_Zinta" title="Preity Zinta">Preity Zinta</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Bendre also starred in the theatre in a play called, <i>Aap Ki Soniya</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> She had also welcomed <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a> on his arrival In India in traditional Maharashtrian nauvari sari and she performed aarti for Jackson on November 1, 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_career">Television career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonali_Bendre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Television career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>She also hosted a dance show on television named <i>Kya Masti Kya Dhoom....!</i> and was one of the judges of <i><a href="/wiki/Indian_Idol" title="Indian Idol">Indian Idol</a></i> on Sony Entertainment television. She also hosted the 50th Filmfare Awards with <a href="/wiki/Saif_Ali_Khan" title="Saif Ali Khan">Saif Ali Khan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Farida_Jalal" title="Farida Jalal">Farida Jalal</a> on 26 February 2005. She was a judge on <i>Mr &amp; Mrs Television</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Indian_Idol" title="Indian Idol">Indian Idol</a> 4</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/India%27s_Got_Talent" title="India's Got Talent">India's Got Talent</a></i>. In 2012, she also appeared as a judge on reality dance series, <i><a href="/wiki/Hindustan_Ke_Hunarbaaz" title="Hindustan Ke Hunarbaaz">Hindustan Ke Hunarbaaz</a></i>, on <a href="/wiki/Life_OK" title="Life OK">Life OK</a>, along with choreographer <a href="/wiki/Terence_Lewis_(choreographer)" title="Terence Lewis (choreographer)">Terence Lewis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>She also appeared as a judge in <a href="/wiki/India%27s_Best_Dramebaaz" title="India's Best Dramebaaz">India's Best Dramebaaz</a> on <a href="/wiki/Zee_TV" title="Zee TV">Zee TV</a>. She is next appearing as narrator in the TV Show <i><a href="/wiki/Mission_Sapne" title="Mission Sapne">Mission Sapne</a></i> to be aired on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colors_(TV_channel)" title="Colors (TV channel)">Colors</a> in March–April 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-missionsapne_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-missionsapne-12">[12]</a></sup> Sonali has been finalized to play the lead role in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Star_Life_OK" title="Star Life OK">Star Life OK</a>'s television serial <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ajeeb_Dastaan_Hai_Yeh" title="Ajeeb Dastaan Hai Yeh">Ajeeb Dastaan Hai Yeh</a></i>. The serial started airing in October 2014. She plays the main character called Shoba Sachdev.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bollywood_comeback">Bollywood comeback</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonali_Bendre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bollywood comeback">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the end of June 2012, Sonali Bendre was rumoured to quit judging <i><a href="/wiki/India%27s_Got_Talent" title="India's Got Talent">India's Got Talent</a></i> 4. She was said to be likely replaced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ritesh_Deshmukh" title="Ritesh Deshmukh">Ritesh Deshmukh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> However, later, it turned out she opted out of the show in order to give her dates for her comeback vehicle, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Once_Upon_a_Time_in_Mumbai_Dobaara!" title="Once Upon a Time in Mumbai Dobaara!">Once Upon a Time in Mumbai Dobaara!</a></i>, for some period, the channel hasn't spoken to Bendre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indiatimes1_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-indiatimes1-16">[16]</a></sup> Later, at the beginning of July, Sonali Bendre announced<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indiatimes1_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-indiatimes1-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> she will be making her comeback by portraying a character connected to the one played by <a href="/wiki/Akshay_Kumar" title="Akshay Kumar">Akshay Kumar</a> in the movie. Her part was described by the director <a href="/wiki/Milan_Luthria" title="Milan Luthria">Milan Luthria</a>: "Sonali's character is called Mumtaz. The name spells enigma and is very crucial to the plot of the film. The character doesn't fit into the usual mould of a mother or a sister or bhabhi but has an identity of its own. That is what makes it special. And that's why Sonali agreed to be a part of the film."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indiatimes1_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-indiatimes1-16">[16]</a></sup> She took a sabbatical from movies after 2004, then after giving birth to her son she started doing television. She refused to star in many movies, saying she wants to focus on her family, especially her son. But after she was offered <i>Once Upon a Time in Mumbaai Dobara</i>, she said yes because of her friendship with Ekta and Shobha Kapoor and the fact she worked with both Luthria (<i><a href="/wiki/Chori_Chori_(2003_film)" title="Chori Chori (2003 film)">Chori Chori</a></i>) and Kumar (<i><a href="/wiki/Tarazu" title="Tarazu">Tarazu</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sapoot" title="Sapoot">Sapoot</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Angaaray_(1998_film)" title="Angaaray (1998 film)">Angaaray</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keemat_(film)" title="Keemat (film)">Keemat</a></i>) before.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
