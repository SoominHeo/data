<p><b>Sony Pictures Classics</b> (abbreviated as <b>SPC</b>) is a film production and distribution studio division of <a href="/wiki/Sony_Pictures" title="Sony Pictures">Sony Pictures</a> founded in 1992 by former <a href="/wiki/Orion_Classics" title="Orion Classics">Orion Classics</a> heads Michael Barker, Tom Bernard, and Marcie Bloom (similar to <a href="/wiki/Fox_Searchlight_Pictures" title="Fox Searchlight Pictures">Fox Searchlight Pictures</a> and <a href="/wiki/Focus_Features" title="Focus Features">Focus Features</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SPC_at_15_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-SPC_at_15-2">[2]</a></sup> It distributes, produces and acquires specialty films such as <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">documentaries</a>, <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">independent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Art_film" title="Art film">art films</a> in the United States and internationally. As of 2015, Barker and Bernard are co-presidents of the division.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sony_Pictures_Classics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sony Pictures Classics was founded on January 1, 1992, by Michael Barker, Tom Bernard, and Marcie Bloom, set up as an autonomous division of <a href="/wiki/Sony_Pictures" title="Sony Pictures">Sony Pictures</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SPC_at_15_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-SPC_at_15-2">[2]</a></sup> The model of the company is to produce, acquire and/or distribute independent films from the United States and internationally.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sony Pictures Classics has a history of making reasonable investments for small films, and getting a decent return.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SPC_at_15_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-SPC_at_15-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It has a history of not overspending.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SPC_at_15_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-SPC_at_15-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Its largest commercial success of the 2010s is <a href="/wiki/Woody_Allen" title="Woody Allen">Woody Allen</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Midnight_in_Paris" title="Midnight in Paris">Midnight in Paris</a></i> (2011), which grossed over $56 million in the U.S., becoming Allen's highest-grossing film ever in the United States.</p>
<p>Occasionally, Sony Pictures Classics agrees to release films for all other film studio divisions of Sony; However, under Sony Pictures Classics' structure within Sony, all other divisions of Sony (including the parent company) cannot force Sony Pictures Classics to release any film that the division does not want to release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SPC_at_15_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-SPC_at_15-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
