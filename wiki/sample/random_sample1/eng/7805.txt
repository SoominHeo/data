<p><i><b>Project Sonic 2017</b></i> is an upcoming <a href="/wiki/Platform_game" title="Platform game">platformer</a> video game being developed by <a href="/wiki/Sonic_Team" title="Sonic Team">Sonic Team</a> and published by <a href="/wiki/Sega" title="Sega">Sega</a>. It is scheduled for a late 2017 release for <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Microsoft Windows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_Switch" title="Nintendo Switch">Nintendo Switch</a>, <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_4" title="PlayStation 4">PlayStation 4</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Xbox_One" title="Xbox One">Xbox One</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Premise">Premise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Project_Sonic_2017&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Premise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Like <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Generations" title="Sonic Generations">Sonic Generations</a></i>, the game will contain two variants of <a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(character)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (character)">Sonic the Hedgehog</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Classic_Sonic" title="Classic Sonic">Classic Sonic</a>, the design featured more prominently in <a href="/wiki/Sega" title="Sega">Sega</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(1991_video_game)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (1991 video game)"><i>Sonic</i> games for the Sega Genesis</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Modern_Sonic" title="Modern Sonic">Modern Sonic</a>, his revised form in the years after that was first introduced in <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Adventure" title="Sonic Adventure">Sonic Adventure</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Polygon1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Polygon1-1">[1]</a></sup> The trailer has the two teaming up to take on large robots that are destroying a city in a <a href="/wiki/Apocalyptic_and_post-apocalyptic_fiction" title="Apocalyptic and post-apocalyptic fiction">post-apocalyptic</a> setting, with the tagline "Join the resistance".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EGM1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-EGM1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Project_Sonic_2017&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The game was announced at <a href="/wiki/Sega" title="Sega">Sega</a>'s 25th anniversary of the series at <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Comic-Con" title="San Diego Comic-Con">San Diego Comic-Con</a> on July 22, 2016, where it was announced alongside <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Mania" title="Sonic Mania">Sonic Mania</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Polygon1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Polygon1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The game will be developed by <a href="/wiki/Sonic_Team" title="Sonic Team">Sonic Team</a>, the same team that had previously developed <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Colors" title="Sonic Colors">Sonic Colors</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Generations" title="Sonic Generations">Sonic Generations</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and will be headed by <i>Sonic</i> series veteran <a href="/wiki/Takashi_Iizuka_(game_designer)" title="Takashi Iizuka (game designer)">Takashi Iizuka</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The presence of both Classic and Modern Sonic lead some journalists to believe it was a <i>Sonic Generations 2</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> but Iizuka confirmed that it was not a sequel, but a new, stand-alone title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The game will be released for <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Microsoft Windows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_Switch" title="Nintendo Switch">Nintendo Switch</a>, <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_4" title="PlayStation 4">PlayStation 4</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Xbox_One" title="Xbox One">Xbox One</a> in late 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EGM1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-EGM1-2">[2]</a></sup> Along with development, a new character and gameplay component was announced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Project_Sonic_2017&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/International_Business_Times" title="International Business Times">International Business Times</a></i> praised Sega's approach of announcing and releasing the duo of <i>Project Sonic</i> and <i>Sonic Mania</i> in the same year, stating that catering to the new fans with <i>Project</i> while catering to the old-school fans with <i>Mania</i> could help repair the series's poor reputation with more recent releases, and in turn lead to a "<i>Sonic</i> renaissance".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
