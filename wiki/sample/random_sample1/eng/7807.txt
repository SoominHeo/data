<p><i><b>Sonic Advance</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ソニックアドバンス</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sonikku Adobansu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a 2001 <a href="/wiki/Platform_game" title="Platform game">platform game</a> in the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(series)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (series)">Sonic the Hedgehog</a></i> series, developed by <a href="/wiki/Dimps" title="Dimps">Dimps</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sonic_Team" title="Sonic Team">Sonic Team</a> and published in Japan by <a href="/wiki/Sega" title="Sega">Sega</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Game_Boy_Advance" title="Game Boy Advance">Game Boy Advance</a>. It was later published by <a href="/wiki/THQ" title="THQ">THQ</a> in North America and by <a href="/wiki/Atari" title="Atari">Infogrames</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PAL_Region" title="PAL Region">PAL regions</a>. It was released in Japan on December 20, 2001, in North America on February 3, 2002 and in PAL regions on March 8, 2002. There were also releases on three Twin Pack cartridges bundled with <a href="/wiki/Sonic_Pinball_Party" title="Sonic Pinball Party">Sonic Pinball Party</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sonic_Battle" title="Sonic Battle">Sonic Battle</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/ChuChu_Rocket!" title="ChuChu Rocket!">ChuChu Rocket!</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> respectively in 2005. It is one of the first <i>Sonic</i> games to be released on a Nintendo platform.</p>
<p><i>Sonic Advance</i> was ported to the <a href="/wiki/Nokia" title="Nokia">Nokia</a> <a href="/wiki/N-Gage_(device)" title="N-Gage (device)">N-Gage</a> on October 7, 2003 under the title <i>SonicN</i>. A version for <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">Android</a> devices was released in Japan on November 25, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The game was later released on the <a href="/wiki/Wii_U" title="Wii U">Wii U</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Virtual_Console" title="Virtual Console">Virtual Console</a> in Japan in February 2015.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonic_Advance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:SonicAdvanceLoop.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="195" data-file-width="300" height="143" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/da/SonicAdvanceLoop.JPG/220px-SonicAdvanceLoop.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/d/da/SonicAdvanceLoop.JPG 1.5x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:SonicAdvanceLoop.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Sonic runs through a loop in the fifth zone, "Angel Island Zone". <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HUD_(computer_gaming)" title="HUD (computer gaming)">HUD</a> features clockwise from bottom left: lives, ring counter, score, and time elapsed.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The game follows Sonic and his friends as they travel through various zones in order to stop <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dr._Ivo_Robotnik" title="Dr. Ivo Robotnik">Dr. Eggman</a> from taking over South Island. The game features four playable characters; <a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(character)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (character)">Sonic</a>, Tails, <a href="/wiki/Knuckles_the_Echidna" title="Knuckles the Echidna">Knuckles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amy_Rose" title="Amy Rose">Amy</a>. Each character has the same moveset as they did in <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_3" title="Sonic the Hedgehog 3">Sonic the Hedgehog 3</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_%26_Knuckles" title="Sonic &amp; Knuckles">Sonic &amp; Knuckles</a></i>, with the addition of a melee attack. Each character has a special ability, such as Sonic's splice shield, Tails' flight and Knuckles' gliding and climbing abilities. In her playable 2D debut, Amy cannot perform the Spin Dash or get into ball form when jumping like the others, and must rely on her Piko Piko Hammer to defend herself, making her unique amongst the characters. Grind rails first introduced in <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Adventure_2" title="Sonic Adventure 2">Sonic Adventure 2</a></i> also make their first 2D appearance. Sonic and Tails can also be used at the same time during the story by inputting a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cheat_code" title="Cheat code">cheat code</a> at the character selection screen. When playing as both characters, the gameplay is similar to that of <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_2" title="Sonic the Hedgehog 2">Sonic the Hedgehog 2</a></i>: The player can only control Sonic and a CPU-controlled Tails follows him.</p>
<p>There are 6 normal zones, each of which contains two acts. In each zone, Act 1 is cleared by reaching the goal and Act 2 is cleared by destroying the boss robot at the end and opening a capsule which subsequently falls and releasing animals trapped inside it.</p>
<p>If the character is damaged, they will drop all their rings, however, if the character has no rings and takes damage, they will lose a life. A character will also lose a life if they spend too long underwater without replenishing air supply (air underwater lasts for 18 seconds), fall into a gap, or if the time exceeds 9 minutes and 59 seconds. Losing a life will require a character to start from the last checkpoint passed, or from the beginning of the act if they have not passed any checkpoints. If a character loses a live when the number of lives remaining is zero, the game will end.</p>
<p>Special Springs can be found near the top of certain acts. Each normal zone contains one Special Spring, except for Ice Mountain Zone which contains two: one in each act. By jumping onto these springs, the player can reach a Special Stage—each spring goes to a certain special stage every time it is jumped on. The stages see players fall down a tube on snowboards while trying to earn enough rings to complete the target amount. By winning the stage, the player receives a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chaos_Emerald" title="Chaos Emerald">Chaos Emerald</a>, although there is only one emerald per stage, so the same stage cannot be repeated for multiple emeralds. Unlike most other classic 2D <i>Sonic</i> games, the Emeralds are "shared" between all the characters. Once the player has collected all the Emeralds and completed the X-Zone with all four characters, they can access the Moon Zone by completing the X-Zone again with Sonic.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tiny_Chao_Garden">Tiny Chao Garden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonic_Advance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tiny Chao Garden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Sonic Advance</i>, like <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Advance_2" title="Sonic Advance 2">Sonic Advance 2</a></i> and <i>Sonic Pinball Party</i>, features an extra game called the Tiny Chao Garden, in which players can raise <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chao_(Sonic_the_Hedgehog)" title="Chao (Sonic the Hedgehog)">Chao</a>. Players can transfer their Chao between the Tiny Chao Garden and the Chao Garden from the <a href="/wiki/GameCube" title="GameCube">GameCube</a> versions of <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Adventure" title="Sonic Adventure">Sonic Adventure</a></i> and <i>Sonic Adventure 2</i>. Tiny Chao Garden is more limited than the Chao Garden: Chao don't age, can only interact with fruit and three toys (which must be bought in the Tiny Chao Garden itself), and only one Chao can be in it at once. An egg can be stored in the garden too, and hatches as soon as there is no Chao in it. Chao can only leave the garden by being transferred to the Chao Garden or by running away when players don't take care of them. Fruit, toys and eggs must be bought with the rings collected in the normal game or two minigames. Rings, fruit and eggs can be transferred to the Chao Garden, but not from it.</p>

<p>It was generally well received by critics, and by July 2002, had shipped over one million copies worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2009, <i><a href="/wiki/Official_Nintendo_Magazine" title="Official Nintendo Magazine">Official Nintendo Magazine</a></i> ranked <i>Sonic Advance</i> as the 75th greatest game ever released on a Nintendo console.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonic_Advance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Sonic Advance</i> is notable as it was the debut both of Sonic on the Game Boy Advance, and of an original <i>Sonic</i> game on a <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">Nintendo</a> system, the company with which Sega had a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Console_war" title="Console war">notorious rivalry</a> that lasted over a decade, until Sega ceased manufacturing consoles in the early 2000s. The game's strong sales also helped cement Sonic's place on the Game Boy Advance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-IGN-14">[14]</a></sup> Four subsequent original titles were released on the platform – <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Advance_2" title="Sonic Advance 2">Sonic Advance 2</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Advance_3" title="Sonic Advance 3">Sonic Advance 3</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Battle" title="Sonic Battle">Sonic Battle</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Pinball_Party" title="Sonic Pinball Party">Sonic Pinball Party</a></i>.</p>
<p>A port of <i>Sonic Advance</i> titled <i>SonicN</i> was released as a launch title for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nokia_N-Gage" title="Nokia N-Gage">Nokia N-Gage</a> in North America on October 7, 2003. Gameplay is identical to the Game Boy Advance version, except for the exclusion of the Tiny Chao Garden. The game runs slightly slower on the N-Gage. The most apparent difference between the two versions is the screen resolution: The N-Gage's screen is portrait unlike that of the Game Boy Advance, so the player is offered a choice of two modes: a full resolution mode with a narrower field of view, or a letterboxed 3:2 mode with scaled-down graphics.</p>
<p>A port for Android devices was released in Japan on November 25, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Another port was also released for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J2ME" title="J2ME">J2ME</a> (Java 2 Platform, Micro Edition).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The game was released on the Wii U Virtual Console in Japan on February 18, 2015.</p>
