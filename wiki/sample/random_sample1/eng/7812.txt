<p><b>Hideaki Sorachi</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">空知 英秋</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sorachi Hideaki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born May 25, 1979)</span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manga_artist" title="Manga artist">manga artist</a> best known for his work <i><a href="/wiki/Gin_Tama" title="Gin Tama">Gin Tama</a></i> which has been written since December 8, 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The <i>Gin Tama</i> manga has sold 50 million units in Japan as of May 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sorachi became interested in manga during his childhood. However, during the 4th grade he showed his manga work to his father who immediately laughed at him, after which he abandoned his dream of being a manga artist. After graduating from college, he could not find a job and began drawing manga again to make money. He was able to live from his first work <i>Dandelion</i>, which was featured in the first volume of <i>Gintama</i>, along with a prologue from Sorachi calling it his first official work. When starting serialization the manga was unpopular and was close to being cancelled. Although Sorachi was pleased with the first <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> selling all of its copies, he later learned Shueisha was afraid of poor sales which resulted in the minimum printed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-secondguidebook_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-secondguidebook-4">[4]</a></sup> In order to increase its popularity, the author introduce new characters, the Shinsengumi, who felt memorable to his assistants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-quickjapan_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-quickjapan-5">[5]</a></sup> Sorachi had little hope on the manga's popularity, as he noted that people used to tell him the manga would not surpass the number of two <i>tankōbon</i> volumes. However, once the third volume was released, Sorachi found that he did not have "any fresh material to use."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> During the first year of the series, Sorachi believed that the source of the popularity of <i>Gin Tama</i> was partially connected to the Shinsengumi drama. While the drama ran during the first year of the series, when the manga was mostly shorter stories that established the characters and the world, he felt uncomfortable of making things related to the drama. By the second year and beyond, he became more daring in his stories and concepts, creating longer storylines that included more drama while keeping his sense of humor and satirization of modern Japan by way of his fictionalized past.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vol6pg26_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-vol6pg26-7">[7]</a></sup> Although Sorachi has already planned the series' ending, he is not sure when the manga is going to reach that point due to the characters requiring development to behave the way he wants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-secondguidebook_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-secondguidebook-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kenta_Shinohara" title="Kenta Shinohara">Kenta Shinohara</a> (author of <i><a href="/wiki/Sket_Dance" title="Sket Dance">Sket Dance</a></i>) used to be his assistant,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tan_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tan-8">[8]</a></sup> as well as <a href="/wiki/Y%C5%8Dichi_Amano" title="Yōichi Amano">Yōichi Amano</a> (author of <i><a href="/wiki/Akaboshi:_Ibun_Suikoden" title="Akaboshi: Ibun Suikoden">Akaboshi: Ibun Suikoden</a></i>). <i>Gintama</i> and <i>Sket Dance</i> had a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_crossover" title="Fictional crossover">crossover</a> chapter in <i><a href="/wiki/Weekly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Jump" title="Weekly Shōnen Jump">Weekly Shōnen Jump</a></i> which was the 349th for <i>Gintama</i> and the 180th for <i>Sket Dance</i>.</p>
<p>Sorachi has appeared both in the manga and anime of <i>Gintama</i>, several times as a monkey/ape.</p>
