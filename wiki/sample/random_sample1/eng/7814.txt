<p>The <b>State Emblem of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Государственный герб Советского Союза</span>, <i>Gosudarstvenny gerb Sovetskogo Soyuza</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>) was adopted in 1923 and was used until the <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Dissolution of the Soviet Union">dissolution of the Soviet Union</a> in 1991. Although it technically is an emblem rather than a coat of arms, since it does not follow traditional <a href="/wiki/Heraldry" title="Heraldry">heraldic</a> rules, in Russian it is called <i>герб</i> (<i>gerb</i>), the word used for a traditional coat of arms.</p>
<p>It was the first state insignia created in the style known as <a href="/wiki/Socialist_heraldry" title="Socialist heraldry">socialist heraldry</a>, a style also seen in e.g. the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emblem_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Emblem of the People's Republic of China">emblem of the People's Republic of China</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=State_Emblem_of_the_Soviet_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_version_.281923.E2.80.931936.29">First version (1923–1936)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=State_Emblem_of_the_Soviet_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: First version (1923–1936)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The project of the first version of the state emblem was accepted on 6 July 1923 by the 2nd session of the <a href="/wiki/Central_Executive_Committee_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Central Executive Committee of the Soviet Union">Central Executive Committee</a> (CIK) and the version was completed on September 22 of that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NiZh_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NiZh-2">[2]</a></sup> This design was fixed in the <a href="/wiki/1924_Soviet_Constitution" title="1924 Soviet Constitution">1924 Soviet Constitution</a>: "The State Emblem of the USSR is composed of a sickle and a hammer on a globe depicted in the rays of the sun and framed by ears of wheat, with the inscription <a href="/wiki/Workers_of_the_world,_unite!" title="Workers of the world, unite!">"proletariats of the world, unite!"</a> in six languages - Russian, <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_language" title="Belarusian language">Belarusian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgian_language" title="Georgian language">Georgian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armenian_language" title="Armenian language">Armenian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijani_language" title="Azerbaijani language">Azerbaijani</a>. At the top of the Emblem is a five-pointed star."</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ivan_Dubasov" title="Ivan Dubasov">Ivan Dubasov</a> was an important contributor when creating the emblem.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_version_.281936.E2.80.931946.29">Second version (1936–1946)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=State_Emblem_of_the_Soviet_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Second version (1936–1946)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/1936_Soviet_Constitution" title="1936 Soviet Constitution">1936 Soviet Constitution</a>, the USSR consisted of eleven republics. Hence the major new version's difference from the previous one was eleven ribbons bearing USSR State Motto inscriptions in eleven languages.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Third_version_.281946.E2.80.931956.29">Third version (1946–1956)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=State_Emblem_of_the_Soviet_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Third version (1946–1956)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The number of republics in the USSR became 16 after September 1939, before the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Patriotic_War" title="Great Patriotic War">Great Patriotic War</a> began in June 1941, but the state emblem was changed to reflect this only after the war. By a Decision of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Presidium_of_the_Supreme_Soviet_of_the_USSR" title="Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the USSR">Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the USSR</a> on June 26, 1946, all 16 constituent republics were represented on the emblem. The USSR State Motto was inscribed on 16 ribbons in 16 languages. The (<a href="/wiki/Estonian_language" title="Estonian language">Estonian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latvian_language" title="Latvian language">Latvian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lithuanian_language" title="Lithuanian language">Lithuanian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moldovan_language" title="Moldovan language">Moldavian</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Finnish_language" title="Finnish language">Finnish</a> were added to the previous version). Inscriptions in Azerbaijani, <a href="/wiki/Turkmen_language" title="Turkmen language">Turkmen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uzbek_language" title="Uzbek language">Uzbek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tajik_language" title="Tajik language">Tajik</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kazakh_language" title="Kazakh language">Kazakh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kyrgyz_language" title="Kyrgyz language">Kyrgyz</a> languages were updated due to the transfer from <a href="/wiki/Latin_alphabet" title="Latin alphabet">Latin</a> to <a href="/wiki/Cyrillic_script" title="Cyrillic script">Cyrillic</a> script of the respective languages within the USSR.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NiZh_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NiZh-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fourth_version_.281956.E2.80.931991.29">Fourth version (1956–1991)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=State_Emblem_of_the_Soviet_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Fourth version (1956–1991)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In 1956, the <a href="/wiki/Karelo-Finnish_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Karelo-Finnish Soviet Socialist Republic">Karelo-Finnish SSR</a> was transformed into the <a href="/wiki/Karelian_Autonomous_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Karelian Autonomous Soviet Socialist Republic">Karelo-Finnish ASSR</a>, and soon this was reflected on the USSR state emblem.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NiZh_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-NiZh-2">[2]</a></sup> By a decision of the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the USSR on September 12, 1956, the ribbon bearing the USSR State Motto in Finnish was removed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Heraldicum_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Heraldicum-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>A minor change in the Belarusian inscription was a decision of the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the USSR on April 1, 1958.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Heraldicum_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Heraldicum-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=State_Emblem_of_the_Soviet_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The state emblem shows the traditional Soviet emblems of the Hammer and Sickle and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Star" title="Red Star">Red Star</a> over a globe, and two wreaths of covered by the ("Workers of the world, unite!") in the <a href="/wiki/Official_language" title="Official language">official languages</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Republics_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Republics of the Soviet Union">Soviet Republics</a>, in the reverse order they were mentioned in the Soviet Constitution.</p>
<p>Each Soviet Republic (SSR) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autonomous_republics_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Autonomous republics of the Soviet Union">Autonomous Soviet Republic</a> (ASSR) had its own coat of arms, largely inspired by the state emblem of the Union.</p>
