<p><b>Sorocaba</b> (<small>Portuguese pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">[so̞ɾo̞ˈkabɐ]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Brazil" title="Municipalities of Brazil">municipality</a> in the <a href="/wiki/States_of_Brazil" title="States of Brazil">state</a> of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_(state)" title="São Paulo (state)">São Paulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. Sorocaba is the eighth-largest city in the state of São Paulo. Outside the <a href="/wiki/Greater_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="Greater São Paulo">Greater São Paulo</a> region, it ranks behind only <a href="/wiki/Campinas" title="Campinas">Campinas</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Jos%C3%A9_dos_Campos" title="São José dos Campos">São José dos Campos</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ribeir%C3%A3o_Preto" title="Ribeirão Preto">Ribeirão Preto</a>. It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Region_of_Sorocaba" title="Metropolitan Region of Sorocaba">Metropolitan Region of Sorocaba</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The population is 644,919 (2015 est.) in an area of 450.38 km<sup>2</sup>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibge_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ibge-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Over the past twelve years the <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> has been undergoing extensive <a href="/wiki/Urbanization" title="Urbanization">urbanization</a> projects,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> improving streets and avenues, as well as infrastructure for the traffic which the city receives every day.</p>
<p>It is the eighth and fourth municipality in the consumer market in the state outside the Greater São Paulo metropolitan area, with a potential annual per capita consumption estimated at $2,400 for the urban population and $917 for rural areas (7200 people) and twenty-ninth-largest city in Brazil with potential for consumption. Still, it is the fourth-largest city of the state to receive new investments and one of the largest in the country, figuring in the list of 30 cities that create more jobs in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sorocaba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Dom Francisco de Sousa, general governor of <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> (1591 to 1602), believing in the existence of gold in the region, settled the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pelourinho" title="Pelourinho">Pelourinho</a>—a Portuguese translation of the English "pillory", symbol of the Royal power, as the village Nova Vila de Nossa Senhora da Ponte de Mont Serrat. When gold wasn't found there, the governor returned to the Royal Court. Twelve years later, Dom Francisco de Sousa changed the name of the village to Itavuvu.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Baltasar_Fernandes" title="Baltasar Fernandes">Baltasar Fernandes</a>, a member of an expeditionary group called Bandeirantes, laid the foundation of Sorocaba in the 1654. The chapel of Nossa Senhora da Ponte (which is now the Cathedral located in Fernando Prestes square, downtown) was built by him, along with the São Bento de Parnaíba monastery (now São Bento monastery) years later. It was also Fernandes who brought the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benedictine" title="Benedictine">Benedictine</a> monks to teach, assist the poor and the ill, and convert the Native Americans of the region. The monastery was donated to the Benedictines in the year 1660, after which Friar Anselmo da Anunciação and Friar Mauro were chosen to take office. The first streets and houses started to spread around the neighborhood.</p>
<p>In the year of 1661, Baltasar Fernandes went to São Paulo to request that Sorocaba be named a village from the governor, Correia de Sá e Benevides. Thus, on March 3, 1661, Sorocaba became known as Vila de Nossa Senhora da Ponte de Sorocaba. The organization of the Municipal Council followed shortly, with the main nominees being: Baltasar Fernandes and André de Zunega (judges), Cláudio Furquim and Pascoal Leite Pais (city councillors), Domingos Garcia (procurator) and Francisco Sanches (clerk).</p>
<p>With the arrival of colonel Cristóvão Pereira de Abreu and his troops in 1773, begins the main chapter of the history of Sorocaba: the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tropeirismo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tropeirismo (page does not exist)">Tropeirismo</a>.</p>
<p>The transportation of goods on the back of mules, that traversed the North-South route of the country came across Sorocaba, which was strategically placed in the main route between <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_do_Sul" title="Rio Grande do Sul">Rio Grande do Sul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minas_Gerais" title="Minas Gerais">Minas Gerais</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro_(state)" title="Rio de Janeiro (state)">Rio de Janeiro</a>. Soon Sorocaba had its own Feira de Muares (mule/horse fair), where troops from all states came to feed and rest their cavalry on the way to the mineral and forest expeditions, and buy and sell goods, horses and enslaved Africans. Given the growing number of people working in the city, the commerce and the first industries began to appear. Goods bought in Sorocaba were known from across the Country, spread by the merchant troops. The main events of Tropeirismo comprehended a hundred years of the Sorocaba history, from 1770 to 1870.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>, English textile industries ran out of cotton, which was imported from the Southern United States. Soon, manufactures from <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> started to search around the world for alternative places to cultivate cotton - one of them was the then-Province of São Paulo, which included Sorocaba. In 1862, Lieutenant-Colonel Francisco Gonçalves de Oliveira Machado started the first cotton plantation in Sorocaba. The local environment and weather were ideal, and the plantation flourished. The first harvest exceeded all expectations, starting another cycle of industrial and economic development. Several textile industries from England built branches in the city, changing the landscape with chimneys, saw-styled roofs, large, orange brick-built buildings and smoke. During this time, Sorocaba received the title of Manchester Paulista--"São Paulo native" in Portuguese, given the resemblance with its laboring <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">twin</a> city.</p>
<p>With the opening of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sorocabana&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sorocabana (page does not exist)">Sorocabana</a> railway on June 20, 1872 and its transport of cotton products, animals and passengers to São Paulo, Sorocaba had a major leap in development. Six locomotives and 62 bandwagons were brought from <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, with seven stations initially planned.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telegraph" title="Telegraph">Telegraphic services</a> started on April 3, 1873. Years later, the railway expanded to the borders of the state, with Sorocaba station as the hub.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sorocaba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sorocaba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The city is located in the southeastern region of the state of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_(state)" title="São Paulo (state)">São Paulo</a>, 92 km away from the state capital. The main highways are Castelo Branco (SP-280) and Raposo Tavares (SP-270). It straddles the <a href="/wiki/Sorocaba_River" title="Sorocaba River">Sorocaba River</a>, a tributary of the left bank of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tiete_river" title="Tiete river">Tiete river</a>. The municipality of Sorocaba is located on the <a href="/wiki/Tropic_of_Capricorn" title="Tropic of Capricorn">Tropic of Capricorn</a>, at latitude 23° 26 '16 " for the season of 2011, passes along the neighborhoods of Aparecidinha and Parque São Bento districts. At the junction of Highway José Ermírio José de Morais (SP-75) with the connection to the Raposo Tavares highway is a milestone signaling the Tropic. Among Brazil's largest cities (by population), Sorocaba is in 30th place. Sorocaba is among the major cities in São Paulo (population), is ranked ninth.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Demography">Demography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sorocaba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Demography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According IBGE, 2010 Census, the population of Sorocaba 586,311 inhabitants. There was growth of 18.81% compared to 2000. The population density is 1,305.46 inhabitants per square kilometer. Women are the majority in Sorocaba, with 51.1% of the total. In absolute numbers, are 299,513 women and 286,798 men. indeed, Sorocaba has 12,715 women more than men. There are large predominance of urban population in Sorocaba, with only 1% of the population (5971 residents) living in rural areas, compared with 580,340 in urban areas. Among the largest cities in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> (in population), Sorocaba is in 30th place. Already among the largest cities in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_(state)" title="São Paulo (state)">São Paulo</a> (in population), is ranked ninth.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sorocaba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sorocaba climate is subtropical, temperate, warm the coldest month being July and warmest in February. Precipitation is around 1,300 mm (51.2 inches). According to <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen</a>, Sorocaba can be classified as a <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a> "Cwa" featuring a warm climate with summer rainfall and temperature in the warmest month ≥ 22 °C (72 °F).</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Land_relief">Land relief</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sorocaba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Land relief">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The terrain is classified as a wavy strands and characterized by local ridges, with an average altitude of 632 meters above the sea level. The highest altitude is 1028 meters, in the Serra de Sao Francisco. The lower altitude is 539 meters, in the valley of the <a href="/wiki/Sorocaba_River" title="Sorocaba River">Sorocaba River</a>. Sorocaba is located on the edge of the Peripheral Depression of São Paulo state, in the Appalachian Fall Line, as defined by Professor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aziz_Ab%27Saber" title="Aziz Ab'Saber">Aziz Ab'Saber</a>. This configuration is due to the fact Sorocaba lies at the boundary between the Atlantic Plateau, which covers the area of crystalline rocks, with higher relief and the rocks of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Parana%27_Basin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Parana' Basin (page does not exist)">Parana Sedimentary Basin</a> and gently rolling topography with lower altitudes. The <a href="/wiki/Sorocaba_River" title="Sorocaba River">Sorocaba River</a> and its hydrographic basin are responsible for the relief dissection.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soil">Soil</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sorocaba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Soil">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The soil is characterized as red-yellow podzolic type, with gravelly clay, heavy clay, Typic dark clayey; oxisol with clay loam. Lithosols also occur, which are usually developed with little depressions.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vegetation">Vegetation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sorocaba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Vegetation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The original natural vegetation was the Atlantic Forest, with local regions in a Montana Tropical Rainforest. Predominate the savannah vegetation in various stages of secondary succession.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sorocaba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sorocaba is located exactly on the limit between the sedimentary rocks of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parana_Basin" title="Parana Basin">Parana Sedimentary Basin</a> (Itararé Group, in glacial and deltaic palaeoenvironments of Permian-Carboniferous age) and crystalline basement (Neoproterozoic). Important <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">granite</a> massifs are Sorocaba Massif (calc-alkaline I-Type) and São Francisco Massif (Subalkaline, A-Type). Metamorphic low grade rocks as phyllites, metacalcareous, metarenites, are marine metassediments are included in Sao Roque Group (<a href="/wiki/Neoproterozoic" title="Neoproterozoic">Neoproterozoic</a>) with structural trend northeast-southwest. The <a href="/wiki/Ipanema_Hill" title="Ipanema Hill">Ipanema Hill</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ipanema_Hill" title="Ipanema Hill">Araçoiaba Ridge</a> is a prominent and isolated topographic elevation, it comprises ultrabasic-alkaline intrusion age (<a href="/wiki/Mesozoic" title="Mesozoic">Mesozoic</a>, Early <a href="/wiki/Cretaceous" title="Cretaceous">Cretaceous</a>), remnant of ancient <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcano</a>. It was the first iron mine, <a href="/wiki/Ironworks" title="Ironworks">ironworks</a> and metallurgy of Brazil, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_St._John_of_Ipanema_Iron_Foundry" title="Royal St. John of Ipanema Iron Foundry">Ipanema</a>. The ore was exploited from <a href="/wiki/Magnetite" title="Magnetite">magnetite</a>, probably associated to <a href="/wiki/Carbonatite" title="Carbonatite">carbonatite</a> body.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry">Industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sorocaba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sorocaba's industrial park is well-served by roads, highways, and public transportation. The industrial park boasts more than 25 million square meters and over 1,600 industries. Main economic activities are: industrial <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Machinery" title="Machinery">machinery</a>, heavy <a href="/wiki/Metallurgy" title="Metallurgy">metallurgy</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ironmaking&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ironmaking (page does not exist)">ironmaking</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steelmaking" title="Steelmaking">steelmaking</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automotive" title="Automotive">automotive</a> parts, industrial <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Textiles" title="Textiles">textiles</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Farm_equipment" title="Farm equipment">agricultural equipment</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chemical_industry" title="Chemical industry">chemical</a> and <a href="/wiki/Petrochemistry" title="Petrochemistry">petrochemical</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cement" title="Cement">cement</a> production, eolic energy, <a href="/wiki/Pharmaceutics" title="Pharmaceutics">pharmaceutics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paper" title="Paper">paper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cellulose" title="Cellulose">cellulose</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electronics" title="Electronics">electronics</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telecommunications" title="Telecommunications">telecommunications</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tool" title="Tool">tools</a>, commerce and services. Its tools, automotive parts and pharmaceutics industry are the biggest in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>.</p>
<p>The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ferroban&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ferroban (page does not exist)">Ferroban</a> railroad (formerly <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sorocabana_Railway&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sorocabana Railway (page does not exist)">Sorocabana Railway</a> and <a href="/wiki/RFFSA" title="RFFSA">FEPASA</a>) are connected to <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Santos" title="Port of Santos">Santos</a> Seaport, the largest seaport in South America. The railroad is also an important link to <a href="/wiki/Mercosur" title="Mercosur">Mercosur</a>, reaching both <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a>.</p>
<p>Sorocaba also boasts a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dry_port_terminal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dry port terminal (page does not exist)">dry port terminal</a>, which provides efficient road linkages to the various ports and airports of the region including <a href="/wiki/Sorocaba_Airport" title="Sorocaba Airport">Bertram Luiz Leupolz Airport</a>, which has one of the busiest cargo terminals in the State. A second airport, with much higher traffic and passenger capacity is currently being planned for the upcoming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sorocaba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sorocaba's economic development is fifth in the state, with investments in the order of US$3,5 billion, with US$3 billion in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gross_Domestic_Product" title="Gross Domestic Product">Gross Domestic Product</a>. Its industries export to over 115 countries, with an income of US$370 million per year. There are over eight thousand commerce organizations and another 8,000 service businesses.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sorocaba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sorocaba has nine universities, seven private: University of Medical and Health Sciences at the Catholic University of São Paulo (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PUC-SP" title="PUC-SP">PUC</a>-SP), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Sorocaba&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="University of Sorocaba (page does not exist)">University of Sorocaba</a> (UNISO), ESAMC Sorocaba, Anhanguera, Uirapuru Superior, Academia de Letras de Sorocaba, University Paulista (UNIP), and two public Universidade Estadual Paulista Julio de Mesquita Filho | Universidade Estadual Paulista (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UNESP" title="UNESP">UNESP</a>) and University of Sao Carlos (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFSCar" title="UFSCar">UFSCar</a>).</p>
<p>It also has nine colleges: <a href="/wiki/Law_School_of_Sorocaba" title="Law School of Sorocaba">Law School of Sorocaba</a> (FADI), recommended by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ordem_dos_Advogados_do_Brasil" title="Ordem dos Advogados do Brasil">OAB</a> the year 2007 the FADI received the seal of quality issued by the institution to law courses that have had the best quality indexes in recent years. Engineering College of Sorocaba (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FACENS" title="FACENS">FACENS</a>), College of Technology of São Paulo - Sorocaba (FATEC-SO), College of Physical Education <a href="/wiki/YMCA" title="YMCA">YMCA</a> Sorocaba (FEFISO), Manchester Paulista Institute of Higher Education (IMAP), Academy of Higher Education (AES), Graduate School of Management, Marketing and Communication (ESAMC) Anhanguera Educational Sorocabano Uirapuru Education Center and the Union of Educational Institutions of São Paulo (UNIESP), among others schools.</p>
<p>There are four hundred public schools between municipal and private ones from elementary to high school, many with professionalizing courses. National Industry and Commerce Service Institutions like <a href="/wiki/SENAI" title="SENAI">SENAI</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=SENAC&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="SENAC (page does not exist)">SENAC</a> | Senac, are also present in the city, besides the State Technical School of London | State Technical School and Rubens de Faria e Souza, State Technical School of São Paulo | Technical School Fernando Prestes de Albuquerque and the Polytechnic School of Sorocaba (free of charge), which also houses the Standart School of Sorocaba, Antonio Padilha school.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Law_School_of_Sorocaba" title="Law School of Sorocaba">Law School of Sorocaba</a> (FADI), recommended by the Bar Association of Brazil in 2004 and 2007, received the seal of quality issued by the institution to law courses that have had the best quality indexes in recent years.</p>

<p>The city's health care is well served with many hospitals like Hospital Santa Lucinda, Conjunto Hospitalar de Sorocaba, Santa Casa de Misericórdia (founded over 200 years ago), Evangelical Hospital, Samaritan Hospital, Hospital Unimed, etc. The Ophthalmological Hospital<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> is the hospital that performs more transplants and corneal uptake in Brazil. It was honored at the Distinction Award in Organ Donation of the Secretariat of Health of Brazil and has succeeded in eliminating the waiting list for transplants in the region of Sorocaba and the entire city of São Paulo.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Hospital Santa Lucinda achieved its 100th kidney transplant in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2007, we opened 12 rooms with high-tech equipment, the result of an investment of about $2.5 million from the St. Paul Foundation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Sorocaba Hospital Complex is responsible for servicing the tertiary level of 48 municipalities in southwest São Paulo with a population of over 3 million inhabitants. Reference state is the treatment of burn victims<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and in 2009 over R$5 million were investing into building a new matternity ward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The scientific work in the area of burns in the year 2009 awarded by the Brazilian Society of Plastic Surgery and was coordinated by Dr. Hamilton Aleardo Gonella and held at CHS. For a year, Gonella's team researched the application of nanocrystalline silver dressings. The technique, tested in five patients of CHS, showed positive results, expanding the range of dressing change which reduces the patient's suffering and costs in hospital care. The Plastic Surgery Service CHS has 34 awards for scientific work in various areas. About burns, are at least ten.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Ambulatory Hernia Surgery is the CHS model,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> several training sessions are conducted for surgeons throughout the country. Sorocaba is a technique performed the cut is between 2 and 2.5 cm, smaller than the procedures used elsewhere.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Faculty_of_Medicine_of_Sorocaba" title="Faculty of Medicine of Sorocaba">Faculty of Medicine of Sorocaba</a> - the first medical school in the interior of Brazil - was founded in 1950 and is one of the most responsible for Sorocaba be a reference center for health in Brazil having formed many renowned professionals.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sights">Sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sorocaba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Urban_Transport">Urban Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sorocaba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Urban Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sorocaba has two urban bus terminals: São Paulo e Santo Antônio.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bike.2Fcycle_lanes">Bike/cycle lanes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sorocaba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Bike/cycle lanes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The city has 60 kilometers of bike lanes created in the city's main avenues, and it can be crossed only using bicycles for transportation. The planning of the municipality provides for the construction of the largest cycling network in <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a> in coming years and implement a public bike system, similar to European cities as <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>. Sorocaba is currently the second-largest cycling loop of Brazil, after <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>.</p>
