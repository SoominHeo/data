<p>The <b>oligotrichs</b> are a group of <a href="/wiki/Ciliate" title="Ciliate">ciliates</a>, included among the <a href="/wiki/Spirotrich" title="Spirotrich">spirotrichs</a>. They have prominent oral <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cilia" title="Cilia">cilia</a>, which are arranged as a collar and lapel, in contrast to the <a href="/wiki/Choreotrich" title="Choreotrich">choreotrichs</a> where they form a complete circle. The body cilia are reduced to a girdle and ventral cilia. In <i>Halteria</i> and its relatives, they form bristles or cirri; however these forms may be closer relatives of the <a href="/wiki/Stichotrich" title="Stichotrich">stichotrichs</a> than of other oligotrichs. These organisms are very common in plankton communities, especially in marine systems. Usually found in concentrations of about 1 per ml, they are the most important herbivores in the sea, the first link in the <a href="/wiki/Food_chain" title="Food chain">food chain</a>.</p>
