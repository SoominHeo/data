<p>A <b>fog nozzle</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Firefighting" title="Firefighting">firefighting</a> hose <a href="/wiki/Spray_nozzle" title="Spray nozzle">spray nozzle</a> that breaks its stream into <a href="/wiki/Spray_characteristics" title="Spray characteristics">small droplets</a>. By doing so, its stream achieves a greater <a href="/wiki/Surface_area" title="Surface area">surface area</a>, and thus a greater rate of heat absorption, which, when compared to that of a smoothbore nozzle, speeds its transformation into the steam that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Smothering" title="Smothering">smothers</a> the <a href="/wiki/Fire" title="Fire">fire</a> by displacing its <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a>. Specially designed fog nozzles (with no stream adjustment) have been certified by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Underwriters_Laboratories" title="Underwriters Laboratories">Underwriters Laboratories</a> (UL) for use on Class B &amp; C hazards.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Roles">Roles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fog_nozzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Roles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fog nozzles play an important part in firefighting <a href="/wiki/Tactic_(method)" title="Tactic (method)">tactics</a> due to their versatility. The wide variety of fog nozzle manufacturers allows them to accommodate different sizes of <a href="/wiki/Fire_hose" title="Fire hose">fire hose</a>- most often attack hand line- and streams and are capable in both fire protection and attack. With regard to flow rate, it is imperative to be sure that each fog nozzle be able to handle the flow rate of its water supply because the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Master_stream" title="Master stream">master stream</a> devices to which they are sometimes attached can expel up to 2,000 US gallons (7.6 m<sup>3</sup>) per minute. Nevertheless, as with almost all fog nozzles, those on master stream devices come with either automatic or manual spray pattern and stream adjustments. However, one significant disadvantage of fog nozzles is that Compressed Air Foam (CAF) bubbles' ratio of surface area to volume (which are formed by mixing air into a solution of water and foaming agents at the pump) exceeds that of fog nozzles' water droplets; therefore, the mechanical deflection in the nozzles themselves causes a loss of bubble structure, thereby reducing the CAF's ability to absorb heat. Despite this drawback, provided an appropriate nozzle pressure and <a href="/wiki/Water_supply" title="Water supply">water supply</a>, fog nozzles are effective for any ground fire situation.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Variations">Variations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fog_nozzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Variations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fog nozzles come in many in different styles and sizes, but there are three types which encompass most: automatic, selectable, and manually adjustable. All contain an adjustable baffle that, like a thumb placed on the end of a garden hose, keeps their flow rates and stream reaches steady and finely adjustable despite variations in water pressure at the nozzle. To adjust the flow, the first two types use a control handle, or 'bale' located at the top of nozzle; however, selectable nozzles have a fixed set of flow rates (e.g., 60, 95, and 125 GPM (gallons per min)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (March 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>) that are chosen with a ring or <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/bezel" title="wikt:bezel">bezel</a> located at their tips. It is important to note that selectable nozzles will only flow if the pump pressure exceeds the nozzle pressure. Moreover, the selectable nozzle will change its nozzle pressure and reach as the handle is moved, but the automatic nozzle will automatically re-adjust its opening as the firefighter adjusts its flow rate in order to maintain the correct pressure.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pattern">Pattern</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fog_nozzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pattern">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All styles of fog nozzles have a spray pattern adjustment. These nozzles can produce three different types of streams; the <b>straight stream</b> for long reach, the <b>narrow-angle cone</b> for advancing an attack line into a structure or fire room, and the <b>wide-angle cone</b> for protection and ventilation.</p>
