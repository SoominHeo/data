<p>A <b>fire department</b> (<a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>) or <b>fire brigade</b> (<a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and Commonwealth) (also known as a <b>fire and rescue service</b> or simply <b>fire service</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Public_sector" title="Public sector">public</a> or <a href="/wiki/Private_sector" title="Private sector">private</a> organization that provides predominantly emergency <a href="/wiki/Firefighting" title="Firefighting">firefighting</a> services for a specific geographic area, which is typically a <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipality</a>, county, or fire protection district. Other life safety services, such as <a href="/wiki/Technical_rescue" title="Technical rescue">technical rescue</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hazardous_materials" title="Hazardous materials">hazardous materials</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Emergency_medical_services" title="Emergency medical services">emergency medical services</a> may also be provided by the agency. In the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> a fire brigade is the private firefighting organization of a company or enterprise, operated under rules from <a href="/wiki/Occupational_Safety_and_Health_Administration" title="Occupational Safety and Health Administration">OSHA</a>. A fire department usually contains one or more <a href="/wiki/Fire_station" title="Fire station">fire stations</a> within its boundaries, and may be staffed by career <a href="/wiki/Firefighter" title="Firefighter">firefighters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Volunteer_fire_department" title="Volunteer fire department">volunteer firefighters</a>, or a combination thereof (referred to as a <a href="/wiki/Combination_fire_department" title="Combination fire department">combination department</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>A fire department may also provide "<a href="/wiki/Fire_protection" title="Fire protection">fire protection</a>" or fire prevention services, whereby firefighters visit homes and give fire safety advice and fit smoke alarms for members of the public. In many countries fire protection or prevention is seen as an important role for the fire service, as preventing a fire from occurring in the first place can save lives and property.</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organization">Organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fire departments are organized in a system of administration, services, training, and operations; for example:</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Einsatzfahrzeuge.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Einsatzfahrzeuge.jpg/220px-Einsatzfahrzeuge.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Einsatzfahrzeuge.jpg/330px-Einsatzfahrzeuge.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Einsatzfahrzeuge.jpg/440px-Einsatzfahrzeuge.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Einsatzfahrzeuge.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Emergency fire &amp; rescue vehicles of the <a href="/wiki/German_fire_services" title="German fire services">German fire services</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li><b>Administration</b> is responsible for supervision, budgets, policy, and <a href="/wiki/Human_resources" title="Human resources">human resources</a>.</li>
<li><b>Service</b> offers protection, safety, and education to the public.</li>
<li><b>Training</b> prepares skilled people with the knowledge to perform their duties.</li>
<li><b>Operations</b> performs the tasks to successfully save the public from harm.</li>
</ul>
<p>A fire service is normally set up where it can have fire stations and sophisticated <a href="/wiki/Fire_engine" title="Fire engine">fire engines</a> strategically deployed throughout the area it serves, so that <a href="/wiki/Dispatcher" title="Dispatcher">dispatchers</a> can send fire engines, fire trucks, or <a href="/wiki/Ambulance" title="Ambulance">ambulances</a> from the fire stations closest to the incident. Larger departments have branches within themselves to increase efficiency, composed of volunteers, support, and research.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Volunteers</b> give advantages to the department in a state of emergency.</li>
<li><b>Support</b> organizing the resources within and outside of the department.</li>
<li><b>Research</b> is to give advantages in new technologies for the department.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jurisdiction">Jurisdiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Jurisdiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:HoustonFireDepartmentTXHQ.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1704" data-file-width="2272" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/HoustonFireDepartmentTXHQ.JPG/220px-HoustonFireDepartmentTXHQ.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/HoustonFireDepartmentTXHQ.JPG/330px-HoustonFireDepartmentTXHQ.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/HoustonFireDepartmentTXHQ.JPG/440px-HoustonFireDepartmentTXHQ.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:HoustonFireDepartmentTXHQ.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Logistical Center &amp; Maintenance Depot of the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Fire_Department" title="Houston Fire Department">Houston Fire Department</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>A fire department's jurisdiction is organized by the governmental body that controls the department, although there are private fire services as well. This comes from a <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipality</a>, <a href="/wiki/County" title="County">county</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prefecture" title="Prefecture">prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Federated_state" title="Federated state">state</a>, <a href="/wiki/Province" title="Province">province</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Nation_state" title="Nation state">nation</a> type of government. The most common type of government control is at the municipality level. The jurisdiction size and organisation would be set up by a department or the government in charge of these duties. This deals with the placement of fire stations, equipment, and personnel within the area of control. Fire departments periodically survey their jurisdiction areas and use the data for redeploying proper coverage. This data comes from travel time, range from station, and/or a population survey. This brings equal service to the entire community and gives the department efficient places to launch operations.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ancient_Rome">Ancient Rome</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ancient Rome">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The earliest known firefighting service was formed in <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Ancient Rome</a> by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Egnatius_Rufus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Egnatius Rufus (page does not exist)">Egnatius Rufus</a> who used his slaves to provide a free fire service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> These men fought fires using <a href="/wiki/Bucket_brigade" title="Bucket brigade">bucket chains</a> and also patrolled the streets with the authority to impose <a href="/wiki/Corporal_punishment" title="Corporal punishment">corporal punishment</a> upon those who violated fire-prevention codes. The Emperor <a href="/wiki/Augustus" title="Augustus">Augustus</a> established a public fire department in 24 BCE, composed of 600 slaves distributed amongst seven fire stations in Rome.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Coe2009_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Coe2009-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1600s_and_1700s">1600s and 1700s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1600s and 1700s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fire departments were again formed by <a href="/wiki/Property_insurance" title="Property insurance">property insurance</a> companies beginning in the 17th century after the <a href="/wiki/Great_Fire_of_London" title="Great Fire of London">Great Fire of London</a> in 1666. The first insurance brigades were established the following year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CoteBugbee1988_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-CoteBugbee1988-4">[4]</a></sup> Others began to realize that a lot of money could be made from this practice, and ten more insurance companies set up in London before 1832: The Alliance, Atlas, Globe, Imperial, London, Protector, Royal Exchange, Sun Union and Westminster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Each company had its own <a href="/wiki/Fire_insurance_mark" title="Fire insurance mark">fire mark</a>, a durable plaque that would be affixed to the building exterior. A company's fire brigade would not extinguish a burning building if it did not have the correct fire mark.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a> also had a sophisticated firefighting system in the late 17th century, under the direction of artist <a href="/wiki/Jan_van_der_Heyden" title="Jan van der Heyden">Jan van der Heyden</a>, who had improved the designs of both <a href="/wiki/Fire_hose" title="Fire hose">fire hoses</a> and fire pumps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hensler2011_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hensler2011-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boston,_Massachusetts" title="Boston, Massachusetts">Boston, Massachusetts</a> established America's first publicly funded, paid fire department in 1679.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Fire insurance made its debut in the American colonies in South Carolina in 1736, but it was <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Franklin" title="Benjamin Franklin">Benjamin Franklin</a> who imported the London model of insurance. He established the colonies' first fire insurance company in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> named the Philadelphia Contributionship,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CoteBugbee1988_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-CoteBugbee1988-4">[4]</a></sup> as well as its associated <a href="/wiki/Union_Fire_Company" title="Union Fire Company">Union Volunteer Fire Company</a>, which was an unpaid (volunteer) company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1754,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_Nova_Scotia" title="Halifax, Nova Scotia">Halifax, Nova Scotia</a> established the <a href="/wiki/Halifax_Regional_Fire_and_Emergency" title="Halifax Regional Fire and Emergency">Halifax Regional Fire and Emergency</a>, which is today Canada's oldest fire department.</p>
<p>In 1773, the city of <a href="/wiki/Petersburg,_Virginia" title="Petersburg, Virginia">Petersburg, Virginia</a> established one of the first fire departments in the United States<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and it was also made up of unpaid volunteers<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1800s">1800s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 1800s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 19th century, the practice of fire brigades refusing to put out fires in buildings that were uninsured led to the demand of central command for fire companies.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Without a reliable source, this claim is just political conjecture (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Cities began to form their own fire departments as a civil service to the public, obliging private fire companies to shut down, many merging their fire stations into the city's fire department. In 1833, London's ten independent brigades all merged to form the London Fire Engine Establishment (LFEE), with <a href="/wiki/James_Braidwood_(firefighter)" title="James Braidwood (firefighter)">James Braidwood</a> as the Chief Officer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Braidwood had previously been the fire chief in <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>, where the world's first municipal fire service was founded in 1824, and he is now regarded, along with Van der Heyden, as one of founders of modern firefighting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hensler2011_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hensler2011-7">[7]</a></sup> The LFEE then was incorporated into the city's <a href="/wiki/London_Fire_Brigade" title="London Fire Brigade">Metropolitan Fire Brigade</a> in 1865 under <a href="/wiki/Eyre_Massey_Shaw" title="Eyre Massey Shaw">Eyre Massey Shaw</a>.</p>
<p>Established in 1853, the <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Fire_Department" title="Cincinnati Fire Department">Cincinnati Fire Department</a> is the oldest paid fully professional municipal fire department in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1879, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Notre_Dame_University" title="Notre Dame University">Notre Dame University</a> established the first University-based fire department in the United States <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1900s">1900s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 1900s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1906, the first motorized fire department was organized in <a href="/wiki/Springfield,_Massachusetts" title="Springfield, Massachusetts">Springfield, Massachusetts</a>, after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knox_Automobile" title="Knox Automobile">Knox Automobile</a> of Springfield produced the first modern <a href="/wiki/Fire_engine" title="Fire engine">fire engine</a> one year earlier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Emergency_Medical_Services">Emergency Medical Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Emergency Medical Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In many countries or regions (e.g., the United States, Germany, Japan), fire departments are often responsible for providing <a href="/wiki/Emergency_medical_services" title="Emergency medical services">emergency medical services</a>. Many firefighters are cross-trained as <a href="/wiki/Certified_first_responder" title="Certified first responder">certified first responders</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emergency_medical_technicians" title="Emergency medical technicians">emergency medical technicians</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ambulance_technician" title="Ambulance technician">ambulance technicians</a>, or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paramedics" title="Paramedics">paramedics</a>. Some services act only as "first responders" to medical emergencies, stabilizing victims until an ambulance can arrive. Many fire services also operate <a href="/wiki/Emergency_medical_services" title="Emergency medical services">ambulance services</a>.</p>
