<p><b>Sovetsk</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Сове́тск</span>), before 1946 known as <b>Tilsit</b> (<a href="/wiki/Lithuanian_language" title="Lithuanian language">Lithuanian</a>: <span lang="lt" xml:lang="lt"><i>Tilžė</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>: <span lang="pl" xml:lang="pl"><i>Tylża</i></span>) in <a href="/wiki/East_Prussia" title="East Prussia">East Prussia</a>, is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kaliningrad_Oblast" title="Kaliningrad Oblast">Kaliningrad Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located on the south bank of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neman_River" title="Neman River">Neman River</a>. Population: <span class="nowrap">41,705 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-3">[3]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">43,224 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopCensus_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-PopCensus-6">[6]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">41,881 (<a href="/wiki/Soviet_Census_(1989)" title="Soviet Census (1989)">1989 Census</a>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census1989_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census1989-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sovetsk,_Kaliningrad_Oblast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sovetsk lies at the confluence of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tilse" title="Tilse">Tilse</a> (Russian: Тыльжа <i>Tylzha</i>, Lithuanian <i>Tilže</i>) with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neman_River" title="Neman River">Neman</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sovetsk,_Kaliningrad_Oblast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Treaty_of_Tilsit.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="453" data-file-width="640" height="156" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Treaty_of_Tilsit.jpg/220px-Treaty_of_Tilsit.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Treaty_of_Tilsit.jpg/330px-Treaty_of_Tilsit.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Treaty_of_Tilsit.jpg/440px-Treaty_of_Tilsit.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Treaty_of_Tilsit.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_I_of_Russia" title="Alexander I of Russia">Alexander I</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louise_of_Mecklenburg-Strelitz" title="Louise of Mecklenburg-Strelitz">Queen Louise</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Frederick_William_III_of_Prussia" title="Frederick William III of Prussia">Frederick William III</a> in Tilsit</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Tilsit, which received <a href="/wiki/German_town_law" title="German town law">civic rights</a> from <a href="/wiki/Albert,_Duke_of_Prussia" title="Albert, Duke of Prussia">Albert, Duke of Prussia</a> in 1552, grew up around a castle of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teutonic_Knights" title="Teutonic Knights">Teutonic Knights</a>, known as the Schalauer Haus, founded in 1288.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Treaties_of_Tilsit" title="Treaties of Tilsit">Treaties of Tilsit</a> were signed here in July 1807, the preliminaries of which were settled by the emperors <a href="/wiki/Alexander_I_of_Russia" title="Alexander I of Russia">Alexander I of Russia</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_I_of_France" title="Napoleon I of France">Napoleon I of France</a> on a raft moored in the Neman River. This treaty, which created the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Westphalia" title="Kingdom of Westphalia">Kingdom of Westphalia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Warsaw" title="Duchy of Warsaw">Duchy of Warsaw</a>, completed Napoleon's humiliation of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia" title="Kingdom of Prussia">Kingdom of Prussia</a>, when it was deprived of one half of its dominions.</p>
<p>This short-lived peace-treaty is also remarkable for quite another reason. Three days before its signing, Prussian queen <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luise_of_Mecklenburg-Strelitz" title="Luise of Mecklenburg-Strelitz">Louise</a> (1776–1810) tried to persuade Napoleon in a private conversation to ease his hard conditions on Prussia. Though unsuccessful, Louise's effort greatly endeared her to the Prussian people.</p>
<p>Until 1945, a marble tablet marked the house in which King <a href="/wiki/Frederick_William_III_of_Prussia" title="Frederick William III of Prussia">Frederick William III of Prussia</a> and Queen Louise resided. Also, in the former Schenkendorf Platz was a monument to the poet <a href="/wiki/Max_von_Schenkendorf" title="Max von Schenkendorf">Max von Schenkendorf</a> (1783–1817) a native of Tilsit. During the 19th century when the <a href="/wiki/Lithuanian_language" title="Lithuanian language">Lithuanian language</a> was banned within the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a>, Tilsit was an important centre for printing Lithuanian books which then were smuggled by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knygne%C5%A1iai" title="Knygnešiai">Knygnešiai</a> to the Russian-controlled part of <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a>. In general, Tilsit thrived and was an important Prussian town. By 1900 it had electric tramways and 34,500 inhabitants; a direct railway line linked it to <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6nigsberg" title="Königsberg">Königsberg</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Labiau" title="Labiau">Labiau</a> and steamers docked there daily. It was occupied by Russian troops between 26 August 1914 and 12 September 1914 during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Tilsit" title="Act of Tilsit">Act of Tilsit</a> was signed here by leaders of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lietuvininks" title="Lietuvininks">Lietuvininks</a> in 1918.</p>
<p>According to German data in 1890 35% of the Tilsit district (which Tilsit was not part of) population was composed of <a href="/wiki/Prussian_Lithuanians" title="Prussian Lithuanians">Prussian Lithuanians</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hitler" title="Hitler">Hitler</a> visited the town just before <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, and a photo was taken of him on the famous bridge over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neman_River" title="Neman River">Memel River</a>. Tilsit was occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">Red Army</a> on January 20, 1945, and was annexed by the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> in 1945. The remaining <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">Germans</a> who had not <a href="/wiki/Evacuation_of_East_Prussia" title="Evacuation of East Prussia">evacuated</a> were subsequently <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Expulsion_of_Germans_after_World_War_II" title="Expulsion of Germans after World War II">forcibly expelled</a> and replaced with Soviet citizens. The town was renamed Sovetsk by the new <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">communist</a> rulers, in honor of <a href="/wiki/Soviet_(council)" title="Soviet (council)">Soviet</a> rule.</p>
<p>Modern Sovetsk has sought to take advantage of Tilsit's rich traditions of <a href="/wiki/Cheese" title="Cheese">cheese</a> production (<a href="/wiki/Tilsit_cheese" title="Tilsit cheese">Tilsit cheese</a>), but the new name ("Sovetsky cheese") has not inherited its predecessor's reputation.</p>
<p>In April 2007, government restrictions on visits to border areas have been tightened, and for foreigners, and Russians living outside the border zone, travel to the Sovetsk and <a href="/wiki/Bagrationovsk" title="Bagrationovsk">Bagrationovsk</a> areas required advance permission from the Border Guard Service (in some cases up to 30 days beforehand). It was alleged that this procedure slowed the development of these potentially thriving border towns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In June 2012, these restrictions were lifted (the only restricted area is the Neman river shoreline), which gave a boost to local and international tourism.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sovetsk,_Kaliningrad_Oblast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, it is incorporated as the <b><a href="/wiki/City_of_federal_subject_significance" title="City of federal subject significance">town of oblast significance</a> of Sovetsk</b>—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Kaliningrad_Oblast" title="Administrative divisions of Kaliningrad Oblast">districts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref648_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ref648-1">[1]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the town of oblast significance of Sovetsk is incorporated as <b>Sovetsky Urban Okrug</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref723_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ref723-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture">Architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sovetsk,_Kaliningrad_Oblast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Architecture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many of the town's buildings were destroyed during World War II. However, the old town centre still includes several German buildings, including those of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jugendstil" title="Jugendstil">Jugendstil</a> design. The Queen Louise Bridge, now connecting the town to <a href="/wiki/Panemun%C4%97" title="Panemunė">Panemunė</a>/Übermemel in <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a>, retains an arch – all that is left of a more complex pre-war bridge structure.</p>
