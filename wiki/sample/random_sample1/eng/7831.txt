<p><b>Sovicille</b> is a <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> (municipality) in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Siena" title="Province of Siena">Province of Siena</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> region <a href="/wiki/Tuscany" title="Tuscany">Tuscany</a>, located about 60 kilometres (37 mi) south of <a href="/wiki/Florence" title="Florence">Florence</a> and about 10 kilometres (6 mi) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Siena" title="Siena">Siena</a>.</p>
<p>Sovicille borders the <i>comuni</i> of <a href="/wiki/Casole_d%27Elsa" title="Casole d'Elsa">Casole d'Elsa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chiusdino" title="Chiusdino">Chiusdino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monteriggioni" title="Monteriggioni">Monteriggioni</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monteroni_d%27Arbia" title="Monteroni d'Arbia">Monteroni d'Arbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monticiano" title="Monticiano">Monticiano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Murlo" title="Murlo">Murlo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Siena" title="Siena">Siena</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sovicille&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name Sovicille is documented as far back as 1004,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> but the origins of the place probably go back much further. (In 2002 a mosaic from the Roman period was uncovered in the Church of St John the Baptist, and there is no shortage of Etruscan relics in the immediate area.)</p>
<p>During the Middle Ages the region found itself on the borders between <a href="/wiki/Siena" title="Siena">Siena</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volterra" title="Volterra">Volterra</a>: The Abbey of Serena, some 30 kilometers to the west, was under the control of Volterra, and the land around Sovicille was included as property of the abbey around the year 1000.</p>
<p>A written record of the place survives from 23 April 1123, in which the (<a href="/wiki/Siena" title="Siena">by origin Senese</a>) <a href="/wiki/Pope_Alexander_III" title="Pope Alexander III">Pope Alexander III</a> identifies it as Sufficille. The name is thought to derive from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> words "sub" (under) and "ficinulae/ficus" (small fig tree").</p>
<p>The municipal statutes date right back to the thirteenth century. In the thirteenth century the by now democratically administered <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Siena" title="Republic of Siena">Republic of Siena</a> allowed several municipalities, including Sovicille, their own municipal statutes in 1238 (modified in 1293 and then reconfigured, to the format that survives today, in 1303),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> which included the right to make their own laws.</p>
<p>In 1260 Sovicille was occupied by <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Florence" title="Republic of Florence">Florence</a>, but in that year the Florentine armies were defeated at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Montaperti" title="Battle of Montaperti">Battle of Montaperti</a>, whereupon Sovicille reverted to <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Siena" title="Republic of Siena">Siena</a>.</p>
<p>In 1333 Sovicille was overrun and burned down by <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Pisa" title="Republic of Pisa">Pisa</a>.</p>
<p>The Medici family purchased the lands of <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Siena" title="Republic of Siena">Siena</a> from <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor">the Emperor</a> for two million ducats in 1557: this was followed by the abolition of democratic institutions and a return to feudalism. Sovicille became a fief of the Medici and was transformed into an imposing fortress, defending an access point to their newly expanded territories.</p>
<p>After the death of the last of the Medici rulers, power transferred to the <a href="/wiki/List_of_rulers_of_Lorraine" title="List of rulers of Lorraine">Dukes of Lorraine</a> who remained in control till the end of the eighteenth century when the entire region was invaded by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_Bonaparte" title="Napoleon Bonaparte">Napoleon Bonaparte</a>.</p>
<p>The French remained in charge till 1814 when, under the terms agreed at <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Vienna" title="Congress of Vienna">Vienna</a>, Sovicille was annexed to the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Tuscany" title="Grand Duchy of Tuscany">Grand Duchy of Tuscany</a>. In 1859 it came under the control of the short-lived <a href="/wiki/United_Provinces_of_Central_Italy" title="United Provinces of Central Italy">United Provinces of Central Italy</a>, which was effectively a transitional entity leading to incorporation in 1861 within the new <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy" title="Kingdom of Italy">Kingdom of Italy</a>.</p>
