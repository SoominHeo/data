<p><b>Sobinka</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Собинка</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sobinsky_District" title="Sobinsky District">Sobinsky District</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Oblast" title="Vladimir Oblast">Vladimir Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located on the right bank of the <a href="/wiki/Klyazma_River" title="Klyazma River">Klyazma River</a> (<a href="/wiki/Oka_River" title="Oka River">Oka</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tributary" title="Tributary">tributary</a>), 37 kilometers (23 mi) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Vladimir,_Russia" title="Vladimir, Russia">Vladimir</a>, the administrative center of the <a href="/wiki/Oblast" title="Oblast">oblast</a>. Population: <span class="nowrap">19,482 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-4">[4]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">21,054 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopCensus_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-PopCensus-6">[6]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">23,720 (<a href="/wiki/Soviet_Census_(1989)" title="Soviet Census (1989)">1989 Census</a>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census1989_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census1989-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sobinka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was called <b>Komavangard</b> (<span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Комавангард</span>) for a short period in the 1920s.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It was granted town status in 1939.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sobinka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, Sobinka serves as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sobinsky_District" title="Sobinsky District">Sobinsky District</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OKATO_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-OKATO-2">[2]</a></sup> to which it is directly subordinated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VladimirO_admlist_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-VladimirO_admlist-1">[1]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> of Sobinka is incorporated within Sobinsky Municipal District as <b>Sobinka Urban Settlement</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VladimirOSobinskyD_mun_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-VladimirOSobinskyD_mun-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
