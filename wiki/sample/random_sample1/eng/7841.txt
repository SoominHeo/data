<p><i><b>Soul Surfer</b></i> is a 2011 American <a href="/wiki/Biographical_film" title="Biographical film">biographical</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_film" title="Drama film">drama film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Sean_McNamara_(director)" title="Sean McNamara (director)">Sean McNamara</a>, based on the 2004 autobiography <i>Soul Surfer: A True Story of Faith, Family, and Fighting to Get Back on the Board</i> by <a href="/wiki/Bethany_Hamilton" title="Bethany Hamilton">Bethany Hamilton</a> about her life as a surfer after a horrific <a href="/wiki/Shark_attack" title="Shark attack">shark attack</a> and her recovery. The film stars <a href="/wiki/AnnaSophia_Robb" title="AnnaSophia Robb">AnnaSophia Robb</a>, <a href="/wiki/Helen_Hunt" title="Helen Hunt">Helen Hunt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Quaid" title="Dennis Quaid">Dennis Quaid</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lorraine_Nicholson" title="Lorraine Nicholson">Lorraine Nicholson</a> with <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Sorbo" title="Kevin Sorbo">Kevin Sorbo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sonya_Balmores" title="Sonya Balmores">Sonya Balmores</a>, <a href="/wiki/Branscombe_Richmond" title="Branscombe Richmond">Branscombe Richmond</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Craig_T._Nelson" title="Craig T. Nelson">Craig T. Nelson</a>.</p>
<p>Filming took place in Hawaii in early 2010, with additional filming taking place in Tahiti in August 2010. <i>Soul Surfer</i> was released in theaters on <span class="nowrap">April 8</span>, 2011 in the United States and Canada by a partnership between <a href="/wiki/FilmDistrict" title="FilmDistrict">FilmDistrict</a> and <a href="/wiki/TriStar_Pictures" title="TriStar Pictures">TriStar Pictures</a>, and was a commercial success, earning $47,088,990<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bom_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-bom-1">[1]</a></sup> on a $18 million budget, but received mixed reviews from critical audiences.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soul_Surfer_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2003, teenager Bethany Hamilton lives in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kauai,_Hawaii" title="Kauai, Hawaii">Kauai, Hawaii</a> with her parents Tom and Cheri, and two brothers, Noah and Timmy. All are surfers, but she and her best friend <a href="/wiki/Alana_Blanchard" title="Alana Blanchard">Alana Blanchard</a> have grown up with a passion for the sport and enter a competition. Her church youth ministry leader, Sarah Hill, is disappointed when she has to withdraw from a planned mission trip to Mexico because of the contest.</p>
<p>In the competition, Bethany and Alana place first and third, respectively. The following day, Tom goes to the hospital for knee surgery, and the girls go surfing with Alana's father Holt and brother Byron. As Bethany dangles her left arm in the water, a <a href="/wiki/Tiger_shark" title="Tiger shark">tiger shark</a> is swimming just under her surfboard and notices her arm floating in the water. It unexpectedly attacks, ripping off her arm below the shoulder. Holt, Alana, and Byron get her out of the water where Holt makes a <a href="/wiki/Tourniquet" title="Tourniquet">tourniquet</a> out of his swimshirt to put on her while Byron calls 911 as Cheri is also informed. An ambulance meets them on the way to the hospital. Just before starting Tom's knee surgery, Dr. David Rovinsky is called to the emergency room to treat Bethany. Besides losing her left arm, she also lost 60% of her blood and Dr. Rovinsky calls her survival a miracle.</p>
<p>The onslaught of paparazzi also proves to be a great strain on her family and their privacy. The Hamiltons are grateful to Holt for his quick and active thinking and decisive action that saved her life. Her injury prevents her from participating in the Rip Curl photo shoots, but she wishes Alana well.</p>
<p>Bethany perseveres and after a recuperation period, gets back in the water, and learns to surf with one arm. <i><a href="/wiki/Inside_Edition" title="Inside Edition">Inside Edition</a></i> offers to provide a prosthetic one that is cosmetically perfect and has bendable joints, in exchange for an interview. She angrily rejects it when she learns it will not help her surf as it is not weight bearing, as a result of the size of her arm stump.</p>
<p>Bethany eventually re-enters the competition, telling rival Malina not to go easy on her, and rejects a five-minute head start offered by the judges. She does not perform well because she cannot stay on the board long enough to go out and catch a competitive wave so Malina wins. Disappointed at this loss, she decides to give up competitive surfing and her friendship with Alana is strained following an argument.</p>
<p>Bethany decides to surprise Sarah by joining the youth group on another mission trip to help the people of <a href="/wiki/Phuket_Province" title="Phuket Province">Phuket</a>, Thailand who were devastated by the <a href="/wiki/2004_Indian_Ocean_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2004 Indian Ocean earthquake and tsunami">2004 Indian Ocean tsunami</a>. Despite her recent tragedy, she joins her youth group to help the Thai children get over their fear of the ocean. They are understandably afraid of the water, including a little boy. She decides to go into it with a surfboard, hoping this will coax him into it. It works, and the realization that she can use her gift to inspire people motivates her to take up surfing again.</p>
<p>Tom, who believes that Bethany possesses a great surfer's instinct for sensing when the best waves will form, rigs a handle on her surfboard which she can use to prevent falling off while paddling out to the waves, which is not prohibited by the competition's rules. Bethany trains for the competition while rekindling her friendship with Alana. She enters the national championship. During the competition, she performs respectably, though she is still chasing third place. Suddenly, with only minutes left on the clock, the waves die down and all the surfers can only loiter, waiting for the waves to start back up. Tom's belief in his daughter's instinct is proven when she is the only one to sense a big wave forming, and she alone paddles out. When it forms, the others cannot get out in time and she catches it just as the horn sounds. If it is in time, she will win, but the judges rule that the time has expired. Malina is the winner, but she has finally gotten over her differences with Bethany, inviting her up on the platform to share first place.</p>
<p>Subsequently, Bethany lets the reporters interview her. One asks her what she would do if given the chance to undo the loss of her arm. She says that she would still lose it because she can embrace more people now than she ever could with both.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soul_Surfer_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Plans for a biopic film about Hamilton have existed since months after her shark attack and her subsequent recovery in 2004. During Hamilton's media attention, the father of Bethany's friends Chantilly and Tiffany, Roy "Dutch" Hofstetter, became the Hamilton family's media manager. Hofstetter, in February 2004, envisioned a film based on Bethany's experience, provisionally titled <i>The Bethany Hamilton Story</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-power_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-power-2">[2]</a></sup> Bethany published her biographical book <i>Soul Surfer</i> in 2004,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> reported that a film about her life was scheduled to begin filming in January 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Production did not begin as anticipated, and <i><a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">Time</a></i> reported in July 2006 that production was scheduled for later in the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> reported that the project at one point had an investment of <span class="nowrap">$7.5 million</span> and the backing of Peter Schlessel, a Sony Pictures executive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-splashy_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-splashy-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Though production had not begun by the end of 2006, in January 2007 Sean McNamara was announced to be directing the biographical film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> While Hamilton had a series of surfing successes, turning pro in 2007, McNamara and producer David Brookwell with her manager Roy "Dutch" Hofstetter sought more material for the film. The book was considered "a straightforward account" that was targeted to Christian readers, so the filmmakers met with the Hamilton family to determine if there were any unpublished conflicts that could be highlighted in the film. They discovered that the incident had strained the family, that family members questioned their Christian faith, and that Bethany Hamilton struggled with her physical appearance and how boys would perceive her. The media attention on the family was described by Brookwell as "a second shark attack" that had made their lives uncomfortably public.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-splashy_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-splashy-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:30em; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>"She was actually my suggestion. I'd seen her in several films like <i><a href="/wiki/Bridge_to_Terabithia_(2007_film)" title="Bridge to Terabithia (2007 film)">Bridge to Terabithia</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Charlie_and_the_Chocolate_Factory_(film)" title="Charlie and the Chocolate Factory (film)">Charlie and the Chocolate Factory</a></i> and thought she could play me really well. She visited me in Hawaii and my surf coach and I taught her how to surf, so she at least looked like she knew what she was doing!"</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;">— Bethany Hamilton on suggesting AnnaSophia Robb to portray her<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interview405_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-interview405-8">[8]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>McNamara, Brookwell, Hofstetter and Douglas Schwartz spent several years raising money for production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-splashy_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-splashy-6">[6]</a></sup> The director wrote an adapted screenplay with Michael Berk, Douglas Schwartz and Deborah Schwartz. Additional uncredited writing was performed by Ron Bass, Jen Smolka and Kara Holden. Before the film entered production, Sony Pictures Worldwide Acquisitions acquired distribution rights for North America and most other territories. The production companies Mandalay Vision, Brookwell McNamara Entertainment and Life's a Beach Entertainment collaborated for the production, with Enticing Entertainment and Island Film Group providing financing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-first_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-first-9">[9]</a></sup> Bethany Hamilton chose with her mother AnnaSophia Robb to portray her, as well as Sonia Balmores Chung and Jeremy Sumpter to play Malina and Alana's brother, Byron.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-what.21_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-what.21-10">[10]</a></sup> In February 2010, Robb was announced to be part of the film as Bethany Hamilton, along with Dennis Quaid and Helen Hunt who were cast as Bethany's parents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-variety1-11">[11]</a></sup> Singer Carrie Underwood, in her feature film debut, was cast as a church youth leader.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety1_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-variety1-11">[11]</a></sup> All the surfing scenes after the shark attack were done by Hamilton herself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interview405_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-interview405-8">[8]</a></sup> Filming began the same month in <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety1_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-variety1-11">[11]</a></sup> Principal photography and second-unit aerial work took place for 40 days; cinematographer <a href="/wiki/John_R._Leonetti" title="John R. Leonetti">John R. Leonetti</a> shot on 35mm film. During filming, Robb wore a green sleeve on her arm so visual effects could be included later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-splashy_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-splashy-6">[6]</a></sup> Though McNamara was editing the film by May 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> additional filming took place in August 2010 in <a href="/wiki/Tahiti" title="Tahiti">Tahiti</a>. During post-production, the VFX company Engine Room worked on 450 arm-removal shots, digitally inserting the upper arm residuum in place of Robb's green sleeve.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-splashy_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-splashy-6">[6]</a></sup> The Hamilton family was involved in the choice of music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-what.21_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-what.21-10">[10]</a></sup> Ultimately, the film's production budget was <span class="nowrap">$18 million</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LABox_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-LABox-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soul_Surfer_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In July 2010, <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i> reported <i>Soul Surfer</i> as one of several faith-based films similar to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Blind_Side_(film)" title="The Blind Side (film)">The Blind Side</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Get_Low_(film)" title="Get Low (film)">Get Low</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Like_Dandelion_Dust" title="Like Dandelion Dust">Like Dandelion Dust</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Jumping_the_Broom" title="Jumping the Broom">Jumping the Broom</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In September 2010, independent studio FilmDistrict was launched, and the company formed a partnership with <a href="/wiki/TriStar_Pictures" title="TriStar Pictures">TriStar Pictures</a> to release <i>Soul Surfer</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-first_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-first-9">[9]</a></sup> FilmDistrict originally committed to release the film at <span class="nowrap">300 theaters</span>, but when executives saw the final product, they invested <span class="nowrap">$26 million</span> in a print and advertising commitment with the goal of releasing <i>Soul Surfer</i> in <span class="nowrap">2,000 theaters</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-splashy_6-5"><a href="#cite_note-splashy-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Prior to the film's commercial release, it was screened for religious leaders. A scene in which Dennis Quaid's character reads the Bible in the hospital at his daughter's bedside had the words "Holy Bible" digitally removed from the cover. Bethany Hamilton's father said that David Zelon, an executive at Mandalay Pictures, lobbied to reduce the <i>Soul Surfer</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s Christian elements so the film could appeal more to non-Christian audiences. The Hamilton family objected, and the words "Holy Bible" were restored in the scene in a follow-up screening. Another debated scene was one in which Carrie Underwood's character, a church youth leader, quotes biblical scripture (<a href="/wiki/Book_of_Jeremiah" title="Book of Jeremiah">Jeremiah</a> 29:11<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>). While those involved with the film were fine with the verse, they did not want the scene to explicitly indicate that its origin was the Bible. Their stance was challenged, and the scene indicates the verse being from the Bible. <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i> cited the dust-up as an example of Hollywood learning to appeal to the faith-based community while still attracting secular audiences. <i>The Blind Side</i>, which accomplished both, had grossed <span class="nowrap">$256 million</span> in the United States and Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soul_Surfer_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film was released in <span class="nowrap">2,214 theaters</span> in the United States and Canada on <span class="nowrap">April 8</span>, 2011. It grossed <span class="nowrap">$10.6 million</span> over its opening weekend, ranking fourth at the box office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bom_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-bom-1">[1]</a></sup> Sony Pictures reported that 80% of the audience was female and that 56% were under 25 years old.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soul_Surfer_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Soul Surfer</i> has received mixed reviews from critics. Review aggregation website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> gives a score of 46% based on reviews from 101 critics, with an average rating of 5.3/10. The site's consensus is: "There's an amazing true story at the heart of <i>Soul Surfer</i> -- and unfortunately, it's drowned by waves of Hollywood cheese."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/CinemaScore" title="CinemaScore">CinemaScore</a> reported that audiences gave the film a rare "A+" grade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Sun-Times" title="Chicago Sun-Times">Chicago Sun-Times</a></i> was mildly positive in his review, giving the film two-and-a-half stars out of four and writing "<i>Soul Surfer</i> is a wholesome movie, intended as inspirational. Whether it will cheer viewers who are not as capable as Bethany is an excellent question. AnnaSophia Robb is a convincing, cheerful heroine. Dennis Quaid and Helen Hunt, as Bethany's parents, are stalwart and supportive, although the script indeed leaves them with no other choice."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Owen_Gleiberman" title="Owen Gleiberman">Owen Gleiberman</a> of <i>Entertainment Weekly</i> gave the film a B grade, writing "[t]he more cynical viewers out there may say, 'Not for me.' But <i>Soul Surfer</i>, while formulaic in design, is an authentic and heartfelt movie."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> S. Jhoanna Robledo of <a href="/wiki/Common_Sense_Media" title="Common Sense Media">Common Sense Media</a> gave the film three stars out of five, writing "Yes, it's a message movie, but the message burrows deep enough under your skin to make the movie, given its utter conventionality, unexpectedly stirring."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite mixed critical reception, it was a hit among audiences; <a href="/wiki/CinemaScore" title="CinemaScore">CinemaScore</a> polls reports that the average grade moviegoers gave it was a rare A+.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soul_Surfer_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Soul Surfer</i> was released on DVD and Blu-ray on August 2, 2011 by <a href="/wiki/TriStar_Pictures" title="TriStar Pictures">TriStar Pictures</a> and <a href="/wiki/FilmDistrict" title="FilmDistrict">FilmDistrict</a>.</p>
