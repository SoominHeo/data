<p><b>Sōka Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">草加駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sōka-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station on the <a href="/wiki/Tobu_Skytree_Line" title="Tobu Skytree Line">Tobu Skytree Line</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S%C5%8Dka,_Saitama" title="Sōka, Saitama">Sōka, Saitama</a>, Japan, operated by the private railway operator <a href="/wiki/Tobu_Railway" title="Tobu Railway">Tobu Railway</a>. It is one stop away from the border between <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Prefecture" title="Saitama Prefecture">Saitama Prefecture</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Line">Line</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C5%8Dka_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Line">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station is served by the <a href="/wiki/Tobu_Skytree_Line" title="Tobu Skytree Line">Tobu Skytree Line</a>, and is 17.5 kilometers from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terminal_station" title="Terminal station">terminus</a> of the line at <a href="/wiki/Asakusa_Station_(Tokyo_Metro,_Toei,_Tobu)" title="Asakusa Station (Tokyo Metro, Toei, Tobu)">Asakusa Station</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C5%8Dka_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station has two elevated <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platforms</a> with four tracks abutting the platforms, going in opposite directions. The station building is located underneath the platforms. Tracks one and six are used for through traffic and are not served by platforms, so the platform numbering starts with platform 2.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C5%8Dka_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station opened on 27 August 1899.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-terada2013_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-terada2013-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 17 March 2012, station numbering was introduced on all Tobu lines, with Sōka Station becoming "TS-16".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tobu20120209_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-tobu20120209-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Passenger_statistics">Passenger statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C5%8Dka_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Passenger statistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In fiscal 2014, the station was used by an average of 82,844 passengers daily.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
