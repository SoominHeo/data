<p><b>Sokollu Mehmed Pasha</b> (<a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Turkish_language" title="Ottoman Turkish language">Ottoman Turkish</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ota-Arab" xml:lang="ota-Arab">سوکلو محمد پاشا</span>‎, <i>Sokollu Mehmed Paşa</i> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Modern_Turkish" title="Modern Turkish">modern Turkish</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serbo-Croatian_language" title="Serbo-Croatian language">Serbo-Croatian</a>: <span lang="sh" xml:lang="sh"><i>Mehmed-paša Sokolović</i></span>/Мехмед-паша Соколовић, <a href="/wiki/Arebica" title="Arebica">Arebica</a>: مەحمەد-پاشا سۉقۉلۉوٖىݘ, <small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Serbo-Croatian" title="Help:IPA for Serbo-Croatian">[měxmet pâʃa sokǒːloʋit͡ɕ]</a></span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> 1506 – 11 October 1579) was an <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> statesman. Born in <a href="/wiki/Sanjak_of_Bosnia" title="Sanjak of Bosnia">Ottoman Bosnia</a> into a <a href="/wiki/Serbian_Orthodox_Church" title="Serbian Orthodox Church">Serbian Orthodox</a> family, Mehmed was taken away at an early age as part of the Ottoman <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dev%C5%9Firme" title="Devşirme">devşirme</a> system of collection of Christian boys to be raised to serve as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Janissary" title="Janissary">janissary</a>. These boys were forcefully converted into Islam, raised and educated, but in turn were offered great opportunities to excel and to rise within the Ottoman imperial system; Sokollu Mehmed Pasha is one of many that made the best of their careers (reaching Grand Vizier rank).</p>
<p>He rose through the ranks of the Ottoman imperial system, eventually holding positions as commander of the imperial guard (1543–1546), <a href="/wiki/List_of_Kapudan_Pashas" title="List of Kapudan Pashas">High Admiral of the Fleet</a> (1546–1551), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Governor-General" title="Governor-General">Governor-General</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rumelia" title="Rumelia">Rumelia</a> (1551–1555), Third <a href="/wiki/Vizier" title="Vizier">Vizier</a> (1555–1561), Second Vizier (1561–1565), and as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Vizier_of_the_Ottoman_Empire" title="Grand Vizier of the Ottoman Empire">Grand Vizier</a> (1565–1579, for a total of 14 years, three months, 17 days) under three sultans: <a href="/wiki/Suleiman_the_Magnificent" title="Suleiman the Magnificent">Suleiman the Magnificent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Selim_II" title="Selim II">Selim II</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Murad_III" title="Murad III">Murad III</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Imamovic_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Imamovic-3">[3]</a></sup> He was assassinated in 1579, ending his near 15-year rule as <i>de facto</i> ruler of the Ottoman Empire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Imamovic_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Imamovic-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sokollu_Mehmed_Pasha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sokollu_Mehmed_Pasha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sokollu's birth name was probably <i>Bajica</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kocan_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kocan-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Samarcic_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Samarcic-5">[5]</a></sup> and he was of ethnic <a href="/wiki/Serbs" title="Serbs">Serb</a> origin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMalcolm199446_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMalcolm199446-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHastings1997132_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHastings1997132-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-veinstein_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-veinstein-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He was said to be born into a modest shepherd family,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Samarcic_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Samarcic-5">[5]</a></sup> adherent to the <a href="/wiki/Serbian_Orthodox_Church" title="Serbian Orthodox Church">Serbian Orthodox Church</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> in or near <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sokolovi%C4%87i,_Rudo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sokolovići, Rudo (page does not exist)">Sokolovići</a> (tr. <i>Sokol</i>) in the vicinity of modern-day <a href="/wiki/Rudo" title="Rudo">Rudo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Imamovic_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Imamovic-3">[3]</a></sup> "Sokollu" is a <a href="/wiki/Demonym" title="Demonym">demonym</a>, derived from his place of birth (<a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a> <i>-lu</i> indicates <i>from a place</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Imamovic_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Imamovic-3">[3]</a></sup> His father was named Dimitrije.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Imamovic_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-Imamovic-3">[3]</a></sup> He had two brothers and a sister, who married the older brother of <a href="/wiki/H%C3%BCseyin_Pasha_Boljani%C4%87" title="Hüseyin Pasha Boljanić">Hüseyin Pasha Boljanić</a>, as well as at least one uncle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Imamovic_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-Imamovic-3">[3]</a></sup> However, details about his family and relations are disputed on two major counts. One is his relationship to <a href="/wiki/Makarije_Sokolovi%C4%87" title="Makarije Sokolović">Makarije Sokolović</a>. Traditionally identified as his brother, today some historians consider him to have been either a nephew or distant relative.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Imamovic_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-Imamovic-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kocan_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Kocan-4">[4]</a></sup> The second is the matter of Mehmed's uncle. By some accounts, his uncle was a monk at the <a href="/wiki/Mile%C5%A1eva_monastery" title="Mileševa monastery">Mileševa monastery</a> who had his two nephews, Bajica and Makarije (taken to be brothers according to this view), educated there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kocan_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Kocan-4">[4]</a></sup> Other sources suggest that his uncle converted to <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> early.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Imamovic_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-Imamovic-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1516, an Ottoman expedition gathering up boys as part of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dev%C5%9Firme" title="Devşirme">devşirme</a></i> system reached Sokol. According to folklore, Bajica was either simply taken from his father's home or, due to a reputation as a gifted child, specifically chosen by the commanding Yeşilce Mehmed Bey. The story further holds that his uncle unsuccessfully appealed to the authorities, even offering gold for the child's return.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kocan_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Kocan-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Janissary_education">Janissary education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sokollu_Mehmed_Pasha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Janissary education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He was imposed on the name of Mehmed and, first in <a href="/wiki/Edirne" title="Edirne">Edirne</a> and then in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Constantinople</a>, received a thorough Ottoman education as a recruit, first as an apprentice <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Janissary" title="Janissary">Janissary</a> (in <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a> <i>Acemi Oğlan</i>); then in the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enderun" title="Enderun">Enderun</a></i> or palace school in <a href="/wiki/Topkap%C4%B1_Palace" title="Topkapı Palace">Topkapı Palace</a>.</p>
<p>As proclaimed in <a href="/wiki/Baghdad" title="Baghdad">Baghdad</a> on 13 March 1535, Mehmed was sent to be one of the seven retainers of the Imperial Treasurer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iskender_%C3%87elebi" title="Iskender Çelebi">Iskender Çelebi</a>. Upon Iskender's death, Mehmed returned to Constantinople. In addition to <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Turkish_language" title="Ottoman Turkish language">Turkish</a>, he spoke <a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a> (<a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a>), <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Venetian-Italian</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin language</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sokollu_Mehmed_Pasha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mehmed in 1541 first became an Imperial Chamberlain and then the head of the Sultan's <a href="/wiki/Squire" title="Squire">squires</a>. In these positions he became very close to Sultan <a href="/wiki/Suleiman_the_Magnificent" title="Suleiman the Magnificent">Suleiman the Magnificent</a> and learned from him.</p>
<p>As a soldier, Mehmed excelled at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Moh%C3%A1cs" title="Battle of Mohács">Battle of Mohács</a> and the first <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Vienna" title="Siege of Vienna">Siege of Vienna</a>. In 1546 the <a href="/wiki/Kapudan_Pasha" title="Kapudan Pasha">Kapudan Pasha</a> <a href="/wiki/Hayreddin_Barbarossa" title="Hayreddin Barbarossa">Hayreddin Barbarossa</a> died and Mehmed was appointed his successor. In this capacity he was present at the naval expedition against Trablus (present-day <a href="/wiki/Tripoli" title="Tripoli">Tripoli</a> in <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a>). During his five years in this position, Mehmed Pasha greatly strengthened the arsenal of the naval fleet.</p>
<p>Mehmed became <i><a href="/wiki/Beylerbey" title="Beylerbey">Beylerbey</a></i> (Governor-General) of <a href="/wiki/Rumelia" title="Rumelia">Rumelia</a> in 1551, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Sofia" title="Sofia">Sofia</a>. While he was visiting the area of his birth, his mother recognized him by the <a href="/wiki/Birthmark" title="Birthmark">birthmark</a> on his face and embraced her child for the first time in more than thirty years.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>After the death of <a href="/wiki/John_Z%C3%A1polya" title="John Zápolya">John Zápolya</a>, king of <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> as an Ottoman vassal, in 1540, <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_I,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Ferdinand I, Holy Roman Emperor">Ferdinand I, Holy Roman Emperor</a> looked to annex Zápolya's lands (the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Hungarian_Kingdom" title="Eastern Hungarian Kingdom">Eastern Hungarian Kingdom</a>). The Hungarian diet had elected infant <a href="/wiki/John_Sigismund_Z%C3%A1polya" title="John Sigismund Zápolya">John Sigismund Zápolya</a>, the son of Zápolya and <a href="/wiki/Isabella_Jagiellon" title="Isabella Jagiellon">Isabella Jagiellon</a>, as King of Hungary, which broke the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Nagyv%C3%A1rad" title="Treaty of Nagyvárad">Treaty of Nagyvárad</a>, and Ferdinand I invaded Hungary. Isabella struggled to rule Hungary as <a href="/wiki/Queen_dowager" title="Queen dowager">Queen dowager</a> for her son. Frater <a href="/wiki/George_Martinuzzi" title="George Martinuzzi">George Martinuzzi</a>, appointed by John as regent, opposed her (he would later be created a Cardinal as reward for his accomplishments in this conflict). Ferdinand I sent mercenary leader (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Condottiero" title="Condottiero">condottiero</a></i>) Bartolomeo Castoldo with more than 7,000 mercenaries who beat a contingent led by <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A9ter_Petrovics" title="Péter Petrovics">Péter Petrovics</a>, killing more than 2,500 of them, near <a href="/wiki/Csan%C3%A1d" title="Csanád">Csanád</a>. The Sultan immediately ordered Sokollu Mehmed Pasha to move into <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>, so he assembled an army of 90,000 soldiers and fifty-four cannons and also summoned the <a href="/wiki/Pasha" title="Pasha">pashas</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sanjak_of_Smederevo" title="Sanjak of Smederevo">Smederevo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sanjak_of_Vidin" title="Sanjak of Vidin">Vidin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sanjak_of_Nicopolis" title="Sanjak of Nicopolis">Nicopolis</a>. When his forces reached <a href="/wiki/Stari_Slankamen" title="Stari Slankamen">Slankamen</a> in <a href="/wiki/Syrmia" title="Syrmia">Syrmia</a>, George Martinuzzi begged Mehmed not to attack Transylvania, arguing that it had remained in the possession of the Sultan. Mehmed rejected negotiation proposals, led Ottoman forces into Transylvania and soon captured 16 cities, including <a href="/wiki/Be%C4%8Dej" title="Bečej">Bečej</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zrenjanin" title="Zrenjanin">Becskerek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Csan%C3%A1d" title="Csanád">Csanád</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lipova,_Arad" title="Lipova, Arad">Lippa</a>. In this campaign, Sokollu won over to his side local Serb-manned garrisons by pointing out to his common ethnicity with them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-veinstein_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-veinstein-8">[8]</a></sup> Martinuzzi responded by raising a rebellion in Transylvania, mustering one soldier from every household. Mehmed had to fall back and once again laid siege to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temesv%C3%A1r" title="Temesvár">Temesvár</a> on 14 October with the main part of his army and 50 cannons. Mehmed demanded surrender, but the city's commander, István Losonci, replied with a recommendation for Mehmed's return to Rumelia.</p>
<p>Mehmed besieged the city until 28 October but could not seize it. Retreating to <a href="/wiki/Belgrade" title="Belgrade">Belgrade</a>, he initiated peace negotiations with the Monk-Viceroy. Martinuzzi was assassinated on 17 December 1551, and peace talks ended. Sokollu Mehmed renewed his military campaign in 1552, seizing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temesv%C3%A1r" title="Temesvár">Temesvár</a> (see <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Temesv%C3%A1r_(1552)" title="Siege of Temesvár (1552)">Siege of Temesvár (1552)</a>), <a href="/wiki/Holl%C3%B3k%C5%91" title="Hollókő">Hollókő</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buj%C3%A1k" title="Buják">Buják</a>, <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9ts%C3%A1g" title="Rétság">Rétság</a>, <a href="/wiki/Balassagyarmat" title="Balassagyarmat">Balassagyarmat</a>, the whole of <a href="/wiki/Banat" title="Banat">Banat</a> and <a href="/wiki/Szolnok" title="Szolnok">Szolnok</a>. Sokollu Mehmed's forces then joined with those of <a href="/wiki/Kara_Ahmed_Pasha" title="Kara Ahmed Pasha">Ahmet Pasha</a> advancing towards <a href="/wiki/Eger" title="Eger">Eger</a>. Mehmed's army assembled on the Hill of Egid but <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Eger_(1552)" title="Siege of Eger (1552)">could not take the city itself</a>.</p>
<p>In 1532, Sultan Suleiman <a href="/wiki/Ottoman%E2%80%93Safavid_War_(1532%E2%80%9355)" title="Ottoman–Safavid War (1532–55)">had declared war</a> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Safavid_Persia" title="Safavid Persia">Safavid Persia</a> following two decades of peace after the climactic <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Chaldiran" title="Battle of Chaldiran">Battle of Chaldiran</a>, when the Persian Shah <a href="/wiki/Tahmasp_I" title="Tahmasp I">Tahmasp</a> wanted to take advantage of the Sultan's preoccupation with Hungary and started making armed incursions into Ottoman territory. Sokollu Mehmed was dispatched to spend the winter of 1553/1554 in <a href="/wiki/Tokat" title="Tokat">Tokat</a> to take charge of the final stages of the war against Persia. In June 1554, Mehmed Pasha and the Rumelian troops joined the Sultan's army and took part in the <a href="/wiki/Safavid_Campaign_(1554%E2%80%9355)" title="Safavid Campaign (1554–55)">Safavid Campaign (1554–55)</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vizier">Vizier</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sokollu_Mehmed_Pasha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Vizier">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Third_Vizier">Third Vizier</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sokollu_Mehmed_Pasha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Third Vizier">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Impressed by Sokollu Mehmed's skills, the Sultan made him the Third Vizier in 1555 and he was given a place in the Imperial Council (<i><a href="/wiki/Divan" title="Divan">Divan</a></i>). His position as Governor-General of Rumelia was given to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serb_clans" title="Serb clans">Herzegovinian</a> Janissary <i><a href="/wiki/Agha_(Ottoman_Empire)" title="Agha (Ottoman Empire)">agha</a></i>, Pertev Pasha, an old companion of Mehmed's from when they had both served under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iskender_%C3%87elebi" title="Iskender Çelebi">Iskender Çelebi</a>.</p>
<p>Almost immediately Sokollu Mehmed had to quell a rebellion around <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thessalonika" title="Thessalonika">Salonica</a>, led by Mustafa Bey, who pretended to be the Sultan's late son Mustafa. Sokollu Mehmed took 4,000 horsemen and 3,000 janissaries and quelled the rebellion. Mustafa Bey was hanged.</p>
<p>Mehmed's brother, Topuzli-Makarije, became a monk of the Serb <a href="/wiki/Hilandar" title="Hilandar">Chilandar Monastery</a> on <a href="/wiki/Mount_Athos" title="Mount Athos">Mount Athos</a>. Makarije Sokolović paid a visit to his brother's palace in Constantinople in 1557, where the two brothers discussed the possibility of re-establishing the <a href="/wiki/Autocephaly" title="Autocephaly">autocephaly</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Serbian_Orthodox_Church" title="Serbian Orthodox Church">Serbian Orthodox Church</a>. Later that same year, Sokollu Mehmed issued an edict (<i><a href="/wiki/Firman" title="Firman">firman</a></i>) declaring the restoration of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serbian_Patriarchate" title="Serbian Patriarchate">Serbian Patriarchate</a>, with Makarije Sokolović as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serbian_Patriarch" title="Serbian Patriarch">Serbian Patriarch</a> Makarije I. The edict also guaranteed the rights and religious freedom of all inhabitants of the Ottoman Empire.</p>
<p>When the former Grand Vizier Ahmet Pasha was deposed and hanged, he was replaced by <a href="/wiki/R%C3%BCstem_Pasha" title="Rüstem Pasha">Rüstem Pasha</a>, who had numerous enemies. One of them was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lala_Kara_Mustafa_Pasha" title="Lala Kara Mustafa Pasha">Lala Mustafa</a>, who instigated the Sultan's third son, Bayezid, then <i>Beylerbey</i> of <a href="/wiki/Karaman" title="Karaman">Karaman</a>, to raise a rebellion against his brother and heir-apparent <a href="/wiki/Selim_II" title="Selim II">Selim</a>. Sokollu Mehmed mustered an army and went to <a href="/wiki/Konya" title="Konya">Konya</a>, where he decisively defeated Bayezid's forces in May 1559. Bayezid fled to Persia. Sokollu Mehmed remained in Asia and spent the winter negotiating with the Persian Shah regarding Bayezid's extradition. After long negotiations, the Shah handed over Bayezid and his four sons, who were subsequently executed.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_Vizier">Second Vizier</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sokollu_Mehmed_Pasha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Second Vizier">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Grand Vizier Rüstem Pasha died in 1561 and was succeeded by the Second Vizier, <a href="/wiki/Semiz_Ali_Pasha" title="Semiz Ali Pasha">Semiz Ali Pasha</a>. Sokollu Mehmed Pasha in turn became Second Vizier, while Pertev Pasha became Third Vizier.</p>
<p>On 17 August 1562, Sokollu Mehmed married Sultan Suleiman's granddaughter – Prince Selim's daughter – Ismihan Sultan (some sources read her name as Esma Han Sultan) . Mehmed spent the following years in peace, governing and administrating the realm.</p>
<p>In 1564, Mehmed's nephew, Sokollu Mustafa Bey, became Viceroy of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eyalet_of_Bosnia" title="Eyalet of Bosnia">Eyalet of Bosnia</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Grand_Vizier">Grand Vizier</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sokollu_Mehmed_Pasha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Grand Vizier">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 1565, Grand Vizier Semiz Ali Pasha died. Sultan Suleiman had much confidence in Sokollu Mehmed Pasha and promoted him to this position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETezcan201098_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETezcan201098-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="War_with_the_Habsburgs">War with the Habsburgs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sokollu_Mehmed_Pasha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: War with the Habsburgs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>In late 1565 and early 1566, tensions between the Holy Roman Emperor <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Maximilian II, Holy Roman Emperor">Maximilian II</a> and Sultan Suleiman grew. Maximilian wanted the cities previously taken by <a href="/wiki/Telli_Hasan_Pasha" title="Telli Hasan Pasha">Telli Hasan Pasha</a> restored to him. When negotiations failed, Maximilian declared war and Grand Vizier Sokollu Mehmed Pasha ordered his nephew, Sokollu Mustafa Bey of Bosnia, to advance against Maximilian. Mustafa managed to capture the cities of Krupa and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dvor_na_Uni" title="Dvor na Uni">Dvor na Uni</a>. The Sultan immediately declared war against the Holy Roman Empire, and Sokollu Mehmed began the preparations for the army's advance. The Grand Vizier went ahead, preparing for the arrival of the Sultan, who was leading the main part of the Ottoman forces. After 50 days, they arrived in Belgrade.</p>
<p>Passing through <a href="/wiki/Zemun" title="Zemun">Zemun</a>, one part of the army crossed <a href="/wiki/Vara%C5%BEdin" title="Varaždin">Varaždin</a> and struck <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Egar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Egar (page does not exist)">Egar</a> before proceeding towards <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nikola_%C5%A0ubi%C4%87_Zrinski" title="Nikola Šubić Zrinski">Nikola Šubić Zrinski</a> (Miklós Zrínyi) had defeated the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sanjakbey" title="Sanjakbey">sanjakbey</a></i> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tirhal_Mohammed&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tirhal Mohammed (page does not exist)">Tirhal Mohammed</a>, executing him and his son, and capturing 17,000 <a href="/wiki/Ducat" title="Ducat">ducats</a>. This incurred the Sultan's wrath, and he dispatched Sokollu Mehmed's forces to besiege <a href="/wiki/Szigetv%C3%A1r" title="Szigetvár">Szigetvár</a>, while Suleiman remained in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Harsang&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Harsang (page does not exist)">Harsang</a>. The commander of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Budin" title="Budin">Budin</a>, Arslan Pasha, lost the cities of <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A1rpalota" title="Várpalota">Várpalota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Veszpr%C3%A9m" title="Veszprém">Veszprém</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tata,_Hungary" title="Tata, Hungary">Tata</a>. The Sultan sent a platoon of fifteen troopers to bring him Arslan Pasha's head, but Arslan had already left his forces three days earlier and was on his way to the Sultan. The Sultan showed Sokollu Mehmed a letter in which Arslan had insulted him, and on 3 August, when Arslan reported to Mehmed's tent with 15 heavily armed horsemen, Sokollu Mehmed criticized Arslan for his conduct, accused him of treason and stripped him of his post, giving it instead to his nephew Sokollu Mustafa Bey<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<p>The Sultan arrived with Sokollu Mehmed's sons, Kurt Bey and Hasan Bey, at <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A9cs" title="Pécs">Pécs</a>. Finally, the large Ottoman force, which numbered between 100,000<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shelton_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shelton-15">[15]</a></sup> and 300,000 soldiers and 300 cannons, laid siege to <a href="/wiki/Szigetv%C3%A1r" title="Szigetvár">Szigetvár</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Szigetv%C3%A1r" title="Battle of Szigetvár">Battle of Szigetvár</a> was an Ottoman victory, with heavy losses on both sides. Both commanders died during the battle: while Zrinsky was killed in the final charge, Suleiman the Magnificent died in his tent from natural causes, before the Turks achieved victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Turnbull.2C_57_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Turnbull.2C_57-16">[16]</a></sup> According to Robert William Fraser, more than 10,000 large cannonballs where shot into the fortress during the siege.</p>
<p>Sokollu Mehmed Pasha had all witnesses to the Sultan's death executed, and announced that Suleiman was too sick to perform his duties and that he would be healing in Szigetvár, while he would be acting on the Sultan's behalf<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. Sokollu Mehmed rewarded those involved in the capture of Szigetvár and increased the soldiers' wages. He sent a part of the army to capture <a href="/wiki/Bab%C3%B3csa" title="Babócsa">Babócsa</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tartars" title="Tartars">Tartars</a>, however, spread the news of the Sultan's death, and Sokollu Mustafa Bey wrote to Prince Selim about his father's death. Selim marched immediately towards <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Srem" title="Srem">Srem</a>. Upon his arrival in <a href="/wiki/Vukovar" title="Vukovar">Vukovar</a>, Sokollu Mehmed wrote him that it would be best if he went to Belgrade to greet his army for a more formal and effective take-over of the Empire. Selim returned to Belgrade, and Mehmed ordered the army to march towards the town. Forty days after the Sultan's death, in October 1566, the army set out for Belgrade. At the fourth stop on the way to Belgrade, forty-eight days after Suleiman's death, Sokollu Mehmed announced the Sultan's death ceremonially, during the traditional reading of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Koran" title="Koran">Koran</a>. Sokollu Mehmed had Suleiman's body embalmed and ordered the army to proceed to meet the new Sultan in Belgrade. After three marches, the army arrived in <a href="/wiki/Sremska_Mitrovica" title="Sremska Mitrovica">Sremska Mitrovica</a>. Mehmed reminded Selim to send gifts to the viziers, pashas and the army, but Selim's advisors convinced the new Sultan not to do so. Sokollu Mehmed went to Belgrade and swore allegiance to Selim II as his Sultan, and Selim confirmed him as his Grand Vizier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETezcan201098_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETezcan201098-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Expecting a mutiny among the military in the capital, Sokollu Mehmed had Suleiman's body sent to Constantinople to restore order amongst the janissaries and other officials, who now demanded more compensation for their past efforts. In Belgrade, Sultan Selim II called a council, as even some of his closest officials were openly mocking him<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. Sokollu Mehmed assured him that he would manage everything effectively, and distributed gifts to the troops, rewarding them handsomely to regain their loyalty<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<p>On the fifth day of their stay in Belgrade, the Sultan, Sokollu Mehmed and the army departed for Constantinople. Before they managed to return to the Empire's capital, a mutiny broke out and the road to the city was blocked, and Sokollu Mehmed and Ahmed Pasha had to bribe their way into the city. Order was restored after Sokollu Mehmed convinced the Sultan to promise to send handsome gifts and higher wages to the janissaries. The next morning, each janissary was given standard pay of 40 ducats and an additional 20 ducats as an accession bonus. Soon, the other branches of the military, the <a href="/wiki/Spahi" title="Spahi">spahis</a> and mercenaries, demanded higher wages as well. Mehmed arrested and replaced their <i>aghas</i> at once, finally stopping all dissent<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<p>Two years after Selim's accession, on 17 February 1568, Sokollu Mehmed succeeded in concluding at <a href="/wiki/Edirne" title="Edirne">Edirne</a> a peace treaty with Emperor Maximilian II, whereby the Emperor agreed to pay an annual "honary present" of 30,000 ducats.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Wars_with_Russia.2C_Venice_and_the_Holy_League">Wars with Russia, Venice and the Holy League</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sokollu_Mehmed_Pasha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Wars with Russia, Venice and the Holy League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Mehmed Pasha had little success against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muscovy" title="Muscovy">Russia</a>, and the first encounter between the Ottoman Empire and her future northern rival presaged the disaster to come. A plan had been devised at Constantinople for connecting the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volga" title="Volga">Volga</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Don_River,_Russia" title="Don River, Russia">Don</a> by a canal, and in the summer of 1569 a large force of janissaries and cavalry was sent to lay siege to <a href="/wiki/Astrakhan" title="Astrakhan">Astrakhan</a> and begin the canal works, while an Ottoman fleet besieged <a href="/wiki/Azov" title="Azov">Azov</a>. However, a sortie of the garrison of Astrakhan drove back the besiegers. A Russian army of 15,000 men attacked and scattered the workmen and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tatar" title="Tatar">Tatar</a> force sent for their protection, and the Ottoman fleet was destroyed by a storm. Early in 1570 the ambassadors of <a href="/wiki/Ivan_the_Terrible" title="Ivan the Terrible">Ivan the Terrible</a> concluded at Constantinople a treaty which restored friendly relations between the Sultan and the <a href="/wiki/Tsar" title="Tsar">Tsar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Selim II was a very weak ruler, and as Grand Vizier, Sokollu Mehmed held the real power in the administration, thanks to the reforms of Selim's predecessor, Suleiman the Magnificent. Although the government was weakening, Sokollu Mehmed Paşa managed to expand the borders of the Ottoman Empire greatly. In 1570 he dispatched <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sinan_Pasha" title="Sinan Pasha">Sinan Pasha</a> to conquer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabia" title="Arabia">Arabia</a>. Sinan Pasha solemnly declared the reign of Sultan Selim II in <a href="/wiki/Mecca" title="Mecca">Mecca</a> upon finishing his military campaign in <a href="/wiki/Hejaz" title="Hejaz">Hejaz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yemen" title="Yemen">Yemen</a>.</p>
<p>In 1571-1572, on the order Mehmed's wife Ismihan Sultan (or Esma Han Sultan), the famous architect <a href="/wiki/Mimar_Sinan" title="Mimar Sinan">Mimar Sinan</a> built the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sokollu_Mehmed_Pa%C5%9Fa_Mosque" title="Sokollu Mehmed Paşa Mosque">Sokollu Mehmed Paşa Mosque</a>, which an authoritative guide to Constantinople states to be "The most beautiful of the smaller mosques in Istanbul, a minor masterpiece by Sinan".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-freely_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-freely-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the rule of Sokollu Mehmed Pasha as Grand Vizier, the Ottoman navy and army <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turkish%E2%80%93Venetian_War_(1570%E2%80%931573)" title="Turkish–Venetian War (1570–1573)">took</a> <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a> in 1571 from <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Venice" title="Republic of Venice">Venice</a>. The administration of Cyprus was given to Mehmed's old friend, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arab" title="Arab">Arab</a> Ahmed Pasha. The invasion of Cyprus led to the formation of a so-called <a href="/wiki/Holy_League_(1571)" title="Holy League (1571)">Holy League</a>, comprising the <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">Pope</a>, <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Spain" title="Habsburg Spain">Spain with Naples and Sicily</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Venice" title="Republic of Venice">Republic of Venice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Genoa" title="Republic of Genoa">Genoa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Tuscany" title="Grand Duchy of Tuscany">Tuscany</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Knights_Hospitaller" title="Knights Hospitaller">Knights of Malta</a>. On 7 October 1571, the coalition's fleet under the command of <a href="/wiki/John_of_Austria" title="John of Austria">Don Juan of Austria</a> decisively defeated the Ottoman fleet under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M%C3%BCezzinzade_Ali_Pasha" title="Müezzinzade Ali Pasha">Müezzinzade Ali Pasha</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lepanto_(1571)" title="Battle of Lepanto (1571)">Battle of Lepanto</a>.</p>
<p>Sokollu Mehmed Pasha immediately ensured that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Piyale_Pasha" title="Piyale Pasha">Piyale Pasha</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ulu%C3%A7_Ali_Reis" title="Uluç Ali Reis">Uluç Ali Reis</a>, the new Grand Admiral, were provided with all the necessary means and resources to rebuild the Empire's shattered fleet. By July 1572 the Ottoman fleet already numbered 250 fully equipped warships "including eight of the largest capital ships ever seen in the Mediterranean".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> It is reported in Turkish chronicles that Sokollu Mehmed Pasha had said to the Venetian Ambassador "By conquering Cyprus we have cut off one of your arms; at Lepanto by defeating our navy you have only shaved off our beard. However, you know that a cut-off arm cannot be replaced but shaved-off beard grows thicker." Indeed, the Holy League ships had to retire to ports and Ottoman naval supremacy in the Mediterranean was restored. The new Ottoman Navy that started a naval expedition in summer of 1573 under Uluç Ali Reis found no rivals in the Mediterranean and ravaged the coasts of Sicily and southern Italy and in 1574 it captured <a href="/wiki/Tunis" title="Tunis">Tunis</a> from the Hafsids, who for some time had been supported by Spanish troops, thus restoring Ottoman domination of Western Mediterranean.</p>
<p>On 3 March 1573, Venetian Republic signed a new peace treaty with the Ottomans under Mehmed Pasha, thereby bringing the Holy League to an end, accepting the loss of Cyprus and increasing the tribute payments. He also extended for eight more years the peace treaty with the Holy Roman Empire and maintained good relations with <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_France" title="Kingdom of France">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Lithuanian_Commonwealth" title="Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth">Poland-Lithuania</a> and Russia. He was preparing for a fresh attack on Venice when the Sultan's death on 12 December 1574 cut short his plans.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Final_years">Final years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sokollu_Mehmed_Pasha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Final years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Sokollu Mehmed Pasha's wealth reached its peak around 1573, when the value of his personal property (cash, goods, accounts, objects) amounted to 18 million ducats. Mehmed received the standard Grand Vizier's wage of 20 ducats every day. His wealth increased greatly through gifts and taxes of Ottoman officials: anyone who became a vizier had to pay Mehmed Pasha 50,000-60,000 ducats, and every Governor-General had to pay 15,000-20,000 or even sometimes 30,000-40,000 ducats upon ascending to the office. The provincial governor of Egypt at <a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">Cairo</a> alone dispatched 100,000 ducats to the Grand Vizier every year.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On 30 August 1574, Grand Vizier Sokollu Mehmed Pasha installed his nephew <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Antonije_Sokolovi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Antonije Sokolović (page does not exist)">Antonije Sokolović</a>, then the <a href="/wiki/Bishop" title="Bishop">Metropolitan</a> of <a href="/wiki/Herzegovina" title="Herzegovina">Hum</a>, as the new <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Eastern Orthodox</a> <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Ohrid" title="Archbishop of Ohrid">Archbishop of Ohrid</a>. On 23 October the same year, upon the death of Patriarch Makarije's death, Antonije became the new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serbian_Patriarch" title="Serbian Patriarch">Serbian Patriarch</a>. Antonije died soon, in 1575, and was replaced by yet another one of Mehmed's nephews, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gerasim_Sokolovi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gerasim Sokolović (page does not exist)">Gerasim Sokolović</a>.</p>
<p>When Sultan Selim II died, Sokollu Mehmed Pasha again kept this secret until Selim's oldest son Murad arrived from his governor's post in <a href="/wiki/Manisa" title="Manisa">Manisa</a>. Sokollu Mehmed Pasha acknowledged the new Sultan, <a href="/wiki/Murad_III" title="Murad III">Murad III</a>, and remained Grand Vizier, but now he had to cope with the rising political influence of the palace women, first with Sultan's mother <a href="/wiki/Nurbanu_Sultan" title="Nurbanu Sultan">Nurbanu Sultan</a> and then his wife, of Venetian origin, <a href="/wiki/Safiye_Sultan" title="Safiye Sultan">Safiye Sultan</a>. Murad III gradually soured on Sokollu Mehmed's overwhelming power within the Empire, and the Grand Vizier's influence declined.</p>
<p>Sokollu Mehmed Pasha was involved in the succession disputes of the Polish Crown in 1576 and 1577, but this did not reach greater measures.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Sokollu Mehmed signed numerous treaties of friendship with Venice, <a href="/wiki/Florence" title="Florence">Florence</a>, Spain, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. He also managed to force a number of European states to pay tribute: Austria paid 9,000 ducats; Transylvania 3,000; <a href="/wiki/Wallachia" title="Wallachia">Wallachia</a> 7,000; <a href="/wiki/Moldavia" title="Moldavia">Moldavia</a> 3,000. Eventually, even Venice had to pay him 4,000 ducats annually. This altogether gave him an annual income of 31,000 gold ducats.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Mehmed was initially known to be opposed to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ottoman%E2%80%93Safavid_War_(1578%E2%80%931590)" title="Ottoman–Safavid War (1578–1590)">war with Persia</a>, which began in 1578, but was overruled upon eventually, amongst one of the reasons being the constant urgings by Sokollu Mehmed to take advantage of the Ottomans neighboring rival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESicker19862-3_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESicker19862-3-1">[1]</a></sup> Sultan Murad III time afterwards, began to limit his Grand Vizier's powers by slowly removing his allies from high offices. The state secretary Feridun, an old companion of Sokollu mehmed's since the siege of Szigetvár, was sent to Belgrade, away from Constantinople. Mehmed's faithful Arab friend, the Governor-General of Cyprus, was lynched by mutinous soldiers. Mehmed's greatest rivals, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hamid_Efendi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hamid Efendi (page does not exist)">Hamid Efendi</a> and Piyale Pasha, arranged the execution of the Grand Vizier's Greek protege, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Kantakouzenos_%C5%9Eeytano%C4%9Flu" title="Michael Kantakouzenos Şeytanoğlu">Michael Kantakouzenos</a>. On 10 October 1578, Sokollu Mustafa Bey, Sokollu Mehmed Pasha's nephew and Governor-General of Budin, was assassinated. On the anniversary of this day, on 10 October 1579, Sokollu Mehmed had his servant Hasan Bey read to him about the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Kosovo" title="Battle of Kosovo">Battle of Kosovo</a>.</p>
<p>The next day, on 11 October, a mentally unstable <a href="/wiki/Dervish" title="Dervish">dervish</a> demanded to see the Grand Vizier. Sokollu Mehmed Pasha allowed the dervish to enter his quarters, whereupon the latter took out a knife and stabbed the Grand Vizier. After three hours, Sokollu Mehmed Pasha died. This ended his near 15-year rule as <i>de facto</i> ruler of the Ottoman Empire. There are some<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (March 2013)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> who claim that the assassin was a janissary in disguise in employment of Safiye Sultan, the wife of Murad III. Also, some sources<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (March 2013)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> claim that Sokollu Mehmed was a target of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hashshashin" title="Hashshashin">Hashshashin</a> agent, as he was opposed to war with Persia where this order was stationed, which was not in their interest, although this is a very controversial claim as this order was destroyed by <a href="/wiki/Mongols" title="Mongols">Mongols</a> long time before.</p>
<p>He is buried at his complex, <i>Sokollu Mehmed Paşa Külliyesi</i> at the back of <a href="/wiki/Ey%C3%BCp_Sultan_Mosque" title="Eyüp Sultan Mosque">Eyüp Mosque</a>, in Istanbul, at the <i>Sokollu Mehmed Paşa Türbe</i> built by famous architect <a href="/wiki/Mimar_Sinan" title="Mimar Sinan">Mimar Sinan</a> for him c. 1572. His wife Ismihan (or Esma Han) is buried near him and in the little garden of the Türbe are buried the family and descendants of Sokollu Mehmed Pasha.</p>
<p>After his death Sultan Murad III changed grand vezirs ten times in sixteen years. These frequent changes in government were part of the general instability in the Ottoman government that followed the death of Sokollu Mehmet Pasha, evidence of a decline in the empire that he had at its pinnacle while he was in office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFreely201680_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFreely201680-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Heritage_and_legacy">Heritage and legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sokollu_Mehmed_Pasha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Heritage and legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Na_drini_cuprija.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Na_drini_cuprija.JPG/250px-Na_drini_cuprija.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Na_drini_cuprija.JPG/375px-Na_drini_cuprija.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Na_drini_cuprija.JPG/500px-Na_drini_cuprija.JPG 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Na_drini_cuprija.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Mehmed_Pa%C5%A1a_Sokolovi%C4%87_Bridge" title="Mehmed Paša Sokolović Bridge">Mehmed Paša Sokolović Bridge</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vi%C5%A1egrad" title="Višegrad">Višegrad</a>, Mehmed's most famous endowment from 1577.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Sokollu Mehmed Pasha has left numerous architecturally well known buildings in Constantinople and throughout Ottoman territories.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (October 2013)">which?</span></a></i>]</sup> Foundations of his buildings are spread over <a href="/wiki/Edirne" title="Edirne">Edirne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aleppo" title="Aleppo">Halep</a>, <a href="/wiki/Medina" title="Medina">Medina</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Be%C4%8Dkerek" title="Bečkerek">Bečkerek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgrade" title="Belgrade">Belgrade</a> and alongside<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (August 2012)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Bosnia, where he is especially remembered for his bridges. <a href="/wiki/Mecca" title="Mecca">Mecca</a> and <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Constantinople</a> alone contained a number of his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mosques" title="Mosques">mosques</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Sokollu_Mehmed_Pasha_Mosque" title="Sokollu Mehmed Pasha Mosque">Sokollu Mehmed Pasha Mosque</a> and the complex built at Kadirga district of İstanbul by architect <a href="/wiki/Mimar_Sinan" title="Mimar Sinan">Mimar Sinan</a> is considered to be the most beautiful of the smaller mosques in İstanbul.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-freely_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-freely-18">[18]</a></sup> It is known for its unusually fine ordering of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medrese" title="Medrese">medrese</a> over the entry stairs, its lofty elegant interior, the first hooded fountain, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ogival_arch#Architecture" title="Ogival arch">ogival</a> arches of the arcades, and especially, the fine and well preserved <a href="/wiki/%C4%B0znik_pottery" title="İznik pottery">Iznik</a> tiles.</p>
<p>His most renowned endowment is the eleven-arched <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vi%C5%A1egrad_bridge" title="Višegrad bridge">Višegrad bridge</a> in his hometown of <a href="/wiki/Vi%C5%A1egrad" title="Višegrad">Višegrad</a>. The construction and history of the bridge is the topic of the novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bridge_on_the_Drina" title="The Bridge on the Drina">The Bridge on the Drina</a></i> (Serbian: <i>Na Drini ćuprija - На Дрини ћуприја</i>), written by <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Literature" title="Nobel Prize in Literature">Nobel</a> laureate <a href="/wiki/Ivo_Andri%C4%87" title="Ivo Andrić">Ivo Andrić</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslav</a> novelist<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Radovan_Popovi.C4.87_page_240_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Radovan_Popovi.C4.87_page_240-22">[22]</a></sup> His life also interested the Yugoslav writer <a href="/wiki/Me%C5%A1a_Selimovi%C4%87" title="Meša Selimović">Meša Selimović</a>. There were numerous <a href="/wiki/Serbs" title="Serbs">Serbian</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Croat" title="Croat">Croatian</a> and <a href="/wiki/History_of_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="History of Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnian</a> legends about the bridge's construction. According to one, Mehmed Paşa built the bridge in his son's name. Another is the tale of its architect Rade, which is described in "Bridge on the Drina".</p>
<p>Other elements of his architectural legacy include:</p>
<ul>
<li>The <i>Azapkapi Mosque</i>, built by Sinan in 1577-1578 in the Azapkapi district of İstanbul, is considered most important Ottoman monument in Galata.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-freely_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-freely-18">[18]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <i>Sokollu Mehmed Paşa Kulliyesi</i>, built by Sinan in ca. 1572 in the Eyub district of İstanbul, is a complex including a medrese, a school and his tomb</li>
<li>The <i>Sokollu Mehmed Paşa Complex</i> built in 1549 and extended at 1569, both times by Sinan. Located on the main highway between the two Ottoman capitals of İstanbul and Edirne, at Luleburgaz, it is a complex of <a href="/wiki/Caravanserai" title="Caravanserai">caravanserai</a>, bathhouse, mosque, madrasah, a school, market streets and later, private apartments for Sultan's use.</li>
<li>Complexes built at Havsa, a city on the Istanbul-Edirne highway and in Payas, in southern Turkey near Antakya.</li>
<li>Bridges at Alpullu, Luleburgaz and Corlu, built by Sinan</li>
<li>The bridge at <a href="/wiki/Arslanagi%C4%87a_Most" title="Arslanagića Most">Arslanagića Most</a> in <a href="/wiki/Trebinje" title="Trebinje">Trebinje</a></li>
<li><i>Vizier's bridge</i> in <a href="/wiki/Podgorica" title="Podgorica">Podgorica</a></li>
<li>The bridge on <a href="/wiki/%C5%BDepa" title="Žepa">Žepa</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Goat%27s_Bridge" title="Goat's Bridge">Goat's Bridge</a> (<i>Kozija ćuprija</i>) in <a href="/wiki/Sarajevo" title="Sarajevo">Sarajevo</a></li>
<li>Public bathhouses in Havsa, Yesildirek (İstanbul), Edirne and Luleburgaz public bathhouses, built by Sinan</li>
<li>The Black Mosque in Sofia, built by Mimar Sinan during the years when Sokollu Mehmed Pasha was governor of Rumelia and later converted into a church in the 19th century</li>
<li>A Road of four paces<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (January 2014)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and a castle between <a href="/wiki/Vi%C5%A1egrad" title="Višegrad">Višegrad</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sarajevo" title="Sarajevo">Sarajevo</a>, on Glasinac. Of the castle, only a drinking-fountain remains, which is known as the <i>Mehmed Sokolović's han</i>.</li>
<li>A mosque, <a href="/wiki/Maktab" title="Maktab">maktab</a> and musafirhana (guest house) in his native village of Sokolovići</li>
</ul>
