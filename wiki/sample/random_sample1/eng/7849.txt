<p><b>Soki</b> (<a href="/wiki/Okinawan_language" title="Okinawan language">Okinawan</a>: <span lang="ryu" xml:lang="ryu">ソーキ</span> <i>sooki</i>) is a specialty of the cuisine of <a href="/wiki/Okinawa_Prefecture" title="Okinawa Prefecture">Okinawa Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. Soki are (usually boneless) stewed <a href="/wiki/Pork" title="Pork">pork</a> <a href="/wiki/Spare_ribs" title="Spare ribs">spare ribs</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Cartilage" title="Cartilage">cartilage</a> still attached. They are often served with <a href="/wiki/Okinawa_soba" title="Okinawa soba">Okinawa soba</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Dishes">Dishes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Dishes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soki_soba">Soki soba</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Soki soba">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Okinawa_soba" title="Okinawa soba">Okinawa soba</a> with stewed soki on top. The soki are prepared by first boiling to remove excess fat, then stewing in a mixture of <a href="/wiki/Awamori" title="Awamori">awamori</a> (to soften the meat), <a href="/wiki/Soy_sauce" title="Soy sauce">soy sauce</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sugar" title="Sugar">sugar</a> for three to four hours. They are then placed in a bowl of Okinawa soba.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soki_jiru">Soki jiru</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Soki jiru">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Soup containing soki, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Konbu" title="Konbu">konbu</a> (edible seaweed), and <a href="/wiki/Daikon" title="Daikon">daikon</a> (Japanese radish), with <a href="/wiki/Salt" title="Salt">salt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Soy_sauce" title="Soy sauce">soy sauce</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flavoring" title="Flavoring">flavoring</a>. The soki are first boiled to remove excess fat and drippings, then stewed in the soup until tender.</p>
<p>In mainland <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, konbu is usually used just for flavoring and then discarded<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute#Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (July 2013)">dubious</span></a> <span class="metadata">– <a href="/wiki/Talk:Soki#Dubious" title="Talk:Soki">discuss</a></span></i>]</sup>, but a distinctive feature of Okinawan soups is that the konbu is left in.</p>
