<p><b>Sophia Violet "Sophie" Crumb</b> (born September 27, 1981) is an American-French <a href="/wiki/Comics_artist" title="Comics artist">comics artist</a>. She is the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Underground_comix" title="Underground comix">underground comix</a> artists <a href="/wiki/Robert_Crumb" title="Robert Crumb">Robert Crumb</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aline_Kominsky-Crumb" title="Aline Kominsky-Crumb">Aline Kominsky-Crumb</a>.</p>
<p>Crumb was born in <a href="/wiki/Woodland,_California" title="Woodland, California">Woodland, California</a> and lived in the nearby farming town of <a href="/wiki/Winters,_California" title="Winters, California">Winters</a> with her parents until she was nine years old. In 1991, she relocated with her family to <a href="/wiki/Sauve" title="Sauve">Sauve</a>, a village in <a href="/wiki/Southern_France" title="Southern France">Southern France</a>. Her parents reported that they wanted to remove her from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Political_conservative" title="Political conservative">political conservatives</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_fundamentalist" title="Christian fundamentalist">Christian fundamentalists</a> of the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-solomon_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-solomon-1">[1]</a></sup> In a 2010 interview, Sophie told <i><a href="/wiki/The_Philadelphia_Inquirer" title="The Philadelphia Inquirer">The Philadelphia Inquirer</a></i> that her mother was afraid Sophie would "turn into a <a href="/wiki/Valley_girl" title="Valley girl">Valley girl</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deluca_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-deluca-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was after this relocation that <a href="/wiki/Terry_Zwigoff" title="Terry Zwigoff">Terry Zwigoff</a> released <i><a href="/wiki/Crumb_(film)" title="Crumb (film)">Crumb</a></i> (1994), a critically acclaimed <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">documentary film</a> about Sophie's father and their family. Zwigoff later commissioned Sophie to prepare some original drawings for inclusion in his 2001 <a href="/wiki/Comedy-drama" title="Comedy-drama">comedy-drama</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Ghost_World_(film)" title="Ghost World (film)">Ghost World</a></i>, an adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Clowes" title="Daniel Clowes">Daniel Clowes</a>' <a href="/wiki/Comics" title="Comics">comics</a> serial of the same name.</p>
<p>After completing her <a href="/wiki/Secondary_education" title="Secondary education">secondary education</a> in France, Crumb studied <a href="/wiki/Acrobatics" title="Acrobatics">acrobatics</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clowning" title="Clowning">clowning</a> at a French circus school. While living in Brooklyn in the mid-2000s she sold her comics on the street and apprenticed herself to a <a href="/wiki/Tattoo_artist" title="Tattoo artist">tattoo artist</a>. At another stage she earned a living by <a href="/wiki/Teaching_English_as_a_foreign_language" title="Teaching English as a foreign language">teaching English as a foreign language</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-decarlo_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-decarlo-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>She lives in Southern France with her husband (a construction worker) and their son, Eli, who was born in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-solomon_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-solomon-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Published_work">Published work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sophie_Crumb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Published work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When she was a child, Crumb's parents published some of her drawings in their <a href="/wiki/Comics_anthology" title="Comics anthology">comics anthology</a>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weirdo_(magazine)" title="Weirdo (magazine)">Weirdo</a></i>; she later contributed to their <a href="/wiki/Comic_book" title="Comic book">comic book</a> series <i>Dirty Laundry Comics</i>, originally published from 1977 to 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Crumb illustrated a sketchbook for the American film <i><a href="/wiki/Ghost_World_(film)" title="Ghost World (film)">Ghost World</a></i> (2001).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-decarlo_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-decarlo-4">[4]</a></sup> Her drawings were meant to reflect the personality and inner life of Enid (<a href="/wiki/Thora_Birch" title="Thora Birch">Thora Birch</a>), the film's protagonist.</p>
<p>In 2002, <a href="/wiki/Fantagraphics_Books" title="Fantagraphics Books">Fantagraphics Books</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oog_%26_Blik" title="Oog &amp; Blik">Oog &amp; Blik</a> published Crumb's first <a href="/wiki/Comic_book" title="Comic book">comic book</a>, <i>Belly Button</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-decarlo_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-decarlo-4">[4]</a></sup> followed by <i>Belly Button Comix #2</i> in 2004. She contributed multiple pieces to installments of <i><a href="/wiki/Mome_(comics)" title="Mome (comics)">Mome</a></i> published between 2005 and 2008.</p>
<p>The development of her draftsmanship and sensibilities as a graphic artist are documented in <i>Sophie Crumb: Evolution of a Crazy Artist</i> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/W.W._Norton" title="W.W. Norton">W.W. Norton</a> 2010).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deluca_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-deluca-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Her debut <a href="/wiki/Solo_show_(art_exhibition)" title="Solo show (art exhibition)">solo show</a>, which featured more than 20 drawings and <a href="/wiki/Gicl%C3%A9e" title="Giclée">giclée</a> prints, coincided with the book's publication.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The show ran from 4 November through 30 December 2010 at DCKT Contemporary, Dennis Christie and Ken Tyburski's <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_art_gallery" title="Contemporary art gallery">contemporary art gallery</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.</p>
