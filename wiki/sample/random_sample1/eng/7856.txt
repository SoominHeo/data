<p><b>Sofía Margarita Vergara Vergara</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[soˈfi.a βerˈɣaɾa]</a></span>; born July 10, 1972)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> is a Colombian-American actress and model.</p>
<p>Vergara rose to prominence while co-hosting two television shows for Spanish-language television network <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Univisi%C3%B3n" title="Univisión">Univisión</a> in the late 1990s. Her first notable acting job in English was in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Chasing_Papi" title="Chasing Papi">Chasing Papi</a></i> (2003). Subsequently, she appeared in other films, including <i><a href="/wiki/Four_Brothers_(film)" title="Four Brothers (film)">Four Brothers</a></i> (2005) and two <a href="/wiki/Tyler_Perry" title="Tyler Perry">Tyler Perry</a> films: <i><a href="/wiki/Meet_the_Browns_(film)" title="Meet the Browns (film)">Meet the Browns</a></i> (2008) and <i><a href="/wiki/Madea_Goes_to_Jail" title="Madea Goes to Jail">Madea Goes to Jail</a></i> (2009), receiving an <a href="/wiki/ALMA_Award" title="ALMA Award">ALMA Award</a> nomination for the latter. Vergara's success on television has earned her roles in films <i><a href="/wiki/The_Smurfs_(film)" title="The Smurfs (film)">The Smurfs</a></i> (2011), <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Eve_(film)" title="New Year's Eve (film)">New Year's Eve</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/Happy_Feet_Two" title="Happy Feet Two">Happy Feet Two</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Three_Stooges_(2012_film)" title="The Three Stooges (2012 film)">The Three Stooges</a></i> (2012), <i><a href="/wiki/Escape_from_Planet_Earth" title="Escape from Planet Earth">Escape from Planet Earth</a></i> (2013), <i><a href="/wiki/Machete_Kills" title="Machete Kills">Machete Kills</a></i> (2013), <i><a href="/wiki/Chef_(film)" title="Chef (film)">Chef</a></i> (2014), and <i><a href="/wiki/Hot_Pursuit_(2015_film)" title="Hot Pursuit (2015 film)">Hot Pursuit</a></i> (2015). In 2012, 2013, and 2016, she was the top-earning actress on US television.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forbes.com_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-forbes.com-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vergara stars on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Modern_Family" title="Modern Family">Modern Family</a></i> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gloria_Delgado-Pritchett" title="Gloria Delgado-Pritchett">Gloria Delgado-Pritchett</a>, for which she has been nominated for four <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe Awards</a>, four <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award" title="Primetime Emmy Award">Primetime Emmy Awards</a>, and seven <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award" title="Screen Actors Guild Award">Screen Actors Guild Awards</a>. In 2014, she was ranked as the 32nd most powerful woman in the world by <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forbes14_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes14-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (August 2016)">not in citation given</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Vergara was born to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> family<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Barranquilla" title="Barranquilla">Barranquilla</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people-bio_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-people-bio-6">[6]</a></sup> Her mother, Margarita Vergara de Vergara, was a homemaker, and her father, Julio Enrique Vergara Robayo, was a cattle rancher for the meat industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fredericksburg_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fredericksburg-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miamiherald_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-miamiherald-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-diarioref_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-diarioref-9">[9]</a></sup> She was nicknamed "Toti" by her five siblings and many cousins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYDailyNews_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYDailyNews-10">[10]</a></sup> Vergara initially studied three years of <a href="/wiki/Dentistry" title="Dentistry">dentistry</a> at a university in <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, but left two semesters away from her degree to pursue opportunities in modeling and show business.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fredericksburg_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Fredericksburg-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> She subsequently moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miami,_Florida" title="Miami, Florida">Miami, Florida</a>, leaving behind unrest in Colombia, where her older brother Rafael was murdered in 1998 during an attempt to kidnap him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people-bio_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-people-bio-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fredericksburg_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Fredericksburg-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYDailyNews_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYDailyNews-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Her cousin and adopted sister, Sandra, is also a television actress in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<p>She then began studies at the Creative Workshops School of Acting, where <a href="/wiki/Gloria_Estefan" title="Gloria Estefan">Gloria Estefan</a> also trained.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Vergara was supposed to star in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Paperboy_(2012_film)" title="The Paperboy (2012 film)">The Paperboy</a></i>, an independent drama directed by <a href="/wiki/Lee_Daniels" title="Lee Daniels">Lee Daniels</a>. When shooting was delayed for a week and conflicted with her shooting schedule for the third season of <i>Modern Family</i>, she dropped out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2011, she finished filming the <a href="/wiki/Farrelly_brothers" title="Farrelly brothers">Farrelly brothers</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/The_Three_Stooges_(2012_film)" title="The Three Stooges (2012 film)">The Three Stooges</a></i>, which was her first major leading role in a film. Vergara said, "I play a mean woman that tries to manipulate the Three Stooges into killing her husband so that she gets all the money."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2012, she appeared in her son Manolo's <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> web series <i>Vida con Toty</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Vergara was the highest-earning woman in U.S. television, earning $19 million for the previous 12 months on a list released by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forbes.com" title="Forbes.com">Forbes.com</a> on July 18, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Vergara was named one of <i><a href="/wiki/People_(magazine)" title="People (magazine)">People</a></i> magazine's "50 most beautiful people" and named by the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hollywood_Reporter" title="Hollywood Reporter">Hollywood Reporter</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> as one of the most influential Latin women in Hollywood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CoverGirl_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-CoverGirl-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vergara received a star on the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Walk_of_Fame" title="Hollywood Walk of Fame">Hollywood Walk of Fame</a> on May 7, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-walk_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-walk-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2016, the <a href="/wiki/Human_Rights_Campaign" title="Human Rights Campaign">Human Rights Campaign</a> released a video in tribute to the victims of the <a href="/wiki/2016_Orlando_nightclub_shooting" title="2016 Orlando nightclub shooting">2016 Orlando gay nightclub shooting</a>; in the video, Vergara and others told the stories of the people killed there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>She stars on the ABC sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Modern_Family" title="Modern Family">Modern Family</a></i> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gloria_Delgado-Pritchett" title="Gloria Delgado-Pritchett">Gloria Delgado-Pritchett</a>, for which she was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Supporting_Actress_in_a_Comedy_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series">Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series</a> for 2010, 2011, 2012, and 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2011, it was announced that Vergara is designing a clothing line for <a href="/wiki/Kmart" title="Kmart">Kmart</a> targeting <a href="/wiki/Soccer_mom" title="Soccer mom">soccer moms</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> and appeared in a number of commercials for <a href="/wiki/Comcast" title="Comcast">Comcast</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xfinity" title="Xfinity">Xfinity</a> brand and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/State_Farm" title="State Farm">State Farm</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>

<p>Vergara was married at the age of 18 to her high-school sweetheart, Joe Gonzalez.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people-bio_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-people-bio-6">[6]</a></sup> They have a son named Manolo born September 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vidatoday_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-vidatoday-33">[33]</a></sup> They divorced in 1993.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people-bio_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-people-bio-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vergara was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Thyroid_cancer" title="Thyroid cancer">thyroid cancer</a> in 2000. She had her thyroid removed, underwent <a href="/wiki/Iodine-131" title="Iodine-131">radioiodine</a> therapy, and made a full recovery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> She takes medication to prevent <a href="/wiki/Hypothyroidism" title="Hypothyroidism">hypothyroidism</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 9, 2011, Vergara's younger brother Julio was deported from the United States to Colombia after being arrested in April that year; Julio also had a longtime drug addiction and previous brushes with the law.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Vergara told <i><a href="/wiki/Parade_(magazine)" title="Parade (magazine)">Parade</a></i> magazine, "To see somebody dying over 10 years, little by little, that's the worst punishment. Now he's like another person."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 10, 2012, it was reported that Vergara and her boyfriend <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_M._Loeb" title="Nicholas M. Loeb">Nicholas M. Loeb</a> were engaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> On May 23, 2014, Vergara announced that the engagement had been called off.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2014, it was reported that Vergara began dating <i>True Blood</i> star <a href="/wiki/Joe_Manganiello" title="Joe Manganiello">Joe Manganiello</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> The pair became engaged on Christmas Day 2014 after dating for six months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> They married in <a href="/wiki/Palm_Beach,_Florida" title="Palm Beach, Florida">Palm Beach, Florida</a> on November 21, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vergara has naturally <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blonde" title="Blonde">blonde</a> hair, although she is sometimes asked to dye it brown to make her characters look more <a href="/wiki/Stereotypes_of_Hispanic_and_Latino_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Stereotypes of Hispanic and Latino Americans in the United States">stereotypically Hispanic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>During her December 2014 appearance on <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live!" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live!">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a></i>, Vergara revealed that she had become a United States citizen after getting a perfect score on her citizenship test.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, <a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a> estimated Vergara's annual income at $28.5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> As of September 2016, Forbes reported that Vergara was the highest paid actress in television, bringing in $43 million in the past year. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Embryo_lawsuit">Embryo lawsuit</span></h2>
<p>On May 1, 2015, it was reported that Vergara and her former fiance Loeb were in dispute regarding the future of two <a href="/wiki/Embryo" title="Embryo">embryos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc2015_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc2015-54">[54]</a></sup> These embryos were produced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/In_vitro_fertilization" title="In vitro fertilization">in vitro fertilization</a> while they were together<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc2015_54-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc2015-54">[54]</a></sup> and are in storage in <a href="/wiki/Cryopreservation" title="Cryopreservation">cryopreservation</a> in a medical clinic in California.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zadrozny_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-zadrozny-55">[55]</a></sup> In December 2016 a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right_to_live" title="Right to live">right-to-live</a> lawsuit against Vergara was initiated in Louisiana by three plaintiffs, namely Vergara's embryos, named "Emma" and "Isabella", and their "trustee", James Charbonnet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-esmith_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-esmith-56">[56]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zadrozny_55-1"><a href="#cite_note-zadrozny-55">[55]</a></sup> The purpose of the suit is to give the embryos a chance to further develop using a <a href="/wiki/Surrogacy" title="Surrogacy">surrogate carrier</a>, hence to be born, and to benefit from an <a href="/wiki/Inheritance" title="Inheritance">inheritance</a> trust that had been created for them and is administered by Charbonnet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-esmith_56-1"><a href="#cite_note-esmith-56">[56]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zadrozny_55-2"><a href="#cite_note-zadrozny-55">[55]</a></sup> While a contract between Vergara and Loeb had been signed prior to the creation of the embryos stipulating that neither party could use the embryos without the consent of the other, the lawsuit tries to void this agreement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-esmith_56-2"><a href="#cite_note-esmith-56">[56]</a></sup> The suit also tries to terminate parental rights of Vergara because by keeping them in a tank in a medical clinic she allegedly abandoned and neglected the embryos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zadrozny_55-3"><a href="#cite_note-zadrozny-55">[55]</a></sup> Loeb is not part of the law suit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zadrozny_55-4"><a href="#cite_note-zadrozny-55">[55]</a></sup> The legal case is novel and takes advantage of Louisiana's embryo laws.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zadrozny_55-5"><a href="#cite_note-zadrozny-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
