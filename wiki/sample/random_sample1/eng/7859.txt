<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Minor Planet Center</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>Minor Planet Center</b> (<b>MPC</b>) is the official worldwide organization in charge of collecting observational data for <a href="/wiki/Minor_planet" title="Minor planet">minor planets</a> (such as <a href="/wiki/Asteroid" title="Asteroid">asteroids</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Comet" title="Comet">comets</a>, calculating their <a href="/wiki/Orbit" title="Orbit">orbits</a> and publishing this information via the <i><a href="#Publications">Minor Planet Circulars</a></i>. Under the auspices of the <a href="/wiki/International_Astronomical_Union" title="International Astronomical Union">International Astronomical Union</a>, it operates at the <a href="/wiki/Smithsonian_Astrophysical_Observatory" title="Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory">Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory</a>, which is part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Center_for_Astrophysics" title="Center for Astrophysics">Center for Astrophysics</a> along with the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College_Observatory" title="Harvard College Observatory">Harvard College Observatory</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAU-Centres_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-IAU-Centres-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The MPC runs a number of free online services for observers to assist them in observing minor planets and comets. The complete catalogue of minor planet orbits (sometimes referred to as the "Minor Planet Catalogue") may also be freely downloaded. In addition to <a href="/wiki/Astrometry" title="Astrometry">astrometric</a> data, the MPC collects light curve photometry of minor planets. A key function of the MPC is helping observers coordinate follow up observations of possible <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Near_Earth_Object" title="Near Earth Object">Near Earth Objects</a> (NEOs) via its NEO web form and blog.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MPC-NEO-confirmation_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-MPC-NEO-confirmation-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MPC-NEO-blog_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-MPC-NEO-blog-3">[3]</a></sup> The MPC is also responsible for identifying, and alerting to, new NEOs with a risk of impacting Earth in the few weeks following their discovery <i>(see <a href="/wiki/Potentially_hazardous_object" title="Potentially hazardous object">Potentially hazardous objects</a> and <a href="#Videos">§ Videos</a>)</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAU-Centres_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-IAU-Centres-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minor_Planet_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Minor Planet Center was set up at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cincinnati" title="University of Cincinnati">University of Cincinnati</a> in 1947, under the direction of <a href="/wiki/Paul_Herget" title="Paul Herget">Paul Herget</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hergetbio_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-hergetbio-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marsden1980_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Marsden1980-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:63</sup> Upon Herget's retirement on June 30, 1978,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marsden1980_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Marsden1980-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:67</sup> the MPC was moved to the SAO, under the direction of <a href="/wiki/Brian_G._Marsden" title="Brian G. Marsden">Brian G. Marsden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marsden1980_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Marsden1980-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:67</sup> From 2006–2015,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MPC-blog-Marsden-stepdown_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-MPC-blog-Marsden-stepdown-6">[6]</a></sup> the director of the MPC was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Timothy_Spahr" title="Timothy Spahr">Timothy Spahr</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mpec2010W10_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-mpec2010W10-7">[7]</a></sup> who oversaw a staff of five. As of February 2015<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Minor_Planet_Center&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the Minor Planet Center is headed by interim director <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matthew_Holman" title="Matthew Holman">Matthew Holman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MPC-blog-Interim-Director_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-MPC-blog-Interim-Director-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Periodical_publications"><span id="Publications"></span> Periodical publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minor_Planet_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Periodical publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">"Minor Planet Circulars" and "Minor Planet Electronic Circulars" redirect here. For the publication by <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Lunar_and_Planetary_Observers" title="Association of Lunar and Planetary Observers">ALPO</a>, see <a href="/wiki/The_Minor_Planet_Bulletin" title="The Minor Planet Bulletin">The Minor Planet Bulletin</a>.</div>
<p>The MPC periodically releases <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astrometric" title="Astrometric">astrometric</a> observations of minor planets, as well as of <a href="/wiki/Comet" title="Comet">comets</a> and <a href="/wiki/Natural_satellite" title="Natural satellite">natural satellites</a>. These publications are the Minor Planet Circulars (MPCs), the Minor Planet Electronic Circulars (MPECs), and the Minor Planet Supplements (MPSs and MPOs).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MPC-Publications_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-MPC-Publications-9">[9]</a></sup> An extensive archive of publications in a PDF format is available at the Minor Planet Center's website. The archive's oldest publication dates back to 1 November 1977 (MPC 4937–5016).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MPC-Circulars-Archive_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-MPC-Circulars-Archive-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><i><b>Minor Planet Circulars</b></i> (<i><b>MPCs</b></i>), established 1947, is a <a href="/wiki/Scientific_journal" title="Scientific journal">scientific journal</a> that is generally published by the Minor Planet Center on the date of each <a href="/wiki/Full_moon" title="Full moon">full moon</a>, when the number of reported observations are minimal due to the brighter night sky. The <i>Circulars</i> contain astrometric observations, orbits and ephemerides of minor planets, comets and certain natural satellites. The astrometric observations of comets are published in full, while the minor planet observations are summarised by observatory code (the full observations now being given in the <i>Minor Planet Circulars</i> Supplement). New numberings and namings of minor planets <i>(also see <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naming_of_Minor_Planets" title="Naming of Minor Planets">Naming of Minor Planets</a>)</i>, as well as numberings of periodic comets, are announced in the <i>Circulars</i>. New orbits for comets and natural satellites appear in the <i>Circulars</i>; new orbits for minor planets appear in the Minor Planets and Comets Orbit Supplement <i>(see below)</i>.</li>
<li>The <i><b>Minor Planet Electronic Circulars</b></i> (<i><b>MPECs</b></i>) are published by the Minor Planet Center. They generally contain <a href="/wiki/Astrometry" title="Astrometry">positional observations</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kepler_orbit" title="Kepler orbit">orbits</a> of unusual <a href="/wiki/Minor_planet" title="Minor planet">minor planets</a> and all <a href="/wiki/Comet" title="Comet">comets</a>. Monthly lists of observable unusual objects, observable distant objects, observable comets and the critical list of numbered minor planets also appear on these circulars. Daily Orbit Update MPECs, issued every day, contain new identifications and orbits of minor planets, obtained over the previous 24 hours.</li>
<li>The <i><b>Minor Planets and Comets Supplement</b> (MPS) is published on behalf of <a href="/wiki/International_Astronomical_Union" title="International Astronomical Union">IAU</a>'s Division F by the Minor Planet Center.</i></li>
<li>The <i><b>Minor Planets and Comets Orbit Supplement</b> (MPO) is published on behalf of IAU's Division F by the Minor Planet Center.</i></li>
</ul>
