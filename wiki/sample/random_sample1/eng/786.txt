<p>"<b>Anaconda</b>" is a song by <a href="/wiki/Trinidad" title="Trinidad">Trinidadian-born</a> American rapper <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>, from her third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pinkprint" title="The Pinkprint">The Pinkprint</a></i> (2014). It was released on August 4, 2014 by <a href="/wiki/Young_Money_Entertainment" title="Young Money Entertainment">Young Money Entertainment</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cash_Money_Records" title="Cash Money Records">Cash Money Records</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Republic_Records" title="Republic Records">Republic Records</a> as the second single from the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The song was produced by <a href="/wiki/Polow_da_Don" title="Polow da Don">Polow da Don</a>, Anonymous, and <a href="/wiki/Da_Internz" title="Da Internz">Da Internz</a>. The song heavily samples "<a href="/wiki/Baby_Got_Back" title="Baby Got Back">Baby Got Back</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Sir_Mix-a-Lot" title="Sir Mix-a-Lot">Sir Mix-a-Lot</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The song peaked at number two on the US <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Hot 100</a>, consequently becoming Minaj's highest charting single in the United States to date. It went on to spend eight consecutive weeks in the top ten of the Hot 100 chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The song also peaked within the top ten in Australia, Canada, Ireland, New Zealand and the United Kingdom.</p>
<p>An accompanying video for the song was directed by <a href="/wiki/Colin_Tilley" title="Colin Tilley">Colin Tilley</a> and released on August 19, 2014. Following the video's release, it broke the 24-hour streaming record on <a href="/wiki/Vevo" title="Vevo">Vevo</a> by accumulating 19.6 million views in its first day of release, being surpassed by <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Swift" title="Taylor Swift">Taylor Swift</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Bad_Blood_(Taylor_Swift_song)" title="Bad Blood (Taylor Swift song)">Bad Blood</a>" in 2015. To further promote the song, Minaj performed it live at the <a href="/wiki/2014_MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="2014 MTV Video Music Awards">2014 MTV Video Music Awards</a> and also performed it at <a href="/wiki/Fashion_Rocks" title="Fashion Rocks">Fashion Rocks</a> and the <a href="/wiki/IHeartRadio_Music_Festival" title="IHeartRadio Music Festival">iHeartRadio Music Festival</a>. "Anaconda" was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Song" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Song">Best Rap Song</a> at the <a href="/wiki/57th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="57th Annual Grammy Awards">2015 Grammy Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The song was also nominated for two categories at the <a href="/wiki/2015_MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="2015 MTV Video Music Awards">2015 MTV Video Music Awards</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Female_Video" title="Best Female Video">Best Female Video</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Hip-Hop_Video" title="Best Hip-Hop Video">Best Hip-Hop Video</a>, winning the latter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_composition">Background and composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anaconda_(Nicki_Minaj_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On August 5, 2014, producer <a href="/wiki/Polow_da_Don" title="Polow da Don">Polow da Don</a> claimed the "Anaconda" instrumental was originally intended for fellow female rapper and musician <a href="/wiki/Missy_Elliott" title="Missy Elliott">Missy Elliott</a>, but was scrapped and then re-recorded by Minaj.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xxl_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-xxl-8">[8]</a></sup> However, on August 24, 2014, Elliott revealed to fans and followers via her Twitter account that Polow's recollections were untrue and additionally confirmed that she never received the alleged instrumental.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The cover art for the single went viral on the web after being revealed. On various digital music stores, the cover art is censored with the <a href="/wiki/Parental_Advisory" title="Parental Advisory">Parental Advisory</a> label over Minaj's <a href="/wiki/Buttocks" title="Buttocks">buttocks</a>. The song heavily samples "<a href="/wiki/Baby_Got_Back" title="Baby Got Back">Baby Got Back</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Sir_Mix-a-Lot" title="Sir Mix-a-Lot">Sir Mix-a-Lot</a>, who also has vocals in the chorus of the song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xxl_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-xxl-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Musically, the song is a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> and pop-rap song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-poprap_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-poprap-2">[2]</a></sup> The song is written in the key of <a href="/wiki/C-sharp_minor" title="C-sharp minor">C<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;">♯</span> minor</a> with a tempo of 130 beats per minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<p>Carolyn Menyes from <i>Music Times</i> said it is a bit "raunchy" though this was "expected" and saying "Anaconda" continues Minaj's promise to move away from her poppier singles such as "<a href="/wiki/Super_Bass" title="Super Bass">Super Bass</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Starships_(song)" title="Starships (song)">Starships</a>", and go back to her hardcore rap roots.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Kory Grow from <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone_(magazine)" title="Rolling Stone (magazine)">Rolling Stone</a></i> praised the use of the "<a href="/wiki/Baby_Got_Back" title="Baby Got Back">Baby Got Back</a>" sample and said the song stayed true to the theme of the original.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Kevin Goddard from <i>HotNewHipHop</i> said "Nicki's raunchy new version may be the next big thing to hit the industry."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Dan Reilly from <i><a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)">Spin</a></i> said "Minaj takes the good Sir's '92 ode to protruding posteriors and adds a heavy dose of 2014 sex lingo".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Amy Davidson from <i><a href="/wiki/Digital_Spy" title="Digital Spy">Digital Spy</a></i> gave the song three stars out of five and said that "Nicki's breaking down barriers".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sir_Mix-a-Lot" title="Sir Mix-a-Lot">Sir Mix-a-Lot</a> said he had "a whole new level of respect for Minaj" after hearing the record and that he became a "fan for life" of Minaj.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The song made Spin's end of year 'The 101 Best Songs of 2014' list. It ranked #3 on the list of 101 entries and Spin herald it "much more than just a song" but a "cultural movement". She was the only rapper to make the top ten of the list as a lead artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> "Anaconda" was also placed at number two on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s Best Songs of 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>However, despite the positive reviews the song received negative reactions. The London Economic called it "one of the worst songs ever".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Critics from <a href="/wiki/National_Review" title="National Review">National Review</a> referred to the song as "degrading", promotes prostitution and drug abuse and promotes immorality to young girls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> A city in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> banned the song and prohibited it from concerts and sports events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> They claimed the record is "violations of women's dignity", encouragement of "a culture of rape, humiliation of women", and lyrics "full of racist words, insults, sexism and homophobia".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anaconda_(Nicki_Minaj_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the United States, the song debuted at No. 3 on the <a href="/wiki/Digital_Songs" title="Digital Songs">Digital Songs</a> chart selling 141,000 digital downloads in its first week. Additionally, as Minaj shares lead credit on "<a href="/wiki/Bang_Bang_(Jessie_J,_Ariana_Grande_and_Nicki_Minaj_song)" title="Bang Bang (Jessie J, Ariana Grande and Nicki Minaj song)">Bang Bang</a>", which was No. 2 on that same chart in the same week, Minaj became the first lead act to have two concurrent songs in the top three of the chart since <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Swift" title="Taylor Swift">Taylor Swift</a> did it on the list dated September 22, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Hot 100</a>, the song debuted at No. 19, marking Minaj's 51st Hot 100 entry and subsequently breaking out of a tie with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a> for overall career Hot 100 entries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the week ending September 6, 2014, "Anaconda" jumped 39-2 on the Hot 100, becoming Minaj's highest charting single on that chart, and marking her eleventh top ten single. It was kept from the top spot by Taylor Swift's "<a href="/wiki/Shake_It_Off" title="Shake It Off">Shake It Off</a>", which debuted atop the chart the same week. Its leap into the runner-up position was brought on primarily by the release of its music video, which garnered 32.1 million domestic streams in its first week. That streaming total caused it to consequentially skyrocket 42-1 on <i>Billboard'</i>s <a href="/wiki/Streaming_Songs" title="Streaming Songs">Streaming Songs</a> chart. It is the largest one week streaming sum since <a href="/wiki/Miley_Cyrus" title="Miley Cyrus">Miley Cyrus</a>' "<a href="/wiki/Wrecking_Ball_(Miley_Cyrus_song)" title="Wrecking Ball (Miley Cyrus song)">Wrecking Ball</a>" received 36.4 million streams for the week ending September 28, 2013. Additionally, the 39-2 ascent for "Anaconda" marks the second-biggest jump ever within the Hot 100's top 40. Only <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Eyed_Peas" title="The Black Eyed Peas">The Black Eyed Peas</a> made a bigger leap in the region when "<a href="/wiki/Boom_Boom_Pow" title="Boom Boom Pow">Boom Boom Pow</a>" blasted 39-1 on April 18, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> "Anaconda" was certified double platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) in November 2014 and has sold 2 million copies in the US as of December 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-us_sales_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-us_sales-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anaconda_(Nicki_Minaj_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A music video for the track was shot in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> by <a href="/wiki/Colin_Tilley" title="Colin Tilley">Colin Tilley</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> and was released on August 19, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The video features a cameo appearance by fellow rapper and labelmate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The video broke the 24-hour streaming record on <a href="/wiki/Vevo" title="Vevo">Vevo</a> by accumulating 19.6 million views in its first day of release, beating the 19.3 million 24-hour record previously held by <a href="/wiki/Miley_Cyrus" title="Miley Cyrus">Miley Cyrus</a> for the music video of her song, "<a href="/wiki/Wrecking_Ball_(Miley_Cyrus_song)" title="Wrecking Ball (Miley Cyrus song)">Wrecking Ball</a>", in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> On August 30, 2014, eleven days after the release of the music video, "Anaconda" was <a href="/wiki/Vevo" title="Vevo">Vevo</a> certified for receiving over 100 million views. In 2015 <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Swift" title="Taylor Swift">Taylor Swift</a> surpassed Minaj's record with the video for her single <a href="/wiki/Bad_Blood_(Taylor_Swift_song)" title="Bad Blood (Taylor Swift song)">"Bad Blood"</a> which got 20.1 million views in 24 hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a review of Minaj's video, Lucas Villa of <i><a href="/wiki/AXS_(ticket_merchant)" title="AXS (ticket merchant)">AXS</a></i> wrote, "It's butts abound in her bananas clip for 'Anaconda.'"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> NBC host <a href="/wiki/Al_Roker" title="Al Roker">Al Roker</a> called the music video "vile" and "desperate". Roker said, "It almost seems like Minaj has gotten desperate and needs to do something to get her crown back."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Molly Lambert of <a href="/wiki/Grantland" title="Grantland">Grantland</a> was complimentary, writing "the video is about Nicki asserting her power, not as a sexual object but a sexual subject."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> It was named one of the sexiest videos of 2014 by <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>

<p>On 24 August 2014, Minaj first performed the song live at the <a href="/wiki/2014_MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="2014 MTV Video Music Awards">2014 MTV Video Music Awards</a>. The performance followed <a href="/wiki/Ariana_Grande" title="Ariana Grande">Ariana Grande</a>'s individual performance of her single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Break_Free_(Ariana_Grande_song)" title="Break Free (Ariana Grande song)">Break Free</a>" and was followed by a performance of <a href="/wiki/Jessie_J" title="Jessie J">Jessie J</a>, Grande, and Minaj's joint single "<a href="/wiki/Bang_Bang_(Jessie_J,_Ariana_Grande_and_Nicki_Minaj_song)" title="Bang Bang (Jessie J, Ariana Grande and Nicki Minaj song)">Bang Bang</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> On September 9, 2014, Minaj performed the song live at <a href="/wiki/Fashion_Rocks" title="Fashion Rocks">Fashion Rocks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> On September 19, 2014, Minaj performed the song at the 2014 <a href="/wiki/IHeartRadio_Music_Festival" title="IHeartRadio Music Festival">iHeartRadio Music Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> On November 9, 2014, Minaj performed "Anaconda" at the <a href="/wiki/2014_MTV_Europe_Music_Awards" title="2014 MTV Europe Music Awards">2014 MTV Europe Music Awards</a> in a three song medley including her other songs "<a href="/wiki/Super_Bass" title="Super Bass">Super Bass</a>" and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bed_of_Lies_(Nicki_Minaj_song)" title="Bed of Lies (Nicki Minaj song)">Bed of Lies</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Skylar_Grey" title="Skylar Grey">Skylar Grey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
