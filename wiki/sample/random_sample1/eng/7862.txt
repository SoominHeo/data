<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Small arms</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>In international <a href="/wiki/Arms_control" title="Arms control">arms control</a>, <b>small arms</b> include <a href="/wiki/Revolver" title="Revolver">revolvers</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pistols" title="Pistols">pistols</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rifle" title="Rifle">rifles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carbine" title="Carbine">carbines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Assault_rifle" title="Assault rifle">assault rifles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Submachine_gun" title="Submachine gun">submachine guns</a> and <a href="/wiki/Light_machine_gun" title="Light machine gun">light machine guns</a>. Together with <b>light weapons</b> (<a href="/wiki/Heavy_machine_gun" title="Heavy machine gun">heavy machine guns</a>; hand-held <a href="/wiki/Grenade_launcher" title="Grenade launcher">grenade launchers</a>; portable anti-aircraft and anti-tank guns; recoilless rifles; portable launchers of anti-aircraft and anti-tank missile systems; and mortars of calibres of less than 100 mm), they comprise the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SALW" title="SALW">Small Arms and Light Weapons (SALW)</a> protocol.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN-a52298_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-UN-a52298-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SASDefinitions_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-SASDefinitions-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to the United Nations: "Since weapons in this class are capable of being carried, if a small arm, by one person or, if a light arm, by two or more people, a pack animal or a light vehicle, they allow for mobile operations where heavy mechanized and air forces are not available or are restricted in their capabilities owing to difficult mountain, jungle or urban terrain."</p>
<p>In the U.S. military, small arms are "man portable, individual, and crew-served weapon systems used mainly against personnel and lightly armored or unarmored equipment".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DODSmallarms_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-DODSmallarms-3">[3]</a></sup> However, in regard to inventory management, the U.S. Army says small arms/light weapons (SA/LW) are: "Handguns, shoulder-fired weapons, light automatic weapons up to and including 12.7mm machine guns, recoilless rifles up to and including 106mm, mortars up to and including 81mm, man-portable rocket launchers, rifle-/shoulder-fired grenade launchers, and individually operated weapons that are portable or can be fired without special mounts or firing devices and that have potential use in civil disturbances and are vulnerable to theft.</p>
<p>The world's top small arms producing companies are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Browning_Arms" title="Browning Arms">Browning Arms</a>, <a href="/wiki/Remington_Arms" title="Remington Arms">Remington Arms</a>, Colt Arms, Sturm, Ruger &amp; Co (US), <a href="/wiki/Heckler_%26_Koch" title="Heckler &amp; Koch">Heckler &amp; Koch</a>, <a href="/wiki/SIG_Sauer" title="SIG Sauer">SIG Sauer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Walther_Arms" title="Walther Arms">Walther</a> (Germany), <a href="/wiki/FN_Herstal" title="FN Herstal">FN Herstal</a> (Belgium), <a href="/wiki/Beretta" title="Beretta">Beretta</a> (Italy), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tula_Arms" title="Tula Arms">Tula Arms</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IZhMASh" title="IZhMASh">Kalashnikov</a> (Russia) while former top producers were Mauser &amp; Springfield Armory.</p>
<p>Much of the international work to address the <a href="/wiki/Small_arms_trade" title="Small arms trade">illegal arms trade</a> (or <a href="/wiki/Arms_trafficking" title="Arms trafficking">arms trafficking</a>) is raised in the <a href="/wiki/Small_arms_trade#United_Nations_Conference_on_the_Illicit_Trade_in_Small_Arms" title="Small arms trade">United Nations Conference on the Illicit Trade in Small Arms</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GPFonSALW_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-GPFonSALW-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_distribution_of_small_arms">Global distribution of small arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Small_arms&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Global distribution of small arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2013, the <a href="/wiki/Small_Arms_Survey" title="Small Arms Survey">Small Arms Survey</a> said that of the 875 million small arms distributed globally, 650 million — almost 75 percent — are in <a href="/wiki/Civilian" title="Civilian">civilian</a> hands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> (U.S. civilians account for 270 million of this total.)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[a]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SAS2007-2_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-SAS2007-2-7">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:39</sup> About 200 million are controlled by <a href="/wiki/Armed_forces" title="Armed forces">armed forces</a> and about 26 million by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Law_enforcement_agencies" title="Law enforcement agencies">law enforcement agencies</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SAS2010-4_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-SAS2010-4-8">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:102</sup> <a href="/wiki/Gang" title="Gang">Gang members</a> hold between 2 and 10 million small arms and armed, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-state_actors" title="Non-state actors">non-state actors</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[b]</a></sup> hold about 1.4 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SAS2010-4_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-SAS2010-4-8">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:101</sup> Together, the small-arms arsenals of non-state actors and gangs account for 0.4 to 1.3 percent of the global total.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SAS2010-4_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-SAS2010-4-8">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:101</sup></p>
