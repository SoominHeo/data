<p>A <b>hydrant wrench</b> is a tool used to remove <a href="/wiki/Fire_hydrant" title="Fire hydrant">fire hydrant</a> caps and open the valve of the hydrant. They are usually adjustable so as to fit different sized hydrant nuts.</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_France">In France</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hydrant_wrench&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In France">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ensemble_cles_tricoises.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="350" data-file-width="491" height="214" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Ensemble_cles_tricoises.jpg/300px-Ensemble_cles_tricoises.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Ensemble_cles_tricoises.jpg/450px-Ensemble_cles_tricoises.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/57/Ensemble_cles_tricoises.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ensemble_cles_tricoises.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Several tricoise wrenches; the red ones at the top are in vehicles, the yellow in the middle hang at the fire belts, the bottom one is a polycoise.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, The wrench is called "Guillemin wrench" (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>clef Guillemin</i></span>, pronounced <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[kle ɡijəmɛ̃]</a></span>), but the firefighters call it "tricoise wrench" (<i>clef tricoise</i>, <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[kle tʁikwaz]</span>). The curved part applies on the ring, and the hooked part clutches a notch.</p>
<p>Big tricoise wrenches are placed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fire_apparatus" title="Fire apparatus">fire trucks</a>, and are mainly used to fasten the big hoses, e.g. ∅110 mm hoses to feed the pumper tank from the hydrant (∅100 mm for the clutch). Small tricoise are made of <a href="/wiki/Brass" title="Brass">brass</a> and hang at the fire belts; they are used to fasten the small hoses, <i>e.g.</i>, ∅70mm to ∅22mm hoses (∅65mm to ∅20mm clutches).</p>
<p>The shape is sometimes adapted so it can be used as a tool for other purposes; it is then called "polycoise wrench" or "Deschamps wrench". These tools are used to</p>
<ul>
<li>Open <a href="/wiki/Gaz_de_France" title="Gaz de France">GDF</a> standard <a href="/wiki/Gas" title="Gas">gas</a> enclosures and <a href="/wiki/%C3%89lectricit%C3%A9_de_France" title="Électricité de France">EDF</a> standard <a href="/wiki/Electricity" title="Electricity">electrical</a> enclosures (12mm female triangle wrench);</li>
<li>Open fire hydrants (15.6mm female triangle);</li>
<li>Open doors without knobs, ventilation accesses and closets containing firefighting equipment in some public locations (5×5mm to 8×8mm square female wrenches);</li>
<li>Open <a href="/wiki/Dry_riser" title="Dry riser">dry risers</a> (12.5×12.5mm square female wrench);</li>
<li>Open bathroom and toilet doors (<a href="/wiki/Screwdriver" title="Screwdriver">screwdriver</a>); and</li>
<li>Remove <a href="/wiki/Nut_(hardware)" title="Nut (hardware)">nuts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Screw" title="Screw">bolts</a> (13, 17 and 19mm six-point wrenches).</li>
</ul>

<p>The shape of a hydrant wrench is sometimes adapted so it can be used as a tool for other purposes, such as:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Storz" title="Storz">Storz</a> and spanner hose couplings</li>
<li>Rocker lug couplings</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Water_metering" title="Water metering">Water meter</a> shutoff valves</li>
<li>Gas cock valves</li>
<li>Pentagonal nuts</li>
<li>Square nuts.</li>
</ul>
