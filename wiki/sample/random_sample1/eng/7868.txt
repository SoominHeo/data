<p><b>Son Dong-woon</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">손동운</span>; born June 6, 1991), commonly known as <b>Dongwoon</b>, is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Korean_idol" title="South Korean idol">South Korean idol</a> singer. He is a member of the boy group <a href="/wiki/Beast_(South_Korean_band)" title="Beast (South Korean band)">Beast</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Son_Dong-woon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Son Dong-woon was born in <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">Busan</a>, South Korea, on June 6, 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Russell2014_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Russell2014-1">[1]</a></sup> His father, Son Il-rak, is a professor at <a href="/wiki/Cheongju_University" title="Cheongju University">Cheongju University</a>. Dong Woon stated in an interview and in the KBS show <i>Win Win</i> that he studied in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Rosa,_Laguna" title="Santa Rosa, Laguna">Santa Rosa, Laguna</a>, Philippines. Dong Woon was a trainee from <a href="/wiki/JYP_Entertainment" title="JYP Entertainment">JYP Entertainment</a> for two years, and was the last member chosen to join Beast. He is the youngest of the group.</p>
<p>Dong Woon attended Hanyoung High School and currently attends <a href="/wiki/Konkuk_University" title="Konkuk University">Konkuk University</a> majoring in image and film.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Son_Dong-woon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Beast">Beast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Son_Dong-woon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Beast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Beast_(South_Korean_band)" title="Beast (South Korean band)">Beast (South Korean band)</a></div>
<p>The group has released eight Korean mini-albums and various singles. Dong Woon is a vocalist of Beast.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_career">Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Son_Dong-woon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In December 2010, Dong Woon released a digital single album <i>Udon</i> together with Davichi’s Kang Min Kyung.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> However, it is said that by the composers of ‘Udon' that the title of the song is in Japanese, hence the main three broadcasting networks confirmed that they will not be showing on television.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2012, Dong Woon released “In the Cloud” for Shinsadong Tiger’s 'Supermarket_another Half'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 22, 2015, <a href="/wiki/Cube_Entertainment" title="Cube Entertainment">Cube Entertainment</a> revealed that Dong Woon will release his first solo album <i>Kimishika</i> on July 1 before officially beginning his Japanese debut promotions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
