<div class="hatnote" role="note">This is a <a href="/wiki/Chinese_name" title="Chinese name">Chinese name</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_surname" title="Chinese surname">family name</a> is <a href="/wiki/Sun_(surname)" title="Sun (surname)">Sun</a>.</div>
<p><b>Sun Deng</b> (209–241), <a href="/wiki/Courtesy_name" title="Courtesy name">courtesy name</a> <b>Zigao</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/Crown_prince" title="Crown prince">crown prince</a> of the state of <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Wu" title="Eastern Wu">Eastern Wu</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Three_Kingdoms" title="Three Kingdoms">Three Kingdoms</a> period. He was the eldest son of <a href="/wiki/Sun_Quan" title="Sun Quan">Sun Quan</a>, the founding emperor of Wu. It is known that Sun Deng was both wise and virtuous, having all the makings of a great man. He studied the <i><a href="/wiki/I_Ching" title="I Ching">Classic of Changes</a></i>, and was a poet and a musician.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> However, Sun Deng was destined never to succeed his father because he died from illness at a relatively young age of 31. Sun Deng married <a href="/wiki/Zhou_Yu" title="Zhou Yu">Zhou Yu</a>'s daughter and they had three children: Sun Fan, Sun Ying, and Sun Xi. He was <a href="/wiki/Posthumous_name" title="Posthumous name">posthumously honoured</a> as "Crown Prince Xuan".</p>
