<p><i><b>Another</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">アナザー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Anazā</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Mystery_fiction" title="Mystery fiction">mystery</a> <a href="/wiki/Horror_fiction" title="Horror fiction">horror</a> novel by <a href="/wiki/Yukito_Ayatsuji" title="Yukito Ayatsuji">Yukito Ayatsuji</a>, published on October 29, 2009 by <a href="/wiki/Kadokawa_Shoten" title="Kadokawa Shoten">Kadokawa Shoten</a>. The story focuses on a boy named Kōichi Sakakibara who, upon transferring into Yomiyama Middle School and meeting the curious Mei Misaki, finds himself in a mystery revolving around students and people related to his class falling victim to gruesome, senseless deaths.</p>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> adaptation by Hiro Kiyohara was serialized between May 2010 and January 2012 in the issues of Kadokawa Shoten's <i><a href="/wiki/Young_Ace" title="Young Ace">Young Ace</a></i>. Both the novel and the manga have been licensed in North America by <a href="/wiki/Yen_Press" title="Yen Press">Yen Press</a>. A 12-episode <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> TV series produced by <a href="/wiki/P.A.Works" title="P.A.Works">P.A.Works</a> aired in Japan between January 10 and March 27, 2012, with an <a href="/wiki/Original_video_animation" title="Original video animation">original video animation</a> episode released on May 26, 2012, and a live-action film <a href="/wiki/Another_(film)" title="Another (film)">of the same name</a> was released in Japanese theatres on August 4, 2012.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Another_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1972, Misaki, a popular student of Yomiyama North Middle School's class 3-3, suddenly died partway through the school year. Devastated by the loss, the students and teacher behaved like Misaki was still alive, leading to a strange presence on the graduation photo. In Spring 1998, Kōichi Sakakibara transfers into Yomiyama's class 3-3, where he meets Mei Misaki, a quiet student whom their classmates and teacher seemingly ignore. The class is soon caught up in a strange phenomenon, in which students and their relatives begin to die in often gruesome ways. Realizing that these deaths are related to the "Misaki of 1972", a yearly calamity that has struck most every class 3-3 since 1972, Kōichi and Mei seek to figure out how to stop it before it kills anymore of their classmates or them.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Another_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Novels">Novels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Another_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Novels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Another</i> is a 677-page novel written by <a href="/wiki/Yukito_Ayatsuji" title="Yukito Ayatsuji">Yukito Ayatsuji</a>. The novel was originally serialized in Kadokawa Shoten's literary magazine <i>Yasai Jidai</i> in intermittent periods between the August 2006 and May 2009 issues. A compiled <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> volume was published on October 29, 2009<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[15]</a></sup> and a two-volume <i><a href="/wiki/Bunkobon" title="Bunkobon">bunkobon</a></i> edition was released on September 25, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[17]</a></sup> A re-release of the <i>bunkobon</i> edition featuring illustrations by <a href="/wiki/Noizi_Ito" title="Noizi Ito">Noizi Ito</a> was released under the <a href="/wiki/Imprint_(trade_name)" title="Imprint (trade name)">imprint</a> Sneaker Bunko on March 1, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[19]</a></sup> A spin-off novel titled <i>Another: Episode S/0</i> was released on July 31, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[20]</a></sup> It is two novels in one binding. <i>Another S</i> is a side novel set in the same time period, an adventure of Mei Misaki during summer vacation. <i>Another 0</i> is set during Aunt Reiko's year in class 3-3, in 1983. A sequel novel with the tentative title <i>Another 2</i> has been announced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[21]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Yen_Press" title="Yen Press">Yen Press</a> has licensed the novel in North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yenpress_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-yenpress-23">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Another_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> adaptation illustrated by Hiro Kiyohara was serialized between the May 2010 and January 2012 issues of Kadokawa Shoten's <i><a href="/wiki/Young_Ace" title="Young Ace">Young Ace</a></i> magazine. Four <i>tankōbon</i> volumes were released between October 4, 2010 and December 29, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[24]</a></sup> A "0th" volume of the manga was released on May 26, 2012. <a href="/wiki/Yen_Press" title="Yen Press">Yen Press</a> has licensed the original four-volume manga in North America and released it as an omnibus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yenpress_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-yenpress-23">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Another_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

The screenplay was written by Ryō Higaki, and the chief animation director Yuriko Ishii based the character design used in the anime on Noizi Ito's original concept art.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ANN-0811_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-ANN-0811-26">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official-about_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-official-about-27">[26]</a></sup> The music was produced by <a href="/wiki/Kow_Otani" title="Kow Otani">Kow Otani</a>, and the sound director is Yoshikazu Iwanami.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official-about_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-official-about-27">[26]</a></sup> The anime has two theme songs: the opening theme "Kyōmu Densen" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">凶夢伝染</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e;
