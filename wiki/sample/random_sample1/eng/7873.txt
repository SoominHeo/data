<p><b>Sohn Suk-hee</b> is a South Korean announcer and interviewer. He is now a radio announcer, a professor at <a href="/wiki/Sungshin_Women%27s_University" title="Sungshin Women's University">Sungshin Women's University</a> and the president of <a href="/wiki/JTBC" title="JTBC">JTBC</a>'s news reporting division.</p>
<p>He is considered one of the most influential figures in South Korean media.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sohn_Suk-hee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sohn Suk-hee was born in 1956. He was born in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, studied <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_Literature" title="Korean Literature">Korean Literature</a> and graduated from <a href="/wiki/Kookmin_University" title="Kookmin University">Kookmin University</a> in 1982. Sohn went to the United States in 1997 to enroll in the graduate school of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Minnesota" title="University of Minnesota">University of Minnesota</a>, majoring in <a href="/wiki/Journalism" title="Journalism">Journalism</a>. After getting his master's degree, he returned to <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">MBC</a> as a vice chairman of department of announcer. He is well known for his career as an <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">MBC</a> announcer and presenter. He had preserved his position as a host of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=100-Minute_Debate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="100-Minute Debate (page does not exist)">100-Minute Debate</a> for 12 years, which is one of the most popular TV debate programs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 9, 2013, Sohn Suk-hee was named the new president of <a href="/wiki/JTBC" title="JTBC">JTBC</a>’s news reporting department. And he said that officially, “I know there is opposition to my choice. But I hope people can wait for me to carry out what I have been contemplating about true journalism. I will do my best to let them understand it in the future.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
