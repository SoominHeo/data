<p><b>Sun Xin</b> was a military general of <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Wu" title="Eastern Wu">Eastern Wu</a> during the late <a href="/wiki/Three_Kingdoms" title="Three Kingdoms">Three Kingdoms</a> period of <a href="/wiki/History_of_China" title="History of China">Chinese history</a>. He was a great-grandson of <a href="/wiki/Sun_Qiang" title="Sun Qiang">Sun Qiang</a> (孫羌), the older brother of <a href="/wiki/Sun_Jian" title="Sun Jian">Sun Jian</a> (father of Eastern Wu's founding emperor <a href="/wiki/Sun_Quan" title="Sun Quan">Sun Quan</a>). It is known that during the <a href="/wiki/Conquest_of_Wu_by_Jin" title="Conquest of Wu by Jin">Conquest of Wu by Jin</a>, Sun Xin cried out in disbelief, "Did the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jin_Dynasty_(265%E2%80%93420)" title="Jin Dynasty (265–420)">Jin</a> army fly down the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yangtze_River" title="Yangtze River">Yangtze River</a>?" Following this event Sun Xin was defeated by <a href="/wiki/Zhou_Zhi" title="Zhou Zhi">Zhou Zhi</a>.</p>
