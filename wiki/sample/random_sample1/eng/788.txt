<p><i><b>Diario AS</b></i> is a Spanish daily sports newspaper, concentrating particularly on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Profile">Profile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diario_AS&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Profile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Diario AS</i> is part of <i>PRISA</i> which also owns <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_Pais" title="El Pais">El Pais</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Cinco_D%C3%ADas" title="Cinco Días">Cinco Días</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The paper particularly covers news of the Madrid football teams: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_Madrid" title="Real Madrid">Real Madrid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rayo_Vallecano" title="Rayo Vallecano">Rayo Vallecano</a>. It competes directly with <a href="/wiki/Marca_(newspaper)" title="Marca (newspaper)"><i>MARCA</i></a>.</p>
<p>In addition to Madrid, the newspaper also has satellite bureaus in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bilbao" title="Bilbao">Bilbao</a>, <a href="/wiki/A_Coru%C3%B1a" title="A Coruña">A Coruña</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valencia,_Spain" title="Valencia, Spain">Valencia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zaragoza" title="Zaragoza">Zaragoza</a>.</p>
<p>In May 2012 the newspaper launched an English language sub-site, <a class="external text" href="http://en.as.com/en/" rel="nofollow">AS English</a>, offering articles translated from the original Spanish by a dedicated team of native English-language speakers.</p>
<p>The circulation of <i>Diario AS</i> was 181,172 copies in 2001<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and 176,892 copies in 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It rose to 214,654 copies in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
