<p><b>Solaseed Air</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ソラシド エア</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sorashido Ea</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, incorporated as <b>Solaseed Air Inc.</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">株式会社ソラシドエア</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kabushiki_Kaisha" title="Kabushiki Kaisha">Kabushiki-gaisha</a> Sorashido Ea <b>SNA</b></i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low-cost_airline" title="Low-cost airline">low-cost airline</a> headquartered on the property of <a href="/wiki/Miyazaki_Airport" title="Miyazaki Airport">Miyazaki Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Miyazaki,_Miyazaki" title="Miyazaki, Miyazaki">Miyazaki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miyazaki_Prefecture" title="Miyazaki Prefecture">Miyazaki Prefecture</a>, Japan.</p>
<p>It operates services mainly between destinations on <a href="/wiki/Kyushu" title="Kyushu">Kyushu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>. The airline changed its branding to Solaseed Air in July 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solaseed_Air&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Miyazaki_Airport_Feb_2008.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="810" data-file-width="1440" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Miyazaki_Airport_Feb_2008.jpg/220px-Miyazaki_Airport_Feb_2008.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Miyazaki_Airport_Feb_2008.jpg/330px-Miyazaki_Airport_Feb_2008.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Miyazaki_Airport_Feb_2008.jpg/440px-Miyazaki_Airport_Feb_2008.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The airline was established as Pan Asia Airlines in 1997 as the fourth new airline in a deregulated Japanese market. The corporate name was changed to Skynet Asia Airways Co., Ltd. in August 1998, and started operations in July 2002. It established "Solaseed Air" as corporate brand in July 2011. As of December 2015, the corporate name was changed to Solaseed Air Inc. The airline is owned by Development Bank of Japan (22.41%), Miyazaki Kotsu Co., Ltd. (17.03%) and ANA Holdings Inc. (17.03%).</p>
<p>Solaseed executive vice president Nobuyuki Suzuki committed suicide by jumping from a building in August 2016; his wife was found dead in their apartment two days later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_affairs">Corporate affairs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solaseed_Air&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Corporate affairs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Solaseed established the head office in the second floor of Miyazaki Airport Building <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">宮崎空港ビル</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Miyazaki Kūkō Biru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> in <a href="/wiki/Miyazaki,_Miyazaki" title="Miyazaki, Miyazaki">Miyazaki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miyazaki_Prefecture" title="Miyazaki Prefecture">Miyazaki Prefecture</a>, Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It used to have a headquarters in another facility in Miyazaki City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The headquarters later moved to the SNA Center (SNAセンター <i>SNA Sentā</i>) in the Again Building <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">アゲインビル</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Agein Biru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> in Miyazaki.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fleet_Notes">Fleet Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solaseed_Air&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Fleet Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In September 2014, Solaseed Air stored its last remaining 737-400 aircraft, and became an airline having only 737-800. operator.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The all fleet's cabin specifications are Boeing Sky Interior, and all attached winglets on wing tip.</p>
