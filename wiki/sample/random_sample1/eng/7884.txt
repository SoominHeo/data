<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solomon_Islands_national_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the 2004 Oceania World Cup qualification/Oceania Cup the team drew 2–2 with Australia and qualified for the second leg. However, in the second leg the Solomon Islands national men's team was humiliated by Australia 5–1 and 6–0 in the two matches, with Australia qualifying for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2005_Confederations_Cup" title="2005 Confederations Cup">2005 Confederations Cup</a>.</p>
<p>The Solomons got a second chance against the Socceroos in a two-legged series in September 2005, this time with the winner advancing to a two-legged series against <a href="/wiki/CONMEBOL" title="CONMEBOL">CONMEBOL</a>'s fifth-place finisher for a berth in the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a>, and the team was thrashed by Australia 7–0 on the first leg and 2–1 in the second played at home.</p>
<p>The Solomons were knocked out of the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> – having got off to a good start winning every game in their qualifying group and comfortably progressing to the knockout rounds, defeats to <a href="/wiki/New_Caledonia_national_football_team" title="New Caledonia national football team">New Caledonia</a> and then to <a href="/wiki/Vanuatu_national_football_team" title="Vanuatu national football team">Vanuatu</a> saw them knocked out of the competition.</p>
<p>In 2012, the Solomon Islands held the <a href="/wiki/2012_OFC_Nations_Cup" title="2012 OFC Nations Cup">2012 OFC Nations Cup</a> which was also the second round of World Cup qualifying for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> where they finished in fourth place after qualifying through to the knockout stage by defeating Papua New Guinea and having draws against Fiji and New Zealand. They lost in the semi-final after they lost to the champions Tahiti after <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Tehau" title="Jonathan Tehau">Jonathan Tehau</a> scored the only goal. They later lost to New Zealand in the third-place playoff. The third round of World Cup qualifying saw the team finish bottom of the group after only taking one win against <a href="/wiki/Tahiti_national_football_team" title="Tahiti national football team">Tahiti</a>.</p>
