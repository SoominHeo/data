<p><i><b>My Too Perfect Sons</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">솔약국집 아들들</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Solyakgukjib Adeuldeul</span></i>; lit. <i><b>The Sons of Sol Pharmacy House</b></i>) is a 2009 <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">South Korean television series</a> starring <a href="/wiki/Son_Hyun-joo" title="Son Hyun-joo">Son Hyun-joo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Pil-mo" title="Lee Pil-mo">Lee Pil-mo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Han_Sang-jin" title="Han Sang-jin">Han Sang-jin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ji_Chang-wook" title="Ji Chang-wook">Ji Chang-wook</a>, <a href="/wiki/Park_Sun-young_(actress)" title="Park Sun-young (actress)">Park Sun-young</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yoo_Sun" title="Yoo Sun">Yoo Sun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yoo_Ha-na" title="Yoo Ha-na">Yoo Ha-na</a>, Kang Eun-bi, <a href="/wiki/Byun_Hee-bong" title="Byun Hee-bong">Byun Hee-bong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baek_Il-seob" title="Baek Il-seob">Baek Il-seob</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yoon_Mi-ra" title="Yoon Mi-ra">Yoon Mi-ra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Yong-gun" title="Kim Yong-gun">Kim Yong-gun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Hye-ok" title="Kim Hye-ok">Kim Hye-ok</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cho_Jin-woong" title="Cho Jin-woong">Cho Jin-woong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Choi_Ji-na" title="Choi Ji-na">Choi Ji-na</a>. It aired on <a href="/wiki/KBS2" title="KBS2">KBS2</a> from April 11 to October 11, 2009 on Saturdays and Sundays at 19:55 for 54 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=My_Too_Perfect_Sons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Song family is headed by patriarch Kwang-ho and spunky mother Ok-hee. They have four sons—Jin-poong, Dae-poong, Sun-poong and Mi-poong. Ok-hee has doted on her four sons all their lives, but has interfered with their relationships when deeming the girlfriends "not good enough" for her perfect sons. Now she despairs that they are growing older and will never marry.</p>
<p>Eldest son Jin-poong is nearing his 40th birthday. He's a pharmacist whose store is in the same neighborhood as his home. His personality is shy and awkward around women, although he's a gentle and caring person. He's never quite gotten over his first love Kim Hye-rim, and feels conflicted when she moves back to her childhood home across the street from the pharmacy, along with her husband Brutus and their two children. Then Jin-poong meets Brutus's younger sister Lee Soo-jin, a smart, tough lawyer.</p>
<p>In contrast to his older brother's cautious and responsible personality, second son Dae-poong is a playboy doctor who gets out of scrapes with his boyish charm. He runs a small clinic right above Jin-poong's pharmacy, with one employee, nurse Kim Bok-shil. Bok-shil doesn't have any family and lives by herself in an apartment in the neighborhood, and comes by to the Song household daily to help their mother cook and clean. Dae-poong takes Bok-shil for granted, not knowing that she's a little bit in love with him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Third son Sun-poong is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vegetarian" title="Vegetarian">vegetarian</a> and animal-lover who works as a news reporter at broadcast station KBC. He's earnest, sweet, honest, and a little dense when it comes to romance. Sun-poong's direct boss, TV station director Oh Young-dal sets him up with his daughter, newcomer actress Eun-ji. Eun-ji has a spoiled princess complex and she doesn't think Sun-poong is her type, but when he doesn't seem interested in her, her ego takes a blow and his obliviousness makes him attractive to her.</p>
<p>The youngest son is 19-year-old Mi-poong, who just graduated from high school and has failed to be accepted to university, so he's in the middle of studying for a retest. He's extremely sensitive, and speaks to everyone in super-formal language. Gifted in sewing and crafts, Mi-poong often gets mocked for being too girly. Mi-poong's best friend Park Yong-chul got a girl, Choi Soo-hee pregnant, and she leaves her five-month-old baby Hana with him. As Yong-chul works multiple jobs to earn money, Mi-poong looks after Hana and grows attached to the baby. When Yong-chul receives his <a href="/wiki/Conscription_in_South_Korea" title="Conscription in South Korea">army papers</a>, Mi-poong agrees to take care of Hana until he gets discharged.</p>
