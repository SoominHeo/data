<p><b>Soltsy</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Сольцы́</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Soletsky_District" title="Soletsky District">Soletsky District</a> in <a href="/wiki/Novgorod_Oblast" title="Novgorod Oblast">Novgorod Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located on the left bank of the <a href="/wiki/Shelon_River" title="Shelon River">Shelon River</a>, 78 kilometers (48 mi) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Veliky_Novgorod" title="Veliky Novgorod">Veliky Novgorod</a>, the administrative center of the <a href="/wiki/Oblast" title="Oblast">oblast</a>. Population: <span class="nowrap">10,086 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-5">[5]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">11,264 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopCensus_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-PopCensus-9">[9]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">11,782 (<a href="/wiki/Soviet_Census_(1989)" title="Soviet Census (1989)">1989 Census</a>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census1989_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census1989-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soltsy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Soltsy, whose name owes to the nearby salt water springs,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> was first mentioned in a chronicle in 1390 and in the following played an important role as an intermediate station on the trade route connecting <a href="/wiki/Veliky_Novgorod" title="Veliky Novgorod">Novgorod</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pskov" title="Pskov">Pskov</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-distgen_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-distgen-7">[7]</a></sup> In 1471, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Shelon" title="Battle of Shelon">Battle of Shelon</a> between <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Moscow" title="Grand Duchy of Moscow">Muscovite</a> forces led by <a href="/wiki/Ivan_III_of_Russia" title="Ivan III of Russia">Ivan III</a> and the army of the <a href="/wiki/Novgorod_Republic" title="Novgorod Republic">Novgorod Republic</a> took place near Soltsy, which marked the end of political independence of the Novgorod Republic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-distgen_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-distgen-7">[7]</a></sup> Soltsy eventually became a part of the Moscow State.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-distgen_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-distgen-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the course of the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Russia_in_1708%E2%80%931710" title="Administrative divisions of Russia in 1708–1710">administrative reform</a> carried out in 1708 by <a href="/wiki/Peter_the_Great" title="Peter the Great">Peter the Great</a>, the territory was included into Ingermanland Governorate (known since 1710 as <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg_Governorate" title="Saint Petersburg Governorate">Saint Petersburg Governorate</a>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1727, separate <a href="/wiki/Novgorod_Governorate" title="Novgorod Governorate">Novgorod Governorate</a> was split off, and in 1772, <a href="/wiki/Pskov_Governorate" title="Pskov Governorate">Pskov Governorate</a> was established (existing between 1777 and 1796 as <a href="/wiki/Pskov_Viceroyalty" title="Pskov Viceroyalty">Pskov Viceroyalty</a>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1776, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Porkhovsky_Uyezd&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Porkhovsky Uyezd (page does not exist)">Porkhovsky Uyezd</a> was transferred from Novgorod Governorate to Pskov Governorate.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Soltsy was the seat of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Soletskaya_Volost&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Soletskaya Volost (page does not exist)">Soletskaya Volost</a> of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Porkhovsky_Uyezd&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Porkhovsky Uyezd (page does not exist)">Porkhovsky Uyezd</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In August 1927, the uyezds were abolished and, effective October 1, 1927, Soletsky District was established, with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> in Soltsy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Novobl85_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Novobl85-11">[11]</a></sup> Pskov Governorate was abolished as well and the district became a part of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Novgorod_Okrug&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Novgorod Okrug (page does not exist)">Novgorod Okrug</a> of <a href="/wiki/Leningrad_Oblast" title="Leningrad Oblast">Leningrad Oblast</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Novobl85_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Novobl85-11">[11]</a></sup> At the same time, Soltsy was granted town status.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> On July 23, 1930, the okrugs were abolished and the districts were directly subordinated to the oblast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Novobl85_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-Novobl85-11">[11]</a></sup> Between July 13 to 16, 1941 and again from July 22, 1941 to February 21, 1944, Soltsy was occupied by German troops.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Novobl135_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Novobl135-12">[12]</a></sup> On July 5, 1944, Soltsy, together with Soletsky District, was transferred to newly established Novgorod Oblast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Novobl135_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Novobl135-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soltsy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, Soltsy serves as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Soletsky_District" title="Soletsky District">Soletsky District</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NovgorodO_admlaw_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-NovgorodO_admlaw-1">[1]</a></sup> As an administrative division, it is, together with two <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">rural localities</a>, incorporated within Soletsky District as the <b><a href="/wiki/Town_of_district_significance" title="Town of district significance">town of district significance</a> of Soltsy</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NovgorodO_admlist_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-NovgorodO_admlist-2">[2]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the town of district significance of Soltsy is incorporated within Soletsky Municipal District as <b>Soletskoye Urban Settlement</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NovgorodOSoletskyD_mun_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-NovgorodOSoletskyD_mun-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soltsy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry">Industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soltsy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The main branches of industry in Soltsy are electronic industry and food industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-distgen_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-distgen-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soltsy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The railway connecting <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">St. Petersburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nevel_(town)" title="Nevel (town)">Nevel</a> via <a href="/wiki/Dno" title="Dno">Dno</a> crosses the district from north to south, running close to Soltsy. The railway connecting <a href="/wiki/Bologoye,_Tver_Oblast" title="Bologoye, Tver Oblast">Bologoye</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pskov" title="Pskov">Pskov</a> via <a href="/wiki/Staraya_Russa" title="Staraya Russa">Staraya Russa</a> crosses the southern tip of the district; however, there are no significant stations on this line within the district's territory.</p>
<p>The town is located along the <a href="/wiki/Veliky_Novgorod" title="Veliky Novgorod">Veliky Novgorod</a>–<a href="/wiki/Pskov" title="Pskov">Pskov</a> highway and is also connected by road to <a href="/wiki/Staraya_Russa" title="Staraya Russa">Staraya Russa</a> via <a href="/wiki/Volot,_Volot_Settlement,_Volotovsky_District,_Novgorod_Oblast" title="Volot, Volot Settlement, Volotovsky District, Novgorod Oblast">Volot</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Shelon_River" title="Shelon River">Shelon River</a> is navigable downstream of Soltsy; however, there is no passenger navigation.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soltsy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Soltsy contains one cultural heritage monument of federal significance and additionally fifty-one objects classified as cultural and historical heritage of local significance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rkn_base_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-rkn_base-13">[13]</a></sup> The federal monument is the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Ilia_Cathedral_(Soltsy)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Saint Ilia Cathedral (Soltsy) (page does not exist)">St. Ilia Cathedral</a>.</p>
