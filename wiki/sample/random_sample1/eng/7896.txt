<p><b>Pol.Gen. Somyot Poompanmoung</b> (<a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>: <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">สมยศ พุ่มพันธุ์ม่วง</span>; <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_General_System_of_Transcription" title="Royal Thai General System of Transcription"><span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant:small-caps;">rtgs</span></a>: <span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Thai transliteration"><i>Somyot Phumphanmuang</i></span>; born 27 December 1954) is a former commissioner-general of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_Police" title="Royal Thai Police">Royal Thai Police</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and current president of the <a href="/wiki/Football_Association_of_Thailand" title="Football Association of Thailand">Football Association of Thailand</a> (FAT).</p>
<p>Following the military coup of 22 May 2014, Somyot was appointed to the National Legislative Council (NLC). Members are required to reveal their assets and properties to determine if they are "unusually rich". Somyot and his wife's net worth was reported to be about 355.8 million baht (roughly US$11 million). One government critic said that this raised "...questions about how a lifelong career in the public service could have made him a millionaire."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 11 February 2016, Somyot was elected as the new president of the Football Association of Thailand.</p>
