<p><b>Sopdu</b> (also rendered <b>Septu</b> or <b>Sopedu</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egyptian_deities" title="Ancient Egyptian deities">god</a> of the sky and of eastern border regions in <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egyptian_religion" title="Ancient Egyptian religion">ancient Egyptian religion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wilkinson_211_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wilkinson_211-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a sky god, Sopdu was connected with the god <a href="/wiki/Sah_(god)" title="Sah (god)">Sah</a>, the personification of the constellation <a href="/wiki/Orion_(constellation)" title="Orion (constellation)">Orion</a>, and the goddess <a href="/wiki/Sopdet" title="Sopdet">Sopdet</a>, representing the star <a href="/wiki/Sirius" title="Sirius">Sirius</a>. According to the <i><a href="/wiki/Pyramid_Texts" title="Pyramid Texts">Pyramid Texts</a></i>, Horus-Sopdu, a combination of Sopdu and the greater sky god <a href="/wiki/Horus" title="Horus">Horus</a>, is the offspring of <a href="/wiki/Osiris" title="Osiris">Osiris</a>-Sah and <a href="/wiki/Isis" title="Isis">Isis</a>-Sopdet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wilkinson_211_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Wilkinson_211-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">

Sopdu</div>

<p>As a god of the east, Sopdu was said to protect Egyptian outposts along the frontiers and to help the <a href="/wiki/Pharaoh" title="Pharaoh">pharaoh</a> control those regions' foreign inhabitants. He was referred to as <i>Lord of the East</i>, and had his greatest <a href="/wiki/Cult_(religious_practice)" title="Cult (religious practice)">cult</a> centre at the easternmost <a href="/wiki/Nome_(Egypt)" title="Nome (Egypt)">nome</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lower_Egypt" title="Lower Egypt">Lower Egypt</a>, which was named <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Per-Sopdu" title="Per-Sopdu">Per-Sopdu</a>, meaning <i>place of Sopdu</i>. He also had shrines at Egyptian settlements in the <a href="/wiki/Sinai_Peninsula" title="Sinai Peninsula">Sinai Peninsula</a>, such as the turquoise mines at <a href="/wiki/Serabit_el-Khadim" title="Serabit el-Khadim">Serabit el-Khadim</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wilkinson_211_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Wilkinson_211-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sopdu's name is composed of the <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_hieroglyphs" title="Egyptian hieroglyphs">hieroglyph</a> for <i>sharp</i>, a pointed <a href="/wiki/Triangle" title="Triangle">triangle</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_person" title="Grammatical person">3rd person plural</a> <a href="/wiki/Suffix" title="Suffix">suffix</a> (a <a href="/wiki/Quail" title="Quail">quail</a>); thus a literal translation of his name is <i>sharp ones</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He was said, in the <i>Pyramid Texts</i>, to protect the teeth of the deceased pharaoh.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wilkinson_211_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Wilkinson_211-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sopdu was depicted as a falcon sitting on a religious standard, often with a two-feathered crown on his head and a <a href="/wiki/Flail" title="Flail">flail</a> over his shoulder. In his border-guarding role he was shown as a <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Near_East" title="Ancient Near East">Near Eastern</a> warrior, with a <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shemset&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shemset (page does not exist)">shemset</a></i> girdle and an axe or spear.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wilkinson_211_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Wilkinson_211-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
