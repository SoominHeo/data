<p><b>Song Seung-hyun</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">송승현</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: <span lang="ko-Hani">宋承炫</span>; <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>:ソン•スンヒョン born 21 August 1992<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Russell2014_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Russell2014-1">[1]</a></sup>) is a South Korean musician and actor. He is a member of rock band <a href="/wiki/F.T._Island" title="F.T. Island">F.T. Island</a>, where he serves as the guitarist, vocalist, lyricist, and composer. He is the newest member after replacing Oh Won Bin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He is the second youngest member of the group after <a href="/wiki/Choi_Min-hwan" title="Choi Min-hwan">Choi Min-hwan</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Song_Seung-hyun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>F.T. Island</b></p>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/F.T._Island" title="F.T. Island">F.T. Island</a></div>
<p>Song Seung-hyun was a FNC trainee for 3 years before joining <a href="/wiki/F.T._Island" title="F.T. Island">F.T. Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kbitesii_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-kbitesii-3">[3]</a></sup> He joined the band as a guitarist and backup vocalist<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> after former member Oh Won Bin left the group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Right after joining FT Island as Oh Won Bin's replacement, "I Believe Myself" became the first single with member Song Seung-Hyun's vocal.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Song_Seung-hyun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2009 Song Seung-hyun appeared on SBS's variety show program Idol Maknae Rebellion ("아이돌 막내반란시대").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>He also did a cameo in Style [Ep6] (SBS, 2009)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012 Song Seung-hyun makes his musical debut on 'Jack the Ripper' as Daniel.He performed for 8 days in Korea (Korea Haeorum Theater) and 3 days in Japan (Tokyo Aoyama Theater)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> His character Daniel, is one of the main characters who is part of an extremely sad romance. He begins a dangerous deal for the woman he loves. He was the youngest Daniel to be cast in 'Jack the Ripper'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, Song was announced to be joining the cast of the webdrama 'Detective Alice 2'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_Activities">Other Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Song_Seung-hyun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>With fellow band member <a href="/wiki/Choi_Jong-hoon" title="Choi Jong-hoon">Choi Jong-hoon</a>, Seung hyun modeled at the 2009 Autumn Seoul Fashion Week on 17 October 2009 for Lee Ju Young, the designer of the "Resurrection" collection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li>With fellow band member <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lee_Hongki" title="Lee Hongki">Lee Hongki</a>, Seunghyun becomes ambassadors for Gangwon-Do Province. They introduced Gangwon-Do by experiencing the place themselves. They went to Kyungpo Beach, Chamsori Museum, rode on rail bikes as well as the beach train at Jungdong-jin, East Sea. The series of photoshoot by famous Japanese photographer, Shimokoshi Haruki, portrays the beauty and charm of Gangwon-Do.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
