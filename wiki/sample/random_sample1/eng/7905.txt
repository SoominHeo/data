<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Son et lumière (show)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>

<p><i><b>Son et lumière</b></i> <small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[sɔ̃ e lymjɛʁ]</a></span> (French, lit. "sound and light"), or a <b>sound and light show</b>, is a form of nighttime entertainment that is usually presented in an outdoor venue of historic significance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Encyclop.C3.A6dia_Britannica_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Encyclop.C3.A6dia_Britannica-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Special <a href="/wiki/Lighting" title="Lighting">lighting</a> effects are projected onto the façade of a building or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ruin" title="Ruin">ruin</a> and synchronized with recorded or live <a href="/wiki/Narration" title="Narration">narration</a> and music to dramatize the history of the place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Encyclop.C3.A6dia_Britannica_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Encyclop.C3.A6dia_Britannica-1">[1]</a></sup> The invention of the concept is credited to Paul Robert-Houdin, who was the <a href="/wiki/Curator" title="Curator">curator</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Ch%C3%A2teau_de_Chambord" title="Château de Chambord">Château de Chambord</a> in France, which hosted the world's first <i>son et lumière</i> in 1952.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Encyclop.C3.A6dia_Britannica_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Encyclop.C3.A6dia_Britannica-1">[1]</a></sup> Another was established in the early 1960s at the site of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Pyramid_of_Giza" title="Great Pyramid of Giza">Great Pyramid of Giza</a> in Egypt.</p>
<p>This nighttime medium naturally lends itself to ecclesiastical buildings, stately homes and ruins,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title='"The'>citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and has rapidly become very popular in France where about 50 annual productions take place,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Encyclop.C3.A6dia_Britannica_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Encyclop.C3.A6dia_Britannica-1">[1]</a></sup> principally in the <a href="/wiki/Loire_Valley" title="Loire Valley">Loire Valley</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/Palace_of_Versailles" title="Palace of Versailles">Palace of Versailles</a> and at <a href="/wiki/Les_Invalides" title="Les Invalides">Les Invalides</a> in Paris.</p>
<p>The format usually involves no active participation by actors but a recorded narrative of the history of the building concerned by one or a cast of voices. To this is added music or <a href="/wiki/Sound_effect" title="Sound effect">sound effects</a> as appropriate, all of which is synchronised to lighting and/or projection effects which provide the visual dimension. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pyrotechnic" title="Pyrotechnic">Pyrotechnic</a> effects are occasionally included for added spectacle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Encyclop.C3.A6dia_Britannica_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Encyclop.C3.A6dia_Britannica-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>A relatively recent variation is that, rather than the music and narration coming through a concert-like sound system, they may use <a href="/wiki/Headset_(audio)" title="Headset (audio)">headsets</a>, such as in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, Pennsylvania's "Lights of Liberty". This allows an audience to move through a historic district as the show proceeds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Performances_around_the_world">Performances around the world</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Son_et_lumi%C3%A8re_(show)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Performances around the world">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In France, <i>son et lumière</i> shows are performed in and around France's historical <a href="/wiki/Ch%C3%A2teau" title="Château">châteaux</a>.</p>
<p>In neighboring <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a>, a <i>son et lumière</i> event took place in 2007 at the Chateau de Septfontaines.</p>
<p>In other countries, <i>son et lumière</i> has been mounted at the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Forum" title="Roman Forum">Forum</a> in Rome and at the <a href="/wiki/Parthenon" title="Parthenon">Parthenon</a> in Athens.</p>
<p>In the United States, the first such presentation took place at <a href="/wiki/Independence_Hall" title="Independence Hall">Independence Hall</a> in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> in 1962. Another presentation took place at George Washington's <a href="/wiki/Mount_Vernon" title="Mount Vernon">Mount Vernon</a> in May 1976 as France's bicentennial gift to the United States. The opening performance was attended by French President <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Giscard_d%27Estaing" title="Giscard d'Estaing">Giscard d'Estaing</a> and American President <a href="/wiki/Gerald_Ford" title="Gerald Ford">Gerald Ford</a>.</p>
<p>Further afield, the first African production was at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pyramids_of_Giza" title="Pyramids of Giza">Pyramids of Giza</a> at <a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">Cairo</a>, Egypt, in 1961, while the first in Asia was the <a href="/wiki/Red_Fort" title="Red Fort">Red Fort</a> in <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a>, India in 1965 by the <a href="/wiki/India_Tourism_Development_Corporation" title="India Tourism Development Corporation">India Tourism Development Corporation</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Canterbury_Cathedral" title="Canterbury Cathedral">Canterbury Cathedral</a> in England featured a <i>son et lumière</i> in 1965 and more recently in 2005, along with <a href="/wiki/Rochester_Cathedral" title="Rochester Cathedral">Rochester Cathedral</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rochester_Castle" title="Rochester Castle">Castle</a> as part of the European "Cathedrales en Lumière" project.</p>
<p>In Britain, where the majority of such productions have been staged at churches, <a href="/wiki/Cathedral" title="Cathedral">cathedrals</a> and <a href="/wiki/Abbey" title="Abbey">abbeys</a>, indoor presentations are frequently preferred, particularly as architectural gems might otherwise not be shown to best advantage. The first British production was at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenwich_Palace" title="Greenwich Palace">Greenwich Palace</a> in south London in 1957. In England in particular, where <i>son et lumière</i> is as well received as it is in France, many of the nations's principal historic houses and church buildings have enjoyed such a production. Of particular interest have been the ones at <a href="/wiki/Canterbury_Cathedral" title="Canterbury Cathedral">Canterbury Cathedral</a> (produced as part of the European "Cathedrales en Lumière" events), <a href="/wiki/Rochester_Castle" title="Rochester Castle">Rochester Castle</a>, <a href="/wiki/St_Augustine%27s_Abbey" title="St Augustine's Abbey">St Augustine's Abbey</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._George%27s_Hall,_Liverpool" title="St. George's Hall, Liverpool">St. George's Hall, Liverpool</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tewkesbury_Abbey" title="Tewkesbury Abbey">Tewkesbury Abbey</a>, <a href="/wiki/St_Paul%27s_Cathedral" title="St Paul's Cathedral">St Paul's Cathedral</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Court_Palace" title="Hampton Court Palace">Hampton Court Palace</a>, and at <a href="/wiki/Chartwell" title="Chartwell">Chartwell</a>, the country home of <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a>. Part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London_2012_Olympic_bid" title="London 2012 Olympic bid">London 2012 Olympic bid</a> involved the use of <i>son et lumière</i> inspired light projections of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sportsperson" title="Sportsperson">athletes</a> upon various London buildings.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, popular shows are at <a href="/wiki/Red_Fort" title="Red Fort">Red Fort</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amer_Fort" title="Amer Fort">Amer Fort</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Akbari_Quila&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Akbari Quila (page does not exist)">Akbari Quila</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ajmer" title="Ajmer">Ajmer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jantar_Mantar,_Jaipur" title="Jantar Mantar, Jaipur">Jantar Mantar, Jaipur</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Purana_Qila,_Delhi" title="Purana Qila, Delhi">Purana Qila, Delhi</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golconda_Fort" title="Golconda Fort">Golconda Fort</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>, a <i>son et lumière</i> show opened in <a href="/wiki/Park_of_the_Reserve" title="Park of the Reserve">Park of the Reserve</a>, Lima in 2007, called "Circuito Mágico del Agua" (<i>magic water circuit</i>) which has a <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">Guinness record</a> as the world's largest water source exposed in a public park.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Canada, the <a href="/wiki/National_Capital_Commission" title="National Capital Commission">National Capital Commission</a> has sponsored a sound and light show on <a href="/wiki/Parliament_Hill" title="Parliament Hill">Parliament Hill</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ottawa" title="Ottawa">Ottawa</a> every year since 1984, from early July to mid-September.</p>
<p>In Australia, <i>Blood Under the Southern Cross</i>, shown at <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_Hill" title="Sovereign Hill">Sovereign Hill</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ballarat" title="Ballarat">Ballarat</a> since 1992, depicts the events of the 1854 <a href="/wiki/Eureka_Rebellion" title="Eureka Rebellion">Eureka Rebellion</a>.</p>
<p>In Israel, a son et lumière production is presented both at the <a href="/wiki/Tower_of_David" title="Tower of David">Tower of David</a> in the Citadel of the <a href="/wiki/Old_City_(Jerusalem)" title="Old City (Jerusalem)">Old City of Jerusalem</a>, and at the desert butte of <a href="/wiki/Masada" title="Masada">Masada</a>.</p>
<p>Composers who have produced son et lumière shows include <a href="/wiki/Jacqueline_Nova" title="Jacqueline Nova">Jacqueline Nova</a> and <a href="/wiki/Halim_El-Dabh" title="Halim El-Dabh">Halim El-Dabh</a>. French electronic music composer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jean_Michel_Jarre" title="Jean Michel Jarre">Jean Michel Jarre</a> has incorporated son et lumière productions into his live concerts, which often take place outdoors at historical sites, sometimes with more than one million spectators.</p>
