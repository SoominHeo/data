<p><b>Song Ik-pil</b> (February 10, 1534 - August 8, 1599) was a politician and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neo-Confucian" title="Neo-Confucian">Neo-Confucian</a> scholar and educator.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> His pennames were Gubong (구봉, 龜峰) and Hyunseung (현승, 玄繩). A <a href="/wiki/Courtesy_name" title="Courtesy name">courtesy name</a> was Woonjang (운장, 雲長). Song was best friends of <a href="/wiki/Yi_I" title="Yi I">Yi I</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seong_Hon" title="Seong Hon">Seong Hon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jeong_Cheol" title="Jeong Cheol">Jeong Cheol</a> and taught <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jang-saeng" title="Kim Jang-saeng">Kim Jang-saeng</a>.</p>
