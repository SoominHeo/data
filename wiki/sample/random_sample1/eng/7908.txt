<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Invoice</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="hatnote" role="note">For the Japanese company, see <a href="/wiki/Invoice_(company)" title="Invoice (company)">Invoice (company)</a>.</div>
<dl>
<dd><i>See also <a href="/wiki/Voucher" title="Voucher">Voucher</a>: an invoice is within the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_union" title="European union">European union</a> primarily legally defined by the EU VAT directive as an <a href="/wiki/Accounting" title="Accounting">accounting</a> <a href="/wiki/Voucher" title="Voucher">voucher</a> (to verify tax and VAT reporting) and secondly as a <a href="/wiki/Civil_law_(common_law)" title="Civil law (common law)">Civil law (common law)</a> document.</i></dd>
</dl>
<p>An <b>invoice</b>, <b>bill</b> or <b>tab</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Commerce" title="Commerce">commercial</a> document issued by a <a href="/wiki/Sales" title="Sales">seller</a> to a <a href="/wiki/Buyer" title="Buyer">buyer</a>, relating to a sale transaction and indicating the <a href="/wiki/Product_(business)" title="Product (business)">products</a>, quantities, and agreed <a href="/wiki/Price" title="Price">prices</a> for products or <a href="/wiki/Service_(economics)" title="Service (economics)">services</a> the seller had provided the buyer.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Payment_terms" title="Payment terms">Payment terms</a> are usually stated on the invoice. These may specify that the buyer has a maximum number of days in which to pay and is sometimes offered a discount if paid before the due date. The buyer could have already paid for the products or services listed on the invoice.</p>
<p>In the rental industry, an invoice must include a specific reference to the duration of the time being billed. So in addition to quantity, price, and discount, the invoice amount is also based on duration. Generally, each line of a rental invoice will refer to the actual hours, days, weeks, months, etc., being billed.</p>
<p>From the point of view of a seller, an invoice is a <i>sales invoice</i>. From the point of view of a buyer, an invoice is a <i>purchase invoice</i>. The document indicates the buyer and seller, but the term <i>invoice</i> indicates money is owed <i>or</i> owing.</p>

<p><b>A typical invoice may contain:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></b></p>
<ul>
<li>The word <i>invoice</i> (or <i>Tax Invoice</i>);</li>
<li>A unique reference number (in case of correspondence about the invoice);</li>
<li>Date of the invoice;</li>
<li>Credit terms;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>Tax payments, if relevant (e.g., GST or <a href="/wiki/Value-added_tax" title="Value-added tax">VAT</a>);</li>
<li>Name and contact details of the seller;</li>
<li>Tax or company registration details of seller, if relevant [e.g. Australia Business Number (ABN) for Australian businesses];</li>
<li>Name and contact details of the buyer;</li>
<li>Date that the goods or service was sent or delivered;</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Purchase_order" title="Purchase order">Purchase order</a> number (or similar tracking numbers requested by the buyer to be mentioned on the invoice);</li>
<li>Description of the product(s);</li>
<li>Unit price(s) of the product(s), if relevant;</li>
<li>Total amount charged (optionally with breakdown of taxes, if relevant);</li>
<li>Payment terms (including method of payment, date of payment, and details about charges for late payment);</li>
</ul>
<p>In countries where <a href="/wiki/Wire_transfer" title="Wire transfer">wire transfer</a> is the preferred method of settling debts, the printed bill will contain the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bank_account_number" title="Bank account number">bank account number</a> of the creditor and usually a reference code to be passed along the transaction identifying the payer.</p>
<p>The US Defense Logistics Agency requires an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Employer_identification_number" title="Employer identification number">employer identification number</a> on invoices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The European Union requires a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Value_added_tax_identification_number" title="Value added tax identification number">VAT (value added tax) identification number</a>.</p>
<p>In Canada, the registration number for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goods_and_Services_Tax_(Canada)" title="Goods and Services Tax (Canada)">GST</a> purposes must be furnished for all supplies over $30 made by a registered supplier in order to claim input tax credits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Recommendations about invoices used in international trade are also provided by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Economic_Commission_for_Europe" title="United Nations Economic Commission for Europe">UNECE</a> Committee on Trade, which involves a more detailed description of the logistics aspect of merchandise and therefore may be convenient for international logistics and customs procedures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>The EU VAT union Invoice definition</b></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/European_Union_value_added_tax" title="European Union value added tax">European Union value added tax</a> union - <i>Council Directive 2006/112/EC of 28 November 2006 on the common system of value added tax</i> article 226 is a concise definition of invoices within the union member states.</p>
<p>Without prejudice to the particular provisions laid down in this Directive, only the following details are required for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VAT" title="VAT">VAT</a> purposes on invoices issued pursuant to Articles 220 and 221:</p>
<ul>
<li>(1) the date of issue;</li>
<li>(2) a sequential number, based on one or more series, which uniquely identifies the invoice;</li>
<li>(3) the <a href="/wiki/VAT_identification_number" title="VAT identification number">VAT identification number</a> referred to in Article 214 under which the taxable person supplied the goods or services;</li>
<li>(4) the customer's <a href="/wiki/VAT_identification_number" title="VAT identification number">VAT identification number</a>, as referred to in Article 214, under which the customer received a supply of goods or services in respect of which he is liable for payment of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VAT" title="VAT">VAT</a>, or received a supply of goods as referred to in Article 138;</li>
<li>(5) the full name and address of the taxable person and of the customer;</li>
<li>(6) the quantity and nature of the goods supplied or the extent and nature of the services rendered;</li>
<li>(7) the date on which the supply of goods or services was made or completed or the date on which the <a href="/wiki/Payment" title="Payment">payment</a> on account referred to in points (4) and (5) of Article 220 was made, in so far as that date can be determined and differs from the date of issue of the invoice;</li>
<li>(7a) where the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VAT" title="VAT">VAT</a> becomes chargeable at the time when the <a href="/wiki/Payment" title="Payment">payment</a> is received in accordance with Article 66(b) and the right of deduction arises at the time the deductible tax becomes chargeable, the mention ‘<a href="/wiki/Cash_method_of_accounting" title="Cash method of accounting">Cash accounting</a>’;</li>
<li>(8) the taxable amount per rate or exemption, the unit price exclusive of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VAT" title="VAT">VAT</a> and any <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Discounts" title="Discounts">discounts</a> or <a href="/wiki/Rebate_(marketing)" title="Rebate (marketing)">rebates</a> if they are not included in the unit price;</li>
<li>(9) the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VAT" title="VAT">VAT</a> rate applied;</li>
<li>(10) the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VAT" title="VAT">VAT</a> amount payable, except where a special arrangement is applied under which, in accordance with this Directive, such a detail is excluded;</li>
<li>(10a) where the customer receiving a supply issues the invoice instead of the supplier, the mention ‘Self-billing’;</li>
<li>(11) in the case of an <a href="/wiki/Tax_exemption" title="Tax exemption">exemption</a>, reference to the applicable provision of this Directive, or to the corresponding national provision, or any other reference indicating that the supply of goods or services is exempt;</li>
<li>(11a) where the customer is liable for the payment of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VAT" title="VAT">VAT</a>, the mention ‘Reverse charge’;</li>
<li>(12) in the case of the supply of a new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Means_of_transport" title="Means of transport">means of transport</a> made in accordance with the conditions specified in Article 138(1) and (2)(a), the characteristics as identified in point (b) of Article 2(2);</li>
<li>(13) where the margin scheme for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Travel_agent" title="Travel agent">travel agents</a> is applied, the mention ‘Margin scheme — <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Travel_agent" title="Travel agent">Travel agents</a>’;</li>
<li>(14) where one of the special arrangements applicable to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second-hand_goods" title="Second-hand goods">second-hand goods</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Works_of_art" title="Works of art">works of art</a>, collectors’ items and antiques is applied, the mention ‘Margin scheme — <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second-hand_goods" title="Second-hand goods">Second-hand goods</a>’; ‘Margin scheme — <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Works_of_art" title="Works of art">Works of art</a>’ or ‘Margin scheme — Collector’s items and antiques’ respectively;</li>
<li>(15) where the person liable for payment of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VAT" title="VAT">VAT</a> is a tax representative for the purposes of Article 204, the (<a href="/wiki/VAT_identification_number" title="VAT identification number">VAT identification number</a>, referred to in Article 214, of that tax representative, together with his full name and address.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>The EU VAT union Receipt (simple invoice) definition</b></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/European_Union_value_added_tax" title="European Union value added tax">European Union value added tax</a> union - <i>Council Directive 2006/112/EC of 28 November 2006 on the common system of value added tax</i> article 226b is a concise definition of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Receipts" title="Receipts">Receipts</a> within the union member states.</p>
<p>As regards simplified invoices issued pursuant to Article 220a and Article 221(1) and (2), Member States shall require at least the following details:</p>
<ul>
<li>(a) the date of issue;</li>
<li>(b) identification of the taxable person supplying the goods or services (<a href="/wiki/VAT_identification_number" title="VAT identification number">VAT identification number</a>);</li>
<li>(c) identification of the type of goods or services supplied;</li>
<li>(d) the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VAT" title="VAT">VAT</a> amount payable or the information needed to calculate it;</li>
<li>(e) where the invoice issued is a document or message treated as an invoice pursuant to Article 219, specific and unambiguous reference to that initial invoice and the specific details which are being amended.</li>
</ul>
<p>They may not require details on invoices other than those referred to in Articles 226, 227 and 230.</p>
<p><b>The Australian Tax Office Tax Invoice Definition</b></p>
<p>Details about the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian_Tax_Office" title="Australian Tax Office">Australian Tax Office</a> (ATO) requirements for a Tax Invoice can be found on their website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> For all GST purposes, a seller must issue a Tax Invoice to the buyer regardless the sale being in cash or credit. Hence, a Tax Invoice in Australia serves as an Invoice as well as a receipt in the conventional sense. The words 'Paid' or 'Payable' will differentiate meaning. The Tax Invoice must contain seven facts as per the GST Tax Law.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Variations">Variations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Invoice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Variations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are different types of invoices:</p>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Pro_forma" title="Pro forma">Pro forma</a></b> invoice — In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foreign_trade" title="Foreign trade">foreign trade</a>, a <i>pro forma</i> invoice is a document that states a commitment from the seller to provide specified goods to the buyer at specific prices. It is often used to declare value for <a href="/wiki/Customs" title="Customs">customs</a>. It is not an actual invoice, and thus the seller does not record a <i>pro forma</i> invoice as an <a href="/wiki/Accounts_receivable" title="Accounts receivable">account receivable</a> and the buyer does not record a <i>pro forma</i> invoice as an <a href="/wiki/Accounts_payable" title="Accounts payable">account payable</a>. A <i>pro forma</i> invoice is not issued by the seller until the seller and buyer have agreed to the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Order_(business)" title="Order (business)">order</a>. In a few cases, a <i>pro forma</i> invoice is issued to request advance payments from the buyer, either to allow production to start or for security of the goods produced.</li>
<li><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Credit_memo" title="Credit memo">Credit memo</a></b> - If the buyer returns the goods, the seller usually issues a credit memo for the same or lower amount than the invoice, and then refunds the money to the buyer, or the buyer can apply that credit memo to another invoice.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Commercial_invoice" title="Commercial invoice">Commercial invoice</a></b> - a <a href="/wiki/Customs_declaration" title="Customs declaration">customs declaration</a> form used in international trade that describes the parties involved in the shipping transaction, the goods being transported, and the value of the goods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It is the primary document used by customs, and must meet specific customs requirements, such as the <a href="/wiki/Harmonized_System" title="Harmonized System">Harmonized System</a> number and the country of manufacture. It is used to calculate <a href="/wiki/Tariff" title="Tariff">tariffs</a>.</li>
<li><b>Debit memo</b> - When a company fails to pay or short-pays an invoice, it is common practice to issue a <i>debit memo</i> for the <a href="/wiki/Balance_(accounting)" title="Balance (accounting)">balance</a> and any late fees owed. In function, debit memos are identical to invoices.</li>
<li><b>Self-billing invoice</b> - A <i>self billing invoice</i> is when the buyer issues the invoice to himself (e.g. according to the consumption levels he is taking out of a <a href="/wiki/Vendor-managed_inventory" title="Vendor-managed inventory">vendor-managed inventory</a> stock).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The buyer (i.e. the issuer) should treat the invoice as an account payable, and the seller should treat it as an account receivable. If there is tax on the sale, e.g. VAT or GST, then buyer and seller may need to adjust their tax accounts in accordance with tax legislation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Evaluated receipt settlement (ERS)</b> - ERS is a process of paying for goods and services from a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Packing_slip" title="Packing slip">packing slip</a> rather than from a separate invoice document. The payee uses data in the packing slip to apply the payments. "In an ERS transaction, the supplier ships goods based upon an Advance Shipping Notice (ASN), and the purchaser, upon <a href="/wiki/Receipt" title="Receipt">receipt</a>, confirms the existence of a corresponding purchase order or contract, verifies the identity and quantity of the goods, and then pays the supplier."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Timesheet</b> - Invoices for hourly services issued by businesses such as <a href="/wiki/Lawyer" title="Lawyer">lawyers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Consultant" title="Consultant">consultants</a> often pull data from a <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/timesheet" title="wiktionary:timesheet">timesheet</a>. A timesheet invoice may also be generated by <a href="/wiki/Operated_equipment_rental" title="Operated equipment rental">Operated equipment rental companies</a> where the invoice will be a combination of timesheet based charges and equipment rental charges.</li>
<li><b>Statement</b> - A periodic customer statement includes opening balance, invoices, payments, credit memos, debit memos, and ending balance for the customer's account during a specified period. A monthly statement can be used as a summary invoice to request a single payment for accrued monthly charges.</li>
<li><b>Progress billing</b> used to obtain partial payment on extended contracts, particularly in the construction industry (see <a href="/wiki/Schedule_of_values" title="Schedule of values">Schedule of values</a>)</li>
<li><b>Collective Invoicing</b> is also known as monthly invoicing in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. Japanese businesses tend to have many orders with small amounts because of the outsourcing system (<a href="/wiki/Keiretsu" title="Keiretsu">Keiretsu</a>), or of demands for less inventory control (<a href="/wiki/Kanban" title="Kanban">Kanban</a>). To save the administration work, invoicing is normally processed on monthly basis.</li>
<li><b>Continuation or Recurring Invoicing</b> is standard within the equipment rental industry, including tool rental. A recurring invoice is one generated on a cyclical basis during the lifetime of a rental contract. For example, if you rent an excavator from 1 January to 15 April, on a calendar monthly arrears billing cycle, you would expect to receive an invoice at the end of January, another at the end of February, another at the end of March and a final Off-rent invoice would be generated at the point when the asset is returned. The same principle would be adopted if you were invoiced in advance, or if you were invoiced on a specific day of the month.</li>
<li><b>Electronic Invoicing</b> is not necessarily the same as EDI invoicing. Electronic invoicing in its widest sense embraces EDI as well as XML invoice messages as well as other format such as pdf. Historically, other formats such as pdf were not included in the wider definition of an electronic invoice because they were not machine readable and the process benefits of an electronic message could not be achieved. However, as data extraction techniques have evolved and as environmental concerns have begun to dominate the business case for the implementation of electronic invoicing, other formats are now incorporated into the wider definition.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Electronic">Electronic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Invoice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Electronic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some invoices are no longer paper-based, but rather transmitted electronically over the Internet. It is still common for electronic remittance or invoicing to be printed in order to maintain paper records. Standards for electronic invoicing vary widely from country to country. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electronic_Data_Interchange" title="Electronic Data Interchange">Electronic Data Interchange</a> (EDI) standards such as the United Nation's <a href="/wiki/EDIFACT" title="EDIFACT">EDIFACT</a> standard include message encoding guidelines for electronic invoices. The <a href="/wiki/EDIFACT" title="EDIFACT">EDIFACT</a> is followed up in the <a href="/wiki/UN/CEFACT" title="UN/CEFACT">UN/CEFACT</a> <a href="/wiki/EbXML" title="EbXML">ebXML</a> syntax cross industry invoice.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="EDIFACT">EDIFACT</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Invoice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: EDIFACT">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The United Nations standard for electronic invoices ("INVOIC") includes standard codes for transmitting header information (common to the entire invoice) and codes for transmitting details for each of the line items (products or services). The "INVOIC" standard can also be used to transmit credit and debit memos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The "IFTMCS" standard is used to transmit freight invoices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the European Union legislation was passed in 2010 in the form of directive 2010/45/EU to facilitate the growth of Electronic Invoicing across all its member states. This legislation caters for varying VAT and inter-country invoicing requirements within the EU, in addition to legislating for the authenticity and integrity of invoices being sent electronically. It is estimated that in 2011 alone roughly 5 million EU businesses will have sent Electronic Invoices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Open_Application_Group_Integration_Specification_.28OAGIS.29_from_OAGi">Open Application Group Integration Specification (OAGIS) from OAGi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Invoice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Open Application Group Integration Specification (OAGIS) from OAGi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/XML" title="XML">XML</a> message format for electronic invoices has been used since the inception of XML in 1998. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Open_Application_Group_Integration_Specification&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Open Application Group Integration Specification (page does not exist)">Open Application Group Integration Specification</a> (OAGIS) has included an invoice since 2001. <a class="external text" href="http://www.oagi.org/dnn2/" rel="nofollow">OAGi (Open Applications Group)</a> has a working relationship with UN/CEFACT where OAGi and its members participate in defining many of the Technology and Methodology specifications. OAGi also includes support for these Technology and Methodology specifications within OAGIS.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="UBL">UBL</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Invoice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: UBL">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/XML" title="XML">XML</a> message format for electronic invoices has been used in recent years. There are two standards currently being developed. One is the cross industry invoice under development by the United Nations standards body <a href="/wiki/UN/CEFACT" title="UN/CEFACT">UN/CEFACT</a> and the other is <a href="/wiki/Universal_Business_Language" title="Universal Business Language">UBL (Universal Business Language)</a> which is issued by <a href="/wiki/OASIS_(organization)" title="OASIS (organization)">OASIS (Organization for the Advancement of Structured Information Standards)</a>. Implementations of invoices based on UBL are common, most importantly in the public sector in <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> as it was the first country where UBL is mandated by law for all the invoices of the public sector. Further implementations are under way in the Scandinavian countries as result of the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Business_Language" title="Universal Business Language">NES (North European Subset) project</a>. Implementations are also underway in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> (UBL 2.0)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and with the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> itself.</p>
<p>The NES work has been transferred to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Committee_for_Standardisation" title="European Committee for Standardisation">CEN (European Committee for Standardisation)</a>, (the standards body of the European Union) workshop CEN/BII, for public procurement in Europe. The result of that work is a pre-condition for PEPPOL, pan European pilots for public procurement, financed by the European commission. There UBL procurement documents will be implemented in cross border pilots between European countries.</p>
<p>Agreement has been made between UBL and UN/CEFACT for convergence of the two XML messages standards with the objective of merging the two standards into one before end of 2009 including the provision of an upgrade path for implementations started in either standard.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="ISDOC">ISDOC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Invoice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: ISDOC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="external text" href="http://www.isdoc.org/" rel="nofollow">ISDOC</a> is a standard that was developed in the Czech Republic as a universal format for electronic invoices. On 16 October 2008, 14 companies and the Czech government signed a declaration to use this format within one year in their products.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Payment">Payment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Invoice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Payment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Organizations purchasing goods and services usually have a process in place for approving payment of invoices based on an employee's confirmation that the goods or services have been received.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Typically, when paying an invoice, a <a href="/wiki/Remittance_advice" title="Remittance advice">remittance advice</a> will be sent to the supplier to inform them their invoice has been paid.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Standardisation">Standardisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Invoice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Standardisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Invoices are different from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Receipts" title="Receipts">receipts</a>. Both invoices and receipts are ways of tracking purchases of goods and services. In general the content of the invoices can be similar to that of receipts including tracking the amount of the sale, calculating sales tax owed and calculating any discounts applied to the purchase.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Invoices differ from receipts in that invoices serve to notify customers of payments owed, whereas receipts serve as proof of completed payment.</p>
