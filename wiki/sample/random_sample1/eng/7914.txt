<p><b>Södertälje</b> (<small>Swedish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Swedish" title="Help:IPA for Swedish">[sœdɛ²ʈɛlːjɛ]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:Sv-S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje.ogg" title="File:Sv-Södertälje.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c8/Sv-S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje.ogg" title="Sv-Södertälje.ogg">listen</a>)</small>) is a <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> and the seat of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje_Municipality" title="Södertälje Municipality">Södertälje Municipality</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_County" title="Stockholm County">Stockholm County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> with 75,283 inhabitants in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scb_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-scb-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrial_city" title="Industrial city">industrial city</a>, about 30 kilometers (19 mi) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>, is the home to truck maker <a href="/wiki/Scania_AB" title="Scania AB">Scania AB</a> and the research and development arm of a top 10 pharmaceutical company <a href="/wiki/AstraZeneca" title="AstraZeneca">AstraZeneca</a>. Södertälje is an international municipality and this fact is reflected in business, cultural life and the population.</p>
<p>About 39 percent of Södertälje's inhabitants have foreign backgrounds, and this proportion increases by 1.5 percent per year. <a href="/wiki/Syriac_Orthodox_Christians_(Middle_East)" title="Syriac Orthodox Christians (Middle East)">Western-Assyrians</a> are one of the largest groups of immigrants in Södertälje.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to 600 AD, the lake <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A4laren" title="Mälaren">Mälaren</a> was connected to the sea. Due to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Land_elevation" title="Land elevation">land elevation</a>, the lake was slowly cut off, and boats had to be carried over land to and from the lake.</p>
<p>The name <i>Telje</i> is mentioned in the 11th century. To resolve a name conflict with another town that was founded north of Stockholm in the 17th century, <i>Söder</i> (meaning <i>south</i>) was added to create <i>Södertälje</i>; <i>Norr</i> (<i>North</i>) was added to the other to create <i><a href="/wiki/Norrt%C3%A4lje" title="Norrtälje">Norrtälje</a>.</i></p>
<p>In the 18th century Södertälje had a <a href="/wiki/Charter" title="Charter">charter</a>. Due to the <a href="/wiki/Great_Northern_War" title="Great Northern War">Great Northern War</a> and a series of <a href="/wiki/Bubonic_plague" title="Bubonic plague">plague</a> epidemics, the population of the city dipped to barely above 200.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Assyrians/Syriacs_in_Sweden" title="Assyrians/Syriacs in Sweden">Assyrians/Syriacs in Sweden</a></div>
<p>The first <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_people" title="Assyrian people">Assyrians</a> arrived in 1967 as refugees from Turkey and were invited to settle here as workers for the understaffed factories in the area. However, the small community skyrocketed within a decade due to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turkey-PKK_conflict" title="Turkey-PKK conflict">a war between Kurdish freedom fighters and the Turkish Government</a> during the 1980s which displaced tens of thousands of Assyrians because it made the region they lived in, known as <a href="/wiki/Tur_Abdin" title="Tur Abdin">Tur Abdin</a>, unsafe for them. In more recent times, the <a href="/wiki/Iraqi_insurgency_(2003%E2%80%9311)" title="Iraqi insurgency (2003–11)">Iraqi insurgency (2003–11)</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Civil_War" title="Syrian Civil War">Syrian Civil War</a> have caused the Assyrian community to grow even larger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the city, Assyrians have five churches, two bishops, two soccer teams (<a href="/wiki/Assyriska_FF" title="Assyriska FF">Assyriska FF</a> and <a href="/wiki/Syrianska_FC" title="Syrianska FC">Syrianska FC</a>), several shops, an Assyrian/Syriac association and the headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/Syriac_language" title="Syriac language">Syriac language</a> TV Channels <a href="/wiki/Suroyo_TV" title="Suroyo TV">Suroyo TV</a> and <a href="/wiki/Suryoyo_Sat" title="Suryoyo Sat">Suryoyo Sat</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sodertalje.se_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-sodertalje.se-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Outside of the Assyrian community, Other immigrant groups are mainly from <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a> and former <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>. During the Iraq war 1,500 <a href="/wiki/Mandaeans" title="Mandaeans">Mandaeans</a> also fled to Södertälje, and now make up one of the largest communities of Mandeans in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> However, not many Muslim immigrants live in Sodertalje, as they often suffer hate crimes by the Assyrians of the city, who were oppressed by Muslims in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Patheos1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Patheos1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The most spoken languages in Södertälje besides <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a>, which is the national language, are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turoyo" title="Turoyo">Turoyo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_Neo-Aramaic" title="Assyrian Neo-Aramaic">Assyrian Neo-Aramaic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a>. To a lesser extent, <a href="/wiki/Finnish_language" title="Finnish language">Finnish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a> are also relatively common second languages.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Truck" title="Truck">Truck</a> manufacturer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scania_(company)" title="Scania (company)">Scania AB</a> has its main location in Södertälje. It is also one of the main sites for pharmaceutical company <a href="/wiki/AstraZeneca" title="AstraZeneca">AstraZeneca</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Port" title="Port">port</a> of Södertälje is the second in the <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> region. <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Group" title="Volkswagen Group">Volkswagen Group</a> has its Swedish headquarters located in Södertälje, and Lantmännen Axa Foodservice AB is located in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J%C3%A4rna" title="Järna">Järna</a> 10 km south of Södertälje.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje_BBK" title="Södertälje BBK">Södertälje BBK</a>, SBBK is one of the best in the country, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%B6dertalje_KINGS&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Södertalje KINGS (page does not exist)">Södertalje KINGS</a> became Swedish Champions in 2013, 2014 and 2015. In total SBBK has got 10 Gold for the male team <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%B6dertalje_KINGS&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Södertalje KINGS (page does not exist)">Södertalje KINGS</a> and 12 Gold for the female team <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Telge_Basket&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Telge Basket (page does not exist)">Telge Basket</a>. SBBK has in total 132 Swedish Championships since the star in 1968. <a href="/wiki/T%C3%A4ljehallen" title="Täljehallen">Täljehallen</a> is the home for SBBK.</p>
<p>The city is home to <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje_SK" title="Södertälje SK">Södertälje SK</a>, a classic and successful <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> team currently playing in Sweden's second highest league – <a href="/wiki/HockeyAllsvenskan" title="HockeyAllsvenskan">HockeyAllsvenskan</a> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scaniarinken" title="Scaniarinken">Scaniarinken</a> as their home arena. <a href="/wiki/Assyriska_FF" title="Assyriska FF">Assyriska FF</a> and <a href="/wiki/Syrianska_FC" title="Syrianska FC">Syrianska FC</a> are also two successful <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> clubs started in 1974 and 1977. They play in the same arena, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje_Fotbollsarena" title="Södertälje Fotbollsarena">Södertälje Fotbollsarena</a>.</p>
<p>Södertälje Storm Rugby League club are a pioneering <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rugby_League" title="Rugby League">Rugby League</a> team, playing in the Swedish National Rugby League, which was formed in 2015.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town is situated on a <a href="/wiki/Headlands_and_bays" title="Headlands and bays">bay</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lake_M%C3%A4laren" title="Lake Mälaren">Lake Mälaren</a>, which is here connected with the <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Sea" title="Baltic Sea">Baltic Sea</a> by the <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje_Canal" title="Södertälje Canal">Södertälje Canal</a>, 35 miles (56 km) in length, with a minimum depth of 20 ft (6.1 m). This is on the route followed by the <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6ta_Canal" title="Göta Canal">Göta Canal</a> <a href="/wiki/Steamboat" title="Steamboat">steamboats</a> between <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gothenburg" title="Gothenburg">Gothenburg</a>. It was opened in 1819 and much enlarged in 1924, though a <a href="/wiki/Canal" title="Canal">canal</a> was begun here in the first half of the 15th century at the instigation of the patriot <a href="/wiki/Engelbrekt_Engelbrektsson" title="Engelbrekt Engelbrektsson">Engelbrekt Engelbrektsson</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Södertälje, and the rest of Stockholm region has a <a href="/wiki/Humid_continental_climate" title="Humid continental climate">humid continental climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen</a> <i>Dfb</i>) and displays four distinct seasons. Due to the city's high northerly latitude, daylight varies widely from more than 18 hours around midsummer, to only around 6 hours in midwinter. Södertälje has much warmer and sunnier weather than other locations at the same latitude mainly because of the influence of <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a>. The city enjoys 1,981 hours of sunshine annually.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Summers have an average daytime high temperatures of 20–23 °C (68–73 °F) and lows of around 15 °C (59 °F), but there are periods of heat waves and many days with temperatures above 25 °C (77 °F)+). Winters are cold, though sometimes milder with temperatures ranging from −3 to 1 °C (27 to 34 °F), while spring and autumn mainly cool.</p>
<p>Annual precipitation is 539 mm (21.2 in) with light to moderate rainfall throughout the year. Snow mainly occurs from December through March, but snow covers does not remain for a long time.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_transport">Public transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Public transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Södertälje is served by the <a href="/wiki/Public_transport_in_Stockholm" title="Public transport in Stockholm">Stockholm public transport</a> system, but has also a main line railway station for connections outside <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_County" title="Stockholm County">Stockholm County</a>. The main <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_station" title="Railway station">railway station</a> is <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje_Syd_railway_station" title="Södertälje Syd railway station">Södertälje Syd railway station</a> (<i>South</i>) and located some 5 kilometres (3.1 mi) southeast of the city center where most national trains stop. From Södertälje Syd, it is possible to take the <a href="/wiki/Public_transport_in_Stockholm" title="Public transport in Stockholm">Stockholm public transport</a> to all stations in the Södertälje area.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_commuter_rail" title="Stockholm commuter rail">Stockholm commuter rail</a> system has six stations in the municipality. 4 of them in the Södertälje city itself, Södertälje Centrum (centre), Södertälje Hamn (harbour), Södertälje Syd (south) and Östertälje, and 2 stations outside the city but within the municipality; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J%C3%A4rna" title="Järna">Järna</a> and <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B6lnbo" title="Mölnbo">Mölnbo</a>.</p>
