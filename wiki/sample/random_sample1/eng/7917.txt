<p><i><b>Scorzonera</b></i> is a genus of flowering plants in the <a href="/wiki/Cichorieae" title="Cichorieae">dandelion tribe</a> within the <a href="/wiki/Asteraceae" title="Asteraceae">daisy family</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fna_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-fna-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>They are distributed in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fna_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-fna-3">[3]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-duran_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-duran-4">[4]</a></sup> Its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Center_of_diversity" title="Center of diversity">center of diversity</a> is in the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Basin" title="Mediterranean Basin">Mediterranean</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Well-known species include the edible <a href="/wiki/Scorzonera_hispanica" title="Scorzonera hispanica">black salsify</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Scorzonera_hispanica" title="Scorzonera hispanica">Scorzonera hispanica</a></i>). <i><a href="/wiki/Scorzonera_tau-saghyz" title="Scorzonera tau-saghyz">Scorzonera tau-saghyz</a></i> is a source of natural <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rubber" title="Rubber">rubber</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Scorzonera</i> is recorded as a food plant for the <a href="/wiki/Larva" title="Larva">larva</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Nutmeg_(moth)" title="Nutmeg (moth)">Nutmeg</a>, a species of <a href="/wiki/Moth" title="Moth">moth</a>.</p>

<p>One possible origin of the genus name is the <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> <i>scorzonère</i> ("viper’s grass").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fna_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-fna-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Secondary_metabolites">Secondary metabolites</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scorzonera&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Secondary metabolites">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some <i>Scorzonera</i> species contain <a href="/wiki/Lactone" title="Lactone">lactones</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Sesquiterpene_lactone" title="Sesquiterpene lactone">sesquiterpene lactones</a> known as guaianolides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Flavonoid" title="Flavonoid">Flavonoids</a> found in <i>Scorzonera</i> include <a href="/wiki/Apigenin" title="Apigenin">apigenin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kaempferol" title="Kaempferol">kaempferol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luteolin" title="Luteolin">luteolin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Quercetin" title="Quercetin">quercetin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Other <a href="/wiki/Secondary_metabolite" title="Secondary metabolite">secondary metabolites</a> reported from the genus include <a href="/wiki/Caffeoylquinic_acid" title="Caffeoylquinic acid">caffeoylquinic acids</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coumarin" title="Coumarin">coumarins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lignan" title="Lignan">lignans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stilbenoid" title="Stilbenoid">stilbenoids</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Triterpenoid" title="Triterpenoid">triterpenoids</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> One unique class of stilbenoid derivative was first isolated from <i><a href="/wiki/Scorzonera_humilis" title="Scorzonera humilis">Scorzonera humilis</a></i>. They were named the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tyrolobibenzyl&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tyrolobibenzyl (page does not exist)">tyrolobibenzyls</a> after <a href="/wiki/Tyrol%E2%80%93South_Tyrol%E2%80%93Trentino_Euroregion" title="Tyrol–South Tyrol–Trentino Euroregion">Tyrol</a> in the eastern <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">Alps</a>, where the plant was collected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
