<p><i><b>Shomuni</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ショムニ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (also called <b>Power Office Girls</b>) is a comedic TV drama serial based on the <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> of the same name by Hiroyuki Yasuda <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">安田弘之</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yasuda Hiroyuki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, though much of the details (all besides the company name and the characters) have departed from the comic. Released in 1998, Shomuni was a surprise winner of the ratings war for drama serials aired in Japan between April to June, despite being projected to finish a lowly thirteenth in a survey conducted by a major TV station.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Story">Story</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shomuni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Story">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The stories revolve around the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Office_Lady" title="Office Lady">office ladies (OL)</a></i> of General Administration Section 2 (<i>Shomu ni</i>, or GA-2) in a large multinational company called Manpan Corporation.</p>
<p>GA-2 is called "the graveyard for female office ladies", simply because it is the place where female employees are dumped should they mess up big time elsewhere. Rarely is anyone ever allowed to leave for other more purposeful departments. Jobs include replacing used toilet rolls, changing light bulbs, organizing company outings, and other menial tasks. GA-2 is located in the basement, which is in fact a disused store room.</p>
<p>The purpose of GA-2 is to convince these errant – or simply useless – employees to quit (and typically people last three days at most) due to the monotonous and meaningless nature of the job, just so that the company, headed by sex-crazed executives and a Personnel Section willing to stoop to any means, will save on the costs of firing them.</p>
<p>However, the employees posted to GA-2 exhibit no intention to quit, to the chagrin of the Personnel Section Chief Torao Terasaki <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">寺崎寅男</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Terasaki Torao</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> and his assistant Nonomura <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">野々村</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, since the people at Shomuni are happy their menial jobs give them more than enough time to pursue their other interests during office hours.</p>
<p>As the drama series progresses, it is evident that these seemingly lowly employees have their own pride despite them being the most despised employees in the company, and in every episode, they end up saving the company from a potential catastrophe, inadvertently or otherwise.</p>
<p>GA-2 has one male employee, the friendly and affable Section Chief Kōji Inoue <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">井上洸二</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Inoue Kōji</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, whose role can at best be described as that of a nominal caretaker. Save for caring for a female tabby cat, he is an old man who appears to be only interested in waiting for his retirement age while idling in the GA-2 office.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shomuni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>GA-2 was composed of four women from the start of the first episode, and is seen first through the eyes of Tsukahara Sawako (the fifth woman to join GA-2).</p>
<ol>
<li><b>Chinatsu Tsuboi</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">坪井千夏</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tsuboi Chinatsu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the tallest person in GA-2 and is also often their leader. She is responsible for carrying the ladder which GA-2 uses for fixing lightbulbs. Her uniform skirt is shorter than those of the other women in GA-2. Chinatsu believes that a woman's worth is determined by how many men she "conquers", or sleeps with (Episode 1). Before she entered Manpan Corporation, she used to work as a nightclub hostess (episode 1).</li>
<li><b>Kana Miyashita</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">宮下佳奈</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Miyashita Kana</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> has a past which few people know about. She is often not seen having lunch with her fellow GA-2 colleagues in the company canteen, for she spends lunch hour with different male high-ranking executives of the company. In episode 6 of the first season, it is revealed that Kana was the tenth mistress of Manpan's late president, and that he sent her expensive gifts of jewelry</li>
<li><b>Azusa Tokunaga</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">徳永あずさ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tokunaga Azusa</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the second oldest employee in GA-2, after Section Chief Inoue. Nobody in GA-2 seems to remember how she ended up in GA-2. She is responsible for making announcements as well as drawing up the daily company routines of GA-2. Highly interested in stock investments. Azusa is also trying to find a husband, and once slipped a personals ad into the folder of a high powered executive.</li>
<li><b>Rie Himukai</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">日向リエ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Himukai Rie</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the quirkiest of the GA-2 members. Her mother sells antiques and her father has a photocopy store. She has a night job as a fortune teller at night after work at GA-2 - and her predictions are never wrong. Her foresight often sparks an entire episode, depending on GA-2's interpretation on her prediction. Much of her comic appeal comes from her deadpan delivery.</li>
<li><b>Sawako Tsukahara</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">塚原佐和子</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tsukuhara Sawako</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the youngest (at age 21) employee in GA-2, and is very incompetent at getting the job done correctly due to her extremely simple and innocent manner. After knocking down the president of Manpan Corporation in an elevator lobby (as the result of a fight between her and a woman in an elevator) she was posted to GA-2. She is in love with Mr. Ukyo, and Azusa labels her to be 'ambitious'.</li>
<li><b>Ume Maruhashi</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">丸橋梅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Maruhashi Ume</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> At the end of the third episode, she became the sixth member of GA-2. She attended elementary school with Chinatsu, and is one of the few people who can get Chinatsu visibly angry. In episode 7, Ume tells Sawako she remembers Chinatsu as "...a big crybaby who got nicknamed Gassy Chinatsu." Highly ambitious, she is learning several languages (such as Arabic) and aims to leave GA-2 as soon as she can. She is a computer analyst, and specializes in acquiring information.</li>
</ol>
<p>Each of the twelve episodes usually focuses on the developments of one character of Shomuni. Each episode is independent of another, except for the season's finale.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequel">Sequel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shomuni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sequel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Two sequels to Shomuni were made, as well as three Special movie editions. Shomuni was followed up by two TV movies: Shomuni Special 1 (aired on October 7, 1998), Shomuni Special 2 (aired on January 2, 2000), and two sequel drama serials: Shomuni 2 (aired from April 12, 2000 to June 28, 2000) and the other was Shomuni Final (July 3, 2002 - September 18, 2002). A third TV movie, Shomuni Forever, was aired on January 1, 2003 to round off the drama series.[2] In the end, each individual leave GA-2 2 months apart from one another. Tsukahara is last.</p>
<p>In 2013, another Shomuni were made since the previous season departure on 2002, known as Shomuni 2013 or Shomuni 4/Power Office Girls 4, premiering on Fuji TV on July 10th. Led by the same actress Esumi Makiko as the main protagonist, this season were completely backed up by a total new cast as supporting.</p>
