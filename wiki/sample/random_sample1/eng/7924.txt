<p><i><b>Shortland Street</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> prime-time soap opera centring on the fictitious <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shortland_Street_Hospital" title="Shortland Street Hospital">Shortland Street Hospital</a>, first broadcast on <a href="/wiki/TVNZ_2" title="TVNZ 2">TVNZ 2</a> on 25 May 1992. It is the country's longest-running drama and soap opera, being broadcast continuously for over 6,000 episodes and 24 years, and is one of the most watched television programmes in New Zealand.</p>
<p>The show was originally screened as five half-hour episodes each week and initially receiving mixed reviews on its premiere.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> After its launch it dropped in ratings and would have been cancelled if TVNZ had not ordered a year's worth of episodes in advance. TVNZ renewed the production in early 1993 when the show's rating had picked up, and it now has <i>long-term public enthusiasm</i> .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Today, it is one of New Zealand's highest-rated shows, frequently making AGB Nielsen Media Research's top 5 programmes of the week.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortland_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the cancellation of <i><a href="/wiki/Gloss_(TV_series)" title="Gloss (TV series)">Gloss</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Television_New_Zealand" title="Television New Zealand">Television New Zealand</a> noticed the lack of New Zealand content on their channel and in 1990 set about creating a local equivalent of the Australian soap <i><a href="/wiki/Neighbours" title="Neighbours">Neighbours</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-production_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-production-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Greg_McGee" title="Greg McGee">Greg McGee</a> at <a href="/wiki/South_Pacific_Pictures" title="South Pacific Pictures">South Pacific Pictures</a> wanted to do a series about the new private clinics emerging under New Zealand's Labour government, and suggested the idea to scriptwriter Dean Parker, who declined due to a dislike of private medicine, so TV2 and <a href="/wiki/South_Pacific_Pictures" title="South Pacific Pictures">South Pacific Pictures</a> purchased a formula from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grundy_Television" title="Grundy Television">Grundy Television</a>, who get a royalty cheque for every broadcast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> $10 million was given for an initial 230 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-road_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-road-7">[7]</a></sup> Caterina De Nave was hired as the show's producer and subsequently travelled to Australia to work with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grundy_Television" title="Grundy Television">Grundy Television</a> to work out an idea for the five times a week soap.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-production_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-production-5">[5]</a></sup> TV2 programmer Bettina Hollings suggested the setting of a hospital after reading an article detailing ideal locations of a drama, which included a hospital, a police station and a school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-production_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-production-5">[5]</a></sup> De Nave worked with several storyliners including <a href="/wiki/Jason_Daniel" title="Jason Daniel">Jason Daniel</a> and they worked out an outline of the show.</p>
<p>De Nave noticed <i>Neighbours</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Home_and_Away" title="Home and Away">Home and Away</a></i> had a generally straightforward Australian cast and wished for the cast of her show to be culturally diverse to reflect New Zealand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-production_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-production-5">[5]</a></sup> She also wanted the cast to have strong female characters to attract the necessary female audience.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-production_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-production-5">[5]</a></sup> Daniel created the character of <a href="/wiki/Kirsty_Knight_(Shortland_Street)" title="Kirsty Knight (Shortland Street)">Kirsty</a> while De Nave created <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meredith_Fleming" title="Meredith Fleming">Meredith</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ken_Catran" title="Ken Catran">Ken Catran</a> created <a href="/wiki/Hone_Ropata" title="Hone Ropata">Hone</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-production_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-production-5">[5]</a></sup> The character of <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Neilson" title="Stuart Neilson">Stuart</a> was originally planned to be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homosexual" title="Homosexual">gay</a> but the plans made TVNZ nervous and were scrapped.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-road_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-road-7">[7]</a></sup> De Nave also wished to counter-stereotype races and made <a href="/wiki/Polynesians" title="Polynesians">Polynesian</a> <a href="/wiki/Sam_Aleni" title="Sam Aleni">Sam Aleni</a> a paramedic as there was only one paramedic of Polynesian descent throughout New Zealand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-production_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-production-5">[5]</a></sup> De Nave decided to make the setting that of a private hospital as it reflected New Zealand medicine at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-production_5-7"><a href="#cite_note-production-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>The working title was <i>The Shortland Street Project</i> after its planned filming location in a TVNZ-owned studio at <a href="/wiki/Gus_Fisher_Gallery" title="Gus Fisher Gallery">74 Shortland Street</a> in <a href="/wiki/Auckland_CBD" title="Auckland CBD">Auckland Central</a>. However, the studio was found to be too small for the required sets, and the production studio was moved to a warehouse in <a href="/wiki/Browns_Bay,_New_Zealand" title="Browns Bay, New Zealand">Browns Bay</a>. After running through many name options, the original working-title was chosen and subsequently truncated to simply <i>Shortland Street</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The name subsequently is a homage to the Shortland Street studios, which were home to New Zealand's first regular television broadcast in 1960, and were home to TVNZ and its predecessors' Auckland operations until TVNZ moved to its new purpose-built television centre on Victoria Street West in 1990.</p>
<p><i>Shortland Street'</i>s <a href="/wiki/Episode_1_(Shortland_Street)" title="Episode 1 (Shortland Street)">first episode</a> aired on 25 May 1992 to mixed reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Ratings were originally high after a successful marketing campaign but fell steadily throughout the year until 1993 when they rose dramatically.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-production_5-8"><a href="#cite_note-production-5">[5]</a></sup> The show would have been cancelled in the first year if it was not for the fact TVNZ ordered a year's worth of episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-production_5-9"><a href="#cite_note-production-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>With high public interest in the show and a viewing figure of a constant 600,000, <i>Shortland Street</i> received more sets and the nursing uniform which was considered outdated was redone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-production_5-10"><a href="#cite_note-production-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2000 an Australian consultant drastically changed the show so that it would suit the 21st century tone of New Zealand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-real_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-real-10">[10]</a></sup> This included transitioning the hospital to a public hospital, bringing in low income families and writing off 14 characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-real_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-real-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Michael_Galvin" title="Michael Galvin">Michael Galvin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Angela_Bloomfield" title="Angela Bloomfield">Angela Bloomfield</a> were brought back as their characters <a href="/wiki/Chris_Warner" title="Chris Warner">Chris Warner</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rachel_McKenna" title="Rachel McKenna">Rachel McKenna</a> and the show received a new producer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-real_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-real-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The show reached huge ratings in 2007 when a <a href="/wiki/Joey_Henderson" title="Joey Henderson">serial killer storyline</a> was introduced, with five characters meeting their demise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Episode 4000 saw the return of series original Dr. <a href="/wiki/Hone_Ropata" title="Hone Ropata">Hone Ropata</a> for a six-week stint in 2008.</p>
<p>Shortland Street aired its first ever 90 minute episode on 2 August 2010. The episode featured Chris discovering he had a son with series original <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alison_Raynor" title="Alison Raynor">Alison Raynor</a> in 1996, and the conclusion to the three-year <a href="/wiki/Kieran_Mitchell" title="Kieran Mitchell">Kieran Mitchell</a> storyline which saw the shows second highest ever ratings. Shortland Street has since made the 90-minute episode an annual feature. On 18 April 2011, Shortland Street screened its first episode in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_definition_television" title="High definition television">high definition</a>.</p>
<p>In July 2011, Shortland Street achieved a New Zealand first when it made its 2011 feature-length episode available to purchase via Facebook, becoming the second ever TV show in the world to use this technology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In August 2011 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_Blacks" title="All Blacks">All Blacks</a> <a href="/wiki/Keven_Mealamu" title="Keven Mealamu">Keven Mealamu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Boric" title="Anthony Boric">Anthony Boric</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jerome_Kaino" title="Jerome Kaino">Jerome Kaino</a> filmed a scene that aired on the opening night of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Rugby_World_Cup" title="2011 Rugby World Cup">2011 Rugby World Cup</a> on 9 September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allblacks_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Allblacks-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cameo_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-cameo-14">[14]</a></sup> The show added short transition scenes in which two characters in the hospital elevator discussed the wins or losses (the latter never actually happening) the All Blacks endured during the tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-seamless_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-seamless-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worldcup_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-worldcup-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the 15th December 2016, Shortland Street released a charity single featuring Lionel Wellington &amp; JJ Fong to raise money for <a href="/wiki/Starship_Children%27s_Health" title="Starship Children's Health">Starship Children's Health</a> hospital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortland_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Current_Shortland_Street_Characters" title="Current Shortland Street Characters">Current Shortland Street Characters</a>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Shortland_Street_characters" title="List of Shortland Street characters">List of Shortland Street characters</a>, and <a href="/wiki/List_of_original_Shortland_Street_characters" title="List of original Shortland Street characters">List of original Shortland Street characters</a></div>
<p><i>Shortland Street</i> stars an ensemble cast. Most of the characters either work at Shortland Street Hospital, or are relations to employees of the hospital.</p>
<p>Since 1992 many notable faces have appeared on the soap.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Only one character remains from the original cast: <a href="/wiki/Chris_Warner" title="Chris Warner">Chris Warner</a> Though taking a 4-year break, Chris has featured in the show the longest of the current cast, outstaying all of his family, who either died or left. <a href="/wiki/Rachel_McKenna" title="Rachel McKenna">Rachel McKenna</a>, another long-standing character as of 2014<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Shortland_Street&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, first appeared in the soap in 1993 and has made regular appearances since. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Harrison" title="Nick Harrison">Nick Harrison</a>, another long-running character, disappeared from the show in 2005.</p>
<p>Characters on the show attribute and portray several different demographics found in New Zealand. These range from the rich and well-off (such as <a href="/wiki/Chris_Warner" title="Chris Warner">Chris Warner</a>) to the struggling and poor (<a href="/wiki/Wendy_Cooper" title="Wendy Cooper">Wendy Cooper</a>). Other areas covered include different ethnicities, with Asian, <a href="/wiki/Polynesians" title="Polynesians">Polynesian</a>, Romanian people and even <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zimbabwean" title="Zimbabwean">Zimbabwean</a> characters appearing on the show. In the first year of the show, it was decided<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (April 2014)">by whom?</span></a></i>]</sup> that CEO <a href="/wiki/Michael_McKenna_(Shortland_Street)" title="Michael McKenna (Shortland Street)">Michael McKenna</a>'s personal assistant, <a href="/wiki/Jenny_Harrison_(Shortland_Street)" title="Jenny Harrison (Shortland Street)">Jenny</a>, should be a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solo_mother" title="Solo mother">solo mother</a> to help draw in that demographic, who statistically would be watching TV at 7 pm. The show has also had a long string of families, such as the <a href="/wiki/Warner_Family_(Shortland_Street)" title="Warner Family (Shortland Street)">Warners</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=McKenna/Wilson_Family_(Shortland_Street)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="McKenna/Wilson Family (Shortland Street) (page does not exist)">McKennas</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harrison_Family_(Shortland_Street)" title="Harrison Family (Shortland Street)">Harrisons</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Crombie_Family_(Shortland_Street)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Crombie Family (Shortland Street) (page does not exist)">Crombies</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hudson_Family_(Shortland_Street)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hudson Family (Shortland Street) (page does not exist)">Hudsons</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jeffries_Family_(Shortland_Street)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jeffries Family (Shortland Street) (page does not exist)">Jeffries</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Valentine_Family_(Shortland_Street)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Valentine Family (Shortland Street) (page does not exist)">Valentines</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=McKay_Family_(Shortland_Street)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="McKay Family (Shortland Street) (page does not exist)">McKays</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cooper_Family_(Shortland_Street)" title="Cooper Family (Shortland Street)">Coopers</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Avia/Levi_Family_(Shortland_Street)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Avia/Levi Family (Shortland Street) (page does not exist)">Avia-Levis</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hannah_Family_(Shortland_Street)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hannah Family (Shortland Street) (page does not exist)">Hannahs</a>, and various teenagers, helping young audiences and families relate to the show.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortland_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Shortland Street</i> is produced by <a href="/wiki/South_Pacific_Pictures" title="South Pacific Pictures">South Pacific Pictures</a>, with assistance from <a href="/wiki/FremantleMedia" title="FremantleMedia">FremantleMedia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Television_New_Zealand" title="Television New Zealand">Television New Zealand</a>. In the first few years, the production was also assisted by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Zealand_On_Air" title="New Zealand On Air">New Zealand On Air</a>.</p>
<p>Today, most of the filming for <i>Shortland Street</i> occurs at South Pacific Pictures <a href="/wiki/Waitakere_City" title="Waitakere City">Waitakere City</a> studios, with Ferndale High School scenes being filmed at the nearby Waitakere College.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The exterior shots of the Hospital are filmed on location at the Waitakere Studios at an existing section of a building dressed up to appear as the facade of a hospital entrance. Location scenes are filmed in Auckland, but other locations, including <a href="/wiki/Fiji" title="Fiji">Fiji</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mt_Ruapehu" title="Mt Ruapehu">Mt Ruapehu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rotorua" title="Rotorua">Rotorua</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rarotonga" title="Rarotonga">Rarotonga</a> have been used.</p>
<p>Originally, <i>Shortland Street</i> was filmed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Shore_City" title="North Shore City">North Shore City</a> at South Pacific Pictures Browns Bay studios until their relocation to purpose built studios in Waitakere City in 2000. The original Ferndale High School was played by a North Shore college until the studio relocated. When cast members are hired their contracts are either 4 days, 1 week, 2 weeks, 6 weeks, 6 months or a year.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HDTV" title="HDTV">High definition</a> production of Shortland Street started in early 2011, with the first HD episode broadcast on 18 April 2011 on the <a href="/wiki/Freeview_(New_Zealand)" title="Freeview (New Zealand)">Freeview HD</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sky_Network_Television" title="Sky Network Television">Sky</a> platforms.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortland_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The show has had a reputation for being raunchy and controversial with the very first episode of the show featuring a sex scene between resident "Dr. Love" <a href="/wiki/Chris_Warner" title="Chris Warner">Chris Warner</a> and his aerobics instructor. Another early controversy was that of a lesbian kiss between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meredith_Fleming" title="Meredith Fleming">Meredith Fleming</a> and Annie Flynn with several complaints made to the <a href="/wiki/Broadcasting_Standards_Authority" title="Broadcasting Standards Authority">Broadcasting Standards Authority</a> (BSA) but it went no further. It was not until 2008 that the show received its first ever BSA warning, when it featured an <a href="/wiki/Oral_sex" title="Oral sex">oral sex</a> scene in mid-2008 between sexually confused <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gerald_Tippett" title="Gerald Tippett">Gerald</a> and a fellow man.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> A few months later, the show received a second warning for an episode in August 2008 depicting the brutal murder of <a href="/wiki/Craig_Valentine" title="Craig Valentine">Craig Valentine</a>, who was beaten unconscious, then set alight in his car.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Again, a few months later, the show received yet another warning about a scene in January 2009 where <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tania_Jeffries" title="Tania Jeffries">Tania Jeffries</a> hit a gang leader in the head with a hammer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Scenes aired in April 2010 sparked criticism when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leanne_Miller" title="Leanne Miller">Leanne Miller</a> and her daughter <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nicole_Miller_(character)" title="Nicole Miller (character)">Nicole</a> stated that the city of <a href="/wiki/Tauranga" title="Tauranga">Tauranga</a> was not "gay friendly".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3News_153594_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-3News_153594-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The show caused controversy in August 2010 when the character of <a href="/wiki/Sophie_McKay" title="Sophie McKay">Sophie McKay</a> was shown to be being stalked by her university lecturer who she had been dating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NZ_Herald_10662764_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-NZ_Herald_10662764-24">[24]</a></sup> This upset the family of murdered girl <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sophie_Elliott" title="Sophie Elliott">Sophie Elliott</a> who was killed by her university tutor, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clayton_Weatherston" title="Clayton Weatherston">Clayton Weatherston</a>, who she had been dating. The similarities upset the families with producer Steven Zanoski saying: "the storyline was a classic and not inspired by real events."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>The shows production department received several complaints following the lesbian love storyline involving <a href="/wiki/Maia_Jeffries" title="Maia Jeffries">Maia Jeffries</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jennifer_Mason_(Shortland_Street)" title="Jennifer Mason (Shortland Street)">Jennifer Mason</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mother_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-mother-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>A storyline aired in April 2011 which featured the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cooper_Family_(Shortland_Street)" title="Cooper Family (Shortland Street)">Cooper Family</a> try to go to the beach, but they get confronted by a group of Maori who demand money. This sparked criticism with some saying it was discrimination against Maori people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NZ_Herald_10721983_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-NZ_Herald_10721983-27">[27]</a></sup> Maori adviser Ngamaru Raerino stated that viewers shouldn't have jumped to conclusions and should have let the storyline completely unfold which reveals the group of Maori are protesting against a corrupt camp owner who had been polluting the beach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2011, Shortland Street was identified as one of the main influences to people who self-harm, airing two storylines involving suicide attempts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-suicide_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-suicide-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortland_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortland_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Australia, it airs on <a href="/wiki/Foxtel" title="Foxtel">Foxtel</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Arena_(TV_network)" title="Arena (TV network)">Arena</a> channel weekdays at 11.30 am. Episodes are six weeks behind New Zealand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Free-to-air digital channel <a href="/wiki/7TWO" title="7TWO">7TWO</a> was airing episodes about three years behind however no longer airs episodes as of August 2015. Previous Australian broadcasters include: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABC1" title="ABC1">ABC1</a> 2010–2011, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UKTV_(Australia_and_New_Zealand)" title="UKTV (Australia and New Zealand)">UKTV</a> 1997–2000, and <a href="/wiki/SBS_(Australian_TV_channel)" title="SBS (Australian TV channel)">SBS TV</a> briefly between 1994–1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ireland">Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortland_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Ireland, it airs on <a href="/wiki/RT%C3%89_One" title="RTÉ One">RTÉ One</a> weekdays around 12:00 pm and repeated late night at around 03:00 am. Episodes are almost four years behind current New Zealand episodes. RTÉ has broadcast Shortland Street since 1996.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fiji">Fiji</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortland_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Fiji">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Fiji, the show has been on air since 1994 screening weekdays on <a href="/wiki/Fiji_One" title="Fiji One">Fiji One</a> usually around 8:30 pm weekdays. It is one of the most popular shows in Fiji.The show is screened on Cook Island Television at 8.00 pm weekdays. It is one of the most popular shows in the Cook Islands.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="UK">UK</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortland_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: UK">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the United Kingdom, the series was originally screened on various regions of the ITV network from 1993 to 2003. From 29 March 1993, <a href="/wiki/ITV_Central" title="ITV Central">Central Television</a> were the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ITV_Network" title="ITV Network">ITV</a> region to screen the soap, beginning in an afternoon timeslot, 1520–1550. From April 1994 to 2000, it was shown in an early evening timeslot, at either 1710–1740 and, later at 1730–1800. Eventually the serial was moved to a lunchtime slot, 1300–1330 from 12 June 2000 and it remained here for over two years.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meridian_Broadcasting" title="Meridian Broadcasting">Meridian</a> become the second region to start the series in mid 1993 around early afternoons until late 2002. Anglia start in April 1994 and followed central pattern, of the 17.10 slot, dropped the series around 2000.</p>
<p>From May 1994, Border, Granada started showing the series as a replacement for <i><a href="/wiki/A_Country_Practice" title="A Country Practice">A Country Practice</a></i>, broadcast in the afternoons, at the own piece, although did use the early-evening example. In January 1995, Tyne tees and Yorkshire started the series as a replacement for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_Doctors" title="The Young Doctors">The Young Doctors</a></i>. By 1999, Granada, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Television" title="Yorkshire Television">Yorkshire Television</a> and Tyne tees dropped the series entirely, opting for local programming instead, while Border switched the series to 17.10 slot by 2001 it was back in the afternoon slot until it dropped in the summer of 2003.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Grampian_Television" title="Grampian Television">Grampian Television</a> also started the series in 1994 but it was dropped in late 1998. <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Television" title="Scottish Television">Scottish Television</a> have never shown <i>Shortland Street</i>. <a href="/wiki/ITV_Wales_%26_West" title="ITV Wales &amp; West">HTV</a> started the series in August 1994, showing the series in the afternoons, at the own piece, including early-evening example for a short time. The series was dropped in early 2000 but reappeared in by May 2000.</p>
<p>From January 2003, the <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Television" title="Carlton Television">Carlton</a>-owned ITV regions including <a href="/wiki/ITV_Central" title="ITV Central">Central Television</a>, <a href="/wiki/ITV_Westcountry" title="ITV Westcountry">Westcountry</a>, <a href="/wiki/ITV_Wales_%26_West" title="ITV Wales &amp; West">HTV</a> and Carlton-London <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Networked" title="Networked">networked</a> <i>Shortland Street</i> in an afternoon slot, 1430–1500, Monday to Wednesday, with a Thursday episode added a few months later. A special programme was aired (presented by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Galvin" title="Michael Galvin">Michael Galvin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Angela_Bloomfield" title="Angela Bloomfield">Angela Bloomfield</a>) introducing new viewers from <a href="/wiki/ITV_Westcountry" title="ITV Westcountry">Westcountry</a> and Carlton-London to the show. However, Shortland Street failed to attract a significant audience in its new afternoon slot and it was axed completely by ITV and was last shown on 28 August 2003, finishing at episode 2367.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Central had shown the serial consecutively for over 10 years.</p>
<p><i>Shortland Street</i> returned to British screens on 23 August 2010, when <a href="/wiki/Sky_Living" title="Sky Living">Living</a> began airing two episodes a day from the 2007 season with episodes 3736/3737 which originally aired in New Zealand on 21/22 May 2007 beginning on 23 August 2010 at 15.00–16.00 (and repeated at 10.00 the following morning). However four weeks into its run, the morning repeats were dropped by Living, and as of Monday 20 September moving from 15.00–16.00 and 12.00–13.00. As from Monday 27 September 2010, it was shown on <a href="/wiki/Sky_Living_Loves" title="Sky Living Loves">Living Loves</a> from 18.00–19.00 Monday to Friday with five repeat episodes shown on Saturday and Sunday mornings. After just over two months on-air, it was cancelled by Sky with the final episode shown on 29 October 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>In previous years South Pacific Pictures publicity has claimed the show was sold to the South African <a href="/wiki/Bantustan" title="Bantustan">black homeland</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bophuthatswana" title="Bophuthatswana">Bophuthatswana</a>, which journalists have used to demonstrate <i>Shortland Street'</i>s interracial appeal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ukraine">Ukraine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortland_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Ukraine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A licensed version of the show started production in Ukraine in 2016 called "Central Hospital" (ЦЕНТРАЛЬНА ЛІКАРНЯ) with an initial run of 60 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
