<p><b>Sean Mackin</b> (born on April 30th, 1979) is an American rock musician. He is best known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Violinist" title="Violinist">violinist</a> and backing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vocalist" title="Vocalist">vocalist</a> for the American <a href="/wiki/Pop_punk" title="Pop punk">pop punk</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_group" title="Music group">music group</a> <a href="/wiki/Yellowcard" title="Yellowcard">Yellowcard</a></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sean_Mackin_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mackin attended <a href="/wiki/Florida_State_University" title="Florida State University">Florida State University</a> majoring in electrical engineering and international business. He is a member of <a href="/wiki/Phi_Sigma_Kappa" title="Phi Sigma Kappa">Phi Sigma Kappa</a> fraternity. Mackin also got a job at <a href="/wiki/Chili%27s" title="Chili's">Chili's</a> with bandmate and friend <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Key" title="Ryan Key">Ryan Key</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-r1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sean_Mackin_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Yellowcard_.281997.E2.80.932017.29">Yellowcard (1997–2017)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sean_Mackin_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Yellowcard (1997–2017)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Yellowcard" title="Yellowcard">Yellowcard</a></div>
<p>Mackin joined the band in 1997, from high school friends <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ben_Harper_(Yellowcard_and_HeyMike!)" title="Ben Harper (Yellowcard and HeyMike!)">Ben Harper</a> and Longineu W. Parsons III. At first, Mackin had been asked not to play the violin in some songs by Yellowcard, but ever since the group decided to keep Mackin as a permanent member, he has taken the role of violinist and back-up vocalist. Yellowcard released their first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Midget_Tossing" title="Midget Tossing">Midget Tossing</a></i>, in 1997 and released their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Where_We_Stand" title="Where We Stand">Where We Stand</a></i> in 1999. After lead singer Ben Dobson left the band he was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Key" title="Ryan Key">Ryan Key</a>, who was formerly in the California band <a href="/wiki/Craig%27s_Brother" title="Craig's Brother">Craig's Brother</a> and joined after being asked by guitarist Ben Harper. As Ryan's friend Dan McLintock from Jacksonville pop punk band <a href="/wiki/Inspection_12" title="Inspection 12">Inspection 12</a> was still with Craig's Brother at the time and they were currently in the studio recording their second album <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_at_Sea_(Craig%27s_Brother_album)" title="Lost at Sea (Craig's Brother album)">Lost at Sea</a></i>, Sean was drafted to record violin for their song <i>"Back and Forth"</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The band then released the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Still_Standing_EP" title="Still Standing EP">Still Standing EP</a></i> in early 2000. Shortly after the release of their EP Todd Clary left the band, Key then filled in as the rhythm guitarist. The band then moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Camarillo,_CA" title="Camarillo, CA">Camarillo, CA</a>, after being signed to <a href="/wiki/Lobster_Records" title="Lobster Records">Lobster Records</a>. They then begin working on their next album.</p>
<p>The group released their third album, <i><a href="/wiki/One_for_the_Kids" title="One for the Kids">One for the Kids</a></i> in 2001 and followed up with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Underdog_EP" title="The Underdog EP">The Underdog EP</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> in 2002. After the release of The Underdog EP, Warren Cooke left the band due to personal reasons. The band later asked close friend <a href="/wiki/Peter_Mosely" title="Peter Mosely">Peter Mosely</a> from Inspection 12, to become the band's new bass guitarist. After Yellowcard released The Underdog EP the band signed with <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol Records</a>, who were looking to sign <a href="/wiki/Pop_punk" title="Pop punk">pop punk</a> bands at the time. Yellowcard then began the recording of their major-label debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Ocean_Avenue_(album)" title="Ocean Avenue (album)">Ocean Avenue</a></i> in late 2002, gaining the band major success. Mosely left during recording of the album due to personal problems. The band then asked <a href="/wiki/Alex_Lewis_(musician)" title="Alex Lewis (musician)">Alex Lewis</a> to join as the bass guitarist. Yellowcard released their album <i>Ocean Avenue</i> on July 22, 2003 hitting at number 23 on U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, the album was a commercial success in the United States with the hit singles <a href="/wiki/Way_Away" title="Way Away">Way Away</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ocean_Avenue_(song)" title="Ocean Avenue (song)">Ocean Avenue</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Only_One_(Yellowcard_song)" title="Only One (Yellowcard song)">Only One</a>.</p>
<p>In late 2004 Lewis left the band after Peter Mosely rejoined as the band's bassist. In 2005 Mackin moved with the other band members to Los Angeles while Key and Mosely moved to New York City to create new material for their next album. Lead guitarist Ben Harper left the band in April after finishing guitar tracks for the album. The band then asked Ryan Mendez from the band Staring Back to become the new lead guitarist.</p>
<p>The album <i><a href="/wiki/Lights_and_Sounds" title="Lights and Sounds">Lights and Sounds</a></i> was released in January 2006, unlike their last album <i>Ocean Avenue</i>, Lights and Sounds broke away from the band's pop punk sound to a more alternative rock album. The album peaked at number five on the U.S. Billboard 200. The title track, "<a href="/wiki/Lights_and_Sounds_(song)" title="Lights and Sounds (song)">Lights and Sounds</a>", was the first single, released a week before the album. It peaked at number four on the Billboard Hot Modern Rock Tracks.</p>
<p>October 16, 2006, the band re-entered the studio to begin pre-production on their next album. <i><a href="/wiki/Paper_Walls" title="Paper Walls">Paper Walls</a></i> was released in the U.S. on July 17, 2007, the album debuted at number 13 on the U.S. Billboard 200 selling about 40,000 copies in its first week. The band spent the rest of the year promoting their new album nonstop since its release while touring with <a href="/wiki/Linkin_Park" title="Linkin Park">Linkin Park</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blue_October" title="Blue October">Blue October</a>.</p>
<p>The band needed to re-schedule several dates during their European leg of their tour in January while Parsons went home to spend time with family. Yellowcard released their live album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live_from_Las_Vegas_at_the_Palms" title="Live from Las Vegas at the Palms">Live from Las Vegas at the Palms</a></i> on January 22, 2008. It was recorded during their Blue October Tour in October 2007 in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Las Vegas</a>.</p>
<p>In April 2008 Yellowcard officially announced an "indefinite hiatus" canceling their European and acoustic tours. The band stated the reason why they went on hiatus was to focus on their personal lives for the time.</p>
<p>On August 1, 2010, it was confirmed that Yellowcard had ended their hiatus and was working on a new record, titled <i><a href="/wiki/When_You%27re_Through_Thinking,_Say_Yes" title="When You're Through Thinking, Say Yes">When You're Through Thinking, Say Yes</a></i> to be released on Hopeless Records in early 2011. The band toured Europe in March 2011 alongside <a href="/wiki/All_Time_Low" title="All Time Low">All Time Low</a>.</p>
<p>In December 2011, Mackin was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Thyroid_cancer" title="Thyroid cancer">thyroid cancer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In response to Mackin's announcement regarding his diagnosis, a group of fans started the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Strong_For_Sean_project&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Strong For Sean project (page does not exist)">Strong For Sean project</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> They made and sold a limited number of bracelets to show their support. All money raised was donated to the American Cancer Society in Mackin's name.</p>
