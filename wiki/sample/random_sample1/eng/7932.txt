<p><b>Foluwashola</b> "<b>Shola</b>" <b>Ameobi</b> (born 12 October 1981) is a Nigerian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>.</p>
<p>He spent 14 years at <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a>, where he received a winner's medal in 2010 when Newcastle lifted the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> trophy. He holds the record for the most appearances as a <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> On leaving Newcastle in 2014, he briefly represented <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaziantep_BB" title="Gaziantep BB">Gaziantep BB</a> in Turkey and back in England with <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a> before joining <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a>.</p>
<p>Born in Nigeria but raised in England, Ameobi was capped by the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England under-21 team</a> before making his <a href="/wiki/Nigeria_national_football_team" title="Nigeria national football team">Nigeria</a> debut in 2012 and representing them at the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shola_Ameobi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Zaria" title="Zaria">Zaria</a>, Nigeria to a family from <a href="/wiki/Ijumu" title="Ijumu">Ijumu Local Government Area</a> in modern-day <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kogi" title="Kogi">Kogi</a> State, Ameobi moved to <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_upon_Tyne" title="Newcastle upon Tyne">Newcastle upon Tyne</a>, England when he was five. His initial promise was spotted whilst attending the Walker Central Boys Club, before he was invited to attend Newcastle United's Academy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NUFC_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NUFC-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shola_Ameobi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Newcastle_United">Newcastle United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shola_Ameobi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Newcastle United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shola_Ameobi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Ameobi signed a pre-contract with Newcastle on 1 July 1997.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NUFC_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-NUFC-2">[2]</a></sup> He progressed through the youth setup, making his debut in the reserves in 1998 on 11 October against Sunderland. He made his first team debut two years later, 9 September 2000, in a home game against <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NUFC_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-NUFC-2">[2]</a></sup> He went on to make 22 appearances for Newcastle that season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NUFC_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-NUFC-2">[2]</a></sup> in lieu of the injured <a href="/wiki/Alan_Shearer" title="Alan Shearer">Alan Shearer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carl_Cort" title="Carl Cort">Carl Cort</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NUFC_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-NUFC-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2002–03 Champions League saw some of Ameobi's finest performances including an equalising goal against <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a> in a 3–1 defeat at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nou_Camp" title="Nou Camp">Nou Camp</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> as well as scoring two goals against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bayer_Leverkusen" title="Bayer Leverkusen">Bayer Leverkusen</a> in a 3–1 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>His erratic form has earned him some criticism in the past, and although never having been claimed as a prolific goalscorer, many of his games were <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">as a substitute</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Due to injuries in the latter stages of the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Newcastle_United_F.C._season" title="2005–06 Newcastle United F.C. season">2005–06 season</a>, Ameobi gained a regular starting place, and scored six goals in Newcastle's last 12 Premier League games.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2006_UEFA_Intertoto_Cup" title="2006 UEFA Intertoto Cup">2006 UEFA Intertoto Cup</a>, Ameobi scored twice in Newcastle's away tie at <a href="/wiki/Lillestr%C3%B8m_SK" title="Lillestrøm SK">Lillestrøm SK</a> (3–0),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> taking him to second place in the club's table of leading goalscorers in European competition with twelve goals, behind only Shearer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Injury.2C_loan.2C_and_transfer_attempts">Injury, loan, and transfer attempts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shola_Ameobi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Injury, loan, and transfer attempts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>During the 2006–07 season, Ameobi needed a <a href="/wiki/Hip" title="Hip">hip</a> operation, a problem which had been plaguing him for two seasons. Newcastle's shortage of match-fit first team strikers during the previous season had forced him to postpone this surgery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The problem eventually forced <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Roeder" title="Glenn Roeder">Glenn Roeder</a> to send Ameobi for surgery two months before the transfer window, making the 0–0 draw away game to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a> his last game and ruling him out for the rest of the season.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He had made 13 appearances (three as a substitute) and scored five goals.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Ameobi and the Newcastle medical team reported his hip operation in the United States was a success and that the club doctors and medical staff would work on his rehabilitation.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It was originally thought that he would not be fit to play until the start of the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Newcastle_United_F.C._season" title="2007–08 Newcastle United F.C. season">2007–08 season</a>, but his rehabilitation progressed quickly and he returned with three games remaining in the season, clocking approximately 30 minutes of match action against <a href="/wiki/Reading_F.C." title="Reading F.C.">Reading</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and making a further two appearances.</p>
<p>However, after failing to impress Newcastle manager <a href="/wiki/Sam_Allardyce" title="Sam Allardyce">Sam Allardyce</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> or <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Keegan" title="Kevin Keegan">Kevin Keegan</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> on 27 March 2008, Ameobi agreed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loan_(association_football)" title="Loan (association football)">loan</a> deal with <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a> until the end of the season, to help their promotion campaign. It is understood that a loan fee of around £500K was agreed and that a possible permanent transfer was also discussed subject to Stoke winning promotion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Ameobi made his debut for Stoke against <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a> on 29 March, the game ended 1–1. After playing for six games and not managing to score despite Stoke's gaining of promotion, Ameobi returned to Newcastle, and Stoke decided not to sign him permanently.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 14 August, <a href="/wiki/Ipswich_Town_F.C." title="Ipswich Town F.C.">Ipswich Town</a> manager <a href="/wiki/Jim_Magilton" title="Jim Magilton">Jim Magilton</a> confirmed that the club were in talks with Ameobi and that the club were keen to get the deal finalised as long as Ameobi was willing to play in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a>. However, on 17 August, Ameobi failed a medical, showing hamstring problems, and the deal collapsed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<p>Due to long term injuries to <a href="/wiki/Mark_Viduka" title="Mark Viduka">Mark Viduka</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alan_Smith_(footballer_born_1980)" title="Alan Smith (footballer born 1980)">Alan Smith</a>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> as well as shorter term injuries to <a href="/wiki/Michael_Owen" title="Michael Owen">Michael Owen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Obafemi_Martins" title="Obafemi Martins">Obafemi Martins</a>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Ameobi was able to start the 2008–09 season in the first team squad. He managed to score his first goal in two years for Newcastle in a 2–2 home draw with Manchester City on 20 October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> After the match, interim manager <a href="/wiki/Joe_Kinnear" title="Joe Kinnear">Joe Kinnear</a> praised Ameobi and backed him to kick-start his career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He followed this up with a goal in his next game away to <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a> in a 2–1 defeat on 25 October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> He signed a new deal with the club in January 2009 to keep him contracted until 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>He scored a penalty to mark his 50th goal for Newcastle on 1 February 2009 against local rivals <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a> to make the final score 1–1. He scored his first ever career hat-trick in Newcastle's first home match in the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Football_League_Championship" title="2009–10 Football League Championship">Championship</a> against <a href="/wiki/Reading_F.C." title="Reading F.C.">Reading</a> on 15 August. The first two goals were diving headers, and he completed his hat-trick with a penalty, which he placed in the bottom left corner, sending the keeper the wrong way.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> He scored in the next game in a 1–0 victory over Sheffield Wednesday<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> taking his tally to four goals in three games, equalling his total for the previous season.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Following these early season performances he was named the Championship player of the month for August.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-potm-aug-bbc_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-potm-aug-bbc-22">[22]</a></sup></p>

<p>He got injured in Newcastle's 4–3 win over <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The injury kept him out for three months but on his return he scored against Coventry with a left footed strike from the edge of the box.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> He followed this up by scoring the second in Newcastle's 2–0 home win over Middlesbrough<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and their 2–2 away draw with <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a> on Boxing Day<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> taking his tally to an impressive 8 goals from 9 starts.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Ameobi was subsequently sidelined with a hamstring injury but made the bench against <a href="/wiki/Nottingham_Forest_F.C." title="Nottingham Forest F.C.">Nottingham Forest</a> on 29 March 2010. Ameobi appeared early in the second half as a substitute, and soon made his presence felt, firing in an angled left-footed shot off the post to give United the lead in a 2–0 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> On 3 April 2010, Ameobi scored the decisive goal in a 3–2 victory at <a href="/wiki/Peterborough_United_F.C." title="Peterborough United F.C.">Peterborough United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 22 September 2010 Ameobi scored two goals in a surprise victory away at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C" title="Chelsea F.C">Chelsea</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a>, including the winner in the 90th minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> On 16 October 2010, Ameobi came off the bench to score Newcastle's first in a 2–2 draw against <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> After his good performance against Wigan, he started the next game against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_Ham_United" title="West Ham United">West Ham United</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Andy_Carroll" title="Andy Carroll">Andy Carroll</a> putting in a good performance as The Magpies came from 1–0 down to win 2–1. He scored twice in Newcastle's next league game on 31 October 2010 as Newcastle crushed local rivals Sunderland 5–1 at St. James' Park. On 2 January 2011, he scored the winner in a 1–0 win at <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite suffering with a cheek bone injury in February which required him to wear a mask which impaired his vision upon his return, he was a regular starter after Christmas with the sale of <a href="/wiki/Andy_Carroll" title="Andy Carroll">Andy Carroll</a>. He signed a contract extension to the end of the 2013–14 season on 10 August 2011. His current deal was due to expire at the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_career">Later career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shola_Ameobi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Later career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the 2011–12 season, Ameobi became something of a fringe player, with the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Demba_Ba" title="Demba Ba">Demba Ba</a> and later, <a href="/wiki/Papiss_Ciss%C3%A9" title="Papiss Cissé">Papiss Cissé</a>, pushing him onto the substitutes bench. But on Sunday 16 October 2011, against <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham</a>, Ameobi, a 71st-minute substitute for <a href="/wiki/Leon_Best" title="Leon Best">Leon Best</a>, scored a goal late on to make it 2–2. The next few games were an annoyance for Ameobi, with Ba finding his shooting boots and pairing well with Best and with Ameobi's inclusion in the starting line-up against Norwich ending up in a 4–2 defeat. On 4 March, Ameobi was yet again on the bench for a game against <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a>, and brought on as a substitute for Cissé he scored a 91st-minute equaliser to get Newcastle back on terms and bringing his tally to 7 goals in 12 games against Sunderland for Newcastle, a record only bettered by <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Milburn" title="Jackie Milburn">Jackie Milburn</a>. His prolific goal scoring form against Sunderland has led many sections of the Newcastle support to refer to him as "The Mackem Slayer".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> His next league goal came nine months later, in a 1–0 win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen%27s_Park_Rangers" title="Queen's Park Rangers">QPR</a> on 22 December.</p>
<p>During Newcastle's <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2012–13 UEFA Europa League">Europa League</a> campaign, Ameobi scored three goals in the competition; the opening goal in a 3–0 win over <a href="/wiki/FC_Girondins_de_Bordeaux" title="FC Girondins de Bordeaux">Bordeaux</a> on 4 October 2012; the equaliser in a 2–2 draw with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Brugge_K.V." title="Club Brugge K.V.">Club Brugge</a> on 8 November 2012, and the only goal of the game from the penalty spot to secure a 1–0 aggregate victory over <a href="/wiki/FC_Metalist_Kharkiv" title="FC Metalist Kharkiv">Metalist Kharkiv</a> on 21 February 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ameobi scored in a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Morecambe_F.C." title="Morecambe F.C.">Morecambe</a> in the second round in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> (with his <a href="/wiki/Sammy_Ameobi" title="Sammy Ameobi">younger brother</a> scoring the second goal) on 28 August 2013. Ameobi's next goal would come in the league when he scored the opening goal in a 2–1 home loss to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swansea_City_F.C." title="Swansea City F.C.">Swansea City</a> on 19 April 2014, and he also scored the opening goal in a 3–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City</a>. On the final day of the season and also, his final appearance for Newcastle, Ameobi was sent off for dissent in a 2–1 away loss to <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>.</p>
<p>On 23 May 2014, it was confirmed by Newcastle United that they would be parting company with Ameobi after 14 years of service for the Tyneside club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gaziantep_BB">Gaziantep BB</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shola_Ameobi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Gaziantep BB">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 11 August 2014, Ameobi signed for Turkish club <a href="/wiki/Gaziantep_B%C3%BCy%C3%BCk%C5%9Fehir_Belediyespor" title="Gaziantep Büyükşehir Belediyespor">Gaziantep Büyükşehir Belediyespor</a> on a one-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> He made his debut on 13 September, in a goalless draw against <a href="/wiki/Kayserispor" title="Kayserispor">Kayserispor</a>, playing the full match and getting booked in the third minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> He scored his first goal through a 35th-minute penalty in the 1–1 draw with <a href="/wiki/Adanaspor" title="Adanaspor">Adanaspor</a> on 19 September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ameobi left the club on 29 December 2014, citing family reasons for his departure. He made 11 appearances and scored 4 goals during his time there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Crystal_Palace">Crystal Palace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shola_Ameobi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Crystal Palace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 29 January 2015, Ameobi returned to the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> by signing for <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a> until the end of the season. He was signed by <a href="/wiki/Alan_Pardew" title="Alan Pardew">Alan Pardew</a>, his last manager at Newcastle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> On 21 February 2015, he made his debut for the club after being substituted on for <a href="/wiki/Joe_Ledley" title="Joe Ledley">Joe Ledley</a> in the eightieth minute against <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a>.</p>
<p>On 10 June 2015, it was announced that Ameobi had been released by Crystal Palace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bolton_Wanderers">Bolton Wanderers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shola_Ameobi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Bolton Wanderers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 23 October 2015, Ameobi signed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> side <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a> on a short-term contract and was handed the number 26 shirt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> He made his debut a day later coming on as a 12th-minute substitute replacing <a href="/wiki/Gary_Madine" title="Gary Madine">Gary Madine</a> in a 1-1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a>, in which Ameobi gave Bolton the lead in the 32nd minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> He left the club after his contract ended following Bolton's 3-1 victory over <a href="/wiki/Milton_Keynes_Dons_F.C." title="Milton Keynes Dons F.C.">MK Dons</a> in which he scored in the 90th minute on 23 January 2016. Following the end of this contract, Ameobi offered to play for Bolton for free but Bolton were unable to do this, due to being under a transfer embargo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fleetwood_Town">Fleetwood Town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shola_Ameobi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Fleetwood Town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 12 February 2016, Ameobi signed for <a href="/wiki/Fleetwood_Town_F.C." title="Fleetwood Town F.C.">Fleetwood Town</a> until the end of the season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> and was handed the number 9 shirt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shola_Ameobi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He is the older brother of fellow footballers <a href="/wiki/Tomi_Ameobi" title="Tomi Ameobi">Tomi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sammy_Ameobi" title="Sammy Ameobi">Sammy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> His father, Dr John Ameobi, is a pastor in Newcastle Apostolic church, a Pentecostal church. All of the Ameobi boys were brought up in the church and have a strong faith. Ameobi is on the board of reference for CSW, an organisation working for religious freedom through advocacy and human rights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shola_Ameobi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nigerian-born Ameobi had a three-year spell with <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England's under-21 team</a>, netting seven goals between 2000 and 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NUFC_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-NUFC-2">[2]</a></sup> Ameobi is the joint eighth highest goal scorer for the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">under-21s</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NUFC_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-NUFC-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late 2009, Ameobi declared his interest in playing for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nigeria_National_Football_Team" title="Nigeria National Football Team">Nigeria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> On 14 January 2011, he was announced in the Nigerian national squad for the first time, for a friendly against <a href="/wiki/Guatemala_national_football_team" title="Guatemala national football team">Guatemala</a> in the United States, in February.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> He withdrew from the replacement game against <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Leone_national_football_team" title="Sierra Leone national football team">Sierra Leone</a> after breaking his cheekbone in a Premier League match.</p>
<p>On 1 November 2011, it was announced that FIFA had cleared Ameobi to play for Nigeria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2012, Nigeria head coach <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Keshi" title="Stephen Keshi">Stephen Keshi</a> expressed his interest in Ameobi joining the squad for the <a href="/wiki/2013_Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="2013 Africa Cup of Nations">2013 Africa Cup of Nations</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2012, Ameobi received his second call up for the Nigeria national team against <a href="/wiki/Venezuela_national_football_team" title="Venezuela national football team">Venezuela</a>. He made his debut on 14 November, appearing as a substitute in the 60th minute for former Newcastle teammate <a href="/wiki/Obafemi_Martins" title="Obafemi Martins">Obafemi Martins</a>. In stoppage time at the end of the game, Ameobi laid on a pass for <a href="/wiki/Ogenyi_Onazi" title="Ogenyi Onazi">Ogenyi Onazi</a> to make the score 3–1 to Nigeria.</p>
<p>He scored his first international goal for Nigeria on 10 September 2013, against <a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso_national_football_team" title="Burkina Faso national football team">Burkina Faso</a> in a friendly in Kaduna, Nigeria. In November 2013, he scored his second goal against Italy, in a friendly at <a href="/wiki/Craven_Cottage" title="Craven Cottage">Craven Cottage</a>.</p>
<p>On 12 May 2014, he was named in Nigeria's 30-man provisional squad for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> and later made the final 23-man squad. During the tournament, Ameobi made a total of two substitute appearances for the Super Eagles, failing to score in either and ultimately seeing out his side's 2–0 exit to <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> as an unused substitute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
