<p><b>Sughd Region</b> (<a href="/wiki/Tajik_language" title="Tajik language">Tajik</a>: <span lang="tg" xml:lang="tg">Вилояти Суғд</span> <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Tajik transliteration">Viloyati Suğd/Vilojati Suƣd</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="fa" xml:lang="fa"><i>Velâyate soqd</i> ولایت سغد</span>‎‎, transliterated as <i><a href="/wiki/Sogdia" title="Sogdia">Sogdia</a> Province</i>) is one of the four administrative divisions and one of the three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Tajikistan" title="Provinces of Tajikistan">provinces</a> (<a href="/wiki/Tajik_language" title="Tajik language">Tajik</a>: <span lang="tg" xml:lang="tg">вилоятҳо</span>, <i><a href="/wiki/Wilayah" title="Wilayah">viloyatho</a></i>) that make up <a href="/wiki/Tajikistan" title="Tajikistan">Tajikistan</a>. Centered in the historical <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sogdiana" title="Sogdiana">Sogdiana</a>, it is located in the northwest of the country, with an area of some 25,400 square kilometers and a population of 2,132,100 (2008 est.),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pop2008_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-pop2008-1">[1]</a></sup> up from 1,870,000 according to the 2000 census and 1,558,000 in 1989. It was founded in 1924 as part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uzbek_SSR" title="Uzbek SSR">Uzbek SSR</a> and became part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tajik_SSR" title="Tajik SSR">Tajik SSR</a> in 1929 after efforts of <a href="/wiki/Shirinsho_Shotemur" title="Shirinsho Shotemur">Shirinsho Shotemur</a>.</p>
<p>The region shares a border with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jizakh_Province" title="Jizakh Province">Jizakh</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Namangan_Province" title="Namangan Province">Namangan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samarkand_Province" title="Samarkand Province">Samarkand</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fergana_Province" title="Fergana Province">Fergana</a> provinces of <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Osh_Region" title="Osh Region">Osh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Batken_Region" title="Batken Region">Batken</a> regions of <a href="/wiki/Kyrgyzstan" title="Kyrgyzstan">Kyrgyzstan</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Syr_Darya" title="Syr Darya">Syr Darya</a> river flows through it. It contains the <a href="/wiki/Aktash_Massif_Important_Bird_Area" title="Aktash Massif Important Bird Area">Akash Massif</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mogoltau_Massif_Important_Bird_Area" title="Mogoltau Massif Important Bird Area">Mogoltau Massif Important Bird Areas</a>. Sughd is separated from the rest of Tajikistan by the <a href="/wiki/Gissar_Range" title="Gissar Range">Gissar Range</a> (passes may be closed in winter). The southern part of the province is the east-west valley of the upper <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zarafshan_River" title="Zarafshan River">Zarafshan River</a>. North, over the Turkestan Range, is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ferghana_Valley" title="Ferghana Valley">Ferghana Valley</a>. The province has 30% of Tajikistan's population<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pop2008_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-pop2008-1">[1]</a></sup> and one-third of its arable land.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It produces two thirds of the country's GDP.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was known as Leninabad until 1991, then Leninobod until 2000, then Sogd until 2004.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sughd_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The economy of Sughd has been growing steadily since 2000, at the average rate of 13.2% in 2008 and 13.3% in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2009, farming, trade and industrial production contributed 28.2%, 25.8% and 14.0% to the GRP (gross regional product) of Sughd, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-4">[4]</a></sup> Since 2000, the output of industrial production increased two-fold, at an average annual growth rate of 5–8%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Free_economic_zone" title="Free economic zone">free economic zone</a> has been established in the region called <a href="/wiki/Sughd_Free_Economic_Zone" title="Sughd Free Economic Zone">Sughd Free Economic Zone</a>.</p>
