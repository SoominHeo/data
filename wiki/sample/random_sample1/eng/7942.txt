<p><b>Sudogda</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Су́догда</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sudogodsky_District" title="Sudogodsky District">Sudogodsky District</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Oblast" title="Vladimir Oblast">Vladimir Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located on the left bank of the <a href="/wiki/Sudogda_River" title="Sudogda River">Sudogda River</a> (<a href="/wiki/Klyazma_River" title="Klyazma River">Klyazma</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tributary" title="Tributary">tributary</a>) 40 kilometers (25 mi) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Vladimir,_Russia" title="Vladimir, Russia">Vladimir</a>, the administrative center of the <a href="/wiki/Oblast" title="Oblast">oblast</a>. Population: <span class="nowrap">11,848 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-4">[4]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">13,328 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopCensus_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-PopCensus-6">[6]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">14,191 (<a href="/wiki/Soviet_Census_(1989)" title="Soviet Census (1989)">1989 Census</a>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census1989_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census1989-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sudogda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>First mentioned in the 17th century documents as the <i><a href="/wiki/Sloboda" title="Sloboda">sloboda</a></i> of <b>Sudogodskaya</b> (<span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Судогодская</span>), it was later known as the <i><a href="/wiki/Village#Russia" title="Village">selo</a></i> of Sudogda.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Town status was granted to it in 1778.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sudogda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, Sudogda serves as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sudogodsky_District" title="Sudogodsky District">Sudogodsky District</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OKATO_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-OKATO-2">[2]</a></sup> to which it is directly subordinated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VladimirO_admlist_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-VladimirO_admlist-1">[1]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> of Sudogda is incorporated within Sudogodsky Municipal District as <b>Sudogda Urban Settlement</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VladimirOSudogodskyD_mun_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-VladimirOSudogodskyD_mun-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

Опубликован: "Владимирские ведомости", №158–165, 18 мая 2005 г.</span> (Legislative Assembly of Vladimir Oblast. Law #60-OZ of May 13, 2005 <i>On Granting Sudogodsky District and the Newly Established Municipal Formations Comprising It an Appropriate Status of the Municipal Formations and on Establishing Their Borders</i>, as amended by the Law #85-OZ of October 6, 2010 <i>On Amending the Law of Vladimir Oblast "On Granting Sudogodsky District and the Newly Established Municipal Formations Comprising It an Appropriate Status of the Municipal Formations and on Establishing Their Borders"</i>. Effective as of the day of the official publication (May 18, 2005).).</li>
</ul>
