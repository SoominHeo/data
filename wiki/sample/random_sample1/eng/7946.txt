<p><b>Sur</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">صور</span>‎‎) is a capital city of <a href="/wiki/Ash_Sharqiyah_Region_(Oman)" title="Ash Sharqiyah Region (Oman)">Ash Sharqiyah Region</a>, northeastern <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a>, on the coast of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Oman" title="Gulf of Oman">Gulf of Oman</a>. It is located at around <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Sur%2C_Oman&amp;params=22_34_0_N_59_31_44_E_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">22°34′0″N</span> <span class="longitude">59°31′44″E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">22.56667°N 59.52889°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">22.56667; 59.52889</span></span></span></a></span>, and is 93 miles (150 km) southeast of the Omani capital <a href="/wiki/Muscat,_Oman" title="Muscat, Oman">Muscat</a>. Historically the city is known for being an important destination point for sailors. Today the sea still plays an important part of life in Sur.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sur,_Oman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nearby villages include <a href="/wiki/Dughmur" title="Dughmur">Dughmur</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sur,_Oman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>By the 6th century, Sur was an established centre for trade with <a href="/wiki/East_Africa" title="East Africa">East Africa</a>. In the 16th century, it was under <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> rule but was liberated by the Omani <a href="/wiki/Imam" title="Imam">imam</a> Nasir ibn Murshid and underwent an economic revival, as a trade centre with <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and East Africa. This continued until the mid-19th century, when the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">British</a> outlawed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slave_trade" title="Slave trade">slave trade</a>. The city was further ruined by the opening of the <a href="/wiki/Suez_Canal" title="Suez Canal">Suez Canal</a>, which saw it lose trade with India.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sur,_Oman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The main college in Sur is the Sur College of Applied Sciences. The college has over 4000 students and offers degrees in Business, Communication, Information Technology and Design. It is considered one of the better institutions of higher learning in Oman. The second college is Sur University College.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Shipbuilding">Shipbuilding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sur,_Oman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Shipbuilding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>One of the famous cities in the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a> in building wooden ships. Its historical location gives it the hand to monitor the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Oman" title="Gulf of Oman">Gulf of Oman</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>. Many ships have been built in this city, like the <a href="/wiki/Sambuk" title="Sambuk">sambuk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ghanjah" title="Ghanjah">ghanjah</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> They formerly went as far as <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zanzibar" title="Zanzibar">Zanzibar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> and many other countries. These vessels were also used in <a href="/wiki/Pearl" title="Pearl">pearl</a> fishing.</p>
<p>Today the city has retained its reputation as a major <a href="/wiki/Dhow" title="Dhow">dhow</a>-building town, the very same vessels that were used for trade two centuries previously.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sur,_Oman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sur experiences a hot <a href="/wiki/Desert_climate" title="Desert climate">desert climate</a> with very little rainfall and high temperatures. Because of its coastal location, Sur's night-time temperatures are never very low. There is no distinct wetter season, but March tends to be the wettest month, and September the driest.</p>
<p>Occasionally, Sur gets battered by cyclones. In 2007, <a href="/wiki/Cyclone_Gonu" title="Cyclone Gonu">Cyclone Gonu</a> battered the city, and in 2010, Sur was hit by <a href="/wiki/Cyclone_Phet" title="Cyclone Phet">Cyclone Phet</a>.</p>
