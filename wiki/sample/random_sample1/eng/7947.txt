<p><b>Suree Sukha</b> (<a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>: <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">สุรีย์ สุขะ</span>, born July 27, 1982), is a Thai professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">Defender</a> for <a href="/wiki/Thai_League" title="Thai League">Thai League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Ubon_UMT_United_F.C." title="Ubon UMT United F.C.">Ubon UMT United</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suree_Sukha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Suree's twin younger brother <a href="/wiki/Surat_Sukha" title="Surat Sukha">Surat</a> is also a footballer and plays for <a href="/wiki/Buriram_United_F.C." title="Buriram United F.C.">Buriram United</a> as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Midfielder_(association_football)" title="Midfielder (association football)">Midfielder</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suree_Sukha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Suree formerly played for <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City F.C.</a> following a two-week trial with fellow Thai internationals <a href="/wiki/Kiatprawut_Saiwaeo" title="Kiatprawut Saiwaeo">Kiatprawut Saiwaeo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Teerasil_Dangda" title="Teerasil Dangda">Teerasil Dangda</a>,.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Man_City_take_Thai_trio_on_trial_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Man_City_take_Thai_trio_on_trial-1">[1]</a></sup> Sukha's contract was set to be signed by the English club, but his work permit application was denied by the British <a href="/wiki/Home_Office" title="Home Office">Home Office</a> as players originating from countries outside of the FIFA-ranked top 70 do not qualify according to Home Office guidelines. He also failed to pass on a clause requiring players to have participated in 75% of their country's internationals in the two years previous to the submission of the permit application.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Suree.27s_Man_City_move_in_doubt_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Suree.27s_Man_City_move_in_doubt-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>After a long wait, Sukha finally signed for Manchester City along with fellow Thai internationals and City trialists <a href="/wiki/Kiatprawut_Saiwaeo" title="Kiatprawut Saiwaeo">Kiatprawut Saiwaeo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Teerasil_Dangda" title="Teerasil Dangda">Teerasil Dangda</a> on 16 November 2007, on the same day that his former club, Chonburi, announced a partnership agreement with his new club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Three_more_clubs_join_global_alliance_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Three_more_clubs_join_global_alliance-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 9 May 2008, Sukha was quoted in the <a href="/wiki/Bangkok_Post" title="Bangkok Post">Bangkok Post</a>, as stating his future would lie in either China or Australia after his loan spell at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grasshoppers_Zurich" title="Grasshoppers Zurich">Grasshoppers Zurich</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Suree_wants_a_way_out_of_Grasshopper_Zurich_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Suree_wants_a_way_out_of_Grasshopper_Zurich-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
