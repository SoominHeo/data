<p>The <b>Sumatran serow</b> (<i>Capricornis sumatraensis</i>), also known as the southern serow, is a species of <a href="/wiki/Caprinae" title="Caprinae">goat-antelope</a> native to mountain forests in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thai-Malay_Peninsula" title="Thai-Malay Peninsula">Thai-Malay Peninsula</a> and on the <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesian</a> island of <a href="/wiki/Sumatra" title="Sumatra">Sumatra</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-2">[2]</a></sup> The former name of this species is "mainland serow", as all the mainland species of serow (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_Serow" title="Chinese Serow">Chinese</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Serow" title="Red Serow">red</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Himalayan_Serow" title="Himalayan Serow">Himalayan</a>) were previously considered <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> of this species. The Sumatran serow is threatened due to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Habitat_loss" title="Habitat loss">habitat loss</a> and hunting, leading to it being evaluated as <a href="/wiki/Vulnerable_species" title="Vulnerable species">vulnerable</a> by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IUCN" title="IUCN">IUCN</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
