<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Hydrometeorology</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Hydrometeorology</b> is a branch of <a href="/wiki/Meteorology" title="Meteorology">meteorology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hydrology" title="Hydrology">hydrology</a> that studies the transfer of <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a> and <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a> between the land surface and the lower <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere" title="Atmosphere">atmosphere</a>. <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> has several programmes and activities in place that deal with the study of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natural_hazards" title="Natural hazards">natural hazards</a> of hydrometeorological origin and the mitigation of their effects. Among these hazards are the results of natural processes or phenomena of atmospheric, hydrological or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oceanographic" title="Oceanographic">oceanographic</a> nature such as <a href="/wiki/Flood" title="Flood">floods</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drought" title="Drought">drought</a> and <a href="/wiki/Desertification" title="Desertification">desertification</a>. Many countries have established an operational hydrometeorological capability to assist with <a href="/wiki/Weather_forecasting" title="Weather forecasting">forecasting</a>, warning and informing the public of these developing hazards.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operational_Hydrometeorology_in_Practice">Operational Hydrometeorology in Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hydrometeorology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Operational Hydrometeorology in Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Countries with a current operational hydrometeorology service include, among others:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Meteorology" title="Bureau of Meteorology">Australia</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environment_Canada" title="Environment Canada">Canada</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flood_Forecasting_Centre" title="Flood Forecasting Centre">England and Wales (Flood Forecasting Centre)</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>France<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>India<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>Scotland<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>Serbia<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hydrometeorological_Centre_of_Russia" title="Hydrometeorological Centre of Russia">Russia</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydrometeorological_Prediction_Center" title="Hydrometeorological Prediction Center">The United States of America</a></li>
</ul>
