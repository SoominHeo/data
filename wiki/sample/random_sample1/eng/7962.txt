<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Surface combatant</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Surface combatants</b> (or <b>surface ships</b> or <b>surface vessels</b>) are a subset of naval <a href="/wiki/Warship" title="Warship">warships</a> which are designed for <a href="/wiki/Naval_warfare" title="Naval warfare">warfare</a> on the surface of the water, with their own weapons. They are generally ships built to fight other ships, submarines or aircraft, and can carry out several other missions including counter-narcotics operations and <a href="/wiki/Maritime_interdiction" title="Maritime interdiction">maritime interdiction</a>. Their primary purpose is to engage space, air, surface, and submerged targets with weapons deployed from the ship itself, rather than by manned carried craft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-naval_transform_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-naval_transform-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The term is primarily used to mean any modern vessel type that is not a <a href="/wiki/Submarine" title="Submarine">submarine</a>; although a "surface ship" may range in size from a small <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cutter_(ship)" title="Cutter (ship)">cutter</a> to an <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_carrier" title="Aircraft carrier">aircraft carrier</a>, the weapons and tactics have some commonality, more so than for submerged vessels. They look unusual and different from cruise and other ships.</p>
<p>Surface ships include <a href="/wiki/Cruiser" title="Cruiser">cruisers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frigate" title="Frigate">frigates</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Corvette" title="Corvette">corvettes</a>, and several outdated types including <a href="/wiki/Battleship" title="Battleship">battleships</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battlecruiser" title="Battlecruiser">battlecruisers</a>. The category does not include <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_carrier" title="Aircraft carrier">aircraft carriers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amphibious_assault_ship" title="Amphibious assault ship">amphibious assault ships</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mine_hunter" title="Mine hunter">mine hunters</a>, as these generally do not use on board weapons system (i.e. aircraft carriers generally only attack with their aircraft, and mine hunters are not primarily combat vessels). However some warships combine aspects of the surface combatant and other roles, such as the Russian <a href="/wiki/Kuznetsov-class_aircraft_carrier" title="Kuznetsov-class aircraft carrier"><i>Kuznetsov</i>-class aircraft carrier</a>, which carries both aircraft and an array of conventional armament (the class is sometimes termed a <i>"heavy aircraft-carrying cruiser"</i>).</p>
<p>Modern <a href="/wiki/Naval_warfare" title="Naval warfare">naval warfare</a> is divided into three operational areas: <a href="/wiki/Anti-surface_warfare" title="Anti-surface warfare">anti-surface warfare</a> (ASUW), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anti-air_warfare" title="Anti-air warfare">anti-air warfare</a> (AAW) and <a href="/wiki/Anti-submarine_warfare" title="Anti-submarine warfare">anti-submarine warfare</a> (ASW). The current canonical combined arms naval <a href="/wiki/Task_force" title="Task force">task force</a> or task group centers around a <a href="/wiki/Flagship" title="Flagship">flagship</a> hosting dedicated command elements to conduct tactical operations within each of these areas. In smaller surface action groups (i.e. a single or a few task elements, such as a lone <a href="/wiki/Aegis_Combat_System" title="Aegis Combat System">Aegis-equipped</a> destroyer or cruisers on patrol), the same <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Combatant_commander" title="Combatant commander">combatant commander</a> may be responsible for managing all three areas as part of his duty in carrying out his vessel's mission, while larger formations such as a <a href="/wiki/Carrier_strike_group" title="Carrier strike group">carrier strike group</a> may have an individual commander in charge of each separate warfare element. Western naval career advancement for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unrestricted_line_officer" title="Unrestricted line officer">unrestricted line officers</a> also follow this model: a career line officer in a command-oriented track will specialize, train, and be billeted into distinct surface, naval aviation, or subsurface warfare posts.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Missions">Missions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surface_combatant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Missions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> there are two divisions of surface combatant <a href="/wiki/Military_operation" title="Military operation">missions</a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Battle Force Capability (BFC): defines ships capable of <a href="/wiki/Command_of_the_sea" title="Command of the sea">command of the sea</a> and power projection:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-naval_transform_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-naval_transform-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[nb 1]</a></sup>
<ul>
<li>in a <a href="/wiki/Carrier_battle_group" title="Carrier battle group">carrier battle group</a></li>
<li>as units of a Surface Action Group (SAG) without a carrier.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Protection of Shipping (POS): defines ships intended to protect:
<ul>
<li>traditional <a href="/wiki/Convoy" title="Convoy">convoys</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Underway_replenishment" title="Underway replenishment">underway replenishment</a> groups</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Amphibious_warfare" title="Amphibious warfare">amphibious warfare</a> groups</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
