<p><i><b>The Aquariums of Pyongyang</b></i>, by <a href="/wiki/Kang_Chol-hwan" title="Kang Chol-hwan">Kang Chol-hwan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Rigoulot" title="Pierre Rigoulot">Pierre Rigoulot</a>, is an account of the imprisonment of Kang Chol-Hwan and his family in the <a href="/wiki/Yodok_concentration_camp" title="Yodok concentration camp">Yodok concentration camp</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>It begins with an introduction by co-author Pierre Rigoulot describing Kang's new life in the <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">Republic of Korea</a>, then continues with a brief history of both North and South Korea since the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a> in 1953.</p>
<p>It shows how a powerful family with money and material goods has everything taken from them by the <a href="/wiki/Workers%27_Party_of_Korea" title="Workers' Party of Korea">Workers' Party of Korea</a>. Kang's family, while of Korean ethnicity, originally <a href="/wiki/Koreans_in_Japan" title="Koreans in Japan">lived in Japan</a> before emigrating to the DPRK at the behest of his <a href="/wiki/Chongryon" title="Chongryon">communist</a> grandmother. When Kang was nine years old, his grandfather was imprisoned for suspected activity against the State. Because the policy at the time<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="at the time? isn't it still the policy? (February 2014)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> was to incarcerate the immediate family of political prisoners, Kang Chol-Hwan, his grandmother, father, uncle and younger sister Miho were all imprisoned at the <a href="/wiki/Yodok_concentration_camp" title="Yodok concentration camp">Yodok concentration camp</a> #2915. There they suffered and viewed many atrocities over a period of ten years including <a href="/wiki/Disease" title="Disease">disease</a>, <a href="/wiki/Starvation" title="Starvation">starvation</a>, torturous punishments and <a href="/wiki/Public_execution" title="Public execution">public executions</a>.</p>
<p>In the book, Kang states that, while in the camp, he met <a href="/wiki/Pak_Seung-zin" title="Pak Seung-zin">Pak Seung-zin</a>, a member of the North Korean football squad in the <a href="/wiki/1966_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1966 FIFA World Cup">1966 FIFA World Cup</a>. He says that Pak and other players had been imprisoned after returning from the tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> However, in the documentary film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Game_of_Their_Lives_(2002_film)" title="The Game of Their Lives (2002 film)">The Game of Their Lives</a></i>, Pak and the other players were interviewed and they denied Kang's claim that there had been any retribution.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following his family's release (presumably upon the death of his grandfather, the original offender against the State), Kang worked in assigned occupations before becoming at risk of again being sent to a concentration camp. The end of the book details his subsequent escape to <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and his attempts to seek asylum before escaping to South Korea.</p>
<p>The most recent publication, in 2005, includes an account of his meeting with former U.S. President <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Originally published in French in 2000, and translated into English in 2001 by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yair_Reiner&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yair Reiner (page does not exist)">Yair Reiner</a> and later into <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>, it is one of the first published accounts of the <a href="/wiki/Prisons_in_North_Korea" title="Prisons in North Korea">North Korean prison system</a>.</p>
