<p><b>BBC North West</b> is the <a href="/wiki/BBC_English_Regions" title="BBC English Regions">BBC English Region</a> serving <a href="/wiki/Cheshire" title="Cheshire">Cheshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester" title="Greater Manchester">Greater Manchester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Merseyside" title="Merseyside">Merseyside</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a> (western <a href="/wiki/Craven" title="Craven">Craven</a>), <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a> (Walsden), <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">Derbyshire</a> (western <a href="/wiki/High_Peak,_Derbyshire" title="High Peak, Derbyshire">High Peak</a>), <a href="/wiki/Cumbria" title="Cumbria">Cumbria</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barrow-in-Furness_(borough)" title="Barrow-in-Furness (borough)">Barrow-in-Furness</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Lakeland" title="South Lakeland">South Lakeland</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a>.</p>
<p>The region also covered the rest of Cumbria during the late 1980s, complete with an opt-out television news service for the area, before it was transferred to the BBC North East region owing to high viewer demand. Today, the region is part of the larger <a href="/wiki/BBC_North" title="BBC North">BBC North division</a> based at <a href="/wiki/MediaCityUK" title="MediaCityUK">MediaCityUK</a> in <a href="/wiki/Salford_Quays" title="Salford Quays">Salford Quays</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_North_West&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_North_West&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>BBC North West</i> produces regional TV news, current affairs and sports output including the flagship nightly news programme <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_West_Tonight" title="North West Tonight">North West Tonight</a></i>, alongside daytime <i>North West Today</i> bulletins and opt-out updates on weekdays during <i><a href="/wiki/BBC_Breakfast" title="BBC Breakfast">BBC Breakfast</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Non-news output consists of the topical magazine programme <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inside_Out_(BBC_TV_series)" title="Inside Out (BBC TV series)">Inside Out North West</a></i>, a 20-minute opt-out during <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sunday_Politics" title="Sunday Politics">Sunday Politics</a></i> and a late night football magazine show <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Kick_Off" title="Late Kick Off">Late Kick Off</a></i>. The rugby league highlights programme <i><a href="/wiki/Super_League_Show" title="Super League Show">The Super League Show</a></i>, produced by PDI Media at <a href="/wiki/BBC_Yorkshire" title="BBC Yorkshire">BBC Yorkshire's</a> studio in Leeds, is also simulcast for North West viewers.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio">Radio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_North_West&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Radio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The region is the controlling centre for <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Lancashire" title="BBC Radio Lancashire">BBC Radio Lancashire</a>, <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Manchester" title="BBC Radio Manchester">BBC Radio Manchester</a> and <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Merseyside" title="BBC Radio Merseyside">BBC Radio Merseyside</a>.</p>
<p>Radio Manchester broadcasts 24 hours a day, with Radio Merseyside and Radio Lancashire broadcasting between 5am &amp; 1am on weekdays, 6am &amp; 12am on Radio Lancashire at weekends and 6am &amp; 1am on Radio Merseyside at weekends. The three stations only simulcast for <i>BBC News in the North West</i> bulletins each evening, with Radio Manchester and Radio Lancashire also simulcasting the weekday late show and <i>Indus</i>, an Asian community programme on Sunday evenings.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Online_and_Interactive">Online and Interactive</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_North_West&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Online and Interactive">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>BBC North West</i> also produces regional news &amp; local radio pages for <a href="/wiki/BBC_Red_Button" title="BBC Red Button">BBC Red Button</a> and <i>BBC Local News</i> websites for each county and the Isle of Man.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_North_West&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio_2">Radio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_North_West&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Radio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Regional broadcasting in the North West had begun on 15 November 1922 when the BBC opened its Manchester radio station <a href="/wiki/2ZY" title="2ZY">2ZY</a>, followed two years later by the opening of a Liverpool-based relay station, 2LY. At this point, the Manchester operation covered the entire North of England and northern parts of the Midlands, with relay stations in Leeds (2LS), Hull (6KH), Nottingham (5NG) and Stoke-on-Trent (6ST) carrying 2ZY output. Early broadcasts were made from studios at the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan-Vickers" title="Metropolitan-Vickers">Metropolitan-Vickers</a> electricity works in Old Trafford.</p>
<p>When the <a href="/wiki/BBC_National_Programme" title="BBC National Programme">BBC National Programme</a> commenced from London, output from Manchester was moved to the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Regional_Programme" title="BBC Regional Programme">BBC Regional Programme</a>, and in 1929 began operations from Broadcasting House, a leased studio complex above a bank in Piccadilly Gardens. From March 1936, the Manchester-based Northern radio service began serving Northern Ireland after the opening of a new transmitter at <a href="/wiki/Lisnagarvey_transmitting_station" title="Lisnagarvey transmitting station">Lisnagarvey</a> (albeit with an additional local opt-out). A year later, the opening of the Stagshaw transmitter also allowed listeners in the North East and Cumbria to receive an opt-out from the main service.</p>
<p>When regional radio services resumed after the Second World War, Manchester-based output was broadcast on a Northern version of the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Home_Service" title="BBC Home Service">Home Service</a>, which would continue to serve Northern Ireland until January 1963. <a href="/wiki/BBC_Local_Radio" title="BBC Local Radio">BBC Local Radio</a> was introduced to the North West on 22 November 1967 with the launch of <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Merseyside" title="BBC Radio Merseyside">Radio Merseyside</a> in Liverpool, followed on 10 September 1970 by <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Manchester" title="BBC Radio Manchester">Radio Manchester</a> and on 26 January 1971 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Blackburn" title="BBC Radio Blackburn">Radio Blackburn</a> (later relaunched as BBC Radio Lancashire).</p>
<p>The expansion of local radio services led to a gradual reduction of regional radio programmes (covering both the North West and Yorkshire areas from the Holme Moss transmitter) on what had now become <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_4" title="BBC Radio 4">BBC Radio 4</a>. The last regional programme aired on Radio 4 North was an early evening news opt-out on Friday 12 September 1980. Regional output for the North West would later resurface on the BBC's local radio stations in evening and late night time slots.</p>
<p>The BBC has never produced radio services for the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a> although national BBC radio is available. Local radio services on the island are provided by Manx stations like <a href="/wiki/Manx_Radio" title="Manx Radio">Manx Radio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Energy_FM_(Isle_of_Man)" title="Energy FM (Isle of Man)">Energy FM</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_2">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_North_West&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When the BBC launched daily regional television news in September 1957, the whole of northern England was initially covered as one region from Manchester. Short bulletins called <i>News from the North</i> began broadcasting on 30 September to what are now the North West, <a href="/wiki/BBC_Yorkshire" title="BBC Yorkshire">Yorkshire &amp; North Midlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/BBC_Yorkshire_and_Lincolnshire" title="BBC Yorkshire and Lincolnshire">East Yorkshire &amp; Lincolnshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/BBC_North_East_and_Cumbria" title="BBC North East and Cumbria">North East &amp; Cumbria</a> areas.</p>
<p>Two years later, the opt-outs were refocused to cover the North West and Yorkshire &amp; Lincolnshire areas, as the North East and Cumbria was now receiving its own news bulletins. By 1962, the bulletins had been expanded into a 20-minute magazine programme and renamed <i>North at Six</i>. The current North West region was introduced on 25 March 1968 when a separate Leeds-based edition of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Look_North_(Yorkshire_and_North_Midlands)" title="Look North (Yorkshire and North Midlands)">Look North</a></i> for Yorkshire, Lincolnshire and the North Midlands was launched.</p>
<p>Between 5 April 1976 and 12 September 1980, BBC North West made use of regional presentation and continuity announcers during weekday evening programmes on BBC1. Also in 1980, the Manchester edition of <i>Look North</i> was renamed <i>Look North West</i> in an attempt to avoid confusion for neighbouring viewers in Yorkshire. Regional television news remained at the BBC's Old Broadcasting House in Piccadilly until finally moving into New Broadcasting House in Oxford Road on 18 May 1981. On 3 September 1984, the programme changed to its current title of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_West_Tonight" title="North West Tonight">North West Tonight</a></i>. Between 1986 and 1989, the region also covered parts of Cumbria that were previously served by the Newcastle edition of <i>Look North</i> and provided a news opt-out for the area served by the Caldbeck transmitter at lunchtime. However, viewers' complaints led to Cumbrian news coverage being switched back to <i>Look North</i> in Newcastle.</p>
<p>After 30 years of operation from New Broadcasting House, BBC North West television began broadcasting from MediaCityUK at Salford Quays on the morning of Monday 28 November 2011.</p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/New_Broadcasting_House_(Manchester)" title="New Broadcasting House (Manchester)">New Broadcasting House (Manchester)</a> and <a href="/wiki/MediaCityUK" title="MediaCityUK">MediaCityUK</a></div>
<p>From 1929 until 1981, many of the BBC's Manchester operations were based at Broadcasting House in the Piccadilly Gardens area of Manchester City Centre. The building had been leased to the Corporation from a bank on the ground floor and became the central control room for regional and network radio production from the city.</p>
<p>In 1954, a former Methodist church on Dickenson Road in <a href="/wiki/Rusholme" title="Rusholme">Rusholme</a> became the BBC's first Manchester television studio, having been brought from <a href="/wiki/Mancunian_Films" title="Mancunian Films">Mancunian Films</a>, who had converted the building. The Dickenson Road facilities became the main home of the city's network production base where early editions of long-running series including <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Top_Of_The_Pops" title="Top Of The Pops">Top Of The Pops</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/A_Question_of_Sport" title="A Question of Sport">A Question of Sport</a></i> were produced. The BBC's northern outside broadcast units were located at another former church in <a href="/wiki/Longsight" title="Longsight">Longsight</a>. Meanwhile, regional television news and presentation began in September 1957, firstly from the crypt at Dickenson Road and subsequently at Broadcasting House in Piccadilly Gardens, Manchester.</p>
<p>By 1967, planning had begun on creating a new broadcasting centre which would house radio and television on both network &amp; regional levels as well as rehearsal space (Studio 7) for the BBC's Northern Symphony Orchestra (later to become the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Philharmonic" title="BBC Philharmonic">BBC Philharmonic</a>). <a href="/wiki/New_Broadcasting_House_(Manchester)" title="New Broadcasting House (Manchester)">New Broadcasting House</a> on Oxford Road began operations in July 1975 when network television production was transferred from the Dickenson Road studio into the much larger Studio A. Studio B, which would be used for regional TV news (<i>Look North West</i>, <i>North West Tonight</i>) was opened in May 1981, allowing the BBC to vacate its studios at Piccadilly after 52 years.</p>
<p>Regular production in Studio A continued until 2000, when it was mothballed. Meanwhile, the BBC launched a joint facilities venture with <a href="/wiki/ITV_Granada" title="ITV Granada">ITV Granada</a>, called <a href="/wiki/3SixtyMedia" title="3SixtyMedia">3SixtyMedia</a>, which saw both broadcasters pool their studio resources at both Oxford Road and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Manchester_Studios" title="The Manchester Studios">Granada's Quay Street complex</a>. As a result, Studio A was re-opened in 2005 as a four-waller, primarily for drama productions including <i><a href="/wiki/Life_on_Mars_(UK_TV_series)" title="Life on Mars (UK TV series)">Life on Mars</a></i>.</p>
<p>BBC North West moved into new studios at <a href="/wiki/MediaCityUK" title="MediaCityUK">MediaCityUK</a> in <a href="/wiki/Salford_Quays" title="Salford Quays">Salford Quays</a> during Autumn 2011. The new development houses all departments previously based at New Broadcasting House alongside <a href="/wiki/CBBC" title="CBBC">BBC Children's</a>, <a href="/wiki/BBC_Sport" title="BBC Sport">BBC Sport</a>, <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_5_Live" title="BBC Radio 5 Live">BBC Radio 5 Live</a>, <a href="/wiki/BBC_Breakfast" title="BBC Breakfast">BBC Breakfast</a>, <a href="/wiki/BBC_Learning" title="BBC Learning">BBC Learning</a> and BBC Future Media departments and services, creating the biggest broadcast operation outside London. As part of the move, New Broadcasting House was sold off to Reality Estates Ltd for £10 million in April 2011.</p>
<p>In addition to their headquarters, BBC North West has <a href="/wiki/BBC_Local_Radio" title="BBC Local Radio">local radio</a> stations and news bureaux located in <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blackburn" title="Blackburn">Blackburn</a> and a district office in <a href="/wiki/Chester" title="Chester">Chester</a>.</p>
