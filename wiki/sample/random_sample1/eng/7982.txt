<p><b>Sucumbíos</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[sukumˈbi.os]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Ecuador" title="Provinces of Ecuador">province</a> in northeast <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a>. The capital and largest city is <a href="/wiki/Nueva_Loja" title="Nueva Loja">Nueva Loja</a> (normally referred to as Lago Agrio). It is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Ecuadorian_provinces_by_area" title="List of Ecuadorian provinces by area">fifth largest province in the country</a>, with an area of 18,084 km². In 2010, it had a <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 176,472 inhabitants.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sucumb%C3%ADos_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sucumbíos is bounded on the north by <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, on the south by <a href="/wiki/Napo_Province" title="Napo Province">Napo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Orellana_Province" title="Orellana Province">Orellana</a>, on the west by <a href="/wiki/Carchi_Province" title="Carchi Province">Carchi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Imbabura_Province" title="Imbabura Province">Imbabura</a>, on the southwest by <a href="/wiki/Pichincha_Province" title="Pichincha Province">Pichincha</a>, and on the east by <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>. Sucumbíos is the only province in Ecuador that borders two different countries.</p>
<p>The province is one of the six provinces in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazon_Basin" title="Amazon Basin">Amazon Region</a>, a natural region of Ecuador.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Orography">Orography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sucumb%C3%ADos_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Orography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The western area of the province belongs to the Eastern <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a> Mountains, where most <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">rivers</a> in the province have their sources. The most important elevation in the province is the <a href="/wiki/Reventador" title="Reventador">Reventador</a>, an active <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcano</a>. The eastern portion of the province is part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazon_Basin" title="Amazon Basin">Amazon Basin</a>, with high <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperatures</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hydrography">Hydrography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sucumb%C3%ADos_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hydrography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The main river in the province is the <a href="/wiki/Aguarico_River" title="Aguarico River">Aguarico River</a>. It passes close to Nueva Loja, and empties into the <a href="/wiki/Napo_River" title="Napo River">Napo</a>, on the Peruvian border. Other important rivers are the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I%C3%A7%C3%A1_River" title="Içá River">Putumayo</a>, which marks the border with Colombia, and the <a href="/wiki/Coca_River" title="Coca River">Coca</a> and <a href="/wiki/Napo_River" title="Napo River">Napo Rivers</a>, in the south.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sucumb%C3%ADos_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sucumbíos was an unexplored area, where only indigenous people lived, until <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">oil</a> was discovered in its soil. In 1979, after nine years of foundation, Nueva Loja became the seat of its own <a href="/wiki/Cantons_of_Ecuador" title="Cantons of Ecuador">canton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lago_Agrio_Canton" title="Lago Agrio Canton">Lago Agrio Canton</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Napo_Province" title="Napo Province">Napo Province</a>. On February 13, 1989, Sucumbíos became the 21st province in the country when it separated from Napo province.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sucumb%C3%ADos_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The most important <a href="/wiki/Natural_resource" title="Natural resource">natural resource</a> in the province is oil, the <a href="/wiki/Lago_Agrio_oil_field" title="Lago Agrio oil field">Lago Agrio oil field</a>. Thus, Sucumbíos is one of the most important provinces in the country, economically.</p>
