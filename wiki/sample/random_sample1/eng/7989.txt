<p><b>Suppiluliuma II</b>, the son of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tudhaliya_IV" title="Tudhaliya IV">Tudhaliya IV</a>, was the last known king of the New Kingdom of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hittite_Empire" title="Hittite Empire">Hittite Empire</a>, ruling c.<span style="white-space:nowrap;"> 1207</span>–1178 BC (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Short_chronology" title="Short chronology">short chronology</a>), contemporary with <a href="/wiki/Tukulti-Ninurta_I" title="Tukulti-Ninurta I">Tukulti-Ninurta I</a> of Assyria.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="His_life_and_reign">His life and reign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suppiluliuma_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: His life and reign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1210 BC, a fleet under his command defeated one manned by the <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cypriots</a>, the first recorded naval battle in history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> According to some historians (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Claude_F._A._Schaeffer" title="Claude F. A. Schaeffer">Claude Schaeffer</a>, Horst Nowacki, Wolfgang Lefèvre), this and following two victories against Cypriots were probably won by using <a href="/wiki/Ugarit" title="Ugarit">Ugartic</a> ships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-naval_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-naval-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He is known from two inscriptions in <a href="/wiki/Hieroglyphic_Luwian" title="Hieroglyphic Luwian">Hieroglyphic Luwian</a>. They record wars against former vassal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tarhuntassa" title="Tarhuntassa">Tarhuntassa</a>, and against Alasiya in <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>. One inscription is found at the base of Nisantepe in the Upper City of <a href="/wiki/Hattusa" title="Hattusa">Hattusa</a>; the other is on the northern corner of the East Pond (Pond 1), in what is known as Chamber 2. This served as a water reservoir for Hattusa.</p>
<p>The chamber 2 reliefs are historically important since it records major political instability which plagued Hatti during Suppiluliuma's reign. It states that this ruler sacked the city of Tarhutassa which was a Hittite city and had briefly served as the Empire's political capital under the reign of <a href="/wiki/Muwatalli_II" title="Muwatalli II">Muwatalli II</a>.</p>
<p>The Hittite kingdom was ultimately destroyed by the invading <a href="/wiki/Sea_Peoples" title="Sea Peoples">Sea Peoples</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kaskians" title="Kaskians">Kaskians</a> in the late 1170s BCE. Based on records in <a href="/wiki/Ugarit" title="Ugarit">Ugarit</a>, the threat originated in the west, and the Hittite king asked for assistance from Ugarit.</p>
<dl>
<dd>"The enemy [advances(?)] against us and there is no number [...]. Our number is pure(?) [. . .] Whatever is available, look for it and send it to me."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ammurapi" title="Ammurapi">Ammurapi</a>, an ally of Suppululiuma II and the last <a href="/wiki/King" title="King">king</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ugarit" title="Ugarit">Ugarit</a>, wrote a letter outlining the threat posed by the invading Sea Peoples and pleaded for help from <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Eshuwara&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Eshuwara (page does not exist)">Eshuwara</a>, the ruler of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alasiya" title="Alasiya">Alasiya</a> (Cyprus):</p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;"><i>My father, behold, the enemy's ships came (here); my cities(?) were burned, and they did evil things in my country. Does not my father know that all my troops and chariots(?) are in the Hittite country, and all my ships are in the <a href="/wiki/Lukka_lands" title="Lukka lands">land of Lukka</a>? . . . Thus, the country is abandoned to itself. May my father know it: the seven ships of the enemy that came here inflicted much damage upon us</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>
</tr>
</table>
<p>After Suppiluliuma's kingdom collapsed, the Kaskian tribes were probably in control of Hatti. Hattusa itself was destroyed by fire, its site only re-occupied by a <a href="/wiki/Phrygians" title="Phrygians">Phrygian</a> fortress some 500 years later. <a href="/wiki/Kuzi-Teshub" title="Kuzi-Teshub">Kuzi-Teshub</a>, a ruler of <a href="/wiki/Carchemish" title="Carchemish">Carchemish</a>, would later assume the title of "Great King" since he was a direct descendent of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suppiluliuma_I" title="Suppiluliuma I">Suppiluliuma I</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some sources indicate Suppiluliuma II's end is unknown or he was simply "vanished",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-naval_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-naval-2">[2]</a></sup> while some claim he was killed during the sack of Hattusa in 1190 BCE.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
