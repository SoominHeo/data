<p><b>BEC Tero Sasana Football Club</b> (<a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>: <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">สโมสรฟุตบอลบีอีซี เทโรศาสน</span>) is a Thai professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>. The club finished runners-up in the inaugural <a href="/wiki/AFC_Champions_League" title="AFC Champions League">AFC Champions League</a> competition in 2003.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_history">Club history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BEC_Tero_Sasana_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The team was established in 1992. It was previously known as <b>Sasana Witthaya School</b> team and was formed by Mr. <a href="/wiki/Worawi_Makudi" title="Worawi Makudi">Worawi Makudi</a>. The first football match this team entered was in Division 3 of the football royal cup in 1993. In 1994, the team played in Division 2 of the football royal cup In 1995, the team played in Division 1 of the football royal cup and in 1996, the team entered the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thai_Premier_League" title="Thai Premier League">Thai League</a> for the first time. It was during this year, in 1996, that Mr. Worawi Makudi and Mr. Brian L. Marcar, managing director of <a href="/wiki/BEC-TERO" title="BEC-TERO">BEC-TERO Entertainment Co., Ltd.</a>, joined hands and renamed the team as Football Club Tero Sasana. The team was placed in the 12th position among 18 teams in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thai_Premier_League" title="Thai Premier League">Thai League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1997, Football Club Tero Sasana also played in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thai_Premier_League" title="Thai Premier League">Thai League</a> and this time was placed in the fifth place. Later in 1998, BEC-World Public Company Limited supported the Football Club Tero Sasana and changed the team's name to Tero Sasana Sasana FC. The team won the third place when they played the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thai_Premier_League" title="Thai Premier League">Thai League</a>. They were also one of the eight final teams to enter the final round of the <a href="/wiki/Thai_FA_Cup" title="Thai FA Cup">Thai FA Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1999, the team entered the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thai_Premier_League" title="Thai Premier League">Thai League</a> and again won the third place. In the same year, they were among the final teams in the <a href="/wiki/Thai_FA_Cup" title="Thai FA Cup">Thai FA Cup</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The year 2000 was a very eventful year for the BEC-Tero Sasana FC. The team won its first championship award by winning the Thai League. It also received the championship award for the <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Cup" title="King's Cup">King's Cup</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2001, BEC-Tero Sasana FC was able to keep its championship and won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thai_Premier_League" title="Thai Premier League">Thai League</a> for the second consecutive year. This greatly increased the fan base.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The club's success continued for a few years and culminated with finishing <b>Runner-up</b> in the 2002–03 <a href="/wiki/AFC_Champions_League" title="AFC Champions League">AFC Champions League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2012 The club appointed <a href="/wiki/Robert_Procureur" title="Robert Procureur">Robert Procureur</a> former general manager of <a href="/wiki/Muangthong_United_F.C." title="Muangthong United F.C.">Muangthong United</a> to be new Director of Football, He build up a great team by pushing up many young players such as <a href="/wiki/Peerapat_Notchaiya" title="Peerapat Notchaiya">Peerapat Notchaiya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tanaboon_Kesarat" title="Tanaboon Kesarat">Tanaboon Kesarat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chanathip_Songkrasin" title="Chanathip Songkrasin">Chanathip Songkrasin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tristan_Do" title="Tristan Do">Tristan Do</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chenrop_Samphaodi" title="Chenrop Samphaodi">Chenrop Samphaodi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaturong_Pimkoon" title="Jaturong Pimkoon">Jaturong Pimkoon</a> and the fan called "Golden Generation" that was the important to help the team win the <a href="/wiki/Thai_League_Cup" title="Thai League Cup">Thai League Cup</a> Trophy in 2014, is the first tropy in 12 years. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016 after the club was relegation (Saraburi F.C. was out of the thai league due to their money problem in 2016 so the club was place in the place of Saraburi F.C.) Mr. Brian L. Marcar the club president sold the team to Inspire Entertainment related company to Siamsport the owner of <a href="/wiki/Muangthong_United_F.C." title="Muangthong United F.C.">Muangthong United</a>. Mr.<a href="/wiki/Robert_Procureur" title="Robert Procureur">Robert Procureur</a> was quit and the superstar player as <a href="/wiki/Peerapat_Notchaiya" title="Peerapat Notchaiya">Peerapat Notchaiya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tanaboon_Kesarat" title="Tanaboon Kesarat">Tanaboon Kesarat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chanathip_Songkrasin" title="Chanathip Songkrasin">Chanathip Songkrasin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tristan_Do" title="Tristan Do">Tristan Do</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tristan_Do" title="Tristan Do">Tristan Do</a> joined <a href="/wiki/Muangthong_United_F.C." title="Muangthong United F.C.">Muangthong United</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
