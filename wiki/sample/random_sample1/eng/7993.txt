<p><i><b>Moribito: Guardian of the Spirit</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">Á≤æÈúä„ÅÆÂÆà„Çä‰∫∫</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Seirei no Moribito</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the first in the twelve volume <i><a href="/wiki/Moribito_series" title="Moribito series">Moribito</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ÂÆà„Çä‰∫∫</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> series of Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fantasy_novels" title="Fantasy novels">fantasy novels</a> by <a href="/wiki/Nahoko_Uehashi" title="Nahoko Uehashi">Nahoko Uehashi</a>. It has since been adapted into numerous media, including radio, <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Moribito:_Guardian_of_the_Spirit#Anime" title="Moribito: Guardian of the Spirit">anime</a> adaptations. Scholastic released the first novel in English in June 2008. <a href="/wiki/Media_Blasters" title="Media Blasters">Media Blasters</a> has confirmed that they acquired the rights to the anime.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The series premiered on <a href="/wiki/Adult_Swim" title="Adult Swim">Adult Swim</a> in the U.S. at 1:30¬†a.m. on August 24, 2008 <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Time_Zone" title="Eastern Time Zone">EST</a>, but was dropped from the schedule without warning or explanation on January 15, 2009 after two runs of the first ten episodes. The program returned to Adult Swim during the summer 2009 line-up with an airing of the entire series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_introduction">Plot introduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moribito:_Guardian_of_the_Spirit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot introduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Balsa, spear wielder and bodyguard, is a wandering warrior who has vowed to atone for eight deaths in her past by saving an equivalent number of lives. On her journey, she saves Prince Chagum, and is tasked with becoming his bodyguard. His own father, the emperor, has ordered his assassination. The two begin a perilous journey to ensure the survival of the prince. Balsa's complicated past begins to come to light and they uncover Chagum's mysterious connection to a legendary water spirit with the power to destroy the kingdom.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Protagonists">Protagonists</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moribito:_Guardian_of_the_Spirit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Protagonists">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt><span id="Balsa"></span><span id="Balsa">Balsa</span></dt>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: Mabuki Andou (Japanese); <a href="/wiki/Cindy_Robinson" title="Cindy Robinson">Cindy Robinson</a> (English)</span></li>
<li>Played by: <a href="/wiki/Haruka_Ayase" title="Haruka Ayase">Haruka Ayase</a></li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>A skilled warrior from the faraway land of Kanbal. She is 30 years old. Balsa claims to be a simple bodyguard-for-hire; in reality she wields her spear in order to save lives in atonement for the eight lives that were taken to protect her when she was a child. Balsa is pragmatic and intelligent and she does not hold much regard for class distinction customs. Despite her deadly skill with a spear, Balsa tries not to kill in combat. As a result her body is covered with scars. At one point it's revealed that she is the daughter of the former Kanbal King's physician who had discovered that the new King had poisoned his predecessor. Worried that his crime would be revealed the new King ordered the physician and his daughter, Balsa, be executed for treason. To protect his daughter the physician asked the one warrior he could trust, Jiguro who was the first of the King's 9 Spears, to take her and run while he stayed behind. Unfortunately the King sent the other 8 Spears (while holding their families hostage) after them. Her guardian was able to protect her but had to kill all 8 of his former comrades to do so. Later he taught Balsa how to use a spear responsibly after seeing her perfectly re-enact the moves she'd seen him use. During their journey, Balsa becomes attached to Chagum and even teaches him some important lessons in life. At the end of the series, she is seen returning to Kanbal for reasons unspecified but is hinted to be putting Jiguro's soul to rest.</dd>

<p>The series has been adapted into an <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a>, produced by <a href="/wiki/Production_I.G" title="Production I.G">Production I.G</a> and directed by <a href="/wiki/Kenji_Kamiyama" title="Kenji Kamiyama">Kenji Kamiyama</a>, which premiered in Japan on <a href="/wiki/NHK" title="NHK">NHK</a> from April 7, 2007. The anime runs 26 episodes and is based entirely on the first novel in the Guardian series, and greatly expands the midsection of the novel.</p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_International_Anime_Fair" title="Tokyo International Anime Fair">Tokyo International Anime Fair</a> 2007 in March, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geneon" title="Geneon">Geneon</a> announced that they had acquired the license to the anime and <a href="/wiki/Scholastic_Corporation" title="Scholastic Corporation">Scholastic</a> announced they had US distribution rights to the novels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tokyo_Animation_Fair_news_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tokyo_Animation_Fair_news-4">[4]</a></sup> After Geneon discontinued its US distribution division, the rights transferred to <a href="/wiki/Media_Blasters" title="Media Blasters">Media Blasters</a>. The series premiered in the United States at 1:30¬†a.m. on August 24, 2008 on <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Adult_Swim" title="Adult Swim">Adult Swim</a> block, but was dropped from the schedule without warning or explanation on January 15, 2009 after two runs of the first ten episodes. On June 13, 2009, the series was back on <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Adult_Swim" title="Adult Swim">Adult Swim</a> block in the United States at 1:30am Sundays, but was moved to 2:30¬†a.m. Eastern time,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> swapping it with <i><a href="/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist" title="Fullmetal Alchemist">Fullmetal Alchemist</a></i> in November. On January 8, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Viz_Media" title="Viz Media">Viz Media</a> announced that they have acquired the series and will be re-releasing the series on DVD and Blu-ray, as well as airing the series on their broadcasting channel, <a href="/wiki/Neon_Alley" title="Neon Alley">Neon Alley</a> beginning on January 17, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The series feature two theme songs. The opening title is Shine by L'Arc - en - Ciel, while Sachi Tainaka performs Itoshii Hito e for ending title.</p>
