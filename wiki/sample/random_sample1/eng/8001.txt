<p><i><b>Hello, Schoolgirl</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">순정만화</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Sunjeong Manhwa</span></i>; literally "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sunjung_Manhwa" title="Sunjung Manhwa">pure/romantic comic</a>," the Korean version of <a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Djo_manga" title="Shōjo manga">shōjo manga</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_South_Korean_films_of_2008" title="List of South Korean films of 2008">2008 South Korean film</a>. Adapted from <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Love_Story_(webcomic)" title="Love Story (webcomic)">Love Story</a></i>, a popular <a href="/wiki/Webtoon" title="Webtoon">webtoon</a> by <a href="/wiki/Kang_Full" title="Kang Full">Kang Full</a>, it is the second film directed by Ryoo Jang-ha. It stars <a href="/wiki/Yoo_Ji-tae" title="Yoo Ji-tae">Yoo Ji-tae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Yeon-hee" title="Lee Yeon-hee">Lee Yeon-hee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chae_Jung-an" title="Chae Jung-an">Chae Jung-an</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kang-in" title="Kang-in">Kang-in</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hello,_Schoolgirl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kwon Yeon-woo is a somewhat naive 30-year-old low-level civil servant who works in a city hall branch office. After moving into a new apartment, he encounters a high school girl, Soo-young, and sees her on his way to work every morning. She is a cheerful and eccentric 18-year-old who lives with her mother downstairs from him. Over time, the two begin to develop feelings for one another.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, 22-year-old Kang Sook, has just started working at the branch office. He falls head over heels for 29-year-old Kwon Ha-kyeong, a melancholy woman who wanders around taking pictures. She is still holding onto an old flame, and keeps looking for traces of that lost love every day. Kang Sook continues to woo her, regardless of her living in the past.</p>
<p>Can these two relationships ever lead to a happy ending?</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hello,_Schoolgirl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Hello Schoolgirl</i> is the second feature film directed by Ryoo Jang-ha, following his 2004 debut <i><a href="/wiki/Springtime_(2004_film)" title="Springtime (2004 film)">Springtime</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The film is adapted from the first <a href="/wiki/Webtoon" title="Webtoon">webtoon</a> by <a href="/wiki/Kang_Full" title="Kang Full">Kang Full</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Joon_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Joon-3">[3]</a></sup> which was serialized on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daum_Communications" title="Daum Communications">Daum</a> and attracted a record-breaking 60 million page views and 500,000 visitor comments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> As with the webtoon, the story of the film revolves around two unconventional romances with a noticeable <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Age_disparity" title="Age disparity">age disparity</a>, though other details of the original serial were changed, such as setting the story in the summer as opposed to winter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Joon_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Joon-3">[3]</a></sup> The script took two years to complete,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and filming commenced on April 1, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hello,_Schoolgirl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Hello, Schoolgirl</i> was released in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> on November 27, 2008, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Box_office_number-one_films_of_2008_(South_Korea)" title="Box office number-one films of 2008 (South Korea)">topped the box office</a> on its opening weekend with 309,065 admissions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> As of December 21, the film had received a total of 740,379 admissions.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hello,_Schoolgirl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Joon Soh of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Korea_Times" title="The Korea Times">The Korea Times</a></i> praised the film for retaining the essence of the webtoon despite deviations from the original story, saying, "Ryoo succeeds in capturing the tenderness of the online comic, which slowly comes to the surface as the relationships unfold. There is a rich, layered quality to the film, where each little decision or gesture leads to further meanings and possibilities"; however, he also noted that "there are times when the movie aims too much for the conventionally beautiful, sacrificing the awkward, self-deprecating humor that drives much of Kang Full's works."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Joon_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Joon-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
