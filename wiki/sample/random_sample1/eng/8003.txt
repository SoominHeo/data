<p><b>Suncheon Bay National Garden</b>(<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">순천만국가정원</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: <span lang="ko-Hani" xml:lang="ko-Hani">順天灣國家庭園</span>) is located in <a href="/wiki/Suncheon" title="Suncheon">Suncheon</a> (Suncheon-si) in <a href="/wiki/South_Jeolla_Province" title="South Jeolla Province">South Jeolla Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. The area of the garden is 1.112 km² plus 28 km² of <a href="/wiki/Suncheon_Bay" title="Suncheon Bay">Suncheon Bay</a>. There are about 860,000 trees and 650,000 autumn flowers such as roses, sunflowers, and cosmos flowers are planted in the garden.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The city opened <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2013_Suncheon_Garden_Expo_Korea" title="2013 Suncheon Garden Expo Korea">Suncheon Bay International Garden Expo 2013</a> from April 20 to October 20, attracting over 4.4 million visitors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">[3]</a></sup> One of the expo officials said that the main purpose of the expo was to preserve Suncheon Bay by building a garden.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Suncheon Bay Garden was designated as the No.1 National Garden in August and held a proclamation ceremony on September 5, 2015. According to the data collected from Suncheon on October 13, the total visitors of the garden is now at the 400 million mark.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The workshop on how to spread the garden culture and to activate the garden industry was held on November 5 at the Suncheon Bay International Wetland Center. This workshop was co-organized by <a href="/wiki/Korea_Forest_Service" title="Korea Forest Service">Korea Forest Service</a> and Korea Green Promotion Agency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Arboretum">Arboretum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suncheon_Bay_National_Garden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Arboretum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Arboretum zone features various kinds of path including Autumn Tint path, Maple Tree path, and Meditation path. It also features Korean Traditional Garden where the Palace Garden, the Noblemen's Garden, and the Garden of Hope can be found. The Royal Azalea Garden has locally grown <a href="/wiki/Rhododendron_schlippenbachii" title="Rhododendron schlippenbachii">royal azalea trees</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> with over a hundred species of royal azaleas. Another feature of arboretum is the Tree Ground which has over 200 species of naturally grown trees such as <a href="/wiki/Magnolia_grandiflora" title="Magnolia grandiflora">southern magnolia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zelkova" title="Zelkova">zelkova</a>, and hackberry tree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wetland_Center">Wetland Center</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suncheon_Bay_National_Garden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Wetland Center">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sucheon,_Dream_Bridge,_Nov_2015.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="823" data-file-width="747" height="242" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/ee/Sucheon%2C_Dream_Bridge%2C_Nov_2015.jpg/220px-Sucheon%2C_Dream_Bridge%2C_Nov_2015.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/ee/Sucheon%2C_Dream_Bridge%2C_Nov_2015.jpg/330px-Sucheon%2C_Dream_Bridge%2C_Nov_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/ee/Sucheon%2C_Dream_Bridge%2C_Nov_2015.jpg/440px-Sucheon%2C_Dream_Bridge%2C_Nov_2015.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The Wetland Center Zone features the Dream Bridge designed by artist Kang Ik-Jung. The wall of the bridge is decorated with 145,000 drawings on old shipping container by children around the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> It also operates SkyCube which runs between the Expo grounds and Suncheon Bay Ecological Park. The Suncheon Bay International Wetland Center which is one of the main buildings of the Expo is built in the Wetland Center Zone. This center is where visitors can experience activities and learn about importance of Suncheon Bay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It also features Water Bird's Playground, Wildlife Conversation Park, and ECOGEO Greenhouse.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Garden_Zone">World Garden Zone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suncheon_Bay_National_Garden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: World Garden Zone">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Suncheon.jpeg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="960" data-file-width="960" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Suncheon.jpeg/220px-Suncheon.jpeg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Suncheon.jpeg/330px-Suncheon.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Suncheon.jpeg/440px-Suncheon.jpeg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Suncheon.jpeg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Netherlands Garden</div>

</div>
<p>The World Garden Zone is a collaborative space created by designers and artists from Korea and all over the world. It consists of indoor, outdoor gardens, and gardens of 11 countries including German, Dutch, Japanese, and Spanish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wetland_Zone">Wetland Zone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suncheon_Bay_National_Garden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Wetland Zone">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Wetland Zone is where visitors can experience natural environments. The Biotope Wetland was built to protect the Suncheon Bay wetland ecology. A walking trail work as a guide for visitors to explore ecology of inland wetland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The Kindness Forest is for visitors in all-age, where the smooth pavements make wheelchairs and strollers accessible.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The Eco-Training Playground is where visitors can learn about wetlands by examining plants of the wetland and ecology with a help of ecology experts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
