<p><b>Shuozhou</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh">朔州</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Shuòzhōu</span></i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Prefecture-level_city" title="Prefecture-level city">prefecture-level city</a> in the north of <a href="/wiki/Shanxi" title="Shanxi">Shanxi</a> province, People's Republic of China, situated along the upper reaches of the <a href="/wiki/Fen_River" title="Fen River">Fen River</a>. In 2010, its population was approximately 1.71 million, and the city has an area of approximately 5,737 km<sup>2</sup> (2,215 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2010_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Census2010-1">[1]</a></sup> Administratively, Shuozhou is a <a href="/wiki/Prefecture-level_city" title="Prefecture-level city">prefecture-level city</a>, with its seat in <a href="/wiki/Shuocheng_District" title="Shuocheng District">Shuocheng District</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dayun_Expressway" title="Dayun Expressway">Dayun Expressway</a> (<a href="/wiki/Datong" title="Datong">Datong</a>—<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuncheng,_Shanxi" title="Yuncheng, Shanxi">Yuncheng</a>) passes through it, and it has 5 specialized train lines.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shuozhou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is a centre of industry, and its notable industries are primarily mining of <a href="/wiki/Coal_mining" title="Coal mining">coal</a> and other ores such as <a href="/wiki/Iron" title="Iron">iron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bauxite" title="Bauxite">bauxite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mica" title="Mica">mica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manganese" title="Manganese">manganese</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Graphite" title="Graphite">graphite</a>. Other economic sectors include <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chemical_industry" title="Chemical industry">chemical industry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ceramic" title="Ceramic">ceramics</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">fishing</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shuozhou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Western tourists rarely come to this area of China, but there are some attractions. The <a href="/wiki/Pagoda_of_Fogong_Temple" title="Pagoda of Fogong Temple">Yingxian Tower</a>, built in 1056 during the Liao Dynasty, is one of the main sites of the region. It was built entirely of wood, without using nails, and serves as a museum of <a href="/wiki/Calligraphy" title="Calligraphy">calligraphy</a>. There are also some <a href="/wiki/Paleolithic" title="Paleolithic">paleolithic</a> ruins, and ancient gravesites from the <a href="/wiki/Dongyi" title="Dongyi">Dongyi</a> people.</p>
