<p><b>Xuanwumen Station</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">宣武门站</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">宣武門站</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Xuānwǔmén Zhàn</span></i>) is an interchange <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subway_station" title="Subway station">station</a> between <a href="/wiki/Line_2,_Beijing_Subway" title="Line 2, Beijing Subway">Line 2</a> and <a href="/wiki/Line_4,_Beijing_Subway" title="Line 4, Beijing Subway">Line 4</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Beijing_Subway" title="Beijing Subway">Beijing Subway</a>. It is named for <a href="/wiki/Xuanwumen_(Beijing)" title="Xuanwumen (Beijing)">Xuanwumen</a>, a former gate in Beijing's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beijing_city_wall" title="Beijing city wall">city wall</a> that was demolished during construction of the subway. The station opened in 1971 and handles an average of 350,000 passengers per day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
