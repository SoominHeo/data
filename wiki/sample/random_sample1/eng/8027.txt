<p><b>Xuchang</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E8%AE%B8%E6%98%8C" title="wikt:许昌">许昌</a></span>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Postal_romanization" title="Postal romanization">postal</a>: Hsuchang) is a <a href="/wiki/Prefecture-level_city" title="Prefecture-level city">prefecture-level city</a> in central <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Province_of_China" title="Province of China">province</a> in <a href="/wiki/Central_China" title="Central China">Central China</a>. It borders the provincial capital of <a href="/wiki/Zhengzhou" title="Zhengzhou">Zhengzhou</a> to the northwest, <a href="/wiki/Kaifeng" title="Kaifeng">Kaifeng</a> to the northeast, <a href="/wiki/Zhoukou" title="Zhoukou">Zhoukou</a> to the east, <a href="/wiki/Luohe" title="Luohe">Luohe</a> to the southeast, and <a href="/wiki/Pingdingshan" title="Pingdingshan">Pingdingshan</a> to the southwest.</p>
<p>Its population was 4,307,488 inhabitants at the final 2010 census, of whom 1,952,666 lived in the built-up (or "metro") area made up of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weidu" title="Weidu">Weidu</a> district, Xuchang county and Changge City largely being urbanized.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2007, the city was named as one of China's top ten livable cities by Chinese Cities Brand Value Report, which was released at 2007 Beijing Summit of China Cities Forum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xuchang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to tradition, the city was named after Xu, an ancient state led by tribal leader Xuyou during the <a href="/wiki/Spring_and_Autumn_period" title="Spring and Autumn period">Spring and Autumn period</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zhou_Dynasty" title="Zhou Dynasty">Zhou Dynasty</a>. In ancient times, the city occupied a strategic location in central China.</p>
<p>Xuchang served as the warlord <a href="/wiki/Cao_Cao" title="Cao Cao">Cao Cao</a>'s de facto capital during the late <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Han_Dynasty" title="Eastern Han Dynasty">Eastern Han Dynasty</a>. After finding the old capital <a href="/wiki/Luoyang" title="Luoyang">Luoyang</a> ravaged by war, Cao moved the imperial court and <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Xian_of_Han" title="Emperor Xian of Han">Emperor Xian</a> to what is now Xuchang in 196.</p>
<p>In 220, Cao Cao's son and successor <a href="/wiki/Cao_Pi" title="Cao Pi">Cao Pi</a> officially declared the city as the capital of the newly established state of <a href="/wiki/Cao_Wei" title="Cao Wei">Cao Wei</a>. The city was renamed "Xuchang", meaning "Xu Rising". The Wei emperors held court at Xuchang until the capital was moved to Luoyang in the 220s.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_climate">Geography and climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xuchang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography and climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Xuchang ranges in latitude from 33° 16' to 34° 24' N and in longitude from 113° 03' to 114° 190' E.</p>
<p>Xuchang has a <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoon</a>-influenced <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen</a> <i>Cwa</i>), with four distinct seasons. Winters are cool and dry, summers hot and humid, spring begins early and is warm, and autumn is mild and provides a reasonable transition. Rain mainly falls from May to September, as more than 70% of the annual precipitation occurs then. The city has an annual mean temperature of at 14.5 °C (58.1 °F), and its highest average monthly temperature is 27.1 °C (80.8 °F) in July and the lowest is 0.7 °C (33.3 °F) in January. Just over 700 millimetres (28 in) of precipitation falls each year, and there is on average 217 frost-free days and 2280 hours of sunshine per year.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Agriculture_and_industry">Agriculture and industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xuchang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Agriculture and industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Xuchang is an important center of the Chinese <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">tobacco</a> industry. However, due to economic reasons many farmers have chosen not to plant tobacco anymore.</p>
<p>The city is also famous for its man-made human hair exports.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xuchang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Xuchang_University" title="Xuchang University">Xuchang University</a> is in the city.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xuchang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Famous tourist attractions include Baling Bridge and Chunqiu Tower.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sister_city">Sister city</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xuchang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sister city">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 18 December 2006, the <a href="/wiki/Oromia_Region" title="Oromia Region">Oromia Region</a> government in <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a> signed an agreement with Henan Province to establish a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Town_twinning" title="Town twinning">sister city</a> program with <a href="/wiki/Ambo,_Ethiopia" title="Ambo, Ethiopia">Ambo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bolingbrook,_Illinois" title="Bolingbrook, Illinois">Bolingbrook, Illinois</a> is also a sister city of Xuchang.</p>
