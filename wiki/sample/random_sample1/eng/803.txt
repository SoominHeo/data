<ul>

</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="People">People</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BIN&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: People">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Places">Places</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BIN&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Places">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Science_and_mathematics">Science and mathematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BIN&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Science and mathematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>In mathematics, the <a href="/wiki/Histogram" title="Histogram">histogram</a> of a discrete variable that can acquire <i>m</i> different values is called an "<i>m</i>-bin histogram"</li>
<li>In statistics, each of a series of ranges of numerical value into which data are sorted in statistical analysis <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (see <a href="/wiki/Data_binning" title="Data binning">Data binning</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bin_(computational_geometry)" title="Bin (computational geometry)">Bin (computational geometry)</a>, space partitioning data structure to enable fast region queries and nearest neighbor search</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_uses">Other uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BIN&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>

<li>In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_personal_names" title="Arabic personal names">Arabic personal names</a>, "son of", e.g. in "Hamad bin Khalid bin Hamad" (a variant of <i>ibn</i>)</li>
