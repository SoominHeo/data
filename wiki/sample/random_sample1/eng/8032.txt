<p><i><b>Shunosaurus</b></i>, meaning "Shu Lizard", is a <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sauropod" title="Sauropod">sauropod</a> <a href="/wiki/Dinosaur" title="Dinosaur">dinosaur</a> from <a href="/wiki/Middle_Jurassic" title="Middle Jurassic">Middle Jurassic</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bathonian" title="Bathonian">Bathonian</a>–<a href="/wiki/Callovian" title="Callovian">Callovian</a>) beds in <a href="/wiki/Sichuan" title="Sichuan">Sichuan</a> Province in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, 170 million years ago. The name derives from "Shu", an ancient name for the Sichuan province.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Discovery_and_species">Discovery and species</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shunosaurus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Discovery and species">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first fossil of <i>Shunosaurus</i> was discovered in 1977 by a group of students, practising paleontological excavation at a road bank. The <a href="/wiki/Type_species" title="Type species">type species</a>, <i>Shunosaurus lii</i>, was described and named by <a href="/wiki/Dong_Zhiming" title="Dong Zhiming">Dong Zhiming</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Zhou_Shiwu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Zhou Shiwu (page does not exist)">Zhou Shiwu</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Zhang_Yihong&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Zhang Yihong (page does not exist)">Zhang Yihong</a> in 1983. The generic name derives from "Shu", an ancient name for Sichuan. The <a href="/wiki/Specific_name_(zoology)" title="Specific name (zoology)">specific name</a> honours <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydrologist" title="Hydrologist">hydrologist</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Li_Bing_(administrator)" title="Li Bing (administrator)">Li Bing</a>, the governor of Sichuan in the third century BC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Holotype" title="Holotype">holotype</a>, <b>IVPP V.9065</b>, was collected from the Lower <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xiashaximiao_Formation" title="Xiashaximiao Formation">Xiashaximiao Formation</a> near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dashanpu" title="Dashanpu">Dashanpu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zigong" title="Zigong">Zigong</a>. It consists of a partial skeleton. Later about twenty more major specimens were discovered, including several complete or near-complete skeletons, skulls and juveniles<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, making <i>Shunosaurus</i> one of the best anatomically known sauropods, with 94% of all skeletal elements identified. <i>Shunosaurus</i> skeletons are on display at the Zigong Dinosaur Museum in <a href="/wiki/Zigong" title="Zigong">Zigong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sichuan" title="Sichuan">Sichuan</a> Province, and the <a href="/wiki/Beijing_Museum_of_Natural_History" title="Beijing Museum of Natural History">Beijing Museum of Natural History</a>.</p>
<p>A second species, <i>S. ziliujingensis</i>, a name mentioned in the Zigong museum guide to indicate a smaller and older form, has never been formally described, and thus remains a <i><a href="/wiki/Nomen_nudum" title="Nomen nudum">nomen nudum</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shunosaurus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Shunosaurus.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="714" data-file-width="1000" height="157" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Shunosaurus.jpg/220px-Shunosaurus.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Shunosaurus.jpg/330px-Shunosaurus.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Shunosaurus.jpg/440px-Shunosaurus.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Shunosaurus.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Artist's impression</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><i>Shunosaurus</i> was first estimated to be 11 metres (36 ft) long; later and more complete finds indicated a somewhat smaller size. In 2010 <a href="/wiki/Gregory_S._Paul" title="Gregory S. Paul">Gregory S. Paul</a> estimated the length at 9.5 metres (31 ft), the weight at 3 metric tons (3.3 short tons).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <i>Shunosaurus</i> was very short-necked for a sauropod, being only "surpassed" in this respect by <i><a href="/wiki/Brachytrachelopan" title="Brachytrachelopan">Brachytrachelopan</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The skulls found are mostly compressed or disarticulated and the interpretation of the head form has varied from broad, short and deep<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> to extremely narrow and pointed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The upper and lower jaws were strongly curved upwards, allowing them to function as a pair of garden shears. The teeth were fairly robust but elongated with a crown length of up to 8 centimetres (3.1 in). They show a unique combination of a cylindrical body ending in a spatulate tip. In 1989 it was disclosed that the tail ended in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_(zoology)" title="Club (zoology)">club</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> equipped on its top with two successive spikes formed by 5 centimetres (2.0 in)-long cone-shaped <a href="/wiki/Osteoderm" title="Osteoderm">osteoderms</a> probably used to fend off enemies.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">Classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shunosaurus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Classification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Shunosaurus_lii.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1268" data-file-width="1054" height="205" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Shunosaurus_lii.jpg/170px-Shunosaurus_lii.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Shunosaurus_lii.jpg/255px-Shunosaurus_lii.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Shunosaurus_lii.jpg/340px-Shunosaurus_lii.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Shunosaurus_lii.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Skeleton, <a href="/wiki/Beijing_Museum_of_Natural_History" title="Beijing Museum of Natural History">Beijing Museum of Natural History</a></div>

<p><i>Shunosaurus</i> was originally classified as a member of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cetiosaurinae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cetiosaurinae (page does not exist)">Cetiosaurinae</a>; in 1992 Dong assigned it to <b>Shunosaurinae</b> within the <a href="/wiki/Cetiosauridae" title="Cetiosauridae">Cetiosauridae</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cladistic" title="Cladistic">Cladistic</a> analyses have rendered conflicting results. In 1995 <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Upchurch&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Paul Upchurch (page does not exist)">Paul Upchurch</a> published a study in which <i>Shunosaurus</i> belonged to the <a href="/wiki/Euhelopodidae" title="Euhelopodidae">Euhelopodidae</a> together with other Jurassic Chinese sauropods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> However, an analysis by <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_A._Wilson" title="Jeffrey A. Wilson">Jeffrey Wilson</a> in 2002 indicated it had a very basal position within the <a href="/wiki/Eusauropoda" title="Eusauropoda">Eusauropoda</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <i>Shunosaurus</i> is perhaps related to <i><a href="/wiki/Rhoetosaurus" title="Rhoetosaurus">Rhoetosaurus</a></i> from <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a> in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Paleobiology">Paleobiology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shunosaurus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Paleobiology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Shunosaurus_tooth.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3264" data-file-width="4896" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Shunosaurus_tooth.jpg/220px-Shunosaurus_tooth.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Shunosaurus_tooth.jpg/330px-Shunosaurus_tooth.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Shunosaurus_tooth.jpg/440px-Shunosaurus_tooth.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Shunosaurus_tooth.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Tooth</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Its neck length indicates that <i>Shunosaurus</i> was a low browser. The form of its jaws is well-adapted to processing large amounts of coarse plant material.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Shunosaurus</i> accounts for 90% of the fossils found in the Dashanpu fauna, showing it was a dominant member of its <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitat</a>. It shared the local Middle Jurassic landscape with other sauropods, <i><a href="/wiki/Datousaurus" title="Datousaurus">Datousaurus</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Omeisaurus" title="Omeisaurus">Omeisaurus</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Protognathosaurus" title="Protognathosaurus">Protognathosaurus</a></i>, the possible <a href="/wiki/Ornithopod" title="Ornithopod">ornithopod</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Xiaosaurus" title="Xiaosaurus">Xiaosaurus</a></i>, and the early <a href="/wiki/Stegosauria" title="Stegosauria">stegosaur</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Huayangosaurus" title="Huayangosaurus">Huayangosaurus</a></i>, as well as the carnivorous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theropod" title="Theropod">theropod</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Gasosaurus" title="Gasosaurus">Gasosaurus</a></i>.</p>
