<p><b>Maria da Graça Meneghel</b> (<small>Portuguese: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">[maˈɾia da ˈgɾasa ˈʃuʃa mẽneˈgɛw]</a></span>; born March 27, 1963), commonly known as <b>Xuxa</b> (<span class="nowrap"><small>English pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/ʃ/ 'sh' in 'shy'">ʃ</span><span title="/uː/ long 'oo' in 'food'">uː</span><span title="/ʃ/ 'sh' in 'shy'">ʃ</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>/</a></span></span> <span title="English pronunciation respelling"><a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i><b><span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">SHOO</span></span></b>-shə</i></a></span>; <small>Portuguese: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">[ˈʃuʃɐ]</a></span>), is a <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> <a href="/wiki/Television_presenter" title="Television presenter">television presenter</a>, film <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Businesswoman" title="Businesswoman">businesswoman</a>. Her various shows have been broadcast in <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>, English, and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> worldwide. She is a two-time winner of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_Grammy" title="Latin Grammy">Latin Grammy</a> award for Best Children's Album, and is also known for her <a href="/wiki/Epithet" title="Epithet">epithet</a>, "<b>Queen of the Shorties</b>".</p>
<p>Xuxa began her television carrer with the <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Clube_das_Crian%C3%A7as&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Clube das Crianças (page does not exist)">Clube das Crianças</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Rede_Manchete" title="Rede Manchete">Rede Manchete</a> in the early 1980s. She became a national superstar when she moved to <a href="/wiki/Rede_Globo" title="Rede Globo">Rede Globo</a> in 1986 for the <i><a href="/wiki/Xou_da_Xuxa" title="Xou da Xuxa">Xou da Xuxa</a></i>. She was the first Brazilian to appear on <a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes Magazine's</a> list of richest artists in 1991, taking 37th place with an annual gross income of US$19 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Over her 30-year career, Xuxa Meneghel has sold over 40 million copies of her records worldwide, which makes her the second-highest selling Brazilian female singer after <a href="/wiki/Rita_Lee" title="Rita Lee">Rita Lee</a>. Her net worth was estimated at US$100 million in the early 1990s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http:.2F.2Farticles.baltimoresun.com_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-http:.2F.2Farticles.baltimoresun.com-10">[10]</a></sup> As of 2015, she continues to be among Brazil's most prominent celebrities. Also successful as a businesswoman, she has the highest net worth of any Brazilian female entertainer, estimated at US$400 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http:.2F.2Fwww.cmjornal.xl.pt_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-http:.2F.2Fwww.cmjornal.xl.pt-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p>Maria da Graça Meneghel was born in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Rosa,_Rio_Grande_do_Sul" title="Santa Rosa, Rio Grande do Sul">Santa Rosa, Rio Grande do Sul</a>. During her birth, Meneghel's father, Luiz Floriano Meneghel, was told that both mother and child were at risk. He opted to save his wife, Alda Meneghel (<a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">née</a> Alda Flores da Rocha), and prayed to St. Mary of Graces, promising to name his daughter after the Virgin if all went well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although she was originally named after Saint Mary of Graces, Xuxa, the youngest member of the Meneghel family, received the nickname by which she came to be known from her brother, Bladimir. When their mother arrived home, she said to him: "Look at the baby that I bought to play with you." To this, he replied: "I know, it's my Xuxa."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The nickname stuck, though it was not until 1988 that she officially became <b>Maria da Graça Xuxa Meneghel.</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Xuxa is of <a href="/wiki/Italians" title="Italians">Italian</a> descent from the town of <a href="/wiki/Imer" title="Imer">Imer</a>, in the Autonomous <a href="/wiki/Trentino" title="Trentino">Province of Trento</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Her great-grandfather emigrated to <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> at the end of the <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a>. In 2013, Xuxa obtained Italian citizenship by descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Xuxa spent her early years in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Rosa,_California" title="Santa Rosa, California">Santa Rosa</a>. When she was seven, she and her family moved to <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a> where they lived in the <a href="/wiki/Bento_Ribeiro,_Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Bento Ribeiro, Rio de Janeiro">Bento Ribeiro</a> neighborhood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globo_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-globo-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>At 15 years, she was discovered by a modeling agency and began her professional career as a model at 16. During this time period Xuxa continued to model in Brazil and the United States for both fashion and men's magazines, such as Playboy, and began a famous love affair with Brazilian soccer star <a href="/wiki/Pel%C3%A9" title="Pelé">Pelé</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In 1984, she was hired as a model by <a href="/wiki/Ford_Models" title="Ford Models">Ford Models</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_career">Television career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xuxa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Television career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1983.E2.80.9386:_Rede_Manchete">1983–86: Rede Manchete</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xuxa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1983–86: Rede Manchete">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Established as national beauty and sex symbol, Xuxa got the chance to move toward a career in television though the offer to host a small regional children's program, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Clube_da_Crian%C3%A7a&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Clube da Criança (page does not exist)">Clube da Criança</a></i> for <a href="/wiki/Rede_Manchete" title="Rede Manchete">Rede Manchete</a>. In this period, she worked as a model during the week in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> and was taping her show during the weekend in Brazil. In 1986 this opportunity was expanded when she received an offer to host a national children's program through the multimedia conglomerate <a href="/wiki/Rede_Globo" title="Rede Globo">Globo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globo_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-globo-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1986.E2.80.9392:_Xou_da_Xuxa">1986–92: <i><a href="/wiki/Xou_da_Xuxa" title="Xou da Xuxa">Xou da Xuxa</a></i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xuxa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1986–92: Xou da Xuxa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since the Xou's debut in Brazil, Xuxa has enjoyed unprecedented following.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In Brazil alone, her program has achieved an estimated viewership of 33 million, the largest audience ever garnered by <a href="/wiki/Rede_Globo" title="Rede Globo">TV Globo</a>, the dominant television network in that country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Those kids who tuned in every weekday saw Xuxa arrived for each episode in a pink spaceship. The show had singing, dancing, skits, games, and jokes, and the actress would end the show with the famous "Xuxa kiss." For the kiss, Xuxa would put on bright lipstick and kiss the children participating in the show and children in the audience on the cheek, leaving the imprimit of her lipstick. The <i>Xou da Xuxa</i> stopped filming in 1992 but was followed by a program that only ran on Sunday called, simply, <i>Xuxa</i>. In all, 2 000 episode were recorded in Brazil and aired over six years and seven months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The immense popularity of the original <i>Xou da Xuxa</i> lead to a series of others children's television programs starring the actress. In 1991, a purely musical program called the <i>Paradão dos Baixinhos</i> (The Little Ones Charts) premiered and ran concurrently with the <i>Xou</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Spanish-language version of this show was produced in Argentina, and broadcast via satellite across <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. Since it was launched on <a href="/wiki/Univision" title="Univision">Univision</a> on August 9, 1991, <i><a href="/wiki/El_Show_de_Xuxa" title="El Show de Xuxa">El Show de Xuxa</a></i> became a phenomenal success, continuing the pattern it established in it place of origin-Brazil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1991.E2.80.9393:_International_career">1991–93: International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xuxa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 1991–93: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As early as in the 1980s, Xuxa had international recognition for her work on TV. In 1987, the French newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Lib%C3%A9ration" title="Libération">Libération</a></i> included Xuxa on the list of the most remarkable women in the world.</p>
<p>After reaching success with her record sales in Brazil, Xuxa released her first Spanish-language album, <i><a href="/wiki/Xuxa_1" title="Xuxa 1">Xuxa 1</a></i>, which performed well in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_American" title="Latin American">Latin American</a> market.</p>
<p>Xuxa widened her appeal among Spanish-speaking audiences when she recorded a program in <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, called <i><a href="/wiki/El_Show_de_Xuxa" title="El Show de Xuxa">El Show de Xuxa</a></i>. The <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> speculated that "more than 20 million Spanish- and Portuguese-speaking children watched <i>El Show de Xuxa</i> in 16 countries of Latin America every day, as well as <a href="/wiki/Univision" title="Univision">Univision</a> in the United States. The first two seasons of the show, the most popular, were produced by Argentine TV channel <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telef%C3%A9" title="Telefé">Telefé</a> while the third season, in 1993, was produced independently and then sold for broadcast to <a href="/wiki/El_Trece" title="El Trece">El Trece</a>. Her last Spanish speaking show aired in Latin America on December 31, 1993.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Times" title="New York Times">New York Times</a></i> highlighted her success in Brazil and Latin America in an article by correspondent, <a href="/wiki/James_Brooke_(journalist)" title="James Brooke (journalist)">James Brooke</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> The publication highlighted the record sales of albums of the singer, which in 1990 reached 12 million copies, and its success in the Hispanic market, where it reached 300,000 copies with their first album in Spanish. At the time, she was called by <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Magazine" title="New York Magazine">New York Magazine</a></i> as "Latin American Madonna".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> In 1992, the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> said that Xuxa was "probably better known to most Latin American pre-adolescents than Michael Jackson."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>Xuxa was recognized by <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> in 1991 as one of the world's 40 highest-paid entertainers. Her $19 million income accumulated between 1990 and 1991, and put her ahead of actor <a href="/wiki/Mel_Gibson" title="Mel Gibson">Mel Gibson</a>, cartoonist <a href="/wiki/Matt_Groening" title="Matt Groening">Matt Groening</a>, and rapper <a href="/wiki/Vanilla_Ice" title="Vanilla Ice">Vanilla Ice</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>Not only was she successful in <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">America</a>, but in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> as well. In 1992, taping programs in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, Xuxa was invited to hosted the program <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Xuxa_Park_(game_show)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Xuxa Park (game show) (page does not exist)">Xuxa Park</a></i>, in Spain. Released by <a href="/wiki/Telecinco" title="Telecinco">Telecinco</a> channel, the game show was shown on Sundays, with high ratings. The show lasted two years. The theme song of the show, <i>Sabor de la Vida</i>, had huge success in Spain, being among the 100 most played in the <a href="/wiki/European_Hot_100_Singles" title="European Hot 100 Singles">European Hot 100 Singles</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (May 2016)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> Her <i>Xuxa Park</i> album also sold well for 8 weeks and was certified gold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_magazine" title="Billboard magazine">Billboard magazine</a></i> published in September 1992, that the album <i><a href="/wiki/Xuxa_2" title="Xuxa 2">Xuxa 2</a></i> was at the top of the Spanish charts, and appeared in position 77 of the 100 most sold albums in the world. The biggest hits on this disc were: <i>Loquita Por Ti</i> (#29 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">billboard chart</a>), <i>Luna de Cristal</i> (#35 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">billboard chart</a>) and <i>Chindolele</i> (#10 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">billboard chart</a>). The album reached the fifth position in the U.S. <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_Top_Latin_Albums" title="Billboard Top Latin Albums">Billboard Top Latin Albums</a></i> of 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> In 1992, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People_Magazine" title="People Magazine">People Magazine</a> chose Xuxa as one of the 50 most beautiful people in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1993, Xuxa hosted an English language series in the United States titled <i><a href="/wiki/Xuxa_(TV_series)" title="Xuxa (TV series)">Xuxa</a></i>. However, it did not achieve the popularity she had enjoyed throughout Brazil, Latin America and Spain, and was cancelled after one season due to low ratings. It was initially broadcast by 124 stations across the country. The shows were produced on Sound Stage 36 at <a href="/wiki/CBS_Television_City" title="CBS Television City">CBS Television City</a> in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>. 65 episodes were taped for the first season of the show. Taping of the episodes was done in a 5-week period in the summer of 1993. The shows were broadcast Monday through Friday, generally in the early morning or mid-afternoon. All 65 episodes were broadcast during the initial 13 weeks before there was a repeat. Helping Xuxa on the show were the Pixies (three U.S. "Paquitas" plus one Brazilian Paquita), the Mellizas (uncredited), Jelly, Jam, and ten "child wranglers" for 150 kids on the set. Starting September 1994, Xuxa began airing on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Family_Channel_(United_States)" title="The Family Channel (United States)">The Family Channel</a> cable network, at 8:00am ET/PT. They reprised original episodes on a new children's block until February 19, 1996 when Xuxa stopped airing on The Family Channel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> The show was sold to a great number of countries throughout the world, including Japan, Israel, Russia, Australia, <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a> and some Arab countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> Her international ambitions apparently ended after the grueling taping schedule for her American show. She was hospitalized for several days due to exhaustion, and decided to give up her international career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the U.S., <a href="/wiki/Sony_Wonder" title="Sony Wonder">Sony Wonder</a> has released two of her videos and a record that includes English translations of some of her most successful songs in Portuguese and Spanish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1994.E2.80.932000:_Weekend_shows">1994–2000: Weekend shows</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xuxa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 1994–2000: Weekend shows">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1994, Xuxa returned to Brazilian TV with the debut of <i><a href="/wiki/Xuxa_Park" title="Xuxa Park">Xuxa Park</a></i>, which soon became a big hit with younger audiences, now displayed on Saturdays. The following year, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Xuxa_Hits&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Xuxa Hits (page does not exist)">Xuxa Hits</a></i> began to air on Sundays.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>Inspired by <i>Xuxa Hits</i>, the <i><a href="/wiki/Planeta_Xuxa" title="Planeta Xuxa">Planeta Xuxa</a></i> was created, which premiered on April 5, 1997, and became an immediate hit with those who had grown up following Xuxa's career. Initially, <i>Planeta Xuxa</i> was broadcast on Saturday afternoons and <i>Xuxa Park</i> held all the morning air time. <i>Planeta Xuxa</i> was moved to Sundays in 1998, and it stayed on the air until 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2001.E2.80.9304:_Xuxa_no_Mundo_da_Imagina.C3.A7.C3.A3o">2001–04: <i>Xuxa no Mundo da Imaginação</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xuxa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2001–04: Xuxa no Mundo da Imaginação">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 11, 2001, a <a href="/wiki/Xuxa_Park_fire" title="Xuxa Park fire">fire destroyed the entire set</a> of <i>Xuxa Park</i> program, leaving 26 people injured with burns. Xuxa was unharmed, but the program was removed from the Globo TV program schedule.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<p>In early 2002, the second and final edition of the <i>Planeta Verão</i> was shown. In the same year, <i>Planeta Xuxa</i> ended in July when Xuxa's partnership with director Marlene Mattos ended. In October, the television program <i>Xuxa no Mundo da Imaginação</i> premiered in Globo and marked a return to a daily schedule. Unlike other children's programs, this was aimed at children 0–10 years, with more educational content. Due to low ratings, but the program went off the air in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005.E2.80.9314:_TV_Xuxa">2005–14: <i>TV Xuxa</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xuxa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2005–14: TV Xuxa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The children's show changed its name and target age group in 2005. <i><a href="/wiki/TV_Xuxa" title="TV Xuxa">TV Xuxa</a></i>, which was still a daily morning show, included games, cartoons, contest, and musical numbers to amuse kids of all ages. In 2008, TV Xuxa was reformulated to appeal to a wider audience and adopted a weekly talk show format that aired on Saturday mornings. The program stopped showing cartoons, and Xuxa began to receive her guests on a stage designed for interviews and musical numbers. On this stage, Xuxa talked with singers <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a> (outside the studio, when he came to the show in Brazil), and <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Swift" title="Taylor Swift">Taylor Swift</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> In 2011, the show was moved to Saturday afternoons by <a href="/wiki/Rede_Globo" title="Rede Globo">Rede Globo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<p>The last edition of the program aired on January 25, 2014. Xuxa developed <a href="/wiki/Sesamoiditis" title="Sesamoiditis">sesamoiditis</a> and had to stop television work. Her problem might have been caused by over-use of high heels in dances and presentations. <a href="/wiki/Rede_Globo" title="Rede Globo">Rede Globo</a> issued a statement saying that the decision was made in agreement with Xuxa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> The last episode reached 12 rating points, according to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IBOPE" title="IBOPE">IBOPE</a> data. Xuxa renewed her employment contract with the network for another three years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.E2.80.93present">2015–present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xuxa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2015–present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After two years off the TV, Xuxa signed a contract with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rede_Record" title="Rede Record">Rede Record</a> on March 5, 2015, and announced that her program will be inspired by U.S. television personality, <a href="/wiki/Ellen_DeGeneres" title="Ellen DeGeneres">Ellen DeGeneres</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_in_music">Career in music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xuxa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Career in music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1986, the album <i>Xou da Xuxa</i>, sold over two million copies, breaking the South American record for sales, earning eight platinum awards (granted every 250,000 copies sold).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> In the following years, Xuxa launched six discs, including <i>Xou da Xuxa 2</i> and <i>Xou da Xuxa 3</i>, and recorded two LPs with the songs translated into Spanish, which sold 2.4 million copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 1989–96, Xuxa had sold 18 million albums, a record in Latin American music sales. Xuxa recorded about 915 songs, recorded 28 albums that together have sold over 45 million copies, and were awarded 400 gold records in Brazil. The album, <i><a href="/wiki/Xou_da_Xuxa_3" title="Xou da Xuxa 3">Xou da Xuxa 3</a>,</i> had more than 3,216,000 copies sold, making it the best-selling children's album, according to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guinness_Book" title="Guinness Book">Guinness Book</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2002, <a href="/wiki/Veja_(magazine)" title="Veja (magazine)">Veja magazine</a> named Xuxa the richest artist in Brazil, with an estimated net worth of $250 million. According to the same magazine, Xuxa's earnings were comparable to Hollywood stars like <a href="/wiki/Julia_Roberts" title="Julia Roberts">Julia Roberts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Keanu_Reeves" title="Keanu Reeves">Keanu Reeves</a>. She was first in the list of artists with highest sales over the past ten years (1998–2008).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Audiovisual" title="Audiovisual">music video</a>, <i>Xuxa só para Baixinhos</i> sold over eight million copies, and won five nominations and two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_Grammy" title="Latin Grammy">Latin Grammy</a> awards for "<a href="/wiki/Latin_Grammy_Award_for_Best_Latin_Children%27s_Album" title="Latin Grammy Award for Best Latin Children's Album">Best Children's Album</a>".</p>
<p>In 2012, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Associa%C3%A7%C3%A3o_Brasileira_dos_Produtores_de_Discos" title="Associação Brasileira dos Produtores de Discos">Associação Brasileira dos Produtores de Discos</a> (ABPF) released the list of the best-selling DVDs in the country. According to ABPD, Xuxa had two DVDs among the top ten in 2011, XSPB Volume 1–8 (sixth place) and XSPB 11 (ninth).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 1987, Xuxa became involved in a children's campaign against <a href="/wiki/Poliomyelitis" title="Poliomyelitis">polio</a>. Subsequently, more than 90% of Brazil's infant population was <a href="/wiki/Polio_vaccine" title="Polio vaccine">vaccinated</a>. Two years into the campaign, the disease was eliminated and Xuxa received a medal of honor from the president of Brazil, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Sarney" title="José Sarney">José Sarney</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p>
<p>Xuxa has also participated in campaigns against <a href="/wiki/Smoking" title="Smoking">smoking</a>, was a spokesperson for the campaign against <a href="/wiki/Breast_cancer" title="Breast cancer">breast cancer</a>, and was also the organizer of a public parade of protest in Rio, asking for peace in the city. The growing need to care for young children led her to set up the <a class="extiw" href="https://pt.wikipedia.org/wiki/Funda%C3%A7%C3%A3o_Xuxa_Meneghel" title="pt:Fundação Xuxa Meneghel">Xuxa Meneghel Foundation</a> on October 12, 1989.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup></p>
<p>Xuxa met with President <a href="/wiki/Luiz_In%C3%A1cio_Lula_da_Silva" title="Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva">Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva</a> in June 2007, to launch (in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brasilia" title="Brasilia">Brasilia</a>) a nationwide campaign called <i>Não Bata, Eduque!</i> (Don’t spank, Educate!).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup></p>
<p>Xuxa was chosen to represent Brazil on the <i><a href="/wiki/Live_Earth_(2007_concert)" title="Live Earth (2007 concert)">Live Earth</a></i> show and became the cause's spokeswoman in Brazil. The show took place in seven countries simultaneously on July 7, 2007, and emphasized the negative effects of <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup></p>

<p>In March 2008, Xuxa launched the campaign, <i>Uso Responsável da Internet</i> (Responsible Internet Use), at the Providencia ghetto in Rio de Janeiro. The initiative was a partnership with the Xuxa Meneghel Foundation. In the same year, she received a medal of honor at <i>ECO 2008</i> held in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brasilia" title="Brasilia">Brasilia</a>, for the social and environmental work carried out by the Xuxa Meneghel Foundation. She also took on the cause to fight against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prostitution_of_children" title="Prostitution of children">child prostitution</a>. That November, Xuxa received a prize from <a href="/wiki/Albert_II,_Prince_of_Monaco" title="Albert II, Prince of Monaco">Prince Albert of Monaco</a> for her work at the Xuxa Meneghel Foundation. This prize is the highest honor awarded to personalities for their work on social issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2010, Xuxa was invited by the president of <a class="extiw" href="https://pt.wikipedia.org/wiki/Servi%C3%A7o_Social_da_Ind%C3%BAstria" title="pt:Serviço Social da Indústria">SESI</a>, Jair Meneguelli, to be godmother to a nationwide campaign combating child prostitution called <i><a class="extiw" href="https://pt.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carinho_de_Verdade" title="pt:Carinho de Verdade">Carinho de Verdade</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2011, Colombian singer, Shakira, and Xuxa joined forces through their respective charitable foundations to aid children younger than six years old who live in Brazil’s poorest communities. The two artists, together with Brazilian government officials, signed an accord in Rio de Janeiro for a program of cooperation. In its first four years, it plans to provide better access to education to children from 100 schools in Brazil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup></p>
<p>Xuxa is the godmother and poster girl of the campaign "Tri-national to Combat Sexual Exploitation of Children and Adolescents" which aims to encourage people to report cases of exploitation and abuse of minors living on the border between <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>. Since May 2013, the campaign has integrated the actions of several groups: the <a href="/wiki/Itaipu_Dam" title="Itaipu Dam">Itaipu Dam</a>, the <a href="/wiki/International_Labour_Organization" title="International Labour Organization">International Labour Organization</a> (ILO), the Municipal Tourism Council, Childhood and Youth, <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Labor_and_Employment_(Brazil)" title="Ministry of Labor and Employment (Brazil)">Ministry of Labor and Employment</a>, and entities of Paraguay and Argentina who work in the same area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2014, President <a href="/wiki/Dilma_Rousseff" title="Dilma Rousseff">Dilma Rousseff</a> signed a law that applied stronger penalties to the crime of sexual exploitation of children and adolescents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup> On June 4, 2014, Xuxa, was present when the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Senate_of_Brazil" title="Senate of Brazil">Senate of Brazil</a> passed the <i>Lei da Palmada</i> (PLC 58/2014), which had been adopted by the Commission on Human Rights of the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_(Brazil)" title="Chamber of Deputies (Brazil)">Chamber of Deputies</a>. When asked about criticism from some parents about how they will educate their children after the passage of this law, Xuxa said that they can educate, but without violence: "We have to show that people can and should educate without violence". Xuxa said that the project should be treated as <i>Lei Menino Bernardo</i>, not <i>Lei da Palmada</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup><span class="cleanup-needed-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">Lei Menino Bernardo, Lei da Palmada</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="These terms need to be translated to English. (May 2016)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xuxa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Xuxa has one daughter, <a href="/wiki/Sasha_Meneghel" title="Sasha Meneghel">Sasha</a>, who was born in 1998. Sasha's father is actor <a href="/wiki/Luciano_Szafir" title="Luciano Szafir">Luciano Szafir</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-89"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup> Xuxa dated Brazilian soccer legend, <a href="/wiki/Pel%C3%A9" title="Pelé">Pelé</a>, in the 1980s, and later, Formula 1 legend, <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-92"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-93"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup></p>
<p>She reported in 2012, that reclusive singer <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a> had once courted her, inviting her to dinner at his <a href="/wiki/Neverland_Ranch" title="Neverland Ranch">Neverland estate</a> and then had a subordinate ask whether she would consider living with Jackson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-95"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup> She recently began a relationship with actor and singer, Junno Andrade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-96"><a href="#cite_note-96">[96]</a></sup></p>
<p>Xuxa's mother, Dona Alda Meneghel, suffers from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parkinson%27s_Disease" title="Parkinson's Disease">Parkinson's Disease</a> and is in an advanced stage of the illness. She underwent several surgeries in an attempt to slow down or reverse its progress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-97"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup></p>
