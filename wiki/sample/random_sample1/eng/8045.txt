<p><b>Army Detachment Steiner</b> (<i>Armeeabteilung Steiner</i>), was a temporary military unit, something more than a corps but less than an army, created on paper by German dictator <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a> on 21 April 1945 during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Berlin" title="Battle of Berlin">Battle of Berlin</a>, and placed under the command of SS-<i><a href="/wiki/Obergruppenf%C3%BChrer" title="Obergruppenführer">Obergruppenführer</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Felix_Steiner" title="Felix Steiner">Felix Steiner</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Army_Detachment_Steiner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hitler hoped that the units assigned to Steiner would be able to stage an effective counterattack against the northern pincer of the Soviet assault on Berlin. In the event, Steiner realised that the forces under his command were inadequate, and refused to attack.</p>
<p>On the second day of the Battle of Berlin, 17 April, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Generaloberst" title="Generaloberst">Generaloberst</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Gotthard_Heinrici" title="Gotthard Heinrici">Gotthard Heinrici</a>, the Commander-in-Chief of <a href="/wiki/Army_Group_Vistula" title="Army Group Vistula">Army Group Vistula</a>, stripped Steiner's <a href="/wiki/III_(Germanic)_SS_Panzer_Corps" title="III (Germanic) SS Panzer Corps">III (Germanic) SS Panzer Corps</a>, (the Army Group's reserve), of its two strongest divisions, the <a href="/wiki/11th_SS_Volunteer_Panzergrenadier_Division_Nordland" title="11th SS Volunteer Panzergrenadier Division Nordland">SS <i>Nordland</i> Division</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/4th_SS_Volunteer_Panzergrenadier_Brigade_Netherlands" title="4th SS Volunteer Panzergrenadier Brigade Netherlands">SS <i>Nederland</i> Division</a>. He placed them under the command of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_der_Infanterie" title="General der Infanterie">General der Infanterie</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Theodor_Busse" title="Theodor Busse">Theodor Busse</a>, commander of the <a href="/wiki/9th_Army_(Wehrmacht)" title="9th Army (Wehrmacht)">Ninth Army</a>, as Busse had most of the other units in the III Corps. The <i>Nordland</i> was sent to join <a href="/wiki/Helmuth_Weidling" title="Helmuth Weidling">Helmuth Weidling's</a> <a href="/wiki/LVI_Panzer_Corps" title="LVI Panzer Corps">LVI Panzer Corps</a> defending the <a href="/wiki/Seelow_Heights" title="Seelow Heights">Seelow Heights</a>, to stiffen the sector held by the <a href="/wiki/9th_Parachute_Division_(Germany)" title="9th Parachute Division (Germany)">9th Parachute Division</a>. The <i>Nederland</i> Division was sent south-west of <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt_(Oder)" title="Frankfurt (Oder)">Frankfurt (Oder)</a> and assigned to the <a href="/wiki/V_SS_Mountain_Corps" title="V SS Mountain Corps">V SS Mountain Corps</a>, where it was destined to be destroyed in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Halbe" title="Battle of Halbe">Battle of Halbe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Heinrici ordered the III SS Panzer Corps, reduced to three battalions and a few tanks, to scrape together whatever forces it could find to set up a screening line along the <a href="/wiki/Finow_Canal" title="Finow Canal">Finow Canal</a> to protect the southern flank of the <a href="/wiki/3rd_Panzer_Army" title="3rd Panzer Army">Third Panzer Army</a> from an attack by the Soviet Marshal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Georgi_Zhukov" title="Georgi Zhukov">Zhukov's</a> <a href="/wiki/1st_Belorussian_Front" title="1st Belorussian Front">1st Belorussian Front</a>, which had broken through the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seelow_Heights" title="Battle of the Seelow Heights">Seelow Heights' defences</a> and was encircling Berlin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beevor-267.E2.80.93268_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Beevor-267.E2.80.93268-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ziemke-87.E2.80.9388_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ziemke-87.E2.80.9388-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>By 21 April <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a>, ignoring the facts, started to call the ragtag units that came under Steiner's command "Army Detachment Steiner". He ordered Steiner to attack the northern flank of the huge <a href="/wiki/Salients,_re-entrants_and_pockets" title="Salients, re-entrants and pockets">salient</a> created by the 1st Belorussian Front's breakout. Simultaneously the Ninth Army, which had been pushed south of the salient, was to attack north in a pincer attack. To facilitate this attack Steiner was assigned the three divisions of the Ninth Army's <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=CI_Army_Corps&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="CI Army Corps (page does not exist)">CI Army Corps</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/4th_SS_Polizei_Division" title="4th SS Polizei Division">4th SS Polizei Division</a>, the <a href="/wiki/5th_J%C3%A4ger_Division_(Wehrmacht)" title="5th Jäger Division (Wehrmacht)">5th Jäger Division</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/25th_Panzergrenadier_Division" title="25th Panzergrenadier Division">25th Panzergrenadier Division</a> — all were north of the Finow Canal<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[note 1]</a></sup> — and Weidling's LVI Panzer Corps, which was still east of Berlin with its northern flank just below <a href="/wiki/Werneuchen" title="Werneuchen">Werneuchen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beevor-267.E2.80.93268_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Beevor-267.E2.80.93268-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ziemke-87.E2.80.9388_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ziemke-87.E2.80.9388-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The three divisions to the north were to attack south from <a href="/wiki/Eberswalde" title="Eberswalde">Eberswalde</a> (on the Finow Canal and 24 km (15 miles) east of Berlin) towards the LVI Panzer Corps, so cutting the 1st Belorussian Front's salient in two. Steiner called Heinrici and informed him that the plan could not be implemented because the 5th Jäger Division and the 25th Panzergrenadier Division were deployed defensively and could not be redeployed until the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2nd_Naval_Infantry_Division_(Germany)" title="2nd Naval Infantry Division (Germany)">2nd Naval Division</a> arrived from the coast to relieve them. This left only two battalions of the 4th SS Police Division available, and they had no combat weapons. Heinrici called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hans_Krebs_(general)" title="Hans Krebs (general)">Hans Krebs</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chief_of_staff_(military)" title="Chief of staff (military)">Chief</a> <a href="/wiki/German_General_Staff" title="German General Staff">German General Staff</a> of (<a href="/wiki/Oberkommando_des_Heeres" title="Oberkommando des Heeres">OKH</a>), told him that the plan could not be implemented and asked to speak to Hitler, but was told Hitler was too busy to take his call.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beevor-267.E2.80.93268_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Beevor-267.E2.80.93268-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ziemke-87.E2.80.9388_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ziemke-87.E2.80.9388-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>When, on 22 April, at his afternoon conference Hitler became convinced that Steiner was not going to attack he fell into a tearful rage against his generals. He declared that the war was lost, he blamed the generals and announced that he would stay on in Berlin until the end and then kill himself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beevor-275_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Beevor-275-5">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ziemke-89_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ziemke-89-6">[5]</a></sup> After 22 April "Army Detachment Steiner" was little mentioned in the <i><a href="/wiki/F%C3%BChrerbunker" title="Führerbunker">Führerbunker</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beevor-298_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Beevor-298-7">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Army_Detachment_Steiner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hitler's rage at Steiner's failure to mount the attack was made famous by its depiction in a scene from the <a href="/wiki/2004_in_film" title="2004 in film">2004 movie</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Downfall_(2004_film)" title="Downfall (2004 film)">Downfall</a></i>. The scene has been made into numerous <a href="/wiki/Downfall_(2004_film)#Parodies" title="Downfall (2004 film)">parody videos</a> in which new subtitles are added that typically have no correspondence to what the characters are actually saying in <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Army_Detachment_Steiner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eleventh_SS_Panzer_Army" title="Eleventh SS Panzer Army">Eleventh SS Panzer Army</a>, was part of <a href="/wiki/Army_Group_Vistula" title="Army Group Vistula">Army Group Vistula</a> and was under the command of Steiner while it was fighting east of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oder_River" title="Oder River">Oder River</a> during February 1945. The Eleventh was assigned to <a href="/wiki/OB_West" title="OB West">OB West</a> and reorganized (and given the command of new units) for combat against the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Allies" title="Western Allies">Western Allies</a> in March 1945. Steiner remained in command of the III (Germanic) SS Panzer Corps and it remained in Army Group Vistula, transferring from the Eleventh to <a href="/wiki/3rd_Panzer_Army" title="3rd Panzer Army">Third Panzer Army</a>. As Steiner also commanded the Eleventh army during 1945, it can easily be confused with Army Detachment Steiner.</li>
</ul>

For example in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Bulge" title="Battle of the Bulge">Battle of the Bulge</a> General <a href="/wiki/Dwight_D._Eisenhower" title="Dwight D. Eisenhower">Eisenhower</a> reassigned the <a href="/wiki/First_United_States_Army" title="First United States Army">US First</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ninth_United_States_Army" title="Ninth United States Army">US Ninth</a> armies on the north side of the salient to the <a href="/wiki/21st_Army_Group" title="21st Army Group">21st Army Group</a> (also on the north side), because it was now fighting side by side with the armies of the 21st Army Group, while its parent army group (the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/12th_Army_Group" title="12th Army Group">12th</a>) was on the south side of the salient with the rest of the 12th's armies.</span></li>
</ol>
