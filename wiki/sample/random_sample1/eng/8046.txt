<p><b>Stefan Konrad Raab</b> (born 20 October 1966) is a German entertainer, comedian, musician and former television host. Raab began his television career hosting the comedy show <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vivasion&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vivasion (page does not exist)">Vivasion</a></i> in 1993. He became well known in 1994 after composing a hit single spoofing national football coach <a href="/wiki/Berti_Vogts" title="Berti Vogts">Berti Vogts</a>. From 1999 to 2015, he hosted the <a href="/wiki/Late-night_talk_show" title="Late-night talk show">late-night</a> comedy show <i><a href="/wiki/TV_total" title="TV total">TV total</a></i> and has also created a number of other television shows, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Schlag_den_Raab" title="Schlag den Raab">Schlag den Raab</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Bundesvision_Song_Contest" title="Bundesvision Song Contest">Bundesvision Song Contest</a></i>. Raab has been considered the "most powerful man in German entertainment television".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spiegel_2011_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spiegel_2011-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Raab is also known for his recurring role as producer, writer and performer of German entries to the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Eurovision Song Contest">Eurovision Song Contest</a>. He was the initiator of the national pre-selection show <a href="/wiki/Germany_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2010" title="Germany in the Eurovision Song Contest 2010">Unser Star für Oslo</a> (Our Star for Oslo), in which <a href="/wiki/Satellite_(Lena_Meyer-Landrut_song)" title="Satellite (Lena Meyer-Landrut song)">Germany's winning entry</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2010" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2010">2010 contest</a> in Oslo was determined.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_family">Early life and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefan_Raab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Raab grew up in <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a> with his sister and his parents, who owned a butchers shop. He attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jesuit" title="Jesuit">Jesuit</a> boarding school <a href="/wiki/Aloisiuskolleg" title="Aloisiuskolleg">Aloisiuskolleg</a> in Bonn. Before entering the entertainment business, he worked as a butcher in his parents' shop and studied <a href="/wiki/Law_school" title="Law school">law</a> before dropping-out of university after five semesters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stefan_Raab_biography_at_laut.de_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stefan_Raab_biography_at_laut.de-2">[2]</a></sup> Raab lives in a suburb of Cologne and has two daughters (born 2004 and 2006) with his girlfriend Nike.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Welt-Vater_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Welt-Vater-3">[3]</a></sup> Other than this, very little is known about Raab, who deliberately hides his private life from the media.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefan_Raab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefan_Raab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Stefan Raab became popular as host of the comedy show <i>Vivasion</i> for the German <a href="/wiki/Music_television" title="Music television">music television</a> channel <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viva_(TV_station)" title="Viva (TV station)">VIVA</a> from 1993 to 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zeit_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-zeit-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1999, he created <i>TV total</i> which began airing in April 2001 on <a href="/wiki/ProSieben" title="ProSieben">ProSieben</a>, four times a week. While <i>TV total</i> started as a comedy format mainly showing and satirizing funny and embarrassing sequences from other television programs, it soon came to be more of a late night show featuring musical performances-- in some ways similar to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_with_Jay_Leno" title="The Tonight Show with Jay Leno">The Tonight Show with Jay Leno</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_with_Conan_O%27Brien" title="Late Night with Conan O'Brien">Late Night with Conan O'Brien</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zeit_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-zeit-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stefan_Raab_biography_at_tvtotal.de_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stefan_Raab_biography_at_tvtotal.de-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>After a <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">boxing</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Regina_Halmich" title="Regina Halmich">Regina Halmich</a> in 2001 (rematch 2007) and a <a href="/wiki/Speed_skating" title="Speed skating">speed skating</a> race against <a href="/wiki/Claudia_Pechstein" title="Claudia Pechstein">Claudia Pechstein</a> in 2002, he also created several other celebrity sports events that are produced regularly, including <i>TV total Turmspringen</i> (<a href="/wiki/High_diving" title="High diving">high diving</a>) and <i>TV total Stock Car Crash Challenge</i> (<a href="/wiki/Stock_car_racing" title="Stock car racing">stock car racing</a>). He also brought fun and variety to the show by invented new sports: In November 2003, he initiated the first "official" World <a href="/wiki/Wok_racing" title="Wok racing">Wok racing</a> Championship in <a href="/wiki/Winterberg" title="Winterberg">Winterberg</a>, Germany. Modified Chinese woks are used to make timed runs down an Olympic bobsled track. The championship has taken place every year since, most recently in March 2011 in <a href="/wiki/Innsbruck" title="Innsbruck">Innsbruck</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stefan_Raab_biography_at_tvtotal.de_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stefan_Raab_biography_at_tvtotal.de-5">[5]</a></sup> The first <i>Autoball EM</i>, autoball being a version of football played in cars and using an <a href="/wiki/Exercise_ball" title="Exercise ball">exercise ball</a> to score goals was made in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stefan_Raab_biography_at_mtv.de_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stefan_Raab_biography_at_mtv.de-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006, Raab invented the game show <i><a href="/wiki/Schlag_den_Raab" title="Schlag den Raab">Schlag den Raab</a></i> (German for <i>Beat the Raab</i>, remade for British television as <a href="/wiki/Beat_the_Star" title="Beat the Star">Beat the Star</a> and as "Beat your <i>host</i>" in several other European countries), in which he competes against a contestant in various disciplines – some episodes of the show lasted more than five hours with excellent quotas.</p>
<p>Raab frequently organizes <i>PokerStars.de Nacht</i>, a poker event featuring some celebrity names in German entertainment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stefan_Raab_biography_at_tvtotal.de_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Stefan_Raab_biography_at_tvtotal.de-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2012, he announced that he will be hosting a new talk show on <a href="/wiki/ProSieben" title="ProSieben">ProSieben</a> which will include political guests and begin airing on 11 November 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> It will stand in direct competition with the self-titled talk show hosted by <a href="/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Jauch" title="Günther Jauch">Günther Jauch</a>.</p>
<p>In September 2013 Raab was one of four hosts (one from each big television network) at the federal election debate between chancellor <a href="/wiki/Angela_Merkel" title="Angela Merkel">Angela Merkel</a> and her challenger <a href="/wiki/Peer_Steinbr%C3%BCck" title="Peer Steinbrück">Peer Steinbrück</a>.</p>
<p>In mid-2015, Raab announced his retirement from television and stated that he would remain hosting until the end of the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The last episode of his show <i>TV total</i> aired on December 16 and the last episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Schlag_den_Raab" title="Schlag den Raab">Schlag den Raab</a></i> aired on December 19, 2015.</p>

<p>Raab began working as a freelance producer and composer of jingles and radio commercials in 1990 at his own studio in Cologne.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stefan_Raab_biography_at_laut.de_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stefan_Raab_biography_at_laut.de-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 1994 onwards, he produced a number of popular songs, including "Böörti Böörti Vogts" (a song about <a href="/wiki/Berti_Vogts" title="Berti Vogts">Berti Vogts</a>), "Hier kommt die Maus" ("Here comes the mouse", a tribute to the children's television series <a href="/wiki/Die_Sendung_mit_der_Maus" title="Die Sendung mit der Maus">Die Sendung mit der Maus</a>), "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maschendrahtzaun" title="Maschendrahtzaun">Maschendrahtzaun</a>", "Wir kiffen", "Gebt das Hanf frei!" ("Legalise Dope!", featuring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shaggy_(artist)" title="Shaggy (artist)">Shaggy</a> and samples from German politician <a href="/wiki/Hans-Christian_Str%C3%B6bele" title="Hans-Christian Ströbele">Hans-Christian Ströbele</a>), "Hol' mir ma' 'ne Flasche Bier" ("Get me a bottle o' beer", containing samples from then-Chancellor <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Schr%C3%B6der" title="Gerhard Schröder">Gerhard Schröder</a>), and "Space Taxi" from the soundtrack of the movie <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Traumschiff_Surprise_-_Periode_1" title="Traumschiff Surprise - Periode 1">Traumschiff Surprise - Periode 1</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stefan_Raab_biography_at_mtv.de_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stefan_Raab_biography_at_mtv.de-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>He wrote the song for the German entry to the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_1998" title="Eurovision Song Contest 1998">Eurovision Song Contest 1998</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guildo_Horn" title="Guildo Horn">Guildo Horn</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Guildo_hat_euch_lieb!" title="Guildo hat euch lieb!">Guildo hat euch lieb!</a>",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stefan_Raab_biography_at_tvtotal.de_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-Stefan_Raab_biography_at_tvtotal.de-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Germany_country_profile_at_eurovision.tv_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Germany_country_profile_at_eurovision.tv-9">[9]</a></sup> and in the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2000" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2000">Eurovision Song Contest 2000</a> he took part himself, performing the <a href="/wiki/Nonsense" title="Nonsense">nonsense</a> song "<a href="/wiki/Wadde_hadde_dudde_da%3F" title="Wadde hadde dudde da?">Wadde hadde dudde da?</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Germany_country_profile_at_eurovision.tv_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Germany_country_profile_at_eurovision.tv-9">[9]</a></sup> Raab cast the German entry for the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2004" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2004">2004 Eurovision Song Contest</a> in a talent show contest called <i>SSDSGPS</i> ("Stefan sucht den Super-Grand-Prix-Star", "Stefan seeks the super Grand-Prix star", satirising the title of the German <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Idol_series" title="Idol series">Idol series</a> <a href="/wiki/Deutschland_sucht_den_Superstar" title="Deutschland sucht den Superstar">Deutschland sucht den Superstar</a>, <i>DSDS</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The winner, <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_Mutzke" title="Maximilian Mutzke">Maximilian Mutzke</a>, came in eighth place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Germany_country_profile_at_eurovision.tv_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-Germany_country_profile_at_eurovision.tv-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bored with the Eurovision Song Contest, Raab came up with the <a href="/wiki/Bundesvision_Song_Contest" title="Bundesvision Song Contest">Bundesvision Song Contest</a> in 2005 (<i>Bundes</i>republik Deutschland = Federal Republic of Germany). The contest features representatives from each of the 16 <a href="/wiki/States_of_Germany" title="States of Germany">states of Germany</a> and stipulates that their song has to be at least partly in German.</p>
<p>In 2009, Raab was approached by the public broadcaster <a href="/wiki/Norddeutscher_Rundfunk" title="Norddeutscher Rundfunk">NDR</a>, a member of the <a href="/wiki/ARD_(broadcaster)" title="ARD (broadcaster)">ARD</a> broadcasting consortium, to jointly organise a national preselection in order to determine the German entry for the Eurovision Song Contest 2010. It was revealed that Raab initially refused the request, but that his television network ProSieben accepted the offer to work with ARD/NDR.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> As a result of the cooperation, the talent show contest Unser Star für Oslo (<i>Our Star for Oslo</i>) took place from 2 February 2010 onwards, stretching across 8 shows. Raab took a lead role in the programmes as head of the jury. In the national final on 12 March 2010, <a href="/wiki/Lena_Meyer-Landrut" title="Lena Meyer-Landrut">Lena Meyer-Landrut</a> emerged as winner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On 29 May 2010, Meyer-Landrut won the Eurovision Song Contest 2010 with the song "<a href="/wiki/Satellite_(Lena_Meyer-Landrut_song)" title="Satellite (Lena Meyer-Landrut song)">Satellite</a>", claiming the first German Eurovision victory in 28 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lena_wins_Eurovision_Eurovision.tv_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lena_wins_Eurovision_Eurovision.tv-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a musician, Raab is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autodidact" title="Autodidact">autodidact</a>, playing various instruments, such as piano, drums, guitar, <a href="/wiki/Ukulele" title="Ukulele">ukulele</a>, and some wind instruments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zeit_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-zeit-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>On 19 May 2011 Raab eventually ended his Eurovision involvement as a host, chairman of the jury, composer and musical producer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefan_Raab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For his television show <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vivasion&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vivasion (page does not exist)">Vivasion</a> Raab received the <i>Goldener Löwe</i> (Golden Lion), the predecessor to <i><a href="/wiki/Deutscher_Fernsehpreis" title="Deutscher Fernsehpreis">Deutscher Fernsehpreis</a></i> (German Television Award), in 1996. For <i><a href="/wiki/TV_total" title="TV total">TV total</a></i> he received the <i>Deutscher Fernsehpreis</i> in 1999 as well as the comedy award <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rose_d%27or" title="Rose d'or">Rose d'or</a></i> in 2001.</p>
<p>For his talent show <i>SSDSGPS</i> he was awarded the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adolf-Grimme-Preis" title="Adolf-Grimme-Preis">Adolf-Grimme-Preis</a></i> in 2005 in for the "discovery and support of young music talents". On 29 May 2005, Stefan Raab received the <i>Deutsch-Türkischer Freundschaftspreis</i> (German-Turkish Friendship Award) for his TV total specials from <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a> in preparation for the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2004" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2004">Eurovision Song Contest 2004</a>. He won the <i><a href="/wiki/Bravo_Otto" title="Bravo Otto">Bravo Otto</a></i> in the category "Comedystar" from 2000 to 2003. In 2000 and 2005 Raab was awarded the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Echo_(music_award)" title="Echo (music award)">ECHO</a></i> (German music award) as "Best National Producer". In 2005 he also received the ECHO award as "media partner of the year").</p>
<p>On 29 September 2007, Raab received the <i>Deutscher Fernsehpreis</i> for "best entertainment show" for the fifth episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Schlag_den_Raab" title="Schlag den Raab">Schlag den Raab</a></i>. On 6 February 2008 the show was awarded the <i><a href="/wiki/Goldene_Kamera" title="Goldene Kamera">Goldene Kamera</a></i> (Golden Camera). Furthermore, Raab received the media award <i>Bambi</i> on 27 November 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In 2009 Raab won the <i>Herbert Award 2009</i> for <i>best television sports show</i> with the <i>TV total Turmspringen</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Madame_Tussauds" title="Madame Tussauds">Madame Tussauds</a> in Berlin displays a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wax_figure" title="Wax figure">wax figure</a> of Stefan Raab since April 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>As the initiator and president of the jury of the show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unser_Star_f%C3%BCr_Oslo" title="Unser Star für Oslo">Unser Star für Oslo</a></i> he was awarded the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bayerischer_Fernsehpreis" title="Bayerischer Fernsehpreis">Bayerischer Fernsehpreis</a></i> (Bavarian television award) in 2010.</p>
<p>On 12 November 2011, Stefan Raab came second in Synchronspringen teaming with Elton in Munich at the TV Total Turmspringen 2011 event. This was his first ever podium along with Elton at this event.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefan_Raab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Raab has been the subject of <a href="/wiki/Lawsuit" title="Lawsuit">lawsuits</a> throughout his career, some of which have gained public attention. The two most referenced cases are:</p>
<ul>
<li>The case of then 16-year-old model Lisa Loch, who was ridiculed during some shows through Raab's assertion that her name ("Loch" meaning "hole" in German) made her more likely to find a job as an actress in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pornographic_movie" title="Pornographic movie">pornographic movies</a>. Loch claimed she received anonymous obscene telephone calls, was mocked by classmates and in public and as a result suffered sleeplessness and was afraid to leave her home. A court in <a href="/wiki/Hamm" title="Hamm">Hamm</a> ordered Raab to pay €70,000 in compensation due to infringement of personal rights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></li>
<li>In an episode broadcast on 6 September 2004, Raab showed a clip, originally destined for a news broadcast, of a 28-year-old <a href="/wiki/Turkish_people" title="Turkish people">Turkish</a> mother Nil S. holding a <a href="/wiki/Schult%C3%BCte" title="Schultüte">Schultüte</a> (a cardboard cone, typically full of sweets, traditionally given to children in Germany on their first school day). Raab's comment, "Unbelievable! The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drug_trafficker" title="Drug trafficker">drug traffickers</a> are disguising themselves better and better" ("eine Tüte" also means "a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marijuana_joint" title="Marijuana joint">marijuana joint</a>" in German slang, hence the pun), resulted in repeated lawsuits, and after a <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a> court ruled against Raab, Nil S. settled for €20,000 compensation accompanied by a written apology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
