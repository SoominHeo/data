<p><i><b>Super Mario 3D World</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">スーパーマリオ3Dワールド</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">Hepburn</a>: <i>Sūpā Mario Surī Dī Wārudo</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the sixth original 3D platform game in the <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Mario" title="Super Mario">Super Mario</a></i> series and the sequel to the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_3DS" title="Nintendo 3DS">Nintendo 3DS</a> game <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Mario_3D_Land" title="Super Mario 3D Land">Super Mario 3D Land</a></i>, developed and published by <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">Nintendo</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Wii_U" title="Wii U">Wii U</a> video game console, and was released worldwide in November 2013. The game follows <a href="/wiki/Mario" title="Mario">Mario</a> and friends attempting to rescue fairy-like creatures called Sprixies from Bowser. It received critical acclaim, with its level design, multiplayer, visuals and music all receiving high praise.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Mario_3D_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Super_mario_3d_world_screenshot.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="288" data-file-width="512" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/ce/Super_mario_3d_world_screenshot.png/220px-Super_mario_3d_world_screenshot.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/ce/Super_mario_3d_world_screenshot.png/330px-Super_mario_3d_world_screenshot.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/ce/Super_mario_3d_world_screenshot.png/440px-Super_mario_3d_world_screenshot.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Super_mario_3d_world_screenshot.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/E3_2013" title="E3 2013">E3 2013</a> <a href="/wiki/Screenshot" title="Screenshot">screenshot</a> of <i>Super Mario 3D World</i> depicting <a href="/wiki/Mario" title="Mario">Mario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luigi" title="Luigi">Luigi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Princess_Peach" title="Princess Peach">Princess Peach</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toad_(Nintendo)" title="Toad (Nintendo)">Toad</a>, who are controlled by players, running through one of the game's <a href="/wiki/Level_(video_gaming)" title="Level (video gaming)">levels</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The game follows a similar play style to <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Mario_3D_Land" title="Super Mario 3D Land">Super Mario 3D Land</a></i>, which combines the free-roaming gameplay of 3D <i>Mario</i> titles with the mechanics of 2D side-scrolling platforming games in the series, going so far as to include a flagpole and timer on every level.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Four players control every <a href="/wiki/Player_character" title="Player character">playable characters</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Mario" title="Mario">Mario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luigi" title="Luigi">Luigi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Princess_Peach" title="Princess Peach">Princess Peach</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toad_(Nintendo)" title="Toad (Nintendo)">Toad</a>. Additionally, <a href="/wiki/Rosalina_(character)" title="Rosalina (character)">Rosalina</a> is a hidden, unlockable fifth playable character. Similar to their appearances in <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Mario_Bros._2" title="Super Mario Bros. 2">Super Mario Bros. 2</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> each of the characters possesses unique abilities and controls differently: Mario has balanced running speed and jump height, Luigi jumps higher and falls slower, but has lower traction; Peach can jump and shortly float through the air, but runs slowly, Toad runs the fastest, but cannot jump as high and falls faster; and Rosalina can use the spin attack move as seen in the <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Mario_Galaxy" title="Super Mario Galaxy">Super Mario Galaxy</a></i> titles, but has the slowest running speed. The player can select any of the five characters to use before beginning a level, and up to four players may explore the same level simultaneously with each character, sharing from a pool of <a href="/wiki/1-up" title="1-up">extra lives</a>. Like <i><a href="/wiki/New_Super_Mario_Bros._Wii" title="New Super Mario Bros. Wii">New Super Mario Bros. Wii</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/New_Super_Mario_Bros._U" title="New Super Mario Bros. U">New Super Mario Bros. U</a></i>, players are able to pick up and carry each other to help them across certain areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Levels are accessed via a map overworld, which players can freely explore to find hidden secrets or access bonus areas to earn more items or coins. Each level contains three hidden Green Stars which are required to access certain levels, and each main level also contains a hidden Stamp which can be used in handwritten posts to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miiverse" title="Miiverse">Miiverse</a> within the game's community. Players can also view messages left by other players, both on the map overworld and after clearing a level, and can download Mii Ghosts to appear in levels and they sometimes carry each gift boxes containing either coins or power-ups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Additional Green Stars can be earned in Captain Toad levels, where players control Captain Toad, who cannot jump, as he navigates puzzle-like stages using the GamePad's functionality, and Mystery Houses, where players must clear a series of challenges with short time limits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-modes_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-modes-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Along with several returning items from previous games, such as the Super Leaf, Mega Mushroom, Propeller Box and <a href="/wiki/Boomerang" title="Boomerang">Boomerang</a> Flower, several new items are introduced. The Super Bell gives players a Cat Suit, allowing them to run faster, perform unique attacks and climb up walls to reach new areas. A variation of the Super Bell, the Lucky Bell, allows the player to temporarily transform their character into a <a href="/wiki/Maneki-neko" title="Maneki-neko">lucky cat statue</a> that awards coins under certain conditions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Cherry" title="Cherry">Double Cherries</a> make a <a href="/wiki/Cloning" title="Cloning">clone</a> of the player, allowing for more effective attacks. The more Double Cherries the player grabs, the more clones that appear. Players can also wear Cannon Boxes, which fire shots at enemies, Light Blocks, which can defeat ghosts and <a href="/wiki/Goomba" title="Goomba">Goomba</a> masks, which let them blend in with enemy Goombas. Players are also able to pick up various objects, such as bombs, baseballs and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Piranha_Plants" title="Piranha Plants">Piranha Plants</a>, which can be used to defeat enemies or solve puzzles, and ride around in a Skating Shoe or on an aquatic dinosaur named Plessie.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>The game features functionality with the <a href="/wiki/Wii_U_GamePad" title="Wii U GamePad">Wii U GamePad</a>, allowing players to rub the <a href="/wiki/Touchscreen" title="Touchscreen">touchscreen</a>, or blow into the <a href="/wiki/Microphone" title="Microphone">microphone</a> to reveal hidden blocks or items, hinder enemies and activate mechanisms, and also supports <a href="/wiki/Off-TV_Play" title="Off-TV Play">Off-TV Play</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SM3WIGN_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-SM3WIGN-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-polygon1_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-polygon1-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-venturebeat1_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-venturebeat1-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The game is also compatible with the <a href="/wiki/Wii_Remote" title="Wii Remote">Wii Remote</a>, Nunchuk, <a href="/wiki/Classic_Controller" title="Classic Controller">Classic Controller</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wii_U_Pro_Controller" title="Wii U Pro Controller">Wii U Pro Controller</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> An unlockable Luigi-themed version of <i><a href="/wiki/Mario_Bros." title="Mario Bros.">Mario Bros.</a></i>, titled <i>Luigi Bros.</i>, can be accessed either by clearing the game, or by having save data from <i><a href="/wiki/New_Super_Luigi_U" title="New Super Luigi U">New Super Luigi U</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Mario_3D_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mario, Luigi, Peach and Toad find a tilted glass <a href="/wiki/Super_Mario#Warp_Pipes_and_Warp_Cannons" title="Super Mario">pipe</a>. After Mario and Luigi fix it, a green Sprixie Princess appears and tells them that <a href="/wiki/Bowser_(character)" title="Bowser (character)">Bowser</a> kidnapped and trapped the rest of the Sprixie Princesses in <a href="/wiki/Jar" title="Jar">jars</a>. He arrives and captures her before escaping through the pipe, while Mario and friends enter it and pursue him. They find themselves in a land known as "The Sprixie Kingdom" and set off to find the fairies.</p>
<p>After Mario and friends save the final one, Bowser re-captures all seven Sprixie Princesses and heads to his <a href="/wiki/Amusement_park" title="Amusement park">amusement park</a> fortress. There after Bowser uses the Super Bell to transform into "Meowser", one of them hits a giant POW Block that Bowser stands on and he falls through the sky. While Bowser is trapped in the jar, Mario and friends say goodbye to the fairies, and travel through the pipe back to the <a href="/wiki/Mushroom_Kingdom" title="Mushroom Kingdom">Mushroom Kingdom</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Mario_3D_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Nintendo_Direct_presentations#Wii_U_Direct" title="List of Nintendo Direct presentations">January 2013 Nintendo Direct</a>, Nintendo announced that a new 3D <i>Mario</i> game was being produced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nintendo_EAD_Tokyo" title="Nintendo EAD Tokyo">the development team</a> behind <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Mario_Galaxy" title="Super Mario Galaxy">Super Mario Galaxy</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The game's official title was announced during the <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Entertainment_Expo_2013" title="Electronic Entertainment Expo 2013">E3 2013</a> Nintendo Direct on June 11, 2013, along with its tentative release date of November 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-na_date_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-na_date-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to Koichi Hayashida, Peach was originally not going to be a playable character. Yoshiaki Koizumi suggested for Peach to be playable, which would then be agreed upon. Koizumi reasoned, "I think she adds a lot to the sense of competition when you get in multiplayer ... You can have different people choosing different characters based on their personality or whoever they like."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Similarly, Rosalina was later added as a playable character. Kenta Motokura said, "I was thinking about what would be pleasing after the ending and wanted to bring in another female character in addition to Princess Peach. Rosalina has a following among the <i>Super Mario Galaxy</i> fanbase, and she's appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Mario_Kart" title="Mario Kart">Mario Kart</a></i> recently, so I think she's well known."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>The game's soundtrack was composed by <a href="/wiki/Mahito_Yokota" title="Mahito Yokota">Mahito Yokota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toru_Minegishi" title="Toru Minegishi">Toru Minegishi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Koji_Kondo" title="Koji Kondo">Koji Kondo</a> and Yasuaki Iwata, and performed by the Mario 3D World Big Band. An official soundtrack was released for <a href="/wiki/Club_Nintendo" title="Club Nintendo">Club Nintendo</a> members in Japan, Australia and Europe, featuring 77 songs across two CDs.</p>

<p><i>Super Mario 3D World</i> was critically acclaimed. It gained scores of 92.77% and 93/100 on aggregate review websites <a href="/wiki/GameRankings" title="GameRankings">GameRankings</a> and <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-GR-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MC_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-MC-29">[29]</a></sup> The title also won many awards from media outlets, including Game of The Year (GOTY) from <a href="/wiki/Eurogamer" title="Eurogamer">Eurogamer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Digital_Spy" title="Digital Spy">Digital Spy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/MSN" title="MSN">MSN</a> UK.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Famitsu" title="Famitsu">Famitsu</a> gave the game a score of 38/40.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Famitsu_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-Famitsu-33">[33]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a>'s Jose Otero gave <i>3D World</i> a 9.6/10, praising the visual design's "youthful energy," the "interesting risks" taken with the level designs, the "maddening challenge" offered by later worlds, and the "genuinely funny and memorable co-op". He said, "I reveled in the sheer brilliance of how much energy and gameplay mileage Nintendo has packed into every world." Otero's only complaint was that "the camera becomes a slight obstacle in four-player multiplayer".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_37-1"><a href="#cite_note-IGN-37">[37]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/GameTrailers" title="GameTrailers">GameTrailers</a> gave the game a score of 9.5, praising its gameplay and presentation, while criticizing camera issues and some odd control choices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GamesRadar" title="GamesRadar">GamesRadar</a> gave the game a score of 4.5/5, praising improved multiplayer and some moments so brilliant that they can make some other levels seem dull by comparison.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRadar_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-GRadar-36">[36]</a></sup> Luke Plunkett of <a href="/wiki/Kotaku" title="Kotaku">Kotaku</a> called the game "a terrific Mario game... just not a very good Wii U game", praising the game itself, but lamenting that it doesn't do much to show off the Wii U's capabilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Patrick Klepek at <a href="/wiki/Giant_Bomb" title="Giant Bomb">Giant Bomb</a>, saying "<i>World</i> continues to make the case that it's possible to reinvent a classic over and over again".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Anime_News_Network" title="Anime News Network">Anime News Network</a> gave the game an A grade, calling it "fun, imaginative, and filled with little surprises and delights."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Edge_(magazine)" title="Edge (magazine)">Edge</a></i> rated the game 9/10, calling it "Wii U's best game to date," and "the most next-gen game that 2013 has yet produced," and stating that Mario is the most fun character to play as because "his cohorts' abilities aren't anything special".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Edge_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-Edge-31">[31]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Destructoid" title="Destructoid">Destructoid</a>'s Chris Carter awarded the game a 10/10, praising the power-ups (especially the catsuit, which "open[s] up [doors] platforming-wise that weren't there before," and the double cherry, which "Nintendo really outdid themselves in terms of the technical mechanics" to make possible), the "incredibly sharp" graphics, and "one of the best OSTs I've ever heard". Carter wrote that "the only downfall of the design is the selection of boss fights," as Nintendo "had achieved a level of platforming design that's close to perfection".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Destruct_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-Destruct-30">[30]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Joystiq" title="Joystiq">Joystiq</a>'s Richard Mitchell gave the game 5/5 stars, praising the "astounding" visual craftsmanship, "excellent soundtrack," and "artful, purposeful design" which "communicates goals with complete clarity".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Joystiq_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-Joystiq-38">[38]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/GameZone" title="GameZone">GameZone</a>'s Mike Splechta gave it a 9.5/10, stating "With multiple characters to play as (harking back to <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Mario_Bros._2" title="Super Mario Bros. 2">Super Mario Bros. 2</a></i>) and an unlockable fifth, a fantastic and unobtrusive multiplayer component and enough new power-ups to make even Mario's head spin, you're bound to have a highly enjoyable time with one of the best 3D platformers released thus far."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Eurogamer" title="Eurogamer">Eurogamer</a>'s Christian Donlan gave the game a 10/10, describing it as "an endless freewheeling treat of a game" with an "ad-libbed drive to...explore how many different situations [Mario] can be squashed into". He noted that this inventiveness carries over to the "lavish and quick-changing" soundtrack: "This is pastiche at its most skillful, its most panoramic."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EuroG_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-EuroG-32">[32]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Cheat_Code_Central" title="Cheat Code Central">Cheat Code Central</a>'s Jenni Lada gave the game a 5/5, calling it "the closest we will ever come to a perfect <i>Mario</i> game, one that is a culmination of every right decision ever made in the series."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CCC_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-CCC-46">[46]</a></sup> Nintendo World Report stated that it's a <i>Mario</i> game that one would want to continue playing once the game is beaten and may be the killer app of the Wii U, saying "Thankfully <i>3D World</i> isn’t just a refinement of the <i>Mario</i> formula, it’s a powerful re-imagining. It’s an explosive cavalcade of color and excitement as well as a well thought out evolution of <i>Mario</i>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NWR_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-NWR-39">[39]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/The_Escapist_(magazine)" title="The Escapist (magazine)">The Escapist</a> was slightly more critical of it, saying "Buy it if you love that little plumber, and enjoy the new toys, but don't expect your mind to be blown." However, it gave a score of four stars out of five.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Escapist_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-Escapist-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sales">Sales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Mario_3D_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sales">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Japan, the game's first week sales in three days at retail, totaled to 99,588 copies sold (not including eShop download sales)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> and 57% of its initial shipment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> The sales were considered low at first, but it maintained strong sales during the following weeks. By January 5, the game's sales totaled about 400,000 units in Japan and was still on the weekly top 10 charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the UK, the game debuted at number 14, behind its competitor, <i><a href="/wiki/Knack_(video_game)" title="Knack (video game)">Knack</a></i>, which debuted at number 13.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> During its first eight days on the market, there were 215,000 units sold in the US according to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NPD_Group" title="NPD Group">NPD Group</a>, debuting out of the Top 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of September 30, 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Super_Mario_3D_World&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the game had yielded sales of 5.19 million units worldwide, making it the <a href="/wiki/List_of_best-selling_Wii_U_video_games" title="List of best-selling Wii U video games">Wii U's fourth best-selling game</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Spin-off">Spin-off</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Mario_3D_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Spin-off">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Captain_Toad:_Treasure_Tracker" title="Captain Toad: Treasure Tracker">Captain Toad: Treasure Tracker</a></div>
<p>Based on and expanding upon the game's <i>The Adventures of Captain Toad</i> puzzle-based levels, a separate game, <i><a href="/wiki/Captain_Toad:_Treasure_Tracker" title="Captain Toad: Treasure Tracker">Captain Toad: Treasure Tracker</a></i>, was released for the Wii U on November 13, 2014 in Japan, December 5, 2014 in North America and in January 2015 in PAL regions.</p>
