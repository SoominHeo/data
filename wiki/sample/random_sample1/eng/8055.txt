<p>The <b>Super Sentai Series</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">スーパー戦隊シリーズ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sūpā Sentai Shirīzu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the name given to the long-running Japanese <a href="/wiki/Superhero" title="Superhero">superhero</a> team franchise of TV series produced by <a href="/wiki/Toei_Company" title="Toei Company">Toei Co., Ltd.</a>, Toei Agency and <a href="/wiki/Bandai" title="Bandai">Bandai</a>, and aired by <a href="/wiki/TV_Asahi" title="TV Asahi">TV Asahi</a> ("<i><a href="/wiki/Sentai" title="Sentai">Sentai</a></i>" is the Japanese word for "task force" or "fighting squadron"). The shows are of the <a href="/wiki/Tokusatsu" title="Tokusatsu">tokusatsu</a> genre, featuring live action characters and colorful special effects, and are aimed at children. The Super Sentai Series is one of the most prominent <a href="/wiki/Tokusatsu" title="Tokusatsu">tokusatsu</a> franchises in Japan, alongside the <a href="/wiki/Ultra_Series" title="Ultra Series">Ultra Series</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Series" title="Kamen Rider Series">Kamen Rider Series</a>, which it currently airs alongside in the <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Hero_Time" title="Super Hero Time">Super Hero Time</a></i> programming block on Sundays. Outside Japan, the Super Sentai Series is best known as the source material for the <i><a href="/wiki/Power_Rangers" title="Power Rangers">Power Rangers</a></i> franchise.</p>

<p>In every Super Sentai Series, the protagonists are a team of people who transform into superheroes and gain superpowers – color-coded costumes, <a href="/wiki/Signature_weapon" title="Signature weapon">signature weapons</a>, sidearms and fighting skills – in order to battle a group of evil beings that threaten to take over the Earth. In a typical episode, the heroes thwart the enemies' plans and defeats an army of enemy soldiers and the <a href="/wiki/Villain_of_the_week" title="Villain of the week">monster of the week</a>, and in one last effort to defeat the heroes, an enlarged version of the monster appears to confront them, only to be defeated again when the heroes call for their <a href="/wiki/Mecha" title="Mecha">mecha</a> (huge robotic machines that can combine to form one or more giant robots) to fight it. While each Super Sentai Series is set in its own fictional universe, various TV, video and film specials feature a team-up between one or more teams.</p>
<p>The series was originally created by <a href="/wiki/Shotaro_Ishinomori" title="Shotaro Ishinomori">Shotaro Ishinomori</a>, known for his creation of <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider" title="Kamen Rider">Kamen Rider</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Cyborg_009" title="Cyborg 009">Cyborg 009</a></i>, and he developed the first two television series <i><a href="/wiki/Himitsu_Sentai_Gorenger" title="Himitsu Sentai Gorenger">Himitsu Sentai Gorenger</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/J.A.K.Q._Dengekitai" title="J.A.K.Q. Dengekitai">J.A.K.Q. Dengekitai</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Toei_Company" title="Toei Company">Toei Company</a> put the franchise on hiatus for a year, collaborating with <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a> to produce the live action <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_(Toei_TV_series)" title="Spider-Man (Toei TV series)">Spider-Man</a></i>, which added giant robots to the concept of tokusatsu shows. This concept was carried over to Toei and Marvel's next show, <i><a href="/wiki/Battle_Fever_J" title="Battle Fever J">Battle Fever J</a></i>. The giant robot was used throughout the following Super Sentai programs.</p>
<p>The term <i>sentai</i> is also occasionally used to describe shows with similar premises such as the <a href="/wiki/Magical_girl" title="Magical girl">magical girl</a> team in <i><a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon" title="Sailor Moon">Sailor Moon</a></i>, as <a href="/wiki/Naoko_Takeuchi" title="Naoko Takeuchi">Naoko Takeuchi</a> used Sentai ideas as inspiration in the creation of the Sailor Soldiers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McCarterInterview_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-McCarterInterview-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Power_Rangers"><i>Power Rangers</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Sentai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Power Rangers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Power_Rangers" title="Power Rangers">Power Rangers</a></div>
<p>In 1993, American production company <a href="/wiki/Saban_Entertainment" title="Saban Entertainment">Saban Entertainment</a> turned 1992's <i>Kyōryū Sentai Zyuranger</i> into <i><a href="/wiki/Mighty_Morphin_Power_Rangers" title="Mighty Morphin Power Rangers">Mighty Morphin Power Rangers</a></i> for the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Kids" title="Fox Kids">Fox Kids</a> programming block, combining the original Japanese action footage with new footage featuring American actors for the story sequences. Since then nearly every <i>Super Sentai</i> series that followed (with a few exceptions) became a new Americanized season of <i><a href="/wiki/Power_Rangers" title="Power Rangers">Power Rangers</a></i>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> most countries also switched from <i>Super Sentai</i> to <i>Power Rangers</i>, such as France, Brazil, Thailand, and the Philippines. In 2002, Saban sold the rights to broadcasting of <i>Power Rangers</i> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney" title="Disney">Disney</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buena_Vista_Entertainment" title="Buena Vista Entertainment">Buena Vista Entertainment</a>, who handled the franchise until 2010, broadcasting on <a href="/wiki/ABC_Kids_(United_States)" title="ABC Kids (United States)">ABC Kids</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABC_Family_Channel" title="ABC Family Channel">ABC Family Channel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jetix" title="Jetix">Jetix</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Toon_Disney" title="Toon Disney">Toon Disney</a>. In 2010, Saban bought back the rights to <i>Power Rangers</i> and moved the show to the <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a> network for 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-buy_back_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-buy_back-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="TV_series">TV series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Sentai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: TV series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Himitsu_Sentai_Gorenger" title="Himitsu Sentai Gorenger">Himitsu Sentai Gorenger</a></i> (1975–1977)</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/J.A.K.Q._Dengekitai" title="J.A.K.Q. Dengekitai">J.A.K.Q. Dengekitai</a></i> (1977)</li>

<p>Although the Super Sentai Series originated in Japan, various Sentai series have been imported and <a href="/wiki/Dubbing_(filmmaking)" title="Dubbing (filmmaking)">dubbed</a> in other languages for broadcast in several other countries.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Sentai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Choudenshi_Bioman" title="Choudenshi Bioman">Bioman</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Choushinsei_Flashman" title="Choushinsei Flashman">Flashman</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hikari_Sentai_Maskman" title="Hikari Sentai Maskman">Maskman</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Choujuu_Sentai_Liveman" title="Choujuu Sentai Liveman">Liveman</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Kousoku_Sentai_Turboranger" title="Kousoku Sentai Turboranger">Turboranger</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Chikyu_Sentai_Fiveman" title="Chikyu Sentai Fiveman">Fiveman</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%8Djin_Sentai_Jetman" title="Chōjin Sentai Jetman">Jetman</a></i> were broadcast in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> in the 1980s and early 1990s, with <i>Maskman</i> and <i>Liveman</i> marketed as <i>Bioman 2</i> and <i>Bioman 3</i>, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Additionally, <i>Liveman</i>, <i>Turboranger</i>, and <i>Jetman</i> were broadcast in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/Denshi_Sentai_Denziman" title="Denshi Sentai Denziman">Denziman</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dai_Sentai_Goggle-V" title="Dai Sentai Goggle-V">Goggle-V</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Taiyo_Sentai_Sun_Vulcan" title="Taiyo Sentai Sun Vulcan">Sun Vulcan</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Battle_Fever_J" title="Battle Fever J">Battle Fever J</a></i> were broadcast in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. In addition, some episodes of <i>Bioman</i> and <i>Turboranger</i> were released on VHS in <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Sentai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Various Sentai Series such as <i>Fiveman</i> and <i>Choushinsei Flashman</i> were also broadcast in <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a> sometime in the 1990s dubbed in English and starting with Hikari Sentai Maskman, these are later dubbed in <a href="/wiki/Malay_language" title="Malay language">Malay</a>. Almost all Super Sentai shows were broadcast in <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a> since the 1980s; there, most new shows were exclusively broadcast on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Modernine_TV" title="Modernine TV">Channel 9</a> a year late from its Japanese debut in the late 1980s to early 1990s until Power Rangers replaced <i><a href="/wiki/Ky%C5%8Dry%C5%AB_Sentai_Zyuranger" title="Kyōryū Sentai Zyuranger">Kyōryū Sentai Zyuranger</a></i> in the mid-1990s. Since then, the series have appeared on various channels. In Vietnam, most of the Super Sentai series are netizens Vietnamese subtitles and posted on public internet sites.</p>
<p>Phuong Nam Film Company is a leading brand in Vietnam and licensed units, distribution, exploitation and disclosures Vietnamese and issued series of Super Sentai series by Toei Company production. Starting from 2003 released <i>Hyakujuu Sentai Gaoranger</i> series, produced VCD and DVD format, is the best-selling series Vietnam at that time. By 2007, Phuong Nam Film continue to release the <i><a href="/wiki/Tokusou_Sentai_Dekaranger" title="Tokusou Sentai Dekaranger">Tokusou Sentai Dekaranger</a></i> series. Then the Sentai series to be released is: <i><a href="/wiki/Mahou_Sentai_Magiranger" title="Mahou Sentai Magiranger">Mahou Sentai Magiranger</a></i> (2008); <i><a href="/wiki/GoGo_Sentai_Boukenger" title="GoGo Sentai Boukenger">GoGo Sentai Boukenger</a></i> (2009); <i><a href="/wiki/Engine_Sentai_Go-onger" title="Engine Sentai Go-onger">Engine Sentai Go-onger</a></i> (2011); <i><a href="/wiki/Samurai_Sentai_Shinkenger" title="Samurai Sentai Shinkenger">Samurai Sentai Shinkenger</a></i> (2012); <i><a href="/wiki/Tensou_Sentai_Goseiger" title="Tensou Sentai Goseiger">Tensou Sentai Goseiger</a></i> (2013); then the just released DVD as <i><a href="/wiki/Kaizoku_Sentai_Gokaiger" title="Kaizoku Sentai Gokaiger">Kaizoku Sentai Gokaiger</a></i> series (2014) and <i><a href="/wiki/Tokumei_Sentai_Go-Busters" title="Tokumei Sentai Go-Busters">Tokumei Sentai Go-Busters</a></i> (2015). Since 2016, Phuong Nam Film with Children's TV channel broadcast -Sao TV premiere series <i><a href="/wiki/Zyuden_Sentai_Kyoryuger" title="Zyuden Sentai Kyoryuger">Zyuden Sentai Kyoryuger</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Ressha_Sentai_ToQger" title="Ressha Sentai ToQger">Ressha Sentai ToQger</a></i>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Philippines">Philippines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Sentai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Philippines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/J.A.K.Q._Dengekitai" title="J.A.K.Q. Dengekitai">J.A.K.Q. Dengekitai</a></i> was the first Sentai Series to be shown in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> in the late-1970s, but it was <i><a href="/wiki/Himitsu_Sentai_Gorenger" title="Himitsu Sentai Gorenger">Himitsu Sentai Gorenger</a></i> (retitled <i>Star Rangers</i>) that most fans are familiar with (Both shown on <a href="/wiki/Radio_Philippines_Network" title="Radio Philippines Network">RPN</a>). Just as in France and Brazil, <i>Choudenshi Bioman</i> (first aired on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IBC-13" title="IBC-13">IBC-13</a> and later in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=RPN9&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="RPN9 (page does not exist)">RPN9</a> and <a href="/wiki/ABS-CBN" title="ABS-CBN">ABS-CBN</a> was the last tokusatsu <a href="/wiki/Dubbing_(filmmaking)" title="Dubbing (filmmaking)">dubbed</a> in English on Philippine television) and <i>Hikari Sentai Maskman</i> (the first Super Sentai Series dubbed in Filipino on <a href="/wiki/Intercontinental_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Intercontinental Broadcasting Corporation">IBC</a>) were broadcast in the Philippines in the 1980s. <a href="/wiki/ABS-CBN" title="ABS-CBN">ABS-CBN</a> became the first network in most records to air Super Sentai and Power Rangers. In fact, <i>Choudenshi Bioman</i> was so popular that many people from that generation came to associate all the Super Sentai series as continuations of it that Hikari Sentai Maskman was marketed as Bioman 2 Maskman on local TV. During the early 1990s, some other series were shown, such as <i>Kousoku Sentai Turboranger</i>, on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IBC-13" title="IBC-13">IBC-13</a> <i>Chikyu Sentai Fiveman</i> and <i>Chōjin Sentai Jetman</i> which became the last Sentai series to broadcast on <a href="/wiki/TV5_(Philippines)" title="TV5 (Philippines)">TV5</a> in 1995 and <i>Dai Sentai Goggle V</i>. In 1995, ABS-CBN aired the American-produced <i>Power Rangers</i> until 1999.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Korea">South Korea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Sentai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: South Korea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Super Sentai has also been broadcast in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, dubbed from <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a> to <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>. The first series to air in South Korea was <i>Choushinsei Flashman</i> titled <i>Jigu Bangwidae Flash Man</i> (<i>Earth Defence Squadron Flashman</i>), released in video format in 1989 by the Daeyung Panda video company followed by <i>Hikari Sentai Maskman</i> and <i>Chodenshi Bioman</i>. Throughout the 90s, <i>Dai Sentai Goggle Five</i>, <i>Dengeki Sentai Changeman</i>, <i>Choujuu Sentai Liveman</i>, <i>Kousoku Sentai Turboranger</i> were also released in video format. Recently, <a href="/wiki/Tooniverse" title="Tooniverse">Tooniverse</a> (formerly Orion Cartoon Network), JEI-TV (Jaeneung Television), CHAMP TV/ANIONE TV (DAEWON BROADCASTING), Cartoon Network South Korea, and <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon_(South_Korea)" title="Nickelodeon (South Korea)">Nickelodeon South Korea</a> have broadcast Super Sentai series a year following their original Japanese broadcast, but have changed the titles to "Power Rangers".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Latin_America">Latin America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Sentai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Latin America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Choushinsei_Flashman" title="Choushinsei Flashman">Flashman</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liveman" title="Liveman">Liveman</a> were broadcast in <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> in the early 1990s and later repetitions in the early 2000s. <i>Flashman</i> arrived with Spanish dubbing done in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> and <i>Liveman</i> in <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Dengeki_Sentai_Changeman" title="Dengeki Sentai Changeman">Changeman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Choushinsei_Flashman" title="Choushinsei Flashman">Flashman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hikari_Sentai_Maskman" title="Hikari Sentai Maskman">Maskman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dai_Sentai_Goggle-V" title="Dai Sentai Goggle-V">Goggle V</a> were broadcast in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. The first season to air was <i>Changeman</i> in 1988, on the TV Manchete channel, currenttly "RedeTV!", and caused a tremendous impact in its time, being considered a cult classic.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Sentai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/Honolulu" title="Honolulu">Honolulu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KIKU-TV" title="KIKU-TV">KIKU-TV</a> had success with <i><a href="/wiki/Android_Kikaider" title="Android Kikaider">Android Kikaider</a></i> (called <i>Kikaida</i>) and <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_V3" title="Kamen Rider V3">Kamen Rider V3</a></i> in the 1970s, multiple Super Sentai series, including <i>Himitsu Sentai Gorenger</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Battle_Fever_J" title="Battle Fever J">Battle Fever J</a></i>, were brought to the Hawaiian market (all broadcast in Japanese with English subtitles by JN Productions).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 1985, <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a> produced a pilot for an American adaptation of a Super Sentai series, but the show was rejected by the major US TV networks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In 1986, <a href="/wiki/Saban_Entertainment" title="Saban Entertainment">Saban Productions</a> produced a pilot for an American adaptation of <i>Choudenshi Bioman</i> titled <i>Bio Man</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In 1987, some episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Kagaku_Sentai_Dynaman" title="Kagaku Sentai Dynaman">Kagaku Sentai Dynaman</a></i> were dubbed and aired as a parody on the <a href="/wiki/USA_Network" title="USA Network">USA Network</a> television show <i><a href="/wiki/Night_Flight_(TV_series)" title="Night Flight (TV series)">Night Flight</a></i>.</p>

Factory then released box sets of <i>Gosei Sentai Dairanger</i> in 2015<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="This claim needs references to better sources. (June 2016)">better source needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and <i>Ninja Sentai Kakuranger</i> on May 17, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> <i>Chōriki Sentai Ohranger</i> will be released on DVD in November 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<p>The Super Sentai Series has been parodied as well as emulated in various ways throughout the world.</p>
<p>There have been many tribute series that pay homage to the long-running franchise, starting with the Japanese fan film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aikoku_Sentai_Dai-Nippon" title="Aikoku Sentai Dai-Nippon">Patriotic Squadron Great Japan</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja"><a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%84%9B%E5%9C%8B%E6%88%B0%E9%9A%8A%E5%A4%A7%E6%97%A5%E6%9C%AC" title="ja:愛國戰隊大日本">愛國戰隊大日本</a></span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Aikoku Sentai Dai-Nippon</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> created by what is now <a href="/wiki/Gainax" title="Gainax">Gainax</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The most recent tributes have been the <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thai</a> TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Sport_Ranger" title="Sport Ranger">Sport Ranger</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> online series <i><a href="/wiki/France_Five" title="France Five">Jushi Sentai France Five</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> (now <i>Shin Kenjushi France Five</i>); the latter being so popular in Japan that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_in_Japanese_animation" title="Music in Japanese animation">anison</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Akira_Kushida" title="Akira Kushida">Akira Kushida</a> recorded its opening theme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>As part of the <i>Omoikkiri Ii!! TV</i> television program, they produced a series of features on various spas and <a href="/wiki/Onsen" title="Onsen">onsen</a> around Japan titled <i>Bihada Sentai Sparanger</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">美肌泉隊ＳＰＡレンジャー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Bihada Sentai Suparenjā</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, <i>Handsome Muscle Spring Corps Sparanger</i>)</span>. This featured tokusatsu and drama actors <a href="/wiki/Takashi_Hagino" title="Takashi Hagino">Takashi Hagino</a> (Changéríon of <i><a href="/wiki/Choukou_Senshi_Chang%C3%A9r%C3%ADon" title="Choukou Senshi Changéríon">Choukou Senshi Changéríon</a></i> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Ouja" title="Kamen Rider Ouja">Kamen Rider Ouja</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Ryuki" title="Kamen Rider Ryuki">Kamen Rider Ryuki</a></i>) as Spa Red <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ＳＰＡレッド</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Supa Reddo</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, <a href="/wiki/Kento_Handa" title="Kento Handa">Kento Handa</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_Faiz" title="Kamen Rider Faiz">Kamen Rider Faiz</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Kamen_Rider_555" title="Kamen Rider 555">Kamen Rider 555</a></i>) as Spa Blue <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ＳＰＡブルー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Supa Burū</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, <a href="/wiki/Masashi_Mikami" title="Masashi Mikami">Masashi Mikami</a> (Bouken Blue in <i><a href="/wiki/GoGo_Sentai_Boukenger" title="GoGo Sentai Boukenger">GoGo Sentai Boukenger</a></i>) as Spa Yellow <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ＳＰＡイエロー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Supa Ierō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; <p>In 2013, Gainax produced <i>EA's Rock</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">エアーズロック</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Eāzu Rokku</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, a 13 episode miniseries of live action shorts which parodied the Super Sentai Series. The series' characters are all former members of a fighting troup called Sensation Warriors Gokan Five <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">感覚戦士ゴカンファイブ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kankaku Senshi Gokan Faibu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, "<i>gokan</i>" is Japanese for the "five <a href="/wiki/Sense" title="Sense">senses</a>")</span>. <i>EA's Rock</i> is broadcast on the Tōmeihan Net 6 <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Association_of_Independent_Television_Stations" title="Japanese Association of Independent Television Stations">Japanese Association of Independent Television Stations</a> (<a href="/wiki/Television_Saitama" title="Television Saitama">TVS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chiba_Television_Broadcasting" title="Chiba Television Broadcasting">CTC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Television_Kanagawa" title="Television Kanagawa">tvk</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mie_Television&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mie Television (page does not exist)">MTV</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Broadcasting_System" title="Kyoto Broadcasting System">KBS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sun_Television" title="Sun Television">SUN</a>) as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nico_Nico_Douga" title="Nico Nico Douga">Nico Nico Douga</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Akibaranger"><i>Akibaranger</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Sentai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Akibaranger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Unofficial_Sentai_Akibaranger" title="Unofficial Sentai Akibaranger">Unofficial Sentai Akibaranger</a></div>
<p><i>Unofficial Sentai Akibaranger</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">非公認戦隊アキバレンジャー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Hikōnin Sentai Akibarenjā</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Toei-produced parody series that premiered in April 2012 on <a href="/wiki/TV_Asahi" title="TV Asahi">BS Asahi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metropolitan_Television" title="Tokyo Metropolitan Television">Tokyo MX</a>. <i>Akibaranger</i> is made for adult fans who were fans of the Super Sentai Series as children. The story features three <a href="/wiki/Otaku" title="Otaku">otaku</a> who live in the <a href="/wiki/Akihabara" title="Akihabara">Akihabara</a> district of Tokyo who receive technology from a scientist to fight an evil threat that at first only exists in their delusions, but eventually starts materializing itself in the real world. Like <i>Kaizoku Sentai Gokaiger</i>, <i>Akibaranger</i> also features guest appearances by veteran Super Sentai actors (as themselves rather than their characters), as well as voice actors and musicians who have worked in both anime and tokusatsu. A second season aired on April 2013.</p>
