<p><b>Bollklubben Häcken</b>, also known as <b>BK Häcken</b> or simply <b>Häcken</b>, is a Swedish professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football club</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Gothenburg" title="Gothenburg">Gothenburg</a>. The club is affiliated to <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6teborgs_Fotbollf%C3%B6rbund" title="Göteborgs Fotbollförbund">Göteborgs Fotbollförbund</a> and play their home games at <a href="/wiki/Bravida_Arena" title="Bravida Arena">Bravida Arena</a>. The club colours, reflected in their crest and kit, are yellow and black. Formed on 2 August 1940, the club has played eleven seasons in Sweden's highest football league <a href="/wiki/Allsvenskan" title="Allsvenskan">Allsvenskan</a>, the club's first season in the league was in <a href="/wiki/1983_Allsvenskan" title="1983 Allsvenskan">1983</a>. The club is currently playing in Allsvenskan, the top tier of Swedish football, with a season lasting from March to November.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_beginning">The beginning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BK_H%C3%A4cken&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The beginning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The club was started by a group of 14- to 15-year-old youngsters, and they had a wish to not just play football for fun but to create an organisation and play in the national league system. The boys turned to <i><a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6teborgs_Fotbollf%C3%B6rbund" title="Göteborgs Fotbollförbund">Göteborgs Fotbollförbund</a></i>, the local FA, the boys had been playing under the name BK Kvick earlier, however the name was already taken by another organisation, the name was then change to Bollklubben Häcken after the huge <a href="/wiki/Hedge" title="Hedge">hedge</a> (sv: häck) which grew around the field the boys used to practise on. The organisation Bollklubben Häcken came into existence on 2 August 1940.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_decades">First decades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BK_H%C3%A4cken&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: First decades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the first years the club only played sporadic matches, youth championships and a few other matches, but eventually they started to play at the senior level. As of 1943 the club started to take in the league system and success came in an instant. Between 1944 and 1947 the club won their league and climbed from <i>west seniorclass 4</i> to <i>seniorclass 1</i>.</p>

<p>The start of the 1950s went as well as the 1940s had. The club won Division 4 in 1950 and when they won Division 3 in 1951 they went from a local club to be known as a "comet team" in the national media. The club fought for a place in <a href="/wiki/Allsvenskan" title="Allsvenskan">Allsvenskan</a> in 1953 but saw themselves defeated by <a href="/wiki/Kalmar_FF" title="Kalmar FF">Kalmar FF</a>, and in 1954 the club was relegated from Division 2. In 1955 the club set the record for the most spectators during a Division 3 game against <a href="/wiki/IK_Oddevold" title="IK Oddevold">IK Oddevold</a>, when 18,229 people showed up for the game. BK Häcken eventually won the league but returned soon again<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title='"was soon relegated back to div 3"? (March 2011)'>clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and played in Division 3 for the rest of the decade.</p>
<p>The 1960s started with the opening of the club's new clubhouse, now known as <i>Häckensborg</i>. Häcken spent most of the decade as a stable Division 3 team, but in 1967 they were relegated to Division 4 and it was struck by some severe economical and internal problems.</p>
<p>The 1970s started just as badly as the previous decade ended. The club fell out of Division 4 and held discussions with IF Warta about a merger. A discussion had been up several times earlier but had not gone as far as now, at the day of the voting the IF Warta members voted for a merging while the BK Häcken members voted against it. In 1971 the club won Division 5, the club then played in Division 4 until 1975 when they won it and in 1977 they won Division 3 with former Swedish international <a href="/wiki/Agne_Simonsson" title="Agne Simonsson">Agne Simonsson</a> as manager, when the club earlier won Division 3 in 1951 they climbed directly up to Division 2, however this time they had to play a qualifier against <a href="/wiki/IK_Brage" title="IK Brage">IK Brage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Motala_AIF" title="Motala AIF">Motala AIF</a> and <a href="/wiki/Degerfors_IF" title="Degerfors IF">Degerfors</a>. The club won and took the step up to Division 2. During 1978–79 the club played successfully in Division 2 with a 3rd place as the best position. In 1978 BK Häcken also started a ladies team.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reaching_Allsvenskan">Reaching Allsvenskan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BK_H%C3%A4cken&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reaching Allsvenskan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The new decade started as strong as the last one ended, Agne Simonsen continued to bring success to the club as they won Division 2 and qualified for a position in Allsvenskan, however <a href="/wiki/IF_Elfsborg" title="IF Elfsborg">IF Elfsborg</a> proved too strong with a 0–1 home defeat at <a href="/wiki/Gamla_Ullevi_(1916)" title="Gamla Ullevi (1916)">Gamla Ullevi</a> and then a 1–1 result in <a href="/wiki/Bor%C3%A5s" title="Borås">Borås</a> at <a href="/wiki/Ryavallen" title="Ryavallen">Ryavallen</a>. However, during the game at Gamla Ullevi, BK Häcken sets the club record for biggest home audience with 19 205. However the club was not let down by this and came in 2nd place in 1982 and this time they qualified for the promotion play-offs against <a href="/wiki/IFK_Norrk%C3%B6ping" title="IFK Norrköping">IFK Norrköping</a>, BK Häcken is able to reach a 2–0 result home at <a href="/wiki/Rambergsvallen" title="Rambergsvallen">Rambergsvallen</a> and then achieve a 1–1 away at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Idrottsparken" title="Idrottsparken">Idrottsparken</a> in <a href="/wiki/Norrk%C3%B6ping" title="Norrköping">Norrköping</a> and the club reached Allsvenskan for the first time in club history. The time that BK Häcken spends in Allsvenskan is however short, the club's successful manager Agne Simonsen leaves for a local competitor and Reine Almqvist takes over, the club ends up last in the league and is relegated back to Division 2 where they play for the rest of the 1980s.</p>
<p>BK Häcken starts the 1990s with winning Division 1 söder and faces <a href="/wiki/GIF_Sundsvall" title="GIF Sundsvall">GIF Sundsvall</a> in the promotion play-offs, and despite an impressive 5–2 victory at home for Häcken, Sundsvall is able to come back in and win the match in <a href="/wiki/Sundsvall" title="Sundsvall">Sundsvall</a> and gets promoted to Allsvenskan. The club was also successful plying in <a href="/wiki/Svenska_Cupen" title="Svenska Cupen">Svenska Cupen</a> and reaches the final, but are defeated again, this time 3–0 by <a href="/wiki/Djurg%C3%A5rdens_IF_Fotboll" title="Djurgårdens IF Fotboll">Djurgårdens IF</a>. The club is close in reaching Allsvenskan again in 1991, but a 2–2 home and 1–1 away sees them losing the position to <a href="/wiki/Helsingborgs_IF" title="Helsingborgs IF">Helsingborgs IF</a>. 1992 is more successful as they reach Allsvenskan through a 4th place in Kvalsvenskan and this time the spell in the top league is not as short as earlier, BK Häcken ends up on in 6th place in 1993 and gets to play a second year in Allsvenskan, however the club ends up last in the league in 1994 and following some turbulence on the leadership side of the club it stays in Division 1 until 1997 as the club ended up in 2nd place and qualifies for the play-offs against <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4ster%C3%A5s_SK_Fotboll" title="Västerås SK Fotboll">Västerås SK</a>, with a 1–1 home and with a 4–2 away BK Häcken climbs up to Allsvenskan again. The club is however relegated again in 1998 after ending up second last, but returns in 1999 after winning Division 1 Södra, this year <a href="/wiki/Kim_K%C3%A4llstr%C3%B6m" title="Kim Källström">Kim Källström</a> also makes his debut for the club.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_millennium">New millennium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BK_H%C3%A4cken&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: New millennium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The new millennium continues as the last decade did, the club is able to defend its Allsvenska position in a relegation/promotion play-off against <a href="/wiki/Mj%C3%A4llby_AIF" title="Mjällby AIF">Mjällby AIF</a> in the end of the <a href="/wiki/2000_in_Swedish_football" title="2000 in Swedish football">2000 season</a> after penalties, Kjell Pettersson leaves the club and <a href="/wiki/Torbj%C3%B6rn_Nilsson" title="Torbjörn Nilsson">Torbjörn Nilsson</a> takes over as manager. 2001 BK Häcken plays its sixth season overall in Allsvenskan but ends second last and is relegated to <a href="/wiki/Superettan" title="Superettan">Superettan</a>, through a cooperation with <a href="/wiki/Floda_BoIF" title="Floda BoIF">Floda BoIF</a> future Liberian national <a href="/wiki/Dulee_Johnson" title="Dulee Johnson">Dulee Johnson</a> arrives at the club and in 2002 and he gets company from <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Dixon" title="Jimmy Dixon">Jimmy Dixon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dioh_Williams" title="Dioh Williams">Dioh Williams</a>. BK Häcken stays in Superettan until 2004, being close to take the step up both in 2002, failing on goal difference, and in 2003, defeated by GIF Sundsvall in the play-offs, in 2004 the clubs reaches Allsvenskan by winning Superettan. The club brings in former Swedish international <a href="/wiki/Teddy_Lu%C4%8Di%C4%87" title="Teddy Lučić">Teddy Lučić</a> and Danish international <a href="/wiki/Stig_T%C3%B8fting" title="Stig Tøfting">Stig Tøfting</a> in 2005 in an attempt to reach a higher position the earlier, the club reaches 8th places. The 2006 season gets harder as Stig Tøfting and Dulee Johnson leaves the club and the club is forced to play against a qualify against <a href="/wiki/IF_Brommapojkarna" title="IF Brommapojkarna">IF Brommapojkarna</a>, BK Häcken is defeated by a total of 4–1 and is relegated to Superettan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010, Häcken earned the Fair Play award for the Allsvenskan. This resulted in the team earning a spot in the 1st Qualifying Round of the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2011–12 UEFA Europa League">2011–12 UEFA Europa League</a> when Sweden won third place in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Fair_Play_ranking" title="UEFA Fair Play ranking">UEFA Fair Play ranking</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rivalries">Rivalries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BK_H%C3%A4cken&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Rivalries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Häcken's main rivals are the three other <a href="/wiki/Gothenburg" title="Gothenburg">Gothenburg</a> teams <a href="/wiki/IFK_G%C3%B6teborg" title="IFK Göteborg">IFK Göteborg</a>, <a href="/wiki/GAIS" title="GAIS">GAIS</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C3%96rgryte_IS" title="Örgryte IS">Örgryte IS</a> with IFK being considered the biggest rival. Since Häcken is from the Island of Hisingen in Gothenburg it is also considered to be the team of Hisingen while the other three teams, widely supported, all come from mainland Gothenburg.</p>
<p>A small rivalry also consists between Häcken and <a href="/wiki/IF_Elfsborg" title="IF Elfsborg">IF Elfsborg</a> and like all teams from Gothenburg, the teams from <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> (<a href="/wiki/AIK_Fotboll" title="AIK Fotboll">AIK</a>, <a href="/wiki/Djurg%C3%A5rdens_IF_Fotboll" title="Djurgårdens IF Fotboll">Djurgårdens IF</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hammarby_IF" title="Hammarby IF">Hammarby</a>) are considered as rivals as well.</p>
