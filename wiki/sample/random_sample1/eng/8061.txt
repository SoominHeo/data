<p><i><b>Superstore</b></i> is an American single-camera <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">sitcom</a> television series that premiered on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> on November 30, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2016_Premieres_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-2016_Premieres-1">[1]</a></sup> The series was created by <a href="/wiki/Justin_Spitzer" title="Justin Spitzer">Justin Spitzer</a>, who also serves as an executive producer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Starring <a href="/wiki/America_Ferrera" title="America Ferrera">America Ferrera</a> (who also serves as a producer) and <a href="/wiki/Ben_Feldman_(actor)" title="Ben Feldman (actor)">Ben Feldman</a>, <i>Superstore</i> follows a group of employees working at "Cloud 9", store number 1217, a fictional <a href="/wiki/Big-box_store" title="Big-box store">big-box store</a> in <a href="/wiki/St._Louis" title="St. Louis">St. Louis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>. The ensemble and supporting cast features <a href="/wiki/Lauren_Ash" title="Lauren Ash">Lauren Ash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colton_Dunn" title="Colton Dunn">Colton Dunn</a>, Nico Santos, <a href="/wiki/Nichole_Bloom" title="Nichole Bloom">Nichole Bloom</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mark_McKinney" title="Mark McKinney">Mark McKinney</a>.</p>
<p>On February 23, 2016, the series was renewed for a second season by NBC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> On May 15, 2016, NBC announced that <i>Superstore</i> is expected to lead off its Thursday night primetime programming in the 2016–17 season. The second season premiered on September 22, 2016,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NBC_Fall_Premiere_Dates_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-NBC_Fall_Premiere_Dates-4">[4]</a></sup> with a 22-episode order that was announced on September 23, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Andreeva_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Andreeva-5">[5]</a></sup> A special Olympics-themed episode aired on August 19, 2016 during the network's coverage of the <a href="/wiki/2016_Summer_Olympics" title="2016 Summer Olympics">2016 Summer Olympic Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cast_and_characters">Cast and characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superstore_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Cast and characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Superstore_characters" title="List of Superstore characters">List of Superstore characters</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main">Main</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superstore_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Main">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/America_Ferrera" title="America Ferrera">America Ferrera</a> as Amy Dubanowski, a <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduran</a> Cloud 9 employee of eleven years who worked as an associate, then floor supervisor, then assistant manager, and very briefly store manager. Amy always wears a different name tag because she doesn't like strangers using her real name.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ben_Feldman_(actor)" title="Ben Feldman (actor)">Ben Feldman</a> as Jonah Simms, Cloud 9 sales associate. He and Mateo were both hired in the "Pilot" episode.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lauren_Ash" title="Lauren Ash">Lauren Ash</a> as Dina Fox, Cloud 9's assistant store manager.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Colton_Dunn" title="Colton Dunn">Colton Dunn</a> as Garrett McNeill, a Cloud 9 associate who is paralyzed from the waist down.</li>
<li>Nico Santos as Mateo Fernando Aquino Liwanag, a gay Filipino Cloud 9 associate who was hired the same time as Jonah. In second season, Mateo learns that he is an illegal alien because his grandmother received counterfeit passports.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nichole_Bloom" title="Nichole Bloom">Nichole Bloom</a> as Cheyenne Tyler Lee, a Cloud 9 associate. She is a pregnant, 17-year old high school student at the start of the series. She gave birth to her daughter, Harmonica, in the store in the first season finale.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mark_McKinney" title="Mark McKinney">Mark McKinney</a> as Glenn Sturgis, Cloud 9's store manager. Glenn is a devout Christian.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recurring">Recurring</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superstore_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Recurring">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Johnny Pemberton as Bo Derek Thompson, Cheyenne's immature, wannabe-rapper fiancé and her baby's father.</li>
<li>Kaliko Kauahi as Sandra, a Cloud 9 employee who has trouble bringing up legitimate concerns and being heard. She will often attempt to make her voice heard only to shoot herself down shortly after, or during. She also has superior autobiographical memory in which she can remember every single day of her life.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Josh_Lawson" title="Josh Lawson">Josh Lawson</a> as Tate Stasklewicz, a Cloud 9 pharmacist who is often rude, panicky, sarcastic and full of himself.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Linda_Porter_(actress)" title="Linda Porter (actress)">Linda Porter</a> as Myrtle, an elderly Cloud 9 employee who is often quite confused and forgetful.</li>
<li>Jon Barinholtz as Marcus, a dim-witted Cloud 9 employee who briefly dates Dina after Jonah rejects her. Marcus was briefly sent to jail after his mother sold him out.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ryan_Gaul" title="Ryan Gaul">Ryan Gaul</a> as Adam Dubanowski, Amy's husband and Emma's father. Adam and Amy were high school sweethearts, got married at 19, and had Emma thereafter. In the episode "Color Wars" it is revealed that he's a YouTube personality who makes grilling videos.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jon_Miyahara&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jon Miyahara (page does not exist)">Jon Miyahara</a> as Brett, a Cloud 9 employee who is always stated to break every sales and competition record, despite never actually moving on camera.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Michael_Bunin" title="Michael Bunin">Michael Bunin</a> as Jeff Sutin (season two), District Manager who oversees the St. Louis Cloud 9, among others. He is revealed to be gay, and has a romantic interest in Mateo.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superstore_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_and_filming">Development and filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superstore_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Development and filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The series was one of three pilots picked up by NBC on January 14, 2015, along with the sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Crowded_(TV_series)" title="Crowded (TV series)">Crowded</a></i>; both were green lighted to series status the same day (May 7, 2015).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The series was the first project for <a href="/wiki/Ruben_Fleischer" title="Ruben Fleischer">Ruben Fleischer</a>'s newly formed company The District as part of a two-year deal with Universal, as he directed the pilot episode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <i>Superstore</i> was officially picked up as a series on May 7, 2015, by <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pick-up_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-pick-up-11">[11]</a></sup> The first season consisted of eleven episodes, after the episode order was reduced from thirteen on October 19, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> It was announced on November 2, 2015, that the show would air the premiere on January 4, 2016, but would be airing two back-to-back episodes on November 30, 2015, following <i><a href="/wiki/The_Voice_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Voice (U.S. TV series)">The Voice</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casting">Casting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superstore_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Casting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It was announced on February 20, 2015, that <a href="/wiki/Lauren_Ash" title="Lauren Ash">Lauren Ash</a> had been cast as a series regular, and would be playing Dina, the store’s assistant manager.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> On March 2, 2015, <i>Deadline</i> reported that <i>Superstore</i> had added three other cast members, which was <a href="/wiki/Colton_Dunn" title="Colton Dunn">Colton Dunn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_McKinney" title="Mark McKinney">Mark McKinney</a> and Nico Santos. The website reported that Dunn would be playing Garret, the often-sarcastic narrator of the piece, McKinney would be playing Glenn, the intense store manager, and Santos would be playing Mateo, another new employee and a brown-noser from an impoverished background.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On March 12, 2015, <a href="/wiki/Nichole_Bloom" title="Nichole Bloom">Nichole Bloom</a> was announced to have joined the show as Cheyenne, a very pregnant teenage employee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Deadline.com" title="Deadline.com">Deadline</a></i> announced on March 13, 2015, that <a href="/wiki/Ben_Feldman_(actor)" title="Ben Feldman (actor)">Ben Feldman</a> had landed the male lead in <i>Superstore</i>, as Jonah, a new employee in the superstore Cloud 9.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Three days later, <i><a href="/wiki/TVLine" title="TVLine">TVLine</a></i> announced on March 16, 2015, that <a href="/wiki/America_Ferrera" title="America Ferrera">America Ferrera</a> had landed the female lead as the floor supervisor Amy in the Cloud 9 store. It was also reported that Ferrera was also a producer for the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Mindy_Project_crossover"><i>The Mindy Project</i> crossover</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superstore_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The Mindy Project crossover">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The series is implied to take place in the same fictional universe as <a href="/wiki/Hulu" title="Hulu">Hulu</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mindy_Project" title="The Mindy Project">The Mindy Project</a></i>, another series also produced by NBCUniversal Television. The episode "Under the Texan Sun" has Mindy Lahiri and Peter Prentice shopping at an <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a> Cloud 9 location.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superstore_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ratings">Ratings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superstore_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Ratings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The series debuted as a "preview" on November 30, 2015 following an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Voice_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Voice (U.S. TV series)">The Voice</a></i> with 7 million viewers making the second highest new comedy behind <i><a href="/wiki/Life_in_Pieces" title="Life in Pieces">Life in Pieces</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-s01e01-02_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-s01e01-02-20">[20]</a></sup> The series then moved to its regular Monday at 8:00 pm timeslot on January 4, 2016 with more than 6 million viewers making the highest rated comedy that did not have <i><a href="/wiki/The_Voice_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Voice (U.S. TV series)">The Voice</a></i> as a lead-in since <i><a href="/wiki/The_Michael_J._Fox_Show" title="The Michael J. Fox Show">The Michael J. Fox Show</a></i> back in September 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2016_Premieres_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-2016_Premieres-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superstore_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Early reviews for the series were mixed. According to <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, the first season of <i>Superstore</i> holds a score of 58 out of 100, indicating "mixed to average reviews" based on 21 critics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> On another review aggregate website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, the first season holds a 54% with a "Rotten" rating, based on 24 critics, with an average rating of 4.4/10. The general consensus is: "<i>Superstore</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s talented cast and obvious potential are slightly overshadowed by a tonally jumbled presentation and thin, formulaic writing."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> As the first season went along, however, reviews started to become more positive. Following the finale "Labor", the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> called it one of TV's best new comedies."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Pilot Viruet of <i><a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">The A.V. Club</a></i> wrote that the "first season ... got better and more confident as it moved on", and that the first season finale "is a nice little cap to a nice little sitcom that could’ve used a little more attention."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> After the series aired its Olympics special, <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> wrote that the show was "a funny, pointed and essential workplace comedy", and that "there are no weak links in [the] ensemble".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
