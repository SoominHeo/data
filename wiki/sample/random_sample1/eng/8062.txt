<p><i><b>Super R-Type</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ã¹ã¼ãã¼ã»ã¢ã¼ã«ã»ã¿ã¤ã</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>SÅ«pÄ Äru Taipu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Shooter_game" title="Shooter game">shooter game</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SNES" title="SNES">SNES</a>, developed and published by <a href="/wiki/Irem" title="Irem">Irem</a> in 1991. The game has been re-released on the <a href="/wiki/Wii" title="Wii">Wii</a> <a href="/wiki/Virtual_Console" title="Virtual Console">Virtual Console</a> in Japan, North America, and PAL regions in 2008.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_R-Type&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Super R-Type</i> borrows four stages from <i><a href="/wiki/R-Type_II" title="R-Type II">R-Type II</a></i> and adds three new ones. The game is known for its high difficulty (even compared to other games in the series), particularly because of its lack of checkpoints, since dying means restarting the level from scratch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Also, this game suffers from slowdown, which was also a problem in many early games for this system. But unlike the others <i>Super R-Type</i> slows down to virtual standstill when there are many things on the screen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> However, this proved useful for players when there were many obstacles on screen because it was easier to avoid them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Weapon_types">Weapon types</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_R-Type&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Weapon types">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Red: The Counter-air Laser, a powerful red and blue double transverse wave. This also fires its first-level equivalents, a red and a blue arrow-shaped laser shot, from the Bits, should the player have any.</li>
<li>Blue: The Rebound Laser, three long, thin, cyan lasers, one straight, two at 45Â° angles from the Force, that reflect off walls.</li>
<li>Yellow: The Counter-ground Laser, two long fireball chains released vertically from the Force that slink along the ceiling and floor.</li>
<li>Green: The Spread Laser, which fires a spread arc of eight orange fireballs.</li>
<li>Gray: The Shotgun Bomb, which launches a shell that explodes powerfully a short ways from the R-9.</li>
</ul>
<p>Refer to the main <i><a href="/wiki/R-Type" title="R-Type">R-Type</a></i> article for more about the Force and Bits.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Re-releases">Re-releases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_R-Type&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Re-releases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On March 14, 2008, the game was released on the Nintendo <a href="/wiki/Wii" title="Wii">Wii</a> <a href="/wiki/Virtual_Console" title="Virtual Console">Virtual Console</a> service in Europe and Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vcpal_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-vcpal-3">[3]</a></sup> The game was made available on the Virtual Console in North America on March 17, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vcna_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-vcna-2">[2]</a></sup> However, it was removed from the Virtual Console on March 30, 2012 in North America and on March 31, 2012 in Europe.</p>
