<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Super Free</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Super Free</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">スーパーフリー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sūpā-furī</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> or its shortened form <b>Sūfuri</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">スーフリ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> was an inter-university rave event club, mainly comprising students of <a href="/wiki/Waseda_University" title="Waseda University">Waseda University</a>, The leader of the club along with various students from Waseda and other universities in Tokyo was arrested and convicted for rape and subsequently, the club was dubbed by several English media outlets as a "rape club".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Its leader was Shin'ichirō Wada <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">和田 真一郎</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Wada Shin'ichirō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born July 30, 1974, in <a href="/wiki/Kawasaki,_Kanagawa" title="Kawasaki, Kanagawa">Kawasaki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kanagawa_Prefecture" title="Kanagawa Prefecture">Kanagawa Prefecture</a>)</span>. They were convicted of raping three women, but the real number of victims is unknown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Since their arrests, and the club's dissolution, twelve other women have been identified as victims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gang_rape_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-gang_rape-6">[6]</a></sup> The club was also incorporated as Super Free <a href="/wiki/Y%C5%ABgen_gaisha" title="Yūgen gaisha">Yūgen gaisha</a> (Limited) <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">有限会社スーパーフリー</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.</p>
<p>Super Free was established as a university social club in 1982.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Wada was a student at Waseda University before being arrested.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He entered Waseda University in 1994 and became the organizer of "Super Free" in 1995. It had 14 members as of 2003. Super Free was a highly successful inter-university rave event club, to the point that it was incorporated, having 5 branches nationwide, 30-odd employees and an office in Roppongi. According to the indictment, the club organized parties and social gatherings at a public night-club in <a href="/wiki/Roppongi" title="Roppongi">Roppongi</a>. The members of Super Free would scout for potential victims among the public, and befriend them offering drinks. Once their victims were intoxicated and incapable of resisting, they would lure them to an abandoned room.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gang_rape_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-gang_rape-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Wada was arrested on June 9, 2003. His circle's 13 other current members were also arrested for gang rape.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gang_rape_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-gang_rape-6">[6]</a></sup> Super Free was dissolved on June 22, 2003.</p>
<p>Wada was indicted for three counts of rape. On November 2, 2004, the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_District_Court" title="Tokyo District Court">Tokyo District Court</a> sentenced him to 14 years in prison, a comparatively heavy sentence under Japan's postwar sentencing guidelines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He appealed the verdict.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On June 2, 2005, the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_High_Court" title="Tokyo High Court">Tokyo High Court</a> rejected the appeal and upheld the original sentence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On November 1, 2005, the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Japan" title="Supreme Court of Japan">Supreme Court of Japan</a> followed suit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>After their arrest, a statute for the prosecution of gang rape was established under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Criminal_Code_of_Japan" title="Criminal Code of Japan">Criminal Code of Japan</a>. In January 2006, three students in <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_University" title="Kyoto University">Kyoto University</a> were arrested for gang rape under this penal code.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Seiichi_Ota" title="Seiichi Ota">Seiichi Ota</a>, a Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cabinet_minister" title="Cabinet minister">cabinet minister</a>, was heavily criticized after he issued a statement regarding the case, "At least gang rapists are still vigorous. Isn't that at least a little closer to normal?" on June 26, 2003. He later reported that his statement was taken out of context, and that he didn't have the chance to further comment on the topic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Then Chief Cabinet Secretary <a href="/wiki/Yasuo_Fukuda" title="Yasuo Fukuda">Yasuo Fukuda</a> was reported to have made highly controversial comments during an off-the-record discussion with reporters in June 2003 regarding the victims of rape by the members of Super Free, according to an article in the weekly magazine Shukan Bunshun. The magazine quoted Fukuda as saying: "There are women who look like they are saying 'Do it to me'. Those who have that kind of appearance are at fault, because men are black panthers." In response, Fukuda claimed that the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sh%C5%ABkan_Bunshun" title="Shūkan Bunshun">Shūkan Bunshun</a></i> had distorted his comments, stating that he had never intended to defend rape, and told a parliamentary panel afterwards that rape was "a criminal act and an atrocious crime".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
