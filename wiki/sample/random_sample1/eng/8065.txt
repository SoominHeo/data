<p>The <b>Spree</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_German" title="Help:IPA for German">[ˈʃpʁeː]</a></span>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sorbian_language" title="Sorbian language">Sorbian</a>: <span lang="wen" xml:lang="wen"><i>Sprjewja</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/Czech_language" title="Czech language">Czech</a>: <span lang="cs" xml:lang="cs"><i>Spréva</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">river</a> that flows through the <a href="/wiki/Saxony" title="Saxony">Saxony</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brandenburg" title="Brandenburg">Brandenburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> <a href="/wiki/States_of_Germany" title="States of Germany">states</a> of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, and in the <a href="/wiki/%C3%9Ast%C3%AD_nad_Labem_Region" title="Ústí nad Labem Region">Ústí nad Labem</a> region of the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>. Approximately 400 kilometres (250 mi) in length, it is a left bank <a href="/wiki/Tributary" title="Tributary">tributary</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Havel" title="Havel">River Havel</a>, which itself flows into the <a href="/wiki/Elbe" title="Elbe">Elbe</a> and then the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a>. It is the river on which the original centre of Berlin was built.</p>
<p>The reach of the river between the <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A4meritzsee" title="Dämeritzsee">Dämeritzsee</a> and <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCggelsee" title="Müggelsee">Müggelsee</a> to the east of Berlin is known as the <b>Müggelspree</b>.</p>

<p>The source of the Spree is located in Neugersdorf, Germany, in the <a href="/wiki/Lusatian_Highlands" title="Lusatian Highlands">Lusatian Highlands</a> (<i>Lausitzer Bergland</i>) near the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech</a> border. It runs on the border for a short distance at two points (near <a href="/wiki/Ebersbach,_G%C3%B6rlitz" title="Ebersbach, Görlitz">Ebersbach</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oppach" title="Oppach">Oppach</a>) before leaving the hills and passing through the old city of <a href="/wiki/Bautzen" title="Bautzen">Bautzen/Budyšin</a>, the center of the <a href="/wiki/Sorbs" title="Sorbs">Sorbs</a> in <a href="/wiki/Upper_Lusatia" title="Upper Lusatia">Upper Lusatia</a>. Just to the north of Bautzen the river flows through the <a href="/wiki/Bautzen_Reservoir" title="Bautzen Reservoir">Bautzen Reservoir</a>. Further north the river passes through the city of <a href="/wiki/Spremberg" title="Spremberg">Spremberg</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spremberg_Reservoir" title="Spremberg Reservoir">Spremberg Reservoir</a> before reaching the city of <a href="/wiki/Cottbus" title="Cottbus">Cottbus</a>. To the north of Cottbus the river enters the <a href="/wiki/Spreewald" title="Spreewald">Spreewald</a>, a large wetlands area in <a href="/wiki/Lower_Lusatia" title="Lower Lusatia">Lower Lusatia</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In the Spreewald the river passes through the towns of <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BCbbenau" title="Lübbenau">Lübbenau</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L%C3%BCbben" title="Lübben">Lübben</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leibsch" title="Leibsch">Leibsch</a>. Just below Leibsch, the <a href="/wiki/Dahme_Flood_Relief_Canal" title="Dahme Flood Relief Canal">Dahme Flood Relief Canal</a> diverts water from the Spree to run into the <a href="/wiki/Dahme_(river)" title="Dahme (river)">River Dahme</a> at <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A4rkisch_Buchholz" title="Märkisch Buchholz">Märkisch Buchholz</a>. The Spree continues north from Leibsch before flowing into the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Neuendorfer_See&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Neuendorfer See (page does not exist)">Neuendorfer See</a> at the northern edge of the Spreewald. From the Neundorfer See it then flows in an easterly direction to the <a href="/wiki/Schwielochsee_(lake)" title="Schwielochsee (lake)">Schwielochsee</a>, and then in a northerly and westerly direction to the town of <a href="/wiki/F%C3%BCrstenwalde" title="Fürstenwalde">Fürstenwalde</a>. From Fürstenwalde the river continues to flow westwards, through the <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A4meritzsee" title="Dämeritzsee">Dämeritzsee</a> and <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCggelsee" title="Müggelsee">Müggelsee</a>, to <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6penick" title="Köpenick">Köpenick</a> in the southeastern part of <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, where it is joined by its tributary, the River Dahme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bsiwg_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-bsiwg-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The final reach of the Spree is where it is best known. It flows through the city centre of Berlin to join the <a href="/wiki/Havel" title="Havel">River Havel</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spandau_(locality)" title="Spandau (locality)">Spandau</a>, one of Berlin’s western boroughs, which itself ultimately merges with the <a href="/wiki/Elbe" title="Elbe">Elbe</a> to enter the sea in <a href="/wiki/Cuxhaven" title="Cuxhaven">Cuxhaven</a>, after flowing through <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a>. On its route through Berlin, the river passes <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Cathedral" title="Berlin Cathedral">Berlin Cathedral</a> (<i>Berliner Dom</i>), the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reichstag_(building)" title="Reichstag (building)">Reichstag</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Schloss_Charlottenburg" title="Schloss Charlottenburg">Schloss Charlottenburg</a>. The renowned <a href="/wiki/Museum_Island" title="Museum Island">Museum Island</a> (<i>Museumsinsel</i>), with its collection of five major <a href="/wiki/Museum" title="Museum">museums</a>, is actually an island in the Spree. The <a href="/wiki/Badeschiff" title="Badeschiff">Badeschiff</a> is a floating <a href="/wiki/Swimming_pool" title="Swimming pool">swimming pool</a> moored in the Spree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bsiwg_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-bsiwg-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dwbs_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-dwbs-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Navigation">Navigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spree&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Navigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Small craft, such as <a href="/wiki/Punt_(boat)" title="Punt (boat)">punts</a>, are widely used in wetlands of the Spreewald. Larger craft can reach as far upstream as Leibsch, although the upper reaches are relatively shallow and are generally only used by leisure craft. Some intermediate reaches are unnavigable and by-passed by canals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bsiwg_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-bsiwg-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>For a stretch of about 20 kilometres (12 mi) east of and flowing through Fürstenwalde, the river forms part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oder-Spree_Canal" title="Oder-Spree Canal">Oder-Spree Canal</a>. On this reach, and on the reach west of the confluence with the River Dahme at Köpenick, the river forms part of secondary commercially link between Berlin and the <a href="/wiki/Oder" title="Oder">River Oder</a> and hence <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bsiwg_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-bsiwg-1">[1]</a></sup> The canal diverges from the Spree just east of Fürstenwalde and later joins the River Dahme at the (lake) <a href="/wiki/Seddinsee" title="Seddinsee">Seddinsee</a>.</p>
<p>In Berlin, the Spree forms part of a dense network of navigable waterways, many of which are artificial, and which provide a wide choice of routes. Several important commercial harbours can be found on this network, and tugs and barges move sand, grain, bricks, and beer. Tour boats tour the central section of the Spree and its adjoining waterways on a frequent basis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bsiwg_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-bsiwg-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spree&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name of the river Spree was recorded by <a href="/wiki/Thietmar_of_Merseburg" title="Thietmar of Merseburg">Thietmar of Merseburg</a> as <i>Sprewa</i> (either from Middle German <i>sprejen, sprewen</i>, High German <i>sprühen</i> meaning <i>to spray water</i>, or rather from <i>spreizen</i> meaning <i>to spread</i>, referring to the numerous bifurcations). People living at the Spree river (<i>Anwohner</i>) were in old German language (and are still) called <i>Spreewaner</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The river gives its name to several <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Germany" title="Districts of Germany">German districts</a>:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Spree-Nei%C3%9Fe" title="Spree-Neiße">Spree-Neiße</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Oder-Spree" title="Oder-Spree">Oder-Spree</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Berlin_Wall">Berlin Wall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spree&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Berlin Wall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deaths_at_the_Berlin_Wall" title="Deaths at the Berlin Wall">Deaths at the Berlin Wall</a></div>
<p>Many people died in the Spree during the Cold War while trying to cross the <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Wall" title="Berlin Wall">Berlin Wall</a>, including children who drowned with rescuers not allowed to enter the river to save them.</p>
