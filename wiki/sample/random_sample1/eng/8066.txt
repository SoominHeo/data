<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Spreewald</h1>

<p>The <b>Spreewald</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> for "<a href="/wiki/Spree" title="Spree">Spree</a> Woods"; in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lower_Sorbian" title="Lower Sorbian">Lower Sorbian</a>: <i>Błota</i>) is a picturesque section of the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> state of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brandenburg,_Germany" title="Brandenburg, Germany">Brandenburg</a> located about 100 km south-east of <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>. Spreewald was designated a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biosphere_reserve" title="Biosphere reserve">biosphere reserve</a> by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> in 1991. It is known for its traditional irrigation system, consisting of more than 200 small canals (called "Fließe"; total length: 1,300 km ) within the 484-square-kilometre (187 sq mi) area. The landscape was shaped during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ice-age" title="Ice-age">ice-age</a>. <a href="/wiki/Alder" title="Alder">Alder</a> forests on wetlands and pine forests on sandy dry areas are characteristic for the region. Grasslands and fields can be found as well.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spreewald&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Spreewaldhaus.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="768" data-file-width="1024" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Spreewaldhaus.jpg/220px-Spreewaldhaus.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Spreewaldhaus.jpg/330px-Spreewaldhaus.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Spreewaldhaus.jpg/440px-Spreewaldhaus.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Spreewaldhaus.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Historical housing at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lehde&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lehde (page does not exist)">Lehde</a> (a district of Lübbenau)</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>About 50,000 people live in the biosphere reserve (1998). Many of them are descendants of the first settlers in the Spreewald region, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slavic_peoples" title="Slavic peoples">Slavic</a> tribes of the <a href="/wiki/Sorbs" title="Sorbs">Sorbs</a>/<a href="/wiki/Wends" title="Wends">Wends</a>. They have preserved their traditional language, customs and clothing to this day.</p>
<p>People mostly depend on tourism. Many tourists enjoy the exploration of the Spreewald in <a href="/wiki/Punt_(boat)" title="Punt (boat)">punts</a>. Agriculture, forestry and fishery are other important sources of income. The principal town of the area is <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BCbbenau" title="Lübbenau">Lübbenau</a>.</p>
<p>Spreewald gave its name to the following <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Germany" title="Districts of Germany">German districts</a>:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dahme-Spreewald" title="Dahme-Spreewald">Dahme-Spreewald</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Oberspreewald-Lausitz" title="Oberspreewald-Lausitz">Oberspreewald-Lausitz</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nature">Nature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spreewald&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Nature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>About 18,000 species of flora and fauna have been counted. In 1991, the Spreewald was designated a UNESCO <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Man_and_Biosphere_Reserve_Programme" title="Man and Biosphere Reserve Programme">"Biosphärenreservat" (Man and Biosphere Reserve Programme)</a></i>.</p>
