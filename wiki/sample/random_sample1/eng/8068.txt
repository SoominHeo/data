<p><b>Schleswig</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_German" title="Help:IPA for German">[ˈʃleːsvɪç]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Danish_language" title="Danish language">Danish</a>: <span lang="da" xml:lang="da"><i>Slesvig</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/South_Jutlandic" title="South Jutlandic">South Jutlandic</a>: <i>Sljasvig</i>; archaic English: <i>Sleswick</i>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_German_language" title="Low German language">Low German</a>: <span lang="nds" xml:lang="nds"><i>Sleswig</i></span>) is a town in the northeastern part of <a href="/wiki/Schleswig-Holstein" title="Schleswig-Holstein">Schleswig-Holstein</a>, Germany. It is the capital of the <i><a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Germany" title="Districts of Germany">Kreis</a></i> (district) <a href="/wiki/Schleswig-Flensburg" title="Schleswig-Flensburg">Schleswig-Flensburg</a>. It has a population of about 27,000, the main industries being leather and food processing. It takes its name from the <a href="/wiki/Schlei" title="Schlei">Schlei</a>, an inlet of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baltic_sea" title="Baltic sea">Baltic sea</a> at the end of which it sits, and "vik" or "vig" which means bay in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Norse_language" title="Old Norse language">Old Norse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Danish_language" title="Danish language">Danish</a>. Schleswig or Slesvig therefore means <i>bay of the Schlei</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Schleswig,_Schleswig-Holstein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Schleswig-Slesvig.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="601" data-file-width="572" height="231" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Schleswig-Slesvig.png/220px-Schleswig-Slesvig.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Schleswig-Slesvig.png/330px-Schleswig-Slesvig.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Schleswig-Slesvig.png/440px-Schleswig-Slesvig.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Schleswig-Slesvig.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map over Schleswig / Slesvig.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The city lies at the western end of the <a href="/wiki/Schlei" title="Schlei">Schlei</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F%C3%B6rde" title="Förde">Förde</a>, which separates the two peninsulas of <a href="/wiki/Angeln" title="Angeln">Angeln</a> and <a href="/wiki/Schwansen" title="Schwansen">Schwansen</a> and is on the western edge of the <a href="/wiki/Schleswig-Holstein_Uplands" title="Schleswig-Holstein Uplands">Schleswig-Holstein Uplands</a> on the transition to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geest_(topography)" title="Geest (topography)">Geest</a> country. The urban area ranges from 0 to 20 m above sea level. <a href="/wiki/Brautsee" title="Brautsee">Brautsee</a> (lake) is in the town.</p>
<p>The nearest major cities are <a href="/wiki/Flensburg" title="Flensburg">Flensburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Husum" title="Husum">Husum</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kiel" title="Kiel">Kiel</a>. <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_7" title="Bundesautobahn 7">Autobahn 7</a> runs immediately west of the city. <a href="/wiki/Bundesstra%C3%9Fe" title="Bundesstraße">Highways</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bundesstra%C3%9Fe_76&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bundesstraße 76 (page does not exist)">76</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bundesstra%C3%9Fe_77&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bundesstraße 77 (page does not exist)">77</a> end in Schleswig and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bundesstra%C3%9Fe_201&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bundesstraße 201 (page does not exist)">B 201</a> runs to the north of the town. <a href="/wiki/Schleswig_station" title="Schleswig station">Schleswig station</a> is a stop for <a href="/wiki/InterCity" title="InterCity">InterCity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Intercity-Express" title="Intercity-Express">Intercity-Express</a> trains and is on the <a href="/wiki/Neum%C3%BCnster%E2%80%93Flensburg_railway" title="Neumünster–Flensburg railway">Hamburg–Neumünster–Flensburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Husum%E2%80%93Kiel_railway" title="Husum–Kiel railway">Husum–Kiel lines</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Schleswig,_Schleswig-Holstein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The climate is humid and maritime. The annual mean temperature is 8 °C and precipitation averages 925 mm.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Schleswig,_Schleswig-Holstein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Schleswig_Braun-Hogenberg.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1545" data-file-width="2249" height="151" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Schleswig_Braun-Hogenberg.jpg/220px-Schleswig_Braun-Hogenberg.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Schleswig_Braun-Hogenberg.jpg/330px-Schleswig_Braun-Hogenberg.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Schleswig_Braun-Hogenberg.jpg/440px-Schleswig_Braun-Hogenberg.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Schleswig_Braun-Hogenberg.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Schleswig in 1600</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viking" title="Viking">Viking</a> settlement of <a href="/wiki/Hedeby" title="Hedeby">Hedeby</a>, located south of the modern town, was first mentioned in 804. It was a powerful settlement in the <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Sea" title="Baltic Sea">Baltic region</a>, dominating the area for more than 200 years. In 1050, following several destructions, the population was moved to the opposite shore of the Schlei, becoming the city of Schleswig. In 1066 Hedeby was finally destroyed, and Schleswig remained as a part of the Danish kingdom.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag needs to be fact-checked with the cited source(s). (June 2007)">verification needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1544 <a href="/wiki/Gottorf_Castle" title="Gottorf Castle">Gottorf Castle</a> became the residence of the local rulers. The dukes of Gottorf were vassals of the Danish kings and ruled over much of present-day Schleswig-Holstein. In 1721, when the <a href="/wiki/Great_Northern_War" title="Great Northern War">Great Northern War</a> ended, the dukes of Gottorf lost their power and their land became Danish crown land. After the <a href="/wiki/Second_Schleswig_War" title="Second Schleswig War">Second Schleswig War</a> (1864), Schleswig was annexed by the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia" title="Kingdom of Prussia">Kingdom of Prussia</a>.</p>
