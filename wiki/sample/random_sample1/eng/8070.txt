<p><b>Risako Sugaya</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">菅谷 梨沙子</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sugaya Risako</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born 4 April 1994 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kanagawa" title="Kanagawa">Kanagawa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>)</span> is a Japanese pop singer, <a href="/wiki/Japanese_idol" title="Japanese idol">idol</a>, and former member of the musical group <a href="/wiki/Berryz_Kobo" title="Berryz Kobo">Berryz Kobo</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Risako_Sugaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2002, Sugaya successfully passed the <a href="/wiki/Hello!_Project_Kids" title="Hello! Project Kids">Hello! Project Kids</a> Audition. Her debut within the <a href="/wiki/Hello!_Project" title="Hello! Project">Hello! Project</a> world came with the premiere of the first <a href="/wiki/Minimoni" title="Minimoni">Minimoni</a> feature film, as part of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=4Kids_(J-pop_group)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="4Kids (J-pop group) (page does not exist)">4Kids</a>, a shuffles group formed exclusively for the movie.</p>
<p>In 2003, Sugaya starred in the movie <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hotaru_no_Hoshi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hotaru no Hoshi (page does not exist)">Hotaru no Hoshi</a></i> (蛍の星), as Hikari, a fragile elementary school student who, with the help of her caring teacher, has a chance to be momentarily reunited with her deceased mother and overcome her emotional problems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> She also starred in the drama, <i>Shonan Kawarayane Monogatari</i>.</p>
<p>That year also marked her first appearance on <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dhaku_Uta_Gassen" title="Kōhaku Uta Gassen">Kōhaku Uta Gassen</a>, as one of <a href="/wiki/Aya_Matsuura" title="Aya Matsuura">Aya Matsuura</a>'s backup dancers, during the 54th edition of the renowned <a href="/wiki/NHK" title="NHK">NHK</a> New Year program.</p>
<p>In early 2004, Sugaya became part of the newly formed group <a href="/wiki/Berryz_Kobo" title="Berryz Kobo">Berryz Kobo</a>. Since joining the group, she has also participated in the band's weekly radio show <i>Berryz Kobo Kiritsu! Rei! Chakuseki!</i>.</p>
<p>She has also, like other Hello! Project members, appeared in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Musume_Dokyu!&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Musume Dokyu! (page does not exist)">Musume Dokyu!</a> segments, namely episodes 39, 40, 54, 55, and 56.</p>
<p>Additionally, Sugaya has done a commercial for the Nihon Shokuniku Shōhi Sōgō Center in October 2003.</p>
<p>In August 2006, Sugaya was interviewed by MC <a href="/wiki/Maki_Goto" title="Maki Goto">Maki Goto</a> in the 12th installment of the online only show <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hello!_Pro_Hour&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hello! Pro Hour (page does not exist)">Hello! Pro Hour</a>. She later re-appeared alongside <a href="/wiki/Momoko_Tsugunaga" title="Momoko Tsugunaga">Momoko Tsugunaga</a> on the 20th and final episode.</p>
<p>On October 12, 2006, Sugaya became the first member of Berryz Kobo, and the youngest of all the Hello! Project members, to release a solo photobook. Even prior to its release, the photobook was met with mixed feelings both by Japanese and overseas fans. Sugaya herself admitted, in an interview with Sanspo.com, to initially having doubts regarding the photoshoot, but claims to have had a pleasant experience and hopes her fans enjoy the various facets of her personality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 31, 2006, Sugaya once again took the stage at the <a href="/wiki/57th_NHK_K%C5%8Dhaku_Uta_Gassen" title="57th NHK Kōhaku Uta Gassen">57th NHK Kōhaku Uta Gassen</a> as a backup dancer in <a href="/wiki/Morning_Musume" title="Morning Musume">Morning Musume</a>'s performance of <i><a href="/wiki/Aruiteru" title="Aruiteru">Aruiteru</a></i>, along with the remaining members of Berryz Kobo, <a href="/wiki/Country_Musume" title="Country Musume">Country Musume</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cute_(Japanese_idol_group)" title="Cute (Japanese idol group)">Cute</a>.</p>
<p>In mid-2009, Sugaya became a member of new unit <a href="/wiki/Guardians_4" title="Guardians 4">Guardians 4</a>.</p>
<p>On August 2, 2014, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Berryz_Kobo" title="Berryz Kobo">Berryz Kobo</a> would be going on an indefinite hiatus from the 3rd of March, 2015. Further, Sugaya announced she would be taking a break from show business following the cessation of group activities.</p>
