<p>A <b>baseball cap</b> is a type of soft <a href="/wiki/Cap" title="Cap">cap</a> with a rounded crown and a stiff peak projecting in front. The front of the cap typically contains designs or <a href="/wiki/Logos" title="Logos">logos</a> of sports teams (namely <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> teams, or names of relevant companies, when used as a commercial marketing technique). The back of the cap may be "fitted" to the wearer's head size or it may have a <a href="/wiki/Plastic" title="Plastic">plastic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Velcro" title="Velcro">Velcro</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Elastomer" title="Elastomer">elastic</a> adjuster so that it can be quickly adjusted to fit different wearers. The baseball cap is a part of the traditional <a href="/wiki/Baseball_uniform" title="Baseball uniform">baseball uniform</a> worn by players, with the brim pointing forward to shield the eyes from the sun. The cap is often seen in everyday casual wear.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baseball_cap&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:1888_Reds.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1296" data-file-width="1784" height="182" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/1888_Reds.jpg/250px-1888_Reds.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/1888_Reds.jpg/375px-1888_Reds.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/1888_Reds.jpg/500px-1888_Reds.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:1888_Reds.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Portrait of <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Reds" title="Cincinnati Reds">Cincinnati Reds</a> players, 1888.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1860, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Excelsiors" title="Brooklyn Excelsiors">Brooklyn Excelsiors</a> wore the ancestor of the modern rounded-top baseball cap, which featured a long peak and a button on top, and by 1900, the "Brooklyn style" cap became popular.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-1">[1]</a></sup> During the 1940s, latex rubber became the stiffening material inside the hat and the modern baseball cap was born. The peak, also known in certain areas as the "bill" or "brim", was designed to protect a player's eyes from the sun. Typically, the peak was much shorter in the earlier days of the baseball hat. Also, the hat has become more structured, versus the overall "floppy" cap of the 19th and early 20th centuries. The baseball cap was and still is an important means by which to identify a team. Often the logo, <a href="/wiki/Mascot" title="Mascot">mascot</a>, or team's initial was placed on the cap. Usually, the cap was also fashioned in the official colors of a particular team.</p>
<p>The basic shape, including curved peak, is similar to some styles of 19th century sun bonnets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bonnet_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-bonnet-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baseball_cap&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fitted baseball caps, those without an adjuster, are normally sewn in six sections, and may be topped with a matching fabric-covered <a href="/wiki/Button" title="Button">button</a> (also called a squatchee) on the crown. Metal grommets or fabric eyelets are often sewn or attached near the top of each of the six sections of fabric to provide ventilation. In some cases, the rear sections of the crown are made of net-like mesh material for extra ventilation. The peak is typically stiffened by a sewn-in piece of <a href="/wiki/Paperboard" title="Paperboard">paperboard</a> or stiff plastic.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Variations">Variations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baseball_cap&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Variations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Baseball caps are made of many types of material and shaped in various styles for different purposes. Major and minor league baseball players wear classic-style caps made of <a href="/wiki/Wool" title="Wool">wool</a> (or, more recently, <a href="/wiki/Polyester" title="Polyester">polyester</a>) with their team's simple logo and colors; the logo is usually <a href="/wiki/Embroidery" title="Embroidery">embroidered</a> into the fabric.</p>
<p>Formerly, baseball caps only came in standard hat sizes. Since 1980, they have also been available in a one-size-fits-all form, with an adjustment strap in the back. The style, commonly called snapback, has become increasingly popular as fashion accessories.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mensfashion_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-mensfashion-3">[3]</a></sup> Advances in textiles have led to the "stretch-fit" hat, which uses Lycra or rubber to allow a hat to have a fitted style while still being "adjustable" within sizes.</p>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Truckerhat.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Trucker cap</div>

</div>
<p>The front may be soft, or may be stiffened by <a href="/wiki/Buckram" title="Buckram">buckram</a> to display a logo more clearly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Another version of the baseball cap is a plastic mesh cap with a foam front imprinted with a company logo. This style is sometimes called a <a href="/wiki/Trucker_hat" title="Trucker hat">trucker cap</a> or a "gimme cap" because it is given away for free as a promotional item.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Athletic_use">Athletic use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baseball_cap&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Athletic use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Paul_Hoover.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="650" data-file-width="728" height="196" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Paul_Hoover.jpg/220px-Paul_Hoover.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Paul_Hoover.jpg/330px-Paul_Hoover.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Paul_Hoover.jpg/440px-Paul_Hoover.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Paul_Hoover.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
In baseball, caps are worn backwards to accommodate for a <a href="/wiki/Catcher#Equipment" title="Catcher">face mask</a> by some catchers and are worn under the mask of umpires.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Beginning with the 2014 season, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MLB" title="MLB">MLB</a> pitchers are permitted to wear a special reinforced cap to protect their heads from line drives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dailynews_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-dailynews-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Athletes in other sports wear caps with their team's logo and colors as "sideline" caps; both types are also sold as authentic team merchandise in retail stores. Other caps may simply have a maker's logo, such as <a href="/wiki/Reebok" title="Reebok">Reebok</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nike,_Inc." title="Nike, Inc.">Nike</a> or <a href="/wiki/Carhartt" title="Carhartt">Carhartt</a>; these hats are often made of brushed <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a>. Golfers tend to prefer the <a href="/wiki/Sports_visor" title="Sports visor">sports visor</a> form which does not cover the head but keeps the sun out of their eyes; women also traditionally have worn visors casually but a trend in certain youth subcultures sees an increase in feminine use of full caps.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_use">Professional use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baseball_cap&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Professional use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Military">Military</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baseball_cap&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Military">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Some armed forces also use baseball caps as part of their uniforms, including the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a>. Used mostly with the utility uniform and coveralls, the baseball cap usually has a command logo on the front to denote command affiliation. Also baseball caps of a particular color are worn to denote a specific function of a person or particular job. Two examples are in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> <a href="/wiki/Submarine" title="Submarine">submarine</a> force, red baseball caps are worn by drill monitors who facilitate and critique members of the boat's crew during drills. Also in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parachute_rigger" title="Parachute rigger">parachute riggers</a> wear red baseball caps and parachute instructors wear black baseball caps as part of their uniform. In various squadrons of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">United States Air Force</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Civil_Air_Patrol" title="Civil Air Patrol">civilian auxiliary</a>, squadron-distinctive baseball caps have been issued as headgear (or "cover") for the <a href="/wiki/Battle_Dress_Uniform" title="Battle Dress Uniform">Battle Dress Uniform</a>, typically displaying squadron colors, squadron number, and/or squadron patch. Though the BDUs have their own cover, a <a href="/wiki/Patrol_cap" title="Patrol cap">patrol cap</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M81_Woodland" title="M81 Woodland">M81 Woodland</a>, some squadrons have opted for more distinctive covers.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Police">Police</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baseball_cap&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Police">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In many United States police forces, the baseball cap is worn as a more practical alternative to the traditional <a href="/wiki/Peaked_cap" title="Peaked cap">peaked cap</a> or <a href="/wiki/Campaign_hat" title="Campaign hat">campaign hat</a>, the latter of which is generally used by <a href="/wiki/Sheriffs_in_the_United_States" title="Sheriffs in the United States">Sheriff's</a> departments and <a href="/wiki/State_police_(United_States)" title="State police (United States)">state police</a> forces. This is more true on the <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="West Coast of the United States">West Coast</a>, whereas in eastern states the traditional peaked cap is more prominent. A notable exception is the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Police_Department" title="San Francisco Police Department">San Francisco Police Department</a>, where peaked caps are still worn regularly. In <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>, policemen on motorcycles wear baseball caps as a part of their uniform, when they remove the helmet. The baseball cap is also commonly used by private security companies as a cheap, practical piece of uniform headgear.</p>
<p>There has been a marked trend in recent years amongst police forces and other uniformed bodies throughout the world to substitute the cheap and comfortable baseball cap for peaked caps and other traditional headdresses. Many armed police units around the world, notably <a href="/wiki/SWAT" title="SWAT">SWAT</a> in the United States and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Police" title="Metropolitan Police">Metropolitan Police</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Specialist_Firearms_Command" title="Specialist Firearms Command">Specialist Firearms Command</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, often wear baseball caps to shield their eyes from the sun where a full helmet would be excessive.</p>
