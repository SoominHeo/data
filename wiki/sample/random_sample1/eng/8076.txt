<p><b>Snowdonia</b> (<a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a>: <span lang="cy" xml:lang="cy"><i>Eryri</i></span>) is a mountainous region in north Wales and a <a href="/wiki/National_park" title="National park">national park</a> of 823 square miles (2,130 km<sup>2</sup>) in area. It was the first to be designated of the three <a href="/wiki/National_parks_of_England_and_Wales" title="National parks of England and Wales">national parks in Wales</a>, in 1951.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name_and_extent">Name and extent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snowdonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name and extent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The English name for the area derives from <a href="/wiki/Snowdon" title="Snowdon">Snowdon</a>, which is the highest mountain in Wales at 3,560 ft (1,085 m). In <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a>, the area is named <i>Eryri</i>. A commonly held belief is that the name is derived from <i>eryr</i> ("eagle"), and thus means 'the abode/land of eagles',<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> but recent evidence is that it means quite simply <i>Highlands</i>, and is related to the Latin <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/oriri" title="wikt:oriri">oriri</a> (to rise)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> as leading Welsh scholar Sir <a href="/wiki/Ifor_Williams" title="Ifor Williams">Ifor Williams</a> proved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The term ‘Eryri’ first appeared in a manuscript in the 9th-century <i><a href="/wiki/Historia_Brittonum" title="Historia Brittonum">Historia Brittonum</a></i>, in an account of the downfall of the semi-legendary 5th-century king Gwrtheyrn (<a href="/wiki/Vortigern" title="Vortigern">Vortigern</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> the title <i>Prince of Wales and Lord of Snowdonia</i> (<i>Tywysog Cymru ac Arglwydd Eryri</i>) was used by <a href="/wiki/Llywelyn_ap_Gruffudd" title="Llywelyn ap Gruffudd">Llywelyn ap Gruffudd</a>; his grandfather <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Llywelyn_Fawr" title="Llywelyn Fawr">Llywelyn Fawr</a> used the title <i>Prince of north Wales and Lord of Snowdonia.</i></p>
<p>Before the boundaries of the national park were designated, "Snowdonia" was generally used to refer to a smaller area, namely the upland area of northern <a href="/wiki/Gwynedd" title="Gwynedd">Gwynedd</a> centred on the <a href="/wiki/Snowdon" title="Snowdon">Snowdon</a> massif, whereas the national park covers an area more than twice that size extending far to the south into <a href="/wiki/Meirionnydd" title="Meirionnydd">Meirionnydd</a>. This is apparent in books published prior to 1951, such as the classic travelogue <i>Wild Wales</i> by <a href="/wiki/George_Borrow" title="George Borrow">George Borrow</a> (1862) and <i>The Mountains of Snowdonia</i> by H. Carr &amp; G. Lister (1925). F. J. North, as editor of the book <i>Snowdonia</i> (1949), states "When the Committee delineated provisional boundaries, they included areas some distance beyond Snowdonia proper." The traditional Snowdonia thus includes the ranges of Snowdon and its satellites, the <a href="/wiki/Glyderau" title="Glyderau">Glyderau</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Carneddau" title="Carneddau">Carneddau</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Moel_Siabod" title="Moel Siabod">Moel Siabod</a> group. It does not include the hills to the south of <a href="/wiki/Maentwrog" title="Maentwrog">Maentwrog</a>. As <i>Eryri</i> (see above), this area has a unique place in Welsh history, tradition and culture.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Snowdonia_National_Park">Snowdonia National Park</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snowdonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Snowdonia National Park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Snowdonia National Park (<a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a>: <span lang="cy" xml:lang="cy"><i>Parc Cenedlaethol Eryri</i></span>) was established in 1951 as the third <a href="/wiki/National_parks_of_England_and_Wales" title="National parks of England and Wales">national park</a> in Britain, following the <a href="/wiki/Peak_District" title="Peak District">Peak District</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Lake_District" title="Lake District">Lake District</a>. It covers 827 square miles (2,140 km<sup>2</sup>), and has 37 miles (60 km) of coastline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The park is governed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Snowdonia_National_Park_Authority" title="Snowdonia National Park Authority">Snowdonia National Park Authority</a>, which is made up of local government and Welsh representatives, and its main offices are at <a href="/wiki/Penrhyndeudraeth" title="Penrhyndeudraeth">Penrhyndeudraeth</a>. Unlike national parks in other countries, Snowdonia (and other such parks in Britain) are made up of both public and private lands under central planning authority. <p>More than 26,000 people live within the park. 58.6% of the population could speak Welsh in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>While most of the land is either open or mountainous land, there is a significant amount of <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agricultural activity</a> within the park.</p>
<p>Since the local government re-organisation of 1998, the park lies partly in the county of <a href="/wiki/Gwynedd" title="Gwynedd">Gwynedd</a>, and partly in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conwy_(county_borough)" title="Conwy (county borough)">county borough of Conwy</a>. It is governed by the 18-member Snowdonia National Park Authority; nine members are appointed by Gwynedd, 3 by Conwy, and the remaining six by the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_for_Wales" title="National Assembly for Wales">National Assembly for Wales</a> to represent the national interest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Unusually, Snowdonia National Park has a hole in the middle, around the town of <a href="/wiki/Blaenau_Ffestiniog" title="Blaenau Ffestiniog">Blaenau Ffestiniog</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Slate" title="Slate">slate</a> <a href="/wiki/Quarry" title="Quarry">quarrying</a> centre. This was deliberately excluded from the park when it was set up to allow the development of new light industry to replace the reduced <a href="/wiki/Slate_industry_in_Wales" title="Slate industry in Wales">slate industry</a>. (There is a similar situation in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peak_District_National_Park" title="Peak District National Park">Peak District National Park</a> where the boundaries were drawn to exclude large built-up areas and industrial sites from the park with the town of Buxton and the adjacent quarries outside but surrounded on three sides by the park.)</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Snowdonia_Society" title="Snowdonia Society">Snowdonia Society</a> is a registered charity formed in 1967. It is a voluntary group of people with an interest in the area and its protection. <a href="/wiki/Amory_Lovins" title="Amory Lovins">Amory Lovins</a> led the successful 1970s opposition to stop <a href="/wiki/Rio_Tinto_Group" title="Rio Tinto Group">Rio Tinto</a> digging up the area for a massive mine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snowdonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Research indicates that there were 3.67 million visitors to Snowdonia National Park in 2013, with approximately 9.74 million tourist days spent in the park during that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Total tourist expenditure was £433.6 million in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mountain_ranges">Mountain ranges</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snowdonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mountain ranges">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Snowdonia may be divided into four areas:</p>
<ul>
<li>The northernmost area is the most popular with tourists, and includes (from west to east) <a href="/wiki/Moel_Hebog" title="Moel Hebog">Moel Hebog</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mynydd_Mawr" title="Mynydd Mawr">Mynydd Mawr</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Nantlle_Ridge" title="Nantlle Ridge">Nantlle Ridge</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Snowdon_Massif" title="Snowdon Massif">Snowdon Massif</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Glyderau" title="Glyderau">Glyderau</a>; and the <a href="/wiki/Carneddau" title="Carneddau">Carneddau</a>. These last three groups are the highest mountains in Wales, and include all Wales' <a href="/wiki/Welsh_3000s" title="Welsh 3000s">3000-foot mountains</a>.</li>
<li>The second area includes peaks such as <a href="/wiki/Moel_Siabod" title="Moel Siabod">Moel Siabod</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cnicht" title="Cnicht">Cnicht</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Moelwynion" title="Moelwynion">Moelwynion</a>, and the mountains around <a href="/wiki/Blaenau_Ffestiniog" title="Blaenau Ffestiniog">Blaenau Ffestiniog</a>.</li>
<li>The third area includes the <a href="/wiki/Rhinogydd" title="Rhinogydd">Rhinogydd</a> in the west as well as the <a href="/wiki/Arenig" title="Arenig">Arenig</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Migneint" title="Migneint">Migneint</a> (this last being an area of bog), and <a href="/wiki/Rhobell_Fawr" title="Rhobell Fawr">Rhobell Fawr</a>. This area is not as popular with tourists as the other areas, due to its remoteness.</li>
<li>The southernmost area includes <a href="/wiki/Cadair_Idris" title="Cadair Idris">Cadair Idris</a>, the Tarren range, the <a href="/wiki/Dyfi_hills" title="Dyfi hills">Dyfi hills</a>, and the Aran group, including <a href="/wiki/Aran_Fawddwy" title="Aran Fawddwy">Aran Fawddwy</a>, the highest mountain in the United Kingdom south of Snowdon.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mountain_walking">Mountain walking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snowdonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mountain walking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Badgernet_Snowdonia_walks_1.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2112" data-file-width="2816" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Badgernet_Snowdonia_walks_1.JPG/220px-Badgernet_Snowdonia_walks_1.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Badgernet_Snowdonia_walks_1.JPG/330px-Badgernet_Snowdonia_walks_1.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Badgernet_Snowdonia_walks_1.JPG/440px-Badgernet_Snowdonia_walks_1.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Badgernet_Snowdonia_walks_1.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Southern edge. Waymarked path near Llyn Barfog in <a href="/wiki/Gwynedd" title="Gwynedd">Gwynedd</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Many of the <a href="/wiki/Hiking" title="Hiking">hikers</a> in the area concentrate on <a href="/wiki/Snowdon" title="Snowdon">Snowdon</a> itself. It is regarded as a fine mountain, but can become quite crowded, particularly with the <a href="/wiki/Snowdon_Mountain_Railway" title="Snowdon Mountain Railway">Snowdon Mountain Railway</a> running to the summit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The other high mountains with their boulder-strewn summits—as well as <a href="/wiki/Tryfan" title="Tryfan">Tryfan</a>, one of the few mountains in the UK south of Scotland whose ascent needs hands as well as feet—are also very popular. However, there are also some spectacular walks in Snowdonia on the lower mountains, and they tend to be relatively unfrequented. Among hikers' favourites are <a href="/wiki/Y_Garn_(Glyderau)" title="Y Garn (Glyderau)">Y Garn</a> (east of Llanberis) along the ridge to <a href="/wiki/Elidir_Fawr" title="Elidir Fawr">Elidir Fawr</a>; Mynydd Tal-y-Mignedd (west of Snowdon) along the <a href="/wiki/Nantlle_Ridge" title="Nantlle Ridge">Nantlle Ridge</a> to Mynydd Drws-y-Coed; <a href="/wiki/Moelwyn_Mawr" title="Moelwyn Mawr">Moelwyn Mawr</a> (west of Blaenau Ffestiniog); and <a href="/wiki/Pen_Llithrig_y_Wrach" title="Pen Llithrig y Wrach">Pen Llithrig y Wrach</a> north of <a href="/wiki/Capel_Curig" title="Capel Curig">Capel Curig</a>. Further south are <a href="/wiki/Y_Llethr" title="Y Llethr">Y Llethr</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Rhinogydd" title="Rhinogydd">Rhinogydd</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cadair_Idris" title="Cadair Idris">Cadair Idris</a> near <a href="/wiki/Dolgellau" title="Dolgellau">Dolgellau</a>.</p>
<p>The park has 1,479 miles (2,380 km) of public footpaths, 164 miles (264 km) of public <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bridleway" title="Bridleway">bridleways</a>, and 46 miles (74 km) of other public rights of way.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> A large part of the park is also covered by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right_to_Roam" title="Right to Roam">Right to Roam</a> laws.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nature.2C_landscape_and_the_environment">Nature, landscape and the environment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snowdonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Nature, landscape and the environment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Rain_coming_in_over_the_lake.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1944" data-file-width="2592" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Rain_coming_in_over_the_lake.jpg/220px-Rain_coming_in_over_the_lake.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Rain_coming_in_over_the_lake.jpg/330px-Rain_coming_in_over_the_lake.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Rain_coming_in_over_the_lake.jpg/440px-Rain_coming_in_over_the_lake.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

</div>

<p>The park's entire coastline is a <a href="/wiki/Special_Area_of_Conservation" title="Special Area of Conservation">Special Area of Conservation</a>, which runs from the <a href="/wiki/Ll%C5%B7n_Peninsula" title="Llŷn Peninsula">Llŷn Peninsula</a> down the mid-Wales coast, the latter containing valuable sand dune systems.</p>
<p>The park's natural forests are of the mixed <a href="/wiki/Deciduous" title="Deciduous">deciduous</a> type, the commonest tree being the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Welsh_oak_(tree)" title="Welsh oak (tree)">Welsh oak</a>. <a href="/wiki/Birch" title="Birch">Birch</a>, ash, mountain-ash and hazel are also common. The park also contains some large (planted) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coniferous" title="Coniferous">coniferous</a> forested areas such as <a href="/wiki/Gwydir_Forest" title="Gwydir Forest">Gwydir Forest</a> near <a href="/wiki/Betws-y-Coed" title="Betws-y-Coed">Betws-y-Coed</a>, although some areas, once harvested, are now increasingly being allowed to regrow naturally.</p>
<p>Northern Snowdonia is the only place in Britain where the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Snowdon_lily" title="Snowdon lily">Snowdon lily</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Arctic%E2%80%93alpine" title="Arctic–alpine">arctic–alpine</a> plant, and the rainbow-coloured <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Snowdon_beetle" title="Snowdon beetle">Snowdon beetle</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Chrysolina_cerealis" title="Chrysolina cerealis">Chrysolina cerealis</a></i>) are found, and the only place in the world where the Snowdonia hawkweed <i><a href="/wiki/Hieracium_snowdoniense" title="Hieracium snowdoniense">Hieracium snowdoniense</a></i> grows.</p>
<p>A large proportion of the park is today under designation (or under consideration for designation) as <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">Sites of Special Scientific Interest</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_nature_reserve_(United_Kingdom)" title="National nature reserve (United Kingdom)">national nature reserves</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Special_Areas_of_Conservation" title="Special Areas of Conservation">Special Areas of Conservation</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Special_Protection_Areas" title="Special Protection Areas">Special Protection Areas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biosphere" title="Biosphere">Biosphere</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ramsar_Convention" title="Ramsar Convention">Ramsar</a> sites.</p>

<p>One of the major problems facing the park in recent years has been the growth of <i><a href="/wiki/Rhododendron_ponticum" title="Rhododendron ponticum">Rhododendron ponticum</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> This fast-growing <a href="/wiki/Invasive_species" title="Invasive species">invasive species</a> has a tendency to take over and stifle native species. It can form massive towering growths and has a companion fungus that grows on its roots producing toxins that are poisonous to any local flora and fauna for a seven-year period after the <i>Rhododendron</i> infestations have been eradicated. As a result, there are a number of desolate landscapes.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wildlife">Wildlife</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snowdonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Wildlife">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Snowdonia's importance in the conservation of habitat and wildlife in the region reflects in the fact that nearly 20% of its total area is protected by UK and European law. Half of that area was set aside by the government under the European Habitats Directive as a Special Area of Conservation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Snowdonia_NP_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Snowdonia_NP-16">[16]</a></sup> Rare mammals in the park include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Otters" title="Otters">otters</a>, <a href="/wiki/European_polecat" title="European polecat">polecats</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Feral_goat" title="Feral goat">feral goat</a>, although the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pine_marten" title="Pine marten">pine marten</a> has not been seen for many years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Rare birds include <a href="/wiki/Raven" title="Raven">raven</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peregrine_falcon" title="Peregrine falcon">peregrine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Osprey" title="Osprey">osprey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Merlin_(bird)" title="Merlin (bird)">merlin</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Red_kite" title="Red kite">red kite</a>. Another of Snowdonia's famous inhabitants is <a href="/wiki/Chrysolina_cerealis" title="Chrysolina cerealis">the Snowdon or rainbow beetle</a>. The park has three RAMSAR Sites: the Dyfi Estuary Biosphere Reserve, Cwm Idwal and Llyn Tegid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Snowdonia_NP_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-Snowdonia_NP-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snowdonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Snowdonia is one of the <a href="/wiki/Climate_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Climate of the United Kingdom">wettest parts of the United Kingdom</a>; <a href="/wiki/Crib_Goch" title="Crib Goch">Crib Goch</a> in Snowdonia is the wettest spot in the United Kingdom, with an average rainfall of 4,473 millimetres (176.1 in) a year over the past 30 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
