<p><i><b>Thrasher</b></i> is a monthly <a href="/wiki/Skateboarding" title="Skateboarding">skateboarding</a> magazine, founded in January 1981 by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kevin_Thatcher&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kevin Thatcher (page does not exist)">Kevin Thatcher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eric_Swenson" title="Eric Swenson">Eric Swenson</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fausto_Vitello" title="Fausto Vitello">Fausto Vitello</a>, and published by High Speed Productions, Inc. of San Francisco, USA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The publication consists primarily of skateboard- and music-related articles, photography, interviews and <a href="/wiki/Skatepark" title="Skatepark">skatepark</a> reviews. The magazine also maintains a website, which includes segments with names such as "Firing Line" and "Hall of Meat", an online store, a video collection, a radio show, and a forum for registered users.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The company also owns and operates the Double Rock indoor skateboarding facility.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Skater_of_the_Year">Skater of the Year</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thrasher_(magazine)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Skater of the Year">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The title of "Skater of the Year" is awarded annually by <i>Thrasher</i> magazine. The tradition was started in 1990, and the accolade remains one of the most respected awards in global skateboarding culture. The title is bestowed to one skater annually, based on a public vote, and announced by thrasher's editor, Jake Phelps. Chris Cole, and Dann4 y Way are the only double recipients.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="King_of_the_Road">King of the Road</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thrasher_(magazine)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: King of the Road">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Road_(title)" title="King of the Road (title)">King of the Road (title)</a></div>
<p>In 2003, <i>Thrasher</i> started the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_of_the_Road_(skateboarding)" title="King of the Road (skateboarding)">King of the Road</a> (KOTR) skateboarding competition. In the KOTR contest, a group of pre-invited teams of professional skaters are each given a booklet containing a series of challenges. Points are awarded at the completion of each challenge. The teams compete at the same time over a two-week period, in which they travel across the U.S. to complete as many challenges as possible.</p>
<p>KOTR has been held annually since its inception, with the exceptions of 2008 and 2009. In 2011, <i>Thrasher</i> and <a href="/wiki/Converse_(shoe_company)" title="Converse (shoe company)">Converse</a> hosted the competition in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, with the participation of the four biggest Chinese skateboard deck companies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Skate_Rock">Skate Rock</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thrasher_(magazine)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Skate Rock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Thrasher</i> released a music compilation series titled "Skate Rock" under the High Speed Productions label. The first release was in 1983, with <i>Volume One</i>, and continued until <i>Volume Seven</i> in 1989. The series started out featuring <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">Californian</a> <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk rock</a> bands, including bands fronted by skateboarders, such as <a href="/wiki/Steve_Caballero" title="Steve Caballero">Steve Caballero</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Alva" title="Tony Alva">Tony Alva</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Klaus_Grabke&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Klaus Grabke (page does not exist)">Klaus Grabke</a> under the name <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Eight_Dayz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Eight Dayz (page does not exist)">Eight Dayz</a>.</p>
<p>Of the seven volumes of Skate Rock, all volumes were produced as a cassette available through <i>Thrasher Magazine</i>. Some volumes were also available as vinyl records.</p>
