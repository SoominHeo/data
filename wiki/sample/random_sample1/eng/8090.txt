<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Smaky</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>Smaky</b> is a line of mostly 8-bit <a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">personal computers</a> and accompanying operating system developed by Professor <a href="/wiki/Jean-Daniel_Nicoud" title="Jean-Daniel Nicoud">Jean-Daniel Nicoud</a> and others at the <a href="/wiki/%C3%89cole_Polytechnique_F%C3%A9d%C3%A9rale_de_Lausanne" title="École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne">École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne</a> in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> beginning in 1974. The computers were used at École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne and in Swiss schools. The names derives from SMArt KeYboard, reflecting the form factor that contained a compact <a href="/wiki/Motherboard" title="Motherboard">motherboard</a> which fit within the same housing as the <a href="/wiki/Computer_keyboard" title="Computer keyboard">keyboard</a>.</p>
<p>The first three models, Smaky 1, Smaky 2, and Smaky 4, were based on the <a href="/wiki/Intel_8080" title="Intel 8080">Intel 8080</a> microprocessor (Smaky 3 was a prototype that was never completed). In 1978 the Smaky switched to using the 8-bit <a href="/wiki/Zilog_Z80" title="Zilog Z80">Zilog Z80</a> processor. A portable Smaky was made during this period, which resembled <a href="/wiki/Osborne_1" title="Osborne 1">Osborne</a> portable computers. In 1981 the platform again changed to the 32-bit <a href="/wiki/Motorola_68000" title="Motorola 68000">Motorola 68000</a> processor. A new operating system was written, called <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Psos&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Psos (page does not exist)">Psos</a>, which was developed specifically for the Smaky, and used on all subsequent models.</p>
<p>Development work on the Smaky hardware ended in 1995. A Smaky emulator for <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Microsoft Windows</a> was developed in 2000.</p>
