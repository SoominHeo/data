<p><b>Smolyan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a>: <span lang="bg">Смолян</span>) <a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_and_towns_in_Bulgaria" title="List of cities and towns in Bulgaria">is a town</a> and ski resort in the far south of <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a> near the border with <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>. It is the administrative and industrial centre of the homonymous <a href="/wiki/Smolyan_Province" title="Smolyan Province">Smolyan Province</a>. The town is situated in the valley of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cherna&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cherna (page does not exist)">Cherna</a> ("Black") and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Byala_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Byala River (page does not exist)">Byala</a> ("White") Rivers in the central <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhodopes" title="Rhodopes">Rhodopes</a> at the foot of the <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountains</a>' highest part south of the popular <a href="/wiki/Ski" title="Ski">ski</a> resorts <a href="/wiki/Pamporovo" title="Pamporovo">Pamporovo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chepelare" title="Chepelare">Chepelare</a>. As of February 2011, it has a population of 30,283 inhabitants.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Smolyan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town comes from the local Slavic tribe of the <a href="/wiki/Smolyani" title="Smolyani">Smolyani</a>, probably cognate to the Slavic word <i>smola</i> ("resin").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smolyan_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-smolyan-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Smolyan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to archaeological evidence, the area around Smolyan was first settled in the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium_BC" title="2nd millennium BC">2nd</a>-1st millennium BC. In the Middle Ages it acquired its name from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slavic_peoples" title="Slavic peoples">Slavic</a> tribe, the <i><a href="/wiki/Smolyani" title="Smolyani">Smolyani</a></i>, who settled in the region in the 7th century. During the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>, it was ruled by the Part of the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine</a> and <a href="/wiki/First_Bulgarian_Empire" title="First Bulgarian Empire">Bulgarian Empires</a>. For a while during the 14th century it came under the control of the Bulgarian feudal lord <a href="/wiki/Momchil" title="Momchil">Momchil</a>, alongside the whole <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhodope_mountains" title="Rhodope mountains">Rhodope mountains</a>, before eventually being subjugated by the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>. Smolyan remained under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_of_Ottoman_Bulgaria" title="History of Ottoman Bulgaria">Ottoman rule</a> for five centuries, a township of the Ottoman <a href="/wiki/Sanjak_of_G%C3%BCm%C3%BClcine" title="Sanjak of Gümülcine">Sanjak of Gümülcine</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Adrianople_Vilayet" title="Adrianople Vilayet">Adrianople Vilayet</a> between 1867 and 1912. It was known in <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Turkish_language" title="Ottoman Turkish language">Ottoman Turkish</a> as <i>Paşmaklı</i> or <i>Ahiçelebi</i>.</p>
<p>The area was liberated by the 21st <a href="/wiki/Sredna_Gora" title="Sredna Gora">Sredna Gora</a> Regiment led by Vladimir Serafimov in 1912, during the <a href="/wiki/First_Balkan_War" title="First Balkan War">First Balkan War</a>. The modern town of Smolyan was formed by the merger of three existing villages — Ustovo, Raykovo and Ezerovo — in 1960.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ethnic_linguistic_and_religious_composition">Ethnic linguistic and religious composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Smolyan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ethnic linguistic and religious composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the latest 2011 census data, the individuals declared their ethnic identity were distributed as follows:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bulgarians" title="Bulgarians">Bulgarians</a>: 25,045 (97.0%)</li>

<li>Others: 120 (0.5%)</li>
<li>Indefinable: 248 (1.0%)
<ul>
<li>Undeclared: 4,818 (15.7%)</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Total: 30,642</p>
<p>In <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Smolyan_Municipality&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Smolyan Municipality (page does not exist)">Smolyan Municipality</a> 32708 declared as Bulgarians, 301 as Gypsies and 170 as Turks and 9,000 did not declare their ethnic group.</p>
<p>In 1912, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Aha_Celebi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Aha Celebi (page does not exist)">Aha Celebi</a> said that 8,252 families already live, including 2,815 of <a href="/wiki/Bulgarians" title="Bulgarians">Bulgarians</a> Exarchists, 140 of Bulgarians Patriarchists and 5,297 of <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_Muslims" title="Bulgarian Muslims">Bulgarian Muslims</a> or <a href="/wiki/Pomaks" title="Pomaks">Pomaks</a>. (<a href="/wiki/Lyubomir_Miletich" title="Lyubomir Miletich">Lyubomir Miletich</a>, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Destruction_of_Thracian_Bulgarians_in_1913" title="The Destruction of Thracian Bulgarians in 1913">The Destruction of Thracian Bulgarians in 1913</a>") In 1912, in the town live 320 families of Bulgarians Muslims, 80 of Bulgarians Exarchists and 40 Patriarchists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ustuvo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ustuvo (page does not exist)">Ustuvo</a> live 420 families Bulgarians Exarchists, in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Raykovo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Raykovo (page does not exist)">Raykovo</a> - a total of 700 families of Bulgarians Exarchists, 100 of Bulgarians Patriarchists и 70 of Bulgarian Muslims. Currently the religion of the vast majority is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Christianity" title="Eastern Orthodox Christianity">Eastern Orthodox Christianity</a>, the rest are mostly the Muslim minority.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_and_sports">Culture and sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Smolyan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Culture and sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Due to its suitable location on top of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mount_Rozhen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mount Rozhen (page does not exist)">Mount Rozhen</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bulgarian_National_Astronomical_Observatory" title="Bulgarian National Astronomical Observatory">Bulgarian National Astronomical Observatory</a> is located nearby, with a <a href="/wiki/Planetarium" title="Planetarium">planetarium</a> in operation in the town. There is one theatre, the Rhodope Drama Theatre, and a gathering of theatre practitioners and scholars known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Rhodopi_International_Theatre_Laboratory" title="The Rhodopi International Theatre Laboratory">The Rhodopi International Theatre Laboratory</a> is held every summer. There is also a regional historical museum founded in 1935.</p>
<p>Smolyan has an elite division football team, <a href="/wiki/PFC_Rodopa_Smolyan" title="PFC Rodopa Smolyan">PFC Rodopa Smolyan</a>, that had been playing in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Professional_Football_Group" title="A Professional Football Group">A Professional Football Group</a> between 2003-2007.</p>
<p>The largest church in southern Bulgaria, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cathedral_of_Saint_Vissarion_of_Smolyan" title="Cathedral of Saint Vissarion of Smolyan">Cathedral of Saint Vissarion of Smolyan</a>, was inaugurated in the city in July 2006.</p>
