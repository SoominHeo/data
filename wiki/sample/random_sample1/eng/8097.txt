<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cuisine_of_Denmark" title="Cuisine of Denmark">Cuisine of Denmark</a></div>
<p><b>Smørrebrød</b> (<small>Danish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Danish" title="Help:IPA for Danish">[ˈsmɶɐ̯ɐˌb̥ʁœðˀ]</a></span>; originally <i>smør og brød</i>, "butter and bread") usually consists of a piece of buttered rye bread (<i><a href="/wiki/Rugbr%C3%B8d" title="Rugbrød">rugbrød</a></i>), a dense, dark <a href="/wiki/Brown_bread" title="Brown bread">brown bread</a>. <i>Pålæg</i> (literally "on-lay"), the topping, that among others can refer to commercial or homemade <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cold_cut" title="Cold cut">cold cuts</a>, pieces of <a href="/wiki/Meat" title="Meat">meat</a> or <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cheese" title="Cheese">cheese</a> or <a href="/wiki/Spread_(food)" title="Spread (food)">spreads</a>. This daily practice is the base of the famous Danish <a href="/wiki/Open_sandwich" title="Open sandwich">open sandwich</a>, <i>smørrebrød</i>: a slice or two of <i>pålæg</i> is placed on the buttered bread, and then <i>pyntet</i> (decorated) with the right accompaniments, to create a tasty and visually appealing food item.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bread">Bread</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sm%C3%B8rrebr%C3%B8d&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Bread">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bread is a very important part of the Scandinavian table, primarily <i><a href="/wiki/Rugbr%C3%B8d" title="Rugbrød">rugbrød</a></i>, which is sour-dough rye bread. It is a dark, heavy bread which is often bought pre-sliced, in varieties from light-coloured rye, to very dark, and refined to whole grain. It forms the basis of <i>smørrebrød</i>, which is closely related to the Swedish <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sm%C3%B6rg%C3%A5s" title="Smörgås">smörgås</a></i>. Some toppings are served on <i>franskbrød</i>, a very light, crusty wheat bread. The bread is usually buttered, though for some variants a spread of lard is customary.</p>

<p>Traditional toppings include <i>marinerede sild</i>, which are pickled herrings (plain, <i>krydret</i> – spiced, or <i>karry</i> – curried), slightly sweeter than Dutch or German herrings; thinly sliced cheese in many varieties; sliced cucumber, tomato and boiled eggs; <i>leverpostej</i>, which is pork liver-paste; dozens of types of cured or processed meat in thin slices, or smoked fish such as salmon; mackerel in tomato sauce; pickled cucumber; boiled egg, and rings of red onion. Mayonnaise mixed with peas, sliced boiled asparagus and diced carrot, called <i>italiensk salat</i> (lit. Italian salad, so named because the colours match the Italian flag), remoulade or other thick sauces often top the layered open sandwich, which is usually eaten with utensils. It is custom to pass the dish of sliced breads around the table, and then to pass around each dish of toppings, and people help themselves.</p>
<p>More festive meals can be loosely divided into courses: Fish toppings first (such as herring, shrimp, or smoked salmon) followed by cold cuts and salads, and finally cheese with bread or crackers and a little fruit. One or several warm dishes are often served with the meats on special occasions: For example breaded plaice filet, fried <a href="/wiki/Medisterp%C3%B8lse" title="Medisterpølse"><i>medister</i></a> sausage, <i><a href="/wiki/Frikadeller" title="Frikadeller">frikadeller</a></i> with pickled red cabbage, or <i>mørbradbøf</i> (pork tenderloin with sauteed onions or a creamy mushroom sauce). Toppings change with the seasons and some are mostly associated with Easter or Christmas lunches, like <i>sylte</i> (<a href="/wiki/Head_cheese" title="Head cheese">head cheese</a>) and <i>æbleflæsk</i> (roast pork or bacon in apple sauce). Summer offers lighter fare such as smoked mackerel, <i>sommersalat</i> (radish and cucumber in a smoked cheese dressing), new potatoes, and freshly peeled shrimp.</p>
<p>Hundreds of combinations and varieties of <i>smørrebrød</i> are available, and some traditional examples include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dyrl%C3%A6gens_natmad" title="Dyrlægens natmad">Dyrlægens natmad</a> (Danish: Veterinarian's midnight snack) — On a piece of dark rye bread, a layer of liver pâté (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leverpostej" title="Leverpostej">leverpostej</a></i>), topped with a slice of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salt_beef" title="Salt beef">salt beef</a> (<i>salt kød</i>) and a slice of meat <a href="/wiki/Aspic" title="Aspic">aspic</a> (<i>sky</i>). This is all decorated with raw <a href="/wiki/Onion" title="Onion">onion</a> rings and <a href="/wiki/Garden_cress" title="Garden cress">garden cress</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Eel" title="Eel">Eel</a> — Smoked eel on dark rye bread, topped with scrambled eggs and sliced <a href="/wiki/Radish" title="Radish">radishes</a> or chopped <a href="/wiki/Chives" title="Chives">chives</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leverpostej" title="Leverpostej">Leverpostej</a> — Warm rough-chopped liverpaste served on dark rye bread, topped with <a href="/wiki/Bacon" title="Bacon">bacon</a>, and sauteed <a href="/wiki/Mushroom" title="Mushroom">mushrooms</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Roast_beef" title="Roast beef">Roast beef</a> — thin sliced and served on dark rye bread, topped with a portion of <a href="/wiki/Remoulade" title="Remoulade">remoulade</a>, and decorated with a sprinkling of shredded <a href="/wiki/Horseradish" title="Horseradish">horseradish</a> and toasted (<i>ristet</i>) onion.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fl%C3%A6skesteg" title="Flæskesteg">Roast pork</a> (<i>Ribbensteg</i>) — thin sliced and served on dark rye bread, topped with red sweet and sour <a href="/wiki/Cabbage" title="Cabbage">cabbage</a>, and decorated with a slice of <a href="/wiki/Orange_(fruit)" title="Orange (fruit)">orange</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spiced_meat_roll" title="Spiced meat roll">Spiced meat roll</a> (<i>Rullepølse</i>) -- thin sliced and topped with meat aspic, raw onion rings and garden cress.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Steak_tartare" title="Steak tartare">Tartar</a> — raw lean beef mince with salt and pepper, served on dark rye bread, topped with raw onion rings, grated horseradish and a raw egg yolk.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Smoked_salmon" title="Smoked salmon">Smoked salmon</a> (<i>laks</i>) — Slices of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cold_smoking" title="Cold smoking">cold smoked</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cured_salmon" title="Cured salmon">cured salmon</a> (gravad laks) on white bread, topped with <a href="/wiki/Shrimp" title="Shrimp">shrimp</a> and decorated with a slice of <a href="/wiki/Lemon" title="Lemon">lemon</a> and fresh <a href="/wiki/Dill" title="Dill">dill</a>.</li>
<li>Stjerneskud (Danish: Shooting Star) — On a base of buttered white bread, two pieces of <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a>: a piece of steamed white fish on one half, a piece of fried, battered plaice (<i>rødspætte</i>) on the other half. On top is piled a mound of shrimp, which is then decorated with a dollop of <a href="/wiki/Mayonnaise" title="Mayonnaise">mayonnaise</a>, red <a href="/wiki/Caviar" title="Caviar">caviar</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Lemon" title="Lemon">lemon</a> slice.</li>
</ul>
<p>A lavish piece of restaurant smørrebrød can almost be a meal unto itself, whereas everyday toppings are much simpler and often ungarnished.</p>
