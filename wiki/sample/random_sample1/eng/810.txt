<p>The <b>BMW M4</b> is a high-performance version of the <a href="/wiki/BMW_4_Series" title="BMW 4 Series">BMW 4 Series</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automobile" title="Automobile">automobile</a> developed by <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Motorsport" title="Motorsport">motorsport</a> division, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BMW_M_GmbH" title="BMW M GmbH">BMW M GmbH</a>. Part of the renumbering that splits the <a href="/wiki/BMW_3_Series" title="BMW 3 Series">3 Series</a> coupe and convertible models to the 4 Series (to further differentiate it from the 3 Series), the M4 replaced the <a href="/wiki/BMW_M3" title="BMW M3">M3 Coupe and Convertible</a> models. Upgrades over the standard BMW 4-Series include: a very powerful and responsive, twin turbocharged engine; improved handling, suspension, and braking systems; aerodynamic body enhancements; interior/exterior accents with the tri-colour "M" (Motorsport) badging; the significantly reduced weight compared to the standard 4 series and its predecessor with an all-new stiffer <a href="/wiki/Chassis" title="Chassis">chassis</a> and extensive use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon_fiber" title="Carbon fiber">carbon fiber</a>, especially the roof of the car.</p>

<p>On September 25, 2013, BMW released technical specifications of the M4 powered by a M-tuned S55B30 engine, although it is a 3.0 L inline engine. BMW states that it is completely different to the N55 engine seen in other BMW 35i models, this engine has been built specifically for the New M4/M3, displacing 3.0 L (180 cu in) and redlining at 7,500 rpm. The rev limiter coming in at 7,600rpm. The engine uses two mono-scroll <a href="/wiki/Turbocharger" title="Turbocharger">turbochargers</a> with boost peaking at 18.1 PSI. The power is rated at 425 hp (317 kW; 431 PS) while torque will be 550 N·m (410 lb·ft). The weight of the 6 speed manual M4 is 3,556 lb (1,613 kg), with the M-DCT some 88 lb (40 kg) heavier. Both 6-speed manual and 7-speed M-DCT transmissions are available. The 7-speed M-DCT transmission accelerates the car from 0 to 100 km/h (62 mph) in 4.1 seconds). The 6-speed manual transmission does 0 to 100 km/h (62 mph) in 4.3 seconds).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon_fiber_reinforced_plastic" title="Carbon fiber reinforced plastic">Carbon fiber reinforced plastic</a> is used extensively throughout the car, including drive shaft, roof, roof bow, trunk, and front strut brace. For the first time in the M vehicle, an electric power steering unit is being used. The steering system is specifically tuned for the M3 and M4. The 18 inches (460 mm) and 19 inches (480 mm) wheel options are available with lightweight forged alloys being standard. The M compound brakes come standard (in blue), while carbon ceramic brakes (in gold) are available as an option.</p>
<p>The M4 features Active Sound. A live amplification of the engine's natural sound will be inducted into the passenger cabin via the speakers. BMW says this technology has been used so that the well insulated cabin can reduce road/wind noise but still provide the driver with the sporty sound of the M powered engine. There are no artificial sound or any pre-recorded track.</p>
<p>Overall, 50 percent of the components on the M4 are standalone compared to the <a href="/wiki/BMW_4_Series_(F32)" title="BMW 4 Series (F32)">F32 4 Series</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="F83_M4">F83 M4</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BMW_M4&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: F83 M4">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The convertible was announced along with its coupe sibling. It shares almost everything with the coupe version, but weighs more due to its retractable hardtop. The convertible weighs 1,750 kilograms (3,858 lb) (manual), 60 kilograms (132 lb) less than its predecessor. The three-piece retractable hardtop folds in 20 seconds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autoweek_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-autoweek-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Because of its extra weight it accelerates slower, taking it 0.3s longer to 100 km/h (62 mph), 4.6s with the manual and 4.3s with the M-DCT transmission.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Competition_Package">Competition Package</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BMW_M4&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Competition Package">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During February 2016, BMW announced the M3/M4 Competition Package. With this package, the M4 boasts 444 horsepower and a revised suspension for better handling. New springs, dampers and anti-roll bars complement the included Adaptive M Suspension. BMW also re-tuned the electronic differential and the Dynamic Stability Control to match the upgraded hardware. The interior remains largely unchanged, but Competition Package cars get new lightweight sport seats along with the M-striped woven seat belts. The exterior include the M Sport exhaust with black chrome tailpipes and high gloss Shadow Line exterior trim. Gloss black trim are added to the kidney grille, side gills, and model badge on the trunk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-motorauthority1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-motorauthority1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>With the competition package the Coupe version goes from standstill to 100 km/h (62 mph) in 4.0s, while the convertible version does the run in 4.2s, both using the dual clutch transmission.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-motorauthority1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-motorauthority1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Competition package costs $4,750 for the M3/M4 and $4,250 for the M4 convertible on top of its base price.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-motorauthority1_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-motorauthority1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="F82_M4_GTS">F82 M4 GTS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BMW_M4&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: F82 M4 GTS">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:M4gts-pb-001_0.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="596" data-file-width="900" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/M4gts-pb-001_0.jpg/220px-M4gts-pb-001_0.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/M4gts-pb-001_0.jpg/330px-M4gts-pb-001_0.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/M4gts-pb-001_0.jpg/440px-M4gts-pb-001_0.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:M4gts-pb-001_0.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
BMW M4 GTS</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>BMW introduced the M4 GTS concept in August 2015 at the <a href="/wiki/Pebble_Beach_Concours_d%27Elegance" title="Pebble Beach Concours d'Elegance">Pebble Beach Concours d'Elegance</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-roundel201510_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-roundel201510-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2016, BMW released track-focused version of the standard M4 coupe and will be limited to 700 units with price tag of $133,205. It is powered by the same 3.0-litre twin-turbo straight-six engine as in the normal M4, but the power has been raised to 500 PS (368 kW; 493 bhp), largely due to a novel <a href="/wiki/Water_injection_(engine)" title="Water injection (engine)">water injection</a> system that is the first to be used on a production automobile in almost twenty years. In addition to the increased engine power, the M4 GTS is 27 kg (60 lb) lighter than the standard M4 Coupé with the DCT transmission, so the weight now stands at 1,510 kg (3,330 lb). The 0 to 100 km/h (62 mph) is reduced to just 3.8 seconds, while the top speed is 305 km/h (190 mph). The M4 GTS has, according to BMW, lapped the Nürburgring Nordschleife in 7min 28sec, 24 seconds faster than the base M4, 20 seconds faster than the M3 GTS and the same time as a <a href="/wiki/Porsche_Carrera_GT" title="Porsche Carrera GT">Porsche Carrera GT</a>.</p>

<p>Four teams used M4s in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2014_Deutsche_Tourenwagen_Masters_season" title="2014 Deutsche Tourenwagen Masters season">2014 DTM season</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Marco_Wittmann" title="Marco Wittmann">Marco Wittmann</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maxime_Martin" title="Maxime Martin">Maxime Martin</a> winning five races between them.</p>
