<p><b>Sumida</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">墨田区</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sumida-ku</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, "Field of Ink")</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Special_wards_of_Tokyo" title="Special wards of Tokyo">special ward</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo Metropolis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. The English translation of its Japanese self-designation is <b>Sumida City</b>.</p>
<p>As of May 1, 2015, the ward has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 257,300 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 18,690 persons per km². The total area is 13.77 km².</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sumida,_Tokyo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sumida is in the northeastern part of the mainland portion of Tokyo. The Sumida and Arakawa are the major rivers, and form parts of its boundaries. Its neighbors are all special wards: <a href="/wiki/Adachi,_Tokyo" title="Adachi, Tokyo">Adachi</a> to the north; <a href="/wiki/Arakawa,_Tokyo" title="Arakawa, Tokyo">Arakawa</a> to the northwest; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Katsushika,_Tokyo" title="Katsushika, Tokyo">Katsushika</a> to the east; <a href="/wiki/Edogawa,_Tokyo" title="Edogawa, Tokyo">Edogawa</a> to the southeast; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tait%C5%8D,_Tokyo" title="Taitō, Tokyo">Taitō</a> to the west; <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%AB%C5%8D,_Tokyo" title="Chūō, Tokyo">Chūō</a> to the southwest; and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dt%C5%8D,_Tokyo" title="Kōtō, Tokyo">Kōtō</a> to the south.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Landmarks">Landmarks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sumida,_Tokyo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Landmarks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Skytree" title="Tokyo Skytree">Tokyo Skytree</a>: A <a href="/wiki/Digital_terrestrial_television" title="Digital terrestrial television">digital terrestrial television</a> broadcasting tower used by <a href="/wiki/NHK" title="NHK">NHK</a> and other broadcasters. It is the tallest freestanding tower in the world and the tallest man-made structure in Japan.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ry%C5%8Dgoku_Kokugikan" title="Ryōgoku Kokugikan">Ryōgoku Kokugikan</a> (National Sumo Stadium)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Edo-Tokyo_Museum" title="Edo-Tokyo Museum">Edo-Tokyo Museum</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Asahi_Breweries" title="Asahi Breweries">Asahi Breweries</a> Headquarters: The <a href="/wiki/Asahi_Beer_Hall" title="Asahi Beer Hall">Asahi Beer Hall</a> with the <i>Asahi flame</i> created by French designer <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Starck" title="Philippe Starck">Philippe Starck</a> in 1989, is one of Tokyo's most recognizable modern structures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ek%C5%8D-in" title="Ekō-in">Eko-in</a>: <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhist</a> temple</li>
<li>Honjo Matsuzaka-cho Park: the residence of <a href="/wiki/Kira_Yoshinaka" title="Kira Yoshinaka">Kira Yoshinaka</a> stood on this site. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forty-seven_Ronin" title="Forty-seven Ronin">Forty-seven Ronin</a> took his life during the <a href="/wiki/Genroku" title="Genroku">Genroku</a> era.</li>
<li>Hokusai-dori (<i>street</i>), with a series of prints by famed Japanese artist <a href="/wiki/Hokusai" title="Hokusai">Hokusai</a> who was born in the Kamezawa area of Sumida.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sumida_Triphony_Hall" title="Sumida Triphony Hall">Sumida Triphony Hall</a>, concert hall</li>
<li>Tobu Museum</li>
<li>Tokyo Irei-do (Tokyo Memorial Hall): a memorial to those unidentified people who died in the <a href="/wiki/1923_Great_Kant%C5%8D_earthquake" title="1923 Great Kantō earthquake">Great Kantō earthquake</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bombing_of_Tokyo_in_World_War_II" title="Bombing of Tokyo in World War II">Bombing of Tokyo in World War II</a> and other catastrophes; by <a href="/wiki/It%C5%8D_Ch%C5%ABta" title="Itō Chūta">Itō Chūta</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Yokoamicho_Park" title="Yokoamicho Park">Yokoamicho Park</a>, in the Yokoami district</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Places">Places</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sumida,_Tokyo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Places">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>In the north (the former Mukojima Ward): Sumida, Tsutsumi-dori, Higashi Sumida, Yahiro, Higashi Mukojima, Tachibana, Bunka, Kyojima, Oshiage</li>
<li>In the center (former Honjo Ward): Azuma-bashi, Higashi Komagata, Honjo, Narihira, Yokokawa</li>
<li>In the south (former Honjo Ward): Yokoami, Ryogoku, Chitose, Ishiwara, Kamezawa, Midori, Tatekawa, Kikukawa, Taihei, Kinshi, Koto-bashi</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sumida,_Tokyo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The ward was founded on March 15, 1947. It was previously the (ordinary) wards Honjo and Mukojima. Mukojima, formed in 1932, contained the former town of Sumida, which along with the river gave the ward its name.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Companies">Companies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sumida,_Tokyo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Companies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Asahi_Breweries" title="Asahi Breweries">Asahi Breweries</a> has its headquarters in Azuma-bashi.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Japan_Tobacco" title="Japan Tobacco">Japan Tobacco</a> has a plant in Yokokawa.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Keisei_Electric_Railway" title="Keisei Electric Railway">Keisei Electric Railway</a> has its headquarters in Oshiage.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lion_Corporation" title="Lion Corporation">Lion Corporation</a>, the detergent and toiletries giant, has its home office in Honjo.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tobu_Railway" title="Tobu Railway">Tobu Railway</a> has its headquarters in Oshiage.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sumida,_Tokyo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
