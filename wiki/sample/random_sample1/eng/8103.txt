<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Sumiyoshi-ku, Osaka</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><b>Sumiyoshi-ku</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">住吉区</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is one of 24 <a href="/wiki/Wards_of_Japan" title="Wards of Japan">wards</a> of <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It is located on the southern part of the <a href="/wiki/Uemachi_Plateau" title="Uemachi Plateau">Uemachi Plateau</a>, in the southernmost part of Osaka City, and is separated from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sakai_City" title="Sakai City">Sakai City</a>'s Sakai-ku and Kita-ku by the Yamato River. There are six rail lines, and three main thoroughfares - Abiko-Suji and Abeno-Suji, which run north-south through the centre of the ward - Abeno-Suji, continues north through the area of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tennoji" title="Tennoji">Tennoji</a> - and Nagai Koen-Dori, which runs east-west and connects the area with the port to the west. The northern part of Sumiyoshi-ku is a residential area which is a continuation of the southern part of <a href="/wiki/Abeno-ku,_Osaka" title="Abeno-ku, Osaka">Abeno-ku</a>. The Tezukayama 1-Chome neighbourhood in Abeno-ku, and Tezukayama-naka and Tezukayama-nishi neighbourhoods in Sumiyoshi-ku are upper-class residential areas. South of this, around the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sumiyoshi_Taisha" title="Sumiyoshi Taisha">Sumiyoshi Grand Shrine</a>, are the Sumiyoshi and Kamisumiyoshi neighbourhoods, home to many long established local families. Surrounding these are the middle-class residential neighbourhoods of Shimizugaoka, Suminoe, Oriono, Minamisumiyoshi, Yamanouchi, and Nagai, which lies at the eastern end of the ward. Sumiyoshi-ku is home to about 157,000 residents, and has a population density of 16,800 people per square kilometer.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Area_history">Area history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sumiyoshi-ku,_Osaka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Area history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sumiyoshi-taisya_seimon.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="408" data-file-width="520" height="173" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Sumiyoshi-taisya_seimon.jpg/220px-Sumiyoshi-taisya_seimon.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Sumiyoshi-taisya_seimon.jpg/330px-Sumiyoshi-taisya_seimon.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Sumiyoshi-taisya_seimon.jpg/440px-Sumiyoshi-taisya_seimon.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sumiyoshi-taisya_seimon.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sumiyoshi_Taisha" title="Sumiyoshi Taisha">Sumiyoshi Taisha</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In ancient times, the <i><a href="/wiki/Kanji" title="Kanji">kanji</a></i> combination for the current day Sumiyoshi, 住吉, was pronounced <i>Suminoe</i>, and even appeared in <a href="/wiki/Man%27y%C5%8Dsh%C5%AB" title="Man'yōshū">Man'yōshū</a> (8th-century Japanese poetry). At present, Sumiyoshi, Suminoe, and Sumie represent different area names.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Suminoe_no_Tsu" title="Suminoe no Tsu">Suminoe no Tsu</a> (Suminoe Port), which in ancient times was located south of Sumiyoshi Grand Shrine, was home to Japan's first international port, and until Naniwa no Tsu (Naniwa Port) was constructed, it was the country's international point of contact. The Japanese envoy to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sui_Dynasty" title="Sui Dynasty">Sui</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tang_Dynasty" title="Tang Dynasty">T'ang</a> Dynasties of <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> departed there, and it was also Japan's portal to the <a href="/wiki/Silk_Road" title="Silk Road">Silk Road</a>. Importantly, <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhism</a> was also introduced to Japan (via China and Korea) through this port.</p>
<p>In the Middle Ages, the throne of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Go-Murakami" title="Emperor Go-Murakami">Emperor Go-Murakami</a> of the Southern Court was placed in the house of the chief priest of Sumiyoshi Grand Shrine for ten years. It was known as Sumiyoshi-angou (住吉行宮) and was the base of the Southern Court.</p>
<p>There was also a town built around Sumiyoshi Grand Shrine, and until the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji period</a> and ensuing <a href="/wiki/Land_reclamation" title="Land reclamation">land reclamation</a> projects, the sea shore was quite close to the shrine. <i>Sumiyoshi Moyou</i>, a famous picture map showing a Japanese <i>place of scenic beauty</i> (風光明媚な風景) depicts the landscape in the area of the shrine at the time.</p>
<p>In ancient times, the estates of a number of powerful family clans were located in the Tezukayama area. Included among these was the house of the famous Ootomo No Kanamura, who supported Emperor Keitai. Tezukayama Tumulus is the burial site of either Ootomo No Kanamura or one of his children.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ward_history">Ward history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sumiyoshi-ku,_Osaka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ward history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sumiyoshi-ku (ward) was originally called Sumiyoshi-gun (district) of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Settsu_province" title="Settsu province">Settsu province</a>. In ancient times the area prospered independently of the central Osaka area.</p>
<p>In 1878, an act was passed for the organization of areas into a county system of <i>gun</i> (district), <i>ku</i> (ward), <i>machi</i> (town) and <i>mura</i> (village/neighbourhood). From this Sumiyoshi-gun (district) was created. The district town hall was built in the only town of the district, Anryuu-machi. In April 1896, a new county system was introduced which resulted in Sumiyoshi-gun being absorbed into Higashinari-gun.</p>
<p>Sumiyoshi-ku was formed in 1925 with the second expansion of Osaka city into an area that was formerly part of the original Sumiyoshi-gun. In 1943, two sections were separated from Sumiyoshi-ku to form <a href="/wiki/Abeno-ku,_Osaka" title="Abeno-ku, Osaka">Abeno-ku</a> and <a href="/wiki/Higashisumiyoshi-ku,_Osaka" title="Higashisumiyoshi-ku, Osaka">Higashisumiyoshi-ku</a>. Also, in 1943, part of <a href="/wiki/Nishinari-ku,_Osaka" title="Nishinari-ku, Osaka">Nishinari-ku</a> and all of Kohamachi-ku were absorbed into Sumiyoshi-ku. In 1974 Sumiyoshi-ku was further subdivided, resulting in <a href="/wiki/Sumiyoshi_Park" title="Sumiyoshi Park">Sumiyoshi Park</a>, Anryuu, and Hamaguchi becoming part of the newly formed <a href="/wiki/Suminoe-ku,_Osaka" title="Suminoe-ku, Osaka">Suminoe-ku</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Places_of_interest">Places of interest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sumiyoshi-ku,_Osaka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Places of interest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aside from Sumiyoshi Taisha, there is also a large number of restaurants along Abiko-Suji and in the Nagai area (the intersection of Nagai Koen-Dori and Abiko-Suji), as well as numerous small shops and businesses on Abeno-Suji. Nagai also abuts <a href="/wiki/Nagai_Park" title="Nagai Park">Nagai Park</a> in neighboring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Higashisumiyoshi_Ward" title="Higashisumiyoshi Ward">Higashisumiyoshi Ward</a> which includes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nagai_Stadium" title="Nagai Stadium">Nagai Stadium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nagai_Botanical_Garden" title="Nagai Botanical Garden">Nagai Botanical Garden</a>, as well as the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Osaka_Museum_of_Natural_History&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Osaka Museum of Natural History (page does not exist)">Osaka Museum of Natural History</a>.</p>
