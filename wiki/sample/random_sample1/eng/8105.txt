<p><b>Sveinn Björnsson</b> (pronounced <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Icelandic" title="Help:IPA for Icelandic">[ˈsveitn̥ ˈpjœsːɔn]</a></span>; 27 February 1881 – 25 January 1952) was the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Iceland#Presidents_of_Iceland" title="President of Iceland">first President</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Iceland" title="Republic of Iceland">Republic of Iceland</a> (1944–1952).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background.2C_education_and_legal_career">Background, education and legal career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sveinn_Bj%C3%B6rnsson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background, education and legal career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sveinn was born in <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a>, Denmark as the son of <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rn_J%C3%B3nsson" title="Björn Jónsson">Björn Jónsson</a> (editor and later minister) and Elísabet Sveinsdóttir. Sveinn graduated from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Menntaskolinn_i_Reykjavik" title="Menntaskolinn i Reykjavik">Latin School in Reykjavík</a> in 1900 and obtained a law degree from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Copenhagen" title="University of Copenhagen">University of Copenhagen</a> 1907. He was licensed to practice before the "upper courts" in 1907 and before the Superior Court in 1920, and served as public prosecutor in Reykjavík 1907–20 and 1924–26. From September 29, 1919 to December 31 the same year, he served as prosecutor at the National Upper Court.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_and_diplomatic_career">Political and diplomatic career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sveinn_Bj%C3%B6rnsson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political and diplomatic career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sveinn was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Reykjav%C3%ADk_City_Council" title="Reykjavík City Council">Reykjavík City Council</a> 1912–1920 and its chairman 1918–1920. He was elected to the Althing for Reykjavík 1914-15 and 1919-20.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> After Iceland's independence from Denmark in 1918 he acted as minister to Denmark during 1920–24 and 1926–40.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regent_and_President">Regent and President</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sveinn_Bj%C3%B6rnsson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Regent and President">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:212px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Standard_of_the_Regent_of_Iceland_(1941%E2%80%931944).svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="720" data-file-width="1280" height="118" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Standard_of_the_Regent_of_Iceland_%281941%E2%80%931944%29.svg/210px-Standard_of_the_Regent_of_Iceland_%281941%E2%80%931944%29.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Standard_of_the_Regent_of_Iceland_%281941%E2%80%931944%29.svg/315px-Standard_of_the_Regent_of_Iceland_%281941%E2%80%931944%29.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Standard_of_the_Regent_of_Iceland_%281941%E2%80%931944%29.svg/420px-Standard_of_the_Regent_of_Iceland_%281941%E2%80%931944%29.svg.png 2x" width="210"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Standard_of_the_Regent_of_Iceland_(1941%E2%80%931944).svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Standard as Regent of Iceland</div>

<p>Although Iceland had become a sovereign state in 1918, its foreign affairs had been conducted by Denmark until the beginning of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. The German <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Occupation_of_Denmark" title="Occupation of Denmark">occupation of Denmark</a> in April 1940, however, resulted in Iceland's de facto autonomy and Sveinn was elected <a href="/wiki/Regent" title="Regent">Regent</a> of Iceland three times during 1941–43, assuming all the prerogatives in Icelandic affairs previously held by the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Iceland" title="Kingdom of Iceland">Icelandic king</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christian_X_of_Denmark" title="Christian X of Denmark">Christian X</a>, who was also King of and resided in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Occupation_of_Denmark" title="Occupation of Denmark">Nazi occupied Denmark</a>. In July 1941, United States troops entered Iceland on the invitation of Sveinn's government and remained, in reduced numbers, after the war; their continued presence provoked the main controversy of the nation's postwar foreign policy.</p>
<p>He was elected president by the Althing on the inauguration of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Iceland" title="Republic of Iceland">Republic of Iceland</a> in 1944. His first term was only one year, since the people of Iceland were to elect their president directly for the first time in 1945. Shortly after the creation of the Republic, Sveinn called on Icelandic embassies to send him all books on diplomatic protocol that they could find, so that he could follow the right customs as head of state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> Foreign diplomars often remarked on how strict Sveinn was in following diplomatic protocol in his interactions with them, perhaps too strict.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sveinn ran unopposed in 1945 and 1949. Sveinn set new precedent and went outside of the formal powers given to the President after the 1949 parliamentary elections when he said that he would form a government if the parties could not agree on forming in four months' time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">[4]</a></sup> He claimed to have these powers according to the Constitution, but historian (and current president) Guðni Th. Jóhannesson rejects that these powers are set in the Constitution.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sveinn had poor relations with the Danish King, Christian X, after Iceland became fully independent. Christian X maintained that the Icelanders had given him false assurances in 1940 that the relationship between Iceland and Denmark would return to normal after the Nazi occupation had ended, something that Sveinn rejected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> For this reason, Sveinn did not go on an official visit to Denmark following the creation of the Republic. Christian X died in 1947, but due to poor health Sveinn was unable to visit Denmark during the final part of his tenure as president.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>He died in Reykjavík in January 1952, more than one year before his third term of office was due to expire and is the only president to die in office. Sveinn had been in poor health since 1949.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_activities">Business activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sveinn_Bj%C3%B6rnsson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Business activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sveinn was one of the founders of <a href="/wiki/Eimskipaf%C3%A9lag_%C3%8Dslands" title="Eimskipafélag Íslands">Eimskipafélag Íslands</a>, the main shipping company in Iceland, in 1914 and its chairman 1914–1920 and 1924–1926. He was the founder of the insurance company Brunabótafélag Íslands and its director from its foundation in 1916 until 1920. He was also one of the founders of the insurance company Sjóvátryggingafélag Íslands in 1918 and its chairman in 1918–1920 and 1924–1926. Sveinn was one of the founders of the Icelandic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a> on 10 December 1924 and its first chairman, serving until 1926.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sveinn_Bj%C3%B6rnsson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 2. September 1908 he married Georgia Björnsson, born Hansen (born 18 January 1884, died 18 September 1957). They had six children: Björn (1909), Anna Catherine Aagot (1911), Henrik (1914), Sveinn Christen (1916), Ólafur (1919), Elísabet (1922). His eldest son Björn Sveinsson Björnsson served in the German military as a part of the <a href="/wiki/Schutzstaffel" title="Schutzstaffel">Schutzstaffel</a> in World War II.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>His great grandson is Henrik Björnsson, singer and lead guitarist in the shoegaze rock band <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Sling_(band)" title="Singapore Sling (band)">Singapore Sling</a>.</p>
