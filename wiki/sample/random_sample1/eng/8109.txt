<p><b>Svobodny</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Свобо́дный</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> in <a href="/wiki/Amur_Oblast" title="Amur Oblast">Amur Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located on the right bank of the <a href="/wiki/Zeya_River" title="Zeya River">Zeya River</a>, 167 kilometers (104 mi) north of <a href="/wiki/Blagoveshchensk" title="Blagoveshchensk">Blagoveshchensk</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Oblast" title="Oblast">oblast</a>. Population: <span class="nowrap">58,778 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-4">[4]</a></sup> 63,889 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2002Census_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-2002Census-8">[8]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">80,006 (<a href="/wiki/Soviet_Census_(1989)" title="Soviet Census (1989)">1989 Census</a>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census1989_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census1989-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Svobodny,_Amur_Oblast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was founded in 1912 in conjunction with the construction of the Amur Railway<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pospelov_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Pospelov-6">[6]</a></sup> (the <a href="/wiki/Trans-Siberian_Railway" title="Trans-Siberian Railway">Trans-Siberian Railway</a>'s "bypass" route, which was to provide a railway connection from European Russia to the Pacific entirely over the Russian soil, without <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Eastern_Railway" title="Chinese Eastern Railway">crossing the north-eastern China</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>). It was originally named <b>Alexeyevsk</b> (<span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Алексе́евск</span>), in honor of the then <a href="/wiki/Alexei_Nikolaevich,_Tsarevich_of_Russia" title="Alexei Nikolaevich, Tsarevich of Russia">crown prince Alexey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pospelov_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-Pospelov-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1917, the town was renamed Svobodny,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pospelov_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-Pospelov-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a> for <i>free</i>.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Stalin" title="Joseph Stalin">Stalin</a> era, the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAMLag" title="BAMLag">BAMLag</a></i> prison camp was built in Svobodny, with the intention of providing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forced_labor" title="Forced labor">forced labor</a> for the planned construction of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baikal-Amur_Mainline" title="Baikal-Amur Mainline">Baikal-Amur Mainline</a>. The camp became one of the largest in the <a href="/wiki/Gulag" title="Gulag">gulag</a> system, with ca. 190,300 convicts in October 1935. The camp claimed the lives of thousands of political and clerical prisoners.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Svobodny,_Amur_Oblast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, Svodobny serves as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Svobodnensky_District" title="Svobodnensky District">Svobodnensky District</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref513_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ref513-2">[2]</a></sup> even though it is not a part of it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref7_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-Ref7-1">[1]</a></sup> As an administrative division, it is incorporated separately as <b>Svobodny <a href="/wiki/City_of_federal_subject_significance" title="City of federal subject significance">Urban Okrug</a></b>—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Amur_Oblast" title="Administrative divisions of Amur Oblast">districts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref7_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-Ref7-1">[1]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, this administrative unit also has urban okrug status.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref458_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ref458-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Svobodny,_Amur_Oblast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town is home to factories producing machinery and furniture, as well as the administrative center for mining operations in the region, including the gold mining concern <i>Amurzoloto</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Svobodny,_Amur_Oblast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The town is an important transportation hub for both rail and river traffic, with two railway stations on the <a href="/wiki/Trans-Siberian_Railway" title="Trans-Siberian Railway">Trans-Siberian Railway</a> including rolling-stock repair facilities, and a river port on the <a href="/wiki/Zeya_River" title="Zeya River">Zeya</a>.</p>
<p>It is served by the <a href="/wiki/Svobodny_Airport" title="Svobodny Airport">Svobodny Airport</a> and is near the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orlovka" title="Orlovka">Orlovka</a> interceptor air base and other locations maintained by the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Air_Force" title="Russian Air Force">Russian Air Force</a>.</p>
