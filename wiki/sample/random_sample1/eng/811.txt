<p>The <b>BMW X3</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Compact_car" title="Compact car">compact</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luxury_car" title="Luxury car">luxury</a> <a href="/wiki/Crossover_(automobile)" title="Crossover (automobile)">crossover SUV</a> manufactured by German <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automaker" title="Automaker">automaker</a> <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a> since 2003. Based on the <a href="/wiki/BMW_3_Series" title="BMW 3 Series">BMW 3 Series</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automobile_platform" title="Automobile platform">platform</a>, and now in its second generation, BMW markets the crossover as a <i>Sports Activity Vehicle</i>, the company's proprietary descriptor for its X-line of vehicles. The first generation X3 was designed by BMW in conjunction with <a href="/wiki/Magna_Steyr" title="Magna Steyr">Magna Steyr</a> of <a href="/wiki/Graz" title="Graz">Graz</a>, Austria—who also manufactured all X3s under contract to BMW. BMW manufactures the second generation X3 at their <a href="/wiki/BMW_US_Manufacturing_Company" title="BMW US Manufacturing Company">Spartanburg plant</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a>, United States.</p>

<p>The first generation BMW X3, internally designated as the <i>E83</i>, was produced from 2003 to 2010 and based on the <a href="/wiki/BMW_3_Series" title="BMW 3 Series">BMW 3 Series</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automobile_platform" title="Automobile platform">platform</a>. The E83 was designed by BMW in conjunction with <a href="/wiki/Magna_Steyr" title="Magna Steyr">Magna Steyr</a> of <a href="/wiki/Graz" title="Graz">Graz</a>, Austria—who also manufactured all first generation X3s under contract to BMW.</p>

<p>As a precursor to the X3, BMW presented the <i>xActivity</i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Concept_vehicle" title="Concept vehicle">concept vehicle</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Detroit_Auto_Show" title="Detroit Auto Show">Detroit Auto Show</a> in 2003—based on the 3 Series platform and featuring a vestigial frame-structure convertible roof with reinforced, longitudinal rails connect the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A-pillar" title="A-pillar">A-pillars</a> to the rear of the car on both sides, eliminating B- or C-pillars and creating a distinct profile.</p>
<p>The X3 premiered in September 2003 at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frankfurt_Motor_Show" title="Frankfurt Motor Show">Frankfurt Auto Show (Internationale Automobil-Ausstellung)</a>, sharing its rear suspension with the E46 330xi and using an automatic four-wheel drive system marketed as <i>xDrive.</i> All X3 models feature <a href="/wiki/BMW_xDrive" title="BMW xDrive">BMW's all wheel drive system</a>, with a default 40:60 torque split between the front and rear axles and the ability to direct all torque to either axle. The system used an electronically controlled multiple-plate clutch to enable infinitely adjustable, fully variable distribution of torque from front to rear with the capability of up to 100 percent of engine torque going to either axle. BMW markets the crossover as a <i>Sports Activity Vehicle</i>, the company's descriptor for its X-line of vehicles.</p>
<p>The X3 was conceived to combine the agility of a compact model with the driving experience of the company's X5. The X3 featured an upright, high <a href="/wiki/H-point" title="H-point">H-point</a> seating configuration, marketed as "command seating". Styling exhibited interacting concave and convex surfacing, characteristic for the company at the time along with a reinterpreted <a href="/wiki/Hofmeister_kink" title="Hofmeister kink">Hofmeister kink</a>.</p>
<p>BMW upgraded the model in 2005 and <a href="/wiki/Facelift_(automotive)" title="Facelift (automotive)">facelifted</a> the model in 2007, with revised body bumpers, engine, interior trim, and suspension.</p>
<p>The X3 <i>3.0i</i> won the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Car_of_the_Year" title="Canadian Car of the Year">Canadian Car of the Year</a> <i>Best Sports Utility Vehicle</i> award for 2005. The X3 was initially criticised for its harsh ride, austere interior, lack of off-road capability and high price.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2003.E2.80.932006">2003–2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BMW_X3&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2003–2006">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bluetooth" title="Bluetooth">Bluetooth</a> could actually be ordered straight from the factory (part of the Premium Package) and functioned without dealer intervention. (Although the BMW X3 was originally offered and promised with Bluetooth support in 2004, none of the cars that shipped from the factory actually had it installed. Few were able to get it to work with servicing from their dealer.)</li>
<li>The interior was upgraded with more consistent plastic panels. Also instead of having grey carpeting which was present on all 2004 X3s, the 2005 X3 came with carpeting that matched the leather ordered (with the exception of the Terracotta interior with black carpeting).</li>
<li>Slightly softer suspension.</li>
<li>Front fenders are one piece, as opposed to the two piece found on 2004 models.</li>
<li>A 2.0d engine became available in some markets – joining the 2.5i and 3.0i <a href="/wiki/Internal_combustion_engine" title="Internal combustion engine">engines</a> (found stateside) as well as the 3.0d engine sold elsewhere in the world.</li>
<li>An 'Open-Door' indicator was added on the 2004 models.</li>
</ul>

<p>In September 2006, the E83 received a <a href="/wiki/Facelift_(automotive)" title="Facelift (automotive)">facelift</a> with revised headlights, grille, front bumper, tail lights, rear bumper, and interior.</p>
<p>For the 2007 model year, the US market X3 was powered by the <a href="/wiki/BMW_N52" title="BMW N52">N52</a> inline <a href="/wiki/Straight-six_engine" title="Straight-six engine">6-cylinder</a> engine that debuted on the 2006 E90 3 Series. Internationally, the X3 received smaller engines including the 4-cylinder petrol and diesel options. In Europe, the range started with a 2.0-litre <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Straight-4" title="Straight-4">4-cylinder</a> petrol, a 4cyl. turbo diesel and continues with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Straight-6" title="Straight-6">6-cylinder</a> turbo &amp; <a href="/wiki/Twin-turbo" title="Twin-turbo">bi-turbo diesels</a>. The largest diesel offered was the sport diesel with 286 bhp (213 kW; 290 PS).</p>
<p>U.S. market 2006 X3's were equipped with a version of the 'M' technik body kit with unpainted door sills and wheel arches. U.S. spec Sport Package 2006 X3's received the full 'M' body kit (with painted front, side and rear plastic) available internationally as a factory accessory. 2.5i was removed for sale from the U.S. and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adaptive_headlamp" title="Adaptive headlamp">Adaptive headlamps</a> and a panoramic sunroof were made available, as well as upgraded dashboard components and leather seating.</p>
<p>Since September 2008, the Edition Exclusive and Edition lifestyle were available. The M-Sport Package was replaced in March 2009 by the Sport Limited Edition.</p>

<p>The vehicle was unveiled at the 2010 Paris Motor Show,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The F25 is the second generation of BMW X3 being released in 2010 for the 2011 model year. For this generation production moved from Austria to BMW's US factory in Greer, South Carolina.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The F25-based X3 is almost as large as the original <a href="/wiki/BMW_X5_(E53)" title="BMW X5 (E53)">E53 series X5</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theglobeandmail.com_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-theglobeandmail.com-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>At launch, all models use <a href="/wiki/All-wheel_drive" title="All-wheel drive">all-wheel drive</a>, badged as xDrive. Transmission choices are a six-speed <a href="/wiki/Manual_transmission" title="Manual transmission">manual</a> or eight-speed <a href="/wiki/ZF_8HP_transmission" title="ZF 8HP transmission">ZF 8HP</a> <a href="/wiki/Automatic_transmission" title="Automatic transmission">automatic transmission</a>. The emission standard for all engines is Euro 5. A rear-wheel drive only model called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SDrive" title="SDrive">sDrive</a> was added to the range in some markets in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Production of the X3 at <a href="/wiki/BMW_US_Manufacturing_Company" title="BMW US Manufacturing Company">BMW's United States plant</a> in <a href="/wiki/Greer,_South_Carolina" title="Greer, South Carolina">Greer, South Carolina</a> started on 1 September 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The two millionth vehicle built at BMWUSM, a Vermillion Red Metallic X3 xDrive35i with Oyster Nevada interior and an M Sport Package, was driven by BMW Associate Terry Gardner, a 16-year BMW veteran, with accompanying associates Renita Williams, Trung Phan and Olga Yurchenko. These associates represent teams from each of the plant's manufacturing technologies: Body, Paint and Assembly. The vehicle was retained on display at the Spartanburg Plant to commemorate the milestone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>Europe</dt>
</dl>
<p>UK models went on sale on 18 November 2010. Early UK models included the xDrive20d).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> xDrive30d was added as of April 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> xDrive35d was added as of September 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> sDrive18d was added as of 20 August 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> From Spring 2013, the latest generation of BMW Professional Navigation System became available.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>North America</dt>
</dl>
<p>US models arrived in US BMW Centers by the end of 2010 as 2011 model year vehicles. Early US models included xDrive28i, xDrive35i.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In 2013 model year (produced in April 2012 and delivered in May 2012), xDrive28i was introduced as replacement for xDrive28i, with new features such as standard Auto Start/Stop, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Driving_Dynamics_Control&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Driving Dynamics Control (page does not exist)">Driving Dynamics Control</a> with ECO PRO, New <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Driver_Assistance" title="Driver Assistance">Driver Assistance</a> Package (includes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lane_Departure_Warning" title="Lane Departure Warning">Lane Departure Warning</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> All US market vehicles come with an 8-speed automatic transmission.</p>
<p>For 2011, the F25 X3 is available in the US, only with a 3.0-liter inline six-cylinder gasoline engine either normally aspirated or with a twin scroll turbocharger.</p>

<p>In 2014 for the 2015 model year, the F25 received a LCI (Life Cycle Impulse) facelift. Changes include new modified twin circular headlights (optional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LED_headlight" title="LED headlight">LED headlights</a>), redesigned kidney grille, new front and rear bumpers, and exterior mirrors with integrated turn signal indicators; a centre console with optional automatic climate control system in high-gloss black-panel look, new cup holders with a sliding cover for the centre console, new exterior colours, upholstery designs, interior trim strips and light-alloy wheels; new xLine equipment package, optional Smart Opener for the automatic tailgate and storage packages.</p>
<p>The vehicle was unveiled in 84th Geneva International Motor Show 2014,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> followed by 114th New York International Auto Show 2014 (xDrive28d).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>

<p>Due to arrive in 2017, the next generation BMW X3 will have a new design created by BMW's Australian designer, Calvin Luk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>The new version is codenamed G01 and is currently in testing as a prototype. The new X3 crossover will adopt a <a href="/wiki/Plug-in_hybrid" title="Plug-in hybrid">plug-in hybrid</a> technology, and will be made available in two 2.0 litre diesel units known as B47, a next generation 3.0 diesel engine (B57), and a petrol variant (B58): a gasoline-powered, turbo-charged straight six with a displacement of 2998 cc..<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
