<p><i><b>Swallowtail</b></i>, also known as <i><b>Swallowtail Butterfly</b></i> (スワロウテイル <i>Suwarōteiru</i>), is a 1996 Japanese <a href="/wiki/Crime_film" title="Crime film">crime film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Shunji_Iwai" title="Shunji Iwai">Shunji Iwai</a>, starring <a href="/wiki/Hiroshi_Mikami" title="Hiroshi Mikami">Hiroshi Mikami</a>, pop-singer <a href="/wiki/Chara_(singer)" title="Chara (singer)">Chara</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ayumi_Ito" title="Ayumi Ito">Ayumi Ito</a>.</p>
<p>The film was shot on hand-held cameras using <a href="/wiki/Jump_cut" title="Jump cut">jump cuts</a> and other visual techniques.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It covers a wide array of themes and genres, from social realism to coming-of-age to crime.</p>
<p>A theme song for the film under <i>Yen Town Band</i>, titleds "<a href="/wiki/Swallowtail_Butterfly_(Ai_no_Uta)" title="Swallowtail Butterfly (Ai no Uta)">Swallowtail Butterfly (Ai no Uta)</a>", gained first place on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> Weekly Singles Chart.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (September 2016)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Swallowtail_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film is set in Tokyo at an unspecified point in the near future when the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_yen" title="Japanese yen">Japanese yen</a> has become the strongest currency in the world. This attracts an influx of <a href="/wiki/Immigration" title="Immigration">immigrants</a>, legal and illegal, to work in the city. The immigrants give the city the nickname <i><b>Yen Town</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">円都</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>ien taun</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. The Japanese natives, however, despise the nickname, and in retribution call the immigrants by the homophone <i><b>Yen Thieves</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">円盗</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>ien taun</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, anglicised as "Yentowns" in the film's English subtitles. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yomota_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yomota-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The story centers around a sixteen-year-old girl (Ito) whose mother has just died. The girl is passed on from person to person until she is taken in by a Chinese Yentown <a href="/wiki/Prostitution_in_Japan" title="Prostitution in Japan">prostitute</a> named Glico (Chara), who names her Ageha (Japanese for <a href="/wiki/Swallowtail_butterfly" title="Swallowtail butterfly"><i>swallowtail</i></a>). Under Glico's care, Ageha starts a new life.</p>
<p>The immigrant characters, who speak Japanese, English, Mandarin, or Cantonese, earn their living by committing petty crimes and engaging in prostitution. Ageha does not participate in any of these activities, but is protected by Glico and the other immigrants. The film does not make clear whether Ageha is Japanese or an Asian immigrant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yomota_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Yomota-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Eventually, due to a sudden twist of fate, the immigrants are given a chance to realize their various dreams. But in doing so, they destroy their solidarity, and have to face their problems separately.</p>
