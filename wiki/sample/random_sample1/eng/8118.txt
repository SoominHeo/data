<p><b>Słubice</b> <span class="IPA" style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Polish" title="Help:IPA for Polish">[swuˈbʲit͡sɛ]</a></span> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> <i>Dammvorstadt</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Border_town" title="Border town">border town</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Lubusz_Voivodeship" title="Lubusz Voivodeship">Lubusz Voivodeship</a> of western <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>. Located on the <a href="/wiki/Oder" title="Oder">Oder</a> river, directly opposite the city of <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt_(Oder)" title="Frankfurt (Oder)">Frankfurt (Oder)</a> in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, of which it was a part until 1945 (as <i>Dammvorstadt</i>). At the 2011 census, the town had a total population of 18,000 (urban agglomeration Słubice-Frankfurt 85,000). Previously located in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gorz%C3%B3w_Wielkopolski_Voivodeship" title="Gorzów Wielkopolski Voivodeship">Gorzów Wielkopolski Voivodeship</a> (1975–1998), the town is currently the capital of <a href="/wiki/S%C5%82ubice_County" title="Słubice County">Słubice County</a> and the administrative seat of the <a href="/wiki/Gmina_S%C5%82ubice,_Lubusz_Voivodeship" title="Gmina Słubice, Lubusz Voivodeship">Gmina Słubice</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Town">Town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C5%82ubice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:1706_Frankfurt_(Oder).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="347" data-file-width="550" height="139" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/1706_Frankfurt_%28Oder%29.jpg/220px-1706_Frankfurt_%28Oder%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/1706_Frankfurt_%28Oder%29.jpg/330px-1706_Frankfurt_%28Oder%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/1706_Frankfurt_%28Oder%29.jpg/440px-1706_Frankfurt_%28Oder%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:1706_Frankfurt_(Oder).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Frankfurt with eastern bridgehead (above), 1701</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The name is a modern <a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a> restored version of <i>Zliwitz</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Polabian_Slavs" title="Polabian Slavs">West Slavic</a> settlement east of the <a href="/wiki/Brandendamm" title="Brandendamm">Brandendamm</a> causeway across the Oder, mentioned in Frankfurt's city charter of 1253.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/House_of_Ascania" title="House of Ascania">Ascanian</a> margraves of <a href="/wiki/Margraviate_of_Brandenburg" title="Margraviate of Brandenburg">Brandenburg</a> had purchased the surrounding <a href="/wiki/Lubusz_Land" title="Lubusz Land">Lubusz Land</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Silesia" title="Duchy of Silesia">Silesian</a> Duke <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boles%C5%82aw_II_the_Bald" title="Bolesław II the Bald">Bolesław II the Bald</a> in 1248.</p>
<p>Słubice is closely linked to its German sister city <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt_(Oder)" title="Frankfurt (Oder)">Frankfurt (Oder)</a>, of which it was a part until 1945. The two cities share many urban amenities and collaborate on various projects, such as a <a href="/wiki/Sewage_treatment" title="Sewage treatment">wastewater treatment plant</a> in Słubice that serves both towns, as well as the <i>Collegium Polonicum</i> extension of some of the <a href="/wiki/Viadrina_European_University" title="Viadrina European University">Viadrina European University's</a> departments on the Polish side of the border. Furthermore, Słubice is part of a special <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=S%C5%82ubice-Kostrzyn_Economic_Zone&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Słubice-Kostrzyn Economic Zone (page does not exist)">Słubice-Kostrzyn Economic Zone</a>.</p>
<p>Słubice was the setting for the 2003 film <i><a href="/wiki/Distant_Lights_(2003_film)" title="Distant Lights (2003 film)">Distant Lights</a></i> (<i>Lichter</i>) as well as for scenes in the 2002 film <i><a href="/wiki/Grill_Point" title="Grill Point">Grill Point</a></i>.</p>
<p>On October 22, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia_Monument" title="Wikipedia Monument">a monument to Wikipedia</a> was unveiled in the town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thenews_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-thenews-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
