<p>The <b>BMW X6</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Mid-size_car" title="Mid-size car">mid-size</a> <a href="/wiki/Luxury_vehicle" title="Luxury vehicle">luxury</a> <a href="/wiki/Crossover_(automobile)" title="Crossover (automobile)">crossover</a> by German automaker <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a>.</p>
<p>The first generation (E71) was released for sale in April 2008 for the 2009 model year The X6 was marketed as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BMW_Sports_Activity_Series" title="BMW Sports Activity Series">Sports Activity Coupé (SAC)</a> by BMW. It combines the attributes of an SUV (high ground clearance, all-wheel drive and all-weather ability, large wheels and tires) with the stance of a coupé (styling featuring a sloping roof). It was based on the previous generation <a href="/wiki/BMW_5_Series_(E60)" title="BMW 5 Series (E60)">BMW 5</a> and <a href="/wiki/BMW_6_Series_(E63)" title="BMW 6 Series (E63)">6-series</a>. E71 development began in 2003 under Peter Tuennermann, after start of E70 X5 development in 2001. Design work by E70 X5 designer Pierre Leclercq was frozen in 2005, with test mules being run from the summer of 2005 and prototypes being tested from late 2006. Production began on December 3, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The second generation X6 (F16) was launched at the <a href="/wiki/Paris_Motor_Show" title="Paris Motor Show">Paris Motor Show</a> in 2014.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="BMW_Concept_X6_.282007.29">BMW Concept X6 (2007)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BMW_X6&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: BMW Concept X6 (2007)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The concept model debuted at the 2007 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frankfurt_Auto_Show" title="Frankfurt Auto Show">Frankfurt Auto Show</a> and the production X6 officially debuted at the 2008 <a href="/wiki/North_American_International_Auto_Show" title="North American International Auto Show">North American International Auto Show</a> in <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Montreal_International_Auto_Show" title="Montreal International Auto Show">Montreal International Auto Show</a>. While slightly longer and wider than the <a href="/wiki/BMW_X5" title="BMW X5">X5</a>, it is significantly lower and seated initially only four, and since 2011, optionally five.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-frankfurt_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-frankfurt-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>It is built in <a href="/wiki/BMW_US_Manufacturing_Company" title="BMW US Manufacturing Company">BMW's North American plant</a> in <a href="/wiki/Greer,_South_Carolina" title="Greer, South Carolina">Greer, South Carolina</a> alongside the <a href="/wiki/BMW_X5" title="BMW X5">BMW X5</a>, whose platform it shares. It is dubbed a "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BMW_Sports_Activity_Series" title="BMW Sports Activity Series">Sports Activity Coupé (SAC)</a>" by BMW. A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Petroleum_electric_hybrid_vehicle" title="Petroleum electric hybrid vehicle">hybrid</a> version, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BMW_Concept_X6_ActiveHybrid" title="BMW Concept X6 ActiveHybrid">BMW Concept X6 ActiveHybrid</a>, which is the first such vehicle from BMW, was also announced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-frankfurt_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-frankfurt-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoonews_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-yahoonews-6">[6]</a></sup> In April 2009, the X6 M version was announced, with a 547 hp (408 kW) 4.4-liter turbocharged V8.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-drivearabia_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-drivearabia-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="First_generation_.28E71.3B_2008.E2.80.932014.29"><span id="E71"></span>First generation (E71; 2008–2014)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BMW_X6&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: First generation (E71; 2008–2014)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chassis_technology">Chassis technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BMW_X6&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chassis technology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The X6 marks BMW's first use of its new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dynamic_Performance_Control" title="Dynamic Performance Control">Dynamic Performance Control</a> system, which works in unison with <a href="/wiki/BMW_xDrive" title="BMW xDrive">xDrive</a> <a href="/wiki/All-wheel_drive" title="All-wheel drive">all-wheel drive</a>, both being standard on the X6. DPC is a drivetrain and chassis control system that works to regulate <a href="/wiki/Traction_(engineering)" title="Traction (engineering)">traction</a> and especially correct over- and understeer by actively spreading out drive forces across the rear axle. <a href="/wiki/Torque" title="Torque">Torque</a> is distributed not only between the front and rear wheels (xDrive) but also from side to side at the rear, for improved agility and added stability (through the DPC rear axle).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> This lateral distribution of torque is commonly known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Torque_Vectoring" title="Torque Vectoring">torque vectoring</a>.</p>
<p>The DPC differential features clutch packs on both output sides that are actuated by an electric motor. The clutch pack activates a <a href="/wiki/Epicyclic_gearing" title="Epicyclic gearing">planetary</a> gearset which causes one wheel to be <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/overdrive" title="wikt:overdrive">overdriven</a>. A conventional control system will use the brakes to reduce the speed of the faster moving wheel (which is the one with less traction)and reduce engine power. This leads to increased brake wear and slower than optimal progress. The DPC system speeds up the slower moving wheel (the one with the most traction) in order to maintain stability when needed. For example; while turning, the outer wheel is overdriven to provide greater acceleration using the traction advantage through the dynamic loading of the outboard wheel in cornering. In an oversteer situation, the inner wheel is overdriven to regain traction balance.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="BMW_X6_M_.282009.E2.80.932014.29"><span id="X6_M"></span>BMW X6 M (2009–2014)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BMW_X6&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: BMW X6 M (2009–2014)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The BMW X6 M and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BMW_X5_M" title="BMW X5 M">X5 M</a> are the first vehicles from <a href="/wiki/BMW_M" title="BMW M">BMW M</a> GmbH to have <a href="/wiki/BMW_xDrive" title="BMW xDrive">xDrive</a> all-wheel-drive system and automatic transmissions, and are also crossovers as opposed to passenger cars. The X6 M was unveiled at the 2009 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Auto_Show" title="New York Auto Show">New York Auto Show</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and first went on sale in the 2010 model year.</p>

<p>The high-performance <a href="/wiki/BMW_M" title="BMW M">M</a> derivative features a twin scroll twin turbo version of the 4.4-liter V8 <a href="/wiki/BMW_N63" title="BMW N63">BMW N63</a> engine with the Cylinder-bank Comprehensive Manifold (CCM).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The engine is rated 555 PS (408 kW; 547 hp) at 6000 rpm and 680 N·m (500 lb·ft) at 1500–5650 rpm.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <p>Other features include 6-speed M Sports automatic transmission with aluminum pull-style paddles on steering wheel, M Dynamic Mode feature, 10 mm (0.4 in) lower Adaptive Drive suspension, 4-piston fixed calipers with 15.6" rotor at front and single piston floating calipers with 15.2" rotor at rear, 20-inch alloy wheels with 275/40R20 front and 315/35R20 run flat tires, hill descent control, dynamic stability control, special gills in the front fenders, 20-inch light-alloy wheels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The car can accelerate from zero to 60 mph (0–97 km/h) in 4.5 seconds and 0–62 mph (0–100 km/h) in 4.7 seconds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-R.26T_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-R.26T-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="ActiveHybrid_X6_.28E72.3B_2009.E2.80.932010.29">ActiveHybrid X6 (E72; 2009–2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BMW_X6&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: ActiveHybrid X6 (E72; 2009–2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The <b>BMW Concept X6 ActiveHybrid</b> is the concept vehicle of <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMWs</a> first full-hybrid vehicle and was revealed at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internationale_Automobil-Ausstellung" title="Internationale Automobil-Ausstellung">Internationale Automobil-Ausstellung</a> Frankfurt in September 2007. BMWs first mild-hybrid <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BMW_Concept_7_Series_ActiveHybrid_Generation_1" title="BMW Concept 7 Series ActiveHybrid Generation 1">BMW Concept 7 Series ActiveHybrid Generation 1</a> was revealed at the Paris Motor Show in October 2008. Both cars made their market debuts at the end of 2009<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and the X6 Hybrid can drive in pure electrical mode at low speeds. The Active Hybrid 7 Generation 1 does not have this ability.</p>
<p>The BMW Concept X6 ActiveHybrid uses the V8 Twin Turbo with High Precision Injection that powers the BMW X6 xDrive50i.</p>
<p>A defining feature of the BMW Concept X6 ActiveHybrid is its Two-Mode active transmission, an innovative technology that controls the interplay of the electrical motors and the internal combustion engine. The car can operate in two modes: mode 1 delivers high torque when pulling away from a standing start and at low speeds, while mode 2 is suited for higher speeds. The result is that the car always uses the available power resources in the most efficient possible way, whatever speed it is travelling at.</p>
<p>The ActiveHybrid X6 made its world premiere at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frankfurt_Motor_Show" title="Frankfurt Motor Show">Frankfurt Motor Show</a> (IAA) in September 2009.</p>
<p>In late 2009, BMW introduced an X6 featuring a version of the <a href="/wiki/Global_Hybrid_Cooperation" title="Global Hybrid Cooperation">Global Hybrid Cooperation</a> <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_vehicle_drivetrain" title="Hybrid vehicle drivetrain">hybrid vehicle drivetrain</a>, popularly known as the two-mode hybrid system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-an_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-an-15">[15]</a></sup> This car was confirmed as being called the BMW ActiveHybrid X6, and it is the world's most powerful hybrid vehicle; it is not sold in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The production vehicle was unveiled alongside a 7 Series hybrid at the 2009 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frankfurt_Motor_Show" title="Frankfurt Motor Show">Frankfurt Motor Show</a>. The ActiveHybrid X6 went on sale in December 2009 in the US market with a base price of US$89,765.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>The drive system featured in the BMW ActiveHybrid X6 consists of a 300 kW (407 hp) V8 power unit with BMW TwinPower Turbo Technology and two electric engines developing 67 kW (91 hp) and, respectively, 63 kW (86 hp). Maximum system output is 357 kW (485 hp), peak torque is 780 Newton-meters (575 lb-ft.)</p>
<p>BMW ActiveHybrid technology offers the driver three significant options: to drive under electric power alone, to use the power of the combustion engine, or to benefit from the combination of both drive modes for short periods of maximum acceleration, using the 485 hp maximum. Driving completely free of CO2 in the electric mode is possible up to a speed of 37 mph (60 km/h). The hybrid also employs stop-start technology and other energy saving measures to help improve efficiency. The core-vehicle is however very heavy and the petrol power unit limits the extent to which fuel consumption can be reduced in absolute terms. The Turbo-Diesel models in the X6 range use less fuel, for example.</p>

<p>The second generation X6 was launched at the 2014 <a href="/wiki/Paris_Motor_Show" title="Paris Motor Show">Paris Motor Show</a> featuring a new eight-speed automatic and a slightly larger luggage bay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Sales in most markets commenced by the end of 2014.</p>
<p>Like the other models in the BMW range powered by the twin-turbo 4.4-liter V-8, the xDrive50i benefits from increased power, up 50 hp and 30 lb-ft from its predecessor to 450hp and 480 lb-ft of torque. The X6M is one of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_fastest_accelerating_SUVs&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="List of fastest accelerating SUVs (page does not exist)">the quickest of its type</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lieb_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-lieb-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
