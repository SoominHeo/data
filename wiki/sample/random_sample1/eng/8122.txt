<p><b>Princess Margaretha of Sweden</b> (Margaretha Sofia Lovisa Ingeborg; 25 June 1899 – 4 January 1977) was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Royal_Family" title="Swedish Royal Family">Swedish Royal Family</a> and a Princess of Denmark by marriage, the eldest sister of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Princess_Martha_of_Sweden" title="Princess Martha of Sweden">Princess Martha of Sweden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Astrid_of_Sweden" title="Astrid of Sweden">Astrid of Sweden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prince_Carl_Bernadotte" title="Prince Carl Bernadotte">Prince Carl Bernadotte</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Margaretha_of_Sweden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The eldest child and daughter of <a href="/wiki/Prince_Carl,_Duke_of_V%C3%A4sterg%C3%B6tland" title="Prince Carl, Duke of Västergötland">Prince Carl, Duke of Västergötland</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Princess_Ingeborg_of_Denmark" title="Princess Ingeborg of Denmark">Princess Ingeborg of Denmark</a>, she was born Princess Margaretha of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sweden_and_Norway" title="Sweden and Norway">Sweden and Norway</a> (later just "of Sweden", due to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_union_between_Norway_and_Sweden_in_1905" title="Dissolution of the union between Norway and Sweden in 1905">dissolution of the union between Norway and Sweden in 1905</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>.</p>
<p>In 1916 Margaretha's <a href="/wiki/Confirmation" title="Confirmation">confirmation</a> attracted enthusiastic press coverage; the event was said to mark the beginning of a new age for the Swedish royal house, which had lacked princesses for so long.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_and_family">Marriage and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Margaretha_of_Sweden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Marriage and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 22 May 1919, at the <a href="/wiki/Storkyrkan" title="Storkyrkan">Storkyrkan</a>, Stockholm, she was married to <a href="/wiki/Prince_Axel_of_Denmark" title="Prince Axel of Denmark">Prince Axel of Denmark</a>, her maternal first cousin once removed. The marriage was a love match; her mother remarked that the couple were so much in love that they could not be left alone in a furnished room.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Her wedding was celebrated with great festivities in Stockholm.</p>
<p>They had two sons:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Prince_George_Valdemar_of_Denmark" title="Prince George Valdemar of Denmark">Prince George Valdemar Karl Axel of Denmark</a> (16 April 1920–20 September 1986) he married <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Princess_George_of_Denmark" title="Princess George of Denmark">Anne Ferelith Fenella Bowes-Lyon</a> on 16 September 1950.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Count_Flemming_Valdemar_of_Rosenborg" title="Count Flemming Valdemar of Rosenborg">Prince Flemming Valdemar Carl Axel</a> (3 March 1922–19 June 2002) he married Alice Ruth Nielson, daughter of Kai Nielson and Edith Fischer, on 24 May 1949. They have four children, ten grandchildren and four great-grandchildren.</li>
</ul>
<p>She was a maternal aunt of King <a href="/wiki/Harald_V_of_Norway" title="Harald V of Norway">Harald V of Norway</a> and Kings <a href="/wiki/Baudouin_of_Belgium" title="Baudouin of Belgium">Baudouin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Albert_II_of_Belgium" title="Albert II of Belgium">Albert II of Belgium</a>; and paternal grandaunt of King <a href="/wiki/Philippe_of_Belgium" title="Philippe of Belgium">Philippe of Belgium</a> and Grand Duke <a href="/wiki/Henri,_Grand_Duke_of_Luxembourg" title="Henri, Grand Duke of Luxembourg">Henri of Luxembourg</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Margaretha_of_Sweden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Margaretha adjusted herself well in Denmark, which she had often visited on family occasions during her upbringing. She lived a private life devoted to her family on the estate Bernstorffshøj in Gentofte and generally avoided publicity, and kept in close contact with her relations abroad. She was interested in social issues in Sweden, and became the patron of several charity organisations in Denmark, and was the chairperson of Gentofte Børnevenner.</p>
<p>She was a leading guest at the 1947 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wedding_of_Princess_Elizabeth_and_Philip,_Duke_of_Edinburgh" title="Wedding of Princess Elizabeth and Philip, Duke of Edinburgh">wedding of Princess Elizabeth and Philip, Duke of Edinburgh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the death of her sister, the Norwegian <a href="/wiki/Princess_M%C3%A4rtha_of_Sweden" title="Princess Märtha of Sweden">Crown Princess Märtha</a> in 1954, she became a great support for her sister's children in Norway; she was the godmother of princess <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M%C3%A4rtha_Louise_of_Norway" title="Märtha Louise of Norway">Märtha Louise of Norway</a>.</p>
<p>Her spouse died in 1964. As a widow, she was often back in Sweden, where she would join other members of the Swedish royal house in representative duties at official ceremonies — most notably, the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize" title="Nobel Prize">Nobel Prize</a>. To her family, she was affectionately known as "Tante Ta" ("Aunt Ta").</p>
<p>She died in <a href="/wiki/Kongsted" title="Kongsted">Kongsted</a>, near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fakse" title="Fakse">Fakse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, in 1977.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Margaretha_of_Sweden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The popular Swedish layer cake <a href="/wiki/Princess_cake" title="Princess cake">princess cake</a> was named for Margaretha and her two sisters when they were children.</p>
