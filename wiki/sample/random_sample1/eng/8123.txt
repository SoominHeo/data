<p><b>Swissôtel Hotels and Resorts</b>, more normally simply known as <b>Swissôtel</b>, is a group of hotels pitched at the higher end of the hotel market. The chain currently includes 37 properties in 17 countries. The corporate offices for Swissôtel are located in the Prioria Business Center on the property of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zurich_Airport" title="Zurich Airport">Zurich Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kloten" title="Kloten">Kloten</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zurich" title="Zurich">Zurich</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and the company is headed by President, Meinhard Huck.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_history">Corporate history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Swiss%C3%B4tel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Corporate history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Swissotel_Zurich-Oerlikon_2010.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1280" data-file-width="960" height="227" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Swissotel_Zurich-Oerlikon_2010.jpg/170px-Swissotel_Zurich-Oerlikon_2010.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Swissotel_Zurich-Oerlikon_2010.jpg/255px-Swissotel_Zurich-Oerlikon_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Swissotel_Zurich-Oerlikon_2010.jpg/340px-Swissotel_Zurich-Oerlikon_2010.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>

<p>Swissôtel was founded in 1980 as a joint venture between the <a href="/wiki/Swissair" title="Swissair">Swissair</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nestl%C3%A9" title="Nestlé">Nestlé</a> groups with its headquarters in the <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Swiss</a> city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zurich" title="Zurich">Zurich</a>. Initially hotels were operated in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Berne" title="Berne">Berne</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zurich" title="Zurich">Zurich</a>. In 1990, the hotel group became a fully owned subsidiary of the Swissair group and in 1996 its head office moved from Zurich to New York.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-swhist_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-swhist-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2001, with the parent airline in the serious financial difficulties that eventually lead to its demise, Swissôtel was sold to Raffles Holdings Limited, the owners of the famous <a href="/wiki/Raffles_Hotel" title="Raffles Hotel">Raffles Hotel</a> in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>. In July 2005, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colony_Capital" title="Colony Capital">Colony Capital</a>, a private international investment firm, acquired Raffles International Limited, the company that by this stage owned both Raffles and Swissôtel brands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-swhist_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-swhist-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2006, Colony Capital, together with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kingdom_Hotels_International&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kingdom Hotels International (page does not exist)">Kingdom Hotels International</a>, acquired <a href="/wiki/Fairmont_Hotels_and_Resorts" title="Fairmont Hotels and Resorts">Fairmont Hotels &amp; Resorts</a>. With the completion of the transaction, the Fairmont and Raffles International portfolios were combined, transforming the companies into a global hotel leader headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fairmont_Raffles_Hotels_International" title="Fairmont Raffles Hotels International">Fairmont Raffles Hotels International</a>, with 120 hotels in 23 countries under three brands – Fairmont, Raffles and Swissôtel.</p>
<p>After the transaction, the corporate office of Swissôtel Hotels &amp; Resorts moved back to Zurich, where it was headed by President, Mr Meinhard Huck, who retired in 2013. Ms Lilian Roten was appointed Vice President Swissôtel Brand in November 2013.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_responsibility">Corporate responsibility</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Swiss%C3%B4tel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Corporate responsibility">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Swissôtel has a high profile approach to corporate social responsibility, which concentrates on charities who help underprivileged children around the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CSR_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-CSR-10">[10]</a></sup> The company has formed a link with <a href="/wiki/SOS_Children%27s_Villages" title="SOS Children's Villages">SOS Children's Villages</a> for which it organises fundraising events such as an annual summer party in Zürich.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SOS_Children.27s_Villages_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-SOS_Children.27s_Villages-11">[11]</a></sup> The company has supported particular projects including that in <a href="/wiki/Lavrovo" title="Lavrovo">Lavrovo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oryol_Oblast" title="Oryol Oblast">Oryol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>.</p>
