<p>The <b>2011–12 <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Super_League" title="Swiss Super League">Swiss Super League</a></b> season was the 115th season of top-tier <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. It began on 16 July 2011 and ended on 23 May 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/FC_Basel" title="FC Basel">Basel</a> successfully defended their title. No team was directly relegated after <a href="/wiki/Neuch%C3%A2tel_Xamax" title="Neuchâtel Xamax">Neuchâtel Xamax</a> were expelled midway through the season over severe financial irregularities.</p>
<p>The league comprised the best eight sides from the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Swiss_Super_League" title="2010–11 Swiss Super League">2010–11 season</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Swiss_Challenge_League" title="2010–11 Swiss Challenge League">2010–11 Swiss Challenge League</a> champions <a href="/wiki/FC_Lausanne-Sport" title="FC Lausanne-Sport">Lausanne-Sport</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Servette_FC" title="Servette FC">Servette</a>, the winners of the relegation/promotion play-off between the ninth-placed Super League team and the Challenge League runners-up.</p>
<p>Since Switzerland dropped from thirteenth to sixteenth place in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_coefficient#Association_coefficient_.28.22League_coefficient.22.29" title="UEFA coefficient">UEFA association coefficient rankings</a> at the end of the 2010–11 season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> the league lost its second spot for the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>. The league champions will now enter the second qualifying round of the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2012–13 UEFA Champions League">2012–13 tournament</a>, while the runners-up and third-placed sides will enter the second qualifying round of the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2012–13 UEFA Europa League">2012–13 UEFA Europa League</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Teams">Teams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011%E2%80%9312_Swiss_Super_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Teams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/FC_St._Gallen" title="FC St. Gallen">FC St. Gallen</a> were relegated after finishing in last place of the table after the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Swiss_Super_League" title="2010–11 Swiss Super League">2010–11 season</a>. The club thus completed a two-year tenure in the Super League. St. Gallen were replaced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010-11_Swiss_Challenge_League" title="2010-11 Swiss Challenge League">2010–11 Challenge League</a> champions <a href="/wiki/FC_Lausanne-Sport" title="FC Lausanne-Sport">FC Lausanne-Sport</a>, who returned to the highest football league of Switzerland after a nine-year absence.</p>
<p>A further spot in the league was contested in a relegation/promotion playoff between ninth-placed <a href="/wiki/AC_Bellinzona" title="AC Bellinzona">AC Bellinzona</a> and Challenge League runners-up <a href="/wiki/Servette_FC" title="Servette FC">Servette FC</a> from <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a>. Both teams played a two-legged series, which was won by Servette, 3–2 on aggregate. The Geneva side thus returned to the Super League after six years, while Bellinzona were relegated to the Challenge League after three years in the highest Swiss football league.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Results">Results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011%E2%80%9312_Swiss_Super_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All ten clubs played twice against each other during the first half of the season, once at home and once away, for a total of 18 matches. As <a href="/wiki/Neuch%C3%A2tel_Xamax" title="Neuchâtel Xamax">Neuchâtel Xamax</a> had their license revoked during the winter break, the club's second-half matches were entirely cancelled. The second half of the season thus was competed by only nine clubs, which played another double round-robin schedule; each of these nine clubs hence had played 34 matches at the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xamaxLicenseRevokenSchedulingOfMatches_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-xamaxLicenseRevokenSchedulingOfMatches-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
