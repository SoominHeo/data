The traditionally Catholic regions of Switzerland are shown in red<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <p>The <b>Catholic Church in Switzerland</b> is organised into six dioceses and two <a href="/wiki/Territorial_abbey" title="Territorial abbey">territorial abbeys</a>, comprising approximately 3 million Catholics, about 38.6%<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Der_Bund_kurz_erkl.C3.A4rt_2013_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Der_Bund_kurz_erkl.C3.A4rt_2013-1">[1]</a></sup> of the Swiss population.</p>
<p>The dioceses are:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Basel" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Basel">Roman Catholic Diocese of Basel</a> whose <a href="/wiki/Ordinary_(officer)" title="Ordinary (officer)">ordinary</a> is Bishop <a href="/wiki/Felix_Gm%C3%BCr" title="Felix Gmür">Felix Gmür</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Lausanne,_Geneva_and_Fribourg" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Lausanne, Geneva and Fribourg">Roman Catholic Diocese of Lausanne, Geneva and Fribourg</a> whose <a href="/wiki/Ordinary_(officer)" title="Ordinary (officer)">ordinary</a> is Bishop <a href="/wiki/Charles_Morerod" title="Charles Morerod">Charles Morerod</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Chur" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Chur">Roman Catholic Diocese of Chur</a> whose ordinary is Bishop <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vitus_Huonder" title="Vitus Huonder">Vitus Huonder</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Lugano" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Lugano">Roman Catholic Diocese of Lugano</a> whose ordinary is Bishop <a href="/wiki/Pier_Giacomo_Grampa" title="Pier Giacomo Grampa">Pier Giacomo Grampa</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Saint_Gallen" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Saint Gallen">Roman Catholic Diocese of Saint Gallen</a> whose ordinary is Bishop <a href="/wiki/Markus_B%C3%BCchel" title="Markus Büchel">Markus Büchel</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Sion" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Sion">Roman Catholic Diocese of Sion</a> whose ordinary is Bishop <a href="/wiki/Norbert_Brunner" title="Norbert Brunner">Norbert Brunner</a></li>
</ul>
<p>The two <a href="/wiki/Territorial_abbey" title="Territorial abbey">territorial abbeys</a>, which do not belong to any bishopric, are</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Maurice%27s_Abbey" title="St. Maurice's Abbey">St. Maurice's Abbey</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canton_of_Valais" title="Canton of Valais">Canton of Valais</a>, which is the longest continuously inhabited monastery in Europe, whose <a href="/wiki/Abbot" title="Abbot">Abbot</a> is <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Roduit" title="Joseph Roduit">Joseph Roduit</a>,</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Einsiedeln_Abbey" title="Einsiedeln Abbey">Einsiedeln Abbey</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_Schwyz" title="Canton of Schwyz">Canton of Schwyz</a></li>
</ul>
<p>In contrast to most Catholic dioceses, Swiss bishoprics are <a href="/wiki/Exemption_(church)" title="Exemption (church)">exempt</a>, i.e. immediately subject to the jurisdiction of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">Holy See</a>, without any <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_bishop" title="Metropolitan bishop">Metropolitan see</a>. The bishops and the two territorial abbots are organised within the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Bishops_Conference" title="Swiss Bishops Conference">Swiss Bishops Conference</a> whose chairman is the current Bishop of Sion, <a href="/wiki/Norbert_Brunner" title="Norbert Brunner">Norbert Brunner</a>.</p>
<p>In the last thirty years, mainly during the conflict over the appointment of <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Haas" title="Wolfgang Haas">Wolfgang Haas</a> as Bishop of Chur, there have been discussions to make a major reform of the structure of the Catholic Church in Switzerland, which would probably also lead to the establishment of a metropolitan see (probably in <a href="/wiki/Lucerne" title="Lucerne">Lucerne</a>). However, discussions remain unresolved especially about the status of the <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_Z%C3%BCrich" title="Canton of Zürich">Canton of Zürich</a> as part of the Diocesis of Chur, the large but splinted extend of the Diocesis of Basel and the lack of a Metropolitan see stay unresolved.</p>
<p>The status of Catholicism in Switzerland is complicated further by the existence of <a href="/wiki/Landeskirche#Roman_Catholic_cantonal_churches" title="Landeskirche">Landeskirchen (Roman Catholic cantonal churches)</a>, imposed by anti-clerical cantonal governments in the 19th century and organised along democratic lines and control the application of funds collected through <a href="/wiki/Church_tax#Switzerland" title="Church tax">church taxes</a>. Most cantonally delineated Roman Catholic church bodies are members in their umbrella <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Roman_Catholic_Central_Conference_of_Switzerland&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Roman Catholic Central Conference of Switzerland (page does not exist)">Roman Catholic Central Conference of Switzerland</a> (RKZ, official names in German: <span lang="de"><i>Römisch-Katholische Zentralkonferenz der Schweiz</i></span>, French: <span lang="fr"><i>Conférence centrale catholique romaine de Suisse</i></span>, Italian: <span lang="it"><i>Conferenza centrale cattolica romana della Svizzera</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/Romansh_language" title="Romansh language">Romansh</a>: <span lang="rm"><i>Conferenza centrala catolica romana da la Svizra</i></span>).</p>
<p>Currently, there are three Cardinals from Switzerland: <a href="/wiki/Henri_Schwery" title="Henri Schwery">Henri Schwery</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kurt_Koch" title="Kurt Koch">Kurt Koch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gilberto_Agustoni" title="Gilberto Agustoni">Gilberto Agustoni</a>, of whom one (Cardinal Koch) participated in the 2013 <a href="/wiki/Papal_conclave" title="Papal conclave">conclave</a>.</p>
