<p><i><b>Dummy</b></i> is a 2002 American <a href="/wiki/Comedy-drama" title="Comedy-drama">comedy-drama</a> film written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Greg_Pritikin" title="Greg Pritikin">Greg Pritikin</a>. It stars <a href="/wiki/Adrien_Brody" title="Adrien Brody">Adrien Brody</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milla_Jovovich" title="Milla Jovovich">Milla Jovovich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illeana_Douglas" title="Illeana Douglas">Illeana Douglas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vera_Farmiga" title="Vera Farmiga">Vera Farmiga</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jared_Harris" title="Jared Harris">Jared Harris</a>. The film had its premiere on February 21, 2002, and was released in a <a href="/wiki/Limited_release" title="Limited release">limited release</a> in the United States on September 12, 2003 by <a href="/wiki/Artisan_Entertainment" title="Artisan Entertainment">Artisan Entertainment</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dummy_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Steven Schoichet (<a href="/wiki/Adrien_Brody" title="Adrien Brody">Adrien Brody</a>) is a recently unemployed ne'er-do-well who has difficulty expressing himself. Steven finds he has a knack for <a href="/wiki/Ventriloquism" title="Ventriloquism">ventriloquism</a>. Steven's best friend is Fangora "Fanny" Gurkel (<a href="/wiki/Milla_Jovovich" title="Milla Jovovich">Milla Jovovich</a>), an aspiring <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk rock</a> singer who, along with Steven, is just looking for her niche. Eventually, Fanny takes a shine to <a href="/wiki/Klezmer" title="Klezmer">klezmer</a> music when she learns of an opportunity to get an actual gig. Through his newfound talent, Steven discovers that he is able to overcome his social problems through his dummy and decides to try impressing and winning the heart of Lorena Fanchetti (<a href="/wiki/Vera_Farmiga" title="Vera Farmiga">Vera Farmiga</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dummy_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casting">Casting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dummy_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Casting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In May 2000, it was reported by <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> that <a href="/wiki/Adrien_Brody" title="Adrien Brody">Adrien Brody</a> and <a href="/wiki/Milla_Jovovich" title="Milla Jovovich">Milla Jovovich</a> had been cast in the film, Brody as the lead character of Steven, and Jovovich as the best friend of Brody's character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The following month, in June 2000, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Illeana_Douglas" title="Illeana Douglas">Illeana Douglas</a> had joined the cast in a supporting role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Vera_Farmiga" title="Vera Farmiga">Vera Farmiga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ron_Leibman" title="Ron Leibman">Ron Leibman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jared_Harris" title="Jared Harris">Jared Harris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Walter" title="Jessica Walter">Jessica Walter</a> were also cast in supporting roles. Brody (who had no previous experience as a ventriloquist) performed all of the ventriloquism and puppetry live during shooting, doing so without puppeteering stand-ins or voiceover dubbing. He was coached prior to and during production by veteran actor-ventriloquist-puppetmaker Alan Semok, who is credited in the film as special consultant and designer/creator of the title character.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dummy_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Principal_photography" title="Principal photography">Principal photography</a> took place in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wayne,_New_Jersey" title="Wayne, New Jersey">Wayne, New Jersey</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long_Island,_New_York" title="Long Island, New York">Long Island, New York</a> in July and August 2000.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dummy_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dummy_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film was given a <a href="/wiki/Limited_release" title="Limited release">limited release</a> in the United States. <i>Dummy</i> made $30,130 from 5 theaters in its opening weekend. It made an additional $41,516 at the box office for a total domestic gross of $71,646.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mojo_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mojo-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dummy_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Dummy</i> received mostly positive reviews from film critics. Review aggregator website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> gave the film a 70% rating, based on 33 reviews, with an average rating of 6.1/10. The site's consensus reads, "<i>Dummy</i> is a sweet family comedy that succeeds due to charming performances, even if the final product feels slightly undercooked."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The film currently holds a 48% rating on <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, based on 12 critical reviews, indicating "mixed or average reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
