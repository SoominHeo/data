<p><b>Airi Suzuki</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">鈴木 愛理</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Suzuki Airi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born April 12, 1994 in <a href="/wiki/Gifu_Prefecture" title="Gifu Prefecture">Gifu, Japan</a>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_pop" title="Japanese pop">Japanese pop</a> singer and currently member of such <a href="/wiki/Japanese_idol" title="Japanese idol">idol</a> groups as <a href="/wiki/Cute_(Japanese_idol_group)" title="Cute (Japanese idol group)">Cute</a> and <a href="/wiki/Buono!" title="Buono!">Buono!</a>, among others.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Airi_Suzuki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Airi_Suzuki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Her parents, <a href="/wiki/Toru_Suzuki" title="Toru Suzuki">Toru and Kyoko Suzuki</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> were both <a href="/wiki/Professional_golfer" title="Professional golfer">professional golfers</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Airi_Suzuki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2002, Suzuki participated and passed, along with 14 other girls, the audition of <a href="/wiki/Hello!_Project_Kids" title="Hello! Project Kids">Hello! Project Kids</a> after performing <i>Kimochi wa Tsutawaru</i> by <a href="/wiki/BoA" title="BoA">BoA</a>. She made her first appearance in the entertainment industry as a member of 4Kids, a temporary group that appeared in <a href="/wiki/Minimoni" title="Minimoni">Minimoni</a>'s movie <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mini_Moni_ja_Movie:_Okashi_na_Daib%C5%8Dken!" title="Mini Moni ja Movie: Okashi na Daibōken!">Mini Moni ja Movie: Okashi na Daibōken!</a></i>, which was released in late 2002. In addition, she was also in a shuffle group called <a href="/wiki/Aa!" title="Aa!">Aa!</a> later that year and received a lead in the song.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>She was assigned as a member of <a href="/wiki/Cute_(Japanese_idol_group)" title="Cute (Japanese idol group)">Cute</a>, whose formation was announced on June 11, 2005, along with seven other <a href="/wiki/Hello!_Project_Kids" title="Hello! Project Kids">Hello! Project Kids</a> members, and she is the lead singer in most songs and concerts. Furthermore, it was announced on July 21, 2007 that Suzuki would participate in a unit named <a href="/wiki/Buono!" title="Buono!">Buono!</a> with <a href="/wiki/Momoko_Tsugunaga" title="Momoko Tsugunaga">Momoko Tsugunaga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Miyabi_Natsuyaki" title="Miyabi Natsuyaki">Miyabi Natsuyaki</a> from <a href="/wiki/Berryz_Kobo" title="Berryz Kobo">Berryz Kobo</a>. In 2010 Suzuki had once injured her ankle before a concert.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the end of October, 2010, Airi Suzuki appeared on the cover of the December issue of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UP_to_boy" title="UP to boy">UP to boy</a></i> magazine together with <a href="/wiki/Mayu_Watanabe" title="Mayu Watanabe">Mayu Watanabe</a> from <a href="/wiki/AKB48" title="AKB48">AKB48</a>. That was the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gravure_idol" title="Gravure idol">gravure</a> collaboration between <a href="/wiki/AKB48" title="AKB48">AKB48</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hello!_Project" title="Hello! Project">Hello! Project</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
