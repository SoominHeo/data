<p><b>Shichahai</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">什刹海</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Shíchàhǎi</span></i>) is a historic scenic area consisting of three lakes in the north of central <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. They are located to the north-west of the <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_City" title="Forbidden City">Forbidden City</a> and north of the <a href="/wiki/Beihai_Park" title="Beihai Park">Beihai Lake</a>. Shichahai consists of the following three lakes: Qianhai (前海), Xihai (西海) and <a href="/wiki/Houhai" title="Houhai">Houhai</a> (后海). In imperial times it was called the <i>Riverbank</i> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">河沿</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">héyán</span></i>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shichahai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shichahai consists of 147 hectares and dates back to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jin_Dynasty,_1115%E2%80%931234" title="Jin Dynasty, 1115–1234">Jin Dynasty</a>. From the time of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuan_Dynasty" title="Yuan Dynasty">Yuan Dynasty</a> it was the northernmost part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Canal_of_China" title="Grand Canal of China">Grand Canal</a> linking <a href="/wiki/Hangzhou" title="Hangzhou">Hangzhou</a> in the south to Beijing in the North of China. Because of this, the Shichahai area used to be the most important commercial district with all kinds of activities going on. It harbors several temples and mansions.</p>
<p>Sichahai literally means "the lake of ten temples". Around the lake there are ten famous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taoist" title="Taoist">Taoist</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buddhist" title="Buddhist">Buddhist</a> temples and several formal royal mansions and gardens. The most well known are the <a href="/wiki/Prince_Gong_Mansion" title="Prince Gong Mansion">Prince Gong Mansion</a> (恭亲王府) and the <a href="/wiki/Prince_Chun_Mansion" title="Prince Chun Mansion">Prince Chun Mansion</a> (醇亲王府).</p>
<p>Shichahai is a famous scenic spot in Beijing, and it is near the north-gate of the <a href="/wiki/Beihai_Park" title="Beihai Park">Beihai Park</a>. The borders of the lakes are lined by tall trees. In the summer tourists rent boats to paddle on the lakes. In the winter many people come to ice-skate.</p>
<p>In 1992 the municipal government of Beijing declared the district an "Historical and Cultural Scenic District".</p>
