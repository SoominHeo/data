<p><b>Scarlino</b> is a <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> (municipality) in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Grosseto" title="Province of Grosseto">Province of Grosseto</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> region <a href="/wiki/Tuscany" title="Tuscany">Tuscany</a>, located about 100 km (62 mi) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Florence" title="Florence">Florence</a> and about 25 km (16 mi) northwest of <a href="/wiki/Grosseto" title="Grosseto">Grosseto</a>.</p>
<p>Scarlino borders the following municipalities: <a href="/wiki/Castiglione_della_Pescaia" title="Castiglione della Pescaia">Castiglione della Pescaia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Follonica" title="Follonica">Follonica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gavorrano" title="Gavorrano">Gavorrano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massa_Marittima" title="Massa Marittima">Massa Marittima</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scarlino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Scarlino appeared before the year 1000 as a possession of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aldobrandeschi" title="Aldobrandeschi">Aldobrandeschi</a> family, and was later handed over to the bishops of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roselle,_Italy" title="Roselle, Italy">Roselle</a> and then the Alberti family. In the 13th century it was acquired again by the Aldobrandeschi, but later it passed to <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Pisa" title="Republic of Pisa">Pisa</a> and then the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Appiani" title="Appiani">Appiani</a> of <a href="/wiki/Piombino" title="Piombino">Piombino</a>.</p>
<p>Scarlino remained part of the <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Piombino" title="Principality of Piombino">Principality of Piombino</a> until the early 19th century, when it became part of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Tuscany" title="Grand Duchy of Tuscany">Grand Duchy of Tuscany</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_sights">Main sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scarlino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Main sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Walls, built from the 11th century and restored in the 13th century.</li>
<li>Rocca Aldobrandesca ("Aldobrandeschi Castle"), including an angular and a round towers, plus stone defensive walls.</li>
<li>Church and convent of San Donato, which was the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Augustinians" title="Augustinians">Augustinians</a> from the 14th century. The church is in <a href="/wiki/Romanesque_architecture" title="Romanesque architecture">Romanesque</a> style.</li>
<li>Oratory of Santa Croce, with Renaissance frescoes.</li>
<li>Church of San Martino (14th century).</li>
<li>Convent of Monte Muro.</li>
</ul>
