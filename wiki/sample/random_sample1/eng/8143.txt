<p><b>Sky Tonia Ferreira</b> (born July 8, 1992) is an American singer, songwriter, model, and actress. As a teenager, Ferreira began uploading videos on <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">Myspace</a> of herself singing songs she had written, which led to her discovery by producers <a href="/wiki/Bloodshy_%26_Avant" title="Bloodshy &amp; Avant">Bloodshy &amp; Avant</a> and an eventual recording contract with <a href="/wiki/Parlophone" title="Parlophone">Parlophone</a> in 2009. She released her first <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/As_If!_(EP)" title="As If! (EP)">As If!</a></i>, in 2011, which combined elements of <a href="/wiki/Synthpop" title="Synthpop">electropop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance music</a>. Ferreira's second extended play <i><a href="/wiki/Ghost_(Sky_Ferreira_EP)" title="Ghost (Sky Ferreira EP)">Ghost</a></i> (2012), however, incorporated pop with more stripped-down song structures and featured collaborations with <a href="/wiki/Jon_Brion" title="Jon Brion">Jon Brion</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shirley_Manson" title="Shirley Manson">Shirley Manson</a>, as well as the critically acclaimed track "<a href="/wiki/Everything_Is_Embarrassing" title="Everything Is Embarrassing">Everything Is Embarrassing</a>", which she composed with <a href="/wiki/Dev_Hynes" title="Dev Hynes">Dev Hynes</a>.</p>
<p>After multiple delays and disputes with her record label, Ferreira's debut <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Night_Time,_My_Time" title="Night Time, My Time">Night Time, My Time</a></i> was released on October 29, 2013, and marked a departure from her former style, incorporating more experimental <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a> with <a href="/wiki/Synthpop" title="Synthpop">synthpop</a> elements. The album was preceded by its <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> "<a href="/wiki/You%27re_Not_the_One" title="You're Not the One">You're Not the One</a>". That year, she ventured into the film industry after appearing in <a href="/wiki/Eli_Roth" title="Eli Roth">Eli Roth</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Green_Inferno_(film)" title="The Green Inferno (film)">The Green Inferno</a></i> and garnered media attention after being arrested for <a href="/wiki/Drug_possession" title="Drug possession">drug possession</a>. She was exposed to a mainstream audience after having been announced as an opening act for the international <a href="/wiki/Bangerz_Tour" title="Bangerz Tour">Bangerz Tour</a>, headlined by <a href="/wiki/Miley_Cyrus" title="Miley Cyrus">Miley Cyrus</a>, which began on February 14, 2014.</p>
<p>Ferreira's earlier work incorporated elements of <a href="/wiki/Dance-pop" title="Dance-pop">dance-pop</a>, while her recent projects experiment with <a href="/wiki/Acoustic_music" title="Acoustic music">acoustic</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_wave_music" title="New wave music">new wave</a> and primarily <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a> musical styles. Her lyrical content originally incorporated themes of rebellion and teenage romance, and has since evolved to discuss personal insecurities and more mature romantic themes. Outside of her work in the entertainment industry, she has modeled for several brands and magazines, including serving as the spokeswoman for the hair care brand <a href="/wiki/Redken" title="Redken">Redken</a> in 2014.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_Ferreira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career_beginnings">Early life and career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_Ferreira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life and career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sky Tonia Ferreira was born on July 8, 1992 in <a href="/wiki/Venice,_Los_Angeles" title="Venice, Los Angeles">Venice Beach, Los Angeles, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marieclaire_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-marieclaire-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> About her mixed background, Ferreira has stated that “My dad is Brazilian and Portuguese and my mom is Native American. It's funny because I came out looking like, pretty white.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ferreira was largely raised by her grandmother, who had been <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>'s personal hairstylist for over 30 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-details_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-details-9">[9]</a></sup> She commented, saying: "I was raised around him, I always saw him [and] had holidays with him and stuff like that", adding that she was unaware of his fame until she was 13 years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Ferreira expressed interest in singing as a child and, with Jackson's encouragement, Ferreira's grandmother enrolled her in opera lessons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Ferreira has stated that she was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sexually_assaulted" title="Sexually assaulted">sexually assaulted</a> in her adolescence twice, firstly involving a neighbor, and secondly in an incident that involved a stranger who had broken into her home.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rookie_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-rookie-12">[12]</a></sup> In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Rookie_(magazine)" title="Rookie (magazine)">Rookie</a></i>, Ferreira said: "I've never said anything [about this publicly] because I don't want it to define me, but I feel like it's appropriate to say something [here]. People feel like it defines you, and it doesn't. It's really unfortunate and disgusting and traumatizing, but it doesn't make you who you are."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rookie_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-rookie-12">[12]</a></sup> Ferreira dropped out of high school after completing her sophomore year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ferreira later began maintaining a <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">Myspace</a> profile where she uploaded demo versions of self-written tracks. Shortly before her 15th birthday, she wrote a letter to producers <a href="/wiki/Bloodshy_%26_Avant" title="Bloodshy &amp; Avant">Bloodshy &amp; Avant</a> requesting that they offer a recording contract to her, which she says happened "kind of right off the bat" after they came across her profile.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In 2009, she was signed to <a href="/wiki/Parlophone" title="Parlophone">Parlophone</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and began finalizing the debut studio album that she says was first begun when she was 14 years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In 2010, she ventured into the film and modelling industries after starring in the independent film <i><a href="/wiki/Putty_Hill" title="Putty Hill">Putty Hill</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-17">[17]</a></sup> and appearing in the magazines <i><a href="/wiki/Dazed" title="Dazed">Dazed</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Interview_(magazine)" title="Interview (magazine)">Interview</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.9312:_As_If.21_and_Ghost">2011–12: <i>As If!</i> and <i>Ghost</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_Ferreira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2011–12: As If! and Ghost">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After releasing the tracks "17" and "<a href="/wiki/One_(Sky_Ferreira_song)" title="One (Sky Ferreira song)">One</a>", the latter having peaked at number 64 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Ferreira announced that her debut studio album would be released on January 11, 2011. Its planned <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> "<a href="/wiki/Obsession_(Sky_Ferreira_song)" title="Obsession (Sky Ferreira song)">Obsession</a>" had first appeared on the compilation album <i><a href="/wiki/Now_That%27s_What_I_Call_Music!_35_(U.S._series)" title="Now That's What I Call Music! 35 (U.S. series)">Now That's What I Call Music! 35</a></i> in 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> and peaked at number 37 on the US <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Club_Songs" title="Hot Dance Club Songs">Hot Dance Club Songs</a> chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Awards_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Awards-22">[22]</a></sup> These plans never came to fruition, and were substituted for the <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> (EP) <i><a href="/wiki/As_If!_(EP)" title="As If! (EP)">As If!</a></i> and its lead single "<a href="/wiki/Sex_Rules" title="Sex Rules">Sex Rules</a>" that March.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> A writer for <a href="/wiki/MuuMuse" title="MuuMuse">MuuMuse</a> spoke favorably of her "vocal and musical versatility" and commended the project as a "rock solid 5-track collection of next-level crunchy pop cuts."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MuuMuse_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-MuuMuse-24">[24]</a></sup> During this time, Ferreira was featured in the advertising campaign for the <a href="/wiki/Calvin_Klein" title="Calvin Klein">Calvin Klein</a> "CK One" product line alongside <a href="/wiki/Cassie_Ventura" title="Cassie Ventura">Cassie Ventura</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The following year, she was photographed by <a href="/wiki/Ellen_von_Unwerth" title="Ellen von Unwerth">Ellen von Unwerth</a> for the cover of <i><a href="/wiki/Vs._(magazine)" title="Vs. (magazine)">Vs.</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> and appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a> "Originals" commercial with rappers <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2011, Ferreira announced that her debut studio album would be released in 2012, with a lead single planned to precede its release that February.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> She was later revealed to have been working with <a href="/wiki/Jon_Brion" title="Jon Brion">Jon Brion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greg_Kurstin" title="Greg Kurstin">Greg Kurstin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Shirley_Manson" title="Shirley Manson">Shirley Manson</a> on the project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> In early 2012, Ferreira revealed <i>Wild at Heart</i> as the title of her record,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and confirmed "24 Hours" and "Swamp Girl" for its track listing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> "Lost in My Bedroom", described by Ferreira as "the most electro pop [song] on the album", surfaced online that March.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> After releasing the music video for the track "<a href="/wiki/Red_Lips" title="Red Lips">Red Lips</a>" in June, she stated that she renamed the record <i>I'm Not Alright</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-complex_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-complex-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>With her debut studio album still unreleased, Ferreira announced that her second extended play <i><a href="/wiki/Ghost_(Sky_Ferreira_EP)" title="Ghost (Sky Ferreira EP)">Ghost</a></i> would be released in October. It respectively peaked at numbers 8 and 71 on the US <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" title="Top Heatseekers">Top Heatseekers</a> and Alternative Albums component charts to the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Awards_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-Awards-22">[22]</a></sup> Its second single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Everything_is_Embarrassing" title="Everything is Embarrassing">Everything is Embarrassing</a>" was described by Katherine St. Asaph of <a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_(website)" title="Pitchfork (website)">Pitchfork</a> as the track where Ferreira "could well have her breakout moment", complimenting it for beginning with "snappy, crisply sung dance-pop about long-awaited moments" and eventually transitioning into "what should be a buoyant chorus".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pitchfork_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pitchfork-34">[34]</a></sup> In her debut television performance, Ferreira performed the track on <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_with_Jimmy_Fallon" title="Late Night with Jimmy Fallon">Late Night with Jimmy Fallon</a></i> in January 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The previously leaked track "Lost in My Bedroom" was included on <i>Ghost</i>, and was featured on an episode of <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Grey%27s_Anatomy" title="Grey's Anatomy">Grey's Anatomy</a></i> in December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.9314:_Night_Time.2C_My_Time_and_support_touring">2013–14: <i>Night Time, My Time</i> and support touring</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_Ferreira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2013–14: Night Time, My Time and support touring">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In June 2013, Ferreira starred in the short film <i>IRL</i> playing Angel, which has been described as "an eye-opening look into the reality of young life in New York".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IRL_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-IRL-37">[37]</a></sup> That month, she again renamed her debut studio album <i>I Will</i>, and stated that it would be released "later this summer".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marieclaire_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-marieclaire-5">[5]</a></sup> She noted that it would be composed of "ten up-tempo songs, all still pretty <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic</a>, although for some reason it comes across more <a href="/wiki/Pop_rock" title="Pop rock">pop-rocky</a> live."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> It was again renamed <i><a href="/wiki/Night_Time,_My_Time" title="Night Time, My Time">Night Time, My Time</a></i>, and announced to be released on October 29, 2013 by <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Upon its release, it received favorable reviews from <a href="/wiki/Music_journalism" title="Music journalism">music critics</a>; at <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, which assigns a <a href="/wiki/Standard_score" title="Standard score">normalized</a> rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream critics, the album received an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weighted_mean" title="Weighted mean">average</a> score of 79, which indicates "generally favorable reviews", based on 30 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> listed it as the fifth-best debut album of 2013,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> noting its potent blending of synthpop elements with a more grunge-inspired <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a> sound. The album was also listed as the 25th best overall album of the year by <a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> The record debuted at number 45 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Awards_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-Awards-22">[22]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/You%27re_Not_the_One" title="You're Not the One">You're Not the One</a>" was released as the lead single from <i>Night Time, My Time</i> on September 24, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September, Ferreira starred as Kaycee in the independent film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Green_Inferno_(film)" title="The Green Inferno (film)">The Green Inferno</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheGreenInferno_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-TheGreenInferno-44">[44]</a></sup> In the early morning of September 14, she and her boyfriend, <a href="/wiki/DIIV" title="DIIV">DIIV</a> frontman <a href="/wiki/Zachary_Cole_Smith" title="Zachary Cole Smith">Zachary Cole Smith</a>, were arrested at a traffic stop in <a href="/wiki/Saugerties_(village),_New_York" title="Saugerties (village), New York">Saugerties, New York</a> for <a href="/wiki/Drug_possession" title="Drug possession">drug possession</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Ferreira, who was in possession of <a href="/wiki/MDMA" title="MDMA">ecstasy</a> tablets, was arrested on drug possession and <a href="/wiki/Resisting_arrest" title="Resisting arrest">resisting arrest</a>; Smith was in possession of <a href="/wiki/Heroin" title="Heroin">heroin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> She bailed herself out of $1,500 with her <a href="/wiki/American_Express" title="American Express">American Express</a> credit card.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> In October, Ferreira cancelled the final week of her tour with <a href="/wiki/Vampire_Weekend" title="Vampire Weekend">Vampire Weekend</a> after sustaining a hemorrhage to her vocal cords,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> which she later added was caused by a vocal node that was repeatedly misdiagnosed as laryngitis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> In November, Ferreira and <a href="/wiki/Icona_Pop" title="Icona Pop">Icona Pop</a> were announced as the opening acts for <a href="/wiki/Miley_Cyrus" title="Miley Cyrus">Miley Cyrus</a>' <a href="/wiki/Bangerz_Tour" title="Bangerz Tour">Bangerz Tour</a>, which began on February 14, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> Later that month, she released her third extended play, <i>Night Time, My Time: B-Sides Part 1</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> In December, Ferreira was announced as the spokeswoman for hair care brand <a href="/wiki/Redken" title="Redken">Redken</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redken_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-redken-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 20, 2014, during the third performance opening for the Bangerz Tour, Ferreira fell and split open her shin onstage. After completing the performance, Ferreira was taken by ambulance to an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anaheim" title="Anaheim">Anaheim</a> hospital and treated with 60 stitches. As a result of the injury, she was forced to temporarily pull out of support for the tour, but returned shortly after.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> On November 20, 2014, while on her North American tour, Ferreira debuted a new track called "Guardian".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.E2.80.93present:_Masochism_and_upcoming_film_roles">2015–present: <i>Masochism</i> and upcoming film roles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_Ferreira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2015–present: Masochism and upcoming film roles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 16, 2014, Ferreira announced via Twitter that she was working on her second album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> She described the sound of the album as "more aggressive" and is working with <a href="/wiki/Ariel_Rechtshaid" title="Ariel Rechtshaid">Ariel Rechtshaid</a> and <a href="/wiki/Justin_Raisen" title="Justin Raisen">Justin Raisen</a>, with whom she produced <i>Night Time, My Time</i>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Gillespie" title="Bobby Gillespie">Bobby Gillespie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Innes" title="Andrew Innes">Andrew Innes</a> of Scottish band <a href="/wiki/Primal_Scream" title="Primal Scream">Primal Scream</a>. Ferreira also expressed the wish to collaborate with <a href="/wiki/Gesaffelstein" title="Gesaffelstein">Gesaffelstein</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> She also appeared in the 2016 comedy-drama film <i><a href="/wiki/Elvis_%26_Nixon" title="Elvis &amp; Nixon">Elvis &amp; Nixon</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Liza_Johnson" title="Liza Johnson">Liza Johnson</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FiveCast_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-FiveCast-57">[57]</a></sup> and has joined the cast of <i>The Trust</i>, which stars <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Cage" title="Nicolas Cage">Nicolas Cage</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elijah_Wood" title="Elijah Wood">Elijah Wood</a>, and is directed by Benjamin and <a href="/wiki/Alex_Brewer" title="Alex Brewer">Alex Brewer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thetrust_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-thetrust-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following fan speculation, Ferreira confirmed on April 7 that her upcoming second studio album will be titled <i>Masochism</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> On May 12, 2015, the singer announced that she would be releasing a film co-written and directed by her frequent collaborator Grant Singer accompanied by a new single with the album scheduled to be released later in the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Choo" title="Jimmy Choo">Jimmy Choo</a> revealed on June 4 that Ferreira would be the face of their newest fragrance named "Illicit".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> She collaborated with Primal Scream on the song "Where the Light Gets In", which was released on February 1, 2016 as the lead single from the band's eleventh studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Chaosmosis" title="Chaosmosis">Chaosmosis</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> It was announced on April 25, 2016 that Ferreira will star in the <a href="/wiki/Twin_Peaks_(2017_TV_series)" title="Twin Peaks (2017 TV series)">2017 sequel</a> to <a href="/wiki/David_Lynch" title="David Lynch">David Lynch</a>'s 1990s television series <i><a href="/wiki/Twin_Peaks" title="Twin Peaks">Twin Peaks</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TwinPeaks_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-TwinPeaks-63">[63]</a></sup> In October 2016, she was cast in <a href="/wiki/Jonas_%C3%85kerlund" title="Jonas Åkerlund">Jonas Åkerlund</a>'s upcoming film <i>Lords of Chaos</i>, based on the 1998 <a href="/wiki/Lords_of_Chaos_(book)" title="Lords of Chaos (book)">book of the same name</a> by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jenkins_Moynihan" title="Michael Jenkins Moynihan">Michael Moynihan</a> and Didrik Søderlind.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">Musical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_Ferreira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Musical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ferreira is primarily a <a href="/wiki/Synthpop" title="Synthpop">synthpop</a> singer,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> also being described as <a href="/wiki/Dance-pop" title="Dance-pop">dance-pop</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Indie_pop" title="Indie pop">indie pop</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-details_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-details-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">alternative rock</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Art_pop" title="Art pop">art pop</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> Her earlier work incorporated elements of dance-pop and <a href="/wiki/Electropop" title="Electropop">electropop</a>, most notably seen in tracks from <i><a href="/wiki/As_If!_(EP)" title="As If! (EP)">As If!</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Ghost_(Sky_Ferreira_EP)" title="Ghost (Sky Ferreira EP)">Ghost</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Popdust_76-0"><a href="#cite_note-Popdust-76">[76]</a></sup> this one also included elements of <a href="/wiki/Acoustic_music" title="Acoustic music">acoustic</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_wave_music" title="New wave music">new wave</a>. Her lyrical content originally incorporated themes of rebellion and teenage romance; Andrew Unterberger from Popdust opined that "17" plays the girl-gone-wild tale as a near-horror story, clearly meant to shock and upset", while noting that "One" "instead features a robo'd out Sky bemoaning the breakdown of connection in a relationship, and her own inability to feel anything as a result."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Popdust_76-1"><a href="#cite_note-Popdust-76">[76]</a></sup> As her career progressed, Ferreira more frequently experimented with elements of <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a>, which were commonly heard in her first album <i><a href="/wiki/Night_Time,_My_Time" title="Night Time, My Time">Night Time, My Time</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup></p>
<p>Julianne Escobedo Shepberd from <i>Rolling Stone</i> wrote that Ferreira's "songs are catchy, but they're also thickly glazed with fuzz and synths, evoking influences like <a href="/wiki/Suicide_(band)" title="Suicide (band)">Suicide</a>, <a href="/wiki/Siouxsie_Sioux" title="Siouxsie Sioux">Siouxsie Sioux</a> and the krautrock group <a href="/wiki/Harmonia_(band)" title="Harmonia (band)">Harmonia</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> Karolina Ramos from <i><a href="/wiki/The_GW_Hatchet" title="The GW Hatchet">The GW Hatchet</a></i> compared Ferreira to New Zealand recording artist <a href="/wiki/Lorde" title="Lorde">Lorde</a>, both of whom she felt "tackle contemporary love, desire and insecurity with depth, composure and frankness." She compared her musical style to 1980s <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop music</a>, and noted that "her throaty, sultry vocals call to mind <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lana_del_Rey" title="Lana del Rey">Lana del Rey</a>, abandoning brightness and vivacity for a colder tone. Still, Ferreira can drop the edge, with gentler tracks like "Sad Dream" showcasing her vocal range and seldom-observed softness."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> Andrew Unterberger stated that "through a variety of singles, EPs, guest features and live appearances, Sky has proven herself one of the most talented singers, creative songwriters and savvy collaborators currently working in the genre", but blamed "bad marketing, label disputes and her own perfectionism" for the extended delay of <i>Night Time, My Time</i>; he also stated that the repeated renaming of the record itself "should give you some idea of the musical identity issues she's suffered over the years."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Popdust_76-2"><a href="#cite_note-Popdust-76">[76]</a></sup></p>
<p>After establishing a friendship with recording artist <a href="/wiki/Miley_Cyrus" title="Miley Cyrus">Miley Cyrus</a> in 2013, Gregory E. Miller from the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Post" title="New York Post">New York Post</a></i> noted that the media and general public would continue "following her every move". Ferreira commented that "[Miley's] obviously way bigger and all eyes [are] on her, but I feel like we're kind of going through a similar thing where everyone's trying to say that we're trying to make ourselves objects, but really, everyone else is making it more like that—sensationalizing sex and reading into things. It's nice to have someone that gets it." Ferreira had also generated a minor controversy after appearing topless on the cover for <i>Night Time, My Time</i>, to which she responded "there's nothing about that photo that's pornographic; we came on this earth nude."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup> Ferreira is also a <a href="/wiki/Feminism" title="Feminism">feminist</a>, which has influenced her work; she has said "I feel like I'm doing a bad job of being a feminist if I'm not making someone angry."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Philanthropy">Philanthropy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_Ferreira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Philanthropy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In July 2014, Ferreira performed at the <a href="/wiki/Music_Hall_of_Williamsburg" title="Music Hall of Williamsburg">Music Hall of Williamsburg</a> in New York City to benefit the <a href="/wiki/David_Lynch_Foundation" title="David Lynch Foundation">David Lynch Foundation</a>. Ferreira announced that the funds raised would "go towards <a href="/wiki/Transcendental_Meditation_technique" title="Transcendental Meditation technique">Transcendental Meditation</a> (TM) programs for at-risk students; veterans with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PTSD" title="PTSD">PTSD</a>; women who are survivors of domestic violence; American Indians suffering from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diabetes" title="Diabetes">diabetes</a>; the homeless and incarcerate." Of her own practice she said, "TM sort of saved my life."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hollywood_88-0"><a href="#cite_note-hollywood-88">[88]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fader_89-0"><a href="#cite_note-fader-89">[89]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bv_90-0"><a href="#cite_note-bv-90">[90]</a></sup> In support of the <a href="/wiki/Dakota_Access_Pipeline_protests" title="Dakota Access Pipeline protests">Dakota Access Pipeline protests</a>, Ferreira performed at a benefit concert entitled <i>We Rock With Standing Rock</i> in December 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup></p>
