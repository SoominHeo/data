<p><b>Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher &amp; Flom LLP and Affiliates</b> (often shortened to <b>Skadden Arps</b>; <b>Skadden</b>; or <b>SASM&amp;F</b>), founded in 1948, is an <a href="/wiki/International" title="International">international</a> <a href="/wiki/Law_firm" title="Law firm">law firm</a> based in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. With approximately 1,700 attorneys, it is one of the highest-grossing law firms in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmLawGlobal100_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-AmLawGlobal100-8">[8]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> magazine has called Skadden "<a href="/wiki/Wall_Street" title="Wall Street">Wall Street</a>'s most powerful law firm." The firm has been ranked "America's Best Corporate Law Firm" in <i>Corporate Board Member</i>'s survey of public company directors every year since 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fticonsulting1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-fticonsulting1-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Timeline">Timeline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skadden,_Arps,_Slate,_Meagher_%26_Flom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Timeline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><b>1948</b> — The firm is founded in New York by Marshall Skadden, John Slate and Les Arps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-History_Skadden_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-History_Skadden-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1959</b> — <a href="/wiki/William_Meagher" title="William Meagher">William Meagher</a> joins the firm. Elizabeth Head, the firm's first female attorney, is hired.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-History_Skadden_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-History_Skadden-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1960</b> — The firm's name becomes Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher &amp; Flom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-History_Skadden_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-History_Skadden-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1961</b> — Peter Mullen, who will later serve as Skadden's first executive partner, joins the firm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1973</b> — The firm opens its second office, in Boston.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-History_Skadden_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-History_Skadden-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1981</b> — <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Peggy_Kerr&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Peggy Kerr (page does not exist)">Peggy L. Kerr</a> becomes Skadden's first female partner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1985</b> — Skadden ranks as one of the U.S.'s three largest law firms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-History_Skadden_11-4"><a href="#cite_note-History_Skadden-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1987</b> — The firm opens its first international office, in Tokyo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-History_Skadden_11-5"><a href="#cite_note-History_Skadden-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1988</b> — The firm founds the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Skadden_Fellowship_Foundation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Skadden Fellowship Foundation (page does not exist)">Skadden Fellowship Foundation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-History_Skadden_11-6"><a href="#cite_note-History_Skadden-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>2000</b> — Skadden's New York City headquarters moves to <a href="/wiki/4_Times_Square" title="4 Times Square">4 Times Square</a>, the "Condé Nast Building."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-History_Skadden_11-7"><a href="#cite_note-History_Skadden-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>2008 —</b> With the City College of New York, the firm launches the Skadden, Arps Honors Program, to increase diversity in law schools and the legal profession.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>2011</b> — <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Flom" title="Joseph Flom">Joseph Flom</a>, the last living name partner, dies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>2014 —</b> The firm opens its 22nd office, in Seoul.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>2015 —</b> Skadden becomes the first law firm to advise on more than $1 trillion worth of deals in a single year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Locations">Locations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skadden,_Arps,_Slate,_Meagher_%26_Flom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Locations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of December 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Skadden,_Arps,_Slate,_Meagher_%26_Flom&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, Skadden has 22 offices worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Key_people">Key people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skadden,_Arps,_Slate,_Meagher_%26_Flom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Key people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In February 2011, there were 432 Skadden partners worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AttorneyList_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-AttorneyList-19">[19]</a></sup> As of December 2016, there are 381 partners worldwide. Unlike some firms that have introduced two-tier partnerships with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Equity_partner" title="Equity partner">equity and non-equity partners</a>, Skadden maintains a one-tier partnership, in which all partners are equity partners and share ownership of the firm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-how_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-how-20">[20]</a></sup> Among the more notable partners, of counsel and counsel are:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Eileen_Nugent&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Eileen Nugent (page does not exist)">Eileen T. Nugent</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> co-global leader of the firm's transaction practices.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_V._Simpson&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Scott V. Simpson (page does not exist)">Scott V. Simpson</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> co-global leader of the firm's transaction practices.</li>

<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=David_Rievman&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="David Rievman (page does not exist)">David M. Rievman</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> global leader of the firm's regulatory practices.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Thomas_H._Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Thomas H. Kennedy (page does not exist)">Thomas H. Kennedy</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> global leader of knowledge strategy.</li>

<li>Judge <a href="/wiki/Stephen_C._Robinson" title="Stephen C. Robinson">Stephen C. <p>In 2015 and 2016, Skadden was the fourth largest law firm in the U.S. by revenue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> In the 2015 Global 100 survey by <i>The American Lawyer</i>, Skadden ranked as the fourth highest grossing law firm in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-americanlawyer.com_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-americanlawyer.com-31">[31]</a></sup> In 2016, Skadden had approximately 1,700 attorneys in 22 offices;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-americanlawyer.com_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-americanlawyer.com-31">[31]</a></sup> in 2011, the firm had approximately 1,900 attorneys in 23 offices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Measured by the number of attorneys, Skadden is the fifth largest law firm in New York and 12th largest in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-americanlawyer.com_31-2"><a href="#cite_note-americanlawyer.com-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> In 2016, Skadden was 187th on <i>Forbes'</i> list of America's Largest Private Companies by revenue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Previously, the firm ranked 335th<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> in 1995, 194th<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> in 2003 and 213th in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Based on the 2017 Vault Law 100 survey of law firm associates by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vault.com" title="Vault.com">Vault.com</a>, Skadden is the third most prestigious U.S. firm at which to work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> The NYSE Governance Services and advisory firm FTI Consulting named Skadden the top U.S. law firm in 2016, a position held by the firm since 2001; the annual findings are published by <i>Corporate Board Member</i> in its list of "America's Best Corporate Law Firms."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> In 2015, Skadden became the first law firm ever to handle more than $1 trillion in M&amp;A deals in a single year<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> and, for the third time in six years, the <i>Financial Times</i>' "Innovative Lawyers" report named Skadden the most innovative law firm in North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> In the 2016 edition of <i>Chambers USA: America's Leading Lawyers for Business</i>, Skadden received the most top rankings among law firms, including 67 Band 1 attorney rankings, 35 Band 1 practice rankings and five Star Individual rankings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skadden,_Arps,_Slate,_Meagher_%26_Flom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Skadden_Fellowship_Foundation">Skadden Fellowship Foundation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skadden,_Arps,_Slate,_Meagher_%26_Flom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Skadden Fellowship Foundation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Through the Skadden Fellowship Foundation, the firm sponsors law school graduates who wish to practice public interest law. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foundation_(charity)" title="Foundation (charity)">foundation</a> was established in 1988 in honor of the firm's 40th anniversary. The <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> has called the program "a legal Peace Corps."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fellowship_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-fellowship-48">[48]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Fellow" title="Fellow">Fellows</a> work with a sponsoring organization that provides legal services to the <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Old_age" title="Old age">elderly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Homelessness" title="Homelessness">homeless</a>, <a href="/wiki/Disability" title="Disability">disabled</a> and <a href="/wiki/Disfranchisement" title="Disfranchisement">disenfranchised</a>. Skadden pays fellows a <a href="/wiki/Salary" title="Salary">salary</a> of $52,000 (as of 2016),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> plus all the fringe benefits the sponsoring organization offers its employees. Through 2016, the firm has awarded 791 fellowships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> Since the program's inception, nearly 90 percent of its fellows have remained in public interest or public sector work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_contributions">Political contributions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skadden,_Arps,_Slate,_Meagher_%26_Flom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Political contributions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Skadden partners and employees tend to support and contribute more to <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic</a> political candidates than to <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republicans</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> Prominent lawyers at the firm endorsed and financially supported <a href="/wiki/John_Kerry" title="John Kerry">John Kerry</a> in <a href="/wiki/John_Kerry_presidential_campaign,_2004" title="John Kerry presidential campaign, 2004">his campaign to become president of the United States in 2004</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> In the run-up to <a href="/wiki/Super_Tuesday" title="Super Tuesday">Super Tuesday</a> 2008, Skadden hosted a phone bank in support of <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama_presidential_campaign,_2008" title="Barack Obama presidential campaign, 2008">2008 presidential campaign</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Center_for_Responsive_Politics" title="Center for Responsive Politics">Center for Responsive Politics</a>, Skadden was one of the top law firms contributing to federal candidates during the 2012 election cycle, donating $1.98 million, 76% to Democrats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-topcontributors_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-topcontributors-57">[57]</a></sup> By comparison, during that same period <a href="/wiki/Akin_Gump_Strauss_Hauer_%26_Feld" title="Akin Gump Strauss Hauer &amp; Feld">Akin Gump Strauss Hauer &amp; Feld</a> donated $2.56 million, 66% to Democrats<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-topcontributors_57-1"><a href="#cite_note-topcontributors-57">[57]</a></sup> and oil conglomerate <a href="/wiki/ExxonMobil" title="ExxonMobil">ExxonMobil</a> donated $2.66 million, 88% to Republicans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> From 1990 through 2008, Skadden contributed $11.93 million to federal campaigns; between 2000 and 2008 the firm spent $2.2 million on lobbying.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
