<p><i><b>Sketchbook</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">スケッチブック</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Suketchibukku</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series written and illustrated by <a href="/wiki/Totan_Kobako" title="Totan Kobako">Totan Kobako</a>. First serialized in the April 2002 issue of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comic_Blade" title="Comic Blade">Comic Blade</a></i>, the individual chapters are collected and published by <a href="/wiki/Mag_Garden" title="Mag Garden">Mag Garden</a>. Chapters have also appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Comic_Blade_Masamune" title="Comic Blade Masamune">Comic Blade Masamune</a></i>. An <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> adaptation, under the title <i>Sketchbook ~full color's~</i>, aired in Japan between October 2 and December 25, 2007. The story revolves around a group of high school students, mostly female, in the art club at their high school. The main character is a young, shy and quiet girl named Sora Kajiwara who goes through life at her own pace.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sketchbook_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Sketchbook</i> begins with Sora Kajiwara joining the art club of her unnamed <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka" title="Fukuoka">Fukuoka</a> high school, and goes on to chronicle her subsequent experiences and those of her fellow club members.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sketchbook_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Art_club">Art club</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sketchbook_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Art club">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Freshmen">Freshmen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sketchbook_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Freshmen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<dl>
<dt>Sora Kajiwara <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">梶原 空</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kajiwara Sora</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Kana_Hanazawa" title="Kana Hanazawa">Kana Hanazawa</a></span></dd>
<dd>The protagonist of the series, a girl with a playful spirit and an observant nature. Her undiminished, childlike and playful behavior is shown throughout the series and tends to drive her actions. The world is her toybox; she rarely encounters an object she does not want to experiment with. Her active, somewhat impractical imagination envisions a multitude of bizarre scenarios, leading her to often absurd assumptions and conclusions. She is particularly fond of cats, befriending and naming various neighborhood strays, of tea, always keeping a flask-full handy, and of drawing, which induces her to join her school's art club, thus commencing the events of <i>Sketchbook</i>.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>Sora is extremely quiet, rarely speaking in the anime. She is easily frightened, and even mildly startling events can send her into hiding. On more than one occasion she flees from her close friend, Asō, because her hairstyle is unfamiliar. Among the things she has admitted or been shown to find scary are bowling balls, high voltage towers, gym instructors, escalators, security cameras, frozen pike, passing trains, Daichi Negishi, and a balloon-seller in a cat costume.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>Sora has long, straight hair, reaching about to her waist. Its color is uncertain: in different illustrations, it has appeared in various shades of blue, green, purple, and grey; in the strip it is not distinguishable from black; in the animation, it is a light blue-grey. When not in her school uniform, which comprises a white dress shirt, green tie, double-breasted blue blazer and knee-length skirt, she usually wears a light collared shirt over a dark undershirt, with knee-length trousers. When out on a walk or hike she accouters herself with a wide-brimmed straw hat, backpack, umbrella, tea flask, and sketchbook. She will also occasionally wear a watch on her left wrist.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Natsumi Asō <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">麻生 夏海</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Asō Natsumi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Asuka_Nakase" title="Asuka Nakase">Asuka Nakase</a></span></dd>
<dd>A <a href="/wiki/Hichiku_dialect" title="Hichiku dialect">Hakata-ben</a> speaker with twin ponytails and reddish-purple hair. She has the fairly unique hobby of making hand-puppets (which resemble the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muppets" title="Muppets">Muppets</a> of <a href="/wiki/Fraggle_Rock" title="Fraggle Rock">Fraggle Rock</a>) and conducting conversations among them; they serve alternatively as diversion, study tools, and gloves. There are five, all siblings, named Kerokichi, Ranran, Michael, Robert, and Tom-Tom. Asō joins the art club together with Kajiwara, whom she meets on the way to the club room; the two remain good friends afterward. Her best friend appears to be Hazuki, without whom she is rarely seen. While generally a very thoughtful and pragmatic person, Asō can occasionally let her boisterousness get the better of her.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Hazuki Torikai <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">鳥飼 葉月</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Torikai Hazuki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Yui_Makino" title="Yui Makino">Yui Makino</a></span></dd>
<dd>Hazuki is a parsimonious blond-haired girl and the third freshman to join the art club. Her extreme frugality, coupled with her perfectionist, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blood_types_in_Japanese_culture" title="Blood types in Japanese culture">Type-A</a> personality, can turn everyday errands into protracted ordeals and even the simplest transaction into an onerous travail. Even her approach to art is largely determined by economic considerations. Despite these tendencies, she is probably the most overall well-adjusted, level-headed, and dependable of the major characters. She is also extremely polite and very caring toward animals. Hazuki lives alone. She regularly appears in the strip by herself, the only character other than Kajiwara to do so.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Kate <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ケイト</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Keito</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Y%C5%ABko_Got%C5%8D" title="Yūko Gotō">Yūko Gotō</a></span></dd>
<dd>A transfer student from Canada. Kate is already fluent, albeit prone to sometimes outlandish mistakes, in Japanese before coming to the art club. Perhaps indicative of her accent, much of her speech is rendered in <a href="/wiki/Katakana" title="Katakana">katakana</a>. She seems fascinated by the Japanese language and writing system and by the workings of language in general, constantly pointing out oddities and questioning rough spots. Why she came to Japan is never mentioned.</dd>
</dl>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Upperclassmen">Upperclassmen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sketchbook_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Upperclassmen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<dl>
<dt>Kokage Kuga <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">空閑 木陰</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kuga Kokage</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Momoko_Sait%C5%8D" title="Momoko Saitō">Momoko Saitō</a></span></dd>
<dd>A dark-haired girl of diminutive stature. Kuga's perpetually expressionless face and bizarre behavior suggest a morose, perhaps <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/sinister" title="wikt:sinister">sinister</a> character, but rather than depressed, withdrawn, or morbid, she is in fact playful, mischievous, and jocular (and occasionally morbid). <a href="/wiki/Totan_Kobako" title="Totan Kobako">The author</a> suggests that her eccentricity is actually an affectation, designed to help her communicate with others. Asō and Kuga sometimes ride the train together in the manga.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Daichi Negishi <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">根岸 大地</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Negishi Daichi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Hiro_Shimono" title="Hiro Shimono">Hiro Shimono</a></span></dd>
<dd>An outburst-prone second-year student who is one of two males in the art club. Even minor annoyances can provoke him to fits of rage, shouting and throwing whatever object happens to be handy. Despite his temper, Negishi is generally a fairly pleasant individual. He is also the preferred target of the inveterate goofball and prankster Kamiya Asaka. He and Kajiwara seem to be fairly close, although she is frightened of him.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Juju Sasaki <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">佐々木 樹々</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sasaki Juju</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tomoe_%C5%8Cmi" title="Tomoe Ōmi">Tomoe Ōmi</a></span></dd>
<dd>Juju is tall, plays guitar, and is terrified of caterpillars. She once thought that dinosaurs breathed fire. She is drawn so that her eyes appear to be perpetually closed, and her general inattentiveness to her surroundings seems to corroborate this possibility.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Suzukaze Combo <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">涼風コンビ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Suzukaze Konbi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd>The Suzukaze Combo is a pair of second-year students who usually appear together and who evidently view life as one long comedy routine. They seem to be aware of their status as comic strip characters, and often <a href="/wiki/Fourth_wall" title="Fourth wall">address the audience directly</a>, although it has alternatively been suggested that they're just talking to thin air. Their considerable arsenal of jokes, tricks, and gags was at least partially honed under the tutelage of Asakura Soyogi. The "Suzukaze" nickname derives from an alternate reading of the characters for "Ryō" and "Fū".</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>
<dl>
<dt>Ryō Tanabe <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">田辺 涼</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tanabe Ryō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Masumi_Asano" title="Masumi Asano">Masumi Asano</a></span></dd>
<dt>Fū Himuro <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">氷室 風</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Himuro Fū</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Natsuko_Kuwatani" title="Natsuko Kuwatani">Natsuko Kuwatani</a></span></dd>
</dl>
</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Nagisa Kurihara <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">栗原 渚</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kurihara Nagisa</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Yukari_Tamura" title="Yukari Tamura">Yukari Tamura</a></span></dd>
<dd>An inquisitive but easy-going girl with keen biological interests. Kurihara's knowledge of this field is literally encyclopedic, in the sense that it is only limited by what appears in scientific encyclopedias. She identifies and interacts with a variety of plant and animal life, sharing some of their unique characteristics with other club members and with the audience. Her resourcefulness and adaptability have drawn great admiration from Kajiwara. Classmate and best friend of Juju.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Asaka Kamiya <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">神谷 朝霞</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamiya Asaka</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Ami_Koshimizu" title="Ami Koshimizu">Ami Koshimizu</a></span></dd>
<dd>A dangerously imaginative troublemaker with pink hair and a ponytail. In contrast to the other club members, Kamiya's artwork is featured frequently and is a major facet of her character. Her creations usually can only marginally be called art, and are often inspired by puns; among them: a plush screwdriver, a kangaroo costume, a fanning machine, a kiwifruit kiwi, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maneki_Neko" title="Maneki Neko">Maneki Neko</a> beckoning with both paws, and a t-shirt with a hanger attached. She is physically strong. Identified as the "art club's trump card".</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Tsukiyo Ōba <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">大庭 月夜</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ōba Tsukiyo</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Mamiko_Noto" title="Mamiko Noto">Mamiko Noto</a></span></dd>
<dd>A second-year student who rarely shows up to art club. Ōba has unkempt hair and long bangs which hang over and conceal her face (and obstruct her vision). These factors contribute to her apparent anonymity and forgettability, as other members of the art club often fail to recognize her, both facetiously and otherwise.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Ujō Sugyō <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">須尭 雨情</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sugyō Ujō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Hideki_Tasaka" title="Hideki Tasaka">Hideki Tasaka</a></span></dd>
<dd>The art club's president, a senior. He is referred to as simply "President"; his actual name is used so infrequently that newer club members not only forget what it is, but forget that he has one at all. He is the most normal and level-headed character of them all, remaining calm and collected at all times. He is also a responsible student and can speak English.</dd>
</dl>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Teachers">Teachers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sketchbook_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Teachers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<dl>
<dt>Hiyori Kasugano <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">春日野 日和</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kasugano Hiyori</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Ry%C5%8D_Hirohashi" title="Ryō Hirohashi">Ryō Hirohashi</a></span></dd>
<dd>The exceedingly immature art teacher in charge of the club. Unmotivated, unambitious, and uninterested in society's expectations of her, Kasugano plays ball in the art room, cooks food and watches television in the preparation room, tends to forget what year it is, and keeps a pet chicken named Pī-chan. In contrast to her reckless personality, her driving is very slow and deliberate.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Soyogi Asakura <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">朝倉 そよぎ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Asakura Soyogi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd>A friend of Kasugano's, and a fellow art teacher at the high school. Asakura is a talented magician and has demonstrated the capacity to perform extraordinary feats of skill. She at some point served as a mentor to the Suzukaze Combo, who address her as "master" and continue to augment their repertoire of tricks and gags by emulating her. Whereas Kasugano's exact age is never given, Asakura is identified as being 24 years old.</dd>
</dl>

<dl>

<dd>Another former art club member, Shibata was Ueno's classmate and is evidently still in contact with him. She is good friends with Kuga, whom she meets on a return visit to the art club. Shibata is the only character to <a href="/wiki/Meganekko" title="Meganekko">wear glasses</a>.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Sekka Kamiya <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">神谷 雪花</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamiya Sekka</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd>Asaka's elder sister, the cook among the three Kamiya siblings. Not fond of tomatoes or competition.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Raika Kamiya <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">神谷 雷火</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kamiya Raika</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd>The 23-year-old elder brother of Asaka and Sekka. While never a member of the art club, he knows Kasugano; he also met Kate and Kajiwara in an art supply store. On a visit to the high school for a parent-teacher conference, he demonstrates himself to be as forgetful and absent-minded as his goofy youngest sister.</dd>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Cats">Cats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sketchbook_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Cats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<dl>
<dt>Mike <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ミケ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>

<dd>A Hakata-ben speaking calico cat who functions as the protagonist among the cat characters. Mike is noted for her mean glare and rough demeanor, but has a surprisingly weak, cute cry that belies these traits. Her name derives from the Japanese term for a calico cat.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Hā <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ハー</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Shizuka_It%C5%8D" title="Shizuka Itō">Shizuka Itō</a></span></dd>
<dd>A dark-eyed female, named for the sound of her cry. She has an affected manner of speech, adding "-nya" to the end of each sentence. Initially unable to climb to high places, but seems to have overcome this. Is bilingual, and thus able to communicate with the foreign cat Buchi.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Grey <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">グレ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Gure</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>

<dd>Mike's son, with white and gray fur and thin gray stripes on his back and cheeks. Temporarily a house-cat, but appears to have returned to being a stray. Appears during the anthropomorphic episodes, but remains incapable of speech.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Shamu <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">シャム</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd>Though terrified of humans, Shamu is an extremely tough, powerful cat, strong enough to single-handedly defeat the three-cat alliance of Mike, Hā, and Grey. He is not a stray, but an owned cat, and wears a collar.</dd>
</dl>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sketchbook_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Written and illustrated by <a href="/wiki/Totan_Kobako" title="Totan Kobako">Totan Kobako</a>, <i>Sketchbook</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Yonkoma" title="Yonkoma">four-panel</a> <a href="/wiki/Comic_strip" title="Comic strip">comic strip</a> <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series first serialized in <a href="/wiki/Mag_Garden" title="Mag Garden">Mag Garden</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comic_Blade" title="Comic Blade">Comic Blade</a></i> magazine starting with the April 2002 issue. The manga has made additional appearances in another manga magazine published by Mag Garden named <i><a href="/wiki/Comic_Blade_Masamune" title="Comic Blade Masamune">Comic Blade Masamune</a></i>. An additional volume, <i>Sketchbook Shucchōban</i>, was published on January 10, 2006 as a collection of bonus material which initially appeared in <i>Comic Blade Masamune</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MagGardenShucch.C5.8Dban-vol01_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-MagGardenShucch.C5.8Dban-vol01-1">[1]</a></sup> When <i>Comic Blade</i> ceased publication with the September 2014 issue,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <i>Sketchbook</i> continued serialized in the online version of <i>Comic Blade</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> in addition to a concurrent serialization in Mag Garden's <i><a href="/wiki/Monthly_Comic_Garden" title="Monthly Comic Garden">Monthly Comic Garden</a></i> magazine with the October 2014 issue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The manga is published in <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> in Chinese by <a href="/wiki/Tong_Li_Publishing" title="Tong Li Publishing">Tong Li Publishing</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sketchbook_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> series adaptation, under the title <i>Sketchbook ~full color's~</i>, is produced by the <a href="/wiki/Animation_studio" title="Animation studio">animation studio</a> <a href="/wiki/Hal_Film_Maker" title="Hal Film Maker">Hal Film Maker</a> and directed by <a href="/wiki/Yoshimasa_Hiraike" title="Yoshimasa Hiraike">Yoshimasa Hiraike</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The series aired 13 episodes in Japan between October 2 and December 25, 2007 on <a href="/wiki/TV_Tokyo" title="TV Tokyo">TV Tokyo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Four pieces of <a href="/wiki/Theme_music" title="Theme music">theme music</a> are used for the episodes; two opening themes and two ending themes. The first opening theme is "Kaze Sagashi" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">風さがし</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> by Natsumi Kiyoura which is used for most of the episodes, and the second opening theme used only for episode 12 is "Natsu no Kioku" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">夏の記憶</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> by Natsumi Kiyoura. The first ending theme is "Sketchbook o Motta mama" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">スケッチブックを持ったまま</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> by <a href="/wiki/Yui_Makino" title="Yui Makino">Yui Makino</a> which is used for the first 12 episodes, and the second ending theme used for episode 13 is "Tanpopo Suisha" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">たんぽぽ水車</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> by <a href="/wiki/Kana_Hanazawa" title="Kana Hanazawa">Kana Hanazawa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asuka_Nakase" title="Asuka Nakase">Asuka Nakase</a>, and Yui Makino. Other music used in the series is by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ken_Muramatsu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ken Muramatsu (page does not exist)">Ken Muramatsu</a>.</p>
