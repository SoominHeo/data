<p><b>Schoepfiaceae</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> of <a href="/wiki/Flowering_plant" title="Flowering plant">flowering plants</a> recognized in the <a href="/wiki/APG_III_system" title="APG III system">APG III system</a> of 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-APG3_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-APG3-1">[1]</a></sup> The family has previously only been recognized by few taxonomists; the plants in question usually being assigned to family <a href="/wiki/Olacaceae" title="Olacaceae">Olacaceae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Santalaceae" title="Santalaceae">Santalaceae</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-APweb_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-APweb-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The genus <i><a href="/wiki/Schoepfia" title="Schoepfia">Schoepfia</a></i> is according to molecular studies more closely related to the families <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Misodendraceae" title="Misodendraceae">Misodendraceae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Loranthaceae" title="Loranthaceae">Loranthaceae</a>, and to uphold the criteria of monophyly it must be excluded from <a href="/wiki/Olacaceae" title="Olacaceae">Olacaceae</a>. Further investigations have also shown that the South American genera <i><a href="/wiki/Arjona_(plant)" title="Arjona (plant)">Arjona</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Quinchamalium" title="Quinchamalium">Quinchamalium</a></i> (previously in the family <a href="/wiki/Santalaceae" title="Santalaceae">Santalaceae</a>) are part of this family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Thus the family contains three genera comprising a total of 58 known species.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Christenhusz-Byng2016_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Christenhusz-Byng2016-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Genera:</p>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Arjona_(plant)" title="Arjona (plant)">Arjona</a></i> <small>Cavanilles</small> (8 species) - temperate South America</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Quinchamalium" title="Quinchamalium">Quinchamalium</a></i> <small>Molina</small> (25 species) - temperate South America</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Schoepfia" title="Schoepfia">Schoepfia</a></i> <small>Schreber</small> (25 species) - temperate and tropical East Asia, tropical America</li>
</ul>
