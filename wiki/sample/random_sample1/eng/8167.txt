<p><b>Scott Stewart Bakula</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'b' in 'buy'">b</span><span title="/æ/ short 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/j/ 'y' in 'yes'">j</span><span title="/ʊ/ short 'oo' in 'foot'">ʊ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born October 9, 1954)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmref_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-filmref-1">[1]</a></sup> is an American <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a> best known for his lead roles in two science-fiction television series: as <a href="/wiki/Sam_Beckett" title="Sam Beckett">Sam Beckett</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Quantum_Leap" title="Quantum Leap">Quantum Leap</a></i> (for which he received four <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> nominations and a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Actor_%E2%80%93_Television_Series_Drama" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Television Series Drama">Golden Globe Award</a>), and as Captain <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Archer" title="Jonathan Archer">Jonathan Archer</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Enterprise" title="Star Trek: Enterprise">Star Trek: Enterprise</a></i>.</p>
<p>Bakula starred on the comedy-drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Men_of_a_Certain_Age" title="Men of a Certain Age">Men of a Certain Age</a></i>, and guest-starred in seasons two and three of <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Chuck_(TV_series)" title="Chuck (TV series)">Chuck</a></i> as the title character's father <a href="/wiki/Stephen_J._Bartowski" title="Stephen J. Bartowski">Stephen J. Bartowski</a>. He guest starred on <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Looking" title="Looking">Looking</a></i> as entrepreneur Lynn. In 2014 he began playing Special Agent Dwayne Cassius "King" Pride on <i><a href="/wiki/NCIS:_New_Orleans" title="NCIS: New Orleans">NCIS: New Orleans</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_Bakula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bakula was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Louis,_Missouri" title="St. Louis, Missouri">St. Louis, Missouri</a>, the son of Sally (née Zumwinkel) and J. Stewart Bakula, a lawyer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmref_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-filmref-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nl.newsbank.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-nl.newsbank.com-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He has a younger brother and a younger sister.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unexpurgated-p2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-unexpurgated-p2-4">[4]</a></sup> His surname comes from partial <a href="/wiki/Czechs" title="Czechs">Czech</a> ancestry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nl.newsbank.com_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-nl.newsbank.com-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_College_(Missouri)" title="Jefferson College (Missouri)">Jefferson College</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-www.tvguide.com_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-www.tvguide.com-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http:.2F.2Fwww.websterkirkwoodtimes.com_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-http:.2F.2Fwww.websterkirkwoodtimes.com-8">[8]</a></sup> followed by the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Kansas" title="University of Kansas">University of Kansas</a> for a time, but left, saying:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>"...because I was offered a tour of <i><a href="/wiki/Godspell" title="Godspell">Godspell</a></i>, a national tour that was from St. Louis. I thought that sounded great, and I went to my parents and I said 'I want to do this tour', and they said, 'Go ahead, maybe it will get it out of your system. And you come back to school in a year or two, you come back.' The tour was gonna start in August and the tour never started and school did, and then the tour fell apart, and there I was sitting at home. So I was left holding the bag, basically, and then had to decide where to go from there. And I applied, was applying to other schools. I was gonna go to a Mormon theatrical kind of school, and the more I looked at it the more I spent time examining the school side of it. I just realized what I really needed to do was just pick up, pack up and go to New York."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unexpurgated-p2_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-unexpurgated-p2-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_Bakula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bakula moved to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> in 1976, where he made his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broadway_(theater)" title="Broadway (theater)">Broadway</a> debut playing baseball legend <a href="/wiki/Joe_DiMaggio" title="Joe DiMaggio">Joe DiMaggio</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Marilyn:_An_American_Fable" title="Marilyn: An American Fable">Marilyn: An American Fable</a></i>, and appeared in the well-received <a href="/wiki/Off-Broadway" title="Off-Broadway">Off-Broadway</a> production <i>Three Guys Naked from the Waist Down</i>; he would later appear in its <a href="/wiki/Pasadena_Playhouse" title="Pasadena Playhouse">Pasadena Playhouse</a> production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-universalbio_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-universalbio-9">[9]</a></sup> The success of <i>Three Guys</i> Off-Broadway brought him attention, and when his next show, the musical <i>Nightclub Confidential</i>, which co-starred his wife Krista Neumann, moved to Los Angeles, he moved there at the urging of his California agent, Maggie Henderson, and his New York agent, Jerry Hogan. As Bakula recalled in 2000,</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>I call [Henderson] up and said I got a show, I'm gonna be out there, I'm coming out in January. So it'll work out because it's time for <a href="/wiki/Television_pilot" title="Television pilot">pilot</a> season and I'll be doing something so people can come and see me. ... And then I coincidentally had done a Disney Sunday Night <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> movie that was gonna come out some time in the winter. It was the time to go. Came out here on <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Day" title="New Year's Day">New Year's Day</a>, 1986. The show I did turned out to be a big hit out here. It got me a lot of attention out here and I jumped onto <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">the TV sitcom</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Designing_Women" title="Designing Women">Designing Women</a></i> in the beginning and was able to do that pilot ... and things kind of took off.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unexpurgated-p3_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-unexpurgated-p3-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>He was cast in two short-lived series: <i><a href="/wiki/Gung_Ho_(TV_series)" title="Gung Ho (TV series)">Gung Ho</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eisenhower_%26_Lutz" title="Eisenhower &amp; Lutz">Eisenhower &amp; Lutz</a></i>. During a Hollywood writers' strike, he returned to New York to star in <i><a href="/wiki/Romance/Romance" title="Romance/Romance">Romance/Romance</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unexpurgated-p3_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-unexpurgated-p3-10">[10]</a></sup> and then afterward landed the lead role opposite co-star <a href="/wiki/Dean_Stockwell" title="Dean Stockwell">Dean Stockwell</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a> television series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quantum_Leap_(TV_series)" title="Quantum Leap (TV series)">Quantum Leap</a></i>. Bakula played time traveler Dr. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samuel_Beckett_(Quantum_Leap)" title="Samuel Beckett (Quantum Leap)">Sam Beckett</a>, who was trapped by a malfunction of his time machine to correct things gone wrong in the past.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> His performance in the show earned him a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe Award</a> (along with three nominations) and four <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> nominations for Best Actor, as well as five consecutive <a href="/wiki/Viewers_for_Quality_Television" title="Viewers for Quality Television">Viewers for Quality Television</a> Awards for Best Actor in a Quality Drama Series.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1995, Bakula appeared on the cover of <i><a href="/wiki/Playgirl" title="Playgirl">Playgirl</a></i>. He voiced Danny Cat in the animated film <i><a href="/wiki/Cats_Don%27t_Dance" title="Cats Don't Dance">Cats Don't Dance</a></i> (1997), singing in one number with <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Cole" title="Natalie Cole">Natalie Cole</a>. He played the aging veteran pitcher Gus Cantrell in <i><a href="/wiki/Major_League:_Back_to_the_Minors" title="Major League: Back to the Minors">Major League: Back to the Minors</a></i> (1998), the final movie in the <i><a href="/wiki/Major_League_(film)" title="Major League (film)">Major League</a></i> trilogy. He also played Jim Olmeyer, the same-sex partner of <a href="/wiki/Sam_Robards" title="Sam Robards">Sam Robards</a>' Jim Berkley, in the film <i><a href="/wiki/American_Beauty_(1999_film)" title="American Beauty (1999 film)">American Beauty</a></i> (1999).</p>
<p>As <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Archer" title="Jonathan Archer">Jonathan Archer</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Enterprise" title="Star Trek: Enterprise">Star Trek: Enterprise</a></i>, Bakula played the captain of Earth's first long-range interstellar ship. In 2006, he reprised the role of Archer for the <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Legacy" title="Star Trek: Legacy">Star Trek: Legacy</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">PC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a> video games as a <a href="/wiki/Voice-over" title="Voice-over">voice-over</a>.</p>
<p>Bakula starred in the musical <i><a href="/wiki/Shenandoah_(musical)" title="Shenandoah (musical)">Shenandoah</a></i>, a play which also provided his first professional theatrical role in 1976, at <a href="/wiki/Ford%27s_Theatre" title="Ford's Theatre">Ford's Theatre</a>, in 2006. Bakula is heard singing "Pig Island" on <a href="/wiki/Sandra_Boynton" title="Sandra Boynton">Sandra Boynton</a>'s children's CD <i>Philadelphia Chickens</i>, which is labeled as being "For all ages except 43." Scott Bakula said that he might be starring as Sam in a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quantum_Leap_(TV_series)" title="Quantum Leap (TV series)">Quantum Leap</a></i> film as stated in <i>TV Guide Magazine</i> along with <a href="/wiki/Dean_Stockwell" title="Dean Stockwell">Dean Stockwell</a>. At Comic Con 2010, he announced that a script was being worked on and that while he would be in the movie, he would not have the main role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newleap_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-newleap-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bakula performed various songs from his career for a one-night-only performance entitled <i>An Evening with Scott Bakula</i> at Sidney Harman Hall on January 18, 2008, as a benefit for the restoration of the historic Ford's Theater.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Bakula had three appearances in 2008. He appeared as Atty. Jack Ross in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Legal" title="Boston Legal">Boston Legal</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Boston_Legal_episodes#Glow_In_the_Dark" title="List of Boston Legal episodes">"Glow in the Dark"</a>, which aired on February 12, 2008 on the ABC network.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> From March 4 - April 20, he starred as Tony Hunter in the world premiere of <i>Dancing in the Dark</i> at The Old Globe in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego,_California" title="San Diego, California">San Diego, California</a>. <i>Dancing in the Dark</i> is based on the movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Band_Wagon" title="The Band Wagon">The Band Wagon</a></i> (1953), which starred <a href="/wiki/Fred_Astaire" title="Fred Astaire">Fred Astaire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cyd_Charisse" title="Cyd Charisse">Cyd Charisse</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Bakula appeared as the character Chris Fulbright in the five-episode run of the <a href="/wiki/Tracey_Ullman" title="Tracey Ullman">Tracey Ullman</a> sketch comedy series <i>State of the Union</i> on Showtime.</p>
<p>Bakula appeared in the dark comedy film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Informant_(2009_film)" title="The Informant (2009 film)">The Informant!</a></i> (2009) as Brian Shepard, an FBI agent working with the title character <a href="/wiki/Mark_Whitacre" title="Mark Whitacre">Mark Whitacre</a>, (played by <a href="/wiki/Matt_Damon" title="Matt Damon">Matt Damon</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In April 2009, he began a recurring role on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Chuck_(TV_series)" title="Chuck (TV series)">Chuck</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/Stephen_J._Bartowski" title="Stephen J. Bartowski">Stephen J. Bartowski</a>, the eponymous character's long-lost father. From July 31 to August 2, 2009, he starred as Nathan Detroit in three performances of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guys_and_Dolls_(musical)" title="Guys and Dolls (musical)">Guys and Dolls</a></i> at <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Bowl" title="Hollywood Bowl">Hollywood Bowl</a>. Beginning in December 2009, Bakula began appearing as Terry, one of the three lead characters, along with <a href="/wiki/Ray_Romano" title="Ray Romano">Ray Romano</a> (Joe) and <a href="/wiki/Andre_Braugher" title="Andre Braugher">Andre Braugher</a> (Owen), in TNT's hour-long comedy/drama <i><a href="/wiki/Men_of_a_Certain_Age" title="Men of a Certain Age">Men of a Certain Age</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2011, Bakula performed a voice cameo in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Source_Code" title="Source Code">Source Code</a></i> as a slight nod to his character on <i>Quantum Leap</i>, with his catchphrase of "Oh, boy."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and appeared in the feature-length documentary <i><a href="/wiki/The_Captains_(film)" title="The Captains (film)">The Captains</a></i>, which was written and directed by <a href="/wiki/William_Shatner" title="William Shatner">William Shatner</a>, Bakula is interviewed by the original <i>Star Trek</i> captain about his life and career leading up to his performances as Captain Jonathan Archer in <i>Star Trek: Enterprise</i>. In the movie, Shatner interviews Bakula at his ranch in California where the pair ride horses and discuss the pitfalls that come with a career in television.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2011, Bakula starred in <i>Terrible Advice</i> by <a href="/wiki/Saul_Rubinek" title="Saul Rubinek">Saul Rubinek</a> at the Menier Chocolate Factory.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In April to May 2012, he guest starred in the last five episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Desperate_Housewives" title="Desperate Housewives">Desperate Housewives</a></i> as Bree Van de Kamp's criminal defense lawyer and third husband. In April 2013, he made a guest appearance on <i><a href="/wiki/Two_and_a_Half_Men" title="Two and a Half Men">Two and a Half Men</a></i> as a car dealer.In August 2013, it was announced Bakula would have a recurring role in the first season of HBO's new series, <i><a href="/wiki/Looking" title="Looking">Looking</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He also appeared in the film <i>Geography Club</i> (2013).</p>
<p>In February 2014, Bakula was cast as the lead in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Backdoor_pilots" title="Backdoor pilots">backdoor pilot</a> for the current <i><a href="/wiki/NCIS_(TV_series)" title="NCIS (TV series)">NCIS</a></i> spin-off series, <i><a href="/wiki/NCIS:_New_Orleans" title="NCIS: New Orleans">NCIS: New Orleans</a></i>, which began as a two-part episode of its parent series in the spring season of 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The third season of <i>NCIS: New Orleans</i> premiered on September 20, 2016.</p>
<p>He made a cameo appearance on the season 12 opener of It's Always Sunny in Philadelphia as himself.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_Bakula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bakula married Krista Neumann in 1981 and had two children, Chelsy and Cody. They divorced in 1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmref_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-filmref-1">[1]</a></sup> He married actress <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_Field" title="Chelsea Field">Chelsea Field</a> in 1996 and they have two children, Wil Botfield and Owen Barrett.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmref_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-filmref-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bakula says he was "...hardly ever home for four and a half seasons" of <i>Quantum Leap</i>, so he chose to prioritize his family life on later projects. His <i>Star Trek: Enterprise</i> contract required that filming be completed by 6 PM every Wednesday so he could have dinner with his family. During filming of <i><a href="/wiki/NCIS:_New_Orleans" title="NCIS: New Orleans">NCIS: New Orleans</a></i>, he returns home to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> every <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weekend" title="Weekend">weekend</a> to spend time with his wife.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
