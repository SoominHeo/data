<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_Shepherd_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shepherd began his acting career in theater. He has appeared in several productions of <a href="/wiki/The_Wooster_Group" title="The Wooster Group">The Wooster Group</a> and is also known for his work as narrator in the play <i>Gatz</i> by <a href="/wiki/Elevator_Repair_Service" title="Elevator Repair Service">Elevator Repair Service</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, Shepherd appeared in the comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/And_So_It_Goes_(film)" title="And So It Goes (film)">And So It Goes</a></i> as Luke, along with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Douglas" title="Michael Douglas">Michael Douglas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diane_Keaton" title="Diane Keaton">Diane Keaton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sterling_Jerins" title="Sterling Jerins">Sterling Jerins</a>. <a href="/wiki/Rob_Reiner" title="Rob Reiner">Rob Reiner</a> directed the film, which released on July 25, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, Shepherd played a supporting role of a CIA operative named Hoffman in the Cold War-era spy film <i><a href="/wiki/Bridge_of_Spies_(film)" title="Bridge of Spies (film)">Bridge of Spies</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hanks" title="Tom Hanks">Tom Hanks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Rylance" title="Mark Rylance">Mark Rylance</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The film was directed by <a href="/wiki/Steven_Spielberg" title="Steven Spielberg">Steven Spielberg</a>, which released on October 16, 2015 by <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Studios_Motion_Pictures" title="Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures">Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shepherd has also filmed a role of Cardinal Dussolier in the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a>'s series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_Pope" title="The Young Pope">The Young Pope</a></i> along with <a href="/wiki/Jude_Law" title="Jude Law">Jude Law</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He will next be seen in the thriller film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oppenheimer_Strategies" title="Oppenheimer Strategies">Oppenheimer Strategies</a></i> along with <a href="/wiki/Richard_Gere" title="Richard Gere">Richard Gere</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oct2015V_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Oct2015V-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shepherd had a supporting role as a deputy director of the CIA, opposite <a href="/wiki/Matt_Damon" title="Matt Damon">Matt Damon</a>, in the thriller film <i><a href="/wiki/Jason_Bourne_(film)" title="Jason Bourne (film)">Jason Bourne</a></i>, which was released on July 29, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oct2015V_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Oct2015V-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
