<p><b>Scott Wilson</b> (born March 29, 1942) is an American film and television actor. He has more than 50 film credits, including <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Heat_of_the_Night_(film)" title="In the Heat of the Night (film)">In the Heat of the Night</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/In_Cold_Blood_(film)" title="In Cold Blood (film)">In Cold Blood</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Great_Gatsby_(1974_film)" title="The Great Gatsby (1974 film)">The Great Gatsby</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Man_Walking_(film)" title="Dead Man Walking (film)">Dead Man Walking</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Pearl_Harbor_(film)" title="Pearl Harbor (film)">Pearl Harbor</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Junebug_(film)" title="Junebug (film)">Junebug</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nge_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nge-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1980, Wilson received a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor_%E2%80%93_Motion_Picture" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actor – Motion Picture">Golden Globe nomination for Best Supporting Actor – Motion Picture</a> for his role in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ninth_Configuration" title="The Ninth Configuration">The Ninth Configuration</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ajc_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ajc-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-3">[3]</a></sup> He played veterinarian <a href="/wiki/Hershel_Greene" title="Hershel Greene">Hershel Greene</a> on the <a href="/wiki/AMC_(TV_channel)" title="AMC (TV channel)">AMC</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Walking_Dead_(TV_series)" title="The Walking Dead (TV series)">The Walking Dead</a></i> from 2011 to 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digitalspy_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-digitalspy-4">[4]</a></sup> He had a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recurring_role" title="Recurring role">recurring role</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI: Crime Scene Investigation</a></i> as casino mogul Sam Braun.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_Wilson_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wilson was born in <a href="/wiki/Thomasville,_Georgia" title="Thomasville, Georgia">Thomasville, Georgia</a> (a small town). He made his screen debut portraying characters suspected of murder in his first three films. In his debut film, Wilson played a murder suspect in <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Heat_of_the_Night_(film)" title="In the Heat of the Night (film)">In the Heat of the Night</a></i> (1967).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ajc_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ajc-2">[2]</a></sup> His follow-up role, in the same year, was <i><a href="/wiki/In_Cold_Blood_(film)" title="In Cold Blood (film)">In Cold Blood</a></i>, based on the <a href="/wiki/In_Cold_Blood" title="In Cold Blood">book of the same name</a> by <a href="/wiki/Truman_Capote" title="Truman Capote">Truman Capote</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-3">[3]</a></sup> Wilson portrayed real-life murderer <a href="/wiki/Richard_Hickock" title="Richard Hickock">Richard Hickock</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Robert_Blake_(actor)" title="Robert Blake (actor)">Robert Blake</a> played his partner, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perry_Smith_(murderer)" title="Perry Smith (murderer)">Perry Smith</a>.</p>
<p>Director <a href="/wiki/Richard_Brooks" title="Richard Brooks">Richard Brooks</a> cast Wilson and Blake in the starring roles specifically because they were unknown at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-3">[3]</a></sup> The director passed over better-known actors, including <a href="/wiki/Steve_McQueen" title="Steve McQueen">Steve McQueen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Newman" title="Paul Newman">Paul Newman</a>, for the parts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-3">[3]</a></sup> Wilson later explained Brooks' casting motivations: "Brooks hired two 'unknowns' and he wanted to keep it that way. We were treated like two killers he had somehow run across."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-3">[3]</a></sup> Wilson appeared in Sparta on March 15, 2014 to celebrate the city's 175th anniversary in reference to his debut appearance in the film.</p>
<p>The film earned Wilson an appearance on the cover of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Life_Magazine" title="Life Magazine">Life Magazine</a></i>, published on May 12, 1967.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-3">[3]</a></sup> Wilson was just 25 years old at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-3">[3]</a></sup> The cover features <a href="/wiki/Truman_Capote" title="Truman Capote">Truman Capote</a> standing between Wilson and Blake on an empty <a href="/wiki/Highway" title="Highway">highway</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">Kansas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-3">[3]</a></sup> The <i>caption</i>, <i>Nightmare Revisited</i>, appears with them on the cover.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_3-8"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-3">[3]</a></sup> Wilson appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Great_Gatsby_(1974_film)" title="The Great Gatsby (1974 film)">The Great Gatsby</a></i> in 1974 opposite <a href="/wiki/Robert_Redford" title="Robert Redford">Robert Redford</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ajc_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-ajc-2">[2]</a></sup> He received a 1980 <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe</a> nomination for Best Supporting Actor for his performance in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ninth_Configuration" title="The Ninth Configuration">The Ninth Configuration</a></i> by director and friend <a href="/wiki/William_Peter_Blatty" title="William Peter Blatty">William Peter Blatty</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ajc_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-ajc-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_3-9"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-3">[3]</a></sup> He lost the Golden Globe to <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Hutton" title="Timothy Hutton">Timothy Hutton</a>. In 1995 Wilson got attention for his role as a prison chaplain in <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Man_Walking_(film)" title="Dead Man Walking (film)">Dead Man Walking</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Susan_Sarandon" title="Susan Sarandon">Susan Sarandon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sean_Penn" title="Sean Penn">Sean Penn</a>, based on <a href="/wiki/Dead_Man_Walking_(book)" title="Dead Man Walking (book)">the book of the same name</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sister_Helen_Prejean" title="Sister Helen Prejean">Sister Helen Prejean</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ajc_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-ajc-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_3-10"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wilson's long <a href="/wiki/Filmography" title="Filmography">filmography</a> also includes <i><a href="/wiki/The_Gypsy_Moths" title="The Gypsy Moths">The Gypsy Moths</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Right_Stuff_(film)" title="The Right Stuff (film)">The Right Stuff</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Year_of_the_Quiet_Sun" title="A Year of the Quiet Sun">A Year of the Quiet Sun</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Malone_(film)" title="Malone (film)">Malone</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Grass_Harp_(film)" title="The Grass Harp (film)">The Grass Harp</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Junebug_(film)" title="Junebug (film)">Junebug</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Host_(2006_film)" title="The Host (2006 film)">The Host</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Monster_(2003_film)" title="Monster (2003 film)">Monster</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Young_Guns_II" title="Young Guns II">Young Guns II</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Pearl_Harbor_(film)" title="Pearl Harbor (film)">Pearl Harbor</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Stan" title="Big Stan">Big Stan</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Judge_Dredd_(film)" title="Judge Dredd (film)">Judge Dredd</a></i>, the <i><a href="/wiki/Shiloh_(film)" title="Shiloh (film)">Shiloh</a></i> film series and <i><a href="/wiki/Behind_the_Mask:_The_Rise_of_Leslie_Vernon" title="Behind the Mask: The Rise of Leslie Vernon">Behind the Mask: The Rise of Leslie Vernon</a></i>. Wilson has filmed on location in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ajc_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-ajc-2">[2]</a></sup> Wilson had a recurring role in several episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI: Crime Scene Investigation</a></i> as Sam Braun, father of crime-scene investigator <a href="/wiki/Catherine_Willows" title="Catherine Willows">Catherine Willows</a> (portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Marg_Helgenberger" title="Marg Helgenberger">Marg Helgenberger</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvguide_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvguide-5">[5]</a></sup> Braun was killed off in the episode "Built to Kill, Part 2". In the fall of 2011 he also made an appearance opposite <a href="/wiki/Laura_Dern" title="Laura Dern">Laura Dern</a> in the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> series, <i><a href="/wiki/Enlightened_(TV_series)" title="Enlightened (TV series)">Enlightened</a></i>.</p>
<p>Wilson was cast as veterinarian, <a href="/wiki/Hershel_Greene" title="Hershel Greene">Hershel Greene</a> in the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Walking_Dead_(TV_series)" title="The Walking Dead (TV series)">The Walking Dead</a></i> in June 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digitalspy_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-digitalspy-4">[4]</a></sup> The role has earned him positive reviews, including a "cheer" from <i><a href="/wiki/TV_Guide" title="TV Guide">TV Guide</a></i>, which wrote that he had contributed "subtle shades of <a href="/wiki/Human_nature" title="Human nature">humanity</a> to the character of Hershel Greene."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvguide_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-tvguide-5">[5]</a></sup> Wilson was offered the opportunity to join the show while visiting his 97-year-old mother in Georgia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ajc_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-ajc-2">[2]</a></sup> He has described his mother as "a fan of the show."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ajc_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-ajc-2">[2]</a></sup> Wilson left the show in December 2013 after his character was killed off in the Season 4 mid-season finale <a href="/wiki/Too_Far_Gone_(The_Walking_Dead)" title="Too Far Gone (The Walking Dead)">Too Far Gone</a>. However, he has made two appearances since his character's death.</p>
<p>Wilson reflected on his career in a 2011 <a href="/wiki/Interview" title="Interview">interview</a> with <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Access_Atlanta" title="Access Atlanta">Access Atlanta's</a></i> Rodney Ho: "It's been up and down. It's always been. You have dry spells. At different times, you are starting over. If you love it, you stay with it. That's what I'm doing. I've accomplished more than I would have hoped to have accomplished. I don't want to be a big movie star. I can be someone who walks the streets and not get mobbed. I want to be as fine an actor as I can be. I am still striving to be as good as I can be."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ajc_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-ajc-2">[2]</a></sup> Wilson was filming scenes for <i>The Walking Dead</i> in <a href="/wiki/Senoia,_Georgia" title="Senoia, Georgia">Senoia, Georgia</a>, at the time the interview took place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ajc_2-9"><a href="#cite_note-ajc-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2014, Wilson was cast in a recurring role as Dr. Guyot in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazon_Instant_Video" title="Amazon Instant Video">Amazon</a> original series <i><a href="/wiki/Bosch_(TV_series)" title="Bosch (TV series)">Bosch</a></i>. In 2016, he appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/The_OA" title="The OA">The OA</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
