<p><b>Matthew Scott Porter</b> (born July 14, 1979),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ScottPorter_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ScottPorter-1">[1]</a></sup> better known as <b>Scott Porter</b>, is an American actor and occasional singer known for his role as <a href="/wiki/Jason_Street" title="Jason Street">Jason Street</a> in the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> television drama <i><a href="/wiki/Friday_Night_Lights_(TV_series)" title="Friday Night Lights (TV series)">Friday Night Lights</a></i>. His character was injured during a football game in the pilot episode and became disabled. The character was inspired by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/David_Edwards_(football_player_and_motivational_speaker)" title="David Edwards (football player and motivational speaker)">David Edwards</a>, a high school football player.</p>
<p>Porter was paired with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alyson_Michalka" title="Alyson Michalka">Alyson Michalka</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Hudgens" title="Vanessa Hudgens">Vanessa Hudgens</a> in the 2009 film <i><a href="/wiki/Bandslam" title="Bandslam">Bandslam</a>.</i> He performed the song "Pretend", which is featured in the <i>Bandslam</i> soundtrack. In 2010, he played the role of Blake, a law firm investigator, in <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a>' legal drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Good_Wife" title="The Good Wife">The Good Wife</a></i>.</p>
<p>He starred as George Tucker in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_CW_Television_Network" title="The CW Television Network">The CW</a> comedy-drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Hart_of_Dixie" title="Hart of Dixie">Hart of Dixie</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_Porter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Porter was born in <a href="/wiki/Omaha,_Nebraska" title="Omaha, Nebraska">Omaha, Nebraska</a> and attended <a href="/wiki/Lake_Howell_High_School" title="Lake Howell High School">Lake Howell High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Winter_Park,_Florida" title="Winter Park, Florida">Winter Park, Florida</a>. At Lake Howell, Porter was a <a href="/wiki/Wide_receiver" title="Wide receiver">wide receiver</a> on the <a href="/wiki/High_school_football" title="High school football">football</a> team and graduated in 1997.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sentinel_2006-10-01_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sentinel_2006-10-01-2">[2]</a></sup> While a student at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Central_Florida" title="University of Central Florida">University of Central Florida</a>, Porter sang <a href="/wiki/Vocal_percussion" title="Vocal percussion">vocal percussion</a> in an <a href="/wiki/A_cappella" title="A cappella">a cappella</a> vocal group, 4:2:Five.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Porter also worked as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baby-sitter" title="Baby-sitter">baby-sitter</a> and "didn't have time to party."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-starcam_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-starcam-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_Porter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Scott_Porter_at_the_premiere_of_Push_(cropped).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="554" data-file-width="390" height="241" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Scott_Porter_at_the_premiere_of_Push_%28cropped%29.jpg/170px-Scott_Porter_at_the_premiere_of_Push_%28cropped%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Scott_Porter_at_the_premiere_of_Push_%28cropped%29.jpg/255px-Scott_Porter_at_the_premiere_of_Push_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Scott_Porter_at_the_premiere_of_Push_%28cropped%29.jpg/340px-Scott_Porter_at_the_premiere_of_Push_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>

<p>Prior to joining the cast of <i>Friday Night Lights</i>, Porter played Matthew in the original cast of the <a href="/wiki/Off-Broadway" title="Off-Broadway">Off-Broadway</a> hit <i><a href="/wiki/Altar_Boyz" title="Altar Boyz">Altar Boyz</a></i> in 2004. In 2006, he temporarily took over the role of <a href="/wiki/Casey_Hughes" title="Casey Hughes">Casey Hughes</a> in the soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/As_the_World_Turns" title="As the World Turns">As the World Turns</a></i>, a role originally performed by <a href="/wiki/Zach_Roerig" title="Zach Roerig">Zach Roerig</a>. Porter also played the role of Colin Thompson, the more visible part of the duo that fronts the fictional band PoP!, in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Music_and_Lyrics" title="Music and Lyrics">Music and Lyrics</a></i> (2007). Porter also played the character Rex in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Speed_Racer_(film)" title="Speed Racer (film)">Speed Racer</a></i> (2008), which was directed and written by the <a href="/wiki/The_Wachowskis" title="The Wachowskis">The Wachowskis</a>. He played Bobby in the <a href="/wiki/Prom_Night_(2008_film)" title="Prom Night (2008 film)">remake</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Prom_Night_(1980_film)" title="Prom Night (1980 film)">Prom Night</a></i> and Ben Wheatly in <i><a href="/wiki/Bandslam" title="Bandslam">Bandslam</a></i>, released in August 2009. In the latter film, he co-starred alongside <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alyson_Michalka" title="Alyson Michalka">Aly Michalka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gaelan_Connell" title="Gaelan Connell">Gaelan Connell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Hudgens" title="Vanessa Hudgens">Vanessa Hudgens</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Kudrow" title="Lisa Kudrow">Lisa Kudrow</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In the same year, he was also paired with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sisterhood_of_the_Traveling_Pants_(film)" title="The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants (film)">The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants</a></i> star <a href="/wiki/Alexis_Bledel" title="Alexis Bledel">Alexis Bledel</a> in the romantic comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Good_Guy_(film)" title="The Good Guy (film)">The Good Guy</a></i>, which premiered at the <a href="/wiki/Tribeca_Film_Festival" title="Tribeca Film Festival">Tribeca Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Porter is a fan of comic books and has auditioned for several comic book hero roles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He is also a fan of <a href="/wiki/HeroClix" title="HeroClix">HeroClix</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> filming unboxing videos of upcoming sets for the WizKids Facebook page. Porter performed and sang the song "Someone to Fall Back On" in <i>Bandslam</i>, and his song "Pretend" was included in the <a href="/wiki/Bandslam_(soundtrack)" title="Bandslam (soundtrack)">soundtrack</a>. He was part of his high school's choral department and was a founding member of the groups 4:2:Five and Mosaic. He eventually performed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Off_Broadway" title="Off Broadway">Off Broadway</a> with another A Cappella band named Toxic Audio.</p>
<p>Porter portrayed Randy in the 2010 film <i><a href="/wiki/Dear_John_(2010_film)" title="Dear John (2010 film)">Dear John</a></i>, which was adapted from <a href="/wiki/Dear_John_(novel)" title="Dear John (novel)">the novel of the same name</a> by <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Sparks" title="Nicholas Sparks">Nicholas Sparks</a>.</p>
<p>In 2010, Porter joined the cast of <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a>'s legal drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Good_Wife" title="The Good Wife">The Good Wife</a></i> as Blake, an investigator for a law firm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Also in 2010, Porter starred in the pilot of the television series <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nomads_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nomads (TV series) (page does not exist)">Nomads</a></i>, which was developed for <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">The CW</a>, but it was not picked up by the network.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/BuddyTV" title="BuddyTV">BuddyTV</a> ranked him #10 on its "TV's 100 Sexiest Men of 2010" list<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and #34 in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Porter played a lawyer in the medical dramedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Hart_of_Dixie" title="Hart of Dixie">Hart of Dixie</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Bilson" title="Rachel Bilson">Rachel Bilson</a>. The series debuted on The CW in September 2011.</p>
<p>In 2013, he debuted in the second season of the hugely popular video game series of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Walking_Dead:_Season_Two" title="The Walking Dead: Season Two">The Walking Dead</a></i> by <i><a href="/wiki/Telltale_Games" title="Telltale Games">Telltale Games</a></i> as Luke, he served as one of the main characters in the series Scott also voiced Lukas in "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minecraft_Story_Mode" title="Minecraft Story Mode">Minecraft Story Mode</a>" by "<a href="/wiki/Telltale_Games" title="Telltale Games">Telltale Games</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Mojang" title="Mojang">Mojang</a>" Lukas is a main character who is part of the new Order of The Stone and his behavior can change based off the players choices.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_Porter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In April 2013, Porter married casting director Kelsey Mayfield, a former <a href="/wiki/University_of_Texas_at_Austin" title="University of Texas at Austin">University of Texas</a> cheerleader whom he had met on the set of <i><a href="/wiki/Friday_Night_Lights_(TV_series)" title="Friday Night Lights (TV series)">Friday Night Lights</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> One of his groomsmen was screenwriter and director <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Linden_(writer)" title="Jamie Linden (writer)">Jamie Linden</a>, who was his schoolmate at Lake Howell. On May 23, 2015, Mayfield gave birth to their son McCoy Lee Porter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
