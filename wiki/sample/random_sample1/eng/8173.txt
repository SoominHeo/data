<p><i><b>School Daze</b></i> is a 1988 American <a href="/wiki/Musical_film" title="Musical film">musical</a> <a href="/wiki/Comedy" title="Comedy">comedy</a> <a href="/wiki/Drama" title="Drama">drama</a> film, written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Spike_Lee" title="Spike Lee">Spike Lee</a>, and starring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Larry_Fishburne" title="Larry Fishburne">Larry Fishburne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Esposito" title="Giancarlo Esposito">Giancarlo Esposito</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tisha_Campbell-Martin" title="Tisha Campbell-Martin">Tisha Campbell-Martin</a>. Based in part on Spike Lee's experiences at <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Morehouse_College" title="Morehouse College">Morehouse College</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spelman_College" title="Spelman College">Spelman College</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morris_Brown_College" title="Morris Brown College">Morris Brown College</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clark_Atlanta_University" title="Clark Atlanta University">Clark Atlanta University</a>, it is a story about <a href="/wiki/Fraternities_and_sororities" title="Fraternities and sororities">fraternity and sorority</a> members clashing with other students at a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Historically_black_college" title="Historically black college">historically black college</a> during homecoming weekend. It also touches upon issues of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colorism" title="Colorism">colorism</a> and hair texture bias within the African-American community. The second feature film by Spike Lee, <i>School Daze</i> was released on February 12, 1988 by <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Pictures" title="Columbia Pictures">Columbia Pictures</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Daze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vaughn "Dap" Dunlap (<a href="/wiki/Laurence_Fishburne" title="Laurence Fishburne">Fishburne</a>) is a politically conscious black American student at Mission College, a leading <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Historically_black_college" title="Historically black college">historically black college</a> whose motto is "Uplift the Race."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup> The college administration is portrayed as inept.</p>
<p>Dunlap leads <a href="/wiki/Anti-Apartheid_Movement" title="Anti-Apartheid Movement">anti-apartheid</a> demonstrations encouraging students and school administrators to <a href="/wiki/Disinvestment_from_South_Africa" title="Disinvestment from South Africa">divest from South Africa</a>. When his buddies go into town, they find the local boys are not impressed with their activities, but think of them as privileged college boys. Open conflict breaks out between the groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dunlap feuds with Julian Eaves (<a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Esposito" title="Giancarlo Esposito">Esposito</a>) aka Dean Big Brother Almighty of Gamma Phi Gamma Fraternity, Incorporated. This group is characterized as "wannabees," as in "wannabe better than me." The fraternity brothers are preparing for a big college football weekend and Homecoming parties. Meanwhile, Dap's younger <a href="/wiki/Cousin" title="Cousin">cousin</a>, Darrell (<a href="/wiki/Spike_Lee" title="Spike Lee">Lee</a>), aka "Half-Pint," is a Gamma pledge.</p>
<p>The Gamma women's auxiliary, the Gamma Rays, who are sleek and light-skinned, confront non-Greek black co-eds, particularly over skin color and the nature of their hair. Some of the Rays use contact lens to change eye color.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vanessa_L._Williams" title="Vanessa L. Williams">Vanessa L. Williams</a> was originally considered for the role of Jane Toussaint. However, due to her recent dethroning from her <a href="/wiki/Miss_America" title="Miss America">Miss America</a> title because of controversy over nude photos published in <i><a href="/wiki/Penthouse_(magazine)" title="Penthouse (magazine)">Penthouse</a></i>, Williams turned down the role. She cited a sex scene between Jane and Julian (Dean Big Brother Almighty) and said she wanted to distance herself from that image.</p>
<p>Spike Lee was so impressed by Tisha Campbell's singing performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Shop_of_Horrors_(1986_film)" title="Little Shop of Horrors (1986 film)">Little Shop of Horrors</a></i> (1986) and as a result, he cast her for the role of Jane.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Daze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film received positive reviews for its exploration of issues within the black community. <a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Sun-Times" title="Chicago Sun-Times">Chicago Sun-Times</a></i> noted, "There is no doubt in my mind that 'School Daze,' in its own way, is one of the most honest and revealing movies I've ever seen about modern middle-class black life in America."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ebert_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ebert-3">[3]</a></sup> He also noted its frank exploration of issues of discrimination within the black community related to skin tone and nature of hair. He said it was significant as a film with a "completely black orientation. All of the characters, good and bad, are black, and all of the character's references are to each other."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ebert_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ebert-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some people resented Lee's portrayal of a dysfunctional black college. Many officials of black colleges and faculty heads attacked the film for its use of racial language, epithets and portrayal of a college in trouble. They objected to "frizzy" and "nappy-headed" as among the words used by the students for each other, referring to their hair. As a result of the film, some colleges excluded Lee from their lecture programs.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Daze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Spike Lee arranged for the two groups of actors to stay in separate hotels during filming. The actors playing the "wannabees" were given better accommodations than the ones playing the "jigaboos." This favoritism contributed to tension on the set, which showed in the on-camera animosity between the two camps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-castcommentary_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-castcommentary-4">[4]</a></sup> (The producers used a similar tactic in filming <i><a href="/wiki/Animal_House" title="Animal House">Animal House</a>,</i> with similar results.) In <i>School Daze,</i> the method approach yielded strong results — the fight that occurs at the <a href="/wiki/Stepping_(African-American)" title="Stepping (African-American)">step show</a> between Dap's crew and the Gammas was not in the script. On the day the scene was shot, the fight broke out between the two sides. Lee ordered the cameras to keep rolling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-castcommentary_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-castcommentary-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Officials of <a href="/wiki/Morehouse_College" title="Morehouse College">Morehouse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spelman_College" title="Spelman College">Spelman</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Clark_Atlanta_University" title="Clark Atlanta University">Clark Atlanta University</a> asked Lee to stop filming on the campuses before he completed his work because the colleges' Boards of Directors had concerns on how he was portraying the <a href="/wiki/Historically_black_colleges_and_universities" title="Historically black colleges and universities">historically black colleges</a> in the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-castcommentary_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-castcommentary-4">[4]</a></sup> Lee had to finish filming at the neighboring <a href="/wiki/Morris_Brown_College" title="Morris Brown College">Morris Brown College</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-castcommentary_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-castcommentary-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Daze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kadeem_Hardison" title="Kadeem Hardison">Kadeem Hardison</a>, <a href="/wiki/Darryl_M._Bell" title="Darryl M. Bell">Darryl M. Bell</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jasmine_Guy" title="Jasmine Guy">Jasmine Guy</a> became principal cast members on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cosby_Show" title="The Cosby Show">The Cosby Show</a></i> spin-off, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Different_World_(TV_series)" title="A Different World (TV series)">A Different World</a></i> — a TV series about life at a historically black college.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-castcommentary_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-castcommentary-4">[4]</a></sup> Other <i>School Daze</i> cast members also appeared on <i>A Different World</i>, including <a href="/wiki/Dominic_Hoffman" title="Dominic Hoffman">Dominic Hoffman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tisha_Campbell-Martin" title="Tisha Campbell-Martin">Tisha Campbell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Art_Evans" title="Art Evans">Art Evans</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Guy_Killum&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Guy Killum (page does not exist)">Guy Killum</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roger_Guenveur_Smith" title="Roger Guenveur Smith">Roger Guenveur Smith</a>.</p>
<p>In 2009, <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Keys" title="Alicia Keys">Alicia Keys</a> paid homage to <i>School Daze</i> in the music video for her song "<a href="/wiki/Teenage_Love_Affair" title="Teenage Love Affair">Teenage Love Affair</a>".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> (She imitated scenes including the rally in front of the school building, the pajama party, and the scene where Tisha Campbell and her court perform at coronation.)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Soundtrack">Soundtrack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Daze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Soundtrack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Da_Butt" title="Da Butt">Da Butt</a>," written by Marcus Miller and Mark Stevens, and performed by the group <a href="/wiki/Experience_Unlimited" title="Experience Unlimited">E.U.</a> (who appear in the film), hit number 1 on Billboard's R&amp;B chart and number 35 on its Pop chart. The <i>School Daze</i> soundtrack also features the song, "Be One," written by <a href="/wiki/Bill_Lee_(musician)" title="Bill Lee (musician)">Bill Lee</a> and performed by <a href="/wiki/Phyllis_Hyman" title="Phyllis Hyman">Phyllis Hyman</a>, who also appears in the film.</p>
