<p><i><b>School Days</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">スクールデイズ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sukūru Deizu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Visual_novel" title="Visual novel">visual novel</a> developed by <a href="/wiki/0verflow" title="0verflow">0verflow</a>, released on April 28, 2005 for <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Windows</a> as an <a href="/wiki/Eroge" title="Eroge">adult game</a>. It was later <a href="/wiki/Porting" title="Porting">ported</a> as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DVD_game" title="DVD game">DVD game</a> and for the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a> (PS2) and <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Portable" title="PlayStation Portable">PlayStation Portable</a> (PSP). The story, a dramatic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slice-of-life" title="Slice-of-life">slice-of-life</a>, follows Makoto Ito, a high school student who becomes the ambivalent love-interest of several girls during his second term, and the effects this has on himself and his relationships with other characters. Though the game requires little interaction from users, <i>School Days</i> engages the player through a plot that they are given opportunities to change the course of during play. The game concludes with an ending specific to the outcome of the story, some of which became notorious for their <a href="/wiki/Graphic_violence" title="Graphic violence">graphic violence</a>.</p>
<p>0verflow announced work on <i>School Days</i> in October 2004 and marketed it through public venues that showcased the game's innovative use of <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a>-like cinematics and voice. The game ranked as the best-selling visual novel in Japan for the time of its release, continuing to chart in the national top 50 for nearly five months afterward. 0verflow would then go on to produce <a href="#Sequels">multiple sequels</a> including a <a href="/wiki/Spin-off_(media)" title="Spin-off (media)">spin-off</a> of the original story called <i><a href="/wiki/Summer_Days" title="Summer Days">Summer Days</a></i>, and a <a href="/wiki/Sequel#Parallel" title="Sequel">parallel</a> story called <i><a href="/wiki/Cross_Days" title="Cross Days">Cross Days</a></i>. Another spin-off, <i><a href="/wiki/Island_Days" title="Island Days">Island Days</a></i>, has been developed by Klon for the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_3DS" title="Nintendo 3DS">Nintendo 3DS</a>. <i>School Days</i> was remastered as <i>School Days HQ</i> on October 8, 2010 and localized in North America on June 27, 2012. The original game was officially discontinued April 21, 2011.</p>
<p>Following the game's release, <i>School Days</i> made several transitions into other media. The original story was adapted into a <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> and serialized in the <a href="/wiki/Kadokawa_Shoten" title="Kadokawa Shoten">Kadokawa Shoten</a> magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Comp_Ace" title="Comp Ace">Comp Ace</a></i>; it was later published into two volumes. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comic_anthology" title="Comic anthology">Comic anthologies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Light_novel" title="Light novel">light novels</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Art_book" title="Art book">art books</a> were also published, as were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Audio_drama" title="Audio drama">audio dramas</a> and several albums of music. An animated television series, two <a href="/wiki/Direct-to-video" title="Direct-to-video">direct-to-video</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OVA" title="OVA">OVA</a>) single releases, and a <a href="/wiki/Concert_film" title="Concert film">concert film</a> were also produced, the first of which became a precursor for an <a href="/wiki/Internet_meme" title="Internet meme">internet meme</a> when its finale was pulled from broadcast.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Days_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>As a visual novel, <i>School Days</i> contains extremely minimal gameplay. The game's core onscreen presentation is composed of scenes that are viewed from a mostly third-person perspective. At predetermined intervals, the game pauses, and players are presented with one to two responses or actions relevant to the scene in progress to make, or not make, on behalf of characters. Each selection branches the game's progress up to that point in an alternate direction, while also causing the player's love toward a character to blossom, plateau, or diminish,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-schoolspec_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-schoolspec-1">[1]</a></sup> thus providing for a nonlinear storytelling experience. Being an erotic title, relationships between characters may become sexual; scenes of this kind depict a varying combination of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_kissing" title="French kissing">French kissing</a>, masturbation, oral sex and intercourse. Genitalia are <a href="/wiki/Pixelization" title="Pixelization">pixelated</a> in the original Japanese releases, but the Western release of <i>School Days HQ</i> is uncensored.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Sex scenes are omitted from the PlayStation 2 and PlayStation Portable ports of the game.</p>
<p>Each route the game takes invariably concludes with an ending specific to the choices made up to that point. Depending on these choices, the outcome of the story will either be good or bad. <i>School Days</i> became popularly known for its bad endings,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> which depict the deaths of characters. In one ending titled "The Bloody Conclusion", a character's neck is slit open with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D%C5%8Dzuki" title="Dōzuki">dōzuki</a>, causing her to collapse and die in a fountain of blood to the manic laughter of her assailant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In another called "Eternally", a character commits suicide leaning over and falling off the roof of a school, landing headfirst on a sidewalk to the horror of other characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The game's good endings, by contrast, depict the well-being of characters and their relationships. In the ending "Christmas Eve", a couple share an embrace in a hotel room as snow falls around a surrounding cityscape.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In another titled "Two Lovers", a pair of characters agree to have a <a href="/wiki/Polyamory" title="Polyamory">polyamorous</a> relationship with the male lead instead of rivaling with each other.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Because of the numerous <a href="/wiki/Alternate_ending" title="Alternate ending">alternate endings</a> that can be achieved, players who wish to watch additional endings and sex scenes will have to play through the game more than once.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vn_definition_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-vn_definition-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Unlike traditional visual novels that consist of static characters with subtitled dialogue,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vn_definition_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-vn_definition-8">[8]</a></sup> <i>School Days</i> is unusual in that it incorporates motion and voice, making it reminiscent of an animated series. Cinematics naturally play on their own, and players are afforded the ability to pause, fast-forward, and even skip those they've seen before. Male and female voices can be muted, subtitles can be turned on or off and the game can be played from a window or fullscreen ratio. Progress can be saved at any time in up to 100 slots and loaded from either the main menu or during play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-schoolspec_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-schoolspec-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Days_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_School_Days_characters" title="List of School Days characters">List of School Days characters</a></div>
<p><i>School Days</i> focuses on the life of Makoto Ito, a first-year high school student<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> living with his divorced and <a href="/wiki/Unseen_character" title="Unseen character">unseen</a> mother in the fictional city of Haramihama. During his second term, he becomes infatuated with Kotonoha Katsura, a soft-spoken schoolmate who shares train rides with him to and from campus. When his homeroom seating plan is rearranged, he becomes acquaintances with Sekai Saionji, an upbeat girl who takes a special interest in his newfound crush, befriending the two and providing them the grounds to meet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In spite of her triumphant efforts however, Sekai becomes jealous of the new couple, having developed feelings for Makoto herself.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Days_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>School Days</i> was 0verflow's tenth game to develop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> News of <i>School Days</i> first surfaced on October 5, 2004 when 0verflow posted a link to the game's then-live website and development log on their official site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-overflowhp_910_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-overflowhp_910-12">[12]</a></sup> In the blog, 0verflow revealed that <i>School Days</i> had been in pre-production for roughly two years and would be a fully animated game. Updates on the development of the game were scheduled for Tuesdays and Fridays, and the company encouraged fans to attend Dream Party 2004, an upcoming anime convention at <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Big_Sight" title="Tokyo Big Sight">Tokyo Big Sight</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ariake,_Tokyo" title="Ariake, Tokyo">Ariake</a>, on October 11, where it would make its first public showcase of the game and characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_blog_10_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-sd_blog_10-13">[13]</a></sup> A follow-up of the venue was made on October 15. Toward the end of the month on October 26, 0verflow posted that new information about <i>School Days</i> would be circulated in the November issue of <i>Tech Gian</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_blog_10_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-sd_blog_10-13">[13]</a></sup> an adult magazine published by <a href="/wiki/Enterbrain" title="Enterbrain">Enterbrain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Promotion for the game began shortly after. In a November 6 development post, 0verflow announced that it was planning a public screening of new game material, but was experiencing difficulty acquiring space for it. The company had initially chosen to have the venue in <a href="/wiki/Akihabara" title="Akihabara">Akihabara</a> but was unable to find a retailer willing to host it, prompting a visit to <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a> the following week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_blog_11_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-sd_blog_11-15">[15]</a></sup> News that the game would contain music by artists such as <a href="/wiki/Minami_Kuribayashi" title="Minami Kuribayashi">Minami Kuribayashi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haruko_Momoi" title="Haruko Momoi">Haruko Momoi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yozuca" title="Yozuca">Yozuca*</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/YURIA" title="YURIA">YURIA</a> was posted on November 30, along with a release date change to February 25, 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_blog_11_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-sd_blog_11-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 28, 2004,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> 0verflow released a public trial of <i>School Days</i> and announced that the company would be attending <a href="/wiki/Comiket" title="Comiket">Comiket</a> 67 at Tokyo Big Sight from December 29 to 30, handing out <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phonecard" title="Phonecard">phonecards</a> to the first 50 visitors to their booth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Two months later, on February 2, 2005, the company announced that the game had been postponed again to April 28.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> From April 5 to April 8, 0verflow concluded their development log with comments from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soyogi_T%C5%8Dno" title="Soyogi Tōno">Soyogi Tōno</a>, Kaname Yuzuki, Tatsuya Hirai, <a href="/wiki/Yuki_Matsunaga" title="Yuki Matsunaga">Yuki Matsunaga</a>, Hikaru Isshiki and Hana Yamamoto, the respective voices of Kotonoha, Sekai, Makoto, Otome, Hikari and Setsuna.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> To address bugs that were later found in the game, 0verflow released a patch to bring <i>School Days</i> to a stable build of 1.11.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On June 26, 2007, 0verflow and Lantis-net radio began to air an internet radio drama called "Radio School Days".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Broadcasts finished up March 28, 2008 with 39 episodes aired.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 21, 2011,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-overflowoffical_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-overflowoffical-23">[23]</a></sup> 0verflow announced via their blog that support for <i>School Days</i> was being discontinued.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_history">Release history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Days_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Release history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>School Days</i> was <a href="/wiki/Porting" title="Porting">ported</a> to three other platforms. The first of these was by AiCherry, an <a href="/wiki/Interactive_movie" title="Interactive movie">interactive movie</a> developer, who announced on August 20, 2007 that it had picked up the game for development,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-506summerblog_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-506summerblog-25">[25]</a></sup> releasing it as a four disc <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DVD_game" title="DVD game">DVD game</a> on September 28.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> That same year, on August 31,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Interchannel" title="Interchannel">Interchannel</a> posted a link on their blog to the official website of <i>School Days L×H</i>, a <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a> (PS2) port in development by Guyzware,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> that would be edited of explicit sexual content. The game was released on January 17, 2008<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> and assessed by the <a href="/wiki/Computer_Entertainment_Rating_Organization" title="Computer Entertainment Rating Organization">Computer Entertainment Rating Organization</a> (CERO), Japan's primary <a href="/wiki/Video_game_content_rating_system" title="Video game content rating system">video game content rating system</a>, receiving a 15-years-or-older "C" rating for sexual themes and profanity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The third and final port was developed by PalaceGame for the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Portable" title="PlayStation Portable">PlayStation Portable</a> (PSP). After postponing the game on May 11, it was released as four <a href="/wiki/Universal_Media_Disc" title="Universal Media Disc">UMDs</a> on June 30, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>0verflow opened what would become the homepage for <i>School Days HQ</i> on May 21, 2010, a remastering of the original game initially scheduled for release in August.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> The site was finished June 3, and on July 16, the game was postponed to September 24. A trial was publicly released for download on August 7, and from August 20 to September 28, 0verflow promoted the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sdhq_whatsnew_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-sdhq_whatsnew-33">[33]</a></sup> Customers who had purchased the original <i>School Days</i> would be given the opportunity to upgrade to <i>HQ</i> for a fee until October 11, by either mailing in their game disc or visiting stores in Akihabara, Osaka or Nagoya.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Distributors offered collectable phonecards for reservations,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> and after a second date change on September 10, the game was released October 8.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sdhq_whatsnew_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-sdhq_whatsnew-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 3, 2011, 0verflow acknowledged <a href="/wiki/Sekai_Project" title="Sekai Project">Sekai Project</a>, an unofficial <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fan_translation_(video_gaming)" title="Fan translation (video gaming)">fan translation</a> group, who had reportedly begun <a href="/wiki/Language_localisation" title="Language localisation">localizing</a> <i>School Days</i> in April 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Partnering with American distributor <a href="/wiki/JAST_USA" title="JAST USA">JAST USA</a>, <i>School Days HQ</i>, uncensored in English, was announced for a North American release on March 11, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Development instead continued into 2012, and on May 18, JAST announced that the company had begun taking pre-orders for the Collector's Edition, a bundled release of the game packaged with a keychain and mousepad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> The company announced weeks later on June 1 that <i>School Days HQ</i> had <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_master" title="Golden master">gone gold</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_gold_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-sd_gold-39">[39]</a></sup> Following news on June 26 that the company would exhibit the game at <a href="/wiki/Anime_Expo" title="Anime Expo">Anime Expo</a> 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> JAST made an update to the June 1 announcement that <i>School Days HQ</i> had begun shipping.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_gold_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-sd_gold-39">[39]</a></sup> The downloadable version of the game was later released on August 6.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sales">Sales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Days_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sales">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In a national sales ranking of <a href="/wiki/Bish%C5%8Djo_game" title="Bishōjo game">bishōjo games</a> in <i>PCNEWS</i>, a now-defunct Japanese <a href="/wiki/Online_magazine" title="Online magazine">online magazine</a>, <i>School Days</i> premiered as the number one game sold for the second half of April 2005,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> the second and seventeenth for the first and second halves of May,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> the fifth and twenty-sixth for the first and second halves of June,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> and twenty-seventh for the first half of July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> The <i>School Days</i> renewal edition, released a week after the previous ranking, continued to chart on behalf of the game; it ranked as the thirty-third most sold game for the second half of July,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> before ending as the thirty-fifth and forty-ninth for the first and second halves of August.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>Getchu.com, a major distributor of visual novels and domestic anime products, recorded similar sales. <i>School Days</i> for Windows premiered as the number one game sold for the month of its release,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> and seventh most for May,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> ranking as the number one game sold for the first half of 2005<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> and ninth for the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> The following year, the <i>School Days</i> renewal edition charted as the twentieth most sold game for July 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> dropping to thirtieth from August to October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> <i>School Days HQ</i> ranked as the sixth most sold game for October 2010<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> but failed to chart thereafter.</p>
<p>According to <i><a href="/wiki/Gamasutra" title="Gamasutra">Gamasutra</a></i>, a video gaming news site, <i>School Days L×H</i> ranked as the number one PlayStation 2 game sold for January 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Days_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Related_visual_novels">Related visual novels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Days_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Related visual novels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>0verflow developed several visual novels related to <i>School Days</i>, sharing the same universe. Prior to the development of <i>School Days</i>, 0verflow developed the <i>Radish Vacation</i> visual novel series. The first is <i>Snow Radish Vacation</i> released on December 28, 2001, followed by <i>Summer Radish Vacation</i> on April 1, 2003 and finally <i>Summer Radish Vacation 2</i> on August 13, 2004.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Spin-off_(media)" title="Spin-off (media)">spin-off</a> titled <i><a href="/wiki/Summer_Days" title="Summer Days">Summer Days</a></i>, was released on June 23, 2006, retelling the original game during summer vacation and from the perspective of Setsuna Kiyoura.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> Unlike its predecessor, however, <i>Summer Days</i> was almost universally panned for its heavily bugged state,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> loads of patches,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> and recall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> Another spin-off titled <i><a href="/wiki/Cross_Days" title="Cross Days">Cross Days</a></i> was released on March 19, 2010. Set in the same continuity as <i>School Days</i>, <i>Cross Days</i> follows the life of another protagonist, Yuuki Ashikaga, a high school freshman who also finds himself caught between the affection of two girls, Roka Kitsuregawa and Kotonoha Katsura, during his second term at Sakakino Academy. The game also features <a href="/wiki/Yaoi" title="Yaoi">yaoi</a> scenarios, during which Yuuki <a href="/wiki/Cross-dressing" title="Cross-dressing">cross-dresses</a> as a maid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-story_65-0"><a href="#cite_note-story-65">[65]</a></sup> 0verflow released <i>Shiny Days</i> on February 2, 2012 which establishes a solid link between the <i>Days</i> series and <i>Radish Vacation</i> series.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> A third spin-off, <i><a href="/wiki/Island_Days" title="Island Days">Island Days</a></i>, was developed for the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_3DS" title="Nintendo 3DS">Nintendo 3DS</a> and was released in Japan on July 3, 2014. The game, developed by Klon, focuses on Makoto and the girls becoming trapped on a remote island and includes <a href="/wiki/Tower_defense" title="Tower defense">tower defense</a> elements.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Days_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Based on the story of the original game, <i>School Days</i> was reimagined as a <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a>, illustrated by Homare Sakazuki and serialized in the <a href="/wiki/Kadokawa_Shoten" title="Kadokawa Shoten">Kadokawa Shoten</a> magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Comp_Ace" title="Comp Ace">Comp Ace</a></i> from May 26, 2006<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> to September 26, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> On July 12, 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> 0verflow announced that the manga had been collected into its first volume, comprising five chapters set to be released on July 26.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_manga01_70-0"><a href="#cite_note-sd_manga01-70">[70]</a></sup> Later that year, the second and final volume, comprising the remaining seven chapters, was released on November 26.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_manga02_71-0"><a href="#cite_note-sd_manga02-71">[71]</a></sup></p>

<p>Various artists also produced short manga of <i>School Days</i> that were compiled into two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comic_anthology" title="Comic anthology">comic anthologies</a>. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Books_and_publications">Books and publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Days_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Books and publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

School Days Visual Collection" on August 31, 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Days_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_School_Days_episodes" title="List of School Days episodes">List of School Days episodes</a></div>
<p><i>School Days</i> was adapted into a twelve-episode <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> television series by <a href="/wiki/TNK_(company)" title="TNK (company)">TNK</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup> Concrete news of this first appeared on June 1, 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-0verflow_topics_84-0"><a href="#cite_note-0verflow_topics-84">[84]</a></sup> when 0verflow announced via its blog that the show's official website had gone live.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup> Stations participating in the broadcast included <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_Kanagawa" title="TV Kanagawa">TV Kanagawa</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chiba_TV" title="Chiba TV">Chiba TV</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_Aichi" title="TV Aichi">TV Aichi</a>, <a href="/wiki/TV_Osaka" title="TV Osaka">TV Osaka</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_Saitama" title="TV Saitama">TV Saitama</a> and <a href="/wiki/AT-X_(company)" title="AT-X (company)">AT-X</a>, the premiere of which would air on TV Kanagawa on July 3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sdanime_homepage_86-0"><a href="#cite_note-sdanime_homepage-86">[86]</a></sup> The anime was aired until September 27 and finished its broadcast on AT-X.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> From September 26, 2007 to February 27, 2008, the series was compiled into six limited and regular edition <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVDs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup></p>
<p>TNK also produced two <a href="/wiki/Direct-to-video" title="Direct-to-video">direct-to-video</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OVA" title="OVA">OVA</a>) episodes of <i>School Days</i>. The first, titled "Valentine Days", was bundled with limited edition copies of <i>School Days L×H</i>, and features an unrelated comedic romp through <a href="/wiki/Valentine%27s_Day" title="Valentine's Day">Valentine's Day</a> as Kotonoha, Sekai, and Otome try to give Makoto <a href="/wiki/Giri_choco" title="Giri choco">giri chocos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-89"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup> The second, "Magical Heart Kokoro-chan", jaunts the series into <a href="/wiki/Magical_girl" title="Magical girl">magical girl</a> territory, portraying Kokoro Katsura as the superheroine Magical Heart; it was released on March 26, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Discotek_Media" title="Discotek Media">Discotek Media</a> has acquired the television series and the "Magical Heart Kokoro-chan" OVA to be released on DVD on June 24, 2014 with English subtitles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Delay_of_finale">Delay of finale</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Days_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Delay of finale">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Schooldaysniceboat.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="225" data-file-width="300" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f9/Schooldaysniceboat.jpg/220px-Schooldaysniceboat.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/f/f9/Schooldaysniceboat.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/></a>

<p>On September 17, 2007, the day before the twelfth and final episode of the televised anime was to air on TV Kanagawa, a sixteen-year-old girl murdered her forty-five-year-old father in their <a href="/wiki/Kyoto" title="Kyoto">Kyoto</a> home with an axe. TV Kanagawa promptly cancelled the Tuesday airing of the finale for its similarly violent content,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mainichi_92-0"><a href="#cite_note-mainichi-92">[92]</a></sup> replacing it instead with a thirty-minute video compilation of scenery footage from <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, played to <a href="/wiki/August_Wilhelmj" title="August Wilhelmj">August Wilhelmj</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Air_on_the_G_String" title="Air on the G String">Air on the G String</a>". Newspapers such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Japan_Times" title="The Japan Times">The Japan Times</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Mainichi_Shimbun" title="Mainichi Shimbun">Mainichi Shimbun</a></i> reported on the killing and episode preempt on September 19.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mainichi_92-1"><a href="#cite_note-mainichi-92">[92]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-japantimes_93-0"><a href="#cite_note-japantimes-93">[93]</a></sup> According to <i>Mainichi Shimbun</i>, Chiba TV and TV Aichi had also cancelled their airings, with AT-X the only station remaining indecisive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mainichi_92-2"><a href="#cite_note-mainichi-92">[92]</a></sup> 0verflow issued an apology through their blog the same day,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-0verflow_topics_84-1"><a href="#cite_note-0verflow_topics-84">[84]</a></sup> asking viewers to stay tuned for updates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup> In the following week, 0verflow announced that it had arranged for two screenings of the edited finale at the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Anime_Center" title="Tokyo Anime Center">Akihabara 3D Theater</a> on September 27. Those wishing to attend would be required to register a seat by email, be at least 18 years old with photo ID, and bring a Windows copy of <i>School Days</i> or <i>Summer Days</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-95"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup> That same day AT-X announced that it had decided to air the unedited finale on September 27 and October 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-96"><a href="#cite_note-96">[96]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the wake of the broadcast change, a screen capture of the <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norwegian</a> M/S Skagastøl<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-97"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup> from the slideshow surfaced on the Internet alongside the caption "Nice boat.", a phrase which gained popularity in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-98"><a href="#cite_note-98">[98]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Google_Trends" title="Google Trends">Google Trends</a> recorded a spike in "Nice Boat" searches around the third-quarter of 2007<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-99"><a href="#cite_note-99">[99]</a></sup> while <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yomiuri_Shinbun" title="Yomiuri Shinbun">Yomiuri Shinbun</a></i>, a Japanese newspaper, reported that "Nice boat" was the tenth most popular <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!_Japan" title="Yahoo! Japan">Yahoo! Japan</a> search from September 17 to September 23.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100"><a href="#cite_note-100">[100]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Nice Boat</i> had become so well known that it was used in other media. The meme appeared as an <a href="/wiki/Easter_egg_(media)" title="Easter egg (media)">Easter egg</a> in the first episode of <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_Ef_episodes#Ef:_A_Tale_of_Memories." title="List of Ef episodes">Ef: A Tale of Memories.</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-101"><a href="#cite_note-101">[101]</a></sup> and was parodied on February 13, 2009 when the <a href="/wiki/Kadokawa_Pictures" title="Kadokawa Pictures">Kadokawa Pictures</a> <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel uploaded a short <a href="/wiki/Montage_(filmmaking)" title="Montage (filmmaking)">montage</a> of sailboats instead of the previously scheduled premiere of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Melancholy_of_Haruhi_Suzumiya" title="The Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya">The Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-102"><a href="#cite_note-102">[102]</a></sup> 0verflow capitalized on the popularity of the phrase, naming their booth at Comiket 73 "Nice boat." and selling meme inspired merchandise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-103"><a href="#cite_note-103">[103]</a></sup> TNK also paid homage to the meme in "Magical Heart Kokoro-chan", a special direct-to-video episode of the animated series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-104"><a href="#cite_note-104">[104]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Concert_film">Concert film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Days_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Concert film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Besides the video releases of the <i>School Days</i> anime, a <a href="/wiki/Concert_film" title="Concert film">concert film</a>, the <i>School Days Secret Live DVD</i>, was also produced by HOBiRECORDS.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HOBiDVD_105-0"><a href="#cite_note-HOBiDVD-105">[105]</a></sup> Released on June 26, 2006 in conjunction with <i>Summer Days</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-106"><a href="#cite_note-106">[106]</a></sup> the film contains footage of a concert held on June 15, 2005<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-107"><a href="#cite_note-107">[107]</a></sup> featuring the performances of <a href="/wiki/Miyuki_Hashimoto" title="Miyuki Hashimoto">Miyuki Hashimoto</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/YURIA" title="YURIA">YURIA</a>, <a href="/wiki/CooRie" title="CooRie">rino</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yozuca*" title="Yozuca*">yozuca*</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minami_Kuribayashi" title="Minami Kuribayashi">Minami Kuribayashi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HOBiDVD_105-1"><a href="#cite_note-HOBiDVD-105">[105]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Audio_CDs">Audio CDs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Days_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Audio CDs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 2005 to 2010, <a href="/wiki/Lantis_(company)" title="Lantis (company)">Lantis</a> published six albums of <i>School Days</i> music. The "School Days Vocal Album", a <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">compilation</a> featuring all nine of the game's closing themes, as performed by artists yozuca*, Miyuki Hashimoto, YURIA, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Halko_Momoi" title="Halko Momoi">Halko Momoi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minami_Kuribayashi" title="Minami Kuribayashi">Minami Kuribayashi</a>, rino, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kanako_Ito" title="Kanako Ito">Kanako Ito</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-108"><a href="#cite_note-108">[108]</a></sup> was the first to be distributed, sharing its April 28, 2005 date with the release of the game itself. The remaining twenty-eight background scores, composed by KIRIKO/HIKO Sound, were released on July 21, officially completing the game's soundtrack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-109"><a href="#cite_note-109">[109]</a></sup> Another compilation, the "School Days Vocal Complete Album [<i><a href="/wiki/Sic" title="Sic">sic</a></i>]" featuring songs from <i>Summer Days</i> and <i>Cross Days</i>, was released on October 8, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-110"><a href="#cite_note-110">[110]</a></sup></p>
<p>Three weeks after the premiere of the televised anime on July 25, 2007, Lantis published the <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a> "Innocent Blue" by DeviceHigh, a four-track disc featuring the anime opening of the same name, a disco-inspired song called Dancin' Joker, and their <a href="/wiki/Instrumental" title="Instrumental">instrumentals</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-111"><a href="#cite_note-111">[111]</a></sup> Lantis followed with "School Days: Ending Theme+" on August 22, a sixteen-track disc containing all of the show's closing themes and background scores on September 26.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-112"><a href="#cite_note-112">[112]</a></sup></p>
<p>In addition to music albums, six <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Audio_drama" title="Audio drama">audio dramas</a> were also produced. "School Days Little Promise", a chronicle of Sekai and Setsuna's childhoods, was the first. Featuring music by KIRIKO/HIKO Sound and Kanako Ito, HOBiRECORDS published "Little Promise" as a two-disc set, which 0verflow scheduled for release January 27, 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-113"><a href="#cite_note-113">[113]</a></sup> Pre-releases were sold at Comiket 69 from December 29 to 30, 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-114"><a href="#cite_note-114">[114]</a></sup> Sometime afterward however, 0verflow announced that pre-released copies of Little Promise were defective, and asked customers to mail in their discs for replacements. The release was also postponed to February 24.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-115"><a href="#cite_note-115">[115]</a></sup> Lantis released the remaining five dramas. "School Days Drama CD Vol. 1 Himitsu Hanazono" (ヒ・ミ・ツの花園), a merrymaking of the series six main girls, was released on August 8, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-116"><a href="#cite_note-116">[116]</a></sup> A second, "School Days Drama CD Vol 2. Koi no Nou-hau" (恋のノ・ウ・ハ・ウ), was released October 24, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-117"><a href="#cite_note-117">[117]</a></sup> Radio School Days was compiled into three separate albums: "Futakumidake no Taiikusai" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">二組だけの体育祭</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> was released on November 21, 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-118"><a href="#cite_note-118">[118]</a></sup> "Futakumidake no Shakaika Kengaku" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">二組だけの社会科見学</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> on February 6, 2008,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-119"><a href="#cite_note-119">[119]</a></sup> and "Futakumi Ijou no Rakkasan Butai" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">二組以上の落下傘部隊</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> on June 21.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-120"><a href="#cite_note-120">[120]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Merchandise">Merchandise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Days_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Merchandise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Considerable effort was made to market and promote <i>School Days</i> before and after its release, including the sale of brand merchandise and use of incentives. Through public venues and through the company's online store, 0verflow sold brand keystraps, mousepads, phonecards, book covers, mugs, t-shirts,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-121"><a href="#cite_note-121">[121]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dakimakura" title="Dakimakura">dakimakura</a> cases, <a href="/wiki/Polyvinyl_chloride" title="Polyvinyl chloride">PVC</a> figures,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-122"><a href="#cite_note-122">[122]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lanyard" title="Lanyard">lanyards</a>, business card holders and <a href="/wiki/Cosplay" title="Cosplay">cosplay</a> material such as the girls' school uniforms and plastic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D%C5%8Dzuki" title="Dōzuki">dōzuki</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-123"><a href="#cite_note-123">[123]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_Days_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>UK Anime Network</i>, a British online anime and manga magazine, gave the television series a 7 out of 10, summarizing it as "An utter subversion of the high school romance genre, which is disturbing and unsettling... yet oddly compelling in its own way."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-124"><a href="#cite_note-124">[124]</a></sup> <i>THEM Anime Reviews</i>, a website devoted to the review of anime, gave the series a less favorable 1 out of 5 stars, citing "overblown, shallow, and flat" characters and that although "School Days looks fascinating when you see screenshots of it and read episode guides", the reviewer found it to be "annoying, stupid, mean-spirited, and full of some of the biggest idiots [he had] seen in an anime in a long time."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-125"><a href="#cite_note-125">[125]</a></sup></p>
