<p><i><b>Skin Trade</b></i> is a 2014 <a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action</a> <a href="/wiki/Thriller_film" title="Thriller film">thriller film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Ekachai_Uekrongtham" title="Ekachai Uekrongtham">Ekachai Uekrongtham</a>, and starring <a href="/wiki/Dolph_Lundgren" title="Dolph Lundgren">Dolph Lundgren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Jaa" title="Tony Jaa">Tony Jaa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jai_White" title="Michael Jai White">Michael Jai White</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ron_Perlman" title="Ron Perlman">Ron Perlman</a>. Lundgren wrote the film with <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Dowrick" title="Gabriel Dowrick">Gabriel Dowrick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steven_Elder" title="Steven Elder">Steven Elder</a>, while <a href="/wiki/John_Hyams" title="John Hyams">John Hyams</a> performed uncredited script revisions. The film centers around New Jersey police detective Nick Cassidy, as he travels to Asia intent on killing the man who murdered his family, mobster Viktor Dragovic. He is also set on destroying Dragovic's human trafficking network.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KenberInterview_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-KenberInterview-5">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Development started in 2007 after Lundgren read a news report about a group of girls being smuggled into the United States from Mexico. The girls were left in a vehicle along the border, and trapped inside, they all died of heat stroke and suffocation. <i>Skin Trade</i> had a $9 million production budget, and was shot over 50 days in Canada and Thailand.</p>
<p>The film premiered at the <a href="/wiki/American_Film_Market" title="American Film Market">American Film Market</a> on November 7, 2014. This was followed by a limited theatrical release, starting on April 9, 2015 in the United Arab Emirates, and succeeded by Thailand (on April 23), Malaysia (on April 30), and the United States (on May 8). The film grossed $384 thousand at the worldwide box office, but it received mostly negative reviews. Particular criticism was aimed at Jaa's fluency in English, and that mainly revolved around <a href="/wiki/Human_trafficking" title="Human trafficking">human trafficking</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skin_Trade_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a <a href="/wiki/Prologue" title="Prologue">prologue</a>. a nameless Cambodian girl leaves her village for Bangkok. Upon arriving, she is kidnapped, drugged, and sold in the <a href="/wiki/Human_trafficking" title="Human trafficking">skin trade</a>.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, as this movie starts in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_of_Newark" title="City of Newark">city of Newark</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newark_Police_Department" title="Newark Police Department">Newark Police Department</a> <a href="/wiki/Detective" title="Detective">detective</a> named Nick Cassidy discovers that Serbian mobster Viktor Dragovic is wreaking havoc with his hitmen. Meanwhile, in Bangkok, detective Tony Vitayakul attempts to buy a Thai girl from a group of human traffickers. When his cover is blown, he takes down the traffickers and frees the girl from captivity.</p>
<p>Captain Costello and Cassidy brief the police department on Dragovic; revealing he runs the largest human trafficking network in the world. As a cargo ship owned by Dragovic approaches America, Cassidy and the department prepare to intercept it at the docks. When the ship arrives, Dragovic discovers the trafficked women have died during transport. The ship's captain is held responsible and shot in the head. A shootout erupts as the police move in for an arrest. Cassidy chases Dragovic and his son, Andre, as they flee. He fatally shoots Andre in self-defense and Dragovic is arrested. While in custody, Dragovic arranges to have Cassidy and his entire family murdered; having his house blown up the same night. Cassidy's wife and daughter are killed instantly, while Cassidy survives after being shot in the back.</p>
<p>Costello and Reed, an FBI agent, visit Cassidy in hospital. They tell him Dragovic fled the US after being bailed. As soon as they leave, Cassidy staggers out of the hospital unnoticed. He gathers a few weapons and travels to the restaurant of Dragovic's attorney. After forcing the attorney to reveal Dragovic's whereabouts, Cassidy kills the attorney and blows up the restaurant.</p>
<p>In Cambodia, Senator Khat warns Dragovic that unless he leaves the country immediately, he will be arrested and extradited to the United States. Dragovic blackmails the Senator into giving him two weeks to put his affairs in order and flee.</p>
<p>Cassidy travels to Thailand in pursuit of Dragovic. Believing that Cassidy has experienced a <a href="/wiki/Psychotic_break" title="Psychotic break">nervous breakdown</a>, the US authorities have ordered Reed to detain him as soon as he arrives. Tony and his partner, Nung, are told to assist with the arrest. At Suvarnabhumi airport, Cassidy flees as the police try to arrest him. Reed, who has been <a href="/wiki/Corruption" title="Corruption">bought off</a> by Dragovic, kills Nung and frames Cassidy for the murder. Tony pursues Cassidy through the streets, but Cassidy escapes; he travels to a nightclub in Poipet, where - after torturing one of Dragovic's men - he discovers the location of Dragovic's current operations. Tony and Reed arrive at the nightclub and attempt to arrest Cassidy. After fighting with Tony, Cassidy escapes injured. Reed then uses Tony's cell phone to uncover an informant, who happens to be Tony's girlfriend, Min. While attempting to sabotage Dragovic's operations, a shootout erupts between Cassidy and Dragovic's men. Ivan and Goran shoot their half-brother, Janko, on behalf of their father. Tony arrives and attempts to kill Cassidy, but after learning the truth about his partner's death, he kills Reed instead. Before dying, Janko reveals his father's location.</p>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:220px; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 85%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="background-color: #F9F9F9; color: black; text-align: center; font-size: larger; font-weight: bold;">Closing narration</div>
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>I don't know where she is. But whatever it takes, wherever I have to go, I'm gonna find my daughter.</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: right;">Nick Cassidy</cite></div>
<p>The next day, Cassidy and Tony storm Dragovic's compound. While there, Cassidy learns that his daughter, Sofia, was not killed, but placed in the human trafficking trade. Ivan tries to kill Min, but Tony shoots him in the head. Cassidy destroys a vehicle with a rocket-launcher, and as a result, Dragovic's helicopter leaves without him. During a shootout between Cassidy and Dragovic's men, Goran is killed in a hand-to-hand fight with Tony. After the shootout, Cassidy fights with Dragovic; ultimately stabbing him in the chest. He attempts to retrieve the whereabouts of his daughter from the dying Dragovic, but fails.</p>
<p>In the aftermath, Cassidy says goodbye to Tony and Min. Before leaving, he gives them a picture of Sofia and tells them to hold onto it until he has found her. He then sets out in search of his daughter.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skin_Trade_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lundgren started researching human trafficking in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LundgrenParade_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-LundgrenParade-6">[5]</a></sup> He claimed there were "20 million slaves in the world", and that human trafficking was a "$20 billion industry"; the world's "second largest" illegal trafficking enterprise (as of 2015).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LundgrenTEDxFulbright_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-LundgrenTEDxFulbright-7">[6]</a></sup> He started developing <i>Skin Trade</i> in 2007; after reading a news report about a group of girls being smuggled into the United States from Mexico. The girls were left in a vehicle along the border; trapped inside and with no means of escape, they all died of heat stroke and suffocation. Lundgren, who had "two young daughters" at the time, felt the story of human trafficking "had to be told".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ExLundgrenSTrade_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ExLundgrenSTrade-8">[7]</a></sup> He related with each of the victims, saying: "these people are physically humiliated [and] psychologically abused to have no self worth, sort of like [how] I used to feel".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LundgrenTEDxFulbright_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-LundgrenTEDxFulbright-7">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Screenplay">Screenplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skin_Trade_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Screenplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lundgren wrote the screenplay with <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Dowrick" title="Gabriel Dowrick">Gabriel Dowrick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steven_Elder" title="Steven Elder">Steven Elder</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bh_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-bh-9">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twitch_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-twitch-10">[9]</a></sup> while <a href="/wiki/John_Hyams" title="John Hyams">John Hyams</a> performed uncredited script revisions;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twitch_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-twitch-10">[9]</a></sup> seven in total, frequently regarding the setting as Lundgren sought financing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CS_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-CS-11">[10]</a></sup> The original script was set in Russia. Lundgren even went as far as to scout for locations and actors; and to seek financing in Moscow, but it "didn't work out". He changed the setting to Southeast Asia after meeting "someone" interested in financing the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Radar_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Radar-12">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casting">Casting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skin_Trade_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Casting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Originally, Lundgren planned on playing a supporting role, with a more famous actor in the lead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dolph_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-dolph-13">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BorKit_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-BorKit-15">[14]</a></sup> He also considered directing the film,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KenberInterview_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-KenberInterview-5">[4]</a></sup> but decided against it, as he desired to learn more about producing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LundgrenProducing_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-LundgrenProducing-16">[15]</a></sup> In 2013, Lundgren announced the casting of <a href="/wiki/Tony_Jaa" title="Tony Jaa">Tony Jaa</a>, in the role of Tony Vitayakul; and <a href="/wiki/Ekachai_Uekrongtham" title="Ekachai Uekrongtham">Ekachai Uekrongtham</a> as the director.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tfilm_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-tfilm-17">[16]</a></sup> Lundgren chose Uekrongtham after being impressed with his film, <i><a href="/wiki/Beautiful_Boxer" title="Beautiful Boxer">Beautiful Boxer</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TatatBunnag_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-TatatBunnag-18">[17]</a></sup> He contacted him through a mutual friend in Los Angeles, and in mid-2013, they arranged to meet in the city. While he had previously turned down scripts offered to him for international markets, Uekrongtham found <i>Skin Trade</i> "riveting". He said the script "[had] the potential to work as a character-driven piece while saying something about how we deal with scars, literal and otherwise".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WongInterview_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-WongInterview-1">[1]</a></sup> Jaa, on the other hand, met with Lundgren through his manager and film agent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LGolden_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-LGolden-19">[18]</a></sup> He couldn't speak any English when cast, but claimed to have taken "intensive" lessons in preparation for his role; stating he is now "reasonably fluent" in "conversational" English.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tony_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-tony-20">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tasya Teles was cast as Rosa Cassidy on Christmas Day, 2013. She was heading to Thailand for a holiday "after a year of hard work", and her agent phoned to tell her about the "perfect role"; even insisting on her having a "quick look" at the script. Once Teles realized <i>Skin Trade</i> was about human trafficking, she was "instantly hooked".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TelesInterview_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-TelesInterview-21">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 7, 2014, SC Films announced the casting of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jai_White" title="Michael Jai White">Michael Jai White</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ron_Perlman" title="Ron Perlman">Ron Perlman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Weller" title="Peter Weller">Peter Weller</a>, <a href="/wiki/Celina_Jade" title="Celina Jade">Celina Jade</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cary-Hiroyuki_Tagawa" title="Cary-Hiroyuki Tagawa">Cary-Hiroyuki Tagawa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Broadway_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Broadway-22">[21]</a></sup> The film's co-producer, Craig Baumgarten, was White and Weller's manager;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GLWhiteInterview_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-GLWhiteInterview-23">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WellerInterview_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-WellerInterview-24">[23]</a></sup> he secured them both roles in the film. Weller was the original choice to play Viktor Dragovic, but due to scheduling issues, the role went to Perlman instead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WellerInterview_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-WellerInterview-24">[23]</a></sup> Furthermore, Lundgren considered having White to co-star;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MJWInterview4_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-MJWInterview4-25">[24]</a></sup> White only accepted the role of FBI agent Reed after finding the script "appealing".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morales_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Morales-26">[25]</a></sup></p>

<p>Filming began on February 2, 2014 in Thailand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Broadway_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-Broadway-22">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dh_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-dh-27">[26]</a></sup> <i>Skin Trade</i> was shot over 50 days: 43 filming in Thailand, and four filming in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dolph_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-dolph-13">[12]</a></sup> In Thailand, filming locations included: <a href="/wiki/Suvarnabhumi_Airport" title="Suvarnabhumi Airport">Suvarnabhumi Airport</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Min_Buri_District" title="Min Buri District">Min Buri District</a>, the Siam Kempinski Hotel, a rice mill, a leather-bleaching factory, and a century-old mansion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WongInterview_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-WongInterview-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TatatBunnag_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-TatatBunnag-18">[17]</a></sup> Filming was briefly disrupted by public protests relating to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Thai_political_crisis" title="2013–14 Thai political crisis">Thai political crisis</a>. To avoid any further interference, the cast and crew had to travel early to get through the traffic and to the set on time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DShamon_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-DShamon-28">[27]</a></sup> Most of the film was shot on location, but some scenes were filmed at Baanrig Studios.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WongInterview_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-WongInterview-1">[1]</a></sup> <i>Skin Trade</i> was the first film to be shot in English by an organization based in Asia (outside of Hong Kong) for an international theatrical release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tony_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-tony-20">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Uekrongtham arranged a "few lunches and dinners" between Lundgren and his on-screen family (Tasya Teles and Chloe Babcook), so they could bond "on a personal level" before filming scenes together.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DShamon_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-DShamon-28">[27]</a></sup> Babcook spent a week and a half filming her scenes in Bangkok, followed by a few days of filming in Vancouver.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BabcookInterview_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-BabcookInterview-29">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>The "heavily choreographed" fight scene between Lundgren and Jaa was planned over "[one] or two" months. It was rehearsed for two weeks and took a further week to film. Due to Lundgren's role as co-producer, the cast had a lot of freedom to improvise their scenes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Radar_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Radar-12">[11]</a></sup> Jaa filmed his fight scenes without using wirework or CGI. He felt this would give his character "more depth" and allow him to better display his acting abilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Covered_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Covered-30">[29]</a></sup> Jaa also performed all of his own stunts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TatatBunnag_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-TatatBunnag-18">[17]</a></sup> Lundgren claimed he was "very impressed" by Jaa's acting and fighting abilities. In his opinion, the majority of people wouldn't be able to perform Jaa's stunts without using wirework.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Covered_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-Covered-30">[29]</a></sup></p>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:700px; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>"In a less sophisticated movie they'll just fight forever, on this rooftop, that rooftop, the street, on the bus - I mean, it's okay to do that in a comedy. But in a real fight? That's why the <i><a href="/wiki/Rocky_(film_series)" title="Rocky (film series)">Rocky</a></i> movies are great: there's a story within the fights. Who wins, who's on top at the beginning, and then this changes, and then that one gets injured and the other one takes over; there's a beginning, middle, and an end to every fight. That's what you need. You need to figure that out before you throw the moves in there."</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;">— Lundgren discussing the choreography in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Radar_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-Radar-12">[11]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>According to White, his fight scenes with Jaa were "largely" choreographed ten minutes before filming.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JaiWhiteInterview_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-JaiWhiteInterview-31">[30]</a></sup> On the contrary, Jaa insisted it was something they were practicing "right up until the shoot". He recalled that while they were rehearsing the sequences and moves together "quite extensively" for a "number of days", they didn't rehearse them on the film set.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SamoRC_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-SamoRC-32">[31]</a></sup> The fight was filmed in three takes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MJW3_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-MJW3-33">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Effects">Effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skin_Trade_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Explosions were shot at Baanrig Studios;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WongInterview_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-WongInterview-1">[1]</a></sup> by the special effects team that had worked on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Expendables_(2010_film)" title="The Expendables (2010 film)">The Expendables</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TatatBunnag_18-3"><a href="#cite_note-TatatBunnag-18">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Editing">Editing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skin_Trade_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Editing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to Lundgren, the "large" number of editors resulted in him having less "[creative] control" over the final product. As co-producer of the film, Lundgren assumed he would have been more involved in the editing process.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LundgrenProducing_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-LundgrenProducing-16">[15]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skin_Trade_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A teaser poster was released in 2013,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TRigney_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-TRigney-37">[36]</a></sup> followed by a teaser trailer on March 26, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TFilmBrown_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-TFilmBrown-38">[37]</a></sup> On April 7 of the same year, Lundgren promoted <i>Skin Trade</i> on <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">CNN</a>. He appeared <a href="/wiki/Live_television" title="Live television">live</a> in the studio and discussed the film with news anchor <a href="/wiki/Richard_Quest" title="Richard Quest">Richard Quest</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LundgrenCNNLive_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-LundgrenCNNLive-39">[38]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/2014_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="2014 Cannes Film Festival">2014 Cannes Film Festival</a>, Hyde Park International presented potential buyers with nine minutes of footage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-screen_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-screen-40">[39]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Magnolia_Pictures" title="Magnolia Pictures">Magnolia Pictures</a> acquired the U.S. distribution rights on February 20, 2015, and announced they were releasing the film through their subsidiary label, Magnet Releasing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Keahon_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-Keahon-41">[40]</a></sup> The film's official trailer was released on March 12, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OfficialTrailerSkinTrade_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-OfficialTrailerSkinTrade-42">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skin_Trade_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Skin Trade</i> received mostly negative reviews. <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> reports that 25 percent of critics gave a positive review; the "average" rating being 4.8 out of 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.27Skin_Trade.27_reviews_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-.27Skin_Trade.27_reviews-43">[42]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, the film has a score of 39 out of 100, also indicating "generally unfavorable" reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MetacriticSkinTrade_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-MetacriticSkinTrade-44">[43]</a></sup></p>

<p>Martin Tsai, of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LA_Times" title="LA Times">LA Times</a>, described the film as a "movie where cops self-righteously act as judge, jury and executioner", without considering any protocols or procedures. He also said the film barely touches upon human trafficking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes-47">[46]</a></sup> Nick Schager, of <a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a>, came to a similar conclusion, calling the issue of human trafficking (within the film) a "window dressing" for standard "revenge-driven" action. Schager also criticized Jaa's lack of fluency in English.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Variety_46-1"><a href="#cite_note-Variety-46">[45]</a></sup> Other critics have had similar feelings; Ignatiy Vishnevetsky, of <a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">A.V. Club</a>, called it one of the film's "biggest drawbacks".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vishnevetsky_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-Vishnevetsky-45">[44]</a></sup></p>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:297px; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>"Offering literally nothing original, <i>Skin Trade</i> is just a bargain bin action vehicle for an aging star."</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;">— Adam DiLeo, of <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN Movies</a> (2015).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dileo_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dileo-48">[47]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>Chuck Bowen, of <a href="/wiki/Slant_Magazine" title="Slant Magazine">Slant Magazine</a>, heavily criticized the film; he awarded zero out of four stars. He said <i>Skin Trade</i> "fails" to satisfy even the "qualified expectations" that someone brings to a "low-rent Dolph Lundgren ass-kicker". Bowen went on to say there is "no beauty to this film, little rhythm", and none of the "physical grace" that action-film fans "crave".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBowen_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBowen-49">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Frank_Scheck" title="Frank Scheck">Frank Scheck</a>, of <a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a>, rated the film as poor, saying the film is "more suitable for late night cable viewing" than a theatrical release. He went on to criticize Uekrongtham's directing, saying that "[Uekrongtham] clearly paid more attention to the casting than the onscreen mechanics. And for a film so seemingly interested in educating audiences about the evils of sex trafficking", Uekrongtham has no problem with "including [a] copious [amount] of female nudity".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ScheckReview_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-ScheckReview-50">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>Scott Tobias, of <a href="/wiki/The_Dissolve" title="The Dissolve">The Dissolve</a>, rated the film two and a half out of five. Calling the film a "throwback to the one-man-army [action films]" of the 1980s, he said "if the film happens to raise awareness [of human trafficking]", then it's more of a "bonus than an objective". Tobias took a disliking to the film's "needlessly complicated setup", which - according to him - "posits <i>Skin Trade</i> as the splashy global production that it [clearly] is not".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TobDissolve_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-TobDissolve-51">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>Simon Abrams, of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rogerebert.com" title="Rogerebert.com">Rogerebert.com</a>, gave the film a positive review; he awarded three out of four stars, and praised the film's choreography and fight scenes. He said that "while time hasn't been kind to the best ass-kicking knuckle-draggers, <i>Skin Trade</i> is energetic and winsome". In his opinion, the movie "hails" from a "decadent period of action cinema"; "when every renegade cop had a private vendetta, a pet charity/social concern, and a few lousy quips in his back pocket". He summed his review saying: "you can teach a new dog old tricks, though why you would want to is anyone's guess".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AbramsReview_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-AbramsReview-52">[51]</a></sup> The Action Elite's Eoin Friel also enjoyed the film; he awarded four out of five stars, and praised the choreography, stunts and fight scenes. Unlike Schager or Vishnevetsky, Friel had no issues with Jaa's fluency in English, and even felt he "handled" the language "pretty well".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EoinFr_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-EoinFr-53">[52]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office_performance">Box office performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skin_Trade_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Box office performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film debuted in the United Arab Emirates on April 9, 2015. It peaked in fifth place at the box office, and made $79,286 from 19 screenings ($4,173 per theater).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mojo_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-4">[3]</a></sup> No information is available for the film's Thailand debut, but it peaked in fifth place during the second week, and grossed $137,643 from 40 screenings ($3,441 per theater). By the end of the third week, the film dropped to eleventh place at the Thai box office, making a further $3,686 (bringing the entire gross to $141,329 in Thailand).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Thailand_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-Thailand-54">[53]</a></sup> For its debut in Malaysia, <i>Skin Trade</i> came in sixth place, and made $98,559 from 42 screenings ($3,861 per theater). By the end of the second week, it dropped two places at the box office, making a further $32,917 from 39 screenings (bringing the entire gross to $162,163 in Malaysia).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Malaysia_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-Malaysia-55">[54]</a></sup> <i>Skin Trade</i> grossed a total of $382,784 at the foreign box office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Limitedgross_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-Limitedgross-56">[55]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film debuted in the United States on May 8, 2015, making $162 (from one theater showing) during its opening weekend. It remained in the one theater for a second week, grossing a further $510. By the end of its third and final week of release in the US, <i>Skin Trade</i> grossed a total of $1,242 at the domestic box office,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Domestic_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-Domestic-57">[56]</a></sup> bringing the film's entire theatrical gross to $384,026.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mojo_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-4">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p><i>Skin Trade</i> was released through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Video-on-Demand" title="Video-on-Demand">Video-on-Demand</a> on April 23, 2015,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SkinTradeVODRelease_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-SkinTradeVODRelease-58">[57]</a></sup> On August 25, Magnet Releasing distributed the film on Blu-ray and DVD.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SkinTradeRelease1_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-SkinTradeRelease1-59">[58]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SkinTradeRelease3_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-SkinTradeRelease3-60">[59]</a></sup> In the United States, the film was given an R rating by the <a href="/wiki/Motion_Picture_Association_of_America" title="Motion Picture Association of America">Motion Picture Association of America</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SkinTradeRelease3_60-1"><a href="#cite_note-SkinTradeRelease3-60">[59]</a></sup> while in the United Kingdom, it was issued a 15 rating by the <a href="/wiki/British_Board_of_Film_Classification" title="British Board of Film Classification">British Board of Film Classification</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBFCRating_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBFCRating-61">[60]</a></sup> As of January 1, 2016, <i>Skin Trade</i> has grossed $959 thousand in domestic home video sales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-numbers_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-numbers-3">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Potential_sequel">Potential sequel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skin_Trade_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Potential sequel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Teles said she would be open to returning for a sequel;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PSeqTeles_66-0"><a href="#cite_note-PSeqTeles-66">[65]</a></sup> she feels the film was "nicely set up" for one, and claims "[the cast and crew are] wondering" if there will be a follow-up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TelesSequel_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-TelesSequel-67">[66]</a></sup> On the possibility of a sequel, Lundgren said: "I didn't consider [<i>Skin Trade</i>] as a franchise, but when I was over there [in Thailand], I started thinking, 'How would I do this different? How would I stay close to the subject matter?' Organ trafficking is quite big as well, so I thought that could be interesting. We could follow up with some kind of other trade".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BCairnsInterview_68-0"><a href="#cite_note-BCairnsInterview-68">[67]</a></sup></p>
