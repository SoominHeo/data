<p><b>Star Destroyers</b> are <a href="/wiki/Capital_ship" title="Capital ship">capital ships</a> in the fictional <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars" title="Star Wars">Star Wars</a></i> universe. The Imperial Star Destroyer, which first appears in the first seconds of <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_(film)" title="Star Wars (film)">Star Wars</a></i> (1977), is "the signature vessel of the Imperial fleet".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW.comISD_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-SW.comISD-1">[1]</a></sup> The term "Star Destroyer" also refers to other vessels in the franchise.</p>
<p>Numerous Star Destroyer models and toys have been released, and iconic scene featuring the vessel's first appearance has been called "a milestone in special effects history".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concept_and_design">Concept and design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Destroyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Concept and design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In draft scripts for the film that would become <i>Star Wars</i>, the term "Stardestroyer" refers to two-man fighters flown by what would become the <a href="/wiki/Galactic_Empire_(Star_Wars)" title="Galactic Empire (Star Wars)">Galactic Empire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW.comISD_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-SW.comISD-1">[1]</a></sup> The film's second draft features four Star Destroyers chasing a single Rebel ship,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taylor-115_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-taylor-115-2">[2]</a></sup> but the tremendous costs incurred by <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Light_%26_Magic" title="Industrial Light &amp; Magic">Industrial Light &amp; Magic</a> (ILM) when production began helped lead Lucas to use a single "terrifyingly large" Star Destroyer instead of four.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taylor-121-22_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-taylor-121-22-3">[3]</a></sup> ILM built a 91-centimeter (36 in) shooting model that was about half the size of the model for the <i>Tantive IV</i> the Star Destroyer was chasing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW.comISD_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-SW.comISD-1">[1]</a></sup> Lucas asked ILM to build a larger Star Destroyer model to match the <i>Tantive IV</i>'s scale, but ILM convinced him that the <a href="/wiki/Dykstraflex" title="Dykstraflex">Dykstraflex</a> camera invented for the film made this unnecessary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taylor-171-73_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-taylor-171-73-4">[4]</a></sup> Nevertheless, they added additional hull details to the Star Destroyer model.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taylor-171-73_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-taylor-171-73-4">[4]</a></sup> The 13-second opening shot was the first special effects piece ILM completed,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taylor-171-73_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-taylor-171-73-4">[4]</a></sup> and its success was an essential test for the Dykstraflex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taylor-171-73_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-taylor-171-73-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>ILM built a 259-centimeter (102 in) Star Destroyer, equipped with internal lighting to provide a better sense of scale, for <i>The Empire Strikes Back</i> (1980).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW.comISD_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-SW.comISD-1">[1]</a></sup> During production, Lucas decided that every <i>Star Wars</i> film would open in space with a shot of a Star Destroyer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taylor-247-48_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-taylor-247-48-5">[5]</a></sup> <i>The Empire Strikes Back</i> also introduces the "Super Star Destroyer", also referred to as a "Star Dreadnought".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The shooting model for the Super Star Destroyer <i>Executor</i> in <i>The Empire Strikes Back</i> and <i>Return of the Jedi</i> was 282 centimeters (111 in) long.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW.comSD_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-SW.comSD-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The design of the <i>Venator</i>-class Star Destroyers appearing in <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_%E2%80%93_Revenge_of_the_Sith" title="Star Wars: Episode III – Revenge of the Sith">Revenge of the Sith</a></i> (2005) are meant to bridge the appearance of the <i>Acclamator</i>-class transports in <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_II_%E2%80%93_Attack_of_the_Clones" title="Star Wars: Episode II – Attack of the Clones">Attack of the Clones</a></i> (2002) and the <i>Imperial</i> class in the original trilogy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-venator_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-venator-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Depiction">Depiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Destroyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Depiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Films_and_television">Films and television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Destroyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Films and television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The iconic Star Destroyer first appears in the opening scene of <i>Star Wars</i>, as the <a href="/wiki/Darth_Vader" title="Darth Vader">Darth Vader</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Flagship" title="Flagship">flagship</a> <i>Devastator</i> chasing the <i><a href="/wiki/Tantive_IV" title="Tantive IV">Tantive IV</a></i>. This appearance shows the Imperial ship's massive size in comparison to the <i>Tantive IV</i>. <i>Imperial</i>-class Star Destroyers appear not only throughout the original trilogy, but also in <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Rebels" title="Star Wars Rebels">Star Wars Rebels</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Rogue_One" title="Rogue One">Rogue One</a></i> (2016). <a href="/wiki/Rey_(Star_Wars)" title="Rey (Star Wars)">Rey</a> scavenges material from a crashed <i>Imperial</i>-class Star Destroyer in <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Force_Awakens" title="Star Wars: The Force Awakens">Star Wars: The Force Awakens</a></i> (2015).</p>
<p>The original trilogy also feature a massive Super Star Destroyer that serves as <a href="/wiki/Darth_Vader" title="Darth Vader">Darth Vader</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Flagship" title="Flagship">flagship</a> in <i>The Empire Strikes Back,</i> and it is the Imperial command ship at the climax of <i>Return of the Jedi</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW.comSD_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-SW.comSD-7">[7]</a></sup> Rey pilots the <i><a href="/wiki/Millennium_Falcon" title="Millennium Falcon">Millennium Falcon</a></i> through the wreckage of the crashed Super Star Destroyer <i>Ravager</i> during a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chase_scene" title="Chase scene">chase scene</a> in <i>The Force Awakens</i>.</p>
<p>Other Star Destroyer varieties appear in the <i>Star Wars</i> prequel and sequel films. <i>Star Wars</i> creator <a href="/wiki/George_Lucas" title="George Lucas">George Lucas</a> calls the cigar-shaped <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trade_Federation_Cruiser" title="Trade Federation Cruiser">Separatist cruisers</a> visible at the beginning of <i>Revenge of the Sith</i> "Star Destroyers" in the movie's DVD commentary track.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <i>Venator</i>-class Star Destroyers are introduced in <i>Revenge of the Sith</i> and later appear throughout <a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars_(2008_TV_series)" title="Star Wars: The Clone Wars (2008 TV series)"><i>Star Wars: The Clone Wars</i></a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-venator_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-venator-8">[8]</a></sup> The <i>Venator</i>-class ships initially appear with a red and grey Republic color scheme in <i>Revenge of the Sith</i>; the <i>Venator</i> at the film's end is gray and white, signifying the Empire's rise to power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-venator_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-venator-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>The Force Awakens</i> introduces the <i>Resurgent</i>-class of Star Destroyers belonging to the First Order, one of which is commanded by <a href="/wiki/Kylo_Ren" title="Kylo Ren">Kylo Ren</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Expanded_Universe">Expanded Universe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Destroyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Expanded Universe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Expanded_Universe" title="Star Wars Expanded Universe">Star Wars Expanded Universe</a> materials offer details about the Star Destroyers shown in <i>Star Wars</i> film and television, and they also introduce other varieties. The Empire had a dozen Super Star Destroyers in service during the time of the Battle of Endor,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> including Vader's <i>Executor</i> command ship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The final surviving Super Star Destroyer, the <i>Ravager</i>, is the vessel whose wreckage appears in <i>The Force Awakens</i>. The <i>Ravager</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s crash onto Jakku is depicted in the game <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Battlefront_(2015_video_game)" title="Star Wars Battlefront (2015 video game)">Star Wars Battlefront</a></i> (2015).</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Star_Wars_Legends"><i>Star Wars Legends</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Destroyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Star Wars Legends">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In April 2014, most of the licensed <i>Star Wars</i> novels, games, and comics produced since 1977 were rebranded by <a href="/wiki/Lucasfilm" title="Lucasfilm">Lucasfilm</a> as Star Wars Legends and declared non-canon to the franchise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THR_Legends_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-THR_Legends-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW_Legends_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-SW_Legends-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to <i>Star Wars</i> roleplaying books and other texts, <i>Imperial</i>-class Star Destroyers are constructed by Kuat Drive Yards and hold a distinguished place in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_Navy_(Star_Wars)" title="Imperial Navy (Star Wars)">Imperial Navy</a>, symbolizing the Empire's military might with a peak number of more than 25,000 vessels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ImpSB_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-ImpSB-14">[14]</a></sup> Like the <i>Victory</i>- and <i>Venator</i>-class ships that precede it, this Star Destroyer is notable for its massive size and overwhelming firepower; a single <i>Imperial</i>-class ship is capable of overwhelming most starships or devastating a hostile planet, and its mere presence is often enough to deter rebellion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ImpSB_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-ImpSB-14">[14]</a></sup> At 1,600 meters (5,200 ft) long, <i>Imperial</i>-class Star Destroyers are armed with turbolasers, ion cannons and tractor beam projectors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ImpSB_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-ImpSB-14">[14]</a></sup> They carry 72 <a href="/wiki/TIE_fighter" title="TIE fighter">TIE fighters</a>, numerous ground forces (including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_stormtroopers" title="Imperial stormtroopers">stormtroopers</a>, 20 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AT-AT" title="AT-AT">AT-ATs</a> and 30 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AT-ST" title="AT-ST">AT-STs</a>), a prefabricated base for rapid deployment to planetary surfaces, and a variety of support craft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ImpSB_14-3"><a href="#cite_note-ImpSB-14">[14]</a></sup> After the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Endor" title="Battle of Endor">Battle of Endor</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Rebel_Alliance" title="Rebel Alliance">Rebel Alliance</a> captured several <i>Imperial</i>-class ships and added them to their own fleet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DESB_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-DESB-15">[15]</a></sup> Although the New Republic eventually upgrades its starfleet with newer ship types, the <i>Imperial</i>-class Star Destroyer remains in service well into the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Jedi_Order" title="New Jedi Order">New Jedi Order</a></i> era and fights during the <a href="/wiki/Yuuzhan_Vong" title="Yuuzhan Vong">Yuuzhan Vong</a> War.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NJOSB_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-NJOSB-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kevin_J._Anderson" title="Kevin J. Anderson">Kevin J. Anderson</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/Darksaber" title="Darksaber">Darksaber</a></i> describes a Super Star Destroyer as being "worth twenty Imperial Star Destroyers".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Darksaber155_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Darksaber155-17">[17]</a></sup> Described in <i>A Guide to the Star Wars Universe</i> (1984) as being 8 kilometers (5.0 mi) long, <i>Executor</i>-class Super Star Destroyers were later described as being 19 kilometers (12 mi) long.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In addition to Vader's command ship, <i>Star Wars</i> novels introduce the prison ship <i>Lusankya</i> and stealth-armored <i>Night Hammer</i> as ships in the class.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Isard_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Isard-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Darksaber155_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-Darksaber155-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>The labels "Star Destroyer" and "Super Star Destroyer" are applied to several other massive <i>Star Wars</i> warships, such as the <i>Pellaeon</i>-class in the <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_Legacy" title="Star Wars: Legacy">Legacy</a></i> comic series (2006–2010), the reborn Emperor Palpatine's flagships <i>Eclipse</i> and <i>Eclipse II</i> in the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Empire" title="Dark Empire">Dark Empire</a> series (1991–1995), and the <i>Victory</i> class first described in the early <i>Star Wars</i> novella <i><a href="/wiki/The_Han_Solo_Adventures" title="The Han Solo Adventures">Han Solo's Revenge</a></i> (1979).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Merchandise">Merchandise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Destroyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Merchandise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lego" title="Lego">Lego</a> has released numerous Star Destroyer kits, including a 1-metre (3 ft 3 in) 3,104-piece <i>Imperial</i>-class Star Destroyer<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-legoultimate_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-legoultimate-22">[22]</a></sup> and a 50-inch (130 cm) 3,152-piece model of the <i>Executor</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Super Star Destroyer has also been merchandised. <a href="/wiki/Kenner_Products" title="Kenner Products">Kenner</a> wanted to use a less ominous name than <i>Executor</i> for the toy playset of Darth Vader's meditation chamber.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW.comSD_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-SW.comSD-7">[7]</a></sup> An advertisement agency's list of 153 alternatives included Starbase Malevolent, Black Coven, Haphaestus VII, and Cosmocurse;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW.comSD_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-SW.comSD-7">[7]</a></sup> ultimately, the toy was labeled "Darth Vader's Star Destroyer".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW.comSD_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-SW.comSD-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2006, Wizards of the Coast created an <i>Executor</i> miniature as part of its Star Wars Miniatures Starship Battles game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wizards_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-wizards-24">[24]</a></sup> An electronic Super Star Destroyer toy released by <a href="/wiki/Hasbro" title="Hasbro">Hasbro</a> "is the rarest among Hasbro's Collector Fleet".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
