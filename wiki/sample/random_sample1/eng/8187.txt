<p><i><b>Star Trek: Discovery</b></i> is an upcoming American television series created by <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Fuller" title="Bryan Fuller">Bryan Fuller</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alex_Kurtzman" title="Alex Kurtzman">Alex Kurtzman</a> for <a href="/wiki/CBS_All_Access" title="CBS All Access">CBS All Access</a>. It is the first series developed specifically for that service, and the first <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek" title="Star Trek">Star Trek</a></i> series since <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Enterprise" title="Star Trek: Enterprise">Star Trek: Enterprise</a></i> concluded in 2005. Set roughly a decade before the events of <a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_The_Original_Series" title="Star Trek: The Original Series">the original <i>Star Trek</i> series</a>, separate from the timeline of the <a href="/wiki/Star_Trek_(film_series)#2009_.22reboot.22" title="Star Trek (film series)">concurrent feature films</a>, <i>Discovery</i> explores a previously mentioned event from the history of <i>Star Trek</i> while following the crew of the USS <i>Discovery</i>. Gretchen J. Berg and Aaron Harberts serve as <a href="/wiki/Showrunner" title="Showrunner">showrunners</a> on the series, with producing support from <a href="/wiki/Akiva_Goldsman" title="Akiva Goldsman">Akiva Goldsman</a>.</p>
<p>The new series was announced in November 2015, and Fuller joined as showrunner the next February. In addition to Fuller and Kurtzman, who wrote for previous <i>Star Trek</i> series and films, respectively, the crew includes several other previous <i>Star Trek</i> creatives. The production put emphasis on carrying on the legacy of the previous series, including making efforts to feature a diverse cast. In October 2016, Fuller stepped back from showrunner due to other commitments, after establishing the series' mythology and broad story arc. Berg and Harberts took over day-to-day production, and Goldsman joined as support. <i>Discovery</i> stars <a href="/wiki/Sonequa_Martin-Green" title="Sonequa Martin-Green">Sonequa Martin-Green</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doug_Jones_(actor)" title="Doug Jones (actor)">Doug Jones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Rapp" title="Anthony Rapp">Anthony Rapp</a>, and <a href="/wiki/James_Frain" title="James Frain">James Frain</a>, and is filming in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>.</p>
<p><i>Star Trek: Discovery</i> is set to debut on <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> before moving to All Access. The first season will consist of 13 episodes.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Premise">Premise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Trek:_Discovery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Premise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Set roughly a decade before the events of <a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_The_Original_Series" title="Star Trek: The Original Series">the original <i>Star Trek</i> series</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerAug2016_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FullerAug2016-3">[3]</a></sup> the series follows the crew of the USS <i>Discovery</i> as they discover new worlds and civilizations, while exploring the franchise's signature contemporary themes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Announcement_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Announcement-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SDCC2016_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-SDCC2016-5">[5]</a></sup> The season-long storyline revolves around "an incident and an event in <i>Star Trek</i> history that's been talked about but never been explored".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerAug2016_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-FullerAug2016-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cast_and_characters">Cast and characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Trek:_Discovery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cast and characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main">Main</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Trek:_Discovery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Main">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sonequa_Martin-Green" title="Sonequa Martin-Green">Sonequa Martin-Green</a> as Rainsford: The lieutenant commander of the USS <i>Discovery</i>, referred to as "Number One".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Martin-Green_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Martin-Green-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerRadioAug2016_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-FullerRadioAug2016-7">[7]</a></sup> The decision to not make the series' protagonist a starship captain, like those of previous <i>Star Trek</i> series, was made "to see a character from a different perspective on the starship—one who has different dynamic relationships with a captain, with subordinates, it gave us richer context".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerAug2016_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-FullerAug2016-3">[3]</a></sup> The decision to call her Number One was made in honor of <a href="/wiki/Number_One_(Star_Trek)" title="Number One (Star Trek)">the character of the same name</a> portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Majel_Barrett" title="Majel Barrett">Majel Barrett</a> in the original <i>Star Trek</i> pilot "<a href="/wiki/The_Cage_(Star_Trek:_The_Original_Series)" title="The Cage (Star Trek: The Original Series)">The Cage</a>". The character was initially pitched to CBS as only being called Number One in the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerRadioAug2016_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-FullerRadioAug2016-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Doug_Jones_(actor)" title="Doug Jones (actor)">Doug Jones</a> as Saru: A Science Officer serving as a lieutenant aboard the <i>Discovery</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JonesRapp_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-JonesRapp-8">[8]</a></sup> Jones, portraying a member of a species created for the series, was excited that he was able to, "from the ground up, develop and find this character and his species" and not have to honor a previous fan-favorite representation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JonesJan2017_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-JonesJan2017-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Anthony_Rapp" title="Anthony Rapp">Anthony Rapp</a> as Stamets: A Science Officer specializing in astromycology (the study of fungi in space).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JonesRapp_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-JonesRapp-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/James_Frain" title="James Frain">James Frain</a> as <a href="/wiki/Sarek" title="Sarek">Sarek</a>: A <a href="/wiki/Vulcan_(Star_Trek)" title="Vulcan (Star Trek)">Vulcan</a> astrophysicist, and the father of <a href="/wiki/Spock" title="Spock">Spock</a>. Frain appears as a younger version of the character who was first portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Lenard" title="Mark Lenard">Mark Lenard</a> in the original <i>Star Trek</i> series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frain_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Frain-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FrainStar_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-FrainStar-11">[11]</a></sup></li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Trek:_Discovery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Trek:_Discovery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 2, 2015, <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> announced a new <i>Star Trek</i> television series to premiere in January 2017, "on the heels" of the <a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_The_Original_Series" title="Star Trek: The Original Series">original series</a>' 50th anniversary in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Announcement_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Announcement-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VultureEnterprise_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-VultureEnterprise-17">[17]</a></sup> It is the first <i>Star Trek</i> television series since <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Enterprise" title="Star Trek: Enterprise">Star Trek: Enterprise</a></i> concluded in 2005,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VultureEnterprise_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-VultureEnterprise-17">[17]</a></sup> and is "not related" to the 2016 film <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek_Beyond" title="Star Trek Beyond">Star Trek Beyond</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Announcement_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Announcement-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Alex_Kurtzman" title="Alex Kurtzman">Alex Kurtzman</a>, co-writer of the films <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek_(film)" title="Star Trek (film)">Star Trek</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek_Into_Darkness" title="Star Trek Into Darkness">Star Trek Into Darkness</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Heather_Kadin" title="Heather Kadin">Heather Kadin</a> were set as executive producers on the series, the first to be developed specifically for the <a href="/wiki/CBS_All_Access" title="CBS All Access">CBS All Access</a> on demand service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Announcement_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Announcement-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bryan_Fuller_at_2016_Comic-Con_International.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3840" data-file-width="3344" height="195" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Bryan_Fuller_at_2016_Comic-Con_International.jpg/170px-Bryan_Fuller_at_2016_Comic-Con_International.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Bryan_Fuller_at_2016_Comic-Con_International.jpg/255px-Bryan_Fuller_at_2016_Comic-Con_International.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Bryan_Fuller_at_2016_Comic-Con_International.jpg/340px-Bryan_Fuller_at_2016_Comic-Con_International.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bryan_Fuller_at_2016_Comic-Con_International.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Co-creator Bryan Fuller did initial work as showrunner on <i>Star Trek: Discovery</i>, establishing the story and mythology.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In January 2016, CBS president Glenn Geller revealed that he and the CBS network were not involved in the production of the series, saying, "It really is for All Access. While the network will be broadcasting the pilot, I actually can't answer any creative questions about it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GellerJan2016_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-GellerJan2016-18">[18]</a></sup> The next month, <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Fuller" title="Bryan Fuller">Bryan Fuller</a>, who began his career writing for the series <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Deep_Space_Nine" title="Star Trek: Deep Space Nine">Star Trek: Deep Space Nine</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Voyager" title="Star Trek: Voyager">Star Trek: Voyager</a></i>, was announced as the new series' <a href="/wiki/Showrunner" title="Showrunner">showrunner</a> and co-creator alongside Kurtzman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fuller_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fuller-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-International_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-International-20">[20]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Meyer" title="Nicholas Meyer">Nicholas Meyer</a>, writer and director of <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek_II:_The_Wrath_of_Khan" title="Star Trek II: The Wrath of Khan">Star Trek II: The Wrath of Khan</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek_VI:_The_Undiscovered_Country" title="Star Trek VI: The Undiscovered Country">Star Trek VI: The Undiscovered Country</a></i>, also joined the series as a writer and consulting producer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meyer_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Meyer-21">[21]</a></sup> In March, <a href="/wiki/Rod_Roddenberry" title="Rod Roddenberry">Rod Roddenberry</a>, the son of <i>Star Trek</i> creator <a href="/wiki/Gene_Roddenberry" title="Gene Roddenberry">Gene Roddenberry</a>, and Trevor Roth of Roddenberry Entertainment also joined the series, as executive producers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Roddenberry_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Roddenberry-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>At CBS's upfront presentation in May 2016, a teaser for the series was released promising "new crews, new villains, new heroes, new worlds", simply titling the series <i>Star Trek</i>, with "a more specific title to be unveiled at a later date".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TeaserLogo_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-TeaserLogo-23">[23]</a></sup> The next month, Fuller announced that the first season would consist of 13 episodes,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerColliderJune2016_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-FullerColliderJune2016-24">[24]</a></sup> though he would prefer to produce 10 episodes of the series per year moving forward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerRadioAug2016_7-5"><a href="#cite_note-FullerRadioAug2016-7">[7]</a></sup> Fuller revealed that <a href="/wiki/Vincenzo_Natali" title="Vincenzo Natali">Vincenzo Natali</a> had been hired as producing director for the series, and described each episode's runtime as "flexible", with All Access having given the team parameters ("It was sort of, 'No more than this, no less than that'") rather than a set length to aim for like they would for a traditionally broadcast series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerColliderJune2016_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-FullerColliderJune2016-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July, at <i>Star Trek</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s 50th anniversary <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Comic-Con" title="San Diego Comic-Con">San Diego Comic-Con</a> panel, Fuller announced the series' title to be <i>Star Trek: Discovery</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Title_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Title-25">[25]</a></sup> and revealed that it would be set in the "Prime Timeline" (which includes the previous <i>Star Trek</i> series, but not the <a href="/wiki/Star_Trek_(film_series)#2009_.22reboot.22" title="Star Trek (film series)">reboot films</a> of the "Kelvin Timeline").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prime_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Prime-26">[26]</a></sup> Fuller explained that <i>Discovery</i> was developed to fit into either timeline, but he felt there was a "cleanliness" to keeping the concurrent series and films separated, so "we don't have to track anything [happening in the films] and they don't have to track what we're doing".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerRadioAug2016_7-6"><a href="#cite_note-FullerRadioAug2016-7">[7]</a></sup> Also in July, <a href="/wiki/CBS_Studios_International" title="CBS Studios International">CBS Studios International</a> licensed the series to <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> for release around the world,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-International_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-International-20">[20]</a></sup> a "blockbuster" deal that paid for the show's entire budget.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NetflixBudget_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-NetflixBudget-27">[27]</a></sup> At around $6–7 million per episode, the series' high allowance is attributed to the importance of <i>Star Trek</i> as one of CBS's "crown-jewel franchises", and CBS's need for the series to be "the marquee selling point for subscriptions" to All Access.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChangeOct2016_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-ChangeOct2016-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>By August 2016, Fuller had hired Gretchen J. Berg and Aaron Harberts, who he had worked with on <i><a href="/wiki/Pushing_Daisies" title="Pushing Daisies">Pushing Daisies</a></i>, to serve as co-showrunners with him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerRadioAug2016_7-7"><a href="#cite_note-FullerRadioAug2016-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChangeOct2016_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-ChangeOct2016-2">[2]</a></sup> A month later, CBS announced that the series premiere had been pushed back to May 2017. This happened after Fuller and Kurtzman had asked for more time to meet the high expectations for the series, "particularly with special effects", saying that "these extra few months will help us achieve a vision we can all be proud of."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Delay_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Delay-1">[1]</a></sup> By the end of October, production company <a href="/wiki/CBS_Television_Studios" title="CBS Television Studios">CBS Television Studios</a> had become concerned with the continued lack of progress on the series' production thanks to Fuller's other commitments showrunning <i><a href="/wiki/American_Gods_(TV_series)" title="American Gods (TV series)">American Gods</a></i> and rebooting <i><a href="/wiki/Amazing_Stories_(TV_series)" title="Amazing Stories (TV series)">Amazing Stories</a></i>. The company was pleased with the work Fuller had done so far, but was unwilling to postpone the premiere date again. To keep Fuller actively involved with the series, though not on a day-to-day production level as was originally intended, the production was "quickly" restructured: Berg and Harberts were made sole showrunners, working off of a broad story arc and overall mythology established by Fuller; Kurtzman and Fuller would continue as executive producers, with Fuller still helping the writers break stories; and <a href="/wiki/Akiva_Goldsman" title="Akiva Goldsman">Akiva Goldsman</a> would join the series in a supporting producer role, similar to the role he held on <i><a href="/wiki/Fringe_(TV_series)" title="Fringe (TV series)">Fringe</a></i> alongside Kurtzman, to help the showrunners and other producers "juggle the demands of the series". In a statement, CBS reiterated that they were "extremely happy with [Fuller's] creative direction" for the series, and were committed to "seeing this vision through".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChangeOct2016_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-ChangeOct2016-2">[2]</a></sup> In December, Fuller described the situation as "bittersweet ... I can only give them the material I've given them and hope that it is helpful for them. I'm curious to see what they do with it." He expressed interest in returning to work on future seasons of the series if asked to.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerDec2016_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-FullerDec2016-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2017, CBS stated that, with production beginning that month, they were willing to be flexible with the series' release date so that it would be the best possible date for the series. This feeling was attributed to "a lot of careful deliberation continuing to go into making <i>Discovery</i> special, from the choice of directors, to set design, to the special effects." Another potential consideration was the recent casting of <a href="/wiki/Sonequa_Martin-Green" title="Sonequa Martin-Green">Sonequa Martin-Green</a> as the series' lead, with CBS looking to avoid "marketplace confusion" by not "promoting her as the star of a new sci-fi show" while she appears in the popular horror series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Walking_Dead_(TV_series)" title="The Walking Dead (TV series)">The Walking Dead</a></i>, which was still airing through April 2017. It was believed that another release change would not affect <i>Discovery</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s viewership given the All Access format avoids the "seasonal premieres or launch windows" to which traditional broadcast series are beholden.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frain_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Frain-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing">Writing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Trek:_Discovery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Writing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fuller explained that after the more than 700 <i>Star Trek</i> episodes already made, "we have to tell stories differently than they've been told for fifty years". Therefore, he looked to take advantage of the streaming format of All Access by telling a single story arc across the entire first season, which he and the writers had completely written by the end of June 2016. They had also broken the first six episodes at that point. Fuller stated that when he first met with CBS, the network did not have a plan for the series, and so Fuller and Kurtzman developed a story for the series "that ties in so many elements of <i>Star Trek</i>", taking certain episodes of the original series and using their "DNA" to find "the spirit of what <i>Star Trek</i> offers, both in terms of high-concept science fiction storytelling and really wonderful metaphors for the human condition".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerColliderJune2016_24-2"><a href="#cite_note-FullerColliderJune2016-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>The titular ship was named after <i><a href="/wiki/Discovery_One" title="Discovery One">Discovery One</a></i> from <i><a href="/wiki/2001:_A_Space_Odyssey_(film)" title="2001: A Space Odyssey (film)">2001: A Space Odyssey</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Discovery" title="Space Shuttle Discovery">Space Shuttle <i>Discovery</i></a>, and "the sense of discovery ... what [that] means to <i>Star Trek</i> audiences who have been promised a future by Gene Roddenberry where we come together as a planet and seek new worlds and new alien races to explore and understand and collaborate with".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-io9Discovery_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-io9Discovery-29">[29]</a></sup> Fuller saw the series as a bridge between <i>Enterprise</i> and the original series—which are set around 150 years apart—but set much closer to the latter to allow the series to "play with all the iconography of those ships and those uniforms".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerAug2016.2F_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-FullerAug2016.2F-30">[30]</a></sup> The story arc for the first season revolves around "an event in <i>Star Trek</i> history that's been talked about but never been explored", 10 years before the events of the original series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerAug2016_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-FullerAug2016-3">[3]</a></sup> Fuller elaborated that the original series episode "<a href="/wiki/Balance_of_Terror" title="Balance of Terror">Balance of Terror</a>", one of his favorites, would be "a touchstone" for the season's story arc.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerSep2016_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-FullerSep2016-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Fuller discussed how much the series would "push the content envelope since it won't be constrained by broadcast standards", saying, "We're going to have a broader spectrum to explore those issues, but it's still <i>Star Trek</i>. It will probably be slightly more graphic content. We discuss language every day. Is it appropriate for somebody to see a bridge blow up and say 'Oh shit.' I imagine we're going to shoot scenes a couple of ways and see what feels more authentic in the editing room."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerAug2016_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-FullerAug2016-3">[3]</a></sup> On using <a href="/wiki/Time_travel" title="Time travel">time travel</a> in the season, a plot device used in at least two episodes of every previous live-action <i>Star Trek</i> season, Fuller said that it had not yet been used for any episode by the end of August 2016—the first three episodes had been written, and the stories through episode eleven were known—and stated, "You never know when you want to pull out that device but I am not anticipating an over-reliance on time travel to tell this season's stories."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerRadioAug2016_7-8"><a href="#cite_note-FullerRadioAug2016-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casting">Casting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Trek:_Discovery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Casting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By June 2016, Fuller had "met with a few actors, and it's an interesting process. There's a few people that we like and we want to carry on what <i>Star Trek</i> does best, which is being progressive. So it's fascinating to look at all of these roles through a colorblind prism and a gender-blind prism".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerColliderJune2016_24-3"><a href="#cite_note-FullerColliderJune2016-24">[24]</a></sup> A month later, Kaden clarified that the series would feature minority, female, and LGBTQ characters, with the latter being of particular importance to Fuller.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SDCC2016_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-SDCC2016-5">[5]</a></sup> In August, Fuller revealed that the series would star a female lieutenant commander, rather than a starship captain like previous <i>Star Trek</i> series, to be played by a non-white actress;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerAug2016_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-FullerAug2016-3">[3]</a></sup> he stated that the series cast would include more alien characters than other <i>Star Trek</i> series, "to paint a picture of <a href="/wiki/Starfleet" title="Starfleet">Starfleet</a> that is indicative of a universe where we’re encountering people much different than we are"; and he confirmed that the series would feature at least one openly gay character, explaining that Kurtzman had been the first to propose the series include a gay character, but that Fuller, who is gay himself, had been determined to create a gay <i>Star Trek</i> character since receiving hate mail while working on <i>Voyager</i> when a character on that show was rumored to be coming out as gay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerAug2016.2F_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-FullerAug2016.2F-30">[30]</a></sup> At this time, Fuller had discussed the casting of the series with <a href="/wiki/Mae_Jemison" title="Mae Jemison">Mae Jemison</a>, the first black woman in space.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerAug2016_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-FullerAug2016-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sonequa_Martin-Green_2016.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2336" data-file-width="1800" height="221" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Sonequa_Martin-Green_2016.jpg/170px-Sonequa_Martin-Green_2016.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Sonequa_Martin-Green_2016.jpg/255px-Sonequa_Martin-Green_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Sonequa_Martin-Green_2016.jpg/340px-Sonequa_Martin-Green_2016.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sonequa_Martin-Green_2016.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Sonequa Martin-Green portrays the series' protagonist, Lt Cdr. Rainsford.</div>

<p>Fuller had anticipated casting announcements in October 2016, saying, "We've met with fantastic actors and of course there are people I've worked with before that I'd love to see on <i>Star Trek</i>. We're trying to figure out everybody's schedules."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerRadioAug2016_7-9"><a href="#cite_note-FullerRadioAug2016-7">[7]</a></sup> However, no announcements had been made by the end of that month. The majority of the series main characters were believed to have been cast by then, but no actress had been cast for the series' lead role. This was a source of "some internal stress" at CBS, with the casting of the character deemed "a far tougher assignment" than expected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChangeOct2016_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-ChangeOct2016-2">[2]</a></sup> Several African American and Latina actresses were being looked at for the part. CBS was "not seeking a huge star and would prefer a fresh face for the part."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YeohVariety_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-YeohVariety-32">[32]</a></sup> In December, Martin-Green was cast as lieutenant commander Rainsford. The lead role of the series, Rainsford is the first female, African American <i>Star Trek</i> lead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Martin-Green_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Martin-Green-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October, the series cast was believed to also include "a female admiral, a male <a href="/wiki/Klingon" title="Klingon">Klingon</a> captain, a male admiral, a male adviser and a British male doctor", with one of those male leads played by an openly gay actor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReportedCharacters_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReportedCharacters-33">[33]</a></sup> The next month, <a href="/wiki/Doug_Jones_(actor)" title="Doug Jones (actor)">Doug Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Rapp" title="Anthony Rapp">Anthony Rapp</a> were revealed to be cast as Science Officers Saru and Stamets, respectively. The former is a member of an alien race created for the series, while the latter is the first <i>Star Trek</i> character to be conceived and announced as gay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JonesRapp_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-JonesRapp-8">[8]</a></sup> Also in November, Meyer mentioned that <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Yeoh" title="Michelle Yeoh">Michelle Yeoh</a> had been cast in the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yeoh_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yeoh-34">[34]</a></sup> She was soon confirmed to be cast as Starfleet Captain Georgiou,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YeohDeadline_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-YeohDeadline-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JonesRapp_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-JonesRapp-8">[8]</a></sup> a recurring character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YeohVariety_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-YeohVariety-32">[32]</a></sup> Three actors were cast as Klingons in December: <a href="/wiki/Shazad_Latif" title="Shazad Latif">Shazad Latif</a> as Kol;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Latif_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-Latif-13">[13]</a></sup> Chris Obi as T'Kuvma;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ObiChieffo_14-3"><a href="#cite_note-ObiChieffo-14">[14]</a></sup> and Mary Chieffo as L'Rell.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ObiChieffo_14-4"><a href="#cite_note-ObiChieffo-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On including previously established characters from the original series, Fuller said in August 2016 that "once we get through this first season and establish our own <i>Star Trek</i> universe" and crew of characters, the series will look in a "second season to open up to more familiar characters and how they can feed into the [show]. First and foremost, I think we really want to convince you and establish the greatness of the [new] characters that are going to be introduced". However, Fuller did express interest in including the character of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amanda_Grayson" title="Amanda Grayson">Amanda Grayson</a>, the mother of <a href="/wiki/Spock" title="Spock">Spock</a>, saying, "there's much to be told about that".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerAug2016_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-FullerAug2016-3">[3]</a></sup> By January 2017, <a href="/wiki/James_Frain" title="James Frain">James Frain</a> had joined the series as Spock's father, <a href="/wiki/Sarek" title="Sarek">Sarek</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frain_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-Frain-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Trek:_Discovery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fuller said on the general approach to design on the show, "we're producing the show in 2016. We have to update the style of the effects, the style of the sets, the style of the makeup ... all of the other series have been produced [at a time that] isn't as sophisticated as we are now with what we can do production-wise, we're going to be reestablishing an entire look for the series" and for <i>Star Trek</i> moving forward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerAug2016_3-8"><a href="#cite_note-FullerAug2016-3">[3]</a></sup> Mark Worthington serves as production designer for the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FilmingDate_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-FilmingDate-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The design of the USS <i>Discovery</i> is based on an unused <a href="/wiki/Ralph_McQuarrie" title="Ralph McQuarrie">Ralph McQuarrie</a> design for the <a href="/wiki/Starship_Enterprise" title="Starship Enterprise">USS <i>Enterprise</i></a> from the unproduced film <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Planet_of_the_Titans" title="Star Trek: Planet of the Titans">Star Trek: Planet of the Titans</a></i>, "to a point that we can't legally comment on it until [our legal team] figures out some things", Fuller stated in July 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prime_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-Prime-26">[26]</a></sup> McQuarrie's designs were based on the concepts created by <a href="/wiki/Ken_Adam" title="Ken Adam">Ken Adam</a> to feature a vessel with a flattened secondary hull.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEReeves-StevensReeves-Stevens199717_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEReeves-StevensReeves-Stevens199717-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMcQuarrieSticeScoleri2007124-129_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMcQuarrieSticeScoleri2007124-129-36">[36]</a></sup> Fuller wanted "something distinct about what our <i>Star Trek</i> was going to look" like, and after seeing McQuarrie's design "saw sort of harder lines of a ship and started talking about race cars and Lamborghinis in the '70s and James Bond cars and started working on the designs, taking those inspirations and coming up with something completely unique to us."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ShipDesign_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-ShipDesign-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Trek:_Discovery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The series began filming at <a href="/wiki/Pinewood_Toronto_Studios" title="Pinewood Toronto Studios">Pinewood Toronto Studios</a> on January 24, 2017, under the <a href="/wiki/Working_title" title="Working title">working title</a> <i>Green Harvest</i> (a reference to the working title <i>Blue Harvest</i> used for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Return_of_the_Jedi" title="Return of the Jedi">Return of the Jedi</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TorontoFilming_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-TorontoFilming-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WorkingTitle_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-WorkingTitle-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FilmingDate_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-FilmingDate-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Guillermo_Navarro" title="Guillermo Navarro">Guillermo Navarro</a> and Colin Hoult serve as cinematographers for the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FilmingDate_16-3"><a href="#cite_note-FilmingDate-16">[16]</a></sup> Previously, set construction was to begin within a month of June 2016 for a filming period of that September to around March 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerColliderJune2016_24-4"><a href="#cite_note-FullerColliderJune2016-24">[24]</a></sup> However, by that September, production was not expected to begin until November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Delay_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Delay-1">[1]</a></sup> The next month, after Fuller stepped down as showrunner, set construction was expected to be completed by the end of 2016, with filming to begin "shortly thereafter".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBRFilmingUpdate_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBRFilmingUpdate-40">[40]</a></sup> Filming is set to continue in Toronto until September 7.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FilmingDate_16-4"><a href="#cite_note-FilmingDate-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TorontoFilming_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-TorontoFilming-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Visual_effects">Visual effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Trek:_Discovery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Visual effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Visual effects producers were hired to begin work on the series during the initial writing period, with Fuller explaining that the series would require such things as "digital augmentation on certain alien species" and "the transporter beams"; "We're trying to cultivate distinct looks for all of those things that are unique to our version of <i>Star Trek</i> and carry through the themes we love seeing in fifty years of <i>Star Trek</i>, but doing a slightly different approach."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FullerColliderJune2016_24-5"><a href="#cite_note-FullerColliderJune2016-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Trek:_Discovery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Star Trek: Discovery</i> is set to premiere with a "special preview broadcast" on CBS, with that pilot and all subsequent first-run episodes to then be streamed weekly in the United States on CBS All Access.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StreamedWeekly_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-StreamedWeekly-41">[41]</a></sup> The premiere was set for January 2017,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Announcement_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-Announcement-4">[4]</a></sup> and then May 2017,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Delay_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Delay-1">[1]</a></sup> and has now been delayed indefinitely.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Delay2_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-Delay2-42">[42]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/CBS_Studios_International" title="CBS Studios International">CBS Studios International</a> licensed the series to <a href="/wiki/Bell_Media" title="Bell Media">Bell Media</a> for broadcast in Canada, and to <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> for another 188 countries. For Canada, the premiere will be broadcast on the <a href="/wiki/CTV_Television_Network" title="CTV Television Network">CTV</a> network on the same night as the U.S. premiere, with subsequent episodes initially aired on Bell's science fiction cable channels—<a href="/wiki/Space_(TV_channel)" title="Space (TV channel)">Space</a> in English, <a href="/wiki/Z_(TV_channel)" title="Z (TV channel)">Z</a> in French—before being streamed on <a href="/wiki/CraveTV" title="CraveTV">CraveTV</a>. For the other countries, Netflix will release each episode of the series for streaming within 24 hours of its U.S. debut. This agreement also saw Bell and Netflix acquire all previous <i>Star Trek</i> series to stream in their entirety.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-International_20-2"><a href="#cite_note-International-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Trek:_Discovery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With the announcement of the series' title in July 2016 came a promotional video giving a first look at the USS <i>Discovery</i>. The video did not feature final designs, as the producers had "three weeks to throw that together. We wanted to show fans ... The concepts of the ship are totally what we're going for and they'll be honed up until, I think, the day we deliver". Music for the video was composed by Fil Eisler.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SDCC2016_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-SDCC2016-5">[5]</a></sup> In January 2017, a <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> video presented by <a href="/wiki/Alcatel_(mobile_device_brand)" title="Alcatel (mobile device brand)">alcatel</a> was released, using <a href="/wiki/360-degree_video" title="360-degree video">360° technology</a> to showcase digital models of previous <i>Star Trek</i> ships. The video ended with the <i>Discovery</i> logo and a "coming May 2017" tag.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-360dVideo_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-360dVideo-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_media">Other media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Trek:_Discovery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Other media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In September 2016, writer Kristen Beyer announced that CBS was working with <a href="/wiki/IDW_Publishing" title="IDW Publishing">IDW</a> and <a href="/wiki/Simon_%26_Schuster" title="Simon &amp; Schuster">Simon &amp; Schuster</a> to produce more content revolving around the setting of <i>Discovery</i>, starting with at least one novel and a comic series tied to the television show. Beyer, the writer of many <a href="/wiki/List_of_Star_Trek:_Voyager_novels" title="List of Star Trek: Voyager novels"><i>Star Trek: Voyager</i> novels</a>, explained that she would work with fellow <i>Star Trek</i> novelist <a href="/wiki/David_Alan_Mack" title="David Alan Mack">David Mack</a> and <i>Star Trek</i> comic writer Mike Johnson to ensure that all three mediums "are coming from the same place". The release of the books and comics is set to coincide with the series' premiere.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Content_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-Content-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
