<p><b>Stanislav Konstantinovich Smirnov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Станисла́в Константи́нович Cмирно́в</span>; born 3 September 1970) is a <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> <a href="/wiki/Mathematician" title="Mathematician">mathematician</a> currently working at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Geneva" title="University of Geneva">University of Geneva</a>. He was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Fields_Medal" title="Fields Medal">Fields Medal</a> in 2010. His research involves <a href="/wiki/Complex_analysis" title="Complex analysis">complex analysis</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dynamical_systems" title="Dynamical systems">dynamical systems</a> and <a href="/wiki/Probability_theory" title="Probability theory">probability theory</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stanislav_Smirnov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Smirnov's Ph.D was conducted at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caltech" title="Caltech">Caltech</a> under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thesis_advisor" title="Thesis advisor">advisor</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nikolai_G._Makarov&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nikolai G. Makarov (page does not exist)">Nikolai G. Makarov</a>, a St Petersburg mathematician. In 1998 he was employed as part of the faculty at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Institute_of_Technology" title="Royal Institute of Technology">Royal Institute of Technology</a> in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>, after which he took up his second position as a professor in the Analysis, Mathematical Physics and Probability group at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Geneva" title="University of Geneva">University of Geneva</a> in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unige_press_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-unige_press-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unige_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-unige-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stanislav_Smirnov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Smirnov did some work on critical <a href="/wiki/Percolation_theory" title="Percolation theory">percolation theory</a>, where he proved <a href="/wiki/John_Cardy" title="John Cardy">Cardy</a>'s formula for critical site percolation on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Triangular_lattice" title="Triangular lattice">triangular lattice</a>, and deduced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conformally_invariant" title="Conformally invariant">conformal invariance</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clay_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-clay-5">[5]</a></sup> The conjecture was proved in the special case of site percolation on the triangular lattice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Smirnov's theorem has led to a fairly complete theory for percolation on the triangular lattice, and to its relationship to the <a href="/wiki/Schramm%E2%80%93Loewner_evolution" title="Schramm–Loewner evolution">Schramm–Loewner evolution</a> introduced by <a href="/wiki/Oded_Schramm" title="Oded Schramm">Oded Schramm</a>. He also established conformality for the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Random-cluster_model&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Random-cluster model (page does not exist)">random-cluster model</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ising_model" title="Ising model">Ising model</a> in two dimensions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stanislav_Smirnov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Smirnov was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg_Mathematical_Society" title="Saint Petersburg Mathematical Society">Saint Petersburg Mathematical Society</a> Prize (1997), the <a href="/wiki/Clay_Research_Award" title="Clay Research Award">Clay Research Award</a> (2001), the <a href="/wiki/Salem_Prize" title="Salem Prize">Salem Prize</a> (joint with <a href="/wiki/Oded_Schramm" title="Oded Schramm">Oded Schramm</a>, 2001), the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=G%C3%B6ran_Gustafsson_Research_Prize&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Göran Gustafsson Research Prize (page does not exist)">Göran Gustafsson Research Prize</a> (2001), the <a href="/wiki/Rollo_Davidson_Prize" title="Rollo Davidson Prize">Rollo Davidson Prize</a> (2002), and the Prize of the <a href="/wiki/European_Mathematical_Society" title="European Mathematical Society">European Mathematical Society</a> (2004).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unige_press_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-unige_press-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2010 Smirnov was awarded the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fields_medal" title="Fields medal">Fields medal</a> for his work on the mathematical foundations of <a href="/wiki/Statistical_physics" title="Statistical physics">statistical physics</a>, particularly finite lattice models.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-science_now_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-science_now-8">[8]</a></sup> His citation read "for the proof of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conformally_invariant" title="Conformally invariant">conformal invariance</a> of <a href="/wiki/Percolation_theory" title="Percolation theory">percolation</a> and the planar <a href="/wiki/Ising_model" title="Ising model">Ising model</a> in statistical physics".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fields_profile_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-fields_profile-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>It looks like Smirnov gets very little recognition in Russia: he was nominated twice for a memberships of the Russian Academy of Science, in 2011 and in 2016, each time unsuccessfully.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Lacks reliable sources regarding Smirnov's reason for being snubbed (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
