<p><b>Saputo Stadium</b> (French: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i><b>Stade Saputo</b></i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Soccer-specific_stadium" title="Soccer-specific stadium">soccer-specific stadium</a> at <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Park,_Montreal" title="Olympic Park, Montreal">Olympic Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, Canada. The stadium opened on May 21, 2008, and is the current home of the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Impact" title="Montreal Impact">Montreal Impact</a>. The stadium is built on the former practice track and field site on the grounds of the <a href="/wiki/1976_Summer_Olympics" title="1976 Summer Olympics">1976 Summer Olympics</a>, while the stadium's west side has a view of <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Stadium_(Montreal)" title="Olympic Stadium (Montreal)">Olympic Stadium's</a> inclined tower. It has a capacity of 20,801,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-capacity_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-capacity-1">[1]</a></sup> making it the second-largest soccer-specific stadium in Canada, after <a href="/wiki/BMO_Field" title="BMO Field">BMO Field</a> in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Construction">Construction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saputo_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Construction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The stadium cost <span style="white-space: nowrap"><a href="/wiki/Canadian_dollar" title="Canadian dollar">CA$</a>17 million</span> ($18.9 million in 2017 dollars)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inflation-CA_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-CA-6">[6]</a></sup> to build, with $7.5 million paid by the Saputo family and the rest financed on a 25-year term.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Saputo Stadium is now the Impact's administrative headquarters and also includes a training field, 34 corporate suites and full player welfare areas. The complex covers approximately 1,600,000 square feet (150,000 m<sup>2</sup>). Designed and fabricated by Dant Clayton Corporation and built by Broccolini Construction Inc.</p>
<p>The stadium features a natural grass playing surface and was reportedly preferred over BMO Field for this reason by members of the <a href="/wiki/Canada_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="Canada men's national soccer team">Canada men's national soccer team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> BMO Field has since installed a heated and fully irrigated natural grass field similar to those found in the English <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>.</p>
<p>Anticipating a Montreal entry into <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">Major League Soccer</a>, plans were made to expand the stadium from its initial 13,034 capacity to 20,000 to cope with the anticipated boost in attendance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The Quebec government put $23 million for the renovation and expansion of the stadium (the total cost of the stadium was therefore about $40 million). The construction plans went into effect after MLS granted Montreal their <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Impact" title="Montreal Impact">nineteenth franchise</a>, which began play in the <a href="/wiki/2012_Major_League_Soccer_season" title="2012 Major League Soccer season">2012 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_usage">Sports usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saputo_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sports usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The stadium welcomed its first Impact home game on May 19, 2008, a scoreless draw against the <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_Whitecaps_(1986%E2%80%932010)" title="Vancouver Whitecaps (1986–2010)">Vancouver Whitecaps</a>. The Impact's first goal in the stadium was scored by <a href="/wiki/Rocco_Placentino" title="Rocco Placentino">Rocco Placentino</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Charleston_Battery" title="Charleston Battery">Charleston Battery</a> on June 13, 2008. This also gave the Impact its first victory in the stadium, with a score of 1–0. The Impact's first game in the newly renovated and expanded Saputo was played on June 16, 2012 against the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Sounders_FC" title="Seattle Sounders FC">Seattle Sounders FC</a>. The Impact won the game 4–1.</p>
<p>The first international at Saputo Stadium was the second leg of Canada's second stage <a href="/wiki/CONCACAF" title="CONCACAF">CONCACAF</a> World Cup Qualifying match on June 20, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
