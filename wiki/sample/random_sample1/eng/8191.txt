<p><b>Stadio San Paolo</b> is a stadium in the western suburb of <a href="/wiki/Fuorigrotta" title="Fuorigrotta">Fuorigrotta</a> in <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, and is the third largest football stadium in Italy after the <a href="/wiki/San_Siro" title="San Siro">San Siro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stadio_Olimpico" title="Stadio Olimpico">Stadio Olimpico</a>. For the <a href="/wiki/1960_Summer_Olympics" title="1960 Summer Olympics">1960 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, it hosted the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1960_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 1960 Summer Olympics">football</a> preliminaries. It is currently used mostly for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> matches and is the home of <a href="/wiki/S.S.C._Napoli" title="S.S.C. Napoli">Napoli</a>. The stadium was built in 1959 and underwent extensive renovations in 1989 for the <a href="/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1990 FIFA World Cup">1990 World Cup</a>. The present capacity of the San Paolo is 60,240.</p>
<p>The stadium is probably most famous for hosting the 1990 World Cup semi-final between <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a>. Considered to be the most intriguing match of that World Cup, <a href="/wiki/Diego_Maradona" title="Diego Maradona">Diego Maradona</a>, who played for Naples's Italian 1st division team, asked for the Napoli fans to cheer for Argentina. The Napoli <a href="/wiki/Tifosi" title="Tifosi">tifosi</a> responded by hanging a flag in their "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Curva_(stadia)" title="Curva (stadia)">curva</a>" of the stadium saying "Maradona, Naples loves you, but Italy is our homeland".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It was touching for Maradona as Napoli was the only stadium during that World Cup that the Argentinian national anthem was not jeered. The match finished 1–1 after extra time. A penalty shoot out ensued with Maradona fittingly scoring the winning penalty for Argentina.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_times">Recent times</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stadio_San_Paolo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Recent times">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Even with <a href="/wiki/S.S.C._Napoli" title="S.S.C. Napoli">Napoli</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lega_Pro_Prima_Divisione" title="Lega Pro Prima Divisione">Serie C1</a> during the 2005–06 season, Napoli achieved the feat of having the 3rd highest average home attendance in Italy for the season with only two <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> clubs, <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Internazionale_Milano" title="F.C. Internazionale Milano">Internazionale</a> having higher attendances. Napoli's final game of the season drew a crowd of 51,000 which now stands as a Serie C record.</p>
<p>San Paolo also hosted Italy's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_European_Football_Championship" title="2008 European Football Championship">Euro 2008 Qualifier</a> vs <a href="/wiki/Lithuania_national_football_team" title="Lithuania national football team">Lithuania</a> on 2 September 2006 with the possibility of other qualifiers to be played there in the future.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Renaming">Renaming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stadio_San_Paolo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Renaming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Naples city council asked the Italian government for permission to rename the stadium after <a href="/wiki/Diego_Maradona" title="Diego Maradona">Diego Maradona</a>. The Argentine legend helped Napoli to win Serie A titles in 1987 and 1990, and he remains a popular figure in the city.</p>
<p>The council voted to ask for the renaming of the San Paolo stadium, but one stumbling block is an Italian law prohibiting public buildings to be named after any person who has not been dead for at least 10 years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="1990_FIFA_World_Cup">1990 FIFA World Cup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stadio_San_Paolo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1990 FIFA World Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The stadium was one of the venues of the <a href="/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1990 FIFA World Cup">1990 FIFA World Cup</a>, and held five matches The first two were <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina’s</a> Group B matches. The first was against <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union_national_football_team" title="Soviet Union national football team">Soviet Union</a> on June 13 winning 2 - 0 and the second was against <a href="/wiki/Romania_national_football_team" title="Romania national football team">Romania</a> on June 18 ending in a 1 - 1 draw. The next two were <a href="/wiki/Cameroon_national_football_team" title="Cameroon national football team">Cameroon</a> matches. The first was a Round of 16 match against <a href="/wiki/Colombia_national_football_team" title="Colombia national football team">Colombia</a> on June 23 winning 2 - 1 after extra time and the second was a Quarter-finals match against <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> on July 1 losing 3 - 2 after extra time. The fifth and last was the Semi-finals between Argentina and hosts <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> on July 3 with Argentina winning on penalties 1 - 1 (4 - 3).</p>
