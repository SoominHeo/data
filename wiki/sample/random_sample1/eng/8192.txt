<p><b>Stožice Stadium</b> (<a href="/wiki/Slovene_language" title="Slovene language">Slovene</a>: <span lang="sl" xml:lang="sl"><i>Stadion Stožice</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Multi-purpose_stadium" title="Multi-purpose stadium">multi-purpose</a> stadium located in <a href="/wiki/Ljubljana" title="Ljubljana">Ljubljana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>. It was designed by Slovenian Sadar Vuga d.o.o. architects and is the biggest football stadium in the country. It is one of two main stadiums in the city and lies in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Be%C5%BEigrad,_Ljubljana" title="Bežigrad, Ljubljana">Bežigrad</a> district, north of the city centre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-olimpija_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-olimpija-2">[2]</a></sup> The stadium is part of the <a href="/wiki/Sto%C5%BEice_Sports_Park" title="Stožice Sports Park">Stožice Sports Park</a> sports complex.</p>
<p>The stadium is the home ground of the football club <a href="/wiki/NK_Olimpija_Ljubljana_(2005)" title="NK Olimpija Ljubljana (2005)">Olimpija Ljubljana</a> and is one of the two main venues of <a href="/wiki/Slovenia_national_football_team" title="Slovenia national football team">Slovenia national football team</a>. Besides football the stadium is designed to host cultural events as well.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sto%C5%BEice_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The stadium was named after the area in which it is located, and the change of the name is possible in the future due to sponsorship rights. Together with an indoor arena, it is a part of the <a href="/wiki/Sto%C5%BEice_Sports_Park" title="Stožice Sports Park">Stožice Sports Park</a>. The stadium building area measures 24.614 square metres.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stozice_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-stozice-1">[1]</a></sup> It was constructed in just 14 months and was opened on 11 August 2010 on a football friendly match between the national teams of <a href="/wiki/Slovenia_national_football_team" title="Slovenia national football team">Slovenia</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australia_national_football_team" title="Australia national football team">Australia</a>, won by Slovenia 2–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-australia_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-australia-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The stadium has a capacity of 16,038 seats and is laid out under the plateau of the park. The stadium also has 558 VIP seats and 97 spots for people with disabilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stozice_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-stozice-1">[1]</a></sup> The stadium's press area can accommodate 210 journalists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stozice_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-stozice-1">[1]</a></sup> As a structure, the stadium is ‘sunk’ into the park. Only the roof over the stands rises above the plane of the park as a monolithic crater.</p>
<p>For cultural purposes such as music concerts, the stadium capacity is increased to over 20,000.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Football">Football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sto%C5%BEice_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The stadium is mainly used for football and is the home ground of football club <a href="/wiki/NK_Olimpija_Ljubljana_(2005)" title="NK Olimpija Ljubljana (2005)">Olimpija Ljubljana</a>. Besides being the home ground of Olimpija the stadium is also the home venue of the <a href="/wiki/Slovenia_national_football_team" title="Slovenia national football team">Slovenia national football team</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sto%C5%BEice_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although the stadium was primarily build for football it is also planned to host many cultural events. The first was a joint project of two comedians, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lado_Bizovi%C4%8Dar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lado Bizovičar (page does not exist)">Lado Bizovičar</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jurij_Zrnec&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jurij Zrnec (page does not exist)">Jurij Zrnec</a>, that was titled <i>Notpadu lajv?!</i>, on 20 September 2010. Over 20,000 people gathered on this event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stozice_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-stozice-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Records">Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sto%C5%BEice_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The stadium is a record holder for most spectators on a home football match of the <a href="/wiki/Slovenia_national_football_team" title="Slovenia national football team">Slovenia national football team</a>. This was achieved on 11 August 2010 on the opening match, when Slovenia played against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australia_national_football_team" title="Australia national football team">Australia</a>. 16,155 people gathered to see that match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-australia_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-australia-3">[3]</a></sup> That is also the highest attendance on any football match in <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a> after the <a href="/wiki/Ten-Day_War" title="Ten-Day War">independence</a> of the country in 1991.</p>
