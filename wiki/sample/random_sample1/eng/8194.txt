<p><b>Stara Pazova</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serbian_Cyrillic_language" title="Serbian Cyrillic language">Serbian Cyrillic</a>: <span lang="sr" xml:lang="sr"><span class="Unicode">Стара Пазова</span></span>, <small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Serbo-Croatian" title="Help:IPA for Serbo-Croatian">[stâːraː pâzɔv̞a]</a></span>) is a town and municipality located in the <a href="/wiki/Srem_District" title="Srem District">Srem District</a> of the autonomous province of <a href="/wiki/Vojvodina" title="Vojvodina">Vojvodina</a>, Serbia. The town has a population of 18,602, while Stara Pazova municipality has 65,792 inhabitants.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stara_Pazova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a>, the town is known as <i>Stara Pazova</i> (Стара Пазова), formerly also <i>Pazova</i> (Пазова); in <a href="/wiki/Slovak_language" title="Slovak language">Slovak</a> as <i>Stará Pazova</i>; in <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> as <i>Alt-Pasua</i>, <i>Alt-Pazua</i> or <i>Pazua</i>; and in <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a> as <i>Ópazova</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stara_Pazova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> administration (16th-18th century), Pazova was populated by ethnic <a href="/wiki/Serbs" title="Serbs">Serbs</a> and was part of the Ottoman <a href="/wiki/Sanjak_of_Syrmia" title="Sanjak of Syrmia">Sanjak of Syrmia</a>. In 1718, the town became part of the <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Monarchy" title="Habsburg Monarchy">Habsburg Monarchy</a>. In the 18th century (after 1760) <a href="/wiki/Lutheranism" title="Lutheranism">Lutheran</a> <a href="/wiki/Slovaks" title="Slovaks">Slovaks</a> settled in Pazova, and in 1791 <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">Germans</a> arrived here as well. Germans lived in separate settlement known as <a href="/wiki/Nova_Pazova" title="Nova Pazova">Nova Pazova</a> ("New Pazova"), thus the old settlement was named Stara Pazova ("Old Pazova"). Until the second half of the 20th century, Slovaks were largest ethnic group in the town of Stara Pazova, while largest ethnic group in the surrounding municipality were Serbs.</p>
<p>For most part of the Habsburg rule, Stara Pazova was part of the Habsburg <a href="/wiki/Military_Frontier" title="Military Frontier">Military Frontier</a> (abolished in 1882), while in 1848-1849 was part of <a href="/wiki/Serbian_Vojvodina" title="Serbian Vojvodina">Serbian Vojvodina</a>. In the late 19th and early 20th century, Stara Pazova was a district center in the <a href="/wiki/Syrmia_County" title="Syrmia County">Syrmia County</a> (part of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Croatia-Slavonia" title="Kingdom of Croatia-Slavonia">Kingdom of Croatia-Slavonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary" title="Kingdom of Hungary">Kingdom of Hungary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Austria-Hungary" title="Austria-Hungary">Austria-Hungary</a>). According to the 1910 census, population of the Stara Pazova municipality numbered 46,430 inhabitants, of whom 24,262 spoke <a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a>, 9,348 <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>, 5,779 <a href="/wiki/Slovak_language" title="Slovak language">Slovak</a>, and 5,670 <a href="/wiki/Croatian_language" title="Croatian language">Croatian</a>. <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.talmamedia.com/php/district/district.php?county=Szer%E9m%20(Srijem)" rel="nofollow">[1]</a> The town itself had Slovak majority in 1910.</p>
<p>After 1918, the town was part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Serbs,_Croats_and_Slovenes" title="Kingdom of Serbs, Croats and Slovenes">Kingdom of Serbs, Croats and Slovenes</a> and subsequent South Slavic states. According to the 1971 census, 56% of population of the town of Stara Pazova were Slovaks. Today, the largest ethnic group in both town and municipality, are Serbs.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ethnic_groups_in_the_municipality">Ethnic groups in the municipality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stara_Pazova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ethnic groups in the municipality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Serbs" title="Serbs">Serbs</a> = 54,850 (81.17%)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Slovaks" title="Slovaks">Slovaks</a> = 5,992 (8.87%)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Croats" title="Croats">Croats</a> = 1,615 (2.39%)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Romani_people" title="Romani people">Romani</a> = 1,085 (1.61%)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Yugoslavs" title="Yugoslavs">Yugoslavs</a> = 754 (1.12%)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Montenegrins_(ethnic_group)" title="Montenegrins (ethnic group)">Montenegrins</a> = 252 (0.37%)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Macedonians_(ethnic_group)" title="Macedonians (ethnic group)">Macedonians</a> = 209 (0.31%)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hungarians" title="Hungarians">Hungarians</a> = 130 (0.19%)</li>
<li>others.</li>
</ul>
<p>All settlements in the municipality have an ethnic Serb majority.</p>

<td class="navbox-abovebelow" colspan="3">
<div><sup><small>1</small></sup> <span class="wrap"><a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Kosovo</a> is the subject of a territorial dispute between the <a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Republic of Kosovo</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Republic of Serbia</a>. The Republic of Kosovo <a href="/wiki/2008_Kosovo_declaration_of_independence" title="2008 Kosovo declaration of independence">unilaterally declared independence</a> on 17 February 2008, but <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serbia%27s_reaction_to_the_2008_Kosovo_declaration_of_independence" title="Serbia's reaction to the 2008 Kosovo declaration of independence">Serbia continues to claim</a> it as part of its <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_Province_of_Kosovo_and_Metohija" title="Autonomous Province of Kosovo and Metohija">own sovereign territory</a>. The two governments <a href="/wiki/Belgrade%E2%80%93Pristina_negotiations" title="Belgrade–Pristina negotiations">began to normalise relations</a> in 2013, as part of the <a href="/wiki/Brussels_Agreement_(2013)" title="Brussels Agreement (2013)">Brussels Agreement</a>. Kosovo has received recognition as an independent state from <a href="/wiki/International_recognition_of_Kosovo" title="International recognition of Kosovo">110</a> out of 193 <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> <a href="/wiki/Member_states_of_the_United_Nations" title="Member states of the United Nations">member states</a>.</span></div>
