<p><b>Starodub</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Староду́б</span>, lit. <i>old oak</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bryansk_Oblast" title="Bryansk Oblast">Bryansk Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located on the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Babinets_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Babinets River (page does not exist)">Babinets River</a> (the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dnieper_River" title="Dnieper River">Dnieper</a> <a href="/wiki/Drainage_basin" title="Drainage basin">basin</a>), 169 kilometers (105 mi) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Bryansk" title="Bryansk">Bryansk</a>. Population: <span class="nowrap">19,010 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-4">[4]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">18,643 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopCensus_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-PopCensus-6">[6]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">18,906 (<a href="/wiki/Soviet_Census_(1989)" title="Soviet Census (1989)">1989 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census1989_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census1989-7">[7]</a></sup> 16,000 (1975).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Starodub&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Starodub has been known since the 11th century, when it was a part of <a href="/wiki/Severia" title="Severia">Severia</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It was burned to the ground by the <a href="/wiki/Mongols" title="Mongols">Mongols</a> in the 13th century. It became a part of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Lithuania" title="Grand Duchy of Lithuania">Grand Duchy of Lithuania</a> in the 14th century, and later the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polish-Lithuanian_Commonwealth" title="Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth">Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth</a>. In 1503–1618, Starodub was a part of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Moscow" title="Grand Duchy of Moscow">Grand Duchy of Moscow</a> and then Poland until 1648. During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khmelnytskyi_Uprising" title="Khmelnytskyi Uprising">Khmelnytskyi Uprising</a>, it became the center of Starodub Cossack Regiment and enjoyed a large measure of autonomy between 1666 and 1686.</p>
<p>Starodub remained a part of autonomous <a href="/wiki/Cossack_Hetmanate" title="Cossack Hetmanate">Cossack Hetmanate</a> until 1781 when it became an <a href="/wiki/Uyezd" title="Uyezd">uyezd</a> town. In 1796, Starodub was incorporated into <a href="/wiki/Chernigov_Governorate" title="Chernigov Governorate">Chernigov Governorate</a>. In 1918, the town was occupied by the Germans. The Soviet authority was reinstated in November 1918.</p>
<p>During the Soviet period, Starodub was a part of <a href="/wiki/Gomel_Governorate" title="Gomel Governorate">Gomel Governorate</a> (1919–1926), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bryansk_Governorate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bryansk Governorate (page does not exist)">Bryansk Governorate</a> (1926–1929), <a href="/wiki/Western_Oblast" title="Western Oblast">Western Oblast</a> (1929–1937), and <a href="/wiki/Oryol_Oblast" title="Oryol Oblast">Oryol Oblast</a> (1937–1944). The town was once again occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Germans</a> between August 18, 1941 and September 22, 1943. Starodub finally became a part of Bryansk Oblast in 1944.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Starodub&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, Starodub serves as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Starodubsky_District" title="Starodubsky District">Starodubsky District</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref25_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ref25-1">[1]</a></sup> even though it is not a part of it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref1456_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Ref1456-2">[2]</a></sup> As an administrative division, it is incorporated separately as <b>Starodubsky <a href="/wiki/City_of_federal_subject_significance" title="City of federal subject significance">Urban Administrative Okrug</a></b>—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Bryansk_Oblast" title="Administrative divisions of Bryansk Oblast">districts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref25_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Ref25-1">[1]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, Starodubsky Urban Administrative Okrug is incorporated as <b>Starodub Urban Okrug</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref1456_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-Ref1456-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Prior to January 1, 2013, Starodub was administratively incorporated as a <a href="/wiki/Town_of_district_significance" title="Town of district significance">town of district significance</a> within Starodubsky District.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture">Architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Starodub&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Architecture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a significant center of the Cossack Hetmanate, Starodub is the only place in Russia where authentic examples of <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Baroque" title="Ukrainian Baroque">Ukrainian Baroque</a> may be seen. The Nativity Cathedral, built in 1617 and overhauled after a conflagration in 1677, is a typical example of Cossack Baroque. The Epiphany Church goes back to 1789, while the Church of St. Nicholas was erected in the Neoclassical style in 1802.</p>
