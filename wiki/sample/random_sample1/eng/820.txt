<p>"<b>Beautiful World</b>" is a song by Japanese musician <a href="/wiki/Utada_Hikaru" title="Utada Hikaru">Utada Hikaru</a>. It served as the theme song for <i><a href="/wiki/Evangelion:_1.0_You_Are_(Not)_Alone" title="Evangelion: 1.0 You Are (Not) Alone">Evangelion: 1.0 You Are (Not) Alone</a></i>, the 2007 film reboot of the anime <i><a href="/wiki/Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_(franchise)" title="Neon Genesis Evangelion (franchise)">Neon Genesis Evangelion</a></i>. It was released as a double A-side single on August 29, 2007 along with her song "<a href="/wiki/Kiss_%26_Cry_(song)" title="Kiss &amp; Cry (song)">Kiss &amp; Cry</a>", which had been released digitally three months earlier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Amazon_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Amazon-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2009, a remix of the song, "Beautiful World (Planitb Acoustica Mix)" served as the theme song of the second film in the series, <i><a href="/wiki/Evangelion:_2.0_You_Can_(Not)_Advance" title="Evangelion: 2.0 You Can (Not) Advance">Evangelion: 2.0 You Can (Not) Advance</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_development">Background and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beautiful_World_(Utada_Hikaru_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2006, Utada released her fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Ultra_Blue" title="Ultra Blue">Ultra Blue</a></i>, led by the digital single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/This_Is_Love_(Hikaru_Utada_song)" title="This Is Love (Hikaru Utada song)">This Is Love</a>". In February 2007, Utada released "<a href="/wiki/Flavor_of_Life" title="Flavor of Life">Flavor of Life</a>", the theme song for the drama <i><a href="/wiki/Hana_Yori_Dango_Returns" title="Hana Yori Dango Returns">Hana Yori Dango Returns</a></i>, which became one of her most commercially successful singles, selling over eight million downloads and becoming the second most digitally successful song of 2007 globally.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 20, Utada's song "<a href="/wiki/Kiss_%26_Cry_(song)" title="Kiss &amp; Cry (song)">Kiss &amp; Cry</a>" began to be used to commercials for <a href="/wiki/Nissin_Foods" title="Nissin Foods">Nissin Foods</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Freedom_Project" title="Freedom Project">Freedom Project</a></i> advertising campaign, and was released as a digital download on May 31.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-K.26Cannouncement_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-K.26Cannouncement-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RIAJ-jun2007digi_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-RIAJ-jun2007digi-5">[5]</a></sup> Utada's recording of the jazz standard "<a href="/wiki/Fly_Me_to_the_Moon" title="Fly Me to the Moon">Fly Me to the Moon</a>", originally found on her "<a href="/wiki/Wait_%26_See_(Risk)" title="Wait &amp; See (Risk)">Wait &amp; See (Risk)</a>" (2000) single, was rearranged and released as a digital download on June 29.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OriconFly_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-OriconFly-6">[6]</a></sup> Renditions of this song had previously been used in the original <i><a href="/wiki/Music_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion#Fly_Me_to_the_Moon" title="Music of Neon Genesis Evangelion">Neon Genesis Evangelion</a></i> animation's soundtrack, and Utada's version was used in trailers for <i>Evangelion: 1.0 You Are (Not) Alone</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OriconFly_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-OriconFly-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion_and_release">Promotion and release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beautiful_World_(Utada_Hikaru_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Promotion and release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Beautiful World" debuted on the radio and was released as a <a href="/wiki/Ringtone" title="Ringtone">ringtone</a> on July 23, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RadioRingtone_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-RadioRingtone-7">[7]</a></sup> In early September, Utada performed the song at many music entertainment programs in Japan: at <i><a href="/wiki/Count_Down_TV" title="Count Down TV">Count Down TV</a></i> on the night of September 1,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Hey!_Hey!_Hey!_Music_Champ" title="Hey! Hey! Hey! Music Champ">Hey! Hey! Hey! Music Champ</a></i> on September 3,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Utaban" title="Utaban">Utaban</a></i> on September 6,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and both <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Station" title="Music Station">Music Station</a></i> and <i>Music Fighter</i> on September 7.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> "Beautiful World" was performed during Utada's two date concert series <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_Life_(concert)" title="Wild Life (concert)">Wild Life</a></i> in December 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Articles about Utada appeared in music and fashion magazines in August and September 2007, to promote the single. This included R&amp;R Newsmaker, What's In?, CD Data, Barfout!, Oricon Style, Pia, Pop Teen and Patipati.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Planitb_Acoustica_Remix">Planitb Acoustica Remix</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beautiful_World_(Utada_Hikaru_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Planitb Acoustica Remix">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, the song was rearranged for the second film of the <i>Rebuild of Evangelion</i> series, as "Beautiful World (Planitb Acoustica Mix)". It was released as a digital download on June 28, 2009. Utada worked with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Russell_McNamara&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Russell McNamara (page does not exist)">Russell McNamara</a>, who had previously made remixes of her songs "<a href="/wiki/Final_Distance" title="Final Distance">Distance</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Traveling_(song)" title="Traveling (song)">Traveling</a>", "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hikari_(Hikaru_Utada_song)" title="Hikari (Hikaru Utada song)">Hikari</a>" and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hikari_(Hikaru_Utada_song)" title="Hikari (Hikaru Utada song)">Simple and Clean</a>" between 2001–2002. The song was commercially successful, reaching number 8 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_Hot_100" title="Japan Hot 100">Japan Hot 100</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BillboardPos_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-BillboardPos-15">[15]</a></sup> It was added as the final track of Utada's second compilation album <i><a href="/wiki/Utada_Hikaru_Single_Collection_Vol._2" title="Utada Hikaru Single Collection Vol. 2">Utada Hikaru Single Collection Vol. 2</a></i> in late 2010.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_videos">Music videos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beautiful_World_(Utada_Hikaru_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music videos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Two animated music videos were produced for the song, depicting scenes from <i>Evangelion</i>. The first was released at the time of the single's release, and featured scenes of <i>Evangelion: 1.0 You Are (Not) Alone</i>, directed by the film's chief director <a href="/wiki/Hideaki_Anno" title="Hideaki Anno">Hideaki Anno</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> This music video won a Japanese MTV Video Music Award for the best video used for a film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014 to promote Utada's 15th anniversary, as well as the tribute album <i>Utada Hikaru no Uta</i>, a music video of Evangelion scenes was produced by <a href="/wiki/Kazuya_Tsurumaki" title="Kazuya Tsurumaki">Kazuya Tsurumaki</a>, one of the co-directors of the <i><a href="/wiki/Rebuild_of_Evangelion" title="Rebuild of Evangelion">Rebuild of Evangelion</a></i> film series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beautiful_World_(Utada_Hikaru_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>CDJournal</i> reviewers described Utada as singing about "earnest love and deep prayers in a rhythmic tempo", and noted "a painful beauty where transience and passion live together" in the song. They praised the "melancholy" way that Utada sings the lyric "it's only love", as well as the lyrics' sense of ironic while coming from an everyday perspective.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Tetsuo Hiraga of <i>Hot Express</i> gave an extremely positive review of the song, feeling in awe of the emotional sensitivity of Utada's lyrics and how well they resonated with Evangelion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
