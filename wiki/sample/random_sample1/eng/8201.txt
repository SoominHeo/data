<p><b>Starlin DeJesus Castro</b> (born March 24, 1990) is a Dominican <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Second_baseman" title="Second baseman">second baseman</a> for the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB). He previously played for the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a>. Castro is a three-time <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_All-Star_Game" title="Major League Baseball All-Star Game">MLB All-Star</a> and holds the record for most <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Runs_batted_in" title="Runs batted in">runs batted in</a> in an MLB debut. In 2011, he led the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hit_(baseball)" title="Hit (baseball)">hits</a>, becoming the youngest player to do so. He is listed at 6 foot 2 inches (1.88 meters) and 230 lbs (104 kg).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Starlin_Castro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minor_leagues">Minor leagues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Starlin_Castro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Minor leagues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, Castro was named to the <a href="/wiki/Florida_State_League" title="Florida State League">Florida State League</a> All-Star team, and won MVP honors, going 4-for-4 with an <a href="/wiki/Inside-the-park_home_run" title="Inside-the-park home run">inside-the-park home run</a> in the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He was also named <a href="/wiki/All-Star_Futures_Game" title="All-Star Futures Game">All-Star Futures Game</a> for the World Team. After the season, Castro was named the best prospect in Chicago's organization by <i><a href="/wiki/Baseball_America" title="Baseball America">Baseball America</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chicago_Cubs">Chicago Cubs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Starlin_Castro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chicago Cubs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2010">2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Starlin_Castro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Castro was called up to the majors on May 7, 2010, from the <a href="/wiki/Tennessee_Smokies" title="Tennessee Smokies">Tennessee Smokies</a>, the Cubs <a href="/wiki/Double-A_(baseball)" title="Double-A (baseball)">Double-A</a> affiliate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He became the first major league player born in the 1990s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In his first major league at-bat, he hit a <a href="/wiki/Home_run" title="Home run">home run</a> off of <a href="/wiki/Homer_Bailey" title="Homer Bailey">Homer Bailey</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Reds" title="Cincinnati Reds">Cincinnati Reds</a>. He became the sixth player in Cubs history and 106th player overall to do so. Later in the game he hit a <a href="/wiki/Triple_(baseball)" title="Triple (baseball)">triple</a> with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bases_loaded" title="Bases loaded">bases loaded</a>, setting the record for most <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Runs_batted_in" title="Runs batted in">runs batted in</a> (RBIs) in a major league debut with six. He finished the game two for five in the Cubs' 14 to 7 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Castro finished the season with a .300/.347/.408 line with three home runs and 41 RBIs in 463 at-bats over 125 games. Despite his significant offensive contribution, Castro led the team and was second in the National League in <a href="/wiki/Error_(baseball)" title="Error (baseball)">errors</a> with 27.</p>
<p>On October 19, 2010, Castro was named the shortstop on <i>Baseball America'</i>s 2010 All-Rookie Team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He was also named the shortstop on the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Topps" title="Topps">Topps</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Topps_All-Star_Rookie_Rosters" title="Topps All-Star Rookie Rosters">Major League Rookie All-Star Team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He finished tied for fifth in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_Rookie_of_the_Year_Award" title="Major League Baseball Rookie of the Year Award">Rookie of the Year</a> voting.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2011:_All-Star_season">2011: All-Star season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Starlin_Castro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2011: All-Star season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>2011 was Castro's first full season as a MLB player, and also marked his first <a href="/wiki/2011_Major_League_Baseball_All-Star_Game" title="2011 Major League Baseball All-Star Game">All-Star Game</a> selection, as he was one of three shortstops selected to the National League All-Star team. Castro was the youngest Chicago Cub player to make an All-Star team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The same day, Castro received Edward Jones Player of the Month Award honors. Castro was the <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_Player_of_the_Week_Award" title="Major League Baseball Player of the Week Award">National League Player of the Week</a> twice during the 2011 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 23, 2011, Castro got his 200th base hit of the season, at <a href="/wiki/Busch_Stadium" title="Busch Stadium">Busch Stadium</a> against the rival <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Cardinals" title="St. Louis Cardinals">St. Louis Cardinals</a>, and was the youngest member of the Cubs to ever do so.</p>
<p>Castro was the youngest player to ever lead the National League in hits, achieving the feat with his 207th hit of the season on September 28, 2011. Castro's game jersey was sent to the <a href="/wiki/National_Baseball_Hall_of_Fame_and_Museum" title="National Baseball Hall of Fame and Museum">Hall of Fame</a>, in a similar manner to his MLB debut's game jersey. His line for the 2011 season was .307/.341/.432, and it was the first season in which he hit double digit home runs (10). Overall, Castro finished the 2011 season with a .307 batting average, 10 home runs and 66 RBI. His 207 base hits and 674 at-bats both led the National League.</p>
<p>On defense, he led all major league shortstops in errors, with 29. He also had the lowest fielding percentage (.961).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012:_All-Star_season">2012: All-Star season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Starlin_Castro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2012: All-Star season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Castro was selected to the National League <a href="/wiki/2012_Major_League_Baseball_All-Star_Game" title="2012 Major League Baseball All-Star Game">All-Star team</a> along with teammate <a href="/wiki/Bryan_LaHair" title="Bryan LaHair">Bryan LaHair</a>. Castro finished the 2012 season playing all 162 games with a .283 average, 14 homers, and 78 RBI. He was caught stealing 13 times which led the MLB. He also led the National League again with 646 at-bats.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013">2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Starlin_Castro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Castro had his worst year ending with a career low batting average of .245. He hit 10 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Home_runs" title="Home runs">home runs</a> and had 44 RBIs, nine steals, and 2 <a href="/wiki/Triple_(baseball)" title="Triple (baseball)">triples</a> while playing in 161 games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014:_All-Star_season">2014: All-Star season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Starlin_Castro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2014: All-Star season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:232px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Starlin_Castro_Chicago_Cub_player_2014.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1866" data-file-width="1488" height="288" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Starlin_Castro_Chicago_Cub_player_2014.jpg/230px-Starlin_Castro_Chicago_Cub_player_2014.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Starlin_Castro_Chicago_Cub_player_2014.jpg/345px-Starlin_Castro_Chicago_Cub_player_2014.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Starlin_Castro_Chicago_Cub_player_2014.jpg/460px-Starlin_Castro_Chicago_Cub_player_2014.jpg 2x" width="230"/></a>

<p>After a disappointing 2013, Castro was selected to appear in his third <a href="/wiki/2014_Major_League_Baseball_All-Star_Game" title="2014 Major League Baseball All-Star Game">All-Star game</a> along with teammate <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Rizzo" title="Anthony Rizzo">Anthony Rizzo</a> in 2014. Unfortunately, Castro's All-Star year came to an early end when he was injured on September 2 when he awkwardly slid into home plate in a game against the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Brewers" title="Milwaukee Brewers">Milwaukee Brewers</a>. He did not return for the balance of the year and finished with a strong batting average of .292 with 14 home runs, 65 RBI's, 4 stolen bases, 33 doubles and 1 triple in 528 at bats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Castro was the top hitting shortstop (for average) in the National League ahead of the Dodgers' <a href="/wiki/Hanley_Ram%C3%ADrez" title="Hanley Ramírez">Hanley Ramírez</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On defense he had only 15 errors and a fielding percentage of .973.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015">2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Starlin_Castro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2015">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>During a game on April 20, 2015 against the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pirates</a> in Pittsburgh, Castro hit a ball that went foul behind home plate and accidentally struck a fan in her head. The game was delayed for 23 minutes until the fan was escorted on a stretcher and hospitalized due to a concussion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Come the trade deadline, there was much speculation of a trade involving Castro. Potential deals might have involved him going to the Phillies<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> or the Padres.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Amid the speculation, Castro was told by manager <a href="/wiki/Joe_Maddon" title="Joe Maddon">Joe Maddon</a> that he would not be traded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August, Castro was benched by Cubs manager <a href="/wiki/Joe_Maddon" title="Joe Maddon">Joe Maddon</a> due to poor performance, including a career low .236 batting average.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> With rookie <a href="/wiki/Addison_Russell" title="Addison Russell">Addison Russell</a> taking over as the everyday shortstop, Castro made the switch to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_base" title="Second base">second base</a> on August 12.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On September 18, in an important game against the division leading Cardinals, Castro hit two home runs and matched his career high with six RBIs to lead the Cubs to an 8-3 win.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_York_Yankees">New York Yankees</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Starlin_Castro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: New York Yankees">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On December 8, 2015, Castro was traded to the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> in exchange for pitcher <a href="/wiki/Adam_Warren_(baseball)" title="Adam Warren (baseball)">Adam Warren</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Player_to_be_named_later" title="Player to be named later">player to be named later</a>. On December 15, 2015, <a href="/wiki/Brendan_Ryan_(baseball)" title="Brendan Ryan (baseball)">Brendan Ryan</a> was also traded to the Cubs to finalize the transaction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2016">2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Starlin_Castro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2016">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On April 6, 2016, Castro had 5 RBI in a win over the Houston Astros, and he broke the franchise record for most RBI in the first two career games with the Yankees with 7, and the fourth player to get 7 RBI in the first two games in a season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On April 9, Castro recorded his 1000th career hit, in a win over the Detroit Tigers. On June 22, Castro hit his first ever walk-off home run in a 9-8 win over the Rockies. Castro finished the season with a .270 batting average and a career high 21 home runs and 70 RBI.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Starlin_Castro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2011, Castro was sought by Chicago Police for questioning regarding an alleged sexual assault. A woman claimed to have met Castro in a bar and later woken up to Castro sexually assaulting her in his apartment. No charges were filed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 9, 2014,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Castro was detained for questioning by the police regarding a shooting in one of the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>'s nightclubs. It was alleged that he and his brother got into an argument with a government official. According to Castro's agent Paul Kinzer, he was cleared of any involvement and was not arrested, though his home was searched and Castro was questioned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
