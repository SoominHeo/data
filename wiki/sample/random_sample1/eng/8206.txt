<p><b>Fort Stanwix</b> was a colonial fort whose construction commenced on August 26, 1758, under the direction of British General <a href="/wiki/John_Stanwix" title="John Stanwix">John Stanwix</a>, at the location of present-day <a href="/wiki/Rome,_New_York" title="Rome, New York">Rome, New York</a>, but was not completed until about 1762. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Star_fort" title="Star fort">star fort</a> was built to guard a <a href="/wiki/Portage" title="Portage">portage</a> known as the <i><a href="/wiki/Oneida_Carry" title="Oneida Carry">Oneida Carrying Place</a></i> during the <a href="/wiki/French_and_Indian_War" title="French and Indian War">French and Indian War</a>. <a href="/wiki/Fort_Stanwix_National_Monument" title="Fort Stanwix National Monument">Fort Stanwix National Monument</a>, a reconstructed structure built by the <a href="/wiki/National_Park_Service" title="National Park Service">National Park Service</a>, now occupies the site.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Treaty_of_Fort_Stanwix">Treaty of Fort Stanwix</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fort_Stanwix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Treaty of Fort Stanwix">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fort Stanwix was built to guard a portage between the main waterway southeastward to the Atlantic seacoast, down the <a href="/wiki/Mohawk_River" title="Mohawk River">Mohawk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hudson_River" title="Hudson River">Hudson</a> rivers, and an important interior waterway northwestward to <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ontario" title="Lake Ontario">Lake Ontario</a>, down <a href="/wiki/Wood_Creek" title="Wood Creek">Wood Creek</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oneida_Lake" title="Oneida Lake">Oneida Lake</a> to <a href="/wiki/Oswego,_New_York" title="Oswego, New York">Oswego</a>.</p>
<p>In 1768, Fort Stanwix was the site of <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Fort_Stanwix" title="Treaty of Fort Stanwix">an important treaty</a> conference between the British and the <a href="/wiki/Iroquois" title="Iroquois">Iroquois</a>, arranged by <a href="/wiki/Sir_William_Johnson,_1st_Baronet" title="Sir William Johnson, 1st Baronet">William Johnson</a>. By the time of this treaty, the fort had become dilapidated and inactive. The purpose of the conference was to renegotiate the boundary line between Indian lands and white settlements set forth in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proclamation_of_1763" title="Proclamation of 1763">Proclamation of 1763</a>. The British government hoped a new boundary line might bring an end to the rampant frontier violence, which had become costly and troublesome. Indians hoped a new, permanent line might hold back white colonial expansion.</p>
<p>The final treaty was signed on November 5 and extended the earlier proclamation which happened much further west. The Iroquois had effectively ceded <a href="/wiki/Kentucky" title="Kentucky">Kentucky</a> to the whites. However, the Indians who actually used the Kentucky lands, primarily <a href="/wiki/Shawnee" title="Shawnee">Shawnee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cherokee" title="Cherokee">Cherokee</a>, had no role in the negotiations. Rather than secure peace, the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Fort_Stanwix" title="Treaty of Fort Stanwix">Fort Stanwix treaty</a> helped set the stage for the next round of hostilities.</p>
<p>Fort Stanwix was abandoned in 1768 and allowed to go to ruin.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fort_Schuyler">Fort Schuyler</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fort_Stanwix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Fort Schuyler">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Fort_Stanwix" title="Siege of Fort Stanwix">Siege of Fort Stanwix</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Oriskany" title="Battle of Oriskany">Battle of Oriskany</a></div>
<p>The fort was reoccupied by Colonial troops under the command of Colonel <a href="/wiki/Elias_Dayton" title="Elias Dayton">Elias Dayton</a> on July 12, 1776. They began reconstruction and renamed it Fort Schuyler, although many continued to call it Fort Stanwix. Colonel <a href="/wiki/Peter_Gansevoort" title="Peter Gansevoort">Peter Gansevoort</a> took over command of the fort on May 3, 1777.</p>
<p>On August 3, 1777 the fort was besieged by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_King%27s_8th_Regiment&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The King's 8th Regiment (page does not exist)">The King's 8th Regiment</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Loyalists, and Indians, under the command of Brigadier General <a href="/wiki/Barry_St._Leger" title="Barry St. Leger">Barry St. Leger</a>, as part of a three-pronged <a href="/wiki/Saratoga_campaign" title="Saratoga campaign">campaign</a> to divide the American colonies. Gansevoort refused the terms of surrender offered by the British, and the siege commenced.</p>
<p>According to local folklore, when the Colonial troops raised the flag over the fort on August 3, 1777, it was the first time that the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_United_States" title="Flag of the United States">Flag of the United States</a> was flown in battle. It is more likely that the flag flown at Fort Schuyler was one that consisted only of thirteen stripes, an early version of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flag_of_New_York_(state)" title="Flag of New York (state)">Flag of New York</a>, or the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Union_Flag" title="Grand Union Flag">Grand Union Flag</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Oriskany" title="Battle of Oriskany">Battle of Oriskany</a> was fought a few miles away when an American relief column, led by General <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Herkimer" title="Nicholas Herkimer">Nicholas Herkimer</a>, was ambushed by <a href="/wiki/Loyalist_(American_Revolution)" title="Loyalist (American Revolution)">Tories</a> and their Indian allies. While many of the besiegers were attending to that battle, the defenders of the fort sallied forth and attacked the enemy camp, looting and destroying enemy stores. Demoralized and reduced in strength, the British withdrew when they heard reports of the approach of yet another relief column, led by General <a href="/wiki/Benedict_Arnold" title="Benedict Arnold">Benedict Arnold</a>. The British forces withdrew through Canada and joined Burgoyne's campaign at <a href="/wiki/Fort_Ticonderoga" title="Fort Ticonderoga">Fort Ticonderoga</a>.</p>
<p>The British failure to capture the fort and proceed down the <a href="/wiki/Mohawk_Valley" title="Mohawk Valley">Mohawk Valley</a> was a severe setback and helped lead to the defeat of General <a href="/wiki/John_Burgoyne" title="John Burgoyne">John Burgoyne</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Saratoga" title="Battle of Saratoga">Battle of Saratoga</a>.</p>
<p>In April 1779, an expedition from Fort Schuyler against the <a href="/wiki/Onondaga_people" title="Onondaga people">Onondaga people</a> was begun by the <a href="/wiki/Continental_Army" title="Continental Army">Continental Army</a> led by Col. <a href="/wiki/Goose_Van_Schaick" title="Goose Van Schaick">Goose Van Schaick</a>.</p>
<p>The fort burned to the ground on May 13, 1781 and was not rebuilt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It was abandoned and the garrison took up quarters at <a href="/wiki/Fort_Herkimer" title="Fort Herkimer">Fort Herkimer</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_treaty">Second treaty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fort_Stanwix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Second treaty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Fost_FTST3697.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="998" data-file-width="1470" height="149" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Fost_FTST3697.jpg/220px-Fost_FTST3697.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Fost_FTST3697.jpg/330px-Fost_FTST3697.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Fost_FTST3697.jpg/440px-Fost_FTST3697.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Fost_FTST3697.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
View of the reconstructed Fort Stanwix</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Fort_Stanwix_(1784)" title="Treaty of Fort Stanwix (1784)">second Treaty of Fort Stanwix</a> was conducted at the fort between the Americans and the Indians in 1784. During the <a href="/wiki/War_of_1812" title="War of 1812">War of 1812</a> a blockhouse was built on the parade ground. Beginning in 1828 the fortifications were dismantled.</p>
<p>The fort was designated a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Monument" title="National Monument">National Monument</a> on August 21, 1935.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Between 1974 and 1978 the National Park Service reconstructed the fort; a new visitor center was added in 2005. The monument is currently open year around, operated by the National Park Service.</p>
