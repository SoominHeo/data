<p><b>Stann Creek District</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Belize" title="Districts of Belize">district</a> in the south east region of <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">Belize</a>. According to the 2010 census, the district had a <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 32,166 people. Its capital is the city of <a href="/wiki/Dangriga" title="Dangriga">Dangriga</a>, formerly known as "Stann Creek Town." Stann comes from "stanns," or safe havens used by colonialists coming from the "old world" to the "new world."</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_divisions">Political divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stann_Creek_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The district is split into two <a href="/wiki/Constituencies_of_Belize" title="Constituencies of Belize">constituencies</a>. They are <a href="/wiki/Dangriga_(Belize_House_constituency)" title="Dangriga (Belize House constituency)">Dangriga</a>, which contains the town proper and two villages (including Hope Creek and Sarawee) and the offshore islands (cayes), and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Stann_Creek_West&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Stann Creek West (page does not exist)">Stann Creek West</a>, which contains most of the remaining villages including, Placencia and Independence.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stann_Creek_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The district is served by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hummingbird_Highway" title="Hummingbird Highway">Hummingbird Highway</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_Highway" title="Southern Highway">Southern Highway</a>, connecting to <a href="/wiki/Belize_City" title="Belize City">Belize City</a> by way of the <a href="/wiki/George_Price_Highway" title="George Price Highway">George Price Highway</a> at <a href="/wiki/Belmopan" title="Belmopan">Belmopan</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Attractions">Attractions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stann_Creek_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Attractions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Stann Creek District is also home to the <a href="/wiki/Cockscomb_Basin_Wildlife_Sanctuary" title="Cockscomb Basin Wildlife Sanctuary">Cockscomb Basin Wildlife Sanctuary</a> where <a href="/wiki/South_Stann_Creek" title="South Stann Creek">South Stann Creek</a> rises. Within the preserve is <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Peak_(Belize)" title="Victoria Peak (Belize)">Victoria Peak</a>, the second highest point in Belize, at 1,120 meters (3,670 feet) above sea level. The ethnic Garifuna village of Hopkins has recently undergone a huge transformation due to the growing tourism industry. The Garifuna are direct descendants of a group of African slaves who escaped two ship-wrecked Spanish slave ships near St. Vincent in 1635. They celebrate a diverse and rich culture that is a blend of African traditions of music, dance and religious ceremonies; Native American farming, hunting, and fishing techniques; and a French and Arawak influenced language.</p>
