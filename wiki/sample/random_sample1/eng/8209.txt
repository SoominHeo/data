<p>The <b>StubHub Center</b>, formerly the <b>Home Depot Center</b>, is a multiple-use sports complex located on the campus of <a href="/wiki/California_State_University,_Dominguez_Hills" title="California State University, Dominguez Hills">California State University, Dominguez Hills</a> in <a href="/wiki/Carson,_California" title="Carson, California">Carson, California</a>. It is located approximately 10 miles (16 km) south of <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Los_Angeles" title="Downtown Los Angeles">Downtown Los Angeles</a>. Its title sponsor is online ticket marketplace <a href="/wiki/StubHub" title="StubHub">StubHub</a>, replacing hardware retailer <a href="/wiki/The_Home_Depot" title="The Home Depot">The Home Depot</a>. The $150 million complex was developed and is operated by the <a href="/wiki/Anschutz_Entertainment_Group" title="Anschutz Entertainment Group">Anschutz Entertainment Group</a>. With a set capacity of 27,000,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> it is the second-largest <a href="/wiki/Soccer-specific_stadium" title="Soccer-specific stadium">soccer-specific stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">Major League Soccer</a>. Its primary tenant is the <a href="/wiki/LA_Galaxy" title="LA Galaxy">LA Galaxy</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Chargers" title="Los Angeles Chargers">Los Angeles Chargers</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">National Football League</a> will use the stadium from 2017 until they move into <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Stadium_at_Hollywood_Park" title="Los Angeles Stadium at Hollywood Park">Los Angeles Stadium at Hollywood Park</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Rams" title="Los Angeles Rams">Los Angeles Rams</a>, scheduled for the 2019 NFL season.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_facilities">History and facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=StubHub_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For a decade from its opening the complex was called the Home Depot Center. It was renamed StubHub Center on June 1, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 27,000 seat main stadium was designed specifically for <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">soccer</a>. The StubHub Center was the second stadium <a href="/wiki/Soccer-specific_stadium" title="Soccer-specific stadium">designed specifically</a> for soccer in the MLS era. When the venue opened in June 2003 as the new home of the Galaxy, a number of special events took place in celebration. <a href="/wiki/Pel%C3%A9" title="Pelé">Pelé</a> was in attendance at the opening match along with many dignitaries from the soccer world and other celebrities.</p>
<p>In addition to the soccer stadium, StubHub Center features a 2,450-seat <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ADT_Event_Center" title="ADT Event Center">velodrome</a>, an 8,000-seat <a href="/wiki/Stubhub_Center_(tennis)" title="Stubhub Center (tennis)">tennis stadium</a> and an outdoor track and field facility that has 2,000 permanent seats and is expandable to 20,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Soccer stadium building costs within the $150 million complex were around $87 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<p>The StubHub Center is home to the <a href="/wiki/LA_Galaxy" title="LA Galaxy">LA Galaxy</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">Major League Soccer</a> (MLS). It was also home to the defunct <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Sol" title="Los Angeles Sol">Los Angeles Sol</a> of <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Professional_Soccer" title="Women's Professional Soccer">Women's Professional Soccer</a> as well as defunct MLS team <a href="/wiki/Chivas_USA" title="Chivas USA">Chivas USA</a>. It hosted the 2003 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MLS_All-Star_Game" title="MLS All-Star Game">MLS All-Star Game</a> and the <a href="/wiki/MLS_Cup" title="MLS Cup">MLS Cup</a> in 2003, 2004, 2008, 2011, 2012<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and 2014.</p>
<p>The stadium was the site of the <a href="/wiki/2003_FIFA_Women%27s_World_Cup" title="2003 FIFA Women's World Cup">2003 FIFA Women's World Cup</a> final. Both the United States <a href="/wiki/United_States_women%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States women's national soccer team">women's</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States men's national soccer team">men's</a> national soccer teams often use the facility for training camps and select home matches.</p>
<p>It also hosted the <a href="/wiki/2004_NCAA_Division_I_Men%27s_Soccer_Championship" title="2004 NCAA Division I Men's Soccer Championship">2004 NCAA Men's College Cup</a>, with Duke, Indiana, Maryland, and UC Santa Barbara qualifying.</p>
<p>The track and field stadium on the site is also home to the <a href="/wiki/LA_Galaxy_II" title="LA Galaxy II">LA Galaxy II</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Soccer_League" title="United Soccer League">United Soccer League</a>, farm club to the parent Galaxy.</p>
<p>On July 30, 2016, it hosted a <a href="/wiki/2016_International_Champions_Cup" title="2016 International Champions Cup">2016 International Champions Cup</a> match between <a href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain_F.C." title="Paris Saint-Germain F.C.">Paris Saint-Germain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a>. Paris Saint-Germain won the match 4–0 to complete a perfect record in the ICC.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_sports">Other sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=StubHub_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Other sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The stadium hosted the first three editions (2004–06) of the <a href="/wiki/USA_Sevens" title="USA Sevens">USA Sevens</a>, an annual international <a href="/wiki/Rugby_sevens" title="Rugby sevens">rugby</a> competition that is part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IRB_Sevens_World_Series" title="IRB Sevens World Series">IRB Sevens World Series</a>. The stadium has also hosted all <a href="/wiki/United_States_national_rugby_union_team" title="United States national rugby union team">United States national team</a> matches for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Nations_Cup" title="Pacific Nations Cup">Pacific Nations Cup</a> since 2013.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:HomeDepotCenter3.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1344" data-file-width="1792" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/HomeDepotCenter3.jpg/200px-HomeDepotCenter3.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/HomeDepotCenter3.jpg/300px-HomeDepotCenter3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/HomeDepotCenter3.jpg/400px-HomeDepotCenter3.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:HomeDepotCenter3.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A <a href="/wiki/Fireworks" title="Fireworks">fireworks</a> display at The StubHub Center.</div>

<p>It also was the location for the State Championship Bowl Games for high school football in the state of California from 2006 to 2014. The Semper Fidelis All America game was held there on January 5, 2014, featuring an East vs West high school matchup. The first college football game was held at the stadium on January 21, 2012 as the AstroTurf NFLPA Collegiate Bowl, with the National Team beating the American Team 20–14.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The track played host to the 2005 <a href="/wiki/USA_Outdoor_Track_and_Field_Championships" title="USA Outdoor Track and Field Championships">USA Outdoor Track and Field Championships</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It is also the home of the <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a> Running Club, a member of the USA Elite Running Circuit, and the <a href="/wiki/Adidas_Track_Classic" title="Adidas Track Classic">Adidas Track Classic</a>. StubHub Center is also home to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athletes%27_Performance" title="Athletes' Performance">Athletes' Performance</a> which trains athletes in a variety of sports.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Riptide" title="Los Angeles Riptide">Los Angeles Riptide</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Lacrosse" title="Major League Lacrosse">Major League Lacrosse</a> played their home games at the track and field stadium.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The soccer and tennis stadiums of the Center have also served as the main venues for <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a>'s Summer <a href="/wiki/X_Games" title="X Games">X Games</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>From 2010 to 2016, it hosted the Reebok <a href="/wiki/CrossFit_Games" title="CrossFit Games">CrossFit Games</a>. Initially only utilizing the tennis stadium, over the years it also expanded to the running field and the soccer stadium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The facility has also served as the venue for high-profile professional boxing, including <a href="/wiki/Andre_Ward_vs._Arthur_Abraham" title="Andre Ward vs. Arthur Abraham">Andre Ward vs. Arthur Abraham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brandon_R%C3%ADos_vs._Urbano_Antill%C3%B3n" title="Brandon Ríos vs. Urbano Antillón">Brandon Ríos vs. Urbano Antillón</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Porter_vs._Kell_Brook" title="Shawn Porter vs. Kell Brook">Shawn Porter vs. Kell Brook</a> and matches featuring other notable fighters.</p>
<p>On August 16, 2013, Resurrection Fighting Alliance held an MMA event <i>RFA 9: Curran vs. Munhoz</i> with the main event crowning a new Bantamweight Champion.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Chargers" title="Los Angeles Chargers">Los Angeles Chargers</a> will play at the StubHub Center beginning in the <a href="/wiki/2017_NFL_season" title="2017 NFL season">2017 NFL season</a> while the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Stadium_at_Hollywood_Park" title="Los Angeles Stadium at Hollywood Park">Los Angeles Stadium at Hollywood Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Inglewood,_California" title="Inglewood, California">Inglewood</a> is being built.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Entertainment">Entertainment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=StubHub_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Entertainment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vans_Warped_Tour" title="Vans Warped Tour">Vans Warped Tour</a> is held annually in the stadium parking lot. It also served as the host facility for the first two seasons of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spike_TV" title="Spike TV">Spike TV</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pros_vs_Joes" title="Pros vs Joes">Pros vs Joes</a></i> reality sports contests. In 2007 it received the bands <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9roes_del_Silencio" title="Héroes del Silencio">Héroes del Silencio</a>, in their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tour_2007" title="Tour 2007">Tour 2007</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Soda_Stereo" title="Soda Stereo">Soda Stereo</a> in their <a href="/wiki/Me_Ver%C3%A1s_Volver" title="Me Verás Volver">Me Verás Volver</a> tour 2007.</p>
<p>The facility is often used by film and television productions, along with advertising to provide a 'stadium background'.</p>
