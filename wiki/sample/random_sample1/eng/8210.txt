<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Sthenelus</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>In <a href="/wiki/Greek_mythology" title="Greek mythology">Greek mythology</a>, <b>Sthenelus</b><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Pronunciation" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Pronunciation"><span title="This word should have a pronunciation transcription or recording.">pronunciation?</span></a></i>]</sup> (<a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a>: <span lang="grc" xml:lang="grc">Σθένελος</span>) was a name attributed to several different individuals.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sthenelus_(son_of_Andromeda_and_Perseus)" title="Sthenelus (son of Andromeda and Perseus)">Son of Perseus and Andromeda</a>, and king of Mycenae. By <a href="/wiki/Nicippe" title="Nicippe">Nicippe</a>, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Pelops" title="Pelops">Pelops</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hippodamia" title="Hippodamia">Hippodamia</a>, he fathered <a href="/wiki/Eurystheus" title="Eurystheus">Eurystheus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Medusa" title="Medusa">Medusa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alcyone" title="Alcyone">Alcyone</a>.</li>
<li>Son of <a href="/wiki/Capaneus" title="Capaneus">Capaneus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Evadne" title="Evadne">Evadne</a>, he fought alongside <a href="/wiki/Diomedes" title="Diomedes">Diomedes</a> and the other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argives" title="Argives">Argives</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Trojan_War" title="Trojan War">Trojan War</a> and was one of the men who hid in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trojan_horse" title="Trojan horse">Trojan horse</a>. In the <i><a href="/wiki/Iliad" title="Iliad">Iliad</a></i>, he boasts that he captured the city of <a href="/wiki/Thebes,_Greece" title="Thebes, Greece">Thebes</a>, while his father, fighting among the <a href="/wiki/Seven_Against_Thebes" title="Seven Against Thebes">Seven Against Thebes</a>, died attempting the same thing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He ruled <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Iphis_(disambiguation)" title="Iphis (disambiguation)">Iphis'</a> half of <a href="/wiki/Argos" title="Argos">Argos</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Diomedes" title="Diomedes">Diomedes</a>, after both <a href="/wiki/Adrastus" title="Adrastus">Adrastus</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aegialeus_(king_of_Argos)" title="Aegialeus (king of Argos)">Aegialeus</a> had died. His sons were <a href="/wiki/Cylarabes" title="Cylarabes">Cylarabes</a> and Cometes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>Son of <a href="/wiki/Actor_(mythology)" title="Actor (mythology)">Actor</a> (or of <a href="/wiki/Androgeos" title="Androgeos">Androgeos</a>) and a companion of <a href="/wiki/Heracles" title="Heracles">Heracles</a>, whom he accompanied to the land of the <a href="/wiki/Amazons" title="Amazons">Amazons</a> to steal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hippolyte" title="Hippolyte">Hippolyte</a>'s girdle.</li>
<li>Son of <a href="/wiki/Aegyptus" title="Aegyptus">Aegyptus</a> and Tyria, who married (and was killed by) Sthenele, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Danaus" title="Danaus">Danaus</a> and Memphis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>Father of <a href="/wiki/Cycnus" title="Cycnus">Cycnus</a> and King of <a href="/wiki/Liguria" title="Liguria">Liguria</a>.</li>
<li>Sthenelās, Father of <a href="/wiki/Gelanor" title="Gelanor">Gelanor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
