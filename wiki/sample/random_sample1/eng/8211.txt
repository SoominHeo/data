<p><b>Glass knifefishes</b> are fishes in the family <b>Sternopygidae</b> in the order <a href="/wiki/Gymnotiformes" title="Gymnotiformes">Gymnotiformes</a>. Species are also known as <b>rattail knifefishes</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nelson_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nelson-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>These fishes inhabit <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freshwater" title="Freshwater">freshwater</a> streams and rivers in <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nelson_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nelson-1">[1]</a></sup> Many sternopygid species are specialized for life in the deep (more than 20 m or 66 ft) swiftly moving waters of large river channels, like that of the Amazon and its major tributaries<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> where they have been observed swimming vertically.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Sternopygus" title="Sternopygus">Sternopygus</a></i> species inhabit both streams and rivers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fishbase_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fishbase-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Many species are highly compressed laterally and translucent in life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fishbase_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Fishbase-4">[4]</a></sup> These fish have villiform (brush-like) teeth on the upper and lower jaws. The snout is relatively short. The eyes are relatively large, with a diameter equal to or greater than the distance between nares. The anal fin originates at the isthmus (the strip of flesh on the ventral surface between the gill covers). The maximum length is 140 cm (55 in) in <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sternopygus_macrurus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sternopygus macrurus (page does not exist)">Sternopygus macrurus</a></i>.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Eigenmannia_vicentespelaea" title="Eigenmannia vicentespelaea">Eigenmannia vicentespelaea</a></i> is the only <a href="/wiki/Cavefish" title="Cavefish">cave-dwelling</a> gymnotiform. <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Humboldtichthys_kirschbaumi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Humboldtichthys kirschbaumi (page does not exist)">Humboldtichthys kirschbaumi</a></i> (formerly genus <i>Ellisella</i>) from Upper <a href="/wiki/Miocene" title="Miocene">Miocene</a> of Bolivia is the only fossil gymnotiform.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nelson_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Nelson-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>These fish have a tone-like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electric_organ_discharge" title="Electric organ discharge">electric organ discharge</a> (EOD) that occurs monophasically.</p>
<p>Some of these species are <a href="/wiki/Aquarium" title="Aquarium">aquarium</a> fishes.</p>
