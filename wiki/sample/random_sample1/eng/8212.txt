<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Staves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Staves began performing together at open-mic nights in Watford hosted by a local pub, <a class="external text" href="http://www.thehornswatford.co.uk/" rel="nofollow">The Horns</a>. Originally performing as The Staveley-Taylors, the trio later changed their name to The Staves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Observer_-_11Nov2012-_No.2C_we_don.27t_live_in_a_log_cabin_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Observer_-_11Nov2012-_No.2C_we_don.27t_live_in_a_log_cabin-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The group appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Tom_Jones_(singer)" title="Tom Jones (singer)">Tom Jones</a> album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Praise_and_Blame" title="Praise and Blame">Praise and Blame</a></i>, released in July 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMG_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-AMG-3">[3]</a></sup> They supported <a href="/wiki/Mt._Desolation" title="Mt. Desolation">Mt. Desolation</a> on their UK tour in autumn 2010. Jessica Staveley-Taylor also performed as part of Mt. Desolation and provided vocals for their eponymous album. The Staves released the <i>Live at Cecil Sharp House</i> EP and the <i>Mexico</i> EP in 2011,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMG_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-AMG-3">[3]</a></sup> and appeared on <a href="/wiki/Fionn_Regan" title="Fionn Regan">Fionn Regan</a>'s third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/100_Acres_of_Sycamore" title="100 Acres of Sycamore">100 Acres of Sycamore</a></i>.</p>
<p>The Staves attracted attention in the United States while supporting <a href="/wiki/The_Civil_Wars" title="The Civil Wars">The Civil Wars</a> in January 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMG_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-AMG-3">[3]</a></sup> They followed these appearances with performances at <a href="/wiki/South_by_Southwest" title="South by Southwest">South by Southwest</a> and a tour in March in April with <a href="/wiki/Bear%27s_Den_(band)" title="Bear's Den (band)">Bear's Den</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nathaniel_Rateliff" title="Nathaniel Rateliff">Nathaniel Rateliff</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ben_Howard" title="Ben Howard">Ben Howard</a>. This tour was the subject of the film, "Austin to Boston", a documentary by <a href="/wiki/James_Marcus_Haney" title="James Marcus Haney">Marcus Haney</a>. The band supported <a href="/wiki/Bon_Iver" title="Bon Iver">Bon Iver</a> on their May and June 2012 US and Canadian tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The band's debut album, <i>Dead &amp; Born &amp; Grown</i>, was released in November 2012 and produced by <a href="/wiki/Glyn_Johns" title="Glyn Johns">Glyn Johns</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ethan_Johns" title="Ethan Johns">Ethan Johns</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMG_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-AMG-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dead_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dead-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>An excerpt from their performance of "Facing West" on <i><a href="/wiki/Later..._with_Jools_Holland" title="Later... with Jools Holland">Later... with Jools Holland</a></i> was featured on the <i>This is BBC Two</i> promotional programme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The second full-length album, <i>If I Was</i>, was released 23 March 2015 on Atlantic Records in the UK/EU and on 31 March on Nonesuch Records in the US; the record was produced by Justin Vernon of Bon Iver.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The album debuted at #14 on the Official UK Album Chart. In July 2015 The Staves performed with Bon Iver as guests artists along with <a href="/wiki/Colin_Stetson" title="Colin Stetson">Colin Stetson</a>, yMusic, and No BS! Brass for Bon Iver's much anticipated return to the stage as part of the Eaux Claires Festival in Eau Claire, Wisconsin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> They have also performed as the warm-up act for <a href="/wiki/Florence_and_the_Machine" title="Florence and the Machine">Florence and the Machine</a> on Florence's <i>How Big Tour</i> supporting <i><a href="/wiki/How_Big,_How_Blue,_How_Beautiful" title="How Big, How Blue, How Beautiful">How Big, How Blue, How Beautiful</a></i>.</p>
<p>At <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glastonbury_2015" title="Glastonbury 2015">Glastonbury 2015</a>, the Staves performed a set on The Park Stage on Sunday afternoon.</p>
<p>The Staves joined the live line-up of Bon Iver for the latter's Asia tour in late February and early March 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_Life">Personal Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Staves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Staves learned to play guitar from their father while growing up in Watford.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> As children, the girls aspired to create a sketch comedy television program together. They enjoy <a href="/wiki/Guinness" title="Guinness">Guinness</a> and are vocal in challenging tropes about sensitive wilderness-bound artists that dominate modern folk music culture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In addition to touring with Bon Iver and producing <i>If I Was</i> with Justin Vernon, the sisters have become close personal friends with the Wisconsin-born musician.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
