<p><b>Stacey Chanelle Charlene Solomon</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-birth_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-birth-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> (born 4 October 1989)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> is an English singer, television presenter and reality television star. She rose to fame on the <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_series_6)" title="The X Factor (UK series 6)">sixth series</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_TV_series)" title="The X Factor (UK TV series)">The X Factor</a></i>, coming third overall on the show. She gained a number one single on both the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Singles_Chart" title="Irish Singles Chart">Irish Singles Chart</a> when her <a href="/wiki/List_of_The_X_Factor_finalists_(UK_series_6)" title="List of The X Factor finalists (UK series 6)">fellow <i>The X Factor</i> finalists</a> released a cover of "<a href="/wiki/You_Are_Not_Alone" title="You Are Not Alone">You Are Not Alone</a>".</p>
<p>Solomon won the tenth series of reality television show <i><a href="/wiki/I%27m_a_Celebrity...Get_Me_Out_of_Here!_(UK_series_10)" title="I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here! (UK series 10)">I'm a Celebrity... Get Me Out of Here!</a></i> and was named "Queen of the Jungle".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thetelly_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-thetelly-4">[4]</a></sup> Her debut single, a cover of "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Driving_Home_for_Christmas_(Stacey_Solomon_version)" title="Driving Home for Christmas (Stacey Solomon version)">Driving Home for Christmas</a>", was released on 19 December 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digitalspy_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-digitalspy-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thesun_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-thesun-6">[6]</a></sup> Solomon released her debut album <i>Shy</i> on 18 April 2015, followed by the single "Shy".</p>
<p>Although Solomon was made famous by becoming a singer, she is now also a popular TV personality and presenter. In September 2016, she began appearing as a panellist on <i><a href="/wiki/Loose_Women" title="Loose Women">Loose Women</a></i> and in November of the same year she began presenting <i>I'm a Celebrity</i> <a href="/wiki/ITV2" title="ITV2">ITV2</a> spin-off show <i>I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here!: Extra Camp</i> alongside partner <a href="/wiki/Joe_Swash" title="Joe Swash">Joe Swash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vicky_Pattison" title="Vicky Pattison">Vicky Pattison</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Ramsey_(comedian)" title="Chris Ramsey (comedian)">Chris Ramsey</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stacey_Solomon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career_beginnings">Early life and career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stacey_Solomon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life and career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Solomon was born in Dagenham, East London, the daughter of Fiona (née Nash), a nurse, and David Solomon, a photographer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-peppa1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-peppa1-8">[8]</a></sup> Her father is from a Jewish family that had emigrated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK" title="UK">Britain</a> from <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> and <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amz1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-amz1-9">[9]</a></sup> Her mother was born into a <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a> family (the daughter of a <a href="/wiki/Vicar" title="Vicar">vicar</a>), and had <a href="/wiki/Conversion_to_Judaism" title="Conversion to Judaism">converted</a> to Judaism before marrying Stacey's father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-peppa1_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-peppa1-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amz1_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-amz1-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Her parents divorced when she was nine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-peppa1_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-peppa1-8">[8]</a></sup> She attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_Solomon_High_School" title="King Solomon High School">King Solomon High School</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.9310:_The_X_Factor">2009–10: <i>The X Factor</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stacey_Solomon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2009–10: The X Factor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, Solomon auditioned for the sixth series of <i>The X Factor</i>, performing "<a href="/wiki/What_a_Wonderful_World" title="What a Wonderful World">What a Wonderful World</a>". Solomon was among the top 12 contestants and advanced to the live shows and was mentored by <a href="/wiki/Dannii_Minogue" title="Dannii Minogue">Dannii Minogue</a>. During the first week of the live shows she performed <a href="/wiki/Coldplay" title="Coldplay">Coldplay</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/The_Scientist_(song)" title="The Scientist (song)">The Scientist</a>", which gained positive reviews from the judges. During week two, she performed "<a href="/wiki/At_Last" title="At Last">At Last</a>", receiving the most public votes. She sang "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/When_You_Wish_Upon_a_Star" title="When You Wish Upon a Star">When You Wish Upon a Star</a>" during the third week. On the fourth week, all contestants had to sing rock songs; Minogue selected "<a href="/wiki/Somewhere_Only_We_Know" title="Somewhere Only We Know">Somewhere Only We Know</a>", and Solomon's performance resulted in positive reviews. On the fifth week, she sang "<a href="/wiki/Son_of_a_Preacher_Man" title="Son of a Preacher Man">Son of a Preacher Man</a>". The sixth week into the competition, Solomon sang <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Who_Wants_to_Live_Forever" title="Who Wants to Live Forever">Who Wants to Live Forever</a>". During the sixth week, she also released a cover of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/You_Are_Not_Alone" title="You Are Not Alone">You Are Not Alone</a>", which topped the charts on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Singles_Chart" title="Irish Singles Chart">Irish Singles Chart</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chart_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-chart-11">[11]</a></sup> On the seventh week, she performed "<a href="/wiki/I_Can%27t_Make_You_Love_Me" title="I Can't Make You Love Me">I Can't Make You Love Me</a>" and again received positive reviews from the judges and the audience. In the eighth week of the competition Solomon performed "<a href="/wiki/Rule_the_World" title="Rule the World">Rule the World</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Take_That" title="Take That">Take That</a> and "<a href="/wiki/Something_About_the_Way_You_Look_Tonight" title="Something About the Way You Look Tonight">Something About the Way You Look Tonight</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>. In the semi-final she performed another Michael Jackson song, "<a href="/wiki/The_Way_You_Make_Me_Feel" title="The Way You Make Me Feel">The Way You Make Me Feel</a>", and the song "<a href="/wiki/Somewhere_(song)" title="Somewhere (song)">Somewhere</a>" for which she received a standing ovation again by the judges.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> During the final week, Solomon performed a duet "<a href="/wiki/Feeling_Good" title="Feeling Good">Feeling Good</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Bubl%C3%A9" title="Michael Bublé">Michael Bublé</a>. She also performed "What a Wonderful World" and "Who Wants to Live Forever". Solomon was awarded third place in the competition, behind <a href="/wiki/Joe_McElderry" title="Joe McElderry">Joe McElderry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olly_Murs" title="Olly Murs">Olly Murs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JC_20091212_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-JC_20091212-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Solomon went on tour with her fellow <a href="/wiki/List_of_The_X_Factor_finalists_(UK_series_6)" title="List of The X Factor finalists (UK series 6)">X Factor finalists</a>. In December 2010, she returned to the show, where she presented the show from <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a> for winner, <a href="/wiki/Matt_Cardle" title="Matt Cardle">Matt Cardle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.9316:_Shy">2011–16: <i>Shy</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stacey_Solomon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2011–16: Shy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2011, Solomon began working on her debut album, due to be released in 2012. Her first single from the album was originally to be released in February, but was delayed. On 5 November 2011, Solomon performed to thousands as the headline act at Paisley's Christmas Lights Switch On event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In September 2011, it was reported that Solomon was in a bidding war between record labels. However, she stated that she will have complete control. She was also asked to join <i><a href="/wiki/The_Only_Way_Is_Essex" title="The Only Way Is Essex">The Only Way Is Essex</a></i>, but turned it down.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 18 December 2011, Solomon released a cover of <a href="/wiki/Chris_Rea" title="Chris Rea">Chris Rea</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Driving_Home_for_Christmas" title="Driving Home for Christmas">Driving Home for Christmas</a>" as her Christmas single. The single was used for the <a href="/wiki/Iceland_(supermarket)" title="Iceland (supermarket)">Iceland</a> Christmas commercial and reached number 27 in the UK Singles Chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digitalspy_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-digitalspy-5">[5]</a></sup> In June 2012, she said she recorded a number of singles with an album planned in September 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> but later moved that release date to February 2013, and then to November 2013.</p>
<p>On 29 November 2013, Solomon was announced as the headline artist to lead a choir of over 110,000 primary school children. She joined the Young Voices tour, which took place from 14 January to 5 February 2014 in Birmingham, Sheffield, London and Manchester.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The singer performed tracks from her forthcoming debut album on the tour, scheduled for release in 2014. Her debut album "Shy" was released in April 2015 along with the title track "Shy." In 2016, she released her second single from the album, "My Big Mistake."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stacey_Solomon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Solomon took part in the tenth series of the reality show <i><a href="/wiki/I%27m_a_Celebrity...Get_Me_Out_of_Here!_(UK_series_10)" title="I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here! (UK series 10)">I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here!</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> She beat <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Ryder" title="Shaun Ryder">Shaun Ryder</a> in the final and was crowned "Queen of the Jungle".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> She gained 80.07% of the public votes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, she became the face of supermarket <a href="/wiki/Iceland_(supermarket)" title="Iceland (supermarket)">Iceland</a>, taking over from <a href="/wiki/Coleen_Nolan" title="Coleen Nolan">Coleen Nolan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-album_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-album-24">[24]</a></sup> Solomon performed a duet with Ryder at the 16th <a href="/wiki/National_Television_Awards" title="National Television Awards">National Television Awards</a> – "<a href="/wiki/Feeling_Good" title="Feeling Good">Feeling Good</a>" and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Step_On" title="Step On">Step On</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Happy_Mondays" title="Happy Mondays">Happy Mondays</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In October 2010, she won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Living_TV" title="Living TV">Living TV</a> show <i>Party Wars</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2011, Solomon and <a href="/wiki/Keith_Lemon" title="Keith Lemon">Keith Lemon</a> presented <i><a href="/wiki/Sing_If_You_Can" title="Sing If You Can">Sing If You Can</a>.</i></p>
<p>From 11 March 2012, Solomon and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Moyles" title="Chris Moyles">Chris Moyles</a> presented <a href="/wiki/Sky_Living" title="Sky Living">Sky Living</a>'s dating show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Love_Machine_(TV_series)" title="The Love Machine (TV series)">The Love Machine</a></i> . In Spring 2012, Solomon starred in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Mad_Mad_World_(TV_series)" title="Mad Mad World (TV series)">Mad Mad World</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> with <a href="/wiki/Eamonn_Holmes" title="Eamonn Holmes">Eamonn Holmes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stephen_K._Amos" title="Stephen K. Amos">Stephen K. Amos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 5 September 2012, Solomon was part of the judging panel for <a href="/wiki/ITV2" title="ITV2">ITV2</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Top_Dog_Model" title="Top Dog Model">Top Dog Model</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>From February 2015, Solomon took part in the second series of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jump_(series_2)" title="The Jump (series 2)">The Jump</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and was eliminated on 4 February.</p>
<p>Throughout Summer 2016, Solomon appeared several times as a guest panellist on <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> daytime show <i><a href="/wiki/Loose_Women" title="Loose Women">Loose Women</a>.</i> It was later announced that she would become a regular panellist alongside <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Riley" title="Lisa Riley">Lisa Riley</a> after <a href="/wiki/Vicky_Pattison" title="Vicky Pattison">Vicky Pattison</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jamelia" title="Jamelia">Jamelia</a> left the show.</p>
<p>On 22 September 2016, it was announced that Solomon would take over from <a href="/wiki/Laura_Whitmore" title="Laura Whitmore">Laura Whitmore</a> and become co-host of newly named <i><a href="/wiki/I%27m_a_Celebrity...Get_Me_Out_of_Here!" title="I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here!">I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here!</a></i> spin-off <i>I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here! Extra Camp</i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Vicky_Pattison" title="Vicky Pattison">Vicky Pattison</a>, Joe Swash and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Ramsey_(comedian)" title="Chris Ramsey (comedian)">Chris Ramsey</a>.</p>
<p>On a celebrity special of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chase_(UK_game_show)" title="The Chase (UK game show)">The Chase</a></i> aired on 30 October 2016, Solomon won £60,000 in her individual chase, opposing <a href="/wiki/Anne_Hegerty" title="Anne Hegerty">Anne Hegerty</a> (The Governess).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stacey_Solomon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the age of 17, Solomon became pregnant by her then-boyfriend, Dean Cox. The couple split up some time before the birth of their son, Zachary, on 21 March 2008. In 2012, Solomon gave birth to her second son Leighton with then-fiance Aaron Barnham.</p>
<p>She has been in a relationship with <a href="/wiki/Joe_Swash" title="Joe Swash">Joe Swash</a> since 2015.</p>
