<p><b>Stefanus Johannes "Stijn" Schaars</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[ˈstɛin ˈsxaːrs]</a></span>; born 11 January 1984 in <a href="/wiki/Gendt" title="Gendt">Gendt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gelderland" title="Gelderland">Gelderland</a>) is a Dutch <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Midfielder_(association_football)" title="Midfielder (association football)">midfielder</a> for <a href="/wiki/SC_Heerenveen" title="SC Heerenveen">SC Heerenveen</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stijn_Schaars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vitesse">Vitesse</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stijn_Schaars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Vitesse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He was first discovered by Dutch coach <a href="/wiki/Theo_Bos_(footballer)" title="Theo Bos (footballer)">Theo Bos</a>, who went on to manage Schaars' U13 Vitesse youth team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Following successful development in Vitesse's youth team, he was promoted to the main squad for the final few games of the <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_Eredivisie" title="2002–03 Eredivisie">2002–03 Eredivisie</a>. He made his debut for the club on 9 March 2003 in a 4–1 away defeat to <a href="/wiki/FC_Utrecht" title="FC Utrecht">FC Utrecht</a>. The following season he became a regular in Vitesse's side at the age of 19. In his first full season he played twenty one league games. In the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_Eredivisie" title="2004–05 Eredivisie">2004–05 Eredivisie</a>, he helped his club reach a credible seventh place in the league contributing four goals in twenty-one appearances.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="AZ_Alkmaar">AZ Alkmaar</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stijn_Schaars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: AZ Alkmaar">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Stijn-schaars-1312532657.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1943" data-file-width="1793" height="238" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Stijn-schaars-1312532657.jpg/220px-Stijn-schaars-1312532657.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Stijn-schaars-1312532657.jpg/330px-Stijn-schaars-1312532657.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Stijn-schaars-1312532657.jpg/440px-Stijn-schaars-1312532657.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>In the summer of 2005, he was signed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AZ_(football_club)" title="AZ (football club)">AZ Alkmaar</a>. In his first full season at AZ, he suffered a serious hip injury at the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Eredivisie" title="2005–06 Eredivisie">2005–06 Eredivisie season</a> which kept him out for several months. He returned in February 2006 against <a href="/wiki/FC_Utrecht" title="FC Utrecht">FC Utrecht</a> in a 2–3 defeat. In the next season he missed most of it due to sustaining a number of serious injuries which also kept him out for the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Eredivisie" title="2007–08 Eredivisie">2007–08 Eredivisie</a> season.</p>
<p>His return came in the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Eredivisie" title="2008–09 Eredivisie">2008–09 Eredivisie</a> season, where he played a pivotal role in midfield along with <a href="/wiki/David_Mendes_da_Silva" title="David Mendes da Silva">David Mendes da Silva</a>, <a href="/wiki/Demy_de_Zeeuw" title="Demy de Zeeuw">Demy de Zeeuw</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maarten_Martens" title="Maarten Martens">Maarten Martens</a> to claim AZ's second league title in their history. Following the capture of the league title, he helped AZ claim their very first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Johan_Cruijff_Shield" title="Johan Cruijff Shield">Johan Cruijff Shield</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Johan_Cruijff-schaal_XIV" title="Johan Cruijff-schaal XIV">2009 edition</a> of the tournament where AZ defeated <a href="/wiki/SC_Heerenveen" title="SC Heerenveen">SC Heerenveen</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Eredivisie" title="2009–10 Eredivisie">2009–10</a> season he was named team captain of the side in which he held that position until the end of <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Eredivisie" title="2010–11 Eredivisie">2010–11 Eredivisie</a> where he left the club in the summer after six seasons to join Sporting Lisbon of Portugal. During his time with AZ Alkmaar, the Dutch media frequently compared him to English midfielder <a href="/wiki/Steven_Gerrard" title="Steven Gerrard">Steven Gerrard</a> due to his strong presence in midfield, his vision and his technical ability.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sporting">Sporting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stijn_Schaars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sporting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Schaars signed for the Portuguese side <a href="/wiki/Sporting_Clube_de_Portugal" title="Sporting Clube de Portugal">Sporting Clube de Portugal</a> on 16 June 2011 on a transfer worth 850,000 euros.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He was one of many new players bought by Luís Godinho Lopes under the management of <a href="/wiki/Domingos_Paci%C3%AAncia" title="Domingos Paciência">Domingos Paciência</a> who also bought other foreign players like <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Junior_Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Alberto Junior Rodríguez">Alberto Rodríguez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Carrillo" title="André Carrillo">André Carrillo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elias_Mendes_Trindade" title="Elias Mendes Trindade">Elias Trindade</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fabi%C3%A1n_Rinaudo" title="Fabián Rinaudo">Fabián Rinaudo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oguchi_Onyewu" title="Oguchi Onyewu">Oguchi Onyewu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ricky_van_Wolfswinkel" title="Ricky van Wolfswinkel">Ricky van Wolfswinkel</a>.</p>
<p>Under the management of Domingos Paciência and later on in the season <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_S%C3%A1_Pinto" title="Ricardo Sá Pinto">Ricardo Sá Pinto</a> he was a regular in Sporting's season. He scored his first goal for the club in a 3–2 away win against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rio_Ave" title="Rio Ave">Rio Ave</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The following matchday, Schaars scored his first goal at Estádio José de Alvalade. He opened the scoresheet in Sporting's 3–0 home win against <a href="/wiki/Vit%C3%B3ria_F.C." title="Vitória F.C.">Vitória de Setúbal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Following excellent displays during his first few months with Sporting, he attracted interest from several big European clubs of which included <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Olympique de Marseille</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Despite Sporting having a disappointing domestic club finish where they finished behind <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Porto" title="F.C. Porto">FC Porto</a>, <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">Benfica</a> and <a href="/wiki/S.C._Braga" title="S.C. Braga">Braga</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Primeira_Liga" title="2011–12 Primeira Liga">2011–12 Primeira Liga</a>, Schaars played a pivotal role in Sporting's cup competitions. He helped Sporting reach the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Europa_League_knockout_phase#Semi-finals" title="2011–12 UEFA Europa League knockout phase">2011–12 UEFA Europa League semi final stage</a> where they lost to <a href="/wiki/Athletic_Bilbao" title="Athletic Bilbao">Athletic Bilbao</a>, 4–3 on aggregate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Schaars was also part of the side that reached the <a href="/wiki/2012_Ta%C3%A7a_de_Portugal_Final" title="2012 Taça de Portugal Final">2012 Taça de Portugal Final</a> where they lost to <a href="/wiki/Associa%C3%A7%C3%A3o_Acad%C3%A9mica_de_Coimbra_%E2%80%93_O.A.F." title="Associação Académica de Coimbra – O.A.F.">Académica de Coimbra</a>, 1–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Following the 2011–12 season, he was linked with a move to <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the start of the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Primeira_Liga" title="2012–13 Primeira Liga">2012–13 Primeira Liga</a>, he suffered an injury, for which he received surgery, which led him to miss several league, cup and European games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Schaars returned in Sporting's European away game to <a href="/wiki/Videoton_FC" title="Videoton FC">Videoton FC</a> on 4 October 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="PSV">PSV</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stijn_Schaars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: PSV">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 13 July 2013, it was announced that Schaars returned to the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> by making a transfer to <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV</a>. PSV paid €1.6 million for both Schaars and his teammate <a href="/wiki/Santiago_Arias" title="Santiago Arias">Santiago Arias</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Heerenveen">Heerenveen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stijn_Schaars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Heerenveen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After his contract was not renewed at PSV, Schaars joined <a href="/wiki/SC_Heerenveen" title="SC Heerenveen">Heerenveen</a> in July 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stijn_Schaars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>His debut for the national team came on 16 August 2006, as he started in a friendly versus <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Republic of Ireland</a> which the Netherlands won 4–0. Earlier that year he was the captain of the Dutch side that won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_U-21_Championship_2006" title="UEFA U-21 Championship 2006">UEFA U-21 Championship 2006</a>.</p>
<p>An ankle injury prevented Schaars from taking part in most of the 2006–07 season and he didn't play a single Eredivisie game during the following season. This effectively ruled out his chances of taking part in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EURO_2008" title="EURO 2008">EURO 2008</a> with the Netherlands. Schaars was included in the preliminary squad for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> in South Africa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On 27 May 2010, Netherlands manager <a href="/wiki/Bert_van_Marwijk" title="Bert van Marwijk">Bert van Marwijk</a> announced that the player would be part of the final squad of 23 participating in the competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following frequent call ups to the Dutch side's qualification campaign to participate at <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">UEFA Euro 2012</a>, he was selected by manager by Van Marwijk to participate in the tournament finals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> At Euro 2012, he did not participate in any of the group stage matches where his nation finished bottom of the group following three defeats to <a href="/wiki/Denmark_national_football_team" title="Denmark national football team">Denmark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a> and <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
