<p><b>Stefano Baldini</b> (born 25 May 1971 in <a href="/wiki/Castelnovo_di_Sotto" title="Castelnovo di Sotto">Castelnovo di Sotto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emilia-Romagna" title="Emilia-Romagna">Emilia-Romagna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>) is a retired <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athletics_(sport)" title="Athletics (sport)">runner</a> who specialized in the <a href="/wiki/Marathon" title="Marathon">marathon</a>. He was the Olympic champion in <a href="/wiki/2004_Summer_Olympics" title="2004 Summer Olympics">Athens</a> and was twice European champion (1998 and 2006).</p>
<p>Baldini was also a world champion in the <a href="/wiki/Half_marathon" title="Half marathon">half marathon</a>, taking the title in <a href="/wiki/1996_IAAF_World_Half_Marathon_Championships" title="1996 IAAF World Half Marathon Championships">Palma de Mallorca</a> in 1996.</p>
<p>In the 2004 Summer Olympics, Baldini finished first in the men's marathon, winning a gold medal. He finished ahead of Brazilian Vanderlei Cordeiro de Lima, who was leading the marathon until being pushed off the course by protester Neil Horan.</p>
<p>After a disappointing 2007 season in which he did not complete the London Marathon, Baldini chose to run the New York City Marathon, finishing 4th. Baldini closed his Olympic career at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_Beijing_Olympics" title="2008 Beijing Olympics">2008 Beijing Olympics</a>, finishing 12th.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Baldini ended his career in October 2010, having originally planned a final race at the <a href="/wiki/Giro_al_Sas" title="Giro al Sas">Giro al Sas</a> but deciding against the race because of injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
