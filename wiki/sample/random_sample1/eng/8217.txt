<p><b>Stefano Fiore</b> (born 17 April 1975 in <a href="/wiki/Cosenza" title="Cosenza">Cosenza</a>) is an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> manager and former player, who played as an <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Attacking_midfielder" title="Midfielder">attacking</a> <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> or on the <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Winger" title="Midfielder">wing</a>. He was in charge as manager technical area of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuova_Cosenza_Calcio" title="Nuova Cosenza Calcio">Nuova Cosenza Calcio</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Serie_D" title="Serie D">Serie D</a>.</p>
<p>Fiore played for several Italian clubs throughout his career; he started out with <a href="/wiki/Cosenza_Calcio_1914" title="Cosenza Calcio 1914">Cosenza</a> in 1992, before moving to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AC_Parma" title="AC Parma">Parma</a> for a season in 1994, where he made his <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> debut and won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a>. He spent two seasons at <a href="/wiki/Calcio_Padova" title="Calcio Padova">Padova</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Chievo_Verona" title="A.C. Chievo Verona">Chievo</a>, before returning to Parma again for two more seasons in 1997, where he broke into the starting line-up and won a double which consisted of his second UEFA Cup and the <a href="/wiki/Coppa_Italia" title="Coppa Italia">Coppa Italia</a> in 1999. He subsequently moved to <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese</a>, where he spent two successful seasons, winning the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Intertoto_Cup" title="UEFA Intertoto Cup">UEFA Intertoto Cup</a> in 2000 and thus helping his team qualify for Europe. A move to <a href="/wiki/S.S._Lazio" title="S.S. Lazio">Lazio</a> ensued, where he won his second Coppa Italia in 2004, finishing as the competition's top-scorer. His performances led to a transfer to Spanish side <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF" title="Valencia CF">Valencia</a> later that year, where he struggled to replicate his previous form despite initially winning the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Supercup" title="UEFA Supercup">UEFA Supercup</a>, and he was subsequently sent back to Italy on loan to <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Torino_F.C." title="Torino F.C.">Torino</a>, and <a href="/wiki/A.S._Livorno_Calcio" title="A.S. Livorno Calcio">Livorno</a> during his three seasons with the club. In 2007, he returned permanently to Italy, signing with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Mantova" title="A.C. Mantova">Mantova</a> for a season; after remaining inactive during the 2008–09 season, he moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cosenza_Calcio_1914_(2008)" title="Cosenza Calcio 1914 (2008)">Cosenza</a> in 2009, where he ended his career after two seasons.</p>
<p>At international level, Fiore won 38 caps for the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy national football team</a> between 2000 and 2004, and scored twice. At youth level, he was a member of the team that won the <a href="/wiki/1997_Mediterranean_Games" title="1997 Mediterranean Games">1997 Mediterranean Games</a>, while at senior level he was selected to the Italy squads for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2000" title="UEFA Euro 2000">UEFA Euro 2000</a>, in which he scored one goal as his team reached the final, and <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2004" title="UEFA Euro 2004">UEFA Euro 2004</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefano_Fiore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Parma_and_early_years">Parma and early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefano_Fiore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Parma and early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:187px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:StefanoFiore.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="435" data-file-width="281" height="286" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/StefanoFiore.jpg/185px-StefanoFiore.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/StefanoFiore.jpg/278px-StefanoFiore.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/10/StefanoFiore.jpg 2x" width="185"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:StefanoFiore.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Stefano Fiore during his spell at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Chievo_Verona" title="A.C. Chievo Verona">Chievo</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Fiore was born in Cosenza, and he began his professional footballing career with <a href="/wiki/Cosenza_Calcio_1914" title="Cosenza Calcio 1914">his domestic club</a> in 1992. He played just 11 games before moving to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parma_F.C." title="Parma F.C.">Parma</a> in 1994. He made his <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> debut with the club at the age of nineteen, in a 0–0 away draw against <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a>, on the 11th December 1994. During that season, Parma came in third in <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_Serie_A" title="1994–95 Serie A">Serie A</a>, and reached the final of the <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_Coppa_Italia" title="1994–95 Coppa Italia">Coppa Italia</a>. Their most prestigious success was achieved with their <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_UEFA_Cup" title="1994–95 UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> victory over season rivals <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a>, and Fiore was inserted into the starting eleven by manager <a href="/wiki/Nevio_Scala" title="Nevio Scala">Nevio Scala</a>, in the return leg of the final, which finished 1–1. This allowed Fiore to gain international experience alongside his established team-mates such as <a href="/wiki/Gianfranco_Zola" title="Gianfranco Zola">Gianfranco Zola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Couto" title="Fernando Couto">Fernando Couto</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dino_Baggio" title="Dino Baggio">Dino Baggio</a>. His limited playing time with them persuaded him to move to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Padova_Calcio" title="Padova Calcio">Padova</a> for the following season, where he scored 1 goal in 24 games. In the <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%9397_Serie_B" title="1996–97 Serie B">1996–97</a> season, Fiore moved to <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Chievo_Verona" title="A.C. Chievo Verona">Chievo</a>, where he was impressive, notching up 2 goals and plenty of assists. This prompted former club, Parma, to re-sign the midfielder in 1997.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> For the next 2 seasons he became a more permanent member of the squad; although he was mainly left on the bench during the <a href="/wiki/1997%E2%80%9398_Serie_A" title="1997–98 Serie A">1997–98 season</a>, he looked far more impressive than his main starting eleven contender, the aging <a href="/wiki/Dino_Baggio" title="Dino Baggio">Dino Baggio</a>, when he was given a chance, and he became a member of the starting line-up during the following <a href="/wiki/1998%E2%80%9399_Serie_A" title="1998–99 Serie A">1998–99 season</a>. This season was Fiore's most successful season, as Parma finished fourth in Serie A, and won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1998%E2%80%9399_Coppa_Italia" title="1998–99 Coppa Italia">Coppa Italia</a> over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AC_Fiorentina" title="AC Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a>. Fiore also won his second career <a href="/wiki/1998%E2%80%9399_UEFA_Cup" title="1998–99 UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> with Parma that season, as they defeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympique_Marseille" title="Olympique Marseille">Olympique Marseille</a> 3–0 in the <a href="/wiki/1999_UEFA_Cup_Final" title="1999 UEFA Cup Final">final</a> in Moscow. Fiore was one of the protagonists of Parma's triumphant European campaign that season, notching 2 goals in 10 UEFA Cup appearances.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Udinese">Udinese</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefano_Fiore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Udinese">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 1999 he moved to <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese</a> under <a href="/wiki/Luigi_De_Canio" title="Luigi De Canio">Luigi De Canio</a>, originally a cash-plus-player deal in which Parma would receive <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Appiah" title="Stephen Appiah">Stephen Appiah</a> and <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1rcio_Amoroso" title="Márcio Amoroso">Márcio Amoroso</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> for a combined 90 billion lire transfer fees to Udinese, Fiore priced as 15 billion and the rest as cash.</p>
<p>His breakthrough with the club came during the <a href="/wiki/1999%E2%80%932000_Serie_A" title="1999–2000 Serie A">1999–2000 Serie A</a> season, which saw him score a personal best of 9 goals in 33 appearances. His fine form earned him a call to the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">national side</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2000_UEFA_European_Football_Championship" title="2000 UEFA European Football Championship">Euro 2000</a> at the expense of Dino Baggio, the man who had kept him out of the Parma team for so long. His fine form continued and he scored 9 goals in 34 games in the <a href="/wiki/2000%E2%80%9301_Serie_A" title="2000–01 Serie A">2000–01 season</a>, during which he also won the <a href="/wiki/2000_UEFA_Intertoto_Cup" title="2000 UEFA Intertoto Cup">2000 UEFA Intertoto Cup</a> with Udinese, which allowed them to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/2000%E2%80%9301_UEFA_Cup" title="2000–01 UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> that season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lazio">Lazio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefano_Fiore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Lazio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Eventually, Fiore did move to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SS_Lazio" title="SS Lazio">Lazio</a> in June 2001 along with teammate <a href="/wiki/Giuliano_Giannichedda" title="Giuliano Giannichedda">Giuliano Giannichedda</a>, for a deal over 80 billion <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_lire" title="Italian lire">Italian lire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Lazio had big money moves that season, they sold midfielder <a href="/wiki/Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Ver%C3%B3n" title="Juan Sebastián Verón">Juan Sebastián Verón</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pavel_Nedv%C4%9Bd" title="Pavel Nedvěd">Pavel Nedvěd</a> that month (June), and sold striker <a href="/wiki/Marcelo_Salas" title="Marcelo Salas">Marcelo Salas</a> to <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> for cash and <a href="/wiki/Darko_Kova%C4%8Devi%C4%87" title="Darko Kovačević">Darko Kovačević</a>. They also got <a href="/wiki/Jaap_Stam" title="Jaap Stam">Jaap Stam</a> to compensate part of Verón's transfer fees and signed <a href="/wiki/Gaizka_Mendieta" title="Gaizka Mendieta">Gaizka Mendieta</a> from Valencia, initially under his former Italy manager at Euro 2000 <a href="/wiki/Dino_Zoff" title="Dino Zoff">Dino Zoff</a>.</p>
<p>Fiore could not find his best form for Lazio during the <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_Serie_A" title="2001–02 Serie A">2001–02 season</a>, as the coach that replaced Zoff, <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Zaccheroni" title="Alberto Zaccheroni">Alberto Zaccheroni</a>, persisted in playing him on the left side of midfield. This resulted in Fiore losing his place in the national side for the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 FIFA World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>Zaccheroni was dismissed in 2002, and Fiore began to improve with a new coach, <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Mancini" title="Roberto Mancini">Roberto Mancini</a>. Fiore seemed more at ease playing in the centre of the squad's midfield, and he guided Lazio to fourth in <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a>, thus earning them a place in next season's <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>, scoring 6 goals, and also notably reaching the semi-finals of the <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_UEFA_Cup" title="2002–03 UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> that season, only to lose to the eventual champions <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">Porto</a>. Fiore was in good form for Lazio the following season, despite the loss of several key players in the summer (and then <a href="/wiki/Dejan_Stankovi%C4%87" title="Dejan Stanković">Dejan Stanković</a> in January 2004). Lazio won the <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_Coppa_Italia" title="2003–04 Coppa Italia">2003–04 Coppa Italia</a>, in which Fiore was an inspirational player, finishing the tournament as top scorer with 6 goals in 7 appearances. His impressive showings earned him a place on the national side for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2004_UEFA_European_Football_Championship" title="2004 UEFA European Football Championship">Euro 2004</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Valencia">Valencia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefano_Fiore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Valencia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Due to the financial problems which Lazio were suffering, Fiore, along with <a href="/wiki/Bernardo_Corradi" title="Bernardo Corradi">Bernardo Corradi</a>, were off-loaded to Spanish club <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF" title="Valencia CF">Valencia</a>, where he joined the Italian coach <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Ranieri" title="Claudio Ranieri">Claudio Ranieri</a>, and compatriot <a href="/wiki/Marco_Di_Vaio" title="Marco Di Vaio">Marco Di Vaio</a>, signing a 3+1 years contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Corradi was priced at €10 million and Fiore at€6.6 million. The sale compensated the unpaid €16.6 million of <a href="/wiki/Gaizka_Mendieta" title="Gaizka Mendieta">Gaizka Mendieta</a>'s remaining transfer fees from Valencia to Lazio.</p>
<p>After a promising start, which included winning the <a href="/wiki/2004_UEFA_Super_Cup" title="2004 UEFA Super Cup">UEFA Super Cup</a> over Champions League winners Porto, Valencia suffered a disastrous losing streak in October, from which they never fully recovered. They exited the UEFA Champions League early, and coach Claudio Ranieri was dismissed by mid February. Fiore could not adequately adapt to the demands of Spanish football, and was often left on the substitutes' bench.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Loans">Loans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefano_Fiore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Loans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In July 2005, Fiore and Corradi returned to Serie A, with Fiorentina taking the midfielder on a loan spell.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> They had lost <a href="/wiki/Enzo_Maresca" title="Enzo Maresca">Enzo Maresca</a> and holding midfielder <a href="/wiki/Christian_Obodo" title="Christian Obodo">Christian Obodo</a> earlier in June. Fiore linked up well with striker <a href="/wiki/Luca_Toni" title="Luca Toni">Luca Toni</a>, and together they brought Fiorentina to a higher level, guiding them to 4th in Serie A, before the <a href="/wiki/2006_Italian_football_scandal" title="2006 Italian football scandal">Calciopoli verdicts</a> saw them lose this place.</p>
<p>Fiorentina decided not to take Fiore on a permanent basis and he sealed a loan move to <a href="/wiki/Torino_F.C." title="Torino F.C.">Torino</a>, who were returning to Serie A, on deadline day. On January 31, 2007, the closing day of the transfer window, he was loaned to <a href="/wiki/A.S._Livorno_Calcio" title="A.S. Livorno Calcio">Livorno</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 11, 2007 he played his first Serie A match for Livorno against <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">A.C. Milan</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_career">Later career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefano_Fiore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Later career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the summer of 2007, he failed to find a club at which to settle, until August 22, when he signed a 1-year contract with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Mantova" title="A.C. Mantova">A.C. Mantova</a> of <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> which he last experienced in 1997.</p>
<p>Successively, Fiore did not play for any team during the 2008–09, but on September 2009 he finally made his comeback into active football, agreeing a three-year contract with hometown club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cosenza_Calcio_1914_(2008)" title="Cosenza Calcio 1914 (2008)">Cosenza</a>, in the third-tier <a href="/wiki/Lega_Pro_Prima_Divisione" title="Lega Pro Prima Divisione">Lega Pro Prima Divisione</a>, where he remained until his retirement in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefano_Fiore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fiore made 8 appearances for <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">Italy's Under-21 side</a>, and 3 appearances for the Under-23 side, with which he won the <a href="/wiki/1997_Mediterranean_Games" title="1997 Mediterranean Games">Mediterranean Games in 1997</a>. He made his <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy national football team</a> senior debut during his time with Udinese, under manager Dino Zoff, on the 23rd February 2000, taking part in an international friendly against <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_football_team" title="Sweden national football team">Sweden</a>, in Palermo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Fiore's fine form during the 1999–2000 Serie A season soon allowed him to become a permanent and important member of the national side, earning him a place in <a href="/wiki/Dino_Zoff" title="Dino Zoff">Dino Zoff's</a> <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">national side</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2000_UEFA_European_Football_Championship" title="2000 UEFA European Football Championship">Euro 2000</a>. He had a very successful tournament and scored what many regard as the goal of the tournament in the 2–0 victory over co-hosts <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_football_team" title="Belgium national football team">Belgium</a>, in Italy's second group match;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> he also assisted a goal for his creative, offensive midfield team-mate <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Totti" title="Francesco Totti">Francesco Totti</a> in the quarter-finals of the tournament, a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Romania_national_football_team" title="Romania national football team">Romania</a>, which sealed Italy's place in the semi-finals against co-hosts the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> His fine form continued as he went on to take part in all of Italy's matches throughout the competition, as they reached the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2000_Final" title="UEFA Euro 2000 Final">final</a>, only to lose to the defending World Champions <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> on a golden-goal in extra-time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Under Zoff's replacement <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Trapattoni" title="Giovanni Trapattoni">Giovanni Trapattoni</a>, Fiore continued to be a member of the national side, scoring his second international goal on the 28th February 2001, in a friendly defeat to <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a>, at the Stadio Olimpico in Rome.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He missed out on the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 FIFA World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, however, where Italy were disappointingly and controversially eliminated in the second round by co-hosts <a href="/wiki/South_Korea_national_football_team" title="South Korea national football team">South Korea</a>.</p>
<p>Fiore's impressive showings for Lazio during the 2003–04 season allowed him to return to the national side in 2003, and he earned a place in Italy's 23-man squad for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2004_UEFA_European_Football_Championship" title="2004 UEFA European Football Championship">Euro 2004</a>. However, Fiore was used sparingly by coach <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Trapattoni" title="Giovanni Trapattoni">Giovanni Trapattoni</a>, who preferred to play the <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentine</a> born winger, <a href="/wiki/Mauro_Camoranesi" title="Mauro Camoranesi">Mauro Camoranesi</a>, only using Fiore as a substitute in the opening two group matches. When he did start, the Azzurri looked a lot more creative, with Fiore nearly scoring from a spectacular volley against <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria_national_football_team" title="Bulgaria national football team">Bulgaria</a> in the final group game. Despite the Azzurri winning the match 2–1, and not losing a match throughout the competition, the two draws in the previous group games led to Italy's first-round elimination from the tournament on direct encounters, following a three-way five-point tie with <a href="/wiki/Denmark_national_football_team" title="Denmark national football team">Denmark</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_football_team" title="Sweden national football team">Sweden</a>, who both progressed on to the quarter-finals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following Trapattoni's departure in 2004, due to Italy's negative performance in the European Championships, Fiore featured in certain matches under the new Italy manager <a href="/wiki/Marcello_Lippi" title="Marcello Lippi">Marcello Lippi</a>. Fiore retired from the national side later that year. In total, Fiore won 38 caps for the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy national football team</a> between 2000 and 2004, scoring two goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefano_Fiore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Throughout his career, Fiore was usually deployed as an <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Attacking_midfielder" title="Midfielder">attacking</a> <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> or on the <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Winger" title="Midfielder">wing</a>. A combative and mobile player, who was a talented, creative, and technically gifted <a href="/wiki/Playmaker" title="Playmaker">playmaker</a>, with good vision, Fiore was renowned for his passing ability and his eye for goal, which also allowed him to play as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Second_striker" title="Forward (association football)">supporting striker</a> on occasion, as well as in several midfield roles, due to his ability to both create and finish off chances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
