<p><i><b>BECK</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ベック</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">Hepburn</a>: <i>Bekku</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series written and illustrated by <a href="/wiki/Harold_Sakuishi" title="Harold Sakuishi">Harold Sakuishi</a>. It was originally serialized in <i><a href="/wiki/Monthly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Magazine" title="Monthly Shōnen Magazine">Monthly Shōnen Magazine</a></i> from 1999 to 2008, with the 103 chapters later published into 34 <a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a> volumes by <a href="/wiki/Kodansha" title="Kodansha">Kodansha</a>. It tells the story of a group of Japanese teenagers who form a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock_band" title="Rock band">rock band</a> and their struggle to fame, focusing on 14-year-old Yukio "Koyuki" Tanaka, who until meeting guitar prodigy Ryusuke Minami was an average teen with a boring life.</p>
<p>It was adapted into a 26-episode <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> television series, titled <i><b>BECK: Mongolian Chop Squad</b></i>, by <a href="/wiki/Madhouse_(company)" title="Madhouse (company)">Madhouse</a> and aired on <a href="/wiki/TV_Tokyo" title="TV Tokyo">TV Tokyo</a> from October 2004 to March 2005. A live-action film adaptation was released in 2010 and stars <a href="/wiki/Takeru_Satoh" title="Takeru Satoh">Takeru Satoh</a> as Koyuki and <a href="/wiki/Hiro_Mizushima" title="Hiro Mizushima">Hiro Mizushima</a> as Ryusuke.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-animenewsnetwork.com_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-animenewsnetwork.com-1">[1]</a></sup> The series has also spawned three guidebooks, four soundtracks, a video game and a line of guitars.</p>
<p>The original manga was licensed for an English-language release in North America by <a href="/wiki/Tokyopop" title="Tokyopop">Tokyopop</a>. Volume 1 was published in July 2005, but the series was discontinued after the release of the 12th volume in June 2008. The anime was licensed for an English-language release by <a href="/wiki/Funimation" title="Funimation">Funimation</a>. The first DVD was released in 2007, and the last in January 2008.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_summary">Plot summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beck_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yukio Tanaka, known as "Koyuki" by his friends, is a regular 14-year-old Japanese boy starting eighth grade in junior high school. His boring life is changed when he saves an odd-looking dog, named Beck, from some kids. Beck's owner turns out to be an emerging rock musician, 16-year-old Ryusuke Minami, who soon influences Koyuki to start playing the guitar. The story focuses on the trials and tribulations of their <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band named <i>Beck</i>, and Koyuki's relationships with its members, in particular Ryusuke and his 14-year-old half-sister Maho.</p>
<p>After hanging out with Ryusuke and seeing him play with his former band, Koyuki slowly becomes interested in Western rock music. Ryusuke gives him a guitar, but when Koyuki breaks it, Ryusuke tells him never to speak to him again. At the same time, Ryusuke forms his new band Beck, with vocalist Tsunemi Chiba, bassist Yoshiyuki Taira, and Togo, the drummer from his previous band. Koyuki begins working for, and learning guitar from, 44-year-old Kenichi Saitou in exchange to have the guitar fixed.</p>
<p>He reunites with Ryusuke a year later, and begins to rehearse with Beck. Koyuki then makes friends with his classmate Yuji "Saku" Sakurai. When Togo leaves the band, Ryusuke has Koyuki and Saku join Beck as support musicians, becoming full members only when the band hears Koyuki sing. Eventually Beck releases their first album, which gets released on an independent record label in the United States, under the band name Mongolian Chop Squad. After gaining popularity from their US album and Koyuki being in an internationally screened concert documentary, Beck earns a spot at the music festival Grateful Sound 5, where they put on the most talked about show of the whole festival. (The live-action film adaptation ends here.) However, circumstances cause them to part ways, making it their last performance.</p>
<p>Finding life tedious without being in Beck, Koyuki slowly gets the members back together, except Ryusuke, whose whereabouts are unknown. They perform a few shows as a quartet, before getting an offer to tour the US based on their Grateful Sound 5 performance. After Koyuki and Saku drop out of school to do the tour, Beck heads to America. But after several bad performances, they are about to get kicked off the tour before reuniting with Ryusuke in <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>. (The anime adaptation ends here.) The rest of the tour is a hit and they end up appearing on national TV before heading back to Japan.</p>
<p>After releasing two singles, Beck goes on a nationwide tour of Japan and earn a spot at Grateful Sound 7. However, they are later cut from the lineup. They slowly bounce back after forming a tour with several similar-sounding bands, get signed to a popular British indie record label, and start recording their first full album. The now-famous director who created the concert documentary Koyuki once appeared in ends up directing their first music video. Their album and music video do well both in Japan and England, earning them numerous magazine articles in both countries. After another nationwide tour of Japan, they do a short tour of England, including a spot at the relaunch of the legendary Avalon Festival. The band then signs to a major international record label and records their major debut album in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>. With the album hugely successful worldwide, they tour Japan and America extensively, and the series then ends with Beck headlining the main stage at Grateful Sound 9.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beck_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl>
<dt><span id="Yukio_Tanaka"></span><span id=".22Yukio">Yukio "Koyuki" Tanaka</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">田中 幸雄</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tanaka Yukio</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd>The main character; the series follows his rapid development from living a boring average life to becoming an outstanding guitarist and singer. Before meeting Ryusuke, he only listened to Japanese pop music, having never heard a foreign band before. He is the last member recruited into Beck (along with Saku), playing rhythm guitar and singing slower songs. Koyuki is voiced by <a href="/wiki/Daisuke_Namikawa" title="Daisuke Namikawa">Daisuke Namikawa</a> in the Japanese anime, with Kazuya Hirabayashi providing his singing voice, and by <a href="/wiki/Greg_Ayres" title="Greg Ayres">Greg Ayres</a> in the English dub. He is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Takeru_Satoh" title="Takeru Satoh">Takeru Satoh</a> in the live-action film.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt><span id="Ryusuke_Minami"></span><span id=".22Ryusuke">Ryusuke "Ray" Minami</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">南 竜介</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Minami Ryūsuke</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd>A slacker, but incredibly talented guitarist, who inspires Koyuki to pick up the instrument. Ryusuke speaks better English than Japanese, having lived in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a> for 8 years. He is the lead guitarist of Beck as well as its founding member. A large amount of the story revolves around him and his bullet-hole ridden <a href="/wiki/Gibson_Les_Paul" title="Gibson Les Paul">Gibson Les Paul</a> guitar, named <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lucille_(manga)" title="Lucille (manga)">Lucille</a> (which is the same name as <a href="/wiki/B.B._King" title="B.B. King">B.B. King</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lucille_(guitar)" title="Lucille (guitar)">famous black Gibson</a>). Ryusuke is voiced by Yuuma Ueno in the Japanese anime and by <a href="/wiki/Eric_Vale" title="Eric Vale">Eric Vale</a> in the English dub. He is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Hiro_Mizushima" title="Hiro Mizushima">Hiro Mizushima</a> in the live-action film.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt><span id="Maho_Minami">Maho Minami</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">南 真帆</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Minami Maho</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd>Maho is Ryusuke's younger half-sister and a talented singer. She is brash but emotionally fragile, and gradually builds a romantic relationship with Koyuki. Beautiful and fairly popular, Koyuki often has a hard time approaching her romantically. While she is obviously a gifted singer, Maho confides in Koyuki that she would actually like to be a film maker. She is also an amateur model. Maho is voiced by Miho Saiki in the Japanese anime, with <a href="/wiki/Sowelu" title="Sowelu">Sowelu</a> providing her singing voice, and by <a href="/wiki/Brina_Palencia" title="Brina Palencia">Brina Palencia</a> in the English dub. She is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Shiori_Kutsuna" title="Shiori Kutsuna">Shiori Kutsuna</a> in the live-action film.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt><span id="Tsunemi_Chiba">Tsunemi Chiba</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">千葉 恒美</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Chiba Tsunemi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd>The main vocalist of Beck, Chiba's vocals are more punk and rap-oriented than Koyuki's and thus more fitting for the majority of Beck's songs. He is easily the most volatile member of Beck, never shying away from a fight or hiding his feelings during dire situations. He is also very good at <a href="/wiki/Karate" title="Karate">karate</a>, which he uses in his performances. He originally took up karate because he was bullied severely in his younger days. When not busy with the band, he helps manage his family's ramen shop; he says that if Beck doesn't work out, he would open up a chain of ramen shops. Towards the end of the series, Ryusuke tells him that he should leave the band because he thinks he is not as passionate about music as the rest of the band. He complies, but quickly returns to play with the band. He is voiced by Shintaro Ohata in the Japanese anime, with Tatsuzo of YKZ providing his singing voice, and by <a href="/wiki/Justin_Cook" title="Justin Cook">Justin Cook</a> in the English dub. He is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Kenta_Kiritani" title="Kenta Kiritani">Kenta Kiritani</a> in the live-action film.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt><span id="Yoshiyuki_Taira">Yoshiyuki Taira</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">平 義行</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Taira Yoshiyuki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd>Beck's bassist, Taira is the second member recruited by Ryusuke. Although he can sometimes seem uncaring or apathetic, he is actually the most mature of the band members and often offers helpful advice. He usually performs shirtless, much like the real-life person he is based on: <a href="/wiki/Flea_(musician)" title="Flea (musician)">Flea</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers" title="Red Hot Chili Peppers">Red Hot Chili Peppers</a>. His main live bass is a white <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_Man_Stingray" title="Music Man Stingray">Music Man Stingray</a> (a 1993-1996 model as identified from the type of bridge) with 3-band EQ and a trans bridge. Taira is voiced by <a href="/wiki/Kenji_Nojima" title="Kenji Nojima">Kenji Nojima</a> in the Japanese anime and by <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Jewell" title="Jerry Jewell">Jerry Jewell</a> in the English dub. He is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Osamu_Mukai" title="Osamu Mukai">Osamu Mukai</a> in the live-action film.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt><span id="Kenichi_Saitou">Kenichi Saitou</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">斉藤 研一</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Saitō Ken'ichi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd>Saitou is a perverted middle-aged man and former Olympic swimmer who teaches Koyuki both guitar and swimming in exchange for Koyuki working for his business. Though he can be a demanding instructor, he opens up to Koyuki, even asking him for relationship advice on occasion. Saitou is voiced by Porsche Okite in the Japanese anime and by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Bruce_Elliott" title="Robert Bruce Elliott">R Bruce Elliott</a> in the English dub. He is portrayed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Takanori_Takeyama" title="Takanori Takeyama">Takanori Takeyama</a> in the live-action film.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt><span id="Yuji_Sakurai"></span><span id=".22Yuji">Yuji "Saku" Sakurai</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">桜井 裕志</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sakurai Yūji</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd>Saku is Beck's drummer and the last member to join (along with Koyuki). He first becomes good friends with Koyuki at school, being the only person to talk to him. He is voiced by <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dru_Nara" title="Tōru Nara">Tōru Nara</a> in the Japanese anime and by <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Yong_Bosch" title="Johnny Yong Bosch">Johnny Yong Bosch</a> in the English dub. He is closer to Koyuki than the other members of the band, and Koyuki often confides in him during moments of self-doubt. Towards the end of the series, he moves away to attend high school, but promises that he will return on the condition that Beck reunites. As Koyuki does reform Beck, he returns, telling the band that he essentially ran away from home to do so. He is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Aoi_Nakamura" title="Aoi Nakamura">Aoi Nakamura</a> in the live-action film.</dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beck_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beck_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Beck_chapters" title="List of Beck chapters">List of Beck chapters</a></div>
<p>The original manga was written and illustrated by <a href="/wiki/Harold_Sakuishi" title="Harold Sakuishi">Harold Sakuishi</a> and published by <a href="/wiki/Kodansha" title="Kodansha">Kodansha</a> in its <i><a href="/wiki/Monthly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Magazine" title="Monthly Shōnen Magazine">Monthly Shōnen Magazine</a></i> from 1999 to its May 2008 issue (which was released on April 5).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-manga_end_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-manga_end-2">[2]</a></sup> A special 77-page side-story was published in the October 2008 issue of <i>Monthly Shōnen Magazine</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eddie_lee_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-eddie_lee-3">[3]</a></sup> It depicts the last day of Eddie Lee, a popular American rock musician and Ryusuke's friend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eddie_lee_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-eddie_lee-3">[3]</a></sup> The 103 chapters (including the Eddie Lee special) were combined into 34 <a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a> volumes.</p>
<p>The manga was licensed for an English-language release by <a href="/wiki/Tokyopop" title="Tokyopop">Tokyopop</a>. Tokyopop's German branch published the <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>-language version. In January 2009, it was announced that Kodansha let all of Tokyopop's German licenses expire, thus including <i>Beck</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TP_manga_expire_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-TP_manga_expire-4">[4]</a></sup> This subsequently led to Tokyopop's English license of <i>Beck</i> expiring as well. Only 12 volumes were published. The series has also been released in <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>, by <a href="/wiki/Delcourt_(publisher)#Manga_.28Delcourt.2FAkata.29" title="Delcourt (publisher)">Delcourt/Akata</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dynit" title="Dynit">Dynit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haksan_Culture_Company" title="Haksan Culture Company">Haksan Culture Company</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tong_Li_Publishing" title="Tong Li Publishing">Tong Li Publishing</a> respectively.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beck_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_BECK:_Mongolian_Chop_Squad_episodes" title="List of BECK: Mongolian Chop Squad episodes">List of BECK: Mongolian Chop Squad episodes</a></div>
<p>The 26-episode anime television series was titled <i><b>BECK: Mongolian Chop Squad</b></i>, after the subtitle used by the band in the series in the United States, and aired on Japan's TV Tokyo from October 2004 to March 2005. It was directed by <a href="/wiki/Osamu_Kobayashi_(illustrator)" title="Osamu Kobayashi (illustrator)">Osamu Kobayashi</a>, animated by <a href="/wiki/Madhouse_(company)" title="Madhouse (company)">Madhouse</a> and produced by Takeshi Shukuri and Yoshimi Nakajima.</p>
<p>The anime has been dubbed and broadcast on TV networks in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="English_adaptation">English adaptation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beck_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: English adaptation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On Saturday, May 27 at <a href="/wiki/Anime_Boston" title="Anime Boston">Anime Boston</a> 2006, U.S.-based anime distributor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FUNimation_Entertainment" title="FUNimation Entertainment">FUNimation Entertainment</a> announced that they have acquired the license for the <i>Beck: Mongolian Chop Squad</i> anime. <a href="/wiki/Taliesin_Jaffe" title="Taliesin Jaffe">Taliesin Jaffe</a> (known for directing the dub of <i><a href="/wiki/Hellsing" title="Hellsing">Hellsing</a></i>) and <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Bevins" title="Christopher Bevins">Christopher Bevins</a> (known for directing the dubs of <i><a href="/wiki/Speed_Grapher" title="Speed Grapher">Speed Grapher</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Samurai_7" title="Samurai 7">Samurai 7</a></i>) are the directors of the English version. The first DVD was released in 2007, and the last in January 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eddie_lee_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-eddie_lee-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The English dub was released by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Revelation_films" title="Revelation films">Revelation films</a> in the UK and <a href="/wiki/Madman_Entertainment" title="Madman Entertainment">Madman Entertainment</a> in Australia and New Zealand.</p>
<p><i>Beck</i> made its North American television debut on the Canadian music channel, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MuchMusic" title="MuchMusic">MuchMusic</a>, on March 9, 2007. The series finished on June 3. Despite the fact that this is a shōnen series, the show was rated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV-14" title="TV-14">TV-14</a> for its language and violence, but the DVD edition is rated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV-MA" title="TV-MA">TV-MA</a> for strong language, including near-constant use of the word <i><a href="/wiki/Fuck" title="Fuck">fuck</a></i> in the first episode and frequently in subsequent episodes.</p>
<p>In the English adaptation, many songs were re-recorded with English lyrics. The sung lyrics of some English songs in the anime, such as "Moon on the Water" and "Follow Me", were slightly altered to correct grammar, although the <a href="/wiki/Engrish" title="Engrish">incorrect grammar</a> still appears in the English <a href="/wiki/Subtitle_(captioning)" title="Subtitle (captioning)">subtitles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The Beatles' song "<a href="/wiki/I%27ve_Got_a_Feeling" title="I've Got a Feeling">I've Got a Feeling</a>" has the lyrics replaced in the American DVDs.</p>

<p>A live-action film adaptation of the <i>Beck</i> manga was announced in 2009, with filming beginning in July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-animenewsnetwork.com_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-animenewsnetwork.com-1">[1]</a></sup> It was produced and directed by <a href="/wiki/Yukihiko_Tsutsumi" title="Yukihiko Tsutsumi">Yukihiko Tsutsumi</a>, who has directed manga to film adaptations in the past (most notably the <i><a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Boys" title="20th Century Boys">20th Century Boys</a></i> trilogy). It stars <a href="/wiki/Takeru_Satoh" title="Takeru Satoh">Takeru Satoh</a> as Koyuki, <a href="/wiki/Hiro_Mizushima" title="Hiro Mizushima">Hiro Mizushima</a> as Ryusuke, <a href="/wiki/Kenta_Kiritani" title="Kenta Kiritani">Kenta Kiritani</a> as Chiba, <a href="/wiki/Aoi_Nakamura" title="Aoi Nakamura">Aoi Nakamura</a> as Saku and <a href="/wiki/Osamu_Mukai" title="Osamu Mukai">Osamu Mukai</a> as Taira. The actors were given proper training on their instruments for the 30 original songs that were written for the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-animenewsnetwork.com_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-animenewsnetwork.com-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Beck</i> was released nationwide in movie theaters on September 4, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-film_debut_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-film_debut-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers" title="Red Hot Chili Peppers">Red Hot Chili Peppers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oasis_(band)" title="Oasis (band)">Oasis</a> provide the opening and ending theme songs, "<a href="/wiki/Around_the_World_(Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers_song)" title="Around the World (Red Hot Chili Peppers song)">Around the World</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Look_Back_in_Anger" title="Don't Look Back in Anger">Don't Look Back in Anger</a>" respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-film_debut_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-film_debut-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Grand Funk Inc. was given the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_Academy_Prize_(film)" title="Japan Academy Prize (film)">Japan Academy Prize</a> for Outstanding Achievement in Music for its music work in the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The movie was released on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray</a> on February 2, 2011. The DVD came in "standard" and "luxury" editions, with the luxury edition including a bonus DVD.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soundtracks">Soundtracks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beck_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Soundtracks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Beck_soundtracks" title="List of Beck soundtracks">List of Beck soundtracks</a></div>
<p>In 2002, the manga received a tribute album featuring several different artists. The songs used in the anime that were performed by the character's voice actors with <a href="/wiki/Beat_Crusaders" title="Beat Crusaders">Beat Crusaders</a> were released on a soundtrack in 2002. That same day an album featuring those same songs, but in their original versions, was released. A soundtrack to the live-action movie was released in 2010.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_media">Other media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beck_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Other media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Action figures of each band member were created, equipped with amplifiers, interchangeable guitars and in the case of Saku, an elaborate drum kit.</p>
<p>On March 31, 2005, <a href="/wiki/Marvelous_Entertainment" title="Marvelous Entertainment">Marvelous Interactive</a> released an adventure <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video game</a> for the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a> based on the series, titled <i>BECK: The Game</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Fender_Japan" title="Fender Japan">Fender Japan</a> produced two signature <i>Beck</i> guitars. A <a href="/wiki/Fender_Telecaster" title="Fender Telecaster">Fender Telecaster</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and a <a href="/wiki/Fender_Mustang" title="Fender Mustang">Fender Mustang</a> which were both played by Koyuki. The signature is written on the back of the guitars' <a href="/wiki/Headstock" title="Headstock">headstock</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beck_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl>
<dt>Manga</dt>
</dl>
