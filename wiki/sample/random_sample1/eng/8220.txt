<p><b>Stéphane Mbia Etoundi</b> (born 20 May 1986) is a Cameroonian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Defensive_midfielder" title="Midfielder">defensive midfielder</a> for Chinese club <a href="/wiki/Hebei_China_Fortune_F.C." title="Hebei China Fortune F.C.">Hebei China Fortune F.C.</a> and for the <a href="/wiki/Cameroon_national_football_team" title="Cameroon national football team">Cameroon national team</a>, whom he <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captains</a>.</p>
<p>He played 174 games and scored nine goals in <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> over eight seasons playing for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stade_Rennais" title="Stade Rennais">Rennes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Marseille</a>. He spent two years at <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla</a>, winning the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">Europa League</a> in both.</p>
<p>He has played for Cameroon over 60 times since winning his first cap in 2005 and has scored five goals for the national team. He was included in the squads for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_-_Men%27s_tournament" title="Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics - Men's tournament">2008 Olympics</a>, two <a href="/wiki/Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="Africa Cup of Nations">Africa Cup of Nations</a> tournaments and two <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cups</a>. He was given the captain's armband on 26 August 2014.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St%C3%A9phane_Mbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stade_Rennais">Stade Rennais</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St%C3%A9phane_Mbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Stade Rennais">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Yaound%C3%A9" title="Yaoundé">Yaoundé</a>, Mbia made his debut for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stade_Rennais" title="Stade Rennais">Rennes</a> first team during the 2005–06 season, going on to make a handful of appearances over the campaign. The Cameroonian scored his first <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> goal in November 2006 against the incumbent French champions <a href="/wiki/Olympique_Lyonnais" title="Olympique Lyonnais">Lyon</a>.</p>
<p>On 18 April 2007, Mbia was abused by Lyon player <a href="/wiki/Milan_Baro%C5%A1" title="Milan Baroš">Milan Baroš</a>. This led to disciplinary action being taken against Baroš by the <a href="/wiki/French_Football_Federation" title="French Football Federation">French Football Federation</a>, who banned the striker for three matches for implying that Mbia smelt. The Federation later ruled that although Baroš was guilty of implying Mbia smelt badly, the remark was not made in a racial context.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marseille">Marseille</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St%C3%A9phane_Mbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Marseille">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 14 July 2009, Mbia completed his £10.4 million move to <a href="/wiki/Marseille" title="Marseille">Marseille</a> from Rennes, going on to play a vital role in Marseille's victorious Ligue 1 campaign that season, starting off as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defensive_midfielder" title="Defensive midfielder">defensive midfielder</a> but later making the transition to the role of centre back alongside former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charlton_Athletic" title="Charlton Athletic">Charlton Athletic</a> player <a href="/wiki/Souleymane_Diawara" title="Souleymane Diawara">Souleymane Diawara</a>. His redeployment at centre back was a success and he went on to play the rest of the season in this position, where he was considered to be one of the best performers in <a href="/wiki/Didier_Deschamps" title="Didier Deschamps">Didier Deschamps</a>' talented squad, as he formed a formidable defensive partnership with Diawara.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Queens_Park_Rangers">Queens Park Rangers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St%C3%A9phane_Mbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Queens Park Rangers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 31 August 2012 Mbia signed a two-year deal with English <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers" title="Queens Park Rangers">Queens Park Rangers</a>, signing for an undisclosed fee, with <a href="/wiki/Joey_Barton" title="Joey Barton">Joey Barton</a> heading in the opposite direction on a season-long loan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 27 October 2012, Mbia was sent off seventeen minutes from the end of only his fourth league appearance for QPR, a 1–0 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Emirates_Stadium" title="Emirates Stadium">Emirates Stadium</a>. He was dismissed in the 79th minute for violent conduct after kicking Gunners defender <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Vermaelen" title="Thomas Vermaelen">Thomas Vermaelen</a> in retaliation to a strong challenge that had left Mbia floored.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He received the standard three-match ban from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_FA" title="The FA">FA</a>, and became ineligible for QPR's league games against <a href="/wiki/Reading_F.C." title="Reading F.C.">Reading</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On 6 May 2013, Mbia tweeted to Joey Barton, asking him if he would like to switch seats with him at Marseille in hinting that he wanted to leave Queen Park Rangers after they were relegated from the Premier League. In July 2013 Mbia scored his first goal for the club in a pre-season friendly against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southend_United" title="Southend United">Southend United</a> and expressed desire to stay at QPR.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sevilla">Sevilla</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St%C3%A9phane_Mbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sevilla">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Zenit-Sevilla_(2).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="840" data-file-width="560" height="300" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Zenit-Sevilla_%282%29.jpg/200px-Zenit-Sevilla_%282%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Zenit-Sevilla_%282%29.jpg/300px-Zenit-Sevilla_%282%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Zenit-Sevilla_%282%29.jpg/400px-Zenit-Sevilla_%282%29.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Zenit-Sevilla_(2).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Mbia playing for Sevilla against <a href="/wiki/FC_Zenit_Saint_Petersburg" title="FC Zenit Saint Petersburg">Zenit</a> in 2015</div>

<p>On 26 August 2013, Mbia was loaned out to Spanish <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> club <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla FC</a> for the rest of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Mbia made his Sevilla debut on 1 September, providing an assist for <a href="/wiki/K%C3%A9vin_Gameiro" title="Kévin Gameiro">Kévin Gameiro</a>'s first goal in a 2–2 draw with <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1laga_CF" title="Málaga CF">Málaga CF</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On 27 October, Mbia provided assists for both <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Rakiti%C4%87" title="Ivan Rakitić">Ivan Rakitić</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jairo_Samperio" title="Jairo Samperio">Jairo Samperio</a> as Sevilla overcame 10-man <a href="/wiki/CA_Osasuna" title="CA Osasuna">Osasuna</a> 2–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> His first goal in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2013-14_La_Liga" title="2013-14 La Liga">La Liga</a> came in the <a href="/wiki/Seville_derby" title="Seville derby">Seville derby</a> against <a href="/wiki/Real_Betis" title="Real Betis">Real Betis</a> on 24 November, heading in Sevilla's second goal in a 4–0 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mbia scored with a back-heel in the first leg of their Europa League semi-final against compatriots <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF" title="Valencia CF">Valencia CF</a> on 24 April 2014 in a 2–0 home win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In the second leg on 1 May, he scored the winning away goal with a header in the fourth minute of added time to take Sevilla into <a href="/wiki/2014_UEFA_Europa_League_Final" title="2014 UEFA Europa League Final">the final</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Sevilla subsequently won the final in a shootout against <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">Benfica</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Juventus_Stadium" title="Juventus Stadium">Juventus Stadium</a> in Turin on 14 May, with Mbia converting his penalty kick after the match had ended 0—0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> On 3 June, Mbia was selected in the 18-man Europa League "Team of the Year" along with teammates <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Rakiti%C4%87" title="Ivan Rakitić">Ivan Rakitić</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicol%C3%A1s_Pareja" title="Nicolás Pareja">Nicolás Pareja</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beto_(Portuguese_footballer,_born_1982)" title="Beto (Portuguese footballer, born 1982)">Beto</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> At the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LFP_Awards" title="LFP Awards">LFP Awards</a>, he was a nominee for the league's best African player, alongside the <a href="/wiki/Granada_CF" title="Granada CF">Granada CF</a> duo of <a href="/wiki/Yacine_Brahimi" title="Yacine Brahimi">Yacine Brahimi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Youssef_El-Arabi" title="Youssef El-Arabi">Youssef El-Arabi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 31 August 2014, Mbia rejoined Sevilla on permanent basis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He scored his first goal of the new season on 18 September, heading in a cross from <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Deulofeu" title="Gerard Deulofeu">Gerard Deulofeu</a> as Sevilla started the defense of their Europa League title with a 2–0 win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feyenoord_Rotterdam" title="Feyenoord Rotterdam">Feyenoord</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Mbia netted a brace as Sevilla earned a comfortable 4–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/Deportivo_de_La_Coru%C3%B1a" title="Deportivo de La Coruña">Deportivo de La Coruña</a> at the Ramon Sanchez Pizjuan stadium on 5 October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 27 May 2015, Mbia started for Sevilla in the <a href="/wiki/2015_UEFA_Europa_League_Final" title="2015 UEFA Europa League Final">2015 UEFA Europa League Final</a> defeat of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FC_Dnipro" title="FC Dnipro">FC Dnipro</a> at Poland's <a href="/wiki/National_Stadium,_Warsaw" title="National Stadium, Warsaw">National Stadium</a>, thus retaining their <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">Europa League</a> crown, and thus qualifying the team for <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2015–16 UEFA Champions League">next season's Champions League</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Trabzonspor">Trabzonspor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St%C3%A9phane_Mbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Trabzonspor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 2 July 2015, Mbia joined <a href="/wiki/Trabzonspor" title="Trabzonspor">Trabzonspor</a> on a free transfer, signing a three-year deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hebei_China_Fortune">Hebei China Fortune</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St%C3%A9phane_Mbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Hebei China Fortune">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 29 January 2016 Mbia signed for Chinese club Hebei China Fortune. Mbia wrote on his Twitter page: "Proud to join Hebei China Fortune, ready for this adventure. Let's go."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St%C3%A9phane_Mbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:272px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Gol_fred.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3210" data-file-width="4384" height="198" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Gol_fred.jpg/270px-Gol_fred.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Gol_fred.jpg/405px-Gol_fred.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Gol_fred.jpg/540px-Gol_fred.jpg 2x" width="270"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Gol_fred.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Fred_(footballer)" title="Fred (footballer)">Fred</a> scoring a goal for <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a> as Mbia (left) and goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Charles_Itandje" title="Charles Itandje">Charles Itandje</a> look on alongside their compatriots during a group stage match at the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a></div>

<p>Mbia has played international football at junior level for Cameroon. He was part of the Cameroon squad that competed in the <a href="/wiki/2003_FIFA_U-17_World_Championship" title="2003 FIFA U-17 World Championship">2003 FIFA U-17 World Championship</a> in <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, where his side were eliminated at the group stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>He made his debut for the full <a href="/wiki/Cameroon_national_football_team" title="Cameroon national football team">Cameroon national team</a> in 2005. He was part of the squad which were runners up at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="2008 Africa Cup of Nations">2008 Africa Cup of Nations</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a>, where he scored twice in a 3–2 win against <a href="/wiki/Tunisia_national_football_team" title="Tunisia national football team">Tunisia</a> in the quarter-finals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mbia was part of the Cameroon squad for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_Olympics" title="2008 Olympics">2008 Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, playing in all four of his teams matches, and scoring once against <a href="/wiki/Honduras_national_football_team" title="Honduras national football team">Honduras</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Mbia was also selected to represent Cameroon at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="2010 Africa Cup of Nations">2010 Africa Cup of Nations</a> in Angola<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">World Cup</a> in South Africa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 2 June 2014, Mbia was included by Cameroon manager <a href="/wiki/Volker_Finke" title="Volker Finke">Volker Finke</a> in the final 23-man squad for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">World Cup</a> in Brazil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The team were eliminated after losing all of their group games amidst a bonuses dispute that led to Mbia replacing <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Eto%27o" title="Samuel Eto'o">Samuel Eto'o</a> as <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captain</a> of the national team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St%C3%A9phane_Mbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mbia is the older brother of footballer <a href="/wiki/Franck_Etoundi" title="Franck Etoundi">Franck Etoundi</a>, who currently plays for <a href="/wiki/FC_Z%C3%BCrich" title="FC Zürich">FC Zürich</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Super_League" title="Swiss Super League">Swiss Super League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mbia also holds a French passport, having previously resided in the country for more than five years.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
