<p><b>Stephen Tataw Eta</b> (born March 31, 1963) is a <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroonian</a> former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football (soccer)</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defender_(football)" title="Defender (football)">defender</a> who captained the <a href="/wiki/Cameroon_national_football_team" title="Cameroon national football team">Cameroon national team</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1990 FIFA World Cup">1990 FIFA World Cup</a>.</p>
<p>At the club level, he played with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tonnerre_Yaounde" title="Tonnerre Yaounde">Tonnerre Yaounde</a> from 1988 to 1991, and for <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Mvoly%C3%A9" title="Olympic Mvolyé">Olympic Mvolyé</a> from 1992 to 1994. He then joined <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> side <a href="/wiki/Tosu_Futures" title="Tosu Futures">Tosu Futures</a> where many viewed him as the first African-born professional player in Japan. While he was in Japan, he unsuccessfully tried to guide his club to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J-League" title="J-League">J-League</a>, the top division.</p>
<p>He is probably best remembered as the captain of <a href="/wiki/Cameroon_national_football_team" title="Cameroon national football team">Cameroon</a> during their historical run at the <a href="/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1990 FIFA World Cup">1990 FIFA World Cup</a>, where the team became the first African nation to reach quarterfinals.</p>
