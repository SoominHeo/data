<p><b>Stefan Petrucha</b> (born January 27, 1959)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is an American writer of comics and <a href="/wiki/Young_adult_fiction" title="Young adult fiction">young adult fiction</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He has written graphic novels in the <i><a href="/wiki/The_X-Files_(comics)" title="The X-Files (comics)">The X-Files</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Nancy_Drew#Papercutz_Nancy_Drew_graphic_novels" title="Nancy Drew">Nancy Drew</a></i> series, as well as science fiction and horror.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefan_Petrucha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stefan Petrucha has been a tech writer, an educational writer, a public relations writer and an editor for trade journals.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He is also a teacher of comics, and teaches three online courses on writing for comics and other skills through the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Massachusetts" title="University of Massachusetts">University of Massachusetts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
