<p><b>Stepney</b> is a district of the <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Tower_Hamlets" title="London Borough of Tower Hamlets">London Borough of Tower Hamlets</a> in <a href="/wiki/East_End_of_London" title="East End of London">London's East End</a> that grew out of a medieval village around <a href="/wiki/St_Dunstan%27s,_Stepney" title="St Dunstan's, Stepney">St Dunstan's church</a> and the 15th century <a href="/wiki/Ribbon_development" title="Ribbon development">ribbon development</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mile_End_Road" title="Mile End Road">Mile End Road</a>. The area built up rapidly in the 19th century, mainly to accommodate immigrant workers and displaced London poor, and developed a reputation for poverty, overcrowding, violence and political dissent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-London_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-London-2">[2]</a></sup> It was severely damaged during <a href="/wiki/The_Blitz" title="The Blitz">the Blitz</a>, with over a third of housing totally destroyed; and then, in the 1960s, <a href="/wiki/Slum_clearance" title="Slum clearance">slum clearance</a> and development replaced most residential streets with <a href="/wiki/Tower_block" title="Tower block">tower blocks</a> and modern housing estates. Some <a href="/wiki/Georgian_architecture" title="Georgian architecture">Georgian architecture</a> and <a href="/wiki/Victorian_era" title="Victorian era">Victorian era</a> terraced housing survive in patches: for example <a href="/wiki/Arbour_Square" title="Arbour Square">Arbour Square</a>, the eastern side of Stepney Green, and the streets around Matlock Street.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-London_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-London-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timeout_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-timeout-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The area has not yet experienced the levels of <a href="/wiki/Gentrification" title="Gentrification">gentrification</a> seen in nearby <a href="/wiki/Bow,_London" title="Bow, London">Bow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wapping" title="Wapping">Wapping</a> and <a href="/wiki/Limehouse" title="Limehouse">Limehouse</a> but some redevelopment has taken place, including the former Arbour Square Police Station and the East End Mission building.</p>
<p>Stepney is roughly bounded by <a href="/wiki/Commercial_Road" title="Commercial Road">Commercial Road</a>, part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A13_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A13 road (Great Britain)">A13</a>, in the south, Mile End Road, part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A11_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A11 road (Great Britain)">A11</a>, in the north and the <a href="/wiki/Regent%27s_Canal" title="Regent's Canal">Regent's Canal</a> in the east. The western boundary with <a href="/wiki/Whitechapel" title="Whitechapel">Whitechapel</a> is rather ambiguous. It is administered by the <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Tower_Hamlets" title="London Borough of Tower Hamlets">London Borough of Tower Hamlets</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stepney&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As with most of the <a href="/wiki/East_End_of_London" title="East End of London">East End of London</a>, Stepney was sparsely populated marshland until the 19th century, when the development of London's docks and railways, combined with slum clearance, pushed the displaced poor and various immigrants looking for work into cheap housing being built in the area.</p>
<p>The first community developed around the church of <a href="/wiki/St_Dunstan%27s,_Stepney" title="St Dunstan's, Stepney">St Dunstan's</a>, which was founded in 923. Its name was recorded around 1000 AD as <i>Stybbanhyð</i>, "Stybba's landing-place". The <a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a> survey of 1086 gives the name as <i>Stibanhede</i> and says that the land was held by the <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_London" title="Bishop of London">Bishop of London</a> and was 32 <a href="/wiki/Hide_(unit)" title="Hide (unit)">hides</a> large, mainly used for ploughing, meadows, woodland for 500 pigs, and 4 mills. There were over 100 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serfs" title="Serfs">serfs</a>, split between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Villeins" title="Villeins">villeins</a> who ploughed the land, and <a href="/wiki/Serfdom#Cottagers" title="Serfdom">cottars</a> who assisted the villeins in return for a hut or cottage.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Bishop William held this land in demesne, in the manor of Stepney, on the day on which King Edward was alive and dead. In the same vill <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ranulph_Flambard" title="Ranulph Flambard">Ranulph Flambard</a> holds 3½ hides of the bishop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>The Manor of Stepney was held by the Bishop of London in compensation for his duties in maintaining and garrisoning the <a href="/wiki/Tower_of_London" title="Tower of London">Tower of London</a>. Further ecclesiastic holdings came about from the need to enclose the marshes and create flood defences along the Thames. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edward_VI" title="Edward VI">Edward VI</a> passed the land to the <a href="/wiki/Baron_Wentworth" title="Baron Wentworth">Wentworth</a> family, and thence to their descendants, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earl_of_Cleveland" title="Earl of Cleveland">Earls of Cleveland</a>. The ecclesiastic system of <a href="/wiki/Copyhold" title="Copyhold">copyhold</a>, whereby land was leased to tenants for terms as short as seven years, prevailed throughout the manor. This severely limited scope for improvement of the land and new building until the estate was broken up in the 19th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stepney_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stepney-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the early 20th century, Stepney was one of the most <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jewish" title="Jewish">Jewish</a> neighbourhoods in England;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> it was eventually replaced by <a href="/wiki/Stamford_Hill" title="Stamford Hill">Stamford Hill</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Sidney_Street" title="Siege of Sidney Street">Siege of Sidney Street</a> took place in Stepney in 1911.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stepney&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stepney formed a <a href="/wiki/Stepney_(parish)" title="Stepney (parish)">large ancient parish</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ossulstone" title="Ossulstone">Ossulstone</a> hundred of <a href="/wiki/Middlesex" title="Middlesex">Middlesex</a>; bounded by <a href="/wiki/Bromley-by-Bow" title="Bromley-by-Bow">Bromley</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Ham" title="West Ham">West Ham</a> to the east, the <a href="/wiki/River_Thames" title="River Thames">River Thames</a> to the south, <a href="/wiki/Shoreditch_(parish)" title="Shoreditch (parish)">Shoreditch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hackney_(parish)" title="Hackney (parish)">Hackney</a> to the north and the <a href="/wiki/City_of_London" title="City of London">City of London</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Liberties_of_the_Tower_of_London" title="Liberties of the Tower of London">Liberties of the Tower of London</a> to the west.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-early_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-early-8">[8]</a></sup> The parish included the hamlets of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mile_End_Old_Town" title="Mile End Old Town">Mile End Old Town</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mile_End_New_Town" title="Mile End New Town">Mile End New Town</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ratcliff" title="Ratcliff">Ratcliff</a>. At its early extent it additionally included <a href="/wiki/Whitechapel" title="Whitechapel">Whitechapel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wapping" title="Wapping">Wapping</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bow,_London" title="Bow, London">Stratford Bow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shadwell" title="Shadwell">Shadwell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spitalfields" title="Spitalfields">Spitalfields</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bethnal_Green" title="Bethnal Green">Bethnal Green</a>, <a href="/wiki/Limehouse" title="Limehouse">Limehouse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Poplar,_London" title="Poplar, London">Poplar</a>. Over time the parish was broken up with these settlements forming new independent parishes, leaving a residual parish of 830 acres (340 ha) comprising Mile End Old Town, Mile End New Town and Ratcliff.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-early_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-early-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Stepney is an episcopal area in the Anglican <a href="/wiki/Diocese_of_London" title="Diocese of London">Diocese of London</a>, which covers the London boroughs of <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Hackney" title="London Borough of Hackney">Hackney</a>, <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Islington" title="London Borough of Islington">Islington</a> and <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Tower_Hamlets" title="London Borough of Tower Hamlets">Tower Hamlets</a>, and has its own <a href="/wiki/Suffragan_bishop" title="Suffragan bishop">suffragan bishop</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough_of_Stepney" title="Metropolitan Borough of Stepney">Metropolitan Borough of Stepney</a> was formed in 1900, then dissolved in 1965 when it was absorbed into the newly created London Borough of Tower Hamlets which currently administers the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-London_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-London-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stepney&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stepney is located 3.6 miles (5.8 km) east north-east of <a href="/wiki/Charing_Cross" title="Charing Cross">Charing Cross</a>. It is roughly bounded by <a href="/wiki/Commercial_Road" title="Commercial Road">Commercial Road</a>, part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A13_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A13 road (Great Britain)">A13</a>, in the south, Mile End Road, part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A11_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A11 road (Great Britain)">A11</a>, in the north and the <a href="/wiki/Regent%27s_Canal" title="Regent's Canal">Regent's Canal</a> in the east. The Western Boundary with <a href="/wiki/Whitechapel" title="Whitechapel">Whitechapel</a> is rather ambiguous.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demography">Demography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stepney&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Demography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Due to cheap housing the <a href="/wiki/East_End_of_London" title="East End of London">East End of London</a> and Stepney has been home to various immigrants who contributed to the culture and history of the area, such as the <a href="/wiki/Huguenot" title="Huguenot">Huguenots</a> in the 17th century,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a> in the 18th century,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ashkenazi_Jews" title="Ashkenazi Jews">Ashkenazi Jews</a> fleeing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pogroms" title="Pogroms">pogroms</a> in Eastern Europe towards the end of the 19th century,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/British_Bangladeshi" title="British Bangladeshi">Bangladeshi</a> community settling in the East End from the 1960s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The area still contains a range of immigrants, particularly young Asian families, as well as elderly East Enders, some students, and the beginnings of a young middle class.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timeout_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-timeout-3">[3]</a></sup> Based on 2001 census, Bangladeshis were the largest ethnic group (43%), then the White British (39%).</p>
<p>In 2011, Stepney was 47% Bengali, the highest in Southern England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stepney_Green">Stepney Green</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stepney&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Stepney Green">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area is located in between Stepney, <a href="/wiki/Bethnal_Green" title="Bethnal Green">Bethnal Green</a>, <a href="/wiki/Whitechapel" title="Whitechapel">Whitechapel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mile_End" title="Mile End">Mile End</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Globe_Town" title="Globe Town">Globe Town</a></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="History_2">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stepney&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Stepney Green</b> developed as a street of residential housing off the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mile_End_Road" title="Mile End Road">Mile End Road</a> in the 15th century, and is now a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conservation_area" title="Conservation area">conservation area</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Conservation_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Conservation-16">[16]</a></sup> A brewery was founded in 1738 that developed into <a href="/wiki/Charrington_Brewery" title="Charrington Brewery">Charrington and Co.</a> in 1897. The brewery building, the Anchor Brewery, was on the north side of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mile_End_Road" title="Mile End Road">Mile End Road</a>, opposite Stepney Green; and is now the site of the Anchor Retail Unit, owned by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henderson_Global_Investors" title="Henderson Global Investors">Henderson Global Investors</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> though the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adams_House_(East_London)" title="Adams House (East London)">Brewery Offices</a> still remain on the corner of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mile_End_Road" title="Mile End Road">Mile End Road</a> and Cephas Avenue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Conservation_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-Conservation-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport_in_Stepney_Green">Transport in Stepney Green</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stepney&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Transport in Stepney Green">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Stepney_Green_tube_station" title="Stepney Green tube station">Stepney Green tube station</a> serves the area. <a href="/wiki/London_Buses" title="London Buses">London Buses</a> Routes 25, 205 and 309 and Night Route N205 serve the area. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cycle_Superhighway_CS3&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cycle Superhighway CS3 (page does not exist)">Cycle Superhighway CS3</a> passes the area on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mile_End_Road" title="Mile End Road">Mile End Road</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Landmarks">Landmarks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stepney&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Landmarks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/St_Dunstan%27s,_Stepney" title="St Dunstan's, Stepney">St Dunstan's</a>, founded in 923, is Stepney's oldest church. The present building dates principally from the 15th century. St Dunstan's has a long association with the sea, being responsible for registration of British maritime births, marriages and deaths until the 19th century. <a href="/wiki/Malplaquet_House" title="Malplaquet House">Malplaquet House</a> is a Grade II listed building.</p>
<p><a class="external text" href="http://www.stepneycityfarm.org/" rel="nofollow">Stepney City Farm</a> has been a feature in Stepney since opening as Stepping Stones Farm in 1979. A community resource, the farm aims to provide the people of East London a chance to experience rural life and engage in educational, environmental and creative projects. Stepney City farm is home to donkeys, cows, goats, pigs, sheep, ducks, geese, chickens, rabbits, ferrets and guinea pigs. As a working farm, eggs and vegetables are regularly sold.</p>

<p>In the northern part of the district, the nearest <a href="/wiki/List_of_London_Underground_stations" title="List of London Underground stations">London Underground stations</a> are <a href="/wiki/Mile_End_tube_station" title="Mile End tube station">Mile End</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stepney_Green_tube_station" title="Stepney Green tube station">Stepney Green</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Whitechapel_tube_station" title="Whitechapel tube station">Whitechapel</a>. All are on the <a href="/wiki/Hammersmith_%26_City_line" title="Hammersmith &amp; City line">Hammersmith &amp; City</a> and <a href="/wiki/District_line" title="District line">District line</a>. Mile End tube station is an interchange with the <a href="/wiki/Central_line_(London_Underground)" title="Central line (London Underground)">Central line</a>. Whitechapel tube station is also an interchange with the <a href="/wiki/London_Overground" title="London Overground">London Overground</a> <a href="/wiki/East_London_Line" title="East London Line">East London Line</a>.</p>
<p>In the southern part of the district, the nearest <a href="/wiki/List_of_Docklands_Light_Railway_stations" title="List of Docklands Light Railway stations">Docklands Light Railway station</a> is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Limehouse_DLR_station" title="Limehouse DLR station">Limehouse</a>. The station is also served by <a href="/wiki/C2c" title="C2c">c2c</a>, from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fenchurch_Street_station" title="Fenchurch Street station">Fenchurch Street station</a>. It was formerly known as Stepney East.</p>
<p>Many <a href="/wiki/London_Buses" title="London Buses">London Buses</a> Routes serve the area. Check {<a href="/wiki/Limehouse_station" title="Limehouse station">Connections</a>} at <a href="/wiki/Limehouse_station" title="Limehouse station">Limehouse station</a> and {<a href="/wiki/Stepney_Green_tube_station" title="Stepney Green tube station">Connections</a>} at <a href="/wiki/Stepney_Green_tube_station" title="Stepney Green tube station">Stepney Green tube station</a> for more information. 339 which runs in the heart of Stepney does not pass any of these stations.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people">Notable people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stepney&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Notable people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The great English physician <a href="/wiki/Richard_Mead" title="Richard Mead">Richard Mead</a>, responsible for advances in understanding <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transmissible_diseases" title="Transmissible diseases">transmissible diseases</a>, was born in Stepney. The entertainer <a href="/wiki/Des_O%27Connor" title="Des O'Connor">Des O'Connor</a> was born in Stepney,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> as were actors <a href="/wiki/Steven_Berkoff" title="Steven Berkoff">Steven Berkoff</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Terence_Stamp" title="Terence Stamp">Terence Stamp</a> and <a href="/wiki/Craig_Fairbrass" title="Craig Fairbrass">Craig Fairbrass</a>, playwright <a href="/wiki/Arnold_Wesker" title="Arnold Wesker">Arnold Wesker</a>, gardener and television presenter <a href="/wiki/Rachel_De_Thame" title="Rachel De Thame">Rachel De Thame</a>, television executive, presenter and former <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC1</a> and <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two" title="BBC Two">BBC2</a> controller <a href="/wiki/Alan_Yentob" title="Alan Yentob">Alan Yentob</a>, artist <a href="/wiki/Frank_Paton" title="Frank Paton">Frank Paton</a>, drummer <a href="/wiki/Kenney_Jones" title="Kenney Jones">Kenney Jones</a>, musician and writer <a href="/wiki/Jah_Wobble" title="Jah Wobble">Jah Wobble</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> singer/record producer <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Lynch" title="Kenny Lynch">Kenny Lynch</a> and his sister, also a singer, <a href="/wiki/Maxine_Daniels" title="Maxine Daniels">Maxine Daniels</a>, also singer <a href="/wiki/Charles_Coborn" title="Charles Coborn">Charles Coborn</a>. In sport, Stepney lays claim to <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballers</a> <a href="/wiki/Ledley_King" title="Ledley King">Ledley King</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Cole" title="Ashley Cole">Ashley Cole</a> and <a href="/wiki/Darren_Purse" title="Darren Purse">Darren Purse</a>, and heavyweight <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">boxer</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Thomas_Wells" title="William Thomas Wells">"Bombardier" Billy Wells</a>. Charlie Magri became world flyweight champion in 1983. He learnt his trade at the Arbour Youth Boxing Club in Stepney Green from the trainer, Jimmy Graham. Former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armed_robbery" title="Armed robbery">armed robber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bare-knuckle_boxing" title="Bare-knuckle boxing">bare-knuckle boxer</a> and businessman <a href="/wiki/Roy_Shaw" title="Roy Shaw">Roy Shaw</a> was born in Stepney, as was former British featherweight boxing champion <a href="/wiki/Sammy_McCarthy" title="Sammy McCarthy">Sammy McCarthy</a>, and sportswriter and author <a href="/wiki/Norman_Giller" title="Norman Giller">Norman Giller</a>, whilst clergymen <a href="/wiki/John_Sentamu" title="John Sentamu">John Sentamu</a>, formerly Bishop of Stepney, and Father Richard Wilson, founder of the <i>Hoppers' Hospitals</i> at <a href="/wiki/Five_Oak_Green" title="Five Oak Green">Five Oak Green</a>, Kent, lived in the borough at one time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Actors <a href="/wiki/John_Lyons_(actor)" title="John Lyons (actor)">John Lyons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Onwukwe" title="Ben Onwukwe">Ben Onwukwe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roy_Marsden" title="Roy Marsden">Roy Marsden</a> were born in Stepney, as was <a href="/wiki/EastEnders" title="EastEnders">EastEnders</a> actress <a href="/wiki/Anita_Dobson" title="Anita Dobson">Anita Dobson</a>. <a href="/wiki/Danny_Shea_(footballer)" title="Danny Shea (footballer)">Danny Shea</a>, the first footballer to be transferred for £2,000, was born in Wapping in 1887. <a href="/wiki/Monty_Norman" title="Monty Norman">Monty Norman</a> (The composer of the <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a> theme) was also born in Stepney. Actor <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Bresslaw" title="Bernard Bresslaw">Bernard Bresslaw</a> who starred in many popular <a href="/wiki/Carry_On_(franchise)" title="Carry On (franchise)">Carry On</a> films. <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Bart" title="Lionel Bart">Lionel Bart</a> known for creating the book, music and lyrics to the production <i><a href="/wiki/Oliver!" title="Oliver!">Oliver!</a></i>.</p>

<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rolling_Stones" title="Rolling Stones">Rolling Stones</a>' song <i><a href="/wiki/Play_with_Fire_(The_Rolling_Stones_song)" title="Play with Fire (The Rolling Stones song)">Play with Fire</a></i>, the lyrics detail remarks made by the protagonist (singer) to a woman with whom he is having a dalliance. The woman is told to view her situation in comparison to her mother, who went from living extravagantly to living among the poor: "Now she gets her kicks in Stepney/Not in Knightsbridge anymore." Earlier in the song it is indicated that the woman's father owned "a block in St. John's Wood," another pricey neighbourhood in London. Living in Stepney is used as an indicator for the woman's descent from privilege.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>'s partly autobiographical album <a href="/wiki/Captain_Fantastic_and_the_Brown_Dirt_Cowboy" title="Captain Fantastic and the Brown Dirt Cowboy">Captain Fantastic and the Brown Dirt Cowboy</a>, the song "Bitter Fingers" contains the lyrics "When I worked the good old pubs in Stepney", referring to his early days as a musician in and around London.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Sondheim" title="Stephen Sondheim">Stephen Sondheim</a> musical <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Overtures" title="Pacific Overtures">Pacific Overtures</a>, the song "Pretty Lady" is sung by three British Sailors who lament at being "a million miles away from Stepney Green."</p>
<p>In her 2002 memoir <a href="/wiki/Call_the_Midwife_(book)" title="Call the Midwife (book)"><i>Call the Midwife</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Worth" title="Jennifer Worth">Jennifer Worth</a> writes a graphic account of 1950's Stepney at the height of its urban decay describing bombsites, condemned buildings, filth, and rampant prostitution.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stepney_Historical_Trust">Stepney Historical Trust</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stepney&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Stepney Historical Trust">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Stepney Historical Trust was set up in 1989 to advance the public's education in the history of Stepney and the surrounding areas. It is based in the London Dockers Athletic and Social Club<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> and has installed a series of plaques on sites of historic interest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
