<p>"<b>Beep!!</b>" is a song by Japanese musical act <a href="/wiki/Superfly_(band)" title="Superfly (band)">Superfly</a>. It was released as a double A-side single along with "<a href="/wiki/Sunshine_Sunshine" title="Sunshine Sunshine">Sunshine Sunshine</a>" in 2011, and was used as the theme song for the Hiroshi Shinagawa film <i>Manzai Gang</i> (2011).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_development">Background and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beep!!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In March 2010, Superfly released <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wildflower_%26_Cover_Songs:_Complete_Best_%27Track_3%27" title="Wildflower &amp; Cover Songs: Complete Best 'Track 3'">Wildflower &amp; Cover Songs: Complete Best 'Track 3'</a></i>, a two-CD set featuring a four-track single and a full-length <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cover_album" title="Cover album">cover album</a> of classic rock songs. It was led by the song "<a href="/wiki/Wildflower_(Superfly_song)" title="Wildflower (Superfly song)">Wildflower</a>", the theme song for the drama <i><a href="/wiki/Gold_(TV_series)" title="Gold (TV series)">Gold</a></i>, and also featured "<a href="/wiki/Tamashii_Revolution" title="Tamashii Revolution">Tamashii Revolution</a>", a song used to promote the <a href="/wiki/NHK" title="NHK">NHK</a> broadcast of the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Both songs were commercially successful, with "Wildflower" becoming certified Platinum for downloads by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_Japan" title="Recording Industry Association of Japan">Recording Industry Association of Japan</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RIAJ-jan2014digi_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-RIAJ-jan2014digi-2">[2]</a></sup> and "Tamashii Revolution" certified platinum twice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RIAJ-jul2011digi_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-RIAJ-jul2011digi-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RIAJ-jun2012digi_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-RIAJ-jun2012digi-4">[4]</a></sup> This was followed by "<a href="/wiki/Eyes_on_Me_(Superfly_song)" title="Eyes on Me (Superfly song)">Eyes on Me</a>", a ballad single released in December used as the theme song for the <a href="/wiki/Square_Enix" title="Square Enix">Square Enix</a> shooting game <i><a href="/wiki/The_3rd_Birthday" title="The 3rd Birthday">The 3rd Birthday</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>"Beep!!" was written before "Sunshine Sunshine". Director Hiroshi Shinagawa wanted a Superfly song similar to the "high-paced feel" of "<a href="/wiki/Alright!!_(Superfly_song)" title="Alright!! (Superfly song)">Alright!!</a>" (2009) for the theme song for his film <i>Manzai Gang</i> (2011), so asked Ochi to write the theme song. Ochi was inspired to write about feelings of change after reading the script for the film and meeting with Shinagawa. Conceptually, the song was recorded with the style of American rock band <a href="/wiki/The_Knack" title="The Knack">The Knack</a> as a basis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EMTG_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-EMTG-6">[6]</a></sup> Ochi wrote the lyrics together with musician Jam. At the time Ochi had wanted to write tender love songs such as "Eyes on Me", so had trouble mentally creating the lyrics. Together they wrote the song from the perspective of a big-sister like character, much like the lyrics of Superfly's song "<a href="/wiki/Manifesto_(Superfly_song)" title="Manifesto (Superfly song)">Manifesto</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EMTG_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-EMTG-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion_and_release">Promotion and release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beep!!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Promotion and release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Beep!!" was announced as the theme song for the Hiroshi Shinagawa film <i>Manzai Gang</i> in late December, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Superfly intended to perform "Beep!!" live at <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Station" title="Music Station">Music Station</a></i> on March 11, 2011, however the program was cancelled due to the <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The song was released as a single also featuring Superfly's song "Sunshine Sunshine" as the other A-side. The limited edition version of the single featured an additional DVD, with a performance from the Superfly &amp; The Lemon Bats Special Live "Rock'N'Roll Muncher", a three-date tour in November 2010 commissioned by <i>Switch!</i> music magazine to celebrate the magazine's 25th anniversary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The Lemon Bats are a group formed by Superfly, featuring <a href="/wiki/Tatsuya_Nakamura" title="Tatsuya Nakamura">Tatsuya Nakamura</a> of Losalios, Kōichi Tsutaya, Kazuhiro Momo of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mo%27Some_Tonebender&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mo'Some Tonebender (page does not exist)">Mo'Some Tonebender</a>, Yoshiyuki Yatsuhashi and Hidekazu Hinata of <a href="/wiki/Straightener_(band)" title="Straightener (band)">Straightener</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DVDdetails_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-DVDdetails-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beep!!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A music video was produced for the song, directed by Wataru Saitō.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The video begins in a bedroom set, featuring an indoor apple tree. Further scenes depict vocalist Shiho Ochi outside turning a fake pig on a spit, driving in a car surrounded by police men and her walking along train tracks. The police men are featured in later scenes shot at night, where they perform a dance routine.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beep!!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Haruna Takekawa of <i>Hot Express</i> praised the song's heavy, simple riffs and edgy guitar sound, and noted Ochi's voice was powerful and full. She felt that the song had strong imagery of a woman with long hair driving wildly in a convertible. She noted that both "Sunshine Sunshine" and "Beep!!" were much more simple than her previous songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> <i>CDJournal</i> reviewers described the song as a "wild rock tune" centred on hard rock-style guitar riffs. They praised Superfly's vocals as "excellent" and being the heart of the song, and felt that sound developed by the intertwining drums and guitars was "thrilling".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Tomoki Takahashi of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rockin%27_On_Japan" title="Rockin' On Japan">Rockin' On Japan</a></i> focused on the song's powerful chorus, and praised Ochi's vocals. He felt that the song showed Ochi as a strong-willed person, and was indicative of her ever-changing style of music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
