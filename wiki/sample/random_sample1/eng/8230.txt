<p><b>Stoya</b> (born 1986<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iafd_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-iafd-2">[2]</a></sup>) is an Serbian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pornographic_actor" title="Pornographic actor">pornographic actress</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Model_(profession)" title="Model (profession)">model</a>, regarded as <a href="/wiki/Digital_Playground" title="Digital Playground">Digital Playground's</a> first <a href="/wiki/Alt_porn" title="Alt porn">alt porn</a> contract girl. She is co-owner of pornographic web site TRENCHCOATx,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trenchcoat_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-trenchcoat-5">[5]</a></sup> as well as a writer, having written for publications including <a href="/wiki/Vice" title="Vice">Vice</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a> and <a href="/wiki/Esquire" title="Esquire">Esquire</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stoya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stoya was born in <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_North_Carolina" title="Wilmington, North Carolina">Wilmington, North Carolina</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rog_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rog-6">[6]</a></sup> to a <a href="/wiki/Serbs" title="Serbs">Serbian</a> mother and a <a href="/wiki/Scottish_people" title="Scottish people">Scottish</a> father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Her mother named her after <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Savitch" title="Jessica Savitch">Jessica Savitch</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes20150309_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes20150309-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a child, she wanted to become a dancer, starting dance lessons at the age of three. She was <a href="/wiki/Homeschooling" title="Homeschooling">home-schooled</a> and got her high-school diploma before the age of sixteen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tna_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-tna-8">[8]</a></sup> Because her father worked in <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">IT</a>, Stoya had access to a lot of electronics and gaming equipment, which she says developed her love for technology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Technically_Philly_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Technically_Philly-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>"I was three when I was using <a href="/wiki/DOS" title="DOS">DOS</a>. My mom taught me how to read, and my dad taught me how to navigate DOS", she has said.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Technically_Philly_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Technically_Philly-9">[9]</a></sup> At a later age, Stoya moved to <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a>, where she spent a semester at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Delaware_College_of_Art_%26_Design" title="Delaware College of Art &amp; Design">Delaware College of Art &amp; Design</a>, but she later dropped out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tna_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-tna-8">[8]</a></sup> After relocating to <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, she attended a summer program at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UArts" title="UArts">UArts</a>. Some of her jobs in Philadelphia included being a <a href="/wiki/Secretary" title="Secretary">secretary</a>, flier distributor, and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Go-go_dancer" title="Go-go dancer">go-go dancer</a>. Stoya appeared in several <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music videos</a> for bands that, according to her, "no one will ever hear of."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fanzine_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-fanzine-3">[3]</a></sup> In May 2009, Stoya said that she would be moving from Philadelphia and relocating to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> in the fall of that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Technically_Philly_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-Technically_Philly-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_life">Professional life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stoya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> "Stoya" was her nickname before appearing in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adult_film" title="Adult film">adult films</a>, and it is the shortened version of her grandmother's last name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fanzine_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-fanzine-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Stoya began posing for adult pictures for a friend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes20150309_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes20150309-1">[1]</a></sup> That eventually led her to modelling and working for <a href="/wiki/Alt_porn" title="Alt porn">alt-erotic</a> websites.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tna_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-tna-8">[8]</a></sup> One of the websites she had worked for asked if she was willing to do a <a href="/wiki/Softcore_pornography" title="Softcore pornography">softcore</a> scene for a <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> they were putting out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fanzine_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-fanzine-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Stoya claims that she discovered <a href="/wiki/Pornography" title="Pornography">pornography</a> through an interest in <a href="/wiki/BDSM" title="BDSM">BDSM</a> and via fetish newsgroups on the Internet. She has stated that few adult films interested her and that she prefers those with artistic and fetish themes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Stoya was featured in two DVD productions for Razordolls, and she made a non-sex, <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">cameo appearance</a> in two <a href="/wiki/Vivid_Entertainment#Vivid_and_Alt-Porn" title="Vivid Entertainment">Vivid Alt</a> titles<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-debut_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-debut-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afdbnote_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-afdbnote-12">[12]</a></sup> before being contacted by <a href="/wiki/Digital_Playground" title="Digital Playground">Digital Playground</a> with a proposition to perform in a <a href="/wiki/Hardcore_pornography" title="Hardcore pornography">hardcore</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lesbian_pornographic_film" title="Lesbian pornographic film">lesbian scene</a> with Sophia Santi. The scene in question never happened, but, in August 2007, she met with several Digital Playground representatives and was asked if she would star in a <a href="/wiki/Hardcore_pornography" title="Hardcore pornography">pornographic movie</a> with male talent. After careful consideration, Stoya agreed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fanzine_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-fanzine-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2007, Digital Playground signed her to an exclusive, three-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Stoya is regarded as the company's first <a href="/wiki/Alt_porn" title="Alt porn">alt porn</a> contract girl.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stroud_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-stroud-14">[14]</a></sup> The first scene that she shot for them was for <i>Stoya Video Nasty</i> (promoted on the DVD box as the first film featuring her engaging in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heterosexual_intercourse" title="Heterosexual intercourse">heterosexual intercourse</a>), but her first movie released by the company was <i>Jack's POV 9</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fanzine_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-fanzine-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-debut_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-debut-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2009, Stoya appeared in her first mainstream crossover project as <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kamikazi_Shegun_5000&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kamikazi Shegun 5000 (page does not exist)">Kamikazi Shegun 5000</a> in the award winning <a href="/wiki/48_Hour_Film_Project" title="48 Hour Film Project">48 Hour Film Project</a> "The Kingpin of Pain".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2013, Stoya was featured in a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Village_Voice" title="Village Voice">Village Voice</a></i> cover story issue profiling her career as well as her life in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hndh_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-hndh-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Even though Stoya was under exclusive contract with Digital Playground, <a href="/wiki/Evil_Angel_(studio)" title="Evil Angel (studio)">Evil Angel</a> director <a href="/wiki/John_Stagliano" title="John Stagliano">John Stagliano</a> received "special permission" in 2013 to cast her in the sequel for the adult film series <i>Voracious</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> As of 2014, she left Digital Playground and is now focusing her career on directing. She is financing her first film to be shot in February 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Stoya appears as a main cast member in the 2016 mainstream lesbian fantasy series <i><a href="/wiki/Dagger_Kiss" title="Dagger Kiss">Dagger Kiss</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>She and <a href="/wiki/Kayden_Kross" title="Kayden Kross">Kayden Kross</a> created the pay-per-scene pornographic website TRENCHCOATx.
