<p><b>Stoke Mandeville</b> is a village and also a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> within <a href="/wiki/Aylesbury_Vale" title="Aylesbury Vale">Aylesbury Vale</a> district 3 miles (4.8 km) to the south-east of <a href="/wiki/Aylesbury" title="Aylesbury">Aylesbury</a> in the county of <a href="/wiki/Buckinghamshire" title="Buckinghamshire">Buckinghamshire</a>, England. Although a separate civil parish, the village falls within the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aylesbury_Urban_Area" title="Aylesbury Urban Area">Aylesbury Urban Area</a>. According to the Census Report the area of this parish is 1,460 acres (5.9 km<sup>2</sup>).</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Stoke_Mandeville_Hospital" title="Stoke Mandeville Hospital">Stoke Mandeville Hospital</a>, although named after the village, is located on the <a href="/wiki/Parish" title="Parish">parish</a>'s border with <a href="/wiki/Aylesbury" title="Aylesbury">Aylesbury</a>, has the largest spinal injuries ward in Europe, and is the birthplace of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paralympics" title="Paralympics">Paralympic</a> movement.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stoke_Mandeville&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The village was originally recorded as <i>Stoches</i> in the <a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a> of 1086, from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_English_language" title="Old English language">Old English</a> word <i>stoc</i> meaning an outlying <a href="/wiki/Farm" title="Farm">farm</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hamlet_(UK_place)" title="Hamlet (UK place)">hamlet</a>. The suffix Mandeville was first recorded in 1284 when the <a href="/wiki/Manorialism" title="Manorialism">manor</a> was listed as being in the hands of the powerful Norman de Mandeville family. The former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medieval" title="Medieval">medieval</a> parish church on the outskirts of the village was condemned in the mid 20th Century and was demolished in January 1966. The newer red brick parish church of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mary,_the_mother_of_Jesus" title="Mary, the mother of Jesus">St Mary</a>, consecrated in July 1866 by Bishop of Oxford Samuel Wilberforce, remains as the only church in the village apart from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Methodist" title="Methodist">Methodist</a> church in Eskdale Road.</p>
<p>Stoke Mandeville was also the location of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stoke_Mandeville_Games" title="Stoke Mandeville Games">Stoke Mandeville Games</a>, which first took place in 1948 thanks to doctor <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_Guttmann" title="Ludwig Guttmann">Ludwig Guttmann</a> and are now known as the <a href="/wiki/IWAS_World_Games" title="IWAS World Games">IWAS World Games</a>. The Games, which were held eight times at Stoke Mandeville, were the inspiration for the first <a href="/wiki/Paralympic_Games" title="Paralympic Games">Paralympic Games</a>, also called <i>The Stoke Mandeville Games</i>, which were organised in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> in 1960. The wheelchair aspects of the <a href="/wiki/1984_Summer_Paralympics" title="1984 Summer Paralympics">1984 Paralympics</a> were also held in the village. The London <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Paralympics" title="2012 Summer Paralympics">2012 Summer Paralympics</a> mascot, <a href="/wiki/Wenlock_and_Mandeville" title="Wenlock and Mandeville">Mandeville</a>, was named after the village due to its legacy with the Games. <a href="/wiki/Stoke_Mandeville_Stadium" title="Stoke Mandeville Stadium">Stoke Mandeville Stadium</a> was developed alongside the hospital and is the National Centre for Disability Sport in the United Kingdom, enhancing the hospital as a world centre for paraplegics and spinal injuries.</p>
<p>On 13 May 2000, the new Stoke Mandeville Millennium sign<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> was unveiled. It stands on a small brick <a href="/wiki/Plinth" title="Plinth">plinth</a> on the green outside the <a href="/wiki/Primary_school" title="Primary school">primary school</a>. The sign shows aspects of village life over the centuries.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stoke_Mandeville&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Stoke_Mandeville_railway_station" title="Stoke Mandeville railway station">Stoke Mandeville railway station</a> is also a station stop on the <a href="/wiki/London_to_Aylesbury_Line" title="London to Aylesbury Line">London to Aylesbury Line</a> between <a href="/wiki/Aylesbury_railway_station" title="Aylesbury railway station">Aylesbury station</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wendover_railway_station" title="Wendover railway station">Wendover station</a>, served by <a href="/wiki/Chiltern_Railways" title="Chiltern Railways">Chiltern Railways</a>, which terminates at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aylesbury_Vale_Parkway" title="Aylesbury Vale Parkway">Aylesbury Vale Parkway</a> northbound and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London_Marylebone" title="London Marylebone">London Marylebone</a> southbound.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arriva_the_Shires_and_Essex" title="Arriva the Shires and Essex">Arriva</a> service 50 serves the area on Station Road, as do services 164 and 300 - which stops in the centre of the village.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stoke_Mandeville&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stoke Mandeville Combined School is a mixed <a href="/wiki/Community_school_(England_and_Wales)" title="Community school (England and Wales)">community school</a> which takes children from the age of four through to the age of eleven. The school has approximately 220 pupils. It also has a hearing impaired department, which currently helps up to 15 children through their school day.</p>
