<p><b>Stockton-on-Tees</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Borough_status_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Borough status in the United Kingdom">borough</a> by prescription <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authorities_of_England" title="Unitary authorities of England">unitary authority area</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tees_Valley" title="Tees Valley">Tees Valley</a> region situated in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_East" title="North East">North East</a> of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, with a population of 191,600 shown in the 2011 census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The borough of Stockton-on-Tees consists of the market town of <a href="/wiki/Stockton-on-Tees" title="Stockton-on-Tees">Stockton</a> (population 82,880), and the smaller outlying settlements of <a href="/wiki/Billingham" title="Billingham">Billingham</a> (pop. 36,720) and <a href="/wiki/Thornaby-on-Tees" title="Thornaby-on-Tees">Thornaby-on-Tees</a> (pop. 23,200), including <a href="/wiki/Ingleby_Barwick" title="Ingleby Barwick">Ingleby Barwick</a> (pop.16,280). <a href="/wiki/Durham_Tees_Valley_Airport" title="Durham Tees Valley Airport">Durham Tees Valley Airport</a> is also partly within the borough. The Stockton-on-Tees borough accounts for the largest number of residents within the <a href="/wiki/Teesside" title="Teesside">Teesside</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hartlepool" title="Hartlepool">Hartlepool</a> urban area.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Borough_of_Stockton-on-Tees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Stockton_Borough_and_Tees.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="329" data-file-width="312" height="105" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/3d/Stockton_Borough_and_Tees.PNG/100px-Stockton_Borough_and_Tees.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/3d/Stockton_Borough_and_Tees.PNG/150px-Stockton_Borough_and_Tees.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/3d/Stockton_Borough_and_Tees.PNG/200px-Stockton_Borough_and_Tees.PNG 2x" width="100"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Stockton_Borough_and_Tees.PNG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The borough with the River Tees shown</div>

<p>The core of the town was anciently in <a href="/wiki/County_Durham" title="County Durham">County Durham</a>, but the borough spilled over the river into <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>. The borough was formed on 1 April 1974, from the Stockton part of Teesside <a href="/wiki/County_borough" title="County borough">county borough</a>, along with part of <i>Stockton Rural District</i> in <a href="/wiki/County_Durham" title="County Durham">County Durham</a> and part of <a href="/wiki/Stokesley_Rural_District" title="Stokesley Rural District">Stokesley Rural District</a> from the <a href="/wiki/North_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="North Riding of Yorkshire">North Riding</a>. At that time it was designated a <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">non-metropolitan district</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cleveland,_England" title="Cleveland, England">Cleveland</a>.</p>
<p>It became a <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authority</a> on 1 April 1996 and part of the <a href="/wiki/Tees_Valley" title="Tees Valley">Tees Valley</a> region. For ceremonial purposes the borough is split between County Durham and North Yorkshire, along the line of the <a href="/wiki/River_Tees" title="River Tees">River Tees</a> as shown in the map (left) with County Durham to the north and North Yorkshire to the south. It is the only council area in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> to be split between two ceremonial counties.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Council">Council</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Borough_of_Stockton-on-Tees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Council">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stockton-on-Tees_local_elections" title="Stockton-on-Tees local elections">Stockton-on-Tees local elections</a></div>
<p>The Borough has 26 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ward_(country_subdivision)" title="Ward (country subdivision)">wards</a> with either one, two or three Councillors representing each. There are 56 Councillors in total in the Borough of Stockton. Following the elections that took place in May 2015, 32 Councillors are Labour, 13 Conservative, 5 Ingleby Barwick Independent Society, 3 Thornaby Independent Association, 2 West Words and 1 Liberal Democrat .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_Nature_Reserves">Local Nature Reserves</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Borough_of_Stockton-on-Tees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Local Nature Reserves">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The council maintains a number of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Local_Nature_Reserve" title="Local Nature Reserve">Local Nature Reserves</a> including Barwick Pond, Charlton's Pond, Greenvale, Hardwick Dene and Elm Tree Woods, Norton Grange Marsh, <a href="/wiki/Quarry_Wood,_Stockton-on-Tees" title="Quarry Wood, Stockton-on-Tees">Quarry Wood</a> (Eaglescliffe) and Stillington Forest Park.</p>
