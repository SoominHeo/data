<p>The <b>Age of the Sturlungs</b> or the <b>Sturlung Era</b> (<a href="/wiki/Icelandic_language" title="Icelandic language">Icelandic</a>: <span lang="is" xml:lang="is"><i>Sturlungaöld</i></span>) was a 42–44 year period of internal strife in mid-13th century <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>. It may have been the bloodiest and most violent period in Icelandic history. It is documented in the <a href="/wiki/Sturlunga_saga" title="Sturlunga saga">Sturlunga saga</a>.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:VassalToTheKing-Skardsbok.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="436" data-file-width="388" height="225" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/VassalToTheKing-Skardsbok.jpg/200px-VassalToTheKing-Skardsbok.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/VassalToTheKing-Skardsbok.jpg/300px-VassalToTheKing-Skardsbok.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/74/VassalToTheKing-Skardsbok.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:VassalToTheKing-Skardsbok.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A man swears vassalage to the King of Norway. From the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Skar%C3%B0sb%C3%B3k&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Skarðsbók (page does not exist)">Skarðsbók</a> manuscript</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>This period is marked by the conflicts of powerful chieftains, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Go%C3%B0i" title="Goði">goðar</a></i>, who amassed followers and did battle, and is named for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sturlungs" title="Sturlungs">Sturlungs</a>, the most powerful family clan in Iceland at the time. At the end of the era, the <a href="/wiki/Icelandic_Commonwealth" title="Icelandic Commonwealth">Icelandic Commonwealth</a> ceased to exist and Iceland became a vassal of <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>.</p>
<p>Historians generally regard the year 1220 as the first year of the Age of the Sturlungs, although some wish to place its beginning at an earlier date because of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_V%C3%AD%C3%B0ines" title="Battle of Víðines">Battle of Víðines</a>. Power in the country had consolidated within the grasp of a few family clans. They were:</p>
<ul>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Haukd%C3%A6lir" title="Haukdælir">Haukdælir</a>, of Árnesþing</li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oddaverjar" title="Oddaverjar">Oddaverjar</a>, of Rangárþing</li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%81sbirnings" title="Ásbirnings">Ásbirningar</a>, of <a href="/wiki/Skagafj%C3%B6r%C3%B0ur" title="Skagafjörður">Skagafjörður</a></li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vatnsfir%C3%B0ings" title="Vatnsfirðings">Vatnsfirðingar</a> of <a href="/wiki/%C3%8Dsafj%C3%B6r%C3%B0ur" title="Ísafjörður">Ísafjörður</a></li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sv%C3%ADnfellings" title="Svínfellings">Svínfellingar</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Region_(Iceland)" title="Eastern Region (Iceland)">Eastern Region</a></li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sturlungs" title="Sturlungs">Sturlungar</a>, of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hvammur&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hvammur (page does not exist)">Hvammur</a> in Dalir</li>
</ul>
<p>At this time, <a href="/wiki/Haakon_IV_of_Norway" title="Haakon IV of Norway">Hákon the Old</a>, King of Norway, was trying to extend his influence in Iceland. Many Icelandic chieftains became his <a href="/wiki/Vassal" title="Vassal">vassals</a> and were obliged to do his bidding—in exchange they received gifts, followers and a status of respect. Consequently, the greatest Icelandic chieftains were soon affiliated with the King of Norway in one way or the other.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Go.C3.B0ar"><i>Goðar</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Age_of_the_Sturlungs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Goðar">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Icelandic_Commonwealth" title="Icelandic Commonwealth">Icelandic Commonwealth</a>, power was mostly in the hands of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Go%C3%B0i" title="Goði"><i>goðar</i></a> (local chieftains). Iceland was effectively divided into <a href="/wiki/Farthings_of_Iceland" title="Farthings of Iceland">farthings</a> (quarters). Within each farthing were nine <i>Goði</i>-dominions (<i>"Goðorð"</i>). The North farthing had an additional three dominions due to its size. All in all they were 39.</p>
<p>The <i>Goði</i>-chieftains protected the farmers in their territory, and exacted compensation or vengeance if their followers' rights were violated. In exchange, the farmers pledged their support to the <i>Goði</i>, both by voting in his favor in the <i><a href="/wiki/Althing" title="Althing">Alþingi</a></i> parliament and (if needed) by taking up arms against his enemies.</p>
<p>The powers of the <i>Goði</i>-chieftains, however, were neither permanent nor inherited. This status came about by a combination of respect, honour, influence and wealth. The chieftains constantly had to demonstrate their qualities as leaders, either by giving gifts to their followers or by holding great feasts. If the chieftain was seen as failing in any respect, his followers could simply choose another, more qualified <i>Goði</i> to support.</p>
<p>The greatest chieftains of the 12th and 13th century started amassing great wealth, and subsuming lesser dominions. This may be one of the causes of the civil war.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Course_of_events">Course of events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Age_of_the_Sturlungs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Course of events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Beginning">Beginning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Age_of_the_Sturlungs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Beginning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Age of Sturlungs began in 1220, when <a href="/wiki/Snorri_Sturluson" title="Snorri Sturluson">Snorri Sturluson</a>, chieftain of the Sturlung clan and one of the great <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Icelandic_saga" title="Icelandic saga">Icelandic saga</a> writers, became a vassal of <a href="/wiki/Haakon_IV_of_Norway" title="Haakon IV of Norway">King Hákon of Norway</a>. The king insisted that Snorri help him bring Iceland under the sovereignty of Norway. Snorri returned home, and although he soon became the country's most powerful chieftain, he did little to enforce the king's will. According to one historian, "we do not know whether [Snorri's] inactivity was due to lack of will or his conviction that the case was hopeless".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1235, Snorri's nephew <a href="/wiki/Sturla_Sighvatsson" title="Sturla Sighvatsson">Sturla Sighvatsson</a> also accepted vassalage under the king. Sturla was more aggressive: He sent his uncle back to Norway, and started warring on the chieftains who refused to accept the king's demands. However, Sturla and his father Sighvatur were soundly defeated by <a href="/wiki/Gissur_%C3%9Eorvaldsson" title="Gissur Þorvaldsson">Gissur Þorvaldsson</a>, the chief of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Haukd%C3%A6lir" title="Haukdælir">Haukdælir</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kolbeinn_the_young" title="Kolbeinn the young">Kolbeinn the young</a>, chief of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%81sbirnings" title="Ásbirnings">Ásbirnings</a>, in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%96rlygssta%C3%B0ir&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Örlygsstaðir (page does not exist)">Örlygsstaðir</a> in <a href="/wiki/Skagafj%C3%B6r%C3%B0ur" title="Skagafjörður">Skagafjörður</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_%C3%96rlygssta%C3%B0ir" title="Battle of Örlygsstaðir">Battle of Örlygsstaðir</a> was the largest armed conflict in the history of Iceland—the Sturlungs (Sturla Sighvatsson) had 1000 armed men and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%81sbirnings" title="Ásbirnings">Ásbirningar</a> (Kolbeinn the young) had 1200 armed men. More than 50 people were killed. After this crushing defeat, Gissur and Kolbeinn became the most powerful chieftains in the country.</p>
<p>Snorri Sturluson returned home to Iceland, having fallen out of favor with the king due to his support for Earl Skúli in an attempted coup. Gissur Þorvaldsson, also a vassal of the Norwegian king, received instructions that Snorri be killed. In 1241, Gissur went with many men to Snorri's home and murdered him. Snorri's last words are said to have been "<i>Eigi skal höggva!</i>" (English: "Do not strike!").</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id=".C3.9E.C3.B3r.C3.B0ur_kakali_stirs_up_trouble">Þórður kakali stirs up trouble</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Age_of_the_Sturlungs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Þórður kakali stirs up trouble">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>A year later, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%9E%C3%B3r%C3%B0ur_Sighvatsson" title="Þórður Sighvatsson">Þórður kakali Sighvatsson</a> (the nickname <i>kakali</i> probably means "The Stutterer"), son of Sighvatur, Snorri's brother, returned home to Iceland from abroad. He had cause for vengeance, for his brothers and father had fallen in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_%C3%96rlygssta%C3%B0ir" title="Battle of Örlygsstaðir">Battle of Örlygsstaðir</a>. He soon showed himself to be a formidable tactician and leader. Four years later, the rule of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%81sbirnings" title="Ásbirnings">Ásbirnings</a> was effectively over, after fierce battles with Þórður. The battles <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fl%C3%B3abardagi" title="Flóabardagi">Flóabardagi</a> (1244 – the only naval battle in Icelandic history with Icelanders on both sides) and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Haugsnes" title="Battle of Haugsnes">Battle of Haugsnes</a> (1246 – the bloodiest battle in Icelandic history with about 110 fatalities) both take place during this period.</p>
<p>Þórður kakali and Gissur Þorvaldsson, however, did not fight each other. Both were vassals of the king of Norway, and they appealed to him as dispute mediator. The king decided in favor of Þórður and from 1247–1250 Þórður ruled Iceland almost alone. He died in Norway, six years later.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gissur_returns_and_the_Commonwealth_ends">Gissur returns and the Commonwealth ends</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Age_of_the_Sturlungs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Gissur returns and the Commonwealth ends">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1252 the king sent Gissur to Iceland. The followers of Þórður kakali were displeased and tried to kill him by putting his residence in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Flugum%C3%BDri&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Flugumýri (page does not exist)">Flugumýri</a> in <a href="/wiki/Skagafj%C3%B6r%C3%B0ur" title="Skagafjörður">Skagafjörður</a> to the torch—this incident is known as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flugum%C3%BDrarbrenna" title="Flugumýrarbrenna">Flugumýrarbrenna</a></i> ("The Flugumýri Arson"). Despite his influence and power, Gissur was unable to find the leader of the arsonists, and was forced to return to Norway in 1254 to bear the censure of the king, who was displeased with his failure in bringing Iceland under the Norwegian throne.</p>
<p>Minor conflicts continued throughout Iceland. Meanwhile, Gissur was given the title of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jarl_(title)" title="Jarl (title)">Jarl</a> and sent home to negotiate. Only when the king had sent his special emissary, Hallvarður gullskór, did the Icelanders agree on Norwegian kingship. The Commonwealth came to an end with the signing of the <i><a href="/wiki/Old_Covenant_(Iceland)" title="Old Covenant (Iceland)">Gamli sáttmáli</a></i> ("Old Covenant") agreement in 1264.</p>
