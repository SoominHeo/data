<p><i><b>The Strad</b></i> is a UK-based monthly <a href="/wiki/Classical_music" title="Classical music">classical music</a> <a href="/wiki/Magazine" title="Magazine">magazine</a> about <a href="/wiki/String_instrument" title="String instrument">string instruments</a>, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cellist" title="Cellist">cellists</a>, <a href="/wiki/Violin" title="Violin">violinists</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Violist" title="Violist">violists</a> for amateur and professional musicians. Founded in 1889, the magazine provides information, photographs and reviews of instruments, related feature articles and news, and information about musical concerts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Taggart2010_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Taggart2010-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The magazine offers practical advice on technique, profiles of leading performers, master classes by some of the great teachers in the world and the craft of instrument making such as <a href="/wiki/Luthier" title="Luthier">luthiers</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It also includes articles about <a href="/wiki/Orchestra" title="Orchestra">orchestras</a> and music schools.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The first issue was released in June 1890.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Faber2012_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Faber2012-3">[3]</a></sup> It is now<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (November 2016)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> owned by <a href="/wiki/Newsquest" title="Newsquest">Newsquest</a> and edited by Chloe Cutts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Taggart2010_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Taggart2010-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
