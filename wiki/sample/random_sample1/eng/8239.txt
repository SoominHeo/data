<p><b><a href="/wiki/Baron" title="Baron">Baron</a> Wolfgang von Strucker</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_character" title="Fictional character">fictional</a> <a href="/wiki/Supervillain" title="Supervillain">supervillain</a> appearing in <a href="/wiki/American_comic_book" title="American comic book">American comic books</a> published by <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a>.</p>
<p>Baron Strucker, a former Nazi officer, is one of the leaders of <a href="/wiki/Hydra_(comics)" title="Hydra (comics)">Hydra</a> and an enemy of <a href="/wiki/S.H.I.E.L.D." title="S.H.I.E.L.D.">S.H.I.E.L.D.</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Avengers_(comics)" title="Avengers (comics)">Avengers</a>, and the interests of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">United States of America</a> and of the free world in general. He has been physically augmented to be nearly ageless. He has been seemingly killed in the past only to return to plague the world with schemes of world domination and <a href="/wiki/Genocide" title="Genocide">genocide</a>, time and time again. The character has been portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Campbell_Lane" title="Campbell Lane">Campbell Lane</a> in the 1998 TV film <i><a href="/wiki/Nick_Fury:_Agent_of_S.H.I.E.L.D._(film)" title="Nick Fury: Agent of S.H.I.E.L.D. (film)">Nick Fury: Agent of S.H.I.E.L.D.</a></i>, and by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Kretschmann" title="Thomas Kretschmann">Thomas Kretschmann</a> in the 2014 film <i><a href="/wiki/Captain_America:_The_Winter_Soldier" title="Captain America: The Winter Soldier">Captain America: The Winter Soldier</a></i> and the 2015 film <i><a href="/wiki/Avengers:_Age_of_Ultron" title="Avengers: Age of Ultron">Avengers: Age of Ultron</a></i>.</p>

<p>Created by <a href="/wiki/Stan_Lee" title="Stan Lee">Stan Lee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jack_Kirby" title="Jack Kirby">Jack Kirby</a>, the character first appears in <i><a href="/wiki/Sgt._Fury_and_his_Howling_Commandos" title="Sgt. Fury and his Howling Commandos">Sgt. Fury and his Howling Commandos</a></i> #5 (1964).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fictional_character_biography">Fictional character biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baron_Strucker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Fictional character biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in the late 19th century to a <a href="/wiki/Old_Prussians" title="Old Prussians">Prussian</a> noble family who had relocated to Strucker Castle in <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a> following the <a href="/wiki/Franco-Prussian_War" title="Franco-Prussian War">Franco-Prussian War</a>, Wolfgang von Strucker became a <a href="/wiki/Heidelberg" title="Heidelberg">Heidelberg</a> <a href="/wiki/Academic_fencing" title="Academic fencing">fencing</a> champion and was disfigured by <a href="/wiki/Dueling_scar" title="Dueling scar">facial scars</a>.</p>
<p>Strucker fought for Germany during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, during which he first encountered the jewel "Momentary Princess", which was fated to appear and disappear at regular intervals of time. Wolfgang pursued the jewel in the decades that followed.</p>
<p>When <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a> rose to power in Germany in 1933, Baron Strucker joined the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Party" title="Nazi Party">Nazi Party</a>, becoming infamous in the following years. In 1936, he and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geist_(Marvel_comics)" title="Geist (Marvel comics)">Geist</a>, one of Hitler's top men, allied themselves with the Egyptian mentalist Amahl Farouk (secretly the <a href="/wiki/Shadow_King" title="Shadow King">Shadow King</a>) in an attempt to dispute the lineage of England's royal family and install a new king who would be sympathetic to the Nazis. Their plot was thwarted by the Canadian adventurer <a href="/wiki/Wolverine_(character)" title="Wolverine (character)">Logan</a> and the time-traveling members of <a href="/wiki/Excalibur_(comics)" title="Excalibur (comics)">Excalibur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kitty_Pryde" title="Kitty Pryde">Kitty Pryde</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Summers" title="Rachel Summers">Phoenix</a>.</p>
<p>In 1937, German Intelligence agent Strucker was sent to the United States to assassinate Senator Fulton, but he was foiled by brigand-for-hire <a href="/wiki/Dominic_Fortune" title="Dominic Fortune">Dominic Fortune</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_II">World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baron_Strucker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> began, Strucker was appointed wing commander of the Death's Head Squadron by Hitler, and, in 1941, he was sent to Madripoor to help the Hand ninja cult transform young <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Widow_(Natalia_Romanova)" title="Black Widow (Natalia Romanova)">Natasha Romanova</a> into their master assassin, only to be defeated by Logan (yet to acquire the adamantium skeleton which would transform into <a href="/wiki/Wolverine_(character)" title="Wolverine (character)">Wolverine</a>), <a href="/wiki/Captain_America" title="Captain America">Captain America</a>, and Ivan Petrovitch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Later in 1941, Strucker invaded Latveria, seeking to use the Sturm-Fånger ("Storm-Catcher") to control cosmic energies; the device was wrecked by time-traveling members of <a href="/wiki/X-Force" title="X-Force">X-Force</a>.</p>
<p>After the United States entered the war, the First Attack Squad of Able Company (the <a href="/wiki/Sgt._Fury_and_his_Howling_Commandos" title="Sgt. Fury and his Howling Commandos">Howling Commandos</a>) became a constant thorn in the Nazis' side, and Hitler turned to Strucker, ordering him to publicly humiliate the Howlers' leader <a href="/wiki/Nick_Fury" title="Nick Fury">Nick Fury</a>. Strucker challenged Fury to a duel on the island of Norsehaven, and Fury violated direct orders to meet Strucker and satisfy his pride. Before their duel with plywood swords, Strucker offered Fury a toast, and secretly drugged Fury's drink. As they fought, Strucker was surprised to find that despite his superior swordsmanship and the effects of the drug, Fury was a tenacious foe, but Fury finally succumbed to the drug's effects. Strucker had the event recorded and sent back to Germany as propaganda material to destroy Fury's reputation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hitler demanded that Strucker capture the Howlers so they could be executed in Berlin. Strucker assembled the Blitzkrieg Squad, who could match each of the Howlers skill for skill, but the Howlers defeated them at every turn. Strucker also led the Blitzkrieg Squad to England to assassinate Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a> and Field Marshal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bernard_Law_Montgomery" title="Bernard Law Montgomery">Bernard Law Montgomery</a>, but they were foiled by the <a href="/wiki/Invaders_(comics)" title="Invaders (comics)">Invaders</a>. Strucker finally abandoned the Blitzkrieg Squad, which was later led by Colonel Klaue.</p>
<p>While vacationing in Berlin, Strucker witnessed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asgardians_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Asgardians (Marvel Comics)">Asgardian</a> <a href="/wiki/Thor_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Thor (Marvel Comics)">Thor</a>'s attack on the Chancellery, after which Strucker followed a bandaged man through a portal in space. Realizing he had traveled decades into the future, Strucker stole some history books about the end of World War II. He encountered the time machine's creator <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Doom" title="Doctor Doom">Doctor Doom</a>, who allowed him to return to the past, not believing that Strucker could win the war even with the knowledge he had obtained.</p>
<p>Tiring of Strucker's failures, Hitler assigned him to topple the resistance in the French town of Cherbeaux, or else destroy the town. Strucker rounded up the resistance to send them to camps to avoid having to destroy the town, but the Howling Commandos derailed his train, freeing the captives. Hitler demanded that Strucker destroy the town, and Strucker set the explosives, but then came face-to-face with Fury, who had rigged an explosive powerful enough to kill Strucker. Caught in a stalemate, Strucker agreed to let the people of Cherbeaux evacuate so that he and Fury could battle again. When the evacuation was complete, Strucker and Fury fought while Cherbeaux was destroyed around them, and Fury lost Strucker in the upheaval. For allowing the evacuation of Cherbeaux, Hitler had Strucker marked for death by the <a href="/wiki/Gestapo" title="Gestapo">Gestapo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Strucker escaped Germany because of the <a href="/wiki/Red_Skull" title="Red Skull">Red Skull</a>, Hitler's right-hand man, who foresaw Hitler's fall, and wanted an alternative power base. The Red Skull sent Strucker to Japan to form this organization, but Strucker intended to run it for himself. In Japan, Strucker joined forces with the <a href="/wiki/The_Hand_(comics)" title="The Hand (comics)">Hand</a> and an underground subversive movement which later became <a href="/wiki/Hydra_(comics)" title="Hydra (comics)">Hydra</a>. Strucker was instrumental in Hydra's earliest raids on war plants to supply their troops, and finally confronted the Supreme Hydra, claiming that as the organization's true mastermind, he should be Hydra's leader. The Supreme Hydra refused and shot him, but Strucker had worn a bulletproof vest, and slew the Supreme Hydra, claiming his title. Strucker also slew the jonin (leader) of the Hand in single combat, severing ties with the Hand to prevent Hydra agents from being seduced by promises of the dark power of the demonic Beast of the Hand.</p>
<p>Strucker moved Hydra's operations to a private island in the Pacific, named Hydra Island. After he used an undetectable "phantom" submarine to assault allied and Japanese ships, Hydra Island was investigated by the allies' <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leatherneck_Raiders" title="Leatherneck Raiders">Captain Savage and his Leatherneck Raiders</a>, and the Japanese Samurai Squad. Captain Savage and the others managed to overcome Hydra's forces, and Strucker was forced to detonate the island. With Hydra's plans temporarily waylaid, Strucker was forced to return to Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>To regain Hitler's favor, Strucker subjected Fury to a hallucinogenic drug in Africa, offering the formula to Hitler if it proved successful in the field. Fury was overcome by the hallucinations, but the Howlers rescued him, enabling his recovery. Ever more uncomfortable with the progress of the war, Strucker continued to operate Hydra from the shadows while pretending to be a loyal Nazi, secretly funneling Hitler's resources into his own operation. He also obtained the Dragon of Death, a powerful Japanese submarine, and made it Hydra's new mobile headquarters. Using his knowledge of the future, Strucker attempted to complete the atom bomb before the allies, sending Hydra agents to obtain the materials. Learning of this plot, the Invaders ultimately scuttled the Dragon, again delaying Strucker's plans, and during the clash Strucker lost the books he had brought from the future.</p>
<p>In 1944, Strucker encountered the Gnobians, a benevolent race of empathic extraterrestrials who had crashed near Gruenstadt. Realizing what their technology could do for Hydra, Strucker befriended the naïve creatures and killed the population of Gruenstadt to cover his tracks. The Howlers arrived, too late to prevent the tragedy, but fatally wounded Strucker. The Gnobians healed Strucker with their powers, but they went insane from absorbing some of his hate-filled psyche in the process. Strucker fled their crashed ship, taking discs of their information to his scientists. As Germany fell in 1945, Strucker had the Nazis' two greatest superhumans <a href="/wiki/Master_Man_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Master Man (Marvel Comics)">Master Man</a> and <a href="/wiki/Warrior_Woman_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Warrior Woman (Marvel Comics)">Warrior Woman</a> placed into suspended animation in opposite sides of Berlin to await later revival. Strucker also gave the Red Skull the gas used to preserve them, and it also preserved the Skull when he was buried alive in his bunker during a battle with Captain America.</p>
<p>Avoiding prosecution for war crimes, Baron Strucker had his scientists provide him with age-retarding serum so he could personally oversee Hydra's progress for decades to come. He and the Baroness Adelicia Von Krupp once captured the CIA's Agent Ten, who proved to be Strucker's old enemy Logan, but Logan was rescued by CIA agents <a href="/wiki/Richard_and_Mary_Parker" title="Richard and Mary Parker">Richard and Mary Parker</a>. Needing more finances for Hydra, Strucker abducted <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gabrielle_Haller" title="Gabrielle Haller">Gabrielle Haller</a>, who had a map leading to a fortune in lost Nazi gold hidden within her mind, but Haller was defended by her friends, the mutants <a href="/wiki/Professor_X" title="Professor X">Charles Xavier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Magneto_(comics)" title="Magneto (comics)">Erik Magnus</a>, and Magnus took the gold for his own purposes.</p>
<p>Strucker married more than once in the years that followed, resulting in a son, <a href="/wiki/Werner_von_Strucker" title="Werner von Strucker">Werner</a>, who resisted his father's influence, and twins, <a href="/wiki/Fenris_(comics)" title="Fenris (comics)">Andrea and Andreas</a>, who were genetically altered by Hydra scientists and so, developed super powers later in life. Strucker rebuilt Hydra Island, but concealed his role in the organization by using a front called "THEM", in which he was Grand Imperator. In this role, he assigned Arnold Brown to be his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_Hydra" title="Imperial Hydra">Imperial Hydra</a>, supposedly the true master of Hydra. Brown's rule of Hydra ended when he was overcome by <a href="/wiki/S.H.I.E.L.D." title="S.H.I.E.L.D.">S.H.I.E.L.D.</a>, an espionage organization led by Nick Fury and designed to thwart Hydra, and the unmasked Brown was mistakenly shot by his own men as Hydra crumbled about him. THEM also found and revived the long-lost Red Skull, but Strucker finally abandoned THEM when the scientific research arm of Hydra and another branch splintered away to become <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Idea_Mechanics" title="Advanced Idea Mechanics">A.I.M.</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Secret_Empire" title="Secret Empire">Secret Empire</a>, respectively.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baron_Strucker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Strucker continued to conceal his identity, acting as Don Antonio Caballero, Emir Ali Bey, and S.H.I.E.L.D. agent John Bronson. He finally revealed himself as Hydra's true leader when he prepared to unleash the Death Spore bomb from Hydra Island. Invading the island, Nick Fury used masks to trick Strucker's men into shooting their leader. In a panic, Strucker ran into a chamber where nuclear processes were taking place, and was immediately incinerated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_Model_Decoy">Life Model Decoy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baron_Strucker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Life Model Decoy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the ensuing years, the roboticist <a href="/wiki/Machinesmith" title="Machinesmith">Machinesmith</a> built a robot duplicate of Strucker, using it to test the abilities and morality of Captain America. The robot fooled even S.H.I.E.L.D. into thinking they had captured the real Strucker. Strucker also lived on in the form of several different android LMDs (<a href="/wiki/Life_Model_Decoy" title="Life Model Decoy">Life Model Decoy</a>) based on his mind; one was activated on Hydra Island after Strucker's death and believed itself to be the true Strucker, finally meeting its doom battling Captain America, Fury, and the one-time Howling Commandos. Another tricked Canada's Department H into selling Hydra the battle-suit worn by their hero <a href="/wiki/Guardian_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Guardian (Marvel Comics)">Guardian</a>. The most destructive was the Deltite, an LMD Strucker had planted within S.H.I.E.L.D. to tear the organization apart, and ultimately wound up running S.H.I.E.L.D. and Hydra simultaneously, playing the two against each other. By the time the Deltite was finally revealed and destroyed, it had managed to sow distrust throughout S.H.I.E.L.D., and killed many agents, leading Fury to disband the organization.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Resurrection">Resurrection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baron_Strucker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Resurrection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hydra suffered without Strucker's leadership, and the Red Skull championed an effort to bring the Baron back, providing a distraction to occupy Fury's newly-rebuilt S.H.I.E.L.D. while Hydra scientists found Strucker's body and sacrificed their lives to revive him with the Death Spore bonded to his form.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Thunderbolts"><i>Thunderbolts</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baron_Strucker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Thunderbolts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Strucker places the hero <a href="/wiki/Abner_Jenkins" title="Abner Jenkins">Abner Jenkins</a> of the Thunderbolts into his debt by funding his team, and pits the team against threats he helped fund, including the <a href="/wiki/Wrecking_Crew_(comics)" title="Wrecking Crew (comics)">Wrecking Crew</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fathom_Five_(comics)" title="Fathom Five (comics)">Fathom Five</a>, and the Great Game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gorgon and Elsbeth stage a coup of Hydra and kill Strucker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> This Strucker was subsequently revealed to be a clone, as the real Strucker was rescued and kept in stasis before being freed by the Swordsman (who turned out to be his son, Andreas) and then Baron Helmut Zemo. Strucker agreed to work with Zemo in his grand plan to take over the world and left together.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Helmut Zemo is also responsible for the murder of Strucker's daughter Andrea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Secret_Invasion">Secret Invasion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baron_Strucker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Secret Invasion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the <i><a href="/wiki/Secret_Invasion" title="Secret Invasion">Secret Invasion</a></i> storyline, Strucker is attacked by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skrulls" title="Skrulls">Skrulls</a> who are attempting to take over Hydra. However, he is able to defeat them and destroys his own headquarters in order to eliminate any remaining invaders. Strucker also reveals that he no longer adheres to orthodox Nazi beliefs, since he does not wish to "cleanse" the world, but simply to rule it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SecretWarriors2_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-SecretWarriors2-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dark_Reign">Dark Reign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baron_Strucker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Dark Reign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_Reign_(comics)" title="Dark Reign (comics)">Dark Reign</a></i> storyline, Strucker's longtime nemesis, Nick Fury, discovers that S.H.I.E.L.D. is, and always has been, controlled by Hydra.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Strucker seems happy finding this out saying that "We've been dancing to the melody for so long, and finally you hear it too". Strucker calls forth all Hydra leaders (<a href="/wiki/Kraken_(character)" title="Kraken (character)">Kraken</a>, <a href="/wiki/Viper_(Madame_Hydra)" title="Viper (Madame Hydra)">Viper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Valentina_Allegra_de_Fontaine" title="Valentina Allegra de Fontaine">Madame Hydra</a>, and the Hive), and resurrects the <a href="/wiki/Gorgon_(Tomi_Shishido)" title="Gorgon (Tomi Shishido)">Gorgon</a> for the purpose of "letting the world become aware of Hydra's true nature".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SecretWarriors2_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-SecretWarriors2-12">[12]</a></sup> However, Nick Fury was interfering in von Strucker's plans, so Strucker enlists the help of Norman Osborn, director of the corrupt H.A.M.M.E.R. agency, to take care of his nemesis. The news that Osborn had recently murdered his son Andreas had little impact on Von Strucker, who calmly declared that he now owed Osborn "two favors".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>It is revealed that, at some point in the past, Baron Strucker, Nick Fury, and Hand grandmaster Soji Soma had a meeting with representatives of the organization <a href="/wiki/Leviathan_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Leviathan (Marvel Comics)">Leviathan</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Along with Nick Fury, Baron Strucker was captured by the Kraken. It was revealed that not only was Kraken really Nick Fury's presumably dead half-brother <a href="/wiki/Scorpio_(comics)" title="Scorpio (comics)">Jake Fury</a> (working with Nick the entire time), but it was revealed to a disbelieving von Strucker that in fact S.H.I.E.L.D. had been controlling Hydra all along. Before being able to speak his final words, Nick Fury shot him in the head.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="All-New.2C_All-Different_Marvel">All-New, All-Different Marvel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baron_Strucker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: All-New, All-Different Marvel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

He is described as no longer being Hydra's leader, though shown to still be heavily involved in international subversive dealings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Powers_and_abilities">Powers and abilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baron_Strucker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Powers and abilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baron Strucker is a highly intelligent man in peak human physical condition. He is an exceptional hand-to-hand combatant, swordsman, and marksman. He is also a consummate military strategist and spy, and a master of disguise and excellent actor. He carries a sword and traditional firearms, but also wears the Satan Claw on his right hand: this metal gauntlet amplifies his strength and emits powerful electrical shocks. Strucker also uses serums developed by Hydra, enabling him to maintain his physical vigor at its height and retard his aging process, so that physically he remains the same, despite his advanced age.</p>
<p>Exposure to his Death Spore has bonded Strucker's DNA with the Death Spore, enabling him to be revived after seemingly being killed by gunshot wounds and radiation poisoning. Strucker can now release the Death Spore virus from his body at will, killing his victims nearly instantly. If Strucker is killed, the Death Spore viruses will allegedly be set free from his body and risk infecting the entire Earth.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_versions">Other versions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baron_Strucker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Other versions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Earth-Charnel">Earth-Charnel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baron_Strucker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Earth-Charnel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A sixth Baron Strucker was the dictator of Earth-9939 (Earth-Charnel) under the name Charnel. He was depicted as a necromantic, cybernetic monster who took over that Earth and created demon-drones called Spawn of Charnel to do his bidding. He was responsible for wiping out most of its heroes there. He paralyzed <a href="/wiki/Thing_(comics)" title="Thing (comics)">Thing</a>, turned <a href="/wiki/Mister_Fantastic" title="Mister Fantastic">Mister Fantastic</a> into putty, and destroyed <a href="/wiki/Thor_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Thor (Marvel Comics)">Thor</a>'s people. However, Charnel along with his timeline was later destroyed by the bounty-hunter <a href="/wiki/Death%27s_Head" title="Death's Head">Death's Head</a>.</p>
<p>This universe was used by <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Idea_Mechanics" title="Advanced Idea Mechanics">Advanced Idea Mechanics</a> Supreme Scientist <a href="/wiki/Monica_Rappaccini" title="Monica Rappaccini">Monica Rappaccini</a> to conceal her hidden base until <a href="/wiki/Hank_Pym" title="Hank Pym">Hank Pym</a> disrupted her machines, stranding her and her followers on Earth-Charnel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>

After that, Strucker was attacked and killed by Mangaverse's <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Strange" title="Doctor Strange">Doctor Strange</a> in a magical duel.</p>

<li>Baron Strucker appears in the television special <i><a href="/wiki/Lego_Marvel_Super_Heroes:_Avengers_Reassembled" title="Lego Marvel Super Heroes: Avengers Reassembled">Lego Marvel Super Heroes: Avengers Reassembled</a></i>, voiced by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=John_Paul_Karliak&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="John Paul Karliak (page does not exist)">J.P. Karliak</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
<li>Baron Strucker appears in <i><a href="/wiki/Avengers_Assemble_(TV_series)" title="Avengers Assemble (TV series)">Avengers: Ultron Revolution</a></i>, voiced by <a href="/wiki/Robin_Atkin_Downes" title="Robin Atkin Downes">Robin Atkin Downes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Besides being a member of HYDRA, this version was shown to have reactivated the <a href="/wiki/Red_Room_(comics)" title="Red Room (comics)">Red Room</a> while operating in Siberia. In the episode "Seeing Double", he sends the new <a href="/wiki/Black_Widow_(Yelena_Belova)" title="Black Widow (Yelena Belova)">Black Widow</a> (Yelena Belova) to abduct <a href="/wiki/Hulk_(comics)" title="Hulk (comics)">Bruce Banner</a> in order to use the Winter Soldier program that would turn Banner into the Winter Hulk, revealing all of this to the original <a href="/wiki/Black_Widow_(Natasha_Romanova)" title="Black Widow (Natasha Romanova)">Black Widow</a> (Natasha Romanova). When <a href="/wiki/Iron_Man" title="Iron Man">Iron Man</a> and <a href="/wiki/Captain_America" title="Captain America">Captain America</a> show up, the two <a href="/wiki/Avengers_(comics)" title="Avengers (comics)">Avengers</a> help Natasha to fight Strucker and Winter Hulk until Yelena goes against Strucker's orders and knocks him out. In the episode "Civil War, Part 2: The Might Avengers," Baron Strucker leads a HYDRA raid on a secret facility to gain control of an exo-suit. During his fight with the <a href="/wiki/The_Mighty_Avengers" title="The Mighty Avengers">Mighty Avengers</a>, Baron Strucker also fights the Avengers and is defeated.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/J._M._DeMatteis" title="J. M. DeMatteis">J. M. DeMatteis</a> wrote a <a href="/wiki/Film_treatment" title="Film treatment">film treatment</a> for a <i><a href="/wiki/Daredevil_(Marvel_Comics_character)" title="Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)">Daredevil</a></i> film, which included the character as the leader of Hydra, an international arms cartel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Kretschmann" title="Thomas Kretschmann">Thomas Kretschmann</a> portrayed Baron Strucker in the <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Cinematic_Universe" title="Marvel Cinematic Universe">Marvel Cinematic Universe</a>. The character makes his first appearance in a mid-credits scene of <i><a href="/wiki/Captain_America:_The_Winter_Soldier" title="Captain America: The Winter Soldier">Captain America: The Winter Soldier</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Winter_Soldier_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Winter_Soldier-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Based in a compound located in Sokovia, he has been experimenting to create superhumans with <a href="/wiki/Loki_(comics)" title="Loki (comics)">Loki</a>'s scepter. Following <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Goodwin_Pierce" title="Alexander Goodwin Pierce">Alexander Pierce</a>'s demise, Strucker tells Dr. List that he expressed no concern for Hydra's existence now being public knowledge and instead focuses on his surviving test subjects: Pietro Maximoff and Wanda Maximoff. In the opening of <i><a href="/wiki/Avengers:_Age_of_Ultron" title="Avengers: Age of Ultron">Avengers: Age of Ultron</a></i>, Strucker's HYDRA compound is raided by the <a href="/wiki/Avengers_(comics)" title="Avengers (comics)">Avengers</a> to which <a href="/wiki/Quicksilver_(comics)" title="Quicksilver (comics)">Quicksilver</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Scarlet_Witch" title="Scarlet Witch">Scarlet Witch</a> manage to run away. After being personally defeated by Steve Rogers, Strucker is handed over to <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a>. Later, <a href="/wiki/Ultron" title="Ultron">Ultron</a> kills Strucker in his cell and then writes "PEACE" in Strucker's own blood as a message to the Avengers.</li>
</ul>
