<p><i><b>Beloved</b></i> is the third studio <a href="/wiki/Album" title="Album">album</a> by <a href="/wiki/K-pop" title="K-pop">K-pop</a> group, <a href="/wiki/Jewelry_(band)" title="Jewelry (band)">Jewelry</a>. It was released on July 4, 2003 on <a href="/wiki/Star_Empire_Entertainment" title="Star Empire Entertainment">Star Empire Entertainment</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beloved_(Jewelry_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2003, Jewelry returned to their previous "cute" concept, but less emphasis on the cuteness, unlike the <a href="/wiki/Discovery_(Jewelry_album)" title="Discovery (Jewelry album)">first album</a>. The group performed their comeback performance in June 2003, performing the lead single, <i>니가 참 좋아 (I Really Like You)</i>, and <i>Be My Love</i>. The album consists of pop songs, departing from the <a href="/wiki/Again_(Jewelry_album)" title="Again (Jewelry album)">R&amp;B ballad sound they once excelled in.</a> The lead single, <i>니가 참 좋아 (I Really Like You)</i>, is a song about strong affection for another person, and was very popular upon its release, charting high on the charts and was performed frequently. The music video contained all four members unknowingly trying to get the attention of one guy, only to find out that he is interested in another girl.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Another single was released, <i>바보야 (Fool)</i>, a song about a girl who adores a guy, but the guy is too oblivious to her feelings. The music video is a montage consisting of footage during promotion of <i>Beloved</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Despite being the second single, the song was never performed live. <i>Be My Love</i> was also promoted, and ended up being a popular song from the album, and was performed live many times (altered from the album version, also known as the TV version, including a dance break performed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seo_In_Young" title="Seo In Young">Seo In Young</a>). <i>Beloved</i> was a great seller, but Jewelry was unhappy with their current image, and wanted to break out of their "cutesy" concept.</p>
