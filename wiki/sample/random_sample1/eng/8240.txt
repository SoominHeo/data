<p><i><b>Strangerland</b></i> is a 2015 Australian-Irish <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_film" title="Drama film">drama</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suspense_film" title="Suspense film">suspense film</a> directed by Kim Farrant at her <a href="/wiki/List_of_directorial_debuts" title="List of directorial debuts">directorial debut</a>, and written by Michael Kinirons and Fiona Seres.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THR-Strangerland_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-THR-Strangerland-3">[3]</a></sup> The film stars <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Kidman" title="Nicole Kidman">Nicole Kidman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Fiennes" title="Joseph Fiennes">Joseph Fiennes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Weaving" title="Hugo Weaving">Hugo Weaving</a>. The film premiered at the <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Film Festival</a> on 23 January 2015<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sundance1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-sundance1-4">[4]</a></sup> and in a <a href="/wiki/Limited_release" title="Limited release">limited release</a> in cinemas and through <a href="/wiki/Video_on_demand" title="Video on demand">video on demand</a> on 10 July 2015 by <a href="/wiki/Alchemy" title="Alchemy">Alchemy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-comingsoon1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-comingsoon1-5">[5]</a></sup> It was a <a href="/wiki/Box_office_bomb" title="Box office bomb">box office bomb</a>, grossing $1 million against a $10 million budget.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strangerland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Catherine and Matthew Parker move with their children Lily and Tom to the remote Australian desert town of Nathgari. Although the family is unhappy with the move, Matthew and Tom blame Lily for forcing them to leave the larger town of Coonaburra. When he can't sleep, Tom sometimes walks around the neighborhood at night, an activity his parents discourage. One night, Matthew sees Tom leave the house, followed by Lily. He says nothing and goes back to sleep. Catherine oversleeps, and when she cannot find the kids, calls Matthew at work. He reassures her that they must have gone to school before she woke up. When the school calls to report that Tom and Lily have not been attending, she once again becomes worried, as a sandstorm is scheduled to hit the town. Matthew discourages her from contacting anyone, as he does not wish the family's business to once again become public knowledge. Against his wishes, Catherine contacts several people and finally the police.</p>
<p>While taking their report, Detective David Rae finds that Lily has once before been reported missing, Catherine and Matthew reluctantly reveal that she had disappeared for a few days and turned up at a friend's house. After further investigation, Rae learns that one of Lily's Coonaburra teachers, Neil McPherson, had sex with her. When Matthew found out, he beat up McPherson. The Parkers admit to Rae that they left Coonaburra to escape the attention. Catherine becomes frustrated with Matthew's focus on his job, though he insists that he has no choice. Rae's queries in town reveal that Lily had multiple sexual relationships despite being underage. When Catherine finds Lily's diary, it includes pictures and poetry about several people: McPherson, the Parker's mentally handicapped handyman Burtie, and a local youth nicknamed Slug. It also describes her parents' marriage as a sham, which causes Catherine to cry. Catherine hands the diary to Rae with the proviso that he not reveal it to Matthew.</p>
<p>Matthew drives to Coonaburra and threatens McPherson. Coreen, Rae's girlfriend and Burtie's sister, becomes annoyed when Rae won't share details of the case with her. Rae secretly shreds the evidence that connects Burtie to Lily and discourages Burtie from telling him anything incriminating. Rae arrests Slug, but without any evidence must release him. When the kids remain missing, the town organizes a search party. Matthew refuses to participate, further angering Catherine. Rae suggests to Catherine that her children could have run away for a reason. Catherine confronts Matthew with Lily's sexual history and asks if he molested her. Incredulous, he denies it and asks why she would even ask; she cites his apparent reluctance to help find the kids. Knowing now that Burtie had a sexual relationship with Lily, Matthew beats up Burtie and searches his house. Coreen demands Rae arrest Matthew, and when she accuses him of taking the Parkers' side, Rae reveals Burtie's connection.</p>
<p>Burtie visits the Parkers and asks to speak with Matthew. Catherine invites him in, but he leaves when she attempts to seduce him in Lily's clothes. When Matthew drives into the desert alone, he finds Tom dehydrated and delirious. Tom initially refuses to speak, and Catherine yells at him; Tom eventually tells Matthew that he saw Lily get into a car. Catherine listens as locals discuss Lily's disappearance and likely death, and becomes distraught when she receives prank calls that call Lily promiscuous. She visits Rae, and when she fails to seduce him, wanders into the desert alone. She later reappears in town, naked. In bed together, as they hold each other, Catherine admits that she hid the diary from Matthew, and Matthew admits he saw the kids leave at night. He says he did not stop them because he wanted to punish Lily. Catherine hugs Tom and walks onto the porch, where she and Matthew console each other. While holding Lily's diary, Matthew cries and says that he wishes he could take back one moment.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strangerland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 20 October 2013 <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Kidman" title="Nicole Kidman">Nicole Kidman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guy_Pearce" title="Guy Pearce">Guy Pearce</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Weaving" title="Hugo Weaving">Hugo Weaving</a> joined the cast of the thriller film, <a href="/wiki/Screen_Australia" title="Screen Australia">Screen Australia</a> announced it would finance the film. Macdara Kelleher and Naomi Wench were announced as producers of the film, and Kim Farrant is announced as director, with a script by Fiona Seres and Michael Kinirons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THR-Strangerland_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-THR-Strangerland-3">[3]</a></sup> The other cast members announced included <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Flanagan" title="Lisa Flanagan">Lisa Flanagan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martin_Dingle_Wall" title="Martin Dingle Wall">Martin Dingle Wall</a>, Meyne Wyatt and Nicholas Hamilton.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shooting-THR_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shooting-THR-8">[8]</a></sup> On 13 March 2014 <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Fiennes" title="Joseph Fiennes">Joseph Fiennes</a> joined the film, replacing Pearce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Joseph_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Joseph-6">[6]</a></sup> On 27 March 2014 it was announced that Maddison Brown had landed a lead role in the film to debut her acting career, which <a href="/wiki/Worldview_Entertainment" title="Worldview Entertainment">Worldview Entertainment</a> would finance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maddison_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Maddison-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strangerland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 27 March 2014 it was announced that the shooting of the film was set to start in the last week of March in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The filming was also set to take place in <a href="/wiki/Canowindra" title="Canowindra">Canowindra</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broken_Hill,_New_South_Wales" title="Broken Hill, New South Wales">Broken Hill</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shooting-THR_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Shooting-THR-8">[8]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Alice_Springs" title="Alice Springs">Alice Springs</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Territory" title="Northern Territory">Northern Territory</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Later the shooting began on 31 March 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strangerland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film had its world premiere at the <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Film Festival</a> on 23 January 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sundance1_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-sundance1-4">[4]</a></sup> Shortly after the premiere, <a href="/wiki/Alchemy" title="Alchemy">Alchemy</a> acquired distribution rights to the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The film went on to screen at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgrade_Film_Festival" title="Belgrade Film Festival">Belgrade Film Festival</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seattle_International_Film_Festival" title="Seattle International Film Festival">Seattle International Film Festival</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Film_Festival" title="Sydney Film Festival">Sydney Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The film will be released in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> on 10 July 2015 in a <a href="/wiki/Limited_release" title="Limited release">limited release</a> and through <a href="/wiki/Video_on_demand" title="Video on demand">video on demand</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-comingsoon1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-comingsoon1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strangerland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strangerland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>An image and a new synopsis was revealed on 5 May 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On 3 June 2015 the official poster was released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-comingsoon1_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-comingsoon1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical">Critical</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strangerland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Critical">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Strangerland</i> was met with mixed to negative reviews, earning a 39% approval rating on <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> based on 62 critics with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weighted_average" title="Weighted average">weighted average</a> score of 5.2/10. The site's consensus: "<i>Strangerland</i> has a marvelous cast, but their efforts aren't enough to overcome the story's blandly predictable melodrama.".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> reports a <a href="/wiki/Standard_score" title="Standard score">normalized score</a> of 42 out of 100, based on 13 critics, indicating "mixed or average reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Brad Wheeler of "Globe and Mail" gave a positive review, calling it "Eerie and unpredictable, <i>Strangerland</i> holds attention, even if traditional suspense tricks are avoided like they were dingos at the daycare." Matthew Lickona of "San Diego Reader" commented ""Director Kim Farrant goes for a feeling that's as harsh, unforgiving, and wild as the land she lets the camera linger on, and it's the right idea when handling the potential melodrama of the material." Staci Layne Wilson of "AtHomeInHollywood.com" stated that the film is "presented through a prism of womanly wantoness juxtaposed with a mother's pain and fear." Joe McGovern of <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> complimented <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Kidman" title="Nicole Kidman">Nicole Kidman</a>, saying "her best performances have often been as grieving moms (<i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Calm_(film)" title="Dead Calm (film)">Dead Calm</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Others_(2001_film)" title="The Others (2001 film)">The Others</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Rabbit_Hole_(film)" title="Rabbit Hole (film)">Rabbit Hole</a></i>) and here she provides the flaccid movie's sole flash of daring and unpredictability."</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Richard_Roeper" title="Richard Roeper">Richard Roeper</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Sun-Times" title="Chicago Sun-Times">Chicago Sun-Times</a></i> gave a negative review, saying the film "runs out of gas, leaving us with a couple of final "Forget You" (shall we say) moments. Thanks. Thanks for nothing."</p>
