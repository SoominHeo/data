<p><b>Paul Van Haver</b> (born 12 March 1985, Brussels), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Stromae</b> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[stÊÉmaj]</a></span>, is a <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgian</a>-Rwandan <a href="/wiki/Musician" title="Musician">musician</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper">rapper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a>. Stromae was born to a <a href="/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">Rwandan</a> father, Pierre Rutare, and a Belgian mother, Miranda Marie Van Haver. He is originally from <a href="/wiki/Laeken" title="Laeken">Laeken</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He established himself in both the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic music</a> genres. Stromae came to wide public attention in 2009 with his song "<a href="/wiki/Alors_on_danse" title="Alors on danse">Alors on danse</a>", which became a <a href="/wiki/Record_chart" title="Record chart">number one</a> in several <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> countries. In 2013, his second album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Racine_carr%C3%A9e" title="Racine carrÃ©e">Racine carrÃ©e</a></i> was a commercial success, selling 2 million copies in France<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stromae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stromae was born in <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a> to a <a href="/wiki/Tutsi" title="Tutsi">Tutsi</a> father and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian" title="Belgian">Belgian</a> mother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sayare_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sayare-2">[2]</a></sup> His father, a successful Rwandan architect, was killed during the 1994 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rwandan_Genocide" title="Rwandan Genocide">Rwandan Genocide</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sayare_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sayare-2">[2]</a></sup> His mother raised him and his four siblings in <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>, and later in a nearby suburb. She encouraged each of her children to play a sport and an instrument. Stromae studied percussion. His mother placed him in a Roman Catholic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jesuit" title="Jesuit">Jesuit</a> school after he failed in the public school system at the age of sixteen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sayare_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Sayare-2">[2]</a></sup> He formed a small rap group with friends while still in school. His early influences included Belgian singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Brel" title="Jacques Brel">Jacques Brel</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cuban_son" title="Cuban son">Cuban son</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Congolese_rumba" title="Congolese rumba">Congolese rumba</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Even though he was raised as a Belgian, he has stated that he did not always feel that he was from Belgium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sayare_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Sayare-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2008, Stromae worked as a young trainee at the music radio station NRJ in Brussels. Vincent Verbelen, Music Manager, was impressed by the talent he perceived in Stromae's first single "Alors on danse" and decided to air it for the first time on NRJ. The response from listeners (including influential personalities ranging from <a href="/wiki/Anna_Wintour" title="Anna Wintour">Anna Wintour</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean-Claude_Van_Damme" title="Jean-Claude Van Damme">Jean-Claude Van Damme</a>, to the then-president of France <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Sarkozy" title="Nicolas Sarkozy">Nicolas Sarkozy</a>)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> was one of extreme enthusiasm and Stromae came to wider public attention. In a few weeks from that point, sales of the song established it as number one in Belgium.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Vertigo_Records" title="Vertigo Records">Vertigo Records</a>, a label of <a href="/wiki/Mercury_Records" title="Mercury Records">Mercury Records</a> France (<a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal Music Group</a>), signed him for a worldwide licensing deal soon after this.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>By May 2010 the track "Alors on danse" had reached number one in Belgium, France, Sweden, Greece, Germany, Austria, Turkey, Switzerland, Italy, Denmark, Romania and the Czech Republic.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On 2 September, Stromae collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a> in a remix of his hit "Alors on danse".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2010, he was nominated for Best Dutch and Belgian Act at the <a href="/wiki/2010_MTV_Europe_Music_Awards" title="2010 MTV Europe Music Awards">MTV Europe Music Awards</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Of his music and influences, he has said: "I was in a little group doing rap music. I thought that rather than copying the French sound, I'd focus on a more American style but give it a European spin. And then I rediscovered 90s <a href="/wiki/Eurodance" title="Eurodance">Eurodance</a>. For a long time we were ashamed of that sound but in fact there's a lot to discover. It has its roots in everything from house to salsa. I also really admire <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Brel" title="Jacques Brel">Jacques Brel</a> â he has been a huge influence on me â but also all sorts of other stuff, Cuban <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Son_(music)" title="Son (music)">son</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Congolese_rumba" title="Congolese rumba">Congolese rumba</a>, that I heard as a child; that music rocked the whole of Africa." Also, Stromae insists he wanted to collaborate with up and coming rap group Swag+5 featuring rapper T-Jay. In the end, the collaboration did not take place, but Stromae is a big fan of the America-based group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The critic Molloy WoodcraftÂ : "He combines 90s electro synths and beats with a laidback delivery to mesmeric effect".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>He is one of the ten winners of the <i><a href="/wiki/European_Border_Breakers_Award" title="European Border Breakers Award">European Border Breakers Award</a></i> 2011. On 9 February 2011, his debut album <i>Cheese</i> (including the hit single "Alors on danse") won the award for Best Dance Album at the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Victoires_de_la_musique" title="Victoires de la musique">Victoires de la musique</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On 23 May 2011, during the recording of the French TV show <a href="/wiki/Taratata" title="Taratata">Taratata</a>, Stromae did a <a href="/wiki/Mashup_(music)" title="Mashup (music)">mashup</a> of "<a href="/wiki/Alors_on_danse" title="Alors on danse">Alors on danse</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Stop_the_Party_(The_Black_Eyed_Peas_song)" title="Don't Stop the Party (The Black Eyed Peas song)">Don't Stop the Party</a>" with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Eyed_Peas" title="Black Eyed Peas">Black Eyed Peas</a>. Stromae also announced he would perform as opening support act for the first of two concerts by the band in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> on 24 and 25 June 2011 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stade_De_France" title="Stade De France">Stade De France</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Stromae had previously met <a href="/wiki/Will.i.am" title="Will.i.am">will.i.am</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NRJ_Music_Awards" title="NRJ Music Awards">NRJ Music Awards</a> in January. will.i.am told him that he liked "Alors on danse" and said he wanted to collaborate with Stromae.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2011, he received one nomination for Best Belgian Act at the <a href="/wiki/2011_MTV_Europe_Music_Awards" title="2011 MTV Europe Music Awards">MTV Europe Music Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.93present:_Racine_carr.C3.A9e">2013âpresent: <i>Racine carrÃ©e</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stromae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2013âpresent: Racine carrÃ©e">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first single "<a href="/wiki/Papaoutai" title="Papaoutai">Papaoutai</a>" from his second album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Racine_carr%C3%A9e" title="Racine carrÃ©e">Racine carrÃ©e</a></i> (English: <a href="/wiki/Square_root" title="Square root">Square root</a>) was digitally released on 13 May 2013. It went on to chart number 1 in Belgium and France, number 2 in the Netherlands, as well as number 7 in Germany and Switzerland.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On 22 May 2013, amateur-looking videos appeared on YouTube showing Stromae, apparently drunk and wandering at the <a href="/wiki/Avenue_Louise" title="Avenue Louise">Louiza tram station in Brussels</a> at dawn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The videos went viral. A few days later, it was revealed that this was linked with the filming of a professional music video. This was confirmed during an appearance by Stromae on the French TV show <i>Ce soir ou jamais</i> in which he discussed and also performed his new single "<a href="/wiki/Formidable_(song)" title="Formidable (song)">Formidable</a>", which is about the story of a drunk man just separated from his girlfriend. The clip was made from edited hidden camera footage taken on 22 May. It also shows people taking pictures with their cellphones, people who are clearly irritated by his drunk actions, people helping him stand up, and three policemen asking if the artist is okay or if he needs some help.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The policemen also got some critiques for not acting against the drunk Stromae; however, they later declared that the entire story was not shown. The part where they returned and Stromae had to explain his case was not broadcast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 26 July 2013, Stromae announced on his Facebook<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and Twitter<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> accounts that he had completed his second studio album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Racine_carr%C3%A9e" title="Racine carrÃ©e">Racine carrÃ©e</a></i> or <i>â</i> and that it would be released on 16 August 2013. On 25 August 2013, Stromae joined <a href="/wiki/Major_Lazer" title="Major Lazer">Major Lazer</a>'s show at French festival <a href="/wiki/Rock_en_Seine" title="Rock en Seine">Rock en Seine</a>, where he performed <i>Papaoutai</i>. In September 2013, Belgian rock band Mintzkov covered his song "Formidable/Wonderful" for a live session on Studio Brussel, with lyrics in English. In November 2013, Stromae received the award for Best Belgian Act at the <a href="/wiki/2013_MTV_Europe_Music_Awards" title="2013 MTV Europe Music Awards">MTV Europe Music Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 17 March 2014, it was made public that Stromae's song "<a href="/wiki/Ta_f%C3%AAte" title="Ta fÃªte">Ta fÃªte</a>" would become the official song for the <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_football_team" title="Belgium national football team">Belgian national football team</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2014_World_Cup" title="2014 World Cup">2014 World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2014, in support of <i>Racine carrÃ©e</i>, Stromae made his US television debut on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_with_Seth_Meyers" title="Late Night with Seth Meyers">Late Night with Seth Meyers</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He then embarked on a tour across the United States and performed at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Buy_Theater" title="Best Buy Theater">Best Buy Theater</a> in New York City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> While in New York, on 22 June 2014, Stromae guest starred in a <a href="/wiki/Snapchat" title="Snapchat">Snapchat</a> story with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jerome_Jarre" title="Jerome Jarre">Jerome Jarre</a> as The Carrot's Father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 21 October 2014, it was revealed that New Zealand singer <a href="/wiki/Lorde" title="Lorde">Lorde</a> invited Stromae to work on the <a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games:_Mockingjay,_Part_1_%E2%80%93_Original_Motion_Picture_Soundtrack" title="The Hunger Games: Mockingjay, Part 1 â Original Motion Picture Soundtrack">soundtrack</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games:_Mockingjay_%E2%80%93_Part_1" title="The Hunger Games: Mockingjay â Part 1">The Hunger Games: Mockingjay â Part 1</a></i> along with artists like <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grace_Jones" title="Grace Jones">Grace Jones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diplo" title="Diplo">Diplo</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Chemical_Brothers" title="The Chemical Brothers">The Chemical Brothers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He contributed to the song "<a href="/wiki/Meltdown_(Stromae_song)" title="Meltdown (Stromae song)">Meltdown</a>" which also features <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a>, <a href="/wiki/Q-Tip_(musician)" title="Q-Tip (musician)">Q-Tip</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haim_(band)" title="Haim (band)">Haim</a> and Lorde herself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Earlier that month she declared herself a fan of Stromae after attending his concert.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In March 2015, Stromae released a music video for the song "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Carmen_(Stromae_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Carmen (Stromae song) (page does not exist)">Carmen</a>", which was directed by <a href="/wiki/Sylvain_Chomet" title="Sylvain Chomet">Sylvain Chomet</a>. The video warns people against the addiction of social websites such as <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Stromae continued touring the United States, performing in March 2015 in several places among which the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SXSW" title="SXSW">SXSW</a> in Austin, Texas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> He was also among the line-up of the <a href="/wiki/Coachella_Valley_Music_and_Arts_Festival" title="Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival">Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> He then began a tour in Africa on 13 May. However, in mid-June Stromae was forced to cancel the remaining of his shows until 2 August for health reasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> He performed at <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">Madison Square Garden</a> in October, becoming the first exclusively French-singing performer to sell out that venue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 14 September 2015, Stromae released a single "Quand c'est?", a song about <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">cancer</a>, which plays on the fact that "Quand c'est?" (When is it?) and "cancer" are phonetically similar in French.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>By the end of 2015, the album became the first in over a decade to reach 2 million sales in France.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fashion_label">Fashion label</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stromae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Fashion label">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Stromae is known for his distinctive physique and clothing style. He has used this image in launching a limited fashion line, Mosaert (an anagram of his stage name). The collection features bright colors and geometric patterns, and has been described as "hipster" and "retro kitsch". Stromae has said that he wanted to "create a bridge between British style and African aesthetic."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first collection was a success, with stocks almost sold out after three days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The second collection, Capsule No. 2, was released at Colette in Paris on 3 December 2014, and features the square root design on each item in different colors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Capsule No. 3 was released on 7 April 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The new designs are the first to use patterns not previously worn by Stromae, reflecting Mosaert's intention to gradually move into a separate stylistic identity for the clothing line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
