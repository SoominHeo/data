<p><i><b>Strobe Edge</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ストロボ・エッジ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">Hepburn</a>: <i>Sutorobo Ejji</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series written and illustrated by <a href="/wiki/Io_Sakisaka" title="Io Sakisaka">Io Sakisaka</a>. It began serialization in 2007 in the <a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Djo" title="Shōjo">shōjo</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manga_magazine" title="Manga magazine">manga magazine</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Bessatsu_Margaret" title="Bessatsu Margaret">Bessatsu Margaret</a></i> and ended in 2010. The chapters are collected and bound in <a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a> format by <a href="/wiki/Shueisha" title="Shueisha">Shueisha</a> under the Margaret Comics label. The manga is licensed in North America by <a href="/wiki/Viz_Media" title="Viz Media">Viz Media</a> with its first volume released in November 2012. A live-action film adaptation had its theatrical release on March 14, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-live-action_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-live-action-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strobe_Edge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Freshman high school student Ninako Kinoshita has never been in love with anyone until she meets with popular schoolmate Ren Ichinose while boarding a train for school. She gradually falls in love with him as she gets to know about him, though she has to face the truth that Ren is already in a committed relationship with Mayuka Korenaga, a model and the older sister of Daiki, whose love confession Ninako rejects and whose subsequent up-and-down relationship with Ninako's best friend, Sayuri Uehara, becomes the series' secondary plot. Through Ren, Ninako is also introduced to his friend, Takumi Ando, who, despite his womanizing tendency, genuinely falls in love with her.</p>
<p>Ninako and Ren are then thrown into situations that require them to be close together, such as working part-time at the same restaurant and being chosen to buy supplies for the summer festival. Gradually, Ren begins to develop feelings for Ninako, but he is adamant in his choice to stay with Mayuka. Realizing that this act is hurting the both of them, Mayuka chooses to break up with Ren. At the same time, Ando continues to urge Ninako to move on from Ren and be his girlfriend. Once while with Ninako, Ando is confronted by Mao Sugimoto, an ex-girlfriend who cheated him by kissing Ren, causing their breakup and Ando's friendship with Ren becoming looser.</p>
<p>In the next school year, Ninako gets to be in the same class as Ren and Ando, while Mao enrolls in the same school as them as a freshman, irritating Ando. Ninako and Ren continue to develop feelings for each other, but at one point, Mao meets with Ninako and urges her to stop following Ren, with the reason that she wants him and Ando reconcile. When Ando confronts Mao about this, she states that she wants Ninako and Ren to drift apart so <i>Ando</i> can pursue Ninako, to atone for what Mao, who still loves Ando despite all things, has done to him before. Not knowing the reason, Ninako begins to avoid and ignore Ren, who has begun to pursue her so he can confess. When he does, Ninako declines without reason, and when he insists, she becomes upset and tells him to leave her alone. She is, however, encouraged by Ando to stop pitying him and instead follow her heart. Realizing that what she has done is selfish, Ninako goes after the dejected Ren and confesses to him, which he immediately accepts.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_characters">Main characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strobe_Edge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Main characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><b>Ninako Kinoshita</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">木下　仁菜子</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kinoshita Ninako</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Ayako_Kawasumi" title="Ayako Kawasumi">Ayako Kawasumi</a> (vomic), <a href="/wiki/Aki_Toyosaki" title="Aki Toyosaki">Aki Toyosaki</a> (drama CD)</span>, Actor: <a href="/wiki/Kasumi_Arimura" title="Kasumi Arimura">Kasumi Arimura</a> (movie)</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>Ninako is the main female <a href="/wiki/Protagonist" title="Protagonist">protagonist</a> around whom the story revolves. She is a very kind and gentle girl who falls in love with Ren Ichinose. When she finds out that Ren already has a girlfriend, she resigns herself to being just a friend and to quietly hold onto to her feelings. She is good at most of her subjects with the exclusion of mathematics, which she mentions a number of times is her weakest subject. Ninako is very caring and modest, willing to return any favor even if it is something small. Later on, when Andou tries to convince her to go out with him, she rejects him, saying that being in a relationship with someone she does not love is not right. She wants to remain in her feelings of liking Ren, even if they are not reciprocated, because that is whom she is in love with.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><b>Ren Ichinose</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">一ノ瀬　蓮</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ichinose Ren</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Mamoru_Miyano" title="Mamoru Miyano">Mamoru Miyano</a> (vomic), <a href="/wiki/Yuichi_Nakamura_(voice_actor)" title="Yuichi Nakamura (voice actor)">Yūichi Nakamura</a> (drama CD)</span>, Actor: <a href="/wiki/Sota_Fukushi" title="Sota Fukushi">Sota Fukushi</a> (movie)</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>The male school idol who is basically good at everything and plays the role of main male protagonist. He likes to keep himself isolated from most students and is admired by all of the female populous as a result. He has a close group of friends which are male however the story gives the impression most other male students are jealous of his position. He also has a strong passion for mathematics. Although he has a girlfriend, Mayuka, who is older than he is, Ren tends to lose his guard around Ninako. He only later on realizes his feelings for Ninako, but tries to forget about her by distancing himself for Mayuka's sake. In the end, Mayuka breaks up with him because she realized his feelings toward Ninako and that she treats work more important than love. Ren's feelings toward Ninako grows stronger, eventually making him unable to hide it any longer.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><b>Daiki Korenaga</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">是永　大樹</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Korenaga Daiki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Tomoaki_Maeno" title="Tomoaki Maeno">Tomoaki Maeno</a> (vomic), <a href="/wiki/Tetsuya_Kakihara" title="Tetsuya Kakihara">Tetsuya Kakihara</a> (drama CD)</span>, Actor: <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jingi_Irie&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jingi Irie (page does not exist)">Jingi Irie</a> (movie)</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>Daiki is introduced as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Childhood_friend" title="Childhood friend">childhood friend</a> of Ninako's and has strong feelings towards her at the beginning of the story. He grew attached to Ninako after his parents <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Divorced" title="Divorced">divorced</a> two or three years prior to the start of the manga to which he had to make the choice of living with his mother and older sister or his father. Since choosing to live with his father his <a href="/wiki/Interpersonal_relationship" title="Interpersonal relationship">relationship</a> with his older sister has not been so good. He eventually falls in love with and starts dating Sayuri, Ninako's friend.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><b>Takumi Ando</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">安堂　拓海</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Andō Takumi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Tsubasa_Yonaga" title="Tsubasa Yonaga">Tsubasa Yonaga</a> (vomic), <a href="/wiki/Kenn_(Japanese_actor)" title="Kenn (Japanese actor)">Kenn</a> (drama CD)</span>, Actor: <a href="/wiki/Yuki_Yamada" title="Yuki Yamada">Yuki Yamada</a> (movie)</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>Ando is Ren's middle school friend and also has an idol-like status. He often finds himself competing with Ren like a rival at times. Ando takes advantage of his position and is a big time player among the female students; however, he forms no relationships with them and believes himself not fit for a relationship. His opinion changes after meeting Ninako, who has a strong effect upon him. He eventually falls madly in love with her, saying that he loves her honesty, and often tries to win her favor. He is also very much aware of her feelings for Ren and often tries to help her through Ren's relationship with Mayuka or discourage her from pursuing Ren.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><b>Mayuka Korenaga</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">是永　麻由香</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Korenaga Mayuka</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yui_Sh%C5%8Dji&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yui Shōji (page does not exist)">Yui Shōji</a> (vomic and drama CD)</span>, Actor: Arisa Sato (movie)</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>Mayuka is Daiki's older sister. Her existence is also kept low profile until a certain point where it is found that she is actually the girlfriend of the unobtainable Ren Ichinose. She has a job which is in the <a href="/wiki/Model_(person)" title="Model (person)">modelling</a> business which she pursued for what she thought was Ren's sake and she is currently studying for entrance exams to university. As the series progresses, she becomes wary of Ninako's relationship with Ren but remains friendly toward her. She eventually realizes how much her relationship with Ren is strained: she is almost always busy and has to cancel their dates. In chapter 19, she breaks up with Ren because she now knows that Ren is in love with someone else and she doesn't want Ren to continually repress his feelings just for her. She acknowledges that nothing will ever stay the same, as she herself has changed, and that before she didn't want Ren to change and leave her.</dd>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Drama_CDs">Drama CDs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strobe_Edge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Drama CDs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Strobe Edge</i> originally had a vomic (voiceover comic) produced by <a href="/wiki/Shueisha" title="Shueisha">Shueisha</a>, where voice actors provided voices to manga panels. Once plans for drama CDs were made, Shueisha closed down the vomic website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vomic_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-vomic-24">[24]</a></sup> Two drama CDs were released, the first on August 25, 2010 and the second on September 22, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dramaCD1_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-dramaCD1-25">[25]</a></sup> The drama CDs used an entirely new voice cast with the exception of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yui_Sh%C5%8Dji&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yui Shōji (page does not exist)">Yui Shōji</a>, who was the only one to reprise her role as Mayuka Korenaga.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Live-action_film">Live-action film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strobe_Edge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Live-action film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A live-action film adaptation of <i>Strobe Edge</i> was announced to have been greenlit on the August 2014 issue of <i>Bessatsu Margaret</i>. The adaptation had its theatrical release on March 14, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-live-action_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-live-action-1">[1]</a></sup> The film is directed by <a href="/wiki/Ry%C5%ABichi_Hiroki" title="Ryūichi Hiroki">Ryūichi Hiroki</a> and stars <a href="/wiki/Kasumi_Arimura" title="Kasumi Arimura">Kasumi Arimura</a> as Ninako Kinoshita and <a href="/wiki/Sota_Fukushi" title="Sota Fukushi">Sota Fukushi</a> as Ren Ichinose.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-live-action2_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-live-action2-26">[26]</a></sup> Japanese vocal group <a href="/wiki/Greeeen" title="Greeeen">Greeeen</a> will be providing a cover version of their hit song<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-live-action3_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-live-action3-27">[27]</a></sup> "Ai Uta" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">愛唄</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> sung by a sister female vocalist group <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Whiteeeen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Whiteeeen (page does not exist)">Whiteeeen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-live-action4_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-live-action4-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strobe_Edge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Strobe Edge</i> received moderate success in Japan. The fourth volume charted at #29 on <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> during the first week of sales, selling a total of 34,158 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The fifth volume ranked at #19 and sold 46,299 copies on its first week of sales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The release of the sixth volume peaked at #24 and sold 51,402 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The seventh volume charted at #9 and sold 82,575 copies on its first week<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> and 109,758 in total.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The eighth volume charted at #7 and sold 76,372 copies on its first week<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> and 137,087 in total.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The ninth volume charted at #4 and sold 152,907 copies on its first week<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> and 211,188 in total.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> The final volume charted at #3 and sold 132,738 copies on its first week of sales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> It became the 43rd best-selling volume of the first half of 2011, selling a total of 356,021 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a poll done by Escala Cafe in April 2011, out of 316 votes from women from the ages of 22 and 29, 2.5% were interested in a live-action adaptation of <i>Strobe Edge</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was chosen as one of the Great Graphic Novels 2014 in the fiction section by the Young Adult Library Services Association<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> and also as one of the 2014 Top Ten Great Graphic Novels for Teens in the fiction section.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>The live-action film adaptation "earned $2.74 million in its opening frame for the number three spot" at the Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Box_Office" title="Box Office">Box Office</a>, according to <a href="/wiki/Mark_Schilling" title="Mark Schilling">Mark Schilling</a>, writing for <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> after its opening weekend. Schilling noted that "distributor <a href="/wiki/Toho" title="Toho">Toho</a> expects the film to finish with JPY2.5 billion ($20.6 million)."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> The film grossed <span style="white-space: nowrap">¥2.32 billion</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
