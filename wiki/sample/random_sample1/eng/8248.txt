<p>He first came to prominence playing in <a href="/wiki/Midfield" title="Midfield">midfield</a> for <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Football_League_First_Division" title="Scottish Football League First Division">Scottish First Division</a> side <a href="/wiki/Clyde_F.C." title="Clyde F.C.">Clyde</a>, but was converted to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Striker_(football)" title="Striker (football)">striker</a> when he signed for <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen_F.C." title="Aberdeen F.C.">Aberdeen</a>, forging a prolific partnership with <a href="/wiki/Joe_Harper" title="Joe Harper">Joe Harper</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dons_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-dons-2">[2]</a></sup> After winning the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Football_League_Premier_Division" title="Scottish Football League Premier Division">Scottish Premier Division</a> title in <a href="/wiki/1979%E2%80%9380_in_Scottish_football" title="1979–80 in Scottish football">1980</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dons_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-dons-2">[2]</a></sup> he moved to London club <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> where he finished the top scorer in the First Division and won the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> in his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1980-81_in_English_football" title="1980-81 in English football">first season</a> there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dons_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-dons-2">[2]</a></sup> He won a second FA Cup in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1981-82_in_English_football" title="1981-82 in English football">1982</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dons_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-dons-2">[2]</a></sup> and then the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> in 1984, where he scored his penalty in the final as Spurs overcame Anderlecht in a shootout.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spurs_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-spurs-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uefa_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-uefa-4">[4]</a></sup> He also scored for Spurs in their defeat by Liverpool in the <a href="/wiki/1982_Football_League_Cup_Final" title="1982 Football League Cup Final">1982 Football League Cup Final</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spurs_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-spurs-3">[3]</a></sup> Archibald scored 77 goals in 189 appearances for Spurs between 1980 and 1984,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spurs_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-spurs-3">[3]</a></sup> forming successful striking partnerships with <a href="/wiki/Garth_Crooks" title="Garth Crooks">Garth Crooks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Falco" title="Mark Falco">Mark Falco</a>.</p>
<p>In 1984 he joined <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, where in his first season he helped them win the <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> title for the first time in 11 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dons_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-dons-2">[2]</a></sup> He then helped Barcelona reach the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Cup" title="European Cup">European Cup</a> final in 1986, only for his side to lose on penalty kicks to <a href="/wiki/FC_Steaua_Bucure%C8%99ti" title="FC Steaua București">Steaua Bucharest</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hibs_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-hibs-5">[5]</a></sup> He was a popular figure at the Camp Nou until restrictions on fielding foreign players led to him being excluded from the squad in favour of <a href="/wiki/Gary_Lineker" title="Gary Lineker">Gary Lineker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Hughes" title="Mark Hughes">Mark Hughes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hibs_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-hibs-5">[5]</a></sup> He was loaned out to <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> for a spell before to returning to Britain in 1988 to sign for <a href="/wiki/Hibernian_F.C." title="Hibernian F.C.">Hibernian</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hibs_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-hibs-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Archibald scored 16 goals in first season at Hibernian, including the winning goal in November 1988 in a 2-1 win away against <a href="/wiki/Heart_of_Midlothian_F.C." title="Heart of Midlothian F.C.">Hearts</a>, Hibs' first away win against their city rivals in over 10 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hibs_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-hibs-5">[5]</a></sup> He left Hibs in 1990 after falling out with manager <a href="/wiki/Alex_Miller" title="Alex Miller">Alex Miller</a>, and had a brief return to La Liga with <a href="/wiki/RCD_Espanyol" title="RCD Espanyol">RCD Espanyol</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hibs_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-hibs-5">[5]</a></sup> He then joined <a href="/wiki/St_Mirren_F.C." title="St Mirren F.C.">St Mirren</a> and was instrumental in bringing former <i>Barça</i> team-mate <a href="/wiki/V%C3%ADctor_Mu%C3%B1oz" title="Víctor Muñoz">Víctor Muñoz</a> to the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hibs_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-hibs-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Archibald's later career saw him make a handful of appearances at a number of clubs in Scotland, England and Ireland, including a nostalgic return to Clyde.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hibs_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-hibs-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Archibald&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Archibald was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_football_team" title="Scotland national football team">Scottish national team</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1982_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1982 FIFA World Cup">1982</a> and <a href="/wiki/1986_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1986 FIFA World Cup">1986 World Cups</a>.</p>
<p>On 15 November 2009 he was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Football_Hall_of_Fame" title="Scottish Football Hall of Fame">Scottish Football Hall of Fame</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hof_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-hof-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Management_and_the_Airdrie_experiment">Management and the Airdrie experiment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Archibald&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Management and the Airdrie experiment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Archibald's time at East Fife saw the club gain promotion to the First Division, and was also notable for the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_national_football_team" title="Trinidad and Tobago national football team">Trinidad and Tobago internationalists</a> <a href="/wiki/Arnold_Dwarika" title="Arnold Dwarika">Arnold Dwarika</a> and <a href="/wiki/Craig_Demmin" title="Craig Demmin">Craig Demmin</a>. He was sacked in 1996 and retired to his home in Spain for the next few years, working on a number of business interests, including as a <a href="/wiki/Sports_agent" title="Sports agent">football agent</a>.</p>
<p>In 2000 Archibald re-emerged in Scottish football when he mounted a bid to buy financially troubled First Division club <a href="/wiki/Airdrieonians_F.C._(1878)" title="Airdrieonians F.C. (1878)">Airdrieonians</a>. Archibald was allowed to take over the running of the club after being awarded preferred bidder status by the administrators. He installed himself as manager and, using his contacts in Europe, introduced a contingent of Spaniards and other foreign players to the squad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hibs_5-7"><a href="#cite_note-hibs-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although Airdrie supporters were impressed by Archibald and the skills of the imports, he ultimately failed to conclude the purchase of the club, leading to the departure of himself and the foreign players in March 2001,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hibs_5-8"><a href="#cite_note-hibs-5">[5]</a></sup> and causing them to narrowly miss out on relegation to the Second Division. They had also been expelled from the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Cup" title="Scottish Cup">Scottish Cup</a> for failing to fulfill a fixture. Airdrieonians went out of business on 1 May 2002, and a new club called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Airdrie_United_F.C." title="Airdrie United F.C.">Airdrie United</a> was formed and took <a href="/wiki/Clydebank_F.C." title="Clydebank F.C.">Clydebank's</a> place in the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Football_League" title="Scottish Football League">Scottish Football League</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Archibald&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Archibald appeared twice in the same episode of <a href="/wiki/Top_of_the_Pops" title="Top of the Pops">Top of the Pops</a> in 1982, firstly singing "<a href="/wiki/We_Have_a_Dream" title="We Have a Dream">We Have a Dream</a>" with the Scotland World Cup squad starring <a href="/wiki/B._A._Robertson" title="B. A. Robertson">B. A. Robertson</a> and then alongside his Tottenham Hotspur team mates and <a href="/wiki/Chas_%26_Dave" title="Chas &amp; Dave">Chas &amp; Dave</a> singing "<a href="/wiki/Tottenham,_Tottenham" title="Tottenham, Tottenham">Tottenham, Tottenham</a>".</p>
