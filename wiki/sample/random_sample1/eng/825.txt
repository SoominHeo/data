<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bii_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bii_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bii was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. His father is <a href="/wiki/Taiwanese_people" title="Taiwanese people">Taiwanese</a> and his mother is <a href="/wiki/Koreans" title="Koreans">Korean</a>. At the time of his birth, the <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_nationality_law" title="South Korean nationality law">South Korean nationality law</a> did not allow South Korean women who were married to foreigners to transmit their nationality to their children, so he was regarded as <a href="/wiki/Overseas_Chinese" title="Overseas Chinese">overseas Chinese</a> in South Korea. He also has a younger brother, Pil Seo-yeong (Pil Seo Young).</p>
<p>His life in South Korea was reported to be a bit challenging as he did not qualify for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_identity_card_(South_Korea)" title="National identity card (South Korea)">national identity card</a>. He could not afford any health insurance and he had to pay higher tuition fees than his Korean peers.</p>
<p>At the age of 17, SM talent agent scouted Bii, who was a high school student then, but his mother rejected the idea of him being a singer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>His native language is Korean. He did not learn <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Standard_Mandarin" title="Standard Mandarin">Standard Mandarin</a> until he came to <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nationality">Nationality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bii_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Nationality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2011, he finally acquired his nationality in Taiwan (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_China" title="Republic of China">Republic of China</a>). He took a hiatus from the entertainment industry to serve his <a href="/wiki/Conscription_in_Taiwan" title="Conscription in Taiwan">mandatory military service in Taiwan</a> between November 2011 and November 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bii_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Audition">Audition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bii_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Audition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bii was always interested in music. He was the lead singer in his high school band. Later he went to <a href="/wiki/Dongguan" title="Dongguan">Dongguan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mainland_China" title="Mainland China">Mainland China</a> to find his father, who worked there, to find a job. Afterwards, a friend of his father's took him to Taiwan and introduced him to the boss of Eagle Music, where Bii started to pursue a musical career.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Debut">Debut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bii_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Debut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 9, 2010, Bii released his first EP which was titled "Bii." This EP contains a total of 4 songs: 1 in <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a> and the remaining 3 in <a href="/wiki/Mandarin_Chinese" title="Mandarin Chinese">Mandarin</a>. On October 6, 2010, Bii released his first album, "Bii Story" which has been sung in both Korean and Mandarin.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stage_Name">Stage Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bii_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Stage Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The name "Bii" stands for "Be-I-I," indicating his dual personas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bii_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As a singer and songwriter, he has composed a lot of songs or written the lyrics in Korean and Mandarin. Many of Bii's songs were the theme songs of TV series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> His songs have been featured in many TV series, such as <i>Zhong Wu Yan</i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Around" title="Love Around">Love Around</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Someone_Like_You_(TV_series)" title="Someone Like You (TV series)">Someone Like You</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Bromance_(2015_TV_series)" title="Bromance (2015 TV series)">Bromance</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Wolf" title="Prince of Wolf">Prince of Wolf</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Better_Man_(2016_TV_series)" title="Better Man (2016 TV series)">Better Man</a></i> in Taiwan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">Acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bii_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bii made a cameo appearance in the TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Around" title="Love Around">Love Around</a></i> (2013) as Peter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He appeared in Episode 1 of the TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Someone_Like_You_(TV_series)" title="Someone Like You (TV series)">Someone Like You</a></i> (2015) as Gu Long, who raced against Fang Zan Cheng played by <a href="/wiki/Kingone_Wang" title="Kingone Wang">Kingone Wang</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He appeared as Wei Qingyang in a romance comedy, <i><a href="/wiki/Bromance_(2015_TV_series)" title="Bromance (2015 TV series)">Bromance</a></i> (2015), co-starring with Baron Chen, Megan Lai, Sean Lee, and Katie Chen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
