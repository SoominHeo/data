<p><i><b>The Life Aquatic with Steve Zissou</b></i> is a 2004 American <a href="/wiki/Comedy-drama" title="Comedy-drama">comedy-drama</a> film directed, co-written, and co-produced by <a href="/wiki/Wes_Anderson" title="Wes Anderson">Wes Anderson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It is Anderson's fourth feature-length film and was released in the United States on 25 December, 2004. It was written by Anderson and <a href="/wiki/Noah_Baumbach" title="Noah Baumbach">Noah Baumbach</a> and was filmed in and around <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ponza" title="Ponza">Ponza</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Riviera" title="Italian Riviera">Italian Riviera</a>.</p>
<p>The film stars <a href="/wiki/Bill_Murray" title="Bill Murray">Bill Murray</a> as the eponymous Zissou, an eccentric <a href="/wiki/Oceanography" title="Oceanography">oceanographer</a> who sets out to exact revenge on the "jaguar <a href="/wiki/Shark" title="Shark">shark</a>" that ate his partner Esteban. Zissou is both a <a href="/wiki/Parody" title="Parody">parody</a> of and <a href="/wiki/Homage_(arts)" title="Homage (arts)">homage</a> to French diving pioneer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jacques-Yves_Cousteau" title="Jacques-Yves Cousteau">Jacques-Yves Cousteau</a> (1910–1997), to whom the film is dedicated. <a href="/wiki/Cate_Blanchett" title="Cate Blanchett">Cate Blanchett</a>, <a href="/wiki/Willem_Dafoe" title="Willem Dafoe">Willem Dafoe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Gambon" title="Michael Gambon">Michael Gambon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Goldblum" title="Jeff Goldblum">Jeff Goldblum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anjelica_Huston" title="Anjelica Huston">Anjelica Huston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Owen_Wilson" title="Owen Wilson">Owen Wilson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seu_Jorge" title="Seu Jorge">Seu Jorge</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bud_Cort" title="Bud Cort">Bud Cort</a> are also featured in the film.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Life_Aquatic_with_Steve_Zissou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While oceanographer Steve Zissou is working on his latest documentary at sea, his best friend Esteban du Plantier is eaten by a creature Zissou describes as a "Jaguar shark." For his next project, Zissou is determined to document the shark's destruction.</p>
<p>The crew aboard Zissou's aging research vessel <i>Belafonte</i> includes his estranged wife Eleanor, chief strategist and financial backer; Pelé dos Santos, a safety expert and Brazilian guitarist who sings <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a> songs in Portuguese; and Klaus Daimler, the German first mate who views Zissou and Esteban as father figures. Minor crew members include Vikram Ray, cameraman; Bobby Ogata, frogman; Vladimir Wolodarsky, physicist and soundtrack composer; Renzo Pietro, sound man; and Anne-Marie Sakowitz, script girl. Also included is a recent group of unpaid interns from the University of North Alaska. The "Team Zissou" venture has hit a decline, however; having not released a successful documentary in nine years.</p>
<p>Ned Plimpton is a longtime Zissou fan whose mother had recently died, and he believes that Zissou is his father. After they meet at Zissou's latest premiere, Ned takes leave from his job as an airline pilot in Kentucky to join his crew. As Oseary Drakoulias, Zissou's producer, can not find anyone to finance their latest documentary, Ned offers his inheritance. Eleanor feels her husband is taking advantage of Ned and leaves. A reporter, Jane Winslett-Richardson, who is pregnant comes, to chronicle the voyage. Both Ned and Zissou are infatuated with Jane, and a rivalry develops between them. Klaus becomes envious of the attention Zissou pays to Ned.</p>
<p>On their mission to find the Jaguar shark, the <i>Belafonte</i> steals tracking equipment from a remote station owned by Alistair Hennessey, a more successful oceanographer and Zissou's nemesis. They then sail into unprotected waters and are attacked by Filipino pirates, who steal Ned's money and kidnap Bill Ubell, a "<a href="/wiki/Corporate_bond" title="Corporate bond">bond company</a> stooge" assigned to the project. They are then rescued by Hennessey and towed to <a href="/wiki/Ponza" title="Ponza">Port-au-Partois</a>. Sakowitz, along with all but one of the interns, jump ship once they reach port. Zissou convinces Eleanor to rejoin the <i>Belafonte</i>, and then leads the crew on a rescue mission; they save Bill, along with Hennessey, who also becomes kidnapped by the pirates.</p>
<p>While Ned and Zissou make one last search for the shark in the ship's helicopter, the aircraft malfunctions and they crash. Ned dies from his injuries and is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buried_at_sea" title="Buried at sea">buried at sea</a>. Prior to Ned's death, Eleanor revealed to Jane that Zissou is actually sterile, therefore Ned could not have been his son.</p>
<p>Zissou finally tracks down the shark in a submersible but he decides not to kill it, both because of its beauty and not having any dynamite. At the premiere of the finished documentary (which is dedicated to Ned), Zissou receives a standing ovation while waiting outside the theater for the premiere to finish. The crew returns triumphantly to the ship the next day.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Life_Aquatic_with_Steve_Zissou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Literary_inspiration">Literary inspiration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Life_Aquatic_with_Steve_Zissou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Literary inspiration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Though the characters were inspired by such American novels as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Great_Gatsby" title="The Great Gatsby">The Great Gatsby</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Magnificent_Ambersons" title="The Magnificent Ambersons">The Magnificent Ambersons</a></i>, the plot has been compared to <i><a href="/wiki/Moby-Dick" title="Moby-Dick">Moby-Dick</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Writing about the metaphorical aspects of the film's setting — somewhere in the Mediterranean — film critic Elena Past says that the underwater scenes, because they are central to the storyline, make <i>The Life Aquatic</i> similar in some ways to <i><a href="/wiki/Respiro" title="Respiro">Respiro</a></i>. Both films set out a "Mediterranean state of being" where "having left the security of land, the characters in both films are suddenly confronted with the precarious nature of human existence, as the films that depict them tackle the challenges of representing the submarine world."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music <span id="Music"></span><span id="Score"></span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Life_Aquatic_with_Steve_Zissou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/The_Life_Aquatic_with_Steve_Zissou_(soundtrack)" title="The Life Aquatic with Steve Zissou (soundtrack)">The Life Aquatic with Steve Zissou (soundtrack)</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Life_Aquatic_Studio_Sessions" title="The Life Aquatic Studio Sessions">The Life Aquatic Studio Sessions</a></div>
<p>The soundtrack to <i>The Life Aquatic with Steve Zissou</i> contains a style typical of other Wes Anderson films. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Mothersbaugh" title="Mark Mothersbaugh">Mark Mothersbaugh</a>, a member of <a href="/wiki/Devo" title="Devo">Devo</a>, composed the score, as he has for many of Anderson's other films. The film also features many rock songs from the 1960s-1980s, and several instrumental pieces composed by <a href="/wiki/Sven_Libaek" title="Sven Libaek">Sven Libaek</a> for the underwater documentary television series <i>Inner Space</i>. Additionally, the film and soundtrack feature <a href="/wiki/Seu_Jorge" title="Seu Jorge">Seu Jorge</a> performing <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a> songs in Portuguese on the acoustic guitar. Jorge, who also plays the character of Pelé dos Santos, performs some of these cover songs live, in character during the film, mostly with modified lyrics reflecting Jorge's own experiences working on the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The ending scene depicting the beauty of the shark features the song "<a href="/wiki/%C3%81g%C3%A6tis_byrjun" title="Ágætis byrjun">Starálfur</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Sigur_R%C3%B3s" title="Sigur Rós">Sigur Rós</a>.</p>
<p><i>The Life Aquatic</i> is Anderson's first film not to feature a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rolling_Stones" title="Rolling Stones">Rolling Stones</a> song.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Life_Aquatic_with_Steve_Zissou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Life_Aquatic_with_Steve_Zissou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film grossed a total of $24,020,403 domestically after twelve weeks in release, less than half its $50 million production budget. It took in a further $10,788,000 internationally, bringing the total gross to $34,808,403.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mojo_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response <span id="Critics"></span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Life_Aquatic_with_Steve_Zissou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>The Life Aquatic with Steve Zissou</i> has a 56% approval rating on <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, based on 199 reviews, with an average rating of 6.1/10; the website's consensus states: "<i>The Life Aquatic with Steve Zissou</i> is getting soaked by many critics, who call it smug, ironic and artificial. Still, others have praised the movie's sheer uniqueness, eccentricity and whimsy."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The film has a 62/100 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weighted_average" title="Weighted average">weighted average</a> score on <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, indicating "generally favorable reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Anthony_Lane" title="Anthony Lane">Anthony Lane</a>, a film reviewer for <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Yorker" title="The New Yorker">The New Yorker</a></i>, agreed with the conventional criticism of Anderson's <a href="/wiki/Deadpan" title="Deadpan">deadpan</a> style: that the underreaction of Anderson's characters used to be "hip" but has now become "frozen into a mannerism." He said that "some stretches of action" in the film are being "lightly held within quotation marks," with an "unmistakable air of playacting" in even the most violent scenes. He also criticized the film's deliberately "weird" set ups, which leave the viewer with "the impression of having nearly drowned in some secret and melancholy game."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Life_Aquatic_with_Steve_Zissou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Two home video formats of the film were released on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> in May 10, 2005: a 1-disc version and a 2-disc version, both as part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Criterion_Collection" title="Criterion Collection">Criterion Collection</a>. This is Anderson's third film to be released in the collection, after <i><a href="/wiki/Rushmore_(film)" title="Rushmore (film)">Rushmore</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Royal_Tenenbaums" title="The Royal Tenenbaums">The Royal Tenenbaums</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The Criterion <a href="/wiki/Blu-ray" title="Blu-ray">Blu-ray</a> was released on 27 May 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
