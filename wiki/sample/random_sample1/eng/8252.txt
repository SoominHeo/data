<p><b>Stephen "Steve" Kean</b> (born 30 September 1967) is a <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scottish</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> <a href="/wiki/Manager_(association_football)" title="Manager (association football)">manager</a> and former player. He is currently the manager of <a href="/wiki/Brunei_DPMM_FC" title="Brunei DPMM FC">Brunei DPMM</a> in the <a href="/wiki/S.League" title="S.League">S.League</a>. A winger, he spent his playing career in the lower divisions of Scottish, English and Portuguese leagues in the 1980s and 1990s, before moving into coaching. After a 21-month spell in charge of <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a>, he resigned on 28 September 2012.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_career">Playing career</span></h2>
<p>Kean grew up in <a href="/wiki/Cumbernauld" title="Cumbernauld">Cumbernauld</a>, a new town on the outskirts of <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a>, attending St. Maurice's High School. Upon leaving school, he joined <a href="/wiki/Celtic_F.C." title="Celtic F.C.">Celtic</a> as a professional, but was unable to break into a first team then dominated by Celtic legends <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Burns_(footballer)" title="Tommy Burns (footballer)">Tommy Burns</a>, <a href="/wiki/Murdo_MacLeod" title="Murdo MacLeod">Murdo MacLeod</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_McStay" title="Paul McStay">Paul McStay</a>.</p>
<p>He joined <a href="/wiki/Swansea_City_A.F.C." title="Swansea City A.F.C.">Swansea City</a> on loan in 1987,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> at the same time as <a href="/wiki/Chris_Coleman_(footballer)" title="Chris Coleman (footballer)">Chris Coleman</a>. They formed a lasting professional relationship, which carried on after both of their playing days had ended.</p>
<p>Kean then played for <a href="/wiki/Associa%C3%A7%C3%A3o_Acad%C3%A9mica_de_Coimbra_%E2%80%93_O.A.F." title="Associação Académica de Coimbra – O.A.F.">Academica Coimbra</a> in Portugal, making 12 appearances, and scoring one goal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Statistics_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Statistics-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kean_linked_to_Chelsea_coach_role_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kean_linked_to_Chelsea_coach_role-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_and_management_career">Coaching and management career</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">Coaching career</span></h3>
<p>Since retiring from playing Kean has worked as a coach for <a href="/wiki/Reading_F.C." title="Reading F.C.">Reading</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Real_Sociedad" title="Real Sociedad">Real Sociedad</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coventry_City_F.C." title="Coventry City F.C.">Coventry City</a>, working as an assistant to <a href="/wiki/Chris_Coleman_(footballer)" title="Chris Coleman (footballer)">Chris Coleman</a> at Fulham, Real Sociedad and Coventry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kean_linked_to_Chelsea_coach_role_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Kean_linked_to_Chelsea_coach_role-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2008, it was reported that Kean was in talks with <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> to become assistant manager to <a href="/wiki/Luiz_Felipe_Scolari" title="Luiz Felipe Scolari">Luiz Felipe Scolari</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kean_linked_to_Chelsea_coach_role_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Kean_linked_to_Chelsea_coach_role-4">[4]</a></sup> Kean's fluency in Portuguese from his playing days was believed to be a key reason for Chelsea's interest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kean_linked_to_Chelsea_coach_role_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Kean_linked_to_Chelsea_coach_role-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Blackburn_Rovers">Blackburn Rovers</span></h3>
<p>On 4 August 2009, <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> manager <a href="/wiki/Sam_Allardyce" title="Sam Allardyce">Sam Allardyce</a> appointed Kean as his new first team coach. Kean replaced <a href="/wiki/Karl_Robinson" title="Karl Robinson">Karl Robinson</a> who returned to <a href="/wiki/Milton_Keynes_Dons_F.C." title="Milton Keynes Dons F.C.">Milton Keynes Dons</a>. Allardyce commented that he had received numerous applications for the first team coaching position and Kean "stood out above the rest through his personality, experience and knowledge of football at the highest level".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stoodout_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-stoodout-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the sacking of Allardyce on 13 December 2010 by new Blackburn owners <a href="/wiki/Venky%27s" title="Venky's">Venky's</a>, Kean was initially installed as <a href="/wiki/Caretaker_manager" title="Caretaker manager">caretaker manager</a> but on 22 December 2010, he was appointed manager on a contract until the end of the season to prove his abilities in that position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blackburn_Rovers_sack_manager_Sam_Allardyce_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Blackburn_Rovers_sack_manager_Sam_Allardyce-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Steve_Kean_put_in_charge_by_Blackburn_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Steve_Kean_put_in_charge_by_Blackburn-7">[7]</a></sup> On 4 January 2011, it was revealed by Anuradha Desai, chairperson of Venky's, that Kean would be offered a new two-to-three-year long-term contract to manage the club and on 20 January 2011, it was confirmed by Blackburn that Kean had signed a contract until June 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kean_to_land_long-term_deal_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kean_to_land_long-term_deal-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kean_signs_contract_to_June_2013_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kean_signs_contract_to_June_2013-9">[9]</a></sup> Kean's appointment was shrouded in a great deal of controversy since his agent <a href="/wiki/Jerome_Anderson_(football_agent)" title="Jerome Anderson (football agent)">Jerome Anderson</a> had earlier played a major role in advising Venky's during the takeover of the club in the preceding months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-How_an_agent_came_to_hold_so_much_power_at_Blackburn_Rovers_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-How_an_agent_came_to_hold_so_much_power_at_Blackburn_Rovers-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Steve_Kean_finds_value_of_friends_in_high_places_at_Blackburn_Rovers_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Steve_Kean_finds_value_of_friends_in_high_places_at_Blackburn_Rovers-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Furthermore, Allardyce later received "substantial", but undisclosed, damages from Kean after Kean had been recorded in a bar in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> falsely alleging that Allardyce had been sacked from his post at Blackburn Rovers because he was a crook.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SteveKeandamages_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-SteveKeandamages-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kean started brightly as manager, but <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Blackburn_Rovers_F.C._season#February" title="2010–11 Blackburn Rovers F.C. season">ten games without a win</a> saw Rovers dragged into relegation trouble. A 15th-place finish was secured with a 3-2 win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers" title="Wolverhampton Wanderers">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a> on the final day of the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Premier_League" title="2010–11 Premier League">2010–11 Premier League</a> season.</p>
<p>In November 2011, Blackburn confirmed that Kean had signed a new contract with improved terms, despite the club being in the bottom place of the league. This came despite Kean's comments less than a week earlier stating: "Yes, there have been discussions about a new contract, but I have told the owners that this is an inappropriate time to conclude any new contract discussions".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Steve_Kean_contradicts_earlier_statement_he_made_to_ESPN_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Steve_Kean_contradicts_earlier_statement_he_made_to_ESPN-14">[14]</a></sup> The new deal came after months of protests at the club in which fans campaigned to have Kean removed as Rovers manager as he had recorded just one league win all season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Anti-Steve_Kean_banner_flown_by_Blackburn_Rovers_fans_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Anti-Steve_Kean_banner_flown_by_Blackburn_Rovers_fans-15">[15]</a></sup> At the time, Steve Kean was Blackburn Rovers' second worst manager since 1992, with only <a href="/wiki/Paul_Ince" title="Paul Ince">Paul Ince</a> scoring a lower win percentage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kean_out.3F_How_Kean_fares_historically_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kean_out.3F_How_Kean_fares_historically-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Further controversy was created when Kean admitted 'forfeiting' a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carling_Cup" title="Carling Cup">Carling Cup</a> quarter-final at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardiff_City" title="Cardiff City">Cardiff City</a> for the sake of their league form, with fans subsequently demanding a refund for the ticket price, travel expenses and money lost due to not working on the day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blackburn_boss_Steve_Kean_admits_Carling_Cup_.27forfeit.27_at_Cardiff_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Blackburn_boss_Steve_Kean_admits_Carling_Cup_.27forfeit.27_at_Cardiff-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rovers_fans_demand_refund_after_Kean_admits_to_.27forfeiting.27_Cup_game_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rovers_fans_demand_refund_after_Kean_admits_to_.27forfeiting.27_Cup_game-18">[18]</a></sup> In December 2011, the <a href="/wiki/Lancashire_Telegraph" title="Lancashire Telegraph">local newspaper</a> and MP <a href="/wiki/Jack_Straw" title="Jack Straw">Jack Straw</a> joined the fans in calling for Kean's resignation ahead of a crucial home match with local rivals <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers" title="Bolton Wanderers">Bolton Wanderers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_to_go_Steve_Kean_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Time_to_go_Steve_Kean-19">[19]</a></sup> Another defeat concluded Kean's first 38 league games as Blackburn manager in which he had amassed just 32 points and left Rovers <a href="/wiki/Premier_League%E2%80%93Football_League_gulf#Curse_of_Christmas" title="Premier League–Football League gulf">bottom of the table at Christmas.</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blackburn_Rovers_1-2_Bolton_Wanderers.2C_20th_December_2011_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Blackburn_Rovers_1-2_Bolton_Wanderers.2C_20th_December_2011-20">[20]</a></sup> Despite all of this, Kean remained defiant by insisting that he would stay in his job long enough to fashion a revival and that he would be "100% shocked" if he was sacked.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Under-fire_Kean_banking_on_season_of_goodwill_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Under-fire_Kean_banking_on_season_of_goodwill-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blackburn_Rovers_boss_Steve_Kean_.27100.25_shocked.27_if_he_was_sacked_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Blackburn_Rovers_boss_Steve_Kean_.27100.25_shocked.27_if_he_was_sacked-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Results were somewhat improved in the second half of the season but <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Blackburn_Rovers_F.C._season#Matches" title="2011–12 Blackburn Rovers F.C. season">five successive defeats</a> in April dragged Rovers deep into relegation trouble again with Kean having recorded just 12 wins from his 55 league games since he took over. On 7 May 2012, a home loss against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic" title="Wigan Athletic">Wigan Athletic</a> relegated Blackburn Rovers to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Football League Championship</a> and ended an 11-year run in the top flight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kean left his position as Rovers' manager on 28 September 2012 with the club third in the Championship table. He declared that he had been "forced to resign" and that his position had been "untenable".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brunei_DPMM">Brunei DPMM</span></h3>
<p>In October 2013 Kean agreed a deal to become the new manager of <a href="/wiki/S.League" title="S.League">S.League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Brunei_DPMM_FC" title="Brunei DPMM FC">Brunei DPMM</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Kean signed an initial one-year contract with the club in <a href="/wiki/Brunei" title="Brunei">Brunei</a> on 28 November 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Brunei led the <a href="/wiki/2014_S.League" title="2014 S.League">2014 S.League</a> for the majority of the campaign but ended up finishing as runners up to <a href="/wiki/Warriors_FC" title="Warriors FC">Warriors</a> following a 2-1 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Tampines_Rovers_FC" title="Tampines Rovers FC">Tampines Rovers</a> on the final day of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> They were also knocked out of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Singapore_Cup" title="2014 Singapore Cup">2014 Singapore Cup</a> in the semi-final by <a href="/wiki/Home_United_FC" title="Home United FC">Home United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The team did lift some silverware however, as they won the <a href="/wiki/2014_Singapore_League_Cup" title="2014 Singapore League Cup">2014 Singapore League Cup</a>, beating <a href="/wiki/Tanjong_Pagar_United_FC" title="Tanjong Pagar United FC">Tanjong Pagar United</a> 2-0 in the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kean was also appointed as the head coach of <a href="/wiki/Brunei_national_football_team" title="Brunei national football team">Brunei national football team</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2014_AFF_Suzuki_Cup_qualification" title="2014 AFF Suzuki Cup qualification">2014 AFF Suzuki Cup qualification</a> tournament in Laos in October 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Brunei DPMM clinched the S-League championship on 21 November 2015, with a 4-0 win at home to <a href="/wiki/Balestier_Khalsa_FC" title="Balestier Khalsa FC">Balestier Khalsa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sleague15_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-sleague15-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>On 15 August 2011, Kean was convicted of <a href="/wiki/Driving_under_the_influence" title="Driving under the influence">drink driving</a> at Macclesfield Magistrates' Court. The Court rejected his defence that his drinks had been spiked following a match against Manchester United. He was banned from driving for 18 months and fined £1,800.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blackburn_manager_Steve_Kean_banned_for_drink_driving_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Blackburn_manager_Steve_Kean_banned_for_drink_driving-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
