<p><b>Stephen John "Steve" Finnan</b> (born 24 April 1976)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hugman0506_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hugman0506-1">[1]</a></sup> is a retired Irish <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">international</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">right back</a>.</p>
<p>He is the only player to have played in the World Cup, UEFA Champions League, UEFA Cup, Intertoto Cup, all four levels of the English league football and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_Conference" title="Football Conference">Football Conference</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Among the honours won during his career was the <a href="/wiki/2005_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2005 UEFA Champions League Final">2005 UEFA Champions League Final</a> for <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>. Finnan is known for his attacking role and ability to cross the ball.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>He played 52 internationals for the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Republic of Ireland</a> from his debut in 2000, scoring twice. He played for them at the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 FIFA World Cup</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Finnan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Finnan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Finnan was born in the Janesboro area of <a href="/wiki/Limerick" title="Limerick">Limerick</a>, and moved to <a href="/wiki/Chelmsford" title="Chelmsford">Chelmsford</a>, England, at a young age.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He began his career playing in <a href="/wiki/Wimbledon_F.C." title="Wimbledon F.C.">Wimbledon</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Youth_system" title="Youth system">youth system</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-What.27s_in_a_name:_Steve_Finnan_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-What.27s_in_a_name:_Steve_Finnan-5">[5]</a></sup> but he joined <a href="/wiki/Non-League_football" title="Non-League football">non-League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Welling_United_F.C." title="Welling United F.C.">Welling United</a> in 1993 after being released at the age of 16. Finnan turned professional when he signed with <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C." title="Birmingham City F.C.">Birmingham City</a> in 1995, who paid a <a href="/wiki/Fee" title="Fee">fee</a> of £100,000 to Welling United to acquire his services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He scored his first professional goal against <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Notts_County">Notts County</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Finnan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Notts County">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Finnan made a <a href="/wiki/Loan_(sports)" title="Loan (sports)">loan</a> switch to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Notts_County" title="Notts County">Notts County</a> in March 1996, making his debut in a 2–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/Walsall_F.C." title="Walsall F.C.">Walsall</a> and scoring his first goal a month later in a 4–2 victory over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bristol_Rovers" title="Bristol Rovers">Bristol Rovers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Magpies_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Magpies-9">[9]</a></sup> He appeared in all of Notts' game for the remainder of the season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Magpies_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Magpies-9">[9]</a></sup> helping the team finish fourth in the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Second_Division" title="Football League Second Division">Second Division</a> and qualify for the <a href="/wiki/Promotion_and_relegation" title="Promotion and relegation">promotion</a> play-offs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In the first leg of the semi-finals held at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexandra_Stadium" title="Alexandra Stadium">Alexandra Stadium</a> he scored against <a href="/wiki/Crewe_Alexandra_F.C." title="Crewe Alexandra F.C.">Crewe Alexandra</a>, with the match ending 2–2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Magpies_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-Magpies-9">[9]</a></sup> The return leg at home saw Notts win 1–0, winning 3–2 on aggregate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Magpies_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-Magpies-9">[9]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/1996_Football_League_Second_Division_play-off_Final" title="1996 Football League Second Division play-off Final">play-offs final</a> against <a href="/wiki/Bradford_City_A.F.C." title="Bradford City A.F.C.">Bradford City</a> at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium_(1923)" title="Wembley Stadium (1923)">Wembley Stadium</a>, Notts County lost 2–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Magpies_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-Magpies-9">[9]</a></sup> He returned to Birmingham following the conclusion of the loan deal.</p>
<p>During the following season, <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%9397_in_English_football" title="1996–97 in English football">1996–97</a>, Notts County signed Finnan for a fee £300,000 on 30 October 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He played his first match as a permanent player for Notts County after being introduced as a <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> against <a href="/wiki/Shrewsbury_Town_F.C." title="Shrewsbury Town F.C.">Shrewsbury Town</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The season was not a successful one for Finnan as Notts County finished 24th in the table and were relegated to the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Third_Division" title="Football League Third Division">Third Division</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/1997%E2%80%9398_in_English_football" title="1997–98 in English football">1997–98</a> season, his first full season at the club, Finnan established himself as a key member of the team and was an ever present, appearing in 51 matches,<cite class="citation web"><a class="external text" href="http://www.soccerbase.com/teams/team.sd?team_id=1871&amp;teamTabs=stats&amp;season_id=127" rel="nofollow">"Notts County 1997/1998 player appearances"</a>. Soccerbase<span class="reference-accessdate">. Retrieved <span class="nowrap">12 December</span> 2013</span>.</cite><span class="Z3988" title="ctx_ver=Z39.88-2004&amp;rfr_id=info%3Asid%2Fen.wikipedia.org%3ASteve+Finnan&amp;rft.btitle=Notts+County+1997%2F1998+player+appearances&amp;rft.genre=unknown&amp;rft_id=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.soccerbase.com%2Fteams%2Fteam.sd%3Fteam_id%3D1871%26teamTabs%3Dstats%26season_id%3D127&amp;rft.pub=Soccerbase&amp;rft_val_fmt=info%3Aofi%2Ffmt%3Akev%3Amtx%3Abook"><span style="display:none;"> </span></span> helping the club win the Third Division title with record points and time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The other notable record Finnan established with Notts County during this season was a streak of 10 consecutive victories; a club record and one which still stands today.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fulham">Fulham</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Finnan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Fulham">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After strong showings for Notts County in the 1997–98 season, <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a> manager <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Keegan" title="Kevin Keegan">Kevin Keegan</a> paid £600,000 for his services in November 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He made his Fulham debut against <a href="/wiki/Chesterfield_F.C." title="Chesterfield F.C.">Chesterfield</a> on 21 November 1998 and scored his first goal for Fulham against <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_F.C." title="Blackpool F.C.">Blackpool</a> on 20 March 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> While at Fulham, he also became a favourite among the fans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> His first season with the club was a success, with Fulham winning the Second Division championship and being promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_First_Division" title="Football League First Division">First Division</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Finnan finished ninth with Fulham the following season under new manager <a href="/wiki/Paul_Bracewell" title="Paul Bracewell">Paul Bracewell</a>, despite promising early season form and an unbeaten run lasting over two months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Bracewell was replaced with <a href="/wiki/Jean_Tigana" title="Jean Tigana">Jean Tigana</a> in May 2000, and under him in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2000-01_in_English_football" title="2000-01 in English football">2000–01</a> season, Fulham won the First Division championship, winning promotion to the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>, with Finnan appearing in all but one of the league matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In his debut season in the Premier League, he helped Fulham qualify for the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Intertoto_Cup" title="UEFA Intertoto Cup">UEFA Intertoto Cup</a>, was voted into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2001-02_in_English_football" title="2001-02 in English football">2001–02</a> <a href="/wiki/Professional_Footballers%27_Association" title="Professional Footballers' Association">Professional Footballers' Association</a> team of the year and was also voted as the Fulham player of the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Fulham then went on to win the Intertoto Cup, giving Finnan his first taste of European football.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Next season saw Jean Tigana replaced by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Coleman_(footballer)" title="Chris Coleman (footballer)">Chris Coleman</a>, Finnan's fourth manager in five seasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> By April 2003 with few games to go, Fulham were close to the relegation zone but picked up 10 points out of a possible 15 to stay in the top-flight, finishing in 14th position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the summer of 2003 Finnan found himself courted by many of England's top clubs,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> and eventually Fulham agreed to sell Finnan to Liverpool for a fee of £3.5m.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hugman0506_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hugman0506-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Liverpool">Liverpool</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Finnan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Liverpool">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:162px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Steve_Finnan_2010.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="987" data-file-width="555" height="285" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Steve_Finnan_2010.jpg/160px-Steve_Finnan_2010.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Steve_Finnan_2010.jpg/240px-Steve_Finnan_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Steve_Finnan_2010.jpg/320px-Steve_Finnan_2010.jpg 2x" width="160"/></a>

<p>Finnan made his Liverpool debut against Chelsea on 17 August 2003. His first season was disrupted by injury,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> but in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2004-05_in_English_football" title="2004-05 in English football">2004–05</a>, he established himself as the first-choice right back and a firm fan favourite.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The season was a highly successful one for Finnan. He scored his first and only goal for Liverpool against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion" title="West Bromwich Albion">West Bromwich Albion</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> played in the <a href="/wiki/2005_Football_League_Cup_Final" title="2005 Football League Cup Final">League Cup final</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> and was in the starting eleven as Liverpool won the <a href="/wiki/2005_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2005 UEFA Champions League Final">Champions League Final</a>, though a thigh injury meant he was substituted at half time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Finnan's goal against WBA meant that he had scored in each of the five highest divisions of English football, a record he shares with <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Willis" title="Jimmy Willis">Jimmy Willis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2005-06_in_English_football" title="2005-06 in English football">2005–06</a> season, Liverpool broke their record for number of points in a Premier League season, finishing on 82 points with Finnan being ever present.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> At the end of the season he picked up another medal with Liverpool, winning the <a href="/wiki/2006_FA_Cup_Final" title="2006 FA Cup Final">FA Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>Finnan played for Liverpool as first choice right back for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006-07_in_English_football" title="2006-07 in English football">2006–07</a> season despite heavy competition from newly arrived team-mate <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lvaro_Arbeloa" title="Álvaro Arbeloa">Álvaro Arbeloa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> He started in the <a href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2007 UEFA Champions League Final">2007 UEFA Champions League Final</a> and was subbed off after 88 minutes, Liverpool went on to lose the game 2–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> In July 2007, Finnan agreed a new two-year contract to remain with Liverpool.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>The following season, Liverpool's success continued as they finished third in the League and reached the semi-finals of the Champions League. Finnan took his total appearances for Liverpool past the 200 mark and also made it into the top 100 capped Liverpool players of all time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Finnan featured in 35 games, but lost his starting place, manager <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rafa_Ben%C3%ADtez" title="Rafa Benítez">Rafa Benítez</a> often preferring Arbeloa to start.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> In the summer of 2008, after the arrival of fullback <a href="/wiki/Philipp_Degen" title="Philipp Degen">Philipp Degen</a> at the club from <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a>, Finnan was linked with a move away from <a href="/wiki/Anfield" title="Anfield">Anfield</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> He was offered by Liverpool as a makeweight in a deal to sign <a href="/wiki/Gareth_Barry" title="Gareth Barry">Gareth Barry</a> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aston_Villa" title="Aston Villa">Aston Villa</a>; the player accepted the move but the clubs were unable to agree a fee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> He made a guest appearance in <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Carragher" title="Jamie Carragher">Jamie Carragher</a>'s testimonial match in September 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Espanyol">Espanyol</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Finnan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Espanyol">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On transfer deadline day, 1 September 2008, Finnan signed a two-year deal with La Liga club <a href="/wiki/RCD_Espanyol" title="RCD Espanyol">Espanyol</a> for an undisclosed fee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Injuries limited his first team appearances,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> and rumours of a return to the Premier League in the January 2009 transfer window with <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> or <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> were dismissed by Espanyol's sports director <a href="/wiki/Paco_Herrera" title="Paco Herrera">Paco Herrera</a> and by coach <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Manuel_Esnal" title="José Manuel Esnal">Mané</a>, who considered him an important member of the team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> By mid-January, Espanyol were "surprised" that a move to <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a> did not proceed, apparently for medical reasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> In July 2009, Espanyol and Finnan agreed to mutually terminate the remainder of his contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Portsmouth">Portsmouth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Finnan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Portsmouth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Finnan signed a one-year contract with Premier League side <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a> on 31 July 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> He made his debut for the club on 3 October in a 1–0 victory against <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a> at <a href="/wiki/Molineux_Stadium" title="Molineux Stadium">Molineux</a>, and played regularly in the second half of the season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> but with the club in <a href="/wiki/Administration_(law)" title="Administration (law)">administration</a> and relegated to the Championship, he was not offered a new deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> His last appearance for the club was the <a href="/wiki/2010_FA_Cup_Final" title="2010 FA Cup Final">2010 FA Cup Final</a> against <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Finnan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Republic of Ireland</a> Under-21 international, Finnan stepped up to the senior side in 2000 against <a href="/wiki/Greece_national_football_team" title="Greece national football team">Greece</a>. He cemented his place in the Irish starting XI during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup_(qualification_UEFA)" title="2002 FIFA World Cup (qualification UEFA)">2002 World Cup qualification</a> matches, supplying the cross for <a href="/wiki/Jason_McAteer" title="Jason McAteer">Jason McAteer</a> to score the only goal in a crucial 1–0 home win against the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a> in September 2001.</p>
<p>Finnan appeared in all three of Ireland's group E games in the 2002 FIFA World Cup against <a href="/wiki/Cameroon_national_football_team" title="Cameroon national football team">Cameroon</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_national_football_team" title="German national football team">Germany</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia_national_football_team" title="Saudi Arabia national football team">Saudi Arabia</a>. Ireland progressed to the second round of the tournament for only the third time in their history where they faced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_national_football_team" title="Spanish national football team">Spain</a>. The match ended 1–1 in extra time and went to penalties. Finnan took a penalty and scored, but Ireland eventually lost 3–2.</p>
<p>Injury disrupted Finnan's participation in the unsuccessful attempt to reach the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2004_European_Football_Championship" title="2004 European Football Championship">Euro 2004</a> finals. He was a regular choice in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup_(qualification_UEFA)" title="2006 FIFA World Cup (qualification UEFA)">2006 World Cup qualification</a> games but Ireland failed to qualify for the finals.</p>
<p>He was again a regular selection in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008_qualifying_Group_D" title="UEFA Euro 2008 qualifying Group D">2008 Euro qualification</a> games but Ireland did not qualify for the finals. He scored his second international goal in the group game against <a href="/wiki/Cyprus_national_football_team" title="Cyprus national football team">Cyprus</a> in a 1-1 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> He then announced his retirement from international football on 22 January 2008 after making 50 appearances for the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Republic of Ireland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> Tempted by the prospect of working with the newly appointed Ireland manager <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Giovanni_Trappatoni" title="Giovanni Trappatoni">Giovanni Trappatoni</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> Finnan came out of retirement in August 2008 to be included in the squad to face <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_football_team" title="Norway national football team">Norway</a> in an international friendly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Finnan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Finnan was arrested in June 2005 after running over Henry Nelson, an 81-year-old man from Liverpool,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> who later died of the injuries he sustained.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> Finnan faced no charges over the death of Nelson,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> despite travelling more than twice the speed-limit at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup></p>
<p>Finnan spent the majority of the early 2010s in <a href="/wiki/The_Gambia" title="The Gambia">The Gambia</a>, providing <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">irrigation</a> for impoverished children.</p>
<p>In 2015, he was living in London and working in property development.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
