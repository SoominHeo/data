<p><b>Stephen Rea</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span></span>/</a></span></span> <span title="English pronunciation respelling"><a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i>ray</i></a></span>; born 31 October 1946) is an Irish<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> film and stage actor. Rea has appeared in high-profile films such as <i><a href="/wiki/V_for_Vendetta_(film)" title="V for Vendetta (film)">V for Vendetta</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Michael_Collins_(film)" title="Michael Collins (film)">Michael Collins</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Interview_with_the_Vampire" title="Interview with the Vampire">Interview with the Vampire</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Breakfast_on_Pluto_(film)" title="Breakfast on Pluto (film)">Breakfast on Pluto</a></i>. Rea was nominated for an <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Actor" title="Academy Award for Best Actor">Academy Award</a> for his lead performance as Fergus in the <a href="/wiki/1992_in_film" title="1992 in film">1992</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Crying_Game" title="The Crying Game">The Crying Game</a></i>. He has during later years had important roles in the <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Blick" title="Hugo Blick">Hugo Blick</a> TV series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Shadow_Line_(TV_series)" title="The Shadow Line (TV series)">The Shadow Line</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Honourable_Woman" title="The Honourable Woman">The Honourable Woman</a></i>, for which he won a <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_Television_Awards" title="British Academy Television Awards">BAFTA Award</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stephen_Rea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rea was born in <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">Belfast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, to Protestant parents; his father was a bus driver and his mother a housewife.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Belfast_High_School" title="Belfast High School">Belfast High School</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_University_Belfast" title="Queen's University Belfast">Queen's University Belfast</a>, taking a degree in <a href="/wiki/English_studies" title="English studies">English</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Rea trained at the <a href="/wiki/Abbey_Theatre" title="Abbey Theatre">Abbey Theatre</a> School in <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>. In the late 1970s, he acted in the Focus Company in Dublin with <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Byrne" title="Gabriel Byrne">Gabriel Byrne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Colm_Meaney" title="Colm Meaney">Colm Meaney</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stephen_Rea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After appearing on the stage and in television and film for many years in Ireland and the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, Rea came to international attention when he was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Actor" title="Academy Award for Best Actor">Academy Award for Best Actor</a> for the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Crying_Game" title="The Crying Game">The Crying Game</a></i>. He is a frequent collaborator with Irish film-maker <a href="/wiki/Neil_Jordan" title="Neil Jordan">Neil Jordan</a>. Rea has long been associated with some of the most important writers in Ireland. His association with playwright <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Parker" title="Stewart Parker">Stewart Parker</a>, for example, began when they were students together at the <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_University" title="Queen's University">Queen's University</a> of Belfast.</p>
<p>Rea helped establish the <a href="/wiki/Field_Day_Theatre_Company" title="Field Day Theatre Company">Field Day Theatre Company</a> in 1980 with <a href="/wiki/Tom_Paulin" title="Tom Paulin">Tom Paulin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brian_Friel" title="Brian Friel">Brian Friel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seamus_Heaney" title="Seamus Heaney">Seamus Heaney</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seamus_Deane" title="Seamus Deane">Seamus Deane</a>. In recognition for his contribution to theatre and <a href="/wiki/Performing_arts" title="Performing arts">performing arts</a>, Rea was given <a href="/wiki/Honorary_degree" title="Honorary degree">honorary degrees</a> from both the Queen's University Belfast<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Ulster_University" title="Ulster University">Ulster University</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> in 2004.</p>
<p>Rea's friendship with American playwright and actor <a href="/wiki/Sam_Shepard" title="Sam Shepard">Sam Shepard</a> dates back to the early 1970s, and he starred in Shepard's directorial début of his play <i><a href="/wiki/Geography_of_a_Horse_Dreamer" title="Geography of a Horse Dreamer">Geography of a Horse Dreamer</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Court_Theatre" title="Royal Court Theatre">Royal Court Theatre</a> in 1974. In 2007, Rea began a successful and acclaimed relationship with both the Abbey Theatre and Sam Shepard, appearing in <i><a href="/wiki/Kicking_a_Dead_Horse" title="Kicking a Dead Horse">Kicking a Dead Horse</a></i> (2007) and <i>Ages of the Moon</i> (2009), both penned by Shepard and also both transferred to New York.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Rea returned to the Abbey in 2009 to appear in the world première of <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Barry" title="Sebastian Barry">Sebastian Barry</a>'s <i>Tales of Ballycumber</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rea was hired to speak the words of <a href="/wiki/Gerry_Adams" title="Gerry Adams">Gerry Adams</a> when <a href="/wiki/Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Sinn Féin">Sinn Féin</a> was under a <a href="/wiki/1988%E2%80%9394_British_broadcasting_voice_restrictions" title="1988–94 British broadcasting voice restrictions">1988–94 broadcasting ban</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Rea featured in the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> crime drama <a href="/wiki/The_Shadow_Line_(TV_series)" title="The Shadow Line (TV series)"><i>The Shadow Line</i></a>, playing antagonist Gatehouse.</p>
<p>In April 2012, Rea read <a href="/wiki/James_Joyce" title="James Joyce">James Joyce</a>'s short story "<a href="/wiki/The_Dead_(short_story)" title="The Dead (short story)">The Dead</a>" on <a href="/wiki/RT%C3%89_Radio_1" title="RTÉ Radio 1">RTÉ Radio 1</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He also narrated for the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_4" title="BBC Radio 4">BBC Radio 4</a> production of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ulysses_(BBC_Radio_4_broadcast)" title="Ulysses (BBC Radio 4 broadcast)">Ulysses</a></i> for <a href="/wiki/Bloomsday" title="Bloomsday">Bloomsday</a>, 16 June 2012.</p>
<p>Rea starred in <a href="/wiki/Enda_Walsh" title="Enda Walsh">Enda Walsh</a>'s 2014 play <i><a href="/wiki/Ballyturk" title="Ballyturk">Ballyturk</a></i> and portrayed Jordan in <i><a href="/wiki/Out_of_the_Dark_(2014_film)" title="Out of the Dark (2014 film)">Out of the Dark</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> in which he co-stars <a href="/wiki/Julia_Stiles" title="Julia Stiles">Julia Stiles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scott_Speedman" title="Scott Speedman">Scott Speedman</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Alejandro_Furth&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Alejandro Furth (page does not exist)">Alejandro Furth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stephen_Rea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rea was married for 17 years to <a href="/wiki/Dolours_Price" title="Dolours Price">Dolours Price</a>, a former <a href="/wiki/Provisional_Irish_Republican_Army" title="Provisional Irish Republican Army">Provisional Irish Republican Army</a> bomber and <a href="/wiki/Hunger_strike" title="Hunger strike">hunger striker</a> who later became a critic of <a href="/wiki/Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Sinn Féin">Sinn Féin</a>. (Another news source suggests a marriage of 20 years, from 1983 to 2003.)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> They had been divorced when she died on 23 January 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> They had two sons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rea is an Ambassador for <a href="/wiki/UNICEF_Ireland" title="UNICEF Ireland">UNICEF Ireland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
