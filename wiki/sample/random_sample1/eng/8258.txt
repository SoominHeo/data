<p><b>Steven Price</b> (born 22 April 1977) is a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> film composer, best known for scoring <i><a href="/wiki/Gravity_(film)" title="Gravity (film)">Gravity</a></i>, which won him the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Original_Score" title="Academy Award for Best Original Score">Academy Award for Best Original Score</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDB_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-IMDB-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AllMusic_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-AllMusic-2">[2]</a></sup> Before making his debut as a composer with <i><a href="/wiki/Attack_the_Block" title="Attack the Block">Attack the Block</a></i>, he worked on the music department for various notable films, such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Two_Towers" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers">The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Return_of_the_King" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King">The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_Begins" title="Batman Begins">Batman Begins</a></i>, or <i><a href="/wiki/Scott_Pilgrim_vs._the_World" title="Scott Pilgrim vs. the World">Scott Pilgrim vs. the World</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steven_Price_(composer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Price's passion for music began early: a guitarist from the age of five, he went on to achieve a First Class degree in Music from <a href="/wiki/Emmanuel_College,_Cambridge" title="Emmanuel College, Cambridge">Emmanuel College, Cambridge</a>. Following graduation, he went to work in the <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> studio of <a href="/wiki/Gang_of_Four_(band)" title="Gang of Four (band)">Gang of Four</a> guitarist/producer <a href="/wiki/Andy_Gill" title="Andy Gill">Andy Gill</a>, for whom he would program, contribute string arrangements, and play on albums alongside artists such as <a href="/wiki/Michael_Hutchence" title="Michael Hutchence">Michael Hutchence</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bono" title="Bono">Bono</a>.</p>
<p>Price went on to work as a programmer, arranger, and performer with film music composer <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Jones_(composer)" title="Trevor Jones (composer)">Trevor Jones</a>. He provided additional music for projects such as <a href="/wiki/Roger_Donaldson" title="Roger Donaldson">Roger Donaldson</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Thirteen_Days_(film)" title="Thirteen Days (film)">Thirteen Days</a></i>; <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Norrington" title="Stephen Norrington">Stephen Norrington</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_League_of_Extraordinary_Gentlemen" title="The League of Extraordinary Gentlemen">The League of Extraordinary Gentlemen</a></i>; <a href="/wiki/Frank_Coraci" title="Frank Coraci">Frank Coraci</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Around_the_World_in_80_Days_(2004_film)" title="Around the World in 80 Days (2004 film)">Around the World in 80 Days</a></i>; the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Dinotopia_(miniseries)" title="Dinotopia (miniseries)">Dinotopia</a></i>; and <a href="/wiki/Tamra_Davis" title="Tamra Davis">Tamra Davis</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Crossroads_(2002_film)" title="Crossroads (2002 film)">Crossroads</a></i>, on which he was also the featured guitar soloist with the <a href="/wiki/London_Symphony_Orchestra" title="London Symphony Orchestra">London Symphony Orchestra</a>.</p>
<p>A recommendation from <a href="/wiki/Abbey_Road_Studios" title="Abbey Road Studios">Abbey Road Studios</a> brought him to the attention of <a href="/wiki/Howard_Shore" title="Howard Shore">Howard Shore</a>, leading to Price's work with the composer as <a href="/wiki/Music_editor_(filmmaking)" title="Music editor (filmmaking)">music editor)</a> on <a href="/wiki/Peter_Jackson" title="Peter Jackson">Peter Jackson</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings_(film_series)" title="The Lord of the Rings (film series)">The Lord of the Rings</a></i> film trilogy. His subsequent films as music editor included <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Nolan" title="Christopher Nolan">Christopher Nolan</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_Begins" title="Batman Begins">Batman Begins</a></i>, for which he shared with his fellow music editors a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Reel_Award_(Motion_Picture_Sound_Editors)" title="Golden Reel Award (Motion Picture Sound Editors)">Golden Reel Award</a> nomination; and, in his first project with <i><a href="/wiki/The_World%27s_End_(film)" title="The World's End (film)">The World's End</a></i> director <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Wright" title="Edgar Wright">Edgar Wright</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Scott_Pilgrim_vs._the_World" title="Scott Pilgrim vs. the World">Scott Pilgrim vs. the World</a></i>, on which he also collaborated with the film's composer <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Godrich" title="Nigel Godrich">Nigel Godrich</a>. Among the other composers that he has worked with and learned from are <a href="/wiki/Hans_Zimmer" title="Hans Zimmer">Hans Zimmer</a>, <a href="/wiki/James_Newton_Howard" title="James Newton Howard">James Newton Howard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Gregson-Williams" title="Harry Gregson-Williams">Harry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rupert_Gregson-Williams" title="Rupert Gregson-Williams">Rupert</a> Gregson-Williams, <a href="/wiki/George_Fenton" title="George Fenton">George Fenton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dario_Marianelli" title="Dario Marianelli">Dario Marianelli</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Anne_Dudley" title="Anne Dudley">Anne Dudley</a>.</p>
<p>He has composed music for advertising campaigns in both the U.K. and U.S. After contributing additional music to <a href="/wiki/Richard_Curtis" title="Richard Curtis">Richard Curtis</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boat_That_Rocked" title="The Boat That Rocked">Pirate Radio</a></i>, he composed the original score for <a href="/wiki/Joe_Cornish" title="Joe Cornish">Joe Cornish</a>'s sleeper success <i><a href="/wiki/Attack_the_Block" title="Attack the Block">Attack the Block</a></i>, winning awards from both the <a href="/wiki/Austin_Film_Critics_Association" title="Austin Film Critics Association">Austin Film Critics Association</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Sitges_Film_Festival" title="Sitges Film Festival">Sitges-Catalonian International Film Festival</a>.</p>
<p>In 2013, Price composed the score for <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_Cuar%C3%B3n" title="Alfonso Cuarón">Alfonso Cuarón</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Gravity_(film)" title="Gravity (film)">Gravity</a></i>, for which he won the Academy Award for Best Original Score.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
