<p>"<b>Black and Yellow</b>" is a song by American rapper <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a> from his third studio album, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rolling_Papers_(album)" title="Rolling Papers (album)">Rolling Papers</a></i>. It was released on September 14, 2010, as the <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> from the album. The song was written by Khalifa, along with <a href="/wiki/Stargate_(production_team)" title="Stargate (production team)">Stargate</a>, who produced it. It was released as a <a href="/wiki/CD_single" title="CD single">CD single</a> in honor of <a href="/wiki/Record_Store_Day" title="Record Store Day">Record Store Day</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Record_Store_Day_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Record_Store_Day-1">[1]</a></sup> The song peaked at No. 1 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, becoming Wiz Khalifa's first number-one single in the US;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-2">[2]</a></sup> he would top the chart again in 2015 with "<a href="/wiki/See_You_Again_(Wiz_Khalifa_song)" title="See You Again (Wiz Khalifa song)">See You Again</a>".</p>
<p>The song is about Khalifa's car, a yellow <a href="/wiki/Dodge_Challenger" title="Dodge Challenger">Dodge Challenger</a> Hemi with black stripes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Songfacts_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Songfacts-3">[3]</a></sup> He has stated that he got the car in those colors as a tribute to his hometown of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pittsburgh,_Pennsylvania" title="Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania">Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania</a>, whose official colors are black and gold, and its professional sports teams, most of whose colors are black and some variation of gold or yellow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Songfacts_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Songfacts-3">[3]</a></sup> The song itself does not mention Pittsburgh or sports, although the song's <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> made the connection to Pittsburgh explicit, showing various iconic locations in the city, as well as apparel associated with the <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">football</a> team the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Steelers" title="Pittsburgh Steelers">Pittsburgh Steelers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> team the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a>. In the year after it was released, "Black and Yellow" spawned dozens of remixes, parodies and remakes, both in the US and internationally, many of them made in tribute to a local sports team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YouTube_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-YouTube-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YouTube2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-YouTube2-6">[6]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLV" title="Super Bowl XLV">Super Bowl XLV</a> in 2011, which featured the Steelers competing against the <a href="/wiki/Green_Bay_Packers" title="Green Bay Packers">Green Bay Packers</a>, the Steelers used "Black and Yellow" as their <a href="/wiki/Fight_song" title="Fight song">fight song</a>, while the Packers used a remix by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lil%27_Wayne" title="Lil' Wayne">Lil' Wayne</a> called "Green and Yellow", marking the first time both teams at the Super Bowl had used the same song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_and_Yellow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On the issue dated October 2, 2010, "Black and Yellow" debuted at No. 100 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. It then dropped out the following week and re-entered at No. 64 on the issue dated October 30, 2010. On its eighteenth charting week, the song rose to No. 1 on the issue dated February 19, 2011, selling 198,000 digital copies that week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> "Black and Yellow" has sold 4,144,922 digital copies since release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_and_Yellow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The music video was directed by Bill Paladino. It was filmed in <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a> and features sights of the city, including the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Steel_Tower" title="U.S. Steel Tower">U.S. Steel Tower</a>, <a href="/wiki/BNY_Mellon_Center_(Pittsburgh)" title="BNY Mellon Center (Pittsburgh)">BNY Mellon Center</a>, <a href="/wiki/PPG_Place" title="PPG Place">PPG Place</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Penn_Hotel" title="William Penn Hotel">William Penn Hotel</a>, <a href="/wiki/525_William_Penn_Place" title="525 William Penn Place">Citizens Bank Tower</a>, <a href="/wiki/Union_Trust_Building_(Pittsburgh)" title="Union Trust Building (Pittsburgh)">Union Trust Building</a>, <a href="/wiki/One_PNC_Plaza" title="One PNC Plaza">One PNC Plaza</a>, <a href="/wiki/K%26L_Gates_Center" title="K&amp;L Gates Center">K&amp;L Gates Center</a> the <a href="/wiki/Three_Sisters_(Pittsburgh)" title="Three Sisters (Pittsburgh)">Three Sisters</a> and <a href="/wiki/Smithfield_Street_Bridge" title="Smithfield Street Bridge">Smithfield Street bridges</a>, <a href="/wiki/Station_Square" title="Station Square">Station Square</a>, <a href="/wiki/Try_Street_Terminal" title="Try Street Terminal">Shannon Hall</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Art_Institute_of_Pittsburgh" title="Art Institute of Pittsburgh">Art Institute of Pittsburgh</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Smoke_stacks" title="Smoke stacks">smoke stacks</a> of the former <a href="/wiki/U.S._Steel" title="U.S. Steel">U.S. Steel</a> Homestead Works at <a href="/wiki/The_Waterfront" title="The Waterfront">The Waterfront</a> adjacent to the city. The video also prominently features city icons such as the <a href="/wiki/Terrible_Towel" title="Terrible Towel">Terrible Towel</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Rally_towel" title="Rally towel">rally towel</a> for the Pittsburgh Steelers, and city sports in general, as well as Pittsburgh Pirates apparel. It was recorded in Chatsworth Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_and_Yellow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song was featured in the multiple trailers for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lego_Batman_Movie" title="The Lego Batman Movie">The Lego Batman Movie</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Remixes">Remixes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_and_Yellow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Remixes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The official remix, "Black &amp; Yellow (G-Mix)" features west-coast rapper <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juicy_J" title="Juicy J">Juicy J</a> and R&amp;B singer <a href="/wiki/T-Pain" title="T-Pain">T-Pain</a>. Wiz Khalifa has a new verse on the track. The song leaked on December 12, 2010 but was officially released on December 16.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> A video for the remix was shot and was released on January 8, 2011. A remix by <a href="/wiki/T-Pain" title="T-Pain">T-Pain</a>, named "Black &amp; Yellow (T-Mix)", featured the verse that was later put on the official remix.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Remakes_and_freestyles">Remakes and freestyles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_and_Yellow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Remakes and freestyles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many remakes have been made, mostly in tribute to other sports teams, referring to their respective pair of colors. Some of the notable remakes include:</p>
<ul>
<li>"Purp &amp; Yellow" by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Game_(rapper)" title="Game (rapper)">Game</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG</a>, representing the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Lakers" title="Los Angeles Lakers">Los Angeles Lakers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-2">[2]</a></sup> This version also had a remix produced by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Skee" title="DJ Skee">DJ Skee</a>, with a <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a>-instrumented backing track; a music video for the DJ Skee remix featured the artists as well as members of the Lakers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> An extended mix was released the next day, also featuring <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Joe_Moses&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Joe Moses (page does not exist)">Joe Moses</a> and Thurzday.</li>
<li>"Rossonero (Red and Black)" by Denny La Home feat. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jake_La_Furia" title="Jake La Furia">Jake La Furia</a> - Italian version performed by two Italian rappers. The colours refer to Italian football club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AC_Milan" title="AC Milan">AC Milan</a>.</li>
<li>"Eff Australia" by <a href="/wiki/Rucka_Rucka_Ali" title="Rucka Rucka Ali">Rucka Rucka Ali</a> - an anti-Australian song.</li>
<li>"Green and Yellow" by <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, representing the <a href="/wiki/Green_Bay_Packers" title="Green Bay Packers">Green Bay Packers</a>, who defeated the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Steelers" title="Pittsburgh Steelers">Pittsburgh Steelers</a> in <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLV" title="Super Bowl XLV">Super Bowl XLV</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
<li>"White and Navy" by <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a> representing New York City, and the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>"Black and Yellow (Mike Tomlin)" by <a href="/wiki/Wale_(rapper)" title="Wale (rapper)">Wale</a>, in reference to the Steelers' head coach <a href="/wiki/Mike_Tomlin" title="Mike Tomlin">Mike Tomlin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prefix_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Prefix-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li>"Yeah Carmelo" by <a href="/wiki/Maino" title="Maino">Maino</a>, in reference to <a href="/wiki/Carmelo_Anthony" title="Carmelo Anthony">Carmelo Anthony</a> of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Knicks" title="New York Knicks">New York Knicks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prefix_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Prefix-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li>"Black and Red" by <a href="/wiki/Jermaine_Dupri" title="Jermaine Dupri">Jermaine Dupri</a> representing the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Falcons" title="Atlanta Falcons">Atlanta Falcons</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Musique_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Musique-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li>"Black and Orange" by <a href="/wiki/San_Quinn" title="San Quinn">San Quinn</a> representing the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a> in their <a href="/wiki/2010_World_Series" title="2010 World Series">2010 World Series</a> victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SFWeekly_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-SFWeekly-17">[17]</a></sup></li>
<li>"White and Purple" by Chet Haze (son of actor <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hanks" title="Tom Hanks">Tom Hanks</a>), representing <a href="/wiki/Northwestern_University" title="Northwestern University">Northwestern University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Candy95_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Candy95-18">[18]</a></sup></li>
<li>"Black and Yellow" by <a href="/wiki/Tyler_Ward" title="Tyler Ward">Tyler Ward</a> and Crew and Cobus, a rock version.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></li>
<li>"Black and Ghetto" by <a href="/wiki/Serius_Jones" title="Serius Jones">Serius Jones</a> describing the lifestyle of being an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American" title="African American">African American</a> <a href="/wiki/Gangster" title="Gangster">gangster</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Youtube_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Youtube-20">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li>"Green and Purple" by Kritikal which refers to marijuana and the culture surrounding it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
<li>"Black and Yellow" by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Cobbins" title="Chris Cobbins">Chris Cobbins</a></li>
</ul>
<p>In addition, a number of <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle</a> versions have been released, of artists rapping over "Black and Yellow"'s instrumental track, without any overt reference to the song or to sports teams. These include versions by <a href="/wiki/Crooked_I" title="Crooked I">Crooked I</a> (who included a chorus of "packing metal"), <a href="/wiki/Donnis" title="Donnis">Donnis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Young_Jeezy" title="Young Jeezy">Young Jeezy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tyga" title="Tyga">Tyga</a>, Novi Novak, and Layzie Bone and Flesh-n-Bone of <a href="/wiki/Bone_Thugs-n-Harmony" title="Bone Thugs-n-Harmony">Bone Thugs-n-Harmony</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prefix_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-Prefix-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
