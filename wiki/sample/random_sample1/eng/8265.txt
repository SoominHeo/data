<p>The <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <b>Stylidiaceae</b> is a taxon of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dicot" title="Dicot">dicotyledonous</a> <a href="/wiki/Flowering_plant" title="Flowering plant">flowering plants</a>. It consists of five genera with over 240 species, most of which are <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> to <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>. Members of Stylidiaceae are typically grass-like herbs or small shrubs and can be <a href="/wiki/Perennial_plant" title="Perennial plant">perennials</a> or <a href="/wiki/Annual_plant" title="Annual plant">annuals</a>. Most species are free standing or self-supporting, though a few can be climbing or scrambling (<i><a href="/wiki/Stylidium_scandens" title="Stylidium scandens">Stylidium scandens</a></i> uses leaf tips recurved into hooks to climb).</p>
<p>The pollination mechanisms of <i><a href="/wiki/Stylidium" title="Stylidium">Stylidium</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Levenhookia" title="Levenhookia">Levenhookia</a></i> are unique and highly specialized. In <i>Stylidium</i> the <a href="/wiki/Column_(botany)" title="Column (botany)">floral column</a> that consists of the fused <a href="/wiki/Stamen" title="Stamen">stamen</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carpel" title="Carpel">style</a> springs violently from one side (usually under the flower) when triggered, which deposits the pollen on a visiting insect. In <i>Levenhookia</i>, however, the column is immobile, but the hooded <a href="/wiki/Labellum_(botany)" title="Labellum (botany)">labellum</a> is triggered and sheds pollen.</p>
<p>In 1981, only about 155 species were known in the family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The current number of species by genus (reported in 2002) is as follows: <i>Forstera</i> - 5, <i>Levenhookia</i> - 10, <i>Oreostylidium</i> - 1, <i>Phyllachne</i> - 4, and <i>Stylidium</i> - 221. These numbers, especially for <i>Stylidium</i>, are changing rapidly as new species are described.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wagstaff_and_Wege_2002_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wagstaff_and_Wege_2002-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stylidiaceae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The genus <i><a href="/wiki/Donatia" title="Donatia">Donatia</a></i> is sometimes included in Stylidiaceae in the monogeneric subfamily <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Donatioideae" title="Donatioideae">Donatioideae</a>. The <a href="/wiki/APG_II_system" title="APG II system">APG II system</a> recommends its inclusion in Stylidiaceae but allows for the optional recognition of the family <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Donatiaceae" title="Donatiaceae">Donatiaceae</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-APG_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-APG-3">[3]</a></sup> Molecular and <a href="/wiki/Phylogenetics" title="Phylogenetics">phylogenetic analysis</a> have determined that <i>Donatia</i> is a sister-group to Stylidiaceae and therefore placing <i>Donatia</i> in its own family has been recommended by several authorities. Including <i>Donatia</i> within the Stylidiaceae would endanger its status as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monophyletic" title="Monophyletic">monophyletic</a> group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Laurent_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Laurent-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Donatioideae and Stylidioideae were described by <a href="/wiki/Johannes_Mildbraed" title="Johannes Mildbraed">Johannes Mildbraed</a> in his 1908 taxonomic monograph of the family. The subfamilies were created to distinguish the difference between the five typical <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genera</a> of the Stylidiaceae from the single genus <i><a href="/wiki/Donatia" title="Donatia">Donatia</a></i>, which Mildbraed placed in Donatioideae.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mildbraed_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mildbraed-5">[5]</a></sup> The subfamily taxonomy represents the taxonomic uncertainty of <i>Donatia</i>, which has often been placed in its own family, Donatiaceae, or other families such as the <a href="/wiki/Saxifragaceae" title="Saxifragaceae">Saxifragaceae</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wagstaff_and_Wege_2002_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Wagstaff_and_Wege_2002-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Good_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Good-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mildbraed's classification also included two <a href="/wiki/Tribe_(biology)" title="Tribe (biology)">tribes</a>: Phyllachneae, which included the genera <i>Forstera</i> and <i>Phyllachne</i>, and Stylidieae, which included <i>Levenhookia</i>, <i>Oreostylidium</i>, and <i>Stylidium</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mildbraed_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mildbraed-5">[5]</a></sup> This level of infraspecific taxonomy is not used in recent research, but the groupings are supported by molecular data that suggest <i>Forstera</i> and <i>Phyllachne</i> are closely related but distinct from the other three.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wagstaff_and_Wege_2002_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Wagstaff_and_Wege_2002-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>APG II places Stylidiaceae and Donatiaceae in the <a href="/wiki/Asterales" title="Asterales">Asterales</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Cronquist_system" title="Cronquist system">Cronquist system</a> placed both families in the <a href="/wiki/Campanulales" title="Campanulales">Campanulales</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Takhtajan_system" title="Takhtajan system">Takhtajan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reveal_system" title="Reveal system">Reveal</a> systems place both families in the order Stylidiales. The <a href="/wiki/Dahlgren_system" title="Dahlgren system">Dahlgren system</a> uses the same Stylidiales order, but it omits Donatiaceae. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thorne_system_(1992)" title="Thorne system (1992)">Thorne system</a> shifts Stylidiaceae into the <a href="/wiki/Saxifragales" title="Saxifragales">Saxifragales</a> order.</p>
