<p><i><b>Blue &amp; Lonesome</b></i> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Covers_album" title="Covers album">covers album</a> by <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">the Rolling Stones</a>—their 23rd British and 25th American studio album—released on 2 December 2016. It is the band's first album to feature only cover songs, and their first studio release since 2005's <i><a href="/wiki/A_Bigger_Bang" title="A Bigger Bang">A Bigger Bang</a></i>. "<a href="/wiki/Just_Your_Fool" title="Just Your Fool">Just Your Fool</a>", a <a href="/wiki/Buddy_Johnson" title="Buddy Johnson">Buddy Johnson</a> cover (though the Rolling Stones version is based on <a href="/wiki/Little_Walter" title="Little Walter">Little Walter</a>'s arrangement) was released as the first single from the album on 6 October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pitchfork_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pitchfork-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Eric_Clapton" title="Eric Clapton">Eric Clapton</a> contributed guitar on two tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NME_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-NME-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_%26_Lonesome_(The_Rolling_Stones_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Blue &amp; Lonesome</i> was recorded in just three days<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-standard.co.uk_The_Rolling_Stones_Blue_.26_Lonesome_review_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-standard.co.uk_The_Rolling_Stones_Blue_.26_Lonesome_review-11">[11]</a></sup> in December 2015. In April 2016, at the launch of the Rolling Stones career retrospective <i>Exhibitionism</i>, the band confirmed that their new album was due to be released "some time in the autumn". Richards said the album would feature "a lot of Chicago blues".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Eric Clapton plays guitar on two tracks. Clapton was recording his own album in the same studio as the Stones were and was asked to play on a few tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The album is entirely blues-based, consisting of covers of artists such as <a href="/wiki/Howlin%27_Wolf" title="Howlin' Wolf">Howlin' Wolf</a> and <a href="/wiki/Little_Walter" title="Little Walter">Little Walter</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>This is the first album since <i><a href="/wiki/Dirty_Work_(The_Rolling_Stones_album)" title="Dirty Work (The Rolling Stones album)">Dirty Work</a></i> to not feature any guitar playing from Jagger (who instead concentrates completely on vocals and harmonica) and also the first since <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=It%27s_Only_Rock_n_Roll&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="It's Only Rock n Roll (page does not exist)">It's Only Rock n Roll</a></i> to not feature a lead vocal from Richards.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion">Promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_%26_Lonesome_(The_Rolling_Stones_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 6 October 2016, the Rolling Stones changed their iconic "tongue and lips" logo, which first appeared on their <i><a href="/wiki/Sticky_Fingers" title="Sticky Fingers">Sticky Fingers</a></i> album, from red to blue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 8 November 2016, the Rolling Stones released a video for "Hate to See You Go".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube_Rolling_Stones_Hate_To_See_You_Go_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-youtube_Rolling_Stones_Hate_To_See_You_Go-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 25 November 2016, the Stones released a one-track limited edition electric blue 10" vinyl record of "Ride 'Em On Down" (on the UMC label) on the occasion of the <a href="/wiki/Record_Store_Day" title="Record Store Day">Record Store Day</a> Black Friday 2016. The track is a cover of <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Taylor" title="Eddie Taylor">Eddie Taylor</a>'s "Ride 'Em On Down" originally recorded by Taylor in Chicago on 5 December 1955 for the Vee-Jay Label (and released as VJ 185).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-roughtrade.com_The_Rolling_Stones_Ride_.27Em_on_Down_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-roughtrade.com_The_Rolling_Stones_Ride_.27Em_on_Down-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 December 2016, they released a video for "Ride 'Em on Down".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube_Rolling_Stones_Ride_.27Em_on_Down_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-youtube_Rolling_Stones_Ride_.27Em_on_Down-23">[23]</a></sup> The video features actress <a href="/wiki/Kristen_Stewart" title="Kristen Stewart">Kristen Stewart</a> riding through Los Angeles driving a blue 1968 Ford Mustang Fastback.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_and_reception">Release and reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_%26_Lonesome_(The_Rolling_Stones_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Release and reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During its first week the album moved 106,000 sales to debut at number 1 on the UK Albums Chart, the second-highest opening sales week for an album in the UK in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> It also debuted at number 4 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> with 123,000 <a href="/wiki/Album-equivalent_unit" title="Album-equivalent unit">album-equivalent units</a>, of which 120,000 were pure album sales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The album was a commercial success upon release, having sold 1,475,000 units worldwide as of January 18 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
