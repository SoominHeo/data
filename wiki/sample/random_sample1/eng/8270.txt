<p>The <b>Total 24 Hours of Spa</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Endurance_racing_(motorsport)" title="Endurance racing (motorsport)">endurance racing</a> event for cars held annually in <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a> since 1923 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a>. It is currently sponsored by <a href="/wiki/Total_S.A." title="Total S.A.">Total S.A.</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spa_24_Hours&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Alfa_Romeos_at_the_1930_24_Hours_of_Spa.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4808" data-file-width="6528" height="162" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Alfa_Romeos_at_the_1930_24_Hours_of_Spa.jpg/220px-Alfa_Romeos_at_the_1930_24_Hours_of_Spa.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Alfa_Romeos_at_the_1930_24_Hours_of_Spa.jpg/330px-Alfa_Romeos_at_the_1930_24_Hours_of_Spa.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Alfa_Romeos_at_the_1930_24_Hours_of_Spa.jpg/440px-Alfa_Romeos_at_the_1930_24_Hours_of_Spa.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Alfa_Romeos_at_the_1930_24_Hours_of_Spa.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo" title="Alfa Romeo">Alfa Romeos</a> after triple win in 1930</div>

<p>The Spa 24 Hours was conceived by Jules de Their and Henri Langlois Van Ophem just one year after the inaugural <a href="/wiki/24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="24 Hours of Le Mans">24 Hours of Le Mans</a> was run. It debuted in 1924 over a 15 kilometres (9.3 mi) circuit on public roads between the towns of Francorchamps, Malmedy and Stavelot, under the auspices of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Automobile_Club_Belgium&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Royal Automobile Club Belgium (page does not exist)">Royal Automobile Club Belgium</a> (RACB). The present 7.004 kilometres (4.352 mi) circuit was inaugurated in 1979.</p>
<p>The Spa 24 Hours was part of the <a href="/wiki/European_Touring_Car_Championship" title="European Touring Car Championship">European Touring Car Championship</a> from 1966 to 1973, again in 1976 and from 1982 to 1988 (with the exception of <a href="/wiki/1987_Spa_24_Hour" title="1987 Spa 24 Hour">1987</a> when it was part of the <a href="/wiki/1987_World_Touring_Car_Championship" title="1987 World Touring Car Championship">inaugural</a> <a href="/wiki/World_Touring_Car_Championship" title="World Touring Car Championship">World Touring Car Championship</a>). The event also counted towards the <a href="/wiki/World_Sportscar_Championship" title="World Sportscar Championship">World Sportscar Championship</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1953_World_Sportscar_Championship_season" title="1953 World Sportscar Championship season">1953</a> and the <a href="/wiki/World_Sportscar_Championship" title="World Sportscar Championship">World Endurance Championship</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1981_World_Sportscar_Championship_season" title="1981 World Sportscar Championship season">1981</a>. As on the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a>, both a 24h and a 1000 km race is held at Spa, as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1000_km_Spa" title="1000 km Spa">1000 km Spa</a> for <a href="/wiki/Sports_car_racing" title="Sports car racing">sports car racing</a> were introduced in 1966.</p>
<p>Cars entered have spanned from the Russian <a href="/wiki/Moskvitch" title="Moskvitch">Moskvitch</a> and models with sub-1 liter engines such as the <a href="/wiki/NSU_Prinz" title="NSU Prinz">NSU Prinz</a> TT to the luxurious V8-powered <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_W108" title="Mercedes-Benz W108">Mercedes-Benz 300 SEL 6.3</a>. Tuned by <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-AMG" title="Mercedes-AMG">Mercedes-AMG</a>, the 6834 cc and 420 hp (313 kW) so-called <a class="external text" href="http://www.spiegel.de/auto/aktuell/0,1518,415796,00.html" rel="nofollow">"Red pig"</a> finished as high as second in 1971.</p>
<p>With the participation of <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Swiss</a> <a href="/wiki/Lilian_Bryner" title="Lilian Bryner">Lilian Bryner</a> on the victorious <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_550" title="Ferrari 550">Ferrari 550</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/BMS_Scuderia_Italia" title="BMS Scuderia Italia">BMS Scuderia Italia</a></i> team, the 2004 race marked the first time in history that a female driver was part of the winning team of a 24-hour endurance race in a <a href="/wiki/Grand_tourer" title="Grand tourer">Gran Turismo</a> with more than 500 hp (370 kW).</p>
<p>The best manufacturer wins the <b>Coupe du Roi</b> (King's Cup), which is not necessarily the race winners. The cup is won by the manufacturer with the most points, accrued by cars that are made by the same manufacturer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> For example, Australian car manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Holden" title="Holden">Holden</a> won the Coupe du Roi in 1986 despite their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holden_VK_Commodore_SS_Group_A" title="Holden VK Commodore SS Group A">cars</a> finishing the race in 18th, 22nd and 23rd positions outright.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

Currently, the cars run fall under the FIA GT3 and GT3 Cup classifications.</p>
