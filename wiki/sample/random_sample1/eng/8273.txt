<p><i><b>Spy</b></i> is a 2015 American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Action_comedy_film" title="Action comedy film">action comedy film</a> written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Paul_Feig" title="Paul Feig">Paul Feig</a>. Starring <a href="/wiki/Melissa_McCarthy" title="Melissa McCarthy">Melissa McCarthy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Statham" title="Jason Statham">Jason Statham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rose_Byrne" title="Rose Byrne">Rose Byrne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miranda_Hart" title="Miranda Hart">Miranda Hart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Cannavale" title="Bobby Cannavale">Bobby Cannavale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Allison_Janney" title="Allison Janney">Allison Janney</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jude_Law" title="Jude Law">Jude Law</a>, the film follows the transformation of desk-bound CIA analyst Susan Cooper (McCarthy) into a field agent who attempts to foil the black market sale of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suitcase_nuke" title="Suitcase nuke">suitcase nuke</a>.</p>
<p>Produced by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Chernin" title="Peter Chernin">Peter Chernin</a>, Jenno Topping, Feig and Jessie Henderson, the film was theatrically released on June 5, 2015. It received praise from critics and was a box office success.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It was nominated for two <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe Awards</a>: <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Motion_Picture_%E2%80%93_Musical_or_Comedy" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Motion Picture – Musical or Comedy">Best Motion Picture – Musical or Comedy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Actress_%E2%80%93_Motion_Picture_Comedy_or_Musical" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Motion Picture Comedy or Musical">Best Actress in a Motion Picture – Musical or Comedy</a> for McCarthy.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spy_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Susan Cooper (<a href="/wiki/Melissa_McCarthy" title="Melissa McCarthy">Melissa McCarthy</a>) is a 40-year-old, single, desk-bound <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CIA" title="CIA">CIA</a> employee who remotely assists her partner, <a href="/wiki/Field_agent" title="Field agent">field agent</a> Bradley Fine (<a href="/wiki/Jude_Law" title="Jude Law">Jude Law</a>), on a mission. Fine accidentally kills arms dealer Tihomir Boyanov as he sneezes during a confrontation before extracting the location of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suitcase_nuke" title="Suitcase nuke">suitcase nuke</a> from him. Susan uncovers evidence that Rayna (<a href="/wiki/Rose_Byrne" title="Rose Byrne">Rose Byrne</a>), Boyanov's daughter, has contacted Sergio De Luca (<a href="/wiki/Bobby_Cannavale" title="Bobby Cannavale">Bobby Cannavale</a>), a suspected broker with ties to various terrorist groups, so Fine infiltrates her home. However, Rayna shoots Fine dead, while Susan watches helplessly online, then reveals that she knows the identities of the agency's top agents, including Rick Ford (<a href="/wiki/Jason_Statham" title="Jason Statham">Jason Statham</a>) and Karen Walker (<a href="/wiki/Morena_Baccarin" title="Morena Baccarin">Morena Baccarin</a>). Susan, who is almost certainly unknown to Rayna, volunteers to track her. (She was a top trainee agent, albeit over ten years ago.) When her boss, Elaine Crocker (<a href="/wiki/Allison_Janney" title="Allison Janney">Allison Janney</a>), reluctantly agrees, the ultra <a href="/wiki/Machismo" title="Machismo">macho</a> Ford quits in disgust.</p>
<p>With her best friend Nancy (<a href="/wiki/Miranda_Hart" title="Miranda Hart">Miranda Hart</a>) providing intelligence, Susan goes to <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> undercover. That night, Ford shows up and tells Susan she will fail because of her inexperience. The next morning, Susan discovers that De Luca's office has burned down. She finds a photo of a man standing next to the fire. Ford appears, argues with Susan again and leaves. Susan sees the man in the photo follow him and switch his backpack with another one containing a bomb while Ford is distracted. Susan warns Ford in time and pursues the man into an abandoned building. During the ensuing fight, he falls to his death. When she checks the man's video camera, Susan learns that De Luca is going to Rome.</p>
<p>In Rome, Susan meets her contact Aldo (<a href="/wiki/Peter_Serafinowicz" title="Peter Serafinowicz">Peter Serafinowicz</a>). She follows Sergio into a casino, where she saves Rayna's life. Rayna brings Susan into her inner circle and takes Susan on her private plane to <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>. In mid-flight, the steward kills Rayna's bodyguard and pilots, but Susan subdues him. Rayna believes Susan to be a CIA agent, but Susan convinces her that she was hired by Boyanov to protect his daughter.</p>
<p>In Budapest, Susan meets Nancy, who was sent by Crocker. After being shot at, Susan pursues and catches up with the would-be assassin: Karen, who sold Rayna the names of the other agents. She tries to shoot Susan, but an unknown sniper kills her. Susan, Nancy and Aldo accompany Rayna to a party to meet Rayna's contact. That turns out to be Lia (<a href="/wiki/Nargis_Fakhri" title="Nargis Fakhri">Nargis Fakhri</a>), the woman who distracted Ford in Paris. Nancy creates a diversion (by pretending to be a crazed fan of guest performer <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>) so that Susan can try to apprehend Lia unnoticed. Because of Ford's inopportune intervention, however, Lia runs off. Susan chases after her. After a brutal fight, Lia is about to eliminate Susan, but she is killed by Fine, who earlier faked his death and is now Rayna's lover and associate.</p>
<p>Rayna imprisons Susan and Aldo in a bunker. Later, Fine reveals to Susan that he is trying to gain Rayna's trust to locate the nuke, and he was the one who killed Karen. Susan and Aldo escape. At De Luca's mansion, Fine, Rayna and Sergio wait for Solsa Dudaev (<a href="/wiki/Richard_Brake" title="Richard Brake">Richard Brake</a>), head of an al-Qaeda-funded terrorist group. Susan convinces Rayna and Deluca that, even though she works for the CIA, she will do anything to protect Fine, admitting that she loves him. Dudaev gives De Luca a suitcase full of diamonds, and Rayna produces the device. De Luca has Dudaev and his men killed, then reveals his plan to sell the device to another buyer (though they also intend to bomb <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>), before pointing his gun at Rayna. Ford distracts him, allowing Susan to kill his men. Sergio escapes to his helicopter with the device and the diamonds, but Susan grabs onto the landing gear. In the ensuing struggle, Susan throws the diamonds and the device into a lake below. De Luca attempts to shoot Susan, but Nancy, following in another helicopter, shoots him in the back before he can. He grabs on to Susan's necklace (a gift from Fine), but she releases the catch and De Luca falls out of the helicopter to his death.</p>
<p>The nuke is retrieved by the CIA, and Rayna is arrested. Ford, to Susan's surprise, compliments her on her job. Crocker tells Susan that she will remain a field agent, and that her next assignment will take her to Prague to infiltrate a drug smuggling ring. Fine invites Susan to dinner, but she instead opts for a night out with Nancy.</p>
<p>The next morning, Susan wakes up in bed next to Ford and screams. It is implied that they slept together, and Ford says that she loved it.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spy_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spy_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 18, 2013, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Paul_Feig" title="Paul Feig">Paul Feig</a> was developing <i>Susan Cooper</i>, a female spy comedy, for <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox" title="20th Century Fox">20th Century Fox</a>. Feig wrote and directed the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Paul_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Paul-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Peter_Chernin" title="Peter Chernin">Peter Chernin</a> and Jenno Topping produced the film under the Chernin Entertainment banner, with Feig and Jessie Henderson for Feigco Entertainment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cent-THR_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cent-THR-6">[6]</a></sup> On November 12, 2013, Fox announced a release date of May 22, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-May22nd-2015_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-May22nd-2015-7">[7]</a></sup> On March 28, 2014, the film's title was changed to <i>Spy</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bobby-Nia_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bobby-Nia-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casting">Casting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spy_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Casting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 25, 2013, it was confirmed that <a href="/wiki/Melissa_McCarthy" title="Melissa McCarthy">Melissa McCarthy</a> was in negotiations to play the title role of Susan Cooper, a female comic version of James Bond. <a href="/wiki/Zooey_Deschanel" title="Zooey Deschanel">Zooey Deschanel</a> was originally slated to play the role of Elaine Crocker but left the project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Melissa_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Melissa-9">[9]</a></sup> On October 17, <a href="/wiki/Rose_Byrne" title="Rose Byrne">Rose Byrne</a> joined the cast of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rose_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rose-10">[10]</a></sup> On October 21, it was reported that <a href="/wiki/Jason_Statham" title="Jason Statham">Jason Statham</a> met with Feig to begin talks about joining the cast;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> he later joined on February 26, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jason_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jason-12">[12]</a></sup> On March 6, 2014, it was reported that actor <a href="/wiki/Jude_Law" title="Jude Law">Jude Law</a> was in final talks to join the cast of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jude_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jude-13">[13]</a></sup> On March 12, it was revealed that internationally known actress <a href="/wiki/Nargis_Fakhri" title="Nargis Fakhri">Nargis Fakhri</a> would make her Hollywood debut with the film, playing the role of a secret agent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nargis_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nargis-14">[14]</a></sup> On March 28, <a href="/wiki/Miranda_Hart" title="Miranda Hart">Miranda Hart</a> also signed on to star in the film,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Miranda_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Miranda-15">[15]</a></sup> while <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Cannavale" title="Bobby Cannavale">Bobby Cannavale</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nia_Long" title="Nia Long">Nia Long</a> were in final talks to join (Long did not appear in the finished film).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bobby-Nia_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bobby-Nia-8">[8]</a></sup> McCarthy played Susan Cooper, a CIA analyst who goes into the field following the disappearance of a suave super-spy portrayed by Law. Statham played an overconfident yet clumsy spy, Hart played as Nancy Artingstall,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and Cannavale played a villain, an Italian playboy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bobby-Nia_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Bobby-Nia-8">[8]</a></sup> On April 1, <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> joined the cast and played himself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cent-THR_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cent-THR-6">[6]</a></sup> On April 24, Feig confirmed the role of Fakhri and added two more comic actors: <a href="/wiki/Peter_Serafinowicz" title="Peter Serafinowicz">Peter Serafinowicz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rn_Gustafsson" title="Björn Gustafsson">Björn Gustafsson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Peter-Bjorn_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Peter-Bjorn-17">[17]</a></sup> On April 30, both <a href="/wiki/Morena_Baccarin" title="Morena Baccarin">Morena Baccarin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Allison_Janney" title="Allison Janney">Allison Janney</a> joined the cast. Baccarin played one of the agency’s top spies,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morena_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Morena-18">[18]</a></sup> while Janney played top CIA agent Elaine Crocker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allison_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Allison-19">[19]</a></sup> On May 2, <a href="/wiki/Zach_Woods" title="Zach Woods">Zach Woods</a> joined the cast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zach_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Zach-20">[20]</a></sup> On May 29, <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Chaffin" title="Jessica Chaffin">Jessica Chaffin</a> was added to the cast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jessica_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jessica-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spy_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Principal_photography" title="Principal photography">Principal photography</a> and production began on March 31, 2014, in <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Miranda_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Miranda-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> On May 27, filming was under way in Budapest and was about to wrap up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Apart from tax breaks, shooting was primarily done in Budapest because its architecture and location could allow it to appear as other places where the story took place, including Paris.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spy_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film was originally scheduled to be released on May 22, 2015, by <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox" title="20th Century Fox">20th Century Fox</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-May22nd-2015_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-May22nd-2015-7">[7]</a></sup> In March 2015, the date was pushed back to June 5, 2015, which was first assigned to <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B.O.O.:_Bureau_of_Otherworldly_Operations" title="B.O.O.: Bureau of Otherworldly Operations">B.O.O.: Bureau of Otherworldly Operations</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Paper_Towns_(film)" title="Paper Towns (film)">Paper Towns</a></i>, the former of which being taken off the schedule and the latter moved to July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Prior to its official release, Paul Feig stated that <i>Spy</i> went through about 10 <a href="/wiki/Test_screening" title="Test screening">test screenings</a>, a process - which includes recording the audience laughter for each version - he does "religiously", with <a href="/wiki/Judd_Apatow" title="Judd Apatow">Judd Apatow</a> (who produced the Feig-directed <i><a href="/wiki/Bridesmaids_(2011_film)" title="Bridesmaids (2011 film)">Bridesmaids</a></i>) commenting on its usefulness for a comedy film: "It doesn’t work very well if a movie is supposed to make you feel difficult emotions. If you’re making a <a href="/wiki/David_Lynch" title="David Lynch">David Lynch</a> movie, it doesn’t work at all. But for comedy it’s often the best way to refine jokes."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Spy</i> received an early release of May 21, 2015 in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> and of May 28, 2015 in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> and May 29, 2015 in <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spy_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first official full-length trailer of the film was released on January 13, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spy_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Spy</i> grossed $110.8 million in North America and $124.8 million in other territories for a worldwide total of $235.6 million, against a budget of $65 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BOM_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-BOM-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In North America, the film made $1.5 million from its early Thursday night showings<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TT_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-TT-29">[29]</a></sup> and an estimated $10.3 million on its opening day from 3,711 theaters, coming at second place at the box office behind <i><a href="/wiki/Insidious:_Chapter_3" title="Insidious: Chapter 3">Insidious: Chapter 3</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THROP_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-THROP-30">[30]</a></sup> It topped the box office in its opening weekend earning $29 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Outside North America, <i>Spy</i> opened in ten foreign markets on May 22, 2015, earning $12.7 million in its opening weekend from 1,810 screens, and coming in fourth place at the box office (behind <i><a href="/wiki/Mad_Max:_Fury_Road" title="Mad Max: Fury Road">Mad Max: Fury Road</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Tomorrowland_(film)" title="Tomorrowland (film)">Tomorrowland</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Pitch_Perfect_2" title="Pitch Perfect 2">Pitch Perfect 2</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1stweek_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-1stweek-32">[32]</a></sup> In the UK, Ireland and Malta, it opened with $3.9 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2ndweek_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-2ndweek-33">[33]</a></sup> The film had successful openings in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> ($4.8 million), <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and the CIS ($3.1 million), <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> ($2.9 million), <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> ($1.6 million) and <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> ($1.3 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1stweek_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-1stweek-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2ndweek_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-2ndweek-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spy_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spy_(2015_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Spy</i> received critical acclaim, with critics praising McCarthy and Byrne's performances, as well as Statham's surprise comedic role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">Review aggregator</a> website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> lists a 94% approval rating, based on 227 reviews, with a <a href="/wiki/Weighted_arithmetic_mean" title="Weighted arithmetic mean">rating average</a> of 7.2/10. The site's consensus reads, "Simultaneously broad and progressive, <i>Spy</i> offers further proof that Melissa McCarthy and writer-director Paul Feig bring out the best in one another — and delivers scores of belly laughs along the way."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> the film has a score of 75 out of 100, based on 40 critics, indicating "generally favorable reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/CinemaScore" title="CinemaScore">CinemaScore</a> polls, cinema audiences gave the film an average score of "B+" on an A+ to F scale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THROP_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-THROP-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>McCarthy's performance was praised by critics. <a href="/wiki/Richard_Roeper" title="Richard Roeper">Richard Roeper</a> of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Chicago_Sun_Times" title="The Chicago Sun Times">The Chicago Sun Times</a></i> called her "as funny and as winning as anyone in the movies these days".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Tom Russo of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boston_Globe" title="The Boston Globe">The Boston Globe</a></i> credited the film's success to McCarthy, writing, "part of what makes the action comedy such a loopy blast is the identity shifts she pulls on the audience."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Bill Goodykoontz of <i>Arizona Republic</i> called the film McCarthy's return to form, writing "Finally, after the promise shown in <i><a href="/wiki/Bridesmaids_(2011_film)" title="Bridesmaids (2011 film)">Bridesmaids</a></i>, but sold short since by weak scripts in films like <i><a href="/wiki/Tammy_(film)" title="Tammy (film)">Tammy</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Identity_Thief" title="Identity Thief">Identity Thief</a></i>, Melissa McCarthy gets a movie vehicle worthy of her talents."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>In addition to McCarthy's, many of the supporting cast members' performances were praised, particularly Byrne's and Statham's. John Boone of <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Tonight" title="Entertainment Tonight">Entertainment Tonight</a></i> said Statham "twists his action hero persona into a delightfully delusional version of the same thing", and praised Byrne's performance, writing "For every joke that McCarthy’s Susan Cooper ends up as the butt of, Byrne is the one teeing it up with perfectly understated wickedness. She can so easily spit out lines as offensive as, after Cooper delivers a punny cheers, "What a stupid, f**king toast,” but make it...charming?"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Peter_Travers" title="Peter Travers">Peter Travers</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> called Byrne's comedic timing "bitchy perfection".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
