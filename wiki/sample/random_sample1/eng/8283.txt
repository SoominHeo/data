<p><b>Sparta</b> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock_band" title="Rock band">rock band</a> from <a href="/wiki/El_Paso,_Texas" title="El Paso, Texas">El Paso, Texas</a>, formed in 2001. Founding members <a href="/wiki/Jim_Ward_(musician)" title="Jim Ward (musician)">Jim Ward</a> (vocalist/guitarist) and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Hajjar" title="Tony Hajjar">Tony Hajjar</a> (drummer) are also members of post-hardcore group <a href="/wiki/At_the_Drive-In" title="At the Drive-In">At the Drive-In</a>. <a href="/wiki/Keeley_Davis" title="Keeley Davis">Keeley Davis</a> (guitarist) is the former frontman of <a href="/wiki/Engine_Down" title="Engine Down">Engine Down</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sparta_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Austere"><i>Austere</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sparta_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Austere">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the demise of their previous band, <a href="/wiki/At_the_Drive-In" title="At the Drive-In">At the Drive-In</a>, drummer <a href="/wiki/Tony_Hajjar" title="Tony Hajjar">Tony Hajjar</a> and bassist <a href="/wiki/Paul_Hinojos" title="Paul Hinojos">Paul Hinojos</a> got together and talked about forming a new band.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Hinojos contacted former <a href="/wiki/At_the_Drive-In" title="At the Drive-In">At the Drive-In</a> guitarist and back-up vocalist <a href="/wiki/Jim_Ward_(musician)" title="Jim Ward (musician)">Jim Ward</a> to take the role of lead vocalist. After a brief stint with Erick Sanger, the three recruited <a href="/wiki/Matt_Miller_(musician)" title="Matt Miller (musician)">Matt Miller</a>, a native to El Paso and then bassist of the band Belknap to permanently fill the position of bassist,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> with Hinojos switching to guitar. The band then scored a deal with <a href="/wiki/DreamWorks_Records" title="DreamWorks Records">Dreamworks</a> in 2001. The band released their first EP entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Austere_(EP)" title="Austere (EP)">Austere</a></i> in early 2002 under the name Sparta. The EP met with mixed reviews, with most believing that the group had talent and the ability, but had yet to find their voice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wiretap_Scars"><i>Wiretap Scars</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sparta_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Wiretap Scars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In August 2002, Sparta released their debut full-length album, <i><a href="/wiki/Wiretap_Scars" title="Wiretap Scars">Wiretap Scars</a></i>. The album, although not well received by all of the mainstream <a href="/wiki/Post-hardcore" title="Post-hardcore">Post-hardcore</a> audience, brought well-earned respect and relieved some of the pressure brought on by the shadow of At the Drive-In. The record was deemed a cleaner, more accessible sound than ATDI's distortion fueled offerings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-4">[4]</a></sup> True to their roots, the opening song "Cut Your Ribbon" was described as "splintering, power-hungry rock",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-4">[4]</a></sup> and other tracks such as "Cataract", "Glasshouse Tarot" and "Mye" were noted for their emotion and "absurdly exuberant melody".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Sparta maintained its underground following with shows at smaller venues throughout <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> and <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. They were also exposed to a wider audience in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/April_2003" title="April 2003">April</a> and May 2003, opening several shows for <a href="/wiki/Pearl_Jam" title="Pearl Jam">Pearl Jam</a>. While on tour for <i>Wiretap Scars</i>, Sparta employed the help of their friend Gabriel Gonzalez to play third guitar and keyboards, he can be seen in the <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music videos</a> from 2003's Big Day Out festival, among other videos circulating online. Gonzalez now performs with his own band, Volador, and is a member of Ward's side project <a href="/wiki/Sleepercar" title="Sleepercar">Sleepercar</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Loss_of_Jeremy_Ward">Loss of Jeremy Ward</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sparta_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Loss of Jeremy Ward">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On May 25, 2003, Ward's cousin, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeremy_Michael_Ward" title="Jeremy Michael Ward">Jeremy</a>, died of a <a href="/wiki/Heroin" title="Heroin">heroin</a> <a href="/wiki/Drug_overdose" title="Drug overdose">overdose</a>. Jeremy was at the time the sound manipulator for <a href="/wiki/The_Mars_Volta" title="The Mars Volta">The Mars Volta</a> created by former <a href="/wiki/At_the_Drive-In" title="At the Drive-In">At the Drive-In</a> bandmates <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Bixler-Zavala" title="Cedric Bixler-Zavala">Cedric Bixler-Zavala</a> and <a href="/wiki/Omar_Rodr%C3%ADguez-L%C3%B3pez" title="Omar Rodríguez-López">Omar Rodríguez-López</a>, and frontman of <a href="/wiki/De_Facto_(band)" title="De Facto (band)">De Facto</a>. The loss of his cousin would have a profound and lasting impact on Ward.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_at_La_Zona_Rosa"><i>Live at La Zona Rosa</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sparta_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Live at La Zona Rosa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The band resumed writing new material, and on March 19, 2004 recorded a live album at La Zona Rosa. The album had seven tracks and six songs, including two previously unrecorded tracks: "Lines in Sand" and "La Cerca". The album captured some of the live performance experience, but was not sold in stores and only available as a promotional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CD" title="CD">CD</a>. Videos for the songs "Cut Your Ribbon", "Mye", and "La Cerca" can be found online, recorded by the music television station <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fuse_TV" title="Fuse TV">Fuse TV</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Porcelain"><i>Porcelain</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sparta_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Porcelain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Porc_cover.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="478" data-file-width="500" height="143" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/17/Porc_cover.jpg/150px-Porc_cover.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/17/Porc_cover.jpg/225px-Porc_cover.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/17/Porc_cover.jpg/300px-Porc_cover.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a>

<i><a href="/wiki/Porcelain_(Sparta_album)" title="Porcelain (Sparta album)">Porcelain</a></i> (2004)</div>

<p>In July 2004, the band released their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Porcelain_(Sparta_album)" title="Porcelain (Sparta album)">Porcelain</a></i>. Despite popular belief, none of the tracks are fueled by the loss of Jeremy Ward. Tracks such as "Death in the Family" and "Travel by Bloodline" stem from other personal losses, such as Jim Ward's grandfather and a close friend. In a 2004 issue of the magazine Alternative Press, Ward describes his feelings towards these events, and how they have affected him. "...he will admit that he hasn't yet made sense of it, and, perhaps for that reason, there isn't a song on <i>Porcelain</i> about Jeremy. I don't think I'll ever make peace with that," he says. Immediately prior to the release, Sparta toured as the opening act for <a href="/wiki/Incubus_(band)" title="Incubus (band)">Incubus</a>, after which followed their own headlining tour.</p>
<p>On the title of the album, Ward remarked:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote>
<p>"I just liked the word Porcelain and what it implied: Something that's really beautiful and durable, but at the same time vulnerable, easily shattered. I like the duality."</p>
</blockquote>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Line-up_changes">Line-up changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sparta_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Line-up changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 2005, after pulling the band off the road mid-tour, Ward said he needed to re-group. "I walked out in the middle of a tour.... I needed to get away from everything and everyone. I wasn't enjoying myself at all, and I didn't feel my life or the band was where I wanted it to be... I needed to step back and reassess everything." For two months, he retreated to his home without speaking to anyone in his professional life. In an article with PE.com, Ward said, "When I left, I honestly didn't know if I was going to continue the band or not, so I told everyone to pursue whatever they wanted,". During that time, Hinojos left to join <a href="/wiki/The_Mars_Volta" title="The Mars Volta">The Mars Volta</a> as their new secondary guitarist and sound manipulator, filling the void left by Jeremy Ward's death. Hinojos was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Keeley_Davis" title="Keeley Davis">Keeley Davis</a>.</p>
<p>Eventually, Ward went to visit Hajjar in Los Angeles. They began writing new material without the restrictions of a deadline, and soon Ward had rekindled his desire to perform.</p>
<p>In January 2006, the band announced that they had signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Records" title="Hollywood Records">Hollywood Records</a>, and announced their intention to release a short film, titled <i>Eme Nakia</i>, based on Hajjar's experiences as a child in <a href="/wiki/Beirut" title="Beirut">Beirut</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Threes"><i>Threes</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sparta_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Threes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 9, 2006, by way of their official website, Sparta announced a new album, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Threes_(album)" title="Threes (album)">Threes</a></i>. Two days before the release, the entire album was available for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Streaming_audio" title="Streaming audio">streaming</a> on their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MySpace" title="MySpace">MySpace</a> page. The album was released on October 24, 2006. Their first single off the album was "Taking Back Control", and "Erase It Again" has been released as the second single in mid-2007.</p>
<p>Davis' first show with the band was at the Viper Room in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, in July 2006. However, the band looked at the show as a "warm-up" and the show at Los Angeles' Troubadour, July 29, 2006, was referred to as their "first show to the public in 15 months" by Ward. They played three songs off the upcoming album: "Crawl", "Weather The Storm", and the single "Taking Back Control". At this show Ward also told the crowd about what happened when he left the band the previous year, saying he would rather have the crowd hear it from him, than the press in the following months. Sparta played at <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lollapalooza" title="Lollapalooza">Lollapalooza</a> festival the following week. The band toured the United States, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> heavily after the release of the album with bands such as <a href="/wiki/Lola_Ray" title="Lola Ray">Lola Ray</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aloha" title="Aloha">Aloha</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/As_Tall_As_Lions" title="As Tall As Lions">As Tall As Lions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sound_Team" title="Sound Team">Sound Team</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moneen" title="Moneen">Moneen</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MeWithoutYou" title="MeWithoutYou">MeWithoutYou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lovedrug" title="Lovedrug">Lovedrug</a>, <a href="/wiki/Straylight_Run" title="Straylight Run">Straylight Run</a>, <a href="/wiki/Deftones" title="Deftones">Deftones</a> and <a href="/wiki/My_Chemical_Romance" title="My Chemical Romance">My Chemical Romance</a>. On February 19, 2007, the band played a free concert with <a class="external text" href="http://www.myspace.com/lydiavance" rel="nofollow">Lydia Vance</a> in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Las Vegas</a> for the XPOZ Coalition, an anti-smoking organization. While touring with <a href="/wiki/Straylight_Run" title="Straylight Run">Straylight Run</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lovedrug" title="Lovedrug">Lovedrug</a>, Sparta allowed Lovedrug to continue touring by letting them use their instruments and equipment after Lovedrug's gear was stolen following a show in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Detroit,_Michigan" title="Detroit, Michigan">Detroit, Michigan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Eme_Nakia"><i>Eme Nakia</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sparta_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Eme Nakia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In conjunction with the album <i><a href="/wiki/Threes_(album)" title="Threes (album)">Threes</a></i>, Sparta has also released a 16-minute short film entitled "Eme Nakia". The film, the soundtrack for which is performed by Sparta, is about drummer Hajjar's early life, set during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lebanese_civil_war" title="Lebanese civil war">Lebanese civil war</a> of 1975-1990. Hajjar fled at the age of five to the United States, settling in the sprawling west Texas border town of El Paso. When Hajjar was 14, his mother ("Eme") died after a bout with <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">cancer</a> and his father, who had been distant, left the family. His brother, 18 years old at the time, assumed responsibility for the family and raised Hajjar and his sister. The film follows Hajjar's early life story, showing his challenges and ending with him in present day.</p>
<p>The independent film received good reviews and is available for free with purchase of the <i>Threes</i> album.</p>
<p>The word "Eme" is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a> for mother, and "Nakia" was Hajjar's mother's name.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Hiatus">Hiatus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sparta_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Hiatus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In June 2008, Ward stated that Sparta is "currently on hiatus and label-less", and that he will be focusing on his project, <a href="/wiki/Sleepercar" title="Sleepercar">Sleepercar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In January 2009, when asked if a return to Sparta was foreseeable, Ward stated that he has no plans to reunite with his band mates: "I don’t right now, no. Who knows what the future will bring?"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In July 2010, on sleepercarmusic.com, it was stated that "Sparta is not broken up, Sparta is taking a nap."</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return">Return</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sparta_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Return">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following a three-year “nap,” Sparta played their first show since 2008 in November 2011 in their hometown of El Paso, TX.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> They performed on November 17 and 18 at Tricky Falls, the newly renovated venue co-owned by frontman Jim Ward. The band headed into the studio that fall to begin work on their fourth LP, the follow-up to 2006’s Threes. A short tour of the southwestern US followed in May 2012.</p>
<p>Sparta released a new song on May 17, 2012 called "Chemical Feel". The song is free to download, and is the first new song the band has released in six years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<ul>
<li>"<a href="/wiki/Taking_Back_Control" title="Taking Back Control">Taking Back Control</a>" was featured in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Invisible_(film)" title="The Invisible (film)">The Invisible</a></i>. In addition to being in the album released for the movie, the music video is a special feature on the <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li>"Each Brave Eye" was released in 2002 as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B-side" title="B-side">B-side</a> on the "Cut Your Ribbon" single.</li>
<li>The band also performed a cover of "Kiss the Bottle" for the <a href="/wiki/Jawbreaker_(band)" title="Jawbreaker (band)">Jawbreaker</a> tribute album, <i>Bad Scene, Everyone's Fault</i>.</li>
<li>The songs "Farewell Ruins" and "Bombs and Us" were released on the Japanese version of <i>Porcelain</i>. "Farewell Ruins" was also released on a 7" vinyl and <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>. Ward confirmed <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> that "Bombs and Us" was written about the late songwriter, <a href="/wiki/Elliott_Smith" title="Elliott Smith">Elliott Smith</a>.</li>
<li>An earlier version of "Taking Back Control", originally titled "Future Needs", appears on the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Madden_NFL_07" title="Madden NFL 07">Madden NFL 07</a></i>.</li>
<li>There are also three B-sides for <i>Threes</i>: "As Far As We Go", which was handed out upon purchase of <i>Threes</i> at participating stores, "Blood Spills", an iTunes exclusive, and "Born and Buried," a bonus track released on the European version of the album.</li>
<li>The intro to the song "Cut Your Ribbon" has been used for the show <i><a href="/wiki/True_Life" title="True Life">True Life</a></i>, which can be seen on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>.</li>
</ul>
