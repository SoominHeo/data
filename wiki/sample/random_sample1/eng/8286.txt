<p><i><b>Sphenoclea</b></i> is a genus of succulent erect annual herbs. They occur in damp habitats throughout the tropics.</p>
<p>There are two species,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Christenhusz-Byng2016_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Christenhusz-Byng2016-1">[1]</a></sup> <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sphenoclea_zeylanica&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sphenoclea zeylanica (page does not exist)">S. zeylanica</a></i> and <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sphenoclea_pongatium&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sphenoclea pongatium (page does not exist)">S. pongatium</a></i>. The genus is placed alone in family <b>Sphenocleaceae</b>. The position of the family is somewhat uncertain; it is now usually placed in <a href="/wiki/Solanales" title="Solanales">Solanales</a>, but it has previously been placed in <a href="/wiki/Asterales" title="Asterales">Asterales</a>, especially near <a href="/wiki/Campanulaceae" title="Campanulaceae">Campanulaceae</a>, and there is some evidence to support this.</p>
