<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_National_Orchestra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although the orchestra originated as of 1937, during the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War" title="Spanish Civil War">Spanish Civil War</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> it was legally founded in 1940,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> by the merging of Pérez Casas' <i>Filarmónica</i> and the <i>Orquesta Sinfónica</i> of <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Fern%C3%A1ndez_Arb%C3%B3s" title="Enrique Fernández Arbós">Enrique Fernández Arbós</a>.</p>
<p>The first official concert of the newly founded orchestra was in March 1941 at the Teatro Maria Guerrero in Madrid conducted by eminent Portuguese conductor Pedro de Freitas Branco (1896-1963). The principal conductors of these first years of the orchestra were <a href="/wiki/Ernesto_Halffter" title="Ernesto Halffter">Ernesto Halffter</a>, José María Franco, <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Jord%C3%A1" title="Enrique Jordá">Enrique Jordá</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eduard_Toldr%C3%A0" title="Eduard Toldrà">Eduard Toldrà</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_Ar%C3%A1mbarri" title="Jesús Arámbarri">Jesús Arámbarri</a>, until the designation of the first principal conductor of the orchestra, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bartolom%C3%A9_P%C3%A9rez_Casas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bartolomé Pérez Casas (page does not exist)">Bartolomé Pérez Casas</a>. After his death the new principal conductor was <a href="/wiki/Ata%C3%BAlfo_Argenta" title="Ataúlfo Argenta">Ataúlfo Argenta</a>, who was in the orchestra from 1945 as the keyboard instruments player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
