<p><b>Letizia Ortiz Rocasolano</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[leˈtiθja]</a></span>; born 15 September 1972) is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_consort_of_Spain" title="Queen consort of Spain">Queen of Spain</a> as the wife of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_Felipe_VI" title="King Felipe VI">King Felipe VI</a>, who ascended on 19 June 2014 on the abdication of his father <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juan_Carlos_I" title="Juan Carlos I">Juan Carlos I</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ElPais_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ElPais-1">[1]</a></sup> Before her marriage to Felipe, Letizia was a <a href="/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalist</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/News_anchor" title="News anchor">news anchor</a>. Letizia and Felipe have two daughters, <a href="/wiki/Leonor,_Princess_of_Asturias" title="Leonor, Princess of Asturias">Leonor, Princess of Asturias</a>, who is now the heir presumptive; and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Infanta_Sof%C3%ADa" title="Infanta Sofía">Infanta Sofía</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_Letizia_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Letizia was born in <a href="/wiki/Oviedo" title="Oviedo">Oviedo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asturias" title="Asturias">Asturias</a>, to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle-class" title="Middle-class">middle-class</a> family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> She is the eldest daughter of Jesús José Ortiz Álvarez, a journalist, and his first wife, María de la Paloma Rocasolano Rodríguez, a registered nurse and hospital union representative.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She has two younger sisters, Telma (b. 1973) and Érika (1975–2007), whose death was widely reported by press as due to an intentional prescription drug overdose.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her parents divorced in 1999 and her father remarried on 1 September 2000 in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> on 18 March 2004 to fellow journalist Ana Togores, born ca. 1955.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Letizia's paternal grandparents were José Luis Ortiz Velasco (ca. 1923–2005), a commercial employee at <a href="/wiki/Olivetti" title="Olivetti">Olivetti</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and María del Carmen "Menchu" Álvarez del Valle (born in ca. 1928), a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio_broadcaster" title="Radio broadcaster">radio broadcaster</a> in Asturias for over 40 years. Her maternal grandfather was Francisco Julio Rocasolano Camacho (1918–2015), a mechanic and cab driver in Madrid for over 20 years who was of French and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Occitan" title="Occitan">Occitan</a> origin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Letizia's maternal grandmother, Enriqueta Rodriguez Figueredo (1919-2008) was born in the Philippines to Spanish parents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wargs_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wargs-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Medieval_lineage">Medieval lineage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_Letizia_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Medieval lineage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>British <a href="/wiki/Genealogy" title="Genealogy">genealogists</a> have provided evidence that through her mother's Rocasolano lineage, Letizia descends from Astorg Roquesoulane (died c.1564) and her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coat-of-arms" title="Coat-of-arms">coat-of-arms</a> incorporates the arms of the Rocasolano family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wargs_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-Wargs-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riag.es_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-riag.es-15">[15]</a></sup> Reports have suggested – and remain unproven – that on her paternal grandfather's side, Letizia is a descendant of an untitled family descended from medieval nobility who served as <a href="/wiki/Constable_of_Castile" title="Constable of Castile">Constables of Castile</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education_and_career">Education and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_Letizia_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Education and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Letizia attended La Gesta School in Oviedo, before her family moved to <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, where she attended high school at the Ramiro de Maeztu High School.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-navarra_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-navarra-18">[18]</a></sup> She completed a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">bachelor's degree</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Licentiate" title="Licentiate">licentiate</a>'s degree in journalism, at the <a href="/wiki/Complutense_University_of_Madrid" title="Complutense University of Madrid">Complutense University of Madrid</a>, as well as a master's degree in audiovisual journalism at the Institute for Studies in Audiovisual Journalism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-official-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>During her studies, Letizia worked for the Asturian daily newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/La_Nueva_Espa%C3%B1a" title="La Nueva España">La Nueva España</a></i> and later for the newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/ABC_(newspaper)" title="ABC (newspaper)">ABC</a></i> and the news agency <a href="/wiki/EFE" title="EFE">EFE</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-navarra_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-navarra-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-official-19">[19]</a></sup> After completing her studies, she spent some time in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guadalajara,_Jalisco" title="Guadalajara, Jalisco">Guadalajara</a>, Mexico, working at the newspaper <i>Siglo 21</i>. After returning to Spain, she worked for the Spanish version of the economic channel <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Television" title="Bloomberg Television">Bloomberg</a> before moving to the news network <a href="/wiki/CNN%2B" title="CNN+">CNN+</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-official-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2000, she moved to <a href="/wiki/Televisi%C3%B3n_Espa%C3%B1ola" title="Televisión Española">TVE</a>, where she started working for the news channel <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canal_24_Horas" title="Canal 24 Horas">24 Horas</a>. In 2002, she anchored the weekly news report programme <i>Informe Semanal</i> and later the daily morning news programme <i><a href="/wiki/Telediario" title="Telediario">Telediario</a> Matinal</i> on TVE 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-navarra_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-navarra-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official_19-3"><a href="#cite_note-official-19">[19]</a></sup> In August 2003, a few months before her engagement to Felipe, Letizia was promoted to anchor of the TVE daily evening news programme <i><a href="/wiki/Telediario" title="Telediario">Telediario 2</a></i>, the most viewed newscast in Spain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2000, Letizia reported from Washington, D.C., on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._presidential_election,_2000" title="U.S. presidential election, 2000">presidential elections</a>. In September 2001, she broadcast live from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ground_Zero" title="Ground Zero">Ground Zero</a> following the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">9/11 attacks</a> in New York and in 2003, she filed reports from <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> following the <a href="/wiki/2003_invasion_of_Iraq" title="2003 invasion of Iraq">war</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In 2002 she sent several reports from <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">Galicia</a> in northern Spain following the ecological disaster when the oil tanker <i><a href="/wiki/Prestige_oil_spill" title="Prestige oil spill">Prestige</a></i> sank.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="First_marriage">First marriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_Letizia_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: First marriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Letizia married Alonso Guerrero Pérez (born in 1962), a writer and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High-school" title="High-school">high-school</a> literature teacher, on 7 August 1998, in a simple <a href="/wiki/Civil_marriage" title="Civil marriage">civil ceremony</a> at <a href="/wiki/Almendralejo" title="Almendralejo">Almendralejo</a>, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Badajoz_(province)" title="Badajoz (province)">Badajoz</a>, after a 10-year courtship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The marriage was dissolved by divorce in 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_marriage_and_children">Second marriage and children</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_Letizia_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Second marriage and children">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>On 1 November 2003, to the surprise of many, the Royal Household announced Letizia's engagement to the Prince of Asturias.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official_19-4"><a href="#cite_note-official-19">[19]</a></sup> Afterwards, she moved to live in a wing of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palacio_de_la_Zarzuela" title="Palacio de la Zarzuela">Zarzuela Palace</a> until the day of her wedding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The Prince of Asturias had proposed to her with a 16-<a href="/wiki/Diamond_cut#Choice_of_cut" title="Diamond cut">baguette</a> diamond engagement ring with a white gold trim. She marked the occasion by giving him white gold and sapphire cufflinks and a classic book.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>The wedding took place on 22 May 2004 in the <a href="/wiki/Almudena_Cathedral" title="Almudena Cathedral">Cathedral Santa María la Real de la Almudena</a> in Madrid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> It was the first royal wedding in this cathedral, which was consecrated by Pope <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Paul_II" title="John Paul II">John Paul II</a> in 1993. It had been nearly a century since the capital celebrated a royal wedding, as the prince's parents married in <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>, and his sisters, <a href="/wiki/Infanta_Elena,_Duchess_of_Lugo" title="Infanta Elena, Duchess of Lugo">Infanta Elena</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Infanta_Cristina,_Duchess_of_Palma_de_Mallorca" title="Infanta Cristina, Duchess of Palma de Mallorca">Infanta Cristina</a>, married in <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> respectively.</p>
<p>Letizia's bridal gown was designed by Spanish fashion designer <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Pertegaz" title="Manuel Pertegaz">Manuel Pertegaz</a>, her bridal shoes by Pura López; and the veil, a gift from Felipe to his bride, was made of off-white silk tulle and hand-embroidered with beautiful details.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>As Letizia's previous marriage involved only a civil ceremony, the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a> does not consider it canonically valid and therefore did not require an <a href="/wiki/Annulment" title="Annulment">annulment</a> to proceed with a Catholic marriage to the Prince of Asturias.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Letizia and Felipe have two daughters: <a href="/wiki/Leonor,_Princess_of_Asturias" title="Leonor, Princess of Asturias">Leonor, Princess of Asturias</a>, born on 31 October 2005;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Infanta_Sof%C3%ADa_of_Spain" title="Infanta Sofía of Spain">Infanta Sofía</a>, born on 29 April 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>

<p>The Princess immediately joined in the duties of her husband and traveled extensively through Spain representing her father-in-law. They also represented Spain in other countries: the Princess has traveled along with the Prince to <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">United States of America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>. She also greeted international dignitaries, along with other members of the royal family. Letizia also attended gatherings of foreign royalty in <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a>, for the silver wedding anniversary of the <a href="/wiki/Henri,_Grand_Duke_of_Luxembourg" title="Henri, Grand Duke of Luxembourg">Grand Duke</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maria_Teresa,_Grand_Duchess_of_Luxembourg" title="Maria Teresa, Grand Duchess of Luxembourg">Grand Duchess of Luxembourg</a>, and in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> for the 40th birthday of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Willem-Alexander,_Prince_of_Orange" title="Willem-Alexander, Prince of Orange">Prince of Orange</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her solo agenda was announced in 2006, shortly after the announcement of her second pregnancy. Letizia has performed a couple of audiences and her work will focus on social issues such as children rights, culture, and education. In late 2007, her solo agenda started to grow in the quantity of events she performed by herself and Felipe's and Letizia's agendas became more distinct and separate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>Letizia supported Spanish designers, from couturiers such as <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Varela" title="Felipe Varela">Felipe Varela</a> and Lorenzo Caprile to <a href="/wiki/Zara_(retailer)" title="Zara (retailer)">Zara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mango_(clothing)" title="Mango (clothing)">Mango</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> and continues to as queen consort.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Queen_consort">Queen consort</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_Letizia_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Queen consort">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>On 19 June 2014, Letizia became Queen of Spain; as such, she holds the style of <a href="/wiki/Majesty" title="Majesty">Majesty</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> She is the first Spanish-born queen consort since <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_of_Orl%C3%A9ans" title="Mercedes of Orléans">Mercedes of Orléans</a>, the first wife of <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_XII_of_Spain" title="Alfonso XII of Spain">Alfonso XII</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> She is also the first Spanish queen to have been born as a <a href="/wiki/Commoner" title="Commoner">commoner</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Express_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-Express-37">[37]</a></sup> Letizia undertook her first solo engagement as queen on 23 June 2014 at the inauguration of the <a href="/wiki/El_Greco" title="El Greco">El Greco</a> and modern painting exhibition at the <a href="/wiki/Museo_del_Prado" title="Museo del Prado">National Prado museum</a> in Madrid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>In their first overseas trip as monarch and consort, King Felipe VI and Queen Letizia met <a href="/wiki/Pope_Francis" title="Pope Francis">Pope Francis</a> on 30 June 2014, in the Apostolic Palace. They later met with <a href="/wiki/Cardinal_Secretary_of_State" title="Cardinal Secretary of State">Cardinal Secretary of State</a> <a href="/wiki/Pietro_Parolin" title="Pietro Parolin">Pietro Parolin</a>, accompanied by Mgsr. <a href="/wiki/Antoine_Camilleri" title="Antoine Camilleri">Antoine Camilleri</a>, under-secretary for Relations with States. The visit followed one by King Juan Carlos I and Queen Sofia on 28 April.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, Letizia was named Special Ambassador for Nutrition for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Food_and_Agriculture_Organization" title="United Nations Food and Agriculture Organization">United Nations Food and Agriculture Organization</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>Letizia and Felipe and the princesses still reside at the residence where they have lived since 2004. It is stated that the <a href="/wiki/Palace_of_Zarzuela" title="Palace of Zarzuela">Palace of Zarzuela</a> is a working place only for Felipe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2016, leaked texted messages between Letizia and businessman Javier Lopez Madrid created a controversy. Together with other executives and board members of the Caja Madrid and Bankia financial group, Madrid had been accused of corruption. In October 2014 Letizia pledged her support for him, texting "We know who you are and you know who we are. We know each other, like each other, respect each other. To hell with the rest. Kisses yoga mate (miss you!!!)". Felipe also joined in, texting "We do indeed!" Publication of the texts by the newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/El_Diario_Vasco" title="El Diario Vasco">El Diario</a></i> threatened to undo the line Felipe had drawn between himself and the scandal of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Infanta_Cristina" title="Infanta Cristina">Infanta Cristina</a> and her husband, as well as questions over the origins of ex-King Juan Carlos's private fortune. A palace official subsequently stated that the King and Queen were no longer friends with Madrid due to his legal issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Titles.2C_honours.2C_and_arms">Titles, honours, and arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_Letizia_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Titles, honours, and arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_titles_and_honours_of_Queen_Letizia_of_Spain" title="List of titles and honours of Queen Letizia of Spain">List of titles and honours of Queen Letizia of Spain</a></div>
<p>At the time of her marriage to Prince Felipe, Letizia was appointed to the Grand Cross of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Charles_III" title="Order of Charles III">Royal and Distinguished Order of Charles III</a> as dame on 21 May 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Since then, Letizia has received different appointments and decorations by foreign states and other Spanish honours.</p>

<dt>Notes</dt>

The Queen's coat of arms has no official status. In Spain only the coats of arms of the <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms_of_the_King_of_Spain" title="Coat of arms of the King of Spain">King</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms_of_the_Princess_of_Asturias" title="Coat of arms of the Princess of Asturias">Princess of Asturias</a> are official.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PictureSummaryWikimediaCommons-1_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-PictureSummaryWikimediaCommons-1-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PictureSummaryWikimediaCommons-2_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-PictureSummaryWikimediaCommons-2-46">[46]</a></sup></dd>
