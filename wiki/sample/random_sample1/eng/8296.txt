<p>The modern country of <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> was <a href="/wiki/History_of_Spain" title="History of Spain">formed</a> in the wake of the expansion of the Christian states in northern Spain, a process known as the <a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">Reconquista</a>. The Reconquista spanned approximately 770 years between the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Covadonga" title="Battle of Covadonga">Battle of Covadonga</a> c. 720 CE and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fall_of_Granada" title="Fall of Granada">Fall of Granada</a> in 1492.</p>
<p>Several independent Christian kingdoms and mostly independent political entities (<a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Asturias" title="Kingdom of Asturias">Asturias</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="Kingdom of León">León</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Galicia" title="Kingdom of Galicia">Galicia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile" title="Kingdom of Castile">Castile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Navarre" title="Kingdom of Navarre">Navarre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Aragon" title="Kingdom of Aragon">Aragon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Catalonia" title="Principality of Catalonia">Catalonia</a>) were formed by their own inhabitants' efforts under aristocratic leadership, coexisting with the <a href="/wiki/Al-Andalus" title="Al-Andalus">Muslim Iberian states</a> and having their own identities and borders.</p>
<p>Eventually, the kingdoms of Castile and Aragon eclipsed the others in power and size through conquest and dynastic inheritance, their Crowns ultimately merging in 1469 with the marriage of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Monarchs" title="Catholic Monarchs">Catholic Monarchs</a>. After this, the Muslim Kingdom of Granada was conquered in 1492, and Navarre invaded and forced into the union in 1512, through a combination of conquest and collaboration of the local elites.</p>
<p>Portugal, formerly part of León, gained independence in 1128 after a split in the inheritance of the daughters of <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_VI_of_Le%C3%B3n_and_Castile" title="Alfonso VI of León and Castile">Alfonso VI</a> and remained independent throughout the Reconquista.</p>
<p>For a short time starting with <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Spain" title="Philip II of Spain">Philip II</a> of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Habsburg" title="House of Habsburg">House of Habsburg</a>, Portugal was united with all the other realms under the same Head of State, in the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Union" title="Iberian Union">Iberian Union</a>. However the Portuguese revolted as the Dutch provinces and other Habsburg dominions and became officially independent again from their Sovereigns in 1640.</p>
<p>These kingdoms sometimes collaborated when they fought against Al-Andalus and sometimes allied themselves with the Muslims against rival Christian neighbors. The common non-Christian enemy has been usually considered the single crucial catalyst for the union of the different Christian realms. However, it was effective only for permanently reconquered territories. The unifications that came later were and are a whole different subject, as some of them happened long after the departure of the last Muslim rulers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>All these different kingdoms were ruled together, or separately in <a href="/wiki/Personal_union" title="Personal union">personal union</a>, but maintained their particular ethnic differences, regardless of similarities through common origins or borrowed customs.</p>
<p>Since the reign of <a href="/wiki/Philip_V_of_Spain" title="Philip V of Spain">Philip V</a>, there has been a process of <a href="/wiki/Nueva_Planta_decrees" title="Nueva Planta decrees">uniformization</a> by the central authorities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>This uniformization has been resisted by some of the local elites, who have their own national consciousness based on traditional, historical, linguistic and cultural attributes.</p>
<p>Some <a href="/wiki/Monarchy" title="Monarchy">Kingdoms</a>, like <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">Navarre</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Lord" title="Lord">Lordships</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)" title="Basque Country (autonomous community)">Basque Country</a>, maintained <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitutions</a> based on their historical rights and laws, while other <a href="/wiki/Monarchy" title="Monarchy">Kingdoms</a> revolted against this process of <a href="/wiki/Centralized_government" title="Centralized government">centralism</a> demanding a return of their derogated laws as well as better living conditions (<a href="/wiki/Revolt_of_the_Comuneros" title="Revolt of the Comuneros">Revolt of the Comuneros</a>, <a href="/wiki/Revolt_of_the_Brotherhoods" title="Revolt of the Brotherhoods">Revolt of the Brotherhoods</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catalan_Revolt" title="Catalan Revolt">Catalan Revolt</a>). Nationalistic movements with significant support appear by the end of the 19th century, coinciding with the loss of the last parts of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a>, the abolition of privileges, and continued high mercantile traditions with later industrial developments of some regions in comparison to others.</p>
<p>Following the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War" title="Spanish Civil War">Spanish Civil War</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Francoist_Spain" title="Francoist Spain">Francoist</a> imposition of <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> as the only official language, and the persecution of all remaining historical languages and identities, had the effect of putting the constituent nations' survival in danger, leading to many powerful expressions of nationalism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_transition_to_democracy" title="Spanish transition to democracy">Spanish transition to democracy</a> after the dictatorship of <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Franco" title="Francisco Franco">Francisco Franco</a>, there have been many movements for more autonomy in certain regions of the country, advocating full independence in some cases, and an autonomous "community" in others. It is a controversial topic in Spain, and references to it can be found almost every day in the press, especially in the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)" title="Basque Country (autonomous community)">Basque Country</a> and <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>.</p>
<p>The two most popular parties in Spain have different views on the subject. The <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Party_(Spain)" title="People's Party (Spain)">People’s Party</a> supports a more centralized Spain, with a unitary market, and usually does not support movements advocating greater regional autonomy. The <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Socialist_Workers%27_Party" title="Spanish Socialist Workers' Party">Spanish Socialist Workers' Party</a> supports a federal state with greater autonomy for the regions, but is opposed to total independence for any region.</p>
<p>The structure of this article is determined by the amount of popular support for the individual movements, so that even though there are political parties demanding independence from Spain for Castile, Cantabria, Valencia, Andalusia and Murcia, these parties hardly get any votes, and thus do not represent popular sentiment in their regions (percentages of nationalist and regionalist votes are given in parentheses according to figures of the elections held at <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipality</a> level in May 2007).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nationalism">Nationalism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Nationalism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Andalusia">Andalusia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Andalusia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">Andalusia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andalusians" title="Andalusians">Andalusians</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Andalusian_Spanish" title="Andalusian Spanish">Andalusian Spanish</a></div>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Andalusian_nationalism" title="Andalusian nationalism">Andalusian nationalism</a></div>
<ul>
<li>(Nationalism-Regionalism: 2.51% <a href="/wiki/Andalusian_Party" title="Andalusian Party">PA</a>). The <a href="/wiki/Unitarian_Candidacy_of_Workers" title="Unitarian Candidacy of Workers">Unitarian Candidacy of Workers</a> has one deputy in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Andalusian_parliament&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Andalusian parliament (page does not exist)">Andalusian parliament</a>, but he was elected in the lists of the <a href="/wiki/United_Left_(Spain)" title="United Left (Spain)">United Left</a> (<a href="/wiki/Andalusian_parliamentary_election,_2012" title="Andalusian parliamentary election, 2012">Andalusian parliamentary election, 2012</a>)</li>
</ul>
<p>Andalusia first Statute of Autonomy could not be enacted during the <a href="/wiki/Second_Spanish_Republic" title="Second Spanish Republic">Republican government</a> because of the Spanish Civil War, and, although it is not considered a historical community in the literal sense, it expanded the autonomy after a referendum (1981).</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Monument_to_Blas_Infante,_Seville.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1600" data-file-width="1200" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Monument_to_Blas_Infante%2C_Seville.jpg/220px-Monument_to_Blas_Infante%2C_Seville.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Monument_to_Blas_Infante%2C_Seville.jpg/330px-Monument_to_Blas_Infante%2C_Seville.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Monument_to_Blas_Infante%2C_Seville.jpg/440px-Monument_to_Blas_Infante%2C_Seville.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Monument_to_Blas_Infante,_Seville.jpg" title="Aumentar"></a></div>
Monument to <a href="/wiki/Blas_Infante" title="Blas Infante">Blas Infante</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Also, the Andalusians speak varieties of southern Spanish that collectively are perceived in Spain as being the most different from standard Spanish. However, there is no marked dialectal discontinuity with neighbouring regions, Extremaduran Spanish is quite close to western Andalusian and Murcian is also not very different from eastern varieties of Andalusian. In its extreme form the speech may be difficult to understand by those who are not familiar with Southern Spanish. <a href="/wiki/Andalusian_Spanish" title="Andalusian Spanish">Andalusian Spanish</a> is traditionally considered a dialect, even though it has a lot of internal variation.</p>
<p>Its old <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Autonomy" title="Statute of Autonomy">Statute of Autonomy</a> defines this region as a <a href="/wiki/Nationality" title="Nationality">nationality</a>. In the new Statute of Autonomy, approved in referendum on February 18, 2007, Andalusia is defined as a national entity in the preamble ('Andalusian manifesto of Cordoba described Andalusia as a national reality in 1919...') and as a historic nationality in its first section. According to a poll <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> 18.1% supported declaring Andalusia a nation in the new statute as a nation, while 60.7% of Andalusians did not agree with it.</p>
<p>Andalusian nationalism has a strong presence in the <a href="/wiki/Trade_union" title="Trade union">trade union</a> movement. In fact, the nationalist <a href="/wiki/Sindicato_Andaluz_de_Trabajadores" title="Sindicato Andaluz de Trabajadores">Sindicato Andaluz de Trabajadores</a> (SAT) has 25.000 members and a very strong presence in the rural areas.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Basque_Country">Basque Country</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Basque Country">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(greater_region)" title="Basque Country (greater region)">Basque Country (greater region)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Basques" title="Basques">Basques</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Basque_language" title="Basque language">Basque language</a></div>

<p><a href="/wiki/Basque_nationalism" title="Basque nationalism">Basque nationalism</a> runs the range from full independence to further devolution to the Basque government.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_the_Basque_Country.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="560" data-file-width="1000" height="123" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Flag_of_the_Basque_Country.svg/220px-Flag_of_the_Basque_Country.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Flag_of_the_Basque_Country.svg/330px-Flag_of_the_Basque_Country.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Flag_of_the_Basque_Country.svg/440px-Flag_of_the_Basque_Country.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_the_Basque_Country.svg" title="Aumentar"></a></div>
The <a href="/wiki/Ikurri%C3%B1a" title="Ikurriña">Ikurriña</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>For instance, the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Nationalist_Party" title="Basque Nationalist Party">Basque Nationalist Party</a> (PNV) regularly wins elections at municipal, regional or Spanish levels in the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)" title="Basque Country (autonomous community)">Basque Country</a>, but the fact that it achieves a mere <a href="/wiki/Plurality_(voting)" title="Plurality (voting)">plurality</a> and that electors of PNV do not unanimously support (full) independence, counters the belief that independence is generally supported by the Basque population. In the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_general_election,_2011" title="Spanish general election, 2011">2011 Spanish elections</a> the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Left-wing_abertzale&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Left-wing abertzale (page does not exist)">left-wing abertzale</a> coalition <a href="/wiki/Amaiur" title="Amaiur">Amaiur</a> obtained 6 seats in the Spanish parliament and 24.12% of votes in the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)" title="Basque Country (autonomous community)">Basque Country</a> and one seat and the 14.86% of votes in <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">Navarre</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Basque_Nationalist_Party" title="Basque Nationalist Party">Basque Nationalist Party</a> obtained 5 seats and the 27.42% of votes. The navarrese coalition <a href="/wiki/Geroa_Bai" title="Geroa Bai">Geroa Bai</a> obtained one seat and the 12.84% of votes in <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">Navarre</a>. Basque <a href="/wiki/Trade_union" title="Trade union">trade unions</a> are also very strong, the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Workers%27_Solidarity" title="Basque Workers' Solidarity">Basque Workers' Solidarity</a> union has 109,318 members and is the most important union in the Basque Country, while <a href="/wiki/LAB_(Basque_union)" title="LAB (Basque union)">LAB</a>, linked with the <a href="/wiki/Batasuna" title="Batasuna">abertzale left</a> is the third trade union and has 40,000 members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to recent studies,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> a plurality (31%) of the population in the Basque Country autonomous community would vote YES and 39% NO. Different results appear when the options are independence, further devolution or the current status. The option for a restoration of centralization is barely recorded. According to a 2012 poll made by the <a href="/wiki/Centro_de_Investigaciones_Sociol%C3%B3gicas" title="Centro de Investigaciones Sociológicas">CIS</a>, 49.8% of the Basques and 43.7% of Navarrese want more autonomy.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Only 3.1% of Basques and 7.3% of Navarrese want less autonomy.</p>
<p>The nationalists consider <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">Navarre</a> and the <a href="/wiki/French_Basque_Country" title="French Basque Country">French Basque Country</a> as part of the same nation, the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(greater_region)" title="Basque Country (greater region)">Basque Country</a>. In the current Basque Statute of Autonomy it is stated that Navarre has the legal right to belong to the autonomous community of the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)" title="Basque Country (autonomous community)">Basque Country</a>, and the Spanish Constitution has a transitory provision allowing it to join at any time, but Navarrese politicians chose not to enter the agreement and became a Foral Community instead.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Autonomy_of_the_Basque_Country" title="Statute of Autonomy of the Basque Country">Statute of Autonomy of the Basque Country</a> defines this region as a <a href="/wiki/Nationality" title="Nationality">nationality</a>.</p>
<p>In 2003, <a href="/wiki/Lehendakari" title="Lehendakari">Lehendakari</a> <a href="/wiki/Juan_Jos%C3%A9_Ibarretxe" title="Juan José Ibarretxe">Juan José Ibarretxe</a> proposed a <a class="extiw" href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plan_Ibarretxe" title="es:Plan Ibarretxe">plan</a> that would have changed the current status of the Basque Country as an <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_communities_of_Spain" title="Autonomous communities of Spain">autonomous community</a> to a "status of free association" (see <a href="/wiki/Associated_state" title="Associated state">Associated state</a> and <a href="/wiki/Free_state_(government)" title="Free state (government)">Free State</a>). It was approved 39-35 by the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Parliament" title="Basque Parliament">Basque Parliament</a>, but the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Deputies_(Spain)" title="Congress of Deputies (Spain)">Spanish Congress of Deputies</a> rejected it 29-313 in 2005, thus halting the progress of the reform.</p>
<p>The then President of the Spanish Government, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Luis_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Zapatero" title="José Luis Rodríguez Zapatero">José Luis Rodríguez Zapatero</a>, stated that he will support any reform to the Statute of Autonomy which is supported by 2/3 of the Basque Parliament (a verbal condition not legally written anywhere, for the only condition needed for a statute to be approve is the half of the total plus one votes in the Basque Parliament).</p>
<p>On 29 September 2007 <a href="/wiki/Juan_Jos%C3%A9_Ibarretxe" title="Juan José Ibarretxe">Juan José Ibarretxe</a> declared that an <a href="/wiki/Basque_referendum,_2008" title="Basque referendum, 2008">autonomic referendum</a> or a popular poll about the will of the population of the Basque Country on independence would be held on 25 October 2008, but it was declared illegal and forbidden by the <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_Court_of_Spain" title="Constitutional Court of Spain">Constitutional Court</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Catalonia">Catalonia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Catalonia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Catalan_nationalism" title="Catalan nationalism">Catalan nationalism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catalan_independence" title="Catalan independence">Catalan independence</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catalans" title="Catalans">Catalans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catalan_constitutions" title="Catalan constitutions">Catalan constitutions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan language</a>, <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Catalonia" title="Principality of Catalonia">Principality of Catalonia</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pa%C3%AFsos_Catalans" title="Països Catalans">Països Catalans</a></div>

<ul>
<li>Moderate Catalanism / federalism (<a href="/wiki/Socialists%27_Party_of_Catalonia" title="Socialists' Party of Catalonia">PSC-PSOE</a>, <a href="/wiki/Initiative_for_Catalonia_Greens" title="Initiative for Catalonia Greens">ICV</a>) = 34.72% of vote General elections 2011 (50.31% in 2008)</li>
<li>Independentist/sovereignty Catalanism (<a href="/wiki/Republican_Left_of_Catalonia" title="Republican Left of Catalonia">ERC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Convergence_and_Union" title="Convergence and Union">CiU</a>) = 36.41% of vote General elections 2011 (28.76% in 2008)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>Historically catalanism has supported a federal system that includes a Catalan nation within Spain. Not all catalanist parties openly call for an independent state; the ones that do it are <a href="/wiki/Republican_Left_of_Catalonia" title="Republican Left of Catalonia">Republican Left of Catalonia</a> (ERC), <a href="/wiki/Catalan_Solidarity_for_Independence" title="Catalan Solidarity for Independence">Catalan Solidarity for Independence</a> (SI), Catalan Democracy (DC), <a href="/wiki/Popular_Unity_Candidacy" title="Popular Unity Candidacy">Popular Unity Candidacy</a> (CUP), and <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Convergence_of_Catalonia" title="Democratic Convergence of Catalonia">Democratic Convergence of Catalonia</a> (CDC) all with representation in the Catalan Parliament. Other independentist parties, with no parliamentary representation, include, <a href="/wiki/Estat_Catal%C3%A0" title="Estat Català">Estat Català</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reagrupament" title="Reagrupament">Reagrupament</a>, among others. (Note that Convergence and Union (CiU) is a coalition of Convergència Democràtica de Catalunya (CDC) and Unió Democràtica de Catalunya (UDC), which has been holding the regional government, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Presidents_of_the_Generalitat_de_Catalunya" title="List of Presidents of the Generalitat de Catalunya">Generalitat de Catalunya</a> between 1980-2003 and from 2010. This coalition has been finished in June 18, 2015, when <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Union_of_Catalonia" title="Democratic Union of Catalonia">UDC</a> didn't accept CDC position supporting Catalonia independence<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>)</p>
<p>Despite some Catalan nationalists considering the Catalan-speaking regions (<a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Catalonia" title="Principality of Catalonia">Principality of Catalonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Valencian_Community" title="Valencian Community">Valencian Community</a>, <a href="/wiki/Balearic_Islands" title="Balearic Islands">Balearic Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andorra" title="Andorra">Andorra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Catalonia" title="Northern Catalonia">Northern Catalonia</a>, (France), and some adjacent territories) as part of the same nation or ethnic group, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pa%C3%AFsos_Catalans" title="Països Catalans">Països Catalans</a>, and more broadly the territories where the Catalan language is spoken, Catalan nationalism is only widespread within the borders of the Autonomy.</p>
<p>The inhabitants of <a href="/wiki/Val_d%27Aran" title="Val d'Aran">Val d'Aran</a> still speak <a href="/wiki/Aranese_dialect" title="Aranese dialect">their own dialect</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Occitan_language" title="Occitan language">Occitan language</a> in addition to Catalan and Spanish, and are considered part of the Occitan nation despite the fact that the Aranese territory has been part of Catalonia for centuries.</p>
<p>In 2005, in a vote to pass a <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Autonomy_of_Catalonia" title="Statute of Autonomy of Catalonia">draft of a new Statute of Autonomy</a>, 88.9% of the members of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Catalonia" title="Parliament of Catalonia">Parliament of Catalonia</a> declared Catalonia a <a href="/wiki/Nation" title="Nation">nation</a>, but this was finally changed back to 'nationality' by the decision of the Spanish Parliament and approved in a referendum; however, this statute mentions the word "nation", referring to Catalonia, in its preamble (with declaratory, but not legal value).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, during the celebration of the Catalan National Day, a <a href="/wiki/2012_Catalan_independence_demonstration" title="2012 Catalan independence demonstration">huge demonstration</a> jolted the political debate on independence of Catalonia. President <a href="/wiki/Artur_Mas" title="Artur Mas">Artur Mas</a> called <a href="/wiki/Catalan_parliamentary_election,_2012" title="Catalan parliamentary election, 2012">snap elections for the Parliament of Catalonia</a> to be held on 25 September and argued, referring to the demonstration, that "the street vocal must be moved to the polls".</p>

<p>In the past it was an <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Aragon" title="Kingdom of Aragon">independent kingdom</a> that, along with others, created the <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Aragon" title="Crown of Aragon">Crown of Aragon</a>, that later merged with the <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">Crown of Castile</a> to forge Spain. While there is some pro-independence support, most of Aragon's population does not seek an independent state but to be fully recognized as a distinct and important region in Spain. There is also a claim for the <a href="/wiki/Aragonese_language" title="Aragonese language">Aragonese language</a>, spoken in the northernmost area, to enjoy full official support. Its <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Autonomy" title="Statute of Autonomy">Statute of Autonomy</a> defines this region as a <a href="/wiki/Nationality" title="Nationality">nationality</a>.</p>
<p>At the moment, the most important independentist organizations or parties like Puyalon de Cuchas, Purna, A Enrestida, SOA or SEIRA are included in the Bloque Independentista de Cuchas (BIC)</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Galicia">Galicia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Galicia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">Galicia (Spain)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Galicians" title="Galicians">Galicians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Galician_language" title="Galician language">Galician language</a>, <a href="/wiki/Galician-Asturian" title="Galician-Asturian">Galician-Asturian</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Galician_nationalism" title="Galician nationalism">Galician nationalism</a></div>
<ul>
<li>(25.6% <a href="/wiki/Galician_Nationalist_Bloc" title="Galician Nationalist Bloc">BNG</a>+<a href="/wiki/Galician_Left_Alternative" title="Galician Left Alternative">AGE</a>+<a href="/wiki/Commitment_to_Galicia" title="Commitment to Galicia">CxG</a>+<a href="/wiki/Land_Party" title="Land Party">PT</a>+<a href="/wiki/Converxencia_XXI" title="Converxencia XXI">C.XXI</a>) (<a href="/wiki/Galician_parliamentary_election,_2012" title="Galician parliamentary election, 2012">Galician parliamentary election, 2012</a>)</li>

<p>From 2005 to 2009 Galicia was ruled by a coalition government between the <a href="/wiki/Socialists%27_Party_of_Galicia" title="Socialists' Party of Galicia">Socialists' Party of Galicia</a> (PSdeG-PSOE) and the nationalist <a href="/wiki/Galician_Nationalist_Bloc" title="Galician Nationalist Bloc">Galician Nationalist Bloc</a> (BNG). Unlike in other Spanish autonomous communities, the conservative <a href="/wiki/Galician_People%27s_Party" title="Galician People's Party">Galician People's Party</a> includes "<a href="/wiki/Galicianism" title="Galicianism">Galicianism</a>" (strong regionalism or even moderate nationalism) as one of its ideological principles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Even the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Socialist_Workers%27_Party" title="Spanish Socialist Workers' Party">Spanish Socialist Workers' Party</a> has a quite strong regional flavour in Galicia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> This issue somehow explains electoral behaviour in Galicia and why nationalist parties have a reduced representation when compared to Catalonia or the Basque Country, as voters in Galicia may choose to go for Spanish parties promoting Galicianism depending on the circumstances. Spanish parties in Catalonia and Basque Country, namely the People's Party, do not have such a strong regional identity.</p>
<p>The BNG is itself a coalition of parties, some of which endorses independence, like the <a href="/wiki/Galician_People%27s_Union" title="Galician People's Union">UPG</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Galician_Movement_for_Socialism" title="Galician Movement for Socialism">Galician Movement for Socialism</a>. Since the 2013 national assembly, BNG claims for national sovereignty, independence<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and strong promotion of Galician culture and language. Other nationalist parties stand for outright independence, and until recently they only had representatives in local councils and not in the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Galicia" title="Parliament of Galicia">Parliament of Galicia</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Galician_parliamentary_election,_2012" title="Galician parliamentary election, 2012">2012 election</a> the newly formed <a href="/wiki/Galician_Left_Alternative" title="Galician Left Alternative">Galician Left Alternative</a> (AGE), which includes independentist groups, overtook the BNG in Parliament, winning 9 seats. Galician nationalism is present in the majority of galician social movements, especially in the <a href="/wiki/Galician_language" title="Galician language">Galician language</a> defense movement (<a href="/wiki/A_Mesa_pola_Normalizaci%C3%B3n_Ling%C3%BC%C3%ADstica" title="A Mesa pola Normalización Lingüística">A Mesa pola Normalización Lingüística</a> (<i>The Panel for Language Normalization</i>), Queremos Galego (<i>We Want Galician</i>), <a href="/wiki/Galician_Language_Association" title="Galician Language Association">AGAL</a>, ...). It's also present in the ecologist movement (ADEGA, Verdegaia, <a href="/wiki/Plataforma_Nunca_M%C3%A1is" title="Plataforma Nunca Máis">Nunca Máis</a> (<i>Never Again</i>),...). Nationalism is also present in organized labour and trade unions, in fact, the most important union of Galicia is the <a href="/wiki/Left-wing_nationalism" title="Left-wing nationalism">left-wing nationalist</a> <a href="/wiki/Confederaci%C3%B3n_Intersindical_Galega" title="Confederación Intersindical Galega">Confederación Intersindical Galega</a> (<i>Galician Interunion Confederation</i>), with more than 80,000 members and 5,623 delegates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The present <a href="/wiki/Galician_Statute_of_Autonomy_of_1981" title="Galician Statute of Autonomy of 1981">Galician Statute of Autonomy of 1981</a> defines Galicia as a <a href="/wiki/Nationality" title="Nationality">nationality</a>. The former (2005–2009) <a href="/wiki/Xunta_de_Galicia" title="Xunta de Galicia">Galician Government</a> tried to draft a new Statute of Autonomy where Galicia would most probably have been defined as a <a href="/wiki/Nation" title="Nation">nation</a> (with declaratory, but not legal value).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> This was put on halt after the 2009 elections, following the win of the conservative People's Party.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Asturias">Asturias</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Asturias">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Asturias" title="Asturias">Asturias</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asturian_nationalism" title="Asturian nationalism">Asturian nationalism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asturians" title="Asturians">Asturians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asturian_language" title="Asturian language">Asturian language</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Galician-Asturian" title="Galician-Asturian">Galician-Asturian</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">

</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li>(Nationalism: 1.25% <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=URAS&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="URAS (page does not exist)">URAS</a> <a href="/wiki/Part%C3%ADu_Asturianista" title="Partíu Asturianista">PAS</a>, 0.65% <a href="/wiki/Unid%C3%A1" title="Unidá">Unidá</a>, 0.46% <a href="/wiki/Andecha_Astur" title="Andecha Astur">Andecha Astur</a> Regionalism: 24.83% <a href="/wiki/Asturias_Forum" title="Asturias Forum">Asturias Forum</a>)</li>

<p>Nationalist parties (e.g., <a href="/wiki/Andecha_Astur" title="Andecha Astur">Andecha Astur</a>) do not get much support from the population, but they clearly have an identity.</p>
<p>A wish for independence is stated sometimes by those parties, but as the independent and pre-Spanish <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Asturias" title="Kingdom of Asturias">Kingdom of Asturias</a> was the initial core of the <a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">Reconquista</a>, most of the people do not feel that there is any incompatibility in being Asturian and Spanish. Moreover, Asturian nationalist and regionalist claims are divided among independence, regionalism itself, conforming an autonomous community with Leon. Their sign of identity is the <a href="/wiki/Asturian_language" title="Asturian language">Asturian language</a>.</p>
<p>The most important <a href="/wiki/Regionalism_(politics)" title="Regionalism (politics)">regionalist</a> (not <a href="/wiki/Nationalism" title="Nationalism">nationalist</a>) party is <a href="/wiki/Asturias_Forum" title="Asturias Forum">Asturias Forum</a> (Forum Asturias,FAC), which split from the national centre-right <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Party_(Spain)" title="People's Party (Spain)">People's Party</a> in 2011. It was in government from 2011 to 2012.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canary_Islands">Canary Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Canary Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islanders" title="Canary Islanders">Canary Islanders</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Canarian_Spanish" title="Canarian Spanish">Canarian Spanish</a></div>
<ul>
<li>33.87% <a href="/wiki/Canarian_parliamentary_election,_2015" title="Canarian parliamentary election, 2015">Canarian parliamentary election, 2015</a>: <a href="/wiki/Canarian_Coalition" title="Canarian Coalition">CC</a> (19.9%), <a href="/wiki/New_Canaries" title="New Canaries">New Canaries</a> (10.2%), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalist_Canarian_Centre-United&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nationalist Canarian Centre-United (page does not exist)">Nationalist Canarian Centre-United</a> (3.59%), <a href="/wiki/Canarian_Nationalist_Alternative" title="Canarian Nationalist Alternative">Canarian Nationalist Alternative</a> (0.62%) and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Movement_for_the_Canarian_People%27s_Unity&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Movement for the Canarian People's Unity (page does not exist)">Movement for the Canarian People's Unity</a> (0.19%)</li>
</ul>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bandera_Siete_Estrellas_Verdes.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="400" data-file-width="600" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Bandera_Siete_Estrellas_Verdes.svg/220px-Bandera_Siete_Estrellas_Verdes.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Bandera_Siete_Estrellas_Verdes.svg/330px-Bandera_Siete_Estrellas_Verdes.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Bandera_Siete_Estrellas_Verdes.svg/440px-Bandera_Siete_Estrellas_Verdes.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bandera_Siete_Estrellas_Verdes.svg" title="Aumentar"></a></div>
Canarian nationalist banner</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Canarian nationalism has its roots in a number of events in the 19th century. Wars for independence in South America, self-government during the Napoleonic invasions, and the crisis of 1898 were the catalyst for figures such as <a href="/wiki/Nicol%C3%A1s_Est%C3%A9vanez" title="Nicolás Estévanez">Nicolás Estévanez</a> or <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Secundino_Delgado&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Secundino Delgado (page does not exist)">Secundino Delgado</a>. After a pause of several decades, a new nationalist movement has emerged there.</p>
<p>Its insularity requires several specific treatments. Over history the Canary Islands acquired special competences and privileges. In former times they even had the right to issue currency and their inhabitants were only obliged to perform military service <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Milicias_insulares&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Milicias insulares (page does not exist)">milicias insulares</a> within the Islands. The Islands were also governed by unique institutions called <a href="/wiki/Cabildo_insular" title="Cabildo insular">Cabildos</a> insulares.</p>
<p>Franco's government continued this tradition and conceded several privileges to the islands to compensate for their remoteness.</p>
<p>Its <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Autonomy" title="Statute of Autonomy">Statute of Autonomy</a> defines this region as a <a href="/wiki/Nationality" title="Nationality">nationality</a>.</p>
<p>The Canarian Government is drafting a new Statute of Autonomy where the Canary Islands will be defined as a <a href="/wiki/Nation" title="Nation">nation</a>. However this nationalism is mild in its formulation; thus independence is not even in the nationalist agenda. Historically, the <a href="/wiki/Canarian_Coalition" title="Canarian Coalition">Canarian Coalition</a> can be deemed more as a lobby in order to favour Canarian interests within Spain rather than a nationalist movement like the ones formulated in other areas.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Valencia">Valencia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Valencia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>(Nationalism: 18.78% <a href="/wiki/Coalici%C3%B3_Comprom%C3%ADs" title="Coalició Compromís">Coalició Compromís</a> (18.46%), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Som_Valencians&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Som Valencians (page does not exist)">Som Valencians</a> (0.28%); Other 4.32% <a href="/wiki/Acord_Ciutad%C3%A0" title="Acord Ciutadà">Acord Ciutadà</a> includes the independentist <a href="/wiki/Republican_Left_of_the_Valencian_Country" title="Republican Left of the Valencian Country">Republican Left of the Valencian Country</a> and the more moderate valencianist <a href="/wiki/United_Left_of_the_Valencian_Country" title="United Left of the Valencian Country">United Left of the Valencian Country</a>)</li>
</ul>
<p><a href="/wiki/Valencian" title="Valencian">Valencian</a> (a southern dialect of the <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan language</a>) is spoken alongside Spanish in around two thirds of the territory of the <a href="/wiki/Valencian_Community" title="Valencian Community">Valencian Community</a> and in most of the more densely populated coastal areas. It is absent in one third of the territory (mostly inland and in the far south) where only Spanish is spoken. Furthermore, in the last 2 centuries Spanish has become the dominant language in the two main cities of <a href="/wiki/Alicante" title="Alicante">Alicante</a> and <a href="/wiki/Valencia" title="Valencia">Valencia</a>. Nationalist sentiment is not widespread and most of the population consider themselves as much Valencian as Spanish. It should also be highlighted that the agenda of <a href="/wiki/Coalici%C3%B3_Comprom%C3%ADs" title="Coalició Compromís">the Compromís coalition</a> focuses on fighting corruption which has affected the region during the decades of Partido Popular government and a significant portion of their voters do not identify with Valencian nationalism. Indeed, Compromís have significantly reduced their nationalist discourse in order to gain wider appeal among Valencian voters and have been often accused of camouflaging their ideology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>The nationalist sentiment is not significantly higher in any province (electoral results show that just about 8% of the votes in Castellon, the closest province to Catalonia, are nationalist, higher in the provinces of Valencia with 10.43% and Alicante with 9.06%, according to municipal elections held in May 2007).</p>
<p>Notwithstanding, their electoral stronghold yielding most favourable results is an area split in two provinces: the southernmost end of the Valencia province and the northernmost end of Alicante province. The fact that this area is split between two provinces reduces relative percentages in both provinces.</p>
<p>It is in the local elections that the nationalists obtain their best results; thus they hold several town councils and significant representation - mostly in the areas mentioned above. Conversely, it is in the general elections to the Spanish Parliament where they score worst (approximately 2% of the votes). In the regional elections to the Autonomous Parliament, the main nationalist party <a href="/wiki/Valencian_Nationalist_Bloc" title="Valencian Nationalist Bloc">Valencian Nationalist Bloc</a> (BNV) usually gets around 4% of the votes, not having yet achieved the 5% threshold which grants representation in the regional Parliament.</p>
<p>There are territories in the Valencian autonomous community which are solely Spanish-speaking areas, where Valencian either was never spoken (roughly the inner 1/3 of the territory) or was historically sparsely spoken and finally disappeared (the southernmost part of the autonomous community, around the city of <a href="/wiki/Orihuela" title="Orihuela">Orihuela</a>). These territories comprise approximately 25% of the whole autonomous community. Since Valencian nationalism is primarily built around the <a href="/wiki/Valencian" title="Valencian">Valencian</a> language, this political option is virtually non-existent in these areas.</p>
<p>In contrast to the <a href="/wiki/Valencian_Union" title="Valencian Union">Valencian Union</a>, the BNV and its forebears favour cooperation and ties with the other Catalan speaking territories and greater autonomy - if not independence itself - from Spain, in form of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pa%C3%AFsos_Catalans" title="Països Catalans">Països Catalans</a></i>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Valencian_Left" title="Valencian Left">Esquerra Valenciana</a> is a party "of national, republican and transforming left of the Valencian Country; that fights for the political sovereignty and defends the free confederation of this territory with Catalonia and the Balearic Islands". It has not so far achieved electoral representation of any kind.</p>
<p>Its <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Autonomy" title="Statute of Autonomy">Statute of Autonomy</a> defines this region as a <a href="/wiki/Nationality" title="Nationality">nationality</a>.</p>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Politics_of_Valencia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Politics of Valencia (page does not exist)">Politics of Valencia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Valencian_Left" title="Valencian Left">Valencian Left</a>, <a href="/wiki/Valencian_Nationalist_Bloc" title="Valencian Nationalist Bloc">Valencian Nationalist Bloc</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Blaverism" title="Blaverism">Blaverism</a></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regionalism">Regionalism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Regionalism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In most of these following regions people do not sense a conflict between Spanish nationality and their own claimed national or regional identity.</p>
<p>There are two main political streams in regionalism:<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Nationalism-Regionalism, which supports the definition of the region as a nationality or nation but usually within Spain, and Regionalism, which originally supported the creation of an autonomous community for its region, and now acts as a promoter of its region but within Spain and respecting the current status of autonomous community, as in the <a href="/wiki/Regionalist_Party_of_Cantabria" title="Regionalist Party of Cantabria">Regionalist Party of Cantabria</a>, that currently rules Cantabria supported by the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Socialist_Workers%27_Party" title="Spanish Socialist Workers' Party">Spanish Socialist Workers' Party</a> (PSOE)). Some of these regionalist parties are associated with <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Party_(Spain)" title="People's Party (Spain)">People's Party</a> in its region or acting as its substitute or branch, as in the <a href="/wiki/Navarrese_People%27s_Union" title="Navarrese People's Union">Navarrese People's Union</a> (UPN)), (see <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Federation_of_Regionalist_Parties&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Federation of Regionalist Parties (page does not exist)">Federation of Regionalist Parties</a>). The following regions have belonged to different kingdoms, realms, states or regions for a time, and their population regularly consider themselves differently mostly depending on the part of the region. Some of these want to be identified with their own <a href="#Regionalism">regional identity</a>, such as Navarre, Cantabria or Valencia.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Castile">Castile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Castile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Castile_and_Le%C3%B3n" title="Castile and León">Castile and León</a>, <a href="/wiki/Castilla-La_Mancha" title="Castilla-La Mancha">Castilla-La Mancha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Castilians" title="Castilians">Castilians</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Castilian_Spanish" title="Castilian Spanish">Castilian Spanish</a></div>

<p>Regionalists and nationalists in Castile (such as <a href="/wiki/Commoners%27_Land" title="Commoners' Land">Commoners' Land</a>) usually want to unify the <a href="/wiki/Castile_(historical_region)" title="Castile (historical region)">traditional provinces</a> mentioned in the Castilian Federal Pact signed by the Partido Republicano Federal in 1869, and that would include the modern communities of <a href="/wiki/Castile_and_Le%C3%B3n" title="Castile and León">Castile and León</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cantabria" title="Cantabria">Cantabria</a>, <a href="/wiki/La_Rioja_(Spain)" title="La Rioja (Spain)">La Rioja</a>, <a href="/wiki/Castilla-La_Mancha" title="Castilla-La Mancha">Castilla-La Mancha</a> and <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, and sometimes even some areas in the provinces of Valencia, Alicante and Murcia (since Commoners' Land makes no mention of those once Castilian possessions in its ideological bases).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The territory claimed by Castilian regionalists or nationalists contains both areas from the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile" title="Kingdom of Castile">Kingdom of Castile</a> (both <a href="/wiki/Old_Castile" title="Old Castile">Old Castile</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Castile_(Spain)" title="New Castile (Spain)">New Castile</a>) and areas from the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="Kingdom of León">Kingdom of León</a>. Their claims are not usually based on the territory of the historical <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">Crown of Castile</a>, as it included the Basque or the Galician nations, they just hold a claim over the provinces that can be identified with Castilian identity according to them. They don't usually hold any claim over <a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">Andalusia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Extremadura" title="Extremadura">Extremadura</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Murcia" title="Murcia">Murcia</a>.</p>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Castilian_nationalism" title="Castilian nationalism">Castilian nationalism</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Le.C3.B3n">León</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: León">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Leonese_dialect" title="Leonese dialect">Leonese dialect</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leonese_people" title="Leonese people">Leonese people</a></div>

<p>Regionalists and nationalists from León fight for an independent autonomous community for the <i>Leonese Country</i>, the Spanish provinces of <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="Province of León">León</a>, <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Zamora" title="Province of Zamora">Zamora</a> and <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Salamanca" title="Province of Salamanca">Salamanca</a>.<br/>
Nationalists from León claims too for the Unity, Selfgovernment and Officiality for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leonese_language" title="Leonese language">Leonese language</a> in the Bragança District in Portugal and other irredent territories like Valdeorras in Galicia or other ones in the border with Castile.<br/>
Leonesism is the third political force in this territory after the autonomous electionships (2007), with more than 300 city councillors in the provinces of León, Zamora and Salamanca. Leonesist party UPL (<a href="/wiki/Leonese_People%27s_Union" title="Leonese People's Union">Leonese People's Union</a>), the biggest one, reached three seats in the Leonese City Council, where they govern with the PSOE; one deputy in the Diputación de León (provincial council) and two autonomical seats in the Castile and León's Parliament. There is also a small independentist movement led by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=AGORA_Pa%C3%ADs_Llion%C3%A9s&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="AGORA País Llionés (page does not exist)">AGORA País Llionés</a> and <a href="/wiki/Conceyu_Xoven" title="Conceyu Xoven">Conceyu Xoven</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cantabria">Cantabria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Cantabria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cantabrian_L%C3%A1baru_Flag.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="442" data-file-width="662" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Cantabrian_L%C3%A1baru_Flag.svg/220px-Cantabrian_L%C3%A1baru_Flag.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Cantabrian_L%C3%A1baru_Flag.svg/330px-Cantabrian_L%C3%A1baru_Flag.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Cantabrian_L%C3%A1baru_Flag.svg/440px-Cantabrian_L%C3%A1baru_Flag.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cantabrian_L%C3%A1baru_Flag.svg" title="Aumentar"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Cantabrian_labarum" title="Cantabrian labarum">Cantabrian labarum</a>, unofficial people's flag of Cantabria without necessarily political connotations.</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Cantabrian_dialect" title="Cantabrian dialect">Cantabrian dialect</a></div>
<ul>
<li>(Regionalism: 30.00% <a href="/wiki/Regionalist_Party_of_Cantabria" title="Regionalist Party of Cantabria">PRC</a>, main governing party in coalition with the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Socialist_Workers%27_Party" title="Spanish Socialist Workers' Party">Spanish Socialist Workers' Party</a>)</li>
</ul>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Cantabria" title="Duchy of Cantabria">Duchy of Cantabria</a> founded the Kingdom of Asturias and later the region formed part of the Kingdom of Castile, being known outside the territory as <i>La Montaña</i> ("The Mountain").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bar_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-bar-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-es_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-es-21">[21]</a></sup> However Cantabria kept its particular culture and economy due to its geographic peculiarities and isolation from Castile, relating more naturally to the Northern peoples of <a href="/wiki/Asturias" title="Asturias">Asturias</a> and <a href="/wiki/Biscay" title="Biscay">Biscay</a>. Note that the eastern coast (<a href="/wiki/Castro_Urdiales" title="Castro Urdiales">Castro Urdiales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laredo,_Cantabria" title="Laredo, Cantabria">Laredo</a>) is a residential area for Basques of Biscay. The pre-Roman, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Visigoths" title="Visigoths">Visigothic</a> name of <i>Cantabria</i> (after the <a href="/wiki/Cantabri" title="Cantabri">Cantabri</a>) was officially established for the first <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Province_of_Cantabria_in_1778&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Province of Cantabria in 1778 (page does not exist)">province of Cantabria in 1778</a> instead of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Exonim&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Exonim (page does not exist)">exonim</a> <i>La Montaña</i> or the subsequent name imposed by the central government in 1833 <i>province of Santander</i>. Its Statute of Autonomy could not be enacted during the <a href="/wiki/Second_Spanish_Republic" title="Second Spanish Republic">Second Spanish Republic</a> (1931–39) because of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War" title="Spanish Civil War">Spanish Civil War</a>. In its current Statute of Autonomy, Cantabria is named a 'Historic Community'.</p>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Cantabrian_Nationalist_Council" title="Cantabrian Nationalist Council">Cantabrian Nationalist Council</a> and <a href="/wiki/Regionalist_Party_of_Cantabria" title="Regionalist Party of Cantabria">Regionalist Party of Cantabria</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Navarre">Navarre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Navarre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Vascuenceren_Legea_eta_2001eko_euskaldun_kopuruak_udalerrika.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="560" height="214" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Vascuenceren_Legea_eta_2001eko_euskaldun_kopuruak_udalerrika.PNG/200px-Vascuenceren_Legea_eta_2001eko_euskaldun_kopuruak_udalerrika.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Vascuenceren_Legea_eta_2001eko_euskaldun_kopuruak_udalerrika.PNG/300px-Vascuenceren_Legea_eta_2001eko_euskaldun_kopuruak_udalerrika.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Vascuenceren_Legea_eta_2001eko_euskaldun_kopuruak_udalerrika.PNG/400px-Vascuenceren_Legea_eta_2001eko_euskaldun_kopuruak_udalerrika.PNG 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Vascuenceren_Legea_eta_2001eko_euskaldun_kopuruak_udalerrika.PNG" title="Aumentar"></a></div>
Distribution of Basque speaking people in Navarre 2001 and the zones where the basque language is official</div>
</div>

<ul>
<li>(Basque nationalism: 30.08% <a href="/wiki/Geroa_Bai" title="Geroa Bai">Geroa Bai</a> + <a href="/wiki/EH_Bildu" title="EH Bildu">EH Bildu</a> "Regionalism": 27.44% <a href="/wiki/Navarrese_People%27s_Union" title="Navarrese People's Union">UPN</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>)</li>
</ul>
<p>People from <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">Navarre</a> may feel to be either Basque or Spanish, and their culture is more akin to either Aragon or La Rioja in the southern and eastern parts, but in the northern part they identify mostly as <a href="/wiki/Basques" title="Basques">Basques</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Basque_language" title="Basque language">Basque language</a> is spoken. In historical times Basque was also the local language in several areas of central Navarre where it is no longer spoken natively, with a general south-to-north movement of the Basque-Romance <a href="/wiki/Isogloss" title="Isogloss">isogloss</a>. Basque is not known to have ever been spoken in southern Navarre, though.</p>
<p>As stated by the Basque Statute of Autonomy, if approved by the Navarrese Parliament and popular referendum by majority, Navarre can join the autonomous community of the Basque Country at any time when its government and population so desires; no further action is required. Navarre is not an Autonomous Community <i>de jure</i> (although it is <i>de facto</i>) because a Statute of Autonomy was not made or approved by popular referendum (as happened in each Autonomous Community). Instead, it is ruled by a document called "Amejoramiento del Fuero" (in Spanish) or "Foru hobekuntza" (in Basque) (Improvement of the <a href="/wiki/Fuero" title="Fuero">Fuero</a>) and the region is considered a "Foral Community".</p>
<p>According to the <i>Ley Foral del Vascuence</i> ("<a href="/wiki/Fuero" title="Fuero">Foral</a> Law regarding Basque Language") of the Navarrese Parliament is divided in three linguistical areas: Basque speaking area (<i>Zona Vascófona</i>); Spanish speaking area with some facilities to the Basque speakers (<i>Zona Mixta</i>), and Spanish monolingual speaking area (<i>Zona No</i> Vascófona<i>).</i></p>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Nafarroa_Bai" title="Nafarroa Bai">Nafarroa Bai</a> and <a href="/wiki/Navarrese_People%27s_Union" title="Navarrese People's Union">Navarrese People's Union</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Valencia_2">Valencia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Valencia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Blaverism" title="Blaverism">Blaverism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Valencian_Community" title="Valencian Community">Valencian Community</a></div>
<p>Valencian regionalism marked with <a href="/wiki/Anti-Catalanism" title="Anti-Catalanism">anti-Catalan sentiment</a> is also called valencianism or <a href="/wiki/Blaverism" title="Blaverism">blaverism</a> (named after the fringe which distinguishes the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_Valencian_Community" title="Flag of the Valencian Community">Valencian flag</a> from <a href="/wiki/Senyera" title="Senyera">other flags with a common origin</a>, particularly from the Catalan). Its adherents consider <a href="/wiki/Valencian" title="Valencian">Valencian</a> to be distinct from <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan</a> and called for autonomous community to be named "<a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Valencia" title="Kingdom of Valencia">Kingdom of Valencia</a>", as opposed to the term <i>País Valencià</i> which may imply an identification with the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pa%C3%AFsos_Catalans" title="Països Catalans">Països Catalans</a></i> or Catalan Countries. Only a minor tendency within valencianism/blaverism proposed independence of the Kingdom of Valencia from both Catalonia and Spain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Balearic_Islands">Balearic Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Balearic Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>("Balearic" nationalism or regionalism: 23.29% <a href="/wiki/More_for_Majorca" title="More for Majorca">More for Majorca</a>, <a class="extiw" href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partit_Socialista_de_Menorca" title="es:Partit Socialista de Menorca">Més per Menorca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Proposta_per_les_Illes" title="Proposta per les Illes">Proposta per les Illes</a>)</li>
</ul>
<p><a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan</a> is the co-official language in the region. It is very used in rural zones and a little less in the capital and in places with a high density of tourists. Balearic Catalan has developed into various dialectal variants that take their names from the names of the islands ("mallorquí", "menorquí", "eivissenc" and "formenterenc"). Though there are more Catalan nationalistic sympathizers than in Valencia, Spanish nationalism is highly present on the islands and it is considered a traditionally right-winged territory.</p>

<p>There is a regionalist movement in the eastern part of <a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">Andalusia</a> (mainly <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Granada" title="Province of Granada">Granada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Almer%C3%ADa" title="Province of Almería">Almería</a> and <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Ja%C3%A9n_(Spain)" title="Province of Jaén (Spain)">Jaén</a>, but with also some support in <a href="/wiki/Province_of_M%C3%A1laga" title="Province of Málaga">Málaga</a>) which seeks to create an own <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_communities_of_Spain" title="Autonomous communities of Spain">autonomous community</a> separated from western Andalusia. Historically, <a href="/wiki/Emirate_of_Granada" title="Emirate of Granada">Granada</a> was the last Arab kingdom inside the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a>, and had its own administrative region until 1933 when <a href="/wiki/Blas_Infante" title="Blas Infante">Blas Infante</a> unified the Andalusian provinces. The <a href="/wiki/Platform_for_Eastern_Andalusia" title="Platform for Eastern Andalusia">Platform for Eastern Andalusia</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> has contributed to expand the movement. Among the reasons for the movement, the most important are economical, like benefiting from Spanish decentralization, as opposed to <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Sevilian</a> centralism, but also historical. The movement is not associated to a particular political thought,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> and there is not a particular political party aiming to create the new autonomous community.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rioja">Rioja</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Rioja">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The autonomous community of <a href="/wiki/La_Rioja_(Spain)" title="La Rioja (Spain)">La Rioja</a> has a regionalist political party, <a href="/wiki/Riojan_Party" title="Riojan Party">Riojan Party</a>, with 6% of the vote in 2007. There is a strong regionalist sentiment in the community according to surveys. The 90% of the people say feel only Riojan and Spaniard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Extremadura">Extremadura</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Extremadura">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Extremadurans" title="Extremadurans">Extremadurans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Extremaduran_language" title="Extremaduran language">Extremaduran language</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fala_language" title="Fala language">Fala language</a></div>
<ul>
<li>("Regionalism": 0.06% <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Extremadura_Unida&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Extremadura Unida (page does not exist)">EU</a>)</li>
</ul>
<p>This region <a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">was conquered</a> partly by the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile" title="Kingdom of Castile">Kingdom of Castile</a>, partly by the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="Kingdom of León">Kingdom of León</a> and partly by the united <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">Crown of Castile</a>, but following the reconquest it received numerous settlers from Leon region, particularly in the north of the Caceres province. However, overall its culture is markedly southern and close to that of western Andalusia. Historically, <a href="/wiki/Extremadura" title="Extremadura">Extremadura</a> grew to become what it is now when some Extremaduran towns united to buy the right to vote in the <a href="/wiki/Cortes_Generales" title="Cortes Generales">Cortes</a> for 80.000 <a href="/wiki/Ducat" title="Ducat">ducats</a>.</p>
<p>The Spanish spoken in Extremadura is typically southern, but it also has its own distinctive features which are more prominent in the North-West area, where the local rural dialect is even considered a language of its own, the <a href="/wiki/Extremaduran_language" title="Extremaduran language">Extremaduran language</a>; it has common points with the <a href="/wiki/Astur-Leonese_languages" title="Astur-Leonese languages">Astur-Leonese</a> linguistic group.</p>
<p>There are a few border areas where varieties close to <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a> are spoken, for example near <a href="/wiki/Olivenza" title="Olivenza">Olivenza</a>, town over which <a href="/wiki/Portugal%E2%80%93Spain_relations#Later_disputes" title="Portugal–Spain relations">the Portuguese Republic holds a claim</a>.</p>
<p>Regionalist movements also exist here.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="La_Mancha">La Mancha</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: La Mancha">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bandera_del_regionalismo_manchego.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="416" data-file-width="624" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Bandera_del_regionalismo_manchego.svg/220px-Bandera_del_regionalismo_manchego.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Bandera_del_regionalismo_manchego.svg/330px-Bandera_del_regionalismo_manchego.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Bandera_del_regionalismo_manchego.svg/440px-Bandera_del_regionalismo_manchego.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>

<ul>

</ul>
<p>Mancheguian regionalism proposes that La Mancha is a region with its own identity, in the territories of the four provinces; <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Albacete" title="Province of Albacete">Albacete</a>, <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Ciudad_Real" title="Province of Ciudad Real">Ciudad Real</a>, <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Cuenca" title="Province of Cuenca">Cuenca</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Toledo" title="Province of Toledo">Toledo</a>. It has his its origins <i>Mancheguismo</i> that opposed the pan-Castilian thesis manifested foremost in Castilian nationalism.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Murcia">Murcia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Murcia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Murcian_Spanish" title="Murcian Spanish">Murcian Spanish</a></div>
<ul>
<li>(&gt;0%)</li>
</ul>
<p>This Mediterranean region belonged to the <a href="/wiki/Taifa" title="Taifa">Taifa kingdoms</a> of <a href="/wiki/Al-Andalus" title="Al-Andalus">Al-Andalus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Aragon" title="Crown of Aragon">Aragon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile" title="Kingdom of Castile">Castile</a>; therefore, it shares many similarities with Andalusia, Valencia - due to relatively recent immigration a dialect of Valencian-Catalan is spoken among some of the 697 inhabitants (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=INE_Espa%C3%B1a&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="INE España (page does not exist)">INE</a> 2006) of <a href="/wiki/Carche" title="Carche">Carche</a>- and Castilla-La Mancha.</p>
<p>There have been and there are some regionalist movements too. Their goal is to restore the traditional region of Murcia (including <a href="/wiki/Albacete" title="Albacete">Albacete</a> and maybe <a href="/wiki/Almer%C3%ADa" title="Almería">Almería</a>, and to create the province of <a href="/wiki/Cartagena,_Spain" title="Cartagena, Spain">Cartagena</a>).</p>
<p>The haven of Cartagena declared itself an independent <a href="/wiki/Canton_(country_subdivision)" title="Canton (country subdivision)">canton</a> in 1868.</p>
<p>Murcian and Manchegue identity are related by historical links.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ceuta_and_Melilla">Ceuta and Melilla</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Ceuta and Melilla">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Plazas_de_soberan%C3%ADa" title="Plazas de soberanía">Plazas de soberanía</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ceuta" title="Ceuta">Ceuta</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Melilla" title="Melilla">Melilla</a></div>
<p>There are two identities in these African cities. The Spanish-speaking Christians feel similar to Andalusians, a minority of Christians (around 25% in Ceuta) also having Catalan roots, but Ceuta also has a bit of <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> essence and Melilla was in close contact with the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> in the 19th century.</p>
<p>The bilingual Muslims speak Arabic in Ceuta and Berber in Melilla, besides Spanish and have familiar, commercial and cultural relations with neighbour Morocco. Nevertheless, they generally maintain their political allegiance to Spain, despite the Moroccan claim on the two cities. This Berber language is used sometimes between the Spanish People of the city (Muslims, Christians, Jews, others), specially of Melilla like the <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">Franco-Moroccan language</a>. (<i>lingua franca</i>), many words are adapted by daily trade with Morocco and <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a> during the <a href="/wiki/French_Algeria" title="French Algeria">French</a> colonial period from 1830 to 1962.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sephardi_Jews" title="Sephardi Jews">Sephardic</a> minorities evidently feel more strongly Spanish and many have emigrated or changed their <i>work home</i> as business headquarters to other towns in Southern Spain, especially <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1laga" title="Málaga">Málaga</a>. Nevertheless, they too have strong cultural ties with Morocco. Also there is an Indostai (<a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asian</a>) minority from two centuries when the Spaniards, Portuguese and French had colonies in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conflicts_with_.22nationality.22_and_.22nation.22_and_related_controversy_in_Spain">Conflicts with "nationality" and "nation" and related controversy in Spain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title='Edit section: Conflicts with "nationality" and "nation" and related controversy in Spain'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nacionalidadesespa%C3%B1a.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="950" data-file-width="1250" height="228" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/9b/Nacionalidadesespa%C3%B1a.png/300px-Nacionalidadesespa%C3%B1a.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/9b/Nacionalidadesespa%C3%B1a.png/450px-Nacionalidadesespa%C3%B1a.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/9b/Nacionalidadesespa%C3%B1a.png/600px-Nacionalidadesespa%C3%B1a.png 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nacionalidadesespa%C3%B1a.png" title="Aumentar"></a></div>
This map shows the regions in whose Statutes of Autonomy it is stated that they are <i>nationalities</i>.<br/>
The rest of the autonomous communities (Asturias, Cantabria, Castile and León, La Rioja, Navarre, Madrid, Extremadura, Castile La Mancha, Murcia, and Balearic Islands) are defined as <i>regions</i>.<br/>
Aragon, Catalonia, the Balearic Islands and Valencia were part of the same state-kingdom under the Crown of Aragon before the Spanish unification.</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li>The two terms do not have the same meaning, but are used indistinctively by nationalist parties when justifying their political plans within the Spanish Constitution (nationality is regarded as a euphemism of nation).</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Socialist_Workers%27_Party" title="Spanish Socialist Workers' Party">Spanish Socialist Workers' Party</a> government under <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Luis_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Zapatero" title="José Luis Rodríguez Zapatero">José Luis Rodríguez Zapatero</a> has promoted the concept of Spain as a "<a href="/wiki/Plurinationalism" title="Plurinationalism">Nation of nations</a>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> to integrate nationalist claims within Spain, despite the ambiguous statement in the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Constitution_of_1978" title="Spanish Constitution of 1978">Spanish Constitution of 1978</a>. It is non-constitutional for a <i>region</i> or <i>nationality</i> to declare itself a <i>nation</i> while inside the Spanish Nation.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>Apparently, it is stated that an <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_communities_of_Spain" title="Autonomous communities of Spain">autonomous community</a> of Spain can be either a <i>nationality</i> or a <i>region</i>, thus composing Spain of <i>nationalities</i> (Basque Country, Catalonia, Galicia, Andalusia, Aragon, Valencia, Balearic Islands and Canary Islands) and <i>regions</i> (the rest of Spain), but this is not explicitly specified anywhere in the constitution.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Constitution_of_1978" title="Spanish Constitution of 1978">Spanish Constitution of 1978</a> makes Spain a decentralized state, which functions almost as a federation of states in fact.</li>
<li>Even if an <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_communities_of_Spain" title="Autonomous communities of Spain">autonomous community</a> declares itself a <i>nationality</i> (and it does have the constitutional right to do that) that does not actually mean anything radically different from a <i>region</i>, since the degree of autonomy is determined both by historical identity, <i>i.e.</i>, whether they received a <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Autonomy" title="Statute of Autonomy">Statute of Autonomy</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Second_Spanish_Republic" title="Second Spanish Republic">Second Republic</a> or not, and by the will of the people. In the 1980s, the statutes of the "historic nationalities" were approved in a "fast track" before those of the rest of the regions.</li>
<li>The Spanish Government does not recognize the right of self-determination for the hypothetical underlying nationalities or nations and will not respect the outcome of an eventual regional referendum regarding the subject of self-determination or independence. However, the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Parliament" title="Basque Parliament">Basque Parliament</a> voted for recognizing this right in its region.</li>
<li>The term <i>nationality</i> refers only to the "autonomous community", and not to its citizens. That is, an autonomous community can be a <i>nationality</i>, but that does not imply that their citizens (also) have the nationality of that community, but only Spanish nationality. There is only a Spanish citizenship officially recognized.</li>
<li><i>Nationalities</i> and hypothetical <i>nations</i> in Spain are not always based on ethnic criteria (as in the case of Basque Country, Catalonia and Galicia) but on <u>historical</u>, <u>linguistic</u> and <u>cultural</u> facts which any person in those regions can assume and become identified with, regardless of his origin, family homeland or the fact that his ancestors belonged to different nationalities.</li>
<li>Modern "peripheral" (opposed to "central" nationalism) nationalist movements in Spain (such as Basque nationalism, Catalan nationalism, Galician nationalism, Canarian nationalism, etc.) do not regard their "nations" as superior or better in any sense than any other one (although the <a href="/wiki/Sabino_Arana" title="Sabino Arana">founder of Basque nationalism</a> thought so), just as distinct from the Spanish one.</li>
</ul>
<p>This is further complicated by the fact that while in most countries nationalism at a local level equals separatism, in Spain, nationalists are not necessarily separatists (although admittedly many are). As an example, there are clearly defined nationalist parties that support separation from the Spanish state, like the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Left_of_Catalonia" title="Republican Left of Catalonia">Republican Left of Catalonia</a>. But, there are nationalist parties that purposefully do not state clearly their position on independence (as to maximize electoral success) and sway repeatedly between greater descentralization of the Spanish state and outright separation, like <a href="/wiki/Convergence_and_Union" title="Convergence and Union">Convergence and Union</a>, <a href="/wiki/Basque_Nationalist_Party" title="Basque Nationalist Party">Basque Nationalist Party</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Galician_Nationalist_Bloc" title="Galician Nationalist Bloc">Galician Nationalist Bloc</a>.</p>
