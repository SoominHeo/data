<p><a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> uses the system of <a href="/wiki/Primogeniture#male-preference_cognatic_primogeniture" title="Primogeniture">male-preference cognatic primogeniture</a>. <a href="/wiki/Dynasty#dynasts" title="Dynasty">Dynasts</a> who marry against the express prohibition of the king and the <i><a href="/wiki/Cortes_Generales" title="Cortes Generales">Cortes</a></i> are excluded from the succession together with their descendants. A prohibition expressed only by the King or only by the <i>Cortes</i> will have no consequences to the succession. Disputes about the succession are to be settled by legislation.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Succession_uncertainties">Succession uncertainties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Line_of_succession_to_the_Spanish_throne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Succession uncertainties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Coat_of_Arms_of_Spanish_Monarch.svg"><img alt="Coat of Arms of Spanish Monarch.svg" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="949" data-file-width="630" height="331" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Coat_of_Arms_of_Spanish_Monarch.svg/220px-Coat_of_Arms_of_Spanish_Monarch.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Coat_of_Arms_of_Spanish_Monarch.svg/330px-Coat_of_Arms_of_Spanish_Monarch.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Coat_of_Arms_of_Spanish_Monarch.svg/440px-Coat_of_Arms_of_Spanish_Monarch.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Coat_of_Arms_of_Spanish_Monarch.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Section 1 of Article 57 of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Constitution_of_1978" title="Spanish Constitution of 1978">Spanish Constitution</a> provides that "The Crown of Spain shall be inherited by the successors of H. M. Juan Carlos I de Borbón."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> To date, the Spanish government has not seen fit to clarify whether this provision includes anyone beyond the descendants of King Juan Carlos: "Successors" may not be presumed to be <a href="/wiki/Synonym" title="Synonym">synonymous</a> with "descendants".</p>
<p>King Juan Carlos's two sisters renounced their rights of succession, but those renunciations took place before the adoption of the Constitution and were not ratified by the <i>Cortes</i> as required by Article 57.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-badts_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-badts-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enache_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-enache-3">[3]</a></sup> The rights of earlier generations are similarly clouded by numerous renunciations and unapproved marriages which may or may not exclude the individuals involved from the throne.</p>
<p>Article 57 further provides that "<a href="/wiki/Abdication" title="Abdication">Abdications</a> and renunciations and any doubt in fact or in law that may arise in connection with the succession to the Crown shall be settled by an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Organic_law_(Spain)" title="Organic law (Spain)">organic act</a>." Article 57 further states that "Should all the lines designated by law become extinct, the <i><a href="/wiki/Cortes_Generales" title="Cortes Generales">Cortes Generales</a></i> shall provide for succession to the Crown in the manner most suitable for the interests of Spain."</p>
<p>Unless and until an organic act clarifies the rights of other members of the King's family, it is unknown who, if anyone, follows Infanta Cristina's descendants in the line of succession.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Possible_interpretations">Possible interpretations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Line_of_succession_to_the_Spanish_throne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Possible interpretations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>If the word "successors" is deemed to mean "descendants", the line ends with Irene Urdangarín y de Borbón.
<ul>
<li>If not and if renunciations made by King Juan Carlos's sisters are not deemed valid, <a href="/wiki/Infanta_Cristina_of_Spain" title="Infanta Cristina of Spain">Infanta Cristina</a>'s descendants are followed by the <a href="/wiki/Infanta_Pilar,_Duchess_of_Badajoz" title="Infanta Pilar, Duchess of Badajoz">Duchess of Badajoz</a> and her descendants and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Infanta_Margarita,_2nd_Duchess_of_Hernani" title="Infanta Margarita, 2nd Duchess of Hernani">Duchess of Soria</a> and her descendants.</li>
<li>If not and if renunciations made by King Juan Carlos's sisters are deemed valid, <a href="/wiki/Infante_Carlos,_Duke_of_Calabria" title="Infante Carlos, Duke of Calabria">Infante Carlos, Duke of Calabria</a>'s heirs follow Infanta Cristina's descendants in the line of succession.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Planned_absolute_primogeniture">Planned absolute primogeniture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Line_of_succession_to_the_Spanish_throne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Planned absolute primogeniture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In its 2004 election manifesto, the victorious <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Socialist_Workers%27_Party" title="Spanish Socialist Workers' Party">Spanish Socialist Workers' Party</a> (PSOE) included plans to adopt <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Absolute_primogeniture" title="Absolute primogeniture">absolute primogeniture</a>, a proposal which was supported by the leader of the main opposition party, the conservative <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Party_(Spain)" title="People's Party (Spain)">People's Party</a>. It was initially thought that the change would only apply to future generations but with all the major political parties in agreement that the system of male-preference primogeniture conflicts with the constitutionally established principle of gender equality, it was planned that the law would be changed before the then-Princess of Asturias bore a son, thereby demoting Infanta Leonor to second in line. The subsequent announcement, in 2006, that the Princess was pregnant with a second daughter, however, removed any immediate urgency in the passage of the necessary legislation.</p>
