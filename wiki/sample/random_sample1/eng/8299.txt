<p><b>Rail transport in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a></b> operates on four <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rail_gauge" title="Rail gauge">rail gauges</a> and services are operated by a variety of private and public operators. The total route length in 2012 was 16,026 km (10,182 km electrified).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Most railways are operated by <a href="/wiki/Renfe_Operadora" title="Renfe Operadora">Renfe Operadora</a>; metre and narrow-gauge lines are operated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FEVE" title="FEVE">FEVE</a> and other carriers in individual autonomous communities. It is proposed and planned to build or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gauge_conversion" title="Gauge conversion">convert</a> more lines to standard gauge,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> including some <a href="/wiki/Dual_gauge" title="Dual gauge">dual gauging</a> of broad-gauge lines, especially where these lines link to France, including <a href="/wiki/Railway_platform" title="Railway platform">platforms</a> to be <a href="/wiki/Railway_platform_height" title="Railway platform height">heightened</a>.</p>
<p>Spain is a member of the <a href="/wiki/International_Union_of_Railways" title="International Union of Railways">International Union of Railways</a> (UIC). The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UIC_Country_Code" title="UIC Country Code">UIC Country Code</a> for Spain is 71.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rail_transport_in_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_rail_transport_in_Spain" title="History of rail transport in Spain">History of rail transport in Spain</a></div>
<p>The first railway line in the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a> was built in 1848 between <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> and <a href="/wiki/Matar%C3%B3" title="Mataró">Mataró</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1851 the <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>-<a href="/wiki/Aranjuez" title="Aranjuez">Aranjuez</a> line was opened. In 1852 the first narrow gauge line was built; in 1863 a line reached the Portuguese border. By 1864 the Madrid-Irun line had been opened, and the French border reached.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-ch-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1900 the first line to be electrified was the La Poveda-Madrid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1941 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RENFE" title="RENFE">RENFE</a> was created.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-ch-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The last steam locomotive was withdrawn in 1975, in 1986 the maximum speed on the railways was raised to 160 km/h, and in 1992 the Madrid-Seville high-speed line opened,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-ch-3">[3]</a></sup> beginning the process of building a nationwide high-speed network.</p>
<p>The current plans of the Spanish government plans to link all the <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Spain" title="Provinces of Spain">provincial capitals</a> with high-speed rail, with a total estimated length of over 9000 km<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> of high-speed railways for 2020.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operators">Operators</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rail_transport_in_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operators">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Renfe_Operadora" title="Renfe Operadora">Renfe Operadora</a> is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/State-owned" title="State-owned">state-owned</a> company which operates freight and passenger trains on the <span class="nowrap">1,668 mm</span> (<span class="nowrap">5 ft <span class="frac">5<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>21</sup>⁄<sub>32</sub></span> in</span>) "<a href="/wiki/Iberian_gauge" title="Iberian gauge">Iberian gauge</a>" and <span class="nowrap">1,435 mm</span> (<span class="nowrap">4 ft <span class="frac">8<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>2</sub></span> in</span>) <a href="/wiki/Standard_gauge" title="Standard gauge">standard gauge</a> networks of the Spanish nationalized infrastructure company <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adif" title="Adif">ADIF</a> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es"><i>Administrador de Infraestructuras Ferroviarias</i></span>). Both were formed from the break-up of the former national carrier <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RENFE" title="RENFE">RENFE</a> (Spanish: <i>Red Nacional de los Ferrocarriles Españoles</i>, "Spanish National Railway Network").</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FEVE" title="FEVE">FEVE</a> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es"><i>Ferrocarriles Españoles de Vía Estrecha</i></span>, "Narrow-Gauge Spanish Railways") is another state-owned Spanish railway company, which operates most of Spain's 1,250 km (780 mi) of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narrow_gauge_railway" title="Narrow gauge railway">narrow gauge rail</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EuskoTren" title="EuskoTren">EuskoTren</a> (<a href="/wiki/Basque_language" title="Basque language">Basque</a>: <span lang="eu"><i>Eusko Trenbideak</i></span>, Spanish: <i>Ferrocarriles Vascos</i>, "Basque Railways") operates trains on part of the narrow gauge railway network in the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)" title="Basque Country (autonomous community)">Basque Country</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ferrocarrils_de_la_Generalitat_de_Catalunya" title="Ferrocarrils de la Generalitat de Catalunya">FGC</a> (<a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan</a>: <span lang="ca"><i>Ferrocarrils de la Generalitat de Catalunya</i></span>, "Catalan Government Railways") operates several unconnected lines in <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>. It operates 140 km (87 mi) of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narrow_gauge_railway" title="Narrow gauge railway">narrow gauge</a>, 42 km (26 mi) of <a href="/wiki/Standard_gauge" title="Standard gauge">standard gauge</a>, and 89 km (55 mi) of <a href="/wiki/Iberian_gauge" title="Iberian gauge">Iberian gauge</a> routes, two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metre_gauge" title="Metre gauge">metre gauge</a> <a href="/wiki/Rack_railway" title="Rack railway">rack railways</a> and four <a href="/wiki/Funicular" title="Funicular">funicular railways</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ferrocarrils_de_la_Generalitat_Valenciana" title="Ferrocarrils de la Generalitat Valenciana">FGV</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valencian_language" title="Valencian language">Valencian</a>: <span lang="ca-valencia"><i>Ferrocarrils de la Generalitat Valenciana</i></span>, "Valencian Government Railways") operates several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metre_gauge" title="Metre gauge">metre gauge</a> lines in the <a href="/wiki/Valencian_Community" title="Valencian Community">Valencian Community</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ferrocarril_de_S%C3%B3ller" title="Ferrocarril de Sóller">FS</a> (Catalan: <i>Ferrocarril de Sóller</i>, "<a href="/wiki/S%C3%B3ller" title="Sóller">Sóller</a> Railways") operates an <a href="/wiki/Electrification" title="Electrification">electrified</a> <span class="nowrap">914 mm</span> (<span class="nowrap">3 ft</span>) narrow gauge railway on the Spanish island of <a href="/wiki/Majorca" title="Majorca">Majorca</a> between the towns of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palma_de_Mallorca" title="Palma de Mallorca">Palma</a> and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B3ller" title="Sóller">Sóller</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Serveis_Ferroviaris_de_Mallorca" title="Serveis Ferroviaris de Mallorca">SFM</a> (Catalan: <i>Serveis Ferroviaris de Mallorca</i>, "Majorcan Railway Servicies") operates the <span class="nowrap">1,000 mm</span> (<span class="nowrap">3 ft <span class="frac">3<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>3</sup>⁄<sub>8</sub></span> in</span>) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metre_gauge" title="Metre gauge">metre gauge</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway" title="Railway">railway</a> network on the <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> island of <a href="/wiki/Majorca" title="Majorca">Majorca</a>.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Acciona_Rail_Services&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Acciona Rail Services (page does not exist)">Acciona Rail Services</a>, a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Acciona" title="Acciona">Acciona</a>, operates a coal cargo line between <a href="/wiki/Asturias" title="Asturias">Asturias</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="Province of León">province of León</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/COMSA_Rail_Transport" title="COMSA Rail Transport">COMSA Rail Transport</a>, a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/COMSA" title="COMSA">COMSA</a>, operates a cargo line from the Port of <a href="/wiki/Gij%C3%B3n" title="Gijón">Gijón</a> to <a href="/wiki/Valladolid" title="Valladolid">Valladolid</a>.</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subsidies">Subsidies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rail_transport_in_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Subsidies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2004, the Spanish government adopted a new strategic plan for transportation through 2020 called the PEIT (Strategic Plan for Infrastructures and Transport). This detailed <a href="/wiki/Rail_subsidies" title="Rail subsidies">rail subsidies</a> of around €9.3 billion annually on average from 2005-2020. In 2010, it rolled out a two-year plan to invest an extra €11 billion each year for two years, as a part of a financial stimulus in response to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_recession" title="Great recession">global downturn</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2015, the federal budget for the railways was €5.1 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
