<p><br/>
Year <b>1417</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MCDXVII</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Friday" title="Common year starting on Friday">common year starting on Friday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1417&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93December">January–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1417&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: January–December">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_27" title="July 27">July 27</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Avignon_Pope_Benedict_XIII" title="Avignon Pope Benedict XIII">Avignon Pope Benedict XIII</a> is deposed, bringing to an end the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Western_Schism" title="Great Western Schism">Great Western Schism</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_12" title="August 12">August 12</a> – King <a href="/wiki/Henry_V_of_England" title="Henry V of England">Henry V of England</a> begins using English in correspondence (back to <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">England</a> from <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_France" title="Kingdom of France">France</a> whilst on campaign), marking the beginning of this king's continuous usage of English in prose, and the beginning of the restoration of English as an official language for the first time since the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norman_Conquest" title="Norman Conquest">Norman Conquest</a>, some 350 years earlier.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_11" title="November 11">November 11</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pope_Martin_V" title="Pope Martin V">Pope Martin V</a> succeeds <a href="/wiki/Pope_Gregory_XII" title="Pope Gregory XII">Pope Gregory XII</a> (who abdicated in 1415) as the 206th <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">pope</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Date_unknown">Date unknown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1417&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Date unknown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>The earliest extant description of <a href="/wiki/Tynwald_Day" title="Tynwald Day">Tynwald Day</a>; the annual meeting of the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Parliament" title="Parliament">parliament</a> (<a href="/wiki/Tynwald" title="Tynwald">Tynwald</a>) is written down in law.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>The use of street lighting is first recorded in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, England when Sir Henry Barton, the mayor, orders lanterns with lights to be hung out on the winter evenings between <a href="/wiki/Halloween" title="Halloween">Hallowtide</a> and <a href="/wiki/Candlemas" title="Candlemas">Candlemas</a>.</li>
</ul>
