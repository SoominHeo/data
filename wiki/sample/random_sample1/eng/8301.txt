<p>The <b>Spengler Cup</b> is an annual invitational ice hockey tournament held in <a href="/wiki/Davos" title="Davos">Davos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. First held in 1923, the Spengler Cup is often cited as the oldest invitational <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> tournament in the world. The event is hosted by the Swiss team <a href="/wiki/HC_Davos" title="HC Davos">HC Davos</a> and played each year in Davos, Switzerland, between <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> (December 25) and <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Day" title="New Year's Day">New Year's Day</a>. Currently, all games are held at <a href="/wiki/Vaillant_Arena" title="Vaillant Arena">Vaillant Arena</a>.</p>
<p>It was originally devised by Dr. Carl Spengler as a means to promote teams from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_language_in_Europe" title="German language in Europe">German-speaking Europe</a>, who might have suffered ostracism in the aftermath of <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. Eventually, the tournament grew well beyond expectations. Many of Europe's most prestigious clubs and national programs have appeared, including Soviet, Swedish, Finnish, Czechoslovak and German powerhouses.</p>
<p>Among non-European organizations, <a href="/wiki/Canada_men%27s_national_ice_hockey_team" title="Canada men's national ice hockey team">Team Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_ice_hockey_team" title="United States men's national ice hockey team">Team USA</a>, nationally ranked <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/College_hockey" title="College hockey">NCAA</a> schools, reigning <a href="/wiki/American_Hockey_League" title="American Hockey League">AHL</a> <a href="/wiki/Calder_Cup" title="Calder Cup">Calder Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/OHA_Senior_A_League_(1890%E2%80%931979)" title="OHA Senior A League (1890–1979)">Ontario Hockey Association</a> champions, and even <a href="/wiki/Japan_men%27s_national_ice_hockey_team" title="Japan men's national ice hockey team">Team Japan</a> (in 1971 as hosts of the then-upcoming <a href="/wiki/1972_Winter_Olympics" title="1972 Winter Olympics">Sapporo Winter Olympics</a>) have traveled to Davos through the years. The <a href="/wiki/2013_Spengler_Cup" title="2013 Spengler Cup">2013</a> tournament marked the return of a <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North American</a> league team, the AHL's <a href="/wiki/Rochester_Americans" title="Rochester Americans">Rochester Americans</a>, who also were the last such team to take part, in <a href="/wiki/1996_Spengler_Cup" title="1996 Spengler Cup">1996</a>.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2016_Spengler_Cup" title="2016 Spengler Cup">2016 tournament</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada_2016_Spengler_Cup_roster" title="Canada 2016 Spengler Cup roster">Team Canada</a> successfully defended their Spengler Cup championship from <a href="/wiki/2015_Spengler_Cup" title="2015 Spengler Cup">2015</a>, defeating <a href="/wiki/HC_Lugano" title="HC Lugano">HC Lugano</a> 5–2 in the final.</p>
<p>The Spengler Cup is broadcast on <a href="/wiki/Schweizer_Radio_und_Fernsehen" title="Schweizer Radio und Fernsehen">Schweizer Radio und Fernsehen</a> in Switzerland, on <a href="/wiki/Eurosport_2" title="Eurosport 2">Eurosport 2</a> in most of Europe, on <a href="/wiki/Match_TV" title="Match TV">Match TV</a> and <a href="/wiki/NTv2" title="NTv2">NTv2</a> in Russia, on <a href="/wiki/Sport1_(Eastern_Europe)" title="Sport1 (Eastern Europe)">Sport1</a> in the Czech Republic, Slovakia and Hungary, on <a href="/wiki/Sport_Klub" title="Sport Klub">Sport Klub</a> in Slovenia and on <a href="/wiki/The_Sports_Network" title="The Sports Network">TSN</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reseau_des_Sports" title="Reseau des Sports">RDS</a> in Canada.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spengler_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Spengler_Cup.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3072" data-file-width="2304" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Spengler_Cup.JPG/220px-Spengler_Cup.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Spengler_Cup.JPG/330px-Spengler_Cup.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Spengler_Cup.JPG/440px-Spengler_Cup.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The Cup was first awarded in 1923 to the <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University_Ice_Hockey_Club" title="Oxford University Ice Hockey Club">Oxford University Ice Hockey Club</a>, composed of <a href="/wiki/Canadians" title="Canadians">Canadian</a> students.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The tournament was then dominated by <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovak_Socialist_Republic" title="Czechoslovak Socialist Republic">Czechoslovak</a> and <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> teams between 1965 and 1983. In 1984 <a href="/wiki/Canada_men%27s_national_ice_hockey_team" title="Canada men's national ice hockey team">Team Canada</a> began participating and has since won the Cup fourteen times. Team Canada is predominantly made up of Canadians playing in Europe, as well as <a href="/wiki/American_Hockey_League" title="American Hockey League">American Hockey League</a> prospects. Notable free agents (like veteran goaltender <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Joseph" title="Curtis Joseph">Curtis Joseph</a>) or coaches without an <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">NHL</a> contract (like 2015's winning coach <a href="/wiki/Guy_Boucher" title="Guy Boucher">Guy Boucher</a>) have also used the Spengler Cup to keep their name around. During the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_NHL_lockout" title="2012–13 NHL lockout">2012 NHL lockout</a>, huge stars like <a href="/wiki/Joe_Thornton" title="Joe Thornton">Joe Thornton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rick_Nash" title="Rick Nash">Rick Nash</a> were already playing for HC Davos, so they played in the <a href="/wiki/2012_Spengler_Cup" title="2012 Spengler Cup">2012 Spengler Cup</a> for the host team.</p>
<p>From its inception until 1978, the tournament was played on an <a href="/wiki/Ice_rink" title="Ice rink">outdoor rink</a>. The outdoor rink still exists outside the indoor arena, and is one of the largest outdoor rinks in the world. Starting in 1978, all tournament games have been played indoors. <span class="citation-needed-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">In 1956, the championship trophy was given its classic and best-known look (pictured left), which stood until a new gold design was unveiled in 2010.</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Temporary_reinforcements">Temporary reinforcements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spengler_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Temporary reinforcements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A frequent point of contention among hockey observers discussing the Spengler Cup's relevance is the use of temporary reinforcements. Since the tournament is open to both clubs and national teams, a rule exists that allows club teams to hire up to four additional players (three skaters and one goaltender) for the duration of the competition. Prior to the <a href="/wiki/2010_Spengler_Cup" title="2010 Spengler Cup">2010</a> edition, six reinforcement players (five skaters and a goaltender) were allowed per team.</p>
<p>In practice, not all participants choose to take advantage of the rule to the same extent, and it works mostly to the host team's benefit. HC Davos, for whom the event is a significant source of income, tends to upgrade its roster with experienced talent in order to guarantee a competitive performance and maintain high interest from the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_people" title="Swiss people">Swiss audience</a>. Other teams don't have such incentives to reinforce their squad and will often be much less selective.</p>
