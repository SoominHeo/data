<p><b>Spokane Spiders</b> was an American <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">soccer</a> team based in <a href="/wiki/Spokane,_Washington" title="Spokane, Washington">Spokane, Washington</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. Founded in 2006, the team played in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USL_Premier_Development_League" title="USL Premier Development League">USL Premier Development League</a> (PDL), the fourth tier of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Soccer_Pyramid" title="American Soccer Pyramid">American Soccer Pyramid</a>, in the Northwest Division of the Western Conference. The franchise folded at the end of the 2010 season and left the league thereafter.</p>
<p>The team played its home games at <a href="/wiki/Joe_Albi_Stadium" title="Joe Albi Stadium">Joe Albi Stadium</a>. The team's colors were red, black and white.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spokane_Spiders&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Spokane Spiders entered the PDL in 2007, continuing the tradition of top-level minor league soccer in <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Washington" title="Eastern Washington">Eastern Washington</a> that began with <a href="/wiki/Spokane_Shadow" title="Spokane Shadow">Spokane Shadow</a> in the mid-1990s. The Spiders, under head coach <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dan_Philp&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dan Philp (page does not exist)">Dan Philp</a>, began quite strongly, beating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tacoma_Tide" title="Tacoma Tide">Tacoma Tide</a> 3–2 and <a href="/wiki/Cascade_Surge" title="Cascade Surge">Cascade Surge</a> 2–1 in their first competitive games. Spokane were generally strong at home for the duration of their freshman season, enjoying four more victories at <a href="/wiki/Spokane_Falls_Community_College" title="Spokane Falls Community College">Spokane Falls Community College</a>, but their form on the road was abysmal: they conceded 38 goals away from home while scoring just 7, and lost all of their 8 games on their travels. The nadir was a shocking 0–11 defeat to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abbotsford_Mariners" title="Abbotsford Mariners">Abbotsford Mariners</a> on the final day of the season, which left the Spiders in 5th place in the standings, 33 points behind divisional champions <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BYU_Cougars_soccer" title="BYU Cougars soccer">BYU Cougars</a>. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jake_Moug&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jake Moug (page does not exist)">Jake Moug</a> was by far Spokane's dominant player, scoring 11 of their 25 goals himself, while <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ben_Funkhouser&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ben Funkhouser (page does not exist)">Ben Funkhouser</a> contributed 4 assists.</p>
<p>Things went from bad to worse for Spokane in 2008. Despite the appointment of new head coach <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Abbas_Faridnia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Abbas Faridnia (page does not exist)">Abbas Faridnia</a>, the Spiders lost their first game of the campaign 3–0 at home to <a href="/wiki/Yakima_Reds" title="Yakima Reds">Yakima Reds</a>; their next game was an impressive 5–2 win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tacoma_Tide" title="Tacoma Tide">Tacoma Tide</a> on 'neutral territory' in Idaho which saw <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jay_Vela&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jay Vela (page does not exist)">Jay Vela</a> score a brace; unfortunately, this was the high point of the season. Spokane took just eight points the entire rest of the season – a 1–1 tie with <a href="/wiki/Cascade_Surge" title="Cascade Surge">Cascade Surge</a>, a battling 3–2 win over Yakima again, a 3–3 tie with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abbotsford_Mariners" title="Abbotsford Mariners">Abbotsford Mariners</a>, and a surprising 3–0 win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vancouver_Whitecaps_Residency" title="Vancouver Whitecaps Residency">Vancouver Whitecaps Residency</a> at home in their penultimate match of the season. Everything else was a struggle – Tacoma made up for their early season defeat with a 5–0 walloping at the end of May, Yakima put five past them in late June, and Cascade ended Spokane's season on a sour note with another 5-goal drubbing in the final game. Spokane were rooted to the bottom of the Northwest Division for much of the campaign, and finished three points adrift of Cascade in 8th place. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jay_Vela&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jay Vela (page does not exist)">Jay Vela</a> was Spokane's top scorer, with just three goals, although <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_Davidson_(soccer)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Scott Davidson (soccer) (page does not exist)">Scott Davidson</a> did tally four assists.</p>
<p><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jake_Moug&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jake Moug (page does not exist)">Jake Moug</a> took over as head coach for the 2009 season, but things did not improve for the Spiders, who finished rock bottom of the Northwest Division for the second straight year, with just three wins and twelve goals all season long. The high points included a 3–1 win over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seattle_Wolves" title="Seattle Wolves">Seattle Wolves</a> in early May in which the Spiders took control in the second half after the Wolves' <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Casey_Cunningham&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Casey Cunningham (page does not exist)">Casey Cunningham</a> was sent off, and the 3–2 win over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abbotsford_Mariners" title="Abbotsford Mariners">Abbotsford Mariners</a> in June in which the Spiders held off two spirited comebacks by the Canadians and scored a 70th-minute winner through substitute <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tye_Perdido&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tye Perdido (page does not exist)">Tye Perdido</a>, despite playing for over an hour with 10 men. Elsewhere, the Spokane defense was horribly leaky: they lost 5–0 and 6–1 to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitsap_Pumas" title="Kitsap Pumas">Kitsap Pumas</a>, 5–1 to <a href="/wiki/Cascade_Surge" title="Cascade Surge">Cascade Surge</a>, 6–0 to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vancouver_Whitecaps_Residency" title="Vancouver Whitecaps Residency">Vancouver Whitecaps Residency</a>, and conceded four goals on three other occasions. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Zack_Smith_(soccer)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Zack Smith (soccer) (page does not exist)">Zachary Smith</a> was the token top scorer of a dispiriting year with just 3 goals.</p>
<p>The team's last win was 5–4 slugfest over the Abbotsford Mariners on July 16, 2010. The following day, Sunday July 17, 2010, the Spokane Spiders lost their last game 1–5 to the Whitecaps Residency. John Prugh opening the scoring in the 24th minute. Vancouver went up 1–3 before going down to 10 men, but subsequently scored two additional late goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
