<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Lambert of Italy</h1>

<p><b>Lambert</b> (c. 880 – 15 October 898) was the <a href="/wiki/King_of_Italy" title="King of Italy">King of Italy</a> from 891, <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Holy Roman Emperor">Holy Roman Emperor</a>, co-ruling with his father from 892, and <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Spoleto" title="Duke of Spoleto">Duke of Spoleto and Camerino</a> (as Lambert II) from his father's death in 894. He was the son of <a href="/wiki/Guy_III_of_Spoleto" title="Guy III of Spoleto">Guy III of Spoleto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ageltrude" title="Ageltrude">Ageltrude</a>, born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Rufino" title="San Rufino">San Rufino</a>. He was the last ruler to issue a <a href="/wiki/Capitulary" title="Capitulary">capitulary</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carolingian" title="Carolingian">Carolingian</a> tradition.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Confronting_Arnulf">Confronting Arnulf</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lambert_of_Italy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Confronting Arnulf">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lambert was crowned King in May 891 at <a href="/wiki/Pavia" title="Pavia">Pavia</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and joint Emperor alongside his father on 30 April 892 at <a href="/wiki/Ravenna" title="Ravenna">Ravenna</a> by a reluctant <a href="/wiki/Pope_Formosus" title="Pope Formosus">Pope Formosus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He and his father signed a pact with the pontiff confirming the <a href="/wiki/Donation_of_Pepin" title="Donation of Pepin">Donation of Pepin</a> and subsequent Carolingian gifts to the papacy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 893, however, Formosus sent an embassy to <a href="/wiki/Regensburg" title="Regensburg">Regensburg</a> to request <a href="/wiki/Arnulf_of_Carinthia" title="Arnulf of Carinthia">Arnulf of Carinthia</a> liberate Italy and come to <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> to be crowned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Arnulf sent his son <a href="/wiki/Zwentibold" title="Zwentibold">Zwentibold</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavarian</a> army to join with <a href="/wiki/Berengar_I_of_Italy" title="Berengar I of Italy">Berengar of Friuli</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> They defeated Guy, but bribes, along with an outbreak of fever, saw him leave in the autumn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Arnulf then personally led an army across the <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">Alps</a> early in 894. He conquered all of the territory north of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Po_River" title="Po River">Po River</a>, but went no further before Guy died suddenly in late autumn. Lambert became sole king and emperor, as well as succeeded his father to the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Spoleto" title="Duchy of Spoleto">Duchy of Spoleto</a>. Still young though, he was left under the <a href="/wiki/Regent" title="Regent">regency</a> of his mother, a staunch anti-German. While Berengar occupied Pavia, Lambert and Ageltrude travelled to Rome to receive papal confirmation of his imperial title,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> but Pope <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Formosus" title="Formosus">Formosus</a> wanted instead to crown Arnulf and was imprisoned in the <a href="/wiki/Castel_Sant%27Angelo" title="Castel Sant'Angelo">Castel Sant'Angelo</a>.</p>
<p>Lambert was preoccupied in thwarting the attempts of both Arnulf of Carinthia and Berengar of Friuli to take Italy for themselves during his reign. Early on, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adalbert_II_of_Tuscany" title="Adalbert II of Tuscany">Adalbert II of Tuscany</a> rallied to his cause, menacing Berengar in Pavia. By January 895, Lambert could take up residence in the royal capital. In that same year, his cousin <a href="/wiki/Guy_IV_of_Spoleto" title="Guy IV of Spoleto">Guy IV</a> conquered the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Principality_of_Benevento" title="Principality of Benevento">Principality of Benevento</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantines</a>. Despite the urging of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fulk_(Archbishop_of_Reims)" title="Fulk (Archbishop of Reims)">Fulk of Rheims</a> on his behalf, Lambert found himself abandoned by the pope, who feared the increased power of the Spoletan house. In September, an embassy arrived in Regensburg beseeching Arnulf's aid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In October, Arnulf undertook his second campaign into Italy. He crossed the Alps quickly and took Pavia, but then he continued slowly. While Lambert refused to offer battle, Arnful was garnering support among the nobility of Tuscany. Even Adalbert joined him. Finding Rome locked against him and held by Ageltrude, he took the city by force on 21 February 896, freeing the pope.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Arnulf was there crowned King and Emperor by Formosus, who declared Lambert deposed. Arnulf marched on <a href="/wiki/Spoleto" title="Spoleto">Spoleto</a>, where Ageltrude had fled to Lambert, but he suffered a <a href="/wiki/Stroke" title="Stroke">stroke</a> and had to call off the campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> That same year, Formosus died, leaving Lambert once again in power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Renovatio_regni_Francorum"><i>Renovatio regni Francorum</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lambert_of_Italy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Renovatio regni Francorum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After Arnulf returned to Germany and until his death,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Lambert and his supporters, most powerful in the northeast and the centre of the peninsula, were in complete control of Italy. He retook Pavia and decapitated <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Maginulf,_Count_of_Milan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Maginulf, Count of Milan (page does not exist)">Maginulf, Count of Milan</a>, who had joined Arnulf. In October and November, he met Berengar outside of Pavia and the two reached an agreement whereby they parcelled the kingdom out between them, Berengar keeping the realm between the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adda_River" title="Adda River">Adda</a> and the Po and Lambert the rest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> They shared <a href="/wiki/Bergamo" title="Bergamo">Bergamo</a>. This was a confirmation of the <i>status quo</i> of 889. Lambert also pledged to marry Gisela, Berengar's daughter. It was this partitioning which caused the later chronicler <a href="/wiki/Liutprand_of_Cremona" title="Liutprand of Cremona">Liutprand of Cremona</a> to remark that the Italians always suffered under two monarchs.</p>
<p>In early 897, Lambert journeyed to Rome with Ageltrude and Guy to receive reconfirmation of his imperial title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The vengeful Lambert and Ageltrude also persuaded <a href="/wiki/Pope_Stephen_VI" title="Pope Stephen VI">Pope Stephen VI</a>, elected by their influence, to put the corpse of Formosus on trial for various crimes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The body, stripped of its papal robes and mutilated, was thrown into the river <a href="/wiki/Tiber" title="Tiber">Tiber</a> after the "<a href="/wiki/Cadaver_Synod" title="Cadaver Synod">Cadaver Synod</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In January 898, <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_IX" title="Pope John IX">Pope John IX</a> rehabilitated Formosus against their will. Lambert convened a diet at Ravenna in February. Seventy bishops met and confirmed the pact of 891, the invalidity of Arnulf's coronation, and the validity of Lambert's imperial title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> They legitimised the election of John IX. They also solved the Formosan question and confirmed his rehabilitation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Most significantly for Lambert, however, they reaffirmed the <i>Constitutio Romana</i> of <a href="/wiki/Lothair_I" title="Lothair I">Lothair I</a> (824), which required the imperial presence at papal elections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lambert hereafter governed with the church and continued the policy of his father of <i>renovatio regni Francorum</i>: renewal of the Frankish kingdom. He was able to issue <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capitularies" title="Capitularies">capitularies</a> in the Frankish fashion as his father had done. In fact, he was the last ruler to do so. In 898, he legislated against the exploitation of the services owed by <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arimanni" title="Arimanni">arimanni</a></i> to create <a href="/wiki/Benefice" title="Benefice">benefices</a> for <a href="/wiki/Vassal" title="Vassal">vassals</a>. The <i>Lex Romana Utinensis</i> was composed at his court.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle_of_Marengo">Battle of Marengo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lambert_of_Italy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Battle of Marengo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>However, Lambert still had Berengar of Friuli and the rebellious Adalbert of Tuscany to face.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In 898, the latter marched on Pavia. The emperor, who had been hunting near <a href="/wiki/Spinetta_Marengo" title="Spinetta Marengo">Marengo</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> south of <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>, was given word and surprised and defeated his rival at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Borgo_San_Donnino" title="Borgo San Donnino">Borgo San Donnino</a>, taking him prisoner to Pavia. On his return to Marengo however, he was killed, either by assassination (by Hugh, son of Maginulf), a theory about which Liutprand, our primary source, is reserved, or by falling from his horse.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> He was buried in <a href="/wiki/Piacenza" title="Piacenza">Piacenza</a>. Liutprand remembered him as an <i>elegans iuvenis</i> and <i>vir severus</i>: "an elegant youth and a stern man". His epitaph (in Latin <a href="/wiki/Elegiac_couplet" title="Elegiac couplet">elegiac couplets</a>) is:</p>

<tr>
<td>
<dl>
<dd><i>Sanguine præcipuō Francōrum germinis ortus</i></dd>
<dd><i>Lambertus fuit hīc Caesar in Urbe potēns</i></dd>
<dd><i>Alter erat Cōnstantīnus, Theodōsius alter</i></dd>
<dd><i>Et prīnceps pācis clārus amōre nimis</i></dd>
</dl>
</td>
<td>
<dl>
<dd>Born with the distinguished blood of the stock of the Franks,</dd>
<dd>Lambert was here Emperor, holding power in the City (of Rome);</dd>
<dd>He was another <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Constantine_I" title="Constantine I">Constantine</a>, another <a href="/wiki/Theodosius_I" title="Theodosius I">Theodosius</a>,</dd>
<dd>and a prince of peace, excessively renowned with love.</dd>
</dl>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<p>He was succeeded in Spoleto by <a href="/wiki/Guy_IV_of_Spoleto" title="Guy IV of Spoleto">Guy IV</a> while the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regnum_Italicum" title="Regnum Italicum">regnum Italicum</a></i> and the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperium_Romanum" title="Imperium Romanum">imperium Romanum</a></i> were thrown into chaos, contested by multiple candidates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Within days, Berengar had taken Pavia.</p>
