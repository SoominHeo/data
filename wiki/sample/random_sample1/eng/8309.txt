<p><b>Spoon</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band formed in <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a>. The band is composed of <a href="/wiki/Britt_Daniel" title="Britt Daniel">Britt Daniel</a> (vocals, guitar); <a href="/wiki/Jim_Eno" title="Jim Eno">Jim Eno</a> (drums); <a href="/wiki/Rob_Pope" title="Rob Pope">Rob Pope</a> (bass guitar, guitar, keyboards, backing vocals), Eric Harvey (keyboard, guitar, percussion, backing vocals), and Alex Fischel (keyboard, guitar).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Critics have described the band's musical style as <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Indie_pop" title="Indie pop">indie pop</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Art_rock" title="Art rock">art rock</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Experimental_rock" title="Experimental rock">experimental rock</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spoon_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The band was formed in late 1993 by the lead singer and guitarist <a href="/wiki/Britt_Daniel" title="Britt Daniel">Britt Daniel</a> and the drummer <a href="/wiki/Jim_Eno" title="Jim Eno">Jim Eno</a>, after the two met as members of <a href="/wiki/The_Alien_Beats" title="The Alien Beats">The Alien Beats</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The name Spoon was chosen to honor the 1970s German <a href="/wiki/Avant-garde" title="Avant-garde">avant-garde</a> band <a href="/wiki/Can_(band)" title="Can (band)">Can</a>, whose hit song "<a href="/wiki/Spoon_(Can_song)" title="Spoon (Can song)">Spoon</a>" was the theme song to the 1985 movie <i>Das Messer</i> aka <i>Jagged Edge</i> in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Eno describes Spoon's music as "psychedelic."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spoon_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Spoon's first recording was the vinyl release of the EP <i><a href="/wiki/Nefarious_(EP)" title="Nefarious (EP)">Nefarious</a></i> in May 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chronicle_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-chronicle-13">[13]</a></sup> In 1995 the band signed with <a href="/wiki/Matador_Records" title="Matador Records">Matador Records</a>, and within a year, Spoon released its first full-length <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LP_album" title="LP album">LP</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Telephono" title="Telephono">Telephono</a></i> in 1996. The album was met with mixed reviews, with critics often comparing their sound with the likes of <a href="/wiki/Pavement_(band)" title="Pavement (band)">Pavement</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pixies" title="Pixies">Pixies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wire_(band)" title="Wire (band)">Wire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> But comparisons aside, <i>Telephono</i> showed signs of a band slipping free of its influences, mixing post-punk with a blend of pop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Less than a year later, Spoon released its second <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Soft_Effects" title="Soft Effects">Soft Effects</a></i>, which served as a transition to their more distinctive, honed sound.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Unlike its predecessors, <i>Soft Effects</i> was less noisy and brash, showcasing a more sophisticated, minimalist approach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stylus_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-stylus-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late 1996, Spoon was playing a gig at the Argo in <a href="/wiki/Denton,_Texas" title="Denton, Texas">Denton, Texas</a> with Ed Cooper and local band Maxine's Radiator, which then featured Josh Zarbo on bass. Zarbo was invited to audition for Daniel and Eno in 1997, and subsequently became the band's full-time bassist until his permanent departure in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zarbo_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-zarbo-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_label_debut">Major label debut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spoon_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Major label debut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Soft_Effects" title="Soft Effects">Soft Effects</a></i>, Spoon signed to <a href="/wiki/Elektra_Records" title="Elektra Records">Elektra Records</a> in 1998. Appropriately enough, Elektra had also been the record label that found and developed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pixies_(band)" title="Pixies (band)">Pixies (band)</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>Through Elektra the band released <i><a href="/wiki/A_Series_of_Sneaks" title="A Series of Sneaks">A Series of Sneaks</a></i> in May 1998. The album did not sell as well as the label had hoped; merely four months after the release of <i>Sneaks</i>, Spoon's Elektra A&amp;R man Ron Laffitte quit his job and that week the band was dropped from the label.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lazyi2001_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-lazyi2001-20">[20]</a></sup> Angry with Laffitte, who had promised to stick with the band, Spoon recorded a vindictive yet humorously-titled two-song concept single entitled "The Agony of Laffitte," which was released by Saddle Creek Records. They lamented their experience with the music business executive and questioned his motivations with the songs "The Agony of Laffitte" and "Laffitte Don't Fail Me Now".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_success">Commercial success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spoon_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Commercial success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Spoon signed with the indie rock label <a href="/wiki/Merge_Records" title="Merge Records">Merge Records</a> and released the <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Ways" title="Love Ways">Love Ways</a></i> EP in 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stylus_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-stylus-17">[17]</a></sup> They did this without bassist Josh Zarbo, who had briefly left the band.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mixednotes_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-mixednotes-22">[22]</a></sup> In 2001, Spoon released its third LP entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Girls_Can_Tell" title="Girls Can Tell">Girls Can Tell</a></i>. The new record was a success, selling more copies than both their previous LP releases combined.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pop_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pop-23">[23]</a></sup> The band’s next release in 2002, <i><a href="/wiki/Kill_the_Moonlight_(album)" title="Kill the Moonlight (album)">Kill the Moonlight</a></i> had similar success.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pop_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-Pop-23">[23]</a></sup> <i>Kill the Moonlight</i> also contained the single, "The Way We Get By", which was popularized by its placement on the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Stranger_than_Fiction_(2006_film)" title="Stranger than Fiction (2006 film)">Stranger than Fiction</a></i> (released later, in 2006) as well as the teen drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_O.C." title="The O.C.">The O.C.</a></i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and on the first episode of the American version of the series Shameless. Their next album, <i><a href="/wiki/Gimme_Fiction" title="Gimme Fiction">Gimme Fiction</a></i>, was released in May 2005, and debuted at number 44 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, selling more than 160,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-a360_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-a360-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Spoon’s Britt Daniel collaborated with Brian Reitzell to compose and arrange the soundtrack for the 2006 film <i><a href="/wiki/Stranger_than_Fiction_(2006_film)" title="Stranger than Fiction (2006 film)">Stranger than Fiction</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Stranger_than_Fiction_(soundtrack)" title="Stranger than Fiction (soundtrack)">The soundtrack</a> consists chiefly of music performed by Spoon, and according to the liner notes of the official soundtrack, Brian Reitzell collaborated with Britt Daniel to compose the score, while also adapting several tracks from <i>Kill the Moonlight</i> and <i>Gimme Fiction</i> into instrumental versions of the songs.</p>
<p>On July 10, 2007, <i><a href="/wiki/Ga_Ga_Ga_Ga_Ga" title="Ga Ga Ga Ga Ga">Ga Ga Ga Ga Ga</a></i> was released and debuted at number 10 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Since the release of <i>Kill the Moonlight</i> in 2002, Spoon has performed on late night talk shows, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Show_with_David_Letterman" title="Late Show with David Letterman">Late Show with David Letterman</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_with_Conan_O%27Brien" title="Late Night with Conan O'Brien">Late Night with Conan O'Brien</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Late_Late_Show_with_Craig_Ferguson" title="The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson">The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Last_Call_with_Carson_Daly" title="Last Call with Carson Daly">Last Call with Carson Daly</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_with_Conan_O%27Brien" title="The Tonight Show with Conan O'Brien">The Tonight Show with Conan O'Brien</a></i> as well as the PBS show <i><a href="/wiki/Austin_City_Limits" title="Austin City Limits">Austin City Limits</a></i>. They were also musical guests on <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i> on October 6, 2007, where they performed "<a href="/wiki/The_Underdog_(song)" title="The Underdog (song)">The Underdog</a>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> and "You Got Yr. Cherry Bomb".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 18, 2010, the band released its seventh studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Transference_(album)" title="Transference (album)">Transference</a></i>. It debuted at No. 4 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200, selling 53,000 copies in its first week.</p>
<p>"I Turn My Camera On", from <i>Gimme Fiction</i>, was used in the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/Veronica_Mars" title="Veronica Mars">Veronica Mars</a></i>, as well as on the second episode ("<a href="/wiki/The_Man_in_the_S.U.V." title="The Man in the S.U.V.">The Man in the S.U.V.</a>") of <i><a href="/wiki/Bones_(TV_series)" title="Bones (TV series)">Bones</a></i>, and the May 4, 2008, episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Simpsons" title="The Simpsons">The Simpsons</a></i>. "Don't Make Me a Target", "Don't You Evah" and "Got Nuffin" have been used on <i><a href="/wiki/Chuck_(TV_series)" title="Chuck (TV series)">Chuck</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Adam_Buxton" title="Adam Buxton">Adam Buxton</a> used the song "Don't Make Me a Target" for a segment of the pilot of his <a href="/wiki/BBC_Three" title="BBC Three">BBC Three</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/MeeBOX" title="MeeBOX">MeeBOX</a></i>. "I Summon You", from <i>Gimme Fiction</i>, was used in the sixth season of <i><a href="/wiki/Scrubs_(TV_series)" title="Scrubs (TV series)">Scrubs</a></i>, in the episode, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/My_Perspective" title="My Perspective">My Perspective</a>", as well as in Season 2 of <a href="/wiki/Veronica_Mars" title="Veronica Mars">Veronica Mars</a> in the episode "<a href="/wiki/Rashard_and_Wallace_Go_to_White_Castle" title="Rashard and Wallace Go to White Castle">Rashard and Wallace Go to White Castle</a>". "Don't You Evah" and "I Turn My Camera On" have become popular on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> as dance soundtracks for the Japanese robot <a href="/wiki/Keepon" title="Keepon">Keepon</a>, accumulating over two million hits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The song "Take A Walk" from <i>Girls Can Tell</i> was used on the soundtrack to the popular video game <i>Matt Hoffman's Pro BMX 2</i>. The song "The Underdog" was used in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Cloverfield" title="Cloverfield">Cloverfield</a></i>, and included in the movie's soundtrack, <i>Rob's Party Mix</i>. It was also used in episode 10 of the fourth season of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Numb3rs" title="Numb3rs">Numb3rs</a></i>, in episode 8 of the third season of <i><a href="/wiki/How_I_Met_Your_Mother" title="How I Met Your Mother">How I Met Your Mother</a></i>, as well as the movies <i><a href="/wiki/17_Again_(film)" title="17 Again (film)">17 Again</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/I_Love_You,_Man" title="I Love You, Man">I Love You, Man</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Horrible_Bosses" title="Horrible Bosses">Horrible Bosses</a></i>. "Don't You Evah" was used on the soundtrack for the video game <i><a href="/wiki/MLB_09:_The_Show" title="MLB 09: The Show">MLB 09: The Show</a></i>. The song "The Infinite Pet" from <i>Gimme Fiction</i> was used in the film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/(500)_Days_of_Summer" title="(500) Days of Summer">(500) Days of Summer</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> "Got Nuffin" was the closing track in the season 7 finale of <i><a href="/wiki/House_(TV_series)" title="House (TV series)">House</a></i>. In 2010 Spoon recorded the title track on the album <i><a href="/wiki/See_My_Friends" title="See My Friends">See My Friends</a></i> in collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Ray_Davies" title="Ray Davies">Ray Davies</a>.</p>
<p>Their song "The Mystery Zone" was chosen as the <a href="/wiki/Starbucks" title="Starbucks">Starbucks</a> <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> Pick of the Week on January 26, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, the review aggregator <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> ranked Spoon as its "Top overall artist of the decade", based on the band's consistently high review scores between 2000 and 2009, amongst other factors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 12, 2010, Spoon made a guest appearance on Conan O'Brien's <a href="/wiki/The_Legally_Prohibited_from_Being_Funny_on_Television_Tour" title="The Legally Prohibited from Being Funny on Television Tour">The Legally Prohibited from Being Funny on Television Tour</a> in <a href="/wiki/Eugene,_Oregon" title="Eugene, Oregon">Eugene, Oregon</a>, where they performed the song "I Summon You" from their album <i><a href="/wiki/Gimme_Fiction" title="Gimme Fiction">Gimme Fiction</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>Spoon released their eighth full-length album, <i><a href="/wiki/They_Want_My_Soul" title="They Want My Soul">They Want My Soul</a></i>, on August 5, 2014. It is their first record released by <a href="/wiki/Loma_Vista_Recordings" title="Loma Vista Recordings">Loma Vista Recordings</a>, after five records on Merge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> It was recorded with Joe Chicarelli and Dave Fridmann.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 30, 2014, Spoon appeared as the featured musical guests during The Daily Show's Democalypse 2014: South By South Mess. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
