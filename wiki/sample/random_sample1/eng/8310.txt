<p><b>Sprague Grayden</b> (born July 21, 1980) is an American <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a>, <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">film</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theater" title="Theater">theater</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a>.</p>
<p>She played schoolteacher <a href="/wiki/List_of_Jericho_characters#Heather_Lisinski" title="List of Jericho characters">Heather Lisinski</a> in the television drama <i><a href="/wiki/Jericho_(2006_TV_series)" title="Jericho (2006 TV series)">Jericho</a></i>, Karen Kawalski in <i><a href="/wiki/John_Doe_(TV_series)" title="John Doe (TV series)">John Doe</a></i>, first daughter <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olivia_Taylor" title="Olivia Taylor">Olivia Taylor</a> in the television thriller <i><a href="/wiki/24_(TV_series)" title="24 (TV series)">24</a></i>, and Kristi Rey in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Paranormal_Activity_2" title="Paranormal Activity 2">Paranormal Activity 2</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Paranormal_Activity_3" title="Paranormal Activity 3">3</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sprague_Grayden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Grayden was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchester,_Massachusetts" title="Manchester, Massachusetts">Manchester, Massachusetts</a> to two schoolteachers. Sprague is her mother's maiden name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interview_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-interview-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>She had a younger brother, Benjamin Grayden, who died in 1999 due to injuries sustained in a fall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Grayden graduated with a degree in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Studies" title="American Studies">American Studies</a> from <a href="/wiki/Barnard_College" title="Barnard College">Barnard College</a>, where she was a performer of the drama department.</p>
<p>In January, 2013, she announced she had married her "24" co-star Lex Cassar (born Alexis Cassar).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She gave birth to their son in September 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>She describes herself as a science fiction geek and likes to collect Pez dispensers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sprague_Grayden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She was a performing member of the drama department at Barnard College, <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interview_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-interview-2">[2]</a></sup> Her theater credits include productions of <i>Hopscotch: The New York Sex Comedy</i>, <a href="/wiki/Eve_Ensler" title="Eve Ensler">Eve Ensler</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Vagina_Monologues" title="The Vagina Monologues">The Vagina Monologues</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Sam_Shepard" title="Sam Shepard">Sam Shepard</a>'s <i>Fool for Love</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hamlet" title="Hamlet">Hamlet</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Clifford_Odets" title="Clifford Odets">Clifford Odets</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Waiting_for_Lefty" title="Waiting for Lefty">Waiting for Lefty</a></i>, and <i>Ordinary Day</i>.</p>
<p>In 2009, <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i> called her "one of TV's most interesting young actresses".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
