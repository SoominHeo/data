<p><b>Sprint Center</b> is a large, multi-use indoor <a href="/wiki/Arena" title="Arena">arena</a> in downtown <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The building is located at 14th Street and Grand Boulevard, on the east side of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Power_%26_Light_District" title="Power &amp; Light District">Power &amp; Light District</a>. The arena's naming rights partner is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telecommunications" title="Telecommunications">telecommunications</a> company <a href="/wiki/Sprint_Corporation" title="Sprint Corporation">Sprint</a>, whose headquarters is in nearby <a href="/wiki/Overland_Park,_Kansas" title="Overland Park, Kansas">Overland Park, Kansas</a>.</p>
<p>Sprint Center opened to the public on October 10, 2007, and a concert by <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a> three days later was the first event held at the arena.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The arena seats more than 19,000 people and has 72 suites. Sprint Center has effectively replaced <a href="/wiki/Kemper_Arena" title="Kemper Arena">Kemper Arena</a>, which was built in 1974, just a few miles away in the southern portion of the <a href="/wiki/West_Bottoms" title="West Bottoms">West Bottoms</a>. Additionally, the <a href="/wiki/College_Basketball_Experience" title="College Basketball Experience">College Basketball Experience</a>, which includes the National Collegiate Basketball Hall of Fame, is connected to and located directly north of Sprint Center.</p>
<p>Sprint Center has hosted the <a href="/wiki/Big_12_Men%27s_Basketball_Tournament" title="Big 12 Men's Basketball Tournament">Big 12 Men's Basketball Tournament</a> in 2008 and every year since 2010. It also hosted the first and second rounds of the <a href="/wiki/2009_NCAA_Men%27s_Division_I_Basketball_Tournament" title="2009 NCAA Men's Division I Basketball Tournament">2009 NCAA Men's Tournament</a> and <a href="/wiki/2013_NCAA_Men%27s_Division_I_Basketball_Tournament" title="2013 NCAA Men's Division I Basketball Tournament">2013 NCAA Men's Tournament</a>, as well as the regional rounds of the <a href="/wiki/2010_NCAA_Women%27s_Division_I_Basketball_Tournament" title="2010 NCAA Women's Division I Basketball Tournament">2010 NCAA Women's Tournament</a>. The arena also served as the home of the former <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Command" title="Kansas City Command">Kansas City Command</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arena_Football_League_(2010)" title="Arena Football League (2010)">Arena Football League</a> (AFL).</p>
<p>The city of Kansas City has entered into discussions with the <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">National Hockey League</a> (NHL) and the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a> (NBA) regarding possible <a href="/wiki/Expansion_team" title="Expansion team">expansion</a> or <a href="/wiki/Relocation_of_professional_sports_teams" title="Relocation of professional sports teams">relocation</a> of a professional <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">hockey</a> and/or <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> franchise for the arena; however, neither the NHL nor the NBA have yet to approve a team to play in the Sprint Center.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Groundbreaking" title="Groundbreaking">Ground was broken</a> for the arena on June 24, 2005, and construction completed on October 11, 2007. The final design, by the Downtown Arena Design Team (a collaboration of the architectural firms Populous, <a href="/wiki/360_Architecture" title="360 Architecture">360 Architecture</a>, Rafael Architects, and <a href="/wiki/Ellerbe_Becket" title="Ellerbe Becket">Ellerbe Becket</a>), was selected in August 2005. The construction manager responsible for the entire project was <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=M.A._Mortenson_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="M.A. Mortenson Company (page does not exist)">M.A. Mortenson Company</a>, based out of Minneapolis, Minnesota.</p>
<p>The complete exterior glass facade system, all metal panels for the adjacent buildings and all accessory metal cladding was custom designed, detailed and supplied by Overgaard Ltd. Hong Kong to Architectural Wall Systems, the Des Moines, Iowa based <a href="/wiki/Glazing_(window)" title="Glazing (window)">glazing</a> contractor who installed the building envelope. In total there are approximately 13,000 m² (139,932 square feet) of double insulated glass and 5,000 m² (53,820 square feet) painted aluminum curtain wall panels. In addition there are roughly 200 tons of system profiles and accessories. All of the 2,404 individual glass units on the main building were produced sequentially and completely assembled prior to shipping. The 5,000,000 lbs of rebar used in construction was detailed, farbricated and supplied by The Carter-Waters Corporation of Kansas City. The arena also features a work of <a href="/wiki/Public_art" title="Public art">public art</a>, The Moons, by artist <a href="/wiki/Chris_Doyle_(artist)" title="Chris Doyle (artist)">Chris Doyle</a>, commissioned by the Kansas City Municipal Arts Commission (KCMAC).</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Big_12_Conference" title="Big 12 Conference">Big 12 Conference</a> <a href="/wiki/Big_12_Men%27s_Basketball_Tournament" title="Big 12 Men's Basketball Tournament">Men's Basketball Tournament</a> was held at Sprint Center in 2008, marking the tournament's return to Kansas City after three years in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dallas,_Texas" title="Dallas, Texas">Dallas</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City,_Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma City, Oklahoma">Oklahoma City</a>. After returning to Oklahoma City in 2009, the Sprint Center again hosted the tournament in 2010 and 2011. It is scheduled to be the tournament host site through 2016.</p>
<p>The arena also houses the College Basketball Experience and the <a href="/wiki/National_Collegiate_Basketball_Hall_of_Fame" title="National Collegiate Basketball Hall of Fame">National Collegiate Basketball Hall of Fame</a>, which is located at 1301 Grand Boulevard and connected to Sprint Center.</p>
<p>The outside of the arena is entirely glass. Inside it has state-of-the-art technology with a 360-degree <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LED" title="LED">LED</a> video screen. This facility allows Kansas City to draw most concerts touring the United States. There has been speculation of an NBA and/or NHL team relocating to Sprint Center, but no definitive plans have been announced yet.</p>
<p>Sprint Center opened on time and on budget<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> on October 10, 2007 at 10:10 am. A tour lasted from 10:10 am – 10:10 pm for those who wanted to see it to grab a ticket at the box office. The tour consisted of the <a href="/wiki/College_Basketball_Experience" title="College Basketball Experience">College Basketball Experience</a>, two open concession stands: "Taco Taco" and "Oak Street Pizza"; a free drink from <a href="/wiki/UMB_Financial_Corporation" title="UMB Financial Corporation">UMB Bank</a> and other free items: a UMB Bank cup, a UMB Bank frisbee, and a UMB Bank ice scraper. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UMB_Bank" title="UMB Bank">UMB Bank</a> is the only <a href="/wiki/Automated_teller_machine" title="Automated teller machine">ATM</a> in the new arena because it is a Sprint Center Founding Partner along with <a href="/wiki/Farmland_Industries" title="Farmland Industries">Farmland</a>, The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Kansas" title="University of Kansas">University of Kansas</a> Hospital, <a href="/wiki/QuikTrip" title="QuikTrip">QuikTrip</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Syntax-Brillian_Corporation" title="Syntax-Brillian Corporation">Olevia</a>. There are other Sprint Center Founding Partners: <a href="/wiki/H%26R_Block" title="H&amp;R Block">H&amp;R Block</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toyota" title="Toyota">Toyota</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_Century_Investments" title="American Century Investments">American Century Investments</a>, <a href="/wiki/YRC_Worldwide" title="YRC Worldwide">YRC Worldwide</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Time_Warner_Cable" title="Time Warner Cable">Time Warner Cable</a> of Kansas City.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_concerts">Notable concerts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sprint_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notable concerts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Possible_major-league_sports">Possible major-league sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sprint_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Possible major-league sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Kansas City Brigade played at the Sprint Center for one season before the original AFL folded after the 2008 postseason. However, the revamped AFL brought arena football back to the Sprint Center in 2011, with the team playing as the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Command" title="Kansas City Command">Kansas City Command</a>.</p>
<p>William "Boots" Del Biaggio, III, made an offer to purchase the <a href="/wiki/Nashville_Predators" title="Nashville Predators">Nashville Predators</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">NHL</a> with the intention of bringing the team to the Sprint Center.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> However, Del Biaggio has since joined a group of Nashville investors in an effort to keep the Predators in Nashville.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In June 2008, Kansas City's hopes to land the Predators took another blow as Del Biaggio ran into legal trouble over a multitude of unpaid loans, culminating in him filing for Chapter 11 bankruptcy, effectively ending any chance of Del Biaggio moving the Predators to Missouri. On January 10, 2007, it was reported that the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Clippers" title="Los Angeles Clippers">Los Angeles Clippers</a> have taken an interest in moving to the arena.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/New_York_Islanders" title="New York Islanders">New York Islanders</a> owner <a href="/wiki/Charles_Wang" title="Charles Wang">Charles Wang</a> announced the Islanders will play a preseason game September 22, 2009 at the Sprint Center against the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Kings" title="Los Angeles Kings">Los Angeles Kings</a>. Local media reported that anonymous team sources stated the game was a test of the Kansas City market.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In 2007, Kansas City and the Sprint Center also attempted to attract relocation of the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Penguins" title="Pittsburgh Penguins">Pittsburgh Penguins</a>, but the Penguins reached a deal with the City of Pittsburgh to build a new arena, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Consol_Energy_Center" title="Consol Energy Center">Consol Energy Center</a>, and stay in Western Pennsylvania. The Sprint Center had also been looked at as a possible relocation spot for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBA" title="NBA">NBA</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Orleans_Hornets" title="New Orleans Hornets">New Orleans Hornets</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Sacramento_Kings" title="Sacramento Kings">Sacramento Kings</a> (the latter of which had previously played in Kansas City) before both teams settled their local issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite losing out on pro teams, the NBA and NHL have held preseason games at Sprint Center. The most recent NHL preseason game, between the St. Louis Blues and Washington Capitals, took place on October 5th, 2016; the most recent NBA preseason game, between the Miami Heat and Minnesota Timberwolves, took place on the 8th.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_sporting_events">Other sporting events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sprint_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other sporting events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/College_Basketball_Experience_Classic" title="College Basketball Experience Classic">CBE Classic</a> is held every year at Sprint Center. The event was first held on November 19, 2007 and November 20, 2007.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Association_of_Volleyball_Professionals" title="Association of Volleyball Professionals">AVP</a> Pro Beach Volleyball's <i>Hot Winter Nights Tour</i> was held at Sprint Center on January 12, 2008.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Professional_Bull_Riders" title="Professional Bull Riders">PBR</a> holds a <a href="/wiki/Built_Ford_Tough_Series" title="Built Ford Tough Series">Built Ford Tough Series</a> event at Sprint Center annually since 2008. Prior to 2008, the event was held at <a href="/wiki/Kemper_Arena" title="Kemper Arena">Kemper Arena</a>.</li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Brigade" title="Kansas City Brigade">Kansas City Brigade</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arena_Football_League_(1987-2008)" title="Arena Football League (1987-2008)">Arena Football League</a> played their 2008 season at Sprint Center. They returned in 2011 after the 2009 AFL season was canceled due to financial reasons and the 2010 season saw Kansas City decide to take a break from Arena Football. The team shut down after the 2012 season.</li>
<li>The NCAA Women's Volleyball Final Four was held at the Sprint Center on December 16 and 18, 2010.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Penguins" title="Pittsburgh Penguins">Pittsburgh Penguins</a> faced the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Kings" title="Los Angeles Kings">Los Angeles Kings</a> on September 27, 2011 in front of a sold-out crowd of 17,779. The Penguins won the <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">NHL</a> pre-season game 3-2 in a shootout.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Kansas_Jayhawks_men%27s_basketball" title="Kansas Jayhawks men's basketball">Kansas Jayhawks men's basketball</a> team has played at least one regular season non-conference game in the Sprint Center every season since the arena opened, starting with an 88-51 victory over the <a href="/wiki/Ohio_Bobcats_men%27s_basketball" title="Ohio Bobcats men's basketball">Ohio Bobcats</a> on December 15, 2007, carrying over a tradition from Kemper Arena. As of December 17, 2016, Kansas' record in such games is 8-2.</li>
</ul>
