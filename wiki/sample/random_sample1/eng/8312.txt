<p><i><b>Spring Breakers</b></i> is a 2013 American <a href="/wiki/Satire" title="Satire">satirical</a> <a href="/wiki/Crime_film" title="Crime film">crime</a> <a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_(modern_genre)" title="Drama (modern genre)">drama</a> <a href="/wiki/Comedy_film" title="Comedy film">comedy</a> film written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Harmony_Korine" title="Harmony Korine">Harmony Korine</a>. It stars <a href="/wiki/James_Franco" title="James Franco">James Franco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Hudgens" title="Vanessa Hudgens">Vanessa Hudgens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Selena_Gomez" title="Selena Gomez">Selena Gomez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Benson" title="Ashley Benson">Ashley Benson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Korine" title="Rachel Korine">Rachel Korine</a> and follows four college-aged girls on their spring break in Florida where they meet an eccentric local drug dealer named Alien who helps them in a time of desperation, and their eventual descent into a world of drugs, crime, and violence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rachel_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rachel-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Korine had devised the concept for <i>Spring Breakers</i> over several years prior to production, with fleeting ideas about the plot and what should transpire. His initial desire was to create a "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sensory_perception" title="Sensory perception">sensory</a> film" that was more about feeling than action and placed little importance on <a href="/wiki/Narrative" title="Narrative">narrative</a> or plot, the idea for which came later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Once Korine developed the backbone of the story, which takes place around the American spring break period, he travelled to Florida to write the screenplay. Production began in 2012, on an estimated budget of $5 million–a relatively small amount in today's film industry, albeit Korine's second most expensive film to date. The film is also one of Korine's first theatrical works to receive a wide release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Spring Breakers</i> was released on March 22, 2013 in the United States by <a href="/wiki/A24_(company)" title="A24 (company)">A24</a> and grossed $31 million worldwide, making it a resounding success considering the small budget. It received generally positive reviews from <a href="/wiki/Film_criticism" title="Film criticism">film critics</a>, with some calling it a potential <a href="/wiki/Cult_film" title="Cult film">cult classic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-midnight_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-midnight-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The film was selected to compete for the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Lion" title="Golden Lion">Golden Lion</a> at the <a href="/wiki/69th_Venice_International_Film_Festival" title="69th Venice International Film Festival">69th Venice International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-labiennale_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-labiennale-17">[17]</a></sup> Critics and scholars have read deeper meaning in the film's plot, commenting on its reflection of modern-day superficiality and the younger generation's obsession with highly stylised <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pop_culture" title="Pop culture">pop culture</a> media.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riotous-take_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-riotous-take-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_Breakers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>College students Brit (<a href="/wiki/Ashley_Benson" title="Ashley Benson">Ashley Benson</a>), Candy (<a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Hudgens" title="Vanessa Hudgens">Vanessa Hudgens</a>) and Cotty (<a href="/wiki/Rachel_Korine" title="Rachel Korine">Rachel Korine</a>) spend their time partying while their friend Faith (<a href="/wiki/Selena_Gomez" title="Selena Gomez">Selena Gomez</a>) attends a religious youth group. As their classmates head to spring break, they are stuck behind due to a lack of money. Desperate to make the trip, Brit and Candy, after getting high on <a href="/wiki/Cocaine" title="Cocaine">cocaine</a>, don <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ski_masks" title="Ski masks">ski masks</a> and use <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hammers" title="Hammers">hammers</a> and realistic-looking <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Squirt_guns" title="Squirt guns">squirt guns</a> to rob a local restaurant. Cotty drives and later burns a getaway car stolen from one of their professors.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/St._Petersburg,_Florida" title="St. Petersburg, Florida">St. Petersburg, Florida</a>, the girls attend wild beach <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parties" title="Parties">parties</a> fueled by alcohol, drugs and sex. Cotty, Candy and Brit divulge the details of their crime to a horrified Faith, who keeps quiet about it. After a particularly wild party, all four are arrested. They spend the night in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holding_cell" title="Holding cell">holding cell</a>, but are <a href="/wiki/Bail" title="Bail">bailed</a> out by Alien (<a href="/wiki/James_Franco" title="James Franco">James Franco</a>), a local rapper and gangster. Alien charms Cotty, Candy and Brit with his wealth and "bad boy" swagger, but Faith feels uneasy.</p>
<p>Alien takes the girls to a local club frequented by gang members, where Faith becomes even more uncomfortable with his lifestyle. Despite Alien's attempts to convince her to stay, Faith decides to leave and begs the others to come with her. They refuse, however, and she makes the trip home alone. Alien takes Brit and Candy to a strip club owned by his rival, Big Arch (<a href="/wiki/Gucci_Mane" title="Gucci Mane">Gucci Mane</a>), who warns Alien to stop selling drugs in his territory. The girls return to Alien's mansion, where he flaunts his drug money and cache of weapons, describing his life as the "<a href="/wiki/American_Dream" title="American Dream">American Dream</a>". Brit and Candy suddenly grab one of his guns and threaten to kill him; turned on, Alien performs fellatio on the gun and declares that he has fallen in love with the girls.</p>
<p>Alien arms the girls with pink ski masks and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shotguns" title="Shotguns">shotguns</a>, and they perform several armed robberies. While in Alien's car they are approached by Big Arch and another member of his gang, who threaten them and execute a <a href="/wiki/Drive-by_shooting" title="Drive-by shooting">drive-by shooting</a>, wounding Cotty. Alien promises to retaliate, but a traumatized Cotty chooses to return home. Brit and Candy stay behind and begin a sexual relationship with Alien. The three of them decide to take revenge on Big Arch. In a <a href="/wiki/Flashforward" title="Flashforward">flashforward</a>, the two girls call home, promising to work harder and become better people.</p>
<p>Back in the present, the three travel in a motorboat to Big Arch's mansion. After docking at the pier, Alien is immediately shot and killed by one of Big Arch's guards. Brit and Candy continue on, killing Big Arch's gang before confronting and killing Big Arch himself. During the assault and its aftermath, the camera pans over the dead bodies of Big Arch's gang while Faith speaks in a <a href="/wiki/Voice-over" title="Voice-over">voice-over</a>, first heard earlier in the film, describing the beach's beauty and musing that they have discovered who they truly are. Brit and Candy drive home in Big Arch's <a href="/wiki/Lamborghini" title="Lamborghini">Lamborghini</a>. A final <a href="/wiki/Flashback_(narrative)" title="Flashback (narrative)">flashback</a> shows the pair kissing Alien's dead body.</p>

<p>According to <a href="/wiki/Harmony_Korine" title="Harmony Korine">Harmony Korine</a>, he wrote the film partially to make up for his own spring breaks, as he had been fully devoted to href="#cite_note-interviewmag-19">[19]</a></sup> Korine has referred to the film as a "beach <a href="/wiki/Film_noir" title="Film noir">noir</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riotous-take_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-riotous-take-18">[18]</a></sup> The original lineup of lead actresses was announced as <a href="/wiki/Emma_Roberts" title="Emma Roberts">Emma Roberts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Selena_Gomez" title="Selena Gomez">Selena Gomez</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Hudgens" title="Vanessa Hudgens">Vanessa Hudgens</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Girls_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Girls-20">[20]</a></sup> Roberts however dropped out in early 2012, reportedly after hearing she would need to gain body fat for her role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Director Korine had purposely collected a group of well-known young actresses with a similar reputation to Roberts in Hollywood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Benson" title="Ashley Benson">Ashley Benson</a> was ultimately cast.<sup Florida">St. Petersburg, Florida</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interviewmag_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-interviewmag-19">[19]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Skrillex" title="Skrillex">Skrillex</a> produced the film's score.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_Breakers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Riff_Raff">Riff Raff</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_Breakers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Riff Raff">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On February 15, 2012, Korine contacted rapper <a href="/wiki/Riff_Raff_(rapper)" title="Riff Raff (rapper)">Riff Raff</a> about appearing in an upcoming film of his which would turn out to be <i>Spring Breakers</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Once the trailer was released there was speculation that the character Alien was based around Riff Raff.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> According to Franco, his character is based on the underground rap artist Dangeruss, who has a cameo in the film. "Of course Harmony and I looked at some of Riff Raff's videos as inspiration, but he was one of a number of people we looked at. I would say the biggest influence on the role was this local Florida rapper named Dangeruss. He's fairly unknown, but he was down there in the place, living the life, and he became the biggest model for me, and he's in the movie."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> After much back and forth between both camps about the issue, during July 2013 Riff Raff announced he was suing the creators of <i>Spring Breakers</i> for $10 million for "sampling" his life without his permission or a proper producer credit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Portrayal_of_women">Portrayal of women</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_Breakers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Portrayal of women">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Spring Breakers</i> has generated debate and controversy among critics and bloggers, with some regarding the film as sexist due to its objectification of women, while others viewed the film as a <a href="/wiki/Feminism" title="Feminism">feminist</a> or female empowerment film. In regard to the former perspective, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> suggests that the film "reinforces <a href="/wiki/Rape_culture" title="Rape culture">rape culture</a>" and "turns young women into sex objects",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> while other reviewers state that it "pushes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alcoholic_beverage" title="Alcoholic beverage">booze</a>-and-<a href="/wiki/Bikini" title="Bikini">bikini</a> hedonism to the extreme",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> as the "camera glides up, down and around these women's bodies like a giant tongue."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> In regard to the latter perspective, the women are not depicted as the film's victims, but arguably as <a href="/wiki/Antihero" title="Antihero">antiheroes</a>, acting according to their own power and <a href="/wiki/Agency_(philosophy)" title="Agency (philosophy)">agency</a>. According to <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i>, the film presents "a kind of girl-power camaraderie that could almost be called feminist", a result of the director's intent to "do the most radical work, but put it out in the most commercial way (...) to infiltrate the mainstream".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> In his review of the film, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Roeper" title="Richard Roeper">Richard Roeper</a> wrote "Korine's camera is nearly an intrusive weapon as he lingers over the soft, limber bodies of Vanessa Hudgens, Selena Gomez, Ashley Benson and his wife, Rachel Korine.... I think that's sort of the point. When a pre-med student on spring break loses her top, drinks to the point of passing out and grabs a willing lugnut by the ears for six hours of anonymous fun, is she setting the woman's movement back 40 years, or taking charge of her life like any man would do at that age?"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-roeper_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-roeper-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_Breakers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_Breakers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film received generally favorable reactions from critics. The film holds a 66% approval rating on <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">review aggregator</a> site <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, based on 168 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, which assigns a weighted mean rating out of 100 from film critics, it received a rating score of 63, based on 40 critics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Xan Brooks of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> said the film is Korine's "most fully realised, purely satisfying feature film since <i><a href="/wiki/Gummo" title="Gummo">Gummo</a></i>. "<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Emma Seligman of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Huffington_Post" title="The Huffington Post">The Huffington Post</a></i> described the film as "<i><a href="/wiki/Scarface_(1983_film)" title="Scarface (1983 film)">Scarface</a></i> meets <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Oliver Lyttlelton of <i><a href="/wiki/IndieWire" title="IndieWire">IndieWire</a></i> gave the film a B, stating that the film would be a future cult favorite for "<a href="/wiki/Midnight_movie" title="Midnight movie">midnight moviegoers</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-midnight_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-midnight-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Guy Lodge of <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> gave it a negative review saying, "this attractively fizzy pic may be a shock to the system for fans of teen queens Selena Gomez and Vanessa Hudgens, but remains pretty toothless titillation by its writer-helmer's standards."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> David Rooney of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i> noted that James Franco gives one of his more bizarre performances in his unpredictable career, saying "he's a cross between <a href="/wiki/Bo_Derek" title="Bo Derek">Bo Derek</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/10_(film)" title="10 (film)">10</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Richard_Kiel" title="Richard Kiel">Richard Kiel</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Moonraker_(film)" title="Moonraker (film)">Moonraker</a></i>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Andrew Schenker of <i><a href="/wiki/Slant_Magazine" title="Slant Magazine">Slant Magazine</a></i> gave the film 3 out of 4 stars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Jamie Dunn of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Skinny_(magazine)" title="The Skinny (magazine)">The Skinny</a></i> gave it 4 out of 5 stars, saying: "If <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michael_Mann_(director)" title="Michael Mann (director)">Michael Mann</a> was to take a lot of hallucinogenics and shoot a <a href="/wiki/Girls_Gone_Wild_(franchise)" title="Girls Gone Wild (franchise)">Girls Gone Wild</a> video, it might look something like this."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Richard_Roeper" title="Richard Roeper">Richard Roeper</a> gave the film three-and-a-half stars out of four, praising the character of Alien and the film's sense of humor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-roeper_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-roeper-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Spring Breakers</i> has since appeared on various retrospective "best of" lists, including one honouring the best films of the 21st century. In 2016, British film magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Little_White_Lies_(magazine)" title="Little White Lies (magazine)">Little White Lies</a></i> placed the film at number 40 on their list of the <i>50 best films of the decade (so far)</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> In August of that same year, <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC Magazine</a> conducted a poll on the 21st century's 100 greatest films so far, with <i>Spring Breakers</i> ranking at number 74.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_Breakers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Spring Breakers</i> grossed $14,124,284 in North America and $17,600,000 in other countries for a worldwide total of $31,724,284.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BOM_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-BOM-4">[4]</a></sup> In North America, the film opened to #6 in its first weekend with $4,858,944, behind of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Croods" title="The Croods">The Croods</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Admission_(2013_film)" title="Admission (2013 film)">Admission</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Call_(2013_film)" title="The Call (2013 film)">The Call</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Oz_the_Great_and_Powerful" title="Oz the Great and Powerful">Oz the Great and Powerful</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Olympus_Has_Fallen" title="Olympus Has Fallen">Olympus Has Fallen</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Oscar_campaign_for_James_Franco">Oscar campaign for James Franco</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_Breakers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Oscar campaign for James Franco">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A24_Films" title="A24 Films">A24 Films</a> began a campaign in September 2013 in support of a <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor">Best Supporting Actor</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Oscar</a> nomination for Franco's performance. This was preceded by the <i>Hollywood.com</i> website that produced a "For your consideration" poster in support of a nomination for Franco in March 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> On December 2, 2013, A24 published a YouTube video titled "James Franco - Consider this Sh*t" and also released print advertisements following the "Consider this Sh*t" theme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> Originally, Internet chatter considered the campaign a joke, but A24 films has since made it clear that the campaign was indeed serious.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> He has received the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Film_Critics_Association_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="Los Angeles Film Critics Association Award for Best Supporting Actor">Los Angeles Film Critics Association Award for Best Supporting Actor</a> (tied with <a href="/wiki/Jared_Leto" title="Jared Leto">Jared Leto</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/Dallas_Buyers_Club" title="Dallas Buyers Club">Dallas Buyers Club</a></i>), <a href="/wiki/National_Society_of_Film_Critics_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="National Society of Film Critics Award for Best Supporting Actor">National Society of Film Critics Award for Best Supporting Actor</a>, and <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Film_Critics_Circle_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="San Francisco Film Critics Circle Award for Best Supporting Actor">San Francisco Film Critics Circle Award for Best Supporting Actor</a>, while the <a href="/wiki/Washington_D.C._Area_Film_Critics_Association" title="Washington D.C. Area Film Critics Association">Washington D.C. Area Film Critics Association</a> nominated Franco for its Best Supporting Actor award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_Breakers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film score to <i>Spring Breakers</i> was composed by <a href="/wiki/Cliff_Martinez" title="Cliff Martinez">Cliff Martinez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Skrillex" title="Skrillex">Skrillex</a>, marking the first scoring assignment for the latter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> Skrillex was contacted after Korine sent music supervisor Randall Poster links to the <a href="/wiki/Electronica" title="Electronica">electronica</a> artist's music on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>. "I'm accustomed to being the oldest person at a gig," said Poster, "but when I went to see Skrillex at Roseland this year, it was dramatic. There were a lot of kids that looked like they were 15 years old. But I loved it. I truly loved it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequel">Sequel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spring_Breakers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Sequel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Spring Breakers: The Second Coming</i> was announced in May 6, 2014. Although the storyline will have connections with the previous film, it will include a new cast in addition to the original. Confirmed to be directed by <a href="/wiki/Jonas_%C3%85kerlund" title="Jonas Åkerlund">Jonas Åkerlund</a>, <i>The Second Coming</i>, which <a href="/wiki/Irvine_Welsh" title="Irvine Welsh">Irvine Welsh</a> has been attached to script, will focus on a set of Spring Breakers coming into conflict with Christian extremists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup></p>
<p>Upon the announcement, Franco released a statement saying that the sequel was "not being done with Harmony Korine or my consent" and that the producers were "capitalizing on that innovative film to make money on a weak sequel" and attempting to "make money off someone else's creativity."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p>
