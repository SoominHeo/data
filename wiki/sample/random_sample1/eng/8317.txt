<p><b>Sfântu Gheorghe</b> (<small>Romanian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Romanian" title="Help:IPA for Romanian">[ˈsfɨntu ˈɡe̯orɡe]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>: <i>Sepsiszentgyörgy</i> or <i>Szentgyörgy</i>, <small>Hungarian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Hungarian" title="Help:IPA for Hungarian">[ˈʃɛpʃisɛnɟørɟ]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:Sepsiszentgy%C3%B6rgy.ogg" title="File:Sepsiszentgyörgy.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/90/Sepsiszentgy%C3%B6rgy.ogg" title="Sepsiszentgyörgy.ogg">listen</a>)</small>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yiddish_language" title="Yiddish language">Yiddish</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="yi" xml:lang="yi">סנט דשזארדזש</span>‎) is the capital city of <a href="/wiki/Covasna_County" title="Covasna County">Covasna County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>. Located in the central part of the country and in the historical region of <a href="/wiki/Transylvania" title="Transylvania">Transylvania</a>, it lies on the <a href="/wiki/Olt_River" title="Olt River">Olt River</a> in a valley between the <a href="/wiki/Baraolt_Mountains" title="Baraolt Mountains">Baraolt Mountains</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bodoc_(mountains)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bodoc (mountains) (page does not exist)">Bodoc</a> Mountains. The city administers two villages, Chilieni (<i>Kilyén</i>) and Coșeni (<i>Szotyor</i>).</p>

<p>The majority of the city's inhabitants are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hungarian_people" title="Hungarian people">Hungarians</a>. In the census of 2011, 41,770 (77%) of the city's 54,312 inhabitants classed themselves as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hungarian_minority_in_Romania" title="Hungarian minority in Romania">ethnic Hungarians</a>, 11,921 (22%) as <a href="/wiki/Romanians" title="Romanians">Romanians</a>, 420 (0.8%) as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roma_in_Romania" title="Roma in Romania">Roma</a>, and 201 as "others."</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sf%C3%A2ntu_Gheorghe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sfântu Gheorghe is one of the oldest cities in Transylvania, the settlement first having been documented in 1332. The city takes it name from <a href="/wiki/Saint_George" title="Saint George">Saint George</a>, the patron of the local church. Historically it was also known in <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> as <i>Sankt Georgen</i>. The "sepsi" prefix (<i>sebesi</i> → <i>sepsi</i>, meaning "of Sebes") refers to the area which the ancestors of the local Székely population had inhabited before settling to the area of the town. The previous area of their settlement was around the town of "Sebes" (now: <a href="/wiki/Sebe%C8%99" title="Sebeș">Sebeș</a>) which later became populated mainly by <a href="/wiki/Transylvanian_Saxons" title="Transylvanian Saxons">Transylvanian Saxons</a>.</p>
<p>While part of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary" title="Kingdom of Hungary">Kingdom of Hungary</a>, the city was the economic and administrative center of the Hungarian county of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/H%C3%A1romsz%C3%A9k" title="Háromszék">Háromszék</a>, which spanned the present-day <a href="/wiki/Covasna_County" title="Covasna County">Covasna</a> County and parts of <a href="/wiki/Bra%C8%99ov_County" title="Brașov County">Brașov</a> County. In the second half of the 19th century, Sepsiszentgyörgy witnessed the development of light industry, namely a textile and a cigarette factory was built. It became part of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Romania" title="Kingdom of Romania">Kingdom of Romania</a> following the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Trianon" title="Treaty of Trianon">Treaty of Trianon</a> in 1920, after the end of World War I. After the <a href="/wiki/Second_Vienna_Award" title="Second Vienna Award">Second Vienna Award</a> in 1940 the city came under Hungarian control for four years. Near the end of that period, the <a href="/wiki/Sf%C3%A2ntu_Gheorghe_ghetto" title="Sfântu Gheorghe ghetto">Sfântu Gheorghe ghetto</a> briefly existed in the city. At the end of the Second World War the <a href="/wiki/Paris_Peace_Treaties,_1947" title="Paris Peace Treaties, 1947">Paris Peace Treaties</a> reaffirmed the city and the entirety of Transylvania as a Romanian territory. Between 1952-1960 it was the southernmost town of the <a href="/wiki/Magyar_Autonomous_Region" title="Magyar Autonomous Region">Magyar Autonomous Region</a>, and between 1960-1968 was part of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Regiunea_Bra%C8%99ov&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Regiunea Brașov (page does not exist)">Brașov</a> Region, abolished in 1968 when Romania was reorganised based on counties rather than regions.</p>
<p>Sfântu Gheorghe is one of the centres for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sz%C3%A9kely" title="Székely">Székely</a> people in the region known to them as <a href="/wiki/Sz%C3%A9kely_Land" title="Székely Land">Székelyföld</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a> - which means "Székely Land", and is home to the Székely National Museum. The city hosts two market fairs each year.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sf%C3%A2ntu_Gheorghe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The predominant industry in the city is the <a href="/wiki/Textile" title="Textile">textile</a> industry. The city holds underutilized production capabilities such as a downsized automobile transmission parts and gearboxes factory (IMASA SA) and a tobacco factory (<a class="extiw" href="https://ro.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fabrica_de_%C8%9Aigarete_din_Sf%C3%A2ntu-Gheorghe" title="ro:Fabrica de Țigarete din Sfântu-Gheorghe">TIGARETE SA</a>). At the time of its closing SA was the last place where they produced iconic Romanian cigarette brands such as "Carpați", "Snagov" or "Mărășești".</p>
<p>Services sector contains growing areas such as IT services with ROMARG SRL the leading domain registrar and web hosting provider in Romania having its headquarters here.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sf%C3%A2ntu_Gheorghe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The main sport in the city is football. The city has a men football team called <i><a href="/wiki/ACS_Sepsi_OSK_Sf%C3%A2ntu_Gheorghe" title="ACS Sepsi OSK Sfântu Gheorghe">ACS Sepsi OSK Sfântu Gheorghe</a></i> (Sepsiszentgyörgyi OSK). In the 2015-2016 season the team promoted from the 3rd Division and currently play in the 2nd Division.</p>
<p>The city has also women basketball team called <i>ACS Sepsi SIC</i></p>
<ul>
<li>In the 2007/2008 season the team has finished in the 2nd place in the regular season, and lost the final (2-3) against BC ICIM Arad, but they win the Romanian Cup.</li>
<li>In the 2008/2009 season the team finished in the 3rd position after the regular season and they lost in the final (0-3) against MCM Târgoviste.</li>
<li>In the 2008/2009 season <i>LMK Sepsi BC</i> played in the FIBA EuroCup Women. In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EuroCup_Women_2008-09" title="EuroCup Women 2008-09">EuroCup Women 2008-09</a>, the team was drawn in Group B with Dynamo Kursk (Russia), Bnot Hasharon (Israel) and Challes-les-Eaux (France). They finished in the 3rd place with 3 wins and 3 losts. In the Sixteenth-Finals they meet Cras Basket Taranto (Italy) and they lost the both games.</li>
<li>LMK Sepsi BC participates in <a href="/wiki/EuroCup_Women" title="EuroCup Women">EuroCup Women 2009-10</a> and was drawn in Group G with <a href="/wiki/Dynamo_Moscow_(women%27s_basketball)" title="Dynamo Moscow (women's basketball)">Dynamo Moscow</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hapoel_Hatikva_Tel-Aviv&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hapoel Hatikva Tel-Aviv (page does not exist)">Hapoel Hatikva Tel-Aviv</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dunav_Econt_Rousse&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dunav Econt Rousse (page does not exist)">Dunav Econt Rousse</a>. They finished in the 3rd place with 2 wins and 4 defeats. In the Sixteenth-Finals they met <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mann_Filter_Zaragoza" title="Mann Filter Zaragoza">Mann Filter Zaragoza</a> and they lost the both games.</li>
<li>In the 2009/2010 season the team finished in the 2nd position after the regular season, but they lost in the semifinals of the play-off against BC ICIM Univ. Vasile Goldis Arad (1-2). They finished in the 3rd place beating CSM Satu Mare (2-0) and they won the bronze medals in the Romanian Championship and in the Romanian Cup too.</li>
<li>In the 2010/2011 season the team finished in the 3rd position after the regular season, they lost in the semifinals of the play-off against BC ICIM Univ. Vasile Goldis Arad (1-2). They finished in the 3rd place beating BCM Danzio Timisoara (2-0) and they won the bronze medals in the Romanian Championship and in the Romanian Cup too.</li>
</ul>
<p>In the 2015-2016 season the club wins the Romanian Cup and for the first time was the Romanian Championship beating in the Final CSU Alba Iulia (3-0).</p>
