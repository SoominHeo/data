<p><b>By2</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singaporean" title="Singaporean">Singaporean</a> duo based in <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> and signed under a 10-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Ocean_Butterflies_International" title="Ocean Butterflies International">Ocean Butterflies International</a>. The duo is composed of twin sisters, Miko Bai and Yumi Bai, they debuted with their first studio album, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NC_16_(By2_album)" title="NC 16 (By2 album)">NC 16</a></i>.</p>
<p>The duo gained attention at the start of their career with their first two studio albums, "<a href="/wiki/NC_16_(By2)" title="NC 16 (By2)">NC 16</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Twins_(By2_album)" title="Twins (By2 album)">Twins</a>", but it was not until 2010 when they released their third studio album, "<a href="/wiki/Grown_Up_(album)" title="Grown Up (album)">Grown Up</a>" they gained significant popularity, with the album topping Taiwan's music chart, "G-Music" and several music charts in China. The album was responsible for 33 percent sales of albums in the Chinese market and 18 percent sales of albums in Taiwan during the week of 9 April, to 15 April 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=By2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Their duo band was named "BY2" after their surname "Bai" and that they are twin sisters. Their father died of cancer in 2007 when Miko and Yumi were 15 years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-asiaOne2020_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-asiaOne2020-2">[2]</a></sup> Their father had hoped that Miko and Yumi will not give up on their dreams under any circumstances, thus encouraging them to actively pursue their aspiration of becoming singers.</p>
<p>These sisters have been given different <a href="/wiki/Hairstyle" title="Hairstyle">hairstyles</a> for easy identification due to their uncanny resemblance. However, they claimed to be different in terms of personality. Miko, Bai Wei-Fen has been described as more introverted and quiet while her sister, Yumi, Bai Wei-Ling, has been described as a more extroverted and outgoing person.</p>
<p>They moved to Taiwan in 2007 to further their career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yumi was in a relationship with Mario Ho (何猷君） in November 2014. However, they broke up later in February.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=By2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-debut">Pre-debut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=By2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pre-debut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From a young age, both sisters have been exposed to performing arts and have learned the violin, piano, ballet and many other styles of dancing. As they reached adolescence, their passion for performing grew and they picked up more performing skills. They joined the Chinese Folk Dance in their secondary school, Yio Chu Kang Secondary School, as part of their co-curricular activity.</p>
<p>At the age of 13, both of them joined the Ocean Butterflies' Music Forest's V Singer ("非常歌手") training course. The course aims to teach aspiring singers how to perform on stage and techniques in singing and dancing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-By2WeightLoss_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-By2WeightLoss-4">[4]</a></sup> They graduated from the course clinching a "Best Costume Design" award. Billy Koh of Ocean Butterflies Music noticed their talent and gave them a 10-year record deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> They were sent to other countries such as <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> to gain exposure to different forms of dance styles, as well as to enhance vocal training and stage presence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-By2WeightLoss_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-By2WeightLoss-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008_to_2009:_Debut_with_NC16_and_Twins">2008 to 2009: Debut with <i>NC16</i> and <i>Twins</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=By2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2008 to 2009: Debut with NC16 and Twins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 25 July 2008, By2 released their debut <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">Studio album</a>, <a href="/wiki/NC_16_(By2)" title="NC 16 (By2)">NC 16</a>, consisting of 11 songs in total, with composers under their label composing the songs for the album. On 10 April 2009, the duo released their second studio album, <a href="/wiki/Twins_(By2_album)" title="Twins (By2 album)">Twins (By2 album)</a>, with a total of 11 songs. This is the first album featuring a track written by the duo, titled "Don't Go Away", which was used as the opening theme for the Taiwanese airing of the <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korean</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_series" title="TV series">TV series</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_of_Eden_(TV_series)" title="East of Eden (TV series)">East of Eden</a>. The song was also released in an English version.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_to_2011:_Grown_Up.2C_Love_Always_and_90.27_Now">2010 to 2011: <i>Grown Up</i>, Love Always and <i>90' Now</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=By2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010 to 2011: Grown Up, Love Always and 90' Now">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 9 April 2010, By2 released their third studio album, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grown_Up_(By2_album)" title="Grown Up (By2 album)">Grown Up</a>. The album, was a commercial success, topping several album charts in mainland China and taking first place on Taiwan's G Music Chart. During the week of 9 to 15 April, the album was responsible for 33 percent of sales in the Mandarin language album market. The duo also went into writing, publishing their first book "Love Always" in 2011 with an Asia-wide release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The first edition was sold out in Taiwan within a week after the release date.</p>
<p>On 19 October 2011, the duo released their fourth studio album, 90' Now.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_to_2013:_2020_Love_You_Love_You_and_Paradise">2012 to 2013: <i>2020 Love You Love You</i> and <i>Paradise</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=By2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2012 to 2013: 2020 Love You Love You and Paradise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 3 August 2012, By2 released their first <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a>, <a href="/wiki/2020_Love_You_Love_You" title="2020 Love You Love You">2020 Love You Love You</a>, with a total of 4 tracks including the 2 promotional tracks, "2020 Love You Love You" and "You Don't Know Me". <i>When Love Walked In (爱情闯进门)</i> from the EP was featured as the opening theme of Chinese-Taiwanese drama of the same name, <a href="/wiki/When_Love_Walked_In" title="When Love Walked In">When Love Walked In</a>, which stars <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Song" title="Victoria Song">Victoria Song</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calvin_Chen" title="Calvin Chen">Calvin Chen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zhou_Mi_(singer)" title="Zhou Mi (singer)">Zhou Mi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-asiaOne2020_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-asiaOne2020-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2012, the duo represented Singapore in Chinese reality singing talent show, <a href="/wiki/Asian_Wave" title="Asian Wave">Asian Wave</a> (声动亚洲).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 18 September 2013, the duo released their fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Paradise_(By2_album)" title="Paradise (By2 album)">Paradise</a></i>. It peaked at number 6 on Taiwan's G-Music chart during the week of release from 14 to 20 September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> They held various meet and greet sessions around <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> and <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and also performed on various Taiwanese variety shows to promote the album and its two promotional singles, "No Reason" (沒理由) and "No More Tears" (不哭了).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_to_Present:_Cat_and_Mouse_and_Big_Mouth">2015 to Present: Cat and Mouse and Big Mouth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=By2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2015 to Present: Cat and Mouse and Big Mouth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 28, 2015, By2 released their sixth studio album, <a href="/wiki/Cat_and_Mouse_(By2_album)" title="Cat and Mouse (By2 album)">Cat and Mouse</a>. The duo appeared on various TV shows in China to promote the album. The twins also made numerous stops all around China to perform songs from their album.</p>
<p>On November 26, 2015, not too long after an album release, By2 released a new single for the Chinese-drama [妙女郎烏鴉嘴] (Funny Girl with a Big Mouth), on the Ocean Butterflies Music's official YouTube Channel<a class="external autonumber" href="https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCQLXNMjfZXnEEGYqVwX2CoA" rel="nofollow">[1]</a>, titled "Big Mouth" (烏鴉嘴). This single became the opening credits song for the drama.</p>
