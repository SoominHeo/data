<p><i><b>Spelunker</b></i> is a 1983 <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video game</a> developed by Tim Martin and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MicroGraphicImage" title="MicroGraphicImage">MicroGraphicImage</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Platform_game" title="Platform game">platform game</a> similar to <i><a href="/wiki/Pitfall!" title="Pitfall!">Pitfall!</a></i>.</p>
<p>Originally released by MicroGraphicImage for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atari_8-bit" title="Atari 8-bit">Atari 8-bit</a> computers in 1983, the game was later ported to the <a href="/wiki/Commodore_64" title="Commodore 64">Commodore 64</a> and re-released by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Br%C3%B8derbund" title="Brøderbund">Brøderbund</a> in 1984, with European publishing rights licensed to <a href="/wiki/Ariolasoft" title="Ariolasoft">Ariolasoft</a>. An <a href="/wiki/Arcade_game" title="Arcade game">arcade</a> version followed in 1985, and the game was released for the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_Entertainment_System" title="Nintendo Entertainment System">NES</a> on December 6, 1985 in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> and September 1987 in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, and for the <a href="/wiki/MSX" title="MSX">MSX</a> in 1986. A sequel was also released in arcades and for the NES on September 18, 1987 called <i><a href="/wiki/Spelunker_II:_Y%C5%ABsha_e_no_Ch%C5%8Dsen" title="Spelunker II: Yūsha e no Chōsen">Spelunker II: Yūsha e no Chōsen</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Irem" title="Irem">Irem</a>, in Japan only. The game was re-released for <a href="/wiki/Virtual_Console" title="Virtual Console">Virtual Console</a> in North America on March 17, 2008<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and in Europe on September 5, 2008 for the <a href="/wiki/Wii" title="Wii">Wii</a>, in both regions on June 6, 2013 for the <a href="/wiki/Wii_U" title="Wii U">Wii U</a> and in North America on June 27, 2013 for the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_3DS" title="Nintendo 3DS">Nintendo 3DS</a>.</p>

<p><i>Spelunker</i> is set in a colossal <a href="/wiki/Cave" title="Cave">cave</a>, with the player starting at the cave's entrance at the top, and the objective is to get to the fabulous treasure at the bottom. To achieve this, the player must walk and jump through increasingly challenging parts of the cave, all the while working with a finite supply of fresh air, which could be replenished at various points.</p>
<p>The cave's hazards include <a href="/wiki/Bat" title="Bat">bats</a>, which drop deadly <a href="/wiki/Guano" title="Guano">guano</a> on the player; and a <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">ghost</a> haunting the cave and appearing randomly, attempting to take the player to the <i>shadow world</i>. The player character can send a blast of air<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> to push the ghost away. However, this renders the player's character immobile for a few seconds, thus vulnerable to other dangers and further depleting their air supply. Objects to collect include sticks of <a href="/wiki/Dynamite" title="Dynamite">dynamite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flare" title="Flare">flares</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Key_(lock)" title="Key (lock)">keys</a>. Precise positioning and jumping play key factors in successfully completing the game.</p>
<p>The cave in <i>Spelunker</i> is divided into six levels. Although the levels connect seamlessly to each other, forming one large map, the game clearly signals a level change at certain points by showing the name of the next level and giving the player a bonus, consisting of an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra_life" title="Extra life">extra life</a> and a varying amount of points. Also, depending on memory, the next level or two were loaded from disk at that point.</p>
<p>The splash screen of the game on the original Atari features an excerpt of <a href="/wiki/Modest_Mussorgsky" title="Modest Mussorgsky">Modest Mussorgsky</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Pictures_at_an_Exhibition" title="Pictures at an Exhibition">Pictures at an Exhibition</a></i> as background music. All other versions of the game, including the Atari re-release feature a different title theme. The NES and MSX versions have additional music during gameplay.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spelunker_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Computer_Gaming_World" title="Computer Gaming World">Computer Gaming World</a></i> of 1985 called <i>Spelunker</i> "a thoroughly enjoyable game ... a class act".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stone198501_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-stone198501-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Remakes">Remakes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spelunker_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Remakes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sony Computer Entertainment of Japan has released <i><a href="/wiki/Spelunker_HD" title="Spelunker HD">Spelunker HD</a></i>, known in Japan as <i>Minna de Spelunker</i>, for the PlayStation 3 as a downloadable game through their <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Network" title="PlayStation Network">PlayStation Network</a> <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Store" title="PlayStation Store">Store</a>. It received the PlayStation Store best sales award in 2009 from Sony Computer Entertainment of Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The game is a remake of the NES port, with high definition graphics and 100 levels (10 large caves, with 10 levels in each).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, <a href="/wiki/Square_Enix" title="Square Enix">Square Enix</a> released a free-to-play sequel titled <i>Spelunker World</i> for <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_4" title="PlayStation 4">PlayStation 4</a> and <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Vita" title="PlayStation Vita">PlayStation Vita</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2017, a new title based on <i>Spelunker World</i> was announced for the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_Switch" title="Nintendo Switch">Nintendo Switch</a>, and will be released in spring 2017 in Japan under the title <i>Minna de Wai Wai! Spelunker</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">みんなでワイワイ! スペランカー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>lit. "Everyone's Crazy! Spelunker"</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
