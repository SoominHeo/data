<p><b>Scheveningen</b>, <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[ˈsxeːvənɪŋə(n)]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:Nl-Scheveningen.ogg" title="File:Nl-Scheveningen.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/55/Nl-Scheveningen.ogg" title="Nl-Scheveningen.ogg">listen</a>)</small> is one of the eight districts of <a href="/wiki/The_Hague" title="The Hague">The Hague</a>, as well as a subdistrict (<i>wijk</i>) of that city. Scheveningen is a modern <a href="/wiki/Seaside_resort" title="Seaside resort">seaside resort</a> with a long, sandy <a href="/wiki/Beach" title="Beach">beach</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Esplanade" title="Esplanade">esplanade</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Pier" title="Pier">pier</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Lighthouse" title="Lighthouse">lighthouse</a>. The beach is popular for water sports such as <a href="/wiki/Windsurfing" title="Windsurfing">windsurfing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kitesurfing" title="Kitesurfing">kiteboarding</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harbour" title="Harbour">harbour</a> is used for both fishing and tourism.</p>
<p>There are local politicians who are trying to re-brand Scheveningen to <b>The Hague Beach</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> on account of the name being difficult to pronounce, but this has created a lot of problems with the local population that is very proud of the name Scheveningen. Initiatives have been launched to teach people all over the world how to pronounce the name properly and make videos of saying the name out loud.</p>
<p>It has its own dialect (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Schevenings" title="Schevenings">Schevenings</a></i>), which is different from <a href="/wiki/The_Hague_dialect" title="The Hague dialect">The Hague dialect</a>.</p>

<p>The earliest reference to the name <i>Sceveninghe</i> goes back to around 1280.</p>
<p>The first inhabitants may have been <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxons" title="Anglo-Saxons">Anglo-Saxons</a>. Other <a href="/wiki/Historian" title="Historian">historians</a> favour a <a href="/wiki/Norsemen" title="Norsemen">Scandinavian</a> origin. <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">Fishing</a> was the main source of <a href="/wiki/Food" title="Food">food</a> and <a href="/wiki/Income" title="Income">income</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Scheveningen" title="Battle of Scheveningen">Battle of Scheveningen</a> was fought between English and Dutch fleets off the coast of the village on 10 August 1653. Thousands of people gathered on the shore to watch.</p>
<p>A road to neighbouring The Hague was constructed in 1663 (current name: Scheveningseweg).</p>
<p>In 1470, a heavy storm destroyed the church and half the houses. The village was again hit by storms in 1570, 1775, 1825, 1860, 1881, and 1894. After this last storm, the villagers decided to build a harbour. Until then, the fishing boats had had a flat bottom (<i>bomschuiten</i>), and were pulled up the beach. By around 1870, over 150 of these boats were in use. Once the harbour had been constructed in 1904, more modern ships replaced the <i>bomschuiten</i>.</p>
<p>In 1818, Jacob Pronk constructed a wooden building on a dune near the sea, from where people could bathe from four separate rooms. It marked the start of Scheveningen as a bathing resort. Since then, Scheveningen has attracted numerous tourists from all over Europe, notably from <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.</p>

<p>The hotel and restaurant <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurhaus_(Scheveningen)" title="Kurhaus (Scheveningen)">Kurhaus</a> was opened in 1886.</p>
<p>The picturesque village attracted many Dutch artists over the centuries who painted the <i>bomschuiten</i> drawn up on the beach, or fishermen at work in the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a>. Notable painters who recorded the village include <a href="/wiki/Adriaen_van_de_Velde" title="Adriaen van de Velde">Adriaen van de Velde</a>, <a href="/wiki/Simon_de_Vlieger" title="Simon de Vlieger">Simon de Vlieger</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hendrik_Willem_Mesdag" title="Hendrik Willem Mesdag">Hendrik Willem Mesdag</a>, whose enormous <a href="/wiki/Panorama_Mesdag" title="Panorama Mesdag">panorama</a>, 14 m high and 120 m wide, preserves the view of Scheveningen in 1881.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/International_Skating_Union" title="International Skating Union">International Skating Union</a> was founded in Scheveningen in 1892.</p>
<p>Anecdotal evidence exists of the name <i>Scheveningen</i> being used as a <a href="/wiki/Shibboleth" title="Shibboleth">shibboleth</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> to identify German spies: they would pronounce the initial "Sch" differently from Dutch native speakers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mcnamara_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-mcnamara-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Contrary to popular belief, Scheveningen was never an independent municipality, but it has its own coat of arms, officially recognised by The Hague local council (proposal 136 of 23 March 1984); even in the Middle Ages, it was part of the same administrative region as The Hague;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> the region had a special status within the county of Holland because of the presence of the <a href="/wiki/Count_of_Holland" title="Count of Holland">Count of Holland</a>.</p>
<p>Nevertheless, Scheveningen always had a strong identity of its own. For instance, it had its own football club, playing in the highest Dutch division (its name was "Scheveningen Holland Sport"). In the course of the second half of the former century, this club was forced to merge with <a href="/wiki/ADO_Den_Haag" title="ADO Den Haag">ADO Den Haag</a>.</p>

<p>From 21 April 1960,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> the pirate radio station <a href="/wiki/Radio_Veronica" title="Radio Veronica">Radio Veronica</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> broadcast its programmes from an anchorage in the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> about four miles off the Scheveningen coast, originally calling itself Vrije Radio Omroep Nederland (VRON); Free Radio Station [of the] Netherlands. It was joined by <a href="/wiki/Radio_North_Sea_International" title="Radio North Sea International">Radio Noordzee Internationaal</a> in 1970 <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and the relaunched <a href="/wiki/Radio_Caroline" title="Radio Caroline">Radio Caroline</a> in late 1972.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> When the Netherlands ratified the Treaty of Strasbourg on 1 September 1974,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Veronica applied for legal status and became the VOO, Caroline moved anchorage to the English coast, and RNI closed down completely.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Memorable episodes during this period included the stranding of Radio Veronica's ship, the <i>Norderney</i>, which lost its anchor in a storm and ran aground on Scheveningen beach on 2 April 1973, and a firebomb attack on RNI's ship, the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mebo_II" title="Mebo II">Mebo II</a></i>, on 15 May 1971.</p>

<p>Annual events include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winter_swim" title="Winter swim">Winter swim</a> on New Year's Day, locally known as <i>Nieuwjaarsduik</i> (New Year's dive)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Flag" title="Flag">Flags</a> Day in spring when the first new <a href="/wiki/Herring" title="Herring">herring</a> of the year is auctioned</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fireworks" title="Fireworks">Fireworks</a> in summer: once a week and several days during a festival week</li>
</ul>
<p>A visit to Scheveningen can include:</p>
<ul>
<li>The Muzee Museum (official museum of Scheveningen)</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Scheveningen_Pier" title="Scheveningen Pier">pier</a></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Miniature_park" title="Miniature park">miniature city</a> <a href="/wiki/Madurodam" title="Madurodam">Madurodam</a></li>
<li>The sculptures at sea museum <a href="/wiki/Beelden_aan_Zee" title="Beelden aan Zee">Beelden aan Zee</a></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Panorama_Mesdag" title="Panorama Mesdag">Panorama Mesdag</a></li>
<li>The four different beaches of Scheveningen<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li>Our Lady of Lourdes (RC) Silent Center, in a designated national landmark complex, housing a replica of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lourdes_Grotto" title="Lourdes Grotto">Lourdes Grotto</a> in Massabielle (France). The chapel, located at Berkenbosch Blokstraat 9a, is open daily from 9am to 6pm.</li>
</ul>
<p>Night life centres on <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Movie_theatres_in_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Movie theatres in the Netherlands (page does not exist)">Pathé Scheveningen movie theatre</a>, and the sea-front boulevard with its bars, restaurants, gambling halls, and other entertainment.</p>
