<p><b>Slagelse</b> is a small town in <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> located in west <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zealand_(Denmark)" title="Zealand (Denmark)">Zealand</a>. It is about 100¬†km (62¬†mi) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a>. The population is 32,333 (1 January 2015).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (January 2016)">not in citation given</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In the 11th century, Slagelse had a mint and was an important trading centre. The city has an 11th-century church, and nearby is <a href="/wiki/Trelleborg_(Slagelse)" title="Trelleborg (Slagelse)">Trelleborg</a>, a Viking fortress.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Hans_Christian_Andersen" title="Hans Christian Andersen">Hans Christian Andersen</a> studied in the grammar school in this city for a few years but described it as a "nuisance".</p>
<p>Slagelse is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Town_twinning" title="Town twinning">twinned</a> with <a href="/wiki/Aberdare" title="Aberdare">Aberdare</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stargard_Szczeci%C5%84ski" title="Stargard Szczeci≈Ñski">Stargard Szczeci≈Ñski</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>.</p>
