<p>The current form of the <a href="/wiki/National_flag" title="National flag">national</a> <b>flag of Slovakia</b> was adopted by <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a>'s Constitution, which came into force on 3 September 1992. The flag, <a href="/wiki/Pan-Slavic_colors" title="Pan-Slavic colors">in common</a> with other <a href="/wiki/Slavs" title="Slavs">Slavic</a> nations, uses the colors white, blue and red.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Slovakia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Slovakia's flag in its current form (but with another coat of arms on it or without any arms) can be dated back to the revolutionary year 1848<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> (see: <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Revolutions_of_1848_in_the_Habsburg_areas" title="The Revolutions of 1848 in the Habsburg areas">The Revolutions of 1848 in the Habsburg areas</a></i>). It was also used semi-officially in <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a> before <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slovak_Republic_(1939-1945)" title="Slovak Republic (1939-1945)">Slovak Republic</a> during World War II, and finally adopted (without the coat of arms) on 1 March 1990 as the flag of the Slovak Republic within Czechoslovakia. The coat of arms was added on 3 September 1992 and a special law describing the details of the flag followed in February 1993.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Slovakia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The blue triangle in the current <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_Czech_Republic" title="Flag of the Czech Republic">flag of the Czech Republic</a>, with which Slovakia formed Czechoslovakia up to the <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_Czechoslovakia" title="Dissolution of Czechoslovakia">dissolution of Czechoslovakia</a>, was taken over from the blue strip of Slovakia's flag in 1920 into the flag of Czechoslovakia. The flag of Czechoslovakia was taken over by the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a> in late 1992 in direct violation of the 1992 Act on the Division of Czechoslovakia explicitly forbidding state symbols to be used by the two successor states.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since the Slovak flag without the coat of arms is identical to that of the modern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_Russian_Federation" title="Flag of the Russian Federation">flag of the Russian Federation</a> and it can also be compared to the modern <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Slovenia" title="Flag of Slovenia">flag of Slovenia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Slovakia" title="Constitution of Slovakia">Constitution of Slovakia</a> added the <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms_of_Slovakia" title="Coat of arms of Slovakia">national coat of arms</a> in September 1992.</p>
