<p>The <b>National Assembly</b> (<a href="/wiki/Slovene_language" title="Slovene language">Slovene</a>: <span lang="sl" xml:lang="sl"><i>Državni zbor Republike Slovenije</i></span>, <small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Slovene" title="Help:IPA for Slovene">[dərˈʒàːwni ˈzbɔ́r rɛˈpúːblikɛ slɔˈʋèːnijɛ]</a></span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sp2001_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-sp2001-1">[1]</a></sup> or <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Slovene" title="Help:IPA for Slovene">[-ˈzbɔ̀ːr-]</a></span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sp2001_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-sp2001-1">[1]</a></sup> Slovene abbreviation <span lang="sl" xml:lang="sl"><i>DZ</i></span>), is the <a href="/wiki/Representative_democracy" title="Representative democracy">general representative body</a> of <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>. According to the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Slovenia" title="Constitution of Slovenia">Constitution of Slovenia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_Court_of_Slovenia" title="Constitutional Court of Slovenia">Constitutional Court of Slovenia</a>, it is the major part of the distinctively incompletely <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bicameral" title="Bicameral">bicameral</a> <a href="/wiki/Slovenian_Parliament" title="Slovenian Parliament">Slovenian Parliament</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legislative_branch" title="Legislative branch">legislative branch</a> of the Republic of Slovenia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-US2008_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-US2008-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It is unicameral. It has 90 members, elected for a four-year term. 88 members are elected using the <a href="/wiki/Party-list_proportional_representation" title="Party-list proportional representation">party-list proportional representation</a> system and the remaining two, using the <a href="/wiki/Borda_count" title="Borda count">Borda count</a>, by the <a href="/wiki/Slovenia#Hungarian_and_Italian" title="Slovenia">Hungarian and Italian-speaking</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ethnic_minorities" title="Ethnic minorities">ethnic minorities</a>, who have an absolute veto in matters concerning their ethnic groups.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tradition">Tradition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Assembly_(Slovenia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Tradition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Traditionally the representatives of the Italian and Hungarian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ethnic_minorities" title="Ethnic minorities">ethnic minorities</a> in the National Assembly are expected to support the coalition in power, regardless of its political composition, although they are not formally part of the coalition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Siol2012-02-26_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Siol2012-02-26-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
