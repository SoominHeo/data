<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Coat of arms of Slovenia</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>Slovenian coat of arms</b> consists of a red bordered blue shield on which there is a stylised white <a href="/wiki/Triglav" title="Triglav">Mount Triglav</a>, under which there are two wavy lines representing the sea and the rivers of the country. Above Mount Triglav, there are three golden six-pointed stars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It was designed in 1991 by <a href="/wiki/Marko_Poga%C4%8Dnik" title="Marko Pogačnik">Marko Pogačnik</a> and adopted on 24 June 1991.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coat_of_arms_of_Slovenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kingdom_of_Yugoslavia">Kingdom of Yugoslavia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coat_of_arms_of_Slovenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Kingdom of Yugoslavia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:173px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lesser_Coat_of_Arms_of_the_Kingdom_of_Yugoslavia.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="215" data-file-width="171" height="215" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5b/Lesser_Coat_of_Arms_of_the_Kingdom_of_Yugoslavia.png" width="171"/></a>

<p>Until the dissolution of <a href="/wiki/Austria-Hungary" title="Austria-Hungary">Austria-Hungary</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Slovene_Lands" title="Slovene Lands">Slovene Lands</a> did not have a coat of arms representing the whole nation, instead it had a different coat of arms for every land.</p>
<p>When the <a href="/wiki/State_of_Slovenes,_Croats_and_Serbs" title="State of Slovenes, Croats and Serbs">State of Slovenes, Croats and Serbs</a> merged with <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Serbia" title="Kingdom of Serbia">Kingdom of Serbia</a>, Slovenia got the <a href="/wiki/Emblem_of_Yugoslavia" title="Emblem of Yugoslavia">first real coat of arms</a>, which actually was a heavily modified one from the Serbian Kingdom. The coat of arms presents the Serbian shield with white cross on the left, the Croatian shield on the right and under both there is a blue shield representing Slovenes. An image of the royal Yugoslav coat of arms can be seen on the 10-<a href="/wiki/Yugoslav_dinar" title="Yugoslav dinar">Yugoslav dinar</a> banknote of 1926.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Socialist_Republic_of_Slovenia">Socialist Republic of Slovenia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coat_of_arms_of_Slovenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Socialist Republic of Slovenia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The coat of arms of the Socialist Republic of Slovenia was designed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Branko_Sim%C4%8Di%C4%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Branko Simčič (page does not exist)">Branko Simčič</a> on the basis of the symbol of the <a href="/wiki/Liberation_Front_of_the_Slovene_Nation" title="Liberation Front of the Slovene Nation">Liberation Front of the Slovene Nation</a>. The sea and the mount <a href="/wiki/Triglav" title="Triglav">Triglav</a> motive appeared in the coat of arms of the Socialist Republic of Slovenia, one of six constituent republics of the <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" title="Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia">Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia</a>. The former coat of arms was rounded by <a href="/wiki/Wheat" title="Wheat">wheat</a> with <a href="/wiki/Tilia" title="Tilia">linden</a> leaves and featured a <a href="/wiki/Red_star" title="Red star">red star</a> at the top. There was also the other coat of arms which presented Yugoslavia as a whole.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Republic_of_Slovenia">Republic of Slovenia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coat_of_arms_of_Slovenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Republic of Slovenia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The tender for the design of the new coat of arms of Slovenia was published in May 1991. It was won by Marko Pogačnik. He based his design on the understanding of Slovenia as a macroregional entity and on the work by <a href="/wiki/France_Pre%C5%A1eren" title="France Prešeren">France Prešeren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jo%C5%BEe_Ple%C4%8Dnik" title="Jože Plečnik">Jože Plečnik</a>. In regard, to Prešeren, the coat of arms depicts the description of nature given by the poet in his <a href="/wiki/Epic_poetry" title="Epic poetry">epic</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lyric_poetry" title="Lyric poetry">lyric</a> poem <i><a href="/wiki/The_Baptism_on_the_Savica" title="The Baptism on the Savica">The Baptism on the Savica</a></i>. In regard to Plečnik, the coat of arms follows the coat of arms of Slovenia, carved upon the design by thea architect in 1934 at the column of the Virgin Mary, which stands in front of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Martin%27s_Parish_Church,_Bled" title="St. Martin's Parish Church, Bled">parish church in Bled</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pogačnik's design was proclaimed the new official coat of arms of Slovenia with the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Slovenia" title="Constitution of Slovenia">constitutional amendment</a> C 100 and became valid on 24 June 1991.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Symbolics">Symbolics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coat_of_arms_of_Slovenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Symbolics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Grb_Republike_Slovenije_-_geometrijsko_pravilo.GIF"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="653" data-file-width="396" height="330" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Grb_Republike_Slovenije_-_geometrijsko_pravilo.GIF/200px-Grb_Republike_Slovenije_-_geometrijsko_pravilo.GIF" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Grb_Republike_Slovenije_-_geometrijsko_pravilo.GIF/300px-Grb_Republike_Slovenije_-_geometrijsko_pravilo.GIF 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/74/Grb_Republike_Slovenije_-_geometrijsko_pravilo.GIF 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Grb_Republike_Slovenije_-_geometrijsko_pravilo.GIF" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The geometrical rule for the construction of the national coat of arms of Slovenia</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The designer, <a href="/wiki/Marko_Poga%C4%8Dnik" title="Marko Pogačnik">Marko Pogačnik</a>, has described the coat of arms as a <a href="/wiki/Cosmogram" title="Cosmogram">cosmogram</a>, which creates an energetic field protecting the country and stimulating its inner potentials.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to Pogačnik, the Triglav symbolises the male principle. There are two white bending lines below it, representing the <a href="/wiki/Triglav_Lakes_Valley" title="Triglav Lakes Valley">Triglav Lakes Valley</a> and the Slovene sea and rivers in general, or the female principle. Above Triglav, there are three golden, six-pointed stars, forming a triangle and symbolising <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a>. The stars are taken from the coat of arms of the <a href="/wiki/Counts_of_Celje" title="Counts of Celje">Counts of Celje</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_status">Legal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coat_of_arms_of_Slovenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Legal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a work of arts, published in the official journal <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Official_Gazette_(Slovenia)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Official Gazette (Slovenia) (page does not exist)">Official Gazette</a></i>, the national coat of arms of Slovenia qualifies as an official work and is per Article 9 of the Slovene Copyright and Related Rights Act not protected by the copyrights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Its usage is regulated by the <i>Act Regulating the Coat-of-Arms, Flag and Anthem of the Republic of Slovenia and the Flag of the Slovene Nation</i>, published in the Official Gazette in 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coat_of_arms_of_Slovenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The coat of arms of the Republic of Slovenia has been criticised by the herald Aleksander Hribovšek as heraldically lacking in a number of views. For example, he has stated that it has no official <a href="/wiki/Blazon" title="Blazon">blazon</a>, and its form of shield is not heraldically recognised. Mount Triglav and the sea are represented incorrectly, and he criticised the choice of Triglav as a symbol.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
