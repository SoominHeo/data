<p>The office of <b>President of the <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Republic of Slovenia</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Slovene_language" title="Slovene language">Slovene</a>: <span lang="sl" xml:lang="sl"><i>Predsednik Republike Slovenije</i></span>) was established on 23 December 1991, when the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_(Slovenia)" title="National Assembly (Slovenia)">National Assembly</a> passed a new <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Slovenia" title="Constitution of Slovenia">Constitution</a> as a result of independence from <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" title="Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia">Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia</a>.</p>
<p>Although entrusted with limited power by the Constitution, in practice the position is mostly ceremonial. Among other things the <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">President</a> is also the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commander-in-Chief" title="Commander-in-Chief">Commander-in-Chief</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Slovenian_Armed_Forces" title="Slovenian Armed Forces">Slovenian Armed Forces</a>. The office of the President is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Presidential_Palace,_Ljubljana" title="Presidential Palace, Ljubljana">Presidential Palace</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ljubljana" title="Ljubljana">Ljubljana</a>. The President of Slovenia is directly elected by universal adult suffrage once every five years. Any Slovenian citizen can run for president, but can hold only two consecutive terms in office.</p>
