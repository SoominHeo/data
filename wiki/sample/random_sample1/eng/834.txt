<p><b>Charlie S. Lee</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (born February 24, 1971), known professionally as <b>C. S. Lee</b>, is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_American" title="Korean American">Korean American</a> actor. He is perhaps best known for playing sex-obsessed forensics analyst <a href="/wiki/Vince_Masuka" title="Vince Masuka">Vince Masuka</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a> drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Dexter_(TV_series)" title="Dexter (TV series)">Dexter</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=C._S._Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lee was born in Cheongju, South Korea. Film became his passion in his junior year of high school while playing football at <a href="/wiki/Hudson%27s_Bay_High_School" title="Hudson's Bay High School">Hudson's Bay High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver,_Washington" title="Vancouver, Washington">Vancouver, Washington</a>. Lee attended <a href="/wiki/Cornish_College_of_the_Arts" title="Cornish College of the Arts">Cornish College of the Arts</a> on an acting scholarship and graduated with a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Fine_Arts" title="Bachelor of Fine Arts">Bachelor of Fine Arts</a> degree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He graduated from the <a href="/wiki/Yale_School_of_Drama" title="Yale School of Drama">Yale School of Drama</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Fine_Arts" title="Master of Fine Arts">Master of Fine Arts</a> degree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Lee then spent eight years in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> acting with various theater companies, regional theater, as well as television and film. He is married to Lara Cho.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=C._S._Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lee was made a series regular cast member on the <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a> drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Dexter_(TV_series)" title="Dexter (TV series)">Dexter</a></i>, playing the role of <a href="/wiki/Vince_Masuka" title="Vince Masuka">Vince Masuka</a>, a forensic scientist known for his raunchy sense of humor.</p>
<p>Outside <i>Dexter</i>, Lee played Dr. Ba on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Home_Box_Office" title="Home Box Office">HBO</a> drama series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sopranos" title="The Sopranos">The Sopranos</a></i>. He plays a doctor in the 2009 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Unborn_(2009_film)" title="The Unborn (2009 film)">The Unborn</a></i>. Lee has also appeared as Harry Tang, Chuck's officious co-worker, in the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> comedy-spy series <i><a href="/wiki/Chuck_(TV_series)" title="Chuck (TV series)">Chuck</a></i> and as a deputy in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Monk_(TV_series)" title="Monk (TV series)">Monk</a></i>, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mr._Monk_Is_on_the_Run_(Part_One)" title="Mr. Monk Is on the Run (Part One)">Mr. Monk Is on the Run (Part One)</a>". He can also be seen as the office worker holding the boombox in the award-winning <a href="/wiki/Nextel_Communications" title="Nextel Communications">Nextel</a> <a href="/wiki/Television_advertisement" title="Television advertisement">commercial</a>, "Nextel Dance Party". In the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> Series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Unit_(TV_series)" title="The Unit (TV series)">The Unit</a></i>, Lee plays a small part as a South Korean Submariner in episode 13 of the second season.</p>
<p>He also provides the voice of Jin Jae-Hoon and Illuminati Pyramidion in the video game, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Secret_World" title="The Secret World">The Secret World</a></i>. Overheard referencing various memes and other playful quips.</p>
