<p><i><b>Shōso Strip</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">勝訴ストリップ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, "Lawsuit Winning Strip")</span> is <a href="/wiki/Ringo_Sheena" title="Ringo Sheena">Ringo Sheena</a>'s second album, released on March 31, 2000, by Toshiba EMI. The limited edition version includes a special booklet and case. The album debuted at #1 and has sold over 2,332,000 copies. It was certified two million copies by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIAJ" title="RIAJ">RIAJ</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RIAJ-jun2000_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-RIAJ-jun2000-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sh%C5%8Dso_Strip&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sheena began to record <i>Shōso Strip</i> from the summer of 1999. Since she thought that the track list was almost the same as <i><a href="/wiki/Muzai_Moratorium" title="Muzai Moratorium">Muzai Moratorium</a></i>, she started with a clean slate. There were several songs which were not recorded because of her busy schedule,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (February 2013)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> but had been completed in her head. She decided to record new songs and replaced some of those old songs for the album with them. The recording was finished during that same summer.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (February 2013)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2012, the song "Tsuki ni Makeinu" was covered by <a href="/wiki/Nanou" title="Nanou">Nanou</a> on his album <i>Unsung</i>.</p>
