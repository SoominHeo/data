<p><b>Yang Seung-ho</b> (양승호; born October 16, 1987),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Russell2014_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Russell2014-1">[1]</a></sup> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Korean_idol" title="South Korean idol">South Korean idol</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a>, dancer, actor, model and the leader of Korean <a href="/wiki/Boy_band" title="Boy band">male group</a> <a href="/wiki/MBLAQ" title="MBLAQ">MBLAQ</a>, currently active in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> under <a href="/wiki/J._Tune_Camp" title="J. Tune Camp">J. Tune Camp</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Seung-ho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-debut">Pre-debut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Seung-ho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-debut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When Yang was young, Yang won a couple of singing contests that he joined. In addition, Yang has had a television appearance in <i>Sharp 1 (반올림1; Banolim 1)</i> as a cameo in between 2003 and 2005. He was also featured in its soundtrack entitled "Gray Sky" by Lee Hyun-jung.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="MBLAQ">MBLAQ</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Seung-ho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: MBLAQ">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/MBLAQ" title="MBLAQ">MBLAQ</a></div>
<p>After training under <a href="/wiki/Rain_(entertainer)" title="Rain (entertainer)">Rain</a> and <a href="/wiki/J._Tune_Entertainment" title="J. Tune Entertainment">J. Tune Entertainment</a>, Yang became a member of <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> quintet <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boy_group" title="Boy group">male group</a> MBLAQ, serving as their leader, lead dancer and vocalist. The group made their unofficial debut on October 9, 2009 and officially debut on October 15, 2009 during Rain's <i>Legend of Rainism</i> concert, where they performed various songs from their then-unreleased single album <i><a href="/wiki/Just_BLAQ" title="Just BLAQ">Just BLAQ</a></i>. Their performance that day was met with praise, with many concert spectators and fans considering them to be the next <a href="/wiki/TVXQ" title="TVXQ">TVXQ</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-concert_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-concert-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-debut_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-debut-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>MBLAQ's first single, "Oh Yeah", was released to the public through a music video on October 14, 2009 together with their debut single album, <i>Just BLAQ</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Afterwards, they had their broadcast debut on <a href="/wiki/Mnet_(TV_channel)" title="Mnet (TV channel)">Mnet</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M!_Countdown" title="M! Countdown">M! Countdown</a>, performing their single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mcountdown_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-mcountdown-5">[5]</a></sup> The group also made their Japanese debut on May 3, 2011. They held an event at the Kanagawa Lazona Kawasaki Plaza, which gathered a reported 10,000 fans. A day later, on May 4, they released their first Japanese album <i><a href="/wiki/Your_Luv" title="Your Luv">Your Luv</a></i>. The album immediately reached first position at the <i>Oricon Daily Charts</i>, selling more that 11,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The group has starred in various South Korean variety shows as guests. They also had their own variety shows such as Mnet's <i>MBLAQ - The Art of Seduction</i>, <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">MBC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Idol_Show#Season_5" title="Idol Show">MBLAQ's Idol Army</a></i>, Mnet's <i>Sesame Player</i> and <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">KBS</a>'s <i>Hello Baby Season 5</i>.</p>
<p>Since their debut, MBLAQ has released three single albums, six <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mini-album" title="Mini-album">extended play</a> albums, one studio album, one repackaged studio album, and two EPs in Korean. They also released one compilation album, four singles and four DVDs in Japanese.</p>
<p>His fanclub is named: Seungholic</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_activities">Other activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Seung-ho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Besides the group activities, Yang also guests on various television shows as a solo artist as well. These include <i>Super Junior Miracle</i>, <i>Idol Maknae Rebellion</i>, <i>Kara Bakery</i>, and, <i><a href="/wiki/Running_Man_(TV_series)" title="Running Man (TV series)">Running Man</a></i> among others.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Seung-ho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yang Seung-ho was born on October 16, 1987 in Seoul. His family consists of a mother, a father, and a younger brother named Yang Seung-hoon. When he was young, his family moved to <a href="/wiki/Anyang,_Gyeonggi" title="Anyang, Gyeonggi">Anyang, Gyeonggi</a> and was raised there. He attended Anyang High School of Arts, where he became the school captain and class president during that time. After high school, he enrolled at the <a href="/wiki/Sejong_University" title="Sejong University">Sejong University of Film Arts</a> but left afterwards due to MBLAQ's busy schedule. He now attends at the <a href="/wiki/Kyung_Hee_Cyber_University" title="Kyung Hee Cyber University">Kyung Hee Cyber University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Seungho is the only member in the group who uses his real name; his stage name was originally intended to be "Mir" but he rejected it.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> His hobbies include playing the guitar and piano (for which MBLAQ's fans gave him the nickname "Yang Chopin"). Other hobbies include playing soccer, <a href="/wiki/Beatboxing" title="Beatboxing">beatboxing</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Break_dancing" title="Break dancing">break dancing</a>, performing card tricks, knitting, and mending objects.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Yang had also taken classes in gymnastics, <a href="/wiki/Judo" title="Judo">judo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hapkido" title="Hapkido">hapkido</a>, whilst his favorite genre of music is electronic.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Seungho and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soyeon" title="Soyeon">Soyeon</a> from <a href="/wiki/T-ara" title="T-ara">T-ara</a> attended the same high school and are known to be good friends.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He also knew <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Park_Gyuri" title="Park Gyuri">Park Gyuri</a> from <a href="/wiki/Kara_(South_Korean_band)" title="Kara (South Korean band)">Kara</a> and Jinon from <a href="/wiki/F.Cuz" title="F.Cuz">F.Cuz</a> in his junior years.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
