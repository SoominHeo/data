<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Enthalpy" title="Enthalpy">enthalpy</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sublimation_(physics)" title="Sublimation (physics)">sublimation</a></b>, or <b>heat of sublimation</b>, is the <a href="/wiki/Heat" title="Heat">heat</a> required to change one <a href="/wiki/Mole_(unit)" title="Mole (unit)">mole</a> of a substance from <a href="/wiki/State_of_matter#The_four_fundamental_states" title="State of matter">solid state</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaseous_state" title="Gaseous state">gaseous state</a> at a given combination of <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperature</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pressure" title="Pressure">pressure</a>, usually <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Standard_temperature_and_pressure" title="Standard temperature and pressure">standard temperature and pressure</a> (STP). The heat of sublimation is usually expressed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kilojoule" title="Kilojoule">kJ</a>/mol, although the less customary kJ/kg is also encountered.</p>
