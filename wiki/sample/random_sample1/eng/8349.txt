<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Visual effects</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>In filmmaking, <b>visual effects</b> (abbreviated <b>VFX</b>) are the processes by which imagery is created and/or manipulated outside the context of a <a href="/wiki/Live_action" title="Live action">live action</a> shot.</p>
<p>Visual effects involve the integration of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live-action" title="Live-action">live-action</a> footage and generated imagery to create environments which look realistic, but would be dangerous, expensive, impractical, or impossible to capture on film. Visual effects using <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computer_generated_imagery" title="Computer generated imagery">computer generated imagery</a> have recently become accessible to the independent filmmaker with the introduction of affordable and easy-to-use <a href="/wiki/Animation" title="Animation">animation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Compositing" title="Compositing">compositing</a> software.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Timing">Timing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Visual_effects&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Timing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Visual effects are often integral to a movie's story and appeal. Although most visual effects work is completed during <a href="/wiki/Post-production" title="Post-production">post-production</a>, it usually must be carefully planned and choreographed in <a href="/wiki/Pre-production" title="Pre-production">pre-production</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_production" title="Film production">production</a>. Visual effects primarily executed in Post-Production with the use of multiple tools and technologies such as graphic design, modeling, animation and similar software, while <a href="/wiki/Special_effect" title="Special effect">special effects</a> such as explosions and car chases are made on <a href="/wiki/Theatrical_scenery" title="Theatrical scenery">set</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Visual_effects_supervisor" title="Visual effects supervisor">visual effects supervisor</a> is usually involved with the production from an early stage to work closely with production and the film's <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">director</a> design, guide and lead the teams required to achieve the desired effects.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Categories">Categories</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Visual_effects&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Categories">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Visual effects may be divided into at least four categories:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Matte_painting" title="Matte painting">Matte paintings</a> and stills: digital or traditional paintings or photographs which serve as background plates for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keying_(graphics)" title="Keying (graphics)">keyed</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rotoscope" title="Rotoscope">rotoscoped</a> elements.</li>
<li>Live-action effects: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keying_(graphics)" title="Keying (graphics)">keying</a> actors or models through <a href="/wiki/Chroma_key" title="Chroma key">bluescreening</a> and greenscreening.</li>
<li>Digital animation: <a href="/wiki/3D_computer_graphics" title="3D computer graphics">modeling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_graphics_lighting" title="Computer graphics lighting">computer graphics lighting</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texture_mapping" title="Texture mapping">texturing</a>, rigging, animating, and <a href="/wiki/Rendering_(computer_graphics)" title="Rendering (computer graphics)">rendering</a> computer-generated <a href="/wiki/3D_computer_graphics" title="3D computer graphics">3D characters</a>, particle effects, digital sets, backgrounds.</li>
<li><b>Digital effects</b> (commonly shortened to <b>digital FX</b> or <b>FX</b>) are the various processes by which imagery is created and/or manipulated with or from photographic assets. Digital effects often involve the integration of still photography and <a href="/wiki/Computer-generated_imagery" title="Computer-generated imagery">computer-generated imagery</a> (CGI) to create environments which look realistic but would be dangerous, costly, or impossible to capture in camera. FX is usually associated with the still photography world in contrast to visual effects which is associated with motion film production.</li>
</ul>
