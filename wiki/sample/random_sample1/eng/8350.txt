<p>The <b>Time War</b>, more specifically called <b>the Last Great Time War</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dalek_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dalek-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gridlock_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-gridlock-3">[3]</a></sup> is a conflict within the fictional <a href="/wiki/Whoniverse" title="Whoniverse">universe</a> of the British <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction_on_television" title="Science fiction on television">science fiction television</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">Doctor Who</a></i>. The conflict pitted the <a href="/wiki/Time_Lord" title="Time Lord">Time Lords</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Dalek" title="Dalek">Daleks</a> and culminated in the apparent mutual destruction of both races, caused by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctor_(Doctor_Who)" title="Doctor (Doctor Who)">the Doctor</a>.</p>
<p>In the <i>Doctor Who</i> continuity, the war occurs between the events of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctor_Who_(1996_film)" title="Doctor Who (1996 film)">1996 film</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who_(series_1)" title="Doctor Who (series 1)">2005 revived series</a>. It was frequently mentioned and alluded to from the first series of the show's return,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-end_of_world_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-end_of_world-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unquiet_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-unquiet-5">[5]</a></sup> but was not directly seen until the show's 50th anniversary special.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dotd_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-dotd-1">[1]</a></sup> The war's events and progression have never been fully explained. Short comments in various episodes act as hints, but the war was not thoroughly talked about until the 2007 episode "<a href="/wiki/The_Sound_of_Drums" title="The Sound of Drums">The Sound of Drums</a>";<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-drums_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-drums-6">[6]</a></sup> part two of the two-part special <i><a href="/wiki/The_End_of_Time_(Doctor_Who)" title="The End of Time (Doctor Who)">The End of Time</a></i> (2009–2010)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-end_of_time_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-end_of_time-7">[7]</a></sup> provided further information and revealed the Time Lord leaders' sinister endgame. The closing battles and the climax of the Time War were finally depicted in the 50th anniversary special "<a href="/wiki/The_Day_of_the_Doctor" title="The Day of the Doctor">The Day of the Doctor</a>" which ultimately revealed the Time Lords' fate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dotd_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-dotd-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Last_Great_Time_War">The Last Great Time War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The Last Great Time War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Last Great Time War pitted the <a href="/wiki/Time_Lord" title="Time Lord">Time Lords</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gallifrey" title="Gallifrey">Gallifrey</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Dalek" title="Dalek">Daleks</a> of <a href="/wiki/Skaro" title="Skaro">Skaro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dalek_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Dalek-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[note 1]</a></sup> The specific incident that sparked the conflict remains unclear, but according to executive producer <a href="/wiki/Russell_T_Davies" title="Russell T Davies">Russell T Davies</a>, the origins dated back to conflicts between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctor_(Doctor_Who)" title="Doctor (Doctor Who)">the Doctor</a> and the Daleks. In <i><a href="/wiki/Genesis_of_the_Daleks" title="Genesis of the Daleks">Genesis of the Daleks</a></i> (1975), the Time Lords—having foreseen the possibility of the Daleks conquering the universe—send the <a href="/wiki/Fourth_Doctor" title="Fourth Doctor">Fourth Doctor</a> into the past in an attempt to avert the Daleks' creation, affect their development to make them less aggressive or discover an inherent weakness they could exploit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-genesis_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-genesis-11">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In retaliation for this ultimately unsuccessful mission, the Daleks attempt to infiltrate the High Council of the Time Lords with duplicates of the <a href="/wiki/Fifth_Doctor" title="Fifth Doctor">Fifth Doctor</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Resurrection_of_the_Daleks" title="Resurrection of the Daleks">Resurrection of the Daleks</a></i> (1984), followed by an open declaration of hostilities by one of the Dalek Emperors in <i><a href="/wiki/Remembrance_of_the_Daleks" title="Remembrance of the Daleks">Remembrance of the Daleks</a></i> (1988).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meet_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Meet-12">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Two specific events led up to the outbreak of the war: a peace treaty was attempted by President <a href="/wiki/Romana_(Doctor_Who)" title="Romana (Doctor Who)">Romana</a> under the "Act of Master Restitution". This attempt was followed by the "Etra Prime Incident" (<i><a href="/wiki/The_Apocalypse_Element" title="The Apocalypse Element">The Apocalypse Element</a></i>), which some say "began the escalation of events". Weapons used by the Time Lords included Bowships, Black Hole Carriers and N-Forms (the last from Davies' 1996 New Adventures novel <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Damaged_Goods_(Doctor_Who_novel)" title="Damaged Goods (Doctor Who novel)">Damaged Goods</a></i>), while the Daleks wielded "the full might of the Deathsmiths of Goth" (from the comic strip story <i>Black Legacy</i> by <a href="/wiki/Alan_Moore" title="Alan Moore">Alan Moore</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Lloyd_(comics)" title="David Lloyd (comics)">David Lloyd</a>), and launched a massive fleet led by the Emperor into the vortex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meet_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Meet-12">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Progression">Progression</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Progression">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 'duration' and extent of the War remains unclear. The statement made by <a href="/wiki/Jack_Harkness" title="Jack Harkness">Jack Harkness</a> in "<a href="/wiki/The_Parting_of_the_Ways" title="The Parting of the Ways">The Parting of the Ways</a>" – confirmed by the Doctor – that the Daleks "vanished out of time and space" to fight the War would indicate that it was not waged in normal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space-time" title="Space-time">space-time</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Parting_of_the_Ways_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Parting_of_the_Ways-18">[17]</a></sup> Nonetheless, there was fighting on the planet Gallifrey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dotd_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-dotd-1">[1]</a></sup> At some point, however, the conflict engulfed a sufficient bulk of the cosmos for Cass, a character from "Night of the Doctor", to declare that the Daleks and Time Lords had not yet succeeded in their efforts to destroy it - some of it was "still standing".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-night_of_the_doctor_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-night_of_the_doctor-19">[18]</a></sup> Several races hostile to the Time Lords, e.g. the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sontarans" title="Sontarans">Sontarans</a>, wished to participate but were forbidden to do so.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Doctor claims to have fought on the front lines and was present at the Fall of Arcadia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dotd_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-dotd-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-doomsday_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-doomsday-21">[20]</a></sup> Arcadia was Gallifrey's second city fortified with four hundred "sky trenches". These were thought to be impregnable until an invading Dalek squadron swept them away (as seen in the 2013 minisode "<a href="/wiki/The_Last_Day_(Doctor_Who)" title="The Last Day (Doctor Who)">The Last Day</a>").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2013 minisode "<a href="/wiki/The_Night_of_the_Doctor" title="The Night of the Doctor">The Night of the Doctor</a>" reveals that at first the Doctor (at this point the <a href="/wiki/Eighth_Doctor" title="Eighth Doctor">Eighth Doctor</a>) refused to take part in the war, even as it spiralled out of control and threatened to destroy the universe, instead working to help where he could but refusing to fight. This led him to attempt to save a woman from a spaceship crashing towards the planet Karn, who refused his aid because he was a Time Lord (as she claimed she couldn't "tell the difference" between the two species any more, implying that the Time Lords were just as destructive as the Daleks). The Doctor was killed in the crash, but was restored to life temporarily by the Sisterhood of Karn, who finally convinced him that for the sake of the universe, he had to take a stand and fight. They further offered an elixir that would control his regeneration and allow him to take a form best suited for the task. The Doctor accepted, remarking that there was no need for a Doctor in a universe consumed by war and requested that the Sisterhood make him a "warrior". He drank the elixir and regenerated into the <a href="/wiki/War_Doctor" title="War Doctor">War Doctor</a>, declaring himself "Doctor no more."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-night_of_the_doctor_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-night_of_the_doctor-19">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Davros" title="Davros">Davros</a>, the creator of the Daleks, also fought during the war after his creations, which had turned against him during <i><a href="/wiki/Genesis_of_the_Daleks" title="Genesis of the Daleks">Genesis of the Daleks</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-genesis_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-genesis-11">[10]</a></sup> but caused his revival in <i><a href="/wiki/Destiny_of_the_Daleks" title="Destiny of the Daleks">Destiny of the Daleks</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[22]</a></sup> rehabilitated him to a leadership position. In the first year of the War, Davros' command ship was seemingly destroyed at the Gates of Elysium after flying into the jaws of the Nightmare Child. Unbeknownst to the Doctor, who had tried to save him, Davros was rescued by <a href="/wiki/Cult_of_Skaro#Dalek_Caan" title="Cult of Skaro">Dalek Caan</a>, who had escaped the events of "<a href="/wiki/Evolution_of_the_Daleks" title="Evolution of the Daleks">Evolution of the Daleks</a>" (2007) via an emergency temporal shift.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-evolution_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-evolution-24">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stolen_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-stolen-25">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>The war resulted in countless millions dying endless deaths, as time travel was used by both sides to reverse battles that caused massive fatalities on both sides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-end_of_time_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-end_of_time-7">[7]</a></sup> These excesses of temporal warfare eventually led to the whole of the conflict becoming "time-locked", so that no time traveller could go back into it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stolen_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-stolen-25">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[note 2]</a></sup> The Doctor described the final days of the war as "hell", featuring "the Skaro Degradations, the Horde of Travesties, the Nightmare Child, the Could-Have-Been King with his army of Meanwhiles and Never-Weres".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-end_of_time_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-end_of_time-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>As the war progressed the Time Lords became increasingly aggressive and unscrupulous. Growing in desperation, they accessed a cache of forbidden doomsday weapons fashioned by the ancients of Gallifrey known as the Omega Arsenal. All were wielded against the Dalek menace save one: "the Moment".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dotd_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-dotd-1">[1]</a></sup> Moreover, they resurrected <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Master_(Doctor_Who)" title="Master (Doctor Who)">the Master</a>, a renegade Time Lord and nemesis to the Doctor, as they believed him to be the "perfect warrior for a time war". However, after the <a href="/wiki/Dalek_variants#Dalek_Emperor" title="Dalek variants">Dalek Emperor</a> gained control of the Cruciform, the Master deserted his post, used the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Doctor_Who_items#C" title="List of Doctor Who items">chameleon arch</a> to disguise himself as a human and escaped to a time period shortly before the end of the universe. Genetically a human, he escaped the destruction of all Time Lords as well as detection by the Doctor – who was unaware of his resurrection in the first place. The Master also remained ignorant of the latter phase and outcome of the war until he emerged from hiding, when he was told by the Doctor many years later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-drums_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-drums-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Leadership among the Time Lords remained vague during the earlier phase of the war. Ultimately, <a href="/wiki/Rassilon" title="Rassilon">Rassilon</a>, founder of the Time Lord society and its time travel technology who had discovered the secret of immortality, returned to assume leadership as Lord President, a position he was first to hold.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs additional references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">additional citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Refusing the possibility of his civilization being destroyed by the Daleks, Rassilon prepared a doomsday scenario, the so-called "Ultimate Sanction". This genocidal scheme included sacrificing all of time itself, thereby destroying the Daleks and all life in the universe. The Time Lords themselves would have transcended into a non-corporeal collective consciousness that would be the only sentient form of life in existence. The Time Lords, apparently hardened by the horrors of war, gave near-unanimous support for this plan – only two Time Lords dissented when the issue was put to a full vote. Prior to the vote, when one of the Council, the Partisan, suggested shelving the Ultimate Sanction and that it may be better if Gallifrey were destroyed, Rassilon disintegrated her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-end_of_time_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-end_of_time-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>While the High Council continued to lead Time Lord society during the war, a separate War Council was tasked with overseeing the war itself, as well as Gallifrey's defenses. The War Council was led by an unknown Time Lord general. During the last days of the Time War, the War Council apparently became disillusioned with the High Council.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dotd_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-dotd-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Conclusion">Conclusion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Conclusion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the last day of the Time War, the Dalek fleets surrounding Gallifrey launched what was said to be their biggest attack ever on the planet. The ten million Dalek ships subjected Gallifrey to a massive orbital bombardment and deployed numerous Daleks to invade the surface. Dalek forces captured the city of Arcadia, then laid siege to the capital itself. However, they were unable to break through the capital's sky trenches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dotd_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-dotd-1">[1]</a></sup> Upon learning of Rassilon's "Ultimate Sanction", the War Doctor stole an ancient Gallifreyan weapon known as the Moment, or the Galaxy Eater, and intended to reduce Gallifrey into "rocks and dust" with the inferno wiping out the Dalek fleet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-end_of_world_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-end_of_world-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dotd_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-dotd-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-end_of_time_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-end_of_time-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Parting_of_the_Ways_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_Parting_of_the_Ways-18">[17]</a></sup> By this point, the entire period of war had become "time locked", so that no time traveller could enter or exit it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-end_of_time_7-5"><a href="#cite_note-end_of_time-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stolen_25-2"><a href="#cite_note-stolen-25">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Fearing that the Doctor would use the Moment, Rassilon and his fellow councilors attempted to escape the Lock by retroactively planting a four-note drumbeat (the rhythm of a Time Lord's heartbeats) into the Master's brain as a child and cause his descent into madness. From there, once the signal is made tangible enough, a Whitepoint Star, a diamond only found on Gallifrey, is used to create a link between the final day of the Time War and Earth so the Master could release Gallifrey from the Time Lock. The plan ultimately failed, as the Doctor destroyed the diamond link and the Master apparently sacrificed his life to take revenge on Rassilon, sending the Time Lords back to their apparent doom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-end_of_time_7-6"><a href="#cite_note-end_of_time-7">[7]</a></sup> Unknown to almost everyone involved however, this was not the final end of the Time Lords. As "the Moment" had a will of its own, it showed the War Doctor an alternate solution and ultimately enabled all incarnations of the Doctor to gather to save Gallifrey by freezing it in time and removing it from the universe. The sudden disappearance of Gallifrey left the Daleks firing upon and subsequently annihilating themselves, while the Time Lords remained, albeit powerless and forgotten.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dotd_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-dotd-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>As the War Doctor's regeneration into the <a href="/wiki/Ninth_Doctor" title="Ninth Doctor">Ninth Doctor</a> wiped out the memory of him saving Gallifrey as a means for his timeline to correct itself, the Doctor was haunted with the false knowledge of his home planet's demise,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dotd_1-11"><a href="#cite_note-dotd-1">[1]</a></sup> with even some of his enemies like the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Doctor_Who_villains#Beast" title="List of Doctor Who villains">Beast</a> using that guilt against him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-drums_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-drums-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[26]</a></sup> The Doctor's later incarnations even displayed a self-loathing towards their War Doctor incarnation before the <a href="/wiki/Eleventh_Doctor" title="Eleventh Doctor">Eleventh Doctor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tenth_Doctor" title="Tenth Doctor">Tenth Doctor</a> learned the truth and accepted him as a legitimate Doctor incarnation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dotd_1-12"><a href="#cite_note-dotd-1">[1]</a></sup> Though the destruction of both races marked the end of the war, the Ninth Doctor learns of the Dalek Emperor's survival with Rose wiping out both him and his fleet in "<a href="/wiki/The_Parting_of_the_Ways" title="The Parting of the Ways">The Parting of the Ways</a>" as Bad Wolf while proclaiming the final end of the War.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Parting_of_the_Ways_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-The_Parting_of_the_Ways-18">[17]</a></sup> This is affirmed by the Tenth Doctor, who describes to <a href="/wiki/Jack_Harkness" title="Jack Harkness">Jack Harkness</a> in "<a href="/wiki/Utopia_(Doctor_Who)" title="Utopia (Doctor Who)">Utopia</a>" that his resurrection by Rose was part of the final act of the War.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-utopia_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-utopia-29">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Consequences">Consequences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Consequences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Removal_of_the_Time_Lords">Removal of the Time Lords</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Removal of the Time Lords">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following what was for all intents and purposes the removal of the Time Lords, the Doctor believed himself to be the last of his race. The removal of the Time Lords also had a profound impact on time travel. In the 2006 episode "<a href="/wiki/Rise_of_the_Cybermen" title="Rise of the Cybermen">Rise of the Cybermen</a>" when the <a href="/wiki/Tenth_Doctor" title="Tenth Doctor">Tenth Doctor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rose_Tyler" title="Rose Tyler">Rose Tyler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mickey_Smith" title="Mickey Smith">Mickey Smith</a> are trapped in an alternative reality, the Doctor explains that, when the Time Lords were around, travel between parallel universes was less difficult but, with their demise, the paths between worlds are now closed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[28]</a></sup> The Time Lords could also prevent or repair <a href="/wiki/Physical_paradox" title="Physical paradox">paradoxes</a> such as the one created by Rose in an attempt to save her father's life in a traffic accident. After the Time Lords' disappearance, such a paradox summons the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Doctor_Who_monsters_and_aliens#Reaper" title="List of Doctor Who monsters and aliens">Reapers</a>, who descended to "sterilise the wound" in time by devouring everything in sight, and are only stopped by Pete Tyler killing himself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Because of the Time Lock, along with the danger of creating a paradox, the Doctor is prevented from going back in time and saving the Time Lords. The Tenth Doctor warns another character against trying to alter his own timeline as such meddling would "destroy two-thirds of the universe"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[30]</a></sup> and resists an offer by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Krillitane" title="Krillitane">Krillitane</a> Mr Finch, using the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skasis_Paradigm" title="Skasis Paradigm">Skasis Paradigm</a>, which would have given the Doctor the ability to reorder the universe and allowed him to stop the war.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-school_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-school-33">[31]</a></sup> The Doctor would only change his decision and assure his race's survival after contributing to it as the Eleventh Doctor during the events of "<a href="/wiki/The_Day_of_the_Doctor" title="The Day of the Doctor">The Day of the Doctor</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dotd_1-13"><a href="#cite_note-dotd-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Throughout <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who_(series_5)" title="Doctor Who (series 5)">series 5</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Eleventh_Doctor" title="Eleventh Doctor">Eleventh Doctor</a> encounters cracks in "the skin of the universe".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eleventh_hour_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-eleventh_hour-34">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flesh_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-flesh-35">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cold_blood_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-cold_blood-36">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pandorica_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-pandorica-37">[35]</a></sup> The Doctor learns that the cracks, which erase those they consume from history, are a result of the <a href="/wiki/Silence_(Doctor_Who)" title="Silence (Doctor Who)">Silence</a> causing the Doctor's TARDIS to explode on 26 June 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-totd_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-totd-38">[36]</a></sup> During the events of "<a href="/wiki/The_Big_Bang_(Doctor_Who)" title="The Big Bang (Doctor Who)">The Big Bang</a>", the Doctor manages to close all the cracks using the TARDIS explosion and the Pandorica to produce a second Big Bang that restores the universe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-big_bang_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-big_bang-39">[37]</a></sup> During the events of "<a href="/wiki/The_Time_of_the_Doctor" title="The Time of the Doctor">The Time of the Doctor</a>", the Doctor discovers one remaining crack on the planet Trenzalore, through which the Time Lords transmit a "truth field" that ensures that all inhabitants speak the truth, along with the First Question: "Doctor Who?". The intention is for the Doctor to give his true name, which will verify to the Time Lords it is safe for them to return to the universe. Fleets of various races, some being former War participants, gathered in Trenzalore's orbit to either prevent the Doctor from speaking his name, or resume the War if the Time Lords return. The Church of the Papal Mainframe undergoes a faith change into the Church of the Silence as a dedication to keeping the peace,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-totd_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-totd-38">[36]</a></sup> with a rogue chapter led by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Madame_Kovarian" title="Madame Kovarian">Madame Kovarian</a> unknowingly causing the events in series 5 and <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who_(series_6)" title="Doctor Who (series 6)">6</a> and leading to the Doctor's arrival on Trenzalore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eleventh_hour_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-eleventh_hour-34">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flesh_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-flesh-35">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cold_blood_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-cold_blood-36">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pandorica_37-1"><a href="#cite_note-pandorica-37">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-totd_38-2"><a href="#cite_note-totd-38">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-big_bang_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-big_bang-39">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[45]</a></sup> In the end, the Time Lords remain in exile and close the remaining crack, but not before heeding Clara's plea for them to save the Doctor, old and weary from his years protecting Trenzalore, granting him a new cycle of regenerations, which allow him to destroy the Dalek fleet surrounding the planet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-totd_38-3"><a href="#cite_note-totd-38">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Remnants_of_the_Daleks">Remnants of the Daleks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Remnants of the Daleks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Despite the Doctor's efforts, not all Daleks perished in the war. The <a href="/wiki/Ninth_Doctor" title="Ninth Doctor">Ninth Doctor</a> encounters a single, dysfunctional Dalek in a museum on Earth in 2012, which had apparently been on Earth for 50 years. It eventually commits suicide after gaining human feelings via <a href="/wiki/Rose_Tyler" title="Rose Tyler">Rose Tyler</a> touching it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dalek_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Dalek-2">[2]</a></sup> The Doctor later discovers that the <a href="/wiki/Dalek_variants#Dalek_Emperor" title="Dalek variants">Dalek Emperor</a> itself had also survived, and had gone on to build a whole new Dalek race, using the organic material of human cadavers by completely rewriting their DNA. The destruction of the Emperor and its fleet in the year 200,100 during an attack on Earth at the conclusion of the 2005 series by a <a href="/wiki/Time_vortex_(Doctor_Who)" title="Time vortex (Doctor Who)">time vortex</a>-augmented Rose Tyler is accompanied by her declaration that "the Time War ends".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Parting_of_the_Ways_18-3"><a href="#cite_note-The_Parting_of_the_Ways-18">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>The elite <a href="/wiki/Dalek_variants#Cult_of_Skaro" title="Dalek variants">Cult of Skaro</a> also survived by fleeing into the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Doctor_Who_planets#Others" title="List of Doctor Who planets">Void</a> between dimensions in a specialized ship, taking with them the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Doctor_Who_items#G" title="List of Doctor Who items">Genesis Ark</a>, a Time Lord prison ship containing millions of Daleks. The new Dalek army released from the Ark on Earth in the 21st century – after the Ark is touched by a time-traveler, <a href="/wiki/Mickey_Smith" title="Mickey Smith">Mickey Smith</a> – is sucked back into the Void due to the actions of the <a href="/wiki/Tenth_Doctor" title="Tenth Doctor">Tenth Doctor</a>, but the specially-equipped cult members use an "emergency temporal shift" to escape that fate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-doomsday_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-doomsday-21">[20]</a></sup> They reappear in 1930 in New York where they try to use humans to create a new race of Daleks. While three members of the Cult of Skaro are killed, the fourth – Dalek Caan – escapes through another emergency temporal shift.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-evolution_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-evolution-24">[23]</a></sup> Dalek Caan returns to the Time War and, at the cost of its sanity, rescues the Daleks' creator, Davros. Davros subsequently uses cells from his own body to create a new <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Daleks" title="History of the Daleks">Dalek Empire</a> and keeps Caan close at his side because of the latter's prophetic abilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stolen_25-3"><a href="#cite_note-stolen-25">[24]</a></sup> The Daleks attempt to destroy all reality with a 'Reality Bomb' powered by 27 stolen Worlds, leaving themselves the only creatures. However, Caan manipulated Davros to help the Doctor and <a href="/wiki/Donna_Noble" title="Donna Noble">Donna Noble</a> defeat the Daleks after seeing the damage the Daleks had caused throughout time. The Daleks are destroyed by a clone of the <a href="/wiki/Tenth_Doctor" title="Tenth Doctor">Tenth Doctor</a>, while Davros and Caan are left behind on the Dalek flagship as it is destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>One ship containing three Daleks escaped that defeat after accidentally falling through time, where it then picked up a trace of a Progenitor device that contained pure Dalek DNA. However, because these Daleks had been created from the DNA of Davros, the Progenitor did not accept them as true "Daleks"; in order to restart the Progenitor, the Daleks trick the <a href="/wiki/Eleventh_Doctor" title="Eleventh Doctor">Eleventh Doctor</a> into activating it for them during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> by declaring them Daleks (which the device accepts). Once activated, the Progenitor device created a <a href="/wiki/Dalek_variants#New_Paradigm_Daleks" title="Dalek variants">new "Paradigm" of Daleks</a> that destroyed the previous Daleks and escaped through time, forming a new race of Daleks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[47]</a></sup> Eventually learning of the Time Lords' survival alongside various other races during the events of "<a href="/wiki/The_Time_of_the_Doctor" title="The Time of the Doctor">The Time of the Doctor</a>", the Daleks laid siege on the planet Trenzalore for centuries to either resume the Time War or kill the Doctor before he could release the Time Lords back into the universe. In the end, the Dalek fleet was wiped out by the Eleventh Doctor during the first phase of his regeneration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-totd_38-4"><a href="#cite_note-totd-38">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Survival_of_the_Master">Survival of the Master</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Survival of the Master">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the Time War, the Doctor is convinced that he is the only surviving Time Lord, saying that he would know of any others if they had survived.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dalek_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Dalek-2">[2]</a></sup> The last words of the <a href="/wiki/Face_of_Boe" title="Face of Boe">Face of Boe</a> were "You Are Not Alone".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gridlock_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-gridlock-3">[3]</a></sup> The cryptic statement is explained when the Doctor encounters a man named Professor Yana who is revealed to be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Master_(Doctor_Who)" title="Master (Doctor Who)">the Master</a>. The Master had been hiding in human form at the end of the universe using a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Doctor_Who_items#C" title="List of Doctor Who items">Chameleon Arch</a>, having escaped the destruction of both the Time Lords and the Daleks. By taking human form, he avoided detection by the Doctor, who was unaware of his nemesis' resurrection during the Time War in the first place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-utopia_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-utopia-29">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_on_other_species">Impact on other species</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Impact on other species">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The timelines of other races and planets shifted without the inhabitants of the worlds affected being aware of the <a href="/wiki/Retroactive_continuity" title="Retroactive continuity">changes in history</a>, as they were a part of them. Most affected were the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Doctor_Who_villains#Animus" title="List of Doctor Who villains">Greater Animus</a>, which died;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meet_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-Meet-12">[11]</a></sup> the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zygons" title="Zygons">Zygons</a>, who lost their home worlds and attempted to conquer Earth for its resources;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dotd_1-14"><a href="#cite_note-dotd-1">[1]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Eternal_(Doctor_Who)" title="Eternal (Doctor Who)">Eternals</a>, who apparently fled this reality in despair;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meet_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-Meet-12">[11]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Doctor_Who_universe_creatures_and_aliens_(0%E2%80%939,_A%E2%80%93G)#Gelth" title="List of Doctor Who universe creatures and aliens (0–9, A–G)">Gelth</a>, who lost their physical form and were reduced to gaseous beings, who attempted to possess human corpses in 1869 using a Time Rift in Cardiff. The Gelth described the war's impact as "invisible to lower species but devastating to higher forms",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unquiet_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-unquiet-5">[5]</a></sup> such as the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Doctor_Who_universe_creatures_and_aliens_(0%E2%80%939,_A%E2%80%93G)#Forest_of_Cheem" title="List of Doctor Who universe creatures and aliens (0–9, A–G)">Forest of Cheem</a>, which was distraught at the bloodshed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meet_12-4"><a href="#cite_note-Meet-12">[11]</a></sup> It is also said to have destroyed the unnamed race that <a href="/wiki/List_of_Doctor_Who_universe_creatures_and_aliens_(0%E2%80%939,_A%E2%80%93G)#Eve" title="List of Doctor Who universe creatures and aliens (0–9, A–G)">Eve</a> originated from.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-attic_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-attic-50">[48]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Time_War_and_continuity">The Time War and continuity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: The Time War and continuity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Time War provides a convenient in-story explanation for any contradictions in series continuity: for example, writer <a href="/wiki/Paul_Cornell" title="Paul Cornell">Paul Cornell</a> has suggested that Earth's destruction by an expanding sun in "The End of the World" five billion years hence, as opposed to the original depiction of its demise around the year 10,000,000 AD in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ark_(Doctor_Who)" title="The Ark (Doctor Who)">The Ark</a></i> (1966), can be attributed to changes in history due to the War.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cornell_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cornell-51">[49]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Steven_Moffat" title="Steven Moffat">Steven Moffat</a>, writer and later executive producer for <i>Doctor Who</i>, has gone further, arguing that "a television series which embraces both the ideas of parallel universes and the concept of changing time can't have a continuity error—it's impossible for <i>Doctor Who</i> to get it wrong, because we can just say 'he changed time—it's a time ripple from the Time War<span style="padding-right:.2em;">'</span>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[51]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Time_Wars_in_spin-off_media">Time Wars in spin-off media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Time Wars in spin-off media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Last Great Time War and previous time wars also feature in various Doctor Who spin-off media.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gallifrey_audio_series"><i>Gallifrey</i> audio series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Gallifrey audio series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Gallifrey_(audio_series)" title="Gallifrey (audio series)">Gallifrey</a></i> is the umbrella title of a series of audio plays by <a href="/wiki/Big_Finish_Productions" title="Big Finish Productions">Big Finish Productions</a>, set on Gallifrey during Romana's tenure as President. In <i><a href="/wiki/Gallifrey:_Panacea" title="Gallifrey: Panacea">Gallifrey: Panacea</a></i>, the final chapter of the third series, the Time Lord <a href="/wiki/Irving_Braxiatel" title="Irving Braxiatel">Irving Braxiatel</a> speaks of "rumours out there in the big wide universe—more than rumours, in fact—that something's coming to Gallifrey, something worse than you could possibly imagine." Because of these rumours, Braxiatel engineers the removal of the Time Lord biodata archive from Gallifrey, in order that the Time Lords might someday be restored after their planet meets its doom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[52]</a></sup> Former Big Finish producer <a href="/wiki/Gary_Russell" title="Gary Russell">Gary Russell</a> indicated in a forum posting on <i><a href="/wiki/Outpost_Gallifrey" title="Outpost Gallifrey">Outpost Gallifrey</a></i> that this was a reference to the television series' Time War.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p>The sixth series of <i>Gallifrey</i> features the Daleks invading Gallifrey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p>The later <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Companion_Chronicles" title="Companion Chronicles">Companion Chronicles</a> audio story, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Catalyst_(Doctor_Who_audio)" title="The Catalyst (Doctor Who audio)">The Catalyst</a></i>, implies that <a href="/wiki/Leela_(Doctor_Who)" title="Leela (Doctor Who)">Leela</a> survived the Time War; she mentions that her adopted homeworld no longer exists and she ages rapidly due to the Time Lords no longer being able to keep her young.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[55]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dark_Eyes"><i>Dark Eyes</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Dark Eyes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_Eyes_(audio_drama)" title="Dark Eyes (audio drama)">Dark Eyes</a></i> audio series is also in effect a lead up to the Last Great Time War from the Doctor's perspective, the first series being a complex plan by the Daleks to erase the Time Lords' existence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[56]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_Eyes_2" title="Dark Eyes 2">Dark Eyes 2</a></i> also sees the resurrection of the Master in preparation for the war.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[57]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eighth_Doctor_Adventures">Eighth Doctor Adventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Eighth Doctor Adventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In a <a href="/wiki/Story_arc" title="Story arc">story arc</a> stretching through several of the <a href="/wiki/Eighth_Doctor_Adventures" title="Eighth Doctor Adventures">Eighth Doctor Adventures</a>, sometime in the Doctor's future, a war is fought between the Time Lords and an unnamed Enemy, the <a href="/wiki/Eighth_Doctor" title="Eighth Doctor">Eighth Doctor</a> becoming involved in the events of the war during the events of <i><a href="/wiki/Alien_Bodies" title="Alien Bodies">Alien Bodies</a></i>, when he unintentionally becomes involved in an auction for the body of his future self due to his biodata codes being the only means of accessing dangerous Time Lord secrets,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[58]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Taking_of_Planet_5" title="The Taking of Planet 5">The Taking of Planet 5</a></i>, where he must stop a group of future Time Lords from releasing the monstrous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Doctor_Who_monsters_and_aliens#Fendahl" title="List of Doctor Who monsters and aliens">Fendahl</a> in an attempt to use it as a weapon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[59]</a></sup> In this story arc, Gallifrey is also destroyed as a result of the Eighth Doctor attempting to prevent the war from beginning as the Enemy begin their first assault in, having learned that he unintentionally provoked the War, believing that it would be better for the Time Lords to die now rather than experience a war that would dehumanise them to the point of becoming monsters which all evidence suggests they could not win. This cataclysm also creates an <a href="/wiki/Event_horizon" title="Event horizon">event horizon</a> in time that prevents anyone from entering Gallifrey's relative past or travelling from it to the present or future.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[60]</a></sup> The last Eighth Doctor Adventures novel, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Gallifrey_Chronicles_(Doctor_Who_novel)" title="The Gallifrey Chronicles (Doctor Who novel)">The Gallifrey Chronicles</a></i>, establishes that the Doctor has the ability to restore the planet and its inhabitants, having downloaded the contents of the <a href="/wiki/Matrix_(Doctor_Who)" title="Matrix (Doctor Who)">Matrix</a> into his subconscious mind in the minutes before Gallifrey's destruction, albeit at the cost of his own memories. The novel ends without revealing if he does indeed do this.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[61]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Russell_T_Davies" title="Russell T Davies">Russell T Davies</a>, executive producer of the series, commented that there is no connection between the War of the books and the Time War of the television series, comparing Gallifrey being destroyed twice with Earth's two World Wars. He also said that he was "usually happy for old and new fans to invent the Complete History of the Doctor in their heads, completely free of the production team's hot and heavy hands".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[62]</a></sup></p>
<p>Another version of the Eighth Doctor Adventures' War, referred to as the "War in Heaven", also appears in the <i><a href="/wiki/Faction_Paradox" title="Faction Paradox">Faction Paradox</a></i> novels conceived by <a href="/wiki/Lawrence_Miles" title="Lawrence Miles">Lawrence Miles</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Doctor_Who_comic_strip"><i>Doctor Who</i> comic strip</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Doctor Who comic strip">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In three comic strip stories written by <a href="/wiki/Alan_Moore" title="Alan Moore">Alan Moore</a> in 1980 and 1981, the Time Lords, assisted by the <a href="/wiki/Special_Executive" title="Special Executive">Special Executive</a>, fight a time war early in their history against the "Order of the Black Sun", based some thirty thousand years in their future.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-star_death_65-0"><a href="#cite_note-star_death-65">[63]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4d_66-0"><a href="#cite_note-4d-66">[64]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-black_sun_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-black_sun-67">[65]</a></sup> The first strike of the war, from the Time Lords' point of view, is when a Black Sun agent travels back in time, and attacks the Time Lords just as they are about to turn the star Qqaba into a power source for their time experiments. This also causes the apparent demise of the stellar engineer <a href="/wiki/Omega_(Doctor_Who)" title="Omega (Doctor Who)">Omega</a>. The Time Lords do not know why the Black Sun (whom they had never encountered before the attack) should have wanted to strike at them, and surmise that it was for something they had yet to do.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-star_death_65-1"><a href="#cite_note-star_death-65">[63]</a></sup> Years later, at a diplomatic conference, a representative of the Order is murdered by the <a href="/wiki/Sontaran" title="Sontaran">Sontarans</a>, and the murder is blamed on the Time Lords. This provides the motivation for the war's beginnings, as from the Order's point of view, the Time Lords are the ones who strike first.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-black_sun_67-1"><a href="#cite_note-black_sun-67">[65]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Doctor_Who_Adventures">Doctor Who Adventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Doctor Who Adventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In this comic book series an insectoid race called the Skrawn appeared, whose planet Kolox the Doctor claims was destroyed in the Time War.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[66]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Forgotten"><i>The Forgotten</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: The Forgotten">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/IDW_Publishing" title="IDW Publishing">IDW</a> comic miniseries <i>The Forgotten</i>, the <a href="/wiki/Tenth_Doctor" title="Tenth Doctor">Tenth Doctor</a> recounts to <a href="/wiki/Martha_Jones" title="Martha Jones">Martha Jones</a> a story from the <a href="/wiki/Eighth_Doctor" title="Eighth Doctor">Eighth Doctor</a>'s participation in the Time War. The Eighth Doctor was imprisoned by a race of robots for several weeks on a planet in the middle of the war, before teaming up with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Doctor_Who_monsters_and_aliens#Malmooth" title="List of Doctor Who monsters and aliens">Malmooth</a> fellow prisoner and faking his death in order to escape. It is revealed his capture was staged by him so that he could acquire the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Doctor_Who_items#G" title="List of Doctor Who items">Great Key</a> he needed to arm a modified <a href="/wiki/List_of_Doctor_Who_items#De-mat_gun" title="List of Doctor Who items">De-Mat Gun</a> that could be used to seal the Medusa Cascade. The Tenth Doctor further implies that the Eighth Doctor died, alone and companionless, at the end of the Time War.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[67]</a></sup> This conflicts with "<a href="/wiki/The_Night_of_the_Doctor" title="The Night of the Doctor">The Night of the Doctor</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/The_Day_of_the_Doctor" title="The Day of the Doctor">The Day of the Doctor</a>"'s depiction of the Time War, where it's established that the Eighth Doctor died and regenerated into the <a href="/wiki/War_Doctor" title="War Doctor">War Doctor</a> before becoming the <a href="/wiki/Ninth_Doctor" title="Ninth Doctor">Ninth Doctor</a>, and the Eighth Doctor refused to fight in the War until shortly before his regeneration into the War Doctor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dotd_1-15"><a href="#cite_note-dotd-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-night_of_the_doctor_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-night_of_the_doctor-19">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Sarah_Jane_Adventures"><i>The Sarah Jane Adventures</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: The Sarah Jane Adventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mad_Woman_in_the_Attic" title="The Mad Woman in the Attic">The Mad Woman in the Attic</a></i>, a two-part serial of the <i>Doctor Who</i> spinoff series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sarah_Jane_Adventures" title="The Sarah Jane Adventures">The Sarah Jane Adventures</a></i>, Eve speaks about the Time War, stating that her people could see timelines and this made them a target and they were exterminated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-attic_50-1"><a href="#cite_note-attic-50">[48]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Engines_of_War"><i>Engines of War</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_War_(Doctor_Who)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Engines of War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2014, the BBC released the novel <i>Engines of War</i> by <a href="/wiki/George_Mann_(writer)" title="George Mann (writer)">George Mann</a>, which features the <a href="/wiki/War_Doctor" title="War Doctor">War Doctor</a> in a novel set in the Time War itself. During the novel, the War Doctor and his new companion Cinder—a human resistance fighter on the Dalek-occupied world of Moldox—discover that the Daleks intend to use the temporal anomalies of the Tantalus Eye, a rift in time in the Moldox system, to develop a weapon that could completely erase Gallifrey and the Time Lords from history, with Rassilon's plan to stop the Dalek plot involving the destruction of the Eye and all inhabited planets around it. Refusing to accept this, the Doctor is able to sabotage Rassilon's plan and use the energy of the Tantalus Eye to erase the Daleks' scheme, but the actions of a Time Lord agent result in Cinder's death, leaving the War Doctor resolved to end the war once and for all as he recognizes how far his people have fallen in the name of victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[68]</a></sup></p>
