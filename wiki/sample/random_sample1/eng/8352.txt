<p><b>Keiichi Sigsawa</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">時雨沢 恵一</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shigusawa Keiichi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, born in 1972 in <a href="/wiki/Kanagawa_Prefecture" title="Kanagawa Prefecture">Kanagawa Prefecture</a>, is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Light_novel" title="Light novel">light novel</a> author. In 2000, his work <i><a href="/wiki/Kino%27s_Journey" title="Kino's Journey">Kino's Journey</a></i> was a finalist for the 6th <a href="/wiki/Dengeki_Novel_Prize" title="Dengeki Novel Prize">Dengeki Novel Prize</a>, and was subsequently serialized in <i><a href="/wiki/Dengeki_hp" title="Dengeki hp">Dengeki hp</a></i> magazine in March of the same year, marking his debut. <i>Kino's Journey</i> proved popular, and has continued in serialization (as well as collected <a href="/wiki/Bunkobon" title="Bunkobon">bunko</a> releases) since that time.</p>
<p>The author's name, a pen name, was derived from <a href="/wiki/SIG_Sauer" title="SIG Sauer">SIG Sauer</a>, a firearm manufacturer, and 'a manga hero', definitely <i><a href="/wiki/Keiichi_Morisato" title="Keiichi Morisato">Keiichi Morisato</a></i> from <i><a href="/wiki/Oh_My_Goddess!" title="Oh My Goddess!">Oh My Goddess!</a></i>, according to the afterword in the second volume of <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_Kino%27s_Journey_light_novels#Gakuen_Kino" title="List of Kino's Journey light novels">Gakuen Kino</a></i>. He loves to travel, ride his motorcycle, and is a self-professed "gun maniac". He mentions <a href="/wiki/Galaxy_Express_999" title="Galaxy Express 999">Galaxy Express 999</a> and the works of <a href="/wiki/Hayao_Miyazaki" title="Hayao Miyazaki">Hayao Miyazaki</a> as some of his primary influences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
