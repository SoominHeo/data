<p><b>Sigrún</b> (<a href="/wiki/Old_Norse" title="Old Norse">Old Norse</a> "victory <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Runic_alphabet" title="Runic alphabet">rune</a>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ORCHARD194_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ORCHARD194-1">[1]</a></sup>) is a <a href="/wiki/Valkyrie" title="Valkyrie">valkyrie</a> in <a href="/wiki/Norse_mythology" title="Norse mythology">Norse mythology</a>. Her story is related in <i><a href="/wiki/Helgakvi%C3%B0a_Hundingsbana_I" title="Helgakviða Hundingsbana I">Helgakviða Hundingsbana I</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Helgakvi%C3%B0a_Hundingsbana_II" title="Helgakviða Hundingsbana II">Helgakviða Hundingsbana II</a></i>, in the <i><a href="/wiki/Poetic_Edda" title="Poetic Edda">Poetic Edda</a></i>. The original editor annotated that she was <a href="/wiki/Sv%C3%A1fa" title="Sváfa">Sváfa</a> reborn.</p>
<p>The hero <a href="/wiki/Helgi_Hundingsbane" title="Helgi Hundingsbane">Helgi Hundingsbane</a> first meets her when she leads a band of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Numbers_in_Germanic_paganism" title="Numbers in Germanic paganism">nine</a> Valkyries:</p>

<p>The two fall in love, and Sigrún tells Helgi that her father <a href="/wiki/H%C3%B6gne" title="Högne">Högni</a> has promised her to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/H%C3%B6%C3%B0broddr" title="Höðbroddr">Höðbroddr</a>, the son of king <a href="/wiki/Granmar" title="Granmar">Granmarr</a>. Helgi invades Granmar's kingdom and slays anyone opposing their relationship. Only Sigrún's brother Dagr is left alive on condition that he swears fealty to Helgi.</p>
<p>Dagr is however obliged by honour to avenge his brothers and after having summoned <a href="/wiki/Odin" title="Odin">Odin</a>, the god gives him a spear. In a place called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fjoturlund" title="Fjoturlund">Fjoturlund</a>, Dagr kills Helgi and goes back to his sister to tell her of his deed. Sigrún puts Dagr under a powerful curse after which he is obliged to live on carrion in the woods.</p>
<p>Helgi is put in a <a href="/wiki/Tumulus" title="Tumulus">barrow</a>, but returns from Valhalla one last time so that the two can spend a night together.</p>
<p>Sigrún died early from the sadness, but was reborn again as a Valkyrie. In the next life, she was <a href="/wiki/K%C3%A1ra" title="Kára">Kára</a> and Helgi was <a href="/wiki/Helgi_Haddingjaskati" title="Helgi Haddingjaskati">Helgi Haddingjaskati</a>, whose story is related in <i><a href="/wiki/Hr%C3%B3mundar_saga_Gripssonar" title="Hrómundar saga Gripssonar">Hrómundar saga Gripssonar</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigr%C3%BAn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Sigrún appears in the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dresden_Files" title="The Dresden Files">The Dresden Files</a></i> series under the alias "Gard" as a Valkyrie working as a security consultant from Monoc Securities, under contract for "Gentleman" John Marcone. Her true name and nature is revealed in the short story "Heorot".</li>
<li>Sigrún appears in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Order_of_the_Stick" title="The Order of the Stick">The Order of the Stick</a></i> webcomic as a <a href="/wiki/Demigod" title="Demigod">demigoddess</a> of the "Gods of the North," a version of Norse theology, and is presented as Queen of the Valkyries.</li>
<li>In the video game Overwatch, the playable character "Mercy" has an unlockable skin under the name "Sigrún".</li>
</ul>
