<p>The <b>Sydney Showground</b> is a purpose built venue used each year for the <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Royal_Easter_Show" title="Sydney Royal Easter Show">Sydney Royal Easter Show</a>. Located at <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Olympic_Park" title="Sydney Olympic Park">Sydney Olympic Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, it was opened in 1998, as a venue for the <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">2000 Summer Olympics</a> and to replace the ageing <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Showground_(Moore_Park)" title="Sydney Showground (Moore Park)">old Sydney Showground</a> at <a href="/wiki/Moore_Park,_New_South_Wales" title="Moore Park, New South Wales">Moore Park</a>. Sydney Showground is operated by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Agricultural_Society_of_NSW" title="Royal Agricultural Society of NSW">Royal Agricultural Society of NSW</a> (RAS), under lease from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NSW_Government" title="NSW Government">NSW Government</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-overview_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-overview-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sydney_Showground_Stadium">Sydney Showground Stadium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sydney_Showground_(Olympic_Park)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Sydney Showground Stadium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sydney_Showground_Stadium_2015-8-9.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1918" data-file-width="6904" height="61" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/Sydney_Showground_Stadium_2015-8-9.jpg/220px-Sydney_Showground_Stadium_2015-8-9.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/Sydney_Showground_Stadium_2015-8-9.jpg/330px-Sydney_Showground_Stadium_2015-8-9.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/Sydney_Showground_Stadium_2015-8-9.jpg/440px-Sydney_Showground_Stadium_2015-8-9.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Showground_Stadium" title="Sydney Showground Stadium">Sydney Showground Stadium</a></div>
<p>Sydney Showground Stadium (commercial name Spotless Stadium<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>) is the centrepiece of the Showgrounds and is used for flagship Show events such as the Grand Parade. It has been used for various sports including <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">2000 Summer Olympics</a> and rugby league between 2001 and 2005. From 2012, it is used for Australian rules football as the primary home ground of the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Western_Sydney_Giants" title="Greater Western Sydney Giants">Greater Western Sydney Giants</a>. Twenty20 cricket has been played at the stadium since 2015 when the stadium became the home ground of the <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Thunder" title="Sydney Thunder">Sydney Thunder</a>.</p>

<p><b>The Dome</b>, also known as <b>The Dome and Exhibition Complex</b> is a large indoor venue with a circular shaped floor plan and a ceiling up to 42 metres in height. It has 7,200 square metres of floor space, which can be expanded to 21,600 square metres when combined with the adjoining Exhibitions Halls 2, 3, &amp; 4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the Olympics, it hosted the finals for <a href="/wiki/Handball_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics" title="Handball at the 2000 Summer Olympics">team handball</a> and some of the preliminary <a href="/wiki/Basketball_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics" title="Basketball at the 2000 Summer Olympics">basketball</a> games. Four other pavilions were constructed during the games to host <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics" title="Badminton at the 2000 Summer Olympics">badminton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gymnastics_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics" title="Gymnastics at the 2000 Summer Olympics">rhythmic gymnastics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Handball_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics" title="Handball at the 2000 Summer Olympics">handball</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volleyball_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics" title="Volleyball at the 2000 Summer Olympics">indoor volleyball</a> events, as well as the shooting and fencing part of the <a href="/wiki/Modern_pentathlon_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics" title="Modern pentathlon at the 2000 Summer Olympics">modern pentathlon</a> competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Dome and the adjacent Exhibition Halls are used for trade shows and large corporate functions. The Dome is occasionally used to film TV shows, including the 2008 series of <i><a href="/wiki/Gladiators_(2008_Australian_TV_series)" title="Gladiators (2008 Australian TV series)">Gladiators Australia</a></i> and early episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Junior_MasterChef_Australia" title="Junior MasterChef Australia">Junior MasterChef Australia</a></i> 2011.</p>

<p>Charles Moses Stadium is a U-shaped outdoor stadium. Features include tiered seating with a capacity of 3000, a video screen, PA system and corporate boxes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It is home to the Royal Easter Show's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wood_chopping" title="Wood chopping">wood chopping</a> events.</p>
