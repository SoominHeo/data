<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_family">Early life and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sydney_Tamiia_Poitier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, Poitier is the daughter of Bahamian-American actor <a href="/wiki/Sidney_Poitier" title="Sidney Poitier">Sidney Poitier</a> and Canadian former actress <a href="/wiki/Joanna_Shimkus" title="Joanna Shimkus">Joanna Shimkus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mccann_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-mccann-1">[1]</a></sup> Her mother is of <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuanian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jewish" title="Jewish">Jewish</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and Irish descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She has an older sister, Anika. She also has four older half-sisters, Beverly, Pamela, Sherri, and Gina, from her father's first marriage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Poitier's great-uncle has claimed that the <i>Poitier</i> ancestors on her grandfather's side had migrated from <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and were probably part of the runaway slaves who had established <a href="/wiki/Maroon_(people)" title="Maroon (people)">maroon</a> communities throughout the Bahamas, including Cat Island. He mentions that the surname <i>Poitier</i> is a French name, and there were no white Poitiers from the Bahamas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Poitier attended <a href="/wiki/Tisch_School_of_the_Arts" title="Tisch School of the Arts">Tisch School of the Arts</a> where she earned a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">bachelor's degree</a> in acting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mccann_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-mccann-1">[1]</a></sup> She also studied at <a href="/wiki/Stella_Adler_Studio_of_Acting" title="Stella Adler Studio of Acting">Stella Adler Studio of Acting</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sydney_Tamiia_Poitier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Poitier began her career of acting in the late 1990s. In 2001, she landed her first role on television in the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> drama series <i><a href="/wiki/First_Years" title="First Years">First Years</a></i>. The series was canceled after three episodes. In 2003, she starred in the <a href="/wiki/UPN" title="UPN">UPN</a> sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Abby_(TV_series)" title="Abby (TV series)">Abby</a></i>. That series was also canceled during its first season. Later that same year, she had a recurring role in <i><a href="/wiki/Joan_of_Arcadia" title="Joan of Arcadia">Joan of Arcadia</a></i>, where she played Rebecca Askew, the love interest of Joan's older brother, Kevin (<a href="/wiki/Jason_Ritter" title="Jason Ritter">Jason Ritter</a>). She was also a regular on the first season of <i><a href="/wiki/Veronica_Mars" title="Veronica Mars">Veronica Mars</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> However, she left the show after only appearing in four episodes because of budget cuts.</p>
<p>In 2007, Poitier starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Death_Proof" title="Death Proof">Death Proof</a></i>, director <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino" title="Quentin Tarantino">Quentin Tarantino</a>'s segment of the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Grindhouse_(film)" title="Grindhouse (film)">Grindhouse</a></i>, as radio DJ Jungle Julia. The next year, she had a co-starring role in the new <i><a href="/wiki/Knight_Rider_(2008_TV_series)" title="Knight Rider (2008 TV series)">Knight Rider</a></i> series, as FBI Agent Carrie Rivai.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2011, she guest starred on two episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Private_Practice_(TV_series)" title="Private Practice (TV series)">Private Practice</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sydney_Tamiia_Poitier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sydney Tamiia Poitier married her husband, musician Dorian Heartsong on 2012. Their daughter A'Zalia Delancey Coffey was born on May 14, 2015.</p>
