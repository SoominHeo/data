<p><b>Shiranuka</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">白糠郡</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shiranuka-gun</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Japan" title="Districts of Japan">district</a> located in western <a href="/wiki/Kushiro_Subprefecture" title="Kushiro Subprefecture">Kushiro Subprefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hokkaido" title="Hokkaido">Hokkaido</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Merger">Merger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shiranuka_District,_Hokkaido&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Merger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>On October 11, 2005, the town of <a href="/wiki/Onbetsu,_Hokkaido" title="Onbetsu, Hokkaido">Onbetsu</a>, along with the town of <a href="/wiki/Akan,_Hokkaido" title="Akan, Hokkaido">Akan</a> (from <a href="/wiki/Akan_District,_Hokkaido" title="Akan District, Hokkaido">Akan District</a>), merged into the expanded city of <a href="/wiki/Kushiro,_Hokkaido" title="Kushiro, Hokkaido">Kushiro</a>.</li>
</ul>
