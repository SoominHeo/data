<p>The <b>CAC 40</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>CAC quarante</i></span> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[kak kaʁɑ̃t]</a></span>) (Cotation Assistée en Continu) is a benchmark French <a href="/wiki/Stock_market_index" title="Stock market index">stock market index</a>. The index represents a capitalization-weighted measure of the 40 most significant values among the 100 highest <a href="/wiki/Market_capitalization" title="Market capitalization">market caps</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Euronext_Paris" title="Euronext Paris">Euronext Paris</a> (formerly the <a href="/wiki/Paris_Bourse" title="Paris Bourse">Paris Bourse</a>). It is one of the main national indices of the pan-European stock exchange group <a href="/wiki/Euronext" title="Euronext">Euronext</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>' <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BEL20" title="BEL20">BEL20</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>'s <a href="/wiki/PSI-20" title="PSI-20">PSI-20</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>'s <a href="/wiki/AEX_index" title="AEX index">AEX</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CAC_40&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The CAC 40 takes its name from the <a href="/wiki/Paris_Bourse" title="Paris Bourse">Paris Bourse</a>'s early automation system <b><a href="/wiki/Cotation_Assist%C3%A9e_en_Continu" title="Cotation Assistée en Continu">Cotation Assistée en Continu</a></b> (<i>Continuous Assisted Quotation</i>). Its base value of 1,000 was set on 31 December 1987, equivalent to a market capitalisation of 370,437,433,957.70 <a href="/wiki/French_franc" title="French franc">French francs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-profile-2">[2]</a></sup> In common with many major world stock markets, its all-time high to date (6922.33 points) was reached at the peak of the <a href="/wiki/Dot-com_bubble" title="Dot-com bubble">dot-com bubble</a> in September 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> On 1 December 2003, the index's weighting system switched from being dependent on total market capitalisation to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Float_(finance)" title="Float (finance)">free float</a> market cap only, in line with other leading indices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rules">Rules</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CAC_40&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rules">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Selection">Selection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CAC_40&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Selection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The CAC 40 index composition is reviewed quarterly by an independent Index Steering Committee (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Conseil Scientifique</i></span>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-profile-2">[2]</a></sup> If any changes are made, they are effected a minimum of two weeks after the review meeting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-profile-2">[2]</a></sup> At each review date, the companies listed on Euronext Paris are ranked according to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Float_(finance)" title="Float (finance)">free float</a> <a href="/wiki/Market_capitalization" title="Market capitalization">market capitalisation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stock" title="Stock">share</a> turnover over the prior 12 months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rules_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-rules-5">[5]</a></sup> From the top 100 companies in this ranking, forty are chosen to enter the CAC 40 such that it is "a relevant benchmark for <a href="/wiki/Investment_management" title="Investment management">portfolio management</a>" and "a suitable underlying asset for <a href="/wiki/Derivative_(finance)" title="Derivative (finance)">derivatives</a> products".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rules_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-rules-5">[5]</a></sup> If a company has more than one class of shares traded on the exchange, only the most actively traded of these will be accepted into the index (generally this will be the ordinary share).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rules_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-rules-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Weighting">Weighting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CAC_40&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Weighting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The CAC 40 is a <a href="/wiki/Capitalization-weighted_index" title="Capitalization-weighted index">capitalization-weighted index</a>. The number of shares issued (used to calculate the market cap and hence the index weight) of a company is reviewed quarterly, on the third Friday of March, June, September and December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-profile-2">[2]</a></sup> Since December 2003, the index weightings of companies in the index have been capped at 15% at each quarterly index review,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rules_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-rules-5">[5]</a></sup> but these range freely with <a href="/wiki/Share_price" title="Share price">share price</a> subsequently. A capping factor is used to limit the weights to 15% (if necessary), and is reviewed annually by the Index Steering Committee on the third Friday of September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rules_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-rules-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Holders">Holders</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CAC_40&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Holders">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although the CAC 40 is almost exclusively composed of French-domiciled companies, about 45% of its listed shares are owned by foreign investors, more than any other main European index.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> German, Japanese, American and British investors are amongst the most significant holders of CAC 40 shares. This large percentage is due to the fact that CAC 40 companies are more international, or <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a>, than any other European market. CAC 40 companies conduct over two-thirds of their business and employ over two-thirds of their <a href="/wiki/Workforce" title="Workforce">workforce</a> outside France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
