<p><b>Shirak</b> (<a href="/wiki/Armenian_language" title="Armenian language">Armenian</a>: <span lang="hy" xml:lang="hy"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%D5%87%D5%AB%D6%80%D5%A1%D5%AF" title="wikt:Շիրակ">Շիրակ</a></span>, <small>Armenian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Armenian" title="Help:IPA for Armenian">[ʃiˈɾɑk]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:Hye-Shirak.ogg" title="File:Hye-Shirak.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f1/Hye-Shirak.ogg" title="Hye-Shirak.ogg">listen</a>)</small>), is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Armenia" title="Provinces of Armenia">province</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Armenia" title="Administrative divisions of Armenia">marz</a></i>) of <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>. It is located in the north-west of the country, bordering <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> in the west and <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a> in the north. Its capital and largest city is <a href="/wiki/Gyumri" title="Gyumri">Gyumri</a>. It is as much semi-desert as it is mountain meadow or high alpine. In the south, the high steppes crash into mountain terrain, verdant green in the spring, hues of reddish brown in the summer. The province is served by the <a href="/wiki/Shirak_Airport" title="Shirak Airport">Shirak International Airport</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gyumri" title="Gyumri">Gyumri</a>.</p>

<p>Shirak Province is named after the <b>Shirak</b> canton of the historic <a href="/wiki/Ayrarat" title="Ayrarat">Ayrarat</a> province of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Armenia_(antiquity)" title="Kingdom of Armenia (antiquity)">Ancient Armenia</a>, ruled by the <a href="/wiki/Kamsarakan" title="Kamsarakan">Kamsarakan</a> noble family between the 3rd and 8th centuries.</p>
<p>According to <a href="/wiki/Movses_Khorenatsi" title="Movses Khorenatsi">Movses Khorenatsi</a>, the name Shirak is derived from <b>Shara</b>, who was the great grandson of <a href="/wiki/Hayk" title="Hayk">Hayk</a> the legendary patriarch and founder of the Armenian nation. However, according to the Shirak Regional Museum, many historians assume that the name is derived from the name <b>Eriakhi</b> found in an ancient <a href="/wiki/Urartu" title="Urartu">Urartian</a> cuneiform, where king <a href="/wiki/Argishti_I_of_Urartu" title="Argishti I of Urartu">Argishti I</a> narrated about his invasion of the land of <i>Eriakhi</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shirak_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:%D4%B1%D6%80%D5%A1%D5%A3%D5%A1%D5%AE_%D5%AC%D5%A5%D5%BC%D5%A8_%D5%87%D5%AB%D6%80%D5%A1%D5%AF%D5%AB_%D5%A4%D5%A1%D5%B7%D5%BF%D5%AB%D6%81.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/%D4%B1%D6%80%D5%A1%D5%A3%D5%A1%D5%AE_%D5%AC%D5%A5%D5%BC%D5%A8_%D5%87%D5%AB%D6%80%D5%A1%D5%AF%D5%AB_%D5%A4%D5%A1%D5%B7%D5%BF%D5%AB%D6%81.jpg/220px-%D4%B1%D6%80%D5%A1%D5%A3%D5%A1%D5%AE_%D5%AC%D5%A5%D5%BC%D5%A8_%D5%87%D5%AB%D6%80%D5%A1%D5%AF%D5%AB_%D5%A4%D5%A1%D5%B7%D5%BF%D5%AB%D6%81.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/%D4%B1%D6%80%D5%A1%D5%A3%D5%A1%D5%AE_%D5%AC%D5%A5%D5%BC%D5%A8_%D5%87%D5%AB%D6%80%D5%A1%D5%AF%D5%AB_%D5%A4%D5%A1%D5%B7%D5%BF%D5%AB%D6%81.jpg/330px-%D4%B1%D6%80%D5%A1%D5%A3%D5%A1%D5%AE_%D5%AC%D5%A5%D5%BC%D5%A8_%D5%87%D5%AB%D6%80%D5%A1%D5%AF%D5%AB_%D5%A4%D5%A1%D5%B7%D5%BF%D5%AB%D6%81.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/%D4%B1%D6%80%D5%A1%D5%A3%D5%A1%D5%AE_%D5%AC%D5%A5%D5%BC%D5%A8_%D5%87%D5%AB%D6%80%D5%A1%D5%AF%D5%AB_%D5%A4%D5%A1%D5%B7%D5%BF%D5%AB%D6%81.jpg/440px-%D4%B1%D6%80%D5%A1%D5%A3%D5%A1%D5%AE_%D5%AC%D5%A5%D5%BC%D5%A8_%D5%87%D5%AB%D6%80%D5%A1%D5%AF%D5%AB_%D5%A4%D5%A1%D5%B7%D5%BF%D5%AB%D6%81.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Shirak Province occupies the northwestern part of Armenia and covers an area of 2,681 km<sup>2</sup> (1,035 sq mi) (9% of total area of Armenia). It has borders with <a href="/wiki/Lori_Province" title="Lori Province">Lori Province</a> from the east, <a href="/wiki/Aragatsotn_Province" title="Aragatsotn Province">Aragatsotn Province</a> from the south, <a href="/wiki/Kars_Province" title="Kars Province">Kars Province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> from the west and <a href="/wiki/Samtskhe-Javakheti" title="Samtskhe-Javakheti">Samtskhe-Javakheti</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a> from the north.</p>
<p>Historically, the current territory of the province mainly occupies the <i>Shirak</i> canton of <a href="/wiki/Ayrarat" title="Ayrarat">Ayrarat</a> province of Ancient Armenia.</p>
<p>Shirak is mainly dominated by the Ashotsk Plateue (1900 to 2100 meters height) at the north and the Shirak Plain (1400 to 1800 meters height) at the centre and south of the province. The vast plains of the province are surrounded with the Bazum and Pambak mountains from the east, <a href="/wiki/Javakheti_Range" title="Javakheti Range">Javakheti Range</a> and Yeghnakhagh mountains from the north and the <a href="/wiki/Mount_Aragats" title="Mount Aragats">Aragats mass</a> from the south. <a href="/wiki/Akhurian_River" title="Akhurian River">Akhurian River</a> at the east, separates Shirak from the <a href="/wiki/Kars_Province" title="Kars Province">Kars Province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>.</p>

</div>
<p>Akhurian River with its reservoir is the main water resource in the province. <a href="/wiki/Lake_Arpi" title="Lake Arpi">Lake Arpi</a> at the northwest of Shirak is the only lake of the province. The area is protected by the government as the <a href="/wiki/Lake_Arpi_National_Park" title="Lake Arpi National Park">Lake Arpi National Park</a>.</p>
<p>Shirak is rich with <a href="/wiki/Tufa" title="Tufa">tufa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pumice" title="Pumice">pumice</a> and <a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">limestone</a>.</p>
<p>The climate is characterized with extremely cold snowy winters and mild summers. The annual precipitation level can reach up to 700 mm (28 in).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shirak_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Classical_antiquity_and_Urartu_period">Classical antiquity and Urartu period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shirak_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Classical antiquity and Urartu period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Vahramaberd_amroc.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1944" data-file-width="2592" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Vahramaberd_amroc.jpg/220px-Vahramaberd_amroc.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Vahramaberd_amroc.jpg/330px-Vahramaberd_amroc.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Vahramaberd_amroc.jpg/440px-Vahramaberd_amroc.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Vahramaberd_amroc.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Vahramaberd fortress, 8th c. BC</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Many ancient human settlements were found at the <a href="/wiki/Akhurian_River" title="Akhurian River">Akhurian</a> valley dating back to around 9000 BC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The territory of Shirak has been settled since the early <a href="/wiki/Stone_Age" title="Stone Age">Stone Age</a>. At the higher areas that are above 2000 meters, many remains were found from the early <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a>. Other remains from the 2nd millennium BC, revealed that a civilization was founded between the 20th and 12th centuries BC. Wuth the start of the Iron Age during the 12th century BC, the relations among the ethnic groups of Armenian Highland has been developed. Soon after the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Urartu" title="Urartu">Urartu</a> Kingdom of Van at the end of the 9th century BC, Shirak became part of the kingdom. 2 cuneiform scripts have been found in Shirak left by King <a href="/wiki/Argishti_I_of_Urartu" title="Argishti I of Urartu">Argishti I</a> (786-764 BC), where he narrates about the invasion of the land of <b>Eriakhi</b> (the name that <b>Shirak</b> is derived from, according to many historians). According to the scripts, the region was home to a well developed civilization based on agriculture and cattle-breeding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>in 720 BC, the <a href="/wiki/Cimmerians" title="Cimmerians">Cimmerians</a> conquered the region and probably founded the <i>Kumayri</i> settlement (now <i>Gyumri</i>), which bears phonetic resemblance to the word used by ancient Armenian in reference to <a href="/wiki/Cimmerians" title="Cimmerians">Cimmerians</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Historians believe that <a href="/wiki/Xenophon" title="Xenophon">Xenophon</a> passed through the territories of Shirak during his return to the Black Sea, a journey immortalized in his <a href="/wiki/Anabasis_(Xenophon)" title="Anabasis (Xenophon)">Anabasis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Satrapy_of_Armenia_and_the_ancient_Armenian_Kingdom">Satrapy of Armenia and the ancient Armenian Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shirak_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Satrapy of Armenia and the ancient Armenian Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Beniamin_11.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3456" data-file-width="4608" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Beniamin_11.JPG/220px-Beniamin_11.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Beniamin_11.JPG/330px-Beniamin_11.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Beniamin_11.JPG/440px-Beniamin_11.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Beniamin_11.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Orontid settlement of the 5th-2nd centuries BC</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>By the second half of the 6th century BC, Shirak became part of the <a href="/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire" title="Achaemenid Empire">Achaemenid Empire</a>. The remains of a royal settlement found near the village of <a href="/wiki/Beniamin" title="Beniamin">Beniamin</a> dating back to the 5th to 2nd centuries BC, are a great example of the Achemenid influence in the region. By the beginning of the 5th century BC, Shirak became part of the <a href="/wiki/Satrapy_of_Armenia" title="Satrapy of Armenia">Satrapy of Armenia</a> under the rule of the <a href="/wiki/Orontid_Dynasty" title="Orontid Dynasty">Orontids</a>. Later in 331 BC, the entire territory was included in the <a href="/wiki/Ayrarat" title="Ayrarat">Ayrarat</a> province of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Armenia_(antiquity)" title="Kingdom of Armenia (antiquity)">Ancient Armenian Kingdom</a> as part of the Shirak canton.</p>
<p>During the 1st century AD, Shirak was granted to the <a href="/wiki/Kamsarakan" title="Kamsarakan">Kamsarakan</a> family, who ruled the region during the <a href="/wiki/Arsacid_dynasty_of_Armenia" title="Arsacid dynasty of Armenia">Arsacid Kingdom of Armenia</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foreign_rule_and_Bagratid_Kingdom_of_Armenia">Foreign rule and Bagratid Kingdom of Armenia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shirak_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Foreign rule and Bagratid Kingdom of Armenia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Marmashen...1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3648" data-file-width="5472" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Marmashen...1.jpg/220px-Marmashen...1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Marmashen...1.jpg/330px-Marmashen...1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Marmashen...1.jpg/440px-Marmashen...1.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Marmashen...1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Marmashen_Monastery" title="Marmashen Monastery">Marmashen Monastery</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Following the partition of Armenia in 387 between the Byzantines and the Persians, and as a result of the fall of the Arsacid Kingdom of Armenia in 428, the region of Shirak became part of the <a href="/wiki/Sasanian_Empire" title="Sasanian Empire">Sasanian Empire</a> of Persia.</p>
<p>However, Shirak is home to many early examples of the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_church_architecture" title="Armenian church architecture">Armenian church architecture</a> dating back to the 5th century, including the <a href="/wiki/Yererouk" title="Yererouk">Yererouk</a>, the Saint Mariné Church of Artik, and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hokevank_Monastery&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hokevank Monastery (page does not exist)">Hokevank Monastery</a>.</p>
<p>In 658 AD, during the height of the Arab Islamic invasions, Shirak -along with the rest of the Armenian territories- was conquered during the <a href="/wiki/Muslim_conquest_of_Persia" title="Muslim conquest of Persia">Muslim conquest of Persia</a>, as it was part of Persian-ruled Armenia. It became part of the <a href="/wiki/Arminiya" title="Arminiya">Emirate of Armenia</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate" title="Umayyad Caliphate">Umayyad Caliphate</a>. However, the Kamsarakan family continued to rule the region under the Arab Islamic rule of Armenia.</p>
<p>By the foundation of the <a href="/wiki/Bagratid_Armenia" title="Bagratid Armenia">Bagratid Kingdom of Armenia</a> in 885, Shirak entered e new era of growth and progress, particularly when the city of <a href="/wiki/Ani" title="Ani">Ani</a> of Shirak became the capital of the kingdom in 961. By the second half of the 10th century, Shirak was under the influence of the Armenian <a href="/wiki/Pahlavuni" title="Pahlavuni">Pahlavuni</a> family, who were descendents of the Kamsarakans. The Pahlavunis had a great contribution in the progress of Shirak with the foundation of many fortresses, monastic complexes, educational institutions, etc. The monasteries of <a href="/wiki/Khtzkonk_Monastery" title="Khtzkonk Monastery">Khtzkonk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harichavank_Monastery" title="Harichavank Monastery">Harichavank</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marmashen_Monastery" title="Marmashen Monastery">Marmashen</a> and Horomos were among the prominent religious and educational centres of medieval Armenia.</p>

<p>After the fall of Armenia to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Byzantine" title="Byzantine">Byzantines Empire</a> in 1045 and later to the <a href="/wiki/Seljuk_Empire" title="Seljuk Empire">Seljuk</a> invaders in 1064, the region entered an era of downfall in all social, educational and cultural aspects.</p>
<p>However, with the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Zakarid_Armenia" title="Zakarid Armenia">Zakarid Principality of Armenia</a> in 1201 under the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Georgia" title="Kingdom of Georgia">Georgian</a> protectorate, the Eaastern Armenian territories, mainly Lori and Shirak, entered into a new period of growth and stability, becoming a trade centre between the east and the west. After the Mongols captured <a href="/wiki/Ani" title="Ani">Ani</a> in 1236, Armenia turned into a <a href="/wiki/Mongol_Armenia" title="Mongol Armenia">Mongol protectorate</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/Ilkhanate" title="Ilkhanate">Ilkhanate</a>, and the Zakarids became vassals to the <a href="/wiki/Mongol_Empire" title="Mongol Empire">Mongols</a>. After the fall of the Ilkhanate in the mid-14th century, the Zakarid princes ruled over Lori, Shirak and Ararat plain until 1360 when they fell to the invading Turkic tribes.</p>
<p>By the last quarter of the 14th century, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ag_Qoyunlu" title="Ag Qoyunlu">Ag Qoyunlu</a> Sunni <a href="/wiki/Oghuz_Turks" title="Oghuz Turks">Oghuz Turkic</a> tribe took over Armenia, including Shirak. In 1400, <a href="/wiki/Timur" title="Timur">Timur</a> invaded Armenia and Georgia, and captured more than 60,000 of the survived local people as slaves. Many districts including Shirak were depopulated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 1410, Armenia fell under the control of the <a href="/wiki/Kara_Koyunlu" title="Kara Koyunlu">Kara Koyunlu</a> Shia Oghuz Turkic tribe. According to the Armeian historian <a href="/wiki/Thomas_of_Metsoph" title="Thomas of Metsoph">Thomas of Metsoph</a>, although the Kara Koyunlu levied heavy taxes against the Armenians, the early years of their rule were relatively peaceful and some reconstruction of towns took place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Iranian_and_Russian_rule">Iranian and Russian rule</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shirak_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Iranian and Russian rule">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1501, most of the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Armenia" title="Eastern Armenia">Eastern Armenian</a> territories including Shirak were conquered by the emerging <a href="/wiki/Safavid_dynasty" title="Safavid dynasty">Safavid dynasty</a> of Iran led by Shah <a href="/wiki/Ismail_I" title="Ismail I">Ismail I</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Soon after in 1502, Shirak became part of the newly formed <i>Erivan Beglarbegi</i>, a new administrative territory of Iran formed by the Safavids. During the first half of the 18th century, Kumayri became part of the <a href="/wiki/Erivan_Khanate" title="Erivan Khanate">Erivan Khanate</a> under the rule of the <a href="/wiki/Afsharid_dynasty" title="Afsharid dynasty">Afsharid dynasty</a> and later under the <a href="/wiki/Qajar_dynasty" title="Qajar dynasty">Qajar dynasty</a> of Persia.</p>

<p>In June 1804, the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> took control of Shirak region at the beginning of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russo-Persian_War_(1804-1813)" title="Russo-Persian War (1804-1813)">Russo-Persian War of 1804–1813</a>. The region became officially part of the Russian Empire at the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Gulistan" title="Treaty of Gulistan">Treaty of Gulistan</a> signed on 1 January 1813. During the period of the Russian rule, the region witnessed a swift growth and the town of Gyumri became one of the developing cities in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transcaucasus" title="Transcaucasus">Transcaucasus</a>. In 1829, in the aftermath of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russo-Turkish_War_(1828%E2%80%9329)" title="Russo-Turkish War (1828–29)">Russo-Turkish War</a>, there was a big influx of Armenian population, as around 3,000 families who had migrated from territories in the Ottoman Empire -in particular from the towns of <a href="/wiki/Kars" title="Kars">Kars</a>, <a href="/wiki/Erzurum" title="Erzurum">Erzurum</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Do%C4%9Fubeyaz%C4%B1t" title="Doğubeyazıt">Doğubeyazıt</a>- settled in Shirak. The Russian poet <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Pushkin" title="Alexander Pushkin">Alexander Pushkin</a> visited the region during his journey to <a href="/wiki/Erzurum" title="Erzurum">Erzurum</a> in 1829.</p>
<p>In 1837 Russian <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_I_of_Russia" title="Nicholas I of Russia">Tsar Nicholas I</a> arrived in Shirak and re-founded the city of Gyumri as Alexandropol. The name was chosen in honour of Tsar Nicholas I's wife, Princess <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexandra_Fyodorovna_(Charlotte_of_Prussia)" title="Alexandra Fyodorovna (Charlotte of Prussia)">Charlotte of Prussia</a>, who had changed her name to <i>Alexandra Fyodorovna</i> after converting to <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Orthodox Christianity</a>. A major <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> fortress was built in Alexandropol in 1837. The city was completely rebuilt by 1840 to become the centre of the newly established <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Alexandropol_Uyezd&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Alexandropol Uyezd (page does not exist)">Alexandropol Uyezd</a>, experiencing rapid growth during its first decade. The Alexandropol Uyezd included the northern Armenian territories of Shirak, Lori and Tavush.</p>
<p>In 1849, the Alexandropol Uyezd became part of the <a href="/wiki/Erivan_Governorate" title="Erivan Governorate">Erivan Governorate</a>, and Shirak became an important outpost for the Imperial Russian armed forces in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transcaucasus" title="Transcaucasus">Transcaucasus</a> where their military barracks were established.</p>
<p>During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russo-Turkish_War_(1877%E2%80%9378)" title="Russo-Turkish War (1877–78)">Russo-Turkish War of 1877–78</a> Shirak became one of the major centres of the Russian troops. After the establishment of the railway station in Alexandropol in 1899, Shirak witnessed a significant growth as centre of trade and industry, becoming the most developed region within eastern Armenia.</p>

<p>After the <a href="/wiki/October_Revolution" title="October Revolution">October Revolution</a> of 1917 and the Russian withdrawal from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Caucasus" title="South Caucasus">South Caucasus</a>, the <a href="/wiki/First_Republic_of_Armenia" title="First Republic of Armenia">First Republic of Armenia</a> was proclaimed on 28 May 1918, which included Shirak. On 10 May 1920, the local Bolshevik Armenians aided by the Musilm population, attempted a <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">coup d'état</a> in Alexandropol against the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Revolutionary_Federation" title="Armenian Revolutionary Federation">Dashnak</a> government of Armenia. The <a href="/wiki/May_Uprising" title="May Uprising">uprising</a> was suppressed by the Armenian government on May 14 and its leaders were executed. However, during <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turkish-Armenian_War" title="Turkish-Armenian War">another Turkish invasion</a>, Turkish troops again attacked Shirak and occupied Alexandropol on 7 November 1920. Armenia was forced to sign the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Alexandropol" title="Treaty of Alexandropol">Treaty of Alexandropol</a> on December 3 to stop the Turkish advance towards Yerevan, however a concurrent Soviet invasion led to the fall of the Armenian government on December 2. The Turkish forces withdrew from Alexandropol after the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Kars" title="Treaty of Kars">Treaty of Kars</a> was signed in October 1921 by the unrecognized Soviet and Turkish governments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Under the Bolsheviks, Alexandropol was renamed Leninakan in 1924, after the deceased Soviet leader <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Lenin" title="Vladimir Lenin">Vladimir Lenin</a>. Shirak became a major industrial region within the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Armenian Soviet Socialist Republic">Armenian Soviet Socialist Republic</a>. Leninakan was the second-largest city, after the capital Yerevan. However, Shirak, and particularly Leninakan, suffered major damage during the <a href="/wiki/1988_Armenian_earthquake" title="1988 Armenian earthquake">1988 Armenian earthquake</a> which devastated many parts of northern Armenia. The earthquake occurred along a known thrust fault with a length of 60 kilometers (37 mi). Its <a href="/wiki/Strike_and_dip" title="Strike and dip">strike</a> was parallel to the Caucasus range and dipped to the north-northeast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bolt_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-bolt-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 1930 until 1995, modern-day Shirak was divided into 5 <a href="/wiki/Raion" title="Raion">raions</a> and 1 city of republican subordination within the Armenian SSR: <i>Amasia</i>, <i>Ghukasyan</i>, <i>Akhurian</i>, <i>Ani</i>, <i>Artik</i> and the city of <i>Leniankan</i>. With the territorial administration reform of 1995, the 5 raions and the city of Gyumri (Leninakan) were merged to form the Shirak Province.</p>

<p>According to the 2011 official census, Shirak has a population of 251,941 (121,615 men and 130,326 women), forming around 8.3% of the entire population of Armenia. The urban population is 146,908 (58.3%) and the rural is 105,033 (41.7%). The province has 3 urban and 116 rural communities. The largest urban community is the provincial centre of Gyumri, with a population of 121,976. The other urban centres of Artik and Maralik have a population of 19,534 and 5,398 respectively.</p>
<p>With a population of 4,838, the village of <a href="/wiki/Azatan" title="Azatan">Azatan</a> is the largest rural municipality of Shirak.</p>
<p>The dialect of Shirak is a variant of <a href="/wiki/Karin_dialect" title="Karin dialect">Karin dialect</a>, closely related to Western Armenian.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hovannisian_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hovannisian-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p>The majority of the Shirak Province population are ethnic Armenians who belong to the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Apostolic_Church" title="Armenian Apostolic Church">Armenian Apostolic Church</a>. The northern and middle parts of Shirak are under the jurisdiction of the <a href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Shirak" title="Diocese of Shirak">Diocese of Shirak</a> headed by Bishop Mikayel Ajapahyan of the <a href="/wiki/Cathedral_of_the_Holy_Mother_of_God,_Gyumri" title="Cathedral of the Holy Mother of God, Gyumri">Cathedral of the Holy Mother of God</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gyumri" title="Gyumri">Gyumri</a>, while the southern part is under the jurisdiction of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Diocese_of_Artik&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Diocese of Artik (page does not exist)">Diocese of Artik</a> headed by Archimendrite Narek Avagyan of the Saint Gregory Cathedral in <a href="/wiki/Artik" title="Artik">Artik</a>.</p>
<p>There is a significant minority of <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Catholic_Church" title="Armenian Catholic Church">Armenian Catholics</a> in Shirak. The number of the Catholic population in the province is around 30,000. Gyumri is home to around 20,000, while the rest are found in the surrounding rural settlements. The villages of <a href="/wiki/Arevik,_Shirak" title="Arevik, Shirak">Arevik</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arpeni" title="Arpeni">Arpeni</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bavra" title="Bavra">Bavra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ghazanchi" title="Ghazanchi">Ghazanchi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marmashen" title="Marmashen">Marmashen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mets_Sepasar" title="Mets Sepasar">Mets Sepasar</a> <a href="/wiki/Panik,_Armenia" title="Panik, Armenia">Panik</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sizavet" title="Sizavet">Sizavet</a> have a majority of Armenian Catholics, while the village of <a href="/wiki/Azatan" title="Azatan">Azatan</a> has around 1,500 Catholis (30% of village's population). As of 2016, Shirak is home to 9 catholic operating churches. The <a href="/wiki/Cathedral_of_the_Holy_Martyrs,_Gyumri" title="Cathedral of the Holy Martyrs, Gyumri">Cathedral of the Holy Martyrs</a> in Gyumri is the seat of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armenian_Catholic_Ordinariate_of_Eastern_Europe" title="Armenian Catholic Ordinariate of Eastern Europe">Armenian Catholic Ordinariate of Eastern Europe</a> headed by Archbishop Raphaël François Minassian.</p>
<p>The presence of the small Russian Orthodox community along with the Russian military base personnel in Gyumri is marked with the Saint Alexandra the Martyr's Church, Saint Michael the Archangel's Church and the church of Saint Arsenije.</p>
<p>The small village of <a href="/wiki/Shirakavan,_Armenia" title="Shirakavan, Armenia">Shirakavan</a> has around 30 <a href="/wiki/Yazidis_in_Armenia" title="Yazidis in Armenia">Yazidi</a> residents.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shirak_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Agriculture">Agriculture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shirak_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Agriculture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Buckwheat_fields_in_Shirak_Province,_Armenia_7-15-16,_16_47_38.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="2448" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Buckwheat_fields_in_Shirak_Province%2C_Armenia_7-15-16%2C_16_47_38.jpg/220px-Buckwheat_fields_in_Shirak_Province%2C_Armenia_7-15-16%2C_16_47_38.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Buckwheat_fields_in_Shirak_Province%2C_Armenia_7-15-16%2C_16_47_38.jpg/330px-Buckwheat_fields_in_Shirak_Province%2C_Armenia_7-15-16%2C_16_47_38.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Buckwheat_fields_in_Shirak_Province%2C_Armenia_7-15-16%2C_16_47_38.jpg/440px-Buckwheat_fields_in_Shirak_Province%2C_Armenia_7-15-16%2C_16_47_38.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The economy of the province is mainly based on agriculture, including farming and cattle-breeding. It has a share of 11.6% in the annual total agricultural product of Armenia. Around 80% (2,145.5 km²) of the total area of the province are <a href="/wiki/Arable_land" title="Arable land">arable lands</a>, out of which 36.7% (787 km²) are ploughed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The fertile Shirak plain is the largest producer of grains and potato in Armenia. The irrigation system in the province is highly developed. 9 water reservoirs of different sizes -with a total capacity of 673,000,000 cubic metres (2.38<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup>10</sup> cu ft) are able to irrigate around 300 km<sup>2</sup> (116 sq mi) of farmlands.</p>
<p>Shirak is the 1st among the Armenian provinces in cattle-breeding business. There are also <a href="/wiki/Fish_farming" title="Fish farming">fish farming</a> ponds near Gyumri and many rural communities.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry">Industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shirak_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Barevery_factori_in_Gyumri_02.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3240" data-file-width="4320" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Barevery_factori_in_Gyumri_02.JPG/220px-Barevery_factori_in_Gyumri_02.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Barevery_factori_in_Gyumri_02.JPG/330px-Barevery_factori_in_Gyumri_02.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Barevery_factori_in_Gyumri_02.JPG/440px-Barevery_factori_in_Gyumri_02.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Barevery_factori_in_Gyumri_02.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Gyumri Brewery</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>During the Soviet period, the region was a major industrial hub within the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armenian_SSR" title="Armenian SSR">Armenian SSR</a>. After the independence, the industrial sector of the region has drastically declined. Currently, the province contributes by 3.5% in the annual total industrial product of Armenia. Shirak is the largest producer of building materials in Armenia, mainly <a href="/wiki/Tufa" title="Tufa">tufa</a> stones and <a href="/wiki/Pumice" title="Pumice">pumice</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li>The industrial sector in the provincial centre Gyumri includes the production of building materials (tufa and basalt), hosiery and textile manufacturing, food processing and dairy products, alcoholic drinks, electronic machines, etc. The largest industrial plant in Gyumri is the <i>Gyumri-Beer</i> Brewery opened in 1972. The factory produces a variety of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lager_beer" title="Lager beer">lager beer</a> under the brands <i>Gyumri</i>, <i>Ararat</i> and <i>Aleksandrapol</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The city is also home to the "Factory of Bending Machinses" opened in 1912, the "Arshaluys" hosiery manufacturing enterprise established in 1926, the "Karhat" machine tools plant opened in 1959, the "Chap Chemical LLC" since 1999, the "Armtex Group" clothing factory since 2000, and the "Lentex" hosiery manufacturing plant is operating since 2001. Other industrial firms of the city include the "Aleqpol" factory for dairy products, the "Anusharan" confectionery plant, and the "Gold Plast" plant for building materials.</li>
<li>The town of Artik is famous for its <a href="/wiki/Tufa" title="Tufa">tufa</a> stones. It is home to many stone-processing plants that produce <a href="/wiki/Travertine" title="Travertine">travertine</a>, tufa and <a href="/wiki/Basalt" title="Basalt">basalt</a>, including the "ArtikTuf" form established in 1928, the "TufaBlocks Factory" founded in 1997, and the "Karastgh" stone-processing factory operating since 2005. Artik is also home to the "Vartan-Anahit LLC" for metal-plastic products, the Artik Cheese Factory, the "Eliz Group" for dairy products, the "Artik Factory for Vacuum Stoves", and the "Artik Steklomash" metal casting factory.</li>
<li>During the Soviet period, the town of Maralik had many large industrial firms with a lights and electronics factory and 3 plants for building materials production. Currently, the only surviving plant in the town is the Maralik cotton-spinning factory.</li>
<li>Factories for dairy products are also found in Shirak, with the largest 2 firms are located in the villages of <a href="/wiki/Azatan" title="Azatan">Azatan</a> (Igit Dairy Factory since 1993) and <a href="/wiki/Musayelyan,_Shirak" title="Musayelyan, Shirak">Musayelyan</a> (Ashotsk Cheese Factory since 1996). The village of Akhuryan is home to the "Lusastgh-Sugar" factory (opened in 2010), the largest sugar producers in the Southern Caucasus region. The village of Shirakavan is home to the "Shirak Wine Factory" opened in 2009.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shirak_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<div class="thumbcaption">

<a href="/wiki/Gyumri_City_Stadium" title="Gyumri City Stadium">Gyumri City Stadium</a></div>

<p><a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">Football</a>, <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chess" title="Chess">chess</a> are the most popular sports in the province. However, other Olympic sports including <a href="/wiki/Wrestling" title="Wrestling">wrestling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olympic_weightlifting" title="Olympic weightlifting">weightlifting</a> are also popular, mainly in Gyumri. Shirak is home to many former and current World, Olympic and European champions in several types of sports who competed under the flag of Soviet Union and later under the Armenian flag.</p>
<p>The city is represented by the <a href="/wiki/FC_Shirak" title="FC Shirak">FC Shirak</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Premier_League" title="Armenian Premier League">Armenian Premier League</a> competition. <a href="/wiki/Aragats_FC" title="Aragats FC">Aragats FC</a> were the second football club in Gyumri, but they were dissolved in 2002 due to financial difficulties.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/FC_Tufagorts" title="FC Tufagorts">FC Tufagorts</a> (dissolved in 1995) and <a href="/wiki/FC_Sipan" title="FC Sipan">FC Sipan</a> (dissolved in 1993), both from the town of <a href="/wiki/Artik" title="Artik">Artik</a>, had also represented the province in the football competitions of Armenia.</p>
<p>The largest sport venue of the province is the <a href="/wiki/Gyumri_City_Stadium" title="Gyumri City Stadium">Gyumri City Stadium</a>. Gyumri is also home to the FFA <a href="/wiki/Gyumri_Football_Academy" title="Gyumri Football Academy">Football Academy</a>. The towns of Artik and Maralik have a football stadium each with minor capacities.</p>
<p>Gyumri is also famous for winter sports. The Gyumri school of winter sports renovated in 2015-16, is named after Ludvig Mnatsakanyan.</p>
