<p><b>Masamune Shirow</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">士郎 正宗</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shirō Masamune</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born November 23, 1961)</span> is the <a href="/wiki/Pen_name" title="Pen name">pen name</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> of Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manga_artist" title="Manga artist">manga artist</a> <b>Masanori Ota</b>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The pen name is derived from the legendary sword-smith <a href="/wiki/Masamune" title="Masamune">Masamune</a>. Shirow is best known for the manga <i><a href="/wiki/Ghost_in_the_Shell_(manga)" title="Ghost in the Shell (manga)">Ghost in the Shell</a></i>, which has since been turned into two theatrical <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> movies, two anime television series, an anime television movie, an anime OVA series, and several <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video games</a>. Shirow is also known for creating erotic art.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Masamune_Shirow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in the <a href="/wiki/Hy%C5%8Dgo_Prefecture" title="Hyōgo Prefecture">Hyōgo Prefecture</a> capital city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kobe,_Hyogo" title="Kobe, Hyogo">Kobe</a>, he studied oil painting at <a href="/wiki/Osaka_University_of_Arts" title="Osaka University of Arts">Osaka University of Arts</a>. While in college, he developed an interest in <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a>, which led him to create his own complete work, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Magic_M-66" title="Black Magic M-66">Black Magic</a></i>, which was published in the manga <a href="/wiki/Fanzine" title="Fanzine">fanzine</a> <i>Atlas</i>. His work caught the eye of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Seishinsha&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Seishinsha (page does not exist)">Seishinsha</a> President Harumichi Aoki, who offered to publish him.</p>
<p>The result was best-selling<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> manga <i><a href="/wiki/Appleseed_(manga)" title="Appleseed (manga)">Appleseed</a></i>, a full volume of densely plotted drama taking place in an ambiguous future. The story was a sensation, and won the 1986 <a href="/wiki/Seiun_Award" title="Seiun Award">Seiun Award</a> for Best Manga. After a professional reprint of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Magic_M-66" title="Black Magic M-66">Black Magic</a></i> and a second volume of <i>Appleseed</i>, he released <i><a href="/wiki/Dominion_(manga)" title="Dominion (manga)">Dominion</a></i> in 1986. Two more volumes of <i>Appleseed</i> followed before he began work on <i><a href="/wiki/Ghost_in_the_Shell" title="Ghost in the Shell">Ghost in the Shell</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2007, he collaborated again with <a href="/wiki/Production_I.G" title="Production I.G">Production I.G</a> to co-create the original concept for the anime television series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shinreigari/Ghost_Hound" title="Shinreigari/Ghost Hound">Shinreigari/Ghost Hound</a></i>, Production I.G's 20th year anniversary project. A further original collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Production_I.G" title="Production I.G">Production I.G</a> began airing in April 2008, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Real_Drive" title="Real Drive">Real Drive</a></i>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Galgrease">Galgrease</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Masamune_Shirow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Galgrease">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Galgrease</i> (published in <i>Uppers Magazine</i>, 2002) is the collected name of several erotic manga and poster books by Shirow. The name comes from the fact that the women depicted often look "greased".</p>
<p>The first series of <i>Galgrease</i> booklets included four issues each in the following settings:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>Wild Wet West</i> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wild_West" title="Wild West">Wild West</a>-themed)</li>
<li><i>Hellhound</i> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Horror_(genre)" title="Horror (genre)">Horror</a>-themed)</li>
<li><i>Galhound</i> (Near-future <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a>–themed)</li>
</ul>
<p>The second series included another run of 12 booklets in the following worlds:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>Wild Wet Quest</i> (A <i><a href="/wiki/Tomb_Raider" title="Tomb Raider">Tomb Raider</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Indiana_Jones" title="Indiana Jones">Indiana Jones</a></i>–style sequel to <i>Wild Wet West</i>)</li>
<li><i>Hellcat</i> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pirate" title="Pirate">Pirate</a>-themed)</li>
<li><i>Galhound 2</i> (Near-future <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a>–themed)</li>
</ul>
<p>After each regular series, there were one or more bonus poster books that revisited the existing characters and settings.</p>
