<p><b>Shironishi Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">城西駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shironishi-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_station" title="Railway station">railway station</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Iida_Line" title="Iida Line">Iida Line</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tenry%C5%AB-ku,_Hamamatsu" title="Tenryū-ku, Hamamatsu">Tenryū-ku</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hamamatsu,_Shizuoka" title="Hamamatsu, Shizuoka">Hamamatsu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shizuoka_Prefecture" title="Shizuoka Prefecture">Shizuoka Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It is located 70.5 rail kilometers from the southern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terminal_station" title="Terminal station">terminus</a> of the Iida Line at <a href="/wiki/Toyohashi_Station" title="Toyohashi Station">Toyohashi Station</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_history">Station history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shironishi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Station history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shironishi Station was established on November 11, 1955, as a station on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_National_Railway" title="Japan National Railway">Japan National Railway</a> (JNR), when the Iida line between <a href="/wiki/Sakuma_Station" title="Sakuma Station">Sakuma Station</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Czore_Station" title="Ōzore Station">Ōzore Station</a> was rerouted to avoid the rising waters of the <a href="/wiki/Sakuma_Dam" title="Sakuma Dam">Sakuma Dam</a>. Freight services were discontinued in 1974. The station has been unmanned since 1984. Along with its division and <a href="/wiki/Privatization" title="Privatization">privatization</a> of JNR on April 1, 1987, the station came under the control and operation of the <a href="/wiki/Central_Japan_Railway_Company" title="Central Japan Railway Company">Central Japan Railway Company</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Layout">Layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shironishi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shironishi Station is an unmanned station with a single elevated <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platform</a>, and small station building. The station formerly had a single <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platform</a>, but was rebuilt in 2008.</p>
