<p><i><b>Shirobako</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">シロバコ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. <i>White Box</i>)</span> is an <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> television series produced by <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Entertainment Japan</a> and studio <a href="/wiki/P.A.Works" title="P.A.Works">P.A.Works</a>, which handled the animation. The series was directed by <a href="/wiki/Tsutomu_Mizushima" title="Tsutomu Mizushima">Tsutomu Mizushima</a> and aired in Japan between October 9, 2014 and March 26, 2015. A manga adaptation began serialization in <a href="/wiki/ASCII_Media_Works" title="ASCII Media Works">ASCII Media Works</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Dengeki_Daioh" title="Dengeki Daioh">Dengeki Daioh</a></i> magazine in September 2014, and a novel was published by <a href="/wiki/Shueisha" title="Shueisha">Shueisha</a> in January 2015.</p>
<p>The title <i>Shirobako</i> refers to videos that are distributed to the production staff members prior to its release. These videos were at a time distributed as VHS tapes enclosed in white boxes and are still referred to as "white boxes" (thus the meaning of <i>shirobako</i>) despite the fact that the white enclosures are no longer in use.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shirobako&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The story follows a group of five best friends, Aoi Miyamori, Ema Yasuhara, Shizuka Sakaki, Misa Tōdō, and Midori Imai, who all go into the <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> industry after their experiences in the animation club of their high school, with the dream of working on an actual anime together one day. The girls all go into different fields of specialty: Ema is a <a href="/wiki/Key_frame" title="Key frame">key animator</a>; Shizuka is a <a href="/wiki/Voice_acting_in_Japan" title="Voice acting in Japan">voice actress</a>; Misa is a <a href="/wiki/3D_computer_graphics" title="3D computer graphics">3D computer graphics</a> operator; Midori, still a college student, aspires to become a story writer; and Aoi is a <a href="/wiki/Production_assistant" title="Production assistant">production assistant</a>, though she has no true idea about what to pursue as a career. However, the girls experience various obstacles in their careers: Aoi and Ema, new employees at animation studio Musashino Animation, find themselves under extreme stress every day from their demanding workloads as they work on company projects; Shizuka struggles to find a voice acting job she can be satisfied with; and Misa experiences difficulties in adjusting to her unsatisfactory workplace. The story largely follows Aoi as she struggles to do her job by making all of the necessary deliveries and coordinating efforts between other staff members involved in Musashino Animation's projects.</p>
<p>The first half of the series primarily revolves around Musashino Animation's production of an original anime series called <i>Exodus!</i>. Production of the series is greatly troubled largely due to its lazy and childish director Seiichi Kinoshita, whose reputation was damaged by a previous project of his, which was a massive critical and commercial flop. While Aoi is faced with a number of problems, both personal and professional, Ema becomes temporarily discouraged after being unable to draw a specific set of key frames portraying an impossible movement, though her worries are assuaged by the advice of one of her coworkers. Also, Misa decides to quit her job after receiving some advice from her boss. Eventually, as the finale of the series draws near, Seiichi drastically changes the ending, leaving Aoi to find more animators to draw scenes for it while most of her colleagues are put out of action. In her search, she learns that one of Musashino's key animators, Shigeru Sugie, worked on her favorite childhood anime <i>Andes Chucky</i>. Sugie supervises the animation of the entire ending, which inspires Ema to continue to work harder in her job.</p>
<p>The second half of the series primarily revolves around Musashino Animation's production of an anime series called <i>The Third Aerial Girls Squad</i>, which is adapted from the <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> by Takezō Nogame. With the production desk left severely understaffed, Aoi is promoted to <a href="/wiki/Unit_production_manager" title="Unit production manager">production manager</a> and has to take it upon herself to mentor new production assistants, including Daisuke Hiraoka, who is extremely bitter and jaded. He complicates the production by bringing in unreliable animators and studios for Musashino's outsource work. In addition, Musashino is unable to establish proper communication with Nogame due to his incompetent editor, which stalls production even further. Meanwhile, Ema is promoted to assistant general animation supervisor, Misa finds a new job at a CGI animation studio cooperating with Musashino in its project, and Midori finds a job as Musashino's setting instructor through Aoi. However, Shizuka is turned down while auditioning for one of <i>The Third Aerial Girls Squad</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s characters, which discourages her as she is the only girl in the group unable to participate in the project. As she tries to narrow down the reasons behind Hiraoka's behavior, Aoi learns from Musashino's president Masato Marukawa that the company's predecessor, Musashino Pictures, was the studio that produced <i>Andes Chucky</i>, which gives her resolve to finish the production. Finally, Seiichi is able to have a personal conversation with Nogame about their conflicting views over the anime's ending, and they work out a compromise. This leads to Shizuka being hired to voice a character in the new ending, thus fulfilling the girls' promise of working on an anime together.</p>
<p>While delivering the final episode to a television station on its air-date, Aoi reflects on her past experiences, which causes her to have an epiphany about working in the anime industry. At the celebratory party for the final episode's airing, she is congratulated for her support and delivers a speech praising everyone's pooled efforts for making <i>The Third Aerial Girls Squad</i>. Afterwards, Aoi, Ema, Shizuka, Misa, and Midori meet up at a balcony and reaffirm their resolve to one day remake the anime they made back in high school, also promising each other to continue making great anime along the way.</p>

<ul>
<li><span id="Aoi_Miyamori">Aoi Miyamori</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">宮森 あおい</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Miyamori Aoi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, <span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Juri_Kimura" title="Juri Kimura">Juri Kimura</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-char1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-char1-3">[3]</a></sup></span>)</span> is a production assistant of Musashino Animation<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-char1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-char1-3">[3]</a></sup> and later the production manager. She is a former member of the Kaminoyama High School animation club and is called "Aoi" by Ema and Shizuka, nicknamed "Oi" by Misa and Midori, and referred to as Myāmori <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">みゃーもり</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> by Erika. Having worked at Musashino Animation for a year-and-a-half, she has great driving skills and is acknowledged by her coworkers Erika Yano and Tatsuya Ochiai as suitable for the production industry. However, she admits that she has not officially decided exactly what job she will take in the anime industry.</li>
<li><span id="Ema_Yasuhara">Ema Yasuhara</span> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">安原 絵麻</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yasuhara Ema</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, <span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Haruka_Yoshimura" title="Haruka Yoshimura">Haruka Yoshimura</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-char2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-char2-4">[4]</a></sup></span>)</span> is a key animator at Musashino Animation and a former member of the Kaminoyama High School animation club. She is later promoted to the position of assistant general animation supervisor for <i>The Third Aerial Girls Squad</i>. She admires the work of character designer and general animation supervisor Rinko Ogasawara, who is her higher-up. She has a secret that she shares with Iguchi: they do workout dances on the roof together.</li>
<li>Shizuka Sakaki <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">坂木 しずか</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sakaki Shizuka</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, <span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Haruka_Chisuga" title="Haruka Chisuga">Haruka Chisuga</a></span>)</span> is a voice actress at Akaoni Production and a former member of the Kaminoyama High School animation club. She is called "Zuka" by her friends. She also works part-time as a waitress at a pub. Her first notable role was as Lucy Weller, a character featured in the final episode of Musashino Animation's <i>The Third Girls Aerial Squad</i> anime adaptation.</li>
<li>Misa Tōdō <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">藤堂 美沙</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tōdō Misa</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, <span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: Asami Takano</span>)</span> is an aspiring 3D computer graphics operator and a former member of the Kaminoyama High School animation club. She once worked at computer graphics studio Super Media Creations, but later quits due to her dissatisfaction with her work and finds employment at Studio Kanabun. Nicknamed "Mii" by her friends, she was initially confused in choosing between working in the fields of 2D and 3D, but ultimately chose the latter. She was inspired to work because of Ema's drawings. She dislikes working on projects that have no plot or emotion behind them, which is the reason she leaves Super Media Creations, as she only designed wheels during her time there.</li>
<li>Midori Imai <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">今井 みどり</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Imai Midori</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, <span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Hitomi_%C5%8Cwada" title="Hitomi Ōwada">Hitomi Ōwada</a></span>)</span> is a university student and aspiring scriptwriter. She is a former member of the Kaminoyama High School animation club and is nicknamed "Rii" by her friends. She is the youngest of the group and continues to strive to reach her goal. Her enthusiasm on researching earns her the nickname "Diesel" after she creates a helpful guide for Musashino Animation when they make an informal request for information on engines for <i>Exodus!</i>. She later finds employment at Musashino as a setting instructor, and later assists in the script ordering.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shirobako&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Print_media">Print media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shirobako&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Print media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> adaptation titled <i>Shirobako: Kaminoyama Kōkō Animation Dōkōkai</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">SHIROBAKO 〜上山高校アニメーション同好会〜</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, written by Kenji Sugihara and illustrated by Mizutama, began serialization in the November 2014 issue of <a href="/wiki/ASCII_Media_Works" title="ASCII Media Works">ASCII Media Works</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Dengeki_Daioh" title="Dengeki Daioh">Dengeki Daioh</a></i> magazine sold on September 27, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ann-promo1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-ann-promo1-5">[5]</a></sup> A short manga based on the show's fictional series <i>The Third Girls Aerial Squad</i> was distributed by Takeshi Nogami at <a href="/wiki/Comiket" title="Comiket">Comiket 88</a> in August 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>A 224-page novel adaptation, titled <i>Shirobako Introduction</i> and written by Michiko Itō and Hajime Tanka, was published by <a href="/wiki/Shueisha" title="Shueisha">Shueisha</a> under their Jump j Books <a href="/wiki/Imprint_(trade_name)" title="Imprint (trade name)">imprint</a> on January 27, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shirobako&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Shirobako_episodes" title="List of Shirobako episodes">List of Shirobako episodes</a></div>
<p>The 24-episode <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> television series was produced by <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Entertainment Japan</a> and <a href="/wiki/P.A.Works" title="P.A.Works">P.A.Works</a>, the latter of whom handled the animation. It was directed by <a href="/wiki/Tsutomu_Mizushima" title="Tsutomu Mizushima">Tsutomu Mizushima</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and written by <a href="/wiki/Michiko_Yokote" title="Michiko Yokote">Michiko Yokote</a>, with the music produced by <a href="/wiki/Shir%C5%8D_Hamaguchi" title="Shirō Hamaguchi">Shirō Hamaguchi</a>. The series aired on <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metropolitan_Television" title="Tokyo Metropolitan Television">Tokyo MX</a> between October 9, 2014 and March 26, 2015 and was simulcast by <a href="/wiki/Crunchyroll" title="Crunchyroll">Crunchyroll</a>. <a href="/wiki/Original_video_animation" title="Original video animation">Original video animation</a> (OVA) episodes featuring fictional anime are included on the series' third and seventh Blu-ray Disc/DVD volumes, released on February 25, 2015 and July 29, 2015, respectively. <a href="/wiki/Sentai_Filmworks" title="Sentai Filmworks">Sentai Filmworks</a> has licensed the series for release in North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Madman_Entertainment" title="Madman Entertainment">Madman Entertainment</a> licensed the series in Australia and New Zealand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the first twelve episodes, the opening theme is "Colorful Box" by <a href="/wiki/Yoko_Ishida" title="Yoko Ishida">Yoko Ishida</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ann-promo1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-ann-promo1-5">[5]</a></sup> while the ending theme is "Animetic Love Letter" sung by Juri Kimura, Haruka Yoshimura, and Haruka Chisuga. For episode one, the opening theme is "I'm Sorry Exodus" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">あいむそーりーEXODUS</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Aimu Sōrī Ekusodasu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> sung by Tracy (<a href="/wiki/Mai_Nakahara" title="Mai Nakahara">Mai Nakahara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shizuka_It%C5%8D" title="Shizuka Itō">Shizuka Itō</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ai_Kayano" title="Ai Kayano">Ai Kayano</a>), which is later used as the opening theme for the first OVA episode. For episodes 13 onwards, the opening theme is "Takarabako (Treasure Box)" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">宝箱-TREASURE BOX-</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> by <a href="/wiki/Masami_Okui" title="Masami Okui">Masami Okui</a>, while the ending theme is "Platinum Jet" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">プラチナジェット</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Purachina Jetto</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> by Donuts Quintet (Kimura, Yoshimura, Chisuga, Asami Takano, and Hitomi Ōwada). The ending theme for episode 19 is "Yama Harinezumi Andes Chucky" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">山はりねずみアンデスチャッキー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yama Harinezumi Andesu Chakkī</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, Mountain Hedgehog Andes Chucky)</span> by Miyuki Kunitake. The ending theme for the first original video animation episode is "C Melo kara Ai o Komete" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">Cメロから愛を込めて</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> by Mai Nakahara. For the second original video animation episode, the opening theme is "Alice in Blue" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">アリス・イン・ブルー</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> and the ending theme is "Angel Fly"; both songs are by <a href="/wiki/Rita_(Japanese_singer)" title="Rita (Japanese singer)">Rita</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shirobako&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In a review by <a href="/wiki/Anime_News_Network" title="Anime News Network">Anime News Network</a>, reviewer Rose Bridges gave the series an A rating and lauded it for its concept and character development. She particularly praised the characterization and development of main character Aoi Miyamori. She went on to say that "<i>Shirobako</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s presence brightens the anime world, so I hope this isn't the end. I'm glad it set itself up so that it doesn't have to be." Bridges also alluded to the possibility of a second season several times in her review.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Shirobako</i> won the <a href="/wiki/Animation_Kobe#Television_Award" title="Animation Kobe">Animation Kobe Television Award</a> in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> It also won Animation of the Year at the 2016 <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Anime_Award" title="Tokyo Anime Award">Tokyo Anime Awards</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and Animation Department Committee Recommended Works Award at the 19th <a href="/wiki/Japan_Media_Arts_Festival" title="Japan Media Arts Festival">Japan Media Arts Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
