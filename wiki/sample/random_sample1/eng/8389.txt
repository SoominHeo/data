<p><b>Yu Shirota</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">城田 優</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shirota Yū</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, born December 26, 1985, is a Japanese-Spanish actor and singer, most known for his role as Kagurazaka Makoto in the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_television_drama" title="Japanese television drama">Japanese television drama</a> live-action adaption of <i><a href="/wiki/Hanazakari_no_Kimitachi_e" title="Hanazakari no Kimitachi e">Hana-Kimi</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Kunimitsu_Tezuka" title="Kunimitsu Tezuka">Kunimitsu Tezuka</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Tenimyu" title="Tenimyu">Prince of Tennis Musicals</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Tuxedo_Mask" title="Tuxedo Mask">Tuxedo Mask</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_Musicals" title="Sailor Moon Musicals">Sailor Moon Musicals</a>, and as Kei Shinjo in <i><a href="/wiki/Rookies_(manga)#Drama" title="Rookies (manga)">Rookies</a></i>. In 2009, he was named one of the most promising actors and actresses, placing 8th in a poll conducted by Oricon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oricon_Poll_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Oricon_Poll-1">[1]</a></sup> He is a former member of the Watanabe Entertainment group, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D-BOYS" title="D-BOYS">D-BOYS</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yu_Shirota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shirota was born on December 26, 1985 to a Japanese father (城田光男 Shirota Mitsuo) and a Spanish mother Pepi Fernandez.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Shirota lived in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> from when he was three to about seven years. He returned to Japan for elementary school and can still speak Spanish in ordinary conversation. He attended high school at <a href="/wiki/Horikoshi_High_School" title="Horikoshi High School">Horikoshi High</a> along with <a href="/wiki/News_(band)" title="News (band)">NEWS</a> member <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yamashita_Tomohisa" title="Yamashita Tomohisa">Yamashita Tomohisa</a>, <a href="/wiki/WaT" title="WaT">WaT</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Teppei_Koike" title="Teppei Koike">Teppei Koike</a>, Japanese actor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ikuta_Toma" title="Ikuta Toma">Ikuta Toma</a> and <a href="/wiki/KAT-TUN" title="KAT-TUN">KAT-TUN</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Akanishi_Jin" title="Akanishi Jin">Akanishi Jin</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tanaka_Koki" title="Tanaka Koki">Tanaka Koki</a>.</p>
<p>According to Shirota, when he was little, his family liked to sing songs, so he would borrow his older sister's keyboard, while his mother taught him piano. Thus spawned Shirota's love for composing. He composed his first piece when he was in the fourth grade, titled "The Tear".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Early_Life_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Early_Life-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yu_Shirota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yu Shirota's acting career began from 2003 to 2004, when Shirota became the 7th actor to play <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tuxedo_Kamen" title="Tuxedo Kamen">Tuxedo Kamen</a> (Tuxedo Mask) in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_Musicals" title="Sailor Moon Musicals">Sailor Moon Musicals</a>. His first official performance as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tuxedo_Kamen" title="Tuxedo Kamen">Tuxedo Kamen</a> was on January 2, 2003, in the <i>2003 Winter Special Mugen Gakuen - Mistress Labyrinth (Kaiteiban)</i>. He also demonstrated his ability to play acoustic guitar on stage live, during the <i>Starlights - Ryuusei Densetsu</i> performances. The <i>2004 New Legend of Kaguya Island</i> was Shirota's last performance as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tuxedo_Kamen" title="Tuxedo Kamen">Tuxedo Kamen</a>. That night, on September 5, 2004, after he performed his final rendition of "Prince of Earth", he announced his graduation from the role and received flowers from <a href="/wiki/Kousei_Amano" title="Kousei Amano">Kousei Amano</a> who had previously played <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tuxedo_Kamen" title="Tuxedo Kamen">Tuxedo Kamen</a> in the original <i>1999 Kaguya Island</i> musical. He also began working for a year on the Japanese program, <i>Hanamaru Cafe</i>, as part of the Hanamaru apron team, often leaving set for locations and displaying prizes for viewers during game corners.</p>
<p>During this time, Shirota won the role of <a href="/wiki/Kunimitsu_Tezuka" title="Kunimitsu Tezuka">Kunimitsu Tezuka</a>, the stoic captain of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seigaku" title="Seigaku">Seigaku</a> Middle School's tennis club, in the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince_of_Tennis" title="Prince of Tennis">Prince of Tennis</a></i> musicals series, <i><a href="/wiki/Tenimyu" title="Tenimyu">Tenimyu</a></i>. From 2005 to 2006, he became the third actor to play Tezuka as a member of the second-generation Seigaku cast. He made his debut as Tezuka on January 8, 2005, in the <i>Side Yamabuki</i> performance in <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>. That same year, his role as Tezuka was carried into <a href="/wiki/The_Prince_of_Tennis_(film)" title="The Prince of Tennis (film)">the live adaptation film of the manga</a> and marked his first major film appearance. He also released a Best Actor's album in 2005, for his role as Tezuka. On March 29, 2006, in the Dream Live 3rd Live concert, he along with the majority of the second generation Seigaku cast, graduated from their roles.</p>
<p>Through 2006, Shirota began appearing in a couple dramas and movies including the 2005 Watanabe net drama, <i>Hice Cool</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hice_Cool_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hice_Cool-4">[4]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_television_drama" title="Japanese television drama">TV drama</a> special, <i>The Hit Parade</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hit_Parade_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hit_Parade-5">[5]</a></sup> portraying the real-life Mickey Curtis, and the <i>June Bride</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-June_Bride_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-June_Bride-6">[6]</a></sup> movie in which he played the title character, Natsumi, based on the original manga <i>Jyun Bride</i> by Satoshi Yoshida, resulted in a made for DVD release sequel, <i>Zoku June Bride</i>. In 2007, after nearly a year away from musicals, Shirota returned to stage and starred as Anthony Hope, the cheerful young sailor, in the Japanese stage production of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sweeney_Todd,_the_Demon_Barber_of_Fleet_Street" title="Sweeney Todd, the Demon Barber of Fleet Street">Sweeney Todd</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sweeney_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sweeney-7">[7]</a></sup> The musical opened on January 5, 2007 and ran through January 29, 2007 at the Nissay Theatre with additional performances during February in Osaka. Shirota then began appearing in a string of dramas, the first being the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_television_drama" title="Japanese television drama">TV drama</a>, <i>Dignity of Haken</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dignity_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dignity-8">[8]</a></sup> featured Shirota as the half-Spanish Ryuto Amaya, premiered on January 10, 2007, on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nippon_Television" title="Nippon Television">NTV</a> and ended its run on March 21, 2007. Shirota next appeared in his second <a href="/wiki/Japanese_television_drama" title="Japanese television drama">TV drama</a> appearance, in the April 2007 drama, <i><a href="/wiki/Seito_Shokun!" title="Seito Shokun!">Seito Shokun!</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shokun_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shokun-9">[9]</a></sup> which is an adaptation of the manga series, <i>Seito Shokun</i>. He played Ken Mizuhara, a physical education teacher. In his third <a href="/wiki/Japanese_television_drama" title="Japanese television drama">TV drama</a> role of the year, Shirota appeared in the popular adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Hana-Kimi" title="Hana-Kimi">Hanazakari No Kimitachi e</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hana-Kimi_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hana-Kimi-10">[10]</a></sup> which began on July 3, 2007, playing the character of Kagurazaka Makoto, a high-jumping rival to Sano Izumi.</i></p>
<p>Shirota also appeared in a string of films in 2007. The first being <i>Arakure KNIGHT</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KNIGHT_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-KNIGHT-11">[11]</a></sup> the live-action movie adaptation of the 1982 to 1988 manga series, <i>Aratanaru Shobaku</i> by Satosi Yoshida. The film opened in limited release, premiering at Cinema GAGA on April 28, 2007. The second of his three films, Shirota played a main supporting actor in the film adaptation of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gettsu_Itaya&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gettsu Itaya (page does not exist)">Gettsu Itaya</a>'s semi-autobiographical novel, <i>Waruboro</i> and was released on September 8, 2007<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Waruboro_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Waruboro-12">[12]</a></sup> as principle filmography began on September 20, 2006. Before October 2006, Shirota finished filming for his third movie, <i>Heat Island</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Heat_Island_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Heat_Island-13">[13]</a></sup> which was released on October 20, 2007 and is based on the book of the same title written by Kakine Ryuosuke. Shirota played Aki, a street gangster who moves around Shibuya (a district in Tokyo) causing trouble and accidentally obtains a large sum of money that belongs to a crime group; however, the money once belonged to an illegal casino operated by the <a href="/wiki/Yakuza" title="Yakuza">Yakuza</a> and both groups are after the money.</p>
<p>After Shirota's run as <a href="/wiki/Charles_Lindbergh" title="Charles Lindbergh">Charles Lindbergh</a> in the 2007 year-end Japanese production of the <i>Take Flight</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Take_Flight_Musical_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Take_Flight_Musical-14">[14]</a></sup> musical, Shirota starred in the January 2008 <a href="/wiki/Japanese_television_drama" title="Japanese television drama">TV drama</a>, <i>Koshonin - The Negotiator</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Koshonin_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Koshonin-15">[15]</a></sup> portraying Mariya Kyousuke, a condemned criminal. Next, Shirota starred in <a href="/wiki/Rookies_(manga)#Drama" title="Rookies (manga)">Rookies</a>, the successful baseball drama based on the manga of the same name by Morita Masanori, playing Kei Shinjo. The series first aired on April 19, 2008 and ended July 26, 2008 after 11 episodes. He would reprise his role as Kagurazaka Makoto in the 2008 <a href="/wiki/Hanazakari_no_Kimitachi_e" title="Hanazakari no Kimitachi e">Hana Kimi</a> special episode. In 2009, Shirota starred in the made-for-television movie, <i>Go Ape</i>, reprised his role as Shinjo Kei in the feature film for <i><a href="/wiki/Rookies_(manga)" title="Rookies (manga)">Rookies - Sotsugyou</a></i>, and co-starred with <a href="/wiki/Haruma_Miura" title="Haruma Miura">Haruma Miura</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Samurai_High_School" title="Samurai High School">Samurai High School</a></i> as the soft-spoken, Nakamura Tsuyoshi. He also reprised his role as Mariya Kyousuke for the second season of <i>Koshonin - The Negotiator</i> and the 2010 feature film release of <i>Koshonin - The Movie</i>. He is also set to be in the Japanese stage production of <i>Elisabeth</i> as Der Tod from August 2010 to October 2010.</p>
<p>He also plays Ryuji Danma in the 2012 live-action version of <a href="/wiki/Great_Teacher_Onizuka" title="Great Teacher Onizuka">Great Teacher Onizuka</a>.</p>
<p>Shirota makes his solo singing debut as "U" in May 2011 under <a href="/wiki/Avex_Trax" title="Avex Trax">Avex Trax</a>, releasing his debut triple A-side single, "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=U/Heart_of_glass/Sisyphus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="U/Heart of glass/Sisyphus (page does not exist)">U/Heart of glass/Sisyphus</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> His first album is called <i>UNO</i>.</p>
<p>For the Japanese edition of his solo debut album, BACK TO ME, <a href="/wiki/Backstreet_Boys" title="Backstreet Boys">Backstreet Boys</a> member <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Howie_D" title="Howie D">Howie D</a> (aka Howard Dorough) sought out Japanese idol Shirota for one of the bonus tracks, "If I Say" which was digitally released as a single on October 12, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="With_the_D-BOYS">With the D-BOYS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yu_Shirota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: With the D-BOYS">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shirota joined the Watanabe Entertainment group, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D-BOYS" title="D-BOYS">D-BOYS</a> in October 2004. As a member, he participated in photobook releases, stage performances, radio show hosting, event and meeting greetings, and filming variety shows.</p>
<p>With the D-BoYS, he was featured in their first two photobooks, the self-titled <i>D-Boys</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and the second called <i>Start</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He hosted a radio show called <i>"Marvelous Radio Vibration"</i> with fellow D-BOYS member <a href="/wiki/Kotaro_Yanagi" title="Kotaro Yanagi">Kotaro Yanagi</a> and Yukki.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The show aired on May 5, 2005 and ended its run on April 1, 2006. The D-BOYS then starred in their own drama documentary variety show called, <i>DD-Boys</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dd-boys_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-dd-boys-21">[21]</a></sup> in which Shirota's appeared in multiple episodes as himself. The show was 23 episodes long and ran from April 10 to September 25, 2006.</p>
<p>In June 2007, the D-BOYS starred in their first musical together called, <i>D-BOYS STAGE</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D-Boys_musical_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-D-Boys_musical-22">[22]</a></sup> which ran from June 3 to June 10 at the Space Zero theater in Tokyo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Shirota appeared in the musical only as a guest performer, making select appearances on June 3, June 8 and June 10, due to his busy schedule. The D-BOYS then starred in their 2nd musical, <i>The Last Game</i> to which on June 27, 2008, Shirota announced his graduation during the encore of the play while assuring the media that he would still remain under the Watanabe Entertainment management and still participate in certain D-BOYS projects and events. He officially graduated from the group on July 6, 2008.</p>
<p>Despite having graduated from the D-BOYS, in December 2009, he was featured in both the sixth DVD release of the D-BOYS' Boy Friend series, called <i>YU Channel</i> and their "Kotoshi no Natsu wa Mainichi Dokoka de D-BOYS ☆Uki-uki♪Yatta♪Hohohoy♪" performance events, resulting in appearances in all three <i>Natsu Doko 2009</i> CD and DVD releases, as well as appearances for promoting their second tour, <i>Fuyu Doko 2009</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yu_Shirota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In April 2006, Shirota experienced language study studying abroad for 1 week in the Australian Gold Coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> His experiences were recorded in the Language Study studying abroad 2007 journal series and covered in detail over 13 pages of his thoughts after the experience and the return home.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In June 2006, he then visited New York to see five Broadway musicals: <i><a href="/wiki/The_Producers_(musical)" title="The Producers (musical)">The Producers</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Rent_(musical)" title="Rent (musical)">Rent</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_25th_Annual_Putnam_County_Spelling_Bee" title="The 25th Annual Putnam County Spelling Bee">Spelling Bee</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sweeney_Todd_(musical)" title="Sweeney Todd (musical)">Sweeney Todd</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Phantom_of_the_Opera_(1986_musical)" title="The Phantom of the Opera (1986 musical)">The Phantom of the Opera</a></i>. Shirota appears regularly in many magazines and journals. In January 2007, Wink up Magazine (Wanibooks) started a monthly column written by Shirota. This column is titled "How are you?" and usually features one picture taken by Shirota.</p>
<p>He, with <a href="/wiki/Hirofumi_Araki" title="Hirofumi Araki">Hirofumi Araki</a>, also attended the opening night for the <i>Advancement Side Rokkaku feat. Hyotei Gakuen</i> of Tenimyu on August 3, 2006, to support <a href="/wiki/Hiroki_Aiba" title="Hiroki Aiba">Hiroki Aiba</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kousuke_Kujirai" title="Kousuke Kujirai">Kousuke Kujirai</a>.</p>
<p>Shirota has dated "<a href="/wiki/Seventeen_(Japanese_magazine)" title="Seventeen (Japanese magazine)">Seventeen</a>" model <a href="/wiki/Keiko_Kitagawa" title="Keiko Kitagawa">Keiko Kitagawa</a> and actress Misako Yasuda.</p>
<p>In October 2011, he became an honorary fan of <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FCB_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-FCB-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shirota has an older brother named Jun Shirota, who is also an actor and a singer. His other siblings are Maria, Rina and Dai.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Official_DVDs">Official DVDs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yu_Shirota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Official DVDs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On November 16, 2005, Shirota released an Idol DVD called <i>With U</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-With_U_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-With_U-32">[32]</a></sup> The DVD includes an acoustic performance for the song "Natsu no Owari ~ Mou Kako no Koi", for which he had composed and written lyrics. Despite having graduated from the D-BOYS, in December 2009, he was featured in both the sixth DVD release of the D-BOYS' Boy Friend series, called <i>YU Channel</i> and their "Kotoshi no Natsu wa Mainichi Dokoka de D-BOYS Ukiuki Yatta Hohhohoi" event, resulting in appearances in all three <i>Natsu Doko</i> CD and DVD releases.</p>
