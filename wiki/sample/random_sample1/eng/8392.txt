<p>The <b>Syrian opposition</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">المعارضة السورية <i>al-Muʕaraḍah as-Sūrīyah</i></span>‎‎ <small>Arabic pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Arabic" title="Help:IPA for Arabic">[ʔalmuʕaɾaˈdˤa ʔas.suːˈɾiːja]</a></span>) is an <a href="/wiki/Umbrella_term" title="Umbrella term">umbrella term</a> for the political structure represented by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Syrian_National_Coalition" title="Syrian National Coalition">Syrian National Coalition</a> and associated anti-regime Syrian groups with certain territorial control and an <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Interim_Government" title="Syrian Interim Government">alternative Syrian government</a>, claiming to be the legitimate <i>Syrian Arab Republic</i>. The Syrian opposition evolved since the beginning of the Syrian conflict, from groups calling for <a href="/wiki/Regime_change" title="Regime change">regime change</a> in <a href="/wiki/Syria_(region)" title="Syria (region)">Syria</a> and who have opposed its <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Ministers_(Syria)" title="Council of Ministers (Syria)">Ba'athist government</a>. Prior to the Syrian Civil War, the term "opposition" (Arabic "mu'araDah") had been used to refer to traditional political actors, for example the <a href="/wiki/National_Coordination_Committee_for_Democratic_Change" title="National Coordination Committee for Democratic Change">National Coordination Committee for Democratic Change</a>; that is, groups and individuals who have had a history of dissidence against the Syrian state.</p>
<p>The first opposition structures to form in the <a href="/wiki/Civil_uprising_phase_of_the_Syrian_Civil_War" title="Civil uprising phase of the Syrian Civil War">Syrian uprising</a> were local protest-organizing committees. These formed in April 2011, as protesters graduated from spontaneous protests to protests organized by meetings beforehand.</p>
<dl>
<dd>"The core of the grassroots civil opposition is the youth, mainly from the working and middle-classes, in which women and diverse religious and ethnic groups play active roles. Many of these activists remain non-affiliated to traditional political ideologies but are motivated by concerns for freedom, dignity, social justice and basic human rights."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Civil_uprising_phase_of_the_Syrian_Civil_War" title="Civil uprising phase of the Syrian Civil War">Syrian uprising phase</a>, from March 2011 until the start of August 2011, was characterized by a consensus for nonviolent struggle among the uprising's participants. Thus the conflict could not have been yet characterized as a "civil war", until the organization of armed struggle began on the anti-government side. This occurred beginning from 29 July 2011, allowing the conflict to meet the international political definition of "civil war."</p>
<p>Opposition groups in Syria took a new turn in late 2011, during the <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Civil_War" title="Syrian Civil War">Syrian Civil War</a>, as they united to form the <a href="/wiki/Syrian_National_Council" title="Syrian National Council">Syrian National Council</a> (SNC),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> which has received significant <a href="/wiki/Friends_of_Syria_Group" title="Friends of Syria Group">international support</a> and recognition as a <a href="/wiki/International_recognition_of_the_Syrian_National_Council" title="International recognition of the Syrian National Council">partner for dialogue</a>. The Syrian National Council was recognized or supported in some capacity by at least 17 <a href="/wiki/Member_states_of_the_United_Nations" title="Member states of the United Nations">member states of the United Nations</a>, with three of those (France, United Kingdom and the United States) being permanent members of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council" title="United Nations Security Council">Security Council</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The Syrian National Council is considered to be influenced by the Muslim Brotherhood and include many affiliated members. The <a href="/wiki/Islamic_Front_(Syria)" title="Islamic Front (Syria)">Islamic Front</a>, a major anti-regime Islamist Sunni militia during the Syrian Civil War, is affiliated with the Syrian National Council.</p>
<p>A broader opposition umbrella group, the <a href="/wiki/National_Coalition_for_Syrian_Revolutionary_and_Opposition_Forces" title="National Coalition for Syrian Revolutionary and Opposition Forces">National Coalition for Syrian Revolutionary and Opposition Forces</a>, was formed in November 2012 and has gained recognition as the "legitimate representative of the Syrian people" by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cooperation_Council_for_the_Arab_States_of_the_Gulf" title="Cooperation Council for the Arab States of the Gulf">Cooperation Council for the Arab States of the Gulf</a> (CCASG) and as a "representative of aspirations of Syrian people" by the <a href="/wiki/Arab_League" title="Arab League">Arab League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The Syrian National Coalition was subsequently given the seat of Syria in the Arab League, with Ba'athist Syria representative suspended. The Syrian National Council, initially a part of the Syrian National Coalition, withdrew on 20 January 2014 in protest at the decision of the coalition to attend the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geneva_II_Middle_East_peace_conference" title="Geneva II Middle East peace conference">Geneva talks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-12">[12]</a></sup> Despite tensions, the Syrian National Council retains a degree of ties with the National Coalition for Syrian Revolutionary and Opposition Forces. Syrian opposition groups held reconciliation talks in Astana, Kazakhstan in October 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-opptalks_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-opptalks-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syrian_opposition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Arab_Socialist_Ba%27ath_Party_%E2%80%93_Syria_Region" title="Arab Socialist Ba'ath Party – Syria Region">Ba'ath Party</a> seized power in Syria in 1963 after a <a href="/wiki/1963_Syrian_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="1963 Syrian coup d'état">coup d'état</a>. The head of state since 1971 has been a member of the <a href="/wiki/Al-Assad_family" title="Al-Assad family">al-Assad family</a>, beginning with <a href="/wiki/Hafez_al-Assad" title="Hafez al-Assad">Hafez al-Assad</a> (1971–2000). Syria was under <a href="/wiki/State_of_emergency" title="State of emergency">emergency law</a> from the time of the 1963 Syrian coup d'état until 21 April 2011, when it was rescinded by <a href="/wiki/Bashar_al-Assad" title="Bashar al-Assad">Bashar al-Assad</a>, Hafez's eldest surviving son and the current <a href="/wiki/President_of_Syria" title="President of Syria">President of Syria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> As the <a href="/wiki/Revolutionary_wave" title="Revolutionary wave">revolutionary wave</a> commonly referred to as the <a href="/wiki/Arab_Spring" title="Arab Spring">Arab Spring</a> began to take shape in early 2011, Syrian protesters began consolidating opposition councils.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syrian_opposition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Istanbul_Meeting_for_Syria&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Istanbul Meeting for Syria (page does not exist)">Istanbul Meeting for Syria</a> took place on 26 April 2011, during <a href="/wiki/Civil_uprising_phase_of_the_Syrian_Civil_War" title="Civil uprising phase of the Syrian Civil War">Syrian civil uprising</a>, being the first convention of the Syrian opposition. It was followed by the <a href="/wiki/Antalya_Conference_for_Change_in_Syria" title="Antalya Conference for Change in Syria">Antalya Conference for Change in Syria</a> or <i>Antalya Opposition Conference</i>, which was a three-day <a href="/wiki/Convention_(meeting)" title="Convention (meeting)">conference</a> of representatives of the Syrian opposition held from 31 May until 3 June 2011 in <a href="/wiki/Antalya" title="Antalya">Antalya</a>, Turkey.</p>
<p>Organized by <a href="/wiki/Ammar_al-Qurabi" title="Ammar al-Qurabi">Ammar al-Qurabi</a>'s <i>National Organization for Human Rights in Syria</i> and financed by the wealthy Damascene Sanqar family, it led to a final statement refusing compromise or reform solutions, and to the election of a 31-member leadership.</p>
<p>The Antalya conference was succeeded by a <a href="/wiki/Muslim_Brotherhood" title="Muslim Brotherhood">Muslim Brotherhood</a>-organized followup meeting two days later in <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>, and another one in Paris that was addressed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bernard_Henri_Levy" title="Bernard Henri Levy">Bernard Henri Levy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Aita302f_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Aita302f-15">[15]</a></sup> It however took a number of further meetings in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a> and <a href="/wiki/Doha" title="Doha">Doha</a>, before at yet another meeting on 23 August 2011 in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a> created a permanent transitional council in form of the <a href="/wiki/Syrian_National_Council" title="Syrian National Council">Syrian National Council</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ulutas_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ulutas-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_groups">Political groups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syrian_opposition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Political groups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Syrian opposition is not a rigid political structure, but a loose alliance of anti-regime political and armed groups dominated by Syrian Sunni Arabs, with Syrian National Coalition being the most prominent of those. In December 2015, the Syrian opposition convened in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc08122015_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc08122015-17">[17]</a></sup> with 34 groups present at the convention. The convention of the opposition was dominated by the <a href="/wiki/National_Coalition_for_Syrian_Revolutionary_and_Opposition_Forces" title="National Coalition for Syrian Revolutionary and Opposition Forces">National Coalition for Syrian Revolutionary and Opposition Forces</a> and included also the <a href="/wiki/National_Coordination_Committee_for_Democratic_Change" title="National Coordination Committee for Democratic Change">National Coordination Committee for Democratic Change</a>, as well as militant groups of <a href="/wiki/Jaysh_al-Islam" title="Jaysh al-Islam">Jaysh al-Islam</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_Front_of_the_Free_Syrian_Army" title="Southern Front of the Free Syrian Army">Southern Front</a>. As of December 2015 convention, the Syrian opposition notably excluded the Kurdish PYD party and its affiliates, as well as Salafist groups such as Al-Nusra Front; the powerful <a href="/wiki/Ahrar_al-Sham" title="Ahrar al-Sham">Ahrar al-Sham</a> organization was invited for the Riyadh convention, but decided to withdraw due to disagreements with other parties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc08122015_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc08122015-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Syrian_National_Coalition">Syrian National Coalition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syrian_opposition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Syrian National Coalition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:SyrianNationalCoalitionOfficialLogo.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1400" data-file-width="1100" height="216" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/SyrianNationalCoalitionOfficialLogo.svg/170px-SyrianNationalCoalitionOfficialLogo.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/SyrianNationalCoalitionOfficialLogo.svg/255px-SyrianNationalCoalitionOfficialLogo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/SyrianNationalCoalitionOfficialLogo.svg/340px-SyrianNationalCoalitionOfficialLogo.svg.png 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:SyrianNationalCoalitionOfficialLogo.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Official logo of the National Coalition for Syrian Revolutionary and Opposition Forces.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/National_Coalition_for_Syrian_Revolutionary_and_Opposition_Forces" title="National Coalition for Syrian Revolutionary and Opposition Forces">National Coalition for Syrian Revolutionary and Opposition Forces</a> is a coalition of opposition groups and individuals, mostly exilic, who support the Syrian revolution side and are against the Assad government ruling Syria. It formed on 11 November 2012 at a conference of opposition groups and individuals held in <a href="/wiki/Doha" title="Doha">Doha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Qatar" title="Qatar">Qatar</a>. It has relations with other opposition organizations such as the Syrian National Council, the previous iteration of an exilic political body attempting to represent the grassroots movement; the union of the two was planned, but has failed to realize. Moderate Islamic preacher <a href="/wiki/Moaz_al-Khatib" title="Moaz al-Khatib">Moaz al-Khatib</a>, who had protested on the Syrian street in the early nonviolent phase of the uprising, served a term as the president of the coalition, but soon resigned his post, frustrated with the gap between the body and the grassroots of the uprising inside Syria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Riad_Seif" title="Riad Seif">Riad Seif</a> and <a href="/wiki/Suheir_Atassi" title="Suheir Atassi">Suheir Atassi</a>, both of whom had also protested on the street in Syria early in the uprising, were elected as vice presidents. Mustafa Sabbagh is the coalition's secretary-general.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Notable members of the Coalition:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Assyrian_Democratic_Organization" title="Assyrian Democratic Organization">Assyrian Democratic Organization</a>: A party representing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Assyrians_in_Syria" title="Assyrians in Syria">Assyrians in Syria</a> and long repressed by the Assad government, it has been a participant in opposition structures since the beginning of the conflict. Abdul-Ahad Astepho is a member of the SNC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Syrian_National_Council">Syrian National Council</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syrian_opposition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Syrian National Council">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Syrian_National_Council" title="Syrian National Council">Syrian National Council</a> (<i>al-<a href="/wiki/Majlis" title="Majlis">Majlis</a> al-Waṭanī as-Sūri</i>) sometimes known as <i>SNC</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian20120712_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian20120712-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-cnn-23">[23]</a></sup> the <i>Syrian National Transitional Council</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> or the <i>National Council of Syria</i>, is a Syrian opposition coalition, based in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a> (<a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>), formed in August 2011 during the <a href="/wiki/Civil_uprising_phase_of_the_Syrian_Civil_War" title="Civil uprising phase of the Syrian Civil War">Syrian civil uprising</a> against the government of <a href="/wiki/Bashar_al-Assad" title="Bashar al-Assad">Bashar al-Assad</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miami_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-miami-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Edmond_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Edmond-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Initially, the council denied seeking to play the role of a <a href="/wiki/Government_in_exile" title="Government in exile">government in exile</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters1_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-reuters1-27">[27]</a></sup> but this changed a few months later when violence in Syria intensified.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The Syrian National Council seeks the end of Bashar al-Assad's rule and the establishment of a modern, civil, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_state" title="Democratic state">democratic state</a>. The SNC National Charter lists <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">human rights</a>, <a href="/wiki/Judicial_independence" title="Judicial independence">judicial independence</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Press_freedom" title="Press freedom">press freedom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a> and <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Political_pluralism" title="Political pluralism">political pluralism</a> as its guiding principles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2012, the Council agreed to unify with several other opposition groups to form the <a href="/wiki/National_Coalition_for_Syrian_Revolutionary_and_Opposition_Forces" title="National Coalition for Syrian Revolutionary and Opposition Forces">National Coalition for Syrian Revolutionary and Opposition Forces</a> with the SNC having 22 out of 60 seats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> The Council withdrew from the Coalition on 20 January 2014 in protest at the decision of the coalition to attend the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geneva_II_Middle_East_peace_conference" title="Geneva II Middle East peace conference">Geneva talks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> However the Council still sends representatives to the Coalition.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Notable members of the Council:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Syrian_Democratic_People%27s_Party" title="Syrian Democratic People's Party">Syrian Democratic People's Party</a>: A socialist party which played a "key role" in the creation of the SNC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The party's leader <a href="/wiki/George_Sabra" title="George Sabra">George Sabra</a> (a secularist born to a Christian family) is the official spokesman of the SNC, and also ran for chairman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Supreme_Council_of_the_Syrian_Revolution" title="Supreme Council of the Syrian Revolution">Supreme Council of the Syrian Revolution</a>: Syrian opposition group supporting the overthrow of Bashar al-Assad's government. It grants local opposition groups representation in its national organization.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Syrian_Democratic_Turkmen_Movement" title="Syrian Democratic Turkmen Movement">Syrian Democratic Turkmen Movement</a>: An opposition party of Syrian Turkmens, which was constituted in Istanbul on 21 March 2012. The leader of Syrian Democratic Turkmen Movement is Ziyad Hasan.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Coordination_Committee_for_Democratic_Change">National Coordination Committee for Democratic Change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syrian_opposition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: National Coordination Committee for Democratic Change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/National_Coordination_Committee_for_Democratic_Change" title="National Coordination Committee for Democratic Change">National Coordination Committee for Democratic Change</a> (<i>NCC</i>), or <i>National Coordination Body for Democratic Change</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hatham_former_ACHR_spokesperson_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hatham_former_ACHR_spokesperson-38">[38]</a></sup> (<i>NCB</i>), is a Syrian bloc chaired by Hassan Abdel Azim consisting of 13 left-wing political parties and "independent political and youth activists".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> It has been defined by <a href="/wiki/Reuters" title="Reuters">Reuters</a> as the internal opposition's main umbrella group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReutersDamascusmeeting_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReutersDamascusmeeting-40">[40]</a></sup> The NCC initially had several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurdish_people" title="Kurdish people">Kurdish</a> political parties as members, but all except for the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Union_Party_(Syria)" title="Democratic Union Party (Syria)">Democratic Union Party</a> left in October 2011 to join the <a href="/wiki/Kurdish_National_Council" title="Kurdish National Council">Kurdish National Council</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Some opposition activists<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (November 2013)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> have accused the NCC of being a "<a href="/wiki/Front_organization" title="Front organization">front organization</a>" for <a href="/wiki/Bashar_al-Assad" title="Bashar al-Assad">Bashar al-Assad</a>'s government and some of its members of being ex-government insiders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ft_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-ft-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>Relations with other Syrian political opposition groups are generally poor. The <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Revolution_General_Commission" title="Syrian Revolution General Commission">Syrian Revolution General Commission</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Local_Coordination_Committees_of_Syria" title="Local Coordination Committees of Syria">Local Coordination Committees of Syria</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Council_of_the_Syrian_Revolution" title="Supreme Council of the Syrian Revolution">Supreme Council of the Syrian Revolution</a> oppose the NCC calls to dialogue with the Syrian government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> In September 2012, the <a href="/wiki/Syrian_National_Council" title="Syrian National Council">Syrian National Council</a> (SNC) reaffirmed that despite broadening its membership, it would not join with "currents close to [the] NCC".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Despite recognizing the <a href="/wiki/Free_Syrian_Army" title="Free Syrian Army">Free Syrian Army</a> on 23 September 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NBC_statement_September_23_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-NBC_statement_September_23-45">[45]</a></sup> the FSA has dismissed the NCC as an extension of the government, stating that "this opposition is just the other face of the same coin".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReutersDamascusmeeting_40-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReutersDamascusmeeting-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>Notable members of the Committee:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Syriac_Union_Party_(Syria)" title="Syriac Union Party (Syria)">Syriac Union Party</a>: A party representing the interests of <a href="/wiki/Syriac_Christianity" title="Syriac Christianity">Syriac Christians</a> and affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/Syriac_Union_Party_(Lebanon)" title="Syriac Union Party (Lebanon)">Syriac Union Party</a> in Lebanon (itself part of the anti-Assad <a href="/wiki/March_14_Alliance" title="March 14 Alliance">March 14 Alliance</a>). It has taken part in numerous opposition demonstrations, including storming the Syrian embassy in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> in August 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_groups_affiliated_with_Syrian_opposition">Other groups affiliated with Syrian opposition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syrian_opposition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Other groups affiliated with Syrian opposition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muslim_Brotherhood_in_Syria" title="Muslim Brotherhood in Syria">Muslim Brotherhood</a>: Islamist party founded in 1930. The brotherhood was behind the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamic_uprising_in_Syria" title="Islamic uprising in Syria">Islamic uprising in Syria</a> between 1976 until 1982. The party is banned in Syria and membership became a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capital_offence" title="Capital offence">capital offence</a> in 1980. The Muslim Brotherhood has issued statements of support for the Syrian uprising.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Other sources have described the group as having "risen from the ashes",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> "resurrected itself"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> to be a dominant force in the uprising.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> The Muslim Brotherhood has constantly lost influence with militants on the ground, who have defected from the Brotherhood affiliated <a href="/wiki/Shields_of_the_Revolution_Council" title="Shields of the Revolution Council">Shields of the Revolution Council</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_Front_(Syria)" title="Islamic Front (Syria)">Islamic Front</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Coalition_of_Secular_and_Democratic_Syrians" title="Coalition of Secular and Democratic Syrians">Coalition of Secular and Democratic Syrians</a>: nucleus of a Syrian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secular" title="Secular">secular</a> and democratic opposition that appeared during the Syrian civil war. It came about through the union of a dozen Muslim and Christian, Arab and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurd" title="Kurd">Kurd</a> parties, who called the minorities of Syria to support the fight against the government of <a href="/wiki/Bashar_al-Assad" title="Bashar al-Assad">Bashar al-Assad</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> The Coalition has also called for military intervention in Syria, under the form of a <a href="/wiki/No-fly_zone" title="No-fly zone">no-fly zone</a> similar to that of <a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Kosovo</a>, with a safe zone and cities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> The president of the coalition, who is also a member of the SNC, is <a href="/wiki/Randa_Kassis" title="Randa Kassis">Randa Kassis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Syrian_Turkmen_Assembly" title="Syrian Turkmen Assembly">Syrian Turkmen Assembly</a>: A recently formed assembly of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Syrian_Turks" title="Syrian Turks">Syrian Turkmens</a> which constitutes a coalition of Turkmen parties and groups in Syria. It is against the partition of Syria after the collapse of Baath government. The common decision of Syrian Turkmen Assembly is: <i>"Regardless of any ethnic or religious identity, a future in which everybody can be able to live commonly under the identity of Syrian is targeted in the future of Syria."</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Syrian_Turkmen_National_Bloc" title="Syrian Turkmen National Bloc">Syrian Turkmen National Bloc</a>: An opposition party of Syrian Turkmens, which was founded in February 2012. The chairman of the political party is Yusuf Molla.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Local_Coordination_Committees_of_Syria" title="Local Coordination Committees of Syria">Local Coordination Committees of Syria</a>: Network of local protest groups that organise and report on protests as part of the Syrian civil war, founded in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> As of August 2011<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Syrian_opposition&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the network supported <a href="/wiki/Civil_disobedience" title="Civil disobedience">civil disobedience</a> and opposed local armed resistance and international military intervention as methods of opposing the Syrian government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> Key people are activists <a href="/wiki/Razan_Zaitouneh" title="Razan Zaitouneh">Razan Zaitouneh</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suhair_al-Atassi" title="Suhair al-Atassi">Suhair al-Atassi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Syrian_National_Democratic_Council" title="Syrian National Democratic Council">Syrian National Democratic Council</a>: formed in Paris on 13 November 2011 during the Syrian civil war by <a href="/wiki/Rifaat_al-Assad" title="Rifaat al-Assad">Rifaat al-Assad</a>, uncle of Bashar al-Assad. Rifaat al-Assad has expressed the wish to replace Bashar al-Assad with the authoritarian state apparatus intact, and to guarantee the safety of government members, while also making vague allusions to a "transition".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> Rifaat has his own political organisation, the United National Democratic Rally.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Syrian_Revolution_General_Commission" title="Syrian Revolution General Commission">Syrian Revolution General Commission</a>: Syrian coalition of 40 Syrian opposition groups to unite their efforts during the Syrian civil war that was announced<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (July 2015)">by whom?</span></a></i>]</sup> on 19 August 2011 in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hizb_ut-Tahrir" title="Hizb ut-Tahrir">Hizb ut-Tahrir</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Syrian_Interim_Government">Syrian Interim Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syrian_opposition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Syrian Interim Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Interim_Government" title="Syrian Interim Government">Syrian Interim Government</a></div>
<p>At a conference held in Istanbul on 19 March 2013, members of the National Coalition elected Ghassan Hitto as prime minister of an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interim_government" title="Interim government">interim government</a> for Syria. Hitto has announced that a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Technical_government" title="Technical government">technical government</a> will be formed which will be led by between 10 and 12 ministers. The minister of defense is to be chosen by the <a href="/wiki/Free_Syrian_Army" title="Free Syrian Army">Free Syrian Army</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_forces">Military forces</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syrian_opposition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Military forces">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Revolutionary_Command_Council" title="Syrian Revolutionary Command Council">Syrian Revolutionary Command Council</a></div>
<p>The military forces associated with the Syrian Opposition are currently largely defined by the <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Revolutionary_Command_Council" title="Syrian Revolutionary Command Council">Syrian Revolutionary Command Council</a>, which is mainly relying on the <a href="/wiki/Free_Syrian_Army" title="Free Syrian Army">Free Syrian Army</a> (with links to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Syrian_National_Coalition" title="Syrian National Coalition">Syrian National Coalition</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_Front_(Syria)" title="Islamic Front (Syria)">Islamic Front (Syria)</a> (linked to <a href="/wiki/Syrian_National_Council" title="Syrian National Council">Syrian National Council</a>) coalitions. Initially, the <a href="/wiki/Free_Syrian_Army" title="Free Syrian Army">Free Syrian Army</a> was perceived as the ultimate military force of the Syrian Opposition, but with the collapse of many FSA factions and emergence of powerful Islamist groups, it became clear to the opposition that only a cooperation of secular military forces and moderate Islamists could form a sufficient coalition to battle both the Syrian Baathist forces and radical Jihadists such as ISIL and in some cases al-Nusra Front.</p>
<p>Members of the Syrian Revolutionary Command Council:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Free_Syrian_Army" title="Free Syrian Army">Free Syrian Army</a>: <a href="/wiki/Paramilitary" title="Paramilitary">Paramilitary</a> that has been active during the Syrian civil war.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> Composed mainly of defected <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Armed_Forces" title="Syrian Armed Forces">Syrian Armed Forces</a> personnel,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> its formation was announced on 29 July 2011 in a video released on the Internet by a uniformed group of deserters from the Syrian military who called upon members of the Syrian army to defect and join them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> The leader of the group, who identified himself as Colonel <a href="/wiki/Riad_al-Asaad" title="Riad al-Asaad">Riad al-Asaad</a>, announced that the Free Syrian Army would work with demonstrators to bring down the system, and declared that all security forces attacking civilians are justified targets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> It has also been reported that many former Syrian Consulates are trying to band together a Free Syrian Navy from fishermen and defectors to secure the coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Syrian_Turkmen_Brigades" title="Syrian Turkmen Brigades">Syrian Turkmen Brigades</a>: An armed opposition structure of Syrian Turkmens fighting against Syrian Armed Forces. It is also the military wing of Syrian Turkmen Assembly. It is led by Colonel Muhammad Awad and Ali Basher.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Islamic_Front_(Syria)" title="Islamic Front (Syria)">Islamic Front</a>: An Islamist rebel group formed in November 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
