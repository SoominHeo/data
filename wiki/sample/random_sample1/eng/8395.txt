<p><i><b>Kachō Kōsaku Shima</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">課長島耕作</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the first in a number of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series, by <a href="/wiki/Kenshi_Hirokane" title="Kenshi Hirokane">Kenshi Hirokane</a>, about a fictional <a href="/wiki/Salaryman" title="Salaryman">salaryman</a> named Kōsaku Shima. The series ran from 1983–1992 under this title, winning the 1991 <a href="/wiki/Kodansha_Manga_Award" title="Kodansha Manga Award">Kodansha Manga Award</a> in the General category.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KodanshaHahn_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-KodanshaHahn-1">[1]</a></sup> As of 2013, the series has sold over 40 million copies in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83debut_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-83debut-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>It depicts the life of a <a href="/wiki/Salaryman" title="Salaryman">salaryman</a>, a Japanese <a href="/wiki/White-collar_worker" title="White-collar worker">white-collar worker</a> who devotes his life to his company. The manga have been translated from Japanese into English (partially; through Kodansha Bilingual Editions), French and German. In addition to various manga there has also been one comedic ONA, and two comedic anime adaptations lasting 11 episodes each, a live action film, and a single episode TV drama. The character also acts as the "host" or framing device for a business documentary series on <a href="/wiki/NHK_World" title="NHK World">NHK World</a>, <i>Shima Kosaku's Asian Entrepreneurs</i>.</p>
<p>At the start of the long-running series, Shima Kōsaku is a <i>kachō</i>, or section chief, of a huge conglomerate, Hatsushiba Electric. He is later promoted to <i>buchō</i> (division chief) and eventually promoted to manager and executive-director, and with each promotion the title of the series changed as well. He was promoted to the president of Hatsushiba Electric, and the title changed to "Shachō" Shima Kōsaku, which means "President Kōsaku Shima". The fictional conglomerate is modeled after <a href="/wiki/Panasonic" title="Panasonic">Panasonic</a>, or Matsushita Electric, where Hirokane worked before and is highly accurate in its portrayal of Japanese corporate culture.</p>
