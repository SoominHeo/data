<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taipei_Rapid_Transit_System" title="Taipei Rapid Transit System">Taipei Metro</a> <b>Ximen Station</b> (formerly transliterated as <b>Hsimen Station</b> from 1999 until 2003) is located between the <a href="/wiki/Zhongzheng_District" title="Zhongzheng District">Zhongzheng</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wanhua_District" title="Wanhua District">Wanhua</a> districts in <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>. It is a transfer station on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nangang_Line_(Taipei_Metro)" title="Nangang Line (Taipei Metro)">Nangang Line</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Banqiao_Line_(Taipei_Metro)" title="Banqiao Line (Taipei Metro)">Banqiao Line</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xiaonanmen_Line_(Taipei_Metro)" title="Xiaonanmen Line (Taipei Metro)">Xiaonanmen Line</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Songshan_Line_(Taipei_Metro)" title="Songshan Line (Taipei Metro)">Songshan Line</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The station is named for the old west gate of the city, whose location is roughly where the current station is located.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_overview">Station overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ximen_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Station overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:XimenStation-Platform2.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2736" data-file-width="3648" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/XimenStation-Platform2.JPG/250px-XimenStation-Platform2.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/XimenStation-Platform2.JPG/375px-XimenStation-Platform2.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/XimenStation-Platform2.JPG/500px-XimenStation-Platform2.JPG 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:XimenStation-Platform2.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Ximen Station Platform 2.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The station is a three-level,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> underground structure with two <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platforms</a> and six exits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The two platforms are stacked, thus allowing for <a href="/wiki/Cross-platform_interchange" title="Cross-platform interchange">cross-platform interchange</a> between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taipei_Metro_Line_3" title="Taipei Metro Line 3">Line 3</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taipei_Metro_Line_5" title="Taipei Metro Line 5">Line 5</a>. Washrooms are inside the entrance area. In November 2010, the daily <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ridership" title="Ridership">ridership</a> at Ximen Station was 112,000,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> making it the fourth busiest station on the network, just behind <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taipei_Station" title="Taipei Station">Taipei Main Station</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taipei_City_Hall_Station" title="Taipei City Hall Station">Taipei City Hall</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zhongxiao_Fuxing_Station" title="Zhongxiao Fuxing Station">Zhongxiao Fuxing Station</a>.</p>
<p>It is situated under Zhonghua Rd, at the intersection of Chengdu Rd, Hengyang Rd, and Baoqing Rd. The station is also connected to the Ximen Intelligent Library (an unmanned branch of the Taipei Public Library).</p>
<p><b>Exit 6</b> is heavily used by young people accessing the <a href="/wiki/Ximending" title="Ximending">Ximending</a> shopping area. The exit opening is set at the top of the pedestrian zone, although there is still vehicular traffic in that area. The station is often crowded on weekends, especially in the afternoon.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ximen_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="TRA_Station">TRA Station</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ximen_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: TRA Station">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>During <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_under_Japanese_rule" title="Taiwan under Japanese rule">Japanese rule</a>, the station was called <i>Shinkichō</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">新起町</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> Field, opened on November 5, 1930. It was specifically for refueling and its location was roughly where the current station is today.</li>
<li>After the war during post-war rebuilding, the station's name was changed to the current "Ximen Station". However, the station location had disappeared due to illegal construction, so the station could not be reconstructed. An underground emergency station opened on September 2, 1987 as part of the <a href="/wiki/Taipei_Railway_Underground_Project" title="Taipei Railway Underground Project">Taipei Railway Underground Project</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>Ximen Station used to be a station on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taiwan_Railway_Administration" title="Taiwan Railway Administration">TRA</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Line,_Taiwan" title="Western Line, Taiwan">Western Line</a>. However, the underground area is nowadays only used as an emergency station and not open for revenue service.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Taipei_Metro_Station">Taipei Metro Station</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ximen_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Taipei Metro Station">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>December 24, 1999: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nangang_Line_(Taipei_Metro)" title="Nangang Line (Taipei Metro)">Nangang Line</a> platforms 1 and 3 open with the opening of the <a href="/wiki/Taipei_City_Hall_Station" title="Taipei City Hall Station">Taipei City Hall</a> to <a href="/wiki/Longshan_Temple_Station" title="Longshan Temple Station">Longshan Temple</a> segment.</li>
<li>August 31, 2000: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xiaonanmen_Line_(Taipei_Metro)" title="Xiaonanmen Line (Taipei Metro)">Xiaonanmen Line</a> platforms 2 and 4 open with the opening of the Ximen to <a href="/wiki/Chiang_Kai-shek_Memorial_Hall_Station" title="Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall Station">Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall</a> segment.</li>
<li>November 15, 2014: The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Songshan_Line" title="Songshan Line">Songshan Line</a> opens for revenue service, hence all Xiaonanmen Line trains continue to the Songshan Line and Xindian Line.</li>
</ul>
<p>In the past, plans for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taoyuan_International_Airport_Access_MRT_System" title="Taoyuan International Airport Access MRT System">Taoyuan Airport MRT</a> had this station as a terminus. However, the route has since been altered and the station is no longer on the planned route. (Ximen Underground Passageway)</p>
