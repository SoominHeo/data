<p><b>Shimogyō-ku</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">下京区</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is one of the eleven <a href="/wiki/Wards_of_Kyoto" title="Wards of Kyoto">wards</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Japan" title="Municipalities of Japan">city</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kyoto,_Kyoto" title="Kyoto, Kyoto">Kyoto</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Prefecture" title="Kyoto Prefecture">Kyoto Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. First established in 1879, it has been merged and split, and took on its present boundaries in 1955, with the establishment of a separate <a href="/wiki/Minami-ku,_Kyoto" title="Minami-ku, Kyoto">Minami-ku</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Tower" title="Kyoto Tower">Kyoto Tower</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kyoto_Station" title="Kyoto Station">Kyoto Station</a> are major landmarks in Shimogyō-ku. <a href="/wiki/Shij%C5%8D_Street" title="Shijō Street">Shijō Street</a> on the northern edge of the area, especially around the <a href="/wiki/Shij%C5%8D_Kawaramachi" title="Shijō Kawaramachi">Shijō Kawaramachi</a> intersection, is the busiest shopping district in the city. Kyoto Station has an extensive shopping center, including a department store in the station building, and the underground Porta mall.</p>
<p>Shimogyō-ku has a population of 82,682 and an area of 6.78 km². Three rivers, Horikawa, <a href="/wiki/Kamo_River" title="Kamo River">Kamogawa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Takase_River" title="Takase River">Takasegawa</a>, are in the ward.</p>
