<p>The <b>Shimonoseki Campaign</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">下関戦争/馬関戦争</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">Hepburn</a>: <i>Shimonoseki Sensō/Bakan Sensō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> refers to a series of military engagements in 1863 and 1864, fought to control <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shimonoseki_Straits" title="Shimonoseki Straits">Shimonoseki Straits</a> of Japan by joint naval forces from <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">Great Britain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Second_French_Empire" title="Second French Empire">France</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, against the <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> feudal domain of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ch%C5%8Dsh%C5%AB_domain" title="Chōshū domain">Chōshū</a>, which took place off and on the coast of <a href="/wiki/Shimonoseki" title="Shimonoseki">Shimonoseki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>The Japanese term for this event translates to the <b>Shimonoseki War</b>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shimonoseki_Campaign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Despite efforts of appeasement by the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a> to establish an atmosphere of peaceful solidarity, many feudal <i><a href="/wiki/Daimy%C5%8D" title="Daimyō">daimyōs</a></i> remained bitterly resentful of the shogunate's open-door policy to foreign trade. Belligerent opposition to European and American influence erupted into open conflict when the <a href="/wiki/Emperor_K%C5%8Dmei" title="Emperor Kōmei">Emperor Kōmei</a>, breaking with centuries of imperial tradition, began to take an active role in matters of state and issued on March 11 and April 11, 1863 his "<a href="/wiki/Order_to_expel_barbarians" title="Order to expel barbarians">Order to expel barbarians</a>" (攘夷実行の勅命 – <i>Jōi jikkō no chokumei</i>).</p>
<p>The Chōshū clan, under the <i>daimyō</i> <a href="/wiki/M%C5%8Dri_Takachika" title="Mōri Takachika">Mōri Takachika</a>, began to take action to expel all foreigners after the deadline of the 10th day of the 5th month, by the traditional <a href="/wiki/Japanese_calendar" title="Japanese calendar">Japanese calendar</a>. Openly defying the shogunate, Takachika ordered his forces to fire without warning on all foreign ships traversing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shimonoseki_Strait" title="Shimonoseki Strait">Shimonoseki Strait</a>. This strategic but treacherous 600-meter waterway separates the islands of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honsh%C5%AB" title="Honshū">Honshū</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB" title="Kyūshū">Kyūshū</a> and provides a passage connecting the <a href="/wiki/Seto_Inland_Sea" title="Seto Inland Sea">Inland Sea</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Japan" title="Sea of Japan">Sea of Japan</a>.</p>
<p>Even before tensions escalated in Shimonoseki Strait, foreign diplomats and military experts, notably U.S. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foreign_Minister" title="Foreign Minister">Foreign Minister</a> to Japan <a href="/wiki/Robert_H._Pruyn" title="Robert H. Pruyn">Robert Pruyn</a> and U.S. Navy <a href="/wiki/Captain_(naval)" title="Captain (naval)">Captain</a> <a href="/wiki/David_McDougal" title="David McDougal">David McDougal</a> had been aware of the precarious state of affairs in Japan. McDougal wrote a letter to the Secretary of the Navy, <a href="/wiki/Gideon_Welles" title="Gideon Welles">Gideon Welles</a>, dated June 12, 1863, stating, "General opinion is that the government of Japan is on the eve of revolution, the principal object of which is the expulsion of foreigners."</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id=".22Revere_the_Emperor_and_expel_the_barbarians.21.22">"Revere the Emperor and expel the barbarians!"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shimonoseki_Campaign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title='Edit section: "Revere the Emperor and expel the barbarians!"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:104px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:SonnoJoi.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="439" data-file-width="102" height="439" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/85/SonnoJoi.png" width="102"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:SonnoJoi.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<i><a href="/wiki/Sonn%C5%8D_j%C5%8Di" title="Sonnō jōi">Sonnō jōi</a></i>: "Revere the Emperor and expel the barbarians!"</div>

</div>
<p>The Chōshū clan was equipped with mostly antiquated cannons firing cannonballs, but also some modern armament, such as five 8-inch (200 mm) <a href="/wiki/Dahlgren_gun" title="Dahlgren gun">Dahlgren guns</a>, which had been presented to Japan by the United States, and three steam warships of American construction: the <a href="/wiki/Barque#Bark" title="Barque">bark</a> <i>Daniel Webster</i> of six guns, the <a href="/wiki/Brig" title="Brig">brig</a> <i>Lanrick</i>, or <i>Kosei</i>, with ten guns, and the steamer <i>Lancefield</i>, or <i>Koshin</i>, of four guns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first attack occurred on June 25, 1863, soon after the Imperial "<a href="/wiki/Order_to_expel_barbarians" title="Order to expel barbarians">Order to expel barbarians</a>" came into effect. The U.S. merchant steamer <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=SS_Pembroke&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="SS Pembroke (page does not exist)">SS <i>Pembroke</i></a>, under Captain Simon Cooper, was riding at anchor unsuspectingly outside Shimonoseki Strait when intercepted and fired upon by two <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a>-built warships belonging to the rebel forces.</p>
<p>The crew of one enemy vessel taunted the frantic American seamen with a loud and unnerving cry: "Revere the Emperor and expel the barbarians!" (<span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">尊皇攘夷</span> <i><a href="/wiki/Sonn%C5%8D_j%C5%8Di" title="Sonnō jōi">sonnō jōi</a></i>). Under incessant cannon fire, <i>Pembroke</i> managed to get underway and escape through the adjacent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bungo_Strait" title="Bungo Strait">Bungo Strait</a>, miraculously with only slight damage and no casualties.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:KienChang.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="383" data-file-width="539" height="156" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/KienChang.jpg/220px-KienChang.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/KienChang.jpg/330px-KienChang.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/KienChang.jpg/440px-KienChang.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:KienChang.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Attack on the French warship <a href="/wiki/French_aviso_Kien_Chan_(1860)" title="French aviso Kien Chan (1860)"><i>Kien Chan</i></a>.</div>

</div>
<p>Upon arrival in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, Cooper filed a report of the attack and dispatched it to the U.S. Consulate in <a href="/wiki/Yokohama" title="Yokohama">Yokohama</a>, Japan. The next day, the French <a href="/wiki/Aviso" title="Aviso">naval dispatch steamer</a> <a href="/wiki/French_aviso_Kien_Chan_(1860)" title="French aviso Kien Chan (1860)"><i>Kien Chan</i></a> was also riding at anchor outside the strait, when rebel Japanese <a href="/wiki/Artillery" title="Artillery">artillery</a> atop the bluffs surrounding Shimonoseki opened fire on her. <i>Kien Chan</i> sustained damage to its engine and suffered four casualties before escaping to the open ocean.</p>
<p>On July 11, despite warnings from the crew of the <i>Kien Chan</i>, the 16-gun Dutch warship <i>Medusa</i> cruised into Shimonoseki Strait. Her skipper, Captain <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_de_Casembroot" title="François de Casembroot">François de Casembroot</a>, was convinced that Lord Mori would not dare fire on his vessel due to the strength of his ship and longstanding relations between the Netherlands and Japan.</p>
<p>But Takachika did just that, pounding <i>Medusa</i> with more than thirty shells and killing or wounding nine seamen. De Casembroot returned fire and ran the rebel gauntlet at full speed, fearful of endangering the life of the Dutch Consul General, who was on board. Within a short time, the Japanese warlord had managed to fire on the flags of most of the nations with consulates in Japan.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle_of_Shimonoseki_Straits">Battle of Shimonoseki Straits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shimonoseki_Campaign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Battle of Shimonoseki Straits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

The <a href="/wiki/USS_Wyoming_(1859)" title="USS Wyoming (1859)">USS <i>Wyoming</i></a> battling in the Shimonoseki Straits against the Choshu steam warships <i>Daniel Webster</i>, <i>Lanrick</i> and <i>Lancefield</i></div>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Shimonoseki_Straits" title="Battle of Shimonoseki Straits">Battle of Shimonoseki Straits</a></div>
<p>In the morning of July 16, 1863, under sanction by Minister Pruyn, in an apparent swift response to the attack on the <i>Pembroke</i>, the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Frigate" title="Frigate">frigate</a> <a href="/wiki/USS_Wyoming_(1859)" title="USS Wyoming (1859)">USS <i>Wyoming</i></a>, under Captain McDougal, sailed into the strait and single-handedly engaged the U.S.-built but poorly manned local fleet for almost two hours before withdrawing. McDougal sank two enemy vessels and severely damaged another one, along with inflicting some forty Japanese casualties, while the <i>Wyoming</i> suffered extensive damage, four crew dead and seven wounded, one later dying of his injuries. The two Japanese steamers sunk by the <i>Wyoming</i> were raised again by Chōshū in 1864 and attached to the harbor of <a href="/wiki/Hagi,_Yamaguchi" title="Hagi, Yamaguchi">Hagi</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campaign">Campaign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shimonoseki_Campaign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Campaign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_Battle.2C_July_20.2C_1863">First Battle, July 20, 1863</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shimonoseki_Campaign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: First Battle, July 20, 1863">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ShimonosekiFrenchEngagement.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="582" data-file-width="1083" height="118" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/ShimonosekiFrenchEngagement.jpg/220px-ShimonosekiFrenchEngagement.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/ShimonosekiFrenchEngagement.jpg/330px-ShimonosekiFrenchEngagement.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/ShimonosekiFrenchEngagement.jpg/440px-ShimonosekiFrenchEngagement.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

The French engagement at Shimonoseki, with the warships <a href="/wiki/French_aviso_Trancr%C3%A8de_(1862)" title="French aviso Trancrède (1862)"><i>Tancrède</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/French_corvette_Dupleix_(1861)" title="French corvette Dupleix (1861)"><i>Dupleix</i></a>, under Captain <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Jaur%C3%A8s" title="Benjamin Jaurès">Benjamin Jaurès</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/Le_Monde_illustr%C3%A9" title="Le Monde illustré">Le Monde illustré</a></i>, October 10th, 1863.</div>

<p>On the heels of McDougal's engagement, on July 20, the <a href="/wiki/French_Navy" title="French Navy">French Navy</a> retaliated for the attack on their merchant ship. The French force consisted of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marine_(military)" title="Marine (military)">marines</a> and two warships, the <a href="/wiki/Aviso" title="Aviso">aviso</a> <a href="/wiki/French_aviso_Trancr%C3%A8de_(1862)" title="French aviso Trancrède (1862)"><i>Tancrède</i></a> and the corvette <a href="/wiki/French_corvette_Dupleix_(1861)" title="French corvette Dupleix (1861)"><i>Dupleix</i></a>. With 250 men, under Captain <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Jaur%C3%A8s" title="Benjamin Jaurès">Benjamin Jaurès</a>, they swept into Shimonoseki and destroyed a small town, together with at least one artillery emplacement.</p>
<p>The intervention was supported by the French <a href="/wiki/Plenipotentiary" title="Plenipotentiary">plenipotentiary</a> in Japan, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duchesne_de_Bellecourt" title="Duchesne de Bellecourt">Duchesne de Bellecourt</a>, but the French government, once informed, strongly criticized their representatives in Japan for taking such bellicose steps, for the reason that France had much more important military commitments to honour in other parts of the world, and could not afford a conflict in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Duchesne de Bellecourt would be relieved from his position in 1864.</p>
<p>Jaurès was also congratulated by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shogunate" title="Shogunate">Shogunal government</a> for taking such decisive steps against anti-foreign forces, and was awarded a special banner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Diplomatic_negotiations">Diplomatic negotiations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shimonoseki_Campaign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Diplomatic negotiations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Meanwhile, the Americans, French, British and Dutch feverishly opened diplomatic channels in an effort to negotiate the reopening of the passage to the Inland Sea. Months dragged by with no end in sight to the growing dilemma. By May 1864, various bellicose Japanese factions had destroyed thousands of dollars in foreign property, including homes, churches and shipping. This wanton destruction included the U.S. Legation in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, which housed <a href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador_to_Japan" title="United States Ambassador to Japan">Minister</a> Robert Pruyn.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:FrenchAtShimonoseki.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="764" data-file-width="1013" height="166" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/FrenchAtShimonoseki.jpg/220px-FrenchAtShimonoseki.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/FrenchAtShimonoseki.jpg/330px-FrenchAtShimonoseki.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/FrenchAtShimonoseki.jpg/440px-FrenchAtShimonoseki.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<a href="/wiki/French_Navy" title="French Navy">French Navy</a> troops taking possession of Japanese cannons at Shimonoseki.</div>

<p>Throughout the first half of 1864, as Shimonoseki Strait remained closed to foreign shipping, threats and rumors of war hung in the air, while diplomatic efforts remained deadlocked. Then the British Minister to Japan, <a href="/wiki/Sir" title="Sir">Sir</a> <a href="/wiki/Rutherford_Alcock" title="Rutherford Alcock">Rutherford Alcock</a>, discussed with his treaty counterparts such as <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> Minister Robert Pruyn, the feasibility of a joint military strike against Takachika.</p>
<p>They were soon making preparations for a combined show of force. Under the wary eyes of the Japanese, fifteen British warships rode anchor alongside four Dutch vessels, while a British regiment from Hong Kong augmented their display of military might. The French maintained a minimal naval presence, with the bulk of their forces in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> trying to bolster Emperor <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_I_of_Mexico" title="Maximilian I of Mexico">Maximilian's</a> unstable regime.</p>
<p>The U.S., engaged in its <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">Civil War</a>, limited itself to demonstrate diplomatic and minimal military support for the allies. In the meantime, Takachika procrastinated in negotiations by requesting additional time to respond to the allied demands, a response unacceptable to the treaty powers. The allies decided that the time for united action had arrived.</p>
<p>Despite retaliatory action from the treaty powers, another attack occurred in July 1864 when the rebel forces fired upon the U.S. steamer <i>Monitor</i> after she entered a harbor for coal and water. This provoked further outrage, even after a British squadron delivered a multi-national ultimatum to Takachika, threatening military force if the strait was not opened.</p>

<p>On August 17, 1864, a squadron consisting of nine British (<a href="/wiki/HMS_Euryalus_(1853)" title="HMS Euryalus (1853)"><i>Euryalus</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/HMS_Waterloo_(1833)" title="HMS Waterloo (1833)"><i>Conqueror</i></a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Tartar_(1854)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HMS Tartar (1854) (page does not exist)"><i>Tartar</i></a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Leopard_(1850)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HMS Leopard (1850) (page does not exist)"><i>Leopard</i></a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Barrosa_(1860)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HMS Barrosa (1860) (page does not exist)"><i>Barrosa</i></a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Perseus_(1861)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HMS Perseus (1861) (page does not exist)"><i>Perseus</i></a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Argus_(1849)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HMS Argus (1849) (page does not exist)"><i>Argus</i></a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Coquette_(1855)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HMS Coquette (1855) (page does not exist)"><i>Coquette</i></a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Bouncer_(1856)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HMS Bouncer (1856) (page does not exist)"><i>Bouncer</i></a>), four Dutch (<i>Amsterdam</i>, <i>Medusa</i>, <i>Metalen-Kruis</i>, and <i>D'Jambi</i>), and three French warships (<a href="/wiki/French_aviso_Trancr%C3%A8de_(1862)" title="French aviso Trancrède (1862)"><i>Tancrède</i></a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=French_frigate_S%C3%A9miramis_(1861)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="French frigate Sémiramis (1861) (page does not exist)"><i>Sémiramis</i></a>, and <a href="/wiki/French_corvette_Dupleix_(1861)" title="French corvette Dupleix (1861)"><i>Dupleix</i></a>), together with 2,000 <a href="/wiki/Soldier" title="Soldier">soldiers</a>, marines and sailors, all under the command of <a href="/wiki/Admiral" title="Admiral">Admiral</a> Sir <a href="/wiki/Augustus_Leopold_Kuper" title="Augustus Leopold Kuper">Augustus Leopold Kuper</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a>, steamed out of Yokohama to open Shimonoseki Strait.</p>
<p>The U.S. chartered steamer <a href="/wiki/USS_Ta-Kiang_(1862)" title="USS Ta-Kiang (1862)"><i>Ta-Kiang</i></a> accompanied the operation in a token show of support. The two-day battle that followed on September 5 and 6 did what the previous operations could not; it destroyed the <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%8Dsh%C5%AB_Domain" title="Chōshū Domain">Chōshū Domain</a>'s ability to wage war on the western powers. Unable to match the firepower of the international fleet, and amid mounting casualties, Chōshū forces finally surrendered two days later on September 8, 1864.</p>
<p>Allied casualties included 72 killed or wounded and two severely damaged British ships. A full account of the battle is contained in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ernest_Satow" title="Ernest Satow">Ernest Satow</a>'s <i>A Diplomat in Japan</i>. Satow was present as a young interpreter for the British admiral, Augustus Kuper on the British flagship <a href="/wiki/HMS_Euryalus_(1853)" title="HMS Euryalus (1853)">HMS <i>Euryalus</i></a>, commanded by Captain J. H. I. Alexander. It was also the action at which <a href="/wiki/Duncan_Gordon_Boyes" title="Duncan Gordon Boyes">Duncan Gordon Boyes</a> won his <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Cross" title="Victoria Cross">Victoria Cross</a> (VC) at the age of seventeen. Satow described Boyes as receiving the award "for conduct very plucky in one so young." Another VC winner at Shimonoseki was <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Pride_(VC)" title="Thomas Pride (VC)">Thomas Pride</a>, and the third was the first American to win the medal, <a href="/wiki/William_Henry_Harrison_Seeley" title="William Henry Harrison Seeley">William Seeley</a>. De Casembroot wrote his account of the events in <i>De Medusa in de wateren van Japan, in 1863 en 1864</i>.</p>
<p>The stringent accord, drawn up in the wake of the ceasefire and negotiated by U.S. Minister Pruyn, included an indemnity of $3,000,000 from the Japanese, an amount equivalent to the cost of about 30 steamships at that time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a> proved unable to pay such an amount, and this failure became the basis of further foreign pressure to have the treaties<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="What treaties? (January 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> ratified by the Emperor, the harbor of <a href="/wiki/Hy%C5%8Dgo_Prefecture" title="Hyōgo Prefecture">Hyōgo</a> opened to foreign trade, and customs tariffs lowered uniformly to 5%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1883, twenty years after the first battle to reopen the strait, the United States quietly returned $750,000 to Japan, which represented its share of the reparation payment.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shimonoseki_Campaign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hamaguri_rebellion.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="628" data-file-width="846" height="163" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Hamaguri_rebellion.jpg/220px-Hamaguri_rebellion.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Hamaguri_rebellion.jpg/330px-Hamaguri_rebellion.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Hamaguri_rebellion.jpg/440px-Hamaguri_rebellion.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hamaguri_rebellion.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Chōshū forces attacking Shogunal forces in Kyōto on August 20, 1864, in the <a href="/wiki/Kinmon_incident" title="Kinmon incident">Kinmon incident</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Right after the foreign interventions, the Shogunal government also launched its own preparations for a <a href="/wiki/Punitive_expedition" title="Punitive expedition">punitive expedition</a> against Chōshū, the <a href="/wiki/First_Ch%C5%8Dsh%C5%AB_expedition" title="First Chōshū expedition">First Chōshū expedition</a>. The expedition was aimed at punishing the 1864 <a href="/wiki/Kinmon_incident" title="Kinmon incident">Kinmon incident</a> in which Chōshū forces attacked Shogunal forces in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ky%C5%8Dto" title="Kyōto">Kyōto</a>. The expedition was however cancelled after a compromise was brokered, involving the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beheading" title="Beheading">beheading</a> of the leaders of the rebellion.</p>
<p>At the same time as this campaign, the British Royal Navy engaged <a href="/wiki/Satsuma_Domain" title="Satsuma Domain">Satsuma</a> samurai at the <a href="/wiki/Bombardment_of_Kagoshima" title="Bombardment of Kagoshima">Bombardment of Kagoshima</a>, one of the several engagements of the Japanese conflict of 1863 and 1864.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_significance">Historical significance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shimonoseki_Campaign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Historical significance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Closely resembling the series of little conflicts fought by the European powers in Asia, Africa and elsewhere during the nineteenth century, the troubles in Japan seemed to exemplify their <a href="/wiki/Gunboat_diplomacy" title="Gunboat diplomacy">gunboat diplomacy</a>, a prevalent tool in imperialism. Bitter resentment against foreign influence made the Chōshū clan feel justified in engaging in foolish acts of military provocation in defiance of their own government.</p>
<p>The same nationalistic anger directed against foreigners would later flare up in the Chinese <a href="/wiki/Boxer_Rebellion" title="Boxer Rebellion">Boxer Rebellion</a>. The U.S. and its European allies then felt compelled to use military force to uphold the treaty with Japan. For the U.S., July 1863 was a momentous month, with the battles of <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Gettysburg" title="Battle of Gettysburg">Gettysburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vicksburg_Campaign" title="Vicksburg Campaign">Vicksburg</a>.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>While it was bitterly embroiled in the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>, President <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a>'s government was carefully watched by the world for signs of weakness and indecision. The actions of <i>USS Wyoming</i> made it the first foreign warship to offensively uphold treaty rights with Japan; this fact coupled with the possibility that the events would mire the U.S. in a foreign war made the battle of Shimonoseki a significant engagement.</p>
<p>While the battles of Shimonoseki Strait were mere footnotes in the histories of the European powers, an interesting aspect of the affair was the resourcefulness displayed by the Japanese, something another generation of Europeans and Americans would come to appreciate eighty years later. The feudal Japanese had not set eyes on a steam-powered ship until Commodore Perry's arrival only a decade before <i>USS Wyoming<span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span></i>s battle. Yet they had rapidly learned the ways of the Europeans within that brief span, purchasing foreign vessels and arming them with foreign weaponry. The quality and abundance of these armaments in 1860s Japan shocked the world.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The Shimonoseki city government in 2004, in recognition of the importance of the bombardment in Japanese history, placed several life-size replicas of the guns used by Chōshū where they were captured.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The replicas are made of hollow steel and include coin-operated sound effects and smoke from the barrels.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
