<p><b>Capsule</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">カプセル</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kapuseru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, stylized as <b>CAPSULE</b> since autumn 2013<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Musicman-NET_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Musicman-NET-1">[1]</a></sup> and previously as <b>capsule</b>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Electronica" title="Electronica">electronica</a> band consisting of record producer <a href="/wiki/Yasutaka_Nakata" title="Yasutaka Nakata">Yasutaka Nakata</a> and vocalist <a href="/wiki/Toshiko_Koshijima" title="Toshiko Koshijima">Toshiko Koshijima</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capsule_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Capsule formed in November 1997, after Nakata Yasutaka and Koshijima Toshiko met at the Teens' Music Festival in the Hokuriku area convention when they were 17 years old. Their first single was "Sakura", released in March 2001 on Yamaha Music Communications. Their first album, <i>High Collar Girl</i>, was significantly different from their later works in that it did not involve nearly as much use of synthesizers or contain futuristic/electronic sounds.</p>
<p>Capsule's style in albums up to <i>L.D.K. Lounge Designers Killer</i> (2005) was frequently referred to as "neo-Shibuya-kei" due to their stylistic similarities, both aesthetically and musically, to acts from the <a href="/wiki/Shibuya-kei" title="Shibuya-kei">Shibuya-kei</a> movement of the 1990s, most notably <a href="/wiki/Pizzicato_Five" title="Pizzicato Five">Pizzicato Five</a>. It contained elements of <a href="/wiki/Bossa_nova" title="Bossa nova">bossa nova</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lounge_music" title="Lounge music">lounge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Breakbeat" title="Breakbeat">breakbeat</a>. From <i>Fruits Clipper</i> (2006) on their style was increasingly <a href="/wiki/Electro_house" title="Electro house">electro house</a>.</p>
<p>Capsule is known for frequently licensing their music for use on television programs in Japan, such as <a href="/wiki/Utawara_Hot_Hit_10" title="Utawara Hot Hit 10">Utawara Hot Hit 10</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hello!_Morning" title="Hello! Morning">Hello! Morning</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nankai_Paradise&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nankai Paradise (page does not exist)">Nankai Paradise</a>. <a href="/wiki/Yugo_Nakamura" title="Yugo Nakamura">Yugo Nakamura</a>'s studio, Tha of Japan, used Capsule's music for promotional TV and web advertisements for KDDI. Their song Portable Airport (remix) is featured in promos for <a href="/wiki/The_Comedy_Channel" title="The Comedy Channel">The Comedy Channel</a> on Australian pay TV. The album <i><a href="/wiki/More!_More!_More!_(Capsule_album)" title="More! More! More! (Capsule album)">More! More! More!</a></i> ranked 6th in its first week on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> weekly album chart, and ranked 3rd on the daily chart, marking their first time in the top 10.</p>
<p>On January 19, 2010, Capsule released "Love or Lies" which was used as an insert song for the show <i>Liar Game Season 2</i> and the movie <i>Liar Game: The Final Stage</i>. It was included on their album, <i><a href="/wiki/Player_(Capsule_album)" title="Player (Capsule album)">Player</a></i>, which was released on March 3, 2010.</p>
<p>Their twelfth studio album, initially titled <i>KILLER WAVE</i>, was originally slated for a March 23, 2011 release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-2">[2]</a></sup> But following the <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami</a>, the album title was changed to <i><a href="/wiki/World_of_Fantasy_(album)" title="World of Fantasy (album)">World of Fantasy</a></i> and its release was postponed to May 25, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The band's fourteenth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Caps_Lock_(album)" title="Caps Lock (album)">Caps Lock</a></i>, was released on October 23, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
