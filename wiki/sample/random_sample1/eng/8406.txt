<p><b>Simon "Sima" Vukčević</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cyrillic" title="Cyrillic">Cyrillic</a>: Симон "Сима" Вукчевић; born 29 January 1986) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Montenegrins_(ethnic_group)" title="Montenegrins (ethnic group)">Montenegrin</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/G.D._Chaves" title="G.D. Chaves">Chaves</a> as an attacking midfielder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vukčević started learning his craft at <a href="/wiki/FK_Zabjelo" title="FK Zabjelo">FK Zabjelo</a> before moving to the Serbian capital to join the <a href="/wiki/FK_Partizan" title="FK Partizan">FK Partizan</a> youth system at 13.</p>
<p>Vukčević's father Ilija is the former sporting director at <a href="/wiki/FK_Zeta" title="FK Zeta">FK Zeta</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Vuk%C4%8Devi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Partizan">Partizan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Vuk%C4%8Devi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Partizan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Blessed with boundless confidence, he spent four years in the youth ranks of <a href="/wiki/FK_Partizan" title="FK Partizan">Partizan</a> before bursting into the first team, scoring and creating goals from midfield. He was awarded the number 1 shirt, in recognition of his popularity among Partizan supporters.</p>
<p>His best season with the club was the 2004–05 campaign during which he starred with eye-catching midfield displays in domestic league and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a>. For his efforts with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serbia_and_Montenegro_national_under-21_football_team" title="Serbia and Montenegro national under-21 football team">Serbia and Montenegro U21 team</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2004_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship" title="2004 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship">2004 UEFA U-21 Championship</a> in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serbia_and_Montenegro_national_under-23_football_team" title="Serbia and Montenegro national under-23 football team">Serbia and Mentenegro Olympic football team</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Summer Olympics</a> as well as his contribution at Partizan, at the end of 2004, Vukčević received the Golden Badge Award for the Best Young Athlete of the Year in Serbia and Montenegro given out by <a href="/wiki/DSL_Sport" title="DSL Sport">DSL Sport</a>. However, the form didn't spill over into 2005–06 season as Vukčević faced criticism over his excessive solo play and poor shot selection, as well as overall deteriorating form.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Saturn_Ramenskoye">Saturn Ramenskoye</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Vuk%C4%8Devi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Saturn Ramenskoye">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Despite criticism, his move to Russian side <a href="/wiki/FC_Saturn_Ramenskoye" title="FC Saturn Ramenskoye">Saturn Ramenskoye</a> in January 2006 came as somewhat of a shock. The transfer, said to have cost the Russian club about €7 million, failed to raise Vukčević's form to what it once was at Partizan. In late July 2006, he was even demoted to Saturn's reserve squad with coach <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vladim%C3%ADr_Weiss_(footballer_born_1964)" title="Vladimír Weiss (footballer born 1964)">Vladimír Weiss</a> publicly blasting him in the Russian press for displaying bad attitude in training and having a lack of manners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Saturn finished the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Premier_League_2006" title="Russian Premier League 2006">2006 season</a> at disappointing 11th place in <a href="/wiki/Russian_Premier_League" title="Russian Premier League">Russian league</a>, and as the 2007 season was about to start Vukčević expressed frustration with coach Weiss playing him out of position. He also said that coming to Saturn was a mistake and that he would have preferred a more ambitious club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Premier_League_2007" title="Russian Premier League 2007">2007 season</a> started somewhat more promisingly for Vukčević as he finally got some playing time, which he used to finally score his first league goal. However, things soon went awry again and he completely lost a spot on the team, which prompted Saturn brass to start looking at options to move him. The departure of coach Weiss and subsequent arrival of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gadzhi_Gadzhiev" title="Gadzhi Gadzhiev">Gadzhi Gadzhiev</a> to Saturn bench hardly changed matters for Vukčević as he was completely out of the first team picture.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sporting_CP">Sporting CP</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Vuk%C4%8Devi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sporting CP">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 25 June 2007, Saturn reportedly agreed a €4 million transfer fee with <a href="/wiki/Sporting_Clube_de_Portugal" title="Sporting Clube de Portugal">Sporting Clube de Portugal</a>, but the deal stalled when the Russian club also demanded 20% of any subsequent transfer fees involving Vukčević. Eventually, on 28 June, both clubs agreed a €2 million fee in addition to an agreement about splitting his future transfer fee 50–50.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vukčević scored on his debut in a pre-season friendly against <a href="/wiki/Lille_OSC" title="Lille OSC">Lille OSC</a> and soon made himself a mainstay of Paulo Bento's team. His <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portuguese_Liga" title="Portuguese Liga">Portuguese Liga</a> debut came against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Associa%C3%A7%C3%A3o_Acad%C3%A9mica_de_Coimbra_-_O.A.F." title="Associação Académica de Coimbra - O.A.F.">Académica de Coimbra</a> in the season opener where he was man of the match, but the midfielder was especially impressive during a rough period Sporting endured around Christmas, several times carrying the side with his never-say-die attitude and eye for goal.</p>
<p>Vukčević scored an important goal against rivals <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">Benfica</a> on 2 March 2008, which ended in a 1–1 home draw. On 27 January, he scored against leaders <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Porto" title="F.C. Porto">FC Porto</a> in Sporting's 2–0 win. He ranked the team's second best league scorer, only behind <a href="/wiki/Li%C3%A9dson" title="Liédson">Liédson</a>.</p>
<p>During the summer 2008 transfer window, <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> were at the top of the list of clubs reportedly interested in signing the Montenegrin player. Nonetheless, Sporting reiterated their desire to keep Vukčević for the 2008–09 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the start of the 2008–09 season, Vukčević got involved in a controversial dispute with Sporting manager <a href="/wiki/Paulo_Bento" title="Paulo Bento">Paulo Bento</a> over losing his place in the starting XI. After playing 45 minutes as a substitute in Sporting's 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/C.F._Os_Belenenses" title="C.F. Os Belenenses">Belenenses</a>, Vukčević declared his intention to leave the club in the December transfer window.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 21 November 2008, without any warning, he missed training to join his national team one day before expected. Sporting fined him €5,000 and placed him on individual training until further notice. On 29 November, after holding a press conference stating his wish to remain in Sporting and apologizing for his behavior, he was allowed to train with the rest of the team. Sporting told him that "words impress no one," and his future on the club would depend on how he took this second chance. On 5 December, he substituted teammate <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9lder_Postiga" title="Hélder Postiga">Hélder Postiga</a> in the 75th minute of the away league game against <a href="/wiki/C.F._Estrela_da_Amadora" title="C.F. Estrela da Amadora">Estrela da Amadora</a> and scored a goal two minutes later, the last of Sporting's 3–1 win.</p>
<p>In January 2010, Sporting bought the remain 50% interests in future transfer revenue from Saturn for another £2 million.</p>
<p>On 20 August 2011, it was revealed by <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> manager <a href="/wiki/Steve_Kean" title="Steve Kean">Steve Kean</a> that Vukčević would undergo a medical at the club after agreeing personal terms in the hope of imminently completing a transfer carrying a fee reported to be around £3 million.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Blackburn_Rovers">Blackburn Rovers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Vuk%C4%8Devi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Blackburn Rovers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 26 August 2011, it was confirmed that Vukčević had signed a three-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Vukčević made his Blackburn debut in the 4–3 victory against <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> at <a href="/wiki/Ewood_Park" title="Ewood Park">Ewood Park</a> on 17 September 2011. He scored his first goal for Blackburn on 20 September 2011 in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> against <a href="/wiki/Leyton_Orient_F.C." title="Leyton Orient F.C.">Leyton Orient</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On 11 December 2011, he scored his first league goal for Blackburn away at <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>During his first season at the club, Vukčević made little impact in helping the club survive the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> season due to a lack of appearances. Vukčević only appeared 10 times in the season and only managed to score 2 goals. It was rumoured that Vukčević had contract issues midway through the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> This meant that he did not make a single appearance after 7 January 2012 until the end of the season.</p>
<p>Vukčević made his first start of the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_in_English_football" title="2012–13 in English football">2012–13 season</a> on 29 August 2012, in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> second round match against <a href="/wiki/Milton_Keynes_Dons_F.C." title="Milton Keynes Dons F.C.">Milton Keynes Dons</a>, in which he played the full 90 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> This appearance looked to be the last for Vukčević for the season and the club as they allowed him to go out on loan for the rest of the season with a view to a permanent deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> However, this looked unlikely as in the first game under new manager <a href="/wiki/Henning_Berg" title="Henning Berg">Henning Berg</a> on 3 November 2012 against <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a>, Vukčević came on as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Olsson" title="Marcus Olsson">Marcus Olsson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Over the next few months, Vukčević made more first team appearances and was even chosen by new manager <a href="/wiki/Michael_Appleton" title="Michael Appleton">Michael Appleton</a>. Although on 31 January 2013, Vukčević's contract at the club was terminated with immediate effect by mutual consent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Before the contract termination, there was reports that he was potentially going to spend the rest of the season on loan at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/APOEL" title="APOEL">APOEL</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Cypriot_First_Division" title="Cypriot First Division">Cypriot First Division</a>, but no deal was struck.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He spent a total of 2 years at the club, but struggled to break into the regular first team.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Karpaty_Lviv">Karpaty Lviv</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Vuk%C4%8Devi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Karpaty Lviv">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 28 February 2013, Vukčević signed a contract with <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Premier_League" title="Ukrainian Premier League">Ukrainian Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/FC_Karpaty_Lviv" title="FC Karpaty Lviv">Karpaty Lviv</a> until the end of the season with the option to extend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Several days after signing for the club, Vukčević made his debut, coming on as a substitute for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oleksandr_Gladkiy" title="Oleksandr Gladkiy">Oleksandr Gladkiy</a>, in a win against <a href="/wiki/SC_Tavriya_Simferopol" title="SC Tavriya Simferopol">Tavriya Simferopol</a>. Having made two appearance for the club, Vukčević left the club by mutual consent, citing personal reasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vojvodina">Vojvodina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Vuk%C4%8Devi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Vojvodina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On August 10, 2013, it was announced that Vukčević signed a one-year contract with Serbian club <a href="/wiki/FK_Vojvodina" title="FK Vojvodina">FK Vojvodina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He made his debut on August 22, 2013 in a game against <a href="/wiki/FC_Sheriff_Tiraspol" title="FC Sheriff Tiraspol">FC Sheriff Tiraspol</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2013-14_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2013-14 UEFA Europa League">2013-14 UEFA Europa League</a> play-off round of qualification, during which he made an assist.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Vuk%C4%8Devi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth_level">Youth level</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Vuk%C4%8Devi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Youth level">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2002 he was part of the FR Yugoslavia U-17 squad at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2002_UEFA_European_Under-17_Football_Championship" title="2002 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship">2002 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the age of 18, Vukčević was selected by head coach <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Petrovi%C4%87" title="Vladimir Petrović">Vladimir Petrović</a> to be part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serbia_and_Montenegro_national_under-21_football_team" title="Serbia and Montenegro national under-21 football team">Serbia and Montenegro under-21 national team</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_U-21_Championship_2004" title="UEFA U-21 Championship 2004">2004 European Under-21 Football Championship</a> in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. Vukčević had a notable tournament, scoring in a group stage loss to <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">Italy</a> (1–2), as well as in a semi-final penalty shootout against <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_under-21_football_team" title="Sweden national under-21 football team">Sweden</a>. Somewhat surprisingly, Serbia-Montenegro progressed all the way to the final where it lost again to Italy, led by rising 21-year-old star <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Gilardino" title="Alberto Gilardino">Alberto Gilardino</a> and 20-year-old <a href="/wiki/Daniele_De_Rossi" title="Daniele De Rossi">Daniele De Rossi</a>.</p>
<p>Barely two months later, Vukčević was then part of the <a href="/wiki/Serbia_and_Montenegro_national_football_team" title="Serbia and Montenegro national football team">Serbian and Montenegrin</a> <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Olympic</a> football team that exited in the first round, finishing fourth in Group C behind gold-medal winners <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australia_national_association_football_team" title="Australia national association football team">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tunisia_national_football_team" title="Tunisia national football team">Tunisia</a>.</p>
<p>At the end of the year, he received the award for Best Young Athlete of <a href="/wiki/Serbia_and_Montenegro" title="Serbia and Montenegro">Serbia and Montenegro</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<p>He also played for the national under-21 squad that qualified for another European championships, this time in <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> 2006. Capped five times already for the senior national side, Vukčević was hoping to further hone his talents with the Under-21s in Portugal, but had a poor tournament. Following an indifferent display in the first group game (0–1 loss to <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_under-21_football_team" title="Germany national under-21 football team">Germany</a>), head coach <a href="/wiki/Dragan_Okuka" title="Dragan Okuka">Dragan Okuka</a> dropped him from the first team and Vukčević rarely featured in the side for the rest of the tournament. Serbia-Montenegro ended up progressing to semifinal where it lost on penalites to <a href="/wiki/Ukraine_national_under-21_football_team" title="Ukraine national under-21 football team">Ukraine</a>.</p>
<p>Vukčević was once again called up, this time at Under-21 level, to represent <a href="/wiki/Montenegro_national_under-21_football_team" title="Montenegro national under-21 football team">Montenegro</a> in its premier match against <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria_national_under-21_football_team" title="Bulgaria national under-21 football team">Bulgaria</a> in the qualifications for Under-21 European Championship 2009, scoring a goal and missing a penalty on his debut in a 2–1 victory against the Bulgarians.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Senior_level">Senior level</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Vuk%C4%8Devi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Senior level">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following his noted under-21 displays, Vukčević started getting call-ups to the Serbia-Montenegro senior side from head coach <a href="/wiki/Ilija_Petkovi%C4%87" title="Ilija Petković">Ilija Petković</a>. His debut came at the age of 18 during July 2004 friendly, as a substitute against <a href="/wiki/Slovakia_national_football_team" title="Slovakia national football team">Slovakia</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Kirin_Cup" title="Kirin Cup">Kirin Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka" title="Fukuoka">Fukuoka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. He was among a large number of younger players from the domestic league or those who have been out of the national team picture (<a href="/wiki/Bojan_Zaji%C4%87" title="Bojan Zajić">Bojan Zajić</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C4%90or%C4%91e_Joki%C4%87" title="Đorđe Jokić">Đorđe Jokić</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bojan_Neziri" title="Bojan Neziri">Bojan Neziri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jovan_Markoski" title="Jovan Markoski">Jovan Markoski</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nenad_Jestrovi%C4%87" title="Nenad Jestrović">Nenad Jestrović</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dragan_%C5%A0arac" title="Dragan Šarac">Dragan Šarac</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milo%C5%A1_Kolakovi%C4%87" title="Miloš Kolaković">Miloš Kolaković</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aleksandar_Panti%C4%87_(footballer_born_1978)" title="Aleksandar Pantić (footballer born 1978)">Aleksandar Pantić</a>) who were able to get an opportunity to show what they could do in the Kirin Cup.</p>
<p>A couple of days later Vukčević featured in another Kirin Cup match – this time versus Japan, again as a sub. As his form at Partizan stabilized, he received another call-up in February 2005 for a friendly at Bulgaria, which was his first national team start before being substituted for <a href="/wiki/Zvonimir_Vuki%C4%87" title="Zvonimir Vukić">Zvonimir Vukić</a>. Couple of months later in June 2005, coach Petković gave Vukčević his competitive debut, putting him in for <a href="/wiki/Ognjen_Koroman" title="Ognjen Koroman">Ognjen Koroman</a> during the second half of the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_-_UEFA_Group_7" title="2006 FIFA World Cup qualification - UEFA Group 7">qualifier</a> at home versus <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_football_team" title="Belgium national football team">Belgium</a>. Serbia-Montenegro played to a sluggish and forgettable 0–0 draw as Vukčević almost scored on his competitive debut when the Belgian keeper misjudged his harmless free kick and had to scramble back to push it against the cross bar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Four days later, Vukčević was on a plane to <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> where he played the full 90 minutes in a friendly versus <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Rogers_Centre" title="Rogers Centre">Rogers Centre</a>. This also turned out to be his last ever appearance for Serbia-Montenegro. His club form at Partizan took a dip in the first part of 2005–06 league season and his transfer to Saturn failed to improve his performances during the early part of 2006 Russian league season, all of which meant that he wasn't getting any more senior call-ups.</p>
<p>As <a href="/wiki/Montenegro" title="Montenegro">Montenegro</a> seceded from the <a href="/wiki/Serbia_and_Montenegro" title="Serbia and Montenegro">State Union of Serbia-Montenegro</a> in May 2006, and Vukčević is from <a href="/wiki/Podgorica" title="Podgorica">Podgorica</a>, he chose to play for <a href="/wiki/Montenegro_national_football_team" title="Montenegro national football team">Montenegro</a> in the future. He played in their first ever game, when Montenegro won over <a href="/wiki/Hungary_national_football_team" title="Hungary national football team">Hungary</a> in a friendly match that ended 2–1 on 24 March 2007.</p>
