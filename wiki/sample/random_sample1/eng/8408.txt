<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simmone_Jade_Mackinnon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mackinnon was born <b>Simone Jade Mackinnon</b> in <a href="/wiki/Mount_Isa" title="Mount Isa">Mount Isa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a> in 1973 to Ian and Annette Mackinnon. She has a brother and a sister, Robbie and Kym.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Simmone_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Simmone-1">[1]</a></sup> She and her family later uprooted to <a href="/wiki/Coffs_Harbour" title="Coffs Harbour">Coffs Harbour</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simmone_Jade_Mackinnon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mackinnon began her acting career in 1988, appearing in the Australian feature film <i>Something About Love</i>. Two further features followed, <i><a href="/wiki/Dating_the_Enemy" title="Dating the Enemy">Dating the Enemy</a></i> and <i>Dust of the Wings</i>, for which she appeared in small roles, although her role in <i>Dust of the Wing</i> was a much larger part. In 1997, she began to appear on television. She had a guest appearance on the fantasy television series <i><a href="/wiki/Spellbinder:_Land_of_the_Dragon_Lord" title="Spellbinder: Land of the Dragon Lord">Spellbinder: Land of the Dragon Lord</a></i>, a sequel to the original series <i><a href="/wiki/Spellbinder_(TV_series)" title="Spellbinder (TV series)">Spellbinder</a></i>. Mackinnon is known for her role in the <i><a href="/wiki/Baywatch" title="Baywatch">Baywatch</a></i> spin-off series <i>Baywatch Hawaii</i>, where she played the role of 'Allie Reese' opposite <a href="/wiki/David_Hasselhoff" title="David Hasselhoff">David Hasselhoff</a>. She appeared as a regular during the shows first season. MacKinnon is best known for her role as '<a href="/wiki/List_of_McLeod%27s_Daughters_characters" title="List of McLeod's Daughters characters">Stevie Hall</a>' (later 'Hall-Ryan') in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Logie_Award" title="Logie Award">Logie Award</a>-winning Australian television series <i><a href="/wiki/McLeod%27s_Daughters" title="McLeod's Daughters">McLeod's Daughters</a></i>, where she appeared at the end of <a href="/wiki/McLeod%27s_Daughters_(season_3)" title="McLeod's Daughters (season 3)">season three</a> through the <a href="/wiki/McLeod%27s_Daughters_(season_8)" title="McLeod's Daughters (season 8)">final season</a> in 2009. Her role as Stevie had earned her several Logie Award nominations, for Most Popular New Female Talent<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Logie_Award_for_Most_Popular_Actress" title="Logie Award for Most Popular Actress">Most Popular Actress</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Logie_Award_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Logie_Award-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Logie_Award_2_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Logie_Award_2-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2007 &amp; 2009, she received <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gold_Logie_Award_for_Most_Popular_Personality_on_Australian_Television" title="Gold Logie Award for Most Popular Personality on Australian Television">Gold Logie Award</a> nominations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Logie_Award_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Logie_Award-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Logie_Award_2_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Logie_Award_2-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Mackinnon's other television work includes roles on <i><a href="/wiki/Water_Rats_(TV_series)" title="Water Rats (TV series)">Water Rats</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/All_Saints_(TV_series)" title="All Saints (TV series)">All Saints</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_Arthur_Conan_Doyle%27s_The_Lost_World" title="Sir Arthur Conan Doyle's The Lost World">Sir Arthur Conan Doyle's The Lost World</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cut_(Australian_TV_series)" title="The Cut (Australian TV series)">The Cut</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Rescue:_Special_Ops" title="Rescue: Special Ops">Rescue: Special Ops</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cops_L.A.C." title="Cops L.A.C.">Cops L.A.C.</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/City_Homicide" title="City Homicide">City Homicide</a></i>. In 2001, she appeared with <a href="/wiki/Powers_Boothe" title="Powers Boothe">Powers Boothe</a> in the television mini-series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Attila_(TV_miniseries)" title="Attila (TV miniseries)">Attila</a></i>, which also featured <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Butler" title="Gerard Butler">Gerard Butler</a>, and in 2003, she starred in the <a href="/wiki/Syfy" title="Syfy">Syfy</a> television film <i><a href="/wiki/Deep_Shock" title="Deep Shock">Deep Shock</a></i>. Mackinnon had also played roles in three direct-to-video films, <i>Python</i>, <i>Dark Waters</i> and <i>Submission</i>, in which she also served as producer.</p>
<p>From 1993 to 1995 she toured with the musical <i><a href="/wiki/Cats_(musical)" title="Cats (musical)">Cats</a></i> (in the role of the cat "Cassandra") through Australia and Asia. In June 2009, Mackinnon appears as a presenter along host <a href="/wiki/Karl_Stefanovic" title="Karl Stefanovic">Karl Stefanovic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scott_Cam" title="Scott Cam">Scott Cam</a> in <i>Random Acts of Kindness</i> on the <i>Nine Network</i>. Mackinnon completed a second season of <i>Random Acts of Kindness</i> in January 2010.</p>
<p>On 4 March 2012, it was announced Mackinnon had joined the cast of <i><a href="/wiki/Neighbours" title="Neighbours">Neighbours</a></i> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zoe_Alexander" title="Zoe Alexander">Zoe Alexander</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FarmLife_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-FarmLife-8">[8]</a></sup> The actress began appearing on screen from May and she relocated to Melbourne for filming.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FarmLife_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-FarmLife-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simmone_Jade_Mackinnon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mackinnon gave birth to her first child, a boy, Madigan James Mackinnon, on 19 March 2010, her 37th birthday.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dogs have always been a part of her life. She was named an RSPCA superhero "for giving love, respect and support to all animals".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DogsLife_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-DogsLife-10">[10]</a></sup> As a child traveling with her parents, she found an abandoned Australian Cattle Dog, and since then has had ACDs in her life. Her current dogs are Australian Cattle Dog Maha Kala and terrier Mr Miyagi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DogsLife_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-DogsLife-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
