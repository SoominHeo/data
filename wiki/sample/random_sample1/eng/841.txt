<p><b>Club Atlético Lanús</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[ˈkluβ aˈtletiko laˈnus]</a></span>) is an <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentine</a> <a href="/wiki/Sports_club" title="Sports club">sports club</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Lan%C3%BAs" title="Lanús">Lanús</a> district of <a href="/wiki/Greater_Buenos_Aires" title="Greater Buenos Aires">Greater Buenos Aires</a>. Founded on 3 January 1915, the club's main sports are <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> and basketball. In both sports, Lanús plays in Argentina's top divisions: <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Argentine Primera División">Primera División</a> (football) and <a href="/wiki/Liga_Nacional_de_B%C3%A1squet" title="Liga Nacional de Básquet">Liga Nacional de Básquet</a> (basketball). In football, Lanús has won five major championships in its history: the <a href="/wiki/1996_Copa_CONMEBOL" title="1996 Copa CONMEBOL">1996 Copa CONMEBOL</a> (the predecessor of the current <a href="/wiki/Copa_Sudamericana" title="Copa Sudamericana">Copa Sudamericana</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n#Torneo_Apertura" title="2007–08 Argentine Primera División">2007 Apertura</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2013_Copa_Sudamericana" title="2013 Copa Sudamericana">2013 Copa Sudamericana</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2016_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2016 Argentine Primera División">2016 Argentine Primera División</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/2016_Copa_Bicentenario" title="2016 Copa Bicentenario">2016 Bicentenario</a> national cup.</p>
<p>Apart from football, Lanús hosts many other sports such as athletics, gymnastics, martial arts, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Team_handball" title="Team handball">handball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Field_hockey" title="Field hockey">field hockey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roller_skating" title="Roller skating">roller skating</a>, swimming, tennis, volleyball and <a href="/wiki/Olympic_weightlifting" title="Olympic weightlifting">weightlifting</a>. The club has a <a href="/wiki/Futsal" title="Futsal">futsal</a> team in Colombia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Lan%C3%BAs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins_and_foundation">Origins and foundation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Lan%C3%BAs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins and foundation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1854 Anacarsis Lanus arrived from France and acquired the lands where the city of <a href="/wiki/Lan%C3%BAs" title="Lanús">Lanús</a> is placed nowadays. Two institutions were named "Lanús" by that time. One of them was Lanús Athletic Club, which took part of the 1897 <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Argentine Primera División">Argentine Primera División</a> championship<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> although the club then abandoned the tournament. The other club was Lanús United (predecessor of current Club Atlético Lanús) which participated in the Copa de la Confederación Argentina in 1913 and 1914.</p>
<p>Club Atlético Lanús was founded on 3 January 1915, being formed from the merger of Lanús United (that was in a desperate financial situation) and Club El Progreso. Miguel Usaray was designed as President, the first in the history of the club. In an assemble held on 27 January 1915, the name "Club Atlético Lanús" was officially established.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-legado_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-legado-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Debut_in_Primera:_Decade_of_1920">Debut in Primera: Decade of 1920</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Lan%C3%BAs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Debut in Primera: Decade of 1920">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:232px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lanus_1927.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="479" data-file-width="768" height="143" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Lanus_1927.jpg/230px-Lanus_1927.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Lanus_1927.jpg/345px-Lanus_1927.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Lanus_1927.jpg/460px-Lanus_1927.jpg 2x" width="230"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lanus_1927.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Lanús finished 3rd in the 1927 season, its first campaign in the amateur era.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The club begins to play its matches in División Intermedia (current <a href="/wiki/Primera_B_Metropolitana" title="Primera B Metropolitana">Primera B Metropolitana</a>) at Lanús United old stadium, located in Margarita Wield and General Deheza streets. In 1919 the club got promotion to Primera División after beating <a href="/wiki/Argentino_de_Quilmes" title="Argentino de Quilmes">Argentino de Quilmes</a>. In Primera División, Lanús played its first games in the Asociación Argentina de Football, then switching to dissident Asociación Amateurs de Football (AAm), where the team joined on 8 August 1920. That time Lanús was beat by <a href="/wiki/Racing_Club_de_Avellaneda" title="Racing Club de Avellaneda">Racing Club</a> by 1–0. That first season in the top division Lanús finished 11th of 20th.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>During successive years, Lanús did not achieve great campaigns in Primera, even finishing last in 1923. That season the squad only achieved two wins and lost 14 games of 20. In 1926 Lanús finished 6th and the 1927 season the team made its best performance in the amateur era, finishing 3rd to San Lorenzo and Boca Juniors. Lanús earned 50 points with 22 wins over 33 matches played, being defeated 5 times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 24 February 1929 Lanús opened its new stadium in the intersection of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/H%C3%A9ctor_Guidi" title="Héctor Guidi">Héctor Guidi</a> and General Arias streets. The stadium (with wood stands as it was usual for that time) was built on a 50,000 m2 land given by the British-owned company <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires_Great_Southern_Railway" title="Buenos Aires Great Southern Railway">Ferrocarril del Sud</a>. Then president of the club Sivlio Peri made the arrangements to get the cession did not have any cost for the institution, at least for the first years. On 24 March 1929 Lanús played its first match there facing <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Platense" title="Club Atlético Platense">Platense</a>, defeating it by 5–2.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1930_and_1940_decades">1930 and 1940 decades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Lan%C3%BAs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1930 and 1940 decades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1931 football became professional in Argentina. Lanús did not make a good campaign, finishing penultimate achieving only 22 points, 28 less than champion Boca Juniors. One year later the club inaugurated a new grandstand in its stadium. The first version of the Lanús Anthem, composed by Domingo Ilvento (music) and Daniel Cao (lyrics) was also released.</p>
<p>In 1933 founding member Miguel Iguzguiza made the arrangements to acquire the lands where the new headquarters would be built, on José C. Paz avenue (current 9 de Julio avenue). This was approved in a meeting held on 23 December. One year later the Association obliged both Club Lanús and rival team <a href="/wiki/Talleres_de_Remedios_de_Escalada" title="Talleres de Remedios de Escalada">Talleres de Remedios de Escalada</a> to join in order to play the tournament under the name "Unión Talleres-Lanús", threatening them to be relegated if they did not accept. This fusion was ended in 1935 when both clubs played again separately.</p>
<p>Some of the most notable players of those years were Atilio Ducca (the most capped player in Lanús' history with 291 games). In 1939 forward Luis Arrieta came to the club, scoring 31 goals during his first season with the club. Arrieta was also the top-scorer in 1943 along with <a href="/wiki/%C3%81ngel_Labruna" title="Ángel Labruna">Ángel Labruna</a> (River Plate) and Raúl Frutos (Platense). Arrieta would later become the all-time top scorer of Lanús, with 120 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lanusweb_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-lanusweb-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lanús remained in Primera until 1949 when the team was relegated after a controversial decision from the Association. At the end of the tournament, Boca Juniors was placed last and Lanús penultimate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On 8 December Boca smashed Lanús by 5–1, which finished last along with <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Hurac%C3%A1n" title="Club Atlético Huracán">Huracán</a>. In order to define which team would be relegated, Lanús and Huracán had to play a relegation series. Huracán won the first game 1–0 and Lanús took revenge by 4–1 in the second match so a third game was played. With a partial score of 3–3 the referee awarded a penalty kick to Lanús. The Huracán players, in disagreement with the decision, abandoned the field being the match suspended. The Argentine Association not only did not punished Huracán but it decided to play a new match. During that fourth game the referee did not award a penalty kick to Lanús while Huracán was winning the match by 3–2. As a result, the Lanús players left the field (as their rivals had done before). But the Association decided to punish Lanús relegating the club to Primera B.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lanusweb_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-lanusweb-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Primera">Return to Primera</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Lan%C3%BAs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Return to Primera">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:232px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lanus_1950.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="526" data-file-width="904" height="134" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Lanus_1950.jpg/230px-Lanus_1950.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Lanus_1950.jpg/345px-Lanus_1950.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Lanus_1950.jpg/460px-Lanus_1950.jpg 2x" width="230"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lanus_1950.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The 1950 <a href="/wiki/Primera_B_Metropolitana" title="Primera B Metropolitana">Primera B</a> champion.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>After the controversial decision made by the AFA, Lanús played the 1950 season in the second division. With still 1 fixture to play, Lanús won the championship when the team defeated <a href="/wiki/Argentinos_Juniors" title="Argentinos Juniors">Argentinos Juniors</a> by 3–1 therefore promoting to Primera División. In Primera B, Lanús played 22 matches with 15 wins and 3 losses. The team achieved large victories over <a href="/wiki/Club_El_Porvenir" title="Club El Porvenir">El Porvenir</a> (5–1), <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Col%C3%B3n" title="Club Atlético Colón">Colón</a> (4–2), <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Temperley" title="Club Atlético Temperley">Temperley</a> (4–1 and 4–0), Argentinos Juniors (4–0), <a href="/wiki/Uni%C3%B3n_de_Santa_Fe" title="Unión de Santa Fe">Unión de Santa Fe</a> (6–1) and <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Nueva_Chicago" title="Club Atlético Nueva Chicago">Nueva Chicago</a> (6–1).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The line-up for the game that set up the return to Primera was: Alvarez Vega; Daponte, Mercado; Vargas, Strembel, Vivas; Contreras, Gil, Pairoux, Florio, Moyano.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id=".22The_Globetrotters.22">"The Globetrotters"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Lan%C3%BAs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title='Edit section: "The Globetrotters"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:232px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lanus_1956.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="573" data-file-width="784" height="168" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Lanus_1956.jpg/230px-Lanus_1956.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Lanus_1956.jpg/345px-Lanus_1956.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Lanus_1956.jpg/460px-Lanus_1956.jpg 2x" width="230"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lanus_1956.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The 1956 runner-up, nicknamed <i>The Globetrotters</i>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Back in the top division, Lanús would be the sensation of <a href="/wiki/1951_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="1951 Argentine Primera División">1951 championship</a>, finishing the first round in the 1st place along with Independiente. Forward José Florio was the top scorer with 21 goals, having been sold to Italian club <a href="/wiki/Torino_F.C." title="Torino F.C.">Torino</a> for a record <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argentine_Peso" title="Argentine Peso">$</a> 1,500,000. The club later used the money to build a gym.</p>
<p>Lanús finished 5th in 1954 and 1955. One year later Lanús would achieve its best performance in Primera División until then, finishing 2nd. to River Plate. Due to an outstanding line of <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">forwards</a> (that scored 49 goals in 30 games) and their skills with the ball, that squad was nicknamed <i>The Globetrotters</i> honoring <a href="/wiki/Harlem_Globetrotters" title="Harlem Globetrotters">the famous basketball team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-perfil_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-perfil-17">[17]</a></sup> The team suffered a lot of injuries all season long, with only Dante Lugo playing all matches. The usual team was Vega; Prato, Beltrán; Daponte, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hector_Guidi" title="Hector Guidi">Hector Guidi</a>, Nazionale; Carranza, Lugo, <a href="/wiki/Alfredo_Rojas_(Argentine_footballer)" title="Alfredo Rojas (Argentine footballer)">Alfredo Rojas</a>, Urbano Reynoso, Moyano.</p>
<p>The <i>Globetrotters</i> thrashed their rivals with large victories over Argentinos Juniors (4–0), <a href="/wiki/Club_de_Gimnasia_y_Esgrima_La_Plata" title="Club de Gimnasia y Esgrima La Plata">Gimnasia y Esgrima La Plata</a> (5–3), <a href="/wiki/San_Lorenzo_de_Almagro" title="San Lorenzo de Almagro">San Lorenzo</a> (4–0) and Huracán (4–2). Nevertheless, the team was beaten by 3–1 by River Plate, that would be later the champion. River Plate squad had players with less individual skills than Lanús', but they were more experienced and those qualities helped them to get the championship at the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-perfil_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-perfil-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Decades_of_1960_and_1970">Decades of 1960 and 1970</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Lan%C3%BAs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Decades of 1960 and 1970">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:232px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lanus_1964.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="957" data-file-width="1277" height="172" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Lanus_1964.jpg/230px-Lanus_1964.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Lanus_1964.jpg/345px-Lanus_1964.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Lanus_1964.jpg/460px-Lanus_1964.jpg 2x" width="230"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lanus_1964.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Managed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/H%C3%A9ctor_Guidi" title="Héctor Guidi">Héctor Guidi</a>, Lanús won the Primera B title in 1964.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>After some very irregular performances in Primera División, Lanús was relegated in 1961. The team finished 12th of 24th but it was relegated (along with <a href="/wiki/Club_Atletico_Los_Andes" title="Club Atletico Los Andes">Los Andes</a>) due to the league system calculated the average during the last three championships (1959, 1960, 1961).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Three years later Lanús won the Primera B title, returning to Primera División. The two forwards of the team, Manuel Silva and Bernardo Acosta, soon got recognition due to the famous "wall pass" they did together, being nicknamed <i>"Los Albañiles" ("The Construction Workers)</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1966 Héctor Guidi, one of the most notable players in club's history, left football. Three years later Acosta would be transfer to <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla</a>. Manuel Silva moved to his new club <a href="/wiki/Newell%27s_Old_Boys" title="Newell's Old Boys">Newell's Old Boys</a> in 1970, finishing with the <i>Albañiles</i> era. That same year the team was relegated to the second division (along with <a href="/wiki/Uni%C3%B3n_de_Santa_Fe" title="Unión de Santa Fe">Unión de Santa Fe</a>) after finishing 7th of 7 in the "Torneo reclasificatorio".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1971 Guidi became manager and Lanús started a new season in Primera B. The team made a great campaign winning a new title in second division and therefore promoting to Primera División. Lanús played a total of 28 games, winning 17 with 4 losses. The squad scored 68 goals and received 31.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Unfortunately for the club, the tenure of Lanús on 1972 Primera División season was the worst in its history, being relegated again at the end of the championship.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_worst_crisis">The worst crisis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Lan%C3%BAs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The worst crisis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Four years later, Lanús returned to Primera after winning the promotion tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Once more, the team remained a very short time in Primera so Lanús was relegated after the 1977 season. Lanús was relegated after a controversial decision by penalty kicks in a match against <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Platense" title="Club Atlético Platense">Platense</a>. After 20 penalties shot by all outfield players, it was the goalkeepers' turn. The Lanús goalkeeper shot first, but missed. It was the Platense goalkeeper's turn, but instead, Platense striker Miguel Ángel Juárez took it, breaking the rules. The referee allowed the goal, and Lanús were relegated illegitimately. The club reclaimed, but the Argentine football association did not respond.</p>
<p>In Primera B Lanús had a very poor season and was eventually relegated to the Third Division (<a href="/wiki/Primera_C" title="Primera C">Primera C</a>). With debts of over US$2 million, the club faced its worst crisis. By 1979 the club only had 2,000 members facing its first season in the Third Division. The political groups linked with the club's debts decided to forget its differences with the club and helped the club face forward.</p>
<p>Lanús won the 1981 Primera C title various fixtures before the season ended. The club, with help from the fans, was promoted to the Second Division once again having more than 10,000 members. In 1984 the team reached the semifinals of the promotion playoff to the First Division, when the team had to face <a href="/wiki/Racing_Club_de_Avellaneda" title="Racing Club de Avellaneda">Racing Club</a>. In the first leg, Racing Club beat Lanús 2–0. In the second leg, played in <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Independiente" title="Club Atlético Independiente">Independiente</a>'s stadium, the referee gave Racing Club a controversial penalty kick after disallowing a Lanús goal. Racing Club scored a goal, but the match was eventually suspended because of Lanús' fans. The match was continued at <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Atlanta" title="Club Atlético Atlanta">Atlanta</a>'s stadium some days after, and Lanús were down 2–1 after dominating the game. The referee, Emilio Misic, mistakenly gave the final whistle 5 minutes before the end of regulation. The Racing Club players already started celebrating, so the referee used that excuse not to reverse the decision. Lanús was once again disadvantaged because of a referee error, therefore losing the series and failing to gain promotion to the First Division.</p>
<p>Already having more than 25,000 members, the team was promoted to the Second Division in 1986. With <a href="/wiki/Miguel_%C3%81ngel_Russo" title="Miguel Ángel Russo">Miguel Ángel Russo</a> as manager, the team returned to the First Division after 13 years. Thanks to the team's goalkeeper, Alcides Herrera, the team beat <a href="/wiki/Quilmes_Atl%C3%A9tico_Club" title="Quilmes Atlético Club">Quilmes</a> in the finals of the promotion playoff. The club also started to repair the old stadium made of wood. Lanús made a poor campaign in Primera División being relegated to <a href="/wiki/Primera_B_Nacional" title="Primera B Nacional">Primera B Nacional</a> (the second division since 1986). The club's management decided to keep Miguel Ángel Russo as manager, regardless of relegation.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_resurrection:_1990s">The resurrection: 1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Lan%C3%BAs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: The resurrection: 1990s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lanús returned to the top division on 24 May 1992, when more than 30,000 supporters saw how Lanús beat <a href="/wiki/Deportivo_Maip%C3%BA" title="Deportivo Maipú">Deportivo Maipú</a> 2–0 winning the 1991–92 Primera B Nacional title. The line-up for the final was Ojeda, Gómez, Agüero, Mainardi, González; <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9ctor_Enrique" title="Héctor Enrique">H. Enrique</a>, Kuzemka, <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Sch%C3%BCrrer" title="Gabriel Schürrer">Schurrer</a>, Angelello; Gambier, Villagrán. Russo was the coach.</p>
<p>The good campaign of the team during the 1993 Apertura allowed it to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/Copa_CONMEBOL" title="Copa CONMEBOL">Copa CONMEBOL</a>, where Lanús participated for the first time in an international tournament. The team was eventually eliminated by <a href="/wiki/San_Lorenzo_de_Almagro" title="San Lorenzo de Almagro">San Lorenzo</a> in the quarter-finals. On 1 October, <a href="/wiki/Ariel_Ibagaza" title="Ariel Ibagaza">Ariel Ibagaza</a> made his debut for the team, where he and <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Morales" title="Hugo Morales">Hugo Morales</a> formed a midfield duo highly praised by Lanús' fans.</p>
<p>In 1995 <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9ctor_C%C3%BAper" title="Héctor Cúper">Héctor Cúper</a> took charge of the team at the start of the Apertura. Lanús finished 3rd by goal difference and 2nd in points. The next year turned out to be one of the club's most important. Lanús brought in <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Enr%C3%ADa" title="Claudio Enría">Claudio Enría</a> from Newell's and <a href="/wiki/Gonzalo_Belloso" title="Gonzalo Belloso">Gonzalo Belloso</a>. In the Clausura, Lanús was placed 1st with 3 fixtures remaining, but couldn't maintain the spot and eventually finished 3rd. In the second spell of the year, the club brought in Oscar Mena, Gustavo Falaschi and <a href="/wiki/Gustavo_Siviero" title="Gustavo Siviero">Gustavo Siviero</a>. Lanús had to face two tournaments at once for the first time in their history, the local tournament and the Copa CONMEBOL. In the local tournament, they finished 3rd once again.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_success">International success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Lan%C3%BAs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: International success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1996 Lanús won the <a href="/wiki/Copa_CONMEBOL" title="Copa CONMEBOL">Copa CONMEBOL</a>, a tournament created in 1992. The team eliminated <a href="/wiki/Club_Bol%C3%ADvar" title="Club Bolívar">Bolívar</a> in the first stage (4–1, 0–1), then passed <a href="/wiki/Club_Guaran%C3%AD" title="Club Guaraní">Guaraní</a> (2–0, 6–2). In semi-finals defeated <a href="/wiki/Rosario_Central" title="Rosario Central">Rosario Central</a> (3–0, 3–1) reaching the finals against <a href="/wiki/Independiente_Santa_Fe" title="Independiente Santa Fe">Independiente Santa Fe</a> of <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>. In the first leg, Lanús won 2–0. In the second leg, Lanús lost 1–0, resulting in an aggregate score of 2–1, which made Lanús champion. It was Lanús' first major and international title. The line-up for the final match was: <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Roa" title="Carlos Roa">Roa</a>; <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Serrizuela" title="José Serrizuela">Serrizuela</a>, Falaschi, <a href="/wiki/Gustavo_Siviero" title="Gustavo Siviero">Siviero</a>, Bresen; Mena, Cravero, <a href="/wiki/Ariel_Ibagaza" title="Ariel Ibagaza">Ibagaza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Walter_Coyette" title="Walter Coyette">Coyette</a>; Enría, <a href="/wiki/Ariel_L%C3%B3pez" title="Ariel López">Ariel López</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Lanús also reached the cup final in 1997 but was defeated by Brazilian team <a href="/wiki/Clube_Atl%C3%A9tico_Mineiro" title="Clube Atlético Mineiro">Atlético Mineiro</a> (1–4, 1–1).</p>
<p>In 1998, with <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Carlos_Mario_G%C3%B3mez" title="Roberto Carlos Mario Gómez">Mario Gómez</a> as manager, Lanús finished 2nd once more with 40 points behind Vélez Sarsfield, which to date being the club's best campaign in terms of points. Four years later Lanús had to play a relegation playoff versus <a href="/wiki/Hurac%C3%A1n_de_Tres_Arroyos" title="Huracán de Tres Arroyos">Huracán de Tres Arroyos</a>, winning 2–1 in Platense's stadium and drawing 1–1 in their stadium. With an aggregate score of 3–2 in favor, Lanús remained in Primera División.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s:_First_title_in_Primera">2000s: First title in Primera</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Lan%C3%BAs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2000s: First title in Primera">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2003 the stadium repairs were finished. Three years later, with a team based from its youth divisions, Lanús finished runner-up of Torneo Clausura. It was the 3rd. time a team finished 3rd. in club's history. In 2007 the club got qualification for the 2nd time in a row to the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Sudamericana" title="Copa Sudamericana">Copa Sudamericana</a> and -for the first time in club's history- to the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Libertadores" title="Copa Libertadores">Copa Libertadores</a>.</p>
<p>Lanús also won its first Primera División title, the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n_season" title="2007–08 Argentine Primera División season">2007 Apertura</a>, being coached by Ramón Cabrero. Lanús celebrated in the 18th fixture with an 1–1 tie to <a href="/wiki/Boca_Juniors" title="Boca Juniors">Boca Juniors</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Estadio_Alberto_J._Armando" title="Estadio Alberto J. Armando">La Bombonera</a>. The line-up for that match was: <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Bossio" title="Carlos Bossio">Bossio</a>; Graieb, Ribonetto, Hoyos, Velázquez; Blanco, Pelletieri, Fritzler, <a href="/wiki/Diego_Valeri" title="Diego Valeri">Valeri</a>; Acosta, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Sand" title="José Sand">Sand</a>. The squad totalized 38 points in 19 matches, with Sand as the topscorer of the tournament with 15 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>The next year Lanús participated in the <a href="/wiki/2008_Copa_Libertadores" title="2008 Copa Libertadores">2008 Copa Libertadores</a>, finishing unbeaten in the first stage. In the second round, Lanús was eliminated by Mexican <a href="/wiki/Club_Atlas" title="Club Atlas">Atlas</a>. In domestic competitions, Lanús once again finished sub-champions of <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n_season" title="2010–11 Argentine Primera División season">2011 Clausura</a>, behind Vélez Sarsfield.</p>
<p>Lanús qualified to play the <a href="/wiki/2012_Copa_Libertadores" title="2012 Copa Libertadores">2012 Copa Libertadores</a>, where the squad got its biggest win in international competitions in history (6–0 against Paraguayan <a href="/wiki/Club_Olimpia" title="Club Olimpia">Olimpia</a> in La Fortaleza).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The team led by <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Sch%C3%BCrrer" title="Gabriel Schürrer">Gabriel Schürrer</a> won its group and reached the round of 16, where it was eliminated by Vasco Da Gama in <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shoot-out_(association_football)" title="Penalty shoot-out (association football)">penalty shootout</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_international_title">Second international title</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Lan%C3%BAs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Second international title">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, Lanús obtained its second international title, the <a href="/wiki/2013_Copa_Sudamericana" title="2013 Copa Sudamericana">Copa Sudamericana</a> after beating Brazilian club <a href="/wiki/Associa%C3%A7%C3%A3o_Atl%C3%A9tica_Ponte_Preta" title="Associação Atlética Ponte Preta">Ponte Preta</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2013_Copa_Sudamericana_Finals" title="2013 Copa Sudamericana Finals">finals</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
