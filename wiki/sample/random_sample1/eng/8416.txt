<p><i><b>The Edge</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Край</span>, <a href="/wiki/Transliteration" title="Transliteration">translit.</a> Kray) is a 2010 Russian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_film" title="Drama film">drama film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Alexei_Uchitel" title="Alexei Uchitel">Alexei Uchitel</a>. The film was nominated for the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Foreign_Language_Film" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Foreign Language Film">Golden Globe Award for Best Foreign Language Film</a> and was also selected as the Russian entry for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Foreign_Language_Film" title="Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film">Best Foreign Language Film</a> at the <a href="/wiki/83rd_Academy_Awards" title="83rd Academy Awards">83rd Academy Awards</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Russia_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Russia-1">[1]</a></sup> but it didn't make the final shortlist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oscar_Shortlist_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Oscar_Shortlist-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Samuel_Goldwyn_Films" title="Samuel Goldwyn Films">Samuel Goldwyn Films</a> has acquired all U.S. rights to <i>The Edge</i> which was negotiated with Sergei Bespalov, president of Sunrise Films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
