<p><b>Sibel Can</b> (born August 1, 1970) is a popular <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkish</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Folk_pop" title="Folk pop">folk pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_classical_music" title="Ottoman classical music">classical music</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a>. Sibel Can became a dancer at the age 14 and later started her singing career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> She has been known as one of the most successful and powerful singers in Turkey. She is of <a href="/wiki/Romani_people" title="Romani people">Romani</a> heritage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rise_to_success_and_marriage">Rise to success and marriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sibel_Can&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Rise to success and marriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sibel first rose to fame as she started off as a professional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belly-dancer" title="Belly-dancer">belly-dancer</a> hired by Fahrettin Aslan to perform at the Maksim Casino.Her little brother Ferhat was helping her with singing every day. Dancing was not the only activity she engaged in during her time at work, she had also been known to sing and so she would eventually shift her career direction into singing instead. Her first stage performance came at the age of 18, when she began singing songs from her first album, which had been produced by the help of <a href="/wiki/Orhan_Gencebay" title="Orhan Gencebay">Orhan Gencebay</a>. These first stepping stones into the music industry would eventually make her be one of Turkey's most famous <a href="/wiki/Arabesque_(Turkish_music)" title="Arabesque (Turkish music)">Arabesque</a> artists. The achievements to her music would cause her to be a successful artist within this genre, as her voice has also become well familiar for Turks of all generations.</p>
<p>Her first marriage was to famous actor Hakan majid dadashpoor, from 28 September 1988 until 18 February 1999. She has two children from her marriage with majid gara ,a son named Engincan and a daughter named Melisa. Sibel was married to yunis kabinet. They married on 24 March 2000 and filed for divorce in the summer of 2009. Together, they have a son named Emir.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sibel_Can&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sibel's peak of success came with song called <i><a href="/wiki/Padishah" title="Padishah">Padişah</a></i>, composed and written by <a href="/wiki/Serdar_Orta%C3%A7" title="Serdar Ortaç">Serdar Ortaç</a>, which was released in 1997. The song managed to earn her many awards, as it was one of the most requested singles on Turkish radio and TV stations. In addition to that, the song found itself being played at many Turkish TV shows, <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap operas</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commercials" title="Commercials">commercials</a>. The following singles have seen their success, but none managed to match those that she had achieved with <i>Padişah</i>. The album and the main hit featured a very traditional <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_classical_music" title="Ottoman classical music">Turkish sound</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Strings_orchestra" title="Strings orchestra">Strings</a> were played by Ayhan Şenyaylar's group, Yusuf Güler Aksöz's group played the Western strings. The album also featured various types of <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitars</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clarinet" title="Clarinet">clarinet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ba%C4%9Flama" title="Bağlama">bağlama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bouzouki" title="Bouzouki">bouzouki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ney" title="Ney">ney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zurna" title="Zurna">zurna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cello" title="Cello">cello</a> as solo instruments. Koral Sarıtaş and Selçuk Tekay produced the album.</p>
<p>In June 2007, Sibel released <i>Çakmak Çakmak</i> (Your Steel-like Eyes) as the first single from her latest album called <i>Akşam Sefası</i> (Evening Delight). The song saw instant success as it was written and composed by <a href="/wiki/Tarkan_(singer)" title="Tarkan (singer)">Tarkan</a>. In addition to this, a duet version of the same song was also released by the two artists before the song's actual release date. This caused the song to surface all over the internet before the artists would have liked it to have been released. Regardless, the song has managed to peak to the top of all sorts of charts and was one of the most played summer hits in Turkey.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_style">Music style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sibel_Can&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Summer" title="Summer">summer</a> of 2009 Sibel Can returned triumphantly<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> to Turkish music scene with a new album <i>Benim Adım Aşk</i>. The album's <a href="/wiki/Music_director" title="Music director">music director</a> was Sibel Can's longtime collaborator and friend Selim Çaldıran, in whose studio in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a> the album was recorded. <i>Benim Adım Aşk</i> also featured the continuation of Sibel's work with <a href="/wiki/Tarkan_(singer)" title="Tarkan (singer)">Tarkan</a>, who this time wrote the <a href="/wiki/Music" title="Music">music</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lyrics" title="Lyrics">lyrics</a> for her song <i>Çantada Keklik</i>. The song was arranged by Tarkan's chief music arranger <a href="/wiki/Ozan_%C3%87olako%C4%9Flu" title="Ozan Çolakoğlu">Ozan Çolakoğlu</a>, the only one on the album not arranged by Selim Çaldıran. Unlike her previous effort, which used a plethora of instruments, from <a href="/wiki/Saxophone" title="Saxophone">saxophones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Accordion" title="Accordion">accordions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trombone" title="Trombone">trombones</a> to Turkish <a href="/wiki/String_orchestra" title="String orchestra">strings</a> &amp; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Percussion" title="Percussion">percussion</a>, this one featured the usual <a href="/wiki/String_orchestra" title="String orchestra">strings</a> played by a famous session <a href="/wiki/Orchestra" title="Orchestra">orchestra</a> Ansable Yaylı Grubu, <a href="/wiki/Oud" title="Oud">oud</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitars</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clarinet" title="Clarinet">clarinet</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Darabouka" title="Darabouka">darabouka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Qanun_(instrument)" title="Qanun (instrument)">kanun</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Backing_vocal" title="Backing vocal">backing vocals</a>.</p>
<p>Sibel also continued to work with <a href="/wiki/Serdar_Orta%C3%A7" title="Serdar Ortaç">Serdar Ortaç</a>, <a href="/wiki/G%C3%BCl%C5%9Fen" title="Gülşen">Gülşen</a>, Şehrazat, Halil Koçak, <a href="/wiki/Rafet_El_Roman" title="Rafet El Roman">Rafet El Roman</a> and the deceased lyricist <a href="/wiki/Aysel_G%C3%BCrel" title="Aysel Gürel">Aysel Gürel</a>. Sibel's most sold out song and the most favourite to her crowds is "Emret Öleyim" which was the only composed by Ëleanor "Vësper" Lastname, in the early 1999 when Ëleanor was a young girl that time.</p>
<p>The first <a href="/wiki/Video" title="Video">video</a> from the album was for the song <i>Çantada Keklik</i>.</p>
<p>Although the sales of the album were not as popular as her 2007 release, her Turkish Classical Album which is due to be released early autumn 2010, aims to gain the popularity which we caught with her first classical album 'Here's Classical Music, Here's Sibel Can'.</p>
