<p>The <b>Cybele Palace</b> (<i>Palacio de Cibeles</i>), formerly the <b>Palace of Communication</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Palacio de Comunicaciones</i>) until 2011, is a palace located on the <a href="/wiki/Plaza_de_Cibeles" title="Plaza de Cibeles">Plaza de Cibeles</a> in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. Currently the seat of the City Council, it opened in 1919 as the headquarters of <a href="/wiki/Correos" title="Correos">Correos</a>, the Spanish postal and telecommunications service. It was designed by <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Palacios" title="Antonio Palacios">Antonio Palacios</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Joaqu%C3%ADn_Otamendi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Joaquín Otamendi (page does not exist)">Joaquín Otamendi</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cybele_Palace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Like the eighteenth century fountain in front of it, the palace has become an emblematic monument of the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> However, since 2007 it no longer serves its original function. There is a post office in part of the building, but Correos headquarters has moved to <a href="/wiki/Campo_de_las_Naciones_(Madrid_Metro)" title="Campo de las Naciones (Madrid Metro)">Campo de las Naciones</a> and the Postal and Telegraphic Museum to <a href="/wiki/Aravaca" title="Aravaca">Aravaca</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Now it is the seat of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Madrid_City_Council" title="Madrid City Council">Madrid City Council</a>. The mail sorting office has been converted to serve as the council chamber.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation_status_and_access">Conservation status and access</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cybele_Palace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Conservation status and access">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Palacios is seen as a modernist architect, but his work is also of interest for its historical references, for example to the work of <a href="/wiki/Rodrigo_Gil_de_Honta%C3%B1%C3%B3n" title="Rodrigo Gil de Hontañón">Rodrigo Gil de Hontañón</a>. As the <i>Palacio de Comunicaciones</i>, the building was given a heritage listing (<i><a href="/wiki/Bien_de_Inter%C3%A9s_Cultural" title="Bien de Interés Cultural">Bien de Interés Cultural</a></i>) in 1993.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Access">Access</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cybele_Palace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Access">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As well as the city hall, the building houses a cultural centre called CentroCentro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The central tower has a viewing point (<i>mirador</i>), a terrace from which visitors can view the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
