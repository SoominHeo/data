<p><b>Club Atlético Progreso</b>, also known simply as <b>Progreso</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Montevideo" title="Montevideo">Montevideo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a>. Progreso currently plays in the nation's <a href="/wiki/Uruguayan_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Uruguayan Segunda División">Segunda División</a>. The team's red and yellow striped <a href="/wiki/Kit_(association_football)" title="Kit (association football)">kit</a> emulates the <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalan</a> flag. Progreso's <a href="/wiki/Parque_Abraham_Paladino" title="Parque Abraham Paladino">Parque Abraham Paladino</a> stadium is capable of holding 8,000 spectators.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=C.A._Progreso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The club was founded on April 30, 1917. In 1989, Progreso won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n_Uruguaya" title="Primera División Uruguaya">Uruguayan Primera División</a>, the only championship in the history of the Uruguayan league to use a single <a href="/wiki/Round-robin_tournament" title="Round-robin tournament">round-robin</a> format (13 games). This format was due to a calendar conflict with some national and international cups that year. Progreso's president at that time was Dr. <a href="/wiki/Tabar%C3%A9_V%C3%A1zquez" title="Tabaré Vázquez">Tabaré Vázquez</a>, who was later the president of Uruguay from 2005 to 2010.</p>
<p>Progreso's first team kit in 1917 was white with black stripes. The kit expressed the team's affinity with the <a href="/wiki/Anarchism" title="Anarchism">anarchist movement</a>. The colors were later changed to red and yellow, the colors of Catalonia, which was known for its identification with the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Revolution_of_1936" title="Spanish Revolution of 1936">Spanish Revolution</a>.</p>
