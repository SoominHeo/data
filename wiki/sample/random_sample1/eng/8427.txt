<p><b>Cisnes</b> (Spanish for: "port <a href="/wiki/Black-necked_swan" title="Black-necked swan">swans</a>") is a <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chilean</a> <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_Chile" title="Communes of Chile">commune</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Ays%C3%A9n_Province" title="Aysén Province">Aysén Province</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ays%C3%A9n_del_General_Carlos_Ib%C3%A1%C3%B1ez_del_Campo_Region" title="Aysén del General Carlos Ibáñez del Campo Region">Aysén del General Carlos Ibáñez del Campo Region</a>. The commune spans an area of 15,831.4 km<sup>2</sup> (6,113 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-INE_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-INE-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cisnes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to data from the 2002 Census of Population and Housing, Cisnes had 5,353 inhabitants; of these, 2,507 (43.7%) lived in <a href="/wiki/Urban_area" title="Urban area">urban areas</a> and 3,232 (56.3%) in <a href="/wiki/Rural_area" title="Rural area">rural areas</a>. At that time, there were 3,414 men and 2,325 women.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-INE_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-INE-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cisnes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a commune, Cisnes is a third-level <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_division_of_Chile" title="Administrative division of Chile">administrative division of Chile</a> administered by a <a href="/wiki/Municipal_council" title="Municipal council">municipal council</a>, headed by an <a href="/wiki/Alcalde" title="Alcalde">alcalde</a> who is directly elected every four years. The 2008–2012 alcalde (mayor) is Luis Valdés Gutiérrez (<a href="/wiki/Socialist_Party_of_Chile" title="Socialist Party of Chile">PS</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Munitel_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Munitel-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Official_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Official-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Electoral_divisions_of_Chile" title="Electoral divisions of Chile">electoral divisions of Chile</a>, Cisnes is represented in the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_Chile" title="Chamber of Deputies of Chile">Chamber of Deputies</a> by René Alinco (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_Democratic_Party" title="Christian Democratic Party">PDC</a>) and David Sandoval (<a href="/wiki/Independent_Democratic_Union" title="Independent Democratic Union">UDI</a>) as part of the 59th electoral district, which includes the entire <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ays%C3%A9n_del_General_Carlos_Ib%C3%A1%C3%B1ez_del_Campo_Region" title="Aysén del General Carlos Ibáñez del Campo Region">Aysén del General Carlos Ibáñez del Campo Region</a>. The commune is represented in the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Chile" title="Senate of Chile">Senate</a> by Antonio Horvath Kiss (<a href="/wiki/National_Renewal_(Chile)" title="National Renewal (Chile)">RN</a>) and Patricio Walker Prieto (PDC) as part of the 18th senatorial constituency (Aysén del General Carlos Ibáñez del Campo Region).</p>
