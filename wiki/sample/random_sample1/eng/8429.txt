<p><b>Sistan and Baluchestan Province</b> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="fa" xml:lang="fa">استان سيستان و بلوچستان</span>‎‎, <i>Ostān-e Sīstān-o Balūchestān</i>) is one of the 31 <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Iran" title="Provinces of Iran">provinces of Iran</a>. It is in the southeast of the country, bordering <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a> and its capital is <a href="/wiki/Zahedan" title="Zahedan">Zahedan</a>.</p>
<p>The province is the largest in Iran, with an area of 181,785 km² and a population of 2.5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The counties of the province are <a href="/wiki/Chabahar_County" title="Chabahar County">Chabahar</a>, Qasar-qand, <a href="/wiki/Dalgan_County" title="Dalgan County">Dalgan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hirmand_County" title="Hirmand County">Hirmand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iranshahr_County" title="Iranshahr County">Iranshahr</a>, <a href="/wiki/Khash_County" title="Khash County">Khash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Konarak_County" title="Konarak County">Konarak</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nikshahr_County" title="Nikshahr County">Nikshahr</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saravan_County" title="Saravan County">Saravan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sarbaz_County" title="Sarbaz County">Sarbaz</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sib_o_Soran_County" title="Sib o Soran County">Soran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zabol_County" title="Zabol County">Zabol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mehrestan_County" title="Mehrestan County">Zaboli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zahedan_County" title="Zahedan County">Zahedan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zehak_County" title="Zehak County">Zehak</a>.</p>
<p>The population comprises the <a href="/wiki/Baloch_people" title="Baloch people">Baluch</a> who form a majority in the province, followed by the relatively large minority, the Sistani <a href="/wiki/Persian_people" title="Persian people">Persians</a>. Smaller communities of <a href="/wiki/Kurds" title="Kurds">Kurds</a> (in the eastern highlands and near <a href="/wiki/Iranshahr_(city)" title="Iranshahr (city)">Iranshahr</a>), the expatriate <a href="/wiki/Brahui_people" title="Brahui people">Brahui</a> (on the borders between Iran and Pakistan), and other resident and itinerant ethnic groups such as the <a href="/wiki/Romani_people" title="Romani people">Gypsies</a> are also found in the province.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_culture">Geography and culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sistan_and_Baluchestan_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography and culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The province comprises two sections, <i>Sistan</i> in the north and <i>Baluchestan</i> in the south. The combined Sistan and Baluchestan province today accounts for one of the driest regions of <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> with a slight increase in rainfall from east to west, and an obvious rise in humidity in the coastal regions. The province is subject to seasonal winds from different directions, the most important of which are the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/120-day_wind" title="120-day wind">120-day wind</a> of Sistan known as <i>Levar</i>, the <i>Qousse</i> wind, the seventh (<i>Gav-kosh</i>) wind, the <i>Nambi</i> or south wind, the <i>Hooshak</i> wind, the humid and seasonal winds of the Indian Ocean, the North or (<i>Gurich</i>) wind and the western (<i>Gard</i>) wind.</p>

<p>In the south, east and west of Sistān and Balūchestān, the people are mostly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baluch_people" title="Baluch people">Balōch</a> and speak the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baluchi_language" title="Baluchi language">Baluchi language</a>. In the far north of Sistān and Balūchestān, the people are mostly <a href="/wiki/Persian_people" title="Persian people">Persians</a> and speak a dialect of the <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian language</a> known as Sistani/Seestani, similar to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dari_Persian" title="Dari Persian">Dari Persian</a> language in Afghanistan. The name <i>Balūchestān</i> means "Land of the <a href="/wiki/Baloch_people" title="Baloch people">Balōch</a>" and is used to represent the majority Baloch peoples inhabiting the province, Sistan was added to the name to represent the minority Persian peoples who speak the Sistani dialect of Persian.</p>
<p>Many scholars, orators, and literary personalities have sprung up from this part of Iran, amongst which are <a href="/wiki/Farrukhi_Sistani" title="Farrukhi Sistani">Farrukhi Sistani</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ya%27qub_bin_Laith_as-Saffar" title="Ya'qub bin Laith as-Saffar">Ya'qub bin Laith as-Saffar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rostam" title="Rostam">Rostam</a>. <a href="/wiki/Ali_al-Sistani" title="Ali al-Sistani">Ayatollah Sistani</a> is also from Sistān; though he currently resides in <a href="/wiki/Najaf" title="Najaf">Najaf</a>, Iraq .</p>

Statue of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ya%27qub-i_Laith_Saffari" title="Ya'qub-i Laith Saffari">Ya'qub-i Laith Saffari</a> in <a href="/wiki/Zabol" title="Zabol">Zabol</a></div>

<p>In the epigraphs of Bistoon and <a href="/wiki/Persepolis" title="Persepolis">Persepolis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sistan" title="Sistan">Sistan</a> is mentioned as one of the eastern territories of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Darius_the_Great" title="Darius the Great">Darius the Great</a>. The name Sistan, as mentioned above, is derived from <a href="/wiki/Saka" title="Saka">Saka</a> (also sometimes <i>Saga</i>, or <i><a href="/wiki/Sakastan" title="Sakastan">Sagastan</a></i>), one of the <a href="/wiki/Aryan" title="Aryan">Aryan</a> tribes that had taken control over this area in the year 128 BCE. During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arsacid_Empire" title="Arsacid Empire">Arsacid Dynasty</a> (248 BC to 224 CE), the province became the seat of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suren-Pahlav_Clan" title="Suren-Pahlav Clan">Suren-Pahlav Clan</a>. From the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sassanid" title="Sassanid">Sassanid</a> period till the early Islamic period, Sistan flourished considerably.</p>
<p>During the reign of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ardashir_I_of_Persia" title="Ardashir I of Persia">Ardashir I of Persia</a>, Sistan came under the jurisdiction of the Sassanids, and in 644CE, the <a href="/wiki/Arab_Muslims" title="Arab Muslims">Arab Muslims</a> gained control as the Persian empire was in its final moments of collapsing.</p>
<p>During the reign of the second caliph of <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>,Omar ibn Al-Khattab, this territory was conquered by the Arabs and an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arab" title="Arab">Arab</a> commander was assigned as governor. The famous <a href="/wiki/Persian_people" title="Persian people">Persian</a> ruler <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ya%27qub-i_Laith_Saffari" title="Ya'qub-i Laith Saffari">Ya'qub-i Laith Saffari</a>, whose descendants dominated this area for many centuries, later became governor of this province. In 916 CE, Baluchestan was ruled by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buwayhid" title="Buwayhid">Daylamids</a> and thereafter the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seljuk_Turks" title="Seljuk Turks">Seljuqids</a>, when it became a part of <a href="/wiki/Kerman_Province" title="Kerman Province">Kerman</a>. Dynasties such as the <a href="/wiki/Saffarid_dynasty" title="Saffarid dynasty">Saffarids</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samanid" title="Samanid">Samanids</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ghaznavids" title="Ghaznavids">Qaznavids</a>, and Seljuqids, also ruled over this territory.</p>
<p>In 1508 CE, Shah <a href="/wiki/Ismail_I" title="Ismail I">Ismail I</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Safavids" title="Safavids">Safavid</a> dynasty conquered Sistan, and during the reign of <a href="/wiki/Nader_Shah" title="Nader Shah">Nader Shah</a> there was further turmoil.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sistan_and_Baluchestan_today">Sistan and Baluchestan today</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sistan_and_Baluchestan_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sistan and Baluchestan today">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Chabahar_coast.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="366" data-file-width="640" height="126" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Chabahar_coast.jpg/220px-Chabahar_coast.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Chabahar_coast.jpg/330px-Chabahar_coast.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Chabahar_coast.jpg/440px-Chabahar_coast.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The province today is the most underdeveloped, desolate, and poorest of Iran's provinces. The government of Iran has been trying to reverse this situation by implementing new plans such as creating the <a href="/wiki/Chabahar_Free_Trade-Industrial_Zone" title="Chabahar Free Trade-Industrial Zone">Chabahar Free Trade-Industrial Zone</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Road_transport">Road transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sistan_and_Baluchestan_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Road transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="National_rail_network">National rail network</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sistan_and_Baluchestan_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: National rail network">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The city of <a href="/wiki/Zahedan" title="Zahedan">Zahedan</a> has been connected to <a href="/wiki/Quetta" title="Quetta">Quetta</a> in Pakistan for a century with a broad gauge railway. It has weekly trains for <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kovaitah&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kovaitah (page does not exist)">Kovaitah</a>. Recently a railway from <a href="/wiki/Bam,_Iran" title="Bam, Iran">Bam, Iran</a> to Zahedan has been inaugurated. There may be plans to build railway lines from Zahedan to <a href="/wiki/Chabahar" title="Chabahar">Chabahar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ports">Ports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sistan_and_Baluchestan_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Ports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Port_of_Chabahar" title="Port of Chabahar">Port of Chabahar</a> in South of province is the main port to be connected by a new railway to Zahedan in future.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry_and_mining">Industry and mining</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sistan_and_Baluchestan_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Industry and mining">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Industry is new to the province. Efforts have been done and tax, customs and financial motivations have caused more industrial investment, new projects, new producing jobs and improvement of industry.</p>
<p>The most important factories are: – Khash cement factory with production of 2600 tons cement daily and three other cement.</p>
<p>Factories under construction: – Cotton cloth and fishing net weaving factories and the brick factory can be named as well.</p>
<p>The province has important geological and metal mineral potentials such as chrome, copper, granite, antimony, talc, manganese, iron, lead, zinc, tin, nickel, platinum, gold and silver.</p>
<p>One of the main mines in this province is Chel Kooreh copper mine in 120 km north of Zahedan.</p>
