<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Shiiya Domain</h1>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Shiiya Domain <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">椎谷藩</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shiiya-han</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></b> was a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fudai" title="Fudai">fudai</a></i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Han_(Japan)" title="Han (Japan)">feudal domain</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a> of <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a> <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It is located in <a href="/wiki/Echigo_Province" title="Echigo Province">Echigo Province</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honsh%C5%AB" title="Honshū">Honshū</a>. The domain was centered at <a href="/wiki/Jin%27ya" title="Jin'ya">Shiiya Jin'ya</a>, located in what is now part of the city of <a href="/wiki/Kashiwazaki,_Niigata" title="Kashiwazaki, Niigata">Kashiwazaki</a> in <a href="/wiki/Niigata_Prefecture" title="Niigata Prefecture">Niigata Prefecture</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shiiya_Domain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shiiya Domain began as a 5,500 <i>koku</i> holding awarded to Hori Naoyuki, the 4th son of the famous general Hori Naomasa, for his role in the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Osaka" title="Siege of Osaka">Siege of Osaka</a>. Naoyuki's son Hori Naokage, served as <a href="/wiki/Machi-bugy%C5%8D" title="Machi-bugyō">Edo bugyō</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jisha-bugy%C5%8D" title="Jisha-bugyō">Jisha-bugyō</a>, and amassed holdings valued at 9,500 <i>koku</i>, to which he added an additional 2,000 <i>koku</i> of newly developed rice lands to qualify for the status of <a href="/wiki/Daimy%C5%8D" title="Daimyō">daimyō</a>. He established his seat in <a href="/wiki/Kazusa_Province" title="Kazusa Province">Kazusa Province</a> at Kazusa-Kariya Domain (1642-1668), which is son Hori Naoyoshi moved to Kazusa-Hachiman Domain (1668-1698). Tori Naoyoshi's son Hori Naosada then moved the clan's seat to Echigo Province in 1698, which marked the creation of Shiiya Domain. The clan headquarters remained at Shiiya until the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meiji_restoration" title="Meiji restoration">Meiji restoration</a>; however, the daimyō remained in permanent residence in <a href="/wiki/Edo" title="Edo">Edo</a> and managed the domain as absentee landlords.</p>
<p>During the period of the 8th daimyo, Hori Akitomo, fiscal reforms were implemented; however, Akitomo was of weak constitution and was unable to see the reforms through. The domain was also hit hard by the <a href="/wiki/Great_Tenmei_famine" title="Great Tenmei famine">Great Tenmei famine</a>, which resulted in considerable peasant unrest.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Boshin_War" title="Boshin War">Boshin War</a>, the domain was a battleground in <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hokuetsu" title="Battle of Hokuetsu">Battle of Hokuetsu</a>. In July 1871, with the <a href="/wiki/Abolition_of_the_han_system" title="Abolition of the han system">abolition of the han system</a>, Shiiya Domain briefly became Shiiya Prefecture, and was merged into the newly created <a href="/wiki/Niigata_Prefecture" title="Niigata Prefecture">Niigata Prefecture</a>. Under the new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meiji_government" title="Meiji government">Meiji government</a>, Hori Yukiyoshi, the final daimyo of Shiiya Domain was given the <i><a href="/wiki/Kazoku" title="Kazoku">kazoku</a></i> peerage title of <i>danshaku</i> (<a href="/wiki/Baron" title="Baron">baron</a>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bakumatsu_period_holdings">Bakumatsu period holdings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shiiya_Domain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Bakumatsu period holdings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As with most domains in the <a href="/wiki/Han_system" title="Han system">han system</a>, Shiiya Domain consisted of several discontinuous territories calculated to provide the assigned <i><a href="/wiki/Kokudaka" title="Kokudaka">kokudaka</a></i>, based on periodic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cadastral" title="Cadastral">cadastral</a> surveys and projected agricultural yields.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
