<p>The <b>Republic of Siena</b> (<a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <span lang="it" xml:lang="it"><i>Repubblica di Siena</i></span>) was a historic state consisting of the city of <a href="/wiki/Siena" title="Siena">Siena</a> and its surrounding territory in <a href="/wiki/Tuscany" title="Tuscany">Tuscany</a>, central Italy. It existed for over four hundred years, from 1125 to 1555. In the <a href="/wiki/Italian_War_of_1551%E2%80%9359" title="Italian War of 1551–59">Italian War of 1551–59</a> the republic was defeated by the rival <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Florence" title="Duchy of Florence">Duchy of Florence</a> in alliance with the Spanish crown. After 18 months of resistance, Republic of Siena surrendered to the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a> on 21 April 1555, marking the end of the republic.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Siena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Siena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The oldest <a href="/wiki/Aristocracy_(class)" title="Aristocracy (class)">aristocratic</a> families in Siena date their line to the Lombards' surrender in 774 to <a href="/wiki/Charlemagne" title="Charlemagne">Charlemagne</a>. At this point, the city was inundated with a swarm of <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">Frankish</a> overseers who married into the existing Sienese nobility and left a legacy that can be seen in the abbeys they founded throughout Sienese territory. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feudal" title="Feudal">Feudal</a> power waned however, and by the death of <a href="/wiki/Matilda_of_Tuscany" title="Matilda of Tuscany">Countess Matilda</a> in 1115 the border territory of the <a href="/wiki/March_of_Tuscany" title="March of Tuscany">Mark of Tuscia</a> which had been under the control of her family, the Canossa, broke up into several autonomous regions.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Growth">Growth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Siena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Growth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Denaro_siena.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="244" data-file-width="529" height="101" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Denaro_siena.jpg/220px-Denaro_siena.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Denaro_siena.jpg/330px-Denaro_siena.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Denaro_siena.jpg/440px-Denaro_siena.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Denaro_siena.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Medieval coin of the Republic of Siena (12th century)</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Siena prospered as a <a href="/wiki/City-state" title="City-state">city-state</a>, becoming a major centre of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Money_lending" title="Money lending">money lending</a> and an important player in the wool trade. It was governed at first directly by its bishop, but episcopal power declined during the 12th century. The bishop was forced to concede a greater say in the running of the city to the nobility in exchange for their help during a territorial dispute with <a href="/wiki/Arezzo" title="Arezzo">Arezzo</a>, and this started a process which culminated in 1167 when the commune of Siena declared its independence from episcopal control. By 1179, it had a written constitution.</p>
<p>In 1286 the Nova government was established to rule Siena. The Nova was backed by the <a href="/wiki/Noveschi" title="Noveschi">Noveschi</a>, a political party formed by the noble families that sat on the council. Eventually, the Noveschi Party grew to include not only include members of the Nova council, but many prominent noble families of the city. Under the guide of the Nova and the Noveschi, Siena grew in both economic and militaristic dominance.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Battle_of_Montaperti">The Battle of Montaperti</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Siena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Battle of Montaperti">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

</div>
<p>In the 13th century, Siena was predominantly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ghibelline" title="Ghibelline">Ghibelline</a>, in opposition to Florence's <a href="/wiki/Guelphs_and_Ghibellines" title="Guelphs and Ghibellines">Guelph</a> position (this conflict formed the backdrop for some of <a href="/wiki/Dante_Alighieri" title="Dante Alighieri">Dante Alighieri</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Divine_Comedy" title="Divine Comedy">Divine Comedy</a></i>, completed in 1320).</p>
<p>On 4 September 1260 the Sienese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ghibellines" title="Ghibellines">Ghibellines</a>, supported by the forces of King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manfred_of_Sicily" title="Manfred of Sicily">Manfred of Sicily</a>, defeated the Florentine <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guelphs" title="Guelphs">Guelphs</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Montaperti" title="Battle of Montaperti">Battle of Montaperti</a>. Before the battle, the Sienese army of around 20,000 faced a much larger Florentine army of around 33,000. Prior to the battle, the entire city was dedicated to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Virgin_Mary" title="Virgin Mary">Virgin Mary</a> (this was done several times in the city's history, most recently in 1944 to guard the city from <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allied</a> bombs). The man given command of Siena for the duration of the war, Bonaguida Lucari, walked barefoot and bareheaded, a <a href="/wiki/Halter" title="Halter">halter</a> around his neck, to the <a href="/wiki/Siena_Cathedral" title="Siena Cathedral">Siena Cathedral</a> (<i>Duomo di Siena</i>). Leading a procession composed of all the city's residents, he was met by all the clergy. Lucari and the bishop embraced to show the unity of church and state, then Lucari formally gave the city and <a href="/wiki/Contrade_of_Siena" title="Contrade of Siena"><i>contrade</i></a> to the Virgin. Legend has it that a thick white cloud descended on the battlefield, giving the Sienese cover and aiding their attack. The reality was that the Florentine army launched several fruitless attacks against the Sienese army during the day, then when the Sienese army countered with their own offensive, traitors within the Florentine army killed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Standard_bearer" title="Standard bearer">standard bearer</a> and in the resulting chaos, the Florentine army broke up and fled the battlefield. Almost half the Florentine army (some 15,000 men) were killed as a result. So crushing was the defeat that even today if the two cities meet in any sporting event, the Sienese supporters are likely to exhort their Florentine counterparts to "Remember Montaperti!".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Decline">Decline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Siena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Decline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Siena was devastated by the <a href="/wiki/Black_Death" title="Black Death">Black Death</a> of 1348, and also suffered from ill-fated financial enterprises. In 1355, with the arrival of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charles_IV_of_Luxembourg" title="Charles IV of Luxembourg">Charles IV of Luxembourg</a> in the city, the population rose and suppressed the government of the Nova council and expelled any family associated with the Noveschi Party. They established a <i>Dodici</i> (council of 12 nobles), assisted by a council with a popular majority. This form of government was also short-lived, soon replaced by the <i>Quindici</i> ("The Fifteen") reformers in 1385, the <i>Dieci</i> ("The Ten", 1386–1387), <i>Undici</i> ("The Eleven", 1388–1398) and the Twelve Priors (1398–1399) who, in the end, gave the city's lordship to <a href="/wiki/Gian_Galeazzo_Visconti" title="Gian Galeazzo Visconti">Gian Galeazzo Visconti</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duke_of_Milan" title="Duke of Milan">Duke of Milan</a>, in order to defend it from Florentine expansionism.</p>
<p>Only five years later, the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Visconti" title="House of Visconti">House of Visconti</a> was expelled in 1404, and a new government of Ten Priors was established, this time in alliance with Florence against King <a href="/wiki/Ladislaus_of_Naples" title="Ladislaus of Naples">Ladislaus of Naples</a>. With the election of the Sienese Enea Silvio Piccolomini of the prominent <a href="/wiki/Piccolomini" title="Piccolomini">Piccolomini</a> noble family as <a href="/wiki/Pope_Pius_II" title="Pope Pius II">Pope Pius II</a> in 1458, the nobles who had been expelled due to association with the Noveschi party were allowed to return to the city.</p>
<p>In 1472 the Siena magistrates founded a "<a href="/wiki/Mount_of_piety" title="Mount of piety">mount of piety</a>", the <a href="/wiki/Banca_Monte_dei_Paschi_di_Siena" title="Banca Monte dei Paschi di Siena">Banca Monte dei Paschi di Siena</a> - which would survive into the 21st Century and come to be considered "the world's oldest bank".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Petrucci_Era">Petrucci Era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Siena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Petrucci Era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Noveschi returned to the city under <a href="/wiki/Pandolfo_Petrucci" title="Pandolfo Petrucci">Pandolfo Petrucci</a> in 1487. Pandolfo slowly gathered many political offices and grew his influence until 1500, when with the execution of his father-in-law Niccolò Borghese for conspiracy to murder him, as well as with the support of Florence and of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alfonso_of_Calabria" title="Alfonso of Calabria">Alfonso of Calabria</a>, Pandolfo was able to seize complete control of the city. Though a tyrant, Pandolfo brought Siena back to prosperity, favoring arts and sciences, as well as improving its economy. Pandolfo was succeeded by his son <a href="/wiki/Borghese_Petrucci" title="Borghese Petrucci">Borghese Petrucci</a>. After only 4 years of rule, Borghese was ousted by his cousin, cardinal <a href="/wiki/Raffaello_Petrucci" title="Raffaello Petrucci">Raffaello Petrucci</a>, helped by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Leo_X" title="Pope Leo X">Pope Leo X</a>. In the wake of more responsibilities from the Church, Raffaello was forced to cede the city to his nephew, Franceso Petrucci, who only ruled for a year before being ousted by Pandolfo's youngest son, Fabio. Fabio was exiled in 1525 by the Sienese people, marking the end of the Petrucci era.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="End_of_the_Republic">End of the Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Siena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: End of the Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With the end of the Petrucci era internal strife resumed, with the popular faction ousting the Noveschi party supported by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Clement_VII" title="Pope Clement VII">Pope Clement VII</a>: the latter sent an army, but was defeated at Camollia in 1526. Emperor <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles V</a> took advantage of the chaotic situation to put a <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> garrison in Siena. The citizens expelled it in 1552, allying with <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>: this was unacceptable for Charles, who sent his general <a href="/wiki/Gian_Giacomo_Medici" title="Gian Giacomo Medici">Gian Giacomo Medici</a> to lay siege to it with a Florentine-Imperial army.</p>
<p>The Sienese government entrusted its defence to <a href="/wiki/Piero_Strozzi" title="Piero Strozzi">Piero Strozzi</a>. When the latter was defeated at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Marciano" title="Battle of Marciano">Battle of Marciano</a> in August 1554, any hope of relief was lost. After 18 months of resistance, Siena surrendered to Spain on 21 April 1555, marking the end of the Republic. The new Spanish King <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Spain" title="Philip II of Spain">Philip II of Spain</a>, owing huge sums to the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Medici" title="House of Medici">House of Medici</a>, ceded Siena's entire territory to the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Tuscany" title="Grand Duchy of Tuscany">Grand Duchy of Tuscany</a> (apart from a series of coastal fortress annexed to the <a href="/wiki/State_of_the_Presidi" title="State of the Presidi">State of the <i>Presidi</i></a>), to which it belonged for the next three centuries until the <a href="/wiki/Italian_unification" title="Italian unification">Italian unification</a> in the 19th century. An <a href="/wiki/Government_in_exile" title="Government in exile">exiled Sienese republican government</a> of 700 Sienese families in <a href="/wiki/Montalcino" title="Montalcino">Montalcino</a> did not concede defeat in the Battle of Marciano and continued to resist Tuscan rule until 1559.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Territory">Territory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Siena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Territory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Siena possessed most of the modern day provinces of <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Grosseto" title="Province of Grosseto">Grosseto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Siena" title="Province of Siena">Siena</a> in the region of <a href="/wiki/Tuscany" title="Tuscany">Tuscany</a> in central Italy, including a coastline on the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrhenian_Sea" title="Tyrrhenian Sea">Tyrrhenian Sea</a>. A notable exception is the hill town of <a href="/wiki/Montepulciano" title="Montepulciano">Montepulciano</a> 70 km southeast of Siena, which was claimed by the neighbouring <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Florence" title="Republic of Florence">Florence</a>, and was a constant source of hostility between the two city-states. In the mid-16th century the Republic's territory had an area of approximately 8,000 km<sup>2</sup> and a population of 80,000, including some 15,000 living in the town of Siena.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Siena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Art">Art</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Siena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Art">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Siena rivaled Florence in the arts throughout the 13th and 14th centuries: the important late medieval painter <a href="/wiki/Duccio" title="Duccio">Duccio</a> (1253–1319) was a Sienese, but worked across the peninsula. The mural titled "Allegory of Good Government" (<a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <span lang="it" xml:lang="it"><i>Allegoria del Buon Governo</i></span>) painted by <a href="/wiki/Ambrogio_Lorenzetti" title="Ambrogio Lorenzetti">Ambrogio Lorenzetti</a> in 1338–39 at the <a href="/wiki/Palazzo_Pubblico" title="Palazzo Pubblico">Palazzo Pubblico</a>, Siena's town hall, is considered a magnificent example of late-Medieval/early <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a> art as well as a representation of the utopia of urban society as conceived during that period.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture">Architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Siena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Architecture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the early 13th century that the majority of the construction of the <a href="/wiki/Siena_Cathedral" title="Siena Cathedral">Siena Cathedral</a> (Duomo) was completed. During the same period the <a href="/wiki/Piazza_del_Campo" title="Piazza del Campo">Piazza del Campo</a> grew in importance as the centre of secular life. New streets were constructed leading to it, and it served as the site of the market and the location of various sporting events (perhaps better thought of as riots, in the fashion of the Florentine football matches that are still practised to this day). A wall was constructed in 1194 at the current site of the <a href="/wiki/Palazzo_Pubblico" title="Palazzo Pubblico">Palazzo Pubblico</a> to stop soil erosion, an indication of how important the area was becoming as a civic space.</p>

<p>The limits on the Roman town were the earliest known walls to the city. During the 10th and 11th centuries, the town grew to the east and later to the north, in what is now the Camollia district. Walls were built to totally surround the city, and a second set was finished by the end of the 13th century. Much of these walls still exist today.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Siena_University">Siena University</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Siena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Siena University">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/University_of_Siena" title="University of Siena">University of Siena</a></div>
<p>On December 26, 1240, Ildebrandino Cacciaconti, the then <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Podest%C3%A0" title="Podestà">podestà</a> of Siena, signed a decree imposing a tax on citizens of Siena who rented rooms to students of the local "<i>Studium Senese</i>". The money from this tax went to pay for the salaries of the <i>maestri</i> (teachers) of the new <a href="/wiki/University_of_Siena" title="University of Siena">University of Siena</a>,.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unihis_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-unihis-2">[2]</a></sup> The studium was further supported when, in 1252, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Innocent_IV" title="Innocent IV">Pope Innocent IV</a> declared both its teachers and students immune from taxes and forced labour levied on their person or property by the city of Siena.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Moreover, the commune exempted teachers of law and Latin from their public duties. By the early 14th century, there were five teachers of Latin, logic and law and two doctors of natural sciences (medicine).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Nowadays, the university is still among the most important Italian universities.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Business">Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Siena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1472 the Republic founded the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monte_dei_paschi_di_siena" title="Monte dei paschi di siena">Monte dei Paschi</a>, a bank that is still active today and is the oldest surviving bank in the world.</p>
