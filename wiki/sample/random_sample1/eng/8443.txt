<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Sierra Leonean dollar</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>dollar</b> was a currency issued in <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Leone" title="Sierra Leone">Sierra Leone</a> between 1791 and 1805. It was subdivided into 100 <a href="/wiki/Cent_(currency)" title="Cent (currency)">cents</a> and was issued by the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Leone_Company" title="Sierra Leone Company">Sierra Leone Company</a>. The dollar was pegged to <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">sterling</a> at a rate of 1 dollar = 4 <a href="/wiki/Shilling" title="Shilling">shillings</a> 2 <a href="/wiki/Penny" title="Penny">pence</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coins">Coins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_Leonean_dollar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Coins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1791, coins were issued in denominations of 1, 10, 20, 50 cents, 1 penny and 1 dollar. The 1 cent and 1 penny were minted in bronze, the rest in silver. All the coins featured a <a href="/wiki/Lion" title="Lion">lion</a> on the obverse and two shaking hands, one white, one black, on the reverse.</p>
