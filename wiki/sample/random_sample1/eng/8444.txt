<p>The <b>coat of arms of <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Leone" title="Sierra Leone">Sierra Leone</a></b> (along with the flag), were developed by the <a href="/wiki/College_of_Arms" title="College of Arms">College of Arms</a> and granted in 1960.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coat_of_arms_of_Sierra_Leone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The shield on the arms depicts a lion beneath a zigzag border, representing the Lion Mountains, after which the country was named. It also shows three torches which are meant to symbolize peace and dignity. At the base are wavy bars depicting the sea.</p>
<p>The supporters of the shield are lions, similar to those on the colonial badge. The three main colours from the shield – green, white and blue – were used to form the flag.</p>
<p>The green represents agricultural and natural resources, the blue represents the Harbour of <a href="/wiki/Freetown" title="Freetown">Freetown</a> and the white represents unity and justice. At the bottom of the shield, the national motto can be seen.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Blazon">Blazon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coat_of_arms_of_Sierra_Leone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Blazon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Coat of Arms of Sierra Leone are described as:</p>
<p>(a) for arms-</p>
<ol>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vert_(heraldry)" title="Vert (heraldry)">Vert</a> a lion passant <a href="/wiki/Or_(heraldry)" title="Or (heraldry)">Or</a> armed and langued <a href="/wiki/Gules" title="Gules">Gules</a>.</li>
<li>A chief dancetée of three <a href="/wiki/Argent" title="Argent">Argent</a> with three <a href="/wiki/Torch" title="Torch">torches</a> <a href="/wiki/Sable" title="Sable">Sable</a> with flames <a href="/wiki/Gules" title="Gules">Gules</a>.</li>
<li>In base <a href="/wiki/Argent" title="Argent">Argent</a>, two wavy bars <a href="/wiki/Azure_(heraldry)" title="Azure (heraldry)">Azure</a>.</li>
</ol>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:80px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Badge_of_Sierra_Leone_(1889-1914).svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="600" height="78" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Badge_of_Sierra_Leone_%281889-1914%29.svg/78px-Badge_of_Sierra_Leone_%281889-1914%29.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Badge_of_Sierra_Leone_%281889-1914%29.svg/117px-Badge_of_Sierra_Leone_%281889-1914%29.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Badge_of_Sierra_Leone_%281889-1914%29.svg/156px-Badge_of_Sierra_Leone_%281889-1914%29.svg.png 2x" width="78"/></a>

Badge of Sierra Leone (1901–1914)</div>

<p>(b) for the supporters-</p>
<ol>
<li>Two lions rampant <a href="/wiki/Or_(heraldry)" title="Or (heraldry)">Or</a> armed and langued <a href="/wiki/Gules" title="Gules">Gules</a>, each supporting a coconut tree,</li>
<li>They stand on a grassy field proper,</li>
</ol>
<p>(c) with the motto "Unity, Freedom, Justice”.</p>
