<p><b>Siemowit</b> (Polish pronunciation: [ɕɛˈmɔvit], also <b>Ziemowit</b> [ʑɛˈmɔvit]) was, according to the chronicles of <a href="/wiki/Gallus_Anonymus" title="Gallus Anonymus">Gallus Anonymus</a>, the son of <a href="/wiki/Piast_the_Wheelwright" title="Piast the Wheelwright">Piast the Wheelwright</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rzepicha" title="Rzepicha">Rzepicha</a>. He is considered to be the first ruler of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Piast" title="Piast">Piast</a> dynasty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>He became the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duke_of_the_Polans" title="Duke of the Polans">Duke of the Polans</a> in the 9th century after his father, <a href="/wiki/Piast_the_Wheelwright" title="Piast the Wheelwright">Piast the Wheelwright</a>, son of <a href="/wiki/Cho%C5%9Bcisko" title="Chościsko">Chościsko</a>, refused to take the place of legendary Duke <a href="/wiki/Popiel" title="Popiel">Popiel</a>. Siemowit was elected as new duke by the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wiec" title="Wiec">wiec</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Roszko1980_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Roszko1980-2">[2]</a></sup> According to a popular legend, Popiel was then eaten by mice in his tower on the <a href="/wiki/Gop%C5%82o" title="Gopło">Gopło</a> lake.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marvels_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Marvels-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The only mention of Siemowit, along with his son, <a href="/wiki/Lestek" title="Lestek">Lestek</a>, and grandson, <a href="/wiki/Siemomys%C5%82" title="Siemomysł">Siemomysł</a>, comes in the medieval chronicle of Gallus Anonymus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gall_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gall-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Siemowit's great grandson, <a href="/wiki/Mieszko_I_of_Poland" title="Mieszko I of Poland">Mieszko I</a>, is considered the first ruler of Poland.</p>
