<p><b>Yasuhisa Shiozaki</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">塩崎 恭久</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shiozaki Yasuhisa</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, b. November 7, 1950)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> politician who served as <a href="/wiki/Chief_Cabinet_Secretary" title="Chief Cabinet Secretary">Chief Cabinet Secretary</a> to Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Shinz%C5%8D_Abe" title="Shinzō Abe">Shinzō Abe</a> until August 2007.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yasuhisa_Shiozaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Michael_Howard_William_Hague_and_Yasuhisa_Shiozaki_cropped_3_William_Hague_and_Members_of_the_UK-Japan_21st_Century_Group_20130502.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="387" data-file-width="453" height="171" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Michael_Howard_William_Hague_and_Yasuhisa_Shiozaki_cropped_3_William_Hague_and_Members_of_the_UK-Japan_21st_Century_Group_20130502.jpg/200px-Michael_Howard_William_Hague_and_Yasuhisa_Shiozaki_cropped_3_William_Hague_and_Members_of_the_UK-Japan_21st_Century_Group_20130502.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Michael_Howard_William_Hague_and_Yasuhisa_Shiozaki_cropped_3_William_Hague_and_Members_of_the_UK-Japan_21st_Century_Group_20130502.jpg/300px-Michael_Howard_William_Hague_and_Yasuhisa_Shiozaki_cropped_3_William_Hague_and_Members_of_the_UK-Japan_21st_Century_Group_20130502.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Michael_Howard_William_Hague_and_Yasuhisa_Shiozaki_cropped_3_William_Hague_and_Members_of_the_UK-Japan_21st_Century_Group_20130502.jpg/400px-Michael_Howard_William_Hague_and_Yasuhisa_Shiozaki_cropped_3_William_Hague_and_Members_of_the_UK-Japan_21st_Century_Group_20130502.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>

with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Howard" title="Michael Howard">Michael Howard</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_Hague" title="William Hague">William Hague</a>, <a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E4%BC%9A%E7%94%B0%E5%BC%98%E7%B6%99" title="ja:会田弘継">Hirotsugu Aida</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tim_Hitchens" title="Tim Hitchens">Tim Hitchens</a></div>

<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Matsuyama,_Ehime" title="Matsuyama, Ehime">Matsuyama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ehime_Prefecture" title="Ehime Prefecture">Ehime Prefecture</a>, he was an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Field_Service" title="American Field Service">AFS</a> exchange student in high school, graduated with a liberal arts degree from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Tokyo" title="University of Tokyo">University of Tokyo</a> and attended the <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_School_of_Government" title="John F. Kennedy School of Government">John F. Kennedy School of Government</a> at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>. He spent three years working at the Economic Planning Agency (of which his father, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jun_Shiozaki&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jun Shiozaki (page does not exist)">Jun Shiozaki</a>, was then director) and at the <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Japan" title="Bank of Japan">Bank of Japan</a>. He then worked as a secretary to his father.</p>
<p>Following his father's resignation, he ran for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diet_of_Japan" title="Diet of Japan">Diet</a> in 1993 and was elected to represent the first district of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ehime" title="Ehime">Ehime</a>. He served as Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs in the 2005 cabinet of <a href="/wiki/Junichiro_Koizumi" title="Junichiro Koizumi">Junichiro Koizumi</a> and was appointed Chief Cabinet Secretary on September 26, 2006,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and held the position until a cabinet shuffle at the end of August 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>His appointment as Minister of Health, Labor and Welfare in the second Abe administration was announced on September 3, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shiozaki's profile on the LDP website:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Bank of Japan</li>
<li>Parliamentary Vice－Minister of Finance (Hashimoto Cabinet)</li>
<li>Senior Vice-Minister for Foreign Affairs (Koizumi Cabinet)</li>
<li>Chief Cabinet Secretary (Abe Cabinet)</li>
<li>Minister of State for Abduction issue (AbeCabinet)</li>
<li>Acting Chairman, Policy Research Council</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Right-wing_positions">Right-wing positions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yasuhisa_Shiozaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Right-wing positions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Like most member of Shinzo Abe's Cabinet, Shiozaki is affiliated to the openly revisionist organization <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Kaigi" title="Nippon Kaigi">Nippon Kaigi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He also is a member of the following <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right-wing" title="Right-wing">right-wing</a> group at the Diet:</p>
<ul>
<li>Japan Rebirth (創生「日本」 <i>Sōsei Nippon</i>)</li>
<li>Nippon Kaigi Diet discussion group (日本会議国会議員懇談会 <i>Nippon kaigi kokkai giin kondankai</i>)</li>
<li>Conference of parlementarians on the Shinto Association of Spiritual Leadership (神道政治連盟国会議員懇談会) - NB: SAS a.k.a. Sinseiren, Shinto Political League, Shinto Seiji Renmei Kokkai Giin Kondankai</li>
</ul>
<p>Shiozaki gave the following answers to the questionnaire submitted by Mainichi to parlementarians in 2012:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>no answer regarding the revision of the Constitution</li>
<li>in favor of the right of collective self-defense (revision of <a href="/wiki/Article_9_of_the_Japanese_Constitution" title="Article 9 of the Japanese Constitution">Article 9</a>)</li>
<li>no answer regarding the reform of the National assembly (unicameral instead of bicameral)</li>
<li>in favor of reactivating nuclear power plants</li>
<li>against the goal of zero nuclear power by 2030s</li>
<li>in favor of the relocation of <a href="/wiki/Marine_Corps_Air_Station_Futenma" title="Marine Corps Air Station Futenma">Marine Corps Air Station Futenma</a> (Okinawa)</li>
<li>in favor of evaluating the purchase of Senkaku Islands by the Government</li>
<li>in favor of a strong attitude versus China</li>
<li>no answer regarding the participation of Japan to the <a href="/wiki/Trans-Pacific_Partnership" title="Trans-Pacific Partnership">Trans-Pacific Partnership</a></li>
<li>no answer regarding a nuclear-armed Japan</li>
<li>no answer regarding the reform of the Imperial Household that would allow women to retain their Imperial status even after marriage</li>
</ul>
<p>On October 17, 2014, Shiozaki sent an offering ('<i>masakaki</i>) to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yasukuni_Shrine_Controversy" title="Yasukuni Shrine Controversy">controversial</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yasukuni" title="Yasukuni">Yasukuni</a> shrine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
