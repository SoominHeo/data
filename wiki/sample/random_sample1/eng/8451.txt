<p><b>Kazuhiko Shiotani</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">塩谷 和彦</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shiotani Kazuhiko</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born May 27, 1974 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Takasago,_Hyogo" title="Takasago, Hyogo">Takasago, Hyogo</a>, Japan)</span> is a Japanese former baseball utility player who spent 11 seasons in <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Professional_Baseball" title="Nippon Professional Baseball">Nippon Professional Baseball</a> as well as a stint in the United States and a campaign in Korea.</p>
<p>Prior to playing professionally, he attended Shinko Gakuen High School.</p>
<p>After playing in <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ni-gun&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ni-gun (page does not exist)">ni-gun</a></i> for a spell, Shiotani was loaned to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bristol_Tigers" title="Bristol Tigers">Bristol Tigers</a> of the American <a href="/wiki/Appalachian_League" title="Appalachian League">Appalachian League</a> in 1994, with whom he went hitless in 14 at-bats. He returned to Japan and, from 1995 to 2001, earned playing time with the Tigers each season. From 1995 to 1997, he had a total of 17 at-bats and hit .118, though in 1996 he had a home run and four RBI. In 19 at-bats in 1998, he hit .105. He hit .282 in 117 at-bats in 1999, .252 in 107 at-bats in 2000 and .210 in 81 at-bats in 2001.</p>
<p>He was traded to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orix_Blue_Wave" title="Orix Blue Wave">Orix Blue Wave</a> following the 2001 season. His playing time increased. In 2002, he hit .230 with five home runs and 20 RBI in 274 at-bats and in 2003 -- his only 'full' season of work -- he hit .307 with eight home runs, 46 RBI and 134 hits in 123 games (436 at-bats). The next year, he hit .269 with nine home runs and 48 RBI in 360 at-bats and in 2005, his last campaign, he hit .176 with no home runs and three RBI in 34 at-bats.</p>
<p>All told, he hit .264 with 29 home runs, 145 RBI and 381 hits in an 11-year NPB career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006, he went to play in Korea, suiting up for <a href="/wiki/SK_Wyverns" title="SK Wyverns">SK Wyverns</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Korea_Baseball_Organization" title="Korea Baseball Organization">Korea Baseball Organization</a>. In 23 games, he hit .297 with three home runs and 19 RBI.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
