<p><b>Guo Zongxun</b> (郭宗訓) (14 September 953<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wds120_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-wds120-2">[2]</a></sup> – 973), also known by his <a href="/wiki/Posthumous_name" title="Posthumous name">posthumous name</a> Gongdi (恭帝; "Emperor Gong"), was the third and last <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_China" title="Emperor of China">emperor</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient_China" title="Ancient China">ancient China</a>'s short-lived <a href="/wiki/Later_Zhou" title="Later Zhou">Later Zhou</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Five_Dynasties_and_Ten_Kingdoms_period" title="Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms period">Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms period</a>. He reigned from July 959, when he succeeded his father <a href="/wiki/Chai_Rong" title="Chai Rong">Chai Rong</a>, until February 960, when he was overthrown by general <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Taizu_of_Song" title="Emperor Taizu of Song">Zhao Kuangyin</a>, who founded the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Song_Dynasty" title="Song Dynasty">Song Dynasty</a>.</p>
<p>Gongdi was sent away with his mother to Xijing (西京). Despite assurance by Emperor Taizu that the Chai family would be treated with respect, Gongdi was killed in 973 by Xin Wenyue, an official trying to gain favour with the emperor. Upon hearing the news, Emperor Taizu ordered a period of mourning and buried the dethroned emperor next to Emperor Shizong's tomb.</p>
