<p>The <b>CBS Sports Network</b> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Digital_cable" title="Digital cable">digital cable</a> and <a href="/wiki/Satellite_television" title="Satellite television">satellite</a> <a href="/wiki/Television_network" title="Television network">television network</a> that is owned by the <a href="/wiki/CBS_Corporation" title="CBS Corporation">CBS Corporation</a>. When it launched in 2002 as the <b>National College Sports Network</b> (later <b>College Sports Television</b> a.k.a. <b>CSTV</b>), it operated as a multi-platform media brand which also included its primary website, collegesports.com, and a network of websites operated for the athletic departments of 215 colleges and universities.</p>
<p>After CSTV was acquired by CBS in 2008, the network was re-branded as the <b>CBS College Sports Network</b>. The network initially maintained its college sports focus, but in February 2011, the service was re-branded as CBS Sports Network to re-position it as a mainstream sports service. The network continues to have a particular focus on college sports, along with coverage of smaller leagues and events, simulcasts of sports radio shows from CBS-owned stations, and studio and analysis programming.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CBS_Sports_Network&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:CBS_Sports_Network_Logo.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="153" data-file-width="609" height="50" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/CBS_Sports_Network_Logo.png/200px-CBS_Sports_Network_Logo.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/CBS_Sports_Network_Logo.png/300px-CBS_Sports_Network_Logo.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/CBS_Sports_Network_Logo.png/400px-CBS_Sports_Network_Logo.png 2x" width="200"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:CBS_Sports_Network_Logo.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
logo used from 2011-2016</div>

<p>The network's roots began in 1999 when Chris Bevilacqua approached the co-founders of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Classic_Sports_Network" title="Classic Sports Network">Classic Sports Network</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brian_Bedol" title="Brian Bedol">Brian Bedol</a> and Stephen D. Greenberg (son of <a href="/wiki/Hank_Greenberg" title="Hank Greenberg">Hank Greenberg</a>) – at that time, running Fusient Media Ventures, a New York-based sports and media company – with the idea for a cable network featuring college sports 24 hours a day. Under the leadership of Bedol as CEO, the network was originally named the <b>National College Sports Network</b> in June 2002, was subsequently renamed <b>College Sports Television</b> (CSTV) and launched on February 23, 2003. From their headquarters and studio operations at <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_Piers" title="Chelsea Piers">Chelsea Piers</a> in New York City, CSTV was the first independent cable channel to be distributed nationwide, having been carried on satellite provider <a href="/wiki/DirecTV" title="DirecTV">DirecTV</a> at launch.</p>
<p>In November 2005, College Sports Television was purchased by <a href="/wiki/CBS_Corporation" title="CBS Corporation">CBS Corporation</a> (which had just recently split from <a href="/wiki/Viacom" title="Viacom">Viacom</a> at the time) for $325 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> On January 3, 2008, it was announced that CSTV would be integrated into <a href="/wiki/CBS_Sports" title="CBS Sports">CBS Sports</a>, with the sports division's executive vice president and executive producer, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Petitti" title="Tony Petitti">Tony Petitti</a>, taking over day-to-day operational management of CSTV, which would be overseen by <a href="/wiki/CBS_News" title="CBS News">CBS News</a> and Sports president <a href="/wiki/Sean_McManus_(television_executive)" title="Sean McManus (television executive)">Sean McManus</a>. CSTV co-founder Brian Bedol would become a senior advisor to CBS Corporation president and CEO <a href="/wiki/Leslie_Moonves" title="Leslie Moonves">Leslie Moonves</a> (Petitti has since left CBS to take on the same role for the <a href="/wiki/MLB_Network" title="MLB Network">MLB Network</a>).</p>
<p>In the fall of 2006, CSTV launched more than 100 <a href="/wiki/Broadband" title="Broadband">broadband</a> channels dedicated to college sports, which feature more than 10,000 live events. The subscription/pay-per-view service, called CBS College Sports XXL, and its portfolio of broadband channels in its All-Access suite, include coverage of <a href="/wiki/Notre_Dame_Fighting_Irish" title="Notre Dame Fighting Irish">Notre Dame</a>, <a href="/wiki/USC_Trojans" title="USC Trojans">Southern California</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kansas_Jayhawks" title="Kansas Jayhawks">Kansas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio_State_Buckeyes" title="Ohio State Buckeyes">Ohio State</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_Tar_Heels" title="North Carolina Tar Heels">North Carolina</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="CBS_reorganization">CBS reorganization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CBS_Sports_Network&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: CBS reorganization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On February 12, 2008, CBS Corporation announced that, as part of the ongoing integration of CSTV into CBS Sports, that the network would be renamed the <b>CBS College Sports Network</b> on March 16, coinciding with the start of <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a>'s coverage of the <a href="/wiki/2008_NCAA_Men%27s_Division_I_Basketball_Tournament" title="2008 NCAA Men's Division I Basketball Tournament">NCAA's basketball tournament</a>. Studio shows moved from the original Chelsea Piers headquarters to the <a href="/wiki/CBS_Broadcast_Center" title="CBS Broadcast Center">CBS Broadcast Center</a> on West 57th Street in 2012. As part of the relaunch, the network added a new news program, <i>College Sports Tonight</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> That program was canceled in 2010, however other studio shows (including <i>Inside College Football</i> and <i>Inside College Basketball</i>) still originate from the Chelsea Piers location.</p>
<p>On February 15, 2011, CBS announced that the network would be relaunched as <b>CBS Sports Network</b> on April 4 (coinciding with the end of the <a href="/wiki/2011_NCAA_Men%27s_Division_I_Basketball_Tournament" title="2011 NCAA Men's Division I Basketball Tournament">2011 NCAA basketball tournament</a>), repositioning it as a mainstream sports network.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="High_definition">High definition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CBS_Sports_Network&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: High definition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>CBS Sports Network HD is a <a href="/wiki/1080i" title="1080i">1080i</a> <a href="/wiki/High-definition_television" title="High-definition television">high definition</a> simulcast feed of CBS Sports Network that launched in August 2008. Prior to the launch of the feed, the two NCAA basketball tournament games that aired in March 2008, which were presented in HD on CBS, were converted to a <a href="/wiki/Standard-definition_television" title="Standard-definition television">standard definition</a> feed. CBS Sports Network uses the <a href="/wiki/Active_Format_Description" title="Active Format Description">AFD</a> #10 broadcast flag to present programming on its standard definition feed in <a href="/wiki/Letterboxing_(filming)" title="Letterboxing (filming)">letterboxed</a> <a href="/wiki/Widescreen" title="Widescreen">widescreen</a> for viewers watching on <a href="/wiki/Cable_television" title="Cable television">cable television</a> through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/4:3" title="4:3">4:3</a> television sets.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Programming">Programming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CBS_Sports_Network&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Programming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since airing its first game, CBS Sports Network has televised thousands of hours of original programming, features, talk shows and documentaries as well as extensive women’s sports coverage. Its regular season and championship event coverage draws from every major collegiate athletic conference and division, in addition to nine NCAA championships. CBS Sports Network televises 35 men's and women's college sports including <a href="/wiki/College_football" title="College football">football</a>, <a href="/wiki/College_basketball" title="College basketball">basketball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ultimate_(sport)" title="Ultimate (sport)">ultimate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">hockey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lacrosse" title="Lacrosse">lacrosse</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">soccer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Collegiate_wrestling" title="Collegiate wrestling">wrestling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volleyball" title="Volleyball">volleyball</a> from every major conference. The network holds multi-media and marketing rights for the <a href="/wiki/Mountain_West_Conference" title="Mountain West Conference">Mountain West Conference</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_10_Conference" title="Atlantic 10 Conference">Atlantic 10 Conference</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conference_USA" title="Conference USA">Conference USA</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Patriot_League" title="Patriot League">Patriot League</a>, <a href="/wiki/Army_Black_Knights" title="Army Black Knights">Army football</a> and <a href="/wiki/Navy_Midshipmen" title="Navy Midshipmen">Navy football</a>.</p>
<p>In April 2006, the network organized the first <a href="/wiki/Collegiate_Nationals" title="Collegiate Nationals">Collegiate Nationals</a>, a festival of championships dedicated to crowning champions in a wide variety of collegiate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Action_sports" title="Action sports">action sports</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Snowboarding" title="Snowboarding">snowboarding</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wakeboarding" title="Wakeboarding">wakeboarding</a> and <a href="/wiki/Beach_volleyball" title="Beach volleyball">beach volleyball</a>. More than 1,000 competitors converged on <a href="/wiki/Reno,_Nevada" title="Reno, Nevada">Reno</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lake_Tahoe" title="Lake Tahoe">Tahoe</a> to compete, the largest number ever for an event of its kind. For its second installment in 2007, the Collegiate Nationals added sports and other events such as national film and music competitions, as well as a second venue – <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a>. The third year, 2008, brought further changes, as the winter sports events were moved to the Keystone Resort near <a href="/wiki/Boulder,_Colorado" title="Boulder, Colorado">Boulder, Colorado</a> and <a href="/wiki/Competitive_eating" title="Competitive eating">competitive eating</a> was added.</p>
<p>In the fall of 2006, CSTV and <a href="/wiki/Comcast" title="Comcast">Comcast</a> launched the <a href="/wiki/MountainWest_Sports_Network" title="MountainWest Sports Network">MountainWest Sports Network</a> (colloquially known as <i>The Mtn.</i>), a network focusing exclusively on the <a href="/wiki/Mountain_West_Conference" title="Mountain West Conference">Mountain West Conference</a>. The relation with the network also gave CSTV exclusive online and broadcasting rights to <a href="/wiki/Notre_Dame_Fighting_Irish_football" title="Notre Dame Fighting Irish football">Notre Dame</a>'s game at <a href="/wiki/Air_Force_Falcons_football" title="Air Force Falcons football">Air Force</a> on November 11, 2006 – which caused controversy since CSTV did not have carriage as widely distributed as other networks that have aired Notre Dame games. The Irish did not revisit a Mountain West team until a 2013 game at Air Force, which once again aired on CBSSN.</p>
<p>On April 3, 2012, CBS Sports Network premiered <i>Rome</i>, a sports news and talk program hosted by nationally syndicated radio host <a href="/wiki/Jim_Rome" title="Jim Rome">Jim Rome</a>; Rome had recently left <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a> and his previous show, <i><a href="/wiki/Jim_Rome_Is_Burning" title="Jim Rome Is Burning">Jim Rome Is Burning</a></i>, after signing on a new contract with CBS Sports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday-rome_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-rome-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On June 7, 2012, CBS Sports Network began to air the remaining games of the <a href="/wiki/American_Hockey_League" title="American Hockey League">American Hockey League</a>'s <a href="/wiki/2012_Calder_Cup_playoffs" title="2012 Calder Cup playoffs">2012 Calder Cup Final</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_Admirals_(AHL)" title="Norfolk Admirals (AHL)">Norfolk Admirals</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Marlies" title="Toronto Marlies">Toronto Marlies</a>, starting with Game 3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbssn-ahlfinal_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-cbssn-ahlfinal-6">[6]</a></sup> On July 26, 2012, the network signed a deal with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Football_League_(2009)" title="United Football League (2009)">United Football League</a> – a second-tier professional football league that began play in October 2009 – to televise its games for the <a href="/wiki/2012_UFL_season" title="2012 UFL season">2012 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The UFL paid for all production expenses and received no rights fee from CBS Sports Network for the broadcasts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VirginianPilot10032012_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-VirginianPilot10032012-8">[8]</a></sup> The league lasted approximately four weeks on CBS Sports Network before suspending operations halfway through the season.</p>
<p>In September 2012, CBS Sports Network introduced <i><a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">NFL</a> Monday QB</i>, a weekly NFL recap show. In September 2013, the network expanded its supplemental NFL coverage with <i>That Other Pre-Game Show</i>, a four-hour "fan-focused" program positioned as an interactive, "casual" counterpart to other pre-game shows, hosted by <a href="/wiki/Adam_Schein" title="Adam Schein">Adam Schein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bart_Scott" title="Bart Scott">Bart Scott</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amy_Trask" title="Amy Trask">Amy Trask</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Brandon_Tierney&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Brandon Tierney (page does not exist)">Brandon Tierney</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-svg-tops_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-svg-tops-9">[9]</a></sup> On April 26, 2013, the network announced that it had signed a deal with the <a href="/wiki/AMA_Superbike_Championship" title="AMA Superbike Championship">GEICO Motorcycle AMA Pro Road Racing Series</a> for the remainder of the 2013 and the 2014 seasons. The network will air flag to flag coverage for the races as well as live coverage for several of the races. The network broadcast the <a href="/wiki/FIA_WTCC_Race_of_the_United_States" title="FIA WTCC Race of the United States">FIA WTCC Race of the United States</a> from <a href="/wiki/Sonoma_Raceway" title="Sonoma Raceway">Sonoma Raceway</a> on September 8, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 2, 2013, CBS Sports Network announced that it will begin simulcasting the <i><a href="/wiki/Boomer_and_Carton" title="Boomer and Carton">Boomer and Carton</a></i> morning show from co-owned <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sports_talk" title="Sports talk">sports talk</a> radio station <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WFAN" title="WFAN">WFAN</a> in New York City in January 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bobsblitz-boomerandcarton_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-bobsblitz-boomerandcarton-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2014, CBSSN announced a new talk show, <i>We Need To Talk</i>, which features an all-female panel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mcn-weneedtotalk_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-mcn-weneedtotalk-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2015, the network premiered <i>Time to Schein</i>, a new program hosted by <a href="/wiki/Adam_Schein" title="Adam Schein">Adam Schein</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Carriage">Carriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CBS_Sports_Network&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Carriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>CBS Sports Network is available nationally on satellite providers <a href="/wiki/DirecTV" title="DirecTV">DirecTV</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dish_Network" title="Dish Network">Dish Network</a>, and regionally on fiber optic television provider <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Verizon_FiOS" title="Verizon FiOS">Verizon FiOS</a>, and cable providers such as <a href="/wiki/Comcast" title="Comcast">Comcast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Time_Warner_Cable" title="Time Warner Cable">Time Warner Cable</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cox_Communications" title="Cox Communications">Cox Communications</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cablevision" title="Cablevision">Cablevision</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charter_Communications" title="Charter Communications">Charter Communications</a>, <a href="/wiki/Insight_Communications" title="Insight Communications">Insight Communications</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bright_House_Networks" title="Bright House Networks">Bright House Networks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blue_Ridge_Communications" title="Blue Ridge Communications">Blue Ridge Communications</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mediacom" title="Mediacom">Mediacom</a>, <a href="/wiki/RCN_Corporation" title="RCN Corporation">RCN Corporation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Cable_Communications" title="Advanced Cable Communications">Advanced Cable Communications</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bresnan_Communications" title="Bresnan Communications">Bresnan</a>.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rogers_Cable" title="Rogers Cable">Rogers Cable</a> began carrying CBS Sports Network on October 9, 2008. Satellite provider <a href="/wiki/Bell_TV" title="Bell TV">Bell TV</a> started carrying the channel on September 3, 2009 and stopped on August 21, 2014 (they subsequently brought the channel back August 2015). Certain programs aired by the network (particularly NFL and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NCAA_Men%27s_Division_I_Basketball_Championship" title="NCAA Men's Division I Basketball Championship">NCAA basketball tournament</a> related programs and other programs whose rights are owned by other broadcasters) are blacked out in Canada, and replaced with reruns of other events or studio programs. CBS Sports Network is the only U.S.-based mainstream sports service that is carried on pay television in Canada.</p>
<p>In late February 2009, CBS Sports Network reached a new carriage agreement with DirecTV, which allowed the satellite provider to move the channel from its add on "Sports Pack" to its "Choice Xtra" base package; the move became effective on February 25, 2009, expanding the channel's distribution to 30 million subscribers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 7, 2009, Cox Communications announced that it would add the channel to its systems in <a href="/wiki/Orange_County,_California" title="Orange County, California">Orange County, California</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a> on August 1, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/AT%26T_U-verse" title="AT&amp;T U-verse">AT&amp;T U-verse</a> added the network on February 17, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> On August 1, 2011, <a href="/wiki/Cable_One" title="Cable One">Cable One</a> added the network in select markets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
