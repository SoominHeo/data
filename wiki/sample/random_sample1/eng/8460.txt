<p><b>Shizukuishi</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">雫石町</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shizukuishi-chō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Towns_of_Japan" title="Towns of Japan">town</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Iwate_District,_Iwate" title="Iwate District, Iwate">Iwate District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iwate_Prefecture" title="Iwate Prefecture">Iwate Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of February 2014, the town had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 17,277 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 28.4 persons per km². The total area was 609.01  km².</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shizukuishi,_Iwate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shizukuishi is located in the <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cu_Mountains" title="Ōu Mountains">Ōu Mountains</a> of west-central Iwate Prefecture, bordering <a href="/wiki/Akita_Prefecture" title="Akita Prefecture">Akita Prefecture</a> to the west. <a href="/wiki/Mount_Iwate" title="Mount Iwate">Mount Iwate</a>, an active volcano, lies just to the north of Shizukuishi and dominates the landscape. The <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cu_Mountains" title="Ōu Mountains">Ōu Mountains</a> form the boundary to the west as well with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Akita_Komagatake" title="Akita Komagatake">Akita Komagatake</a>, another active volcano, just across the border in <a href="/wiki/Akita_Prefecture" title="Akita Prefecture">Akita Prefecture</a>. The downtown area is located where the <a href="/wiki/Shizukuishi_River" title="Shizukuishi River">Shizukuishi</a> and Kakkonda Rivers meet. Gosho Lake was created in 1981 with the completion of <a href="/wiki/Gosho_Dam" title="Gosho Dam">Gosho Dam</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shizukuishi,_Iwate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of present-day Shizukuishi has been inhabited since the earliest times. Archaeologists have found remains dating to the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Paleolithic" title="Japanese Paleolithic">Japanese Paleolithic</a> period. The area come under the control of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yamato_dynasty" title="Yamato dynasty">Yamato dynasty</a> during the early <a href="/wiki/Heian_period" title="Heian period">Heian period</a> under the campaigns of <a href="/wiki/Sakanoue_no_Tamuramaro" title="Sakanoue no Tamuramaro">Sakanoue no Tamuramaro</a>, and was part of ancient <a href="/wiki/Mutsu_Province" title="Mutsu Province">Mutsu Province</a>. The area was a battlefield during the <a href="/wiki/Former_Nine_Years_War" title="Former Nine Years War">Former Nine Years War</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Gosannen_War" title="Gosannen War">Gosannen War</a>. It eventually came under the control of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nambu_clan" title="Nambu clan">Nambu clan</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>, who ruled <a href="/wiki/Morioka_Domain" title="Morioka Domain">Morioka Domain</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a>.</p>
<p>The villages of Shizukuishi, Gosho, Nishiyama and Omyojin within Minami-Iwate District were created on April 1, 1889 with the establishment of the municipality system. Kita-Iwate District and Minami-Iwate Districts merged to form Iwate District on March 29, 1896. Shizukuishi was raised to town status on December 23, 1940. On April 1, 1955, Shizukuishi merged with Gosho, Nishiyama and Omyojin, but lost a portion of its territory in a border adjustment with Morioka City on October 1 of the same year. The <a href="/wiki/All_Nippon_Airways_Flight_58" title="All Nippon Airways Flight 58">All Nippon Airways Flight 58</a> accident occurred over Shizukuishi on July 30, 1971.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_attractions">Local attractions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shizukuishi,_Iwate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Local attractions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In winter, numerous ski areas and resorts come to life. Koiwai Farm is a working dairy and tourist attraction. Every year in February it hosts a large snow festival.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>This "remote farming village"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdh19910825_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-cdh19910825-1">[1]</a></sup> is known for its annual hemp festival every year in August which draws "crowds of sightseers".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Due to the town's rural nature, low income and low population <a href="/wiki/Yoshiaki_Tsutsumi" title="Yoshiaki Tsutsumi">Yoshiaki Tsutsumi</a>, Japan's richest man at the time, was able to build a large ski area and hotel without the kind of "organized opposition of environmentalists" which stymied his plans elsewhere in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdh19910825_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-cdh19910825-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
