<p>The <b>Tibet Area</b> was a province-level administrative division of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_(1912-49)" title="Republic of China (1912-49)">Republic of China</a> and early People's Republic of <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. The Republic of China never had any real control over the area, which was <i>de facto</i> controlled by the <a href="/wiki/Ganden_Phodrang" title="Ganden Phodrang">Ganden Phodrang</a> government in <a href="/wiki/Lhasa" title="Lhasa">Lhasa</a>. When the republic was founded in 1912, the <a href="/wiki/Kashag" title="Kashag">Kashag</a> controlled about the same area as the Tibet Area, but later also took control of the western portion of <a href="/wiki/Xikang" title="Xikang">Sikang Province</a>; thus for the most of the Republic of China period, Lhasa controlled an area which was near identical to the contemporary <a href="/wiki/Tibet_Autonomous_Region" title="Tibet Autonomous Region">Tibet Autonomous Region</a>. The People's Republic of China <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Chamdo" title="Battle of Chamdo">invaded Chamdo</a> in 1950 and <a href="/wiki/Incorporation_of_Tibet_into_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Incorporation of Tibet into the People's Republic of China">incorporated</a> the Dalai Lama-controlled regions in 1951. Following the <a href="/wiki/1959_Tibetan_uprising" title="1959 Tibetan uprising">1959 Tibetan uprising</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/State_Council_of_the_PRC" title="State Council of the PRC">State Council of the PRC</a> ordered to replace the Kashag government with the "Preparatory Committee for the Tibet Autonomous Region" which was established in 1956. The current <a href="/wiki/Tibet_Autonomous_Region" title="Tibet Autonomous Region">Tibet Autonomous Region</a> was eventually established in 1965.</p>
