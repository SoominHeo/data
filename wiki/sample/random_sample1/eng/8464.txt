<p><b>Chicago Red Stars</b> is an American professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">soccer</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, competing in the <a href="/wiki/National_Women%27s_Soccer_League" title="National Women's Soccer League">National Women's Soccer League</a>, who play their home games in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toyota_Park_(Bridgeview)" title="Toyota Park (Bridgeview)">Toyota Park</a>. The team was a founding member of <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Professional_Soccer" title="Women's Professional Soccer">Women's Professional Soccer</a>, and played in the league in 2009 and 2010. After leaving the WPS in December 2010, due to financial issues, the team reorganized and joined the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Premier_Soccer_League" title="Women's Premier Soccer League">Women's Premier Soccer League</a> for the 2011 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2012, the team competed in <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Premier_Soccer_League_Elite" title="Women's Premier Soccer League Elite">Women's Premier Soccer League Elite</a>, the single year this league existed. Since 2013, the team has been competing in <a href="/wiki/National_Women%27s_Soccer_League" title="National Women's Soccer League">National Women's Soccer League</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chicago_Red_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Women.27s_Professional_Soccer_.28WPS.29">Women's Professional Soccer (WPS)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chicago_Red_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Women's Professional Soccer (WPS)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Establishment_.282006.E2.80.932009.29">Establishment (2006â2009)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chicago_Red_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Establishment (2006â2009)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 2006, it was announced that Chicago would have a team in a new league being developed for women's professional soccer. It would eventually join six other teams, <a href="/wiki/Washington_Freedom" title="Washington Freedom">Washington Freedom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boston_Breakers" title="Boston Breakers">Boston Breakers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Sol" title="Los Angeles Sol">Los Angeles Sol</a>, <a href="/wiki/FC_Gold_Pride" title="FC Gold Pride">FC Gold Pride</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sky_Blue_FC" title="Sky Blue FC">Sky Blue FC</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Saint_Louis_Athletica" title="Saint Louis Athletica">Saint Louis Athletica</a> in <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Professional_Soccer" title="Women's Professional Soccer">Women's Professional Soccer</a> (WPS) for the <a href="/wiki/2009_Women%27s_Professional_Soccer_season" title="2009 Women's Professional Soccer season">league's inaugural season</a> in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Marcia McDermott, a former head coach of the <a href="/wiki/Northwestern_University" title="Northwestern University">Northwestern University</a> women's soccer team and WUSA team <a href="/wiki/Carolina_Courage" title="Carolina Courage">Carolina Courage</a>, was named as the Red Stars' general manager.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>On June 3, 2008, the "Red Stars" name was announced in a ceremony at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toyota_Park_(Bridgeview)" title="Toyota Park (Bridgeview)">Toyota Park</a>. The name refers to the four six-pointed red stars on the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Chicago" title="Flag of Chicago">municipal flag of Chicago</a>. Each star represents a landmark event in Chicago history: <a href="/wiki/Fort_Dearborn" title="Fort Dearborn">Fort Dearborn</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Great_Chicago_Fire" title="Great Chicago Fire">Great Chicago Fire</a>, the <a href="/wiki/World%27s_Columbian_Exposition" title="World's Columbian Exposition">1893 Worldâs Fair</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Century_of_Progress" title="Century of Progress">1933 World's Fair</a>. Other names considered were Progress, <a href="/wiki/List_of_tallest_buildings_in_Chicago" title="List of tallest buildings in Chicago">Towers</a>, Union, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chicago_Blues" title="Chicago Blues">Blues</a>, <a href="/wiki/Great_Chicago_Fire" title="Great Chicago Fire">1871</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Origin_of_Chicago%27s_%22Windy_City%22_nickname" title="Origin of Chicago's &quot;Windy City&quot; nickname">Wind</a>. <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.womensprosoccer.com/soccer_ektid1950.aspx" rel="nofollow">[1]</a>. "Red Stars" was chosen by popular vote in a two-month fan ballot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2009_inaugural_season">2009 inaugural season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chicago_Red_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2009 inaugural season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2009_Chicago_Red_Stars_season" title="2009 Chicago Red Stars season">2009 Chicago Red Stars season</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:342px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Redstar_Line.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="368" data-file-width="615" height="203" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Redstar_Line.jpg/340px-Redstar_Line.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Redstar_Line.jpg/510px-Redstar_Line.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/fa/Redstar_Line.jpg 2x" width="340"/></a>

The Red Stars line up for the National Anthem.</div>

<p>The Chicago Red Stars won their first game of the season, 1â0, at <a href="/wiki/Saint_Louis_Athletica" title="Saint Louis Athletica">Saint Louis Athletica</a>, and followed with two ties (1â1 at <a href="/wiki/Washington_Freedom" title="Washington Freedom">Washington Freedom</a> and 0â0 hosting <a href="/wiki/Sky_Blue_FC" title="Sky Blue FC">Sky Blue FC</a>) and a 4â0 thrashing of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boston_Breakers_(WPS)" title="Boston Breakers (WPS)">Boston Breakers</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toyota_Park_(Bridgeview)" title="Toyota Park (Bridgeview)">Toyota Park</a>. This gave the team the best starting record of any team in the league besides eventual season champions <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Sol" title="Los Angeles Sol">Los Angeles Sol</a>, seeming to live up to preseason expectations based on the strong core of USWNT and international players on the Red Stars roster.</p>
<p>Despite their strong start, the Red Stars then went winless for their next nine games, only getting three away draws in that streak, which included a 451-minute-long scoring drought. Their opponents, the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Freedom" title="Washington Freedom">Washington Freedom</a> scored first in the next game and it appeared the streak would continue. However, the Red Stars made WPS history by becoming the first team to successfully come from behind, to win 2â1. They also beat cellar-dwellers <a href="/wiki/FC_Gold_Pride" title="FC Gold Pride">FC Gold Pride</a> in their next game 3â1, but then lost four of their last five games, eliminating them from the playoffs as they ended the season in sixth place.</p>
<p>True to his agreement to see the Red Stars organization through its first season, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Wilt" title="Peter Wilt">Peter Wilt</a> chose to step down as team CEO at the end of 2009, moving on to become general manager of the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Wave" title="Milwaukee Wave">Milwaukee Wave</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Indoor_Soccer_League" title="National Indoor Soccer League">NISL</a> team in his hometown. General Manager, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Marcia_McDermott&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Marcia McDermott (page does not exist)">Marcia McDermott</a>, took on many of his responsibilities at the Red Stars, although he continued to support the team as one of its representatives on the Women's Professional Soccer Board of Governors and as President of the Chicago Red Stars Charitable Foundation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_season">2010 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chicago_Red_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2010_Chicago_Red_Stars_season" title="2010 Chicago Red Stars season">2010 Chicago Red Stars season</a></div>
<p>The Red Stars opened up their second season with high hopes and many new faces.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In their first game, the Red Stars lost 1â0 at Sky Blue FC despite dominating much of the game. A 1â1 home tie in their second game earned the Stars their first point of the 2010 season.</p>
<p>After a successful 2010, the team left WPS on December 13; unable to meet the league's funding requirements for the 2011 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Women.27s_Premier_Soccer_League_.28WPSL.29">Women's Premier Soccer League (WPSL)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chicago_Red_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Women's Premier Soccer League (WPSL)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2011_season">2011 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chicago_Red_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2011 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2011_Chicago_Red_Stars_season" title="2011 Chicago Red Stars season">2011 Chicago Red Stars season</a></div>
<p>Red Stars joined the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Premier_Soccer_League" title="Women's Premier Soccer League">Women's Premier Soccer League</a> for 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> They played their home games at <a href="/wiki/Village_of_Lisle-Benedictine_University_Sports_Complex" title="Village of Lisle-Benedictine University Sports Complex">Village of Lisle-Benedictine University Sports Complex</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> in west suburban Chicago.</p>
<p>The new roster consisted of players from the WNT U-20 and U-23 pools, as well as collegiate stars. The first players announced included three players with WPS experience, including two from the Red Stars' 2010 squad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> It was coached by Rory Dames, a Chicago native. Red Stars also continued their activity in the Chicago soccer community through their soccer camp programs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Red Stars finished the regular season with a 10â1 record, and hosted the playoff semifinals and championship matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> While they beat <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Hellenic" title="Tampa Bay Hellenic">Tampa Bay Hellenic</a> 2â1 in their semifinal, they lost by the same score in the final to the Orange County Waves, another WPSL team that has past WPS connections.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Women.27s_Premier_Soccer_League_Elite_.28WPSLE.29">Women's Premier Soccer League Elite (WPSLE)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chicago_Red_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Women's Premier Soccer League Elite (WPSLE)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_season">2012 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chicago_Red_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2012 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2012_Chicago_Red_Stars_season" title="2012 Chicago Red Stars season">2012 Chicago Red Stars season</a></div>
<p>In 2012, Red Stars joined two other former WPS clubs, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boston_Breakers_(WPSL_Elite)" title="Boston Breakers (WPSL Elite)">Boston Breakers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Western_New_York_Flash" title="Western New York Flash">Western New York Flash</a>, in the new WPSL Elite League. Five other former WPSL teams joined the league, including <a href="/wiki/ASA_Chesapeake_Charge" title="ASA Chesapeake Charge">ASA Chesapeake Charge</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Indiana_(W-League)" title="F.C. Indiana (W-League)">FC Indiana</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_England_Mutiny" title="New England Mutiny">New England Mutiny</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_Fury" title="New York Fury">New York Fury</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Fever" title="Philadelphia Fever">Philadelphia Fever</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Red Stars finished fourth in the league, making the playoffs and advanced to the final, where they lost to <a href="/wiki/Western_New_York_Flash" title="Western New York Flash">Flash</a> in a 2â4 penalty shootout, after a 1â1 draw at the end of extra time. Impressively, the Red Stars also won the <a href="/wiki/2012_National_Women%27s_Cup" title="2012 National Women's Cup">2012</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USASA_National_Women%27s_Cup" title="USASA National Women's Cup">National Women's Cup</a> with just half the roster, as the Cup final occurred at the same time as their final WPSL Elite regular season match against eventual third-placed <a href="/wiki/New_York_Fury" title="New York Fury">New York Fury</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Women.27s_Soccer_League_.28NWSL.29">National Women's Soccer League (NWSL)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chicago_Red_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: National Women's Soccer League (NWSL)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In November 2012, it was announced that the Red Stars would be one of eight teams in a new women's professional soccer league, then yet to be named, which would be subsidized by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Soccer_Federation" title="United States Soccer Federation">United States Soccer Federation</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Soccer_Association" title="Canadian Soccer Association">Canadian Soccer Association</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Football_Federation" title="Mexican Football Federation">Mexican Football Federation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The league started competition in Spring of 2013.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013_season">2013 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chicago_Red_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2013 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2013_Chicago_Red_Stars_season" title="2013 Chicago Red Stars season">2013 Chicago Red Stars season</a></div>
<p>In the inaugural season of <a href="/wiki/National_Women%27s_Soccer_League" title="National Women's Soccer League">NWSL</a>, Red Stars started with 2 draws and 4 losses. The mid-season arrival of 2 Germans, <a href="/wiki/Sonja_Fuss" title="Sonja Fuss">Sonja Fuss</a> and <a href="/wiki/Inka_Grings" title="Inka Grings">Inka Grings</a>, from Europe revived the team somewhat, but not enough to qualify for the post-season playoffs. Red Stars played 22 matches with 8 wins, 8 ties and 6 losses to finish the season at sixth place in an eight team league.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_season">2014 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chicago_Red_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2014 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2014_Chicago_Red_Stars_season" title="2014 Chicago Red Stars season">2014 Chicago Red Stars season</a></div>
<p>In 2014, Red Stars finished one place better than previous season, in fifth place in regular season. This season, the club was waiting for United States international <a href="/wiki/Christen_Press" title="Christen Press">Christen Press</a> to join after completing her commitment to a European club, and the Canadian international <a href="/wiki/Melissa_Tancredi" title="Melissa Tancredi">Melissa Tancredi</a> to gain full fitness after her education. In addition, the team signed 2 other internationals, <a href="/wiki/Abby_Erceg" title="Abby Erceg">Abby Erceg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emily_van_Egmond" title="Emily van Egmond">Emily van Egmond</a> in May. Despite the late arrival of international stars, at the end of May the team had 6 wins, 1 draw and 2 losses; a competitive team performance enabled by rookies <a href="/wiki/Julie_Johnston" title="Julie Johnston">Julie Johnston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_DiBernardo" title="Vanessa DiBernardo">Vanessa DiBernardo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hayley_Brock" title="Hayley Brock">Hayley Brock</a>, young players <a href="/wiki/Jen_Hoy" title="Jen Hoy">Jen Hoy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Quon" title="Rachel Quon">Rachel Quon</a>, and veteran stalwarts <a href="/wiki/Lori_Chalupny" title="Lori Chalupny">Lori Chalupny</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michelle_Wenino" title="Michelle Wenino">Michelle Wenino</a>, and goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Karina_LeBlanc" title="Karina LeBlanc">Karina LeBlanc</a>. With a mid-season slump of 1 win in 11 matches during June and July, the team eventually did not qualify for post-season playoffs; edged out by head to head results with <a href="/wiki/Washington_Spirit" title="Washington Spirit">Washington Spirit</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_season">2015 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chicago_Red_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2015 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2015_Chicago_Red_Stars_season" title="2015 Chicago Red Stars season">2015 Chicago Red Stars season</a></div>
<p>The Red Stars started the 2015 season with home games, a 3â2 win over previous season's Supporting Shield winner <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Reign_FC" title="Seattle Reign FC">Seattle Reign FC</a>, followed by a 2â2 draw with <a href="/wiki/Portland_Thorns_FC" title="Portland Thorns FC">Portland Thorns FC</a>, and a 1-0 win over <a href="/wiki/Sky_Blue_FC" title="Sky Blue FC">Sky Blue FC</a>. With <a href="/wiki/Christen_Press" title="Christen Press">Christen Press</a> scoring 4 of the first 5 goals for Red Stars, Press was named the <a href="/wiki/NWSL_Player_of_the_Month#2015" title="NWSL Player of the Month">Player of the Month of April</a>. With the national teams members out training for <a href="/wiki/2015_FIFA_Women%27s_World_Cup" title="2015 FIFA Women's World Cup">2015 FIFA Women's World Cup</a>, rookie <a href="/wiki/Sofia_Huerta" title="Sofia Huerta">Sofia Huerta</a> scored multiple goals in 2 matches and was named <a href="/wiki/NWSL_Player_of_the_Week" title="NWSL Player of the Week">NWSL Player of the Week</a> for week 5, as well as week 6; and was also named <a href="/wiki/NWSL_Player_of_the_Month" title="NWSL Player of the Month">NWSL Player of the Month</a> of June. The team held first place during regular season from week 4 to week 13 and finished second. However, in the playoff the team lost in the semi-final to <a href="/wiki/FC_Kansas_City" title="FC Kansas City">FC Kansas City</a>.</p>

<p>The primary logo combines two heraldic Chicago images: the shape of the shield is taken from it equivalent inside the city of Chicagoâs official seal. The colors, stripes, and stars in the logo are all inspired by the symbolism of Chicagoâs municipal flag, with the blue stripes representing the two branches of the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_River" title="Chicago River">Chicago River</a>. The six points on the large red star in the center of the Chicago Red Stars logo also have their own meaning to the team:</p>
<ul>
<li>Entertainment (top point)</li>
<li>Service (next point moving clockwise)</li>
<li>Success</li>
<li>Community</li>
<li>Teamwork</li>
<li>Leadership</li>
</ul>
<p>Adrenalin, Inc., and jlmvisual partnered to jointly develop the Red Stars identity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stadiums">Stadiums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chicago_Red_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Stadiums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NWSL_stadiums" title="NWSL stadiums">NWSL stadiums</a></div>
<p>During the team's membership in <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Professional_Soccer" title="Women's Professional Soccer">Women's Professional Soccer</a>, Chicago Red Stars played at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toyota_Park_(Bridgeview)" title="Toyota Park (Bridgeview)">Toyota Park</a>, a world-class <a href="/wiki/Soccer-specific_stadium" title="Soccer-specific stadium">soccer-specific stadium</a>, with 20,000 dedicated seats for soccer. It is best known as the home of <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Fire_Soccer_Club" title="Chicago Fire Soccer Club">Chicago Fire Soccer Club</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 2011 to 2015, in <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Premier_Soccer_League" title="Women's Premier Soccer League">Women's Premier Soccer League</a>, <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Premier_Soccer_League_Elite" title="Women's Premier Soccer League Elite">Women's Premier Soccer League Elite</a> league and <a href="/wiki/National_Women%27s_Soccer_League" title="National Women's Soccer League">National Women's Soccer League</a>, Red Stars played their matches at the <a href="/wiki/Village_of_Lisle-Benedictine_University_Sports_Complex" title="Village of Lisle-Benedictine University Sports Complex">Village of Lisle-Benedictine University Sports Complex</a>. The university stadium seats 3,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In 2012 season, in the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Premier_Soccer_League_Elite" title="Women's Premier Soccer League Elite">WPSL-Elite</a> league, some matches were played at Concordia University Chicago Athletic Complex in <a href="/wiki/River_Forest,_Illinois" title="River Forest, Illinois">River Forest, Illinois</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> as well as the Lakeside Athletic Field at <a href="/wiki/Northwestern_University" title="Northwestern University">Northwestern University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Evanston,_Illinois" title="Evanston, Illinois">Evanston, Illinois</a> due to renovation construction at Benectine University.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2015, Chicago Red Stars announced the return to Toyota Park for their home games for <a href="/wiki/2016_Chicago_Red_Stars_season" title="2016 Chicago Red Stars season">2016 NWSL season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crs_toy_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-crs_toy-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Owners">Owners</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chicago_Red_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Owners">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="WPS_.282009.E2.80.932010.29">WPS (2009â2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chicago_Red_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: WPS (2009â2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Chicago Professional Women's Soccer, LLC investor and partner group was the owner of the Chicago Red Stars from 2009â2010 during the team's membership in <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Professional_Soccer" title="Women's Professional Soccer">Women's Professional Soccer</a>. In April 2010, the team's management group consisted of Gary Weaver, Jim Willett, and Arnim Whisler. The Illinois Women's Soccer League (IWSL) also signed on as a partner. In addition to the IWSL, the team's leadership included shareholders John (Jack) Cummins, Robert and Susan Morrison, Jim and Kathi Willett, Dale Weaver, Gary Weaver, Pin Ni, Dong Li, Arnim Whisler, Dean Egerter, Stephen Ritchie and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Wilt" title="Peter Wilt">Peter Wilt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="WPSL_.282011.29.2C_WPSL_E_.282012.29_and_NWSL_.282013.E2.80.93present.29">WPSL (2011), WPSL E (2012) and NWSL (2013âpresent)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chicago_Red_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: WPSL (2011), WPSL E (2012) and NWSL (2013âpresent)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Arnim Whisler was the only investor left after the 2010 season, he decided to keep the team going for the 2011 season in the WPSL while working to help reorganize a professional women's soccer league. WPS.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>He remains the owner of the Chicago Red Stars during the inaugural season of the NWSL <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
