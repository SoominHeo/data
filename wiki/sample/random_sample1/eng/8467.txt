<p><b>Shikotsu-Tōya National Park</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">支笏洞爺国立公園</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shikotsu Tōya Kokuritsu Kōen</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a national park in the western part of the island of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hokkaid%C5%8D" title="Hokkaidō">Hokkaidō</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. Named after the volcanic caldera lakes of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Shikotsu" title="Lake Shikotsu">Lake Shikotsu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lake_T%C5%8Dya" title="Lake Tōya">Lake Tōya</a>, it has a total area of 993.02 square kilometers. The popular <a href="/wiki/Hot_spring" title="Hot spring">hot spring</a> resort of <a href="/wiki/Noboribetsu,_Hokkaido" title="Noboribetsu, Hokkaido">Noboribetsu</a> is also within the park.</p>
