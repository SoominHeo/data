<p><i><b>Secret Garden</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">시크릿 가든</span>; <a href="/wiki/McCune%E2%80%93Reischauer" title="McCune–Reischauer">MR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Sikeurit Gadeun</span></i>) is a 2010 <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> television drama starring <a href="/wiki/Ha_Ji-won" title="Ha Ji-won">Ha Ji-won</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hyun_Bin" title="Hyun Bin">Hyun Bin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yoon_Sang-hyun" title="Yoon Sang-hyun">Yoon Sang-hyun</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kim_Sa-rang_(actress)" title="Kim Sa-rang (actress)">Kim Sa-rang</a>. It aired on <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a> from November 13, 2010 to January 16, 2011 on Saturdays and Sundays at 22:00 for 20 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Secret Garden</i> was a huge ratings success, with an estimation of more than 20 billion won worth of economic effect.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> It won a number of awards at both the <a href="/wiki/2010_SBS_Drama_Awards" title="2010 SBS Drama Awards">2010 SBS Drama Awards</a> and the <a href="/wiki/47th_Paeksang_Arts_Awards" title="47th Paeksang Arts Awards">47th Paeksang Arts Awards</a>, including "Grand Prize (Daesang)" for <a href="/wiki/Hyun_Bin" title="Hyun Bin">Hyun Bin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sbsdrama_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-sbsdrama-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baeksang_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-baeksang-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secret_Garden_(South_Korean_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A rationalizing Cinderella story between Gil Ra-im (<a href="/wiki/Ha_Ji-won" title="Ha Ji-won">Ha Ji-won</a>), a stuntwoman and Kim Joo-won (<a href="/wiki/Hyun_Bin" title="Hyun Bin">Hyun Bin</a>). a high-end department store CEO. Through a misunderstanding while trying to resolve a scandal of his cousin, Oska (<a href="/wiki/Yoon_Sang-hyun" title="Yoon Sang-hyun">Yoon Sang-hyun</a>), a famous singer, Ra-im and Joo-won meet and he finds himself stunned by her coolness. Joo-won does not understand why Ra-im is constantly on his mind, and decides to pursue her. Though she initially is repelled by him, Ra-im gradually starts reciprocating his feelings.</p>
<p>As two different worlds collide, Ra-im and Joo-won gradually learns about each other's worlds. Meanwhile, Oska reunites with his first love, Yoon Seul (<a href="/wiki/Kim_Sa-rang_(actress)" title="Kim Sa-rang (actress)">Kim Sa-rang</a>), who has made a successful directing career for herself. However, Yoon Seul wants nothing to do with Oska, and instead has her eyes set on Joo-won. The four become tangled in a love square, which becomes more confusing when Ra-im and Joo-won switched bodies.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cast">Cast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secret_Garden_(South_Korean_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main">Main</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secret_Garden_(South_Korean_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Main">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ha_Ji-won" title="Ha Ji-won">Ha Ji-won</a> as Gil Ra-im</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>Tough but secretly lonely, Gil Ra-im works as a stuntwoman for an action school that is run by director Im Jong-soo. Due to her father's death when she was in high school, she works hard and doesn't like relying on other people. Her one obsession is Oska, a famous Hallyu star. She meets the arrogant CEO, Kim Joo-won and although she initially dislikes him, their body swap brings about confusing feelings.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hyun_Bin" title="Hyun Bin">Hyun Bin</a> as Kim Joo-won</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>The CEO of a high-end departmental store, he is good-looking but arrogant. A childhood trauma caused him to have a defective memory and claustrophobia, causing him to be unable to take lifts. As a rational person, he always seeks answers and solutions to explain things that happen to him. When he meets the spunky Gil Ra-im, he is left confused as he does not understand why she is constantly on his mind.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Yoon_Sang-hyun" title="Yoon Sang-hyun">Yoon Sang-hyun</a> as Choi Woo-young (Oska)</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>A famous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hallyu" title="Hallyu">Hallyu</a> star who is slowly losing his popularity. He is Joo-won's cousin, as well as the first love of Yoon Seul. Frivolous and free-spirited, Oska used to rely on his charming looks and money, but seeing Seul again and discovering new talent in a young man named Tae-sun triggers something inside him, causing him to change.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kim_Sa-rang_(actress)" title="Kim Sa-rang (actress)">Kim Sa-rang</a> as Yoon Seul</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>Oska's first love and Kim Joo-won's fiancée. She is a music video director. Unable to forgive Oska, she is bent on making him hurt as much as possible.</dd>
</dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Supporting">Supporting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secret_Garden_(South_Korean_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Supporting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lee_Phillip" title="Lee Phillip">Lee Phillip</a> as Im Jong-soo</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>The director of an action school. Having studied abroad, Im Jong-soo speaks English fluently and is well connected with Hollywood. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_broadcast">International broadcast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secret_Garden_(South_Korean_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: International broadcast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>In January 2011, <a href="/wiki/SBS_Contents_Hub" title="SBS Contents Hub">SBS Contents Hub</a> announced that the drama had been purchased by a number of countries across Asia and the Americas: Peru and the United States. Japan, Taiwan, Hong Kong, China, the Philippines,Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, Singapore, Vietnam, Myanmar, and Cambodia .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li>In the Philippines, it aired on <a href="/wiki/GMA_Network" title="GMA Network">GMA Network</a> in 2011, and re-run in 2015. Then from October 17 to November 25, 2016, the series re-aired on <a href="/wiki/GMA_News_TV" title="GMA News TV">GMA News TV</a> replacing <i><a href="/wiki/The_1st_Shop_of_Coffee_Prince" title="The 1st Shop of Coffee Prince">Coffee Prince</a></i>.</li>
<li>In Indonesia, it aired on <a href="/wiki/Indosiar" title="Indosiar">Indosiar</a> in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In 2015, it was re-aired on the <a href="/wiki/GMA_Network" title="GMA Network">same network</a> replacing the Mexican telenovela <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_at_Heart_(telenovela)" title="Wild at Heart (telenovela)">Corazón indomable</a></i>, shuffled with <i><a href="/wiki/Two_Mothers_(TV_series)" title="Two Mothers (TV series)">Two Mothers</a></i>. It is also available to stream on <a href="/wiki/Iflix" title="Iflix">Iflix</a> with subtitles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></li>
<li>In Japan, it first aired on cable channel KNTV from August 29 to September 23, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Reairings followed on cable channel BS Premium in 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and on major network <a href="/wiki/NHK" title="NHK">NHK</a> in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li>In Malaysia, it aired on <a href="/wiki/8TV_(Malaysia)" title="8TV (Malaysia)">8TV</a> (dubbed in Mandarin with Malay subtitles) later in NTV7 in original Korean; <a href="/wiki/Astro_Prima" title="Astro Prima">Astro Prima</a> (in original Korean with Malay subtitles); <a href="/wiki/Shuang_Xing" title="Shuang Xing">Astro Shuang Xing</a> (in both original Korean and dubbed in Mandarin, with Malay and Chinese subtitles); and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ONE_TV_ASIA" title="ONE TV ASIA">ONE TV ASIA</a> (in original Korean and dubbed in Mandarin or Malay, with English, Chinese and Malay subtitles). It is also available to stream on <a href="/wiki/Iflix" title="Iflix">Iflix</a> with subtitles.</li>
<li>In Vietnam, it began airing on October 29, 2012 on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HTV2" title="HTV2">HTV2</a> under the name "Khu vườn bí ẩn".</li>
<li>In Chile, it began airing on 2012 on Etc...TV and sister station <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MEGA" title="MEGA">MEGA</a> under the name "Jardín Secreto"</li>
<li>In the United States, it was dubbed in Spanish and aired on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MundoFox" title="MundoFox">MundoFox</a> retitled as <i>Jardin secreto</i>.</li>
<li>In Cuba, it aired on the state-run network Canal Habana in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
<li>In India, it aired on TV channel <a href="/wiki/Puthuyugam_TV" title="Puthuyugam TV">Puthuyugam TV</a>, from 11 June 2015 dubbed in <a href="/wiki/Tamil_language" title="Tamil language">Tamil</a> replacing <i><a href="/wiki/To_the_Beautiful_You" title="To the Beautiful You">To the Beautiful You</a></i>.</li>
<li>In Thailand, it aired on <a href="/wiki/Channel_7_(Thailand)" title="Channel 7 (Thailand)">Channel 7</a>, from December 15, 2012 dubbed in <a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> It is also available to stream on <a href="/wiki/Iflix" title="Iflix">Iflix</a> with subtitles.</li>
<li>In Sri Lanka, the drama premiered on <a href="/wiki/TV_One_(Sri_Lanka)" title="TV One (Sri Lanka)">TV One</a> on August 15, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sinhalese_(language)" title="Sinhalese (language)">Sinhalese language</a> theme song, "Me Adambarakari" performed by Gayan &amp; Shanika premiered on the network's official <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> page on June 21, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> It is also available to stream on <a href="/wiki/Iflix" title="Iflix">Iflix</a> with subtitles.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Remake">Remake</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secret_Garden_(South_Korean_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Remake">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In China, a film remake with the same title is released in 2012 starring <a href="/wiki/Wallace_Chung" title="Wallace Chung">Wallace Chung</a>, Tan Weiwei and Korean singer <a href="/wiki/Kangta" title="Kangta">Kangta</a>.</p>
<p>In Thailand, a remake with the title <i>Secret Garden Thailand</i> is released in 2016 starring <a href="/wiki/Ananda_Everingham" title="Ananda Everingham">Ananda Everingham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pimchanok_Luevisadpaibul" title="Pimchanok Luevisadpaibul">Pimchanok Luevisadpaibul</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
