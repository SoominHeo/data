<p>The <b>City of Chelmsford</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/tʃ/ 'ch' in 'china'">tʃ</span><span title="/ɛ/ short 'e' in 'bed'">ɛ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="'z' in 'Zion'">z</span><span title="'f' in 'find'">f</span><span title="/ər/ 'er' in 'finger'">ər</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span></span>/</a></span></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">local government district</a> in <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It is named after its main settlement, <a href="/wiki/Chelmsford" title="Chelmsford">Chelmsford</a>, which is also the <a href="/wiki/County_town" title="County town">county town</a> of Essex. On 1 June 2012 Chelmsford was granted <a href="/wiki/City_status_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="City status in the United Kingdom">city status</a> to mark the <a href="/wiki/Diamond_Jubilee_of_Elizabeth_II" title="Diamond Jubilee of Elizabeth II">Diamond Jubilee of Elizabeth II</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-city1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-city1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-city2_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-city2-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=City_of_Chelmsford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The current district was formed on 1 April 1974 from the borough of Chelmsford, and most of the <a href="/wiki/Chelmsford_Rural_District" title="Chelmsford Rural District">Chelmsford Rural District</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chelmsford_City_Council">Chelmsford City Council</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=City_of_Chelmsford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Chelmsford City Council">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">mayor</a> of the city is Councillor Paul Hutchinson and is the first citizen of the borough and an ambassador for Chelmsford.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> A cabinet of ten <a href="/wiki/Councillor" title="Councillor">councillors</a>, from the 57 that constitute the council, are led by Cllr Roy Whitehead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chelmsford_local_elections" title="Chelmsford local elections">Local elections</a> are held every four years in the borough. After being under <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democrats" title="Liberal Democrats">Liberal Democrat</a> control between 1995 and 1999, the council moved to <a href="/wiki/No_overall_control" title="No overall control">no overall control</a> before coming the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative party</a> gained a majority at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chelmsford_Council_election,_2003&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chelmsford Council election, 2003 (page does not exist)">2003 elections</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Boundaries">Boundaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=City_of_Chelmsford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Boundaries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>To the north-west it borders the district of <a href="/wiki/Uttlesford" title="Uttlesford">Uttlesford</a> and to the north-east the boundary is with the district of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Braintree_(district)" title="Braintree (district)">Braintree</a>. It shares its eastern border with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maldon_(district)" title="Maldon (district)">Maldon</a>. To the south-east its borders the district of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rochford_(district)" title="Rochford (district)">Rochford</a>, to the south the district of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basildon_(district)" title="Basildon (district)">Basildon</a> and to the south-west it the borough of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brentwood_(borough)" title="Brentwood (borough)">Brentwood</a>. To the west the borough has a boundary with the district of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Epping_Forest_(district)" title="Epping Forest (district)">Epping Forest</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=City_of_Chelmsford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The total population of the area covered by the borough is 157,748 (ONS 2004 estimate). Approximately 60,000 live in the city and over 42,400 in the surrounding suburban and semi-rural parishes. Around 16,600 live in <a href="/wiki/South_Woodham_Ferrers" title="South Woodham Ferrers">South Woodham Ferrers</a>. The remainder of the population live in the surrounding rural areas, including the villages of <a href="/wiki/Galleywood" title="Galleywood">Galleywood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bicknacre" title="Bicknacre">Bicknacre</a>, East, West and <a href="/wiki/South_Hanningfield" title="South Hanningfield">South Hanningfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boreham" title="Boreham">Boreham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ford_End" title="Ford End">Ford End</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pleshey" title="Pleshey">Pleshey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Highwood,_Essex" title="Highwood, Essex">Highwood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Good_Easter" title="Good Easter">Good Easter</a>, <a href="/wiki/High_Easter" title="High Easter">High Easter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chignal_St_James" title="Chignal St James">Chignal St James</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chignal_Smealy" title="Chignal Smealy">Chignal Smealy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Howe_Green,_Chelmsford" title="Howe Green, Chelmsford">Howe Green</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roxwell" title="Roxwell">Roxwell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Great_Leighs" title="Great Leighs">Great Leighs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Little_Leighs" title="Little Leighs">Little Leighs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Broomfield,_Essex" title="Broomfield, Essex">Broomfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Great_Waltham" title="Great Waltham">Great Waltham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Little_Waltham" title="Little Waltham">Little Waltham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Great_Baddow" title="Great Baddow">Great Baddow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Little_Baddow" title="Little Baddow">Little Baddow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Danbury,_Essex" title="Danbury, Essex">Danbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sandon,_Essex" title="Sandon, Essex">Sandon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rettendon" title="Rettendon">Rettendon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Runwell" title="Runwell">Runwell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Margaretting" title="Margaretting">Margaretting</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stock,_Essex" title="Stock, Essex">Stock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Writtle" title="Writtle">Writtle</a>. (Source: population derived from 2001 census). However this number is expected to rise dramatically, with 40,000 homes to be built in the metropolitan area of the borough which could bring the size of Chelmsford up to over 170,000 to be concentrated around Boreham Airfield, Broomfield, North Springfield and Beaulieu Park. These communities would be primarily built as an urban village design; a place to work, live and play.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=City_of_Chelmsford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The borough has two major centres, the principal settlement <a href="/wiki/Chelmsford" title="Chelmsford">Chelmsford</a> in the centre and the town of <a href="/wiki/South_Woodham_Ferrers" title="South Woodham Ferrers">South Woodham Ferrers</a> to the south east. The north west of the borough is predominantly rural. Villages are found throughout the borough.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/River_Chelmer" title="River Chelmer">River Chelmer</a> flows into the borough near North End and flows to Chelmsford where the <a href="/wiki/River_Can" title="River Can">River Can</a> converges with it at the city centre, the River Chelmer then flows out of the borough between Boreham and Little Baddow. The River Can's source is in the north west of the borough. The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=River_Wid&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="River Wid (page does not exist)">River Wid</a> flows from the South of the borough through <a href="/wiki/Widford,_Essex" title="Widford, Essex">Widford</a> towards the centre past Writtle to join the River Can between Writtle and Chelmsford. The <a href="/wiki/River_Crouch" title="River Crouch">River Crouch</a> flows along the part of the south-eastern border below South Woodham Ferrers. <a href="/wiki/Hanningfield_Reservoir" title="Hanningfield Reservoir">Hanningfield Reservoir</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">Site of Special Scientific Interest</a>, is located in the south of the borough.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=City_of_Chelmsford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The rail links, both national and international, which run through the borough, are all operated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abellio_Greater_Anglia" title="Abellio Greater Anglia">Abellio Greater Anglia</a>. Two national rail services operate. The <a href="/wiki/Great_Eastern_Main_Line" title="Great Eastern Main Line">Great Eastern Main Line</a> runs through the centre, stopping at <a href="/wiki/Chelmsford_railway_station" title="Chelmsford railway station">Chelmsford railway station</a>. Services via Chelmsford operate between <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Street_station" title="Liverpool Street station">London Liverpool Street</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ipswich" title="Ipswich">Ipswich</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clacton" title="Clacton">Clacton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harwich" title="Harwich">Harwich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Braintree,_Essex" title="Braintree, Essex">Braintree</a> or <a href="/wiki/Norwich" title="Norwich">Norwich</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crouch_Valley_Line" title="Crouch Valley Line">Crouch Valley Line</a> also runs through the far south-east tip of the borough, stopping at <a href="/wiki/South_Woodham_Ferrers" title="South Woodham Ferrers">South Woodham Ferrers</a> running services between <a href="/wiki/Wickford" title="Wickford">Wickford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southminster" title="Southminster">Southminster</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Dutchflyer" title="Dutchflyer">Amsterdam Express</a>, a rail/sea/rail international service owned by also stops in the borough at Chelmsford, connecting the town to the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, via the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a>.</p>
<p>There are several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primary_status" title="Primary status">primary routes</a> within the borough. One of these, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A12_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A12 road (Great Britain)">A12</a>, is also a <a href="/wiki/Trunk_road" title="Trunk road">trunk road</a> and runs from <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and the <a href="/wiki/M25_motorway" title="M25 motorway">M25</a>, centrally in a north-easterly direction through the borough bypassing the city of Chelmsford onwards to <a href="/wiki/Suffolk" title="Suffolk">Suffolk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norfolk" title="Norfolk">Norfolk</a>. Two other significant primary routes are the <a href="/wiki/A130_road" title="A130 road">A130</a> which runs north-south across <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A414" title="A414">A414</a>, which begins as a primary route in Chelmsford but its terminus us Maldon in Essex. The A414 then runs west through Essex, meets the <a href="/wiki/M11_motorway" title="M11 motorway">M11</a> and then its path continues into <a href="/wiki/Hertfordshire" title="Hertfordshire">Hertfordshire</a> via <a href="/wiki/Harlow" title="Harlow">Harlow</a>. Various other <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain_road_numbering_scheme" title="Great Britain road numbering scheme">A- and B-Roads</a> connect the borough, especially the city of Chelmsford to the rest of the county and beyond.</p>
<p>There is, additionally, a large integrated bus network provided primarily by <a href="/wiki/First_Essex" title="First Essex">First Essex</a> which connects the city to towns and villages across the county. Other smaller operators also provide services, such as Stephensons of Essex and TGM Group to name a few.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=City_of_Chelmsford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Henry_VIII_of_England" title="Henry VIII of England">Henry VIII</a>'s former <a href="/wiki/Palace_of_Beaulieu" title="Palace of Beaulieu">Palace of Beaulieu</a> is situated in <a href="/wiki/Boreham" title="Boreham">Boreham</a>, now occupied by the <a href="/wiki/New_Hall_School" title="New Hall School">New Hall School</a>. Other places to visit in the borough include the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RHS_Garden,_Hyde_Hall" title="RHS Garden, Hyde Hall">RHS Garden, Hyde Hall</a> at Rettendon, and numerous open spaces in Chelmsford, including Admirals and Central Parks. <a href="/wiki/Writtle" title="Writtle">Writtle</a>, where <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_I_of_Scotland" title="Robert I of Scotland">Robert the Bruce</a> is said to have married his second wife <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_de_Burgh" title="Elizabeth de Burgh">Elizabeth de Burgh</a> in 1302, has English Royal connections, with King John building a hunting lodge there in 1211. Much of the site now lies within the grounds of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Writtle_College" title="Writtle College">Writtle College</a>, the internationally famous centre for horticulture and agriculture. A few miles away is the attractive village of <a href="/wiki/Pleshey" title="Pleshey">Pleshey</a>, where stand the ruins of a once important castle mentioned in <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>'s play <i><a href="/wiki/Richard_II_(play)" title="Richard II (play)">Richard II</a></i>. The entire circuit of the castle walls can still be traced in the village streets. American tourists often visit the village of <a href="/wiki/Springfield,_Essex" title="Springfield, Essex">Springfield, Essex</a>, the origin of "<a href="/wiki/Springfield_(toponym)" title="Springfield (toponym)">Springfield</a>" as a popular place name, first giving its name to the important City of <a href="/wiki/Springfield,_Massachusetts" title="Springfield, Massachusetts">Springfield, Massachusetts</a>, and subsequently <a href="/wiki/Springfield,_Illinois" title="Springfield, Illinois">Springfield, Illinois</a>, the state capital of <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Springfield,_Missouri" title="Springfield, Missouri">Springfield, Missouri</a>, among numerous others.</p>
