<p><b>The Citizen Athletic Association</b> (<a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%85%AC" title="wikt:公">公</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%B0%91" title="wikt:民">民</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E9%AB%94" title="wikt:體">體</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E8%82%B2" title="wikt:育">育</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%9C%83" title="wikt:會">會</a></span>), simply known as <b>Citizen</b> or <b>TCAA</b>, is a Hong Kong <a href="/wiki/Sports_club" title="Sports club">athletic club</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Tsing_Yi_Sports_Ground" title="Tsing Yi Sports Ground">Tsing Yi</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> team plays in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_First_Division_League" title="Hong Kong First Division League">Hong Kong First Division League</a> and is the holder of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_FA_Cup_2007%E2%80%9308" title="Hong Kong FA Cup 2007–08">Hong Kong FA Cup in 2007–08</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Senior_Challenge_Shield" title="Hong Kong Senior Challenge Shield">Hong Kong Senior Challenge Shield</a> champions.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_history">Recent history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Citizen_AA&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Recent history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.9308_season">2007–08 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Citizen_AA&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2007–08 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Citizen won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007-08_Hong_Kong_FA_Cup" title="2007-08 Hong Kong FA Cup">2007-08 Hong Kong FA Cup</a>. The club beat <a href="/wiki/Tai_Po_FC" title="Tai Po FC">Tai Po FC</a> in the final by 2:0.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.9310_season">2009–10 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Citizen_AA&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2009–10 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Due to <a href="/wiki/Mong_Kok_Stadium" title="Mong Kok Stadium">Mong Kok Stadium</a>'s renovation, Citizen used the <a href="/wiki/Siu_Sai_Wan_Sports_Ground" title="Siu Sai Wan Sports Ground">Siu Sai Wan Sports Ground</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009-10_Hong_Kong_First_Division_League" title="2009-10 Hong Kong First Division League">2009-10 Hong Kong First Division League</a> season for its home games. Former South China striker <a href="/wiki/Detinho" title="Detinho">Detinho</a> joined the club.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.9311_season">2010–11 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Citizen_AA&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010–11 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Citizen won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010-11_Hong_Kong_Senior_Challenge_Shield" title="2010-11 Hong Kong Senior Challenge Shield">2010-11 Hong Kong Senior Challenge Shield</a> by beating <a href="/wiki/South_China_AA" title="South China AA">South China AA</a> in the final after penalty kicks, thus earning the club the right to represent Hong Kong in the <a href="/wiki/2012_AFC_Cup" title="2012 AFC Cup">2012 AFC Cup</a>. To celebrate this victory, the club announced a prize fund of HK$800,000 (approx US$100,000), an <a href="/wiki/IPhone_4" title="IPhone 4">iPhone 4</a> for every player and an end-of-season vacation for the whole team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.9312_season">2011–12 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Citizen_AA&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2011–12 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The club moved from the remote <a href="/wiki/Siu_Sai_Wan_Sports_Ground" title="Siu Sai Wan Sports Ground">Siu Sai Wan Sports Ground</a> to the renovated <a href="/wiki/Mong_Kok_Stadium" title="Mong Kok Stadium">Mong Kok Stadium</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011-12_Hong_Kong_First_Division_League" title="2011-12 Hong Kong First Division League">2011-12 Hong Kong First Division League</a> season. To compete in both local football and AFC Cup, Citizen has expanded its squad to include 27 players, adding <a href="/wiki/Michael_Campion" title="Michael Campion">Michael Campion</a>, <a href="/wiki/So_Loi_Keung" title="So Loi Keung">So Loi Keung</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chiu_Chun_Kit" title="Chiu Chun Kit">Chiu Chun Kit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amaury_Nunes" title="Amaury Nunes">Amaury Nunes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yuto_Nakamura" title="Yuto Nakamura">Yuto Nakamura</a> to the club, while promoting two young players Chan Si-Chun and Yeung Ho Wan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Citizen is spending an unprecedented HK$400,000 on the team's 12 days pre-season training camp in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, Canada, during which time the club will play 3 warm-up games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The club announced on 7 September that <a href="/wiki/Rasonic" title="Rasonic">Rasonic</a> has become its new shirt sponsor for the 2011–12 and 2012–13 seasons. The sponsorship is worth HK$1 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.9314_season">2013–14 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Citizen_AA&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2013–14 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The club moved from <a href="/wiki/Mong_Kok_Stadium" title="Mong Kok Stadium">Mong Kok Stadium</a> to <a href="/wiki/Tsing_Yi_Sports_Ground" title="Tsing Yi Sports Ground">Tsing Yi Sports Ground</a>, which Citizen will share with <a href="/wiki/Sun_Hei_SC" title="Sun Hei SC">Sun Hei SC</a> as their common home ground.</p>
