<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Siheung County</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Siheung County</b> (formally officially written as <b>Shihŭng</b> until 2000) was a <a href="/wiki/County" title="County">county</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gyeonggi_Province" title="Gyeonggi Province">Gyeonggi Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. This county was abolished in 1989 as its one town(읍) of Sorae and two townships(면) of Gunja and Suam became <a href="/wiki/Siheung" title="Siheung">Siheung</a> City at the same time. Today's Siheung City area did not belong to Siheung County before 1914. The area rather was part of old <a href="/wiki/Incheon" title="Incheon">Incheon</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ansan" title="Ansan">Ansan</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Original_Siheung.2C_prior_to_1914">Original Siheung, prior to 1914</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Siheung_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Original Siheung, prior to 1914">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Today's eastern part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guro-gu" title="Guro-gu">Guro-gu</a>, most part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yeongdeungpo-gu" title="Yeongdeungpo-gu">Yeongdeungpo-gu</a>(excepting Yeouido and Yanghwa-dong), most part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gwanak-gu" title="Gwanak-gu">Gwanak-gu</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geumcheon-gu" title="Geumcheon-gu">Geumcheon-gu</a>, part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dongjak-gu" title="Dongjak-gu">Dongjak-gu</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gwangmyeong" title="Gwangmyeong">Gwangmyeong</a> City, and some part(Seoksu-dong and Bakdal-dong) of <a href="/wiki/Anyang" title="Anyang">Anyang</a> City in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gyeonggi" title="Gyeonggi">Gyeonggi</a> belonged to the "original" Siheung Country, before the rearrangement of administrative districts by the Japanese colonial government in 1914. The county office was in <a href="/wiki/Siheung-dong" title="Siheung-dong">Siheung-dong</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geumcheon-gu" title="Geumcheon-gu">Geumcheon-gu</a>, Seoul.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Goguryeo" title="Goguryeo">Goguryeo</a> era, this area was called "Ingbeollo(잉벌노)"(or Neumnae, 늠내), in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unified_Silla_Dynasty" title="Unified Silla Dynasty">Unified Silla Dynasty</a> era Gogyang-hyeon (穀壤縣), and in <a href="/wiki/Goryeo" title="Goryeo">Goryeo</a> era its name changed to "Geumju(금주, 衿州)." In 1413, it was rebranded as Geumcheon(금천, 衿川) Prefecture and its name changed again "Siheung" in 1785.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The prefecture became a county as the rearrangement of administrative districts in 1895 was implemented.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Most part of <a href="/wiki/Anyang" title="Anyang">Anyang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gunpo" title="Gunpo">Gunpo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gwacheon" title="Gwacheon">Gwacheon</a> in Gyeonggi, and part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dongjak-gu" title="Dongjak-gu">Dongjak-gu</a>, most of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seocho-gu" title="Seocho-gu">Seocho-gu</a>, Namhyeon-dong of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gwanak-gu" title="Gwanak-gu">Gwanak-gu</a> in Seoul consisted of <a href="/wiki/Gwacheon" title="Gwacheon">Gwacheon</a> County; southern part of today's southern part of <a href="/wiki/Siheung" title="Siheung">Siheung</a> City and most of <a href="/wiki/Ansan" title="Ansan">Ansan</a> City belonged to <a href="/wiki/Ansan" title="Ansan">Ansan</a> County. Northern part of <a href="/wiki/Siheung" title="Siheung">Siheung</a> City belonged to <a href="/wiki/Incheon" title="Incheon">Incheon</a> Metropolitan Prefecture (i.e. Incheon Dohobu, 인천도호부).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1914, the two counties of Gwacheon and Ansan were annexed into Siheung County as part of rearrangement policy of administrative districts.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_districts_under_original_Siheung_County">Administrative districts under original Siheung County</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Siheung_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative districts under original Siheung County">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>South Township (남면) - western <a href="/wiki/Gwangmyeong" title="Gwangmyeong">Gwangmyeong</a></li>
<li>West Township (서면) - eastern Gwangmyeong</li>
<li>Upper-North Township (상북면) - the west part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yeongdeungpo-gu" title="Yeongdeungpo-gu">Yeongdeungpo-gu</a> and the eastern part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guro-gu" title="Guro-gu">Guro-gu</a></li>
<li>Lower-North Township (하북면) - the east part of Yeongdeungpo-gu (including <a href="/wiki/Yeongdeungpo_Station" title="Yeongdeungpo Station">"central" Yeongdeungpo</a>, but excepting <a href="/wiki/Yeouido" title="Yeouido">Yeouido</a>) and Sindaebang-dong, Daebang-dong in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dongjak-gu" title="Dongjak-gu">Dongjak-gu</a></li>
<li>East Township (동면) - Gasan-dong and Doksan-dong in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geumcheon-gu" title="Geumcheon-gu">Geumcheon-gu</a>, most part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gwanak-gu" title="Gwanak-gu">Gwanak-gu</a>(except for today's Namhyeon-dong) and Sangdo-dong in Dongjak-gu</li>
<li>County Centre Township (군내면) - Siheung-dong in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geumcheon-gu" title="Geumcheon-gu">Geumcheon-gu</a>(the county centre ) and Seoksu-dong, Bakdal-dong in <a href="/wiki/Anyang" title="Anyang">Anyang</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="After_1914_and_the_end_in_1989">After 1914 and the end in 1989</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Siheung_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: After 1914 and the end in 1989">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>In 1914, <a href="/wiki/Ansan" title="Ansan">Ansan</a> County and <a href="/wiki/Gwacheon" title="Gwacheon">Gwacheon</a> County were annexed to Siheung County</b> as a result of the reorganisation scheme of districts by the Japanese colonial government in Korea. Throughout modern Korean history, many parts of Siheung County were separated or annexed into/to several administrative districts. In 1936, the township of Yeongdeungpo and some part of North township (now most part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dongjak-gu" title="Dongjak-gu">Dongjak-gu</a>) were annexed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gyeongseong" title="Gyeongseong">Gyeongseongbu</a>, or colonial <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1949, the villages(리) of Guro and Sindorim were annexed to Seoul.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1963, Seoul absorbed several Siheung County townships of East (now <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geumcheon-gu" title="Geumcheon-gu">Geumcheon-gu</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gwanak-gu" title="Gwanak-gu">Gwanak-gu</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dongjak-gu" title="Dongjak-gu">Dongjak-gu</a>) and Sindong (now <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seocho-gu" title="Seocho-gu">Seocho-gu</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> while Hwaseong County's township of <a href="/wiki/Uiwang" title="Uiwang">Uiwang</a> was absorbed by Siheung<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and the county office in Yeongdeungpo moved to Anyang township. In 1973, the township of <a href="/wiki/Anyang" title="Anyang">Anyang</a> separated as <a href="/wiki/Anyang" title="Anyang">Anyang</a> City and <b>Sorae township of <a href="/wiki/Bucheon" title="Bucheon">Bucheon</a> County, now northern part of Siheung City, was ceded to Siheung County</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 1981, the township of Soha and the county branch of Gwangmyeong, instead of being annexed to Seoul, became <a href="/wiki/Gwangmyeong" title="Gwangmyeong">Gwangmyeong</a> City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 1986, the county branch of <a href="/wiki/Gwacheon" title="Gwacheon">Gwacheon</a>(formerly Gwacheon township) became <a href="/wiki/Gwacheon" title="Gwacheon">Gwacheon</a> City, while some part of Gunja and Suam townships were annexed into a new-born city, <a href="/wiki/Ansan" title="Ansan">Ansan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In 1989, Siheung County was abolished and its townships became <a href="/wiki/Siheung" title="Siheung">Siheung</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gunpo" title="Gunpo">Gunpo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Uiwang" title="Uiwang">Uiwang</a> City respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
