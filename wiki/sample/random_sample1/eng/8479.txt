<p><b>Siheung</b> <small>Korean: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Korean" title="Help:IPA for Korean">[ɕʰihɯŋ]</a></span> is a <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_South_Korea" title="Administrative divisions of South Korea">city</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gyeonggi" title="Gyeonggi">Gyeonggi</a> Province, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>.</p>
<p>In the early <a href="/wiki/Three_Kingdoms_of_Korea" title="Three Kingdoms of Korea">Three Kingdoms period</a>, the Siheung area was part of the <a href="/wiki/Baekje" title="Baekje">Baekje</a> kingdom. It was lost to <a href="/wiki/Goguryeo" title="Goguryeo">Goguryeo</a> along with the Han River valley in 475.</p>
<p>Siheung acquired its current administrative identity on January 1, 1989, when the old <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_South_Korea" title="Administrative divisions of South Korea">county</a> of Siheung (<a href="/wiki/Siheung_County" title="Siheung County">Siheung County</a>) was divided into the cities of Siheung, <a href="/wiki/Gunpo" title="Gunpo">Gunpo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Uiwang" title="Uiwang">Uiwang</a>. At the time Siheung city had a population of only 93,000. This number more than tripled in the following decade, as massive suburban apartment complexes were developed.</p>
<p>Today's Siheung City area belonged to old Incheon and Ansan before 1914.</p>
<p>Notable landmarks include <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Oi_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Oi Island (page does not exist)">Oi Island</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mount_Sorae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mount Sorae (page does not exist)">Mount Sorae</a>, and the port and inlet of <a href="/wiki/Wolgot" title="Wolgot">Wolgot</a>. Siheung includes two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_town" title="New town">new cities</a> that were developed in the 1990s, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sihwa_New_City&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sihwa New City (page does not exist)">Sihwa</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Wolgot_New_City&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Wolgot New City (page does not exist)">Wolgot New City</a>. The new city of Sihwa includes a very large industrial complex. Companies headquartered there include <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hansung_Machinery_Co.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hansung Machinery Co. (page does not exist)">Hansung Machinery Co.</a> <a href="/wiki/Korea_Polytechnic_University" title="Korea Polytechnic University">Korea Polytechnic University</a> established in 1997, and also <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kyonggi_Institute_of_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kyonggi Institute of Technology (page does not exist)">Kyonggi Institute of Technology</a> established in 1998.</p>
<p>In 2006, <a href="/wiki/Korea_Polytechnic_University" title="Korea Polytechnic University">Korea Polytechnic University</a> built TIP (Techno Innovation Park). The building has 18 floors used for studying, research, and development for students, faculty, companies and government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2010 <a href="/wiki/Seoul_National_University" title="Seoul National University">Seoul National University</a> acquired 826 thousand square-meters or 204 acres of Siheung property to establish its global campus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The university plans to build a bio-medical complex, research hospital, apartments, and education-related infrastructure including lecture halls, dormitories, and an international middle and high school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_districts">Administrative districts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Siheung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Administrative districts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

As of 2013, the population of the city is 421,192 people including 156,945 households.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recreation_.26_Tourism">Recreation &amp; Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Siheung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Recreation &amp; Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Siheung_Lotus_Theme_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Siheung Lotus Theme Park (page does not exist)">Siheung Lotus Theme Park</a></li>
</ul>
<p>The Lotus Theme Park can be visited year-round by bikers and pedestrians, but the peak months for seeing the lotus plants in bloom are July and August. It is also the site of a summer festival. Seven varieties of lotus and over thirty varieties of waterlilies are cultivated at the park.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Admission to the theme park during the summer months is free, and lotus viewing tours take place from 9:00 a.m. to 2:00 p.m. local time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The theme park can be reached by several city bus routes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Siheung Gaetgol Ecology Park</li>
</ul>
<p>There is inland <a href="/wiki/Mudflat" title="Mudflat">gaetgol</a> that is rare in the world. This gaetgol is exposed twice a day at low tide. It is called serpentinizing mudflat because it looks like snake.</p>
