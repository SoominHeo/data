<p><b>Flora</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Plant" title="Plant">plant</a> life occurring in a particular region or time, generally the naturally occurring or <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_(ecology)" title="Indigenous (ecology)">indigenous</a>—<a href="/wiki/Native_plant" title="Native plant">native plant</a> life. The corresponding term for <a href="/wiki/Animal" title="Animal">animal</a> life is <a href="/wiki/Fauna" title="Fauna">fauna</a>. <i>Flora</i>, <i>fauna</i> and other forms of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_(biology)" title="Kingdom (biology)">life</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Fungus" title="Fungus">fungi</a> are collectively referred to as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biota_(taxonomy)" title="Biota (taxonomy)">biota</a>. Sometimes bacteria and fungi are also referred to as flora, as in the terms <a href="/wiki/Gut_flora" title="Gut flora">gut flora</a> or <a href="/wiki/Skin_flora" title="Skin flora">skin flora</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flora&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The word "flora" comes from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> name of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flora_(mythology)" title="Flora (mythology)">Flora</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Goddess" title="Goddess">goddess</a> of <a href="/wiki/Plant" title="Plant">plants</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flower" title="Flower">flowers</a>, and fertility in <a href="/wiki/Roman_mythology" title="Roman mythology">Roman mythology</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="may be the other way round, i.e. the goddess was named using a pre-existing word for flowers (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The distinction between vegetation (the general appearance of a community) and flora (the taxonomic composition of a community) was first made by <a href="/wiki/Jules_Thurmann" title="Jules Thurmann">Jules Thurmann</a> (1849). Prior to this, the two terms were used indiscriminately.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Martins_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Martins-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora_classifications">Flora classifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flora&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Flora classifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Plants are grouped into floras based on region (<a href="/wiki/Phytochorion" title="Phytochorion">floristic regions</a>), period, special environment, or climate. Regions can be geographically distinct <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitats</a> like mountain vs. flatland. Floras can mean plant life of a historic era as in <i>fossil flora</i>. Lastly, floras may be subdivided by special environments:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>Native flora</i>. The native and indigenous flora of an area.</li>
<li><i>Agricultural and horticultural flora (garden flora)</i>. The plants that are deliberately grown by humans.</li>
<li><i>Weed flora</i>. Traditionally this classification was applied to plants regarded as undesirable, and studied in efforts to control or eradicate them. Today the designation is less often used as a classification of plant life, since it includes three different types of plants: <a href="/wiki/Weed" title="Weed">weedy</a> species, <a href="/wiki/Invasive_species" title="Invasive species">invasive species</a> (that may or may not be weedy), and native and introduced non-weedy species that are agriculturally undesirable. Many native plants previously considered weeds have been shown to be beneficial or even necessary to various <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystems</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Documentation_of_floras">Documentation of floras</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flora&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Documentation of floras">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Flora_(publication)" title="Flora (publication)">Flora (publication)</a></div>

<p>The flora of a particular area or time period can be documented in a publication also known as a "<a href="/wiki/Flora_(publication)" title="Flora (publication)">flora</a>" (often capitalized as "Flora" to distinguish the two meanings when they might be confused). Floras may require specialist botanical knowledge to use with any effectiveness. Traditionally they are <a href="/wiki/Book" title="Book">books</a>, but some are now published on <a href="/wiki/CD-ROM" title="CD-ROM">CD-ROM</a> or <a href="/wiki/Website" title="Website">websites</a>.</p>
<p>It is said that the <i><a href="/wiki/Flora_Sinensis" title="Flora Sinensis">Flora Sinensis</a></i> by the Polish <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jesuit" title="Jesuit">Jesuit</a> <a href="/wiki/Micha%C5%82_Boym" title="Michał Boym">Michał Boym</a> was the first book that used the name "Flora" in this meaning, a book covering the plant world of a region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-boym_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-boym-6">[6]</a></sup> However, despite its title it covered not only plants, but also some animals of the region.</p>
<p>A published flora often contains diagnostic keys. Often these are <a href="/wiki/Identification_key" title="Identification key"><i>dichotomous</i> keys</a>, which require the user to repeatedly examine a plant, and decide which one of two alternatives given best applies to the plant.</p>
