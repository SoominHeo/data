<p><i><b>Let's Eat</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">식샤를 합시다</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Siksyareul Habsida</span></i>) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">television series</a> starring <a href="/wiki/Lee_Soo-kyung" title="Lee Soo-kyung">Lee Soo-kyung</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yoon_Doo-joon" title="Yoon Doo-joon">Yoon Doo-joon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shim_Hyung-tak" title="Shim Hyung-tak">Shim Hyung-tak</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yoon_So-hee" title="Yoon So-hee">Yoon So-hee</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It aired on <a href="/wiki/TVN_(South_Korea)" title="TVN (South Korea)">tvN</a> from November 28, 2013 to March 13, 2014 on Thursdays at 23:00 for 16 episodes. The series is about four single people living in one apartment complex who are brought together by their love of food.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Let%27s_Eat_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Four single people: happily divorced <a href="/wiki/Paralegal" title="Paralegal">paralegal</a> Lee Soo-kyung (<a href="/wiki/Lee_Soo-kyung" title="Lee Soo-kyung">Lee Soo-kyung</a>), mysterious <a href="/wiki/Gourmand" title="Gourmand">gourmand</a> Goo Dae-young (<a href="/wiki/Yoon_Doo-joon" title="Yoon Doo-joon">Yoon Doo-joon</a>), design student and former rich girl Yoon Jin-yi (<a href="/wiki/Yoon_So-hee" title="Yoon So-hee">Yoon So-hee</a>), and petty lawyer Kim Hak-moon (<a href="/wiki/Shim_Hyung-tak" title="Shim Hyung-tak">Shim Hyung-tak</a>). Who enjoy living alone, except for that pesky problem that dining out is not designed for one. At Jin-yi's request, she, Soo-kyung and Dae-young start eating out together and thus get involved in each other's lives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Let%27s_Eat_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The drama features eating scenes of the characters who live alone. Park Joon-hwa, producer-director of the drama, said "The drama focuses on building relationships between strangers through having a meal and ultimately relieving their solitude. It portrays the process of how people improve relations via food", and further explained that "Korean dramas have lots of eating scenes in which conflict erupts or settles down".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
