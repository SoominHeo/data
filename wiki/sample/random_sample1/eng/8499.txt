<p><i><b>Singijeon</b></i> or <i><b>shinkichon</b></i> (<i>magical machine arrows</i>) was a type of <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korean</a> (<a href="/wiki/Joseon" title="Joseon">Joseon</a>) <a href="/wiki/Fire_arrow" title="Fire arrow">fire arrow</a> rocket, used during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseon_Dynasty" title="Joseon Dynasty">Joseon Dynasty</a> (1392–1897). Multiple <i>singijeon</i> could be launched by <i><a href="/wiki/Hwacha" title="Hwacha">hwacha</a></i> (multiple rocket launcher).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Singijeon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the late 14th century, in order to gain ascendancy at sea against Japanese pirates (<i><a href="/wiki/Wokou" title="Wokou">wokou</a></i>, also known as <i>waegu</i> in Korean), fire arrows called <i>hwajeon</i> or <i>hwajon</i> were used, which were the predecessor of the <i>singijeon</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The Koreans had tried to acquire rockets and gunpowder and their production methods from China. The Chinese, however, regarded the technology of <a href="/wiki/Gunpowder" title="Gunpowder">gunpowder</a> as a state secret and restricted access to it and trade in its nitrous raw materials (which could only be found in China). The Koreans therefore sought to acquire the manufacturing secrets of gunpowder for themselves and, in 1374 (~1376), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Choe_Mu-seon" title="Choe Mu-seon">Choe Mu-seon</a> was able to bribe a <a href="/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Han Chinese</a> merchant to obtain the secret formula for manufacturing <a href="/wiki/Gunpowder" title="Gunpowder">gunpowder</a>, as well as technical knowledge about <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_of_Science_and_Technology_in_China" title="History of Science and Technology in China">Chinese</a> <a href="/wiki/Firearm" title="Firearm">firearm</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cannon" title="Cannon">cannon</a> technology. He also successfully extracted <a href="/wiki/Potassium_nitrate" title="Potassium nitrate">potassium nitrate</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Soil" title="Soil">soil</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and rocks from Japanese trade routes, and developed Korea's first gunpowder.</p>
<p>The <i>singijeon</i> saw most of its early use in the northern borders of Joseon, in the campaign to expand its northern borders by driving out the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orangkae" title="Orangkae">Orangkae</a>" ("Barbarians", especially referring the <a href="/wiki/Jurchen_people" title="Jurchen people">Jurchen people</a>). Later, its uses expanded to coastal defence against Japanese pirates and was much used throughout the conflicts during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseon_Dynasty" title="Joseon Dynasty">Joseon Dynasty</a>. During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imjin_War" title="Imjin War">Imjin War</a>, General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gwon_Yul" title="Gwon Yul">Gwon Yul</a> attributed his successful defense of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Haengju_Sansong" title="Haengju Sansong">Haengju</a> mountain fortress against numerically superior Japanese forces to the <i>singijeon</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Details of the <i>singijeon</i> were not known until very recently. Korean historians had found the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Schematics" title="Schematics">schematics</a> added as an appendix in the book <i>Gukjo Orye Seorye</i> (국조오례서례, 國朝五禮序例) but did not realize what they were until the academic Chae Yeon-suk identified them as the lost schematics of the <i>singijeon</i><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. The schematics detail the lengths of wooden materials, using units down to 0.3mm. The schematics are one of the best representations of the acute scientific understanding of the Joseon Dynasty.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Singijeon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Singijeon</i> were divided among three major types: large, medium, and small. The 52 cm long <a href="/wiki/Rocket" title="Rocket">rocket</a> was launched individually on a handheld gun, and the launch was initiated by a fuse in the tube. Even after launch, the fuse would remain in the tube, consuming the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_powder" title="Black powder">black powder</a> until it hit the "warhead" and caused detonation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-koreandb_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-koreandb-6">[6]</a></sup> The fuse length was determined by the amount of gunpowder in the paper tube, and was adjusted depending on the distance that the missile had to cover so that it would explode on the target. Its range was around 1 to 2 kilometers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-7">[7]</a></sup> The medium singijeon was of the same construction and function as the large singijeon, but, due to its smaller size of 13 cm,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-koreandb_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-koreandb-6">[6]</a></sup> its range was limited to 150 meters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chol_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-chol-8">[8]</a></sup> However, its explosive warhead was still powerful enough to make a 30 cm-deep crater in a patch of sand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-5">[5]</a></sup> The small singijeon was simply an arrow with a gunpowder pouch attached to it, and had no explosive capabilities. It was launched in multiples of 100 by a <a href="/wiki/Hwacha" title="Hwacha">hwacha</a>, and had a range of 100 meters. All gunpowder weapons including the singijeon used black powder.</p>
