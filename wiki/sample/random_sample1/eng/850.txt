<p><b>Club Deportivo Godoy Cruz Antonio Tomba</b>, known simply as <b>Godoy Cruz</b>, is an <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentine</a> <a href="/wiki/Sports_club" title="Sports club">sports club</a> from <a href="/wiki/Godoy_Cruz,_Mendoza" title="Godoy Cruz, Mendoza">Godoy Cruz, Mendoza</a>. The club is best known for its <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> team, that plays in the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Argentine Primera División">Primera División</a>, the top level of the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_football_league_system" title="Argentine football league system">Argentine football league system</a>.</p>
<p>Other activities practised at Godoy Cruz are <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Team_handball" title="Team handball">team handball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Field_hockey" title="Field hockey">field hockey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volleyball" title="Volleyball">volleyball</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Godoy_Cruz_Antonio_Tomba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Godoy Cruz was born as an institution in 1921. It all started when a group of friends, gathered in the "Victoria Bar" (located opposite the departmental square at that time), decided to found a club, taking advantage of the boom at the time . Thus was born on June 1, 1921, the Club Sportivo Godoy Cruz with the novice presidency of Don Romero Garay.</p>

<p>The club was founded on 21 June 1921 under the name sportivo Godoy Cruz, and changed to its current name on 25 April 1930 after the fusion with Deportivo Bodega Antonio Tomba. In 1959, Godoy Cruz' stadium, the [Estadio Feliciano Gambarte], was constructed. The stadium is nicknamed <i>La Bodega</i> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> for "wine cellar") and holds 21,000 people.</p>
<p>Godoy Cruz played in the regional league for several years before reaching the national level. The club won the Mendoza first division championship in 1944, 1947, 1950, 1951, 1954, 1968, and also in 1989 and 1990 that qualified to play in the defunct <i>Torneo del Interior</i> national-level tournament.</p>
<p>Winning the Torneo del Interior in 1994, Godoy Cruz reached the <a href="/wiki/Primera_B_Nacional" title="Primera B Nacional">Primera B Nacional</a> (Argentine second division) that year. After more than ten years in the second division, the club was finally promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Argentine Primera División">Primera División</a> in 2006, after winning the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_in_Argentine_football" title="2005–06 in Argentine football">2005–06 season</a> of the Primera B Nacional, defeating <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Nueva_Chicago" title="Club Atlético Nueva Chicago">Nueva Chicago</a> in the final.</p>
<p>Twenty-year-old <a href="/wiki/Enzo_P%C3%A9rez" title="Enzo Pérez">Enzo Pérez</a> scored the first goal by Godoy Cruz in the Argentine Primera, in a 1–1 draw with <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Belgrano" title="Club Atlético Belgrano">Belgrano</a> on 9 September 2006.</p>
<p>At the end of the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2006–07 Argentine Primera División">2006–07 season</a>, Godoy Cruz was relegated from the Primera after losing their promotion/relegation play-off with <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Hurac%C3%A1n" title="Club Atlético Huracán">Huracán</a>. Their stay in the second division was short, as they earned automatic promotion to the first division after finishing runners-up to <a href="/wiki/San_Mart%C3%ADn_de_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="San Martín de Tucumán">San Martín de Tucumán</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_in_Argentine_football" title="2007–08 in Argentine football">2007–08 season</a>.</p>
<p>Godoy Cruz secured their best ever league position of 3rd place under <a href="/wiki/Omar_Asad" title="Omar Asad">Omar Asad</a>'s management during the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n_season#Torneo_Clausura" title="2009–10 Argentine Primera División season">2010 Clausura</a>. In that tournament, they also achieved the best-ever point total (37) for a team indirectly affiliated to the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Football_Association" title="Argentine Football Association">Argentine Football Association</a> (meaning clubs under the administration of the Federal Council branch of AFA, which are teams outside <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greater_Buenos_Aires" title="Greater Buenos Aires">Greater Buenos Aires</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rosario" title="Rosario">Rosario</a> and <a href="/wiki/Santa_Fe,_Argentina" title="Santa Fe, Argentina">Santa Fe</a>). These results qualified them for the <a href="/wiki/2011_Copa_Libertadores" title="2011 Copa Libertadores">2011 Copa Libertadores</a>, their first appearance in this tournament.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="South_American_Tournament_Qualification">South American Tournament Qualification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Godoy_Cruz_Antonio_Tomba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: South American Tournament Qualification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Godoy Cruz is the first club in Mendoza and the fifth regional to play <a href="/wiki/CONMEBOL" title="CONMEBOL">CONMEBOL</a> tournaments. When Godoy Cruz made their <a href="/wiki/Copa_Libertadores" title="Copa Libertadores">Copa Libertadores</a> debut in February 2011 in a 2–1 win against 2008 champions <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LDU_Quito" title="LDU Quito">LDU Quito</a> they became the first indirectly affiliated side ever to represent Argentina in the Copa Libertadores. In that same group I dispute parties with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Pe%C3%B1arol" title="Club Atlético Peñarol">Peñarol</a> of Montevideo and Independente of Avellaneda. The "Tomba" was eliminated in the group stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September of 2011 plays the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Sudamericana" title="Copa Sudamericana">Copa Sudamericana</a>, arriving until 8 ° of end, after losing by penalties with <a href="/wiki/Club_Universitario_de_Deportes" title="Club Universitario de Deportes">Universitario</a> of Perú.</p>
<p>In 2012 he plays his second Copa Libertadores, playing in the group stage against Peñarol, <a href="/wiki/Club_Universidad_de_Chile" title="Club Universidad de Chile">Universidad de Chile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Nacional" title="Atlético Nacional">Atlético Nacional</a> The "Express" could not pass another instance more than this phase.</p>
<p>In 2014, Godoy Cruz would play the second Copa Sudamericana, being eliminated before <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_River_Plate" title="Club Atlético River Plate">Club Atlético River Plate</a> (champion of the edition of the match) in parties of ida (0:1) and return (0:2).</p>
<p>In 2017, Tomba will play for the third time in its history of the Copa Libertadores, fruit of the good championship that secured in 2016, and thus achieving the classification of the name of the tournament.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rivalries">Rivalries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Godoy_Cruz_Antonio_Tomba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rivalries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Godoy Cruz's classic rival is Andes Talleres Sport Club, a neighborhood classic that has not been played since 1993, thanks to the "Tomba" promotions and the "Azulgrana" soccer debacle.</p>
<p>In addition, Godoy Cruz has provincial classics such as <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Club_San_Mart%C3%ADn" title="Atlético Club San Martín">San Martín de Mendoza</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Sportivo_Independiente_Rivadavia" title="Club Sportivo Independiente Rivadavia">Independiente Rivadavia</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hurac%C3%A1n_Las_Heras&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Huracán Las Heras (page does not exist)">Huracán Las Heras</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gimnasia_y_Esgrima_de_Mendoza" title="Gimnasia y Esgrima de Mendoza">Gimnasia y Esgrima de Mendoza</a>.</p>
<p>He also has a rivalry with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Martin_de_San_Juan" title="San Martin de San Juan">San Martin de San Juan</a>, with whom he plays the Cuyo Classic.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nickname">Nickname</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Godoy_Cruz_Antonio_Tomba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Nickname">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Godoy Cruz is nicknamed <i>Tomba</i> and <i>Bodeguero</i>, in reference to the wine selling activity of the Deportivo Bodega Antonio Tomba, one of the merging clubs of 1921. Since the stadium is located near a railway, the club is also called <i>El Expreso</i> ("The Express").</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Friendly_matches">Friendly matches</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Godoy_Cruz_Antonio_Tomba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Friendly matches">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Godoy Cruz played friendly matches against patriotic clubs and other countries. A memorable friendly played in 1964, against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santos_Football_Club" title="Santos Football Club">Santos Football Club</a>, with soccer star Pele ended in favor of the Brazilian team by a score of 3-2. In 1969, against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universidad_de_Chile" title="Universidad de Chile">Universidad de Chile</a>, the "Expreso" claimed a 3-0 victory. Another match in 1990, against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sevilla_F%C3%BAtbol_Club" title="Sevilla Fútbol Club">Sevilla</a> in which the team from Mendoza claimed victory by a score of 2-1. In 2007, a visiting Godoy Cruz squad were defeated 5-nil by <a href="/wiki/Club_Nacional_de_Football" title="Club Nacional de Football">Nacional de Montevideo</a>. Godoy Cruz also played several friendly matches against national teams, such as Argentina in 1969, Chile in 1970, and Poland in 1977.</p>
<p>A source of historical pride for the team's supporters are two friendly matches won against Argentine giants: in 1965, they defeated <a href="/wiki/Boca_Juniors" title="Boca Juniors">Boca Juniors</a> 4–0, and in 1997 they defeated <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_River_Plate" title="Club Atlético River Plate">River Plate</a> by the same margin.</p>
<p>Godoy Cruz won the summer 2009 edition of the friendly pentagonal cup known as the "<a href="/wiki/Torneos_de_Verano_(Argentina)" title="Torneos de Verano (Argentina)">Copa Ciudad de Tandil</a>", after defeating both <a href="/wiki/Chacarita_Juniors" title="Chacarita Juniors">Chacarita Juniors</a> and <a href="/wiki/Quilmes_Atl%C3%A9tico_Club" title="Quilmes Atlético Club">Quilmes</a> in penalty shootouts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stadium">Stadium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Godoy_Cruz_Antonio_Tomba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Stadium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Feliciano_Gambarte.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2191" data-file-width="3937" height="156" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Feliciano_Gambarte.jpg/280px-Feliciano_Gambarte.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Feliciano_Gambarte.jpg/420px-Feliciano_Gambarte.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Feliciano_Gambarte.jpg/560px-Feliciano_Gambarte.jpg 2x" width="280"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Feliciano_Gambarte.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Feliciano Gambarte, <i>La Bodega</i>, in 2015.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The official Stadium Club is the <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Feliciano_Gambarte" title="Estadio Feliciano Gambarte">Estadio Feliciano Gambarte</a> opened in 1959. In 2004, the team played his last professional match, pitting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Tiro_Federal_Argentino" title="Club Atlético Tiro Federal Argentino">Tiro Federal</a>, matching ending 0–0. Since that time, the team plays its home games at the provincial <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Malvinas_Argentinas" title="Estadio Malvinas Argentinas">Estadio Malvinas Argentinas</a>. Since 2014, the fans campaigned to return to play in the Stadium Club.</p>
