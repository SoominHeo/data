<p><b>Club Deportivo Godoy Cruz Antonio Tomba</b>, known simply as <b>Godoy Cruz</b>, is an <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentine</a> <a href="/wiki/Sports_club" title="Sports club">sports club</a> from <a href="/wiki/Godoy_Cruz,_Mendoza" title="Godoy Cruz, Mendoza">Godoy Cruz, Mendoza</a>. The club is best known for its <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> team, that plays in the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Argentine Primera DivisiÃ³n">Primera DivisiÃ³n</a>, the top level of the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_football_league_system" title="Argentine football league system">Argentine football league system</a>.</p>
<p>Other activities practised at Godoy Cruz are <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Team_handball" title="Team handball">team handball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Field_hockey" title="Field hockey">field hockey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volleyball" title="Volleyball">volleyball</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Godoy_Cruz_Antonio_Tomba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Godoy Cruz was born as an institution in 1921. It all started when a group of friends, gathered in the "Victoria Bar" (located opposite the departmental square at that time), decided to found a club, taking advantage of the boom at the time . Thus was born on June 1, 1921, the Club Sportivo Godoy Cruz with the novice presidency of Don Romero Garay.</p>

<p>The club was founded on 21 June 1921 under the name sportivo Godoy Cruz, and changed to its current name on 25 April 1930 after the fusion with Deportivo Bodega Antonio Tomba. In 1959, Godoy Cruz' stadium, the [Estadio Feliciano Gambarte], was constructed. The stadium is nicknamed <i>La Bodega</i> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> for "wine cellar") and holds 21,000 people.</p>
<p>Godoy Cruz played in the regional league for several years before reaching the national level. The club won the Mendoza first division championship in 1944, 1947, 1950, 1951, 1954, 1968, and also in 1989 and 1990 that qualified to play in the defunct <i>Torneo del Interior</i> national-level tournament.</p>
<p>Winning the Torneo del Interior in 1994, Godoy Cruz reached the <a href="/wiki/Primera_B_Nacional" title="Primera B Nacional">Primera B Nacional</a> (Argentine second division) that year. After more than ten years in the second division, the club was finally promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Argentine Primera DivisiÃ³n">Primera DivisiÃ³n</a> in 2006, after winning the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_in_Argentine_football" title="2005â06 in Argentine football">2005â06 season</a> of the Primera B Nacional, defeating <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Nueva_Chicago" title="Club AtlÃ©tico Nueva Chicago">Nueva Chicago</a> in the final.</p>
<p>Twenty-year-old <a href="/wiki/Enzo_P%C3%A9rez" title="Enzo PÃ©rez">Enzo PÃ©rez</a> scored the first goal by Godoy Cruz in the Argentine Primera, in a 1â1 draw with <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Belgrano" title="Club AtlÃ©tico Belgrano">Belgrano</a> on 9 September 2006.</p>
<p>At the end of the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2006â07 Argentine Primera DivisiÃ³n">2006â07 season</a>, Godoy Cruz was relegated from the Primera after losing their promotion/relegation play-off with <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Hurac%C3%A1n" title="Club AtlÃ©tico HuracÃ¡n">HuracÃ¡n</a>. Their stay in the second division was short, as they earned automatic promotion to the first division after finishing runners-up to <a href="/wiki/San_Mart%C3%ADn_de_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="San MartÃ­n de TucumÃ¡n">San MartÃ­n de TucumÃ¡n</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_in_Argentine_football" title="2007â08 in Argentine football">2007â08 season</a>.</p>
<p>Godoy Cruz secured their best ever league position of 3rd place under <a href="/wiki/Omar_Asad" title="Omar Asad">Omar Asad</a>'s management during the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n_season#Torneo_Clausura" title="2009â10 Argentine Primera DivisiÃ³n season">2010 Clausura</a>. In that tournament, they also achieved the best-ever point total (37) for a team indirectly affiliated to the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Football_Association" title="Argentine Football Association">Argentine Football Association</a> (meaning clubs under the administration of the Federal Council branch of AFA, which are teams outside <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greater_Buenos_Aires" title="Greater Buenos Aires">Greater Buenos Aires</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rosario" title="Rosario">Rosario</a> and <a href="/wiki/Santa_Fe,_Argentina" title="Santa Fe, Argentina">Santa Fe</a>). These results qualified them for the <a href="/wiki/2011_Copa_Libertadores" title="2011 Copa Libertadores">2011 Copa Libertadores</a>, their first appearance in this tournament.</p>

In that same group I dispute parties with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Pe%C3%B1arol" title="Club AtlÃ©tico PeÃ±arol">PeÃ±arol</a> of Montevideo and Independente of Avellaneda. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rivalries">Rivalries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Godoy_Cruz_Antonio_Tomba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rivalries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Godoy Cruz's classic rival is Andes Talleres Sport Club, a neighborhood classic that has not been played since 1993, thanks to the "Tomba" promotions and the "Azulgrana" soccer debacle.</p>
<p>In addition, Godoy Cruz has provincial classics such as <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Club_San_Mart%C3%ADn" title="AtlÃ©tico Club San MartÃ­n">San MartÃ­n de Mendoza</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Sportivo_Independiente_Rivadavia" title="Club Sportivo Independiente Rivadavia">Independiente Rivadavia</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hurac%C3%A1n_Las_Heras&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HuracÃ¡n Las Heras (page does not exist)">HuracÃ¡n Las Heras</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gimnasia_y_Esgrima_de_Mendoza" title="Gimnasia y Esgrima de Mendoza">Gimnasia y Esgrima de Mendoza</a>.</p>
<p>He also has a rivalry with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Martin_de_San_Juan" title="San Martin de San Juan">San Martin de San Juan</a>, with whom he plays the Cuyo Classic.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nickname">Nickname</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Godoy_Cruz_Antonio_Tomba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Nickname">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Godoy Cruz is nicknamed <i>Tomba</i> and <i>Bodeguero</i>, in reference to the wine selling activity of the Deportivo Bodega Antonio Tomba, one of the merging clubs of 1921. Since the stadium is located near a railway, the club is also called <i>El Expreso</i> ("The Express").</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Friendly_matches">Friendly matches</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Godoy_Cruz_Antonio_Tomba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Friendly matches">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Godoy Cruz played friendly matches against patriotic clubs and other countries. A memorable friendly played in 1964, against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santos_Football_Club" title="Santos Football Club">Santos Football Club</a>, with soccer star Pele ended in favor of the Brazilian team by a score of 3-2. In 1969, against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universidad_de_Chile" title="Universidad de Chile">Universidad de Chile</a>, the "Expreso" claimed a 3-0 victory. Another match in 1990, against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sevilla_F%C3%BAtbol_Club" title="Sevilla FÃºtbol Club">Sevilla</a> in which the team from Mendoza claimed victory by a score of 2-1. In 2007, a visiting Godoy Cruz squad were defeated 5-nil by <a href="/wiki/Club_Nacional_de_Football" title="Club Nacional de Football">Nacional de Montevideo</a>. Godoy Cruz also played several friendly matches against national teams, such as Argentina in 1969, Chile in 1970, and Poland in 1977.</p>
<p>A source of historical pride for the team's supporters are two friendly matches won against Argentine giants: in 1965, they defeated <a href="/wiki/Boca_Juniors" title="Boca Juniors">Boca Juniors</a> 4â0, and in 1997 they defeated <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_River_Plate" title="Club AtlÃ©tico River Plate">River Plate</a> by the same margin.</p>
<p>Godoy Cruz won the summer 2009 edition of the friendly pentagonal cup known as the "<a href="/wiki/Torneos_de_Verano_(Argentina)" title="Torneos de Verano (Argentina)">Copa Ciudad de Tandil</a>", after defeating both <a href="/wiki/Chacarita_Juniors" title="Chacarita Juniors">Chacarita Juniors</a> and <a href="/wiki/Quilmes_Atl%C3%A9tico_Club" title="Quilmes AtlÃ©tico Club">Quilmes</a> in penalty shootouts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>The official Stadium Club is the <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Feliciano_Gambarte" title="Estadio Feliciano Gambarte">Estadio Feliciano Gambarte</a> opened in 1959. In 2004, the team played his last professional match, pitting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Tiro_Federal_Argentino" title="Club AtlÃ©tico Tiro Federal Argentino">Tiro Federal</a>, matching ending 0â0. Since that time, the team plays its home games at the provincial <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Malvinas_Argentinas" title="Estadio Malvinas Argentinas">Estadio Malvinas Argentinas</a>. Since 2014, the fans campaigned to return to play in the Stadium Club.</p>
