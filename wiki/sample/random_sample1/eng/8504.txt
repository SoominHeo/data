<p><i><b>Immortals</b></i> is a 2011 American epic <a href="/wiki/Fantasy" title="Fantasy">fantasy</a>-<a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action</a>-<a href="/wiki/Adventure_film" title="Adventure film">adventure film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Tarsem_Singh" title="Tarsem Singh">Tarsem Singh</a> and starring <a href="/wiki/Henry_Cavill" title="Henry Cavill">Henry Cavill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freida_Pinto" title="Freida Pinto">Freida Pinto</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mickey_Rourke" title="Mickey Rourke">Mickey Rourke</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The film also stars <a href="/wiki/Luke_Evans_(actor)" title="Luke Evans (actor)">Luke Evans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Byers" title="Steve Byers">Steve Byers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kellan_Lutz" title="Kellan Lutz">Kellan Lutz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Morgan_(actor)" title="Joseph Morgan (actor)">Joseph Morgan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Dorff" title="Stephen Dorff">Stephen Dorff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Sharman" title="Daniel Sharman">Daniel Sharman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alan_van_Sprang" title="Alan van Sprang">Alan van Sprang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isabel_Lucas" title="Isabel Lucas">Isabel Lucas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corey_Sevier" title="Corey Sevier">Corey Sevier</a>, and <a href="/wiki/John_Hurt" title="John Hurt">John Hurt</a>. The film was previously named <i>Dawn of War</i> and <i>War of the Gods</i> before being officially named <i>Immortals</i>, and is loosely based on the <a href="/wiki/Greek_mythology" title="Greek mythology">Greek myths</a> of <a href="/wiki/Theseus" title="Theseus">Theseus</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Minotaur" title="Minotaur">Minotaur</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Titanomachy" title="Titanomachy">Titanomachy</a>.</p>
<p>Principal photography started on April 5, 2010 in Montreal, and the film was released in 2D and in <a href="/wiki/3D_film" title="3D film">3D</a> (using the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_D_3D" title="Real D 3D">Real D 3D</a> and <a href="/wiki/Digital_3D" title="Digital 3D">Digital 3D</a> formats) on November 11, 2011 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Pictures" title="Universal Pictures">Universal Pictures</a> and <a href="/wiki/Relativity_Media" title="Relativity Media">Relativity Media</a>, receiving mixed reviews but became a commercial success at the box office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immortals_(2011_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before the dawn of man and beast, Immortals waged war against each other in Heaven. The victors declared themselves <a href="/wiki/Twelve_Olympians" title="Twelve Olympians">gods</a> while the vanquished were renamed the <a href="/wiki/Titan_(mythology)" title="Titan (mythology)">Titans</a> and imprisoned beneath Mount <a href="/wiki/Tartarus" title="Tartarus">Tartarus</a>. The Epirus Bow, a weapon of immense power, was lost on Earth during the war. In 1228 B.C., the mortal king Hyperion<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Mickey_Rourke" title="Mickey Rourke">Mickey Rourke</a>) of <a href="/wiki/Heraklion" title="Heraklion">Heraklion</a> searches for the bow, intending to use it to release the Titans to get revenge on the gods for failing to save his family from disease. Hyperion captures the virgin <a href="/wiki/Oracle" title="Oracle">oracle</a> <a href="/wiki/Phaedra_(mythology)" title="Phaedra (mythology)">Phaedra</a> (<a href="/wiki/Freida_Pinto" title="Freida Pinto">Freida Pinto</a>), believing that she can use her visions to find the Epirus Bow's resting place.</p>
<p>In a small village nearby, the inhabitants prepare to flee to Mount Tartarus to avoid Hyperion's army. One inhabitant named Theseus (<a href="/wiki/Henry_Cavill" title="Henry Cavill">Henry Cavill</a>) is a skilled warrior trained by his mentor, a mysterious old man (<a href="/wiki/John_Hurt" title="John Hurt">John Hurt</a>). <a href="/wiki/Theseus" title="Theseus">Theseus</a> and his mother Aethra (Anne Day-Jones), are considered undesirables because Theseus was born from Aethra being raped and are forced to stay behind by Athenian soldiers including Lysander (<a href="/wiki/Joseph_Morgan_(actor)" title="Joseph Morgan (actor)">Joseph Morgan</a>). Theseus is able to beat multiple opponents until the Athenian officer Helios (<a href="/wiki/Peter_Stebbings" title="Peter Stebbings">Peter Stebbings</a>) intervenes and discharges Lysander from the army for his actions. Lysander travels to Hyperion, offering his service and the village's location. Hyperion accepts, but hammers Lysander's testicles for being a traitor so he can never father children. Hyperion's forces attack Theseus's village, murdering the villagers and Aethra, and taking Theseus captive.</p>
<p>The old man is revealed to be <a href="/wiki/Zeus" title="Zeus">Zeus</a> (<a href="/wiki/Luke_Evans_(actor)" title="Luke Evans (actor)">Luke Evans</a>) when he meets with his fellow gods <a href="/wiki/Athena" title="Athena">Athena</a> (<a href="/wiki/Isabel_Lucas" title="Isabel Lucas">Isabel Lucas</a>), <a href="/wiki/Poseidon" title="Poseidon">Poseidon</a> (<a href="/wiki/Kellan_Lutz" title="Kellan Lutz">Kellan Lutz</a>), <a href="/wiki/Ares" title="Ares">Ares</a> (<a href="/wiki/Daniel_Sharman" title="Daniel Sharman">Daniel Sharman</a>), <a href="/wiki/Apollo" title="Apollo">Apollo</a> (<a href="/wiki/Corey_Sevier" title="Corey Sevier">Corey Sevier</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Heracles" title="Heracles">Heracles</a> (<a href="/wiki/Steve_Byers" title="Steve Byers">Steve Byers</a>). Zeus warns them not to interfere in mortal affairs as gods unless the Titans are released and must have faith in mankind to defeat Hyperion. Theseus is enslaved alongside the thief Stavros (<a href="/wiki/Stephen_Dorff" title="Stephen Dorff">Stephen Dorff</a>). Phaedra, who is held captive nearby, sees a vision of Theseus. Phaedra organizes a riot, using the chaos to escape with Theseus, Stavros, and the other slaves. Theseus decides to pursue Hyperion and attempts to capture a boat, but he and his allies are overwhelmed by Hyperion's forces. Poseidon disobeys Zeus' orders and falls from Olympus into the ocean causing a tsunami that wipes out Hyperion's men. Phaedra sees another vision of Theseus standing near a shrouded body. She determines that Theseus must return home to bury Aethra.</p>
<p>While laying Aethra to rest in the village's <a href="/wiki/Labyrinth" title="Labyrinth">Labyrinth</a>, Theseus discovers the Epirus Bow embedded in nearby rock. He frees the Epirus Bow, but is attacked by Hyperion's henchman the <a href="/wiki/Minotaur" title="Minotaur">Minotaur</a> (<a href="/wiki/Robert_Maillet" title="Robert Maillet">Robert Maillet</a>). Theseus kills the Minotaur and uses the Bow to save his allies from being executed before collapsing from poisoned scratches inflicted by the Minotaur. Phaedra tends to Theseus and later falls in love with him and they make love to each other, stripping her of the visions she deemed a curse. The group returns to Phaedra's temple while Hyperion and his forces are away at Mount Tartarus. At the temple, Stavros and Theseus are lured into an ambush and Theseus loses the Epirus Bow. Outnumbered by Hyperion's men, Ares directly intervenes to save Theseus and Athena provides the men with horses to reach Mount Tartarus. Zeus arrives and angrily kills Ares for disobeying his command. Zeus tells Theseus and his allies to let Ares' death be a warning to both the gods and them that they will receive no more help from the gods and he must justify the faith Zeus has in him alone. Before leaving with Athena, Zeus tells Theseus to prove him right. The lost Epirus Bow is brought to Hyperion.</p>
<p>Theseus, Stavros, and Phaedra travel to Mount Tartarus. Theseus tries in vain to warn <a href="/wiki/Greeks" title="Greeks">Hellenics</a>' King Cassander (<a href="/wiki/Stephen_McHattie" title="Stephen McHattie">Stephen McHattie</a>) of Hyperion's plans, but Cassander dismisses his talk of gods as myth, intending to negotiate peace with Hyperion. The following day, Hyperion uses the Bow to destroy Mount Tartarus' seemingly indestructible gate. Theseus leads the Hellenic army to war against the Hyperion forces, killing Lysander. Hyperion ignores the battle, storms through to Mount Tartarus, and kills Helios and Cassander, using the Epirus Bow to breach the mountain and free the Titans before Stavros and Theseus can stop him. The force of the release knocks the mortals down. Stavros takes the Epirus Bow and kills a Titan, but is killed by the other Titans. Zeus, Poseidon, Athena, Heracles, and Apollo arrive and battle the Titans while Theseus fights Hyperion. Zeus destroys the Epirus Bow, and the gods prove more than a match for the Titans, but they are overwhelmed by sheer numbers, with all but Zeus and Poseidon being killed. Theseus kills Hyperion while Zeus collapses Mount Tartarus on the Titans. As the mountain is collapsing, Zeus picks up Athena's body and ascends to Olympus along with a badly wounded Poseidon. The collapsing mountain wipes out all Hyperion's men. The mortally wounded Theseus is also transported to Olympus for his sacrifice and given a place among the gods.</p>
<p>Several years later, Theseus' story has become legend and Phaedra has given birth to Theseus' son <a href="/wiki/Acamas_(son_of_Theseus)" title="Acamas (son of Theseus)">Acamas</a> (Gage Munroe), a gift from the Gods. Acamas is met by the old man who informs the child that in the future, he too will one day fight against evil and that he mustn't fear his visions. Acamas sees a vision of the sky filled with thousands of gods and Titans fighting (including Zeus and a fully healed Poseidon) with Theseus leading the charge.</p>

<p>This film incorporates some elements from <a href="/wiki/Classical_antiquity" title="Classical antiquity">classical</a> <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Greek</a> <a href="/wiki/Myth" title="Myth">myths</a> and was filmed using <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3-D_film" title="3-D film">3D</a> technology.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="The material near this tag possibly contains original research. (July 2011)">original research?</span></a></i>]</sup> Director <a href="/wiki/Tarsem_Singh" title="Tarsem Singh">Tarsem Singh</a> said that he was planning an action film using <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a> painting styles. He then went on to say that the film is "Basically, <a href="/wiki/Caravaggio" title="Caravaggio">Caravaggio</a> meets <i><a href="/wiki/Fight_Club" title="Fight Club">Fight Club</a></i>. It's a really hardcore action film done in Renaissance painting style. I want to see how that goes; it's turned into something really cool. I'm going for a very contemporary look on top of that so I'm kind of going with, you know, Renaissance time with electricity. So it's a bit like <a href="/wiki/Baz_Luhrmann" title="Baz Luhrmann">Baz Luhrmann</a> doing <i><a href="/wiki/Romeo_%2B_Juliet" title="Romeo + Juliet">Romeo + Juliet</a></i> in Mexico; it's just taking a particular Greek tale and half (make it contemporary) and telling it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The film had a production budget of $80 million ($75 million after tax rebates)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> to $120 million<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and cost "at least" $35 million to market.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LABox_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-LABox-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immortals_(2011_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immortals_(2011_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Immortals</i> received mixed reviews from critics. On <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, the film has a rating of 35%, based on 114 reviews, with an average rating of 5/10. The site's critical consensus states: 'The melding of real sets, CG work, and Tarsem's signature style produces fireworks, though the same can't be said for <i>Immortals'</i> weak storytelling.'<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, the film has a score of 46 out of 100, based on 23 critics, indicating "mixed or average reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> According to <a href="/wiki/CinemaScore" title="CinemaScore">CinemaScore</a>, the moviegoers gave the film was a "B" on an A+ to F scale, and a "B+" from the under-25 crowd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CinemaScore_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-CinemaScore-18">[18]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a> gave the film two stars out of four, saying ""Immortals" is without doubt the best-looking awful movie you will ever see".,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> while <i>The Guardian</i> gave the film three stars out of five, commenting 'Theseus battles the Titans in a cheerfully idiotic mythological yarn ballasted by Tarsem's eyecatching image-making'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Todd McCarthy of <i>Hollywood Reporter</i> said 'Thuddingly ponderous, heavy-handed and lacking a single moment that evinces any relish for movie-making, this lurch back from the “history” of 300 into the mists of Greek myth is a drag in nearly every way, from the particulars of physical torture to the pounding score that won't quit.'<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Of those who praised the picture, it received an honorable mention from MTV.com as one of the year's best films<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> as well as making Guy Lodge's top twenty films of 2011 list on HitFlix.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Furthermore, it was on Toro Magazine's Top Ten list<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> as well as Glasgow To The Movies' Top Ten Films of 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Marc Eastman, of Are You Screening.com, named IMMORTALS the #3 film of 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> It also was nominated for several Saturn Awards, including Best Fantasy Film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immortals_(2011_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In North America, it was released on November 11, 2011. <i>Immortals</i> had a $1.4 million midnight showings and then grossed a total of $14.8 million on its opening day, topping the daily box office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> It then finished the weekend of November 11–13, 2011 at #1 with $32.2 million, ranking as <a href="/wiki/Relativity_Media" title="Relativity Media">Relativity Media</a>'s biggest opening weekend to date, against newcomers <i>J. Edgar</i> and <i>Jack and Jill</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> 3D showings accounted for a substantial 66% of the weekend gross. The film's audience was 60 percent male, 75 percent under the age of 35.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CinemaScore_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-CinemaScore-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Outside North America, it earned $38 million overseas from 35 countries on its opening weekend. Its highest-grossing territories were Russia ($8.2 million), China ($5.7 million) and South Korea ($4.5 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The film has earned $83,504,017 in the United States and Canada and $143,400,000 in other countries, for a worldwide total of $226,904,017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immortals_(2011_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Immortals</i> was released on DVD, Blu-ray Disc, and Blu-ray 3D on March 5, 2012 in the United Kingdom and on March 6, 2012 in the United States and Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In its first week of release 20th Century Fox Home Entertainment sold more than 1.2 million units of the film<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> making it the week's #1 film in Home Entertainment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> It sold 648,947 DVD units for a total of $11,116,462 and 926,964 Blu-ray Disc units for a total of $21,310,902 for the week ending March 11, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> An additional 100,000 3-D units sold totaling almost $40,000,000 in home entertainment sales in its first week of release in the US.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_media">Other media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immortals_(2011_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Other media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Archaia Press released a graphic novel tie-in. Called <i>Immortals: Gods and Heroes</i>, the hardcover book featured new stories that expanded on the universe established in the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
