<p>The <b>New Russia Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Партия Новороссия</span>, <small><a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Russian" title="Romanization of Russian">tr.</a></small> <i>Partiya Novorossiya</i>), or <b>Novorossiya Party</b>, is a political party operating in <a href="/wiki/Novorossiya_(confederation)" title="Novorossiya (confederation)">Novorossiya</a>, a union of self-declared separatist states within the internationally recognized borders of <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The organization was founded by pro-Russian separatists, under the leadership of <a href="/wiki/Pavel_Gubarev" title="Pavel Gubarev">Pavel Gubarev</a>, on 14 May 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ross1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-ross1-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The party is formally known as the Social-Political Movement "New Russia Party" (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Общественно-политическое движение «Партия Новороссия»</span>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It is not registered with the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Justice_(Ukraine)" title="Ministry of Justice (Ukraine)">Ministry of Justice of Ukraine</a>.</p>
<p>The party was banned from participating in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Donbass_parliamentary_elections,_2014" title="Donbass parliamentary elections, 2014">2014 Donbass parliamentary elections</a> because they "were not able to hold a founding conference."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http:.2F.2Fwww.mk.ru.2Fpolitics.2F2014.2F10.2F30.2Fdadut-li-novorossii-sdelat-vybor.html_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-http:.2F.2Fwww.mk.ru.2Fpolitics.2F2014.2F10.2F30.2Fdadut-li-novorossii-sdelat-vybor.html-10">[10]</a></sup> Members of the party took part in the election on the election lists of "Free Donbass".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http:.2F.2Fwww.mk.ru.2Fpolitics.2F2014.2F10.2F30.2Fdadut-li-novorossii-sdelat-vybor.html_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-http:.2F.2Fwww.mk.ru.2Fpolitics.2F2014.2F10.2F30.2Fdadut-li-novorossii-sdelat-vybor.html-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Objectives">Objectives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Russia_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Objectives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the party, their aim is "the withdrawal of all south-eastern Ukrainian lands from the jurisdiction of the Kiev authorities ... based on the principle of <a href="/wiki/Direct_democracy" title="Direct democracy">direct democracy</a> ... creating a new, truly fair, scientifically and technologically advanced state."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Russia_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/2014_pro-Russian_unrest_in_Ukraine" title="2014 pro-Russian unrest in Ukraine">2014 pro-Russian unrest in Ukraine</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Founding">Founding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Russia_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Founding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The New Russia Party was founded on 13 May 2014 in <a href="/wiki/Donetsk" title="Donetsk">Donetsk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kp13_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-kp13-12">[12]</a></sup> Its creation was announced by <a href="/wiki/Pavel_Gubarev" title="Pavel Gubarev">Pavel Gubarev</a>, then acting as "People's Governor" of Donetsk, who stated, "The new party will be led only by those people who in this difficult time showed themselves as true patriots of their Motherland and proved themselves as true fighters and defenders of their Fatherland."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kp13_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-kp13-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first congress was attended by pro-Russian separatist officials of the <a href="/wiki/Donetsk_People%27s_Republic" title="Donetsk People's Republic">Donetsk People's Republic</a>, Donbass Militia. Notable figures were involved, including: <a href="/wiki/Donetsk_Republic_(political_party)" title="Donetsk Republic (political party)">Donetsk Republic</a> leader <a href="/wiki/Pavel_Gubarev" title="Pavel Gubarev">Pavel Gubarev</a> (member of the Neo-Nazi <a href="/wiki/Russian_National_Unity" title="Russian National Unity">Russian National Unity</a>), writer <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Prokhanov" title="Alexander Prokhanov">Alexander Prokhanov</a>, fascist<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Aing_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Aing-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Palin_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Palin-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shenfield_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-shenfield-15">[15]</a></sup> political scientist and <a href="/wiki/Eurasia_Party" title="Eurasia Party">Eurasia Party</a> leader <a href="/wiki/Aleksandr_Dugin" title="Aleksandr Dugin">Aleksandr Dugin</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Valery_Korovin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Valery Korovin (page does not exist)">Valery Korovin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ross1_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-ross1-7">[7]</a></sup> The congress announced the creation of a new self-declared confederate state called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_State_of_New_Russia" title="Federal State of New Russia">'New Russia'</a>. The state would, according to Dugin, have its capital city in <a href="/wiki/Donetsk" title="Donetsk">Donetsk</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Orthodox_Christianity" title="Russian Orthodox Christianity">Russian Orthodox Christianity</a> as the state religion, and would nationalize major industries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-welcomenr_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-welcomenr-16">[16]</a></sup> According to Gubarev the state would also include (the major cities currently not under control of separatists) <a href="/wiki/Kharkiv" title="Kharkiv">Kharkiv</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kherson" title="Kherson">Kherson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dnipropetrovsk" title="Dnipropetrovsk">Dnipropetrovsk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mykolaiv" title="Mykolaiv">Mykolaiv</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zaporizhia" title="Zaporizhia">Zaporizhia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NPUP23512_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-NPUP23512-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ukraine_crisis_timeline_BBC_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ukraine_crisis_timeline_BBC-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Donbass_elections">Donbass elections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Russia_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Donbass elections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Donbass_general_elections,_2014" title="Donbass general elections, 2014">Donbass general elections, 2014</a></div>
<p>On 2 November 2014, internationally unrecognized elections were held in the Donetsk and Lugansk People's Republics. The New Russia Party was refused registration in the DPR because it had not held a founding conference.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http:.2F.2Fwww.mk.ru.2Fpolitics.2F2014.2F10.2F30.2Fdadut-li-novorossii-sdelat-vybor.html_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-http:.2F.2Fwww.mk.ru.2Fpolitics.2F2014.2F10.2F30.2Fdadut-li-novorossii-sdelat-vybor.html-10">[10]</a></sup> It subsequently stood candidates under the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Free_Donbass&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Free Donbass (page does not exist)">Free Donbass</a> grouping, which received 31.6% of the vote.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http:.2F.2Fwww.mk.ru.2Fpolitics.2F2014.2F10.2F30.2Fdadut-li-novorossii-sdelat-vybor.html_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-http:.2F.2Fwww.mk.ru.2Fpolitics.2F2014.2F10.2F30.2Fdadut-li-novorossii-sdelat-vybor.html-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sanctions">Sanctions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Russia_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sanctions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As part of the <a href="/wiki/International_sanctions_during_the_Ukrainian_crisis" title="International sanctions during the Ukrainian crisis">sanctions imposed</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_crisis" title="Ukrainian crisis">Ukrainian crisis</a>, the party was placed on US sanctions lists on 19 December 2014. The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_the_Treasury" title="United States Department of the Treasury">Treasury Department</a> stated that the party, which had been "created to unite all supporters for the establishment of an independent federal state of Novorossiya and to withdraw all southeastern lands in Ukraine from the authority of Kyiv", was "designated because it has engaged in actions or policies that threaten the peace, security, stability, sovereignty, or territorial integrity of Ukraine".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
