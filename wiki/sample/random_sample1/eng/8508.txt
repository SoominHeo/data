<p><b>Shin-Rifu Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">新利府駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shin-Rifu-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rifu,_Miyagi" title="Rifu, Miyagi">Rifu, Miyagi</a>, Japan, operated by <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">East Japan Railway Company</a> (JR East).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shin-Rifu_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shin-Rifu Station is the only intermediate station on the Rifu Branch of the <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dhoku_Main_Line" title="Tōhoku Main Line">Tōhoku Main Line</a> from <a href="/wiki/Iwakiri_Station" title="Iwakiri Station">Iwakiri Station</a> to <a href="/wiki/Rifu_Station" title="Rifu Station">Rifu Station</a>. It lies 2.5 km from Iwakiri Station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jnr1985_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-jnr1985-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shin-Rifu_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most services operate through to and from <a href="/wiki/Sendai_Station_(Miyagi)" title="Sendai Station (Miyagi)">Sendai Station</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shin-Rifu_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The unstaffed station consists of a single <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platform</a> served by the single track. Two entrances lead to the platform; one for general passengers, and one linking directly to the adjacent JR East Sendai General Shinkansen Depot for use by JR East employees only.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shin-Rifu_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station opened on 1 April 1982.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-teishajo1998_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-teishajo1998-2">[2]</a></sup> With the privatization of <a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a> (JNR) on 1 April 1987, the station came under the control of JR East.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-teishajo1998_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-teishajo1998-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Surrounding_area">Surrounding area</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shin-Rifu_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Surrounding area">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station is primarily used by employees at the adjacent JR East Sendai General Shinkansen Depot.</p>
