<p><i><b>Bride and Prejudice</b></i> is a 2004 <a href="/wiki/Romance_film" title="Romance film">romantic</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_(modern_genre)" title="Drama (modern genre)">drama film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Gurinder_Chadha" title="Gurinder Chadha">Gurinder Chadha</a>. The screenplay by Chadha and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Mayeda_Berges" title="Paul Mayeda Berges">Paul Mayeda Berges</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Bollywood" title="Bollywood">Bollywood</a>-style <a href="/wiki/Film_adaptation" title="Film adaptation">adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Pride_and_Prejudice" title="Pride and Prejudice">Pride and Prejudice</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Jane_Austen" title="Jane Austen">Jane Austen</a>. It was filmed primarily in English, with some <a href="/wiki/Hindi" title="Hindi">Hindi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Punjabi_language" title="Punjabi language">Punjabi</a> dialogue. The film released in the United States on 11 February 2005 and was well received by critics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rottentomatoes_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-rottentomatoes-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bride_and_Prejudice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The plot is based on Austen's novel <i><a href="/wiki/Pride_and_Prejudice" title="Pride and Prejudice">Pride and Prejudice</a></i>. Some character names remain the same, while others are changed slightly, using localized names with similar pronunciation (such as Lalita for Elizabeth).</p>
<p>Set in <a href="/wiki/Amritsar" title="Amritsar">Amritsar</a>, the story follows Lalita Bakshi (<a href="/wiki/Aishwarya_Rai" title="Aishwarya Rai">Aishwarya Rai</a>), a young woman living with her doting father and helping him run the family farming enterprise; her mother, who is determined to marry off her daughters to respectable and wealthy men; and her three sisters, Jaya (<a href="/wiki/Namrata_Shirodkar" title="Namrata Shirodkar">Namrata Shirodkar</a>), Maya (<a href="/wiki/Meghna_Kothari" title="Meghna Kothari">Meghna Kothari</a>), and Lakhi. At a friend's wedding, Lalita meets Will Darcy (<a href="/wiki/Martin_Henderson" title="Martin Henderson">Martin Henderson</a>), a handsome and wealthy American working in his family's hotel business, who has arrived in Amritsar with his long-time friend, the British-Indian <a href="/wiki/Barrister" title="Barrister">barrister</a> Balraj, and Balraj's sister Kiran.</p>
<p>Events roughly parallel those in the novel, punctuated by <a href="/wiki/Bollywood" title="Bollywood">Bollywood</a>-style song and dance numbers. Darcy resists his attraction to Lalita, who considers him conceited, arrogant, and intolerant toward India and Indian culture. At parties and gatherings, Mrs. Bakshi's mindless chatter, Maya's kitschy dancing, and Lakhi's uninhibited flirting astonish Darcy and his friends, and mortify Jaya and Lalita.</p>
<p>Balraj and Jaya fall quickly in love, but misunderstandings and interference by others drag out their courtship. On a trip to Goa with Darcy, Kiran, Balraj and Jaya, Lalita meets and is attracted to Johnny Wickham (<a href="/wiki/Daniel_Gillies" title="Daniel Gillies">Daniel Gillies</a>), Darcy's former friend, and he validates her low opinion of Darcy. Mr Kholi, a rich, crass, clumsy, and ostentatious Americanized relative, proposes to Lalita. After she turns him down, her best friend Chandra agrees to marry him, much to Lalita's confusion and consternation.</p>
<p>When the youngest sister Lakhi runs off with Wickham in London, Darcy and Lalita find the couple and separate them before he can ruin her life, as he almost did to Will's young sister Georgie.</p>
<p>Ultimately, back in India for Jaya and Balraj's wedding, Darcy surprises and wins over Lalita by joining in the traditional drumming, showing that he is learning to appreciate Indian culture. The film ends with the double wedding of Jaya to Balraj and Lalita to Darcy, with the two couples riding on elephants down the streets of Amritsar.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bride_and_Prejudice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Bride and Prejudice</i> received funding from the UK Film Council with the stipulation that a majority of filming had to take place in the UK. Locations used include <a href="/wiki/Halton_House" title="Halton House">Halton House</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stoke_Park_Club" title="Stoke Park Club">Stoke Park Club</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turville" title="Turville">Turville</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cobstone_Windmill" title="Cobstone Windmill">Cobstone Windmill</a> in Buckinghamshire, and <a href="/wiki/Southall" title="Southall">Southall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Somerset_House" title="Somerset House">Somerset House</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maida_Vale" title="Maida Vale">Little Venice</a>, the <a href="/wiki/London_Eye" title="London Eye">London Eye</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Film_Theatre" title="National Film Theatre">National Film Theatre</a> in London. Other locations include the <a href="/wiki/Harmandir_Sahib" title="Harmandir Sahib">Golden Temple</a> of <a href="/wiki/Amritsar" title="Amritsar">Amritsar</a>, the beaches of <a href="/wiki/Goa" title="Goa">Goa</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Canyon" title="Grand Canyon">Grand Canyon</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Concert_Hall" title="Walt Disney Concert Hall">Walt Disney Concert Hall</a> in Los Angeles, and <a href="/wiki/Santa_Monica,_California" title="Santa Monica, California">Santa Monica Beach</a>.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ashanti_(entertainer)" title="Ashanti (entertainer)">Ashanti</a> sings "Take Me to Love" and "Touch My Body" in the film. According to director Gurinder Chadha in "making-of" extras on the DVD release, Ashanti's appearance is an homage to the tradition of a celebrity making a cameo appearance to sing an "<a href="/wiki/Item_number" title="Item number">item number</a>", a song that has no direct involvement in the plot in Bollywood films.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bride_and_Prejudice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film opened to generally positive reviews. Review aggregation website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> gives <i>Bride and Prejudice</i> a score of 79% based on reviews from 33 critics, with a rating average of 6.6 out of 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rottentomatoes_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-rottentomatoes-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Stella Papamichael at the BBC noted that "swapping corsets for saris, and polite pianoforte for the bhangra beat, director Gurinder Chadha reinvigorates Jane Austen's Pride And Prejudice with fun and flamboyance".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
