<p><b>Club Deportivo El Nacional</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuadorian</a> sports club from <a href="/wiki/Quito" title="Quito">Quito</a>, known best for their professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> team. The team currently plays in the <a href="/wiki/Ecuadorian_Serie_A" title="Ecuadorian Serie A">Serie A</a>, the top-flight football league in the country.</p>
<p>El Nacional has thirteen national championships (one less than <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barcelona_Sporting_Club" title="Barcelona Sporting Club">Barcelona</a>). The club has participated in more <a href="/wiki/Copa_Libertadores" title="Copa Libertadores">Copa Libertadores</a> than any other club in Ecuador with 22 appearances. Their best performance in the continental tournament was as a semi-finalist in <a href="/wiki/1985_Copa_Libertadores" title="1985 Copa Libertadores">1985</a>.</p>
<p>El Nacional was founded on June 1, 1964, and was administered by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_of_Ecuador" title="Military of Ecuador">Ecuadorian Military</a> since the foundation until 2013, when the club celebrates his first ever democratic elections. The club has maintained a tradition of only playing Ecuadorian footballers, which has given them the nickname of <i>Puros Criollos</i> ("Pure Natives"). Rival clubs included crosstown clubs <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LDU_Quito" title="LDU Quito">LDU Quito</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sociedad_Deportivo_Quito" title="Sociedad Deportivo Quito">Deportivo Quito</a> and the Guayaquileans <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barcelona_Sporting_Club" title="Barcelona Sporting Club">Barcelona</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Sport_Emelec" title="Club Sport Emelec">Emelec</a>. Their home stadium is <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Ol%C3%ADmpico_Atahualpa" title="Estadio Olímpico Atahualpa">Estadio Olímpico Atahualpa</a>.</p>
<p>Other sports the organization participates in are <a href="/wiki/Gymnastics" title="Gymnastics">gymnastics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Table_tennis" title="Table tennis">table tennis</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecuavolley" title="Ecuavolley">Ecuavolley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chess" title="Chess">chess</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shooting_sports" title="Shooting sports">shooting</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=C.D._El_Nacional&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Bi-Tri_Campeon">The Bi-Tri Campeon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=C.D._El_Nacional&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Bi-Tri Campeon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 1970s, El Nacional was able to achieve what no Ecuadorian team had done in the history of the league. In 1976, 1977, 1978 they were unbeatable champions. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fabi%C3%A1n_Paz_y_Mi%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fabián Paz y Miño (page does not exist)">Fabián Paz y Miño</a> stepped up as the leading goal scorer in the league in 1977 to help El Nacional during those three years of glory. In 1982, 1983, 1984 El Nacional repeated this feat to become double three time championship winners. Their title count became eight. This was gained by help from two legendary players <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jose_Villafuerte" title="Jose Villafuerte">Jose Villafuerte</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ermen_Benitez" title="Ermen Benitez">Ermen Benitez</a>. In 1985 they accomplished their most important feat in international competition reaching semi-finals in the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Libertadores" title="Copa Libertadores">Copa Libertadores</a>. One year later they added to their titles by being crowned for the ninth time in their history.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_1990s">The 1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=C.D._El_Nacional&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The 1990s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>El Nacional produced some of the greatest players in Ecuador and won two more championships in 1992 and 1996 with the help of <a href="/wiki/Cl%C3%A9ber_Chal%C3%A1" title="Cléber Chalá">Cléber Chalá</a>, <a href="/wiki/Agust%C3%ADn_Delgado" title="Agustín Delgado">Agustín Delgado</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Oswaldo_Ibarra" title="Oswaldo Ibarra">Oswaldo Ibarra</a>. They also disputed the semi-finals of the Copa Conmebol 1992 and the 1994 edition.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Present">Present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=C.D._El_Nacional&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The current success of the team has added two more championships in 2005 and 2006 bringing their total to 13 titles. They have equaled rivals <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barcelona_Sporting_Club" title="Barcelona Sporting Club">Barcelona</a> to become one of the best teams in Ecuador. El Nacional has brought up talented players such as <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Valencia" title="Antonio Valencia">Antonio Valencia</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Segundo_Alejandro_Castillo" title="Segundo Alejandro Castillo">Segundo Alejandro Castillo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christian_Ben%C3%ADtez" title="Christian Benítez">Christian Benítez</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Felix_Borja" title="Felix Borja">Felix Borja</a>. There have been plans to build a new stadium but no official decision has been taken yet. Former coach <a href="/wiki/Juan_Carlos_Burbano" title="Juan Carlos Burbano">Juan Carlos Burbano</a> resigned after an unsuccessful first stage in the league. <a href="/wiki/Julio_Asad" title="Julio Asad">Julio Asad</a> was appointed head coach on July 16, 2009.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stadium">Stadium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=C.D._El_Nacional&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Stadium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>El Nacional play their home matches in the <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Ol%C3%ADmpico_Atahualpa" title="Estadio Olímpico Atahualpa">Estadio Olímpico Atahualpa</a>. Plans have been enabled to build a new stadium, however no official decision has been taken. The management even showed the model of what the 42,000 seat stadium would look like.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Supporters">Supporters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=C.D._El_Nacional&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Supporters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On May 13, 1998, a group of friends met and decided to form a highly dedicated fanbase called <b>La Marea Roja</b> (English: <i>The Red Tide</i>). Over the years, they aimed to integrate more people, and becoming a better organized to be the largest fan group of El Nacional. It is made up of over 250 members, with members owning proper identification cards. La Marea Roja stands at the south part of the stadium for 90 minutes every game, which attracts adults, women and large numbers of children.</p>
