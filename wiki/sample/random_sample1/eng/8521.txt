<p>This is a list of characters in the <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Neon_Genesis_Evangelion" title="Neon Genesis Evangelion">Neon Genesis Evangelion</a></i> and the movies <i><a href="/wiki/Neon_Genesis_Evangelion:_Death_%26_Rebirth" title="Neon Genesis Evangelion: Death &amp; Rebirth">Evangelion: Death &amp; Rebirth</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_End_of_Evangelion" title="The End of Evangelion">The End of Evangelion</a></i> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Rebuild_of_Evangelion" title="Rebuild of Evangelion">Rebuild of Evangelion</a></i> tetralogy. The characters were designed by <a href="/wiki/Yoshiyuki_Sadamoto" title="Yoshiyuki Sadamoto">Yoshiyuki Sadamoto</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Creation_and_conception">Creation and conception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Creation and conception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to director <a href="/wiki/Hideaki_Anno" title="Hideaki Anno">Hideaki Anno</a>, <i>Evangelion</i> was an attempt to make all perspectives into one, creating characters that represent different things to different viewers to make it impossible for everyone to arrive at a single theory. To some viewers, the characters are psychological representations, while to others, they are philosophical, religious, historical, and even themselves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eng-DVD-commentary_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Eng-DVD-commentary-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Anno was influenced by <a href="/wiki/Ry%C5%AB_Murakami" title="Ryū Murakami">Ryu Murakami</a>'s political economy novel <i>The Fascism of Love and Fantasy</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">愛と幻想のファシズム</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ai to Gensō no Fashizumu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, and the names "Toji Suzuhara", "Kensuke Aida", and the surname "Horaki" and "Aida" come from this book. Many of the surnames of the characters in <i>Evangelion</i> are named after Japanese warships in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.</p>
<p>Most of <i>Evangelion</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s characters' ages were plotted before broadcasting the TV series, but their birthdays were not settled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> After the TV series finished, GAINAX released birthdays which were similar to those of each character's voice actors, except Rei Ayanami, Toji Suzuhara, Kensuke Aida and Kaworu Nagisa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> This time, the characters' birth years were still unrevealed.</p>
<p>Adult characters' birth years were simply calculated from their age, but there was an additional aspect in determining the children's ones. According to the Japanese educational system, in 2015 second graders of junior high school (eighth graders) are basically made up of children who were born from April 2, 2001 to April 1, 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Children's birth years were set in proportion to this rule. However these birth years did not change the children's ages of 14 in year 2015. This is the reason why Asuka Langley Soryu and Toji Suzuhara were born in December 2001 and Hikari Horaki was born in February 2002, despite all children except Kaworu are 14 in 2015.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_characters">Main characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Main characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the Japanese dialogue for the original anime, the Eva pilots were always referred to as "Children", even when referring to an individual pilot. The English translation referred to the individual pilots as First Child, etc. In the <i><a href="/wiki/Rebuild_of_Evangelion" title="Rebuild of Evangelion">Rebuild of Evangelion</a></i> series, the expression "(n)th Children" is not used. Instead, the pilots are referred to as <i>Ichibanme no tekikakusha</i> <span style="font-weight:normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">一番目の適格者</span><span class="t_nihongo_help"><sup><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span style="color:#00e; font:bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration:none; padding:0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup></span>, "The First Qualified Person/Candidate")</span> <i>Dai Ichi no Shōjo</i> <span style="font-weight:normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">第一の少女</span><span class="t_nihongo_help"><sup><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span style="color:#00e; font:bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration:none; padding:0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup></span>, "First Girl")</span>, <i>Sanninme no kodomo</i> <span style="font-weight:normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">三人目の子供</span><span class="t_nihongo_help"><sup><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span style="color:#00e; font:bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration:none; padding:0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup></span>, "Third Child")</span>, or <i>Dai San no Shōnen</i> <span style="font-weight:normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">第三の少年</span><span class="t_nihongo_help"><sup><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span style="color:#00e; font:bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration:none; padding:0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup></span>, "Third Boy")</span>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shinji_Ikari">Shinji Ikari</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Shinji Ikari">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Shinji_Ikari" title="Shinji Ikari">Shinji Ikari</a></div>
<p><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Megumi_Ogata" title="Megumi Ogata">Megumi Ogata</a> (Japanese); <a href="/wiki/Spike_Spencer" title="Spike Spencer">Spike Spencer</a> (English)</span></p>
<p>Shinji Ikari <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">碇 シンジ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ikari Shinji</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the Third Child and the pilot of <a href="/wiki/Evangelion_(mecha)#Eva_Unit_01" title="Evangelion (mecha)">Unit 01</a>. He is the son of Gendo and the "late" Yui Ikari. Abandoned by his father when he was a toddler, Shinji has grown up to be reclusive and withdrawn, choosing to often run away from difficult situations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_4_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_4-5">[5]</a></sup> He suffers from social anxiety at the beginning of the series, but is gradually freed from this as he socializes with his new classmates and fellow pilots. Shinji is emotionally hyper-sensitive and sometimes does as expected out of fear of rejection, but he has often rebelled and refused to pilot the Eva because of the extremely excruciating harm that has been done to him, or done to his friends. Over time and with encouragement from Misato, he learns to be less cowardly, more outspoken, and more confident, but his progress is nearly undone near the end of the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_24_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_24-6">[6]</a></sup> At some point in the series he begins to confront his inner self, forming arguments and questions about life and reality. He becomes friends with Toji Suzuhara and Kensuke Aida, in addition to his complicated relationships with Rei and Asuka.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rei_Ayanami">Rei Ayanami</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Rei Ayanami">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Rei Ayanami <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">綾波 レイ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ayanami Rei</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the First Child, and the pilot of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EVA00" title="EVA00">Unit 00</a>. At the start of the series, she is shown to be <a href="/wiki/Extraversion_and_introversion#Introversion" title="Extraversion and introversion">socially withdrawn</a>, seemingly emotionless, and remote, with her only apparent relationship being with Gendo Ikari.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_5_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_5-8">[8]</a></sup> As the series progresses, she and Shinji grow closer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_6_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_6-9">[9]</a></sup> It is eventually revealed that Rei is a vessel for the soul of the Angel <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Angel_(Neon_Genesis_Evangelion)#Lilith" title="Angel (Neon Genesis Evangelion)">Lilith</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_24_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_24-6">[6]</a></sup> and was created specifically by Gendo to be used as a tool for accomplishing instrumentality;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_25_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_25-10">[10]</a></sup> she is a clone made from the salvaged remains of Yui and is plagued by a sense of negative self-worth stemming from the realization that she is an expendable asset.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Soulless clones of her are kept hidden in the deepest levels of NERV headquarters to be used as the supposed "cores" of the Dummy Plugs and as replacement bodies for Rei if she should die.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_23_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_23-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Y%C5%ABko_Miyamura" title="Yūko Miyamura">Yūko Miyamura</a> (Japanese); <a href="/wiki/Tiffany_Grant" title="Tiffany Grant">Tiffany Grant</a> (English)</span></p>
<p>Asuka Langley Soryu <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">惣流・アスカ・ラングレー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sōryū Asuka Rangurē</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the Second Child and the pilot of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eva_Unit-02" title="Eva Unit-02">Unit 02</a>. She is of mixed German and Japanese descent. Asuka is brash, egotistical and often verbally abusive (especially to Shinji), and has an exaggeratedly high opinion of her appearance and her skill as an Eva pilot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_8_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_8-13">[13]</a></sup> Her attitude, however, is a front masking deep-seated insecurities stemming from her traumatic childhood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_22_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_22-14">[14]</a></sup> Asuka is openly infatuated with Ryoji Kaji, but is also attracted to Shinji; however, she cannot properly express her affections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_15_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_15-15">[15]</a></sup> In the <i><a href="/wiki/Rebuild_of_Evangelion" title="Rebuild of Evangelion">Rebuild of Evangelion</a></i> series, her name is changed to Asuka Shikinami Langley (式波) and ranked as captain of the Euro airforce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Misato_Katsuragi">Misato Katsuragi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Misato Katsuragi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Misato_Katsuragi" title="Misato Katsuragi">Misato Katsuragi</a></div>
<p><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Kotono_Mitsuishi" title="Kotono Mitsuishi">Kotono Mitsuishi</a> (Japanese); <a href="/wiki/Allison_Keith" title="Allison Keith">Allison Keith</a> (English)</span></p>
<p>Misato Katsuragi <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">葛城 ミサト</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Katsuragi Misato</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the chief operations officer at NERV. In the series, she initially holds the rank of Captain, with a later promotion to Major;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_12_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_12-17">[17]</a></sup> in the first <i><a href="/wiki/Rebuild_of_Evangelion" title="Rebuild of Evangelion">Rebuild of Evangelion</a></i> movie, she holds the rank of Lieutenant Colonel and then Colonel in the second movie. She is the field commander for the Eva pilots, issuing orders and giving battle strategies as well as handling some bureaucratic matters. She is also Shinji and Asuka's guardian,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_9_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_9-18">[18]</a></sup> letting them live in her apartment rather than have them live alone. While she is professional and diligent in her duties, she is a slob and a drunkard while off-duty. She is the only survivor of the Katsuragi Expedition, the scientific study led by her father which triggered the Second Impact; the cross pendant she wears was given to her by her father just before his death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_21_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_21-19">[19]</a></sup> She and Ryoji Kaji were lovers during college, and slowly rekindle their romance over the course of the TV series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_15_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_15-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>She also drives an <a href="/wiki/Alpine_A310" title="Alpine A310">Alpine A310 V6</a>, which serves as her personal vehicle.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Gendo_Ikari">Gendo Ikari</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Gendo Ikari">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Gendo_Ikari" title="Gendo Ikari">Gendo Ikari</a></div>
<p><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Fumihiko_Tachiki" title="Fumihiko Tachiki">Fumihiko Tachiki</a> (Japanese); Tristan MacAvery (TV and films), <a href="/wiki/John_Swasey" title="John Swasey">John Swasey</a> (Director's Cut and <i>Rebuild</i>) (English)</span></p>
<p>Gendo Ikari <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">碇 ゲンドウ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ikari Gendō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the commander of NERV <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ネルフ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nerufu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> who is responsible for the research done on the Evas, Rei Ayanami, and the Human Instrumentality Project. He is the father of the Third Child, Shinji Ikari, and the guardian of Rei. His original surname was Rokubungi (六分儀), but he changed it to Ikari following his marriage to Yui.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_21_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_21-19">[19]</a></sup> At the start of the series, he is the only person having an effective connection to Rei.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_5_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_5-8">[8]</a></sup> As the series progresses, his unscrupulous past is revealed, as is the intensity with which he loved Yui. It is later revealed that he wished to use the Human Instrumentality Project as a means of reuniting with his wife inside Unit 01, where all human consciousness would be merged into one, with the initiator of the project becoming god-like.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Ritsuko_Akagi">Ritsuko Akagi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Ritsuko Akagi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuriko_Yamaguchi_(voice_actor)" title="Yuriko Yamaguchi (voice actor)">Yuriko Yamaguchi</a> (Japanese); <a href="/wiki/Sue_Ulu" title="Sue Ulu">Sue Ulu</a> (TV and films), <a href="/wiki/Colleen_Clinkenbeard" title="Colleen Clinkenbeard">Colleen Clinkenbeard</a> (Rebuild) (English)</span></p>
<p>Ritsuko Akagi <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">赤木 リツコ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Akagi Ritsuko</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is NERV's head scientist and, along with Gendo and Fuyutsuki, is the only person at NERV fully aware of the Evas' true nature and the purpose of the Instrumentality Project. She is a workaholic and heavy smoker, and has been friends with both Misato and Kaji since college.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_21_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_21-19">[19]</a></sup> She is also romantically involved with Gendo,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_24_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_24-6">[6]</a></sup> but feels betrayed by Gendo when she realizes that he was using her in the same way that he used her mother. As revenge, she destroys the Dummy Plug system and is shot dead in <i>The End of Evangelion</i>, when she tries and fails to activate NERV's self-destruct mechanism to stop Gendo from initiating Third Impact.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Kozo_Fuyutsuki">Kozo Fuyutsuki</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Kozo Fuyutsuki">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Motomu_Kiyokawa" title="Motomu Kiyokawa">Motomu Kiyokawa</a> (Japanese); <a href="/wiki/Guil_Lunde" title="Guil Lunde">Guil Lunde</a> (TV), Michael Ross (films), <a href="/wiki/Kent_Williams_(voice_actor)" title="Kent Williams (voice actor)">Kent Williams</a> (Rebuild) (English)</span></p>
<p>Kozo Fuyutsuki <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">冬月 コウゾウ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Fuyutsuki Kōzō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is NERV's Deputy Commander and Gendo Ikari's right-hand man. He does not especially like Gendo, but he is the closest thing to a confidant that Gendo has in the series.</p>
<p>Once a professor of <a href="/wiki/Metaphysics" title="Metaphysics">metaphysical</a> <a href="/wiki/Biology" title="Biology">biology</a> at <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_University" title="Kyoto University">Kyoto University</a>, Fuyutsuki became interested in the research and theories undertaken by Yui Ikari, and became her sponsor and mentor. Shortly thereafter, he was directed by his superiors to initiate a similar arrangement with Gendo Rokobungi. Later, after the Second Impact, Fuyutsuki traveled with Gendo to the ruins of Antarctica, and began to suspect that Gendo and his associates in SEELE <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ゼーレ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Zēre</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> were more powerful and dangerous than he originally realized. In 2003, Fuyutsuki discovered for himself the true nature of the events leading up to Second Impact and SEELE's involvement in initiating that catastrophe. Gendo led him into the GeoFront and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Neon Genesis Evangelion glossary (page does not exist)">Central Dogma</a>, and asked Fuyutsuki to join him in "creating a new future for mankind."</p>
<p>During a flashback scene in <i>Evangelion: Death and Rebirth</i>, Fuyutsuki mentions that SEELE had threatened to make him "disappear," implying that his recruitment may have been extortionary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_21_19-3"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_21-19">[19]</a></sup> Although Fuyutsuki was never more than a mentor and friend to Yui, it is implied that he was in love with her to some degree, as he was shocked to learn that she was marrying Gendo. In <i>The End of Evangelion</i> Lilith/Rei takes the form of Yui to achieve Fuyutsuki's Instrumentality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LCL_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-LCL-20">[20]</a></sup></p>

Michael Tatum">J. Michael Tatum</a> (Rebuild) (English)</span></p>
<p>Ryoji Kaji <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">加持 リョウジ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kaji Ryōji</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> appears to be a triple agent, working as the chief inspection officer for NERV (Internal Affairs) while secretly investigating NERV for the Japanese government, and at times seemingly acting as a cat's-paw both for and against Gendo Ikari and SEELE - in addition, he appears to have his own personal agenda independent of any organization, searching for the "truth" behind NERV, SEELE and the Human Instrumentality Project. He is eventually shot and killed by an unnamed assailant after defying his employers one time too many,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_19_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_19-21">[21]</a></sup> but not before giving Misato information regarding the truth of the Instrumentality Project.</p>

<p>Makoto Hyuga <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">日向 マコト</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Hyūga Makoto</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a First Lieutenant at NERV and one of the three main computer technicians directly serving under Commanders Ikari and Fuyutsuki in the command post of Central Dogma, the others being Aoba and Ibuki. The true extent of his feelings for Misato is shown in <i>The End of Evangelion</i> as Rei/Lilith takes the form of Misato during Instrumentality and passionately kisses him; he gets reverted to LCL.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LCL_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-LCL-20">[20]</a></sup></p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Maya_Ibuki">Maya Ibuki</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Maya Ibuki">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>Maya Ibuki <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">伊吹 マヤ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ibuki Maya</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a First Lieutenant at NERV and a computer technician who works primarily with Ritsuko Akagi. Her main role during the battles against the Angels is to constantly monitor the Eva pilots’ synchronization ratios and send various emergency commands to the Evas as ordered by Misato or Gendo. In spite of her profession, Maya despises violence of any sort, and is frequently shown becoming nauseated and averting her eyes during battles with the Angels. In <i>The End of Evangelion</i>, Maya is the only NERV officer who is shown refusing to fight back against the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Neon Genesis Evangelion glossary (page does not exist)">JSSDF</a> invaders. Maya holds Ritsuko in very high regard, and always refers to her as "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Senpai" title="Senpai">senpai</a>" in the original anime; <i>The End of Evangelion</i> heavily implies that Maya is actually in love with Ritsuko. Rei/Lilith penetrates Maya's AT field by assuming the form of Ritsuko. Maya exclaims "Ritsuko!/Senpai!", eagerly returns "Ritsuko"'s embrace, and immediately gets reverted to LCL.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LCL_20-2"><a href="#cite_note-LCL-20">[20]</a></sup></p>

<p>Shigeru Aoba <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">青葉 シゲル</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Aoba Shigeru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a First Lieutenant at NERV and a computer technician. <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Kaworu_Nagisa">Kaworu Nagisa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Kaworu Nagisa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kaworu_Nagisa" title="Kaworu Nagisa">Kaworu Nagisa</a></div>
<p><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Akira_Ishida" title="Akira Ishida">Akira Ishida</a> (Japanese); Kyle Sturdivant (TV), <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Krohn" title="Aaron Krohn">Aaron Krohn</a> (films), <a href="/wiki/Greg_Ayres" title="Greg Ayres">Greg Ayres</a> (Director's Cut), <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Jewell" title="Jerry Jewell">Jerry Jewell</a> (Rebuild) (English)</span></p>
<p>Kaworu Nagisa <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">渚 カヲル</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nagisa Kaoru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the Fifth Child, and the seventeenth Angel, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tabris" title="Tabris">Tabris</a>. He is sent to NERV by SEELE as a replacement pilot for Unit 02 after Asuka's synchronization ratio falls below viability; however, his true purpose is to hasten the Human Instrumentality Project's implementation. He breaks into Terminal Dogma to return to Adam and initiate the Third Impact, but upon discovering that the being there is actually Lilith, he implores Shinji to destroy him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_24_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_24-6">[6]</a></sup> After his death, Kaworu appears in the beginning and middle of Third Impact in <i>The End of Evangelion</i>.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Toji_Suzuhara">Toji Suzuhara</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Toji Suzuhara">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Tomokazu_Seki" title="Tomokazu Seki">Tomokazu Seki</a> (Japanese); Joe Pisano (TV, episodes 3-18), Michael O'Connor (TV, episodes 19 and 20), <a href="/wiki/Brett_Weaver" title="Brett Weaver">Brett Weaver</a> (TV, episode 26, and films), <a href="/wiki/Justin_Cook" title="Justin Cook">Justin Cook</a> (Rebuild) (English)</span></p>
<p>Toji Suzuhara <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">鈴原 トウジ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Suzuhara Tōji</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the Fourth Child. Toji is a tough boy, the stereotypical "jock," and is in constant conflict with Asuka. His younger sister was injured in the battle between the third Angel, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Angels_in_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion#Sachiel" title="List of Angels in Neon Genesis Evangelion">Sachiel</a>, and Unit 01, and he beats Shinji up soon after for being indirectly responsible. However, after witnessing firsthand the suffering Shinji experiences piloting Unit 01 in the battle against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shamshel" title="Shamshel">Shamshel</a>, Toji comes to respect Shinji, apologizes to Shinji for beating him up (his sister was angry at him for beating him up and demanded he apologize), and the two eventually become friends.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_4_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_4-5">[5]</a></sup> When the Tokyo branch of NERV gains custody of Unit-03, Toji is identified as the Fourth Child and approached to become an EVA pilot; he agrees in order for his sister to receive better medical treatment. During the activation test for Unit-03, the EVA is possessed by the Angel <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bardiel" title="Bardiel">Bardiel</a> and the unit is destroyed by Unit-01 under the control of the Dummy Plug system when Shinji refuses to fight against the possessed EVA out of concern for the pilot. Unit-01 savagely destroys the possessed Evangelion under extreme protest of Shinji, and crushes the pilot's cockpit capsule, crippling Toji. In the manga, the incident results in Toji's death. In <i><a href="/wiki/Rebuild_of_Evangelion" title="Rebuild of Evangelion">Rebuild of Evangelion</a></i>, Toji does not become a pilot; instead, Asuka pilots Unit-03.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Kensuke_Aida">Kensuke Aida</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Kensuke Aida">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Tetsuya_Iwanaga" title="Tetsuya Iwanaga">Tetsuya Iwanaga</a> (Japanese); Kurt Stoll (TV and films), <a href="/wiki/Greg_Ayres" title="Greg Ayres">Greg Ayres</a> (Rebuild) (English)</span></p>
<p>Kensuke Aida <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">相田 ケンスケ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Aida Kensuke</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is an <a href="/wiki/Otaku" title="Otaku">otaku</a>, a military buff, Toji's best friend, and one of the "Other Children," Eva pilot candidates in the same class as Shinji, Toji, Rei, Asuka, and Hikari. His hobbies include camping in the mountains surrounding Tokyo-3 and playing at simulated war reenacting - by himself. Kensuke solidifies his friendship with Shinji after Shinji saves him and Toji during the fight against the fourth Angel, Shamshel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_4_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_4-5">[5]</a></sup> Kensuke dreams of becoming an Eva pilot and ceaselessly begs Shinji to pull strings in NERV so he can be selected as a pilot, despite Shinji telling him that he has no say in the matter. He also seems oblivious to the intense physical and psychological distress endured by Eva pilots. Nevertheless, Kensuke enjoys the privileges of being the friend of an Eva pilot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_8_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_8-13">[13]</a></sup> Like Toji, Kensuke has a crush on Misato Katsuragi. He is rarely seen without his camcorder at hand. In the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PS2</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Neon_Genesis_Evangelion:_Battle_Orchestra" title="Neon Genesis Evangelion: Battle Orchestra">Battle Orchestra</a></i> game, Kensuke is the appointed pilot to the EVA Unit 04, wearing a green plugsuit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Extra_Pilots_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Extra_Pilots-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Hikari_Horaki">Hikari Horaki</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Hikari Horaki">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Junko_Iwao" title="Junko Iwao">Junko Iwao</a> (Japanese); Carol Amerson (TV), Kimberly Yates (films), <a href="/wiki/Leah_Clark" title="Leah Clark">Leah Clark</a> (Rebuild) (English)</span></p>
<p>Hikari Horaki <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">洞木 ヒカリ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Horaki Hikari</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the class representative of the class that Shinji and the other Eva pilots attend. She has a by-the-book demeanor and takes her responsibilities as "class rep" seriously. Although she frequently bickers with Toji, she reveals to Asuka that she has an enormous crush on Toji but is unable to openly express her feelings to him. She also appears to be Asuka's only friend. Hikari is an accomplished cook who appears to take care of her two sisters. Like presumably every one of Shinji's classmates, Hikari's mother is dead. However, no references were ever made as to whether her father is, either. Her given name, and that of her unseen younger sister, Nozomi, and elder sister, Kodama, is also in reference to the names of the trains of the <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dkaid%C5%8D_Shinkansen" title="Tōkaidō Shinkansen">Tōkaidō Shinkansen</a> line. In a Gainax artwork picture she is shown in an orange plugsuit as the pilot for EVA Unit-05.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Extra_Pilots_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-Extra_Pilots-22">[22]</a></sup> She is also featured in a live action deleted scene in <i>The End of Evangelion</i>.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pen_Pen">Pen Pen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Pen Pen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: Megumi Hayashibara (Japanese); <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Winn-Lee" title="Amanda Winn-Lee">Amanda Winn-Lee</a> (TV and films), <a href="/wiki/Mandy_Clark" title="Mandy Clark">Mandy Clark</a> (Director's Cut), <a href="/wiki/Monica_Rial" title="Monica Rial">Monica Rial</a> (Rebuild) (English)</span></p>
<p>Pen Pen, or Pen² <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ペンペン</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Penpen</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, is a "hot spring penguin" and Misato's pet. He appears to be modeled after a <a href="/wiki/Crested_penguin" title="Crested penguin">crested penguin</a> with some minor coloration change. He lives in a custom refrigerator in Misato's apartment, enjoys taking warm baths, eating whatever food he can get, and going to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_springs" title="Hot springs">hot springs</a>. He also appears to have a relatively high degree of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intelligence_(trait)" title="Intelligence (trait)">intelligence</a>, as he is also shown watching TV and reading newspapers. In the manga, he is the result of a <a href="/wiki/Genetics" title="Genetics">genetics</a> experiment at Misato's former workplace, and she adopted him after the experiment's conclusion rather than let him be <a href="/wiki/Animal_euthanasia" title="Animal euthanasia">euthanised</a>. His wingtips have retractable <a href="/wiki/Claw" title="Claw">claws</a>, which modern birds do not have.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Naoko_Akagi">Naoko Akagi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Naoko Akagi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Mika_Doi" title="Mika Doi">Mika Doi</a> (Japanese); <a href="/wiki/Laura_Chapman" title="Laura Chapman">Laura Chapman</a> (English)</span></p>
<p>Naoko Akagi <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">赤木 ナオコ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Akagi Naoko</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> was Ritsuko's mother. She was the creator of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Neon Genesis Evangelion glossary (page does not exist)">MAGI</a> system<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and the former GEHIRN <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ゲへリン</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Geherin</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> head technician. Naoko was involved in a romantic relationship with the recently widowed Gendo Ikari while working at GEHIRN. However, after Rei I called her an "old hag" and said that Gendo himself called Naoko that frequently, she realized that he had merely been using her affections to ensure her performance for him. In a fit of rage, Naoko strangled Rei to death and then committed suicide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_21_19-4"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_21-19">[19]</a></sup> Soon afterward, GEHIRN was disbanded and the organization NERV was formed in its place. In vol. 10 of <a href="/wiki/Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_(manga)" title="Neon Genesis Evangelion (manga)">the manga</a>, Naoko is shown to commit suicide after killing Rei I. Ritsuko witnesses both Rei's murder and Naoko's suicide.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Yui_Ikari">Yui Ikari</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Yui Ikari">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: Megumi Hayashibara (Japanese); <a href="/wiki/Kim_Sevier" title="Kim Sevier">Kim Sevier</a> (TV), <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Winn-Lee" title="Amanda Winn-Lee">Amanda Winn-Lee</a> (films), <a href="/wiki/Stephanie_Young" title="Stephanie Young">Stephanie Young</a> (Rebuild) (English)</span></p>
<p>Yui Ikari <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">碇 ユイ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ikari Yui</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> was a student of genetic engineering at Kyoto University, where she met her future husband Gendo and her mentor and trusted friend Kozo Fuyutsuki. Afterwards, she joined the UN Artificial Evolution Laboratory (the future GEHIRN) in Hakone, Japan, and played an integral role in the E Project. She served as the test subject for the Contact Experiment of Unit 01 in 2004, but "disappeared" during testing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_21_19-5"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_21-19">[19]</a></sup> Though proclaimed dead, a part of her (her "soul") lives on within Unit 01.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_20_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_20-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>The final volume of the <i>Evangelion</i> manga reveals that Yui had made a conscious choice to remain with EVA unit 01 during the test that caused her disappearance. She confided her choice only to Fuyutsuki days prior to the test happening and made him promise never to reveal it to Gendo.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Kyoko_Zeppelin_Soryu">Kyoko Zeppelin Soryu</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Kyoko Zeppelin Soryu">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Maria_Kawamura" title="Maria Kawamura">Maria Kawamura</a> (Japanese); Yvonne Aguirre (TV), Kimberly Yates (films) (English)</span></p>
<p>Kyoko Zeppelin Soryu <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">惣流・キョウコ・ツェッペリン</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sōryū Kyōko Tsepperin</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, Asuka's mother, was a key scientist in the German division of GEHIRN (what would later become NERV's Third Branch) and the development of Unit 02, the first Production Type (the second and third of which were later produced by the two American branches of NERV). She was the subject of the Contact Experiment with Unit 02, but during the experiment her soul bonded with the EVA, causing severe mental damage to Kyoko. She became mentally unstable, believing that one of Asuka's dolls was her daughter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Episode_22_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Episode_22-14">[14]</a></sup> She was admitted to a mental institution shortly after the experiment. Her husband had an affair with Kyoko's own doctor, which eventually prompted Kyoko's suicide in 2005.</p>
<p>In the manga version, Kyoko and her husband were unable to conceive a child, and divorced after he had an affair. He remarried and had a daughter, while Kyoko visited a <a href="/wiki/Sperm_bank" title="Sperm bank">sperm bank</a> and became pregnant. She tried to poison Asuka to spite her ex-husband and his other child, referring to Asuka coldly as "that girl." At one point, Kyoko tried to strangle Asuka. After the failed contact experiment, her violence was instead transferred to Asuka's doll, as she was clearly in a dissociative state from the trauma. In the <a href="/wiki/Rebuild_of_Evangelion" title="Rebuild of Evangelion">Rebuild of Evangelion</a> movies, however, her story is omitted.</p>
<p>According to Anno's notes on the origins of his characters' names, "Soryu" is from the same Japanese warship as Asuka, "Kyoko" is also from a character in Shinji Wada's manga (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chou-Shoujo_Asuka&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chou-Shoujo Asuka (page does not exist)">Chou-Shoujo Asuka</a>), and "Zeppelin" is from a warship of the German navy that was envisioned but never made (named after <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_von_Zeppelin" title="Ferdinand von Zeppelin">Ferdinand von Zeppelin</a>, German general and aircraft manufacturer).</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Keel_Lorenz">Keel Lorenz</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Keel Lorenz">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Mugihito" title="Mugihito">Mugihito</a> (Japanese); Rick Peeples (TV), Tom Booker (films), Bill Jenkins (Rebuild) (English)</span></p>
<p>Keel Lorenz <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">キール・ローレンツ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kīru Rōrentsu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the leader of SEELE, represented as SEELE 01 in the monoliths and the main antagonist of the series. Among his most defining characteristics are his ancient appearance and the futuristic visor that he wears throughout the series. Little is revealed about him in the series, but he is believed to hold great political power in the UN. At some time between Second Impact and 2015, most of his body below the neck was replaced with cybernetic implants. Although he uses his visor to see, production drawings show that he still has eyes. The inspiration for his name (and, arguably, his character) originates from <a href="/wiki/Konrad_Lorenz" title="Konrad Lorenz">Konrad Lorenz</a>, a zoologist known especially for his research on animal psychology. In the manga, his name is given as <i>Lorenz Kiel</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Angels">Angels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Angels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <b>Angels</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">使徒</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shito</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> are the fictional supernatural beings that serve as the series' antagonists, attacking <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>-3 throughout the story in both the anime and manga.</p>
<p>Though they all have very different forms and abilities, Angels are often depicted with several common elements, such as the ability to project <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Force_shield" title="Force shield">AT fields</a> and an internal power source called an S² engine, the destruction of which is often the only way an Angel can be exterminated. Even though they are composed of an altogether different form of matter ("characterized by <a href="/wiki/Wave%E2%80%93particle_duality" title="Wave–particle duality">both particulate and wave properties</a>, like light"), the Angels' genetic code bears a 99.89% similarity to that of humans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first two Angels, Adam and Lilith, are the progenitors of the other sixteen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Extras:_Eva_Glossary_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Extras:_Eva_Glossary-26">[26]</a></sup> While the first fifteen Angels are descendants of Adam, <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">humans</a> themselves descended from Lilith. Using the Biblical story of the <a href="/wiki/Tree_of_the_knowledge_of_good_and_evil" title="Tree of the knowledge of good and evil">tree of the knowledge of good and evil</a>, the Angels are described as having eaten the "Fruit of Life", giving them immortality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> At another point, it is stated that the Angels are merely different evolutionary paths that mankind could have taken.</p>
<p>While the original Japanese word used in the series to refer to the Angels is <i>shito</i> <span style="font-weight:normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">使徒</span><span class="t_nihongo_help"><sup><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span style="color:#00e; font:bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration:none; padding:0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup></span>, apostle or disciple)</span>, as opposed to the typical translation of "angel" as <i>tenshi</i> <span style="font-weight:normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">天使</span><span class="t_nihongo_help"><sup><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span style="color:#00e; font:bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration:none; padding:0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup></span>, messenger of heaven)</span>, the English translation was one specified by <a href="/wiki/Gainax" title="Gainax">Gainax</a>. The word "<a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/angel" title="wikt:angel">angel</a>" itself is derived from the Greek for "messenger" (<span lang="el" xml:lang="el">ἄγγελος</span>, <i>angelos</i>), which is also the source of the word "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evangelium" title="Evangelium">Evangelion</a>".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Adam">Adam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Adam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first Angel is <b>Adam</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">アダム</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Adamu</i>)</span>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> named for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adam_(Bible)" title="Adam (Bible)">the first human</a> created by <a href="/wiki/God" title="God">God</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Genesis" title="Book of Genesis">Book of Genesis</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Abrahamic_religions" title="Abrahamic religions">Abrahamic</a> religious beliefs. All Angels, except for Lilith and the Lilin, are descended from Adam and possess the "Fruit of Life", the source of the Angels' power and immortality (also known as an S² Engine). In the <a href="/wiki/Spin-off_(media)" title="Spin-off (media)">spin off</a> <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video game</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_2" title="Neon Genesis Evangelion 2">Neon Genesis Evangelion 2</a></i>, it is stated that Adam and the White Moon were planted by an unknown "<a href="/wiki/Ancient_astronauts" title="Ancient astronauts">First Ancestral Race</a>" as the intended progenitor of life on Earth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Adam is discovered within the White Moon buried under <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a> and, during the Katsuragi Expedition, is subjected to a "contact experiment" with the Spear of Longinus. The failure of the experiment results in the brief appearance of a "giant of light" (resembling an Evangelion), the occurrence of Second Impact, and Adam being reduced to an embryonic form while its soul is reincarnated in <a href="/wiki/Kaworu_Nagisa" title="Kaworu Nagisa">Kaworu Nagisa</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ryoji_Kaji" title="Ryoji Kaji">Ryoji Kaji</a> later delivers the embryonic Adam to NERV commander <a href="/wiki/Gendo_Ikari" title="Gendo Ikari">Gendo Ikari</a>, who later grafts it to his right palm and, in <i><a href="/wiki/The_End_of_Evangelion" title="The End of Evangelion">The End of Evangelion</a></i>, tries to physically merge Rei with Adam by pushing his right hand into her body.</p>
<p>Cells cultivated from Adam serve as the basis for all Evangelion units except for Unit-01, which is grown from cells cultivated from Lilith.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Yoshiyuki_Sadamoto" title="Yoshiyuki Sadamoto">Yoshiyuki Sadamoto</a>'s official <a href="/wiki/Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_(manga)" title="Neon Genesis Evangelion (manga)">manga</a> of <i>Evangelion</i>, Gendo swallows the embryonic Adam and gains the ability to manifest an AT field.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lilith">Lilith</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Lilith">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The second Angel is <b>Lilith</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">リリス</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ririsu</i>)</span>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> named for the first wife of Adam created in <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Jewish</a>-oriented mythologies (while not Judaism proper). Lilith is discovered in the Black Moon underneath <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, which is converted into the GeoFront, with Lilith's resting place being called the Terminal Dogma. After <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yui_Ikari" title="Yui Ikari">Yui Ikari</a>'s contact experiment with <a href="/wiki/Evangelion_(mecha)#EVA01" title="Evangelion (mecha)">Unit-01</a>, Lilith's soul was placed into <a href="/wiki/Rei_Ayanami" title="Rei Ayanami">Rei Ayanami</a>.</p>
<p>Lilith initially appears in the series as a white legless giant with the waists and legs of small humanoid bodies emerging from the stump of its waist. It is <a href="/wiki/Crucifixion" title="Crucifixion">crucified</a> onto a giant cross and wearing a mask with three eyes on the right side and four eyes on the left, the same motif as the Seele logo. Lilith's torso constantly leaks LCL, an amber-colored, translucent liquid, which allows an Eva pilot to mentally link with their Evangelion Unit, from an unseen wound, forming a vast reservoir used to supply its use in the Evangelion entry plugs. In the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_2" title="Neon Genesis Evangelion 2">Neon Genesis Evangelion 2</a></i>, it is stated that the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spear_of_Longinus" title="Spear of Longinus">Spear of Longinus</a> used to hold Lilith was originally created for Adam, Lilith's own assigned Spear presumably having been lost during <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Giant_impact_hypothesis" title="Giant impact hypothesis">First Impact</a>. When Kaji revealed Lilith to <a href="/wiki/Misato_Katsuragi" title="Misato Katsuragi">Misato Katsuragi</a>, both assume they are in fact looking at Adam (Kaworu first correctly identifies Lilith).</p>
<p>In <i>The End of Evangelion</i>, after Rei rejects Gendo's attempt to initiate his version of Instrumentality using the grafted embryonic Adam, Rei and the absorbed embryo merge with Lilith. The resultant being, taking the form of a giant winged Rei, generates a massive anti-AT field that nullifies the AT fields of all humans, reverting them to a <a href="/wiki/Primordial_soup" title="Primordial soup">primordial soup</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tabris">Tabris</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Tabris">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kaworu_Nagisa" title="Kaworu Nagisa">Kaworu Nagisa</a></div>
<p><b>Tabris</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">タブリス</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Taburisu</i>)</span>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ADV_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-ADV-32">[32]</a></sup> the seventeenth and final Angel, holds the form of a human, Kaworu Nagisa. Like the other Angels, he is "born of Adam", although it is implied that he was created by Seele. He contains the soul of Adam, similar to how Rei contains the soul of Lilith. Tabris' AT field is the most powerful ever detected, strong enough to block out "<a href="/wiki/Light" title="Light">light</a>, <a href="/wiki/Magnetism" title="Magnetism">magnetism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Subatomic_particle" title="Subatomic particle">subatomic particles</a>, <i>everything</i>". He may control any Evangelion unit he wishes, even from outside the entry plug, so long as the soul inhabiting the Eva is dormant. Within the entry plug, he can set his synchronization ratio at any level he wishes. The nature or even the very existence of his core is not revealed.</p>
<p>Tabris, as Kaworu, infiltrated NERV under the pretense of being sent to replace Asuka as Unit 02's pilot. During his stay in Tokyo-3, he formed an extremely close relationship with Shinji. Kaworu later revealed himself as an Angel, seizing control of Unit 02 and descending with it towards Terminal Dogma. Shinji, in Unit 01, was sent to stop him, but was forced to battle Unit 02 while Tabris proceeded to Terminal Dogma. Tabris' AT field manifested during the battle, effectively cutting both of them off from all contact with anyone outside. After he reached Terminal Dogma, Tabris was surprised to discover that the Angel held there was not Adam, but Lilith. Shinji incapacitated Unit 02 and proceeded to try to stop the Angel. Tabris, gripped in Unit 01's hand, stated his unwillingness to destroy mankind by causing Third Impact, and asked Shinji to kill him. Shinji, after a long period of hesitation, crushed the Angel in his Eva's hand.</p>
<p>He is named after the Angel of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Free_Will" title="Free Will">Free Will</a> and Alternatives <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">自由の天使</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Jiyū no Tenshi</i>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TV_guide_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-TV_guide-33">[33]</a></sup> (This naming convention breaks from most of the other Angels in that it uses a Yah <a href="/wiki/Theophory_in_the_Bible" title="Theophory in the Bible">theophory</a>, rather than an El theophory.) The back of the DVD containing episodes 24–26 spells his name as "Kaoru". However, in <i>The End of Evangelion</i>, his name is written as "Kaworu" on the Dummy Plugs for the <a href="/wiki/Evangelion_(mecha)#Mass_Production_.28MP.29_Evangelions_.28Units_05_-_13.29" title="Evangelion (mecha)">mass-production Evas</a>. Kaworu Nagisa's first appearance in the <i>Rebuild of Evangelion</i> series is shortly after the battle with Ramiel. Unlike in the anime, Kaworu and Shinji do not battle. When the Fourth Impact seems imminent, he reveals himself as an Angel and subsequently sacrifices himself.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lilin">Lilin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Lilin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Lilin" title="Lilin">Lilin</a></div>
<p>The eighteenth Angel, <b>Lilin</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">リリン</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ririn</i>)</span>, is said by Misato in <i>The End of Evangelion</i> to be mankind itself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-End_of_Evangelion_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-End_of_Evangelion-34">[34]</a></sup> It is also the name used by Tabris to describe mankind, in reference to their progenitor, Lilith. As Lilith's descendants, the Lilin are endowed with the "Fruit of Wisdom", which is the source of their intelligence and technology. Like the Angels, Lilin also have AT fields, but unlike the Angels who can physically manifest theirs, the AT fields of Lilin are merely what separates each human from all others, creating individual identity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Misato tells Shinji that humans are the eighteenth Angel born from Lilith, not on Earth.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearances_in_other_media">Appearances in other media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: Appearances in other media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Rebuild_of_Evangelion"><i>Rebuild of Evangelion</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: Rebuild of Evangelion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the <i><a href="/wiki/Rebuild_of_Evangelion" title="Rebuild of Evangelion">Rebuild of Evangelion</a></i> film series, many of the Angels return along with some differences and new original designs. The order of the Angels have been changed and some Angels that were originally in the anime do not appear in the remake. Most of the angels die differently from in the original version.</p>
<p>Two new unnamed Angels are introduced in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evangelion:_2.22_You_Can_(Not)_Advance" title="Evangelion: 2.22 You Can (Not) Advance">Evangelion: 2.22 You Can (Not) Advance</a></i>. The first is the new Third Angel, designed by illustrator <a href="/wiki/Mohiro_Kitoh" title="Mohiro Kitoh">Mohiro Kitoh</a>, which appears as a skeleton's spinal column atop a small body with miniature legs and had a bird-like head which contained the Angel's core. The Angel is being studied at NERV's Bethany Base in Russia when it escapes, and is ultimately destroyed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mari_Illustrious_Makinami" title="Mari Illustrious Makinami">Mari Illustrious Makinami</a> in the Provisional Evangelion Unit 05. Kaji claims that it appears as a skeleton because the NERV scientists performed such extensive tests that what was shown is all that is left of the Angel's body. The second unnamed Angel is the new Seventh Angel, designed by <a href="/wiki/Kazuya_Tsurumaki" title="Kazuya Tsurumaki">Kazuya Tsurumaki</a>, which appears as a <a href="/wiki/Drinking_bird" title="Drinking bird">drinking bird</a>-like body made out of floating metal shapes with a large pendulum that holds the Angel's core and two stilt-like double helix legs that freeze water to allow it to walk on the ocean. Its head also has two <a href="/wiki/Clock" title="Clock">clock</a> hand-like appendages that can spin to unleash an attack. The Angel is ultimately destroyed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asuka_Langley_Shikinami" title="Asuka Langley Shikinami">Asuka Langley Shikinami</a> in Evangelion Unit 02. A third unnamed Angel is introduced in <i><a href="/wiki/Evangelion:_3.0_You_Can_(Not)_Redo" title="Evangelion: 3.0 You Can (Not) Redo">Evangelion: 3.0 You Can (Not) Redo</a></i> as the new Twelfth Angel. It was sealed within Mark.06 and was released when Mark.09 decapitates Mark.06, after which the Angel gets absorbed by Unit-13.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Other">Other</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: Other">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Gainax has several other Angels appearing in other Neon Genesis Evangelion titles. In the game <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evangelion_2nd_Impression" title="Evangelion 2nd Impression">Evangelion 2nd Impression</a></i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion#Mayumi_Yamagishi" title="List of characters in Neon Genesis Evangelion">Mayumi Yamagishi</a> is an Angel known as the Insubstantial Angel. Gainax has released a number of tenth-anniversary figurines under the Angel Chromosome XX label. The figurines are designed by <i><a href="/wiki/Sgt._Frog" title="Sgt. Frog">Keroro Gunso</a></i> artist <a href="/wiki/Mine_Yoshizaki" title="Mine Yoshizaki">Mine Yoshizaki</a> and are female <a href="/wiki/Anthropomorphism" title="Anthropomorphism">anthropomorphized</a> versions of several Angels. The packaging for the figurines gives the appearance of being a NERV lab sample, and is marked with a <a href="/wiki/Biological_hazard" title="Biological hazard">biohazard</a> symbol.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Lilith, Sachiel, Zeruel, Armisael, and Tabris are featured under the "Angel-XX" line, as well as a figure called "Code:BE", which is virtually identical to the Lilith figure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Shamshel, Sahaquiel, and Arael are featured under the "Angel-XX: Next" line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Another line, "Angel-XX: nano!", features <a href="/wiki/Moe_(slang)" title="Moe (slang)">moe</a> versions of the Lilith, Sachiel, Zeruel, and Tabris figurines, plus a Leliel figure unique to the 'nano' range.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Two additional Angels also appear in the Evangelion <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tabletop_roleplaying_game" title="Tabletop roleplaying game">tabletop roleplaying game</a>. Both are named after figures from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qur%27an" title="Qur'an">Qur'an</a>, these being <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iblis" title="Iblis">Iblis</a> which appears as a turtle-like creature and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baraqijal" title="Baraqijal">Baraqijal</a>, a coral-like being with a single enormous eye encased in a gelatinous orb that uses electricity-based attacks. In <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neon_Genesis_Evangelion:_Gakuen_Datenroku" title="Neon Genesis Evangelion: Gakuen Datenroku">Neon Genesis Evangelion: Gakuen Datenroku</a></i>, the Angels are souls made up into a red spherical object called "Core". They can take over the body of a creature but it also kills the soul of the original host.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rebuild_of_Evangelion_2"><i>Rebuild of Evangelion</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=36" title="Edit section: Rebuild of Evangelion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mari_Illustrious_Makinami">Mari Illustrious Makinami</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=37" title="Edit section: Mari Illustrious Makinami">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Maaya_Sakamoto" title="Maaya Sakamoto">Maaya Sakamoto</a> (Japanese); <a href="/wiki/Trina_Nishimura" title="Trina Nishimura">Trina Nishimura</a> (English)</span></p>
<p>Mari Illustrious Makinami<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">真希波・マリ・イラストリアス</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Makinami Mari Irasutoriasu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is an enigmatic Evangelion pilot introduced in <i><a href="/wiki/Rebuild_of_Evangelion" title="Rebuild of Evangelion">Rebuild of Evangelion</a>'</i>s second installment <i><a href="/wiki/Evangelion:_2.0_You_Can_(Not)_Advance" title="Evangelion: 2.0 You Can (Not) Advance">Evangelion: 2.0 You Can (Not) Advance</a></i>.</p>
<p>She is the confident and eccentric pilot of the Provisional Evangelion Unit-05, which differs drastically from previous incarnations, featuring four mechanical "legs" that end in wheels and a lack of hands which have been replaced with a jousting lance and "claw". It also is worth noting she appears several years older than the other Children. Because of its unique design, Mari pilots her Eva wearing a helmet-like contraption, and the wrists of her plugsuit have several attachments that connect to tubing that apparently helps her in moving the EVA. Even while piloting Unit-02, her pink plugsuit features a symbolized 5 on the back. She has poor vision, wearing glasses at all times and having difficulty finding them when dropped after colliding with Shinji while parachuting. Mari's designation amongst the other Children is mysterious, though she is once referred to as "Problem Child". She self-destructs Unit-05 to defeat one of the Angels in the film's opening, but manages to escape unharmed. During the film's climax, she uses Unit-02 to battle the 10th Angel but is unable to, she sees Shinji who, after seeing the destruction, runs to NERV HQ. She has a fondness for LCL, telling Shinji, "You smell good... Smell of LCL." She also differs from the other pilots as she takes things in great strides as a pilot, even the immense pains of being attacked while synced with an EVA, falling on Shinji while using a parachute, smiling when the 3rd impact seems inevitable, and even singing a song in Unit-05 when about to fight an Angel. Since Toji did not pilot an Eva in the Rebuild series, Mari is sometimes referred to as the "Fourth Child" of the series by fans, but this has not been referenced in official material.</p>
<p>Sadamoto designed her to look British, and added glasses as a 'simple yet effective way to set her apart from the other characters'<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> The June 2010 issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Newtype" title="Newtype">Newtype</a></i> magazine ranked Mari #1 in its monthly top-10-character survey.</p>
<p>In the last volume of the Evangelion Manga, an extra chapter titled "Eden in Summer" introduces her as a 16-year-old student in 1998. She knew Yui Ikari and Ritsuko as a fellow student under Professor Fuyutsuki. It appears she's a genius, having arrived at Kyoto University at an early age. However, she is no match for Yui Ikari's brilliance and is very jealous of her. In a fit of jealousy, she steals Yui's glasses. Later on, while helping Yui clean up a mess caused by her impaired vision, her purse falls to the floor revealing the missing glasses. At first Mari tells Yui she hates her, but once she calms down she actually admits she likes Yui. She allows Yui to fix her hair and tells her that she will leave to study abroad in England. Yui then gives her the glasses as a gift and finishes fixing Mari's hair, which is how she continues to wear it through the Eva movies.</p>
<p>Based on her age in 1998, she would be in her mid-thirties during the events prior to Third Impact.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_games">Video games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=39" title="Edit section: Video games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mana_Kirishima">Mana Kirishima</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=40" title="Edit section: Mana Kirishima">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: Megumi Hayashibara</span></p>
<p>Mana Kirishima <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">霧島 マナ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kirishima Mana</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-girlfriend_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-girlfriend-42">[42]</a></sup> is the main focus of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Girlfriend_of_Steel" title="Girlfriend of Steel">Girlfriend of Steel</a></i>, a game that is like an episode of the series. She appears as a transfer student at the Children's school, and becomes the focus of Shinji's interest and Asuka's jealous ire. She is the pilot of another man-made replacement for the Evas, the <i>Trident</i>, whose combat skills are somewhat more on par with the Evangelions', to whom they bear a resemblance in design compared to the <i>Jet Alone</i> robot. She also appears in <i>Shinji Ikari Raising Project</i>. Like many <i>Evangelion</i> characters, Mana's family name, Kirishima, is taken from a World War II battleship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>A shy female character, Mana Kirishima, starts at Shinji Ikari's school and immediately admits to finding him cute. Gradually, a relationship unfolds and they end up going on a date where they kiss. It turns out, however, that she is one of three test pilots of TRIDENT, a new competitor to the Evangelions, similar to Jet Alone (thus the name "Girlfriend of Steel"). Eventually, the other robot goes berserk and the Evas have to destroy it. Throughout the game, Asuka is constantly jealous of Shinji and Mana's relationship, consistent with her romantic feelings for Shinji. Rei is present but plays a rather background role in the story.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mayumi_Yamagishi">Mayumi Yamagishi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=41" title="Edit section: Mayumi Yamagishi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kyoko_Hikami" title="Kyoko Hikami">Kyoko Hikami</a></span></p>
<p>Mayumi Yamagishi <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">山岸 マユミ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yamagishi Mayumi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> is the new character introduced in the <a href="/wiki/Sega_Saturn" title="Sega Saturn">Sega Saturn</a> game <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evangelion_2nd_Impression" title="Evangelion 2nd Impression">Evangelion 2nd Impression</a></i>. In the game, Mayumi is a bespectacled bookworm who somehow has the core of an Angel inside her body. She joins Shinji's class and befriends him, and like many of the <i>Evangelion</i> cast, has a dark history (her mother apparently committed suicide while Mayumi was a girl, much like Asuka's mother).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=42" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The character designs by <a href="/wiki/Yoshiyuki_Sadamoto" title="Yoshiyuki Sadamoto">Yoshiyuki Sadamoto</a> have also contributed to the popularity of <i>Evangelion</i>. Sadamoto's attractive designs of the three main female leads, Asuka, Rei and Misato, led to extremely high sales of merchandise<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> (especially of Rei, the "Premium Girl"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup>), and they have been immortalized in the <a href="/wiki/D%C5%8Djinshi" title="Dōjinshi">dōjinshi</a> community,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-azuma_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-azuma-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
