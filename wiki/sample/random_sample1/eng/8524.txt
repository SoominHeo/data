<p>The <b>Cincinnati Mohawks</b> were a professional <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> team in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cincinnati,_Ohio" title="Cincinnati, Ohio">Cincinnati, Ohio</a>. They were a member of the <a href="/wiki/American_Hockey_League" title="American Hockey League">American Hockey League</a> (AHL) between 1949 and 1952. They were founded as the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Lions" title="Washington Lions">Washington Lions</a>, then were relocated from <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a></p>
<p>They played their home games in the <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Gardens" title="Cincinnati Gardens">Cincinnati Gardens</a>. After leaving the AHL, the Mohawks team transferred to the <a href="/wiki/International_Hockey_League_(1945%E2%80%932001)" title="International Hockey League (1945–2001)">International Hockey League</a> (IHL) until folding in 1958. The Mohawks won an IHL-record five consecutive <a href="/wiki/Turner_Cup" title="Turner Cup">Turner Cup</a> Championships starting with the <a href="/wiki/1952%E2%80%9353_IHL_season" title="1952–53 IHL season">1952–53 season</a> and ending with the <a href="/wiki/1956%E2%80%9357_IHL_season" title="1956–57 IHL season">1956–57</a> season.</p>
<p>The market was subsequently home to:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Swords" title="Cincinnati Swords">Cincinnati Swords</a> (1971–1974 <a href="/wiki/American_Hockey_League" title="American Hockey League">AHL</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Stingers" title="Cincinnati Stingers">Cincinnati Stingers</a> (1975–1979 <a href="/wiki/World_Hockey_Association" title="World Hockey Association">WHA</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Cyclones" title="Cincinnati Cyclones">Cincinnati Cyclones</a> (1990–1992, 2001–2004, 2006–present <a href="/wiki/ECHL" title="ECHL">ECHL</a>, 1992–2001 IHL)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Mighty_Ducks" title="Cincinnati Mighty Ducks">Cincinnati Mighty Ducks</a> (1997–2005 <a href="/wiki/American_Hockey_League" title="American Hockey League">AHL</a>)</li>
</ul>
