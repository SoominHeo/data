<p><i><b>Faith Fighter</b></i> is an online <a href="/wiki/Fighting_game" title="Fighting game">fighting game</a> developed by the <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> website <a href="/wiki/Molleindustria" title="Molleindustria">Molleindustria</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> in which players fight as religious figures such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buddha" title="Buddha">Buddha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jesus" title="Jesus">Jesus</a> or <a href="/wiki/Muhammad" title="Muhammad">Muhammad</a> and must fight <a href="/wiki/Xenu" title="Xenu">Xenu</a> after beating all playable characters.</p>
<p>The game was temporarily pulled from its hosting web site in late April 2009, in response to protests from the Islamophobia Observatory of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Organisation_of_the_Islamic_Conference" title="Organisation of the Islamic Conference">Organisation of the Islamic Conference</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In response, the game's creators posted a sequel to the game with the figure of Muhammad's face censored, in which the player must bestow "love" by clicking on each religious figure in turn: without this action, the figures slowly fade away.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The original game has since been reposted on the maker's website.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Universo_Online" title="Universo Online">Universo Online</a> was forced to remove the game from its servers following a judicial ruling, in a case sponsored by a mosque in <a href="/wiki/Barretos" title="Barretos">Barretos</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_state" title="São Paulo state">São Paulo state</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
