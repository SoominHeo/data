<p><b>Club Deportivo Castellón, S.A.D.</b> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_football_clubs_in_Spain" title="List of football clubs in Spain">Spanish football team</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Castell%C3%B3n_de_la_Plana" title="Castellón de la Plana">Castellón de la Plana</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Valencian_Community" title="Valencian Community">Valencian Community</a>. Founded in 1922, it currently plays in <a href="/wiki/Tercera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Tercera División">Tercera División – Group 6</a>, holding home games at <i><a href="/wiki/Nou_Estadi_Cast%C3%A0lia" title="Nou Estadi Castàlia">Nou Estadi Castàlia</a></i>, which has a capacity of 16,000.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CD_Castell%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Football first appeared in the town in 1911 and, after a period of time under the consecutive denominations "Deportivo", "Castalia", "Gimnástico", "Cultural" and "Cervantes", Club Deportivo Castellón was founded on 22 July 1922.</p>
<p>The club featured periodically in the <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">top flight</a>, finishing fifth in <a href="/wiki/1972%E2%80%9373_La_Liga" title="1972–73 La Liga">1972–73</a> and adding a <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Cup</a> final appearance with a team featuring <a href="/wiki/Vicente_del_Bosque" title="Vicente del Bosque">Vicente del Bosque</a>, who later moved to <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a>, serving the club as both a player and coach.</p>
<p>On 29 August 1991, in an extraordinary assembly, the conversion of the club to S.A.D was approved. The first team had just <a href="/wiki/1990%E2%80%9391_La_Liga" title="1990–91 La Liga">relegated</a> into <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">division two</a>, and would drop another level to <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="Segunda División B">the third</a> <a href="/wiki/1993%E2%80%9394_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="1993–94 Segunda División">three years later</a>, in a spell which would last more than one decade.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="2004–05 Segunda División B">2004–05 season</a>, Castellón finished fourth in <i>Segunda B</i>, eventually winning its promotion playoffs (both matches) and achieving a return to the silver category. The club's stint in the division would last five years, as relegation would befall in <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2009–10 Segunda División">2009–10</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Valencian_Community" title="Valencian Community">Valencian Community</a> outfit ranking last, 13 points behind the following team.</p>
<p>On 18 July 2011, due to the team not paying its players, Castellón was excluded from the third division, being relegated to <a href="/wiki/Tercera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Tercera División">the fourth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
