<p><b>Hyewon pungsokdo</b> is an album of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genre_paintings" title="Genre paintings">genre paintings</a> (<i>pungsokhwa</i> or <i>pungsokdo</i>) drawn by the Korean painter <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shin_Yunbok" title="Shin Yunbok">Shin Yunbok</a> during the late <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseon_dynasty" title="Joseon dynasty">Joseon dynasty</a>. It was named after Shin's <a href="/wiki/Pen_name" title="Pen name">pen name</a>, <b>Hyewon</b>, and comprises 30 paintings in total.</p>
<p>In 1930, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jeon_Hyeopil&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jeon Hyeopil (page does not exist)">Jeon Hyeopil</a> (전형필:全鎣弼), later the founder of the <a href="/wiki/Gansong_Art_Museum" title="Gansong Art Museum">Gansong Art Museum</a>, purchased it from an antique dealer in <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> and newly mounted the album. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Oh_Sechang&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Oh Sechang (page does not exist)">Oh Sechang</a> (오세창), who was a journalist and pro-independence activist, wrote the subtitles and postface for the album. <i>Hyewon pungsokdo</i> is designated as the 135th <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_treasures_of_South_Korea" title="National treasures of South Korea">National Treasure of South Korea</a> and is held in the collection of the <a href="/wiki/Gansong_Art_Museum" title="Gansong Art Museum">Gansong Art Museum</a> located in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seongbuk-gu" title="Seongbuk-gu">Seongbuk-gu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, South Korea.</p>
