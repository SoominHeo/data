<p>The <b>Xinyi District</b> is the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei</a> mayor's office and the <a href="/wiki/Taipei_City_Government" title="Taipei City Government">Taipei City Government</a>. Its key buildings including <a href="/wiki/Taipei_101" title="Taipei 101">Taipei 101</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taipei_City_Council" title="Taipei City Council">Taipei City Council</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taipei_International_Convention_Center" title="Taipei International Convention Center">Taipei International Convention Center</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taipei_World_Trade_Center" title="Taipei World Trade Center">Taipei World Trade Center</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sun_Yat-sen_Memorial_Hall" title="Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall">National Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall</a> and various <a href="/wiki/Shopping_mall" title="Shopping mall">shopping malls</a> and entertainment venues make Xinyi the most modern cosmopolitan district of Taipei. Xinyi District is also considered the financial district of Taipei.</p>
<p>The district is today a prime shopping area in Taipei, if not all of <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>. Xinyi District is anchored by a number of department stores and malls. In addition, numerous high-end restaurants are located in the area, especially <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chain_restaurants#Restaurant_chains" title="Chain restaurants">chain restaurants</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_geography">History and geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinyi_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_under_Japanese_rule" title="Taiwan under Japanese rule">Japanese rule</a> (1895-1945), Matsuyama Village <span style="font-weight: normal">(<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">松山庄</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> covered modern day Xinyi and <a href="/wiki/Songshan_District,_Taipei" title="Songshan District, Taipei">Songshan</a>. The village was named after <a href="/wiki/Matsuyama,_Ehime" title="Matsuyama, Ehime">Matsuyama City</a> in Japan and formed part of Shichisei District, <a href="/wiki/Taihoku_Prefecture" title="Taihoku Prefecture">Taihoku Prefecture</a>. Matsuyama was incorporated into Taihoku City (modern-day Taipei) in 1938.</p>
<p>In 1990, as Taiwan began its modern era of multiparty <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Songshan_District,_Taipei" title="Songshan District, Taipei">Songshan District</a> was split into two as part of a city-wide reorganization. The southern half became Xinyi District while the northern half retained its name. The border between the two shifted slightly in May 1994 when the <a href="/wiki/Keelung_River" title="Keelung River">Keelung River</a> was artificially moved slightly to the south. The present border of Xinyi runs along <a href="/wiki/Civic_Blvd_Expressway" title="Civic Blvd Expressway">Civic Boulevard</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taiwan_Railway_Administration" title="Taiwan Railway Administration">Taiwan Railway Administration</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Line,_Taiwan" title="Western Line, Taiwan">Western Line</a>. Xinyi is separated from the <a href="/wiki/Nangang_District,_Taipei" title="Nangang District, Taipei">Nangang District</a> on the east by Fude St.; from <a href="/wiki/Wenshan_District" title="Wenshan District">Wenshan District</a> in the south by Baozijiang and Poneikeng (坡內坑) Mountains; and from <a href="/wiki/Da%27an_District,_Taipei" title="Da'an District, Taipei">Da'an District</a> in the west by the Zhuangjing Tunnel (莊敬隧道), <a href="/wiki/Keelung_Road" title="Keelung Road">Keelung Road</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guangfu_Road" title="Guangfu Road">Guangfu Road</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> New development of the area in the 1990s transformed Xinyi into a modern international shopping area which by 2010 boasted the highest property values in Taiwan.</p>

<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Taipei_101" title="Taipei 101">Taipei 101</a></b></li>
</ul>
<p>At 509.2 m (1,671 ft) tall, Taipei 101 is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World%27s_tallest_structures" title="World's tallest structures">fifth tallest building in the world</a>. Visitors can visit the 89th floor observatory by means of the world's second fastest elevator (63 km/h or 37.5 mph). In addition, there is the Taipei 101 Mall, which features name brands like <a href="/wiki/Shiatzy_Chen" title="Shiatzy Chen">Shiatzy Chen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dolce_%26_Gabbana" title="Dolce &amp; Gabbana">Dolce &amp; Gabbana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gucci" title="Gucci">Gucci</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coach,_Inc." title="Coach, Inc.">Coach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louis_Vuitton" title="Louis Vuitton">Louis Vuitton</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roots_Canada_Ltd." title="Roots Canada Ltd.">Roots</a>. The mall also has a <a href="/wiki/Page_One_(bookstore)" title="Page One (bookstore)">Page One Bookstore</a> on the 4th floor terrace, along with <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A4agen-Dazs" title="Häagen-Dazs">Häagen-Dazs</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barista_Coffee" title="Barista Coffee">Barista Coffee</a>, Dante Coffee, and a handful of fancy restaurants. Also, there is a large food court with <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s" title="McDonald's">McDonald's</a>, <a href="/wiki/KFC" title="KFC">KFC</a>, and many more.</p>
<ul>
<li><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taipei_City_Hall" title="Taipei City Hall">Taipei City Hall</a></b></li>
</ul>
<p>Taipei City Hall is located on City Hall Rd. (市府路). The plaza outside city hall, Citizen's Square (市民廣場), is often used as a concert venue including the annual New Year's celebrations.</p>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Mitsukoshi" title="Mitsukoshi">Shin Kong Mitsukoshi</a></b></li>
</ul>
<p>Shin Kong Mitsukoshi, a high-end <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> department store, operates four buildings in the district. The buildings are (in order from least floor space to most floor space): <b>A4</b>, which specializes in women's fashion, has, among other retail stores, a <a href="/wiki/Tokyu_Hands" title="Tokyu Hands">Hands Tailung</a> store, a <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A4agen-Dazs" title="Häagen-Dazs">Häagen-Dazs</a>, and a supermarket; <b>A9</b>, which houses expensive international boutiques like <a href="/wiki/Gucci" title="Gucci">Gucci</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chanel" title="Chanel">Chanel</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coach_(company)" title="Coach (company)">Coach</a>, and has a large <a href="/wiki/Fnac" title="Fnac">fnac</a> store on B2; <b>A11</b>, which features products for the entire family, a <a href="/wiki/Mister_Donut" title="Mister Donut">Mister Donut</a>, a food court, a <a href="/wiki/Starbucks" title="Starbucks">Starbucks</a>, and a supermarket; and <b>A8</b>, which holds just about everything for anyone and a food court featuring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beard_Papa" title="Beard Papa">Beard Papa</a>, <a href="/wiki/MOS_Burger" title="MOS Burger">MOS Burger</a>, <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s" title="McDonald's">McDonald's</a>, <a href="/wiki/Au_Bon_Pain" title="Au Bon Pain">Au Bon Pain</a>, etc. Each building has a number of restaurants, featuring cuisines from around the world.</p>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Eslite_Bookstore" title="Eslite Bookstore">Eslite Bookstore</a></b></li>
</ul>
<p>Eslite Bookstore, Taiwan's premier bookstore, opened their flagship store in the district in early 2006. Spanning over 7 floors, this bookstore is the largest in Taiwan. The bookstore has an expansive magazine collection (especially foreign magazines), a floor dedicated to children's books, a Japanese bookstore, art bookstore, Simplified Chinese book collection, and more. There are event halls where authors address their articles or topics related to their books. In addition to books, the bookstore also offers clothing and accessories (with brands like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fcuk" title="Fcuk">fcuk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lacoste" title="Lacoste">Lacoste</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a>), stationery, CDs, a <a href="/wiki/Starbucks" title="Starbucks">Starbucks</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple</a> retailer, and a food court.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Neo19</b></li>
</ul>
<p>Neo19, located on Songshou Rd. (松壽路), houses a number of restaurants, a health club, a nightclub and lounge bar. The restaurants at Neo 19 include <a href="/wiki/Chili%27s" title="Chili's">Chili's</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romano%27s_Macaroni_Grill" title="Romano's Macaroni Grill">Romano's Macaroni Grill</a>, Azabusabo, Mo-Mo-Paradise, Gloucester, and Watami. The nightclub is Room18, renowned for hosting international DJs and local celebrities. The lounge bar is BarCode, located on the 5th floor which features an open-air patio, and an exclusive VIP area, named The Den.</p>
<ul>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vieshow_Cinemas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vieshow Cinemas (page does not exist)">Vieshow Cinemas</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A8%81%E7%A7%80%E5%BD%B1%E5%9F%8E" title="zh:威秀影城">zh</a>)</span></b></li>
</ul>
<p>With 17 screens, this <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Megaplex" title="Megaplex">megaplex</a> is a popular place in Taipei to see films from all over the world, often released on the same day as the country of origin. The complex also has a food court that includes outlets of <a href="/wiki/Burger_King" title="Burger King">Burger King</a>, <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A4agen-Dazs" title="Häagen-Dazs">Häagen-Dazs</a>, etc., <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sasa_(retailer)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sasa (retailer) (page does not exist)">Sasa</a>, and an arcade.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>ATT 4 FUN (formerly New York New York)</b></li>
</ul>
<p>Located at 12, Songshou Rd (台北市松壽路12號9樓), this building was formerly called New York New York. After ATT Group bought the mall in 2010 it was closed, remodeled, and opened under the name <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=ATT_4_FUN&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="ATT 4 FUN (page does not exist)">ATT 4 FUN</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/ATT_4_FUN" title="zh:ATT 4 FUN">zh</a>)</span> in the second half of 2011. The mall features 11 floors of shopping, restaurants, and clubs. Among the many attractions are a Starbucks, McDonald's, Mister Donut, and Myst Club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><b>Historic Coal Mines</b></li>
</ul>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hexing_Mine.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1936" data-file-width="2592" height="164" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Hexing_Mine.jpg/220px-Hexing_Mine.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Hexing_Mine.jpg/330px-Hexing_Mine.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Hexing_Mine.jpg/440px-Hexing_Mine.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hexing_Mine.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Plaque of the Hexing Coal Mine</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Xinyi District has several historic coal mines from the Japanese colonial period. Two of them have been renovated: the Hexing Mine (和興炭坑),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Alley 471, Lane 150, Section 5, Xinyi Road, and the Dexing Mine (德興炭坑) <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> near the trailhead of the Tiaomi Trail,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Alley 100, Lane 600, Wuxing Road.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Martial Law Era Political Victim Memorial Park</b></li>
</ul>
<p>This historic cemetery is located on a hillside overlooking the present Taipei 101 shopping district.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinyi_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">Rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinyi_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Xinyi District is served by the <a href="/wiki/Taipei_Metro" title="Taipei Metro">Taipei Metro</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nangang_Line_(TRTS)" title="Nangang Line (TRTS)">Nangang Line</a> in the north and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wenshan_Line_(TRTS)" title="Wenshan Line (TRTS)">Wenshan Line</a> in the south. When completed in 2012, the <a href="/wiki/Taipei_Metro" title="Taipei Metro">Taipei Metro</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xinyi_Line_(TRTS)" title="Xinyi Line (TRTS)">Xinyi Line</a> will serve the district at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taipei_101/World_Trade_Center_Station" title="Taipei 101/World Trade Center Station">Taipei 101/World Trade Center</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elephant_Mountain_Station" title="Elephant Mountain Station">Elephant Mountain</a>. In addition, the north of the district is served by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taiwan_Railway_Administration" title="Taiwan Railway Administration">Taiwan Railway Administration</a> <a href="/wiki/Songshan_Station" title="Songshan Station">Songshan Station</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Road">Road</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinyi_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Road">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Buses to Xinyi District are also plentiful from all parts of Taipei, with a major bus terminal at <a href="/wiki/Taipei_City_Hall_Bus_Station" title="Taipei City Hall Bus Station">Taipei City Hall Bus Station</a>. There are also many arteries and freeways in the area, including as <a href="/wiki/Zhongxiao_Road" title="Zhongxiao Road">Zhongxiao E. Rd.</a> (Sec. 4), <a href="/wiki/Keelung_Road" title="Keelung Road">Keelung Rd.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xinyi_Road" title="Xinyi Road">Xinyi Rd.</a> (Sec. 2), <a href="/wiki/Renai_Road" title="Renai Road">Ren'ai Rd.</a> (Sec. 3), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Provincial_Highway_No._5_(Taiwan)" title="Provincial Highway No. 5 (Taiwan)">Provincial Highway No. 5</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Civic_Blvd_Expressway" title="Civic Blvd Expressway">Civic Blvd Expressway</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pedestrian">Pedestrian</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinyi_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Pedestrian">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shin Kong Mitsukoshi (all buildings), Vieshow Cinemas, ATT 4 Fun, and Taipei 101 Mall are interconnected by an elevated walkway.</p>
