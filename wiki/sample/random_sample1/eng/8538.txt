<p>The <b>New Japan Philharmonic</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">新日本フィルハーモニー交響楽団</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shin Nihon Firuhāmonī Kōkyōgakudan</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Orchestra" title="Orchestra">symphony orchestra</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It was founded in 1972 with <a href="/wiki/Seiji_Ozawa" title="Seiji Ozawa">Seiji Ozawa</a> as honorary <a href="/wiki/Conducting" title="Conducting">conductor</a> laureate. The Philharmonic's primary concert venue is the <a href="/wiki/Sumida_Triphony_Hall" title="Sumida Triphony Hall">Sumida Triphony Hall</a>. Since 2003, its <a href="/wiki/Music_director" title="Music director">music director</a> is <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Christian_Arming&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Christian Arming (page does not exist)">Christian Arming</a>.</p>
<p>Their video game performances include songs for the <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Smash_Bros._Melee" title="Super Smash Bros. Melee">Super Smash Bros. Melee</a></i> orchestral arrangement soundtrack <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Smash_Bros._Melee#Music" title="Super Smash Bros. Melee">Smashing...Live!</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil" title="Resident Evil">Resident Evil</a>'s music in <i><a href="/wiki/Discography_of_the_Resident_Evil_video_game_series#Biohazard_Orchestra" title="Discography of the Resident Evil video game series">Resident Evil Orchestra</a></i>, orchestral arrangements by <a href="/wiki/Kaoru_Wada" title="Kaoru Wada">Kaoru Wada</a> for the <i><a href="/wiki/Music_of_Kingdom_Hearts#Kingdom_Hearts_Original_Soundtrack" title="Music of Kingdom Hearts">Kingdom Hearts Original Soundtrack</a></i>, the music of <a href="/wiki/Go_Shiina" title="Go Shiina">Go Shiina</a> from <i><a href="/wiki/Tales_of_Legendia" title="Tales of Legendia">Tales of Legendia</a></i>, and the first stop, Yokohama, on <i><a href="/wiki/Final_Fantasy" title="Final Fantasy">Final Fantasy</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s <a href="/wiki/Final_Fantasy_concerts" title="Final Fantasy concerts">Tour de Japon</a>. On May 6, 2009, the 5th Anniversary <a href="/wiki/Monster_Hunter" title="Monster Hunter">Monster Hunter</a> Orchestral Concert took place at the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metropolitan_Theatre" title="Tokyo Metropolitan Theatre">Tokyo Metropolitan Theatre</a> in Ikebukuro, Tokyo.</p>
<p>The orchestra is also known for their film soundtrack productions, notably <i><a href="/wiki/Spirited_Away" title="Spirited Away">Spirited Away</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Howl%27s_Moving_Castle_(film)" title="Howl's Moving Castle (film)">Howl's Moving Castle</a></i>, both soundtracks composed, arranged, conducted and performed by <a href="/wiki/Joe_Hisaishi" title="Joe Hisaishi">Joe Hisaishi</a>; and a number of works for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_(anime)" title="Neon Genesis Evangelion (anime)">Neon Genesis Evangelion</a></i>, composed, arranged and conducted by <a href="/wiki/Shir%C5%8D_Sagisu" title="Shirō Sagisu">Shirō Sagisu</a>. The orchestra also performed the film score for an English-language animated film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Brave_Little_Toaster" title="The Brave Little Toaster">The Brave Little Toaster</a></i>, composed by <a href="/wiki/David_Newman_(composer)" title="David Newman (composer)">David Newman</a>.</p>
<p>Lastly, the orchestra has also done live performances with various musicians, notably <a href="/wiki/Yngwie_Malmsteen" title="Yngwie Malmsteen">Yngwie Malmsteen</a> and his Concerto Suite in Eb Minor.</p>
