<p>The <b>Sinjar Alliance</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurdish_language" title="Kurdish language">Kurdish</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ku" xml:lang="ku"><i>Fermandariya Hevbeş a Şengalê</i></span>‎, i.e. <i>Sinjar Joint Command</i>), full name as <b>Ezidkhan Command for Liberating Sinjar</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurdish_language" title="Kurdish language">Kurdish</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ku" xml:lang="ku"><i>Fermandariya Êzîdxana Ji Bo Rizgariya Şengalê</i></span>‎), is a joint command of three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yazidi" title="Yazidi">Yazidi</a> <a href="/wiki/Militia" title="Militia">militias</a>: the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Protection_Force_of_Sinjar" title="Protection Force of Sinjar">Protection Force of Sinjar</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sinjar_Resistance_Units" title="Sinjar Resistance Units">Sinjar Resistance Units</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%8Azidxan_Women%27s_Units" title="Êzidxan Women's Units">Êzidxan Women's Units</a>, in <a href="/wiki/November_2015_Sinjar_offensive" title="November 2015 Sinjar offensive">the Liberation of Sinjar</a>. The last two militias are supported by the <a href="/wiki/Kurdistan_Workers%27_Party" title="Kurdistan Workers' Party">PKK</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The translation "Sinjar Alliance", which in certain content is not correct, firstly appeared in an Ezidipress's news.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The accordance of <a href="/wiki/Haydar_Shesho" title="Haydar Shesho">Haydar Shesho</a>'s utterance in the Ezidipress's English news with that in other Kuridsh news of Sinjar Joint Command's foundation<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> proves that "Sinjar Alliance" is just a translation of "Fermandariya Hevbeş a Şengalê".</p>
<p>The alliance was created in the end of October 2015<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and aims to establish <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_confederalism" title="Democratic confederalism">democratic confederalism</a> and a Yazidi autonomous region in <a href="/wiki/Ezidkhan" title="Ezidkhan">Ezidkhan</a> within the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurdish_Regional_Government" title="Kurdish Regional Government">Kurdish Regional Government</a> in <a href="/wiki/Iraqi_Kurdistan" title="Iraqi Kurdistan">Iraqi Kurdistan</a>. There are tensions between the Sinjar Alliance and the Kurdish Regional Government because the president of <a href="/wiki/Iraqi_Kurdistan" title="Iraqi Kurdistan">Iraqi Kurdistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Masoud_Barzani" title="Masoud Barzani">Masoud Barzani</a> aims to control all of <a href="/wiki/Sinjar_Mountains" title="Sinjar Mountains">Sinjar</a> and demands the withdrawal of <a href="/wiki/Kurdistan_Workers%27_Party" title="Kurdistan Workers' Party">PKK-linked</a> militias.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
