<p><b>Xinzheng</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans">新郑</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant">新鄭</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Xīnzhèng</span></i>) is a small county-level city of <a href="/wiki/Zhengzhou" title="Zhengzhou">Zhengzhou</a> in the south of <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a> province of <a href="/wiki/Central_China" title="Central China">Central China</a>. The city has a population of 600,000 people and covers an area of 15 square kilometres (5.8 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ccpit_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ccpit-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinzheng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Xinzheng is considered as one of the birth places of Chinese nation. More than 8000 years ago, <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">neolithic</a> people of <a href="/wiki/Peiligang_culture" title="Peiligang culture">Peiligang culture</a> lived in the vicinity. The legendary <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Emperor" title="Yellow Emperor">Yellow Emperor</a> was said to have been born in Xinzheng 5000 years ago. During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zhou_Dynasty" title="Zhou Dynasty">Zhou Dynasty</a> it was the capital of the state of <a href="/wiki/Zheng_(state)" title="Zheng (state)">Zheng</a> and later, the state of <a href="/wiki/Han_(state)" title="Han (state)">Han</a>, after the conquest of the former by the latter. It was the seat of government for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qin_Dynasty" title="Qin Dynasty">Qin Dynasty</a>, from about 221 BCE, which is considered the beginning of a unified China. The word <i>qin</i> (秦), which is pronounced similar to "chin", is thought to be the basis for the word "China."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Historically, this prefecture was an integrated part of Zhengzhou. However, in 1994, the Henan Provincial Council authorized the incorporation of Xinzheng into a separate municipality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinzheng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Xinzheng has one of the largest concentrations of foreign teachers in central China due to the presence of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sias_International_University" title="Sias International University">Sias International University</a>. The school has over 26,000 students and over 120 foreign teachers and their 40 dependent family members. Along with Sias University, the city is also home to another university: ShengDa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shengda_Foreign_Affairs_Office_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shengda_Foreign_Affairs_Office-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SIAS_Website_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-SIAS_Website-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinzheng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Xinzheng has a diversified economy, though agriculture and heavy industry dominate the economic landscape.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Agriculture">Agriculture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinzheng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Agriculture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Xinzheng's agriculture involves the cultivation of <a href="/wiki/Maize" title="Maize">maize</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">tobacco</a> and other industrial crops. The city is well known throughout <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Jujube" title="Jujube">jujube</a> or Chinese Date. At harvest time, large quantities of these fruits are sold in markets, as well as on the side of the roads into the town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry">Industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinzheng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Xinzheng is home to several industrial factories, namely cigarette factories, which are located near the center of town. In 2006, more than 2 billion USD was invested in a nearby industrial project,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> although the status of this investment is currently unknown. Mining coal locally has also been an important source of income.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_of_educational_institutions">Impact of educational institutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinzheng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Impact of educational institutions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two large private universities, ShengDa University and <a href="/wiki/SIAS_International_University" title="SIAS International University">SIAS International University</a> play a major role in the local economy as well. ShengDa has 14,000 students,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shengda_Foreign_Affairs_Office_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Shengda_Foreign_Affairs_Office-3">[3]</a></sup> and Sias University has updwards of up to 26,000 students.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SIAS_Website_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-SIAS_Website-4">[4]</a></sup> Service industries (restaurants, hotels, banks, retail shops, etc.) to accommodate the students have sprung out around the universities in abundance. At SIAS, the administration tacitly allows students to live off campus, creating a large community of landlords around the university. The presence of 40,000 students has had an undeniable and long lasting impact on the community.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinzheng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Huangdi.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="720" height="200" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Huangdi.jpg/300px-Huangdi.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Huangdi.jpg/450px-Huangdi.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Huangdi.jpg/600px-Huangdi.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Huangdi.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Yellow Emperor Celebrations on the streets of Henan</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Due to limited accommodation, lack of infrastructure, pollution and other factors, tourism has been slow to take off in Xinzheng. Every Spring, the city hosts a ceremony honoring <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Emperor" title="Yellow Emperor">Yellow Emperor</a>, the legendary ancestor of the Chinese nation. In 2009, this celebration attracted over 10,000 Chinese from within the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>, as well as many <a href="/wiki/Overseas_Chinese" title="Overseas Chinese">Overseas Chinese</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>However, many of these guests find accommodations in nearby Zhengzhou, and the economic impact to the city is minimal. Xinzheng is said to be an 'open-air' museum, and the nearby Shizu mountain is said to be the exact birthplace of <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Emperor" title="Yellow Emperor">Yellow Emperor</a>. The Zhengzhou Dragon realization company began construction at the turn of the 21st century of a large concrete dragon that would straddle the mountain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>A giant <a href="/wiki/Chinese_dragon" title="Chinese dragon">dragon</a> was being constructed by private investors near the city out of <a href="/wiki/Concrete" title="Concrete">concrete</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marble" title="Marble">marble</a> with a 29.9m tall head and a body that will eventually stretch 21 km (13 mi) along the ridge line of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shizu_Mountain&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shizu Mountain (page does not exist)">Shizu Mountain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Xinhua_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Xinhua-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ananova_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ananova-10">[10]</a></sup> The hollow body of the dragon was slated to be 9m high and 6m wide and will contain a <a href="/wiki/Light_rail" title="Light rail">light rail</a> system along with facilities for sight seeing and leisure, such as shops, restaurants and luxury clubs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ananova_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ananova-10">[10]</a></sup> The project was expected to be completed before October 1, 2009 to celebrate the 60th anniversary of the founding of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Xinhua_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Xinhua-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ShanghaiDaily_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ShanghaiDaily-11">[11]</a></sup> However, lack of funding, and a general lack of interest, left the project unfinished.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinzheng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Air">Air</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinzheng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Air">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The airport that serves the provincial capital, <a href="/wiki/Zhengzhou_Xinzheng_International_Airport" title="Zhengzhou Xinzheng International Airport">Zhengzhou Xinzheng International Airport</a>, is located in Xinzheng. This is of particular importance not only because it is an airport for a large urban center, but because it is the only international airport for the province of 100 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 8, 2009, the direct air route between Zhengzhou and <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> was launched in <a href="/wiki/Zhengzhou_Xinzheng_International_Airport" title="Zhengzhou Xinzheng International Airport">Zhengzhou Xinzheng International Airport</a>, the Zhengzhou-Taipei flight was operated by <a href="/wiki/Shenzhen_Airlines" title="Shenzhen Airlines">Shenzhen Airlines</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The airport has already been a major hub for domestic travel. The airport Code is CGO.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">Rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinzheng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As in the rest of China, rail transport in Xinzheng is handled by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/China_Railway_Corporation" title="China Railway Corporation">China Railway Corporation</a>. Xinzheng itself has a small railroad station at the edge of town that accommodates trains to and from Zhengzhou as well as trains to other limited destinations within China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>However, nearby Zhengzhou is a major railway hub. Its station serves all major points in China and it lays claims to be the busiest rail station in China. As of 2007, high-speed rail service began between Zhengzhou and Beijing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> To do any travel beyond the Zhengzhou immediate area, most residents travel to Zhengzhou, then onward.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mass_transit">Mass transit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinzheng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Mass transit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The city has a primitive, yet extensive, bus system. Within the urban city, it's possible to ride anywhere with one or two <a href="/wiki/Renminbi" title="Renminbi">renminbi</a>. There are no clear bus stops, so passengers wave the bus down as it speeds down the streets, and yell at it to stop when they've reached their destination.</p>
<p>The bus station to the west of the town's city center serves Zhengzhou, <a href="/wiki/Kaifeng" title="Kaifeng">Kaifeng</a> and other municipalities within close proximity to Xinzheng.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxi">Taxi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinzheng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Taxi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>All Xinzheng taxis belong to the same local company, and taxis barrels throughout the city's streets with a starting fare of 10 <a href="/wiki/Renminbi" title="Renminbi">renminbi</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roads">Roads</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinzheng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Roads">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Xinzheng is well connected to Zhengzhou and points south by <a href="/wiki/China_National_Highway_220" title="China National Highway 220">China National Highway 220</a>. All inter-city roads are well maintained. Within the city limits, asin the rest of many small Chinese cities, many residential roads remain in a serious state of disrepair.</p>
