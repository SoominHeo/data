<p>The <b>Boxer <a href="/wiki/Protocol_(politics)" title="Protocol (politics)">Protocol</a></b> was signed on September 7, 1901, between the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" title="Qing Dynasty">Qing Empire</a> of China and the <a href="/wiki/Eight-Nation_Alliance" title="Eight-Nation Alliance">Eight-Nation Alliance</a> that had provided military forces (Austria-Hungary, France, Germany, Italy, Japan, Russia, the United Kingdom, and the United States) plus Belgium, Spain and the Netherlands after China's defeat in the intervention to put down the <a href="/wiki/Boxer_Rebellion" title="Boxer Rebellion">Boxer Rebellion</a> at the hands of the Eight-Power Expeditionary Force. It is often regarded as one of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unequal_Treaties" title="Unequal Treaties">Unequal Treaties</a>.</p>

<p>In Western countries, it was also known as the <b>Treaty of 1901</b>, <b>Peace Agreement between the Great Powers and China</b>. The full name of the protocol is <b>Austria-Hungary, Belgium, France, Germany, Great Britain, Italy, Japan, The Netherlands, Russia, Spain, United States and China—Final Protocol for the Settlement of the Disturbances of 1900</b>, reflecting its nature as a diplomatic protocol rather than a peace treaty at the time of signature.</p>
<p>In China, it was known as the <b>Xinchou Treaty</b>. It was later regarded as one of the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unequal_Treaties" title="Unequal Treaties">Unequal Treaties</a>".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Negotiations_during_the_Boxer_Rebellion">Negotiations during the Boxer Rebellion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boxer_Protocol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Negotiations during the Boxer Rebellion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Qing dynasty was by no means defeated when the Allies took control of Beijing. The Allies had to temper the demands they sent in a message to Xi'an to get the Dowager Empress to agree with them; for instance, China did not have to give up any land. Many of the Dowager Empress's advisers in the Imperial Court insisted that the war be carried on against the foreigners, arguing that China could defeat the foreigners since it was the disloyal and traitorous people within China who allowed Beijing and Tianjin to be captured by the Allies, and the interior of China was impenetrable. The Dowager was practical, and decided that the terms were generous enough for her to acquiesce and stop the war, when she was assured of her continued reign after the war.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Signatories">Signatories</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boxer_Protocol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Signatories">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Boxer Protocol was signed on September 7, 1901, in the Spanish Legation in Beijing:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Espana_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Espana-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><b><i>Foreign Powers</i></b></p>

<p>450 million <a href="/wiki/Tael" title="Tael">taels</a> of fine silver (~ <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USD" title="USD">US$</a>333 million or <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>67 million at the <a href="/wiki/Exchange_rate" title="Exchange rate">exchange rates</a> of the time) were to be paid as <a href="/wiki/Indemnity" title="Indemnity">indemnity</a> over a course of 39 years to the eight nations involved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spence_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-spence-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Chinese paid the indemnity in gold on a rising scale with a 4% <a href="/wiki/Interest" title="Interest">interest charge</a> until the debt was <a href="/wiki/Amortization" title="Amortization">amortized</a> on December 31, 1940. After 39 years, the amount was almost 1 billion taels (precisely 982,238,150),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spence_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-spence-3">[3]</a></sup> or ~1,180,000,000 troy ounces (37,000 tonnes) at 1.2 ozt/tael.</p>
<p>The sum was to be distributed as follows: Russia 28.97%, Germany 20.02%, France 15.75%, United Kingdom 11.25%, Japan 7.73%, United States 7.32%, Italy 7.32%, Belgium 1.89%, Austria-Hungary 0.89%, Netherlands 0.17%, Spain 0.03%, Portugal 0.021%, Sweden and Norway 0.014%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Also, additional 16,886,708 taels was paid at local level in 17 provinces. By 1938, 652.37 million taels had been paid. The interest rate (of 4% per annum) was to be paid semi-annually with the first payment being the July 1, 1902.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The Qing government was also to allow the foreign countries to base their troops in Beijing. In addition, the foreign powers had placed the <a href="/wiki/Empress_Dowager_Cixi" title="Empress Dowager Cixi">Empress Dowager Cixi</a> on their list of war criminals, although provincial officers such as <a href="/wiki/Li_Hongzhang" title="Li Hongzhang">Li Hongzhang</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yuan_Shikai" title="Yuan Shikai">Yuan Shikai</a> defended her, claiming that she had no control whatsoever over the whole escapade. She was later removed from the list.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p><b>Other clauses included:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>To prohibit the importation of arms and ammunition, as well as materials for the production of arms or ammunition for a period of 2 years, extensible further 2 years as the Powers saw necessary.</li>
<li>The destruction of <a href="/wiki/Taku_Forts" title="Taku Forts">Taku Forts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sondhaus_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sondhaus-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Beijing_Legation_Quarter" title="Beijing Legation Quarter">Legation Quarters</a> occupied by the Powers shall be considered as a special area reserved for their use under exclusive control, in which Chinese shall not have the right to reside, and which may be defensible. China recognised the right of each Power to maintain a permanent guard in the said Quarters for the defense of its Legation.</li>
<li>Boxer and Government officials were to be punished for crimes or attempted crimes against the foreign Governments or their nationals. Many were either sentenced to execution, deportation to <a href="/wiki/Turkestan" title="Turkestan">Turkestan</a>, imprisoned for life, commit suicide, or suffer posthumous degradation.</li>
<li>The "<a href="/wiki/Zongli_Yamen" title="Zongli Yamen">Office in Charge of Affairs of All Nations</a>" (<a href="/wiki/Zongli_Yamen" title="Zongli Yamen">Zongli Yamen</a>) was replaced with a Foreign Office, which ranked above the other six boards in the government.</li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qing_government" title="Qing government">Chinese Government</a> was to prohibit forever, under the pain of death, membership in any anti-foreign society, civil service examinations were to be suspended for 5 years in all areas where foreigners were massacred or subjected to cruel treatment, provincial and local officials would personally be held responsible for any new anti-foreign incidents.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_China" title="Emperor of China">Emperor of China</a> was to convey his regrets to the <a href="/wiki/German_Emperor" title="German Emperor">German Emperor</a> for the assassination of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Klemens_von_Ketteler" title="Klemens von Ketteler">Baron von Ketteler</a>.</li>
<li>The Emperor of China was to appoint Na't'ung to be his <a href="/wiki/Diplomatic_rank" title="Diplomatic rank">Envoy Extraordinary</a> and direct him to also convey to the <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Japan" title="Emperor of Japan">Emperor of Japan</a>, his expression of regrets and that of his Government at the assassination of Mr. Sugiyama.</li>
<li>The Chinese Government would have to erect on the spot of the assassination of Baron von Ketteler a commemorative arch inscribed in <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>, German, and <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>.</li>
<li>Concede the right to the Powers to occupy the following places:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hoax_demands">Hoax demands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boxer_Protocol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Hoax demands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The French Catholic vicar apostolic, Msgr. Alfons Bermyn, wanted foreign troops garrisoned in <a href="/wiki/Christianity_in_Inner_Mongolia" title="Christianity in Inner Mongolia">Inner Mongolia</a>, but the Governor refused. Bermyn resorted to lies, and falsely petitioned the Manchu <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Enming&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Enming (page does not exist)">Enming</a> to send troops to <a href="/wiki/Hetao" title="Hetao">Hetao</a> where Prince Duan's Mongol troops and General Dong Fuxiang's Muslim troops allegedly threatened Catholics. It turned out that Bermyn had created the incident as a hoax.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ann_Heylen_2004_203_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ann_Heylen_2004_203-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> One of the false reports claimed that Dong Fuxiang wiped out Belgian missionaries in Mongolia and was going to massacre Catholics in Taiyuan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demands_rejected_by_China">Demands rejected by China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boxer_Protocol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Demands rejected by China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Qing did not capitulate to all the foreign demands. The Manchu Governor <a href="/wiki/Yuxian_(Qing_dynasty)" title="Yuxian (Qing dynasty)">Yuxian</a> was executed, but the Imperial court refused to execute the Chinese General <a href="/wiki/Dong_Fuxiang" title="Dong Fuxiang">Dong Fuxiang</a>, although both were anti-foreign and had been accused of encouraging the killing of foreigners during the rebellion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Instead, General Dong Fuxiang lived a life of luxury and power in "exile" in his home province of Gansu.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-M._Th._Houtsma.2C_A._J._Wensinck_1993_850_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-M._Th._Houtsma.2C_A._J._Wensinck_1993_850-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In addition to sparing Dong Fuxiang, the Qing also refused to exile the Boxer supporter Prince <a href="/wiki/Zaiyi" title="Zaiyi">Zaiyi</a> to Xinjiang, as the foreigners demanded. Instead, he moved to Alashan, west of <a href="/wiki/Ningxia" title="Ningxia">Ningxia</a>, and lived in the residence of the local Mongol prince. He then moved to Ningxia during the <a href="/wiki/Xinhai_Revolution" title="Xinhai Revolution">Xinhai Revolution</a> when the Muslims took control of Ningxia, and finally, moved to Xinjiang with Sheng Yun.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Prince Duan "went no farther than Manchuria for exile, and was heard of there in 1908".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stipulations_subsequently_violated_by_China">Stipulations subsequently violated by China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boxer_Protocol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Stipulations subsequently violated by China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>In the hunting-park, three miles to the south of Peking, is quartered the Sixth Division, which supplies the Guards for the Imperial Palace, consisting of a battalion of infantry and a squadron of cavalry. With this Division Yuan Shi Kai retains twenty-six modified <a href="/wiki/Krupp_gun" title="Krupp gun">Krupp guns</a>, which are the best of his artillery arm, and excel any guns possessed by the foreign legations in Peking.</p>
<p>The Manchu Division moves with the Court, and is the pride of the modern army.</p>
<p>By his strategic disposition Yuan Shi Kai completely controls all the approaches to the capital, and holds a force which he may utilize either to protect the Court from threatened attack or to crush the Emperor should he himself desire to assume Imperial power. Contrary to treaty stipulations made at the settlement of the Boxer trouble, the Chinese have been permitted to build a great tower over the Chien Men, or central southern gate, which commands the foreign legations and governs the Forbidden City. In the threatening condition of Chinese affairs it might be assumed that this structure had been undermined by the foreign community, but this has not been done, and if trouble again arise in Peking the fate of the legations will depend upon the success of the first assault which will be necessary to take it. The foreign legations are as much in the power of Yuan Shi Kai's troops in 1907 as they were at the mercy of the Chinese rabble in 1900.</p>
<p>The ultimate purpose of the equipped and disciplined troops is locked in the breast of the Viceroy of Chihli. Yuan Shi Kai's yamen in Tientsin is connected by telegraph and telephone with the Imperial palaces and with the various barracks of his troops. In a field a couple of hundred yards away is the long pole of a wireless telegraph station, from which he can send the message that any day may set all China ablaze.</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;">To-morrow in the East, <i>Douglas Story</i>, pp. 224-226<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>The Chien Men gate refers to the <a href="/wiki/Zhengyangmen" title="Zhengyangmen">Zhengyangmen</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Remittance">Remittance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boxer_Protocol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Remittance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On December 28, 1908, the United States remitted $11,961,121.76 of its share of the Indemnity to support the <a href="/wiki/Boxer_Indemnity_Scholarship_Program" title="Boxer Indemnity Scholarship Program">education of Chinese students in the United States</a> and the construction of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qinghua_University" title="Qinghua University">Tsinghua University</a> in Beijing,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> thanks to the efforts of its ambassador <a href="/wiki/Liang_Cheng" title="Liang Cheng">Liang Cheng</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-culturalchina_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-culturalchina-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>When China declared war on Germany and Austria in 1917, it suspended the combined German and Austrian share of the Boxer Indemnity, which totaled 20.91 percent. At the <a href="/wiki/Paris_Peace_Conference,_1919" title="Paris Peace Conference, 1919">Paris Peace Conference</a>, Beijing succeeded in completely revoking the German and Austrian shares of the Boxer Indemnity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>The history surrounding Russia's share of the Boxer Indemnity is the most complex of all the nations involved. On December 2, 1918 the Bolsheviks issued an official decree abolishing Russia's share of the Indemnity (146). Upon the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Lev_Karakhan" title="Lev Karakhan">Lev Karakhan</a> in Beijing during the Fall of 1923, however, it became clear that the Soviet Union expected to retain control over how the Russian share was to be spent. Though Karakhan was initially hesitant to follow the United States' example of directing the funds toward education, he soon insisted in private that the Russian share had to be used for that purpose and during February 1924, presented a proposal stating that the "Soviet portion of the Boxer Indemnity would be allocated to Chinese educational institutions."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 14, 1924, Karakhan completed a draft Sino-Soviet agreement stating "The government of the USSR agrees to renounce the Russian portion of the Boxer Indemnity." Copies of these terms were published in the Chinese press, and the ensuing positive public reaction encouraged other countries to match the USSR's terms. On May 21, 1924, the U.S. Congress agreed to remit to China the final $6,137,552.90 of the American share. Ten days later, however, it became apparent that the USSR did not intend to carry through on its earlier promise of full renunciation. When the final Sino-Soviet agreement was announced, it specified that Russia's share would be used to promote education in China and that the Soviet government would retain control over how the money was to be used, an exact parallel to the U.S. remittance of 1908.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 3, 1925, Great Britain completed arrangements to use its share of the Boxer Indemnity to support railway construction in China. On April 12, France asked that its indemnity be used to reopen a defunct Sino-French Bank. Italy signed an agreement on October 1 to spend its share on the construction of steel bridges. The Netherlands' share paid for harbor and land reclamation, and the Belgian funds were earmarked to be spent on railway material in Belgium. Finally, Japan's indemnity was transferred to develop aviation in China under Japanese oversight<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Once these countries' approximately 40 percent of the Boxer Indemnity was added to Germany's and Austria's combined 20.91 percent, the United States' 7.32 percent, and the Soviet Union's 28.97 percent share, the Beijing government had accounted for over 98 percent of the entire Boxer Indemnity. Hence, by 1927, Beijing had almost completely revoked Boxer Indemnity payments abroad and had succeeded in redirecting the payments for use within China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
