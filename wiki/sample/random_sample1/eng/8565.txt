<div class="hatnote" role="note">This is a <a href="/wiki/Korean_name" title="Korean name">Korean name</a>; the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Korean_family_names" title="List of Korean family names">family name</a> is <a href="/wiki/Shin_(Korean_surname)" title="Shin (Korean surname)"><i>Shin</i></a>.</div>
<p><b>Shin Hyun-joon</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Korean</a>: <span lang="KO" xml:lang="KO">신현준</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: <span lang="KO" xml:lang="KO">申鉉俊</span>; October 23, 1915 – October 14, 2007) was a <a href="/wiki/Jungjang" title="Jungjang">lieutenant general</a> and the first Commandant of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea_Marine_Corps" title="Republic of Korea Marine Corps">Republic of Korea Marine Corps</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paik_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-paik-1">[1]</a></sup> He served from April 15, 1949 until October 15, 1953 with a US 1950 <a href="/wiki/Silver_Star" title="Silver Star">Silver Star</a> medal<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-star_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-star-2">[2]</a></sup> including 5 more prizes and decorations that were awarded to him by the Korean government. After he joined the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea_Navy" title="Republic of Korea Navy">Republic of Korea Navy</a> as lieutenant in 1946, he was assigned as one of the core members of ROK Marine and founded ROK Marine Corps on April 15, 1949.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-leatherneck_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-leatherneck-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-herald_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-herald-4">[4]</a></sup> Throughout the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a>, he led ROK Marine Corps as commandant.</p>
<p>Following his military service he entered the Korean Foreign Service, and served as the Ambassador of the Republic of Korea to the <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">Holy See</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nwfdn_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-nwfdn-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In his later years, he lived in <a href="/wiki/Niceville,_Florida" title="Niceville, Florida">Niceville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>. He died on October 14, 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nwfdn_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-nwfdn-5">[5]</a></sup> in Okaloosa, Florida<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">[6]</a></sup> and was buried at the <a href="/wiki/Daejeon_National_Cemetery" title="Daejeon National Cemetery">Daejeon National Cemetery</a> in South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">[6]</a></sup> He was survived by his two sons Ong-In Shin and Ong-Mok; he was also survived by his 4 daughters Cecila LaForet, Soon Mi Shin, Soon Wa Shin, and Maria Abbot<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">[6]</a></sup> Shin Hyin-joon's wife had passed six years before in 2001. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nwfdn_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-nwfdn-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
