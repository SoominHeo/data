<p><b>Shin Hyun-joon</b> (born October 28, 1968) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>. He is best known for his roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Barefoot_Ki-bong" title="Barefoot Ki-bong">Barefoot Ki-bong</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stairway_to_Heaven_(TV_series)" title="Stairway to Heaven (TV series)">Stairway to Heaven</a></i> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Marrying_the_Mafia_II" title="Marrying the Mafia II">Marrying the Mafia</a></i> sequels, and as the photographer in the popular music video "Because I'm A Girl" by <a href="/wiki/Kiss_(South_Korean_band)" title="Kiss (South Korean band)">KISS</a>. In the Korean press he is nicknamed as 아랍왕자 ("Prince of Arab") due to his foreign look and long eyelashes.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shin_Hyun-joon_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shin Hyun-joon was an athletics major at <a href="/wiki/Yonsei_University" title="Yonsei University">Yonsei University</a> before starting a career in modeling and acting in 1989. His film debut came in director <a href="/wiki/Im_Kwon-taek" title="Im Kwon-taek">Im Kwon-taek</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Son_of_a_General" title="Son of a General">Son of a General</a></i> series, set under the <a href="/wiki/Korea_under_Japanese_rule" title="Korea under Japanese rule">Japanese occupation</a> in the 1920s. For the first half of the 1990s he continued working with Im Kwon-taek and also acted in <i><a href="/wiki/Hwa-Om-Kyung" title="Hwa-Om-Kyung">Hwa-Om-Kyung</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Jang_Sun-woo" title="Jang Sun-woo">Jang Sun-woo</a>'s award-winning film based on the <a href="/wiki/Avatamsaka_Sutra" title="Avatamsaka Sutra">Avatamsaka Sutra</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-june_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-june-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In recent years Shin has turned more towards popular cinema, finding his greatest success in fantasy/sci-fi works such as <i>The Gingko Bed</i>, <i>The Soul Guardians</i>, and the Korean-Chinese co-production <i><a href="/wiki/Bichunmoo" title="Bichunmoo">Bichunmoo</a></i>, as well as gangster comedies <i><a href="/wiki/Marrying_the_Mafia_II" title="Marrying the Mafia II">Marrying the Mafia II</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Guns_%26_Talks" title="Guns &amp; Talks">Guns &amp; Talks</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-june_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-june-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In one of his most iconic roles, Shin played a 40-year-old <a href="/wiki/Marathon" title="Marathon">marathon</a> runner with the mental faculties of an 8-year-old boy in <i><a href="/wiki/Barefoot_Ki-bong" title="Barefoot Ki-bong">Barefoot Ki-bong</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>A devout <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christian</a>, he published a photo-essay book <i>Shin Hyun-joon's Confessions</i> in 2008, which documented his personal insights about life and faith. The first printing sold out in two days, and the book was also released in China and Japan. <i>Confessions</i> contained a foreword by international star <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Chan" title="Jackie Chan">Jackie Chan</a> as well as religious testimonies by other Korean celebrities such as <a href="/wiki/Choi_Ji-woo" title="Choi Ji-woo">Choi Ji-woo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Won-hee" title="Kim Won-hee">Kim Won-hee</a> and Nam Hee-seok.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He is also known for his missionary work, charitable endeavors and volunteerism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redcross_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-redcross-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Aside from his job as an actor, Shin currently hosts the entertainment news program <i>Entertainment Weekly</i> on <a href="/wiki/KBS2" title="KBS2">KBS2</a>, the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KBS_Nine_O%27Clock_News" title="KBS Nine O'Clock News">KBS Nine O'Clock News</a></i> segment <i>KBS Entertainment 925</i> on <a href="/wiki/KBS1" title="KBS1">KBS1</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Channel_A_(TV_channel)" title="Channel A (TV channel)">Channel A</a> talk show <i>Show King</i>. He has written two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Children%27s_book" title="Children's book">children's books</a>: <i>Aladdin and the Magic Lamp</i> (2012) and <i>Clown with Smiles and Tears</i> (2013).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He is also an acting professor at <a href="/wiki/Induk_University" title="Induk University">Induk University</a> since March 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shin established the production company <a class="external text" href="http://www.hjfilm.net/hj/" rel="nofollow">HJ Film</a> and is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CEO" title="CEO">CEO</a> of <a class="external text" href="http://www.3jaloe.co.kr/3jaloe/main.do" rel="nofollow">3J Cosmetics</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shin_Hyun-joon_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2009, Shin's then-manager of five years (identified only as Jang) filed a complaint against the actor, alleging that Shin had <a href="/wiki/Assault" title="Assault">assaulted</a> him after a heated argument at a bar in <a href="/wiki/Sangsu-dong" title="Sangsu-dong">Sangsu-dong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mapo_District" title="Mapo District">Mapo District</a> on September 22.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> At a press conference on September 25, Shin admitted to hitting Jang "lightly" and made a public apology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> After the medical report showed no serious injuries, Jang declined to press charges, and the lawsuit was dropped on September 27.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shin married Kim Kyung-mi (Linda Kim), a Korean-American woman 12 years his junior at the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Hyatt_Seoul" title="Grand Hyatt Seoul">Grand Hyatt Seoul</a> on May 26, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> His wife is studying for a doctorate in music in the United States, and Shin related on his show <i>Entertainment Weekly</i> that he bumped into her in the doorway of a building and fell in love at first sight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
