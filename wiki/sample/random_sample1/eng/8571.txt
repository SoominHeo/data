<p>The <b>Shield Portable</b> (originally the <b>NVIDIA Shield</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Handheld_game_console" title="Handheld game console">handheld game console</a> developed by <a href="/wiki/Nvidia" title="Nvidia">Nvidia</a>, released on July 31, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shield Portable runs on <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">Android</a> Lollipop 5.1, featuring a <a href="/wiki/Flip_(form)" title="Flip (form)">flip</a> 130mm (5-inch) touchscreen display<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> with <a href="/wiki/720p" title="720p">1280x720</a> resolution.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EIR_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-EIR-5">[5]</a></sup> The device is similar in shape to an <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360_controller" title="Xbox 360 controller">Xbox 360 controller</a> and similar in control setup to a <a href="/wiki/DualShock" title="DualShock">DualShock</a> controller, with two analog joysticks, a <a href="/wiki/D-pad" title="D-pad">D-pad</a>, and other buttons. It is the first device to use Nvidia's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tegra_4" title="Tegra 4">Tegra 4</a> processor. It was originally called Shield or Nvidia Shield, but since the launch of the <a href="/wiki/Shield_Tablet" title="Shield Tablet">Shield Tablet</a>, it is called the Shield Portable.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shield_Tablet_released_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shield_Tablet_released-6">[6]</a></sup> Due to being out of stock for many months, it is thought to be discontinued, but there has not yet been any official announcement from Nvidia.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shield_Portable&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Through Nvidia's GameStream suite, Shield Portable allows the streaming of games running on a <a href="/wiki/Desktop_computer" title="Desktop computer">desktop</a> PC equipped with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GeForce_600_Series" title="GeForce 600 Series">GeForce GTX 650</a> or higher video card (formerly known as <i>GeForce PC Streaming</i>). The Android 4.4.2 update for Shield Portable adds Console Mode, which allows a Shield Portable to be connected to a TV at either 720p, 1080p or 4K resolution (either using a wireless connection or MicroHDMI) and controlled with a <a href="/wiki/Bluetooth" title="Bluetooth">Bluetooth</a> controller, and software for mapping on-screen control buttons to the device's hardware buttons for Android games which do not natively support them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-anandtech-consolemode_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-anandtech-consolemode-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pcw-shieldworth_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-pcw-shieldworth-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Alongside the exclusive games available through Nvidia's own TegraZone market and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GeForce_NOW" title="GeForce NOW">GeForce NOW</a> game-streaming service, Shield Portable can also access the <a href="/wiki/Google_Play" title="Google Play">Google Play</a> store, as with most other Android-based devices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nvidia1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-nvidia1-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Software_updates">Software updates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shield_Portable&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Software updates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nvidia released SHIELD Portable Software Upgrade 103 on July 23, 2015, which added Android 5.1, <a href="/wiki/Chromecast" title="Chromecast">Chromecast</a> support to connect to a TV and removes some games that do not work with Android 5.1. Miracast support was also removed. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nvidia released SHIELD Portable Software Upgrade 106 on September 3, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nvidia released SHIELD Portable Software Upgrade 110 on July 6, 2016. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shield_Portable&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nvidia Shield Portable has received mixed reception from critics. Generally, reviewers praised the performance of the device, but criticized the cost and lack of worthwhile games. <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a>'s Scott Lowe rated the Shield Portable a 6.8/10, noting the "impractical cost and a scarcity of compelling games".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Engadget" title="Engadget">Engadget</a>'s review noted the system's "extremely impressive PC gaming", but also that due to its high price, the device was "a hard sell as a portable game console", especially when compared to similar handhelds on the market.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/CNET" title="CNET">CNET</a>'s Eric Franklin states in his review of the device that "The Nvidia Shield is an extremely well made device, with performance that pretty much obliterates any mobile product before it; but like most new console launches, there is currently a lack of available games worth your time."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Eurogamer" title="Eurogamer">Eurogamer</a>'s comprehensive review of the device provides a detailed account of the device and its features; concluded by saying: "In the here and now, the first-gen Shield Portable is a gloriously niche, luxury product - the most powerful Android system on the market by a clear stretch and possessing a unique link to PC gaming that's seriously impressive in beta form, and can only get better." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
