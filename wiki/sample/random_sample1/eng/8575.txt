<p><i><b>Silmido</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_South_Korean_films_of_2003" title="List of South Korean films of 2003">2003 South Korean film</a>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Kang_Woo-suk" title="Kang Woo-suk">Kang Woo-suk</a>. It is based on the true story of <a href="/wiki/209th_Detachment,_2325th_Group" title="209th Detachment, 2325th Group">Unit 684</a>, though parts of the film are extrapolations as the actual details of events are unknown. The film was both critically well received and a financial success, and was the first film in South Korea to attract a box office audience of over 10 million viewers.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silmido_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 21 January 1968, 31 <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korean</a> commandos of <a href="/wiki/Unit_124" title="Unit 124">Unit 124</a> are shown to have infiltrated <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blue_House_Raid" title="Blue House Raid">failed mission to assassinate</a> President <a href="/wiki/Park_Chung-hee" title="Park Chung-hee">Park Chung-hee</a>.</p>
<p>As a means of retaliation, the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea_Armed_Forces" title="Republic of Korea Armed Forces">Republic of Korea Armed Forces</a> assembled a team of 31 social outcasts including criminals on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Death_penalty" title="Death penalty">death row</a> and <a href="/wiki/Life_imprisonment" title="Life imprisonment">life imprisonment</a>, in a plot to kill <a href="/wiki/Kim_Il-sung" title="Kim Il-sung">Kim Il-sung</a>. The team is designated 'Unit 684'. The recruits are taken to the island of <a href="/wiki/Silmido" title="Silmido">Silmido</a> for training. The mission is offered to the recruits as the only way to redeem themselves and show their loyalty to their country. If they succeed, they will win their freedom and a new life. With this goal in mind, they endure their training. The training is shown over several months, with the recruits enduring various forms of extremely vigorous training and regular physical punishment, including being <a href="/wiki/Human_branding" title="Human branding">branded</a>. One recruit is killed after he falls from a <a href="/wiki/Ropes_course" title="Ropes course">ropes course</a>.</p>
<p>At the end of their training, they are dispatched on their mission to North Korea, but are recalled not long after their departure. It is revealed that the project has been called off, as the government attempts a peaceful reunification with the North. The recruits return to Silmido discouraged and frustrated. Shortly afterwards two of the Unit 684 members escape from their barracks and rape a female doctor. They are quickly discovered, and believing that they will be executed, decide to commit suicide. One kills the other at his request but is apprehended before he is able to kill himself. The apprehended soldier is then returned to the camp, tied up, and made to watch his fellow Unit 684 members being beaten by the guards for the two men's betrayal. Enraged, one of the Unit 684 members being beaten is able to take his guard's bat and kills the tied up soldier for bringing disgrace to the unit.</p>
<p>To keep the plot to kill Kim Il-sung unknown to the outside world, the South Korean intelligence agency decide to kill all the members of Unit 684. The unit's commander protests, but is told that if his troops failed to follow this order, they too would be killed alongside Unit 684. Torn between his duty to follow orders and his personal honor, the commander intentionally leaks this information to one of the Unit 684 members. Unit 684, realizing they are going to be killed that same night, make plans to mutiny. They attack and kill the majority of their guards, and find out from one of the guards that they legally no longer exist, and never would have received recognition for their mission if it succeeded, nor even be allowed to return to society. Unit 684 decide to escape from the island and make their story known. The 20 remaining members of Unit 684 capture a bus containing civilians and head to <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>. An official pronouncement is heard over the radio that 20 "armed communist agents" have infiltrated the country, and a state of emergency is declared. After charging through one army roadblock and winning a firefight they are eventually stopped and surrounded by soldiers in front of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuhan_Corporation" title="Yuhan Corporation">Yuhan Corporation</a> building in <a href="/wiki/Dongjak_District" title="Dongjak District">Dongjak District</a>, Seoul. A firefight ensues, with the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea_Army" title="Republic of Korea Army">Republic of Korea Army</a> showing no regard for the welfare of the civilians on board the bus. All of the Unit 684 members are either killed or wounded, and many South Korean soldiers are also killed. The surviving Unit 684 members release the civilians who have not been killed by the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea_Army" title="Republic of Korea Army">Republic of Korea Army</a>, before committing suicide using their own hand grenades. An investigation into the incident is shown to have been carried out; however the report is not read and is seen to be filed away in storage.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silmido_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Filming took place mainly in South Korea, but underwater and winter training scenes were filmed in <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-variety-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silmido_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Silmido was well received by audiences in South Korea, where it was viewed by over 6 million people in its first 26 days, taking in over <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>30 million in South Korea during this time alone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-variety-1">[1]</a></sup> Silmido would also gross US$4,540,528 in Japan, as well as US$298,347 from its limited release in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Silmido would go on to become the first film to have a box office audience of over 10 million in South Korea, and held the record for the most viewed film in theatres until the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Taegukgi_(film)" title="Taegukgi (film)">Taegukgi</a></i> in 2004, which received 11.74 million viewings.</p>
<p>Derek Elley from <a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a> gave the film a positive review, praising both its production and casting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-variety-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_accuracy">Historical accuracy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silmido_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Historical accuracy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Silmido_map_nasa_world_wind.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="655" data-file-width="1280" height="113" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Silmido_map_nasa_world_wind.jpg/220px-Silmido_map_nasa_world_wind.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Silmido_map_nasa_world_wind.jpg/330px-Silmido_map_nasa_world_wind.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Silmido_map_nasa_world_wind.jpg/440px-Silmido_map_nasa_world_wind.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Silmido_map_nasa_world_wind.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map of the Incheon coastal area showing the location of Silmido.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The release of this film brought public attention to Unit 684, and in 2006 the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_of_National_Defense_(Republic_of_Korea)" title="Ministry of National Defense (Republic of Korea)">South Korean Ministry of National Defense</a> released an official report on the unit and the uprising, officially acknowledging its existence for the first time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JAD_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-JAD-4">[4]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brigadier_General" title="Brigadier General">Brigadier General</a> Nam Dae-yeon said that the 31 Silmido recruits who made up Unit 684 were part of a <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea_Air_Force" title="Republic of Korea Air Force">Republic of Korea Air Force</a> squadron. Seven died in training and 20 were killed in the uprising. The four who survived were executed after a military trial in 1972.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-5">[5]</a></sup> Nam stated that documents describing Unit 684's mission no longer exist, but the government has not denied that its mission was to kill Kim Il-sung.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>What actually caused the uprising on August 23, 1971, is unclear. The film shows the government deciding that the recruits had to be killed because they knew too much. The recruits find out and revolt. <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Kim" title="Jonathan Kim">Jonathan Kim</a>, the film's producer, acknowledges that history is unclear at this point.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Six guards survived the Silmido uprising. One of the guards, Yang Dong Su, confirmed that the unit's mission had been to infiltrate North Korea and kill Kim Il-sung. Yang stated that though the film portrays the 31 recruits as death-row inmates, most were petty criminals. Yang stated that "They were the kind who would get into street fights a lot". Yang also gives his version of why the uprising occurred.</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>They revolted because they felt that they were never going to get the chance to go to North Korea and that they would never be allowed to leave the island. They were in despair.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<div class="templatequotecite"><cite>— Yang Dong Su</cite></div>
</blockquote>
<p>On May 19, 2010, the Seoul Central District Court ordered that the government pay 273 million <a href="/wiki/Korean_won" title="Korean won">won</a> in compensation to the families of 21 members of Unit 684. The court found that "the Silmido agents were not informed of the level of danger involved with their training, and the harshness of the training violated their basic human rights" and also acknowledged the emotional pain the government caused by not officially disclosing the agents’ deaths to family members until 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JAD_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-JAD-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
