<p><i><b>Silver Service</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Brand" title="Brand">brand</a> applied by <a href="/wiki/Amtrak" title="Amtrak">Amtrak</a> to rail services on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_East_Coast" title="United States East Coast">United States East Coast</a> between <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miami,_Florida" title="Miami, Florida">Miami, Florida</a>. This comprises two long-distance trains:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Silver_Meteor" title="Silver Meteor">Silver Meteor</a></i></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Silver_Star_(Amtrak_train)" title="Silver Star (Amtrak train)">Silver Star</a></i></li>
</ul>
<p>These two services follow the same general path between New York City and Miami, but diverge between <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mount,_North_Carolina" title="Rocky Mount, North Carolina">Rocky Mount, North Carolina</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Savannah,_Georgia" title="Savannah, Georgia">Savannah, Georgia</a>. The <i>Silver Meteor</i> takes a coastal route through <a href="/wiki/Fayetteville,_North_Carolina" title="Fayetteville, North Carolina">Fayetteville, North Carolina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florence,_South_Carolina" title="Florence, South Carolina">Florence</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charleston,_South_Carolina" title="Charleston, South Carolina">Charleston, South Carolina</a>, while the <i>Silver Star</i> takes an inland route through <a href="/wiki/Raleigh,_North_Carolina" title="Raleigh, North Carolina">Raleigh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cary,_North_Carolina" title="Cary, North Carolina">Cary, North Carolina</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Camden,_South_Carolina" title="Camden, South Carolina">Camden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Columbia,_South_Carolina" title="Columbia, South Carolina">Columbia, South Carolina</a>. The Silver Star also serves <a href="/wiki/Tampa,_Florida" title="Tampa, Florida">Tampa, Florida</a>.</p>
<p>Between 1996 and 2002 Amtrak operated a third New York–Miami train under this brand named the <i><a href="/wiki/Palmetto_(train)" title="Palmetto (train)">Silver Palm</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 1970s Amtrak used the brand <b>Florida Fleet</b> to include the <i>Silver Meteor</i>, <i>Silver Star</i>, and the now-discontinued <i><a href="/wiki/Champion_(train)" title="Champion (train)">Champion</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
