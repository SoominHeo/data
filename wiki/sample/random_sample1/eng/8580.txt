<p><b>Sylvie Vartan</b> (born <b>Sylvie Georges Vartanian</b>; 15 August 1944) is a French singer and actress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup> She is known as one of the most productive and tough-sounding <a href="/wiki/Y%C3%A9-y%C3%A9" title="Yé-yé">yé-yé</a> artists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yeye_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-yeye-3">[3]</a></sup> Her performances often featured elaborate show-dance choreography,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yeye_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-yeye-3">[3]</a></sup> and she made many appearances on French and Italian TV. Yearly shows with then-husband <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Hallyday" title="Johnny Hallyday">Johnny Hallyday</a> attracted full houses at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris_Olympia" title="Paris Olympia">Olympia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Palais_des_congr%C3%A8s_de_Paris" title="Palais des congrès de Paris">Palais des congrès de Paris</a> throughout the 1960s and mid-1970s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yeye_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-yeye-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2004, after a break in performances, she began recording and giving concerts of jazz ballads in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Francophone" title="Francophone">francophone</a> countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sylvie_Vartan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vartan was born in <a href="/wiki/Iskrets" title="Iskrets">Iskrets</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sofia_Province" title="Sofia Province">Sofia Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bulgaria" title="Kingdom of Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>. Her father, Georges Vartanian (1912-1970), was born in France to a Bulgarian mother and an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armenian_people" title="Armenian people">Armenian</a> father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He worked as an <a href="/wiki/Attach%C3%A9" title="Attaché">attaché</a> at the French embassy in Sofia. The family shortened the name to Vartan. Her mother, Ilona (<i>née</i> Mayer), daughter of prominent architect Rudolf Mayer, was of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hungarian_people" title="Hungarian people">Hungarian</a> Jewish background.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (March 2014)">page needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. When the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Army" title="Soviet Army">Soviet Army</a> occupied Bulgaria in September 1944, the Vartan family house was nationalised and they moved to <a href="/wiki/Sofia" title="Sofia">Sofia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1952, Sylvie's father's friend, film director Dako Dakovski, offered her the role of a schoolgirl in the movie <i>Pod Igoto</i>, a film about Bulgarian rebels against the Ottoman occupation. Participating in the film made her dream of becoming an entertainer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The hardships of postwar Bulgaria made the family emigrate to Paris in December 1952.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup> At first they stayed in the Lion d'Argent hotel near <a href="/wiki/Les_Halles" title="Les Halles">Les Halles</a>, where Georges found a job, then for the next four years they stayed in a single room at the Angleterre Hotel. Young Sylvie had to work hard to keep up at school and blend in with her schoolmates. She spent two years learning French. In 1960, her family moved to an apartment in Michel Bizot Avenue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup> Thanks to the influence of her music producer brother Eddie, music became teenage Sylvie's main interest. Her most influential genres were <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> and, out of spite toward her strict high school, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock_%27n%27_roll" title="Rock 'n' roll">rock 'n' roll</a>. Her favourite artists included <a href="/wiki/Brenda_Lee" title="Brenda Lee">Brenda Lee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Haley" title="Bill Haley">Bill Haley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis Presley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sylvie_Vartan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Twisting_schoolgirl">Twisting schoolgirl</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sylvie_Vartan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Twisting schoolgirl">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1961, Eddie offered Sylvie the chance to record the song "Panne d'essence" with French rocker Frankie Jordan. The <a href="/wiki/Decca_Records" title="Decca Records">Decca Records</a> EP was a surprise hit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup> Although she was not credited on the sleeve, "Panne d'essence" provided Vartan her first appearance on <a href="/wiki/TF1" title="TF1">French television</a>. The journalists gave her the nickname <i>la collégienne du twist</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-9"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup> After the "twisting schoolgirl" had finished the Victor Hugo High School, she was free to sign a contract with Decca Records to start recording her own EP; carrying the title song "Quand le film est triste," a cover of <a href="/wiki/Sue_Thompson" title="Sue Thompson">Sue Thompson</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Sad_Movies_(Make_Me_Cry)" title="Sad Movies (Make Me Cry)">Sad Movies (Make Me Cry)</a>", the EP was on sale by the beginning of December 1961.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-10"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup> It was yet another hit, that served her first concert in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris_Olympia" title="Paris Olympia">Paris Olympia</a> Hall on 12 December 1961. "Est-ce que tu le sais?", her version of <a href="/wiki/Ray_Charles" title="Ray Charles">Ray Charles</a>' "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/What%27d_I_Say_(song)" title="What'd I Say (song)">What'd I Say</a>", was quickly released. She opened for <a href="/wiki/Vince_Taylor" title="Vince Taylor">Vince Taylor</a> at Paris Olympia for her second concert.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-11"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup> In July she toured France with <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_B%C3%A9caud" title="Gilbert Bécaud">Gilbert Bécaud</a>. In autumn 1962 she released her version of "<a href="/wiki/The_Loco-Motion" title="The Loco-Motion">The Loco-Motion</a>" on EP and her first LP, <i>Sylvie</i>. Her next hit was "Tous mes copains", issued on EP in 1962.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-12"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup> She also had her first adult part in a movie with the small part of a singer in the film <i>Un clair de lune à Maubeuge</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yeye_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-yeye-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 1963, Paul Anka offered her "I'm Watching You," which became her first international hit in Japan and Korea. She rounded out the year with four TV specials and becoming the darling of the teen magazine <i>Salut les Copains</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yeye_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-yeye-3">[3]</a></sup> Six of her 31 songs released in 1962/1963 became Top-20 European hits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yeye_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-yeye-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late winter 1963, Vartan toured France with <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Hallyday" title="Johnny Hallyday">Johnny Hallyday</a> and starred with him in the movie <i>D'où viens-tu, Johnny?</i>. Later, after announcing their engagement over the radio, the young couple performed to a noisy audience of 200,000 at Paris' La Nation Square.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yeye_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-yeye-3">[3]</a></sup> At the year's end they traveled to <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville, Tennessee</a> to record an album with <a href="/wiki/The_Jordanaires" title="The Jordanaires">The Jordanaires</a>. With the special treatment of vocalists, strings, and brass sections in the true Nashville style, these recordings were a great commercial success in France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yeye_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-yeye-3">[3]</a></sup> The album <i>Sylvie à Nashville</i> included the hits "La plus belle pour aller danser" and "Si je chante" and three new songs in English (one featuring Paul Anka). Accompanied by the movie <i>Cherchez l'idole</i>, the EP with "La plus belle pour aller danser" became Number 1 in France, sold over a million copies in Japan, was very successful in Korea and Spain, and was her first release in Italy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-13"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup> "Il n'a rien retrouvé" became another international hit that year.</p>
<p>In 1964 she appeared at the Paris Olympia as the main attraction (third performance) on the same bill as <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">The Beatles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trini_Lopez" title="Trini Lopez">Trini Lopez</a>. A commercial for the "Renown" clothing line and a supporting role in the film "Patate" increased her fame. She also appeared on U.S. TV: <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ed_Sullivan_Show" title="The Ed Sullivan Show">The Ed Sullivan Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Shindig!" title="Shindig!">Shindig!</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hullabaloo_(TV_series)" title="Hullabaloo (TV series)">Hullabaloo</a></i>, and an international concert tour, including Canada, South America and Polynesia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-14"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup> In Tokyo she gave 13 concerts in 12 days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-15"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Y.C3.A9-y.C3.A9_diva">Yé-yé diva</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sylvie_Vartan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Yé-yé diva">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In December 1964, as Hallyday went into military service, Sylvie's brother Eddie hired two English songwriting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Session_musicians" title="Session musicians">session musicians</a>, Tommy Brown on drums and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mick_Jones_(Foreigner)" title="Mick Jones (Foreigner)">Mick Jones</a> on guitar, as they went to record in New York; they wrote her the hit "Cette lettre-là".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yeye_3-8"><a href="#cite_note-yeye-3">[3]</a></sup> Set in the smart design of the <i>J.C. Averty Show</i>, "Cette lettre-là" was the first of Sylvie Vartan's TV variety night performances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TV_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-TV-8">[8]</a></sup> In 1966–67 she recorded more original hits. "Dis moi, que tu m'aimes" premiered on TV on 9 July 1967 was accompanied by a group of male dancers. "Le jour qui vient" was aired on the <i>Dim dam dom</i> program of the French Television 1 in color on 14 January 1968. "2'35 de bonheur" and "Comme un garçon" were other #1s in France, Italy, Belgium, Japan, or Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TV_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-TV-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>On 11 April 1968 Vartan suffered injuries in a traffic accident but was back on tour in August. She showed off her new sexy <a href="/wiki/Cabaret" title="Cabaret">cabaret</a>-dancer image<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-16"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup> to the French TV audience in the "Jolie poupée" show on 4 December 1968; the collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Maritie_and_Gilbert_Carpentier" title="Maritie and Gilbert Carpentier">Maritie and Gilbert Carpentier</a> produced a highly entertaining result.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TV_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-TV-8">[8]</a></sup> The variety night included the performance of "Irrésistiblement", accompanied by elaborate girl-group choreography, and "Jolie Poupée", a dialogue between a doll Sylvie and an adult one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TV_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-TV-8">[8]</a></sup> The hits of the "Jolie poupée" show were released as the <i>Maritza</i> album. In Italy, she released a cover version of <a href="/wiki/Mina_(singer)" title="Mina (singer)">Mina</a>'s "Zum zum zum". Being the 23rd-biggest-selling single of 1969 in Italy, Vartan's version did better in Italian charts than Mina's original.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Vartan continued with her one-woman shows at the Paris Olympia in December 1968; they were published as the documentary <i>Sylvie à L'Olympia</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmography_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-filmography-10">[10]</a></sup> For nine Saturday nights in winter of 1969, Vartan starred in the <i>Doppia coppia</i> variety nights of <a href="/wiki/RAI" title="RAI">RAI</a>, performing her hits "Irrésistiblement" ("Irresistibilmente") and "Le jour qui vient" ("Una cicala canta") in Italian.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-17"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TV_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-TV-8">[8]</a></sup> In March 1969, "Irrestibilmente" entered the Italian charts at No. 3, peaked at No. 2 and stayed in Top Ten for 10 weeks. In 1969 the single was the No. 22 single on the Italian chart. The tender ballad "Blam blam blam", written especially for <i>Doppia coppia</i>, peaked at No. 14 on the Italian charts and was among the 100 biggest-selling singles in Italy in 1969.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>After her world tour in autumn 1969, she returned to Italy to perform "Festa negli occhi, festa nel cuore" on a <i><a href="/wiki/Canzonissima" title="Canzonissima">Canzonissima</a></i> variety night and "C'est un jour à rester couché" on the <i>Incontro con Sylvie Vartan</i> TV show, recorded in the Gattopardo Club of <a href="/wiki/Messina" title="Messina">Messina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TV_8-5"><a href="#cite_note-TV-8">[8]</a></sup> Her appearances were popular in Italy and France; she donned a different costume for each song, including plenty of short skirts and <a href="/wiki/Barbarella_(film)" title="Barbarella (film)">Barbarella</a> boots. The cartoonists of both countries even drew Sylvie Vartan lookalikes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-18"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1970.E2.80.93today">1970–today</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sylvie_Vartan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1970–today">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Despite suffering a second serious car accident in 1970,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-19"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup> Sylvie Vartan continued to perform and record extensively;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yeye_3-9"><a href="#cite_note-yeye-3">[3]</a></sup> and in 1972 she starred in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Malpertuis_(film)" title="Malpertuis (film)">Malpertuis</a></i>. She sold millions of records on the RCA label, which made her its most prolific artist after Elvis Presley. Her most famous songs are "J'ai un probleme", "L'amour au Diapason" (73), "Parle moi de ta vie"(71), "Petit Rainbow" (77), "Bye Bye Leroy Brown" (74), "Disco Queen" and "Nicolas" (79), and "Caro Mozart", an international hit in Italian (72). However, her annual world tours and Italian TV shows continued to include her biggest late-1960s hits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-20"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1976 she recorded "Qu'est-ce qui fait pleurer les blondes?", a disco-style cover of a <a href="/wiki/John_Kongos" title="John Kongos">John Kongos</a> track, which topped the French chart for several weeks. Her disco era climaxed at the <i>Dancing star</i> TV show on <a href="/wiki/TF1" title="TF1">TF1</a>, produced by Maritie and Gilbert Carpentier, on 10 September 1977. <a href="/wiki/RCA" title="RCA">RCA</a> published the show as the <i>Dancing star</i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-21"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup> After a week of concerts in Las Vegas in 1982, she released a duet with <a href="/wiki/John_Denver" title="John Denver">John Denver</a>: "Love Again," a #85 single on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> followed in 1985 by a new album in the USA with "Double Exposure" and "One-Shot Lover." In 1986 she took a break from show business; she returned in 1989 with "C'est Fatal" ("E' Fatale" in Italy).</p>
<p>In 1990 Sylvie gave a concert at Sofia's Palace of Culture, opening and closing with a Bulgarian song. This was her first visit to the city since her emigration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-22"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1991 she performed for three weeks at the Palais des Sports in Paris and hit the charts with "Quand tu es là". In 1995, she starred in "L'ange noir" directed by Jean-Claude Brisseau. In 1996 Sylvie recorded a successful album ("Toutes les femmes ont un secret") featuring "Je n'aime encore que toi" written by Quebecer Luc Plamondon. After her brother Eddie died in 2001, she took another break from performing in public. In fall 2004 she started recording and giving concerts of jazz ballads in francophone countries and Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-23"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup> She performed two weeks in the <a href="/wiki/Palais_des_congr%C3%A8s_de_Paris" title="Palais des congrès de Paris">Palais des congrès</a> in 2004 and 2008—all sold-out concerts. In 2009 she toured Turkey, France, Japan, Switzerland, Quebec, USA, Italy, Spain, Netherlands and Belgium.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sylvie_Vartan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sylvie Vartan was one of the first <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_roll" title="Rock and roll">rock</a> girls in France. Before her, it was not common for a French pop artist to sing and dance at the same time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taivaan_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-taivaan-12">[12]</a></sup> Sylvie Vartan was the most productive and active of the <a href="/wiki/Y%C3%A9-y%C3%A9" title="Yé-yé">yé-yé</a> girls;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yeye_3-10"><a href="#cite_note-yeye-3">[3]</a></sup> and her rock-and-roll voice and rhythms made her the toughest-sounding of those.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yeye_3-11"><a href="#cite_note-yeye-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Influence">Influence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sylvie_Vartan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Influence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vartan's concerts were said to be an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rationalization_(making_excuses)" title="Rationalization (making excuses)">excuse</a> for some of the first violent acts of <a href="/wiki/Rocker_(subculture)" title="Rocker (subculture)">rockers</a> in French theatres.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-24"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup> There is a Sylvie Vartan <a href="/wiki/List_of_rose_cultivars_named_after_people" title="List of rose cultivars named after people">rose variety</a> and a toy factory named after her. The <a href="/wiki/Mattel" title="Mattel">Mattel</a> toy company designed a dummy model after her body.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taivaan_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-taivaan-12">[12]</a></sup> In 2005, the singer was appointed as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WHO_Goodwill_Ambassador" title="WHO Goodwill Ambassador">WHO Goodwill Ambassador</a> for Maternal and Child Health in the European Region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WHO_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-WHO-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sylvie_Vartan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1962, during her second concert in Paris Olympia Hall, Vartan met rock musician <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Hallyday" title="Johnny Hallyday">Johnny Hallyday</a>; they were married in <a href="/wiki/Loconville" title="Loconville">Loconville</a> on April 12, 1965. They had a son, David Michael Benjamin, who was later known as <a href="/wiki/David_Hallyday" title="David Hallyday">David Hallyday</a>. Sylvie Vartan and Johnny Hallyday were their generation's "golden couple" in France, and their marriage was among the most carefully watched relationships for the nation's publicity. But Hallyday openly preferred his pals to parenting, sparking many disagreements, and the couple finally divorced on November 4, 1980. Vartan is currently married to record producer <a href="/wiki/Tony_Scotti" title="Tony Scotti">Tony Scotti</a> and they have adopted a young Bulgarian girl, Darina. Vartan is an aunt to Eddie Vartan's son <a href="/wiki/Michael_Vartan" title="Michael Vartan">Michael Vartan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officielle_2-25"><a href="#cite_note-officielle-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
