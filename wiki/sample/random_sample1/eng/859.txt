<p><b>CJ E&amp;M Corporation</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">씨제이이앤엠 주식회사</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">CJ E&amp;M Jusikhoesa</span></i>), formerly <b>O Media Holdings Limited</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">(주)오미디어홀딩스</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Jusikhoesa O Midieo Holdingseu</span></i>) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> entertainment and media contents company. It is a subsidiary of the <a href="/wiki/CJ_Group" title="CJ Group">CJ Group</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CJ_E%26M&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>2010 – <b>O Media Holdings</b> was founded as a merger of the following companies: <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=CJ_Media&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="CJ Media (page does not exist)">CJ Media</a>, <a href="/wiki/On-Media" title="On-Media">On-Media</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mnet_Media" title="Mnet Media">Mnet Media</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CJ_Entertainment" title="CJ Entertainment">CJ Entertainment</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=CJ_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="CJ Games (page does not exist)">CJ Games</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=CJ_Internet&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="CJ Internet (page does not exist)">CJ Internet</a>, and the media division of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=CJ_O_Shopping&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="CJ O Shopping (page does not exist)">CJ O Shopping</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>

</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Present">Present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CJ_E%26M&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>CJ E&amp;M currently has four main <a href="/wiki/Division_(business)" title="Division (business)">divisions</a>, namely:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=CJ_E%26M_Broadcasting_Division&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="CJ E&amp;M Broadcasting Division (page does not exist)">CJ E&amp;M Broadcasting Division</a> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">CJ E&amp;M 방송사업부문</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">CJ E&amp;M bangsongsa yeobbumun</span></i>) - owner of cable television channels</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/CJ_E%26M_Film_Division" title="CJ E&amp;M Film Division">CJ E&amp;M Film Division</a> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">CJ E&amp;M 영화사업부문</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">CJ E&amp;M yeonghwasa yeobbumun</span></i>) - producer and distributor of movies and some TV dramas</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/CJ_E%26M_Music_Performance_Division" title="CJ E&amp;M Music Performance Division">CJ E&amp;M Music Performance Division</a> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">CJ E&amp;M 음악공연사업부문</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">CJ E&amp;M eum-ak gongyeonsa yeobbumun</span></i>) - producer and distributor of music releases, also an agency for musicians and some actors</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=CJ_E%26M_Smart_Media_Division&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="CJ E&amp;M Smart Media Division (page does not exist)">CJ E&amp;M Smart Media Division</a> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">CJ E&amp;M 스마트미디어사업부문</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">CJ E&amp;M seumateu medio-sa yeobbumun</span></i>) - operator of various entertainment and media websites</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CJ_E%26M&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In April 2015, CJ E&amp;M was accused of mobilizing young employees to fill the seats of its annual general meeting (AGM) of shareholders in an attempt to silence shareholders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2015, CJ E&amp;M and its <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">US</a>-based subsidiary CJ E&amp;M America were sued by Seoul-based music agency <a href="/wiki/DFSB_Kollective" title="DFSB Kollective">DFSB Kollective</a> for <a href="/wiki/Copyright_infringement" title="Copyright infringement">copyright infringement</a> and violation of the <a href="/wiki/Digital_Millennium_Copyright_Act" title="Digital Millennium Copyright Act">Digital Millennium Copyright Act</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court_for_the_Central_District_of_California" title="United States District Court for the Central District of California">Central District Court of California</a> (case number 5:2015mc80265), with the latter seeking to get 50 million <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">dollars</a> for damages (therefore making it the biggest copyright infringement case of 2015 in the US based on monetary damages sought). Responding to the suit, CJ E&amp;M accused DFSB of being unhappy on the final decision on a similar lawsuit filed in Seoul in 2011. The first trial was set for March 1, 2016 after the court rejected CJ E&amp;M's motion to dismiss the case.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
