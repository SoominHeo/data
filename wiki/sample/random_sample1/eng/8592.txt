<p><b>Symbian Ltd.</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Software_development" title="Software development">software development</a> and licensing company, known for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Symbian_OS" title="Symbian OS">Symbian OS</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Smartphone" title="Smartphone">smartphone</a> <a href="/wiki/Operating_system" title="Operating system">operating system</a>, and other related technologies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BusinessWeek_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-BusinessWeek-1">[1]</a></sup> Its headquarters were in <a href="/wiki/Southwark" title="Southwark">Southwark</a>, London, England, with other offices opened in Cambridge UK, Sweden, Silicon Valley, Japan, India, China, South Korea, Sydney Australia.</p>
<p>It was established on 24 June 1998 as a partnership between <a href="/wiki/Nokia" title="Nokia">Nokia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ericsson" title="Ericsson">Ericsson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Motorola" title="Motorola">Motorola</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SONY" title="SONY">SONY</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Psion_(computers)" title="Psion (computers)">Psion</a> to exploit the convergence between PDAs and mobile phones. Ten years to the day after it was established, on 24 June 2008, Nokia announced that they intended to acquire the shares that they did not already own, at a cost of €264 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nokia_2008-06-24_Purchase_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nokia_2008-06-24_Purchase-2">[2]</a></sup> On the same day the <a href="/wiki/Symbian_Foundation" title="Symbian Foundation">Symbian Foundation</a> was announced, with the aim to "provide royalty-free software and accelerate innovation",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nokia_2008-06-24_Symbian_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nokia_2008-06-24_Symbian-3">[3]</a></sup> and the pledged contribution of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Symbian_OS" title="Symbian OS">Symbian OS</a> and user interfaces.</p>
<p>The acquisition of Symbian Ltd. by Nokia was completed on 2 December 2008,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nokia_2008-12-02_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nokia_2008-12-02-4">[4]</a></sup> at which point all Symbian employees became Nokia employees. Transfer of relevant Symbian Software Ltd. leases, trademarks and domain names from Nokia to the Symbian Foundation was completed in April 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Symbian_Blog_2009-04-02_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Symbian_Blog_2009-04-02-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 18 July 2009, Nokia's Symbian professional services department, which was not transferred to the Symbian Foundation, was sold to the <a href="/wiki/Accenture" title="Accenture">Accenture</a> <a href="/wiki/Information_technology_consulting" title="Information technology consulting">consulting</a> company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters_2009-07-17_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters_2009-07-17-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Symbian_OS">Symbian OS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Symbian_Ltd.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Symbian OS">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Symbian_OS" title="Symbian OS">Symbian OS</a></div>
<p>Symbian Ltd formerly developed and licensed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Symbian_OS" title="Symbian OS">Symbian OS</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Operating_system" title="Operating system">operating system</a> for advanced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2.5G" title="2.5G">2.5G</a> and <a href="/wiki/3G" title="3G">3G</a> <a href="/wiki/Mobile_phone" title="Mobile phone">mobile phones</a>. <a href="/wiki/User_interface" title="User interface">User interface</a> layers were provided by third parties. These included <a href="/wiki/S60_(software_platform)" title="S60 (software platform)">Series 60</a>, <a href="/wiki/Series_80_(software_platform)" title="Series 80 (software platform)">Series 80</a> and <a href="/wiki/Series_90_(software_platform)" title="Series 90 (software platform)">Series 90</a> by Nokia, <a href="/wiki/UIQ" title="UIQ">UIQ</a> from <a href="/wiki/UIQ_Technology" title="UIQ Technology">UIQ Technology</a> and <a href="/wiki/MOAP" title="MOAP">MOAP(S)</a> for <a href="/wiki/NTT_DoCoMo" title="NTT DoCoMo">NTT DoCoMo</a>.</p>
<p>All three interfaces, as well as the OS, are now owned and maintained by the <a href="/wiki/Symbian_Foundation" title="Symbian Foundation">Symbian Foundation</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Shareholding">Shareholding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Symbian_Ltd.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Shareholding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before its outright purchase by Nokia in December 2008, Symbian Ltd. was owned by Nokia (56.3%), <a href="/wiki/Ericsson" title="Ericsson">Ericsson</a> (15.6%), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Ericsson" title="Sony Ericsson">Sony Ericsson</a> (13.1%), <a href="/wiki/Panasonic" title="Panasonic">Matsushita</a> (10.5%), and <a href="/wiki/Samsung" title="Samsung">Samsung</a> (4.5%).</p>
<p>The company's founder shareholders were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Psion_(computers)" title="Psion (computers)">Psion</a>, Nokia, Ericsson and Motorola. Motorola sold its stake in the company to Psion and Nokia in September 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Psion's stake was bought by Nokia, Matsushita, Siemens AG and Sony Ericsson in July 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Much of Symbian's initial intellectual property came from the software arm of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Psion_(computers)" title="Psion (computers)">Psion PLC</a>.</p>
