<p><i><b>Shinya Shokudō</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">深夜食堂</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> also known in English as "Midnight Diner" is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yar%C5%8D_Abe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yarō Abe (page does not exist)">Yarō Abe</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%AE%89%E5%80%8D%E5%A4%9C%E9%83%8E" title="ja:安倍夜郎">ja</a>)</span>. It won the 55th <a href="/wiki/Shogakukan_Manga_Award" title="Shogakukan Manga Award">Shogakukan Manga Award</a> for general manga<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and it was nominated for the 2nd <a href="/wiki/Manga_Taish%C5%8D" title="Manga Taishō">Manga Taishō</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It was adapted into a 10-episode live-action television drama in 2009, followed by second season in 2011. In 2015, a live action film was released by director <a href="/wiki/Joji_Matsuoka" title="Joji Matsuoka">Joji Matsuoka</a> starring <a href="/wiki/Kaoru_Kobayashi_(actor)" title="Kaoru Kobayashi (actor)">Kaoru Kobayashi</a> as The Master. The show is about a late night diner open from midnight to dawn and the stories of its odd-ball patrons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
