<p>Year <b>1453</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MCDLIII</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Monday" title="Common year starting on Monday">common year starting on Monday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. It is sometimes cited as the notional end of the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> by historians who define the medieval period as the time between the Fall of the <a href="/wiki/Western_Roman_Empire" title="Western Roman Empire">Western Roman Empire</a> and the fall of the Byzantine (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Roman_Empire" title="Eastern Roman Empire">Eastern Roman</a>) Empire.</p>

<ul>
<li>April – <a href="/wiki/Tarabya" title="Tarabya">Tarabya</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studius" title="Studius">Studius</a> are taken by the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> in preparation for the assault on <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a>, as are the <a href="/wiki/Prince_Islands" title="Prince Islands">Prince Islands</a>, by the Ottoman fleet under Admiral Baltaoglu.</li>
</ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/April_2" title="April 2">April 2</a>–<a href="/wiki/May_29" title="May 29">May 29</a> – Siege and <a href="/wiki/Fall_of_Constantinople" title="Fall of Constantinople">Fall of Constantinople</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> <a href="/wiki/Sultan" title="Sultan">Sultan</a> <a href="/wiki/Mehmed_the_Conqueror" title="Mehmed the Conqueror">Mehmed the Conqueror</a> ends the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine</a> (or Eastern Roman) Empire after more than a thousand years by capturing the capital, <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a> (modern-day <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>). <a href="/wiki/Mortar_(weapon)" title="Mortar (weapon)">Mortars</a> are perhaps used in battle for the first time in this action. The consequent closure of the traditional overland route from <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">Western Europe</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Far_East" title="Far East">Far East</a> and need to identify new <a href="/wiki/Sea" title="Sea">maritime</a> routes leads to the <a href="/wiki/Age_of_Discovery" title="Age of Discovery">Age of Discovery</a> and ends the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_22" title="May 22">May 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/May_1453_lunar_eclipse" title="May 1453 lunar eclipse">Partial lunar eclipse</a>.</li>
<li>July – <a href="/wiki/Revolt_of_Ghent_(1449%E2%80%9353)" title="Revolt of Ghent (1449–53)">Revolt of Ghent</a>: <a href="/wiki/Philip_the_Good" title="Philip the Good">Philip the Good</a>, Duke of Burgundy, crushes the Ghent rebellion.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_17" title="July 17">July 17</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Castillon" title="Battle of Castillon">Battle of Castillon</a>: The French under <a href="/wiki/Jean_Bureau" title="Jean Bureau">Jean Bureau</a> defeat the <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> under the <a href="/wiki/John_Talbot,_1st_Earl_of_Shrewsbury" title="John Talbot, 1st Earl of Shrewsbury">Earl of Shrewsbury</a>, who was killed.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_10" title="October 10">October 10</a> – <a href="/wiki/Sejo_of_Joseon" title="Sejo of Joseon">Sejo of Joseon</a> killed his enemy Kim Jongseo, and gained control of the government. In Korean, this rebellion is called Gyeyujeongnan.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_19" title="October 19">October 19</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Hundred_Years%27_War" title="Hundred Years' War">Hundred Years' War</a> comes to a close with the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_France" title="Kingdom of France">French</a> recapture of <a href="/wiki/Bordeaux" title="Bordeaux">Bordeaux</a>, leaving the English retaining only <a href="/wiki/Calais" title="Calais">Calais</a> on French soil.</li>
</ul>
