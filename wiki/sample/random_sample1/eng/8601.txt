<p><b>Shim Eun-kyung</b> (born May 31, 1994) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a>. She has starred in a number of films and <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">television dramas</a>, notably the box office hits <i><a href="/wiki/Sunny_(2011_film)" title="Sunny (2011 film)">Sunny</a></i> (2011) and <i><a href="/wiki/Miss_Granny" title="Miss Granny">Miss Granny</a></i> (2014).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-name_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-name-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-glamorous_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-glamorous-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shim_Eun-kyung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shim Eun-kyung made her acting debut at age 9 in the 2004 TV series <i>The Woman Who Wants to Marry</i>, and subsequently made a career as one of the best child actresses of her generation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She starred in horror fairytale <i><a href="/wiki/Hansel_and_Gretel_(2007_film)" title="Hansel and Gretel (2007 film)">Hansel and Gretel</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> as well as TV series such as <i><a href="/wiki/Hwang_Jini_(TV_series)" title="Hwang Jini (TV series)">Hwang Jini</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Legend_(TV_series)" title="The Legend (TV series)">The Legend</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Women_of_the_Sun_(2008_TV_series)" title="Women of the Sun (2008 TV series)">Women of the Sun</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Great_Merchant" title="The Great Merchant">The Great Merchant</a></i>, critics say at times even outshining her adult counterparts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, she successfully transitioned as a lead actress via <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Retro" title="Retro">retro</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dramedy" title="Dramedy">dramedy</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Sunny_(2011_film)" title="Sunny (2011 film)">Sunny</a></i>, which tells the story of seven women, crosscutting between their lives in the present and the experiences that first brought them together as teenagers in the 1980s. Distinguished for its period detail, witty dialogue and the performances of its ensemble cast,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> the film shattered the common belief that women-centered pictures can't achieve blockbuster-level success. By appealing to a wide range of age groups,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <i>Sunny'</i>s box office total stood at more than 7 million admissions at the end of its long run.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Director <a href="/wiki/Kang_Hyeong-cheol" title="Kang Hyeong-cheol">Kang Hyeong-cheol</a> described Shim as someone "who is already complete as an actress."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rookie_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-rookie-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shim's other notable roles include a girl caught amidst the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a> in <a href="/wiki/Black_comedy" title="Black comedy">black comedy</a> <i>Kyung-sook, Kyung-sook's Father</i> (also known as <i>My Dad Loves Trouble</i>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> a sister with seemingly supernatural abilities in <i><a href="/wiki/Living_Death_(film)" title="Living Death (film)">Possessed</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> a <a href="/wiki/Depression_(mood)" title="Depression (mood)">depressive</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Quiz_Show_Scandal" title="The Quiz Show Scandal">The Quiz Show Scandal</a></i>, and a <a href="/wiki/Gungnyeo" title="Gungnyeo">court taster</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Masquerade_(2012_film)" title="Masquerade (2012 film)">Masquerade</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 2014 comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Miss_Granny" title="Miss Granny">Miss Granny</a></i>, Shim played a woman in her 70s who miraculously finds herself back in her 20-year-old body.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rookie_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-rookie-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> She said, "This film is quite close to my heart as it is the first film for me to star in not as a child actor, but as full-fledged actress."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> With 8.65 million admissions, its box office success surpassed <i>Sunny'</i>s, proving Shim's ability to carry a film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-glamorous_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-glamorous-2">[2]</a></sup> She also won Best Actress at the <a href="/wiki/Chunsa_Film_Art_Awards" title="Chunsa Film Art Awards">Chunsa Film Art Awards</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baeksang_Arts_Awards" title="Baeksang Arts Awards">Baeksang Arts Awards</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Director%27s_Cut_Awards" title="Director's Cut Awards">Director's Cut Awards</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Buil_Film_Awards" title="Buil Film Awards">Buil Film Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shim next starred as the eccentric <a href="/wiki/Savant_syndrome" title="Savant syndrome">savant</a> pianist heroine in <i><a href="/wiki/Cantabile_Tomorrow" title="Cantabile Tomorrow">Cantabile Tomorrow</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">Korean drama</a> adaptation of Japanese <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Nodame_Cantabile" title="Nodame Cantabile">Nodame Cantabile</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Her upcoming films include the animated <i>Seoul Station</i> directed by <a href="/wiki/Yeon_Sang-ho" title="Yeon Sang-ho">Yeon Sang-ho</a>, and <i>Marital Harmony</i>, the second installment of the "divining art trilogy" after <i><a href="/wiki/The_Face_Reader" title="The Face Reader">The Face Reader</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shim_Eun-kyung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shim is the youngest daughter in her family. Her hobbies are listening to music, reading <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction_comics" title="Science fiction comics">science fiction comics</a>, and doing <a href="/wiki/Taekwondo" title="Taekwondo">taekwondo</a>. She's the drummer of a band she formed with friends, <i>Chick and Candy</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-name_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-name-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>After graduating from Eonbuk Elementary School and Cheongdam Middle School, in 2010 Shim temporarily quit acting to move to the U.S., explaining, "I chose a place where there aren’t a lot of Korean people and decided on a high school in Pittsburgh. Studying is a goal, but I also chose this because I've grown tired mentally and physically after having acted since a young age." She graduated the <a href="/wiki/Professional_Children%27s_School" title="Professional Children's School">Professional Children's School</a> in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shim_Eun-kyung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She shot a commercial with rocker <a href="/wiki/Seo_Taiji" title="Seo Taiji">Seo Taiji</a> in 2008 and played the younger version of <a href="/wiki/Lee_Ji-ah" title="Lee Ji-ah">Lee Ji-ah</a>'s character Sujini in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Legend_(TV_series)" title="The Legend (TV series)">The Legend</a></i>, but she didn't know that Lee and Seo were secretly married until 2011 when Shim was implicated in rumors that she was Seo and Lee's daughter, which she denied.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>Originally nominated for the Best Actress award at the <a href="/wiki/48th_Grand_Bell_Awards" title="48th Grand Bell Awards">2011 Grand Bell Awards</a> for her role in <i><a href="/wiki/Sunny_(2011_film)" title="Sunny (2011 film)">Sunny</a></i>, Shim complained publicly through her <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> account when the awards committee removed her name from the official list of the nominees after she notified them she could not attend the event because she was currently studying in the U.S. She was one of four actors and actresses removed from the initial nomination. Shim ended up winning Best Supporting Actress for her role in <i><a href="/wiki/Romantic_Heaven" title="Romantic Heaven">Romantic Heaven</a></i>; <a href="/wiki/Chun_Woo-hee" title="Chun Woo-hee">Chun Woo-hee</a>, her castmate in <i>Sunny</i>, accepted the honor on Shim’s behalf.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
