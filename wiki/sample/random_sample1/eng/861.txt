<p><b>CLC</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">씨엘씨</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Girl_group" title="Girl group">girl group</a> formed by <a href="/wiki/Cube_Entertainment" title="Cube Entertainment">Cube Entertainment</a> in 2015. The group consists of seven members: Seunghee, Yujin, Seungyeon, Sorn, Yeeun, Elkie and Eunbin. CLC is an initialism which stands for <b>C</b>rysta<b>L</b> <b>C</b>lear.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Koreaboo_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Koreaboo-1">[1]</a></sup> CLC made their debut with the release of their <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> <i>First Love</i> on March 19, 2015 promoting the title track <i>Pepe</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>Sorn is the winner of the first season of <i><a href="/wiki/K-Pop_Star_Hunt" title="K-Pop Star Hunt">K-Pop Star Hunt</a></i> in 2011 after which she subsequently became a trainee under Cube Entertainment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KSH1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-KSH1-3">[3]</a></sup> Yujin and Seunghee (credited as Cube Girls) were part of a collaborative track entitled "Perfume" by <a href="/wiki/Beast_(South_Korean_band)" title="Beast (South Korean band)">Beast</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yoseob" title="Yoseob">Yoseob</a> for Cube Voice Project which was released in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PERFUME_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-PERFUME-4">[4]</a></sup> Seunghee together with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BTOB" title="BTOB">BTOB</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yook_Sungjae" title="Yook Sungjae">Sungjae</a> released the soundtrack "Curious" for the drama <a href="/wiki/Plus_Nine_Boys" title="Plus Nine Boys">Plus Nine Boys</a> in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PLUSNINEBOYS_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-PLUSNINEBOYS-5">[5]</a></sup> Seunghee also appeared on BTOB's music videos, "<a href="/wiki/2nd_Confession" title="2nd Confession">2nd Confession</a>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and "The Winter's Tale".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> CLC also participated in <a href="/wiki/G.NA" title="G.NA">G.NA</a>'s "Secret" music video and acted as background dancers during stage performances at music shows. Seungyeon, Yujin, and Yeeun were also spotted at BTOB's "Beep Beep" music video as the dancers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The group started gaining attention in 2014 for their non-profit street performances to help children with disabilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Koreaboo_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Koreaboo-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It was featured on their first documentary, "CLC's Love Chemistry".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015:_Debut_with_First_Love.2C_Eighteen_and_Question">2015: Debut with <i>First Love</i>, <i>Eighteen</i> and <i>Question</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CLC_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2015: Debut with First Love, Eighteen and Question">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>CLC originally debuted with 5 members including Seunghee, Yujin, Seungyeon, Sorn and Yeeun. In March 2015, CLC revealed teasers for their upcoming debut with the track "Pepe".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> They held their debut showcase on March 18 at the Acts Hotel in Seoul, performing their debut song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> They made their official debut on March 19 with the release of the music video for "Pepe" from their debut album <i>First Love</i>. On the same day, they also made their debut performance on <a href="/wiki/Mnet_(TV_channel)" title="Mnet (TV channel)">Mnet</a>'s <a href="/wiki/M_Countdown" title="M Countdown">M! Countdown</a> music show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> "Pepe" is a track written by <a href="/wiki/Duble_Sidekick" title="Duble Sidekick">Duble Sidekick</a> and Yang Geng, and was made in the style of a retro dance number.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Former label-mate <a href="/wiki/Rain_(entertainer)" title="Rain (entertainer)">Rain</a> choreographed part of Pepe's choreography.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 16, CLC released a special single entitled "Eighteen". On the 17th, the group started their promotions for "Eighteen" at <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">KBS</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Music_Bank_(TV_series)" title="Music Bank (TV series)">Music Bank</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> "Eighteen" was described as song that is about teenage love and has a 60-70s <a href="/wiki/Motown" title="Motown">motown</a> feel that is combined with synthpop from the 80s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 28, CLC made their comeback with their second mini-album <i>Question</i>, promoting their lead single "Curious (Like)".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> They held their first comeback stage at Mnet's M! Countdown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 10–11, CLC held their first overseas promotional tour in Malaysia entitled "First Love Promo Tour in Malaysia". <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal Music Malaysia</a> had specially released an "Asia Special Edition" album version of <i>Question</i>. The album includes songs from <i>First Love</i>, <i>Question</i> and their digital single "Eighteen".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016:_New_members.2C_Refresh.2C_Japanese_debut.2C_NU.CLEAR_and_Chamisma">2016: New members, <i>Refresh</i>, Japanese debut, <i>NU.CLEAR</i> and <i>Chamisma</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CLC_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2016: New members, Refresh, Japanese debut, NU.CLEAR and Chamisma">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On February 24, 2016, CUBE Entertainment announced that CLC would comeback on February 29 with the mini album <i>Refresh</i> and two additional new members, who were later revealed to be Elkie Chong from Hong Kong and Kwon Eunbin, a Cube Entertainment trainee participating in <a href="/wiki/Mnet_(TV_channel)" title="Mnet (TV channel)">Mnet</a>'s girl group survival show <i><a href="/wiki/Produce_101" title="Produce 101">Produce 101</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to Cube Entertainment, Eunbin originally joined CLC in 2015 but was converted back to trainee status following delays in the group's new album. Due to <i>Produce 101</i> contractual restrictions, she will not promote the album's single "High Heels" on music shows or other broadcasts and will have her scenes in the song's music video removed. The agency plans for Eunbin to join "High Heels" promotions if she is eliminated from the show or to postpone her CLC activities until after <i>Produce 101</i> promotions if she is a winner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EUNBEAN_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-EUNBEAN-28">[28]</a></sup> On February 29, CLC released a short version of the "High Heels" music video that included Elkie but omitted Eunbin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The full version of the music video that includes Eunbin's parts was released on March 21.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>

<p>CLC made their Japanese debut on April 13 with the release of their first Japanese EP, "High Heels". The album includes the Japanese version of "Pepe", "First Love", "Like", "High Heels" and a cover of <a href="/wiki/Kylie_Minogue" title="Kylie Minogue">Kylie Minogue</a>'s <a href="/wiki/I_Should_Be_So_Lucky" title="I Should Be So Lucky">I Should Be So Lucky</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 12, CLC launched their official <a href="/wiki/Naver" title="Naver">Naver</a> V App channel, followed by a broadcast of Eunbin's first live appearance with the group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> The group released their fourth mini-album "Nu.Clear" on May 30, with the title track "No Oh Oh" written by <a href="/wiki/Shinsadong_Tiger" title="Shinsadong Tiger">Shinsadong Tiger</a>. "Nu.Clear" represents "New" and "Crystal Clear", a name that signifies the new change for CLC in music. It was also their first come back as an official 7-member group, and they started album promotions in June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 27, CLC released their second Japanese EP, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chamisma_(EP)" title="Chamisma (EP)">Chamisma</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> The EP peaked at number 9 on the <i>Oricon Daily Albums Chart</i>, making it their first release to enter the Top 10 of the <i>Oricon</i> chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2017.E2.80.93present:_Crystyle">2017–present: <i>Crystyle</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CLC_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2017–present: Crystyle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On December 27, 2016, Cube Entertainment announced that CLC would be releasing a new album in January, with a revamped image, different concept from the group's existing image. It was also stated that CLC's new album aims to show a more charismatic, hip-hop and chic image.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> On January 4, 2017, CLC released an album art for their 5th mini album titled <i>Crystyle</i> which was released on January 17, 2017, with the title song Hobgoblin. In the making of this album, they were assisted by their senior labelmate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HyunA" title="HyunA">HyunA</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
