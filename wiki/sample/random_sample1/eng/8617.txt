<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Administrative_divisions_of_Singapore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Historically, these subdivisions have been based on postal districts, especially during the colonial era. When local elections necessitated the setting up of electoral districts, however, it began to supplement postal districts as an alternative form of local governance, since each electoral district is headed by a member of parliament who represents and speaks for the respective electorates.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_Electoral_Divisions">Administrative and Electoral Divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Administrative_divisions_of_Singapore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and Electoral Divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Community_Development_Council_Districts">Community Development Council Districts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Administrative_divisions_of_Singapore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Community Development Council Districts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Community_Development_Council" title="Community Development Council">Community Development Council</a></div>
<p>Established in 1997 by the PA Act, there were 9 districts formerly, governed by 9 different <a href="/wiki/Community_Development_Council" title="Community Development Council">Community Development Councils (CDCs)</a>. In 2001, the 9 districts and CDCs were then reformed into 5, namely the <a href="/wiki/North_East_Community_Development_Council" title="North East Community Development Council">North East CDC</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_West_Community_Development_Council" title="North West Community Development Council">North West CDC</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_East_Community_Development_Council" title="South East Community Development Council">South East CDC</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_West_Community_Development_Council" title="South West Community Development Council">South West CDC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_Singapore_Community_Development_Council" title="Central Singapore Community Development Council">Central Singapore CDC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PAact1997_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-PAact1997-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Each district is then further divided into <a href="/wiki/Constituencies_of_Singapore" title="Constituencies of Singapore">electoral constituencies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Town_council#Singapore" title="Town council">town councils</a>.</p>
<p>The council boundaries follow that of the existing political divisions, with each handling between four and six <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Group_representation_constituency" title="Group representation constituency">GRCs</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Single_member_constituency" title="Single member constituency">SMCs</a> and roughly dividing the country's population into equal parts. Each CDC is managed by a Council, which in turn is headed by a <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">mayor</a> and has between 12 and 80 members. The members are appointed by the Chairman or Deputy Chairman of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Association" title="People's Association">People's Association</a>.</p>
<p>The role of the CDCs is to initiate, plan and manage community programmes to promote community bonding and social cohesion within local communities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The electoral boundaries of Singapore are relatively fluid, and are reviewed prior to each general election. The districts are composed of the constituencies and electoral districts (the latter as of the 2015 General Elections).</p>
<p>There are currently five CDCs, namely the</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Central_Singapore_Community_Development_Council" title="Central Singapore Community Development Council">Central Singapore Community Development Council</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/North_East_Community_Development_Council" title="North East Community Development Council">North East Community Development Council</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/North_West_Community_Development_Council" title="North West Community Development Council">North West Community Development Council</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/South_East_Community_Development_Council" title="South East Community Development Council">South East Community Development Council</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/South_West_Community_Development_Council" title="South West Community Development Council">South West Community Development Council</a></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Town_councils">Town councils</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Administrative_divisions_of_Singapore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Town councils">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first town councils were set up in September 1986 by the Town Councils Act, with the main purpose of <a href="/wiki/Property_manager" title="Property manager">estate management</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Prior to the introduction of town councils, housing estates were managed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Housing_Development_Board" title="Housing Development Board">Housing Development Board</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MyKindOfTown_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-MyKindOfTown-5">[5]</a></sup> As the estates were centrally managed, the standardised rules that the board had set for all housing estates made HDB towns monotonous in appearance and problems faced by residents in the different estates were not addressed fast enough.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MyKindOfTown_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-MyKindOfTown-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Town councils boundaries are drawn based on electoral district boundaries. A town council area can consist of a <a href="/wiki/Group_Representation_Constituency" title="Group Representation Constituency">Group Representation Constituency</a> (GRC), a Single Member Constituency (SMC), or a collection of neighbouring GRCs and SMCs controlled by the same political party. The Members of <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Singapore" title="Parliament of Singapore">Parliament</a> head the town councils of their constituencies. Town councils boundaries do not correspond to <a href="/wiki/New_towns_of_Singapore" title="New towns of Singapore">new town</a> boundaries; different parts of the same HDB town may be managed by different town councils.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Constituencies">Constituencies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Administrative_divisions_of_Singapore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Constituencies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Town councils are then further subdivided into different constituencies, which are classified as either Single Member Constituencies (SMCs) or Group Representation Constituencies (GRCs). The boundaries of the electoral constituencies are decided by the Elections Department, which is under the control of the Prime Minister's Office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_administrative_subdivisions">Other administrative subdivisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Administrative_divisions_of_Singapore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other administrative subdivisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="URA_Master_Plan_boundaries">URA Master Plan boundaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Administrative_divisions_of_Singapore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: URA Master Plan boundaries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Regions">Regions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Administrative_divisions_of_Singapore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Regions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_Singapore" title="Regions of Singapore">Regions of Singapore</a></div>
<p>The regions are groupings of the planning areas.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Planning_Areas">Planning Areas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Administrative_divisions_of_Singapore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Planning Areas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Planning_Areas_of_Singapore" title="Planning Areas of Singapore">Planning Areas of Singapore</a></div>
<p>Regions are further subdivided into planning areas. In the 1990s, the <a href="/wiki/Urban_Redevelopment_Authority" title="Urban Redevelopment Authority">Urban Redevelopment Authority</a> carved up the country into 55 of these planning areas. Their boundaries became increasingly accepted as an alternative method of subdividing the country, made all the more popular as the boundaries do not change compared to the more fluid nature of electoral boundaries. The Singapore <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Statistics_Singapore" title="Statistics Singapore">Department of Statistics</a> adopted these boundaries for the latest 2000 nationwide population census, and the <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Police_Force" title="Singapore Police Force">Singapore Police Force</a> uses them as an approximate guide when demarcating boundaries for its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neighbourhood_Police_Centre" title="Neighbourhood Police Centre">Neighbourhood Police Centres</a>, as opposed to the former Neighbourhood Police Post system which was also based on electoral divisions.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Survey_Districts">Survey Districts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Administrative_divisions_of_Singapore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Survey Districts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Singapore is divided into 64 survey districts, of which 34 are <a href="/wiki/Mukim" title="Mukim">mukims</a> (originally, rural districts) and 30 are town subdivisions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Postal_Districts">Postal Districts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Administrative_divisions_of_Singapore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Postal Districts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Postal districts were numbered from 01 to 83 under the new system implemented on 1 September 1995. Census data and most forms of internal boundaries had been based on postal districts until the introduction of new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urban_planning_areas_in_Singapore" title="Urban planning areas in Singapore">planning boundaries</a> in the 1990s.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Whether census data used postal districts is questionable (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
