<p><i><b>Singles</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/2003_in_film" title="2003 in film">2003</a> <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Korea" title="Cinema of Korea">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Romantic_comedy_film" title="Romantic comedy film">romantic comedy film</a> starring <a href="/wiki/Jang_Jin-young" title="Jang Jin-young">Jang Jin-young</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uhm_Jung-hwa" title="Uhm Jung-hwa">Uhm Jung-hwa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Beom-soo" title="Lee Beom-soo">Lee Beom-soo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kim_Joo-hyuk" title="Kim Joo-hyuk">Kim Joo-hyuk</a>. It is based on the novel <i>Christmas at Twenty-nine</i> by <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a> writer Kamato Toshio. The film was one of the highest grossing Korean films of all time earning 2,203,164 admissions nationwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Jang won Best Actress at the 2003 <a href="/wiki/Blue_Dragon_Film_Awards" title="Blue Dragon Film Awards">Blue Dragon Film Awards</a> for her performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Singles_(2003_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Na-nan, soon to turn 30, is at a crossroads in her life. She has been dumped by her boyfriend, then gets demoted at work from designer to restaurant manager. For support she relies on her best friend, promiscuous party girl Dong-mi, who shares an apartment with the shy and studious Jeong-joon. Eventually Na-nan attracts the attention of Su-heon, and the two start dating. But when she learns that Su-heon is being transferred to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, she must choose between making a go of her career as a restaurateur, or starting a new life overseas.</p>
