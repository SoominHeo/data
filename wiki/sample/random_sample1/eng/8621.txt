<p><b>Singida</b> is one of the regions of <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a>. The regional capital is the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Singida_(town)" title="Singida (town)">Singida</a>. The region is bordered to the North by <a href="/wiki/Shinyanga_Region" title="Shinyanga Region">Shinyanga Region</a>, <a href="/wiki/Simiyu_Region" title="Simiyu Region">Simiyu Region</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arusha_Region" title="Arusha Region">Arusha Region</a>, to the Northeast by <a href="/wiki/Manyara_Region" title="Manyara Region">Manyara Region</a>, to the East by <a href="/wiki/Dodoma_Region" title="Dodoma Region">Dodoma Region</a>, to the Southeast by <a href="/wiki/Iringa_Region" title="Iringa Region">Iringa Region</a>, to the Southwest by <a href="/wiki/Mbeya_Region" title="Mbeya Region">Mbeya Region</a> and to the West by <a href="/wiki/Tabora_Region" title="Tabora Region">Tabora Region</a>. It is one of the poorest <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_Tanzania" title="Regions of Tanzania">regions in Tanzania</a>.</p>
<p>It is accessible from <a href="/wiki/Arusha" title="Arusha">Arusha</a> through <a href="/wiki/Babati" title="Babati">Babati</a> and <a href="/wiki/Katesh" title="Katesh">Katesh</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manyara_Region" title="Manyara Region">Manyara Region</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dar_es_salaam" title="Dar es salaam">Dar es salaam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iringa" title="Iringa">Iringa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mbeya" title="Mbeya">Mbeya</a> through <a href="/wiki/Dodoma" title="Dodoma">Dodoma</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tabora" title="Tabora">Tabora</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shinyanga" title="Shinyanga">Shinyanga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mwanza" title="Mwanza">Mwanza</a> through <a href="/wiki/Nzega" title="Nzega">Nzega</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tabora_Region" title="Tabora Region">Tabora Region</a>. Although not all the roads are passable all the year round, they expected to be so in the near future when the connections to <a href="/wiki/Arusha" title="Arusha">Arusha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dodoma" title="Dodoma">Dodoma</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mbeya" title="Mbeya">Mbeya</a> will be tarmac. Otherwise the connection to <a href="/wiki/Shinyanga" title="Shinyanga">Shinyanga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mwanza" title="Mwanza">Mwanza</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tabora" title="Tabora">Tabora</a> via <a href="/wiki/Nzega" title="Nzega">Nzega</a> is passable all the year round.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographical_Location">Geographical Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Singida_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geographical Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Singida region is located below the equator between latitudes 3052’ and 7034’. Longitudinally the region is situated between 33027’ and 350 26’ east of <a href="/wiki/Greenwich" title="Greenwich">Greenwich</a>. To the north, it shares borders with <a href="/wiki/Shinyanga_Region" title="Shinyanga Region">Shinyanga Region</a>; <a href="/wiki/Arusha" title="Arusha">Arusha</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manyara" title="Manyara">Manyara</a> and on the east borders <a href="/wiki/Dodoma" title="Dodoma">Dodoma</a>. To the south it shares borders with <a href="/wiki/Iringa" title="Iringa">Iringa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mbeya" title="Mbeya">Mbeya</a> while on the west there is <a href="/wiki/Tabora_Region" title="Tabora Region">Tabora Region</a>.</p>
<p>Singida region has a total surface area of 49,438 km<sup>2</sup>, out of which 95.5 km<sup>2</sup> or 0.19 percent are covered by water bodies of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Eyasi" title="Lake Eyasi">Lake Eyasi</a>, Kitangiri, Singidani, Kindai and Balengida. The remaining 49,342.5 km<sup>2</sup> is land area. Singida region is deemed to be neither small nor big. It is the 13th in size and occupies about 5.6 percent of mainland Tanzania's total area of 881,289 km<sup>2</sup>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Singida_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In regard to climate there are two key features which are temperature and rainfall. The region forms part of the semi-arid central zone of Tanzania, which experiences low rainfall and short rainy seasons which are often erratic, with fairly widespread drought in one year out of four. Total rainfall ranges from 500 mm to 800 mm per annum, with high geographical, seasonal and annual variation. There are two rather well defined seasons, the short rainy season during the months of December to March or sometimes goes to April and the long dry season from April to November.</p>
<p>The wetter areas in Singida region are along the escarpment near Kiomboi in Iramba district and in the south-west of Manyoni district near Rungwa, where the long-term mean annual rainfall exceeds 800 mm. The mean annual rainfall is in the range of 600 mm to 800 mm over large areas of Iramba and Singida districts. On the eastern side of Manyoni district near the Bahi Swamp and the Rift Valley depression of Mgori and Shelui divisions lies the drier area in the region where the mean annual rainfall is less than 550 mm. The regional mean annual average rainfall is 700 mm.</p>
<p>The temperatures in the region vary according to altitude but generally range from about 15 °C in July to 30 °C during the month of October. Moreover, temperature differences are observed between day and night and may be very high, with hot afternoons going up to 35 °C and chilly nights going down to 10 °C.</p>
<p>Winds follow a monsoonal pattern being north-easterly during the months of November to March and south-easterly for the rest of the year (dry season). In May to October, the winds are usually dry and contribute to the semi-aridity of the region. The fact that maximum wind velocities coincide with the period of greatest water deficiency underlines the climatic impact of these winds on moisture losses and hence desertification.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Singida_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the 2012 Tanzania National Census, the population of Singida Region was 1,370,637.</p>
<p>According to the 2002 Tanzania National Census, the population of Singida Region was 1,090,758.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Town">Town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Singida_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Singida_(town)" title="Singida (town)">Singida town</a> (i.e. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singida_Urban" title="Singida Urban">Singida municipal district</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Singida_Rural" title="Singida Rural">Singida Rural District</a>, the main tribe is the Nyaturu. The town is also home to immigrants from different parts of <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a>. Iramba district belongs to Nyiramba tribe and Manyoni district belongs to Gogo and few of Nyaturu tribes.</p>
<p>The Photo below featured one of the Chief Saidi Gwau's wife Bibi Nyamwagele of Samumba. Gwau was chief of Singida South around the 1940s, after he died his brother Chief Mange Gwau took over the crown until after Tanzania independence, when chief leadership was abolished. The Gwau family is well known in Singida for the Nyaturu tribe development. Bibi Nyamwagele died in 2004.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Singida_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Singida has a railway station on a branch off the <a href="/wiki/Central_Line_(Tanzania)" title="Central Line (Tanzania)">Central Railway</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tanzanian_Railways" title="Tanzanian Railways">Tanzanian Railways</a>. It is a branch terminal. The railway from Singida joins the Central line at <a href="/wiki/Manyoni" title="Manyoni">Manyoni</a>.</p>
<p>As of year 2012 a new Asphalt Road has been built(completed) between Singida and Dodoma. There is also an asphalt road built between Singida and Mwanza.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
