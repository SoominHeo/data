<p><b>Sansha</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Prefecture-level_city" title="Prefecture-level city">prefecture-level city</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hainan" title="Hainan">Hainan</a> province of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>. It administers (actually or nominally) several island groups and undersea atolls in the <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a> including the <a href="/wiki/Spratly_Islands" title="Spratly Islands">Spratly Islands</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Paracel_Islands" title="Paracel Islands">Paracel Islands</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Macclesfield_Bank" title="Macclesfield Bank">Macclesfield Bank</a>, and a number of other ungrouped maritime features.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SanshaRectified_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-SanshaRectified-2">[2]</a></sup> The entire territory is disputed,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and the PRC's <i><a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">de facto</a></i> control over the area varies.</p>
<p>Sansha has equal administrative status to a <a href="/wiki/Prefecture-level_city" title="Prefecture-level city">prefecture-level city</a>, and was created on 24 July 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xinhuaen0_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-xinhuaen0-6">[6]</a></sup> The establishment of Sansha is simply an upgrade of the administrative status of these island groups from the previous county-level Administrative Office,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SanshaRectified_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-SanshaRectified-2">[2]</a></sup> and construction of a city is not actually underway. However, a <a href="/wiki/Garrison" title="Garrison">garrison</a> may be established.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-garrison_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-garrison-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hawkishreuters_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-hawkishreuters-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Foreign reaction to the declaration was not positive. The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_State" title="United States Department of State">United States Department of State</a> called the change in the administrative status of the territory "<a href="/wiki/Unilateralism" title="Unilateralism">unilateral</a>", and the move has received criticism from nations engaged in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_China_Sea_dispute" title="South China Sea dispute">South China Sea dispute</a>, particularly the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The centre of government for Sansha is located on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Woody_Island,_South_China_Sea" title="Woody Island, South China Sea">Woody Island</a> (Yongxing Dao) in the Paracels,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-icgpp5_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-icgpp5-10">[10]</a></sup> where about 1,000 Chinese (PRC) reside.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hawkishreuters_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-hawkishreuters-8">[8]</a></sup> With a land area of 210ha, Woody Island is the largest contiguous land area in the <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The total land area of Sansha, which includes the islands in the Paracel and Spratly groups, is less than 5 square miles (13 km<sup>2</sup>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Nationally, Sansha is the smallest prefecture-level city, by both population and land area, but the largest by total area,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chr_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-chr-13">[13]</a></sup> and is also the southernmost prefecture-level city of the PRC.</p>
<p>On 16 February 2016, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Department_of_Defense" title="U.S. Department of Defense">U.S. Department of Defense</a> announced that the Chinese had deployed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HQ%E2%80%919" title="HQ‑9">HQ‑9</a> surface‑to‑air missiles on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forsythe2016_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Forsythe2016-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sansha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Following being given approval by the <a href="/wiki/State_Council_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="State Council of the People's Republic of China">State Council</a> on 24 March 1953,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beijing_Review_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Beijing_Review-15">[15]</a></sup> the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guangdong_Province" title="Guangdong Province">Guangdong Province</a> Paracels, Spratlys, and Zhongsha Islands Authority" was established as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China#County_level" title="Administrative divisions of the People's Republic of China">county-level administrative division</a> on Woody Island (Yongxing Dao). During the Cultural Revolution, when <a href="/wiki/Revolutionary_committee_(China)" title="Revolutionary committee (China)">Revolutionary Committees</a> were being set up across the country, the Authority was upgraded to the "Guangdong Province Paracels, Spratlys, and Zhongsha Islands Revolutionary Committee" on 4 March 1959.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beijing_Review_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Beijing_Review-15">[15]</a></sup> On 22 October 1981 the original Authority was restored.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Administration was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Hainan" title="Hainan">Hainan</a> on National Day,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (October 2014)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> 1984, when the Hainan Administrative Region was established,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> followed by the establishment of Hainan Province on 13 April 1988.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> On 19 September 1988,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beijing_Review_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-Beijing_Review-15">[15]</a></sup> the Authority's name was officially changed to the "Hainan Province Paracels, Spratlys, and Zhongsha Islands Authority". On 25 December 2006, Woody Island census-town's first-ever Residents' and Fishermen's Congress was held.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Three representatives at the township and village levels were selected to represent the census-town's Neighborhood Committee of the North and South Villages. The Neighborhood Committee began work on Woody Island the following day with an office at the Border Guards of the Paracels' Police Station. These were the first ever actual subdivisions created within the county-level authority.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beijing_Review_15-3"><a href="#cite_note-Beijing_Review-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The prospect of the establishment of a "city" on Woody Island was first mooted on 19 November 2007 in a report by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mingpao" title="Mingpao">Mingpao</a>, a Hong Kong-based newspaper, through a telephone interview with a Mr. Zhang of the Propaganda Department of <a href="/wiki/Wenchang" title="Wenchang">Wenchang</a>, Hainan. This report claimed that a <a href="/wiki/County-level_city" title="County-level city">county-level city</a> was to be established by the <a href="/wiki/State_Council_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="State Council of the People's Republic of China">PRC State Council</a> in November 2007 to administer three disputed <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelagos</a> in the South China Sea:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stratfor_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stratfor-16">[16]</a></sup> the Paracel Islands, Macclesfield Bank (Zhongsha Islands) and the Spratly Islands. This was to replace the county-level "Paracels, Spratlys, and Zhongsha Islands Authority". The city of Wenchang would provide<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vagueness" title="Wikipedia:Vagueness"><span title="This information is too vague. (October 2014)">vague</span></a></i>]</sup> supplies and logistics to the to-be-established city.</p>
<p>On 23 July 2012 the PRC <a href="/wiki/Central_Military_Commission" title="Central Military Commission">Central Military Commission</a> announced it had authorized the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army" title="People's Liberation Army">People's Liberation Army</a> Guangzhou Military Command to form a "garrison command" in Sansha City. The troops would be responsible for managing the city's national defence mobilisation, military reserve, and carrying out military operations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 24 July 2012, the PRC officially established the city of Sansha in Yongxing Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xinhuaen0_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-xinhuaen0-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sansha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy_and_protests">Controversy and protests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sansha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Controversy and protests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Because Sansha's jurisdiction comprises island groups that several nations besides the PRC claim as their own, the "city" is considered by some as controversial.</p>
<p>In 2007, the Foreign Ministry of <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a> protested against the city's proposed establishment, which had been recently <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> approved by the PRC's State Council, as Vietnam claims two of the three island groups that comprise Sansha.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> This was later followed by student demonstrations at the Chinese embassy in <a href="/wiki/Hanoi" title="Hanoi">Hanoi</a>, and at a consulate in <a href="/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh_City" title="Ho Chi Minh City">Ho Chi Minh City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Days prior to the official establishment of the city in late July 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xinhuaen0_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-xinhuaen0-6">[6]</a></sup> Vietnam again stated its opposition and was joined in its protests by the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The United States Department of State also weighed in on the issue stating, "we remain concerned should there be any unilateral moves of this kind that would seem to prejudge an issue."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The chair of the US Senate Foreign Relations East Asian and Pacific Affairs Subcommittee, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Webb" title="Jim Webb">Jim Webb</a>, also made a statement regarding Sansha and questioned whether the city's creation was a violation of international law.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-outofnothing_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-outofnothing-28">[28]</a></sup> While Senator Webb stated that the establishment of Sansha was China "creating a governmental system out of nothing",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-outofnothing_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-outofnothing-28">[28]</a></sup> the Chinese government has included the island groups that comprise Sansha in its administrative structure since 1959.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Before these island groups were under the nominal administration of Sansha, they were nominally administered by an administrative office under the provincial government of <a href="/wiki/Hainan" title="Hainan">Hainan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-icgpp5_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-icgpp5-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Initiatives">Initiatives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sansha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Initiatives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id=".22Greening_the_islands.22">"Greening the islands"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sansha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title='Edit section: "Greening the islands"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The second stage of a greening project for Sansha was announced on 30 June 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> It states: "According to the investment plan, 18 million yuan (about 2.92 million U.S. dollars) will be used to build desalination systems and grow trees on Xishazhou Island (<a href="/wiki/West_Sand" title="West Sand">West Sand</a> - NW Crescent Group) in the hope of turning the island into a new oasis." The article makes references to the greening of:</p>
<ul>
<li>Ganquan Island (<a href="/wiki/Robert_Island_(Paracel_Islands)" title="Robert Island (Paracel Islands)">Robert Island</a>) - W Crescent Group between Antelope Reef and Pattle Island, where an environmental protection station was set up in February 2014</li>
<li>Jinqin (Drummond Island) - E Crescent Group</li>
<li>Lingyangjiao (Antelope Reef) - W Crescent Group between <a href="/wiki/Money_Island,_Paracel_Islands" title="Money Island, Paracel Islands">Money</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Island_(Paracel_Islands)" title="Robert Island (Paracel Islands)">Robert</a> Islands</li>
<li>Xishazhou Island (<a href="/wiki/West_Sand" title="West Sand">West Sand</a>) - NW Crescent Group</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Yagong_Island" title="Yagong Island">Yagong Island</a> - NE Crescent Group, between Tree Island and Observation Bank</li>
<li>Yinyu (Observation Bank / Silver Islet) - NE Crescent Group</li>
<li>Yongxing Island (<a href="/wiki/Woody_Island_(South_China_Sea)" title="Woody Island (South China Sea)">Woody Island</a>) - Centre of Amphitrite group</li>
<li>Zhaoshu (<a href="/wiki/Tree_Island,_South_China_Sea" title="Tree Island, South China Sea">Tree Island</a>) - N Crescent Group</li>
</ul>
<p>It also mentions substantial infrastructure upgrades to Woody Island, including waste-water treatment, garbage aggregation and treatment, desalination (1 Mega-litre/day) and upgrades to photovoltaic equipment.</p>
