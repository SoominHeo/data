<p><b>Sanshui District</b>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Postal_Map_romanization" title="Postal Map romanization">formerly</a> <a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Chinese" title="Romanization of Chinese">romanized</a> as <b>Samshui</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[a]</a></sup> is an <a href="/wiki/District_(China)" title="District (China)">urban district</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prefecture_(China)" title="Prefecture (China)">prefecture</a>-level <a href="/wiki/Prefecture-level_city" title="Prefecture-level city">city</a> <a href="/wiki/Foshan" title="Foshan">Foshan</a> in <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. It had about 386,000 inhabitants in 2002. It is known for the "<a href="/wiki/Samsui_women" title="Samsui women">Samsui women</a>", emigrants who labor in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, and for a large fireworks explosion in 2008.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanshui_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Samshui was formerly a town which served as the <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">seat</a> of an eponymous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/County_(China)" title="County (China)">county</a>. It was elevated to city status before becoming an urban district of Foshan.</p>
<p>In February 2008, twenty fireworks warehouses exploded in Sanshui. In total over 15,000 cartons of fireworks were set off over a period of 24 hours. The loss of inventory was extensive enough that U.S. markets faced real shortages of supply.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanshui_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Oil, coal, and slate are found in the surrounding area. However, Sanshui is also an important producer of food for Guangdong Province, especially fruit. The mainstay of the former city's economy was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jianlibao_Company" title="Jianlibao Company">Jianlibao Company</a>, which produced a soft drink made from oranges and honey. During the 1990s, Jianlibao was a popular drink throughout China and it even made it into the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> market. However, embezzlement by the company's senior management has reduced its success. Despite Sanshui's proximity to <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou" title="Guangzhou">Guangzhou</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Pearl_River_Delta" title="Pearl River Delta">Pearl River Delta</a> cities, it remains a relatively poor place with many people only earning a few hundred yuan a month.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanshui_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sanshui is on the railway line from <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou" title="Guangzhou">Guangzhou</a> to <a href="/wiki/Maoming" title="Maoming">Maoming</a>. The district is served by <a href="/wiki/G15_Shenyang%E2%80%93Haikou_Expressway" title="G15 Shenyang–Haikou Expressway">G15 Shenyang–Haikou Expressway</a> (which provides access to <a href="/wiki/Kaiping" title="Kaiping">Kaiping</a> and the southern suburbs of Foshan), <a href="/wiki/G55_Erenhot%E2%80%93Guangzhou_Expressway" title="G55 Erenhot–Guangzhou Expressway">G55 Erenhot–Guangzhou Expressway</a> (which provides direct access to downtown Guangzhou), <a href="/wiki/China_National_Highway_321" title="China National Highway 321">China National Highway 321</a> (which provides access to <a href="/wiki/Zhaoqing" title="Zhaoqing">Zhaoqing</a>) and <a href="/wiki/China_National_Highway_324" title="China National Highway 324">China National Highway 324</a>.</p>
