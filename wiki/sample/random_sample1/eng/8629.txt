<p><b>Sanhe</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">三河</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Sānhé Shì</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/County-level_city" title="County-level city">county-level city</a> administered by <a href="/wiki/Langfang" title="Langfang">Langfang</a> prefecture in eastern <a href="/wiki/Hebei" title="Hebei">Hebei</a> province, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>. The name of the city literally means "Three Rivers". Sanhe has 10 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Town_(China)" title="Town (China)">towns</a> and 395 villages within those towns.</p>
<p>Sanhe city, <a href="/wiki/Dachang_Hui_Autonomous_County" title="Dachang Hui Autonomous County">Dachang Hui Autonomous County</a> and <a href="/wiki/Xianghe_County" title="Xianghe County">Xianghe County</a> form the Sanhe Exclave, an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exclave" title="Exclave">exclave</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hebei" title="Hebei">Hebei</a> province surrounded by the <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tianjin" title="Tianjin">Tianjin</a> municipalities.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanhe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In February, 2013 German television crew from <a href="/wiki/ARD_(broadcaster)" title="ARD (broadcaster)">ARD</a> was attacked by a group of men in vehicles and their van driven off the road after filming in Sanhe. The windshield of the van was broken with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baseball_bats" title="Baseball bats">baseball bats</a>. One of the vehicles involved in the attack was identified as belong to the local Communist party boss. The crew taken into custody and questioned for 16 hours then released. The men from Sanhe said they were offended because the crew did not ask for permission to film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT022813_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT022813-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
