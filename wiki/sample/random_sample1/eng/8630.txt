<p>The <b>Meliceae</b> are a tribe of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grass" title="Grass">grasses</a> near the base of the <a href="/wiki/Pooideae" title="Pooideae">Pooideae</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> They include two relatively large genera, <i><a href="/wiki/Melica" title="Melica">Melica</a></i> (based on accounts in multiple regional floras) with about 80-90 species and <i><a href="/wiki/Glyceria" title="Glyceria">Glyceria</a></i> with about 55 species.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Its other genera are <i><a href="/wiki/Lycochloa" title="Lycochloa">Lycochloa</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Pleuropogon" title="Pleuropogon">Pleuropogon</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Schizachne" title="Schizachne">Schizachne</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Triniochloa" title="Triniochloa">Triniochloa</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SorengPeterson2015_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-SorengPeterson2015-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distinguishing_characteristics">Distinguishing characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meliceae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distinguishing characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Members of the Meliceae have closed leaf sheaths; lemma veins that do not or only scarcely converge distally; and short, truncate, lodicules. They differ from <a href="/wiki/Bromus" title="Bromus">Bromeae</a>, another tribe with closed leaf sheaths, in their glabrous ovaries as well as their lemma venation and short short lodicules. They also differ from other members of the Pooideae in having chromosome base numbers of 9, 10, and 8.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_Ecology">Geography and Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meliceae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography and Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Meliceae are most abundant in temperate regions of Eurasia but are also well represented in temperate regions of North and South America but there are great differences between the genera. <i>Glyceria</i> and <i>Pleuropogon</i> grow in wet areas, often in standing water; <i>Melica</i> and <i>Schizachne</i> tend to grow in dry, well-drained sites.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_importance">Economic importance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meliceae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economic importance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Members of the Meliceae have little economic importance. A few species of both <i>Melica</i> and <i>Glyceria</i> are grown as ornamentals; more merit consideration but caution should be used. Some species of <i>Glyceria</i>, notably <i>G. declinata</i>, are invasive.</p>
