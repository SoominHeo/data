<p><b>Secernentea</b> are the main <a href="/wiki/Class_(biology)" title="Class (biology)">class</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nematodes" title="Nematodes">nematodes</a>, characterised by numerous <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Caudal_papilla&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Caudal papilla (page does not exist)">caudal papillae</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Excretory_system" title="Excretory system">excretory system</a> possessing lateral canals. Like all nematodes, they have no circulatory or respiratory system. These are also called as <b>phasmidians</b> as they have the phasmids which are the posterior glandulo sensory and excretory structure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tol2002_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tol2002-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Secernentea contain several significant <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parasitic" title="Parasitic">parasitic</a> <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> among the <a href="/wiki/Rhabditia" title="Rhabditia">Rhabditia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spiruria" title="Spiruria">Spiruria</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/Caenorhabditis_elegans" title="Caenorhabditis elegans">Caenorhabditis elegans</a></i>, the famous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laboratory_animal" title="Laboratory animal">laboratory animal</a>, is one of the many harmless members of the Rhabditia.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Systematics">Systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secernentea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_nematode_families" title="List of nematode families">List of nematode families</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Class_(biology)" title="Class (biology)">Subclasses</a> and <a href="/wiki/Order_(biology)" title="Order (biology)">orders</a> of Secernentea are:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tol2002_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tol2002-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Subclass <a href="/wiki/Rhabditia" title="Rhabditia">Rhabditia</a> (paraphyletic?)
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rhabditida" title="Rhabditida">Rhabditida</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Strongylida" title="Strongylida">Strongylida</a></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Subclass <a href="/wiki/Spiruria" title="Spiruria">Spiruria</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ascaridida" title="Ascaridida">Ascaridida</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Camallanida" title="Camallanida">Camallanida</a> (sometimes included in Spirurida)</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Drilonematida&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Drilonematida (page does not exist)">Drilonematida</a> (sometimes included in Spirurida)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Oxyurida" title="Oxyurida">Oxyurida</a> (= <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rhabdiasida&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rhabdiasida (page does not exist)">Rhabdiasida</a>)</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rhigonematida&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rhigonematida (page does not exist)">Rhigonematida</a> (formerly in Tylenchia)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Spirurida" title="Spirurida">Spirurida</a></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Subclass <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diplogasteria" title="Diplogasteria">Diplogasteria</a> (may belong in Rhabditia)
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Diplogasterida" title="Diplogasterida">Diplogasterida</a></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Subclass <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tylenchia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tylenchia (page does not exist)">Tylenchia</a> (may belong in Rhabditia)
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Aphelenchida" title="Aphelenchida">Aphelenchida</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tylenchida" title="Tylenchida">Tylenchida</a></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Some families traditionally considered to be Rhabditida seem to be closer to the Tylenchida. If the Tylenchia are to be maintained as separate, they probably will be included therein.</p>
