<p>The <b>SsangYong Chairman</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Full-size_car" title="Full-size car">full-size</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luxury_vehicles" title="Luxury vehicles">luxury car</a> manufactured by the South Korean automaker <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SsangYong_Motor_Company" title="SsangYong Motor Company">SsangYong</a> since 1997. The original model was renamed to Chairman H in 2008, with a new model introduced in the same year and designated Chairman W. The Chairman H was discontinued in 2014.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SsangYong_Chairman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On release to South Korean market in 1997, the Chairman was a high-end executive sedan. Its manufacturer, SsangYong, was best known for SUVs and RVs and the Chairman has been its only sedan. The first generation Chairman was built on the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_W124" title="Mercedes-Benz W124">W124</a> <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_E-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz E-Class">Mercedes-Benz E-Class</a> platform,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> but styled to resemble the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_W140" title="Mercedes-Benz W140">W140</a> <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_S-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz S-Class">S-Class</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SsangYong_Motor_Company" title="SsangYong Motor Company">SsangYong</a> renamed the original pre-2008 Chairman model as Chairman H (which has the same design as the 2003–2008 model) to differentiate from the Chairman W. Chairman H kept the same exterior, interior, engine, transmission and features as the previous models. In 2003, the Chairman received a restyled front grille, front and rear headlights and improved interior assembly.</p>
<p>The 2005 Chairman, based on the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_W124" title="Mercedes-Benz W124">Mercedes-Benz W124</a> had the following features: heated and cooled cup holders, rear <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parking_sensors" title="Parking sensors">parking sensors</a>, rain-sensing wipers, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anti-lock_brakes" title="Anti-lock brakes">anti-lock brakes</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stability_control" title="Stability control">stability</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Traction_control" title="Traction control">traction control</a>.</p>
<p>Although sold globally, the Chairman H sold very poorly outside Korea, due to both poor reviews and a lack of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SsangYong_Motor_Company" title="SsangYong Motor Company">SsangYong</a> dealerships. This was in part rectified by labelling the car Daewoo in some countries, which also included the Daewoo corporate grille.</p>
<p>In 2011, the Chairman H was once again redesigned, still keeping its Mercedes-Benz design cues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of January 2015, official website of SsangYong Motor Company does not show Chairman H anymore. According to KAMA (Korea Automobile Manufacturers Association), SsangYong Motor Company sold 1,718 of Chairman H in Korea during 2014.</p>

<p>SsangYong developed their own all-new flagship Chairman W, New Chairman with V8 5-litre engine and Mercedes-Benz <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/7G-Tronic" title="7G-Tronic">7G-Tronic</a> automatic transmission. The Chairman W is derived from the <a href="/wiki/SsangYong_WZ" title="SsangYong WZ">SsangYong WZ</a> concept car. The 'W' stands for 'World Class.'</p>
<p>Chairman W is offered in six trims with two engines, a limo version, and an additional <a href="/wiki/Four-wheel_drive#4WD_versus_AWD" title="Four-wheel drive">AWD</a> 4-Tronic (Mercedes-Benz <a href="/wiki/4Matic" title="4Matic">4Matic</a>). XGi360, once used for New Chairman, is transferred to the entry model with a straight 6-cylinder engine, and the 5-liter V8 Mercedes-Benz <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_M113_engine" title="Mercedes-Benz M113 engine">M113</a> engine and the 7G-Tronic transmission (the largest V8 engine produced in South Korea<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>) are applied to two high-end models, VVIP and the long-wheel-base version. It has an <a href="/wiki/Active_suspension" title="Active suspension">electronically controlled</a> <a href="/wiki/Self-levelling_suspension" title="Self-levelling suspension">self-levelling</a> <a href="/wiki/Air_suspension" title="Air suspension">air suspension</a> system with a rebound coil that helps reduce noise and vibration, as well as an extended rear passenger space<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Joongang_Daily_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Joongang_Daily-3">[3]</a></sup> and the front and rear both have their own screen to control the car's entertainment system, similar to <a href="/wiki/Chrysler" title="Chrysler">Chrysler</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MyGig" title="MyGig">MyGig</a>.</p>
<p>The Chairman W is the first Korean luxury sedan to apply a four-wheel drive system, a feature common in high-end European sedans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Joongang_Daily_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Joongang_Daily-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The license-built Chinese version of the Chairman is called <b><a href="/wiki/Roewe" title="Roewe">Roewe</a> 850</b>.</p>
