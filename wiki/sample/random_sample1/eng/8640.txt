<p><i><b>Saw</b></i> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">horror</a> <a href="/wiki/Media_franchise" title="Media franchise">franchise</a> distributed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lions_Gate_Entertainment" title="Lions Gate Entertainment">Lions Gate Entertainment</a> and produced by <a href="/wiki/Twisted_Pictures" title="Twisted Pictures">Twisted Pictures</a> that consists of seven feature films and additional media. In 2003, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian" title="Australian">Australian</a> director <a href="/wiki/James_Wan" title="James Wan">James Wan</a> and screenwriter <a href="/wiki/Leigh_Whannell" title="Leigh Whannell">Leigh Whannell</a> created a <a href="/wiki/Saw_(2003_film)" title="Saw (2003 film)">short film</a> to help <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sales_pitch" title="Sales pitch">pitch</a> as a potential <a href="/wiki/Feature_film" title="Feature film">feature film</a>. This was successfully done in 2004 with the release of <a href="/wiki/Saw_(2004_film)" title="Saw (2004 film)">the first installment</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Film Festival</a>. It was released theatrically that October. The sequels were directed by <a href="/wiki/Darren_Lynn_Bousman" title="Darren Lynn Bousman">Darren Lynn Bousman</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Hackl" title="David Hackl">David Hackl</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Greutert" title="Kevin Greutert">Kevin Greutert</a>, and were written by Wan, Whannell, Bousman, <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Melton" title="Patrick Melton">Patrick Melton</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Dunstan" title="Marcus Dunstan">Marcus Dunstan</a>, and were released subsequently every October, on the Friday before <a href="/wiki/Halloween" title="Halloween">Halloween</a>, between 2004 and 2010. Both of the creators remained with the franchise as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Executive_producers" title="Executive producers">executive producers</a>. On July 22, 2010, producer <a href="/wiki/Mark_Burg" title="Mark Burg">Mark Burg</a> confirmed that the seventh film, <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_3D" title="Saw 3D">Saw 3D</a></i>, is the final installment of the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Series creators James Wan and Leigh Whannell are still open to continuing the series, however, if they can do something "different" with the material. The films collectively grossed over <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">$</a>873 million at the box office worldwide. Lionsgate reportedly expressed interest in continuing the franchise in 2012 with a reboot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lionsgate_Remaking_Saw_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lionsgate_Remaking_Saw-2">[2]</a></sup> However, in November 2013, it was reported that they were in active development of a sequel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloody-disgusting.com_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-bloody-disgusting.com-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The franchise revolves around the main antagonist, <a href="/wiki/Jigsaw_(Saw)" title="Jigsaw (Saw)">John Kramer</a>, also called the "Jigsaw Killer" or simply "Jigsaw". He was introduced briefly in <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_(2004_film)" title="Saw (2004 film)">Saw</a></i> and developed in more detail in <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_II" title="Saw II">Saw II</a></i>. Rather than killing his <a href="/wiki/List_of_Saw_characters" title="List of Saw characters">victims</a> outright, Jigsaw traps them in situations that he calls "tests" or "games" to test their will to live through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Physical_torture" title="Physical torture">physical</a> or <a href="/wiki/Psychological_torture" title="Psychological torture">psychological torture</a>. Despite the fact that John was murdered in <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_III" title="Saw III">Saw III</a></i>, the films continue to focus on the posthumous influence of the Jigsaw Killer and his apprentices by exploring his character via <a href="/wiki/Flashback_(narrative)" title="Flashback (narrative)">flashbacks</a>.</p>
<p>The film series as a whole has received mostly mixed to negative reviews by critics, but has been a financial success at the <a href="#Box_office">box office</a>. While the films are often compared to <i><a href="/wiki/Hostel_(2005_film)" title="Hostel (2005 film)">Hostel</a></i> and classified as <a href="/wiki/Splatter_film#Torture_porn" title="Splatter film">torture porn</a> by critics,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> the creators of <i>Saw</i> disagree with the term "torture porn".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AVClub_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-AVClub-7">[7]</a></sup> Writer Luke Y. Thompson of <i><a href="/wiki/OC_Weekly" title="OC Weekly">OC Weekly</a></i> argued that, unlike <i>Hostel</i>, the <i>Saw</i> films actually have less torture than most in the sense of <a href="/wiki/Sadomasochism" title="Sadomasochism">sadism or masochism</a>, as Jigsaw believes that those who survive his methods will be stronger people for it. He called him a kind of a "(deranged) <a href="/wiki/Philanthropy" title="Philanthropy">philanthropist</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saw_(franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Flashbacks from <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_IV" title="Saw IV">Saw IV</a></i> reveal the roots of the series, presenting John Kramer as a successful civil engineer and devoted husband to his wife <a href="/wiki/List_of_Saw_characters#Jill_Tuck" title="List of Saw characters">Jill Tuck</a>, who opened a rehab clinic for drug addicts. Jill lost her unborn baby, Gideon, due to the unwitting actions of a drug addict named <a href="/wiki/List_of_Saw_characters#Cecil_Adams" title="List of Saw characters">Cecil</a>, who fled the scene. <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_VI" title="Saw VI">Saw VI</a></i> later showed that another drug addict, <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Young" title="Amanda Young">Amanda Young</a>, also had an unintentional role in the death of Gideon. John grieved over the loss of his child, and distanced himself from his friends and his wife.</p>
<p>John and Jill eventually drifted apart and divorced. After this turn of events, John found himself trapped by his own complacency, until he was diagnosed with inoperable <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">cancer</a>. Extremely bitter over his squandered life and the loss of his unborn son, John began observing the lives of others and became even more depressed as he saw those around him squandering the gift of life that he had just been denied. John went to a man named William Easton to get money for a cancer treatment, but was denied. Flashbacks from <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_II" title="Saw II">Saw II</a></i> show that, after surviving a <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">suicide</a> attempt where he drove his car off a cliff, John was "reborn", and nurtured the idea that the only way for someone to change is for them to change themselves. Then, in <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_IV" title="Saw IV">Saw IV</a></i> flashbacks, he designed the first trap and test for Cecil and decided to use the rest of his existence to design more of these "tests" or "games" as a form of "instant rehabilitation" that would change the world, "one person at a time". John was soon given the name "The Jigsaw Killer" (or "Jigsaw"), because he removed a puzzle-piece-shaped chunk of flesh from those who did not escape his traps. John stated that this name was given to him by the media, and that the cut piece of flesh was meant to represent that these victims were each missing something — what he called the "survival instinct".</p>
<p>Few of Jigsaw's victims are able to survive his brutal mechanical traps, which are often <a href="/wiki/Irony" title="Irony">ironically</a> <a href="/wiki/Symbol" title="Symbol">symbolic</a> representations of the problems in the victim's life and require them to undergo severe physical and psychological <a href="/wiki/Torture" title="Torture">torture</a> to escape.</p>
<p>In <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_V" title="Saw V">Saw V</a></i>, police lieutenant <a href="/wiki/Mark_Hoffman" title="Mark Hoffman">Mark Hoffman</a>'s ties with John are revealed in a series of flashbacks during the film. Hoffman's sister is murdered by her boyfriend, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Saw_characters#Seth_Baxter" title="List of Saw characters">Seth Baxter</a>. Seth is arrested; however, a technicality allowed him to be released, and Hoffman, feeling Seth had not served the full capacity of his sentence, kills him in an inescapable trap designed to look like one of Jigsaw's, laying the blame on him. Jigsaw then kidnaps Hoffman and blackmails him into becoming his apprentice in his "rehabilitation" methods, though eventually Hoffman would become a willing apprentice, helping set up John's tests from almost the beginning, starting with <a href="/wiki/List_of_Saw_characters#Paul_Leahy" title="List of Saw characters">Paul</a>'s trap.</p>
<p>The first surviving victim, <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Young" title="Amanda Young">Amanda Young</a>, views Jigsaw as a hero who ultimately changed her life for the better. Amanda, upon Jigsaw's request, agrees to become his <a href="/wiki/Mentorship" title="Mentorship">protégée</a>. After Amanda survives, John shows Jill her rehabilitation, and Jill then becomes knowledgeable of John's traps and becomes somewhat of an accomplice as well.</p>
<p>In <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_(2004_film)" title="Saw (2004 film)">Saw</a></i>, Jigsaw has chained the man who diagnosed his cancer, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Saw_characters#Lawrence_Gordon" title="List of Saw characters">Dr. Lawrence Gordon</a>, in a dilapidated industrial washroom with <a href="/wiki/List_of_Saw_characters#Adam_Faulkner-Stanheight" title="List of Saw characters">Adam Stanheight</a>, a photographer who has been tailing the doctor due to a former police detective's suspicions that Gordon is Jigsaw. Lawrence has instructions to kill Adam by six o'clock, or else his wife and daughter will be killed. Flashbacks show detectives <a href="/wiki/David_Tapp" title="David Tapp">David Tapp</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Saw_characters#Steven_Sing" title="List of Saw characters">Steven Sing</a>, who suspect Lawrence of being Jigsaw, following a trail of clues from other Jigsaw traps. Sing's death from a shotgun trap after saving a victim named Jeff causes Tapp to obsess over catching Jigsaw. Later on, he chases <a href="/wiki/List_of_Saw_characters#Zep_Hindle" title="List of Saw characters">Zep Hindle</a>, who monitors Adam and Lawrence's tests, and gets shot in the chest. Eventually, Lawrence saws his own foot off in order to escape, leaving Adam in the bathroom while Lawrence goes to try to save his family and get help for Adam. It is later seen in <i>Saw 3D</i> that Dr. Gordon found a steaming hot pipe and cauterized his wound, stopping the bleeding and ultimately surviving the trap. John catches up with Gordon and makes him his next apprentice, a fact which he hides from his other accomplices. Flashbacks from <i>Saw III</i> show that Amanda kidnapped Adam and later returned to suffocate him as an act of "mercy killing".</p>

<p><i><a href="/wiki/Saw_II" title="Saw II">Saw II</a></i> begins with the police tracking a severely weakened Jigsaw to his lair. However, another test is in place, as he and Amanda have kidnapped the son of Detective <a href="/wiki/Eric_Matthews_(Saw)" title="Eric Matthews (Saw)">Eric Matthews</a> and trapped him and a group of seven convicts, previously framed by Matthews, in a house that is slowly being filled with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sarin_gas" title="Sarin gas">sarin gas</a>, with Amanda Young among them. He will trade <a href="/wiki/List_of_Saw_characters#Daniel_Matthews" title="List of Saw characters">Daniel Matthews</a>' life for Detective Matthews' time, conversing with him until the game is concluded. Matthews loses his patience and assaults Jigsaw, forcing him to take him to the house, only to discover that the video feed from inside the house had been pre-recorded, the events actually taking place much earlier; Matthews' son was locked in a safe in Jigsaw's warehouse, being kept alive with an oxygen tank. Matthews is knocked unconscious by a masked figure and wakes up imprisoned in the bathroom from <i>Saw</i>, which is part of the foundation of the house. Amanda reveals herself to Eric as Jigsaw's protégée before leaving him to die. In a flashback from <i>Saw III</i>, Matthews manages to escape the bathroom by breaking his foot. He confronts and beats Amanda, demanding to know where his son is. Amanda fights him off and leaves him for dead. A flashback from <i>Saw IV</i> shows Hoffman later dragging Eric to a prison cell, keeping him alive for a future game.</p>
<p>The events of <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_III" title="Saw III">Saw III</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_IV" title="Saw IV">Saw IV</a></i> occur concurrently. <i>Saw III</i> begins with Jigsaw, weakened and near death, confined to a makeshift hospital bed. Amanda has taken over his work, designing traps of her own; however, these traps are inescapable, as Amanda is convinced that Jigsaw's traps have no effect and that people don't change. A <a href="/wiki/List_of_Saw_characters#Lynn_Denlon" title="List of Saw characters">kidnapped doctor</a> is forced to keep Jigsaw alive while another test is performed on <a href="/wiki/List_of_Saw_characters#Jeff_Denlon" title="List of Saw characters">Jeff</a>, a man obsessed with vengeance against the drunk driver who killed his son. Jigsaw, unwilling to allow a murderer to continue his legacy, designs a test for Amanda as well; she ultimately fails, and it results in the deaths of both Jigsaw and Amanda. <i>Saw IV</i>, meanwhile, revolves around tests meant for <a href="/wiki/List_of_Saw_characters#Daniel_Rigg" title="List of Saw characters">Officer Daniel Rigg</a>, which are overseen by Hoffman. Rigg fails his test, resulting in the death of Eric Matthews. Rigg is left to bleed to death by Hoffman, who later discovers the bodies of Jigsaw and Amanda. When an <a href="/wiki/Autopsy" title="Autopsy">autopsy</a> is performed on Jigsaw, a <a href="/wiki/Compact_Cassette" title="Compact Cassette">cassette tape</a> coated in wax is found in his <a href="/wiki/Stomach" title="Stomach">stomach</a>; the tape informs Hoffman that he is wrong to think that it is all over just because Jigsaw is dead, and he should not expect to go untested.</p>
<p>The events of <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_V" title="Saw V">Saw V</a></i> show one of Hoffman's first solo tests, five people connected together by different roles in a disastrous fire that killed several others are put into four interconnected tests of teamwork, killing off one person in each trap. The two remaining test subjects realize at the final trap that each previous trap was meant to be completed by each of the five people doing a small part, rather than killing one person per trap. With this knowledge, the two work together and barely manage to escape. They are found alive by Special Agent Erikson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Meanwhile, Hoffman has set up FBI Agent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peter_Strahm" title="Peter Strahm">Peter Strahm</a> to appear to be Jigsaw's accomplice, while Strahm pursues Hoffman and is eventually killed due to his inability to follow Hoffman's rules, leaving Hoffman free to continue Jigsaw's "work".</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Saw_VI" title="Saw VI">Saw VI</a></i> begins with Hoffman setting up a game as per John's instructions left in a box for Jill during <i>Saw V</i>. This game centers on insurance executive named <a href="/wiki/List_of_Saw_characters#William_Easton" title="List of Saw characters">William Easton</a> who oversees a team responsible for rejecting two-thirds of all insurance claims. As William progresses through four tests, he saves as many people as he can and learns the error of his choice to reject so many policies, which inherently "kill" the rejected. His last test is revealed to be a test of forgiveness by the family of <a href="/wiki/List_of_Saw_characters#Harold_Abbott" title="List of Saw characters">Harold Abbott</a>, a man to whom William rejected a policy in the past, whose son ultimately chooses to kill William using <a href="/wiki/Hydrofluoric_acid" title="Hydrofluoric acid">hydrofluoric acid</a>. Meanwhile, Agent Erickson and the previously thought to be dead agent <a href="/wiki/List_of_Saw_characters#Lindsey" title="List of Saw characters">Lindsey Perez</a> search for Agent Strahm with the assistance of Hoffman. Upon finding irregularities in previous murder scenes, Perez and Erickson discover Hoffman's identity, but are killed by him before they can go public with his involvement. Hoffman travels back to the site of William's tests in which Jill attacks him to obey John's final request. She leaves Hoffman in a new Reverse Beartrap left behind by John, though does not leave a key for him to free himself. He is able to manipulate the trap and escapes wounded.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Saw_3D" title="Saw 3D">Saw 3D</a></i> picks up with Jill and Hoffman battling for control of Jigsaw's legacy. As Jill enters protective custody and makes Hoffman's true identity public, Hoffman sets up a new game involving skinheads to find a way to Jill. Meanwhile, Bobby Dagen, a fraud who has written a book about escaping a Jigsaw trap he never experienced, is captured and forced to confront people who knew that he lied about being in a trap. Three of Dagen's friends die and his test concludes with him being forced to reenact the trap that he claimed to have survived before. He fails, which results in the death of his wife. Meanwhile, Hoffman has posed as a corpse and killed several officers to infiltrate the police station. He finds and kills Jill using the Reverse Beartrap. Hoffman attempts to leave town but is captured by Lawrence Gordon and his accomplices, then placed in the bathroom from the first film. Revealing Jigsaw aided Gordon after his game and, in return, Gordon helped with subsequent traps. Hoffman is then left shackled in the bathroom to die.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_elements">Production elements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saw_(franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production elements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Billy">Billy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saw_(franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Billy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Billy_the_Puppet" title="Billy the Puppet">Billy the Puppet</a></div>
<p>Billy is a puppet resembling a ventriloquist's dummy, sometimes seen riding a tricycle, that has appeared throughout the films and has become a type of mascot for the series. It is used by the <a href="/wiki/Jigsaw_(Saw)" title="Jigsaw (Saw)">Jigsaw Killer</a> to communicate with his victims by delivering televised messages or occasionally in person to describe details of the sadistic traps and the means by which the victims could survive. Viewers have sometimes incorrectly identified the puppet itself as Jigsaw, because of its presence and connection to the killer.</p>
<p>Age and use of the puppet over the course of the films necessitated its reconstruction. According to Wan, the original puppet's face for <i>Saw</i> involved clay, papier-mâché, and black ping-pong balls with the irises painted in for the eyes, but in later films more sophisticated construction included waterjet-cut foam for the body and remote-controlled <a href="/wiki/Audio-Animatronics" title="Audio-Animatronics">animatronics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GreggJoBlopt2_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-GreggJoBlopt2-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The endurance and popularity of the franchise has resulted in the production of <i>Billy</i> merchandise, as well as references in other media and its use in promotions for the films.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id=".22Hello_Zepp.22">"Hello Zepp"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saw_(franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title='Edit section: "Hello Zepp"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hello_Zepp" title="Hello Zepp">Hello Zepp</a></div>
<p>"Hello Zepp" is a piece of instrumental music that was originally composed by <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Clouser" title="Charlie Clouser">Charlie Clouser</a> for the first film in the series. In <i>Saw</i>, the implied villain, Zep Hindle, is revealed to actually be a victim of the Jigsaw Killer. (The character's name in the script is spelled "Zep", whereas the music titles are spelled "Zepp".)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> As the series continued, the piece was reused in every film as a <a href="/wiki/Leitmotif" title="Leitmotif">leitmotif</a>, often being renamed and remixed to accommodate the changing situations and characters. The music was used in every <i>Saw</i> ending, usually during the revealing of plot revelations and twists which <i>Saw</i> films often use and it serves as the main theme tune for the whole films.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Traps">Traps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saw_(franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Traps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Shawneesmithjigsawtrap.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="267" data-file-width="321" height="166" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/69/Shawneesmithjigsawtrap.PNG/200px-Shawneesmithjigsawtrap.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/69/Shawneesmithjigsawtrap.PNG/300px-Shawneesmithjigsawtrap.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/6/69/Shawneesmithjigsawtrap.PNG 2x" width="200"/></a>

<a href="/wiki/Amanda_Young" title="Amanda Young">Amanda Young</a> in the Reverse Beartrap.</div>

<p>An important component of each film is the variety of mechanical traps Jigsaw and his apprentices use on their captives to communicate his message.</p>
<p>According to David Hackl, all of the traps are real objects, and not <a href="/wiki/Computer-generated_imagery" title="Computer-generated imagery">CGI</a>. They were designed to look horrific but ultimately be safe for the actors in them. Writer Marcus Dunstan said: "It's built to function there on the day", and added: "It works. So if there's a scalping chair — there really was a chair with working gears to grind and pull your scalp back." The most potentially dangerous item was a "water box" used in <i>Saw V</i>, in which one of the actors (Scott Patterson, as Peter Strahm) had to keep his head submerged as long as possible. Another element of the traps is that Hackl desired a specific look of rust and menace, but he also wanted them to have a type of beauty about them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saw_(franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saw_(franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Saw_Guinness_award_cropped.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="382" height="276" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Saw_Guinness_award_cropped.jpg/220px-Saw_Guinness_award_cropped.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Saw_Guinness_award_cropped.jpg/330px-Saw_Guinness_award_cropped.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/93/Saw_Guinness_award_cropped.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Saw_Guinness_award_cropped.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The <i>Saw</i> series was placed in the <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">Guinness World Records</a> as the "Most Successful Horror Franchise".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Pictured here is director <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Greutert" title="Kevin Greutert">Kevin Greutert</a> receiving the award at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego_Comic-Con_International" title="San Diego Comic-Con International">2010 Comic-Con</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></div>
</div>
</div>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Saw_(2004_film)" title="Saw (2004 film)">Saw</a></i> grossed $18.2 million its opening weekend and had become <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lions_Gate_Entertainment" title="Lions Gate Entertainment">Lionsgate</a>'s second best opening, after <i><a href="/wiki/Fahrenheit_9/11" title="Fahrenheit 9/11">Fahrenheit 9/11</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span> <span class="nowrap">$23.9 million</span> (2004).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BOMOpening_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-BOMOpening-16">[16]</a></sup> <i>Saw</i> went on to gross <span class="nowrap">$103 million</span> worldwide,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SawBOM_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-SawBOM-17">[17]</a></sup> and is the second lowest-grossing film in the series after <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_VI" title="Saw VI">Saw VI</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saw.vs_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Saw.vs-18">[18]</a></sup> At the time, it became the most profitable horror film after <i><a href="/wiki/Scream_(1996_film)" title="Scream (1996 film)">Scream</a></i> (1996). It is the seventh highest-grossing <a href="/wiki/Halloween" title="Halloween">Halloween</a> opening weekend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Halloween_opener_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Halloween_opener-19">[19]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_II" title="Saw II">Saw II</a></i> opened at number one with $31.7 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BOMWeekend_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-BOMWeekend-20">[20]</a></sup> and set a Lionsgate Halloween opening weekend record and is also the third highest-grossing Halloween opener. It became at the time, the widest release in Lionsgate history and one of the best opening weekends for a horror sequel. It is Lionsgate's fourth highest-grossing film in the United States and Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LionsgateBO_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-LionsgateBO-21">[21]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_III" title="Saw III">Saw III</a></i> placed first by grossing $33.6 million its opening weekend, making it the biggest Halloween debut ever<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Halloween_opener_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-Halloween_opener-19">[19]</a></sup> and at the time, Lionsgate's highest-opening weekend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> It is the highest-grossing film in the series worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saw.vs_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-Saw.vs-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saw3BOM_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Saw3BOM-23">[23]</a></sup> It has the highest-grossing weekend in the series and also Lionsgate's fifth highest-grossing film in the United States and Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saw.vs_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-Saw.vs-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LionsgateBO_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-LionsgateBO-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Saw_IV" title="Saw IV">Saw IV</a></i> premiered at number one with $32.1 million making it the second best Halloween weekend opener.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Halloween_opener_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-Halloween_opener-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_V" title="Saw V">Saw V</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s opening weekend it placed second, being beat by <i><a href="/wiki/High_School_Musical_3:_Senior_Year" title="High School Musical 3: Senior Year">High School Musical 3: Senior Year</a></i>, and made $30.1 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> It is Lionsgate's tenth highest-grossing film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LionsgateBO_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-LionsgateBO-21">[21]</a></sup> <i>Saw VI</i> opened in second place behind <i><a href="/wiki/Paranormal_Activity" title="Paranormal Activity">Paranormal Activity</a></i> to $14.1 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> which is the lowest of all the <i>Saw</i> films worldwide. It is also the lowest-grossing film in the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saw.vs_18-3"><a href="#cite_note-Saw.vs-18">[18]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_3D" title="Saw 3D">Saw 3D</a></i> placed first grossing $22.5 million its opening weekend, with 92% of tickets coming from more than 2,100 3D-equipped locations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BoxOpeningWeekend_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-BoxOpeningWeekend-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VarietyBox_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-VarietyBox-28">[28]</a></sup> It had the fifth best opening weekend in the <i>Saw</i> series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SawOpenings_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-SawOpenings-29">[29]</a></sup> It is the most successful film in the franchise since <i>Saw IV</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saw7BOM_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Saw7BOM-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <i>Saw</i> series, when compared to the other high-grossing American horror franchises - <i><a href="/wiki/A_Nightmare_on_Elm_Street_(franchise)" title="A Nightmare on Elm Street (franchise)">A Nightmare on Elm Street</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chucky_(franchise)" title="Chucky (franchise)">Chucky</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Friday_the_13th_(franchise)" title="Friday the 13th (franchise)">Friday the 13th</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Halloween_(franchise)" title="Halloween (franchise)">Halloween</a></i>, the <a href="/wiki/Hannibal_Lecter" title="Hannibal Lecter">Hannibal Lecter</a> series, <i><a href="/wiki/Hellraiser_(franchise)" title="Hellraiser (franchise)">Hellraiser</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Psycho_(franchise)" title="Psycho (franchise)">Psycho</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scream_(film_series)" title="Scream (film series)">Scream</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Exorcist" title="The Exorcist">The Exorcist</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Texas_Chainsaw_Massacre_(franchise)" title="The Texas Chainsaw Massacre (franchise)">The Texas Chainsaw Massacre</a></i> — with figures adjusted for 2011 inflation,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inflationCalculator_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-inflationCalculator-31">[31]</a></sup> is the fifth highest-grossing horror franchise in the United States and Canada at $457.4 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> This list is topped by <i>Friday the 13th</i> at $687.1 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> followed by the <i>Nightmare on Elm Street</i> series with $592.8 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> with the <i>Hannibal Lecter</i> film series closely behind with $588.7 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Then comes <i>Halloween</i> with $557.5 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> the <i>Scream</i> series with $442.9 million and <i>The Texas Chainsaw Massacre</i> with $304.6 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> and lastly comes the <i>Chucky</i> film series with approximately $203 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><br/></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_games">Video games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saw_(franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Video games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> video game company <a href="/wiki/Konami" title="Konami">Konami</a> currently owns the rights to the <i>Saw</i> interactive video game property. Konami stated in mid-2009 that they wanted to make <i>Saw</i> a series of video games to supplement the films. They also wish to make <i>Saw</i> their next big survival horror franchise next to their other property, <i><a href="/wiki/Silent_Hill" title="Silent Hill">Silent Hill</a></i>. They stated that because <i>Saw</i> focuses on visual intensity and <i>Silent Hill</i> focuses on psychological terror, both could exist in the video game industry without directly competing against each other.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Will_Saw_Be_Konami.27s_Second_Great_Horror_Franchise.3F_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-Will_Saw_Be_Konami.27s_Second_Great_Horror_Franchise.3F-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first game in the series, <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_(video_game)" title="Saw (video game)">Saw</a></i>, set between first and second film in the series, was initially released on October 6, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WanWhannell_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-WanWhannell-62">[62]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> with a sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_II:_Flesh_%26_Blood" title="Saw II: Flesh &amp; Blood">Saw II: Flesh &amp; Blood</a></i>, released on October 19, 2010 for the Xbox 360 and PlayStation 3, ten days before the release of the seventh film, <i>Saw 3D</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Comic">Comic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saw_(franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Comic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Saw: Rebirth</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Comic_book" title="Comic book">comic book</a> prequel to the original film released to promote <i>Saw II</i>. Its <a href="/wiki/Canon_(fiction)" title="Canon (fiction)">canonicity</a> was later contradicted by events in <i>Saw IV</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Attractions">Attractions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saw_(franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Attractions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>"<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saw:_The_Ride" title="Saw: The Ride">Saw: The Ride</a>" is a <a href="/wiki/Gerstlauer_Euro-Fighter" title="Gerstlauer Euro-Fighter">Gerstlauer Euro-Fighter</a> roller coaster themed around the franchise, which opened on March 13, 2009 at <a href="/wiki/Thorpe_Park" title="Thorpe Park">Thorpe Park</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. It features an enclosed dark ride section with special effects, before cars travel outside and are pulled up a 100-foot vertical lift hill into a steep 100-degree drop.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thorpe_Park" title="Thorpe Park">Thorpe Park</a> opened a temporary <i>Saw</i> attraction called "Saw — Movie Bites" for their 2009 Fright Nights event.</li>
<li>It was announced in October 2009 that Thorpe Park would also be adding a permanent, year-round <a href="/wiki/Live_action" title="Live action">live action</a> horror <a href="/wiki/Maze" title="Maze">maze</a> themed around the <i>Saw</i> movies. It opened at the start of the 2010 season, and features six scenes representing one iconic trap from each movie to date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup></li>
<li>"<a href="/wiki/Halloween_Horror_Nights" title="Halloween Horror Nights">Saw: Game Over</a>" was a 2009 maze made by <a href="/wiki/Universal_Parks_%26_Resorts" title="Universal Parks &amp; Resorts">Universal Studios</a> for <a href="/wiki/Halloween_Horror_Nights" title="Halloween Horror Nights">Halloween Horror Nights</a>, based on characters, traps and scenes from the films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sneek_Peak_At_Universal.27s_Saw:_Game_Over_Maze.21_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sneek_Peak_At_Universal.27s_Saw:_Game_Over_Maze.21-67">[67]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios_Hollywood" title="Universal Studios Hollywood">Universal Studios Hollywood</a> rendition of Horror Nights it was titled <i>Saw: Game Over</i>, while at the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios_Florida" title="Universal Studios Florida">Universal Studios Florida</a> rendition, it was simply titled <i>Saw</i>.</li>
<li>"Saw", a themed haunted house, operated during the month of October at <a class="external text" href="http://www.frightdome.com" rel="nofollow">Fright Dome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Circus_Circus_Las_Vegas" title="Circus Circus Las Vegas">Circus Circus Las Vegas</a>. It featured interactive, handcrafted replicas of the "games" set by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jigsaw_Killer" title="Jigsaw Killer">Jigsaw</a>. It was introduced in 2009 and partnered with <a href="/wiki/Lionsgate" title="Lionsgate">Lionsgate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Twisted_Pictures" title="Twisted Pictures">Twisted Pictures</a>.</li>
<li>In 2010, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Halloween_Fright_Nights" title="Halloween Fright Nights">Halloween Fright Nights</a>" at <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Movie_World" title="Warner Bros. Movie World">Warner Bros. Movie World</a> featured a maze created by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sudden_Impact_Entertainment" title="Sudden Impact Entertainment">Sudden Impact Entertainment</a>, featuring characters, traps and scenes from the films. The maze was simply titled "Saw Maze".</li>
<li>"Saw Haunted Attraction" was a maze at the Brea Plaza Shopping Center in 2008 created by Sinister Pointe Haunted Attractions.</li>
<li>"SAWMANIA" was a fan event in New York. Eventgoers were able to meet actors and directors, and view props used in the films.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future">Future</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saw_(franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Future">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since Saw 3D's release there had been numerous reports and rumors about a new entry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lionsgate_Remaking_Saw_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Lionsgate_Remaking_Saw-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloody-disgusting.com_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-bloody-disgusting.com-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> In a 2011 interview with <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a> Wan stated "[The series] is finished for now, but since it's such a huge, well-known franchise, it's gonna come back at some point." When asked in the same interview what it would take to come back and Wan stated "I would come back if we were allowed to do something different and really cool."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<p>During February, 2016, Lionsgate confirmed it had hired screenwriters Josh Stolberg and Pete Goldfinger to write an eighth installment in the franchise, which has been tentatively titled <i>Saw: Legacy</i> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spierig_brothers" title="Spierig brothers">Peter and Michael Spierig</a> directing and filming will start in September 2016 for an October 27, 2017 release date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> According to Clouser, <i>Legacy</i> is a re-invention of the franchise that will "establish a new story line and new characters that can carry the saga into the future."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup></p>
