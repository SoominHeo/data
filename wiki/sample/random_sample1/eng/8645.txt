<p><b>Suzhou District</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/District_(PRC)" title="District (PRC)">district</a> in <a href="/wiki/Jiuquan" title="Jiuquan">Jiuquan</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prefecture_(PRC)" title="Prefecture (PRC)">Prefecture</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gansu" title="Gansu">Gansu</a> <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_China" title="Provinces of China">Province</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>. It was formerly an important city in its own right. Today, as the seat of Jiuquan's administration, it is usually marked <b>Jiuquan</b> on maps.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suzhou_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Suzhou is named for the former <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Su_Prefecture_(Gansu)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Su Prefecture (Gansu) (page does not exist)">Su Prefecture</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_China" title="Imperial China">imperial China</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suzhou_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Su_Prefecture_(Gansu)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Su Prefecture (Gansu) (page does not exist)">Su Prefecture</a> was established under the <a href="/wiki/Sui_dynasty" title="Sui dynasty">Sui</a> and renamed <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jiuquan_Commandery&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jiuquan Commandery (page does not exist)">Jiuquan Commandery</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Tang_dynasty" title="Tang dynasty">Tang</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Its seat was established just within the extreme northwest angle of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Wall_of_China" title="Great Wall of China">Great Wall</a> near the <a href="/wiki/Yumen_Pass" title="Yumen Pass">Jade Gate</a>. It sometimes served as the capital of the province of Gansu.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271887_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271887-2">[2]</a></sup> Along with its role protecting trade along the <a href="/wiki/Silk_Road" title="Silk Road">Silk Road</a>, Suzhou was the great center of the <a href="/wiki/Rhubarb" title="Rhubarb">rhubarb</a> trade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271887_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271887-2">[2]</a></sup> The old town was completely destroyed in the <a href="/wiki/Dungan_Revolt_(1862%E2%80%9377)" title="Dungan Revolt (1862–77)">First Dungan Revolt</a> but was recovered by the <a href="/wiki/Qing_dynasty" title="Qing dynasty">Qing</a> in 1873 and was swiftly rebuilt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271887_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271887-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
