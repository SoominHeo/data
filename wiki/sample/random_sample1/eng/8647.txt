<p><b>Sun Jihai</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">孙继海</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">孫繼海</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Sūn Jìhǎi</span></i>; born 30 September 1977) is a <a href="/wiki/Chinese_people" title="Chinese people">Chinese</a> retired footballer. As the first Chinese player who played in English Premier League, Sun was also the first Chinese footballer who kicked goals in EPL as well as UEFA Cup. In February 2016, Sun founded Beijing Haiqiu Technology Company (HQ Sports), while at that moment he still played for <a href="/wiki/Beijing_Renhe_F.C." title="Beijing Renhe F.C.">Beijing Renhe</a> in the <a href="/wiki/China_League_One" title="China League One">China League One</a>. In December, Sun officially announced his retirement from football. Also in this month, as the Chairman of HQ Sports, Sun announced that HQ Sports had raised tens of millions of investment, led by <a href="/wiki/China_Media_Capital" title="China Media Capital">China Media Capital</a> (CMC). Other investors also include <a href="/wiki/Tencent" title="Tencent">Tencent</a> and Yuan Xu Fund.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sun_Jihai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dalian_Shide">Dalian Shide</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sun_Jihai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Dalian Shide">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sun Jihai started his football career with <a href="/wiki/Dalian_Shide_F.C." title="Dalian Shide F.C.">Dalian Shide</a> in 1995 and made his first appearance on 28 May 1995 against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sichuan_Quanxing" title="Sichuan Quanxing">Sichuan Quanxing</a>. Like many of the top young Chinese players of his generation, he wanted to go to the Chinese youth training camp in Brazil that was sponsored by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jianlibao" title="Jianlibao">Jianlibao</a>. However, he was not selected as he was not deemed to possess enough potential. Sun showed great character by not letting this huge disappointment derail him and worked very hard to establish himself at his club. Sun enjoyed a successful spell with Dalian as the club won four league titles and one <a href="/wiki/Chinese_FA_Cup" title="Chinese FA Cup">Chinese FA Cup</a> title during his time there.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Crystal_Palace">Crystal Palace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sun_Jihai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Crystal Palace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After three successful years at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dalian_Shide" title="Dalian Shide">Dalian Shide</a>, Sun along with <a href="/wiki/Fan_Zhiyi" title="Fan Zhiyi">Fan Zhiyi</a> signed for <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a> in August 1998. They became the first ever Chinese footballers to play in the English leagues. Sun made his debut for Crystal Palace in a 3–0 loss against <a href="/wiki/Bury_F.C." title="Bury F.C.">Bury</a> in the first leg of a <a href="/wiki/1998%E2%80%9399_Football_League_Cup" title="1998–99 Football League Cup">1998–99 Football League Cup</a> match. He was recalled by Dalian who struggled heavily at the bottom of league in the 1999 season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manchester_City">Manchester City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sun_Jihai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Manchester City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2002, Sun signed for English side <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a> from <a href="/wiki/Dalian_Shide_F.C." title="Dalian Shide F.C.">Dalian Shide</a> for £2 million and became the first <a href="/wiki/Asian_people" title="Asian people">Asian</a> player to sign for the club. He made his debut for the club in a 4–2 win against <a href="/wiki/Coventry_City_F.C." title="Coventry City F.C.">Coventry City</a>. His solid defence and dangerous attacking forays won over many City fans. He was voted the best player for the club in September 2002. In October 2002, Sun became the first <a href="/wiki/East_Asian_people" title="East Asian people">East Asian</a> footballer to score in the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> when he headed home the first goal in a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C." title="Birmingham City F.C.">Birmingham City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the beginning of the 2004–05 season, Sun damaged his cruciate ligaments in a tackle with <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> striker <a href="/wiki/Ei%C3%B0ur_Gu%C3%B0johnsen" title="Eiður Guðjohnsen">Eiður Guðjohnsen</a> and missed the rest of the season. After recuperating and following a strict physical regimen devised by his father, Sun re-installed his place as a regular in the club's starting lineup. In the 2006–07 season, Sun was set back yet again by another injury and he made his return on 10 February 2007, against <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a> which his side lost 2–1. With the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Sven-G%C3%B6ran_Eriksson" title="Sven-Göran Eriksson">Sven-Göran Eriksson</a> as the new manager, Sun rarely played in the 2007–08 season. He was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Vedran_%C4%86orluka" title="Vedran Ćorluka">Vedran Ćorluka</a> at right back and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Ball_(footballer)" title="Michael Ball (footballer)">Michael Ball</a> played as the defensive substitute instead of Sun.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sheffield_United">Sheffield United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sun_Jihai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sheffield United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 2 July 2008, Sun signed a two-year deal with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_Championship" title="English Championship">English Championship</a> club <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_United_F.C." title="Sheffield United F.C.">Sheffield United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blades_deal_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Blades_deal-2">[2]</a></sup> Sun only managed nineteen minutes in his first match for Sheffield United, getting sent off after picking up two yellows in a friendly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blades_friendly_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Blades_friendly-3">[3]</a></sup> He went on to make his full debut in the opening fixture of the 2008–09 season against <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C." title="Birmingham City F.C.">Birmingham City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blades_debut_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Blades_debut-4">[4]</a></sup> He was sent off during a game against <a href="/wiki/Coventry_City_F.C." title="Coventry City F.C.">Coventry City</a> after a lunging tackle from behind on opposing player <a href="/wiki/Michael_Mifsud" title="Michael Mifsud">Michael Mifsud</a>. <a href="/wiki/The_Football_Association" title="The Football Association">The Football Association</a> charged him for his behavior after receiving a red card even with his past good disciplinary record and handed him an additional one-match ban. Having played regularly until November he succumbed to an injury and subsequently failed to force his way back into the first team and made only a handful of appearances in the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> in the second half of the season. Sun was then released from his contract in July 2009 with Sheffield United claiming he had "failed to settle" in <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blades_release_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Blades_release-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_China">Return to China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sun_Jihai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Return to China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2009, Sun was loaned to <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_United_F.C." title="Sheffield United F.C.">Sheffield United</a>'s satellite team <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chengdu_Blades_F.C." title="Chengdu Blades F.C.">Chengdu Blades</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Super_League" title="Chinese Super League">Chinese Super League</a>, hoping to make an impact. He steadily established himself at Chengdu and helped them to achieve the club's best ever league position at seventh place. However, the club was relegated to <a href="/wiki/China_League_One" title="China League One">China League One</a> in the fallout of a match fixing scandal. He returned to Sheffield United after the loan was finished, but his contract ended and was left without a club to play for.</p>
<p>On 8 January 2010, Sun signed a two-year contract with fellow top tier side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guizhou_Renhe_F.C." title="Guizhou Renhe F.C.">Shaanxi Chanba</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He was promoted as the team captain immediately and he extended his contract for another two years in December 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> At the beginning of the 2012 season, Sun followed the club when it decided to move to Guizhou and rename themselves <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guizhou_Renhe_F.C." title="Guizhou Renhe F.C.">Guizhou Renhe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He played nineteen league matches and five <a href="/wiki/Chinese_FA_Cup" title="Chinese FA Cup">Chinese FA Cup</a> matches in the 2012 season as the club achieved fourth place that league season and were runners-up in the cup which gained them entry into <a href="/wiki/AFC_Champions_League" title="AFC Champions League">AFC Champions League</a> for the first time in the club's history.</p>
<p>On 4 February 2015, Sun moved to fellow top tier side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chongqing_Lifan_F.C." title="Chongqing Lifan F.C.">Chongqing Lifan</a> on a free transfer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He made his debut for the club on 8 March 2014 in a 3–0 loss against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beijing_Guoan_F.C." title="Beijing Guoan F.C.">Beijing Guoan</a>.</p>
<p>On 8 December 2015, Sun returned to second tier side Beijing Renhe on a free transfer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 10 December 2016, Sun announced his retirement from football.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sun_Jihai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sun made his international debut for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_national_football_team" title="Chinese national football team">Chinese national team</a> on 6 December 1996 against <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan_national_football_team" title="Uzbekistan national football team">Uzbekistan</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 FIFA World Cup</a> in a group stage match against <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica_national_football_team" title="Costa Rica national football team">Costa Rica</a>, Sun picked up an ankle injury after a tackle from behind by <a href="/wiki/Mauricio_Sol%C3%ADs" title="Mauricio Solís">Mauricio Solís</a> after just 17 minutes. Unable to play, Sun was replaced after 25 minutes and sat out the rest of group stage matches. On 7 June 2008, during a <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification">2010 FIFA World Cup qualification</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Qatar_national_football_team" title="Qatar national football team">Qatar</a>, Sun was shown a straight red card for dissent while he was warming up as a substitute on the bench. China lost the match 1–0. He was given a ban of five matches by <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a> and did not receive any call-ups to the national team after this incident.</p>
