<p><b>Tsuge Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">柘植駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tsuge-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Train_station" title="Train station">train station</a> of <a href="/wiki/West_Japan_Railway_Company" title="West Japan Railway Company">West Japan Railway Company</a> (JR-West) in <a href="/wiki/Iga,_Mie" title="Iga, Mie">Iga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mie_Prefecture" title="Mie Prefecture">Mie Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It is located 79.9 rail kilometres from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terminal_station" title="Terminal station">terminus</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Kansai_Main_Line" title="Kansai Main Line">Kansai Main Line</a> at <a href="/wiki/Nagoya_Station" title="Nagoya Station">Nagoya Station</a> and 20.0 rail kilometers from <a href="/wiki/Kameyama_Station_(Mie)" title="Kameyama Station (Mie)">Kameyama Station</a> and is 36.7 rail kilometers from the opposing terminus of the <a href="/wiki/Kusatsu_Line" title="Kusatsu Line">Kusatsu Line</a> at <a href="/wiki/Kusatsu_Station_(Shiga)" title="Kusatsu Station (Shiga)">Kusatsu Station</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsuge_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tsuge Station was opened on February 18, 1890 with the extension of the Kansai Railway from <a href="/wiki/Mikumo_Station" title="Mikumo Station">Mikumo Station</a>, making it the oldest station within Mie Prefecture. The Kansai Railway was extended to <a href="/wiki/Yokkaichi_Station" title="Yokkaichi Station">Yokkaichi Station</a> on December 25, 1890 and to <a href="/wiki/Iga-Ueno_Station" title="Iga-Ueno Station">Ueno Station</a> on January 15, 1897. The line was nationalized on October 1, 1907, becoming part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_Government_Railways" title="Imperial Government Railways">Imperial Government Railways</a> (IGR), which became <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_National_Railways" title="Japan National Railways">Japan National Railways</a> (JNR) after World War II. Freight operations were discontinued from August 1, 1972. With the <a href="/wiki/Privatization" title="Privatization">privatization</a> of JNR on April 1, 1987, the station came under the control of JR-West.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Layout">Layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsuge_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tsuge Station has a <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platform</a> and in an <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platform</a> with three tracks on the ground level, connected by an overpass. The Kusatsu line tracks are electrified but the Kansai Line tracks are not.</p>
