<p><b>Tsurumi</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">鶴見区</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (literal translation "place to see crane-district"),is one of the 18 ku (<a href="/wiki/Wards_of_Japan" title="Wards of Japan">wards</a>) of the city of <a href="/wiki/Yokohama" title="Yokohama">Yokohama</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kanagawa_Prefecture" title="Kanagawa Prefecture">Kanagawa Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of 2010, the ward had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 270,433 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">density</a> of 8,140 persons per km². The total area was 33.23 km².</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsurumi-ku,_Yokohama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tsurumi-ku is located in eastern Kanagawa Prefecture, in the northeast corner of the city of Yokohama.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Surrounding_municipalities">Surrounding municipalities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsurumi-ku,_Yokohama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Surrounding municipalities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<li><a href="/wiki/Kanagawa-ku,_Yokohama" title="Kanagawa-ku, Yokohama">Kanagawa Ward</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dhoku-ku,_Yokohama" title="Kōhoku-ku, Yokohama">Kōhoku Ward</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kawasaki,_Kanagawa" title="Kawasaki, Kanagawa">Kawasaki</a></li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsurumi-ku,_Yokohama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area around present-day Tsurumi Ward has been inhabited continuously for thousands of years. Archaeologists have found stone tools from the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Paleolithic" title="Japanese Paleolithic">Japanese Paleolithic</a> period and ceramic shards from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jomon_period" title="Jomon period">Jomon period</a>, and tombs from the <a href="/wiki/Kofun_period" title="Kofun period">Kofun period</a> at numerous locations in the area. Under the <a href="/wiki/Nara_period" title="Nara period">Nara period</a> <a href="/wiki/Ritsury%C5%8D" title="Ritsuryō">Ritsuryō</a> system, it became part of <a href="/wiki/Musashi_Province" title="Musashi Province">Musashi Province</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>, the territory came under the control of <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Ieyasu" title="Tokugawa Ieyasu">Tokugawa Ieyasu</a>. It was administered as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tenry%C5%8D" title="Tenryō">tenryō</a></i> territory controlled directly by the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a>, but administered through various <i><a href="/wiki/Hatamoto" title="Hatamoto">hatamoto</a></i>. During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bakumatsu_period" title="Bakumatsu period">Bakumatsu period</a>, nearby <a href="/wiki/Kanagawa-juku" title="Kanagawa-juku">Kanagawa-juku</a> was the location of the signing of the <a href="/wiki/Convention_of_Kanagawa" title="Convention of Kanagawa">Convention of Kanagawa</a>, which ended Japan’s <a href="/wiki/Sakoku" title="Sakoku">national isolation policy</a> and led to the normalization of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diplomatic_relations" title="Diplomatic relations">diplomatic relations</a> between the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and Japan. The subsequent <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Amity_and_Commerce_(United_States%E2%80%93Japan)" title="Treaty of Amity and Commerce (United States–Japan)">Treaty of Amity and Commerce</a> led to the establishment of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Treaty_port" title="Treaty port">treaty port</a> for foreign commerce and settlement, which was initially stipulated to be Kanagawa. However, for security reasons, the actual settlement was established at neighboring Yokohama (present day Naka-ku). The <a href="/wiki/Namamugi_Incident" title="Namamugi Incident">Namamugi Incident</a>, which led to the 1863 <a href="/wiki/Bombardment_of_Kagoshima" title="Bombardment of Kagoshima">Anglo-Satsuma War</a>, occurred in what is now part of Tsurumi Ward.</p>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a>, the area was transferred to the new Tachibana District of <a href="/wiki/Kanagawa_Prefecture" title="Kanagawa Prefecture">Kanagawa Prefecture</a> in 1868. Tsurumi was connected to Yokohama and Tokyo by train in 1872, and the area rapidly urbanized. <a href="/wiki/S%C5%8Dji-ji" title="Sōji-ji">Sōji-ji</a>, the head temple of the <a href="/wiki/S%C5%8Dt%C5%8D" title="Sōtō">Sōtō</a> sect of <a href="/wiki/Zen" title="Zen">Zen</a> <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhism</a> relocated to Tsurumi from <a href="/wiki/Ishikawa_Prefecture" title="Ishikawa Prefecture">Ishikawa Prefecture</a> in 1911. Tsurumi suffered severe damage from the <a href="/wiki/1923_Great_Kant%C5%8D_earthquake" title="1923 Great Kantō earthquake">1923 Great Kantō earthquake</a>. In April 1924, Tsurumi became a town within Tachibana District. On October 1, 1927 Tsurumi became a ward within the city of Yokohama. The area suffered greatly again during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, and was completely devastated during the massive <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bombing_of_Yokohama_in_World_War_II&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bombing of Yokohama in World War II (page does not exist)">Yokohama air raid</a> of May 29, 1945. The area soon rebuilt after the end of the war, assisted by an influx of educational facilities in the 1950s and rapid re-industrialization of the area in the 1950s and 1960s. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yokohama_rail_crash" title="Yokohama rail crash">Tsurumi railway accident</a> occurred near Tsurumi Station on November 9, 1963, killing 161 people. The population of Tsurumi Ward surpassed 260,000 people in 2007, and celebrated the 80th anniversary of its foundation in 2009.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsurumi-ku,_Yokohama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tsurumi Ward is a regional commercial center and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bedroom_community" title="Bedroom community">bedroom community</a> for central Yokohama and Tokyo. The coastal area is part of the Keihin Industrial Zone, and is the most industrialized region within Yokohama. Major factories are operated by:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/ExxonMobil" title="ExxonMobil">ExxonMobil</a> (Anzencho 2-chome)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/JFE_Steel" title="JFE Steel">JFE Steel</a> (Oogijima) - JFE's main plant for eastern Japan is on the border of Tsurumi-ku and <a href="/wiki/Kawasaki-ku,_Kawasaki" title="Kawasaki-ku, Kawasaki">Kawasaki-ku, Kawasaki</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kirin_Brewery" title="Kirin Brewery">Kirin Brewery</a> (Namamugi 1-chome)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nissan_Motors" title="Nissan Motors">Nissan Motors</a> (Daikokucho)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Toshiba" title="Toshiba">Toshiba</a> (Suehirocho 2-chome) - Toshiba's largest factory is located on Tokyo Bay and has its own JR train station, <a href="/wiki/Umi-Shibaura_Station" title="Umi-Shibaura Station">Umi-Shibaura Station</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Morinaga_%26_Company" title="Morinaga &amp; Company">Morinaga &amp; Company</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Asahi_Glass_Co." title="Asahi Glass Co.">Asahi Glass Co.</a></li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=J-OIL_MILLS,_Inc&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="J-OIL MILLS, Inc (page does not exist)">J-OIL MILLS, Inc</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Electric_Power_Company" title="Tokyo Electric Power Company">Tokyo Electric Power Company</a></li>
</ul>
<p>Daikokufuto, an artificial island in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Bay" title="Tokyo Bay">Tokyo Bay</a>, is a major warehousing center.</p>
