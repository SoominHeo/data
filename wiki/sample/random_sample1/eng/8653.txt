<p><i><b>Mitsudomoe</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">みつどもえ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. <i>Three Way Struggle</i>)</span> is a gag <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series by <a href="/wiki/Mangaka" title="Mangaka">manga creator</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Norio_Sakurai&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Norio Sakurai (page does not exist)">Norio Sakurai</a> about the adventures of the Marui triplets in sixth grade and their newly hired teacher Satoshi Yabe as he deals with his new class. The series was first serialized in <i><a href="/wiki/Weekly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Champion" title="Weekly Shōnen Champion">Weekly Shōnen Champion</a></i> in 2006 before moving to <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bessatsu_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Champion&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bessatsu Shōnen Champion (page does not exist)">Bessatsu Shōnen Champion</a></i> in 2012. <a href="/wiki/Akita_Shoten" title="Akita Shoten">Akita Shoten</a> has published the series in both of their magazines. The series follows the everyday life of these sisters, their classmates, and their unfortunate teacher.</p>
<p>The series has been collected into seventeen <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> manga volumes the first of which was released on January 9, 2006. In July 2010, the manga was adapted into an <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> series that ran for 13 weeks first airing on <a href="/wiki/Chubu-Nippon_Broadcasting" title="Chubu-Nippon Broadcasting">Chubu-Nippon Broadcasting</a>. A second series called <i>Mitsudomoe Zōryōchū!</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">みつどもえ 増量中！</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> was made the following year that aired between January 9, 2011 and February 28, 2011. Although the series has not been licensed for release in North America, it has been simulcast by <a href="/wiki/Crunchyroll" title="Crunchyroll">Crunchyroll</a> which <a href="/wiki/Subtitle_(captioning)" title="Subtitle (captioning)">subbed</a> the series. The first anime adaptation has received mixed reviews with most saying that while the humor will creep some out, others will enjoy it.</p>

<p>Satoshi Yabe is just starting his new life as an <a href="/wiki/Elementary_school" title="Elementary school">elementary school</a> teacher. Little does he know that in his assigned 6th year class, the law is made by the troublesome Marui triplets Mitsuba, Futaba and Hitoha.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mitsudomoe_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mitsudomoe_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mitsudomoe was first written and illustrated by Norio Sakurai in <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> format, and has been serialized by <a href="/wiki/Akita_Shoten" title="Akita Shoten">Akita Shoten</a> in two of their magazines. The original serialization occurred in the <a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Dnen_manga" title="Shōnen manga">Shōnen</a> magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Weekly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Champion" title="Weekly Shōnen Champion">Weekly Shōnen Champion</a></i> starting in 2006, and continued for five years. Due to a "break" by the author the manga then went on hiatus between the spring of 2011 and July 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-break_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-break-2">[2]</a></sup> The manga was briefly returned to Weekly Shōnen Champion before the serialization was moved to the monthly <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bessatsu_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Champion&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bessatsu Shōnen Champion (page does not exist)">Bessatsu Shōnen Champion</a></i> magazine on August 23, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-break_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-break-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The manga has continued its release through this second magazine, and sixteen <i>tankōbon</i> have been collected from the serial chapters. The first release goes back to January 9, 2006, and has continued with the sixteenth volume being released on November 6, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ja-vol01_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ja-vol01-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ja-vol16.2F_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-ja-vol16.2F-5">[5]</a></sup> Outside Japan, the series has been licensed by Doki Doki in France, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tong_Li_Comics" title="Tong Li Comics">Tong Li Comics</a> in Taiwan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In addition to the main series, a spin-off series called <i>Almost Weekly Mitsudomoe</i> (ほぼ週刊みつどもえ) was also released. The chapters featured in the spin-off could not be published with the main series.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="(December 2015)">why?</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>In November 2009 an anime adaptation of the manga by the studio Bridge was announced, to be directed by Masahiko Ohta<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The first season aired on <a href="/wiki/Chubu-Nippon_Broadcasting" title="Chubu-Nippon Broadcasting">Chubu-Nippon Broadcasting</a> between July 2, 2010 and September 26, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-onair_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-onair-11">[11]</a></sup> Others broadcast networks are <a href="/wiki/Nippon_BS_Broadcasting" title="Nippon BS Broadcasting">BS11 Digital</a>, <a href="/wiki/AT-X_(company)" title="AT-X (company)">AT-X</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mainichi_Broadcasting_System" title="Mainichi Broadcasting System">MBS</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metropolitan_Television" title="Tokyo Metropolitan Television">Tokyo MX</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-onair_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-onair-11">[11]</a></sup> New episodes are currently being subtitled by MX International and simulcast on <a href="/wiki/Crunchyroll" title="Crunchyroll">Crunchyroll</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crunchy_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-crunchy-12">[12]</a></sup> The series began release on Blu-ray and DVD from August 25, 2010. An originally unaired episode was included with the seventh volume released on February 23, 2011 and also aired on March 6, 2011 following the second season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> A second season of the anime, titled <i>Mitsudomoe Zōryōchū!</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">みつどもえ 増量中！</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> was announced with the 10th volume of the manga and aired between January 9, 2011 and February 28, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> It was also simulcast on <a href="/wiki/Crunchyroll" title="Crunchyroll">Crunchyroll</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mitsudomoe_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The anime adaptation of Mitsudomoe has received mixed reviews. Four reviewers from <a href="/wiki/Anime_News_Network" title="Anime News Network">Anime News Network</a> gave the first episode of the first season various reviews. Two reviewers, Hope Chapman and Gia Manry, gave the episode a 2 out of 5 rating. Chapman called the humor for the episode an "acquired taste" saying that the opening should make anyone who has babysat before have a "kneejerk reaction". While calling the content revolting she goes on to say it isn't garbage but that it isn't <a href="/wiki/South_Park" title="South Park">South Park</a> material yet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Gia Manry calls the three main character girls "psychotic" and the gags for the most part not funny. She goes on to say that the characters overall are so simplistic their reactions are predictable. In the end she gave the episode a 2 based on Hitoha "who actually develops somewhat".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Carl Kimlinger gave the episode a 3 out of 5 rating saying that while the content may turn some people away it is "their loss". He goes on to say though that if the humor success rate stays at 50% then its future is limited.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The last reviewer, Theron Martin, gave the episode the highest rating of a 4.5 out of 5. He calls the episode "cleverly-written" and "wonderfully-scored" saying that if you like the type of raunchy humor the episode aimed at adults has then it is the one for you.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Overall, Carl Kimlinger from Anime News Network gave the first season a grade of a B− to a B+. He praises the first seven episodes for being very funny from the misunderstandings to the episode-long jokes. He goes on to say though that the episodes are vulgar, more than slightly disgusting, and highly creepy for some.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> For the last six episodes Kimlinger rated them better giving them the B+ rating. He calls the episodes warmer, more character-based, and less of a turnoff than the first seven episodes. He singles out episode 9 as being "downright brilliant". He goes on to say though that even though the final six episodes are less vulgar they will still creep many out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Carl gives the second season a rating of a C+ saying that while the cast is "surprisingly enjoyable" the humor feels recycled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
