<p><b>Agano</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">阿賀野市</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Agano-shi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> located in north-central <a href="/wiki/Niigata_Prefecture" title="Niigata Prefecture">Niigata Prefecture</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Hokuriku_region" title="Hokuriku region">Hokuriku region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of 1 June 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Agano,_Niigata&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the city had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 42,993 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 223 persons per km². Its total area was 192.74 square kilometres (74.42 sq mi).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agano,_Niigata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Agano is located in an inland region of north-central Niigata Prefecture. As the city name implies, the <a href="/wiki/Agano_River" title="Agano River">Agano River</a> flows through the city. The highest elevation is the summit of Mount Gozu at 912 meters.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agano,_Niigata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of present-day Agano was part of ancient <a href="/wiki/Echigo_Province" title="Echigo Province">Echigo Province</a>. The modern city of Agano was established on April 1, 2004, from the merger of the towns of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suibara,_Niigata" title="Suibara, Niigata">Suibara</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yasuda,_Niigata" title="Yasuda, Niigata">Yasuda</a>, and the villages of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ky%C5%8Dgase,_Niigata" title="Kyōgase, Niigata">Kyōgase</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sasakami,_Niigata" title="Sasakami, Niigata">Sasakami</a> (all from <a href="/wiki/Kitakanbara_District,_Niigata" title="Kitakanbara District, Niigata">Kitakanbara District</a>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agano,_Niigata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Agano has eleven public elementary schools and four public middle schools. There is one public high school, and also one special education school.</p>
