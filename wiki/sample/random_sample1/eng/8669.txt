<p><b>Ageo</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">上尾市</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ageo-shi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Prefecture" title="Saitama Prefecture">Saitama Prefecture</a>, in the central <a href="/wiki/Kant%C5%8D_region" title="Kantō region">Kantō region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of 1 February 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Ageo,_Saitama&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the city had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 224,841 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 4940 persons per km². Its total area was 45.51 square kilometres (17.57 sq mi).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ageo,_Saitama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ageo,_Saitama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ageo-shuku" title="Ageo-shuku">Ageo-shuku</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Shukuba" title="Shukuba">post town</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Nakasend%C5%8D" title="Nakasendō">Nakasendō</a> highway flourished during the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>. Ageo's rail station opened in 1883. In 1889, Ageo-juku merged with the hamlets of Ageoshimo, Kashiwaza, Kasugayatsu and Yatsu to form the town of Ageo with the establishment of the municipalities system on April 1, 1889. The two towns of Hirakata and Haraichi and the three villages of Kamihira, Ōishi and Ōya merged with the town of Ageo on January 1, 1955, which was elevated city status on July 15, 1958. A proposal to merge with the neighboring city of Saitama was rejected by referendum in 2001.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ageo,_Saitama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ageo was a noted agricultural area well into the 20th century. In the second half of the 20th century, numerous metallurgical and automobile factories were constructed, and <a href="/wiki/UD_Trucks" title="UD Trucks">UD Trucks</a> and Bridgestone Cycle remain major employers. Due to its geographic location, Ageo is increasingly a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bedroom_community" title="Bedroom community">bedroom community</a> for Saitama City and Tokyo.</p>
