<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Agrippina</b> may refer to:</p>
<p>In <b>people</b>:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vipsania_Agrippina" title="Vipsania Agrippina">Vipsania Agrippina</a> (36 BC–20), first wife of the Emperor Tiberius, daughter of Caecilia Attica and Marcus Vipsanius Agrippa</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vipsania_Marcella" title="Vipsania Marcella">Vipsania Marcella</a> Agrippina (born 27 BC), daughter of Claudia Marcella Major and first wife of general Publius Quinctilius Varus</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Julia_the_Younger" title="Julia the Younger">Julia the Younger</a> or Vipsania Julia Agrippina (19 BC–28 or early 29), daughter of Julia the Elder and wife of Lucius Aemilius Paullus</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Agrippina_the_Elder" title="Agrippina the Elder">Agrippina the Elder</a> or Vipsania Agrippina (14 BC–33), daughter of Julia the Elder, wife of Germanicus and mother of Emperor Caligula</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Agrippina_the_Younger" title="Agrippina the Younger">Agrippina the Younger</a> or Julia Agrippina (15–59), daughter of Agrippina the Elder and Germanicus, wife of Gnaeus Domitius Ahenobarbus and later Claudius, mother of Nero</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Agrippina_of_Mineo" title="Agrippina of Mineo">Agrippina of Mineo</a>, 3rd-century Christian saint and martyr</li>
<li>Asinia Agrippina, a possible granddaughter of <a href="/wiki/Vipsania_Agrippina" title="Vipsania Agrippina">Vipsania Agrippina</a> and possible second cousin to Agrippina the Younger</li>
<li>Antonia Agrippina, a possible granddaughter of Roman Governor of Judea, <a href="/wiki/Antonius_Felix" title="Antonius Felix">Antonius Felix</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vibullia_Alcia_Agrippina" title="Vibullia Alcia Agrippina">Vibullia Alcia Agrippina</a>, mother of the Greek Senator and sophist Herodes Atticus</li>
<li>Osinia Agrippina, a noble woman who descended from Julius or Iulius Capitolinus, who was one of the biographers of the <a href="/wiki/Augustan_History" title="Augustan History">Augustan History</a></li>
</ul>
<p>In <b>other uses</b>:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Agrippina_(opera)" title="Agrippina (opera)"><i>Agrippina</i> (opera)</a>, an opera by George Frideric Handel</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Agrippina_(film)" title="Agrippina (film)"><i>Agrippina</i> (film)</a>, a 1911 Italian film</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Church_of_Saint_Agrippina" title="Church of Saint Agrippina">Church of Saint Agrippina</a>, a Catholic church founded in Mineo and named after Saint Agrippina of Mineo, who is the patron Saint of the town</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chapel_of_Saint_Agrippina_di_Mineo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chapel of Saint Agrippina di Mineo (page does not exist)">Chapel of Saint Agrippina di Mineo</a>, another Catholic Church located in Boston and named after Saint Agrippina of Mineo</li>
</ul>
