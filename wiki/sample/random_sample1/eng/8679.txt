<p>The <i><b>Anaeroplasmatales</b></i> are an order of <a href="/wiki/Mollicutes" title="Mollicutes">mollicute</a> bacteria which are generally found in the <a href="/wiki/Rumen" title="Rumen">rumens</a> of cattle and sheep. The only family in the order is the family <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anaeroplasmataceae" title="Anaeroplasmataceae">Anaeroplasmataceae</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anaeroplasmatales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Members of the order Anaeroplasmatales can appear as different shapes at different times in their life cycle. Cells which are 16–18 hours old tend to be <a href="/wiki/Coccus" title="Coccus">cocci</a> shaped. By the time the cells are older, they can take on various shapes. Anaeroplasmatales are not motile. Anaeroplasmatales <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anaerobic_bacteria" title="Anaerobic bacteria">cannot grow</a> in the presence of oxygen. They do grow at a temperature of 37 °C on microbiological medium, where they form irregular-shaped colonies with a "fried-egg" appearance, similar to other <a href="/wiki/Mycoplasma" title="Mycoplasma">mycoplasmas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Robinson1987_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Robinson1987-2">[2]</a></sup> Anaeroplasmatales are <a href="/wiki/Gram-negative_bacteria" title="Gram-negative bacteria">negative</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gram_stain" title="Gram stain">Gram stain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Robinson1987_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Robinson1987-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anaeroplasmatales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The order Anaeroplasmatales was created in 1987 to encompass the family Anaeroplasmataceae which itself was created to hold the anaerobic <a href="/wiki/Mycoplasma" title="Mycoplasma">mycoplasmas</a> <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Anaeroplasma&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Anaeroplasma (page does not exist)">Anaeroplasma</a></i> and <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Asteroleplasma&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Asteroleplasma (page does not exist)">Asteroleplasma</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Robinson1987_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Robinson1987-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
