<p><b>Anau</b> (also spelled <b>Annau</b>, <a href="/wiki/Turkmen_language" title="Turkmen language">Turkmen</a>: <span lang="tk" xml:lang="tk"><i><b>Änew</b></i></span>) is a city in. <a href="/wiki/Turkmenistan" title="Turkmenistan">Turkmenistan</a>. It is the capital of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ahal_Province" title="Ahal Province">Ahal Province</a> and is 8 km southeast of <a href="/wiki/Ashgabat" title="Ashgabat">Ashgabat</a> which is connected via the <a href="/wiki/M37_highway_(Turkmenistan)" title="M37 highway (Turkmenistan)">M37 highway</a>. Traces of habitation in the area date back to the 4th millennia BC, with fine painted potteries, and a seal that may contain the earliest evidence of the movement of writing Eastward toward <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Langer_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Langer-2">[2]</a></sup> The name Anau is from <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a> <i>Âbe nav</i> (آب نو) meaning "New Water".<a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.stantours.com/tm_rg_ahal_am.html" rel="nofollow">[1]</a></p>
<p>In 2003, the city built a new stadium and in 2005 a major <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Museum_Ak_bugday&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Museum Ak bugday (page does not exist)">Museum Ak bugday</a>.</p>
