<p><b>Anatoliy Andreyevich Banishevskiy</b> (<a href="/wiki/Azerbaijani_language" title="Azerbaijani language">Azerbaijani</a>: <span lang="az" xml:lang="az"><i>Anatoli Andreyeviç Banişevski</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Анатолий Андреевич Банишевский</span>; 23 February 1946, in <a href="/wiki/Baku" title="Baku">Baku</a> – 10 December 1997, in Baku) was a Soviet <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> of Russian descent from <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>. Throughout most of his playing and coaching career, Banishevskiy was committed to his originally domestic club, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neftchi_Baku_PFC" title="Neftchi Baku PFC">Neftchi Baku</a>. He is widely considered the greatest <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijanis" title="Azerbaijanis">Azerbaijani</a> footballer of all time. He played for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSR_national_football_team" title="USSR national football team">USSR national football team</a>, winning 51 caps and scoring 19 goals. Banishevskiy played for the Soviet side in the <a href="/wiki/1966_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1966 FIFA World Cup">1966 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-azsport_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-azsport-1">[1]</a></sup> His club team was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neftchi_Baku_PFC" title="Neftchi Baku PFC">PFC Neftchi</a>, and he scored 136 goals in <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Top_League" title="Soviet Top League">Soviet Top League</a> competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-azsport_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-azsport-1">[1]</a></sup> The striker was unofficially named Azerbaijan's Player of the Year three times-in 1966, 1967, and 1978.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rsssf_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-rsssf-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2003, as part of the celebration of <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a>'s Jubilee, he was selected as the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Jubilee_Awards" title="UEFA Jubilee Awards">Golden Player</a> for Azerbaijan by the <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Football_Federations_of_Azerbaijan" title="Association of Football Federations of Azerbaijan">Association of Football Federations of Azerbaijan</a> as the country's most outstanding player over the past 50 years.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_career">Playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anatoliy_Banishevskiy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anatoliy_Banishevskiy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Banishevskiy started playing football at the age of 16 and played all of his career for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PFC_Neftchi_Baku" title="PFC Neftchi Baku">PFC Neftchi Baku</a>, transforming into one of the best Azerbaijani players.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xron_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-xron-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Neftchi_Baku">Neftchi Baku</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anatoliy_Banishevskiy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Neftchi Baku">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Upon making the club roster, Banishevskiy immediately transformed into one of the electrifying young superstars of his generation, capturing the nation with his vision, speed and the finishing touch. Banishevskiy maintained his status of a premier Azerbaijani player, and remained very influential football figure throughout his entire football profession and beyond.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anatoliy_Banishevskiy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Banishevskiy made his international debut at 19 years old on 4 July 1965 for <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union_national_football_team" title="Soviet Union national football team">USSR</a> against <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a> during a <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_game" title="Exhibition game">friendly match</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ussr_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ussr-4">[4]</a></sup> His international career ended in <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1972_Final" title="UEFA Euro 1972 Final">final of the 1972 European Championship</a> match loss against <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">West Germany</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">Coaching career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anatoliy_Banishevskiy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Coaching career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After retiring as a player, Banishevskiy briefly coached <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PFC_Neftchi_Baku" title="PFC Neftchi Baku">Neftchi Baku</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kapaz" title="Kapaz">Kapaz</a> and Automobilist Mingachevir. He has also worked as youth coach of <a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso_national_football_team" title="Burkina Faso national football team">Burkina Faso</a> during 1987-1988 period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xron_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-xron-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life_and_death">Later life and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anatoliy_Banishevskiy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Later life and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Banishevskiy was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Diabetic_coma" title="Diabetic coma">diabetic coma</a> in 1991, having surviving first attack in 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ussr_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-ussr-4">[4]</a></sup> He suffered <a href="/wiki/Cerebral_atrophy" title="Cerebral atrophy">cerebral atrophy</a> as result of a second attack, which also caused him <a href="/wiki/Amnesia" title="Amnesia">memory loss</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ussr_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-ussr-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Subsequently, following his wife's ill-timed behavior, Banishevskiy lost the ownership of his house, which led him to live a difficult life in <a href="/wiki/Alcoholism" title="Alcoholism">alcoholism</a> on the streets of Baku.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ussr_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-ussr-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> However, he was rescued from this difficult situation by his old supporter and beloved follower Saida, who cared for him in his last years of his life and ultimately married him.</p>
<p>On 10 December 1997, Banishevskiy passed away after a third <a href="/wiki/Diabetic_coma" title="Diabetic coma">diabetic coma</a> attack, having also suffered <a href="/wiki/Pancreatitis" title="Pancreatitis">pancreatitis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anatoliy_Banishevskiy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His grandson Ali Babayev Banishevskiy began to play in the youth team of Neftchi in 2011 when he was 15. Currently, Ali plays professional football in Azerbaijan's First Division for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FC_Shusha" title="FC Shusha">Shusha FK</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uefa_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-uefa-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
