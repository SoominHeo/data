<p><b>Anja Rubik</b> (born <b>Anna Rubik</b>; 12 June 1983)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-test_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-test-1">[1]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Poles" title="Poles">Polish</a> <a href="/wiki/Model_(person)" title="Model (person)">model</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anja_Rubik&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rubik was born in <a href="/wiki/Rzesz%C3%B3w" title="Rzeszów">Rzeszów</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-test_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-test-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1988 she and her family moved to <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>. They later moved to <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Umtata" title="Umtata">Umtata</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>. Among the schools she attended was one in <a href="/wiki/Cz%C4%99stochowa" title="Częstochowa">Częstochowa</a>, Poland. Rubik was introduced to modeling while attending a British high school in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>. She wanted to finish her schooling before officially becoming a model, and so only worked on school holidays. Her mother, father and sister still reside in Poland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anja_Rubik&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Vogue_Paris" title="Vogue Paris">Vogue Paris</a></i> declared Rubik one of the top 30 models of the 2000s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> As of May 2010, she is ranked 3rd, behind <a href="/wiki/Natasha_Poly" title="Natasha Poly">Natasha Poly</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lara_Stone" title="Lara Stone">Lara Stone</a>, on the <i>Top 50 Models Women</i> list and 18th on the Top 25 <i>Money Girls</i> list, which ranks the highest earning high fashion models in the industry, by <i>models.com</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> As of September 2012, she is ranked as an icon.</p>
<p>Rubik is featured in the 2009 fitness <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video game</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Yoga_Wii" title="Yoga Wii">Yoga Wii</a></i> for the <a href="/wiki/Wii" title="Wii">Wii</a> home console.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She was also an inspiration for a character in the manga "The One" as the main character Cane Lele.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since 2010, she has appeared on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Top_Model._Zosta%C5%84_modelk%C4%85" title="Top Model. Zostań modelką">Top Model. Zostań modelką</a></i>, the Polish edition of <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Next_Top_Model" title="America's Next Top Model">America's Next Top Model</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rubik is featured in the 2011 <a href="/wiki/Pirelli_Calendar" title="Pirelli Calendar">Pirelli Calendar</a> photographed by <a href="/wiki/Karl_Lagerfeld" title="Karl Lagerfeld">Karl Lagerfeld</a>. She was one of the models to star in <a href="/wiki/Lacoste" title="Lacoste">Lacoste</a>'s "new look" campaign in January, a different advertising concept for 2011 under the new tagline, "Unconventional Chic". The ads were shot by <a href="/wiki/Mert_and_Marcus" title="Mert and Marcus">Mert and Marcus</a>, showing models wearing the iconic white Lacoste polo shirts worn over fancy black evening wear.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Rubik renewed her contract with <a href="/wiki/Fendi" title="Fendi">Fendi</a> for the third season in a row.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In 2013 she was ranked third on <i>Vogue'</i>s "Top 10 Models of 2013" list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 2014 she became the host and a judge of the Polish version of the reality show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Project_Runway_TVN" title="Project Runway TVN">Project Runway TVN</a></i>.</p>
<p>She is a favourite model of <a href="/wiki/Karl_Lagerfeld" title="Karl Lagerfeld">Karl Lagerfeld</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rubik has appeared on the covers of Italian, French, Spanish, German, Russian, Portuguese, Turkish, Japanese, Chinese, Korean, Mexican, and Australian <i><a href="/wiki/Vogue_(magazine)" title="Vogue (magazine)">Vogue</a></i>, British, German, and Polish <i><a href="/wiki/Elle_(magazine)" title="Elle (magazine)">Elle</a></i>, German and Polish <i><a href="/wiki/Harper%27s_Bazaar" title="Harper's Bazaar">Harper's Bazaar</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/V_(American_magazine)" title="V (American magazine)">V</a></i>, American and French <i><a href="/wiki/Num%C3%A9ro" title="Numéro">Numéro</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Garage_Magazine" title="Garage Magazine">Garage</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/I-D" title="I-D">i-D</a></i>.</p>

<p>In 2012, Rubik founded and created 25 Magazine. Contributors include <a href="/wiki/Inez_and_Vinoodh" title="Inez and Vinoodh">Inez and Vinoodh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marina_Abramovi%C4%87" title="Marina Abramović">Marina Abramović</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nobuyoshi_Araki" title="Nobuyoshi Araki">Nobuyoshi Araki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Arsham" title="Daniel Arsham">Daniel Arsham</a>, Paola Kudacki, <a href="/wiki/Alex_Prager" title="Alex Prager">Alex Prager</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hedi_Slimane" title="Hedi Slimane">Hedi Slimane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isabel_Marant" title="Isabel Marant">Isabel Marant</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yoann_Lemoine" title="Yoann Lemoine">Yoann Lemoine</a>. In September 2015, Rubik made her directorial debut by directing Issue 04's teaser film featuring <a href="/wiki/Andreea_Diaconu" title="Andreea Diaconu">Andreea Diaconu</a> and themed around the power and impact of the five senses. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, Rubik starred in a music video for the song <i>Chleb</i> by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mister_D.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mister D. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anja_Rubik&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rubik and her longtime boyfriend, <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbian</a> model Sasha Knezevic, became engaged just before <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> 2010 in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> They married on 16 July 2011 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deia" title="Deia">Deia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Majorca" title="Majorca">Majorca</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In October 2015, Anja confirmed that they have separated<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and later divorced.</p>
