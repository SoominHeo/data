<p><b>Anoeta</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Multi-purpose_stadium" title="Multi-purpose stadium">multi-purpose stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Sebasti%C3%A1n" title="San Sebastián">San Sebastián</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basque_Autonomous_Community" title="Basque Autonomous Community">Basque Autonomous Community</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> that was inaugurated in 1993. The stadium lies at the <a href="/wiki/Anoeta_Sports_Complex" title="Anoeta Sports Complex">Anoeta Sports Complex</a>, and is mostly used for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> matches where <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> side <a href="/wiki/Real_Sociedad" title="Real Sociedad">Real Sociedad</a> contest their home games.</p>
<p>In recent years, it has also been used for occasional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heineken_Cup" title="Heineken Cup">Heineken Cup</a> <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a> fixtures by nearby <a href="/wiki/Top_14" title="Top 14">French</a> Basque club <a href="/wiki/Biarritz_Olympique" title="Biarritz Olympique">Biarritz Olympique</a>. Since the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Top_14_season" title="2009–10 Top 14 season">2009–10 Top 14 season</a>, both <a href="/wiki/Biarritz_Olympique" title="Biarritz Olympique">Biarritz Olympique</a> and fellow <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Basque_Country" title="Northern Basque Country">Basque</a> club <a href="/wiki/Aviron_Bayonnais" title="Aviron Bayonnais">Aviron Bayonnais</a> took home matches to the Anoeta. Also, in a generous gesture from Real Sociedad, Basque arch rivals, <a href="/wiki/Athletic_Bilbao" title="Athletic Bilbao">Athletic Bilbao</a>, used Anoeta for their first two home games of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2013-14_La_Liga" title="2013-14 La Liga">2013-14 La Liga</a> season, as their new ground <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Mames_Stadium_(2013)" title="San Mames Stadium (2013)">San Mames Stadium</a> was still under construction.</p>
<p>The total seating capacity of the stadium is 32,300.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposals">Proposals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anoeta_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Proposals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2004, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Luis_Astiazar%C3%A1n" title="José Luis Astiazarán">José Luis Astiazarán</a>, then the president of Real Sociedad, launched a project called Gipuzkoarena.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Planned to be completed in 2007, it was intended to increase the stadium's capacity to 42,000, which would include the removal of the athletics track. The project also intended to construct a hotel and shops, among other items. This proposal was soon rejected by the city council and left behind.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Towards the end of 2007, Badiola, as candidate for president of Real Sociedad, proposed that the club buy the stadium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> This was also soon rejected by the city council.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2008, Badiola, already as president, made another two proposals for the stadium,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> one of them similar to Gipuzkoarena. These proposals were going to be studied by the city council, but after 20 December Badiola was no longer the club's president. Later, the next president, Jokin Aperribay, launched a new attempt to reform the stadium.</p>
<p>In 2015 the club announced that it would begin reforms on Anoeta in 2016. The stadium reforms will cost 30 million euros and will enlarge the stadium as well as removing the track around the field.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Complaints">Complaints</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anoeta_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Complaints">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Over the years, fans have never been happy with the stadium due to the athletics track causing a distance between the stands and the field itself, resulting in a "cold" atmosphere during matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
