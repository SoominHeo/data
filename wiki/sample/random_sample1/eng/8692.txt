<p><b>Anita Tsoy</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Анита "Анна" Серге́евна Цой</span>; born <b>Anna Sergeyevna Kim</b>; 7 February 1971, <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> singer-songwriter of <a href="/wiki/Koreans" title="Koreans">Korean</a> descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NI_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NI-1">[1]</a></sup> She is a <a href="/wiki/Meritorious_Artist" title="Meritorious Artist">Meritorious Artist</a> of Russia (2003).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anita_Tsoy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Anita's grandfather, seeking free education, emigrated from <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Far_East" title="Russian Far East">Russian Far East</a>. He, his wife and his children were later caught up in the <a href="/wiki/Deportation_of_Koreans_in_the_Soviet_Union" title="Deportation of Koreans in the Soviet Union">1937 deportation</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Koryo-saram" title="Koryo-saram">Koryo-saram</a></i> to <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Anita's mother was born in 1944 in <a href="/wiki/Tashkent" title="Tashkent">Tashkent</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uzbek_SSR" title="Uzbek SSR">Uzbek SSR</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> and successfully graduated from the <a href="/wiki/Moscow_State_University" title="Moscow State University">Moscow State University</a> and became a chemistry <a href="/wiki/Doctor_(title)" title="Doctor (title)">doctor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rusradio_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-rusradio-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tsoy's mother did everything she could to make her daughter educated in all kinds of arts <a class="external autonumber" href="http://anitatsoy.ru" rel="nofollow">[2]</a>. Anita's favourite one became music. She began <a href="/wiki/Violin" title="Violin">violin</a> lessons at a young age; she later studied <a href="/wiki/Piano" title="Piano">piano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flute" title="Flute">flute</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>When Tsoy became an adult, her mother was branded a "<a href="/wiki/Enemy_of_the_people" title="Enemy of the people">class enemy</a>" in the 1970s in response to her record of speaking out in support of <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Peace_Prize" title="Nobel Peace Prize">Nobel Peace Prize</a>-winning dissident <a href="/wiki/Andrei_Sakharov" title="Andrei Sakharov">Andrei Sakharov</a>. For her dissent, she was briefly committed to a mental institution and became a permanent invalid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> After leaving school Tsoy attended the <a href="/wiki/Normal_school" title="Normal school">teacher training college</a>, then law school at <a href="/wiki/Moscow_State_University" title="Moscow State University">Moscow State University</a>, where she met her husband <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Tsoy" title="Sergey Tsoy">Sergey Tsoy</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anita_Tsoy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After graduation, rather than becoming a teacher or lawyer, she began saving up money in order to launch a singing career, while her husband began working as a press secretary for <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> mayor <a href="/wiki/Yury_Luzhkov" title="Yury Luzhkov">Yury Luzhkov</a> in 1994. Her first album <i>Polyot</i> (Полёт; <i>Flight</i>) was released in 1997; she kept it a secret from her husband until after she had already signed the contract with her production company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NI_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-NI-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sergey_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sergey-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1998 her second album, named "Черный лебедь" ("The Black Swan") was released and in 1999 a concert show "Черный лебедь или храм любви" ("The Black Swan or a Love Palace") was held in the concert hall "Rossiya". This show was accepted as "Best Show of the Year" and Anita won an "Ovation" She went to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> in 2003 to record an <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English-language</a> album, where she worked with cinematic composer <a href="/wiki/Lee_Holdridge" title="Lee Holdridge">Lee Holdridge</a>; while there, she was offered a five-year contract as a solo vocalist in the <a href="/wiki/Cirque_du_Soleil" title="Cirque du Soleil">Cirque du Soleil</a>, but turned it down in order to return to Russia and be near her family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>She records her third Russian dance solo-album "1000000 минут" ("1000000 Minutes") in 2003, which was introduced to public in the "Elevator" club, a former factory in Moscow. In 2005 a major show-concert "ANITA" was held in the "Rossiya" Concert Hall, Moscow, featuring high technology decorations like large LCD-screens and fountains on the stage. The show was considered to be one of the most successful ones in Russia and was released on DVD by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Music" title="Universal Music">Universal Music</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Tsoy recorded her fourth album "На восток" ("To The East") in 2007 and changed her image greatly since the 2003; she turns to <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a>-<a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> style. In autumn of the same year she performs with her "To The East" show, which attracted more than ten thousand people. Following the show, the DVD-recording of this live concert was released.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Both "ANITA" and "To The East" shows are considered to be one of the best live-performances in Russia and the recordings of these concerts were shown on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Channel_One_(Russia)" title="Channel One (Russia)">Channel One</a>, <a href="/wiki/Muz-TV" title="Muz-TV">Muz-TV</a> and other influentable Russian channels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Anita recorded 5 albums in total and won more than 10 major awards for both performing and charity during her 13-year career as a solo artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-russianlyrics1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-russianlyrics1-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rusradio1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-rusradio1-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary_of_music_career">Summary of music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anita_Tsoy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Summary of music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tsoy is known for regularly changing her music style and image. She began performing as a nearly <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> artist in 1997, later turned to dance music, and after 2006 several music styles present in her songs: <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Tsoi" title="Viktor Tsoi">Viktor Tsoi</a>'s rock followed by <a href="/wiki/Blues" title="Blues">blues</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%26B" title="R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a>, and Eastern styled <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1997_.E2.80.93_In_memory_of_Viktor_Tsoi">1997 – In memory of Viktor Tsoi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anita_Tsoy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1997 – In memory of Viktor Tsoi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first album "Полет" ('Flight") was recorded in 1997 and was named after Anita's first hit – single "Полет" ('Flight"). Most songs from it are written in <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> style, similar to the <a href="/wiki/Kino_(band)" title="Kino (band)">Kino</a> band's where <a href="/wiki/Acoustic_guitar" title="Acoustic guitar">acoustic guitar</a> plays the main role. This is because Anita was a great fan of theirs when she was young.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Another hit – "Мама"("Mother") – was dedicated to orphaned children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id=".22The_Black_Swan.22_of_Russian_pop">"The Black Swan" of Russian pop</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anita_Tsoy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title='Edit section: "The Black Swan" of Russian pop'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1998<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rusradio_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-rusradio-4">[4]</a></sup> Anita's second album was released, called "The Black Swan" ("Черный лебедь"). Notable songs from this record are: "Far Away"("Далеко") and "Return"("Возвращайтесь") The second one was remixed in 2009 and became a key – single for the singer's "To Remember so that Life Continues" ("Помнить, чтобы жизнь продолжалась") <a href="/wiki/Charity_(practice)" title="Charity (practice)">charity</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Concert_tour" title="Concert tour">concert tour</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-src1_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-src1-15">[15]</a></sup> In this album's tracks mostly <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Percussion" title="Percussion">percussion</a> instruments take place and most songs are written in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pop-rock" title="Pop-rock">pop-rock</a> style.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rusradio1_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-rusradio1-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2003_.E2.80.93_Switch_to_dance_music">2003 – Switch to dance music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anita_Tsoy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2003 – Switch to dance music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2003 Anita recorded her third <a href="/wiki/Russians" title="Russians">Russian</a> album, called "1000000 Minutes" ("1000000 минут"). Her first two records and this one can't be compared at all – almost all songs here are composed in a <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">Dance</a> style. This album has four singles which became super – hits on radiostations: a title – track "1000000 Minutes" ("Миллион минут"), "Only for You" ("Одному тебе"), "Is that Love?" ("Это ли любовь?") and "Christmas Toys" ("Новогодние игрушки").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007_.E2.80.93_To_The_East">2007 – To The East</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anita_Tsoy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2007 – To The East">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 2003 till 2007 there were no recordings released due to large <a href="/wiki/Piracy" title="Piracy">piracy</a> problems on the <a href="/wiki/Mass_media" title="Mass media">media</a> <a href="/wiki/Market_(economics)" title="Market (economics)">market</a> in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>The album "To The East" ("На восток") was recorded in 2007 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Music" title="Universal Music">Universal Music</a> company. It is filled inside out with hit singles: seven of eleven tracks were tuned on radios and two of them: the title song "To The East" ("На восток") and "The Sky" ("Небо") became very popular among <a href="/wiki/Russians" title="Russians">Russian</a> people; Anita won two "Golden Gramophone" statuettes for these singles: one in 2006 and the other one – in 2007 (The "To The East" ("На восток") single had been written a year earlier before the album was recorded). Combinations of <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitar</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Percussion" title="Percussion">percussion</a> instruments and electronic <a href="/wiki/Music" title="Music">music</a> made this album's songs well distinguished from other artists' ones. Anita and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Music" title="Universal Music">Universal Music</a> announced that all the income from the album sales was going to be delivered to orphan asylums.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Charity">Charity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anita_Tsoy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Charity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aside from singing Anita Tsoy is known as an active contributor to charity. In 2001 she opened a charity foundation "Anita", which helped many children with congenital disablement <a class="external autonumber" href="http://anitatsoy.ru" rel="nofollow">[3]</a>. She also works with such major charity companies as <a href="/wiki/Save_the_Children" title="Save the Children">Save the Children</a> and <a href="/wiki/UNICEF" title="UNICEF">UNICEF</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009 a concert tour was organized and all the profits from the concerts were sent to families, whose relatives died due to terrorist actions and coal mines collapses. The concerts were held in the cities of <a href="/wiki/Grozny" title="Grozny">Grozny</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beslan" title="Beslan">Beslan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Budyonnovsk" title="Budyonnovsk">Budyonnovsk</a> and many others, located in Caucasian and Siberian areas. The tour was named "To Remember so that Life Continues" ("Помнить, чтобы жизнь продолжалась").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Several months before in the Barvikha Hall, <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> a big concert was arranged, where mostly businessmen and celebrities had been invited to. The income was about €160,000 – this amount of money was delivered to more than 50 children who became victims of the Chechen massacre in <a href="/wiki/Beslan" title="Beslan">Beslan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-src1_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-src1-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_career">Current career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anita_Tsoy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Current career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Anita Tsoy was appointed as an "Ambassador of Korean Culture and Tourism" between Russia and South Korea by <a href="/wiki/President_of_South_Korea" title="President of South Korea">South Korean president</a> <a href="/wiki/Roh_Moo-hyun" title="Roh Moo-hyun">Roh Moo-hyun</a> during his official visit to Russia in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was announced on the singer's official site that a new album, called "Глубина"("Depth") is going to be recorded. The release date is not mentioned. Anita Tsoy is going to play in the upcoming "<a href="/wiki/Michael_Strogoff" title="Michael Strogoff">Michael Strogoff</a>" French musical in 2010 – 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
