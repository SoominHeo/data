<p><i><b>Arthur and the War of the Two Worlds</b></i> (French: <i><b>Arthur et la guerre des deux mondes</b></i>, also known in North America as <i><b>Arthur and the Invisibles 3</b></i> or simply <i><b>Arthur 3</b></i>) is a 2010 <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>-language <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Films_with_live_action_and_animation" title="Films with live action and animation">animated/live-action feature film</a> co-written, co-produced and directed by <a href="/wiki/Luc_Besson" title="Luc Besson">Luc Besson</a>, based on his novel of the same title and starring <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Highmore" title="Freddie Highmore">Freddie Highmore</a> and the other actors that reprise for the movie. <a href="/wiki/EuropaCorp" title="EuropaCorp">EuropaCorp</a> produced the film, which is the third in Besson's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arthur_series_(Besson)" title="Arthur series (Besson)">Arthur series</a>, following <i><a href="/wiki/Arthur_and_the_Invisibles" title="Arthur and the Invisibles">Arthur and the Invisibles</a></i> and continuing <i><a href="/wiki/Arthur_and_the_Revenge_of_Maltazard" title="Arthur and the Revenge of Maltazard">Arthur and the Revenge of Maltazard</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur_3:_The_War_of_the_Two_Worlds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the second film of the series, Maltazard has assumed human size, and left Arthur in miniature. Accompanied by Selenia and Betameche, Arthur attempts to retrieve an enlarging potion from his house, which Maltazard seizes to enlarge his followers, whereafter Arthur returns to human form using an Elixir of Life given by a queen bee. Archibald convinces Darkos, Maltazard's son, to change sides, and enlarges him with a second potion. Arthur and Darkos then confront Maltazard, until Selenia and Betameche shrink Maltazard back to his Minimoy size and Arthur captures him, while the U.S. Army overcome Maltazard's forces. Maltazard thereafter remains a prisoner of Arthur's family.</p>
<p>During the credits Darkos sings <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Rebel_Rebel" title="Rebel Rebel">Rebel Rebel</a>".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur_3:_The_War_of_the_Two_Worlds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur_3:_The_War_of_the_Two_Worlds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Arthur and the War of the Two Worlds</i> received mixed to negative reviews. One critic deemed the film "A mild improvement over its two predecessors" and gave the film a score of 2/5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">Review aggregator</a> website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> reported a 20% approval rating based on 5 reviews, with a rating average of 4.8/10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="US_release">US release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur_3:_The_War_of_the_Two_Worlds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: US release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Arthur and the War of Two Worlds</i> was not released theatrically in the US, due to the poor box office showing of <i><a href="/wiki/Arthur_and_the_Invisibles" title="Arthur and the Invisibles">Arthur and the Invisibles</a></i>. Instead, <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox_Home_Entertainment" title="20th Century Fox Home Entertainment">20th Century Fox Home Entertainment</a> released it together with <i>Arthur and the Revenge of Maltazard</i> as a two-movie <a href="/wiki/Direct-to-video" title="Direct-to-video">direct-to-video</a> set on March 22, 2011, under the title <i>Arthur and the Invisibles 2+3: The New Minimoy Adventures</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="UK_release">UK release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur_3:_The_War_of_the_Two_Worlds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: UK release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shortly after the film's initial release, it was edited together with <i><a href="/wiki/Arthur_and_the_Revenge_of_Maltazard" title="Arthur and the Revenge of Maltazard">Arthur and the Revenge of Maltazard</a></i> into a shortened (107 minutes) single sequel film. This version, under the title <i><b>Arthur and the Great Adventure</b></i>, was theatrically released on December 24, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
