<p><b>Ado Grenzstein</b>, pseudonym <b>A. Piirikivi</b> (February 5, 1849 - April 20, 1916) was an Estonian journalist, writer and teacher, brother of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=T%C3%B5nis_Grenzstein&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tõnis Grenzstein (page does not exist)">Tõnis Grenzstein</a>. In 1881 he founded the <i><a href="/wiki/Olevik" title="Olevik">Olevik</a></i> newspaper, which become one of the most important Estonian newspapers of the period. The purpose of the paper was "weaning the Estonian peasant readership away from the 'firebrands and madcaps' who edited <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sakala,_Ethiopia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sakala, Ethiopia (page does not exist)">Sakala</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kirby2014_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kirby2014-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1901 he left Estonia and settled first in <a href="/wiki/Dresden" title="Dresden">Dresden</a>, and then later in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Salupere2005_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Salupere2005-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
