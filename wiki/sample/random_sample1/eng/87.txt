<p><br/>
Year <b>1461</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MCDLXI</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Thursday" title="Common year starting on Thursday">common year starting on Thursday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1461&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93December">January–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1461&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: January–December">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_2" title="February 2">February 2</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mortimer%27s_Cross" title="Battle of Mortimer's Cross">Battle of Mortimer's Cross</a>: Yorkist troops led by <a href="/wiki/Edward_IV_of_England" title="Edward IV of England">Edward, Duke of York</a> defeat Lancastrians under <a href="/wiki/Owen_Tudor" title="Owen Tudor">Owen Tudor</a> and his son <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jasper_Tudor,_Earl_of_Pembroke" title="Jasper Tudor, Earl of Pembroke">Jasper Tudor, Earl of Pembroke</a> in Wales.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_17" title="February 17">February 17</a> – <a href="/wiki/Second_Battle_of_St_Albans" title="Second Battle of St Albans">Second Battle of St Albans</a>, England: The <a href="/wiki/Richard_Neville,_16th_Earl_of_Warwick" title="Richard Neville, 16th Earl of Warwick">Earl of Warwick</a>'s army is defeated by a Lancastrian force under <a href="/wiki/Margaret_of_Anjou" title="Margaret of Anjou">Queen Margaret</a>, who recovers control of her husband.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_4" title="March 4">March 4</a> – The Duke of York seizes London and proclaims himself King <a href="/wiki/Edward_IV_of_England" title="Edward IV of England">Edward IV of England</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_5" title="March 5">March 5</a> – <a href="/wiki/Henry_VI_of_England" title="Henry VI of England">Henry VI of England</a> is deposed by <a href="/wiki/Edward_IV_of_England" title="Edward IV of England">Edward, Duke of York</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Wars_of_the_Roses" title="Wars of the Roses">Wars of the Roses</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_29" title="March 29">March 29</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Towton" title="Battle of Towton">Battle of Towton</a>: Edward IV defeats Queen Margaret to make good his claim to the English throne (thought to be the bloodiest battle ever fought in England).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_10" title="July 10">July 10</a> – <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Toma%C5%A1evi%C4%87_of_Bosnia" title="Stephen Tomašević of Bosnia">Stephen Tomašević</a> becomes last <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_of_Bosnia" title="King of Bosnia">King of Bosnia</a> on the death of his father <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stephen_Thomas_of_Bosnia" title="Stephen Thomas of Bosnia">Stephen Thomas</a>; he is crowned on November 17 in <a href="/wiki/Saint_Mary%27s_Church,_Jajce" title="Saint Mary's Church, Jajce">Saint Mary's Church, Jajce</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_28" title="June 28">June 28</a> – Edward, Richard of York's son, is crowned as <a href="/wiki/Edward_IV_of_England" title="Edward IV of England">Edward IV</a>, King of England (reigns until <a href="/wiki/1483" title="1483">1483</a>).</li>
<li>July – <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine</a> general <a href="/wiki/Graitzas_Palaiologos" title="Graitzas Palaiologos">Graitzas Palaiologos</a> honourably surrenders <a href="/wiki/Salmeniko_Castle" title="Salmeniko Castle">Salmeniko Castle</a>, last garrison of the <a href="/wiki/Despotate_of_the_Morea" title="Despotate of the Morea">Despotate of the Morea</a>, to invading forces of the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> after a year-long siege.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_22" title="July 22">July 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/Louis_XI_of_France" title="Louis XI of France">Louis XI of France</a> succeeds <a href="/wiki/Charles_VII_of_France" title="Charles VII of France">Charles VII of France</a> as king (reigns until <a href="/wiki/1483" title="1483">1483</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_7" title="August 7">August 7</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Ming_dynasty" title="Ming dynasty">Ming dynasty</a> Chinese military general <a href="/wiki/Rebellion_of_Cao_Qin" title="Rebellion of Cao Qin">Cao Qin stages a coup</a> against the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zhengtong_Emperor" title="Zhengtong Emperor">Tianshun Emperor</a>; after setting fire to the eastern and western gates of the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_City,_Beijing" title="Imperial City, Beijing">Imperial City, Beijing</a> (which are doused by pouring rains during the day-long uprising), Cao Qin finds himself hemmed in on all sides by imperial forces, loses three of his own brothers in the fight, and instead of facing execution he flees to his home in the city and commits suicide by jumping down a well located within his walled compound.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_15" title="August 15">August 15</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Trebizond" title="Empire of Trebizond">Empire of Trebizond</a>, the last major Romano-Greek outpost, falls to the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mehmed_II" title="Mehmed II">Mehmed II</a>, after a <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Trebizond_(1461)" title="Siege of Trebizond (1461)">21-day siege</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_26" title="November 26">November 26</a> – Severe earthquake in <a href="/wiki/L%27Aquila" title="L'Aquila">L'Aquila</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Date_unknown">Date unknown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1461&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Date unknown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cirencester_Grammar_School" title="Cirencester Grammar School">Cirencester Grammar School</a> is founded in south-west England by the <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Durham" title="Bishop of Durham">Bishop of Durham</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Leonardo_da_Vinci" title="Leonardo da Vinci">Leonardo da Vinci</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sandro_Botticelli" title="Sandro Botticelli">Sandro Botticelli</a> become students of <a href="/wiki/Andrea_del_Verrocchio" title="Andrea del Verrocchio">Verrocchio</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sarajevo" title="Sarajevo">Sarajevo</a>, capital of <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a>, is founded by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ottomans" title="Ottomans">Ottomans</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Villon" title="François Villon">François Villon</a> writes <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Le_Grand_Testament" title="Le Grand Testament">Le Grand Testament</a></i>.</li>
