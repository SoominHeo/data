<p><i><b>Colorful Pop</b></i> is the second <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> super <a href="/wiki/Girl_group" title="Girl group">girl group</a> <a href="/wiki/E-girls" title="E-girls">E-girls</a>. It was released on March 19, 2014 in two different editions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-release_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-release-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colorful_Pop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>E-girls announced their second album before the release of "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diamond_Only" title="Diamond Only">Diamond Only</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The album's covers, track list and release date were later confirmed after the group released the PV for the album's promotional single, "RYDEEN ~Dance All Night~".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The album features five previously unreleased songs, including pre-released single "RYDEEN ~Dance All Night~", while the rest of the album featured songs previously released as singles or b-sides. The album also includes a Rock Version of the group's single "Follow Me" from their debut album, "<a href="/wiki/Lesson_1_(E-girls_album)" title="Lesson 1 (E-girls album)">Lesson 1</a>".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colorful_Pop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Three songs from the album were released as official singles, with one promotional single:</p>
<ul>
<li>"<a href="/wiki/Gomennasai_no_Kissing_You" title="Gomennasai no Kissing You">Gomennasai no Kissing You</a>" is the first single from the album and sixth overall. The song was used as the theme song for movie <i>Shazai no Osama</i>. It was released digitally on September 25, 2013 and physically on October 2, 2013. It features vocals from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dream_(Japanese_band)" title="Dream (Japanese band)">Dream</a>'s Shizuka and Ami and <a href="/wiki/Flower_(Japanese_band)" title="Flower (Japanese band)">Flower</a>'s Reina. The music video featured all members of E-girls alongside over 500 <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=EXPG&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="EXPG (page does not exist)">EXPG</a> trainees. The single sold 73,332 in its first week and is currently E-girls best selling single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>"<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurukuru_(song)" title="Kurukuru (song)">Kurukuru</a>" is the second single from the album and seventh overall. It was released digitally on November 13, 2013 and physically a week later. It features vocals from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dream_(Japanese_band)" title="Dream (Japanese band)">Dream</a>'s Shizuka and Ami, <a href="/wiki/Flower_(Japanese_band)" title="Flower (Japanese band)">Flower</a>'s Reina and <a href="/wiki/Happiness_(Japanese_band)" title="Happiness (Japanese band)">Happiness</a>'s Karen. The single sold 69,087 in its first week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>"<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diamond_Only" title="Diamond Only">Diamond Only</a>" is the third single from the album and eighth overall. The song was used as the theme song for a Japanese drama named <i>Koibumi Biyori</i>. It was released digitally on September 25, 2013 and physically on October 2, 2013 in four editions: CD Only Version, CD+DVD Version, One Coin CD Version and Music Card editions for each member. It features vocals from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dream_(Japanese_band)" title="Dream (Japanese band)">Dream</a>'s Shizuka, Aya and Ami, <a href="/wiki/Flower_(Japanese_band)" title="Flower (Japanese band)">Flower</a>'s Reina and <a href="/wiki/Happiness_(Japanese_band)" title="Happiness (Japanese band)">Happiness</a>'s Karen. The single sold 72,808 in its first week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>"Rydeen ~Dance All Night~" is a promotional single released prior to the album on March 5, 2014. It features vocals from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dream_(Japanese_band)" title="Dream (Japanese band)">Dream</a>'s Shizuka and Erie, <a href="/wiki/Flower_(Japanese_band)" title="Flower (Japanese band)">Flower</a>'s Reina and <a href="/wiki/Happiness_(Japanese_band)" title="Happiness (Japanese band)">Happiness</a>'s Karen and samples <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Magic_Orchestra" title="Yellow Magic Orchestra">Yellow Magic Orchestra</a>'s "Rydeen".</li>
</ul>
