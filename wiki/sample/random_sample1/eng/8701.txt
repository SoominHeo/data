<p><i><b>Message to Adolf</b></i>, known in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> as <i><b>Adolf ni Tsugu</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">アドルフに告ぐ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Adorufu ni Tsugu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, literally: "<i>Reporting to Adolf</i>")</span> and known in earlier English versions as <i><b>Adolf</b></i>, is a <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series made by <a href="/wiki/Osamu_Tezuka" title="Osamu Tezuka">Osamu Tezuka</a>. The story is set before <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> and is centered on three men with the name Adolf. Adolf Kamil is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ashkenazi_Jew" title="Ashkenazi Jew">Ashkenazi Jew</a> living in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. His best friend Adolf Kaufmann is of both Japanese and German descent. The third Adolf is <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a>, the dictator of <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Germany</a>. <i>Adolf</i> also features Sohei Toge, a Japanese reporter. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vertical,_Inc." title="Vertical, Inc.">Vertical, Inc.</a> currently publishes the series in English with Kumar Sivasubramanian as the translator,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Viz_Media" title="Viz Media">Viz Media</a> formerly published the series in English.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Message_to_Adolf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The story of <i>Adolf</i> begins in 1936 as Japanese reporter Sohei Toge travels to <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> to cover the <a href="/wiki/1936_Summer_Olympics" title="1936 Summer Olympics">Berlin Olympic Games</a>. Upon arriving, he finds that his younger brother, who has been studying in Germany as an international student, has been murdered and had connections with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communist" title="Communist">Communist</a> organisations. Furthermore, all traces of information regarding his younger brother's study in Germany has vanished. It was as if he had never existed. Investigating the matter, it is later learned that his murder is connected to a document he mailed to Japan with information regarding Adolf Hitler. This information is crucial to the Third Reich as it contains proof that Adolf Hitler has Jewish blood.</p>
<p>A member of the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Party" title="Nazi Party">Nazi Party</a> living in Japan named Wolfgang Kaufmann is ordered to find the documents. He expects his son, Adolf Kaufmann, to become a strong supporter of Adolf Hitler. However, Adolf Kaufmann has no interest in Nazi Germany as it would mean the killing of his good friend, Adolf Kamil, who is Jewish.</p>
<p>As events progress, the lives of the three Adolfs intertwine and become more and more tangled as Sohei Toge searches for his brother's murderer.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Did_Adolf_Hitler_have_Jewish_ancestry.3F">Did Adolf Hitler have Jewish ancestry?</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Message_to_Adolf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Did Adolf Hitler have Jewish ancestry?">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This rumor plays a central role in this story. It concerns one of the most frequently asked question about Hitler's ancestry. During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuremberg_Trials" title="Nuremberg Trials">Nuremberg Trials</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hans_Frank" title="Hans Frank">Hans Frank</a>, a Nazi awaiting the gallows, made the claim that he had found evidence that Hitler's father <a href="/wiki/Alois_Hitler" title="Alois Hitler">Alois</a> was an illegitimate child of Hitler's paternal grandmother, <a href="/wiki/Maria_Schicklgruber" title="Maria Schicklgruber">Maria Anna Schicklgruber</a> and a teenage son of her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jew" title="Jew">Jewish</a> employer Leopold Frankenberger, when she worked as a cook for the Frankenberger family in <a href="/wiki/Graz" title="Graz">Graz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>. Given that all Jews had been expelled from the province of Styria (which includes Graz) in the 15th century and were not allowed to return until the 1860s, there is no evidence of a Frankenberger family living in Graz at that time. Scholars such as <a href="/wiki/Ian_Kershaw" title="Ian Kershaw">Ian Kershaw</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brigitte_Hamann" title="Brigitte Hamann">Brigitte Hamann</a> dismiss the Frankenberger hypothesis (which had only Frank's speculation to support it) as baseless. Frank's story contains several inaccuracies and contradictions, such as he said "The fact that Adolf Hitler had no Jewish blood in his veins, had, from what has been his whole manner so blatant that it needs no further word", also the statement Frank had said that Maria Schicklgruber came from "Leonding near Linz", when in fact she came from the hamlet of Strones, near the village of Döllersheim.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Message_to_Adolf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><b>Sohei Toge <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">峠 草平</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tōge Sōhei</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>:</b> A Japanese reporter sent to Germany to cover the 1936 Olympics only to find his younger brother murdered. He then proceeds to investigate who killed his brother and why, leading him into a dangerous web of espionage during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. He was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Niihari_District,_Ibaraki" title="Niihari District, Ibaraki">Niihari, Ibaraki</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Tsuchiura" title="Tsuchiura">Tsuchiura</a>).</li>
<li><b>Isao Toge <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">峠 勲</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tōge Isao</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>:</b> A Japanese international student studying in Germany and a member of the communist party. When his organisation discovers a shocking secret, he is brutally murdered.</li>
<li><b>Adolf Kaufmann <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">アドルフ・カウフマン</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Adorufu Kaufuman</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>:</b> A half-Japanese, half-German boy living in Kobe and who has no real interest in the Nazi Party at first, but later joined <a href="/wiki/Sicherheitsdienst" title="Sicherheitsdienst">Sicherheitsdienst</a>, then <a href="/wiki/Gestapo" title="Gestapo">Gestapo</a>.</li>
<li><b>Wolfgang Kaufmann <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ヴォルフガング・カウフマン</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Vorufugangu Kaufuman</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>:</b> Adolf Kaufmann's father and a strong follower of Adolf Hitler. He works for the German Consulate General in Kobe.</li>
<li><b>Yukie Kaufmann <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">由季江・カウフマン</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yukie Kaufuman</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>:</b> Wolfgang's wife and mother to Adolf. She is unaware of what her husband is doing for the Nazi Party.</li>
<li><b>Adolf Kamil <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">アドルフ・カミル</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Adorufu Kamiru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>:</b> A Jewish-German boy who considers himself Japanese, and who accidentally learns the secret behind Adolf Hitler's ancestry.</li>
<li><b>Isaac Kamil:</b> Adolf Kamil's father and a Jewish man who seeks to actively help other Jews around the world. He was murdered by Adolf Kaufmann while in Germany.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Acetylene_Lamp&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Acetylene Lamp (page does not exist)">Acetylene Lamp</a>:</b> A member of the Nazi Party and the Far East Chief of German Intelligence.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a>:</b> The German dictator himself.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Richard_Sorge" title="Richard Sorge">Richard Sorge</a>:</b> A Communist Russian spy who existed outside of fiction and plays a prominent role towards the end of the story.</li>
<li><b>Eliza Gerd Hymer <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">エリザ・ゲルトハイマー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Eriza Gerudo Haimā</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>:</b> A Chinese Jewish girl living in Germany. She escapes and leaves her family to live with her fiance Adolf Kamil. Kaufmann rapes her. Kamil's mother was killed by an American h-bomb plane. Today, Eliza is the widow of Adolf Kamil and lives in Israel.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Message_to_Adolf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Adolf</i> won the <a href="/wiki/Kodansha_Manga_Award" title="Kodansha Manga Award">Kodansha Manga Award</a> in 1986 for general manga.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KodanshaHahn_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-KodanshaHahn-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Message_to_Adolf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Adolf</i> was published in <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> by Cadence Books and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VIZ_Media" title="VIZ Media">VIZ Media</a>. The English manga is flipped to read left to right to conform to Western practice. The newer two volume release of <i>Adolf</i> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vertical,_Inc." title="Vertical, Inc.">Vertical, Inc.</a> is also flipped and is published under the title <i>Message to Adolf</i>. The manga has also been published in Brazil by <a href="/wiki/Conrad_Editora" title="Conrad Editora">Conrad Editora</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> in France by <a href="/wiki/Tonkam" title="Tonkam">Tonkam</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> in Germany by <a href="/wiki/Carlsen_Verlag" title="Carlsen Verlag">Carlsen Verlag</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> in Italy by Hazard,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> in Spain by <a href="/wiki/Planeta_DeAgostini" title="Planeta DeAgostini">Planeta DeAgostini</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and in the Netherlands by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Xtra_(publisher)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Xtra (publisher) (page does not exist)">Xtra</a>.</p>
