<p><b>Adriano Neves Pereira</b> (born 24 May 1979 in <a href="/wiki/Cap%C3%A3o_da_Canoa" title="Capão da Canoa">Capão da Canoa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_do_Sul" title="Rio Grande do Sul">Rio Grande do Sul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>), commonly known as <b>Adriano Chuva</b>, is a Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who currently plays for <a href="/wiki/Clube_Atl%C3%A9tico_Sorocaba" title="Clube Atlético Sorocaba">Atlético Sorocaba</a>, primarily as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Striker_(football)" title="Striker (football)">striker</a>.</p>
<p>A tall, powerful forward, he is known for his pace, tactical awareness, speed, dribbling and accurate shooting.</p>
<p>Chuva is perhaps best described as a <a href="/wiki/Journeyman_(sports)" title="Journeyman (sports)">journeyman</a>, namely because he has turned out for over twelve different clubs since he started his professional career with <a href="/wiki/Esporte_Clube_Juventude" title="Esporte Clube Juventude">Juventude</a> in 1997. However, despite his inability to settle, he has nevertheless proved himself to be a prolific goal-scorer wherever he has happened to play.</p>
<p>Born Adriano Neves Pereira he was given his nickname, Chuva, at a young age by his mother Simone in deference to a family friend of the same name whom she thought Adriano resembled.</p>
