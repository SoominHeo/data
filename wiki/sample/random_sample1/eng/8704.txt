<p><b>Adygeysk</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Адыге́йск</span>; <a href="/wiki/Adyghe_language" title="Adyghe language">Adyghe</a>: <span lang="ady" xml:lang="ady">Адыгэкъалэ</span>, <i>Adəgăqală</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Adygea" title="Republic of Adygea">Republic of Adygea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located near <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Krasnodar_Reservoir&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Krasnodar Reservoir (page does not exist)">Krasnodar Reservoir</a>, 100 kilometers (62 mi) northwest of <a href="/wiki/Maykop" title="Maykop">Maykop</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital</a> of the republic. Population: <span class="nowrap">12,237 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-3">[3]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">12,209 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopCensus_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-PopCensus-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adygeysk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was founded as the settlement of <b>Adygeysky</b> (<span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Адыге́йский</span>) in 1969 due to the construction of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Krasnodar_Reservoir&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Krasnodar Reservoir (page does not exist)">Krasnodar Reservoir</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gr_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-gr-5">[5]</a></sup> According to some other sources, the original name was "Adygeysk".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KubanATS_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-KubanATS-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pospelov_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Pospelov-7">[7]</a></sup> The name alluded to the <a href="/wiki/Adyghe_people" title="Adyghe people">Adyghe people</a> indigenous to the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pospelov_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Pospelov-7">[7]</a></sup> On July 27, 1976, it was granted town status and renamed <b>Teuchezhsk</b> (<span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Теуче́жск</span>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KubanATS_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-KubanATS-6">[6]</a></sup> for Adyghe poet <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tsug_Teuchezh&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tsug Teuchezh (page does not exist)">Tsug Teuchezh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pospelov_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-Pospelov-7">[7]</a></sup> In 1990, the original name was restored.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gr_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-gr-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adygeysk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, it is, together with two <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">rural localities</a>, incorporated as <b>Adygeysk <a href="/wiki/City_of_federal_subject_significance" title="City of federal subject significance">Republican Urban Okrug</a></b>—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_administrative_and_municipal_divisions_of_the_Republic_of_Adygea" title="List of administrative and municipal divisions of the Republic of Adygea">districts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref1_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Ref1-1">[1]</a></sup> It has the following rural localities under its jurisdiction:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mun_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Mun-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Aul" title="Aul">aul</a></i> of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gatlukay&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gatlukay (page does not exist)">Gatlukay</a></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Khutor" title="Khutor">khutor</a></i> of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Psekups_(rural_locality)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Psekups (rural locality) (page does not exist)">Psekups</a></li>
</ul>
<p>As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, Adygeysk Republican Urban Okrug is incorporated as <b>Adygeysk Urban Okrug</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref1m_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref1m-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
