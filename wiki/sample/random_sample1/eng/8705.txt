<p><b>Adyghe</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/æ/ short 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/ɪ/ or /ə/ 'e' in 'roses'">ᵻ</span><span title="'g' in 'guy'">ɡ</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span></span>/</a></span></span> or <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˌ/ secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="/ɑː/ 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/ɪ/ or /ə/ 'e' in 'roses'">ᵻ</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'g' in 'guy'">ɡ</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <a href="/wiki/Circassian_language" title="Circassian language">Circassian language</a>: Адыгэ <i>Adygè</i>) is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Endonym" title="Endonym">endonym</a> (native name) of the <a href="/wiki/Circassians" title="Circassians">Circassians</a>.</p>
<p>In the southwestern part of <a href="/wiki/European_Russia" title="European Russia">European Russia</a>, there is also a <a href="/wiki/Federal_subjects_of_Russia" title="Federal subjects of Russia">federal subject</a> called <a href="/wiki/Adygea" title="Adygea">Adygea</a> (Russian: Адыгея <i>Adygeja</i>), enclaved within <a href="/wiki/Krasnodar_Krai" title="Krasnodar Krai">Krasnodar Krai</a>, which is named after the Circassians.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian language</a>, Adygea's Circassian residents are called "Адыгейцы" <i>Adygejtsy</i> (meaning those [of the Republic] of Adygea), whereas the Circassians in general are called "Адыги" <i>Adygi</i> (meaning the Adyghe). In English terminology, on the other hand, many foreigners fail to distinguish between these Russian-specific administrative (former) and ethnolinguistic (latter) terms, and misuse the word Adyghe to refer only to the Adyghe in Republic of Adygea (only a small part of the historical Adyghe lands). However, the majority of the Adyghe in Russia today do not reside in Adygea, but in the neighbouring regions.</p>
<p>There are twelve Adyghe tribes, i.e. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abzakh" title="Abzakh">Abzakh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Besleney" title="Besleney">Besleney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bzhedugs" title="Bzhedugs">Bzhedug</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hatuqwai" title="Hatuqwai">Hatuqwai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kabardians" title="Kabardians">Kabardian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mamkhegh" title="Mamkhegh">Mamkhegh</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natukhai" title="Natukhai">Natukhai</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shapsug" title="Shapsug">Shapsug</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chemirgoys" title="Chemirgoys">Temirgoy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ubykh_people" title="Ubykh people">Ubykh</a>, Yegeruqwai, and <a href="/wiki/Zhaney" title="Zhaney">Zhaney</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> each one represented by a star on the green-and-gold Circassian flag which is also used as the flag of the modern-day Republic of Adygea. The Adyghe in the Republic of Adygea are mainly Bzhedug and Temirgoy who speak the Lowland Adyghe dialect (Circassian: КӀах Адыгэбзэ). The Adyghe in the Karachay-Cherkess Republic are mainly Besleney, and the Adyghe in the Kabardino-Balkar Republic are mainly Kabardian. Besleney and other Kabardian families speak the Highland Adyghe dialect (Circassian: Къэбэрдей Адыгэбзэ).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adyghe_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within Russia, the numbers of the Lowland Adyghe in 2010 were 124,835 including 107,048 in <a href="/wiki/Adygea" title="Adygea">Adygea</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2010_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Census2010-2">[2]</a></sup> 13,834 in <a href="/wiki/Krasnodar_Krai" title="Krasnodar Krai">Krasnodar Krai</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2010_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Census2010-2">[2]</a></sup> 569 in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and (in 2002) 584 in <a href="/wiki/Kabardino-Balkaria" title="Kabardino-Balkaria">Kabardino-Balkaria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tribes">Tribes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adyghe_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tribes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Circassians#Circassian_tribes" title="Circassians">Circassians: Circassian tribes</a></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adyghe_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Circassians#History" title="Circassians">Circassians: History</a></div>
<p>The political history of the Adyghe in Adygea since the Russian Revolution is complex. On 27 July 1922, a Circassian (Adygea) Autonomous Oblast was established in the <a href="/wiki/Kuban-Black_Sea_Oblast" title="Kuban-Black Sea Oblast">Kuban-Black Sea Oblast</a>, which would later become <a href="/wiki/Krasnodar_Krai" title="Krasnodar Krai">Krasnodar Krai</a>. After several name changes, the <a href="/wiki/Adyghe_Autonomous_Oblast" title="Adyghe Autonomous Oblast">Adyghe Autonomous Oblast</a> was established on 3 August 1928. On 5 October 1990, the Adygea ASSR was proclaimed and separated from Krasnodar Krai. On 24 March 1992, it became the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Adygea" title="Republic of Adygea">Republic of Adygea</a>. A significant population of the Adyghe community now lives in the Black Sea region of Northern Turkey where their culture is preserved in villages in the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
