<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Adidas Tango</h1>

<p>The <b>Adidas Tango</b> is a successful family and brand of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(association_football)" title="Football (association football)">association footballs</a> first introduced as the <b>Tango Durlast</b> in 1978 for the <a href="/wiki/1978_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1978 FIFA World Cup">1978 FIFA World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>. Variations of the design had been produced for various competitions including the FIFA World Cup, the UEFA European Championship and the Summer Olympics. The Tango balls have had different names applied to them to distinguish them in their construction, the competitions they have been used for, and even if they are match or replica balls.</p>
<p>In 2011, <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a> introduced the <a href="/wiki/Adidas_Tango_12" title="Adidas Tango 12">Tango 12</a>, but besides the name there are no particular similarities between the new ball and the old Adidas Tango family.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tango_Durlast">Tango Durlast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adidas_Tango&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Tango Durlast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 1978 <b>Tango Durlast</b> has twenty identical panels with 'triads' created the impression of 12 circles. Like its predecessors, the Adidas Tango Durlast was made of genuine leather and boasted the shiny waterproofing Durlast coating.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tango_Espa.C3.B1a">Tango Espa√±a</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adidas_Tango&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tango Espa√±a">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Adidas_Tango_Espa%C3%B1a.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1690" data-file-width="1126" height="300" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Adidas_Tango_Espa%C3%B1a.jpg/200px-Adidas_Tango_Espa%C3%B1a.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Adidas_Tango_Espa%C3%B1a.jpg/300px-Adidas_Tango_Espa%C3%B1a.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Adidas_Tango_Espa%C3%B1a.jpg/400px-Adidas_Tango_Espa%C3%B1a.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>

<p>The <b>Tango Espa√±a</b> by <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a> was the official match ball of <a href="/wiki/1982_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1982 FIFA World Cup">1982 FIFA World Cup in Spain</a>. The <i>Tango Espa√±a</i> had improved water resistant qualities through its rubberized seams. These were not very resistant and resulted in the ball having to be changed several times during some games.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> This ball was the last genuine leather ball to be used in the World Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
