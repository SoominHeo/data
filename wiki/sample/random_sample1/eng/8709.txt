<p>The <b>Crown of Aragon</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/ær/ 'arr' in 'marry'">ær</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="'g' in 'guy'">ɡ</span><span title="/ən/ 'on' in 'button'">ən</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <a href="/wiki/Aragonese_language" title="Aragonese language">Aragonese</a>: <span lang="an" xml:lang="an"><i>Corona d'Aragón</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan</a>: <span lang="ca" xml:lang="ca"><i>Corona d'Aragó</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>Corona de Aragón</i></span>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[nb 1]</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Composite_monarchy" title="Composite monarchy">composite monarchy</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-composedmonarchy_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-composedmonarchy-1">[1]</a></sup> also nowadays referred to as a <a href="/wiki/Confederation" title="Confederation">confederation</a> of individual <a href="/wiki/Polity" title="Polity">polities</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-captives_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-captives-3">[3]</a></sup> or <a href="/wiki/Monarchy" title="Monarchy">kingdoms</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Buffery_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Buffery-2">[2]</a></sup> ruled by one king, with a <a href="/wiki/Personal_union" title="Personal union">personal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dynastic_union" title="Dynastic union">dynastic union</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Aragon" title="Kingdom of Aragon">Kingdom of Aragon</a> and the <a href="/wiki/County_of_Barcelona" title="County of Barcelona">County of Barcelona</a>. At the height of its power in the 14th and 15th centuries, the Crown of Aragon was a <a href="/wiki/Thalassocracy" title="Thalassocracy">thalassocracy</a> (a state with primarily maritime realms) controlling a large portion of present-day eastern <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, parts of what is now <a href="/wiki/Northern_Catalonia" title="Northern Catalonia">southern France</a>, and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> "empire" which included the <a href="/wiki/Balearic_Islands" title="Balearic Islands">Balearic Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corsica" title="Corsica">Corsica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">Sardinia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southern_Italy" title="Southern Italy">Southern Italy</a> (from 1442) and parts of <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> (until 1388). The component realms of the Crown were not united politically except at the level of the king, who ruled over each autonomous polity according to its own laws, raising funds under each tax structure, dealing separately with each <i>Corts</i> or <i>Cortes</i>. Put in contemporary terms, it has sometimes been considered that the different lands of the Crown of Aragon (mainly the Kingdom of Aragon, the <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Catalonia" title="Principality of Catalonia">Principality of Catalonia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Valencia" title="Kingdom of Valencia">Kingdom of Valencia</a>) functioned more as a <a href="/wiki/Confederation" title="Confederation">confederation</a> than as a single kingdom. In this sense, the larger Crown of Aragon must not be confused with one of its constituent parts, the Kingdom of Aragon, from which it takes its name.</p>
<p>In 1469, a new dynastic familial union of the Crown of Aragon with the <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">Crown of Castile</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Monarchs" title="Catholic Monarchs">Catholic Monarchs</a>, joining what contemporaries referred to as "the Spains"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[4]</a></sup> led to what would become the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Spain" title="Kingdom of Spain">Kingdom of Spain</a> under King <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Spain" title="Philip II of Spain">Philip II</a>. The Crown existed until it was abolished by the <a href="/wiki/Nueva_Planta_decrees" title="Nueva Planta decrees">Nueva Planta decrees</a> issued by King <a href="/wiki/Philip_V_of_Spain" title="Philip V of Spain">Philip V</a> in 1716 as a consequence of the defeat of <a href="/wiki/Charles_VI,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles VI, Holy Roman Emperor">Archduke Charles</a> (as Charles III of Aragon) in the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Spanish_Succession" title="War of the Spanish Succession">War of the Spanish Succession</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Context">Context</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_of_Aragon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Context">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Formally, the political center of the Crown of Aragon was <a href="/wiki/Zaragoza" title="Zaragoza">Zaragoza</a>, where kings were crowned at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/La_Seo_Cathedral" title="La Seo Cathedral">La Seo Cathedral</a>. The 'de facto' capital and leading cultural, administrative and economic centre of the Crown of Aragon was <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> followed by <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Valencia#Splendour" title="Kingdom of Valencia">Valencia</a>. Finally, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palma_de_Mallorca" title="Palma de Mallorca">Palma</a> (<a href="/wiki/Majorca" title="Majorca">Majorca</a>) was an additional important city and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seaport" title="Seaport">seaport</a>.</p>
<p>The Crown of Aragon eventually included the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Aragon" title="Kingdom of Aragon">Kingdom of Aragon</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Catalonia" title="Principality of Catalonia">Principality of Catalonia</a> (until the 12th century as <a href="/wiki/County_of_Barcelona" title="County of Barcelona">County of Barcelona</a>), the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Valencia" title="Kingdom of Valencia">Kingdom of Valencia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Majorca" title="Kingdom of Majorca">Kingdom of Majorca</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sicily" title="Kingdom of Sicily">Kingdom of Sicily</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Naples" title="Kingdom of Naples">Kingdom of Naples</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sardinia" title="Kingdom of Sardinia">Kingdom of Sardinia</a>. For brief periods the Crown of Aragon also controlled <a href="/wiki/Montpellier" title="Montpellier">Montpellier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Provence" title="Provence">Provence</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corsica" title="Corsica">Corsica</a>, and the twin <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Athens" title="Duchy of Athens">Duchy of Athens</a> and <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Neopatras" title="Duchy of Neopatras">Neopatras</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_Greece" title="Latin Greece">Latin Greece</a>.</p>
<p>The countries that are today known as Spain and Portugal spent the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> after 722 in an intermittent struggle called the <i><a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">Reconquista</a></i>. This struggle pitted the northern Christian kingdoms against the Islamic <a href="/wiki/Taifa" title="Taifa">taifa</a> <a href="/wiki/Petty_kingdom" title="Petty kingdom">petty kingdoms</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Al-Andalus" title="Al-Andalus">South</a> and against each other.</p>
<p>In the Late Middle Ages, the expansion of the Aragonese Crown southwards met with the <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">Castilian</a> advance eastward in the region of <a href="/wiki/Region_of_Murcia" title="Region of Murcia">Murcia</a>. Afterward, the Aragonese Crown focused on the Mediterranean, acting as far as Greece and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barbary" title="Barbary">Barbary</a>, whereas Portugal, which completed its <i>Reconquista</i> in 1249, would focus on the Atlantic Ocean. Mercenaries from the territories in the Crown, known as <i><a href="/wiki/Almogavars" title="Almogavars">almogàvers</a></i> participated in the creation of this Mediterranean "empire", and later found employment in countries all across southern Europe.</p>
<p>The Crown of Aragon has been considered by some<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (December 2011)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> as an empire which ruled in the Mediterranean for hundreds of years, with the power to <a href="/wiki/Thalassocracy" title="Thalassocracy">set rules over the entire sea</a> (for instance, the <i>Llibre del Consolat del Mar</i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Consulate_of_the_Sea" title="Consulate of the Sea">Book of the Consulate of the Sea</a></i>, written in <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan</a>, is one of the oldest compilation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maritime_law" title="Maritime law">maritime laws</a> in the world). It was indeed, at its height, one of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Major_power" title="Major power">major powers</a> in Europe.</p>
<p>However, its different territories were only connected through the person of the monarch, an aspect of empire as early as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Achaemenid_Persia" title="Achaemenid Persia">Achaemenid Persia</a>. A contemporary, the Marqués de Lozoya<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[5]</a></sup> described the Crown of Aragon as being more like a <a href="/wiki/Confederation" title="Confederation">confederacy</a> than a centralised kingdom, let alone an empire. Nor did official documents ever refer to it as an empire (<i>Imperium</i> or any cognate word); instead, it was considered a dynastic union of autonomous kingdoms.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_of_Aragon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin">Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_of_Aragon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Petronila_Ramon_Berenguer.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="856" data-file-width="772" height="244" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Petronila_Ramon_Berenguer.jpg/220px-Petronila_Ramon_Berenguer.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Petronila_Ramon_Berenguer.jpg/330px-Petronila_Ramon_Berenguer.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Petronila_Ramon_Berenguer.jpg/440px-Petronila_Ramon_Berenguer.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The Aragonese empire originated in 1137, when the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Aragon" title="Kingdom of Aragon">Kingdom of Aragon</a> and the <a href="/wiki/County_of_Barcelona" title="County of Barcelona">County of Barcelona</a> (with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/County_of_Provence" title="County of Provence">County of Provence</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/County_of_Girona" title="County of Girona">Girona</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_of_Cerdanya" title="County of Cerdanya">Cerdanya</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_of_Osona" title="County of Osona">Osona</a> and other territories) merged by <a href="/wiki/Dynastic_union" title="Dynastic union">dynastic union</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bisson_31_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-bisson_31-7">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[7]</a></sup> upon the marriage of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Raymond_Berenguer_IV_of_Barcelona" title="Raymond Berenguer IV of Barcelona">Raymond Berenguer IV of Barcelona</a> and <a href="/wiki/Petronilla_of_Aragon" title="Petronilla of Aragon">Petronilla of Aragon</a>; their individual titles combined in the person of their son <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_II_of_Aragon" title="Alfonso II of Aragon">Alfonso II of Aragon</a>, who ascended to the throne in 1162. This union respected the existing institutions and parliaments of both territories. Although the County of Barcelona had more wealth (given its position on the Mediterranean and strong family ties in Occitania), the combined state was initially known as <i>Regno, Dominio et Corona Aragonum et Catalonie</i>, and later as <i>Corona Regum Aragoniae</i>, <i>Corona Aragonum</i> or simply <i>Aragon</i>. This was due to the total loss of Catalan influence, the renunciation of the family rights of the counts of Barcelona in <a href="/wiki/Occitania" title="Occitania">Occitania</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[8]</a></sup> and the extinction of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Barcelona" title="House of Barcelona">House of Aragon and Barcelona</a> in 1410. Consequently, the monarchs denominated themselves <i>de Aragon</i>, and Aragon became prominent as an Iberian kingdom linked to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_of_Jim%C3%A9nez" title="House of Jiménez">House of Jiménez</a> which ruled over Navarre (905–1234), Castile, Leon and Galicia (1035–1126) and Aragon (1035–1137).</p>
<p>Petronilla's father <a href="/wiki/Ramiro_II_of_Aragon" title="Ramiro II of Aragon">King Ramiro, "The Monk"</a> (reigned 1134–1137) who was raised in the Saint Pons de Thomières Monastery, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viscounty_of_B%C3%A9ziers" title="Viscounty of Béziers">Viscounty of Béziers</a> as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benedictine" title="Benedictine">Benedictine</a> monk was the youngest of three brothers. His brothers <a href="/wiki/Peter_I_of_Aragon_and_Navarre" title="Peter I of Aragon and Navarre">Peter I</a> (reigned 1094–1104) and <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_the_Battler" title="Alfonso the Battler">Alfonso I</a> <i>El Batallador</i> (The Battler, reigned 1104–1134) had bravely fought against Castile for the hegemony in the Iberian peninsula. After the death of Alfonso I, the Aragonese nobility that campaigned close him feared being overwhelmed by the influence of Castile. And so, Ramiro was forced to leave his monastic life and proclaimed himself King of Aragon. He married Agnes, sister of the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Aquitaine" title="Duke of Aquitaine">Duke of Aquitaine</a> and betrothed his only daughter to <a href="/wiki/Ramon_Berenguer_IV,_Count_of_Barcelona" title="Ramon Berenguer IV, Count of Barcelona">Raymond Berengar IV of Barcelona</a>, member of one of the most influential families in Southern <a href="/wiki/Occitania" title="Occitania">Occitania</a>. The wedding agreement provided Raymond Berengar IV the title of <i>Princeps Aragonum</i> and <i>Dominator Aragonenesis</i> (Ruler of the Kingdom and Commander of the Military) and kept the title and honors of King of Aragon to Ramiro II.</p>
<p>Raymond Berengar IV, the first ruler of the united dynasty, called himself Count of Barcelona and "Prince of Aragon".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paynefive_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-paynefive-10">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion">Expansion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_of_Aragon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Expansion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Alfonso II inherited two realms and with them, two different expansion processes. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_of_Jim%C3%A9nez" title="House of Jiménez">House of Jiménez</a> looked south in a battle against Castile for the control of the Mediterranean coast in the Iberian peninsula. The <a href="/wiki/House_of_Barcelona" title="House of Barcelona">House of Barcelona</a> looked north to its origins, <a href="/wiki/Occitania" title="Occitania">Occitania</a>, where through family ties it had significant influence, especially in <a href="/wiki/Provence" title="Provence">Provence</a> and <a href="/wiki/County_of_Foix" title="County of Foix">Foix</a>.</p>
<p>Soon, <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_II_of_Aragon" title="Alfonso II of Aragon">Alfonso II of Aragon and Barcelona</a> committed himself to conquer <a href="/wiki/Valencia" title="Valencia">Valencia</a> as the Aragonese nobility demanded. But like his father, he gave priority to the expansion and consolidation of the House of Barcelona's influence in <a href="/wiki/Occitania" title="Occitania">Occitania</a>.</p>
<p>Alfonso II signed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Cazorla" title="Treaty of Cazorla">treaties of Cazorla</a>, a multilateral treaty between <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">Navarre</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arag%C3%B3n" title="Aragón">Aragón</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="Kingdom of León">León</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile" title="Kingdom of Castile">Castile</a> to redefine the frontiers and zones of expansion of each kingdom. Alfonso II assured <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Valencia" title="Kingdom of Valencia">Valencia</a> by renouncing the Aragonese rights of annexing <a href="/wiki/Murcia" title="Murcia">Murcia</a> in exchange for securing the Aragonese frontier with Castile. This action should be seen as result of the aforementioned priority given over the Occitan and Catalan dominions of the Crown of Aragon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bisson_36_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-bisson_36-11">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>From the 9th century, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dukes_of_Aquitaine" title="Dukes of Aquitaine">dukes of Aquitaine</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Navarre" title="Kingdom of Navarre">kings of Navarre</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Counts_of_Foix" title="Counts of Foix">counts of Foix</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Counts_of_Toulouse" title="Counts of Toulouse">counts of Toulouse</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Count_of_Barcelona" title="Count of Barcelona">counts of Barcelona</a> were rivals in their attempts at controlling the various <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pays" title="Pays">pays</a></i> of <a href="/wiki/Occitania" title="Occitania">Occitania</a>. And the House of Barcelona succeeded in extending its influence to the area that is now south of France through strong family ties, in the areas of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/County_of_Provence" title="County of Provence">County of Provence</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_of_Toulouse" title="County of Toulouse">County of Toulouse</a> and <a href="/wiki/County_of_Foix" title="County of Foix">County of Foix</a>. The rebellion of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cathars" title="Cathars">Cathars</a> or Albigensians, who rejected the authority and teachings of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a>, led to the loss of these possessions in southern France. Pope Innocent III called upon <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phillip_II_Augustus" title="Phillip II Augustus">Phillip II of France</a> to suppress the Albigensians—<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Albigensian_Crusade" title="The Albigensian Crusade">The Albigensian Crusade</a>, which led to bringing Occitania firmly under the control of the King of France, and the <a href="/wiki/Capetian_dynasty" title="Capetian dynasty">Capetian dynasty</a> from northern France.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Peter_II_of_Aragon" title="Peter II of Aragon">Peter II of Aragon and I of Barcelona</a> returned from the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Las_Navas_de_Tolosa" title="Battle of Las Navas de Tolosa">Battle of Las Navas de Tolosa</a> in autumn 1212 to find that <a href="/wiki/Simon_de_Montfort,_5th_Earl_of_Leicester" title="Simon de Montfort, 5th Earl of Leicester">Simon de Montfort</a> had conquered <a href="/wiki/Toulouse" title="Toulouse">Toulouse</a>, exiling the Count <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Raymond_VI_of_Toulouse" title="Raymond VI of Toulouse">Raymond VI of Toulouse</a>, who was Peter's brother-in-law and vassal. Peter's army crossed the <a href="/wiki/Pyrenees" title="Pyrenees">Pyrenees</a> and arrived at <a href="/wiki/Muret" title="Muret">Muret</a> where they were joined by Raymond of Foix and Raymond of Toulouse's forces, in September 1213 to confront Montfort's army. The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Muret" title="Battle of Muret">Battle of Muret</a> began on 12 September 1213. The Catalan, Aragonese and Occitan forces were disorganised and disintegrated under the assault of Montfort's squadrons. Peter himself was caught in the thick of fighting, and died as a result of a foolhardy act of bravado. Thus, the nobility of Toulouse, Foix and other vassals of the Crown of Aragon were defeated. The conflict concluded with the Treaty of Meaux-Paris in 1229, in which the Crown of Aragon agreed to renounce its rights over the south of <a href="/wiki/Occitania" title="Occitania">Occitania</a> with the integration of these territories into the dominions of the <a href="/wiki/Louis_VIII_of_France" title="Louis VIII of France">King of France</a>.</p>
<p>King <a href="/wiki/James_I_of_Aragon" title="James I of Aragon">James I</a> (13th century) returned to an era of expansion to the South, by conquering and incorporating <a href="/wiki/Majorca" title="Majorca">Majorca</a> and a good share of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Valencia" title="Kingdom of Valencia">Kingdom of Valencia</a> into the Crown. With the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Corbeil_(1258)" title="Treaty of Corbeil (1258)">Treaty of Corbeil (1258)</a>, which was based upon the principle of natural frontiers,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chaytor_james_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-chaytor_james-12">[11]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Capetian_dynasty" title="Capetian dynasty">Capetians</a> were recognized as heirs of the <a href="/wiki/Carolingian_dynasty" title="Carolingian dynasty">Carolingian dynasty</a>, and the Capetian king renounced his feudal overlordship over Catalonia. The general principle was clear, Catalan influence north of the Pyrenees, beyond the <a href="/wiki/County_of_Roussillon" title="County of Roussillon">Roussillon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vallespir" title="Vallespir">Vallespir</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_of_Conflent" title="County of Conflent">Conflent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Capcir" title="Capcir">Capcir</a>, was to cease.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chaytor_james_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-chaytor_james-12">[11]</a></sup> James I had realized that wasting his forces and distracting his energies in attempts to keep a footing in France would only end in disaster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chaytor_james_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-chaytor_james-12">[11]</a></sup> On January 1266, James I besieged and captured Murcia, then settled his own men, mostly Catalans, there; and handed Murcia over to Castile with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Cazorla" title="Treaty of Cazorla">treaty of Cazorla</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bisson_67_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-bisson_67-13">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Majorca" title="Kingdom of Majorca">Kingdom of Majorca</a>, including the counties of <a href="/wiki/Cerdanya" title="Cerdanya">Cerdanya</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roussillon" title="Roussillon">Roussillon-Vallespir</a> and the city of <a href="/wiki/Montpellier" title="Montpellier">Montpellier</a>, was held independently from 1276 to 1279 by <a href="/wiki/James_II_of_Majorca" title="James II of Majorca">James II of Majorca</a> and as a vassal of the Crown of Aragon after that date, becoming a full member of the Crown of Aragon in 1344.</p>
<p>Valencia was finally made a new kingdom with its own institutions and not an extension of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arag%C3%B3n" title="Aragón">Aragón</a> as the Aragonese noblemen had intended since even before the creation of the Crown of Aragon. The <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Valencia" title="Kingdom of Valencia">Kingdom of Valencia</a> became the third member of the crown together with Aragon and Catalonia. Majorca's legal status was not ranked at the same level of the aforementioned three members.</p>
<p>In 1282, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sicilians" title="Sicilians">Sicilians</a> rose up against the <a href="/wiki/Capetian_House_of_Anjou" title="Capetian House of Anjou">second dynasty of the Angevins</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Sicilian_Vespers" title="Sicilian Vespers">Sicilian Vespers</a> and massacred the garrison soldiers throughout the island. <a href="/wiki/Peter_III_of_Aragon" title="Peter III of Aragon">Peter III</a> responded to their call, and landed in <a href="/wiki/Trapani" title="Trapani">Trapani</a> to an enthusiastic welcome five months later. This caused <a href="/wiki/Pope_Martin_IV" title="Pope Martin IV">Pope Martin IV</a> to excommunicate the king, place Sicily under interdiction, and offer the kingdom of Aragon to a son of <a href="/wiki/Philip_III_of_France" title="Philip III of France">Philip III of France</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bisson.3D87_88_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-bisson.3D87_88-14">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>When Peter III refused to impose the <i>Charters of Aragon</i> in Valencia, the nobles and towns united in <a href="/wiki/Zaragoza" title="Zaragoza">Zaragoza</a> to demand a confirmation of their privileges, which the king had to accept in 1283. Thus began the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Aragon" title="Union of Aragon">Union of Aragon</a>, which developed the power of the <i>Justícia</i> to mediate between the king and the Aragonese bourgeois. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bisson.3D87_88_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-bisson.3D87_88-14">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>When <a href="/wiki/James_II_of_Aragon" title="James II of Aragon">James II of Aragon</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[15]</a></sup> completed the conquest of the kingdom of Valencia, the Crown of Aragon established itself as one of the major powers in Europe.</p>
<p>In 1297, to solve the dispute between the Anjevins and the Aragonese over Sicily, <a href="/wiki/Pope_Boniface_VIII" title="Pope Boniface VIII">Pope Boniface VIII</a> created <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ex_novo" title="Ex novo">ex novo</a></i> a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sardinia_and_Corsica" title="Kingdom of Sardinia and Corsica">Kingdom of Sardinia and Corsica</a> and entrusted it as a <a href="/wiki/Fief" title="Fief">fief</a> to the Aragonese King <a href="/wiki/James_II_of_Aragon" title="James II of Aragon">James II</a>, ignoring already existing, indigenous states.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-17">[16]</a></sup> In 1324, James II finally started to seize the Pisan territories in the former states of <a href="/wiki/Giudicato_of_Cagliari" title="Giudicato of Cagliari">Cagliari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giudicato_of_Gallura" title="Giudicato of Gallura">Gallura</a>. In 1347 Aragon made war on the Genoese <a href="/wiki/Doria_(family)" title="Doria (family)">Doria</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Malaspina_family&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Malaspina family (page does not exist)">Malaspina</a> houses, which controlled most of the lands of the former <a href="/wiki/Giudicato_of_Logudoro" title="Giudicato of Logudoro">Logudoro</a> state in north-western Sardinia, and added them to its direct domains. The <a href="/wiki/Giudicato_of_Arborea" title="Giudicato of Arborea">Giudicato of Arborea</a>, the only remaining independent Sardinian state, proved far more difficult to subdue. The rulers of Arborea developed the ambition to unite all of Sardinia under their rule and create a single Sardinian state, and at a certain point (1368–1388, 1392–1409) almost managed to drive the Aragonese out. The war between Arborea and Aragon was fought on and off for more than 100 years; this situation lasted until 1409, when the army of Arborea suffered a heavy defeat by the Aragonese army in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Sanluri" title="Battle of Sanluri">Battle of Sanluri</a>; the capital <a href="/wiki/Oristano" title="Oristano">Oristano</a> was lost in 1410. After some years during which Arborean rulers failed to organise a successful resurgence, they sold their remaining rights for 100,000 gold florins, and by 1420 the Aragonese Kingdom of Sardinia finally extended throughout the island. The subduing of Sardinia having taken a century<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, <a href="/wiki/Corsica" title="Corsica">Corsica</a>, which had never been wrested from the Genoese, was dropped from the formal title of the Kingdom.</p>
<p>Through the marriage of <a href="/wiki/Peter_IV_of_Aragon" title="Peter IV of Aragon">Peter IV</a> to <a href="/wiki/Maria,_Queen_of_Sicily" title="Maria, Queen of Sicily">Maria of Sicily</a> (1381), the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sicily" title="Kingdom of Sicily">Kingdom of Sicily</a>, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Athens" title="Duchy of Athens">duchies of Athens</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neopatria" title="Neopatria">Neopatria</a>, were finally implemented more fimrly into the Crown. The Greek possessions were permanently lost to <a href="/wiki/Nerio_I_Acciaioli" title="Nerio I Acciaioli">Nerio I Acciaioli</a> in 1388 and Sicily was dissociated in the hands of <a href="/wiki/Martin_I_of_Sicily" title="Martin I of Sicily">Martin I</a> from 1395 to 1409, but the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Naples" title="Kingdom of Naples">Kingdom of Naples</a> was added finally in 1442 by the conquest led by <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_V_of_Aragon" title="Alfonso V of Aragon">Alfonso V</a>.</p>
<p>The King's possessions outside of the Iberian Peninsula and Balearic Islands were ruled by proxy through local elites as <a href="/wiki/Petty_kingdom" title="Petty kingdom">petty kingdoms</a>, rather than subjected directly to a centralised government. They were more an economic part of the Crown of Aragon than a political one.</p>
<p>The fact that the King was keen on settling new kingdoms instead of merely expanding the existing kingdoms was a part of a power struggle that pitted the interests of the king against those of the existing <a href="/wiki/Nobility" title="Nobility">nobility</a>. This process was also under way in most of the European states that successfully effected the transition to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Early_Modern" title="Early Modern">Early Modern</a> state. Thus, the new territories gained from the <a href="/wiki/Moors" title="Moors">Moors</a>—namely Valencia and Majorca—were usually given <i><a href="/wiki/Fuero" title="Fuero">fueros</a></i>—Catalan <i>furs</i>—as an instrument of self-government in order to limit the power of nobility in these new acquisitions and, at the same time, increase their allegiance to the monarchy itself. The trend in the neighbouring <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile" title="Kingdom of Castile">kingdom of Castile</a> was quite similar, both kingdoms giving impetus to the <a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">Reconquista</a> by granting different grades of self-government either to cities or territories, instead of placing the new territories under the direct rule of nobility.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Union_with_Castile">Union with Castile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_of_Aragon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Union with Castile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>In 1410, King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martin_I_of_Aragon" title="Martin I of Aragon">Martin I</a> died without living descendants or heirs. As a result, by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pact_of_Caspe" title="Pact of Caspe">Pact of Caspe</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ferdinand_of_Antequera" title="Ferdinand of Antequera">Ferdinand of Antequera</a> from the Castilian dynasty of <a href="/wiki/House_of_Trast%C3%A1mara" title="House of Trastámara">Trastámara</a>, received the Crown of Aragon as <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_I_of_Aragon" title="Ferdinand I of Aragon">Ferdinand I of Aragon</a>.</p>
<p>Later, his grandson King <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_II_of_Aragon" title="Ferdinand II of Aragon">Ferdinand II of Aragon</a> recovered the northern Catalan counties—Roussillon and Cerdagne—which had been lost to France as well as the kingdom of <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">Navarre</a>, which had recently joined the Crown of Aragon but had been lost after internal dynastic disputes.</p>
<p>In 1469, Ferdinand married <a href="/wiki/Isabella_I_of_Castile" title="Isabella I of Castile">Infanta Isabella of Castile</a>, half-sister of King <a href="/wiki/Henry_IV_of_Castile" title="Henry IV of Castile">Henry IV of Castile</a>, who became Queen of Castile and León after Henry's death in 1474. Their marriage was a dynastic union<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[19]</a></sup> which became the constituent event for the dawn of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Spain" title="Kingdom of Spain">Kingdom of Spain</a>. At that point both the <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">Castile</a> and the Crown of Aragon remained distinct territories, each keeping its own traditional institutions, parliaments and laws. The process of territorial consolidation was completed when King <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles I</a>, known as Emperor Charles V, in 1516 united all the kingdoms on the Iberian peninsula, save the Kingdoms of Portugal and the Algarve, under one monarch—his co-monarch and mother Queen <a href="/wiki/Joanna_of_Castile" title="Joanna of Castile">Joanna I</a> in confinement—thereby furthering the creation of the Spanish state, albeit a decentralised one.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dissolution">Dissolution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_of_Aragon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Dissolution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The literary evocation of past splendour recalls correctly the great age of the 13th and 14th centuries, when Valencia, Majorca and Sicily were conquered, the population growth could be handled without social conflict, and the urban prosperity, which peaked in 1345, created the institutional and cultural achievements of the Crown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bisson_188_189_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-bisson_188_189-21">[20]</a></sup> The Aragonese crown's wealth and power stagnated and its authority was steadily transferred to the new Spanish crown after that date—the demographic growth was partially offset by the <a href="/wiki/Alhambra_Decree" title="Alhambra Decree">expulsion of the Jews</a> from Spain (1492), <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslims</a> (1502) and the <a href="/wiki/Expulsion_of_the_Moriscos" title="Expulsion of the Moriscos">expulsion of the Moriscos</a> (1609).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bisson_189_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-bisson_189-22">[21]</a></sup> It was unable to prevent the loss of Roussillon in 1659, the loss of Minorca and its Italian domains in 1707–1716, and the imposition of French language on Roussillon (1700) and Castilian as the language of government in all the old Aragonese Crown lands in Spain (1707–1716).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bisson_189_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-bisson_189-22">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Crown of Aragon and its institutions were abolished in 1716 only after the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Spanish_Succession" title="War of the Spanish Succession">War of the Spanish Succession</a> (1701–1714) by the <a href="/wiki/Nueva_Planta_decrees" title="Nueva Planta decrees">Nueva Planta decrees</a>, issued by <a href="/wiki/Philip_V_of_Spain" title="Philip V of Spain">Philip V of Spain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bisson_189_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-bisson_189-22">[21]</a></sup> The old regime was swept away, the administration was subsumed into the Castilian administration, the lands of the Crown were united formally with those of Castile to legally form a single state, the kingdom of Spain, as it moved towards a <a href="/wiki/Centralized_government" title="Centralized government">centralized government</a> under the new <a href="/wiki/House_of_Bourbon" title="House of Bourbon">Bourbon</a> dynasty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bisson_189_22-3"><a href="#cite_note-bisson_189-22">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nationalist_revisionism">Nationalist revisionism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_of_Aragon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Nationalist revisionism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some of the <a href="/wiki/Nationalisms_and_regionalisms_of_Spain" title="Nationalisms and regionalisms of Spain">nationalist movements</a> in Spain consider the former kingdoms of the Crown of Aragon to be the foundation of their nations, the Catalan nationalist movement being the most prominent. <a href="/wiki/Spanish_nationalism" title="Spanish nationalism">Spanish nationalism</a>, on the other hand, tends to place more importance on the later dynastic union with the Crown of Castile, considering it the origin of one Spanish nation.</p>
<p>The reprisals inflicted on the territories that had fought against <a href="/wiki/Philip_V_of_Spain" title="Philip V of Spain">Philip V</a> in the War of Succession is given by some <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valencian_nation" title="Valencian nation">Valencian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Catalan_nationalism" title="Catalan nationalism">Catalan nationalists</a> as an argument against the centralism of Spanish nationalism and in favor of federalism, confederation, or even independence. Some Catalans associated their ancient privileges with their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Generalitat_of_Catalonia" title="Generalitat of Catalonia">Generalitat</a> and resistance to Castile.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bisson_188_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-bisson_188-23">[22]</a></sup> Because restoration of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fueros" title="Fueros">fueros</a></i> was one of its tenets, <a href="/wiki/Carlism" title="Carlism">Carlism</a> won support in the lands of the Crown of Aragon during the 19th century.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Romanticism" title="Romanticism">Romanticism</a> of the 19th century Catalan <a href="/wiki/Renaixen%C3%A7a" title="Renaixença">Renaixença</a> evoked a "Pyrenean realm" that corresponded more to the vision of 13th century <a href="/wiki/Troubadour" title="Troubadour">troubadours</a> than to the historical reality of the Crown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bisson_188_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-bisson_188-23">[22]</a></sup> This vision survives today as "a nostalgic programme of politicised culture".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bisson_188_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-bisson_188-23">[22]</a></sup> Thus, the history of the Crown of Aragon remains a politically loaded topic in modern Spain,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[23]</a></sup> especially when it comes to asserting the level of independence enjoyed by constituents of the Crown, like the County of Barcelona, which is sometimes used to justify the level of autonomy (or independence) that should be enjoyed by contemporary Catalonia and other territories.</p>

<p>The origin of Coat of arms of the Crown of Aragon is the familiar coat of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Counts_of_Barcelona" title="Counts of Barcelona">Counts of Barcelona</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kings_of_Aragon" title="Kings of Aragon">Kings of Aragon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[24]</a></sup> The Pennon was used exclusively by the monarchs of the Crown and was expressive of their sovereignty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[25]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/James_III_of_Majorca" title="James III of Majorca">James III of Majorca</a>, vassal of the Kingdom of Aragon, used a coat of arms with four bars, as seen on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leges_Palatinae" title="Leges Palatinae">Leges Palatinae</a> miniatures.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Institutions">Institutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_of_Aragon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Institutions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aragon, Catalonia and Valencia each had a legislative body, known as the <i>Cortes</i> in Aragon or <i>Corts</i> in Catalonia and Valencia. A <i>Diputación del General</i> or <i>Diputació del General</i> was established in each, becoming known as a <i>Generalidad</i> in Aragon and <i><a href="/wiki/Generalitat" title="Generalitat">Generalitat</a></i> in Catalonia and Valencia.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Capital">Capital</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_of_Aragon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Capital">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the reign of the house of Barcelona (from <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_II_of_Aragon" title="Alfonso II of Aragon">Alfonso II</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martin_I_of_Aragon" title="Martin I of Aragon">Martin I</a>) the <i><a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">de facto</a></i> capital was <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, because the kings of this dynasty lived in the city during a long time and it became in the administrative and economic center of the Crown. In the early 15th century the de facto capital was Valencia, until <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_V_of_Aragon" title="Alfonso V of Aragon">Alfonso V</a> came to the throne. During the 15-16th century the Crown's <i><a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">de facto</a></i> capital was <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a>: after <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_V_of_Aragon" title="Alfonso V of Aragon">Alfonso V of Aragon</a>, also <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_II_of_Aragon" title="Ferdinand II of Aragon">Ferdinand II of Aragon</a> settled the capital in Naples. Alfonso, in particular, wanted to transform Naples into a real Mediterranean capital, lavishing also huge sums to embellish it further.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[26]</a></sup> Later the courts were itinerant<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uib_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-uib-28">[27]</a></sup> until <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Spain" title="Philip II of Spain">Philip II of Spain</a>. The Spanish historian Domingo Buesa Conde has argued that Zaragoza ought to be considered the political capital, but not economic or administrative, due to the obligation of the kings to be crowned at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/La_Seo_Cathedral" title="La Seo Cathedral">Seo</a> of Zaragoza.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[nb 2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_of_Aragon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
