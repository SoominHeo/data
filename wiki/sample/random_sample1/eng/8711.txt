<p><b>Ararat</b> (<a href="/wiki/Armenian_language" title="Armenian language">Armenian</a>: <span lang="hy" xml:lang="hy">Արարատ</span>), is a town in the <a href="/wiki/Ararat_Province" title="Ararat Province">Ararat Province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>, located 48 km (30 mi) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Yerevan" title="Yerevan">Yerevan</a> and 19 km (12 mi) south of the provincial centre <a href="/wiki/Artashat,_Armenia" title="Artashat, Armenia">Artashat</a>. As of the 2011 census, the population of the town is 20,235.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ararat,_Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ararat,_Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The cement factory of Ararat has been operating since 1927 along with few apartments to accommodate the workers. In 1930, the settlement was officially formed as a labour accommodation area. In 1935, it was merged with the nearby village of <i>Davalu</i> and renamed <i>Ararat</i> in 1947, after the nearby biblical <a href="/wiki/Mountains_of_Ararat" title="Mountains of Ararat">Mountains of Ararat</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ararat was developed as an industrial centre and considered an <a href="/wiki/Urban-type_settlement" title="Urban-type settlement">urban-type settlement</a>. However, due to its rapid growth and the gradual increase of the population, Ararat was given the status of a town in 1962.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_climate">Geography and climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ararat,_Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography and climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:%D5%88%D6%82%D6%80%D5%AE%D5%AB_%D5%AC%D5%A5%D5%BC%D5%B6%D5%A5%D6%80_15.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3672" data-file-width="4896" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/%D5%88%D6%82%D6%80%D5%AE%D5%AB_%D5%AC%D5%A5%D5%BC%D5%B6%D5%A5%D6%80_15.jpg/220px-%D5%88%D6%82%D6%80%D5%AE%D5%AB_%D5%AC%D5%A5%D5%BC%D5%B6%D5%A5%D6%80_15.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/%D5%88%D6%82%D6%80%D5%AE%D5%AB_%D5%AC%D5%A5%D5%BC%D5%B6%D5%A5%D6%80_15.jpg/330px-%D5%88%D6%82%D6%80%D5%AE%D5%AB_%D5%AC%D5%A5%D5%BC%D5%B6%D5%A5%D6%80_15.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/%D5%88%D6%82%D6%80%D5%AE%D5%AB_%D5%AC%D5%A5%D5%BC%D5%B6%D5%A5%D6%80_15.jpg/440px-%D5%88%D6%82%D6%80%D5%AE%D5%AB_%D5%AC%D5%A5%D5%BC%D5%B6%D5%A5%D6%80_15.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:%D5%88%D6%82%D6%80%D5%AE%D5%AB_%D5%AC%D5%A5%D5%BC%D5%B6%D5%A5%D6%80_15.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The mountains of Urts at the north and east of Ararat</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Ararat is situated at the east of the central part of modern-day Armenia, occupying the southeastern part of the <a href="/wiki/Ararat_plain" title="Ararat plain">Ararat plain</a>, only 7 km east of <a href="/wiki/Aras_(river)" title="Aras (river)">Araks river</a> on the Armenia-Turkey border. Historically, the current territory of the town was part of the <i>Vostan Hayots</i> canton of <a href="/wiki/Ayrarat" title="Ayrarat">Ayrarat</a> province of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Armenia_(antiquity)" title="Kingdom of Armenia (antiquity)">Ancient Armenia</a>.</p>
<p>At an approximate height of 825 meters above sea level, the town is surrounded by the villages of <a href="/wiki/Avshar,_Ararat" title="Avshar, Ararat">Avshar</a> at the northwest, and <a href="/wiki/Ararat_(village),_Armenia" title="Ararat (village), Armenia">Ararat</a> at the west and the south. The <a href="/wiki/Goravan_Sands_Sanctuary" title="Goravan Sands Sanctuary">Goravan Sands Sanctuary</a> is located at the northeast of the town, while the mountains of Urts dominate over the town from the east and the north.</p>
<p>The town is generally characterized with an extremely arid climate, with low precipitation levels. Winters are cold and snowy, with temperature degrees ranging between -15 and -30 °C. Summers are long and hot.</p>
<p>Howvere, the ecology of the town is in danger due to emissions of cement dust and <a href="/wiki/Cyanide" title="Cyanide">cyanide</a> from the gold manufacturing plant. In summer 2005, the impurity of the atmosphere was recorded to be 9.6 times the acceptable norm.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ararat,_Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The majority of the population in Ararat are ethnic Armenians who belong to the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Apostolic_Church" title="Armenian Apostolic Church">Armenian Apostolic Church</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Yerevan" title="Yerevan">Yerevan</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Araratian_Pontifical_Diocese" title="Araratian Pontifical Diocese">Araratian Pontifical Diocese</a> covers the <a href="/wiki/Ararat_Province" title="Ararat Province">Ararat Province</a> including the town of Ararat.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ararat,_Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The population of Ararat are mainly Christians who belong to the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Apostolic_Church" title="Armenian Apostolic Church">Armenian Apostolic Church</a>. The town's Holy Saviour Church is under construction since October 2012.</p>
<p>However, there is a large presence of <a href="/wiki/Jehovah%27s_Witnesses" title="Jehovah's Witnesses">Jehovah's Witnesses</a> in Ararat, as well as adherents of other religious movements.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ararat,_Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Ararat has a cultural palace located at the central square near the town hall. The town is also home to an art academy, a sports school and a youth creativity centre.</p>
<p>On 16 September 2009, the statue of sparapet <a href="/wiki/Vazgen_Sargsyan" title="Vazgen Sargsyan">Vazgen Sargsyan</a> was erected at the central town square of Ararat.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ararat,_Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ararat is a major point on the M-2 Motorway that connects the capital Yerevan with southern Armenia reaching up to the <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iranian</a> border.</p>
<p>The town has a railway station that used to connect Yerevan with the <a href="/wiki/Nakhchivan_Autonomous_Republic" title="Nakhchivan Autonomous Republic">Nakhchivan Autonomous Republic</a> during the Soviet years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ararat,_Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town of Ararat is home to 2 major industrial firms: the "<a href="/wiki/Ararat_Cement" title="Ararat Cement">Ararat Cement</a>" factory and the "Geopromining Gold" recovery plant. Other enterprises include the "AKZ Ararat Cognac Factory" for brandy and wine, opened in 1932 in the nearby village of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Avshar,_Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Avshar, Armenia (page does not exist)">Avshar</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy_over_the_Ararat_Gold_Company">Controversy over the Ararat Gold Company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ararat,_Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Controversy over the Ararat Gold Company">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ararat_gold_tailings_dump_-_Ararat_town_in_background.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2736" data-file-width="3648" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Ararat_gold_tailings_dump_-_Ararat_town_in_background.JPG/220px-Ararat_gold_tailings_dump_-_Ararat_town_in_background.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Ararat_gold_tailings_dump_-_Ararat_town_in_background.JPG/330px-Ararat_gold_tailings_dump_-_Ararat_town_in_background.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Ararat_gold_tailings_dump_-_Ararat_town_in_background.JPG/440px-Ararat_gold_tailings_dump_-_Ararat_town_in_background.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ararat_gold_tailings_dump_-_Ararat_town_in_background.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The massive toxic tailing pond of the Ararat gold processing facility with the town of Ararat in the background</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Besides the various enterprises based on the manufacturing of building materials, the town is also home to the Geopromining Gold recovery plant of Ararat, which extracts gold from the raw ore sent from the gold mine in <a href="/wiki/Sotk" title="Sotk">Sotk</a> which is 20 kilometres (12 miles) east of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Sevan" title="Lake Sevan">Lake Sevan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hetq-Online-May-19-2008-AGRC_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hetq-Online-May-19-2008-AGRC-3">[3]</a></sup> About 0.46 grams of gold is extracted from each ton of sand unearthed at the mines. The extraction process involves first pulverizing the raw material, and then filtering out the gold using a cyanide nitrate chemical process.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hetq-Online-May-19-2008-AGRC_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hetq-Online-May-19-2008-AGRC-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The soupy byproduct of the cyanide nitrate chemical process is both toxic and radioactive and collects in a tailing pond. There have been numerous incidents of animals dying near and around the plant's area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hetq-Online-May-19-2008-AGRC_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hetq-Online-May-19-2008-AGRC-3">[3]</a></sup> Also, within the years 2003 and 2008, there have been at least 10 accidents at the plant, some of which have resulted in the discharge of the cyanide soup into neighboring agricultural lands and fisheries, killing off cows and fish stock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hetq-Online-May-19-2008-AGRC_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Hetq-Online-May-19-2008-AGRC-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ararat,_Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2016, Ararat has 4 public education schools. The town has also its medical centre and number of art, music and sport schools.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ararat,_Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ayg_stadium,_Ararat.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="525" data-file-width="699" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Ayg_stadium%2C_Ararat.jpg/220px-Ayg_stadium%2C_Ararat.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Ayg_stadium%2C_Ararat.jpg/330px-Ayg_stadium%2C_Ararat.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Ayg_stadium%2C_Ararat.jpg/440px-Ayg_stadium%2C_Ararat.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ayg_stadium,_Ararat.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Ayg_Stadium" title="Ayg Stadium">Ayg Stadium</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Araks_Ararat_FC" title="Araks Ararat FC">Araks Ararat</a> had represented the town in domestic and international football competitions. The club was founded in 1960 and dissolved in 2001 due to financial difficulties. They won the Armenian Premier League in 2 occasions: in 1998 as Tsement Ararat and in 2000 as Araks Ararat. In 2001, the president of Araks Ararat; Abraham Babayan was unable to finance the club. Consequently, the rights of the club were sold to a new sponsor based in <a href="/wiki/Yerevan" title="Yerevan">Yerevan</a> in August 2001, and Araks Ararat FC was dissolved.</p>
<p>At the end of 2001, another football club with the name of <a href="/wiki/FC_Araks" title="FC Araks">FC Araks</a> was founded in the town, in an effort to keep the football traditions alive among the new generation of Ararat. However, after 4 years of major difficulties, the financially struggling club was dissolved in 2005.</p>
<p>The town is served by the all-seater <a href="/wiki/Ayg_Stadium" title="Ayg Stadium">Ayg Stadium</a> with a capacity of 1,280 spectators. It is part of a large sport complex with many other structures including an indoor sports hall, indoor swimming pool, billiards saloon, mini-football ground and other facilities.</p>
<p>The retired Olympic cyclist <a href="/wiki/Arsen_Ghazaryan" title="Arsen Ghazaryan">Arsen Ghazaryan</a> is a native of Ararat.</p>
