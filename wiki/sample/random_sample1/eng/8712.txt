<p>The <b>Republic of Ararat</b> or <b>Kurdish Republic of Ararat</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Abbas_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Abbas-8">[8]</a></sup> was a self-proclaimed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurdish_people" title="Kurdish people">Kurdish</a> state. It was located in eastern <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, being centered on <a href="/wiki/A%C4%9Fr%C4%B1_Province" title="Ağrı Province">Karaköse Province</a>. Agirî is the Kurdish name for Ararat.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Ararat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Republic of Ararat, led by the central committee of <a href="/wiki/Xoyb%C3%BBn" title="Xoybûn">Xoybûn party</a>, declared independence on October 28, 1927<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schmidt_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Schmidt-5">[5]</a></sup> or 1928,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Abbas_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Abbas-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> during a wave of rebellion among Kurds in southeastern Turkey.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Ararat_rebellion" title="Ararat rebellion">Ararat rebellion</a> was led by General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ihsan_Nuri_Pasha" title="Ihsan Nuri Pasha">Ihsan Nuri Pasha</a>. In October 1927, Kurd Ava<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jwaideh_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Jwaideh-1">[1]</a></sup> or Kurdava,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sayan_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sayan-2">[2]</a></sup> a village near <a href="/wiki/Mount_Ararat" title="Mount Ararat">Mount Ararat</a> was designated as the provisional capital of <a href="/wiki/Kurdistan" title="Kurdistan">Kurdistan</a>. Xoybûn made appeals to the Great Powers and the <a href="/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a>, and also sent messages to other Kurds in <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> and <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a> to ask for co-operation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Edmonds1971_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Edmonds1971-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Turkish military subsequently defeated the Republic of Ararat in September 1930.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
