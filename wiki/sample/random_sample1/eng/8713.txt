<p><b>Kim Renard Nazel</b> (born June 17, 1965),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> better known by the <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Arabian Prince</b>, is an American singer-songwriter, rapper and producer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arabian_Prince&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Arabian Prince started working with <a href="/wiki/Russ_Parr" title="Russ Parr">Bobby Jimmy &amp; the Critters</a> in 1984. He also produced the hit single and album for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J.J._Fad" title="J.J. Fad">J.J. Fad</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Supersonic_(J.J._Fad_song)" title="Supersonic (J.J. Fad song)">Supersonic</a>".</p>
<p>In 1986, he was a founding member of <a href="/wiki/N.W.A" title="N.W.A">N.W.A</a> but when fellow member <a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a> came back from the Phoenix Institute of Technology in 1988, Arabian Prince found himself to be surplus to the group—<a href="/wiki/Eazy-E" title="Eazy-E">Eazy-E</a>, Ice Cube and <a href="/wiki/MC_Ren" title="MC Ren">MC Ren</a> were the main performers, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Yella" title="DJ Yella">DJ Yella</a> was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turntablist" title="Turntablist">turntablist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a> was the main producer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>After leaving N.W.A, Arabian Prince began a solo career. His first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Brother_Arab" title="Brother Arab">Brother Arab</a></i>, was released in 1989; <i><a href="/wiki/Where%27s_My_Bytches" title="Where's My Bytches">Where's My Bytches</a></i> followed in 1993.</p>
<p>In the mid-2000s, he started releasing music again, with his Professor X project on the Dutch label Clone Records. In 2007 he performed as a DJ on the 2K Sports Holiday Bounce Tour with artists from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stones_Throw" title="Stones Throw">Stones Throw</a> label. In 2008, Stones Throw released a compilation of his electro-rap material from the 1980s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> One of his songs was included on the 2007 video game, <i><a href="/wiki/College_Hoops_2K8" title="College Hoops 2K8">College Hoops 2K8</a></i>.</p>
