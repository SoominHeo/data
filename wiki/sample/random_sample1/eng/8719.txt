<p><b>Masahiro Araki (荒木 雅博)</b> (born September 13, 1977 in <a href="/wiki/Kumamoto" title="Kumamoto">Kumamoto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> player. He currently is the second baseman for the <a href="/wiki/Chunichi_Dragons" title="Chunichi Dragons">Chunichi Dragons</a>. He is noted for his speed and defense. Going into the 2017 season, Araki has a good chance of reaching 2000 hits and joining the <a href="/wiki/Meikyukai" title="Meikyukai">Meikyukai</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Masahiro_Araki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Araki was a first-round draft pick by Chunichi in 1995, out of high school. In <a href="/wiki/1997_in_baseball" title="1997 in baseball">1997</a>, he made his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NPB" title="NPB">NPB</a> debut and hit .179/.203/.209 in 74 plate appearances; he did steal 12 bases in 16 tries to lead his team. Araki was used as a defensive substitute in the outfield and at shortstop.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1998_in_baseball" title="1998 in baseball">1998</a>, the 20-year-old played 7 games for Chunichi but had just one at-bat, appearing as a pinch-runner and defensive sub. <a href="/wiki/1999_in_baseball" title="1999 in baseball">The next year</a>, he went 1 for 4 in 16 games in a similar role; in four steal attempts, he was only successful once. In <a href="/wiki/2000_in_baseball" title="2000 in baseball">2000</a>, Araki remained a substitute, playing 40 games but only getting 12 plate appearances (2 for 10, 2 sacrifice flies, 3 SB in 3 tries). He spent 34 games in the outfield.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Increased_Role">Increased Role</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Masahiro_Araki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Increased Role">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Araki became a semi-regular in <a href="/wiki/2001_in_baseball" title="2001 in baseball">2001</a> and produced at a .338/.384/.438 clip with 13 steals and 9 times caught stealing. He backed up veteran <a href="/wiki/Kazuyoshi_Tatsunami" title="Kazuyoshi Tatsunami">Kazuyoshi Tatsunami</a> at second base. In <a href="/wiki/2002_in_baseball" title="2002 in baseball">2002</a>, Tatsunami moved to third so Araki could take over at second. He batted .259/.279/.296 and stole 16 bases in 20 attempts. He fielded .986 and led Chunichi in steals. In <a href="/wiki/2003_in_baseball" title="2003 in baseball">2003</a>, the speedster hit .237/.283/.314 with 15 steals in 24 attempts. He fielded .990 and again led the Dragons in swipes.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Star">Star</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Masahiro_Araki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Star">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Araki improved significantly in <a href="/wiki/2004_in_baseball" title="2004 in baseball">2004</a>, batting .292/.322/.349, stealing 39 bases in 48 tries and scored 93 runs. He replaced <a href="/wiki/Hirokazu_Ibata" title="Hirokazu Ibata">Hirokazu Ibata</a> at the top of the Dragons batting order, Ibata dropping to second to form a swift 1-2 punch. He fielded .992 and won his first Gold Glove. He was second in the <a href="/wiki/Central_League" title="Central League">Central League</a> in steals behind <a href="/wiki/Norihiro_Akahoshi" title="Norihiro Akahoshi">Norihiro Akahoshi</a> and tied <a href="/wiki/Toshihisa_Nishi" title="Toshihisa Nishi">Toshihisa Nishi</a> for third in hits (176). He made his first All-Star team. That year, Araki and <a href="/wiki/Greg_LaRocca" title="Greg LaRocca">Greg LaRocca</a> tied for the CL <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Best_Nine&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Best Nine (page does not exist)">Best Nine</a> at second base; it was the first tie ever in voting for a Best Nine in NPB history. He hit .267/.333/.433 in the <a href="/wiki/2004_Japan_Series" title="2004 Japan Series">2004 Japan Series</a> as Chunichi fell in 7 games.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/2005_in_baseball" title="2005 in baseball">2005</a>, Araki's batting line was .291/.332/.345; he scored 88 runs and stole 42 bases in 53 tries. He set a CL record with 623 AB, tied Ibata for 5th in the league in hits (181), was 9th in runs (88) and second to Akahoshi in stolen bases. He made his second All-Star team and was named to the Best Nine. He also set NPB records for <a href="/wiki/Total_chances" title="Total chances">total chances</a> (913) and assists (496) by a second sacker. He won his second Gold Glove.</p>
<p>Araki hit .300/.338/.358 in <a href="/wiki/2006_in_baseball" title="2006 in baseball">2006</a> and stole 30 bases in 37 tries. He was 10th in the league in average and third in steals, behind <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norichika_Aoki" title="Norichika Aoki">Norichika Aoki</a> and Akahoshi. He was again an All-Star and Best Nine. His 12 errors were a new career high but he won his third Gold Glove. In the <a href="/wiki/2006_Japan_Series" title="2006 Japan Series">2006 Japan Series</a>, he was just 2 for 18 with two walks and a steal.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/2007_in_baseball" title="2007 in baseball">2007</a>, Masahiro batted .263/.296/.302 with 31 steals in 37 tries. He was dropped to second in the lineup, flipped with double-play partner Ibata in the lineup. He led the CL in steals and was second with 30 sacrifice hits. In the <a href="/wiki/2007_Japan_Series" title="2007 Japan Series">2007 Japan Series</a>, Araki hit .350/.350/.381 with 4 steals and 5 runs in 5 games to help Chunichi to its first <a href="/wiki/Japan_Series" title="Japan Series">Japan Series</a> title in 53 years.</p>
<p>Araki joined the Japanese national team for the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Asian_Championship_Baseball&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="2007 Asian Championship Baseball (page does not exist)">2007 Asian Championship</a>; appearing as a pinch-runner, he scored twice in three games and never batted, played the field or attempted to steal. In the second 2008 NPB All-Star Game, Araki won MVP honors, going 3 for 4 with 3 RBI in leading the CL to victory. In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_Olympics" title="2008 Olympics">2008 Olympics</a>, Araki hit .263/.364/.421 with 2 steals and 5 runs in 8 games, being used as Japan's main second baseman, playing flawless defense. Araki laid down 5 sacrifice bunts for traditionalist manager <a href="/wiki/Senichi_Hoshino" title="Senichi Hoshino">Senichi Hoshino</a>'s squad. In the Bronze Medal Game, Araki hit a first-inning solo homer off of <a href="/wiki/Brett_Anderson_(baseball)" title="Brett Anderson (baseball)">Brett Anderson</a> to put Japan ahead but the USA came back for an 8-4 win as Japan failed to get a Medal. Araki tied for the most steals in the preliminary rounds, as his 2 tied <a href="/wiki/Giorvis_Duvergel" title="Giorvis Duvergel">Giorvis Duvergel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hyun-soo_Kim" title="Hyun-soo Kim">Hyun-soo Kim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tsuyoshi_Nishioka" title="Tsuyoshi Nishioka">Tsuyoshi Nishioka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eduardo_Paret" title="Eduardo Paret">Eduardo Paret</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jong-wook_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jong-wook Lee (page does not exist)">Jong-wook Lee</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lingfeng_Sun" title="Lingfeng Sun">Lingfeng Sun</a>.</p>
