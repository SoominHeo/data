<p><b>Aratama-bashi Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">新瑞橋駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Aratama-bashi-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is an underground <a href="/wiki/Interchange_station" title="Interchange station">Interchange</a> <a href="/wiki/Metro_station" title="Metro station">metro station</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Mizuho-ku,_Nagoya" title="Mizuho-ku, Nagoya">Mizuho-ku</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nagoya" title="Nagoya">Nagoya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aichi_Prefecture" title="Aichi Prefecture">Aichi Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> operated by the <a href="/wiki/Nagoya_Municipal_Subway" title="Nagoya Municipal Subway">Nagoya Municipal Subway</a>. <a href="/wiki/Meij%C5%8D_Line" title="Meijō Line">Meijō Line</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nagoya_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nagoya-1">[1]</a></sup> It is located 20.7 kilometers from the terminus of the <a href="/wiki/Meij%C5%8D_Line" title="Meijō Line">Meijō Line</a> at <a href="/wiki/Kanayama_Station_(Aichi)" title="Kanayama Station (Aichi)">Kanayama Station</a> and 11.8 kilometers from the terminus of the <a href="/wiki/Sakura-d%C5%8Dri_Line" title="Sakura-dōri Line">Sakura-dōri Line</a> at <a href="/wiki/Nakamura_Kuyakusho_Station" title="Nakamura Kuyakusho Station">Nakamura Kuyakusho Station</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aratama-bashi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aratama-bashi Station was opened on 30 March 1974 as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terminal_station" title="Terminal station">terminal station</a> for the Nagoya Municipal Subway Line No.4, which was later renamed the Meijō Line. The Sakura-dōri Line connected to the station on 30 March 1994. The Meijō Line was extended to <a href="/wiki/Nagoya_Daigaku_Station" title="Nagoya Daigaku Station">Nagoya Daigaku Station</a> on 6 October 2004.</p>
