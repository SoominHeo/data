<p>The <b>florin</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Currency_sign" title="Currency sign">sign</a>: <b>Afl.</b>; <a href="/wiki/ISO_4217" title="ISO 4217">code</a>: <b>AWG</b>) is the currency of <a href="/wiki/Aruba" title="Aruba">Aruba</a>. It is subdivided into 100 cents. The florin was introduced in 1986, replacing the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_Antillean_guilder" title="Netherlands Antillean guilder">Netherlands Antillean guilder</a> at par. Although the Aruban florin is pegged with 1.79 US$, the commonly used street value is at 1.75 US$.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coins">Coins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aruban_florin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Coins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1986, coins were introduced in denominations of 5, 10, 25 and 50 cents, 1 and <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap">2<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>2</sub></span> florin. Later, the 5 florin banknote was replaced by a square coin and the <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap">2<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>2</sub></span> florin coin was removed from circulation. The 5 florin was later on in 2005 replaced with a round golden coin, because the old square 5 florin coin was too easy to counterfeit. All coins are struck in nickel-bonded steel with exception of the 5 florin, which is an alloy of copper and other metals. The 50 cent is the only square-shaped coin remaining, also commonly referred to as a "yotin" by the locals.</p>
<p>On the back of each 1, <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap">2<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>2</sub></span> and 5 florin coins is the profile view of the current head of state of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Kingdom of the Netherlands">Kingdom of the Netherlands</a>. From 1986 till 2013 it was queen <a href="/wiki/Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands" title="Beatrix of the Netherlands">Beatrix</a> and from 2014 onwards it is king <a href="/wiki/Willem-Alexander_of_the_Netherlands" title="Willem-Alexander of the Netherlands">Willem-Alexander</a>. Moreover, only these coins have a writing on their edge, 'God Zij Met Ons' meaning 'God Be With Us'.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Banknotes">Banknotes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aruban_florin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Banknotes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Central_Bank_of_Aruba" title="Central Bank of Aruba">Central Bank of Aruba</a> introduced banknotes in denominations of 5, 10, 25, 50 and 100 florin and dated January 1, 1986.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1990, the bank issued the same denominations in a colorful new family of notes designed by Aruban artist Evelino Fingal. As director of the Archaeological Museum, Fingal found inspiration in old Indian paintings and pot shards. Fingal combined decorative motives found on pre-Columbian pottery with pictures of animals unique to the island. The 500-florin notes were introduced in 1993, with the 5-florin note replaced by a square coin in 1995.</p>
<p>As of 2003 a new print was started of the then already existing banknotes of 10, 25, 50, 100 and 500 florin. These new banknotes were made with new safety features to counteract counterfeiting, but retained their look.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
