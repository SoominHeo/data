<p><b>Arnor</b> is a fictional kingdom in <a href="/wiki/J._R._R._Tolkien" title="J. R. R. Tolkien">J. R. R. Tolkien</a>'s writings. Arnor, or the <b>North-kingdom</b>, was a realm of the <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BAnedain" title="Dúnedain">Dúnedain</a> in the region of <a href="/wiki/Eriador" title="Eriador">Eriador</a> in <a href="/wiki/Middle-earth" title="Middle-earth">Middle Earth</a> (their South-kingdom was <a href="/wiki/Gondor" title="Gondor">Gondor</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Middle-earth_narrative">Middle-earth narrative</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Middle-earth narrative">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The name 'Arnor' probably means "Land of the King", from <a href="/wiki/Sindarin" title="Sindarin">Sindarin</a> <i>ara-</i> (high, kingly) + <i>(n)dor</i> (land). Arnor is the territory of Middle-earth associated with the High Kings of the line of <a href="/wiki/Elendil" title="Elendil">Elendil</a>, the kingship of which was restored at the crowning of Elessar (<a href="/wiki/Aragorn" title="Aragorn">Aragorn</a>) after the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Ring" title="War of the Ring">War of the Ring</a> at the start of the <a href="/wiki/Fourth_Age" title="Fourth Age">Fourth Age</a>.</p>
<p>At its greatest, Arnor encompassed almost the whole region of Eriador between the rivers <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bruinen" title="Bruinen">Bruinen</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gwathl%C3%B3" title="Gwathló">Gwathló</a> on the east and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lh%C3%BBn" title="Lhûn">Lhûn</a> on the west. It included Bree and the region which would later be known as <a href="/wiki/Shire_(Middle-earth)" title="Shire (Middle-earth)">the Shire</a>. Arnor's population included Dúnedain in western-central regions and mixed or indigenous peoples. The original capital was <a href="#Ann.C3.BAminas">Annúminas</a> near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lake_Evendim" title="Lake Evendim">Lake Nenuial</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Arnor was founded at the end of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_Age" title="Second Age">Second Age</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_Age" title="Second Age">S.A.</a> 3320) by <a href="/wiki/Elendil" title="Elendil">Elendil</a>, whose sons <a href="/wiki/Isildur" title="Isildur">Isildur</a> and <a href="/wiki/An%C3%A1rion" title="Anárion">Anárion</a> founded <a href="/wiki/Gondor" title="Gondor">Gondor</a> at the same time. The history of the two kingdoms is intertwined; both kingdoms are known as the Realms of the <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BAnedain" title="Dúnedain">Dúnedain</a> in Exile.</p>
<p>Before the foundation of Arnor, some <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BAmenor" title="Númenor">Númenórean</a> already lived there, a result of slow emigration of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/N%C3%BAmen%C3%B3reans" title="Númenóreans">Númenóreans</a> which had started under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tar-Meneldur" title="Tar-Meneldur">Tar-Meneldur</a> and especially <a href="/wiki/Tar-Aldarion" title="Tar-Aldarion">Tar-Aldarion</a>. The principal Númenórean haven was Vinyalondë, later called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lond_Daer" title="Lond Daer">Lond Daer</a>, at the mouth of the Gwathló. Before the arrival of the Dúnedain, Arnor was also home to <a href="/wiki/Men_of_Twilight" title="Men of Twilight">Middle Men</a> of <a href="/wiki/Edain" title="Edain">Edain</a> stock, and the early colonists soon interbred with the indigenous population. The Númenóreans who fell under <a href="/wiki/Sauron" title="Sauron">Sauron</a>'s shadow settled primarily in <a href="/wiki/Umbar" title="Umbar">Umbar</a>, far to the south. Thus, Elendil arrived in an area populated by people who, unlike his contemporaries in Númenor itself, were mainly still friends with the Elves, and who retained knowledge of the <a href="/wiki/Elder_Days" title="Elder Days">Elder Days</a>.</p>
<p>Arnor's second king was Isildur, who was also the King of Gondor, where he had ruled jointly with his brother <a href="/wiki/An%C3%A1rion" title="Anárion">Anárion</a> until the latter's death. When he left Gondor to take up the rule of Arnor, Isildur committed Gondor to Anárion's son; but Isildur was killed en route in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Age" title="Third Age">T.A.</a> 2 by <a href="/wiki/Orc_(Middle-earth)" title="Orc (Middle-earth)">orcs</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disaster_of_the_Gladden_Fields" title="Disaster of the Gladden Fields">Disaster of the Gladden Fields</a>, and his three eldest sons were killed with him. Only his youngest son, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valandil_of_Arnor" title="Valandil of Arnor">Valandil</a>, survived: being only a child at the start of the war, Valandil had remained behind in Rivendell. In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Age" title="Third Age">T.A.</a> 10, after several years being tutored by <a href="/wiki/Elrond" title="Elrond">Elrond</a>, Valandil became the third king of Arnor.</p>
<p>For several centuries, Arnor's rulers styled themselves High King, following the precedent of Elendil, who ruled Arnor directly while holding <a href="/wiki/Suzerainty" title="Suzerainty">suzerainty</a> over Gondor; the rulers of Gondor, by contrast, were merely styled King. Nevertheless, Valandil and his successors never made any serious attempt to assert their overlordship in Gondor; after the death of Isildur, the two realms developed as equal and independent states.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Decline">Decline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Decline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>With the victory of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Last_Alliance" title="War of the Last Alliance">War of the Last Alliance</a>, Arnor's power was apparently at its zenith. The King of Arnor held the overlordship of all the land from the Bay of Forochel to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poros_(Middle-earth)" title="Poros (Middle-earth)">River Poros</a> on the southern borders of <a href="/wiki/Ithilien" title="Ithilien">Ithilien</a>, and from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ered_Luin" title="Ered Luin">Blue Mountains</a> to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ephel_Duath" title="Ephel Duath">Mountains of Shadow</a>. But in reality Arnor's strength had been severely depleted by the war and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disaster_of_the_Gladden_Fields" title="Disaster of the Gladden Fields">Disaster of the Gladden Fields</a>, and the northern Dúnedain never really recovered from their losses. The first few centuries of the Third Age were relatively uneventful, but it seems that Arnor's population gradually began to dwindle even in this early period.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Successor_states">Successor states</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Successor states">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After the death of its tenth king, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/E%C3%A4rendur_of_Arnor" title="Eärendur of Arnor">Eärendur</a>, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Age" title="Third Age">T.A.</a> 861, dissension among his three sons led to the division of Arnor. The eldest son, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amlaith" title="Amlaith">Amlaith</a>, claimed Kingship over all Arnor but was reduced to ruling only the region of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arthedain" title="Arthedain">Arthedain</a> as his kingdom, while the other sons founded the kingdoms of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardolan" title="Cardolan">Cardolan</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhudaur" title="Rhudaur">Rhudaur</a>. The former capital, Annúminas, became depopulated and fell into ruin. The capital of Arthedain was relocated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fornost_Erain" title="Fornost Erain">Fornost Erain</a> on the North Downs.</p>
<p>This division hastened the decline of the northern Dúnedain. The three kingdoms had frequent border skirmishes over boundary disputes, but the relationship of Arthedain and Cardolan remained relatively peaceful. Rhudaur, by contrast, was unfriendly towards the two other successor states, and fought a bitter conflict with Cardolan and Arthedain over the tower of Amon Sûl and the possession of its <i>palantír</i>.</p>
<p>Around <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Age" title="Third Age">T.A.</a> 1300 during the reign of the sixth king at Fornost, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Witch-king" title="Witch-king">Witch-king</a> arose in the mountains north-east of Arnor, where he founded the evil realm of <a href="/wiki/Angmar" title="Angmar">Angmar</a>. Many evil men, orcs, and other fell creatures gathered there with the intent of destroying the realm of Arnor. He was later discovered to be the leader of the <a href="/wiki/Nazg%C3%BBl" title="Nazgûl">Nazgûl</a>, who were dispersed after the first overthrow of Sauron in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_Age" title="Second Age">S.A.</a> 3434 at the hands of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Last_Alliance_of_Elves_and_Men" title="Last Alliance of Elves and Men">Last Alliance of Elves and Men</a>, but survived nonetheless. Since Gondor was still strong, the Witch-king came north to destroy Arnor instead.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Fall">Fall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Fall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The last Kings of Rhudaur were not of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/N%C3%BAmen%C3%B3rean" title="Númenórean">Númenórean</a> blood, but descended from men in the service of Angmar. Rhudaur became a vassal of Angmar and an enemy of Cardolan and Arthedain. Angmar annexed and terminated the kingdom in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Age" title="Third Age">T.A.</a> 1409. By this time no Dúnedain remained in the region, and power had been claimed by an evil chieftain of the hillmen. Constant battle with Angmar began to wear down the forces of Arthedain, while Cardolan as an independent entity came to an end shortly after invasion by Angmar in 1409.</p>
<p>The Kings of Arthedain reclaimed the name of Arnor when the line of Elendil became extinct in Cardolan and Rhudaur, and in token of this chose names using the prefix <i>Ar(a)</i>; but this kingdom was only a small rump of the large and powerful realm of Arnor before its division. For the next several centuries after the destruction of Amon Sûl, Arnor continued to hold back the assaults of Angmar with dwindling strength and resolve. At this time no help could be sent from Gondor as it was under attack by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wainriders" title="Wainriders">Wainriders</a>, even after a re-establishment of relations with the wedding of Arnor's King <a href="/wiki/Arvedui" title="Arvedui">Arvedui</a> to <a href="/wiki/Gondor" title="Gondor">Gondor</a>'s <a href="/wiki/F%C3%ADriel" title="Fíriel">King Ondoher's daughter</a>. Gondor refused Arvedui's claim to joint kingship of Arnor and Gondor, and Arnor continued to stand alone against Angmar.</p>
<p>Arthedain was finally destroyed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Age" title="Third Age">T.A.</a> 1974, when the Witch-king captured Fornost. The next year, in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Fornost" title="Battle of Fornost">Battle of Fornost</a>, a coalition of Elves, forces of Gondor, and the remainder of Arnor's armies routed the Witch-king's forces and destroyed Angmar. Eriador was vastly depopulated by the war, and very few people remained. The <a href="/wiki/Hobbit" title="Hobbit">Hobbits</a> survived relatively unscathed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Shire_(Lord_of_the_Rings)" title="The Shire (Lord of the Rings)">the Shire</a>, men survived in <a href="/wiki/Bree_(Middle-earth)" title="Bree (Middle-earth)">Bree</a> and other villages, and the Dúnedain of Arnor created new homes in the Angle west of <a href="/wiki/Rivendell" title="Rivendell">Rivendell</a>, where they became known as the <a href="/wiki/Rangers_of_the_North" title="Rangers of the North">Rangers of the North</a>. They became an isolated, wandering people, who defended the borders of <a href="/wiki/Bree_(Middle-earth)" title="Bree (Middle-earth)">Bree</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Shire_(Lord_of_the_Rings)" title="The Shire (Lord of the Rings)">the Shire</a> from the perils in the wild.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Reunited_Kingdom">Reunited Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Reunited Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Early in the <a href="/wiki/Fourth_Age" title="Fourth Age">Fourth Age</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aragorn" title="Aragorn">Aragorn</a> as King Elessar refounded the Kingdom of Arnor as part of the <a href="/wiki/Reunited_Kingdom" title="Reunited Kingdom">Reunited Kingdom</a>, and again made Annúminas the capital city.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Successor_kingdoms">Successor kingdoms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Successor kingdoms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Arthedain">Arthedain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Arthedain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Arthedain was bounded upon the north by Forochel and the west by the Lune; upon the east by the Weather Hills and the south by the Baranduin. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardolan" title="Cardolan">Cardolan</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhudaur" title="Rhudaur">Rhudaur</a> lay to the south and east.</p>
<p>The kingdom's capital was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fornost" title="Fornost">Fornost</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bree_(Middle-earth)" title="Bree (Middle-earth)">Bree</a> was one of its important towns. Annúminas lay in the territory of Arthedain, but was mostly ruinous and abandoned.</p>
<p>Around T.A. 1300 the kingdom of <a href="/wiki/Angmar" title="Angmar">Angmar</a> appeared at Arthedain's north-eastern border. Its king, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Witch-king" title="Witch-king">Witch-king</a> of Angmar, was the chief of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ringwraith" title="Ringwraith">Ringwraiths</a>, although this was not known to the <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BAnedain" title="Dúnedain">Dúnedain</a> at the time. Rhudaur, aided by Angmar, attacked in T.A. 1356. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argeleb_I" title="Argeleb I">Argeleb I</a> died in this conflict along the Weather Hills. When this new threat came, Cardolan placed itself under the suzerainty of Arthedain, which then began to call itself <b>Arnor</b> again. Cardolan repeatedly sent aid to Arthedain when needed, but in T.A. 1409 Cardolan and Rhudaur were destroyed by Angmar, and Arthedain survived only with the help of Elvish reinforcements from <a href="/wiki/Lothl%C3%B3rien" title="Lothlórien">Lórien</a> and the Havens.</p>
<p>After 1409 Angmar's power was temporarily broken and the North Kingdom enjoyed relative peace, although its population continued to decline. Indeed, the decline was so severe that in 1601 Argeleb II (r. 1589–1670) granted a large portion of Arthedain's best farmland to Hobbit migrants, as these lands had become deserted. Arthedain was not badly affected by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Plague_(Middle-earth)" title="Great Plague (Middle-earth)">Great Plague</a>, but hostilities with Angmar resumed. King Araval (r. 1813–1891) defeated Angmar in 1851, but Arthedain spent its last decades in desperate conflict with Angmar. In T.A. 1940, Arthedain under Araval's son Araphant (r. 1891–1964) formed an alliance with Gondor, but in the end neither Kingdom was able to provide military assistance to the other. The Witch-king pressed the attack on Arthedain even more vigorously, while Gondor barely survived a massive invasion of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wainrider" title="Wainrider">Wainriders</a> (T.A. 1944), leaving it temporarily unable to send substantial armies abroad. Araphant and his successor <a href="/wiki/Arvedui" title="Arvedui">Arvedui</a> held out against Angmar as long as they could. In T.A. 1973 Arvedui again appealed to Gondor for help, and the King of Gondor, <a href="/wiki/E%C3%A4rnil_II" title="Eärnil II">Eärnil II</a> (r. 1945–2043), sent a fleet north under his son <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/E%C3%A4rnur" title="Eärnur">Eärnur</a>. But it was sent too late: toward the end of T.A. 1974, the Witch-king captured Fornost and overran Arthedain, and the King's sons and most of the other Dúnedain fled across the Lune. Arvedui himself fled northwards and perished in a shipwreck early in T.A. 1975, taking with him the <i>palantíri</i> of Annúminas and Amon Sûl. Eärnur's fleet reached <a href="/wiki/Lindon_(Middle-earth)" title="Lindon (Middle-earth)">Lindon</a> after Arvedui's death. The combined might of Gondor, Lindon and Rivendell, together with soldiers from the former North-kingdom, routed Angmar's army at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Fornost" title="Battle of Fornost">Battle of Fornost</a>.</p>
<p>Though the military threat of Angmar had been destroyed, the North-kingdom was ended. The long wars and a series of natural disasters had taken their toll on the population of Eriador, and the Dúnedain especially were few in number and unable to maintain a kingdom. In T.A. 1976, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aranarth" title="Aranarth">Aranarth</a>, Arvedui's oldest son, took the title of Chieftain of the Dúnedain. He and his descendants led the Rangers of the North; <a href="/wiki/Aragorn" title="Aragorn">Aragorn II</a> was the sixteenth Chieftain until he restored the Kingdom of Arnor in T.A. 3019. The Kingdom of Arthedain had included <a href="/wiki/Shire_(Middle-earth)" title="Shire (Middle-earth)">the Shire</a> (founded in T.A. 1601); in T.A. 1979, the Hobbits there chose a <a href="/wiki/Thain_(Middle-earth)" title="Thain (Middle-earth)">Thain</a> to represent the royal authority.</p>
<p>The name Arthedain appears to be dialect <a href="/wiki/Sindarin" title="Sindarin">Sindarin</a> for "Realm of the Edain".</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Cardolan">Cardolan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Cardolan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The borders of Cardolan extended from the river <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baranduin" title="Baranduin">Baranduin</a> (Brandywine) on the west, the river <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mitheithel" title="Mitheithel">Mitheithel</a> (Hoarwell) on the east and the river <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gwathl%C3%B3" title="Gwathló">Gwathló</a> (Greyflood) on the east and south. Its northern border was parallel to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/East_Road" title="East Road">Great East Road</a>.</p>
<p>Cardolan also claimed the Weather Hills controlled by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arthedain" title="Arthedain">Arthedain</a>, where the fortress of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amon_S%C3%BBl" title="Amon Sûl">Amon Sûl</a> (Weathertop) and its valuable <i><a href="/wiki/Palant%C3%ADr" title="Palantír">palantír</a></i> were located. The Weather Hills were claimed by all three kingdoms. This territorial dispute continued until Rhudaur became a vassal of <a href="/wiki/Angmar" title="Angmar">Angmar</a> after the line of the <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BAnedain" title="Dúnedain">Dúnedain</a> kings failed there.</p>
<p>In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Age" title="Third Age">T.A.</a> 1050, the branch of <a href="/wiki/Hobbit" title="Hobbit">Hobbits</a> known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harfoots" title="Harfoots">Harfoots</a> crossed the <a href="/wiki/Misty_Mountains" title="Misty Mountains">Misty Mountains</a>, and settled in the <a href="#South_Downs">South Downs</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> in the west of Cardolan. They were joined about a century later by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fallohides" title="Fallohides">Fallohides</a>.</p>
<p>When the kingdom of Angmar arose in northern <a href="/wiki/Eriador" title="Eriador">Eriador</a>, Cardolan became an ally of Arthedain against the combined might of Angmar and Rhudaur. In 1356 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argeleb_I" title="Argeleb I">Argeleb I</a> of Arthedain was slain in battle with Rhudaur, now allied with Angmar. For a while Cardolan resisted Angmar, but in 1409 a large army from Angmar broke into Cardolan and devastated the country. Arthedain could provide little aid, as it was itself under attack, and the remnants of the Dunedain fled, taking refuge in the Barrow-downs. The last prince of Cardolan died in this conflict, and Cardolan was shattered. While Arthedain recovered something of its power, Cardolan did not, and the region of the Barrow-downs entered hobbit legend as a place of mystery and danger.</p>
<p>In 1636 the Great Plague claimed the life of the King of Gondor, and withered its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_Tree" title="White Tree">White Tree</a>. The plague spread north along the Great Road that joined the two kingdoms, and the population of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minhiriath" title="Minhiriath">Minhiriath</a> was decimated. About this time the plague also wiped out the Dúnedain hiding in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barrow-downs" title="Barrow-downs">Barrow-downs</a> and evil spirits came to dwell there. When King Araval attempted to resettle Cardolan two centuries later, the settlers were killed and driven off by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barrow-wights" title="Barrow-wights">wights</a>. What few folk survived could offer little aid to Arthedain in 1974, when Angmar overwhelmed the last of the kingdoms of Arnor. Until the end of the Third Age, the Dúnedain of Cardolan were only a memory, their tombs and barrows haunted by the evil wights sent from Angmar; for the Rangers that wandered over the lands were descended from the people of Arthedain. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tharbad" title="Tharbad">Tharbad</a> survived until it was destroyed by floods in 2912.</p>
<p>The name Cardolan appears to be dialect <a href="/wiki/Sindarin" title="Sindarin">Sindarin</a> for "Red Hill Country".</p>
<p>Cardolan nominally included <a href="/wiki/Tom_Bombadil" title="Tom Bombadil">Tom Bombadil</a>'s country, the <a href="/wiki/Old_Forest" title="Old Forest">Old Forest</a>, and the area that later became <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buckland_(Middle-earth)" title="Buckland (Middle-earth)">Buckland</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Rhudaur">Rhudaur</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Rhudaur">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The name Rhudaur appears to be dialect <a href="/wiki/Sindarin" title="Sindarin">Sindarin</a> for "Eastern Forests", and indeed Rhudaur was the most easterly of the three divisions of Arnor. In reality, however, its name means "Evil Wood", although Tolkien did not leave any explanation for its origin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rhudaur stretched from the Weather Hills to the river <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bruinen" title="Bruinen">Bruinen</a> (Loudwater). It shared a long border with Cardolan near the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/East_Road" title="East Road">Great East Road</a>, and with Arthedain along the line of the Weather Hills.</p>
<p>Rhudaur also included land south of the Road between the Bruinen and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mitheithel" title="Mitheithel">Mitheithel</a> (Hoarwell) rivers. This was called <i>the Angle</i>, and it is here that the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stoor" title="Stoor">Stoor</a> <a href="/wiki/Hobbit" title="Hobbit">Hobbits</a> came into <a href="/wiki/Eriador" title="Eriador">Eriador</a> around T.A. 1150.</p>
<p>Rhudaur's <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BAnedain" title="Dúnedain">Dúnedain</a> population was always small, and was always only a small proportion of its people. From its beginning, Rhudaur was unfriendly towards the two other successor states, and waged a long war with Cardolan over the tower of Amon Sûl and the <i><a href="/wiki/Palant%C3%ADr" title="Palantír">palantír</a></i> housed there.</p>
<p>Over time, the more numerous Hillmen came to dominate the population, and one of their leaders, allied with Angmar, seized power from the Dúnedain during the 14th century when the local line of Isildur failed. In T.A. 1356, forces of Rhudaur and Angmar slew the High King Argeleb I in battle; the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stoors" title="Stoors">Stoors</a> who had dwelt in the Angle fled south into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dunland" title="Dunland">Dunland</a>, or returned east over the mountains to the Vale of <a href="/wiki/Anduin" title="Anduin">Anduin</a>. In T.A. 1409, Rhudaur was occupied by "evil Men subject to Angmar",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and the last Dúnedain there were killed or fled the region. Afterward Rhudaur is no longer mentioned as a political entity.</p>
<p>The Great Plague of T.A. 1636 devastated Eriador, including Rhudaur. In T.A. 1975 Angmar and its control over the region were destroyed by a combined army of Gondor and Lindon. The Witch-king fled, and the Hillmen vanished from the histories of Middle-earth. As far south as the Great East Road, Rhudaur became a troll-country; travellers along the Road generally hurried along their way and avoided the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trollshaws" title="Trollshaws">Trollshaws</a>.</p>
<p>There is evidence that after the fall of Angmar at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Fornost" title="Battle of Fornost">Battle of Fornost</a> the Angle became home to the remainder of the Dúnedain, and the <a href="/wiki/Rangers_of_the_North" title="Rangers of the North">Rangers of the North</a> established several villages there, where their people lived until the resurrection of the northern Kingdom under <a href="/wiki/Aragorn" title="Aragorn">King Elessar</a> at the end of the Third Age. But northern Rhudaur remained wild and dangerous for the rest of that Age: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arador" title="Arador">Arador</a> was slain there by hill-trolls in T.A. 2930, and his son <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arathorn_II" title="Arathorn II">Arathorn II</a> fell in battle with orcs in T.A. 2933. In T.A. 2941, <a href="/wiki/Troll_(Middle-earth)" title="Troll (Middle-earth)">trolls</a> captured the company of <a href="/wiki/Thorin_Oakenshield" title="Thorin Oakenshield">Thorin</a> at the start of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit" title="The Hobbit">The Hobbit</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Palant.C3.ADri"><i>Palantíri</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Palantíri">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When <a href="/wiki/Elendil" title="Elendil">Elendil</a> and his sons <a href="/wiki/Isildur" title="Isildur">Isildur</a> and <a href="/wiki/An%C3%A1rion" title="Anárion">Anárion</a> fled the downfall of <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BAmenor" title="Númenor">Númenor</a> at the end of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ages_of_Middle-earth" title="Ages of Middle-earth">Second Age</a>, they carried with them seven of the many <a href="/wiki/Palant%C3%ADr" title="Palantír">seeing-stones</a>, or <i>palantíri</i>, that had been given to them by the Elves. Of these seven, three were kept by <a href="/wiki/Elendil" title="Elendil">Elendil</a> and brought with him to the North-kingdom. These stones were placed in Annúminas, Elostirion, and Amon Sûl, and were used to communicate in the realm, with the exception of the Elostirion stone.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Ann.C3.BAminas_Stone">Annúminas Stone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Annúminas Stone">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <i>palantír</i> of Annúminas was the stone that was placed in the first capital of Arnor on the shores of Lake Evendim. Although it was one of the lesser stones, this was the one that was most used by the Kings of Arnor when it was a unified kingdom. When Annúminas fell into ruin and the capital was removed to Fornost, the <i>palantír</i> was relocated as well. The stone remained in Fornost for centuries, until when the city fell in TA 1974, it was carried to safety by the fleeing King <a href="/wiki/Arvedui" title="Arvedui">Arvedui</a>. The <i>palantír</i> was lost alongside the Amon Sûl stone the next year when the ship that was sent to rescue Arvedui was lost in ice, and it remained lost at the bottom of the ice bay in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forochel" title="Forochel">Forochel</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Amon_S.C3.BBl_Stone">Amon Sûl Stone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Amon Sûl Stone">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <i>palantír</i> of Amon Sûl was the greatest of the three stones that resided in Arnor, and could not be lifted by a single man. The stone was placed in the great watchtower of Amon Sûl on the Weather Hills, and it was a source of great contention between the three successor realms after Arnor's division. In TA 1409, <a href="/wiki/Angmar" title="Angmar">Angmar</a> broke through Arnor's defences and razed the watchtower upon Amon Sûl, but the <i>palantír</i> was carried back to safety at Fornost. The stone shared the same fate as the one of Annúminas when it was drowned in the ice-bay of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forochel" title="Forochel">Forochel</a> with King Arvedui in TA 1975.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Elostirion_Stone">Elostirion Stone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Elostirion Stone">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <i>palantír</i> of Elostirion was unique among the stones of <a href="/wiki/Middle-earth" title="Middle-earth">Middle-earth</a> in that it only looked out upon the sea. The stone was placed in the tower by <a href="/wiki/Elendil" title="Elendil">Elendil</a> after his flight out of <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BAmenor" title="Númenor">Númenor</a>, and it remained safe after the fall of Arnor in the keeping of <a href="/wiki/C%C3%ADrdan" title="Círdan">Círdan</a>, and Elves would make pilgrimages to view the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Straight_Road" title="Straight Road">Straight Road</a> back to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Undying_Lands" title="Undying Lands">Undying Lands</a>. The stone was carried back to the Uttermost West in TA 3022 aboard the White Ship.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Regions">Regions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Regions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Evendim">Evendim</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Evendim">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>Arnor's original capital of <a href="#Ann.C3.BAminas">Annúminas</a> was located on the southern shore of Lake Evendim (Nenuial in <a href="/wiki/Sindarin" title="Sindarin">Sindarin</a>, meaning "Lake of Twilight"). The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hills_of_Evendim" title="Hills of Evendim">Hills of Evendim</a> (Emyn Uial in Sindarin, "Hills of Twilight") were a series of hills that stretched along the western shore of Lake Evendim. The <a href="/wiki/List_of_Middle-earth_rivers#B" title="List of Middle-earth rivers">Baranduin</a> or Brandywine River rose from the lake, and a tributary of the river <a href="/wiki/List_of_Middle-earth_rivers#L" title="List of Middle-earth rivers">Lhûn</a> arose in the hills.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Old_Forest">Old Forest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Old Forest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Old_Forest" title="Old Forest">Old Forest</a></div>
<p>The Old Forest was an ancient wild woodland in central Arnor. It was nominally part of Arnor's territory (and later Cardolan's), but <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Men_(Middle-earth)" title="Men (Middle-earth)">Men</a> rarely entered this forest.</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Shire">The Shire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: The Shire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Shire_(Middle-earth)" title="Shire (Middle-earth)">Shire (Middle-earth)</a></div>
<p>The Shire was a region of gentle low-lying hills in northern Eriador inhabited by <a href="/wiki/Hobbit" title="Hobbit">Hobbits</a>. It was a fertile and well-tilled part of Arnor, but was deserted during the waning days of Arthedain; it had served as hunting grounds for the King of Arnor. The Hobbits (who had migrated from Dunland and parts of Cardolan and Rhudaur) received permission from King Argeleb II to settle the lands. The Shire survived the wars and devastation that brought the North Kingdom to an end.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Trollshaws">Trollshaws</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Trollshaws">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>The Trollshaws were a heavily wooded region north of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Middle-earth_roads#Great_East_Road" title="List of Middle-earth roads">Great East Road</a>. In ancient days the Trollshaws had been inhabited by the men of Arnor and subsequently <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhudaur" title="Rhudaur">Rhudaur</a>, who built many castles and forts among the hills.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Minhiriath">Minhiriath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Minhiriath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>Minhiriath was a large region in southern Eriador between the Brandywine and the Greyflood rivers. The region was mostly an open landscape; the only forest shown on the map is <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eryn_Vorn" title="Eryn Vorn">Eryn Vorn</a></i> along the coast of the sea. Minhiriath belonged to Cardolan after the division of Arnor, although by the end of the Third Age it was largely unpopulated.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Highlands">Highlands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Highlands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Barrow-downs">Barrow-downs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Barrow-downs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barrow-downs" title="Barrow-downs">Barrow-downs</a>, called Tyrn Gorthad in <a href="/wiki/Sindarin" title="Sindarin">Sindarin</a>, were a hilly region east of the <a href="/wiki/Old_Forest" title="Old Forest">Old Forest</a>. The hills derived their name from barrows built there by the Dúnedain. These hills were overrun by fell spirits sent from <a href="/wiki/Angmar" title="Angmar">Angmar</a> after the Great Plague in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Age" title="Third Age">T.A.</a> 1636.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tolkien_2005_1086_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tolkien_2005_1086-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="North_Downs">North Downs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: North Downs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>The North Downs were a range of hills in the northern part of Eriador, east of the Hills of Evendim and north-west of the Weather Hills. From their northernmost point the hills ran southwest to Fornost Erain, one of the principal cities of the Dúnedain of the North and the northern terminus of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Middle-earth_roads#North-South_Road" title="List of Middle-earth roads">North-South Road</a>. In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Age" title="Third Age">T.A.</a> 1974 after the fall of Fornost, King Arvedui escaped the destruction of the city and held out upon the North Downs for a little while before being forced to flee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tolkien_2005_1041_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tolkien_2005_1041-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Downs">South Downs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: South Downs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>A range of hills in the central south of <a href="/wiki/Eriador" title="Eriador">Eriador</a>. The range was an eastern extension of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barrow-downs" title="Barrow-downs">Barrow-downs</a>, from which it was separated by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andrath" title="Andrath">Andrath</a>, the long narrow pass which bore part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Middle-earth_roads#North-South_Road" title="List of Middle-earth roads">North-South Road</a> (also known as the Greenway).</p>
<p>The South Downs lay in the territory of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardolan" title="Cardolan">Cardolan</a>.</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Tower_Hills">Tower Hills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Tower Hills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tower_Hills" title="Tower Hills">Tower Hills</a>, also known as the Emyn Beraid in <a href="/wiki/Sindarin" title="Sindarin">Sindarin</a>, were a range of hills at the west end of <a href="/wiki/Eriador" title="Eriador">Eriador</a> several leagues past <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Shire_(Lord_of_the_Rings)" title="The Shire (Lord of the Rings)">the Shire</a>. These towers were built at the end of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ages_of_Middle-earth" title="Ages of Middle-earth">Second Age</a> by <a href="/wiki/Gil-galad" title="Gil-galad">Gil-galad</a> for <a href="/wiki/Elendil" title="Elendil">Elendil</a>, the tallest of which was named <i>Elostirion</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Weather_Hills">Weather Hills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Weather Hills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>The Weather Hills were a bleak grass-covered<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> range of hills that lay several dozen miles north-eastward of <a href="/wiki/Bree_(Middle-earth)" title="Bree (Middle-earth)">Bree</a>. These hills served as a line of defence against Rhudaur and <a href="/wiki/Angmar" title="Angmar">Angmar</a> early in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Age" title="Third Age">Third Age</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tolkien_2005_1041_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tolkien_2005_1041-8">[8]</a></sup> The tallest peak in the Weather Hills was <a href="/wiki/Weathertop" title="Weathertop">Weathertop</a> (Amon Sûl), where the chief <i>palantir</i> of Arnor was housed in a watch tower at the summit (see below).</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Cities_and_Fortresses">Cities and Fortresses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Cities and Fortresses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Ann.C3.BAminas">Annúminas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Annúminas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>Annúminas (<a href="/wiki/Sindarin" title="Sindarin">Sindarin</a>, "Tower of the West") was the first capital city of the kingdom of Arnor, founded late in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_Age" title="Second Age">Second Age</a> by <a href="/wiki/Elendil" title="Elendil">Elendil</a> the Tall. The city was situated beside Lake Nenuial and housed one of the three seeing stones of the North Kingdom. The city was abandoned and fell into ruin following the division of the kingdom, and its palantír was removed to Fornost, which became the new capital. In the <a href="/wiki/Fourth_Age" title="Fourth Age">Fourth Age</a> Annúminas was rebuilt, serving as the northern capital of the Reunited Kingdom.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Middle-earth_objects#Sceptre_of_Ann.C3.BAminas" title="List of Middle-earth objects">Sceptre of Annúminas</a>, although named after the city, had a longer history.</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Fornost">Fornost</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Fornost">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>Fornost Erain (Sindarin "Northern-fortress of the Kings" from <i>for(n)</i> "north" + <i>ost</i> "fortress"; "Norbury of the Kings" in <a href="/wiki/Westron" title="Westron">Westron</a>) became the capital city of Arthedain. It was located at the south end of the North Downs, about 100 <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BAmenor" title="Númenor">Númenórean</a> miles north of <a href="/wiki/Bree_(Middle-earth)" title="Bree (Middle-earth)">Bree</a>. After Fornost was abandoned, the site became known as <b>Deadmen's Dike</b>, visited only by Rangers. At the time of <i>The Lord of the Rings</i>, Fornost had been abandoned for "nearly a thousand years, and even the ruins of Kings' Norbury were covered with grass".</p>
<p>It is not known when Fornost was founded or exactly when the kings of Arnor moved there from Annúminas, but the capital was transferred sometime after T.A. 861, when Arnor was divided into three kingdoms.</p>
<p>Fornost was first attacked by the forces of the Witch-king in 1409, when the border defence system collapsed with the storming of the Forts of the Weather Hills. The city was successfully defended by the young King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Araphor" title="Araphor">Araphor</a>, and disaster was averted.</p>
<p>In T.A. 1974, Arthedain and Fornost were overrun by the forces of <a href="/wiki/Angmar" title="Angmar">Angmar</a>. King <a href="/wiki/Arvedui" title="Arvedui">Arvedui</a> fled into the northern wastes and was lost in the Ice Bay of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forochel" title="Forochel">Forochel</a> in March 1975. The following summer, a fleet from <a href="/wiki/Gondor" title="Gondor">Gondor</a> led by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/E%C3%A4rnur" title="Eärnur">Eärnur</a> landed at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mithlond" title="Mithlond">Mithlond</a>, fought the <a href="/wiki/Witch-king_of_Angmar" title="Witch-king of Angmar">Witch-king of Angmar</a> in the plains west of Fornost, and defeated his armies, although the Witch-king himself escaped.</p>
<p>Fornost fell into ruin following the end of Arthedain and came to be known as Deadmen's Dike. <a href="/wiki/Gandalf" title="Gandalf">Gandalf</a> indicated to <a href="/wiki/Barliman_Butterbur" title="Barliman Butterbur">Barliman Butterbur</a> that Fornost would probably be rebuilt by King <a href="/wiki/Aragorn" title="Aragorn">Elessar</a>.</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Tower_of_Amon_S.C3.BBl">Tower of Amon Sûl</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: Tower of Amon Sûl">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>The watchtower at Amon Sûl (Weathertop) was built sometime in the Second Age. Elendil and the Army of Arnor waited there for the forces of Gil-galad before marching to <a href="/wiki/Mordor" title="Mordor">Mordor</a> in the War of the Last Alliance. After the division of Arnor, Amon Sûl stood on the borders of all three kingdoms and was contested by each, falling under the control of Arthedain. The tower held the chief <i>palantír</i> of the North Kingdom, and with the rise of Angmar the neighbouring Weather Hills were fortified. In T.A. 1409 a great army came out of Angmar and the Tower was destroyed. The <i>palantír</i> was removed to Fornost.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tolkien_2005_1086_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tolkien_2005_1086-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Lond_Daer">Lond Daer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: Lond Daer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>Lond Daer Enedh (Sindarin, "Great Middle Haven") was a great harbour founded at sometime during the Second Age by <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BAmenor" title="Númenor">Númenórean</a> explorers at the mouth of the Greyflood. The harbour was the first permanent settlement of Númenórean men in <a href="/wiki/Middle-earth" title="Middle-earth">Middle-earth</a>, and it served as shipment center for timber harvested from Minhiriath. By the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Downfall_of_N%C3%BAmenor" title="Downfall of Númenor">downfall of Númenor</a> the port was already in ruins; it was abandoned in favour of Tharbad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Elostirion">Elostirion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: Elostirion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>Elostirion was the tallest and the westernmost of the three towers on the Emyn Beraid. The tower was built by <a href="/wiki/Gil-galad" title="Gil-galad">Gil-galad</a> for <a href="/wiki/Elendil" title="Elendil">Elendil</a> at the end of the Second Age, and it contained one of the three <i>palantíri</i> of Arnor.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Literary_Significance">Literary Significance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=35" title="Edit section: Literary Significance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some stylistic analyses consider the ultimate importance of Arnor is to stand as a literary device contrasting the southern kingdom of Gondor. Arguing that Arnor and Gondor are representative of the classical dichotomy of light and dark, blessed and forsaken, good and evil - these analyses reference the abandonment by the heirs of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valandil_of_Arnor" title="Valandil of Arnor">Valandil</a> and the absence of a <a href="/wiki/White_Tree_of_Gondor" title="White Tree of Gondor">White Tree</a> as points of stylistic comparison to Gondor, with its fortresses and heavily populated capital city, its possession of a White Tree, and its well-equipped military and armaments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The division of the Númenórean realms in exile also mirrors somewhat the <a href="/wiki/Western_Roman_Empire" title="Western Roman Empire">Western</a> and <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Eastern</a> subdivisions of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> One empire, Byzantium, long outlasted the other and enjoyed periods of great glory, as did <a href="/wiki/Gondor" title="Gondor">Gondor</a> in <a href="/wiki/Middle-earth" title="Middle-earth">Middle-earth</a>, and just as Arnor split into smaller kingdoms, the Western Empire soon disintegrated, and whole provinces were lost to invaders such as the Franks and the Visigoths. In both Byzantium and the Western Empire, however, not one but a succession of families ruled, and whereas the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kin-strife" title="Kin-strife">Kin-strife</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gondor" title="Gondor">Gondor</a> was an isolated episode, in both the Eastern and Western Roman Empires, internecine fighting was a common occurrence.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Adaptations">Adaptations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=36" title="Edit section: Adaptations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Peter Jackson's <a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings_(film_series)" title="The Lord of the Rings (film series)">Lord of the Rings trilogy</a> Arnor is never mentioned explicitly, though <a href="/wiki/Galadriel" title="Galadriel">Galadriel</a> speaks of the fall of <a href="/wiki/Angmar" title="Angmar">Angmar</a> in <a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit:_An_Unexpected_Journey" title="The Hobbit: An Unexpected Journey">The Hobbit: An Unexpected Journey</a>.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Battle_for_Middle-earth_II" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Battle for Middle-earth II">The Lord of the Rings: The Battle for Middle-earth II</a> expansion pack <a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Battle_for_Middle-earth_II:_The_Rise_of_the_Witch-king" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Battle for Middle-earth II: The Rise of the Witch-king">The Lord of the Rings: The Battle for Middle-earth II: The Rise of the Witch-King</a>, Arnor appears as a non-playable faction in the story-driven campaign. Many of the most important events in the history of Arnor are depicted in-game, such as the death of King Argeleb I, the destruction of Amon Sûl, and the Fall of Fornost. Several of Arnor's Kings also make an in-game appearance during the campaign, such as Argeleb I, Arveleg I, and Arvedui. Although not playable in multiplayer by default, several mods exist that allow players to use the forces of Arnor in the games skirmish mode.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings_Online" title="The Lord of the Rings Online">Lord of the Rings Online</a>, the majority of the original regions (<a href="/wiki/Bree_(Middle-earth)" title="Bree (Middle-earth)">Bree-Land</a>, the Trollshaws, the Lonelands, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ettenmoors" title="Ettenmoors">Ettenmoors</a>, the North Downs, and <a href="/wiki/Shire_(Middle-earth)" title="Shire (Middle-earth)">the Shire</a>) all occupy what was once part of the kingdom of Arnor. A seventh region, Evendim, was also added to the game after its release which depicts the area surrounding Arnor's abandoned capital of Annúminas. In all seven of these regions, players may explore ruins of what were once fortresses and cities of Arnor including the ruins of Fornost, Annúminas, and Amon Sûl, as well as interact with many <a href="/wiki/Non-player_character" title="Non-player character">Non-Player Characters</a> that make reference to the fallen kingdom.</p>
<p>In its 2007 supplement The Ruin of Arnor to <a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings_Strategy_Battle_Game" title="The Lord of the Rings Strategy Battle Game">The Lord of the Rings Strategy Battle Game</a>, <a href="/wiki/Games_Workshop" title="Games Workshop">Games Workshop</a> also released a line of miniatures that depict the forces of Arnor at different points in time, including the armies of the kingdom before its destruction, as well as the Dúnedain rangers that composed the remnants of Arnor's people.</p>
