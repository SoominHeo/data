<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Arnold Hauser (art historian)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Arnold Hauser</b> (8 May 1892 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Timi%C5%9Foara" title="Timişoara">Timişoara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Temes_County" title="Temes County">Temes County</a> – 28 January 1978 in <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>) was a Hungarian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Art_historian" title="Art historian">art historian</a> who was perhaps the leading <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marxist" title="Marxist">Marxist</a> in the field. He wrote on the influence of change in <a href="/wiki/Social_structure" title="Social structure">social structures</a> on art. His <i>The Social History of Art</i> (1951) argued that art—which, after a paleolithic period of naturalism, began as "flat, symbolic, formalized, abstract and concerned with spiritual beings"—became more <a href="/wiki/Realism_(arts)" title="Realism (arts)">realistic</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naturalism_(arts)" title="Naturalism (arts)">naturalistic</a> as societies became less hierarchical and authoritarian, and more <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mercantile" title="Mercantile">mercantile</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bourgeois" title="Bourgeois">bourgeois</a> (Harrington).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Writings">Writings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnold_Hauser_(art_historian)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Writings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>1951: <i>Sozialgeschichte der Kunst und Literatur</i> (The Social History of Art and Literature)</li>
<li>1958: <i>Philosophie der Kunstgeschichte</i> (The Philosophy of Art History)</li>
<li>1964: <i>Der Manierismus. Die Krise der Renaissance und der Ursprung der modernen Kunst</i> (Mannerism: The Crisis of the Renaissance and the Origin of Modern Art)</li>
<li>1974: <i>Soziologie der Kunst</i> (The Sociology of Art)</li>
<li>1978: <i>Im Gespräch mit Georg Lukács</i> kleiner Sammelband mit drei Interviews und dem Essay „Variationen über das tertium datur bei Georg Lukács“</li>
</ul>
