<p>The <b>Arda</b> (<a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a>: <span lang="bg" xml:lang="bg">Арда, <i>Arda</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <span lang="tr" xml:lang="tr"><i>Arda</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el" xml:lang="el">Άρδας</span>, <i>Árdas</i>) is a 290-kilometre (180 mi) long river in <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>. It is a tributary of the <a href="/wiki/Maritsa" title="Maritsa">Maritsa</a> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el" xml:lang="el">Έβρος</span>, <i>Evros</i>). Its source lies in the Bulgarian <a href="/wiki/Rhodope_Mountains" title="Rhodope Mountains">Rhodope Mountains</a> near the village <a href="/wiki/Arda,_Bulgaria" title="Arda, Bulgaria">Arda</a>, part of the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Smolyan" title="Smolyan">Smolyan</a>. It flows eastward past <a href="/wiki/Rudozem" title="Rudozem">Rudozem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kardzhali" title="Kardzhali">Kardzhali</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ivaylovgrad" title="Ivaylovgrad">Ivaylovgrad</a> and enters Greece in the northern part of the <a href="/wiki/Evros_(regional_unit)" title="Evros (regional unit)">Evros regional unit</a>. It flows into the Maritsa on the border of Greece and <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, between the Greek village <a href="/wiki/Kastanies" title="Kastanies">Kastanies</a> and the Turkish city <a href="/wiki/Edirne" title="Edirne">Edirne</a>. In the Bulgarian section there are three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydroelectric" title="Hydroelectric">hydroelectric</a> and <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">irrigation</a> <a href="/wiki/Dam" title="Dam">dams</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kardzhali_Dam" title="Kardzhali Dam">Kardzhali Dam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Studen_Kladenets" title="Studen Kladenets">Studen Kladenets</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ivaylovgrad_Dam" title="Ivaylovgrad Dam">Ivaylovgrad Dam</a>. The Bulgarian section is 241 kilometres (150 mi) long, making the Arda the longest river in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhodopes" title="Rhodopes">Rhodopes</a>. The medieval <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dyavolski_most_(bridge)" title="Dyavolski most (bridge)">Dyavolski most</a> arch bridge crosses the river 10 kilometres (6 mi) from <a href="/wiki/Ardino" title="Ardino">Ardino</a>.</p>
<p>The three floods of February 18, 2005, when the water level was at 4.8 metres (16 ft), March 1 and March 7, 2005, flooded the low-lying areas, especially in the Kastanies area which turned the area into a lagoon. The merging of the waters of the Maritsa (Evros/Meriç) caused streets and buildings including homes to be flooded and people to be stranded in their homes.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Arda_Peak" title="Arda Peak">Arda Peak</a> on <a href="/wiki/Livingston_Island" title="Livingston Island">Livingston Island</a> in the <a href="/wiki/South_Shetland_Islands" title="South Shetland Islands">South Shetland Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a> and the mineral <a href="/wiki/Ardaite" title="Ardaite">ardaite</a> are named after the Arda river.</p>
