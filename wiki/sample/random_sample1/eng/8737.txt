<p><b>Ardabil Province</b> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="fa" xml:lang="fa">استان اردبیل</span>‎‎ <i>Ostān-e Ardabīl</i>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Azerbaijani_language" title="South Azerbaijani language">South Azerbaijani</a>: <span lang="azb" xml:lang="azb"><i>اردبیل اوستانی</i></span> <i>Ərdəbil ostanı</i>) is one of the thirty-one <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Iran" title="Provinces of Iran">provinces of Iran</a>. It is in the <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan_(Iran)" title="Azerbaijan (Iran)">northwest</a> of the country, in <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_Iran" title="Regions of Iran">Regions</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Region_3,_Iran" title="Region 3, Iran">3</a>, bordering the <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Republic of Azerbaijan</a>, the provinces of <a href="/wiki/East_Azerbaijan_Province" title="East Azerbaijan Province">East Azerbaijan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zanjan_Province" title="Zanjan Province">Zanjan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gilan_Province" title="Gilan Province">Gilan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Its administrative centre is the city of <a href="/wiki/Ardabil" title="Ardabil">Ardabil</a>. The province was established in 1993 from the eastern part of East Azerbaijan.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate_and_geography">Climate and geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ardabil_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Climate and geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Alvars1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Alvars1.jpg/220px-Alvars1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Alvars1.jpg/330px-Alvars1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Alvars1.jpg/440px-Alvars1.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Alvars1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Alvars, Ardabil</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Many tourists come to the region for its cool climate (max 35 °C) during the hot summer months. The winters are bitterly cold, with temperatures plummeting to −25 °C.</p>
<p>Its famous natural region is the <a href="/wiki/Sabalan" title="Sabalan">Sabalan</a> mountains. The province is considered the coldest province in Iran by many. Large parts of the province are green and forested.</p>
<p>Famous athletes such as <a href="/wiki/Ali_Daei" title="Ali Daei">Ali Daei</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hossein_Rezazade" title="Hossein Rezazade">Hossein Rezazade</a> are originally from Ardabil.</p>
<p>Ardabil's capital stands about 70 km from the <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian Sea</a> and has an area of 18011 km². Neighbouring the <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian Sea</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Azerbaijan" title="Republic of Azerbaijan">Republic of Azerbaijan</a>, the city is of great political and economic significance. The province has been blessed with splendid natural beauty and numerous sights.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ardabil_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The natural features of the province of Ardabil are mentioned in the <a href="/wiki/Avesta" title="Avesta">Avesta</a>, according to which <a href="/wiki/Zoroaster" title="Zoroaster">Zoroaster</a> was born by the river <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aras_River" title="Aras River">Aras</a> and wrote his book in the <a href="/wiki/Sabalan" title="Sabalan">Sabalan</a> Mountains. During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamic_conquest_of_Iran" title="Islamic conquest of Iran">Islamic conquest of Iran</a>, Ardabil was the largest city in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Azarbaijan" title="Azarbaijan">Azarbaijan</a>, and remained so until the <a href="/wiki/Mongols" title="Mongols">Mongol</a> invasion period.</p>
<p>Shah <a href="/wiki/Ismail_I" title="Ismail I">Ismail I</a> started his campaign to nationalize Iran's government and land from here, but consequently announced <a href="/wiki/Tabriz" title="Tabriz">Tabriz</a> as his capital in 1500 <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">CE</a>. Yet Ardabil remained an important city both politically and economically until modern times.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ardabil_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Administrative divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The province is divided into 10 <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Iran" title="Counties of Iran">counties</a>: <a href="/wiki/Ardabil_County" title="Ardabil County">Ardabil</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bilasavar_County" title="Bilasavar County">Bilasavar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germi_County" title="Germi County">Germi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Khalkhal_County" title="Khalkhal County">Khalkhal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kowsar_County" title="Kowsar County">Kowsar</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meshginshahr_County" title="Meshginshahr County">Meshginshahr</a>, <a href="/wiki/Namin_County" title="Namin County">Namin</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sarein" title="Sarein">Sarein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nir_County" title="Nir County">Nir</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Parsabad_County" title="Parsabad County">Parsabad</a>. District of <a href="/wiki/Arasbaran" title="Arasbaran">Arasbaran</a> was transferred to the province of <a href="/wiki/Ardabil" title="Ardabil">Ardabil</a> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/East_Azerbaijan" title="East Azerbaijan">East Azerbaijan</a> in 2010 and now appears on the more recent maps of Ardabil Province produced by the Iranian official organs.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ardabil_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ardabil-Meusume.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="250" data-file-width="200" height="250" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/2a/Ardabil-Meusume.jpg" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">Ardabil Museum</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Ardabil is the seat of the sanctuary and tomb of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shaikh" title="Shaikh">Shaikh</a> <a href="/wiki/Safi-ad-din_Ardabili" title="Safi-ad-din Ardabili">Safî ad-Dîn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eponym" title="Eponym">eponym</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Safavid_Dynasty" title="Safavid Dynasty">Safavid Dynasty</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kulliye" title="Kulliye">Kulliye</a>. It has many hot springs and beautiful natural landscapes and these attract tourists. The mineral springs of Ardabil are Beele-Darreh, <a href="/wiki/Sareyn" title="Sareyn">Sareyn</a>, Sardabeh and Booshloo, which are famed throughout Iran for their medicinal qualities. It also has many beauty lakes: the largest of which are Ne'or, Shoorabil, ShoorGel, NouShahr and Alooche, which are the habitats of some species of water birds.</p>
<p>The beautiful Lake Ne'or is located in a mountainous area 48 km south-east of the city of Ardabil. It covers an area of 2.1 km² and has an average depth of 3 metres. It is fed by springs in the lake bed. Lake Shoorabil is located in a hilly area south of the city of Ardabil and covers an area of 640,000 m². The surface of the lake is covered with a thin white layer of minerals, which is useful for healing skin diseases and rheumatism. Near the lake there is the leisure complex of Shoorabil. <i>Ardabil is a city of great antiquity. Its origins go back 4000 to 6000 years (according to historical research in this city).</i> This city was the capital of Azerbaijan province in different times, but its golden age was in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Safavid" title="Safavid">Safavid</a> period.</p>
<p>One of the most ancient cities in Iran is Meshkin Shahr. It is located in the north-west of Iran in Azerbaijan, 839 kilometers from Tehran. It is the closest city to the Sabalan mountains. In the past, it was called "Khiav", "Orami", and "Varavi".</p>
<p>The most important places to visit in the district of Meshkin Shahr are the following : – The hot water springs of Moiel, Eelando and Qaynarja, located in the suburb of the city. – Qara Soo River Sides. – The spring of Qotur Suie, located 42 kilometers from Meshkin shahr. – The old Castle of Meshkin Shahr. – Qahqaheh castle, located 80 kilometers from Meshkin Shahr. – Deev castle, located in Kavij. – The petrograph of Shapour Sasani in Meshkin Shahr. – The old cemetery in Oonar. – The tomb of Sheykh Haydar in Meshkin Shahr. – Imamzadeh Seyyed Soleyman.</p>
<p>The other significant historical monuments are as follows: the mausoleum of Sheikh Jebra'il, located 2 km north of Ardabil, the old but always lively bazaar, the babadavood anbaran, the Friday mosque, and a few ancient bridges. In addition to these, in many villages of Ardabil, including <a href="/wiki/Sadeqlu,_Ardabil" title="Sadeqlu, Ardabil">Sadeqlu</a>, relics of ancient monuments, including tombs, have been found.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Language">Language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ardabil_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Language">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The primary language of Ardebil province is <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijani_language" title="Azerbaijani language">Azerbaijani</a>, a branch of <a href="/wiki/Turkic_languages" title="Turkic languages">Turkic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Iran_A_Country_Study_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Iran_A_Country_Study-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Encyclopedia_of_the_Stateless_Nations_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Encyclopedia_of_the_Stateless_Nations-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Other languages in Ardabil include <a href="/wiki/Tati_language_(Iran)" title="Tati language (Iran)">Tati</a> and <a href="/wiki/Talysh_language" title="Talysh language">Talysh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
