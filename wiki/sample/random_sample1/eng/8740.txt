<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Scientific_racism" title="Scientific racism">racial anthropology</a> of the early 20th century, the <b>Armenoid type</b> is a subtype of the <a href="/wiki/Caucasian_race" title="Caucasian race">Caucasian race</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ripley_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ripley-1">[1]</a></sup> According to anthropologist <a href="/wiki/Carleton_S._Coon" title="Carleton S. Coon">Carleton Coon</a>, the countries of the northern part of <a href="/wiki/Western_Asia" title="Western Asia">Western Asia</a>, namely <a href="/wiki/Anatolia" title="Anatolia">Anatolia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Levant" title="Levant">Levant</a>, were considered the center of distribution of the Armenoid race.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin.2C_distribution_and_physiognomy">Origin, distribution and physiognomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armenoid_race&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin, distribution and physiognomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Armenoid_Lebanese.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="617" data-file-width="1001" height="123" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Armenoid_Lebanese.jpg/200px-Armenoid_Lebanese.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Armenoid_Lebanese.jpg/300px-Armenoid_Lebanese.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Armenoid_Lebanese.jpg/400px-Armenoid_Lebanese.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Armenoid_Lebanese.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A <a href="/wiki/Lebanese_people" title="Lebanese people">Lebanese</a> man of Armenoid type, from Ripley (1911)</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Carleton_S._Coon" title="Carleton S. Coon">Carleton S. Coon</a> wrote that the Armenoid racial type is very similar to the <a href="/wiki/Dinaric_race" title="Dinaric race">Dinaric race</a>. The only difference is that Armenoids have a slightly darker <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pigmentation" title="Pigmentation">pigmentation</a>. He described the Armenoid as a sub-race of the <a href="/wiki/Caucasian_race" title="Caucasian race">Caucasoid race</a>. Armenoids were said to be found throughout <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>. However, the largest concentrations occurred within <a href="/wiki/Anatolia" title="Anatolia">Anatolia</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Caucasus" title="South Caucasus">Transcaucasia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a>. Known as the "true" <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasians</a>, Armenoids were relatively tall, usually with <a href="/wiki/Brown_hair" title="Brown hair">medium to dark brown</a> or <a href="/wiki/Black_hair" title="Black hair">black hair</a>, light to medium skin colour, large round eyes that were usually brown; a round, brachycephalic head shape with a straight backing (planocciput) (see <a href="/wiki/Cephalic_index" title="Cephalic index">Cephalic index</a>), high cheekbones and non-prominent chins. Lips were full, and noses were often <a href="/wiki/Aquiline_nose" title="Aquiline nose">aquiline</a>. Large minority of Armenoids have <a href="/wiki/Blond" title="Blond">blond</a> hair and <a href="/wiki/Eye_color#Blue" title="Eye color">blue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eye_color#Green" title="Eye color">green</a>, or hazel eyes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">It has long been believed by physical anthropologists that the quintessence of Near Eastern brachycephaly is to be found in the Armenians; the racial term Armenoid being named for them. The Armenians have long been established in the territory which is now only partly theirs; they had, before the arrival of the Turks, a powerful kingdom, which covered most of the territory between the Gulf of Alexandretta and the Caucasus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>
</tr>
