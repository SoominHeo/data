<p><a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a> is subdivided into eleven <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Country_subdivision" title="Country subdivision">administrative divisions</a>. Of these, ten are provinces, known as <i>marzer</i> (մարզեր) or in the singular form <i><a href="/wiki/Marz_(country_subdivision)" title="Marz (country subdivision)">marz</a></i> (մարզ) in <a href="/wiki/Armenian_language" title="Armenian language">Armenian</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Yerevan" title="Yerevan">Yerevan</a> is treated separately and granted special administrative status as the country's capital. The chief executive in each of 10 marzer is the <i>marzpet</i>, appointed by the government of Armenia. In Yerevan, the chief executive is the mayor, appointed by the president.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Communities_.28hamaynkner.29">Communities (<i>hamaynkner</i>)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Administrative_divisions_of_Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Communities (hamaynkner)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_municipalities_of_Armenia" title="List of municipalities of Armenia">List of municipalities of Armenia</a></div>
<p>Within each province are communities (<i>hamaynkner</i>, singular <i>hamaynk</i>). Each community is self-governing and consists of one or more settlements (<i>bnakavayrer</i>, singular <i>bnakavayr</i>). Settlements are classified as either towns (<i>kaghakner</i>, singular <i>kaghak</i>) or villages (<i>gyugher</i>, singular <i>gyugh</i>). As of 2007, Armenia includes 915 communities, of which 49 are considered urban and 866 are considered rural. The capital, Yerevan, also has the status of a community.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Additionally, Yerevan is divided into twelve semi-autonomous districts.</p>
