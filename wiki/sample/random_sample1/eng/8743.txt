<p><b>Harmonia Mundi</b> is an independent record label founded in Paris, France, in 1958 by <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Coutaz" title="Bernard Coutaz">Bernard Coutaz</a>. In 1986 Harmonia Mundi (France) moved to <a href="/wiki/Arles" title="Arles">Arles</a>, France.</p>
<p>The label's catalogue is devoted to <a href="/wiki/Classical_music" title="Classical music">classical music</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, and <a href="/wiki/World_music" title="World music">world music</a> (on the World Village label). Harmonia Mundi (US) is a branch of Harmonia Mundi (France). There are also operations in the United Kingdom and in Spain.</p>
<p>The label was acquired by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PIAS_Entertainment_Group" title="PIAS Entertainment Group">PIAS Entertainment Group</a> in September 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Latin phrase <i>harmonia mundi</i> means "world harmony".</p>
<p>The classical label <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Harmonia_Mundi" title="Deutsche Harmonia Mundi">Deutsche Harmonia Mundi</a> is not related to Harmonia Mundi (France) and belongs to <a href="/wiki/Sony_BMG" title="Sony BMG">Sony BMG</a>.</p>
