<p><b>Arvo Jaakko Haavisto</b> (7 January 1900 – 22 April 1977) was a <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finnish</a> wrestler. He won a bronze medal at the 1924 Olympics and a gold medal in 1928, both in <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_wrestling" title="Freestyle wrestling">freestyle wrestling</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sports-reference_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-sports-reference-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Haavisto took up wrestling in 1918 and won four Finnish titles: in 1925–1927 in freestyle and in 1925 in <a href="/wiki/Greco-Roman_wrestling" title="Greco-Roman wrestling">Greco-Roman wrestling</a>. After retiring from competitions he worked as a wrestling coach and referee, attending the 1936 Olympics in this capacity. Since 1992 an annual Greco-Roman wrestling tournament has been held in his honor in his native <a href="/wiki/Ilmajoki" title="Ilmajoki">Ilmajoki</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sports-reference_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-sports-reference-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
