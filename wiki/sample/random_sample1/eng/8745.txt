<p>The <b>Arbutoideae</b> are a <a href="/wiki/Subfamily" title="Subfamily">subfamily</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Flowering_plant" title="Flowering plant">flowering plant</a> <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Ericaceae" title="Ericaceae">Ericaceae</a>. Phylogenetic analysis supported all genera of the subfamily as <a href="/wiki/Monophyly" title="Monophyly">monophyletic</a>, except <i><a href="/wiki/Arbutus" title="Arbutus">Arbutus</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hileman2007_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hileman2007-1">[1]</a></sup> Moreover, it was suggested that the non-sister relationship between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mediterranean_basin" title="Mediterranean basin">Mediterranean</a> and North American species may be explained by a once widespread distribution in the Northern hemisphere before the <a href="/wiki/Neogene" title="Neogene">Neogene</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hileman2007_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hileman2007-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The genera <i>Arbutus</i>, <i>Arctostaphylos</i>, <i>Comarostaphylis</i> form a particular type of <a href="/wiki/Mycorrhiza" title="Mycorrhiza">mycorrhizal</a> symbiosis with fungi resembling <a href="/wiki/Ectomycorrhiza" title="Ectomycorrhiza">ectomycorrhizas</a>; it is termed <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arbutoid_mycorrhiza" title="Arbutoid mycorrhiza">arbutoid mycorrhiza</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Osmundsen2007_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Osmundsen2007-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SmithRead2008_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-SmithRead2008-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
