<p><b>Arbignieu</b> is a former <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_France" title="Communes of France">commune</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ain" title="Ain">Ain</a> <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_France" title="Departments of France">department</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Auvergne-Rh%C3%B4ne-Alpes" title="Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes">Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes</a> region of eastern <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. On 1 January 2016, it was merged into the new commune <a href="/wiki/Arboys-en-Bugey" title="Arboys-en-Bugey">Arboys-en-Bugey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The inhabitants of the commune are known as <i>Arbignolans</i> or <i>Arbignolanes</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arbignieu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The commune is located 4 km south-west of <a href="/wiki/Belley" title="Belley">Belley</a> and 30 km north-west of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chambery" title="Chambery">Chambery</a>. It is traversed by the <i>Tour du Bugey</i> trail. It lies in the <i><a href="/wiki/Appellation_d%27origine_contr%C3%B4l%C3%A9e" title="Appellation d'origine contrôlée">Appellation d'origine contrôlée</a></i> (AOC) zone for wine from Bugey with the label "Roussette du Bugey-Arbignieu".</p>
<p>The commune can be accessed on the D69 from Belley in the north-east which continues west to <a href="/wiki/Colomieu" title="Colomieu">Colomieu</a>. The D100 road also branches from the D49 at the northern edge of the commune and goes south along the eastern side of the commune joining the D10 which passes through the commune from the D992 in the east through the hamlet of Peyzieu then south-west to <a href="/wiki/Saint-Bois" title="Saint-Bois">Saint-Bois</a>. There are three hamlets in the commune other than Arbignieu: Thoys, Slignieu, and Peyzieu. The commune is approximately half farmland and half forest with most of the forest along the western border.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Google_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Google-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <i>Furans</i> river forms the eastern border of the commune and then flows east to join the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhone" title="Rhone">Rhone</a>. The <i>Ruisseau d'Armaille</i> flows from west to east across the commune into the Furans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Google_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Google-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demography">Demography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arbignieu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Demography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2010 the commune had 480 inhabitants. The evolution of the number of inhabitants is known through the population censuses conducted in the town since 1793. From the 21st century, a census of municipalities with fewer than 10,000 inhabitants is held every five years, unlike larger towns that have a sample survey every year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[Note 1]</a></sup></p>
