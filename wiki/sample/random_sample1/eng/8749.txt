<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Arianrhod</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Arianrhod</b> (<small>Welsh pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Welsh" title="Help:IPA for Welsh">[arˈjanr̥ɔd]</a></span>) is a figure in <a href="/wiki/Welsh_mythology" title="Welsh mythology">Welsh mythology</a> who plays her most important role in the <a href="/wiki/Four_Branches_of_the_Mabinogi#Math.2C_son_of_Mathonwy" title="Four Branches of the Mabinogi">Fourth Branch</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Mabinogion" title="Mabinogion">Mabinogi</a></i>. She is the daughter of <a href="/wiki/D%C3%B4n" title="Dôn">Dôn</a> and the sister of <a href="/wiki/Gwydion" title="Gwydion">Gwydion</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gilfaethwy" title="Gilfaethwy">Gilfaethwy</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Triads" title="Welsh Triads">Welsh Triads</a> give her father as <a href="/wiki/Beli_Mawr" title="Beli Mawr">Beli Mawr</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bromwich_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bromwich-1">[1]</a></sup> In the <i>Mabinogi</i> her uncle <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Math_ap_Mathonwy" title="Math ap Mathonwy">Math ap Mathonwy</a> is the King of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Gwynedd" title="Kingdom of Gwynedd">Gwynedd</a>, and during the course of the story she gives birth to two sons, <a href="/wiki/Dylan_ail_Don" title="Dylan ail Don">Dylan ail Don</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lleu_Llaw_Gyffes" title="Lleu Llaw Gyffes">Lleu Llaw Gyffes</a>, through magical means.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mabinogion">Mabinogion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arianrhod&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Mabinogion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the Fourth Branch, Arianrhod's uncle <a href="/wiki/Math_fab_Mathonwy" title="Math fab Mathonwy">Math fab Mathonwy</a> would die if he did not keep his feet in the lap of a virgin when he was not at war. <a href="/wiki/Gilfaethwy" title="Gilfaethwy">Gilfaethwy</a> conceives a lust for Math's original footholder, <a href="/wiki/Goewin" title="Goewin">Goewin</a>, and he and his brother <a href="/wiki/Gwydion" title="Gwydion">Gwydion</a> engineer a war with King <a href="/wiki/Pryderi" title="Pryderi">Pryderi</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Dyfed" title="Kingdom of Dyfed">Dyfed</a>, forcing Math to leave his court. In Math's absence Gilfaethwy rapes Goewin. When Math returns he punishes his nephews severely by turning them into a series of mated pairs of animals, and marries Goewin to alleviate her shame. However, he must find a new virgin to hold his feet.</p>
<p>Gwydion suggests his sister, Arianrhod. To test her virginity, Math tells her to step over his magician's rod. On doing this, however, she immediately gives birth to a young boy, <a href="/wiki/Dylan_ail_Don" title="Dylan ail Don">Dylan ail Don</a>, and a blob-like entity which becomes <a href="/wiki/Lleu_Llaw_Gyffes" title="Lleu Llaw Gyffes">Lleu Llaw Gyffes</a>. Dylan is a sea spirit, who flees to the ocean immediately after he is <a href="/wiki/Baptism" title="Baptism">baptized</a>; Gwydion grabs the entity before anyone else sees it and places it in a chest. Before long it becomes a boy who grows at twice the normal rate; when he is four he is as big as an eight-year-old. Gwydion takes him to see his mother at her home, <a href="/wiki/Caer" title="Caer">Caer</a> Arianrhod.</p>
<p>However, Arianrhod is still angry about her humiliation at Math's court. She places a <i><a href="/wiki/Tynged" title="Tynged">tynged</a></i> (a <i><a href="/wiki/Geis" title="Geis">geis</a></i> or taboo) on the boy that he will never have a name unless she gives it to him. Gwydion disguises the boy as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shoemaker" title="Shoemaker">shoemaker</a> and returns to Caer Arianrhod; while Arianrhod is being fitted, she sees the boy killing a wren with a single stone and remarks that the fair-haired one ("lleu") has a skillful hand ("llaw gyffes"). Gwydion reveals the disguise, and says she has just given her son a name – Lleu Llaw Gyffes. Arianrhod then places a second <i>tynged</i> on Lleu, that he would never take arms unless she armed him. A few years later Gwydion and Lleu return to Caer Arianrhod, this time disguised as <a href="/wiki/Bard" title="Bard">bards</a>. Gwydion is an accomplished storyteller and entertains her court. That night, while everyone sleeps, he conjures a fleet of warships. Arianrhod gives her guests weapons and armor to help her fight, thereby dispelling her second curse. When Gwydion reveals the trickery, Arianrhod places a final <i>tynged</i> on Lleu: he would never have a wife from any race that is on this earth now. Gwydion and Math eventually break this curse by creating a woman out of <a href="/wiki/Oak" title="Oak">oak</a> blossom, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broom_(shrub)" title="Broom (shrub)">broom</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meadowsweet" title="Meadowsweet">meadowsweet</a>; she is named <a href="/wiki/Blodeuwedd" title="Blodeuwedd">Blodeuwedd</a> ("flower face").</p>
<p>With her curses, Arianrhod denied Lleu the three aspects of masculinity: a name, arms, and a wife.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_other_sources">In other sources</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arianrhod&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In other sources">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>One of the Welsh Triads, 35 by <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Bromwich" title="Rachel Bromwich">Rachel Bromwich</a>'s numbering, establishes a different family connection for Arianrhod. Her father is named as <a href="/wiki/Beli_Mawr" title="Beli Mawr">Beli Mawr</a>, and her brother is <a href="/wiki/Cassivellaunus" title="Cassivellaunus">Caswallawn</a> (the historical Cassivellaunus). She has two sons by Lliaws son of Nwyfre, Gwenwynwyn and Gwanar, who both accompany Caswallawn in his pursuit of <a href="/wiki/Julius_Caesar" title="Julius Caesar">Julius Caesar</a> after he has been chased from Britain. This triad is the only source connecting Arianrhod to Beli Mawr and the Caswallawn saga, but it is not incompatible with the tradition recorded in the <i>Mabinogion</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bromwich_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bromwich-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The stories of Welsh mythology changed over time, and the <i>Mabinogion</i> does not contain the only version of them. Welsh scholar <a href="/wiki/William_John_Gruffydd" title="William John Gruffydd">William John Gruffydd</a> noted that 15th- and 16th-century poets apparently knew an alternate tradition in which Arianrhod actually became Math's footholder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bromwich_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Bromwich-1">[1]</a></sup> Additionally, some scholars have suggested that in an earlier form of the Fourth Branch, Gwydion was the father of Arianrhod's sons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MacKillop_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-MacKillop-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Arianrhod's palace, Caer Arianrhod, is connected with a rock formation visible off the coast of northern <a href="/wiki/Gwynedd" title="Gwynedd">Gwynedd</a> at low tide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bromwich_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Bromwich-1">[1]</a></sup> This formation is one of several landmarks that attest to the localization of the events in the Fourth Branch in this area. The name "Caer Arianrhod" is also used in Welsh for the constellation <a href="/wiki/Corona_Borealis" title="Corona Borealis">Corona Borealis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MacKillop_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-MacKillop-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arianrhod&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Arianrhod" (from the Welsh <i>arian</i>, "silver," and <i>rhod</i>, "wheel") may be cognate with <a href="/wiki/Proto-Celtic_language" title="Proto-Celtic language">Proto-Celtic</a> *<i>Arganto-rotā</i>, meaning "silver wheel."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CAWCS_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-CAWCS-3">[3]</a></sup> Alternatively, the earliest form of the name may have been <i>Aranrot</i>, in which case the first part of the name would be related to "Aran."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bromwich_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Bromwich-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
