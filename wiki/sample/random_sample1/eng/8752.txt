<p><b>Arkady Arkadyevich Vyatchanin</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Аркадий Аркадьевич Вятча́нин</span>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serbian_Cyrillic_language" title="Serbian Cyrillic language">Serbian Cyrillic</a>: <span lang="sr" xml:lang="sr"><span class="Unicode">Аркадиј Аркадјевич Вјатчањин</span></span>; born 4 April 1984) is a <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a>-born <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbian</a> <a href="/wiki/Backstroke" title="Backstroke">backstroke</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swimmer" title="Swimmer">swimmer</a>. Vyatchanin has lived and trained in <a href="/wiki/Taganrog" title="Taganrog">Taganrog</a> since 1999 and is a student of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=South_Federal_University&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="South Federal University (page does not exist)">South Federal University</a>. Vyatchanin has been a member of the Russian National Team since 2000. In 2015 he started to compete for Serbia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Change_of_Nationality_and_National_Team">Change of Nationality and National Team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arkady_Vyatchanin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Change of Nationality and National Team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2013 he announced an intention to leave the Russian team and compete for another country saying he gave all he could to team Russia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In January 2015 Serbian swimming federation announced that Vyatchanin is in process of getting Serbian citizenship and that will represent this country in future.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
