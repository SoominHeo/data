<p><b>Artemisa</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[aɾteˈmisa]</a></span>) is a municipality and city in <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, formerly part of <a href="/wiki/La_Habana_Province" title="La Habana Province">La Habana Province</a>. According to a law approved by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cuban_National_Assembly" title="Cuban National Assembly">Cuban National Assembly</a> in August 2010, Artemisa became the capital city of the newly formed <a href="/wiki/Artemisa_Province" title="Artemisa Province">Artemisa Province</a>, which comprises 8 municipalities of the former La Habana Province and 3 from <a href="/wiki/Pinar_del_R%C3%ADo" title="Pinar del Río">Pinar del Río</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Artemisa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The origin of the name of <i>Artemisa</i> is uncertain. It has been argued to have originated from the <a href="/wiki/Greek_mythology" title="Greek mythology">Greek goddess</a> <a href="/wiki/Artemis" title="Artemis">Artemis</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diana_(goddess)" title="Diana (goddess)">Diana</a>, in the Roman version) or that it refers to the name of Ragweed in Spanish, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artemisia_(plant)" title="Artemisia (plant)">Artemisia</a> (<i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ambrosia_artemisifolia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ambrosia artemisifolia (page does not exist)">Ambrosia artemisifolia</a></i>), abundant at the time.</p>
<p>Artemisa was an important source of fighters supporting <a href="/wiki/Fidel_Castro" title="Fidel Castro">Fidel Castro</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Cuban_Revolution" title="Cuban Revolution">Revolution</a> during the attack on "<a href="/wiki/Moncada_Barracks" title="Moncada Barracks">Cuartel Moncada</a>" in Santiago de Cuba (1953) and Sierra Maestra Guerrilla (1956–1959). The Martyrs Mausoleum (<i>Mausoleo de los Mártires</i>) in Artemisa is a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=National_Monument_of_Cuba&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="National Monument of Cuba (page does not exist)">National Monument of Cuba</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnpc_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-cnpc-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Artemisa belonged to <a href="/wiki/Pinar_del_R%C3%ADo_Province" title="Pinar del Río Province">Pinar del Río Province</a> until 1970.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Artemisa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The municipality is divided into the <a href="/wiki/Barrio" title="Barrio">barrios</a> of Las Cañas, Lavandero (El Pilar), Cayajabos, Lincoln (Andorra), Pijirigua, Puerta de la Güira, Las Mangas, Neptuno and El Corojal.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Artemisa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2004, the municipality of Artemisa had a population of 81,209.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-atenas_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-atenas-2">[2]</a></sup> With a total area of 690 km<sup>2</sup> (270 sq mi),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-statoids_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-statoids-1">[1]</a></sup> it has a population density of 117.7/km<sup>2</sup> (305/sq mi).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Artemisa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Artemisa is widely known among Artemiseños by the name <i>Jardín de Cuba</i> (Garden of Cuba). Its ferric red soil is also an outstanding feature, and provides great fertility to the area, which produces banana, tobacco, sugar cane, and minor fruits. Due to the color of the soil, Artemisa is known as <i>Villa Roja</i> (Red Village).</p>

<p>Places of historical importance include the Cafetal Angerona (named after <a href="/wiki/Angerona" title="Angerona">Angerona</a>, the goddess of silence and fertility, and protector of Rome), which is currently in ruins. Its remains evoke a period of great abundance, business development, love affairs, and slavery. The cafetal belonged to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_people" title="German people">German</a> entrepreneur Cornelio Souchay who fell in love with the black <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Haitian_people" title="Haitian people">Haitian</a>, Úrsula Lambert. The couple lived their romance in secret due to the taboos of the period.</p>
<p>The Hotel Campoamor, built by Asturiano Fernando González-Campoamor, has played an important role since it was finished in 1911. Many important figures visited the place, including <a href="/wiki/Rita_Longa" title="Rita Longa">Rita Longa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Hemingway" title="Ernest Hemingway">Ernest Hemingway</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Marinello&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Juan Marinello (page does not exist)">Juan Marinello</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gabriela_Mistral" title="Gabriela Mistral">Gabriela Mistral</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ignacio_Villa" title="Ignacio Villa">Ignacio Villa</a> "Bola de Nieve." Centrales azucareros (Sugar mills) Pilar and Lavandero are landmarks of the city, as well as the mansion of sugar magnate, <a href="/wiki/Julio_Lobo" title="Julio Lobo">Julio Lobo</a>.</p>
<p>Artemisa's <a href="/wiki/Patron_saint" title="Patron saint">patron saint</a> is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Mark_the_Evangelist" title="Saint Mark the Evangelist">Saint Mark the Evangelist</a>. The church of Artemisa is located in The Park (<i>el Parque</i>). The building was renovated due to efforts made by Father Antonio Rodriguez Dias, the hard work and cooperation of many Artemiseños, and generous monetary donations from German brethren.</p>
<p>Other sites include the <a href="/wiki/Artemisa_Municipal_Museum" title="Artemisa Municipal Museum">Artemisa Municipal Museum</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personalities">Personalities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Artemisa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personalities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Important personalities in the history of Cuba were born or lived in Artemisa. Among them Magdalena Peñaredonda, a poet and journalist, appointed as captain of rebel forces during the war of independence, Father Guillermo González Arocha, born in Regla, but whose significant contribution to the independence of Cuba was carried out while a priest in Artemisa, is considered an adopted son of the city. This priest also founded a school and had the cemetery of the city built.</p>
