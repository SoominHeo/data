<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CS_Sfaxien&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The club was founded in 1928 as <i>Club Tunisien</i>, playing in green and red stripes. The team was promoted to the Tunisian First Division in 1947. In 1950, the first supporters group was founded by Béchir Fendri, and in 1962 the club was renamed " Club Sportif Sfaxien " and team colours were changed to the current black and white stripes.</p>
<p>CSS celebrated their half-centenary in 1978 by winning the Tunisian League title, largely thanks to the impressive performances of their Tunisian internationals, notably <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hamadi_Agrebi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hamadi Agrebi (page does not exist)">Hamadi Agrebi</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mohamed_Ali_Akid&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mohamed Ali Akid (page does not exist)">Mohamed Ali Akid</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mokhtar_Dhouib" title="Mokhtar Dhouib">Mokhtar Dhouib</a>.</p>
<p>In November 1998, CSS captured the <a href="/wiki/CAF_Cup" title="CAF Cup">CAF Cup</a> for the first time, beating <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a>'s <a href="/wiki/ASC_Jeanne_d%27Arc" title="ASC Jeanne d'Arc">ASC Jeanne d'Arc</a> in the final.</p>
<p>In more recent times, CSS reached the final of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CAF_Champions_League_2006" title="CAF Champions League 2006">CAF Champions League 2006</a> but were narrowly beaten in dramatic fashion, with a late second leg goal condemning them to a 2–1 aggregate defeat against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al_Ahly" title="Al Ahly">Al Ahly</a> of <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>.</p>
<p>CSS won the 2007 <a href="/wiki/CAF_Confederation_Cup" title="CAF Confederation Cup">CAF Confederation Cup</a>. A 4–2 first leg victory in Sudan against Al Merreikh preceded a 1–0 second leg win, with CSS lifting the trophy in front of their own fans at the Stade Taïeb Mhiri. In November 2008, CSS faced local rivals Etoile du Sahel (ESS) in the final of the CAF Confederation Cup. CSS became the most successful club in recent history of the tournament when a 0–0 draw in Sfax was followed by a 2–2 draw in Sousse, sending the cup back to Sfax for the second year in a row.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Sportif_Sfaxien" title="Club Sportif Sfaxien">Club Sportif Sfaxien</a> is considered by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IFFHS" title="IFFHS">IFFHS</a> as one of the five best teams in this century.</p>
