<p><b>Artvin Province</b> (<a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <span lang="tr" xml:lang="tr"><i><span style="padding-right:0.1em;">Artvin ili</span></i></span>, <a href="/wiki/Georgian_language" title="Georgian language">Georgian</a>: <span lang="ka" xml:lang="ka"><span style="padding-right:0.1em;">ართვინის პროვინცია</span></span> <i>Artvinis provintsia</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Turkey" title="Provinces of Turkey">province</a> in <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, on the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a> coast in the north-eastern corner of the country, on the border with <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a>.</p>
<p>The provincial capital is the city of <a href="/wiki/Artvin" title="Artvin">Artvin</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Artvin_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Artvin2.jpg"><img alt="Artvin2.jpg" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2048" data-file-width="1536" height="320" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Artvin2.jpg/240px-Artvin2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Artvin2.jpg/360px-Artvin2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Artvin2.jpg/480px-Artvin2.jpg 2x" width="240"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Artvin2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Artvin is an attractive area of steep valleys carved by the <a href="/wiki/%C3%87oruh_River" title="Çoruh River">Çoruh River</a> system, surrounded by high mountains of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ka%C3%A7kar" title="Kaçkar">Kaçkar</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kar%C3%A7al&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Karçal (page does not exist)">Karçal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yaln%C4%B1z%C3%A7am_Mountains" title="Yalnızçam Mountains">Yalnızçam</a> (up to 3900 m) and forest with much national parkland including the Karagöl-Sahara, which contains the <a href="/wiki/%C5%9Eav%C5%9Fat" title="Şavşat">Şavşat</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bor%C3%A7ka" title="Borçka">Borçka</a> lakes. The weather in Artvin is very wet and mild at the coast, and as a result is heavily forested. This greenery runs from the top all the way down to the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a> coast. The rain turns to snow at higher altitudes, and the peaks are very cold in winter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The forests are home to <a href="/wiki/Brown_bear" title="Brown bear">brown bears</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gray_wolf" title="Gray wolf">wolves</a>. The Çoruh is now being dammed in 11 places for hydro-electric power, including the 249 m <a href="/wiki/Deriner_Dam" title="Deriner Dam">Deriner Dam</a> and others at <a href="/wiki/Bor%C3%A7ka" title="Borçka">Borçka</a> and <a href="/wiki/Muratl%C4%B1" title="Muratlı">Muratlı</a>.</p>
<p>In addition to the vast majority<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/Turkish_people" title="Turkish people">ethnic Turks</a>, the province is home to communities of <a href="/wiki/Laz_people" title="Laz people">Laz people</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hemshin_peoples" title="Hemshin peoples">Hemshin peoples</a>. Autochthonous Muslim Georgians form the majority in parts of Artvin Province east of the Çoruh River. Immigrant groups of Georgian origins, found scattered in Turkey are known as Chveneburi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In particular, there is a prominent community of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chveneburi" title="Chveneburi">Chveneburi</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Georgian_people" title="Georgian people">Georgians</a> many of them descendants of <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> families from Georgia who migrated during the struggles between the Ottoman Turks and <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> during the 19th century. With such diverse peoples, Artvin has a rich variety of folk song and dance (see <a href="/wiki/Arifana" title="Arifana">Arifana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kochari" title="Kochari">Kochari</a> for examples of folk culture).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Local industries include bee-keeping especially in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macahel" title="Macahel">Macahel</a> region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Artvin is traversed by the northeasterly line of equal latitude and longitude.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Places_of_interest">Places of interest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Artvin_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Places of interest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The city of <a href="/wiki/Artvin" title="Artvin">Artvin</a> has an ancient castle and a number of <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> period houses, mosques, and fountains.</li>
<li>Every June, there is a "bull-wrestling" festival in the high plateau of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kafkas%C3%B6r&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kafkasör (page does not exist)">Kafkasör</a></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Parekhi" title="Parekhi">Parekhi</a> monastery, a <a href="/wiki/Georgian_Orthodox_Church" title="Georgian Orthodox Church">Georgian</a> monastery</li>
</ul>
<p>Popular places for walking and outdoor expeditions.</p>
<ul>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ka%C3%A7kar" title="Kaçkar">Kaçkar</a> Mountains are among the most-popular venues for trekking holidays in Turkey.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macahel" title="Macahel">Macahel</a> Valley on the Georgian border, is another popular location for walking holidays.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Papart&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Papart (page does not exist)">Papart</a> forest in <a href="/wiki/%C5%9Eav%C5%9Fat" title="Şavşat">Şavşat</a></li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Genciyan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Genciyan (page does not exist)">Genciyan</a> Hill in Şavşat, overlooks the border and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Binbo%C4%9Fa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Binboğa (page does not exist)">Binboğa</a> lakes.</li>
<li>The lakes of Şavşat and <a href="/wiki/Bor%C3%A7ka" title="Borçka">Borçka</a> and the crater lake of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kuyruklu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kuyruklu (page does not exist)">Kuyruklu</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/%C3%87oruh_River" title="Çoruh River">Çoruh River</a> is excellent for rafting and championships have been held here</li>
<li>There are a number of Georgian churches in the valleys of <a href="/wiki/Yusufeli" title="Yusufeli">Yusufeli</a>.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bilbilan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bilbilan (page does not exist)">Bilbilan</a> Yaylası - a typical Turkish high meadow.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Savangin" title="Savangin">Savangin</a> pre-historical <a href="/wiki/Cave" title="Cave">cave</a> with an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inscription" title="Inscription">inscription</a> written in an unknown or unsolved <a href="/wiki/Alphabet" title="Alphabet">alphabet</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Well-known_residents">Well-known residents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Artvin_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Well-known residents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The singer and politician <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Z%C3%BClf%C3%BC_Livaneli" title="Zülfü Livaneli">Zülfü Livaneli</a> was born into a family from Yusufeli.</li>
<li>Folk rock singer, guitarist and composer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kaz%C4%B1m_Koyuncu" title="Kazım Koyuncu">Kazım Koyuncu</a> was born in Artvin's Black Sea town of <a href="/wiki/Hopa" title="Hopa">Hopa</a>.</li>
<li>The bard of Artvin, poet <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Turgut_%C3%87elik&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Turgut Çelik (page does not exist)">Turgut Çelik</a></li>
<li>Folk singer <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%9E%C3%BCkriye_Tutkun&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Şükriye Tutkun (page does not exist)">Şükriye Tutkun</a></li>
<li>The father of TV personality <a href="/wiki/Beyaz%C4%B1t_%C3%96zt%C3%BCrk" title="Beyazıt Öztürk">Beyaz</a> was from a village in <a href="/wiki/Ardanu%C3%A7" title="Ardanuç">Ardanuç</a>.</li>
<li>The singer, composer and engineer <a href="/wiki/Mircan_Kaia" title="Mircan Kaia">Mircan Kaia</a> was born into a family from Borcka.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Districts">Districts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Artvin_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Districts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>In 1924, the Liva Sanjak was abolished and the Artvin Vilayet was created. Artvin Vilayet was combined with Rize to form Çoruh Vilayet with the capital at Rize. Later it was separated into Artvin Province with the districts of Ardanuç, Arhavi, Artvin, Borçka, Hopa, Murgul, Şavşat and Yusufeli.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
