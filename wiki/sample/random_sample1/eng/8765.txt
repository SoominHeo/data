<p><b>Għarb</b> (<a href="/wiki/Maltese_language" title="Maltese language">Maltese</a>: <span lang="mt" xml:lang="mt"><i>L-Għarb</i></span>) is a village located at the westernmost point of the island of <a href="/wiki/Gozo" title="Gozo">Gozo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, with a population of 1,539 people (as of March 2014).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Għarb started life as a small hamlet centuries ago. The word <i>Għarb</i> means <i>West in</i> <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a>, so it should be no surprise that the village is the most westerly place on Gozo. One can see its ancient roots in the centre of the village where some houses have fine examples of decorated stone balconies. Għarb was created as a parish in 1679, a move which gave impetus for the building of a new, <a href="/wiki/Basilica_of_the_Visitation,_G%C4%A7arb" title="Basilica of the Visitation, Għarb">baroque parish church</a>. Built between 1699 and 1729, it has an elegant façade which has been compared with <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Borromini" title="Francesco Borromini">Francesco Borromini</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sant%27Agnese_in_Agone" title="Sant'Agnese in Agone">Church of Saint Agnes</a> in <a href="/wiki/Piazza_Navona" title="Piazza Navona">Piazza Navona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>. The village square, so quintessentially <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gozitan" title="Gozitan">Gozitan</a>, has become the view on many a postcard. On the square is a folklore museum housing all sorts of memorabilia retelling the Islands' rural history. Għarb lies in some of Gozo's most scenic countryside, particularly at Dbieġi, the highest hill on the Island. Also at Dbieġi is a centre for Gozitan crafts. Within the limits of Għarb is the Chapel of San Dimitri. According to legend, the first chapel was built on the cliff side by a woman whose son was freed from captivity by St. Demetrius. Also nearby is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basilica_ta%27_Pinu" title="Basilica ta' Pinu">Basilica ta' Pinu</a>, Malta's pre-eminent shrine to the Virgin Mary. It was on this spot in 1883, that a local woman heard the voice of the Virgin. The <a href="/wiki/Basilica_of_the_Visitation,_G%C4%A7arb" title="Basilica of the Visitation, Għarb">parish church</a> is dedicated to the visitation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Mary" title="Saint Mary">Saint Mary</a> to her cousin <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elizabeth_(Biblical_person)" title="Elizabeth (Biblical person)">Saint Elizabeth</a>. The late <a href="/wiki/Karmni_Grima" title="Karmni Grima">Karmni Grima</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fren%C4%8B_tal-G%C4%A7arb" title="Frenċ tal-Għarb">Frenċ tal-Għarb</a> are two of its most renowned villagers.</p>
