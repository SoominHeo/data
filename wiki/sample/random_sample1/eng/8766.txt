<p>The <b>Argentine Football Association</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es"><i>Asociación del Fútbol Argentino</i></span>, <small>locally: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[asosjaˈsjon del ˈfutbol arxenˈtino]</a></span>) is the governing body of <a href="/wiki/Football_in_Argentina" title="Football in Argentina">football in Argentina</a>. It organises the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Argentine Primera División">Primera División</a> and lower divisions (from <a href="/wiki/Primera_B_Nacional" title="Primera B Nacional">Primera B Nacional</a> to <a href="/wiki/Torneo_Argentino_C" title="Torneo Argentino C">Torneo Argentino C</a>), the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Argentina" title="Copa Argentina">Argentine Cup</a>, <a href="/wiki/Supercopa_Argentina" title="Supercopa Argentina">Supercopa Argentina</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina national football team</a>.</p>
<p>The association is based in the city of <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>. Secondly, it also organizes the <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_de_F%C3%BAtbol_Femenino" title="Campeonato de Fútbol Femenino">amateur leagues for women</a>, children, youth, <a href="/wiki/Futsal" title="Futsal">Futsal</a>, and other local leagues, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Argentina_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Argentina women's national football team">national women's team</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentine_Football_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Argentine Association Football League (in <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>) was founded on 21 February 1893 by <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Watson_Hutton" title="Alexander Watson Hutton">Alexander Watson Hutton</a>, considered "the father" of Argentine football.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The Argentine Association is the oldest in South America and one of the oldest to be formed outside <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. In 1906 Florencio Martínez de Hoz became the first Argentine-born President of the Association.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1912 the president of <a href="/wiki/Gimnasia_y_Esgrima_de_Buenos_Aires" title="Gimnasia y Esgrima de Buenos Aires">Gimnasia y Esgrima de Buenos Aires</a>, Ricardo Aldao, broke up with the association establishing an own league, the <i>Federación Argentina de Football</i> which organized a parallel tournament. Some teams moved to the FAF were Gimnasia y Esgrima, <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Independiente" title="Club Atlético Independiente">Independiente</a>, <a href="/wiki/Estudiantes_de_La_Plata" title="Estudiantes de La Plata">Estudiantes de La Plata</a> and <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Atlanta" title="Club Atlético Atlanta">Atlanta</a>. The league lasted until 1914 when rejoining <i>Asociación Argentina de Football</i> forming a unique league for the 1915 season.</p>
<p>The second dissident league was formed in 1919 and named <i>Asociacion Amateurs de Football</i>, organizing its own championships (as FAF had done) until 1926 when it merged to official association. The dissident league included some of the most prominent teams such as <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_River_Plate" title="Club Atlético River Plate">River Plate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Racing_Club_de_Avellaneda" title="Racing Club de Avellaneda">Racing</a>, Independiente and <a href="/wiki/San_Lorenzo_de_Almagro" title="San Lorenzo de Almagro">San Lorenzo</a>, with the exception of <a href="/wiki/Boca_Juniors" title="Boca Juniors">Boca Juniors</a> that remained in the official "Asociación Argentina de Football".</p>
<p>When both leagues merged for the 1927 season, the association was again renamed to "Asociación Amateur Argentina de Football" until the professionalization of the sport in 1931 when it switched to "Liga Argentina de Football". The first round of the recently created professional championship was on 31 May 1931.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-orig_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-orig-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite football turning professional in Argentina, some clubs wanted to remain amateur so they formed a new league, the "Asociación de Football Amateur y Profesionales", which organized a parallel tournament until 1934 when the dissident association merged with LAF on 3 November 1934 to form the "Asociación del Football Argentino" which has remained since.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-orig_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-orig-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, during the presidential elections to elect a new president for the body, there were two candidates to occupate Julio Humberto Grondona's chair, <a href="/wiki/Marcelo_Tinelli" title="Marcelo Tinelli">Marcelo Tinelli</a> –who wanted a change in how things were going, like eliminating corruption between some clubs and the AFA– and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luis_Segura_(Argentina)" title="Luis Segura (Argentina)">Luis Segura</a>, who had taken charge after Grondona's death, with the intention of extending his mandate.</p>
<p>With 75 presidents of different Argentine clubs voting, the day of the elections something went wrong when the final count resulted in a draw of 38 to 38 (76 votes in total). The explanation given was that one of the electors put a double vote and that mistake was not reported. As a result, the executive committee decided to postpone the election.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>After some meetings to put an end to the conflict, both candidates agreed to have another election in June 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2016, AFA president <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luis_Segura_(Argentina)" title="Luis Segura (Argentina)">Luis Segura</a> was charged with "aggravated administrative fraud".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Segura has been replaced on an interim basis by the AFA's executive secretary, Damián Dupiellet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Names">Names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentine_Football_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The body has been renamed several times since its establishment in 1893, in most of cases translating into Spanish the original British names. The list of names is the following:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Argentine Association Football League (1893-1903)</li>
<li>Argentine Football Association (1903–12)</li>
<li>Asociación Argentina de Football (1912–27)</li>
<li>Asociación Amateur Argentina de Football (1927–31)</li>
<li>Asociación de Football Amateurs y Profesionales (1931–34)</li>
<li>Asociación del Football Argentino <sup>5</sup> (1934–present)</li>
</ul>

<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: lower-alpha;">
<ol class="references">
<li id="cite_note-14"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-14">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">The 1891 edition was organized by a precedent association, <i>The Association Argentine Football</i>, dissolved at the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></span></li>
<li id="cite_note-16"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-16">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">The Copa Argentina was re-launched in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[14]</a></sup></span></li>
<li id="cite_note-17"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-17">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Contested by the champions of the Argentine Primera División and Copa Argentina.</span></li>
<li id="cite_note-18"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-18">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Also known as "Campeonato de la República", it received the name "Copa General de División Pedro Pablo Ramírez", due to the trophy had been donated by <i>de facto</i> President of Argentina, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Pablo_Ram%C3%ADrez" title="Pedro Pablo Ramírez">Pedro P. Ramírez</a>.</span></li>
<li id="cite_note-19"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-19">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Held once to keep league teams in readiness while the <a href="/wiki/1958_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1958 FIFA World Cup">1958 FIFA World Cup</a> was being played.</span></li>
<li id="cite_note-21"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-21">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Held once to celebrate the 100th. anniversary of the Association.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[15]</a></sup></span></li>
<li id="cite_note-24"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-24">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Once Inicial and Final tournaments have finished, both winners have to play a match named <i>Superfinal</i>. The association had determined that the first edition (played in 2013) would be considered as a Primera División official title (2012-13 season), therefore <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_V%C3%A9lez_S%C3%A1rsfield" title="Club Atlético Vélez Sársfield">Vélez Sársfield</a> awarded its 10th. official championship after defeating <a href="/wiki/Newell%27s_Old_Boys" title="Newell's Old Boys">Newell's</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-firstsuper_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-firstsuper-22">[16]</a></sup> Nevertheless, from the 2014 edition it was determined that the <i>Superfinal</i> would not be considered as a Primera División title but an official cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[17]</a></sup></span></li>
