<p>The <b>Primera División</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[pɾiˈmeɾa ðiβiˈsjon]</a></span>; English: <b>First Division</b>) is a professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> league in Argentina.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> It is the country's premier football division and is at the top of the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_football_league_system" title="Argentine football league system">Argentine football league system</a>. Contested by thirty clubs, it operates on a system of <a href="/wiki/Promotion_and_relegation" title="Promotion and relegation">promotion and relegation</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Primera_B_Nacional" title="Primera B Nacional">Primera B Nacional</a> (second division), with the team placed lowest at the end of the season being relegated. The season runs from February to May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1891 Argentina was the first country outside the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> to establish a football league. In the early years, only teams from <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires_Province" title="Buenos Aires Province">Buenos Aires</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rosario,_Argentina" title="Rosario, Argentina">Rosario</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> were affiliated to the national association. Teams from other cities would join in later years.</p>
<p>The Primera División turned professional in <a href="/wiki/1931_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="1931 Argentine Primera División">1931</a> when 18 clubs broke away from the amateur leagues to form a professional one. Since then, the season has been contested annually in four different formats and calendars. The league has been under its current format since the <a href="/wiki/2015_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2015 Argentine Primera División">2015</a> season.</p>
<p>The Argentine championship was ranked in the top 10 as one of the strongest leagues in the world (for the 1st January 2015 – 31 December 2015 period) by the <a href="/wiki/International_Federation_of_Football_History_%26_Statistics" title="International Federation of Football History &amp; Statistics">International Federation of Football History &amp; Statistics</a> (IFFHS). Argentina placed 4th after <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> (Spain), <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> (Italy) and <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> (Germany).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_format">Current format</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Current format">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2016–17 Argentine Primera División">2016–17 Argentine Primera División</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Championship">Championship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Championship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are 30 teams competing in the <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2016–17 Argentine Primera División">2016–17</a> season, with all of them playing each other in a single <a href="/wiki/Round-robin_tournament" title="Round-robin tournament">round-robin tournament</a> (29 rounds plus the "<a href="/wiki/Football_rivalries_in_Argentina" title="Football rivalries in Argentina">Fecha de Clásicos</a> – derbies). <i>Clásicos</i> will be played in the round 24th (on April 16, 2017). The complete list of derby games (in brackets, the venue city) to be played are: Boca–River (Buenos Aires), Rosario Central–Newell's (Rosario), Independiente–Racing (Avellaneda), San Lorenzo–Huracán (Buenos Aires), Estudiantes–Gimnasia (La Plata), Colón–Unión (Santa Fe).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Relegation">Relegation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Relegation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Relegation is based on an <a href="/wiki/Average" title="Average">averaging</a> system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> At the end of the season, the team with the worst three-year averages is relegated, while the winner of <a href="/wiki/Primera_B_Nacional" title="Primera B Nacional">Primera B Nacional</a> championship is promoted to Primera.</p>
<p>Averaging was instituted in 1983, two years after <a href="/wiki/San_Lorenzo_de_Almagro" title="San Lorenzo de Almagro">San Lorenzo de Almagro</a> was relegated in 1981. That year, <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_River_Plate" title="Club Atlético River Plate">River Plate</a> finished 18th out of 19 teams and would have been relegated under the old system. <a href="/wiki/Racing_Club_de_Avellaneda" title="Racing Club de Avellaneda">Racing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Nueva_Chicago" title="Club Atlético Nueva Chicago">Nueva Chicago</a> were the first teams to be relegated on average.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-14">[14]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Boca_Juniors" title="Boca Juniors">Boca Juniors</a> was also struggling at that time and had a dismal <a href="/wiki/1984_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="1984 Argentine Primera División">1984</a> season. These facts have led to speculation that the averaging system was instituted to minimize the chance of big teams being relegated.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Domestic_cups">Domestic cups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Domestic cups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Primera División champion gains a place to play the <a href="/wiki/Supercopa_Argentina" title="Supercopa Argentina">Supercopa Argentina</a> v. the winner of <a href="/wiki/Copa_Argentina" title="Copa Argentina">Copa Argentina</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_cups">International cups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International cups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Qualifying_method_of_Copa_Libertadores_in_Argentina" title="Qualifying method of Copa Libertadores in Argentina">Qualifying method of Copa Libertadores in Argentina</a></div>
<p>As of 2016–17, five teams from Argentina are eligible to play the <a href="/wiki/2018_Copa_Libertadores" title="2018 Copa Libertadores">2018 Copa Libertadores</a>. The champion of Primera División automatically qualifies for the tournament. The other four teams best placed in the table at the end of the tournament (2nd to 5th) are also elegible to play the tournament. Teams placed 2° to 4° in the table qualify to play the group stag while the team placed 5° will enter to the first stage of the competition.</p>
<p>For the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Sudamericana" title="Copa Sudamericana">Copa Sudamericana</a>, six teams are elegible. Clubs placed 6° to 11° in the table at the end of the tournament, will be play the cup.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Round-robin_tournaments_.281891.E2.80.931966.29">Round-robin tournaments (1891–1966)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Round-robin tournaments (1891–1966)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Copa_Campeonato.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="527" data-file-width="392" height="229" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Copa_Campeonato.JPG/170px-Copa_Campeonato.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Copa_Campeonato.JPG/255px-Copa_Campeonato.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Copa_Campeonato.JPG/340px-Copa_Campeonato.JPG 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

The <i>Copa Campeonato</i> was the first trophy awarded by the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Football_Association" title="Argentine Football Association">AFA</a>, then abandoned <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and re-issued from 2013<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> to 2015.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1891 the <i>Association Argentine Football League</i> was established by Alex Lamont of <a href="/wiki/St._Andrew%27s_Scots_School" title="St. Andrew's Scots School">St. Andrew's Scots School</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> being the first football league outside of the British Isles.,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> to establish a football league. The first Primera División matches were played on 12 April 1891: Buenos Aires FC vs. <a href="/wiki/St._Andrew%27s_Scots_School" title="St. Andrew's Scots School">St. Andrew's</a> and Old Caledonians vs. Belgrano FC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clarin120_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-clarin120-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rsssf_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-rsssf-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>A single double <a href="/wiki/Round-robin_tournament" title="Round-robin tournament">round-robin tournament</a> was played each year, and the team with the most points was crowned as champion, except for <a href="/wiki/1936_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="1936 Argentine Primera División">1936</a>, during that year the winners of <i>Copa de Honor</i> and the <i>Campeonato</i> played a match for the championship title. The single tournament arrangement lasted until 1966.</p>
<p>During this period, the traditional "big five" clubs, namely, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Boca_Juniors" title="Club Atlético Boca Juniors">Boca Juniors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_River_Plate" title="Club Atlético River Plate">River Plate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Independiente" title="Club Atlético Independiente">Independiente</a>, <a href="/wiki/Racing_Club_de_Avellaneda" title="Racing Club de Avellaneda">Racing</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Lorenzo_de_Almagro" title="San Lorenzo de Almagro">San Lorenzo</a> dominated Argentine football. No other team besides them had won the league championship in these 36 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-22">[22]</a></sup> The most serious title challenge came from <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Banfield" title="Club Atlético Banfield">Banfield</a> in 1951, when they gained the same points with Racing Club in the league table. However, they lost 1–0 in the two-legged first place playoffs and gave the title to Racing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Metropolitano_and_Nacional_.281967.E2.80.931985.29">Metropolitano and Nacional (1967–1985)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Metropolitano and Nacional (1967–1985)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1967, the single tournament format was abandoned and replaced by two championships in each year: the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitano_championship" title="Metropolitano championship">Metropolitano</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Nacional_championship" title="Nacional championship">Nacional</a>. The Metropolitano only allowed clubs competing the old tournament to participate, while the Nacional was open to teams from regional tournaments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-22">[22]</a></sup> The format of competition was also altered, with the double round-robin tournament replaced by the two-group championship Metropolitano and single round-robin Nacional in that year.</p>
<p>This change brought about a revolution in Argentine football, as small teams, like <a href="/wiki/Estudiantes_de_La_Plata" title="Estudiantes de La Plata">Estudiantes de La Plata</a> at first, and <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_V%C3%A9lez_Sarsfield" title="Club Atlético Vélez Sarsfield">Vélez Sarsfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chacarita_Juniors" title="Chacarita Juniors">Chacarita Juniors</a> and others in later years, broke down the hegemony of the five clubs who had won all the championships up to that date.</p>
<p>Between 1967 and 1969, the Metropolitano and Nacional had gone through several format changes. In the first three years, the Metropolitano was a two-group championship, with the best two teams from each group competing the semi-finals of the knock-out stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>The six best teams of each group would advance to the Nacional, with four more teams coming from regional tournaments, to compete for the Nacional championship in a single round-robin format. The seventh and eighth team of each group, alongside four teams from regional tournaments, played the Promocional tournament, which, in 1969, was replaced by the Petit tournament contested without regional teams. The ninth to twelfth teams of each group entered the Reclasificatorio tournament to determine the relegating teams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_22-3"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1970, the format of the Metropolitano and Nacional underwent a reform. Since that year, and until 1985, the Nacional had become a group tournament with playoffs, while the Metropolitano had been competed under a single or double round-robin system, except for the 1974, 1976 and 1979 edition, which were also contested as a group tournament with playoffs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite the format change in 1970, teams still entered the Nacional championship, Petit tournament and Reclasificatorio tournament according to their rankings in the Metropolitano in that year. However, in 1971, the tournaments were separated. Teams did not enter the Nacional by finishing at the top ranks of Metropolitano. On the other hand, the Petit tournament and Reclasificatorio tournament were abandoned. The Metropolitano and Nacional became two truly individual tournaments. Although the old system was reused in 1972, the separation was instituted again in 1973 and was adopted throughout the remaining Metropolitano and Nacional era.</p>
<p>The Metropolitano was always played first, until the order of the tournaments was reversed in 1982.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="European-styled_seasons_.281985.E2.80.931991.29">European-styled seasons (1985–1991)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: European-styled seasons (1985–1991)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the advice of <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina national football team</a>'s then coach <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carlos_Salvador_Bilardo" title="Carlos Salvador Bilardo">Carlos Salvador Bilardo</a>, the structure of play was modified in 1985. Traditionally, like other countries in Southern Hemisphere, football season began and ended according to the calendar year. However, upon the reform, European style season was adopted for the first time among all the South American countries. Moreover, instead of holding two championships every year, only one double <a href="/wiki/Round-robin_tournament" title="Round-robin tournament">round-robin tournament</a> was contested, like football leagues in Europe. The team topping the table at the end of season was crowned the champion.</p>
<p>In 1985, after the Nacional was played, the Metropolitano was not held, while the new single tournament (1985/86) was played for the first time.</p>
<p>In 1988–89 season, three points were given to match winners. If a draw occurred, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Penalty_shootout_(association_football)" title="Penalty shootout (association football)">penalty shootout</a> was taken place and the winner of the shootout would get two points while the loser still had one. This format was waived in the following season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Apertura_and_Clausura_.281991.E2.80.932012.29">Apertura and Clausura (1991–2012)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Apertura and Clausura (1991–2012)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Five years later, the single championship was split into two single-round tournaments, giving birth to the <a href="/wiki/Apertura_and_Clausura_in_Argentine_football" title="Apertura and Clausura in Argentine football">Apertura and Clausura</a> arrangement. In 1991 the two champions played winner-take-all matches. This practice was very controversial, especially since one of the biggest teams <a href="/wiki/Boca_Juniors" title="Boca Juniors">Boca Juniors</a> lost the finals against <a href="/wiki/Newell%27s_Old_Boys" title="Newell's Old Boys">Newell's Old Boys</a>, costing them their first official championship since 1981 despite an unbeaten run in the <i>Clausura</i>. In 1992 the game was held as well (this time between <a href="/wiki/Newell%27s_Old_Boys" title="Newell's Old Boys">Newell's Old Boys</a> and <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_River_Plate" title="Club Atlético River Plate">River Plate</a>), but regardless of the result (which favored <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_River_Plate" title="Club Atlético River Plate">River Plate</a>) both teams were awarded the title of <i>Champion</i>. After 1992, the practice was quickly abandoned, so that two champions (on equal footing) were crowned every season and no deciding game is played.</p>
<p>Originally, two points were given to match winners except the 1989/90 season. In 1995/96, the rule was changed and three points were given for a win, one for a draw and none for a loss since then.</p>
<p>Even though the current structure provides provincial teams a road to promotion, teams from the Buenos Aires-Rosario axis still dominate. Only one team from outside this axis has ever won a title (<a href="/wiki/Estudiantes_de_La_Plata" title="Estudiantes de La Plata">Estudiantes (LP)</a>, 5 times), and a reversal of this trend is unlikely to occur in the foreseeable future.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Inicial_and_Final_.282012.E2.80.932014.29">Inicial and Final (2012–2014)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Inicial and Final (2012–2014)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For the 2012–13 season, the Torneo Apertura and Clausura became "Torneo Inicial" and "Torneo Final," being disputed with the same format as before but proclaiming only one champion each season, unlike the last format that had two champions (Apertura and Clausura, respectively).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Before those changes, a controversial project for the 2012–13 season had been proposed: it consisted in a new tournament that would contain both the Primera División and Primera B Nacional teams: the former was not going to have any relegated team in its 2011–12 season and include sixteen teams from the latter, Primera B Nacional. The tournament would also include a team from the Primera B Metropolitana and one from the Torneo Argentino A, creating a thirty-eight team league. These changes were strongly opposed by the media and the people, and finally the tournament was called off. However, the project for the new format was successfully picked up starting from the 2015 season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Superfinal_.282013.E2.80.9314.29">Superfinal (2013–14)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Superfinal (2013–14)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Copa_Campeonato" title="Copa Campeonato">Copa Campeonato</a></div>
<p>Once Inicial and Final tournaments have finished, both winners had to play a match for the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Campeonato" title="Copa Campeonato">Copa Campeonato</a> (familiarly known as <i>Superfinal</i>). The <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Football_Association" title="Argentine Football Association">AFA</a> had previously determined that the first edition (played in 2013) would be considered as a Primera División official title (2012-13 season), therefore <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_V%C3%A9lez_Sarsfield" title="Club Atlético Vélez Sarsfield">Vélez Sarsfield</a> awarded its 10th official championship after defeating <a href="/wiki/Newell%27s_Old_Boys" title="Newell's Old Boys">Newell's</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-firstsuper_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-firstsuper-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nevertheless, from the 2014 edition it was determined that the <i>Superfinal</i> would not be considered as a Primera División title but an official cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Due to this the 2015 and 2016 seasons were played as single tournaments with only one champion per season, the Copa Campeonato has not held since then.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.93present:_One_tournament_again">2014–present: One tournament again</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2014–present: One tournament again">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Starting August 2014, the "Torneo de Transición" was held, with 20 teams participating (17 from the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n_season" title="2013–14 Argentine Primera División season">2013–14 season</a> and 3 promoted from the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Primera_B_Nacional" title="2013–14 Primera B Nacional">2013–14 Primera B Nacional</a>). No teams were relegated at the end of the championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015 the format switched to a tournament with 30 teams. The first five clubs of the Zonas A &amp; B of <a href="/wiki/2014_Primera_B_Nacional" title="2014 Primera B Nacional">2014 Primera B Nacional</a> season promoted to the Primera División. Those 10 teams, with the addition of the 20 clubs currently participating in the top division, qualified to contest the next season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>That same year, the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Football_Association" title="Argentine Football Association">AFA</a> announced the format for the next five seasons of the Primera División:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>In the first half of 2016, the league will be contested between thirty teams. Four teams will be relegated to and two teams will be promoted from Primera B Nacional.</li>
<li>From August 2016 to June 2017, the league will be contested between twenty-eight teams. Four teams will be relegated to and two teams will be promoted from Primera B Nacional.</li>
<li>From August 2017 to June 2018, the league will be contested between twenty-six teams. Four teams will be relegated to and two teams will be promoted from Primera B Nacional.</li>
<li>From August 2018 to June 2019, the league will be contested between twenty-four teams. Four teams will be relegated to and two teams will be promoted from Primera B Nacional.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Champions">Champions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Champions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Argentine_football_champions" title="List of Argentine football champions">List of Argentine football champions</a></div>
<p>Since the <a href="/wiki/1891_in_Argentine_football" title="1891 in Argentine football">first championship held in 1891</a>, 27 different clubs have been won the Primera División title at least once. The most successful club is <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_River_Plate" title="Club Atlético River Plate">River Plate</a>, with 36 titles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-champions_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-champions-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sobrefutbol_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-sobrefutbol-4">[4]</a></sup> Other successful clubs are <a href="/wiki/Boca_Juniors" title="Boca Juniors">Boca Juniors</a> with 31, <a href="/wiki/Racing_Club_de_Avellaneda" title="Racing Club de Avellaneda">Racing</a> with 17, <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Independiente" title="Club Atlético Independiente">Independiente</a> with 16 and <a href="/wiki/San_Lorenzo_de_Almagro" title="San Lorenzo de Almagro">San Lorenzo</a> with 15.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Top_scorers">Top scorers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Top scorers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n_Argentina_topscorers" title="Primera División Argentina topscorers">Primera División Argentina topscorers</a></div>
<p>The all-time top scorer of Primera División Argentina is <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguayan</a> <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">forward</a> <a href="/wiki/Arsenio_Erico" title="Arsenio Erico">Arsenio Erico</a> with 295 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afaerico_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-afaerico-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afaerico_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-afaerico-7">[7]</a></sup> Most players on the all-time top scorers table had their golden age before the 1970s, with all of the top five all-time scorers having retired before 1973. The only player retired after this year in the top twenty list is <a href="/wiki/Mart%C3%ADn_Palermo" title="Martín Palermo">Martín Palermo</a>, who played for <a href="/wiki/Estudiantes_de_La_Plata" title="Estudiantes de La Plata">Estudiantes (LP)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boca_Juniors" title="Boca Juniors">Boca Juniors</a> in Primera División.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media_coverage">Media coverage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Media coverage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Argentine_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n_broadcasters" title="List of Argentine Primera División broadcasters">List of Argentine Primera División broadcasters</a></div>
<p>In Argentina, Argentine League matches were broadcast nationally by <a href="/wiki/El_Trece" title="El Trece">El Trece</a>, <a href="/wiki/Telefe" title="Telefe">Telefe</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/America_TV" title="America TV">America TV</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_P%C3%BAblica_Digital_(Argentina)" title="TV Pública Digital (Argentina)">Channel 7</a>. Games were free in <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> from 2009 to 2016, when the Government and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asociacion_del_Futbol_Argentino" title="Asociacion del Futbol Argentino">AFA</a> decided rescind the agreement. The <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Government</a> paid 9500 millions (<a href="/wiki/Argentine_peso" title="Argentine peso">Argentine peso</a>) for 7 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2017, The AFA will carry out an international tender for the rights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>The league attracts television audiences beyond <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. The matches are broadcast in over 80 countries; <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fox_Sports_(Latin_America)" title="Fox Sports (Latin America)">Fox Sports</a> (two matches per week) and <a href="/wiki/TyC_Sports" title="TyC Sports">TyC Sports</a> (eleven matches per week) broadcast live Argentine Primera División matches in <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a>. In the United States, the matches are broadcast on TyC Sports (six matches per week). In <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, the Argentine league is broadcast live on <a href="/wiki/Movistar%2B" title="Movistar+">Movistar+</a>. In France and MENA regions, the games are broadcast live on <a href="/wiki/BeIN_Sports" title="BeIN Sports">BeIN Sports</a>.</p>
