<p><b>Radiodifusión Argentina al Exterior</b> (English: Argentine Broadcasting to the Outside), more known as <b>RAE</b>, is <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>'s state-owned <a href="/wiki/International_broadcasting" title="International broadcasting">international broadcaster</a>, which uses <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shortwave" title="Shortwave">shortwave</a>, satellite and the Internet.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radiodifusi%C3%B3n_Argentina_al_Exterior&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>RAE was founded on April 11, 1949, by then Argentine President, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Per%C3%B3n" title="Juan Perón">Juan Perón</a>, as SIRA "<i>Servicio Internacional de la República Argentina</i>" (SIRA, International Service of the Republic of Argentina). SIRA broadcast 24 hours a day in seven languages.</p>
<p>After the military coup of September 1955, which removed Perón from power, SIRA was shut down by the new authorizes. The station was relaunched as "<i>Radiodifusión Argentina al Exterior'</i>"("Argentine Radio Broadcast Abroad"), on February 12, 1958. RAE is operated by LRA1 <a href="/wiki/LRA_Radio_Nacional" title="LRA Radio Nacional">Radio Nacional</a>, Argentina's state radio network.</p>
<p>The shortwave transmitter is located in <a href="/wiki/General_Pacheco" title="General Pacheco">General Pacheco</a>, near the city of Buenos Aires; the station broadcasts its programs from the studios of <a href="/wiki/LRA_Radio_Nacional" title="LRA Radio Nacional">LRA Radio Nacional</a> in Buenos Aires.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Present">Present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radiodifusi%C3%B3n_Argentina_al_Exterior&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The programs that RAE broadcasts focuses on Argentine news, culture, geography, history, and music, among other subjects. Music of all kinds, from Argentine folkloric which is usually gaucho and indigenous fare) and tango music, to Argentine pop music.</p>
<p>The station broadcasts in seven languages: <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>, <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>. RAE used to broadcast in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>, until Arabic programming was cancelled by station management sometime ago; one of RAE's opening jingle still has an Arabic introduction.</p>
<p>RAE also broadcasts programs from its principal channel, Radio Nacional, so that <a href="/wiki/Argentines" title="Argentines">Argentines</a> abroad can get in touch with current events from their homeland. Among the programs broadcast from Radio Nacional are "<i>Panorama Nacional de Noticias</i>" a news program, and professional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> matches from Argentina's professional leagues.</p>
