<p>The <b>flag of Argentina</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Triband_(flag)" title="Triband (flag)">triband</a>, composed of three equally wide horizontal bands coloured <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_blue_(color)" title="Light blue (color)">light blue</a>, and <a href="/wiki/White" title="White">white</a>. There are multiple interpretations on the reasons for those colors. The flag was created by <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Belgrano" title="Manuel Belgrano">Manuel Belgrano</a>, in line with the recent creation of the <a href="/wiki/Cockade_of_Argentina" title="Cockade of Argentina">Cockade of Argentina</a>, and was first raised at the city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rosario" title="Rosario">Rosario</a> on February 27, 1812, during the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_War_of_Independence" title="Argentine War of Independence">Argentine War of Independence</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Flag_Memorial" title="National Flag Memorial">National Flag Memorial</a> was later built on the site. The <a href="/wiki/First_Triumvirate_(Argentina)" title="First Triumvirate (Argentina)">First Triumvirate</a> did not approve the use of the flag, but the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asamblea_del_A%C3%B1o_XIII" title="Asamblea del Año XIII">Asamblea del Año XIII</a> allowed the use of the flag as a <a href="/wiki/War_flag" title="War flag">war flag</a>. It was the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="Congress of Tucumán">Congress of Tucumán</a> which finally designated it as the <a href="/wiki/National_flag" title="National flag">national flag</a>, in 1816. A yellow <a href="/wiki/Sun_of_May" title="Sun of May">Sun of May</a> was added to the center in 1818.</p>
<p>The full flag featuring the sun is called the Official Ceremonial Flag (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>Bandera Oficial de Ceremonia</i></span>). The flag without the sun is considered the Ornamental Flag (<span lang="es" xml:lang="es">Bandera de Ornato</span>). While both versions are equally considered the national flag, the ornamental version must always be hoisted below the Official Ceremony Flag. In <a href="/wiki/Vexillology" title="Vexillology">vexillological</a> terms, the Official Ceremonial Flag is the civil, state and war flag and <a href="/wiki/Ensign" title="Ensign">ensign</a>, while the Ornamental Flag is an alternative civil flag and ensign.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Argentina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Belgrano_y_la_Bandera.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1305" data-file-width="941" height="305" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/7/76/Belgrano_y_la_Bandera.JPG/220px-Belgrano_y_la_Bandera.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/7/76/Belgrano_y_la_Bandera.JPG/330px-Belgrano_y_la_Bandera.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/7/76/Belgrano_y_la_Bandera.JPG/440px-Belgrano_y_la_Bandera.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Belgrano_y_la_Bandera.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Manuel_Belgrano" title="Manuel Belgrano">Manuel Belgrano</a> holding the flag.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The flag of Argentina was created by Manuel Belgrano during the Argentine War of Independence. While in Rosario he noticed that both the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royalist_(Spanish_American_Revolution)" title="Royalist (Spanish American Revolution)">royalist</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patriot_(Spanish_American_Revolution)" title="Patriot (Spanish American Revolution)">patriotic</a> forces were using the same colors, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>'s yellow and red. After realizing this, Belgrano created the <a href="/wiki/Cockade_of_Argentina" title="Cockade of Argentina">Cockade of Argentina</a>, which was approved by the First Triumvirate on February 18, 1812. Encouraged by this success, he created a flag of the same colours nine days later. It used the colors that were used by the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_Criollo_peoples" title="Spanish Criollo peoples">Criollos</a></i> during the <a href="/wiki/May_Revolution" title="May Revolution">May Revolution</a> in 1810. However, recent research and studies would indicate that the colors were chosen from the Spanish <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Charles_III" title="Order of Charles III">Order of Charles III</a> symbolizing the allegiance to the rightful, and then captive King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ferdinand_VII" title="Ferdinand VII">Ferdinand VII</a> of Spain. Most portraits about the creation or first uses of the flag show the modern design of it, but the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Macha" title="Flag of Macha">flag of Macha</a>, a very early design kept at the House of Freedom in <a href="/wiki/Sucre" title="Sucre">Sucre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a> was instead a vertical triband with two white bands and a light blue one in the middle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The flag was first flown for soldiers to swear allegiance to it on 27 February 1812, on the <i>Batería Libertad</i> (Liberty Battery), by the <a href="/wiki/Paran%C3%A1_River" title="Paraná River">Paraná River</a>. On that day, Belgrano said the following words:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>Soldiers of the Fatherland, we have heretofore had the glory of wearing <a href="/wiki/Cockade_of_Argentina" title="Cockade of Argentina">the national cockade</a>; there (pointing to the Independence battery), on the Independence Battery, where our Government has recently had the honor of bestowing it upon, shall our weapons enlarge their glory. Let us swear to defeat our enemies, internal and external, and South America will become the temple of Independence and Freedom. In testament that you so swear it, say with me: <i>LONG LIVE THE FATHERLAND!</i> (after the oath) "Lord Captain and troops chosen for the first time for the Independence Battery: go, take possession of it and fulfill the oath you have just sworn today.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Belgrano dispatched a letter addressed to the First Triumvirate, informing them of the newly created flag. However, unlike with the cockade, the Triumvirate did not accept the use of the flag: policy at the time was to state that the government was ruling on behalf of King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ferdinand_VII" title="Ferdinand VII">Ferdinand VII</a> of Spain who was captive of <a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a>, whereas the creation of a flag was a clear <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independentist</a> act. Thus, the triumvirate sent a warning to Belgrano not to fight under the flag, but by the time the reply had arrived, Belgrano had moved to the north, following the previous orders that requested him to strengthen the patriotic position in the Upper Peru after the defeat of <a href="/wiki/Juan_Jos%C3%A9_Castelli" title="Juan José Castelli">Juan José Castelli</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Huaqui" title="Battle of Huaqui">Battle of Huaqui</a>. Meanwhile, the flag was hoisted for the first time in <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a> atop the Church of Saint Nicholas of Bari on August 23, 1812; where nowadays the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Obelisk_of_Buenos_Aires" title="Obelisk of Buenos Aires">Obelisk of Buenos Aires</a> is located. Still not knowing about the Triumvirate's refusal, Belgrano raised the flag at <a href="/wiki/San_Salvador_de_Jujuy" title="San Salvador de Jujuy">San Salvador de Jujuy</a> and had it blessed by the local church on the second anniversary of the May Revolution. Belgrano accepted the orders from the Triumvirate by time they arrived to Salta and ceased using the flag. As soldiers had already made oaths to the new flag, Belgrano said that he was saving it for the circumstance of a great victory.</p>

<p>The First Triumvirate was later replaced by the Second Triumvirate, with a more liberal ideology, who called the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asamblea_del_A%C3%B1o_XIII" title="Asamblea del Año XIII">Asamblea del Año XIII</a>. Despite being one of its original goals, it did not declare independence, and so did not approve the use of a national flag either; nevertheless, the flag made by Belgrano was authorized to be used as a <a href="/wiki/War_flag" title="War flag">war flag</a>. The first oath to the newly-approved flag was on February 13, 1813, next to the <a href="/wiki/Salado_River_(Argentina)" title="Salado River (Argentina)">Salado River</a>, which became known as the "<i>Río Juramento</i>" ("Oath River"). The first battle fought with the approved flag was the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Salta" title="Battle of Salta">Battle of Salta</a>, a decisive patriotic victory that achieved the complete defeat of royalist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/P%C3%ADo_Trist%C3%A1n" title="Pío Tristán">Pío Tristán</a>.</p>
<p>The flag would be finally declared the <a href="/wiki/National_flag" title="National flag">national flag</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="Congress of Tucumán">Congress of Tucumán</a> on July 20, 1816, shortly after the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Declaration_of_Independence" title="Argentine Declaration of Independence">declaration of independence</a>. The proposal was made by the deputy <a href="/wiki/Juan_Jos%C3%A9_Paso" title="Juan José Paso">Juan José Paso</a> and the text written by the deputy of Charcas, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mariano_Serrano" title="José Mariano Serrano">José Serrano</a>. On February 25, 1818, the Congress (now working at Buenos Aires) included the Sun of May in the <a href="/wiki/War_flag" title="War flag">war flag</a>, after the proposal of deputy Chorroarín. The sun was copied after the one that the first Argentine coin featured in 1813. It was subsequently decided to keep it as part of the regular flag afterwards, and thus the sun no longer represents war.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_de_San_Mart%C3%ADn" title="José de San Martín">José de San Martín</a> was aware of the new flag, but did not employ it during the <a href="/wiki/Crossing_of_the_Andes" title="Crossing of the Andes">crossing of the Andes</a> in 1817. Being a joint operation of both Argentine and Chilean forces, he thought that a new flag would be a better idea than using either the Argentine or the Chilean flag. This led to the creation of the Flag of the Andes, used in the crossing. This flag is currently used as a provincial flag by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mendoza_province" title="Mendoza province">Mendoza province</a>.</p>
<p>On June 8, 1938, president <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roberto_Ortiz" title="Roberto Ortiz">Roberto Ortiz</a> sanctioned national law no. 12,361 declaring June 20 "<a href="/wiki/Flag_Day" title="Flag Day">Flag Day</a>", a national holiday. The date was decided as the anniversary of Belgrano's death in 1820. In 1957 the <a href="/wiki/National_Flag_Memorial_(Argentina)" title="National Flag Memorial (Argentina)">National Flag Memorial</a> (a 10,000 m<sup>2</sup> monumental complex) was inaugurated in Rosario to commemorate the creation of the flag, and the official Flag Day ceremonies have customarily been conducted in its vicinity since then.</p>
<p>In 1978 it was specified, among other measurements, that the Official Ceremony Flag should be 1.4 meters wide and 0.9 meters high, and that the sun must be embroidered.</p>

<p>Popular belief attributes the colors to those of the <a href="/wiki/Sky" title="Sky">sky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cloud" title="Cloud">clouds</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">sun</a>; some anthems to the flag like "Aurora" or "Salute to the flag" state so as well. However, historians usually disregard this idea, and attribute them to loyalty towards the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Bourbon" title="House of Bourbon">House of Bourbon</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>After the May Revolution, the first times of the Argentine War of Independence, the Triumvirate claimed to be acting on behalf of the Spanish King Ferdinand VII, who was prisoner of Napoleon Bonaparte during the <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_War" title="Peninsular War">Peninsular War</a>. Whether such loyalty was real or a trick to conceal independentism is a topic of dispute. The creation of a new flag with those colors would have been then a way to denote autonomy, while keeping the relations with the captive king alive.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shape_and_size">Shape and size</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Argentina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Shape and size">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 1978, the flag's official proportions are 9:14, and its official size is 0.9 by 1.4 meters. It features three stripes alternating sky blue, white and sky blue. Each stripe is 30 centimeters high.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In the center stripe there is an emblem known as the Sun of May (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>Sol de Mayo</i></span>), a golden sun. Historian Diego Abad de Santillán claimed that the Sun of May was a representation of the <a href="/wiki/Andean_civilizations" title="Andean civilizations">Inca</a> sun god <a href="/wiki/Inti" title="Inti">Inti</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Flags with proportions of 1:2 and 2:3 are also in use.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sun_of_May">Sun of May</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Argentina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sun of May">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The sun is called the Sun of May because it is a replica of an engraving on <a href="/wiki/Argentine_real" title="Argentine real">the first Argentine coin</a>, approved in 1813, whose value was eight <i>escudos</i> (one Spanish dollar). It has 16 straight and 16 waved sunbeams.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1978 the sun color was specified to be golden yellow (<span lang="es" xml:lang="es">amarillo oro</span>), to have an inner diameter of 10 cm, and an outer diameter of 25 cm (the diameter of the sun equals <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap"><sup>5</sup>⁄<sub>6</sub></span> the height of the white stripe. The sun's face is <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap"><sup>2</sup>⁄<sub>5</sub></span> of its height). It features 32 rays, alternately wavy and straight, and from 1978 it must be embroidered in the "Official Flag Ceremony".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Influence_of_the_Argentine_flag">Influence of the Argentine flag</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Argentina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Influence of the Argentine flag">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The French privateer <a href="/wiki/Louis-Michel_Aury" title="Louis-Michel Aury">Louis-Michel Aury</a> used the Argentine flag as a model for the blue-white-blue flag of the first independent state in Central America, which was created 1818 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Andr%C3%A9s_and_Providencia" title="San Andrés and Providencia">Isla de Providencia</a>, an island off the east coast of <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a>. This state existed until approximately 1821, before the <a href="/wiki/History_of_Colombia" title="History of Colombia">Gran Colombia</a> took over control of these islands. Somewhat later (1823) this flag was again used as the model for the flag of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Provinces_of_Central_America" title="United Provinces of Central America">United Provinces of Central America</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> a confederation of the current Central American states of <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a>, <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a> and <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a>, which existed from 1823 to 1838. After the dissolution of the Union, the five countries became independent, but even today all of these states except Costa Rica use flags of blue-white-blue stripes (the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Costa_Rica" title="Flag of Costa Rica">Costa Rican flag</a> has a red stripe superimposed on the white one, added to incorporate all the colors of the French flag). The Argentine flag also inspired the flags of <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Uruguay" title="Flag of Uruguay">Uruguay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Paraguay" title="Flag of Paraguay">Paraguay</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pledge_to_the_Flag">Pledge to the Flag</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Argentina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Pledge to the Flag">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As <a href="/wiki/Flag_Day" title="Flag Day">Flag Day</a> is celebrated on June 20, the following pledge is recited to students nationwide on this day by their respective school principals or grade level advisers. In large towns where students are gathered en masse, the pledge is taken by the local town or city executive, preceded by words of advice and honor to the memory of its creator, Manuel Belgrano, using the following or similar formulas:</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Variant_1">Variant 1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Argentina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Variant 1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<table width="80%">
<tr>
<td>
<dl>
<dd>Summons:<br/>
<i>Niños/Alumnos, la Bandera blanca y celeste—Dios sea loado—no ha sido atada jamás al carro triunfal de ningún vencedor de la tierra.</i></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd><i>Niños/Alumnos, esa Bandera gloriosa representa a la Patria de los Argentinos. Prometáis rendirle vuestro más sincero y respetuoso homenaje, quererla con amor inmenso y formarle, desde la aurora de la vida un culto fervoroso e imborrable en vuestros corazones; preparándoos desde la escuela para practicar a su tiempo, con toda pureza y honestidad, las nobles virtudes inherentes a la ciudadanía, estudiar con empeño la historia de nuestro país y la de sus grandes benefactores a fin de seguir sus huellas luminosas y a fin también de honrar la Bandera y de que no se amortigüe jamás en vuestras almas el delicado y generoso sentimiento de amor a la Patria. En una palabra: ¿prometéis lo que esté en las medidas de vuestras fuerzas que la Bandera Argentina flamee por siempre sobra nuestras murallas y fortalezas, en lo alto de los mástiles de nuestras naves y a la cabeza de nuestras legiones y para que el honor sea su aliento, la gloria su aureola, la justicia su empresa?</i></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>Response: <i>¡Sí, prometo!</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
</td>
<td valign="top">
<dl>
<dd>Summons:<br/>
<i>Children/Students, God cannot permit this white and sky-blue flag to be carried in the triumphal carts of the victors of this earth.</i></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd><i>Children/Students, this glorious Flag represents the Fatherland of the Argentinians. I ask you all to promise to produce your most sincere and respectful homage, to love it and form it with immense love, from the dawn of the life a fervent and indelible cult in your hearts; preparing yourselves from the school to practice to its time, with all purity and honesty, the noble virtues inherent in citizenship, studying with determination the history of our country and that of its big benefactors in order to continue its luminous traces and to end also of honoring the Flag and of which there should never get depressed in your souls the delicate and generous love feeling to the Fatherland. In a word: do you promise that what should be in the measurements of your forces that the Flag of Argentina flames forever exceeds our walls and fortitude, on top of the masts of our ships and at the head of our legions and so that the honor should be its breath, the glory its aurora, the justice its company?</i></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>Response:"Yes, we promise!"<br/>
(either using the <a href="/wiki/Bellamy_salute" title="Bellamy salute">Bellamy salute</a> or using the hand over the heart gesture, if not all stand at attention)</dd>
</dl>
</td>
</tr>

<p>Versions of this include references to Belgrano and to all who fought for the country during the Argentine War of Independence and other wars that followed.</p>

Es la expresión de nuestra historia forjada con la esperanza y el esfuerzo de millones de hombres y mujeres, los que nacieron en nuestra tierra y los que vinieron a poblarla al amparo de nuestra bandera y nuestra Constitución. Representa nuestra tierra y nuestros mares, nuestros ríos y bosques, nuestros llanos y montañas, el esfuerzo de sus habitantes sus sueños y realizaciones. Simboliza nuestro presente, en el que, día a día, debemos construir la democracia que nos ennoblece, y conquistar el conocimiento que nos libera; y nuestro futuro, el de nuestros hijos y el de las sucesivas generaciones de argentinos.</i></dd>

<td valign="top">
<dl>
<dd>Summons:<br/>
<i>Children/Students, in the sunrise of our freedom, Manuel Belgrano created this flag, the very symbol of our fatherland, the Republic of Argentina.</i></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd><i>Children/Students, it is the symbol of our free sovereignty, which makes it sacred to the men and women and to all the peoples of the world. It was convoked by the exercise of our duties and our rights, to respect the laws and institutions. It is the expression of our history forged with the hope and the efforts of millions of men and women, those who were born in our land and those who came to settle it under our flag and our Constitution. It represents our land and seas, our rivers and forests, our plains and mountains, the efforts of its inhabitants, their dreams and achievements. It symbolizes our present, in which, day to day, we must build democracy that enables us, and conquer the knowledge that frees us; and our future, for our children and the successive generations of Argentines.</i></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd><i>Children/Students, therefore, do you promise to defend, respect, and love it, with fraternal tolerance and respect, studying with determination, committing to be free and honest citizens, in solidarity accepting their differences to all who populate in our land and manifest, in each and every one of our actions, these permanent and inalienable values?</i></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>Response:"Yes, we promise!"<br/>
(either using the <a href="/wiki/Bellamy_salute" title="Bellamy salute">Bellamy salute</a> or using the hand over the heart gesture, if not all stand at attention)</dd>
</dl>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
<p><br/>
The <i>Glorious Revielle</i> may be sounded by a <a href="/wiki/Military_band" title="Military band">military band</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Marching_band" title="Marching band">marching band</a> at this point when available, and <a href="/wiki/Confetti" title="Confetti">confetti</a> may be or may not be showered on the students.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_of_the_Argentine_Republic" title="Armed Forces of the Argentine Republic">Armed Forces of the Argentine Republic</a> and civil uniformed services the pledge is similar but with a different formula and response of <i>¡Si, juro!</i> (<i>Yes, we pledge!</i>)</p>
