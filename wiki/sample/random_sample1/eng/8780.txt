<p><b>Ariwara no Motokata</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">在原元方</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, dates unknown, fl. late 800s – 900s <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">CE</a>)</span> was a Japanese <i><a href="/wiki/Waka_(poetry)" title="Waka (poetry)">waka</a></i> poet of the early <a href="/wiki/Heian_period" title="Heian period">Heian period</a>.</p>
<p>He was included in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Late_Classical_Thirty-Six_Poetic_Geniuses&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Late Classical Thirty-Six Poetic Geniuses (page does not exist)">Late Classical Thirty-Six Poetic Geniuses</a>, and thirty-three of his poems were included in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chokusensh%C5%AB" title="Chokusenshū">poetry collections commissioned by the court</a>.</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ariwara_no_Motokata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His birth and death dates are unknown, and the details of his life are also uncertain,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nkbd_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nkbd-1">[1]</a></sup> but he was the son of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ariwara_no_Muneyana&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ariwara no Muneyana (page does not exist)">Ariwara no Muneyana</a> (died 898), the first son of <a href="/wiki/Ariwara_no_Narihira" title="Ariwara no Narihira">Ariwara no Narihira</a> (825—880).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nkbd_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nkbd-1">[1]</a></sup> Who his mother was is also unknown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nkbd_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-nkbd-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to the <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kokin_Wakash%C5%AB_Mokuroku&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kokin Wakashū Mokuroku (page does not exist)">Kokin Wakashū Mokuroku</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">古今和歌集目録</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, he was adopted by his brother-in-law <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fujiwara_no_Kunitsune&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fujiwara no Kunitsune (page does not exist)">Fujiwara no Kunitsune</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">藤原国経</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nkbd_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-nkbd-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a courtier, he held the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8D_Go-I" title="Shō Go-I">Senior Fifth Rank</a>, although the 14th century <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chokusen_Sakusha_Burui&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chokusen Sakusha Burui (page does not exist)">Chokusen Sakusha Burui</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">勅撰作者部類</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> attributes to him the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roku-I" title="Roku-I">Sixth Rank</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nkbd_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-nkbd-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Poetry">Poetry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ariwara_no_Motokata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Poetry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was listed as one of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Late_Classical_Thirty-Six_Poetic_Geniuses&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Late Classical Thirty-Six Poetic Geniuses (page does not exist)">Late Classical Thirty-Six Poetic Geniuses</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nkbd_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-nkbd-1">[1]</a></sup> Thirty-three of his poems were included in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chokusensh%C5%AB" title="Chokusenshū">court anthologies</a>: fourteen the <i><a href="/wiki/Kokin_Wakash%C5%AB" title="Kokin Wakashū">Kokin Wakashū</a></i>, eight in the <i><a href="/wiki/Gosen_Wakash%C5%AB" title="Gosen Wakashū">Gosen Wakashū</a></i>, two in the <i><a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%ABi_Wakash%C5%AB" title="Shūi Wakashū">Shūi Wakashū</a></i>, and nine more in later anthologies from the <i><a href="/wiki/Shin_Kokin_Wakash%C5%AB" title="Shin Kokin Wakashū">Shin Kokin Wakashū</a></i> on.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nkbd_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-nkbd-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The following poem was included as the very first entry in the <i>Kokin Wakashū</i>, indicating the high regard in which the compilers likely held his poetry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nkbd_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-nkbd-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<p>Between one and three of his poems survive in the records of each of several <i><a href="/wiki/Uta-awase" title="Uta-awase">uta-awase</a></i> gatherings: the <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ninna_Ninomiya_Uta-awase&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ninna Ninomiya Uta-awase (page does not exist)">Ninna Ninomiya Uta-awase</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">仁和二宮歌合</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, 893 or earlier)</span>, the <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kanpy%C5%8D_no_Oontoki_Kisai_no_Miya_no_Uta-awase&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kanpyō no Oontoki Kisai no Miya no Uta-awase (page does not exist)">Kanpyō no Oontoki Kisai no Miya no Uta-awase</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">寛平御時后宮歌合</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, also written 寛平御時中宮歌合; 893 or earlier)</span>, the <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Teiji-in_no_Uta-awase&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Teiji-in no Uta-awase (page does not exist)">Teiji-in no Uta-awase</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">亭子院歌合</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, 913)</span> and the <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Taira_no_Sadafun_ga_Ie_no_Uta-Awase&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Taira no Sadafun ga Ie no Uta-Awase (page does not exist)">Taira no Sadafun ga Ie no Uta-Awase</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">平定文家歌合</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, also read <i>Taira no Sadafumi-ke Uta-Awase</i>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nkbd_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-nkbd-1">[1]</a></sup> Some of these overlap with the Motokata poems preserved in court anthologies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nkbd_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-nkbd-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_middle_ages&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Japanese middle ages (page does not exist)">middle ages</a> there was apparently a <a href="/wiki/Kash%C5%AB_(poetry)" title="Kashū (poetry)">private collection of his poems</a>, the <i>Motokata-kashū</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">元方家集</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nkbd_1-11"><a href="#cite_note-nkbd-1">[1]</a></sup> but only a four-leaf fragment is known to exist today.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nkbd_1-12"><a href="#cite_note-nkbd-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristic_style">Characteristic style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ariwara_no_Motokata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Characteristic style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His poems are characterized by an intellectual style.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nkbd_1-13"><a href="#cite_note-nkbd-1">[1]</a></sup> They make frequent use of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mitate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mitate (page does not exist)">simile</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">見立て</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>mitate</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nkbd_1-14"><a href="#cite_note-nkbd-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>His poems clearly display the features of the so-called "<i>Kokinshū</i> style".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nkbd_1-15"><a href="#cite_note-nkbd-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
