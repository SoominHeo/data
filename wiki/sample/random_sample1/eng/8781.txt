<p><b>Hiro Arikawa</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">有川 浩</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Arikawa Hiro</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born June 9, 1972)</span> is a female <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Light_novel" title="Light novel">light novelist</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> from <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dchi_Prefecture" title="Kōchi Prefecture">Kōchi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hiro_Arikawa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hiro Arikawa won the tenth annual <a href="/wiki/Dengeki_Novel_Prize" title="Dengeki Novel Prize">Dengeki Novel Prize</a> for new writers for <i>Shio no Machi: Wish on My Precious</i> in 2003, and the book was published the following year. It was praised for its love story between a heroine and hero divided by age and social status, and for its depiction of military structures. Although she is a light novelist, her books from her second work onwards have been published as hardbacks alongside more literary works with Arikawa receiving special treatment in this respect from her publisher, <a href="/wiki/MediaWorks_(publisher)" title="MediaWorks (publisher)">MediaWorks</a>. <i>Shio no Machi</i> was also later published in hardback. Her 2006 light novel <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toshokan_Sens%C5%8D" title="Toshokan Sensō">Toshokan Sensō</a></i> (The Library War) was named as <i>Hon no Zasshi'</i>s number one for entertainment for the first half of 2006, and came fifth in the <i><a href="/wiki/Japan_Booksellers%27_Award" title="Japan Booksellers' Award">Honya Taishō</a></i> for that year, competing against ordinary novels.</p>
<p>She often writes about the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Self-Defense_Forces" title="Japan Self-Defense Forces">Japan Self-Defense Forces</a> (JSDF) and her first three novels concerning its three branches are known as the <i>Jieitai Sanbusaku</i> (The SDF Trilogy); she also wrote about the fictional Library Forces in the <i>Toshokan Sensō</i> series. <i>Raintree no Kuni</i>, which first appeared as a book within a book in <i>Toshokan Nairan</i> was later published by Arikawa as a spin-off with another publisher. It was adapted into a film titled <i><a href="/wiki/World_of_Delight" title="World of Delight">World of Delight</a></i> released in 2015. Her novel <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shokubutsu_Zukan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shokubutsu Zukan (page does not exist)">Shokubutsu Zukan</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%A4%8D%E7%89%A9%E5%9B%B3%E9%91%91_(%E5%B0%8F%E8%AA%AC)" title="ja:植物図鑑 (小説)">ja</a>)</span></i> will be adapted into a film titled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shokubutsu_Zukan:_Unmei_no_Koi,_Hiroimashita" title="Shokubutsu Zukan: Unmei no Koi, Hiroimashita">Shokubutsu Zukan: Unmei no Koi, Hiroimashita</a></i> and scheduled for release on June 4, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
