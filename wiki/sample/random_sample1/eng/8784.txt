<p><b>Armada</b> is the name of the fictional floating city in <a href="/wiki/China_Mi%C3%A9ville" title="China Miéville">China Miéville</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Scar" title="The Scar">The Scar</a></i>. The city is part of the fictional universe <a href="/wiki/Bas-Lag" title="Bas-Lag">Bas-Lag</a>. It is over a thousand years old, and little is known of its origins. In <i>The Scar</i>, Armada is seen and described through the eyes of several recently press-ganged citizens, notably <a href="/wiki/New_Crobuzon" title="New Crobuzon">New Crobuzoner</a> Bellis Coldwine and <a href="/wiki/Remade" title="Remade">Remade</a> prisoner Tanner Sack.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armada_(Bas-Lag)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Armada is made up of hundreds of ships, airships and other sea vessels all interconnected, existing as a movable pirate state on the oceans of Bas-Lag. The ships, the oldest having been part of Armada for more than a thousand years, are mostly built-upon, factories and residences built up on their decks (and in some cases, within their gutted hulls). The city's population is at least a hundred thousand, and is made up of humans, <a href="/wiki/Bas-Lag#Cactacae" title="Bas-Lag">cactacae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bas-Lag#Khepri" title="Bas-Lag">khepri</a>, ab-dead, <a href="/wiki/Remade" title="Remade">Remade</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bas-Lag#Scabmettler" title="Bas-Lag">scabmettlers</a> and many other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Races_of_Bas-Lag" title="Races of Bas-Lag">races</a>. People considered criminals (or otherwise being of lower classes) in their former home states of Bas-Lag are recast as equals upon joining Armada, and often go on to hold positions of power.</p>
<p>Armada thrives on piracy, and through this method adds wealth to itself. Piracy of the seas also leads to acquisitions of knowledge in countless languages (all books are stolen for the Grand Gears Library), and the growth of Armada's population, for any ship intercepted by Armada's forces is assimilated into the city and its crew and passengers are press-ganged into citizenship for life. The language of Armada is Salt, a <a href="/wiki/Constructed_language" title="Constructed language">constructed language</a> deliberately made easy to learn, and consisting of vocabulary from the languages of the many races who inhabit the city.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ridings">Ridings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armada_(Bas-Lag)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ridings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Armada is divided into separate ridings (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boroughs" title="Boroughs">boroughs</a> of the city), each governed by a different body. The ridings are as follows in the time period of <i>The Scar</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Garwater">Garwater</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armada_(Bas-Lag)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Garwater">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The most powerful of the ridings, led by the Lovers, two nameless scar-covered pirates who influence most of Armada's decisions. Garwater's <a href="/wiki/Flagship" title="Flagship">flagship</a> is <i>The Grand Easterly</i>. It is also in possession of two enormous airships, the <i>Arrogance</i> and the <i>Trident</i>, as well as the stolen <a href="/wiki/New_Crobuzon" title="New Crobuzon">New Crobuzon</a> deep-sea rig <i>Sorghum</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dry_Fall">Dry Fall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armada_(Bas-Lag)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Dry Fall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Governed by a <a href="/wiki/Bas-Lag#Vampir" title="Bas-Lag">vampir</a> called the Brucolac, Dry Fall's residents are prosperous and have the most security and individual freedoms of any of the ridings in Armada, but they pay for this way of life through a "goretax" - they donate their own blood to their vampiric leader. Dry Fall, along with Curhouse, stands against Garwater's plans to raise the avanc. Dry Fall's flagship is the <i>Uroc</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Booktown">Booktown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armada_(Bas-Lag)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Booktown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Run by a <a href="/wiki/Bas-Lag#Khepri" title="Bas-Lag">khepri</a> triumvirate. Booktown houses the Grand Gears Library, Armada's immense stronghold of knowledge.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Curhouse">Curhouse</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armada_(Bas-Lag)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Curhouse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Curhouse is the most democratic of the ridings, and is governed by a large Council, who tend to oppose the Lovers. Curhouse is where Bellis Coldwine's ship the <i>Terpsichoria</i> ends up, and it is also the location of Croom Park, a large public garden spread over several ships that have been gutted and filled with soil.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Thee-and-Thine">Thee-and-Thine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armada_(Bas-Lag)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Thee-and-Thine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This riding is led by a trader-king named Friedrich, whose favour is easily bought by the Lovers. It is regarded as a lawless slum-like neighbourhood by the greater populace of Armada, but Bellis observes that it is more mercantile than lawless. The flagship is the <i>Salt Godling</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bask">Bask</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armada_(Bas-Lag)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Bask">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bask's flagship is the <i>Tailor's Moan</i>. It is home to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Races_of_Bas-Lag#Menfish" title="Races of Bas-Lag">menfish</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jhour">Jhour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armada_(Bas-Lag)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Jhour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This riding is led by a <a href="/wiki/Bas-Lag#Cactacae" title="Bas-Lag">Cactacae</a> queen named Braginod. Its flagship is the <i>Saskital</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shaddler">Shaddler</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armada_(Bas-Lag)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Shaddler">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This riding is governed by a general, and is home to the majority of the <a href="/wiki/Bas-Lag#Scabmettler" title="Bas-Lag">Scabmettler</a> population. Its flagship is the <i>Therianthropus</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Haunted_Quarter">The Haunted Quarter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armada_(Bas-Lag)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: The Haunted Quarter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A deserted riding of ancient ships, believed to be inhabited by all manner of ghouls and other supernatural beings. It was used as a hideout for Silas Fennec.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_avanc">The avanc</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armada_(Bas-Lag)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: The avanc">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the course of the events of <i>The Scar</i>, the Lovers devise of and carry out a plan to raise the legendary <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Races_of_Bas-Lag#Avanc" title="Races of Bas-Lag">avanc</a> to give Armada ultimate power over the seas. This has been tried once before in Armada's history, as evidenced by five enormous chains attached to the undersides of the city's largest ships. Along with a team of scientists and engineers, and against the voices of several of the other ridings, Garwater is able to summon the avanc using a great deal of physical and magical energy. This gives Armada the means to travel to the mythical "Scar".</p>
