<p><b>Hamadoun Touré</b> (born September 3, 1953)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> of <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a> was Secretary General of the <a href="/wiki/International_Telecommunication_Union" title="International Telecommunication Union">International Telecommunication Union</a> (ITU), the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Specialized_agencies" title="Specialized agencies">specialized agency</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> dedicated to information and communication technologies (ICTs), from 2007 to December 2014. He was re-elected for a second four-year term in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Since 2007, he has worked to fulfil ITU's mandate to 'connect the world' and help achieve the Millennium Development Goals. He actively promoted the ITU Connect series of events, with the first one, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Connect_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Connect Africa (page does not exist)">Connect Africa</a>, being held in <a href="/wiki/Kigali" title="Kigali">Kigali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">Rwanda</a>, in 2007. Connect Africa raised US$55 billion in investment pledges to improve Africa's telecommunications infrastructure over seven years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In the first two years, 2008 and 2009, US$22 billion was invested in ICT infrastructure, mainly for mobile communications.</p>
<p>Touré serves as co-vice-chair of the <a href="/wiki/Broadband_Commission_for_Digital_Development" title="Broadband Commission for Digital Development">Broadband Commission for Digital Development</a> which was launched in May 2010 by ITU and <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>, with Paul Kagame, President of Rwanda, and Carlos Slim Helú, Honorary Lifetime Chairman of Grupo Carso, as co-chairs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>He is also on the <a href="/wiki/International_Multilateral_Partnership_Against_Cyber_Threats" title="International Multilateral Partnership Against Cyber Threats">International Multilateral Partnership Against Cyber Threats</a> (IMPACT) International Advisory Board <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2011, IMPACT has been appointed the United Nation’s (UN) Cybersecurity Executing Arm by the UN’s Specialised Agency – International Telecommunication Union (ITU). The Memorandum of Agreement was officially signed by Hamadoun Touré and <a href="/wiki/Mohd_Noor_Amin" title="Mohd Noor Amin">Datuk Mohd Noor Amin</a>, Chairman of IMPACT at the ITU’s head office in Geneva.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamadoun_Tour%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hamadoun Touré was born in 1953 in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Mali" title="Republic of Mali">Republic of Mali</a>. He studied at the <a href="/wiki/St._Petersburg_State_University_of_Telecommunications" title="St. Petersburg State University of Telecommunications">Technical Institute of Electronics and Telecommunication of Leningrad</a> (LEIS), receiving a master's degree in <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Candidate_of_Sciences" title="Candidate of Sciences">Candidate of Sciences</a> degree (equivalent to PhD) from the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Moscow_Technical_University_of_Communication_and_Informatics&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Moscow Technical University of Communication and Informatics (page does not exist)">Moscow Technical University of Communication and Informatics</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ru.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D0%9C%D0%BE%D1%81%D0%BA%D0%BE%D0%B2%D1%81%D0%BA%D0%B8%D0%B9_%D1%82%D0%B5%D1%85%D0%BD%D0%B8%D1%87%D0%B5%D1%81%D0%BA%D0%B8%D0%B9_%D1%83%D0%BD%D0%B8%D0%B2%D0%B5%D1%80%D1%81%D0%B8%D1%82%D0%B5%D1%82_%D1%81%D0%B2%D1%8F%D0%B7%D0%B8_%D0%B8_%D0%B8%D0%BD%D1%84%D0%BE%D1%80%D0%BC%D0%B0%D1%82%D0%B8%D0%BA%D0%B8" title="ru:Московский технический университет связи и информатики">ru</a>)</span> (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=MTUCI&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="MTUCI (page does not exist)">MTUCI</a>), in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. Touré was Director of ITU's Telecommunication Development Bureau from 1998 to 2006. Prior to joining ITU he had a distinguished career in the satellite industry. Touré also is a licensed radio amateur with the call sign HB9EHT.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Touré is married, with four children and two grandchildren.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
