<p><b>Akira Amari</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">甘利 明</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Amari Akira</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born August 27, 1949)</span> is a Japanese politician of the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democratic_Party_(Japan)" title="Liberal Democratic Party (Japan)">Liberal Democratic Party</a> and a member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_of_Representatives_of_Japan" title="House of Representatives of Japan">lower house</a>. He was the <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_Economy,_Trade_and_Industry_(Japan)" title="Minister of Economy, Trade and Industry (Japan)">Minister of Economy, Trade and Industry</a> from 2006 to 2008. He also served as Minister of State in charge of Administrative Reform in the <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_Japan" title="Cabinet of Japan">Cabinet</a> of former <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Japan" title="Prime Minister of Japan">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Tar%C5%8D_As%C5%8D" title="Tarō Asō">Tarō Asō</a>.</p>
<p>He is a native of <a href="/wiki/Atsugi,_Kanagawa" title="Atsugi, Kanagawa">Atsugi, Kanagawa</a> and a follower of <a href="/wiki/Tenrikyo" title="Tenrikyo">Tenrikyo</a> as other LDP Representatives <a href="/wiki/Hakuo_Yanagisawa" title="Hakuo Yanagisawa">Hakuo Yanagisawa</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fumio_Kyuma" title="Fumio Kyuma">Fumio Kyuma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sanae_Takaichi" title="Sanae Takaichi">Sanae Takaichi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bunmei_Ibuki" title="Bunmei Ibuki">Bunmei Ibuki</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jinen_Nagase" title="Jinen Nagase">Jinen Nagase</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>He is also a member of the <a href="/wiki/Japan%E2%80%93Korea_Parliamentarians%27_Union" title="Japan–Korea Parliamentarians' Union">Japan–Korea Parliamentarians' Union</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan-China_Friendship_Parliamentarians%27_Union" title="Japan-China Friendship Parliamentarians' Union">Japan-China Friendship Parliamentarians' Union</a>.</p>
<p>In the Cabinet of Prime Minister Taro Aso, appointed on 24 September 2008, Amari was appointed as Minister of State in charge of Administrative Reform.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 26, 2012, Amari was appointed to the newly created cabinet-level position of Minister of State for Economic Revitalization in the second Cabinet of Prime Minister <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shinzo_Abe" title="Shinzo Abe">Shinzo Abe</a>. Amari's responsibilities within the Abe government also include tax and social security reform.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Like Abe and most members of the Cabinet, he is affiliated to the openly revisionist organization <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Kaigi" title="Nippon Kaigi">Nippon Kaigi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, Amari resigned from his Cabinet post amidst allegations of bribery, and was succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Nobuteru_Ishihara" title="Nobuteru Ishihara">Nobuteru Ishihara</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Akira_Amari&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He graduated from a Prefecture senior high school in Atsugi. In 1972, he graduated from the <a href="/wiki/Keio_University" title="Keio University">Keio University</a> law school.</p>
<ul>
<li>1974: He left his job to run independent for the Kanagawa LDP office. He lost and went to work for his father.</li>
<li>1983: Connected to the <a href="/wiki/New_Liberal_Club" title="New Liberal Club">New Liberal Club</a>, he won a seat in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Japan_general_election,_1983&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Japan general election, 1983 (page does not exist)">1983 general election</a>.</li>
<li>1989: Inaugurated as Parliamentary Vice-Minister of the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Economy,_Trade_and_Industry" title="Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry">Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry</a>.</li>
</ul>
