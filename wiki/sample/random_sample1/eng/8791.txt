<p>The <b>Peace of Amasya</b> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="fa" xml:lang="fa">پیمان آماسیه</span>‎‎; <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <span lang="tr" xml:lang="tr"><i>Amasya Antlaşması</i></span>) was a treaty agreed to on May 29, 1555 between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shah_Tahmasp" title="Shah Tahmasp">Shah Tahmasp</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Safavid_Persia" title="Safavid Persia">Safavid Iran</a> and Sultan <a href="/wiki/Suleiman_the_Magnificent" title="Suleiman the Magnificent">Suleiman the Magnificent</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> at the city of <a href="/wiki/Amasya" title="Amasya">Amasya</a>, following the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ottoman%E2%80%93Safavid_War_(1532%E2%80%931555)" title="Ottoman–Safavid War (1532–1555)">Ottoman–Safavid War of 1532–1555</a>.</p>
<p>The treaty defined the border between Iran and the Ottoman Empire and was followed by twenty years of peace. By this treaty, <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a> were divided equally between the two, with <a href="/wiki/Western_Armenia" title="Western Armenia">Western Armenia</a>, western <a href="/wiki/Kurdistan" title="Kurdistan">Kurdistan</a>, and western Georgia (incl. western <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samtskhe" title="Samtskhe">Samtskhe</a>) falling in Turkish hands while <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Armenia" title="Eastern Armenia">Eastern Armenia</a>, eastern Kurdistan, and eastern Georgia (incl. eastern Samtskhe) stayed in Iranian hands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The Ottoman Empire obtained most of <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Baghdad" title="Baghdad">Baghdad</a>, which gave them access to the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a>, while the Persians retained their former capital <a href="/wiki/Tabriz" title="Tabriz">Tabriz</a> and all their other northwestern territories in the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a> and as they were prior to the wars, such as <a href="/wiki/Dagestan" title="Dagestan">Dagestan</a> and all of what is now <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The frontier thus established ran across the <a href="/wiki/Likhi_Range" title="Likhi Range">mountains</a> dividing eastern and western Georgia (under native vassal princes), through Armenia, and via the western slopes of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zagros" title="Zagros">Zagros</a> down to the Persian Gulf.</p>
<p>Several buffer zones were established as well throughout <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Anatolia" title="Eastern Anatolia">Eastern Anatolia</a>, such as in <a href="/wiki/Erzurum" title="Erzurum">Erzurum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shahrizor" title="Shahrizor">Shahrizor</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Van,_Turkey" title="Van, Turkey">Van</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kars" title="Kars">Kars</a> was declared neutral, and its existing fortress was destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Ottomans, further, gave permission for Persian pilgrims to go to the holy places of <a href="/wiki/Mecca" title="Mecca">Mecca</a> and <a href="/wiki/Medina" title="Medina">Medina</a> as well as to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shia" title="Shia">Shia</a> sites of pilgrimages in Iraq.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The decisive parting of the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a> and the irrevocable ceding of <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a> to Ottoman Turkey happened per the next major peace treaty known as the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Zuhab" title="Treaty of Zuhab">Treaty of Zuhab</a> in the mid 17th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-slovari33200_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-slovari33200-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Another term of the treaty was that the Safavids were required to end the ritual cursing of the first three <a href="/wiki/Rashidun" title="Rashidun">Rashidun</a> Caliphs,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Aisha" title="Aisha">Aisha</a> and other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sahaba" title="Sahaba">Sahaba</a> (companions of Muhammad); all held in high esteem by Sunnis. This condition was a common demand of Ottoman-Safavid treaties,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and in this case was considered humiliating for Tahmasp.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
