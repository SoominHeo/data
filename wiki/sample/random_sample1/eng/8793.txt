<p><i><b>Scolopendra gigantea</b></i>, also known as the <b>Peruvian giant yellow-leg centipede</b> or <b>Amazonian giant centipede</b>, is one of the largest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Centipedes" title="Centipedes">centipedes</a> of the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Scolopendra" title="Scolopendra">Scolopendra</a></i> with a length up to 30Â cm (12Â in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cc_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-cc-1">[1]</a></sup> This species is found in various places in South America and the Caribbean, where it preys on a great variety of animals, including other sizable <a href="/wiki/Arthropod" title="Arthropod">arthropods</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amphibians" title="Amphibians">amphibians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mammal" title="Mammal">mammals</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reptile" title="Reptile">reptiles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-centipedes_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-centipedes-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scolopendra_gigantea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The Peruvian giant centipede is among the largest species of centipedes, regularly reaching 26Â cm (10Â in) in length, and sometimes 30Â cm (12Â in) or more.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cc_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-cc-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scolopendra_gigantea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is naturally found in northern <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. Countries from which verified museum specimens have been collected include <a href="/wiki/Aruba" title="Aruba">Aruba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="CuraÃ§ao">CuraÃ§ao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a> (including Margarita Island) and <a href="/wiki/Trinidad" title="Trinidad">Trinidad</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cc_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-cc-1">[1]</a></sup> Records from <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas,_U.S._Virgin_Islands" title="Saint Thomas, U.S. Virgin Islands">Saint Thomas, U.S. Virgin Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Puerto Rico, Mexico</a> and <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a> are assumed to be accidental introductions or labeling errors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cc_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-cc-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Scolopendra gigantea</i> can be found in tropical or sub-tropical rainforest and tropical dry forest.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behavior_and_diet">Behavior and diet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scolopendra_gigantea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behavior and diet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is a <a href="/wiki/Carnivore" title="Carnivore">carnivore</a> that feeds on any other animal it can overpower and kill. It is capable of overpowering not only other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Invertebrates" title="Invertebrates">invertebrates</a> such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Insects" title="Insects">insects</a> and even <a href="/wiki/Tarantula" title="Tarantula">tarantulas</a>, but also small <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vertebrates" title="Vertebrates">vertebrates</a> including small <a href="/wiki/Lizard" title="Lizard">lizards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frog" title="Frog">frogs</a> (up to 95Â mm long), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Snakes" title="Snakes">snakes</a> (up to 25Â cm long), <a href="/wiki/Sparrow" title="Sparrow">sparrow</a>-sized <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mouse" title="Mouse">mice</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bat" title="Bat">bats</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-centipedes_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-centipedes-2">[2]</a></sup> Large individuals of <i>S. gigantea</i> have been known to employ unique strategies to catch bats in which they can climb cave ceilings and hold or manipulate their heavier prey with only a few legs attached to the ceiling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-centipedes_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-centipedes-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Venom">Venom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scolopendra_gigantea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Venom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Venezuela there is a documented case of a four-year-old child's death attributed to the venom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
