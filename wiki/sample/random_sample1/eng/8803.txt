<p><b>Ameristar Air Cargo, Inc.</b> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Passenger_airline" title="Passenger airline">passenger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cargo_airline" title="Cargo airline">cargo airline</a> based in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dallas,_Texas" title="Dallas, Texas">Dallas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">USA</a>. It operates passengers and cargo services in the Americas, as well as acting as a broker to other cargo carriers. Its main base is <a href="/wiki/Addison_Airport" title="Addison Airport">Addison Airport</a> in north Dallas, with hubs at <a href="/wiki/Willow_Run_Airport" title="Willow Run Airport">Willow Run Airport</a>, and <a href="/wiki/El_Paso_International_Airport" title="El Paso International Airport">El Paso International Airport</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FI_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FI-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameristar_Jet_Charter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The airline started operations on 4 September 2000 and is wholly owned by Tom Wachendorfer (airline President), who also owns Ameristar Jet Charter. Passenger operations began with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boeing_737-200" title="Boeing 737-200">Boeing 737-200</a> in September 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FI_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FI-1">[1]</a></sup> Passengers include sports and entertainment industry celebrities, college athletic teams, and high-wealth individuals.</p>
<p>In 2008, an Ameristar 737-200 was painted for use in the film, <a href="/wiki/The_Kingdom_(film)" title="The Kingdom (film)">The Kingdom</a>.</p>
