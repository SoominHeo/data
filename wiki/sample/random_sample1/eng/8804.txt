<p><b>America Football Club</b>, or <b>America</b> as it is usually called, is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brazilian_Football_League_Teams" title="Brazilian Football League Teams">Brazilian football club</a> from <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro_(state)" title="Rio de Janeiro (state)">Rio de Janeiro state</a>, which was founded on September 18, 1904. The club competed in the <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Brasileiro_S%C3%A9rie_A" title="Campeonato Brasileiro Série A">Campeonato Brasileiro Série A</a> several times, winning the <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Carioca" title="Campeonato Carioca">state championship</a> seven times. The club's home stadium is the <a href="/wiki/Est%C3%A1dio_Giulite_Coutinho" title="Estádio Giulite Coutinho">Estádio Giulite Coutinho</a>, which has a capacity of 16,000. They play in red shirts, white shorts and red socks.</p>
<p>The football anthem composer <a href="/wiki/Lamartine_Babo" title="Lamartine Babo">Lamartine Babo</a> was a supporter of America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lamrtinebabo_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-lamrtinebabo-1">[1]</a></sup> America's mascot is a devil. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=America_Football_Club_(Rio_de_Janeiro)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On September 18, 1904, Alberto Koltzbucher, Alfredo Guilherme Koehler, Alfredo Mohrsted, Gustavo Bruno Mohrsted, Henrique Mohrsted, Jayme Faria Machado and Oswaldo Mohrsted founded America Football Club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-foundation_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-foundation-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1905, America, together with <a href="/wiki/Bangu_Atl%C3%A9tico_Clube" title="Bangu Atlético Clube">Bangu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Botafogo_de_Futebol_e_Regatas" title="Botafogo de Futebol e Regatas">Botafogo</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Esporte_Clube_Petr%C3%B3polis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Esporte Clube Petrópolis (page does not exist)">Petrópolis</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fluminense_Football_Club" title="Fluminense Football Club">Fluminense</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Futebol_Atl%C3%A9tico_Clube&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Futebol Atlético Clube (page does not exist)">Futebol Atlético Clube</a> founded Liga de Football do Rio de Janeiro (Rio de Janeiro Football League), which was the first football federation of Rio de Janeiro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-foundation_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-foundation-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1913, the club won the <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Carioca" title="Campeonato Carioca">state championship</a> for the first time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-campeonatocarioca1913_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-campeonatocarioca1913-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the <a href="/wiki/1971_in_Brazilian_football" title="1971 in Brazilian football">1971 season</a>, the club competed in the <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Brasileiro_S%C3%A9rie_A" title="Campeonato Brasileiro Série A">national Championship</a>'s <a href="/wiki/1971_Campeonato_Brasileiro_S%C3%A9rie_A" title="1971 Campeonato Brasileiro Série A">first edition</a>, finishing in 11th place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-campeonatobrasileiroseriea1971_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-campeonatobrasileiroseriea1971-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The yellow star just above their emblem represents their win in the <a href="/wiki/Torneio_dos_Campe%C3%B5es" title="Torneio dos Campeões">Tournament of the Champions</a> (Torneio dos Campeões) in 1982, which was a tournament organized by <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Football_Confederation" title="Brazilian Football Confederation">CBF</a> to serve as a preview to the <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Brasileiro_S%C3%A9rie_A" title="Campeonato Brasileiro Série A">Campeonato Brasileiro Série A</a> tournament. <a href="/wiki/Clube_de_Regatas_do_Flamengo" title="Clube de Regatas do Flamengo">Flamengo</a> declined the invitation so America, the team with the best record after the selected teams, was invited to fill the spot. America won the tournament by beating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guarani_Futebol_Clube" title="Guarani Futebol Clube">Guarani</a> in overtime at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Est%C3%A1dio_do_Maracan%C3%A3" title="Estádio do Maracanã">Maracanã stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-torneiodoscampeoes_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-torneiodoscampeoes-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006, America was the runner-up of <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%A7a_Guanabara" title="Taça Guanabara">Taça Guanabara</a>. America played the final against <a href="/wiki/Botafogo_de_Futebol_e_Regatas" title="Botafogo de Futebol e Regatas">Botafogo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-campeonatocarioca2006_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-campeonatocarioca2006-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2008, America suffered a major blow by being relegated to the <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Carioca_(lower_levels)" title="Campeonato Carioca (lower levels)">Second Division</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Carioca" title="Campeonato Carioca">Campeonato Carioca</a>. However, they won the Second Division in 2009, thus being promoted to the first level in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> However, the club were relegated again in 2011 and continues playing the Second Division in 2015, returns to the elite of the <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Carioca" title="Campeonato Carioca">Campeonato Carioca</a> after five years vying for the Serie B, after beating the <a href="/wiki/Americano_Futebol_Clube" title="Americano Futebol Clube">Americano</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mascot">Mascot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=America_Football_Club_(Rio_de_Janeiro)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Mascot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>One controversial aspect of the club is the official mascot: the Diabo (<a href="/wiki/Devil" title="Devil">devil</a>), depicted as a red demon complete with horns, pointy beard, curled moustache, a long fat arrow-pointed tail, hooved feet and a black cape. The club's old stadium was nicknamed <i>Caldeirão do Diabo</i> ("Devil's Cauldron"). In 2006 some of the club's fans, supported by then-manager <a href="/wiki/Jorginho" title="Jorginho">Jorginho</a> who is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evangelical_Christian" title="Evangelical Christian">Evangelical Christian</a>, attempted to replace the diabo with a <a href="/wiki/Bald_eagle" title="Bald eagle">bald eagle</a>, claiming that the devil was "unlucky". However, as the "diabo" is a traditional part of the club's story and with the original mascot the club conquered its greatest achievements and there was no change in the club's fortunes with the new mascot, the replacement was abandoned and it was considered that Jorginho's opinion was motivated by religious fanaticism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mascot_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-mascot-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Clubs_named_after_America">Clubs named after America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=America_Football_Club_(Rio_de_Janeiro)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Clubs named after America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>America is the Brazilian club with the largest number of other clubs named after it, who copy even its symbols. Some of its clones include: <a href="/wiki/Am%C3%A9rica_Futebol_Clube_(RN)" title="América Futebol Clube (RN)">América of Natal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Am%C3%A9rica_Futebol_Clube_(SP)" title="América Futebol Clube (SP)">América of São José do Rio Preto</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Am%C3%A9rica_Futebol_Clube_(TR)" title="América Futebol Clube (TR)">América of Três Rios</a>, <a href="/wiki/Am%C3%A9rica_Futebol_Clube_(AM)" title="América Futebol Clube (AM)">América of Amazonas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Am%C3%A9rica_Futebol_Clube_(Te%C3%B3filo_Otoni)" title="América Futebol Clube (Teófilo Otoni)">América Futebol Clube (Teófilo Otoni)</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Am%C3%A9rica_Foot-Ball_Club_(CE)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="América Foot-Ball Club (CE) (page does not exist)">América of Ceará</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enciclopedialance_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-enciclopedialance-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
