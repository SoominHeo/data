<p><b>American Authors</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band based in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> currently signed to <a href="/wiki/Island_Records" title="Island Records">Island Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It currently consists of lead vocalist and guitarist Zac Barnett, lead guitarist and banjoist James Adam Shelley, bassist Dave Rublin, and drummer Matt Sanchez. They are best known for their hit singles "<a href="/wiki/Believer_(American_Authors_song)" title="Believer (American Authors song)">Believer</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Best_Day_of_My_Life" title="Best Day of My Life">Best Day of My Life</a>" from their debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Oh,_What_a_Life_(album)" title="Oh, What a Life (album)">Oh, What a Life</a></i>, as well as their Top 20 hit, "<a href="/wiki/Go_Big_or_Go_Home_(song)" title="Go Big or Go Home (song)">Go Big or Go Home</a>", from their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/What_We_Live_For" title="What We Live For">What We Live For</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Authors&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006.E2.80.9311:_Early_years">2006–11: Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Authors&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2006–11: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The members of American Authors met each other while attending <a href="/wiki/Berklee_College_of_Music" title="Berklee College of Music">Berklee College of Music</a> in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The quartet spent their first years in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a> recording and performing music under the name the Blue Pages and recorded <i>Surrounded by Wolves</i>, <i>Bear Fight</i>, <i>Night Hawke</i>, <i>Anthropology</i> and <i>Rich With Love</i>. In May 2010 the band opened up for <a href="/wiki/Cash_Cash" title="Cash Cash">Cash Cash</a> on the Robots in High-Tops tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2010, the band relocated to <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>. On December 1, 2010, the Blue Pages independently released their single "Run Back Home" on iTunes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.93present:_Name_change.2C_Mercury_Records_and_American_Authors">2012–present: Name change, Mercury Records and <i>American Authors</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Authors&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2012–present: Name change, Mercury Records and American Authors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2012, the group changed their name to American Authors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Round_Hill_Music_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Round_Hill_Music-7">[7]</a></sup> In January 2013, the band was signed to <a href="/wiki/Mercury_Records" title="Mercury Records">Mercury Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Their debut single, "<a href="/wiki/Believer_(American_Authors_song)" title="Believer (American Authors song)">Believer</a>," garnered attention through alternative rock radio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Their second single, "<a href="/wiki/Best_Day_of_My_Life" title="Best Day of My Life">Best Day of My Life</a>", was featured in, among other things, a <a href="/wiki/Lowe%27s" title="Lowe's">Lowe's</a> television advertisement in the United States, a <a href="/wiki/Hyundai" title="Hyundai">Hyundai</a> television advertisement in the United Kingdom, an advertisement in South Africa for Castle Lager,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telecom_New_Zealand" title="Telecom New Zealand">Telecom New Zealand</a> advertisement,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> a trailer for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Delivery_Man_(film)" title="Delivery Man (film)">Delivery Man</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Pro_Evolution_Soccer_2015" title="Pro Evolution Soccer 2015">Pro Evolution Soccer 2015</a></i>, the <a href="/wiki/Konami" title="Konami">Konami</a> video game, the opening sequence for ESPN's <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Series_of_Poker" title="2013 World Series of Poker">2013 World Series of Poker</a> coverage, a trailer for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Earth_to_Echo" title="Earth to Echo">Earth to Echo</a></i>, a trailer for the film <i><a href="/wiki/How_to_Train_Your_Dragon_2" title="How to Train Your Dragon 2">How to Train Your Dragon 2</a></i>, the film <i>Humpback Whales</i>, and a trailer for the film <i><a href="/wiki/St._Vincent_(film)" title="St. Vincent (film)">St. Vincent</a></i>. The song is also featured in the video game "<a href="/wiki/Guitar_Hero_Live" title="Guitar Hero Live">Guitar Hero Live</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The song was also used in an advert for Centerparcs in the UK. The song won the newly established Sync Music Award at the SESAC Pop Awards 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Their song "Go Big or Go Home" was featured in NBA videos showing highlights from the 2015 playoffs.</p>

<p>American Authors released their self-titled debut EP on August 27, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Their song "Hit It" is featured on <a href="/wiki/EA_Sports" title="EA Sports">EA Sports</a> game, <i><a href="/wiki/FIFA_14" title="FIFA 14">FIFA 14</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Round_Hill_Music_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Round_Hill_Music-7">[7]</a></sup> Their song "Best Day of My Life" was used in an <a href="/wiki/MLB_Fan_Cave" title="MLB Fan Cave">MLB Fan Cave</a> commercial and is the title music of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sky_Sports_News" title="Sky Sports News">Sky Sports News</a>' My Special Day feature. It has also been featured in a television spot for the movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Secret_Life_of_Walter_Mitty_(2013_film)" title="The Secret Life of Walter Mitty (2013 film)">The Secret Life of Walter Mitty</a></i>, in an episode of the television show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Vampire_Diaries" title="The Vampire Diaries">The Vampire Diaries</a></i> and in 2013 <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a> World Series championships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The song was ranked number one on Billboard's chart of Adult Pop Songs for 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Their song "Home" was featured in a movie trailer for the movie <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_Where_I_Leave_You" title="This Is Where I Leave You">This Is Where I Leave You</a></i> and in a video honoring troops and their families.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In 2013, American Authors won Overall Grand Prize in the 18th Annual USA Songwriting Competition with their song "Believer". The group was number nine on Billboard's chart of Top New Artists for 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Festivals.2C_concerts_and_other_events">Festivals, concerts and other events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Authors&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Festivals, concerts and other events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Band_members">Band members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Authors&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Band members">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Zac Barnett – lead vocals, guitar (2006–present)</li>
<li>James Adam Shelley – lead guitar, backing vocals, keyboards, banjo, mandolin (2006–present)</li>
<li>Dave Rublin – bass, backing vocals, keyboards (2006–present)</li>
<li>Matt Sanchez – drums, percussion, backing vocals (2006–present)</li>
</ul>
