<p><i><b>American Reunion</b></i> (also known as <i><b>American Pie 4: Reunion</b></i> or <i><b>American Pie: Reunion</b></i> in certain countries<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>) is a 2012 <a href="/wiki/Ensemble_cast" title="Ensemble cast">ensemble</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sex_comedy_film" title="Sex comedy film">sex comedy film</a> written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Jon_Hurwitz" title="Jon Hurwitz">Jon Hurwitz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hayden_Schlossberg" title="Hayden Schlossberg">Hayden Schlossberg</a>. It is the fourth installment in the <a href="/wiki/American_Pie_(film_series)#Theatrical_films" title="American Pie (film series)"><i>American Pie</i> theatrical series</a> and eighth installment in the <a href="/wiki/American_Pie_(film_series)" title="American Pie (film series)"><i>American Pie</i> franchise</a> overall.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Reunion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thirteen years after graduating from <a href="/wiki/Secondary_education_in_the_United_States" title="Secondary education in the United States">high school</a>, Jim Levenstein (<a href="/wiki/Jason_Biggs" title="Jason Biggs">Jason Biggs</a>), Chris "Oz" Ostreicher (<a href="/wiki/Chris_Klein_(actor)" title="Chris Klein (actor)">Chris Klein</a>), Kevin Myers (<a href="/wiki/Thomas_Ian_Nicholas" title="Thomas Ian Nicholas">Thomas Ian Nicholas</a>), Paul Finch (<a href="/wiki/Eddie_Kaye_Thomas" title="Eddie Kaye Thomas">Eddie Kaye Thomas</a>), and Steve Stifler (<a href="/wiki/Seann_William_Scott" title="Seann William Scott">Seann William Scott</a>) are well-established in their lives and careers. Jim lives in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chicago_area" title="Chicago area">Chicago area</a> and is still married to Michelle (<a href="/wiki/Alyson_Hannigan" title="Alyson Hannigan">Alyson Hannigan</a>) and now has a two-year-old son, Evan. Since the birth of their son, Jim and Michelle's sex life has deteriorated. Oz is an NFL sportscaster living in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> with his supermodel girlfriend Mia (<a href="/wiki/Katrina_Bowden" title="Katrina Bowden">Katrina Bowden</a>). Kevin is married to Ellie and works from home as an architect. Finch tells his friends that he has been travelling the world, and still searching for his one true love. Stifler works as a temp at an investment firm, where he is also the victim of humiliating <a href="/wiki/Verbal_abuse" title="Verbal abuse">verbal abuse</a> by his cold-hearted employer.</p>
<p>Former classmate John (<a href="/wiki/John_Cho" title="John Cho">John Cho</a>), one half of the 'MILF' duo, organizes a Class of 1999 high school reunion in East Great Falls. Jim and Michelle return to Jim's old home, where his father Noah (<a href="/wiki/Eugene_Levy" title="Eugene Levy">Eugene Levy</a>) is now a widower. Jim encounters his neighbor Kara (<a href="/wiki/Ali_Cobrin" title="Ali Cobrin">Ali Cobrin</a>), whom he used to babysit and who is soon to turn 18. Jim meets Oz, Kevin, and Finch at a bar, where they meet Selena Vega (<a href="/wiki/Dania_Ramirez" title="Dania Ramirez">Dania Ramirez</a>), Michelle's best friend from band. Stifler appears unexpectedly, and joins them for weekend activities.</p>
<p>The next day, the group goes to the beach. Oz meets his high school girlfriend, Heather (<a href="/wiki/Mena_Suvari" title="Mena Suvari">Mena Suvari</a>), who is dating a cardiologist named Ron (<a href="/wiki/Jay_Harrington" title="Jay Harrington">Jay Harrington</a>), and Kevin reconnects with Vicky (<a href="/wiki/Tara_Reid" title="Tara Reid">Tara Reid</a>). The guys have an altercation with Kara's boyfriend, A.J., (<a href="/wiki/Chuck_Hittinger" title="Chuck Hittinger">Chuck Hittinger</a>) and his friends, which ends with Stifler <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defecate" title="Defecate">defecating</a> in their <a href="/wiki/Beer" title="Beer">beer</a> <a href="/wiki/Cooler" title="Cooler">cooler</a> and destroying their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jet_ski" title="Jet ski">jet skis</a>. That night, most of the group go to the falls and find a high school party celebrating Kara's birthday. Finch and Selena reconnect, and they fall in love. Kara gets <a href="/wiki/Alcohol_intoxication" title="Alcohol intoxication">drunk</a>; Jim drives her home, and she tries to seduce him. They are discovered by John, who simply mistakes Kara for Michelle. Oz, Finch, and Stifler help Jim return Kara to her parents' home, but A.J. spots them. Kevin wakes up <a href="/wiki/Hangover" title="Hangover">hungover</a> next to Vicky and assumes they had sex.</p>
<p>Jim and Michelle attend a party thrown by Stifler, hoping to recreate their prom night, and bring Noah along. Noah gets drunk and encounters Stifler's mother Jeanine (<a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Coolidge" title="Jennifer Coolidge">Jennifer Coolidge</a>) for the first time. Kevin confronts Vicky about the night before, but she insists there was no sex. Mia takes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecstasy_(drug)" title="Ecstasy (drug)">ecstasy</a>, and Ron humiliates Oz. A newly sympathetic Heather comforts Oz, and they reconcile. They are interrupted by Mia, who starts a fight with Heather. Stifler performs <a href="/wiki/Oral_sex" title="Oral sex">oral sex</a> on a former classmate named Loni hoping she would return the favour, only to <a href="/wiki/Baseball_metaphors_for_sex" title="Baseball metaphors for sex">strike out</a>. By this point in the story, Jim has told Kara that he does not care about her. A fight ensues between A.J. and his gang against Jim and his friends, which is disrupted by police. But the police only arrest Finch for stealing a motorcycle, which Stifler finds amusing. Exasperated by Stifler's immaturity, the guys blast him, and finally admit that they did not want him to ruin things like he always does. Distraught, Stifler ends the party.</p>
<p>Mia leaves Oz, Stifler decides to skip the reunion, and Michelle goes to her grandmother's. At the reunion, Finch admits that he is an assistant manager at <a href="/wiki/Staples_Inc." title="Staples Inc.">Staples</a> and stole the motorcycle from his boss when he did not receive a raise that was promised. The boys apologize to Stifler at his place of work, and insist that he is their friend; without him, high school would not have been any fun. Stifler is uplifted by this and quits his job - but not before standing up to his patronizing boss - and attends the reunion. Kevin reconciles with Vicky. Finch makes amends with Selena for lying, and they have sex in the bathroom - but not before a premonition of something vague but bad happening, which is that Stifler is having sex with his mother, as revenge on him for having sex with Stifler's mother. Oz reunites with Heather (a result of Stifler beating up Ron), and Jim reconciles with Michelle. Jessica (<a href="/wiki/Natasha_Lyonne" title="Natasha Lyonne">Natasha Lyonne</a>) reveals that she is a <a href="/wiki/Lesbian" title="Lesbian">lesbian</a>, and with Stifler's help, Sherman (<a href="/wiki/Chris_Owen_(actor)" title="Chris Owen (actor)">Chris Owen</a>) hooks up with Loni. Stifler is asked to be a party planner for a wedding for his former lacrosse teammates. He also meets Finch's mother Rachel (<a href="/wiki/Rebecca_De_Mornay" title="Rebecca De Mornay">Rebecca De Mornay</a>) who intrigues Stifler by telling him she wishes her son were more into sports. After Rachel makes a brazen sexual pass at Stifler using lacrosse equipment-related innuendos, Rachel and Stifler proceed to have sex on the lacrosse field, while Rachel loudly and passionately agrees with Stifler's proclamations about his manliness. John is reunited with his estranged buddy, Justin (Justin Isfeld) (MILF Guy #1) and they watch Stifler having sex with Finch's mom while chanting "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MILF_(slang)" title="MILF (slang)">MILF! MILF! MILF!</a>".</p>
<p>The next morning, Jim and Kara share a tender moment while apologizing to each other for their behaviour. Oz plans to stay in town with Heather, Finch plans a trip with Selena to <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, and Stifler drops subtle hints about sleeping with Finch's mom. Kevin proposes a pact for them to reunite once a year. They all agree and make a toast as the franchise's theme song ("<a href="/wiki/Laid_(song)" title="Laid (song)">Laid</a>") plays. As the credits roll, Stifler says, "I fucked Finch's mom!" in a half-whisper, leaving Finch disgusted. In a mid-credits scene, Noah Levenstein receives sexual gratification from Stifler's Mom at a movie theatre.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Reunion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Reunion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2008, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Pictures" title="Universal Pictures">Universal Pictures</a> announced it was planning to produce a fourth theatrically released sequel to the first film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In <span class="nowrap">April 2010</span>, the film entered <a href="/wiki/Pre-production" title="Pre-production">pre-production</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Jon_Hurwitz" title="Jon Hurwitz">Jon Hurwitz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hayden_Schlossberg" title="Hayden Schlossberg">Hayden Schlossberg</a> signing on to write and direct with plans to reunite the whole cast of the primary series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casting">Casting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Reunion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Casting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <span class="nowrap">March 2011</span>, it was announced<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (January 2013)">by whom?</span></a></i>]</sup> that <a href="/wiki/Jason_Biggs" title="Jason Biggs">Jason Biggs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seann_William_Scott" title="Seann William Scott">Seann William Scott</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eugene_Levy" title="Eugene Levy">Eugene Levy</a> had signed on to reprise their roles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Biggs and Scott were granted <a href="/wiki/Executive_producer" title="Executive producer">executive producer</a> credits and also helped convince the other previous cast members to return.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USATodayPeak_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-USATodayPeak-7">[7]</a></sup> In <span class="nowrap">April 2011</span>, <a href="/wiki/Alyson_Hannigan" title="Alyson Hannigan">Alyson Hannigan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Klein_(actor)" title="Chris Klein (actor)">Chris Klein</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mena_Suvari" title="Mena Suvari">Mena Suvari</a> signed on.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The following month, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Ian_Nicholas" title="Thomas Ian Nicholas">Thomas Ian Nicholas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tara_Reid" title="Tara Reid">Tara Reid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Kaye_Thomas" title="Eddie Kaye Thomas">Eddie Kaye Thomas</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Shannon_Elizabeth" title="Shannon Elizabeth">Shannon Elizabeth</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DeadlineShannon_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-DeadlineShannon-12">[12]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Coolidge" title="Jennifer Coolidge">Jennifer Coolidge</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> signed on. In June and <span class="nowrap">July 2011</span>, <a href="/wiki/John_Cho" title="John Cho">John Cho</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Natasha_Lyonne" title="Natasha Lyonne">Natasha Lyonne</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> were the last returning cast to sign on.</p>
<p>On <span class="nowrap">May 18, 2011</span>, a casting call went out for the character "Kara", a role that involved "upper frontal nudity".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ali_Cobrin" title="Ali Cobrin">Ali Cobrin</a> was cast in the role. <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">National Football League</a> wide receiver <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chad_Ochocinco" title="Chad Ochocinco">Chad Ochocinco</a> has a cameo along with other wide receiver <a href="/wiki/Terrell_Owens" title="Terrell Owens">Terrell Owens</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AccessAtlanta_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-AccessAtlanta-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Jason Biggs and Seann William Scott each received a reported $5 million plus a percentage of the profits for their performances. Alyson Hannigan and Eugene Levy were said to have been paid $3 million each, with the rest of the cast receiving payments within the $500,000 to $700,000 range, except Tara Reid who was paid $250,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Reunion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On a budget of $50 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-budget2_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-budget2-20">[20]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Principal_photography" title="Principal photography">principal photography</a> took place from early June to <span class="nowrap">August 2011</span> in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_metropolitan_area" title="Atlanta metropolitan area">metro Atlanta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DeadlineShannon_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-DeadlineShannon-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In late June, filming took place at <a href="/wiki/Conyers,_Georgia" title="Conyers, Georgia">Conyers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monroe,_Georgia" title="Monroe, Georgia">Monroe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Woodruff_Park" title="Woodruff Park">Woodruff Park</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AccessAtlanta_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-AccessAtlanta-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RockDale_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-RockDale-22">[22]</a></sup> Production filmed at <a href="/wiki/Newton_High_School_(Georgia)" title="Newton High School (Georgia)">Newton High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Covington,_Georgia" title="Covington, Georgia">Covington</a> from <span class="nowrap">July 11</span> to <span class="nowrap">July 15</span>. Scenes were filmed at the school's gym for a reunion prom set, football field, commons area and hallways; which included 200 extras. Under the deal the production company paid $10,000 to the <a href="/wiki/Newton_County_School_System" title="Newton County School System">Newton County School System</a> for using the school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RockDale_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-RockDale-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the last week of July, production moved to <a href="/wiki/Cumming,_Georgia" title="Cumming, Georgia">Cumming</a> to film at Mary Alice Park on <a href="/wiki/Lake_Lanier" title="Lake Lanier">Lake Lanier</a> and included about 100 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra_(actor)" title="Extra (actor)">extras</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MaryAlicePark_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-MaryAlicePark-23">[23]</a></sup> Moore said the beach at the lake looks similar to a Lake Michigan setting, which is the state in which the film is set. The production company paid $23,000 to have full access to the property for a week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MaryAlicePark_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-MaryAlicePark-23">[23]</a></sup> Suvari finished filming her scenes on <span class="nowrap">August 4</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Reunion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Reunion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>American Reunion</i> opened in North America on April 6, 2012 in 3,192 theaters for a weekend total of $21,514,080, putting it at number 2 at the box office behind <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games_(film)" title="The Hunger Games (film)">The Hunger Games</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> On its second week of release it dropped to number 5 at the box office with a weekend total of $10,473,810.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film earned $56,758,835 in North America and $177,978,063 internationally, for a worldwide total of $234,736,898.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-budget_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-budget-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_video">Home video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Reunion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Home video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The DVD and Blu-ray discs were released on July 10, 2012 in North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The film was also released in a box set called American Pie Quadrilogy on August 22, 2012 in Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The rated R version was available on iTunes a few days ahead of time as an "Early Digital Release".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> It was released on the September 10, 2012 in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Reunion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> gave the film a rating of 43%, based on 170 reviews, with an average rating of 5.2/10. The site's critical consensus reads, "It'll provide sweetly nostalgic comfort food for fans of the franchise, but <i>American Reunion</i> fails to do anything truly new or interesting – or even very funny – with the characters."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, the film has a rating of 49 out of 100, based on 34 critics, indicating "mixed or average reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to <a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a>, who gave the film three out of four stars:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote>
<p>The charm of <i>American Pie</i> was the relative youth and naïveté of the characters. It was all happening for the first time, and they had the single-minded obsession with sex typical of many teenagers. <i>American Reunion</i> has a sense of déjà-vu, but it still delivers a lot of nice laughs. Most of them for me came thanks to Stifler... If you liked the earlier films, I suppose you gotta see this one. Otherwise, I dunno.</p>
</blockquote>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Village_Voice" title="The Village Voice">The Village Voice</a></i> concludes its review with the following:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote>
<p>After some strained "Remember the time . . ." callbacks to 13-year-old gags, <i>American Reunion</i> gets comfortable and funny, as Hurwitz and Schlossberg hit familiar marks from unexpected angles, while the ensemble interplay is "routine" in the best sense of the word. Taken altogether, the <i>Pie</i> movies offer a cohesive worldview, showing each of life's stages as the setting for fresh-yet-familiar catastrophes, relieved by a belief in sex, however ridiculous it might look, as a restorative force. The recipe is so durable and the sustained character work so second-skin by now, one can imagine the Pie films keeping with the dramatis personae through middle age and into the problems of geriatric love, a raunch-comic version of Britain's documentary <i>Up</i> series. <i>American Midlife Crisis</i>? <i>American Retirement</i>? <i>American Funeral</i>? Let's go!</p>
</blockquote>
<p><a href="/wiki/Peter_Travers" title="Peter Travers">Peter Travers</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> gave <i>American Reunion</i> a positive review of two and half stars out of four saying, "<i>American Reunion</i> reminds us what we liked about the original: the way the movie sweetened its raunch to build a rooting interest in these characters."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
