<p>The <b>American Samoa national football team</b> (<a href="/wiki/Samoan_language" title="Samoan language">Samoan</a>: <span lang="sm" xml:lang="sm"><i>Au soka Amerika Sāmoa</i></span>) represents <a href="/wiki/American_Samoa" title="American Samoa">American Samoa</a> in <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">association football</a> and is controlled by the <a href="/wiki/Football_Federation_American_Samoa" title="Football Federation American Samoa">Football Federation American Samoa</a>, the governing body of the sport in the territory. American Samoa's home ground is Veterans Memorial Stadium in <a href="/wiki/Pago_Pago" title="Pago Pago">Pago Pago</a> and their head coach is <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Larry_Mana%27o&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Larry Mana'o (page does not exist)">Larry Mana'o</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DT_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-DT-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Samoa_national_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Regarded as one of the world's weakest teams,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DT_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-DT-1">[1]</a></sup> until November 2011 they were the joint lowest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIFA_World_ranking" title="FIFA World ranking">FIFA ranking</a> of all countries registered for full internationals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The team holds the world record for the largest defeat in an international match, set after their <a href="/wiki/Australia_31%E2%80%930_American_Samoa" title="Australia 31–0 American Samoa">31–0</a> loss to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australia_national_football_team" title="Australia national football team">Australia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>American Samoa achieved its first victory in a 3–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Wallis_and_Futuna_national_football_team" title="Wallis and Futuna national football team">Wallis and Futuna</a> in the group stage of the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1983_South_Pacific_Games" title="Football at the 1983 South Pacific Games">1983 South Pacific Games</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> when neither team was a member of FIFA.</p>
<p>On November 23, 2011, the team defeated <a href="/wiki/Tonga_national_football_team" title="Tonga national football team">Tonga</a> 2–1 in the first round of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_%E2%80%93_OFC_First_round#Matches" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification – OFC First round">OFC World Cup Qualification</a>, with a long-range goal by <a href="/wiki/Ramin_Ott" title="Ramin Ott">Ramin Ott</a>, and a chip over the keeper by <a href="/wiki/Shalom_Luani" title="Shalom Luani">Shalom Luani</a>, helping them record their first official international victory after 30 consecutive defeats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DT_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-DT-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>They followed this up with a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Cook_Islands_national_football_team" title="Cook Islands national football team">Cook Islands</a>, but failed to progress in their qualifying group, after losing to <a href="/wiki/Samoa_national_football_team" title="Samoa national football team">Samoa</a> 1–0, the only goal being scored in the 89th minute.</p>
<p>On December 2011 the team achieved its then greatest position in the FIFA World Rankings.</p>
<p>The team's efforts to qualify for the 2014 FIFA World Cup was chronicled in 2014 British documentary, <i><a href="/wiki/Next_Goal_Wins" title="Next Goal Wins">Next Goal Wins</a></i>, directed by Mike Brett and Steve Jamison.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> They followed this campaign with their most successful qualifying attempt in their history during the 2018 World Cup qualifiers in summer 2015, winning twice and narrowly missing out on progressing to the next stage on goal difference. This rise in fortunes can be attributed to the increased number of US college based players, who experience a more professional training regime compared to the amateur players that used to dominate previous squads. These victories would take them to a new high FIFA ranking of 164th in October 2015.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1983">1983</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Samoa_national_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 1983">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The football tournament at the 1983 South Pacific Games in <a href="/wiki/Samoa" title="Samoa">Western Samoa</a> was the first time American Samoa had entered, having missed the six tournaments from <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1963_South_Pacific_Games" title="Football at the 1963 South Pacific Games">1963</a> to <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1979_South_Pacific_Games" title="Football at the 1979 South Pacific Games">1979</a>. American Samoa were drawn in Group A along with <a href="/wiki/Samoa_national_football_team" title="Samoa national football team">Samoa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tonga_national_football_team" title="Tonga national football team">Tonga</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Wallis_and_Futuna_national_football_team" title="Wallis and Futuna national football team">Wallis and Futuna Islands</a>. Two points would be awarded for a win, one for a draw, with the top two in each group advancing to the quarter-finals. Their first two games ended in defeat, losing 3–1 to Samoa and 3–2 to Tonga. In their final game against the Wallis and Futuna Islands, however, they achieved their first victory, winning by three goals to nil. The two points gained from this win, though, were not enough to finish in the top two, so American Samoa went out at the group stage.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1987">1987</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Samoa_national_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 1987">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1987_South_Pacific_Games" title="Football at the 1987 South Pacific Games">1987</a> football tournament saw American Samoa compete for the 2nd time in their history. In a format change from the previous tournament in <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1983_South_Pacific_Games" title="Football at the 1983 South Pacific Games">1983</a>, the entered teams were placed in one large group, with the group winner taking the gold medal. In their opening match, American Samoa were on the receiving end of a 10–0 thrashing from the hosts, <a href="/wiki/New_Caledonia_national_football_team" title="New Caledonia national football team">New Caledonia</a>. Their next two matches also ended in defeat, albeit less severe: 7–0 against <a href="/wiki/Vanuatu_national_football_team" title="Vanuatu national football team">Vanuatu</a> and 5–1 against the <a href="/wiki/Wallis_and_Futuna_national_football_team" title="Wallis and Futuna national football team">Wallis and Futuna Islands</a>. <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea_national_football_team" title="Papua New Guinea national football team">Papua New Guinea</a>, however, were less merciful, and scored twenty without reply in what turned out to be the American Samoans' last game of the tournament, their final match against <a href="/wiki/Tahiti_national_football_team" title="Tahiti national football team">Tahiti</a> being cancelled. American Samoa would not enter again until <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2007_South_Pacific_Games" title="Football at the 2007 South Pacific Games">2007</a>, missing the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1991_South_Pacific_Games" title="Football at the 1991 South Pacific Games">1991</a>, <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1995_South_Pacific_Games" title="Football at the 1995 South Pacific Games">1995</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2003_South_Pacific_Games" title="Football at the 2003 South Pacific Games">2003</a> tournaments.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007">2007</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Samoa_national_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2007">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>American Samoa made their 3rd appearance at the South Pacific Games football tournament in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2007_South_Pacific_Games" title="Football at the 2007 South Pacific Games">2007</a>. The competition also doubled as the first stage of the OFC qualification tournament for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a>, with the three medal winners progressing to the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(OFC)" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification (OFC)">final qualifying group</a>. American Samoa were drawn in Group B along with the <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands_national_football_team" title="Solomon Islands national football team">Solomon Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tonga_national_football_team" title="Tonga national football team">Tonga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Samoa_national_football_team" title="Samoa national football team">Samoa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vanuatu_national_football_team" title="Vanuatu national football team">Vanuatu</a>. In their opening match, they were defeated 12–1 by the Solomon Islands, with <a href="/wiki/Ramin_Ott" title="Ramin Ott">Ramin Ott</a> scoring only their second goal in their <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup_qualification" title="FIFA World Cup qualification">FIFA World Cup qualification</a> history. Following two more heavy defeats (7–0 against Samoa and 15–0 against Vanuatu), American Samoa went into their final game versus Tonga unable to qualify from their group. Their 4–0 defeat in this game, however, was their best result since 2006.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011">2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Samoa_national_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the football tournament of the 2011 South Pacific Games, held in <a href="/wiki/Noum%C3%A9a" title="Nouméa">Nouméa, New Caledonia</a>, American Samoa were drawn in Group A alongside the <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands_national_football_team" title="Solomon Islands national football team">Solomon Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vanuatu_national_football_team" title="Vanuatu national football team">Vanuatu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guam_national_football_team" title="Guam national football team">Guam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tuvalu_national_football_team" title="Tuvalu national football team">Tuvalu</a>, and the hosts <a href="/wiki/New_Caledonia_national_football_team" title="New Caledonia national football team">New Caledonia</a>. In their opening two games, they were defeated 4–0 first by Tuvalu and then by the Solomon Islands. Their best result came in game three, a 2–0 loss to Guam, before back-to-back 8–0 defeats to New Caledonia and Vanuatu ensured that once again American Samoa exited at the group stage. Their group stage stats: Played 5, Lost 5, Scored 0, Conceded 26.</p>
