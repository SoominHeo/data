<p>The <b>Battle of Havana</b> was a naval engagement that took place between the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> squadron and a <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> squadron based near <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">Havana</a> during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/War_of_Jenkin%27s_Ear" title="War of Jenkin's Ear">War of Jenkin's Ear</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Thomas_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Thomas-8">[8]</a></sup> The battle occurred on the morning of the 12 and ended on the 14th October 1748. The belligerents consisted of two squadrons under the command of Admiral <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Don_Andres_Reggio" title="Don Andres Reggio">Don Andres Reggio</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Navy" title="Spanish Navy">Spanish Navy</a> and Admiral Sir <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charles_Henry_Knowles" title="Charles Henry Knowles">Charles Henry Knowles</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a>, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allen_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Allen-9">[9]</a></sup> The British succeeded in driving the Spanish back to their harbour after capturing the <i>Conquistador</i> and ran the vice-admiral's ship <i>Africa</i> on shore, where she was blown up by her own crew after being totally dismasted and made helpless. Although the advantage had clearly been with Knowles, he failed to use this to deliver a decisive blow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harding332_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Harding332-10">[10]</a></sup> The battle was the last major action in the War of Jenkin's Ear which had merged with the larger <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Austrian_Succession" title="War of the Austrian Succession">War of the Austrian Succession</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Thomas_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Thomas-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Havana_(1748)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>By 1747 actions fought between Great Britain and Spain in the America's during the War of Jenkin's Ear had led to stalemate; British forces had failed to subdue any of the Spanish colonies and had lost heavy casualties as a result, while Spain had also failed to subdue any British colonies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Naval warfare did not play a significant role in the outcome of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/War_of_Austrian_Succession" title="War of Austrian Succession">War of Austrian Succession</a>. There were however a few individual actions of importance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The rise to prominence of First Baron George Anson of the Royal Navy through his raiding of Spanish possessions off the West Coast of the Americas in 1740 during his <a href="/wiki/George_Anson%27s_voyage_around_the_world" title="George Anson's voyage around the world">circumnavigation of the globe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWilliams1999215_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWilliams1999215-14">[14]</a></sup> Britain's blockade of <a href="/wiki/Toulon" title="Toulon">Toulon</a> which effectively paralysed a combined Franco-Spanish fleet based there and also interdicted this ports potential role as a base for convoy activity until the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Toulon_(1744)" title="Battle of Toulon (1744)">Battle of Toulon</a> on February 11, 1744.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> This battle resulted in the retirement of the blockading fleet by its commander.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> A planned French invasion of England was stopped by severe weather and the Royal Navy in March and April of the same year but after this naval operations were tied mainly with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Privateers" title="Privateers">privateers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 1747 Admiral Sir Charles Henry Knowles had become commander in chief on the <a href="/wiki/Jamaica_Station_(Royal_Navy)" title="Jamaica Station (Royal Navy)">Jamaica station</a> but had <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Santiago_de_Cuba_(1748)" title="Battle of Santiago de Cuba (1748)">had failed to subdue</a> <a href="/wiki/Santiago_de_Cuba" title="Santiago de Cuba">Santiago de Cuba</a> the following year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DNB293_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-DNB293-18">[18]</a></sup> After having his ships had refitted at <a href="/wiki/Port_Royal" title="Port Royal">Port Royal</a> Knowles sailed on a cruise in search of Spanish treasure convoys hoping to intercept the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_treasure_fleet" title="Spanish treasure fleet">Spanish treasure fleet</a> off <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> before news came of a final peace between Spain and Britain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Richmond132_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Richmond132-19">[19]</a></sup> By this time news of the peace between France and Britain had arrived but no news had been received as of the latters peace with Spain so Knowles sailed on.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harding332_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Harding332-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 30 September he fell in with HMS <i>Lenox</i>, under Captain <a href="/wiki/Charles_Holmes_(Royal_Navy_officer)" title="Charles Holmes (Royal Navy officer)">Charles Holmes</a>, who reported that he had encountered a Spanish fleet some days earlier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DNB293_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-DNB293-18">[18]</a></sup> Admiral <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Don_Andres_Reggio" title="Don Andres Reggio">Don Andres Reggio</a>, commanding the Havana Squadron, left Havana on October 2 with the intention of protecting Spain's shipping lanes from raids by British forces.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allen_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Allen-9">[9]</a></sup> His undermanned crews supplemented by a regiment of troops and several hundred conscripts on board.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marley_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Marley-3">[3]</a></sup> The wind was easterly and varied in intensity throughout the day but diminished significantly around mid-day and picked up again in early afternoon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Richmond132_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-Richmond132-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle">Battle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Havana_(1748)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Battle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Knowles_Action.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="684" data-file-width="1280" height="214" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Knowles_Action.jpg/400px-Knowles_Action.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Knowles_Action.jpg/600px-Knowles_Action.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Knowles_Action.jpg/800px-Knowles_Action.jpg 2x" width="400"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Knowles_Action.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<i>Knowles action off Havana</i><br/>
by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Craskell" title="Thomas Craskell">Thomas Craskell</a></div>

<p>On the morning of October 1, 1748 the Havana Squadron under the command of Admiral Don Andres Reggio was sailing North in a disorganized formation off of Havana.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Richmond132_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-Richmond132-19">[19]</a></sup> Reggio sighted what he believed to be a Spanish convoy and thus with the intention of offering escort to this "squadron" he signalled his command to bear directly on a course to intercept it. Around the same time Admiral Sir Charles Henry Knowles, commanding the British Jamaican squadron, sighted a formation of vessels on a course directly towards him and immediately signalled his own squadron to form line ahead bearing North.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harding332_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-Harding332-10">[10]</a></sup> His intention was to put sufficient distance between himself and the Havana Squadron which would enable him to gain the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weather_gauge" title="Weather gauge">weather gauge</a> and close in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Richmond132_19-3"><a href="#cite_note-Richmond132-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Reggio realized the convoy he had sighted was in actuality the British Jamaican squadron.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allen_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-Allen-9">[9]</a></sup> Immediately he signalled his command to steer to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leeward" title="Leeward">leeward</a> to facilitate the formation of a line ahead bringing him to almost the same course as Knowles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Richmond132_19-4"><a href="#cite_note-Richmond132-19">[19]</a></sup> The result of this however meant that he had lost the weather gauge whilst Knowles on the other hand was in a favourable position to obtain it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Richmond135_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Richmond135-20">[20]</a></sup> Knowles gave the signal for the ships in his line to "lead large" with the Spanish on a more convergent course.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tunstall_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tunstall-21">[21]</a></sup> With the afternoon change in the wind the two leading ships <a href="/wiki/HMS_Canterbury_(1693)" title="HMS Canterbury (1693)"><i>Canterbury</i>and</a> <a href="/wiki/HMS_Warwick_(1733)" title="HMS Warwick (1733)">HMS <i>Warwick</i></a> in Knowles' line drifted within long range of Reggio's centre which then opened fire on them. Knowles had issued standing orders to his entire command to hold their fire but despite this the lead ships returned the fire of the Spanish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tunstall_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tunstall-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Due to the slowness of <i>Warwick</i>s progress Knowles ordered HMS <a href="/wiki/HMS_Canterbury_(1693)" title="HMS Canterbury (1693)"><i>Canterbury</i></a> to pass her at 3pm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Richmond138_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Richmond138-22">[22]</a></sup> However it was not until 4pm that the Knowles' flagship HMS <a href="/wiki/HMS_Cornwall_(1692)" title="HMS Cornwall (1692)"><i>Cornwall</i></a>, and HMS <a href="/wiki/HMS_Lenox_(1678)" title="HMS Lenox (1678)"><i>Lenox</i></a> entered the engagement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tunstall_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-Tunstall-21">[21]</a></sup> This time the combined British ships battered the Spanish and inflicted heavy damage on <i>Conquistador</i> which had soon lost fore and mizzen masts and could only manoeuvre in a small way.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Richmond138_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-Richmond138-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Southworth_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Southworth-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:327px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:KnowlesHavana1748.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="743" data-file-width="1280" height="189" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/KnowlesHavana1748.jpg/325px-KnowlesHavana1748.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/KnowlesHavana1748.jpg/488px-KnowlesHavana1748.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/KnowlesHavana1748.jpg/650px-KnowlesHavana1748.jpg 2x" width="325"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:KnowlesHavana1748.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Drawing showing the burning of the dismasted <i>Africa</i> by the <i>Cornwall</i> and <i>Strafford</i></div>
</div>
</div>
<p><i>Cornwall</i> held its fire until shortly after 4pm when it comes within pistol range and unleashed a broadside into Reggio’s <i>Africa</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Richmond140_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Richmond140-24">[24]</a></sup> Ahead, <i>HMS <a href="/wiki/HMS_Strafford_(1735)" title="HMS Strafford (1735)"><i>Strafford</i></a></i> poured broadsides into <i>Conquistador</i> while <i>Lenox</i> joined the action from astern.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marley_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Marley-3">[3]</a></sup> At 4:30pm HMS <i>Strafford</i> came up close and fired a devastating <a href="/wiki/Broadside" title="Broadside">broadside</a> into the <i>Conquistador</i>; after which she was unable to reply.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allen_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-Allen-9">[9]</a></sup> Within less than an hour <i>Conquistador</i> was battered out of the Spanish line, its captain and two lieutenants lying dead and so soon after struck to <i>Strafford</i> before another broadside could any more damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marley_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-Marley-3">[3]</a></sup> <i>Strafford</i> had failed however to send any boats to take possession of her and Reggio recognized this fact and forced <i>Conquistador</i> to re hoist her colours by firing on her from his flagship <i>Africa</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Richmond140_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-Richmond140-24">[24]</a></sup> HMS <i>Cornwall</i> came up in support with an angry Knowles along with <i>Canterbury</i> - finally <i>Conquistador</i> again struck her colours to <i>Cornwall</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tunstall_21-3"><a href="#cite_note-Tunstall-21">[21]</a></sup> <i>Canterbury'</i>s captain however later claimed that <i>Conquistador</i> had struck to her subsequent to her entrance into the battle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clowes_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Clowes-25">[25]</a></sup> <i>HMS <a href="/wiki/HMS_Warwick_(1733)" title="HMS Warwick (1733)"><i>Warwick</i></a></i> finally appeared ready to overtake the Spanish by 5:30pm and with this every Spanish ship attempted to save themselves, <i>Strafford</i> and Canterbury attempted to rest away <i>Africa</i> while <i>HMS <a href="/wiki/HMS_Tilbury_(1745)" title="HMS Tilbury (1745)"><i>Tilbury</i></a></i> and <i>HMS <a href="/wiki/HMS_Oxford_(1674)" title="HMS Oxford (1674)"><i>Oxford</i></a></i> pursued the vice flag <i>Invencible</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marley_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-Marley-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Southworth_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-Southworth-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>By 9:00pm <i>Invencible</i> appeared silenced, but the British were too weak to prevent its escape.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marley_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-Marley-3">[3]</a></sup> HMS <i>Cornwall</i> having been slowed down by the loss of her her fore <a href="/wiki/Topsail" title="Topsail">topsail</a> but <i>Strafford</i> and <i>Canterbury</i> pounded Africa until its main- and mizzenmasts fell.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Richmond143_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Richmond143-26">[26]</a></sup> However, with night falling fast the Royal Navy ships are unable to pursue so break off at 11pm to begin setting up <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jury_rig" title="Jury rig">jury rigging</a> and claw back out to sea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marley_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-Marley-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allen_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-Allen-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Of Regio's Squadron, four ships returned to Havana's harbour whilst <i>Conquistador</i> had been captured during the action <i>Invincible</i> had suffered heavy damage and avoided capture by a very narrow margin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Richmond143_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-Richmond143-26">[26]</a></sup> <i>Africa</i>, the flagship, was dismasted and badly damaged that she retreated into a small bay 25 miles East of Havana to make repairs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marley_3-8"><a href="#cite_note-Marley-3">[3]</a></sup> Knowles with a lead part of his squadron <i>Cornwall</i> and <i>Strafford</i> headed Eastward on October 14 and soon discovered her and opened fire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Southworth_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-Southworth-23">[23]</a></sup> The stranded crew cut <i>Africa'</i>s cables set her on fire and ran on her on shore; an hour later further helped by British cannon fire she blew up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harding332_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-Harding332-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Havana_(1748)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:177px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sir_Charles_Knowles_governor.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="753" data-file-width="540" height="244" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Sir_Charles_Knowles_governor.jpg/175px-Sir_Charles_Knowles_governor.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Sir_Charles_Knowles_governor.jpg/263px-Sir_Charles_Knowles_governor.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Sir_Charles_Knowles_governor.jpg/350px-Sir_Charles_Knowles_governor.jpg 2x" width="175"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sir_Charles_Knowles_governor.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Edward Knowles by <a href="/wiki/Francis_Cotes" title="Francis Cotes">Francis Cotes</a> pointing to the burning <i>Africa</i></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Knowles then reunited with the rest of his ships but before any action could be planned a Spanish sloop was intercepted where news was received of the Treaty of Aix la Chapelle and that the war in Europe was over.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clowes_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-Clowes-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allen_9-5"><a href="#cite_note-Allen-9">[9]</a></sup> Knowles dropped the Spanish prisoners on Cuba and set sail towards Jamaica with his lone prize.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Richmond143_26-2"><a href="#cite_note-Richmond143-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Battle of Havana demonstrated the importance of tactical cohesion within a unit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Richmond143_26-3"><a href="#cite_note-Richmond143-26">[26]</a></sup> Due to a lack of such cohesion Knowles squadron was not able to come to a close engagement quickly enough.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DNB293_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-DNB293-18">[18]</a></sup> If Regio had so desired he could have easily evaded the British squadron by retiring to the west.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tunstall_21-4"><a href="#cite_note-Tunstall-21">[21]</a></sup> The British squadron also fired on the Spanish too soon at too great a range. Casualties aboard the four surviving Spanish ships were more than 150 dead and a like number seriously wounded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marley_3-9"><a href="#cite_note-Marley-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Both commanders, Knowles and Reggio, were reprimanded by their respective commands for their conduct during the engagement, in Knowles' case for not bringing his full fleet to bear and achieving a total rout.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Richmond143_26-4"><a href="#cite_note-Richmond143-26">[26]</a></sup> Knowles vilification of the Captains under his command, excepting David Brodie of the <i>Strafford</i> and Edward Clark of the <i>Canterbury</i>, after this action resulted in there petitioning the Admiralty for his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Court_martial" title="Court martial">court martial</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Thomas_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Thomas-8">[8]</a></sup> He had managed to force and win the battle and was only reprimanded as a result of the proceedings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clowes_25-2"><a href="#cite_note-Clowes-25">[25]</a></sup> Although Knowles was to suffer a mixed reputation as a result the battle he eventually attained the rank of admiral in 1758.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DNB293_18-3"><a href="#cite_note-DNB293-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Regio was Court martialed by Spanish Naval authorities on thirty separate counts dealing with virtually every aspect of the battle and in particular with the destruction of his own his flagship <i>Africa</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tunstall_21-5"><a href="#cite_note-Tunstall-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
