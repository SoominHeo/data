<p><i><b>Ave Maris Stella</b></i> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a>, "Hail Star of the Sea") is a <a href="/wiki/Plainsong" title="Plainsong">plainsong</a> <a href="/wiki/Vespers" title="Vespers">Vespers</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hymn_to_Mary" title="Hymn to Mary">hymn to Mary</a> from about the eighth century. It was especially popular in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> and has been used by many composers as the basis of other compositions. The creation of the original hymn has been attributed to several people, including <a href="/wiki/Bernard_of_Clairvaux" title="Bernard of Clairvaux">Bernard of Clairvaux</a> (12th century), <a href="/wiki/Venantius_Fortunatus" title="Venantius Fortunatus">Saint Venantius Fortunatus</a> (6th century)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CE_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-CE-1">[1]</a></sup> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hermannus_Contractus" title="Hermannus Contractus">Hermannus Contractus</a> (11th century).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The text is found in 9th-century manuscripts, kept in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%96sterreichische_Nationalbibliothek" title="Österreichische Nationalbibliothek">Vienna</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and in the <a href="/wiki/Abbey_of_Saint_Gall" title="Abbey of Saint Gall">Abbey of Saint Gall</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CE_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-CE-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The melody is found in the Irish plainsong "Gabhaim Molta Bríde", a piece in praise of St. Bridget. The popular modern hymn <i><a href="/wiki/Hail_Queen_of_Heaven,_the_Ocean_Star" title="Hail Queen of Heaven, the Ocean Star">Hail Queen of Heaven, the Ocean Star</a></i>, is loosely based on this plainsong original.</p>

<p>The Latin text of the hymn as authorized for use in the <a href="/wiki/Liturgy_of_the_Hours" title="Liturgy of the Hours">Liturgy of the Hours</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Rite" title="Roman Rite">Roman Rite</a> (ordinary form) is the following:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Liber_Hymnarius_1983_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Liber_Hymnarius_1983-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<table>
<tr>
<td><i>Ave, maris stella,<br/></i>
<p><i>Dei mater alma,<br/>
atque semper virgo,<br/>
felix cœli porta.</i></p>
</td>
<td>Hail, star of the sea,<br/>
<p>Nurturing Mother of God,<br/>
And ever Virgin<br/>
Happy gate of Heaven</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><i>Sumens illud «Ave»<br/></i>
<p><i>Gabrielis ore,<br/>
funda nos in pace,<br/>
mutans Evæ<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> nomen.</i></p>
</td>
<td>Receiving that "Ave" (<a href="/wiki/Hail_Mary" title="Hail Mary">hail</a>)<br/>
<p>From the mouth of Gabriel,<br/>
Establish us in peace,<br/>
Transforming the name of "Eva" (<a href="/wiki/Adam_and_Eve" title="Adam and Eve">Eve</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><i>Solve vincla reis,<br/></i>
<p><i>profer lumen cæcis,<br/>
mala nostra pelle,<br/>
bona cuncta posce.</i></p>
</td>
<td>Loosen the chains of the guilty,<br/>
<p>Send forth light to the blind,<br/>
Our evil do thou dispel,<br/>
Entreat (for us) all good things.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><i>Monstra te esse matrem,<br/></i>
<p><i>sumat per te precem <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><br/>
qui pro nobis natus<br/>
tulit esse tuus.</i></p>
</td>
<td>Show thyself to be a Mother:<br/>
<p>Through thee may he receive prayer<br/>
Who, being born for us,<br/>
Undertook to be thine own.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><i>Virgo singularis,<br/></i>
<p><i>inter omnes mitis,<br/>
nos culpis solutos<br/>
mites fac et castos.</i></p>
</td>
<td>O unique Virgin,<br/>
<p>Meek above all others,<br/>
Make us, set free from (our) sins,<br/>
Meek and chaste.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><i>Vitam præsta puram,<br/></i>
<p><i>iter para tutum,<br/>
ut videntes Jesum<br/>
semper collætemur.</i></p>
</td>
<td>Bestow a pure life,<br/>
<p>Prepare a safe way:<br/>
That seeing Jesus,<br/>
We may ever rejoice.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><i>Sit laus Deo Patri,<br/></i>
<p><i>summo Christo decus,<br/>
Spiritui Sancto<br/>
tribus honor unus. Amen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></i></p>
</td>
<td>Praise be to God the Father,<br/>
<p>To the Most High Christ (be) glory,<br/>
To the Holy Spirit<br/>
(Be) honour, to the Three equally. Amen.</p>
</td>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_settings">Musical settings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ave_Maris_Stella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical settings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The plainchant hymn has been developed by many composers from pre-baroque to the present day. The Roman Rite employs four different plainchant tunes for the <i>Ave Maris Stella</i>; the first three are designated for solemnities, feasts, and memorials of the Blessed Virgin Mary;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Liber_Hymnarius_1983_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Liber_Hymnarius_1983-4">[4]</a></sup> a fourth is given in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Little_Office_of_the_Blessed_Virgin_Mary" title="Little Office of the Blessed Virgin Mary">Little Office of the Blessed Virgin Mary</a> as an alternative to the memorial tone. These plainchant tones have been used as the <i><a href="/wiki/Cantus_firmus" title="Cantus firmus">cantus firmus</a></i> for some polyphonic settings of the mass, including those by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Josquin" title="Josquin">Josquin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tom%C3%A1s_Luis_de_Victoria" title="Tomás Luis de Victoria">Victoria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Renaissance settings include those by <a href="/wiki/Felice_Anerio" title="Felice Anerio">Felice Anerio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palestrina" title="Palestrina">Palestrina</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guillaume_Dufay" title="Guillaume Dufay">Dufay</a> and <a href="/wiki/William_Byrd" title="William Byrd">Byrd</a>. Baroque settings include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monteverdi" title="Monteverdi">Monteverdi</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vespro_della_Beata_Vergine_1610" title="Vespro della Beata Vergine 1610">Vespro della Beata Vergine 1610</a></i> and one by <a href="/wiki/Leopold_I,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Leopold I, Holy Roman Emperor">Emperor Leopold I</a>. Romantic settings include those by <a href="/wiki/Anton%C3%ADn_Dvo%C5%99%C3%A1k" title="Antonín Dvořák">Dvorak</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grieg" title="Grieg">Grieg</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liszt" title="Liszt">Liszt</a>. Modern composers who have either set the text or used the hymn as an inspiration include <a href="/wiki/Marcel_Dupr%C3%A9" title="Marcel Dupré">Marcel Dupré</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grace_Williams" title="Grace Williams">Grace Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Maxwell_Davies" title="Peter Maxwell Davies">Peter Maxwell Davies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Otto_Olsson" title="Otto Olsson">Otto Olsson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trond_Kverno" title="Trond Kverno">Trond Kverno</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Langlais" title="Jean Langlais">Jean Langlais</a>, Andrew Cusworth, and Blake Wilson.</p>

<p><b>Ave Maris Stella</b> is the anthem of the <a href="/wiki/Acadians" title="Acadians">Acadians</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Francophone" title="Francophone">francophone</a> community in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_Maritimes" title="Canadian Maritimes">Canadian Maritimes</a> distinct from the French-Canadians of <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>. The Acadians were highly devout, and had and still have a high degree of devotion to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blessed_Virgin_Mary" title="Blessed Virgin Mary">Virgin Mary</a>. As such, <a href="/wiki/Acadia" title="Acadia">Acadia</a>'s symbols reflect its people's beliefs. This is particularly evident in their anthem which instead of being written in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_Language" title="French Language">French</a> is written in <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>.</p>
<p>It was adopted as the anthem of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acadian" title="Acadian">Acadian</a> people at the Second Acadian National Convention, held in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miscouche,_Prince_Edward_Island" title="Miscouche, Prince Edward Island">Miscouche, Prince Edward Island</a> in 1884. To this day, it remains a source of Acadian patriotism.</p>
<p>The hymn was sung in its original version until French lyrics were finally composed in 1994. The French lyrics are attributed to Jacinthe Laforest, from Mont-Carmel, Prince Edward Island, who submitted her lyrics during a contest held throughout the Maritimes by the <i>Société nationale de l'Acadie</i> in search of French lyrics. Out of respect for the original hymn, the first verse in the Acadian national anthem remains in Latin.</p>
<p>These are the lyrics, in French. The first verse is in Latin, and is repeated at the end of the hymn.</p>
<p>Ave Maris Stella<br/>
Dei Mater Alma<br/>
Atque Semper Virgo<br/>
Felix Coeli Porta<br/>
Felix Coeli Porta</p>
<p>Acadie ma patrie<br/>
À ton nom je me lie<br/>
Ma vie, ma foi sont à toi<br/>
Tu me protégeras<br/>
Tu me protégeras</p>
<p>Acadie ma patrie<br/>
Ma terre et mon défi<br/>
De près, de loin tu me tiens<br/>
Mon cœur est acadien<br/>
Mon cœur est acadien</p>
<p>Acadie ma patrie<br/>
Ton histoire je la vis<br/>
La fierté je te la dois<br/>
En l'Avenir je crois<br/>
En l'Avenir je crois</p>
<p>Ave Maris Stella<br/>
Dei Mater Alma<br/>
Atque Semper Virgo<br/>
Felix Coeli Porta<br/>
Felix Coeli Porta</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Anthem_in_English">The Anthem in English</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ave_Maris_Stella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Anthem in English">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Called: <b>Star of the Sea, we hail Thee</b><br/>
This is an English translation of the Acadian National Hymn. The first verse and last verse are still in Latin:</p>
<p>Ave Maris Stella<br/>
Dei Mater Alma<br/>
Atque Semper Virgo<br/>
Felix Coeli Porta<br/>
Felix Coeli Porta</p>
<p>Acadia my homeland<br/>
To your name I draw myself<br/>
My life, my faith belong to you<br/>
You will protect me<br/>
You will protect me</p>
<p>Acadia my homeland<br/>
My land and my challenge<br/>
From near, from far you hold onto me<br/>
My heart is Acadian<br/>
My heart is Acadian</p>
<p>Acadia my homeland<br/>
I live your history<br/>
I owe you my pride<br/>
I believe in your future<br/>
I believe in your future</p>
<p>Ave Maris Stella<br/>
Dei Mater Alma<br/>
Atque Semper Virgo<br/>
Felix Coeli Porta<br/>
Felix Coeli Porta</p>
