<p>The airline was established on 1929 as Aerov√≠as de Guatemala, and was founded by Alfredo Denby Chattfield. In 1945, the airline was nationalized during the government of Juan Jos√© Arevalo and stablished as Empresa Guatemalteca de Aviaci√≥n S. A., which was shortened to Aviateca. One of the original founders was Alfredo Castaneda Duarte who also served as a pilot. Aviateca started operations in March 1946 and early aircraft operated by the carrier included the <a href="/wiki/Douglas_DC-3" title="Douglas DC-3">Douglas DC-3</a>.</p>
<p>In 1961, service to <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a> was originated with four-engined <a href="/wiki/Douglas_DC-6" title="Douglas DC-6">Douglas DC-6</a> airliners. Convair 340/440 twin-engined medium airliners were also acquired to replace some of the DC-3s on short-haul routes in <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a>. Aviateca was a customer for the <a href="/wiki/BAC_One-Eleven" title="BAC One-Eleven">BAC One-Eleven</a> medium twin jet in 1970.</p>
<p>It also temporarily leased a <a href="/wiki/Fokker_F28_Fellowship" title="Fokker F28 Fellowship">Fokker F28 Fellowship</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Boeing_720" title="Boeing 720">Boeing 720</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Douglas_DC-8" title="Douglas DC-8">Douglas DC-8</a> in the 1970s. Aviateca later acquired two <a href="/wiki/Boeing_727" title="Boeing 727">Boeing 727s</a>, which operated for the airline in the 1980s. From 1989 on Aviateca's fleet consisted of several <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737" title="Boeing 737">Boeing 737-200</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737_Classic" title="Boeing 737 Classic">Boeing 737-300</a> jetliners. A full cargo Boeing 737-300 was also operated for a few months.</p>
<p>Aviateca was under government ownership and remained so until 1989 when it joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grupo_TACA" title="Grupo TACA">TACA</a>-organised Airline Alliance of Central America and was privatised. It was fully integrated into TACA, which later merged with Avianca.</p>
<p>The aircraft were referred to by locals as "las papayas voladoras" (the flying papayas) due to the paint scheme used during the 1970s, in which the underbelly was painted a reddish orange. Later Aviateca switched to blue. From 2006 to 2007 Aviateca had a TACA <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A320_family" title="Airbus A320 family">Airbus A319</a> aircraft with the TACA-style Aviateca logo on the engines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Incidents_and_accidents">Incidents and accidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aviateca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Incidents and accidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>On 17 February 1975, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Douglas_C-47" title="Douglas C-47">Douglas C-47A</a> TG-AMA was destroyed by fire at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=El_Pet%C3%A9n_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="El Pet√©n Airport (page does not exist)">El Pet√©n Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tikal" title="Tikal">Tikal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ASN170275_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ASN170275-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>On 18 November 1975, Douglas C-47 TG-AGA crashed within <a href="/wiki/Pet%C3%A9n_Department" title="Pet√©n Department">Pet√©n Department</a> whilst on a passenger flight from <a href="/wiki/Uaxactun_Airport" title="Uaxactun Airport">Uaxactun Airport</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flores_International_Airport" title="Flores International Airport">Flores International Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santa_Elena,_El_Pet%C3%A9n" title="Santa Elena, El Pet√©n">Santa Elena</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ASN181175_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-ASN181175-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>On 27 April 1977, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convair_240" title="Convair 240">Convair 240</a> TG-ACA <a href="/wiki/1977_Aviateca_Convair_240_crash" title="1977 Aviateca Convair 240 crash">crashed</a> near <a href="/wiki/Guatemala_City" title="Guatemala City">Guatemala City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>, killing all 28 people on board.</li>
<li>On 30 September 1977, Douglas C-47A TG-AKA was damaged beyond economic repair in a landing accident at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flores_International_Airport" title="Flores International Airport">Flores International Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santa_Elena,_El_Pet%C3%A9n" title="Santa Elena, El Pet√©n">Santa Elena</a>. One of the three crew members was killed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ASN300977_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ASN300977-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>On 26 July 1978, <a href="/wiki/Douglas_DC-3" title="Douglas DC-3">Douglas DC-3</a> TG-AFA overran the runway at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flores_International_Airport" title="Flores International Airport">Flores International Airport</a> following a birdstrike on take-off and was reported to have been damaged beyond economic repair.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ASN260778_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-ASN260778-9">[9]</a></sup> The aircraft was later repaired and returned to service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TGAFA_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-TGAFA-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li>On August 9, 1995, at approximately 2014 local time (0214 UTC August 10, 1995), a <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737" title="Boeing 737">Boeing 737-200</a> (N125GU) was destroyed when it collided with the side of the San Vicente (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chichontepec" title="Chichontepec">Chichontepec</a>) volcano, 15 miles northeast of San Salvador International Airport, <a href="/wiki/San_Salvador" title="San Salvador">San Salvador</a>. <a href="/wiki/Aviateca_Flight_901" title="Aviateca Flight 901">The flight</a> (GU901) was on an IFR flight plan from La Aurora International Airport, <a href="/wiki/Guatemala_City" title="Guatemala City">Guatemala City</a>, Guatemala to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comalapa_International_Airport" title="Comalapa International Airport">Comalapa International Airport</a>, San Salvador, <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a>. All 7 crew members and 58 passengers died on the crash. Severe weather existed at the time, and the aircraft had deviated from airway G436 to the north to avoid thunderstorms. The aircraft was owned by CIT Leasing Corporation and leased to Aviateca.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
