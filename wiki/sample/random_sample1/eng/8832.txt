<p><b>Asano Nagaakira</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">浅野 長晟</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, March 18, 1586 – October 16, 1632)</span> was a Japanese samurai of the early <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a> who served as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daimyo" title="Daimyo">daimyō</a></i> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wakayama_domain" title="Wakayama domain">Wakayama domain</a>, and was later transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Hiroshima_Domain" title="Hiroshima Domain">Hiroshima Domain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nekhet.ddo.jp_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nekhet.ddo.jp-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asano_Nagaakira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born <b>Asano Iwamatsu</b>, he was the son of <a href="/wiki/Asano_Nagamasa" title="Asano Nagamasa">Asano Nagamasa</a>, who was a senior retainer of Toyotomi Hideyoshi. In 1594, Nagaakira was made a retainer of Toyotomi Hideyoshi, and awarded a stipend of 3,000 <i>koku</i>. Allying his forces to <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Ieyasu" title="Tokugawa Ieyasu">Tokugawa Ieyasu</a> six years later at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Sekigahara" title="Battle of Sekigahara">Battle of Sekigahara</a>, he was subsequently awarded with the 24,000 <i>koku</i> fief of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ashimori_han&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ashimori han (page does not exist)">Ashimori han</a>. As his brother <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asano_Yukinaga" title="Asano Yukinaga">Yukinaga</a> died heirless in 1613, Nagaakira succeeded him, becoming daimyo of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wakayama_han" title="Wakayama han">Wakayama han</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nekhet.ddo.jp_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nekhet.ddo.jp-1">[1]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Osaka" title="Siege of Osaka">Siege of Ōsaka</a>, he commanded a portion of <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Ieyasu" title="Tokugawa Ieyasu">Tokugawa Ieyasu</a>'s army. In the summer of 1615, <a href="/wiki/Toyotomi_Hideyori" title="Toyotomi Hideyori">Toyotomi Hideyori</a>'s Western Army moved to attack Asano's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wakayama_castle" title="Wakayama castle">castle at Wakayama</a>. Though most of Asano's forces were at Ōsaka, besieging Toyotomi's fortress, the remaining garrison outnumbered the Western warriors, and Asano led his men in sallying forth to meet the enemy in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Kashii" title="Battle of Kashii">Battle of Kashii</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Asano also fought in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Tennoji" title="Battle of Tennoji">Battle of Tennoji</a>, the decisive final battle in the Siege of Ōsaka, where he commanded Tokugawa's rear guard. In 1619, he was granted the fief of Hiroshima, in <a href="/wiki/Aki_Province" title="Aki Province">Aki Province</a>, which would be the home of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asano_family" title="Asano family">Asano family</a> for many generations. Nagaakira was married to Furi-hime, the third of Tokugawa Ieyasu`s daughters.</p>
