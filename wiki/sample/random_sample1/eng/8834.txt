<p><b>Asamushi-Onsen Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">浅虫温泉駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Asamushi-Onsen-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_station" title="Railway station">railway station</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Aoimori_Railway_Line" title="Aoimori Railway Line">Aoimori Railway Line</a> located in the city of <a href="/wiki/Aomori,_Aomori" title="Aomori, Aomori">Aomori</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aomori_Prefecture" title="Aomori Prefecture">Aomori Prefecture</a> <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It is 17.2 rail kilometers from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terminal_station" title="Terminal station">terminus</a> of the Aoimori Railway Line at <a href="/wiki/Aomori_Station" title="Aomori Station">Aomori Station</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asamushi-Onsen_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Asamushi-Onsen Station was opened on September 1, 1891 as <b>Asamushi</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">浅虫駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Asamushi-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> on the <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Railway" title="Nippon Railway">Nippon Railway</a>. It became a station on the <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dhoku_Main_Line" title="Tōhoku Main Line">Tōhoku Main Line</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Government_Railways" title="Japanese Government Railways">Japanese Government Railways</a> (JGR), the pre-war predecessor to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_National_Railway" title="Japan National Railway">Japan National Railways</a> (JNR), after the <a href="/wiki/Nationalization" title="Nationalization">nationalization</a> of the Nippon Railway on November 1, 1906. Regularly scheduled freight services were discontinued in February 1962. The station was renamed to its present name on November 1, 1986. With the <a href="/wiki/Privatization" title="Privatization">privatization</a> of the JNR on April 1, 1987, it came under the operational control of <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">East Japan Railway Company</a> (JR East). From April 1, 2005 the station had been operated by Jaster Co., Ltd., with ticketing window for ordinary tickets, express tickets, and reserved-seat tickets for all JR lines.</p>
<p>The section of the Tōhoku Main Line including this station was transferred to Aoimori Railway on December 4, 2010.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asamushi-Onsen_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Asamushi-Onsen Station has an <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platform</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platform</a> serving three tracks, connected the station building by an overpass. However, only tracks 1 and 3 are in use, and the rails for track 2 are used only as a siding, giving the station an effective structure of two opposed side platforms. The station building is attended.</p>
