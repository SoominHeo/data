<p>The <b><i>Asashio</i>-class destroyers</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">朝潮型駆逐艦</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Asashio-gata kuchikukan</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> were a <a href="/wiki/Ship_class" title="Ship class">class</a> of ten <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyers</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Imperial Japanese Navy</a> in service before and during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asashio-class_destroyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Imperial Japanese Navy was not entirely satisfied with the performance of the <a href="/wiki/Shiratsuyu-class_destroyer" title="Shiratsuyu-class destroyer"><i>Shiratsuyu</i>-class</a> <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyer</a>, particularly in terms of operational range and speed. However, given the limitations of the 1930 <a href="/wiki/London_Naval_Treaty" title="London Naval Treaty">London Naval Treaty</a>, it was considered impossible to modify these vessels any further to improve their specifications. This obstacle was removed after the Japanese government allowed the treaty to expire without renewal. The final four vessels of a projected 14 destroyers in the <i>Shiratsuyu</i> class were cancelled, and the larger new <i>Asashio</i>-class vessels were approved under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2nd_Naval_Armaments_Supplement_Programme_(Japan,_1934)" title="2nd Naval Armaments Supplement Programme (Japan, 1934)">Maru-2</a> Supplementary Naval Expansion budget of 1934, with construction spanning 1935-1936. All ten vessels were lost in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_War" title="Pacific War">Pacific War</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asashio-class_destroyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <i>Asashio</i> class was the first Japanese destroyer class to exceed 2,000 tons <a href="/wiki/Displacement_(ship)" title="Displacement (ship)">displacement</a> and the first to be equipped with <a href="/wiki/Sonar" title="Sonar">sonar</a>. Early critical issues with the <i>Asashio</i> class included the reliability of their new <a href="/wiki/Steam_turbine" title="Steam turbine">steam turbines</a> engines, and issues with the design of their <a href="/wiki/Rudder" title="Rudder">rudder</a>, which were addressed by the start of the Pacific War.</p>
<p>In terms of armament, the <i>Asashio</i> class was similar to the previous <a href="/wiki/Fubuki-class_destroyer" title="Fubuki-class destroyer"><i>Fubuki</i></a> and Hatsuharu classes, but with twin mount 5-inch guns like the Fubukis instead of having a single mount like the Hatsuharus. The guns were mounted in Type C gun turrets capable of 55-degree elevation. Also, the position of the "X" turret at the shelter deck level forward of the <a href="/wiki/Quarterdeck" title="Quarterdeck">quarterdeck</a> "Y" turret, gave the <i>Asashio</i> class a different silhouette than the <i>Shiratsuyu</i> class, where both turrets were at quarterdeck level. The torpedo armament of eight 24-inch torpedo in two quadruple launchers was retained, with the torpedo reloads stored in a deckhouse on the centerline. This design became the basic plan for all future destroyers in the Japanese navy.</p>
<p>The <i>Asashio</i>-class vessels had three boilers running two geared turbine shafts with an output of 50,000 hp (37,000 kW) at the screws, giving it a top speed of 35 knots (65 km/h) and a range of 5,700 <a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">nautical miles</a> (10,600 km) at 15 <a href="/wiki/Knot_(unit)" title="Knot (unit)">knots</a> (28 km/h) or 960 nautical miles (1,780 km) at 34 knots (63 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the middle of the Pacific War (1943–44), on surviving vessels the "X" turret was removed and replaced by triple <a href="/wiki/Type_96_25_mm_AT/AA_Gun" title="Type 96 25 mm AT/AA Gun">Type 96 25 mm anti-aircraft guns</a>, increasing the total number to 15. After June 1944, even more anti-aircraft guns were added, raising the total to between 15 and 28 Type 96 guns and four <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/13.2_mm_Hotchkiss_machine_gun" title="13.2 mm Hotchkiss machine gun">Type 93 13 mm guns</a>. Four <a href="/wiki/Depth_charge" title="Depth charge">depth charge</a> throwers and 36 depth charges were also fitted at between 1943 and 1944. The last four surviving vessels were also equipped with Type 13 and Type 22 <a href="/wiki/Radar" title="Radar">radar</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operational_history">Operational history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asashio-class_destroyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Operational history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the war the <i>Asashio</i> class was used extensively in the protection of the <a href="/wiki/Combined_Fleet" title="Combined Fleet">Combined Fleet</a>. <i>Arare</i> was <a href="/wiki/Action_of_5_July_1942" title="Action of 5 July 1942">attacked</a> and sunk off <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kiska_Island" title="Kiska Island">Kiska Island</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Aleutian_Islands_Campaign" title="Aleutian Islands Campaign">Aleutian Islands Campaign</a> by <a href="/wiki/USS_Growler_(SS-215)" title="USS Growler (SS-215)">USS <i>Growler</i></a> on 5 July 1942. <i>Kasumi</i> was also heavily damaged along with one other destroyer. <i>Asashio</i> and <i>Arashio</i> escorted the 7th Cruiser Division at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Midway" title="Battle of Midway">Battle of Midway</a>, where both were damaged by air attack. Although repaired and returned to service, both were sunk in 1943 when a large Japanese transport force was destroyed by American planes in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Bismarck_Sea" title="Battle of the Bismarck Sea">Battle of the Bismarck Sea</a>. <i>Michishio</i>, <i>Asagumo</i>, and <i>Yamagumo</i> were lost in 1944 in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Leyte_Gulf" title="Battle of Leyte Gulf">Battle of Leyte Gulf</a>. <i>Kasumi</i>, last of the class to be laid down, served as escort on the <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">attack on Pearl Harbor</a> and joined the battleship <a href="/wiki/Japanese_battleship_Yamato" title="Japanese battleship Yamato"><i>Yamato</i></a> during <a href="/wiki/Operation_Ten-Go" title="Operation Ten-Go">Operation Ten-Go</a> against the American fleet at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Okinawa" title="Okinawa">Okinawa</a>. None of the <i>Asashio</i>-class ships survived the Pacific War.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
