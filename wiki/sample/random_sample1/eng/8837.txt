<p><b>Asaka Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">朝霞駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Asaka-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tobu_Tojo_Line" title="Tobu Tojo Line">Tobu Tojo Line</a> in <a href="/wiki/Asaka,_Saitama" title="Asaka, Saitama">Asaka, Saitama</a>, Japan, operated by the <a href="/wiki/Private_railway" title="Private railway">private railway</a> operator <a href="/wiki/Tobu_Railway" title="Tobu Railway">Tobu Railway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tobu_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tobu-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asaka_Station_(Saitama)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station is served by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tobu_Tojo_Line" title="Tobu Tojo Line">Tobu Tojo Line</a> from <a href="/wiki/Ikebukuro_Station" title="Ikebukuro Station">Ikebukuro</a> in Tokyo, with some services inter-running via the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metro_Yurakucho_Line" title="Tokyo Metro Yurakucho Line">Tokyo Metro Yurakucho Line</a> to <a href="/wiki/Shin-Kiba_Station" title="Shin-Kiba Station">Shin-Kiba</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metro_Fukutoshin_Line" title="Tokyo Metro Fukutoshin Line">Tokyo Metro Fukutoshin Line</a> to <a href="/wiki/Shibuya_Station" title="Shibuya Station">Shibuya</a> and onward via the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokyu_Toyoko_Line" title="Tokyu Toyoko Line">Tokyu Toyoko Line</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minato_Mirai_Line" title="Minato Mirai Line">Minato Mirai Line</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motomachi-Chukagai_Station" title="Motomachi-Chukagai Station">Motomachi-Chukagai</a>. Located between Wakōshi and Asakadai stations, it is 14.0 km from the Ikebukuro terminus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-terada2002_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-terada2002-2">[2]</a></sup> Only Semi express and Local services stop at this station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timetable2016_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-timetable2016-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asaka_Station_(Saitama)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station consists of two <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platforms</a> serving four tracks, with an elevated station building located above the platforms.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asaka_Station_(Saitama)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station first opened as Hizaori Station <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">膝折駅</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> on 1 May 1914, coinciding with the opening of the Tojo Railway line from Ikebukuro. It was renamed Asaka Station on 10 May 1932.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Railfan570_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Railfan570-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Through-running to and from <a href="/wiki/Shibuya_Station" title="Shibuya Station">Shibuya</a> via the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metro_Fukutoshin_Line" title="Tokyo Metro Fukutoshin Line">Tokyo Metro Fukutoshin Line</a> commenced on 14 June 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timetable2008_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-timetable2008-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 17 March 2012, station numbering was introduced on the Tobu Tojo Line, with Asaka Station becoming "TJ-12".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tobu20120209_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-tobu20120209-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Through-running to and from <a href="/wiki/Yokohama_Station" title="Yokohama Station">Yokohama</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motomachi-Chukagai_Station" title="Motomachi-Chukagai Station">Motomachi-Chukagai</a> via the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokyu_Toyoko_Line" title="Tokyu Toyoko Line">Tokyu Toyoko Line</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minatomirai_Line" title="Minatomirai Line">Minatomirai Line</a> commenced on 16 March 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timetable2013_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-timetable2013-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
