<p><b>Yū Asakawa</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">浅川 悠</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Asakawa Yū</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born March 20, 1975)</span>, is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Voice_acting_in_Japan" title="Voice acting in Japan">voice actress</a> from <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Doi_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Doi-1">[1]</a></sup> She is also a singer, both solo and with the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hinata_Girls&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hinata Girls (page does not exist)">Hinata Girls</a>. She also provides the voice samples for the <a href="/wiki/Crypton_Future_Media" title="Crypton Future Media">Crypton Future Media</a>'s fourth <a href="/wiki/Vocaloid" title="Vocaloid">Vocaloid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Megurine_Luka" title="Megurine Luka">Megurine Luka</a>. Asakawa is currently a freelance voice actress and was affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/Arts_Vision" title="Arts Vision">Arts Vision</a> agency (as of October 1, 2014).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Asakawa_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Asakawa-2">[2]</a></sup> She was previously married to fellow voice actor <a href="/wiki/Showtaro_Morikubo" title="Showtaro Morikubo">Showtaro Morikubo</a> but the two are now divorced. She is also a co-host of an internet webshow Otaku Verse Zero with <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Macias" title="Patrick Macias">Patrick Macias</a>, the editor-in-chief of Otaku USA.</p>
