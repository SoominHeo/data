<p><b>Assane Demoya Gnoukouri</b> (<small>pronounced: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[asan dəmwaja ɲukuʁi]</a></span>; born 28 September 1996) is an Ivorian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Central_midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> for Italian club <a href="/wiki/Inter_Milan" title="Inter Milan">Internazionale</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Assane_Gnoukouri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gnoukouri spent his early career the <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Olympique de Marseille</a> youth academy.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> After being released by the team, in December 2013, he moved to <a href="/wiki/F.C.D._Altovicentino" title="F.C.D. Altovicentino">Altovicentino</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> On 18 September 2014, Gnoukouri, along with his younger brother Zate Wilfried Gnoukouri and other brothers, Miguel Anguel Gnoukouri and Williams Gnoukouri, moved to <a href="/wiki/F.C._Internazionale_Milano_Primavera" title="F.C. Internazionale Milano Primavera">Internazionale Primavera</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Assane_Gnoukouri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He made his <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> debut for the club on 11 April 2015, when he came on as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Gary_Medel" title="Gary Medel">Gary Medel</a> after 80 minutes in 3–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Hellas_Verona_F.C." title="Hellas Verona F.C.">Hellas Verona</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Assane_Gnoukouri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gnoukouri was born in Ivory Coast to Ivorian parents, but was raised in France as child - he then spent his late teens in Italy. He received a callup to the <a href="/wiki/Ivory_Coast_national_football_team" title="Ivory Coast national football team">Ivory Coast national football team</a> for their friendly 0-0 tie against <a href="/wiki/Hungary_national_football_team" title="Hungary national football team">Hungary</a>, wherein he was on the bench.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He debuted for the <a href="/wiki/Ivory_Coast_national_under-23_football_team" title="Ivory Coast national under-23 football team">Ivory Coast U23s</a> in a 5-1 loss to <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-21_football_team" title="France national under-21 football team">France U21on</a> November 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
