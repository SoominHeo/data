<p><b>Asha-Rose Mtengeti Migiro</b> (born 9 July 1956) is a Tanzanian politician and diplomat who was the <a href="/wiki/Deputy_Secretary-General_of_the_United_Nations" title="Deputy Secretary-General of the United Nations">Deputy Secretary-General of the United Nations</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-cnn-3">[3]</a></sup> from 2007 to 2012. She was appointed as the United Nations Secretary-General's Special Envoy for HIV/AIDS in Africa on 13 July 2012.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asha-Rose_Migiro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born at <a href="/wiki/Songea" title="Songea">Songea</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ruvuma_Region" title="Ruvuma Region">Ruvuma Region</a>, Migiro commenced her education at Mnazi Mmoja Primary School in 1963. She later moved on to Korogwe Primary School, <a href="/wiki/Weruweru_Secondary_School" title="Weruweru Secondary School">Weruweru Secondary School</a>, and, finally, Korogwe Secondary School, where she graduated high school in 1975.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-parliament_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-parliament-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>She obtained her LL.B and LL.M from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Dar_es_Salaam" title="University of Dar es Salaam">University of Dar es Salaam</a> and her PhD in 1992 from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Konstanz" title="University of Konstanz">University of Konstanz</a> in Germany. Before entering politics, she was a senior lecturer at the Faculty of Law at the University of Dar es Salaam (UDSM).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unnewscentre_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-unnewscentre-5">[5]</a></sup> She headed the Department of Constitution and Administrative Law from 1992 to 1994 and the Department of Civil and Criminal Law from 1994 to 1997.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Time_in_the_Tanzanian_government">Time in the Tanzanian government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asha-Rose_Migiro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Time in the Tanzanian government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Migiro%26Kikwete.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="540" data-file-width="960" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/1e/Migiro%26Kikwete.JPG/220px-Migiro%26Kikwete.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/1e/Migiro%26Kikwete.JPG/330px-Migiro%26Kikwete.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/1e/Migiro%26Kikwete.JPG/440px-Migiro%26Kikwete.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Migiro%26Kikwete.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Migiro with President <a href="/wiki/Jakaya_Kikwete" title="Jakaya Kikwete">Jakaya Kikwete</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Migiro served as a ward member of <a href="/wiki/Chama_Cha_Mapinduzi" title="Chama Cha Mapinduzi">Chama Cha Mapinduzi</a> from 1994 to 2000, and as a member of a Regional Executive Council from 2000 to 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-parliament_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-parliament-4">[4]</a></sup> From 2000 to 2006, she was the Minister of Community Development, Gender and Children's Affairs. She became the Minister of Foreign Affairs and International Cooperation on 4 January 2006, when the previous foreign minister, <a href="/wiki/Jakaya_Kikwete" title="Jakaya Kikwete">Jakaya Kikwete</a>, who had been elected President, appointed his new cabinet. She was the first woman in that position since the independence of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Republic_of_Tanzania" title="United Republic of Tanzania">United Republic of Tanzania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ippmedia_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ippmedia-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>While in the position of foreign minister, Migiro chaired the Council of Ministers' meetings of the International Conference of the Great Lakes Region and the Southern African Development Community(SADC) Ministerial Committee of the Organ on Politics, Defense and Security Cooperation. She coordinated SADC assistance to the elections in the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a> (DRC), <a href="/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>. She also served as President of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council" title="United Nations Security Council">United Nations Security Council</a> during its open debate on peace, security and development in the Great Lakes Region.</p>
<p>Serving as foreign minister, Migiro accompanied the former president of the <a href="/wiki/Comoros" title="Comoros">Comoros</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azali_Assoumani" title="Azali Assoumani">Azali Assoumani</a>, during a tour of his country's new consulate in Tanzania and inspected a Tanzanian hospital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ippmedia2_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-ippmedia2-7">[7]</a></sup> According to United States officials, <a href="/wiki/Condoleezza_Rice" title="Condoleezza Rice">Condoleezza Rice</a>, the American <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_State" title="United States Secretary of State">Secretary of State</a>, is "personally acquainted" with her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-8">[8]</a></sup> Kikwete appointed <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Membe" title="Bernard Membe">Bernard Membe</a> to succeed Migiro as foreign minister in January 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dailynews-tsn_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-dailynews-tsn-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Nations_appointment">United Nations appointment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asha-Rose_Migiro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: United Nations appointment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Migiro was appointed to the post of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Deputy_Secretary-General" title="United Nations Deputy Secretary-General">United Nations Deputy Secretary-General</a> by <a href="/wiki/Ban_Ki-moon" title="Ban Ki-moon">Ban Ki-moon</a>, the new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Secretary-General" title="United Nations Secretary-General">United Nations Secretary-General</a> from <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, on 5 January 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-cnn-3">[3]</a></sup> According to Ban, "She is a highly respected leader who has championed the cause of developing countries over the years..." He also said that "Through her distinguished service in diverse areas, she has displayed outstanding management skills with wide experience and expertise in socio-economic affairs and development issues."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-myafricatoday_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-myafricatoday-10">[10]</a></sup> According to <a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a>, this was a fulfilment of his promise to pick a woman from the developing world for the post of Deputy Secretary-General.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-8">[8]</a></sup> The UN News Centre noted that Migiro and Ban had worked together while they were foreign ministers of their respective countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unnewscentre_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-unnewscentre-5">[5]</a></sup> She was formally appointed and assumed office on 1 February 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2009, she travelled to Rome and had a meeting with Italian foreign minister <a href="/wiki/Franco_Frattini" title="Franco Frattini">Franco Frattini</a> and Pope <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benedict_XVI" title="Benedict XVI">Benedict XVI</a> to discuss <a href="/wiki/Violence_against_women" title="Violence against women">violence against women</a>. United Nations representatives were reportedly preparing to finalise an initiative aimed at stopping the <a href="/wiki/Female_genital_mutilation" title="Female genital mutilation">genital mutilation of women</a> and genocide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Migiro served as Deputy Secretary-General until June 2012.</p>
