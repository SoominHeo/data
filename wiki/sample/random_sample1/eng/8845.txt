<p><b>Arthur Drewry</b>, <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Order of the British Empire">CBE</a> (3 March 1891 – 25 March 1961) was an English <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> administrator who served as the fifth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Presidents_of_FIFA" title="List of Presidents of FIFA">president of FIFA</a>, the world <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sport_governing_body" title="Sport governing body">governing body</a> of <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">association football</a>, from 1955 to 1961. Drewry had held several football administration posts in his native England, serving as chairman of <a href="/wiki/The_Football_Association" title="The Football Association">The Football Association</a> and president of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Football_League" title="The Football League">The Football League</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur_Drewry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Drewry was born in Grimsby, Lincolnshire, and educated at the Grimsby Collegiate School.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Times_Obituary_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Times_Obituary-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1911 Drewry joined the Lincolnshire Yeomanry and served in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_World_War" title="First World War">First World War</a> with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1/1st_Lincolnshire_Yeomanry" title="1/1st Lincolnshire Yeomanry">1/1st Lincolnshire Yeomanry</a> in Palestine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Times_Obituary_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Times_Obituary-1">[1]</a></sup> Drewry reached the rank of <a href="/wiki/Quartermaster_sergeant" title="Quartermaster sergeant">quartermaster sergeant</a> with his squadron in the Yeomanry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Times_Obituary_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Times_Obituary-1">[1]</a></sup> After the war, in 1919, Drewry married the daughter of a Grimsby fish merchant, and Drewry would run his father-in-law's business until his retirement in 1953.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tomlinson2005_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tomlinson2005-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Times_Obituary_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Times_Obituary-1">[1]</a></sup> In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> Drewry served as North Lincolnshire's head warden and chief fire guard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Times_Obituary_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Times_Obituary-1">[1]</a></sup> Drewry held several civic roles in his native Grimsby, serving both as a borough councilor and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Justice_of_the_Peace" title="Justice of the Peace">Justice of the Peace</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Times_Obituary_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-Times_Obituary-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="English_football_administration">English football administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur_Drewry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: English football administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Drewry's career in football administration began with his serving as a director of <a href="/wiki/Grimsby_Town_F.C." title="Grimsby Town F.C.">Grimsby Town</a>, the chairman of the club was Drewry's father-in-law, and Drewry himself would also become chairman of the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tomlinson2005_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tomlinson2005-2">[2]</a></sup> Drewry later served as President of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League" title="Football League">Football League</a> from 1949 to 1955.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Times_Obituary_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-Times_Obituary-1">[1]</a></sup> Drewry served on the Football Association's International Selection Committee from 1944, and after his presidency of the Football League served as chairman of <a href="/wiki/The_Football_Association" title="The Football Association">The Football Association</a> (FA) from 1955 to 1961.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tomlinson2005_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Tomlinson2005-2">[2]</a></sup> Drewry succeeded <a href="/wiki/Amos_Brook_Hirst" title="Amos Brook Hirst">Amos Brook Hirst</a> as chairman of the FA; Hirst had resigned due to ill health.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> As chair of the FA Drewry once proposed awarding a point to league teams for every goal scored to encourage offensive football.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Drewry's proposals were rejected by the teams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Drewry played a significant role in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_v_England_(1950_FIFA_World_Cup)" title="United States v England (1950 FIFA World Cup)">1950 game between England and the United States</a> in which the English lost 1–0 to the rank outsiders in the <a href="/wiki/1950_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1950 FIFA World Cup">1950 FIFA World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/Belo_Horizonte" title="Belo Horizonte">Belo Horizonte</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GuardMar03_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-GuardMar03-6">[6]</a></sup> The English national coach, <a href="/wiki/Walter_Winterbottom" title="Walter Winterbottom">Walter Winterbottom</a>, had wanted to rest some players for the next game against Spain, but was overruled by Drewry, acting as sole selector, who chose an unchanged team from their last game, a 2–0 win against Chile.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GuardMar03_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-GuardMar03-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Rous" title="Stanley Rous">Stanley Rous</a>, Drewry's secretary at the FA, visited him and urged him to include <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Matthews" title="Stanley Matthews">Stanley Matthews</a> and make other changes to the team, but Drewry was adamant in his desire to keep an unchanged team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morse2013_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Morse2013-7">[7]</a></sup> After the loss against the United States, Drewry acquiesced to four changes to the team, but England lost their next game to Spain 1–0, and as a result were eliminated from the tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morse2013_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Morse2013-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vincent2007_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vincent2007-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1953_Coronation_Honours" title="1953 Coronation Honours">1953 Coronation Honours</a>, Drewry appointed a <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Order of the British Empire">Commander of Order of the British Empire (CBE)</a>.</p>
<p>Following the <a href="/wiki/Munich_air_disaster" title="Munich air disaster">Munich air disaster</a> on 6 February 1958 which claimed the lives of a number of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchester_United" title="Manchester United">Manchester United</a> staff and personnel, Drewry was appointed chairman in March of the fundraising committee for dependents of those involved in the disaster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The funds raised had reached <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>52,000 (£<a href="/wiki/NaN" title="NaN">NaN</a> as of 2017) by the time of their disbursement in October 1958.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inflation-UK_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-UK-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In his capacity as chair of the FA, Drewry was one of six football officials sued by five <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a> players who were suspended in 1957 for refusing to answer questions about possible illegal payments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Rous" title="Stanley Rous">Stanley Rous</a> served as Drewry's secretary at the FA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tomlinson2014_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tomlinson2014-13">[13]</a></sup> Drewry and Rous traveled to Switzerland in 1945, to the headquarters of FIFA in order to successfully negotiate for the re-admittance of the British <a href="/wiki/Home_Nations" title="Home Nations">Home Nations</a> to FIFA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tomlinson2005_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Tomlinson2005-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="President_of_FIFA">President of FIFA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur_Drewry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: President of FIFA">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Drewry had been appointed the vice-president of FIFA by <a href="/wiki/Jules_Rimet" title="Jules Rimet">Jules Rimet</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tomlinson2005_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-Tomlinson2005-2">[2]</a></sup> and served as the interim president for the six months following the death of Rimet's presidential successor, Belgian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rodolphe_William_Seeldrayers" title="Rodolphe William Seeldrayers">Rodolphe William Seeldrayers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FIFA_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-FIFA-14">[14]</a></sup> At the 30th <a href="/wiki/FIFA_Congress" title="FIFA Congress">FIFA Congress</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>, Portugal, Drewry defeated France's M. Larfarge by 38 votes to 16 for the presidency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tomlinson2014_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tomlinson2014-13">[13]</a></sup> Drewry oversaw the <a href="/wiki/1958_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1958 FIFA World Cup">1958 FIFA World Cup</a> during his term in office, and ultimately served as president for five years before his death from a year-long illness in 1961.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tomlinson2014_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-Tomlinson2014-13">[13]</a></sup> Drewry was the third FIFA president to die in office.</p>
