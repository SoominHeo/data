<p><b>Arthur Tudor</b> (20 September 1486Â â 2 April 1502) was <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Wales" title="Prince of Wales">Prince of Wales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Chester" title="Earl of Chester">Earl of Chester</a> and <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Cornwall" title="Duke of Cornwall">Duke of Cornwall</a>. As the eldest son and <a href="/wiki/Heir_apparent" title="Heir apparent">heir apparent</a> of <a href="/wiki/Henry_VII_of_England" title="Henry VII of England">Henry VII of England</a>, Arthur was viewed by contemporaries as the great hope of the newly established <a href="/wiki/House_of_Tudor" title="House of Tudor">House of Tudor</a>. His mother, <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_of_York" title="Elizabeth of York">Elizabeth of York</a>, was the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Edward_IV_of_England" title="Edward IV of England">Edward IV</a>, and his birth cemented the union between the House of Tudor and the <a href="/wiki/House_of_York" title="House of York">House of York</a>.</p>
<p>Plans for Arthur's marriage began before his third birthday; he was installed as Prince of Wales two years later. He grew especially close to his siblings <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Tudor" title="Margaret Tudor">Margaret</a> and <a href="/wiki/Henry_VIII_of_England" title="Henry VIII of England">Henry, Duke of York</a>, with the latter of whom he shared some tutors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir2008b4.E2.80.935_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir2008b4.E2.80.935-1">[1]</a></sup> At the age of eleven, Arthur was formally betrothed to <a href="/wiki/Catherine_of_Aragon" title="Catherine of Aragon">Catherine of Aragon</a>, a daughter of the powerful <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Monarchs" title="Catholic Monarchs">Catholic Monarchs</a> in Spain, in an effort to forge an Anglo-Spanish alliance against France. Arthur was well educated and, contrary to some modern belief, was in good health for the majority of his life. Soon after his marriage to Catherine in 1501, the couple took up residence at <a href="/wiki/Ludlow_Castle" title="Ludlow Castle">Ludlow Castle</a> in <a href="/wiki/Shropshire" title="Shropshire">Shropshire</a>, where Arthur died six months later of an unknown ailment. Catherine would later firmly state that the marriage had not been <a href="/wiki/Consummation" title="Consummation">consummated</a>.</p>
<p>One year after Arthur's death, Henry VII renewed his efforts of sealing a marital alliance with Spain by arranging for Catherine to marry Arthur's brother <a href="/wiki/Henry_VIII_of_England" title="Henry VIII of England">Henry</a>, who had by then become Prince of Wales. Arthur's untimely death paved the way for Henry's accession as Henry VIII in 1509. The potential for a question as to the consummation of Arthur and Catherine's marriage, was much later (and in a completely different political context) exploited by Henry VIII and his Court to cast doubt on the validity of Catherine's union with Henry, eventually leading to the separation between the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic Church</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Infancy">Infancy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur,_Prince_of_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Infancy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Family-of-Henry-VII.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4900" data-file-width="3574" height="233" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Family-of-Henry-VII.jpg/170px-Family-of-Henry-VII.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Family-of-Henry-VII.jpg/255px-Family-of-Henry-VII.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Family-of-Henry-VII.jpg/340px-Family-of-Henry-VII.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>

The family of Henry VII, depicted on an illuminated page.</div>

<p>In 1485, <a href="/wiki/Henry_VII_of_England" title="Henry VII of England">Henry Tudor</a> became King of England upon defeating <a href="/wiki/Richard_III_of_England" title="Richard III of England">Richard III</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Bosworth" title="Battle of Bosworth">Battle of Bosworth</a>. In an effort to strengthen the Tudor claim to the throne and emphasise his family's Welsh, that is to say Romano-British, ancestry, Henry had royal genealogists trace his lineage back to the ancient British rulers and decided on naming his firstborn son after the legendary <a href="/wiki/King_Arthur" title="King Arthur">King Arthur</a>. On this occasion, <a href="/wiki/Camelot" title="Camelot">Camelot</a> was identified as present-day <a href="/wiki/Winchester" title="Winchester">Winchester</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWagnerSchmid20111104_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWagnerSchmid20111104-2">[2]</a></sup> and his wife, <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_of_York" title="Elizabeth of York">Elizabeth of York</a>, was sent to Saint Swithun's Priory (today Winchester Cathedral Priory) in order to give birth there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheelerKindrickSalda2000377_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheelerKindrickSalda2000377-3">[3]</a></sup> Born at <a href="/wiki/Winchester_Cathedral_Priory" title="Winchester Cathedral Priory">Saint Swithun's Priory</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-odnb_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-4">[4]</a></sup> on 20 September 1486 at about 1 am,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton20091_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton20091-5">[5]</a></sup> Arthur was Henry and Elizabeth's eldest child.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir2008a151_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir2008a151-6">[6]</a></sup> Arthur's birth was anticipated by French and Italian <a href="/wiki/Renaissance_humanism" title="Renaissance humanism">humanists</a> eager for the start of a "Virgilian golden age".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton20091_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton20091-5">[5]</a></sup> Sir <a href="/wiki/Francis_Bacon" title="Francis Bacon">Francis Bacon</a> wrote that although the Prince was born one month premature, he was "strong and able".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFuller18406_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFuller18406-7">[7]</a></sup> Young Arthur was viewed as "a living symbol" of not only the union between the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Tudor" title="House of Tudor">House of Tudor</a> and the <a href="/wiki/House_of_York" title="House of York">House of York</a>, but also of the end of the <a href="/wiki/Wars_of_the_Roses" title="Wars of the Roses">Wars of the Roses</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWagnerSchmid20111104_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWagnerSchmid20111104-2">[2]</a></sup> In the opinion of contemporaries, Arthur was the great hope of the newly established House of Tudor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton20091_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton20091-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Arthur became <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Cornwall" title="Duke of Cornwall">Duke of Cornwall</a> at birth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir2008a151_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir2008a151-6">[6]</a></sup> Four days after his birth, the baby was baptised at <a href="/wiki/Winchester_Cathedral" title="Winchester Cathedral">Winchester Cathedral</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Worcester" title="Bishop of Worcester">Bishop of Worcester</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Alcock_(bishop)" title="John Alcock (bishop)">John Alcock</a>, and his baptism was immediately followed by his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Confirmation_(Catholic_Church)" title="Confirmation (Catholic Church)">Confirmation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheelerKindrickSalda2000377_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheelerKindrickSalda2000377-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/John_de_Vere,_13th_Earl_of_Oxford" title="John de Vere, 13th Earl of Oxford">John de Vere, 13th Earl of Oxford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Stanley,_1st_Earl_of_Derby" title="Thomas Stanley, 1st Earl of Derby">Thomas Stanley, 1st Earl of Derby</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_FitzAlan,_16th_Earl_of_Arundel" title="William FitzAlan, 16th Earl of Arundel">William FitzAlan, 16th Earl of Arundel</a>, Queen <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Woodville" title="Elizabeth Woodville">Elizabeth Woodville</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cecily_of_York" title="Cecily of York">Cecily of York</a> served as godparents; the latter two carried the prince during the ceremony.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGrose1784193.E2.80.9397_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGrose1784193.E2.80.9397-8">[8]</a></sup> Initially, Arthur's nursery in <a href="/wiki/Farnham" title="Farnham">Farnham</a> was headed by Elizabeth Darcy, who had served as chief nurse for <a href="/wiki/Edward_IV_of_England" title="Edward IV of England">Edward IV</a>'s children, including Arthur's own mother. After Arthur was created Prince of Wales in 1490, he was awarded a household structure at the behest of his father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-odnb_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-4">[4]</a></sup> Over the next thirteen years, Henry VII and Elizabeth would have six more children, of whom only three â <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Tudor" title="Margaret Tudor">Margaret</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henry_VIII_of_England" title="Henry VIII of England">Henry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mary_Tudor,_Queen_of_France" title="Mary Tudor, Queen of France">Mary</a> â would reach adulthood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECrofton2006129_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECrofton2006129-9">[9]</a></sup> Arthur was especially close to his sister Margaret (b. 1489) and his brother Henry (b. 1491), with whom he shared a nursery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMarshall200385_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMarshall200385-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 29 November 1489, after being made a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Order_of_Bath" title="Order of Bath">Knight of the Bath</a>, Arthur was appointed <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Wales" title="Prince of Wales">Prince of Wales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Chester" title="Earl of Chester">Earl of Chester</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200910_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200910-11">[11]</a></sup> and was invested as such at the <a href="/wiki/Palace_of_Westminster" title="Palace of Westminster">Palace of Westminster</a> on 27 February 1490.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEAllisonRiddell1991605_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAllisonRiddell1991605-12">[12]</a></sup> As part of his investiture ceremony, he progressed down the <a href="/wiki/River_Thames" title="River Thames">River Thames</a> in the royal barge and was met at <a href="/wiki/Chelsea,_London" title="Chelsea, London">Chelsea</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Lord_Mayor_of_London" title="Lord Mayor of London">Lord Mayor of London</a>, John Mathewe, and at <a href="/wiki/Lambeth" title="Lambeth">Lambeth</a> by Spanish ambassadors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton20092.E2.80.933_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton20092.E2.80.933-13">[13]</a></sup> On 8 May 1491, he was made a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knight_of_the_Garter" title="Knight of the Garter">Knight of the Garter</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir2008a151_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir2008a151-6">[6]</a></sup> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_George%27s_Chapel" title="Saint George's Chapel">Saint George's Chapel</a> at <a href="/wiki/Windsor_Castle" title="Windsor Castle">Windsor Palace</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton20093_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton20093-14">[14]</a></sup> It was around this time that Arthur began his formal education under <a href="/wiki/John_Rede" title="John Rede">John Rede</a>, a former headmaster of <a href="/wiki/Winchester_College" title="Winchester College">Winchester College</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-odnb_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-4">[4]</a></sup> His education was subsequently taken over by <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Andr%C3%A9" title="Bernard AndrÃ©">Bernard AndrÃ©</a>, a blind poet,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir2008b5_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir2008b5-15">[15]</a></sup> and then by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Linacre" title="Thomas Linacre">Thomas Linacre</a>, formerly Henry VII's physician.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir2008b150_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir2008b150-16">[16]</a></sup> Arthur's education covered <a href="/wiki/Grammar" title="Grammar">grammar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poetry" title="Poetry">poetry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhetoric" title="Rhetoric">rhetoric</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ethics" title="Ethics">ethics</a> and focused on <a href="/wiki/History" title="History">history</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEIves20072_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEIves20072-17">[17]</a></sup> Arthur was a very skilled pupil and AndrÃ© wrote that the Prince of Wales had either memorised or read a selection of <a href="/wiki/Homer" title="Homer">Homer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virgil" title="Virgil">Virgil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ovid" title="Ovid">Ovid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Terence" title="Terence">Terence</a>, a good deal of <a href="/wiki/Cicero" title="Cicero">Cicero</a> and a wide span of historical works, including those of <a href="/wiki/Thucydides" title="Thucydides">Thucydides</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caesar" title="Caesar">Caesar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Livy" title="Livy">Livy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tacitus" title="Tacitus">Tacitus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEScarisbrick19685_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEScarisbrick19685-18">[18]</a></sup> Arthur was also a "superb archer",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir2008b113_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir2008b113-19">[19]</a></sup> and had learned to dance "right pleasant and honourably" by 1501.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton20099_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton20099-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Childhood">Childhood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur,_Prince_of_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Childhood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Anglo-Flemish_School,_Arthur,_Prince_of_Wales_(Granard_portrait)_-004.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="387" height="264" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Anglo-Flemish_School%2C_Arthur%2C_Prince_of_Wales_%28Granard_portrait%29_-004.jpg/170px-Anglo-Flemish_School%2C_Arthur%2C_Prince_of_Wales_%28Granard_portrait%29_-004.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Anglo-Flemish_School%2C_Arthur%2C_Prince_of_Wales_%28Granard_portrait%29_-004.jpg/255px-Anglo-Flemish_School%2C_Arthur%2C_Prince_of_Wales_%28Granard_portrait%29_-004.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Anglo-Flemish_School%2C_Arthur%2C_Prince_of_Wales_%28Granard_portrait%29_-004.jpg/340px-Anglo-Flemish_School%2C_Arthur%2C_Prince_of_Wales_%28Granard_portrait%29_-004.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

Prince Arthur, c. 1500. This is likely the earliest surviving portrait of Arthur.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMould1995121_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMould1995121-21">[21]</a></sup></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The popular belief that Arthur was sickly during his lifetime stems from a Victorian misunderstanding of a letter from 1502;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200939_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200939-22">[22]</a></sup> on the contrary, there are no reports of Arthur being ill during his lifetime.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJones200923_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJones200923-23">[23]</a></sup> Arthur grew up to be unusually tall for his age,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200939_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200939-22">[22]</a></sup> and was considered handsome by the Spanish court:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200946_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200946-24">[24]</a></sup> he had reddish hair, small eyes, a high-bridged nose and resembled his brother Henry,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir200730_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir200730-25">[25]</a></sup> who was said to be "extremely handsome" by contemporaries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEScarisbrick196813_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEScarisbrick196813-26">[26]</a></sup> As described by historians Steven Gunn and Linda Monckton, Arthur had an "amiable and gentle" personality and was, overall, a "delicate lad".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200948_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200948-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 1490 Arthur was created <a href="/wiki/Lord_Warden_of_the_Marches" title="Lord Warden of the Marches">warden of all the marches</a> towards <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Scotland" title="Kingdom of Scotland">Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Howard,_2nd_Duke_of_Norfolk" title="Thomas Howard, 2nd Duke of Norfolk">the Earl of Surrey</a> was appointed as the Prince's <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/deputy#Noun" title="wiktionary:deputy">deputy</a>. From 1491, Arthur was named on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peace_Commission" title="Peace Commission">peace commissions</a>. In October 1492, when his father travelled to France, he was named <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regency" title="Regency">Keeper of England</a> and King's Lieutenant. Following the example of Edward IV, Henry VII set up the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Wales_and_the_Marches" title="Council of Wales and the Marches">Council of Wales and the Marches</a> for Arthur in <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, in order to enforce royal authority there. Although the council had already been set up in 1490, it was headed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jasper_Tudor" title="Jasper Tudor">Jasper Tudor, Duke of Bedford</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-odnb_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-4">[4]</a></sup> Arthur was first dispatched to Wales in 1501, at the age of fifteen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEScarisbrick19686_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEScarisbrick19686-28">[28]</a></sup> In March 1493, Arthur was granted the power to appoint justices of <a href="/wiki/Oyer_and_terminer" title="Oyer and terminer">oyer and terminer</a> and inquire into <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/franchise#Noun" title="wiktionary:franchise">franchises</a>, thus strengthening the council's authority. In November of that year, the Prince also received an extensive land grant in Wales, including the <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_March" title="Earl of March">County of March</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-odnb_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Arthur was served by sons of English, Irish and Welsh nobility, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gearoid_%C3%93g_FitzGerald,_9th_Earl_of_Kildare" title="Gearoid Ãg FitzGerald, 9th Earl of Kildare">Gearoid Ãg FitzGerald, 9th Earl of Kildare</a>, who had been brought to the English court as a consequence of the involvement of his father, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gerald_Fitzgerald,_8th_Earl_of_Kildare" title="Gerald Fitzgerald, 8th Earl of Kildare">Gerald Fitzgerald, 8th Earl of Kildare</a>, in the crowning of pretender <a href="/wiki/Lambert_Simnel" title="Lambert Simnel">Lambert Simnel</a> in Ireland during Henry VII's reign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200916_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200916-29">[29]</a></sup> Other servants were Anthony Willoughby, a son of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Willoughby,_1st_Baron_Willoughby_de_Broke" title="Robert Willoughby, 1st Baron Willoughby de Broke">Robert Willoughby, 1st Baron Willoughby de Broke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Radcliffe,_1st_Earl_of_Sussex" title="Robert Radcliffe, 1st Earl of Sussex">Robert Radcliffe</a>, the heir of the 9th Baron FitzWalter and Maurice St John, a favourite nephew of Arthur's grandmother <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lady_Margaret_Beaufort" title="Lady Margaret Beaufort">Lady Margaret Beaufort</a>. He was brought up with <a href="/wiki/Gruffydd_ap_Rhys_ap_Thomas" title="Gruffydd ap Rhys ap Thomas">Gruffydd ap Rhys ap Thomas</a>, the son of powerful Welsh nobleman Thomas ap Rhys,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200915_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200915-30">[30]</a></sup> Gruffydd grow quite close to Arthur<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200951_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200951-31">[31]</a></sup> and was buried in Worcester Cathedral upon his death in 1521, alongside the Prince's tomb.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200994_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200994-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage">Marriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur,_Prince_of_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Marriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ArthurCatherine.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="640" data-file-width="632" height="223" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/ArthurCatherine.jpg/220px-ArthurCatherine.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/ArthurCatherine.jpg/330px-ArthurCatherine.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/ArthurCatherine.jpg/440px-ArthurCatherine.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ArthurCatherine.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A Flemish tapestry depicting Arthur and Catherine's court.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Henry VII planned to marry Arthur to a daughter of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Monarchs" title="Catholic Monarchs">Catholic Monarchs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isabella_I_of_Castile" title="Isabella I of Castile">Isabella I of Castile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_II_of_Aragon" title="Ferdinand II of Aragon">Ferdinand II of Aragon</a>, in order to forge an Anglo-Spanish alliance against France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKidner2012380_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKidner2012380-33">[33]</a></sup> It was suggested that the choice of marrying Arthur to Ferdinand and Isabella's youngest daughter, <a href="/wiki/Catherine_of_Aragon" title="Catherine of Aragon">Catherine</a> (b. 1485), would be appropriate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-odnb_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-4">[4]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Medina_del_Campo_(1489)" title="Treaty of Medina del Campo (1489)">Treaty of Medina del Campo</a> (27 March 1489) provided that Arthur and Catherine would be married as soon as they reached <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canonical_age" title="Canonical age">canonical age</a>; it also settled Catherine's dowry at 200,000 crowns (the equivalent of <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">Â£</a>5 million in 2007).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir200717_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir200717-34">[34]</a></sup> Since Arthur, not yet 14, was below the age of consent, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Papal_dispensation" title="Papal dispensation">papal dispensation</a> (i.e., waiver) allowing the marriage was issued in February 1497, and the pair were betrothed by proxy on 25 August 1497.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFraser199224_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFraser199224-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheelerKindrickSalda2000377_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheelerKindrickSalda2000377-3">[3]</a></sup> Two years later, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marriage_by_proxy" title="Marriage by proxy">marriage by proxy</a> took place at Arthur's Tickenhill Manor in <a href="/wiki/Bewdley" title="Bewdley">Bewdley</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Worcester" title="Worcester">Worcester</a>; Arthur said to Roderigo de Puebla, who had acted as proxy for Catherine, that "he much rejoiced to contract the marriage because of his deep and sincere love for the Princess".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir200723_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir200723-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a letter from October 1499, Arthur, referring to Catherine as "my dearest spouse", had written:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"I cannot tell you what an earnest desire I feel to see your Highness, and how vexatious to me is this procrastination about your coming. Let [it] be hastened, [that] the love conceived between us and the wished-for joys may reap their proper fruit."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir200723_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir200723-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>The young couple exchanged letters in <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> until 20 September 1501, when Arthur, having attained the age of 15, was deemed old enough to be married.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESandersLow1910235_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESandersLow1910235-37">[37]</a></sup> Catherine landed in England about two weeks later, on 2 October 1501, at <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-odnb_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-4">[4]</a></sup> The next month, on 4 November 1501, the couple met each other for the first time at <a href="/wiki/Dogmersfield" title="Dogmersfield">Dogmersfield</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hampshire" title="Hampshire">Hampshire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir200727_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir200727-38">[38]</a></sup> Arthur wrote to Catherine's parents that he would be "a true and loving husband"; the couple soon discovered that they had mastered different pronunciations of Latin and so were unable to communicate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFraser199225_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFraser199225-39">[39]</a></sup> Five days later, on 9 November 1501, Catherine arrived in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir200730_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir200730-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 14 November 1501, the marriage ceremony finally took place at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_St._Paul%27s_Cathedral" title="Old St. Paul's Cathedral">Saint Paul's Cathedral</a>; both Arthur and Catherine wore white satin. The ceremony was conducted by <a href="/wiki/Henry_Deane" title="Henry Deane">Henry Deane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Canterbury" title="Archbishop of Canterbury">Archbishop of Canterbury</a>, who was assisted by <a href="/wiki/William_Warham" title="William Warham">William Warham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_London" title="Bishop of London">Bishop of London</a>. Following the ceremony, Arthur and Catherine left the Cathedral and headed for <a href="/wiki/Baynard%27s_Castle" title="Baynard's Castle">Baynard's Castle</a>, where they were entertained by "the best voiced children of the King's chapel, who sang right sweetly with quaint harmony".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir200733_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir200733-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>What followed was a ceremony laid down by Arthur's grandmother <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lady_Margaret_Beaufort" title="Lady Margaret Beaufort">Lady Margaret Beaufort</a>: the bed was sprinkled with holy water, after which Catherine was led away from the wedding feast by her ladies-in-waiting. She was undressed, veiled and "reverently" laid in bed, while Arthur, "in his shirt, with a gown cast about him", was escorted by his gentlemen into the bedchamber, while <a href="/wiki/Viol" title="Viol">viols</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tabor_(instrument)" title="Tabor (instrument)">tabors</a> played. The Bishop of London blessed the bed and prayed for the marriage to be fruitful, after which the couple were left alone. This is the only public bedding of a royal couple recorded in Britain in the 16th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir2008b11_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir2008b11-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur,_Prince_of_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Prince_Arthur_transept_window.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4736" data-file-width="2926" height="356" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Prince_Arthur_transept_window.jpg/220px-Prince_Arthur_transept_window.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Prince_Arthur_transept_window.jpg/330px-Prince_Arthur_transept_window.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Prince_Arthur_transept_window.jpg/440px-Prince_Arthur_transept_window.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Prince_Arthur_transept_window.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Prince Arthur, depicted on a stained glass window at <a href="/wiki/Great_Malvern" title="Great Malvern">Great Malvern</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>After residing at <a href="/wiki/Tickenhill_Palace" title="Tickenhill Palace">Tickenhill Manor</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir2008b35_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir2008b35-42">[42]</a></sup> for a month, Arthur and Catherine headed for the marches in Wales, where they established their household at <a href="/wiki/Ludlow_Castle" title="Ludlow Castle">Ludlow Castle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEO.27Day20121554_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEO.27Day20121554-43">[43]</a></sup> Arthur had been growing weaker since his wedding,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir200735_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir200735-44">[44]</a></sup> and although Catherine was reluctant to follow him, she was ordered by Henry VII to join her husband.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir200736_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir200736-45">[45]</a></sup> Arthur found it easy to govern Wales, as the border had become quiet after many centuries of warfare. In March 1502, Arthur and Catherine were afflicted by an unknown illness, "a malign vapour which proceeded from the air".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir200737_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir200737-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[note 1]</a></sup> While Catherine recovered, Arthur died on 2 April 1502 at Ludlow, six months short of his sixteenth birthday.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEIves20071_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEIves20071-53">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>News of Arthur's death reached Henry VII's court late on 4 April.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-odnb_4-7"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-4">[4]</a></sup> The King was awoken from his sleep by his confessor, who quoted <a href="/wiki/Job_(biblical_figure)" title="Job (biblical figure)">Job</a> by asking Henry "If we receive good things at the hands of God, why may we not endure evil things?" He then told the king that "[his] dearest son hath departed to God", and Henry burst into tears.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir200737.E2.80.9338_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir200737.E2.80.9338-54">[53]</a></sup> "Grief-stricken and emotional", he then had his wife brought into his chambers, so that they might "take the painful news together";<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERichardson197019_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERichardson197019-55">[54]</a></sup> Elizabeth reminded Henry that God had helped him become king and "had ever preserved him", adding that they had been left with "yet a fair Prince and two fair princesses and that God is where he was, and [they were] both young enough".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECrawford2007166_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECrawford2007166-56">[55]</a></sup> Soon after leaving Henry's bedchamber, Elizabeth collapsed and began to cry, while the ladies sent for the King, who hurriedly came and "relieved her".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECrawford2007167_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECrawford2007167-57">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 8 April, a general procession took place for the salvation of Arthur's soul. That night, a <a href="/wiki/Dirge" title="Dirge">dirge</a> was sung in <a href="/wiki/St_Paul%27s_Cathedral" title="St Paul's Cathedral">St Paul's Cathedral</a> and every parish church in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200964_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200964-58">[57]</a></sup> On 23 April,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheelerKindrickSalda2000377_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheelerKindrickSalda2000377-3">[3]</a></sup> Arthur's body, which had previously been embalmed,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir200738_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir200738-59">[58]</a></sup> sprinkled with holy water and sheltered with a canopy, was carried out of Ludlow Castle and into the <a href="/wiki/St_Laurence%27s_Church,_Ludlow" title="St Laurence's Church, Ludlow">Parish Church of Ludlow</a> by various noblemen and gentlemen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200964_58-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200964-58">[57]</a></sup> On 25 April, Arthur's body was taken to <a href="/wiki/Worcester_Cathedral" title="Worcester Cathedral">Worcester Cathedral</a> via the <a href="/wiki/River_Severn" title="River Severn">River Severn</a>, in a "special wagon upholstered in black and drawn by six horses, also caparisoned in black".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200965_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200965-60">[59]</a></sup> As was customary, Catherine did not attend the funeral.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir200738_59-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir200738-59">[58]</a></sup> The Earl of Surrey acted as chief mourner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200971_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton200971-61">[60]</a></sup> At the end of the ceremony, Sir William Uvedale, Sir Richard Croft and Arthur's household ushers broke their staves of office and threw them into the Prince's grave.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHearne1774381_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHearne1774381-62">[61]</a></sup> During the funeral, Arthur's own arms were shown alongside those of <a href="/wiki/Cadwaladr_ap_Gruffydd" title="Cadwaladr ap Gruffydd">Cadwaladr ap Gruffydd</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brutus_of_Troy" title="Brutus of Troy">Brutus of Troy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-odnb_4-8"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-4">[4]</a></sup> Two years later, a <a href="/wiki/Chantry" title="Chantry">chantry</a> was erected over Arthur's grave.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheelerKindrickSalda2000377_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheelerKindrickSalda2000377-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur,_Prince_of_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Arthur,_Prince_of_Wales_(G.P._Harding).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="608" data-file-width="604" height="221" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Arthur%2C_Prince_of_Wales_%28G.P._Harding%29.jpg/220px-Arthur%2C_Prince_of_Wales_%28G.P._Harding%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Arthur%2C_Prince_of_Wales_%28G.P._Harding%29.jpg/330px-Arthur%2C_Prince_of_Wales_%28G.P._Harding%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Arthur%2C_Prince_of_Wales_%28G.P._Harding%29.jpg/440px-Arthur%2C_Prince_of_Wales_%28G.P._Harding%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/English_Reformation" title="English Reformation">English Reformation</a></div>
<p>Shortly after Arthur's death, the idea of betrothing the now-widowed Catherine to the new heir, Henry, had arisen; both the King and Isabella were keen on moving forward with the betrothal, and the pope granted a dispensation towards that end.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELoades200922_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELoades200922-63">[62]</a></sup> After originally rejecting the idea, Henry announced, after his succession on 22 April 1509, that he would marry Catherine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELoades200924_64-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELoades200924-64">[63]</a></sup> The wedding took place on 11 June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWagnerSchmid2011226_65-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWagnerSchmid2011226-65">[64]</a></sup> Catherine bore Henry six children: three sons died before reaching three months of age, a daughter was stillborn and another lived for only a week. The couple's only surviving child was <a href="/wiki/Mary_I_of_England" title="Mary I of England">Mary I</a> (b. 1516).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir2008a154_66-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir2008a154-66">[65]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1526, after eighteen years of marriage, Henry started to pursue the affections of <a href="/wiki/Anne_Boleyn" title="Anne Boleyn">Anne Boleyn</a>, the sister of his former mistress, <a href="/wiki/Mary_Boleyn" title="Mary Boleyn">Mary Boleyn</a>. At the same time, he became troubled by what became known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King%27s_%22great_matter%22" title="King's &quot;great matter&quot;">King's "great matter"</a>, that is, finding an appropriate solution for his lack of male descendants. He found several possible options. He could have attempted to legitimise his illegitimate son <a href="/wiki/Henry_FitzRoy,_1st_Duke_of_Richmond_and_Somerset" title="Henry FitzRoy, 1st Duke of Richmond and Somerset">Henry FitzRoy</a>, but this would not be easy and would require the Pope's intervention. He could have married his daughter Mary off and hoped for a grandson; this was not seen as a real option, because Mary was a sickly child and was unlikely to conceive before Henry's death. Finally, he could somehow reject Catherine and marry a bride more likely to bear children. Probably seeing the possibility of marrying Anne, the third was ultimately the most viable option to Henry,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELoades200988.E2.80.9389_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELoades200988.E2.80.9389-67">[66]</a></sup> and it soon became the King's wish to dissolve his marriage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBrigden2000114_68-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBrigden2000114-68">[67]</a></sup></p>
<p>Henry believed that his marriage was cursed and found confirmation in the Bible, in <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Bible_(King_James)/Leviticus#20:21" title="s:Bible (King James)/Leviticus">Leviticus 20:21</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMacCulloch1995139_69-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMacCulloch1995139-69">[68]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[note 2]</a></sup> Although in the morning following his wedding, Arthur had claimed that he was thirsty "<i>for I have been in the midst of Spain last night</i>" and that "<i>having a wife is a good pastime</i>", these claims are generally dismissed by modern historians as mere boasts of a boy who did not want others to know of his failure,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-odnb_4-9"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir200734_71-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir200734-71">[69]</a></sup> and Catherine maintained the claim that she had married Henry while still a virgin until the day she died.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWhitelock201014_48-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWhitelock201014-48">[48]</a></sup> After Henry's constant support of the claim that Catherine's first marriage had been consummated, an annulment was issued on 23 April 1533,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWeir2008b_72-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWeir2008b-72">[70]</a></sup> while the King had already married Anne on 25 January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWilliams1971124_73-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWilliams1971124-73">[71]</a></sup> Anne was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beheading" title="Beheading">beheaded</a> for <a href="/wiki/High_treason" title="High treason">high treason</a> in 1536, after which Henry proceeded to marry four more times. At the time of his death in 1547, Henry only had three living children; his only son, <a href="/wiki/Edward_VI_of_England" title="Edward VI of England">Edward VI</a> succeeded but died six years later. His successors were Henry's daughters by Catherine and Anne, Mary I and <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_I_of_England" title="Elizabeth I of England">Elizabeth I</a>. Upon Elizabeth's death in 1603, the male line of the House of Tudor came to an end.</p>
<p>In 2002, following the initiative of <a href="/wiki/Canon_(priest)" title="Canon (priest)">canon</a> Ian MacKenzie, Arthur's funeral was reenacted with a requiem mass at Worcester Cathedral, on occasion of the quincentenary of his death. Despite his role in English history, Arthur has remained largely forgotten since his death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton20095_74-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGunnMonckton20095-74">[72]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Popular_culture">Popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur,_Prince_of_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Arthur has been featured in several <a href="/wiki/Historical_fiction" title="Historical fiction">historical fiction</a> novels, such as <i>The King's Pleasure</i>, by <a href="/wiki/Norah_Lofts" title="Norah Lofts">Norah Lofts</a> and <i>Katherine, The Virgin Widow</i>, by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jean_Plaidy" title="Jean Plaidy">Jean Plaidy</a>. In <i><a href="/wiki/The_Constant_Princess" title="The Constant Princess">The Constant Princess</a></i>, by <a href="/wiki/Philippa_Gregory" title="Philippa Gregory">Philippa Gregory</a>, Catherine promises Arthur to marry his brother, thus fulfilling not only her own destiny of becoming Queen of England, but also the couple's plans for the future of the kingdom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[73]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Alteration" title="The Alteration">The Alteration</a></i>, by <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Amis" title="Kingsley Amis">Kingsley Amis</a>, is an <a href="/wiki/Alternate_history" title="Alternate history">alternate history</a> novel centred on the "War of the English Succession", during which Henry VIII attempts to usurp the throne of his nephew, Stephen II, Arthur and Catherine's son.</p>
<p>The historical drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Six_Wives_of_Henry_VIII_(BBC_TV_series)" title="The Six Wives of Henry VIII (BBC TV series)">The Six Wives of Henry VIII</a></i> was broadcast in 1970, with Martin Ratcliffe as "Prince Arthur".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[74]</a></sup> In 1972, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBC2" title="BBC2">BBC2</a> aired a historical <a href="/wiki/Miniseries" title="Miniseries">miniseries</a> titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Shadow_of_the_Tower" title="The Shadow of the Tower">The Shadow of the Tower</a></i>, with "Lord Arthur, Prince of Wales" played by Jason Kemp.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[75]</a></sup></p>
