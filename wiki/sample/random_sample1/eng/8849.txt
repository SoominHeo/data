<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Grey_Wolf_Legend">The Grey Wolf Legend</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The Grey Wolf Legend">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Legend tells of a young boy who survived a battle; a female wolf finds the injured child and nurses him back to health. The wolf, impregnated by the boy, escapes her enemies by crossing the Western Sea to a cave near the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qocho" title="Qocho">Qocho</a> mountains and a city of the <a href="/wiki/Tocharians" title="Tocharians">Tocharians</a>, giving birth to ten half-wolf, half-human boys. Of these, Ashina becomes their leader and establishes the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ashina_(clan)" title="Ashina (clan)">Ashina clan</a>, which ruled over the Göktürk and other <a href="/wiki/Turkic_peoples" title="Turkic peoples">Turkic</a> <a href="/wiki/Nomadic_empire" title="Nomadic empire">nomadic empires</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>These first Turks migrated to the Altai region, where they were known as expert <a href="/wiki/Blacksmith" title="Blacksmith">blacksmiths</a>, akin to the <a href="/wiki/Scythians" title="Scythians">Scythians</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_era">Modern era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Modern era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With the rise of <a href="/wiki/Turkish_nationalism" title="Turkish nationalism">Turkish</a> <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_nationalism" title="Ethnic nationalism">ethnic nationalism</a> in the 1930s, the veneration of figures of Turkic Mythology, such as Bozkurt, Asena and <a href="/wiki/Ergenekon" title="Ergenekon">Ergenekon</a> was resurgent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Arman_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Arman-9">[9]</a></sup> The symbol of Asena is embossed on the stage of the personal theater of the first President of Turkey, <a href="/wiki/Mustafa_Kemal_Atat%C3%BCrk" title="Mustafa Kemal Atatürk">Mustafa Kemal Atatürk</a>, at his residence in Ankara;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Arman_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Arman-9">[9]</a></sup> the Atatürk referenced the motif in speech, such as that of February 13, 1931,<i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=T%C3%BCrk_Oca%C4%9F%C4%B1&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Türk Ocağı (page does not exist)">Türk Ocağı</a></i>, in <a href="/wiki/Malatya" title="Malatya">Malatya</a>.'<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
