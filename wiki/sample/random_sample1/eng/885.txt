<p>"<b>Christmas Tree</b>" is a song by American singer <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a>. It was released on December 16, 2008 for <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital download</a>. It was written and produced by Gaga, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Kierszenbaum" title="Martin Kierszenbaum">Martin Kierszenbaum</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Space_Cowboy_(musician)" title="Space Cowboy (musician)">Space Cowboy</a>, who also provides guest vocals. Musically, "Christmas Tree" is a <a href="/wiki/Christmas_music" title="Christmas music">Christmas</a> song with <a href="/wiki/Dance-pop" title="Dance-pop">dance-pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Synthpop" title="Synthpop">synthpop</a> influences. It samples the classic Christmas song "<a href="/wiki/Deck_the_Halls" title="Deck the Halls">Deck the Halls</a>", as well as briefly using the lyrics "Rum pum pum pum" from the Christmas song "<a href="/wiki/The_Little_Drummer_Boy" title="The Little Drummer Boy">The Little Drummer Boy</a>" in its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intro_(music)" title="Intro (music)">intro</a>. Lyrically, the song uses sexual <a href="/wiki/Innuendo" title="Innuendo">innuendos</a>.</p>
<p>The song received mixed reviews from music critics, with some commending its creativity and individuality from other Christmas songs and others criticizing its sexual innuendos and deeming it "not for the family". Due to strong digital sells, it reached number 79 on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a> in January 2009. It also reached number 18 on the <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/RIAJ_Digital_Track_Chart" title="RIAJ Digital Track Chart">RIAJ Digital Track Chart</a> and number 23 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> Holiday/Seasonal Digital Songs chart in late 2010. In December 2009, it was available for free download on <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" title="Amazon.com">Amazon</a> as a part of their "25 Days of Free" promotion. It has been included in several Christmas <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">compilation albums</a>, including <i><a href="/wiki/Now_That%27s_What_I_Call_Christmas!_4" title="Now That's What I Call Christmas! 4">Now That's What I Call Christmas! 4</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christmas_Tree_(Lady_Gaga_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Christmas Tree" is a Christmas music-themed song,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spacecowboybio_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-spacecowboybio-2">[2]</a></sup> featuring vocals from Gaga and <a href="/wiki/Space_Cowboy_(musician)" title="Space Cowboy (musician)">Space Cowboy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spacecowboybio_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-spacecowboybio-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-urb_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-urb-3">[3]</a></sup> It was written and produced by Gaga, Space Cowboy and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Kierszenbaum" title="Martin Kierszenbaum">Martin Kierszenbaum</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bmi_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-bmi-1">[1]</a></sup> Space Cowboy, whose real name is Nick Dresti, and Gaga were introduced in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> by Martin Kierszenbaum, the head of Gaga's label, <a href="/wiki/Cherrytree_Records" title="Cherrytree Records">Cherrytree Records</a>, an imprint of <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eq_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-eq-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-musicbox_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-musicbox-5">[5]</a></sup> Kierszenbaum recommended a collaboration between them after hearing Space Cowboy's single "<a href="/wiki/My_Egyptian_Lover" title="My Egyptian Lover">My Egyptian Lover</a>",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-urb_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-urb-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eq_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-eq-4">[4]</a></sup> released in January 2007 and featuring vocalist <a href="/wiki/Nadia_Oh" title="Nadia Oh">Nadia Oh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbclover_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbclover-6">[6]</a></sup> Kierszenbaum contacted Space Cowboy during December 2007<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-urb_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-urb-3">[3]</a></sup> and Space Cowboy first spoke to Gaga by phone on New Year's Eve, "[getting] along so well right away"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-andpop_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-andpop-7">[7]</a></sup> talking about sequins, disco balls, <a href="/wiki/Prince_(musician)" title="Prince (musician)">Prince</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a> and body paint.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spacecowboybio_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-spacecowboybio-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-andpop_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-andpop-7">[7]</a></sup> The pair worked together in Los Angeles to create "Christmas Tree" and "Starstruck", a song from some editions of Lady Gaga's then-current album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fame" title="The Fame">The Fame</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-urb_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-urb-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-andpop_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-andpop-7">[7]</a></sup> Space Cowboy commented of working with Lady Gaga:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>We figured out that we shared pretty much the same experiences; we’d been doing similar things on opposite sides of the Atlantic. Then I got invited to the studio to write some songs with Lady Gaga; we did “Starstruck”, we did a song called “Christmas Tree” She’s super-creative, she’s amazing — the best writer I’d ever seen, and best performer.</p>

<p>"Christmas Tree" is a version of the traditional Christmas song "<a href="/wiki/Deck_the_Halls" title="Deck the Halls">Deck the Halls</a>", with the same melody but with lyrics changed to be sexually suggestive,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyu_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyu-8">[8]</a></sup> with many sexual <a href="/wiki/Innuendo" title="Innuendo">innuendos</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metaphors" title="Metaphors">metaphors</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eye_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-eye-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-post_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-post-10">[10]</a></sup> Lyrically, the song is "lewdly celebratory" with lines such as "Light me up put me on top/ Let's fa-la-la-la-la-, la-la, la, la".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyu_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-nyu-8">[8]</a></sup> In an analysis by Stelios Phili of the <i><a href="/wiki/Washington_Square_News" title="Washington Square News">Washington Square News</a></i>, Phili jokes that the meaning of the song is closer to the original version of "Deck the Halls", a Welsh <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk</a> song called "Nos Galan" (Welsh for "New Year's Eve") traditionally sung at New Year's Eve,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-solos_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-solos-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dancebook_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-dancebook-12">[12]</a></sup> than the English Christmas version.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyu_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-nyu-8">[8]</a></sup> He cites the original first line, "Cold is the man who can't love", and says that Gaga "seeks not to warn against becoming a cold, loveless man, but to prevent that fate by way of some hot lovin'".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyu_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-nyu-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christmas_song" title="Christmas song">Christmas</a> song contains <a href="/wiki/Dance-pop" title="Dance-pop">dance-pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Synthpop" title="Synthpop">synthpop</a> music<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-georgia_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-georgia-14">[14]</a></sup> with <a href="/wiki/Synthesizer" title="Synthesizer">synthesizers</a> and a "pounding, grind-worthy beat".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyu_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-nyu-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nouse_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-nouse-15">[15]</a></sup> Space Cowboy described the song as "futuristic".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spacecowboybio_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-spacecowboybio-2">[2]</a></sup> Referenced in the song lyrics is producer Kierszenbaum's nickname of "Cherry Cherry Boom Boom".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eq_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-eq-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-musicbox_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-musicbox-5">[5]</a></sup> Describing the song in episode 25 of her <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> broadcast series <i>Gaga-vision</i>, Gaga said: ""Christmas Tree" is about the spirit of celebrating the most joyous holiday and I'll tell you why: because Christmas is the holiday that most makes boys and girls feel randy."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gagavision25_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-gagavision25-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-popdirt_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-popdirt-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christmas_Tree_(Lady_Gaga_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Christmas Tree" was released as a <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital download</a>-only single on December 16, 2008 on <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> while Gaga's first single "<a href="/wiki/Just_Dance_(song)" title="Just Dance (song)">Just Dance</a>", was charting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gaga_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-gaga-18">[18]</a></sup> It was made available as an <a href="/wiki/MP3" title="MP3">MP3</a> from <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" title="Amazon.com">Amazon.com</a> and <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a>. The song was not included in Gaga's album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fame" title="The Fame">The Fame</a></i>. One year later, in December 2009, it was one of the songs free to download from Amazon.com as part of its "25 Days of Free" offer, whereby for the first twenty-five days in December a Christmas-themed song is made free to download from the website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> "Christmas Tree" was made available on December 6.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-offbeat_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-offbeat-20">[20]</a></sup> The song was included on several <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">compilation albums</a>: Canadian compilation album <i>NOW! Christmas 4</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eye_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-eye-9">[9]</a></sup> Taiwanese compilation album <i>Christmas 101</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taiwan_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-taiwan-21">[21]</a></sup> and the seasonal compilation album <i>It's Christmas Time</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chester_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-chester-23">[23]</a></sup> all released for Christmas 2009. It was also included in the compilation albums <i>Merry Xmas!</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Now_That%27s_What_I_Call_Christmas!_4" title="Now That's What I Call Christmas! 4">Now That's What I Call Christmas! 4</a></i>, part of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Now_That%27s_What_I_Call_Music" title="Now That's What I Call Music">Now That's What I Call Music</a></i> series of compilation albums,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> both released for Christmas 2010. "Christmas Tree" was also included on <i>The Singles</i>, a box set of CD singles released exclusively in Japan in December 2010. It is on the ninth and last CD, which also includes the three live tracks from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cherrytree_Sessions_(Lady_Gaga_EP)" title="The Cherrytree Sessions (Lady Gaga EP)">The Cherrytree Sessions</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-japansingles_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-japansingles-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christmas_Tree_(Lady_Gaga_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Christmas Tree" received mixed reviews from critics. Alex Rawls of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OffBeat_(magazine)" title="OffBeat (magazine)">OffBeat</a></i> gave a positive review as part of the "25 Days of Free" offer from Amazon.com. Joking that the artists of the more religious songs must be "praying" for Lady Gaga, Rawls described the song as "not so po-faced" as the five songs released before it was made available on December 6, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-offbeat_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-offbeat-20">[20]</a></sup> Estrella Adeyeri, the music editor of <i><a href="/wiki/Nouse" title="Nouse">Nouse</a></i>, gave the song a positive review, complimenting its "synth beats and numerous yuletide innuendos".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nouse_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-nouse-15">[15]</a></sup> Ryan Brockington of the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Post" title="New York Post">New York Post</a></i> praised the song, describing it as a "favorite holiday jam" and a "subliminal <a href="/wiki/Vagina" title="Vagina">lady parts</a> pine-tree jingle".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-post_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-post-10">[10]</a></sup> A reviewer from <a href="/wiki/Gay.com" title="Gay.com">gay.com</a> described the song as "merry" and a "naughty choice", praising its "dark decadence".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-planet_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-planet-27">[27]</a></sup> Gino dela Paz of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Philippine_Star" title="The Philippine Star">The Philippine Star</a></i> described the song as going from "zero to crazy"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-philippine_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-philippine-28">[28]</a></sup> and Diana Nabiruma of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Weekly_Observer" title="The Weekly Observer">The Weekly Observer</a></i> called the song "plain naughty and wacky".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-observer_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-observer-29">[29]</a></sup> Jason Lewis for <i><a href="/wiki/Fast_Forward_Weekly" title="Fast Forward Weekly">Fast Forward Weekly</a></i> called the song "charmingly ludicrous" and called it among "the best “new” holiday music out there".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ffweekly_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-ffweekly-30">[30]</a></sup> Chad Bullock of <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> included "Christmas Tree" in a list of "excellent" new Christmas music, describing it as "non-traditional and vaguely sexual".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvlist_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtvlist-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Molly Gamble of <i><a href="/wiki/Marquette_Tribune" title="Marquette Tribune">Marquette Tribune</a></i> gave "Christmas Tree" a negative review, criticising the song's "poorly veiled metaphor" for sex and calling the song "shameless". She commented that "Christmas Tree" is not a song one can listen to in the presence of family and that it "makes Christmas feel dirty".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marquette_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-marquette-32">[32]</a></sup> Katie Hasty and Melinda Newman of HitFix gave the song a very negative review, calling it "stupendously stupid and awful" and the "most terrible Christmas song ever in the whole wide world ever", criticising its obvious sexual metaphors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hitfix_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-hitfix-33">[33]</a></sup> Cassaundra Baber of <i><a href="/wiki/Observer-Dispatch" title="Observer-Dispatch">Observer-Dispatch</a></i> listed the song on list of "Horrible holiday hits", criticising it for being "sexualized" and calling it "not for the kids".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baber_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-baber-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>The song was described as "<a href="/wiki/Pornography" title="Pornography">porn</a>-tastic" in a review for <i>Now! Christmas 4</i> by <i><a href="/wiki/Eye_Weekly" title="Eye Weekly">Eye Weekly</a></i> reviewer Chris Bilton, quoting the lyric "My Christmas tree is delicious". Bilton also called it one of few tracks on the album that make it a "worthy stocking stuffer".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eye_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-eye-9">[9]</a></sup> Also in a review for <i>Now! Christmas 4</i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charlottetown_Guardian" title="Charlottetown Guardian">Charlottetown Guardian</a></i> reviewer Doug Gallant called "Christmas Tree" a "dreadful offering".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-35">[35]</a></sup> Beverley Lunney, a reviewer for <i><a href="/wiki/Winnipeg_Free_Press" title="Winnipeg Free Press">Winnipeg Free Press</a></i>, gave the song a negative review, commenting that listeners would be "offended" by the song's "truly unfestive metaphors" and called it one of a "sea of unbearable original tunes" on <i>Now! Christmas 4</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-winnipeg_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-winnipeg-36">[36]</a></sup> Darryl Sterdan of the <i><a href="/wiki/Ottawa_Sun" title="Ottawa Sun">Ottawa Sun</a></i> called the song "the only reason to buy" <i>Now! Christmas 4</i> and recommended downloading it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ottawa_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-ottawa-37">[37]</a></sup> In a review of <i>Now! Christmas 4</i>, John Lucas of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Georgia_Straight" title="The Georgia Straight">The Georgia Straight</a></i> described the song as a "filthy electro banger" and "so blatantly about [sex] that you have to wonder how in the hell it got past the usually vigilant <i>Now!</i> gatekeepers", calling it the "single exception" to an otherwise bland album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-georgia_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-georgia-14">[14]</a></sup> In a review for <i>It's Christmas Time</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Chester_Chronicle" title="Chester Chronicle">Chester Chronicle</a></i> reviewer Polly Weeks called the song something that "will keep teenagers happy".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chester_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-chester-23">[23]</a></sup> In a review for <i>Now That's What I Call Christmas! 4</i>, J Matthew Cobb of <i>HiFi Magazine</i> called "Christmas Tree" part of a "good list" of new material and described it as "naughty, but nice".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christmas_Tree_(Lady_Gaga_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Due to high digital downloads, "Christmas Tree" entered the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a> chart at number seventy-nine in the first week of January 2009, remaining on the chart for one week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-canada_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-canada-39">[39]</a></sup> In November 2010, "Christmas Tree" charted on the <a href="/wiki/RIAJ_Digital_Track_Chart" title="RIAJ Digital Track Chart">RIAJ Digital Track Chart</a> of Japan, entering at position eighteen, its peak.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riaj_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-riaj-40">[40]</a></sup> The song then dropped to positions 51, 74, 81 and 100 before leaving the chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riaj2_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-riaj2-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riaj3_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-riaj3-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riaj4_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-riaj4-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riaj5_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-riaj5-44">[44]</a></sup> Also in November 2010, "Christmas Tree" charted on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">Holiday/Seasonal Digital Songs chart</a> in the US, peaking at position 23.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-holiday_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-holiday-45">[45]</a></sup> In December 2010, it was also ranked at number 23 on the "Hot 100 Holiday Christmas Songs" by <i>Billboard</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-holiday_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-holiday-45">[45]</a></sup> based on sales and airplay over the winter period as calculated by <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_SoundScan" title="Nielsen SoundScan">Nielsen SoundScan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_Broadcast_Data_Systems" title="Nielsen Broadcast Data Systems">Nielson BDS</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-holidaypageone_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-holidaypageone-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
