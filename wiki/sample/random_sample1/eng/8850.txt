<p><b>Asen Zlatev</b> (<a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a>: <span lang="bg" xml:lang="bg">Асен Златев</span>; born May 23, 1960) is a former <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgarian</a> <a href="/wiki/Olympic_weightlifting" title="Olympic weightlifting">weightlifter</a>.</p>
<p>He became Olympic champion in 1980 in the <a href="/wiki/Weightlifting_at_the_1980_Summer_Olympics" title="Weightlifting at the 1980 Summer Olympics">middleweight</a> class.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-db-olymp_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-db-olymp-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gbrathletics_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-gbrathletics-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Zlatev was originally intended to represent Bulgaria at the <a href="/wiki/Weightlifting_at_the_1988_Summer_Olympics" title="Weightlifting at the 1988 Summer Olympics">1988 Olympic Games</a> in the Light Heavyweight (82,5 kg) category.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes.com_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes.com-3">[3]</a></sup> Two of his teammates who had won gold medals in their weight classes tested positive for banned substances, and the Buglarian weightlifting federation pulled the rest of the team out of the competition the day before Zlatev was scheduled to compete.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes.com_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes.com-3">[3]</a></sup> Mitko Grablev (56 kg) &amp; Angel Guenchev (67.5 kg) were <a href="/wiki/List_of_stripped_Olympic_medals" title="List of stripped Olympic medals">both disqualified</a> after they tested positive for <a href="/wiki/Furosemide" title="Furosemide">Furosemide</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> At the time, Zlatev was a three-time World champion and five-time European champion, and a heavy favorite for the gold medal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes.com_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes.com-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
