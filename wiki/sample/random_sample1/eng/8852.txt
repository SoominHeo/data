<p><b>Ahsoka Tano</b> is a character in the <a href="/wiki/Star_Wars" title="Star Wars"><i>Star Wars</i> franchise</a>. Introduced as the <a href="/wiki/Jedi#Padawan" title="Jedi">Padawan</a> apprentice of Anakin Skywalker (the future <a href="/wiki/Darth_Vader" title="Darth Vader">Darth Vader</a>), she is a central <a href="/wiki/Protagonist" title="Protagonist">protagonist</a> of both the 2008 animated film <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars_(film)" title="Star Wars: The Clone Wars (film)">Star Wars: The Clone Wars</a></i> and the <a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars_(2008_TV_series)" title="Star Wars: The Clone Wars (2008 TV series)">subsequent TV series</a>. Ahsoka reappears in <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Rebels" title="Star Wars Rebels">Star Wars Rebels</a></i> as part of the <a href="/wiki/Rebel_Alliance" title="Rebel Alliance">Rebel Alliance</a>, and she is featured in other media such as books and video games. Ahsoka has received positive critical reaction for her development into a well-rounded, complex character who serves as an important <a href="/wiki/Foil_(literature)" title="Foil (literature)">foil</a> for Anakin Skywalker. She has also been highlighted as one of the stronger female characters in the franchise.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Creation_and_development">Creation and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahsoka_Tano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Creation and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Concept">Concept</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahsoka_Tano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Concept">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ahsoka was developed to illustrate how <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker" title="Anakin Skywalker">Anakin Skywalker</a> develops from the brash, undisciplined Padawan apprentice in <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_II_%E2%80%93_Attack_of_the_Clones" title="Star Wars: Episode II – Attack of the Clones">Star Wars: Episode II – Attack of the Clones</a></i> (2002) to the more reserved Jedi Knight in <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_%E2%80%93_Revenge_of_the_Sith" title="Star Wars: Episode III – Revenge of the Sith">Star Wars: Episode III – Revenge of the Sith</a></i> (2005).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVGuide_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-TVGuide-1">[1]</a></sup> <i>Star Wars</i> creator <a href="/wiki/George_Lucas" title="George Lucas">George Lucas</a>, who had two daughters, also wanted the character to appeal to girls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taylor_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-taylor-2">[2]</a></sup> Early in development, Ahsoka's name was "Ashla".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Clone Wars supervising director and writer <a href="/wiki/Dave_Filoni" title="Dave Filoni">Dave Filoni</a> wrote a fable about Ahsoka's early childhood to help develop the character. He imagined the discovery that she has "the right stuff" to become a Jedi would be a cause for celebration in her hometown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-untoldtales_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-untoldtales-4">[4]</a></sup> Filoni said he is protective of the Ahsoka character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Giving Anakin responsibility for a Padawan was meant to place the character in a role that forced him to become more cautious and responsible. It would also give him insight into his relationship with his own master, <a href="/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi" title="Obi-Wan Kenobi">Obi-Wan Kenobi</a>, and depict how their relationship matured. Ahsoka and Anakin's relationship was seen as an essential story arc spanning both the animated film and <i>Clone Wars</i> television series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing">Writing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahsoka_Tano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Writing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Filoni initially struggled with writing Ahsoka because he had "zero perspective" on what it was like to be a 14-year-old girl.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filonincwit_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-filonincwit-7">[7]</a></sup> He therefore shifted his focus and instead wrote Ahsoka primarily as a <a href="/wiki/Jedi" title="Jedi">Jedi</a> who just happens to be an adolescent female.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filonincwit_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-filonincwit-7">[7]</a></sup> Filoni said he "has always had a story in mind" for Ahsoka's overall development.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-swce2016_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-swce2016-8">[8]</a></sup> He began thinking about the final confrontation between Ahsoka and Vader ever since he created Ahsoka;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> different iterations had different endings,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fatesfulfilled1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-fatesfulfilled1-10">[10]</a></sup> including one in which Vader kills Ahsoka just as she slashes open his helmet to reveal Anakin's scarred face.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ashley Eckstein, who primarily voiced Ahsoka, said she and the writers were aware of audiences initially finding the character annoying, and that there was a "fine line" between Ahsoka being bratty and becoming endearing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ecksteinsw.com_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-ecksteinsw.com-12">[12]</a></sup> Because production was a year ahead of what was broadcast, with Ahsoka developing over that time, Eckstein implored fans to be patient with the character's growth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ecksteinsw.com_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-ecksteinsw.com-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although Ahsoka leaves the Jedi Order at the end of <i>The Clone Wars</i>' fifth season, the storyline initially had her return to the Order.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filoni3_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-filoni3-13">[13]</a></sup> Filoni said this would be the "normal" arc and suggested to Lucas that she instead remain expelled; Lucas agreed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filoni3_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-filoni3-13">[13]</a></sup> Lucas believed Ahsoka survived "Order 66", the command that led the Republic's clone army to murder the Jedi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fatesfulfilled1_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-fatesfulfilled1-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Fulcrum character introduced early in <i>Star Wars Rebels</i> was always conceived as being Ahsoka.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rebels-recon_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-rebels-recon-14">[14]</a></sup> Filoni, who serves as executive producer and co-creator of <i>Rebels</i>, worked with Lucas on identifying what Ahsoka would know about Anakin's fate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fatesfulfilled1_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-fatesfulfilled1-10">[10]</a></sup> Filoni also collaborated with executive producer <a href="/wiki/Simon_Kinberg" title="Simon Kinberg">Simon Kinberg</a> and season one executive producer Greg Wiseman on developing Ahsoka's role as a Rebel agent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fatesfulfilled1_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-fatesfulfilled1-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The show's writers were excited for Ahsoka's return in the second season, and Filoni was anxious about <i>Rebels</i> instead becoming "The Ahsoka Tano Show".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fatesfulfilled1_10-4"><a href="#cite_note-fatesfulfilled1-10">[10]</a></sup> Consequently, Filoni required that Ahsoka play a role in service of <i>Rebels</i>' main characters, Ezra and Kanan;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fatesfulfilled1_10-5"><a href="#cite_note-fatesfulfilled1-10">[10]</a></sup> he saw Ahsoka's new role as similar to the one played by Obi-Wan Kenobi in <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_(film)" title="Star Wars (film)">Star Wars</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-15">[15]</a></sup> Although Ahsoka is more mature in <i>Rebels</i>, Filoni wanted "aspects of that kid who was there to shine through".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-btslothal_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-btslothal-16">[16]</a></sup> He initially envisioned Ahsoka as a more "passive player" not engaged in combat, but later decided it was more appropriate to see Ahsoka as a warrior during a turbulent time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fatesfulfilled1_10-6"><a href="#cite_note-fatesfulfilled1-10">[10]</a></sup> Ahsoka's presence was necessary to allow Darth Vader to encounter the show's lead characters without the latter being "destroyed"; Ahsoka can "stand toe-to-toe" with Vader.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Filoni cites fans' passion for the character as one reason for Ahsoka's prominence in <i>Clone Wars</i> and <i>Rebels</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-swce2016_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-swce2016-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Voice_acting">Voice acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahsoka_Tano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Voice acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Eckstein said Filoni wanted her to bring some of her own personality into the Ahsoka character; he told her that her actions and speech between audition takes had a bigger impact on her getting the role than the actual audition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ecksteinsw.com_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-ecksteinsw.com-12">[12]</a></sup> Once production on <i>The Clone Wars</i> began, it took about six months for Eckstein and the writers to understand Ahsoka well; consequently, much of the dialogue for the first half of season one was re-recorded to better depict the character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ecksteinsw.com_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-ecksteinsw.com-12">[12]</a></sup> Eckstein also credits the casting of <a href="/wiki/Matt_Lanter" title="Matt Lanter">Matt Lanter</a> as Anakin, which occurred halfway through the first season, with helping solidify the Ahsoka character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ecksteinsw.com_12-4"><a href="#cite_note-ecksteinsw.com-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p>Eckstein reprised the role of Ahsoka for <i>Rebels</i>, although she did not do all of Fulcrum's voice work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rebels-recon_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-rebels-recon-14">[14]</a></sup> She learned about Ahsoka's return about a year before the first season's finale aired and said it was hard to keep it a secret.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-techtimes_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-techtimes-18">[18]</a></sup> Eckstein said Ahsoka developed a quiet confidence and quiet strength the younger character doesn't possess, but sometimes she played Ahsoka too seriously; she pointed out that "snippiness" and determination are still part of Ahsoka's personality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ecksteinsw.com_12-5"><a href="#cite_note-ecksteinsw.com-12">[12]</a></sup> Eckstein pitched her voice down a bit but, because she and Ahsoka were close in age, generally spoke like herself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-techtimes_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-techtimes-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Not knowing Ahsoka's experiences between <i>The Clone Wars</i> and <i>Rebels</i> at times presented challenges for Eckstein's performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ecksteinsw.com_12-6"><a href="#cite_note-ecksteinsw.com-12">[12]</a></sup> Filoni avoids giving the actors plot details that might inappropriately affect their performance; for example, he did not tell Eckstein whether Ahsoka survives her duel with Darth Vader lest Eckstein telegraph something in her performance that the character would not know.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-untoldtales_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-untoldtales-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearance">Appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahsoka_Tano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ahsoka initially appears in what <i><a href="/wiki/Wired_(website)" title="Wired (website)">Wired</a></i> called a "tube-top-and-miniskirt costume". In the third season, Ahsoka and other characters received new costumes. Filoni said the changes were meant to bring the show's aesthetic closer to <i>Revenge of the Sith</i> and were made possible by improved animation techniques.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wiredtubetop_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-wiredtubetop-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ahsoka's armor in <i>Rebels</i> is based on a "pseudo-<a href="/wiki/Samurai" title="Samurai">samurai</a> look" influenced by photographs of samurai women.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-untoldtales_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-untoldtales-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rebels-recon_14-3"><a href="#cite_note-rebels-recon-14">[14]</a></sup> The armor is meant to appear as if she found it in an ancient Jedi temple, and her lightsabers' colorless blades indicate she is neither a Jedi nor evil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-untoldtales_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-untoldtales-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rebels-recon_14-4"><a href="#cite_note-rebels-recon-14">[14]</a></sup> Filoni said the white lightsabers appear much better than he anticipated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-btslothal_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-btslothal-16">[16]</a></sup> Her facial markings were changed to show that she has aged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-15">[15]</a></sup> It was "new ground" for the production team to shift Ahsoka's animation style to reflect her older age.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filoni3_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-filoni3-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Depiction">Depiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahsoka_Tano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Depiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars_film"><i>Star Wars: The Clone Wars</i> film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahsoka_Tano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Star Wars: The Clone Wars film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ahsoka first appears in the <i>Clone Wars</i> film as a 14-year-old<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-databank_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-databank-20">[20]</a></sup> Padawan apprentice newly assigned to Anakin Skywalker. <a href="/wiki/Yoda" title="Yoda">Yoda</a> assigns her to Anakin to teach him a greater sense of responsibility, and Anakin is initially frustrated by this decision. Their early interactions are "playfully contentious", with Anakin calling her "Snips" for her "snippy" attitude and Ahsoka calling him "Skyguy" as a pun on his surname.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-databank_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-databank-20">[20]</a></sup> After earning Anakin's respect on a dangerous mission, Ahsoka joins him on a quest to rescue <a href="/wiki/Jabba_the_Hutt" title="Jabba the Hutt">Jabba the Hutt</a>'s infant son. Her impetuous nature both annoys and endears her to her master, and, by the end of the film, Anakin reveals a newfound affection for his apprentice.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars_TV_series"><i>Star Wars: The Clone Wars</i> TV series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahsoka_Tano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Star Wars: The Clone Wars TV series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ahsoka is a lead character in all five broadcast seasons of <i>The Clone Wars</i>. She is a commander in the Republic army and continues to learn the ways of the Jedi as Anakin's apprentice. The two develop a mutual fondness, at times taking great risks to protect or save one another. Some of Anakin's actions taken out concern for Ahsoka expose his darker tendencies, such as his torture of prisoners who may know her location when she goes missing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-io9netflix_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-io9netflix-21">[21]</a></sup> Ahsoka also finds mentorship from Captain Rex, a <a href="/wiki/Clone_trooper" title="Clone trooper">clone trooper</a> with whom she and Anakin serve throughout the war. During the final arc of season five, Ahsoka is framed and imprisoned for a deadly explosion and a subsequent murder. Although eventually exonerated, she is frustrated with the Jedi Council<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> and leaves the Jedi Order in the season finale. Filoni said Ahsoka surrendered her lightsabers to the Jedi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rebels-recon_14-5"><a href="#cite_note-rebels-recon-14">[14]</a></sup> Filoni said an initial finale concept for <i>Clone Wars</i> would have had Rex escape Order 66, and their presence elsewhere would have explained both characters' absence from <i>Revenge of the Sith</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fatesfulfilled1_10-7"><a href="#cite_note-fatesfulfilled1-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Star_Wars_Rebels"><i>Star Wars Rebels</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahsoka_Tano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Star Wars Rebels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ahsoka is a secretive <a href="/wiki/Rebel_Alliance" title="Rebel Alliance">rebel</a> agent in the first season of <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Rebels" title="Star Wars Rebels">Star Wars Rebels</a>,</i> which takes place 14 years<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> after <i>The Clone Wars</i> concludes. Operating under the codename "Fulcrum", she provides the rebel crew of the <i>Ghost</i> with intelligence and supplies. Until her identity is revealed in the season finale, she disguises her appearance by using an altered voice and appearing as a hooded hologram.</p>
<p>She becomes a recurring character in the second season, continuing to help lead a group of rebel forces and working with the <i>Ghost</i>'s crew. Having assumed that Anakin died like most other Jedi at the end of the Clone Wars, she is overwhelmed to recognize her mentor under "a layer of hate" in Darth Vader.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fatesfulfilled1_10-8"><a href="#cite_note-fatesfulfilled1-10">[10]</a></sup> Later in the season, a vision of Anakin blames her for leaving him and allowing him to fall to the dark side. In the season finale, Ahsoka duels with Darth Vader inside a Sith temple, allowing her friends from the <i>Ghost</i> to escape Vader and the temple's destruction. As the episode concludes, she is briefly shown walking through the temple's ruins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-untoldtales_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-untoldtales-4">[4]</a></sup> Filoni said Ahsoka's fate is ambiguous and "a bit open-ended",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and Eckstein believes the character's fate is connected to a third-season plot line of <i>The Clone Wars</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_media">Other media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahsoka_Tano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Other media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Filoni said he does not want Ahsoka thought of as an animated character, but rather as a <i>Star Wars</i> character who can exist "in all forms of media".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-untoldtales_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-untoldtales-4">[4]</a></sup> Ahsoka is a playable character in a variety of video games, and several novels feature the character. At <a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Celebration" title="Star Wars Celebration">Star Wars Celebration Europe 2016</a>, Filoni, Eckstein, and Lucasfilm Story Group member <a href="/wiki/Pablo_Hidalgo" title="Pablo Hidalgo">Pablo Hidalgo</a> held a panel about Ahsoka's "Untold Tales" that occur between <i>The Clone Wars</i> and <i>Rebels</i>. Filoni said E. K. Johnston's <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_Ahsoka" title="Star Wars: Ahsoka">Star Wars: Ahsoka</a></i> novel, which depicts Ahsoka's life between <i>The Clone Wars</i> and <i>Rebels</i>, makes several references to these stories.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-untoldtales_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-untoldtales-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Filoni was heavily involved in developing the novel, and cover art shared at the Celebration panel is based on a sketch he created several years previously.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-untoldtales_4-7"><a href="#cite_note-untoldtales-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahsoka_Tano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After her introduction, some critics called Ahsoka annoying and predicted the character would die before <i>The Clone Wars</i> series ended because she does not appear in <i>Revenge of the Sith</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-io9netflix_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-io9netflix-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blastr_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-blastr-27">[27]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">The Los Angeles Times</a></i> called Ahsoka a "carefully calculated-to-be-cute" character in <i>The Clone Wars</i> film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> <i>Wired</i> criticized Ahsoka's "half-naked" appearance in <i>The Clone Wars</i>' first two seasons, calling her third-season costume change "more appropriate".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wiredtubetop_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-wiredtubetop-19">[19]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Syfy" title="Syfy">Blastr</a></i> said Ahsoka's initial immaturity gives the character room to grow, saying she becomes "a well-rounded and complex character in every sense".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blastr_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-blastr-27">[27]</a></sup> Ahsoka's youth aided her in being a point-of-view character for younger viewers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-io9netflix_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-io9netflix-21">[21]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Io9" title="Io9">io9</a></i> called Ahsoka's development arc one of the best aspects of <i>The Clone Wars</i>, highlighting the character's role in exploring the nuances of war and the flaws of the Jedi Order.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-io9netflix_21-3"><a href="#cite_note-io9netflix-21">[21]</a></sup> According to <i>Tech Times</i>, Ahsoka's maturation and development mirror the show's, and the producers chose wisely in making Ahsoka the audience's "entry point" to <i>The Clone Wars</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Chris_Taylor_(journalist)" title="Chris Taylor (journalist)">Chris Taylor</a> called Ahsoka's decision to leave the Jedi Order "the show's most shocking <a href="/wiki/Cliffhanger" title="Cliffhanger">cliffhanger</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taylor_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-taylor-2">[2]</a></sup> Ashley Eckstein was nominated for the 2012 and 2013 "Best Female Lead Vocal Performance in a Television Series - Action/Drama" awards from Behind the Voice Actors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>The Mary Sue</i> said Ahsoka's relationship with Anakin is critical in understanding his development between <i>Attack of the Clones</i> and <i>Revenge of the Sith</i>, and the publication called Ahsoka a <a href="/wiki/Foil_(literature)" title="Foil (literature)">foil</a> for Anakin's growth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> <i>Blastr</i> commented that Anakin's interactions with Ahsoka help show that he is a powerful Jedi and war hero. <i>Blastr</i> also hypothesized that Anakin's sense of failure when Ahsoka leaves the Jedi contributes to his fall to the dark side;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blastr_27-2"><a href="#cite_note-blastr-27">[27]</a></sup> <i>io9</i> posited that Anakin might have succeeded in reforming the Jedi if Ahsoka had stayed with him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-io9netflix_21-4"><a href="#cite_note-io9netflix-21">[21]</a></sup> <i>io9</i> said Ahsoka, more than Anakin, articulates moral guidance in <i>The Clone Wars</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-io9netflix_21-5"><a href="#cite_note-io9netflix-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Blastr</i> identified Ahsoka as one of the most important characters in Star Wars, especially for young girls who, until that point, had not seen a potent female Jedi depicted on screen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blastr_27-3"><a href="#cite_note-blastr-27">[27]</a></sup> Erika Travis of <a href="/wiki/California_Baptist_University" title="California Baptist University">California Baptist University</a> said Ahsoka is "compassionate and feminine, without being overtly sexualized".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Peter Lee called Ahsoka a feminist icon, adding that Ahsoka is one of several characters that make <i>The Clone Wars</i> superior to the original and prequel trilogies in depicting strong females.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-peterlee_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-peterlee-34">[34]</a></sup> Lee added that Ahsoka probably shows the most growth of any female in the Star Wars canon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-peterlee_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-peterlee-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
