<p><b>Assyriska Fotbollsföreningen</b>, also known simply as <b>Assyriska FF</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football club</a> based in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje" title="Södertälje">Södertälje</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_County" title="Stockholm County">Stockholm County</a>. The club, formed in 1974 by <a href="/wiki/Assyrians/Syriacs_in_Sweden" title="Assyrians/Syriacs in Sweden">Assyrian immigrants</a>, has advanced through the league system and is currently playing in the second highest Swedish league, <a href="/wiki/Superettan" title="Superettan">Superettan</a>. They played in the highest Swedish football league <a href="/wiki/Allsvenskan" title="Allsvenskan">Allsvenskan</a> in 2005 where their games were broadcast in over 80 countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-landslag_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-landslag-1">[1]</a></sup> The club has also played a final in <a href="/wiki/Svenska_Cupen" title="Svenska Cupen">Svenska Cupen</a>, which was lost against <a href="/wiki/IF_Elfsborg" title="IF Elfsborg">IF Elfsborg</a> in 2003.</p>
<p>Assyriska is often viewed as a substitute national team by the <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_people" title="Assyrian people">Assyrian people</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-landslag_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-landslag-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fans_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-fans-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The club has a fan base from all over the world<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fans_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-fans-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fans1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-fans1-3">[3]</a></sup> and has also their own pop song which is called "My Assyrian team – the team of my dream". A documentary film about Assyriska called "Assyriska: A National Team Without A Nation" was also made in 2006 by <a href="/wiki/Nuri_Kino" title="Nuri Kino">Nuri Kino</a> and Erik Sandberg. The success of the documentary film made it possible to win the Golden Palm Award at the <a href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills_Film_Festival" title="Beverly Hills Film Festival">Beverly Hills Film Festival</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Assyriska_FF&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Assyriska Föreningen in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje" title="Södertälje">Södertälje</a> was founded in 1971 by ethnic <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_people" title="Assyrian people">Assyrian</a> refugees from <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> who belonged to the <a href="/wiki/Syriac_Orthodox_Church" title="Syriac Orthodox Church">Syriac Orthodox Church</a>. Three years later, a football team was started and played its first season in the lowest league in 1975. They advanced through the divisions and reached the second highest league, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swedish_Division_1" title="Swedish Division 1">Division 1</a> (today replaced by <a href="/wiki/Superettan" title="Superettan">Superettan</a>), in 1992, as the first immigrant team ever. The team was however relegated in 1993, and the next few years, the club played every second year in Division 1 before managing to stay there for more than one season in 1997.</p>
<p>In 2003 Assyriska made it to the final in <a href="/wiki/Svenska_Cupen" title="Svenska Cupen">Svenska Cupen</a>, where they lost with 0–2 against <a href="/wiki/IF_Elfsborg" title="IF Elfsborg">IF Elfsborg</a>. On the way to the final, they won against <a href="/wiki/IFK_G%C3%B6teborg" title="IFK Göteborg">IFK Göteborg</a> with 4–1 and <a href="/wiki/Djurg%C3%A5rdens_IF_Fotboll" title="Djurgårdens IF Fotboll">Djurgårdens IF</a> with 0–4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>They lost a promotion play-off against <a href="/wiki/%C3%96rebro_SK" title="Örebro SK">Örebro SK</a> in 1999, but five years later, in 2004, Assyriska was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Allsvenskan" title="Allsvenskan">Allsvenskan</a>, the highest league, for the first time as Örebro SK was relegated due to economical problems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Assyriska's debut in <a href="/wiki/Allsvenskan" title="Allsvenskan">Allsvenskan</a> went well in the beginning and they had a couple of notable wins against big clubs as <a href="/wiki/IFK_G%C3%B6teborg" title="IFK Göteborg">IFK Göteborg</a> with 0–3 and against <a href="/wiki/IF_Elfsborg" title="IF Elfsborg">IF Elfsborg</a> with 1–3. However Assyriska were relegated after only one season in the Allsvenskan, where they finished last. Since then Assyriska had fallen into Division 1 North, but enjoyed a strong season in 2007 to finish champions and were promoted back into the Superettan.</p>
<p>At the close of the 2009 season Assyriska, having held off the challenge of rivals <a href="/wiki/Syrianska_FC" title="Syrianska FC">Syrianska FC</a> to finish third in the <a href="/wiki/Superettan" title="Superettan">Superettan</a> table,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> faced <a href="/wiki/Djurg%C3%A5rdens_IF_Fotboll" title="Djurgårdens IF Fotboll">Djurgårdens IF</a> in a play-off for a place in the <a href="/wiki/Allsvenskan" title="Allsvenskan">Allsvenskan</a> in 2010. Assyriska won the first match at home 2–0 but were overcome 3–0 after extra time in the repeat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In season 2010, Assyriska finished 4th in the Superettan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> But 2010 was a season in which football became irrelevant for Assyriska after the murder of player <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Moussa" title="Eddie Moussa">Eddie Moussa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since then, the team has made a series of mid-table finishes and remains in the Superettan for the 2013 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stadium">Stadium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Assyriska_FF&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Stadium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Assyriska Föreningen's primary stadium since 2006 is <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje_Fotbollsarena" title="Södertälje Fotbollsarena">Södertälje Fotbollsarena</a>. Until 2006, the team played at the old stadium <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A5rsta_IP" title="Bårsta IP">Bårsta IP</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Supporters">Supporters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Assyriska_FF&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Supporters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Assyriska Föreningen's official fan club is called Zelge Fans. They were first started 1993 by a group of Assyriska supporters in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje" title="Södertälje">Södertälje</a>. They were known as Neshre, which means eagle (or eagles) in <a href="/wiki/Syriac_language" title="Syriac language">Syriac</a>. Three months after they launched, the club changed its name to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zelge_Fans" title="Zelge Fans">Zelge Fans</a>, Zelge meaning "sun rays" in <a href="/wiki/Syriac_language" title="Syriac language">Syriac</a>. The sun rays are a symbol of the <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_flag" title="Assyrian flag">Assyrian flag</a>, which is where the idea to name the fan club Zelge Fans came from. Most of the members are <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_people" title="Assyrian people">Assyrians</a>, but the Zelge fan club also has many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swedish_people" title="Swedish people">Swedish</a> members.</p>
<p>Assyriska's fans are also growing in numbers as a result of the internet. During its early years, Assyriska did not have the mass means of communication to be able to spread their team information consistently to other mass <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_people" title="Assyrian people">Assyrian</a> areas (Chicago, New Jersey, California, Toronto, Sydney, etc.). With the tech age, Assyriska has been able to spread its fan base outside of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje" title="Södertälje">Södertälje</a> and become known in over 80 countries. Acting as their national team, Assyriska represents the entire population of <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_people" title="Assyrian people">Assyrians</a> throughout the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fans_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-fans-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fans1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-fans1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Assyriska has become a true symbol for <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_people" title="Assyrian people">Assyrians</a> traveling to <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>. Many make it their goal to watch an Assyriska match at least once when they travel to <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>.</p>
