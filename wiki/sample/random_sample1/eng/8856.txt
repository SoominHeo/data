<p><b>Asbel Kiprop</b> (born 30 June 1989) is a Kenyan <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle-distance_runner" title="Middle-distance runner">middle-distance runner</a>, who specialises in the <a href="/wiki/1500_metres" title="1500 metres">1500 metres</a>. Kiprop was awarded the 1500 m <a href="/wiki/Gold_medal" title="Gold medal">gold medal</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a> after the original winner, <a href="/wiki/Rashid_Ramzi" title="Rashid Ramzi">Rashid Ramzi</a>, tested positive for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Use_of_performance-enhancing_drugs_in_sport" title="Use of performance-enhancing drugs in sport">doping</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-confirmed_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-confirmed-3">[3]</a></sup> Kiprop has won three <a href="/wiki/IAAF_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="IAAF World Championships in Athletics">World Championship</a> titles in the event, in <a href="/wiki/2011_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2011 World Championships in Athletics">2011</a>, <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2013 World Championships in Athletics">2013</a> and <a href="/wiki/2015_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2015 World Championships in Athletics">2015</a>.</p>
<p>He won his first major title at the <a href="/wiki/2007_All-Africa_Games" title="2007 All-Africa Games">2007 All-Africa Games</a>, taking the 1500 m gold medal, and also won the event at the <a href="/wiki/2010_African_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2010 African Championships in Athletics">2010 African Championships in Athletics</a>, improving upon a <a href="/wiki/Bronze_medal" title="Bronze medal">bronze medal</a> performance from 2008. His personal best for the distance is 3:26.69.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asbel_Kiprop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 2007 season became his breakthrough year, when he won junior race gold medal at the <a href="/wiki/2007_IAAF_World_Cross_Country_Championships" title="2007 IAAF World Cross Country Championships">2007 IAAF World Cross Country Championships</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and then the 1500 m gold at the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2007_All-Africa_Games" title="Athletics at the 2007 All-Africa Games">All-Africa Games</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He ran a personal best to finish fourth in the 1500 m at the <a href="/wiki/2007_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2007 World Championships in Athletics">2007 World Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In recognition of his achievements, he won the Most Promising Sportsman of the Year category at the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Kenyan_Sports_Personality_of_the_Year" title="Kenyan Sports Personality of the Year">Kenyan Sports Personality of the Year</a> awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>He ran at the <a href="/wiki/2008_African_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2008 African Championships in Athletics">2008 African Championships in Athletics</a> and took the bronze medal in the 800 m and came fourth in the 1500 m race. At the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics" title="Athletics at the 2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Beijing Olympics</a>, Kiprop was narrowly beaten by Bahraini <a href="/wiki/Rashid_Ramzi" title="Rashid Ramzi">Rashid Ramzi</a> for the gold medal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> However, Kiprop was awarded the gold medal after Ramzi tested positive for <a href="/wiki/Continuous_erythropoietin_receptor_activator" title="Continuous erythropoietin receptor activator">CERA</a>, a banned substance which is a newer version of the more commonly known <a href="/wiki/Erythropoietin" title="Erythropoietin">EPO</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He is the youngest ever winner of the title, a record previously held by <a href="/wiki/Arnold_Jackson_(British_Army_officer)" title="Arnold Jackson (British Army officer)">Arnold Jackson</a> since 1912. Kiprop has stated he is not very happy about the way he won the gold medal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He ended the year with a <a href="/wiki/Silver_medal" title="Silver medal">silver medal</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2008_IAAF_World_Athletics_Final" title="2008 IAAF World Athletics Final">2008 IAAF World Athletics Final</a>, finishing behind the African champion <a href="/wiki/Haron_Keitany" title="Haron Keitany">Haron Keitany</a>.</p>
<p>At the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_World_Athletics_Championships" title="2009 World Athletics Championships">2009 World Athletics Championships</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, Kiprop disappointed many when he finished fourth for the second time in the 1500 m.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-results_berlin_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-results_berlin-12">[12]</a></sup> Kiprop also participated in the 800 m but was eliminated in the semi-finals.</p>
<p>He won the gold in the 1500 m at the <a href="/wiki/2010_African_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2010 African Championships in Athletics">2010 African Championships in Athletics</a>, running a <a href="/wiki/List_of_African_Championships_in_Athletics_records" title="List of African Championships in Athletics records">championship record</a> time of 3:36.19 to win in <a href="/wiki/Nairobi" title="Nairobi">Nairobi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He competed on the <a href="/wiki/2010_IAAF_Diamond_League" title="2010 IAAF Diamond League">2010 IAAF Diamond League</a> circuit that year and after victories at the <a href="/wiki/Bislett_Games" title="Bislett Games">Bislett Games</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prefontaine_Classic" title="Prefontaine Classic">Prefontaine Classic</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_Grand_Prix_(athletics)" title="British Grand Prix (athletics)">British Grand Prix</a>, he went on to secure the inaugural 1500 m Diamond League title with a win at the final event at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Memorial_van_Damme" title="Memorial van Damme">Memorial van Damme</a>. He represented Africa at the <a href="/wiki/2010_IAAF_Continental_Cup" title="2010 IAAF Continental Cup">2010 IAAF Continental Cup</a>, but managed only sixth place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>He ran at the 2011 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Edinburgh_Cross_Country" title="Great Edinburgh Cross Country">Great Edinburgh Cross Country</a> in January, taking second place in the short race behind <a href="/wiki/Eliud_Kipchoge" title="Eliud Kipchoge">Eliud Kipchoge</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Later that year he became world champion over 1500 m, defeating his Kenyan rival, <a href="/wiki/Silas_Kiplagat" title="Silas Kiplagat">Silas Kiplagat</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2011_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2011 World Championships in Athletics">2011 World Championships in Athletics</a>. Kiprop ran the final 800 meters of the race well under 1:50 and the final 400 meters in 51 seconds plus. Kiprop was leading in the rankings of the <a href="/wiki/2011_IAAF_Diamond_League" title="2011 IAAF Diamond League">2011 IAAF Diamond League</a>, having won at the <a href="/wiki/Bislett_Games" title="Bislett Games">Bislett Games</a> Dream Mile and placed second at the Shanghai, Paris and Stockholm meets. However, at the event final at the <a href="/wiki/Weltklasse_Z%C3%BCrich" title="Weltklasse Zürich">Weltklasse Zürich</a>, he managed only seventh while <a href="/wiki/Nixon_Chepseba" title="Nixon Chepseba">Nixon Chepseba</a> won to take the seasonal title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>He had his best cross country race since he was a junior runner at the Edinburgh race in 2012, defeating a field which contained Kipchoge and <a href="/wiki/Kenenisa_Bekele" title="Kenenisa Bekele">Kenenisa Bekele</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2013, he won the <a href="/wiki/Herculis" title="Herculis">Herculis</a> (Monaco Diamond League) 1500 in a time of 3:27.72, making him the fourth fastest man ever at the distance yet still not achieving the meet record. On 18 August, at the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2013 World Championships in Athletics">IAAF World Championships</a> in Moscow, Kiprop won the 1500 metres in 3:36.28.</p>
<p>In 2015, Kiprop set a meeting record for 1500 metres at the Monaco Diamond League event in a time of 3:26.69. This puts him third on the all-time list over the distance. One month later, Kiprop won his third consecutive 1500 meter world championship title in Beijing.</p>
<p>In 2016, Kiprop failed to win another Olympic Title in Rio, coming a disappointing sixth place. He blamed his shock loss on the fact that his fellow-countrman Elijah Manangoi was not present in the race.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chasing_forbidden_fruit_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chasing_forbidden_fruit-18">[18]</a></sup> His younger brother <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Victor_Kipchirchir_Kebenei&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Victor Kipchirchir Kebenei (page does not exist)">Victor Kipchirchir Kebenei</a> is also a 1500 metres runner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Mathew Centrowitz of the USA won the race in a time of 3:50:00, one of the slowest 1500m times in history.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asbel_Kiprop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kiprop is from Kaptinga village, near <a href="/wiki/Eldoret" title="Eldoret">Eldoret</a>. He is a son of David and Julia Kebenei. His father David Kebenei was also an athlete, who participated in the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1987_All-Africa_Games" title="Athletics at the 1987 All-Africa Games">1987 All-Africa Games</a> in Kenya and finished fourth in the 1500 metres race. Kiprop started running at the age of 13, while at Simat School. Later he dropped out of high-school to concentrate on training.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rule_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-rule-20">[20]</a></sup> Kiprop trains at the <i>Kipchoge Keino High Performance Training Centre</i> in Eldoret.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He was, however, expelled from the camp in 2009 for breaking the rules by bringing his girlfriend to the centre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chasing_forbidden_fruit_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-Chasing_forbidden_fruit-18">[18]</a></sup> His younger brother <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Victor_Kipchirchir_Kebenei&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Victor Kipchirchir Kebenei (page does not exist)">Victor Kipchirchir Kebenei</a> is also a 1500 metres runner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kiprop has stated his first name, Asbel, means determined.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
