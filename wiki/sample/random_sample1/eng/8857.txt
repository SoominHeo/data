<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Ásbirningar family clan</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>Ásbirnings</b> were a powerful family clan in the medieval <a href="/wiki/Icelandic_Commonwealth" title="Icelandic Commonwealth">Icelandic Commonwealth</a>. They dominated <a href="/wiki/Skagafj%C3%B6r%C3%B0ur" title="Skagafjörður">Skagafjörður</a> in the 12th and 13th centuries until their last leader died in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Haugsnes" title="Battle of Haugsnes">Battle of Haugsnes</a> (<i>Haugsnesbardagi</i>) in 1246. The Ásbirnings were well-known warriors and politicians. The best known Ásbirning is probably <a href="/wiki/Kolbeinn_Tumason" title="Kolbeinn Tumason">Kolbeinn Tumason</a>, a famous Icelandic poet. Other well known Ásbirningur were <a href="/wiki/Kolbeinn_ungi_Arn%C3%B3rsson" title="Kolbeinn ungi Arnórsson">Kolbeinn ungi Arnórsson</a>, nephew of Kolbeinn Tumason, and scholar <a href="/wiki/Ingunn_Arn%C3%B3rsd%C3%B3ttir" title="Ingunn Arnórsdóttir">Ingunn Arnórsdóttir</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
