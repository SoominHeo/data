<p><b>Aswan</b> (<span class="nowrap"><small>English pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˌ/ secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="/æ/ short 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'w' in 'wind'">w</span><span title="/ɑː/ 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Egyptian_Arabic_language" title="Egyptian Arabic language">Egyptian Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="arz" xml:lang="arz">أسوان</span>‎‎ <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Egyptian Spoken Arabic transliteration">Aswān </span></i> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Egyptian_Arabic" title="Help:IPA for Egyptian Arabic">[ʔɑsˈwɑːn]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_language" title="Egyptian language">Ancient Egyptian</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Egyptian transliteration">Swenett</span></i><span style="margin:2.5px;">;</span> <a href="/wiki/Coptic_language" title="Coptic language">Coptic</a>: <span class="script-coptic" style="font-family:'FreeSerif', 'New Athena Unicode', 'MPH 2B Damase', 'Arial Coptic', Quivira, Analecta, Antinoou, 'Sophia Nubian', 'Noto Sans Coptic', 'Segoe UI Historic', 'Segoe UI Symbol';">ⲥⲟⲩⲁⲛ</span> <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Coptic transliteration">Souan</span></i><span style="margin:2.5px;">;</span> <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a>: <span lang="grc" xml:lang="grc">Συήνη</span> <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Ancient Greek transliteration">Syene</span></i>), formerly spelled <i>Assuan</i><span style="margin:1px;">,</span> is a city in the south of <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Aswan_Governorate" title="Aswan Governorate">Aswan Governorate</a>.</p>
<p>Aswan is a busy market and tourist centre located just north of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aswan_Dams" title="Aswan Dams">Aswan Dams</a> on the east bank of the <a href="/wiki/Nile" title="Nile">Nile</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_cataract" title="First cataract">first cataract</a>. The modern city has expanded and includes the formerly separate community on the island of <a href="/wiki/Elephantine" title="Elephantine">Elephantine</a>.</p>

<p>Aswan is the ancient city of <i><b>Swenett</b></i>, which in antiquity was the frontier town of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Ancient Egypt</a> facing the south. Swenett is supposed to have derived its name from an Egyptian <a href="/wiki/Goddess" title="Goddess">goddess</a> with the same name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-altas_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-altas-1">[1]</a></sup> This goddess later was identified as <a href="/wiki/Eileithyia" title="Eileithyia">Eileithyia</a> by the Greeks and <a href="/wiki/Lucina_(goddess)" title="Lucina (goddess)">Lucina</a> by the Romans during their occupation of Ancient Egypt because of the similar association of their goddesses with childbirth, and of which the import is "the opener". The ancient name of the city also is said to be derived from the <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_language" title="Egyptian language">Egyptian</a> symbol for <i>trade</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> or <i>market</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Because the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient_Egyptians" title="Ancient Egyptians">Ancient Egyptians</a> oriented toward the origin of the life-giving waters of the Nile in the south, Swenett was the southernmost town in the country, and <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> always was conceived to "open" or begin at Swenett.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-altas_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-altas-1">[1]</a></sup> The city stood upon a peninsula on the right (east) bank of the <a href="/wiki/Nile" title="Nile">Nile</a>, immediately below (and north of) the first cataract of the flowing waters, which extend to it from <a href="/wiki/Philae" title="Philae">Philae</a>. Navigation to the delta was possible from this location without encountering a barrier.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Stone_quarries_of_ancient_Egypt" title="Stone quarries of ancient Egypt">stone quarries of ancient Egypt</a> located here were celebrated for their stone, and especially for the granitic rock called Syenite. They furnished the colossal statues, <a href="/wiki/Obelisk" title="Obelisk">obelisks</a>, and monolithal shrines that are found throughout Egypt, including the <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_pyramids" title="Egyptian pyramids">pyramids</a>; and the traces of the quarrymen who worked in these 3,000 years ago are still visible in the native rock. They lie on either bank of the <a href="/wiki/Nile" title="Nile">Nile</a>, and a road, 6.5 km (4.0 mi) in length, was cut beside them from Syene to <a href="/wiki/Philae" title="Philae">Philae</a>.</p>
<p>Swenett was equally important as a military station as a place of traffic. Under every dynasty it was a garrison town; and here tolls and customs were levied on all boats passing southwards and northwards. Around 330, the legion stationed here received a bishop from <a href="/wiki/Alexandria" title="Alexandria">Alexandria</a>; this later became the <a href="/wiki/Coptic_Diocese_of_Syene" title="Coptic Diocese of Syene">Coptic Diocese of Syene</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The city is mentioned by numerous ancient writers, including <a href="/wiki/Herodotus" title="Herodotus">Herodotus</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Strabo" title="Strabo">Strabo</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Stephanus_of_Byzantium" title="Stephanus of Byzantium">Stephanus of Byzantium</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ptolemy" title="Ptolemy">Ptolemy</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pliny_the_Elder" title="Pliny the Elder">Pliny the Elder</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Vitruvius" title="Vitruvius">Vitruvius</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and it appears on the <a href="/wiki/Antonine_Itinerary" title="Antonine Itinerary">Antonine Itinerary</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> It also is mentioned in the <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Ezekiel" title="Book of Ezekiel">Book of Ezekiel</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Isaiah" title="Book of Isaiah">Book of Isaiah</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">latitude</a> of the city that would become Aswan – located at 24° 5′ 23″ – was an object of great interest to the ancient geographers. They believed that it was seated immediately under the <a href="/wiki/Tropic_of_Cancer" title="Tropic of Cancer">tropic</a>, and that on the day of the <a href="/wiki/Summer_solstice" title="Summer solstice">summer solstice</a>, a vertical staff cast no shadow. They noted that the sun's disc was reflected in a well at noon. This statement is only approximately correct; at the summer solstice, the shadow was only <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>400</sub></span> of the staff, and so could scarcely be discerned, and the northern limb of the Sun's disc would be nearly vertical. However, <a href="/wiki/Eratosthenes" title="Eratosthenes">Eratosthenes</a> used this information together with measurements of the shadow length on the solstice at <a href="/wiki/Alexandria" title="Alexandria">Alexandria</a> to perform the first known calculation of the <a href="/wiki/Circumference" title="Circumference">circumference</a> of the Earth.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Nile" title="Nile">Nile</a> is nearly 650 m (0.40 mi) wide above Aswan. From this frontier town to the northern extremity of Egypt, the river flows for more than 1,200 km (750 mi) without bar or cataract. The voyage from Aswan to Alexandria usually took 21 to 28 days in favourable weather.</p>

<p>Aswan has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_desert_climate" title="Hot desert climate">hot desert climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> <i>BWh</i>) like the rest of Egypt. Aswan and <a href="/wiki/Luxor#Climate" title="Luxor">Luxor</a> have the hottest summer days of any city in <a href="/wiki/Climate_of_Egypt" title="Climate of Egypt">Egypt</a>. Aswan is one of the hottest, sunniest and driest cities in the world. Averages high temperatures are consistently above 40 °C (104.0 °F) during summer (June, July, August and also September) while averages low temperatures remain above 25 °C (77.0 °F). Summers are long, prolonged and extremely hot. Averages high temperatures remain above 23 °C (73.4 °F) during the coldest month of the year while averages low temperatures remain above 8 °C (46.4 °F). Winters are short, brief and extremely warm. Wintertime is very pleasant and enjoyable while summertime is unbearably hot with blazing sunshine although desert heat is dry.</p>
<p>The climate of Aswan is extremely dry year-round, with less than 1 mm (0 in) of average annual precipitation. The desert city is one of the driest ones in the world, and rainfall doesn't occur every year, as of early 2001, the last rain there was seven years earlier. Aswan is one of the least humid cities on the planet, with an average relative humidity of only 26%, with a maximum mean of 42% during winter and a minimum mean of 16% during summer.</p>
<p>The weather of Aswan is extremely clear, bright and sunny year-round, in all seasons, with a low seasonal variation, with almost 4,000 hours of annual sunshine, very close of the maximum theoretical sunshine duration. Aswan is one of the sunniest places on Earth.</p>
<p>The highest record temperature was 51 °C (124 °F) on May 22, 1973 and the lowest record temperature was −2 °C (28 °F) on January 6, 1989.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Voodoo_Skies_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Voodoo_Skies-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aswan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1999, <a href="/wiki/South_Valley_University" title="South Valley University">South Valley University</a> was inaugurated and it has three branches; Aswan, <a href="/wiki/Qena" title="Qena">Qena</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hurghada" title="Hurghada">Hurghada</a>. The university grew steadily and now it is firmly established as a major institution of higher education in Upper Egypt. Aswan branch of Assiut University began in 1973 with the Faculty of Education and in 1975 the Faculty of Science was opened. Aswan branch has five faculties namely; Science, Education, Engineering, Arts, Social Works and Institute of Energy. The Faculty of Science in Aswan has six departments. Each department has one educational programme: Chemistry, Geology, Physics and Zoology. Except Botany Department, which has three educational programmes: Botany, Environmental Sciences and Microbiology; and Mathematics Department, which has two educational programmes: Mathematics and Computer Science. The Faculty of Science awards the following degrees: Bachelor of Science in nine educational programmes, Higher Diploma, Master of Science and Philosophy Doctor of Science.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aswan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aswan is served by the <a href="/wiki/Aswan_International_Airport" title="Aswan International Airport">Aswan International Airport</a>. Train and bus service is also available. Taxi and rickshaw are used for transport here.</p>
