<p><b>Ascanio Maria Sforza Visconti</b> (3 March 1455 – 28 May 1505) was an Italian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardinal_(Catholicism)" title="Cardinal (Catholicism)">Cardinal</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a>. Generally known as a skilled diplomat who played a major role in the election of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rodrigo_Borgia" title="Rodrigo Borgia">Rodrigo Borgia</a> as <a href="/wiki/Pope_Alexander_VI" title="Pope Alexander VI">Pope Alexander VI</a>, Sforza served as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vice-Chancellor_of_the_Holy_Roman_Church" title="Vice-Chancellor of the Holy Roman Church">Vice-Chancellor of the Holy Roman Church</a> from 1492 until 1505.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ascanio_Sforza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ascanio_Sforza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A member of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Sforza" title="House of Sforza">House of Sforza</a>, Ascanio Sforza was born in <a href="/wiki/Cremona" title="Cremona">Cremona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">Lombardy</a>. His parents were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Francesco_Sforza" title="Francesco Sforza">Francesco Sforza</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duke_of_Milan" title="Duke of Milan">Duke of Milan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bianca_Maria_Visconti" title="Bianca Maria Visconti">Bianca Maria Visconti</a>. He was also the brother of two Milanese dukes, <a href="/wiki/Galeazzo_Maria_Sforza" title="Galeazzo Maria Sforza">Galeazzo Maria Sforza</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ludovico_Sforza" title="Ludovico Sforza">Ludovico Sforza</a>. His teacher was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filelfo" title="Filelfo">Filelfo</a> who introduced him to government and literature. Other cardinals of the family were <a href="/wiki/Guido_Ascanio_Sforza_di_Santa_Fiora" title="Guido Ascanio Sforza di Santa Fiora">Guido Ascanio Sforza di Santa Fiora</a> (1534), <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Sforza_(cardinal)" title="Alessandro Sforza (cardinal)">Alessandro Sforza</a> (1565), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Francesco_Sforza,_Cardinal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Francesco Sforza, Cardinal (page does not exist)">Francesco Sforza</a> (1583) and <a href="/wiki/Federico_Sforza" title="Federico Sforza">Federico Sforza</a> (1645).</p>
<p>At age of 10 he was named <a href="/wiki/Commendatory_abbot" title="Commendatory abbot">commendatory abbot</a> of <a href="/wiki/Chiaravalle_Abbey" title="Chiaravalle Abbey">Chiaravalle</a>. While still an adolescent, Ascanio was promised the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardinal_(Catholicism)" title="Cardinal (Catholicism)">red hat</a> of a cardinal of the Roman Catholic Church by <a href="/wiki/Guillaume_d%27Estouteville" title="Guillaume d'Estouteville">Guillaume d'Estouteville</a>, who wanted to gain Galeazzo Maria Sforza’s support for his candidacy for the papal throne in 1471. However, it was in fact Francesco della Rovere (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sixtus_IV" title="Sixtus IV">Sixtus IV</a>) who won the papal election, and Ascanio’s promotion to cardinal was delayed. Hoping to pacify Milan, Sixtus IV attempted to create Ascanio a cardinal in 1477 but the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sacred_College" title="Sacred College">Sacred College</a> refused to accept him into its ranks.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Episcopate">Episcopate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ascanio_Sforza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Episcopate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nevertheless, Ascanio entered the episcopate when he was appointed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Pavia" title="Bishop of Pavia">Bishop of Pavia</a> in September 1479, retaining the diocese until his death. In 1484 he represented <a href="/wiki/Ludovico_Sforza" title="Ludovico Sforza">Ludovico Sforza</a> at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Congress_of_Cremona&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Congress of Cremona (page does not exist)">Congress of Cremona</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cardinalate">Cardinalate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ascanio_Sforza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cardinalate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:162px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Stemma.Card.Sforza.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="272" data-file-width="287" height="152" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Stemma.Card.Sforza.JPG/160px-Stemma.Card.Sforza.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Stemma.Card.Sforza.JPG/240px-Stemma.Card.Sforza.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/43/Stemma.Card.Sforza.JPG 2x" width="160"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Stemma.Card.Sforza.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Coat of Arms of Cardinal Ascanio Sforza</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Pope_Sixtus_IV" title="Pope Sixtus IV">Pope Sixtus IV</a> finally created him <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardinal_deacon" title="Cardinal deacon">cardinal deacon</a> of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ss._Vito_e_Modesto&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ss. Vito e Modesto (page does not exist)">Ss. Vito e Modesto</a> on 17 March 1484. Ascanio entered <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> on 23 August the same year - just a few days after Sixtus’s death. The formal nomination ceremony had not taken place and some cardinals voiced objections to his participation in the forthcoming <a href="/wiki/Papal_conclave,_1484" title="Papal conclave, 1484">conclave</a>. Due to Cardinal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rodrigo_Borgia" title="Rodrigo Borgia">Rodrigo Borgia</a>’s intervention however, Ascanio was received with full cardinalitial rights and contributed to the election of Giovanni Cybo as <a href="/wiki/Pope_Innocent_VIII" title="Pope Innocent VIII">Pope Innocent VIII</a>. Sforza became administrator of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Novara" title="Diocese of Novara">Novara</a> from 25 October 1484 to 18 April 1485 (occupying the post again in May 1505, a few days before his death).</p>
<p>As a cardinal, Ascanio's main priority was to reconcile <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_I_of_Naples" title="Ferdinand I of Naples">Ferdinand I of Naples</a> with the Sforza dynasty. A dispute with Cardinal <a href="/wiki/Jean_Balue" title="Jean Balue">Jean Balue</a>, the French ambassador to the Papal Court, in March 1486 complicated matters. Balue had suggested that Innocent VIII summon <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rene_d%27Anjou" title="Rene d'Anjou">Rene d'Anjou</a> to retrieve his rights over the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Naples" title="Kingdom of Naples">Neapolitan</a> throne - leading to a violent quarrel with Ascanio; to which only the pope could bring a halt.</p>
<p>Ascanio was appointed administrator of the See of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Cremona" title="Diocese of Cremona">Cremona</a> on 28 July 1486 and occupied the post until his death. He also became administrator of the See of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Pesaro" title="Diocese of Pesaro">Pesaro</a> in 1487 until May 1491.</p>
<p>In his effort to ally <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a> with <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>, he received Ferdinand of Capua, Ferrante’s grandson, in his palace in <a href="/wiki/Trastevere" title="Trastevere">Trastevere</a> in May 1492. The banquet organised in honour of the Neapolitan prince was so extravagant and magnificent that, according to <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Infessura" title="Stefano Infessura">Stefano Infessura</a>, <i>If I were to give an account, no one would believe me</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vice-Chancellor">Vice-Chancellor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ascanio_Sforza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Vice-Chancellor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conclave_of_August_1492" title="Conclave of August 1492">Conclave of August 1492</a>, after having failed to obtain the papal tiara for himself, Ascanio promised his vote to Rodrigo Borgia, then-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vice-Chancellor_of_the_Holy_Roman_Church" title="Vice-Chancellor of the Holy Roman Church">Vice-Chancellor of the Holy Roman Church</a>, in exchange for Rodrigo’s prestigious association. The latter was elected to the papal throne partly due to the former's persuasive manner, becoming <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexander_VI" title="Alexander VI">Alexander VI</a>, and appointed Ascanio his Vice-Chancellor, making him the virtual prime minister of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">Holy See</a>. Sforza resigned his deaconry of S. Vito e Modesto on 26 August 1492 and opted for it again on 31 January 1495 and occupied it until his death. He was named administrator of the Metropolitan See of <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Eger" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Eger">Eger</a> on 31 August 1492, staying in that position until June 1496. In order to strengthen the relationship between his family and the papal house, Ascanio arranged the marriage of Giovanni Sforza, his cousin and governor of <a href="/wiki/Pesaro" title="Pesaro">Pesaro</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Lucrezia_Borgia" title="Lucrezia Borgia">Lucrezia Borgia</a>, the Pope’s illegitimate daughter, in 1493. The marriage was <a href="/wiki/Annulment" title="Annulment">annulled</a> in 1497, on grounds of non-consummation.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="French_invasion">French invasion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ascanio_Sforza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: French invasion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cardinal Sforza was named administrator of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Perpignan-Elne" title="Diocese of Perpignan-Elne">Elne</a> in January 1494 until May 1495. The friendship between Ascanio and Alexander VI came to a deadlock when the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> invaded <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> in September 1494. Aware of Cardinal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Giuliano_della_Rovere" title="Giuliano della Rovere">Giuliano della Rovere</a>’s machinations against him, Alexander decided to resist the French. Ludovico Sforza having secretly allied himself with <a href="/wiki/Charles_VIII_of_France" title="Charles VIII of France">King Charles VIII of France</a>, Ascanio betrayed the Pope together with several cardinals and clamoured for his deposition under della Rovere. After the papal triumph over the King, Milan abandoned the French and Ascanio was received once again in the Vatican. He never managed, however, to regain his former influence over the Pope. When <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Giovanni_Borgia_(1474)" title="Giovanni Borgia (1474)">Giovanni Borgia</a>, the Pope’s son, was stabbed in 1497, Ascanio did not attend the following consistory and was accused of the murder. He was immediately absolved, however, by the Pope.</p>
<p>When the French again invaded Italy with the support of the Holy See, Ascanio watched Ludovico Sforza’s downfall and imprisonment (1500), unable to act. On 15 June 1500, he was taken to France and imprisoned in <a href="/wiki/Lyon" title="Lyon">Lyon</a>, later at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tour_de_Bourges&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tour de Bourges (page does not exist)">Tour de Bourges</a>. He was freed on 3 January 1502 based on his promise to not leave France without royal permission. He participated in the <a href="/wiki/Papal_conclave,_September_1503" title="Papal conclave, September 1503">Papal conclave, September 1503</a>. He made futile efforts to succeed Alexander VI, fighting against Cardinal della Rovere and <a href="/wiki/Georges_d%27Amboise" title="Georges d'Amboise">Georges d'Amboise</a>, the formal nominee of France. When <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pius_III" title="Pius III">Pius III</a> (Francesco Piccolomini) died the same month of his coronation, Cardinal Sforza took part in the <a href="/wiki/Papal_conclave,_October_1503" title="Papal conclave, October 1503">Papal conclave, October 1503</a> and was defeated by Giuliano della Rovere (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Julius_II" title="Julius II">Julius II</a>) . Vanquished by disappointments, the fifty-year-old Ascanio died in Rome, on 25 May 1505. Julius II commissioned the erection of the Cardinal’s tomb in the Cappella Maggiore of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santa_Maria_del_Popolo" title="Santa Maria del Popolo">Santa Maria del Popolo</a> <a class="external autonumber" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20050502070524/http://rubens.anu.edu.au/popolo/pics/0005/P530.JPG" rel="nofollow">[1]</a>.</p>
<p>Ascanio's lifelong focus on public affairs prevented him from being a patron of the arts. However, he was the one to introduce <a href="/wiki/Josquin_des_Prez" title="Josquin des Prez">Josquin des Prez</a>, the most famous musician of the <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a>, to the papal court in 1486. Strenuous and hard-skinned as a politician, Ascanio preferred gambling rather than studying. He was undoubtedly <a href="/wiki/Machiavellianism" title="Machiavellianism">Machiavellian</a>, but also clear-sighted and intelligent, with haughty spirit and unfinished courage. His political morals were typical of his era and he remained dedicated to his love for Milan and for his family.</p>
