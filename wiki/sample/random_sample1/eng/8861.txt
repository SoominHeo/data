<p><b>Ascoli Picchio F.C. 1898 S.p.A.</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Football_in_Italy" title="Football in Italy">Italian football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Ascoli_Piceno" title="Ascoli Piceno">Ascoli Piceno</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marche" title="Marche">Marche</a>. The club was formed in 1898 and currently play in <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a>, having returned to it after two seasons spent in <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> and eight in <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a>. Ascoli played in Serie A during the periods 1974–1976, 1978–1985, 1986–1990, 1991–1992, and 2005–2007.</p>
<p>The team traditionally play in vertical black and white stripes.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ascoli_Picchio_F.C._1898&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:142px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ascoli_calcio.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="425" data-file-width="425" height="140" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/89/Ascoli_calcio.png/140px-Ascoli_calcio.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/89/Ascoli_calcio.png/210px-Ascoli_calcio.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/89/Ascoli_calcio.png/280px-Ascoli_calcio.png 2x" width="140"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ascoli_calcio.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
former logo of Ascoli Calcio 1898 SpA</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Founded in 1898 as "Candido Augusto Vecchi", the team changed its name to Ascoli Vigor in 1905, U.S. Ascolana in 1921, and A.S. Ascoli in 1945. In 1955, the team was acquired and saved from bankruptcy by publishing magnate, <a href="/wiki/Cino_Del_Duca" title="Cino Del Duca">Cino Del Duca</a>, who merged it with his own team forming "Del Duca Ascoli". The stadium still bears his and his brother's name. In 1959 the team finally returned in Serie C for the first time since before the beginning of World War II. In 1971, under the chairmanship of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Costantino_Rozzi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Costantino Rozzi (page does not exist)">Costantino Rozzi</a>, who had acquired the team three years earlier, the team changed its name to the current one, finally being known as <b>Ascoli Calcio 1898</b>. From that same year, the team, led by <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Mazzone" title="Carlo Mazzone">Carlo Mazzone</a> as coach, gained two consecutive promotions, being promoted in Serie B, and then Serie A, for the first time. The team stayed in Serie A for two seasons before being once again relegated to Serie B.</p>
<p>Ascoli returned to Serie A in 1978 and enjoyed a seven-year tenure in the top league, during which time they managed to finish a surprising 5th in 1980 and 6th in 1982. Some of the most notable players under the management of Mazzone and <a href="/wiki/Giovan_Battista_Fabbri" title="Giovan Battista Fabbri">Giovan Battista Fabbri</a> included <a href="/wiki/Adelio_Moro" title="Adelio Moro">Adelio Moro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Scanziani" title="Alessandro Scanziani">Alessandro Scanziani</a>, Gianfranco Bellotto, <a href="/wiki/Walter_Novellino" title="Walter Novellino">Walter Novellino</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Mandorlini" title="Andrea Mandorlini">Andrea Mandorlini</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Walter_De_Vecchi" title="Walter De Vecchi">Walter De Vecchi</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Greco_(footballer_born_1958)" title="Giuseppe Greco (footballer born 1958)">Giuseppe Greco</a>. After relegation in 1985, the club immediately bounced back and stayed for four more years. Another return to Serie A followed in 1991 but this lasted only one season. After just missing out on promotion for two years running, the club went into decline and were relegated to Serie C1 in 1995. They would remain there for seven seasons, returning to B as Serie C1/B champions in 2002.</p>
<p>In August 2005, after the sentence of relegation from <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> for both <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genoa_1893" title="Genoa 1893">Genoa</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Torino_Calcio" title="Torino Calcio">Torino</a> respectively due to fraud and financial troubles, Ascoli, who obtained a place in the Serie B promotion playoffs the previous season under coach <a href="/wiki/Marco_Giampaolo" title="Marco Giampaolo">Marco Giampaolo</a>, were arbitrarily admitted to Serie A as replacements, finishing a highly credible 12th. After the end of the season, Giampaolo, who gained a reputation as one of the finest young Italian coaches, resigned, being replaced by <a href="/wiki/Attilio_Tesser" title="Attilio Tesser">Attilio Tesser</a> for the 2006/2007 season. Tesser, unable to obtain good results in his time with Ascoli, was fired after a 1–0 loss at home to <a href="/wiki/Empoli_F.C." title="Empoli F.C.">Empoli F.C.</a> in the 11th matchday, and replaced by veteran coach <a href="/wiki/Nedo_Sonetti" title="Nedo Sonetti">Nedo Sonetti</a>.</p>
<p>Ascoli were the first team to be officially relegated to Serie B for the 2007–08 season after a 1–0 loss to <a href="/wiki/Torino_F.C." title="Torino F.C.">Torino F.C.</a> on 7 May 2007. They ended the season in nineteenth place, one point above last-placed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Messina_Peloro" title="F.C. Messina Peloro">Messina</a>.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_national_football_team" title="German national football team">German</a> striker <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Bierhoff" title="Oliver Bierhoff">Oliver Bierhoff</a> began his experience in Italian football with Ascoli.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Success">Success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ascoli_Picchio_F.C._1898&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Even though from a small city of 50,000 people, Ascoli have been able to punch above their weight. Ascoli have competed 16 seasons in Serie A since 1974, with an incredible 5th and 6th spot finish respectively in the seasons of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_A_1979-80" title="Serie A 1979-80">1979–80</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_A_1981-82" title="Serie A 1981-82">1981–82</a>.</p>
<p>To complement Ascoli's success in Serie A, the club has also achieved 2 Cadetti (Serie B Championships) in 1977–78, and 1985–86. The 1977–78 season yielded a record championship with achieving 61 points (2 points for a victory). Ascoli have competed in Serie B 13 times, with a total of 5 promotion seasons.</p>
<p>Ascoli have made way to Serie B with only two winning seasons in Serie C and C1B. These were in 1971–72, and 2001–02.</p>
<p>Another one of Ascoli's successes is the fact for a city of 50,000, their record home attendance has reached 36,500 more than 70% than the cities population. In Serie A this was seen against Juventus and the famous season in 1982–83 where Ascoli saved themselves from relegation in the last game of the Serie A season against Cagliari, who were subsequently relegated. However, with stadium restrictions the maximum attendance allowed in the Stadio Cino e Lillo Del Duca is 21,000–24,000. Nonetheless when filled it is still an amazing achievement from such a small city.</p>
<p>Other success were in the Mitropa Cup in 1986–87, the Capodanno Challenge, and the Red Leaf Cup. Ascoli were also finalists in the Anglo-Italia Cup, during Ascoli's last season in Serie B 1994–95, before Ascoli long await in Serie C1 before an emotional return to Serie B for the 2002–03 season.</p>
<p>Under the reign of the great president of Ascoli Calcio – Costantino Rozzi, Ascoli competed 14 of their amazing 16 seasons in Serie A, again another amazing achievement from a club who were once playing in the regional divisions of Marche. Not only this, but Rozzi was able to implement players who were born and raised through the ranks of Ascoli. In the mid to late 1980s saw <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Iachini" title="Giuseppe Iachini">Giuseppe Iachini</a>, Domenico Agostini, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Giuseppe_Carillo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Giuseppe Carillo (page does not exist)">Giuseppe Carillo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_Scarafoni" title="Lorenzo Scarafoni">Lorenzo Scarafoni</a>, help Ascoli ensure a return to Serie A and survival the following season. Once Iachini left Agostini, Carillo and Scarafoni, were first team starters as well as Marino Fioravanti and <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Aloisi" title="Antonio Aloisi">Antonio Aloisi</a> on the bench. Aloisi went on to compete in many season for Ascoli.</p>
<p>Another success is Carlo Mazzone, once at the heart of Del Duca Ascoli's defence playing 9 seasons, led Ascoli for another 10 or so seasons as manager. Mazzone guided Ascoli from Serie C to Serie A, including Ascoli's 6th-placed finish in 1981–82.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bankruptcy_and_re-birth">Bankruptcy and re-birth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ascoli_Picchio_F.C._1898&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bankruptcy and re-birth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Lega_Pro_Prima_Divisione" title="2013–14 Lega Pro Prima Divisione">2013–14 Lega Pro Prima Divisione</a> season, the Court of Ascoli Piceno declared the bankruptcy of the club. The court also estimated the residual value of the club assets were €862,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> A new company, <b>Ascoli Picchio F.C. 1898</b> successfully bid the assets. FIGC also accepted the admission of the new company to the new season without relegation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In summer 2015 Ascoli, originally runners-up to <a href="/wiki/S.S._Teramo_Calcio" title="S.S. Teramo Calcio">Teramo</a> in the new Lega Pro, were declared champions of group B and promoted after Teramo's relegation for involvement in the <a href="/wiki/2015_Italian_football_scandal" title="2015 Italian football scandal">2015 Italian football scandal</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Supporters">Supporters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ascoli_Picchio_F.C._1898&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Supporters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rivalries">Rivalries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ascoli_Picchio_F.C._1898&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Rivalries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The biggest derby in Marche is between Ascoli and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Sambenedettese_1923" title="U.S. Sambenedettese 1923">Sambenedettese</a> (Samb, Samba), although the derby with Ancona is better known. The last time the "derby" was played between Ascoli and Sambenedettese in a league season was in 1986. <a href="/wiki/San_Benedetto_del_Tronto" title="San Benedetto del Tronto">San Benedetto del Tronto</a> is a city in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Ascoli_Piceno" title="Province of Ascoli Piceno">province of Ascoli Piceno</a>, and only 20 minutes from the city of Ascoli Piceno; the derby is fuelled by intense local pride and rivalry on both sides, and remains passionate despite the length of time since it was last played.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Ancona" title="Ancona">Ancona</a> derby was the second largest; <a href="/wiki/History_of_A.C._Ancona#Supporters_and_rivalries" title="History of A.C. Ancona">Ancona</a> and Ascoli have been the two most successful clubs of Marche. This rivalry stems partly from the fact that <a href="/wiki/Ancona" title="Ancona">Ancona</a> is the capital of <a href="/wiki/Marche" title="Marche">Marche</a>, but <a href="/wiki/Ascoli_Piceno" title="Ascoli Piceno">Ascoli</a> (Asculum) was the capital of ancient Picenun. The battles between the Ascolani and Anconetani have been intense, and there is very much a fight to be <i>La Regina Delle Marche</i> (the queen of Marche).</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Fermana" title="U.S. Fermana">Fermana</a> is the final significant derby for Ascoli; both teams are located near each other in the Province of Ascoli Piceno, alongside San Benedetto del Tronto. Despite the fact that Fermana have historically been weaker than Ascoli, this still is a derby for the Ascolani and Fermani, with both supporters not wanting to lose this game.</p>
<p>Other rivalries include <a href="/wiki/A.S._Livorno_Calcio" title="A.S. Livorno Calcio">Livorno</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hellas_Verona_F.C." title="Hellas Verona F.C.">Verona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delfino_Pescara_1936" title="Delfino Pescara 1936">Pescara</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Internazionale_Milano" title="F.C. Internazionale Milano">Inter</a>.</p>
