<p><b>Astara</b>, also known as <b>Azerbaycan Astarasi</b> (<a href="/wiki/Azerbaijani_language" title="Azerbaijani language">Azerbaijani</a>: <span lang="az" xml:lang="az"><i><b>Azərbaycan Astarası</b></i></span>), is a city in and the capital of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astara_Rayon" title="Astara Rayon">Astara Rayon</a> of <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>. Astara is a short walk across the border from <a href="/wiki/Astara,_Iran" title="Astara, Iran">Astara, Iran</a>.</p>

<p>As the demand for television broadcasting and telecommunication rises during the early 1980s, the <a href="/wiki/Astara_TV_Tower" title="Astara TV Tower">Astara TV Tower</a> was built. The television tower was uniquely designed, as its pinnacle is guyed to a horizontal cross-like steel structure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forum_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-forum-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Astara,_Azerbaijan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Located next to the Iranian border, Astara attracts numerous visitors to south of the border, going to Iran to purchase goods and services that may not be as readily available on the Azerbaijani side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Astara,_Azerbaijan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Astara is currently served by a <a href="/wiki/Broad_gauge" title="Broad gauge">broad gauge</a> railway only headed north. A <a href="/wiki/Standard_gauge" title="Standard gauge">standard gauge</a> connection to the Iranian railway network along the shore of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caspian_sea" title="Caspian sea">Caspian sea</a> is planned. This break of gauge station is likely to be equipped with <a href="/wiki/Bogie_exchange" title="Bogie exchange">bogie exchange</a> and <a href="/wiki/SUW_2000" title="SUW 2000">SUW 2000</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Variable_gauge_axles" title="Variable gauge axles">variable gauge axle</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Track_gauge_changing_facility" title="Track gauge changing facility">track gauge changing facility</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
