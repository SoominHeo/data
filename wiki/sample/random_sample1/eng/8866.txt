<p>The <b>Asturian Wikipedia</b> (Before <i>Uiquipedia n'asturianu</i> and now <i>Wikipedia n'asturianu</i>) is the <a href="/wiki/Asturian_language" title="Asturian language">Asturian language</a> edition of Wikipedia <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> started in July 2004. As of December 8, 2015, the Asturian Wikipedia has more than 45,000 <a href="/wiki/Article_(publishing)" title="Article (publishing)">articles</a>, making it the 77-largest Wikipedia. It also has 30,432 registered users, most of them global accounts created automatically, since the monthly active users amount to 94 of which nine are administrators. It has been edited about 999,632 times. The Asturian Wikipedia is classified in the 15th place of Wikipedias by articles per speaker population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-articles_per_pop_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-articles_per_pop-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>After its foundation on July 2004, the Asturian Wikipedia reached 1,000 articles on 1 September 2004.</p>
<p>In 2015, Wikipedia was awarded with the <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Princess_of_Asturias_Award" title="Princess of Asturias Award">Princess of Asturias Award</a> on International Cooperation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Speaking at the Asturian Parliament in Oviedo, the city that hosts the awards ceremony, <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Wales" title="Jimmy Wales">Jimmy Wales</a> praised the work of the <a href="/wiki/Asturian_language" title="Asturian language">Asturian language</a> Wikipedia users.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The night of the ceremony, members of the Wikimedia Foundation held a meeting with Wikipedians from all parts of Spain, including the local Asturian community.</p>
