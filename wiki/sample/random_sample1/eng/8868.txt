<p>An <b>astroid</b> is a particular mathematical <a href="/wiki/Curve" title="Curve">curve</a>: a <a href="/wiki/Hypocycloid" title="Hypocycloid">hypocycloid</a> with four <a href="/wiki/Cusp_(singularity)" title="Cusp (singularity)">cusps</a>. Specifically, it is the locus of a point on a circle as it rolls inside a fixed circle with four times the radius.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> By double generation, it is also the locus of a point on a circle as it rolls inside a fixed circle with 4/3 times the radius. It can also be defined as the <a href="/wiki/Envelope_(mathematics)" title="Envelope (mathematics)">envelope</a> of a line segment with an end point on each of the axes. It is therefore the envelope of the moving bar in the <a href="/wiki/Trammel_of_Archimedes" title="Trammel of Archimedes">Trammel of Archimedes</a>.</p>
<p>Its modern name comes from the <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> word for "<a href="/wiki/Star" title="Star">star</a>". The curve had a variety of names, including <b>tetracuspid</b> (still used), cubocycloid, and paracycle. It is nearly identical in form to the <a href="/wiki/Evolute" title="Evolute">evolute</a> of an ellipse.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Properties">Properties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Astroid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Properties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The astroid has four cusp singularities in the real plane, the points on the star. It has two more complex cusp singularities at infinity, and four complex double points, for a total of ten singularities.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Pl%C3%BCcker_formula" title="Plücker formula">dual curve</a> to the astroid is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cruciform_curve" title="Cruciform curve">cruciform curve</a> with equation <span><span class="mwe-math-mathml-inline mwe-math-mathml-a11y" style="display: none;"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">
