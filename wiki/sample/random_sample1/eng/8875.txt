<p>The <b>Asia–Europe Meeting</b> (<b>ASEM</b>) is an exclusively Asian–European forum to enhance relations and various forms of cooperation between the then 15 members of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> (EU) and its Commission, the then 7 members of the <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Southeast_Asian_Nations" title="Association of Southeast Asian Nations">Association of Southeast Asian Nations</a> (ASEAN), and the individual countries of <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It was officially established on 1 March 1996 at the first summit in <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>. A series of enlargements saw additional EU members join as well as <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ASEAN" title="ASEAN">ASEAN Secretariat</a> in 2008,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Federation" title="Russian Federation">Russian Federation</a> in 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> in 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a> in 2014.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The main components of the ASEM process rest on the following 3 pillars:</p>
<ul>
<li>Political Pillar</li>
<li>Economic Pillar</li>
<li>Social, Cultural &amp; Educational Pillar</li>
</ul>
<p>In general, the process is considered by the parties involved to be a way of deepening the relations between <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> at all levels, which is deemed necessary to achieve a more balanced political and economic world order. The process is enhanced by the biennial meetings of Heads of State and Government, alternately in Europe and Asia, and biennial meetings of Foreign Ministers as well as political, economic, and socio-cultural meetings and events at various levels.</p>
