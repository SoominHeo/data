<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Asia-Pacific Fishery Commission</h1>

<p>The <b>Asia-Pacific Fishery Commission</b> (<b>APFIC)</b>, originally called the <b>Indo-Pacific Fisheries Council</b> (<b>IPFC</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Food_and_Agriculture_Organization" title="Food and Agriculture Organization">Food and Agriculture Organization</a> (FAO) Article XIV Regional Fisheries Body which covers <a href="/wiki/Fishery" title="Fishery">fisheries</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aquaculture" title="Aquaculture">aquaculture</a> and related aquatic resource issues in the <a href="/wiki/Asia-Pacific" title="Asia-Pacific">Asia-Pacific</a> region. APFIC functions as a Regional Consultative Forum raising awareness amongst member countries, fisheries organizations and fisheries professionals in the Asia-Pacific region.</p>
<p>In recent years, APFIC has covered a range of regional fisheries issues, including co-management of fisheries, low value/trash fish (may be referred to as <a href="/wiki/Bycatch" title="Bycatch">bycatch</a> where not targeted catch) in the region, <a href="/wiki/Illegal,_unreported_and_unregulated_fishing" title="Illegal, unreported and unregulated fishing">illegal, unreported and unregulated fishing</a> (IUU) and <a class="external text" href="http://www.fao.org/fishery/topic/14856/en" rel="nofollow">fishing capacity</a> management,certification in fisheries (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Ecolabel" title="Ecolabel">ecolabel</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Aquaculture" title="Aquaculture">aquaculture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem_approach" title="Ecosystem approach">ecosystem approach</a> to fisheries and aquaculture and improving resilience of fishery livelihoods. Most recently work has focussed on developing a training course for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecosystem_Approach" title="Ecosystem Approach">Ecosystem Approach</a> to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fishery_Management&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fishery Management (page does not exist)">Fishery Management</a> and guidelines for tropical <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trawl" title="Trawl">trawl</a> <a href="/wiki/Fisheries_management" title="Fisheries management">fisheries management</a>.</p>
<p>The Secretariat is housed in the FAO Regional Office for Asia and the Pacific in <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Country_membership">Country membership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asia-Pacific_Fishery_Commission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Country membership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <b>Agreement on the Establishment of the Asia-Pacific Fishery Commission</b>, eligibility for membership is based on:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Member Nations and Associate Members of the <a href="/wiki/Food_and_Agriculture_Organization" title="Food and Agriculture Organization">Food and Agriculture Organization</a> (FAO) which accept the Agreement in accordance with Article X thereof. Other States that are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_United_Nations_member_states" title="List of United Nations member states">Members of the United Nations</a>, any of its <a href="/wiki/United_Nations#Specialized_Organizations" title="United Nations">Specialized Agencies</a> or the <a href="/wiki/International_Atomic_Energy_Agency" title="International Atomic Energy Agency">International Atomic Energy Agency</a> may be admitted as members by a two-thirds majority of the Commission's members.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The current member countries are:<br/>
<a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cambodia" title="Cambodia">Cambodia</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea" title="Republic of Korea">Republic of Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">Myanmar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Timor_Leste" title="Timor Leste">Timor Leste</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a></p>
<p>A former member state is the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="APFIC_area_of_competence">APFIC area of competence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asia-Pacific_Fishery_Commission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: APFIC area of competence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Article VI of the APFIC Agreement the 'APFIC area' is described as:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Area - The Commission shall carry out the functions and responsibilities set forth in Article IV in the Asia-Pacific Area.</p>
</blockquote>
<p><b>Comment</b></p>
<p>This description is a broad definition of the area where APFIC will conduct its work. APFIC is competent in both marine and inland waters of its area of competence. The <a class="external text" href="http://www.fao.org/docrep/005/x6950e/x6950e0g.htm" rel="nofollow">abolition of the Indian Ocean Fishery Commission (IOFC)</a> and its Committee for the Development and Management of Fisheries in the Bay of Bengal (BOBC) in June 1999 resulted in closer involvement of APFIC in this sub-region, as the functions of BOBC was transferred to APFIC by the FAO Council’s Resolution 1/116 (<a class="external text" href="http://www.fao.org/docrep/meeting/X1198e.htm" rel="nofollow">Report of the 116th Session of the FAO Council</a>).This was subsequently endorsed by the <a class="external text" href="http://www.apfic.org/session-meetings-1/reportsessions/finish/13-reports-of-the-commission-sessions/202-1998-report-of-the-twenty-sixth-session-of-the-asia-pacific-fishery-commission-apfic.html" rel="nofollow">26th Session of APFIC</a> in Beijing 1998.</p>
<p>There is no change to the APFIC agreement in terms of membership, area of competence and functions of the Commission, there have been subsequent recommendations by the Commission to clarify where its focus of work should lie. The <a class="external text" href="http://www.apfic.org/session-meetings-1/reportsessions/finish/13-reports-of-the-commission-sessions/205-1999-report-of-the-apfic-special-session-rome-italy-17-february-1999-report-of-the-ad-hoc-legal-and-financial-working-group.html" rel="nofollow">Report APFIC Ad hoc Legal and Financial Working Group</a> proposed that APFIC concentrate its activities on three subregions of the Asia-Pacific:</p>
<p>• the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Sea" title="Yellow Sea">Yellow Sea</a> and its adjacent waters<br/>
• the <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a> and its adjacent waters and<br/>
• the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">Bay of Bengal</a>.</p>
<p>As APFIC adapts to the changing needs of fisheries and aquaculture in the Asia-Pacific, its role and function have been clarified as part of the development of a Strategic Plan for the Commission.</p>
