<p><b>Asia Pacific Center for Theoretical Physics</b> (APCTP) is an international research institute for physical sciences. It is located in the Campus of the <a href="/wiki/Pohang_University_of_Science_and_Technology" title="Pohang University of Science and Technology">Pohang University of Science and Technology</a> (POSTECH), in Pohang, South Korea. The centre was founded in 1996 by Nobel Laureate <a href="/wiki/Chen-Ning_Yang" title="Chen-Ning Yang">Chen-Ning Yang</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure_and_history">Structure and history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asia_Pacific_Center_for_Theoretical_Physics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure and history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Asia Pacific Theoretical Physics Center is theoretical physics research leading, international joint research programs, in the Asia-Pacific region. APCTP was founded in June 1996 as an international non-governmental organizations, with current member states: <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea" title="Republic of Korea">Republic of Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Philippines" title="The Philippines">the Philippines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a> (15 countries). Asia Pacific Center for Theoretical Physics is a close-knit, multi-disciplinary research environment that hosts scientists working on challenging problems at the forefront of <a href="/wiki/Biophysics" title="Biophysics">biophysics</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Condensed_matter" title="Condensed matter">condensed matter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quantum_information" title="Quantum information">quantum information</a>, <a href="/wiki/Astrophysics" title="Astrophysics">astrophysics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cosmology" title="Cosmology">cosmology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Particle_physics" title="Particle physics">particle physics</a>. The institute also plays a key role in Korea by inviting international scholars, acting as a conduit for collaboration through focus workshops and training young scientists. APCTP, whose previous presidents include Nobel Laureates C. N. Yang and <a href="/wiki/Robert_B._Laughlin" title="Robert B. Laughlin">Robert B. Laughlin</a>, has a rich history and celebrates its 20th year in 2016. In 2008 APCTP established Junior Research Groups (JRG) under a previous president, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Fulde" title="Peter Fulde">Peter Fulde</a>, in collaboration with the <a href="/wiki/Max_Planck_Society" title="Max Planck Society">Max Planck Society</a> in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, as a means to provide gifted young scientists with their first opportunity of managing research. The institute currently supports eight JRG groups.</p>
<p>APCTP is located at the Pohang University for Science and Technology (POSTECH), which in addition to possessing established departments in both mathematics and the natural sciences, ranks globally within the top 100 universities. Moreover, the boutique campus currently boasts the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=POSTECH-Max_Planck_center_for_quantum_materials&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="POSTECH-Max Planck center for quantum materials (page does not exist)">POSTECH-Max Planck center for quantum materials</a>, as well as four centers from the Korean government flagship <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Institute_for_Basic_Science_(IBS)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Institute for Basic Science (IBS) (page does not exist)">Institute for Basic Science (IBS)</a> program, spanning low-dimensional electronic systems, immunology, self-assembly and notably, the only mathematics IBS center to date.</p>
