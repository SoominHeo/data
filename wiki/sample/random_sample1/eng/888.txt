<p><b>CoKu Tau/4</b> is a pre-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Main-sequence" title="Main-sequence">main-sequence</a> <a href="/wiki/Binary_star" title="Binary star">binary</a> <a href="/wiki/T_Tauri_star" title="T Tauri star">T Tauri star</a> system in the constellation <a href="/wiki/Taurus_(constellation)" title="Taurus (constellation)">Taurus</a>. The stars are surrounded by a <a href="/wiki/Circumstellar_disc" title="Circumstellar disc">circumbinary disc</a> with a central cavity of radius 10 <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_unit" title="Astronomical unit">astronomical units</a>. Before its binary nature was known, the central cavity in the system's disc was thought to have been cleared out by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extrasolar_planet" title="Extrasolar planet">planet</a> of at least 10 <a href="/wiki/Jupiter_mass" title="Jupiter mass">Jupiter masses</a>, a rare example of a so-called "transitional disc". This model was disproven in 2008 when the star was resolved using <a href="/wiki/Adaptive_optics" title="Adaptive optics">adaptive optics</a> as a system of two near-equal-mass stars with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Projected_separation" title="Projected separation">projected separation</a> of 8 AU. The central cavity is thus cleared out by the stars, not by the gravitational influence of a planet.</p>
