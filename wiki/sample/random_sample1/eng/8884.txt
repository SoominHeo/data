<p>The <b>FESPIC Games</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> or the <b>Far East and South Pacific Games for the Disabled</b>, was a multi-sports games in Asia and the South Pacific region.</p>
<p>The FESPIC Games, which started in 1975, was held nine times and bowed out a success<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> in December 2006, at the conclusion of the 9th FESPIC Games<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur" title="Kuala Lumpur">Kuala Lumpur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>.</p>
<p>For Asian countries, the FESPIC Games was replaced by the <a href="/wiki/Asian_Para_Games" title="Asian Para Games">Asian Para Games</a> starting with the inaugural <a href="/wiki/2010_Asian_Para_Games" title="2010 Asian Para Games">1st Asian Para Games</a> in 2010 in <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou" title="Guangzhou">Guangzhou</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> after the <a href="/wiki/2010_Asian_Games" title="2010 Asian Games">16th Asian Games</a>. The Asian Para Games, the parallel event for athletes with physical disabilities, is a <a href="/wiki/Paralympic_sports" title="Paralympic sports">multi-sport event</a> held every four years after every <a href="/wiki/Asian_Games" title="Asian Games">Asian Games</a>. The FESPIC and Asian Games had adopted the strategy used by the Olympic and Paralympic Games of having both games in the same city.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Objectives">Objectives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FESPIC_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Objectives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>To promote general interest and welfare for the disabled in the region through participation in sports events and other activities</li>
<li>To deepen mutual understanding and friendship of the disabled</li>
<li>To promote rehabilitation for the disabled in the region through sports activities</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FESPIC_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first FESPIC Games was held in <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cita,_%C5%8Cita" title="Ōita, Ōita">Oita</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> in 1975. There were limited opportunities for persons with disabilities in Asia and the Pacific to participate in sports at the time. The FESPIC Games was launched to address this issue and promote understanding toward disabled persons in each country, as well as improve their social welfare.</p>
<p>The number of participant countries increased during FESPIC history. Central Asian countries <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kyrgyzstan" title="Kyrgyzstan">Kyrgyzstan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkmenistan" title="Turkmenistan">Turkmenistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tajikistan" title="Tajikistan">Tajikistan</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>, first participated in the 7th FESPIC Games in 1999. The Middle Eastern countries were allowed to compete in the 9th FESPIC Games in 2006, along with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Timor-Leste" title="Timor-Leste">Timor-Leste</a>. Middle East countries were previously not allowed to compete in FESPIC Games from 1975 to 2002 because these countries were Africa/Middle Eastern members.</p>

<p>The FESPIC Games later on became the springboard for athletes from Asia and the Pacific to dream big and to reach the <a href="/wiki/Paralympic_Games" title="Paralympic Games">Paralympic Games</a>. Disabled sailing in Asia is one such sport. Teams from <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> started in the 2006 FESPIC Games and consistently performed to <a href="/wiki/Sailing_at_the_2008_Summer_Paralympics" title="Sailing at the 2008 Summer Paralympics">sail</a> in the <a class="external text" href="http://en.paralympic.beijing2008.cn/photo/sports/sailing/" rel="nofollow">2008 Beijing Paralympics</a>, with Australian Rachel Cox and her helmsman winning the Silver<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/SKUD_18#The_SKUD_in_the_2008_Paralympics" title="SKUD 18">2-person Keelboat</a> event.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"As participation in disabled sailing grows in that area of the world, the event is being considered as a developmental competition and will be sailed in the Access Liberty, of which 18 will be supplied at the event for competitors entering. ... As a development sport at the event, and as disabled sailing is still in a developing form in FESPIC countries, the competition will be open to any sailor who is able to be classified under the IFDS Functional Classification System.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/2006_FESPIC_Games" title="2006 FESPIC Games">9th FESPIC Games</a> was the last and final edition of the series which took place in 2006. For Asian countries, the FESPIC Games was replaced by the <a href="/wiki/Asian_Para_Games" title="Asian Para Games">Asian Para Games</a>, starting with the inaugural <a href="/wiki/2010_Asian_Para_Games" title="2010 Asian Para Games">2010 Asian Para Games</a> which was held in 2010 <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou" title="Guangzhou">Guangzhou</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/16th_Asian_Games" title="16th Asian Games">16th Asian Games</a>.</p>
