<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Asian Science Camp</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>Asian Science Camp (ASC)</b> is an annual forum for pre-<a href="/wiki/College" title="College">collegiate</a> and college students which aims at promoting discussion and cooperation among <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asian</a> students for the betterment of science in the Asian region. The first ASC was held at <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei</a> in 2007, and was subsequently held at <a href="/wiki/Bali" title="Bali">Bali</a> (2008), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tsukuba" title="Tsukuba">Tsukuba (Japan)</a> (2009), <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a> (2010), <a href="/wiki/Daejeon" title="Daejeon">Daejeon (South Korea)</a> (2011), Hebrew University of Jerusalem (<a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>) (2012). This idea of an annual camp was co-proposed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuan_Tseh_Lee" title="Yuan Tseh Lee">Yuan Tseh Lee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Masatoshi_Koshiba" title="Masatoshi Koshiba">Masatoshi Koshiba</a> at the 2005 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nobel_Laureate_Meetings_at_Lindau" title="Nobel Laureate Meetings at Lindau">Nobel Laureate Meetings at Lindau</a>. The ASC is modeled after the Lindau meetings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hbcse_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-hbcse-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-asc2011about_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-asc2011about-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-asc2012_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-asc2012-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asian_Science_Camp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The idea of the Asian Science Camp<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> was co-proposed in September 2005 after the 55th Annual meeting of Nobel Laureates and Students in Lindau, Germany, by Professor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuan_Tseh_Lee" title="Yuan Tseh Lee">Yuan Tseh Lee</a> (1986 Nobel Laureate in Chemistry) from Chinese Taipei, and Professor <a href="/wiki/Masatoshi_Koshiba" title="Masatoshi Koshiba">Masatoshi Koshiba</a> (2002 Nobel Laureate in Physics) from <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. The proposal expressed the aim to enlighten science talented youths through discussions and dialogues with top scholars in the world, and promoting international friendship and cooperation among best young students of the next generation in Asia. The Lindau Meetings will serve as a model. The Asian Science Camp would invite a dozen Nobel Laureates or world-distinguished scientists as speakers and devise an interesting program to attract all the participants, including plenary sessions, round table discussions, student master dialogues, a creative poster competition, social events and excursion. The working language would be English. This proposal was soon discussed in a board meeting of the Wu Chien-Shiung Education Foundation and was approved unanimously by the board.</p>
<p>The governing body of the Asian Science Camp is the International Board of Asian Science Camp (IBASC). IBASC is a not- for- profit organization, consisting of non-governmental educational institutions. The 2007 Asian Science Camp was organized by the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Wu_Chien-Shiung_Education_Foundation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Wu Chien-Shiung Education Foundation (page does not exist)">Wu Chien-Shiung Education Foundation</a> with the assistance of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academia_Sinica_in_Taiwan" title="Academia Sinica in Taiwan">Academia Sinica in Taiwan</a>. This non-government Foundation was established in 1995 to commemorate the monumental contributions of Madame Dr. Wu Chien-Shiung to physics and her lifelong love for science education. Since its inauguration in Taiwan in August 2007, the Asian Science Camp has become an International annual event in Asia. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sixth_Asian_Science_Camp">Sixth Asian Science Camp</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asian_Science_Camp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sixth Asian Science Camp">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sixth_Asian_Science_Camp" title="Sixth Asian Science Camp">Sixth Asian Science Camp</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Sixth_Asian_Science_Camp" title="Sixth Asian Science Camp">Sixth Asian Science Camp</a> hosted by <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> took place in the Hebrew University Safra Campus <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a> in 2012 was, by then, the largest event in terms of the number of students from when the project first began. Approximately 300 students, 5 Nobel Prize recipients, and 20 leading experts attended the event.</p>
