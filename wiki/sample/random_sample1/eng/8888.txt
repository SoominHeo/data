<p>The <b>1971 Asian Club Championship</b> was the fourth edition of the annual Asian club football competition hosted by <a href="/wiki/Asian_Football_Confederation" title="Asian Football Confederation">Asian Football Confederation</a>. Eight clubs from eight countries competed in the tournament. The tournament was held in <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a> from 21 March to 2 April. Eight clubs were split in two groups of four and the group winners and the runners-up advanced to semifinals.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Maccabi_Tel_Aviv_F.C." title="Maccabi Tel Aviv F.C.">Maccabi Tel Aviv F.C.</a> (ISR) recorded their second Asian title after <a href="/wiki/Al-Shorta_SC" title="Al-Shorta SC">Al-Shorta</a> (IRQ) refused to play against them in protest at the <a href="/wiki/Palestinian_territories" title="Palestinian territories">Israeli occupation of Palestine</a>. Al-Shorta players instead waved the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flag_of_Palestine" title="Flag of Palestine">Palestinian flag</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Iraq" title="Flag of Iraq">Iraqi flag</a> around the field, and journalists at the game described a "storm of applause" from the Thai fans in the stadium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AFCCL_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-AFCCL-1">[1]</a></sup> They were regarded as the winners of the tournament in the Iraqi media (the <i>Al-Mal'ab</i> newspaper headline the following day read: "The Champions of Asia Return to Baghdad") and were greeted with a heroes' reception upon their return to the country. Israel were expelled from the AFC three years later (Maccabi now compete in <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a> competitions), leading to Al-Shorta being widely regarded by people across Asia as the winners of the tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Al-Mal.27ab_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Al-Mal.27ab-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
