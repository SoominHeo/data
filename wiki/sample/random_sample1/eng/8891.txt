<p>The <b>Asian Leadership Conference (ALC)</b> is an annual international conference hosted in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, by the <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_ChosunIlbo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The ChosunIlbo (page does not exist)">The ChosunIlbo</a></i>, a major Korean daily newspaper. The inaugural conference was in March 2005. The conference addresses important issues in Asia and the world. The conference has hosted over one hundred and fifty speakers and over one thousand guests.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin">Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asian_Leadership_Conference&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The inaugural conference was convened after the <a href="/wiki/2004_Indian_Ocean_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2004 Indian Ocean earthquake and tsunami">2004 Indian Ocean tsunami</a> with the theme, <i>Cooperation and Rebuilding after the Tsunami</i>. <i>The ChosunIlbo</i> proposed that <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, after overcoming extreme poverty and political turmoil, should take a leading role in the post <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">tsunami</a> period. Korea needed to be a model and provide the vision for future development. While the conference was initially a forum for Asian issues, it developed into an international event finding commonality in political, economic, and social topics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2015">2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asian_Leadership_Conference&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2015">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The conference of 19 to 20 May 2015 was held at the Shilla Hotel, Seoul, Korea. The main theme was <i>Commemorating the Past, Celebrating the Future</i> referring to the 70th anniversary of the end of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> and the 70th anniversary of Korean independence. The agenda included: the imperative of Korean unification with reference to economic development projects such as the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_Tumen_Initiative&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Greater Tumen Initiative (page does not exist)">Greater Tumen Initiative</a>; efficient global aid giving; equality for women in business; models of innovative <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internet_technology" title="Internet technology">internet technology</a> business models such as <a href="/wiki/Sharing_economy" title="Sharing economy">sharing economy</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FinTech" title="FinTech">FinTech</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Big_Data" title="Big Data">Big Data</a> marketing; and the fusion of the culture of Korean business, known as <i>K culture</i> with that of other businesses. Keynote speakers included: <a href="/wiki/Narendra_Modi" title="Narendra Modi">Narendra Modi</a>, Prime Minister of India , <a href="/wiki/Ban_Ki-moon" title="Ban Ki-moon">Ban Ki-moon</a>, Secretary-General of the United Nations , <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Hagel" title="Chuck Hagel">Chuck Hagel</a>, Former US Secretary of Defense, <a href="/wiki/Horst_K%C3%B6hler" title="Horst Köhler">Horst Köhler</a>, Former President of the Federal Republic of Germany, <a href="/wiki/Tang_Jiaxuan" title="Tang Jiaxuan">Tang Jiaxuan</a>, Former State Councilor of the People's Republic of China, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=H.H._Sheikha_Moza_bint_Nasser&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="H.H. Sheikha Moza bint Nasser (page does not exist)">H.H. Sheikha Moza bint Nasser</a>, Chairperson of Education Above All, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Murayama_Tomiichi" title="Murayama Tomiichi">Murayama Tomiichi</a>, Former Prime Minister of Japan, <a href="/wiki/Jack_Ma" title="Jack Ma">Jack Ma</a>, Founder &amp; Executive Chairman of Alibaba Group.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2014">2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asian_Leadership_Conference&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2014">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The conference of March 2014, was titled <i>One Korea, New Asia</i>. It examined Asian regional security, the integration of the Koreas and growth in Korea. The conference agenda included: <i>Envisioning a Unified Korea</i>, <i>Asia’s Turning Point</i>, <i>New Order in the Asia-Pacific</i> and <i>Seeking a New Engine for Creative Growth</i>. In 2014, <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> attended. The keynote speakers were: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Park_Geun-Hye" title="Park Geun-Hye">Park Geun-Hye</a>, President of the Republic of Korea, <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a>, <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hatoyama_Yukio" title="Hatoyama Yukio">Hatoyama Yukio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Japan" title="Prime Minister of Japan">Prime Minister of Japan</a>, *<a href="/wiki/Goh_Chok_Tong" title="Goh Chok Tong">Goh Chok Tong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Singapore" title="Prime Minister of Singapore">Prime Minister of Singapore</a>. <a href="/wiki/Julia_Gillard" title="Julia Gillard">Julia Gillard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Australia" title="Prime Minister of Australia">Prime Minister of Australia</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lothar_De_Maiz%C3%88re&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lothar De MaizÈre (page does not exist)">Lothar De MaizÈre</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_East_Germany" title="Prime Minister of East Germany">Prime Minister of East Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Surin_Pitsuwan" title="Surin Pitsuwan">Surin Pitsuwan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secretary_General_of_ASEAN" title="Secretary General of ASEAN">Secretary General of ASEAN</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leon_Panetta" title="Leon Panetta">Leon Panetta</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secretary_of_Defense_of_the_United_States" title="Secretary of Defense of the United States">Secretary of Defense of the United States</a>.</p>

<p>ALC 2013 was convened on a background of turmoil from the <a href="/wiki/Financial_crisis_of_2007%E2%80%932008" title="Financial crisis of 2007–2008">2008 global financial crisis</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_sovereign-debt_crisis" title="European sovereign-debt crisis">Eurozone crisis</a>. ALC 2013 kept the "tab-ference" initiative that began with the ALC 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> ALC 2013 also continued its partnership with Table for 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-continue_table_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-continue_table-3">[3]</a></sup> One percent of registration fees were to be donated to the <a href="/wiki/International_Vaccine_Institute" title="International Vaccine Institute">International Vaccine Institute</a> to help treat cholera.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-continue_table_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-continue_table-3">[3]</a></sup> The agenda for the conference included: <i>Asia as the Next Solution</i>; <i>In Search of a New Growth Model for Asia: The Case of the Republic of Korea</i>; <i>Path to Good Growth</i> and <i>Preparing for the Asian Century</i>. The keynote speakers were <a href="/wiki/Colin_Powell" title="Colin Powell">Colin Powell</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Secretary_of_State" title="US Secretary of State">US Secretary of State</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yasuo_Fukuda" title="Yasuo Fukuda">Yasuo Fukuda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Japan" title="Prime Minister of Japan">Prime Minister of Japan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tarja_Halonen" title="Tarja Halonen">Tarja Halonen</a>, <a href="/wiki/President_of_Finland" title="President of Finland">President of Finland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Pellerin" title="Fleur Pellerin">Fleur Pellerin</a>, French junior minister for small and medium enterprises, innovation and the digital economy at the Ministry for Productive Renewal; <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2012">2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asian_Leadership_Conference&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2012">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>ALC 2012 was held from 6 to 7 March 2012 at the Shilla Hotel, Seoul. The main theme of the conference was <i>Capitalism 4.0: Searching for Post Crisis Solutions</i>. Over 40 speakers and 900 guests attended. Discussion focussed on topics such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Global_financial_crisis" title="Global financial crisis">global financial crisis</a>, social welfare, and CSR. ALC 2012 was the world's first <i>tab-ference</i>, a paper-free conference conducted entirely through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tablet_PC" title="Tablet PC">tablet PC</a>.</p>
<p>ALC 2012 was a joint venture with <i>Table for 2</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> an international entity that seeks to reduce <a href="/wiki/Obesity" title="Obesity">obesity</a> in developed countries and malnutrition in third world countries through a <i>calorie transfer</i> program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The Shilla hotel, which was catering the conference, reduced the meal to ten to fifteen grams of food per serving (equivalent to 500 calories) for approximately one US dollar per serve. Money saved on the conference meal was donated to feed children in developing countries.</p>
<p>The conference also featured an open session for university students called the <i>Chosun Talk</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chosun_talk_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-chosun_talk-7">[7]</a></sup> One hundred university students were chosen through an essay competition at the official conference website. They were invited to the talks of four speakers including PyeongChang's 2018 Winter Olympics bidding committee spokesperson, Theresa Rah; the founder and executive director of Table for Two, Masa Kogure, <i><a href="/wiki/Wired_(magazine)" title="Wired (magazine)">Wired</a></i> senior writer <a href="/wiki/Steven_Levy" title="Steven Levy">Steven Levy</a>, and the president and chief executive officer of <a href="/wiki/Viacom_International_Media_Networks" title="Viacom International Media Networks">Viacom International Media Networks</a>, Robert Bakish. Each speaker spent approximately twenty-five minutes giving motivational speeches and answering students’ questions.</p>
<p>The 2012 key note speakers were: <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6ran_Persson" title="Göran Persson">Göran Persson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Sweden" title="Prime Minister of Sweden">Prime Minister of Sweden</a>. <a href="/wiki/Thaksin_Shinawatra" title="Thaksin Shinawatra">Thaksin Shinawatra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Thailand" title="Prime Minister of Thailand">Prime Minister of Thailand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stephen_A._Schwarzman" title="Stephen A. Schwarzman">Stephen A. Schwarzman</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blackstone_Group" title="Blackstone Group">Blackstone Group</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anatole_Kaletsky" title="Anatole Kaletsky">Anatole Kaletsky</a>, the author of <i>Capitalism 4.0</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2008">2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asian_Leadership_Conference&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The ALC 2008 was held from 21 to 22 March 2008 at the Shilla Hotel, Seoul, South Korea. The main theme of the conference was <i>Leadership and Change</i>. Over 35 speakers and 500 guests attended. Fundamental changes in international systems and the need for new leadership that embraced regional and multilateral perspectives were discussed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/President_of_South_Korea" title="President of South Korea">President of South Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Myung-bak" title="Lee Myung-bak">Lee Myung-bak</a>, the president elect, gave the welcome speech.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The keynote speakers were: <a href="/wiki/Henry_Kissinger" title="Henry Kissinger">Henry Kissinger</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Secretary_of_State" title="US Secretary of State">US Secretary of State</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Keating" title="Paul Keating">Paul Keating</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Australia" title="Prime Minister of Australia">Prime Minister of Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Ghosn" title="Carlos Ghosn">Carlos Ghosn</a>, president and chief executive officer of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renault-Nissan_Alliance" title="Renault-Nissan Alliance">Renault-Nissan Alliance</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jim_Rogers" title="Jim Rogers">Jim Rogers</a>, co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Quantum_Group_of_Funds" title="Quantum Group of Funds">Quantum Group of Funds</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2005">2005</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asian_Leadership_Conference&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2005">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ALC 2005 was held from 3 to 4 March 2005 at the <a class="extiw" href="https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/%EC%BD%94%EC%97%91%EC%8A%A4" title="ko:코엑스">COEX</a> Grand Ballroom, Seoul, South Korea. It was the inaugural ALC and was titled "Cooperation and Rebuilding after the Tsunami". The conference was held on the 85th anniversary of the first publication newspaper, <i>Chosun</i> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/%EC%A1%B0%EC%84%A0%EC%9D%BC%EB%B3%B4" title="ko:조선일보">ko:조선일보</a>). Its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keynote_speaker" title="Keynote speaker">keynote speakers</a> were: <a href="/wiki/Megawati_Sukarnoputri" title="Megawati Sukarnoputri">Megawati Sukarnoputri</a>, <a href="/wiki/President_of_Indonesia" title="President of Indonesia">President of Indonesia</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mahathir_Mohammad" title="Mahathir Mohammad">Mahathir Mohammad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Malaysia" title="Prime Minister of Malaysia">Prime Minister of Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ong_Keng_Yong" title="Ong Keng Yong">Ong Keng Yong</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Secretaries-General_of_the_Association_of_Southeast_Asian_Nations" title="List of Secretaries-General of the Association of Southeast Asian Nations">ASEAN Secretary General</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nakasone_Yasuhiro" title="Nakasone Yasuhiro">Nakasone Yasuhiro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Japan" title="Prime Minister of Japan">Prime Minister of Japan</a>.</p>
