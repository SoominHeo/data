<p><b>Asyut</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[spelling 1]</a></sup> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">أسيوط</span>‎‎ <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Arabic transliteration">Asyūṭ </span></i> <small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Arabic" title="Help:IPA for Arabic">[ʔɑsˈjuːtˤ]</a></span>, <a href="/wiki/Coptic_language" title="Coptic language">Coptic</a>: <span class="script-coptic" style="font-family:'FreeSerif', 'New Athena Unicode', 'MPH 2B Damase', 'Arial Coptic', Quivira, Analecta, Antinoou, 'Sophia Nubian', 'Noto Sans Coptic', 'Segoe UI Historic', 'Segoe UI Symbol';">ⲥⲓⲟⲟⲩⲧ</span> <i>Siowt</i>) is the capital of the modern <a href="/wiki/Asyut_Governorate" title="Asyut Governorate">Asyut Governorate</a> in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, which has one of the largest Coptic Catholic bishopric churches in the country; the ancient city of the same name, which is situated nearby. The modern city is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Asyut&amp;params=27_11_00_N_31_10_00_E_type:landmark"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">27°11′00″N</span> <span class="longitude">31°10′00″E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">27.18333°N 31.16667°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">27.18333; 31.16667</span></span></span></a></span>, while the ancient city is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Asyut&amp;params=27_10_00_N_31_08_00_E_type:landmark"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">27°10′00″N</span> <span class="longitude">31°08′00″E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">27.16667°N 31.13333°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">27.16667; 31.13333</span></span></span></a></span>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asyut&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Names_and_Etymology">Names and Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asyut&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Names and Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The name of the city is derived from <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_language" title="Egyptian language">early Egyptian</a> <b>Zawty</b> (<i>Z3JW.TJ</i>) (late Egyptian, <b>Səyáwt</b>) adopted into the <a href="/wiki/Coptic_language" title="Coptic language">Coptic</a> as <b>Syowt</b> <span class="script-coptic" style="font-family:'FreeSerif', 'New Athena Unicode', 'MPH 2B Damase', 'Arial Coptic', Quivira, Analecta, Antinoou, 'Sophia Nubian', 'Noto Sans Coptic', 'Segoe UI Historic', 'Segoe UI Symbol';">ⲥⲓⲟⲟⲩⲧ</span>. In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graeco-Roman" title="Graeco-Roman">Graeco-Roman</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%86gyptus" title="Ægyptus">Egypt</a>, it was called <b>Lycopolis</b> or <b>Lykopolis</b> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el" xml:lang="el">Λυκόπολις</span>, "<span lang="grc" xml:lang="grc">ἡ Λύκων πόλις</span>"),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[2]</a></sup> ('wolf city') <b>Lycon</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[3]</a></sup> or <b>Lyco</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ancient_Asyut">Ancient Asyut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asyut&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ancient Asyut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Yuny_and_Renenutet.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2400" data-file-width="1800" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Yuny_and_Renenutet.jpg/220px-Yuny_and_Renenutet.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Yuny_and_Renenutet.jpg/330px-Yuny_and_Renenutet.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Yuny_and_Renenutet.jpg/440px-Yuny_and_Renenutet.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Yuny_and_Renenutet.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Statue of the chief royal scribe <a href="/wiki/Yuny" title="Yuny">Yuny</a> of Asyut and his wife Renenutet, 1290–1270 BCE early Dynasty 19.</div>

<p>Ancient Asyut was the capital of the Thirteenth <a href="/wiki/Nome_(Egypt)" title="Nome (Egypt)">Nome</a> of <a href="/wiki/Upper_Egypt" title="Upper Egypt">Upper Egypt</a> (<i>Lycopolites Nome</i>) around 3100 BC. It was located on the western bank of the <a href="/wiki/Nile" title="Nile">Nile</a>. The two most prominent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Egyptian_pantheon" title="Egyptian pantheon">gods</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Ancient Egyptian</a> Asyut were <a href="/wiki/Anubis" title="Anubis">Anubis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wepwawet" title="Wepwawet">Wepwawet</a>, both funerary deities.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/First_Intermediate_Period_of_Egypt" title="First Intermediate Period of Egypt">First Intermediate Period</a>, the rulers of "<i>Zawty</i>" (<a href="/wiki/Khety_I_(nomarch)" title="Khety I (nomarch)">Khety I</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tefibi" title="Tefibi">Tefibi</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Khety_II_(nomarch)" title="Khety II (nomarch)">Khety II</a>) were supporters of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Herakleopolis_Magna" title="Herakleopolis Magna">Herakleopolitan</a> kings, of whose domain the Nome formed the southern limits. The conflict between this Nome and the southern Nomes under the rule of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eleventh_dynasty_of_Egypt" title="Eleventh dynasty of Egypt">Eleventh dynasty</a> ended with the victory of <a href="/wiki/Thebes,_Egypt" title="Thebes, Egypt">Thebes</a> and the decline of Asyut's importance.</p>

<p>Lycopolis has no remarkable ruins, but in the excavated chambers of the adjacent rocks <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mummies" title="Mummies">mummies</a> of <a href="/wiki/African_golden_wolf" title="African golden wolf">wolves</a> have been found, confirming the origin of its name, as well as a tradition preserved by <a href="/wiki/Diodorus_Siculus" title="Diodorus Siculus">Diodorus Siculus</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[5]</a></sup> to the effect that an <i><a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopian</a></i> army, invading Egypt, was repelled beyond the city of <i><a href="/wiki/Elephantine" title="Elephantine">Elephantine</a></i> by packs of wolves. <a href="/wiki/Osiris" title="Osiris">Osiris</a> was worshipped under the symbol of a wolf at Lycopolis. According to a myth, he had come "from the shades" as a wolf to aid <a href="/wiki/Isis" title="Isis">Isis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Horus" title="Horus">Horus</a> in their combat with <a href="/wiki/Typhon" title="Typhon">Typhon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[6]</a></sup> Other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient_Egyptian" title="Ancient Egyptian">Ancient Egyptian</a> monuments discovered in Asyut include; the Asyut <a href="/wiki/Necropolis" title="Necropolis">necropolis</a> (west of the modern city), tombs which date to dynasties <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ninth_dynasty_of_Egypt" title="Ninth dynasty of Egypt">Nine</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tenth_dynasty_of_Egypt" title="Tenth dynasty of Egypt">Ten</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twelfth_dynasty_of_Egypt" title="Twelfth dynasty of Egypt">Twelve</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ramesside_Period" title="Ramesside Period">Ramessid</a> tombs of <a href="/wiki/Siese" title="Siese">Siese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amenhotep" title="Amenhotep">Amenhotep</a>.</p>

<p>In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graeco-Roman" title="Graeco-Roman">Graeco-Roman</a> times, there was a distinct dialect of <a href="/wiki/Coptic_language" title="Coptic language">Coptic</a> spoken in Asyut, known as "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lycopolitan" title="Lycopolitan">Lycopolitan</a>", after the Greek name for the city. Lesser-used names for this dialect are "Sub-Akhmimic" and "Assiutic".</p>
<p>A large <a href="/wiki/Asyut_Treasure" title="Asyut Treasure">Byzantine Treasure</a> was discovered near the city in the early twentieth century and is now dispersed amongst a number of museums in the West. The <a href="/wiki/Hoard" title="Hoard">hoard</a> is composed of some of the most elaborate jewellery to survive from late <a href="/wiki/Ancient_history" title="Ancient history">antiquity</a>.</p>
<p>Asyut was the end of 40 Day Road that connected the city to <a href="/wiki/Darfur" title="Darfur">Darfur</a> through the Selima and <a href="/wiki/Kharga_Oasis" title="Kharga Oasis">Kharga Oases</a>. The history of the road, known by local herders as <i>Darb al-Arba'in</i>, goes back over 700 years. It was used as a pathway for great caravans of up to 12,000 camels at its peak in the 14th century. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_Asyut">Modern Asyut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asyut&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Modern Asyut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Today, the city of Asyut has almost 400,000 inhabitants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[8]</a></sup> It is the Egyptian city with one of the highest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copt" title="Copt">Coptic Christian</a> concentration of approximately 50%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup> It is also home to the <a class="external text" href="http://www.aun.edu.eg/" rel="nofollow">University of Assiut</a>, one of the largest <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">universities</a> in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, to the <a href="/wiki/Assiut_Barrage" title="Assiut Barrage">Assiut Barrage</a>, and to the <a href="/wiki/Lillian_Trasher" title="Lillian Trasher">Lillian Trasher</a> <a href="/wiki/Orphanage" title="Orphanage">Orphanage</a>.</p>
<p>The city is one of the only cities in the world that still makes silver appliqué-work shawls and is home to a large textile industry. The city also produces fine pottery, inlaid woodwork, and rugs. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brittanica_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-brittanica-11">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Virgin_Mary" title="Virgin Mary">Virgin Mary</a> is reported to have appeared in Asyut on 17 August 2000. This apparition is recognized as an official <a href="/wiki/Marian_apparition" title="Marian apparition">Marian apparition</a> by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coptic_Orthodox_Church" title="Coptic Orthodox Church">Coptic Orthodox Church</a> and remembered in the <a href="/wiki/Deir_el-Muharraq" title="Deir el-Muharraq">Deir el-Muharraq</a>, Monastery of the Virgin Mary.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Asyut is the terminus of the Ras Shukheir-Asyut <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oil_pipeline" title="Oil pipeline">oil pipeline</a>, the terminus of the <a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">Cairo</a>-Asyut <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gas_pipeline" title="Gas pipeline">gas pipeline</a> and the beginning of the proposed Asyut-<a href="/wiki/Qena" title="Qena">Qena</a> gas pipeline, the last two being part of the Nile Valley Gas Company Pipeline Project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[11]</a></sup> Aysut is next to the Aysut Dam across the Nile river in the neighboring port of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Al-Hamra,_Egypt&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Al-Hamra, Egypt (page does not exist)">Al-Hamra</a>. The dam was built in 1902 and a hydroelectric plant was added in the 1980s. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brittanica_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-brittanica-11">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coptic_Catholic_Eparchy">Coptic Catholic Eparchy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asyut&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Coptic Catholic Eparchy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 10 August 1947, a Coptic Catholic eparchy (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Catholic" title="Eastern Catholic">Eastern Catholic</a> Diocese) of Assiut (or Lycopolis) was established on southern Egyptian territory split off from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coptic_Catholic_Eparchy_of_Luqsor" title="Coptic Catholic Eparchy of Luqsor">Coptic Catholic Eparchy of Luqsor</a>, each <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suffragan" title="Suffragan">suffragan</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coptic_Catholic_Patriarch_of_Alexandria" title="Coptic Catholic Patriarch of Alexandria">Coptic Catholic Patriarch of Alexandria</a>. Its episcopal see is the <a href="/wiki/Cathedral" title="Cathedral">cathedral</a> of the Mother of Divine Love, in Assyut.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asyut&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asyut&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen-Geiger_climate_classification_system" title="Köppen-Geiger climate classification system">Köppen-Geiger climate classification system</a> classifies its climate as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_desert_climate" title="Hot desert climate">hot desert</a> (BWh). It is the driest city of <a href="/wiki/Climate_of_Egypt" title="Climate of Egypt">Egypt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Luxor#Climate" title="Luxor">Luxor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minya,_Egypt#Climate" title="Minya, Egypt">Minya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Qena#Climate" title="Qena">Qena</a> and Asyut have the widest difference of temperatures between days and nights of any city in Egypt, with almost 16 °C (29 °F) difference. The city of Asyut is sandwiched between two mountain ranges of about 600m height. There is also a lowering in elevation in mid <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a>. This gives the city and nearby towns and villages the similar properties of a <a href="/wiki/Continental_climate" title="Continental climate">continental climate</a>, meaning that the city has harsh and chilly <a href="/wiki/Cold" title="Cold">cold</a> <a href="/wiki/Winter" title="Winter">winter</a> weather, and very hot but non-humid <a href="/wiki/Summer" title="Summer">summers</a>. During summer the temperature can exceed 42 °C (108 °F). Yet, in winter Asyut gets below 0 °C (32 °F) temperatures during the night and <a href="/wiki/Frost" title="Frost">frost</a> can easily form, while <a href="/wiki/Hail" title="Hail">hail</a> or <a href="/wiki/Snow" title="Snow">snow</a> are rare because of the low average of the city's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">precipitation</a> and general low <a href="/wiki/Humidity" title="Humidity">humidity</a>.</p>
<p>The highest record temperature was 51 °C (124 °F), recorded on July 23, 1994, while the lowest record temperature was −2 °C (28 °F), recorded on January 16, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Voodoo_Skies_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Voodoo_Skies-14">[13]</a></sup></p>
