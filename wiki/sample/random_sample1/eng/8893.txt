<p><b>Asipovichy</b> (<a href="/wiki/Belarusian_language" title="Belarusian language">Belarusian</a>: <span lang="be">Асiповiчы</span>; <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_Latin_alphabet" title="Belarusian Latin alphabet">Łacinka</a>: Asipovičy) or <b>Osipovichi</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Осипо́вичи</span>) is a town in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mahilyow_Voblast" title="Mahilyow Voblast">Mahilyow Voblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a>, located 136 km southwest of <a href="/wiki/Mogilev" title="Mogilev">Mogilev</a>, 3 km north of the <a href="/wiki/Minsk" title="Minsk">Minsk</a>-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homyel" title="Homyel">Homyel</a> expressway. It is located at the junction of railway lines between Minsk, Homel, Mahilyow (Mogilev), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baranavichy" title="Baranavichy">Baranavichy</a>. As of 2009, its population was 32,543.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The active industries of Asipovichy include machine building, building materials, food production and light and wood processing. It is home to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydro-electric" title="Hydro-electric">hydro-electric</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Power_plant" title="Power plant">power plant</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Svislach_River" title="Svislach River">Svislach River</a>.</p>
