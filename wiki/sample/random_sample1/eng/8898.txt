<p><b>Ayako Valentina Hamada Villarreal</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">浜田 文子</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Hamada Ayako</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wave_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-Wave-1">[1]</a></sup> (born February 14, 1981)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Profile_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Profile-2">[2]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mexicans" title="Mexicans">Mexican</a> female <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Professional_wrestler" title="Professional wrestler">professional wrestler</a>, who has wrestled for AAA, TNA, Shimmer Women Athletes, and is currently wrestling for <a href="/wiki/Pro_Wrestling_Wave" title="Pro Wrestling Wave">Pro Wrestling Wave</a> in Japan. She is a second generation wrestler; her father is <a href="/wiki/Gran_Hamada" title="Gran Hamada">Gran Hamada</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Phillips_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Phillips-8">[8]</a></sup> Her older sister <a href="/wiki/X%C3%B3chitl_Hamada" title="Xóchitl Hamada">Xóchitl Hamada</a> is also a professional wrestler.</p>
<p>She has won several titles in Japanese women's pro wrestling, including the <a href="/wiki/WWWA_World_Championship" title="WWWA World Championship">WWWA World Championship</a> and <a href="/wiki/AAAW_Single_Championship" title="AAAW Single Championship">AAAW Single Championship</a> in the early 2000s, and she was a two–time <a href="/wiki/TNA_Knockouts_Tag_Team_Championship" title="TNA Knockouts Tag Team Championship">TNA Knockouts Tag Team Champion</a> during her time with the TNA promotion.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_wrestling_career">Professional wrestling career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ayako_Hamada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Professional wrestling career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Japan_and_Mexico">Japan and Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ayako_Hamada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Japan and Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ayako Hamada debuted at the age of seventeen against Candy Okutsu on August 9, 1998 for the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Arsion" title="Arsion">Arsion</a> promotion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Phillips_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Phillips-8">[8]</a></sup> She received an immediate push and won her first title, the <a href="/wiki/Twin_Star_of_Arsion_Championship" title="Twin Star of Arsion Championship">Twin Star of Arsion Championship</a> with Mika Akino, on June 30, 1999, less than one year after her debut. She won the <a href="/wiki/Queen_of_Arsion_Championship" title="Queen of Arsion Championship">Queen of Arsion Championship</a> from <a href="/wiki/Aja_Kong" title="Aja Kong">Aja Kong</a> on December 3, 2000.</p>
<p>After leaving Arsion in 2001,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Phillips_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Phillips-8">[8]</a></sup> she has won numerous women's titles in several Japanese and Mexican promotions, including the <a href="/wiki/WWWA_World_Championship" title="WWWA World Championship">WWWA World Championship</a> from <a href="/wiki/Momoe_Nakanishi" title="Momoe Nakanishi">Momoe Nakanishi</a> on May 11, 2003 and the <a href="/wiki/AAAW_Single_Championship" title="AAAW Single Championship">AAAW Single Championship</a> from <a href="/wiki/Dynamite_Kansai" title="Dynamite Kansai">Dynamite Kansai</a> on January 11, 2004, just one week after she had lost the WWWA title to <a href="/wiki/Kia_Stevens" title="Kia Stevens">Amazing Kong</a>. From 2005 to 2006 Hamada performed in <a href="/wiki/Hustle_(professional_wrestling)" title="Hustle (professional wrestling)">HUSTLE</a> under the names Arisin Z and Dokron Z, before becoming affiliated with the Kaoru Ito Dojo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hamada was a regular for <a href="/wiki/Asistencia_Asesor%C3%ADa_y_Administraci%C3%B3n" title="Asistencia Asesoría y Administración">AAA</a> in Mexico for most of 2007 and 2008. On November 30, 2007, she and <a href="/wiki/Mr._Niebla" title="Mr. Niebla">Mr. Niebla</a> were involved in four-way mixed tag team match with <a href="/wiki/Billy_Boy_(wrestler)" title="Billy Boy (wrestler)">Billy Boy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Faby_Apache" title="Faby Apache">Faby Apache</a>, <a href="/wiki/Esp%C3%ADritu_(wrestler)" title="Espíritu (wrestler)">Espiritu</a> and <a href="/wiki/La_Diab%C3%B3lica" title="La Diabólica">La Diabólica</a> and Gran and <a href="/wiki/Mari_Apache" title="Mari Apache">Mari Apache</a> for the vacant <a href="/wiki/AAA_World_Mixed_Tag_Team_Championship" title="AAA World Mixed Tag Team Championship">World Mixed Tag Team Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In November 2008, she left the promotion to join <a href="/wiki/Martha_Villalobos" title="Martha Villalobos">Martha Villalobos</a>'s wrestling school, where she worked as a trainer.</p>

<p>On April 8, 2009, it was announced at a press conference that the April 19 Ito Dojo show would be Hamada's final match in Japan as she had signed with <a href="/wiki/Total_Nonstop_Action_Wrestling" title="Total Nonstop Action Wrestling">Total Nonstop Action Wrestling</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On August 6, 2009, <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Borash" title="Jeremy Borash">Jeremy Borash</a> announced on his <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> page that Hamada would be starting with the company after the <a href="/wiki/Hard_Justice_(2009)" title="Hard Justice (2009)">Hard Justice</a> pay-per-view.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On the August 27 edition of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TNA_Impact!" title="TNA Impact!">Impact!</a></i>, Hamada made her debut as a <a href="/wiki/Face_(professional_wrestling)" title="Face (professional wrestling)">face</a> defeating <a href="/wiki/Daffney" title="Daffney">Daffney</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#No_Disqualification_match" title="Professional wrestling match types">No Disqualification match</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The following week on <i>Impact!</i> she and <a href="/wiki/Josette_Bynum" title="Josette Bynum">Sojo Bolt</a> were eliminated in the first round of the <a href="/wiki/TNA_Knockouts_Tag_Team_Championship" title="TNA Knockouts Tag Team Championship">Knockouts Tag Team Championship</a> tournament by <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Marie_Varon" title="Lisa Marie Varon">Tara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christy_Hemme" title="Christy Hemme">Christy Hemme</a>, with Hamada afterwards hitting Bolt with the Hamada Driver.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> On the September 24 edition of <i>Impact!</i> she was assaulted by <a href="/wiki/Cheerleader_Melissa" title="Cheerleader Melissa">Alissa Flash</a> while giving an interview. Flash proceeded to throw Hamada down some stairs, sparking a feud between the two.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On the October 15 edition of <i>Impact!</i> Hamada defeated Flash in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Falls_Count_Anywhere_match" title="Professional wrestling match types">Falls Count Anywhere match</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oct15_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Oct15-16">[16]</a></sup> On the November 19 and December 3 editions of <i>Impact!</i> Hamada beat <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Wilde" title="Taylor Wilde">Taylor Wilde</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Stock" title="Sarah Stock">Sarita</a>, the Knockouts Tag Team Champions, with the Hamada Driver.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On the December 10 edition of <i>Impact!</i>, Hamada was granted a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TNA_Global_Championship" title="TNA Global Championship">TNA Global Championship</a> shot by <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Nash" title="Kevin Nash">Kevin Nash</a>, who was in charge for the evening. The champion, <a href="/wiki/Eric_Young_(wrestler)" title="Eric Young (wrestler)">Eric Young</a>, claimed that his group of foreign wrestlers, named World Elite, had been eyeing Hamada for some time and that this would be her initiation match. Though Hamada dominated the match, Young was able to retain his title by pinning her with his feet on the ropes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The following week Hamada and her new tag team partner <a href="/wiki/Kia_Stevens" title="Kia Stevens">Awesome Kong</a> defeated Sarita and Wilde and the <a href="/wiki/The_Beautiful_People_(professional_wrestling)" title="The Beautiful People (professional wrestling)">Beautiful People</a> (<a href="/wiki/Madison_Rayne" title="Madison Rayne">Madison Rayne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Velvet_Sky" title="Velvet Sky">Velvet Sky</a>) in a three-way non-title tag team match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On the December 31 edition of <i>Impact!</i> Hamada defeated Madison Rayne and <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Raczynski" title="Nicole Raczynski">Roxxi</a> on her way to the finals of a #1 contender's tournament, where she was defeated by <a href="/wiki/ODB_(wrestler)" title="ODB (wrestler)">ODB</a>, after going through a table brought out by Awesome Kong.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The following Monday on the live three-hour edition of <i>Impact!</i> Hamada and Kong defeated Sarita and Wilde to win the Knockouts Tag Team Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jan4_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jan4-22">[22]</a></sup> On March 1 Hamada's tag team partner Awesome Kong was reportedly released from TNA Wrestling, while the team still held the Knockouts Tag Team Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On the March 8 Monday night edition of <i>Impact!</i> Hamada and Kong were stripped of the titles after allegedly failing to defend them in 30 days,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> even though in fact the team defended the titles just 21 days prior against Madison Rayne and Velvet Sky.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>After being off television for four months, Hamada returned on July 27 at the tapings of the August 5 edition of <i>Impact!</i>, teaming with Taylor Wilde to defeat The Beautiful People (Velvet Sky and <a href="/wiki/Lacey_Von_Erich" title="Lacey Von Erich">Lacey Von Erich</a>) to win the TNA Knockouts Tag Team Championship for the second time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In October 2010 Hamada returned to Japan and shortly afterwards requested her release from TNA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> After months of inactivity in TNA, it was reported on December 6 that the promotion had agreed to release Hamada and vacate her Knockouts Tag Team Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shimmer_Women_Athletes_.282009.E2.80.932013.29">Shimmer Women Athletes (2009–2013)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ayako_Hamada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Shimmer Women Athletes (2009–2013)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 8, 2009, Hamada made her debut under her full name for Chicago-based all female wrestling promotion <a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Women_Athletes" title="Shimmer Women Athletes">Shimmer Women Athletes</a>, defeating <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_Martinez" title="Mercedes Martinez">Mercedes Martinez</a> in a match taped for <i><a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_27" title="Shimmer Volumes">Volume 27</a></i> and losing to <a href="/wiki/Sara_Del_Rey" title="Sara Del Rey">Sara Del Rey</a> in a match taped for <i><a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_28" title="Shimmer Volumes">Volume 28</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> On February 5, 2010, Shimmer announced that Hamada would be returning to the company on April 11 for the tapings of Volumes <a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_31" title="Shimmer Volumes">31</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_32" title="Shimmer Volumes">32</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> On <i>Volume 31</i> she defeated <a href="/wiki/Daizee_Haze" title="Daizee Haze">Daizee Haze</a> and on <i>Volume 32</i> she was defeated in the main event by <a href="/wiki/Cheerleader_Melissa" title="Cheerleader Melissa">Cheerleader Melissa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Hamada returned to Shimmer on September 11, 2010, defeating <a href="/wiki/Tomoka_Nakagawa" title="Tomoka Nakagawa">Tomoka Nakagawa</a> in a match taped for <i><a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_33" title="Shimmer Volumes">Volume 33</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Later that day on <i><a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_34" title="Shimmer Volumes">Volume 34</a></i> she was defeated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jessie_McKay" title="Jessie McKay">Jessie McKay</a> in a three–way match, which also included Sara Del Rey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> The following day Hamada defeated <a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Tag_Team_Championship" title="Shimmer Tag Team Championship">Shimmer Tag Team Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Matthews" title="Nicole Matthews">Nicole Matthews</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_35" title="Shimmer Volumes">Volume 35</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> and then took part in an elimination tag team match on <i><a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_36" title="Shimmer Volumes">Volume 36</a></i>, where she, <a href="/wiki/Ayumi_Kurihara" title="Ayumi Kurihara">Ayumi Kurihara</a>, Cheerleader Melissa and <a href="/wiki/Serena_Deeb" title="Serena Deeb">Serena Deeb</a> defeated Daizee Haze, <a href="/wiki/Madison_Eagles" title="Madison Eagles">Madison Eagles</a>, Sara Del Rey and Tomoka Nakagawa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> On March 26, 2011, Hamada took part in the tapings of Volumes <a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_37" title="Shimmer Volumes">37</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_38" title="Shimmer Volumes">38</a>, during which she defeated Jessie McKay and Ayumi Kurihara, respectively, in singles matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ShimmerVol3740_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-ShimmerVol3740-37">[37]</a></sup> The following day, after pinning <a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Championship" title="Shimmer Championship">Shimmer Champion</a> Madison Eagles on <i><a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_39" title="Shimmer Volumes">Volume 39</a></i> in a tag team match, where she teamed with Cheerleader Melissa and Eagles with Sara Del Rey, Hamada was granted a shot at the Shimmer Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ShimmerVol3740_37-1"><a href="#cite_note-ShimmerVol3740-37">[37]</a></sup> Later that day, at the tapings of <i><a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_40" title="Shimmer Volumes">Volume 40</a></i>, Hamada was unsuccessful in her attempt to win the title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 26, 2011, it was announced that Hamada would return to the promotion on their tapings for Volumes <a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_41" title="Shimmer Volumes">41</a> to <a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_44" title="Shimmer Volumes">44</a> on October 1 and 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> On October 1 at the tapings of <i>Volume 41</i>, Hamada and Ayumi Kurihara defeated Daizee Haze and Tomoka Nakagawa to win the Shimmer Tag Team Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shimmer4142_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shimmer4142-40">[40]</a></sup> Hamada and Kurihara made their first title defense later that same day on <i><a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_42" title="Shimmer Volumes">Volume 42</a></i>, defeating the Knight Dynasty (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Britani_Knight" title="Britani Knight">Britani</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sweet_Saraya" title="Sweet Saraya">Saraya Knight</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shimmer4142_40-1"><a href="#cite_note-Shimmer4142-40">[40]</a></sup> The following day the duo made successful defenses against the teams of Madison Eagles and <a href="/wiki/Sara_Del_Rey" title="Sara Del Rey">Sara Del Rey</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_NINJAs" title="Canadian NINJAs">Canadian NINJAs</a> (Nicole Matthews and <a href="/wiki/Portia_Perez" title="Portia Perez">Portia Perez</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> The team made their fourth defense of the title at a <i>Joshi 4 Hope</i> event in Tokyo on February 22, 2012, defeating former champions <a href="/wiki/Hiroyo_Matsumoto" title="Hiroyo Matsumoto">Hiroyo Matsumoto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Misaki_Ohata" title="Misaki Ohata">Misaki Ohata</a>. This was the first time the Shimmer Tag Team Championship was defended outside North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Hamada returned to Shimmer on March 17, when she and Kurihara successfully defended the Shimmer Tag Team Championship against Regeneration X (<a href="/wiki/Allison_Danger" title="Allison Danger">Allison Danger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leva_Bates" title="Leva Bates">Leva Bates</a>) on <i><a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_45" title="Shimmer Volumes">Volume 45</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shimmer4546_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shimmer4546-43">[43]</a></sup> Hamada's and Kurihara's sixth successful title defense took place later that same day on <i><a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_46" title="Shimmer Volumes">Volume 46</a></i>, when they defeated <a href="/wiki/Hailey_Hatred" title="Hailey Hatred">Hailey Hatred</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maggy_Goyette" title="Maggy Goyette">Kalamity</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shimmer4546_43-1"><a href="#cite_note-Shimmer4546-43">[43]</a></sup> The following day on <i><a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_47" title="Shimmer Volumes">Volume 47</a></i>, Hamada and Kurihara successfully defended the title against the team of <a href="/wiki/Leon_(Japanese_wrestler)" title="Leon (Japanese wrestler)">Leon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ray_(wrestler)" title="Ray (wrestler)">Ray</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shimmer4748_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shimmer4748-44">[44]</a></sup> Later that day on <i><a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_48" title="Shimmer Volumes">Volume 48</a></i>, Hamada and Kurihara lost the title to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Courtney_Rush" title="Courtney Rush">Courtney Rush</a> and Sara Del Rey in a four-way elimination match, which also included the Canadian NINJAs and Regeneration X.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shimmer4748_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-Shimmer4748-44">[44]</a></sup> Hamada returned to Shimmer on October 27, 2012, when she defeated Kalamity on <i><a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_49" title="Shimmer Volumes">Volume 49</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Kana_(wrestler)" title="Kana (wrestler)">Kana</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_50" title="Shimmer Volumes">Volume 50</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> The following day on Volumes <a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_51" title="Shimmer Volumes">51</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_52" title="Shimmer Volumes">52</a>, Hamada defeated <a href="/wiki/Portia_Perez" title="Portia Perez">Portia Perez</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athena_(wrestler)" title="Athena (wrestler)">Athena</a>, respectively, to finish her weekend with a clean record of four wins out of four matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> On April 6, 2013, Hamada took part in Shimmer's first internet pay-per-view, <a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Volumes#Volume_53" title="Shimmer Volumes">Volume 53</a>, where she was defeated by Athena.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_promotions_.282010.E2.80.93present.29">Other promotions (2010–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ayako_Hamada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other promotions (2010–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 9, 2010, Hamada made her debut for <a href="/wiki/Jersey_All_Pro_Wrestling" title="Jersey All Pro Wrestling">Jersey All Pro Wrestling</a>'s Women's Division in a match, where she defeated <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Summerlyn" title="Rachel Summerlyn">Rachel Summerlyn</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JAPWJan9_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-JAPWJan9-50">[50]</a></sup> Later in the night she challenged Sara Del Rey to a match for the <a href="/wiki/JAPW_Women%27s_Championship" title="JAPW Women's Championship">Women's Championship</a> and Del Rey agreed to face her at any time and anywhere.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JAPWJan9_50-1"><a href="#cite_note-JAPWJan9-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2011, Hamada took part in American promotion <a href="/wiki/Chikara_(professional_wrestling)" title="Chikara (professional wrestling)">Chikara</a>'s <i>JoshiMania</i> weekend, teaming with Cherry to defeat <a href="/wiki/Mayumi_Ozaki" title="Mayumi Ozaki">Mayumi Ozaki</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mio_Shirai" title="Mio Shirai">Mio Shirai</a> in her night one match on December 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> The following day, Hamada was defeated by Aja Kong in the main event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> On the third and final night of the tour, Hamada was defeated in another main event, this time by Sara Del Rey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pro_Wrestling_Wave_.282011.E2.80.93present.29">Pro Wrestling Wave (2011–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ayako_Hamada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Pro Wrestling Wave (2011–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After returning to Japan from the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> in 2011, Hamada began working for <a href="/wiki/Pro_Wrestling_Wave" title="Pro Wrestling Wave">Pro Wrestling Wave</a>, where she aligned herself with the <a href="/wiki/Heel_(professional_wrestling)" title="Heel (professional wrestling)">villainous</a> Black Dahlia <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Stable" title="Glossary of professional wrestling terms">stable</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> On July 2, 2012, Hamada signed a contract to officially make Wave her new home promotion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> On October 6, 2013, Hamada and her Las Aventureras tag team partner Yuu Yamagata won the <a href="/wiki/Dual_Shock_Wave#2013" title="Dual Shock Wave">2013 Dual Shock Wave</a> tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DualShock2013NS_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-DualShock2013NS-56">[56]</a></sup> On October 30, Hamada and Yamagata defeated the Classic Gals (Cherry and <a href="/wiki/Shuu_Shibutani" title="Shuu Shibutani">Shuu Shibutani</a>) to win the <a href="/wiki/Wave_Tag_Team_Championship" title="Wave Tag Team Championship">Wave Tag Team Championship</a>, Hamada's first title in Wave.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wave103013_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wave103013-57">[57]</a></sup> After a reign of 270 days, the longest in the title's history, Hamada and Yamagata lost the title to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yankii_Nichokenju&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yankii Nichokenju (page does not exist)">Yankii Nichokenju</a> (<a href="/wiki/Isami_Kodaka" title="Isami Kodaka">Isami Kodaka</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yuko_Miyamoto&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yuko Miyamoto (page does not exist)">Yuko Miyamoto</a>) on July 27, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> They regained the title from Revolucion Amandla (<a href="/wiki/Kyoko_Kimura" title="Kyoko Kimura">Kyoko Kimura</a> and Tomoka Nakagawa) on October 29.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wave102914_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wave102914-59">[59]</a></sup> On February 11, 2015, Hamada became a double champion, when she defeated <a href="/wiki/Hikaru_Shida" title="Hikaru Shida">Hikaru Shida</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Wave_Single_Championship" title="Wave Single Championship">Wave Single Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wave021115_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wave021115-60">[60]</a></sup> On March 15, Hamada and Yamagata lost the Wave Tag Team Championship to Mio Shirai and Misaki Ohata.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> On October 30, Hamada and Yamagata won the Wave Tag Team Championship for the third time, when they defeated <a href="/wiki/Kayoko_Haruyama" title="Kayoko Haruyama">Kayoko Haruyama</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tsubasa_Kuragaki" title="Tsubasa Kuragaki">Tsubasa Kuragaki</a> in the finals of the <a href="/wiki/Dual_Shock_Wave#2015" title="Dual Shock Wave">2015 Dual Shock Wave</a> tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wave103015_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wave103015-62">[62]</a></sup> On December 27, Hamada lost the Wave Single Championship to her tag team partner Yuu Yamagata.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> On August 7, 2016, Hamada and Yamagata lost the Wave Tag Team Championship to Misaki Ohata and Ryo Mizunami.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
