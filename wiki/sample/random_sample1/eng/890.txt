<p><i><b>Comedown Machine</b></i> is the fifth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a> by the American <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/The_Strokes" title="The Strokes">The Strokes</a>. It was released on March 26, 2013 in the US, and on March 25 in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <i>Comedown Machine</i> was the final release in fulfilling the band's contract agreement with longtime label <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a>. The band decided to pull a <a href="/wiki/Media_blackout" title="Media blackout">media blackout</a> with the album: no promotion in the form of TV appearances, interviews, photos, shows, or tours.</p>
<p>The album artwork is designed to resemble an old RCA <a href="/wiki/Reel-to-reel_audio_tape_recording" title="Reel-to-reel audio tape recording">tape reel</a> box. The cover lists the album length as 37:49; the actual length is 39:55. The vinyl LP edition of the album was pressed by <a href="/wiki/United_Record_Pressing" title="United Record Pressing">United Record Pressing</a> in Nashville, TN.</p>
<p><i>Comedown Machine</i> received mostly positive reviews and was ranked number 41 in NME's <i>50 Best Albums of 2013</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical">Critical</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Comedown_Machine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Media response to <i>Comedown Machine</i> was generally favorable; the aggregating website <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> reports a weighted average rating of 68%, based on 44 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MC_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-MC-3">[3]</a></sup> "Whether you're in an <i><a href="/wiki/Is_This_It" title="Is This It">Is This It</a></i> vortex or not, this is The Strokes and they've returned with their most thought-provoking, strange and sexiest record yet," said Kieran Mayall of <i><a href="/wiki/Clash_(magazine)" title="Clash (magazine)">Clash</a></i> magazine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clash_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Clash-17">[17]</a></sup> James Skinner of <a href="/wiki/BBC_Music" title="BBC Music">BBC Music</a> added, "Although plenty of the group’s signature sounds are present and correct, they form the backdrop to an unexpectedly wide range of styles and approaches."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6">[6]</a></sup> In contrast, <a href="/wiki/Rob_Sheffield" title="Rob Sheffield">Rob Sheffield</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> questioned why the album was "an official Strokes album instead of another Casablancas solo album."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RS_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-RS-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial">Commercial</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Comedown_Machine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commercial">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Without much promotional effort, <i>Comedown Machine</i> entered the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a> at No. 10 and became the band's first album not to debut within the top three. The album did, however, give the band their fifth consecutive appearance in the top ten.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toptenuk_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-toptenuk-18">[18]</a></sup> Likewise, it reached No.10 in the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">US</a> with an entry sales week of 41,000 units, the band's weakest performance since <i><a href="/wiki/Is_This_It" title="Is This It">Is This It</a></i> in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USPerf_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-USPerf-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
