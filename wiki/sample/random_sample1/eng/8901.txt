<p><b>Aomori Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">青森駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Aomori-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station in the city of <a href="/wiki/Aomori,_Aomori" title="Aomori, Aomori">Aomori, Aomori</a>, Japan, jointly operated by <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">East Japan Railway Company</a> (JR East) and the <a href="/wiki/Public%E2%80%93private_partnership#Japan" title="Public–private partnership">third sector</a> railway operator <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aoimori_Railway" title="Aoimori Railway">Aoimori Railway</a>.</p>

<p>Aomori Station is served by the following lines:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cu_Main_Line" title="Ōu Main Line">Ōu Main Line</a> (for <a href="/wiki/Shin-Aomori_Station" title="Shin-Aomori Station">Shin-Aomori</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hirosaki_Station" title="Hirosaki Station">Hirosaki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Akita_Station" title="Akita Station">Akita</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yamagata_Station" title="Yamagata Station">Yamagata</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Niigata_Station" title="Niigata Station">Niigata</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tsugaru-Kaiky%C5%8D_Line" title="Tsugaru-Kaikyō Line">Tsugaru-Kaikyō Line</a> (for <a href="/wiki/Kanita_Station" title="Kanita Station">Kanita</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minmaya_Station" title="Minmaya Station">Minmaya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hakodate_Station" title="Hakodate Station">Hakodate</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sapporo_Station" title="Sapporo Station">Sapporo</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Aoimori_Railway_Line" title="Aoimori Railway Line">Aoimori Railway Line</a> (for <a href="/wiki/Hachinohe_Station" title="Hachinohe Station">Hachinohe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Morioka_Station" title="Morioka Station">Morioka</a>)</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aomori_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aomori Station has three <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platforms</a> connected to the station building by a footbridge. The station has a "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Midori_no_Madoguchi" title="Midori no Madoguchi">Midori no Madoguchi</a>" staffed ticket office and a View Plaza travel agency.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aomori_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station opened on 1 September 1891.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-teishajo1998_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-teishajo1998-1">[1]</a></sup> With the privatization of <a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a> (JNR) on 1 April 1987, the station came under the control of JR East.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-teishajo1998_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-teishajo1998-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
