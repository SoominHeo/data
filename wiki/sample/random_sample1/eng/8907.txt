<p><b>Schack August Steenberg Krogh</b> <span style="font-size:85%;"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foreign_Member_of_the_Royal_Society" title="Foreign Member of the Royal Society">ForMemRS</a></span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-frs_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-frs-2">[2]</a></sup> (November 15, 1874 – September 13, 1949) was a <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Danish</a> professor at the department of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Animal_physiology" title="Animal physiology">zoophysiology</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Copenhagen" title="University of Copenhagen">University of Copenhagen</a> from 1916 to 1945.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He contributed a number of fundamental discoveries within several fields of <a href="/wiki/Physiology" title="Physiology">physiology</a>, and is famous for developing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Krogh_Principle" title="Krogh Principle">Krogh Principle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1920 August Krogh was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physiology_or_Medicine" title="Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine">Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine</a> for the discovery of the mechanism of regulation of the <a href="/wiki/Capillary" title="Capillary">capillaries</a> in skeletal <a href="/wiki/Muscle" title="Muscle">muscle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Krogh was first to describe the adaptation of blood perfusion in muscle and other organs according to demands through opening and closing the <a href="/wiki/Arteriole" title="Arteriole">arterioles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Capillary" title="Capillary">capillaries</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nobel_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nobel-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Besides his contributions to medicine, Krogh was also one of the founders of what is today <a href="/wiki/Novo_Nordisk" title="Novo Nordisk">Novo Nordisk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=August_Krogh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Krogh was a pioneer in <a href="/wiki/Comparative_physiology" title="Comparative physiology">comparative physiology</a>. He wrote his thesis on the respiration through the skin and lungs in <a href="/wiki/Frog" title="Frog">frogs</a>: <i>Respiratory Exchange of Animals</i>, 1915. Later Krogh took on studies of water and electrolyte <a href="/wiki/Homeostasis" title="Homeostasis">homeostasis</a> of aquatic animals and he published the books: <i>Osmotic Regulation</i> (1939) and <i>Comparative Physiology of Respiratory Mechanisms</i> (1941). He contributed more than 200 research articles in international journals. He was a constructor of scientific instruments of which several had considerable practical importance, <i>e.g.</i> the <a href="/wiki/Spirometer" title="Spirometer">spirometer</a> and the apparatus for measuring <a href="/wiki/Basal_metabolic_rate" title="Basal metabolic rate">basal metabolic rate</a>.</p>
<p>Krogh was the head of the first laboratory for animal <a href="/wiki/Physiology" title="Physiology">physiology</a> (zoophysiology) at the University of Copenhagen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NBI_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-NBI-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Krogh and his wife Marie brought <a href="/wiki/Insulin" title="Insulin">insulin</a> to <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> shortly after its discovery in 1922 by <a href="/wiki/Nicolae_Paulescu" title="Nicolae Paulescu">Nicolae Paulescu</a>. Marie, a doctor who had patients with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Type_1_diabetes" title="Type 1 diabetes">type 1 diabetes</a>, was herself suffering from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Type_2_diabetes" title="Type 2 diabetes">type 2 diabetes</a> and was naturally very interested in the disease.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Novo_Nordisk_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Novo_Nordisk-14">[14]</a></sup> Together with a doctor, <a href="/wiki/Hans_Christian_Hagedorn" title="Hans Christian Hagedorn">Hagedorn</a>, August and Marie Krogh founded <a href="/wiki/Novo_Nordisk" title="Novo Nordisk">Nordisk Insulinlaboratorium</a>, where Krogh made decisive contributions to establishing a Danish production of insulin by <a href="/wiki/Ethanol" title="Ethanol">ethanol</a> extraction of the hormone from the <a href="/wiki/Pancreas" title="Pancreas">pancreatic glands</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pig" title="Pig">pigs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Novo_Nordisk_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Novo_Nordisk-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 1930s, Krogh worked with two other Nobel prizewinners, the radiochemist <a href="/wiki/George_de_Hevesy" title="George de Hevesy">George de Hevesy</a> and the physicist <a href="/wiki/Niels_Bohr" title="Niels Bohr">Niels Bohr</a> on the permeability of membranes to <a href="/wiki/Heavy_water" title="Heavy water">heavy water</a> and radioactive <a href="/wiki/Isotope" title="Isotope">isotopes</a>, and together they managed to obtain Denmark's first <a href="/wiki/Cyclotron" title="Cyclotron">cyclotron</a> for experiments on animal and plant physiology, as well as in dental and medical work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NBI_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-NBI-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=August_Krogh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Much of Krogh's work was carried out in collaboration with his wife, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Marie_Krogh&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Marie Krogh (page does not exist)">Marie Krogh</a> (1874–1943), a renowned scientist in her own right.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nobel_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nobel-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>August and Marie had four children, the youngest of whom, <a href="/wiki/Bodil_Schmidt-Nielsen" title="Bodil Schmidt-Nielsen">Bodil</a>, was born in 1918. She too was a physiologist, and became the first woman president of the <a href="/wiki/American_Physiological_Society" title="American Physiological Society">American Physiological Society</a> in 1975. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BodilObit_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-BodilObit-15">[15]</a></sup> Bodil married another eminent physiologist, <a href="/wiki/Knut_Schmidt-Nielsen" title="Knut Schmidt-Nielsen">Knut Schmidt-Nielsen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=August_Krogh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Torkel_Weis-Fogh" title="Torkel Weis-Fogh">Torkel Weis-Fogh</a>, an eminent pioneer on the study of <a href="/wiki/Insect_flight" title="Insect flight">insect flight</a>, was a student of August Krogh's. Together they wrote a classic paper on that subject in 1951.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-classic_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-classic-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Krogh's name is preserved in two items now named for him:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Krogh_length" title="Krogh length">Krogh length</a>, the distance between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capillaries" title="Capillaries">capillaries</a> which nutrients <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diffuse" title="Diffuse">diffuse</a> to, based on cellular consumption of the nutrients.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Krogh%27s_principle" title="Krogh's principle">Krogh's principle</a>, that "for ... a large number of problems there will be some animal of choice, or a few such animals, on which it can be most conveniently studied."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
