<p><b>Audax Club Sportivo Italiano</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[ˈauðaks itaˈljano]</a></span>) is a Chilean <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/La_Florida,_Chile" title="La Florida, Chile">La Florida</a>. Founded in 1910, it plays in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n_de_Chile" title="Primera División de Chile">Campeonato Nacional</a> and has spent most of its history in the top tier of Chilean football. Audax has been national champions four times, joint sixth alongside <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C.D._Magallanes" title="C.D. Magallanes">Magallanes</a>. The club also achieved two <a href="/wiki/Copa_Chile" title="Copa Chile">Copa Chile</a> runners-up in 1981 and 1988.</p>
<p>The club had their first major success in 1936, when they won the league tournament. During the 1940s, Audax won two leagues in 1946 and 1948, reaching a fourth in 1957.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Audax_Italiano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The club was founded by Italian residents in Santiago, on November 30, 1910 as <b>Audax Club Ciclista Italiano</b> in the Alberto Caffi's hat store, and was originally focused in <a href="/wiki/Cycling" title="Cycling">cycling</a>. As <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> developed quickly in Santiago, in 1917 the club begun participating in that sport, and in 1922, when the popularity of football increased within Audax's supporters, the club changed its name to <b>Audax Club Sportivo Italiano</b>.</p>
<p>In January 2007, the club was transformed into a limited liability corporation, and changed its name to <b>Audax Italiano La Florida</b>, adding the name of the <i>comuna</i> in which it's always been located.</p>
<p>The club has always been a regular protagonist in Chilean football, but had never been able to participate in any major international competition until 2007, when they played in the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Libertadores" title="Copa Libertadores">Copa Libertadores</a>. As a club founded by immigrants, it has a special rivalry with the other immigrant clubs <a href="/wiki/Uni%C3%B3n_Espa%C3%B1ola" title="Unión Española">Unión Española</a> (founded by Spaniards) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palestino" title="Palestino">Palestino</a> (founded by Palestinian Arabs).</p>
