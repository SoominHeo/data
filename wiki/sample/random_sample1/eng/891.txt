<p><i><b>Como Ama una Mujer</b></i> (English: <i>How a Woman Loves</i>) is the fifth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a> and first Spanish album by American entertainer <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>. It was released on March 23, 2007 by <a href="/wiki/Epic_Records" title="Epic Records">Epic Records</a>. After including some Spanish songs on her first two albums, Lopez initially became interested in recording a full-length studio album in 2004 when she recorded a song with her then-husband <a href="/wiki/Marc_Anthony" title="Marc Anthony">Marc Anthony</a> for his ninth studio album. After releasing her fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Rebirth_(Jennifer_Lopez_album)" title="Rebirth (Jennifer Lopez album)">Rebirth</a></i> (2005), Lopez started working heavily on the album with Anthony, <a href="/wiki/Est%C3%A9fano" title="Estéfano">Estéfano</a> and Julio Reyes in a period of two and a half years. Composed entirely of ballads (and the exception of a few other genres), <i>Como Ama una Mujer</i> talks about love and heartbreak, being organic in its instrumentation and introspective in its lyrics.</p>
<p>The album received generally mixed to negative reviews from music critics, with many praising her vocals for being more improved than her previous records, but thought the album was too serious and questioned a lack of highlights within the album. Commercially, the album became one of the first Spanish albums to debut inside the top-ten on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">US <i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and the highest debut sales in the United States for a Spanish record. Elsewhere, it topped the Switzerland charts and became a top-ten album in eight other countries.</p>
<p>Two singles were released from the album: "<a href="/wiki/Qu%C3%A9_Hiciste" title="Qué Hiciste">Qué Hiciste</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Me_Haces_Falta" title="Me Haces Falta">Me Haces Falta</a>". The first was released on January 26, 2007 and became a success in many countries, reaching number-one in Italy, Spain, Switzerland and on the US Hot Latin Songs, and a top-ten hit elsewhere, while the second single was released on March 23, 2007 but failed to make any impact on the charts. Lopez promoted the album with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juntos_en_Concierto_(Jennifer_Lopez_and_Marc_Anthony)" title="Juntos en Concierto (Jennifer Lopez and Marc Anthony)">co-headlining tour with Anthony</a> in 2007.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_development">Background and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Como_Ama_una_Mujer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:142px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Marc_Anthony_2,_2010.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="975" data-file-width="675" height="202" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Marc_Anthony_2%2C_2010.jpg/140px-Marc_Anthony_2%2C_2010.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Marc_Anthony_2%2C_2010.jpg/210px-Marc_Anthony_2%2C_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Marc_Anthony_2%2C_2010.jpg/280px-Marc_Anthony_2%2C_2010.jpg 2x" width="140"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Marc_Anthony_2,_2010.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Lopez's former husband <a href="/wiki/Marc_Anthony" title="Marc Anthony">Marc Anthony</a> inspired her to record a Spanish album and co-wrote and co-produced the majority of the record.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Prior to breaking out into the music scene in May 1999 with her single "<a href="/wiki/If_You_Had_My_Love" title="If You Had My Love">If You Had My Love</a>", Lopez always pictured her music career to be in Spanish. Her first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Demo_tape" title="Demo tape">demo tape</a> was called "Vivir Sin Ti" ("To Live Without You"), however former <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music" title="Sony Music">Sony Music</a> president <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Mottola" title="Tommy Mottola">Tommy Mottola</a> persuaded Lopez to do her first album in English. According to Lopez: "My life took a different turn, which was great for me. But my heart was always kind of on the Spanish side."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-1">[1]</a></sup> Later, throughout her career as a singer, she included two tracks in Spanish on her debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/On_the_6" title="On the 6">On the 6</a></i> (1999),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> with one being the single "<a href="/wiki/No_Me_Ames" title="No Me Ames">No Me Ames</a>" and five on her sophomore studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/J.Lo_(album)" title="J.Lo (album)">J.Lo</a></i> (2001).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-feat_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-feat-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Plans for Lopez to release a full-length Spanish-language album materialized in 2004, when Lopez's then-husband <a href="/wiki/Marc_Anthony" title="Marc Anthony">Marc Anthony</a> was finishing up production of his ninth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Amar_Sin_Mentiras" title="Amar Sin Mentiras">Amar Sin Mentiras</a></i> (2004) with <a href="/wiki/Est%C3%A9fano" title="Estéfano">Estéfano</a> and Julio Reyes. Lopez went into the recording studio with them to record a song entitled "Escapémonos", as a duet with Anthony. She enjoyed working on the song so much that she decided to record a full-length Spanish album; something she had wanted to do for years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-She_is_woman_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-She_is_woman-4">[4]</a></sup> In the same year, she recorded a Spanish version of the song "<a href="/wiki/Sway_(song)" title="Sway (song)">Sway</a>", called "Quién Será", for her film <i><a href="/wiki/Shall_We_Dance%3F_(2004_film)" title="Shall We Dance? (2004 film)">Shall We Dance?</a></i> (2004),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> but the English version recorded by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pussycat_Dolls" title="Pussycat Dolls">Pussycat Dolls</a> was used instead. Before recording began for her first Spanish album, Lopez recorded and released her fourth English studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Rebirth_(Jennifer_Lopez_album)" title="Rebirth (Jennifer Lopez album)">Rebirth</a></i> (2005).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rebirth_Allmusic_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rebirth_Allmusic-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In early 2006 it was announced that Lopez would release her first Spanish album and her fifth studio album <i>Como Ama una Mujer</i> later that year and that she worked mostly with Colombian record producer Estéfano, Reyes and Anthony.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2006_Billboard_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-2006_Billboard-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cobo_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-cobo-8">[8]</a></sup> Amidst the recording process, she was also shooting a film with Anthony, <i><a href="/wiki/El_Cantante" title="El Cantante">El Cantante</a></i> (2007) and providing vocals for <a href="/wiki/El_Cantante_(Marc_Anthony_album)" title="El Cantante (Marc Anthony album)">its soundtrack</a> entirely done by Anthony, as well as recording a more "urban-leaning" full-English album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jennyfromelbarrio_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-jennyfromelbarrio-9">[9]</a></sup> According to Estéfano, <i>Como Ama una Mujer</i> was going to "prove critics wrong" from its "big songs that require a voice"; referring to the critics about Lopez having a "limited" vocal tone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Contactmusic_Rebirth_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Contactmusic_Rebirth-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Contactmusic_Spanish_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Contactmusic_Spanish-11">[11]</a></sup> After working on the album, Lopez said that she matured as singer, stating:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"I've matured as a singer. Marc gave me confidence in the studio as well. When someone believes in you so much, you don't want to let them down. And it's also the material. I think this material lent itself to my voice. And it actually made me approach my new English album in a different way. I have a different standard now".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-She_is_woman_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-She_is_woman-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition_and_themes">Composition and themes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Como_Ama_una_Mujer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Composition and themes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:19em; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>"I love the music. I love the drama. It must be the actress in me. I love the deep, intense passionate way of expressing yourself in those kinds of songs. And it's really not something that lends itself to English music, and not something I'd been able to tap into before."</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;"> — Lopez on the difference between the album and her English-language records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jennyfromelbarrio_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-jennyfromelbarrio-9">[9]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p><i>Como Ama una Mujer</i> is mostly composed by songs written by <a href="/wiki/Est%C3%A9fano" title="Estéfano">Estéfano</a>, Julio Reyes and <a href="/wiki/Marc_Anthony" title="Marc Anthony">Marc Anthony</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cobo_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-cobo-8">[8]</a></sup> Despite the fact that she speaks Spanish, Lopez didn't write any song on the album. However, she wanted to make sure she was expressing everything accurately. She said: "You have to have a total command of the language, so I couldn’t write it all myself, you know what I mean?. I had to really depend on people to express what I was feeling."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvnews_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtvnews-12">[12]</a></sup> Lopez also emphasized that she really expressed to the writers the kind of album she wanted to make and the things that were important for her. She explained: "I would say, 'This songs needs to be about a person, and when he's leaving,' and they would turn around and write in Spanish."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jennyfromelbarrio_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-jennyfromelbarrio-9">[9]</a></sup> She also declared that, emotionally speaking, the album was closer to who she was than any album she had ever done.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jennyfromelbarrio_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-jennyfromelbarrio-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Como Ama una Mujer</i> is a mainstream, traditional <a href="/wiki/Latin_pop" title="Latin pop">Latin pop</a> album,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jennyfromelbarrio_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-jennyfromelbarrio-9">[9]</a></sup> composed mostly of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romantic_ballad" title="Romantic ballad">romantic ballads</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-1">[1]</a></sup> differing from her previous urban sounds as well as the rhythmic Latin pop vibe heard that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jennyfromelbarrio_9-5"><a href="#cite_note-jennyfromelbarrio-9">[9]</a></sup> It is also known for being "organic in its instrumentation and introspective in its lyrics." The first song written for the album was "Por Arriesgarnos" and is a duet with Marc Anthony. Initially, the song wasn't intend to be a duet, with Lopez remarking that "there was a background male voice, and Marc did the backups. I asked to put his voice up, because I think the harmony parts blended so beautifully."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jennyfromelbarrio_9-6"><a href="#cite_note-jennyfromelbarrio-9">[9]</a></sup> Meanwhile, when the album was almost done, the song "Qué Hiciste" was a last minute addition and was born after a dream Anthony had with late singer <a href="/wiki/Roc%C3%ADo_D%C3%BArcal" title="Rocío Dúrcal">Rocío Dúrcal</a>. Lopez explained: "Marc woke up and said, ’I just had the craziest dream.' 'What was the dream?' And he said, 'Rocío Dúrcal was in this room and she was saying', 'Come here, get in this room right now, listen to this. This song is for Jennifer,' and she was singing a melody.'" Then, since she had not studio equipment nearby to capture the essence of the song, she took her phone, called up their answering machine and told him to sing. Later, she called a Spanish writer she was working with to help her with adapting the lyrics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvnews_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-mtvnews-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Songs">Songs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Como_Ama_una_Mujer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Songs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album mainly deals with themes of love and heartache. "Qué Hiciste" ("What Have You Done?") opens the album, starting as a ballad and turning out to a passionate <a href="/wiki/Latin_rock" title="Latin rock">Latin rock</a> track,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvnews_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-mtvnews-12">[12]</a></sup> becoming one of the few uptempo tracks on the record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-1">[1]</a></sup> Lyrically, the song talks about a very intense relationship and how she feels heartbroken with a lover who unraveled her happy home.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-boston_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-boston-13">[13]</a></sup> Lopez remarked that she told Reyes that she wanted a song with a woman saying to her former lover: "What have you done with our relationship? With our life? We had everything and with your own hands, you destroyed our house."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvnews_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-mtvnews-12">[12]</a></sup> "Me Haces Falta" ("I Miss You") talks about missing someone so badly and feeling desperate and it also has rock influences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sputnikmusic_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-sputnikmusic-14">[14]</a></sup> The title track, the delicate ballad "Como Ama una Mujer" ("How a Woman Loves"), opens with piano and a minor-chord progression that was compared to <a href="/wiki/Randy_Newman" title="Randy Newman">Randy Newman</a>'s "In Germany Before the War"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-boston_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-boston-13">[13]</a></sup> and a melodic chorus which was noted for being similar to <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Rogers" title="Kenny Rogers">Kenny Rogers</a>' "<a href="/wiki/She_Believes_in_Me" title="She Believes in Me">She Believes in Me</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sputnikmusic_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-sputnikmusic-14">[14]</a></sup> The fourth track, "Te Voy a Querer" ("I Will Love You") is a <a href="/wiki/Cumbia" title="Cumbia">cumbia</a> flavored track.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sputnikmusic_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-sputnikmusic-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>"Porque te Marchas?" ("Why You Leave?") is a Spanish version of "Can't Believe This Is Me", a "dramatic" ballad from her previous album <i><a href="/wiki/Rebirth_(Jennifer_Lopez_album)" title="Rebirth (Jennifer Lopez album)">Rebirth</a></i> (2005).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-slant_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-slant-15">[15]</a></sup> It features swelling strings in its chorus and it was highlighted for containing strong vocals by Lopez and lyrics about heartbreak.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-boston_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-boston-13">[13]</a></sup> "Por Arriesgarnos" ("By Risking") is a duet with Anthony about love and the risks that two people in love run. "Tú" ("You") is an <a href="/wiki/Italian_popular_music" title="Italian popular music">Italian pop</a> song<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboardreview_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-billboardreview-16">[16]</a></sup> and it features the <a href="/wiki/London_Symphony_Orchestra" title="London Symphony Orchestra">London Symphony Orchestra</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-slant_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-slant-15">[15]</a></sup> According to Lopez, "It was an emotional I had about wanting to sing that to a child one day. That's where the song was born from. To sing it to my own child."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jennyfromelbarrio_9-7"><a href="#cite_note-jennyfromelbarrio-9">[9]</a></sup> "Amarte es Todo" ("Loving You Is Everything") and "Apresúrate" ("Hurry Up") are both romantic and passionate ballads about love, while "Sola" ("Alone") talks about recovering from a heartbreak and features traces of ambient <a href="/wiki/Electronica" title="Electronica">electronica</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-boston_13-3"><a href="#cite_note-boston-13">[13]</a></sup> The last track on the album is "Adiós" ("Goodbye"), a ballad about leaving someone behind and it was recorded live in front of an audience that "immediately erupts into a sing-along."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-slant_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-slant-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_and_promotion">Release and promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Como_Ama_una_Mujer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Release and promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Jennifer_Lopez_and_Marc_Anthony.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1453" data-file-width="1034" height="239" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Jennifer_Lopez_and_Marc_Anthony.jpg/170px-Jennifer_Lopez_and_Marc_Anthony.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Jennifer_Lopez_and_Marc_Anthony.jpg/255px-Jennifer_Lopez_and_Marc_Anthony.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Jennifer_Lopez_and_Marc_Anthony.jpg/340px-Jennifer_Lopez_and_Marc_Anthony.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Jennifer_Lopez_and_Marc_Anthony.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Lopez and Anthony embarked on a co-headling tour to promote the album.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In January 2007, <i>Billboard</i> reported that the album was going to be released on April 3, 2007 in the United States. However, the album was released a week later. The album's artwork was shot by <a href="/wiki/Tony_Duran_(photographer)" title="Tony Duran (photographer)">Tony Duran</a>, who worked with Jennifer in her previous projects. It features Lopez in a serious pose, looking down, with her hair pulled back and her hand around her neck. To promote the album, Lopez traveled back to <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">The Bronx</a> with Marc Athony to meet fans and sign copies of the album in a record shop in her old neighborhood with over 500 people attending it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hometown_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-hometown-17">[17]</a></sup> In an interview, Lopez stated "It touches my heart to be able to bring this album back to my neighborhood, back to my home."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-promotealbum_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-promotealbum-18">[18]</a></sup> Lopez also united with the <a href="/wiki/Univision" title="Univision">Univision</a> TV network to produce a five-part miniseries based on a treatment by Lopez, which was based on the content of the album, while also having <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez_Presents:_Como_Ama_una_Mujer" title="Jennifer Lopez Presents: Como Ama una Mujer">the album's title</a>. At the end of each episode, Lopez performs the theme song that inspired that episode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cobo_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-cobo-8">[8]</a></sup> Lopez also promoted the album with live performances of the album's lead-single in many places, including the <a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a>, while also doing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walmart_Soundcheck" title="Walmart Soundcheck">Walmart Soundcheck</a>, where she performed "Qué Hiciste", "Tú", "Por Arriesgarnos" (with Marc Anthony), "Como Ama Una Mujer", "Porque te Marchas" and "Te voy a Querer". To further promote the album, Lopez embarked on a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juntos_en_Concierto_(Jennifer_Lopez_and_Marc_Anthony)" title="Juntos en Concierto (Jennifer Lopez and Marc Anthony)">co-headling tour with Anthony</a> through September and November in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pr_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-pr-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Como_Ama_una_Mujer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"Qué Hiciste" was released as the album's lead-single on January 26, 2007. It became a huge success on the charts, reaching number-one in four countries, including Italy,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chartit_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-chartit-20">[20]</a></sup> Spain, Switzerland and the United States, where it topped the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Latin_Songs" title="Hot Latin Songs">Hot Latin Songs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboard_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-billboard-21">[21]</a></sup> Elsewhere, the song managed to reach either the top-ten or the top-twenty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chartit_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-chartit-20">[20]</a></sup> The music video for the song was released on February 5, 2007 on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Me_Haces_Falta" title="Me Haces Falta">Me Haces Falta</a>" was released as the album's second and final single on March 23, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-People_en_Espa.C3.B1ol_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-People_en_Espa.C3.B1ol-23">[23]</a></sup> Its music video was released on July 17, 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iTunes_video_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-iTunes_video-24">[24]</a></sup> however, the song failed to chart.</p>

<p><i>Como Ama una Mujer</i> received mixed to lukewarm reviews from most <a href="/wiki/Music_journalism#Popular_music_journalism" title="Music journalism">contemporary music critics</a>. At <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, which assigns a <a href="/wiki/Standard_score" title="Standard score">normalized</a> rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream critics, the album received an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weighted_mean" title="Weighted mean">average</a> score of 45 based on 7 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metacritic_25-2"><a href="#cite_note-metacritic-25">[25]</a></sup> In a favorable analysis, Leila Cobo of <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> noted that "Dovetailing with her choice of sound and arrangements that straddle convention and invention, Lopez mines new emotional depths. It certainly helps that she has the vocal chops needed to interpret material that is often quite demanding."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboardreview_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-billboardreview-16">[16]</a></sup> James Reed was also positive in his review for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boston_Globe" title="Boston Globe">Boston Globe</a></i>, calling it "Lopez's most tasteful and reserved album yet." He emphasized that Lopez "opt[ed] for string sections over canned synthesizers" and praised her vocal performance on "Porque te Marchas" and "Sóla".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-boston_13-5"><a href="#cite_note-boston-13">[13]</a></sup> Chris Willman of <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> acknowledge that the album "does represent a victory for Lopez by offering fairly persuasive proof that, contrary to rumor, she can sing, and without a regiment of background choralists." However he noticed that "[a]ll that bulking up she’s been doing at the vocal gym isn’t enough, though, to turn flaccid torch songs into muscle."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EW_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-EW-27">[27]</a></sup> Chuck Arnold of <i><a href="/wiki/People_(magazine)" title="People (magazine)">People</a></i> gave the album a rating of two-and-a-half-out-of-four-stars, and praised the "writing, production and vocal help" from <a href="/wiki/Marc_Anthony" title="Marc Anthony">Marc Anthony</a>, who made Lopez "smoothly transforms into Jenny from the Barrio." However, he criticized the album's second half for being "dragged down by too many ballads, which, no matter what tongue they're sung in, will never be Lopez's strong suit."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-people-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In his <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a> review, <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Thomas_Erlewine" title="Stephen Thomas Erlewine">Stephen Thomas Erlewine</a> was less favorable, calling it "music for housewives" and that "it's by far the most sedate Lopez has ever been on record." Erlewine was less negative about her vocals though, writing that "[s]he acquits herself well as a vocalist -- she never indulges in vocal gymnastics, and she can carry a tune strongly."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-26">[26]</a></sup> Sal Cinquemani of <a href="/wiki/Slant_Magazine" title="Slant Magazine">Slant Magazine</a> opined that "Lopez’s vocal shortcomings notwithstanding, she sounds surprisingly relaxed and at ease throughout [the album]," but he found problem with that fact that, according to him, "Lopez easily ages herself by a decade or two," calling it "shockingly middle-of-the-road" and "something that’s close to Lopez’s heart, but it’s a less than shrewd move for one of pop culture’s savviest icons."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-slant_15-4"><a href="#cite_note-slant-15">[15]</a></sup> While recognizing that her vocals never lack feeling or conviction, <a href="/wiki/Vibe_(magazine)" title="Vibe (magazine)">Vibe</a>'s Pete L'Official called its music "tacky and overproduced", but observed that "[t]he songwriting here far outstrips Lopez's previous albums, though."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vibe_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-vibe-29">[29]</a></sup> John Cruz of <a href="/wiki/Sputnikmusic" title="Sputnikmusic">Sputnikmusic</a> was negative in his review, feeling that Lopez "forgets to bring the flavor of the language itself," naming it "tepid and dull" and noting that it lacked "any sort of spice," calling it "one of the years most disappointing albums,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sputnikmusic_14-4"><a href="#cite_note-sputnikmusic-14">[14]</a></sup> while <i><a href="/wiki/Q_(magazine)" title="Q (magazine)">Q</a> magazine</i> labeled it "[s]loppy, emotion-free, chicken-in-a-basket ballad."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metacritic_25-3"><a href="#cite_note-metacritic-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Como_Ama_una_Mujer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Como Ama una Mujer</i> debuted at number ten on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">US <i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart with 48,000 copies sold in its first week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> By debuting inside the top-ten, Lopez became the fifth artist to land in the top ten with Spanish-language albums. The album also marked the highest debut sales in the United States for a Spanish album and the highest digital sales for a Spanish album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-recordrecord_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-recordrecord-31">[31]</a></sup> It also topped the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Top_Latin_Albums" title="Billboard Top Latin Albums"><i>Billboard</i> Latin Albums Chart</a> for four consecutive weeks; spending 30 weeks on the chart before leaving the top 100.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uslatin_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-uslatin-32">[32]</a></sup> As of June 2013, it has sold 213,000 copies in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>It also fared well in Spanish-speaking countries, such as Argentina and Spain, where it reached numbers three and two, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arg_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-arg-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spa_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-spa-35">[35]</a></sup> It was also successful in non-Spanish speaking countries, such as Switzerland, where it debuted at number-one to become her second consecutive number-one album there, Italy, where it debuted at number two and became her highest charting album,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ita_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-ita-36">[36]</a></sup> while also reaching the top-ten in Germany, Greece and Poland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-recordrecord_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-recordrecord-31">[31]</a></sup> As of August 2014, it has sold over 2,000,000 copies worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
