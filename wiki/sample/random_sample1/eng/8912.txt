<p>In <a href="/wiki/Parapsychology" title="Parapsychology">parapsychology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spirituality" title="Spirituality">spiritual practice</a>, an <b>aura</b> is a field of subtle, luminous radiation surrounding a person or object like the <a href="/wiki/Halo_(religious_iconography)" title="Halo (religious iconography)">halo</a> or <a href="/wiki/Aureola" title="Aureola">aureola</a> in <a href="/wiki/Religious_art" title="Religious art">religious art</a>. The depiction of such an aura often connotes a person of particular power or holiness.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It is said<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (May 2016)">by whom?</span></a></i>]</sup> that all objects and all living things manifest such an aura. Often it is held to be perceptible, whether spontaneously or with practice: such perception is at times linked with the <a href="/wiki/Third_eye" title="Third eye">third eye</a> of Indian spirituality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Various writers associate various personality traits with the colors of different layers of the aura.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It has also been described as a map of the thoughts and feelings surrounding a person.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Skeptics such as <a href="/wiki/Robert_Todd_Carroll" title="Robert Todd Carroll">Robert Todd Carroll</a> contend that people may perceive auras because of effects within the brain: <a href="/wiki/Synesthesia" title="Synesthesia">synesthesia</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Epilepsy" title="Epilepsy">epilepsy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Migraine" title="Migraine">migraines</a>, or the influence of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Psychedelic_drugs" title="Psychedelic drugs">psychedelic drugs</a> such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LSD" title="LSD">LSD</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fole_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-fole-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ASID_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ASID-10">[10]</a></sup> Other causes may include disorders within the <a href="/wiki/Visual_system" title="Visual system">visual system</a> provoking optical effects. <a href="/wiki/Human_eye" title="Human eye">Eye</a> fatigue can also produce an aura, sometimes referred to as <i><a href="/wiki/Aura_(symptom)" title="Aura (symptom)">eye burn</a></i>.</p>
<p>Attempts to prove the existence of auras scientifically have repeatedly met with failure; for example people are unable to see auras in the dark, and auras have never been successfully used to identify people when their identifying features are otherwise obscured in controlled tests.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nickell_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nickell-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Loftin_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Loftin-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-skepdic_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-skepdic-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Randi_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Randi-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Spiritual_traditions">Spiritual traditions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aura_(paranormal)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Spiritual traditions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:162px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Virgin_Mary_and_Jesus_(old_Persian_miniature).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="290" data-file-width="212" height="219" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Virgin_Mary_and_Jesus_%28old_Persian_miniature%29.jpg/160px-Virgin_Mary_and_Jesus_%28old_Persian_miniature%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f4/Virgin_Mary_and_Jesus_%28old_Persian_miniature%29.jpg 1.5x" width="160"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Virgin_Mary_and_Jesus_(old_Persian_miniature).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
An old Iranian Muslim impression of Jesus and Mary shows an aura after the style of the <i>farr</i></div>
</div>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> the aura is known as <i>farr</i> or "glory": it is depicted in association with <a href="/wiki/Zoroastrianism" title="Zoroastrianism">Zoroastrian</a> kings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ideas of the aura are well represented in Indian religions. In tantric tradition of <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hinduism</a>, aura represents the <a href="/wiki/Subtle_body" title="Subtle body">subtle body</a> of seven colours.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In many <a href="/wiki/Hindu" title="Hindu">Hindu</a> paintings of gods and goddesses, aura is marked on their backhead. The <a href="/wiki/Buddhist_flag" title="Buddhist flag">Buddhist flag</a> represents the colours seen around the enlightened Buddha.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Jainism" title="Jainism">Jainism</a> the concept of <i><a href="/wiki/Lesya" title="Lesya">Lesya</a></i> relates colours to mental and emotional dispositions. To the Indian teacher <a href="/wiki/Meher_Baba" title="Meher Baba">Meher Baba</a> the aura is of seven colours, associated with the <a href="/wiki/Subtle_body" title="Subtle body">subtle body</a> and its store of mental and emotional impressions. Spiritual practice gradually transforms this aura into a spiritual halo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hindu" title="Hindu">Hindu</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buddhist" title="Buddhist">Buddhist</a> sources often link these colours to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kundalini_energy" title="Kundalini energy">Kundalini energy</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Chakra" title="Chakra">chakras</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:244px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nataraja.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="270" data-file-width="242" height="270" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a9/Nataraja.jpg" width="242"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nataraja.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Statue depicting <a href="/wiki/Shiva" title="Shiva">Shiva</a> as <i><a href="/wiki/Nataraja" title="Nataraja">Nataraja</a></i> with auras</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In the classical western mysticism of <a href="/wiki/Neoplatonism" title="Neoplatonism">neoplatonism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kabbalah" title="Kabbalah">Kabbalah</a> the aura is associated with the lustre of the <a href="/wiki/Astral_body" title="Astral body">astral body</a>, a subtle body identified with the planetary heavens, which were in turn associated with various mental faculties in an elaborate system of correspondences with colours, shapes, sounds, perfumes etc.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:1531_Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_Guadalupe_anagoria.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4752" data-file-width="3168" height="330" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/1531_Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_Guadalupe_anagoria.jpg/220px-1531_Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_Guadalupe_anagoria.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/1531_Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_Guadalupe_anagoria.jpg/330px-1531_Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_Guadalupe_anagoria.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/1531_Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_Guadalupe_anagoria.jpg/440px-1531_Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_Guadalupe_anagoria.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:1531_Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_Guadalupe_anagoria.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A 1531 depiction of <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_of_Guadalupe" title="Our Lady of Guadalupe">Our Lady of Guadalupe</a>, often said to represent an aura</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The symbolism of light found in the <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Bible</a> is at times associated with the idea of the aura or "body of light":<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> similar interpretations are found in Islamic traditions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to the literature of <a href="/wiki/Theosophy" title="Theosophy">Theosophy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anthroposophy" title="Anthroposophy">Anthroposophy</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archeosophy" title="Archeosophy">Archeosophy</a> also, each colour of the aura has a meaning, indicating a precise emotional state. A complete description of the aura and its colours was provided by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Webster_Leadbeater" title="Charles Webster Leadbeater">Charles Leadbeater</a>, a theosophist of the 19th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The works of Leadbeater were later developed by <a href="/wiki/Tommaso_Palamidessi" title="Tommaso Palamidessi">Palamidessi</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and others.</p>
<p>The British <a href="/wiki/Occult" title="Occult">occultist</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/W.E._Butler" title="W.E. Butler">W.E. Butler</a> connected auras with <a href="/wiki/Clairvoyance" title="Clairvoyance">clairvoyance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Etheric_body" title="Etheric body">etheric</a>, mental and emotional emanations. He classified the aura into two main types: etheric and spiritual. Auras are thought to serve as a visual measure of the state of the health of the physical body.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Robert Bruce classifies auras into three types: etheric, main, and spiritual.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lindgren_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-lindgren-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:301-309</sup> According to Bruce auras are not actual light but a translation of other unknown sensory readings that is added to our visual processing. They are not seen in complete darkness and cannot be seen unless some portion of the person or object emitting the aura can also be seen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lindgren_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-lindgren-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:293-296</sup> The British Healer, clairvoyant and author Paul Lambillion in his book "Auras and Colours" writes of three visible bodies or layers in the auric field that can be observed whether or not in the physical presence of the individual subject since the aura is not a three dimensional phenomenon and limited to such parameters.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Glenn Morris, grandmaster of the <a href="/wiki/Bud%C5%8D" title="Budō">Hoshin</a> <a href="/wiki/R%C5%8Dshi" title="Rōshi">Roshi</a> <a href="/wiki/Ry%C5%AB_(school)" title="Ryū (school)">Ryu</a> lineage, included perception of the aura in his training of advanced martial artists. His experience was that it consisted of multiple layers. He described the most easily visible of these as being "light and denser than the air in which the body is immersed", typically half to quarter of an inch thick and correlating with the <a href="/wiki/Etheric_body" title="Etheric body">etheric body</a> of an individual. Around this he described a yard thick egg-shaped layer reflecting hormonal state that he linked to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emotional_body" title="Emotional body">emotional body</a>, and outside this, other barely perceptible layers corresponding to the <a href="/wiki/Mental_body" title="Mental body">mental body</a> and beyond.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-morris_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-morris-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:111-112</sup> Recalling the aura of another <a href="/wiki/S%C5%8Dke" title="Sōke">sōke</a>, he wrote, "<i>The first time I saw <a href="/wiki/Masaaki_Hatsumi" title="Masaaki Hatsumi">Hatsumi</a>, he was running continuous bright, lime, neon green a foot wide and was so easy to see he would flash in bright sunlight</i>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-morris_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-morris-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:118</sup></p>
<p>For holistic healers, aura reading is the art of investigating the human energy field, or the energy fields of other sentient beings. It is a basis for using techniques of holistic healing, and includes such practices as <a href="/wiki/Bioenergetics" title="Bioenergetics">bioenergetics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Energy_medicine" title="Energy medicine">energy medicine</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Energy_(spirituality)" title="Energy (spirituality)">energy spirituality</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Energy_(esotericism)" title="Energy (esotericism)">energy psychology</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tests">Tests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aura_(paranormal)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tests of psychic abilities to observe alleged aura emanations have repeatedly met with failure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nickell_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nickell-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>One test involved placing people in a dark room and asking the psychic to state how many auras she could observe. Only chance results were obtained.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Loftin_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Loftin-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Recognition of auras has occasionally been tested on television. One test involved an <i>aura reader</i> standing on one side of a room with an opaque partition separating her from a number of slots which might contain either actual people or mannequins. The aura reader failed to identify the slots containing people, incorrectly stating that all contained people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-skepdic_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-skepdic-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In another televised test another aura reader was placed before a partition where five people were standing. He claimed that he could see their auras from behind the partition. As each person moved out, the reader was asked to identify where that person was standing behind the slot. He identified only 2 out of 5 correctly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Randi_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Randi-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Explanation">Explanation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aura_(paranormal)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Explanation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bridgette Perez in a review for the <i><a href="/wiki/Skeptical_Inquirer" title="Skeptical Inquirer">Skeptical Inquirer</a></i> has written "perceptual distortions, illusions, and hallucinations might promote belief in auras... Psychological factors, including absorption, fantasy proneness, vividness of visual imagery, and after-images, might also be responsible for the phenomena of the aura."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Perez_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Perez-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Another explanation for the belief in auras, given that there is no scientific evidence for their reality, could be cases of <a href="/wiki/Synesthesia" title="Synesthesia">synesthesia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Perez_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-Perez-27">[27]</a></sup> However, a 2012 study discovered no link concluding "the discrepancies found suggest that both phenomena are phenomenologically and behaviourally dissimilar."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Clinical neurologist <a href="/wiki/Steven_Novella" title="Steven Novella">Steven Novella</a> has written "Given the weight of the evidence it seems that the connection between auras and synaesthesia is speculative and based on superficial similarities that are likely coincidental."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
