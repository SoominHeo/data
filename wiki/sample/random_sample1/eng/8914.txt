<p><b>Aurélio Fernández Miguel</b> (born March 10, 1964) is a <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> <a href="/wiki/Judo" title="Judo">judoka</a> and Olympic champion, and later politician. Among his best sporting achievements are his gold medal at the <a href="/wiki/1988_Summer_Olympics" title="1988 Summer Olympics">1988 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-db-ol1996_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-db-ol1996-1">[1]</a></sup> and a bronze medal at the <a href="/wiki/1996_Summer_Olympics" title="1996 Summer Olympics">1996 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-sr-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_career">Sports career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aur%C3%A9lio_Miguel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Sports career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aurélio Miguel was born on March 10, 1964 in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>. Due to <a href="/wiki/Bronchitis" title="Bronchitis">bronchitis</a> and the insistence of his father, Aurélio Marin, Aurélio Fernández Miguel began training in judo at the age of four years. Initially, Aurélio disliked judo, and as a child, was terrified of the roughness of the competitions and tournaments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-canal_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-canal-3">[3]</a></sup> As time passed, he became fond of the sport, and eventually won his first title in 1972. Aurélio Miguel then won the <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_(state)" title="São Paulo (state)">Paulista</a> tournament many times, and by the year 1980, he was recognized as the best judoka in the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr-judo_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-sr-judo-4">[4]</a></sup> Afterwards, Miguel started to compete internationally, winning the silver medal at the <a href="/wiki/Judo_at_the_1983_Pan_American_Games" title="Judo at the 1983 Pan American Games">1983 Pan American Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Caracas" title="Caracas">Caracas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-judo_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-judo-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-judo-panam-95kg_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-judo-panam-95kg-6">[6]</a></sup> He won the gold medal in the <a href="/wiki/Judo_at_the_1987_Pan_American_Games" title="Judo at the 1987 Pan American Games">1987 Pan American Games</a>, again fighting in the under 95 kg category.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-judo-panam-95kg_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-judo-panam-95kg-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1987 he also won a bronze medal at the <a href="/wiki/1987_World_Judo_Championships" title="1987 World Judo Championships">World Judo Championships</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-judo-wc-95kg_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-judo-wc-95kg-7">[7]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/1988_Summer_Olympics" title="1988 Summer Olympics">1988 Summer Olympics</a> in Seoul he became Olympic Champion, winning the -95 kg class by beating <a href="/wiki/Marc_Meiling" title="Marc Meiling">Marc Meiling</a> from West Germany in the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-sr-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr-1988ol_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-sr-1988ol-8">[8]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/1992_Summer_Olympics" title="1992 Summer Olympics">1992 Summer Olympics</a> in Barcelona he placed ninth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-sr-2">[2]</a></sup> He won a silver medal at the <a href="/wiki/1993_World_Judo_Championships" title="1993 World Judo Championships">1993 World Judo Championships</a> in Hamilton, losing the final to Hungarian judoka <a href="/wiki/Antal_Kov%C3%A1cs" title="Antal Kovács">Antal Kovács</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-judo-wc-95kg_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-judo-wc-95kg-7">[7]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/1996_Summer_Olympics" title="1996 Summer Olympics">1996 Summer Olympics</a> in Atlanta he received a bronze medal,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-sr-2">[2]</a></sup> and next year he won a silver medal at the <a href="/wiki/1997_World_Judo_Championships" title="1997 World Judo Championships">1997 World Judo Championships</a> in Paris, behind gold winner <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pawel_Nastula" title="Pawel Nastula">Pawel Nastula</a> from Poland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-judo-wc-95kg_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-judo-wc-95kg-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aur%C3%A9lio_Miguel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aurélio successfully ran for the <a href="/wiki/City_council" title="City council">city council</a> of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a> in October 2004, representing the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(Brazil)" title="Liberal Party (Brazil)">Partido Liberal</a> party, being reelected for a second term in 2008 under the banner of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_Party" title="Republic Party">Republic Party</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
