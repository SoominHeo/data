<p><b>Outback Steakhouse</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_Australia" title="Culture of Australia">Australian-themed</a> American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Casual_dining" title="Casual dining">casual dining</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Restaurant_chain" title="Restaurant chain">restaurant chain</a>, serving American cuisine, based in <a href="/wiki/Tampa,_Florida" title="Tampa, Florida">Tampa, Florida</a>, with almost 1,000 locations in 23 countries throughout <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. It was founded in February 1988 in Tampa by Bob Basham, <a href="/wiki/Chris_T._Sullivan" title="Chris T. Sullivan">Chris T. Sullivan</a>, Trudy Cooper, and Tim Gannon, and it was owned and operated in the United States by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OSI_Restaurant_Partners" title="OSI Restaurant Partners">OSI Restaurant Partners</a> until it was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Bloomin%27_Brands" title="Bloomin' Brands">Bloomin' Brands</a>, and by other franchise and venture agreements internationally.</p>
<p>Canadian Outback restaurants began in 1996. In March 2009, Outback Steakhouse Canada abruptly closed all nine locations in the province of <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, citing poor economic conditions. However, the restaurant later opened a location in <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Falls" title="Niagara Falls">Niagara Falls</a>; its <a href="/wiki/Edmonton" title="Edmonton">Edmonton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>, franchise remains in operation.</p>
<p>In 1997, Outback entered the <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> market through the franchise agreement with Aussie Chung Inc. Currently, there are 101 Outback Steakhouse locations throughout South Korea. On June 14, 2007, OSI Restaurant Partners completed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stock_repurchase" title="Stock repurchase">stock repurchase</a> plan, and the company is now privately held.</p>
<p>In April 2012, Bloomin' Brands, the current owner of Outback Steakhouse, filed with the SEC to raise up to $300 million in an initial public offering.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bloomin%27_Brands,_Inc." title="Bloomin' Brands, Inc.">Bloomin’ Brands, Inc.</a> became a publicly traded company on NASDAQ under the ticker symbol “BLMN.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Menu">Menu</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:227px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Outback_Steakhouse_CA.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2112" data-file-width="2816" height="169" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Outback_Steakhouse_CA.JPG/225px-Outback_Steakhouse_CA.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Outback_Steakhouse_CA.JPG/338px-Outback_Steakhouse_CA.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Outback_Steakhouse_CA.JPG/450px-Outback_Steakhouse_CA.JPG 2x" width="225"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Outback_Steakhouse_CA.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Restaurant at <a href="/wiki/Del_Amo_Fashion_Center" title="Del Amo Fashion Center">Del Amo Fashion Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Torrance,_California" title="Torrance, California">Torrance, California</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>All meat is heavily seasoned; there is a 17-spice blend for the steaks themselves. Outback bills its food as "full flavor". It fries its food in beef tallow and uses real butter and heavy cream in many dishes. <a href="/wiki/Hamburger" title="Hamburger">Burgers</a> are ground from <a href="/wiki/Beef_tenderloin" title="Beef tenderloin">beef tenderloin</a>. Menus are highly regionalized. <a href="/wiki/Crayfish" title="Crayfish">Crawfish</a> appear in some dishes in <a href="/wiki/Southern_United_States" title="Southern United States">Southern American</a> locations, as do <a href="/wiki/Sweet_potato" title="Sweet potato">sweet potatoes</a>. <a href="/wiki/Eastern_United_States" title="Eastern United States">Eastern</a> locations often feature <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a> <a href="/wiki/American_lobster" title="American lobster">lobster</a> tails, while <a href="/wiki/Western_United_States" title="Western United States">western</a> locations frequently serve <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaskan</a> <a href="/wiki/King_crab" title="King crab">king crab</a> legs and cakes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_20_Worst_Foods_in_America_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_20_Worst_Foods_in_America-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Outback's bar selections are highly regionalized. Outback serves <a href="/wiki/Foster%27s_Lager" title="Foster's Lager">Foster's Lager</a>, an Australian brand of beer exported around the world. Other Australian beers often served include Toohey's New, Boag's, and Coopers Premium Lager, Sparkling Ale, and Pale Ale. Wine selections vary but often include Australian wineries Yellow Tail, Lindeman's, and Rosemount.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_20_Worst_Foods_in_America_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_20_Worst_Foods_in_America-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although a number of menu items are <a href="/wiki/Grilling" title="Grilling">grilled</a>, steaks are prepared on a <a href="/wiki/Griddle" title="Griddle">griddle</a> using butter as a heat transfer medium. Because they are cooked solely by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heat_conduction" title="Heat conduction">conduction</a>, their surfaces are cooked much more rapidly than their centers, and Outback follows a standard <a href="/wiki/Doneness" title="Doneness">meat temperature (rarity) scale</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_20_Worst_Foods_in_America_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-The_20_Worst_Foods_in_America-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The use of fats in such liberal quantities has brought the chain under major fire from many health advocates: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Men%27s_Health_(magazine)" title="Men's Health (magazine)">Men's Health</a> magazine condemned Outback's Aussie Cheese Fries as the "Worst Food in America," with 182 grams (1,638 calories) of fat and nearly 3,000 calories per order.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_20_Worst_Foods_in_America_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-The_20_Worst_Foods_in_America-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, Outback Steakhouse offers a pork-free menu to cater to Muslim customers.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dietary_considerations">Dietary considerations</span></h3>
<p>The lamb served at Outback comes from <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> and is certified <a href="/wiki/Halal" title="Halal">halal</a> by the Islamic Council of New Zealand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Outback also offers a <a href="/wiki/Gluten-free_diet" title="Gluten-free diet">gluten free</a> menu which includes most of its normal menu items with slight variations. Outback has recently added gluten-free beverage choices, including <a href="/wiki/Angry_Orchard" title="Angry Orchard">Angry Orchard</a> cider.</p>
<p>Since early 2010, Outback has displayed full nutritional data on its food.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Outback has now added a "light style" option to the menu. This option is designed to prepare meals under 500 calories by carefully proportioning out sides and preparing the meals without butter and added fats and salt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bloomin.27_Onion"><i>Bloomin' Onion</i></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Blooming_onion" title="Blooming onion">Blooming onion</a></div>
<p>The <i>Bloomin' Onion</i> is a signature Outback item. It is a one-pound onion cut to 'bloom' open, breaded, deep-fried and served with mayonnaise-horseradish sauce. Other restaurants offer items similar to the <i>Bloomin' Onion</i>, as in <a href="/wiki/Chili%27s" title="Chili's">Chili's</a> <i>Awesome Blossom</i> (discontinued) and <a href="/wiki/Lone_Star_Steakhouse_%26_Saloon" title="Lone Star Steakhouse &amp; Saloon">Lone Star Steakhouse &amp; Saloon</a>'s <i>Texas Rose</i>. Many of the Blooming onions have come under fire for high-<a href="/wiki/Calorie" title="Calorie">calorie</a> counts, sometimes going over 1,500 calories.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nutritional_Information_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nutritional_Information-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Involvements">Involvements</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Community">Community</span></h3>
<p>Outback Steakhouse has a program to support the communities in which its restaurants are located.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On a national level, Outback partners with Heineken USA and their Heineken with a Heart program to raise funds for various organizations in these communities.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Political">Political</span></h3>
<p>The company and its founders are major contributors, via the Outback Steakhouse <a href="/wiki/Political_action_committee" title="Political action committee">PAC</a>, to the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican Party</a>, contributing $303,015 and $334,197 for the 2000 and 2004 election cycles, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Outback_Steakhouse_Soft_Money_Donations_for_2000_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Outback_Steakhouse_Soft_Money_Donations_for_2000-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Outback_Steakhouse_Soft_Money_Donations_for_2004_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Outback_Steakhouse_Soft_Money_Donations_for_2004-11">[11]</a></sup> The Outback Steakhouse PAC itself is one of the largest donors in the food and beverage sector, second only to the <a href="/wiki/National_Restaurant_Association" title="National Restaurant Association">National Restaurant Association</a>, which itself represents 300,000 restaurants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Food_.26_Beverage_PAC_Contributions_to_Federal_Candidates.2C_2003-2004_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Food_.26_Beverage_PAC_Contributions_to_Federal_Candidates.2C_2003-2004-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span></h3>
<p>Outback Steakhouse was the title sponsor of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Outback_Champions_Series" title="Outback Champions Series">Outback Champions Series</a> <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a> events and NCAA football's <a href="/wiki/Outback_Bowl" title="Outback Bowl">Outback Bowl</a>. The sponsorship will last through 2020.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Outback is also a partner of the Tampa Bay Lightning and has a concession stand in Amalie Arena.</p>

<p>In 2012, Outback Steakhouse became a sponsor of the <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Newman" title="Ryan Newman">Ryan Newman</a> and the No.39 <a href="/wiki/Chevrolet" title="Chevrolet">Chevrolet</a> for <a href="/wiki/Stewart-Haas_Racing" title="Stewart-Haas Racing">Stewart-Haas Racing</a> in the <a href="/wiki/NASCAR" title="NASCAR">NASCAR</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sprint_Cup_Series" title="Sprint Cup Series">Sprint Cup Series</a>. In their first race as sponsor, Newman won the <a href="/wiki/2012_Goody%27s_Fast_Relief_500" title="2012 Goody's Fast Relief 500">2012 Goody's Fast Relief 500</a> at <a href="/wiki/Martinsville_Speedway" title="Martinsville Speedway">Martinsville Speedway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> One of the promotions the company had with Newman is a free Bloomin' Onion on the Monday after a race in which Newman would finish in the Top 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Harvick" title="Kevin Harvick">Kevin Harvick</a> took over Outback's sponsorship and promotion in <a href="/wiki/2014_NASCAR_Sprint_Cup_Series" title="2014 NASCAR Sprint Cup Series">2014</a> for two races in their No. 4, after Newman left the team to drive <a href="/wiki/Richard_Childress_Racing" title="Richard Childress Racing">Richard Childress Racing</a>'s No.31 car.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The company also sponsored retired <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sprint_Cup_Series" title="Sprint Cup Series">Sprint Cup Series</a> driver and current <i><a href="/wiki/Fox_NASCAR" title="Fox NASCAR">Fox NASCAR</a></i> announcer <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Gordon" title="Jeff Gordon">Jeff Gordon</a>'s No. 67 for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AC-Delco_200" title="AC-Delco 200">AC-Delco 200</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Xfinity_Series" title="Xfinity Series">Busch Series</a> during the <a href="/wiki/1990_NASCAR_Busch_Series" title="1990 NASCAR Busch Series">1990 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
