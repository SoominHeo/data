<p><b>I See Stars</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Electronicore" title="Electronicore">electronicore</a> band that formed in 2006 based in <a href="/wiki/Warren,_Michigan" title="Warren, Michigan">Warren, Michigan</a>. The band currently consists of vocalist Devin Oliver, guitarist Brent Allen, keyboardist and vocalist Andrew Oliver, and bass guitarist Jeff Valentine.</p>
<p>Their first album, <i><a href="/wiki/3-D_(I_See_Stars_album)" title="3-D (I See Stars album)">3-D</a></i> (2009), peaked at No. 176 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. Their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_End_of_the_World_Party" title="The End of the World Party">The End of the World Party</a></i>, was released on February 22, 2011. The band's third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Digital_Renegade" title="Digital Renegade">Digital Renegade</a></i>, was released on March 13, 2012, which demonstrated a stylistic change, featuring a more aggressive musical style than their prior albums.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The band also appeared on the song "Beauty and The Beast" by <a href="/wiki/Dubstep" title="Dubstep">Dubstep</a> producer BARE. The band's fourth studio album, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Demons_(I_See_Stars_album)" title="New Demons (I See Stars album)">New Demons</a></i>, was released October 22, 2013, which featured elements of <a href="/wiki/Electronic_dance_music" title="Electronic dance music">EDM</a> while achieving an even further aggressive style than <i>Digital Renegade</i>. On March 25, 2015, the band released their first <a href="/wiki/Remix_album" title="Remix album">remix album</a>, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Phases_(album)" title="Phases (album)">Phases</a></i>. The band's line-up remained unchanged, (excluding the brief departure of vocalist Zach Johnson in 2009, who returned the following year) from their formation in 2006, up until mid-2015, when the group announced the departure of rhythm guitarist Jimmy Gregerson and Johnson. Lead vocalist Devin Oliver now pursues both <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clean_vocals" title="Clean vocals">clean vocals</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unclean_vocals" title="Unclean vocals">unclean vocals</a>. Their fifth studio album and the first album featuring the new line-up, <i><a href="/wiki/Treehouse_(I_See_Stars_album)" title="Treehouse (I See Stars album)">Treehouse</a></i>, was released on June 17, 2016.</p>
<p>I See Stars has been labeled as <a href="/wiki/Electronicore" title="Electronicore">electronicore</a>, incorporating elements of <a href="/wiki/Post-hardcore" title="Post-hardcore">post-hardcore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Metalcore" title="Metalcore">metalcore</a> music with their eclectic <a href="/wiki/Electronica" title="Electronica">electronica</a> sound, becoming one of the first well known electronicore bands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The band has released all five of their studio albums, and other releases, through their record label <a href="/wiki/Sumerian_Records" title="Sumerian Records">Sumerian Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_See_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Green_Light_Go.21_.282006.E2.80.932008.29"><i>Green Light Go!</i> (2006–2008)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_See_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Green Light Go! (2006–2008)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In an interview with BVTV,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BVTV_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-BVTV-4">[4]</a></sup> featuring band members Devin Oliver and Brent Allen, Oliver stated: "me, Brent, the drummer's my brother, and the bass player, Jeff, we all lived on the block from each other since we were kids, [in] elementary school." In the same interview, it is also stated that there is no specific reason for the band's name, I See Stars, other than the fact they needed a title and "someone brought it to the table". The band was officially formed in 2006.</p>
<p>The group released its debut EP <i>Green Light Go!</i> in 2007 and several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Demo_tape" title="Demo tape">demos</a>, including a self-titled EP, before signing with <a href="/wiki/Sumerian_Records" title="Sumerian Records">Sumerian Records</a> in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="3-D.2C_Johnson.27s_departure_and_Moore.27s_arrival_.282008.E2.80.932009.29"><i>3-D</i>, Johnson's departure and Moore's arrival (2008–2009)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_See_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 3-D, Johnson's departure and Moore's arrival (2008–2009)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>I See Stars recorded their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/3-D_(I_See_Stars_album)" title="3-D (I See Stars album)">3-D</a></i>, in 2008 at Chango Gridlock Studios in <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando, Florida</a> with producer Cameron Mizell. The album featured Zach Johnson as the <a href="/wiki/Screaming_(music)" title="Screaming (music)">screamer</a> and keyboardist. Johnson left the band soon after the album's release on April 14, 2009, due to personal problems he felt interfered with him too much while on tour. The band then gained <a href="/wiki/Chris_Moore_(musician)" title="Chris Moore (musician)">Chris Moore</a>, formerly of <a href="/wiki/We_Came_as_Romans" title="We Came as Romans">We Came as Romans</a> and a merch salesman for the group, to join as the new screamer and keyboardist. In August 2008, I See Stars embarked on the second annual "Artery Foundation Across the Nation" tour, headlined by <a href="/wiki/Emarosa" title="Emarosa">Emarosa</a> with supporters <a href="/wiki/Our_Last_Night" title="Our Last Night">Our Last Night</a>, <a href="/wiki/In_Fear_and_Faith" title="In Fear and Faith">In Fear and Faith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burden_of_a_Day" title="Burden of a Day">Burden of a Day</a> and Broadway. In late 2009, I See Stars finished touring with <a href="/wiki/Attack_Attack!" title="Attack Attack!">Attack Attack!</a>, <a href="/wiki/Breathe_Carolina" title="Breathe Carolina">Breathe Carolina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asking_Alexandria" title="Asking Alexandria">Asking Alexandria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bury_Tomorrow" title="Bury Tomorrow">Bury Tomorrow</a> for the third annual <i>Artery Foundation Across the Nation</i> tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Artery_Foundation.2C_LLC_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Artery_Foundation.2C_LLC-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Moore.27s_departure.2C_Johnson.27s_return_and_The_End_of_the_World_Party_.282010.29">Moore's departure, Johnson's return and <i>The End of the World Party</i> (2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_See_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Moore's departure, Johnson's return and The End of the World Party (2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 14, 2010 the band's keyboardist/screamer <a href="/wiki/Chris_Moore_(musician)" title="Chris Moore (musician)">Chris Moore</a> announced his departure from the band, citing "artistic differences". After being asked to re-join, former I See Stars member Zach Johnson returned to the band and joined them on Warped Tour 2010.</p>
<p>The group entered the studio in March 2010 to begin writing and recording their sophomore studio album called <i><a href="/wiki/The_End_of_the_World_Party" title="The End of the World Party">The End of the World Party</a></i> with Sumerian Records and <i>3-D</i> producer Cameron Mizell. Almost immediately after finishing the album, the band played Vans <a href="/wiki/Warped_Tour" title="Warped Tour">Warped Tour</a> from June 24 to July 18, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vans_Warped_Tour_2010_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vans_Warped_Tour_2010-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The band released a preview of their second album's title track, "The End of the World Party", on November 4, 2010 to Sumerian Records' <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> page. They played the song at various live shows in 2010 before the album's release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The full version of the title track was released to <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> on December 7, 2010.</p>
<p>The band revealed the album's track listing on January 2, 2011. Before the album's release, the band played another song entitled "Glow" at various live concerts. On February 1, 2011, <i><a href="/wiki/Alternative_Press" title="Alternative Press">Alternative Press</a></i> started streaming a new song from the album entitled "Wonderland".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The full album was released on February 22, 2011.</p>
<p>The band has played in the Soundwave 2011 tour in Australia. They supported <a href="/wiki/We_the_Kings" title="We the Kings">We the Kings</a> alongside <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VersaEmerge" title="VersaEmerge">VersaEmerge</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. They also participated in the 2011 American AP Tour along with Conditions, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VersaEmerge" title="VersaEmerge">VersaEmerge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Destroy_Rebuild_Until_God_Shows" title="Destroy Rebuild Until God Shows">Destroy Rebuild Until God Shows</a> and <a href="/wiki/Black_Veil_Brides" title="Black Veil Brides">Black Veil Brides</a>.</p>
<p>On May 31, 2011, the band made their national television debut, while performing the songs "Glow" and "What This Means To Me" on <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live!" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live!">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Digital_Renegade_.282011.E2.80.932012.29"><i>Digital Renegade</i> (2011–2012)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_See_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Digital Renegade (2011–2012)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>I See Stars toured on the Monster Outbreak Tour with headliner <a href="/wiki/Of_Mice_%26_Men_(band)" title="Of Mice &amp; Men (band)">Of Mice &amp; Men</a> and supporting bands <a href="/wiki/Iwrestledabearonce" title="Iwrestledabearonce">Iwrestledabearonce</a>, <a href="/wiki/For_the_Fallen_Dreams" title="For the Fallen Dreams">For the Fallen Dreams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Abandon_All_Ships" title="Abandon All Ships">Abandon All Ships</a> and That's Outrageous! from October 26 to November 29. The band toured on the Scream It Like You Mean It tour with <a href="/wiki/Breathe_Carolina" title="Breathe Carolina">Breathe Carolina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chiodos" title="Chiodos">Chiodos</a> as headliners. The tour also featured the rapper Mod Sun, <a href="/wiki/The_Color_Morale" title="The Color Morale">The Color Morale</a> and the new metalcore band from Rise Records, The Air I Breathe. On recent interviews the band stated that they began recording a new album in September 2011, as soon as the Scream It Like You Mean It Tour finished. After a few recording sessions they began another tour in November. The album was produced by <a href="/wiki/Joey_Sturgis" title="Joey Sturgis">Joey Sturgis</a>.</p>
<p>I See Stars headlined the Leave It 2 The Suits Tour with support from <a href="/wiki/Stick_to_Your_Guns" title="Stick to Your Guns">Stick to Your Guns</a>, <a href="/wiki/Memphis_May_Fire" title="Memphis May Fire">Memphis May Fire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Our_Last_Night" title="Our Last Night">Our Last Night</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Make_Me_Famous" title="Make Me Famous">Make Me Famous</a>. In a commercial video for the tour, it was announced that their third album would be released on March 13, 2012, with the album title confirmed to be <i>Digital Renegade</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> At midnight on January 8, 2012, an unmastered version of the song "Filth Friends Unite" from the then-upcoming album was released via the band's Facebook page.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> A finalized version of the song was released as the first official single from the album on January 17.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> I See Stars second single, "NZT48", was released on YouTube on February 23 as a lyric video. The song was released on iTunes February 28,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> also with the album put on pre-order. The full album was released on March 14, 2012.</p>
<p>On August 30, 2012, all members were arrested in <a href="/wiki/Saline_County,_Kansas" title="Saline County, Kansas">Saline County, Kansas</a>, for possessing and intending to use "hallucinogenic drugs", though it was not specified which drugs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arrest_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-arrest-15">[15]</a></sup> The members themselves released their <a href="/wiki/Mug_shot" title="Mug shot">mug shots</a> and Devin commented on his Twitter profile that he "just had the worst 13 hours of my life".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arrest_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-arrest-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>A new single, titled "The Hardest Mistakes," was released on October 9, 2012 and it features <a href="/wiki/Cassadee_Pope" title="Cassadee Pope">Cassadee Pope</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> It is a remix of the song "Electric Forest." They also played on Falling In Reverse's "The Thug In Me Is You" tour for a few dates, but they were kicked off the tour early.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> They have stated that they are going to be touring again soon, regardless of the feud between the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Falling_In_Reverse" title="Falling In Reverse">Falling In Reverse</a> and I See Stars camps. I See Stars then hit the road with <a href="/wiki/Asking_Alexandria" title="Asking Alexandria">Asking Alexandria</a> on the Monster Outbreak tour along with <a href="/wiki/As_I_Lay_Dying_(band)" title="As I Lay Dying (band)">As I Lay Dying</a>, <a href="/wiki/Memphis_May_Fire" title="Memphis May Fire">Memphis May Fire</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Attila_(deathcore_band)" title="Attila (deathcore band)">Attila</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Renegades_Forever_and_New_Demons_.282013.E2.80.932015.29"><i>Renegades Forever</i> and <i>New Demons</i> (2013–2015)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_See_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Renegades Forever and New Demons (2013–2015)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In early to mid 2013, the band finished their fourth studio album, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Demons_(I_See_Stars_album)" title="New Demons (I See Stars album)">New Demons</a></i>, which is slated to be released on October 22. It's been confirmed that Joey Sturgis is the producer for the studio album. <a href="/wiki/Klayton" title="Klayton">Klayton</a> of <a href="/wiki/Celldweller" title="Celldweller">Celldweller</a> programmed four tracks on the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Currently_working_on..._18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Currently_working_on...-18">[18]</a></sup> The band later announced that they have recorded a "heavier" song featuring vocalist Frank Palmeri of <a href="/wiki/Emmure" title="Emmure">Emmure</a> and a cameo appearance by <a href="/wiki/Mattie_Montgomery" title="Mattie Montgomery">Mattie Montgomery</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/For_Today_(band)" title="For Today (band)">For Today</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> That song, "Can We Start Again," was released on YouTube on May 12, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Can_We_Start_Again_.28Video.29_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Can_We_Start_Again_.28Video.29-20">[20]</a></sup> The song's origins "started off as a b-side off our previous album and evolved into much more than that. With help from Sumerian Records and our friends Frankie Palmeri &amp; Mattie Montgomery, it became about a scene that is much like the hallways we walked down as teenagers. We used the iconic lyrics from a song by <a href="/wiki/Bane_(band)" title="Bane (band)">Bane</a> that changed the scene before bands like us were even around and still holds a place in a lot of hearts. We're all here to connect through music and to be a part of a movement that embraces open minds and open hearts. Unfortunately, some people choose to thrive off negativity, disunion and narcissism. This song goes out to all our peers and friends who live on the road doing it every single year, but more importantly to all the fans who make this all possible. This is not a song off the forthcoming album. It's the closing chapter to [digital_renegade] and the opening track to our digital mixtape release titled "Renegades Forever"."</p>
<p>On June 17, 2013, <a href="/wiki/Alternative_Press" title="Alternative Press">Alternative Press</a> published an exclusive premiere on their website of the first single from <i>New Demons</i> entitled "Violent Bounce (People Like You)".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The release date of <i>New Demons</i> has been reset from September 17 to October 22, after previously being selected for an August 13 release date. Via the I See Stars Twitter account, the band had tweeted on September 16 "I know a lot of you think the album is coming out tomorrow. Unfortunately had to push things back. We're so sorry to those expecting it :(".</p>
<p>On Warped Tour 2013, the band started to play a new, unheard song on stage. The song was revealed to be 'Ten Thousand Feet' and will feature on the upcoming album 'New Demons'. Alongside this, a 20-second preview of the song 'Murder Mitten' was added on the end of Sumerian Records' 'Started From The Bottom, Now We Here Tour' uploaded on the record labels YouTube channel. On September 24 at Midnight, the band released the second single titled "Murder Mitten". The single was released alongside a lyric video on YouTube. On October 8, I See Stars released the title track 'New Demons' as a free song to those who pre-ordered the album. The album then leaked on October 10.</p>
<p>On August 27, 2013, the band announced their North American headlining tour Started From The Bottom Now We Here with <a href="/wiki/The_Word_Alive" title="The Word Alive">the Word Alive</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crown_the_Empire" title="Crown the Empire">Crown the Empire</a>, and guests <a href="/wiki/Dayshell" title="Dayshell">Dayshell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Get_Scared" title="Get Scared">Get Scared</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Palisades_(band)" title="Palisades (band)">Palisades</a>, beginning in Albuquerque, NM at The Historic Sunshine Theater on October 29 and ending in St. Louis, MO on November 24.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 13, 2013 the band announced that they will be direct supporting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Attila_(deathcore_band)" title="Attila (deathcore band)">Attila</a>'s "The New King's Tour" with supporting acts <a href="/wiki/Capture_the_Crown" title="Capture the Crown">Capture the Crown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ice_Nine_Kills" title="Ice Nine Kills">Ice Nine Kills</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Myka,_Relocate" title="Myka, Relocate">Myka, Relocate</a> beginning on January 31, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>They headlined The All Stars Tour in Summer 2014. They also toured in December 2014 with Razihel.</p>
<p>An acoustic album could possibly be in the works.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>On June 16, 2015, the band's Facebook page was hacked and used to promote <a href="/wiki/Islamism" title="Islamism">Islamist</a> propaganda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Phases.2C_departure_of_Gregerson.2C_Johnson.27s_second_exit.2C_and_Treehouse_.282015.E2.80.93present.29"><i>Phases</i>, departure of Gregerson, Johnson's second exit, and <i>Treehouse</i> (2015–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_See_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Phases, departure of Gregerson, Johnson's second exit, and Treehouse (2015–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 31, 2015, the band announced their newest release, <i>Phases</i>, an album centered around re-imagining songs from previous releases with an acoustic, clean style.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Incomparable_review_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Incomparable_review-26">[26]</a></sup> The band would also include four cover songs on the track-list. As well as announcing the all acoustic album, I See Stars announced they would be headlining an acoustic tour known as the "Phases Tour". After the release of the album rhythm guitarist Jimmy Gregerson, distanced himself from and quietly left the band after ceasing to perform with the band and not playing on the album, and subsequently became a touring member of <a href="/wiki/Metro_Station_(band)" title="Metro Station (band)">Metro Station</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Although it was revealed on his Instagram account that he had actually left the band during <a href="/wiki/Warped_Tour" title="Warped Tour">Warped Tour 2015</a>, this information was not made public until at least a month after his departure and was later confirmed once again in December 2015, via Twitter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Unclean vocalist and keyboardist Zach Johnson did not appear on Phases, nor did he tour with the band in support of it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 22, 2015, the band released a short teaser video for a forthcoming headline tour of the US, which also included a teaser of new material expected for release in 2016. It's unclear whether this would be a one-off song, or come from a forthcoming EP or album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> On the same day Gregerson and Johnson made separate posts on their Instagram accounts announcing that they had been released from the band a few months ago, although neither of them officially explained what led up to their departure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Johnson later announced in the same post that he had formed a new band called Therefore Farewell later renamed Outer Glow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> It was later stated that clean vocalist Devin Oliver will be taking over both clean and unclean vocal duties, while drummer Andrew Oliver would be switching to keyboards and taking up some of Devin's cleans and Zach's unclean vocals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was officially announced on the band's <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> page that their fifth album, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Treehouse_(I_See_Stars_album)" title="Treehouse (I See Stars album)">Treehouse</a></i> will be released on June 17. The band is currently participating in the "10 Years In The Black" Tour with <a href="/wiki/Asking_Alexandria" title="Asking Alexandria">Asking Alexandria</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Born_Of_Osiris" title="Born Of Osiris">Born Of Osiris</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/After_The_Burial" title="After The Burial">After The Burial</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Upon_A_Burning_Body" title="Upon A Burning Body">Upon A Burning Body</a>, and Bad Omens.</p>
