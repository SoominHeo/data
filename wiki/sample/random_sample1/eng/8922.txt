<p>An <b>"I Quit" Match</b> is a type of <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestling</a> <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types" title="Professional wrestling match types">match</a> in which the only way to win is to make the opponent say the words "I quit" (usually into a <a href="/wiki/Microphone" title="Microphone">microphone</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Encyclopedia_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Encyclopedia-1">[1]</a></sup> It is a variation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Submission_match" title="Submission match">submission match</a>, as it can only be won by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Submission_(combat_sport)" title="Submission (combat sport)">submission</a>, but is special in that the submission has to take the form of the forfeiting opponent actually saying "I quit". Generally, whenever a wrestler knocks down his opponent with a move or inflicts a submission move,the opponent will be asked – either by the referee or the opponent – to say the words into a microphone. "I quit" matches are commonly used to settle <a href="/wiki/Kayfabe" title="Kayfabe">kayfabe</a> grudges and embarrass rivals, since saying "I quit" is usually a sign of admitted inferiority.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%22I_Quit%22_match&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first "I Quit" match was in the <a href="/wiki/National_Wrestling_Alliance" title="National Wrestling Alliance">National Wrestling Alliance</a>/<a href="/wiki/Jim_Crockett_Promotions" title="Jim Crockett Promotions">Jim Crockett Promotions</a> at <a href="/wiki/Starrcade_(1985)" title="Starrcade (1985)">Starrcade</a> on November 28, 1985. <a href="/wiki/Magnum_T.A." title="Magnum T.A.">Magnum T.A.</a> defeated <a href="/wiki/Tully_Blanchard" title="Tully Blanchard">Tully Blanchard</a> in a match (with a cage around the ring) for Blanchard's <a href="/wiki/WWE_United_States_Championship" title="WWE United States Championship">NWA United States Heavyweight Championship</a>. Near the end of that match, Magnum used a piece of wood with a nail in it to pierce Blanchard's forehead. At that point, Blanchard screamed "YES! YES!" into the microphone, indicating that he was quitting the match, and Magnum won the U.S. Championship.</p>
<p>One of the most famous "I Quit" matches took place on January 24, 1999, at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Rumble_(1999)" title="Royal Rumble (1999)">Royal Rumble</a> between <a href="/wiki/Dwayne_Johnson" title="Dwayne Johnson">The Rock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mick_Foley" title="Mick Foley">Mick Foley</a> (as Mankind) in the <a href="/wiki/WWE#World_Wrestling_Federation" title="WWE">World Wrestling Federation</a>. The match lasted just over 20 minutes, ending after Foley took eleven unprotected chair shots to the head while his hands were <a href="/wiki/Handcuffs" title="Handcuffs">handcuffed</a> behind his back. The Rock eventually won the match, but it was later revealed that Foley was completely unconscious and The Rock had played a tape of Foley saying "I quit" over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_address" title="Public address">PA system</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Encyclopedia_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Encyclopedia-1">[1]</a></sup> The match the following evening between Triple H and The Rock ended in a completely different way – <a href="/wiki/Triple_H" title="Triple H">Triple H</a> was <a href="/wiki/Extortion" title="Extortion">extorted</a> into saying "I quit" because <a href="/wiki/Kane_(wrestler)" title="Kane (wrestler)">Kane</a> was about to chokeslam <a href="/wiki/Chyna" title="Chyna">Chyna</a>. <a href="/wiki/John_Cena" title="John Cena">John Cena</a> retained his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_World_Heavyweight_Championship" title="WWE World Heavyweight Championship">WWE Championship</a> by defeating <a href="/wiki/John_Layfield" title="John Layfield">John "Bradshaw" Layfield</a> in an "I Quit" Match at <a href="/wiki/Judgment_Day_(2005)" title="Judgment Day (2005)">Judgment Day</a> on May 22, 2005.</p>
<p>"I Quit" matches involving women have been rare. At <a href="/wiki/No_Mercy_(2003)" title="No Mercy (2003)">No Mercy 2003</a> held an intergender match between WWE chairman <a href="/wiki/Vince_McMahon" title="Vince McMahon">Mr. McMahon</a> and his daughter <a href="/wiki/Stephanie_McMahon" title="Stephanie McMahon">Stephanie</a> partly under "I Quit" rules. However, the match was actually decided when Stephanie's mother <a href="/wiki/Linda_McMahon" title="Linda McMahon">Linda</a> threw in the towel for her daughter, giving Vince the victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Encyclopedia_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Encyclopedia-1">[1]</a></sup> The first "I Quit" match between <a href="/wiki/WWE_Diva" title="WWE Diva">WWE Divas</a> was contested at <a href="/wiki/One_Night_Stand_(2008)" title="One Night Stand (2008)">One Night Stand</a> in 2008, when <a href="/wiki/Beth_Phoenix" title="Beth Phoenix">Beth Phoenix</a> defeated <a href="/wiki/Melina_Perez" title="Melina Perez">Melina</a>.</p>
<p>On the May 14, 2009 edition of <i><a href="/wiki/Impact_Wrestling" title="Impact Wrestling">Impact</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Total_Nonstop_Action_Wrestling" title="Total Nonstop Action Wrestling">Total Nonstop Action Wrestling</a> (TNA) put on their first "I Quit" match, where <a href="/wiki/Booker_T_(wrestler)" title="Booker T (wrestler)">Booker T</a> defeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Mueller" title="William Mueller">Jethro Holiday</a> after an axe kick. TNA had another "I Quit" match on May 24, 2009, where <a href="/wiki/A.J._Styles" title="A.J. Styles">A.J. Styles</a> defeated Booker T to retain the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TNA_Legends_Championship" title="TNA Legends Championship">TNA Legends Championship</a>, after <a href="/wiki/Jenna_Morasca" title="Jenna Morasca">Jenna Morasca</a> threw a towel into the ring for Booker.</p>
<p>On the May 4, 2016, edition of <i><a href="/wiki/Lucha_Underground" title="Lucha Underground">Lucha Underground</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Sexy_Star" title="Sexy Star">Sexy Star</a> defeated <a href="/wiki/Cheerleader_Melissa" title="Cheerleader Melissa">Mariposa</a> in the promotion's first "I Quit" match, known in Spanish as a "No Mas" match.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id=".22I_Respect_You.22_match">"I Respect You" match</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%22I_Quit%22_match&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title='Edit section: "I Respect You" match'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An "I Respect You" match is similar to an "I Quit" match – to win a wrestler must make his opponent say the words, "I respect you". The first "I Respect You" match was held in <a href="/wiki/World_Championship_Wrestling" title="World Championship Wrestling">World Championship Wrestling</a> (WCW) at <a href="/wiki/SuperBrawl#SuperBrawl_VI" title="SuperBrawl">SuperBrawl VI</a> in February 1996 and pitted <a href="/wiki/Brian_Pillman" title="Brian Pillman">Brian Pillman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Sullivan_(wrestler)" title="Kevin Sullivan (wrestler)">"Taskmaster" Kevin Sullivan</a>. At the end of the match, Pillman lost by saying "I respect you, bookerman!" In doing so he Pillman broke <a href="/wiki/Kayfabe" title="Kayfabe">kayfabe</a>, as Sullivan was also WCW <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Book" title="Glossary of professional wrestling terms">booker</a> at the time.</p>
