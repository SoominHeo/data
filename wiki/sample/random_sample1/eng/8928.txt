<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">List of <i>The Idolmaster Cinderella Girls</i> episodes</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Idolmaster_Cinderella_Girls" title="The Idolmaster Cinderella Girls">The Idolmaster Cinderella Girls</a></i> is a 2015 <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> series based on the <a href="/wiki/Simulation_video_game" title="Simulation video game">simulation</a> <a href="/wiki/Social_network_game" title="Social network game">social network game</a> of the same name as part of <a href="/wiki/Bandai_Namco_Entertainment" title="Bandai Namco Entertainment">Bandai Namco Entertainment</a><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Idolmaster" title="The Idolmaster">The Idolmaster</a></i> franchise. The series follows a group of rookie idols as they are recruited by 346 Production's Cinderella Project and aspire to become top idols. The anime is produced by <a href="/wiki/A-1_Pictures" title="A-1 Pictures">A-1 Pictures</a> and directed by Noriko Takao. Takao and writer Tatsuya Takahashi share supervision duty for the series screenplay, and Yūsuke Matsuo based the character designs on designs provided by illustrator Annin Dōfu;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ann_anime_announcement_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ann_anime_announcement-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> the music was composed by Hidekazu Tanaka of Monaca. The series is airing in two separate seasons, alternating between reruns of the first <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_The_Idolmaster_episodes" title="List of The Idolmaster episodes">The Idolmaster</a></i> anime series. The first season aired on <a href="/wiki/Nippon_BS_Broadcasting" title="Nippon BS Broadcasting">BS11</a> via <a href="/wiki/Satellite_television" title="Satellite television">satellite</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metropolitan_Television" title="Tokyo Metropolitan Television">Tokyo MX</a> and nine other local independent terrestrial stations between January 10 and April 11, 2015 and was simulcast by <a href="/wiki/Daisuki_(website)" title="Daisuki (website)">Daisuki</a>. The second season began aired from July 17 to October 17, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The opening theme for the first season is "Star!!" by Cinderella Project (<a href="/wiki/Ayaka_%C5%8Chashi" title="Ayaka Ōhashi">Ayaka Ōhashi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ayaka_Fukuhara" title="Ayaka Fukuhara">Ayaka Fukuhara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sayuri_Hara" title="Sayuri Hara">Sayuri Hara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tomoyo_Kurosawa" title="Tomoyo Kurosawa">Tomoyo Kurosawa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sumire_Uesaka" title="Sumire Uesaka">Sumire Uesaka</a>, Naomi Ōzora, <a href="/wiki/Maaya_Uchida" title="Maaya Uchida">Maaya Uchida</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nozomi_Yamamoto" title="Nozomi Yamamoto">Nozomi Yamamoto</a>, Ruriko Aoki, <a href="/wiki/Aya_Suzaki" title="Aya Suzaki">Aya Suzaki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hiromi_Igarashi" title="Hiromi Igarashi">Hiromi Igarashi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natsumi_Takamori" title="Natsumi Takamori">Natsumi Takamori</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yuka_%C5%8Ctsubo" title="Yuka Ōtsubo">Yuka Ōtsubo</a>, and Rei Matsuzaki). The second season's opening theme is "Shine!!" by Cinderella Project.</p>
