<p><b>Aydın Province</b> (<a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <span lang="tr" xml:lang="tr"><i><span style="padding-right:0.1em;">Aydın ili</span></i></span>) is a province of southwestern <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, located in the <a href="/wiki/Aegean_Region" title="Aegean Region">Aegean Region</a>. The provincial capital is the city of <a href="/wiki/Ayd%C4%B1n" title="Aydın">Aydın</a> which has a population of approx. 150,000 (2000). Other towns in the province include the summer seaside resorts of <a href="/wiki/Didim" title="Didim">Didim</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ku%C5%9Fadas%C4%B1" title="Kuşadası">Kuşadası</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ayd%C4%B1n_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Ayd%C4%B1n#History" title="Aydın">Aydın § History</a></div>
<p>Aydın was founded by the ancient <a href="/wiki/Thracians" title="Thracians">Thracians</a> and was once known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tralles" title="Tralles">Tralles</a>. The area is an earthquake zone and the city was built and rebuilt by a succession of <a href="/wiki/Sparta" title="Sparta">Spartans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phrygians" title="Phrygians">Phrygians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ionians" title="Ionians">Ionians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lydians" title="Lydians">Lydians</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Persia" title="Persia">Persians</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient_Romans" title="Ancient Romans">Ancient Romans</a>. In 1186 the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seljuk_Turks" title="Seljuk Turks">Seljuk Turks</a> took control of the area, followed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anatolian_beylik" title="Anatolian beylik">Anatolian beylik</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aydinids" title="Aydinids">Aydinids</a>. During this period the town was named Aydın Güzelhisar, and was brought into the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> in 1426.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ayd%C4%B1n_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Neighboring provinces are <a href="/wiki/Manisa_Province" title="Manisa Province">Manisa</a> to the north east, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Izmir_Province" title="Izmir Province">İzmir</a> to the north, <a href="/wiki/Denizli_Province" title="Denizli Province">Denizli</a> to the east, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mugla_Province" title="Mugla Province">Muğla</a> to the south.</p>
<p>The central and western parts of the province are fertile plains watered by the largest river in the <a href="/wiki/Aegean_Sea" title="Aegean Sea">Aegean</a> region the <a href="/wiki/B%C3%BCy%C3%BCk_Menderes_River" title="Büyük Menderes River">Büyük Menderes River</a>, with the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ayd%C4%B1n_Mountains&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Aydın Mountains (page does not exist)">Aydın Mountains</a> to the north and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mente%C5%9Fe_Mountains&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Menteşe Mountains (page does not exist)">Menteşe Mountains</a> to the south. The western end of the province is the Aegean coast with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lake_Bafa" title="Lake Bafa">Lake Bafa</a> a major feature of the Menderes delta area. The climate is typical of the Aegean region, very hot in summer. The Germencik region contains a number of hot springs.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora">Flora</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ayd%C4%B1n_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Flora">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Much of the countryside is a mix of fig, olive and citrus trees, especially figs.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ayd%C4%B1n_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The major sources of income are agriculture and tourism.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ayd%C4%B1n_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The coastal towns of <a href="/wiki/Didim" title="Didim">Didim</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ku%C5%9Fadas%C4%B1" title="Kuşadası">Kuşadası</a> in particular are tourist resorts. Kuşadası is near to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dilek_Peninsula" title="Dilek Peninsula">Dilek Peninsula - Büyük Menderes Delta National Park</a>, while Didim has a temple of Apollo, beaches, and the ancient ruins of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miletos" title="Miletos">Miletos</a> nearby. The province contains <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archeology" title="Archeology">archeological</a> sites, including the ancient Carian cities of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alinda_(Caria)" title="Alinda (Caria)">Alinda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alabanda" title="Alabanda">Alabanda</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Agriculture">Agriculture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ayd%C4%B1n_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Agriculture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>Aydın is Turkey's leading producer of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Common_Fig" title="Common Fig">figs</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and exports dried figs worldwide. The very name by which the fruit was called in the world markets was "Smyrna figs" until recently, due to the preponderance of figs exported from <a href="/wiki/%C4%B0zmir" title="İzmir">İzmir</a> over other species of the genus. But İzmir got the name by being the center for the wholesale trade and exports, while in fact the fruit was traditionally cultivated in Aydın. The term used within Turkey is "Aydın figs" (<a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <span lang="tr" xml:lang="tr"><i>Aydın inciri</i></span>). Turkey's yearly production of roughly 50,000 tons of dried figs, is almost all from Aydın,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Within Aydın province, the best figs are reputed to be grown in <a href="/wiki/Germencik" title="Germencik">Germencik</a>. Aydın produces olives, chestnuts, cotton, citrus fruits, water melons and other fruits.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ayd%C4%B1n_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aydın is the home of the Zeybek folk art. This involves a special type of war dance which is performed in a ring to resemble birds. The Zeybek is performed to sounds of the Kiteli and other Turkish folk instruments.</p>
<p>The folk songs of Aydın are famously short, indeed a popular saying in the Aegean region to get someone to stop talking, is <i>Keep it short, make it an Aydın tune</i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The cuisine features the typical Turkish pastries, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%C3%B6fte" title="Köfte">köfte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kebab" title="Kebab">kebab</a>.</p>
