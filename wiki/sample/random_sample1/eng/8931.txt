<p><b>Aira Mitsuki</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">アイラミツキ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Mitsuki Aira</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Electronica" title="Electronica">electronica</a> artist. She debuted in August 2007 with "Colorful Tokyo Sounds No. 9" and released her debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Copy_(album)" title="Copy (album)">Copy</a></i> on September 3, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-airamitsuki.com_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-airamitsuki.com-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_background_and_releases">Musical background and releases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aira_Mitsuki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Musical background and releases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aira was chosen as the winner of Mega Trance 2007 from 6,325 applicants. She released her debut single, "Colorful Tokyo Sounds No. 9", on August 8 of the same year. A song from the single was selected as the official theme for the Transformers Café, based on the <i><a href="/wiki/Transformers_(film)" title="Transformers (film)">Transformers</a></i> film, in <a href="/wiki/Roppongi" title="Roppongi">Roppongi</a>. Aira performed at an <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>-sponsored event for the film on July 25.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Copy_(album)" title="Copy (album)">Copy</a></i>, was released on September 3, 2008. It was released in two different versions: a 12-track album and a 16-track album. The albums differ in that track 8 on the 12 regular edition album is the original version of "High Bash", while the Yakenohara version appears on the extended edition, and the songs "Star Fruits Surf Rider", "Rock'n Roll Is Dead", and "Romantic Rope" are included in the extended version. Additionally, those who purchased the album through <a href="/wiki/Tower_Records" title="Tower Records">Tower Records</a> also received an exclusive track, "Yakenohara Megamix", while those who bought the album through <a href="/wiki/HMV" title="HMV">HMV</a> received the track "L0ne1yBoy L0ne1yGirl" as a bonus track.</p>
<p>Track 1 of the album, titled "Galaxy Boy", was used as the ending song to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Aichi_Television&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Aichi Television (page does not exist)">Aichi Television</a>'s programme <i>Bonita!Bonita!!</i> It was also used to promote the <i>Dream car club of Tamoei Yakushiji</i>. The album reached a peak daily position of number 31 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oricon_chart" title="Oricon chart">Oricon chart</a>, and a peak weekly position of number 48 and sold 3,163 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Mitsuki's first major single was written as a tribute to China for the 2008 Olympics and released on March 5, 2008. She promoted it with a live performance in a Chinese nightclub, her first and to date only overseas show. Her second major (and fourth overall) single, "Robot Honey", was released on October 29, 2008 and followed by "Valentine Step" and "Sayonara Technopolis" in February 2009. She released her seventh single, "Barbie Barbie" on May 20, and an EP exclusive to the Village Vanguard chain of stores, "Aira's Science CD", on June 17, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-airamitsuki.com_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-airamitsuki.com-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Aira released her second studio album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Plastic_(Mitsuki_Aira_album)" title="Plastic (Mitsuki Aira album)">Plastic</a></i>, on July 22, 2009<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-airamitsuki.com_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-airamitsuki.com-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and her third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Three_Questions" title="Three Questions">???</a></i> on November 17, 2010.</p>
<p>She released her final album with D-topia, <i>I'll Be Back</i> on August 21, 2013 and went on a hiatus.</p>
<p>In 2015, Aira returned to music independent of a label, initially using the name "AIRA" and then settling on "アイラミツキ" in time for the release of her new song "LIGHTSAVER".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style_and_influences">Musical style and influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aira_Mitsuki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical style and influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aira's musical style has been compared to the popular <a href="/wiki/Yasutaka_Nakata" title="Yasutaka Nakata">Yasutaka Nakata</a>-produced acts <a href="/wiki/Capsule_(band)" title="Capsule (band)">Capsule</a> and <a href="/wiki/Perfume_(Japanese_band)" title="Perfume (Japanese band)">Perfume</a>. Aira has stated that she is a fan of Nakata's work and cites the <a href="/wiki/Pizzicato_Five" title="Pizzicato Five">Pizzicato Five</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kahimi_Karie" title="Kahimi Karie">Kahimi Karie</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuki_Isoya" title="Yuki Isoya">Yuki</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Justice_(French_band)" title="Justice (French band)">Justice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_(musician)" title="Cornelius (musician)">Cornelius</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Daft_Punk" title="Daft Punk">Daft Punk</a> as influences on her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MySpace" title="MySpace">MySpace</a> page,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> while she has covered songs by Cornelius, Kenji Ozawa, Kahimi Karie, Genki Rockets, Cascada, Lenny Kravitz, and her labelmate Saori@destiny.</p>
<p>Aira's music usually carries themes of <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pop_culture" title="Pop culture">pop culture</a> with ambient sound effects, heavy use of <a href="/wiki/Vocoder" title="Vocoder">vocoder</a> and lyrics exploring concepts such as <a href="/wiki/Space" title="Space">space</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robot" title="Robot">robots</a> and <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a>. "Galaxy Boy", for example, is space-themed and written about a "prototypical galaxy boy" while "Sayonara Technopolis" and its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B-side" title="B-side">B-sides</a> are written about issues such as human <a href="/wiki/Apathy" title="Apathy">apathy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a>, and propose moving to the Moon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-natalie.mu_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-natalie.mu-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Aira has experimented with different genres of music; much of <i><a href="/wiki/Copy_(album)" title="Copy (album)">Copy</a></i> had a dance sound while "Valentine Step" has strong Latin influences and "Sayonara Technopolis" was marketed as "electro meets big beat". She has also recorded several <a href="/wiki/Ballad" title="Ballad">ballads</a> and a heavy techno cover of <a href="/wiki/Lenny_Kravitz" title="Lenny Kravitz">Lenny Kravitz</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll_Is_Dead" title="Rock and Roll Is Dead">Rock and Roll Is Dead</a>". She has, however, remained consistent in that she uses voice filters and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Synthesisers" title="Synthesisers">synthesisers</a>.</p>
<p>Aira's videos make use of <a href="/wiki/Animation" title="Animation">animation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Computer-generated_imagery" title="Computer-generated imagery">CGI</a>, with typically fashion-forward costumes. She has professed to being a fan of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Video_games" title="Video games">video games</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon" title="Pokémon">Pokémon</a> and has stated in interviews that she is inspired by science fiction and technology. She also bought a plot of land on the <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">Moon</a> for the release of "Sayonara Technopolis".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-natalie.mu_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-natalie.mu-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Aira's EP <i>Aira's Science CD</i> contains two tracks, "Science Music" and "Senjō no Merry Christmas" which feature the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thereminvox" title="Thereminvox">thereminvox</a>, played by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Masami_Takeuchi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Masami Takeuchi (page does not exist)">Masami Takeuchi</a>. The CD also contains a cover of <a href="/wiki/Kahimi_Karie" title="Kahimi Karie">Kahimi Karie</a>'s "Mike Alway's Diary" and a remix by <a href="/wiki/I_Am_Robot_and_Proud" title="I Am Robot and Proud">I Am Robot and Proud</a>, as well as versions of "Science Music" and "Senjō no Merry Christmas" sans theremin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-airamitsuki.com_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-airamitsuki.com-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>While Aira is mostly a vocalist, she has incorporated instruments into many of her live performances, playing drums, various synthesizers, drum machines and samplers across her career.</p>
