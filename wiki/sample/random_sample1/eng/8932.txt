<p><b>Eye liner</b> or <b>eyeliner</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Cosmetics" title="Cosmetics">cosmetic</a> used to define the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_eyes" title="Human eyes">eyes</a>. It is applied around the contours of the eye(s) to create a variety of aesthetic effects.</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eye_liner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tomb_of_Nakht.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="702" data-file-width="735" height="162" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Tomb_of_Nakht.jpg/170px-Tomb_of_Nakht.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Tomb_of_Nakht.jpg/255px-Tomb_of_Nakht.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Tomb_of_Nakht.jpg/340px-Tomb_of_Nakht.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tomb_of_Nakht.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Ancient Egyptian</a> women and men wearing <a href="/wiki/Kohl_(cosmetics)" title="Kohl (cosmetics)">kohl</a> eye liner, from the tomb of <a href="/wiki/Nakht" title="Nakht">Nakht</a> in <a href="/wiki/Thebes,_Egypt" title="Thebes, Egypt">Thebes</a> (15th century BC).</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Eye liner was first used in <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Ancient Egypt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a> as a dark black line around the eyes. As early as 10,000 BC, Egyptians and Mo wore various cosmetics including eye liner not only for aesthetics but to protect the skin from the desert sun. Research has also speculated that eye liner was worn to protect the wearer from the <a href="/wiki/Evil_eye" title="Evil eye">evil eye</a>. The characteristic of having heavily lined eyes has been frequently depicted in ancient Egyptian art. They produced eye liner with a variety of materials, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copper_ore" title="Copper ore">copper ore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antimony" title="Antimony">antimony</a>. Ancient Egyptian <a href="/wiki/Kohl_(cosmetics)" title="Kohl (cosmetics)">kohl</a> contained <a href="/wiki/Galena" title="Galena">galena</a>, which was imported from nearby regions in the <a href="/wiki/Land_of_Punt" title="Land of Punt">Land of Punt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Qift" title="Qift">Coptos</a> and <a href="/wiki/Western_Asia" title="Western Asia">Western Asia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stiant_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stiant-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 1920s, <a href="/wiki/Tutankhamun" title="Tutankhamun">Tutankhamun</a>'s tomb was discovered, introducing the use of eye liner to the Western world. The 1920s were an era commonly associated with many changes in women's fashion, and women felt freer to apply make-up more liberally.</p>
<p>In the 1960s, liquid eye liner was used to create thick black and white lines around the eyes in the make-up fashion associated with designers like <a href="/wiki/Mary_Quant" title="Mary Quant">Mary Quant</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_usage">Modern usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eye_liner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Modern usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tekom003.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1544" data-file-width="1024" height="256" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Tekom003.jpg/170px-Tekom003.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Tekom003.jpg/255px-Tekom003.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Tekom003.jpg/340px-Tekom003.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tekom003.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A girl wearing eye liner.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In the late twentieth and early twenty-first century, heavy eye liner use has been associated with <a href="/wiki/Gothic_fashion" title="Gothic fashion">Gothic fashion</a> and <a href="/wiki/Punk_fashion" title="Punk fashion">Punk fashion</a>. Eye liner of varying degrees of thickness has also become associated with the <a href="/wiki/Emo" title="Emo">emo</a> subculture and various alternative lifestyles.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Eye_makeup.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1532" data-file-width="2048" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Eye_makeup.jpg/220px-Eye_makeup.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Eye_makeup.jpg/330px-Eye_makeup.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Eye_makeup.jpg/440px-Eye_makeup.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Eye_makeup.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
An eye with brown eye liner under the bottom lashes.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Eye liner is commonly used in a daily make-up routine to define the eye or create the look of a wider or smaller eye. Eye liner can be used as a tool to create various looks as well as highlighting different features of the eyes. Eye liner can be placed in various parts of the eye to create different looks with a winged eye liner or <a href="#Tight_lining">tight lined</a> at the waterline. Eye liner can be drawn above upper lashes or below lower lashes or both, even on the water lines of your eyes. Its primary purpose is to make the lashes look lush, but it also draws attention to the eye and can enhance or even change the eye's shape. Eye liner is available in a wide range of hues, from the common black, brown and grey to more adventurous shades such as bright primary colors, pastels, frosty silvers and golds, white and even glitter-flecked colors.</p>
<p>Eye liner can also be used for showing depression in photographs, such as the <span class="cleanup-needed-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">famous "Bleeding Mascara"</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (June 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Different_Modern_Uses">Different Modern Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eye_liner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Different Modern Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although many users of eye liner use it on a day-to-day basis for a simple definer of the eyes, there are others who use it for more than just an easy eye enlarger. There are many styles of eye liner. Most of them usually revolve around the winged eye liner, which is defining around your top eyelid shape and a line, about halfway toward the end of the eyebrow. It is usually connected to the defining around the top. However today there are some drastically toned eye liner.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tight_lining">Tight lining</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eye_liner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tight lining">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tight lining is the use of eye liner tight against the <a href="/wiki/Meibomian_gland" title="Meibomian gland">waterline</a> under the lashes of the upper lid, and above the lashes of the lower lid. Due to the proximity to the membranes, and the surface of the eye itself, waterproof eye liner is preferred. Tight lining is a technique which makes the eyelashes appear to start farther back on the eyelid, thus making them look longer. Gel eye liner and a small angled brush may be used to create this look.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types">Types</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eye_liner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Types">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Headshot_of_Model_with_Blue_Eyeliner.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="934" data-file-width="747" height="275" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Headshot_of_Model_with_Blue_Eyeliner.jpg/220px-Headshot_of_Model_with_Blue_Eyeliner.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Headshot_of_Model_with_Blue_Eyeliner.jpg/330px-Headshot_of_Model_with_Blue_Eyeliner.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Headshot_of_Model_with_Blue_Eyeliner.jpg/440px-Headshot_of_Model_with_Blue_Eyeliner.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Headshot_of_Model_with_Blue_Eyeliner.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Woman with turquoise eye liner</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Depending on its texture, eye liner can be softly smudged or clearly defined. There are five main types of eye liner available on the market: each produces a different effect.</p>
<ul>
<li><i>Liquid eye liner</i> is an opaque liquid that usually comes in a small bottle and is applied usually with a small brush with a sharp tip. It creates a sharp, precise line. Because liquid eye liner gives a much heavier appearance, it is often only applied to the upper lash-line, however nowadays it is used in all types of new ways.</li>
<li><i>Powder-based eye pencil</i> is eye liner in a wood pencil. It is generally available in dark matter shades.</li>
<li><i>Wax-based eye pencils</i> are softer pencils and contain waxes that ease application. They come in a wide variety of intense colours as well as paler shades such as white or beige. Wax-based eye liners can also come in a cone or a compact with brush applicator.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Kohl_(cosmetics)" title="Kohl (cosmetics)">Kohl</a></i> eye liner is a soft powder available in dark matte shades. It is most often used in black to outline the eyes. It comes in pencil, pressed powder, or loose powder form. This type of eye liner is more likely to smudge.</li>
<li><i>Gel eye liner</i>, which is a softer gel liner, that can be easily applied with an eye liner brush. It can be precisely applied and is much softer than Kohl.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chemical_composition">Chemical composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eye_liner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Chemical composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Traditional wax-based eye liners are made from about 20 components. About 50% by weight are waxes (e.g., <a href="/wiki/Japan_wax" title="Japan wax">Japan wax</a>, fats, or related soft materials that easily glide on to the skin. Typical pigments include black <a href="/wiki/Iron_oxide" title="Iron oxide">iron oxides</a>, as well as smaller amounts of <a href="/wiki/Titanium_dioxide" title="Titanium dioxide">titanium dioxide</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prussian_blue" title="Prussian blue">Prussian blue</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Color_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Color-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
