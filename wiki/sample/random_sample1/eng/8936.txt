<p><b>Park Eun Hye</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: 박은혜; born November 7, 1982), who uses the stage name <b>Ivy</b> (Hangul: 아이비; stylized as <b>IVY</b>), is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> singer, model and actress.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ivy_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005:_Debut">2005: Debut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ivy_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2005: Debut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A former <a href="/wiki/JYP_Entertainment" title="JYP Entertainment">JYPE</a> trainee, Ivy first appeared in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lee_Soo_Young" title="Lee Soo Young">Lee Soo Young</a>'s music video "Holding onto the Flowers" in 2005. She officially debuted as a singer in 2005 with the <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a> "What Happened Tonight" (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">오늘밤 일</span>). Her official second single "A ha" was an uptempo R&amp;B single whose dance routine was famously parodied by <a href="/wiki/Park_Kyung-lim" title="Park Kyung-lim">Park Kyung-lim</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/X-Man_(TV_series)" title="X-Man (TV series)">X-Man</a></i>. Around the same time, Ivy also promoted "I Am a Fool" (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">바본가봐</span>), a <a href="/wiki/Ballad" title="Ballad">ballad</a>, which was performed in conjunction with "A ha". The album placed 48th for the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Although inactive for most of 2006, she has collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Shinhwa" title="Shinhwa">Shinhwa</a> for their album <i><a href="/wiki/State_of_the_Art_(Shinhwa_album)" title="State of the Art (Shinhwa album)">State of the Art</a></i> on the track "Highway Star".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.932008:_Breakthrough.2C_controversy_and_hiatus">2007–2008: Breakthrough, controversy and hiatus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ivy_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2007–2008: Breakthrough, controversy and hiatus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On February 12, 2007, Ivy's second album, <i>Vol. 2 - A Sweet Moment</i>, was released, with the lead single "Sonata of Temptation", which sampled <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beethoven" title="Beethoven">Beethoven</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fur_Elise" title="Fur Elise">Fur Elise</a></i>, performing very well on the charts. Due to the song's popularity, she received the 2007 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M.net_KM_Music_Festival" title="M.net KM Music Festival">M.net KM Music Festival</a> award for Best Female Solo Artist. Despite its success, the song's music video caused controversy, as it recreated a fight scene between <a href="/wiki/Tifa_Lockhart" title="Tifa Lockhart">Tifa Lockhart</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Final_Fantasy_VII_characters#Advent_Children_characters" title="List of Final Fantasy VII characters">Loz</a> from <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Final_Fantasy_VII_Advent_Children" title="Final Fantasy VII Advent Children">Final Fantasy VII Advent Children</a></i>, without proper permission (the disclaimer appearing on the video did not satisfy the rights to use it). In April, the Seoul Central District Court banned the music video from being shown on television, stating that "most of the clip is noticeably similar to scenes from the film".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The story has received coverage on gaming blogs internationally, including <a href="/wiki/Kotaku" title="Kotaku">Kotaku</a>, which noted that the director has stated that he was unable to find the contact info for <a href="/wiki/Square_Enix" title="Square Enix">Square Enix</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 23 June 2007, Ivy performed at American singer <a href="/wiki/Christina_Aguilera" title="Christina Aguilera">Christina Aguilera</a>'s Seoul concert as part of her <i><a href="/wiki/Back_to_Basics_Tour" title="Back to Basics Tour">Back to Basics Tour</a></i>. Aguilera reportedly viewed video clips sent in by South Korean artists, as she wanted to choose the best. The artists were viewed for their dancing and singing abilities, as well as album sales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She performed as the opening act for the first concert on the first day, while <a href="/wiki/Lee_Min-woo" title="Lee Min-woo">Lee Min-woo</a> of <a href="/wiki/Shinhwa" title="Shinhwa">Shinhwa</a> performed the following day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2008, under the pseudonym "The Lighthouse", she wrote the lyrics for <i>"Dear Mom..."</i> for <a href="/wiki/Girls%27_Generation" title="Girls' Generation">Girls' Generation</a>'s mini-album <a href="/wiki/Gee_(Girls%27_Generation_song)" title="Gee (Girls' Generation song)">Gee</a>, and "마지막 선물" (<i>"Last Gift"</i>) for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SHINee" title="SHINee">SHINee</a>'s first album <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SHINee_World" title="SHINee World">SHINee World</a>. The songs were composed by her boyfriend Kim Tae-Sung.</p>
<p>Ivy had a supporting role in the joint South Korean-Japanese 2008 mini-drama series <i>"Tokyo Sun Showers"</i> as Eun Bi, an aspiring singer.</p>
<p>On September 24, 2009, she was announced to feature on <a href="/wiki/MC_Mong" title="MC Mong">MC Mong</a>'s repackaged album "Horror Show" in the song "나는..." (<i>"I Am..."</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The song was released on October 26, 2009 with a <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">promotional video</a> on the same day.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009:_Comeback">2009: Comeback</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ivy_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2009: Comeback">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After having her comeback further delayed by problems with her management company,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Ivy released her third album <i>"I Be.."</i>, with contributions from artists such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Psy_(rapper)" title="Psy (rapper)">PSY</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nam_Gyuri" title="Nam Gyuri">Nam Gyuri</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> She also switched from Fantom Entertainment to De Chocolate E&amp;TF.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first song released was "눈물아 안녕" (<i>"Goodbye Tears"</i>), along with a promotional video. The song reached #1 on various online charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> However, her title song was chosen to be "Touch Me", a techno dance track written by PSY.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The music video was released on October 23, 2009. However, it was banned from broadcast three days later for being too inappropriate for viewing of minors with its sexual references.</p>
<p>Ivy also stars in her own 13-episode reality show on M.net called "IVY BACK", the first episode airing on October 27, 2009. After performing a special showcase for her new album, Ivy officially made her comeback debut with "Touch Me" on <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Inkigayo" title="Inkigayo">Inkigayo</a> on November 1, 2009.</p>
<p>Due to problems between Ivy's ex-management company and <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">MBC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">KBS</a>, she is unable to perform on weekly music shows such as "Music Core" and "Music Bank". Instead, she is only allowed to promote on M.net's M!Countdown. However, she was later given approval to perform and appear on SBS programs.</p>
<p>On December 13, 2009, Ivy returned for the second time on Inkigayo since her comeback with <i>"Goodbye Tears"</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Promotions for her third album ended on January 24, 2010.</p>
<p>On January 10, 2010, Ivy's social security number was leaked onto the internet after an article reporting about an episode of IVY BACK failed to censor it out in a photo of Ivy holding her documents. Her company has stated they will take legal action against the netizens who have hacked into Ivy's personal accounts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.93present:_Comeback">2012–present: Comeback</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ivy_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2012–present: Comeback">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 27, 2012, Ivy released a mini album titled <i>Interview</i> which featured five ballad-type songs. The song "찢긴 가슴"("Broken Heart"), which was released with a music video, was chosen as the lead single. It debuted on the Mnet Pop singles chart at 18.</p>
<p>In 2013, Ivy made her comeback with the lead single of her new album titled "I Dance" featuring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yubin" title="Yubin">Yubin</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Wonder_Girls" title="Wonder Girls">Wonder Girls</a>.</p>
