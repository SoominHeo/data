<p>The <b>Football Association of Iceland</b> (<a href="/wiki/Icelandic_language" title="Icelandic language">Icelandic</a>: <span lang="is"><i>Knattspyrnusamband Íslands</i></span>, <b>KSÍ</b>) is the governing body of <a href="/wiki/Football_in_Iceland" title="Football in Iceland">football in Iceland</a>. It was founded on 26 March 1947, joined <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a> the same year, and <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a> in 1954.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It organises the football league, <a href="/wiki/%C3%9Arvalsdeild" title="Úrvalsdeild">Úrvalsdeild</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Iceland_national_football_team" title="Iceland national football team">Iceland national football team</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iceland_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Iceland women's national football team">Iceland women's national football team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It is based in <a href="/wiki/Reykjav%C3%ADk" title="Reykjavík">Reykjavík</a>.</p>
