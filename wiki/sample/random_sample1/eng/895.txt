<p><b>Donald Joseph "D. J." Cotrona</b> (born May 23,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABCMN-D187-Cast-Bio_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ABCMN-D187-Cast-Bio-1">[1]</a></sup> 1980) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>, known for his role in the film <i><a href="/wiki/G.I._Joe:_Retaliation" title="G.I. Joe: Retaliation">G.I. Joe: Retaliation</a></i> (2013) and as Seth Gecko in the El Rey Network horror television series <i><a href="/wiki/From_Dusk_till_Dawn:_The_Series" title="From Dusk till Dawn: The Series">From Dusk till Dawn</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D._J._Cotrona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cotrona was born in <a href="/wiki/New_Haven,_Connecticut" title="New Haven, Connecticut">New Haven, Connecticut</a>. His mother, Sheree, is a teacher, and his father, Donald, works for a recycling company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USAToday-20031020_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-USAToday-20031020-2">[2]</a></sup> Cotrona is of three eighths Italian, and one quarter Polish, descent; his other ancestry includes German, Hungarian, Austrian, and English.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USAToday-20031020_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-USAToday-20031020-2">[2]</a></sup> He was studying to be a lawyer at <a href="/wiki/Northeastern_University" title="Northeastern University">Northeastern University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>; however, after doing a summer internship at a law firm, he realized he did not like working with lawyers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USAToday-20031020_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-USAToday-20031020-2">[2]</a></sup> In his sophomore year, he switched to acting; during his spring break, he went to visit a friend in Los Angeles and never returned to college.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D._J._Cotrona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After a few guest starring roles Cotrona was being considered for the role of <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Atwood" title="Ryan Atwood">Ryan Atwood</a> on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_O.C." title="The O.C.">The O.C.</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USAToday-20031020_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-USAToday-20031020-2">[2]</a></sup> but that role went to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benjamin_McKenzie" title="Benjamin McKenzie">Benjamin McKenzie</a>. Shortly thereafter Cotrona was cast as the male lead in another of <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a>'s new dramas of 2003, the <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Bruckheimer" title="Jerry Bruckheimer">Jerry Bruckheimer</a> produced <i><a href="/wiki/Skin_(TV_series)" title="Skin (TV series)">Skin</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USAToday-20031020_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-USAToday-20031020-2">[2]</a></sup> Cotrona played Adam Roam, the son of the Los Angeles District Attorney. His character becomes involved with Jewel Goldman (<a href="/wiki/Olivia_Wilde" title="Olivia Wilde">Olivia Wilde</a>), whose father runs a pornography company. With their fathers' feud looming over them they pursue their "<a href="/wiki/Romeo_and_Juliet" title="Romeo and Juliet">Romeo and Juliet</a>" relationship. <i>Skin</i> was cancelled after three episodes aired due to poor ratings and the controversial story lines. The eight episodes of the series were shown on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SOAPnet" title="SOAPnet">SOAPnet</a> in 2005.</p>
<p>Cotrona appeared in the <a href="/wiki/2005_in_film" title="2005 in film">2005</a> horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Venom_(2005_film)" title="Venom (2005 film)">Venom</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Variety-20050915_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Variety-20050915-3">[3]</a></sup> which was directed by <a href="/wiki/Jim_Gillespie_(director)" title="Jim Gillespie (director)">Jim Gillespie</a>. He also starred in the 2006 <a href="/wiki/Slamdance_Film_Festival" title="Slamdance Film Festival">Slamdance Film Festival</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Is_the_Drug_(film)" title="Love Is the Drug (film)">Love is the Drug</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Variety-20060206_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Variety-20060206-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2005 Cotrona again landed a lead role in a TV series, playing Sean Mathers, a flower shop worker with a dark past who is part of a group of lottery winners, in the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Windfall_(TV_series)" title="Windfall (TV series)">Windfall</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TFC-20050721_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-TFC-20050721-5">[5]</a></sup> The series would end up being shown in the summer of 2006 rather than as a mid-season replacement for the 2005-06 TV season.</p>
<p>Cotrona was cast in the role of <a href="/wiki/Superman" title="Superman">Superman</a> for <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Bros.</a>' planned film <i><a href="/wiki/Justice_League_in_other_media#Justice_League:_Mortal" title="Justice League in other media">Justice League: Mortal</a></i>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-20080301_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-20080301-6">[6]</a></sup> however, after issues resulting from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%932008_Writers_Guild_of_America_strike" title="2007–2008 Writers Guild of America strike">2007–2008 Writers Guild of America strike</a> and other production concerns, the film remains unmade.</p>
<p>In 2010, Cotrona appeared in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Dear_John_(2010_film)" title="Dear John (2010 film)">Dear John</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Variety-20100202_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Variety-20100202-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmovie-dear_john_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmovie-dear_john-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In February he was cast as Detective John Stone<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TFC-20100325_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-TFC-20100325-10">[10]</a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> <a href="/wiki/Television_pilot" title="Television pilot">pilot</a> <i>187 Detroit</i>, which also stars <a href="/wiki/Michael_Imperioli" title="Michael Imperioli">Michael Imperioli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Majumder" title="Shaun Majumder">Shaun Majumder</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Erin_Cummings" title="Erin Cummings">Erin Cummings</a> among others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TFC-187_Detroit_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-TFC-187_Detroit-11">[11]</a></sup> When ABC ordered the series it was renamed <i><a href="/wiki/Detroit_1-8-7" title="Detroit 1-8-7">Detroit 1-8-7</a></i>. His character was killed in "<a href="/wiki/List_of_Detroit_1-8-7_episodes#ep16" title="List of Detroit 1-8-7 episodes">Stone Cold</a>", the sixteenth episode of the series. Cotrona played <a href="/wiki/Flint_(G.I._Joe)" title="Flint (G.I. Joe)">Flint</a>, one of the leading roles, in the sequel <i><a href="/wiki/G.I._Joe:_Retaliation" title="G.I. Joe: Retaliation">G.I. Joe: Retaliation</a></i> (2013).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2014, the horror television series <i><a href="/wiki/From_Dusk_till_Dawn:_The_Series" title="From Dusk till Dawn: The Series">From Dusk till Dawn</a></i> developed by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Rodriguez" title="Robert Rodriguez">Robert Rodriguez</a> based on his own cult horror film <a href="/wiki/From_Dusk_till_Dawn" title="From Dusk till Dawn">of the same title</a>, was aired on the newly launched <a href="/wiki/El_Rey_Network" title="El Rey Network">El Rey Network</a>. Cotrona played Seth Gecko in the series. The series has since been renewed for a third season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
