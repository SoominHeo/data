<p><b>Aizumisato</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">会津美里町</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Aizumisato-machi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Towns_of_Japan" title="Towns of Japan">town</a> located in <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cnuma_District,_Fukushima" title="Ōnuma District, Fukushima">Ōnuma District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fukushima_Prefecture" title="Fukushima Prefecture">Fukushima Prefecture</a>, in northern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honsh%C5%AB" title="Honshū">Honshū</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of September 2014, the town had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 1,332 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 6.36 persons per km². The total area was 209.34 km².</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aizumisato&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aizumisato is located in the eastern portion of the <a href="/wiki/Aizu" title="Aizu">Aizu</a> region, in west-central Fukushima Prefecture. The climate is like many other parts of northern Japan, with cold winters and snowfall averaging 2 meters.</p>
<ul>
<li>Mountains : Mount Hakase, Mount Myojindake (1074 m)</li>
<li>Rivers : Aka River</li>
<li>Lakes : <a href="/wiki/Miyagawa_Dam" title="Miyagawa Dam">Miyagawa Dam</a></li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aizumisato&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of present-day Aizumisato was part of ancient <a href="/wiki/Mutsu_Province" title="Mutsu Province">Mutsu Province</a> and formed part of the holdings of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aizu_Domain" title="Aizu Domain">Aizu Domain</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a>, it was organized as part of <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cnuma_District,_Fukushima" title="Ōnuma District, Fukushima">Ōnuma District</a>.</p>
<p>The town of Aizumisato was created on October 1, 2005 with the merger of the two towns of <a href="/wiki/Aizutakada,_Fukushima" title="Aizutakada, Fukushima">Aizutakada</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aizuhong%C5%8D,_Fukushima" title="Aizuhongō, Fukushima">Aizuhongō</a> and the village of <a href="/wiki/Niitsuru,_Fukushima" title="Niitsuru, Fukushima">Niitsuru</a>, all from <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cnuma_District,_Fukushima" title="Ōnuma District, Fukushima">Ōnuma District</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
