<p><b>Angerme</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">アンジュルム</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Anjurumu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, formerly <b>S/mileage</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">スマイレージ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sumairēji</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_idol" title="Japanese idol">Japanese idol</a> <a href="/wiki/Girl_group" title="Girl group">girl group</a> from <a href="/wiki/Hello!_Project" title="Hello! Project">Hello! Project</a>, which originally consisted of four former Hello! Pro Egg (Hello! Project trainees) members that left Egg in 2010 and became full-time members of <a href="/wiki/Hello!_Project" title="Hello! Project">Hello! Project</a>. In 2010, they won the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Record_Award" title="Japan Record Award">Japan Record Award</a> for Best New Artist. In 2011, five new girls were added to the group as part of the second generation, with two being former Egg members and three new girls, while also losing three members at the same time; three more members were added in 2014. With the Graduations of two members, and the additions of two more members in 2015 and 16, as of July 2016 the total members of the group stands at 9 members.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angerme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="S.2Fmileage_era">S/mileage era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angerme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: S/mileage era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2009:_Formation">2009: Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angerme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2009: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On April 4, Japanese music producer <a href="/wiki/Tsunku" title="Tsunku">Tsunku</a> announced on his blog that a new girl group consisting of Hello! Pro Egg members would be formed. He later revealed that the members would be <a href="/wiki/Ayaka_Wada" title="Ayaka Wada">Ayaka Wada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yuuka_Maeda" title="Yuuka Maeda">Yuuka Maeda</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kanon_Fukuda" title="Kanon Fukuda">Kanon Fukuda</a>, all formerly of <a href="/wiki/Shugo_Chara_Egg!" title="Shugo Chara Egg!">Shugo Chara Egg!</a>, as well as fellow Egg <a href="/wiki/Saki_Ogawa" title="Saki Ogawa">Saki Ogawa</a>, and announced that Ayaka would be the leader.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> A month later, on May 7, 2009, he revealed that the group would be called "S/mileage". The name is a <a href="/wiki/Portmanteau" title="Portmanteau">portmanteau</a> of the English words "smile", "mileage", and "age", and together the name means "the age of smiles".</p>
<p>S/mileage's first single, "Ama no Jaku", was released at the Hello! Pro Egg: Nakano Step! concert on June 7, 2009.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2010:_Debut">2010: Debut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angerme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010: Debut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After releasing three singles, Tsunku announced that the group would become a full-time group in the spring of 2010 and their debut set for May 2010. A "Smile Campaign" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">笑顔キャンペーン</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, "Egao Campaign")</span> was embarked upon to collect 10,000 smiles from people, where fans were asked to send in photographs of themselves smiling, compiled into a big mosaic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The group made their official event debut in Nagoya on March 19, with their fourth single "Otona ni Narutte Muzukashii!!!", released March 14, their first release on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Up-Front_Works" title="Up-Front Works">Up-Front Works</a> label.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It peaked at the #1 spot on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> weekly <a href="/wiki/Independent_record_label" title="Independent record label">indies</a> chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> After the deadline on March 25, it was announced that the result of the campaign would be announced during the joint S/mileage and <a href="/wiki/Erina_Mano" title="Erina Mano">Erina Mano</a> concert on April 3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It was then announced that the group would be leaving from Hello! Pro Eggs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>S/mileage appeared as the opening support act at fellow Hello! Project unit <a href="/wiki/Cute_(Japanese_idol_group)" title="Cute (Japanese idol group)">Cute</a>'s concert on March 20, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On April 3, they performed a joint concert with <a href="/wiki/Erina_Mano" title="Erina Mano">Erina Mano</a>, at which Tsunku announced that the girls would indeed make their major debut, and revealed the completed mosaic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-major_debut_decided_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-major_debut_decided-9">[9]</a></sup> Their first major single, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yume_Miru_Fifteen" title="Yume Miru Fifteen">Yume Miru Fifteen</a>" (A Dreaming Fifteen Year Old), was released on May 26, and debuted on Oricon at number 5. Their first tour, titled "Devil Smile Angel Smile," began on October 9, 2010. In late August, a post on their official website revealed that their first album had been set for a winter release. The album's name was later revealed to be <i><a href="/wiki/Warugaki_1" title="Warugaki 1">Warugaki 1</a></i> (Evil Brats 1) and was released on December 8, 2010. On December 30, S/mileage received the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Record_Award" title="Japan Record Award">Japan Record Award</a> for Best New Artist, beating, among other nominees, <a href="/wiki/Girls%27_Generation" title="Girls' Generation">Girls' Generation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tokyograph_Best_New_Artist_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tokyograph_Best_New_Artist-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2011:_New_Members">2011: New Members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angerme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2011: New Members">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On May 29, 2011, S/mileage revealed at their first-anniversary event that they would be holding auditions for new members to add to the four-member line-up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tokyograph_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tokyograph-11">[11]</a></sup> At the same time, it was also announced that some of the members would depart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tokyograph_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tokyograph-11">[11]</a></sup> The new members were announced on August 14.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tokyograph_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-Tokyograph-11">[11]</a></sup> From Hello! Pro Egg, <a href="/wiki/Akari_Takeuchi" title="Akari Takeuchi">Akari Takeuchi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rina_Katsuta" title="Rina Katsuta">Rina Katsuta</a> were added, while three new girls <a href="/wiki/Kana_Nakanishi" title="Kana Nakanishi">Kana Nakanishi</a>, Fuyuka Kosuga, and <a href="/wiki/Meimi_Tamura" title="Meimi Tamura">Meimi Tamura</a> also joined.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 24, it was announced that on August 27, <a href="/wiki/Saki_Ogawa" title="Saki Ogawa">Saki Ogawa</a> would be leaving S/mileage and, subsequently, from <a href="/wiki/Hello!_Project" title="Hello! Project">Hello! Project</a>. In May 2011, she had asked to return to the life of a normal junior high school student.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On September 9, one of S/mileage's newest additions, Fuyuka Kosuga, was diagnosed with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anaemia" title="Anaemia">anaemia</a>, after missing a concert due to illness. Due to her anaemia, she left the group and resumed her work as a Hello! Project Egg after her recovery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Starting September 18, a second Smile Campaign was held by the new members to determine their place as an official member in the group. On October 16 at S/mileage's "<a href="/wiki/Tachiagirl" title="Tachiagirl">Tachiagirl</a>" event, producer <a href="/wiki/Tsunku" title="Tsunku">Tsunku</a> announced all four sub-members had become full members. On October 25, a week after the sub members were changed to full, it was announced that Yuuka Maeda would leave S/mileage and, subsequently, from <a href="/wiki/Hello!_Project" title="Hello! Project">Hello! Project</a> on December 31. This was due to her wanting to retire from show business and focus on university entrance exams, and she felt that it would be unfair to her supporters to only give the fans half of her concentration on stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the start of 2012, the group consisted of six members - two being first-generation and four being second generation members. On February 1, they released "<a href="/wiki/Choto_Mate_Kudasai!" title="Choto Mate Kudasai!">Choto Mate Kudasai!</a>", their ninth single and first single without <a href="/wiki/Yuuka_Maeda" title="Yuuka Maeda">Yuuka Maeda</a>. Their 10th single "<a href="/wiki/Dot_Bikini" title="Dot Bikini">Dot Bikini</a>" followed on May 2, their first best of album on May 20, and their 11th single "<a href="/wiki/Suki_yo,_Junj%C5%8D_Hank%C5%8Dki" title="Suki yo, Junjō Hankōki">Suki yo, Junjō Hankōki</a>" on August 22. On August 30, S/mileage's 13th single and second album was announced. The single, titled "<a href="/wiki/Samui_ne" title="Samui ne">Samui ne</a>", was released on November 28, and the album <i><a href="/wiki/2_Smile_Sensation" title="2 Smile Sensation">2 Smile Sensation</a></i> was released May 22, 2013.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Angerme_era">Angerme era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angerme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Angerme era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015:_Third_generation_and_a_new_name">2015: Third generation and a new name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angerme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2015: Third generation and a new name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On September 24, 2014, it was announced that a third generation for the group would be added from among the Hello Pro Kenshūsei, as well as a name change for the group to be decided on October 31. On October 4, Mizuki Murota, Maho Aikawa, and <a href="/wiki/Rikako_Sasaki" title="Rikako Sasaki">Rikako Sasaki</a> were revealed as the new members, bringing the group to nine girls.</p>
<p>On December 17, 2014, it was announced that S/mileage would be renamed Angerme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tokyohive_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tokyohive-21">[21]</a></sup> Since this was effectively a new group with a revised membership and a release schedule under the new name, the group known as S/mileage effectively ceased to exist from that date.</p>
<p>On May 20, 2015, Kanon Fukuda announced that she would be leaving Angerme and Hello! Project in the fall to continue with her university studies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tokyo_Girls_Update_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tokyo_Girls_Update-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>On November 11, 2015, the group announced that their fourth generation member auditions had concluded with Moe Kamikokuryou revealed as the new member. With Kanon Fukuda leaving on November 29, 2015, the group remained at nine girls. On December 20, 2015, it was announced that Meimi Tamura would leave Angerme and Hello! Project in spring 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 16, a surprise announcement was made - Momona Kasahara, previously from Hello Pro Kenshūsei, will join the group as the sole 5th generation member.</p>
