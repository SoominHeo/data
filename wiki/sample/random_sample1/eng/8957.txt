<div class="hatnote" role="note">This name uses <a href="/wiki/Spanish_naming_customs" title="Spanish naming customs">Spanish naming customs</a>: the first or paternal <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">family name</a> is <i>Karanka</i> and the second or maternal family name is <i>De la Hoz</i>.</div>
<p><b>Aitor Karanka de la Hoz</b> (<small>Basque and</small> <small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[aiˈtoɾ kaˈɾaŋka]</a></span>; born 18 September 1973) is a former <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played mainly as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">central defender</a> (on occasion, he occupied the <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">left back</a> position), and the current manager of English club <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a>.</p>
<p>Save for a brief spell in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, at the age of 32, he played solely in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> for <a href="/wiki/Athletic_Bilbao" title="Athletic Bilbao">Athletic Bilbao</a> and <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a>, appearing in 275 <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> games over the course of 13 seasons and winning a total of seven major titles with the latter.</p>
<p>In the late 2000s Karanka started a managerial career, notably working as an assistant at Real Madrid<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and in his own right at Middlesbrough.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Club">Club</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aitor_Karanka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Athletic_.2F_Real_Madrid">Athletic / Real Madrid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aitor_Karanka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Athletic / Real Madrid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Vitoria-Gasteiz" title="Vitoria-Gasteiz">Vitoria-Gasteiz</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lava" title="Álava">Álava</a>, Karanka played as a youth with hometown's <a href="/wiki/Deportivo_Alav%C3%A9s" title="Deportivo Alavés">Deportivo Alavés</a> and <a href="/wiki/Athletic_Bilbao_Juvenil" title="Athletic Bilbao Juvenil">finished his formation</a> with <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)" title="Basque Country (autonomous community)">Basque</a> neighbours <a href="/wiki/Athletic_Bilbao" title="Athletic Bilbao">Athletic Bilbao</a>. He made his senior debuts with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bilbao_Athletic" title="Bilbao Athletic">the reserves of the latter</a> in 1992, competing with the team in <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">Segunda División</a>.</p>
<p>Karanka was promoted to the main squad by <a href="/wiki/Jupp_Heynckes" title="Jupp Heynckes">Jupp Heynckes</a> in 1993, making his <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> debut on 7 November in a 1–1 away draw against <a href="/wiki/Celta_de_Vigo" title="Celta de Vigo">Celta de Vigo</a> (90 minutes played)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and going on to feature in exactly 100 league matches in his three full seasons, before joining the German <a href="/wiki/Coach_(sport)" title="Coach (sport)">coach</a> at <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a> in 1997.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> With the <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">capital</a> team he was used mostly as a backup, but appeared in 33 <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> games for the club, including the <a href="/wiki/1999%E2%80%932000_UEFA_Champions_League" title="1999–2000 UEFA Champions League">1999–2000</a> <a href="/wiki/2000_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2000 UEFA Champions League Final">final</a> against <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF" title="Valencia CF">Valencia CF</a> (3–0 win);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> he also missed the vast majority of the <a href="/wiki/1998%E2%80%9399_La_Liga" title="1998–99 La Liga">1998–99 campaign</a> due to a <a href="/wiki/Heart" title="Heart">heart</a> condition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<p>For <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_La_Liga" title="2002–03 La Liga">2002–03</a>, Karanka returned to Athletic Bilbao.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He helped the <i>Lions</i> <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_UEFA_Cup" title="2004–05 UEFA Cup">qualify</a> for the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_La_Liga" title="2003–04 La Liga">his second year</a>, after a fifth place in the league.</p>
<p>Karanka switched to <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">Major League Soccer</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rapids" title="Colorado Rapids">Colorado Rapids</a> in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/2006_Major_League_Soccer_season" title="2006 Major League Soccer season">his only season</a> in the United States he helped his team to the <a href="/wiki/Western_Conference_(MLS)" title="Western Conference (MLS)">Western Conference</a> play-off final, despite having his attempt saved by <a href="/wiki/Dar%C3%ADo_Sala" title="Darío Sala">Darío Sala</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shoot-out_(association_football)" title="Penalty shoot-out (association football)">penalty shootout</a> victory over <a href="/wiki/FC_Dallas" title="FC Dallas">FC Dallas</a> in the semi-finals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aitor_Karanka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Karanka only played once for <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a> at senior level, on 26 April 1995 against <a href="/wiki/Armenia_national_football_team" title="Armenia national football team">Armenia</a> for the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1996" title="UEFA Euro 1996">UEFA Euro 1996</a> <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1996_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 1996 qualifying">qualifiers</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Yerevan" title="Yerevan">Yerevan</a> (2–0 win).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He made 14 appearances for the <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-21_football_team" title="Spain national under-21 football team">under-21s</a>, and was a member of the team that finished runners-up at the <a href="/wiki/1996_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="1996 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">1996 European Championship</a> in a final lost to <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">Italy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Karanka also represented <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-23_football_team" title="Spain national under-23 football team">the nation</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1996_Summer_Olympics" title="1996 Summer Olympics">1996 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, playing in four matches in an eventual <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1996_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 1996 Summer Olympics">quarter-final exit</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Managerial_career">Managerial career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aitor_Karanka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Managerial career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In June 2010, Karanka was appointed assistant manager at former side Real Madrid, by newly appointed manager <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mourinho" title="José Mourinho">José Mourinho</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Three years later he left the club following the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Ancelotti" title="Carlo Ancelotti">Carlo Ancelotti</a>, who brought his own coaching staff.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 13 November 2013, Karanka was appointed manager of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Football League Championship</a> side <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Tony_Mowbray" title="Tony Mowbray">Tony Mowbray</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> His first game in charge ended in a 1–2 away defeat against <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a>, ten days later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 25 April 2015, in the club's penultimate game of <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Football_League_Championship" title="2014–15 Football League Championship">the season</a>, Karanka sent goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Dimitrios_Konstantopoulos" title="Dimitrios Konstantopoulos">Dimitrios Konstantopoulos</a> forward for an added-time corner kick, with the score 3–3 away to <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a>: with the goalkeeper out of position, the opponents scored a winner through <a href="/wiki/Ross_McCormack" title="Ross McCormack">Ross McCormack</a>, which sent <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a> into the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> and jeopardised Middlesbrough's own chances of promotion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> He did qualify his team to <a href="/wiki/2015_Football_League_Championship_play-off_Final" title="2015 Football League Championship play-off Final">the play-off final</a> after a 5–1 aggregate win over <a href="/wiki/Brentford_F.C." title="Brentford F.C.">Brentford</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> but they lost the decisive match 0–2 to <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City</a> at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 7 August 2015, Karanka signed a new four-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Boro were consistently in high positions during <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Football_League_Championship" title="2015–16 Football League Championship">the campaign</a>, but on 11 March 2016 he unexpectedly left the training ground after an argument and considered his future at the club; responsibilities for the subsequent match, at <a href="/wiki/Charlton_Athletic_F.C." title="Charlton Athletic F.C.">Charlton Athletic</a>, were handed to his assistant <a href="/wiki/Steve_Agnew" title="Steve Agnew">Steve Agnew</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> but the Spaniard returned to lead the team to the top division after a seven-year absence, as runners-up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aitor_Karanka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Karanka's younger brother, <a href="/wiki/David_Karanka" title="David Karanka">David</a>, was also a footballer. A striker, he also appeared for Athletic's first team but with much less impact, going on to spend the vast majority of his professional career in the second level or the lower leagues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
