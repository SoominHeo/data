<p><b>D12</b>, an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Initialism" title="Initialism">initialism</a> for <b>The Dirty Dozen</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> group from <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit, Michigan</a>. D12 has had chart-topping albums in the United States, United Kingdom, and Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> D12 was formed in 1996, and achieved mainstream success after <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> rose to international fame.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allmusic_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusic-3">[3]</a></sup> D12 released the album <i><a href="/wiki/Devil%27s_Night_(album)" title="Devil's Night (album)">Devil's Night</a></i> in 2001 and <i><a href="/wiki/D12_World" title="D12 World">D12 World</a></i> in 2004, spawning numerous hits such as "<a href="/wiki/Fight_Music" title="Fight Music">Fight Music</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Purple_Pills" title="Purple Pills">Purple Pills</a>", "<a href="/wiki/My_Band" title="My Band">My Band</a>", "<a href="/wiki/How_Come" title="How Come">How Come</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Shit_on_You" title="Shit on You">Shit on You</a>" throughout that period. Since 2006, <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s hiatus and the death of the member <a href="/wiki/Proof_(rapper)" title="Proof (rapper)">Proof</a> resulted in them being less active in subsequent years.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career_.281996.E2.80.9398.29">Early career (1996–98)</span></h3>
<p>In order to make up the dozen, it was decided the six members at the time would each create an alter-ego. Proof's was Dirty Harry, Bizarre's was Peter S. Bizarre, Kuniva's was Hannz G. (which later became Rondell Beene), Kon Artis's was <a href="/wiki/Mr._Porter" title="Mr. Porter">Mr. Porter</a>, Bugz' was Robert Beck and Eminem created the famous character Slim Shady.</p>
<p>In 1997, they released their debut album, <i>The Underground EP</i>, which was recorded between 1996 and 1997. In 1997 and through 1998, its members began establishing reputations locally. Bizarre was named <i>Inner City Entertainment'</i>s "Flava of the Week" and went on to release an album called <i><a href="/wiki/Attack_of_the_Weirdos" title="Attack of the Weirdos">Attack of the Weirdos</a></i>. Along with Eminem, <a href="/wiki/Rah_Digga" title="Rah Digga">Rah Digga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Young_Zee" title="Young Zee">Young Zee</a>, he became an honorary member of <i><a href="/wiki/Outsidaz" title="Outsidaz">The Outsidaz</a></i>. Proof won a <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_music" title="Freestyle music">freestyling</a> competition run by <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Source_(magazine)" title="The Source (magazine)">The Source</a></i>. In 1999, Bugz released an EP called <i>These Streets EP</i>, and made several appearances on other rapper's songs.</p>
<p>D12 went through many changes during the early days even becoming temporarily inactive when, after being heard by Dr. Dre, Eminem left the group to pursue a solo career. Around the time of Eminem's first record deal, Proof attempted to bring new life to D12. He managed to recruit local Detroit hip hop duo Da Brigade, which was composed of Kuniva, a local MC, and Denaun Porter, a longtime friend of Eminem and Proof. At the time, Porter was the group's producer and later produced Eminem's first album <i><a href="/wiki/Infinite_(Eminem_album)" title="Infinite (Eminem album)">Infinite</a></i>, but was persuaded by his partner Kuniva to join him in the group as an artist. Later the group was joined by Bugz and needing one more member, Bugz introduced his longtime friend Swift, who at the time was a member of rap duo Da Rabeez.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_of_Bugz_and_Eminem.27s_return_.281999.29">Death of Bugz and Eminem's return (1999)</span></h3>
<p>Before a show in Detroit, Bugz attended a picnic where an argument with a man over a water pistol escalated into a fight. The man went to his <a href="/wiki/Sport_utility_vehicle" title="Sport utility vehicle">SUV</a> and retrieved a firearm, which he fired in Bugz' direction. Bugz was shot four times and was hit by the assailant's vehicle as the suspect fled the scene. The attack, on May 21, 1999, was captured on video and aired later that day on local news.</p>
<p>Members of the group were shaken by the tragedy, darkening the mood within the collective. One of Bugz's final acts had been a request that Swift could join the group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-d12worldswift_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-d12worldswift-4">[4]</a></sup> Following the death of Bugz, Eminem volunteered as replacement to fulfill the group's forthcoming obligations. This led to Eminem eventually rejoining the group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the time of his death, Bugz was working on his first solo album, <i>Mr. Obnoxious</i>, which was ultimately released posthumously in February 2000. D12 recorded their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Devil%27s_Night_(album)" title="Devil's Night (album)">Devil's Night</a></i>, in his memory. Eminem's third album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Marshall_Mathers_LP" title="The Marshall Mathers LP">The Marshall Mathers LP</a></i>, is also dedicated to him. The track "Good Die Young", released on D12's second album, <i><a href="/wiki/D12_World" title="D12 World">D12 World</a></i>, is about Bugz. The album also includes a song titled "Bugz '97", which is a 1997 recording originally from the song "Desperados".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Devil.27s_Night_and_D12_World_.282000.E2.80.9305.29"><i>Devil's Night</i> and <i>D12 World</i> (2000–05)</span></h3>
<p>Their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Devil%27s_Night_(album)" title="Devil's Night (album)">Devil's Night</a></i>, referring to the <a href="/wiki/Devil%27s_Night" title="Devil's Night">tradition</a> of setting unoccupied buildings on fire the night before <a href="/wiki/Halloween" title="Halloween">Halloween</a>, was released in June 2001. It debuted at number one on the U.S. and number two on the UK chart,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums-6">[6]</a></sup> also reaching the top of the Canadian charts. <i>Devil's Night</i> went on to sell four million albums worldwide and two million in the U.S.</p>
<p>In August 2001, D12 and <a href="/wiki/Esham" title="Esham">Esham</a> were kicked off the <a href="/wiki/Warped_Tour" title="Warped Tour">Warped Tour</a> after members of the group allegedly physically attacked Esham over the lyrics of his song "Chemical Imbalance", which contained a reference to Eminem's daughter. Eminem was not present during the tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The group's second album was <i><a href="/wiki/D12_World" title="D12 World">D12 World</a></i> released on April 27, 2004, featuring production by Dr. Dre, Eminem, Mr. Porter and <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, and guest appearances by <a href="/wiki/Obie_Trice" title="Obie Trice">Obie Trice</a> on the track "Loyalty", and <a href="/wiki/B-Real" title="B-Real">B-Real</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cypress_Hill" title="Cypress Hill">Cypress Hill</a> on the track "American Psycho II". It debuted at the top of the U.S., UK,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums-6">[6]</a></sup> and Australian albums charts, and number two in Germany—selling over half a million records in its first week of release in the U.S. alone. "My Band", the album's first single, was also successful reaching number one in Australia, New Zealand and the U.S. rhythmic top forty, the top five in the UK<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums-6">[6]</a></sup> and Germany, and the top ten on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.</p>
<p>The group appear in the 2005 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Longest_Yard_(2005_film)" title="The Longest Yard (2005 film)">The Longest Yard</a></i> credited as "Basketball Convicts". For the movie, the group recorded the song "My Ballz". Although Eminem was the only D12 member not to appear in the movie, he does perform the chorus and a verse in "My Ballz".</p>
<p>The group was often overshadowed by Eminem's massive success, and toured without him for the second album while Eminem was busy recording <i><a href="/wiki/Encore_(Eminem_album)" title="Encore (Eminem album)">Encore</a></i>. Although shadowed by <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s success, other members <a href="/wiki/Bizarre_(rapper)" title="Bizarre (rapper)">Bizarre</a> and <a href="/wiki/Proof_(rapper)" title="Proof (rapper)">Proof</a> managed to prevail with mildly successful solo careers with the releases of <i><a href="/wiki/Hannicap_Circus" title="Hannicap Circus">Hannicap Circus</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Searching_for_Jerry_Garcia" title="Searching for Jerry Garcia">Searching for Jerry Garcia</a></i> in 2005. D12 (minus Eminem) appeared on <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a>'s 2002 album entitled <i>Absolute Power</i> on the track "She-Devil". They also decided that Eminem would not be as prominent on their upcoming third album, which remains evident even after Proof's death. Their third album is estimated to be 70% completed without any input from Eminem. However, in June 2009, Eminem confirmed he was also working hard on the group's third studio album on an announcement made on Rap City during an interview with Eminem and Denaun Porter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The group recorded a song for Eminem's album <i><a href="/wiki/Recovery_(Eminem_album)" title="Recovery (Eminem album)">Recovery</a></i> titled "Hit Me with Your Best Shot", but it was not featured on the official album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_of_Proof_and_Swift.27s_arrest_.282006.29">Death of Proof and Swift's arrest (2006)</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Proof_(rapper)" title="Proof (rapper)">Proof (rapper)</a></div>
<p>On April 11, 2006, Proof died from a gunshot wound at a club in Detroit, after allegedly fatally shooting Keith Bender, Jr., following an argument over a game of pool. Proof was then shot by the bouncer Mario Etheridge, Bender's cousin. He was pronounced dead on arrival at St. John Health's Conner Creek Campus shortly thereafter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He was buried on April 19 in The Fellowship Chapel, Detroit, to a full house of 2,660 with thousands mourning outside.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ten days later after Proof's death, Swift was arrested in <a href="/wiki/Novi,_Michigan" title="Novi, Michigan">Novi, Michigan</a> after failing to show up in court. This was due to his attendance as an honorary <a href="/wiki/Pallbearer" title="Pallbearer">pallbearer</a> at the funeral of group member Proof. Already on probation for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DUI" title="DUI">DUI</a> offense, for which he'd served two days in October 2005, he was sentenced by District Court Judge Brian MacKenzie to 93 days in jail for violating the terms of the probation. This resulted in the stalling of recording for D12's third album. Following his release, work commenced on the album.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mixtapes_and_hiatus_.282007.E2.80.9313.29">Mixtapes and hiatus (2007–13)</span></h3>
<p>On May 21, 2008, after four years of inactivity and the passing of Proof, D12 released a new mixtape, <i>Return of the Dozen Vol. 1</i>. The mixtape was designed to get D12 back on their feet, in preparation for new possible content. The mixtape lacked appearance from Eminem (who was working on his solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Relapse_(Eminem_album)" title="Relapse (Eminem album)">Relapse</a></i>). It contains several guest features from rappers such as <a href="/wiki/Royce_da_5%279%22" title="Royce da 5'9&quot;">Royce da 5'9"</a> (whom they had a feud with) and King Gordy. Some tracks were solo performances from a D12 member, such as "If You Want It" by Kuniva, in a similar way to how Eminem performed "Girls" from <i><a href="/wiki/Devil%27s_Night_(album)" title="Devil's Night (album)">Devil's Night</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-return_of_the_dozen_1_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-return_of_the_dozen_1-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>D12 released their mixtape <i>Return of the Dozen Vol. 2</i> on April 12, 2011, which featured Fuzz Scoota. Fuzz Scoota was one of the original members of D12 but left in 1999. He then later joined back with D12 on April 7, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-www.hiphopdx.com_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-www.hiphopdx.com-14">[14]</a></sup> Eminem was only present on one track throughout the whole mixtape, though he wasn't present at all in the previous one. Due to Eminem being on tour for his album Recovery and because of Mr. Porter being his current <a href="/wiki/Hype_man" title="Hype man">hype man</a> there were four active members of D12 at the time (Bizarre, Kuniva, Fuzz and Swift). The others, while not currently active, were still members of D12. In a video posted on YouTube by Eminem &amp; Royce da 5'9" on June 13, 2011, Eminem stated that he was back in the studio with D12 after not being a very active member for nearly three years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-return_of_the_dozen_2_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-return_of_the_dozen_2-15">[15]</a></sup> D12 performed at the <a href="/wiki/Kanrocksas_Music_Festival" title="Kanrocksas Music Festival">Kanrocksas Music Festival</a> on August 5, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kanrocksas_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-kanrocksas-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The track, "Outro", off the <i>Return of the Dozen Vol. 2</i> mixtape, had a music video released on September 1, 2011, though the video did not feature either Eminem or Mr. Porter. The group also stated intentions to celebrate the 10th anniversary of the release of the <i>Devils Night</i> album, though the celebration was held on the actual Devil's Night on October 30 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Andrew%27s_Hall" title="St. Andrew's Hall">St. Andrew's Hall</a> in Detroit, Michigan, even though the album was released months earlier on June 19, 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D12_Release_Music_Video_for_.27Outro.27.2C_Announce_Party_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-D12_Release_Music_Video_for_.27Outro.27.2C_Announce_Party-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, Bizarre left the group, citing creative differences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-proter_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-proter-18">[18]</a></sup> That same year, March 24, Mr. Porter announced that he had left D12 via a series of tweets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In the Twitter posts, he said that he wanted to focus on being a solo artist, but clarified that holds no animosity toward other members of the group. The group now currently has only four members.</p>
<p>While promoting his recent mixtape, Kuniva was interviewed in August 2012 about the group's status. He replied that it's "not over, we're still coming."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> He went on to state that D12 had been back in the studio as a whole. Former D12 member <a href="/wiki/Mr._Porter" title="Mr. Porter">Mr. Porter</a> was asked on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> on January 26, 2013 about a possible new D12 album. He responded "Not happening bro sorry", clarifying that he would not appear on the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shady_XV_and_The_Devil.27s_Night_Mixtape_.282014.E2.80.93present.29"><i>Shady XV</i> and <i>The Devil's Night Mixtape</i> (2014–present)</span></h3>
<p>In January 2014, Mark Bass of the <a href="/wiki/Bass_Brothers" title="Bass Brothers">Bass Brothers</a> confirmed that D12 had been recently recording at the F.B.T. studio and he had been mixing their recorded material. He also confirmed that Eminem was featured on at least three songs that have been completed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In February 2014, Bizarre confirmed that he was back in the group and that D12 would be releasing their third studio album during 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 25, 2014, a press release on Eminem's official website also revealed D12 to be featured on the label's upcoming 2-disc compilation album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Shady_XV" title="Shady XV">Shady XV</a></i>. The album, released on November 24, 2014, featured one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greatest_hits" title="Greatest hits">greatest hits</a> disc and one disc with new material from a variety of Shady Records recording artists including D12. The new D12 song was entitled "Bane" and was produced by group member <a href="/wiki/Mr._Porter" title="Mr. Porter">Mr. Porter</a> and did not feature Eminem or returning member Fuzz Scoota.</p>
<p>On August 18, 2015, D12 confirmed on Tim Westwood TV that they have already recorded a large amount of songs for the album and will release it "when the time is right."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> On October 1, 2015, it was announced that D12 would be releasing a new mixtape, <i>The Devil's Night Mixtape</i> was released on October 30, 2015 and it featured <a href="/wiki/Lazarus_(rapper)" title="Lazarus (rapper)">Lazarus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bizarre_(rapper)" title="Bizarre (rapper)">Bizarre</a>, Swifty McVay and Kuniva. It was hosted by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Whoo_Kid" title="DJ Whoo Kid">DJ Whoo Kid</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Feuds">Feuds</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Natas">Natas</span></h3>
<p>A rivalry with a fellow Detroit-based group known as <a href="/wiki/Natas_(group)" title="Natas (group)">Natas</a> began in the late 1990s, when D12 opened for <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Kim" title="Lil' Kim">Lil' Kim</a> at her Detroit show. It is alleged that Natas band member <a href="/wiki/Esham" title="Esham">Esham</a> Smith was attacked by a group of D12 associates after he was invited onstage and subsequently outperformed them. Initially, D12 member Eminem was on good terms with Natas, but the two had a falling out when he criticized Esham's second album. In speaking with Murder Dog, Esham responded to D12 calling them "the Village People of rap", Eminem "a super bitch", and making reference to Eminem's daughter, Hailie, being in a coma on the track "Chemical Imbalance". D12, in turn, responded with the song "Instigator", dissing Natas, while Proof made two diss tracks aimed specifically at Esham, "Every Sucker Has a Moment" and "Uh Huh", though the latter was not released. Both sides tried to make amends prior to joining the <a href="/wiki/Warped_Tour" title="Warped Tour">Warped Tour</a> 2001. However a fight was provoked after Esham allegedly threw items at D12's tour bus. Smith sustained a broken nose, bruised eardrum, a temporary loss of hearing, and multiple cuts and bruises according to his label's spokeswoman. Natas member <a href="/wiki/Mastamind" title="Mastamind">Mastamind</a> sustained multiple cuts and bruises. Both groups were removed from the Warped Tour line up, and banned from ever performing at the festival again.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Royce_da_5.279.22">Royce da 5'9"</span></h3>
<p>When first signed to Aftermath Entertainment, Eminem was not on good terms with the rest of D12, causing him to initially only focus on furthering the career of his <a href="/wiki/Bad_Meets_Evil" title="Bad Meets Evil">Bad Meets Evil</a> bandmate <a href="/wiki/Royce_da_5%279%22" title="Royce da 5'9&quot;">Royce da 5'9"</a>. Royce performed on Eminem's first solo album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Slim_Shady_LP" title="The Slim Shady LP">The Slim Shady LP</a></i>, and acted as his hype man for the first weeks of his début tour. Eminem eventually reconciled with D12, and also began giving time to establishing their career. This caused a number of projects Royce had planned with Eminem to be postponed indefinitely. In protest, he resigned as Eminem's hype man, with the position being taken up by Proof. D12 saw this as a betrayal to Eminem, as the resignation came at the awkward time of halfway through his first tour. This resulted in an immediate falling out between Royce da 5'9" and D12, with a drawn-out, public rivalry between the rapper and five of the six members ensuing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Royce released three diss tracks aimed at the group; the first diss track, "Shit on You", was recorded over the instrumental of D12's "Shit on You" and mainly attacked group member Bizarre. "Malcolm X" was the second diss track, which featured Tré Little. D12 responded with "Smack Down", which was recorded over 50 Cent's "Back Down" instrumental. Proof also recorded a diss track entitled "Many Men". This was recorded over 50 Cent's "Many Men (Wish Death)" instrumental. Proof later released another diss with The Purple Gang called "Beef Is Ova". Royce responded with his third diss track, which was recorded over 50 Cent's "In Da Club" instrumental titled "Death Day". In the Summer of 2003, D12 member Proof confronted Royce da 5'9" outside a club in Detroit. Violence erupted between the rappers' entourage, leading to the police being called, and Proof and Royce's arrest. The two were detained overnight in adjacent cells where they talked out their differences and ended the feud.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> A few years later Royce would feature on D12's mixtape, <i>Return of the Dozen</i>, as well as on the line-up of their following tour of Europe and Canada and appearing on the Devils Night Mixtape.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canibus">Canibus</span></h3>
<p>During the time Eminem and <a href="/wiki/Mr._Porter" title="Mr. Porter">Mr. Porter</a> started recording <i><a href="/wiki/Recovery_(Eminem_album)" title="Recovery (Eminem album)">Recovery</a></i>, the rest of D12 were featured on a diss track directed at <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> with rapper <a href="/wiki/Canibus" title="Canibus">Canibus</a> on his record <i><a href="/wiki/Melatonin_Magik" title="Melatonin Magik">Melatonin Magik</a></i> titled "Air Strike (Pop Killer)". Canibus had previously been involved in a feud with Eminem. Canibus name-dropped Eminem's longtime deceased friend <a href="/wiki/Proof_(rapper)" title="Proof (rapper)">Proof</a>, saying "if Proof was alive he'd be dying inside". D12 member Swift responded to the record publicly, and had the following to say about DZK (another rapper featured on the track): "[He] asked us to do a track with him when he already was teamed up with Canibus without us knowing. They dissed Em, took our verses and added them to the song so they can bring traffic and make it seem like we turning on Em... as a desperate attempt to be heard after ducking and dodging Em for 7 years. It was a straight hoe move."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
