<p><b>Aykhal</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Айха́л</span>; <a href="/wiki/Yakut_language" title="Yakut language">Yakut</a>: <span lang="sah" xml:lang="sah">Айхал</span>) is an <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">urban locality</a> (an <a href="/wiki/Urban-type_settlement" title="Urban-type settlement">urban-type settlement</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Mirninsky_District" title="Mirninsky District">Mirninsky District</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Sakha_Republic" title="Sakha Republic">Sakha Republic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located 469 kilometers (291 mi) from <a href="/wiki/Mirny,_Sakha_Republic" title="Mirny, Sakha Republic">Mirny</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of the district,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SakhaR_admlist_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-SakhaR_admlist-2">[2]</a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/Drainage_basin" title="Drainage basin">basin</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Vilyuy_River" title="Vilyuy River">Vilyuy River</a>. As of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>, its population was 13,727.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aykhal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name derives from the <a href="/wiki/Yakut_language" title="Yakut language">Yakut</a> word for <i>fame</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aykhal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sokhsolookh&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sokhsolookh (page does not exist)">Sokhsolookh</a> stream is located in the Aykhal's vicinity. The name of the stream in the <a href="/wiki/Yakut_language" title="Yakut language">Yakut language</a> means <i>the river of death</i> or <i>the river of traps</i>, in reference to a large number of <a href="/wiki/Reindeer" title="Reindeer">reindeer</a> who, according to local legend, have drowned while trying to cross the river ice.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aykhal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was founded in 1961 with the beginnings of diamond extraction from the <a href="/wiki/Volcanic_pipe" title="Volcanic pipe">kimberlite pipe</a> found here.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It was granted <a href="/wiki/Urban-type_settlement" title="Urban-type settlement">urban-type settlement</a> status in the following year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SakhaR_admlist_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-SakhaR_admlist-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1985, Yubileynaya diamond mine was opened 20 kilometers (12 mi) from Aykhal.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aykhal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Urban-type_settlement#Inhabited_localities" title="Urban-type settlement">urban-type settlement</a> of Aykhal,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StatusNote_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-StatusNote-1">[1]</a></sup> together with one <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">rural locality</a> (the <i><a href="/wiki/Village#Russia" title="Village">selo</a></i> of <a href="/wiki/Morkoka" title="Morkoka">Morkoka</a>), is incorporated within <a href="/wiki/Mirninsky_District" title="Mirninsky District">Mirninsky District</a> as the <b><a href="/wiki/Urban-type_settlement#Administrative_divisions" title="Urban-type settlement">Settlement</a> of Aykhal</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SakhaR_admlist_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-SakhaR_admlist-2">[2]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the Settlement of Aykhal is incorporated within Mirninsky Municipal District as <b>Aykhal Urban Settlement</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref531_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Ref531-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy_and_infrastructure">Economy and infrastructure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aykhal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy and infrastructure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The local economy is reliant on diamond mining conducted by <a href="/wiki/ALROSA" title="ALROSA">ALROSA</a> at its Aykhal and Yubileynaya mines. It is served by the <a href="/wiki/Aykhal_Airport" title="Aykhal Airport">Aykhal Airport</a>.</p>
