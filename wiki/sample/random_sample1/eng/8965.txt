<p><b>Għajnsielem</b>, meaning <i>"Peaceful Spring"</i>, is a municipality on the southeastern coast of the island of <a href="/wiki/Gozo" title="Gozo">Gozo</a> in <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, including the entire island of <a href="/wiki/Comino" title="Comino">Comino</a>. It has a population of 3,200 residents (as of March 2014),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and is the first Gozitan village that greets the visitor on leaving <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M%C4%A1arr_(Gozo)" title="Mġarr (Gozo)">Mġarr Harbour</a> towards the Gozitan heartland. Its name originated from the water spring, around which in 1700, <a href="/wiki/Grand_Master_(order)" title="Grand Master (order)">Grandmaster</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perellos" title="Perellos">Perellos</a> built an arcade containing public wash basins and fresh water spouts. Attractions include Lourdes Chapel with its sharp steeple and underlying niche of <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_of_Lourdes" title="Our Lady of Lourdes">Our Lady of Lourdes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fort_Chambray" title="Fort Chambray">Fort Chambray</a> and the towering of Ghajnsielem Parish Church.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Village_Motto_and_Coat_of_Arms">Village Motto and Coat of Arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=G%C4%A7ajnsielem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Village Motto and Coat of Arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Latin motto of Ghajnsielem is Ob fontem prosperitas, that is Flourishing because of a spring – Nistaghna b’nixxiegha ilma. The springs in the area were the magnet that attracted the first inhabitants that eventually led to the prosperity of the place.</p>
