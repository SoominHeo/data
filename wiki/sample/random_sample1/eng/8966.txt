<p><b>Island Garden</b> is an indoor <a href="/wiki/Arena" title="Arena">arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Hempstead,_New_York" title="West Hempstead, New York">West Hempstead, New York</a>, USA. The arena was built in 1998, and has a <a href="/wiki/Seating_capacity" title="Seating capacity">seating capacity</a> of 5,000 people. It hosts a local youth basketball league. It was named for the original Island Garden, which stood just south of the present site.</p>
<p>The original <b>Island Garden Arena</b> was built in 1957 by Arnold "Whitey" Carlson<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt-1998-11-08_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-1998-11-08-1">[1]</a></sup> a descendent of Swedish Immigrants, Mr. Carlson's grandfather was Henrik Carlson, a noted San Diego sculptor who was the Foreign Art Director for the San Diego Exposition (now Balboa Park). Over the years many concert acts such as <a href="/wiki/Cream_(band)" title="Cream (band)">Cream</a>, Dave Clark Five, Tiny Tim, Louis Armstrong, <a href="/wiki/The_Byrds" title="The Byrds">The Byrds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sly_and_the_Family_Stone" title="Sly and the Family Stone">Sly and the Family Stone</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Dave_Brubeck_Quartet" title="The Dave Brubeck Quartet">The Dave Brubeck Quartet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bob_Dylan" title="Bob Dylan">Bob Dylan</a> performed at the venue. In addition to concerts there were also boxing matches, professional wrestling,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> circus, rodeos, stamp shows, midget car racing, and boat shows.</p>
<p>The 5,200 seat arena also hosted the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Nets" title="New York Nets">New York Nets</a> of the <a href="/wiki/American_Basketball_Association" title="American Basketball Association">American Basketball Association</a> from 1969 to 1972. Hall of Famer <a href="/wiki/Rick_Barry" title="Rick Barry">Rick Barry</a> led the ABA in scoring during the 1970–71 season. The Nets were unable to play any home playoff games in 1971 because the arena was booked with other events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1971–72, the Nets posted their first winning season, advancing all the way to the ABA Finals, where they lost to the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_Pacers" title="Indiana Pacers">Indiana Pacers</a>. The franchise stabilized. Late in the season, the team moved from the Garden (capacity 8,500) and moved into the new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nassau_Coliseum" title="Nassau Coliseum">Nassau Coliseum</a> (17,800 capacity). In 1976 the Nets franchise were admitted into the National Basketball Association, where they have remained, eventually becoming today's <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Nets" title="Brooklyn Nets">Brooklyn Nets</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The arena was partially demolished in 1973, unable to compete with the newer and larger <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nassau_Coliseum" title="Nassau Coliseum">Nassau Coliseum</a>. A shopping center was built on that portion of the site. The remaining portion of the structure was revitalized into a new youth basketball venue in 1998. Today, the sports center is a well known arena as it hosts 234 Long Island Lightning teams. It has 3 courts for simultaneous gameplay, or practice. Other teams run in this facility is FE Hoops, DAC Marksmen, Crown Basketball, and Rising Stars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt-1998-11-08_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-1998-11-08-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
