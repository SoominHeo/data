<p>The <b>Department of Justice and Equality</b> (<a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a>: <span lang="ga"><i>An Roinn Dlí agus Cirt agus Comhionannais</i></span>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Department_of_state_(Ireland)" title="Department of state (Ireland)">department</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Ireland" title="Government of Ireland">Government of Ireland</a>. It is led by the <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Justice_and_Equality" title="Minister for Justice and Equality">Minister for Justice and Equality</a> who is assisted by two <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_State_(Ireland)" title="Minister of State (Ireland)">Ministers of State</a>. The department's mission is to maintain and enhance community security and to promote a fairer society in <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Department_of_Justice_and_Equality&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:94_Stephen%27s_Green.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2304" data-file-width="3072" height="225" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/94_Stephen%27s_Green.jpg/300px-94_Stephen%27s_Green.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/94_Stephen%27s_Green.jpg/450px-94_Stephen%27s_Green.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/94_Stephen%27s_Green.jpg/600px-94_Stephen%27s_Green.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>

<p>The mission of the Department of Justice and Equality is to maintain and enhance community security and equality through the development of a range of policies and high quality services which underpin:</p>
<ul>
<li>The protection and assertion of human rights and fundamental freedoms consistent with the common good</li>
<li>The security of the State</li>
<li>An effective and balanced approach to tackling crime</li>
</ul>
<p>The official headquarters and Ministerial offices of the department are in 94 <a href="/wiki/St_Stephen%27s_Green" title="St Stephen's Green">St Stephen's Green</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dublin_2" title="Dublin 2">Dublin 2</a>. Over the years its name has changed several times, with some changes in its role. The department has been known as the following:</p>
<ul>
<li>Department of Home Affairs (1919–1923)</li>
<li>Department of Justice (1923–1997)</li>
<li>Department of Justice, Equality and Law Reform (1997–2010)</li>
<li>Department of Justice and Law Reform (2010–2011)</li>
<li>Department of Justice and Equality (2011–present)</li>
</ul>
<p>In July 2014 the department embarked on a comprehensive program of change, including the recruitment of a new secretary-general following an independent review.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JusticeOverhaul_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-JusticeOverhaul-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Responsibilities">Responsibilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Department_of_Justice_and_Equality&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Responsibilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The department's main areas of responsibility include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Implementing government policy on crime and protecting the security of the State (<a href="/wiki/National_Security_Committee_(Ireland)" title="National Security Committee (Ireland)">National Security Committee</a>).</li>
<li>Providing policy advice in relation to the criminal justice system (<a href="/wiki/Garda_S%C3%ADoch%C3%A1na" title="Garda Síochána">Garda Síochána</a>, the Courts, Prisons and Probation and Welfare Services) and supporting the operation of this system.</li>
<li>Continuing reform of the criminal law and certain areas of the civil law.</li>
<li>Playing a central part in the implementation of core elements in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belfast_Agreement" title="Belfast Agreement">Belfast Agreement</a>.</li>
<li>Co-operating in relevant EU and international matters and promoting Ireland's interests within the associated areas of responsibility.</li>
<li>Implementing the Government's asylum strategy and further developing national immigration policy.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Executive_Agencies">Executive Agencies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Department_of_Justice_and_Equality&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Executive Agencies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The department has five <a href="/wiki/Executive_agency" title="Executive agency">Executive Agencies</a>, which legally are integral parts of the Department but which are managed separately:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Irish_Naturalisation_and_Immigration_Service" title="Irish Naturalisation and Immigration Service">Irish Naturalisation and Immigration Service</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Reception_and_Integration_Agency" title="Reception and Integration Agency">Reception and Integration Agency</a></li>
<li>Forensic Science Laboratory</li>
<li>Probation and Welfare Service</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Irish_Prison_Service" title="Irish Prison Service">Irish Prison Service</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Affiliated_bodies">Affiliated bodies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Department_of_Justice_and_Equality&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Affiliated bodies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Among the <a href="/wiki/Public_service_of_the_Republic_of_Ireland" title="Public service of the Republic of Ireland">state agencies</a> and other bodies affiliated to the department in some way are:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Garda_S%C3%ADoch%C3%A1na" title="Garda Síochána">Garda Síochána</a></li>
<li>Courts Service of Ireland</li>
<li>Equality Authority</li>
<li>Equality Tribunal</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Garda_S%C3%ADoch%C3%A1na_Ombudsman_Commission" title="Garda Síochána Ombudsman Commission">Garda Síochána Ombudsman Commission</a></li>
<li>Garda Síochána Inspectorate</li>
<li>Legal Aid Board</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/State_Pathologist%27s_Office" title="State Pathologist's Office">State Pathologist's Office</a></li>
<li>National Disability Authority (NDA)</li>
<li>Office of the Data Protection Commissioner</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Irish_Film_Classification_Office" title="Irish Film Classification Office">Irish Film Classification Office</a></li>
<li>Office of the Refugee Applications Commissioner</li>
<li>Refugee Appeals Tribunal</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Private_Security_Authority" title="Private Security Authority">Private Security Authority</a></li>
<li>Property Registration Authority</li>
</ul>
<p>No <a href="/wiki/State-sponsored_bodies_of_the_Republic_of_Ireland" title="State-sponsored bodies of the Republic of Ireland">state-sponsored bodies of the Republic of Ireland</a> report to the department.</p>
