<p>In the <b>Battle of <i>Ager Sanguinis</i></b>, also known as the <b>Battle of the Field of Blood</b>, the <b>Battle of Sarmada</b>, or the <b>Battle of Balat</b>, <a href="/wiki/Roger_of_Salerno" title="Roger of Salerno">Roger of Salerno</a>'s Crusader army of the <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Antioch" title="Principality of Antioch">Principality of Antioch</a> was annihilated by the army of <a href="/wiki/Ilghazi" title="Ilghazi">Ilghazi of Mardin</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artuqid" title="Artuqid">Artuqid</a> ruler of <a href="/wiki/Aleppo" title="Aleppo">Aleppo</a> on June 28, 1119.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Ager_Sanguinis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Antioch" title="Antioch">Antioch</a> and the other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crusader_States" title="Crusader States">Crusader States</a> were constantly at war with the Muslim states of Northern <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al_Jazira,_Mesopotamia" title="Al Jazira, Mesopotamia">the Jazeerah</a>, principally Aleppo and <a href="/wiki/Mosul" title="Mosul">Mosul</a>. When <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ridwan_of_Aleppo" title="Ridwan of Aleppo">Ridwan of Aleppo</a> died in 1113, there was a period of peace, at least for a few years. However, <a href="/wiki/Roger_of_Salerno" title="Roger of Salerno">Roger of Salerno</a>, who was ruling Antioch as regent for <a href="/wiki/Bohemond_II_of_Antioch" title="Bohemond II of Antioch">Bohemond II</a>, did not take advantage of Ridwan's death; likewise, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baldwin_II_of_Edessa" title="Baldwin II of Edessa">Baldwin II</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_of_Edessa" title="County of Edessa">count of Edessa</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pons_of_Tripoli" title="Pons of Tripoli">Pons</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_of_Tripoli" title="County of Tripoli">count of Tripoli</a>, looked after their own interests and did not ally with Roger against Aleppo. In 1115, Roger defeated a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Seljuq_Empire" title="Great Seljuq Empire">Seljuk</a> Turkish invasion force led by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bursuq_bin_Bursuq&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bursuq bin Bursuq (page does not exist)">Bursuq bin Bursuq</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Sarmin" title="Battle of Sarmin">Battle of Sarmin</a>.</p>
<p>In 1117 Aleppo came under the rule of the Artuqid <a href="/wiki/Atabeg" title="Atabeg">atabeg</a> <a href="/wiki/Ilghazi" title="Ilghazi">Ilghazi</a>. In 1118 Roger captured <a href="/wiki/Azaz" title="Azaz">Azaz</a>, which left Aleppo open to attack from the Crusaders; in response, Ilghazi invaded the Principality in 1119. Roger marched out from <a href="/wiki/Artah" title="Artah">Artah</a> with <a href="/wiki/Bernard_of_Valence" title="Bernard of Valence">Bernard of Valence</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_Patriarch_of_Antioch" title="Latin Patriarch of Antioch">Latin Patriarch of Antioch</a>. Bernard suggested they remain there, as Artah was a well-defended fortress only a short distance away from Antioch, and Ilghazi would not be able to pass if they were stationed there. The <a href="/wiki/Patriarch" title="Patriarch">Patriarch</a> also advised Roger to call for help from Baldwin, now <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Jerusalem" title="Kingdom of Jerusalem">king of Jerusalem</a>, and Pons, but Roger felt he could not wait for them to arrive.</p>
<p>Roger camped in the pass of <a href="/wiki/Sarmada" title="Sarmada">Sarmada</a>, while Ilghazi besieged the fort of <a href="/wiki/Atarib" title="Atarib">al-Atharib</a>. A force under Robert of Vieux-Pont set out to break the siege, and Ilghazi feigned a retreat, Robert's men were drawn out from the fort and ambushed.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_battle">The battle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Ager_Sanguinis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The battle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ilghazi was also waiting for reinforcements from <a href="/wiki/Toghtekin" title="Toghtekin">Toghtekin</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burid" title="Burid">Burid</a> emir of <a href="/wiki/Damascus" title="Damascus">Damascus</a>, but he too was tired of waiting. Using little-used paths, his army quickly surrounded Roger's camp during the night of June 27. The prince had recklessly chosen a campsite in a wooded valley with steep sides and few avenues of escape. Roger's army of 700 knights and 3,000 foot soldiers, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turcopoles" title="Turcopoles">turcopoles</a>, hastily formed into five divisions. These drew up in a V-shaped line with the tip farthest from the Muslim battle array. From left to right, the divisions were commanded by Robert of St. Lo, Prince Roger, Guy de Frenelle, Geoffrey the Monk and Peter. Meanwhile, Roger told off a sixth division under Renaud Mansoer to protect the Antiochene rear.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>As the Muslim army waited, the <i>qadi</i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ibn_al-Khashshab" title="Ibn al-Khashshab">Abu al-Fadl ibn al-Khashshab</a>, wearing his lawyer's turban but brandishing a lance, rode out in front of the troopers. At first they were incredulous at being harangued by a scholar but at the end of his passionate evocation of the duties and merits of the <a href="/wiki/Jihad" title="Jihad">jihad</a> warrior, according to Kamal ad-Din, the contemporary historian of Aleppo, these hardened professionals wept with emotion and rode into battle.</p>
<p>That morning, June 28, the battle was begun by an archery duel between the Antiochene infantry, posted in front of the knights, and the Turkish bowmen. The crusader army was at first successful when the right-hand divisions of Peter and Geoffrey the Monk attacked and defeated the Artuqids opposed to them. Guy de Frenelle's center division had some success also, but the battle was soon decided on the left flank.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_of_St._Lo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Robert of St. Lo (page does not exist)">Robert of St. Lo</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turcopoles" title="Turcopoles">Turcopoles</a> were driven back into Roger's division, disrupting it. A north wind blew dust in the faces of the Antioch knights and footmen, confusing them further. Soon, Artuqid flanking forces enveloped the crusaders.</p>
<p>During the fighting, Roger was killed by a sword in the face at the foot of the great <a href="/wiki/Crux_gemmata" title="Crux gemmata">jewelled cross</a> which had served as his standard. The rest of the army was killed or captured; only two knights survived. Renaud Mansoer, took refuge in the fort of Sarmada to wait for King Baldwin, but was later taken captive by Ilghazi. Among the other prisoners was likely <a href="/wiki/Walter_the_Chancellor" title="Walter the Chancellor">Walter the Chancellor</a>, who later wrote an account of the battle. The massacre led to the name of the battle, <i>ager sanguinis</i>, <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "the field of blood."</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Ager_Sanguinis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The battle proved that the Muslims could defeat a Crusader army without the help of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seljuk_Turks" title="Seljuk Turks">Seljuks</a>. However, Ilghazi soon went on an alcoholic binge<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Smail.2C_p_30_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Smail.2C_p_30-5">[5]</a></sup> and did not advance to Antioch, where Patriarch Bernard was organizing whatever defense he could. Even so, because of the loss of the Antiochene field army, Atharib, <a href="/wiki/Zardana" title="Zardana">Zerdana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sarmin" title="Sarmin">Sarmin</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ma%27arrat_al-Numan" title="Ma'arrat al-Numan">Ma'arrat al-Numan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kafartab" title="Kafartab">Kafr Tab</a> rapidly fell into Muslim hands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Smail.2C_p_30_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Smail.2C_p_30-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ilghazi was defeated by <a href="/wiki/Baldwin_II_of_Jerusalem" title="Baldwin II of Jerusalem">Baldwin II of Jerusalem</a> and Count Pons at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hab" title="Battle of Hab">Battle of Hab</a> on August 14, and Baldwin took over the regency of Antioch. Subsequently, Baldwin recovered some of the lost towns. Even so, the defeat at the Field of Blood left Antioch severely weakened, and subject to repeated attacks by the Muslims in the following decade. Eventually, the Principality came under the influence of a resurgent <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a>.</p>
<p>The Crusaders regained some of their influence in Syria at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Azaz_(1125)" title="Battle of Azaz (1125)">Battle of Azaz</a> six years later in 1125.</p>
<p>The description <i>ager sanguinis</i> is possibly a <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Biblical</a> reference to the field purchased by <a href="/wiki/Judas_Iscariot" title="Judas Iscariot">Judas</a> with the money he had been given to betray <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christ" title="Christ">Christ</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Acts_of_the_Apostles" title="Acts of the Apostles">Acts of the Apostles</a> records that Judas killed himself in the field, and it was thus known as <i>acheldemach</i> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aramaic" title="Aramaic">Aramaic</a>, and <i>ager sanguinis</i> in the <a href="/wiki/Vulgate" title="Vulgate">Vulgate</a>.</p>
