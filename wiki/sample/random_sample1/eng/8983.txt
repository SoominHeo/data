<p><b>Ards</b> (named after the <a href="/wiki/Ards_Peninsula" title="Ards Peninsula">Ards Peninsula</a>) was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Districts_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Districts of Northern Ireland">local government district</a> in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Borough_status_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Borough status in the United Kingdom">status of borough</a>. It was one of twenty-six districts formed on 1 October 1973, and had its headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Newtownards" title="Newtownards">Newtownards</a>. It was merged with neighbouring <a href="/wiki/North_Down_Borough_Council" title="North Down Borough Council">North Down</a> on May 1, 2015 to form the new Borough of <a href="/wiki/Ards_and_North_Down" title="Ards and North Down">Ards and North Down</a> Other towns in the defunct Borough include <a href="/wiki/Portaferry" title="Portaferry">Portaferry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Comber" title="Comber">Comber</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Donaghadee" title="Donaghadee">Donaghadee</a>, and the population of the area was 78,078 according to the 2011 census.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Strangford_Lough" title="Strangford Lough">Strangford Lough</a> is at the heart of the area, and is the largest inlet in <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> with internationally renowned wildlife. The <a href="/wiki/Irish_Sea" title="Irish Sea">Irish Sea</a> coast stretches from Donaghadee to Portaferry. <a href="/wiki/Mount_Stewart" title="Mount Stewart">Mount Stewart</a>, a <a href="/wiki/National_Trust_for_Places_of_Historic_Interest_or_Natural_Beauty" title="National Trust for Places of Historic Interest or Natural Beauty">National Trust</a> property on the shore of Strangford Lough is in the area, as well as Northern Ireland’s only <a href="/wiki/Aquarium" title="Aquarium">aquarium</a>, Exploris, in Portaferry and <a href="/wiki/Grace_Neill%27s" title="Grace Neill's">Grace Neill's</a> pub in Donaghadee.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Borough_council">Borough council</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ards_(borough)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Borough council">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The borough was governed by Ards Borough Council (<a href="/wiki/Ulster_Scots_dialects" title="Ulster Scots dialects">Ulster-Scots</a>: <i>Burgh Cooncil o' the Airds, Newton an' Blathewick</i>). The borough was divided into four district electoral areas: Newtownards, Ards West, Ards East and <a href="/wiki/Ards_Peninsula" title="Ards Peninsula">Ards Peninsula</a>, from which 23 members are elected. As of February 2011 the following parties were represented on the council: 11 <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Unionist_Party" title="Democratic Unionist Party">Democratic Unionist Party</a> (DUP), 6 <a href="/wiki/Ulster_Unionist_Party" title="Ulster Unionist Party">Ulster Unionist Party</a> (UUP), 4 <a href="/wiki/Alliance_Party_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Alliance Party of Northern Ireland">Alliance Party</a>, and 1 <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_and_Labour_Party" title="Social Democratic and Labour Party">Social Democratic and Labour Party</a> (SDLP), and there are one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Independent_(politics)" title="Independent (politics)">Independent</a> councillors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-council_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-council-1">[1]</a></sup> The next election was due to take place in May 2009, but on April 25, 2008, <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Woodward" title="Shaun Woodward">Shaun Woodward</a>, <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Northern_Ireland" title="Secretary of State for Northern Ireland">Secretary of State for Northern Ireland</a> announced that the scheduled 2009 district council elections were to be postponed until the introduction of the eleven new councils in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The proposed reforms were abandoned in 2010, and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_local_elections,_2011" title="Northern Ireland local elections, 2011">district council elections took place in 2011</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mayor_of_Ards">Mayor of Ards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ards_(borough)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mayor of Ards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On creation in 1973 Ards District Council adopted the <a href="/wiki/Royal_charter" title="Royal charter">charter of incorporation</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal borough</a> of Newtownards, to become Ards Borough Council. The charter also granted the chairman of the council the title "Mayor of Ards".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Parliamentary_and_assembly_representation">Parliamentary and assembly representation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ards_(borough)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Parliamentary and assembly representation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In elections for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Westminster_Parliament" title="Westminster Parliament">Westminster Parliament</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Assembly" title="Northern Ireland Assembly">Northern Ireland Assembly</a> most of the borough is included in the <a href="/wiki/Strangford_(Assembly_constituency)" title="Strangford (Assembly constituency)">Strangford</a> constituency, with part (Donaghadee North, Donaghadee South and Millisle) in the <a href="/wiki/North_Down_(Assembly_constituency)" title="North Down (Assembly constituency)">North Down</a> constituency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Town_twinning">Town twinning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ards_(borough)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Town twinning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The borough of Ards has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sister_city" title="Sister city">sister city</a> (twinning) relationship with the city of <a href="/wiki/Peoria,_Arizona" title="Peoria, Arizona">Peoria, Arizona</a>, USA. As a result of this relationship, regular exchange visits are made between the two communities by artists, educators, business, political, and community leaders.</p>
