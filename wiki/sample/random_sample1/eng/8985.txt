<p>The <i><b>Azusa</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">あずさ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> and <i><b>Super Azusa</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">スーパーあずさ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> are <a href="/wiki/Limited_express" title="Limited express">limited express</a> services operated by <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">East Japan Railway Company</a> (JR East), which run between <a href="/wiki/Shinjuku_Station" title="Shinjuku Station">Shinjuku</a> and <a href="/wiki/Matsumoto_Station" title="Matsumoto Station">Matsumoto</a> via the <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%AB%C5%8D_Main_Line" title="Chūō Main Line">Chūō Main Line</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shinonoi_Line" title="Shinonoi Line">Shinonoi Line</a>. The name <i>Azusa</i> is taken from the Azusa River in <a href="/wiki/Matsumoto,_Nagano" title="Matsumoto, Nagano">Matsumoto, Nagano</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azusa_(train)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:JR_East_183_GU_azusa_o.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="655" data-file-width="945" height="152" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/JR_East_183_GU_azusa_o.jpg/220px-JR_East_183_GU_azusa_o.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/JR_East_183_GU_azusa_o.jpg/330px-JR_East_183_GU_azusa_o.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/JR_East_183_GU_azusa_o.jpg/440px-JR_East_183_GU_azusa_o.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:JR_East_183_GU_azusa_o.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A 183 series EMU in original "Grade-up Azusa" livery</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <i>Azusa</i> service began on 1 October 1957 as a semi express operating between Shinjuku and Matsumoto. This operated until 24 April 1960 when it was absorbed into <i>Hakuba</i> semi express services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meikan1995_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-meikan1995-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <i>Azusa</i> name was revived from 12 December 1966 for use on limited express services operating between Shinjuku and Matsumoto.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meikan1995_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-meikan1995-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The premier <i>Super Azusa</i> service commenced on 3 December 1994 with the delivery of the first new <a href="/wiki/E351_series" title="E351 series">E351 series</a> tilting EMUs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meikan1995_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-meikan1995-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>New <a href="/wiki/E257_series" title="E257 series">E257 series</a> EMUs were introduced on <i>Azusa</i> services from the start of the revised timetable on 1 December 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kotsu2008_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-kotsu2008-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Smoking was prohibited in all cars from 18 March 2007.</p>
