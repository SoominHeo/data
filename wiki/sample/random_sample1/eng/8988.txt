<p><i><b>Are We Done Yet?</b></i> is a 2007 American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Family_film" title="Family film">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Comedy_film" title="Comedy film">comedy film</a> starring <a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The film is a remake of the <a href="/wiki/Cary_Grant" title="Cary Grant">Cary Grant</a> comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Blandings_Builds_His_Dream_House" title="Mr. Blandings Builds His Dream House">Mr. Blandings Builds His Dream House</a></i> and a sequel to the 2005 comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Are_We_There_Yet%3F_(film)" title="Are We There Yet? (film)">Are We There Yet?</a></i> The film was directed by Steve Carr from a screenplay by Hank Nelken. It was produced by <a href="/wiki/Revolution_Studios" title="Revolution Studios">Revolution Studios</a> and <a href="/wiki/RKO_Pictures" title="RKO Pictures">RKO Pictures</a> and distributed by <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Pictures" title="Columbia Pictures">Columbia Pictures</a>. The film was shot on location in <a href="/wiki/Tsawwassen" title="Tsawwassen">Tsawwassen</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, Canada, but is set in <a href="/wiki/Newberg,_Oregon" title="Newberg, Oregon">Newberg, Oregon</a>, United States.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Are_We_Done_Yet%3F&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the events of <i><a href="/wiki/Are_We_There_Yet%3F_(film)" title="Are We There Yet? (film)">Are We There Yet?</a></i>, Nick Persons (<a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a>) has married Suzanne (<a href="/wiki/Nia_Long" title="Nia Long">Nia Long</a>) and moved in with her, Kevin (<a href="/wiki/Philip_Daniel_Bolden" title="Philip Daniel Bolden">Philip Daniel Bolden</a>), and Lindsey (<a href="/wiki/Aleisha_Allen" title="Aleisha Allen">Aleisha Allen</a>) into his apartment, also purchasing a 2007 <a href="/wiki/Cadillac_Escalade" title="Cadillac Escalade">Cadillac Escalade</a> (after the destruction of his <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Navigator" title="Lincoln Navigator">Lincoln Navigator</a> SUV). He has also sold his sports memorabilia store to his best friend, Marty (played by <a href="/wiki/Jay_Mohr" title="Jay Mohr">Jay Mohr</a> in the first film). While getting ready for an interview with <a href="/wiki/Magic_Johnson" title="Magic Johnson">Magic Johnson</a>, Suzanne tells Nick that she is pregnant and they later find out that there will be twins.</p>
<p>For more space, Suzanne and Nick go check out a house, although Lindsey and Kevin – mainly the former – are against it. They meet Chuck Mitchell Jr. (<a href="/wiki/John_C._McGinley" title="John C. McGinley">John C. McGinley</a>) there, and after some talking Nick decides to take the house. But he didn't get it inspected first and they find mold in the house, so Nick is angry with Chuck as he almost destroys their house. Meanwhile, Lindsey sneaks out to go to a party and when Nick finds out, he grounds her. He finally decides to fire Chuck, which causes Suzanne to take the kids and move to the guest house, with the parting shot <i>You're the one who's tearing this family apart!</i> After taking some time to think, Nick decides to fix the house on his own and also to apologize to Chuck. Chuck responds by bringing his friends to help, including Lindsey and Kevin.</p>
<p>When Suzanne goes into labor with the hospital half an hour away, Nick, Kevin and Lindsey have to deliver the babies. Chuck tries to get there, but his truck breaks down and he power walks down to the house. While she's still in labor, Nick gets a call from Magic. After Suzanne gives birth to twins, the movie ends six months later with a big barbecue in their backyard at which Nick debuts his new magazine titled <i>Are We Done Yet?</i>, based on his experience building the house.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Are_We_Done_Yet%3F&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film made $58.4 million worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mojo_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mojo-1">[1]</a></sup> The film was released in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> on June 8, 2007, and opened on #3, behind <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oceans_Thirteen" title="Oceans Thirteen">Oceans Thirteen</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pirates_of_the_Caribbean:_At_Worlds_End" title="Pirates of the Caribbean: At Worlds End">Pirates of the Caribbean: At Worlds End</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Are_We_Done_Yet%3F&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film holds an 8% approval rating on aggregate review site <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, based on ninety three collected reviews, and has an average score of 3.3 out of 10. The site's consensus reads: "<i>Are We Done Yet?</i> plays it way too safe with generic slapstick and uninspired domestic foibles."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> gave the film a weighted average score of 36 out of 100, based on twenty one sampled reviews, indicating "generally unfavorable reviews."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metacritic_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-metacritic-5">[5]</a></sup> The film was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Raspberry_Award_for_Worst_Prequel,_Remake,_Rip-off_or_Sequel" title="Golden Raspberry Award for Worst Prequel, Remake, Rip-off or Sequel">Golden Raspberry Award for Worst Prequel, Remake, Rip-off or Sequel</a> (lost to <i><a href="/wiki/I_Know_Who_Killed_Me" title="I Know Who Killed Me">I Know Who Killed Me</a></i>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Audiences polled by <a href="/wiki/CinemaScore" title="CinemaScore">CinemaScore</a> gave the film an average grade of "B" on an A+ to F scale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CinemaScore_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-CinemaScore-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
