<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Academy Honorary Award</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>Academy Honorary Award</b>, instituted in 1948 for the <a href="/wiki/21st_Academy_Awards" title="21st Academy Awards">21st Academy Awards</a> (previously called the <b>Special Award</b>), is given by the discretion of the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Motion_Picture_Arts_and_Sciences#Current_administration_of_the_Academy" title="Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences">Board of Governors</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Motion_Picture_Arts_and_Sciences" title="Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences">Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences</a> (AMPAS) to celebrate motion picture achievements that are not covered by existing <a href="/wiki/Academy_Awards" title="Academy Awards">Academy Awards</a>, although prior winners of competitive Academy Awards are not excluded from receiving the Honorary Award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AboutHonoraryAward_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-AboutHonoraryAward-1">[1]</a></sup> Unless otherwise specified, <b>Honorary Award</b> recipients receive the same gold Oscar statuettes received by winners of the competitive Academy Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AboutHonoraryAward1d_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-AboutHonoraryAward1d-2">[2]</a></sup> Unlike the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Special_Achievement_Award" title="Academy Special Achievement Award">Special Achievement Award</a> instituted in <a href="/wiki/1972_in_film" title="1972 in film">1972</a>, those on whom the Academy confers its Honorary Award do not have to meet "the Academy's eligibility year and deadline requirements."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SpecAch_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-SpecAch-3">[3]</a></sup> Like the Special Achievement Award, the <b>Special Award</b> and <b>Honorary Award</b> have been used to reward significant achievements of the year that did not fit in existing categories, subsequently leading the Academy to establish several new categories, and to honor exceptional career achievements, contributions to the motion picture industry, and service to the Academy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AboutHonoraryAward1c_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-AboutHonoraryAward1c-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The Academy Honorary Award is often awarded in preference to those with noted achievements in motion pictures who have nevertheless never won an Academy Award. Thus, many of its recipients are <a href="/wiki/Classical_Hollywood_cinema" title="Classical Hollywood cinema">Classic Hollywood</a> stars, such as <a href="/wiki/Lillian_Gish" title="Lillian Gish">Lillian Gish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Stanwyck" title="Barbara Stanwyck">Barbara Stanwyck</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lauren_Bacall" title="Lauren Bacall">Lauren Bacall</a>.</p>
<p>Since 2009 the Honorary Award is presented at the annual <a href="/wiki/Governors_Awards" title="Governors Awards">Governors Awards</a> rather than at the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Awards" title="Academy Awards">Academy Awards</a>.</p>
