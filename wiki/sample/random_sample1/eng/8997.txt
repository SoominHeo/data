<p><b>Theta Eridani</b> (<b>θ Eridani</b>, abbreviated <b>Theta Eri</b>, <b>θ Eri</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Binary_star" title="Binary star">binary system</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Eridanus_(constellation)" title="Eridanus (constellation)">Eridanus</a>. Its two components are designated <b>θ¹ Eridani</b>, also named <b>Acamar</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAU-CSN_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-IAU-CSN-7">[7]</a></sup> and <b>θ² Eridani</b>. The system's distance from the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a> as measured by the <a href="/wiki/Hipparcos" title="Hipparcos">Hipparcos</a> astrometry satellite is approximately 120 <a href="/wiki/Light-year" title="Light-year">light-years</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa323_L49_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-aaa323_L49-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa474_2_653_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-aaa474_2_653-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GSM_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-GSM-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nomenclature">Nomenclature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Theta_Eridani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Nomenclature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Theta Eridani</i> is the system's <a href="/wiki/Bayer_designation" title="Bayer designation">Bayer designation</a>; <i>θ¹</i> and <i>θ² Eridani</i> those of its two components.</p>
<p>The system bore the traditional name <i>Acamar</i>, derived from the <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a> آخِر النَّهْر<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <i>Ākhir an-nahr</i> which means "the end of the river", via a Roman-alphabet handwriting misread "rn" to "m". In 2016, the <a href="/wiki/International_Astronomical_Union" title="International Astronomical Union">International Astronomical Union</a> organized a <a href="/wiki/IAU_Working_Group_on_Star_Names" title="IAU Working Group on Star Names">Working Group on Star Names</a> (WGSN)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WGSN_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-WGSN-9">[9]</a></sup> to catalog and standardize proper names for stars. The WGSN approved the name <i>Acamar</i> for <i>θ¹ Eridani</i> on 20 July 2016 and it is now so entered in the IAU Catalog of Star Names.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAU-CSN_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-IAU-CSN-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The term <i>Ākhir an-nahr</i> or <i>Achr al Nahr</i> appeared in the catalogue of stars in the <i>Calendarium of <a href="/wiki/Al_Achsasi_al_Mouakket" title="Al Achsasi al Mouakket">Al Achsasi Al Mouakket</a></i>, which was translated into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a> as <i>Postrema Fluminis</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Historically, Acamar represented the end of the constellation Eridanus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kaler_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-kaler-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baa108_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-baa108-11">[11]</a></sup> Now that distinction is held by the star <a href="/wiki/Achernar" title="Achernar">Achernar</a>, which shares the same Arabic <a href="/wiki/Etymology" title="Etymology">etymology</a>. Achernar is not visible from the Greek isles (<a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">latitudes</a> &gt; 33° North),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-earthsky_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-earthsky-12">[12]</a></sup> hence the choice of Acamar as the river's end during the time of <a href="/wiki/Hipparchus" title="Hipparchus">Hipparchus</a> and later <a href="/wiki/Ptolemy" title="Ptolemy">Ptolemy</a>.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>, <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">天園</span> (<span lang="zh-Latn" xml:lang="zh-Latn">Tiān Yuán</span>), meaning <i><a href="/wiki/Net_(Chinese_constellation)" title="Net (Chinese constellation)">Celestial Orchard</a></i>, refers to an asterism consisting of Theta Eridani, <a href="/wiki/Chi_Eridani" title="Chi Eridani">Chi Eridani</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phi_Eridani" title="Phi Eridani">Phi Eridani</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kappa_Eridani" title="Kappa Eridani">Kappa Eridani</a>, <a href="/wiki/HD_16754" title="HD 16754">HD 16754</a>, <a href="/wiki/HD_23319" title="HD 23319">HD 23319</a>, <a href="/wiki/HD_24072" title="HD 24072">HD 24072</a>, <a href="/wiki/HD_24160" title="HD 24160">HD 24160</a>, <a href="/wiki/Upsilon4_Eridani" title="Upsilon4 Eridani">Upsilon<sup>4</sup> Eridani</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=43_Eridani&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="43 Eridani (page does not exist)">43 Eridani</a>, <a href="/wiki/Upsilon2_Eridani" title="Upsilon2 Eridani">Upsilon<sup>2</sup> Eridani</a> and <a href="/wiki/Upsilon1_Eridani" title="Upsilon1 Eridani">Upsilon<sup>1</sup> Eridani</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Consequently, Theta Eridani itself is known as <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">天園六</span> (<span lang="zh-Latn" xml:lang="zh-Latn">Tiān Yuán liù</span>, English: the Sixth Star of Celestial Orchard.)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stellar_system">Stellar system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Theta_Eridani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Stellar system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Theta Eridani is a <a href="/wiki/Binary_star" title="Binary star">binary system</a> with some evidence suggesting it is part of a multiple <a href="/wiki/Star_system" title="Star system">star system</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nsv1_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-nsv1-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The main star, θ¹ Eridani, is of <a href="/wiki/Stellar_classification" title="Stellar classification">spectral class</a> A4 with a +3.2 <a href="/wiki/Apparent_magnitude" title="Apparent magnitude">apparent magnitude</a>. Its companion star, θ² Eridani, is of spectral class A1 with an apparent magnitude of +4.3. The <a href="/wiki/Angular_distance" title="Angular distance">angular separation</a> of the two stars is equal to 8.3 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minute_of_arc#Symbols.2C_abbreviations_and_subdivisions" title="Minute of arc">arcseconds</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kaler_5-7"><a href="#cite_note-kaler-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ptolemy" title="Ptolemy">Ptolemy</a> described Theta Eridani as a first-magnitude star, which some have taken to be in fact <a href="/wiki/Achernar" title="Achernar">Achernar</a>, which today appears just above the horizon in <a href="/wiki/Alexandria" title="Alexandria">Alexandria</a> where Ptolemy lived. Achernar, however, was not visible to Ptolemy (it is from Alexandria today due to <a href="/wiki/Precession" title="Precession">precession</a>); its declination in 100CE was -67, making it invisible even at <a href="/wiki/Aswan" title="Aswan">Aswan</a>. Ptolemy's Eridanus thus without doubt ended at Theta Eridani, which rose about 10 degrees above the horizon from Alexandria. This fact besides positively identifying Theta Eridani as the original "end of the river", also strengthens the case for it having been first magnitude in ancient times.</p>
