<p><b>Akabira</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">赤平市</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Akabira-shi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> located in central <a href="/wiki/Sorachi_Subprefecture" title="Sorachi Subprefecture">Sorachi Subprefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hokkaido" title="Hokkaido">Hokkaido</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. The name is <a href="/wiki/Ainu_language" title="Ainu language">Ainu</a> for "mountain ridge".</p>
<p>As of 2008, the city has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 13,597 and the <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">density</a> of 105 persons per km<sup>2</sup>. The total area is 129.88 km<sup>2</sup>. The city was once a prosperous coal-mining city, its population reaching 59,430 in 1960. The population has been gradually declining.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Akabira,_Hokkaido&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>1891 Settlement begins</li>
<li>1918 Moshiri Coal Mine opens</li>
<li>1922 Second-class municipality Akabira Village split off of first-class municipality <a href="/wiki/Utashinai,_Hokkaido" title="Utashinai, Hokkaido">Utashinai</a></li>
<li>1929 Akabira becomes a First-Class municipality</li>
<li>1937 Shōwa Denkō Toyosato Mine opened</li>
<li>1938 Sumitomo Coal Mine, Hokkaido Coal Mine Railroad and Akama Coal Mine opened</li>
<li>1943 Akabira Village becomes Akabira Town</li>
<li>July 1, 1954 Akabira Town becomes Akabira City</li>
<li>1967 Shōwa Denkō Toyosato Mine closesd</li>
<li>1969 Moshiri Coal Mine closed</li>
<li>1973 Akama Coal Mine closed</li>
<li>1994 Sumitomo Coal Mine closed</li>
<li>2003 Mid-Sorachi Merger Committee established.</li>
<li>2007 A major elementary school in Akabira closed down. It once had about 5000 students but has dropped rapidly after the closure of the coal mines.</li>
</ul>
