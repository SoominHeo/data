<p><b>Akashi Castle</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">明石城</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Akashi-jō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_castle" title="Japanese castle">Japanese castle</a> in <a href="/wiki/Akashi,_Hy%C5%8Dgo" title="Akashi, Hyōgo">Akashi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hy%C5%8Dgo_Prefecture" title="Hyōgo Prefecture">Hyōgo Prefecture</a>, Japan.</p>
<p>This castle was constructed by <a href="/wiki/Ogasawara_Tadazane" title="Ogasawara Tadazane">Ogasawara Tadazane</a> as his own castle from 1617 to 1619 to watch over the western lords, by the order of <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Hidetada" title="Tokugawa Hidetada">Tokugawa Hidetada</a>, on Mount Akamatsu. It is known <a href="/wiki/Miyamoto_Musashi" title="Miyamoto Musashi">Miyamoto Musashi</a> helped too as he was invited by Ogasawara Tadanao as a "Construction Supervisor". The castle was built in one year, which was a relatively short amount of time. This was done so quickly because as a result of a 1615 law mandating one castle per clan, many castles were dismantled and parts of some were used to construct Akashi Castle.</p>
<p>The castle was built in Akashi, which was an important strategic point between Osaka and western Japan, where many major <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tozama" title="Tozama">tozama daimyo</a> had their feudal estates. Akashi Castle was expected to play the role of a key staging point to stop a hypothetical invasion of Osaka.</p>
<p>Akashi castle underwent major repairs in 1739, and was demolished by the Meiji Government in 1874.</p>

<p>In Akashi Castle, there are two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Important_Cultural_Properties_of_Japan" title="Important Cultural Properties of Japan">Important Cultural Properties</a> selected by Japanese government:</p>
<ul>
<li>Hitsujisaru <a href="/wiki/Yagura" title="Yagura">Yagura</a></li>
<li>Tatsumi Yagura</li>
</ul>
<p>These two <i>yagura</i>, or towers, are the castle's only remaining buildings.</p>
