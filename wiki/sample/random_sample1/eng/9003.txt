<p>The <b>Arkansas RimRockers</b> were an <a href="/wiki/NBA_Development_League" title="NBA Development League">NBA Development League</a> team. They participated in minor league <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> and based in <a href="/wiki/North_Little_Rock,_Arkansas" title="North Little Rock, Arkansas">North Little Rock, Arkansas</a>.<br/>
<b>Logo design:</b> A red, white, and blue ball going through a <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> rim (homage to when they were in the <a href="/wiki/American_Basketball_Association_(2000%E2%80%93present)" title="American Basketball Association (2000–present)">ABA</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Franchise_history">Franchise history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arkansas_RimRockers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Franchise history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Rimrockers began play during the 2004-05 season for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Basketball_Association_(21st_century)" title="American Basketball Association (21st century)">American Basketball Association</a>. They posted a 32-5 record in the team’s inaugural season and won the ABA championship. Soon after winning the title, they left the ABA and began play in the <a href="/wiki/NBA_Development_League" title="NBA Development League">NBA Development League</a> for the 2005-06 season.</p>
<p>On February 10, 2006, the team fired head coach <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Joe_Harge&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Joe Harge (page does not exist)">Joe Harge</a>, the only man to coach the team up to that point. He was replaced by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Stoglin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Andy Stoglin (page does not exist)">Andy Stoglin</a>.</p>
<p>Stoglin said he would bring a much different style that begins with strict discipline. He joined the organization as an assistant during the 2005-06 season after leading the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mississippi_Stingers" title="Mississippi Stingers">Mississippi Stingers</a> to the ABA semifinals, where they lost to Arkansas. Stoglin has been coaching since 1969, including a 13-year run as <a href="/wiki/Jackson_State_Tigers_basketball" title="Jackson State Tigers basketball">Jackson State’s</a> head coach.</p>
<p>The team didn't return to Arkansas for 2007-08 because attendance dropped to the point that the owner could no longer afford the lease. The team hoped to move to <a href="/wiki/La_Crosse,_Wisconsin" title="La Crosse, Wisconsin">La Crosse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a> to play at the <a href="/wiki/La_Crosse_Center" title="La Crosse Center">La Crosse Center</a> for the 2007-08 season, where they would have been known as the <b>La Crosse RimRockers</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> However, due to the Center's lease demands being higher than Little Rock's, the deal fell through. The team was inactive for the 2007-08 season, and again in the 2008-09 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
