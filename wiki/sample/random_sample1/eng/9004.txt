<p><b>Aksu</b> (<a href="/wiki/Uyghur_language" title="Uyghur language">Uyghur</a>: <span lang="ug" style="font-family:'UKIJ Tuz','UKIJ Nasq','UKIJ Basma','UKIJ_Mac Basma','UKIJ Zilwa','UKIJ Esliye','UKIJ Tuz Basma','UKIJ Tuz Kitab','UKIJ Tuz Gezit','UKIJ Tuz Qara','UKIJ Tuz Qara','UKIJ Tuz Tor','UKIJ Kesme','UKIJ Kesme Tuz','UKIJ Qara','UKIJ Basma Aq','UKIJ Basma Qara','UKIJ Basma Tuz','UKIJ Putuk','UKIJ Tuz Xet','UKIJ Tom Xet','UKIJ Tuz Jurnal','UKIJ Arabic','UKIJ CJK','UKIJ Ekran','UKIJ_Mac Ekran','UKIJ Teng','UKIJ Tor','UKIJ Tuz Tom','UKIJ Mono Keng','UKIJ Mono Tar','UKIJ Nokia','UKIJ SimSun','UKIJ Yanfon','UKIJ Qolyazma','UKIJ Saet','UKIJ Nasq Zilwa','UKIJ Sulus','UKIJ Sulus Tom','UKIJ 3D','UKIJ Diwani','UKIJ Diwani Yantu','UKIJ Diwani Tom','UKIJ Esliye Tom','UKIJ Esliye Qara','UKIJ Jelliy','UKIJ Kufi','UKIJ Kufi Tar','UKIJ Kufi Uz','UKIJ Kufi Yay','UKIJ Merdane','UKIJ Ruqi','UKIJ Mejnuntal','UKIJ Junun','UKIJ Moy Qelem','UKIJ Chiwer Kesme','UKIJ Orxun-Yensey','UKIJ Elipbe','UKIJ Qolyazma Tez','UKIJ Qolyazma Tuz','UKIJ Qolyazma Yantu','UKIJ Ruqi Tuz',FZWWBBOT_Unicode,FZWWHQHTOT_Unicode,'Microsoft Uighur'; font-size:135%;" xml:lang="ug">ئاقسۇ, Ақсу</span>‎, <a href="/wiki/Uyghur_Latin_alphabet" title="Uyghur Latin alphabet">ULY</a>: <i>Aqsu</i>, <a href="/wiki/Uyghur_New_Script" title="Uyghur New Script">UYY</a>: <i>Ak̡su</i><sup><a href="/wiki/Uyghur_alphabets#Present_situation" title="Uyghur alphabets">?</a></sup>; <a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">阿克苏</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Ākèsù</span></i>), is a city in and the seat of <a href="/wiki/Aksu_Prefecture" title="Aksu Prefecture">Aksu Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xinjiang" title="Xinjiang">Xinjiang</a>, lying at the northern edge of the <a href="/wiki/Tarim_Basin" title="Tarim Basin">Tarim Basin</a>. The name Aksu literally means <i>white water</i> (in Turkic), and is used for both the <a href="/wiki/Oasis" title="Oasis">oasis</a> town and <a href="/wiki/Aksu_River_(Xinjiang)" title="Aksu River (Xinjiang)">the river</a>.</p>
<p>The economy of Aksu is mostly agricultural, with cotton, in particular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long-staple_cotton" title="Long-staple cotton">long-staple cotton</a>, as the main product. Also produced are grain, fruits, oils, and beets. The industry mostly consists of weaving, cement, and chemical industries.</p>
<p>The land currently under the administration of the Aksu City is divided in two parts, separated by the <a href="/wiki/Aral,_Xinjiang" title="Aral, Xinjiang">Aral</a> City. The northern part hosts the city center, while the southern part is occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Taklamakan_Desert" title="Taklamakan Desert">Taklamakan Desert</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aksu,_Xinjiang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name Aksu comes from the <a href="/wiki/Uyghur_language" title="Uyghur language">Uyghur</a> word for "white water". It is also <a href="/wiki/Transliteration" title="Transliteration">transliterated</a> <b>Akesu</b>, <b>Ak-su</b>, <b>Akshu</b>, <b>Aqsu</b>. It is also known as <b>Bharuka</b> and <b>Bolujia</b> (in <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>), <b>Po-lu-chia</b> (in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wade-Giles" title="Wade-Giles">Wade-Giles</a>)</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aksu,_Xinjiang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Former_Han" title="Former Han">Former Han</a> dynasty (125 BCE to 23 CE) at least until the early <a href="/wiki/Tang_dynasty" title="Tang dynasty">Tang</a> dynasty (618-907 CE), Aksu was known as Gumo <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">姑墨</span> [Ku-mo].<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The ancient capital town of Nan ("Southern Town") was likely well south of the present town.</p>
<p>During the Han dynasty, Gumo is described as a "kingdom" (<i>guo</i>) containing 3,500 households and 24,500 individuals, including 4,500 people able to bear arms. It is said to have produced copper, iron and <a href="/wiki/Orpiment" title="Orpiment">orpiment</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The territory of Gumo was roughly situated in the counties of <a href="/wiki/Baicheng_County" title="Baicheng County">Baicheng</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wensu" title="Wensu">Wensu</a> and the city of Aksu of nowadays.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Chinese pilgrim <a href="/wiki/Xuanzang" title="Xuanzang">Xuanzang</a> visited this "kingdom" in 629 CE and referred to it as Baluka. He recorded that there were tens of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sarvastivadin" title="Sarvastivadin">Sarvastivadin</a> <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhist</a> monasteries in the kingdom and over 1,000 monks. He said the kingdom was 600 <i><a href="/wiki/Li_(unit)" title="Li (unit)">li</a></i> from east to west, and 300 <i>li</i> from north to south. Its capital was said to be six <i><a href="/wiki/Li_(unit)" title="Li (unit)">li</a></i> in circumference. He reported that the "native products, climate, temperament of the people, customs, written language and law are the same as in the country of Kuci or modern <a href="/wiki/Kucha" title="Kucha">Kucha</a>, (some 300 km or 190 mi to the east)], but the spoken language is somewhat different [from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kuchean_language" title="Kuchean language">Kuchean</a>]." He also stated that fine cotton and hemp cloth made in the area was traded in neighbouring countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 7th, 8th, and early 9th centuries, control of the entire region was often contested by the Chinese <a href="/wiki/Tang_dynasty" title="Tang dynasty">Tang dynasty</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Tibetan_Empire" title="Tibetan Empire">Tibetan Empire</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uyghur_Empire" title="Uyghur Empire">Uyghur Empire</a>; cities frequently changed hands. Tibet seized Aksu in 670, but Tang forces reconquered the region in 692. Tibet regained the Tarim Basin in the late 720s, and the Tang dynasty again annexed the region in the 740s. The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Talas" title="Battle of Talas">Battle of Talas</a> led to the gradual withdrawal of Chinese forces, and the region was then contested between the Uyghurs and Tibetans.</p>
<p>Aksu was positioned on a junction of trade routes: the northern-Tarim route <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silk_road" title="Silk road">Silk road</a>, and the dangerous route north via the <a href="/wiki/Tian_Shan" title="Tian Shan">Tian Shan</a>'s <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Muzart_Pass&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Muzart Pass (page does not exist)">Muzart Pass</a> to the fertile <a href="/wiki/Ili_River" title="Ili River">Ili River</a> valley.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1207–08, they submitted to <a href="/wiki/Genghis_Khan" title="Genghis Khan">Genghis Khan</a>. Around 1220, Aksu became the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Mangalai" title="Kingdom of Mangalai">Kingdom of Mangalai</a>. The area had been part of the whole <a href="/wiki/Mongol_Empire" title="Mongol Empire">Mongol Empire</a> before it was occupied by the independent-minded <a href="/wiki/Chagatai_Khanate" title="Chagatai Khanate">Chagatai Khanate</a> under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_of_Ogedei" title="House of Ogedei">House of Ogedei</a> in 1286 from the hands of Kublai's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuan_Dynasty" title="Yuan Dynasty">Yuan Dynasty</a>. After the decline of the Yuan Dynasty in the mid-14th century and subsequently the Chagatai Khanate in the late 14th century, Aksu fell under the power of Turkic and Mongol warlords.</p>
<p>Along with most of <a href="/wiki/Xinjiang" title="Xinjiang">Xinjiang</a>, Aksu fell under the control of the <a href="/wiki/Khoja_(Turkestan)" title="Khoja (Turkestan)">Khojas</a>, and later that of <a href="/wiki/Yaqub_Beg" title="Yaqub Beg">Yaqub Beg</a>, during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dungan_revolt_(1862%E2%80%931877)" title="Dungan revolt (1862–1877)">Dungan Rebellion</a> of 1864–1877. Yakub Beg seized Aksu from <a href="/wiki/Hui_people" title="Hui people">Chinese Muslim</a> forces.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> After the defeat of the rebellion, a learned cleric named <a href="/wiki/Musa_Sayrami" title="Musa Sayrami">Musa Sayrami</a> (1836–1917), who had occupied positions of importance in Aksu under both rebel regimes, authored <i>Tārīkh-i amniyya</i> (History of Peace), which is considered by modern historians as one of the most important historical sources on the period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kim_xvi_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-kim_xvi-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a> officer <a href="/wiki/Francis_Younghusband" title="Francis Younghusband">Francis Younghusband</a> visited Aksu in 1887 on his overland journey from <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> to <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. He described it as being the largest town he had seen on his way from the Chinese capital, with a population of about 20,000, besides other inhabitants of the district and a garrison of about 2,000 soldiers. "There were large bazaars and several inns—some for travellers, others for merchants wishing to make a prolonged stay to sell goods."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Aksu" title="Battle of Aksu">Battle of Aksu</a> occurred here on May 31, 1933.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Isma%27il_Beg" title="Isma'il Beg">Isma'il Beg</a>, a Uighur, became the rebel Tao-yin of Aksu.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> After the outbreak of the <a href="/wiki/Ili_Rebellion" title="Ili Rebellion">Ili Rebellion</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ili_National_Army" title="Ili National Army">Ili National Army</a> forces led by <a href="/wiki/Abdulkerim_Abbas" title="Abdulkerim Abbas">Abdulkerim Abbas</a> attempting to take Aksu were repelled by <a href="/wiki/National_Revolutionary_Army" title="National Revolutionary Army">National Revolutionary Army</a> defenders commanded by Zhao Hanqi after two bitter sieges in September 1945.</p>
<p>Aksu was the site of a <a href="/wiki/2010_Aksu_bombing" title="2010 Aksu bombing">bombing in 2010</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aksu,_Xinjiang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aksu has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cold_desert_climate" title="Cold desert climate">cold desert climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> <i>BWk</i>) with extreme seasonal variation in temperature. The monthly 24-hour average temperature ranges from −7.8 °C (18.0 °F) in January to 23.8 °C (74.8 °F), and the annual mean is 10.25 °C (50.4 °F). Precipitation totals only 74 millimetres (2.9 in) annually, and mostly falls in summer, as compared to an annual evaporation rate of about 1,200 to 1,500 mm (47 to 59 in); there are about 2,800−3,000 hours of bright sunshine annually. The frost-free period averages 200−220 days.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aksu,_Xinjiang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although the Tarim Basin is largely dominated by the Uyghurs, there are many Han Chinese in Aksu due to the presence of <a href="/wiki/Xinjiang_Production_and_Construction_Corps" title="Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps">bingtuan</a> state farms here.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The Chinese government had encouraged migration to Xinjiang from the late 1950s and early 1960s onwards, and by 1998, Han Chinese formed the majority in the urban area of Aksu. The population was recorded as 498,937 in 1998, 41% of which are Uyghurs, and 58% Han Chinese.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 2000 census, a figure of 561,822 was recorded for the city's population. In the 2010 census figure, the population in the city of Aksu dropped slightly to 535,657.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The difference may be partly due to boundary changes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aksu,_Xinjiang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The county is served by the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Xinjiang_Railway" title="Southern Xinjiang Railway">Southern Xinjiang Railway</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Timeline">Timeline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aksu,_Xinjiang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Timeline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Before 600 the region was under control of Huns and Uyghur Turkic tribes.</li>
<li>630: <a href="/wiki/Xuanzang" title="Xuanzang">Xuanzang</a> visited the kingdom.</li>
<li>800: <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kuseen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kuseen (page does not exist)">Kuseen</a> Uyghur kingdom was controlling the region.</li>
<li>1000: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qarakhanids" title="Qarakhanids">Qarakhanids</a> Uyghur Empire started controlling the region.</li>
<li>1250: <a href="/wiki/Chagatai_Khanate" title="Chagatai Khanate">Chagatay</a> Mongol started controlling the region.</li>
<li>1500: <a href="/wiki/Yarkant_County" title="Yarkant County">Yarkant</a> Uyghur Empire started controlling the region.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Neighbours">Neighbours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aksu,_Xinjiang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Neighbours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The kingdom bordered <a href="/wiki/Kashgar" title="Kashgar">Kashgar</a> to the south-west, and <a href="/wiki/Kucha" title="Kucha">Kucha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karasahr" title="Karasahr">Karasahr</a> then <a href="/wiki/Turpan" title="Turpan">Turpan</a> to the east. Across the desert to the south was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khotan" title="Khotan">Khotan</a>.</p>
