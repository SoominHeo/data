<p><b>AccorHotels</b>, formerly known as Accor S.A., is a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> hotel group,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> part of the <a href="/wiki/CAC_40" title="CAC 40">CAC 40</a> index,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-3">[3]</a></sup> which operates in 94 countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Accor_Worldwide_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Accor_Worldwide-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, the group owns, operates and franchises 3,700 hotels (spanning all inhabited continents) representing several brands, from budget and economy lodgings to five-star hotels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Inbrief_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Inbrief-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AccorHotels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1967, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Dubrule" title="Paul Dubrule">Paul Dubrule</a> and Gérard Pélisson founded the Société d'investissement et d'exploitation hôteliers (SIEH) hotel group and opened the first Novotel hotel outside Lille in northern France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gerard_Pelisson_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gerard_Pelisson-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1974, they launched the Ibis brand with the opening of the Ibis <a href="/wiki/Bordeaux" title="Bordeaux">Bordeaux</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gerard_Pelisson_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gerard_Pelisson-6">[6]</a></sup> The following year, SIEH acquired the Courtepaille and Mercure brands, and in 1980 the Sofitel hotel brand, which then consisted of 43 hotels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gerard_Pelisson_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Gerard_Pelisson-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1982, the SIEH bought Jacques Borel International, the then world-leading brand offering restaurant vouchers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gerard_Pelisson_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-Gerard_Pelisson-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1983, the group, which had <a href="/wiki/Meal_voucher" title="Meal voucher">restaurant tickets</a> and hotels, changed its name to the Accor Group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gerard_Pelisson_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-Gerard_Pelisson-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1985, it launched <a href="/wiki/Hotel_Formule_1" title="Hotel Formule 1">Hotel Formule 1</a> brand, offering basic accommodation at low prices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-F1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-F1-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1990, it entered the North American market by acquiring <a href="/wiki/Motel_6" title="Motel 6">Motel 6</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Furlong_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Furlong-8">[8]</a></sup> and later the <a href="/wiki/Red_Roof_Inn" title="Red Roof Inn">Red Roof Inn</a> chain, which it later sold to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Starwood_Hotels_and_Resorts_Worldwide" title="Starwood Hotels and Resorts Worldwide">Starwood</a> and a consortium of <a href="/wiki/Citigroup" title="Citigroup">Citi’s</a> Global Special Situations Group and Westbridge Hospitality Fund, L.P.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-9">[9]</a></sup> respectively.</p>
<p>In the 1990s, it diversified to include Accor Casinos and in 2004, bought a nearly 30 per cent stake in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_M%C3%A9diterran%C3%A9e" title="Club Méditerranée">Club Méditerranée</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clark_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Clark-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2010, the shareholders of Accor approved the demerger of its hotel and voucher businesses. Accor Services became Edenred. The two entities started trading as separate companies on the Paris stock exchange from 2 July 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Breaking_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Breaking-11">[11]</a></sup> In 2011, Accor introduced its new brand positioning with the slogan: "Open new frontiers in hospitality".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2013, Accor redefined its group business model on two core competencies: hotel operator and brand franchisor (HotelServices) and hotel owner and investor (HotelInvest).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Strategy_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Strategy-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2014, Accor transferred management of its Central European operations to <a href="/wiki/Orbis_(Polish_travel_agency)" title="Orbis (Polish travel agency)">Orbis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In December 2014, Accor announced an alliance with Huazhu (China Lodging) to accelerate expansion in China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2015, Accor became AccorHotels and adopted a new slogan "Feel Welcome".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2015, Accor announced the purchase for US$2.9 billion in cash and shares of <a href="/wiki/FRHI_Hotels_%26_Resorts" title="FRHI Hotels &amp; Resorts">FRHI Hotels &amp; Resorts</a>, the owner of the <a href="/wiki/Fairmont_Hotels_and_Resorts" title="Fairmont Hotels and Resorts">Fairmont</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raffles_Hotels_%26_Resorts" title="Raffles Hotels &amp; Resorts">Raffles</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Swiss%C3%B4tel" title="Swissôtel">Swissôtel</a> chains. The transaction will add landmark properties such as the <a href="/wiki/Savoy_Hotel" title="Savoy Hotel">Savoy Hotel</a> in London, <a href="/wiki/Raffles_Hotel" title="Raffles Hotel">Raffles Hotel</a> in Singapore and the <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Hotel" title="Plaza Hotel">Plaza Hotel</a> in New York to Accor's luxury and high-end hotel portfolio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Women_at_Accor_Generation_.28WAAG.29">Women at Accor Generation (WAAG)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AccorHotels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Women at Accor Generation (WAAG)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2012, Accor launched its international women’s network aimed at helping the company to reach its gender parity goals: Women at Accor Generation (WAAG).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Accor has set a target for women to represent about 50% of its hotel managers worldwide, with an intermediary target of 35% by 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Management">Management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AccorHotels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2006, Gilles Pélisson, nephew of Accor co-founder Gerard Pélisson, took over the group as CEO, replacing former CEO Jean-Marc Espalioux.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Economist_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Economist-20">[20]</a></sup> Accor appointed Serge Weinberg, head of Weinberg Capital Partners, chairman of the supervisory board.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SupervisoryBoard_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-SupervisoryBoard-21">[21]</a></sup> In February 2009, CEO Gilles Pélisson was appointed chairman and CEO.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-P.C3.A9lisson_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-P.C3.A9lisson-22">[22]</a></sup> Gilles Pélisson was co-vice-president of the <a href="/wiki/Novotel" title="Novotel">Novotel</a> brand in 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Caterer_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Caterer-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, <a href="/wiki/Denis_Hennequin" title="Denis Hennequin">Denis Hennequin</a> joined the group’s board of directors and replaced Gilles Pélisson as CEO in January 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> He stepped down in April 2013 and Yann Caillère was appointed CEO for a transitional period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Press-Board_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Press-Board-25">[25]</a></sup> In August 2013, Sebastien Bazin was named Chairman and CEO, replacing Yann Caillère who left the group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bazin_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bazin-26">[26]</a></sup></p>

<p>The company's head office, which houses the company's executive management, is located in the ' Immeuble Odyssey in the <a href="/wiki/13th_arrondissement_of_Paris" title="13th arrondissement of Paris">13th arrondissement</a> of <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HQ_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-HQ-27">[27]</a></sup> This facility is the company's <a href="/wiki/Registered_office" title="Registered office">registered office</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-legal_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-legal-28">[28]</a></sup> The seven storey, 14,000-square-metre (150,000 sq ft) building was designed by British architect <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norman_Foster" title="Norman Foster">Norman Foster</a> and features glass plates in its façade. Géraldine Doutriaux of <i><a href="/wiki/Le_Parisien" title="Le Parisien">Le Parisien</a></i> called it "[u]n bel immeuble lumineux" ("a beautiful, bright building").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>The company's other major office facility,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HQ_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-HQ-27">[27]</a></sup> which used to serve as its registered office,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-legal_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-legal-28">[28]</a></sup> is located in <a href="/wiki/Courcouronnes" title="Courcouronnes">Courcouronnes</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Auguy_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Auguy-30">[30]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Essonne" title="Essonne">Essonne</a>, near <a href="/wiki/%C3%89vry,_Essonne" title="Évry, Essonne">Évry</a>, France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HQ_27-2"><a href="#cite_note-HQ-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Tour_Montparnasse" title="Tour Montparnasse">Tour Maine-Montparnasse</a> in Paris' <a href="/wiki/15th_arrondissement_of_Paris" title="15th arrondissement of Paris">15th arrondissement</a> used to house the executive management of Accor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy_brands"><span id="Economy"></span>Economy brands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AccorHotels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy brands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In August 2012, Accor announced its economy brands would be converted into "the ibis family",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibis_2012_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-ibis_2012-32">[32]</a></sup> including three complementary brands - Ibis, Ibis Budget (formerly Etap Hotel) and Ibis Styles (formerly All Seasons). All Ibis Family brands (corporately styled "ibis") feature the SweetBed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bedding_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bedding-33">[33]</a></sup> In 2014, the brand launched its new restaurant concept: Ibis Kitchen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Over1700_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-Over1700-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hotel_F1" title="Hotel F1">Hotel F1</a></b> is a chain of 238 "no frills" budget hotels in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. Guestrooms are equipped with a wash corner and each floor offers shared bathroom and shower facilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HotelF1_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-HotelF1-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Ibis_Budget" title="Ibis Budget">Ibis Budget</a></b> <span style="color:#000080"><i>(blue logo)</i></span> has 541 hotels in 17 countries. The full network uses the brand’s "cocoon concept", awarded Best Interior Design at the 2008 European Design Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibis_budget_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-ibis_budget-36">[36]</a></sup> In 2012 Ibis Budget was created from the re-branding of former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Etap_Hotels" title="Etap Hotels">Etap Hotels</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hotel_Formule_1" title="Hotel Formule 1">Formule 1</a> hotels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibis_etn_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-ibis_etn-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Ibis_Styles" title="Ibis Styles">Ibis Styles</a></b> <span style="color:#008000"><i>(green logo)</i></span> has 293 hotels in 25 countries. In 2012 Ibis Styles was established with the re-branding of the former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_Seasons" title="All Seasons">All Seasons</a> properties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibis_etn_37-1"><a href="#cite_note-ibis_etn-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibisStyles_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-ibisStyles-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hotel_Ibis" title="Hotel Ibis">Ibis</a></b> <span style="color:#800000"><i>(red logo)</i></span> represents Accor's largest portfolio of hotels and resorts with 1,047 hotels in 61 countries. From 1997, Ibis was the first economy hotel brand to be quality certified ISO 9001, an international standard outlining its commitment to service quality. 85% of the network is ISO 9001 certified.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibis_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-ibis-39">[39]</a></sup> Ibis is a standard brand equivalent to two- to three-star hotels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibis_etn_37-2"><a href="#cite_note-ibis_etn-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_economy_and_budget_hotels">Former economy and budget hotels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AccorHotels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Former economy and budget hotels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Red_Roof_Inn" title="Red Roof Inn">Red Roof Inn</a></b></p>
<p>Accor acquired Red Roof Inn in 1999 for $1.115 billion, increasing its presence in North America. In April 2007, Accor sold the majority of its interests in Red Roof Inn to Citigroup Global Special Situations Group and Westbridge Hospitality Fund LP for $1.3 billion. Accor retained some hotels for rebranding into its Motel 6 brand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Motel_6" title="Motel 6">Motel 6</a></b> and <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_6" title="Studio 6">Studio 6</a></b></p>
<p>Accor purchased Motel 6 in 1990. In October 2012, Accor finalized the sale of its 1,102 US Motel 6 and Studio 6 hotels to Blackstone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Midscale_brands"><span id="Midscale"></span>Midscale brands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AccorHotels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Midscale brands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mercure_Hotels" title="Mercure Hotels">Mercure</a></b> is the largest of Accor's midscale brands found internationally with 732 hotels and resorts in 55 countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mercure_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mercure-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Novotel" title="Novotel">Novotel</a></b> has 414 full-service hotels and resorts in 61 countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Novotel_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-Novotel-42">[42]</a></sup> <b>Suite Novotel</b> has 31 all-suite hotels in 10 countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Adagio_(hotel)" title="Adagio (hotel)">Adagio</a></b> offers 96 'ready-to-live' apartment-style accommodations in 11 countries suitable for medium and long-stay business guests.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adagio_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-Adagio-44">[44]</a></sup> The Adagio <a href="/wiki/Apartment_hotel" title="Apartment hotel">aparthotel</a> (apartment hotel) brand is divided into two segments with upscale called Adagio city apartments and midscale called Adagio Access city apartments. Adagio is a joint partnership between Accor hotels and <a href="/wiki/Pierre_%26_Vacances" title="Pierre &amp; Vacances">Pierre &amp; Vacances</a> tourism.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Upscale_brands"><span id="Upscale"></span>Upscale brands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AccorHotels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Upscale brands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Mercure" title="Grand Mercure">Grand Mercure</a></b> is a full-service hotel and resort brand representing 15 hotels and apartment hotels in 8 countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grand_Mercure_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-Grand_Mercure-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Grand Mercure Apartments</b> cater to long-stay travellers with rates that depend on length of stay. The apartments have kitchen and <a href="/wiki/Self-service_laundry" title="Self-service laundry">laundrette</a> facilities. There are 15 apartments in 4 countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grand_Mercure_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-Grand_Mercure-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mei_Jue" title="Mei Jue">Mei Jue</a></b> is a Chinese adaptation of the Grand Mercure brand. It is designed for travellers in China who want an upscale hotel brand. There are 14 hotels and more than 65 new developments scheduled for 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grand_Mercure_45-2"><a href="#cite_note-Grand_Mercure-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Maha Cipta</b> is the Indonesian adaptation of Grand Mercure. It has two hotels in Indonesia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grand_Mercure_45-3"><a href="#cite_note-Grand_Mercure-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>The Sebel</b> is a brand of premium apartments. There are 21 properties in Australia and New Zealand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Sebel_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Sebel-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mama_Shelter" title="Mama Shelter">Mama Shelter</a></b> is a design-oriented lifestyle chain of boutique hotels. (The chief designer of Mama Shelter hotels is world-renowned <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Starck" title="Philippe Starck">Philippe Starck</a>.) Accor acquired a 35% stake in Mama Shelter in October 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mama_is_getting_Bigger_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mama_is_getting_Bigger-47">[47]</a></sup> In April 2015, Mama Shelter had six hotels in <a href="/wiki/Bordeaux" title="Bordeaux">Bordeaux</a>, <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lyon" title="Lyon">Lyon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marseille" title="Marseille">Marseille</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>. The chain’s five-year plan includes properties in: Amsterdam, Barcelona, Lille (France), London, Mexico City, New York City, Seoul, and a second Parisian hotel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mama_is_getting_Bigger_47-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mama_is_getting_Bigger-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Pullman_Hotels_and_Resorts" title="Pullman Hotels and Resorts">Pullman</a></b> has 99 upscale hotels and resorts in 28 countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pullman_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pullman-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MGallery" title="MGallery">MGallery</a></b> is a collection of 77 high-end <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boutique_hotels" title="Boutique hotels">boutique hotels</a> in 23 countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mgallery_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mgallery-49">[49]</a></sup> The brand's official Ambassador is actress <a href="/wiki/Kristin_Scott_Thomas" title="Kristin Scott Thomas">Kristin Scott Thomas</a>.</p>
<p><b>Swissotel Hotels</b> are soon to be part of Accor's upscale brand due to AccorHotels &amp; FRHI merger. Until the merger transaction us finalized in 2016, FRHI and AccorHotels will continue to operate its brands as separate companies and manage their business affairs as usual.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-swissotel.com_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-swissotel.com-50">[50]</a></sup></p>

<p><b><a href="/wiki/Sofitel" title="Sofitel">Sofitel</a></b> is Accor's luxury hotel and resort brand with 121 hotels in 41 countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sofitel_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sofitel-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>SO Sofitel</b> are designer hotels in <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bel_Ombre,_Mauritius" title="Bel Ombre, Mauritius">Bel Ombre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a> and <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>. Each SO is designed by a famous fashion designer: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kenzo_Takada" title="Kenzo Takada">Kenzo Takada</a> designed the SO Sofitel Mauritius, <a href="/wiki/Christian_Lacroix" title="Christian Lacroix">Christian Lacroix</a> designed the SO Sofitel Bangkok and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Isabelle_Miaja&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Isabelle Miaja (page does not exist)">Isabelle Miaja</a> designed the SO Sofitel Singapore.</p>
<p><b>Sofitel Legend</b> is a chain of hotels in five countries. The hotels are usually listed as historic monuments. These include Sofitel Legend Metropole in <a href="/wiki/Hanoi" title="Hanoi">Hanoi</a> (<a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>), Sofitel Legend The Grand Amsterdam (<a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>), Sofitel Old Cataract in <a href="/wiki/Aswan" title="Aswan">Aswan</a> (<a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>), Sofitel Cartagena Santa Clara (<a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>), and Sofitel People’s Grand Hotel of <a href="/wiki/Xi%27an" title="Xi'an">Xi'an</a> (<a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sofitel_51-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sofitel-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Raffles Hotels</b> and <b>Fairmont Hotels</b> are soon to be part of Accor's ultra-luxury brand due to AccorHotels &amp; FRHI merger. Until the merger transaction is finalized in 2016, FRHI and AccorHotels will continue to operate its brands as separate companies and manage their business affairs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-swissotel.com_50-1"><a href="#cite_note-swissotel.com-50">[50]</a></sup></p>

<p>In November 2011, Accor attained exclusive marketing rights to <a href="/wiki/Mirvac" title="Mirvac">Mirvac</a> Hotels &amp; Resorts, greatly expanding Accor's presence in Australia with 48 new hotels and resorts.</p>
<p>Though some Mirvac brands - Sebel, Quay West, Citigate and Sea Temple - will remain under their existing brands, others became part of Accor's up-scale and luxury hotel and resort brands. As a result, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MGallery" title="MGallery">MGallery</a> and Pullman brands expanded their presence in Australia.</p>
<p>Notable rebrandings:</p>
<ul>
<li>Pullman and Mercure Brisbane King George Square - formerly <i>The Sebel and Citigate King George Square Brisbane</i></li>
<li>Pullman Resort Bunker Bay Margaret River - formerly Quay West Resort Bunker Bay.</li>
<li>Pullman Cairns International - formerly the Sebel Cairns Resort<i>.</i></li>
<li>Pullman Palm Cove Sea Temple Resort and Spa - formerly Sea Temple Palm Cove Resort.</li>
<li>Pullman Sydney Hyde Park - formerly Sydney Marriott Hotel Hyde Park.</li>
<li>Pullman Quay Grand Sydney Harbour - formerly Quay Grand Suites Sydney.</li>
<li>Pullman Port Douglas Sea Temple Resort and Spa - formerly Sea Temple Port Douglas Resort.</li>
</ul>
<p>Three former Mirvac hotels joined Accor's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MGallery" title="MGallery">MGallery</a> Collection due to their <a href="/wiki/Boutique_hotel" title="Boutique hotel">boutique hotel</a> status and/or heritage listing:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Harbour_Rocks_Hotel" title="Harbour Rocks Hotel">Harbour Rocks Hotel Sydney</a></li>
<li>Hotel Lindrum Melbourne</li>
<li>The Como Melbourne</li>
</ul>
