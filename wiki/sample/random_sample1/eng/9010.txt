<p><b>Acochlidiacea</b>, common name <b>acochlidians</b>, are a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taxonomic" title="Taxonomic">taxonomic</a> clade of very unusual <a href="/wiki/Sea_snail" title="Sea snail">sea snails</a> and sea and freshwater <a href="/wiki/Slug" title="Slug">slugs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aquatic_animal" title="Aquatic animal">aquatic</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gastropod" title="Gastropod">gastropod</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mollusk" title="Mollusk">mollusks</a> within the large clade <a href="/wiki/Heterobranchia" title="Heterobranchia">Heterobranchia</a>. <b>Acochlidia</b> is a variant spelling.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acochlidiacea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These are mostly very small animals, without a <a href="/wiki/Mollusc_shell" title="Mollusc shell">shell</a> or <a href="/wiki/Gill" title="Gill">gills</a>, distinguished by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viscera" title="Viscera">visceral</a> mass being sharply set off from the rest of the body.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IZ_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-IZ-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Being a small group with only 30 species worldwide known in 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and 32 species described in 2011,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neusser_2011_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Neusser_2011-7">[7]</a></sup> and 33 in 2012 (+9 undescribed <i><a href="/wiki/Pontohedyle" title="Pontohedyle">Pontohedyle</a></i> species),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2012_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2012-2">[2]</a></sup> these slugs are morphologically and biologically highly aberrant and diverse, comprising a series of unusual characters (e.g. secondary <a href="/wiki/Gonochorism" title="Gonochorism">gonochorism</a>, lack of copulatory organs, asymmetric <a href="/wiki/Radula" title="Radula">radulae</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup> Most acochlidians live interstitially in marine sands, while some have conquered limnic systems (uniquely within opisthobranch gastropods).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acochlidiacea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Nils_Hjalmar_Odhner" title="Nils Hjalmar Odhner">Nils Hjalmar Odhner</a> established this taxon as a family in 1937, when he created the families <a href="/wiki/Microhedylidae" title="Microhedylidae">Microhedylidae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Acochlidiidae" title="Acochlidiidae">Acochlidiidae</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Odhner_1937_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Odhner_1937-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bouchet_2005_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bouchet_2005-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1939, he treated this taxon as an order.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bouchet_2005_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Bouchet_2005-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rankin (1979)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rankin_1979_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rankin_1979-10">[10]</a></sup> treated this taxon as an order, the order Acochlidioidea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bouchet_2005_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Bouchet_2005-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gofas_2010_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gofas_2010-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Salvini-Plawen (1983)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> wrote this taxon as Acochlidiomorpha.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gofas_2010_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Gofas_2010-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Anderson (1992)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> treated this taxon as the order Acochlidiida.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bouchet_2005_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-Bouchet_2005-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gofas_2010_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Gofas_2010-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Burn in Beesley et al. (1998), wrote this taxon as the order Acochlidea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gofas_2010_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-Gofas_2010-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wawra (1987)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and various authors (2007–2010)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schr.C3.B6dl_2010_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Schr.C3.B6dl_2010-16">[16]</a></sup> spelled this taxon as Acochlidia.</p>
<p>Three families (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hedylopsidae" title="Hedylopsidae">Hedylopsidae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Microhedylidae" title="Microhedylidae">Microhedylidae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Acochlidiidae" title="Acochlidiidae">Acochlidiidae</a>) are classically recognized.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bouchet_2005_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-Bouchet_2005-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gofas_2010_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-Gofas_2010-4">[4]</a></sup> Two controversial classifications (Rankin 1979,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rankin_1979_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rankin_1979-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Yaroslav_Starobogatov" title="Yaroslav Starobogatov">Starobogatov</a> 1983)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Starobogatov_1983_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Starobogatov_1983-17">[17]</a></sup> have been proposed recently, but they have not been evaluated since.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bouchet_2005_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-Bouchet_2005-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gofas_2010_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-Gofas_2010-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>An alternative classification by Burn (in Beesley et al., 1998) for the Australian species recognizes 2 superfamilies and 5 families.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bouchet_2005_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-Bouchet_2005-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gofas_2010_4-7"><a href="#cite_note-Gofas_2010-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>The Acochlidia, a traditional "order" of the Opisthobranchia since their establishment by Odhner have formed one of the unsolved mysteries within <a href="/wiki/Euthyneura" title="Euthyneura">Euthyneura</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup> Their <a href="/wiki/Monophyly" title="Monophyly">monophyly</a> is widely accepted especially since a proposed sister group relationship of the acochlidian family <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ganitidae" title="Ganitidae">Ganitidae</a> with <a href="/wiki/Sacoglossa" title="Sacoglossa">Sacoglossa</a> (based on the dagger-shaped radula teeth) could be rejected based on a comprehensive <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parsimony_analysis" title="Parsimony analysis">parsimony analysis</a> of morphological characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup> During the last years a series of studies have redescribed key acochlidian taxa in great detail, including <a href="/wiki/3D_reconstruction" title="3D reconstruction">3D reconstructions</a>, and added considerably to the morphological and biological knowledge of this previously little understood group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-5"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Most recent morphological analyses suggested a common origin with either the equally enigmatic <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rhodopemorpha&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rhodopemorpha (page does not exist)">Rhodopemorpha</a>, the diaphanid cephalaspidean <i><a href="/wiki/Toledonia" title="Toledonia">Toledonia</a></i>, or with runcinid or philinoid <a href="/wiki/Cephalaspidea" title="Cephalaspidea">cephalaspideans</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-6"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup> Molecular markers independent from direct ecological pressures suggested an unresolved basal opisthobranch origin for Acochlidia (based on nuclear 18S rRNA and 28S rRNA) (Vonnemann et al. 2005).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-7"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> A first combined multi-gene dataset led to the surprising result of Acochlidia clustering in a pulmonate relationship, united in a clade with <a href="/wiki/Pyramidelloidea" title="Pyramidelloidea">Pyramidelloidea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amphiboloidea" title="Amphiboloidea">Amphiboloidea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eupulmonata" title="Eupulmonata">Eupulmonata</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-8"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup> However, only three derived acochlids were included into analysis prior to 2010, with partially missing data.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-9"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005_taxonomy">2005 taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acochlidiacea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2005 taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_taxonomy">2010 taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acochlidiacea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010 taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A first comprehensive <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cladistic_analysis" title="Cladistic analysis">cladistic analysis</a> of their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phylogeny" title="Phylogeny">phylogeny</a> has been established by Schrödl &amp; Neusser (2010),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schr.C3.B6dl_2010_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-Schr.C3.B6dl_2010-16">[16]</a></sup> but the identity of their sister group remained uncertain. Morphology-based analyses by Schrödl &amp; Neusser,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schr.C3.B6dl_2010_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-Schr.C3.B6dl_2010-16">[16]</a></sup> demonstrated that Acochlidia usually group with other <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mesopsammic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mesopsammic (page does not exist)">mesopsammic</a> (they live in interstitial spaces of marine sands) taxa, if any were included (i.e. with the sacoglossan <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Platyhedyle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Platyhedyle (page does not exist)">Platyhedyle</a></i>, the rhodopemorph <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rhodope_(gastropod)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rhodope (gastropod) (page does not exist)">Rhodope</a></i> or the cephalaspideans <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Philinoglossa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Philinoglossa (page does not exist)">Philinoglossa</a></i> or <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Philine_exigua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Philine exigua (page does not exist)">Philine exigua</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-10"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup> Thus, it is likely that <a href="/wiki/Convergent_evolution" title="Convergent evolution">convergent</a> adaptations to the interstitial habitat mask the truly phylogenetic signals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-11"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Schrödl &amp; Neusser (2010)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schr.C3.B6dl_2010_16-3"><a href="#cite_note-Schr.C3.B6dl_2010-16">[16]</a></sup> split Acochlidiacea into two (unranked) taxa and into six families like this:</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Hedylopsacea">Hedylopsacea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acochlidiacea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Hedylopsacea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Hedylopsacean Acochlidiacea, whose evolution involves several habitat shifts from marine interstitial to amphibious or freshwater benthic habitats, possess complex <a href="/wiki/Excretory_system_of_gastropods" title="Excretory system of gastropods">excretory</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reproductive_system_of_gastropods" title="Reproductive system of gastropods">reproductive systems</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2012_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2012-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>(unranked) Hedylopsacea has no superfamilies defined:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schr.C3.B6dl_2010_16-4"><a href="#cite_note-Schr.C3.B6dl_2010-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Acochlidiidae" title="Acochlidiidae">Acochlidiidae</a>: <i><a href="/wiki/Acochlidium" title="Acochlidium">Acochlidium</a></i>, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Palliohedyle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Palliohedyle (page does not exist)">Palliohedyle</a></i>, including <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Strubellia" title="Strubellia">Strubellia</a></i></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pseudunelidae" title="Pseudunelidae">Pseudunelidae</a>: with the only genus <i><a href="/wiki/Pseudunela" title="Pseudunela">Pseudunela</a></i></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hedylopsidae" title="Hedylopsidae">Hedylopsidae</a>: with the only genus <i><a href="/wiki/Hedylopsis" title="Hedylopsis">Hedylopsis</a></i></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tantulidae" title="Tantulidae">Tantulidae</a>: with the only species <i><a href="/wiki/Tantulum_elegans" title="Tantulum elegans">Tantulum elegans</a></i></li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Microhedylacea">Microhedylacea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acochlidiacea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Microhedylacea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Microhedylacean Acochlidiacea are exclusively found in interstitial spaces in sediment, and show a tendency toward reduction of complexity in major organ systems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2012_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2012-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>(unranked) Microhedylacea has no superfamilies defined:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schr.C3.B6dl_2010_16-5"><a href="#cite_note-Schr.C3.B6dl_2010-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asperspinidae" title="Asperspinidae">Asperspinidae</a>: with the only genus <i><a href="/wiki/Asperspina" title="Asperspina">Asperspina</a></i> – junior synonym: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minicheviellidae" title="Minicheviellidae">Minicheviellidae</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Microhedylidae" title="Microhedylidae">Microhedylidae</a> s.l.: <i><a href="/wiki/Pontohedyle" title="Pontohedyle">Pontohedyle</a></i>, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Parhedyle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Parhedyle (page does not exist)">Parhedyle</a></i>, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Microhedyle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Microhedyle (page does not exist)">Microhedyle</a></i> – including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ganitidae" title="Ganitidae">Ganitidae</a>: <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ganitus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ganitus (page does not exist)">Ganitus</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paraganitus" title="Paraganitus">Paraganitus</a></i>. Inclusion of Ganitidae within Microhedylidae requires further research and higher statistical support.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schr.C3.B6dl_2010_16-6"><a href="#cite_note-Schr.C3.B6dl_2010-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>A multi-locus molecular study by Jörger et al. (2010),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-12"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup> included six out of seven acochlidian families.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-13"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup> It confirmed Acochlidiacea in a pulmonate relationship, as sister to Eupulmonata.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-14"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup> Euthyneura, Opisthobranchia and Pulmonata as traditionally defined were found non-monophyletic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-15"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup> The enigmatic amphibious and insectivorous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aitengidae" title="Aitengidae">Aitengidae</a> clusters within Acochlidiacea, as sister to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meiofauna" title="Meiofauna">meiofaunal</a> and brackish Pseudunelidae and limnic Acochlidiidae.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-16"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup> Inclusion of this small acochlidian group resulted in redefinition of major groups within <a href="/wiki/Heterobranchia" title="Heterobranchia">Heterobranchia</a>, that has led to creation of the new clades <a href="/wiki/Euopisthobranchia" title="Euopisthobranchia">Euopisthobranchia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Panpulmonata" title="Panpulmonata">Panpulmonata</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-17"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Phylogeny">Phylogeny</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acochlidiacea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Phylogeny">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There is no fossil record of Acochlidiacea. Application of a <a href="/wiki/Molecular_clock" title="Molecular clock">molecular clock</a> allowed estimation of divergence times for these groups. The split between <a href="/wiki/Eupulmonata" title="Eupulmonata">Eupulmonata</a> and Acochlidiacea took place in the <a href="/wiki/Mesozoic" title="Mesozoic">Mesozoic</a>, between the Triassic and Jurassic periods. The diversification of Acochlidia is estimated to have happened in the Jurassic with the split between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hedylopsacea" title="Hedylopsacea">Hedylopsacea</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Microhedylacea" title="Microhedylacea">Microhedylacea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2010_8-18"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2010-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acochlidiacea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The life cycle of Acochlidiacea is poorly known. With a typically low reproductive output in Acochlidiacea (max. of 40 eggs in <i><a href="/wiki/Pontohedyle_milaschewitchii" title="Pontohedyle milaschewitchii">Pontohedyle milaschewitchii</a></i>), free <a href="/wiki/Veliger" title="Veliger">veliger</a> larvae are assumed to stay in the interstices of the sand grains rather than entering the water column thereby avoiding long distance <a href="/wiki/Biological_dispersal" title="Biological dispersal">dispersal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2012_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2012-2">[2]</a></sup> Fertilized eggs are attached to sand grains and might promote dispersal via current driven sediment transport along shorelines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J.C3.B6rger_2012_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-J.C3.B6rger_2012-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
