<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Computing_and_gaming">Computing and gaming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Computing and gaming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/ARC_(file_format)" title="ARC (file format)">ARC (file format)</a>, a file name extension for archive files</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Arc_(programming_language)" title="Arc (programming language)">Arc (programming language)</a>, a Lisp dialect designed by Paul Graham</li>
<li><code>arc</code>, the command-line interface for <a href="/wiki/ArcInfo" title="ArcInfo">ArcInfo</a></li>
<li>ARC International, a developer of configurable microprocessor technology now owned by <a href="/wiki/Synopsys#ARC_International" title="Synopsys">Synopsys</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Arc_System_Works" title="Arc System Works">Arc System Works</a>, a video game developer</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Automatic_Reference_Counting" title="Automatic Reference Counting">Automatic Reference Counting</a>, is a memory management enhancement to Objective-C and Swift programming where the burden of keeping track of an object's reference count is lifted from the programmer to the compiler.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Adaptive_replacement_cache" title="Adaptive replacement cache">Adaptive replacement cache</a>, a memory replacement algorithm for better performance</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Google_App_Runtime_for_Chrome" title="Google App Runtime for Chrome">Google App Runtime for Chrome</a>, a compatibility layer and sandboxing technology to run Android applications on Chromebook computers</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Engineering">Engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Engineering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Arc or <a href="/wiki/Arch" title="Arch">arch</a>, a bowed or curved structure capable of spanning a space while supporting significant weight</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arc_chute" title="Arc chute">Arc chute</a>, a protective device in an electrical circuit breaker</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Arc_lamp" title="Arc lamp">Arc lamp</a>, a device that produces light by an electric arc</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Arc_welding" title="Arc welding">Arc welding</a>, a method of welding that uses an electric arc to melt the metals together</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Electric_arc" title="Electric arc">Electric arc</a> or voltaic arc, an ongoing plasma discharge (an electric current through a gas), producing light and heat</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Optics">Optics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Optics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Infralateral_arc" title="Infralateral arc">Infralateral arc</a>, a rare halo, an optical phenomenon appearing similar to a rainbow under a white parhelic circle</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lower_tangent_arc" title="Lower tangent arc">Lower tangent arc</a>, rarely observable halo, an optical phenomenon appearing under and tangent to a 22° halo centred on the sun</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/120%C2%B0_parhelion" title="120° parhelion">120° parhelion</a>, relatively rare halo, an optical phenomenon occasionally appearing along with very bright sun dogs</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Parry_arc" title="Parry arc">Parry arc</a>, a rare halo, an optical phenomenon which occasionally appears over a 22° halo together with an upper tangent arc</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Subhelic_arc" title="Subhelic arc">Subhelic arc</a>, a rare halo that curves upwards from the horizon and touches the tricker arc above the anthelic point</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Supralateral_arc" title="Supralateral arc">Supralateral arc</a>, a rare halo, an optical phenomenon often confused with the indeed infrequently appearing 46° halo</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Upper_tangent_arc" title="Upper tangent arc">Upper tangent arc</a>, halo, an atmospheric optical phenomenon which appears over and tangent to the 22° halo centred on the sun</li>
</ul>
