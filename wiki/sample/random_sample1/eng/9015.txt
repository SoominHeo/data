<p><b>TUI Airlines Netherlands</b> (formerly <i>Arkefly</i> and shortened to <i>Arke</i> in the corporate design to match the travel agency)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charter_airline" title="Charter airline">charter airline</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Schiphol-Rijk" title="Schiphol-Rijk">Schiphol-Rijk</a> on the grounds of <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_Airport_Schiphol" title="Amsterdam Airport Schiphol">Amsterdam Airport Schiphol</a> in <a href="/wiki/Haarlemmermeer" title="Haarlemmermeer">Haarlemmermeer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It is the charter carrier of the Dutch arm of the German travel conglomerate <a href="/wiki/TUI_Group" title="TUI Group">TUI Group</a> and its main base is Schiphol Airport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TUI_Airlines_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ArkeFly_office_Schiphol-Rijk.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2000" data-file-width="3008" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/ArkeFly_office_Schiphol-Rijk.jpg/220px-ArkeFly_office_Schiphol-Rijk.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/ArkeFly_office_Schiphol-Rijk.jpg/330px-ArkeFly_office_Schiphol-Rijk.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/ArkeFly_office_Schiphol-Rijk.jpg/440px-ArkeFly_office_Schiphol-Rijk.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>TUI Airlines Netherlands traces its roots to <a href="/wiki/Air_Holland" title="Air Holland">Air Holland</a>, which was founded in 1981. After financial problems Air Holland was taken over by the Exel Aviation Group and took a new start as <a href="/wiki/HollandExel" title="HollandExel">HollandExel</a> in March 2004. In May 2005 the Exel Aviation Group was declared bankrupt. The German <a href="/wiki/TUI_Group" title="TUI Group">TUI Group</a> took over the airline's activities and renamed it ArkeFly. It is now wholly owned by the TUI Group. Its name was based on the biggest Dutch tour operator, Arke, which is also a subsidiary of the TUI Group. The newly reorganised airline operated its first flight in September 2005.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Operations in <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Curaçao">Curaçao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_Antilles" title="Netherlands Antilles">Netherlands Antilles</a>, began on 15 July 2004 as <a href="/wiki/DutchCaribbeanExel" title="DutchCaribbeanExel">DutchCaribbeanExel</a>, which was originally part of the Exel Aviation Group, but was later taken over, together with parent airline, <a href="/wiki/HollandExel" title="HollandExel">HollandExel</a>, by the TUI Group, and renamed ArkeFly Curaçao.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FI_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FI-4">[4]</a></sup> ArkeFly started weekly flights to St. Maarten from Amsterdam on 2 December 2007, but discontinued this service in November 2008. TUI holds that flights may resume if the volume of tourists travelling to St. Maarten increases.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Operations to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orlando" title="Orlando">Orlando</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a> and <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> began in 2011.</p>
<p>In October 2013, Arkefly changed its marketing name to <i>Arke</i> to reflect the partnership with the travel agency of the same name.</p>
<p>On 13 May 2015, it was announced by the TUI Group that all five of TUI's airline subsidiaries will be named TUI, whilst keeping their separate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air_Operator%27s_Certificate" title="Air Operator's Certificate">Air Operator's Certificate</a>, taking over three years to complete. Arke was the first to undergo the change, and was renamed TUI on 1 October 2015 - therefore changing the airline's name also.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Destinations">Destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TUI_Airlines_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Destinations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/TUI_Airlines_Netherlands_destinations" title="TUI Airlines Netherlands destinations">TUI Airlines Netherlands destinations</a></div>
<p>TUI Airlines Netherlands carries out regular and chartered flights, although most of the chartered flights are operated for the Dutch tour operator TUI Netherlands. It operates to destinations in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Caribbean" title="Dutch Caribbean">Dutch Caribbean</a>.</p>
