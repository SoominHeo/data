<p><b>Prince Hisahito of Akishino</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">悠仁親王</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Hisahito Shinnō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born 6 September 2006)</span> is the youngest child and only son of <a href="/wiki/Fumihito,_Prince_Akishino" title="Fumihito, Prince Akishino">Fumihito, Prince Akishino</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kiko,_Princess_Akishino" title="Kiko, Princess Akishino">Kiko, Princess Akishino</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He is the youngest grandchild of <a href="/wiki/Akihito" title="Akihito">Emperor Akihito</a> and <a href="/wiki/Empress_Michiko" title="Empress Michiko">Empress Michiko</a>. Hisahito is <a href="/wiki/Line_of_succession_to_the_Japanese_throne" title="Line of succession to the Japanese throne">third in line</a> to become <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Japan" title="Emperor of Japan">Emperor of Japan</a>, after his <a href="/wiki/Naruhito,_Crown_Prince_of_Japan" title="Naruhito, Crown Prince of Japan">uncle, Naruhito</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Fumihito,_Prince_Akishino" title="Fumihito, Prince Akishino">father, Fumihito</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_Hisahito_of_Akishino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His personal name <b>Hisahito</b> in this case means "serene and virtuous", according to the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Household_Agency" title="Imperial Household Agency">Imperial Household Agency</a>. An alternative translation is "virtuous, calm, everlasting". His name was chosen by his father, and the Akishino family crest used to mark his belongings is <i>koyamaki</i> (<a href="/wiki/Sciadopitys" title="Sciadopitys">Japanese umbrella-pine</a>) tree.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education_and_family">Education and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_Hisahito_of_Akishino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prince Hisahito is the only son and youngest child of Fumihito, Prince Akishino and Kiko, Princess Akishino. He has two older sisters, <a href="/wiki/Princess_Mako_of_Akishino" title="Princess Mako of Akishino">Princess Mako of Akishino</a> and <a href="/wiki/Princess_Kako_of_Akishino" title="Princess Kako of Akishino">Princess Kako of Akishino</a>.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Prince_Hisahito_of_Akishino_Newspaper_2006-9-12_02.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Prince_Hisahito_of_Akishino_Newspaper_2006-9-12_02.jpg/220px-Prince_Hisahito_of_Akishino_Newspaper_2006-9-12_02.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Prince_Hisahito_of_Akishino_Newspaper_2006-9-12_02.jpg/330px-Prince_Hisahito_of_Akishino_Newspaper_2006-9-12_02.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Prince_Hisahito_of_Akishino_Newspaper_2006-9-12_02.jpg/440px-Prince_Hisahito_of_Akishino_Newspaper_2006-9-12_02.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Prince_Hisahito_of_Akishino_Newspaper_2006-9-12_02.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Japanese newspapers announcing the birth of the third in line</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Hisahito was the first male child born to the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_House_of_Japan" title="Imperial House of Japan">Imperial House of Japan</a> since his father in 1965. In January 2007, the Prime Minister of Japan, <a href="/wiki/Shinz%C5%8D_Abe" title="Shinzō Abe">Shinzō Abe</a>, announced that he would drop an earlier proposal to alter the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Household_Law" title="Imperial Household Law">Imperial Household Law</a> so as to allow females to inherit the throne. The proposal had been made on the basis of the fact that the two sons of Emperor <a href="/wiki/Akihito" title="Akihito">Akihito</a> had, at the time, no sons of their own. Given Hisahito's birth, it now seems increasingly unlikely that the laws will be changed to allow Hisahito's cousin, <a href="/wiki/Aiko,_Princess_Toshi" title="Aiko, Princess Toshi">Princess Aiko</a>, the daughter and only child of the heir apparent to the throne, <a href="/wiki/Naruhito,_Crown_Prince_of_Japan" title="Naruhito, Crown Prince of Japan">Crown Prince Naruhito</a>, to become a reigning Empress and thus end the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_succession_controversy" title="Japanese succession controversy">Japanese succession controversy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The supporters of changes criticized the current law as it placed a burden on the few aging males old enough to perform royal duties as females left the family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hisahito celebrated his first birthday amid the glare of cameras at the imperial palace. Newspapers published front-page pictures of the smiling prince standing on a table in pale blue <a href="/wiki/Romper_suit" title="Romper suit">rompers</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Household_Agency" title="Imperial Household Agency">Imperial Household Agency</a> stated his weight as 9285 grams (20.4 pounds) and his height as 75 centimetres (30 inches).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On his third birthday, he weighed 13.6 kilograms and was 94 centimetres tall. He was shown riding a tricycle, and later in traditional dress. The Imperial Household Agency told press that he enjoys lively activities, like riding a tricycle given by his elder sister Princess Kako, playing ball and chasing, and had gradually become able to eat and change clothes by himself. His parents called him "Yuyu", "Yu-chan" and "Hisahito-kun".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the spring of 2010, Prince Hisahito began kindergarten at a school affiliated with <a href="/wiki/Ochanomizu_University" title="Ochanomizu University">Ochanomizu University</a> in Tokyo; on 14 December 2012, the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Household_Agency" title="Imperial Household Agency">Imperial Household Agency</a> announced he would enter Ochanomizu University Elementary School in April 2013 so he could be with many of his friends from kindergarten. The prince thus became the first member of the Imperial House of Japan to receive his education at a school other than <a href="/wiki/Gakush%C5%ABin" title="Gakushūin">Gakushūin</a> Primary School, which is also in Tokyo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> By his second year, he was reported to be doing well at school, helping to look after first-year pupils and playing with his friends.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
