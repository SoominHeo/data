<div class="hatnote" role="note">This name uses <a href="/wiki/Spanish_naming_customs" title="Spanish naming customs">Spanish naming customs</a>: the first or paternal <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">family name</a> is <i>López</i> and the second or maternal family name is <i>Roa</i>.</div>
<p><b>Aquilino López Roa</b> (born April 21, 1975) is a former <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> player who played in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> and the <a href="/wiki/KBO_League" title="KBO League">KBO League</a>. He bats and throws right-handed.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aquilino_L%C3%B3pez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lopez was first signed by the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Mariners" title="Seattle Mariners">Seattle Mariners</a> on July 3, 1997 and made his Major League debut on April 2, 2003 after being selected by the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Blue_Jays" title="Toronto Blue Jays">Toronto Blue Jays</a> on December 16, 2002 as a <a href="/wiki/Rule_5_draft" title="Rule 5 draft">Rule 5 draftee</a>. He made 72 appearances in 2003 for the Blue Jays, recording 14 saves and posting a 3.42 ERA over <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap">73<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>2</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings.</p>
<p>In 2004, Lopez was still in Toronto where he pitched only 21 innings in 18 appearances. He was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rockies" title="Colorado Rockies">Colorado Rockies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Phillies" title="Philadelphia Phillies">Philadelphia Phillies</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Padres" title="San Diego Padres">San Diego Padres</a> organizations from 2005–2006, mostly as a <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">Minor League</a> player. Lopez signed with the <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Tigers" title="Detroit Tigers">Detroit Tigers</a> before the 2007 season.</p>
<p>In 2008, after spending most of the season with the Tigers, Lopez was sent down to Toledo when <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Rodney" title="Fernando Rodney">Fernando Rodney</a> was activated from the <a href="/wiki/Disabled_list" title="Disabled list">disabled list</a>. Lopez was called up to Detroit July 2 when <a href="/wiki/Zach_Miner" title="Zach Miner">Zach Miner</a> was sent to Toledo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Although his ERA on the season was a comparatively low 3.55, he allowed 50.9% of inherited runners to score, the second-highest in the American League. Lopez became a free agent after the 2008 season when the Tigers declined to offer arbitration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lopez was signed by the <a href="/wiki/Kia_Tigers" title="Kia Tigers">Kia Tigers</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Korea_Baseball_Organization" title="Korea Baseball Organization">Korea Baseball Organization</a> on January 14, <a href="/wiki/2009_in_baseball" title="2009 in baseball">2009</a>. In the 2009 KBO season, he went 14–5 with a 3.12 ERA and 129 strikeouts, leading the league in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Win_(baseball)" title="Win (baseball)">wins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Innings_pitched" title="Innings pitched">innings pitched</a> (190.1). Lopez won <a href="/wiki/2009_Korean_Series#Game_1" title="2009 Korean Series">Game 1</a> and <a href="/wiki/2009_Korean_Series#Game_5" title="2009 Korean Series">Game 5</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2009_Korean_Series" title="2009 Korean Series">2009 Korean Series</a> and recorded two outs in the eighth inning of the seventh game, which Kia won on a walk-off home run in the ninth. The Game 5 win was a complete game shutout. On December 11, 2009, it was announced that Lopez was awarded the KBO Golden Glove Award for pitcher.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> His final season in Korea was 2012 when he started 5 games for the <a href="/wiki/SK_Wyverns" title="SK Wyverns">SK Wyverns</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pitching_style">Pitching style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aquilino_L%C3%B3pez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pitching style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
