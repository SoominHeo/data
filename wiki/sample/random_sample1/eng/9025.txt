<p>The <b>Atacama Giant</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>Gigante de Atacama</i></span>) is a large anthropomorphic <a href="/wiki/Geoglyph" title="Geoglyph">geoglyph</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Atacama_Desert" title="Atacama Desert">Atacama Desert</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Atacama_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Atacama-1">[1]</a></sup> Located at "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cerro_Unitas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cerro Unitas (page does not exist)">Cerro Unitas</a>", this is the largest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prehistoric" title="Prehistoric">prehistoric</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anthropomorphic" title="Anthropomorphic">anthropomorphic</a> figure in the world with a height of 119 metres (390 ft) and represents a deity for the local inhabitants from AD 1000 to 1400.</p>
<p>The figure was an early <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astronomical_calendar" title="Astronomical calendar">astronomical calendar</a> for knowing where the moon would set; by knowing this the day, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Crop_cycle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Crop cycle (page does not exist)">crop cycle</a>, and season could be determined. The points on the top and side of the head would say what season it would be depending on their alignment with the moon, which was very important in determining when the rainy season would come in the barren Atacama.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geoglyphs_of_Chile">Geoglyphs of Chile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Atacama_Giant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geoglyphs of Chile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Atacama Giant is one out of nearly 5,000 <a href="/wiki/Geoglyph" title="Geoglyph">geoglyphs</a> - ancient artwork that are drawn into the landscape - that have been discovered in the Atacama in the last three decades.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Geoglyph_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Geoglyph-2">[2]</a></sup> Although geoglyphs have always been the subject of wild conjectures and bizarre theories, it is now widely believed that they are the work of several successive cultures that dwelt in this region of South America, including the <a href="/wiki/Tiwanaku" title="Tiwanaku">Tiwanaku</a> and <a href="/wiki/Inca_Empire" title="Inca Empire">Inca</a>.</p>
