<p><i><b>Momordica cymbalaria</b></i> (Hook., Fenzl ex Naud.) is a <a href="/wiki/Vine" title="Vine">vine</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Momordica" title="Momordica">Momordica</a></i> genus found in the <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> states of <a href="/wiki/Andhra_Pradesh" title="Andhra Pradesh">Andhra Pradesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karnataka" title="Karnataka">Karnataka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madhya_Pradesh" title="Madhya Pradesh">Madhya Pradesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maharashtra" title="Maharashtra">Maharashtra</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tamil_Nadu" title="Tamil Nadu">Tamil Nadu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dist_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-dist-1">[1]</a></sup> It is used in the local <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Folk_medicine" title="Folk medicine">folk medicine</a> as an abortifacient and for the treatment of <a href="/wiki/Diabetes_mellitus" title="Diabetes mellitus">diabetes mellitus</a>. It is a relative of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bitter_melon" title="Bitter melon">bitter melon</a> plant (<i>M. charantia</i>) which is also used against diabetes. The plant has also been named <i><b>Luffa tuberosa</b></i> (Roxb.) or <i><b>Momordica tuberosa</b></i> (Roxb.)</p>
<p>Pharmacological studies indicate possible action of extracts of the plant on several medical conditions. The water extract was reported to have <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hypoglycaemia" title="Hypoglycaemia">hypoglycemic</a> activity in diabetic rabbits but not in normal rabbits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-raobk_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-raobk-2">[2]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Ethanol" title="Ethanol">ethanol</a> extract was reported to protect rats from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isoproterenol" title="Isoproterenol">isoproterenol</a>-induced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Infarct" title="Infarct">myocardial injury</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-raju_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-raju-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
