<p><b>Madrid Atocha</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>Estación de Madrid Atocha</i></span>, also named <i><b>Madrid Puerta de Atocha</b></i>) is the largest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_station" title="Railway station">railway station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>. It is the primary station serving <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commuter_train" title="Commuter train">commuter trains</a> (<a href="/wiki/Cercan%C3%ADas_Madrid" title="Cercanías Madrid"><i>Cercanías</i></a>), intercity and regional trains from the south, and the <a href="/wiki/AVE" title="AVE">AVE</a> <a href="/wiki/High-speed_rail" title="High-speed rail">high speed trains</a> from <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> (<a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>), <a href="/wiki/Zaragoza" title="Zaragoza">Zaragoza</a> (<a href="/wiki/Aragon" title="Aragon">Aragon</a>), <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a> (<a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">Andalusia</a>) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valencia,_Spain" title="Valencia, Spain">Valencia</a> (<a href="/wiki/Levante,_Spain" title="Levante, Spain">Levante Region</a>). These train services are run by the Spanish national rail company, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renfe" title="Renfe">Renfe</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Madrid_Atocha_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station is in the <a href="/wiki/Atocha_(Madrid)" title="Atocha (Madrid)">Atocha</a> neighborhood of the district of <a href="/wiki/Arganzuela" title="Arganzuela">Arganzuela</a>. The original façade faces the <a href="/wiki/Plaza_del_Emperador_Carlos_V" title="Plaza del Emperador Carlos V">Plaza del Emperador Carlos V</a>, a site at which a variety of streets converge, including the Calle de Atocha, Paseo del Prado, Paseo de la Infanta Isabel, Avenida de la Ciudad de Barcelona, Calle de Méndez Álvaro, Paseo de las Delicias, Paseo de Santa María de la Cabeza, and Ronda de Atocha.</p>
<p>The Atocha station is really a railway complex, formed by the Madrid Atocha Cercanías and Madrid Puerta de Atocha stations of the Spanish national railways and a station of the Madrid underground called Atocha RENFE.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Madrid_Atocha_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>At this site, Madrid's first railway station was inaugurated on 9 February 1851 under the name <i>Estación de Mediodía</i> (Atocha-Mediodía is now the name of an area of the <a href="/wiki/Arganzuela" title="Arganzuela">Arganzuela</a> district, and means <i>south</i> in old Spanish).</p>
<p>After the building was largely destroyed by fire, it was rebuilt and reopened in 1892. The architect for the replacement, in a <a href="/wiki/Wrought_iron" title="Wrought iron">wrought iron</a> renewal style was <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Palacio" title="Alberto Palacio">Alberto de Palacio Elissagne</a>, who collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Gustave_Eiffel" title="Gustave Eiffel">Gustave Eiffel</a>. Engineer Henry Saint James also took part in the project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The name Atocha has become attached to the station because of the nearby basilica dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Basilica_of_Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_Atocha" title="Basilica of Nuestra Señora de Atocha">Our Lady of Atocha</a>. The train platforms were partly covered by a roof in the form of inverted hull with a height of approximately 27 meters and length of 157 meters. The steel and glass roof spreads between two brick flanking buildings.</p>
<p>This complex of railway tracks expanded through the years. In 1985, a project of complete remodeling began, based on designs by <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Moneo" title="Rafael Moneo">Rafael Moneo</a>. In 1992, the original building was taken out of service as a terminal, and converted into a concourse with shops, cafés, and a nightclub. Like the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orsay_Museum" title="Orsay Museum">Orsay Museum</a> in Paris, the concourse has been given a new function, this time a stunning 4,000 m<sup>2</sup> (43,056 sq ft) covered tropical garden.</p>
<p>A modern terminal was also designed by <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Moneo" title="Rafael Moneo">Moneo</a>, and built in adjacent land to serve both the new AVE trains and local commuter lines. The main lines end in the new terminal; commuter train platforms are located underground, at the ingress to a rail tunnel extending northward under the Paseo de la Castellana. The station is served by two <a href="/wiki/Madrid_Metro" title="Madrid Metro">Madrid Metro</a> <a href="/wiki/Metro_station" title="Metro station">stations</a>, <i>Atocha</i> and <i>Atocha Renfe</i>. The latter was added when the new terminal building was constructed and is directly linked to the railway station.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="11-M_Train_Bombings_of_2004">11-M Train Bombings of 2004</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Madrid_Atocha_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 11-M Train Bombings of 2004">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/11_March_2004_Madrid_train_bombings" title="11 March 2004 Madrid train bombings">11 March 2004 Madrid train bombings</a></div>
<p>On 11 March 2004, packed arriving commuter trains were bombed in a series of coordinated bombings, killing 191 people and wounding 1,800. The official investigation by the Spanish Judiciary determined the attacks were directed by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terrorist_cell" title="Terrorist cell">terrorist cell</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Memorials_to_the_2004_Attack">Memorials to the 2004 Attack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Madrid_Atocha_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Memorials to the 2004 Attack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Interior_del_Monumento_a_las_Victimas_del_11-M_en_Madrid.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2592" data-file-width="3888" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Interior_del_Monumento_a_las_Victimas_del_11-M_en_Madrid.jpg/220px-Interior_del_Monumento_a_las_Victimas_del_11-M_en_Madrid.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Interior_del_Monumento_a_las_Victimas_del_11-M_en_Madrid.jpg/330px-Interior_del_Monumento_a_las_Victimas_del_11-M_en_Madrid.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Interior_del_Monumento_a_las_Victimas_del_11-M_en_Madrid.jpg/440px-Interior_del_Monumento_a_las_Victimas_del_11-M_en_Madrid.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Interior_del_Monumento_a_las_Victimas_del_11-M_en_Madrid.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Interior of the <a href="/wiki/Atocha_station_memorial" title="Atocha station memorial">Atocha station memorial</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>On 10 June 2004, a somber and minimalist <a href="/wiki/Atocha_station_memorial" title="Atocha station memorial">Atocha station memorial</a> was dedicated for the victims of the Attack. The monument includes a <a href="#External_links">virtual shrine</a>. Visitors to the attacked stations can leave a <a href="/wiki/Hand" title="Hand">hand</a> silhouette and a message through special-purpose consoles. A second monument to this event, known as 11-M in Spain, is the <i><span lang="es" xml:lang="es">Bosque del Recuerdo</span></i> ("the Forest of Remembrance") in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parque_del_Buen_Retiro" title="Parque del Buen Retiro">Parque del Buen Retiro</a> near Atocha. This monument is made up of 192 olive and cypress trees, one for each person who died on that day, with a tree also planted in remembrance of the police officer who died on 3 April 2004, along with seven of the perpetrators whose capture was underway. Initially inaugurated as the <i><span lang="es" xml:lang="es">Bosque de los Ausentes</span></i> ("<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forest_of_the_Departed" title="Forest of the Departed">Forest of the Departed</a>"), on the first anniversary of the devastating attack, on 11 March 2005, the site was renamed the following year. The forest is surrounded by a stream, with water as the symbol of life.</p>
