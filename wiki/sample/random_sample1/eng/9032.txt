<p><b>Arthur Ira</b> "<b>Art</b>" <b>Garfunkel</b> (born November 5, 1941) is an American singer, poet and actor. He is best known for his partnership in his earlier years with <a href="/wiki/Paul_Simon" title="Paul Simon">Paul Simon</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Folk_rock" title="Folk rock">folk rock</a> duo <a href="/wiki/Simon_%26_Garfunkel" title="Simon &amp; Garfunkel">Simon &amp; Garfunkel</a>.</p>
<p>Highlights of his solo music career include a top 10 hit, three top 20 hits, six top 40 hits, 14 <a href="/wiki/Adult_contemporary_music" title="Adult contemporary music">Adult Contemporary</a> top 30 singles, five Adult Contemporary number ones, two UK number ones and a <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Choice_Awards" title="People's Choice Awards">People's Choice Award</a>. Through his solo and collaborative work, Garfunkel has earned six <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammys</a>, including the Lifetime Achievement Award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grammy_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Grammy-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1990, he and Simon were inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll_Hall_of_Fame" title="Rock and Roll Hall of Fame">Rock and Roll Hall of Fame</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_Garfunkel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_Garfunkel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Garfunkel was born in <a href="/wiki/Forest_Hills,_Queens" title="Forest Hills, Queens">Forest Hills, Queens</a>, New York City, the son of Rose (née Pearlman) and Jacob "Jack" Garfunkel, a traveling salesman. Art is a middle child with two brothers; the older named Jules and the younger named Jerome. Jacob's parents had originally immigrated to the United States at the turn of the century, and chose to settle in Manhattan. Before his career in sales, Jacob worked as an actor in <a href="/wiki/Dayton,_Ohio" title="Dayton, Ohio">Dayton, Ohio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Garfunkel is Jewish;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> his paternal grandparents emigrated from <a href="/wiki/Ia%C8%99i" title="Iași">Iași</a> in Romania. His maternal cousin was <a href="/wiki/Lou_Pearlman" title="Lou Pearlman">Lou Pearlman</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> founder of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%27N_Sync" title="'N Sync">'N Sync</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Backstreet_Boys" title="Backstreet Boys">Backstreet Boys</a>.</p>
<p>According to the <i><a href="/wiki/Across_America_(album)" title="Across America (album)">Across America</a></i> DVD, Garfunkel's love for singing originated in the first grade. "When we were lined up in size order, and after everyone else had left, I'd stay behind and enjoy the echo sound of the stairwell tiles and sing '<a href="/wiki/Unchained_Melody" title="Unchained Melody">Unchained Melody</a>' and '<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/You%27ll_Never_Walk_Alone_(song)" title="You'll Never Walk Alone (song)">You'll Never Walk Alone</a>', learning to love this goose-bumps song from the tender age of five." Later, Garfunkel's father bought him a <a href="/wiki/Wire_recording" title="Wire recording">wire recorder</a> and from then on, Garfunkel spent his afternoons singing, recording, and playing it back, so he could listen for flaws and learn how to improve.</p>
<p>At his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bar_Mitzvah" title="Bar Mitzvah">Bar Mitzvah</a> in 1954, Garfunkel sang as a <a href="/wiki/Hazzan" title="Hazzan">cantor</a> and performed over four hours of his repertoire for his family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> As a young teen, Garfunkel was struck with a lung infection, leading to a love for basketball. He explained in a 1998 Interview: "In the summer of '55, I had a lung infection. I couldn't run around, but I loved basketball and there was a hoop nearby. Much of the summer I spent methodically hitting 96, 98 foul shots out of 100. Then 102! I never played on a team after Junior High School. Just 3 against 3, half court pick up games in the schoolyard."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artgarfunkel.com_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-artgarfunkel.com-11">[11]</a></sup> He met his future singing partner <a href="/wiki/Paul_Simon" title="Paul Simon">Paul Simon</a> in the sixth grade at PS 164, when they were both cast in the elementary school graduation play, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alice_in_Wonderland" title="Alice in Wonderland">Alice in Wonderland</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> It has been said by Garfunkel that Simon first became interested in singing after hearing Garfunkel sing a rendition of <a href="/wiki/Nat_King_Cole" title="Nat King Cole">Nat King Cole</a>'s "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Too_Young_(popular_song)" title="Too Young (popular song)">Too Young</a>" in a school talent show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Between 1956 and 1962, the two performed together as "Tom &amp; Jerry", occasionally performing at school dances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-simonandgarfunkel.com_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-simonandgarfunkel.com-14">[14]</a></sup> Their idols were <a href="/wiki/The_Everly_Brothers" title="The Everly Brothers">The Everly Brothers</a>, whom they imitated in their use of close two-part <a href="/wiki/Vocal_harmony" title="Vocal harmony">vocal harmony</a>. In 1957, Simon &amp; Garfunkel recorded the song "Hey, Schoolgirl" under the name Tom &amp; Jerry, a moniker coined by their label Big Records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-simonandgarfunkel.com_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-simonandgarfunkel.com-14">[14]</a></sup> The single reached forty-ninth on the pop charts. Garfunkel ("Tom Graph") chose his nickname because he liked to track, or "graph" hits, on the pop charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> He also released some singles as a solo artist under the name Artie Garr, a shortened version of his name. In interviews, Garfunkel has noted how these early singles distinguished him as a folk-styled crooner,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> with songs like "Beat Love" and "Dream Alone" (both released 1959).</p>
<p>After graduating from <a href="/wiki/Forest_Hills_High_School_(New_York)" title="Forest Hills High School (New York)">Forest Hills High School</a> alongside Simon, Garfunkel studied Architecture at <a href="/wiki/Columbia_College,_Columbia_University" title="Columbia College, Columbia University">Columbia College, Columbia University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> in the early 1960s, where he was a brother in the <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Epsilon_Pi" title="Alpha Epsilon Pi">Alpha Epsilon Pi</a> <a href="/wiki/Fraternities_and_sororities" title="Fraternities and sororities">fraternity</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Garfunkel was a team member in tennis, skiing, fencing, and bowling at the college.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artgarfunkel.com_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-artgarfunkel.com-11">[11]</a></sup> He was also a member of the all-male <a href="/wiki/A_cappella" title="A cappella">a cappella</a> group on campus, the Columbia Kingsmen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> After initially majoring in architecture, Garfunkel earned a B.A. in <a href="/wiki/History_of_art" title="History of art">art history</a> in 1962<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> or 1965,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> followed by a M.A. in mathematics from Columbia in 1967. Garfunkel also completed coursework toward a doctorate in <a href="/wiki/Mathematics_education" title="Mathematics education">mathematics education</a> at <a href="/wiki/Teachers_College,_Columbia_University" title="Teachers College, Columbia University">Teachers College, Columbia University</a> during the peak of Simon &amp; Garfunkel's commercial success.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Herman.2C_January_1977_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Herman.2C_January_1977-21">[21]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 1963, Garfunkel and Simon (who graduated from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queens_College" title="Queens College">Queens College</a> before dropping out of <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Law_School" title="Brooklyn Law School">Brooklyn Law School</a>) reformed their duo under their own names as "Simon and Garfunkel". They released their first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Wednesday_Morning,_3_A.M." title="Wednesday Morning, 3 A.M.">Wednesday Morning, 3 A.M.</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a> in October 1964.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> It was not a critical success, and the duo subsequently split again. The next year, producer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tom_Wilson_(producer)" title="Tom Wilson (producer)">Tom Wilson</a> lifted the song "<a href="/wiki/The_Sound_of_Silence" title="The Sound of Silence">The Sound of Silence</a>" from the record, dubbed an electric backing onto it,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wednesday_Morning.2C_3_A.M._23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wednesday_Morning.2C_3_A.M.-23">[23]</a></sup> and released it as a single that went to number one on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_magazine" title="Billboard magazine">Billboard</a> pop charts.</p>
<p>Simon had gone to England in 1965 after the initial failure of <i>Wednesday Morning, 3 A.M.</i>, pursuing a solo career. He teamed with songwriter <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Woodley" title="Bruce Woodley">Bruce Woodley</a>, of the band <a href="/wiki/The_Seekers" title="The Seekers">The Seekers</a>. But he returned to the US to reunite with Garfunkel after "The Sounds of Silence" had started to enjoy commercial success, and the duo went on to become one of the most popular acts of the 1960s. Together they recorded four more influential albums, <i><a href="/wiki/Sounds_of_Silence" title="Sounds of Silence">Sounds of Silence</a>;</i> <i><a href="/wiki/Parsley,_Sage,_Rosemary_and_Thyme" title="Parsley, Sage, Rosemary and Thyme">Parsley, Sage, Rosemary and Thyme</a>;</i> <i><a href="/wiki/Bookends_(album)" title="Bookends (album)">Bookends</a>;</i> and the hugely successful <i><a href="/wiki/Bridge_over_Troubled_Water" title="Bridge over Troubled Water">Bridge over Troubled Water</a></i>. Simon &amp; Garfunkel also contributed extensively to the soundtrack of the 1967 <a href="/wiki/Mike_Nichols" title="Mike Nichols">Mike Nichols</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Graduate" title="The Graduate">The Graduate</a></i> (starring <a href="/wiki/Dustin_Hoffman" title="Dustin Hoffman">Dustin Hoffman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anne_Bancroft" title="Anne Bancroft">Anne Bancroft</a>). While writing "<a href="/wiki/Mrs._Robinson" title="Mrs. Robinson">Mrs. Robinson</a>", Simon originally toyed with the title "Mrs. Roosevelt".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mrs._Roosevelt_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mrs._Roosevelt-24">[24]</a></sup> When Garfunkel reported this indecision over the song's name to the director, Nichols replied, "Don't be ridiculous! We're making a movie here! It's Mrs. Robinson!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Simon &amp; Garfunkel returned to England in the fall of 1968 and made a concert appearance at Kraft Hall which was broadcast on the BBC and also featured Garfunkel's solo performance of "<a href="/wiki/For_Emily,_Whenever_I_May_Find_Her" title="For Emily, Whenever I May Find Her">For Emily, Whenever I May Find Her</a>", which received a standing ovation.</p>

<p>While Garfunkel was not a songwriter <i>per se</i>, he did write the poem "Canticle" as a re-write of Simon's "Side of A Hill" from his debut album, for "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scarborough_Fair/Canticle" title="Scarborough Fair/Canticle">Scarborough Fair/Canticle</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Side_of_a_hill_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Side_of_a_hill-26">[26]</a></sup> He also worked as the vocal arranger for the duo, working out who the songs would be sung by and how each song was produced. He is also credited as having written the arrangement on "<a href="/wiki/The_Boxer" title="The Boxer">The Boxer</a>" and creating the Audio montage, "Voices of the Old People" on "<a href="/wiki/Bookends_(song)" title="Bookends (song)">Bookends</a>". Citing personal differences and divergence in career interests, they split following the release of their most critically acclaimed album, <i><a href="/wiki/Bridge_over_Troubled_Water" title="Bridge over Troubled Water">Bridge over Troubled Water</a></i>, in 1970.</p>
<p>Both Simon and Garfunkel pursued solo projects after 1970. Occasionally they would reunite, as in 1975 for their Top Ten single "<a href="/wiki/My_Little_Town" title="My Little Town">My Little Town</a>", which Simon originally wrote for Garfunkel, claiming Garfunkel's solo output was lacking "bite." The song was included on their respective solo albums: Paul Simon's <i><a href="/wiki/Still_Crazy_After_All_These_Years" title="Still Crazy After All These Years">Still Crazy After All These Years</a></i> and Garfunkel's <i><a href="/wiki/Breakaway_(Art_Garfunkel_album)" title="Breakaway (Art Garfunkel album)">Breakaway</a></i>. Contrary to popular belief, the song is not autobiographical of Simon's early life in New York City but of Garfunkel's childhood in Queens.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In 1981, they got together again for the famous <a href="/wiki/The_Concert_in_Central_Park" title="The Concert in Central Park">concert in Central Park</a>, followed by a world tour and an aborted reunion album <i>Think Too Much</i>, which was eventually released (without Garfunkel) as <i><a href="/wiki/Hearts_and_Bones" title="Hearts and Bones">Hearts and Bones</a></i>.</p>
<p>Together, they were inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll_Hall_of_Fame" title="Rock and Roll Hall of Fame">Rock and Roll Hall of Fame</a> in 1990.</p>
<p>In 2003, the two reunited again when they received a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Lifetime_Achievement_Award" title="Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award">Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award</a>. This reunion led to a US tour: the acclaimed "Old Friends" concert series. It was followed by a 2004 international encore, which culminated in a free concert at the <a href="/wiki/Colosseum" title="Colosseum">Colosseum</a> in Rome. The final concert drew 600,000 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1970.E2.80.931975:_Hiatus_and_first_album">1970–1975: Hiatus and first album</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_Garfunkel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1970–1975: Hiatus and first album">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During a three-year hiatus after Simon &amp; Garfunkel's break-up, Garfunkel starred in two Mike Nichols films, <i><a href="/wiki/Catch-22_(film)" title="Catch-22 (film)">Catch-22</a></i> (1970) and <i><a href="/wiki/Carnal_Knowledge" title="Carnal Knowledge">Carnal Knowledge</a></i> (1971). He also spent late 1971 to early 1972 working as a mathematics teacher teaching high school sophomores geometry at a short lived Litchfield Private School <b>Litchfield Academy</b> in Connecticut (by request of his fiancée Linda Marie Grossman).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artgarfunkel.com_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-artgarfunkel.com-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late 1972, with Simon &amp; Garfunkel having released their <i><a href="/wiki/Simon_and_Garfunkel%27s_Greatest_Hits" title="Simon and Garfunkel's Greatest Hits">Greatest Hits</a></i> album and briefly reuniting to perform a benefit concert for presidential candidate <a href="/wiki/George_McGovern" title="George McGovern">George McGovern</a>, Garfunkel felt ready to return to his musical career. His first album was 1973's <i><a href="/wiki/Angel_Clare" title="Angel Clare">Angel Clare</a></i>, which contained "<a href="/wiki/All_I_Know" title="All I Know">All I Know</a>" along with "I Shall Sing" and "Travelling Boy" as singles. The album was received with mixed reviews, reaching no. 5 in the U.S. In 1974, Garfunkel released the hit record "Second Avenue".</p>
<p>On his next album, 1975's <i><a href="/wiki/Breakaway_(Art_Garfunkel_album)" title="Breakaway (Art Garfunkel album)">Breakaway</a></i>, Garfunkel briefly reunited with Paul Simon for the 1975 hit "<a href="/wiki/My_Little_Town" title="My Little Town">My Little Town</a>". The album also included the singles "Breakaway" (B-Side: "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney_Girls" title="Disney Girls">Disney Girls</a>") and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I_Only_Have_Eyes_For_You" title="I Only Have Eyes For You">I Only Have Eyes For You</a>" (a 1934 song written by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Warren" title="Harry Warren">Harry Warren</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> which is noted as Garfunkel's first UK number one.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1976.E2.80.931979:_Diversity_and_disaster">1976–1979: Diversity and disaster</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_Garfunkel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1976–1979: Diversity and disaster">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1976, Garfunkel recorded both background and duet vocals for several artists, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stephen_Bishop_(musician)" title="Stephen Bishop (musician)">Stephen Bishop</a>'s <i>Careless</i> album, <a href="/wiki/James_Taylor" title="James Taylor">James Taylor</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Pocket_(James_Taylor_album)" title="In the Pocket (James Taylor album)">In The Pocket</a></i> album and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J.D._Souther" title="J.D. Souther">J.D. Souther</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Rose_(J._D._Souther_album)" title="Black Rose (J. D. Souther album)">Black Rose</a></i> album. From December 1976 to September 1977, Garfunkel worked on his next album.</p>
<p>Garfunkel's next release was the 1978 album <i><a href="/wiki/Watermark_(Art_Garfunkel_album)" title="Watermark (Art Garfunkel album)">Watermark</a></i> (US #19, UK #26). It failed to make an impression on the public upon release. Its main single, "Crying in My Sleep" ("Mr. Shuck 'N' Jive") (UK #25) didn't reach the US Top 40. After a two-month hiatus, it was re-released in January 1978, with Garfunkel's cover of <a href="/wiki/Sam_Cooke" title="Sam Cooke">Sam Cooke</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Wonderful_World_(Sam_Cooke_song)" title="Wonderful World (Sam Cooke song)">(What a) Wonderful World</a>" (B-Side: "Wooden Planes"), reaching number one on the Adult Contemporary chart and seventeen on the pop chart. Paul Simon and mutual friend <a href="/wiki/James_Taylor" title="James Taylor">James Taylor</a> had contributed backing vocals to the song, making it a huge hit on the US A.C. charts. In 1978, Garfunkel toured the U.S. and Canada extensively with noted guitarist <a href="/wiki/Arlen_Roth" title="Arlen Roth">Arlen Roth</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Barlow_Jarvis" title="John Barlow Jarvis">John Barlow Jarvis</a> on piano, and <a href="/wiki/Leah_Kunkel" title="Leah Kunkel">Leah Kunkel</a> on second vocals.</p>
<p>Garfunkel's last release of the 70s was the 1979 album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fate_For_Breakfast" title="Fate For Breakfast">Fate For Breakfast</a></i> (US #67, UK #2). It was his first US flop. The album's first single, "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=In_A_Little_While_(I%27ll_Be_on_My_Way)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="In A Little While (I'll Be on My Way) (page does not exist)">In A Little While (I'll Be on My Way)</a>" (B-Side: "And I Know") (US AC #12) failed to break the top forty, as did his second single, "<a href="/wiki/Since_I_Don%27t_Have_You" title="Since I Don't Have You">Since I Don't Have You</a>" (B-Side: "When Someone Doesn't Want You") (US #53, US AC #5, UK #38). The album was a huge success in the UK, scoring a number one hit with "<a href="/wiki/Bright_Eyes_(Art_Garfunkel_song)" title="Bright Eyes (Art Garfunkel song)">Bright Eyes</a>" (B-Side: "Sail on a Rainbow") (US AC #29, UK #1) (a song written by <a href="/wiki/Mike_Batt" title="Mike Batt">Mike Batt</a>). A version of "Bright Eyes" also appeared in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Watership_Down_(film)" title="Watership Down (film)">Watership Down</a></i>.</p>
<p>Garfunkel's longtime girlfriend, <a href="/wiki/Laurie_Bird" title="Laurie Bird">Laurie Bird</a>, committed suicide in June 1979 at their Manhattan apartment, three months after the album's release in March. Garfunkel later admitted that the incident left him in a deep depression for most of the 1980s, hence the lack of musical output during that time.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1980.E2.80.931995:_Depression_and_disappearance">1980–1995: Depression and disappearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_Garfunkel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 1980–1995: Depression and disappearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Garfunkel's next album was a low point in his career. The 1981 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Scissors_Cut" title="Scissors Cut">Scissors Cut</a></i> (US #113, UK #51) (dedicated to Bird), contained three singles, "A Heart in New York" (B-Side: "Is This Love") (US #66, US AC #10), "Scissors Cut," and "Hang On In." The latter two failed to chart.</p>
<p>Following disappointing sales of <i>Scissors Cut</i>, Garfunkel reunited with Simon for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Concert_in_Central_Park" title="The Concert in Central Park">The Concert in Central Park</a></i> and a world tour. They had significant disagreements during the tour. In 1984, <i>Stereo Review</i> Magazine reported that Simon mixed out Garfunkel's voice from a new album. It was initially slated to be a Simon &amp; Garfunkel studio reunion, but was ultimately released as a Simon solo album (<i><a href="/wiki/Hearts_and_Bones" title="Hearts and Bones">Hearts and Bones</a></i>). In 1986, Garfunkel played the part of the butcher on the <a href="/wiki/Mike_Batt" title="Mike Batt">Mike Batt</a> <a href="/wiki/Concept_album" title="Concept album">concept album</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunting_of_the_Snark_(musical)" title="The Hunting of the Snark (musical)">The Hunting of the Snark</a></i>.</p>
<p>Garfunkel released his first compilation album in 1984, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Art_Garfunkel_Album" title="The Art Garfunkel Album">The Art Garfunkel Album</a></i> (UK #12), never released in the US,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> which contained the minor hit "Sometimes When I'm Dreaming" (UK #77, US AC #25).</p>
<p>Garfunkel again left the music scene when his father died, leading Garfunkel further into depression. In the fall of 1985, he met his future wife, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kathryn_Garfunkel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kathryn Garfunkel (page does not exist)">Kathryn (Kim) Cermack</a>. They were married in September 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bio_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-32">[32]</a></sup> Garfunkel's retirement lasted a full seven years<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, until his 1988 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Lefty_(album)" title="Lefty (album)">Lefty</a></i> (US, #134), which produced three singles, "<a href="/wiki/So_Much_in_Love" title="So Much in Love">So Much in Love</a>" (US #76 AC #11), "When A Man Loves A Woman", and "This Is The Moment".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1996.E2.80.932006:_Resurgence">1996–2006: Resurgence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_Garfunkel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 1996–2006: Resurgence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Garfunkel's live 1996 concert <i><a href="/wiki/Across_America_(album)" title="Across America (album)">Across America</a></i> (UK #35), recorded at the registry hall on <a href="/wiki/Ellis_Island" title="Ellis Island">Ellis Island</a>, featured musical guests <a href="/wiki/James_Taylor" title="James Taylor">James Taylor</a>, Garfunkel's wife, Kim, and his son James.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>Garfunkel performed the theme song for the 1991 television series, <i><a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Bridge_(TV_series)" title="Brooklyn Bridge (TV series)">Brooklyn Bridge</a></i>, and "The Ballad of Buster Baxter" for a 1998 episode of the children's educational television series <i><a href="/wiki/Arthur_(TV_series)" title="Arthur (TV series)">Arthur</a></i>, where he was depicted as a singing moose.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Garfunkel's performance of <a href="/wiki/Monty_Python" title="Monty Python">Monty Python</a> member <a href="/wiki/Eric_Idle" title="Eric Idle">Eric Idle</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Always_Look_on_the_Bright_Side_of_Life" title="Always Look on the Bright Side of Life">Always Look on the Bright Side of Life</a>" was used in the end credits of the 1997 film <i><a href="/wiki/As_Good_as_It_Gets" title="As Good as It Gets">As Good as It Gets</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2003, Garfunkel made his debut as a songwriter on his <i><a href="/wiki/Everything_Waits_to_Be_Noticed" title="Everything Waits to Be Noticed">Everything Waits to Be Noticed</a></i> album. Teaming up with singer-songwriters <a href="/wiki/Maia_Sharp" title="Maia Sharp">Maia Sharp</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Buddy_Mondlock&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Buddy Mondlock (page does not exist)">Buddy Mondlock</a>, the album contained several songs which were originally poems written by Garfunkel. The album is recognized as his first effort at songwriting since his teenage years with Tom &amp; Jerry.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2003, Simon and Garfunkel reunited again for a successful world tour that extended into 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> That same year, his song "Sometimes When I'm Dreaming" from <i>The Art Garfunkel Album</i> (1984) (written by <a href="/wiki/Mike_Batt" title="Mike Batt">Mike Batt</a>) was re-recorded by ex-<a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Agnetha_F%C3%A4ltskog" title="Agnetha Fältskog">Agnetha Fältskog</a> on her album <i><a href="/wiki/My_Colouring_Book" title="My Colouring Book">My Colouring Book</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2006, Garfunkel signed with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhino_Records" title="Rhino Records">Rhino Records</a> (revived <a href="/wiki/Atco_Records" title="Atco Records">Atco Records</a>), and his first Rhino/Atco album <i>Some Enchanted Evening</i> was released in the United States on January 30, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The album was a dedicated celebration of pop standards of Garfunkel's childhood. In late February 2007, during a German television interview to promote the new album, he expressed interest in reuniting with Paul Simon on a new Simon &amp; Garfunkel album.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008.E2.80.93present:_Recent_events_and_vocal_problems">2008–present: Recent events and vocal problems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_Garfunkel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2008–present: Recent events and vocal problems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, Garfunkel appeared as himself on the HBO television show <i><a href="/wiki/Flight_of_the_Conchords_(TV_series)" title="Flight of the Conchords (TV series)">Flight of the Conchords</a></i> episode entitled "Prime Minister".</p>
<p>He continued to tour in 2009 with four musicians and his son.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 13, 2009, Simon and his band re-opened New York's Beacon Theatre, which had been closed for seven months for renovation. As an encore, Simon brought out "my old friend, Art Garfunkel." They sang three songs: "Sound of Silence," "The Boxer," and "Old Friends."</p>
<p>On April 2, 2009, the duo announced a tour of Australia, New Zealand, and Japan for summer 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> In late October, they participated together in the 25th anniversary of <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll_Hall_of_Fame" title="Rock and Roll Hall of Fame">Rock and Roll Hall of Fame</a> concerts at New York's <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">Madison Square Garden</a>. Other artists on the bill included <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen_%26_The_E_Street_Band" title="Bruce Springsteen &amp; The E Street Band">Bruce Springsteen &amp; The E Street Band</a>, <a href="/wiki/U2" title="U2">U2</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aretha_Franklin" title="Aretha Franklin">Aretha Franklin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Wonder" title="Stevie Wonder">Stevie Wonder</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crosby,_Stills,_%26_Nash" title="Crosby, Stills, &amp; Nash">Crosby, Stills, &amp; Nash</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2010, Simon &amp; Garfunkel announced a 13-date tour, to kick off in April with a performance at the <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans_Jazz_%26_Heritage_Festival" title="New Orleans Jazz &amp; Heritage Festival">New Orleans Jazz &amp; Heritage Festival</a>. Most performances were scheduled for Canada, with four shows in the upper Midwest of the US. According to a press release, the set list would focus on their classic catalog as well as songs from each of their solo careers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> On June 17, 2010, Simon &amp; Garfunkel canceled the tour, previously rescheduled for July 2010, which was postponed indefinitely while Garfunkel attempted to recover from a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vocal_cord_paresis" title="Vocal cord paresis">vocal cord paresis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2010, Garfunkel said in light of quitting smoking two and a half months before, he was recovering from <a href="/wiki/Paresis" title="Paresis">paresis</a> and would be touring in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RS_2010_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-RS_2010-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>He tried to resume touring in August 2012 just after releasing a 34-song retrospective, <i>The Singer</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Garfunkel scheduled 19 solo shows in the United States and Sweden between August and December 2012. 16 of the shows were canceled. His most recent concert appearance was in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> on September 27, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010-tour_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-2010-tour-44">[44]</a></sup> Garfunkel was due to perform at Night of The Proms in <a href="/wiki/Gothenburg" title="Gothenburg">Gothenburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Malm%C3%B6" title="Malmö">Malmö</a>, Sweden, on September 28 and 29, 2012, but cancelled at the last minute due to an "unforeseen vocal issue."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THR_20121001_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-THR_20121001-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Speaking about his voice in February 2013, Garfunkel said "It's getting mostly better; I'm pretty much there" and that he was starting to book small shows again.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TGC_20130212_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-TGC_20130212-47">[47]</a></sup> In 2014, he resumed touring, with Tab Laven accompanying him on acoustic guitar, his voice restored.</p>
<p>On the September 30, 2015, episode of <a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_Starring_Jimmy_Fallon" title="The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon">The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon</a>, Garfunkel took part in the spoof "Black Simon &amp; Garfunkel" skit with members of <a href="/wiki/The_Roots" title="The Roots">The Roots</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Poetry">Poetry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_Garfunkel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Poetry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Garfunkel, an avid reader and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bibliophile" title="Bibliophile">bibliophile</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> has admitted that the Garfunkel household was not a literary family and that it was not until entering Columbia College in 1959 that he began to "read a million books and became a reader." Thus began his interest in poetry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>Garfunkel's poetic career began in 1981 while on the Simon &amp; Garfunkel 1981–1982 tour in Switzerland. He was riding a motorcycle and began writing a poem describing the countryside. In 1989, <i>Still Water</i>, Garfunkel's collection of prose poetry, was released to acclaim. Topics included his depression over the loss of his father; Laurie Bird, his companion who committed suicide; his friendship with Paul Simon; and the joy of returning to music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_49-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>Garfunkel's website contains a year-by-year listing of every book he has read since 1968.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> Currently the list contains more than 1,000 books. He has also read the entire Random House Dictionary. Garfunkel has an interest in the philosopher <a href="/wiki/Jean-Jacques_Rousseau" title="Jean-Jacques Rousseau">Jean-Jacques Rousseau</a>, having read his book <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Confessions_(Jean-Jacques_Rousseau)" title="Confessions (Jean-Jacques Rousseau)">Confessions</a></i> at least three times (according to Garfunkel's website, the book was the 1st, 252nd, and 1000th book he read).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">Acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_Garfunkel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Garfunkel pursued an acting career in the early 70s, appearing in two <a href="/wiki/Mike_Nichols" title="Mike Nichols">Mike Nichols</a> films: <i><a href="/wiki/Catch-22_(film)" title="Catch-22 (film)">Catch-22</a></i> (1970), in which he played a supporting role as the 19-year-old naive Lieutenant Nately, and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carnal_Knowledge_(film)" title="Carnal Knowledge (film)">Carnal Knowledge</a></i> (1971), a co-starring role in which he played the idealistic character Sandy. His role as Sandy secured him a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Globe" title="Golden Globe">Golden Globe</a> nomination for Best Supporting Actor in 1972.</p>
<p>He later appeared in <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Roeg" title="Nicolas Roeg">Nicolas Roeg</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Bad_Timing" title="Bad Timing">Bad Timing</a></i> (1980) as Alex Linden, an American <a href="/wiki/Psychiatrist" title="Psychiatrist">psychiatrist</a> who serves as the film's main <a href="/wiki/Antagonist" title="Antagonist">antagonist</a>. The film received the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="Toronto International Film Festival">Toronto Festival of Festivals</a>'s highest honor, the People's Choice Award, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London_Film_Critics_Circle_Award" title="London Film Critics Circle Award">London Film Critics Circle Award</a> for Best Director.</p>
<p>He appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Good_to_Go_(film)" title="Good to Go (film)">Good to Go</a></i> (1986), directed by Blain Novak, starring as a Washington, D.C., journalist who struggles to clear his name after being framed for rape and murder. Garfunkel then appeared in the medical crime drama <i><a href="/wiki/Boxing_Helena" title="Boxing Helena">Boxing Helena</a></i> (1993), directed by <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lynch" title="Jennifer Lynch">Jennifer Lynch</a>, as Dr. Lawrence Augustine.</p>
<p>Garfunkel's most recent film is <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rebound" title="The Rebound">The Rebound</a></i> (2010), directed by <a href="/wiki/Bart_Freundlich" title="Bart Freundlich">Bart Freundlich</a>. He played Harry Finklestein, the slightly senile and comic-relief father of the film's main character.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Voice_classification">Voice classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_Garfunkel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Voice classification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Art Garfunkel is a <a href="/wiki/Tenor" title="Tenor">tenor</a> who usually sang the higher parts in Simon &amp; Garfunkel's harmonies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph2012_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph2012-51">[51]</a></sup> Garfunkel's voice changed almost imperceptibly until his late fifties, when it began to lower after years of smoking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> He quit smoking around 2010 to aid his recovery from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vocal_cord_paresis" title="Vocal cord paresis">vocal cord paresis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RS_2010_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-RS_2010-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_Garfunkel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Garfunkel married Linda Marie Grossman (b. 1944), an architect,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nashville" title="Nashville">Nashville</a> on October 1, 1972, and they divorced in 1975. He has claimed that the marriage was turbulent and ended bitterly. Garfunkel has not spoken to her since and once said, not only that he never loved her, but that he didn't even like her much during their short marriage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was also romantically involved with actress and photographer <a href="/wiki/Laurie_Bird" title="Laurie Bird">Laurie Bird</a> from March 1974 until her suicide in 1979.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> In a 1986 interview, Garfunkel said "I asked myself constantly why I didn't marry her, because surely she was the apple of my eye. She was everything I was looking for in a woman. But I was very hurt by my first marriage, so as far as marriage to Laurie was concerned, I was extra scared. I was heartbroken. It laid me low. I used to get very sad when the sun went down. The nights were very lonely for me."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>Garfunkel had a brief relationship with actress <a href="/wiki/Penny_Marshall" title="Penny Marshall">Penny Marshall</a> in the mid-1980s and credits her with helping him through his depression. Their friendship stayed strong even after their romantic relationship ended. Garfunkel would later say of Marshall, "Everything changed. Penny is a sweet human being who can bring anybody down to earth. We had a lot of laughs, great sex, and a ton of party nights."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late 1985, Garfunkel met former model Kathryn (Kim) Cermak (b. 1958) while shooting <i><a href="/wiki/Good_to_Go_(film)" title="Good to Go (film)">Good To Go</a></i>. They married on September 18, 1988, and have two children, James, born December 15, 1990, and Beau Daniel, born October 5, 2005, via a surrogate mother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>Garfunkel has undertaken several long walks in his lifetime, writing poetry along the way. In the early 1980s, he walked across Japan in a matter of weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> From 1983 to 1997, Garfunkel walked across the United States,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> taking 40 excursions to complete the route from New York City to the Pacific coast of Washington. In May 1998, Garfunkel began an incremented walk across Europe,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> the last stop of which was Istanbul, in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
<p>His all-time favorite pop song is <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">The Beatles</a>' "<a href="/wiki/Here,_There_and_Everywhere" title="Here, There and Everywhere">Here, There and Everywhere</a>" and his all-time favorite album is <i><a href="/wiki/Rumours_(album)" title="Rumours (album)">Rumours</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Fleetwood_Mac" title="Fleetwood Mac">Fleetwood Mac</a>. When asked about his musical preferences, he answered, "I have a very sure-footed sense of what I like, and exactly how much I like it. Give me two listenings of a song, and I can tell you exactly how it sits with me... I know my musical taste. I know my ears, I know what I respond to."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb.com_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-imdb.com-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
<p>Garfunkel has been arrested twice for the possession of <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_(drug)" title="Cannabis (drug)">cannabis</a>: once in early 2004 and again in August 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
