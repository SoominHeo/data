<p><i><b>Atlantica Online</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Free-to-play" title="Free-to-play">free-to-play</a> (F2P) <a href="/wiki/3D_computer_graphics" title="3D computer graphics">3D</a> <a href="/wiki/Tactical_role-playing_game" title="Tactical role-playing game">tactical</a> <a href="/wiki/Massively_multiplayer_online_role-playing_game" title="Massively multiplayer online role-playing game">massively multiplayer online role-playing game</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MMORPG" title="MMORPG">MMORPG</a>) developed by <a href="/wiki/NDOORS_Corporation" title="NDOORS Corporation">NDOORS Corporation</a>. The game's primary setting is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fantasy_fiction" title="Fantasy fiction">fantasy</a>-themed <a href="/wiki/Alternate_history" title="Alternate history">alternate history</a> Earth composed of a diverse blend of historical, cultural, <a href="/wiki/Allegory" title="Allegory">allegorical</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mythology" title="Mythology">mythological</a> elements. The game world is primarily modeled after real-world geography with accessible locations on six of the seven continents as well as the mythical floating continent of <a href="/wiki/Atlantis" title="Atlantis">Atlantis</a> from which the game's name is derived.</p>

<p>Atlantica Online is a turn-based tactical MMORPG in which the player primarily assumes the role of a descendant of the Atlantean people. The primary goal of the game is to search for your ancestral home to save the world from the effects of Oriharukon, a substance created by the ancient Atlanteans. The player's journey spans the globe and transcends time in search of clues that will lead to the lost civilization. The player battles men, monsters, and machines that have been corrupted by the Oriharukon’s Influence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The game design puts a strong emphasis on membership in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clan_(computer_gaming)" title="Clan (computer gaming)">guilds</a>, which are permitted to own and customize towns like a business, profiting from the transactions that take place within their town. Up to ten guilds can unite under a single banner to form a Nation and go to war with other nations. Guilds and nations offer a variety of benefits to their members including passive boosts, guild-oriented dungeons, and ‘Guild Crafting’ where every member of a guild works together to complete a single craft. Players can also own their own houses.</p>
<p>In addition to the main quest line, the game features a variety of supplementary side quests rich with history and lore, a complex skill system, an in-depth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crafting" title="Crafting">crafting</a> system, and a player housing system. As players journey through the world of Atlantica Online, their characters and mercenaries grow and develop gaining access to new, more powerful skills and equipment along the way. As the level cap has advanced, the developers have implemented a set of milestones in the leveling structure with associated level-specific quest lines that enable players access to powerful class-specific skills known as “talents” upon completion.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Players can recruit up to eight mercenaries to join them in their travels, and each can increase in experience level. There are relatively few equipment options available to players apart from a few class-specific armor sets and limited-time ‘legendary items’. Players can combine armor effects through an "enchantment" process.</p>
<p>The game's battle system is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turn-based" title="Turn-based">turn-based</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_Interview_Kim_Tae_Gon_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-IGN_Interview_Kim_Tae_Gon-17">[17]</a></sup> When a player character and an enemy character come into contact with each other on the game map, both parties are transported into an <a href="/wiki/Instance_dungeon" title="Instance dungeon">instance</a> where the battle takes place. During battle other players' groups and other monster groups may join in to a maximum of three groups on each side. In battle, the characters of each party are positioned on a 3x3 grid. Using attacks and abilities, the goal is to beat the opponent. The rewards include <a href="/wiki/Experience_point" title="Experience point">experience points</a> and loot like the game's virtual currency, various materials, pieces of equipment etc. <a href="/wiki/Player_versus_player" title="Player versus player">Player versus player</a> combat operates through this system. There are two leagues players can join, as well as daily, weekly, and monthly fights and championships.</p>
<p>The Tactical battle system (TBS) is a separate, additional combat system that differs in many respects from the original system. Players and their adversaries are placed in a landscape where moving around characters freely is possible. New decision impacts like the possibility to take cover, different height levels and the movement speed of specific characters are added while certain mechanics of the original combat system are removed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Troy_My_Home_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Troy_My_Home-18">[18]</a></sup> Players complete story-based and objective-driven missions in the tactical battle system.</p>
<p>A dungeon in Atlantica Online is an <a href="/wiki/Instance_dungeon" title="Instance dungeon">instance</a> region that can be opened with a special key and allows up to three players to clean the dungeon of enemies for a final reward in addition to the normal loot. <a href="/wiki/Raid_(gaming)" title="Raid (gaming)">Raid dungeons</a> were added in 2012.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Setting">Setting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Atlantica_Online&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Setting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The world of Atlantica Online features real world locations mixed with mythological and fantasy elements. Countries like <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> and <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> are represented, with cities like <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sapporo" title="Sapporo">Sapporo</a> as visitable hubs that offer services like banks, hospitals, the marketplace where players can trade goods with each other and other facilities. Though the locations on the map feature climates similar to their real world counterparts, the architecture is often a generic recreation of well known structure types, such as the cities in Eastern Europe and Japan.</p>
<p>The locations are based on real world locations, but are often geographically out of place and altered for effect. Most of the civilizations represented are anachronistic: Rome is set in the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a>, while Great Britain is independent, the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> reigns in Turkey, Southeast Asia resembles ancient Indian kingdoms before Western colonization and Islamic influence, and North America is caught up in the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Atlantica_Online&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The North American English version, available worldwide, was first distributed by the USA subsidiary company <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NDOORS_Interactive,_Inc." title="NDOORS Interactive, Inc.">NDOORS Interactive, Inc.</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> A closed beta version was made available to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=FilePlanet&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="FilePlanet (page does not exist)">FilePlanet</a> subscribers on July 15, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The first phase of the open beta ended on July 28, 2008; the start of the second phase began on August 7, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On October 30, 2008 at 11a.m. PDT, Atlantica Online ended its beta period and began its official run at 6p.m. PDT on October 30, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> NDOORS also launches Atlantica Online Germany, for European players, on the October 21. The closed Beta started on September 1 and ended after a month. The Chinese company <a href="/wiki/The9" title="The9">The9</a> has obtained exclusive rights for distributing and operating Atlantica Online in China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Russian localized version open beta test started May 12, 2010 supported by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Innova_Systems&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Innova Systems (page does not exist)">Innova Systems</a> company.</p>
<p>Atlantica's publisher and developer, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NDOORS_Interactive" title="NDOORS Interactive">NDOORS Interactive</a>, regularly releases new content updates. Non-Korean servers generally receive the updates after the Korean servers. Updates include, for instance, new <a href="/wiki/Dungeon" title="Dungeon">dungeons</a>, quests, NPCs, skills, mercenaries, outfits, equipment, mounts, and community features. Updates also <a href="/wiki/Balance_(game_design)" title="Balance (game design)">balance</a> the game's elements. Implemented on August 17, 2010, the Troy Story Arc is a series of 'missions' following the format of the <a href="#Tactical_battle_system">Tactical Battle System</a> (TBS) that was introduced in the same patch. In each mission one or more players take part in a fantasy version of the <a href="/wiki/Trojan_War" title="Trojan War">Trojan War</a>. The rewards include generous amounts of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Experience_Point" title="Experience Point">experience points</a> and valuable chests containing loot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Troy_My_Home_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-Troy_My_Home-18">[18]</a></sup> In April 2013, the first part of the major content update 'Rise of Atlantis' was released. May 2013 saw the second part and the third part is scheduled for June 2013. Previously with The Trojan War and the Romance of the Three Kingdom's reaching a great feedback from the players it was time to add in a new area, the Mythical region of Atlantis, which was divided by two, West and Eastern Atlantis. This update was the most extensive to that date, introducing a new continent to explore (mythical <a href="/wiki/Atlantis" title="Atlantis">Atlantis</a>), new mercenary classes, new equipment, content and several changes to other features of the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RoA1_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-RoA1-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RoA2_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-RoA2-25">[25]</a></sup> Implemented on April 8, 2014, (Europe) the Mayan Hero Arc is the start of the new "Heroes" class line following the format of the <a href="#Tactical_battle_system">Tactical Battle System</a> (TBS) that was introduced previously in some raids and other more classic Content. In each mission one or more players take part in a fantasy version of the ancient mythology. The rewards can be very rewarding if the highest level of content is done. Future content to come will revolution the game, such as Whip using Mercenary's, a Corps Feature and other things that are planned/set to hit the Korean Servers in 2014 and early 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mayan_Hero_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mayan_Hero-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 18, 2008, the North American continent was added, which features <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> as a new explorable city and the United States and Mexico as explorable countries. These contain a few dungeons, but greatly expand the map in Atlantica. Developers have also mentioned the possibility of adding the continents of the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere" title="Southern Hemisphere">Southern Hemisphere</a>, excluding <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a>.</p>

<p>Reception of <i>Atlantica Online</i> has been largely positive. The game received a number of awards upon release in 2008 and was nominated for MMORPG.com's Best New Game of 2008 where it took third place in the voting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ndoors_praise_notice_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-ndoors_praise_notice-36">[36]</a></sup> <i>Atlantica Online</i> was selected as an official game for the Korean Game and Game World Championship (GNGWC) in 2008 and 2009<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> and won the prestigious Golden Plume Award for Best Foreign Online Game at the 2008 China Digital Entertainment Expo and Conference (ChinaJoy).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChinaJoy_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-ChinaJoy-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>The game is acclaimed for its innovative turn-based battle system and its integration with community features. Danny Gourley of Ten Ton Hammer praised its gameplay, stating "it requires gamers to think a little instead of just mashing buttons".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
