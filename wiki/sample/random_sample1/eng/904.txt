<p><i><b>Diamond Wave</b></i> is the sixth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a> by Japanese recording artist <a href="/wiki/Mai_Kuraki" title="Mai Kuraki">Mai Kuraki</a>. It was released on August 2, 2006.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diamond_Wave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album was released in two formats: CD+DVD limited edition and CD only standard edition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-barks_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-barks-1">[1]</a></sup> On August 11, a limited revamped (cover and booklet) standard edition was released. <i>Diamond Wave</i> is Kuraki's first album to be released in various formats. A limited blog called "Diamond Web" was opened in commemoration of the release. To view the blog two passwords enclosed in both the <i>Diamond Wave</i> album and single were needed. To date this is the last studio album released in <a href="/wiki/Giza_Studio" title="Giza Studio">Giza Studio</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diamond_Wave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Diamond Wave</i> debuted on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> albums chart at #3 with 78,228 copies sold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sales_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-sales-2">[2]</a></sup> The album charted for a total of 12 weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DW_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-DW-3">[3]</a></sup> <i>Diamond Wave</i> was the 101st best selling album of 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yearly_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-yearly-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
