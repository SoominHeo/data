<p><b>Atis Slakteris</b> (born 21 November 1956, <a href="/wiki/Code_parish" title="Code parish">Code parish</a>) is a former Minister of Defence of <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Atis_Slakteris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In late 2008, Slakteris gave an interview with <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Television" title="Bloomberg Television">Bloomberg</a> about <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%932009_Latvian_financial_crisis" title="2008–2009 Latvian financial crisis">Latvia's economic crisis</a>, during which he answered a question regarding the causes of Latvia having come to borrow money from the <a href="/wiki/International_Monetary_Fund" title="International Monetary Fund">International Monetary Fund</a> as "nothing special". The phrases "nasing spesal" (mocking Slakteris' poor English and accent during the interview) and "my answer will be, but I will not say" became popular memes in Latvia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
