<p><i><b>Aphanopetalum</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> of twining <a href="/wiki/Shrub" title="Shrub">shrubs</a> or <a href="/wiki/Vine" title="Vine">vines</a> in the family <b>Aphanopetalaceae</b> which are endemic to <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.</p>
<p>The genus is placed alone in family <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aphanopetalaceae" title="Aphanopetalaceae">Aphanopetalaceae</a>, which is in turn now placed in order <a href="/wiki/Saxifragales" title="Saxifragales">Saxifragales</a>. Until recently this family was placed in <a href="/wiki/Oxalidales" title="Oxalidales">Oxalidales</a>, and before that the genus was included in family <a href="/wiki/Cunoniaceae" title="Cunoniaceae">Cunoniaceae</a>, also within <a href="/wiki/Oxalidales" title="Oxalidales">Oxalidales</a>. There are two species:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Christenhusz-Byng2016_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Christenhusz-Byng2016-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-_2-0"><a href="#cite_note--2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Aphanopetalum_clematideum" title="Aphanopetalum clematideum">Aphanopetalum clematideum</a></i> (Harv.) Domin, <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> to limestone cliffs of south western Australia</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Aphanopetalum_resinosum" title="Aphanopetalum resinosum">Aphanopetalum resinosum</a></i> Endl. - Gum Vine, endemic to southern Queensland and New South Wales</li>
</ul>
