<p><b>Apatity</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Апатиты</span>; <small>IPA: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Russian" title="Help:IPA for Russian">[əpɐˈtʲitɨ]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> in <a href="/wiki/Murmansk_Oblast" title="Murmansk Oblast">Murmansk Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located along the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Murman_Railway" title="Murman Railway">Murman Railway</a> between <a href="/wiki/Lake_Imandra" title="Lake Imandra">Lake Imandra</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Khibiny_Mountains" title="Khibiny Mountains">Khibiny Mountains</a>, 23 km (14 mi) west of <a href="/wiki/Kirovsk,_Murmansk_Oblast" title="Kirovsk, Murmansk Oblast">Kirovsk</a> and 185 km (115 mi) south of <a href="/wiki/Murmansk" title="Murmansk">Murmansk</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Oblast" title="Oblast">oblast</a>. The town is named after one of its most abundant natural resources in the area, <a href="/wiki/Apatite" title="Apatite">apatite</a>, the raw mineral used in the production of phosphorus mineral fertilizers. Population: <span class="nowrap">59,672 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apatity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Passing_loop" title="Passing loop">passing loop</a> of Bely (<span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">разъезд Бе́лый</span>) on the <a href="/wiki/Kirov_Railway" title="Kirov Railway">Leningrad-Murmansk Railway</a> was built in 1926 and the settlement of Apatity was founded in 1930. It was classified as an urban locality by the <a href="/wiki/All-Russian_Central_Executive_Committee" title="All-Russian Central Executive Committee">All-Russian Central Executive Committee</a> (VTsIK) Resolution of August 20, 1935, when the settlement of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pri_sovkhoze_%22Industriya%22&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title='Pri sovkhoze "Industriya" (page does not exist)'>pri sovkhoze "Industriya"</a> was merged into Apatity and it was granted <a href="/wiki/Urban-type_settlement" title="Urban-type settlement">work settlement</a> status.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ATSBook49_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ATSBook49-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Many of the early settlers in the Apatity area were former "<a href="/wiki/Kulak" title="Kulak">rich peasants</a>" from several regions of Northwestern Russia, resettled to Murmansk Oblast as part of Stalin's <a href="/wiki/Dekulakization" title="Dekulakization">Dekulakization</a> program. Members of certain ethnic minorities were deported to Apatity as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 6, 1966, the Murmansk Oblast Executive Committee petitioned to transform the work settlement of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Molodyozhny,_Murmansk_Oblast&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Molodyozhny, Murmansk Oblast (page does not exist)">Molodyozhny</a> in jurisdiction of Kirovsk into a town under oblast jurisdiction called Khibinogorsk and on subordinating a part of the area in Kirovsk's jurisdiction to it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ATSBook57_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-ATSBook57-6">[6]</a></sup> The petition was reviewed by the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the Russian SFSR, which, however, decreed on July 7, 1966 to merge the work settlements of Molodyozhny and Apatity into a town under oblast jurisdiction, which would retain the name Apatity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ATSBook57_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-ATSBook57-6">[6]</a></sup> Consequently, the Murmansk Oblast Executive Committee subordinated a part of the territory in Kirovsk's jurisdiction to the new town by the decision of October 13, 1966.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ATSBook57_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-ATSBook57-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>By the November 29, 1979 Decree by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Presidium_of_the_Supreme_Soviet_of_the_Russian_SFSR" title="Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the Russian SFSR">Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the Russian SFSR</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kovdorsky_District" title="Kovdorsky District">Kovdorsky District</a> was formed from the parts of the territory in Apatity's jurisdiction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ATSBook57_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-ATSBook57-6">[6]</a></sup> The work settlement of <a href="/wiki/Polyarnye_Zori" title="Polyarnye Zori">Polyarnye Zori</a> subordinated to Apatity was elevated in status to that of a town under oblast jurisdiction by another Decree of April 22, 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ATSBook57_6-5"><a href="#cite_note-ATSBook57-6">[6]</a></sup> A part of the territory in jurisdiction of Apatity was also transferred to Polyarnye Zori by the Decision of the Presidium of the Murmansk Oblast Soviet of People's Deputies of May 16, 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ATSBook57_6-6"><a href="#cite_note-ATSBook57-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apatity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, it is, together with two rural localities, incorporated as <b>Apatity <a href="/wiki/City_of_federal_subject_significance" title="City of federal subject significance">Town with Jurisdictional Territory</a></b>—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Murmansk_Oblast" title="Administrative divisions of Murmansk Oblast">districts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref395_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Ref395-1">[1]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, Apatity Town with Jurisdictional Territory is incorporated as <b>Apatity Urban Okrug</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref808_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ref808-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apatity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The main employer of Apatity is <a href="/wiki/Joint-stock_company" title="Joint-stock company">JSC</a> "Apatit", the largest mining and concentrating enterprise in Europe and Russia. Other employers include the Kola Science Center of the Russian Academy of Science and various state and private enterprises.</p>
<p>The joint civilian-military <a href="/wiki/Kirovsk-Apatity_Airport" title="Kirovsk-Apatity Airport">Kirovsk-Apatity Airport</a> is located 15 kilometers (9.3 mi) southeast of the town.</p>
