<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Hokusai quadrangle</h1>

<p>The <b>Hokusai</b> <a href="/wiki/Quadrangle_(geography)" title="Quadrangle (geography)">quadrangle</a> (H-5) is one of <a href="/wiki/List_of_quadrangles_on_Mercury" title="List of quadrangles on Mercury">fifteen quadrangles on Mercury</a>. It runs from 360 to 270° longitude and 20 to 70° latitude. Named after the <a href="/wiki/Hokusai_(crater)" title="Hokusai (crater)">Hokusai</a> crater, it was mapped in detail for the first time after <a href="/wiki/MESSENGER" title="MESSENGER">MESSENGER</a> entered orbit around <a href="/wiki/Mercury_(planet)" title="Mercury (planet)">Mercury</a> in 2011. It had not been mapped prior to that point because it was one of the six quadrangles that wasn't illuminated when <a href="/wiki/Mariner_10" title="Mariner 10">Mariner 10</a> made its flybys in 1974 and 1975. These six quadrangles continued to be known by their <a href="/wiki/List_of_albedo_features_on_Mercury" title="List of albedo features on Mercury">albedo feature</a> names, with this one known as the Apollonia quadrangle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
