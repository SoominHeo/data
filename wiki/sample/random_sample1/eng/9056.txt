<p>The <b>Republic of Afghanistan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Dari_language" title="Dari language">Dari</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="prs" xml:lang="prs">جمهوری افغانستان</span>‎, <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Persian transliteration">Jǝmhūri Afġānistān</span></i>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pashto_language" title="Pashto language">Pashto</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ps" xml:lang="ps">د افغانستان جمهوریت</span>‎, <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Pashto transliteration">Dǝ Afġānistān Jumhūriyat</span></i>) was the name of the first <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a> of <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, created in 1973 after <a href="/wiki/Mohammed_Daoud_Khan" title="Mohammed Daoud Khan">Mohammed Daoud Khan</a> deposed his cousin, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_of_Afghanistan" title="King of Afghanistan">King</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mohammad_Zahir_Shah" title="Mohammad Zahir Shah">Mohammad Zahir Shah</a>, in a non-violent <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">coup</a>. Daoud was known for his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Progressive_politics" title="Progressive politics">progressive politics</a> and attempts to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Modernization" title="Modernization">modernise</a> the country with help from both the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, among others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Iranica_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Iranica-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1978, a military coup known as the <a href="/wiki/Saur_Revolution" title="Saur Revolution">Saur Revolution</a> took place, instigated by the communist <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Democratic_Party_of_Afghanistan" title="People's Democratic Party of Afghanistan">People's Democratic Party of Afghanistan</a>, in which Daoud and his family were killed. The "Daoud Republic" was subsequently succeeded by the Soviet-allied <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_Afghanistan" title="Democratic Republic of Afghanistan">Democratic Republic of Afghanistan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daoud_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-daoud-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The current official name of <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a> is <i>Islamic Republic of Afghanistan</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Afghanistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Afghanistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 1973, while <a href="/wiki/Mohammed_Zahir_Shah" title="Mohammed Zahir Shah">Mohammed Zahir Shah</a> was in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> undergoing eye surgery as well as therapy for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lumbago" title="Lumbago">lumbago</a>, his cousin and former <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Afghanistan" title="Prime Minister of Afghanistan">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Mohammed_Daoud_Khan" title="Mohammed Daoud Khan">Mohammed Daoud Khan</a> staged a <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">coup d'état</a> and established a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republican_government" title="Republican government">republican government</a>. Daoud Khan had been forced to resign as prime minister by Zahir Shah a decade earlier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tntimes_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-tntimes-3">[3]</a></sup> The king abdicated the following month rather than risk an all-out civil war.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tntimes_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-tntimes-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_reforms">Political reforms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Afghanistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Political reforms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The same year, former <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Afghanistan" title="Prime Minister of Afghanistan">Prime Minister of Afghanistan</a> <a href="/wiki/Mohammad_Hashim_Maiwandwal" title="Mohammad Hashim Maiwandwal">Mohammad Hashim Maiwandwal</a> was accused of plotting a coup, though it is unclear if the plan was actually targeting the new republican government or the by then abolished monarchy. Maiwandwal was arrested and allegedly committed suicide in jail before his trial, but widespread belief says he was tortured to death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daoud_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-daoud-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>After seizing power, President <a href="/wiki/Mohammed_Daoud_Khan" title="Mohammed Daoud Khan">Mohammed Daoud Khan</a> established his own <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a>, the <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Afghanistan#Former_parties" title="List of political parties in Afghanistan">National Revolutionary Party</a>. This party became the sole focus of political activity in the country. The <a href="/wiki/Loya_jirga" title="Loya jirga">Loya jirga</a> approved a new constitution establishing a presidential <a href="/wiki/One-party_state" title="One-party state">one-party state</a> in January 1977, with political opponents being violently persecuted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daoud_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-daoud-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rise_of_communism">Rise of communism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Afghanistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Rise of communism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Democratic_Party_of_Afghanistan" title="People's Democratic Party of Afghanistan">People's Democratic Party of Afghanistan</a></div>
<p>During Daoud's presidency, relations with especially the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> eventually deteriorated. They saw his shift to a more Western-friendly leadership as dangerous, including criticism of Cuba's membership in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-aligned_Movement" title="Non-aligned Movement">Non-aligned Movement</a> and the expulsion of Soviet military and economic advisers. The suppression of political opposition furthermore turned the Soviet-backed <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Democratic_Party_of_Afghanistan" title="People's Democratic Party of Afghanistan">People's Democratic Party (PDPA)</a>, an important ally in the 1973 coup against the king, against him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daoud_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-daoud-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1976, Daoud established a seven-year <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Economic_plan" title="Economic plan">economic plan</a> for the country. He started military training programs with <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and commenced <a href="/wiki/Economic_development" title="Economic development">economic development</a> talks with <a href="/wiki/Pahlavi_dynasty" title="Pahlavi dynasty">Imperial Iran</a>. Daoud also turned his attention to oil rich <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle Eastern</a> nations such as <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kuwait" title="Kuwait">Kuwait</a> among others for financial assistance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daoud_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-daoud-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Daoud had however achieved little of what he had set out to accomplish in 1978. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Afghan_economy" title="Afghan economy">Afghan economy</a> hadn't made any real progress and the Afghan standard of living had not risen. Daoud had also garnered much criticism for his single party constitution in 1977 which alienated him from his political supporters. By this time, the two main factions of the PDPA, previously locked in a power struggle, had reached a fragile agreement for reconciliation. Communist-sympathizing army officials were by then already planning a move against the government. According to <a href="/wiki/Hafizullah_Amin" title="Hafizullah Amin">Hafizullah Amin</a>, who became Afghan head of state in 1979, the PDPA had started plotting the coup in 1976, two years before it materialized.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daoud_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-daoud-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Saur_Revolution">Saur Revolution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Afghanistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Saur Revolution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The PDPA seized power in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_coup" title="Military coup">military coup</a> in 1978 which is best known as the <a href="/wiki/Saur_Revolution" title="Saur Revolution">Saur Revolution</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-4">[4]</a></sup> On April 27, troops from the <a href="/wiki/Military_base" title="Military base">military base</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kabul_International_Airport" title="Kabul International Airport">Kabul International Airport</a> started to move towards the center of the capital. It took only 24 hours to consolidate power, with the rapid push including an air raid on the <a href="/wiki/Arg_(Kabul)" title="Arg (Kabul)">presidential palace</a> and insurgent army units quickly seizing critical institutions and communication lines. Daoud and most of his family were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Executed" title="Executed">executed</a> the following day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Garthoff.2C_Raymond_L._1994._p._986_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Garthoff.2C_Raymond_L._1994._p._986-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Nur_Muhammad_Taraki" title="Nur Muhammad Taraki">Nur Muhammad Taraki</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_Secretary" title="General Secretary">General Secretary</a> of the PDPA, was proclaimed Chairman of the Presidium of the <a href="/wiki/Revolutionary_Council_(Afghanistan)" title="Revolutionary Council (Afghanistan)">Revolutionary Council</a> and effectively succeeded Mohammed Daoud Khan as head of state. He simultaneously became head of government of the newly established <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_Afghanistan" title="Democratic Republic of Afghanistan">Democratic Republic of Afghanistan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Garthoff.2C_Raymond_L._1994._p._986_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Garthoff.2C_Raymond_L._1994._p._986-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
