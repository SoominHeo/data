<p><b>People's Islamic Unity Party of Afghanistan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="fa" xml:lang="fa">Ø­Ø²Ø¨ ÙØ­Ø¯Øª Ø§Ø³ÙØ§ÙÛ ÙØ±Ø¯Ù Ø§ÙØºØ§ÙØ³ØªØ§Ù</span>ââ, <i>Hezb-e Wahdat Islami Mardum-e Afghanistan</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> in <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, formed after a split in the <i><a href="/wiki/Hezbe_Wahdat" title="Hezbe Wahdat">Hezbe Wahdat</a></i>. The party is led by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mohammed_Mohaqiq" title="Mohammed Mohaqiq">Mohammed Mohaqiq</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The party was founded in 2004 after Mohaqiq, resigned from his post as Minister of Planning in the Afghan government. After resigning from both his government position and <a href="/wiki/Hezbe_Wahdat" title="Hezbe Wahdat">Hezbe Wahdat</a> Mohaqiq announced both the creation of the party and also his candidacy for the <a href="/wiki/Afghan_presidential_election,_2004" title="Afghan presidential election, 2004">2004 Presidential election</a>. Despite having just founded the PIUPA, Mohaqiq ran as an independent on a Hazara nationalist platform. Like many of the other candidates, Mohaqiq's campaign emphasized an "everyman" image, and claimed that he would fight for the people's interests. Despite <a href="/wiki/Karim_Khalili" title="Karim Khalili">Karim Khalili</a>, the Head of Hezbe Wahdat, running as Vice President on President Karzai's ticket Mohaqiq won the bulk of the Hazara vote.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mohaqiq and the party opposed the 2009 <a href="/wiki/Shia_Family_Law" title="Shia Family Law">Personal Status Law</a>, which allowed a man to refuse sustenance to his wife should she refuse to have sex with him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
