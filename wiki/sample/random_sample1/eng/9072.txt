<p><b>Ahmed Muhammad Ahmed el-Tayeb</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">أحمد محمد أحمد الطيب</span>‎‎) is the current <a href="/wiki/Grand_Imam_of_al-Azhar" title="Grand Imam of al-Azhar">Grand Imam of al-Azhar</a> and former president of <a href="/wiki/Al-Azhar_University" title="Al-Azhar University">al-Azhar University</a>. He was appointed by the Egyptian President, <a href="/wiki/Hosni_Mubarak" title="Hosni Mubarak">Hosni Mubarak</a>, following the death of <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_Sayyid_Tantawy" title="Muhammad Sayyid Tantawy">Muhammad Sayyid Tantawy</a> in 2010. He is considered to be one of the most moderate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sunni" title="Sunni">Sunni</a> clerics in Egypt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup> He holds a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Ph. D</a> in Islamic philosophy from the <a href="/wiki/Paris-Sorbonne_University" title="Paris-Sorbonne University">Paris-Sorbonne University</a> and has been president of <a href="/wiki/Al-Azhar_University" title="Al-Azhar University">Al-Azhar University</a> since 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Between 2002 and 2003, el-Tayeb served as <a href="/wiki/Grand_Mufti" title="Grand Mufti">Grand Mufti</a> of Egypt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> El-Tayeb is a hereditary Sufi shaykh from Upper Egypt and has expressed support for a global Sufi league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Democratic_Party">National Democratic Party</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahmed_el-Tayeb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: National Democratic Party">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to his appointment as the Grand Imam of Al-Azhar and president of al-Azhar University, he was a member of Mubarak's <a href="/wiki/National_Democratic_Party_(Egypt)" title="National Democratic Party (Egypt)">National Democratic Party</a>'s Policies Committee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup> He initially refused to resign from his position in the National Democratic Party (NDP) by saying that there was no conflict between his role at Al-Azhar and membership in the party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2010, he resigned from his post in the party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Muslim_Brotherhood">Muslim Brotherhood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahmed_el-Tayeb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Muslim Brotherhood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In an article published shortly after his appointment as president of Al-Azhar University, he was described as "a regime loyalist and member of Mr Mubarak's ruling National Democratic Party [who] takes a firm stance against the Muslim Brotherhood".</p>
<p>Tayeb was quoted as saying that Al-Azhar University would "never be an open field for the Brotherhood".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The same article reported that the Muslim Brotherhood's leader, <a href="/wiki/Mohammed_Badie" title="Mohammed Badie">Mohammed Badie</a>, had congratulated Tayeb on his appointment. At the same time, the Brotherhood senior member, Sheikh Sayed Askar, also an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Azharite" title="Azharite">Azharite</a>, accused the government of "promoting one of its own at the expense of people better suited to the post".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Criticism of intensified after the 2011 <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_revolution_of_2011" title="Egyptian revolution of 2011">Egyptian Revolution</a>. Some Muslim Brotherhood members accused him of being "a remnant of the ousted Mubarak regime and National Democratic Party".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, following the Egyptian revolution, the <a href="/wiki/Muslim_Brotherhood" title="Muslim Brotherhood">Muslim Brotherhood</a> held a rally at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al-Azhar_mosque" title="Al-Azhar mosque">Al-Azhar mosque</a> to oppose what it described as the <a href="/wiki/Judaization_of_Jerusalem" title="Judaization of Jerusalem">Judaization of Jerusalem</a>. He said at the rally that "the al-Aqsa Mosque is currently under an offensive by the Jews" and "we shall not allow the Zionists to Judaize al-Quds [Jerusalem]". He also alleged that Jews around the world were trying to prevent Islamic and Egyptian unity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The rally was criticized by the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daily_News_(New_York)" title="Daily News (New York)">New York Daily News</a></i> as antisemitic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>He backed the <a href="/wiki/2013_Egyptian_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="2013 Egyptian coup d'état">military coup</a> against Egyptian President <a href="/wiki/Mohamed_Morsi" title="Mohamed Morsi">Mohamed Morsi</a>, a leader of the Muslim Brotherhood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Opposition_to_sectarianism">Opposition to sectarianism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahmed_el-Tayeb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Opposition to sectarianism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He has strongly rebuked the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salafi" title="Salafi">Salafi</a> anti-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alawite" title="Alawite">Alawite</a> preaching, which has increased since the Assad government, dominated by Alawites, cracked down on the Islamist rebels in the <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Civil_War" title="Syrian Civil War">Syrian Civil War</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Jews">Jews</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahmed_el-Tayeb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Jews">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In an interview which aired on Egypt's Channel 1 on October 25, 2013 (as translated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MEMRI" title="MEMRI">MEMRI</a>), Tayeb stated, "Since the inception of Islam 1,400 years ago, we have been suffering from Jewish and Zionist interference in Muslim affairs. This is a cause of great distress for the Muslims.".</p>
<p>He also argued that "the Quran said it and history has proven it: 'You shall find the strongest among men in enmity to the believers to be the Jews and the polytheists'". He also claimed that Jews consider non-Jews to be "extremely inferior" and that Jews "practice a terrible hierarchy, and they are not ashamed to admit it, because it is written in the Torah – with regard to killing, enslavement, and so on".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Islamic_State_of_Iraq_and_the_Levant">Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahmed_el-Tayeb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He has strongly condemned the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_State_of_Iraq_and_the_Levant" title="Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant">Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant</a> and stated that it is acting "under the guise of this holy religion and have given themselves the name 'Islamic State' in an attempt to export their false Islam"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and (citing the <a href="/wiki/Quran" title="Quran">Quran</a>).</p>
<p>"The punishment for those who wage war against God and his Prophet and who strive to sow corruption on earth is death, crucifixion, the severing of hands and feet on opposite sides or banishment from the land. This is the disgrace for them in this world and in the hereafter they will receive grievous torment".</p>
<p>He has been criticized for not expressly stating that Islamic State was heretical.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AhramRenewal_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-AhramRenewal-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Ash%27ari" title="Ash'ari">Ash'ari</a> school of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamic_theology" title="Islamic theology">Islamic theology</a> - to which El-Tayeb belongs - does not allow calling a person who follows the <a href="/wiki/Shahada" title="Shahada">shahada</a> an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apostate" title="Apostate">apostate</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AhramRenewal_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-AhramRenewal-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tayeb has strongly come out against the practice of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Takfirism" title="Takfirism">takfirism</a>, declaring a Muslim an apostate, which is used by Islamic State to "judge and accuse anyone who doesn't tow their line with apostasy and outside the realm of the faith" and declares "<a href="/wiki/Jihad" title="Jihad">jihad</a> on peaceful Muslims" by using "flawed interpretations of some Qur'anic texts, the prophet's Sunna, and the Imams' views, believing incorrectly that they are leaders of Muslim armies fighting infidel peoples in unbelieving lands".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWL_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-MWL-21">[21]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Books">Books</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahmed_el-Tayeb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Books">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
