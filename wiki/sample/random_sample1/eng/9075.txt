<p><i><b>Riddle Story of Devil</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">悪魔のリドル</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">Hepburn</a>: <i>Akuma no Ridoru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. <i>Devil's Riddle</i>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series written by <a href="/wiki/Yun_K%C5%8Dga" title="Yun Kōga">Yun Kōga</a> and illustrated by Sunao Minakata. It was serialized in <a href="/wiki/Kadokawa_Shoten" title="Kadokawa Shoten">Kadokawa Shoten</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Newtype" title="Newtype">Newtype</a></i> magazine between the September 2012 and November 2016 issues and is licensed in English by <a href="/wiki/Seven_Seas_Entertainment" title="Seven Seas Entertainment">Seven Seas Entertainment</a>. An <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> adaptation directed by <a href="/wiki/Keiz%C5%8D_Kusakawa" title="Keizō Kusakawa">Keizō Kusakawa</a> and animated by <a href="/wiki/Diomed%C3%A9a" title="Diomedéa">Diomedéa</a> aired in Japan between April and June 2014. The series is licensed for streaming by <a href="/wiki/Funimation" title="Funimation">Funimation</a> in North America and <a href="/wiki/Madman_Entertainment" title="Madman Entertainment">Madman Entertainment</a> in Australia. The original title is a pun on <a href="/wiki/Violin_Sonata_in_G_minor_(Tartini)" title="Violin Sonata in G minor (Tartini)"><i>Devil's Trill</i></a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">悪魔のトリル</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Akuma no Toriru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> by <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Tartini" title="Giuseppe Tartini">Giuseppe Tartini</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riddle_Story_of_Devil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At a private girls' boarding school, Myōjō Academy, thirteen girls are transferred into the academy's "10th Year Class Black" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">10年黒組</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Jū-nen Kuro-gumi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. Of these thirteen students, twelve are assassins from various backgrounds who are all tasked with assassinating the remaining student, a girl named Haru Ichinose. If they succeed, they will be granted any wish they desire, but if they fail, they are expelled from the class. However, one of the assassins, Tokaku Azuma, has sworn to protect Haru from the other assassins.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riddle_Story_of_Devil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl>
<dt>Tokaku Azuma <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">東 兎角</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Azuma Tokaku</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Ayaka_Suwa" title="Ayaka Suwa">Ayaka Suwa</a> (Japanese); Morgan Berry<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eng_cast_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-eng_cast-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-engcast2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-engcast2-3">[3]</a></sup> (English)</span></dd>
<dd>No. 1 of Class Black, the protagonist of the story. Tokaku is a distant and cold girl who comes from the infamous Azuma family, a feared family of skilled assassins. Though Tokaku appears strong with her stoicism, she is mentally rather weak, easily influenced by others, and makes little decisions for herself. She is skilled with using knives, and has exceptionally quick reflexes. It is soon revealed that Tokaku has never killed anyone before, despite possessing very useful skills as an assassin, due to the influence of her mother and aunt not wanting her to become a murderer like them. During her time in Class Black, Tokaku becomes Haru's protector, for reasons even she does not know.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>The reason Tokaku defected to Haru's side was that she was unconsciously influenced by Haru's "queen bee" ability. In the anime she ends up resuming her attempt to assassinate Haru in order to find out for sure if her defection has been an exercise of her own free will all along. She successfully stabs her, only to express in regret her wish to be with Haru. However, Haru ends up surviving the attack, leading to Tokaku's expulsion. After Haru's graduation, Tokaku, now understanding that her attraction to Haru has nothing to do with Haru's manipulative ability, reunites with Haru. In the manga's conclusion, she works alongside Haru and is dating her.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Haru Ichinose <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">一ノ瀬 晴</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ichinose Haru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Hisako_Kanemoto" title="Hisako Kanemoto">Hisako Kanemoto</a> (Japanese); <a href="/wiki/Alexis_Tipton" title="Alexis Tipton">Alexis Tipton</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eng_cast_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-eng_cast-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-engcast2_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-engcast2-3">[3]</a></sup> (English)</span></dd>
<dd>No. 13 of Class Black, the second main character. Haru is a bright and cheerful girl, who aims to become friends with everyone. She is kind and naive to everyone without fail until they become hostile with her. Haru is the target that everyone in the class is after. She has strange scars on her thighs, which were discovered by Tokaku by flipping up her skirt. She is later shown to not only have scars on her thighs, but all over her body as well. She is uncomfortable with people looking at them.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>Haru possesses an ability, exclusive to her clan, known as the "queen bee" ability, that attracts, controls and manipulates people, like how a queen bee controls her worker bees. This ability of Haru's is why Class Black was created, as a rite of passage to see if she was able to become the clan's newest primer. Tokaku became her protector due to being unconsciously influenced by this ability. Upon discovery of Haru's queen bee ability, however, Tokaku resumes attempting on her life. Haru, resolving to graduate the Class Black alive, tries unsuccessfully to fight back. Despite being stabbed in her heart by Tokaku, however, Haru is saved by the knife deflecting off her ribs, which had been replaced with titanium ones, and therefore becomes the sole participant in the graduation ceremony of the Class Black. As per her wish, she is allowed to leave her clan and live a normal life. With diplomas of her other classmates in possession, she is joined by Tokaku and embarks on a trip to personally deliver those diplomas.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>

<p><i>Riddle Story of Devil</i> is written by <a href="/wiki/Yun_K%C5%8Dga" title="Yun Kōga">Yun Kōga</a> and illustrated by Sunao Minakata. It was serialized in <a href="/wiki/Kadokawa_Shoten" title="Kadokawa Shoten">Kadokawa Shoten</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Newtype" title="Newtype">Newtype</a></i> magazine between the September 2012 and November 2016 issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Kadokawa Shoten published five <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> volumes between July 9, 2013 and December 10, 2016. <a href="/wiki/Seven_Seas_Entertainment" title="Seven Seas Entertainment">Seven Seas Entertainment</a> released the series in North America from 2015<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> to 2016. The English version is available on <a href="/wiki/Kadokawa_Corporation" title="Kadokawa Corporation">Kadokawa</a>'s website BookWallker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riddle_Story_of_Devil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> adaptation directed by <a href="/wiki/Keiz%C5%8D_Kusakawa" title="Keizō Kusakawa">Keizō Kusakawa</a> and produced by <a href="/wiki/Diomed%C3%A9a" title="Diomedéa">Diomedéa</a> aired in Japan between April 3, 2014 and June 19, 2014. The screenplay is written by Kiyoko Yoshimura and chief animation director Naomi Ide bases the character designs used in the anime on Sunao Minakata original designs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The opening theme is "Sōshō Innocence" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">創傷イノセンス</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, "Wounded Innocence")</span> by <a href="/wiki/Maaya_Uchida" title="Maaya Uchida">Maaya Uchida</a>, while each episode features a different ending theme sung by the main thirteen voice actresses of the anime. Episode 12 contains an insert song titled "<a href="/wiki/Aogeba_T%C5%8Dtoshi" title="Aogeba Tōtoshi">Aogeba Tōtoshi</a>" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">仰げば尊し</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> sung by the main thirteen voice actresses. The series is licensed for streaming by <a href="/wiki/Funimation" title="Funimation">Funimation</a> in North America and <a href="/wiki/Madman_Entertainment" title="Madman Entertainment">Madman Entertainment</a> in Australia, and is being simulcast by <a href="/wiki/Crunchyroll" title="Crunchyroll">Crunchyroll</a> outside of North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Anime_Limited" title="Anime Limited">Anime Limited</a> acquired the series for a release in the United Kingdom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
