<p><b>Axel Laurent Angel Lambert Witsel</b> (born 12 January 1989) is a Belgian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for Chinese club <a href="/wiki/Tianjin_Quanjian_F.C." title="Tianjin Quanjian F.C.">Tianjin Quanjian</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toChina_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-toChina-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> During his play for the <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_football_team" title="Belgium national football team">Belgium national team</a>, he came into the first team as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Winger" title="Midfielder">right-winger</a>, and can also play <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Attacking_midfielder" title="Midfielder">attacking midfielder</a>, though his natural position is as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Central_midfielder" title="Midfielder">central midfielder</a>.</p>
<p>He made his senior international debut in 2008 and has earned over 70 caps for Belgium, representing the country at the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">UEFA Euro 2016</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Axel_Witsel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Standard_Li.C3.A8ge">Standard Liège</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Axel_Witsel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Standard Liège">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 17 September 2006, a 17-year-old Witsel made his debut with <a href="/wiki/Standard_Li%C3%A8ge" title="Standard Liège">Standard Liège</a> during a competitive game against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FC_Brussels" title="FC Brussels">FC Brussels</a> when he replaced <a href="/wiki/Steven_Defour" title="Steven Defour">Steven Defour</a> in the 89th minute. Eleven days later, he made his European debut as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Substitute_(football)" title="Substitute (football)">substitute</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Belgian_First_Division" title="2007–08 Belgian First Division">2007–08</a> season, at 18 years of age, he was a key member in the squad of Standard Liège, winners of the Belgian League title that year. During this year, he formed a very young midfield together with other Belgian talents Defour and <a href="/wiki/Marouane_Fellaini" title="Marouane Fellaini">Marouane Fellaini</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Belgian_First_Division" title="2008–09 Belgian First Division">following season</a>, he scored the winning goal <a href="/wiki/Penalty_kick_(association_football)" title="Penalty kick (association football)">from the penalty spot</a> in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Two-legged_match" title="Two-legged match">two-legged playoff</a> to decide the championship.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On 30 August 2009, Witsel sparked controversy when he broke <a href="/wiki/Marcin_Wasilewski" title="Marcin Wasilewski">Marcin Wasilewski</a>'s leg by stamping on his ankle during the match between <a href="/wiki/R.S.C._Anderlecht" title="R.S.C. Anderlecht">Anderlecht</a> and Standard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Following the tackle, Witsel protested the red card, claiming that it was an accident without intent, before being sent off the pitch. He soon apologised, but was the subject of criticism from several media outlets and numerous <a href="/wiki/Death_threat" title="Death threat">death threats</a> from angry Anderlecht and <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_football_team" title="Poland national football team">Poland</a> fans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The initial ban until 23 November by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Belgian_Football_Association" title="Royal Belgian Football Association">Belgian Football Association</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> was later reduced on appeal to eight matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Benfica">Benfica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Axel_Witsel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Benfica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Witsel signed for Portuguese club <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">Benfica</a> on a five-year deal on 13 July 2011, including a €40 million buyout clause.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Witsel scored twice in the 3–1 win against <a href="/wiki/FC_Twente" title="FC Twente">FC Twente</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2011–12 UEFA Champions League">Champions League play-off second leg</a>, on 24 August 2011, sending Benfica to the group stages with a 5–3 aggregate victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<p>He scored the opening goal in a 4–1 thrashing of <a href="/wiki/Vit%C3%B3ria_S.C." title="Vitória S.C.">Vitória de Guimarães</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Ta%C3%A7a_da_Liga" title="2011–12 Taça da Liga">Taça da Liga</a> on 9 January 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He then assisted <a href="/wiki/Nolito" title="Nolito">Nolito</a> in a 4–1 league defeat of <a href="/wiki/Vit%C3%B3ria_F.C." title="Vitória F.C.">Vitória de Setúbal</a>. Witsel scored his fourth goal against <a href="/wiki/C.D._Santa_Clara" title="C.D. Santa Clara">Santa Clara</a> on 18 January in a Taça da Liga match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Benfica reached the final on 14 April against <a href="/wiki/Gil_Vicente_F.C." title="Gil Vicente F.C.">Gil Vicente</a> and Witsel was awarded the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Man_of_the_Match" title="Man of the Match">Man of the Match</a> award as Benfica claimed their fourth Taça da Liga title with a 2–1 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Zenit_Saint_Petersburg">Zenit Saint Petersburg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Axel_Witsel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Zenit Saint Petersburg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 3 September 2012, Witsel signed for <a href="/wiki/FC_Zenit_Saint_Petersburg" title="FC Zenit Saint Petersburg">Zenit Saint Petersburg</a> for €40 million, after signing a five-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Witsel made his debut in the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Russian_Premier_League" title="2012–13 Russian Premier League">Russian Premier League</a> on 14 September, replacing <a href="/wiki/Konstantin_Zyryanov" title="Konstantin Zyryanov">Konstantin Zyryanov</a> in the 70th minute as Zenit fell 2–0 to <a href="/wiki/FC_Terek_Grozny" title="FC Terek Grozny">Terek Grozny</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He scored his first two goals for the club on 30 November when Zenit brushed aside <a href="/wiki/FC_Spartak_Moscow" title="FC Spartak Moscow">Spartak Moscow</a> 4–2 at the <a href="/wiki/Luzhniki_Stadium" title="Luzhniki Stadium">Luzhniki Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Witsel scored Zenit's winner against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terek_Grozny" title="Terek Grozny">Terek Grozny</a> on 30 March 2013, an eighth-minute strike, as his side cruised to a 3–0 league victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Zenit's first match of the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_UEFA_Champions_League_group_stage#Group_C" title="2014–15 UEFA Champions League group stage">2014–15 UEFA Champions League group stage</a> on 16 September 2014, Witsel scored Zenit's second goal in a 2–0 away victory over former club Benfica.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> After finishing third in their Champions League group, Zenit were parachuted into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2014-15_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2014-15 UEFA Europa League">Europa League</a> knockout phase; in the first leg of their Round of 16 clash against <a href="/wiki/Torino_F.C." title="Torino F.C.">Torino</a> on 12 March 2015, Witsel scored on a rebound as Zenit defeated the Italian club 2–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On 26 April, Witsel scored the game's only goal in the seventeenth minute as Zenit defeated <a href="/wiki/FC_Arsenal_Tula" title="FC Arsenal Tula">Arsenal Tula</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Russian_Premier_League" title="Russian Premier League">league</a> match at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Petrovskiy_Stadium" title="Petrovskiy Stadium">Petrovskiy Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> On Sunday 17 May, Witsel played the full 90 minutes as Zenit drew 1–1 with <a href="/wiki/FC_Ufa" title="FC Ufa">FC Ufa</a>, earning the point they needed to secure the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Russian_Premier_League" title="2014–15 Russian Premier League">Premier League</a> crown, the club's fourth overall and Witsel's first with Zenit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2015_Russian_Super_Cup" title="2015 Russian Super Cup">2015 Russian Super Cup</a> on 12 July 2015, Witsel converted his penalty shot in a 4–2 penalty shoot-out victory over <a href="/wiki/FC_Lokomotiv_Moscow" title="FC Lokomotiv Moscow">FC Lokomotiv Moscow</a> after the match had finished 1–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tianjin_Quanjian">Tianjin Quanjian</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Axel_Witsel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tianjin Quanjian">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 3 January 2017, Zenit officially announced that Witsel has moved to a Chinese club <a href="/wiki/Tianjin_Quanjian_F.C." title="Tianjin Quanjian F.C.">Tianjin Quanjian</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toChina_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-toChina-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Axel_Witsel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Axel_Witsel_Belgium.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="463" data-file-width="497" height="205" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Axel_Witsel_Belgium.jpg/220px-Axel_Witsel_Belgium.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Axel_Witsel_Belgium.jpg/330px-Axel_Witsel_Belgium.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Axel_Witsel_Belgium.jpg/440px-Axel_Witsel_Belgium.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Prior to making his international debut for the senior side, he played for the <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_under-21_football_team" title="Belgium national under-21 football team">under-21 side</a> in 2007 earning nine caps as well as helping his side reach the semi-finals of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship" title="2007 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship">2007 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship</a>.</p>
<p>On 26 March 2008, Witsel made his international debut during a friendly game against <a href="/wiki/Morocco_national_football_team" title="Morocco national football team">Morocco</a>. The match resulted in a 1–4 loss, but Witsel scored his first goal for <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_football_team" title="Belgium national football team">Belgium</a>.</p>
<p>On 13 May 2014, Witsel was named in Belgium's squad for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> He started in midfield in the team's first game of the tournament, a 2–1 win against <a href="/wiki/Algeria_national_football_team" title="Algeria national football team">Algeria</a> in <a href="/wiki/Belo_Horizonte" title="Belo Horizonte">Belo Horizonte</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
