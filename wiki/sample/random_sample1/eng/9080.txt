<p><b>Gymnophthalmidae</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> of lizards, sometimes known as <b>spectacled lizards</b> or <b>microteiids</b>. They are called 'spectacled' because of their transparent lower eyelids, so they can still see with closed eyes. Like most lizards, but unlike <a href="/wiki/Gecko" title="Gecko">geckos</a>, these eyelids are movable.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description_and_ecology">Description and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gymnophthalmidae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Spectacled lizards are related to the <a href="/wiki/Teiidae" title="Teiidae">Teiidae</a>, but they look like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skinks" title="Skinks">skinks</a> (slightly more distant relatives) with smooth <a href="/wiki/Reptile_scale" title="Reptile scale">scales</a>. They are generally small lizards; many species have reduced limbs. Unusually among lizards, however, it is generally the hind limbs that are reduced or absent, rather than the forelimbs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bauer1998_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-bauer1998-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gymnophthalmids live in a wide variety of <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitats</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a> to <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountain</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rain_forest" title="Rain forest">rain forest</a>, throughout <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. They are usually inhabitants of the <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forest</a> floor or wet areas associated with tropical forests, either <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nocturnal" title="Nocturnal">nocturnal</a> or intermittently active throughout the day. Spectacled lizards eat mostly insects and other invertebrates, and all species <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oviparous" title="Oviparous">lay eggs</a>. Often they can be found under logs, rocks, or other debris or foraging for small <a href="/wiki/Invertebrate" title="Invertebrate">invertebrate</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prey" title="Prey">prey</a>. Some species are able to dive in order to escape.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bauer1998_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-bauer1998-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
