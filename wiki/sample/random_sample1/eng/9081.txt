<p><i><b>Angoroj</b></i> (1964; <a href="/wiki/Esperanto" title="Esperanto">Esperanto</a> for "Agonies") was the first <a href="/wiki/Feature_film" title="Feature film">feature film</a> to be produced entirely in <a href="/wiki/Esperanto" title="Esperanto">Esperanto</a>. It was directed and produced by Jacques-Louis Mahé, a friend of <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Schwartz" title="Raymond Schwartz">Raymond Schwartz</a> who, under the <a href="/wiki/Pseudonym" title="Pseudonym">pseudonym</a> 'Lorjak', had previously produced a silent Esperanto publicity film before <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> titled <i>Antaŭen!</i> (<i>Onwards!</i>).</p>
<p>At the start of the 1960s Mahé, a professional <a href="/wiki/Photography" title="Photography">photographic</a> and cinematic expert, invested in the production of the first fictional film in Esperanto. Using a scenario by Mahé himself, the actors of the Internacia Arta Teatro (International Arts Theatre) presented a <a href="/wiki/Crime_fiction" title="Crime fiction">crime story</a>, set in the <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Parisian</a> periphery of petty thieves and cheats. Other notable people who played parts in the film included <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Schwartz" title="Raymond Schwartz">Schwartz</a> (the commissioner), <a href="/wiki/Gaston_Waringhien" title="Gaston Waringhien">Gaston Waringhien</a> (the voice-over) and many from the environs of the contemporary Paris, including <a href="/wiki/Michel_Duc-Goninaz" title="Michel Duc-Goninaz">Michel Duc-Goninaz</a>.</p>
<p>The film was produced from 1963 to 1964, but the market did not react favourably. Mahé, who lost a large sum of money, accused <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universala_Esperanto-Asocio" title="Universala Esperanto-Asocio">Universala Esperanto-Asocio</a> of a boycott. At the highest point of his depression, he destroyed almost all copies: only two remain (acquired eventually by the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ch%C3%A2teau_de_Gr%C3%A9sillon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Château de Grésillon (page does not exist)">Château de Grésillon</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Esperanto_Association" title="British Esperanto Association">British Esperanto Association</a>, both already used) as well as the original, which <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=LF-koop&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="LF-koop (page does not exist)">LF-koop</a> (Kooperativo de Literatura Foiro) rescued in 1991, distributing a 61-minute <a href="/wiki/Videotape" title="Videotape">videotape</a>.</p>
