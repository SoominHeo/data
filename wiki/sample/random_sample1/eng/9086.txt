<p><b>Anna Maria Jopek</b> (born 14 December 1970 in <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a>) is a Polish musician and singer. She represented her country in the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_1997" title="Eurovision Song Contest 1997">1997 Eurovision Song Contest</a>, with the song "<a href="/wiki/Ale_jestem" title="Ale jestem">Ale jestem</a>" and finished 11th out of 25 participating acts; and in 2002, she collaborated on an album with <a href="/wiki/Jazz_guitar" title="Jazz guitar">jazz guitarist</a> <a href="/wiki/Pat_Metheny" title="Pat Metheny">Pat Metheny</a>. She has received numerous awards for her music, including <a href="/wiki/Michel_Legrand" title="Michel Legrand">Michel Legrand</a>'s Personal Award in <a href="/wiki/Vitebsk" title="Vitebsk">Vitebsk</a> in 1994, as well as all of the awards for music in Poland, together with gold and platinum records.</p>
<p>She is the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Mazowsze_(folk_group)" title="Mazowsze (folk group)">Mazowsze</a> singer <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Stanis%C5%82aw_Jopek&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Stanisław Jopek (page does not exist)">Stanisław Jopek</a> (1935–2006), known as the "First Coachman of Poland" for his signature song "Furman" (The Coachman), and former Mazowsze dancer Maria Stankiewicz. Her 1999 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christmas_album" title="Christmas album">Christmas album</a>, <i>Dzisiaj z Betleyem,</i> features two duets with her father. Anna Maria's sister, Patrycja is a violinist.</p>
<p>In 2007, she recorded the album <i>ID</i> with artists <a href="/wiki/Mino_Cinelu" title="Mino Cinelu">Mino Cinelu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christian_McBride" title="Christian McBride">Christian McBride</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Bona" title="Richard Bona">Richard Bona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Branford_Marsalis" title="Branford Marsalis">Branford Marsalis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dhafer_Youssef" title="Dhafer Youssef">Dhafer Youssef</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tord_Gustavsen" title="Tord Gustavsen">Tord Gustavsen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Castro-Neves" title="Oscar Castro-Neves">Oscar Castro-Neves</a></p>
<p>In April and May 2009, she did a tour with stops in Russia, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, Singapore and Japan.</p>
