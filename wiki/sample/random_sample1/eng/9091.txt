<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Annibale I Bentivoglio</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Annibale I Bentivoglio</b> (August 1415 – July 25, 1445) was a famous member of the <a href="/wiki/Bologna" title="Bologna">Bolognese</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bentivoglio_family" title="Bentivoglio family">Bentivoglio family</a> and the absolute ruler of the Italian city of <a href="/wiki/Bologna" title="Bologna">Bologna</a> from 1443 until his death.</p>
<p>He was a putative son of <a href="/wiki/Antongaleazzo_Bentivoglio" title="Antongaleazzo Bentivoglio">Antongaleazzo Bentivoglio</a>, although his mother, the Sienese Lina Canigiani, was said to be uncertain of the boy's paternity and the matter was decided by dice. He was named Annibale after the <a href="/wiki/Hannibal" title="Hannibal">Carthaginian general</a>. As a child, he was exiled from his native city due to his father's strong anti-Papal stance, and lived an errant life during the years of his youth. In 1438, he returned in Bologna, contributing to its liberation from the alien Milanese <a href="/wiki/House_of_Visconti" title="House of Visconti">Visconti</a> rule. In 1442 however, he was imprisoned by the <a href="/wiki/Perugia" title="Perugia">Perugian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Condotierri" title="Condotierri">condotierro</a> <a href="/wiki/Niccol%C3%B2_Piccinino" title="Niccolò Piccinino">Niccolò Piccinino</a> (at that time at war with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Francesco_Sforza" title="Francesco Sforza">Duke of Milan</a>) in the castle of Varano, from where he was later freed in 1443 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ruspoli#The_origins_of_the_Marescotti-Ruspoli" title="Ruspoli">Tideo and Galeazzo Marescotti</a>.</p>
<p>Bentivoglio was then made effective ruler of Bologna, but was stabbed to death less than two years later by a member of a <a href="/wiki/Feud" title="Feud">rival family</a>, Battista Canelloni, with the silent support of Pope <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eugene_IV" title="Eugene IV">Eugene IV</a>. He was quickly succeeded in the government of Bologna by a vague putative cousin, <a href="/wiki/Sante_Bentivoglio" title="Sante Bentivoglio">Sante Bentivoglio</a>, who was also made the sole tutor of his infant son <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_II_Bentivoglio" title="Giovanni II Bentivoglio">Giovanni</a>, born from his marriage with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Donnina_Visconti&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Donnina Visconti (page does not exist)">Donnina Visconti</a>.</p>
<p>An equestrian statue of Annibale I Bentivoglio is ascribed to the <a href="/wiki/Dalmatia" title="Dalmatia">Dalmatian</a> Early Renaissance sculptor <a href="/wiki/Niccol%C3%B2_dell%27Arca" title="Niccolò dell'Arca">Niccolò dell'Arca</a>.</p>
