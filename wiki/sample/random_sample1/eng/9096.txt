<p><b>An Dehai</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%AE%89" title="wikt:安">安</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%BE%B7" title="wikt:德">德</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%B5%B7" title="wikt:海">海</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%C4%80n" title="wikt:Ān">Ān</a> <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/D%C3%A9" title="wikt:Dé">Dé</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/h%C7%8Ei" title="wikt:hǎi">hǎi</a></span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Wade%E2%80%93Giles" title="Wade–Giles">Wade–Giles</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-wadegile" xml:lang="zh-Latn-wadegile">An Te-hai</span></i>, 1844 – 12 September 1869) was a palace <a href="/wiki/Eunuch" title="Eunuch">eunuch</a> at the imperial court of the <a href="/wiki/Qing_dynasty" title="Qing dynasty">Qing dynasty</a>. In the 1860s, he became the confidant of <a href="/wiki/Empress_Dowager_Cixi" title="Empress Dowager Cixi">Empress Dowager Cixi</a> and was subsequently executed as part of a power struggle between the empress dowager and <a href="/wiki/Prince_Gong" title="Prince Gong">Prince Gong</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rawski2001_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-rawski2001-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-haw2006_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-haw2006-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1869, Empress Dowager Cixi sent An on a mission to the Imperial Textile Factory in <a href="/wiki/Nanjing" title="Nanjing">Nanjing</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rawski2001_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-rawski2001-1">[1]</a></sup> On this trip, An travelled on the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Canal_(China)" title="Grand Canal (China)">Grand Canal</a> with a conspicuous display of imperial authority.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rawski2001_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-rawski2001-1">[1]</a></sup> This was an open violation of palace rules, which prohibited palace eunuchs from leaving the capital without authorisation on the penalty of death, so as to prevent eunuchs from gaining too much power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-haw2006_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-haw2006-2">[2]</a></sup> When An and his entourage reached <a href="/wiki/Shandong" title="Shandong">Shandong Province</a>, the governor <a href="/wiki/Ding_Baozhen" title="Ding Baozhen">Ding Baozhen</a> reported his behaviour back to the <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_City" title="Forbidden City">Forbidden City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rawski2001_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-rawski2001-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-haw2006_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-haw2006-2">[2]</a></sup> Led by Prince Gong, who disliked An, the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Council_(Qing_dynasty)" title="Grand Council (Qing dynasty)">Grand Council</a> ordered the execution of the eunuch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-haw2006_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-haw2006-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Empress_Dowager_Ci%27an" title="Empress Dowager Ci'an">Empress Dowager Ci'an</a> seemed to have supported the decision whereas Empress Dowager Cixi, who favoured An, did not intervene on the eunuch's behalf.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-haw2006_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-haw2006-2">[2]</a></sup> According to one explanation, Empress Dowager Cixi was attending a performance of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beijing_opera" title="Beijing opera">Beijing opera</a> at the time that the decision was taken and had requested not to be disturbed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-haw2006_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-haw2006-2">[2]</a></sup> As a result, An and six other eunuchs in his entourage were beheaded<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rawski2001_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-rawski2001-1">[1]</a></sup> near the Ximizhi Spring in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guandi_Temple_(Communist_Youth_League_Street,_Jinan)" title="Guandi Temple (Communist Youth League Street, Jinan)">Guandi Temple</a> in <a href="/wiki/Jinan" title="Jinan">Jinan</a>. The other members of An's group were made slaves and banished to <a href="/wiki/Heilongjiang" title="Heilongjiang">Heilongjiang</a> in the far northeastern corner of the Qing Empire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rawski2001_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-rawski2001-1">[1]</a></sup> An's execution was likely part of a broader power struggle between Empress Dowager Cixi and Prince Gong.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-haw2006_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-haw2006-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
