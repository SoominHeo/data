<p><b>Ander Herrera Agüera</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[ˈandeɾ eˈreɾa aˈɣweɾa]</a></span>; born 14 August 1989) is a Spanish professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> for <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain national team</a>.</p>
<p>He began his career at <a href="/wiki/Real_Zaragoza" title="Real Zaragoza">Real Zaragoza</a> before moving to <a href="/wiki/Athletic_Bilbao" title="Athletic Bilbao">Athletic Bilbao</a> in 2011, and then to Manchester United for €36 million in 2014.</p>
<p>He has won tournaments with Spain at <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-20_football_team" title="Spain national under-20 football team">under-20</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-21_football_team" title="Spain national under-21 football team">under-21</a> level and represented the nation at the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament">2012 Olympics</a>. He made his <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">senior</a> international debut in November 2016.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ander_Herrera&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Real_Zaragoza">Real Zaragoza</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ander_Herrera&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Real Zaragoza">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Bilbao" title="Bilbao">Bilbao</a>, Herrera began his football career at <a href="/wiki/Real_Zaragoza" title="Real Zaragoza">Real Zaragoza</a>, and made his professional debut in the <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">Segunda División</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2008–09 Segunda División">2008–09 season</a>. He made 19 appearances that season as the Aragonese club made an immediate return to <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a>. He made his top-flight debut on 29 August 2009, in a 1–0 home win against <a href="/wiki/CD_Tenerife" title="CD Tenerife">CD Tenerife</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_La_Liga" title="2009–10 La Liga">2009–10 campaign</a>, Herrera was one of Zaragoza's most used players as the club managed to retain its top-flight status. He scored his first league goal on 6 December, but in a 4–1 away defeat to <a href="/wiki/RCD_Mallorca" title="RCD Mallorca">RCD Mallorca</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_La_Liga" title="2010–11 La Liga">2010–11</a>, Herrera continued to feature regularly for Zaragoza, under both <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Aurelio_Gay" title="José Aurelio Gay">José Aurelio Gay</a> and his successor <a href="/wiki/Javier_Aguirre" title="Javier Aguirre">Javier Aguirre</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Athletic_Bilbao">Athletic Bilbao</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ander_Herrera&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Athletic Bilbao">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 7 February 2011, he agreed to join <a href="/wiki/Athletic_Bilbao" title="Athletic Bilbao">Athletic Bilbao</a> on a five-year contract for a reported €7.5 million effective as of 1 July. Buyout clauses were set at €36 million in his first three seasons and €40 million in the remainder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Herrera made his official debut for Athletic on 18 August 2011, playing the full 90 minutes in a 0–0 home draw against <a href="/wiki/Trabzonspor" title="Trabzonspor">Trabzonspor</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2011–12 UEFA Europa League">2011–12 UEFA Europa League</a>. He appeared in 54 official matches in his first year with the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)" title="Basque Country (autonomous community)">Basque</a> club (four goals), who reached both the <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a> and the Europa League finals.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manchester_United">Manchester United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ander_Herrera&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Manchester United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Herrera was the subject of a <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>24 million offer from <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> in August 2013, but the bid was rejected by <a href="/wiki/Athletic_Bilbao" title="Athletic Bilbao">Athletic Bilbao</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> A year later, Manchester United tried to sign him again. On 26 June 2014, Athletic Bilbao announced on their official website that they had rejected a €36 million (£28.85 million) bid from Manchester United for Herrera.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Athletic Bilbao later confirmed that Herrera had activated his buyout clause, allowing United to sign him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Manchester United announced on the same day they had completed the signing of Herrera on a four-year contract pending the receipt of an International Transfer Certificate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.9315_season">2014–15 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ander_Herrera&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2014–15 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Herrera made his Manchester United debut on 23 July 2014 in the first match of the club's pre-season tour of the United States, playing the full 90 minutes and making three assists in the 7–0 win over <a href="/wiki/LA_Galaxy" title="LA Galaxy">LA Galaxy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> His competitive debut came in the opening game of the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> season on 16 August, a 2–1 home defeat to <a href="/wiki/Swansea_City_A.F.C." title="Swansea City A.F.C.">Swansea City</a>. He played 67 minutes before being substituted for <a href="/wiki/Marouane_Fellaini" title="Marouane Fellaini">Marouane Fellaini</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Herrera picked up an injury during training and missed Manchester United's next two games against <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In his first game back after returning from injury, against <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a> on 14 September 2014, he scored his first goal for the club and made an assist as Manchester United won the game 4–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He scored again in United's next game, flicking in <a href="/wiki/%C3%81ngel_Di_Mar%C3%ADa" title="Ángel Di María">Ángel Di María</a>'s shot with his backheel in their 5–3 defeat to newly promoted <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a> on 21 September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Herrera provided an assist to Van Persie against Hull City on 29 November in a 3–0 win and was then awarded man of the match for his brilliant performance. He provided another assist to Marouane Fellani in a 2–1 win against Stoke City on 2 December 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Herrera scored his first <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> goal with a dipping effort in a third round tie against <a href="/wiki/Yeovil_Town_F.C." title="Yeovil Town F.C.">Yeovil Town</a> on 4 January 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Herrera followed his cup goal against Yeovil with an important equalising goal against <a href="/wiki/Preston_North_End_F.C." title="Preston North End F.C.">Preston North End</a> in the FA Cup fifth round; United would go on to win the tie 3–1 and progress to the quarter final stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Herrera started a Premier League game for the first time since 2 December against Swansea City, scoring United's only goal – his fifth of the season – in a 2–1 defeat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On 22 March 2015, he provided an assist for <a href="/wiki/Juan_Mata" title="Juan Mata">Juan Mata</a>'s goal in a 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The following fixture, Herrera scored the first brace of his career to help United beat <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a> 3–1; he was also named <a href="/wiki/Man_of_the_match" title="Man of the match">man of the match</a> for his performances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.E2.80.9316_season">2015–16 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ander_Herrera&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2015–16 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 26 August 2015, Herrera in his first start of the season, provided an assist for <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Rooney" title="Wayne Rooney">Wayne Rooney</a>'s second goal and scored the fourth goal in a 4–0 win (7–1 aggregate) over Belgian side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Brugge_K.V." title="Club Brugge K.V.">Club Brugge</a> in the second leg of their <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_UEFA_Champions_League_qualifying_phase_and_play-off_round#Play-off_round" title="2015–16 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round">Champions League play-off</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He scored his second goal of the season from the penalty spot in Manchester United's 3–1 home win over Liverpool on 12 September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2016.E2.80.9317_season">2016–17 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ander_Herrera&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2016–17 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Herrera's role initially remained limited to substitute appearances under new manager, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mourinho" title="José Mourinho">José Mourinho</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Marouane_Fellaini" title="Marouane Fellaini">Marouane Fellaini</a> preferred in midfield alongside club record signing <a href="/wiki/Paul_Pogba" title="Paul Pogba">Paul Pogba</a>. Herrera did, however, start in United's <a href="/wiki/EFL_Cup" title="EFL Cup">League Cup</a> third round tie versus <a href="/wiki/Northampton_Town_F.C." title="Northampton Town F.C.">Northampton Town</a>, and duly registered his first goal of the 2016–17 campaign in his side's 3–1 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> He was subsequently retained in the starting lineup for the Premier League game against champions <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a>, and helped his side register a comfortable 4–1 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Herrera was roundly lauded for his performances both against Leicester City and in the games that followed, and was named man of the match for helping Manchester United restrict free-scoring <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> to a 0–0 draw at <a href="/wiki/Anfield" title="Anfield">Anfield</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ander_Herrera&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Herrera was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-20_football_team" title="Spain national under-20 football team">Spain under-20</a> squad which won gold in <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2009_Mediterranean_Games" title="Football at the 2009 Mediterranean Games">football at the 2009 Mediterranean Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pescara" title="Pescara">Pescara</a>, Italy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pescara_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-pescara-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Herrera was selected by <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-21_football_team" title="Spain national under-21 football team">Spanish under-21</a> coach <a href="/wiki/Luis_Milla" title="Luis Milla">Luis Milla</a> to the <a href="/wiki/2011_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2011 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2011 UEFA European Championship</a> in Denmark. On 12 June, in the group stage opener against <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England</a>, he scored a controversial goal in an eventual 1–1 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In the final against <a href="/wiki/Switzerland_national_under-21_football_team" title="Switzerland national under-21 football team">Switzerland</a>, through another header, Herrera netted the first goal in an eventual 2–0 win in <a href="/wiki/Aarhus" title="Aarhus">Aarhus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>He received his first <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">senior</a> call-up on 3 October 2016, for <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2018 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">2018 World Cup qualification</a> fixtures against <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_football_team" title="Albania national football team">Albania</a>, but did not feature in either game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> He made his senior international debut against <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a>, at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a>, on 15 November 2016, coming on as a second-half substitute in a 2–2 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ander_Herrera&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Herrera's style of play led to comparisons with former United legend <a href="/wiki/Paul_Scholes" title="Paul Scholes">Paul Scholes</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> and was praised by the man himself as United's best signing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ander_Herrera&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Herrera's father, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Mar%C3%ADa_Herrera" title="Pedro María Herrera">Pedro María</a>, was also a footballer and a midfielder. He too played for Zaragoza and also for Celta de Vigo (in a total of three professional clubs), and at both clubs he served as general manager.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> His girlfriend is Isabel Collado.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
