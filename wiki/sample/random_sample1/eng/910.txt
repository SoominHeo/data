<p><b>Joshua Paul</b> "<b>Josh</b>" <b>Davis</b> (born June 29, 1972),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>DJ Shadow</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_production" title="Hip hop production">record producer</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DJ" title="DJ">DJ</a>. He first gained notice with the release of his highly acclaimed debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Endtroducing....." title="Endtroducing.....">Endtroducing.....</a></i>. He has a personal record collection of over 60,000 records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Shadow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Shadow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>DJ Shadow was experimenting with a four-track recorder while in high school in <a href="/wiki/Davis,_California" title="Davis, California">Davis, California</a>, and began his music career as a disc jockey for the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Davis" title="University of California, Davis">University of California, Davis</a> campus radio station <a href="/wiki/KDVS" title="KDVS">KDVS</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> During this period he was significant in developing the experimental hip-hop style associated with the London-based <a href="/wiki/Mo%27_Wax" title="Mo' Wax">Mo' Wax</a> record label. His early singles, including "In/Flux" and "Lost and Found (S.F.L.)," were genre-bending, merging elements of <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_roll" title="Rock and roll">rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ambient_music" title="Ambient music">ambient</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a>, and used-bin found records. Andy Pemberton, a music journalist, writing for <i><a href="/wiki/Mixmag" title="Mixmag">Mixmag</a></i>, coined the term "<a href="/wiki/Trip_hop" title="Trip hop">trip hop</a>" in June 1994 to describe Shadow's "In/Flux" single and similar tracks being spun in London clubs at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mixmag_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-mixmag-6">[6]</a></sup> He has cited <a href="/wiki/Kurtis_Mantronik" title="Kurtis Mantronik">Kurtis Mantronik</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steinski" title="Steinski">Steinski</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Prince_Paul_(producer)" title="Prince Paul (producer)">Prince Paul</a> as influences on his sample-based sound, further claiming that "lyrics (...) were confining, too specific."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> His music rarely features more than short clips of voices or vocal work.</p>
<p>During 1991-1992, DJ Shadow remixes were released on <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Records#Hollywood_BASIC" title="Hollywood Records">Hollywood BASIC</a>, a short-lived rap/hip-hop subsidiary label of Hollywood Records. Notable amongst these is a 1992 compilation release titled "BASIC Beats Sampler",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> which features a remix of <i>The Real Deal,</i> a song by Lifers' Group, as well as a 12-plus-minute "Mega Mix" of the rest of the album, including tracks by west-coast underground hip-hop act, <a href="/wiki/Raw_Fusion" title="Raw Fusion">Raw Fusion</a> and east-coast acts, <a href="/wiki/Organized_Konfusion" title="Organized Konfusion">Organized Konfusion</a> and Zimbabwe Legit. Also in 1992, Shadow contributed <a href="/wiki/Scratching" title="Scratching">scratching</a> and production work to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sleeping_with_the_Enemy_(Paris_album)" title="Sleeping with the Enemy (Paris album)">Sleeping with the Enemy</a>, the controversial second release by rapper <a href="/wiki/Paris_(rapper)" title="Paris (rapper)">Paris</a>.</p>
<p>Early in 1993, Shadow was a part of the creation of the <a href="/wiki/Solesides" title="Solesides">Solesides</a> underground hip-hop label, in conjunction with <a href="/wiki/Blackalicious" title="Blackalicious">Blackalicious</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lyrics_Born" title="Lyrics Born">Lyrics Born</a> (whose stage name at the time was Asia Born). The first 12" release on this new imprint was titled "Entropy,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> with the A-side containing the Asia Born track "Send Them" and a dub rendition of the DJ Shadow track "Count and Estimate," and the entire B-side consisting of the 17-plus-minute title track. Rapper <a href="/wiki/Gift_of_Gab_(rapper)" title="Gift of Gab (rapper)">Gift of Gab</a> is featured in the version of "Count and Estimate" that appears in Entropy. Shadow continued to participate in releases on the Solesides label for years to come, until the label was disbanded in favor of <a href="/wiki/Quannum_Projects" title="Quannum Projects">Quannum Projects</a>.</p>
<p>Also in 1993, <a href="/wiki/Mo%27_Wax" title="Mo' Wax">Mo' Wax</a>'s <a href="/wiki/James_Lavelle" title="James Lavelle">James Lavelle</a> contacted Shadow about releasing "In/Flux" on the fledgling imprint. The association with Mo' Wax was a productive one; his tracks "In/Flux" and "Lost and Found" made their way onto some releases over the next few years. Shadow also worked with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Krush" title="DJ Krush">DJ Krush</a> during this period. On a 1995 visit to the Mo' Wax Studios in London, England, Shadow was recruited to perform scratches on a <a href="/wiki/James_Lavelle" title="James Lavelle">James Lavelle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tim_Goldsworthy" title="Tim Goldsworthy">Tim Goldsworthy</a> mix of the <a href="/wiki/Massive_Attack" title="Massive Attack">Massive Attack</a> song <a href="/wiki/Karmacoma" title="Karmacoma">Karmacoma</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Endtroducing....."><i>Endtroducing.....</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Shadow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Endtroducing.....">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shadow's first full-length work, <i><a href="/wiki/Endtroducing....." title="Endtroducing.....">Endtroducing.....</a></i>, was released in late 1996 to critical acclaim. <i>Endtroducing</i> would go on to make the <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">Guinness World Records</a> book for "First Completely Sampled Album" in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The only pieces of equipment Shadow used to produce the album were the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MPC60" title="MPC60">AKAI MPC60</a> 12-bit sampling drum machine, a pair of turntables and a borrowed-by-visiting <a href="/wiki/Pro_Tools" title="Pro Tools">Pro Tools</a> setup from an early adopter of the technology, <a href="/wiki/Dan_the_Automator" title="Dan the Automator">Dan "The Automator" Nakamura</a>. In November 2006 <i>Time</i> magazine named it one of its "All-Time" 100 best albums.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In November 2014, automobile maker Chevrolet used a sample of DJ Shadow's "Building Steam With a Grain of Salt" in a Chevy commercial.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Preemptive_Strike"><i>Preemptive Strike</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Shadow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Preemptive Strike">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1998 Shadow released <i><a href="/wiki/Preemptive_Strike_(album)" title="Preemptive Strike (album)">Preemptive Strike</a></i>, a compilation of early <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">singles</a>. Later that year, he produced <i><a href="/wiki/Psyence_Fiction" title="Psyence Fiction">Psyence Fiction</a></i>, the debut album by <a href="/wiki/Unkle" title="Unkle">Unkle</a>, a long-time Mo' Wax production team that featured guest appearances including <a href="/wiki/Thom_Yorke" title="Thom Yorke">Thom Yorke</a> (<a href="/wiki/Radiohead" title="Radiohead">Radiohead</a>), <a href="/wiki/Richard_Ashcroft" title="Richard Ashcroft">Richard Ashcroft</a> (<a href="/wiki/The_Verve" title="The Verve">The Verve</a>), <a href="/wiki/Mike_D" title="Mike D">Mike D</a> (<a href="/wiki/Beastie_Boys" title="Beastie Boys">Beastie Boys</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kool_G._Rap" title="Kool G. Rap">Kool G. Rap</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jason_Newsted" title="Jason Newsted">Jason Newsted</a> (<a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a>), among others.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dark_Days"><i>Dark Days</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Shadow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Dark Days">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Around 2000 he produced the score for the documentary <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dark_Days_(documentary)" title="Dark Days (documentary)">Dark Days</a></i> filmed by British director <a href="/wiki/Marc_Singer_(documentarian)" title="Marc Singer (documentarian)">Marc Singer</a>. This film is about a community of transients who live underground in a subway tunnel. It has six award wins in various competitions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Private_Press"><i>The Private Press</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Shadow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The Private Press">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nearly six years after his debut production album, his second album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Private_Press" title="The Private Press">The Private Press</a></i>, was released in June 2002. In the same year<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (July 2010)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Scratch_(2001_film)" title="Scratch (2001 film)">Scratch</a></i> (2001, <a href="/wiki/Doug_Pray" title="Doug Pray">Doug Pray</a>) was released to DVD with DJ Shadow appearing several times throughout the movie. A video for his track "<a href="/wiki/Six_Days" title="Six Days">Six Days</a>" was also released in 2002, directed by <a href="/wiki/Wong_Kar-wai" title="Wong Kar-wai">Wong Kar-wai</a>. The album was mixed by <a href="/wiki/Jim_Abbiss" title="Jim Abbiss">Jim Abbiss</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Tune_and_on_Time"><i>In Tune and on Time</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Shadow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: In Tune and on Time">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, Shadow's first feature-length DVD, <i>In Tune and on Time</i>, was released. It features a live performance in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> that emphasizes visuals.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Outsider"><i>The Outsider</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Shadow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The Outsider">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Outsider_(DJ_Shadow_album)" title="The Outsider (DJ Shadow album)">The Outsider</a></i> album was released on 18 September 2006. A special edition CD box set was also released containing <i>The Outsider</i>, the album on CD and a DVD entitled Tour Visuals. <i>The Outsider</i>, which prominently featured several artists from the local <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay_Area" title="San Francisco Bay Area">San Francisco Bay Area</a> <a href="/wiki/Hyphy" title="Hyphy">hyphy</a> <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> movement, got a mixed welcome among Shadow's fans. Responding to criticisms, the DJ/producer explained on his blog why he made no apologies: <i>"Repeat</i> Endtroducing <i>over and over again? That was never, ever in the game plan. Fuck that. So I think it's time for certain fans to decide if they are fans of the album, or the artist."</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> DJ Shadow has also collaborated with fellow DJ <a href="/wiki/Cut_Chemist" title="Cut Chemist">Cut Chemist</a>. Together they have created three popular mixtapes entitled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brainfreeze_(album)" title="Brainfreeze (album)">Brainfreeze</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Product_Placement_(album)" title="Product Placement (album)">Product Placement</a></i>, and the recent <i>The Hard Sell</i>. These mixes fuse jazz, funk, and soul. He has also collaborated with several other artists, including <a href="/wiki/Blackalicious" title="Blackalicious">Blackalicious</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zack_de_la_Rocha" title="Zack de la Rocha">Zack de la Rocha</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keak_Da_Sneak" title="Keak Da Sneak">Keak Da Sneak</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mos_Def" title="Mos Def">Mos Def</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Banner" title="David Banner">David Banner</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dr._Octagon" title="Dr. Octagon">Dr. Octagon</a>, an alter ego of <a href="/wiki/Kool_Keith" title="Kool Keith">Kool Keith</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2006, he signed a deal with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Records" title="Universal Records">Universal Records</a>. DJ Shadow and Cut Chemist toured in 2008 in support of their mixtape <i>The Hard Sell</i> with <a href="/wiki/Kid_Koala" title="Kid Koala">Kid Koala</a> opening for them.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Diminishing_Returns"><i>Diminishing Returns</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Shadow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Diminishing Returns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Diminishing Returns was a 2-hour <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DJ_Mix" title="DJ Mix">DJ Mix</a> originally broadcast on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a> on March 29, 2003. It was released in 2003 on CD in a pressing limited to 1000 copies on the Reconstruction Productions label.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Reconstruction Productions reissued the album in 2009 with a sticker stating "official, last chance limited edition, re-release".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Less_You_Know.2C_the_Better"><i>The Less You Know, the Better</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Shadow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: The Less You Know, the Better">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During Shadow's Outsider 2007 tour, he was spotted filming with Dean Fernando (aka "DINO") as reported on Shadow/Quannum fansite <a href="/wiki/Solesides" title="Solesides">Solesides</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> On April 1, 2009, DJ Shadow announced on his <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> page that he was working on a <i>Diminishing Returns</i> re-release.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On March 7, 2009, Shadow announced that he was working on new music. Though no release date was given, he assured, "It will sound different from the last one."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> DJ Shadow released a track from the new album called "Def Surrounds Us" as a preview. While on the 2010 "Shadowsphere" tour new songs began to emerge, although he stated that a new album was not yet ready and that he would continue working toward having it ready in time for his 2011 tour.</p>
<p>On May 10, 2011, Shadow confirmed via his Facebook page and official website that his new album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Less_You_Know,_the_Better" title="The Less You Know, the Better">The Less You Know, the Better</a></i>, was finished and that it would be released the following September on Verve/Universal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The same day, he also released an EP called <i>I Gotta Rokk</i> featuring remixed tracks from the forthcoming album. A second single "I'm Excited" was also released, featuring <a href="/wiki/Afrikan_Boy" title="Afrikan Boy">Afrikan Boy</a>, on July 29, 2011. However the single was pulled because of copyright infringement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The infringement also delayed the album's release. Another song from the album, titled "Warning Call," featuring <a href="/wiki/Tom_Vek" title="Tom Vek">Tom Vek</a>, was released on 7 September 2011, and was featured as a free <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">download</a> on the official DJ Shadow Facebook page. Shadow announced on September 30, 2011 via Twitter that <i>The Less You Know, the Better</i> would be released on a 3xUSB format.</p>
<p>The artwork for this album, the associated singles and promotional material was done by Tony Papesh, an artist from San Francisco, California.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The album was mixed by <a href="/wiki/Jim_Abbiss" title="Jim Abbiss">Jim Abbiss</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Liquid_Amber">Liquid Amber</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Shadow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Liquid Amber">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On August 12, 2014, Shadow launched his new record label imprint <b>Liquid Amber</b>, releasing <i><a href="/wiki/The_Liquid_Amber_EP" title="The Liquid Amber EP">The Liquid Amber EP</a></i> for streaming in accompaniment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Other acts signed to Liquid Amber include Bleep Bloop and Ruckazoid.</p>
<p>In April 2015, a group called <b>Nite School Klik</b> released a single called "Posse" on Liquid Amber. In May 2015, Shadow revealed to <i>Billboard</i> that the group was in fact himself and <a href="/wiki/Trap_music" title="Trap music">trap</a> producer G Jones working together.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The first self-titled EP by Nite School Klik was released on June 9, 2015.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Mountain_Will_Fall"><i>The Mountain Will Fall</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Shadow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: The Mountain Will Fall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2016 Shadow announced a new album entitled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mountain_Will_Fall" title="The Mountain Will Fall">The Mountain Will Fall</a></i>, which was released on June 24.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Studio_and_equipment">Studio and equipment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Shadow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Studio and equipment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The book <i><a href="/wiki/Behind_the_Beat" title="Behind the Beat">Behind the Beat</a></i> documents Shadow's home studio (named "Reconstruction") through photos and a brief description (as of 2005).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BEHINDTHEBEAT_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-BEHINDTHEBEAT-25">[25]</a></sup> It is "nestled within a leafy San Francisco suburb"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BEHINDTHEBEAT_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-BEHINDTHEBEAT-25">[25]</a></sup> and Shadow apparently moved to the area "to be closer to the local record store".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BEHINDTHEBEAT_25-2"><a href="#cite_note-BEHINDTHEBEAT-25">[25]</a></sup> It also states that "Shadow only keeps a small selection of vinyl at home with the rest occupying storage units around town".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BEHINDTHEBEAT_25-3"><a href="#cite_note-BEHINDTHEBEAT-25">[25]</a></sup> His equipment as of 2005, according to <i>Behind the Beat</i>, consists of two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Akai_MPC" title="Akai MPC">Akai MPC</a> 3000s and a <a href="/wiki/Korg_Triton" title="Korg Triton">Korg Triton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BEHINDTHEBEAT_25-4"><a href="#cite_note-BEHINDTHEBEAT-25">[25]</a></sup> His album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Endtroducing" title="Endtroducing">Endtroducing</a></i> was "created on an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MPC60" title="MPC60">AKAI MPC60</a> and an <a href="/wiki/ADAT" title="ADAT">ADAT</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BEHINDTHEBEAT_25-5"><a href="#cite_note-BEHINDTHEBEAT-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sampling">Sampling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Shadow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Sampling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>DJ Shadow's work involves manipulating samples, taking rare and seldom-heard pieces of music and reworking them into parts and phrases for his songs. Many of his tracks feature dozens of samples from a wide array of styles and influences including rock, soul, funk, experimental, electronic and jazz. Shadow famously sampled American composer <a href="/wiki/David_Axelrod_(musician)" title="David Axelrod (musician)">David Axelrod</a> twice: the piano in "<a href="/wiki/Midnight_in_a_Perfect_World" title="Midnight in a Perfect World">Midnight in a Perfect World</a>" from 1996's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Endtroducing" title="Endtroducing">Endtroducing</a></i> was originally recorded for Axelrod's "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Human_Abstract_(song)" title="The Human Abstract (song)">The Human Abstract</a>"; and on his collaboration with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Krush" title="DJ Krush">DJ Krush</a>, "Duality", sampling Axelrod's "The Warnings" (Part 1). Although Shadow currently uses fewer samples than on his earlier albums, several examples feature on 2006 album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Outsider_(DJ_Shadow_album)" title="The Outsider (DJ Shadow album)">The Outsider</a></i>. His collaboration with <a href="/wiki/David_Banner" title="David Banner">David Banner</a>, "Seein' Thangs", features a <a href="/wiki/Synthesizer" title="Synthesizer">synthesizer</a> and vocals from Cecilia's "Crimson Red" and "Outsider Intro" includes an excerpt from <a href="/wiki/Ron_Geesin" title="Ron Geesin">Ron Geesin</a>'s "Concrete Line Up" from the 1973 album, <i>As He Stands</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
