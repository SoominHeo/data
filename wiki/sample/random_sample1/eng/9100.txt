<p><b>Narisanka</b>, better known by his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hellenized" title="Hellenized">Hellenized</a> name of <b>Andragoras</b> (died 238 BCE) was an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iranian_people" title="Iranian people">Iranian</a> nobleman who served as the <a href="/wiki/Seleucid_Empire" title="Seleucid Empire">Seleucid</a> <a href="/wiki/Satrap" title="Satrap">satrap</a> of the province of Parthia under the Seleucid rulers <a href="/wiki/Antiochus_I_Soter" title="Antiochus I Soter">Antiochus I Soter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antiochus_II_Theos" title="Antiochus II Theos">Antiochus II Theos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Farrokh_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Farrokh-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Andragoras proclaimed independence from the Seleucid Empire in 247–245 BCE, at a time when the Seleucids were embroiled in conflict with <a href="/wiki/Ptolemaic_Kingdom" title="Ptolemaic Kingdom">Ptolemaic</a> Egypt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Farrokh_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Farrokh-1">[1]</a></sup> He revolted soon after the <a href="/wiki/Greco-Bactrian_Kingdom" title="Greco-Bactrian Kingdom">Greco-Bactrian Kingdom</a> had broken away from the Seleucids, and Andragorus may have been allied with the new Bactrian king, <a href="/wiki/Diodotus_I" title="Diodotus I">Diodotus I</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Farrokh_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Farrokh-1">[1]</a></sup> In defiance, he issued coins in which he wears the royal diadem as well as his name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Andragoras ruled only for a few years, before being <a href="/wiki/Parni_conquest_of_Parthia" title="Parni conquest of Parthia">vanquished and killed</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Parni" title="Parni">Parni</a> led by <a href="/wiki/Arsaces_I_of_Parthia" title="Arsaces I of Parthia">Arsaces</a> around 238 BCE, who went on to create the <a href="/wiki/Parthian_Empire" title="Parthian Empire">Parthian Empire</a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Farrokh_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Farrokh-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dd>"He (Arsaces) was used to a life of pillage and theft, when he heard about the defeat of Seleucus against the Gauls. Relieved from his fear of the king, he attacked the Parthians with a band of thieves, vanquished their prefect Andragoras, and, after having killed him took the power over the nation" (<i>"Hic solitus latrociniis et rapto uiuere accepta opinione Seleucum a Gallis in Asia uictum, solutus regis metu, cum praedonum manu Parthos ingressus praefectum eorum Andragoran oppressit sublatoque eo imperium gentis inuasit"</i>) <a class="external text" href="http://www.forumromanum.org/literature/justin/texte41.html" rel="nofollow">Justin, xli. 4</a>.</dd>
</dl>
