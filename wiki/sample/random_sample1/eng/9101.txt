<p><b>André Lauren Benjamin</b> (born May 27, 1975),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bdate_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-bdate-1">[1]</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>André 3000</b> (formerly known as <b>André</b>), is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, record producer, dancer, and actor, best known for being part of <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> duo <a href="/wiki/Outkast" title="Outkast">Outkast</a> alongside fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/Big_Boi" title="Big Boi">Big Boi</a>. As an actor, Benjamin has made appearances in a number of TV series and films, including <i><a href="/wiki/Families_(TV_series)" title="Families (TV series)">Families</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Shield" title="The Shield">The Shield</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Be_Cool" title="Be Cool">Be Cool</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Revolver_(2005_film)" title="Revolver (2005 film)">Revolver</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Semi-Pro" title="Semi-Pro">Semi-Pro</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Four_Brothers_(film)" title="Four Brothers (film)">Four Brothers</a></i>, and the leading role of <a href="/wiki/Jimi_Hendrix" title="Jimi Hendrix">Jimi Hendrix</a> in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_Is_by_My_Side" title="All Is by My Side">All Is by My Side</a></i>. In addition to music and acting, Benjamin has also been an active entrepreneur. In the spring of 2008, he launched a clothing line called Benjamin Bixby. He has also been an advocate for <a href="/wiki/Animal_rights" title="Animal rights">animal rights</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-virginmedia.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-virginmedia.com-2">[2]</a></sup> He is also known for his work on the <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a> animated series <i><a href="/wiki/Class_of_3000" title="Class of 3000">Class of 3000</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2013, <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a></i> included Benjamin on its list of the 10 best rappers of the 2000s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2015, <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> included Benjamin on its list of the 10 Greatest Rappers of All Time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_3000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Benjamin was born in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Only_child" title="Only child">only child</a> of Sharon Benjamin-Hodo (d. 2013), a <a href="/wiki/Single_parent" title="Single parent">single mother</a> who sold real estate, and Lawrence Harvey Walker (d. 2014), a collections agent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He is of <a href="/wiki/African_Americans" title="African Americans">African-American</a> and <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a> descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Growing up in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" title="Atlanta, Georgia">Atlanta, Georgia</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Point,_Georgia" title="East Point, Georgia">East Point, Georgia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bankhead,_Atlanta" title="Bankhead, Atlanta">Bankhead, Georgia</a>, he attended Sarah Smith Elementary School, Sutton Middle School, Northside High School and Tri-Cities High School.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_3000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Outkast:_1991.E2.80.932007.3B_2014">Outkast: 1991–2007; 2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_3000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Outkast: 1991–2007; 2014">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In high school, Benjamin (who was then performing as "Andre") met <a href="/wiki/Big_Boi" title="Big Boi">Antwan "Big Boi" Patton</a>. Benjamin and Patton teamed up to form Outkast. Shortly after graduating from high school, the duo was signed to the Atlanta-based <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LaFace" title="LaFace">LaFace</a> label and released their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Southernplayalisticadillacmuzik" title="Southernplayalisticadillacmuzik">Southernplayalisticadillacmuzik</a></i>, in 1994. Buoyed by the success of the single "Player's Ball", the album went <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Platinum_record" title="Platinum record">platinum</a> by the end of the year and Outkast was named Best New Rap Group of the Year at the 1995 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Source_Awards" title="Source Awards">Source Awards</a>. On their next two albums, <i><a href="/wiki/ATLiens" title="ATLiens">ATLiens</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Aquemini" title="Aquemini">Aquemini</a></i>, Outkast experimented with their sound by adding elements of <a href="/wiki/Trip_hop" title="Trip hop">trip hop</a>, soul, and <a href="/wiki/Oldschool_jungle" title="Oldschool jungle">jungle</a>. The albums were also influenced by a return to traditional black music genres, funk being the prime example. With Outkast's portrayal of themselves as out-of-place extraterrestrials in ATLiens, "the funkadelic, futuristic, and seemingly unfamiliar, weird, or eccentric persona projected by André 3000 creates the chance to transcend the more pronounced characterizations of gangstas and pimps so regularly assumed by black men rap artists."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Outkast's distinctive southern "player" style is combined with these <a href="/wiki/Afrofuturism" title="Afrofuturism">Afrofuturistic</a> elements to create a new, unique space for their brand of rap within popular culture. Benjamin's lyrics, in particular, took on a more <a href="/wiki/Surrealism" title="Surrealism">surreal</a>, space-age tinge. Within the time of his second and third albums, Benjamin took up the guitar, painting, and a new relationship with singer <a href="/wiki/Erykah_Badu" title="Erykah Badu">Erykah Badu</a>. Outkast's fourth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Stankonia" title="Stankonia">Stankonia</a></i>, introduced Benjamin's new alias André 3000 (largely to distinguish himself from <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a> and his new persona after the end of his relationship) and increased the group's crossover appeal with the single "<a href="/wiki/Ms._Jackson" title="Ms. Jackson">Ms. Jackson</a>", which hit number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. The song was written in the aftermath of Benjamin's breakup with Badu and was a fictionalized account of the disintegration of their relationship. In 2002, Outkast released a greatest hits album, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Big_Boi_and_Dre_Present..._OutKast" title="Big Boi and Dre Present... OutKast">Big Boi and Dre Present... OutKast</a></i> which contained three new tracks. One track, "<a href="/wiki/The_Whole_World" title="The Whole World">The Whole World</a>", won a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy" title="Grammy">Grammy</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Rap_Performance_by_a_Duo_or_Group" title="Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group">Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group</a>. Later that year, Benjamin participated in the <a href="/wiki/Dungeon_Family" title="Dungeon Family">Dungeon Family</a> group album, which saw a number of prominent Atlanta-based hip-hop groups combine into a <a href="/wiki/Supergroup_(music)" title="Supergroup (music)">supergroup</a>. In 2002, André 3000 was referenced on the song "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Till_I_Collapse" title="Till I Collapse">Till I Collapse</a>" by Eminem, who considered him one of the best rappers ever.</p>
<p>In 2003, Outkast released <i><a href="/wiki/Speakerboxxx/The_Love_Below" title="Speakerboxxx/The Love Below">Speakerboxxx/The Love Below</a></i>, a double album which highlighted the differences in the musical styles of the group's two members. Benjamin's half of the album, <i>The Love Below</i>, garnered the most attention from mainstream audiences, with the popular singles "<a href="/wiki/Hey_Ya!" title="Hey Ya!">Hey Ya!</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Roses_(Outkast_song)" title="Roses (Outkast song)">Roses</a>". The album's fourth single and <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">video</a> (Benjamin's third), "Prototype", was released shortly after. Big Boi's <i>Speakerboxxx</i> spawned the top ten hit "<a href="/wiki/The_Way_You_Move" title="The Way You Move">The Way You Move</a>" and the relatively successful "Ghetto Musick". <i>The Love Below</i> was, unlike <i>Speakerboxxx</i>, an exercise in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Funk_music" title="Funk music">funk</a>, jazz, and <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">alternative</a> music, featuring sung vocals from Benjamin (instead of rapped). <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> compared Benjamin to an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indie-rock" title="Indie-rock">indie-rock</a> <a href="/wiki/Little_Richard" title="Little Richard">Little Richard</a>" on Outkast's 2003 Number 1 international hit '<a href="/wiki/Hey_Ya!" title="Hey Ya!">Hey Ya!</a>" and later declared the hit one of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_500_Greatest_Songs_of_All_Time" title="The 500 Greatest Songs of All Time">The 500 Greatest Songs of All Time</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In 2006, Outkast released their sixth album as a group, <i><a href="/wiki/Idlewild_(Outkast_album)" title="Idlewild (Outkast album)">Idlewild</a></i>. The album served as a soundtrack to the group's musical film <i><a href="/wiki/Idlewild_(film)" title="Idlewild (film)">Idlewild</a></i>. The film centered on life in a 1930s setting and the album took influences from music of that era, particularly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blues_music" title="Blues music">blues</a>. Benjamin had a few rapped verses on the album, including on the first single "<a href="/wiki/Mighty_O" title="Mighty O">Mighty O</a>", but mostly stuck with singing as he had on <i>The Love Below</i>. The album actually postponed the release of the theatrical <i><a href="/wiki/Idlewild_(film)" title="Idlewild (film)">Idlewild</a>, as Andre 3000</i> and <i>Big Boi</i> were concentrated more on the production of the music than the movie. In an odd turn of events, the movie was finished before the album was, and because of that, the movie was postponed about a year. This was because the crew of Outkast and its associates' had developed the idea of creating a movie before the release of one of their early albums, <i>Aquemini.</i> Thus, when it finally came time to collaborate as a group on the movie, they had most of the details worked out, including a script, as stated by <i>Andre 3000</i> himself in an interview he had conducted with <i>Billboard</i> in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2014, it was announced that Outkast would be reuniting to celebrate their 20th anniversary by performing at more than 40 festivals worldwide during the spring/summer of 2014, beginning with a headline spot at the <a href="/wiki/Coachella_Valley_Music_and_Arts_Festival" title="Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival">Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival</a> in <a href="/wiki/Indio,_California" title="Indio, California">Indio, California</a> in April.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_work:_2007.E2.80.93present">Solo work: 2007–present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_3000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Solo work: 2007–present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Benjamin returned to rapping in 2007, after a hiatus from the genre. Appearing on various <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remixes</a>, including: "<a href="/wiki/Walk_It_Out_(Unk_song)" title="Walk It Out (Unk song)">Walk It Out</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Throw_Some_D%27s" title="Throw Some D's">Throw Some D's</a>", "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/You_(Lloyd_Song)" title="You (Lloyd Song)">You</a>" and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>'s "30 Something." His contributions to original songs included <a href="/wiki/UGK" title="UGK">UGK</a>'s "International Players Anthem", <a href="/wiki/Devin_the_Dude" title="Devin the Dude">Devin the Dude</a>'s "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/What_a_Job" title="What a Job">What a Job</a>", <a href="/wiki/Fonzworth_Bentley" title="Fonzworth Bentley">Fonzworth Bentley</a>'s "Everybody", and with Big Boi and Raekwon on "<a href="/wiki/Royal_Flush_(song)" title="Royal Flush (song)">Royal Flush</a>". Benjamin worked with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Q-Tip" title="Q-Tip">Q-Tip</a> in the song "BE BRAVE" that was supposed to be released on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Q-Tip" title="Q-Tip">Q-Tip</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Renaissance_(Q-Tip_album)" title="The Renaissance (Q-Tip album)">The Renaissance</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He also appeared on <a href="/wiki/John_Legend" title="John Legend">John Legend</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Evolver_(John_Legend_album)" title="Evolver (John Legend album)">Evolver</a></i>, on the track "<a href="/wiki/Green_Light_(John_Legend_song)" title="Green Light (John Legend song)">Green Light</a>", which was released on October 28, 2008. Prior to the release, Benjamin commented: "It's going to be a surprise for a lot of John Legend fans because it is a lot more upbeat than John is—than people think John is. I was actually happy to hear it. This is a cool John Legend song." Benjamin has stated that he plans on recording a solo rap album and that the response to his remixes is part of the motivation for it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In 2010, he was featured on <a href="/wiki/Ciara" title="Ciara">Ciara</a>'s remix for her single "<a href="/wiki/Ride_(Ciara_song)" title="Ride (Ciara song)">Ride</a>", from the album <i><a href="/wiki/Basic_Instinct_(album)" title="Basic Instinct (album)">Basic Instinct</a></i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and on the remix to <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Deuces_(song)" title="Deuces (song)">Deuces</a>".</p>
<p>On January 18, 2011, Kesha released the "The Sleazy Remix", which featured Benjamin. The remix was later re-released on December 13, 2011, also featuring verses by rappers <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a> and <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On June 7, <a href="/wiki/Big_Boi" title="Big Boi">Big Boi</a> leaked a collaboration with Benjamin and <a href="/wiki/Sleepy_Brown" title="Sleepy Brown">Sleepy Brown</a> entitled "Lookin' 4 Ya" – which was originally intended for <a href="/wiki/Big_Boi" title="Big Boi">Big Boi</a>'s debut solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Sir_Lucious_Left_Foot:_The_Son_of_Chico_Dusty" title="Sir Lucious Left Foot: The Son of Chico Dusty">Sir Lucious Left Foot: The Son of Chico Dusty</a></i>, however, it was blocked by <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a> from being on the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>On June 24, 2011, Beyoncé's fourth studio album entitled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/4_(Beyonc%C3%A9_Knowles_album)" title="4 (Beyoncé Knowles album)">4</a></i> was released. Benjamin featured on the track "Party", his first collaboration with the singer. The song was later nominated for the Award for Best Rap/Sung Collaboration at the Grammy Awards held in 2012. Benjamin continued his string of guest appearances throughout the year – featuring on Lloyd's song "<a href="/wiki/Dedication_to_My_Ex_(Miss_That)" title="Dedication to My Ex (Miss That)">Dedication to My Ex (Miss That)</a>", Lil Wayne's "Interlude" and Drake's "The Real Her". He also featured on the track "<a href="/wiki/Play_the_Guitar" title="Play the Guitar">Play the Guitar</a>" by <a href="/wiki/B.o.B" title="B.o.B">B.o.B</a>.</p>
<p>Benjamin was featured alongside <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> on <a href="/wiki/Young_Jeezy" title="Young Jeezy">Young Jeezy</a>'s track "<a href="/wiki/I_Do_(Young_Jeezy_song)" title="I Do (Young Jeezy song)">I Do</a>", released on January 10, 2012. It later appeared on Young Jeezy's fourth studio album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thug_Motivation_103:_Hustlerz_Ambition" title="Thug Motivation 103: Hustlerz Ambition">Thug Motivation 103: Hustlerz Ambition</a></i>. Benjamin's verse, which was initially recorded as a song for his debut solo album, was leaked onto the Internet in 2010. The following month, it was announced that Benjamin would be featured on a song with LCD Soundsystem frontman <a href="/wiki/James_Murphy_(electronic_musician)" title="James Murphy (electronic musician)">James Murphy</a> and the alternative rock virtual band <a href="/wiki/Gorillaz" title="Gorillaz">Gorillaz</a> titled "<a href="/wiki/DoYaThing" title="DoYaThing">DoYaThing</a>", to promote the Gorillaz <a href="/wiki/Converse_(shoe_company)" title="Converse (shoe company)">Converse</a> collection. It was released on February 23, 2012, in two different versions – one approximately 4-and-a-half-minute radio edit and the explicit 13-minute version. The song was written and recorded over three days, with the 13-minute version being recorded on the last one.</p>
<p>On July 10, 2012, <i><a href="/wiki/Channel_Orange" title="Channel Orange">Channel Orange</a></i>, the debut album by singer-songwriter and <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future</a> member <a href="/wiki/Frank_Ocean" title="Frank Ocean">Frank Ocean</a> was released. Benjamin was featured on the song "Pink Matter," supplying vocals and guitar. Later, on July 30, 2012, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/God_Forgives,_I_Don%27t" title="God Forgives, I Don't">God Forgives, I Don't</a></i> was released where Benjamin appeared on the song "Sixteen", once again supplying vocals and guitar. Later in the year, Benjamin also featured on <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Trouble_Man:_Heavy_Is_the_Head" title="Trouble Man: Heavy Is the Head">Trouble Man: Heavy Is the Head</a></i> on the song "Sorry". <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a> noted that the collaboration was a "proud moment" for him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 6, 2013, the <a href="/wiki/The_Great_Gatsby:_Music_from_Baz_Luhrmann%27s_Film" title="The Great Gatsby: Music from Baz Luhrmann's Film">Great Gatsby Soundtrack</a> was released, featuring a cover by Benjamin and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9_Knowles" title="Beyoncé Knowles">Beyoncé</a> of <a href="/wiki/Amy_Winehouse" title="Amy Winehouse">Amy Winehouse</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Back_to_Black_(song)" title="Back to Black (song)">Back to Black</a>, marking their second collaboration. The following month, <a href="/wiki/Capital_Cities_(band)" title="Capital Cities (band)">Capital Cities</a> released their debut album <a href="/wiki/In_a_Tidal_Wave_of_Mystery" title="In a Tidal Wave of Mystery">In a Tidal Wave of Mystery</a> on which Benjamin was featured on the song "Farrah Fawcett Hair".'</p>
<p>After being seen in the studio with producer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mike_WiLL_Made_It" title="Mike WiLL Made It">Mike WiLL Made It</a>, it was reported on August 3, 2013, that Andre 3000 would be releasing a new solo album in early 2014. However, the following day a representative for Benjamin told <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a> that "there is no official confirmation on that report."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In 2014, Benjamin appeared on fellow <a href="/wiki/Dungeon_Family" title="Dungeon Family">Dungeon Family</a> member <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a>'s second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Honest_(Future_album)" title="Honest (Future album)">Honest</a></i>, on a song titled "Benz Friendz (Whatchutola)".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2015, Benjamin made a surprise guest appearance on the track "Hello" from <a href="/wiki/Erykah_Badu" title="Erykah Badu">Erykah Badu</a>'s mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/But_You_Caint_Use_My_Phone" title="But You Caint Use My Phone">But You Caint Use My Phone</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, Benjamin presented background vocals on the track "30 Hours" by <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a> off his album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Life_of_Pablo" title="The Life of Pablo">The Life of Pablo</a></i>. Earlier that year, Benjamin revealed that he was in the process of recording new music. He also appeared on the song "Solo (Reprise)" by <a href="/wiki/Frank_Ocean" title="Frank Ocean">Frank Ocean</a> off his album <i><a href="/wiki/Blonde_(Frank_Ocean_album)" title="Blonde (Frank Ocean album)">Blonde</a></i>. In the song, Andre is disappointed with the direction that hip-hop has taken since he first entered the rap game over 20 years ago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Benjamin also appeared on the track, "the ends" on Travis Scott's second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Birds_in_the_Trap_Sing_McKnight" title="Birds in the Trap Sing McKnight">Birds in the Trap Sing McKnight</a></i>. Also in 2016, he appears on the song "Kids..." By <a href="/wiki/A_Tribe_Called_Quest" title="A Tribe Called Quest">A Tribe Called Quest</a> from the album "<a href="/wiki/We_Got_It_from_Here..._Thank_You_4_Your_Service" title="We Got It from Here... Thank You 4 Your Service">We Got It from Here... Thank You 4 Your Service</a>" He also featured on 2 songs on Kid Cudi's 2016 album Passion, Pain, and Demon Slayin'.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_3000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Benjamin has made appearances in <i><a href="/wiki/Families_(TV_series)" title="Families (TV series)">Families</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Shield" title="The Shield">The Shield</a></i> (he plays Robert Huggins, a character that originated in an episode titled "On Tilt" from Season 3 in 2004), <i><a href="/wiki/Be_Cool" title="Be Cool">Be Cool</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Revolver_(2005_film)" title="Revolver (2005 film)">Revolver</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Semi-Pro" title="Semi-Pro">Semi Pro</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Four_Brothers_(film)" title="Four Brothers (film)">Four Brothers</a></i>. He was also cast as Percival in <a href="/wiki/Idlewild_(film)" title="Idlewild (film)"><i>Idlewild</i></a>, released on August 26, 2006. He voiced a crow in <i><a href="/wiki/Charlotte%27s_Web_(2006_film)" title="Charlotte's Web (2006 film)">Charlotte's Web</a></i>, a movie adaptation of the <a href="/wiki/Charlotte%27s_Web" title="Charlotte's Web">1952 children's book</a>. As of November 2006, he voiced "Sunny Bridges," a prize-winning musician who gives up touring to teach at his alma mater, in <i><a href="/wiki/Class_of_3000" title="Class of 3000">Class of 3000</a></i>, an animated series he produced on <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a>. Benjamin was accused by Timothy Mcgee for supposedly 'stealing' his idea of the show. He has also worked with <a href="/wiki/Esthero" title="Esthero">Esthero</a> on a promotional version of "Jungle Book" which was on a <i>Wikked lil' grrrls</i> sampler, but never made it to the actual album due to issues with Esthero's label, <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Bros.</a>. The following year, he appeared in the basketball comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Semi-Pro" title="Semi-Pro">Semi-Pro</a></i>, with <a href="/wiki/Woody_Harrelson" title="Woody Harrelson">Woody Harrelson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Will_Ferrell" title="Will Ferrell">Will Ferrell</a>. He also starred in the 2007 film <i><a href="/wiki/Battle_in_Seattle" title="Battle in Seattle">Battle in Seattle</a></i>, a film about the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WTO_Ministerial_Conference_of_1999_protest_activity" title="WTO Ministerial Conference of 1999 protest activity">1999 Seattle World Trade Organization protests</a>. Benjamin was a member of <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino" title="Quentin Tarantino">Quentin Tarantino</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lawrence_Bender" title="Lawrence Bender">Lawrence Bender</a>'s production company <a href="/wiki/A_Band_Apart" title="A Band Apart">A Band Apart</a> until its close in 2006; he then formed his own company, Moxie Turtle. In May 2012, Benjamin began filming a biographical film about <a href="/wiki/Jimi_Hendrix" title="Jimi Hendrix">Jimi Hendrix</a> entitled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_Is_By_My_Side" title="All Is By My Side">All Is By My Side</a>.</i> It premiered at the <a href="/wiki/2013_Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="2013 Toronto International Film Festival">2013 Toronto International Film Festival</a> on September 7, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> His performance was met with critical acclaim and earned him a nomination for the <a href="/wiki/Independent_Spirit_Award_for_Best_Male_Lead" title="Independent Spirit Award for Best Male Lead">Independent Spirit Award for Best Male Lead</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In 2016, Benjamin had a recurring role as Michael LaCroix on the second season of the anthology crime drama series <i><a href="/wiki/American_Crime_(TV_series)" title="American Crime (TV series)">American Crime</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fashion">Fashion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_3000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Fashion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Benjamin launched the "Benjamin Bixby" clothing line in the spring of 2008, inspired by college football circa 1935.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> In February 2012, Benjamin announced that he would be returning with another line soon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_3000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2004, alongside <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Silverstone" title="Alicia Silverstone">Alicia Silverstone</a>, Benjamin was voted by <a href="/wiki/People_for_the_Ethical_Treatment_of_Animals" title="People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals">People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals</a> (PETA) to be the 'World's Sexiest Vegetarian Celebrity'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-virginmedia.com_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-virginmedia.com-2">[2]</a></sup> He is also childhood friends with <a href="/wiki/Fonzworth_Bentley" title="Fonzworth Bentley">Fonzworth Bentley</a>. Benjamin also has a son named Seven Sirius Benjamin (born November 18, 1997) with <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Erykah_Badu" title="Erykah Badu">Erykah Badu</a>. Benjamin was arrested in <a href="/wiki/Henry_County,_Georgia" title="Henry County, Georgia">Henry County, Georgia</a> in March 2009 after being caught driving his 2007 Porsche Carrera 109 mph in a 65 mph speed zone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Benjamin was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vegan" title="Vegan">vegan</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> for fifteen years. By 2014, he had given it up stating:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>I was a hardcore vegan for fifteen years. I've even done raw. But socially it became horrible. I was kind of just sitting at home eating a salad. You become mean. That's not good for you.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Benjamin posed for a print advertising campaign by <a href="/wiki/Declare_Yourself" title="Declare Yourself">Declare Yourself</a>, a campaign encouraging voter registration among youth for the <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_2008" title="United States presidential election, 2008">2008 United States presidential election</a>. In the ads by photographer <a href="/wiki/David_LaChapelle" title="David LaChapelle">David LaChapelle</a>, he has his mouth gagged by a bow-tie in a symbolic function.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
