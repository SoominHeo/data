<p><b>André Francisco Moritz</b>, or simply <b>André Moritz</b> (born 6 August 1986), is a Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_people" title="Italian people">Italian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a> descent who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">forward</a> for <a href="/wiki/Denizlispor" title="Denizlispor">Denizlispor</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Moritz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moritz began his career with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ava%C3%AD_Futebol_Clube" title="Avaí Futebol Clube">Avaí FC</a>'s youth categories, signing a professional contract with <a href="/wiki/Sport_Club_Internacional" title="Sport Club Internacional">SC Internacional</a> in 2003, aged 16. He was assigned to the academy and was top goal scorer in most of the Youth tournaments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He was promoted to first team in 2006, playing some matches in State League.</p>
<p>Due to limited chances, Moritz signed a contract with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fluminense_Football_Club" title="Fluminense Football Club">Fluminense FC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He helped the club to avoid relegation in the last game of the season, and also to win the <a href="/wiki/2007_Copa_do_Brasil" title="2007 Copa do Brasil">2007 Copa do Brasil</a>. Moritz left <i>Flu</i> and signed a contract with Turkish club <a href="/wiki/Kas%C4%B1mpa%C5%9Fa_S.K." title="Kasımpaşa S.K.">Kasımpaşa</a>, in 2007. After three full seasons in Kasımpaşa, he signed a three-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Kayserispor" title="Kayserispor">Kayserispor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> And signed a two-year contract with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mersin_%C4%B0dmanyurdu_SK" title="Mersin İdmanyurdu SK">Mersin</a> in 2011 summer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Moritz then went on trial at <a href="/wiki/Rangers_F.C." title="Rangers F.C.">Rangers</a> in Scotland. On 15 August 2012, his trial period at Rangers came to and end. Moritz said "My time with Rangers has come to an end, I enjoyed it but it was not meant to be... Talking with english clubs..."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 24 August, he signed a one-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On 6 November 2012 he scored his first goal for Crystal Palace in the 5–0 victory over Ipswich Town, in Ian Holloway's first game in charge. In his next match, he scored the equaliser for Crystal Palace in a 1–2 win against Peterborough united on 10 November 2012, continuing his goalscoring run, before finding the net again on 17 November 2012 with a volley against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Derby_County" title="Derby County">Derby County</a> – his third goal in three games. He then went on to score two free kicks against Wolverhampton Wanderers on 1 January 2013, receiving praise from Crystal Palace manager Ian Holloway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Moritz joined <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a> on a one-year deal on 1 August 2013. On 9 November, Moritz scored his first goal for Bolton Wanderers against Millwall in a 3–1 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On 1 July 2014 Bolton confirmed that at the expiration of his contract, Moritz had left the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 29 September 2014, South Korean Club, <a href="/wiki/Pohang_Steelers" title="Pohang Steelers">Pohang Steelers</a> announced that Moritz joined the club on a two-year contract which is starting on 1 January 2015<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 10 October 2014, it was announced that Moritz will play for <a href="/wiki/Mumbai_City_FC" title="Mumbai City FC">Mumbai City FC</a> in the inaugural <a href="/wiki/Indian_Super_League" title="Indian Super League">Indian Super League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 18 October 2014, Moritz scored his first goal for the club en route to the league's first ever hat-trick in a 5-0 win over <a href="/wiki/FC_Pune_City" title="FC Pune City">FC Pune City</a> at the <a href="/wiki/DY_Patil_Stadium" title="DY Patil Stadium">DY Patil Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2015, Moritz agreed to return to Mumbai City for the <a href="/wiki/2015_Mumbai_City_FC_season" title="2015 Mumbai City FC season">2015</a> season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> but After only 1 game in October, Moritz returned to parent club <a href="/wiki/Pohang_Steelers" title="Pohang Steelers">Pohang Steelers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 6 December 2015, Moritz joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buriram_United" title="Buriram United">Buriram United</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thai_Premier_League" title="Thai Premier League">Thai Premier League</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2016_Thai_Premier_League" title="2016 Thai Premier League">2016</a> season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
