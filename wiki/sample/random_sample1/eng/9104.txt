<p><b>Andrés Bello</b> is one of the 21 <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Venezuela" title="Municipalities of Venezuela">municipalities</a> (<a href="/wiki/Municipio" title="Municipio">municipios</a>) that makes up the <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuelan</a> state of <a href="/wiki/Miranda_(state)" title="Miranda (state)">Miranda</a> and, according to a 2007 population estimate by the National Institute of Statistics of Venezuela, the municipality has a population of 25,208.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The town of <a href="/wiki/San_Jos%C3%A9_de_Barlovento" title="San José de Barlovento">San José de Barlovento</a> is the <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">shire town</a> of the Andrés Bello Municipality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-2">[2]</a></sup> The municipality is one of a number in Venezuela named "<a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Bello_Municipality_(disambiguation)" title="Andrés Bello Municipality (disambiguation)">Andrés Bello Municipality</a>", in honour of the writer <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Bello" title="Andrés Bello">Andrés Bello</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9s_Bello,_Miranda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Andrés Bello Municipality, according to a 2007 population estimate by the National Institute of Statistics of Venezuela, has a population of 25,208 (up from 21,725 in 2000). This amounts to 0.9% of the state's population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The municipality's population density is 221.12 inhabitants per square kilometre (572.7/sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9s_Bello,_Miranda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The mayor of the Andrés Bello Municipality is Albaro Ramón Hidalgo Rudas, elected on October 31, 2004 with 50% of the vote.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He replaced Ramon Lobo shortly after the elections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The municipality is divided into two <a href="/wiki/Parish" title="Parish">parishes</a>; San José de Barlovento and Cumbo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
