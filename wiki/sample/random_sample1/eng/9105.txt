<p><b>Andrea Coda</b> (born 25 April 1985) is an Italian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">defender</a> for <a href="/wiki/Delfino_Pescara_1936" title="Delfino Pescara 1936">Pescara</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrea_Coda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Empoli">Empoli</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrea_Coda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Empoli">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Coda began playing youth football for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.S._Lucchese-Libertas" title="A.S. Lucchese-Libertas">Lucchese</a> before joining <a href="/wiki/Empoli_F.C." title="Empoli F.C.">Empoli</a>, located an hour away from his hometown. He made his debut for Empoli when they were in <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> and helped them gain promotion to <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> for the 2005–06 season. In August 2005, he made his Serie A debut and became a regular starter.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Udinese">Udinese</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrea_Coda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Udinese">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese</a> bought 50% of his contract in January 2006 for €1 million<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Empoli2007bilancio_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Empoli2007bilancio-2">[2]</a></sup> and he remained at Empoli until the summer. During the 2006–07 season, Coda had to contend with a place on the bench but he eventually earned a place in the starting eleven by the second half of the season. In June 2007, Udinese acquired the remainder of his contract for €1.315 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Empoli2007bilancio_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Empoli2007bilancio-2">[2]</a></sup> By the 2008–09 season, he became first choice, often partnering <a href="/wiki/Maurizio_Domizzi" title="Maurizio Domizzi">Maurizio Domizzi</a>, and made his European debut in September 2008 in the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_UEFA_Cup" title="2008–09 UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a>. In March 2010, he was ruled out for the rest of the season after injuring his knee just barely five minutes into the match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chievo_Verona" title="Chievo Verona">Chievo Verona</a> and had to be substituted.</p>
<p>In June 2010, <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese</a> agreed a double loan deal with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.S._Bari" title="A.S. Bari">Bari</a>, subject to Coda and <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Romero" title="Jaime Romero">Jaime Romero</a>'s decision.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Eventually Coda remained in Udine.</p>
<p>On 21 June 2012 Coda signed a new five-year contract<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> after the club qualified for the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> playoffs round for a second successive season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sampdoria">Sampdoria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrea_Coda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sampdoria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 22 January 2015 fellow Serie A club <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">U.C. Sampdoria</a> signed Coda and <a href="/wiki/Luis_Muriel" title="Luis Muriel">Luis Muriel</a> in temporary deals, with an obligation to buy them outright for €12 million total fee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Coda signed a contract lasting until 30 June 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pescara">Pescara</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrea_Coda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Pescara">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 1 February 2016, Coda joined <a href="/wiki/Delfino_Pescara_1936" title="Delfino Pescara 1936">Pescara</a> on loan for the rest of the season, with an option to purchase.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrea_Coda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Coda played for Italy national team at every youth level, from the under-16 (was renamed from under-15 after 2000–01 season), to the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament">Olympic team in Beijing</a>. He also participated in <a href="/wiki/2004_UEFA_European_Under-19_Championship" title="2004 UEFA European Under-19 Championship">2004 UEFA European Under-19 Championship</a>, <a href="/wiki/2005_FIFA_World_Youth_Championship" title="2005 FIFA World Youth Championship">2005 FIFA World Youth Championship</a>, <a href="/wiki/2006_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2006 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2006 UEFA European Under-21 Championship</a> and <a href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2007 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2007 UEFA European Under-21 Championship</a>.</p>
<p>In order to prepare for the Olympics, he also played the match against <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_under-21_football_team" title="Netherlands national under-21 football team">Dutch Olympic team</a> in February 2008, as well as in <a href="/wiki/2008_Toulon_Tournament" title="2008 Toulon Tournament">2008 Toulon Tournament</a>.</p>
