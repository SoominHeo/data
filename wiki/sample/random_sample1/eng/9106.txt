<p><b>Andreas Hinkel</b> (born 26 March 1982) is a retired German <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a>. He played as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full_back" title="Defender (association football)">right back</a> and played 21 caps for <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a>. Hinkel was known for his attacking play on the flanks and defensive solidity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He is now the manager of <a href="/wiki/VfB_Stuttgart_II" title="VfB Stuttgart II">VfB Stuttgart II</a>.</p>
<p>He started his career with <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> side VfB Stuttgart in 2000, before moving to <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> club <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla</a> in 2006. In January 2008, he signed for <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Premier_League" title="Scottish Premier League">Scottish Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Celtic_F.C." title="Celtic F.C.">Celtic</a> for £1.9 million. He left <a href="/wiki/Celtic_F.C." title="Celtic F.C.">Celtic</a> and joined Bundesliga side <a href="/wiki/SC_Freiburg" title="SC Freiburg">Freiburg</a> after his contract expired in summer 2011. He had spent the entire <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Celtic_F.C._season" title="2010–11 Celtic F.C. season">2010–11 season</a> out due to a cruciate ligament injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hinkel played 21 matches for Germany between 2003 and 2009, and was in their squad for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euro_2004" title="Euro 2004">Euro 2004</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andreas_Hinkel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andreas_Hinkel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Stuttgart">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After finishing second with <a href="/wiki/VfB_Stuttgart" title="VfB Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_Bundesliga" title="2002–03 Bundesliga">2002–03 season</a>, and already established as first-choice, Hinkel took part in <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> for the first time in his career. In March 2004, however, he suffered a <a href="/wiki/Knee" title="Knee">knee</a> <a href="/wiki/Ligament" title="Ligament">ligament</a> injury.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sevilla">Sevilla</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andreas_Hinkel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sevilla">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 23 June 2006, Hinkel signed a four-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla</a>, where he won the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a> and the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Super_Cup" title="UEFA Super Cup">UEFA Super Cup</a>. The transfer fee was estimated to be around €4 million. However, he found himself unable to dislodge Sevilla's regular right-back <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daniel_Alves" title="Daniel Alves">Daniel Alves</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CelticSign_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-CelticSign-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Celtic">Celtic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andreas_Hinkel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Celtic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 4 January 2008, Hinkel was confirmed as a <a href="/wiki/Celtic_F.C." title="Celtic F.C.">Celtic</a> player, for a fee of £1.9 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CelticSign_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-CelticSign-3">[3]</a></sup> Hinkel played his first Celtic game in a 3–0 <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Cup" title="Scottish Cup">Scottish Cup</a> win over <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Albion_F.C." title="Stirling Albion F.C.">Stirling Albion</a> eight days later, and scored his first goal for Celtic a month later, in a 3–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Heart_of_Midlothian_F.C." title="Heart of Midlothian F.C.">Hearts</a> at <a href="/wiki/Celtic_Park" title="Celtic Park">Celtic Park</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Celtic won the SPL title on a dramatic last day of the season and earned Hinkel his first league title medal.</p>
<p>The following season, Hinkel won a <a href="/wiki/Scottish_League_Cup" title="Scottish League Cup">Scottish League Cup</a> winners medal after a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Rangers_F.C." title="Rangers F.C.">Rangers</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2009_Scottish_League_Cup_Final" title="2009 Scottish League Cup Final">final</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hinkel played a vital part in Celtic's <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> third qualifying round second-leg victory over Russian outfit <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FC_Dinamo_Moscow" title="FC Dinamo Moscow">Dinamo Moscow</a> on 5 August 2009. The match ended 2–0 to <i>the Hoops</i> with Hinkel assisting <a href="/wiki/Scott_McDonald" title="Scott McDonald">Scott McDonald</a> for the opener then hitting a long hopeful punt up the pitch which <a href="/wiki/Georgios_Samaras" title="Georgios Samaras">Georgios Samaras</a> latched onto before netting a <a href="/wiki/Last-minute_goal" title="Last-minute goal">last-minute</a> winner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The German also cleared two goal-bound Dinamo strikes off the line. Hinkel was seen as an inherent and important part of the team and his performances were not put into question,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> as shown by his 29 SPL matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the first match of the 2010–11 season on 14 August, Hinkel was dropped in favour of new signing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cha_Du-Ri" title="Cha Du-Ri">Cha Du-Ri</a>. The following week, he suffered an <a href="/wiki/Anterior_cruciate_ligament_injury" title="Anterior cruciate ligament injury">anterior cruciate ligament injury</a> and was ruled out for nine months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He resumed training the following January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On 1 July 2011, Hinkel left Celtic after his contract expired.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="SC_Freiburg">SC Freiburg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andreas_Hinkel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: SC Freiburg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 6 October 2011, <a href="/wiki/SC_Freiburg" title="SC Freiburg">SC Freiburg</a> signed Hinkel on a free transfer.</p>
<p>On 10 September 2012, Hinkel announced his immediate retirement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andreas_Hinkel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hinkel has represented <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a> on 21 occasions at senior level, making his debut in 2003 against <a href="/wiki/Serbia_national_football_team" title="Serbia national football team">Serbia and Montenegro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He was part of the German squad during <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2004" title="UEFA Euro 2004">Euro 2004</a> (having recovered from aforementioned knee injury), but missed the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FIFA_player_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-FIFA_player-13">[13]</a></sup> Hinkel's international involvement was kept to a minimum when at Sevilla due to lack of first-team football and the emergence of <a href="/wiki/Philipp_Lahm" title="Philipp Lahm">Philipp Lahm</a> as first-choice right back ahead of him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Players_Info_Hinkel_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Players_Info_Hinkel-14">[14]</a></sup> Hinkel was also not called up for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008" title="UEFA Euro 2008">Euro 2008</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Players_Info_Hinkel_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Players_Info_Hinkel-14">[14]</a></sup> or the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FIFA_player_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-FIFA_player-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Managerial_career">Managerial career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andreas_Hinkel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Managerial career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Between 2014 and 2016, Hinkel was an assistant coach in Stuttgart's youth system, the first team, and the <a href="/wiki/VfB_Stuttgart_II" title="VfB Stuttgart II">reserve team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On 19 December 2016, Hinkel became the manager of Stuttgart II.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hinkel_.C3.BCbernimmt_den_VfB_Stuttgart_II_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hinkel_.C3.BCbernimmt_den_VfB_Stuttgart_II-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
