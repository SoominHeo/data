<p><b>Andrej Sekera</b> (born June 8, 1986) is a <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovak</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> player currently playing for the <a href="/wiki/Edmonton_Oilers" title="Edmonton Oilers">Edmonton Oilers</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">National Hockey League</a> (NHL). A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defenseman" title="Defenseman">defenseman</a>, he was drafted in the third round, 71st overall, by the <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_Sabres" title="Buffalo Sabres">Buffalo Sabres</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2004_NHL_Entry_Draft" title="2004 NHL Entry Draft">2004 NHL Entry Draft</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_career">Playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrej_Sekera&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minor">Minor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrej_Sekera&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Minor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After playing one season in the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_Extraliga" title="Slovak Extraliga">Slovak Extraliga</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HC_Dukla_Tren%C4%8D%C3%ADn" title="HC Dukla Trenčín">Dukla Trenčín</a>, Sekera came to North America. He played two seasons with the <a href="/wiki/Owen_Sound_Attack" title="Owen Sound Attack">Owen Sound Attack</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Ontario_Hockey_League" title="Ontario Hockey League">Ontario Hockey League</a> (OHL), where in the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_OHL_season" title="2005–06 OHL season">2005–06 season</a>, he won the <a href="/wiki/Max_Kaminsky_Trophy" title="Max Kaminsky Trophy">Max Kaminsky Trophy</a> as the League's most outstanding defenceman.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional">Professional</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrej_Sekera&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Professional">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 14, 2006, Sekera signed a three-year contract with the <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_Sabres" title="Buffalo Sabres">Buffalo Sabres</a>. He made his professional debut with the Sabres' <a href="/wiki/American_Hockey_League" title="American Hockey League">American Hockey League</a> (AHL) affiliate, the <a href="/wiki/Rochester_Americans" title="Rochester Americans">Rochester Americans</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_AHL_season" title="2006–07 AHL season">2006–07 season</a>. He also appeared in two NHL games with the Sabres early in <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_NHL_season" title="2006–07 NHL season">the season</a>.</p>
<p>Sekera recorded his first NHL goal on November 28, <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_NHL_season" title="2007–08 NHL season">2007</a>, against <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Blues" title="St. Louis Blues">St. Louis Blues</a>' goaltender <a href="/wiki/Manny_Legace" title="Manny Legace">Manny Legace</a>.</p>
<p>In 2009, Sekera was selected to represent <a href="/wiki/Slovakia_men%27s_national_ice_hockey_team" title="Slovakia men's national ice hockey team">Slovakia</a> internationally at the <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey_at_the_2010_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Ice hockey at the 2010 Winter Olympics – Men's tournament">2010 Winter Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>. Slovakia ultimately finished fourth in the tournament.</p>
<p>On July 19, 2011, Sekera signed a four-year contract extension with the Sabres worth $11 million and in doing so, avoided salary arbitration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_NHL_lockout" title="2012–13 NHL lockout">2012–13 NHL lockout</a>, which cancelled the majority of the first half of the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_NHL_season" title="2012–13 NHL season">2012–13 season</a>, Sekera played in his native Slovakia with <a href="/wiki/HC_Slovan_Bratislava" title="HC Slovan Bratislava">Slovan Bratislava</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Kontinental_Hockey_League" title="Kontinental Hockey League">Kontinental Hockey League</a> (KHL). He returned, however, to the Sabres following the return of NHL play in January 2013.</p>
<p>On June 30, 2013, during the <a href="/wiki/2013_NHL_Entry_Draft" title="2013 NHL Entry Draft">2013 NHL Entry Draft</a>, Sekera was traded by the Sabres to the <a href="/wiki/Carolina_Hurricanes" title="Carolina Hurricanes">Carolina Hurricanes</a> in exchange for the team's second-round draft pick (#35 overall) and defenseman <a href="/wiki/Jamie_McBain" title="Jamie McBain">Jamie McBain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, Sekera was again selected to represent Slovakia, participating in the <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey_at_the_2014_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Ice hockey at the 2014 Winter Olympics – Men's tournament">2014 Winter Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sochi" title="Sochi">Sochi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. Slovakia finished the tournament in a disappointing 11th place.</p>
<p>In the midst of the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_NHL_season" title="2014–15 NHL season">2014–15 season</a>, in the last year of contract before approaching unrestricted free agency, and with the Hurricanes out of <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Cup_playoffs" title="Stanley Cup playoffs">playoff</a> contention, on February 25, 2015, Sekera was traded to the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Kings" title="Los Angeles Kings">Los Angeles Kings</a> in exchange for a conditional first-round pick and defensive prospect <a href="/wiki/Roland_McKeown" title="Roland McKeown">Roland McKeown</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 1, 2015, Sekera signed as a free agent to a six-year, $33 million contract with the Edmonton Oilers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
