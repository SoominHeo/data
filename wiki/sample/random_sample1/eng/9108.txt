<p><b>Andrei Gennadyevich Karlov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Андре́й Генна́дьевич Ка́рлов</span>; 4 February 1954 – 19 December 2016) was a Russian career diplomat who served as the <a href="/wiki/List_of_ambassadors_of_Russia_to_Turkey" title="List of ambassadors of Russia to Turkey">Russian Ambassador to Turkey</a> and earlier as the nation's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Ambassador_to_North_Korea" title="Russian Ambassador to North Korea">ambassador to North Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the evening of 19 December 2016, while speaking at an art gallery exhibition in <a href="/wiki/Ankara" title="Ankara">Ankara, Turkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_Andrei_Karlov" title="Assassination of Andrei Karlov">Karlov was assassinated</a> by Mevlüt Mert Altıntaş, an off-duty Turkish policeman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrei_Karlov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Karlov was born in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> on 4 February 1954.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aif_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-aif-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1976, Karlov graduated from the <a href="/wiki/Moscow_State_Institute_of_International_Relations" title="Moscow State Institute of International Relations">Moscow State Institute of International Relations</a>. That same year he joined the diplomatic service.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrei_Karlov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1992, he graduated from the <a href="/wiki/Diplomatic_Academy_of_the_Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_of_the_Russian_Federation" title="Diplomatic Academy of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Russian Federation">Diplomatic Academy of the Russian Foreign Ministry</a>. He was fluent in <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>, and served in different roles in the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">USSR</a> embassy in North Korea from 1979 to 1984, and 1986 to 1991. Between 1992 and 1997 he worked in the Russian embassy in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, and served as Russia's ambassador to <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a> from 2001 to 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup> While as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Ambassador_to_North_Korea" title="Russian Ambassador to North Korea">ambassador to North Korea</a>, he was instrumental in the building of the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_the_Life-Giving_Trinity" title="Church of the Life-Giving Trinity">Church of the Life-Giving Trinity</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Russian_Orthodox_Church" title="Russian Orthodox Church">Russian Orthodox</a> church in <a href="/wiki/Pyongyang" title="Pyongyang">Pyongyang</a>, through discussions with <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jong-il" title="Kim Jong-il">Kim Jong-il</a>. The church was consecrated in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pravmir_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-pravmir-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 2007 to 2009, Karlov served as Deputy Director of the Consular Department of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Foreign_Ministry" title="Russian Foreign Ministry">Russian Foreign Ministry</a>. He was promoted to director of the department in January 2009. He was appointed ambassador to Turkey in July 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aif_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-aif-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Karlov was ambassador to Turkey during a tumultuous period between the two countries. Russia and Turkey experienced worst diplomatic crisis in recent years following the <a href="/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown" title="2015 Russian Sukhoi Su-24 shootdown">shooting down of a Russian jet</a> in November 2015, after Turkey claimed it had violated its airspace. <a href="/wiki/Russia%E2%80%93Turkey_relations" title="Russia–Turkey relations">Russia–Turkey relations</a> were severely strained after the incident, with Russia posing economic sanctions and travel restrictions for its citizens. Karlov blamed Turkey for the crisis, and it was not until June 2016 that diplomatic relations were normalized. During an interview two months after the incident, Karlov also claimed there was no evidence that Russian warplanes were bombing civilians in <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Assassination">Assassination</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrei_Karlov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Assassination">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_Andrei_Karlov" title="Assassination of Andrei Karlov">Assassination of Andrei Karlov</a></div>

<p>On 19 December 2016, at 20:15, Karlov was shot and fatally wounded by Mevlüt Mert Altıntaş, a 22-year-old off-duty Turkish police officer, at an art exhibition in <a href="/wiki/Ankara" title="Ankara">Ankara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The attacker, who was dressed in a suit and tie, opened fire at Karlov at <a href="/wiki/Point-blank_range" title="Point-blank range">point-blank range</a> while the ambassador was delivering his speech in front of journalists, fatally wounding the ambassador and injuring several others. The attacker gained access to the gallery after he showed his police ID to security guards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>A video of the attack showed the assassin crying out: "Don't forget <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Aleppo_(2012%E2%80%9316)" title="Battle of Aleppo (2012–16)">Aleppo</a>, don't forget <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Civil_War" title="Syrian Civil War">Syria</a>!" and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allahu_Akbar" title="Allahu Akbar">Allahu Akbar</a>" (God is Greatest) while holding a gun in one hand and waving the other in the air in the <i><a href="/wiki/Tawhid" title="Tawhid">tawhid</a></i> salute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The assailant shouted in Arabic and Turkish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-3">[3]</a></sup> Altıntaş was subsequently shot by Turkish security forces.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Both were rushed to hospital, but they died from their injuries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The assassination took place after a long period of highly polarized and incited political atmosphere in Turkey,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and after several days of protests by <a href="/wiki/Islamism" title="Islamism">Islamist</a> elements of the Turkish public against <a href="/wiki/Russian_involvement_in_the_Syrian_Civil_War" title="Russian involvement in the Syrian Civil War">Russian involvement in the Syrian Civil War</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Aleppo_offensive_(November%E2%80%93December_2016)" title="Aleppo offensive (November–December 2016)">battle over Aleppo</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> as well as recent negotiations between Russian and Turkish governments for a ceasefire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-3">[3]</a></sup> The Russian president <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a>, qualified this terrorist attack as an attempt to damage Turkish–Russian ties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Army_of_Conquest" title="Army of Conquest">Army of Conquest</a>, which includes the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al-Nusra_Front" title="Al-Nusra Front">Al-Nusra Front</a> claimed responsibility for the assassination, according to <a href="/wiki/Russian_News_Agency_TASS" title="Russian News Agency TASS">Russian News Agency TASS</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Andrei Karlov is the fourth Russian ambassador to have died in the line of duty since the 1829 murder of <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Griboyedov" title="Alexander Griboyedov">Alexander Griboyedov</a> in Tehran.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lenta.ru.Kupriyanov_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-lenta.ru.Kupriyanov-19">[19]</a></sup> (Before Karlov, <a href="/wiki/Pyotr_Voykov" title="Pyotr Voykov">Pyotr Voykov</a>, a Soviet envoy to Poland, was shot to death in Warsaw in 1927;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Vatslav_Vorovsky" title="Vatslav Vorovsky">Vatslav Vorovsky</a> was assassinated in Lausanne in 1923.)</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrei_Karlov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Karlov was married to Marina Karlova. Their son Gennady graduated from the Moscow State Institute of International Relations and serves in the consulate department in the Russian embassy in North Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aif_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-aif-4">[4]</a></sup> Karlov was an Orthodox Christian.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pravmir_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-pravmir-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_legacy">Awards and legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrei_Karlov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Awards and legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Karlov was awarded the Order of <a href="/wiki/Seraphim_of_Sarov" title="Seraphim of Sarov">Seraphim of Sarov</a>, 3rd degree, for his role in establishing a Russian Orthodox Church in Pyongyang while ambassador to North Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On 21 December 2016, the day after his death, he was posthumously awarded the title of <a href="/wiki/Hero_of_the_Russian_Federation" title="Hero of the Russian Federation">Hero of the Russian Federation</a> by the Kremlin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>The city of Ankara announced that the exhibition hall where Karlov was assassinated would be named after Andrei Karlov. <a href="/wiki/List_of_Ministers_of_Foreign_Affairs_(Turkey)" title="List of Ministers of Foreign Affairs (Turkey)">Turkish Foreign Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Mevl%C3%BCt_%C3%87avu%C5%9Fo%C4%9Flu" title="Mevlüt Çavuşoğlu">Mevlüt Çavuşoğlu</a> said Karyağdı Street in Ankara – where the Russian embassy is located – will also be named after Karlov.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/President_of_Russia" title="President of Russia">Russian President</a> <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a> announced that he has requested that the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_(Russia)" title="Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Russia)">Ministry of Foreign Affairs</a> "make a proposal for the perpetuation of [Karlov's] memory".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lenta.ru.Kupriyanov_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-lenta.ru.Kupriyanov-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
