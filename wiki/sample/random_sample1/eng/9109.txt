<p><b>Andrei Vladimirovich Kobyakov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Belarusian_language" title="Belarusian language">Belarusian</a>: <span lang="be" xml:lang="be">ÐÐ½Ð´ÑÑÐ¹ Ð£Ð»Ð°Ð´Ð·ÑÐ¼ÑÑÐ°Ð²ÑÑ ÐÐ°Ð±ÑÐºÐ¾Ñ</span>, <i>Andrey Uladzimiravich Kabyakow</i>; <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">ÐÐ½Ð´ÑÐµÐ¹ ÐÐ»Ð°Ð´Ð¸Ð¼Ð¸ÑÐ¾Ð²Ð¸Ñ ÐÐ¾Ð±ÑÐºÐ¾Ð²</span>; born 21 November 1960) is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Belarus" title="Prime Minister of Belarus">Prime Minister of Belarus</a>. He was appointed by president <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Lukashenko" title="Alexander Lukashenko">Alexander Lukashenko</a> on 27 December 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Before this appointment, Kobyakov served as the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chief_of_staff_to_the_President_of_Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chief of staff to the President of Belarus (page does not exist)">President's Chief of Staff</a> from 2012 to 2014.</p>
<p>He was a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Deputy_Prime_Minister_of_Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Deputy Prime Minister of Belarus (page does not exist)">Deputy Prime Minister</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> from December 2003 to December 28, 2010.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrei_Kobyakov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andrei Kobyakov was born in Moscow in 1960.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-council_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-council-4">[4]</a></sup> From the age of three he lived in the territory of Belarus.</p>
<p>In 1983, he graduated from the Moscow Aviation Institute. From 1985-1988 he worked first as a senior master, then deputy shop superintendent, and deputy chief of Minsk Mechanical Works assembly plant factory.</p>
<p>In 1991, he graduated from the Belarusian State Institute of National Economy. From 1989-1991 he was a student at the Institute of Political Science and Social Management of the PBC.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrei_Kobyakov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 1995 to 1996 he served as Deputy Chief of the Control Service and from 1996 to 1998 he was the Deputy Chairman of the Belarusian State Control Committee. In June 1998, he became President of the Belarusian State Concern for production and sale of light industry goods.</p>
<p>He was appointed chairman of the State Control Committee on 2 December 1998 by order of the President. On 6 October 2000 he was appointed representative of the State in OAO "Belpromstroibank."</p>
<p>From 2000 to 2001 he was the First Deputy Prime Minister of Belarus. On 24 September 2001 the President appointed him Deputy Prime Minister.</p>
<p>He was the Economy Minister from 3 July 2002 to 24 December 2003.</p>
<p>On 28 December 2010, he served as the Deputy Head of the Presidential Administration of Belarus.</p>
<p>He was appointed Ambassador to Russia on 8 December 2011 and served until 2012.</p>
<p>He was <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chief_of_Staff_to_the_President_of_Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chief of Staff to the President of Belarus (page does not exist)">Chief of Staff to the President</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-council_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-council-4">[4]</a></sup> from 27 August 2012 to 27 December 2014.</p>
<p>He is married with a daughter and a son.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-council_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-council-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
