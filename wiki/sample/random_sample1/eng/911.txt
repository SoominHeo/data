<p><b>William Sami Etienne Grigahcine</b> (born 13 June 1986), known professionally as <b>DJ Snake</b>, is a French DJ and record producer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref3_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref3-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref4_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref4-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref7_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref7-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref17_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref17-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>DJ Snake is a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy-nominated</a> producer and artist who debuted into the international scene with singles "Bird Machine" and "<a href="/wiki/Turn_Down_for_What" title="Turn Down for What">Turn Down for What</a>" in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ref1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref2-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref5_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref5-7">[7]</a></sup> "Bird Machine" is a collaboration with fellow French act Alesia. The single was picked up by <a href="/wiki/Mad_Decent" title="Mad Decent">Mad Decent</a>, a Los Angeles-based record label run by <a href="/wiki/Diplo" title="Diplo">Diplo</a>, and released in February 2013. In June 2013, DJ Snake was invited by Diplo to do a live mix on his radio show, "Diplo &amp; Friends", which airs on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref21_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref21-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>DJ Snake was announced to be working on a collaboration with Diplo, originally slated to debut in 2014; it eventually released in 2015 as the single "<a href="/wiki/Lean_On" title="Lean On">Lean On</a>" in collaboration with <a href="/wiki/M%C3%98" title="MØ">MØ</a> and Diplo's <a href="/wiki/Major_Lazer" title="Major Lazer">Major Lazer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref22_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref22-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref23_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref23-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 30 April 2014, DJ Snake was labeled as an "Artist to Watch" by FoxWeekly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He and <a href="/wiki/Dillon_Francis" title="Dillon Francis">Dillon Francis</a> were announced as alternating supporting artists for the summer Mothership Tour 2014 with <a href="/wiki/Skrillex" title="Skrillex">Skrillex</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mothership_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-mothership-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Snake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>DJ Snake was born to Algerian parents in a <a href="/wiki/Banlieue" title="Banlieue">Banlieue</a> district outside Paris, which he describes as a <a href="/wiki/Ghetto" title="Ghetto">ghetto</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forbes_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-14">[14]</a></sup> He cites French film <i><a href="/wiki/La_Haine" title="La Haine">La Haine</a></i> as well as hip-hop artists <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cypress_Hill" title="Cypress Hill">Cypress Hill</a> as early influences. In his youth, DJ Snake produced <a href="/wiki/Graffiti" title="Graffiti">graffiti</a> art, earning him the <a href="/wiki/Nickname" title="Nickname">moniker</a> "Snake" because he was able to consistently evade the police. Of his name, DJ Snake says “When I started DJing, everyone called me ‘Snake’ in my city first, I was like ‘DJ Snake, OK let’s go for it.’ The name sucks, but it’s too late now.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forbes_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-14">[14]</a></sup> He started DJ-ing at age 14, and later producing at 19 years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In his early career, DJ Snake performed at clubs including <a class="extiw" href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Les_Bains_Douches" title="fr:Les Bains Douches">Les Bains Douches</a> in Paris. In 2005, he met his manager Steve Goncalves, who encouraged DJ Snake to begin creating his own music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forbes_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Snake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2011, DJ Snake produced for <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a>’s album <i><a href="/wiki/Born_This_Way_(album)" title="Born This Way (album)">Born This Way</a></i>. This earned him a Grammy nomination for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Album_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Album of the Year">Album of the Year</a> in February 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref14_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref14-16">[16]</a></sup> DJ Snake was co-producer for the song "<a href="/wiki/Government_Hooker" title="Government Hooker">Government Hooker</a>", which was voted best song by Lady Gaga fans after the release of the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref18_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref18-17">[17]</a></sup> This song was supposed to be released as a single before <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> decided to stop the marketing of the album after its release. He remixed <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/New_Slaves" title="New Slaves">New Slaves</a>", <a href="/wiki/AlunaGeorge" title="AlunaGeorge">AlunaGeorge</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/You_Know_You_Like_It" title="You Know You Like It">You Know You Like It</a>", Duck Sauce’s "It's You" and Major Lazer's "<a href="/wiki/Bubble_Butt" title="Bubble Butt">Bubble Butt</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref9_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref9-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref10_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref10-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref11_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref11-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref12_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref12-21">[21]</a></sup> Junior Senior sampled Michael Jackson for their track "Move Your Feet". DJ Snake remixed the song in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref13_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref13-22">[22]</a></sup> In 2013, DJ Snake produced three songs with Paul "DJ White Shadow" Blair on Lady Gaga’s album <i><a href="/wiki/Artpop" title="Artpop">ARTPOP</a></i> including "<a href="/wiki/Applause_(Lady_Gaga_song)" title="Applause (Lady Gaga song)">Applause</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Sexxx_Dreams" title="Sexxx Dreams">Sexxx Dreams</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Do_What_U_Want" title="Do What U Want">Do What U Want</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref8_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref8-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref24_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref24-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref25_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref25-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>DJ Snake and Dillon Francis were alternating supporting artists on the Mothership Tour 2014 with Skrillex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mothership_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-mothership-13">[13]</a></sup> He performed at the 2015 <a href="/wiki/Coachella_Valley_Music_and_Arts_Festival" title="Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival">Coachella Music Festival</a> with guest appearances by AlunaGeorge and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M%C3%98_(singer)" title="MØ (singer)">MØ</a>, who did vocals on "<a href="/wiki/Lean_On" title="Lean On">Lean On</a>" with Major Lazer. "Lean On" and DJ Snake's remix of AlunaGeorge's "You Know You Like It" were both top 10 <a href="/wiki/Shazam_(service)" title="Shazam (service)">Shazamed</a> songs from Coachella 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-coachella_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-coachella-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shazam_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-shazam-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2015, DJ Snake and fellow electronic producer <a href="/wiki/Tchami" title="Tchami">Tchami</a> were injured in a car crash, causing them both to miss a scheduled stop at Toronto's Monster Mash festival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In January 2016, <a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a> named him one of their "30 Under 30 In Music" for the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Standalone_singles_.282013.E2.80.932015.29">Standalone singles (2013–2015)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Snake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Standalone singles (2013–2015)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In December 2013, "<a href="/wiki/Turn_Down_for_What" title="Turn Down for What">Turn Down for What</a>", DJ Snake's collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Lil_Jon" title="Lil Jon">Lil Jon</a>, was released on <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref26_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref26-30">[30]</a></sup> The song was featured in a commercial for the <a href="/wiki/Sol_Republic" title="Sol Republic">Sol Republic</a> DECK, a wireless speaker, which aired in October 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref27_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref27-31">[31]</a></sup> "Turn Down for What" entered the top ten of the <i>Billboard</i> Dance/Electronic chart during the last week of December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboard1_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-billboard1-32">[32]</a></sup> In January 2014, the single entered the top 5 on the Dance/Electronic chart and debuted No. 38 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboard2_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-billboard2-33">[33]</a></sup> The song was certified gold in February 2014 and platinum in March 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riaa_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-riaa-35">[35]</a></sup> The song reached the top ten of the Hot 100 in April 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The music video for the single was released in March 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> "Turn Down for What" was nominated for <a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Award" title="MTV Video Music Award">MTV Video Music Awards</a> for Best Direction, Best Art Direction, Best Visual Effects, and the MTV Clubland Award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2014, DJ Snake and <a href="/wiki/Dillon_Francis" title="Dillon Francis">Dillon Francis</a> released "<a href="/wiki/Get_Low_(Dillon_Francis_and_DJ_Snake_song)" title="Get Low (Dillon Francis and DJ Snake song)">Get Low</a>" on their official <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> and <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> pages, as the lead single from Francis's debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Money_Sucks,_Friends_Rule" title="Money Sucks, Friends Rule">Money Sucks, Friends Rule</a></i>. Both "Get Low" and "Turn Down for What" were featured on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Furious_7_(soundtrack)" title="Furious 7 (soundtrack)"><i>Furious 7</i> motion picture soundtrack</a>. Along with the exposure gained from commercials for <a href="/wiki/Dodge" title="Dodge">Dodge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taco_Bell" title="Taco Bell">Taco Bell</a> featuring the song, the inclusion of "Get Low" on the soundtrack helped the song re-enter the Billboard Hot Dance/Electronic chart while DJ Snake re-entered the Billboard Artist 100.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-getlowbillboard_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-getlowbillboard-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2014, DJ Snake's remix of the <a href="/wiki/AlunaGeorge" title="AlunaGeorge">AlunaGeorge</a> song "<a href="/wiki/You_Know_You_Like_It" title="You Know You Like It">You Know You Like It</a>" was released on <a href="/wiki/Island_Records" title="Island Records">Island Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> "You Know You Like It" reached number one on <i>Billboard's</i> <a href="/wiki/Dance/Mix_Show_Airplay" title="Dance/Mix Show Airplay">Dance/Mix Show Airplay</a> chart and <a href="/wiki/Rhythmic_(chart)" title="Rhythmic (chart)">Rhythmic Songs</a> chart in June 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> It peaked at 13 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> The same month, the <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a> listed the remix as the 8th most streamed track on <a href="/wiki/Spotify" title="Spotify">Spotify</a> in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> In November 2015, the song received <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">RIAA</a> double-platinum certification.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> For the remix, DJ Snake used a fusion of European dance music and hip hop beats. It took him about four hours to write. The collaboration was done via the Internet, with the vocals emailed to DJ Snake, who worked in Paris.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2015 "<a href="/wiki/Lean_On" title="Lean On">Lean On</a>", DJ Snake's collaborative track with <a href="/wiki/Major_Lazer" title="Major Lazer">Major Lazer</a> featuring <a href="/wiki/M%C3%98" title="MØ">MØ</a>, was released on <a href="/wiki/Mad_Decent" title="Mad Decent">Mad Decent</a>, as the lead single from Major Lazer's third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Peace_Is_the_Mission" title="Peace Is the Mission">Peace Is the Mission</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> It made it on <i>Billboard's</i> <a href="/wiki/Rhythmic_(chart)" title="Rhythmic (chart)">Rhythmic Songs</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mainstream_Top_40" title="Mainstream Top 40">Mainstream Top 40</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Latin_Pop_Airplay" title="Latin Pop Airplay">Latin Pop Songs</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dance_Club_Songs" title="Dance Club Songs">Dance Club Songs</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dance/Electronic_Songs" title="Dance/Electronic Songs">Hot Dance/Electric Songs</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Adult_Top_40" title="Adult Top 40">Adult Top 40</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> charts. It peaked at 4 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> In June, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Time_Magazine" title="Time Magazine">Time Magazine</a></i> named it one of the "Best Songs of 2015 So Far"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> In September 2015, "Lean On" was named Spotify's global song of the summer. In November 2015, the song surpassed 526 million Spotify plays, making it the service's most streamed song ever.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> The video for "Lean On" was released in March 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> It was shot in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and directed by Tim Erem.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> <i>Amplify</i> magazine named "Lean On" one of "The Best Videos of 2015".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> In January 2016, the "Lean On" video reached 1 billion views on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Encore_.282015.E2.80.93present.29"><i>Encore</i> (2015–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Snake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Encore (2015–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>DJ Snake released his next single "<a href="/wiki/Middle_(DJ_Snake_song)" title="Middle (DJ Snake song)">Middle</a>" on 16 October 2015. The song was released by <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mad_Decent" title="Mad Decent">Mad Decent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spinnin%27_Records" title="Spinnin' Records">Spinnin' Records</a>. It features the vocals from English singer <a href="/wiki/Bipolar_Sunshine" title="Bipolar Sunshine">Bipolar Sunshine</a>. The song's accompanying music video premiered on 16 March 2016 on DJ Snake's YouTube account on <a href="/wiki/Vevo" title="Vevo">Vevo</a>.</p>
<p>On 2 June 2016 the single "<a href="/wiki/Talk_(DJ_Snake_song)" title="Talk (DJ Snake song)">Talk</a>" was released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> DJ Snake had hinted the possibility of releasing new music via Twitter a few days before the single's release. It features vocals from George Maple's song "Talk Talk" that was released in December 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> The track infuses <a href="/wiki/Tropical_house" title="Tropical house">tropical house</a> sounds and was released by <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> and made available for purchase on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> on 10 June 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> The music video for the song was released on DJ Snake's YouTube account on 11 July 2016. In July 2016, he announced the release of his debut studio album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Encore_(DJ_Snake_album)" title="Encore (DJ Snake album)">Encore</a></i>, released on 5 August 2016. "Middle" and "Talk" serve as singles from the album.</p>
<p>On 5 August 2016, with the release of <i>Encore</i>, DJ Snake released the album's third single, "<a href="/wiki/Let_Me_Love_You_(DJ_Snake_song)" title="Let Me Love You (DJ Snake song)">Let Me Love You</a>", featuring the vocals of Canadian singer <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a>. On January 2, 2017, DJ Snake started promoting the album's fourth single "The Half", featuring <a href="/wiki/Jeremih" title="Jeremih">Jeremih</a>, <a href="/wiki/Young_Thug" title="Young Thug">Young Thug</a> and <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup></p>
