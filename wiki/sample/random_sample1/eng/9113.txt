<p><b>Eta Andromedae</b> (<b>Eta And</b>, <b>η Andromedae</b>, <b>η And</b>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spectroscopic_binary" title="Spectroscopic binary">spectroscopic binary</a> <a href="/wiki/Star" title="Star">star</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Andromeda_(constellation)" title="Andromeda (constellation)">Andromeda</a>. It consists of two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G-type_star" title="G-type star">G-type</a> <a href="/wiki/Subgiant" title="Subgiant">subgiant</a> or <a href="/wiki/Giant_star" title="Giant star">giant stars</a> orbiting each other with a period of 115.7 days and has an overall <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apparent_visual_magnitude" title="Apparent visual magnitude">apparent visual magnitude</a> of approximately 4.403.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sb0_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-sb0-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-intf_3-11"><a href="#cite_note-intf-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eta_Andromedae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:232px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Eta_And_AB_Orbit.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="531" data-file-width="863" height="142" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Eta_And_AB_Orbit.jpg/230px-Eta_And_AB_Orbit.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Eta_And_AB_Orbit.jpg/345px-Eta_And_AB_Orbit.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Eta_And_AB_Orbit.jpg/460px-Eta_And_AB_Orbit.jpg 2x" width="230"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Eta_And_AB_Orbit.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
η Andromedae system as seen from earth orbit, <a href="/wiki/Triangulum_Galaxy" title="Triangulum Galaxy">M 33</a> in background</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Eta Andromedae was discovered to be a double-lined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spectroscopic_binary" title="Spectroscopic binary">spectroscopic binary</a> in a series of spectra taken in 1899 and 1900.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Its orbit was computed in 1946 from spectroscopic observations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Because spectroscopy only reveals the <a href="/wiki/Radial_velocity" title="Radial velocity">radial velocity</a> of a star towards or away from the viewer, such a computation does not determine all <a href="/wiki/Orbital_elements" title="Orbital elements">orbital elements</a>. In observations made from 1990 to 1992, Eta Andromedae was resolved <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interferometrically" title="Interferometrically">interferometrically</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Mark_III_Stellar_Interferometer" title="Mark III Stellar Interferometer">Mark III Stellar Interferometer</a> at <a href="/wiki/Mount_Wilson_Observatory" title="Mount Wilson Observatory">Mount Wilson Observatory</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. This allowed a more complete orbit to be computed and, in 1993, published.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-intf_3-12"><a href="#cite_note-intf-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naming">Naming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eta_Andromedae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Naming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>, <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">奎宿</span> (<span lang="zh-Latn" xml:lang="zh-Latn">Kuí Sù</span>), meaning <i><a href="/wiki/Legs_(Chinese_constellation)" title="Legs (Chinese constellation)">Legs</a> (asterism)</i>, refers to an asterism consisting of η Andromedae, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=65_Piscium&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="65 Piscium (page does not exist)">65 Piscium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zeta_Andromedae" title="Zeta Andromedae">ζ Andromedae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Epsilon_Andromedae" title="Epsilon Andromedae">ε Andromedae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delta_Andromedae" title="Delta Andromedae">δ Andromedae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pi_Andromedae" title="Pi Andromedae">π Andromedae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nu_Andromedae" title="Nu Andromedae">ν Andromedae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mu_Andromedae" title="Mu Andromedae">μ Andromedae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beta_Andromedae" title="Beta Andromedae">β Andromedae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sigma_Piscium" title="Sigma Piscium">σ Piscium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tau_Piscium" title="Tau Piscium">τ Piscium</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=91_Piscium&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="91 Piscium (page does not exist)">91 Piscium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Upsilon_Piscium" title="Upsilon Piscium">υ Piscium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phi_Piscium" title="Phi Piscium">φ Piscium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chi_Piscium" title="Chi Piscium">χ Piscium</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Psi%C2%B9_Piscium" title="Psi¹ Piscium">ψ¹ Piscium</a>. Consequently, η Andromedae itself is known as <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">奎宿一</span> (<span lang="zh-Latn" xml:lang="zh-Latn">Kuí Sù yī</span>, English: the First Star of Legs.)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
