<p><b>Andriy Valeriyovych Pyatov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>: <span lang="uk">Андрій Валерійович П'ятов</span>, born 28 June 1984 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kirovohrad" title="Kirovohrad">Kirovohrad</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ukrainian_SSR" title="Ukrainian SSR">Ukrainian SSR</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Ukrainians" title="Ukrainians">Ukrainian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">goalkeeper</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/FC_Shakhtar_Donetsk" title="FC Shakhtar Donetsk">FC Shakhtar Donetsk</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Premier_League" title="Ukrainian Premier League">Ukrainian Premier League</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andriy_Pyatov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Club">Club</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andriy_Pyatov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pyatov started his career in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vorskla_Poltava" title="Vorskla Poltava">Vorskla Poltava</a> in 2002. He was bought by Shakhtar from Vorskla for approximately £880,000 on 13 December 2006. He spent the rest of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Premier_League_2006-07" title="Ukrainian Premier League 2006-07">2006–07 season</a> on loan at Vorskla Poltava. In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Ukrainian_Premier_League" title="2007–08 Ukrainian Premier League">2007–08 season</a>, Pyatov replaced <a href="/wiki/Bohdan_Shust" title="Bohdan Shust">Bohdan Shust</a> as the main goalkeeper of <a href="/wiki/FC_Shakhtar_Donetsk" title="FC Shakhtar Donetsk">Shakhtar Donetsk</a>, playing in league, cup, and UEFA Champions League matches, keeping two clean sheets. For the 2008–2009 season Pyatov will have serious competition for his No.1 spot as Shakhtar loaned out <a href="/wiki/Bohdan_Shust" title="Bohdan Shust">Bohdan Shust</a>, and signed <a href="/wiki/FC_Kharkiv" title="FC Kharkiv">FC Kharkiv</a> 'keeper <a href="/wiki/Rustam_Khudzhamov" title="Rustam Khudzhamov">Rustam Khudzhamov</a> who is reaching his prime and saved FC Kharkiv from relegation. But after Shakhtars' 2–0 loss to <a href="/wiki/FC_Lviv" title="FC Lviv">FC Lviv</a>, Pyatov regained his place as the clubs' No.1. In a home Champions League fixture against <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a> Pyatov made 7 crucial saves as Shakhtar were leading 1–0, until <a href="/wiki/Bojan_Krki%C4%87" title="Bojan Krkić">Bojan Krkić</a> sent in a cross which was spilled by Pyatov for <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Messi" title="Lionel Messi">Lionel Messi</a> to easily tap in to make it 1–1 in the 85th minute. But things went from bad to worse as another cross was sent in Messi rose and headed home giving Barcelona the win, and ruining Pyatov's perfect game. Pyatov has cemented his spot as Shakhtar's No.1 after great performances in the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_UEFA_Cup" title="2008–09 UEFA Cup">2008–09 UEFA Cup</a> which saw Shakhtar reach the final, beating fellow Ukrainian giants <a href="/wiki/FC_Dynamo_Kyiv" title="FC Dynamo Kyiv">Dynamo Kyiv</a> in the first ever all Ukrainian semi-final. His appearance in the final was marred as he spilled Naldo's freekick into the net to give <a href="/wiki/SV_Werder_Bremen" title="SV Werder Bremen">Werder Bremen</a> an equaliser but Shakhtar won 2–1 in extra time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On 1 November 2009 vs <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chornomorets_Odessa" title="Chornomorets Odessa">Chornomorets Odessa</a> Pyatov played his 100th game for Shakhtar (conceding 72 goals), and in this game he won the Man of the Match, and is also the 1st time coach (Mircea Lucescu) ever named a goalkeeper Man of the Match.</p>

<p>In 2015 Pyatov broke the record of penalties stopped in European competitions by stopping his fourth penalty, a record previously held by Shovkovsky.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andriy_Pyatov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pyatov was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Ukraine_national_under-21_football_team" title="Ukraine national under-21 football team">Ukrainian national under-21 team</a> where he has played 20 matches. He was also part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup_(squads)#Ukraine" title="2006 FIFA World Cup (squads)">Ukraine's 2006 World Cup squad</a> which got to the quarter-finals. He has made 23 appearances for the <a href="/wiki/Ukraine_national_football_team" title="Ukraine national football team">Ukrainian national team</a> and has let in 4 goals, keeping clean sheets against <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_football_team" title="Poland national football team">Poland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_football_team" title="Sweden national football team">Sweden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Croatia_national_football_team" title="Croatia national football team">Croatia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Faroe_Islands_national_football_team" title="Faroe Islands national football team">Faroe Islands</a>. He started the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">2010 FIFA World Cup qualifying campaign</a> with a clean sheet against <a href="/wiki/Belarus_national_football_team" title="Belarus national football team">Belarus</a> to help Ukraine win 1–0 in <a href="/wiki/Lviv" title="Lviv">Lviv</a>. In the 0–0 draw against Croatia, Pyatov was instrumental in denying <a href="/wiki/Luka_Modri%C4%87" title="Luka Modrić">Luka Modrić</a> on two occasions, and as well as other to secure a point for his country. In November 2013 Pyatov set a new record of minutes without a goal for the national team, beating the record of <a href="/wiki/Oleksandr_Shovkovskiy" title="Oleksandr Shovkovskiy">Oleksandr Shovkovskiy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
