<p><b>Andris Bērziņš</b> (born 10 December 1944) is a <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvian</a> businessman and politician who was <a href="/wiki/President_of_Latvia" title="President of Latvia">President of Latvia</a> from 2011 to 2015. Bērziņš was the President of <a href="/wiki/SEB_Unibanka" title="SEB Unibanka">Unibanka</a> from 1993 to 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He was <a href="/wiki/Latvian_presidential_election,_2011" title="Latvian presidential election, 2011">elected as President</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Saeima" title="Saeima">Saeima</a> on 2 June 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andris_B%C4%93rzi%C5%86%C5%A1_(Latvian_President)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andris Bērziņš was born 10 December 1944 in <a href="/wiki/N%C4%ABtaure" title="Nītaure">Nītaure</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latvian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Latvian Soviet Socialist Republic">Latvian SSR</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>. In 1958 he completed studies at a Nītaure primary school, and attended the 1st secondary school of <a href="/wiki/Sigulda" title="Sigulda">Sigulda</a> from 1958 to 1962. He then began studying at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Riga_Polytechnical_Institute" title="Riga Polytechnical Institute">Riga Polytechnical Institute</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baltic_News_Network_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Baltic_News_Network-3">[3]</a></sup> which he graduated in 1971, and became a radio engineer at the "Elektrons" factory. He worked his way up to director and in 1988 was appointed deputy minister of municipal services of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latvian_SSR" title="Latvian SSR">Latvian SSR</a>. He also studied at the Economic Faculty of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latvian_State_University" title="Latvian State University">Latvian State University</a>, which he graduated in 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baltic_News_Network_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Baltic_News_Network-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1989 Bērziņš was elected to the Valmiera district Council (Soviet) of People's Deputies and was appointed chairman of the district's executive commission. In 1990 he was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Council_of_the_Republic_of_Latvia" title="Supreme Council of the Republic of Latvia">Supreme Council</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latvian_SSR" title="Latvian SSR">Latvian SSR</a>, representing <a href="/wiki/Valmiera" title="Valmiera">Valmiera</a>. He joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latvian_Popular_Front" title="Latvian Popular Front">Latvian Popular Front</a> faction in the Supreme Council. On 4 May 1990 he voted in favor of the declaration that restored the independence of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Latvia" title="Republic of Latvia">Republic of Latvia</a>.</p>
<p>After the completion of his term as deputy in 1993, Bērziņš became chairman of the Privatization Fund of the <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Latvia" title="Bank of Latvia">Bank of Latvia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He also became president of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latvijas_Unibanka" title="Latvijas Unibanka">Latvijas Unibanka</a> (a joint-stock company; 1993–2004).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Bērziņš became a successful businessman and his estate exceeded 1 000 000 lats (by the mid 2000s). He also owned 30 various land properties. Bērziņš worked as an advisor to the president of the <a href="/wiki/Stockholms_Enskilda_Bank" title="Stockholms Enskilda Bank">SEB</a> Latvian branch and was a board member of several joint-stock companies, including <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Valmiera_stikla_%C5%A1%C4%B7iedra&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Valmiera stikla šķiedra (page does not exist)">Valmiera stikla šķiedra</a> (Valmiera fiberglass) and <a href="/wiki/Lode" title="Lode">Lode</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andris_B%C4%93rzi%C5%86%C5%A1_(Latvian_President)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andris Bērziņš returned to politics in 2005, when he unsuccessfully ran for the office of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Riga" title="Mayor of Riga">Mayor of Riga</a> as the leader of the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Greens_and_Farmers" title="Union of Greens and Farmers">Union of Greens and Farmers</a> party list. From 2006 to 2010 he served as the president of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Latvian_Chamber_of_Industry_and_Commerce&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Latvian Chamber of Industry and Commerce (page does not exist)">Latvian Chamber of Industry and Commerce</a> and was also <a href="/wiki/Latvenergo" title="Latvenergo">Latvenergo</a>'s board chairman (until 2009).</p>
<p>In 2010 Andris Bērziņš was <a href="/wiki/Latvian_parliamentary_election,_2010" title="Latvian parliamentary election, 2010">elected</a> deputy of the <a href="/wiki/Saeima" title="Saeima">Saeima</a> from the slate of candidates presented by the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Greens_and_Farmers" title="Union of Greens and Farmers">Union of Greens and Farmers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011_presidential_election">2011 presidential election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andris_B%C4%93rzi%C5%86%C5%A1_(Latvian_President)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2011 presidential election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On May 23, 2011 Andris Bērzinš was nominated as a presidential candidate by five <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Greens_and_Farmers" title="Union of Greens and Farmers">Union of Greens and Farmers</a> deputies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the first round of the <a href="/wiki/Latvian_presidential_election,_2011" title="Latvian presidential election, 2011">election</a> (2 June 2011), Bērziņš received 50 votes for and 48 against, while <a href="/wiki/Valdis_Zatlers" title="Valdis Zatlers">Valdis Zatlers</a> got 43 for and 55 against (99 lawmakers cast votes, but one was invalid), meaning no one was elected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In the second round, held later on the same day, Bērziņš received 53 votes, winning the election.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Andris Bērziņš took office as President of Latvia on 8 July 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_presidential_election">2015 presidential election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andris_B%C4%93rzi%C5%86%C5%A1_(Latvian_President)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2015 presidential election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 10, 2015 he announced he would not be seeking the Presidency for a second term.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On June 3, 2015 <a href="/wiki/Raimonds_V%C4%93jonis" title="Raimonds Vējonis">Raimonds Vējonis</a> was elected as Bērziņš's successor and took office on July 8, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andris_B%C4%93rzi%C5%86%C5%A1_(Latvian_President)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andris Bērziņš married <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dace_Seisuma&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dace Seisuma (page does not exist)">Dace Seisuma</a> a couple of days before assuming office as President. It is his second marriage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Aside from his native <a href="/wiki/Latvian_language" title="Latvian language">Latvian</a>, he speaks <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On 3 September 2012, Bērziņš threatened TV news crew which was filming him coming alongside his son on the first day of the school year: "Guys, you want me to beat you over the head? It's been a long time since you've been beaten?..."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
