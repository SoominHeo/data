<p><b>Andrijevica</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Montenegrin_Cyrillic" title="Montenegrin Cyrillic">Montenegrin Cyrillic</a>: Андријевица, <small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Serbo-Croatian" title="Help:IPA for Serbo-Croatian">[andrǐjɛv̞it͡sa]</a></span>) is a town and the seat of <a href="/wiki/Andrijevica_Municipality" title="Andrijevica Municipality">Andrijevica Municipality</a> in eastern <a href="/wiki/Montenegro" title="Montenegro">Montenegro</a>. It has a population of 1,073 (2003 census).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrijevica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town of Andrijevica is a relatively new settlement, as its first rudiments begin in the middle of the nineteenth century. However, the territory the municipality is for a long time the area of human activity and space with numerous settlements that appeared and disappeared during the history.</p>
<p>Archaeological research of the <i>Institute for monuments protection</i> from <a href="/wiki/Cetinje" title="Cetinje">Cetinje</a> in 1956, it was discovered that traces of materialculture from <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">Neolithic</a> period exist in <a href="/wiki/Berane" title="Berane">Berane</a> valley. In addition, traces of the <a href="/wiki/Illyria" title="Illyria">Illyrian</a> culture were found also, so it can be assumed that the environment of Andrijevica was ecumenical space of numerous groups of people. Remainders of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> settlements are also present in Berane valley, as well as in the vicinity of Andrijevica town.</p>
<p>During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle_ages" title="Middle ages">Middle ages</a>, the fort of <i>Grace</i> existed, 1 km from today's town of Andrijevica. The fort was probably guarding the road along the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lim_River" title="Lim River">Lim River</a>, which was part of the main road network between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ra%C5%A1ka_(state)" title="Raška (state)">Raška</a> and <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Zeta" title="Principality of Zeta">Zeta</a>. This period saw emergence of a number of smaller villages and settlements, all of which predate the Andrijevica town.</p>
<p>During the rule of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_of_Nemanji%C4%87" title="House of Nemanjić">House of Nemanjić</a>, it was noted that the area of Andrijevica was densely populated with <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Orthodox Christian</a> population. In the Nemanjić era, the center of the area was <i>Budim grad</i>, some 15 km from today's town. A descendant of the Nemanjić house, Andrija, built a church called Andrijevina, which was razed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ottomans" title="Ottomans">Ottomans</a> in 1765.</p>
<p>In nineteenth century, the warrior tribe of <a href="/wiki/Vasojevi%C4%87i" title="Vasojevići">Vasojevići</a>, which was frequently rebelling against <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> rule, began forming a settlement around the new church of <i>Andrijevina</i>, which will eventually become Andrijevica. In 1858, Vasojević Duke <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Miljan_Vukov&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Miljan Vukov (page does not exist)">Miljan Vukov</a> proclaims Vasojevići region a part of <a href="/wiki/Montenegro" title="Montenegro">Montenegro</a>.</p>
<p>From that point, the settlement gradually expands into town, but World War I interrupt its development. Andrijevica was center of the county (<i>Okrug</i>) from the 1918 to 1921, and the inter-war period was a time of rapid development for the town.</p>
<p>In July 1944, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skanderbeg_division" title="Skanderbeg division">Skanderbeg division</a> massacred more than 400 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orthodox_Christian" title="Orthodox Christian">Orthodox Christian</a> civilians in the town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMorrison200965_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMorrison200965-1">[1]</a></sup> After World War II, the town was first the seat of the district (<i>srez</i>), and then of municipality (<i>opština</i>) until 1960. Andijevica was restored with municipality status in 1991.</p>
<p>The economy of Andrijevica was struck heavily with the decline of the industry during the <a href="/wiki/Yugoslav_Wars" title="Yugoslav Wars">Yugoslav Wars</a>, hence population of municipality is declining slowly but steadily.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrijevica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andrijevica is connected with rest of Montenegro by two-laned motorways. Local roads connect Andrijevica with <a href="/wiki/Berane" title="Berane">Berane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kola%C5%A1in" title="Kolašin">Kolašin</a> (both around 30 km (19 mi) away), where local roads merge with <a href="/wiki/European_route_E65" title="European route E65">E65</a>/<a href="/wiki/European_route_E80" title="European route E80">E80</a> road, which is main road connection of Montenegro's coast and <a href="/wiki/Podgorica" title="Podgorica">Podgorica</a> with northern Montenegro and <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>. Andrijevica is on the corridor of the future <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beograd_-_Bar_motorway" title="Beograd - Bar motorway">Beograd - Bar motorway</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Podgorica_Airport" title="Podgorica Airport">Podgorica Airport</a> is around 120 km (75 mi) away, and has regular flights to major European destinations.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrijevica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andrijevica is surrounded by the mountains of Komovi, Bjelasica and Prokletije, situated on a terrace 40m above river Lim. Tourist activities in the area includes mountaineering (alpinism, hiking), biking, rafting, sports (soccer, bascetball), fishing, etc. Main tourist attraction is mountain Komovi (2461 m). This mountain can be accessed by a car in less than 45 minutes, by the mountainous Andrijevica-Mateševo road.</p>
