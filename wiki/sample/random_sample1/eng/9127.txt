<p><b>Andi Lila</b> (born 12 February 1986 in <a href="/wiki/Kavaj%C3%AB" title="Kavajë">Kavajë</a>) is an <a href="/wiki/Albanians" title="Albanians">Albanian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for Greek club <a href="/wiki/PAS_Giannina_F.C." title="PAS Giannina F.C.">PAS Giannina</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_football_team" title="Albania national football team">Albania national team</a>. He is a versatile player, having played primarily as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right_back_(association_football)" title="Right back (association football)">right back</a> early in his career as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Left_back" title="Left back">left back</a>, but in recent years he has predominately played in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defensive_midfielder" title="Defensive midfielder">defensive midfield</a> role, as well as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right_midfielder" title="Right midfielder">right midfielder</a>.</p>
<p>Lila began his career with local side <a href="/wiki/Besa_Kavaj%C3%AB" title="Besa Kavajë">Besa Kavajë</a>, where he made his debut in 2002 as a 16 year old and soon became a regular first team player, which earned him a move to Greek club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iraklis_FC" title="Iraklis FC">Iraklis</a> in January 2007. He soon returned to Besa Kavajë after failing to settle at Iraklis, and he shortly became the club captain, guiding them to a third-place finish in the <a href="/wiki/Albanian_Superliga" title="Albanian Superliga">Albanian Superliga</a> before moving to <a href="/wiki/KF_Tirana" title="KF Tirana">KF Tirana</a> after just one year back in Kavajë. He was a fan favourite at KF Tirana as during his three years at the club, and he won the Albanian Superliga title in his first season there, as well as helping his side reach the <a href="/wiki/Albanian_Cup" title="Albanian Cup">Albanian Cup</a> final which he missed and his side lost in. His last game with the club came in the 2011 Albanian Cup final which his side lost on penalties after extra time, before moving to Greece for a second stint, this time with <a href="/wiki/PAS_Giannina_F.C." title="PAS Giannina F.C.">PAS Giannina</a>.</p>
<p>He quickly established himself in the first team at PAS Giannina and helped his side achieve a mid table finish in his first season back in Greece, and then reach the Superleague playoffs in his second season at the club before a bottom half finish in his third season. He joined struggling <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parma_F.C." title="Parma F.C.">Parma</a> on loan for the second half of the 2014–15 campaign, but he could not help the club escape relegation and returned to his parent club PAS Giannina following the bankruptcy of Parma. Since his return he has struggled to recapture his place in the first team, and has been limited to mostly substitute appearances in the league.</p>
<p>Lila has represented Albania at <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_under-17_football_team" title="Albania national under-17 football team">under-17</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_under-19_football_team" title="Albania national under-19 football team">under-19</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_under-21_football_team" title="Albania national under-21 football team">under-21</a> and <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_football_team" title="Albania national football team">senior</a> levels. He made his senior international debut in 2007 against <a href="/wiki/Romania_national_football_team" title="Romania national football team">Romania</a> and has since earned 59 caps but has yet to score a senior international goal. He became Albania's first choice right back in 2010 under <a href="/wiki/Josip_Ku%C5%BEe" title="Josip Kuže">Josip Kuže</a>, and he also began to play in the defensive midfield position. Under <a href="/wiki/Gianni_De_Biasi" title="Gianni De Biasi">Gianni De Biasi</a> he has primarily played as a right midfielder or right winger since 2014, and he has helped Albania qualify for its first major international tournament as they reached <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euro_2016" title="Euro 2016">Euro 2016</a>. He was also heavily involved in the infamous <a href="/wiki/Serbia_v_Albania_(UEFA_Euro_2016_qualifying)" title="Serbia v Albania (UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying)">Serbia v Albania match</a> on 14 October 2014, which resulted in the match being abandoned and Albania eventually being awarded a walkover victory over Serbia.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andi_Lila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Besa_Kavaj.C3.AB">Besa Kavajë</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andi_Lila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Besa Kavajë">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lila started his career with hometown club, <a href="/wiki/Besa_Kavaj%C3%AB" title="Besa Kavajë">Besa Kavajë</a>. He made his debut for the club during the 2002–03 season in the <a href="/wiki/Albanian_Superliga" title="Albanian Superliga">Albanian Superliga</a> on 14 December 2002 in an away match against <a href="/wiki/KF_Teuta_Durr%C3%ABs" title="KF Teuta Durrës">Teuta Durrës</a>. Lila came on as a substitute at half time by replacing Ervin Bardhi. However it was not a game to remember for the young Lila as it finished 5–0 to Teuta Durrës.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Iraklis">Iraklis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andi_Lila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Iraklis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since making his debut in 2002 he started to impress more and more people with his performances as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right_back" title="Right back">right back</a>, this paved the way for his move to Greek club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iraklis_FC" title="Iraklis FC">Iraklis FC</a> in January 2007, where he joined fellow <a href="/wiki/Albanians" title="Albanians">Albanians</a> <a href="/wiki/Enea_Koliqi" title="Enea Koliqi">Enea Koliqi</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elvis_Kaja" title="Elvis Kaja">Elvis Kaja</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indrit_Fortuzi" title="Indrit Fortuzi">Indrit Fortuzi</a> who were already at the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Despite making his dream move abroad a reality, Lila struggled to settle in Greece and after only 6 months and 3 appearances he moved back to his original club <a href="/wiki/Besa_Kavaj%C3%AB" title="Besa Kavajë">Besa Kavajë</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Besa_Kavaj.C3.AB">Return to Besa Kavajë</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andi_Lila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Return to Besa Kavajë">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following his return, he was made captain of the team. During the 2007–08 season he played 26 times without scoring a goal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He was also a beaten <a href="/wiki/Albanian_Supercup" title="Albanian Supercup">Albanian Supercup</a> finalist after losing 4–2 to <a href="/wiki/KF_Tirana" title="KF Tirana">Tirana</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The defender played his final game with Besa Kavajë in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Intertoto_Cup" title="UEFA Intertoto Cup">Intertoto Cup</a> on 12 July 2008. He came off in the 57th minute of the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The game ended in a 3–0 home loss and ended the club's run in Europe and Lila's time in <a href="/wiki/Kavaj%C3%AB" title="Kavajë">Kavajë</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tirana">Tirana</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andi_Lila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tirana">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lila_Andi.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1437" data-file-width="1030" height="307" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Lila_Andi.jpg/220px-Lila_Andi.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Lila_Andi.jpg/330px-Lila_Andi.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Lila_Andi.jpg/440px-Lila_Andi.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Lila completed his move to <a href="/wiki/KF_Tirana" title="KF Tirana">Tirana</a> on 21 July 2008 after him and his agent Sokol Haxhia agreed to terms with <a href="/wiki/KF_Tirana" title="KF Tirana">KF Tirana</a> and its president <a href="/wiki/Refik_Halili" title="Refik Halili">Refik Halili</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Since making his move to the capital, Lila has been able to settle quickly and he has played the best football of his career so far. His first game as a Tirana player was the opening game of the season against <a href="/wiki/KF_Vllaznia_Shkod%C3%ABr" title="KF Vllaznia Shkodër">Vllaznia Shkodër</a>, he played the full 90 minutes of the goalless draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> His first goal in a blue and white jersey came on only his third game with the club in a <a href="/wiki/Tirana" title="Tirana">Tirana</a> derby against <a href="/wiki/FK_Partizani_Tirana" title="FK Partizani Tirana">Partizani Tirana</a>, he scored the winner in the 90th minute and secured the three points for his club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lila didn't have to wait long for another goal as it came less than a week later on 20 September 2008 against <a href="/wiki/KF_Apolonia_Fier" title="KF Apolonia Fier">Apolonia Fier</a>, his goal came in the 28th minute of the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> During the entire 2008–09 season he became the club's first choice <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right_back" title="Right back">right back</a> and he played 29 games, he also proved that he has attacking qualities as well by scoring 4 goals during the campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Tirana concluded the league season by winning its 24 title in history, leaving Vllaznia Shkodër four points behind.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Tirana also went into the final of <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Albanian_Cup" title="2008–09 Albanian Cup">2008–09 Albanian Cup</a>, where "The White and Blues" were defeated 2–1 by <a href="/wiki/Flamurtari_Vlor%C3%AB" title="Flamurtari Vlorë">Flamurtari Vlorë</a>, in a match where Lila was an unused substitute.</p>
<p>Lila started his second season with Tirana on 15 July 2009 by making his European debut with Tirana against <a href="/wiki/Stab%C3%A6k_Fotball" title="Stabæk Fotball">Stabæk</a> in the first leg of <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_UEFA_Champions_League_qualifying_phase_and_play-off_round" title="2009–10 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round">2009–10 UEFA Champions League</a>'s first round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He was a starter even in the returning leg with Tirana who was defeated 4–0 at <a href="/wiki/Telenor_Arena" title="Telenor Arena">Telenor Arena</a>, resulting in a 5–1 aggregate exit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Later, on 16 August, Lila won his second trophy with Tirana, the <a href="/wiki/Albanian_Supercup" title="Albanian Supercup">Albanian Supercup</a>, where he aided the team to beat Flamurtari Vlorë 1–0 at <a href="/wiki/Qemal_Stafa_Stadium" title="Qemal Stafa Stadium">Qemal Stafa Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="PAS_Giannina">PAS Giannina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andi_Lila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: PAS Giannina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He signed a three-year contract with Greek side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PAS_Giannina" title="PAS Giannina">PAS Giannina</a> on 24 June 2011 worth €700,000 plus bonuses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> He played his first game for the club on 27 August 2011 against <a href="/wiki/Aris_Thessaloniki" title="Aris Thessaloniki">Aris Thessaloniki</a> in a goalless draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 12 July 2014, Lila had even declared that his contract with PAS Giannina had come to an end, allowing him to sign a contract with <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatian</a> side <a href="/wiki/HNK_Rijeka" title="HNK Rijeka">HNK Rijeka</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> however he eventually opted against this and signed a new three-year contract with PAS Giannina.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_to_Parma">Loan to Parma</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andi_Lila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Loan to Parma">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After much speculation over his future, on 28 December 2014 he confirmed his move to <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parma_F.C." title="Parma F.C.">Parma F.C.</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He joined the club on 1 January 2015, when the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Italian_football_transfers_winter_2014%E2%80%9315" title="List of Italian football transfers winter 2014–15">transfer window</a> reopened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> He said that moving in <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> is a dream come true, adding that Serie A is the best league in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On 12 January 2015 Parma announced that the deal was a temporary deal with an option to purchase,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> due to ongoing financial difficulties of the club.</p>
<p>He made his debut for Parma on 14 January 2015, starting in the <a href="/wiki/Coppa_Italia" title="Coppa Italia">Coppa Italia</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Cagliari_Calcio" title="Cagliari Calcio">Cagliari</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> After 13 minutes however Lila was forced off the field with a thigh muscle problem.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In his first game back after returning from injury, Lila scored his first goal for Parma against <a href="/wiki/U.S._Sassuolo_Calcio" title="U.S. Sassuolo Calcio">Sassuolo</a> on 15 March 2015, having played from the start, with the match ending as a 4–1 defeat for the <i>Gialloblù</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_PAS_Giannina">Return to PAS Giannina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andi_Lila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Return to PAS Giannina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the first days of August 2015, Lila started training with <a href="/wiki/KF_Sk%C3%ABnderbeu_Kor%C3%A7%C3%AB" title="KF Skënderbeu Korçë">Skënderbeu Korçë</a>, although he eventually didn't signed a contract with them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Following that, Lila left the team to return to his parent club <a href="/wiki/PAS_Giannina_F.C." title="PAS Giannina F.C.">PAS Giannina</a> for the new season, where he was acollated his old squad number 3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andi_Lila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lila was first called up to the <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_football_team" title="Albania national football team">Albania national team</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Otto_Baric" title="Otto Baric">Otto Baric</a> in late 2007; it was his performances as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right_back" title="Right back">right back</a> for <a href="/wiki/Besa_Kavaj%C3%AB" title="Besa Kavajë">Besa Kavajë</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Albanian_Superliga" title="Albanian Superliga">Albanian Superliga</a> that caught the eye of the Croatian. He made his debut for the senior team in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2008 qualifying">Euro 2008 qualifiers</a> against <a href="/wiki/Romania_national_football_team" title="Romania national football team">Romania</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bucarest" title="Bucarest">Bucarest</a> on 21 November 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Lila started the game at right back and played the full 90 minutes. However it wasn't a debut to remember because the match ended 6–1 to Romania and the coach that had called the young defender up to the senior squad quit as the head coach of the <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_football_team" title="Albania national football team">Albanian National Team</a> just one hour after the final whistle of the embarrassing defeat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>The next manager of the national team also liked Lila's play and this earned him a call up by <a href="/wiki/Arie_Haan" title="Arie Haan">Arie Haan</a> to his first game in charge which was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friendly_match" title="Friendly match">friendly</a> against <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_football_team" title="Poland national football team">Poland</a> on 27 May 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Lila started the game but was substituted at half time for <a href="/wiki/Endrit_Vrapi" title="Endrit Vrapi">Endrit Vrapi</a>. The game ended in a 1–0 loss but the <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_football_team" title="Albania national football team">Albanians</a> played well after conceding in the 3rd minute. Andi Lila managed to play another two games under <a href="/wiki/Arie_Haan" title="Arie Haan">Arie Haan</a>, in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friendly_match" title="Friendly match">friendly</a> against <a href="/wiki/Liechtenstein_national_football_team" title="Liechtenstein national football team">Liechtenstein</a> and a <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup_qualification" title="FIFA World Cup qualification">2010 World Cup qualifier</a> against <a href="/wiki/Malta_national_football_team" title="Malta national football team">Malta</a>. In both matches he came on as a substitute.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Euro_2016">Euro 2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andi_Lila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Euro 2016">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 21 May 2016, Lila was named in Albania's preliminary 27-man squad for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">UEFA Euro 2016</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> and in Albania's final 23-man UEFA Euro 2016 squad on 31 May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lila was an unused substitute against <a href="/wiki/Switzerland_national_football_team" title="Switzerland national football team">Switzerland</a> in the opening match then played as a starter against hosts of <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> where they lost 2–0 and played the full 90-minutes match against <a href="/wiki/Romania_national_football_team" title="Romania national football team">Romania</a> in an Albania's historical 1–0 win with a goal scored by <a href="/wiki/Armando_Sadiku" title="Armando Sadiku">Armando Sadiku</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Albania finished the group in the third position with three points and with a goal difference –2, and was ranked last in the third-placed teams, which eventually eliminated them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andi_Lila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He is married with his partner Ornela Lila, and they become parents for the first time on 20 March 2015 when Lila's wife gave birth to the couple's first son, named Ayan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> On 16 July 2015 Lila was decorated with the <i>"Honorary Citizen of Kavaja"</i> award for protecting the national symbols and for his contribution to Albanian football.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Andi_Lila_shpallet_.E2.80.9CQytetar_Nderi_i_Kavaj.C3.ABs.E2.80.9D_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-Andi_Lila_shpallet_.E2.80.9CQytetar_Nderi_i_Kavaj.C3.ABs.E2.80.9D-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
