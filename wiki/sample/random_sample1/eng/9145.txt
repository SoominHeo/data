<p>The <b>province of Ancona</b> (<a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <span lang="it" xml:lang="it"><i><b>provincia di Ancona</b></i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Italy" title="Provinces of Italy">province</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Marche" title="Marche">Marche</a> region of central <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. Its capital is the city of <a href="/wiki/Ancona" title="Ancona">Ancona</a>, and the province borders the <a href="/wiki/Adriatic_Sea" title="Adriatic Sea">Adriatic Sea</a>. The city of Ancona is also the capital of Marche.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>To the north, the province is bordered by the <a href="/wiki/Adriatic_Sea" title="Adriatic Sea">Adriatic Sea</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-italia.it_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-italia.it-2">[2]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Apennine_Mountains" title="Apennine Mountains">Apennine Mountains</a> to the west. The population of the province is mostly located in coastal areas and in the provincial capital Ancona, which has a population of 101,518; the province has a total population of 477,892 as of 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tutt_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-tutt-3">[3]</a></sup> Due to its coastal location, it is strategically important.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-italia.it_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-italia.it-2">[2]</a></sup> The president of the province is Liana Serrani.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tutt_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-tutt-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Its coastline of sandy beaches is popular to Italians but has not been greatly affected by tourism. A large area of the province's land is farmland often used for wine production; the province produces wines using the Montepulciano, Sangiovese, and Verdicchio varieties of grape. Annually, feasts occur in the province during the harvesting period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It contains mountainous regions and the Conero Regional Park, which contain dense forests<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-italia.it_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-italia.it-2">[2]</a></sup> where <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_truffle" title="Black truffle">black truffles</a> are found. These are sold in <a href="/wiki/Acqualagna" title="Acqualagna">Acqualagna</a> in the neighbouring province of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Province_of_Pesaro_e_Urbino" title="Province of Pesaro e Urbino">Pesaro e Urbino</a>.</p>
<p>Famous people born of the province of Ancona include <a href="/wiki/Frederick_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Frederick II, Holy Roman Emperor">Frederick II, Holy Roman Emperor</a> (Jesi);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> International Gothic painter <a href="/wiki/Gentile_da_Fabriano" title="Gentile da Fabriano">Gentile da Fabriano</a> (Fabriano); writer <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Sabatini" title="Rafael Sabatini">Rafael Sabatini</a> (Jesi);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> composer <a href="/wiki/Gaspare_Spontini" title="Gaspare Spontini">Gaspare Spontini</a> (Maiolati, which has since been named after him as <a href="/wiki/Maiolati_Spontini" title="Maiolati Spontini">Maiolati Spontini</a>); composer <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Battista_Pergolesi" title="Giovanni Battista Pergolesi">Giovanni Battista Pergolesi</a> (Jesi); mathematician and physicist <a href="/wiki/Vito_Volterra" title="Vito Volterra">Vito Volterra</a> (Ancona); footballer <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Mancini" title="Roberto Mancini">Roberto Mancini</a> (Jesi); <a href="/wiki/Pope_Leo_XII" title="Pope Leo XII">Pope Leo XII</a> (Genga); <a href="/wiki/Pope_Pius_IX" title="Pope Pius IX">Pope Pius IX</a> (Senigallia);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and actress <a href="/wiki/Virna_Lisi" title="Virna Lisi">Virna Lisi</a> (Jesi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Domenico2002_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Domenico2002-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
