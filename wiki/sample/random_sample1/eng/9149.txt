<p><b>Antônio Naelson Matias</b> (May 23, 1976), known as <b>Sinha</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> or <b>Zinha</b>, is a Mexican professional footballer who plays for <a href="/wiki/Deportivo_Toluca_F.C." title="Deportivo Toluca F.C.">Deportivo Toluca</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Liga_MX" title="Liga MX">Liga MX</a>. He played for <a href="/wiki/Mexico_national_football_team" title="Mexico national football team">Mexico</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 World Cup</a>.</p>
<p>A creative <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Attacking_midfielder" title="Attacking midfielder">attacking midfielder</a>, Naelson is known for his vision, pace, dribbling and passing ability. He is also an accurate set-piece taker.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sinha_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sinha was born in <a href="/wiki/Itaj%C3%A1,_Rio_Grande_do_Norte" title="Itajá, Rio Grande do Norte">Itajá</a>, a city located in the northwest of the <a href="/wiki/States_of_Brazil" title="States of Brazil">Brazilian state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_do_Norte" title="Rio Grande do Norte">Rio Grande do Norte</a>. He began his career playing for Rio Grande do Norte's capital city team <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/America_de_Natal" title="America de Natal">América FC</a>.</p>
<p>Sinha measures 163 cm (5 ft 4 in) and weighs 67 kg (147 lb).</p>
<p>Sinha arrived in 1998 to Mexico, when he joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CF_Monterrey" title="CF Monterrey">CF Monterrey</a>. On 1999, he moved to Toluca. Sinha is widely known for his dribbling skills, his accurate passing and his intelligence on the pitch as well as his leadership and long distance shot.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sinha_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He was part of the Mexican 2004 Olympic football team, who exited in the first round, having finished third in group A, below group winners <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>.</p>
<p>He has also been called up to play for <a href="/wiki/Mexico_national_football_team" title="Mexico national football team">Mexico</a>, although he is a Brazilian-born player, he became a <a href="/wiki/Naturalization" title="Naturalization">naturalized</a> Mexican citizen, after settling in Mexico for several years. During the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a> in Germany, Sinha became the first foreign-born player to score a goal for Mexico in a World Cup tournament, scoring against <a href="/wiki/Iran_national_football_team" title="Iran national football team">Iran</a>. He was called up to play again for Mexico, thus making him the first naturalized to be called up by <a href="/wiki/Hugo_S%C3%A1nchez" title="Hugo Sánchez">Hugo Sánchez</a>.</p>
<p>In 2005, he played in the <a href="/wiki/2005_FIFA_Confederations_Cup" title="2005 FIFA Confederations Cup">Confederations Cup</a> scoring a goal against <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_football_team" title="Japan national football team">Japan</a> that helped Mexico win the match. Mexico finished in fourth place in the tournament. He was also called up for the national team to play in the <a href="/wiki/CONCACAF_Gold_Cup" title="CONCACAF Gold Cup">Gold Cup</a>. On May 23, 2010 Sinha won his 5th championship on his birthday, but missed a penalty in the penalty kicks phase.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/2011_CONCACAF_Gold_Cup" title="2011 CONCACAF Gold Cup">2011 CONCACAF Gold Cup</a>, Sinha, and four other members of the <a href="/wiki/Mexico_national_football_team" title="Mexico national football team">Mexico national team</a> tested positive for the banned substance of <a href="/wiki/Clenbuterol" title="Clenbuterol">Clenbuterol</a> and were removed from the team's tournament squad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> However, all players were exonerated as FIFA determined that the accused had accidentally ingested the banned substance through contaminated meat that had been served during a pre-tournament training camp.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>However, <a href="/wiki/World_Anti-Doping_Agency" title="World Anti-Doping Agency">World Anti-Doping Agency</a> appealed to the <a href="/wiki/Court_of_Arbitration_for_Sport" title="Court of Arbitration for Sport">Court of Arbitration for Sport</a> to request a ban.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> But on 12 October 2011 WADA withdrew this request after the full file was available for them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
