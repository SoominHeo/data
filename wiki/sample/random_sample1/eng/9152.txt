<div class="hatnote" role="note">This name uses <a href="/wiki/Spanish_naming_customs" title="Spanish naming customs">Spanish naming customs</a>: the first or paternal <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">family name</a> is <i>López</i> and the second or maternal family name is <i>Guerrero</i>.</div>
<p><b>Antonio López Guerrero</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[anˈtonjo ˈlopeθ ɣeˈreɾo]</a></span>; born 13 September 1981) is a Spanish former <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played mainly as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">left back</a>.</p>
<p>He spent most of his professional career with <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a>, appearing in more than 300 official games and winning four major titles, including two <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">Europa League</a> trophies. Also in <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a>, he played for <a href="/wiki/CA_Osasuna" title="CA Osasuna">Osasuna</a>.</p>
<p>López represented <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 World Cup</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_L%C3%B3pez_(footballer,_born_1981)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atl.C3.A9tico_Madrid">Atlético Madrid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_L%C3%B3pez_(footballer,_born_1981)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Atlético Madrid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A product of <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a>'s youth system, López was born in <a href="/wiki/Benidorm" title="Benidorm">Benidorm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Alicante" title="Province of Alicante">Province of Alicante</a>. He first appeared for the first team in <a href="/wiki/2000%E2%80%9301_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2000–01 Segunda División">2000–01</a> whilst the <i>Colchoneros</i> were in the <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">second division</a>, and contributed with 20 matches <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2001–02 Segunda División">the following season</a> for a <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> return, after a two-year <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/hiatus" title="wikt:hiatus">hiatus</a>.</p>
<p>Subsequently, López was loaned for two seasons to fellow top division club <a href="/wiki/CA_Osasuna" title="CA Osasuna">CA Osasuna</a>, where he missed only five league games combined. During his <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">Navarre</a> spell he scored twice (both of the goals coming in <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_La_Liga" title="2002–03 La Liga">2002–03</a>), most notably in a 1–0 home success against <a href="/wiki/RCD_Espanyol" title="RCD Espanyol">RCD Espanyol</a> on 15 December 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In his second spell with Atlético, López quickly gained first-choice status. However, during the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_La_Liga" title="2007–08 La Liga">2007–08 campaign</a> he lost his position to <a href="/wiki/Mariano_Pern%C3%ADa" title="Mariano Pernía">Mariano Pernía</a>, and would end serving more time at right-back due to injuries to teammates <a href="/wiki/Giourkas_Seitaridis" title="Giourkas Seitaridis">Giourkas Seitaridis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Valera_Esp%C3%ADn" title="Juan Valera Espín">Juan Valera</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> when selected, he was the side's undisputed <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 15 March 2009, López scored the winner through a rare header as Atlético came from behind 0–2 to beat <a href="/wiki/Villarreal_CF" title="Villarreal CF">Villarreal CF</a> at home.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> After the coach change, with former club player <a href="/wiki/Abel_Resino" title="Abel Resino">Abel Resino</a> taking over for <a href="/wiki/Javier_Aguirre" title="Javier Aguirre">Javier Aguirre</a>, he was made the starter, but an injury made Pernía finish <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_La_Liga" title="2008–09 La Liga">the season</a> in the starting XI.</p>
<p>Benefitting from a road accident to Pernía in the summer, López played most of <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_La_Liga" title="2009–10 La Liga">2009–10</a>. On 2 January 2010, again with his head – but in the <a href="/wiki/Last-minute_goal" title="Last-minute goal">last minute</a> – he netted against <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla FC</a> at home to make it 2–1;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> on 1 April he scored in the same fashion, making it 2–1 at <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF" title="Valencia CF">Valencia CF</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2009–10 UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> (eventually 2–2),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> a competition which Atlético won in <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a> with the player playing the entire 120 minutes of the <a href="/wiki/2010_UEFA_Europa_League_Final" title="2010 UEFA Europa League Final">2–1 win</a> against <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a>, and lifting the trophy as captain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mallorca">Mallorca</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_L%C3%B3pez_(footballer,_born_1981)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mallorca">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In late May 2012, after 343 official games in ten senior seasons, López was released by Atlético.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The following month he signed for two years with fellow league side <a href="/wiki/RCD_Mallorca" title="RCD Mallorca">RCD Mallorca</a>, with an option for a further season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_L%C3%B3pez_(footballer,_born_1981)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>López made his debut with <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a> on 30 March 2005, in a <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a> <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2006 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">qualifier</a> against <a href="/wiki/Serbia_and_Montenegro_national_football_team" title="Serbia and Montenegro national football team">Serbia and Montenegro</a> in Belgrade (0–0). During the successful campaign he scored his first international goal, at <a href="/wiki/San_Marino_national_football_team" title="San Marino national football team">San Marino</a> on 12 October, hitting home from 25 metres in the first minute of a 6–0 routing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Supposed to back up <a href="/wiki/Asier_del_Horno" title="Asier del Horno">Asier del Horno</a> during the final stages, López would not benefit from the former's injury days before the tournament started, as <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentine</a>-born Pernía would be promoted to first-choice. He would only appear against <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia_national_football_team" title="Saudi Arabia national football team">Saudi Arabia</a> in Germany,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> with Spain being eliminated in the <a href="/wiki/Single-elimination_tournament" title="Single-elimination tournament">round-of-16</a>.</p>
