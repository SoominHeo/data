<p><b>Antonio Pettigrew</b> (November 3, 1967 – August 10, 2010) was an American sprinter who specialized in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/400_meters" title="400 meters">400 meters</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_Pettigrew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was born in <a href="/wiki/Macon,_Georgia" title="Macon, Georgia">Macon, Georgia</a>.</p>
<p>While attending <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Augustine%27s_University" title="St. Augustine's University">St. Augustine's College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Raleigh,_North_Carolina" title="Raleigh, North Carolina">Raleigh, North Carolina</a>, he was a four-time <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NCAA" title="NCAA">NCAA</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Division_II_(NCAA)" title="Division II (NCAA)">Division II</a> champion in the 400 meter race.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SI_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-SI-1">[1]</a></sup> He came to prominence at the <a href="/wiki/1991_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1991 World Championships in Athletics">1991 World Championships</a>, where he won the 400 m gold medal and a silver medal in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/4_x_400_meters_relay" title="4 x 400 meters relay">4 x 400 meters relay</a>.</p>
<p>At the Summer Olympics in Sydney 2000, Pettigrew threw his gold medal-winning Adidas spikes into the crowd after winning the 4 × 400 m final for the USA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Doping">Doping</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_Pettigrew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Doping">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2008, prosecution documents related to the trial of coach <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Graham" title="Trevor Graham">Trevor Graham</a> listed Pettigrew as one of Graham's athletes to have used performance-enhancing drugs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Pettigrew then admitted to using performance-enhancing drugs and testified against Graham at his trial in May 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Although <a href="/wiki/International_Association_of_Athletics_Federations" title="International Association of Athletics Federations">IAAF</a> rules currently do not retroactively alter results more than eight years after the event, Pettigrew returned the medals he won in that period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC1-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The 2000 Sydney Olympics 4 × 400 m relay U.S. team was <a href="/wiki/List_of_stripped_Olympic_medals" title="List of stripped Olympic medals">stripped of their medals</a> after Pettigrew admitted that he had used performance-enhancing drugs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He received a two-year athletics ban in 2008, although he had already retired from the track.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_Pettigrew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pettigrew was found dead aged 42 in the back seat of his locked car in <a href="/wiki/Chatham_County,_North_Carolina" title="Chatham County, North Carolina">Chatham County, North Carolina</a>, on August 10, 2010, and evidence of sleeping pills was found by police. On October 13, an autopsy report stated that he had committed <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">suicide</a> by overdosing on a medication containing <a href="/wiki/Diphenhydramine" title="Diphenhydramine">diphenhydramine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Pettigrew was an assistant coach at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_North_Carolina_at_Chapel_Hill" title="University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill">University of North Carolina</a> at the time of his death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
