<p><b>Antonio Mariano Floro Flores</b> (born 18 June 1983) is an Italian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/F.C._Bari_1908" title="F.C. Bari 1908">Bari</a> on loan from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Chievo_Verona" title="A.C. Chievo Verona">Chievo</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_Floro_Flores&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Floro Flores was born in <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a>, and started his career at <a href="/wiki/S.S.C._Napoli" title="S.S.C. Napoli">S.S.C. Napoli</a>. He made his Serie A debut on 28 January 2001 against <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Roma</a>. After six months on loan to Serie A <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">Sampdoria</a>, Floro Flores transferred to <a href="/wiki/A.C._Perugia_Calcio" title="A.C. Perugia Calcio">Perugia</a> after the club had gone bankrupt. At Perugia, Floro Flores scored 8 goals in Serie B, and two more against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Treviso_F.B.C._1993" title="Treviso F.B.C. 1993">Treviso</a> in the promotion play-off second legs. But Perugia also faced bankruptcy, and Floro Flores joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Arezzo" title="A.C. Arezzo">Arezzo</a> in the summer of 2005. He remained at the club for two seasons and scored 28 goals in 79 league matches.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Udinese">Udinese</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_Floro_Flores&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Udinese">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After Arezzo got relegated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C1" title="Serie C1">Serie C1</a> on summer 2007, he was signed by <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese Calcio</a> in <a href="/wiki/Co-ownership_(football)" title="Co-ownership (football)">co-ownership deal</a>. In June 2008 Udinese bought Floro Flores outright. In January 2011 he was loaned to <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a> and was in fine form for his new club scoring 10 goals in 18 matches. In August 2011 he signed a new 5-year contract with Udinese.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> During the summer of 2012 he was sent on loan to <a href="/wiki/Granada_CF" title="Granada CF">Granada</a> in <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a>. On 16 September 2012 Antonio Floro Flores scored his first goal for Granada in the 82nd minute against Deportivo to lead his side to a come from behind draw.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Genoa">Genoa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_Floro_Flores&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Genoa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2013 he left for <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa C.F.C.</a> for about €6.3 million (cash plus 50% rights of <a href="/wiki/Masahudu_Alhassan" title="Masahudu Alhassan">Masahudu Alhassan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Merkel" title="Alexander Merkel">Alexander Merkel</a> which tagged for €1.5M and €4.0M respectively).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Genoa2013bilancio_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Genoa2013bilancio-2">[2]</a></sup> However he only scored 2 goals in half-season, 10 goals behind team top-scorer <a href="/wiki/Marco_Borriello" title="Marco Borriello">Marco Borriello</a>.</p>
<p>In 2013–14 season, despite Genoa did not purchase Borriello, as well as the sales of <a href="/wiki/Ciro_Immobile" title="Ciro Immobile">Ciro Immobile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Gilardino" title="Alberto Gilardino">Alberto Gilardino</a> was recalled; Floro Flores was the substitute in the first game of <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Coppa_Italia" title="2013–14 Coppa Italia">2013–14 Coppa Italia</a> and the first round of <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Serie_A" title="2013–14 Serie A">2013–14 Serie A</a>; in the second round (1 September) Floro Flores was the starting forward, however he was substituted in the 57th minute,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> as well as sold on the next day.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sassuolo">Sassuolo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_Floro_Flores&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sassuolo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 2 September 2013 Floro Flores left for <a href="/wiki/U.S._Sassuolo_Calcio" title="U.S. Sassuolo Calcio">Sassuolo</a> in temporary deal for €1 million<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Genoa2013bilancio_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Genoa2013bilancio-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sassuolo2014bilancio_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sassuolo2014bilancio-5">[5]</a></sup> with option to purchase.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He retained his no.83 shirt number for his new team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the end of season Sassuolo bought Floro Flores and <a href="/wiki/Davide_Biondini" title="Davide Biondini">Davide Biondini</a> outright, for €2.5 million<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sassuolo2014bilancio_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sassuolo2014bilancio-5">[5]</a></sup> and €1.3 million respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sassuolo2014bilancio_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Sassuolo2014bilancio-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chievo">Chievo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_Floro_Flores&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Chievo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 1 February 2016 Floro Flores was signed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Chievo_Verona" title="A.C. Chievo Verona">Chievo</a> in a temporary deal, replacing <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Paloschi" title="Alberto Paloschi">Alberto Paloschi</a>. Chievo also had an obligation to buy at the end of season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
