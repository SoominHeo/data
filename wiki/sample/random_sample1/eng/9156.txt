<p><b>António Alberto Bastos Pimparel</b> (born 1 May 1982), known as <b>Beto</b>, is a Portuguese professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/Sporting_Clube_de_Portugal" title="Sporting Clube de Portugal">Sporting Clube de Portugal</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">goalkeeper</a>.</p>
<p>He made a name for himself at <a href="/wiki/Leix%C3%B5es_S.C." title="Leixões S.C.">Leixões</a> before transferring to <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">Porto</a> in 2009, being a backup during two seasons. After one season in Romania with <a href="/wiki/CFR_Cluj" title="CFR Cluj">CFR Cluj</a> he joined <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla</a> in January 2013, going on appear in 87 competitive games and win two <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">Europa League</a> trophies.</p>
<p>Beto earned 11 <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">caps</a> for <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a>, being part of the squads in two <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cups</a> and <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">Euro 2012</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beto_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Beto was born in <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>. After unsuccessfully developing in local <a href="/wiki/Sporting_Clube_de_Portugal" title="Sporting Clube de Portugal">Sporting Clube de Portugal</a>'s youth system (he only played one season with the first team, being third-choice), he played one season on loan to neighbours <a href="/wiki/Casa_Pia_A.C." title="Casa Pia A.C.">Casa Pia AC</a>, being released in June 2004.</p>
<p>After one year each with <a href="/wiki/G.D._Chaves" title="G.D. Chaves">G.D. Chaves</a> and <a href="/wiki/F.C._Marco" title="F.C. Marco">F.C. Marco</a>, both in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Segunda_Liga" title="Segunda Liga">second division</a>, Beto joined <a href="/wiki/Leix%C3%B5es_S.C." title="Leixões S.C.">Leixões S.C.</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Liga_de_Honra" title="2006–07 Liga de Honra">2006–07 campaign</a>, helping the <a href="/wiki/Matosinhos" title="Matosinhos">Matosinhos</a> club return to the <a href="/wiki/Primeira_Liga" title="Primeira Liga">Primeira Liga</a> and only missing seven games in his three seasons combined.</p>
<p>In March 2009, before <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Primeira_Liga" title="2008–09 Primeira Liga">2008–09</a> ended, Beto agreed to a move to <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">FC Porto</a>, in a four-year deal worth <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>750.000 effective as of June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BETO_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-BETO-1">[1]</a></sup> During his two-season spell he acted almost exclusively as backup to Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Helton_Arruda" title="Helton Arruda">Helton</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> being mostly restricted to <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%A7a_de_Portugal" title="Taça de Portugal">Taça de Portugal</a> matches; on 22 May 2011, with the score at 2–2, he stopped a <a href="/wiki/Penalty_kick_(association_football)" title="Penalty kick (association football)">penalty kick</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2011_Ta%C3%A7a_de_Portugal_Final" title="2011 Taça de Portugal Final">domestic cup final</a>, with his team eventually winning 6–2 in <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>For <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Liga_I" title="2011–12 Liga I">2011–12</a>, Beto was loaned to Romanian side <a href="/wiki/CFR_Cluj" title="CFR Cluj">CFR Cluj</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> only missing seven <a href="/wiki/Liga_I" title="Liga I">Liga I</a> contests during the season for the eventual champions. Released by Porto he returned to his country in the following summer, signing for two years with <a href="/wiki/S.C._Braga" title="S.C. Braga">S.C. Braga</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> however, late into the following <a href="/wiki/Transfer_window" title="Transfer window">transfer window</a>, he moved to <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla FC</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spaniards</a> were looking to strengthen the position following the departure of <a href="/wiki/Diego_L%C3%B3pez_Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Diego López Rodríguez">Diego López</a> to <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a> – the loan was arranged until June, with the possibility of a permanent transfer afterwards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Profitting from injury to <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Palop" title="Andrés Palop">Andrés Palop</a>, Beto made his debut with the <a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">Andalusians</a> one day after arriving, starting in a 1–2 away loss against <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a> for <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Copa_del_Rey" title="2012–13 Copa del Rey">the season</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a> semi-final first leg, with his team playing the last eight minutes with only nine players.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> His first <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> match came on 3 February 2013, in a 2–1 home win over <a href="/wiki/Rayo_Vallecano" title="Rayo Vallecano">Rayo Vallecano</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>Beto was subsequently signed on a permanent basis,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> being first-choice for coach <a href="/wiki/Unai_Emery" title="Unai Emery">Unai Emery</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He contributed with 43 appearances all competitions comprised in the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_La_Liga" title="2013–14 La Liga">2013–14 campaign</a>, including ten in the team's <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2013–14 UEFA Europa League">victorious run</a> in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a>, where he saved two penalties from <a href="/wiki/%C3%93scar_Cardozo" title="Óscar Cardozo">Óscar Cardozo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rodrigo_(footballer,_born_1991)" title="Rodrigo (footballer, born 1991)">Rodrigo</a> in the final's <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shoot-out_(association_football)" title="Penalty shoot-out (association football)">shootout</a> against <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">S.L. Benfica</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Beto spent several months of <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_La_Liga" title="2014–15 La Liga">2014–15</a> on the sidelines, due to injury: first, a shoulder dislocation after a collision with <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Karim_Benzema" title="Karim Benzema">Karim Benzema</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> followed by an ailment to his left leg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> After finishing the season as backup to <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Rico" title="Sergio Rico">Sergio Rico</a>, he was reinstated by Emery for the <a href="/wiki/2015_UEFA_Super_Cup" title="2015 UEFA Super Cup">2015 UEFA Super Cup</a>, performing well overall but conceding five goals in the 4–5 loss to <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the emergence of <a href="/wiki/David_Soria" title="David Soria">David Soria</a> also from the club's youth system, Beto was demoted to third-choice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On 6 August 2016, the <a href="/wiki/Free_agent" title="Free agent">free agent</a> signed a two-year contract with former team Sporting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Beto made his debut in competitive games for his first club on 13 October 2016, at the age of 34 years and five months, keeping a <a href="/wiki/Shutout" title="Shutout">clean sheet</a> in a <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_Ta%C3%A7a_de_Portugal" title="2016–17 Taça de Portugal">1–0 away win</a> against <a href="/wiki/F.C._Famalic%C3%A3o" title="F.C. Famalicão">F.C. Famalicão</a> for the Portuguese Cup. He dedicated the performance to his late father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beto_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beto made his debut for <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a> on 10 June 2009, in a 0–0 <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_game" title="Exhibition game">friendly match</a> in <a href="/wiki/Estonia_national_football_team" title="Estonia national football team">Estonia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BETO_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-BETO-1">[1]</a></sup> After finishing <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Primeira_Liga" title="2009–10 Primeira Liga">2009–10</a> in Porto's starting XI, he was named by manager <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Queiroz" title="Carlos Queiroz">Carlos Queiroz</a> in a provisional 24-player list for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> making the final cut for the finals in South Africa where he did not leave the bench. He was also an unused part of the squad at <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">UEFA Euro 2012</a>.</p>
<p>Beto was also selected <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">four years later</a> for the tournament in Brazil,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> making his debut in <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">the tournament</a> on 22 June against the <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States men's national soccer team">United States</a> in the second group stage match, after starter <a href="/wiki/Rui_Patr%C3%ADcio" title="Rui Patrício">Rui Patrício</a> was injured in the first encounter against <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
