<p><b>Antonio Candido de Mello e Souza</b> (born July 24, 1918, <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> writer, professor, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sociologist" title="Sociologist">sociologist</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Literary_critic" title="Literary critic">literary critic</a>. As a critic of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brazilian_Literature" title="Brazilian Literature">Brazilian Literature</a>, he is regarded as one of the foremost <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scholars" title="Scholars">scholars</a> on the subject by Brazilian universities. He was the co-winner of the <a href="/wiki/Pr%C3%AAmio_Jabuti" title="Prêmio Jabuti">Prêmio Jabuti</a> for essays in 1965 and was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Pr%C3%AAmio_Machado_de_Assis" title="Prêmio Machado de Assis">Prêmio Machado de Assis</a> in 1993, the <a href="/wiki/Cam%C3%B5es_Prize" title="Camões Prize">Camões Prize</a> in 1998 and the <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_Reyes_International_Prize" title="Alfonso Reyes International Prize">Alfonso Reyes International Prize</a> in 2005.</p>
<p>Candido is professor-emeritus at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="University of São Paulo">University of São Paulo</a> and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_State_University" title="São Paulo State University">São Paulo State University</a>, and doctor <i>honoris causa</i> by the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Campinas" title="University of Campinas">University of Campinas</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_Candido&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Antonio Candido is the only child of Aristides Candido de Mello e Souza, M.D., and Clarisse Tolentino de Mello e Souza. Most of his childhood was spent in Brazilian countryside, in the states of <a href="/wiki/Minas_Gerais" title="Minas Gerais">Minas Gerais</a> and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>. During this period, he did not attend school, being taught at home by his mother. In, 1937 he and his family settled down in São Paulo, where he received formal education. In 1939, Candido began studying Law at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Faculdade_de_Direito_da_Universidade_de_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="Faculdade de Direito da Universidade de São Paulo">University of São Paulo School of Law</a>, a course he would eventually abandon in order to study philosophy at the same university.</p>
<p>His first critical works were published in 1941, in the magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Clima" title="Clima">Clima</a></i>, co-founded by himself, and, in the following year, he began teaching at the University of São Paulo, first in the department of sociology and later, a position he would hold, although with a brief interval from 1958 to 1960, for thirty-six years. Candido also taught Brazilian Literature at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Paris" title="University of Paris">University of Paris</a> from 1964 to 1966, and became <a href="/wiki/Visiting_scholar" title="Visiting scholar">visiting scholar</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> two years later.</p>
<p>His major work of literary criticism was published in 1959, entitled <i>Formação da Literatura Brasileira</i> (literary, "Formation of Brazilian Literature), a highly polemic and influential study of the foundations of his country literary arts.</p>
<p>Candido was also active in politics during many periods of his life: he was a militant against <a href="/wiki/Get%C3%BAlio_Vargas" title="Getúlio Vargas">Getúlio Vargas</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Estado_Novo_(Brazil)" title="Estado Novo (Brazil)">Estado Novo</a> and helped to found the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Partido_dos_Trabalhadores" title="Partido dos Trabalhadores">Worker's Party</a> in 1980.</p>
<p>He married Gilda de Melo e Sousa in 1943, a Brazilian essayist and fellow professor at the University of São Paulo, with whom he has three daughters.</p>
