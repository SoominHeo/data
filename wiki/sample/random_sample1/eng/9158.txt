<p><b>Antolín Alcaraz Viveros</b> (born 30 July 1982) is a Paraguayan professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/Club_Libertad" title="Club Libertad">Club Libertad</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">central defender</a>.</p>
<p>He rarely settled with a team in his early career, before signing in 2003 with <a href="/wiki/S.C._Beira-Mar" title="S.C. Beira-Mar">Beira-Mar</a> and remaining with the club five seasons, after which he joined <a href="/wiki/Club_Brugge_KV" title="Club Brugge KV">Club Brugge</a>. He spent most of the remainder of his career in the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>, mainly with <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a>.</p>
<p>Alcaraz represented <a href="/wiki/Paraguay_national_football_team" title="Paraguay national football team">Paraguay</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 World Cup</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2011_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2011 Copa América">2011 Copa América</a>, helping them to the final of the latter tournament.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antol%C3%ADn_Alcaraz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years_.2F_Beira-Mar">Early years / Beira-Mar</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antol%C3%ADn_Alcaraz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years / Beira-Mar">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hailing from a humble background, Alcaraz worked throughout his teenage years as a <a href="/wiki/Construction_worker" title="Construction worker">builder’s assistant</a> in his hometown of <a href="/wiki/San_Roque_Gonz%C3%A1lez_de_Santa_Cruz" title="San Roque González de Santa Cruz">San Roque González de Santa Cruz</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Paraguar%C3%AD_Department" title="Paraguarí Department">Paraguarí Department</a>. After starting at amateur football in his country, the 19-year-old moved to <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> and signed with <a href="/wiki/Racing_Club_de_Avellaneda" title="Racing Club de Avellaneda">Racing Club de Avellaneda</a>, appearing rarely during his spell. In 2002 he was loaned to <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">ACF Fiorentina</a>, but the <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italians</a> soon faced bankruptcy and regrouped in the <a href="/wiki/Lega_Pro_Seconda_Divisione" title="Lega Pro Seconda Divisione">Lega Pro Seconda Divisione</a>; he then had a trial at <a href="/wiki/U.S._Citt%C3%A0_di_Palermo" title="U.S. Città di Palermo">U.S. Città di Palermo</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> but nothing came of it.</p>
<p>In January 2003, Alcaraz signed for <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> club <a href="/wiki/S.C._Beira-Mar" title="S.C. Beira-Mar">S.C. Beira-Mar</a>. After seven appearances in half a <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_Primeira_Liga" title="2002–03 Primeira Liga">season</a> he became a defensive mainstay at the <a href="/wiki/Aveiro,_Portugal" title="Aveiro, Portugal">Aveiro</a> team, eventually also gaining <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">club captaincy</a>; in <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Liga_de_Honra" title="2005–06 Liga de Honra">2005–06</a> he helped them return to the <a href="/wiki/Primeira_Liga" title="Primeira Liga">Primeira Liga</a>, playing 31 matches.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brugge">Brugge</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antol%C3%ADn_Alcaraz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Brugge">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 30 April 2007, Alcaraz was signed by <a href="/wiki/Club_Brugge_KV" title="Club Brugge KV">Club Brugge KV</a> in Belgium, with the deal being effective as of July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>After a slow <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Belgian_First_Division" title="2007–08 Belgian First Division">first season</a> he also eventually became first-choice, helping his team to two third-places and one second.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wigan_Athletic">Wigan Athletic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antol%C3%ADn_Alcaraz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Wigan Athletic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 14 May 2010, Alcaraz signed with <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a> for an undisclosed fee,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> as his contract with Brugge was due to expire at the end of June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He scored his first goal for the club against <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a> on 11 September 2010, netting in the 86th minute for an eventual 1–1 home draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 6 November 2011, Alcaraz caused widespread controversy when replays showed him spitting at <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a> defender <a href="/wiki/Richard_Stearman" title="Richard Stearman">Richard Stearman</a> during Wigan's 1–3 defeat at <a href="/wiki/Molineux_Stadium" title="Molineux Stadium">Molineux Stadium</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and served a three-game suspension due to his actions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He later issued an apology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 7 May 2012, Alcaraz scored the game's only goal at <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> to retain the Latics' top division safety, whilst relegating their opponent to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Football League Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He won the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_FA_Cup" title="2012–13 FA Cup">2013 FA Cup</a>, featuring the full 90 minutes in the <a href="/wiki/2013_FA_Cup_Final" title="2013 FA Cup Final">1–0 upset</a> against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> However, only three days later, with him on the pitch again, Wigan were relegated from the top division following a 1–4 defeat at <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Everton">Everton</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antol%C3%ADn_Alcaraz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Everton">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 9 July 2013, after his contract at Wigan expired,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Alcaraz joined <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> on a two-year deal as both he and goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Joel_Robles" title="Joel Robles">Joel Robles</a> followed their manager <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Mart%C3%ADnez" title="Roberto Martínez">Roberto Martínez</a> to the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He made his competitive debut on 29 December after an injury struck his start to <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Premier_League" title="2013–14 Premier League">the season</a>, featuring the full 90 minutes in a 2–1 home win over <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 26 April 2014, Alcaraz scored an <a href="/wiki/Own_goal" title="Own goal">own goal</a> in the first minute of a 0–2 loss to Southampton.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On 1 January of the following year, he received his first <a href="/wiki/Ejection_(sports)" title="Ejection (sports)">sending off</a> as an Everton player, being shown two yellow cards in a 0–2 defeat at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hull_City_F.C." title="Hull City F.C.">Hull City</a> which was the team's fourth successive of <a href="/wiki/Boxing_Day" title="Boxing Day">the festive period</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 10 June 2015, Alcaraz was released by the Toffees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Las_Palmas">Las Palmas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antol%C3%ADn_Alcaraz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Las Palmas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 2 August 2015, aged nearly 33, Alcaraz signed a one-year deal with newly promoted <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> team <a href="/wiki/UD_Las_Palmas" title="UD Las Palmas">UD Las Palmas</a> with the option of a second one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He scored his first goal on 23 September, helping defeat <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla FC</a> 2–0 at the <i><a href="/wiki/Estadio_Gran_Canaria" title="Estadio Gran Canaria">Estadio Gran Canaria</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antol%C3%ADn_Alcaraz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In November 2008, Alcaraz received his first call-up to the <a href="/wiki/Paraguay_national_football_team" title="Paraguay national football team">Paraguayan national team</a> at the age of 26.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He was picked for the squad present at the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a>; on 14 June, in the group stage opener against <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a>, in just his seventh <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">cap</a>, he headed home through a powerful header following a <a href="/wiki/Set_piece_(football)" title="Set piece (football)">set piece</a> in an eventual 1–1 draw in <a href="/wiki/Cape_Town" title="Cape Town">Cape Town</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> and played all the matches and minutes for the eventual quarter-finalists.</p>
<p>Alcaraz was again first-choice for Paraguay during the <a href="/wiki/2011_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2011 Copa América">2011 Copa América</a>, held in <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>. On 13 July, he scored the 1–1 equalizer against <a href="/wiki/Venezuela_national_football_team" title="Venezuela national football team">Venezuela</a>, in an eventual 3–3 group stage draw;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> four days later, in the quarter-finals with <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a>, he was sent off after an altercation with <a href="/wiki/Lucas_Leiva" title="Lucas Leiva">Lucas Leiva</a>, and the national side eventually finished runner-up.</p>
