<p><b>Antuco</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chilean</a> <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_Chile" title="Communes of Chile">commune</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_towns_in_Chile" title="List of towns in Chile">town</a> in <a href="/wiki/B%C3%ADo_B%C3%ADo_Province" title="Bío Bío Province">Bío Bío Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/B%C3%ADo_B%C3%ADo_Region" title="Bío Bío Region">Bío Bío Region</a>.</p>
<p>The commune includes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laguna_del_Laja" title="Laguna del Laja">Laguna del Laja</a> and <a href="/wiki/Laguna_del_Laja_National_Park" title="Laguna del Laja National Park">Laguna del Laja National Park</a>, which contains <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antuco_Volcano" title="Antuco Volcano">Antuco Volcano</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Velluda" title="Sierra Velluda">Sierra Velluda</a>. The latter is the tallest mountain in Bío Bío Region. The town of Antuco is located slightly south of the south bank of the <a href="/wiki/Laja_River_(Chile)" title="Laja River (Chile)">Laja River</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antuco,_Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the 2002 <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Statistics_Institute_(Chile)" title="National Statistics Institute (Chile)">National Statistics Institute</a>, Antuco spans an area of 1,884.1 km<sup>2</sup> (727 sq mi) and has 3,908 inhabitants (1,981 men and 1,927 women). Of these, 1,978 (50.6%) lived in <a href="/wiki/Urban_area" title="Urban area">urban areas</a> and 1,930 (49.4%) in <a href="/wiki/Rural_area" title="Rural area">rural areas</a>. The population fell by 3.8% (154 persons) between the 1992 and 2002 censuses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-INE_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-INE-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antuco,_Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a commune, Antuco is a third-level <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_division_of_Chile" title="Administrative division of Chile">administrative division of Chile</a> administered by a <a href="/wiki/Municipal_council" title="Municipal council">municipal council</a>, headed by an <a href="/wiki/Alcalde" title="Alcalde">alcalde</a> who is directly elected every four years. The 2008-2012 alcalde is Claudio Edelberto Solar Jara (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_Democratic_Party" title="Christian Democratic Party">PDC</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Munitel_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Munitel-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Electoral_divisions_of_Chile" title="Electoral divisions of Chile">electoral divisions of Chile</a>, Antuco is represented in the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_Chile" title="Chamber of Deputies of Chile">Chamber of Deputies</a> by Juan Lobos (<a href="/wiki/Independent_Democratic_Union" title="Independent Democratic Union">UDI</a>) and José Pérez (<a href="/wiki/Social_Democrat_Radical_Party" title="Social Democrat Radical Party">PRSD</a>) as part of the 47th electoral district, (together with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_%C3%81ngeles,_B%C3%ADo_B%C3%ADo" title="Los Ángeles, Bío Bío">Los Ángeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tucapel" title="Tucapel">Tucapel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quilleco" title="Quilleco">Quilleco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santa_B%C3%A1rbara,_Chile" title="Santa Bárbara, Chile">Santa Bárbara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quilaco" title="Quilaco">Quilaco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mulch%C3%A9n" title="Mulchén">Mulchén</a>, <a href="/wiki/Negrete" title="Negrete">Negrete</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nacimiento,_Chile" title="Nacimiento, Chile">Nacimiento</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Rosendo" title="San Rosendo">San Rosendo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laja,_Chile" title="Laja, Chile">Laja</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alto_B%C3%ADo_B%C3%ADo" title="Alto Bío Bío">Alto Bío Bío</a>). The commune is represented in the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Chile" title="Senate of Chile">Senate</a> by Victor Pérez Varela (UDI) and Mariano Ruiz -Esquide Jara (PDC) as part of the 13th senatorial constituency (Biobío-Coast).</p>
