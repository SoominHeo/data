<p><b>Anthimos Kapsis</b> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el">Άνθιμος Καψής</span>; born 3 September 1950) is a <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek</a> former International <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> player.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthimos_Kapsis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Astypalaia" title="Astypalaia">Astypalaia</a>, his family moved to <a href="/wiki/Keratsini" title="Keratsini">Keratsini</a> when he was young.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Kapsis played for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panathinaikos_FC" title="Panathinaikos FC">Panathinaikos FC</a> from 1969 until 1984 and was a member of that team when it played in <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a>, located in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/1971_European_Cup_Final" title="1971 European Cup Final">1971 European Cup Final</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ev_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ev-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kapsis was capped thirty-six times by the <a href="/wiki/Greece_national_football_team" title="Greece national football team">Greece national football team</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and was a member of the team that competed in the finals of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euro_1980" title="Euro 1980">Euro 1980</a>. He was also selected to an all-European squad that faced a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_American" title="South American">South American</a> one in a charity game.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthimos_Kapsis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He is the father of <a href="/wiki/Michalis_Kapsis" title="Michalis Kapsis">Michalis Kapsis</a> (born 1973), who is also a football player and was included in the squad of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euro_2004" title="Euro 2004">Euro 2004</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> that went on to win the tournament.</p>
