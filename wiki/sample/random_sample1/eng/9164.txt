<p>In <a href="/wiki/Greek_mythology" title="Greek mythology">Greek mythology</a>, <b>Antilochus</b> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/æ/ short 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span></span>/</a></span></span> (Greek: Ἀντίλοχος, <i>Antílokhos</i>) was the son of <a href="/wiki/Nestor_(mythology)" title="Nestor (mythology)">Nestor</a>, king of <a href="/wiki/Pylos" title="Pylos">Pylos</a>, and was one of the Acheans in the Trojan War.</p>
<p>One of the suitors of <a href="/wiki/Helen_of_Troy" title="Helen of Troy">Helen of Troy</a>, he accompanied his father and his brother <a href="/wiki/Thrasymedes_(mythology)" title="Thrasymedes (mythology)">Thrasymedes</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Trojan_War" title="Trojan War">Trojan War</a>. He was distinguished for his beauty, swiftness of foot, and skill as a charioteer. Though the youngest among the Greek princes, he commanded the Pylians in the war and performed many deeds of valour. He was a favorite of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gods_of_Olympus" title="Gods of Olympus">the gods</a> and a friend of <a href="/wiki/Achilles" title="Achilles">Achilles</a>, to whom he was commissioned to announce the death of <a href="/wiki/Patroclus" title="Patroclus">Patroclus</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChisholm1911_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChisholm1911-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>When his father Nestor was attacked by <a href="/wiki/Memnon_(mythology)" title="Memnon (mythology)">Memnon</a>, Antilochus sacrificed himself to save him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> thus fulfilling an oracle which had warned to "beware of an Ethiopian." Antilochus' death was avenged by Achilles. According to other accounts, he was slain by Hector<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> or by <a href="/wiki/Paris_(mythology)" title="Paris (mythology)">Paris</a> in the temple of the Thymbraean <a href="/wiki/Apollo" title="Apollo">Apollo</a> together with Achilles<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> His ashes, along with those of Achilles and Patroclus, were enshrined in a mound on the promontory of <a href="/wiki/Sigeion" title="Sigeion">Sigeion</a>, where the inhabitants of <a href="/wiki/Troy" title="Troy">Ilium</a> offered sacrifice to the dead heroes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Odyssey" title="Odyssey">Odyssey</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> the three friends are represented as united in the underworld and walking together in the <a href="/wiki/Asphodel_Meadows" title="Asphodel Meadows">Asphodel Meadows</a>. According to <a href="/wiki/Pausanias_(geographer)" title="Pausanias (geographer)">Pausanias</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> they dwell together on the island of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leuke" title="Leuke">Leuke</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChisholm1911_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChisholm1911-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Among the Trojans he killed were <a href="/wiki/Melanippus" title="Melanippus">Melanippus</a>, Ablerus, Atymnius, Phalces, and Thoon, although Hyginus records that he only killed two Trojans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> At the funeral games of Patroclus, Antilochus finished second in the chariot race and third in the foot race.</p>
<p>Antilochus left behind in <a href="/wiki/Messenia" title="Messenia">Messenia</a> a son <a href="/wiki/Paeon_(son_of_Antilochus)" title="Paeon (son of Antilochus)">Paeon</a>, whose descendants were among the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neleidae" title="Neleidae">Neleidae</a> expelled from Messenia, by the descendants of <a href="/wiki/Heracles" title="Heracles">Heracles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
