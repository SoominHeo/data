<p>The <b>Algonquian</b> are one of the most populous and widespread <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North American</a> <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">native</a> language groups. Today, thousands of individuals identify with various Algonquian peoples. Historically, the peoples were prominent along the Atlantic Coast and into the interior along the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lawrence_River" title="St. Lawrence River">St. Lawrence River</a> and around the <a href="/wiki/Great_Lakes" title="Great Lakes">Great Lakes</a>. This grouping consists of the peoples who speak <a href="/wiki/Algonquian_languages" title="Algonquian languages">Algonquian languages</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-colonial_period">Pre-colonial period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algonquian_peoples&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pre-colonial period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Algonquins.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="991" data-file-width="818" height="267" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Algonquins.jpg/220px-Algonquins.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Algonquins.jpg/330px-Algonquins.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Algonquins.jpg/440px-Algonquins.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:The_village_of_Pomeioc,_North_Carolina,_1885,_color_-_NARA_-_535753.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1600" data-file-width="1576" height="223" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/The_village_of_Pomeioc%2C_North_Carolina%2C_1885%2C_color_-_NARA_-_535753.jpg/220px-The_village_of_Pomeioc%2C_North_Carolina%2C_1885%2C_color_-_NARA_-_535753.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/The_village_of_Pomeioc%2C_North_Carolina%2C_1885%2C_color_-_NARA_-_535753.jpg/330px-The_village_of_Pomeioc%2C_North_Carolina%2C_1885%2C_color_-_NARA_-_535753.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/The_village_of_Pomeioc%2C_North_Carolina%2C_1885%2C_color_-_NARA_-_535753.jpg/440px-The_village_of_Pomeioc%2C_North_Carolina%2C_1885%2C_color_-_NARA_-_535753.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Before Europeans came into contact, most Algonquian settlements lived by hunting and fishing, although quite a few supplemented their diet by cultivating <a href="/wiki/Maize" title="Maize">corn</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beans" title="Beans">beans</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Squash_(fruit)" title="Squash (fruit)">squash</a> (the "<a href="/wiki/Three_Sisters_(agriculture)" title="Three Sisters (agriculture)">Three Sisters"</a>). The <a href="/wiki/Ojibwe" title="Ojibwe">Ojibwe</a> cultivated <a href="/wiki/Wild_rice" title="Wild rice">wild rice</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<p>The Algonquians of <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a> (who spoke the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Algonquian_languages" title="Eastern Algonquian languages">Eastern Algonquian</a>) practiced a seasonal economy. The basic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_unit" title="Social unit">social unit</a> was the village: a few hundred people related by a <a href="/wiki/Clan" title="Clan">clan</a> <a href="/wiki/Kinship" title="Kinship">kinship</a> structure. Villages were temporary and mobile. The people moved to locations of greatest natural food supply, often breaking into smaller units or recombining as the circumstances required. This custom resulted in a certain degree of cross-tribal mobility, especially in troubled times.</p>
<p>In warm weather, they constructed light <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wigwams" title="Wigwams">wigwams</a> for portability. Wigwams are a type of hut which usually had buckskin doors. In the winter, they erected the more substantial <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_long_house" title="Native American long house">long houses</a>, in which more than one <a href="/wiki/Clan" title="Clan">clan</a> could reside. They cached food supplies in more permanent, semi-subterranean structures.</p>
<p>In the spring, when the fish were spawning, they left the winter camps to build villages at coastal locations and waterfalls. In March, they caught <a href="/wiki/Smelt_(fish)" title="Smelt (fish)">smelt</a> in nets and weirs, moving about in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Birchbark" title="Birchbark">birchbark</a> <a href="/wiki/Canoe" title="Canoe">canoes</a>. In April, they netted <a href="/wiki/Alewife" title="Alewife">alewife</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sturgeon" title="Sturgeon">sturgeon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salmon" title="Salmon">salmon</a>. In May, they caught <a href="/wiki/Cod" title="Cod">cod</a> with hook and line in the <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">ocean</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Trout" title="Trout">trout</a>, <a href="/wiki/Smelt_(fish)" title="Smelt (fish)">smelt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Striped_bass" title="Striped bass">striped bass</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flounder" title="Flounder">flounder</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Estuaries" title="Estuaries">estuaries</a> and streams. Putting out to sea, the men hunted <a href="/wiki/Whale" title="Whale">whales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Porpoise" title="Porpoise">porpoises</a>, <a href="/wiki/Walrus" title="Walrus">walruses</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pinniped" title="Pinniped">seals</a>. The women and children gathered <a href="/wiki/Scallop" title="Scallop">scallops</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mussel" title="Mussel">mussels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clam" title="Clam">clams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Crab" title="Crab">crabs</a>, all the basis of menus in New England today.</p>
<p>From April through October, natives hunted <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> birds and their eggs: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canada_geese" title="Canada geese">Canada geese</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brent_Goose" title="Brent Goose">brant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mourning_dove" title="Mourning dove">mourning doves</a> and others. In July and August they gathered <a href="/wiki/Strawberry" title="Strawberry">strawberries</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raspberry" title="Raspberry">raspberries</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blueberry" title="Blueberry">blueberries</a> and nuts. In September, they split into small groups and moved up the streams to the forest. There, the men hunted <a href="/wiki/Beaver" title="Beaver">beaver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caribou" title="Caribou">caribou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moose" title="Moose">moose</a> and <a href="/wiki/White-tailed_deer" title="White-tailed deer">white-tailed deer</a>.</p>
<p>In December, when the snows began, the people created larger winter camps in sheltered locations, where they built or reconstructed long houses. February and March were lean times. The tribes in southern New England and other northern latitudes had to rely on cached food. Northerners developed a practice of going hungry for several days at a time. Historians hypothesize that this practice kept the population down, according to <a href="/wiki/Liebig%27s_law_of_the_minimum" title="Liebig's law of the minimum">Liebig's law</a>. The northerners were food gatherers only.</p>
<p>The southern Algonquians of New England relied predominantly on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slash_and_burn" title="Slash and burn">slash-and-burn agriculture</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> They cleared fields by burning for one or two years of cultivation, after which the village moved to another location. This is the reason the <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> found the region relatively cleared and ready for planting. By using various kinds of native corn (maize), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beans" title="Beans">beans</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Squash_(fruit)" title="Squash (fruit)">squash</a>, southern New England natives were able to improve their diet to such a degree that their population increased and they reached a density of 287 people per 100mi<sup>2</sup> as opposed to 41 in the north.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Even with mobile crop rotation, southern villages were necessarily less mobile than northern ones. The natives continued their seasonal occupation but tended to move into fixed villages near their lands. They adjusted to the change partially by developing a gender-oriented <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Division_of_labor" title="Division of labor">division of labor</a>. The women cultivated crops, and the men fished and hunted.</p>
<p>Scholars<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (January 2015)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> estimate that, by the year 1600, the indigenous population of New England had reached 70,000–100,000.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Colonial_period">Colonial period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algonquian_peoples&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Colonial period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the time of the first European settlements in North America, Algonquian tribes occupied <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a>, and much of <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> east of the <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountains" title="Rocky Mountains">Rocky Mountains</a>; what is now <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>, southeastern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a> and down the Atlantic Coast through the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Upper_South" title="Upper South">Upper South</a>; and around the Great Lakes in present-day <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iowa" title="Iowa">Iowa</a>. They were most concentrated in the New England region. The homeland of the Algonquian peoples is not known. At the time of the European arrival, the hegemonic <a href="/wiki/Iroquois" title="Iroquois">Iroquois</a> federation, based in present-day New York and Pennsylvania, was regularly at war with Algonquian neighbours.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tribal_identities">Tribal identities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algonquian_peoples&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tribal identities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algonquian_peoples&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The French and later English encountered the <a href="/wiki/Maliseet" title="Maliseet">Maliseet</a> of present-day <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a>; the Abenaki of Quebec, Vermont and New Hampshire; the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mi%27kmaq_people" title="Mi'kmaq people">Mi'kmaq</a> <a href="/wiki/Band_government" title="Band government">band governments</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maritime_provinces" title="Maritime provinces">Maritime provinces</a> lived primarily on fishing. Further north are the <a href="/wiki/Innu" title="Innu">Betsiamites</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atikamekw" title="Atikamekw">Atikamekw</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anishinaabe" title="Anishinaabe">Anishinaabe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Innu" title="Innu">Montagnais</a>/<a href="/wiki/Naskapi" title="Naskapi">Naskapi</a> (<a href="/wiki/Innu" title="Innu">Innu</a>). The <a href="/wiki/Beothuk" title="Beothuk">Beothuk</a> people of Newfoundland are also believed to have been Algonquian, but their last known speaker died in the early 19th century. Few records of their language or culture remain.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_England_area">New England area</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algonquian_peoples&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: New England area">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Colonists in the Massachusetts Bay area first encountered the <a href="/wiki/Wampanoag_people" title="Wampanoag people">Wampanoag</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusett" title="Massachusett">Massachusett</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nipmuck" title="Nipmuck">Nipmuck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennacook" title="Pennacook">Pennacook</a>, <a href="/wiki/Penobscot" title="Penobscot">Penobscot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Passamaquoddy" title="Passamaquoddy">Passamaquoddy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Quinnipiac" title="Quinnipiac">Quinnipiac</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mohegan" title="Mohegan">Mohegan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pequot" title="Pequot">Pequot</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pocumtuc" title="Pocumtuc">Pocumtuc</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tunxis" title="Tunxis">Tunxis</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narragansett_(tribe)" title="Narragansett (tribe)">Narragansett</a> were based in southern New England. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abenaki_people" title="Abenaki people">Abenaki</a> tribe was located in Maine, New Hampshire, Vermont and eastern Quebec. <a href="/wiki/Mahican" title="Mahican">Mohican</a> tribe was located in western New England and in the upper Hudson River Valley (around Albany, New York). These tribes practiced agriculture, hunting and fishing.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mid-_and_south-Atlantic_areas">Mid- and south-Atlantic areas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algonquian_peoples&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mid- and south-Atlantic areas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Lenape" title="Lenape">Lenape</a>, also called <i>Delaware</i>, were (<a href="/wiki/Munsee_language" title="Munsee language">Munsee</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Unami_language" title="Unami language">Unami</a> speakers that were in what is now known as the eastern Pennsylvania, Delaware, New Jersey, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lower_Hudson_Valley" title="Lower Hudson Valley">lower Hudson Valley</a> and western Long Island areas in New York. They encountered the European explorer <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_da_Verrazzano" title="Giovanni da Verrazzano">Giovanni da Verrazzano</a> in what is now <a href="/wiki/New_York_Harbor" title="New York Harbor">New York Harbor</a> in 1524. Branches of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pequot" title="Pequot">Pequot</a> occupied eastern Long Island.</p>
<p>Further south were the traditional homes of the <a href="/wiki/Powhatan" title="Powhatan">Powhatan</a>, a loose group of tribes numbering into the tens of thousands, who were among the first to encounter English colonists in the Chesapeake Bay. Historic tribes also included the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nanticoke_tribe" title="Nanticoke tribe">Nanticoke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wicocomico" title="Wicocomico">Wicocomico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Secotan" title="Secotan">Secotan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chowanoke" title="Chowanoke">Chowanoke</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Weapemeoc&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Weapemeoc (page does not exist)">Weapemeoc</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Chickahominy_people" title="Chickahominy people">Chickahominy peoples</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Midwest">Midwest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algonquian_peoples&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Midwest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The French encountered Algonquian peoples in this area through their trade and limited colonization of New France along the Mississippi and Ohio rivers. The historic peoples were the <a href="/wiki/Shawnee" title="Shawnee">Shawnee</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Illiniwek" title="Illiniwek">Illiniwek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kickapoo_people" title="Kickapoo people">Kickapoo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Menominee" title="Menominee">Menominee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miami_people" title="Miami people">Miami</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sauk_people" title="Sauk people">Sauk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Meskwaki" title="Meskwaki">Fox</a>, also known as the Sac and Fox Tribe and later known as the Meskwaki Indians, who lived throughout the present-day Midwest of the United States.</p>
<p>During the nineteenth century, many were displaced over great distances through the United States enforcement of <a href="/wiki/Indian_removal" title="Indian removal">Indian removal</a> west of the <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_River" title="Mississippi River">Mississippi River</a>, to what is now <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Upper_west">Upper west</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algonquian_peoples&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Upper west">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ojibwe" title="Ojibwe">Ojibwe</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chippewa" title="Chippewa">Chippewa</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ottawa_(tribe)" title="Ottawa (tribe)">Ottawa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Potawatomi" title="Potawatomi">Potawatomi</a>, and a variety of <a href="/wiki/Cree" title="Cree">Cree</a> groups lived in <a href="/wiki/Upper_Peninsula_of_Michigan" title="Upper Peninsula of Michigan">Upper Michigan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Ontario" title="Western Ontario">Western Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Prairies" title="Canadian Prairies">Canadian Prairies</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Arapaho" title="Arapaho">Arapaho</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blackfoot" title="Blackfoot">Blackfoot</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cheyenne" title="Cheyenne">Cheyenne</a> developed as indigenous to the <a href="/wiki/Great_Plains" title="Great Plains">Great Plains</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Western_areas">Western areas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algonquian_peoples&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Western areas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Algonquian people in the present states of Wyoming, Colorado, southwestern Nebraska and northwestern Kansas were ancestors to the <a href="/wiki/Cheyenne" title="Cheyenne">Cheyenne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arapaho" title="Arapaho">Arapaho</a> tribes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In north central Montana the <a href="/wiki/Gros_Ventre" title="Gros Ventre">Gros Ventre</a> are located.</p>
