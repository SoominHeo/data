<p><b>Algirdas Mykolas Brazauskas</b> (<span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Lithuanian" title="Help:IPA for Lithuanian">[ˈɐ̂ˑlʲɡʲɪrd̪ɐs̪ ˈmʲîːkoːɫɐs̪ brɐˈz̪ɐ̂ˑʊs̪kɐs̪]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:Algirdas_Brazauskas.ogg" title="File:Algirdas Brazauskas.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/9d/Algirdas_Brazauskas.ogg" title="Algirdas Brazauskas.ogg">listen</a>)</small>, <span class="nowrap">22 September</span> 1932 – <span class="nowrap">26 June</span> 2010) was the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/President_of_Lithuania" title="President of Lithuania">President</a> of a newly independent <a href="/wiki/Post-Soviet_states" title="Post-Soviet states">post-Soviet</a> <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a> from 1993 to 1998 and <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Lithuania" title="Prime Minister of Lithuania">Prime Minister</a> from 2001 to 2006.</p>
<p>He also served as head of the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Lithuania" title="Communist Party of Lithuania">Communist Party of Lithuania</a> that broke with Moscow.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algirdas_Brazauskas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brazauskas was born in Rokiškis, Lithuania. He finished <a href="/wiki/Kai%C5%A1iadorys" title="Kaišiadorys">Kaišiadorys</a> High School in 1952 and graduated from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kaunas_Polytechnic_Institute" title="Kaunas Polytechnic Institute">Kaunas Polytechnic Institute</a> in 1956 with a degree in <a href="/wiki/Civil_engineering" title="Civil engineering">civil engineering</a>. He later served as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conscript" title="Conscript">Conscript</a> sailor in the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Navy" title="Soviet Navy">Soviet Navy</a>, serving as a <a href="/wiki/Fire_controlman" title="Fire controlman">Fire controlman</a> onboard the <a href="/wiki/Riga-class_frigate" title="Riga-class frigate">Riga-class frigate</a> <i>Rosomacha</i> until 1960 <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1967 Brazauskas started working in the Governmental Planning Committee, as a Committee's head's assistant. In 1974, Brazauskas received PhD in Economics.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algirdas_Brazauskas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He divorced his first wife, Julia, with whom he had two daughters; he married Kristina Butrimienė in 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_views">Political views</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algirdas_Brazauskas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Political views">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He rose to politics in the 1980s, as the Soviet Union was undergoing radical change. In turn he transformed himself from a Communist Party apparatchik to a moderate reformer. He was seen as cautious by nature, and when confronted by the tide of nationalist feeling in the Soviet empire Brazauskas initially believed that the old USSR might be reconstituted as a looser federation of independent, but communist, states. In seeing the tide of an independent democracy, he joined the reformist cause observing in 1990 that "We are realists now, and we cannot be propagating any utopian ideas. It's no secret [that] the Communist Party has a dirty history."</p>
<p>Though he sought to avoid a breach with Moscow in 1989, as leader of Lithuania's Communist Party, he formally severed the party's links with Moscow. This was rare in that no other former Soviet republics dared to take this step. Some believe that this act confirmed the inevitability of the demise of the Soviet Union.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algirdas_Brazauskas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He took various positions in the government of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lithuanian_SSR" title="Lithuanian SSR">Lithuanian SSR</a> and <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Lithuania" title="Communist Party of Lithuania">Communist Party of Lithuania</a> since 1965:</p>
<ul>
<li>1965–1967, the minister of construction materials industry of Lithuanian SSR</li>
<li>1967–1977, deputy chairman of State Planning Committee of Lithuanian SSR.</li>
<li>1977–1987, secretary of Central Committee of <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Lithuania" title="Communist Party of Lithuania">Communist Party of Lithuania</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>In 1988, he became the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_Secretary" title="General Secretary">first secretary</a> of the Communist Party of Lithuania. Under his leadership, the Communist Party of Lithuania supported the Lithuanian <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> movement, broke away from the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Communist Party of the Soviet Union">Communist Party of the Soviet Union</a> and transformed itself into social-democratic <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Labour_Party_of_Lithuania" title="Democratic Labour Party of Lithuania">Democratic Labour Party of Lithuania</a> (now merged into the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_of_Lithuania" title="Social Democratic Party of Lithuania">Lithuanian Social Democratic Party</a>). Brazauskas was Chairman of the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Lithuanian_rulers" title="List of Lithuanian rulers">(head of state)</a> from 15 January until 11 March 1990.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>After the 1992 parliamentary elections, he became speaker of the parliament and acting President of Lithuania on 25 November 1992. He then won the presidential election with 60% of the vote, was confirmed as President on 25 February 1993, and served until 25 February 1998. He decided not to seek reelection, and retire, in 1998 and was succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Valdas_Adamkus" title="Valdas Adamkus">Valdas Adamkus</a>, who won the 1998 election.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algirdas_Brazauskas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brazauskas said he planned to retire from politics and wanted to be "an ordinary pensioner." During the initial two years in retirement he wrote a book, though it was incomplete. He said he would continue writing it after his second stint in government. He also said he would finish "household work" and that he likes physical work. He added that "I have no estates, but the property I own needs to be put in good order." He wanted to live "in a way that other people live."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baltictimes_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-baltictimes-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_politics">Return to politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algirdas_Brazauskas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Return to politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He subsequently returned to politics saying he "always had something to do in life."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baltictimes_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-baltictimes-3">[3]</a></sup> This time he was Prime Minister from 3 July 2001, appointed by the parliament, until 1 June 2006, when his government resigned as President <a href="/wiki/Valdas_Adamkus" title="Valdas Adamkus">Valdas Adamkus</a> expressed no confidence in two of the Ministers, formerly Labour Party colleagues of Brazauskas, over ethical principles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LR_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-LR-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>His government resigned on <span class="nowrap">31 May</span> 2006 after the large <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(Lithuania)" title="Labour Party (Lithuania)">Labour Party</a> left the governing coalition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Brazauskas decided not to remain in office as acting Prime Minister, and announced that he was finally retiring from politics. He said "I tried to be a pensioner for several years, and I think I was successful. I hope for success this time, as well."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baltictimes_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-baltictimes-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>He led the ruling Social Democratic Party of Lithuania for one more year, until <span class="nowrap">19 May</span> 2007, when he passed the reins to <a href="/wiki/Gediminas_Kirkilas" title="Gediminas Kirkilas">Gediminas Kirkilas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-2">[2]</a></sup> He served as the honorary chairman of the party, and remained an influential voice in party politics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LR_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-LR-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algirdas_Brazauskas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Algirdas Brazauskas was honored with the various decorations, among others the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Vytautas_the_Great" title="Order of Vytautas the Great">Order of Vytautas the Great with the Golden Chain</a>, Grand Cross Order of Vytautas the Great.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Days before his death <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_President" title="Russian President">Russian President</a> <a href="/wiki/Dmitry_Medvedev" title="Dmitry Medvedev">Dmitry Medvedev</a> awarded Brazauskas with the <i>Order of Honour</i> for his significant contribution to cooperation between Russia and Lithuania and good neighbourly relations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Brazauskas was an Honorary Member of <a class="external text" href="http://www.raoulwallenberg.net" rel="nofollow">The International Raoul Wallenberg Foundation</a> also.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Illness_and_death">Illness and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algirdas_Brazauskas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Illness and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brazauskas was diagnosed with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lymphatic_cancer" title="Lymphatic cancer">lymphatic cancer</a> in December 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He died on 26 June 2010 from cancer, aged 77.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tehrantimes_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-tehrantimes-10">[10]</a></sup> At the time of his death, he was still considered an influential figure in Lithuanian politics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following his death the <a href="/wiki/Obituary" title="Obituary">obituaries</a> wrote of him that he had a "frame to match his indefatigable stature and a calm but commanding presence that could fill any stage."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> His successor as president, <a href="/wiki/Valdas_Adamkus" title="Valdas Adamkus">Valdas Adamkus</a>, said that he "dared to decide which side to choose in a critical moment."</p>
<p>Lithuanian President <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dalia_Grybauskaite" title="Dalia Grybauskaite">Dalia Grybauskaite</a> said "The memory of the first directly elected president of Lithuania after it restored its independence, of a strong and charismatic personality, will remain for a long time in the hearts of the Lithuanian people."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tehrantimes_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-tehrantimes-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
