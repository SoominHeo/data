<p>The <b>Aldan River</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Алдан</span>) is the second-longest <a href="/wiki/Tributary" title="Tributary">tributary</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Lena_River" title="Lena River">Lena River</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Sakha_Republic" title="Sakha Republic">Sakha Republic</a> in eastern <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>. The river is 2,273 kilometres (1,412 mi) long, of which around 1,600 kilometres (990 mi) is navigable. It was part of the <a href="/wiki/Siberian_River_Routes" title="Siberian River Routes">River Route</a> to <a href="/wiki/Okhotsk" title="Okhotsk">Okhotsk</a>. In 1639 <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Moskvitin" title="Ivan Moskvitin">Ivan Moskvitin</a> ascended the Aldan and <a href="/wiki/Maya_River" title="Maya River">Maya Rivers</a> and crossed to the <a href="/wiki/Ulya_River" title="Ulya River">Ulya River</a> to reach the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Okhotsk" title="Sea of Okhotsk">Sea of Okhotsk</a>.</p>
<p>It rises in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stanovoy_Mountains" title="Stanovoy Mountains">Stanovoy Mountains</a> southwest of <a href="/wiki/Neryungri" title="Neryungri">Neryungri</a>, then northeast past <a href="/wiki/Aldan,_Russia" title="Aldan, Russia">Aldan</a> and through <a href="/wiki/Tommot" title="Tommot">Tommot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ust-Maya" title="Ust-Maya">Ust-Maya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eldikan" title="Eldikan">Eldikan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Khandyga" title="Khandyga">Khandyga</a> before turning northwest and joining the Lena near <a href="/wiki/Batamay" title="Batamay">Batamay</a>.</p>
<p>The river's main tributaries are the <a href="/wiki/Amga_River" title="Amga River">Amga River</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uchur_River" title="Uchur River">Uchur River</a> and Maya River. Its <a href="/wiki/Drainage_basin" title="Drainage basin">basin</a> is known for gold and for <a href="/wiki/Cambrian" title="Cambrian">Cambrian</a> <a href="/wiki/Fossil" title="Fossil">fossils</a>.</p>
