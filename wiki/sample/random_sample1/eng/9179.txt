<p><b>Ala ad-Din Tekish</b> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: علاء الدين تكش; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Full_name" title="Full name">full name</a>: <i>Ala ad-Dunya wa ad-Din Abul Muzaffar Tekish ibn Il-Arslan</i>) or Tekesh or Takesh was the <a href="/wiki/Shah" title="Shah">Shah</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khwarezmian_Empire" title="Khwarezmian Empire">Khwarezmian Empire</a> from 1172 to 1200. He was the son of <a href="/wiki/Il-Arslan" title="Il-Arslan">Il-Arslan</a>. His rule was contested by his brother, <a href="/wiki/Sultan_Shah_of_Khwarezm" title="Sultan Shah of Khwarezm">Sultan Shah</a>, who held a principality in Khorasan. Tekish inherited Sultan Shah's state after he died in 1193. In <a href="/wiki/Turkic_languages" title="Turkic languages">Turkic</a> the name Tekish means <i>he who strikes in battle</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1194 Tekish defeated the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Seljuq_Empire" title="Great Seljuq Empire">Seljuq</a> sultan of <a href="/wiki/Hamadan" title="Hamadan">Hamadan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toghrul_III" title="Toghrul III">Toghrul III</a>, in an alliance with Caliph <a href="/wiki/Al-Nasir" title="Al-Nasir">Al-Nasir</a>, and conquered his territories. After the war, he broke with the Caliphate and was on the brink of a war with it until the Caliph accepted him as Sultan of Iraq, Khorasan, and Turkestan in 1198.</p>
<p>He died of a <a href="/wiki/Peritonsillar_abscess" title="Peritonsillar abscess">peritonsillar abscess</a> in 1200<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and was succeeded by his son, <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_II_of_Khwarezm" title="Muhammad II of Khwarezm">Ala ad-Din Muhammad</a>.</p>
