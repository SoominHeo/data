<p><i><b>Aladdin: Original Motion Picture Soundtrack</b></i> is the soundtrack for the 1992 <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Animation_Studios" title="Walt Disney Animation Studios">Disney animated feature film</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aladdin_(1992_Disney_film)" title="Aladdin (1992 Disney film)"><i>Aladdin</i></a><i>.</i> The album was released by <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Records" title="Walt Disney Records">Walt Disney Records</a> on cassette and <a href="/wiki/Compact_disc" title="Compact disc">compact disc</a> in October 1992. In 1993, the soundtrack was intertwined with demos and work tapes and unreleased masters, as well as original scores in a four-disc <a href="/wiki/Box_set" title="Box set">box set</a> entitled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Music_Behind_the_Magic" title="The Music Behind the Magic">The Music Behind the Magic: The Musical Artistry of Alan Menken, Howard Ashman &amp; Tim Rice</a></i>. A <a href="/wiki/Audio_mastering" title="Audio mastering">remastered</a> reissue with altered lyrics and new artwork was released in 2001. A special edition reissue featuring two previously released demos and new artwork was released in 2004.</p>
<p>The music on the album earned composer <a href="/wiki/Alan_Menken" title="Alan Menken">Alan Menken</a> the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Original_Score" title="Academy Award for Best Original Score">Academy Award for Best Original Score</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Original_Score" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Original Score">Golden Globe Award for Best Original Score</a>, as well as a nomination for the <a href="/wiki/BAFTA_Award_for_Best_Film_Music" title="BAFTA Award for Best Film Music">BAFTA Award for Best Film Music</a>. Menken also shared the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Original_Song" title="Academy Award for Best Original Song">Academy Award for Best Original Song</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Original_Song" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song">Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song</a> and the first and only Disney song to win a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Song_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Song of the Year">Grammy Award for Song of the Year</a> with lyricist <a href="/wiki/Tim_Rice" title="Tim Rice">Tim Rice</a> for the song "<a href="/wiki/A_Whole_New_World" title="A Whole New World">A Whole New World</a>". The album is one of the best-selling soundtrack albums to an animated film, with 2,481,000 copies sold in the U.S as of April 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sales_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-sales-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Songs">Songs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aladdin_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Songs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arabian_Nights">Arabian Nights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aladdin_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Arabian Nights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"Arabian Nights", performed by The Peddler (<a href="/wiki/Bruce_Adler" title="Bruce Adler">Bruce Adler</a>), opens the film. Initially a longer composition, Howard Ashman and Alan Menken's 1990 score treatment incorporated several reprises allowing The Peddler to comment on plot developments. While the Peddler is voiced by <a href="/wiki/Robin_Williams" title="Robin Williams">Robin Williams</a>, the actor could not hit some of the high notes in "Arabian Nights", leading Disney to hire Adler, who had done crowd singing in <i><a href="/wiki/Beauty_and_the_Beast_(1991_film)" title="Beauty and the Beast (1991 film)">Beauty and the Beast</a></i> and proved himself able to imitate Williams' voice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The original lyric <i>"Where they cut off your ear/if they don't like your face"</i> received complaints from the <a href="/wiki/American-Arab_Anti-Discrimination_Committee" title="American-Arab Anti-Discrimination Committee">American-Arab Anti-Discrimination Committee</a> (ADC) and other groups upon the film and soundtrack's initial release. A new lyric, <i>"Where it's flat and immense/and the heat is intense"</i>, was recorded for subsequent soundtrack pressings and home video releases.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It also appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Aladdin_(2011_musical)" title="Aladdin (2011 musical)">2011 Broadway production</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The subsequent line, "<i>It's barbaric, but hey, it's home,</i>" was left intact.</p>
<p>An alternate version of the song utilizing lyrics from the original demo was later featured in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Return_of_Jafar" title="The Return of Jafar">The Return of Jafar</a></i>, performed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Brian_Hannan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Brian Hannan (page does not exist)">Brian Hannan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> This version was also used as the main titles theme for the <a href="/wiki/Aladdin_(TV_series)" title="Aladdin (TV series)"><i>Aladdin</i> television series</a>.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Aladdin_and_the_King_of_Thieves" title="Aladdin and the King of Thieves">Aladdin and the King of Thieves</a></i> features a reprise performed by Adler originally recorded for the first film.</p>
<p>"Arabian Nights" pops up in the film's underscore quite often, primarily used as a theme for Jafar.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="One_Jump_Ahead">One Jump Ahead</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aladdin_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: One Jump Ahead">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/One_Jump_Ahead_(song)" title="One Jump Ahead (song)">One Jump Ahead</a>" is sung by <a href="/wiki/Aladdin_(Disney_character)" title="Aladdin (Disney character)">Aladdin</a> (<a href="/wiki/Brad_Kane" title="Brad Kane">Brad Kane</a>) while he escapes from the guards carrying a stolen loaf of bread, explaining that it is the life he lives in, he has no choice but to steal, much as he does not wish to. It replaced "You Can Count on Me", a song that would be used to introduce Aladdin but was considered too pokey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ew-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Tim_Rice" title="Tim Rice">Tim Rice</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alan_Menken" title="Alan Menken">Alan Menken</a>'s biggest inspiration for "One Jump Ahead" was another cut song, "Babkak, Omar, Aladdin, Kassim", that would feature Aladdin and three friends removed from the film. During the writing, Rice and Menken also came to a <a href="/wiki/Ballad" title="Ballad">ballad</a> in the same vein, used in a later scene as "One Jump Ahead (Reprise)".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The reprise, titled, "One Jump Ahead (Reprise)", is also used in the score as the theme for Aladdin.</p>
<p>On 2012 album <i>Disney - Koe no Oujisama</i>, which features various Japanese <a href="/wiki/Voice_acting_in_Japan" title="Voice acting in Japan">voice actors</a> covering Disney songs, this song was covered by <a href="/wiki/Tomokazu_Sugita" title="Tomokazu Sugita">Tomokazu Sugita</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Friend_Like_Me">Friend Like Me</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aladdin_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Friend Like Me">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Friend_Like_Me" title="Friend Like Me">Friend Like Me</a>" is sung by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genie_(Aladdin)" title="Genie (Aladdin)">Genie</a> (<a href="/wiki/Robin_Williams" title="Robin Williams">Robin Williams</a>) while he demonstrates his powers to Aladdin in a comedic <a href="/wiki/Big_band" title="Big band">big band</a> number, emphasizing that he is a friend unlike any other. It was the first scene in <i>Aladdin</i> to have its animation finished, and features some differences in character design.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> This song was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Original_Song" title="Academy Award for Best Original Song">Academy Award for Best Original Song</a>; it was Ashman's last nomination.</p>
<p>During the film's end credits, an instrumental reprise of the song is featured.</p>
<p>In 1995, <a href="/wiki/Alvin_and_the_Chipmunks" title="Alvin and the Chipmunks">Alvin and the Chipmunks</a> covered the song for their Disney-themed album <i><a href="/wiki/When_You_Wish_Upon_a_Chipmunk" title="When You Wish Upon a Chipmunk">When You Wish Upon a Chipmunk</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Prince_Ali">Prince Ali</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aladdin_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Prince Ali">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Prince_Ali_(song)" title="Prince Ali (song)">Prince Ali</a>" is another flamboyant number sung by the Genie (<a href="/wiki/Robin_Williams" title="Robin Williams">Robin Williams</a>) as he introduces Agrabah to Prince Ali Ababwa, Aladdin's royal alter ego, with a giant caravan. During the song, <a href="/wiki/Robin_Williams" title="Robin Williams">Robin Williams</a> imitates a Thanksgiving Parade commentor ("Don't they look lovely, <a href="/wiki/June_Lockhart" title="June Lockhart">June</a>?"), <a href="/wiki/Walter_Brennan" title="Walter Brennan">Walter Brennan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ethel_Merman" title="Ethel Merman">Ethel Merman</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. The film version cut a conceived intro for the song and two extra verses in the middle.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> This song is playable in the video game <a href="/wiki/Just_Dance_2014" title="Just Dance 2014">Just Dance 2014</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-comm2_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-comm2-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="A_Whole_New_World">A Whole New World</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aladdin_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: A Whole New World">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/A_Whole_New_World" title="A Whole New World">A Whole New World</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Prince_Ali_.28Reprise.29">Prince Ali (Reprise)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aladdin_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Prince Ali (Reprise)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A parody of "<a href="/wiki/Prince_Ali_(song)" title="Prince Ali (song)">Prince Ali</a>" sung by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jafar_(Aladdin)" title="Jafar (Aladdin)">Jafar</a> (<a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Freeman_(actor)" title="Jonathan Freeman (actor)">Jonathan Freeman</a>), while the villain uses his newly gained powers to reveal Ali as only a street rat, and send him to the ends of the earth. The filmmakers liked Jonathan Freeman's singing, and wanted to give Jafar a song of his own, but they rejected Menken and Ashman's original composition for the scene ("Humiliate the Boy") and a new piece composed by <a href="/wiki/Tim_Rice" title="Tim Rice">Tim Rice</a> ("Why Me"), as they were too long. Instead, they used a reprise of "<a href="/wiki/Prince_Ali_(song)" title="Prince Ali (song)">Prince Ali</a>." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-ew-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-comm2_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-comm2-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cut_songs">Cut songs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aladdin_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Cut songs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Howard_Ashman" title="Howard Ashman">Howard Ashman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alan_Menken" title="Alan Menken">Alan Menken</a> composed several songs for an initial story treatment of Aladdin prior to beginning work on <i><a href="/wiki/Beauty_and_the_Beast_(1991_film)" title="Beauty and the Beast (1991 film)">Beauty and the Beast</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hochman_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-hochman-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> This story treatment incorporated several plot elements from the original folk tale and additional characters that were eliminated during later story development. Three songs from this score - "Arabian Nights," "<a href="/wiki/Friend_Like_Me" title="Friend Like Me">Friend Like Me</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Prince_Ali_(song)" title="Prince Ali (song)">Prince Ali</a>" - survive in the final film.</p>
<p>Menken composed several additional songs for the subsequent story revisions following Ashman's 1991 death, prior to Tim Rice's involvement with the project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hochman_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-hochman-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Score">Score</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aladdin_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Score">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alan Menken's score for <i><a href="/wiki/Aladdin_(1992_Disney_film)" title="Aladdin (1992 Disney film)">Aladdin</a></i> follows the compositional method also used in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Little_Mermaid_(1989_film)" title="The Little Mermaid (1989 film)">The Little Mermaid</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Beauty_and_the_Beast_(1991_film)" title="Beauty and the Beast (1991 film)">Beauty and the Beast</a></i>, with specific cues and motifs derived from the songs' melodies.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Aladdin'</b>s theme echoes the melody of "<a href="/wiki/One_Jump_Ahead_(song)" title="One Jump Ahead (song)">One Jump Ahead</a>." It prominently appears in the score cues "Street Urchins," "To Be Free" and "Aladdin's Word."</li>
<li><b>Jasmine'</b>s theme is introduced in her first scene at the palace. <a href="/wiki/Alan_Menken" title="Alan Menken">Alan Menken</a> later expanded this motif into the song "To Be Free" which is sung by Jasmine in the stage version <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aladdin:_A_Musical_Spectacular" title="Aladdin: A Musical Spectacular">Aladdin: A Musical Spectacular</a> as well as the musical.</li>
<li>The <b>Sultan</b> is usually accompanied by royal fanfare.</li>
<li><b>Jafar'</b>s theme is darker, and resembles an instrumental version of "Arabian Nights." It also shares an instrumental version of the deleted song "My Finest Hour", whether he's alone or accompanied by Iago.</li>
<li><b>Genie</b> shares the same musical motif as Jasmine, symbolizing his desire to also be free. This cue was expanded into the song "To Be Free" for <a href="/wiki/Disney%27s_Aladdin:_A_Musical_Spectacular" title="Disney's Aladdin: A Musical Spectacular">Disney's Aladdin: A Musical Spectacular</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>When Aladdin and Jasmine are together, they are usually accompanied by an instrumental cue of "<a href="/wiki/A_Whole_New_World" title="A Whole New World">A Whole New World</a>".</p>
