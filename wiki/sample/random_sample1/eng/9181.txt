<p><i><b>Alxasaurus</b></i> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˌ/ secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="/ɑː/ 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ʃ/ 'sh' in 'shy'">ʃ</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/ɔː/ 'au' in 'fraud'">ɔː</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span></span>/</a></span></span>; "<a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia" title="Inner Mongolia">Alxa Desert</a> lizard") is a <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> of <a href="/wiki/Therizinosaur" title="Therizinosaur">therizinosauroid</a> alxasaurid <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theropod" title="Theropod">theropod</a> <a href="/wiki/Dinosaur" title="Dinosaur">dinosaur</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Early_Cretaceous" title="Early Cretaceous">Early Cretaceous</a> <a href="/wiki/Period_(geology)" title="Period (geology)">Period</a> of <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia" title="Inner Mongolia">Inner Mongolia</a>. It is one of the earliest known members of the <a href="/wiki/Taxonomic_rank" title="Taxonomic rank">superfamily</a> Therizinosauroidea, but it already possessed the body shape - including the long neck, short tail, and long hand claws - of later therizinosauroids. Like other members of this group, it was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biped" title="Biped">bipedal</a> <a href="/wiki/Herbivore" title="Herbivore">herbivore</a> with a large gut to process plant material. Several specimens are known and the largest was a little over 12 feet (3.8 m) long.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Benton.2C_Michael_J.2C_Prehistoric_Life_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Benton.2C_Michael_J.2C_Prehistoric_Life-1">[1]</a></sup> According to Gregory S. Paul, it was 4 meters long and its weight was about 400 kg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naming">Naming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alxasaurus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Naming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Alxasaurus_YWRA_400.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1456" data-file-width="1960" height="163" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Alxasaurus_YWRA_400.JPG/220px-Alxasaurus_YWRA_400.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Alxasaurus_YWRA_400.JPG/330px-Alxasaurus_YWRA_400.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Alxasaurus_YWRA_400.JPG/440px-Alxasaurus_YWRA_400.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Alxasaurus_YWRA_400.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Restoration</div>

<p>This dinosaur was first described and named by <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paleontologist" title="Paleontologist">paleontologist</a> <a href="/wiki/Dale_Russell" title="Dale Russell">Dale Russell</a> and his Chinese colleague, <a href="/wiki/Dong_Zhiming" title="Dong Zhiming">Dong Zhiming</a>, in a paper published in 1993.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> However, although the paper is technically included in the last issue of the 1993 volume of the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Journal_of_Earth_Sciences" title="Canadian Journal of Earth Sciences">Canadian Journal of Earth Sciences</a>, this issue was actually released in the early weeks of 1994.</p>
<p><i>Alxasaurus</i> is named after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alxa_Desert" title="Alxa Desert">Alxa Desert</a> of Inner Mongolia, also known as the "Alashan" <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a>, and the name also includes the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Greek</a> word <i>sauros</i> ("lizard"). <a href="/wiki/Alxa_League" title="Alxa League">Alxa</a> (or Alashan) is also the name of the <a href="/wiki/Leagues_of_China" title="Leagues of China">league</a>, or <a href="/wiki/List_of_administrative_divisions_of_Inner_Mongolia" title="List of administrative divisions of Inner Mongolia">administrative division</a>, of the Inner Mongolia (Nei Mongol Zizhiqu) region of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>. The single known <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> (<i>A. elesitaiensis</i>) is named after Elesitai, a village found in this region, near which the <a href="/wiki/Fossil" title="Fossil">fossil</a> remains of <i>Alxasaurus</i> were located.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Specimens">Specimens</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alxasaurus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Specimens">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Alxasaurus_SIZE.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1293" data-file-width="1869" height="152" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Alxasaurus_SIZE.jpg/220px-Alxasaurus_SIZE.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Alxasaurus_SIZE.jpg/330px-Alxasaurus_SIZE.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Alxasaurus_SIZE.jpg/440px-Alxasaurus_SIZE.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Alxasaurus_SIZE.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Size compared to a <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a></div>
</div>

<p>Five <i>Alxasaurus</i> skeletons were recovered from the <a href="/wiki/Bayin-Gobi_Formation" title="Bayin-Gobi Formation">Bayin-Gobi Formation</a> of Inner Mongolia, which dates to the <a href="/wiki/Albian" title="Albian">Albian</a> stage of the Early Cretaceous Period, or about 112 to 100 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Million_years_ago" title="Million years ago">million years ago</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Holotype" title="Holotype">holotype</a>, specimen IVPP 88402a, which is considered to exemplify the genus and species, is the largest and most complete of the five, consisting of the <a href="/wiki/Mandible" title="Mandible">mandible</a> (lower jaw) and some teeth, as well as many limb bones, ribs, and <a href="/wiki/Vertebra" title="Vertebra">vertebrae</a>, including all five <a href="/wiki/Sacrum" title="Sacrum">sacral</a> (hip) vertebrae and the first nineteen tail vertebrae. The other four specimens are the <a href="/wiki/Paratype" title="Paratype">paratypes</a>: IVPP 88301, IVPP 88402b, IVPP 88501 and IVPP 88510. Together the skeletons represent most of the bones in the body aside from the <a href="/wiki/Skull" title="Skull">skull</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Benton.2C_Michael_J.2C_Prehistoric_Life_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Benton.2C_Michael_J.2C_Prehistoric_Life-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">Classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alxasaurus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Classification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Alxasaurus.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1413" data-file-width="2511" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Alxasaurus.jpg/220px-Alxasaurus.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Alxasaurus.jpg/330px-Alxasaurus.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Alxasaurus.jpg/440px-Alxasaurus.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>While exhibiting many typical therizinosaur features in overall body shape and in the teeth, the skeleton of <i>Alxasaurus</i> also shows several features present in more typical theropods, and the discovery of this animal provided significant evidence that therizinosaurs were aberrant theropods. Specifically, the semilunate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carpal" title="Carpal">carpal</a> bone of the wrist is found only in <a href="/wiki/Maniraptora" title="Maniraptora">maniraptoran</a> theropods, which also include <a href="/wiki/Oviraptorosauria" title="Oviraptorosauria">oviraptorosaurs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dromaeosauridae" title="Dromaeosauridae">dromaeosaurs</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Troodontid" title="Troodontid">troodontids</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>. Even more <a href="/wiki/Basal_(phylogenetics)" title="Basal (phylogenetics)">basal</a> therizinosaurs such as <i><a href="/wiki/Falcarius" title="Falcarius">Falcarius</a></i> and the <a href="/wiki/Feather" title="Feather">feathered</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Beipiaosaurus" title="Beipiaosaurus">Beipiaosaurus</a></i> have since been discovered with more theropod features and have helped to solidify this arrangement. <i>Alxasaurus</i> is now thought to occupy a position between the early <i><a href="/wiki/Beipiaosaurus" title="Beipiaosaurus">Beipiaosaurus</a></i> and later therizinosaurs such as <i><a href="/wiki/Erlikosaurus" title="Erlikosaurus">Erlikosaurus</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Segnosaurus" title="Segnosaurus">Segnosaurus</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Therizinosaurus" title="Therizinosaurus">Therizinosaurus</a></i> itself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
