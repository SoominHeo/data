<p><b>Alashkert Football Club</b> (<a href="/wiki/Armenian_language" title="Armenian language">Armenian</a>: <span lang="hy" xml:lang="hy">Ալաշկերտ Ֆուտբոլային Ակումբ</span>), commonly known as <b>Alashkert</b>, is an <a href="/wiki/Football_in_Armenia" title="Football in Armenia">Armenian football</a> club from <a href="/wiki/Yerevan" title="Yerevan">Yerevan</a>, founded in 1990 in <a href="/wiki/Martuni,_Armenia" title="Martuni, Armenia">Martuni</a>. They currently play at the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Premier_League" title="Armenian Premier League">Armenian Premier League</a>. The home stadium of the team is the <a href="/wiki/Alashkert_Stadium" title="Alashkert Stadium">Alashkert Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Yerevan" title="Yerevan">Yerevan</a>. The club headquarters are located on Saryan street 25, Yerevan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The club is owned by the «Bagratour» Limited Liability Company, headed by the Armenian businessman Bagrat Navoyan.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alashkert_FC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alashkert Football Club was founded in 1990 in the town of Martuni of <a href="/wiki/Gegharkunik_Province" title="Gegharkunik Province">Gegharkunik Province</a>. In 1992, the team played in the <a href="/wiki/1992_Armenian_Premier_League" title="1992 Armenian Premier League">Premier League</a> representing Martuni and using the City Stadium of the town as their home venue. However, at the end of the season the team finished last in the table, being relegated to the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_First_League" title="Armenian First League">First League</a>. As a result, the club withdrew from the competition.</p>
<p>In 1998, Alashkert FC returned to professional football and participated in the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_First_League" title="Armenian First League">First League</a> finishing the table in the 6th position. In 1999, they did not participate in the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_First_League" title="Armenian First League">First League</a> competition and later in early 2000, the club was dissolved.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Revival_in_2011">Revival in 2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alashkert_FC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Revival in 2011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In late 2011, Alashkert FC was re-founded by Bagrat Navoyan; a businessman native of <a href="/wiki/Martuni,_Armenia" title="Martuni, Armenia">Martuni</a>. They entered the 2012–13 Armenian First League competition and won the championship, booking their position in the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Premier_League" title="Armenian Premier League">Armenian Premier League</a> for the 2013–14 season. In 2012–2013, the team was managed by the former Armenian player <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albert_Sarkisyan_(born_1963)" title="Albert Sarkisyan (born 1963)">Albert Sarkisyan</a> and later by former footballer <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Erzrumyan" title="Sergey Erzrumyan">Sergey Erzrumyan</a>. The assistant manager was <a href="/wiki/Aram_Hakobyan" title="Aram Hakobyan">Aram Hakobyan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Relocation_to_Yerevan_in_2013">Relocation to Yerevan in 2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alashkert_FC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Relocation to Yerevan in 2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2013, the club purchased the <a href="/wiki/Alashkert_Stadium" title="Alashkert Stadium">Nairi Stadium</a> in Yerevan, to become the official venue of their home games. As a result, the club was officially relocated from Martuni to Yerevan starting from the 2013–2014 season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Debut_in_the_European_Tournaments">Debut in the European Tournaments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alashkert_FC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Debut in the European Tournaments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Alashkert first qualified for the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2015–16 UEFA Europa League">Europa League 2015–16 qualification</a> round 1, after gaining 4th place in the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Armenian_Premier_League" title="2014–15 Armenian Premier League">Armenian Premier League 2014–2015</a>. In the first round Alashkert got the Scottish club <a href="/wiki/St_Johnstone_F.C." title="St Johnstone F.C.">St Johnstone</a>. Despite the fact that the <a href="/wiki/St_Johnstone_F.C." title="St Johnstone F.C.">St Johnstone</a> were considered as the favorites, Alashkert was victorious after the two games and were able to create a sensation. In the second round, Alashkert had to meet the Kazakh side <a href="/wiki/FC_Kairat" title="FC Kairat">Kairat</a>. In the first game Alashkert were defeated 3–0. In the second game, Alashkert were able to achieve victory as a result of a last-minute winner scored by Heber Araujo, 2–1. However this was not enough in order to qualify for the third round.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stadiums">Stadiums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alashkert_FC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Stadiums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Between 1990 and 2000, the club was based in the Martuni City Stadium. After the revival in 2011, the team played their home games in several stadiums in Yerevan, mainly at the Nairi Stadium. However, Nairi Stadium – later renamed <a href="/wiki/Alashkert_Stadium" title="Alashkert Stadium">Alashkert Stadium</a> – became owned by Alashkert FC since February 2013.</p>
<p>Due to the large-scale renovation works at the Alashkert Stadium, Alashkert used the <a href="/wiki/Vazgen_Sargsyan_Republican_Stadium" title="Vazgen Sargsyan Republican Stadium">Vazgen Sargsyan Republican Stadium</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hrazdan_Stadium" title="Hrazdan Stadium">Hrazdan Stadium</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Armenian_Premier_League" title="2013–14 Armenian Premier League">2013–14 Armenian Premier League</a> season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The club returned to their own Alashkert Stadium during the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Armenian_Premier_League" title="2014–15 Armenian Premier League">2014–15 Armenian Premier League</a> season.</p>
