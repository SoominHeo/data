<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Alan Wilson (motorsport)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="hatnote" role="note">For other people named Alan Wilson, see <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Alan_Wilson_(disambiguation)" title="Alan Wilson (disambiguation)">Alan Wilson (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p><b>Alan L. Wilson</b> (born 9 May 1946 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Durban,_South_Africa" title="Durban, South Africa">Durban</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-resume_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-resume-1">[1]</a></sup>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_African" title="South African">South African</a> designer of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motorsports" title="Motorsports">motorsports</a> <a href="/wiki/Road_racing" title="Road racing">road course</a> <a href="/wiki/Race_track" title="Race track">race tracks</a>.</p>
<p>Considered one of the most prolific track designers,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Wilson has designed and built more than 20 race courses, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miller_Motorsports_Park" title="Miller Motorsports Park">Miller Motorsports Park</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://www.calabogiemotorsports.com" rel="nofollow">Calabogie Motorsports Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barber_Motorsports_Park" title="Barber Motorsports Park">Barber Motorsports Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Motorsports_Park_Hastings" title="Motorsports Park Hastings">Motorsports Park Hastings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gingerman_Raceway" title="Gingerman Raceway">Gingerman Raceway</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Beaver_Run_Motorsports_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Beaver Run Motorsports Park (page does not exist)">Beaver Run Motorsports Park</a>, as well as overseeing the 2000-2001 redesign of <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Mont-Tremblant" title="Circuit Mont-Tremblant">Circuit Mont-Tremblant</a>.</p>
<p>Wilson has also played major roles in a number of motorsports venues, including serving as the Director of the <a href="/wiki/Sports_Car_Club_of_America" title="Sports Car Club of America">Sports Car Club of America</a>'s Pro Racing series between 2000 and 2004 and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Auto_race_workers" title="Auto race workers">Chief Steward</a> for <a href="/wiki/Champ_Car" title="Champ Car">CART</a> in 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-resume_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-resume-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1982, his work as chairman of the <a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands Hatch</a> racing club (1978-1982) was honored by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Formula_One_Constructors_Association" title="Formula One Constructors Association">Formula One Constructors' Association</a> for orchestrating the best organized <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/1982_British_Grand_Prix" title="1982 British Grand Prix">Grand Prix</a> that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-resume_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-resume-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1975, Wilson married <a href="/wiki/Desir%C3%A9_Wilson" title="Desiré Wilson">Desiré Wilson</a>, one of only five women to compete in the Formula One World Championship series and, thus far, the only woman to win a race in an F1 series. The couple does not have any children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-resume_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-resume-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
