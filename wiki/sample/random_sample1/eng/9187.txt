<p><b>Alan Douglas Borges de Carvalho</b> (born 10 July 1989), simply known as <b>Alan</b>, is a Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">forward</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guangzhou_Evergrande_Taobao" title="Guangzhou Evergrande Taobao">Guangzhou Evergrande Taobao</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Super_League" title="Chinese Super League">Chinese Super League</a>.</p>
<p>After failing to make any impact in Brazil with <a href="/wiki/Londrina_Esporte_Clube" title="Londrina Esporte Clube">Londrina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fluminense_FC" title="Fluminense FC">Fluminense</a>, Alan joined Austrian club <a href="/wiki/FC_Red_Bull_Salzburg" title="FC Red Bull Salzburg">Red Bull Salzburg</a> in 2010. There he established himself in the first team and became an intrinsic part of the squad. His playing time was limited in the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Austrian_Football_Championship" title="2011–12 Austrian Football Championship">2011–12 season</a> owing to an injury. Nevertheless, Alan scored regularly for the club, including many hat-tricks. In 2015, after playing nearly 130 matches for the Austrian side and scoring nearly 100 goals, he signed with <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Super_League" title="Chinese Super League">Chinese Super League</a> club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guangzhou_Evergrande_F.C." title="Guangzhou Evergrande F.C.">Guangzhou Evergrande</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Carvalho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brazil">Brazil</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Carvalho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Brazil">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Barbosa,_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="Barbosa, São Paulo">Barbosa, São Paulo</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SF_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-SF-3">[3]</a></sup> Alan signed for <a href="/wiki/Londrina_Esporte_Clube" title="Londrina Esporte Clube">Londrina</a>, in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SF_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-SF-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2008, he was given a professional contract by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fluminense_Football_Club" title="Fluminense Football Club">Fluminense</a> with whom he played for two seasons till 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-SW-5">[5]</a></sup> He scored 11 league goals for the club<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-SW-5">[5]</a></sup> and also won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010_Campeonato_Brasileiro_Serie_A" title="2010 Campeonato Brasileiro Serie A">2010 Brasileiro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Red_Bull_Salzburg">Red Bull Salzburg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Carvalho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Red Bull Salzburg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 11 August 2010, Alan signed for Austrian club <a href="/wiki/FC_Red_Bull_Salzburg" title="FC Red Bull Salzburg">Red Bull Salzburg</a> for an undisclosed fee, as a replacement for <a href="/wiki/Marc_Janko" title="Marc Janko">Marc Janko</a> who headed to <a href="/wiki/FC_Twente" title="FC Twente">FC Twente</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He made his debut for the club in <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> qualifier against <a href="/wiki/Hapoel_Tel_Aviv_F.C." title="Hapoel Tel Aviv F.C.">Hapoel Tel Aviv</a> which his team lost 2–3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-SW-5">[5]</a></sup> In that match he came as a 67th-minute substitute for <a href="/wiki/Franz_Schiemer" title="Franz Schiemer">Franz Schiemer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In his first season with the club, Alan scored a hat-trick against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sturm_Graz" title="Sturm Graz">Sturm Graz</a> on 16 April 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Alan also scored braces against <a href="/wiki/FK_Austria_Wien" title="FK Austria Wien">Austria Wien</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/SC_Wiener_Neustadt" title="SC Wiener Neustadt">SC Wiener Neustadt</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> as he ended the season with 10 league goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-SW-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>Alan started the 2011–12 season by scoring a hat-trick against Latvian side <a href="/wiki/FK_Liep%C4%81jas_Metalurgs" title="FK Liepājas Metalurgs">FK Liepājas Metalurgs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> His goal scoring streak continued as he scored a brace against <a href="/wiki/FK_Senica" title="FK Senica">FK Senica</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> as well as scoring in a defeat against <a href="/wiki/AC_Omonia" title="AC Omonia">AC Omonia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> However his playing time in his second season with the Austrian club was limited as he ruptured his <a href="/wiki/Cruciate_ligament" title="Cruciate ligament">cruciate ligament</a> in a league match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_Vienna" title="Rapid Vienna">Rapid Vienna</a> on 28 August 2011 and was ruled out of action for at least six months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Alan got back on training on 20 November 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He finished the 2012–13 season with 11 goals in 14 league matches and did not play in any continental match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-SW-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2013–14 season proved to be the most successful season for the Brazilian striker, becoming a goalmachine. On 8 February 2014, Alan scored a hat-trick against relegation threatened <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SV_Grodig" title="SV Grodig">SV Grodig</a>. In that match, the same feat would also be achieved by his Spanish partner <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Soriano" title="Jonathan Soriano">Jonathan Soriano</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He also received two red cards - one against <a href="/wiki/SV_Ried" title="SV Ried">SV Ried</a> and another against <a href="/wiki/FC_Basel" title="FC Basel">FC Basel</a> in an <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> encounter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-SW-5">[5]</a></sup> Alan ended the season winning the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Football_Bundesliga" title="Austrian Football Bundesliga">Austrian Football Bundesliga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Cup" title="Austrian Cup">Austrian Cup</a> - completing a domestic double.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> He was the second topscorer only behind teammate Soriano.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>The following season too, started well for Alan, as he scored regularly for his side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-SW-5">[5]</a></sup> On 23 October 2014, Alan scored a hat-trick as Salzburg won 4–2 against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dinamo_Zagreb" title="Dinamo Zagreb">Dinamo Zagreb</a> in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> group stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> He added another two on 27 November in a 3–1 win at <a href="/wiki/Celtic_Park" title="Celtic Park">Celtic Park</a> which ensured that Salzburg became the first ever Austrian club to win in Scotland, striking in the 8th and 13th minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In his penultimate league match for his club, he scored a hat-trick, scoring in 52nd, 58th and 60th minute against <a href="/wiki/FK_Austria_Wien" title="FK Austria Wien">FK Austria Wien</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Despite leaving halfway through the season, with eight goals he was the joint top scorer in the season's Europa League, alongside <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Romelu_Lukaku" title="Romelu Lukaku">Romelu Lukaku</a>. This was the second consecutive time that a Red Bull player had achieved this honour, after his strike partner Soriano.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guangzhou_Evergrande">Guangzhou Evergrande</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Carvalho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Guangzhou Evergrande">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 16 January 2015, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Super_League" title="Chinese Super League">Chinese Super League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou_Evergrande_Taobao_F.C." title="Guangzhou Evergrande Taobao F.C.">Guangzhou Evergrande Taobao</a> officially announced they had signed Alan from Red Bull Salzburg on a four-year contract for a transfer fee of €11.1 million. He was issued the number 7 jersey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GE_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-GE-24">[24]</a></sup> On 14 February 2015, he made his official debut in the <a href="/wiki/2015_Chinese_FA_Super_Cup" title="2015 Chinese FA Super Cup">2015 Chinese FA Super Cup</a> against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shandong_Luneng" title="Shandong Luneng">Shandong Luneng</a>, coming on as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Gao_Lin" title="Gao Lin">Gao Lin</a>. On 3 March 2015, Alan suffered a cruciate ligament rupture while training before the <a href="/wiki/2015_AFC_Champions_League" title="2015 AFC Champions League">2015 AFC Champions League</a> match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Sydney_Wanderers" title="Western Sydney Wanderers">Western Sydney Wanderers</a>, ruling him out for at least five months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> After <a href="/wiki/Paulinho_(footballer)" title="Paulinho (footballer)">Paulinho</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robinho" title="Robinho">Robinho</a> joined the club in July, Alan, who was till recovering from injury, was degraded to the reserve team squad for the second half of the season due to foreign player limit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> He was named in the 23-man squad for the <a href="/wiki/2015_FIFA_Club_World_Cup" title="2015 FIFA Club World Cup">2015 FIFA Club World Cup</a> but didn't play in the tournament, which manager <a href="/wiki/Luiz_Felipe_Scolari" title="Luiz Felipe Scolari">Luiz Felipe Scolari</a> explained that he just recovered from injury and was not ready to play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> He made his return on 6 March 2016, 370 days after his injury, in a Super League match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chongqing_Lifan_F.C." title="Chongqing Lifan F.C.">Chongqing Lifan</a>, coming on as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Huang_Bowen" title="Huang Bowen">Huang Bowen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Alan scored his first goal in his fifth appearance for Guangzhou on 24 April 2016, in a 4–0 away victory against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tianjin_Teda_F.C." title="Tianjin Teda F.C.">Tianjin Teda</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Carvalho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alan played three matches for the <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_under-20_football_team" title="Brazil national under-20 football team">Brazil U20</a> in an unofficial tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brazil_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Brazil-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2013, Alan called himself as half-Austrian and expressed his desire to play for the <a href="/wiki/Austria_national_football_team" title="Austria national football team">Austrian national team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> He said "Maybe I'll play sometime for Austria." But according to FIFA rules he had to play at least five years in Austria to be able to represent the national team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brazil_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-Brazil-30">[30]</a></sup> However his move to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guangzhou_Evergrande_F.C." title="Guangzhou Evergrande F.C.">Guangzhou Evergrande</a> dented the hope of playing for Austria as he was only six months shy of five years while joining the Chinese outfit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Carvalho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A statement by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guangzhou_Evergrande_F.C." title="Guangzhou Evergrande F.C.">Guangzhou Evergrande</a> described Alan as a "blade", who is physically very strong and make wise use of opportunities. He has also been said as "strong, energetic, skilled" and a player with good control of the ball. Alan is equally adept in his both left and right foot. He is also known for his first-time lay-offs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GE_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-GE-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
