<p><b>Alessandra Mussolini</b> (born 30 December 1962) is an Italian politician, the granddaughter of <a href="/wiki/Benito_Mussolini" title="Benito Mussolini">Benito Mussolini</a>, and a former actress and model. She is since 2013 a member of the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_the_Republic_(Italy)" title="Senate of the Republic (Italy)">Italian Senate</a>, elected for <a href="/wiki/The_People_of_Freedom" title="The People of Freedom">The People of Freedom</a> which later became part of <a href="/wiki/Forza_Italia_(2013)" title="Forza Italia (2013)">Forza Italia</a>, and since 2014 a <a href="/wiki/Member_of_the_European_Parliament" title="Member of the European Parliament">Member of the European Parliament</a> for Forza Italia.</p>
<p>She was the founder and leader of the <a href="/wiki/National_conservatism" title="National conservatism">national conservative</a> political party <a href="/wiki/Social_Action" title="Social Action">Social Action</a>; from 2004 until 2008, Mussolini also served as a Member of the European Parliament, and she has been a member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_Italy" title="Chamber of Deputies of Italy">Chamber of Deputies</a>, the lower house of the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Parliament" title="Italian Parliament">Italian Parliament</a>, for <a href="/wiki/The_People_of_Freedom" title="The People of Freedom">The People of Freedom</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chamberSite_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-chamberSite-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some of Mussolini's noted stances are in regard to social views on <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_rights" title="Women's rights">women's</a> and <a href="/wiki/Children%27s_rights" title="Children's rights">children's rights</a> and the role within both the family unit and society in general.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Independent_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Independent-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alessandra_Mussolini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alessandra Mussolini was born in Rome, the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Romano_Mussolini" title="Romano Mussolini">Romano Mussolini</a>, the fourth son of <a href="/wiki/Benito_Mussolini" title="Benito Mussolini">Benito Mussolini</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fascism" title="Fascism">Fascist</a> dictator of Italy from 1922 to 1943, and <a href="/wiki/Anna_Maria_Villani_Scicolone" title="Anna Maria Villani Scicolone">Anna Maria Villani Scicolone</a> (born 11 May 1938, Rome). The actress <a href="/wiki/Sophia_Loren" title="Sophia Loren">Sophia Loren</a> is her maternal aunt. Her father Romano dedicated his most famous song to her in 1973, composing it in her honor (the song <i>Blues for Alexandra</i> off his album Mirage).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><br/>
From 1976 to 1980 she went to high school at the <a href="/wiki/American_Overseas_School_of_Rome" title="American Overseas School of Rome">American Overseas School of Rome</a>. She graduated in 1986 from <a href="/wiki/Sapienza_University_of_Rome" title="Sapienza University of Rome">Sapienza University of Rome</a>, where she got her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Master_of_science" title="Master of science">master of science</a> in medicine and surgery.</p>
<p>She married customs policeman Mauro Floriani on 28 October 1989. Going against tradition, she proposed to him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimesChronicle_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimesChronicle-4">[4]</a></sup> Together they have three children,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Caterina, Clarissa, and Romano – the last named after his grandfather. Later, the children adopted their mother's surname, but she went through a complex legal process to allow them to do so. She has since campaigned for Italian law to be changed to allow all children to take their mother's last name if they wish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her husband was scheduled to appear at court for a <a href="/wiki/Child_prostitution" title="Child prostitution">child prostitution</a> trial in 2015. In 2013, around 50 men — among them professionals, priests, and politicians — were accused of paying two teenage girls, aged 14 and 15, for sexual services in Rome.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Entertainment_career">Entertainment career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alessandra_Mussolini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Entertainment career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mussolini was taken under the wing of her aunt <a href="/wiki/Sophia_Loren" title="Sophia Loren">Sophia Loren</a> for a while and started a career as an <a href="/wiki/Acting" title="Acting">actress</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian language</a> <a href="/wiki/Film_industry" title="Film industry">film industry</a> during the 1970s. <i><a href="/wiki/A_Special_Day" title="A Special Day">A Special Day</a></i> (1977), in which she had a minor role as "Maria Luisa", won an American <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Foreign_Language_Film" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Foreign Language Film">Golden Globe Award for Best Foreign Language Film</a> and was nominated for an <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Foreign_Language_Film" title="Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film">Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film</a>.</p>
<p>During 1982, Mussolini released a <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop music</a> album of romantic songs under the title <i>Amore</i> on <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Records" title="Alfa Records">Alfa Records</a>; the album was released only in Japan and has since become something of a collectors item.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Orrore_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Orrore-8">[8]</a></sup> Mussolini also appeared as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glamour_model" title="Glamour model">glamour model</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC20040109_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC20040109-9">[9]</a></sup> including on the cover of two European editions of <i><a href="/wiki/Playboy" title="Playboy">Playboy</a></i>, in Italy (August 1983) and Germany (November 1983).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PBCoversIT_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-PBCoversIT-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PBCoversDE_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-PBCoversDE-11">[11]</a></sup> "When you are an actress, you are dealing with the body. Every actress does <a href="/wiki/Toplessness" title="Toplessness">topless</a> and stuff like this; you have to", she has said.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Independent_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Independent-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mussolini continued as an actress into the 1980s. Some of the films she featured in were made for Italian television. However, she still acted in standard cinematic films, such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Assisi_Underground_(film)" title="The Assisi Underground (film)">The Assisi Underground</a></i> in which she played a <a href="/wiki/Nun" title="Nun">nun</a>; the movie focused on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic Church</a> rescuing <a href="/wiki/Italian_Jews" title="Italian Jews">Italian Jews</a> from the Nazis in 1943.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDBAssisi_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-IMDBAssisi-12">[12]</a></sup> She starred in her final film in 1990 and then left the film industry to continue studying after a producer asked her to change her name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Independent_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Independent-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alessandra_Mussolini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1992, she was elected to parliament in a <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Constituency" title="Constituency">constituency</a> as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Social_Movement" title="Italian Social Movement">Movimento Sociale Italiano</a> (MSI).</p>

<p>She later was a candidate for the post of mayor of Naples, but was defeated by <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Bassolino" title="Antonio Bassolino">Antonio Bassolino</a>. Her relations with <a href="/wiki/Gianfranco_Fini" title="Gianfranco Fini">Gianfranco Fini</a>, leader of the Alleanza Nazionale, never were very good, she announced; she then later withdrew, resigning over differences with him at least once.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes1996_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes1996-13">[13]</a></sup> She unsuccessfully challenged him for leadership of the party when he withdrew support for Benito Mussolini in a television interview in January 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph2002_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph2002-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mussolini suddenly left National Alliance on 28 November 2003, following the visit of party leader and the Deputy Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Gianfranco_Fini" title="Gianfranco Fini">Gianfranco Fini</a> to <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, where he described fascism as part "the absolute evil" with regard to <a href="/wiki/The_Holocaust" title="The Holocaust">the Holocaust</a> as he apologised for Italy's role as an <a href="/wiki/Axis_powers" title="Axis powers">Axis Power</a> during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Mussolini, however, defended the right of Israel to exist and declared that the world "should beg forgiveness of Israel".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following her resignation, Mussolini formed her <a href="/wiki/Social_Action" title="Social Action">Social Action</a> party, originally named "Freedom of Action", and organised a coalition named <a href="/wiki/Social_Alternative" title="Social Alternative">Social Alternative</a>. The move was read in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Italian_media&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Italian media (page does not exist)">Italian media</a> as surprising because of Mussolini's "progressive" stances on many issues, including abortion,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Artificial_insemination" title="Artificial insemination">artificial insemination</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gay_rights" title="Gay rights">gay rights</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Civil_union" title="Civil union">civil unions</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> She has been an outspoken feminist<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and has been described by conservative commentators as a "socialist"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and a "left-winger".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament_election,_2004_(Italy)" title="European Parliament election, 2004 (Italy)">2004 European Parliament election</a> her electoral list, <a href="/wiki/Social_Alternative" title="Social Alternative">Social Alternative</a>, gained 1.2% of the vote. Mussolini herself received 133,000 preference votes.</p>
<p>In response to a comment made by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UKIP" title="UKIP">UKIP</a> <a href="/wiki/Member_of_the_European_Parliament" title="Member of the European Parliament">MEP</a> <a href="/wiki/Godfrey_Bloom" title="Godfrey Bloom">Godfrey Bloom</a> in which he said that "No self-respecting small businessman with a brain in the right place would ever employ a lady of child-bearing age. That isn't politically correct, is it, but it's a fact of life. The more women's rights you have, it's actually a bar to their employment."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> and: "I just don't think [women] clean behind the fridge enough,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Mussolini responded by saying</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>I know the English have a sense of humour about themselves, but I am from Naples and I can say that we women do know how to cook and clean the refrigerator and even be politicians, while perhaps Godfrey Bloom does not know either how to clean the refrigerator or how to be a politician.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>In March 2005, Mussolini was banned by a local court from regional elections held the following month for presenting fraudulent signatures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> "This is an affront to democracy, if they're going to exclude the Social Alternative they will have to exclude all the parties, because all the signature lists are false", Mussolini told Reuters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Mussolini went on a hunger strike to protest the decision.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> However, at the end of the month Italy's top administrative court, the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Council_of_State" title="Italian Council of State">Council of State</a>, annulled the decision and she stood for election.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006 she responded to claims by the <a href="/wiki/Transgender" title="Transgender">transgender</a> Italian M.P. candidate <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Luxuria" title="Vladimir Luxuria">Vladimir Luxuria</a> that she was a 'fascist' with the line "Meglio fascista che frocio" ("Better fascist than faggot").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2007, remarks by Mussolini triggered the collapse of the far-right <a href="/wiki/Identity,_Tradition,_Sovereignty" title="Identity, Tradition, Sovereignty">Identity, Tradition, Sovereignty</a> grouping within the European Parliament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Mussolini declared that all <a href="/wiki/Romanians" title="Romanians">Romanians</a> were criminals in remarks regarding immigration policy. This prompted delegates from the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Romania_Party" title="Greater Romania Party">Greater Romania Party</a> to quit the group, bringing the group below the minimum number of members to qualify as a caucus and receive Parliamentary funding.</p>
<p>After the Italian general elections of April 2008, Mussolini served as a member of the Italian parliament within <a href="/wiki/Silvio_Berlusconi" title="Silvio Berlusconi">Silvio Berlusconi</a>'s alliance of right wing parties, <a href="/wiki/The_People_of_Freedom" title="The People of Freedom">The People of Freedom</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chamberSite_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-chamberSite-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mussolini condemned the <a href="/wiki/Vatican_City" title="Vatican City">Vatican's</a> comparison of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homosexuality_and_pedophilia" title="Homosexuality and pedophilia">homosexuality with pedophilia</a>, stating "You can't link sexual orientation to pedophilia … this link risks becoming dangerously misleading for the protection of children."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Italian_general_election,_2013" title="Italian general election, 2013">Italian general election</a> in February 2013, she was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_the_Republic_(Italy)" title="Senate of the Republic (Italy)">Senate of the Republic (Italy)</a> for The People of Freedom. The party was transformed into a new <a href="/wiki/Forza_Italia_(2013)" title="Forza Italia (2013)">Forza Italia</a> in November 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament_election,_2014_(Italy)" title="European Parliament election, 2014 (Italy)">2014 European Parliament election</a>, Mussolini was elected to the EP for <a href="/wiki/Forza_Italia_(2013)" title="Forza Italia (2013)">Forza Italia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
