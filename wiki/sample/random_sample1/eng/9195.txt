<p><b>Alex Rodrigo Dias da Costa</b>, better known as <b>Alex</b> (<small>pronounced: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">[aˈlɛks]</a></span>; born 17 June 1982), is a retired<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who last played for <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">central defender</a>. Alex is known for his physical strength and the power of his shot, which has gained him the nicknames of "The Tank"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tank_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tank-3">[3]</a></sup> and <i>Canhão da Vila</i> ("Cannon of Vila Belmiro").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Having been bought by the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> in 2004, he was loaned to Dutch team <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV</a> and he won 3 <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> titles with them. Alex finally joined the Chelsea squad in the summer of 2007 after three years in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In his time at Chelsea, he won the <a href="/wiki/2009_FA_Cup_Final" title="2009 FA Cup Final">FA Cup in 2009</a> and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009-10_Chelsea_F.C._season" title="2009-10 Chelsea F.C. season">league and FA Cup "double" in 2010</a>, as well as reaching the <a href="/wiki/2008_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2008 UEFA Champions League Final">2008 UEFA Champions League Final</a>. He joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain" title="Paris Saint-Germain">Paris Saint-Germain</a> for £4.2 million in January 2012 and won <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> twice with the club, moving to Milan on the expiration of his contract in 2014.</p>
<p>Alex earned 17 caps for <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a> between 2003 and 2008, winning the <a href="/wiki/2007_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2007 Copa América">2007 Copa América</a> and finishing as runner-up at the <a href="/wiki/2003_CONCACAF_Gold_Cup" title="2003 CONCACAF Gold Cup">2003 CONCACAF Gold Cup</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Santos">Santos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Santos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Alex began his career with <a href="/wiki/Santos_FC" title="Santos FC">Santos</a> of Brazil, making his first team debut in 2002 before later becoming part of the club's central pillars. Alex made his professional league debut in the 1–1 draw against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fluminense_Football_Club" title="Fluminense Football Club">Fluminense</a>. Less than a month after his debut, Alex scored his first goal for the club in a match against <a href="/wiki/CR_Vasco_da_Gama" title="CR Vasco da Gama">Vasco da Gama</a> on 18 September 2002. After multiple impressive performances, Alex secured his place in the team's regular starting lineup. In his first season playing, in 2002, Santos won the <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Brasileiro_S%C3%A9rie_A" title="Campeonato Brasileiro Série A">Série A</a> title, their first since 1968.</p>
<p>In the 2003 season, Alex participated in 34 matches and, despite playing defense, managed to score nine goals. Also in 2003, Alex helped Santos reach the <a href="/wiki/2003_Copa_Libertadores" title="2003 Copa Libertadores">final</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2003_Copa_Libertadores" title="2003 Copa Libertadores">Copa Libertadores</a>, where Santos lost 5–1 on aggregate against Argentine side <a href="/wiki/Boca_Juniors" title="Boca Juniors">Boca Juniors</a>. In the second leg of Copa Libertadores Finals, Alex scored the only goal for Santos in a 3–1 loss.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="PSV_.28loan.29">PSV (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: PSV (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:127px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Alex_psv.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="400" data-file-width="206" height="243" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/Alex_psv.jpg/125px-Alex_psv.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/Alex_psv.jpg/188px-Alex_psv.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/25/Alex_psv.jpg 2x" width="125"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Alex_psv.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Alex with <a href="/wiki/Phillip_Cocu" title="Phillip Cocu">Phillip Cocu</a> (behind) at PSV.</div>

<p>Alex was signed by English <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> in 2004 on the recommendation of famed <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV</a> and Chelsea scout <a href="/wiki/Piet_de_Visser_(football_manager)" title="Piet de Visser (football manager)">Piet de Visser</a>, but because of potential problems in attaining a <a href="/wiki/Work_permit" title="Work permit">work permit</a>, he was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loan_(football)" title="Loan (football)">loaned out</a> to Dutch <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> club PSV.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The joint contract of Chelsea and PSV loaned Alex to the latter, with Chelsea having a €1 million buy-out clause. Alex eventually played three years at PSV, donning the number 4 jersey for the <a href="/wiki/Eindhoven" title="Eindhoven">Eindhoven</a> club. While playing for PSV, he earned himself the nickname "The Tank" due to his cannonball-like free kicks and his strong physical stature.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tank_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tank-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>He formally joined PSV in the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_in_Dutch_football" title="2004–05 in Dutch football">2004–05 season</a>, helping the club win a domestic double of both the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> championship and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dutch_Cup" title="Dutch Cup">Dutch Cup</a> that year. In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League_2004-05" title="UEFA Champions League 2004-05">Champions League</a>, Alex played a key role in the PSV team that was eliminated in the semi-finals against <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a> on away goals (PSV won their home game 3–1 but had previously lost 0–2 at the <a href="/wiki/San_Siro" title="San Siro">San Siro</a>). The team eliminated <a href="/wiki/AS_Monaco_FC" title="AS Monaco FC">AS Monaco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olympique_Lyonnais" title="Olympique Lyonnais">Olympique Lyonnais</a> on their way to the semi-finals.</p>
<p>Alex won a second straight Dutch league championship in the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Eredivisie" title="2005–06 Eredivisie">2005–06 season</a> with PSV. Chelsea did not recall Alex, even though <a href="/wiki/Piet_de_Visser_(football_manager)" title="Piet de Visser (football manager)">Piet de Visser</a> and Chelsea Head of Scouting <a href="/wiki/Frank_Arnesen" title="Frank Arnesen">Frank Arnesen</a> recommended that Alex be brought over to solve the club's defensive problems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-times_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-times-7">[7]</a></sup> Instead, Chelsea manager <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mourinho" title="José Mourinho">José Mourinho</a> insisted on the signing of <a href="/wiki/Khalid_Boulahrouz" title="Khalid Boulahrouz">Khalid Boulahrouz</a> in place of Alex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-times_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-times-7">[7]</a></sup> Therefore, Alex stayed a third season at PSV on loan in <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Eredivisie" title="2006–07 Eredivisie">2006–07</a>. He won a third straight Eredivisie title with PSV, after a dramatic final-day finish to the season in which PSV sensationally trumped both <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">AFC Ajax</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AZ_(football_club)" title="AZ (football club)">AZ</a>. Alex scored in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League_2006-07_knockout_stage#First_knockout_round" title="UEFA Champions League 2006-07 knockout stage">UEFA Champions League first knockout round</a> against <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> for both teams in the second half, turning in an <a href="/wiki/Own_goal" title="Own goal">own goal</a> past his own goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Heurelho_Gomes" title="Heurelho Gomes">Heurelho Gomes</a>, but compensating for it with a storming headed goal with eight minutes left to play to redeem himself and in the process ensure PSV a place in the quarter-finals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He was later injured, however, and played no part in PSV's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League_2006-07_knockout_stage#Quarter-finals" title="UEFA Champions League 2006-07 knockout stage">UEFA Champions League quarter-final</a> against <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>, which PSV lost 4–0 on aggregate.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chelsea">Chelsea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Chelsea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.9308">2007–08</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2007–08">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>In an interview with Dutch newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/De_Telegraaf" title="De Telegraaf">De Telegraaf</a></i>, Alex confirmed that Chelsea were in the process of getting him a work permit and that the possibilities of him joining Chelsea next season were very high.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Alex later confirmed that Chelsea had almost completed the process of acquiring him a work permit and that he would start the season as a Chelsea player.</p>
<p>Alex was granted a work permit on 2 August 2007, and completed his transfer to Chelsea after agreeing to personal terms and passing a medical, where he then signed a three-year deal with the <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> club.</p>
<p>On 14 August 2007, Alex was presented to the English media in a press conference with manager <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mourinho" title="José Mourinho">José Mourinho</a>, where he was given shirt number 33. He made his debut on 19 August when he came on as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Florent_Malouda" title="Florent Malouda">Florent Malouda</a> against <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> in the 85th minute, with the teams tied at 1–1. He also played in the 0–2 defeat away at <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a> and the 4–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Alex scored his first goal for Chelsea on 20 October 2007, a 30-yard free kick against <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He scored from another free kick on 28 November in a Champions League game against <a href="/wiki/Rosenborg_BK" title="Rosenborg BK">Rosenborg BK</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He then scored in Chelsea's 4–4 draw with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aston_Villa" title="Aston Villa">Aston Villa</a>, scoring his first goal at <a href="/wiki/Stamford_Bridge_(stadium)" title="Stamford Bridge (stadium)">Stamford Bridge</a> from an <a href="/wiki/Andriy_Shevchenko" title="Andriy Shevchenko">Andriy Shevchenko</a> pass, taking his tally for the season to three goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2008.E2.80.9309">2008–09</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2008–09">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Alex enjoyed his longest spell in the first team during the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Premier_League" title="2008–09 Premier League">2008–09 season</a>. He was given his chance due to regular starter <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Carvalho" title="Ricardo Carvalho">Ricardo Carvalho</a> being injured for long periods of the campaign. He scored Chelsea's 1,000th Premier League goal against <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a> on 1 November 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Alex started every game for Chelsea under <a href="/wiki/Guus_Hiddink" title="Guus Hiddink">Guus Hiddink</a>, who managed Alex at <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV</a>, and also scored in an <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> quarter-final victory away to <a href="/wiki/Coventry_City_F.C." title="Coventry City F.C.">Coventry City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<p>In the Champions League quarter-finals second leg at Stamford Bridge against <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>, Alex scored a free-kick from 30 yards out to level the score at 2–2. The game eventually ended 4–4 and Chelsea won 7–5 on aggregate,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> but Chelsea were eliminated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Barcelona" title="F.C. Barcelona">Barcelona</a> in the semi-finals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On 10 May, Alex scored a header in Chelsea's 4–1 <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> win over Arsenal from a <a href="/wiki/Didier_Drogba" title="Didier Drogba">Didier Drogba</a> free-kick.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The goal was his second against the Gunners in his career.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.9310">2009–10</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2009–10">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Alex played his first pre-season match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AC_Milan" title="AC Milan">AC Milan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/World_Football_Challenge" title="World Football Challenge">World Football Challenge</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 13 October 2009, Alex signed a new four-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a>, keeping him at <a href="/wiki/Stamford_Bridge_(stadium)" title="Stamford Bridge (stadium)">Stamford Bridge</a> until the summer of 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Alex missed the start of the season, having sustained a groin injury on 15 August 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He started his first game of the season in a League Cup match against <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a> on 28 October 2009, playing the full 90 minutes. He made his first <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> start of the season against <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a> on 16 December 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 13 March 2010, he scored his first goal of the season at Stamford Bridge giving Chelsea the lead in a 4–1 league win over <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.9311">2010–11</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2010–11">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>In light of Ricardo Carvalho's departure to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_Madrid" title="Real Madrid">Real Madrid</a>, Alex started the 2010-11 season as first choice centre back for Chelsea, alongside <a href="/wiki/John_Terry" title="John Terry">John Terry</a>. On 3 October 2010, he scored a 25-yard freekick in a 2–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> at <a href="/wiki/Stamford_Bridge_(stadium)" title="Stamford Bridge (stadium)">Stamford Bridge</a>. On 5 October, he was ruled out for up to three weeks after picking up a thigh injury in the final stages of Sunday's 2–0 win over Arsenal, and subsequently withdrew from the Brazil squad for the friendly matches against <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Alex made his return for Chelsea where he made his return on 30 October 2010 and played 90 minutes against <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> in a 2-1 win. On 1 December 2010, Alex suffered a knee injury and was out of action about 3 months. While rehabilitation on his injury, Manager <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Ancelotti" title="Carlo Ancelotti">Carlo Ancelotti</a> would consider signing a new defender if Alex fails to recover from knee surgery before the end of January but Alex failed to do so and Chelsea went on to sign <a href="/wiki/David_Luiz" title="David Luiz">David Luiz</a> from Benfica.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> By mid March 2011, Alex made his return to training with the first-team squad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>After he recovered from the injury, Alex made his return on 9 April 2011 in a match against <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a> in a 1-0 win, where he came on for <a href="/wiki/Paulo_Ferreira" title="Paulo Ferreira">Paulo Ferreira</a> in the 75th minute. On 8 May 2011, it took a goal-costing mistake from David Luiz against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchester_United" title="Manchester United">Manchester United</a> in the season-deciding match for Alex to break back into the starting line-up, getting substituted on at half time that match, and then starting and playing the full 90 minutes the following match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newcastle_United" title="Newcastle United">Newcastle United</a> at Stamford Bridge on 15 May 2011, where he scored a headed goal in the 82nd minute in a 2–2 draw. In the last league game of the season against <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a>, Alex started the match alongside <a href="/wiki/John_Terry" title="John Terry">John Terry</a> but was substituted on 70 minutes for Ferreira in their 1–0 defeat at <a href="/wiki/Goodison_Park" title="Goodison Park">Goodison Park</a>. At the end of the campaign, Alex made 20 appearances and scored 2 goals in all competitions. Alex was criticized for allegedly stating in an interview that he was a boyhood Liverpool supporter.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.9312">2011–12</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2011–12">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the summer transfer window, Alex was the subject of a bid from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C.-Chelsea_F.C._rivalry" title="Arsenal F.C.-Chelsea F.C. rivalry">local rivals</a> <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> On 20 August 2011, in a home game against <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a>, a defensive mistake from Alex allowed striker <a href="/wiki/Shane_Long" title="Shane Long">Shane Long</a> through to score four minutes into the game. In the end, however, Chelsea won 2–1. On 21 September 2011, in a League Cup tie against <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a>, Alex received a straight red card in the 49th minute after a foul on <a href="/wiki/Kerim_Frei" title="Kerim Frei">Kerim Frei</a>, which also resulted a penalty. <a href="/wiki/Pajtim_Kasami" title="Pajtim Kasami">Pajtim Kasami</a>, however, missed Fulham's penalty after his kick was saved by back-up goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Ross_Turnbull" title="Ross Turnbull">Ross Turnbull</a>. In the end, Chelsea later won 4-3 on penalties to progress to the next round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In November 2011, Alex made his last Chelsea appearance in a League Cup in a quarter-final against <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>, which Chelsea lost 2-0. He started the match but committed a handball in the penalty area, which Turnbull again saved from <a href="/wiki/Andy_Carroll" title="Andy Carroll">Andy Carroll</a>.</p>
<p>On 3 December 2011, Alex submitted a transfer request, which was accepted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> He had been the subject of a bid from <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a> in the first transfer activity under their new manager <a href="/wiki/Mark_Hughes" title="Mark Hughes">Mark Hughes</a>. The move, however, broke down after both parties reportedly failed to agree personal terms; QPR could not match his wage demands and Alex stated he did not want to stay in England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Paris_Saint-Germain">Paris Saint-Germain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Paris Saint-Germain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 27 January 2012, Alex joined <a href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain_F.C." title="Paris Saint-Germain F.C.">Paris Saint-Germain</a> for £4.2 million<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> to reunite with his former manager Carlo Ancelotti. On his move, he publicly stated that the reason for it was that <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> manager <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Villas-Boas" title="André Villas-Boas">André Villas-Boas</a> had not given him enough playing time at the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> He was given the number 13 shirt, free since the departure of <a href="/wiki/Sammy_Traor%C3%A9" title="Sammy Traoré">Sammy Traoré</a>.</p>
<p>On 12 February 2012, Alex made his debut for PSG in a 0–0 draw against <a href="/wiki/OGC_Nice" title="OGC Nice">Nice</a>, where he made his first start before coming off for <a href="/wiki/Christophe_Jallet" title="Christophe Jallet">Christophe Jallet</a> at half-time. The following week, on 19 February 2012, Alex scored a 30-yard goal from a lay-off free kick, his first goal for PSG, against Montpellier on a free-kick combination with compatriot <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nen%C3%AA_(footballer_born_1981)" title="Nenê (footballer born 1981)">Nenê</a>, which the result was 2–2. On 8 April 2012, Alex scored the winning goal in the 61st minute in a 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Olympique de Marseille</a>. On 6 November 2012, in a Champions League match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dinamo_Zagreb" title="Dinamo Zagreb">Dinamo Zagreb</a>, Alex scored the first goal of the match, a half-volley that evaded the reach of Dinamo goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Kelava" title="Ivan Kelava">Ivan Kelava</a> after 16 minutes.</p>
<p>On 3 August 2013, Alex scored the game-winning goal with the last kick of the game in PSG's 2–1 defeat of <a href="/wiki/FC_Girondins_de_Bordeaux" title="FC Girondins de Bordeaux">Bordeaux</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2013_Troph%C3%A9e_des_Champions" title="2013 Trophée des Champions">Trophée des Champions</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Stade_d%27Angondj%C3%A9" title="Stade d'Angondjé">Stade d'Angondjé</a> in <a href="/wiki/Libreville" title="Libreville">Libreville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gabon" title="Gabon">Gabon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="A.C_Milan">A.C Milan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: A.C Milan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 5 June 2014, Alex signed for <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">A.C. Milan</a> on free transfer when his contract with PSG expired.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> He made his competitive debut on 31 August in their first match of the new <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> season, a 3–1 home win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SS_Lazio" title="SS Lazio">Lazio</a> in which he deflected <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Candreva" title="Antonio Candreva">Antonio Candreva</a>'s shot into his own net.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Alex scored his first Milan goal in a 3-2 loss to <a href="/wiki/U.S._Sassuolo_Calcio" title="U.S. Sassuolo Calcio">Sassuolo</a> on 17 May 2015. On 31 January 2016, Alex scored a diving header in the <a href="/wiki/Derby_della_Madonnina" title="Derby della Madonnina">Derby della Madonnina</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 25 May 2016, club owner <a href="/wiki/Silvio_Berlusconi" title="Silvio Berlusconi">Silvio Berlusconi</a> announced his departure, along with those of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phillippe_Mexes" title="Phillippe Mexes">Phillippe Mexes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kevin-Prince_Boateng" title="Kevin-Prince Boateng">Kevin-Prince Boateng</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mario_Balotelli" title="Mario Balotelli">Mario Balotelli</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_(footballer,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alex made his debut for <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a> on 17 July 2003 against <a href="/wiki/Mexico_national_football_team" title="Mexico national football team">Mexico</a> in <a href="/wiki/2003_CONCACAF_Gold_Cup" title="2003 CONCACAF Gold Cup">2003 CONCACAF Gold Cup</a>. Although he played under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brazil_Olympic_football_team" title="Brazil Olympic football team">Brazil U-23 team</a> at the tournament, the tournament was a full international tournament, hence the international cap gained was fully recognized by <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was overlooked for the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a> squad. However, after the World Cup, he earned regular call-ups with the new head coach, <a href="/wiki/Dunga" title="Dunga">Dunga</a>. He was a member of the Brazil squad that won the <a href="/wiki/2007_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2007 Copa América">2007 Copa América</a> with a 3–0 victory over arch-rivals <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a> in the final. He was dropped from the Brazilian squad for the <a href="/wiki/2009_FIFA_Confederations_Cup" title="2009 FIFA Confederations Cup">2009 FIFA Confederations Cup</a> due to injuries.</p>
<p>On 11 May, he was called up to the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> squad as one of the seven back-up players.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
