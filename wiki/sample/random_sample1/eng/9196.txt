<p><b>Alessio Cragno</b> (born 28 June 1994) is an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who currently plays as a <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">goalkeeper</a> for <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> club <a href="/wiki/Benevento_Calcio" title="Benevento Calcio">Benevento</a>, on loan from <a href="/wiki/Cagliari_Calcio" title="Cagliari Calcio">Cagliari</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alessio_Cragno&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cragno made his debut at <a href="/wiki/Brescia_Calcio" title="Brescia Calcio">Brescia Calcio</a> on 25 September 2012 against <a href="/wiki/Modena_F.C." title="Modena F.C.">Modena</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> game, as the starting keeper, ahead <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Russo" title="Stefano Russo">Stefano Russo</a>; first choice <a href="/wiki/Michele_Arcari" title="Michele Arcari">Michele Arcari</a> was rested. Brescia won the game 2–1 after two goals from <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Caracciolo" title="Andrea Caracciolo">Andrea Caracciolo</a>. Before the start of season Brescia had sold their first choice keeper <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Leali" title="Nicola Leali">Nicola Leali</a> to Juventus. Cragno has been the first-choice regular since the 2013–14 season and performed well. Cragno also signed a contract with Brecia which would last until 30 June 2016, in 2012–13 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2014, he moved to <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> side <a href="/wiki/Cagliari_Calcio" title="Cagliari Calcio">Cagliari</a>, penning a four-year deal<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> for a fee of €1 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> His debut for Cagliari was in a <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> match against <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Roma</a> on 21 September 2014, losing 2–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 2016 he his sent on loan to <a href="/wiki/S.S._Virtus_Lanciano_1924" title="S.S. Virtus Lanciano 1924">Lanciano</a> in Serie B,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> the club is then relegated in <a href="/wiki/Lega_Pro" title="Lega Pro">Lega Pro</a> after losing the play-out matches against <a href="/wiki/U.S._Salernitana_1919" title="U.S. Salernitana 1919">Salernitana</a>. On 9 June 2016 Cragno signed a new 4-year deal with Cagliari.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the summer 2016 he is sent again on loan with option to buy, to Serie B newcomer <a href="/wiki/Benevento_Calcio" title="Benevento Calcio">Benevento</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alessio_Cragno&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He made his debut with the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">Italy under-21</a> squad on 4 June 2014, replacing <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Bardi" title="Francesco Bardi">Francesco Bardi</a> in a friendly match won 4–0 against <a href="/wiki/Montenegro_national_under-21_football_team" title="Montenegro national under-21 football team">Montenegro</a>.</p>
