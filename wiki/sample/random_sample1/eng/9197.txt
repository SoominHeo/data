<p><b>Aleandro Rosi</b> (born 17 May 1987) is an Italian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who currently plays for <a href="/wiki/F.C._Crotone" title="F.C. Crotone">Crotone</a> on loan from <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a>. Capable of playing both as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">full-back</a> and as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Winger" title="Midfielder">winger</a> along the right flank, he is known for his versatility, being adept both offensively and defensively, and for his pace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleandro_Rosi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth_career">Youth career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleandro_Rosi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Youth career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rosi was originally in the <a href="/wiki/S.S._Lazio" title="S.S. Lazio">Lazio</a> youth system but switched over to Roma when he was 12 years old. He signed with <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">A.S. Roma</a> his first professional contract on 1 July 2004.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roma">Roma</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleandro_Rosi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roma">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rosi made his <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> debut on 28 May 2005, when Roma drew 0–0 with <a href="/wiki/A.C._ChievoVerona" title="A.C. ChievoVerona">Chievo Verona</a>. That was his only appearance in his first season with <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Roma</a>. He then appeared in fifteen games during the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Serie_A" title="2005–06 Serie A">2005–06 season</a>. He scored his first <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> goal against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parma_F.C." title="Parma F.C.">Parma</a> on 24 September 2006. Rosi made his <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2006–07 UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> debut on 18 October 2006 in a 1–0 away victory over <a href="/wiki/Olympiacos_F.C." title="Olympiacos F.C.">Olympiacos</a>. Rosi was also part of the A.S. Roma team that lost 7–1 against Manchester United in April 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> His second goal was scored against <a href="/wiki/Messina" title="Messina">Messina</a> during the last home game of the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Serie_A" title="2006–07 Serie A">2006–07 season</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Loans_.26_Co-ownership">Loans &amp; Co-ownership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleandro_Rosi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Loans &amp; Co-ownership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After a relatively unsuccessful loan spell with <a href="/wiki/A.C._ChievoVerona" title="A.C. ChievoVerona">Chievo</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Serie_A" title="2007–08 Serie A">2007–08 season</a>, Roma loaned him to <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> club <a href="/wiki/A.S._Livorno_Calcio" title="A.S. Livorno Calcio">Livorno</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Serie_A" title="2008–09 Serie A">2008–2009 season</a> where he made 37 appearances.</p>
<p>In August 2009, Rosi joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Siena" title="A.C. Siena">Siena</a> in a co-ownership deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Rosi played a total of 28 games for Siena, scoring his first and only goal for Siena in a 3–2 win over <a href="/wiki/F.C._Bari_1908" title="F.C. Bari 1908">Bari</a> which proved to be the winner.</p>
<p>For the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Serie_A" title="2010–11 Serie A">2010–11 Serie A</a> season, Rosi was loaned back to Roma. He scored a winner in the club's victory against Bari.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="A.S._Roma_Return">A.S. Roma Return</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleandro_Rosi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: A.S. Roma Return">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>For the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Serie_A" title="2011–12 Serie A">2011–12 season</a>, Rosi returned to Roma.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Parma">Parma</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleandro_Rosi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Parma">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Unwilling to spend another season on loan, Rosi and <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Roma</a> terminated his contract by mutual consent on 7 August 2012. Rosi signed a five-year contract with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parma_F.C." title="Parma F.C.">Parma</a> on the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Genoa">Genoa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleandro_Rosi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Genoa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In summer 2014 Rosi was sold to <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa C.F.C.</a> for €3.825 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> with <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A1niel_T%C5%91zs%C3%A9r" title="Dániel Tőzsér">Dániel Tőzsér</a> moved to Parma for €3 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleandro_Rosi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rosi has represented <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> at different youth levels 15 times. Rosi made his last appearance in 2005 for the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-19_football_team" title="Italy national under-19 football team">Italy under-19 football team</a>.</p>
