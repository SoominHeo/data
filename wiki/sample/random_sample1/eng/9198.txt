<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Science_and_Technology">Science and Technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleph_(disambiguation)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Science and Technology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Aleph, an advanced system for <a href="/wiki/Inductive_logic_programming" title="Inductive logic programming">inductive logic programming</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/ALEPH_experiment" title="ALEPH experiment">ALEPH experiment</a> (Apparatus for LEP Physics at CERN), one of the four detectors of the Large Electron-Positron Collider (LEP) at CERN</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alef_(programming_language)" title="Alef (programming language)">Alef (programming language)</a>, the concurrent programming language used in early editions of <a href="/wiki/Plan_9_from_Bell_Labs" title="Plan 9 from Bell Labs">Plan 9 from Bell Labs</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Aleph_kernel" title="Aleph kernel">Aleph kernel</a>, an operating system kernel, notable primarily for being one of the steps in the evolution of the Mach kernel</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mathematics">Mathematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleph_(disambiguation)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mathematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Aleph_number" title="Aleph number">Aleph number</a>, a sequence of cardinal numbers used to represent the cardinality (or size) of infinite sets.</li>
</ul>

<ul>

</ul>
</li>
<li>Aleph, a character in the Warren Ellis comic series <i><a href="/wiki/Global_Frequency" title="Global Frequency">Global Frequency</a></i></li>
