<div class="hatnote" role="note">This name uses <a href="/wiki/Spanish_naming_customs" title="Spanish naming customs">Spanish naming customs</a>: the first or paternal <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">family name</a> is <i>Alfaro</i> and the second or maternal family name is <i>Ligero</i>.</div>
<p><b>Alejandro Alfaro Ligero</b> (born 23 November 1986) is a Spanish professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/C%C3%B3rdoba_CF" title="Córdoba CF">Córdoba CF</a>. Mainly a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Winger" title="Midfielder">right winger</a>, he can also operate as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Second_striker" title="Forward (association football)">second striker</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alejandro_Alfaro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A product of <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla FC</a>'s youth system, Alfaro was one of <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_Atl%C3%A9tico" title="Sevilla Atlético">Sevilla Atlético</a>'s most important players from 2005 onwards, helping it achieve promotion to the <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">second division</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="2006–07 Segunda División B">2006–07 season</a>. He had previously made his first-team debut on 30 April 2006, playing eight minutes in a 2–1 away win against <a href="/wiki/Real_Sociedad" title="Real Sociedad">Real Sociedad</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Alfaro scored his first <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> goal on 28 January 2007, as a starter in a 4–2 win at <a href="/wiki/Levante_UD" title="Levante UD">Levante UD</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> also appearing in four <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a> games in the club's <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_UEFA_Cup" title="2006–07 UEFA Cup">victorious campaign</a>. However, he was nothing more than a fringe player with the main squad in his first three seasons, barred mainly by <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_Navas" title="Jesús Navas">Jesús Navas</a> (he did net seven goals in 34 matches to help the reserves <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2007–08 Segunda División">maintain their second level status</a>), and would leave the <a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">Andalusians</a> for <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2008–09 Segunda División">2008–09</a>, joining <a href="/wiki/CD_Tenerife" title="CD Tenerife">CD Tenerife</a> on loan;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> during this stint, he was instrumental as the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a> side returned to the top flight after a seven-year-absence by scoring a career-best 20 goals, fourth-best in the competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DV_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-DV-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>After he completed the 2009 preseason with Sevilla, Alfaro was again loaned for a season to Tenerife.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Like <a href="/wiki/Nino_(footballer)" title="Nino (footballer)">Nino</a> he was again the most important offensive unit of the team, which was <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_La_Liga" title="2009–10 La Liga">eventually relegated back</a>, with the player contributing with seven goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DV_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-DV-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Returned to Sevilla for <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_La_Liga" title="2010–11 La Liga">2010–11</a>, Alfaro benefitted from Navas' absence due to injury and featured in some games early into the season, scoring in a 2–1 win at <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1laga_CF" title="Málaga CF">Málaga CF</a> on 19 September 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On 4 November, he netted twice against <a href="/wiki/FC_Karpaty_Lviv" title="FC Karpaty Lviv">FC Karpaty Lviv</a> (4–0 home win) for <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2010–11 UEFA Europa League">the season</a>'s Europa League;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> the following week, he also found the net in two home fixtures, against <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF" title="Valencia CF">Valencia CF</a> (2–0, appearing as a second-half <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>) and <a href="/wiki/Real_Uni%C3%B3n" title="Real Unión">Real Unión</a> (6–1, in <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Copa_del_Rey" title="2010–11 Copa del Rey">the season</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 8 August 2011, <a href="/wiki/RCD_Mallorca" title="RCD Mallorca">RCD Mallorca</a> agreed a deal with Sevilla to buy Alfaro for five years and <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>700,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He started in his first official game, a 1–0 home win against <a href="/wiki/RCD_Espanyol" title="RCD Espanyol">RCD Espanyol</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 18 August 2014, Alfaro terminated his contract with the <i>Bermellones</i> and joined <a href="/wiki/Superleague_Greece" title="Superleague Greece">Superleague Greece</a> side <a href="/wiki/Panathinaikos_F.C." title="Panathinaikos F.C.">Panathinaikos FC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> However, the deal collapsed two days later,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and he signed a two-year deal with <a href="/wiki/Real_Valladolid" title="Real Valladolid">Real Valladolid</a> late in the month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 July 2016, after being sparingly used during his two-year tenure, Alfaro joined <a href="/wiki/C%C3%B3rdoba_CF" title="Córdoba CF">Córdoba CF</a> also in division two, for one season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alejandro_Alfaro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alfaro's two brothers are also footballers: Juan José (1981), a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a>, graduated in <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a>'s <a href="/wiki/La_Masia" title="La Masia">La Masia</a>, but spent his entire career in <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="Segunda División B">Segunda División B</a> or lower. <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">Forward</a> Jesús (1991) appeared several seasons for Sevilla B, after emerging through the club's youth ranks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
