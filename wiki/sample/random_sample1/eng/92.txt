<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Template:Events_by_year_for_decade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="Template:Events by year for decade">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1560">1560</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle selfreference noprint" role="note">This section is <a href="/wiki/Transclusion" title="Transclusion">transcluded</a> from <a href="/wiki/1560" title="1560">1560</a>. <span class="plainlinks" style="font-style: normal"><small>(<a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1560&amp;action=edit">edit</a> | <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1560&amp;action=history">history</a>)</small></span></div>
<p><br/></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1560&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1560">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_27" title="February 27">February 27</a> – <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Berwick_(1560)" title="Treaty of Berwick (1560)">Treaty of Berwick</a>: Terms agreed with the <a href="/wiki/Lords_of_the_Congregation" title="Lords of the Congregation">Lords of the Congregation</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Scotland" title="Kingdom of Scotland">Kingdom of Scotland</a> for forces of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">Kingdom of England</a> to enter Scotland to expel <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_France" title="Kingdom of France">French</a> troops defending the Regency of <a href="/wiki/Mary_of_Guise" title="Mary of Guise">Mary of Guise</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_7" title="March 7">March 7</a> – A Spanish-led expedition, commanded by <a href="/wiki/Juan_de_la_Cerda,_4th_Duke_of_Medinaceli" title="Juan de la Cerda, 4th Duke of Medinaceli">Juan de la Cerda, 4th Duke of Medinaceli</a>, overruns the Tunisian island of <a href="/wiki/Djerba" title="Djerba">Djerba</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_17" title="March 17">March 17</a> – Leaders of the <a href="/wiki/Amboise_conspiracy" title="Amboise conspiracy">Amboise conspiracy</a>, including Godefroy de Barry, seigneur de La Renaudie, make an unsuccessful attempt to storm the château of <a href="/wiki/Amboise" title="Amboise">Amboise</a>, where the young French king and queen are residing. La Renaudie is subsequently caught and executed, along with over a thousand of his followers.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_15" title="April 15">April 15</a> – <a href="/wiki/Denmark%E2%80%93Norway" title="Denmark–Norway">Denmark</a> buys the <a href="/wiki/Terra_Mariana" title="Terra Mariana">Estonian</a> island of <a href="/wiki/Saaremaa" title="Saaremaa">Saaremaa</a> from its last prince bishop.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_11" title="May 11">May 11</a> – At the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Djerba" title="Battle of Djerba">Battle of Djerba</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> fleet, commanded by <a href="/wiki/Piali_Pasha" title="Piali Pasha">Piali Pasha</a>, overwhelms a large joint European (mainly Spanish) fleet, sinking about half its ships.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_12" title="June 12">June 12</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Okehazama" title="Battle of Okehazama">Battle of Okehazama</a>: <a href="/wiki/Oda_Nobunaga" title="Oda Nobunaga">Oda Nobunaga</a> defeats <a href="/wiki/Imagawa_Yoshimoto" title="Imagawa Yoshimoto">Imagawa Yoshimoto</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1560&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1560">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_6" title="July 6">July 6</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Edinburgh" title="Treaty of Edinburgh">Treaty of Edinburgh</a> is signed between England, France and Scotland. The French withdraw from Scotland. This largely ends the <i><a href="/wiki/Auld_Alliance" title="Auld Alliance">Auld Alliance</a></i> between France and Scotland, and ends the wars between England and its northern neighbour.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_2" title="August 2">August 2</a> – <a href="/wiki/Livonian_War" title="Livonian War">Livonian War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Tsardom_of_Russia" title="Tsardom of Russia">Russians</a> defeat the <a href="/wiki/Livonian_Brothers_of_the_Sword" title="Livonian Brothers of the Sword">Livonian Brothers of the Sword</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ergeme" title="Battle of Ergeme">Battle of Ergeme</a>, precipitating the dissolution of the order.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_17" title="August 17">August 17</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Reformation_Parliament" title="Scottish Reformation Parliament">Scottish Reformation Parliament</a> adopts a <a href="/wiki/Protestantism" title="Protestantism">Protestant</a> confession of faith and <a href="/wiki/Papal_Jurisdiction_Act_1560" title="Papal Jurisdiction Act 1560">rejects papal authority</a>, beginning the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Reformation" title="Scottish Reformation">Scottish Reformation</a> and disestablishing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism_in_Scotland" title="Roman Catholicism in Scotland">Roman Catholicism in Scotland</a>.</li>

Her mother-in-law, <a href="/wiki/Catherine_de%27_Medici" title="Catherine de' Medici">Catherine de' Medici</a>, becomes regent of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_France" title="Kingdom of France">France</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_6" title="December 6">December 6</a> – <a href="/wiki/Charles_IX_of_France" title="Charles IX of France">Charles IX of France</a> succeeds his elder brother, <a href="/wiki/Francis_II_of_France" title="Francis II of France">Francis II</a>, at the age of ten.</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June_2">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1561&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1561">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_31" title="January 31">January 31</a> – The Edict of Orleans suspends the persecution of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Huguenots" title="Huguenots">Huguenots</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_France" title="Kingdom of France">France</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_1" title="March 1">March 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Kingston_Grammar_School" title="Kingston Grammar School">Kingston Grammar School</a> is founded in <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">England</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_14" title="April 14">April 14</a> – The citizens of <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a> see what appears to be <a href="/wiki/1561_celestial_phenomenon_over_Nuremberg" title="1561 celestial phenomenon over Nuremberg">an aerial battle</a> followed by the appearance of a large black triangular object and a large crash (with smoke) outside the city. A <i>news notice</i> (an early form of newspaper) is printed on (<a href="/wiki/April_14" title="April 14">April 14</a>) describing the event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_8" title="May 8">May 8</a> – <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> is declared the capital of <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spain</a> by <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Spain" title="Philip II of Spain">Philip II</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_4" title="June 4">June 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/Old_St_Paul%27s_Cathedral" title="Old St Paul's Cathedral">Old St Paul's Cathedral</a> in the <a href="/wiki/City_of_London" title="City of London">City of London</a> is badly damaged by fire and the spire is destroyed after being struck by lightning. The spire is not rebuilt.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_6" title="June 6">June 6</a> – <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> conquers <a href="/wiki/Livonia" title="Livonia">Livonia</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_25" title="June 25">June 25</a> – <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Francis_Coxe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Francis Coxe (page does not exist)">Francis Coxe</a>, an English astrologer, is pilloried at <a href="/wiki/Cheapside" title="Cheapside">Cheapside</a> in London and makes a public confession of his involvement in "sinistral and divelysh artes".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_29" title="June 29">June 29</a> – <a href="/wiki/Eric_XIV_of_Sweden" title="Eric XIV of Sweden">Erik XIV</a> is crowned <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_of_Sweden" title="King of Sweden">King of Sweden</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December_2">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1561&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1561">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>July – Killing of the hated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Encomendero" title="Encomendero">encomendero</a> <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_Avenda%C3%B1o" title="Pedro de Avendaño">Pedro de Avendaño</a> and two other Spaniards triggered the Second Great Rebellion of the <a href="/wiki/Mapuche" title="Mapuche">Mapuche</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Arauco_War" title="Arauco War">Arauco War</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_12" title="July 12">July 12</a> – <a href="/wiki/Saint_Basil%27s_Cathedral" title="Saint Basil's Cathedral">Saint Basil's Cathedral</a> in Moscow (started in <a href="/wiki/1534" title="1534">1534</a>) is finished.</li>
<li>August – <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> merchant <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Jenkinson" title="Anthony Jenkinson">Anthony Jenkinson</a> arrives in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> on his second expedition to the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Moscow" title="Grand Duchy of Moscow">Grand Duchy of Moscow</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_19" title="August 19">August 19</a> – <a href="/wiki/Mary,_Queen_of_Scots" title="Mary, Queen of Scots">Mary, Queen of Scots</a>, is denied passage through England after returning from <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_France" title="Kingdom of France">France</a>. She arrives at <a href="/wiki/Leith" title="Leith">Leith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Scotland" title="Kingdom of Scotland">Scotland</a> on <a href="/wiki/August_19" title="August 19">August 19</a>.</li>
<li>September – The Protestant reformer <a href="/wiki/John_Knox" title="John Knox">John Knox</a> has a three-day debate in <a href="/wiki/Maybole" title="Maybole">Maybole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ayrshire" title="Ayrshire">Ayrshire</a>, Scotland with Quintin Kennedy, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commendator" title="Commendator">commendator</a> of Crossraguel Abbey, on <a href="/wiki/Transubstantiation" title="Transubstantiation">transubstantiation</a>. The result is inconclusive but Kennedy is fighting a losing battle against the Reformation, which had been confirmed by the Scottish government in <a href="/wiki/1560" title="1560">1560</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_18" title="October 18">October 18</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fourth_Battle_of_Kawanakajima" title="Fourth Battle of Kawanakajima">Fourth Battle of Kawanakajima</a>: <a href="/wiki/Takeda_Shingen" title="Takeda Shingen">Takeda Shingen</a> defeats <a href="/wiki/Uesugi_Kenshin" title="Uesugi Kenshin">Uesugi Kenshin</a> in the <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/climax#Noun" title="wikt:climax">climax</a> of their ongoing <a href="/wiki/War" title="War">conflicts</a>.</li>
</ul>

<li>First performance of <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Norton" title="Thomas Norton">Thomas Norton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Sackville,_1st_Earl_of_Dorset" title="Thomas Sackville, 1st Earl of Dorset">Thomas Sackville</a>'s play <i><a href="/wiki/Gorboduc_(play)" title="Gorboduc (play)">Gorboduc</a></i> before Queen Elizabeth I of England. <li><a href="/wiki/May_1" title="May 1">May 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Jean_Ribault" title="Jean Ribault">Jean Ribault</a>, French navigator, lands in <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> and later establishes a <a href="/wiki/Huguenot" title="Huguenot">Huguenot</a> colony at <a href="/wiki/Charlesfort-Santa_Elena_Site" title="Charlesfort-Santa Elena Site">Charlesfort</a> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parris_Island" title="Parris Island">Parris Island</a>, off the coast of <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a>.</li>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_12" title="July 12">July 12</a> – Fray <a href="/wiki/Diego_de_Landa" title="Diego de Landa">Diego de Landa</a>, acting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Yucatan" title="Bishop of Yucatan">Bishop of Yucatan</a>, burns the sacred books of the <a href="/wiki/Maya_civilization" title="Maya civilization">Maya</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_20" title="September 20">September 20</a> – <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Hampton_Court_(1562)" title="Treaty of Hampton Court (1562)">Treaty of Hampton Court</a> between Queen Elizabeth I of England and <a href="/wiki/Huguenot" title="Huguenot">Huguenot</a> leader <a href="/wiki/Louis,_Prince_of_Cond%C3%A9_(1530%E2%80%931569)" title="Louis, Prince of Condé (1530–1569)">Louis, Prince of Condé</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_22" title="September 22">September 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Maximilian II, Holy Roman Emperor">Maximilian</a>, son of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emperor_Ferdinand_I" title="Emperor Ferdinand I">Emperor Ferdinand I</a>, succeeds as King of <a href="/wiki/Bohemia" title="Bohemia">Bohemia</a>.</li>
<li>October – <a href="/wiki/John_Hawkins_(naval_commander)" title="John Hawkins (naval commander)">John Hawkins</a> initiates the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">English</a> trans-Atlantic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slave_trade" title="Slave trade">slave trade</a>, shipping slaves from <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Leone" title="Sierra Leone">Sierra Leone</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guinea_Coast" title="Guinea Coast">Guinea Coast</a> to <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBH_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBH-9">[9]</a></sup> As a result, the Spanish prohibit English ships from trading at their new world ports.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_4" title="October 4">October 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">English</a> forces under <a href="/wiki/Ambrose_Dudley,_3rd_Earl_of_Warwick" title="Ambrose Dudley, 3rd Earl of Warwick">Ambrose Dudley, 3rd Earl of Warwick</a>, land at <a href="/wiki/Le_Havre" title="Le Havre">Le Havre</a> to aid the Huguenots.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_19" title="October 19">October 19</a> – <a href="/wiki/La_Herradura_naval_disaster_(1562)" title="La Herradura naval disaster (1562)">La Herradura naval disaster</a>, 25 ships sank in a storm and some 5000 people were killed.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_26" title="October 26">October 26</a> – <a href="/wiki/Rouen" title="Rouen">Rouen</a> is captured by Royalist forces under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antoine_de_Bourbon" title="Antoine de Bourbon">Antoine de Bourbon</a>, King of <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">Navarre</a>, who is mortally wounded.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/November_20" title="November 20">November 20</a> – <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Maximilian II, Holy Roman Emperor">Maximilian</a> of Bohemia is elected <a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Romans" title="King of the Romans">King of the Romans</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_19" title="December 19">December 19</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Dreux" title="Battle of Dreux">Battle of Dreux</a>: Huguenot forces under Condé and Coligny, and Catholic forces under the ageing <a href="/wiki/Anne_de_Montmorency" title="Anne de Montmorency">Anne, duc de Montmorency</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Francis,_Duke_of_Guise" title="Francis, Duke of Guise">Francis, Duke of Guise</a>, fight a bloody battle, narrowly won by the Catholic side. <ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_1" title="February 1">February 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Sarsa_Dengel" title="Sarsa Dengel">Sarsa Dengel</a> succeeds his father <a href="/wiki/Menas_of_Ethiopia" title="Menas of Ethiopia">Menas</a> as <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Ethiopia" title="Emperor of Ethiopia">Emperor of Ethiopia</a>.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/March_19" title="March 19">March 19</a> – <a href="/wiki/Edict_of_Amboise" title="Edict of Amboise">Edict of Amboise</a> signed at the <a href="/wiki/Ch%C3%A2teau_d%27Amboise" title="Château d'Amboise">Château d'Amboise</a> by <a href="/wiki/Catherine_de%27_Medici" title="Catherine de' Medici">Catherine de' Medici</a>, acting as regent for her son <a href="/wiki/Charles_IX_of_France" title="Charles IX of France">Charles IX of France</a>, having been negotiated between the <a href="/wiki/Huguenot" title="Huguenot">Huguenot</a> <a href="/wiki/Louis,_Prince_of_Cond%C3%A9_(1530%E2%80%931569)" title="Louis, Prince of Condé (1530–1569)">Louis, Prince of Condé</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Anne_de_Montmorency" title="Anne de Montmorency">Anne, duc de Montmorency</a>, <a href="/wiki/Constable_of_France" title="Constable of France">Constable of France</a>. It accords some toleration to the Huguenots, especially to aristocrats. It officially ends the first phase of the <a href="/wiki/French_Wars_of_Religion" title="French Wars of Religion">French Wars of Religion</a> and the combined Huguenot and royal armies then march north to besiege the English in <a href="/wiki/Le_Havre" title="Le Havre">Le Havre</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_30" title="May 30">May 30</a> – At <a href="/wiki/Bornholm" title="Bornholm">Bornholm</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Denmark%E2%80%93Norway" title="Denmark–Norway">Danish</a> fleet fires on the <a href="/wiki/History_of_Sweden_(1523%E2%80%931611)" title="History of Sweden (1523–1611)">Swedish</a> navy, leading to a Danish defeat and precipitating the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Seven_Years%27_War" title="Northern Seven Years' War">Northern Seven Years' War</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December_4">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1563&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1563">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_28" title="July 28">July 28</a> – The English surrender <a href="/wiki/Le_Havre" title="Le Havre">Le Havre</a> to the French after a siege.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_13" title="August 13">August 13</a> – <a href="/wiki/Northern_Seven_Years%27_War" title="Northern Seven Years' War">Northern Seven Years' War</a>: War against the <a href="/wiki/History_of_Sweden_(1523%E2%80%931611)" title="History of Sweden (1523–1611)">Kingdom of Sweden</a> is declared by <a href="/wiki/Denmark%E2%80%93Norway" title="Denmark–Norway">Denmark–Norway</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Free_City_of_L%C3%BCbeck" title="Free City of Lübeck">Free City of Lübeck</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_4" title="September 4">September 4</a> – Northern Seven Years' War: King <a href="/wiki/Frederick_II_of_Denmark" title="Frederick II of Denmark">Frederick II of Denmark</a>, advancing from <a href="/wiki/Halland" title="Halland">Halland</a>, takes <a href="/wiki/%C3%84lvsborg_fortress" title="Älvsborg fortress">Älvsborg fortress</a> from <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_4" title="December 4">December 4</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Trent" title="Council of Trent">Council of Trent</a> (opened <a href="/wiki/December_13" title="December 13">December 13</a>, <a href="/wiki/1545" title="1545">1545</a>) officially closes. It reaffirms all major <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> doctrines, and declares the <a href="/wiki/Deuterocanonical_books" title="Deuterocanonical books">Deuterocanonical books</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Old_Testament" title="Old Testament">Old Testament</a> to be <a href="/wiki/Biblical_canon" title="Biblical canon">canonical</a>, along with the rest of the <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Bible</a>. Chapter 1, Session 24, promulgates the decree <i><a href="/wiki/Tametsi" title="Tametsi">Tametsi</a></i>, stipulating that for a marriage to be valid, consent (the essence of marriage) as expressed in the vows has to be given publicly before witnesses, one of whom has to be the parish priest.</li>
</ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/April_5" title="April 5">April 5</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Compromise_of_Nobles" title="Compromise of Nobles">Compromise of Nobles</a> is presented to <a href="/wiki/Margaret_of_Parma" title="Margaret of Parma">Margaret of Parma</a>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_governors_of_the_Habsburg_Netherlands" title="List of governors of the Habsburg Netherlands">Governor of the Habsburg Netherlands</a>, but succeeds only in delaying the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Eighty_Years%27_War" title="Eighty Years' War">Eighty Years' War</a> in the Netherlands.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/September_7" title="September 7">September 7</a> – <a href="/wiki/Suleiman_the_Magnificent" title="Suleiman the Magnificent">Suleiman the Magnificent</a> dies in his tent at the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Szigetv%C3%A1r" title="Siege of Szigetvár">Siege of Szigetvár</a> and <a href="/wiki/Selim_II" title="Selim II">Selim II</a> succeeds him as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultan_of_the_Ottoman_Empire" title="Sultan of the Ottoman Empire">Sultan of the Ottoman Empire</a>.</li>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June_8">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1567&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1567">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>January – A Spanish force under the command of Captain <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pardo_(explorer)" title="Juan Pardo (explorer)">Juan Pardo</a> establishes <a href="/wiki/Fort_San_Juan_(Joara)" title="Fort San Juan (Joara)">Fort San Juan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a> settlement of <a href="/wiki/Joara" title="Joara">Joara</a>. The fort is the first European settlement in present day <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_23" title="January 23">January 23</a> – After 45 years' reign, the <a href="/wiki/Jiajing_Emperor" title="Jiajing Emperor">Jiajing Emperor</a> dies in the <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_City" title="Forbidden City">Forbidden City</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_4" title="February 4">February 4</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Longqing_Emperor" title="Longqing Emperor">Longqing Emperor</a> ascends the throne of the <a href="/wiki/Ming_dynasty" title="Ming dynasty">Ming dynasty</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_10" title="February 10">February 10</a> – <a href="/wiki/Henry_Stuart,_Lord_Darnley" title="Henry Stuart, Lord Darnley">Henry Stuart, Lord Darnley</a>, husband of <a href="/wiki/Mary,_Queen_of_Scots" title="Mary, Queen of Scots">Mary, Queen of Scots</a>, is murdered at the Provost's House in <a href="/wiki/Kirk_o%27_Field" title="Kirk o' Field">Kirk o' Field</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_15" title="May 15">May 15</a> – Mary, Queen of Scots, marries <a href="/wiki/James_Hepburn,_4th_Earl_of_Bothwell" title="James Hepburn, 4th Earl of Bothwell">James Hepburn, 4th Earl of Bothwell</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_24" title="May 24">May 24</a> – The infamous <a href="/wiki/Sture_Murders" title="Sture Murders">Sture Murders</a> takes place at the <a href="/wiki/Uppsala_Castle" title="Uppsala Castle">Uppsala Castle</a> in Sweden.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_15" title="June 15">June 15</a> – Mary, Queen of Scots, is defeated at <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Carberry_Hill" title="Battle of Carberry Hill">Carberry Hill</a> by the Scottish nobles, and imprisoned in <a href="/wiki/Loch_Leven_Castle" title="Loch Leven Castle">Loch Leven Castle</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December_8">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1567&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1567">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_24" title="July 24">July 24</a> – Mary, Queen of Scots, is forced to abdicate and replaced by her 1-year-old son <a href="/wiki/James_VI_and_I" title="James VI and I">James VI</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_25" title="July 25">July 25</a> – The city of <i>Santiago de León de <a href="/wiki/Caracas" title="Caracas">Caracas</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a> is founded by <a href="/wiki/Diego_de_Losada" title="Diego de Losada">Diego de Losada</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_29" title="July 29">July 29</a> – James VI is crowned at <a href="/wiki/Stirling" title="Stirling">Stirling</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_22" title="August 22">August 22</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Fernando_%C3%81lvarez_de_Toledo,_3rd_Duke_of_Alba" title="Fernando Álvarez de Toledo, 3rd Duke of Alba">Duke of Alba</a> is sent to the Netherlands with a strong Spanish force to suppress unrest there. He replaces <a href="/wiki/Margaret_of_Parma" title="Margaret of Parma">Margaret of Parma</a> as Governor of the Netherlands. Prince <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_I_of_Orange" title="William I of Orange">William of Orange</a> is outlawed, and <a href="/wiki/Lamoral,_Count_of_Egmont" title="Lamoral, Count of Egmont">Lamoral, Count of Egmont</a> imprisoned.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_9" title="September 9">September 9</a> – At a dinner, the <a href="/wiki/Fernando_%C3%81lvarez_de_Toledo,_3rd_Duke_of_Alba" title="Fernando Álvarez de Toledo, 3rd Duke of Alba">Duke of Alba</a> arrests <a href="/wiki/Lamoral,_Count_of_Egmont" title="Lamoral, Count of Egmont">Lamoral, Count of Egmont</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philip_de_Montmorency,_Count_of_Horn" title="Philip de Montmorency, Count of Horn">Philip de Montmorency, Count of Horn</a> for treason.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_29" title="September 29">September 29</a> – The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_War_of_Religion" title="Second War of Religion">Second War of Religion</a> begins in France when the <a href="/wiki/Louis,_Prince_of_Cond%C3%A9_(1530%E2%80%931569)" title="Louis, Prince of Condé (1530–1569)">Louis, Prince of Condé</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gaspard_II_de_Coligny" title="Gaspard II de Coligny">Gaspard de Coligny</a> fail in an attempt to capture <a href="/wiki/Charles_IX_of_France" title="Charles IX of France">King Charles IX</a> and his mother at Meaux. The Huguenots do capture several cities (including Orleans), and march on Paris.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_7" title="October 7">October 7</a> – <a href="/wiki/Bible_translations_into_Welsh" title="Bible translations into Welsh">Bible translations into Welsh</a>: <a href="/wiki/New_Testament" title="New Testament">New Testament</a> first published in <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a>, in <a href="/wiki/William_Salesbury" title="William Salesbury">William Salesbury</a>'s translation from the <a href="/wiki/Koine_Greek#Types" title="Koine Greek">Greek</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_10" title="November 10">November 10</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Saint-Denis_(1567)" title="Battle of Saint-Denis (1567)">Battle of Saint-Denis</a>: <a href="/wiki/Anne_de_Montmorency" title="Anne de Montmorency">Anne de Montmorency</a>, with 16,000 Royalists, falls on Condé's 3,500 Huguenots. The Huguenots surprisingly hold on for some hours before being driven off. Montmorency is mortally wounded.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_6" title="January 6">January 6</a>–<a href="/wiki/January_13" title="January 13">January 13</a> – In the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Hungarian_Kingdom" title="Eastern Hungarian Kingdom">Eastern Hungarian Kingdom</a> the <a href="/wiki/Diet_(assembly)" title="Diet (assembly)">Diet</a> of <a href="/wiki/Turda" title="Turda">Torda</a> declares <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Religious_freedom" title="Religious freedom">religious freedom</a> firstly in Europe.</li>

Again <a href="/wiki/Catherine_de%27_Medici" title="Catherine de' Medici">Catherine de' Medici</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charles_IX_of_France" title="Charles IX of France">Charles IX</a> make substantial concessions to the Huguenots.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/May_19" title="May 19">May 19</a> – Queen <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_I_of_England" title="Elizabeth I of England">Elizabeth I of England</a> arrests <a href="/wiki/Mary,_Queen_of_Scots" title="Mary, Queen of Scots">Mary, Queen of Scots</a>.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/October_20" title="October 20">October 20</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Jodoigne" title="Battle of Jodoigne">Battle of Jodoigne</a>. Spanish forces under the Duke of Alva destroy Orange's rearguard. Orange abandons his offensive.</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June_10">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1569&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="1569">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_11" title="January 11">January 11</a>–<a href="/wiki/May_6" title="May 6">May 6</a> – The first recorded <a href="/wiki/Lottery" title="Lottery">lottery</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">England</a> is performed nonstop at the west door of <a href="/wiki/Old_St_Paul%27s_Cathedral" title="Old St Paul's Cathedral">St Paul's Cathedral</a>. Each share costs ten <a href="/wiki/Shilling" title="Shilling">shillings</a> and proceeds are used to repair harbours and for other public works.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_13" title="March 13">March 13</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Jarnac" title="Battle of Jarnac">Battle of Jarnac</a>: Royalist troops under Marshal Gaspard de Tavannes surprise and defeat the Huguenots under the <a href="/wiki/Louis,_Prince_of_Cond%C3%A9_(1530%E2%80%931569)" title="Louis, Prince of Condé (1530–1569)">Prince of Condé</a>, who is captured and murdered. A substantial proportion of the Huguenot army manages to escape under <a href="/wiki/Gaspard_II_de_Coligny" title="Gaspard II de Coligny">Gaspard de Coligny</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_10" title="June 10">June 10</a> – German Protestant troops reinforce Coligny near <a href="/wiki/Limoges" title="Limoges">Limoges</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December_10">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1569&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="1569">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_1" title="July 1">July 1</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Lublin" title="Union of Lublin">Union of Lublin</a> unites the Kingdom of Poland and the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Lithuania" title="Grand Duchy of Lithuania">Grand Duchy of Lithuania</a> into a single state, the <a href="/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Lithuanian_Commonwealth" title="Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth">Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth</a> following votes in the Assemblies of three Lithuanian provinces (<a href="/wiki/Volhynia" title="Volhynia">Volhynia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Podlasie" title="Podlasie">Podlasie</a>) in favour of the incorporation.</li>
<li>July–September – Huguenot forces under Coligny and 15-year-old Prince <a href="/wiki/Henry_IV_of_France" title="Henry IV of France">Henry of Navarre</a> besiege <a href="/wiki/Poitiers" title="Poitiers">Poitiers</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_24" title="August 24">August 24</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Orthez_1569" title="Battle of Orthez 1569">Battle of Orthez</a>: Huguenot forces under <a href="/wiki/Gabriel,_comte_de_Montgomery" title="Gabriel, comte de Montgomery">Gabriel, comte de Montgomery</a> defeat Royalist forces under General Terride in French Navarre. Catholics surrender under the condition that their lives will be spared. Huguenots agree, but then massacre the Catholics anyway.</li>
<li>September – A Royalist army under the <a href="/wiki/Henry_III_of_France" title="Henry III of France">Duc d'Anjou</a> and Marshal Tavannes forces Coligny to abandon the siege of Poitiers.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_3" title="October 3">October 3</a> – Battle of Moncountour: The Royalist forces of Tavannaes and Anjou defeat Coligny's Huguenots.</li>
<li>November–December – <a href="/wiki/Rising_of_the_North" title="Rising of the North">Rising of the North</a> in England: Three northern Earls lead a rebellion against Queen <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_I_of_England" title="Elizabeth I of England">Elizabeth I</a> in an attempt to place the Catholic <a href="/wiki/Mary,_Queen_of_Scots" title="Mary, Queen of Scots">Mary, Queen of Scots</a>, on the English throne but are driven out of the country.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Date_unknown_9">Date unknown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1569&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-3" title="1569">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Mercator_projection" title="Mercator projection">Mercator projection</a> is first used in <a href="/wiki/Gerardus_Mercator" title="Gerardus Mercator">Gerardus Mercator</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mercator_1569_world_map" title="Mercator 1569 world map">world map</a> <i>Nova et Aucta Orbis Terrae Descriptio ad Usum Navigantium Emendata</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></li>
<li>Damcar appears on the Mercator map or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabia" title="Arabia">Arabia</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>The trade compact of <a href="/wiki/1536" title="1536">1536</a> is renewed, exempting French merchants from <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> law and allowing them to travel, buy and sell throughout the <a href="/wiki/Sultan" title="Sultan">sultan</a>'s dominions and to pay low customs duties on French imports and exports.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Akbar" title="Akbar">Akbar</a> founds <a href="/wiki/Fatehpur_Sikri" title="Fatehpur Sikri">Fatehpur Sikri</a> to honor the Muslim holy man <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shaikh_Salim_Chisti" title="Shaikh Salim Chisti">Shaikh Salim Chisti</a>, who has foretold the birth of Akbar's son and heir, <a href="/wiki/Jahangir" title="Jahangir">Jahangir</a>.</li>
</ul>
