<p><b>Alexander Dennis</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (formerly known as <b>TransBus International</b>) is a British <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bus_building" title="Bus building">bus building</a> company based in Scotland. The largest bus and coach manufacturer in the United Kingdom, Alexander Dennis is one of the world’s fastest-growing bus builders, with manufacturing plants and partnerships in Canada, China, Europe, Malaysia, New Zealand, South Africa and the United States.</p>
<p>In early 2016, Alexander Dennis had a 44% market share in the United Kingdom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> As at April 2014, <a href="/wiki/Brian_Souter" title="Brian Souter">Brian Souter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ann_Gloag" title="Ann Gloag">Ann Gloag</a> collectively held a 55% shareholding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>Alexander Dennis was formed as TransBus International on 1 January 2001,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> when <a href="/wiki/Mayflower_Corporation" title="Mayflower Corporation">Mayflower Corporation</a> (owner of <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Specialist_Vehicles" title="Dennis Specialist Vehicles">Dennis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Walter_Alexander_Coachbuilders" title="Walter Alexander Coachbuilders">Alexander</a>) merged with <a href="/wiki/Henlys_Group" title="Henlys Group">Henlys Group</a> (owner of <a href="/wiki/Plaxton" title="Plaxton">Plaxton</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>TransBus International inherited a number of factories around the United Kingdom from all three merged companies: the former Alexander factories in <a href="/wiki/Falkirk" title="Falkirk">Falkirk</a>, Scotland and <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">Belfast</a>, Northern Ireland, the former Plaxton factories in <a href="/wiki/Anston" title="Anston">Anston</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scarborough,_North_Yorkshire" title="Scarborough, North Yorkshire">Scarborough</a>, the former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Counties_Motor_%26_Engineering_Company" title="Northern Counties Motor &amp; Engineering Company">Northern Counties</a> factory in <a href="/wiki/Wigan" title="Wigan">Wigan</a>, and the Dennis factory in <a href="/wiki/Guildford" title="Guildford">Guildford</a>.</p>
<p>TransBus International produced a range of both bus and coach chassis and bodies as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fire_apparatus" title="Fire apparatus">fire engines</a>. Included among its range of chassis were the <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Dart" title="Dennis Dart">Dennis Dart</a>, one of the all-time best-selling buses in the UK, and the <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Trident_2" title="Dennis Trident 2">Dennis Trident</a>. TransBus also produced <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Trident_3" title="Dennis Trident 3">export variants</a> for service in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> and other locations. The <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Trident_2" title="Dennis Trident 2">Dennis Trident</a> is the most common bus model in service in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>.</p>
<p>Since the creation of TransBus merged both Plaxton and Alexander, the TransBus range included Plaxton coaches as well as two double-decker bodies (the <a href="/wiki/Plaxton_President" title="Plaxton President">Plaxton President</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Alexander_ALX400" title="Alexander ALX400">Alexander ALX400</a>), the <a href="/wiki/Alexander_ALX300" title="Alexander ALX300">Alexander ALX300</a> citybus and the <a href="/wiki/Plaxton_Pointer" title="Plaxton Pointer">Pointer</a> body built in quantity on the aforementioned Dennis Dart chassis, which replaced the <a href="/wiki/Alexander_ALX200" title="Alexander ALX200">Alexander ALX200</a> body built previously on a Dart chassis.</p>
<p>On 31 March 2004, TransBus International was put into administration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On 17 May 2004, the Plaxton coach business was sold in a <a href="/wiki/Management_buyout" title="Management buyout">management buyout</a> and resumed trading as Plaxton.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> TransBus had been in the process of eradicating the traditional company names from the vehicles. On 21 May 2004, the remaining part of TransBus (Alexander and Dennis) was bought by a consortium of merchant bank Noble Grossart and businessmen <a href="/wiki/David_Murray_(Scottish_businessman)" title="David Murray (Scottish businessman)">David Murray</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brian_Souter" title="Brian Souter">Brian Souter</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The new company was named Alexander Dennis. The sale did not include the former Alexander Belfast plant, which closed.</p>
<p>On 26 January 2005, Alexander Dennis' Wigan plant closed, after completing outstanding orders of its <a href="/wiki/Plaxton_President" title="Plaxton President">President</a> body.</p>
<p>Alexander Dennis secured a number of major orders from UK operators. The company has also continued to find success in its predecessors' traditional markets of <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Dennis_Enviro500" title="Alexander Dennis Enviro500">Enviro500</a> securing orders in both markets, and the <a href="/wiki/Alexander_ALX400" title="Alexander ALX400">ALX400</a> double-deck body remaining the <a href="/wiki/Dublin_Bus" title="Dublin Bus">Dublin Bus</a> standard vehicle until production ended in 2006.</p>
<p>Alexander Dennis is also building its share of the <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North American</a> market, securing £25m worth of orders in 2005 for its Enviro500 model from customers in <a href="/wiki/Victoria,_British_Columbia" title="Victoria, British Columbia">Victoria, British Columbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> (open top models), <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Las_Vegas,_Nevada" title="Las Vegas, Nevada">Las Vegas</a>.</p>
<p>In 2006 the company unveiled two new models, the <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Dennis_Enviro400" title="Alexander Dennis Enviro400">Enviro400</a> double-decker and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexander_Dennis_Enviro200_Dart" title="Alexander Dennis Enviro200 Dart">Enviro200 Dart</a> midibus.</p>
<p>In May 2007, Alexander Dennis purchased Plaxton, thus reuniting the two former TransBus businesses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2012, <a href="/wiki/New_Flyer_Industries" title="New Flyer Industries">New Flyer Industries</a> and Alexander Dennis announced a new joint-venture to design and manufacture medium-duty low-floor bus (or midi bus) for the North American market. New Flyer will handle production and marketing, and Alexander Dennis will handle the engineering and testing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 7 June 2012, Alexander Dennis acquired Australian bodybuilder <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Custom_Coaches" title="Custom Coaches">Custom Coaches</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> However, in May 2014, Custom Coaches was placed into administration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In August 2014, Custom Coaches was sold to its former management.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
