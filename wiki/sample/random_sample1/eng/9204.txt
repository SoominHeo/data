<p><b>Alexander Stewart</b> (1214–1283), also known as <b>Alexander of Dundonald</b>, was 4th hereditary <a href="/wiki/High_Steward_of_Scotland" title="High Steward of Scotland">High Steward of Scotland</a> from his father's death in 1246.</p>
<p>A son of <a href="/wiki/Walter_Stewart,_3rd_High_Steward_of_Scotland" title="Walter Stewart, 3rd High Steward of Scotland">Walter Stewart, 3rd High Steward of Scotland</a> by his wife Bethóc, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Gille_Cr%C3%ADst,_Earl_of_Angus" title="Gille Críst, Earl of Angus">Gille Críst, Earl of Angus</a>, Alexander is said to have accompanied <a href="/wiki/Louis_IX_of_France" title="Louis IX of France">Louis IX of France</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Seventh_Crusade" title="Seventh Crusade">Seventh Crusade</a> (1248–1254).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1255 he was one of the councillors of King <a href="/wiki/Alexander_III_of_Scotland" title="Alexander III of Scotland">Alexander III</a>, though under age.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was the principal commander under King <a href="/wiki/Alexander_III_of_Scotland" title="Alexander III of Scotland">Alexander III of Scotland</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Largs" title="Battle of Largs">Battle of Largs</a>, on 2 October 1263, when the Scots defeated the Norwegians under <a href="/wiki/Haakon_IV_of_Norway" title="Haakon IV of Norway">Haakon IV</a>. The Scots invaded and conquered the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a> the following year, which was, with the whole of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Isles" title="Western Isles">Western Isles</a>, then annexed to the Crown of Scotland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_and_children">Marriage and children</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexander_Stewart,_4th_High_Steward_of_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Marriage and children">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The identity of Alexander's wife is uncertain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Some secondary sources erroneously<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> identify her as Jean, daughter of James, son of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aonghas_mac_Somhairle" title="Aonghas mac Somhairle">Angus</a>, son of <a href="/wiki/Somerled" title="Somerled">Somerled</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Simpson_.281713.29_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Simpson_.281713.29-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mackenzie_.281935.29_p.12._9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mackenzie_.281935.29_p.12.-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/James_Stewart,_5th_High_Steward_of_Scotland" title="James Stewart, 5th High Steward of Scotland">James Stewart, 5th High Steward of Scotland</a> (c. 1243-1309)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Stewart_of_Bonkyll_(d.1298)" title="John Stewart of Bonkyll (d.1298)">Sir John Stewart of Bonkill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Berwickshire" title="Berwickshire">Berwickshire</a> (c. 1245-22 July 1298), described as the "second son" who married the Bonkill heiress, had seven sons and one daughter, and was killed in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Falkirk_(1298)" title="Battle of Falkirk (1298)">Battle of Falkirk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Agnatic ancestor of British kings.</li>
<li>Andrew Stewart (a.k.a. Andrew Steward) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Esq." title="Esq.">Esq.</a> (c. 1245),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-10">[10]</a></sup> third son of Alexander Stewart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Married the daughter of James Bethe. Father of Sir Alexander 'the fierce' Steward and direct ancestor of <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Cromwell" title="Oliver Cromwell">Oliver Cromwell</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Great uncle of King <a href="/wiki/Robert_II_of_Scotland" title="Robert II of Scotland">Robert II</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li>Elizabeth Stewart, (c. 1248, d. before 1288) Married <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_William_Douglas_the_Hardy" title="Sir William Douglas the Hardy">Sir William Douglas the Hardy</a>, Governor of <a href="/wiki/Berwick-upon-Tweed" title="Berwick-upon-Tweed">Berwick-upon-Tweed</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Simpson_.281713.29_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Simpson_.281713.29-7">[7]</a></sup> She was the mother of the <a href="/wiki/James_Douglas,_Lord_of_Douglas" title="James Douglas, Lord of Douglas">Good Sir James Douglas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mackenzie_.281935.29_p.12._9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mackenzie_.281935.29_p.12.-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hawise_Stewart&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hawise Stewart (page does not exist)">Hawise Stewart</a> (c. 1262) Married the brother of the <a href="/wiki/Lord_of_Liddesdale" title="Lord of Liddesdale">Lord of Liddesdale</a>, Sir <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_de_Soulis" title="John de Soulis">John de Soulis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Had female issue, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Muriel_de_Soulis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Muriel de Soulis (page does not exist)">Muriel de Soulis</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>Through their eldest son James they were great-grandparents of King <a href="/wiki/Robert_II_of_Scotland" title="Robert II of Scotland">Robert II</a>, the first Stewart to be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_monarchs_of_Scotland" title="List of monarchs of Scotland">King of Scots</a>, and thus ancestors of all subsequent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_monarchs_of_Scotland" title="List of monarchs of Scotland">Scottish monarchs</a> and the later and current <a href="/wiki/List_of_British_monarchs" title="List of British monarchs">monarchs of Great Britain</a>.</p>
<p>Through their second son John, they were the direct ancestors in the male line of <a href="/wiki/Henry_Stuart,_Lord_Darnley" title="Henry Stuart, Lord Darnley">Henry Stuart, Lord Darnley</a>, and of the Stuart monarchs of Scotland and England from Darnley's son <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_I_of_England" title="James I of England">James VI and I</a> onwards.</p>
