<p><b>Aleksandr Panayotov Aleksandrov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a>: <span lang="bg">Александър Панайотов Александров</span>) (born December 1, 1951, <a href="/wiki/Omurtag_(town)" title="Omurtag (town)">Omurtag</a>, <a href="/wiki/Targovishte_Province" title="Targovishte Province">Targovishte Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>) is a retired <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgarian</a> cosmonaut. He is the second Bulgarian to have flown to space, behind <a href="/wiki/Georgi_Ivanov" title="Georgi Ivanov">Georgi Ivanov</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sf_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-sf-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-astronautix_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-astronautix-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleksandr_Panayotov_Aleksandrov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aleksandrov was born in Omurtag, Bulgaria on December 1, 1951. He graduated from the <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_Air_Force" title="Bulgarian Air Force">Bulgarian Air Force</a> Academy in 1974 and obtained a degree in technical sciences in 1983. In the Bulgarian Air Force, Aleksandrov rose to the rank of Lieutenant Colonel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-astronautix_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-astronautix-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Aleksandrov was selected as a Research Cosmonaut on March 1, 1978 as part of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intercosmos" title="Intercosmos">Intercosmos</a> program. The selection featured six semifinalists, including the parachute jump record holder, <a href="/wiki/Chavdar_Djurov" title="Chavdar Djurov">Chavdar Djurov</a>, who was killed during the selection process.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ke_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ke-3">[3]</a></sup> Aleksandrov was selected as backup to <a href="/wiki/Georgi_Ivanov" title="Georgi Ivanov">Georgi Ivanov</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_33" title="Soyuz 33">Soyuz 33</a> mission to the <a href="/wiki/Salyut_6" title="Salyut 6">Salyut 6</a> space station. Subsequently, Aleksandrov was assigned to the prime crew of the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TM-5" title="Soyuz TM-5">Soyuz TM-5</a> mission to the <a href="/wiki/Mir" title="Mir">Mir</a> space station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sf_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-sf-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-astronautix_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-astronautix-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>On June 7, 1988, Aleksandrov launched aboard TM-5 as a Research Cosmonaut along with mission commander <a href="/wiki/Anatoly_Solovyev" title="Anatoly Solovyev">Anatoly Solovyev</a> and <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Savinykh" title="Viktor Savinykh">Viktor Savinykh</a>. Upon arriving at Mir, Aleksandrov became the first Bulgarian to reach a <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> space station, as the Soyuz 33 mission carrying Georgi Ivanov failed to reach the <a href="/wiki/Salyut_6" title="Salyut 6">Salyut 6</a> space station. On June 17, Aleksandrov returned aboard <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TM-4" title="Soyuz TM-4">Soyuz TM-4</a> along with his fellow crew members. He, along with his crew-mates, spent just under 10 days in space.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-astronautix2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-astronautix2-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Aleksandrov later became Deputy Director of the Institute of Space Research, <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_Academy_of_Sciences" title="Bulgarian Academy of Sciences">Bulgarian Academy of Sciences</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-astronautix_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-astronautix-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Currently Aleksandrov works as a research scientist. He is married and has one child.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-astronautix_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-astronautix-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
