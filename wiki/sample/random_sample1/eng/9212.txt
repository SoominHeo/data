<p><b>Alexandra Elliott "Ali" Wentworth</b> (born January 12, 1965) is an American actress, <a href="/wiki/Comedian" title="Comedian">comedian</a>, author, and producer.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexandra_Wentworth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wentworth was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C" title="Washington, D.C">Washington, D.C</a>. to a prominent family. Her mother, <a href="/wiki/Muffie_Cabot" title="Muffie Cabot">Muffie Cabot</a> (née Mabel Brandon Hobart), was <a href="/wiki/Nancy_Reagan" title="Nancy Reagan">Nancy Reagan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/White_House_Social_Secretary" title="White House Social Secretary">White House Social Secretary</a> from 1981 to 1983.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Her father, Eric Wentworth, was a reporter for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Washington_Post" title="The Washington Post">The Washington Post</a>.</i> Her stepfather, Henry Brandon, was the editor of the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sunday_Times" title="The Sunday Times">The Sunday Times</a></i> in London, and her maternal grandmother was explorer <a href="/wiki/Janet_Elliott_Wulsin" title="Janet Elliott Wulsin">Janet Elliott Wulsin</a> (1894–1963).</p>
<p>Wentworth attended the <a href="/wiki/Dana_Hall_School" title="Dana Hall School">Dana Hall School</a> for Girls in <a href="/wiki/Wellesley,_Massachusetts" title="Wellesley, Massachusetts">Wellesley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, and graduated from <a href="/wiki/Bard_College" title="Bard College">Bard College</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Annandale-on-Hudson" title="Annandale-on-Hudson">Annandale-on-Hudson</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> in 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexandra_Wentworth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After Wentworth debuted, she was a cast member on the Fox <a href="/wiki/Sketch_comedy" title="Sketch comedy">sketch comedy</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/In_Living_Color" title="In Living Color">In Living Color</a></i> from 1992 to 1994, where she was known for impressions of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cher_(singer)" title="Cher (singer)">Cher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amy_Fisher" title="Amy Fisher">Amy Fisher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Clinton" title="Hillary Clinton">Hillary Clinton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diana,_Princess_of_Wales" title="Diana, Princess of Wales">Princess Diana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brooke_Shields" title="Brooke Shields">Brooke Shields</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Marie_Presley" title="Lisa Marie Presley">Lisa Marie Presley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sharon_Stone" title="Sharon Stone">Sharon Stone</a>, and other characters. Her recurring characters included Candy Cane, a deranged kids show host who had had a string of bad relationships with her male coworkers, and the promiscuous teenage daughter of Grandpa Jack McGee (<a href="/wiki/Jim_Carrey" title="Jim Carrey">Jim Carrey</a>) on <i>The Dysfunctional Home Show</i>.</p>
<p>Wentworth made correspondent appearances on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_with_Jay_Leno" title="The Tonight Show with Jay Leno">The Tonight Show with Jay Leno</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Oprah_Winfrey_Show" title="The Oprah Winfrey Show">The Oprah Winfrey Show</a>.</i> In 1995, she played <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Seinfeld_(character)" title="Jerry Seinfeld (character)">Jerry Seinfeld's</a> girlfriend, Sheila ("Schmoopie"), in the memorable "<a href="/wiki/The_Soup_Nazi" title="The Soup Nazi">Soup Nazi</a>" episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Seinfeld" title="Seinfeld">Seinfeld</a>.</i> She had a recurring role as the boss of the title character on the <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Bros.</a> (WB) series <i><a href="/wiki/Felicity_(TV_series)" title="Felicity (TV series)">Felicity</a>.</i> In 2003, she co-hosted the syndicated talk show, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Living_It_Up!_With_Ali_%26_Jack" title="Living It Up! With Ali &amp; Jack">Living It Up! With Ali &amp; Jack</a>,</i> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jack_Ford_(news_anchor)" title="Jack Ford (news anchor)">Jack Ford</a>.</p>
<p>Wentworth's most notable film appearances include <i><a href="/wiki/Jerry_Maguire" title="Jerry Maguire">Jerry Maguire</a>,</i> <i><a href="/wiki/Office_Space" title="Office Space">Office Space</a>,</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_Complicated_(film)" title="It's Complicated (film)">It's Complicated</a>.</i></p>
<p>Wentworth starred in the comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Head_Case" title="Head Case">Head Case</a></i> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Starz_(TV_channel)" title="Starz (TV channel)">Starz</a> TV network.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She guest starred on the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Marriage_Ref_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Marriage Ref (U.S. TV series)">The Marriage Ref</a>.</i></p>
<p>Wentworth hosts <i>Daily Shot,</i> a short daily talk show segment on <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo!</a> Shine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of 2016, Wentworth created and stars in <a href="/wiki/Nightcap_(2016_TV_series)" title="Nightcap (2016 TV series)">Nightcap</a> as the lead character Staci Cole.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexandra_Wentworth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wentworth is married to <a href="/wiki/George_Stephanopoulos" title="George Stephanopoulos">George Stephanopoulos</a>, <a href="/wiki/ABC_News" title="ABC News">ABC News</a> Chief Anchor correspondent and former Political Adviser to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clinton_Administration" title="Clinton Administration">Clinton Administration</a>. They met on a <a href="/wiki/Blind_date" title="Blind date">blind date</a> in April 2001, were engaged two months later, and were married on November 20, 2001, at the <a href="/wiki/Archdiocesan_Cathedral_of_the_Holy_Trinity" title="Archdiocesan Cathedral of the Holy Trinity">Archdiocesan Cathedral of the Holy Trinity</a> on New York City's Upper East Side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> They have two daughters: Elliott Anastasia Stephanopoulos (born September 9, 2002) and Harper Andrea Stephanopoulos (born June 2, 2005).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
