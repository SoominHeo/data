<p><b>Aleksandra Andreyevna Fedoriva</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Александра Андреевна Федорива</span>) (born 13 September 1988 in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> <a href="/wiki/Track_and_field" title="Track and field">track and field</a> athlete who competes mainly in <a href="/wiki/Sprint_(running)" title="Sprint (running)">sprinting</a> events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beijing_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-beijing-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Fedoriva began her career as a <a href="/wiki/Hurdling" title="Hurdling">hurdles</a> specialist. She was a semi-finalist in the <a href="/wiki/100_metres_hurdles" title="100 metres hurdles">100 metres hurdles</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2005_World_Youth_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2005 World Youth Championships in Athletics">2005 World Youth Championships in Athletics</a>, took fourth at the <a href="/wiki/2006_World_Junior_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2006 World Junior Championships in Athletics">2006 World Junior Championships in Athletics</a>, then won the <a href="/wiki/Gold_medal" title="Gold medal">gold medal</a> in the event at the <a href="/wiki/2007_European_Athletics_Junior_Championships" title="2007 European Athletics Junior Championships">2007 European Athletics Junior Championships</a> the following year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAAFBio_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-IAAFBio-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2007 she ran a Russian junior record in the <a href="/wiki/60_metres_hurdles" title="60 metres hurdles">60 metres hurdles</a>, completing the distance in 8.22 seconds. This mark stood for five years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2012RIC_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-2012RIC-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Fedoriva represented Russia at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> competing at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/4x100_metres_relay" title="4x100 metres relay">4x100 metres relay</a>, together with <a href="/wiki/Yuliya_Chermoshanskaya" title="Yuliya Chermoshanskaya">Yuliya Chermoshanskaya</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yulia_Gushchina" title="Yulia Gushchina">Yulia Gushchina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yevgeniya_Polyakova" title="Yevgeniya Polyakova">Yevgeniya Polyakova</a>. In their first round heat they placed second behind <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a> with a time of 42.87 seconds (the second fastest time in qualifying). In the final they finished in 42.31 seconds, taking first place and the gold medal, while the Jamaicans failed to finish due to a bad baton exchange.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beijing_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-beijing-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/2009_European_Athletics_U23_Championships" title="2009 European Athletics U23 Championships">2009 European Athletics U23 Championships</a> saw Fedoriva move into longer distances as she won the women's <a href="/wiki/200_metres" title="200 metres">200 metres</a> title.</p>
<p>Fedoriva was a semi-finalist in the 60 m hurdles at the <a href="/wiki/2010_IAAF_World_Indoor_Championships" title="2010 IAAF World Indoor Championships">2010 IAAF World Indoor Championships</a>, but found greater success over longer sprint distances in the outdoor season: she was a bronze medallist in the 200 m at the <a href="/wiki/2010_European_Athletics_Championships" title="2010 European Athletics Championships">2010 European Athletics Championships</a> and took the gold medal for Europe in that event at the <a href="/wiki/2010_IAAF_Continental_Cup" title="2010 IAAF Continental Cup">2010 IAAF Continental Cup</a>. The following year she took bronze over 100 metres at the <a href="/wiki/2011_European_Team_Championships" title="2011 European Team Championships">2011 European Team Championships</a> and reached the semi-finals over that distance at the <a href="/wiki/2011_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2011 World Championships in Athletics">2011 World Championships in Athletics</a>. She anchored the women's relay team to fifth place at that competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAAFBio_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-IAAFBio-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>She made her indoor debut over <a href="/wiki/400_metres" title="400 metres">400 metres</a> at the 2012 Russian Championships and won the hotly contested final in a time of 51.18 seconds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2012RIC_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-2012RIC-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a>, Fedoriva came in third in the 200m semifinal, meaning that she could not advance to the final.</p>
