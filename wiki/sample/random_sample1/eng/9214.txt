<p><b>Alexander II of Macedon</b> (<a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Greek</a>: Ἀλέξανδρος Β΄) was king of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macedon" title="Macedon">Macedon</a> in 371–369 BC, following the death of his father <a href="/wiki/Amyntas_III_of_Macedon" title="Amyntas III of Macedon">Amyntas III</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexander_II_of_Macedon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was the eldest of the three sons of king Amyntas and <a href="/wiki/Eurydice_I_of_Macedon" title="Eurydice I of Macedon">Queen Eurydice I</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reign">Reign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexander_II_of_Macedon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although he had already attained his majority, Alexander was very young when he ascended to the throne in 371 BC. This caused immediate problems for the new king as enemies to the dynasty resumed war. Alexander simultaneously faced an <a href="/wiki/Illyria" title="Illyria">Illyrian</a> invasion from the north-west and an attack from the east by the pretender <a href="/wiki/Pausanias_(pretender)" title="Pausanias (pretender)">Pausanias</a>. Pausanias quickly captured several cities and threatened the queen mother, who was at the palace in <a href="/wiki/Pella" title="Pella">Pella</a> with her young sons. Alexander defeated his enemies with the help of the <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athenian</a> general <a href="/wiki/Iphicrates" title="Iphicrates">Iphicrates</a>, who had been sailing along the Macedonian coast on the way to recapture <a href="/wiki/Amphipolis" title="Amphipolis">Amphipolis</a>.</p>
<p>At the request of the Thessalian <a href="/wiki/Aleuadae" title="Aleuadae">Aleuadae</a>, Alexander intervened in a civil war in <a href="/wiki/Thessaly" title="Thessaly">Thessaly</a>. He successfully gained control of <a href="/wiki/Larissa" title="Larissa">Larissa</a> and several other cities but, betraying a promise he had made, put garrisons in them. This provoked a hostile reaction from <a href="/wiki/Thebes,_Greece" title="Thebes, Greece">Thebes</a>, the leading military power in <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">Greece</a> at the time. The Theban general <a href="/wiki/Pelopidas" title="Pelopidas">Pelopidas</a> drove the Macedonians from Thessaly. He then neutralized Alexander by favoring the ambitions of Alexander's brother-in-law <a href="/wiki/Ptolemy_of_Aloros" title="Ptolemy of Aloros">Ptolemy of Aloros</a>, and forced Alexander to abandon his alliance with Athens in favor of Thebes. As part of this new alliance, Alexander had to hand over hostages, including his younger brother <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Macedon" title="Philip II of Macedon">Philip</a>.</p>
<p>Alexander was assassinated during a festival at the instigation of <a href="/wiki/Ptolemy_of_Aloros" title="Ptolemy of Aloros">Ptolemy</a>. Although Alexander's brother <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perdiccas_III" title="Perdiccas III">Perdiccas III</a> became the next king, he was under age, and Ptolemy became regent.</p>
