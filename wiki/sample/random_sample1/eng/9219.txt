<p><b>Alexandr Vasilievich Kolobnev</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Александр Васильевич Колобнев</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> born 4 May 1981) is a <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> professional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Road_bicycle_racer" title="Road bicycle racer">road bicycle racer</a>, who rides on the <a href="/wiki/UCI_World_Tour" title="UCI World Tour">UCI World Tour</a> with <a href="/wiki/Katusha%E2%80%93Alpecin" title="Katusha–Alpecin">Team Katusha–Alpecin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kolobnev_return_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kolobnev_return-3">[3]</a></sup> His major victories include winning the 2007 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monte_Paschi_Eroica" title="Monte Paschi Eroica">Monte Paschi Eroica</a>, a stage of <a href="/wiki/2007_Paris%E2%80%93Nice" title="2007 Paris–Nice">Paris–Nice</a> and he is a two-time Russian National road Champion. In 2011, he was provisionally suspended after testing positive for a potential drug masking agent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kolobnev_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kolobnev-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He was cleared of intentional doping by the Court of Arbitration for Sport in February 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and returned to Team Katusha in March 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kolobnev_return_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Kolobnev_return-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexandr_Kolobnev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Vyksa" title="Vyksa">Vyksa</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_SFSR" title="Russian SFSR">Russian SFSR</a>, Kolobnev spent his neo-pro year in 2002 with <a href="/wiki/Cantina_Tollo" title="Cantina Tollo">Acqua e Sapone–Cantina Tollo</a>. His second season saw him sign with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Domina_Vacanze" title="Domina Vacanze">Domina Vacanze</a>. There, he took his first victory in Stage 2 of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Settimana_Ciclistica_Coppi-Bartali" title="Settimana Ciclistica Coppi-Bartali">Settimana Ciclistica Coppi-Bartali</a>. He stayed with Domina Vacanze through the 2004 season and signed with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UCI_ProTeam" title="UCI ProTeam">UCI ProTeam</a> <a href="/wiki/Team_LottoNL%E2%80%93Jumbo" title="Team LottoNL–Jumbo">Rabobank</a>. With Rabobank he managed to capture Stage 1 of the <a href="/wiki/Volta_a_la_Comunitat_Valenciana" title="Volta a la Comunitat Valenciana">Volta a la Comunitat Valenciana</a> in 2006.</p>
<p>In 2007 he transferred to <a href="/wiki/Tinkoff" title="Tinkoff">Team CSC</a>. He won the third stage of <a href="/wiki/Paris%E2%80%93Nice" title="Paris–Nice">Paris–Nice</a>, became the inaugural winner of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Strade_Bianche_-_Eroica_Pro" title="Strade Bianche - Eroica Pro">Monte Paschi Eroica</a>, now known as the <i>Strade Bianche</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and finished second at the <a href="/wiki/UCI_Road_World_Championships" title="UCI Road World Championships">UCI Road World Championships</a>. Kolobnev finished fourth in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cycling_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_road_race" title="Cycling at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's road race">road race at the 2008 Olympic Games</a>. Second-place finisher and silver medalist <a href="/wiki/Davide_Rebellin" title="Davide Rebellin">Davide Rebellin</a> later tested positive for <a href="/wiki/Continuous_erythropoietin_receptor_activator" title="Continuous erythropoietin receptor activator">Continuous erythropoietin receptor activator</a>, causing the IOC to strip him of his silver medal. Though the UCI considers Kolobnev promoted to third in the race's standings, he has not, as of January 2010, been awarded a bronze medal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>He lives in <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9nia" title="Dénia">Dénia</a>, Spain, with his wife Daria and two sons, David and Alexander.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2011_Tour_de_France" title="2011 Tour de France">2011 Tour de France</a> he tested positive for the diuretic <a href="/wiki/Hydrochlorothiazide" title="Hydrochlorothiazide">hydrochlorothiazide</a>, a masking agent that can hide the presence of performance-enhancing drugs. He was subsequently pulled from the tour by his team,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and his results for that stage were annulled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ucidec_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ucidec-11">[11]</a></sup> Despite his B sample also testing positive he only received a fine from the Russian cycling federation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> However this was later appealed by the UCI to CAS, although he was subsequently cleared of any charges on 29 February 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Kolobnev rejoined <a href="/wiki/Katusha%E2%80%93Alpecin" title="Katusha–Alpecin">Team Katusha</a> in March 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kolobnev_return_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Kolobnev_return-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, the <a href="/wiki/Union_Cycliste_Internationale" title="Union Cycliste Internationale">Union Cycliste Internationale</a> initiated an investigation of Kolobnev and the <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakh</a> rider <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Vinokourov" title="Alexander Vinokourov">Alexander Vinokourov</a> over allegations brought by the Swiss news magazine <i>L’lllustre</i> and Italian newspaper <a href="/wiki/Corriere_della_Sera" title="Corriere della Sera">Corriere della Sera</a>. They accused Vinokourov of cutting a deal with Kolobnev in 2010 to aid him (Vinokourov) in winning <a href="/wiki/Li%C3%A8ge%E2%80%93Bastogne%E2%80%93Li%C3%A8ge" title="Liège–Bastogne–Liège">Liège–Bastogne–Liège</a>. They alleged 150 thousand euros exchanged hands. The UCI's investigation is still pending.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
