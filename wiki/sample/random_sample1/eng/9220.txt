<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Aleksandr Potashov</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Aleksandr Potashov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Belarusian_language" title="Belarusian language">Belarusian</a>: <span lang="be" xml:lang="be">Аляксандар Паташоў</span>, Russian: <i>Александр Поташёв</i>; born March 12, 1962 in <a href="/wiki/Vitebsk" title="Vitebsk">Vitebsk</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belarusian_SSR" title="Belarusian SSR">Belarusian SSR</a>) is a retired <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_walking" title="Race walking">race walker</a> who represented the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSR" title="USSR">USSR</a> and later <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a>. He won the gold medal over 50 kilometres at the <a href="/wiki/1991_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1991 World Championships in Athletics">1991 World Championships</a>. Potashov and his teammate <a href="/wiki/Andrey_Perlov" title="Andrey Perlov">Andrey Perlov</a> attempted to cross the goal line simultaneously, resulting in a shared gold medal, but the officials declared Potashov the winner by 0.01 second.</p>
<p>Potashov finished fourth at the <a href="/wiki/1988_Summer_Olympics" title="1988 Summer Olympics">1988 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a> and was disqualified at the 1992 Olympics. His personal best time is 3:40:02, achieved in May 1990.</p>
