<p><b>Alexander Gennadyevich Khloponin</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Алекса́ндр Генна́дьевич Хлопо́нин</span>) was born on March 6, 1965 in <a href="/wiki/Colombo" title="Colombo">Colombo</a>, Ceylon (now <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>). Khloponin was the <a href="/wiki/Governor" title="Governor">governor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Krasnoyarsk_Krai" title="Krasnoyarsk Krai">Krasnoyarsk Krai</a> in <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>.</p>
<p>Khloponin has a finance degree from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Finance_University_under_the_Government_of_the_Russian_Federation" title="Finance University under the Government of the Russian Federation">Finance Academy</a> (famous <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> university located in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>) and was chairman of the board of the <a href="/wiki/Norilsk_Nickel" title="Norilsk Nickel">Norilsk Nickel</a> company. In 2001, he became the governor of <a href="/wiki/Taymyr_Autonomous_Okrug" title="Taymyr Autonomous Okrug">Taymyr Autonomous Okrug</a> in northern Siberia, holding that position until 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Khloponin won the election for governor of Krasnoyarsk Krai against Alexander Uss, another man with major business links. The race was relatively close, with Khloponin winning only 48% of the vote even in the runoff.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2002 Alexander Khloponin was named <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Person_of_the_Year_(Expert_magazine)" title="Person of the Year (Expert magazine)">Person of the Year</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Expert_magazine" title="Expert magazine">Expert magazine</a>, the Russian business weekly.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_President" title="Russian President">Russian President</a> <a href="/wiki/Dmitry_Medvedev" title="Dmitry Medvedev">Dmitry Medvedev</a> in January 2010 the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/North_Caucasian_Federal_District" title="North Caucasian Federal District">North Caucasian Federal District</a> (from <a href="/wiki/Southern_Federal_District" title="Southern Federal District">Southern Federal District</a>) and appointed Alexander Khloponin Vice-Premier of the Russian Federation and Plenipotentiary Envoy of the President to the new federal district while relieving him from the governor's office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ukaz-khlo_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-ukaz-khlo-5">[5]</a></sup> President <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a> on May 12 replaced Khloponin as envoy with the interior ministry's Lieut Gen Sergey Melikov<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
