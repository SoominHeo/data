<p><b>Alexe</b> (or <b>Alexei</b>) <b>Mateevici</b> (March 27, 1888 – August 24, 1917) was one of the most prominent Romanian poets in <a href="/wiki/Bessarabia" title="Bessarabia">Bessarabia</a>.</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexei_Mateevici&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was born in the town <a href="/wiki/C%C4%83inari" title="Căinari">Căinari</a>, in Eastern <a href="/wiki/Bessarabia" title="Bessarabia">Bessarabia</a>, which was part of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a>, now in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Moldova" title="Republic of Moldova">Republic of Moldova</a>. He studied at the theological school of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chi%C5%9Fin%C4%83u" title="Chişinău">Chişinău</a>, and published his first poems (<i>Ţăranii</i> (<i>Peasants</i>), <i>Eu cânt</i> (<i>I sing</i>), <i>Ţara</i> (<i>The Country</i>)) in the newspaper <i>Basarabia</i>, where he also published two articles on Moldavian folklore. Mateevici later published several articles on religion in Moldavia.</p>
<p>Mateevici went on to study at the <a href="/wiki/National_University_of_Kyiv-Mohyla_Academy" title="National University of Kyiv-Mohyla Academy">Theological Academy</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kiev" title="Kiev">Kiev</a>, from which he graduated in 1914. In that year he married Teodora Borisovna Novitski. He returned to Chişinău, and became a <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek language</a> teacher at the theological school.</p>
