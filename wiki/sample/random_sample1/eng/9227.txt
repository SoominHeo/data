<p><b>Alexei Yevgenyevich Urmanov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:Ru-Alexei_Yevgenyevich_Urmanov.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a> </span><a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c2/Ru-Alexei_Yevgenyevich_Urmanov.ogg" title="Ru-Alexei Yevgenyevich Urmanov.ogg"><span class="unicode" lang="ru" style="white-space: normal;">Алексей Евгеньевич Урманов​</span></a></span> <small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:Ru-Alexei_Yevgenyevich_Urmanov.ogg" title="File:Ru-Alexei Yevgenyevich Urmanov.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span>; born 17 November 1973) is a Russian <a href="/wiki/Figure_skating" title="Figure skating">figure skating</a> coach and former competitor. He is the <a href="/wiki/Figure_skating_at_the_1994_Winter_Olympics" title="Figure skating at the 1994 Winter Olympics">1994 Olympic</a> champion, the <a href="/wiki/1993_World_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="1993 World Figure Skating Championships">1993 World</a> bronze medalist, the <a href="/wiki/1997_European_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="1997 European Figure Skating Championships">1997 European</a> champion, the <a href="/wiki/1995%E2%80%9396_Champions_Series_Final" title="1995–96 Champions Series Final">1995–96 Champions Series Final</a> champion, a four-time <a href="/wiki/Russian_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="Russian Figure Skating Championships">Russian national</a> champion, and the 1992 <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="Soviet Figure Skating Championships">Soviet national</a> champion. He currently coaches <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Julia_Lipnitskaya" title="Julia Lipnitskaya">Julia Lipnitskaya</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexei_Urmanov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Urmanov was born on 17 November 1973 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leningrad" title="Leningrad">Leningrad</a>, Soviet Union.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISU-AU_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ISU-AU-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2001, his partner, Viktoria, gave birth to twins, Ivan and Andrei. The couple married in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neva040913_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Neva040913-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexei_Urmanov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Urmanov started skating in 1977.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISU-AU_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ISU-AU-1">[1]</a></sup> Early in his career, he was coached by N. Monakhova and Natalia Golubeva.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISU-AU_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-ISU-AU-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Competing for the Soviet Union, Urmanov won the silver medal at the <a href="/wiki/1990_World_Junior_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="1990 World Junior Figure Skating Championships">1990 World Junior Championships</a>. After the end of the Soviet Union, he chose to compete for Russia. In 1991, at age 17, he became the first skater to perform a quadruple jump, at the <a href="/wiki/1991_European_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="1991 European Figure Skating Championships">European Championships</a>.</p>
<p>Urmanov competed at the <a href="/wiki/Figure_skating_at_the_1992_Winter_Olympics" title="Figure skating at the 1992 Winter Olympics">1992 Winter Olympics</a>, where he placed 5th. He won the bronze medal at the <a href="/wiki/1993_World_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="1993 World Figure Skating Championships">1993 World Championships</a>. At the <a href="/wiki/Figure_skating_at_the_1994_Winter_Olympics" title="Figure skating at the 1994 Winter Olympics">1994 Winter Olympics</a> in Lillehammer, he won the gold medal.</p>
<p>Urmanov chose to remain in the competitive ranks. He became the <a href="/wiki/1997_European_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="1997 European Figure Skating Championships">1997 European champion</a>, but an injury forced him out of the <a href="/wiki/1997_World_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="1997 World Figure Skating Championships">1997 World Championships</a> after the short program and kept him from competing for a berth to the 1998 Olympics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SE040113_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-SE040113-3">[3]</a></sup> He retired from Olympic-eligible skating in 1999 and won the World Professional Championships the same year. Urmanov was coached by <a href="/wiki/Alexei_Mishin" title="Alexei Mishin">Alexei Mishin</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Yubileyny_Sports_Palace" title="Yubileyny Sports Palace">Yubileyny Sports Palace</a> in <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Saint Petersburg</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISU-AU_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-ISU-AU-1">[1]</a></sup> During the 1990s, the rink often had poor-quality ice and other problems, resulting in limited training time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SPT1994_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-SPT1994-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SPT1995_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-SPT1995-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Urmanov is an Honoured Masters of Sports of the Russian Federation. He works as a skating coach<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NI080402_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-NI080402-6">[6]</a></sup> and an <a href="/wiki/International_Skating_Union" title="International Skating Union">International Skating Union</a> technical specialist. His former students include <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Voronov_(figure_skater)" title="Sergei Voronov (figure skater)">Sergei Voronov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nodari_Maisuradze" title="Nodari Maisuradze">Nodari Maisuradze</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zhan_Bush" title="Zhan Bush">Zhan Bush</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gordei_Gorshkov" title="Gordei Gorshkov">Gordei Gorshkov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nikol_Gosviani" title="Nikol Gosviani">Nikol Gosviani</a>, <a href="/wiki/Polina_Agafonova" title="Polina Agafonova">Polina Agafonova</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Anastasiia_Gubanova" title="Anastasiia Gubanova">Anastasiia Gubanova</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AS130121_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-AS130121-7">[7]</a></sup> Urmanov currently coaches <a href="/wiki/Yulia_Lipnitskaya" title="Yulia Lipnitskaya">Yulia Lipnitskaya</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Deniss_Vasi%C4%BCjevs" title="Deniss Vasiļjevs">Deniss Vasiļjevs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He was based in <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Saint Petersburg</a> until 2014, when he moved to <a href="/wiki/Sochi" title="Sochi">Sochi</a>, to coach at the <a href="/wiki/Iceberg_Skating_Palace" title="Iceberg Skating Palace">Iceberg Skating Palace</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He sometimes holds summer camps or clinics in other locations such as <a href="/wiki/Lule%C3%A5" title="Luleå">Luleå</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IFS111110_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-IFS111110-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
