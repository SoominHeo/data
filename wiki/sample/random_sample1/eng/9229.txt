<p><b>Alexander Guerrero Perez</b> (born November 20, 1986) is a <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuban</a> <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Left_fielder" title="Left fielder">left fielder</a> and <a href="/wiki/Third_baseman" title="Third baseman">third baseman</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Chunichi_Dragons" title="Chunichi Dragons">Chunichi Dragons</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Professional_Baseball" title="Nippon Professional Baseball">Nippon Professional Baseball</a> (NPB). He defected from Cuba in 2013 to pursue a contract in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB). He signed with the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a> and made his MLB debut in 2014. He played primarily <a href="/wiki/Shortstop" title="Shortstop">shortstop</a> in Cuba, but has played <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_base" title="Third base">third base</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Left_field" title="Left field">left field</a> for the Dodgers, who released him in 2016.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Guerrero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuban_career">Cuban career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Guerrero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cuban career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As a member of Cuba's <a href="/wiki/Las_Tunas_(baseball)" title="Las Tunas (baseball)">Las Tunas</a> team in the <a href="/wiki/Cuban_National_Series" title="Cuban National Series">Cuban National Series</a>, Guerrero was named to the 2010-2011 All-Star team<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> as well as playing for the <a href="/wiki/Cuba_national_baseball_team" title="Cuba national baseball team">Cuba national baseball team</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Haarlem_Baseball_Week" title="2012 Haarlem Baseball Week">2012 Haarlem Baseball Week</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/2011_World_Port_Tournament" title="2011 World Port Tournament">2011 World Port Tournament</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He was part of the initial roster for the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Baseball_Classic" title="2013 World Baseball Classic">2013 World Baseball Classic</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> but did not make the final roster and reportedly sat out the regular season because he was upset at his snub.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U.S._career">U.S. career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Guerrero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: U.S. career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He defected from Cuba in 2013 and obtained residency in <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He began training in the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a> and on September 9, 2013, it was reported that he had been cleared by the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Foreign_Assets_Control" title="Office of Foreign Assets Control">Office of Foreign Assets Control</a> to sign with U.S. teams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Guerrero was rumored to be signing with the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a>, with early reports putting his deal in the seven-year, $32 million area. However, his initial agents turned out to not be licensed and he switched to <a href="/wiki/Scott_Boras" title="Scott Boras">Scott Boras</a>, who said the bidding was now wide open.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On October 21, 2013, it was reported that Guerrero had reached a new agreement with the Dodgers, that would be worth $28 million over 4 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The deal included a $10 million signing bonus and allows him to become a free agent after his age 30 season. The Dodgers officially announced his signing on October 22.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Guerrero made his professional debut with the <a href="/wiki/Gigantes_del_Cibao" title="Gigantes del Cibao">Gigantes del Cibao</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dominican_Winter_League" title="Dominican Winter League">Dominican Winter League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> However, he dealt with hamstring issues and was limited to only 12 games, during which he hit .289.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Los_Angeles_Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Guerrero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Los Angeles Dodgers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014">2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Guerrero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2014">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Guerrero made the Dodgers' 25-man opening day roster for the 2014 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He made his MLB debut as a pinch hitter on March 22, 2014 against the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Diamondbacks" title="Arizona Diamondbacks">Arizona Diamondbacks</a>, but when Diamondbacks manager <a href="/wiki/Kirk_Gibson" title="Kirk Gibson">Kirk Gibson</a> countered by putting in a right-handed pitcher, Dodgers manager <a href="/wiki/Don_Mattingly" title="Don Mattingly">Don Mattingly</a> sent in a left-hander to pinch hit for Guerrero.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Guerrero had his first official at-bat the next night, and struck out. After the two-game opening series in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sydney,_Australia" title="Sydney, Australia">Sydney, Australia</a>, Guerrero was optioned to the Triple-A <a href="/wiki/Albuquerque_Isotopes" title="Albuquerque Isotopes">Albuquerque Isotopes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<p>During a game against the <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_Bees" title="Salt Lake Bees">Salt Lake Bees</a> on May 20, 2014, Guerrero was involved in a dugout scuffle with Albuquerque teammate <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Olivo" title="Miguel Olivo">Miguel Olivo</a> in which Olivo bit off a portion of Guerrero's ear.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He had to undergo a complicated plastic surgery operation to reattach the ear, necessitating several days in the hospital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In 65 games for the Isotopes, he played 51 games at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_base" title="Second base">second base</a>, 9 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Left_field" title="Left field">left field</a>, 4 at <a href="/wiki/Shortstop" title="Shortstop">shortstop</a> and 1 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_base" title="Third base">third base</a>. He hit .329 with 15 homers and 49 RBI. The Dodgers recalled him when rosters expanded on September 1. He recorded his first Major League hit with a single to left field off of <a href="/wiki/Erik_Cordier" title="Erik Cordier">Erik Cordier</a> of the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a> on September 13.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> He appeared in 13 games with the Dodgers, primarily as a pinch hitter though he did play in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Left_field" title="Left field">left field</a> for a few innings. He had just the one hit in 13 at-bats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-br_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-br-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015">2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Guerrero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2015">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In spring training, Guerrero made it clear he intended to exercise an unusual clause in his contract that allowed him to refuse an assignment to the minor leagues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> As it happened, he hit so well during spring training that he convinced the Dodgers he belonged on the opening day roster, despite concerns about his defense.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>In his first start of the season, Guerrero had three hits in five at-bats. One of the hits was his first MLB home run, hit off of <a href="/wiki/%C3%93liver_P%C3%A9rez" title="Óliver Pérez">Óliver Pérez</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Diamondbacks" title="Arizona Diamondbacks">Arizona Diamondbacks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>After two weeks, Guerrero was hitting so well that there was clamor for him to become the regular third baseman, as incumbent <a href="/wiki/Juan_Uribe" title="Juan Uribe">Juan Uribe</a> was getting off to a slow start.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Uribe, however, picked up his production, and Guerrero continued to be used in a <a href="/wiki/Utility_player" title="Utility player">utility</a> role, playing at third base and at left field and <a href="/wiki/Pinch_hitter" title="Pinch hitter">pinch hitting</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-br_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-br-19">[19]</a></sup> Despite not being a regular starter, Guerrero was named <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_Rookie_of_the_Month_Award" title="Major League Baseball Rookie of the Month Award">Rookie of the Month</a> for April 2015, his first full month in the Major Leagues. He hit .423 with five homers and 13 RBI in only 13 games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>On June 2, with the Dodgers trailing the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rockies" title="Colorado Rockies">Colorado Rockies</a> by three runs and down to their last strike in the top of the 9th inning, Guerrero hit the first grand slam of his career just over the center field wall to drive in what would be the winning run for the Dodgers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>His production tailed off down the stretch but he wound up playing in 117 games with 11 homers, 36 RBI and a .224 batting average.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-br_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-br-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2016">2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Guerrero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2016">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Guerrero injured his knee in spring training in 2016, causing him to begin the season on the disabled list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In a minor league rehab stint across three different levels in May, he played in 16 games and hit .136.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> When he was ready to rejoin the team, there was no spot available on the active roster so Guerrero was designated for assignment by the Dodgers on May 31, 2016 and removed from the 40-man roster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The Dodgers released Guerrero on June 8.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chunichi_Dragons">Chunichi Dragons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Guerrero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Chunichi Dragons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 26, 2016, Guerrero signed with the <a href="/wiki/Chunichi_Dragons" title="Chunichi Dragons">Chunichi Dragons</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Professional_Baseball" title="Nippon Professional Baseball">Nippon Professional Baseball</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
