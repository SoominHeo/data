<p><i><b>De Stijl</b></i> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span> <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span></span>/</a></span></span> is the second <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a> by the American <a href="/wiki/Garage_rock" title="Garage rock">garage rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/The_White_Stripes" title="The White Stripes">The White Stripes</a>, released on June 20, 2000 on <a href="/wiki/Sympathy_for_the_Record_Industry" title="Sympathy for the Record Industry">Sympathy for the Record Industry</a>. The album reached number thirty-eight on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_magazine" title="Billboard magazine">Billboard</a>'</i>s <a href="/wiki/Independent_Albums" title="Independent Albums">Independent Albums</a> chart in 2002, when The White Stripes' popularity began to grow. It has since become a cult favorite among White Stripes fans, due to the simplicity of the band's <a href="/wiki/Blues" title="Blues">blues</a>/<a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk</a> fusion.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/De_Stijl" title="De Stijl">De Stijl</a> ("the style" in English) was a Dutch art movement (including the painter <a href="/wiki/Piet_Mondrian" title="Piet Mondrian">Mondrian</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tate_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-tate-6">[6]</a></sup> Vocalist <a href="/wiki/Jack_White" title="Jack White">Jack White</a> had been an admirer of the style for some time, especially of furniture designer <a href="/wiki/Gerrit_Rietveld" title="Gerrit Rietveld">Gerrit Rietveld</a>. Rietveld designed the <a href="/wiki/Rietveld_Schr%C3%B6der_House" title="Rietveld Schröder House">Rietveld Schröder House</a>, which Jack and <a href="/wiki/Meg_White" title="Meg White">Meg White</a> visited while on tour in the Netherlands. <i>De Stijl</i> was dedicated to both Rietveld and <a href="/wiki/Blind_Willie_McTell" title="Blind Willie McTell">Blind Willie McTell</a>.</p>
<p>It was after the tour began for <i>De Stijl</i> that Jack finally closed his upholstery shop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Phipps_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Phipps-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 5, 2008, Canadian media reported that former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio-Canada" title="Radio-Canada">Radio-Canada</a> host Dominique Payette filed a lawsuit against The White Stripes for using a nine-second clip of her interview with a little girl at the beginning of "Jumble, Jumble". She demanded $70,000 in damages and the removal of the album from store shelves. The dispute was settled out of court.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The re-issued vinyl LP version of the record was pressed at <a href="/wiki/United_Record_Pressing" title="United Record Pressing">United Record Pressing</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville, Tennessee</a> and mastered all-analog from the original master tapes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The song 'Apple Blossom' was used in <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino" title="Quentin Tarantino">Quentin Tarantino</a>'s film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hateful_Eight" title="The Hateful Eight">The Hateful Eight</a></i>, and is included on the <a href="/wiki/The_Hateful_Eight_(soundtrack)" title="The Hateful Eight (soundtrack)">official soundtrack</a>.</p>
