<p><b>Alor Setar</b>, (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jawi_language" title="Jawi language">Jawi</a>: الور ستار; Tamil:அலோர் ஸ்டார்;Chinese: 亚罗士打; <i>formerly</i> <b>Alor Star</b> from 2004–2008<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unrename_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-unrename-2">[2]</a></sup>) is a city and the state capital of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kedah_State" title="Kedah State">Kedah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>; which is the second largest city in the state after <a href="/wiki/Sungai_Petani" title="Sungai Petani">Sungai Petani</a>, Kedah (taking over the position in 2010); important cities in the west coast of <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_Malaysia" title="Peninsular Malaysia">Peninsular Malaysia</a> along the longest expressway, located 400 km from Kuala Lumpur. The city is home to the Central State Administration Centre and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_Centre" title="Administrative Centre">Administrative Centre</a> Kota Setar District in the City of Muadzam Shah and Kedah the royal town of Bandar <a href="/wiki/Anak_Bukit" title="Anak Bukit">Anak Bukit</a>.</p>
<p>Alor Setar is unique for Malaysia because the government sector and schools in 'tip of Malaysia' city here operate from Sunday to Thursday every week, which is different from neighbouring states.</p>
<p>Its location along the main travel route from Malaysia to Thailand has long made it a major transportation hub in the northern Malay Peninsula. At present, the city covers a land area of 666 square kilometres, which is occupied by more than 300,000 inhabitants (as per the 2010 census). At the local government level, Alor Setar is administered by the Alor Setar City Council.</p>
<p>The city is served by the <a href="/wiki/Sultan_Abdul_Halim_Airport" title="Sultan Abdul Halim Airport">Sultan Abdul Halim Airport</a>, which began operations in 2006. The airport is not served by any commercial international flights; however, during the Haj season, there are special flights available for Muslim pilgrims journeying to <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a> to perform the Haj. The city is connected to other parts of Peninsular Malaysia by the North-South Expressway, the Shahab Perdana Bus Station and the Alor Setar railway station. The city's Kuala Kedah Jetty is served by ferries linking the city with the popular resort island of <a href="/wiki/Langkawi" title="Langkawi">Langkawi</a>.</p>
<p>Alor Setar is the birthplace of two <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Malaysia" title="Prime Minister of Malaysia">Prime Ministers</a>, YTM <a href="/wiki/Tunku_Abdul_Rahman" title="Tunku Abdul Rahman">Tunku Abdul Rahman</a>, Malaysia's first Prime Minister, and Tun Dr. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mahathir_bin_Mohamad" title="Mahathir bin Mohamad">Mahathir bin Mohamad</a>, Malaysia's fourth Prime Minister.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alor_Setar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name came from the combination of two <a href="/wiki/Malay_language" title="Malay language">Malay</a> words: <i>Alor</i> ("small stream") and <i>Setar</i> (<i><a href="/wiki/Bouea_macrophylla" title="Bouea macrophylla">Bouea macrophylla</a></i> Griff.; known as the marian plum, gandaria, or plum mango), a tree which is related to <a href="/wiki/Mango" title="Mango">mango</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The city's name was changed to <i>Alor Star</i> during 21 December 2003 ceremony proclaiming it a city. The city's previous name was restored on 15 January 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unrename_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-unrename-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alor_Setar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alor Setar was founded in 1735 by Kedah's 19th Ruler, <a href="/wiki/Sultan_Muhammad_Jiwa_Zainal_Adilin_II" title="Sultan Muhammad Jiwa Zainal Adilin II">Sultan Muhammad Jiwa Zainal Adilin II</a> and is the state's eighth administrative centre since the establishment of the Kedah Sultanate in 1136.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The earlier administrative centres were located in Kota Bukit Meriam, Kota Sungai Emas, Kota Siputeh, Kota Naga, Kota Sena, Kota Indera Kayangan and Kota Bukit Pinang.</p>
<p>Significant events held here included the handing back of <a href="/wiki/Perlis" title="Perlis">Perlis</a> and Setul (now <a href="/wiki/Satun_Province" title="Satun Province">Satun</a>) to Kedah by the Siamese in May 1897 (both provinces were separated from <a href="/wiki/Kedah" title="Kedah">Kedah</a> since 1821) and a 90-day festival from June to September 1904 to celebrate the wedding of the five children of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultan_Abdul_Hamid_Halim_Shah" title="Sultan Abdul Hamid Halim Shah">Sultan Abdul Hamid Halim Shah</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Alor Setar had been granted as a city – the ninth in <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, on 21 December 2003. The proclamation ceremony to declare the <a href="/wiki/Kedah" title="Kedah">Kedah</a> capital a city was held at Dataran Tunku, Alor Setar. Among those present at the historic ceremony were the Kedah's Sultan <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tuanku_Abdul_Halim" title="Tuanku Abdul Halim">Tuanku Abdul Halim Mu'adzam Shah</a> and his consort Che' Puan Haminah Hamidun, the Raja Muda of Kedah and Raja Puan Muda (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heir_Apparent" title="Heir Apparent">Heir Apparent</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kedah" title="Kedah">Kedah</a> State and his consort), members of the <a href="/wiki/Kedah" title="Kedah">Kedah</a> royalty, parliament members and state exco members. The ceremony was also witnessed by civilians and tourists.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alor_Setar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alor Setar is located in the north-western part of Peninsular Malaysia. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kota_Setar" title="Kota Setar">Kota Setar</a> district includes the city of Alor Setar with a population of more than 366,787 (2010)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> people in Kedah. It also borders the districts of Kubang Pasu, Pokok Sena and Pendang. Located at Coordinates 6 ° 7'N 100 ° 22'E, Alor Setar is located at the edge of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malacca_Strait" title="Malacca Strait">Malacca Strait</a> which separates <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a> from <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>.</p>
<p>The city once encompassed an area of 666 km²; however, it was reduced to 424 km² due to the creation of the new district of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pokok_Sena" title="Pokok Sena">Pokok Sena</a>. Alor Setar is surrounded by important river systems such as the Kedah River, River Langgar, Tajar River, Anak Bukit River, Alor Malai and Alor Merah.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alor_Setar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As with nearly all parts of Malaysia, Alor Setar features a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_monsoon_climate" title="Tropical monsoon climate">tropical monsoon climate</a> under the Köppen climate classification. Alor Setar has a very lengthy <a href="/wiki/Wet_season" title="Wet season">wet season</a>. As is common in several regions with this climate, precipitation is seen even during the short dry season. Temperatures are relatively consistent throughout the course of the year, with average high temperatures around 32 degrees Celsius and average low temperatures around 23 degrees Celsius. Alor Setar sees on average roughly 2300 mm of precipitation per year.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_sites">Cultural sites</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alor_Setar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Cultural sites">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Balai Besar (Grand Audience Hall)</b></p>
<p>Initially the function of the building was as the Balai Rong Seri or Balai Penghadapan (audience hall), that was situated at the back area of the Kota Setar Palace complex (Istana Kota Setar). The original building was built in 1735 by HRH <a href="/wiki/Sultan_Muhammad_Jiwa_Zainal_Adilin_II" title="Sultan Muhammad Jiwa Zainal Adilin II">Sultan Muhammad Jiwa Zainal Adilin II</a>, the 19th Sultan of <a href="/wiki/Kedah" title="Kedah">Kedah</a>. The pillars, roofs and floors were made of wood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Two Kedah Sultans were officially installed at the Balai Besar:</p>
<ul>
<li>HRH <a href="/wiki/Sultan_Ahmad_Tajuddin_Halim_Shah_II" title="Sultan Ahmad Tajuddin Halim Shah II">Sultan Ahmad Tajuddin Halim Shah II</a>, the 22nd Sultan of Kedah) in 1805</li>
<li>HRH <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tuanku_Abdul_Halim" title="Tuanku Abdul Halim">Sultan Abdul Halim</a> on 20 February 1959.</li>
</ul>

<p><b>Balai Nobat</b></p>
<p>This 18-metre-tall and 5-metre-wide three-tiered octagonal tower sole purpose is to keep all the royal musical instruments including the serunai (wooden flute), nafiri, gendang (drum) and gong, which is known as <a href="/wiki/Nobat" title="Nobat">nobat</a>.</p>
<p>Upon the official opening of the Alor Setar town in 1735 by HRH <a href="/wiki/Sultan_Muhammad_Jiwa_Zainal_Adilin_II" title="Sultan Muhammad Jiwa Zainal Adilin II">Sultan Muhammad Jiwa Zainal Adilin II</a>, the 19th Sultan of <a href="/wiki/Kedah" title="Kedah">Kedah</a>), a number of early buildings had been completed including the Kota Setar Palace, the Balai Besar, the Balai Nobat and the State Mosque. The original design of the Balai Nobat could not be ascertained but certainly it was built using wood.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>During the reign of HRH <a href="/wiki/Sultan_Ahmad_Tajuddin_Mukarram_Shah" title="Sultan Ahmad Tajuddin Mukarram Shah">Sultan Ahmad Tajuddin Mukarram Shah</a>, the 23rd <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultan_of_Kedah" title="Sultan of Kedah">Sultan of Kedah</a>), the new Balai Nobat consisting of five-story building was built. The building was totally built of wood with zinc roofing. During the reign of the HRH <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultan_Abdul_Hamid_Halim_Shah" title="Sultan Abdul Hamid Halim Shah">Sultan Abdul Hamid Halim Shah</a>, the 25th <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultan_of_Kedah" title="Sultan of Kedah">Sultan of Kedah</a>), a new building was built using concrete and metal.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The architecture is the same as the previous wooden building. The height was three-story and the dome at its apex represents the Islamic element such as those found on mosques. This new building was built in 1906.</p>

<p><b>The Tou Mu Kung Temple</b> The Tou Mu Kung Temple (Chinese: 斗母宫) is a Taoist temple situated on Jalan Gangsa. Worshipping the Empress Registrar of Birth or Dou Mu (<a class="extiw" href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%96%97%E5%A7%86%E5%85%83%E5%90%9B" title="zh:斗姆元君">斗母</a>) and Nine Emperor (<a class="extiw" href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E4%B9%9D%E7%9A%87%E5%A4%A7%E5%B8%9D" title="zh:九皇大帝">九皇大帝</a>) deities, the temple has both Taoist and Buddhist influences.</p>
<p><b>Kota Kuala Kedah (Kuala Kedah Fort)</b></p>
<p>Kota Kuala Kedah is the remains of a fort, which for centuries protected the <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Kedah" title="Kuala Kedah">Kuala Kedah</a> Kingdom from naval attacks, on the bank of the Sungai Kedah (Kedah River).</p>
<p>Also known as Kota Kuala Bahang, the fort was originally constructed to provide protection against the Portuguese invaders. However, the fort had to stand againse attacks from the <a href="/wiki/Acehnese_people" title="Acehnese people">Achenese</a> from Sumatra and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bugis_people" title="Bugis people">Bugis</a> as well. The fort was rebuilt in 1771 and was completed nine years later.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<dl>
<dt>Muzium Negeri (State Museum)</dt>
</dl>
<p>As a state museum, it houses a large collection of Kedah's historical, culture, and royal heritage. The collection includes early Chinese porcelain, artefacts from the archaeological excavations in Lembah Bujang (Bujang Valley) and a fabulous pokok bunga emas or 'gold tree' produced as a tribute to the Thais.</p>
<p>The museum is also a research centre which specialises on Kedah's cultural history. Before the present premises were built in 1936, the museum – officially opened by the then Kedah Mentri Besar Tan Sri Tunku Ismail Tunku Yahya (Tunku indera Setia), on 3 February 1957 – was located in the Balai Besar (Grand Hall).</p>
<dl>
<dt>Pusat Sains Negara (National Science Center)</dt>
</dl>
<p>Located at Gunung Keriang next to the Alor Setar Aquatic Centre and Paddy Museum, the centre is the first science centre built in northern Malaysia region.</p>
<dl>
<dt>Muzium Padi (Paddy Museum)</dt>
</dl>
<p>The museum is the first paddy museum in Malaysia, and the fourth to be opened in the world after Japan, Germany and the Philippines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The museum showcases the rice cultivation process in Malaysia and displays the tools and equipment which are used in the trade over the years. Through beautiful murals, featuring the art work of 60 artists from North Korea, the history of rice cultivation in Malaysia is revealed.</p>
<dl>
<dt>Balai Seni Negeri (State Art Gallery)</dt>
</dl>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Kedah_State_Art_Gallery" title="Kedah State Art Gallery">Kedah State Art Gallery</a> was set up with the objective of fostering interest in and an appreciation of art in the state. Its collection includes paintings, photographs, musical instruments and handicrafts.</p>
<dl>
<dt>Galeria Sultan Abdul Halim</dt>
</dl>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Sultan_Abdul_Halim_Mu%27adzam_Shah_Gallery" title="Sultan Abdul Halim Mu'adzam Shah Gallery">Sultan Abdul Halim Mu'adzam Shah Gallery</a> building was the Alor Setar High Court, before it was moved to Kompleks Mahkamah Alor Setar in Suka Menanti. This building is located at the junction of Jalan Putera, adjacent to the Balai Nobat. This building was built for the existing Balai Seni. This building was built on Rabiulawal 1341 Hijrah equivalent to November 1922.</p>

<dt>Muzium Diraja Kedah (Kedah Royal Museum)</dt>
</dl>
<p>This building which originated from the Kota Setar Palace is a wooden building that was built by HRH <a href="/wiki/Sultan_Muhammad_Jiwa_Zainal_Adilin_II" title="Sultan Muhammad Jiwa Zainal Adilin II">Sultan Muhammad Jiwa Zainal Adilin II</a>, the 19th Sultan of Kedah), who was the founder of Alor Setar town in 1735. This building had undergone demolitions several times due to attacks by the <a href="/wiki/Bugis" title="Bugis">Bugis</a> (1770) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siam" title="Siam">Siamese</a> (1821).</p>
<p>The concrete building was completed during the reign of HRH <a href="/wiki/Sultan_Ahmad_Tajuddin_Mukarram_Shah" title="Sultan Ahmad Tajuddin Mukarram Shah">Sultan Ahmad Tajuddin Mukarram Shah</a>). His Royal Highness had once stayed in this palace with his queen, Wan Hajar Wan Ismail who was also known as "Mak Wan Besar". Therefore, this palace is also known as the "Mak Wan Besar Palace".</p>
<p>YTM <a href="/wiki/Tunku_Abdul_Rahman" title="Tunku Abdul Rahman">Tunku Abdul Rahman</a> Putra was also born adjacent to this palace (in the Pagoda building, which no longer exists today) on 8 February 1903 and grew up in this palace with his father the late HRH <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sultan_Abdul_Hamid&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sultan Abdul Hamid (page does not exist)">Sultan Abdul Hamid</a> and his mother Mak Che Manjalara while they resided there.</p>
<p>This palace is also renowned as the "Pelamin Palace" after it was extended to include a pavilion and additional rooms, when HRH <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultan_Abdul_Hamid_Halim_Shah" title="Sultan Abdul Hamid Halim Shah">Sultan Abdul Hamid Halim Shah</a> wanted to marry off his five princes and princesses. The grand wedding ceremony lasted for three months in 1904. After 1941, the palace was used as a school and an office for several organisations including the office for the St. John Ambulance and the <a href="/wiki/Persekutuan_Pengakap_Malaysia" title="Persekutuan Pengakap Malaysia">Scout movements</a>. On 25 July 1983, this palace was declared the "Kedah Royal Museum".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pekan_Rabu_.28Wednesday_Market.29">Pekan Rabu (Wednesday Market)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alor_Setar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Pekan Rabu (Wednesday Market)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Pekan_Rabu" title="Pekan Rabu">Pekan Rabu</a></div>
<p>Pekan Rabu is located near the government offices in Alor Setar. Although the name literally means "Wednesday Market", this market is open all week long, and sells a variety of items from tidbits to beautiful <a href="/wiki/Handicraft" title="Handicraft">handicraft</a>.</p>
<p>This is a popular spot amongst tourists and locals and is a source of pride for Kedahans. Even former Prime Minister <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mahathir_bin_Mohamad" title="Mahathir bin Mohamad">Tun Dr. Mahathir Bin Mohamad</a> was once a trader at Pekan Rabu.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Menara_Alor_Setar_.28Alor_Setar_Tower.29">Menara Alor Setar (Alor Setar Tower)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alor_Setar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Menara Alor Setar (Alor Setar Tower)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Alor_Setar_Tower" title="Alor Setar Tower">Alor Setar Tower</a></div>
<p>Located in the heart of Alor Setar, this tower, standing at 165.5 metres tall is a prominent and modern landmark that signifies the rapid development in the state of <a href="/wiki/Kedah" title="Kedah">Kedah</a>. This landmark of the state of Kedah Darul Aman not only serves as a telecommunication tower, it is also a tourist attraction which offers the public to enjoy a breathtaking and panoramic view of the city of Alor Setar and its surrounding. The Alor Setar Tower also serves as a recreational centre for art and cultural activities, meeting room and 360' Revolving Restaurant.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wan_Mat_Saman_Canal">Wan Mat Saman Canal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alor_Setar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Wan Mat Saman Canal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Wan_Mat_Saman_Aqueduct" title="Wan Mat Saman Aqueduct">Wan Mat Saman Aqueduct</a></div>
<p>Wan Mat Saman Aqueduct is the longest aqueduct in Malaysia. It connects <a href="/wiki/Kedah_River" title="Kedah River">Kedah River</a> in Alor Setar to Gurun at the south. This aqueduct was built to enable Kedah rice farmers to boost the state's rice production and through it, to enable Kedah to earn the nickname "Malaysia's rice bowl".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tun_Dr._Mahathir.27s_Birthplace">Tun Dr. Mahathir's Birthplace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alor_Setar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Tun Dr. Mahathir's Birthplace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

He was born in Alor Star on 20 December 1925 at No 18 Lorong Kilang Ais, off Jalan Pegawai. His home has since been designated as an historical building and it was restored by the National Archives in 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-birthplace_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-birthplace-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<p>Istana Anak Bukit is the birthplace of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tuanku_Abdul_Halim_Muadzam_Shah" title="Tuanku Abdul Halim Muadzam Shah">Tuanku Abdul Halim Muadzam Shah</a>, the current Sultan of Kedah. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Places_of_worship">Places of worship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alor_Setar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Places of worship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Masjid_Zahir" title="Masjid Zahir">Masjid Zahir</a></b> (Zahir State Mosque)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> is the state mosque of <a href="/wiki/Kedah" title="Kedah">Kedah</a> and enjoys a royal history and patronage. Built in 1912, its official opening ceremony was held on Friday, 15 October 1915 by the late HRH <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultan_Abdul_Hamid_Halim_Shah" title="Sultan Abdul Hamid Halim Shah">Sultan Abdul Hamid Halim Shah</a>. The architecture of the mosque was inspired by the Azizi Mosque<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> in <i>Tanjung Pura</i>, <a href="/wiki/Sultanate_of_Langkat" title="Sultanate of Langkat">Langkat Sultanate</a> (present-day <a href="/wiki/North_Sumatra" title="North Sumatra">North Sumatra</a>).</p>
<p><b>Wat Nikrodharam</b> is a significant Buddhist shrine in the city. It is located at Jalan Teluk Wan Jah, just next to the city's primary roundabout. Its architecture reflects strong Thai elements with minor Chinese motifs. It is also a sanctuary and an oasis in the city centre with trees and shades providing spiritual shelter to Buddhists from the city and abroad. Both <a href="/wiki/Theravada" title="Theravada">Theravada</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mahayana" title="Mahayana">Mahayana</a> Buddhist traditions are cherished here.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

</div>
<p>Alor Setar have many sport facilities that are open to public. The facilities includes Stadium Darulaman which is located in Jalan Stadium and Lebuhraya Darul Aman. It is used mostly for <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> matches and is the official stadium for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malaysian_Super_League" title="Malaysian Super League">Malaysian Super League</a>'s club <a href="/wiki/Kedah_FA" title="Kedah FA">Kedah FA</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malaysian_Premier_League" title="Malaysian Premier League">Malaysian Premier League</a>'s club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kuala_Muda_NAZA_FC" title="Kuala Muda NAZA FC">Kuala Muda NAZA FC</a>.. The stadium was built in 1967 and has a capacity of 32,387 seats after the expansion in 1997. It was one of the venues for the <a href="/wiki/1997_FIFA_World_Youth_Championship" title="1997 FIFA World Youth Championship">1997 FIFA World Youth Championship</a>.</p>
<p>Other facilities are Stadium Sultan Abdul Halim, Pusat Akuatik Alor Setar, Kompleks Belia &amp; Sukan.</p>
<p>Kedah hosted the Sukma Games in 2006.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alor_Setar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The capital of <a href="/wiki/Kedah" title="Kedah">Kedah</a> is approachable from all parts of the <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_Malaysia" title="Peninsular Malaysia">Peninsular Malaysia</a> by all transport means.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_Road">By Road</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alor_Setar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: By Road">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North-South_Expressway,_Malaysia" title="North-South Expressway, Malaysia">North-South Expressway</a>, which starts from <a href="/wiki/Bukit_Kayu_Hitam" title="Bukit Kayu Hitam">Bukit Kayu Hitam</a> (Malaysia-Thai border town) until Singapore, has made it easy for any traveller to drive up to Kedah. Roads are accessible from and to major towns and cities in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peninsula_Malaysia" title="Peninsula Malaysia">Peninsula Malaysia</a>. It is a 6–7-hour drive from <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur" title="Kuala Lumpur">Kuala Lumpur</a> and 1.5-hour drive from <a href="/wiki/Penang" title="Penang">Penang</a> via <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North-South_Expressway,_Malaysia" title="North-South Expressway, Malaysia">North-South Expressway</a>. From major towns, such as <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur" title="Kuala Lumpur">Kuala Lumpur</a> and <a href="/wiki/Penang" title="Penang">Penang</a>, air-conditioned express buses are also available.</p>
<p>Travellers can drive to Alor Setar by car from south and north via <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North-South_Expressway,_Malaysia" title="North-South Expressway, Malaysia">North-South Expressway</a>, from east coast via <a href="/wiki/East%E2%80%93West_Highway_(Malaysia)" title="East–West Highway (Malaysia)">East-West Expressway</a> (Lebuhraya Timur Barat). Towns such as <a href="/wiki/Butterworth,_Penang" title="Butterworth, Penang">Butterworth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kangar" title="Kangar">Kangar</a> etc., can also be accessed from Alor Setar. Car rental services are available, which their offices can be found at airport, train station or in the city.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus">Bus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alor_Setar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Bus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Most of bus companies in <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_Malaysia" title="Peninsular Malaysia">Peninsular Malaysia</a> provide air-conditioned express bus services. Tickets can be bought at bus terminals or ticket offices. All buses that arrive in Alor Setar stop at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shahab_Perdana_Terminal_Bus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shahab Perdana Terminal Bus (page does not exist)">Shahab Perdana Terminal Bus</a>, Alor Setar. Those who wish to travel to other towns, including <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, can catch their bus at this terminal. Local bus services are also available. They serve almost all towns in <a href="/wiki/Kedah" title="Kedah">Kedah</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_Rail">By Rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alor_Setar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: By Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Train services are provided by <a href="/wiki/Keretapi_Tanah_Melayu" title="Keretapi Tanah Melayu">Keretapi Tanah Melayu Berhad</a> (KTMB). The train services are between Kuala Lumpur and Alor Setar, Butterworth and Alor Setar, Padang Besar and Alor Setar, Hat Yai and Alor Setar, and, Singapore and Alor Setar. <a href="/wiki/Alor_Setar_railway_station" title="Alor Setar railway station">Alor Setar railway station</a> is located near to the CBD.(Will moved to <a href="/wiki/Anak_Bukit" title="Anak Bukit">Anak Bukit</a> International Railway Station when the electrical twin rail (<a href="/wiki/Ipoh" title="Ipoh">Ipoh</a>, Perak to <a href="/wiki/Padang_Besar,_Malaysia" title="Padang Besar, Malaysia">Padang Besar</a>, Perlis) project is finished.) Tickets can be obtained from train station or online booking.</p>
<p>There is also an international express train service to <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>, Thailand, operated by <a href="/wiki/State_Railway_of_Thailand" title="State Railway of Thailand">State Railway of Thailand</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_Air">By Air</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alor_Setar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: By Air">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The newly built state-of-the-art <a href="/wiki/Sultan_Abdul_Halim_Airport" title="Sultan Abdul Halim Airport">Sultan Abdul Halim Airport</a> (Alor Setar Airport, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IATA" title="IATA">IATA</a>: <b>AOR</b>) is located at Kepala Batas, 15 km north from city. It serves mostly domestic flights. <a href="/wiki/Malaysia_Airlines" title="Malaysia Airlines">Malaysia Airlines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Firefly_(airline)" title="Firefly (airline)">Firefly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malindo_Air" title="Malindo Air">Malindo Air</a> and <a href="/wiki/AirAsia" title="AirAsia">AirAsia</a> fly daily to and from Alor Setar and <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur" title="Kuala Lumpur">Kuala Lumpur</a>. AS from 9 September 2013 Malaysian Airline System connects Alor Setar with a direct flight to Madinah and Jeddah for the Hajj flight.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_Sea">By Sea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alor_Setar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: By Sea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Passenger ferry services to and from <a href="/wiki/Langkawi" title="Langkawi">Langkawi</a> to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kuah_Ferry_Service&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kuah Ferry Service (page does not exist)">Kuah Ferry Service</a> are provided at Jeti Kuala Kedah, Alor Setar. The jetty is located 15 km west from city. Travellers can take bus or taxi from city to the jetty.</p>

<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Ninth_Malaysia_Plan" title="Ninth Malaysia Plan">Ninth Malaysia Plan</a>, by the year 2010, Bandar Muadzam Shah in <a href="/wiki/Anak_Bukit" title="Anak Bukit">Anak Bukit</a> will become the new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_Centre" title="Administrative Centre">Administrative Centre</a> after Alor Setar city. Bandar Muadzam Shah (<a href="/wiki/Anak_Bukit" title="Anak Bukit">Anak Bukit</a>) is going through major development of infrastructures. This includes the new Anak Bukit police station, new National Registration Department(<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jabatan_Pendaftaran_Negara&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jabatan Pendaftaran Negara (page does not exist)">Jabatan Pendaftaran Negara</a>) of Kedah and also new <a href="/wiki/State_government" title="State government">state government</a> buildings.The new railway station (Anak Bukit International Rail Station) will also be built here when the electrified twin railway project is completed.</p>
