<p><b>Ali Bakar</b> (18 November 1947 – 9 August 2003) was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FA_Penang" title="FA Penang">football player</a> who represented the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malaysian_national_football_team" title="Malaysian national football team">Malaysian national football team</a> in the 1970s. He played for <a href="/wiki/Penang_FA" title="Penang FA">Penang FA</a> in Malaysia's domestic competition.</p>
<p>A midfielder, Ali was a squad player for the Malaysia team in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1972_Munich_Olympics" title="1972 Munich Olympics">1972 Munich Olympics</a> <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1972_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 1972 Summer Olympics">football competition</a>, and also represented Malaysia when it finished third in the <a href="/wiki/1974_Asian_Games" title="1974 Asian Games">1974 Asian Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>. Also in 1974, he was part of the <a href="/wiki/Malaysia_Cup" title="Malaysia Cup">Malaysia Cup</a>-winning Penang side. After two years, he also helped Penang side to win the international tournament, <a href="/wiki/Aga_Khan_Gold_Cup" title="Aga Khan Gold Cup">Aga Khan Gold Cup</a> held in Dhaka.</p>
<p>Ali suffered a heart attack and died on the field while playing in a charity football match in Singapore in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> His body was buried in Penang.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>His brother <a href="/wiki/Isa_Bakar" title="Isa Bakar">Isa Bakar</a> was also a football player, also playing for Penang and Malaysia.</p>
