<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Ali Said</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Ali Sa'id Sheik Hassan</b>, also referred to as <b>Ali Sa'id Hassan Awale</b> and <b>Ali Said</b> (died 17 June 2009), was the <a href="/wiki/Chief_of_police" title="Chief of police">chief of police</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mogadishu" title="Mogadishu">Mogadishu</a> and a commander of security forces during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/War_in_Somalia_(2009%E2%80%93)" title="War in Somalia (2009–)">war in Somalia</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> in which he was killed by <a href="/wiki/Sniper" title="Sniper">sniper</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> fire in June 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hassan_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hassan-5">[5]</a></sup> He had been the Mogadishu chief of the <a href="/wiki/Somali_Police_Force" title="Somali Police Force">Somali Police Force</a> for roughly two years,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hassan_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hassan-5">[5]</a></sup> prior to being shot by "Islamist forces" in Mogadishu's Hadan district during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mogadishu_(2009)" title="Battle of Mogadishu (2009)">Battle of Mogadishu (2009)</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/BBC_News" title="BBC News">BBC</a> reported that the police chief's death would be "a significant setback for the pro-government forces as he had often been on the front line encouraging his colleagues to defend their positions".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-4">[4]</a></sup> The <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Times" title="New York Times">New York Times</a></i> reported Somali analysts stating that "the loss of the police chief, Col. Ali Said, would be a major blow to the transitional government led by Sheik <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sharif_Sheik_Ahmed" title="Sharif Sheik Ahmed">Sharif Sheik Ahmed</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-7">[7]</a></sup> Ali Said had survived an earlier assassination attempts in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
