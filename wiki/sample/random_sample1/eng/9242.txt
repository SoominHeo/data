<p><b>Alina Levshin</b> (born September 10, 1984 in <a href="/wiki/Odessa" title="Odessa">Odessa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, then <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a German-Ukrainian actress. She moved with her parents from the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> to Germany when she was six.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fas2012_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-fas2012-2">[2]</a></sup> From 2006 to 2010, she studied acting at the College of Film and Television "Konrad Wolf" in <a href="/wiki/Potsdam" title="Potsdam">Potsdam</a>.</p>
<p>Levshin was one of the main cast members in <a href="/wiki/Dominik_Graf" title="Dominik Graf">Dominik Graf</a>’s television series <i><a href="/wiki/Im_Angesicht_des_Verbrechens" title="Im Angesicht des Verbrechens">Im Angesicht des Verbrechens</a></i> who collectively received the <a href="/wiki/Deutscher_Fernsehpreis" title="Deutscher Fernsehpreis">German Television Award</a> in 2010. She starred in the 2011 German film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Combat_Girls_(film)" title="Combat Girls (film)">Combat Girls</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <i>(Kriegerin)</i>, for which she won the best actress awards at the <a href="/wiki/Deutscher_Filmpreis" title="Deutscher Filmpreis">Deutscher Filmpreis</a> awards and the <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_International_Film_Festival" title="São Paulo International Film Festival">São Paulo International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>She played at <i><a href="/wiki/Generation_War" title="Generation War">Generation War</a></i> (German: <i>Unsere Mütter, unsere Väter</i>, literally "Our mothers, our fathers"), a German <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> TV miniseries in 2013.</p>
