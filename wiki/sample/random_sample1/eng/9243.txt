<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Alidosi family</h1>

<i>Stemma</i> (<a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coat-of-arms" title="Coat-of-arms">coat-of-arms</a>) of the Alidosi family from former Palazzo Alidosi at <a href="/wiki/Cesena" title="Cesena">Cesena</a> in Italy. <a href="/wiki/Istria" title="Istria">Istrian</a> stone, Medieval and Renaissance Gallery, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Victoria_%26_Albert_Museum" title="Victoria &amp; Albert Museum">Victoria &amp; Albert Museum</a>, London <a class="external autonumber" href="http://google.com/search?q=cache:t3wiyQWqA3wJ:m.vam.ac.uk/collections/item/O127005/coat-of-arms/" rel="nofollow">[1]</a>.</div>

<p>The <b>Alidosi</b> or <b>Alodosi</b> are a family of <a href="/wiki/Romagna" title="Romagna">Romagna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, who held the <i><a href="/wiki/Signoria" title="Signoria">signoria</a></i> of the city of <a href="/wiki/Imola" title="Imola">Imola</a> during the Late <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>. They were originary of the <a href="/wiki/Santerno" title="Santerno">Santerno</a> valley. When Imola was stripped from them by <a href="/wiki/Filippo_Maria_Visconti" title="Filippo Maria Visconti">Filippo Maria Visconti</a> in 1424, they retreated to the countryside seigniory of <a href="/wiki/Castel_del_Rio" title="Castel del Rio">Castel del Rio</a>, in the Romagna <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apennines" title="Apennines">Apennines</a>, from which they were ousted in 1638 by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Urban_VIII" title="Pope Urban VIII">Pope Urban VIII</a>.</p>
<p>Another notable member was Cardinal <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Alidosi" title="Francesco Alidosi">Francesco Alidosi</a> (1455–1511), who was a friend of <a href="/wiki/Pope_Julius_II" title="Pope Julius II">Pope Julius II</a>.</p>
