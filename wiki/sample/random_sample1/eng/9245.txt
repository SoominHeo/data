<p><b>Alifu Dhaalu Atoll</b> (also known as <b>Southern Ari Atoll</b> or <b>Ari Atholhu Dhekunuburi</b>) is an administrative division of the <a href="/wiki/Maldives" title="Maldives">Maldives</a>.</p>
<p>The separation of <a href="/wiki/Ari_Atoll" title="Ari Atoll">Ari Atoll</a> (formerly <b>Alifu Atoll</b>) on March 1, 1984, into a Northern and a Southern section formed the two most recent administrative divisions of the Maldives, namely <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alifu_Alifu_Atoll" title="Alifu Alifu Atoll">Alifu Alifu Atoll</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alifu_Dhaalu_Atoll" title="Alifu Dhaalu Atoll">Alifu Dhaalu Atoll</a>. Alifu Dhaalu Atoll lies south of the line between the channels of Himendhoo Dhekunukandu and Genburugau Kandu.</p>
<p>There is a beautiful ancient mosque in <a href="/wiki/Fenfushi_(Alif_Dhaal_Atoll)" title="Fenfushi (Alif Dhaal Atoll)">Fenfushi</a> island having wooden decorated ceilings and lacquerwork panels.</p>
<p>Buddhist remains, including a <a href="/wiki/Stupa" title="Stupa">stupa</a>, have been found in <a href="/wiki/Ariadhoo" title="Ariadhoo">Ariadhoo</a> Island.</p>

<p><b>NOTE:</b> <i>Haa Alifu, Haa Dhaalu, Shaviyani, Noonu, Raa, Baa, Kaafu, etc. (including Alif Dhaalu) are code letters assigned to the present administrative divisions of the Maldives. They are not the proper names of the <a href="/wiki/Atolls_of_the_Maldives" title="Atolls of the Maldives">natural atolls</a> that make up these divisions. Some atolls are divided into two administrative divisions while other divisions are made up of two or more natural atolls. The order followed by the code letters is from North to South, beginning with the first letters of the <a href="/wiki/Thaana" title="Thaana">Thaana</a> alphabet used in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dhivehi_language" title="Dhivehi language">Dhivehi</a>. These code letters are not accurate from the geographical and cultural point of view. However, they have become popular among tourists and foreigners in the Maldives who find them easier to pronounce than the true atoll names in Dhivehi, (save a few exceptions, like Ari Atoll).</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Whale_sharks" title="Whale sharks">Whale sharks</a> are year-round residents of Alif Dhaal Atoll. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
