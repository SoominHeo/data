<p><b>Almarhum Al-Sultan Mahmud Al-Muktafi Billah Shah Al-Haj ibni Almarhum Al-Sultan Ismail Nasiruddin Shah Al-Haj</b> was the 16th Sultan of <a href="/wiki/Terengganu" title="Terengganu">Terengganu</a> from 21 September 1979 to 14 May 1998.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mahmud_of_Terengganu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sultan Mahmud was born on 29 April 1930 in <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Terengganu" title="Kuala Terengganu">Kuala Terengganu</a>. He was married to Sharifah Nong Fatima As-Saggoff binti Sayyid Abdullah As-Saggoff and Tengku Ampuan Bariah binti Almarhum Sultan Sir Hisamuddin Alam Shah, sister of late Sultan Selangor, Almarhum <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultan_Salahuddin" title="Sultan Salahuddin">Sultan Salahuddin Abdul Aziz Shah</a> in 1951. He was a cousin to the late Almarhum <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultan_Salahuddin" title="Sultan Salahuddin">Sultan Salahuddin Abdul Aziz Shah</a> of <a href="/wiki/Selangor" title="Selangor">Selangor</a>.</p>
<p>Twenty eight years later, his father <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultan_Ismail_Nasiruddin_Shah" title="Sultan Ismail Nasiruddin Shah">Sultan Ismail Nasiruddin Shah</a> died in 1979. He was installed as the Sultan of Terengganu in 1981 and Tengku Ampuan Bariah became Tengku Ampuan Besar of Terengganu.</p>
<p>Sultan Mahmud was the <a href="/wiki/Colonel" title="Colonel">colonel</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kor_Armor_DiRaja" title="Kor Armor DiRaja">Royal Armoured Corps</a> (KAD) from 1979 until 1998.</p>
<p>He was a close friend of his advisor <a href="/wiki/Wan_Mokhtar_Ahmad" title="Wan Mokhtar Ahmad">Tan Sri Wan Mokhtar Ahmad</a>, the former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Menteri_Besar" title="Menteri Besar">Menteri Besar</a> (chief minister) of Terengganu from 1974 to 1999. His main goal was to make Terengganu a developed state. Major state projects and developments under his reign including <a href="/wiki/Petronas" title="Petronas">Petronas Petroleum Complex</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kerteh" title="Kerteh">Kerteh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sultan_Ismail_Power_Station" title="Sultan Ismail Power Station">Sultan Ismail Power Station</a> at <a href="/wiki/Paka,_Malaysia" title="Paka, Malaysia">Paka</a> the largest power station in <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kenyir_Dam" title="Kenyir Dam">Kenyir Dam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sultan_Mahmud_Bridge" title="Sultan Mahmud Bridge">Sultan Mahmud Bridge</a>, the bridge linking Kuala Terengganu to Pulau Duyong and Kuala Nerus, <a href="/wiki/Wisma_Darul_Iman" title="Wisma Darul Iman">Wisma Darul Iman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tengku_Tengah_Zaharah_Mosque" title="Tengku Tengah Zaharah Mosque">Tengku Tengah Zaharah Mosque</a> (Floating Mosque).</p>
<p>He performed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hadj" title="Hadj">hadj</a> pilgrimage with Almarhum Sultan Salahuddin of Selangor in 1984.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hobbies">Hobbies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mahmud_of_Terengganu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Hobbies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His favorite hobby was playing golf and photographers. He had taken pictures at every place of interest in the state include the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel_Primula&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hotel Primula (page does not exist)">Hotel Primula</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wisma_Darul_Iman" title="Wisma Darul Iman">Wisma Darul Iman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Istana_Maziah" title="Istana Maziah">Istana Maziah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tengku_Tengah_Zaharah_Mosque" title="Tengku Tengah Zaharah Mosque">Tengku Tengah Zaharah Mosque</a> (Floating Mosque), <a href="/wiki/Abidin_Mosque" title="Abidin Mosque">Abidin Mosque</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rantau_Abang" title="Rantau Abang">Rantau Abang</a>, and many more.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Deaths">Deaths</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mahmud_of_Terengganu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Deaths">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 14 May 1998, he died in <a href="/wiki/Mount_Elizabeth_Hospital" title="Mount Elizabeth Hospital">Mount Elizabeth Hospital</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> and was replaced by his son <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultan_Mizan_Zainal_Abidin" title="Sultan Mizan Zainal Abidin">Sultan Mizan Zainal Abidin</a>. His body was laid to rest in the new Royal Mausoleum near <a href="/wiki/Al-Muktafi_Billah_Shah_Mosque" title="Al-Muktafi Billah Shah Mosque">Al-Muktafi Billah Shah Mosque</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Terengganu" title="Kuala Terengganu">Kuala Terengganu</a>. He was the first sultan of Terengganu to be buried here.</p>
