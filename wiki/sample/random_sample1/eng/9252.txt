<p><b>Santa Mar√≠a la Real de La Almudena</b> is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" title="Roman Catholic Church">Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Cathedral" title="Cathedral">cathedral</a> in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, the seat of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archdiocese_of_Madrid" title="Archdiocese of Madrid">Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Madrid</a>. It was consecrated by <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_Paul_II" title="Pope John Paul II">Pope John Paul II</a> in 1993.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Almudena_Cathedral&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When the capital of Spain was transferred from <a href="/wiki/Toledo,_Spain" title="Toledo, Spain">Toledo</a> to <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> in 1561, the seat of the Church in Spain remained in Toledo and the new capital had no cathedral. Plans to build a cathedral in Madrid dedicated to the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_of_Almudena" title="Virgin of Almudena">Virgin of Almudena</a> were discussed as early as the 16th century but even though Spain built more than 40 cities in the new world during that century and plenty of cathedrals, the cost of expanding and keeping the Empire came first and the construction of Madrid's cathedral was postponed. Making the cathedral the largest that the world had ever seen was then a priority, all other main Spanish cities had centuries old cathedrals, Madrid also has old churches but the construction of Almudena only began in 1879.</p>
<p>The cathedral seems to have been built on the site of a medieval mosque that was destroyed in 1083 when <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_VI_of_Le%C3%B3n_and_Castile" title="Alfonso VI of Le√≥n and Castile">Alfonso VI</a> reconquered Madrid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Francisco_de_Cubas" title="Francisco de Cubas">Francisco de Cubas</a>, the Marquis of Cubas, designed and directed the construction in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gothic_revival" title="Gothic revival">Gothic revival</a> style. Construction ceased completely during the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War" title="Spanish Civil War">Spanish Civil War</a>, and the project was abandoned until 1950, when <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fernando_Chueca_Goitia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fernando Chueca Goitia (page does not exist)">Fernando Chueca Goitia</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">¬†(<a class="extiw" href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fernando_Chueca_Goitia" title="es:Fernando Chueca Goitia">es</a>)</span> adapted the plans of de Cubas to a <a href="/wiki/Baroque" title="Baroque">baroque</a> exterior to match the grey and white fa√ßade of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palacio_Real" title="Palacio Real">Palacio Real</a>, which stands directly opposite. The cathedral was not completed until 1993, when it was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Consecrated" title="Consecrated">consecrated</a> by <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_Paul_II" title="Pope John Paul II">Pope John Paul II</a>. On May 22, 2004, the marriage of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_Felipe_VI" title="King Felipe VI">King Felipe VI</a>, then crown prince, to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Letizia_Ortiz_Rocasolano" title="Letizia Ortiz Rocasolano">Letizia Ortiz Rocasolano</a> took place at the cathedral.</p>
<p>The Neo-Gothic interior is uniquely modern, with chapels and statues of contemporary artists, in heretogeneous styles, from historical revivals to "pop-art" decor. The Blessed Sacrament Chapel features mosaic from known artist Fr. Marko Ivan Rupnik.</p>
<p>The Neo-Romanesque crypt houses a 16th-century image of the <i>Virgen de la Almudena</i>. Nearby along the Calle Mayor excavations have unearthed remains of Moorish and medieval city walls.</p>
<p>On April 28, 2004, Cardinal <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Mar%C3%ADa_Rouco_Varela" title="Antonio Mar√≠a Rouco Varela">Antonio Mar√≠a Rouco Varela</a>, Archbishop of Madrid, blessed the new paintings in the apse, painted by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kiko_Arguello" title="Kiko Arguello">Kiko Arguello</a>, founder of the <a href="/wiki/Neocatechumenal_Way" title="Neocatechumenal Way">Neocatechumenal Way</a>.</p>
