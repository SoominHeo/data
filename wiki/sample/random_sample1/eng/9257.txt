<p><b>Álvaro Negredo Sánchez</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[ˈalβaɾo neˈɣɾeðo ˈsantʃeθ]</a></span>; born 20 August 1985) is a Spanish professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for English club <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a> on loan from <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF" title="Valencia CF">Valencia CF</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>.</p>
<p>Nicknamed <i>La fiera de Vallecas</i> (The beast of <a href="/wiki/Vallecas" title="Vallecas">Vallecas</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> his early career was associated with <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a>, although he could never become a part of its first-team setup, going on to represent other clubs in his country, with his longest spell being at <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla</a>. Over the course of eight seasons, he amassed <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> totals of 264 games and 112 goals.</p>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spanish international</a> since 2009, Negredo was part of the team that won <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">Euro 2012</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81lvaro_Negredo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Real_Madrid">Real Madrid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81lvaro_Negredo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Real Madrid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, Negredo started his career at local <a href="/wiki/Rayo_Vallecano" title="Rayo Vallecano">Rayo Vallecano</a>, making his professional debuts in early 2005 with the club in <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="Segunda División B">Segunda División B</a>. In the middle of the year he switched to another side in the country's capital, ending his training in <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a> affiliate <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_Castilla" title="Real Madrid Castilla">Real Madrid Castilla</a>.</p>
<p>Negredo developed as a striker in <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2006–07 Segunda División">his last season</a>, scoring 18 league goals although he could not prevent Castilla's drop from <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">Segunda División</a>. He did manage to impress first-team coach <a href="/wiki/Fabio_Capello" title="Fabio Capello">Fabio Capello</a> and was called up for a few games in the <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a>, but remained an unused <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a>; finally, he made his debut for the main squad in a <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_game" title="Exhibition game">friendly</a> against <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Almer.C3.ADa">Almería</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81lvaro_Negredo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Almería">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2007, Negredo was sold to <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> newcomers <a href="/wiki/UD_Almer%C3%ADa" title="UD Almería">UD Almería</a>, with Real Madrid having an option to rebuy. He made his top flight debut on 26 August in a 3–0 shock win at <a href="/wiki/Deportivo_de_La_Coru%C3%B1a" title="Deportivo de La Coruña">Deportivo de La Coruña</a>; on 2 February 2008, he scored from the <a href="/wiki/Penalty_kick_(association_football)" title="Penalty kick (association football)">penalty spot</a> in a 2–0 home win against his former team<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and, on 19 April, he added two – after having missed a penalty kick – in a 4–1 away success against <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a> and Spanish Cup holders <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla FC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Negredo finished <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_La_Liga" title="2007–08 La Liga">the campaign</a> as Almería's topscorer with 13 goals, as the side from <a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">Andalusia</a> finished eighth. In <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_La_Liga" title="2008–09 La Liga">2008–09</a> he netted five in the team's first six matches, including a <a href="/wiki/Last-minute_goal" title="Last-minute goal">95th-minute</a> winner against neighbours <a href="/wiki/Recreativo_de_Huelva" title="Recreativo de Huelva">Recreativo de Huelva</a> (1–0) on 28 September 2008; in February of the following year he scored a brace against <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF" title="Valencia CF">Valencia CF</a>, which took his league tally to ten, albeit in a 2–3 away loss,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and finished with 19 successful strikes, with the club producing a slightly less successful season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Real_Madrid">Return to Real Madrid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81lvaro_Negredo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Return to Real Madrid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Real Madrid exercised their buyback option of a reported <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>5 million on Negredo in June 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and the player returned to training with the club on 10 July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F._season_2009%E2%80%9310#Pre-season" title="Real Madrid C.F. season 2009–10">pre-season</a> he scored the fourth goal against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LDU_Quito" title="LDU Quito">LDU Quito</a>, in a <a href="/wiki/2009_Peace_Cup" title="2009 Peace Cup">4–2 win</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Peace_Cup" title="Peace Cup">Peace Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Negredo had been tipped to join <a href="/wiki/Real_Zaragoza" title="Real Zaragoza">Real Zaragoza</a> or <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a>, but finally decided to sign a reported five-year deal with Sevilla for €15 million, with Real Madrid having an option to rebuy the player in the first two years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> New club manager <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Pellegrini" title="Manuel Pellegrini">Manuel Pellegrini</a> could not guarantee him first-team football with the likes of <a href="/wiki/Karim_Benzema" title="Karim Benzema">Karim Benzema</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gonzalo_Higua%C3%ADn" title="Gonzalo Higuaín">Gonzalo Higuaín</a>, <a href="/wiki/Klaas-Jan_Huntelaar" title="Klaas-Jan Huntelaar">Klaas-Jan Huntelaar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ruud_van_Nistelrooy" title="Ruud van Nistelrooy">Ruud van Nistelrooy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cristiano_Ronaldo" title="Cristiano Ronaldo">Cristiano Ronaldo</a> in the squad, and subsequently advised him to leave and join Sevilla in order to fulfill his potential; Negredo later revealed his admiration for Pellegrini's honesty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sevilla">Sevilla</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81lvaro_Negredo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sevilla">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Negredo made his debut for Sevilla on 31 August 2009 against Valencia, coming as a substitute in the 37th minute on a 0–2 away loss. Two weeks later he scored his first goal, against <a href="/wiki/CA_Osasuna" title="CA Osasuna">CA Osasuna</a> in a 2–0 away success.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>A starter throughout most of the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_La_Liga" title="2009–10 La Liga">2009–10 season</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Lu%C3%ADs_Fabiano" title="Luís Fabiano">Luís Fabiano</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Kanout%C3%A9" title="Frédéric Kanouté">Frédéric Kanouté</a> fighting for the other striker berth, Negredo's first year was highly irregular. He suffered a scoring drought that lasted (in the league alone) 12 games, only ending on 2 May 2010 in a 3–1 home win against Atlético Madrid, his two goals coming from penalties (he had already lost his starting position at the time, but the <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Malian</a> was suspended for that game); he added another brace the following match, a 5–1 away routing of <a href="/wiki/Racing_de_Santander" title="Racing de Santander">Racing de Santander</a> for a total of 11 in the league, grabbing another in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>; he was also <a href="/wiki/Ejection_(sports)" title="Ejection (sports)">sent off</a> three times in the season, the last of which almost prevented his participation in the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Copa_del_Rey" title="2009–10 Copa del Rey">domestic cup final</a> against Atlético Madrid after insulting the <a href="/wiki/Assistant_referee_(association_football)" title="Assistant referee (association football)">assistant referee</a> in Sevilla's 3–2 win at former club Almería (the ban was later lifted and he was allowed to play – Fabiano was unavailable for the match through injury – in the <a href="/wiki/2010_Copa_del_Rey_Final" title="2010 Copa del Rey Final">2–0 final win</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<p>Negredo was an undisputed first-choice for Sevilla in the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_La_Liga" title="2010–11 La Liga">2010–11 campaign</a> – Fabiano also returned to <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_FC" title="São Paulo FC">São Paulo FC</a> in March 2011 – scoring 27 official goals, 20 in the league. Highlights included braces against <a href="/wiki/Villarreal_CF" title="Villarreal CF">Villarreal CF</a> (3–3 away draw in <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Copa_del_Rey" title="2010–11 Copa del Rey">the cup</a>, with his team eventually reaching the semifinals), Deportivo (3–3 away draw<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>), Real Madrid (2–6 home loss,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>) Osasuna (2–3 loss, away<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>) and <a href="/wiki/RCD_Espanyol" title="RCD Espanyol">RCD Espanyol</a> (3–2 away win<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>).</p>
<p>Negredo took his <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_La_Liga" title="2012–13 La Liga">2012–13</a> league total to 13 on 4 March 2013, after netting a <a href="/wiki/Hat-trick" title="Hat-trick">hat-trick</a> in a 4–1 home success over <a href="/wiki/Celta_de_Vigo" title="Celta de Vigo">Celta de Vigo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He scored all of his team's goals on the last day of the season, a 4–3 home win against <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF" title="Valencia CF">Valencia CF</a>, clinching the <a href="/wiki/Zarra_Trophy" title="Zarra Trophy">Zarra Trophy</a> in the process.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manchester_City">Manchester City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81lvaro_Negredo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Manchester City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 19 July 2013, <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a> announced the signing of Negredo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The fee was reported as <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>16.4 million plus add-ons,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> and he signed a four-year deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> He moved to the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club shortly after teammate <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_Navas" title="Jesús Navas">Jesús Navas</a>.</p>
<p>Negredo made his league debut on 19 August 2013, coming on as a substitute for countryman <a href="/wiki/David_Silva" title="David Silva">David Silva</a> in a 4–0 home win against <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a>. He scored his first goal for the club the following match on 25 August, netting from a header in a 2–3 defeat at newly promoted <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> He scored his first goal at the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Manchester_Stadium" title="City of Manchester Stadium">City of Manchester Stadium</a> in the next round, a 2–0 home success over Hull City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 5 November 2013, Negredo scored his first hat-trick for City, contributing to a <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2013–14 UEFA Champions League">5–2 group stage home win</a> against <a href="/wiki/PFC_CSKA_Moscow" title="PFC CSKA Moscow">PFC CSKA Moscow</a> which qualified the former to the round-of-16.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> This was the first-ever time a Manchester City player had scored <a href="/wiki/List_of_UEFA_Champions_League_hat-tricks" title="List of UEFA Champions League hat-tricks">three goals in the same match in the Champions League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> He repeated the feat on 8 January of the following year, contributing decisively to a 6–0 home routing of <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a> in the first leg of the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Football_League_Cup" title="2013–14 Football League Cup">Football League Cup</a> semi-final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite not scoring for City since January 2014, Negredo finished <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Manchester_City_F.C._season" title="2013–14 Manchester City F.C. season">the season</a> with 23 goals in all competitions comprised in 48 appearances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Valencia">Valencia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81lvaro_Negredo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Valencia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 1 September 2014, Negredo signed a loan deal with an obligatory purchase clause with Valencia, which required the club to purchase his rights at the end of <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_La_Liga" title="2014–15 La Liga">the campaign</a>, for an amount believed to be around €27 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> He scored his first competitive goal on 7 December, starting in a 1–1 away draw against <a href="/wiki/Granada_CF" title="Granada CF">Granada CF</a> which was also his first appearance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Negredo joined Valencia on a permanent basis on 1 July 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> On 25 August, he helped his team <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2015–16 UEFA Champions League">reach the Champions League group phase</a> by netting in the fourth minute of an eventual 1–2 loss at <a href="/wiki/AS_Monaco_FC" title="AS Monaco FC">AS Monaco FC</a> (4–3 on aggregate).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2015, after criticising manager <a href="/wiki/Nuno_Esp%C3%ADrito_Santo" title="Nuno Espírito Santo">Nuno Espírito Santo</a>'s choice of tactics, Negredo was completely ostracized.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> He again became an option under new boss <a href="/wiki/Gary_Neville" title="Gary Neville">Gary Neville</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> notably scoring three times in a 4–0 home win over Granada for <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Copa_del_Rey" title="2015–16 Copa del Rey">the domestic cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 20 July 2016, Negredo joined <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a> on loan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> He scored in his first appearance, putting the hosts ahead in a 1–1 home draw against <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81lvaro_Negredo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>On 6 October 2009, Negredo received his first call to the <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spanish senior team</a>, for a <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">qualifier</a> against <a href="/wiki/Armenia_national_football_team" title="Armenia national football team">Armenia</a> on the 10th, following the injuries of <a href="/wiki/David_Villa" title="David Villa">David Villa</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daniel_G%C3%BCiza" title="Daniel Güiza">Daniel Güiza</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> He made his debut in that game, replacing <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Torres" title="Fernando Torres">Fernando Torres</a> early into the second half of an eventual 2–1 away win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>Four days later, Negredo started and scored twice – also providing two <a href="/wiki/Assist_(football)" title="Assist (football)">assists</a> – in another away fixture, against <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina_national_football_team" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina national football team">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a> (5–2 triumph), as Spain eventually won all ten group matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> He was, however, overlooked for the final stages in <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, with the national team winning the tournament.</p>
<p>Negredo was chosen by manager <a href="/wiki/Vicente_del_Bosque" title="Vicente del Bosque">Vicente del Bosque</a> for his <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">UEFA Euro 2012</a> squad. He played twice in the tournament in <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, including one start against <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a> in the semifinals (4–2 <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shoot-out_(association_football)" title="Penalty shoot-out (association football)">penalty shootout</a> win, 0–0 after 120 minutes).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>Negredo was one of seven players cut from Spain's final squad for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 World Cup</a>, alongside City teammate Navas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
