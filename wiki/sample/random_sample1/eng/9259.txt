<p><b>Albin Lermusiaux</b> (9 August 1874 – 1940) was a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> runner and <a href="/wiki/Shooting" title="Shooting">shooter</a> who competed at the <a href="/wiki/1896_Summer_Olympics" title="1896 Summer Olympics">1896 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>.</p>
<p>He won his preliminary heat of the 800 metres with a time of 2:16.6, but withdrew from the final. In the 1,500 metres event, which was conducted in a single race, Lermusiaux led from the start, but in the final straight was passed by <a href="/wiki/Edwin_Flack" title="Edwin Flack">Edwin Flack</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Blake_(middle-distance_runner)" title="Arthur Blake (middle-distance runner)">Arthur Blake</a>, and finished third. Lermusiaux also led the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marathon_(sport)" title="Marathon (sport)">marathon</a> for 32 kilometres, but then abandoned the race due to exhaustion. In the military rifle shooting event, Lermusiaux's place and score are unknown; he was not among the top 13 in the 42-man competitions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-r1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Domestically Lermusiaux won the French championships in cross country (1895) and 1500 m (1896) and set national records over 1500 m (in 1895 at 4:18.4 and in 1896 at 4:10.4), 1 mile (in 1895 at 4:37.4), and 3000 m (in 1895 at 9:22.4).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r1_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-r1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
