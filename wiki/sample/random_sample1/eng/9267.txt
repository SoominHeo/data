<p><b>Beta Aquilae</b> (<b>β Aquilae</b>, abbreviated <b>Beta Aql</b>, <b>β Aql</b>), also named <b>Alshain</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAU-CSN_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-IAU-CSN-4">[4]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Star" title="Star">star</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Aquila_(constellation)" title="Aquila (constellation)">Aquila</a>.</p>
<p>It has <a href="/wiki/Apparent_magnitude" title="Apparent magnitude">magnitude</a> 3.71 and is of <a href="/wiki/Stellar_classification" title="Stellar classification">spectral class</a> G8IV. It has a very low level of <a href="/wiki/Stellar_magnetic_field" title="Stellar magnetic field">surface magnetic activity</a> and may be in a state similar to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maunder_minimum" title="Maunder minimum">Maunder minimum</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Andretta2005_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Andretta2005-5">[5]</a></sup> Since 1943, the <a href="/wiki/Spectrum" title="Spectrum">spectrum</a> of this star has served as one of the stable anchor points by which other stars are classified.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baas25_1319_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-baas25_1319-6">[6]</a></sup> It is approximately 44.7 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_years" title="Light years">light years</a> from Earth. It has a 12th magnitude optical companion, <b>Beta Aquilae B</b>, which is 13 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arcsecond" title="Arcsecond">arcseconds</a> away in the sky.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nomenclature">Nomenclature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beta_Aquilae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Nomenclature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>β Aquilae</i> (<a href="/wiki/Latinisation_of_names" title="Latinisation of names">Latinised</a> to <i>Beta Aquilae</i>) is the star's <a href="/wiki/Bayer_designation" title="Bayer designation">Bayer designation</a>.</p>
<p>It bore the traditional name <i>Alshain</i> derived from the Perso-<a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a> term الشاهين <i>aš-šāhīn</i> "the <a href="/wiki/Peregrine_falcon" title="Peregrine falcon">(peregrine) falcon</a>", perhaps by <a href="/wiki/Folk_etymology" title="Folk etymology">folk etymology</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a> <i>šāhīn tarāzū</i> (or possibly <i>šāhīn tara zed</i>; see <a href="/wiki/Gamma_Aquilae" title="Gamma Aquilae">Gamma Aquilae</a>), the Persian name for the <a href="/wiki/Asterism_(astronomy)" title="Asterism (astronomy)">asterism</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alpha_Aquilae" title="Alpha Aquilae">α</a>, β and <a href="/wiki/Gamma_Aquilae" title="Gamma Aquilae">γ Aquilae</a>. In 2016, the <a href="/wiki/International_Astronomical_Union" title="International Astronomical Union">International Astronomical Union</a> organized a <a href="/wiki/IAU_Working_Group_on_Star_Names" title="IAU Working Group on Star Names">Working Group on Star Names</a> (WGSN)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WGSN_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-WGSN-7">[7]</a></sup> to catalogue and standardize proper names for stars. The WGSN approved the name <i>Alshain</i> for this star on 21 August 2016 and it is now so entered in the IAU Catalog of Star Names.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAU-CSN_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-IAU-CSN-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the catalogue of stars in the <i>Calendarium</i> of <a href="/wiki/Al_Achsasi_al_Mouakket" title="Al Achsasi al Mouakket">Al Achsasi al Mouakket</a>, this star was designated <i>Unuk al Ghyrab</i> (<i>عنق ألغراب</i> - <i>únuq al-ghuraab</i>), which was translated into <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> as <i>Collum Corvi</i>, meaning <i>the crow's neck</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>, <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">河鼓</span> (<span lang="zh-Latn" xml:lang="zh-Latn">Hé Gŭ</span>), meaning <i><a href="/wiki/Ox_(Chinese_constellation)" title="Ox (Chinese constellation)">River Drum</a></i>, refers to an <a href="/wiki/Asterism_(astronomy)" title="Asterism (astronomy)">asterism</a> consisting of Beta Aquilae, <a href="/wiki/Altair" title="Altair">Altair</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gamma_Aquilae" title="Gamma Aquilae">Gamma Aquilae</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Consequently, Beta Aquilae itself is known as <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">河鼓一</span> (<span lang="zh-Latn" xml:lang="zh-Latn">Hé Gŭ yī</span>, English: the First Star of River Drum.)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Namesake">Namesake</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beta_Aquilae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Namesake">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/USS_Alshain_(AKA-55)" title="USS Alshain (AKA-55)">USS <i>Alshain</i> (AKA-55)</a> was a United States navy ship.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_culture">In culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beta_Aquilae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Chinese mythology, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Jade_Emperor#The_princess_and_the_cowherd" title="The Jade Emperor">The Princess and the Cowherd</a>, this star and Gamma Aquilae, are children of Niulang (牛郎, The Cowherd, <a href="/wiki/Altair" title="Altair">Altair</a>) and Zhinü (織女, The Princess, <a href="/wiki/Vega" title="Vega">Vega</a>).</p>
<p>The Koori people of <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria</a> knew Alshain and Gamma Aquilae as the <a href="/wiki/Black_swan" title="Black swan">black swan</a> wives of <i>Bunjil</i> (Altair), the <a href="/wiki/Wedge-tailed_eagle" title="Wedge-tailed eagle">wedge-tailed eagle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
