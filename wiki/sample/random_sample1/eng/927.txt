<p>"<b>Demon</b>" is a song by Korean-American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay_Park" title="Jay Park">Jay Park</a>. It was first released as a digital single on September 5, 2011 in South Korea,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-official-1">[1]</a></sup> then as a digital single on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> on September 13, 2011 worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-itunes_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-itunes-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Demon_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Demon" was written and produced by Teddy Riley, originally meant for <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It is an OST song for the upcoming movie <i>Hype Nation 3D</i> starring Park as the main villain, "Darkness".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-collaboration_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-collaboration-4">[4]</a></sup> The rap verse was written by Park himself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-official-1">[1]</a></sup> The song was first released in Korea, on various Korean digital music sites. It was then released worldwide on iTunes on September 13, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-itunes_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-itunes-2">[2]</a></sup> The version of the song released on iTunes is slightly different, with better sound quality, different cover art, and emphasises more on Park's voice during the rap part.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_Video">Music Video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Demon_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music Video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The music video for "Demon" was filmed in August, 2010 in South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The music video was released on September 5, 2011 on YouTube, along with the single. The choreographer is Andrew Baterina, a member of MTV ABDC’s runner-up <a href="/wiki/SoReal_Cru" title="SoReal Cru">SoReal Cru</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-collaboration_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-collaboration-4">[4]</a></sup> who has also choreographed Park's "Abandoned".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> It stars <a href="/wiki/Kim_Sa-rang_(actress)" title="Kim Sa-rang (actress)">Kim Sa-rang</a>, fellow <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SidusHQ" title="SidusHQ">SidusHQ</a> label mate, as the demon who tries to seduce Park.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> However, it actually took over a year to release the music video. Park himself commented on that, stating he had no control over the release of both the music video and the song, and didn't know himself when it was going to be released. He apologized on Twitter in both Korean and English for the low quality production of the music video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
