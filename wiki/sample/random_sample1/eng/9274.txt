<p>An <b>alcázar</b> (<span class="nowrap"><small>pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/æ/ short 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span></span> <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="/ˌ/ secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="'z' in 'zoom'">z</span><span title="/ɑːr/ 'ar' in 'far'">ɑːr</span></span>/</a></span></span>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a type of Moorish <a href="/wiki/Castle" title="Castle">castle</a> or <a href="/wiki/Palace" title="Palace">palace</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> built during <a href="/wiki/Al-Andalus" title="Al-Andalus">Muslim rule</a>, although some were founded by <a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">Christians</a> and others were built on earlier Roman or Visigothic fortifications. Most of the alcázars were built between the 8th and 15th centuries. Many cities in Spain have an <i>alcázar</i>. The term is sometimes used as a synonym for "castillo" or castle; palaces or forts built by Christian rulers were also often called <i>alcázars</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminology">Terminology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alc%C3%A1zar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Terminology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Spanish word <i>alcázar</i> (<small>pronounced: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[alˈkaθar]</a></span>) derives from the <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a> word <span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">القصر</span> <a href="/wiki/Ksar" title="Ksar"><i>al-qasr</i></a> "the fort, castle, or palace", which is possibly in turn derived from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> word <i><a href="/wiki/Castra" title="Castra">castrum</a></i>, meaning an army camp or fort.</p>
<p>Similar words exist in Galician (<i>alcázar</i>, <small>pronounced: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Galician" title="Help:IPA for Galician">[alˈkaθaɾ]</a></span>), Portuguese (<i>alcácer</i>, <small>pronounced: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">[ɐɫˈkasɛɾ]</a></span>), and Catalan (<i>alcàsser</i>, <small>pronounced: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Catalan" title="Help:IPA for Catalan">[əɫˈkasər]</a></span>).</p>
<p>Spain also has <a href="/wiki/Moors" title="Moors">Moorish</a> citadels known as <a href="/wiki/Alcazaba" title="Alcazaba">alcazabas</a> (<span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">القصبة</span> <a href="/wiki/Kasbah" title="Kasbah"><i>al-qasbah</i></a>). However, not all castles in Spain are called <i>alcázar</i>: the majority are called <i>castillo</i>. Nor was every alcázar or alcazaba in Iberia built by the Moors: many castles with these names were built after the Moors had withdrawn from the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Landmark_alc.C3.A1zars">Landmark alcázars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alc%C3%A1zar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Landmark alcázars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alc%C3%A1zar_of_Segovia" title="Alcázar of Segovia">Alcázar of Segovia</a> was first cited in the 12th century, though its foundations date back to Roman times. It was built by the Christian monarchs in the place of a Moorish fort. During the Middle Ages when in the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile" title="Kingdom of Castile">Kingdom of Castile</a>, the alcázar of <a href="/wiki/Segovia" title="Segovia">Segovia</a> was the favorite residence of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Castilian_monarchs" title="List of Castilian monarchs">Castilian monarchs</a>, and almost each king added new parts to the building, transforming the original fortress into a courtier residence and prolonging the construction of the castle until the 16th century, when king <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Spain" title="Philip II of Spain">Philip II</a> added the conical spires and the slate roofs. A fire in 1862 destroyed part of the roofs, but they were restored in the very same style in which they were built 300 years before. This Alcázar was also the place where in 1492 Ferdinand and Isabella signed the Jewish expulsion order from Spain.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alc%C3%A1zar_of_Toledo" title="Alcázar of Toledo">Alcázar of Toledo</a> was used as a military academy in modern times. The <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_the_Alc%C3%A1zar" title="Siege of the Alcázar">Siege of the Alcázar</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War" title="Spanish Civil War">Spanish Civil War</a> references this castle, which was held by the <a href="/wiki/Francoist_Spain" title="Francoist Spain">Nationalist</a> colonel <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Moscard%C3%B3_Ituarte" title="José Moscardó Ituarte">José Moscardó Ituarte</a> against <a href="/wiki/Second_Spanish_Republic" title="Second Spanish Republic">Republican</a> forces. Republican forces captured Moscardó's 24-year-old son Luis, and on 23 July 1936 informed Moscardó that if he did not turn over the alcázar within ten minutes his son would die. When Moscardó did not surrender, Luis was murdered, not immediately but one month later, on 23 August.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alc%C3%A1zar_of_Seville" title="Alcázar of Seville">Alcázar of Seville</a>, on the site of the palace of the <a href="/wiki/Almohad_Caliphate" title="Almohad Caliphate">Almohad Caliphate</a> called <i>al-Muwarak</i>, was built in the 1360s by Moorish craftsmen in <a href="/wiki/Mud%C3%A9jar" title="Mudéjar">Mudéjar</a> style, and frequently remodeled. It was first used by <a href="/wiki/Peter_of_Castile" title="Peter of Castile">Peter of Castile</a> with his mistress <a href="/wiki/Mar%C3%ADa_de_Padilla" title="María de Padilla">María de Padilla</a>. The structures and gardens are a UNESCO <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alc%C3%A1zar_de_los_Reyes_Cristianos" title="Alcázar de los Reyes Cristianos">Alcázar de los Reyes Cristianos</a>, also called "the Alcázar of Córdoba", in <a href="/wiki/C%C3%B3rdoba,_Andalusia" title="Córdoba, Andalusia">Córdoba, Andalusia</a> was a Moorish palace after the 13th century <a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">Reconquista</a> of Córdoba. The Moors had expanded a Visigoth fortress into a large compound with gardens and a large library.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-infocordoba_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-infocordoba-3">[3]</a></sup> This alcázar was the summer home of King <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_II_of_Aragon" title="Ferdinand II of Aragon">Ferdinand</a> and Queen <a href="/wiki/Isabella_I_of_Castile" title="Isabella I of Castile">Isabella</a>, and the site of their meeting with <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> before his famous voyage to the Americas.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alc%C3%A1zar_of_Jerez_de_la_Frontera" title="Alcázar of Jerez de la Frontera">Alcázar of Jerez de la Frontera</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Castle_of_Burgos" title="Castle of Burgos">Castle of Burgos</a>, today just remains of that was once an alcázar and a royal residence.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Disappeared_landmark_alc.C3.A1zars">Disappeared landmark alcázars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alc%C3%A1zar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Disappeared landmark alcázars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Royal_Alcazar_of_Madrid" title="Royal Alcazar of Madrid">Royal Alcazar of Madrid</a> was a palace built by Emperor Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor, (rebuilt by his son, Philip II)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and was the main royal residence in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> until the <a href="/wiki/Buen_Retiro_Palace" title="Buen Retiro Palace">Buen Retiro Palace</a> partly superseded it in the 17th century. It was destroyed by fire in 1734, and the present <a href="/wiki/Royal_Palace_of_Madrid" title="Royal Palace of Madrid">Royal Palace of Madrid</a> was built on the site. This was called <i>Palacio Nuevo</i> and has never been called alcázar.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Castle_Alc%C3%A1zar_of_Segorbe" title="Castle Alcázar of Segorbe">Castle Alcázar of Segorbe</a>, enormous complex that for over a thousand years was the residence of the most important lords, dukes and kings.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Outside_Spain">Outside Spain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alc%C3%A1zar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Outside Spain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Outside Spain, in <a href="/wiki/Palermo" title="Palermo">Palermo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>, the district called <i>Cassaro</i> corresponds to the <a href="/wiki/Punics" title="Punics">Punic</a> settlement of Zis, on high ground that was refortified by Arabs and known as <i>al-qaṣr</i>, and was further expanded as the site of the later Norman palace.</p>
<p>In Portugal there is a city called <a href="/wiki/Alc%C3%A1cer_do_Sal" title="Alcácer do Sal">Alcácer do Sal</a> that was an administrative regional seat for the Moors of <a href="/wiki/Al-Andalus" title="Al-Andalus">al-Andalus</a>.</p>
<p>The former colonial palace in <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo" title="Santo Domingo">Santo Domingo</a>, originally built for <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a>'s son <a href="/wiki/Diego_Columbus" title="Diego Columbus">Diego</a> in 1509, is commonly known as the <a href="/wiki/Alc%C3%A1zar_de_Col%C3%B3n" title="Alcázar de Colón">Alcázar de Colón</a> ("Columbus's alcázar") and is built in the Andalusian style.</p>
