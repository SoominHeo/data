<p><i><b>R-Point</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">알 포인트</span>) is a 2004 Korean <a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">horror film</a> written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Kong_Su-chang" title="Kong Su-chang">Kong Su-chang</a>. Set in 1972 <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a>, it stars <a href="/wiki/Kam_Woo-sung" title="Kam Woo-sung">Kam Woo-sung</a> and <a href="/wiki/Son_Byong-ho" title="Son Byong-ho">Son Byong-ho</a> as members of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_history_of_South_Korea_during_the_Vietnam_War" title="Military history of South Korea during the Vietnam War">South Korean military in Vietnam</a>. Most of the movie was shot in <a href="/wiki/Cambodia" title="Cambodia">Cambodia</a>. <a href="/wiki/Bokor_Hill_Station" title="Bokor Hill Station">Bokor Hill Station</a> plays a prominent part of the movie, in this case doubling as a colonial French plantation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R-Point&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 7 January 1972, the South Korean base in Nha-Trang, Vietnam, receives a radio transmission from a missing platoon that has been presumed dead. The high-command assigns the veteran and decorated Lieutenant Choi Tae-in to lead a squad of eight other soldiers to extract the missing soldiers from the Romeo point or R-Point. When they arrive at the location, they engage and defeat a Vietnamese woman with a machine gun. They later find a tombstone saying that one hundred years previously, local Vietnamese were killed by the Chinese who disposed of them in a lake. A temple built over the site has become a sacred location to the Vietnamese. They find an immense, but empty mansion, where they set up their base. They have a week to find the missing soldiers, but time is passing slowly when one of them dies in a horrific way, and instead of finding answers, they encounter further mysteries.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R-Point&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before the film was released, filmmakers conducted <a href="/wiki/Viral_marketing" title="Viral marketing">viral marketing</a> to promote the film. The official website, www.rpoint.com, carried several fictional articles such as a journal written by an American <a href="/wiki/War_correspondent" title="War correspondent">war correspondent</a>, statements made by various soldiers who witnessed events portrayed in the film, radio transmissions supposedly received by Korean soldiers, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internet_news" title="Internet news">Internet news</a> links about missing Korean soldiers in Vietnam, and a fictional timeline of R-Point.</p>
