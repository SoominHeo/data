<p><b>Alps Electric Co., Ltd.</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">アルプス電気株式会社</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">Hepburn</a>: <i>Arupusu Denki Kabushiki-gaisha</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational corporation</a>, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, producing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electronic_device" title="Electronic device">electronic devices</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Switch" title="Switch">switches</a>, <a href="/wiki/Potentiometer" title="Potentiometer">potentiometers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sensor" title="Sensor">sensors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Encoder" title="Encoder">encoders</a> and <a href="/wiki/Touchpad" title="Touchpad">touchpads</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The company was established in 1948 as Kataoka Electric Co., Ltd. and changed its name to Alps Electric Co., Ltd. in December 1964. Since June 22, 2012, the President has been Toshihiro Kuriyama and Chairman is Masataka Kataoka.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alps_Electric_Corporate_Facts_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Alps_Electric_Corporate_Facts-1">[1]</a></sup> Alps is also well known for the <a href="/wiki/Alpine_Electronics" title="Alpine Electronics">Alpine</a> brand of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Car_audio" title="Car audio">car audio</a>.</p>
<p>The Alps Electric Group has R&amp;D, production and sales bases located in Japan and around the globe—in the Americas, Europe, Southeast Asia, Korea, and Greater China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Since its founding, Alps Electric has supplied around 40,000 types of electronic component to over 2,000 manufacturers of home appliances, mobile devices, automobiles and industrial equipment worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Alps Group comprises 84 <a href="/wiki/Subsidiary" title="Subsidiary">subsidiary</a> companies, 25 through Alps Electric, 32 through Alpine Electronics and 27 through Alps Logistics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The company is listed on the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Stock_Exchange" title="Tokyo Stock Exchange">Tokyo Stock Exchange</a> and a constituent of the <a href="/wiki/Nikkei_225" title="Nikkei 225">Nikkei 225</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stock_index" title="Stock index">stock index</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_divisions">Business divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alps_Electric&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Business divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Alps Automotive</b> division focuses on provision of custom products and modules, including control panels and steering modules, for specific vehicle models, and components compatible with any vehicle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alps_Business_Areas_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Alps_Business_Areas-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Alps Home and Mobile</b> divisions focuses on provision of switches, potentiometers, sensors, and other components through to multi-input devices like touch panels and GlidePoint™ to home, mobile and PC markets. Alps Electric focuses on human-machine and machine-machine interfaces for home appliances, mobile devices and PCs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alps_Business_Areas_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Alps_Business_Areas-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Alps Industry, Healthcare &amp; Energy</b> divisions focuses on provision of a wide variety of products, including sensors, power inductors, switches and communication modules, to industry, healthcare and energy markets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alps_Business_Areas_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Alps_Business_Areas-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Alps touchpad hardware is developed and manufactured by the <a href="/wiki/Cirque_Corporation" title="Cirque Corporation">Cirque Corporation</a>, which they acquired in 2003;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cirque_Corporation_website_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cirque_Corporation_website-9">[9]</a></sup> however, the parent company continues to write their own drivers. Their drivers are <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Windows</a> certified. They are mostly found in <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toshiba" title="Toshiba">Toshiba</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dell" title="Dell">Dell</a> <a href="/wiki/Laptop" title="Laptop">notebooks</a>, as well as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OLPC_XO-1" title="OLPC XO-1">OLPC XO-1</a>. They also manufacture keyboards for Apple computers, including the original Macintosh and the first <a href="/wiki/IMac" title="IMac">iMac</a>.</p>
