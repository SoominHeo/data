<p>The <b>Conquest of al-Hasa</b> was achieved by the Saudi forces of <a href="/wiki/Ibn_Saud" title="Ibn Saud">ibn Saud</a> with support from the <a href="/wiki/Ikhwan" title="Ikhwan">Ikhwan</a> in 1913.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jones_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-jones-1">[1]</a></sup> The Oasis of al-Hasa was conquered from an <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> garrison, who had controlled the area from 1871.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although <a href="/wiki/Shia_Islam" title="Shia Islam">Shia Islam</a> religious community leaders negotiated a quick surrender and recognition of the <a href="/wiki/Sunni_Islam" title="Sunni Islam">Sunni Islam</a> Saudi political authority in exchange for leniency, the <a href="/wiki/Shia_Islam_in_Saudi_Arabia" title="Shia Islam in Saudi Arabia">Shia communities in al-Hasa and Qatif</a> were subjected to harsh oversight and cruelty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jones_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-jones-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
