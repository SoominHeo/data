<p><b>Algeciras</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˌ/ secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="/æ/ short 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/dʒ/ 'j' in 'jam'">dʒ</span><span title="/ɪ/ or /ə/ 'e' in 'roses'">ᵻ</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[alxeˈθiɾas]</a></span>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar"><i>Al Jazīra Al-Khadrā</i> الجزيرة الخضراء</span>‎‎) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_port_cities_of_the_Mediterranean_Sea" title="List of port cities of the Mediterranean Sea">port city</a> in the south of <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, and is the largest city on the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Gibraltar" title="Bay of Gibraltar">Bay of Gibraltar</a> (in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>, the <i>Bahía de Algeciras</i>). The <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Algeciras" title="Port of Algeciras">Port of Algeciras</a> is <a href="/wiki/List_of_busiest_ports_in_Europe" title="List of busiest ports in Europe">one of the largest ports in Europe</a> and in the world in three categories: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_world%27s_busiest_container_ports" title="List of world's busiest container ports">container</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_world%27s_busiest_ports_by_cargo_tonnage" title="List of world's busiest ports by cargo tonnage">cargo</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_world%27s_busiest_transshipment_ports" title="List of world's busiest transshipment ports">transhipment</a>. It is situated 20 km north-east of <a href="/wiki/Tarifa" title="Tarifa">Tarifa</a> on the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_de_la_Miel" title="Río de la Miel">Río de la Miel</a>, which is the southernmost river of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iberian_peninsula" title="Iberian peninsula">Iberian peninsula</a> and continental <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. In 2015, it had a population of 118,920.</p>
<p>It is the biggest city among its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_metropolitan_areas_in_Spain_by_population" title="List of metropolitan areas in Spain by population">metropolitan area</a>, that also includes the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Los_Barrios" title="Los Barrios">Los Barrios</a>, <a href="/wiki/La_L%C3%ADnea_de_la_Concepci%C3%B3n" title="La Línea de la Concepción">La Línea de la Concepción</a>, <a href="/wiki/Castellar_de_la_Frontera" title="Castellar de la Frontera">Castellar de la Frontera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jimena_de_la_Frontera" title="Jimena de la Frontera">Jimena de la Frontera</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Roque,_C%C3%A1diz" title="San Roque, Cádiz">San Roque</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tarifa" title="Tarifa">Tarifa</a>, with a population of 263,739.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algeciras&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The site of Roman cities called <i>Portus Albus</i>, <i>Caetaria</i> (current Getares) and <i>Iuliua Tracta</i>, the current name of <b>Algeciras</b> comes from the Arab period of the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a>: Al-Jazīra Al-Khadrā' <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a> <b>الجزيرة الخضراء</b> or Green Island.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algeciras&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of the city has been populated since prehistory, and the earliest remains belong to <a href="/wiki/Neanderthal" title="Neanderthal">Neanderthal</a> populations from the <a href="/wiki/Paleolithic" title="Paleolithic">Paleolithic</a>.</p>
<p>Due to its strategic position it was an important port under the <a href="/wiki/Phoenicia" title="Phoenicia">Phoenicians</a>, and was the site of the relevant Roman port of <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Portus_Albus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Portus Albus (page does not exist)">Portus Albus</a></i> ("White Port"), with two nearby cities called <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Caetaria&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Caetaria (page does not exist)">Caetaria</a></i> (most likely <a href="/wiki/Iberians" title="Iberians">Iberians</a>) and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iulia_Transducta" title="Iulia Transducta">Iulia Transducta</a></i>, founded by the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Romans</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-O.27Shea1865_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-O.27Shea1865-3">[3]</a></sup> Recently it has been proposed that the site of <i>Iulia Transducta</i> was the <i>Villa Vieja</i> of Algeciras.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>After being destroyed by the <a href="/wiki/Migration_Period" title="Migration Period">Germanic Barbarians</a> and their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vandal" title="Vandal">Vandal</a> allies, the city was founded again in April 711 by the invading <a href="/wiki/Moors" title="Moors">Moors</a>, as the first city created by the Amazigh (Berbers) on the occupied <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> soil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Livermore2006_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Livermore2006-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the year 859 viking troops on board of 62 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drekar" title="Drekar">drekars</a> commanded by the leaders <a href="/wiki/Hastein" title="Hastein">Hastein</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rn_Ironside" title="Björn Ironside">Björn Ironside</a> besieged the city during three days and raided it. After looting the houses of the rich, they burnt the Aljama mosque and the Banderas mosque. Reorganized near the medina, the inhabitants managed to recover the city and make the invaders run away, capturing two boats.</p>
<p>It enjoyed a brief period of independence as a <a href="/wiki/Taifa" title="Taifa">taifa</a> state from 1035-1058. It was named <i>al-Jazirah al-Khadra'</i> ("Green Island") after the offshore <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Isla_Verde,_Algeciras&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Isla Verde, Algeciras (page does not exist)">Isla Verde</a>; the modern name is derived from this original <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a> name (compare also <a href="/wiki/Algiers" title="Algiers">Algiers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Al_Jazeera" title="Al Jazeera">Al Jazeera</a>).</p>
<p>In 1278, Algeciras was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siege_of_Algeciras_(1278)" title="Siege of Algeciras (1278)">besieged by the forces of the Kingdom of Castile</a> under the command of <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_X_of_Castile" title="Alfonso X of Castile">Alfonso X of Castile</a> and his son, <a href="/wiki/Sancho_IV_of_Castile" title="Sancho IV of Castile">Sancho IV</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rogers2010_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rogers2010-7">[7]</a></sup> This siege was the first of a series of attempts to take the city and ended in failure for the Castilian forces. An armada sent by Castile was also annihilated whilst trying to <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Algeciras_(1278)" title="Battle of Algeciras (1278)">blockade the city's harbor</a>.</p>

<p>After many centuries of Muslim rule, the tide of the <a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">reconquista</a> arrived at Algeciras. In July 1309 <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_IV_of_Castile" title="Ferdinand IV of Castile">Ferdinand IV of Castile</a> laid <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siege_of_Algeciras_(1309)" title="Siege of Algeciras (1309)">siege to Algeciras</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rogers2010_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rogers2010-7">[7]</a></sup> The latter fell into Christian hands, but Muslim Algeciras held on for the following three decades, until <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alfonso_XI" title="Alfonso XI">Alfonso XI</a> of <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">Castile</a> resumed its siege. <a href="/wiki/Juan_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_III_de_Lara" title="Juan Núñez III de Lara">Juan Nunez de Lara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel,_Prince_of_Villena" title="Juan Manuel, Prince of Villena">Juan Manuel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Fern%C3%A1ndez_de_Castro" title="Pedro Fernández de Castro">Pedro Fernández de Castro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Alfonso_de_la_Cerda" title="Juan Alfonso de la Cerda">Juan Alfonso de la Cerda</a>, lord of <a href="/wiki/Gibrale%C3%B3n" title="Gibraleón">Gibraleón</a> all participated in the siege, as did knights from France, England and Germany, and even King <a href="/wiki/Philip_III_of_Navarre" title="Philip III of Navarre">Philip III of Navarre</a>, king consort of Navarra, who came accompanied by 100 horsemen and 300 infantry. In March 1344, after several years of siege, Algeciras surrendered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-O.27Shea1865_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-O.27Shea1865-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On winning the city, Alfonso XI made it the seat of a new <a href="/wiki/Diocese" title="Diocese">diocese</a>, established by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Clement_VI" title="Pope Clement VI">Pope Clement VI</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Papal_bull" title="Papal bull">bull</a> <i>Gaudemus et exultamus</i> of 30 April 1344, and entrusted to the governance of the bishop of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Cadiz_y_Ceuta" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Cadiz y Ceuta">Cadiz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The bishops of Cadiz continued to hold the title of Aliezira, as it called, until 1851, when in accordance with a <a href="/wiki/Concordat" title="Concordat">concordat</a> between Spain and the Holy See its territory was incorporated into the diocese of Cadiz. No longer a residential bishopric, Aliezira is today listed by the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Titular_see" title="Titular see">titular see</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The city was retaken by the Moors in 1368. It was destroyed on the orders of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muhammed_V" title="Muhammed V">Muhammed V</a> of Granada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChaucerAndrew1993_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ChaucerAndrew1993-10">[10]</a></sup> The site was subsequently abandoned, but was refounded in 1704 by refugees from Gibraltar following the territory's capture by Anglo-Dutch forces in the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Spanish_Succession" title="War of the Spanish Succession">War of the Spanish Succession</a>. It was fortified to guard against British raids with installations such as the <a href="/wiki/Fuerte_de_Isla_Verde" title="Fuerte de Isla Verde">Fuerte de Isla Verde</a> built to guard key points. The city was rebuilt on its present rectangular plan by <a href="/wiki/Charles_III_of_Spain" title="Charles III of Spain">Charles III of Spain</a> in 1760. In July 1801, the French and Spanish navies fought the British <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> offshore in the <a href="/wiki/Algeciras_Campaign" title="Algeciras Campaign">Battle of Algeciras</a>, which ended in a British victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Musteen2011_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Musteen2011-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The city became the scene for settling a major international crisis as it hosted the <a href="/wiki/Algeciras_Conference" title="Algeciras Conference">Algeciras Conference</a> in 1906. The international forum to discuss the future of <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a> which was held in the Casa Consistorial (town hall). It confirmed the independence of Morocco against threats from Germany, and gave France control of banking and police interests.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OlsonShadle1991_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-OlsonShadle1991-13">[13]</a></sup> In July 1942 Italian frogmen set up in a secret base in the Italian tanker <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olterra" title="Olterra">Olterra</a>, which was interned in Algeciras, in order to attack shipping in Gibraltar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HammondHughes2012_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-HammondHughes2012-14">[14]</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Franco" title="Francisco Franco">Franco</a> era, Algeciras underwent substantial industrial development, creating many new jobs for the local workers made unemployed when the border between Gibraltar and Spain was sealed by Franco between 1969 and 1982.</p>

<p>In 1982 there was a failed plan codenamed <a href="/wiki/Operation_Algeciras" title="Operation Algeciras">Operation Algeciras</a> conceived by the <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentinian</a> military to sabotage the British military facilities in Gibraltar during the <a href="/wiki/Falklands_War" title="Falklands War">Falklands War</a>. The Spanish authorities intervened just before the attack, and deported the two Argentine <a href="/wiki/Montoneros" title="Montoneros">Montoneros</a> and military liaison officer involved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algeciras&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Getares.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="510" data-file-width="1000" height="112" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Getares.jpg/220px-Getares.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Getares.jpg/330px-Getares.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Getares.jpg/440px-Getares.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Getares.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Playa de Getares in Algeciras (<a class="extiw" href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Playa_de_Getares" title="es:Playa de Getares">es</a>)</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Algeciras is principally a transport hub and industrial city. Its principal activities are connected with the port, which serves as the main embarkation point between Spain and <a href="/wiki/Tangier" title="Tangier">Tangier</a> and other ports in Morocco as well as the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a> and the Spanish enclaves of <a href="/wiki/Ceuta" title="Ceuta">Ceuta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Melilla" title="Melilla">Melilla</a>. It is ranked as the 16th busiest port in the world. The city also has a substantial fishing industry and exports a range of agricultural products from the surrounding area, including cereals, tobacco and farm animals.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Port_of_Algeciras" title="Port of Algeciras">Port of Algeciras</a></li>
</ul>
<p>In recent years it has become a significant tourist destination, with popular day trips to <a href="/wiki/Tarifa" title="Tarifa">Tarifa</a> to see bird migrations; to Gibraltar to see the territory's sights and culture; and to the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Gibraltar" title="Bay of Gibraltar">Bay of Gibraltar</a> on <a href="/wiki/Whale_watching" title="Whale watching">whale watching</a> excursions.</p>
<p>Algeciras is the southern terminus of two principal north-south <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euroroute" title="Euroroute">Euroroutes</a>, the <a href="/wiki/European_route_E05" title="European route E05">E05</a> and <a href="/wiki/European_route_E15" title="European route E15">E15</a>. Both routes, moreover, run to Scotland (the E05 terminates at <a href="/wiki/Greenock" title="Greenock">Greenock</a> and the E15 at <a href="/wiki/Inverness" title="Inverness">Inverness</a>) via France and England.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Algeciras_CF" title="Algeciras CF">Algeciras CF</a>, founded in 1912, played in the third-tier <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="Segunda División B">Segunda División B</a> in 2013-14 season<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and relegated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tercera_Division" title="Tercera Division">Tercera Division</a>, fourth level of Spanish league after losing play-out, holding home games at the <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Nuevo_Mirador" title="Estadio Nuevo Mirador">Estadio Nuevo Mirador</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Algeciras_BM" title="Algeciras BM">Algeciras BM</a> was played in <a href="/wiki/Liga_ASOBAL" title="Liga ASOBAL">Liga ASOBAL</a> between 2005 and 2008. The team was dissolved due to enormous debts after relegation to second level in 2008.</p>
