<p><b>Amnat Charoen</b> (<a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>: <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">อำนาจเจริญ</span>, <small>pronounced</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Thai_and_Lao" title="Help:IPA for Thai and Lao">[ʔām.nâːt t͡ɕā.rɤ̄ːn]</a></span>) is one of the <a href="/wiki/Isan" title="Isan">northeastern</a> <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Thailand" title="Provinces of Thailand">provinces</a> (<i>changwat</i>) of <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>. Neighboring provinces are (clockwise from the south) <a href="/wiki/Ubon_Ratchathani_Province" title="Ubon Ratchathani Province">Ubon Ratchathani</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yasothon_Province" title="Yasothon Province">Yasothon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mukdahan_Province" title="Mukdahan Province">Mukdahan</a>. To the east it borders <a href="/wiki/Salavan_Province" title="Salavan Province">Salavan</a> of <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a>. Its name is a concatenation of อำนาจ ("authority, power") and เจริญ ("prosperous").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ThaiLex_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ThaiLex-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amnat_Charoen_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The province is in the <a href="/wiki/Mekong" title="Mekong">Mekong</a> valley. In dry season, from February to May, water in Mekong River declines, and allows islands to appear. Islands include Kaeng Tanglang at Si Sombun Village, close to Amphoe Chanuman, and Kaeng Hin Khan at Ban Hin Khan, 30 kilometres south of Amphoe Chanuman. The other two rivers in the province are the Lam Sae Bok and Lam Sae Bai.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Phu_Sa_Dok_Bua_National_Park" title="Phu Sa Dok Bua National Park">Phu Sa Dok Bua National Park</a> is partially located in Amnat Charoen Province.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amnat_Charoen_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Amnat Charoen gained city status during the reign of King <a href="/wiki/Rama_III" title="Rama III">Rama III</a>. It was first administered from Nakhon Khemarat, and later from Ubon Ratchathani. It became a province in its own right on 12 January 1993, when it was split off from <a href="/wiki/Ubon_Ratchathani_Province" title="Ubon Ratchathani Province">Ubon Ratchathani</a>. It is thus one of the three newest provinces of Thailand, together with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nong_Bua_Lam_Phu_Province" title="Nong Bua Lam Phu Province">Nong Bua Lam Phu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sa_Kaeo_Province" title="Sa Kaeo Province">Sa Kaeo</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Symbols">Symbols</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amnat_Charoen_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Symbols">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the middle of the provincial seal is an image of the <a href="/wiki/Buddhahood" title="Buddhahood">Buddha</a> called Phra Mongkol Ming Muang. Also known as Phra Yai (Big Buddha), this 20 m high statue is the most sacred one in the city of Amnat Charoen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ThaiLex_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ThaiLex-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The provincial tree is <i><a href="/wiki/Hopea_ferrea" title="Hopea ferrea">Hopea ferrea</a></i>.</p>
<p>The provincial slogan is <i>Phra Mongkhon, seven river basins, sacred caves, Phra Lao, beautiful islands and mountain, precious silk and religious people</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amnat_Charoen_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Administrative divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="floatleft"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Amphoe_Amnat_Charoen.png" title="Map of Amphoe"><img alt="Map of Amphoe" data-file-height="445" data-file-width="445" height="160" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Amphoe_Amnat_Charoen.png/160px-Amphoe_Amnat_Charoen.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Amphoe_Amnat_Charoen.png/240px-Amphoe_Amnat_Charoen.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Amphoe_Amnat_Charoen.png/320px-Amphoe_Amnat_Charoen.png 2x" width="160"/></a></div>
<p>The province is divided into seven districts (<i><a href="/wiki/Amphoe" title="Amphoe">amphoe</a></i>). The districts are further subdivided into 56 communes (<i><a href="/wiki/Tambon" title="Tambon">tambon</a></i>) and 653 villages (<i><a href="/wiki/Muban" title="Muban">muban</a></i>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Festivals">Festivals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amnat_Charoen_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Festivals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The people of Amnat Charoen attach their lifestyle to local traditions and festivals that are held throughout year such as the festival to mark the end of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buddhist_Lent" title="Buddhist Lent">Buddhist Lent</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Rocket_Festival" title="Rocket Festival">Rocket Festival</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loy_Krathong" title="Loy Krathong">Loy Krathong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Songkran_(Thailand)" title="Songkran (Thailand)">Songkran</a>. Moreover, they practice 14 moral precepts such as cleaning feet before entering a home, donating food to monks, avoiding stepping on a monk's shadow, paying respect to parents, paying homage to Buddha images with flowers, candles, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joss_stick" title="Joss stick">joss sticks</a> and learning <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dhamma" title="Dhamma">Dhamma</a> every day.</p>
<p>Most people in Amphoe Chanuman are descendants of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phu_Thai" title="Phu Thai">Phu Thai</a> ethnic group who migrated from <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a>. They specialise in weaving "<a href="/wiki/Khit" title="Khit">khit</a>" cloth, preserving its unique patterns. Their traditional musical performance "mor lam" always supports the democratic constitutional monarchy. Amnat Charoen people try to retain the "long khuang" tradition which consisted of teenagers meeting up for communal activities such as weaving or silk spinning accompanied by lively traditional music.</p>
