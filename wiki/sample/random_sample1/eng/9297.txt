<p><b>Ambon Island</b> is part of the <a href="/wiki/Maluku_Islands" title="Maluku Islands">Maluku Islands</a> of <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a> has an area of 775 km<sup>2</sup> (299 sq mi) and is mountainous, well watered, and fertile. Ambon Island consists of two territories. The main city and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seaport" title="Seaport">seaport</a> is <a href="/wiki/Ambon,_Maluku" title="Ambon, Maluku">Ambon</a> (with a 2009 population of 284,809), which is also the capital of <a href="/wiki/Maluku_(province)" title="Maluku (province)">Maluku</a> <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Indonesia" title="Provinces of Indonesia">province</a> and Maluku Tengah (with a 2009 population of 370,931).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Ambon has an <a href="/wiki/Airport" title="Airport">airport</a> and is home to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pattimura_University" title="Pattimura University">Pattimura University</a> and Open University (Universitas Terbuka), state universities, and a few private universities, which include <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Darussalam_University&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Darussalam University (page does not exist)">Darussalam University</a> (Universitas Darussalam, UNDAR) and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Universitas_Kristen_Indonesia_Maluku&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Universitas Kristen Indonesia Maluku (page does not exist)">Universitas Kristen Indonesia Maluku</a> (UKIM).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ambon_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ambon Island lies off the southwest coast of the much larger <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seram" title="Seram">Seram</a> island. It is on the north side of the <a href="/wiki/Banda_Sea" title="Banda Sea">Banda Sea</a>, part of a chain of <a href="/wiki/High_island" title="High island">volcanic islands</a> that encircle the sea. It is 51 kilometres (32 miles) long and is of very irregular shape, being almost divided in two. The southeastern and smaller portion, a peninsula (called <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Leitimor&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Leitimor (page does not exist)">Leitimor</a>) is united to the northern (Hitoe) by a narrow neck of land. Ambon city is on the northwest of Leitimor, facing Hitoe, and has a safe harbor on Amboina Bay.</p>
<p>The highest <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountains</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Wawani&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Wawani (page does not exist)">Wawani</a> at 1,100 metres (3,600 feet) and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Salahutu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Salahutu (page does not exist)">Salahutu</a> at 1,225 metres (4,019 feet), have <a href="/wiki/Hot_spring" title="Hot spring">hot springs</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solfatara" title="Solfatara">solfataras</a>. They are <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcanoes</a>, and the mountains of the neighboring <a href="/wiki/Lease_Islands" title="Lease Islands">Lease Islands</a> are extinct volcanoes. <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">Granite</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serpentine_rock" title="Serpentine rock">serpentine rocks</a> predominate, but the shores of Amboina Bay are of <a href="/wiki/Chalk" title="Chalk">chalk</a> and contain <a href="/wiki/Stalactite" title="Stalactite">stalactite</a> caves.</p>
<p>Wild areas of Ambon Island are covered by <a href="/wiki/Tropical_rainforest" title="Tropical rainforest">tropical rainforest</a>, part of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Seram_rain_forests&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Seram rain forests (page does not exist)">Seram rain forests</a> <a href="/wiki/Ecoregion" title="Ecoregion">ecoregion</a>, together with neighboring Seram. Seram, Ambon, and most of Maluku are part of <a href="/wiki/Wallacea" title="Wallacea">Wallacea</a>, the group of Indonesian islands that are separated by deep water from both the Asian and Australian continents and have never been linked to the continents by land.</p>
<p>As a result of this isolation, Ambon has few indigenous mammals; birds are more abundant. The insect diversity of the island, however, is rich, particularly in <a href="/wiki/Lepidoptera" title="Lepidoptera">butterflies</a>. <a href="/wiki/Seashell" title="Seashell">Seashells</a> are obtained in great numbers and variety. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tortoiseshell_material" title="Tortoiseshell material">Tortoise shell</a> is also exported.</p>
<p>The population of the island (administered as Kota Ambon, Kecamatan Leihitu, Kecamatan Leihitu Barat, and Kecamatan Salahutu), including a tiny sparsely populated island to the north, is just below 441,000 in the 2010 Census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ambon_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The average temperature is 27 °C, rarely falling below 22 °C. Rainfall can be heavy, especially after the eastern <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoons</a>, and the island is vulnerable to violent <a href="/wiki/Typhoon" title="Typhoon">typhoons</a>. The wet season (October to April) coincides with the period of the west monsoon.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ambon_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Cassava" title="Cassava">Cassava</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sago" title="Sago">sago</a> are the chief crops, which also include <a href="/wiki/Breadfruit" title="Breadfruit">breadfruit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sugarcane" title="Sugarcane">sugarcane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cocoa_bean" title="Cocoa bean">cocoa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Black_pepper" title="Black pepper">pepper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a>. In addition to these, hunting and fishing supplement the local diet. <a href="/wiki/Nutmeg" title="Nutmeg">Nutmeg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clove" title="Clove">cloves</a> were once the dominant export crops but are now produced in limited quantities. <a href="/wiki/Copra" title="Copra">Copra</a> is also exported. Amboina wood, obtained from the <a href="/wiki/Pterocarpus_indicus" title="Pterocarpus indicus">angsana tree</a> and highly valued for ornamental woodwork, is now mostly grown on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seram" title="Seram">Seram</a>. The main employers in Ambon Island are the Gubernatorial Office (PEMDA), the Mayoral Office (PEMKOT), Raiders 733 (Indonesian military unit), and Ambon City Center (the only mall in Ambon Island). The whole economy of Ambon Island is starting to shift out of the "Old Towne" (Kota Lama) toward Passo, which is the newly appointed central business district of the island region. The economy of Ambon Island was recently boosted by the investment made by Ciputra Group in creating a whole new satellite city in Lateri, Kotamadya Ambon, Maluku: Citraland Bay View City. Furthermore, the new international standard shopping center, Ambon City Center, opened in 2012.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ambon_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Ambonese" title="Ambonese">Ambonese</a> are of mixed Malay-Papuan origin. They are mostly <a href="/wiki/Christianity_in_Indonesia" title="Christianity in Indonesia">Christians</a> or <a href="/wiki/Islam_in_Indonesia" title="Islam in Indonesia">Muslims</a>. The predominant language of the island is <a href="/wiki/Ambonese_Malay" title="Ambonese Malay">Ambonese Malay</a>, also called <i>Ambonese</i>. It developed as the trade language of central Maluku and is spoken elsewhere in Maluku as a second language. The old creole trade language called <a href="/wiki/Portugis_language" title="Portugis language">Portugis</a> has died out. Bilingualism in <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_language" title="Indonesian language">Indonesian</a> is high around Ambon City. There have been strong religious tensions on the island between Muslims and Christians<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and ethnic tensions between indigenous Ambonese and migrants from <a href="/wiki/Sulawesi" title="Sulawesi">Sulawesi</a>, primarily <a href="/wiki/Butonese_people" title="Butonese people">Butonese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bugis" title="Bugis">Bugis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Makassar_people" title="Makassar people">Makassarese</a> migrants.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ambon_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Colonial_era">Colonial era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ambon_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Colonial era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Governorate_of_Ambon" title="Governorate of Ambon">Governorate of Ambon</a></div>
<p>In 1512, the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_people" title="Portuguese people">Portuguese</a> were the first Europeans to land in Ambon, and it became the new centre for Portuguese activities in Maluku following their expulsion from <a href="/wiki/Ternate" title="Ternate">Ternate</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERicklefs199925_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERicklefs199925-4">[4]</a></sup> The Portuguese, however, were regularly attacked by native Muslims on the island's northern coast, in particular Hitu, which had trading and religious links with major port cities on Java's north coast. They established a factory in 1521 but did not obtain peaceable possession of it until 1580. Indeed, the Portuguese never managed to control the local trade in spices and failed in attempts to establish their authority over the <a href="/wiki/Banda_Islands" title="Banda Islands">Banda Islands</a>, the nearby centre of nutmeg production. The creole trade language <a href="/wiki/Portugis_language" title="Portugis language">Portugis</a>, however, was spoken well into the 19th century, and many families still have Portuguese names and claim Portuguese ancestry, for example Muskita and De Fretes. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Portuguese were dispossessed by the Dutch in 1605, when <a href="/wiki/Steven_van_der_Hagen" title="Steven van der Hagen">Steven van der Hagen</a> took over the fort without a single shot. Ambon was the headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_India_Company" title="Dutch East India Company">Dutch East India Company</a> (VOC) from 1610 to 1619 until the founding of Batavia (now <a href="/wiki/Jakarta" title="Jakarta">Jakarta</a>) by the Dutch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERicklefs199928_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERicklefs199928-6">[6]</a></sup> About 1615 the English formed a settlement on the island at Cambello, which they retained until 1623, when it was destroyed by the Dutch. Frightful tortures inflicted on its unfortunate inhabitants were connected with its destruction. In 1654, after many fruitless negotiations, <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Cromwell" title="Oliver Cromwell">Oliver Cromwell</a> compelled the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">United Provinces</a> to give the sum of 300,000 gulden, as compensation to the descendants of those who suffered in the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ambon_Massacre" title="Ambon Massacre">Ambon Massacre</a>", together with <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 1673, the poet <a href="/wiki/John_Dryden" title="John Dryden">John Dryden</a> produced his tragedy <i>Amboyna; or the Cruelties of the Dutch to the English Merchants</i>. The British, under Admiral Rainier, captured Ambon in 1796, but they restored it to the Dutch at the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Amiens" title="Treaty of Amiens">Peace of Amiens</a> in 1802. They retook the island in 1810 but once more restored it to the Dutch in 1814. Ambon used to be the world center of <a href="/wiki/Clove" title="Clove">clove</a> production; until the nineteenth century, the Dutch prohibited the rearing of the clove tree on all the other islands subject to their rule, in order to secure the monopoly to Ambon.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p>Under the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Empire" title="Dutch Empire">Dutch Empire</a>, Ambon city was the seat of the Dutch resident and military commander of the Moluccas. The town was protected by Fort Victoria, and a 1902 Encyclopædia characterized it as "a clean little town with wide streets, well planted".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The population was divided into two classes: <i>orang burger</i> or citizens and <i>orang negri</i> or villagers, the former being a class of native origin enjoying certain privileges conferred on their ancestors by the old Dutch East India Company. There were also, besides the Dutch, some Arabs, Chinese and a few Portuguese settlers.</p>
<p>Ambon city was the site of a major Dutch military base that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_forces" title="Imperial Japanese forces">Imperial Japanese forces</a> captured from <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allied forces</a> in the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ambon" title="Battle of Ambon">Battle of Ambon</a> in 1942. The battle was followed by the <a href="/wiki/Summary_execution" title="Summary execution">summary execution</a> of more than 300 Allied <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prisoner_of_War" title="Prisoner of War">PoWs</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ambon#Laha_massacre" title="Battle of Ambon">Laha massacre</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Conflicts_since_independence">Conflicts since independence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ambon_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Conflicts since independence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Indonesia won its independence in 1945–49. As a consequence of ethnic and religious tensions, and President <a href="/wiki/Sukarno" title="Sukarno">Sukarno</a> making Indonesia a <a href="/wiki/Unitary_state" title="Unitary state">unitary state</a>, Ambon was the scene of a revolt against the Indonesian government, resulting in the rebellion of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_South_Maluku" title="Republic of South Maluku">Republic of South Maluku</a> in 1950.</p>
<p>In April and May 1958 during the <a href="/wiki/Permesta" title="Permesta">Permesta</a> rebellion in <a href="/wiki/North_Sulawesi" title="North Sulawesi">North Sulawesi</a>, the USA supported and supplied the rebels. Pilots from a <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Central_Intelligence_Agency" title="Central Intelligence Agency">CIA</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Front_organisation" title="Front organisation">front organisation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Civil_Air_Transport" title="Civil Air Transport">Civil Air Transport</a>, flying CIA <a href="/wiki/Douglas_A-26_Invader" title="Douglas A-26 Invader">B-26 Invader</a> aircraft, repeatedly bombed and machine-gunned targets on Ambon. From 27 April until 18 May there were <a href="/wiki/Ambon,_Maluku#Conflicts_since_independence" title="Ambon, Maluku">CIA air raids on Ambon city</a>. Also, on 8 May 1958 CIA pilot <a href="/wiki/Allen_Lawrence_Pope" title="Allen Lawrence Pope">Allen Pope</a> bombed and machine-gunned the <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_Air_Force" title="Indonesian Air Force">Indonesian Air Force</a> base at Liang in the northeast of the island, damaging the runway and destroying a <a href="/wiki/Consolidated_PBY_Catalina" title="Consolidated PBY Catalina">Consolidated PBY Catalina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEConboyMorrison1999122_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEConboyMorrison1999122-9">[9]</a></sup> The Indonesian Air Force had only one serviceable fighter aircraft on Ambon Island, a <a href="/wiki/North_American_P-51_Mustang" title="North American P-51 Mustang">North American P-51 Mustang</a> at Liang. Pope's last air raid was on 18 May, when an Indonesian pilot at Liang, Captain Ignatius Dewanto, was scrambled to the P-51.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEConboyMorrison1999136_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEConboyMorrison1999136-10">[10]</a></sup> Pope had attacked Ambon city before Dewanto could catch him, but Dewanto intercepted him just as Pope was attacking one of a pair of troop ships in an Indonesian fleet west of Ambon Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEConboyMorrison1999138_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEConboyMorrison1999138-11">[11]</a></sup> The B-26 was brought down by fire from both Dewanto and shipborne anti-aircraft gunners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEConboyMorrison1999139_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEConboyMorrison1999139-12">[12]</a></sup> Pope and his Indonesian radio operator bailed out and were captured,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEConboyMorrison1999139.2C_141_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEConboyMorrison1999139.2C_141-13">[13]</a></sup> which immediately exposed the level of CIA support for the Permesta rebellion. Embarrassed, the <a href="/wiki/Presidency_of_Dwight_D._Eisenhower" title="Presidency of Dwight D. Eisenhower">Eisenhower administration</a> quickly ended CIA support for Permesta and withdrew its agents and remaining aircraft from the conflict.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEConboyMorrison1999143_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEConboyMorrison1999143-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Between 1999 and 2002, Ambon was at the centre of <a href="/wiki/Maluku_sectarian_conflict" title="Maluku sectarian conflict">sectarian conflict across the Maluku Islands</a>. In 2007, Ambon resident Leonard Joni Sinay was sentenced to fifteen years' imprisonment for treason after he and other activists protested a visit by President <a href="/wiki/Susilo_Bambang_Yudhoyono" title="Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono">Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono</a> with a dance and a raising of the banned regional flag; both <a href="/wiki/Human_Rights_Watch" title="Human Rights Watch">Human Rights Watch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amnesty_International" title="Amnesty International">Amnesty International</a> called for his release, the latter organization designating him a <a href="/wiki/Prisoner_of_conscience" title="Prisoner of conscience">prisoner of conscience</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
