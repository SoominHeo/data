<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Hamada</h1>

<p>A <b>hamada</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>, حماده <i>ḥammāda</i>) is a type of <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a> landscape consisting of high, largely barren, hard, rocky plateaus, with very little <a href="/wiki/Sand" title="Sand">sand</a> because this has been removed by <a href="/wiki/Aeolian_processes#Wind_erosion" title="Aeolian processes">deflation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Springer_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Springer-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hamadas are produced by the wind removing the fine products of weathering: an aeolian process known as deflation. The finer-grained products are taken away in suspension, whilst the sand is removed through <a href="/wiki/Saltation_(geology)" title="Saltation (geology)">saltation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aeolian_landform#Mechanism" title="Aeolian landform">surface creep</a>, leaving behind a landscape of <a href="/wiki/Gravel" title="Gravel">gravel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boulder" title="Boulder">boulders</a> and bare rock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Related_landforms">Related landforms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Related landforms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hamada is related to <i><a href="/wiki/Desert_pavement" title="Desert pavement">Desert pavement</a></i> (known variously as reg, serir, gibber, or saï), which occurs as stony plains or depressions covered with gravels or boulders, rather than as highland plateaus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Springer_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Springer-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hamadas exist in contrast to <i><a href="/wiki/Erg_(landform)" title="Erg (landform)">ergs</a></i>, which are large areas of shifting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sand_dune" title="Sand dune">sand dunes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
