<p><b>Amto–Musan</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Language_family" title="Language family">language family</a> of two closely related but not mutually intelligible <a href="/wiki/Papuan_languages" title="Papuan languages">Papuan languages</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amto_language" title="Amto language">Amto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Siawi_language" title="Siawi language">Siawi</a>, of the <a href="/wiki/Sandaun_Province" title="Sandaun Province">Sandaun Province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a>. Amto–Musan was left unclassified by Ross (2005) (see <a href="/wiki/Papuan_languages#Ross_classification" title="Papuan languages">Papuan languages#Ross classification</a>) due to lack of data; Wurm (1975) had posited it as an independent family. The family has <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Language_typology" title="Language typology">typological</a> similarities with the <a href="/wiki/Busa_language_(Papuan)" title="Busa language (Papuan)">Busa</a> <a href="/wiki/Language_isolate" title="Language isolate">language isolate</a>, but these do not appear to demonstrate a genetic relationship.</p>
