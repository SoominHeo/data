<p><i><b>E.I.N.S.</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> for <i>O.N.E.</i>) is the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Hard_rock" title="Hard rock">Hard rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/B%C3%B6hse_Onkelz" title="Böhse Onkelz">Böhse Onkelz</a> 12th album. It was released in 1996. The album title is not an abbreviation: the dots between the letters should be seen to strengthen and emphasise the word <i>eins</i> ("one").</p>
<p>"E.I.N.S." was voted several times into the Top 10 of the column "Best metal album of all time" from several German magazines like Rock Hard or <a href="/wiki/Metal_Hammer" title="Metal Hammer">Metal Hammer</a>.</p>
<p>The cover shows a morphed face with one ear (Kevin Russell), one eye (Matthias "Gonzo" Röhr), nose (Peter "Pe" Schorowsky) and mouth (Stephan Weidner). The followed tour had the name "Unter dem Auge des Gonz" <i>(Under the eyes of Gonz)</i>, and at the back of the stage was to see a huge eye.</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Track_notes">Track notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=E.I.N.S.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Track notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Danket_dem_Herrn">Danket dem Herrn</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=E.I.N.S.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Danket dem Herrn">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dd>In this typical opener song, where the band celebrates itself and their fans, there are bible quotes at some lines. "Duftende Blumen in Feldern voll Scheiße" <i>(Smelling flowers in fields full of shit)</i> can be found in the <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Isaiah" title="Book of Isaiah">Book of Isaiah</a> as "All meat is grass, and all his goodness is like a tree at a field". Through the bible writers view, the fields had to be fertilized. The quote "Perlen vor die Säue" <i>(Pearls before the sows)</i> comes from the <a href="/wiki/Gospel_of_Matthew" title="Gospel of Matthew">Gospel of Matthew</a>. The line "Uns liegt das Herz auf der Zunge" <i>(Our hearts are lying at our tongues)</i> is a changed quote from <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Job" title="Book of Job">Job</a> , "My heart is telling the sincere words". The self designation "die fantastischen Vier" <i>(The fantastic four)</i> comes from the song "Onkelz wie wir" from the <a href="/wiki/Onkelz_wie_wir..." title="Onkelz wie wir...">same-named album</a>.</dd>
</dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ihr_sollt_den_Tag_nicht_vor_dem_Abend_loben">Ihr sollt den Tag nicht vor dem Abend loben</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=E.I.N.S.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ihr sollt den Tag nicht vor dem Abend loben">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dd>This song is written for two bands, which saying or singing every time they can something against the Onkelz: <a href="/wiki/Die_%C3%84rzte" title="Die Ärzte">Die Ärzte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Die_Toten_Hosen" title="Die Toten Hosen">Die Toten Hosen</a>. The Ärzte sang "zwischen Störkraft und den Onkelz steht ne Kuschelrock-LP" <i>(between <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=St%C3%B6rkraft&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Störkraft (page does not exist)">Störkraft</a> and the Onkelz stands a Kuschelrock-CD)</i> (From: "Schrei nach Liebe", 1993). Campino of the Tote Hosen compared the Onkelz with "Landser booklet lyrics". Störkraft and Landser are two neonazi bands from Germany.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>"Ihr sollt den Tag nicht vor dem Abend loben" rather means "Don't count your chickens before they are hatched", though the translation in this title is more or less a one-to-one translation, showing the real meanings of the words used.</dd>
</dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Meister_der_L.C3.BCgen">Meister der Lügen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=E.I.N.S.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Meister der Lügen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dd>Meister der Lügen (Masters of lies) is a song against the press.</dd>
</dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kirche">Kirche</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=E.I.N.S.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Kirche">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dd>In opinion of the band, this song is addressed only to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic Church</a> and not against the Christians. Stephan Weidner has the opinion, that the church "should distribute their money to the poor and not their compassion" and that "the organized religion is the biggest obstacle at the way to peace." The song itself requests to not use the church as a sprag. The sentence "Wer keine Angst vor dem Teufel hat, braucht auch keinen Gott" <i>(Who doesn't fear the devil, doesn't need a god)</i> comes from the novel "<a href="/wiki/The_Name_of_the_Rose" title="The Name of the Rose">The Name of the Rose</a>" (<a href="/wiki/Umberto_Eco" title="Umberto Eco">Umberto Eco</a>, 1980).</dd>
</dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Enie_Tfahcstob_r.C3.BCf_Ediona-RAP">Enie Tfahcstob rüf Ediona-RAP</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=E.I.N.S.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Enie Tfahcstob rüf Ediona-RAP">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dd>A journalist from the daily newspaper "<a href="/wiki/Darmstadt" title="Darmstadt">Darmstädter</a> Echo" believed they had found a scandal, when they saw that title of one of the Onkelz' songs, which in their opinion is to read backwards as "Arier On" . In fact, the song title isn't <i>Noreira</i> but <i>Noreia</i>, the name of a celtic god. In reaction of this and other witchhunts, the band released a song on the "E.I.N.S." Album called "Enie Tfahcstob rüf ediona-RAP" - backwards "Eine Botschaft für Paranoide" (A message for the paranoid). This features Stephan Weidner speaking backwards a message, which can be heard if the song is played in reverse:</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>(translated) <i>Congratulations. Must've been a lot of work playing this song backwards. Either you are one of the paranoid assholes we've created this song for, or you're simply curious. For the former, let me say: Anyone expecting satanic or fascist messages to be played backwards on our records must be downright silly and, what's more, probably has a persecution complex. You poor creature, we really feel sorry for you! Better lock yourself up and throw away the key.</i></dd>
</dl>
