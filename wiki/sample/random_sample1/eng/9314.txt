<p><b>Abdul Rahim Hatif</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pashto_language" title="Pashto language">Pashto</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ps" xml:lang="ps">عبدالرحیم هاتف</span>‎; 20 May 1926 – 19 August 2013) was a <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_Afghanistan" title="Politics of Afghanistan">politician in Afghanistan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He served as Vice President during the last years of the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_Afghanistan" title="Democratic Republic of Afghanistan">Democratic Republic of Afghanistan</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He was born in <a href="/wiki/Kandahar" title="Kandahar">Kandahar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Before the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sieges_of_Kabul" title="Sieges of Kabul">first fall of Kabul</a>, he was the acting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Presidents_of_Afghanistan" title="List of Presidents of Afghanistan">President of Afghanistan</a> for two weeks in April 1992, after the resignation of President <a href="/wiki/Mohammad_Najibullah" title="Mohammad Najibullah">Najibullah</a>, before the takeover of power by the <a href="/wiki/Jamiat-e_Islami" title="Jamiat-e Islami">Jamiat-e Islami</a>.</p>
<p>He went into exile in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> where he died on 19 August 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
