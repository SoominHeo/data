<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abdullah_Ensour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ensour was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salt,_Jordan" title="Salt, Jordan">Salt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hazaimeh_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-hazaimeh-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rulers_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-rulers-2">[2]</a></sup> He studied at the <a href="/wiki/American_University_of_Beirut" title="American University of Beirut">American University of Beirut</a> where he obtained his bachelor's degree in statistics. He then continued to get his master's degree in institutions management, at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a> in the United States. He also obtained his Ph.D. in planning at the <a href="/wiki/Pantheon-Sorbonne_University" title="Pantheon-Sorbonne University">Pantheon-Sorbonne University</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jordanian_Prime_Ministry_official_website_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jordanian_Prime_Ministry_official_website-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abdullah_Ensour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ensour was first elected as a deputy to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jordanian_Parliament" title="Jordanian Parliament">Jordanian Parliament</a> in 1989, again in 1992, and a third time in <span class="cleanup-needed-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">2010</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="2001 or 2010? (March 2016)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. His first ministerial post was as Minister of Planning in 1984. He then became Minister of Planning again in 1985; he also served as Minister of Education (1989); Minister of Foreign Affairs (1991);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rulers_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-rulers-2">[2]</a></sup> Minister of Industry and Trade (1993); Minister of Higher Education (1996); Deputy Prime Minister<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and Minister of Administrative Development (1997); and Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Information (1998). He also served in the parliament in the lower house of representatives from 1989 to 2001.</p>
<p>Ensour was also governor of Jordan to the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a>, deputy of Jordan to the <a href="/wiki/International_Monetary_Fund" title="International Monetary Fund">International Monetary Fund</a> and deputy permanent delegate to <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>; he sits on a number of boards including the Arab African Bank, Nuackchott (President); the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Jordan" title="University of Jordan">University of Jordan</a> (Vice-President); and the French Universities' Graduates in Jordan (Honorary President).</p>
<p>In October 2012, he was appointed as Prime Minister by King Abdullah II, replacing <a href="/wiki/Fayez_Tarawneh" title="Fayez Tarawneh">Fayez Tarawneh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hazaimeh_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-hazaimeh-1">[1]</a></sup> After general elections in January 2013, King Abdullah reappointed Ensour as Prime Minister on 9 March 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sul9mar_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-sul9mar-6">[6]</a></sup> His second cabinet was sworn in on 30 March 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jtimes30mar_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-jtimes30mar-7">[7]</a></sup> His term ended on 29 May 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ensour is a member of <a href="/wiki/Prague_Society_for_International_Cooperation" title="Prague Society for International Cooperation">Prague Society for International Cooperation</a>, a respected NGO whose main goals are networking and the development of a new generation of responsible, well-informed leaders and thinkers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abdullah_Ensour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ensour was married to Hala Khleifat, who died on 14 October 2015. The couple had three children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
