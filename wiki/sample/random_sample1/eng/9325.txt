<p>"<b>Angola Avante!</b>" (English: "Onwards Angola") is the <a href="/wiki/National_anthem" title="National anthem">national anthem</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Republic of Angola</a>. Rui Alberto Vieira Dias Mingas composed the music, while the lyrics were authored by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manuel_Rui_Alves_Monteiro" title="Manuel Rui Alves Monteiro">Manuel Rui Alves Monteiro</a>. It was adopted as the national anthem in November 1975, when the country gained its independence from <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>. The lyrics make reference to several key events of the <a href="/wiki/MPLA" title="MPLA">People's Movement for the Liberation of Angola</a> (MPLA), which has been in power since independence and was the <a href="/wiki/One-party_state" title="One-party state">only party</a> in Angola until 1992.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angola_Avante&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Angola Avante" was composed by Rui Alberto Vieira Dias Mingas (1939–).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIA_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-CIA-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The lyrics to the song were penned by Manuel Rui Alves Monteiro<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIA_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-CIA-1">[1]</a></sup> (1941–), an author who studied in <a href="/wiki/Huambo" title="Huambo">Huambo</a> and is affiliated with both the country's Writer Union and Union of Artists and Composers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dictionary_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-dictionary-3">[3]</a></sup> He is one of the best-selling writers in the capital city <a href="/wiki/Luanda" title="Luanda">Luanda</a> – according to an "informal survey" conducted in July 2003<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dictionary_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-dictionary-3">[3]</a></sup> – and writes about "everyday life" in the country using <a href="/wiki/Satire" title="Satire">satire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Irony" title="Irony">irony</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dictionary_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-dictionary-3">[3]</a></sup> The song was officially designated as the country's national anthem in 1975, when the country gained independence on November 11 of that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIA_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-CIA-1">[1]</a></sup> It is enshrined under Article 164 of the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Angola" title="Constitution of Angola">constitution of Angola</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposed_changes">Proposed changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angola_Avante&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Proposed changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The anthem's lyrics refer to several key events in the history of the MPLA. However, this has been rendered "obsolete" since the end of <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_Angola" title="People's Republic of Angola">the one-party state</a> in 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guide_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-guide-5">[5]</a></sup> As a result, there have been efforts to modify the national anthem, along with other national symbols.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guide_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-guide-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> However, none of these have come to fruition, and the anthem remains unchanged as of 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIA_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-CIA-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Context_of_lyrics">Context of lyrics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angola_Avante&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Context of lyrics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The lyrics of "Angola Avante" allude to how the country and its people will progress forward in the future.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> It has been classified by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Daily Telegraph</a></i>′s <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Hewett" title="Ivan Hewett">Ivan Hewett</a> as one of several contemporary national anthems that convey "a more martial tone" inspired by "<a href="/wiki/La_Marseillaise" title="La Marseillaise">La Marseillaise</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lyrics_in_Portuguese">Lyrics in Portuguese</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angola_Avante&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Lyrics in Portuguese">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ó <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Pátria</a>, nunca mais esqueceremos<br/>
Os heróis do <a href="/wiki/Angolan_War_of_Independence" title="Angolan War of Independence">quatro de Fevereiro</a>.<br/>
Ó Pátria, nós saudamos os teus filhos<br/>
Tombados pela nossa Independência.<br/>
Honramos o passado e a nossa História,<br/>
Construindo no Trabalho o Homem novo,<br/>
Honramos o passado e a nossa História,<br/>
Construindo no Trabalho o Homem novo.</p>
<p><i>Chorus:</i><br/>
<a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>, avante!<br/>
Revolução, pelo Poder Popular!<br/>
Pátria Unida, Liberdade,<br/>
Um só povo, uma só Nação!</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angola_Avante&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name of the national anthem is used as a nickname for an inter-community football competition held to boost sports activities both in Angola and among people from the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese-speaking_African_countries" title="Portuguese-speaking African countries">Portuguese-speaking African countries</a> (PALOP) living in <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011_tournament_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-2011_tournament-9">[9]</a></sup> Hosted in Portugal in 2011 and 2013, the latter tournament featured teams from <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guinea-Bissau" title="Guinea-Bissau">Guinea-Bissau</a>, and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe" title="São Tomé and Príncipe">São Tomé and Príncipe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The 2011 sporting event coincided with and honoured the 36th anniversary of the independence of Angola.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011_tournament_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-2011_tournament-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
