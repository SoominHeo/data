<p>The <b>President of the Republic of Angola</b> (<i>Presidente da República de Angola</i> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portuguese_Language" title="Portuguese Language">Portuguese</a>) is both <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> and <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a> in <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>. According to the constitution adopted in 2010, the post of <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Angola" title="Prime Minister of Angola">Prime Minister</a> is abolished; <a href="/wiki/Executive_(government)" title="Executive (government)">executive</a> authority belongs to the President who has also a degree of legislative powers, as he can govern by decree.</p>
<p>The position of President dates from Angola's independence from <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>. <a href="/wiki/Agostinho_Neto" title="Agostinho Neto">Agostinho Neto</a> held the position when his People's Movement for the Liberation of Angola (<a href="/wiki/MPLA" title="MPLA">MPLA</a>) won control of the country from the Portuguese. When Neto died in 1979, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Eduardo_dos_Santos" title="José Eduardo dos Santos">José Eduardo dos Santos</a> succeeded him.</p>
<p>Under Dos Santos's leadership, Angola became a multi-party state, although controlled by Dos Santos. The most recent elections, held in 1992, reelected Dos Santos with 49% of the votes. Dos Santos's opponent, <a href="/wiki/Jonas_Savimbi" title="Jonas Savimbi">Jonas Savimbi</a> of the National Union for Total Independence of Angola (<a href="/wiki/UNITA" title="UNITA">UNITA</a>) party, claimed that the elections were fraudulent.</p>
<p>The office of the President is supposed to be limited to two five-year terms, but only Dos Santos has held the post.</p>
<p>In January 2010 the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Angola" title="National Assembly of Angola">National Assembly</a> approved a new constitution, according to which the leader of the party with the most seats in the Assembly would become president, rather than a public vote taking place. The new constitution also limits a president to serving two terms, although it does not count terms served to date, and abolished the post of <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Angola" title="Prime Minister of Angola">Prime Minister</a>, and introduced instead the post of vice-president.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
