<p><b>Andre De Grasse</b> (born November 10, 1994) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian" title="Canadian">Canadian</a> sprinter. He won the silver medal in the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_200_metres" title="Athletics at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Men's 200 metres">200 m</a> and bronze medals in both the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_100_metres" title="Athletics at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Men's 100 metres">100 m</a> and <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_4_%C3%97_100_metres_relay" title="Athletics at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Men's 4 × 100 metres relay">4 × 100 m relay</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2016_Summer_Olympics" title="2016 Summer Olympics">2016 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>. De Grasse is the reigning <a href="/wiki/Pan_American_Games" title="Pan American Games">Pan American champion</a> in the 100 m and 200 m. He is also the reigning NCAA men's champion in the 100 m and 200 m and current <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_records_in_athletics" title="Canadian records in athletics">Canadian record</a> holder in the 200 m. De Grasse won the bronze in the <a href="/wiki/2015_World_Championships_in_Athletics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_100_metres" title="2015 World Championships in Athletics – Men's 100 metres">100 m</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2015_World_Championships_in_Athletics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_4_%C3%97_100_metres_relay" title="2015 World Championships in Athletics – Men's 4 × 100 metres relay">4 × 100 m relay</a> at the 2015 World Track and Field Championships in Beijing.</p>
<p>De Grasse is the first Canadian sprinter to win three medals in a single Olympics. His feat bettered the two medals in a single Olympics that were won by Canadian legends <a href="/wiki/Donovan_Bailey" title="Donovan Bailey">Donovan Bailey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Percy_Williams_(sprinter)" title="Percy Williams (sprinter)">Percy Williams</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andre_De_Grasse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andre De Grasse was born in <a href="/wiki/Scarborough,_Toronto" title="Scarborough, Toronto">Scarborough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> His mother Beverley De Grasse was a high school sprinter in <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Trinidad and Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</a> before she moved to Canada at age 26.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> His father, Alexander Waithe, moved from <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a> to Canada as a teenager.</p>
<p>Raised in <a href="/wiki/Markham,_Ontario" title="Markham, Ontario">Markham, Ontario</a>, he went to St. Marguerite Bourgeoys Catholic Elementary School in Scarborough from kindergarten to the second grade and later transferred to Mother Teresa Catholic Elementary School in Markham to continue until grade eight where he won his first race in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He also attended <a href="/wiki/Father_Michael_McGivney_Catholic_Academy" title="Father Michael McGivney Catholic Academy">Father Michael McGivney Catholic Academy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Milliken_Mills_High_School" title="Milliken Mills High School">Milliken Mills High School</a>. De Grasse is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> having been influenced by his mother's faith. He has the word "hope" and a prayer tattooed on his arm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>De Grasse started racing in grade eleven. Wearing basketball shorts and Converse shoes and using no starting blocks, he secured second position in his first 100 m race with a time of 10.9 seconds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-panamgold_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-panamgold-7">[7]</a></sup> He was spotted by future coach <a href="/wiki/Tony_Sharpe" title="Tony Sharpe">Tony Sharpe</a> who noticed his potential because of this. He attended <a href="/wiki/Coffeyville_Community_College" title="Coffeyville Community College">Coffeyville Community College</a> in 2013 and 2014 before transferring to <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">USC</a> for his junior season in 2015.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andre_De_Grasse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Amateur_career">Amateur career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andre_De_Grasse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Amateur career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>De Grasse's legal 100 m time of 9.97 to win the <a href="/wiki/Pac-12_Conference" title="Pac-12 Conference">Pac-12</a> championship in May 2015 was the first time he ran under 10 seconds. At the 2015 100 m NCAA championships De Grasse ran a wind assisted time of 9.75 to win; the wind was +2.7 m/s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The 9.75 made him the seventh fastest man in the world and fastest Canadian in any conditions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Also at the 2015 NCAA Championships, De Grasse produced a wind aided 19.58 (+2.4) 200 metres. That was nearly a half a second faster than his own national record set four weeks earlier. After the race with encouragement from his friends to keep going in the pursuit of titles he said "So I ask myself...world champion? Olympic champion? Why not me I’ve come this far, and I’ve only scratched the surface. I want to keep it going. Track is fun to me, and as long as it stays this way, I expect to keep getting better."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_Pan_American_champion_and_World_double_medalist">2015 Pan American champion and World double medalist</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andre_De_Grasse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2015 Pan American champion and World double medalist">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>De Grasse's next major event was the <a href="/wiki/2015_Pan_American_Games" title="2015 Pan American Games">2015 Pan American Games</a> held in his home province of <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, Canada where he won the gold medal in the 100 m with a time of 10.05.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Earlier in the semi-finals he ran a 9.97 on his way to the final. After the race De Grasse said of the home crowd that "Aw, it was great. I think they gave me an extra boost of energy, the crowd was amazing, I hear them cheering my name and screaming my name."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-panamgold_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-panamgold-7">[7]</a></sup> De Grasse also won the gold medal in the 200 m at the 2015 Pan American Games with a time of 19.88.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> De Grasse became the first Canadian in history to run both a sub-10 in the 100 metres and sub-20 in the 200.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He appeared to win a third Pan Am Games gold when he ran the second leg of the 4 x 100 m relay, in which the Canadian team came first in the race with a Games record of 38.06. However, the team was later disqualified two hours after the race for a lane infringement by <a href="/wiki/Gavin_Smellie" title="Gavin Smellie">Gavin Smellie</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/2015_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2015 World Championships in Athletics">2015 World Championships in Athletics</a> took place in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>. At the event De Grasse ran sub-10 second times in the heat and semifinal on his way to the eighth lane in the final. There, De Grasse tied for the bronze medal with American <a href="/wiki/Trayvon_Bromell" title="Trayvon Bromell">Trayvon Bromell</a> in the 100 metres, running a 9.92. He became the first Canadian to win a medal in track's marquee event since <a href="/wiki/Bruny_Surin" title="Bruny Surin">Bruny Surin</a> raced to silver in 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beijingbronze_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Beijingbronze-15">[15]</a></sup> After the final De Grasse said "I didn't know who had won the race, I didn't know you could actually tie with someone for a bronze medal. So I am very happy for Trayvon and proud of myself to come away with a personal best. To race against these guys and make the final, I couldn't end the season any better than that. I can't let [the lane assignment] distract me. At the end of the day it's the 100 m final. This was the biggest race of my life so I wasn't going to think about no lane assignment. I had a lot of confidence after the semifinals, being that close to Bolt, and that raised my confidence for the final."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beijingbronze_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Beijingbronze-15">[15]</a></sup> De Grasse also participated in the 4 × 100 m relay where he won a second bronze medal, running a 38.13 together with <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Brown_(sprinter)" title="Aaron Brown (sprinter)">Aaron Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brendon_Rodney" title="Brendon Rodney">Brendon Rodney</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Justyn_Warner" title="Justyn Warner">Justyn Warner</a>.</p>
<p>De Grasse turned professional in December 2015, signing with Puma for $11.25 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016_Summer_Olympics">2016 Summer Olympics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andre_De_Grasse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2016 Summer Olympics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>In July 2016, he was named to <a href="/wiki/Canada_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics#Athletics_.28track_and_field.29" title="Canada at the 2016 Summer Olympics">Canada's Olympic team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> De Grasse advanced to the finals of the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_100_metres" title="Athletics at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Men's 100 metres">100 m</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2016_Summer_Olympics" title="2016 Summer Olympics">2016 Summer Olympics</a> after running a time of 10.04 in his heat and then 9.92 in the semi-finals. De Grasse placed third and received the bronze medal behind <a href="/wiki/Usain_Bolt" title="Usain Bolt">Usain Bolt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Justin_Gatlin" title="Justin Gatlin">Justin Gatlin</a>, at 9.91 seconds, which was also his personal best time. He was the first male athlete to win a medal for Canada at the 2016 Summer Olympics. Bolt was visibly supportive of De Grasse during both the semis and finals, hugging him after the race and saying to reporters "He came through again. He's going to be good, he runs just like me, I mean he's really slow at the blocks but when he gets going, he gets going."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nextone_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-nextone-21">[21]</a></sup> De Grasse said of his relationship with Bolt after that "We were just having some fun. Me and Usain met back in January, we did a lot of things together. He feels like I'm the next one, and now I'm just trying to live up to it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nextone_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-nextone-21">[21]</a></sup> Former Canadian Olympic Champion <a href="/wiki/Donovan_Bailey" title="Donovan Bailey">Donovan Bailey</a> was seen jumping up and down in the <a href="/wiki/CBC_Sports" title="CBC Sports">CBC Sports</a> studio, cheering on De Grasse. After, he said, "I'm shaking. This is great for track and field in Canada."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nextone_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-nextone-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>De Grasse followed up his bronze medal win in the 100 m by advancing to the semi-finals of the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_200_metres" title="Athletics at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Men's 200 metres">200 m</a> with a time of 20.09, the fastest time in the first round. In the semi-finals, De Grasse set a new <a href="/wiki/List_of_Canadian_records_in_athletics" title="List of Canadian records in athletics">Canadian record</a> and advanced to the finals with a time of 19.80, thereby becoming the first Canadian to make it to the finals of the 200 m since <a href="/wiki/Atlee_Mahorn" title="Atlee Mahorn">Atlee Mahorn</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1988_Summer_Olympics" title="1988 Summer Olympics">1988 Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In the 200 m final, De Grasse won a silver medal taking 20.02 seconds to earn his second Olympic medal, the first Canadian to win a medal in the 200 m since <a href="/wiki/Percy_Williams_(sprinter)" title="Percy Williams (sprinter)">Percy Williams</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1928_Summer_Olympics" title="1928 Summer Olympics">1928 Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>.</p>
<p>De Grasse ran the anchor for the finals of the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_4_%C3%97_100_metres_relay" title="Athletics at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Men's 4 × 100 metres relay">4 x 100 m relay</a> and initially placed fourth with a Canadian record of 37.64s. However, the American team that placed third was disqualified for a zone violation (handing over the baton outside of the designated zone) at the first baton change, and so the Canadians were awarded the bronze medal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> De Grasse ran in the finals with <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Brown_(sprinter)" title="Aaron Brown (sprinter)">Aaron Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brendon_Rodney" title="Brendon Rodney">Brendon Rodney</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Akeem_Haynes" title="Akeem Haynes">Akeem Haynes</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Mobolade_Ajomale" title="Mobolade Ajomale">Mobolade Ajomale</a> ran in his stead in the heats.</p>
<p>De Grasse's success at the Olympics led to his winning the <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Conacher_Award" title="Lionel Conacher Award">Lionel Conacher Award</a> as the Canadian Press' male athlete of the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DeGrasse2014_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-DeGrasse2014-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
