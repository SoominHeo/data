<p><b>Henry François Becque</b> (9 April 1837 – 12 May 1899),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dramatist" title="Dramatist">dramatist</a>. He was born in Paris.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Henry_Becque&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1867, he wrote, in imitation of <a href="/wiki/Lord_Byron" title="Lord Byron">Lord Byron</a>, the libretto for <a href="/wiki/Victorin_de_Jonci%C3%A8res" title="Victorin de Joncières">Victorin de Joncières</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">opera</a> <i>Sardanapale</i>, but his first important work, <i>Michel Pauper</i>, appeared in 1870. The importance of this sombre drama was first realized when it was revived at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Od%C3%A9on" title="Odéon">Odéon</a> in 1886. <i>Les Corbeaux</i> (1882) established Becque's position as an innovator, and in 1885 he produced his most successful play, <i>La Parisienne</i>. Becque produced little during the last years of his life, but his disciples carried on the tradition he had created.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChisholm1911_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChisholm1911-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>His other works include <i>Querelles littéraires</i> (1890), and <i>Souvenirs d'un auteur dramatique</i> (1895), consisting chiefly of reprinted articles in which he does not spare his opponents. His <i>Théâtre complet</i> (3 vols., 1899) includes <i>L'Enfant prodigue</i> (Vaudeville Theatre, 6 November 1868); <i>Michel Pauper</i> (Théâtre de la Porte-Saint-Martin, 17 June 1870); <i>L'Enlèvement</i> (Vaudeville, 18 November 1871); <i>La Navette</i> (Gymnase, 15 November 1878); <i>Les Honnêtes Femmes</i> (Gymnase, 1 January 1880); <i>Les Corbeaux</i> (<a href="/wiki/Com%C3%A9die-Fran%C3%A7aise" title="Comédie-Française">Comédie-Française</a>, 14 September 1882); and <i>La Parisienne</i> (Théâtre de la Renaissance, 7 February 1885).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChisholm1911_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChisholm1911-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
