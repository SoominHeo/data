<p><b>Ensemble Studios</b> was a video game developer initially established in 1995 as an independent company by <a href="/wiki/Tony_Goodman" title="Tony Goodman">Tony Goodman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Goodman" title="Rick Goodman">Rick Goodman</a> and John Boog-Scott, but was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> in 2001 and operated as an internal studio until 2009, when it was officially disbanded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Ensemble developed many <a href="/wiki/Real-time_strategy" title="Real-time strategy">real-time strategy</a> games, including the <i><a href="/wiki/Age_of_Empires" title="Age of Empires">Age of Empires</a></i> game series, <i><a href="/wiki/Age_of_Mythology" title="Age of Mythology">Age of Mythology</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Halo_Wars" title="Halo Wars">Halo Wars</a></i>. In addition to game development, Ensemble Studios also made the <a href="/wiki/Genie_Engine" title="Genie Engine">Genie Game Engine</a> used in <i><a href="/wiki/Age_of_Empires_(video_game)" title="Age of Empires (video game)">Age of Empires</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Age_of_Empires_II:_The_Age_of_Kings" title="Age of Empires II: The Age of Kings">Age of Empires II: The Age of Kings</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_Galactic_Battlegrounds" title="Star Wars: Galactic Battlegrounds">Star Wars: Galactic Battlegrounds</a></i>. The studio sold 20 million games and was worth an estimated $500 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Games">Games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ensemble_Studios&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ensemble Studios developed the <i><a href="/wiki/Age_of_Empires" title="Age of Empires">Age of Empires</a></i> game series of <a href="/wiki/Real-time_strategy" title="Real-time strategy">real-time strategy</a> titles, comprising <i><a href="/wiki/Age_of_Empires_(video_game)" title="Age of Empires (video game)">Age of Empires</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Age_of_Empires_II:_The_Age_of_Kings" title="Age of Empires II: The Age of Kings">Age of Empires II: The Age of Kings</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Age_of_Empires_III" title="Age of Empires III">Age of Empires III</a></i>. They also released <i><a href="/wiki/Age_of_Mythology" title="Age of Mythology">Age of Mythology</a></i>, a spin-off from the original series. <a href="/wiki/Expansion_pack" title="Expansion pack">Expansion packs</a> were also released for all their games including two for <i>Age of Empires III</i>. Their last release was the real-time strategy game called <i><a href="/wiki/Halo_Wars" title="Halo Wars">Halo Wars</a></i> for Xbox 360.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Closure_and_legacy">Closure and legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ensemble_Studios&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Closure and legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2001, Microsoft acquired Ensemble Studios.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Ensemble remained at their original location in a high rise office in Dallas, TX, until April 2008 when Microsoft moved them to the Shops at Legacy in Plano, the same location as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GearBox_Software" title="GearBox Software">GearBox Software</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="GearBox was never located in the Shops at Legacy. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Their office was 50,000 square feet and designed to house 120 employees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2008, Ensemble announced that it would close after the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Halo_Wars" title="Halo Wars">Halo Wars</a></i> in 2009. According to multiple independent reports, all non-essential staff have been laid off and remaining staff have been given incentives to remain until the completion of the project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ensembleclosing_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ensembleclosing-10">[10]</a></sup> Microsoft issued an internal statement on September 10, 2008 which was then leaked to the public.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ensembleclosureconfirmation_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ensembleclosureconfirmation-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The company shut down on January 29, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ensemblestudiosblog_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-ensemblestudiosblog-12">[12]</a></sup> It was also stated that there are at least two new studios being formed by ES employees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ensemblestudiosblogentry_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-ensemblestudiosblogentry-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2009, former Ensemble Studios head <a href="/wiki/Tony_Goodman" title="Tony Goodman">Tony Goodman</a> started a new independent studio, <a href="/wiki/Robot_Entertainment" title="Robot Entertainment">Robot Entertainment</a>, and a number of the existing employees have been offered a position in this company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the announcement of Robot Entertainment, former Ensemble Studios producer David Rippy started a new independent studio, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bonfire_Studios" title="Bonfire Studios">Bonfire Studios</a>, composed entirely of former Ensemble staff members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Bonfire was later renamed <a href="/wiki/Zynga_Dallas" title="Zynga Dallas">Zynga Dallas</a> through its acquisition by Zynga and released only one game as an independent company.</p>
<p>In 2008, a third studio called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newtoy,_Inc." title="Newtoy, Inc.">Newtoy, Inc.</a> was created by several developers from Ensemble,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> which released <i>Chess With Friends</i> for the <a href="/wiki/IPhone" title="IPhone">iPhone</a> in November 2008,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and <i>Words With Friends</i> in August 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Newtoy was also acquired by Zynga and renamed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zynga_With_Friends" title="Zynga With Friends">Zynga With Friends</a>, a moniker off of Newtoy's successful 'with friends' series. Newtoy released two games while they were independent.</p>
<p>In March 2009, a fourth studio, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Windstorm_Studios&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Windstorm Studios (page does not exist)">Windstorm Studios</a> was founded by ex-staffer Dusty Monk as a one-man company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Dusty Monk later closed this studio on March 21, 2012 and joined Robot Entertainment with his former co-workers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2013, a fifth studio emerged, <a href="/wiki/Boss_Fight_Entertainment" title="Boss Fight Entertainment">Boss Fight Entertainment</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> was founded by previous employees of Zynga Dallas and Ensemble Studios. Boss Fight Entertainment is currently located in McKinney, TX and is developing games for mobile platforms.</p>
