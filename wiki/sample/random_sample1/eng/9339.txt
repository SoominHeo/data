<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ensemble_cast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The structure of an ensemble cast contrasts with the popular Hollywood centralization of a sole protagonist, as the ensemble leans more towards a sense of "collectivity and community".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-math_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-math-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ensemble casts in film were introduced as early as September 1916, with <a href="/wiki/D._W._Griffith" title="D. W. Griffith">D. W. Griffith</a>'s silent <a href="/wiki/Epic_film" title="Epic film">epic film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Intolerance_(film)" title="Intolerance (film)">Intolerance</a></i>, featuring four separate though parallel plots.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smith_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-smith-4">[4]</a></sup> The film follows the lives of several characters over hundreds of years, across different cultures and time periods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The unification of different plot lines and character arcs is a key characteristic of ensemble casting in film; whether it's a location, event, or an overarching theme that ties the film and characters together.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smith_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-smith-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Films that feature ensembles tend to emphasize the interconnectivity of the characters, even when the characters are strangers to one another.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-silvey_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-silvey-6">[6]</a></sup> The interconnectivity is often shown to the audience through examples of the "<a href="/wiki/Six_degrees_of_separation" title="Six degrees of separation">six degrees of separation</a>" theory, and allows them to navigate through plot lines using <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cognitive_mapping" title="Cognitive mapping">cognitive mapping</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-silvey_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-silvey-6">[6]</a></sup> Examples of this method, where the six degrees of separation is evident in films with an ensemble cast, are in productions such as <i><a href="/wiki/Babel_(film)" title="Babel (film)">Babel</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Love,_Actually" title="Love, Actually">Love, Actually</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Crash_(2004_film)" title="Crash (2004 film)">Crash</a></i>, which all have strong underlying themes interwoven within the plots that unify each film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smith_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-smith-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cinema">Cinema</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ensemble_cast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cinema">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Other forms of narrative for films with ensemble casts having more or less equal amounts of screen time is demonstrated in recent productions such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Avengers_(2012_film)" title="The Avengers (2012 film)">The Avengers</a></i>, where the cast and their characters have already been established in individual films prior to its release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In <i>The Avengers</i>, there is no need for a protagonist in the feature as each character shares equal importance in the narrative, successfully balancing the ensemble cast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Referential acting is a key factor in executing this balance, as ensemble cast members "play off each other rather than off reality".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-math_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-math-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ensemble_cast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ensemble casting also became more popular in <a href="/wiki/Television_program" title="Television program">television series</a> because it allows flexibility for writers to focus on different characters in different episodes. In addition, the departure of players is less disruptive to the premise than it would be if the star of a production with a regularly structured cast were to leave the series. The television series <i><a href="/wiki/Friends" title="Friends">Friends</a></i> is an archetypal example of an ensemble cast occurring in an American sitcom. Ensemble casts of 20 or more actors are common in <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap operas</a>, a genre that relies heavily on the character development of the ensemble.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The genre also requires continuous expansion of the cast as the series progresses, with soap operas such as <i><a href="/wiki/Days_of_Our_Lives" title="Days of Our Lives">Days of Our Lives</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bold_and_the_Beautiful" title="The Bold and the Beautiful">The Bold and the Beautiful</a></i> staying on air for decades.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>In recent years, there have been numerous successes for television in ensemble casting; the most notable being the epic fantasy <i><a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a></i> series <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones">Game of Thrones</a></i>. The <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> winning series features one of the largest ensemble casts on the small screen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The series is notorious for major character deaths, resulting in constant changes within the ensemble.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Other programmes include the <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> original series <i><a href="/wiki/Orange_Is_the_New_Black" title="Orange Is the New Black">Orange Is the New Black</a></i>, known for its diverse cast, and flashbacks to almost all of the characters.</p>
