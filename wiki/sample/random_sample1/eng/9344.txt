<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ankara_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Ankara" title="History of Ankara">History of Ankara</a></div>
<p>The site of the modern city has been home to settlements by many historic Anatolian civilizations in antiquity and classical times, including <a href="/wiki/Phrygians" title="Phrygians">Phrygians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lydians" title="Lydians">Lydians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire" title="Achaemenid Empire">Persians</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alexander_the_Great" title="Alexander the Great">Alexander the Great</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Romans</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galatians_(People)" title="Galatians (People)">Galatians</a>. The city of <a href="/wiki/Ankara" title="Ankara">Ankara</a> became a fortified stronghold of the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantines</a>; it fell to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seljuk_Turks" title="Seljuk Turks">Seljuk Turks</a> and later the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>. It was finally chosen by <a href="/wiki/Mustafa_Kemal_Atat%C3%BCrk" title="Mustafa Kemal Atatürk">Mustafa Kemal Atatürk</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_National_Movement" title="Turkish National Movement">Turkish National Movement</a> as the site of the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_the_Grand_National_Assembly" title="Government of the Grand National Assembly">provisional government</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Grand_National_Assembly_of_Turkey" title="Grand National Assembly of Turkey">Turkish parliament</a> in 1920, and in 1923 as the capital city of the newly declared <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Turkey" title="Republic of Turkey">Republic of Turkey</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ankara_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ankara is mostly in the <a href="/wiki/Central_Anatolia_Region" title="Central Anatolia Region">Central Anatolia</a> region, and partly in the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea_Region" title="Black Sea Region">Black Sea</a> region. Ankara has mountain forests to its north, and the dry plain of <a href="/wiki/Konya" title="Konya">Konya</a> to its south. The province is irrigated by the <a href="/wiki/K%C4%B1z%C4%B1l%C4%B1rmak_River" title="Kızılırmak River">Kızılırmak</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sakarya_River" title="Sakarya River">Sakarya River</a> systems, the Sarıyar reservoir and many natural lakes and pools. 50% of the land is used for agriculture, 28% is forest and another 10% is meadow and grazing land. <a href="/wiki/Lake_Tuz" title="Lake Tuz">Lake Tuz</a>, the second largest lake in Turkey, partly lies in the Şereflikoçhisar district of the province. The highest point of the province is the 2,015 meters tall Işık Mountain in the Kızılcahamam district.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ankara_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The climate is hot and dry in summer, rainy in spring and autumn, cold and snowy in winter, wetter in the north of the province than the drier plains to the south. The annual precipitation in the city of Ankara located the center of the province is 415 mm,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> the annual precipitation of <a href="/wiki/K%C4%B1z%C4%B1lcahamam" title="Kızılcahamam">Kızılcahamam</a> located in the north of the province is 592 mm (1.942 ft), and the annual precipitation of <a href="/wiki/%C5%9Eerefliko%C3%A7hisar" title="Şereflikoçhisar">Şereflikoçhisar</a> located in the southeast of the province is 357 mm (14.1 in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
