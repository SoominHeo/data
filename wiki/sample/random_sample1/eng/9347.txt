<p>A <b>beauty mark</b> or <b>beauty spot</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Euphemism" title="Euphemism">euphemism</a> for a type of dark facial <a href="/wiki/Melanocytic_nevus" title="Melanocytic nevus">mole</a>, so named because such <a href="/wiki/Birthmark" title="Birthmark">birthmarks</a> are sometimes considered an attractive feature.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ariel_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ariel-2">[2]</a></sup> Medically, such "beauty marks" are generally <a href="/wiki/Melanocytic_nevus" title="Melanocytic nevus">melanocytic nevus</a>, more specifically the compound variant. Moles of this type may also be located elsewhere on the body, and may also be considered beauty marks if located on the face, shoulder, neck or breast.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fashionable_beauty_marks">Fashionable beauty marks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beauty_mark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Fashionable beauty marks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the 20th century, <a href="/wiki/Marilyn_Monroe" title="Marilyn Monroe">Marilyn Monroe</a>'s beauty mark generated a new vogue.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Fashion model <a href="/wiki/Cindy_Crawford" title="Cindy Crawford">Cindy Crawford</a>'s prominent mole helped revive the fashion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-white_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-white-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artificial_beauty_mark">Artificial beauty mark</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beauty_mark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Artificial beauty mark">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Monroe_piercing.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1580" data-file-width="1178" height="201" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Monroe_piercing.jpg/150px-Monroe_piercing.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Monroe_piercing.jpg/225px-Monroe_piercing.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Monroe_piercing.jpg/300px-Monroe_piercing.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Monroe_piercing.jpg" title="Aumentar"></a></div>
A <a href="/wiki/Monroe_piercing" title="Monroe piercing">Monroe piercing</a> is a lip piercing placed off-center, above the upper lip meant to resemble <a href="/wiki/Marilyn_Monroe" title="Marilyn Monroe">Marilyn Monroe</a>'s beauty mark</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>False beauty marks are sometimes applied to the face as a form of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Make-up" title="Make-up">make-up</a>. Beauty marks were particularly highly regarded during the eighteenth century and creating false ones became common, often in fanciful shapes such as hearts or stars. They could be purchased as silk or velvet patches known as "mouches" (flies). <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Pope" title="Alexander Pope">Alexander Pope</a>'s 1712 poem <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rape_of_the_Lock" title="The Rape of the Lock">The Rape of the Lock</a></i> mentions such patches as indicators of "secular love":<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dd>Here Files of Pins extend their shining Rows,</dd>
<dd>Puffs, Powders, Patches, Bibles, Billet-doux.</dd>
<dd>Now awful Beauty puts on all its Arms;</dd>
<dd>The Fair each moment rises in her Charms,</dd>
<dd>Repairs her Smiles, awakens ev'ry Grace,</dd>
<dd>And calls forth all the Wonders of her Face;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Monroe_piercing" title="Monroe piercing">Monroe piercing</a> has gained popularity in recent years as a flexible way of approximating a beauty mark.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_media">In media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beauty_mark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the conclusion of the book <i><a href="/wiki/The_Silence_of_the_Lambs_(novel)" title="The Silence of the Lambs (novel)">The Silence of the Lambs</a></i>, the heroine <a href="/wiki/Clarice_Starling" title="Clarice Starling">Clarice Starling</a> gains an artificial beauty mark when burnt gunpowder gets lodged in the flesh of her cheek. She retains this mark in the sequel novel "Hannibal". This symbolism (along with Dr Lecter's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polydactylism" title="Polydactylism">polydactylism</a>) did not get carried over into the film.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
