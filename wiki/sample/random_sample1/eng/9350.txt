<p><i><b>Androsace filiformis</b></i> is a species of flowering plant in the <a href="/wiki/Primulaceae" title="Primulaceae">primrose family</a> known by the common names <b>filiform rockjasmine</b> and <b>slender-stemmed androsace</b>.</p>
<p>It is an uncommon plant native to the <a href="/wiki/Northwestern_United_States" title="Northwestern United States">Northwestern United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Utah" title="Utah">Utah</a>. It is also known from <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>.</p>
<p>It grows in mountain meadows, including in the <a href="/wiki/Cascade_Range" title="Cascade Range">Cascade Range</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountains" title="Rocky Mountains">Rocky Mountains</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Androsace_filiformis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Androsace filiformis</i> is a small annual herb forming hairy to hairless patches up to 12 centimeters tall. The basal rosette contains finely toothed leaves up to 2 centimeters long, often much smaller, and oval to nearly triangular in shape.</p>
<p>There are generally several open <a href="/wiki/Umbel" title="Umbel">umbels</a> of tiny five-lobed white flowers on long <a href="/wiki/Pedicel_(botany)" title="Pedicel (botany)">pedicels</a>.</p>
