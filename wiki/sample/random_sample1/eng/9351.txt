<p><b>Pilumnoidea</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Taxonomic_rank" title="Taxonomic rank">superfamily</a> of <a href="/wiki/Crab" title="Crab">crabs</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ng_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ng-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grave_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Grave-2">[2]</a></sup> whose members were previously included in the <a href="/wiki/Xanthoidea" title="Xanthoidea">Xanthoidea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Martin_.26_Davis_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Martin_.26_Davis-3">[3]</a></sup> The three families are unified by the free <a href="/wiki/Joint" title="Joint">articulation</a> of all the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Body_segment" title="Body segment">segments</a> of the male crab's <a href="/wiki/Abdomen" title="Abdomen">abdomen</a> and by the form of the <a href="/wiki/Gonopod" title="Gonopod">gonopods</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grave_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Grave-2">[2]</a></sup> The earliest fossils assigned to this group are of <a href="/wiki/Eocene" title="Eocene">Eocene</a> age.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">Classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pilumnoidea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Classification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
