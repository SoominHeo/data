<p>The <b>Anaheim Amigos</b>/<b>Los Angeles Stars</b> were a charter member <a href="/wiki/American_Basketball_Association" title="American Basketball Association">American Basketball Association</a> (ABA) team based in <a href="/wiki/Southern_California" title="Southern California">Southern California</a>. They were the Amigos during their first season in Anaheim and later moved to Los Angeles to become the Stars. The team existed from 1967 to 1976. In 1970, it moved from southern California to Utah.</p>
<p>The Amigos were the first professional team to self-identify as being located in Anaheim; while the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Angels_of_Anaheim" title="Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim">Angels</a> played in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anaheim_Stadium" title="Anaheim Stadium">Anaheim Stadium</a> at the time, they would not use "Anaheim" in their name until the 1990s.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Franchise_history">Franchise history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anaheim_Amigos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Franchise history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anaheim_Amigos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With the founding of the ABA on February 2, 1967, a charter franchise in <a href="/wiki/Anaheim,_California" title="Anaheim, California">Anaheim, California</a> was awarded to <a href="/wiki/Art_Kim" title="Art Kim">Art Kim</a> and James Ackerman for $30,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> A 'name the team' contest resulted in the Amigos name. The team played most of its home games at the <a href="/wiki/Anaheim_Convention_Center" title="Anaheim Convention Center">Anaheim Convention Center</a>. Five home games were scheduled elsewhere in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> and three home games were scheduled in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honolulu,_Hawaii" title="Honolulu, Hawaii">Honolulu, Hawaii</a>. <a href="/wiki/Al_Brightman" title="Al Brightman">Al Brightman</a> was the first head coach. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-remembertheaba1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-remembertheaba1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1967.E2.80.9368_season">1967–68 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anaheim_Amigos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1967–68 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Amigos' roster was highlighted by guards <a href="/wiki/Les_Selvage" title="Les Selvage">Les Selvage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Congdon" title="Jeff Congdon">Jeff Congdon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steve_Chubin" title="Steve Chubin">Steve Chubin</a>, former NBA player <a href="/wiki/Ben_Warley" title="Ben Warley">Ben Warley</a> and 7 foot center <a href="/wiki/Larry_Bunce" title="Larry Bunce">Larry Bunce</a>. Chubin led the team in scoring and assists and was a fan favorite. Selvage led the league in three point field goal attempts. Warley led the team in rebounds and was an effective outside shooter. Congdon played well but was traded in mid-season to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Denver_Rockets" title="Denver Rockets">Denver Rockets</a> for <a href="/wiki/Willis_Thomas" title="Willis Thomas">Willis Thomas</a>. Bunce did not live up to expectations despite his height, but played in the <a href="/wiki/1968_ABA_All-Star_Game" title="1968 ABA All-Star Game">1968 ABA All-Star Game</a>, as did Warley.</p>
<p>The Amigos were not successful on the court. They lost their first five games, including the first ever ABA game, a 134-129 loss to the <a href="/wiki/Oakland_Oaks_(ABA)" title="Oakland Oaks (ABA)">Oakland Oaks</a> on the road. After losing two thirds of their first 36 games Brightman was fired and replaced as head coach by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Dinnel" title="Harry Dinnel">Harry Dinnel</a>. During the season the Amigos lost eight straight games in one stretch and had two other stretches of six losses each. The team finished the season with 25 wins and 53 losses, good for fifth place in Western Division but not good enough to make the playoffs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-remembertheaba1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-remembertheaba1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Move_to_Los_Angeles">Move to Los Angeles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anaheim_Amigos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Move to Los Angeles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The Amigos were not successful off the court either. They averaged 1,293 fans per home game and their games were broadcast on radio and sometimes on television. However, they lost approximately $500,000 on the season and were sold for $450,000 to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=James_J._Kirst&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="James J. Kirst (page does not exist)">James J. Kirst</a> who moved the team to nearby <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Memorial_Sports_Arena" title="Los Angeles Memorial Sports Arena">Los Angeles Memorial Sports Arena</a>, where they became the Stars. After two seasons in Los Angeles the team relocated again and became the <a href="/wiki/Utah_Stars" title="Utah Stars">Utah Stars</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Move_to_Salt_Lake_City.2C_Utah">Move to Salt Lake City, Utah</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anaheim_Amigos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Move to Salt Lake City, Utah">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the 1969-70 season, the franchise once again relocated, this time to Salt Lake City, Utah.</p>
