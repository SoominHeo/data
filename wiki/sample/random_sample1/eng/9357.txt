<p><b>Adam Charles Goldberg</b> (born October 25, 1970) is an American actor, director, producer, and musician. Known for his supporting roles in film and television, Goldberg has appeared in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Dazed_and_Confused_(film)" title="Dazed and Confused (film)">Dazed and Confused</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Saving_Private_Ryan" title="Saving Private Ryan">Saving Private Ryan</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Beautiful_Mind_(film)" title="A Beautiful Mind (film)">A Beautiful Mind</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Zodiac_(film)" title="Zodiac (film)">Zodiac</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hebrew_Hammer" title="The Hebrew Hammer">The Hebrew Hammer</a></i>. His TV appearances include the shows <i><a href="/wiki/Friends" title="Friends">Friends</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Joey_(TV_series)" title="Joey (TV series)">Joey</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Entourage_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Entourage (U.S. TV series)">Entourage</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jim_Gaffigan_Show" title="The Jim Gaffigan Show">The Jim Gaffigan Show</a></i>, and his critically acclaimed role as hitman <a href="/wiki/Mr._Wrench_and_Mr._Numbers" title="Mr. Wrench and Mr. Numbers">Mr. Numbers</a> in the first season of <i><a href="/wiki/Fargo_(TV_series)" title="Fargo (TV series)">Fargo</a></i>. Goldberg will appear in The Cullen Brothers' first film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Going_Under_(2016_film)" title="Going Under (2016 film)">Going Under</a></i>, set for release in 2016.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adam_Goldberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Goldberg was born in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Monica,_California" title="Santa Monica, California">Santa Monica, California</a>, the son of Donna (née Goebel)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goebel_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Goebel-1">[1]</a></sup> and Earl Goldberg, a former lifeguard who ran a wholesale food business.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> His father is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jew" title="Jew">Jewish</a>; his mother is a <a href="/wiki/Lapsed_Catholic" title="Lapsed Catholic">non-practicing</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a>, of Mexican,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Irish, French, and German descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He lives in Los Angeles with his dog, Digger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kristin_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-kristin-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2014, Goldberg and his girlfriend, artist and designer<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Roxanne Daner had a son named Bud.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Previously, the couple had suffered a <a href="/wiki/Stillbirth" title="Stillbirth">stillbirth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adam_Goldberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adam_Goldberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Goldberg's first major screen role was in the <a href="/wiki/Billy_Crystal" title="Billy Crystal">Billy Crystal</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Saturday_Night" title="Mr. Saturday Night">Mr. Saturday Night</a></i> (1992). His career-making role was arguably that of the tough, wise-cracking <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Infantryman" title="Infantryman">infantryman</a> Mellish in <a href="/wiki/Steven_Spielberg" title="Steven Spielberg">Steven Spielberg</a>'s 1998 film <i><a href="/wiki/Saving_Private_Ryan" title="Saving Private Ryan">Saving Private Ryan</a></i>. While he played a lead character in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hebrew_Hammer" title="The Hebrew Hammer">The Hebrew Hammer</a></i>, Goldberg has mostly been cast in supporting roles.</p>
<p>Notable roles include Jerry, the <a href="/wiki/Undead" title="Undead">undead</a> servant to <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Walken" title="Christopher Walken">Christopher Walken</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Gabriel" title="Gabriel">Angel Gabriel</a> in the supernatural thriller <i><a href="/wiki/The_Prophecy" title="The Prophecy">The Prophecy</a></i>. Goldberg has also worked with preeminent voice artists like <a href="/wiki/Dan_Castellaneta" title="Dan Castellaneta">Dan Castellaneta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Adler" title="Charlie Adler">Charlie Adler</a>, his vocal performances in <i><a href="/wiki/Babe:_Pig_in_the_City" title="Babe: Pig in the City">Babe: Pig in the City</a></i> and cartoons like <i><a href="/wiki/Eek!_The_Cat" title="Eek! The Cat">Eek! The Cat</a></i>. Goldberg often portrays stereotypically Jewish characters (most notably in <i><a href="/wiki/Dazed_and_Confused_(film)" title="Dazed and Confused (film)">Dazed and Confused</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hebrew_Hammer" title="The Hebrew Hammer">The Hebrew Hammer</a></i> and in the TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Entourage_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Entourage (U.S. TV series)">Entourage</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Goldberg appeared extensively in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flaming_Lips" title="Flaming Lips">Flaming Lips</a> documentary <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fearless_Freaks" title="The Fearless Freaks">The Fearless Freaks</a></i>, and had a supporting role in <i><a href="/wiki/Christmas_on_Mars" title="Christmas on Mars">Christmas on Mars</a></i>, a science fiction film written and directed by Lips frontman <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Coyne" title="Wayne Coyne">Wayne Coyne</a>. In 1999, he appeared in the '<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sixpence_None_The_Richer" title="Sixpence None The Richer">Sixpence None The Richer</a>' music video "<a href="/wiki/There_She_Goes" title="There She Goes">There She Goes</a>".</p>
<p>Goldberg wrote, produced, directed and edited the features <i>Scotch and Milk</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/I_Love_Your_Work" title="I Love Your Work">I Love Your Work</a></i>, as well as multiple television projects, notably including the philosophical travelogue, <i>Running with the Bulls</i> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Independent_Film_Channel" title="Independent Film Channel">IFC</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adam_Goldberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A guitarist and songwriter, Goldberg composed and arranged the music to the <i>I Love Your Work</i> and <i>Running with the Bulls</i> and provided a song for the <i>Hebrew Hammer</i> soundtrack. He is known to travel with a cheap acoustic guitar, as can be seen in <i>Running with the Bulls</i>. Goldberg has released rock and jazz albums, including <i>Changes,</i> with Latin jazz percussionist Phil Maturano.</p>
<p>He plays in a group named LANDy. LANDy's debut album, "Eros and Omissions" was released on June 23, 2009. Flaming Lips drummer <a href="/wiki/Steven_Drozd" title="Steven Drozd">Steven Drozd</a>, with whom Goldberg collaborated on the score for <i>I Love Your Work</i>, performed on the record. Earlimart's Aaron Espinoza is credited as having done the final mix as well as having engineered many of the more recent songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WiredReview_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-WiredReview-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On June 7, 2011, Goldberg performed as lead singer with his group The Goldberg Sisters, performing "Shush" on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Late_Late_Show_with_Craig_Ferguson" title="The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson">The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adam_Goldberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1995, Goldberg appeared in a signature wise-cracking role as delivery boy Leo in the television comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Double_Rush" title="Double Rush">Double Rush</a></i>. Goldberg then appeared in a three episode arc in the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/Friends" title="Friends">Friends</a></i> (1996) as <a href="/wiki/Chandler_Bing" title="Chandler Bing">Chandler's</a> crazy roommate Eddie. He appeared some years later in a nine episode arc in season two of the <i>Friends</i> spinoff show <i><a href="/wiki/Joey_(TV_series)" title="Joey (TV series)">Joey</a></i> as Jimmy, <a href="/wiki/Joey_Tribbiani" title="Joey Tribbiani">Joey Tribbiani</a>'s best friend from high school. He also appeared as a main character in the short-lived 2005 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fox_Network" title="Fox Network">Fox</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Head_Cases" title="Head Cases">Head Cases</a></i>.</p>
<p>Goldberg appeared in the short-lived ensemble cop show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Unusuals" title="The Unusuals">The Unusuals</a></i>, playing a detective in New York City with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brain_cancer" title="Brain cancer">brain cancer</a> who refuses treatment because of his dislike of doctors. His character was stated as being "sarcastic" and Goldberg had been described as "one of the better reasons to watch".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NJReview_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-NJReview-15">[15]</a></sup> He subsequently appeared in the short-lived ensemble cop show <i><a href="/wiki/NYC_22" title="NYC 22">NYC 22</a>.</i></p>
<p>Goldberg played the role of Dave Marks, a struggling comedian and <a href="/wiki/Jim_Gaffigan" title="Jim Gaffigan">Jim Gaffigan's</a> best friend, for two seasons on the <a href="/wiki/TV_Land" title="TV Land">TV Land</a> sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jim_Gaffigan_Show" title="The Jim Gaffigan Show">The Jim Gaffigan Show</a></i>.</p>
