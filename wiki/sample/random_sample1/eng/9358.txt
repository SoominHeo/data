<p><b>Adam Beach</b> (born November 11, 1972) is a <a href="/wiki/Saulteaux" title="Saulteaux">Saulteaux</a> actor. He is best known for his roles as Victor in <i><a href="/wiki/Smoke_Signals_(film)" title="Smoke Signals (film)">Smoke Signals</a></i>, Frank Fencepost in <i>Dance Me Outside</i>, Tommy in <i><a href="/wiki/Walker,_Texas_Ranger" title="Walker, Texas Ranger">Walker, Texas Ranger</a></i>, Kickin' Wing in <i><a href="/wiki/Joe_Dirt" title="Joe Dirt">Joe Dirt</a></i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Marine" title="U.S. Marine">U.S. Marine</a> <a href="/wiki/Corporal" title="Corporal">Corporal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ira_Hayes" title="Ira Hayes">Ira Hayes</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Flags_of_Our_Fathers_(film)" title="Flags of Our Fathers (film)">Flags of Our Fathers</a></i>, Private Ben Yahzee in <i><a href="/wiki/Windtalkers" title="Windtalkers">Windtalkers</a></i>, Dr. <a href="/wiki/Charles_Eastman" title="Charles Eastman">Charles Eastman</a> (Ohiyesa) in <i><a href="/wiki/Bury_My_Heart_at_Wounded_Knee_(film)" title="Bury My Heart at Wounded Knee (film)">Bury My Heart at Wounded Knee</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Chester_Lake_(Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit)" title="Chester Lake (Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit)">Chester Lake</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Jim_Chee" title="Jim Chee">Officer Jim Chee</a> in the film adaptations of <i><a href="/wiki/Skinwalkers_(2002_film)" title="Skinwalkers (2002 film)">Skinwalkers</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Coyote_Waits" title="Coyote Waits">Coyote Waits</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/A_Thief_of_Time" title="A Thief of Time">A Thief of Time</a></i>. He starred in the Canadian 2012-2014 series <i><a href="/wiki/Arctic_Air" title="Arctic Air">Arctic Air</a></i>, and played <a href="/wiki/Slipknot_(comics)" title="Slipknot (comics)">Slipknot</a> in the 2016 film <i><a href="/wiki/Suicide_Squad_(film)" title="Suicide Squad (film)">Suicide Squad</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adam_Beach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beach was born in <a href="/wiki/Ashern,_Manitoba" title="Ashern, Manitoba">Ashern, Manitoba</a>. His ancestry is mainly <a href="/wiki/Aboriginal_peoples_in_Canada" title="Aboriginal peoples in Canada">Native Canadian</a> but he also has some <a href="/wiki/Icelandic_people_of_American_descent" title="Icelandic people of American descent">Icelandic</a> roots through his great-grandmother.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> As a member of the <a href="/wiki/Saulteaux" title="Saulteaux">Saulteaux</a> <a href="/wiki/First_Nations" title="First Nations">First Nations</a>, Beach spent his early years with his two brothers on the Lake Manitoba/Dog Creek First Nation Reserve at <a href="/wiki/Lake_Manitoba" title="Lake Manitoba">Lake Manitoba</a>. When Beach was eight years old, his mother, Sally Beach, eight months pregnant with a baby girl, was killed by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drunk_driving" title="Drunk driving">drunk driver</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NorthernStars_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NorthernStars-1">[1]</a></sup> Eight weeks after his mother's death, the family, stricken with grief, discovered Adam's father, Dennis, drowned near the community. It is unclear whether Dennis's death was an accident or if he committed suicide. Adam and his two brothers initially went to live with their grandmother. He lived with his grandmother from age 8 to age 12. Five years after their parents' deaths, the three brothers were taken to <a href="/wiki/Winnipeg" title="Winnipeg">Winnipeg</a> to live with an aunt and uncle: Agnes and Chris Beach (his father's brother). Adam refers to his Uncle Chris as "Dad".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GlobeAndMail_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-GlobeAndMail-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Beach attended a drama class at <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Bell_High_School" title="Gordon Bell High School">Gordon Bell High School</a>. He began performing in local theatre productions, and eventually dropped out of school to take a lead role at the <a href="/wiki/Manitoba_Theatre_for_Young_People" title="Manitoba Theatre for Young People">Manitoba Theatre for Young People</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adam_Beach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At age 18, Beach won a small role in the miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_in_the_Barrens" title="Lost in the Barrens">Lost in the Barrens</a></i>, based on the <a href="/wiki/Farley_Mowat" title="Farley Mowat">Farley Mowat</a> novel. In the following years, he continued to work in local theatre and guest-starred on television shows like <i><a href="/wiki/Walker,_Texas_Ranger" title="Walker, Texas Ranger">Walker, Texas Ranger</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Touched_by_an_Angel" title="Touched by an Angel">Touched by an Angel</a></i>. Shortly afterwards, Beach was cast as a regular on the television shows <i><a href="/wiki/North_of_60" title="North of 60">North of 60</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rez" title="The Rez">The Rez</a></i>.</p>
<p>After his performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Dance_Me_Outside" title="Dance Me Outside">Dance Me Outside</a></i> he starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Smoke_Signals_(film)" title="Smoke Signals (film)">Smoke Signals</a></i>, which was nominated for the Grand Jury Prize at the <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NorthernStars_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-NorthernStars-1">[1]</a></sup> He took a small role in Disney's <i><a href="/wiki/Mystery,_Alaska" title="Mystery, Alaska">Mystery, Alaska</a></i> in 1999 and in 2000 accepted a role in the thriller, <i>The Last Stop</i>. Beach then appeared in <a href="/wiki/Helen_Lee_(director)" title="Helen Lee (director)">Helen Lee</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Art_of_Woo" title="The Art of Woo">The Art of Woo</a></i> and the 2001 hit <i><a href="/wiki/Joe_Dirt" title="Joe Dirt">Joe Dirt</a></i> as Kickin' Wing, a Native American fireworks salesman. The producers of <i>Joe Dirt</i> had originally wanted to hire <a href="/wiki/Evan_Adams" title="Evan Adams">Evan Adams</a> for the parody of his character in <i>Smoke Signals</i>, but accidentally called Beach instead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2002, <i><a href="/wiki/Windtalkers" title="Windtalkers">Windtalkers</a></i>, starring Beach, came out in theatres. To prepare for this role, Beach spent six months learning the <a href="/wiki/Navajo_language" title="Navajo language">Navajo language</a>. He was also able to use this skill in his next project, <i><a href="/wiki/Skinwalkers_(2002_film)" title="Skinwalkers (2002 film)">Skinwalkers</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Eyre" title="Chris Eyre">Chris Eyre</a>, who gave him his breakout role in <i><a href="/wiki/Smoke_Signals_(film)" title="Smoke Signals (film)">Smoke Signals</a></i>.</p>
<p>Between large-scale projects, Beach made small television appearances on such shows as <i><a href="/wiki/JAG_(TV_series)" title="JAG (TV series)">JAG</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Third_Watch" title="Third Watch">Third Watch</a></i>. Also, in 2003, he returned to the character that he first portrayed in <i><a href="/wiki/Skinwalkers_(2002_film)" title="Skinwalkers (2002 film)">Skinwalkers</a></i> in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Coyote_Waits" title="Coyote Waits">Coyote Waits</a></i>. He also made a guest appearance in the hit show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dead_Zone_(TV_series)" title="The Dead Zone (TV series)">The Dead Zone</a></i> as a Native <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shaman" title="Shaman">shaman</a> (in the episode "Shaman").</p>
<p>In 2006, Beach portrayed <a href="/wiki/Ira_Hayes" title="Ira Hayes">Ira Hayes</a> in <a href="/wiki/Clint_Eastwood" title="Clint Eastwood">Clint Eastwood</a>'s feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Flags_of_Our_Fathers_(film)" title="Flags of Our Fathers (film)">Flags of Our Fathers</a></i>. During the filming of this movie, both Beach's grandmother and his best friend died. Beach commented, "And if you add those up, there's a lot of emotion. When you're doing a movie, when you let go emotionally, there's nothing to grab onto," he said about the deaths.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MovieHole.net_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-MovieHole.net-4">[4]</a></sup> About playing the part, Beach said, "For me, playing Ira was a meaningful relationship. I would call it – trying to find out how he was, or how he thought and felt".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MovieHole.net_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-MovieHole.net-4">[4]</a></sup> The movie won two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a> nominations and Beach was nominated for multiple Best Supporting Actor honors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2007, Beach starred in <a href="/wiki/HBO_Films" title="HBO Films">HBO Films</a>' adaptation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dee_Brown_(novelist)" title="Dee Brown (novelist)">Dee Brown</a>'s history, <i><a href="/wiki/Bury_My_Heart_at_Wounded_Knee_(film)" title="Bury My Heart at Wounded Knee (film)">Bury My Heart at Wounded Knee</a></i>. In the film, Beach portrays the <a href="/wiki/Sioux" title="Sioux">Sioux</a> advocate, author and medical doctor <a href="/wiki/Charles_Eastman" title="Charles Eastman">Charles Eastman</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dakota_language" title="Dakota language">Dakota</a> name, Ohiyesa) and his changing understanding of Aboriginal-US relations during the period leading up to the <a href="/wiki/Wounded_Knee_Massacre" title="Wounded Knee Massacre">Wounded Knee Massacre</a>.</p>
<p>In addition to starring in the Canadian television series <i><a href="/wiki/Moose_TV" title="Moose TV">Moose TV</a></i>, Beach is executive-producing and starring in the movie <i>Older Than America</i>, the independent feature filmed in Cloquet, Minnesota mostly on the <a href="/wiki/Fond_du_Lac_Band_of_Lake_Superior_Chippewa" title="Fond du Lac Band of Lake Superior Chippewa">Fond du Lac</a> <a href="/wiki/Ojibwe" title="Ojibwe">Ojibwe</a> reservation. He also joined the cast of <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chester_Lake_(Law_%26_Order)" title="Chester Lake (Law &amp; Order)">Detective Chester Lake</a> for the show's ninth season after guest-starring in season eight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> However, in April 2008, <i><a href="/wiki/TV_Guide" title="TV Guide">TV Guide</a></i> announced that he<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> would not return for the show's tenth season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He later stated he grew disillusioned with the strictly procedural nature of the show and opted to leave after the finish of the ninth season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006, Beach unsuccessfully attempted to run for the leadership of his Lake Manitoba First Nation, but has stated he will try again during the next election.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Starting in 2009, Beach played a supporting role on the HBO series, <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Love" title="Big Love">Big Love</a></i> as a manager in an Indian casino with Bill Henrickson (<a href="/wiki/Bill_Paxton" title="Bill Paxton">Bill Paxton</a>).</p>
<p>On February 10, 2010 it was announced that Beach will portray <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Prince" title="Tommy Prince">Tommy Prince</a>, the Canadian war hero, in an upcoming movie about his life. According to Bay Film Studios, the movie will be a "true account of Canada's most highly decorated First Nations soldier." Beach said he is honoured to portray Prince, calling him a positive role model for all First Nations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, he starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Cowboys_%26_Aliens" title="Cowboys &amp; Aliens">Cowboys &amp; Aliens</a></i>, an American science fiction Western film directed by <a href="/wiki/Jon_Favreau" title="Jon Favreau">Jon Favreau</a> and starring <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Craig" title="Daniel Craig">Daniel Craig</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harrison_Ford" title="Harrison Ford">Harrison Ford</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Olivia_Wilde" title="Olivia Wilde">Olivia Wilde</a>. The film is based on the 2006 graphic novel of the same name created by <a href="/wiki/Scott_Mitchell_Rosenberg" title="Scott Mitchell Rosenberg">Scott Mitchell Rosenberg</a>.</p>
<p>Also in 2011, Beach returned to Canada to star in the TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Arctic_Air" title="Arctic Air">Arctic Air</a></i>, which premiered on January 12, 2012.</p>
<p>In 2015, Beach appeared on several episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Backstrom_(TV_series)" title="Backstrom (TV series)">Backstrom</a></i> as Sheriff Jesse Rocha.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adam_Beach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Adam Beach has been married three times and has three children. He had two sons, Noah (born 1996) and Luke (born 1998), with his first wife Meredith Porter (1999–2002). He did not have any children with his second wife, Tara Mason. They married in 2003, but have since separated. In 2008 his now wife Summer Tiger gave birth to a daughter, Phoenix.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canoe20020611_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Canoe20020611-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He later began dating <a href="/wiki/Leah_Gibson" title="Leah Gibson">Leah Gibson</a>, his co-star on "Arctic Air". Beach stated in a <i><a href="/wiki/The_Huffington_Post" title="The Huffington Post">Huffington Post</a></i> interview that the two met each other on set.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
