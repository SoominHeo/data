<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Adam Arkapaw</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Adam Arkapaw</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a> <a href="/wiki/Cinematographer" title="Cinematographer">cinematographer</a>. He is best known for his work on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Top_of_the_Lake" title="Top of the Lake">Top of the Lake</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/True_Detective_(TV_series)" title="True Detective (TV series)">True Detective</a></i>, for which he has won two <a href="/wiki/Creative_Arts_Emmy_Award" title="Creative Arts Emmy Award">Creative Arts Emmy Awards</a>. He is also known for photographing films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Animal_Kingdom_(film)" title="Animal Kingdom (film)">Animal Kingdom</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/Snowtown_(film)" title="Snowtown (film)">Snowtown</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/McFarland,_USA" title="McFarland, USA">McFarland, USA</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Macbeth_(2015_film)" title="Macbeth (2015 film)">Macbeth</a></i> (both 2015). He is known for his collaborations with director <a href="/wiki/Justin_Kurzel" title="Justin Kurzel">Justin Kurzel</a>, whom he worked with on <i>Snowtown</i> and <i>Macbeth</i>, and also <i><a href="/wiki/Assassin%27s_Creed_(film)" title="Assassin's Creed (film)">Assassin's Creed</a></i>, which was released in December 2016.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adam_Arkapaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Arkapaw was born in <a href="/wiki/Bowral" title="Bowral">Bowral</a>, a <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a> town south of <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, and attended <a href="/wiki/Bowral_High_School" title="Bowral High School">Bowral High School</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-variety-1">[1]</a></sup> He studied at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Melbourne" title="University of Melbourne">University of Melbourne</a>'s <a href="/wiki/University_of_Melbourne_Faculty_of_VCA_and_MCM" title="University of Melbourne Faculty of VCA and MCM">Victorian College of the Arts</a>, graduating in 2006 with a Bachelor of Film and Television.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smh_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-smh-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uni_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-uni-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adam_Arkapaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to numerous short films, Arkapaw photographed three Australian features over the next several years: <i><a href="/wiki/Animal_Kingdom_(film)" title="Animal Kingdom (film)">Animal Kingdom</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/Snowtown_(film)" title="Snowtown (film)">Snowtown</a></i> (2011), and <i><a href="/wiki/Lore_(film)" title="Lore (film)">Lore</a></i> (2012).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aust_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-aust-4">[4]</a></sup> He received a nomination for the <a href="/wiki/AACTA_Award_for_Best_Cinematography" title="AACTA Award for Best Cinematography">AACTA Award for Best Cinematography</a> in 2010 for his work on <i>Animal Kingdom</i> and in 2011 he was named one of <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> magazine's "10 Cinematographers to Watch".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-variety-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Arkawpaw next worked on <a href="/wiki/Jane_Campion" title="Jane Campion">Jane Campion</a>'s television miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/Top_of_the_Lake" title="Top of the Lake">Top of the Lake</a></i>, which was filmed in New Zealand and broadcast in 2013. That year he won a <a href="/wiki/Creative_Arts_Emmy_Award" title="Creative Arts Emmy Award">Creative Arts Emmy Award</a> for Outstanding Cinematography for a Miniseries or Movie.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aust_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-aust-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2014 he won his second Emmy Award, for Outstanding Cinematography for a Single-Camera Series, in recognition of his work on the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> television series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/True_Detective_(TV_series)" title="True Detective (TV series)">True Detective</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uni_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-uni-3">[3]</a></sup> <i>True Detective</i> was filmed in <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a> and Arkapaw's cinematography received wide praise, especially for a six-minute single-take <a href="/wiki/Long_take" title="Long take">long take</a> that was planned over months and took one and a half days to film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Arkapaw was the cinematographer of the 2015 film <i><a href="/wiki/Macbeth_(2015_film)" title="Macbeth (2015 film)">Macbeth</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Justin_Kurzel" title="Justin Kurzel">Justin Kurzel</a>, with whom Arkapaw previously worked on <i>Snowtown</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smh_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-smh-2">[2]</a></sup> He also photographed <i><a href="/wiki/McFarland,_USA" title="McFarland, USA">McFarland, USA</a></i>, an American <a href="/wiki/Sports_film" title="Sports film">sports film</a> released in 2015,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and the upcoming drama film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Light_Between_Oceans_(film)" title="The Light Between Oceans (film)">The Light Between Oceans</a></i>. He collaborated with Kurzel for a third time on the action-adventure film <i><a href="/wiki/Assassin%27s_Creed_(film)" title="Assassin's Creed (film)">Assassin's Creed</a></i>, which was released in the United States in December 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wired_film_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-wired_film-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adam_Arkapaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Arkapaw is married to American cinematographer <a href="/wiki/Autumn_Durald" title="Autumn Durald">Autumn Durald</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> the couple has one son, Aedan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
