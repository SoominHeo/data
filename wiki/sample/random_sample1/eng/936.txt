<p><b>House Party (1996)</b> was a supercard that took place on January 5, 1996 in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ECW_Arena" title="ECW Arena">ECW Arena</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philadelphia,_Pennsylvania" title="Philadelphia, Pennsylvania">Philadelphia, Pennsylvania</a>, US. The announcer for the event was <a href="/wiki/Joey_Styles" title="Joey Styles">Joey Styles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Loverro2007_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Loverro2007-1">[1]</a></sup> Several matches from the event aired on the January 23, 1996 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/ECW_Hardcore_TV" title="ECW Hardcore TV">ECW Hardcore TV</a></i>.</p>
<p>House Party (1996) featured the final appearance of <a href="/wiki/Tag_team" title="Tag team">tag team</a> <a href="/wiki/The_Public_Enemy_(professional_wrestling)" title="The Public Enemy (professional wrestling)">The Public Enemy</a>, who had signed contracts with <a href="/wiki/World_Championship_Wrestling" title="World Championship Wrestling">World Championship Wrestling</a>, until their return in January 1999. The event also saw the debut of future ECW mainstay <a href="/wiki/Rob_Van_Dam" title="Rob Van Dam">Rob Van Dam</a>, along with the surprise return of <a href="/wiki/Shane_Douglas" title="Shane Douglas">Shane Douglas</a> from the <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">World Wrestling Federation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Williams2013_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Williams2013-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The event saw a key development in the <a href="/wiki/Feud_(professional_wrestling)" title="Feud (professional wrestling)">feud</a> between <a href="/wiki/Raven_(wrestler)" title="Raven (wrestler)">Raven</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Dreamer" title="Tommy Dreamer">Tommy Dreamer</a>, which had begun upon Raven's debut in ECW one year prior. During the event, Raven's girlfriend and <a href="/wiki/Manager_(professional_wrestling)" title="Manager (professional wrestling)">valet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beulah_McGillicutty" title="Beulah McGillicutty">Beulah McGillicutty</a>, unexpectedly announced that she was pregnant, and that the father of the baby was not Raven, but his arch-rival, Dreamer. After Raven began throttling her, Dreamer stormed to the ring and decisively beat down Raven and his henchmen, <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Richards" title="Stevie Richards">Stevie Richards</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Blue_Meanie" title="The Blue Meanie">The Blue Meanie</a>, before embracing McGillicutty. McGillicutty subsequently joined forces with Dreamer, managing him until her retirement from professional wrestling in 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Williams2013_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Williams2013-2">[2]</a></sup> The storyline of McGillicutty's pregnancy was resolved in April 1996 at <a href="/wiki/Hostile_City_Showdown#1996" title="Hostile City Showdown">Hostile City Showdown</a>, when it was revealed that she had been lying.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Loverro2007_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Loverro2007-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<p>The main event of House Party (1998), a <a href="/wiki/Ladder_match#Other_variations" title="Ladder match">Stairway to Hell match</a> between <a href="/wiki/Sabu_(wrestler)" title="Sabu (wrestler)">Sabu</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Sandman_(wrestler)" title="The Sandman (wrestler)">The Sandman</a>, gained a degree of notoriety after Sabu sustained a <a href="/wiki/Mandibular_fracture" title="Mandibular fracture">broken jaw</a> upon executing a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_aerial_techniques#Plancha" title="Professional wrestling aerial techniques">plancha</a> and inadvertently striking the <a href="/wiki/Guard_rail" title="Guard rail">guard rail</a> with his chin. Sabu continue to wrestle for several minutes, at one point wrapping his heavily bleeding jaw in <a href="/wiki/Duct_tape" title="Duct tape">duct tape</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clapp_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Clapp-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Roberts2009_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Roberts2009-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
