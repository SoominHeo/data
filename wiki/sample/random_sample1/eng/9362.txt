<p><b>Adblock Plus</b> (<b>ABP</b>) is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Open-source" title="Open-source">open-source</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-open-source01_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-open-source01-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-open-source02_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-open-source02-10">[10]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Content_filtering" title="Content filtering">content-filtering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ad_blocking" title="Ad blocking">ad blocking</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_Firefox_extensions" title="List of Firefox extensions">extension</a> developed by Eyeo GmbH (Wladimir Palant), a German software development company. The extension has been released for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mozilla_Firefox" title="Mozilla Firefox">Mozilla Firefox</a> (including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Firefox_for_mobile" title="Firefox for mobile">Firefox for mobile</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mobile_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-mobile-11">[11]</a></sup>), <a href="/wiki/Google_Chrome" title="Google Chrome">Google Chrome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Internet_Explorer" title="Internet Explorer">Internet Explorer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Edge" title="Microsoft Edge">Microsoft Edge</a> (beta version), <a href="/wiki/Opera_(web_browser)" title="Opera (web browser)">Opera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Safari_(web_browser)" title="Safari (web browser)">Safari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yandex_Browser" title="Yandex Browser">Yandex Browser</a> and <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">Android</a>.</p>
<p>In 2011 AdBlock Plus and Eyeo attracted <a href="/wiki/Adblock_Plus#Controversy_over_ad_filtering_and_ad_whitelisting" title="Adblock Plus">considerable controversy</a> from its users when it introduced an "Acceptable Ads"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-12">[12]</a></sup> platform to "allow certain non-intrusive ads" (such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Google_AdWords" title="Google AdWords">Google AdWords</a>) to be allowed (<a href="/wiki/Whitelist" title="Whitelist">whitelisted</a>) under the extension's default settings. While participation in the whitelisting process is free for small websites, large advertising companies are required to pay a fee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Whitelisting was enabled by default for AdBlock Plus users. Developers have launched competing adblockers which do not have Acceptable Ads programs; <a href="/wiki/UBlock_Origin" title="UBlock Origin">uBlock Origin</a> specifically refuses donations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gorhill_readme_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-gorhill_readme-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_statistics">History and statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adblock_Plus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and statistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Michael McDonald created Adblock Plus 0.5, which improved on the original Adblock by incorporating the following features:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Whitelist" title="Whitelist">whitelisting</a></li>
<li>support for blocking background images</li>
<li>subscription to filters with a fixed address and automatic updates</li>
<li>the ability to hide <a href="/wiki/HTML_element" title="HTML element">HTML elements</a>, allowing a greater range of images to be blocked</li>
<li>the ability to hide ads on a per-site basis, instead of globally</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Memory_leak" title="Memory leak">memory leak</a> fixes</li>
<li>improvements to the <a href="/wiki/User_interface" title="User interface">user interface</a></li>
</ul>
<p>McDonald discontinued development and transferred the name to Wladimir Palant, who released Adblock Plus 0.6 with a rewritten codebase in January 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PC_World_(magazine)" title="PC World (magazine)">PC World</a></i> chose Adblock Plus as one of the 100 best products of 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Adblock Plus for Google Chrome has been available since December 2010 and has over 10 million users.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> It has also become the most popular extension for Firefox, with around 18 million users as of March 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Adblock Plus was released as an app for <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">Android</a> devices in November 2012.</p>
<p>On March 3, 2013, the Android app was removed from the <a href="/wiki/Google_Play#Google_Play_Store" title="Google Play">Google Play Store</a> along with similar ad blocking apps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Some apps remain in the Play Store with the caveat that they require <a href="/wiki/Rooting_(Android_OS)" title="Rooting (Android OS)">root access</a> in order to function. Adblock Plus, while not in the Play Store, is still available on the app's website. Users can download the <a href="/wiki/Android_application_package" title="Android application package">.apk file</a> directly and install it as a third-party app if they allow "Unknown Sources" in Android settings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Adblock Plus has been available for <a href="/wiki/Internet_Explorer" title="Internet Explorer">Internet Explorer</a> since August 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Adblock Plus has been available for <a href="/wiki/Safari_(web_browser)" title="Safari (web browser)">Safari</a> since January 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Adblock Plus has been available for <a href="/wiki/Yandex_Browser" title="Yandex Browser">Yandex Browser</a> since December 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>An Adblock Plus <a href="/wiki/Web_browser" title="Web browser">browser</a> beta version was made available in May 2015, called the "<a class="external text" href="https://adblockplus.org/en/adblock-browser" rel="nofollow">Adblock Browser</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Adblock Browser 1.0 was released on 7 September 2015, based on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Firefox_for_mobile" title="Firefox for mobile">Firefox for mobile</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Adblock Plus has created an independent board to review what is an acceptable ad and what is not.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of 27 April 2016, Adblock Plus ranked as the top 1 startup for Germany by StartupRanking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2016, Adblock Plus teamed up with <a href="/wiki/Flattr" title="Flattr">Flattr</a> to release <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Flattr_Plus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Flattr Plus (page does not exist)">Flattr Plus</a>, an add-on that allows Adblock users to automatically give money to website owners when an ad is blocked.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operation">Operation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adblock_Plus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Compaadblocker.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="358" data-file-width="277" height="284" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/7/7d/Compaadblocker.png/220px-Compaadblocker.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/7/7d/Compaadblocker.png 1.5x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Compaadblocker.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A website hosting Google ads with and without Adblock Plus</div>

<p>Like Mozilla's built-in image blocker, Adblock Plus blocks <a href="/wiki/Hypertext_Transfer_Protocol" title="Hypertext Transfer Protocol">HTTP</a> and <a href="/wiki/HTTPS" title="HTTPS">HTTPS</a> requests according to their source address and additional context information and can block <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iframes" title="Iframes">iframes</a>, <a href="/wiki/JavaScript" title="JavaScript">scripts</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Flash" title="Adobe Flash">Flash</a>. It also uses automatically generated user <a href="/wiki/Style_sheet_(web_development)" title="Style sheet (web development)">stylesheets</a> to hide elements such as text ads on a page as they load instead of blocking them, known as element hiding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Android">Android</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adblock_Plus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Android">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On rooted devices, the Android app blocks ads on all web traffic including mobile networks. For non-rooted devices, ads are only blocked through a <a href="/wiki/Wi-Fi" title="Wi-Fi">Wi-Fi connection</a> and requires the user to set up a local proxy server for each network in order for the app to function.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The app uses a local proxy server to intercept web traffic and remove ads before showing content to the user. Most of the content that users are trying to block will be removed, though some content is missed and the app is not as reliable at blocking ads as the browser versions. The app can be configured to auto-start every time the device reboots, minimizing the action required by the user.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filters">Filters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adblock_Plus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Filters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Basic filter rules can include wildcards represented by asterisks (<code>*</code>). Sites and objects can be <a href="/wiki/Whitelist" title="Whitelist">whitelisted</a> with filters that start with two <i>at</i> signs (<code>@@</code>). <a href="/wiki/Regular_expression" title="Regular expression">Regular expressions</a> delimited by slashes (<code>/</code>) can be used. Adblock Plus also supports a more-sophisticated syntax that gives fine-grain control over filters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> An example of the sophisticated filtering would be <code>wikipedia.org##div#centralNotice</code>, which will hide the centralNotice element used by Wikipedia to display donation requests.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filter_subscriptions">Filter subscriptions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adblock_Plus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Filter subscriptions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Users can add external filtersets. Adblock Plus includes the ability to use one or more external filter subscriptions that are automatically updated. <a href="/wiki/Filterset.G" title="Filterset.G">Filterset.G</a> is incompatible with this system (and Adblock Plus specifically recommends against using Filterset.G for other reasons as well), but other filtersets can be added by typing their addresses. A list of known Adblock Plus subscriptions is maintained on the Adblock Plus <a href="#External_links">official website</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>EasyList<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> was the most popular Adblock Plus filter list as of August 2011, with over 12 million subscribers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Created by Rick Petnel,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> it became officially recommended by the Adblock Plus program, and filter lists for other languages were built on top of it. Petnel died in 2009<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> following which Palant placed a user named "Ares2" as the new maintainer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> The filter lists EasyList and EasyPrivacy are both subscribed by default in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UBlock" title="UBlock">uBlock</a> but not in Adblock Plus itself.</p>
<p>In May 2013, the former second most popular Adblock Plus filter list, Fanboy's List, was merged with EasyList.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy_over_ad_filtering_and_ad_whitelisting">Controversy over ad filtering and ad whitelisting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adblock_Plus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Controversy over ad filtering and ad whitelisting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ad_filtering" title="Ad filtering">Ad filtering</a></div>
<p>The owners of some websites which use third-party hosted <a href="/wiki/Online_advertising" title="Online advertising">online advertising</a> to fund the hosting of their websites have argued that the use of ad-blocking software such as Adblock Plus risks cutting off their revenue stream.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> While some websites such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Daily Telegraph</a></i> have successfully implemented subscription and membership-based <a href="/wiki/Paywall" title="Paywall">paywall</a> systems for revenue,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> many websites today rely on third-party hosted online advertising to function. In 2007, web developer Danny Carlton described the use of ad blockers as tantamount to theft,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iw2007-12_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-iw2007-12-44">[44]</a></sup> and called for other site owners to block the <a href="/wiki/Firefox" title="Firefox">Firefox</a> web browser from their websites to deter its use.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 5, 2011, Wladimir Palant announced that certain "acceptable" ads would be whitelisted in upcoming builds of the Adblock Plus software, with the option to remove whitelisted ads via a custom setting in the software. According to Palant, only static advertisements with a maximum of one script will be permitted as "acceptable", with a preference towards text-only content. The announcement created some controversy both at Adblock Plus's website and at <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a> sites like <a href="/wiki/Reddit" title="Reddit">Reddit</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, Adblock Plus's managing director Till Faida told the Swiss newspaper <i>Thurgauer Zeitung</i> that the "strategic partners" on Adblock Plus's whitelist would not be named, but that the partnership is part of the company's "Acceptable Ads" whitelist project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> In February 2013, an anonymous source accused Adblock Plus developer Wladimir Palant of offering to add his site's advertisements to the whitelist in return for one-third of the advertisement revenue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> In June 2013, blogger Sascha Pallenberg accused the developers of Adblock Plus of maintaining business connections to "strategic partners in the advertising industry", and called ABP a "mafia-like advertising network".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thehsa_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-thehsa-49">[49]</a></sup> He alleged that Adblock Plus whitelisted all ads coming from "friendly" sites and subsidiaries, and promoted their product using fake reviews and pornography.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-techeye1_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-techeye1-50">[50]</a></sup> Faida responded to Pallenberg's accusations, stating that "a large part of the information concerning the collaboration with our partners is correct", but that the company did not see these industry connections as a <a href="/wiki/Conflict_of_interest" title="Conflict of interest">conflict of interest</a>. He said that the company is convinced that the "acceptable ads" business model will be successful and says that the whitelisting criteria are "completely transparent".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thehsa_49-1"><a href="#cite_note-thehsa-49">[49]</a></sup> He also stated that "We have an initiative called Acceptable Ads to support websites with unobtrusive ads. Every website can participate. The [Pallenberg] article on purpose just slanders our good name".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-techeye1_50-1"><a href="#cite_note-techeye1-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>In response to the "Acceptable Ads" whitelisting, <a href="/wiki/Fork_(software_development)" title="Fork (software development)">forks</a> of Adblock Plus were created such as the now defunct add-ons Adblock Edge, and Adblock Lite.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> The Adblock Edge developer stated "Discontinued in favor of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UBlock" title="UBlock">uBlock</a>, a general purpose blocker, that not only outperforms Adblock Edge but is also available on other browsers and, of course, without "Acceptable Ads Whitelist"."</p>
<p>Attacks were made in 2016 against ad-blocking with paid whitelists—though Adblock Plus was not mentioned by name—by content providers who provide content free of charge to users, deriving revenue from advertisements, and by industry and government sources who criticise the "unsavoury" business model, which has been described as a "modern-day protection racket".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Eyeo Corporation has partnered with ComboTag<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup>to make it easier for Publishers, Advertisers, Ad Networks, Ad Tech Suppliers to comply with acceptable ads rules,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto-12">[12]</a></sup> however this has proven controversial as some users have switched to alternative adblocking extensions despite that they can uncheck "Allow some non-intrusive advertising" in ABP.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_challenge">Legal challenge</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adblock_Plus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Legal challenge">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In December 2014 it was reported that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zeit_Online" title="Zeit Online">Zeit Online GmbH</a> and <a href="/wiki/Handelsblatt" title="Handelsblatt">Handelsblatt GmbH</a> had brought suit against Eyeo GmbH in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Landgericht_Hamburg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Landgericht Hamburg (page does not exist)">Landgericht Hamburg</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landgericht_Hamburg" title="de:Landgericht Hamburg">de</a>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> In April 2015 the court rejected the suit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Axel_Springer_SE" title="Axel Springer SE">Axel Springer SE</a> has filed a court order for removal of adblock plus post<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> though there is a redacted version<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> and people have posted videos and posts on how to get around the Axel Springer wall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="YouTube videos are self-published and therefore not reliable sources (December 2015)">better source needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Detection">Detection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adblock_Plus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Detection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some webmasters have used <a href="/wiki/JavaScript" title="JavaScript">JavaScript</a> to detect the effects of the popular Adblock Plus filters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> This is done by generating a <a href="/wiki/Honeypot_(computing)" title="Honeypot (computing)">honeypot</a>-like URL, verifying its delivery, and <a href="/wiki/Document_Object_Model" title="Document Object Model">DOM</a> verification after the web page is rendered by the web browser, to ensure the expected advertising elements are present. Detection is simplified since the extension is not yet capable of replacing content; Loopback proxies provide this additional functionality.</p>
<p>These methods do not detect the presence of the Adblock Plus extension directly, only the effects of the filters. They are vulnerable to continued filter updates, and whitelist-filtering web scripts with extensions such as <a href="/wiki/NoScript" title="NoScript">NoScript</a>.</p>
<p>An attempt was made to detect the plug-in itself, but that detection method was rendered unusable by the 0.7.5.2 update of Adblock Plus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> Furthermore, third party developers have created specific browser add-ons (like "Anti-Adblock Killer") to work alongside the Adblock extension that allow users to visit websites that would otherwise restrict their access.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<p>Google Chrome had a defect in <a href="/wiki/Content_Security_Policy" title="Content Security Policy">Content Security Policy</a> that allowed the detection of any installed extension, including Adblock Plus for Google Chrome.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> The solution of this was possible only in Google Chrome 18, and requires each developer to make some changes in their extensions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> Adblock Plus for Google Chrome fixed this in version 1.3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup></p>
