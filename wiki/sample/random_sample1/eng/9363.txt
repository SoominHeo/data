<p><b>Aaron Addison Gordon</b> (born September 16, 1995)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is an American professional <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> player for the <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Magic" title="Orlando Magic">Orlando Magic</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a> (NBA). He played one year of <a href="/wiki/College_basketball" title="College basketball">college basketball</a> for the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Arizona" title="University of Arizona">University of Arizona</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="High_school_career">High school career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aaron_Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: High school career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gordon attended <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_Mitty_High_School" title="Archbishop Mitty High School">Archbishop Mitty High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Jose,_California" title="San Jose, California">San Jose, California</a> and started on the varsity basketball team for four years, winning two Division II state basketball championships in his sophomore and junior seasons. He led Mitty to its third straight state title game in his senior year, but his team lost in the inaugural Open Division final.</p>
<p>As a freshman in 2009–10, Gordon started in 28 of 41 games and averaged 11.8 points, 10.1 rebounds and 2.1 blocks per game. He also competed on the school’s track and field team as a thrower and played summer basketball for the Oakland Soldiers.</p>
<p>As a sophomore in 2010–11, Gordon helped his team win Mitty's first state title in men's basketball. His team also captured the <a href="/wiki/West_Catholic_Athletic_League" title="West Catholic Athletic League">WCAL</a> regular season and playoff crowns, CCS Division II title and Nor-Cal championship. They finished with a 32–2 record and closed the season on a 20–0 winning streak. He started all 34 games and averaged 16.4 points, 12.5 rebounds and 3.6 blocks per game. He scored 17 points and hauled in a state championship record 21 rebounds in the 2011 title game.</p>
<p>As a junior in 2011–12, Gordon averaged 22.9 points, 12.8 rebounds, 2.6 assists and 2.3 blocks per game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-espn.go.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-espn.go.com-2">[2]</a></sup> In the state basketball tournament, he averaged 27.0 points per game before finding out he had been playing with mononucleosis. He was chosen as the <a href="/wiki/California_Mr._Basketball" title="California Mr. Basketball">California Mr. Basketball</a> Player of the Year. The last junior to be Mr. Basketball in California was Tyson Chandler in 2000, and before him, Jason Kidd in 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-espn.go.com_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-espn.go.com-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>As a senior in 2012–13, Gordon averaged 21.6 points, a school-record 15.7 rebounds and 3.3 blocks per game in leading Archbishop Mitty to a 28–6 record and a runner-up finish in the CIF Open Division.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-bio-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gordon committed to the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Wildcats_men%27s_basketball" title="Arizona Wildcats men's basketball">University of Arizona</a> on April 2, 2013, announcing his decision in a press conference before the 2013 <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s_All-American_Game" title="McDonald's All-American Game">McDonald's All-American Game</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> After a 24-point, 8-rebound performance leading the West to a 110–99 victory, Gordon was named the game's MVP.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mcdonalds_mvp_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-mcdonalds_mvp-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="College_career">College career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aaron_Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: College career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On February 13, 2014, Gordon was named one of the 30 finalists for the <a href="/wiki/Naismith_College_Player_of_the_Year" title="Naismith College Player of the Year">Naismith College Player of the Year</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He was named to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_All-Pacific-12_Conference_men%27s_basketball_teams" title="List of All-Pacific-12 Conference men's basketball teams">All-Pac-12</a> first team,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> as well as earning <a href="/wiki/Pac-12_Conference_Men%27s_Basketball_Freshman_of_the_Year" title="Pac-12 Conference Men's Basketball Freshman of the Year">Pac-12 Freshman Player of the Year</a> and Pac-12 All-Freshman team honors.</p>
<p>On April 15, 2014, Gordon declared for the NBA draft, forgoing his final three years of college eligibility.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aaron_Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Orlando_Magic_.282014.E2.80.93present.29">Orlando Magic (2014–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aaron_Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Orlando Magic (2014–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.9315_season">2014–15 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aaron_Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2014–15 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On June 26, 2014, Gordon was selected with the fourth overall pick in the <a href="/wiki/2014_NBA_draft" title="2014 NBA draft">2014 NBA draft</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Magic" title="Orlando Magic">Orlando Magic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On July 2, he signed with the Magic and joined them for the <a href="/wiki/2014_NBA_Summer_League" title="2014 NBA Summer League">2014 NBA Summer League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> After appearing in the first 11 games of the 2014–15 season, Gordon was ruled out indefinitely on November 16 after he fractured a bone in his left foot in the Magic's loss to the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Wizards" title="Washington Wizards">Washington Wizards</a> the night before.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He returned to action on January 18, 2015 against the <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City_Thunder" title="Oklahoma City Thunder">Oklahoma City Thunder</a> after missing 32 games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On April 4, he recorded his first career double-double with 10 points and 12 rebounds in a 97–90 win over the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Bucks" title="Milwaukee Bucks">Milwaukee Bucks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.E2.80.9316_season">2015–16 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aaron_Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2015–16 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In July 2015, Gordon re-joined the Magic for the <a href="/wiki/2015_NBA_Summer_League" title="2015 NBA Summer League">2015 NBA Summer League</a>, where he averaged 21.7 points, 11.7 rebounds, 2.7 assists, 1.3 steals and 1.7 blocks in three games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On November 4, 2015, he scored a career-high 19 points in a loss to the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Rockets" title="Houston Rockets">Houston Rockets</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On January 31, 2016, he tied his career high of 19 points and grabbed 14 rebounds in a 119–114 win over the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Celtics" title="Boston Celtics">Boston Celtics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He went on to record 12 points and a career-high 16 rebounds the following night against the <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio_Spurs" title="San Antonio Spurs">San Antonio Spurs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2016_NBA_All-Star_Weekend" title="2016 NBA All-Star Weekend">2016 NBA All-Star Weekend</a>, Gordon was the runner-up to <a href="/wiki/Zach_LaVine" title="Zach LaVine">Zach LaVine</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Slam_Dunk_Contest" title="Slam Dunk Contest">Slam Dunk Contest</a>. Their battle through two tie-breakers in the final round drew comparisons to the showdown between <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jordan" title="Michael Jordan">Michael Jordan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dominique_Wilkins" title="Dominique Wilkins">Dominique Wilkins</a> in 1988. Gordon utilised <a href="/wiki/Stuff_the_Magic_Dragon" title="Stuff the Magic Dragon">Stuff the Magic Dragon</a>, his team's 6½-ft tall mascot, in his dunks; his final dunk involved him jumping over Stuff while passing the ball under both legs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On February 25, he had another 19-point outing in a 130–114 loss to the <a href="/wiki/Golden_State_Warriors" title="Golden State Warriors">Golden State Warriors</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Three days later, he set a new career high with 22 points in a 130–116 win over the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_76ers" title="Philadelphia 76ers">Philadelphia 76ers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On April 13, in the Magic's season finale, Gordon tied his career high of 22 points in a 117–103 loss to the Charlotte Hornets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2016.E2.80.9317_season">2016–17 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aaron_Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2016–17 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On December 14, 2016, Gordon scored a career-high 33 points in a 113–108 loss to the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Clippers" title="Los Angeles Clippers">Los Angeles Clippers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_team_career">National team career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aaron_Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: National team career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gordon led Team USA to the 2011 FIBA Americas Championship gold medal with team-highs of 17.0 points, 11.2 rebounds and 3.2 blocks per game. He went on to earn MVP honors while leading the United States to a gold medal at the 2013 FIBA U19 World Championship in Prague where he averaged team highs of 16.2 points and 6.2 rebounds per game in addition to shooting 61.2 percent from the field. He was also named to the 2011–12 USA Developmental National Team and participated in the 2010 USA Basketball Developmental National Team mini-camp.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-bio-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aaron_Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gordon is the son of former <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_State_Aztecs_men%27s_basketball" title="San Diego State Aztecs men's basketball">San Diego State basketball</a> star Ed Gordon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Gordon's great-great grandfather, a <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a> <a href="/wiki/Osage_Nation" title="Osage Nation">Osage Indian</a>, was seven feet tall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Gordon's older brother, <a href="/wiki/Drew_Gordon" title="Drew Gordon">Drew</a>, is also a professional basketball player. His older sister, Elise, played collegiately for the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Crimson" title="Harvard Crimson">Harvard</a> women's basketball team from 2010 to 2014. As an eight-year-old, Gordon qualified to compete in the 100- and 200-meter dashes at the Junior Olympics, but chose instead to play in a basketball tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-espn_gordon_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-espn_gordon-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
