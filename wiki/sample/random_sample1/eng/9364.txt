<p>There are 15 <a href="/wiki/County_(United_States)" title="County (United States)">counties</a> in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Four counties (Mohave, Pima, Yavapai and Yuma) were created in 1864 following the organization of the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Territory" title="Arizona Territory">Arizona Territory</a> in 1862. The now defunct <a href="/wiki/Pah-Ute_County,_Arizona_Territory" title="Pah-Ute County, Arizona Territory">Pah-Ute County</a> was split from Mohave County in 1865, but merged back in 1871. All but La Paz County were created by the time Arizona was granted statehood in 1912.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hist_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hist-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The names of many of the counties pay tribute to the state's Native American heritage. Nine of the fifteen counties are named after various native groups that are resident in parts of what is now Arizona. Three of the other counties have Spanish names from the language of the early Hispanic explorers of Arizona: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/La_Paz_County" title="La Paz County">La Paz County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santa_Cruz_County,_Arizona" title="Santa Cruz County, Arizona">Santa Cruz County</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pinal_County" title="Pinal County">Pinal County</a>. Another county, <a href="/wiki/Graham_County,_Arizona" title="Graham County, Arizona">Graham County</a>, is named for a physical feature, <a href="/wiki/Mount_Graham" title="Mount Graham">Mount Graham</a>, with the final county, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenlee_County" title="Greenlee County">Greenlee County</a>, being named after one of the state's early pioneers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Orig_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Orig-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Arizona's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_postal_abbreviations" title="United States postal abbreviations">postal abbreviation</a> is AZ and its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIPS_state_code" title="FIPS state code">FIPS code</a> is 04.</p>
