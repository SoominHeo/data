<p><b>Abbey Road</b> is a thoroughfare in the <a href="/wiki/Borough" title="Borough">borough</a> of <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Camden" title="London Borough of Camden">Camden</a> and the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Westminster" title="City of Westminster">City of Westminster</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, running roughly northwest to southeast through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._John%27s_Wood" title="St. John's Wood">St. John's Wood</a>, near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lord%27s_Cricket_Ground" title="Lord's Cricket Ground">Lord's Cricket Ground</a>. It is part of the B507 road. This road is best known for the <a href="/wiki/Abbey_Road_Studios" title="Abbey Road Studios">Abbey Road Studios</a> and the 1969 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Abbey_Road" title="Abbey Road">Abbey Road</a></i>, by <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">The Beatles</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abbey_Road,_London&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The north-western end of Abbey Road begins in <a href="/wiki/Kilburn,_London" title="Kilburn, London">Kilburn</a>, at the junction with Quex Road and West End Lane. The road was once a track leading to <a href="/wiki/Kilburn_Priory" title="Kilburn Priory">Kilburn Priory</a> and its associated Abbey Farm, and was developed in the early 19th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mills_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mills-2">[2]</a></sup> It continues south-east for roughly a mile, crossing Belsize Road, Boundary Road, and Marlborough Place, ending at the junction of Grove End Road and Garden Road.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abbey_Road,_London&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Abbey_National" title="Abbey National">Abbey National</a> Building Society (now <a href="/wiki/Santander_UK" title="Santander UK">Santander UK</a>) was founded in 1874 as The Abbey Road &amp; St John's Wood Permanent Benefit Building Society in a Baptist church on Abbey Road.</p>

The Beatles' album, Abbey Road, features the Beatles walking across the northwestern zebra crossing on the intersection Abbey Road and Grove End Road.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">EMI</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Abbey_Road_Studios" title="Abbey Road Studios">Abbey Road Studios</a> are located at the south-eastern end, at 3 Abbey Road, St John's Wood. <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">The Beatles</a> and many other famous <a href="/wiki/Popular_music" title="Popular music">popular music</a> performers have recorded at this studio, and The Beatles named <a href="/wiki/Abbey_Road" title="Abbey Road">their last studio LP</a> after this street. The album's cover photograph shows the four group members walking across the <a href="/wiki/Zebra_crossing" title="Zebra crossing">zebra crossing</a> just outside the studio entrance. As a result of its association with The Beatles, since 1969 this part of Abbey Road has been featured on the London <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism</a> circuit. In December 2010 the crossing was given Grade II <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Listed_Building" title="Listed Building">Listed Building</a> status by <a href="/wiki/English_Heritage" title="English Heritage">English Heritage</a> despite its age not being contemporary to that era.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The zebra crossing featured on the Beatles cover, as well as the crossing directly north of it, have become popular photo-opportunity areas, despite the road still being a busy thoroughfare for traffic. The iconic Beatles album cover has been parodied many times over the years on the crossing.</p>

<p>The tin street sign on the corner of Grove End Road and Abbey Road is now mounted high on the building on the corner, to save the local council the expense of cleaning and replacing the sign, which was frequently defaced and <a href="/wiki/Street_sign_theft" title="Street sign theft">stolen</a>. The council repaints the wall next to the zebra crossing every three months to cover fans' <a href="/wiki/Graffiti" title="Graffiti">graffiti</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Abbey Road is also a ward of the City of Westminster. At the 2011 Census this ward had a population of 11,250.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
