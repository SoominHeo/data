<p><b>Ashton Holmes</b> (born February 17, 1978) is an American actor, best known for the role of Jack Stall in <i><a href="/wiki/A_History_of_Violence" title="A History of Violence">A History of Violence</a></i>, Private <a href="/wiki/Sidney_Phillips" title="Sidney Phillips">Sidney Phillips</a> in the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pacific_(miniseries)" title="The Pacific (miniseries)">The Pacific</a></i>, Thom on the <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">CW</a> action-thriller series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nikita_(2010_TV_series)" title="Nikita (2010 TV series)">Nikita</a></i>, and as Tyler Barrol on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Revenge_(TV_series)" title="Revenge (TV series)">Revenge</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashton_Holmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Holmes was born in <a href="/wiki/Albany,_New_York" title="Albany, New York">Albany, New York</a>. His mother, Susan, is a social worker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> At a young age he began taking acting lessons and appeared in community theater. He attended <a href="/wiki/The_Albany_Academy" title="The Albany Academy">The Albany Academy</a>. During his senior year of high school, he attended the intern program of the New York State Theater Institute.</p>
<p>Holmes was involved with the Albany music scene, and was the lead singer for local band Method of Groove.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> During this time, Holmes befriended <a href="/wiki/Nyack,_New_York" title="Nyack, New York">Nyack, New York</a> band <a href="/wiki/Coheed_and_Cambria" title="Coheed and Cambria">Coheed and Cambria</a>, and began shopping around their demos to local indie labels. According to the Coheed and Cambria documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Neverender:_Children_of_The_Fence_Edition" title="Neverender: Children of The Fence Edition">Neverender: Children of The Fence Edition</a></i>, in which Holmes appears, it was Holmes who eventually earned them their first record deal with <a href="/wiki/Equal_Vision_Records" title="Equal Vision Records">Equal Vision Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Coheed_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Coheed-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashton_Holmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Holmes made his debut on television with a recurring role in the daytime soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/One_Life_to_Live" title="One Life to Live">One Life to Live</a></i>. In 2005 he starred in <a href="/wiki/David_Cronenberg" title="David Cronenberg">David Cronenberg</a>'s film <i><a href="/wiki/A_History_of_Violence" title="A History of Violence">A History of Violence</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The next year, he had a recurring role on <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>'s series <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Legal" title="Boston Legal">Boston Legal</a></i>. He also appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Peaceful_Warrior" title="Peaceful Warrior">Peaceful Warrior</a></i> in 2006, <i><a href="/wiki/What_We_Do_Is_Secret_(film)" title="What We Do Is Secret (film)">What We Do Is Secret</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Normal_Adolescent_Behavior" title="Normal Adolescent Behavior">Normal Adolescent Behavior</a></i> in 2007. In 2007 Holmes co-starred with <a href="/wiki/Emily_Blunt" title="Emily Blunt">Emily Blunt</a> in the horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Wind_Chill_(film)" title="Wind Chill (film)">Wind Chill</a></i>. In 2008 he appeared in the comedy-drama film <i><a href="/wiki/Smart_People" title="Smart People">Smart People</a></i>.</p>
<p>On television he appeared in episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cold_Case" title="Cold Case">Cold Case</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Ghost_Whisperer" title="Ghost Whisperer">Ghost Whisperer</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Numbers_(TV_series)" title="Numbers (TV series)">Numbers</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/House_(TV_series)" title="House (TV series)">House</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Criminal_Intent" title="Law &amp; Order: Criminal Intent">Law &amp; Order: Criminal Intent</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI: Crime Scene Investigation</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2010 Holmes played <a href="/wiki/Sidney_Phillips" title="Sidney Phillips">Sid Phillips</a> in the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pacific_(miniseries)" title="The Pacific (miniseries)">The Pacific</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> From 2010 to 2011, he starred as Thom in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_CW_Television_Network" title="The CW Television Network">The CW</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Nikita_(TV_series)" title="Nikita (TV series)">Nikita</a></i>. From 2011 to 2012, he appeared as Tyler Barrol on the ABC drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Revenge_(TV_series)" title="Revenge (TV series)">Revenge</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On February 21, 2013, it was reported that Holmes was cast as Wyatt Bickford in the ABC drama pilot <i>Reckless</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 2002 to 2005, Holmes was also part of the Collins Tour company for <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Larson" title="Jonathan Larson">Jonathan Larson</a>'s musical, <i><a href="/wiki/Rent_(musical)" title="Rent (musical)">Rent</a></i>, in which he played one of the lead characters, Mark Cohen.</p>
