<p><b>Ashley Churchill Williams Dodson</b> (born November 12, 1978) is an American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a>. She is known for her roles in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Good_Morning_Miami" title="Good Morning Miami">Good Morning Miami</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Snow_(2004_film)" title="Snow (2004 film)">Snow</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Snow_2:_Brain_Freeze" title="Snow 2: Brain Freeze">Snow 2: Brain Freeze</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Montana_Sky" title="Montana Sky">Montana Sky</a></i>, and for her appearances on <i><a href="/wiki/How_I_Met_Your_Mother" title="How I Met Your Mother">How I Met Your Mother</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashley_Williams_(actress)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Williams was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Westchester_County" title="Westchester County">Westchester County</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, the daughter of Linda Barbara (née Payne), a fundraiser for <a href="/wiki/The_Michael_J._Fox_Foundation" title="The Michael J. Fox Foundation">The Michael J. Fox Foundation</a>, and Gurney Williams III, a freelance health and science writer. She is the younger sister of actress <a href="/wiki/Kimberly_Williams-Paisley" title="Kimberly Williams-Paisley">Kimberly Williams-Paisley</a> and is sister-in-law to country music star <a href="/wiki/Brad_Paisley" title="Brad Paisley">Brad Paisley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Williams attended Rye High School in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rye_(city),_New_York" title="Rye (city), New York">Rye, New York</a>. In May 2001 she received her Bachelor of Fine Arts from the <a href="/wiki/Boston_University_College_of_Fine_Arts" title="Boston University College of Fine Arts">Boston University College of Fine Arts</a> School of Theatre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Williams married producer Neal Dodson on May 29, 2011. Williams gave birth to their first child, a boy, Gus Williams Dodson, on October 5, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In the summer of 2016, Williams was two months pregnant and suffered a miscarriage. She partnered with the Human Development Project to speak publicly about the experience, in hopes of reducing the stigma of miscarriage and encouraging more women to talk openly about it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In January 2017, Williams revealed that she and Dodson were expecting their second child, a son, due in the spring.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashley_Williams_(actress)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Williams made her big-screen acting debut in a non-speaking role in the 1993 ensemble comedy-drama <i><a href="/wiki/Indian_Summer_(1993_film)" title="Indian Summer (1993 film)">Indian Summer</a></i>, which also featured her sister. She then spent from 1994 to 1996 playing teenage <a href="/wiki/Danielle_Andropoulos" title="Danielle Andropoulos">Danielle Andropoulos</a> on the soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/As_the_World_Turns" title="As the World Turns">As the World Turns</a></i>.</p>
<p>Williams had a role in the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning,_Miami" title="Good Morning, Miami">Good Morning, Miami</a></i> (2002–2004). Since then she has also appeared in episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Psych" title="Psych">Psych</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/How_I_Met_Your_Mother" title="How I Met Your Mother">How I Met Your Mother</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/E-Ring" title="E-Ring">E-Ring</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Huff_(TV_series)" title="Huff (TV series)">Huff</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Side_Order_of_Life" title="Side Order of Life">Side Order of Life</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mentalist" title="The Mentalist">The Mentalist</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Warehouse_13" title="Warehouse 13">Warehouse 13</a></i>. In May 2003, she had a guest starring role on <i><a href="/wiki/American_Dreams" title="American Dreams">American Dreams</a></i>, playing singer <a href="/wiki/Sandie_Shaw" title="Sandie Shaw">Sandie Shaw</a> and performing Shaw's 1964 hit "<a href="/wiki/(There%27s)_Always_Something_There_to_Remind_Me" title="(There's) Always Something There to Remind Me">(There's) Always Something There to Remind Me</a>" on <i><a href="/wiki/American_Bandstand" title="American Bandstand">American Bandstand</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2006, she starred in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Off_Broadway" title="Off Broadway">Off Broadway</a> play <i>Burleigh Grime$</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and appeared as Victoria, a cupcake baker, on six episodes of the television series <i><a href="/wiki/How_I_Met_Your_Mother" title="How I Met Your Mother">How I Met Your Mother</a></i>; a role reprised in the concluding seasons of the show.</p>
<p>In 2010, she starred in the made-for-TV <a href="/wiki/Lifetime_(TV_network)" title="Lifetime (TV network)">Lifetime</a> movie, <a href="/wiki/Patricia_Cornwell" title="Patricia Cornwell">Patricia Cornwell</a>'s <i>The Front</i>, which premiered on the channel on April 17, 2010. She also won an on-line straw poll conducted by the <i><a href="/wiki/How_I_Met_Your_Mother" title="How I Met Your Mother">How I Met Your Mother</a></i> production staff as to which ex-girlfriend of Ted Mosby, the show's main character, is the fans' favorite. Her character, <a href="/wiki/List_of_How_I_Met_Your_Mother_characters#Victoria" title="List of How I Met Your Mother characters">Victoria</a>, won 128 to 117 over "<a href="/wiki/Robin_Scherbatsky" title="Robin Scherbatsky">Robin Scherbatsky</a>", with a smattering of votes for other candidates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011 and 2012, she played the role of Claire in a film adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Something_Borrowed_(film)" title="Something Borrowed (film)">Something Borrowed</a></i> and reprised her role as Victoria on <i>How I Met Your Mother</i>.</p>
<p>She made her Broadway debut in <a href="/wiki/John_Grisham" title="John Grisham">John Grisham</a>'s <a href="/wiki/A_Time_to_Kill_(Grisham_novel)" title="A Time to Kill (Grisham novel)"><i>A Time To Kill</i></a> playing law student Ellen Roark, on September 28, 2013, with opening night on October 20, 2013.</p>
<p>Beginning in 2015, Williams stars as a fictionalized version of <a href="/wiki/Jim_Gaffigan" title="Jim Gaffigan">Jim Gaffigan</a>'s real life wife in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jim_Gaffigan_Show" title="The Jim Gaffigan Show">The Jim Gaffigan Show</a></i>, a sitcom about a couple raising their five children in a two-bedroom New York apartment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
