<p><b>Ashley Jensen Beesley</b> (born 11 August 1969) is a Scottish actress and narrator. She was nominated for an <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy</a> for her role on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Extras_(TV_series)" title="Extras (TV series)">Extras</a></i>, on which she appeared from 2005–2007. She was also a cast member of the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/Ugly_Betty" title="Ugly Betty">Ugly Betty</a></i> and the short-lived <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Accidentally_on_Purpose_(TV_series)" title="Accidentally on Purpose (TV series)">Accidentally on Purpose</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashley_Jensen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jensen was born in <a href="/wiki/Annan,_Dumfries_and_Galloway" title="Annan, Dumfries and Galloway">Annan, Scotland</a> to Margaret and Ivar Jensen. She was raised by her mother, a special-needs teacher. She trained at <a href="/wiki/Queen_Margaret_University" title="Queen Margaret University">Queen Margaret University</a>, Edinburgh and was a member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Youth_Theatre" title="National Youth Theatre">National Youth Theatre</a>.</p>
<p>Jensen's first big television role was as Clare Donnelly, daughter of Glasgow criminal Jo-Jo Donnelly (played by <a href="/wiki/Billy_Connolly" title="Billy Connolly">Billy Connolly</a>) in the 1993 <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> drama <i>Down Among the Big Boys</i>. In 1994 she played eccentric secretary Rosie McConnichy in the final series of BBC comedy <i><a href="/wiki/May_to_December" title="May to December">May to December</a></i>, as a replacement for the character of secretary <a href="/wiki/Rebecca_Lacey" title="Rebecca Lacey">Hilary</a>, and Heather in <i><a href="/wiki/Roughnecks_(TV_series)" title="Roughnecks (TV series)">Roughnecks</a></i>, a BBC television series based around the workers on a <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> <a href="/wiki/Oil_platform" title="Oil platform">Oil platform</a>. Jensen had a small role in a <i><a href="/wiki/Dangerfield_(TV_series)" title="Dangerfield (TV series)">Dangerfield</a></i> episode "Contact" as the mother of a young girl with meningitis.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> She then appeared as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fiona_Morris" title="Fiona Morris">Fiona Morris</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/EastEnders" title="EastEnders">EastEnders</a></i>. She also appeared in <a href="/wiki/Two_Thousand_Acres_of_Sky" title="Two Thousand Acres of Sky">Two Thousand Acres of Sky</a> on the BBC in 2003 along with Michelle Collins and Paul Kaye.</p>
<p>Jensen starred alongside <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Gervais" title="Ricky Gervais">Ricky Gervais</a> in the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two" title="BBC Two">BBC Two</a>/<a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> television programme <i><a href="/wiki/Extras_(TV_series)" title="Extras (TV series)">Extras</a></i> as the socially inept <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maggie_Jacobs" title="Maggie Jacobs">Maggie Jacobs</a>. For her work on the first series, she received best television comedy actress and newcomer awards at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Comedy_Awards_2005" title="British Comedy Awards 2005">2005 British Comedy Awards</a>. In 2006, Jensen received two British Comedy Awards and a <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_of_Film_and_Television_Arts" title="British Academy of Film and Television Arts">BAFTA</a> nomination for her role in <i>Extras.</i> Her role in the 2007 Christmas Special earned her an <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> nomination. In January 2006 she starred in the short-lived drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Eleventh_Hour_(UK_TV_series)" title="Eleventh Hour (UK TV series)">Eleventh Hour</a></i> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ITV_Network" title="ITV Network">ITV</a>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Stewart" title="Patrick Stewart">Patrick Stewart</a>. Later that year she played <a href="/wiki/Steve_Coogan" title="Steve Coogan">Steve Coogan</a>'s agent in <i><a href="/wiki/A_Cock_and_Bull_Story" title="A Cock and Bull Story">A Cock and Bull Story</a></i>. In 2007, she provided the narration for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Taking_Liberties_(film)" title="Taking Liberties (film)">Taking Liberties</a></i>, directed by Chris Atkins.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In September 2006, Jensen's first major role on American television came when she took the role of regular character <a href="/wiki/Christina_McKinney" title="Christina McKinney">Christina McKinney</a> on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Ugly_Betty" title="Ugly Betty">Ugly Betty</a></i>. Her character was originally an American, but when she met with the show's producers they liked Jensen's accent and changed the nationality of the character, keeping McKinney British. Production of <i>Ugly Betty</i> moved to New York from Los Angeles in mid-2008, causing a conflict for Jensen, who became a recurring member of the cast.</p>
<p>In January 2009, Jensen starred in <i>No Holds Bard</i>, a <a href="/wiki/BBC_Scotland" title="BBC Scotland">BBC Scotland</a> one-off special comedy shown on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burns_Night" title="Burns Night">Burns Night</a> as part of a line-up of special programming to mark the 250th Birthday of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Burns" title="Robert Burns">Robert Burns</a>. She is the narrator of <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Embarrassing_Illnesses" title="Embarrassing Illnesses">Embarrassing Illnesses</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Embarrassing_Bodies" title="Embarrassing Bodies">Embarrassing Bodies</a></i>. She also narrates <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Scotland" title="Bank of Scotland">Bank of Scotland</a>'s current advertisement campaign and Persil detergent adverts for both television and radio. From September 2009 to May 2010, she co-starred with Jenna Elfman in the sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Accidentally_on_Purpose_(TV_series)" title="Accidentally on Purpose (TV series)">Accidentally on Purpose</a></i> as Olivia. Jensen starred in the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> comedy <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nativity_(BBC_film)" title="Nativity (BBC film)">Nativity</a></i> as Jennifer Lore, a Hollywood producer's secretary and the love interest of Paul Maddens played by <a href="/wiki/Martin_Freeman" title="Martin Freeman">Martin Freeman</a>. <i>Nativity</i> was released in cinemas on 27 November 2009.</p>
<p>On 21 December 2010, she starred in the one-off/pilot <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> comedy drama <i>Accidental Farmer</i>, playing a London executive who buys a derelict farm with her philandering boyfriend's credit card. In 2011, she voiced Nanette the frog in the animated production <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gnomeo_and_Juliet" title="Gnomeo and Juliet">Gnomeo and Juliet</a>.</i> She starred in the ITV drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Reckoning_(TV_series)" title="The Reckoning (TV series)">The Reckoning</a></i> in April of that year. On 6 October 2012, she appeared as a guest on the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> cookery programme <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Kitchen" title="Saturday Kitchen">Saturday Kitchen</a></i> hosted by <a href="/wiki/James_Martin_(chef)" title="James Martin (chef)">James Martin</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On 22 August 2014, Sky announced that it had commissioned a <a href="/wiki/Agatha_Raisin_and_the_Quiche_of_Death_(film)" title="Agatha Raisin and the Quiche of Death (film)">television film adaptation</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M._C._Beaton" title="M. C. Beaton">M. C. Beaton</a> novel <i><a href="/wiki/Agatha_Raisin_and_the_Quiche_of_Death" title="Agatha Raisin and the Quiche of Death">Agatha Raisin and the Quiche of Death</a></i> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sky1" title="Sky1">Sky1</a>, starring Ashley Jensen as the titular character, Agatha Raisin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, Jensen starred opposite <a href="/wiki/Sharon_Horgan" title="Sharon Horgan">Sharon Horgan</a> and American comedian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rob_Delaney_(comedian)" title="Rob Delaney (comedian)">Rob Delaney</a> in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catastrophe_(sitcom)" title="Catastrophe (sitcom)">Catastrophe</a></i>, a six-part comedy series for the British <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> that aired in the UK from 19 January 2015.</p>
<p>In 2015, Jensen co-starred with <a href="/wiki/Susan_Calman" title="Susan Calman">Susan Calman</a> in the BBC Radio 4 comedy series Sisters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Calman also wrote the series.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashley_Jensen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After six years together, Jensen married actor <a href="/wiki/Terence_Beesley" title="Terence Beesley">Terence Beesley</a> in a ceremony in the woods of <a href="/wiki/Big_Sur" title="Big Sur">Big Sur</a>, California on 29 January 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> They met in 1999 while working on a stage production of <i><a href="/wiki/King_Lear" title="King Lear">King Lear</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The couple also own property in <a href="/wiki/Umbria" title="Umbria">Umbria</a>, Italy. The couple have one son, Francis Jonathan Beesley (born 20 October 2009),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and currently live in <a href="/wiki/Bath,_Somerset" title="Bath, Somerset">Bath, Somerset</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2008, Jensen and Beesley were granted a restraining order against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeff_Lewis_(real_estate_speculator)" title="Jeff Lewis (real estate speculator)">Jeff Lewis</a> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bravo_(US_TV_channel)" title="Bravo (US TV channel)">Bravo TV</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Flipping_Out" title="Flipping Out">Flipping Out</a></i>. Jensen and Beesley then were living next door to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Feliz" title="Los Feliz">Los Feliz</a> house Lewis was renovating, and conflict arose after a deck that Lewis had constructed on the home crossed the property line onto the Jensen-Beesley property. Jensen and Beesley sued, seeking $100,000 for an easement. Lewis reacted negatively and Jensen/Beesley filed a restraining order. In mid-April 2009 the two parties settled for a $30,000 easement and an agreement that the deck would not have to be demolished.</p>
