<p><b>Ashley Rae Fink</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (born November 20, 1986)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> is an American actress and singer, known for her role as <a href="/wiki/Lauren_Zizes" title="Lauren Zizes">Lauren Zizes</a> in the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Glee_(TV_series)" title="Glee (TV series)">Glee</a></i>, and also as Carter McMahon in <i><a href="/wiki/Huge_(TV_series)" title="Huge (TV series)">Huge</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashley_Fink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fink was born in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, and has an older sister named Stephanie and two younger sisters named Amanda and Amy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She began acting at the age of four after playing the lead role in her school's holiday play. After moving to Los Angeles with her family, Ashley immersed herself in acting and began attending an Arts High School, where she began performing for large audiences as lead characters in adaptations including <i><a href="/wiki/Adaptations_of_The_Wizard_of_Oz" title="Adaptations of The Wizard of Oz">The Wizard of Oz</a></i> (as the Lion) and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/You%27re_a_Good_Man_Charlie_Brown" title="You're a Good Man Charlie Brown">You're a Good Man Charlie Brown</a></i> (as Lucy). Fink has said that she was bullied in high school, but that she did not let it get to her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-people-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashley_Fink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ashley came to Hollywood when she starred in the Tribeca Film Festival hit <i>Fat Girls</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fat_Girls_Review_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fat_Girls_Review-5">[5]</a></sup> She has made multiple TV appearances; she has appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Make_It_or_Break_It" title="Make It or Break It">Make It or Break It</a></i>, is the recurring character <a href="/wiki/Lauren_Zizes" title="Lauren Zizes">Lauren Zizes</a> on <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Glee_(TV_series)" title="Glee (TV series)">Glee</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Special_education_review_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Special_education_review-6">[6]</a></sup> and starred as Carter McMahon on the ABC Family show <i><a href="/wiki/Huge_(TV_series)" title="Huge (TV series)">Huge</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huge_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-huge-7">[7]</a></sup> She also appeared in the Walt Disney Pictures film <i><a href="/wiki/You_Again" title="You Again">You Again</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Betty_White" title="Betty White">Betty White</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Lee_Curtis" title="Jamie Lee Curtis">Jamie Lee Curtis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sigourney_Weaver" title="Sigourney Weaver">Sigourney Weaver</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-credits_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-credits-8">[8]</a></sup> and in her indie horror cult hit <i><a href="/wiki/All_About_Evil" title="All About Evil">All About Evil</a></i> appearing beside <a href="/wiki/Mink_Stole" title="Mink Stole">Mink Stole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cassandra_Peterson" title="Cassandra Peterson">Cassandra Peterson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natasha_Lyonne" title="Natasha Lyonne">Natasha Lyonne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Dekker_(actor)" title="Thomas Dekker (actor)">Thomas Dekker</a>. Ashley has also recently broke into filmmaking herself by producing her first short starring <a href="/wiki/Camille_Winbush" title="Camille Winbush">Camille Winbush</a> titled "Olivia", which just had its premiere at the Frameline film festival in San Francisco.</p>
<p>Fink was cast as <a href="/wiki/Lauren_Zizes" title="Lauren Zizes">Lauren Zizes</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Glee_(TV_series)" title="Glee (TV series)">Glee</a></i>, making her initial appearance in the first season episode "<a href="/wiki/Wheels_(Glee)" title="Wheels (Glee)">Wheels</a>" as a champion high school wrestler and cheerleader tryout candidate. She made irregular appearances until the ninth episode of the second season, "<a href="/wiki/Special_Education_(Glee)" title="Special Education (Glee)">Special Education</a>", when she was tapped to become the glee club's twelfth member after <a href="/wiki/Kurt_Hummel" title="Kurt Hummel">Kurt Hummel</a> (<a href="/wiki/Chris_Colfer" title="Chris Colfer">Chris Colfer</a>) transferred to another school. Fink has said that "I was obsessed with Glee before I was a big part of it and I’ve steadily been on it for a while. When I got the script [where I joined the glee club] I texted Chris Colfer and said, 'I think I just joined the Glee club?' I didn’t quite believe it at first, I thought I was getting 'Punk'd.<span style="padding-right:.2em;">'</span>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ashleyinterview_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ashleyinterview-9">[9]</a></sup> She remained in the glee club for the rest of the season, and was given storylines of her own. Critics have responded well to her portrayal of Lauren. Robert Canning of <i><a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a></i> praised Fink's performance as Lauren, and said of the "<a href="/wiki/Silly_Love_Songs_(Glee)" title="Silly Love Songs (Glee)">Silly Love Songs</a>" episode where <a href="/wiki/Puck_(Glee)" title="Puck (Glee)">Puck</a> (<a href="/wiki/Mark_Salling" title="Mark Salling">Mark Salling</a>) falls in love with her, "I can't decide if I want to root for this couple, but I certainly know I'm going to be rooting for Lauren."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-IGN-10">[10]</a></sup> Fink received several positive reviews for her performance in "<a href="/wiki/Comeback_(Glee)" title="Comeback (Glee)">Comeback</a>", in which she sang her first—and so far only—solo on the show, a cover of "<a href="/wiki/I_Know_What_Boys_Like_(song)" title="I Know What Boys Like (song)">I Know What Boys Like</a>" by <a href="/wiki/The_Waitresses" title="The Waitresses">The Waitresses</a>. The song was released as a single, but it did not chart, nor was it included on a <i>Glee</i> soundtrack album. While Todd VanDerWerff of <i><a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">The A.V. Club</a></i> found Lauren's relationship with Puck in "Comeback" "a lot less assured" than in the previous episode, "Silly Love Songs",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-comeback_review_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-comeback_review-11">[11]</a></sup> Sandra Gonzalez of <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> deemed their scenes "as endearing as they are unrealistic".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">CNN</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span> Lisa Respers France commented that she was beginning to really like Lauren, and noted that "for me Lauren represents how Ryan Murphy is able to take the concept of the outcast, flip it on its ear and shove it back in our faces with a side order of fabulousness."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-comeback2_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-comeback2-13">[13]</a></sup> Despite the positive critical response, Fink's character quit the glee club in the series season 3 premiere, "<a href="/wiki/The_Purple_Piano_Project" title="The Purple Piano Project">The Purple Piano Project</a>". While she appeared in a non-speaking capacity in the Season 3 episode "<a href="/wiki/Asian_F" title="Asian F">Asian F</a>" and series creator Murphy alluded to her character's reentry into the plot through interactions with <a href="/wiki/Kurt_Hummel" title="Kurt Hummel">Kurt Hummel</a>, Fink confirmed later that season that she would not be returning to the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-glee_exit_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-glee_exit-14">[14]</a></sup> However, she subsequently reprised her role in the season 4 episode "<a href="/wiki/Sadie_Hawkins_(Glee)" title="Sadie Hawkins (Glee)">Sadie Hawkins</a>".</p>
<p>Fink also appears in the music video for <a href="/wiki/Stacie_Orrico" title="Stacie Orrico">Stacie Orrico</a>'s 2004 hit "<a href="/wiki/I_Could_Be_the_One_(Stacie_Orrico_song)" title="I Could Be the One (Stacie Orrico song)">I Could Be the One</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-I_Could_Be_the_One_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-I_Could_Be_the_One-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
