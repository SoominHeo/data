<p><b>Accrington Stanley Football Club</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">association football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Accrington" title="Accrington">Accrington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a>. The team play in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Two" title="Football League Two">League Two</a>, the fourth tier of the <a href="/wiki/English_football_league_system" title="English football league system">English football league system</a>.</p>
<p>The current club was formed in 1968, two years after the collapse of <a href="/wiki/Accrington_Stanley_F.C._(1891)" title="Accrington Stanley F.C. (1891)">the original Accrington Stanley</a> (founded in 1891). They were first promoted to the Football League in 2006, after winning the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Football_Conference" title="2005–06 Football Conference">2005–06 Football Conference</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ilyas_Khan" title="Ilyas Khan">Ilyas Khan</a> saved the club from possible oblivion in late 2009 and stepped down as chairman in 2012 after keeping the club financially secure. Former club president Peter Marsden was appointed chairman soon after.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_re-formation">History and re-formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Accrington_Stanley_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and re-formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Accrington had been without a football team following the collapse of the original Accrington Stanley in 1966. The original team had been formed in 1891 and played in the Football League from 1921 to March 1962; it had spent its final four seasons in the <a href="/wiki/Lancashire_Combination" title="Lancashire Combination">Lancashire Combination</a>. At a meeting in the town library in October 1968 the revival was initiated by Stanley Wotherington,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and in August 1970 the new club played at a new ground, the <a href="/wiki/Crown_Ground" title="Crown Ground">Crown Ground</a>. The Crown Ground takes its name from The Crown, a pub owned by Accrington Stanley. Since their formation, Stanley have climbed the <a href="/wiki/Non-League_football" title="Non-League football">non-League pyramid</a> to reach <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Football_League" title="The Football League">The Football League</a>. Eric Whalley, a local businessman, took control of the club in 1995 and began the development of the club's ground. After the club was relegated in 1999, Whalley appointed John Coleman as manager. In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_Conference_2005%E2%80%9306" title="Football Conference 2005–06">2005–06</a>, Stanley won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_Conference" title="Football Conference">Football Conference</a> and were promoted to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Two" title="Football League Two">League Two</a>, switching places with relegated <a href="/wiki/Oxford_United_F.C." title="Oxford United F.C.">Oxford United</a> – in a reversal of fortune, the team that had been elected to replace the former <a href="/wiki/Accrington_Stanley_F.C._(1891)" title="Accrington Stanley F.C. (1891)">Accrington Stanley</a> as members of the Football League in 1962.</p>
<p>The club's recent rise to the Premier level, and eventually to the League, is attributed in part to the windfall of hundreds of thousands of pounds reaped by the sell-on clause in the December 2001 transfer of former Stanley star <a href="/wiki/Brett_Ormerod" title="Brett Ormerod">Brett Ormerod</a> to <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a>, which paid <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_F.C." title="Blackpool F.C.">Blackpool</a> over a million <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">pounds</a> for his contract. Stanley had taken £50,000 from Blackpool in 1997 with the agreement that Blackpool would pay Accrington a quarter of what it might have received if it in turn transferred Ormerod to another team. The 2002–03 championship of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Premier_League" title="Northern Premier League">Northern Premier League</a> followed quickly on Accrington's getting the cash.</p>
<p>Ilyas Khan's decision to save the club was largely due to the influence from his eldest son, Hassan, who is a lifelong supporter of the club.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conference_years">Conference years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Accrington_Stanley_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Conference years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the 2002–03 win of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Premier_League" title="Northern Premier League">Northern Premier League</a>, the club was promoted for the first time in its history to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_Conference" title="Football Conference">Football Conference</a>. The club's first-ever game in the league was away to another re-formed club, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aldershot_Town" title="Aldershot Town">Aldershot Town</a>, on Sunday 10 August 2003. The game was shown live on <a href="/wiki/Sky_Sports" title="Sky Sports">Sky Sports</a> and resulted in a 1–2 loss. The season was a success, with a final league position of 10th being achieved. The highlight of that first season back in the 5th tier was a sensational run to the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> 3rd round, only losing in a replay away to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/League_One" title="League One">League One</a> side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colchester_United" title="Colchester United">Colchester United</a>.</p>
<p>The following season 2004–05 also resulted in a 10th-place finish despite the club only being run on a part-time basis. Club legend <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paul_Mullin" title="Paul Mullin">Paul Mullin</a> was yet again amongst the goal scorers, adding another 20 to his tally.</p>
<p>The 2005–06 season saw the return of Stanley to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Football_League" title="The Football League">the Football League</a>. Finishing on 91 points, the club went on a 19-game unbeaten run stretching from October to March, leaving the club an easy passage to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/League_Two" title="League Two">League Two</a>. The likes of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paul_Mullin" title="Paul Mullin">Paul Mullin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rob_Elliot" title="Rob Elliot">Rob Elliot</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gary_Roberts_(footballer_born_1984)" title="Gary Roberts (footballer born 1984)">Gary Roberts</a> led the club back to the league after 46 years away.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_the_Football_League">Return to the Football League</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Accrington_Stanley_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Return to the Football League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Accrington_Stanley_plaque.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2874" data-file-width="2239" height="282" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Accrington_Stanley_plaque.JPG/220px-Accrington_Stanley_plaque.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Accrington_Stanley_plaque.JPG/330px-Accrington_Stanley_plaque.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Accrington_Stanley_plaque.JPG/440px-Accrington_Stanley_plaque.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The club's first Football League game took place on 5 August 2006 away to now-defunct club <a href="/wiki/Chester_City_F.C." title="Chester City F.C.">Chester City</a>; it resulted in a 0–2 loss. The club was involved in a relegation battle throughout its first season in the 4th tier. A run of 5 wins in the last 9 games of the season led to a 20th-place finish and was enough to save the club from relegation in its first season back in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Football_League" title="The Football League">the Football League</a>.</p>
<p>Highlights of that first season back included the club's first-ever <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">Football League Cup</a> match against former European Cup Winners <a href="/wiki/Nottingham_Forest_F.C." title="Nottingham Forest F.C.">Nottingham Forest</a>. The game resulted in a 1–0 win, leaving the club a 2nd-round away tie against then <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> team <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a>, eventually losing 6–5 on penalties after a 0–0 draw and extra-time. The club also took part in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Trophy" title="Football League Trophy">Football League Trophy</a> for the first time as a league club (after playing in the two previous seasons as one of 12 Conference sides, beating <a href="/wiki/Bradford_City_A.F.C." title="Bradford City A.F.C.">Bradford City</a> away in September 2004) and, after defeating <a href="/wiki/Carlisle_United_F.C." title="Carlisle United F.C.">Carlisle United</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_F.C." title="Blackpool F.C.">Blackpool</a> in the early rounds, were knocked out by <a href="/wiki/Doncaster_Rovers_F.C." title="Doncaster Rovers F.C.">Doncaster Rovers</a> in the Area Quarter-finals.</p>
<p>The 2007–08 season produced more of the same, with the club involved in another relegation battle with strugglers <a href="/wiki/Chester_City_F.C." title="Chester City F.C.">Chester City</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wrexham_F.C." title="Wrexham F.C.">Wrexham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mansfield_Town_F.C." title="Mansfield Town F.C.">Mansfield Town</a>. 5 wins in the final 12 games were enough to secure a 17th-place finish and another season in the 4th tier of English Football. However, the club failed to win a game in the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a>, losing to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_F.C." title="Huddersfield Town F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a> respectively.</p>
<p>Performance during the 2008–09 season improved, with the club achieving a modest 16th-place finish in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Two" title="Football League Two">League Two</a>. A run of 6 League wins in the last 12 games was a nice way to finish the season. This season saw the emergence of young prospect <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Grant_(footballer)" title="Bobby Grant (footballer)">Bobby Grant</a>, who finally fulfilled the early promise seen in previous seasons. The club again failed to make it past the early round of any of the domestic clubs, losing in the first round to <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tranmere_Rovers_F.C." title="Tranmere Rovers F.C.">Tranmere Rovers</a> in both the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> (albeit after a replay) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Trophy" title="Football League Trophy">Football League Trophy</a>.</p>
<p>The 2009–10 season was far better, with the club pushing for a playoff place at the turn of the year. A run of 9 wins in 10 League games saw the club with a chance of making the playoffs, only for this to fade in March/April. The emergence of the <a href="/wiki/Michael_Symes" title="Michael Symes">Michael Symes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Grant_(footballer)" title="Bobby Grant (footballer)">Bobby Grant</a> partnership was a key aspect and, following their achievements throughout the season, both moved on to bigger clubs. In terms of cup performance the club was superb, reaching the 2nd round of the League Cup losing only 1–2 to <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a>, the quarter-finals of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Trophy" title="Football League Trophy">Football League Trophy</a> losing 0–2 to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leeds_United_A.F.C." title="Leeds United A.F.C.">Leeds United</a>, and the 4th round of the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> losing 1–3 to <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> team <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a>.</p>
<p>The club reached the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Two_play-offs" title="Football League Two play-offs">Football League Two play-offs</a> during the 2010–11 season, one of the most successful in its history. A run of 1 loss in 19 games, from February till May, saw the club finish in a best-ever 5th position, eventually losing to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Two" title="Football League Two">League Two</a> newcomers <a href="/wiki/Stevenage_F.C." title="Stevenage F.C.">Stevenage</a> in the Playoff Semi-finals. The season saw the emergence of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jimmy_Ryan_(footballer_born_1988)" title="Jimmy Ryan (footballer born 1988)">Jimmy Ryan</a> as a star in the making, along with a number of others, including goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Alex_Cisak" title="Alex Cisak">Alex Cisak</a> and midfielder <a href="/wiki/Sean_McConville" title="Sean McConville">Sean McConville</a>. In the domestic cups, Stanley reached the 2nd round of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a>, losing 2–3 to <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> team <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a>. The club actually won the 1st-round game of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Trophy" title="Football League Trophy">Football League Trophy</a> away to <a href="/wiki/Tranmere_Rovers_F.C." title="Tranmere Rovers F.C.">Tranmere Rovers</a>, but was then forced to resign from the competition after fielding the ineligible <a href="/wiki/Ray_Putterill" title="Ray Putterill">Ray Putterill</a> in the game. The club also reached the 2nd round of the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a>, but lost to fellow League Two side <a href="/wiki/Port_Vale_F.C." title="Port Vale F.C.">Port Vale</a>.</p>
<p>2011–12 was a season of transition for the club. The loss of no less than six of the playoff-chasing side of the previous season was a tough act to follow. Following a shaky start to the season the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Hughes" title="Bryan Hughes">Bryan Hughes</a> in October transformed the club's fortunes. A run of 6 wins in 7 games over the Christmas period saw the club briefly enter the play-offs. However, following the sale of club captain <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Procter" title="Andrew Procter">Andrew Procter</a> to <a href="/wiki/Preston_North_End_F.C." title="Preston North End F.C.">Preston North End</a> in the January 2012 transfer window, the third-longest serving management team of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Coleman_(footballer_born_1962)" title="John Coleman (footballer born 1962)">John Coleman</a> and Jimmy Bell departed for <a href="/wiki/Rochdale_A.F.C." title="Rochdale A.F.C.">Rochdale</a>. Former <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a> and club favourite <a href="/wiki/Paul_Cook_(footballer)" title="Paul Cook (footballer)">Paul Cook</a> was brought in as manager, along with the promotion of <a href="/wiki/Leam_Richardson" title="Leam Richardson">Leam Richardson</a> from caretaker manager to full-time assistant. Only 3 wins in the final 17 games of the season was a pretty poor finish the season. However, this meant the club achieved a solid mid-table finish in 14th position. In terms of the domestic cups Stanley exited both the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> at the 1st round stages, losing to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scunthorpe_United" title="Scunthorpe United">Scunthorpe United</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Notts_County" title="Notts County">Notts County</a> respectively. The club reached the second round of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Trophy" title="Football League Trophy">Football League Trophy</a>, after knocking out holders <a href="/wiki/Carlisle_United_F.C." title="Carlisle United F.C.">Carlisle United</a>, but lost to <a href="/wiki/Tranmere_Rovers_F.C." title="Tranmere Rovers F.C.">Tranmere Rovers</a> in the 2nd round after an eventual replay. This was following a serious head injury to young defender <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thomas_Bender" title="Thomas Bender">Thomas Bender</a> in the initial tie.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Accrington_Stanley_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The original town club, <a href="/wiki/Accrington_F.C." title="Accrington F.C.">Accrington</a>, was amongst the twelve founder members of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Football_League" title="The Football League">the Football League</a> in 1888, before resigning from the league after just five years. A team called Stanley Villa already existed at the time, named as such because they were based at the Stanley W.M.C. on Stanley Street in Accrington. With the demise of <a href="/wiki/Accrington_F.C." title="Accrington F.C.">Accrington</a>, Stanley Villa took the town name to become Accrington Stanley.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since leaving Peel Park, the club has played at the <a href="/wiki/Crown_Ground" title="Crown Ground">Crown Ground</a>. The ground has undergone expansion in recent years, including a new roof section on the Clayton End Terrace as well as a new hospitality suite. Despite these and a number of other recent improvements the ground remains one of the poorest in League Two and talks are still ongoing with a view to a permanent move to a new stadium located in Church, a small town bordering Accrington.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_advertisement">Television advertisement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Accrington_Stanley_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Television advertisement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the 1980s, the club was mentioned in a British <a href="/wiki/Television_advertisement" title="Television advertisement">advert</a> for milk, which briefly brought the club to the attention of the general public.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Thank_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Thank-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The advertisement featured two young boys in <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> replica shirts and made reference to Accrington Stanley's obscurity in comparison to Liverpool's success at the time.<br/>
Boy 1: "Milk! Urghh!"<br/>
Boy 2: "It's what <a href="/wiki/Ian_Rush" title="Ian Rush">Ian Rush</a> drinks."<br/>
Boy 1: "Ian Rush?"<br/>
Boy 2: "Yeah. And he said if I didn't drink lots of milk, when I grow up, I'll only be good enough to play for Accrington Stanley."<br/>
Boy 1: "<a href="/wiki/Accrington_Stanley,_Who_Are_They%3F" title="Accrington Stanley, Who Are They?">Accrington Stanley, who are they?</a>"<br/>
Boy 2: "Exactly."</p>
<p>In the weekly football show, <i><a href="/wiki/Soccer_AM" title="Soccer AM">Soccer A.M.</a></i>, the phrase "Accrington Stanley, who are they?" is said every time a fixture is read out that has the club in it, referring to the milk advert.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Stanley_Ultras">The Stanley Ultras</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Accrington_Stanley_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The Stanley Ultras">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2004 a number of fans founded the Stanley <a href="/wiki/Ultras" title="Ultras">Ultras</a>, in an effort to improve the atmosphere at both home and away matches. "Stanley Ultras are a group of like-minded fans who are dedicated to their role of <a href="/wiki/12th_man_(football)" title="12th man (football)">twelfth man</a>, adding colour, noise and support levels. We may support one of the smaller clubs in the UK, but our pride and passion shown on and off the terrace is second to none" – Hassan Khan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
