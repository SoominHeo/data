<p><b>Apple EarPods</b> are white in-ear <a href="/wiki/Headphones" title="Headphones">headphones</a> included with music players and smartphones designed and marketed by <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple Inc.</a> They are designed to fit the ear while retaining a new design pushed by Apple alongside the <a href="/wiki/IPod" title="IPod">iPod</a> and <a href="/wiki/IPod_Touch" title="IPod Touch">iPod Touch</a> products, with which they were sold together. Having been described as "revolutionary"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> they certainly do embody a unique aesthetic image, but Apple's reinterpretation of the earbud also drew criticisms of functionality, including a lack of rubber earpieces.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apple_earbuds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The basic earbuds have no controls. They ship with all iPods except the fifth generation iPod touch and iPod nano which are shipped with the newer Apple EarPods made in Taiwan rather than Japan.</p>
<p>The fourth generation can be identified by the white border around the speaker and the left and right markings on the front (rather than on the back).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_Generation_Earbuds">First Generation Earbuds</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apple_earbuds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: First Generation Earbuds">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first generation Earbuds were packaged with the original iPod, on October 23, 2001.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Earbuds_with_remote">Earbuds with remote</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apple_earbuds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Earbuds with remote">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Same as standard ones, except they also feature a built-in remote. They shipped only with the third-generation iPod shuffle.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Earbuds_with_remote_and_mic">Earbuds with remote and mic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apple_earbuds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Earbuds with remote and mic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shipped with the <a href="/wiki/IPhone_3GS" title="IPhone 3GS">iPhone 3GS</a>, <a href="/wiki/IPhone_4" title="IPhone 4">iPhone 4</a>, <a href="/wiki/IPhone_4S" title="IPhone 4S">iPhone 4S</a>, the third-generation <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IPod_touch" title="IPod touch">iPod touch</a>, and independently.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Apple_In-Ear_Headphones">Apple In-Ear Headphones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apple_earbuds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Apple In-Ear Headphones">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Apple In-Ear Headphones</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-applestore-inearpro_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-applestore-inearpro-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-apple-inearpro_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-apple-inearpro-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amazon-inearpro_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-amazon-inearpro-5">[5]</a></sup> (introduced on September 9, 2008, revised on September 12, 2012) are only sold independently. Like the regular earbuds they have a remote control and microphone built-in. They are made available as the high-end earbud offering with dual drivers, advertised as "<i>engineered for superior acoustic accuracy, balance, and clarity</i>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-apple-inearpro_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-apple-inearpro-4">[4]</a></sup> The remote and protective case was redesigned on September 12, 2012, with the remote now matching that of the EarPods and the case subsequently modified with elements of the EarPods case while retaining the triangular shape.</p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">"EarPods" redirects here. For other uses, see <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Earpod_(disambiguation)" title="Earpod (disambiguation)">Earpod (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p><b>Apple EarPods</b> (renamed "EarPods with 3.5mm Headphone plug")<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-applestore-earpods_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-applestore-earpods-6">[6]</a></sup> (introduced on September 12, 2012) first shipped with the <a href="/wiki/IPhone_5" title="IPhone 5">iPhone 5</a> and feature a remote control and microphone. They also ship with the <a href="/wiki/IPod_Touch_(5th_generation)" title="IPod Touch (5th generation)">iPod Touch (5th generation)</a> (without remote and mic) and the <a href="/wiki/IPod_Nano#7th_generation" title="IPod Nano">iPod Nano (7th generation)</a> (without remote and mic). They are also sold independently.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> All but the basic earbuds have control capsules allowing users to adjust volume, control music and video playback, and take pictures with Camera application, located on the cable of the right earpiece; those "with Remote and Mic" also include a <a href="/wiki/Microphone" title="Microphone">microphone</a> for phone calls and voice control of certain devices. These in-line controls have a microphone symbol on the reverse to denote their purpose.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Users can adjust volume, control music and video playback (play/pause and next/previous,) and record voice memos on supported iPod and iPhone models and <a href="/wiki/Macintosh" title="Macintosh">Mac</a> computers. There have been many reports of moisture problems with the remote/mic earbuds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/IPhone_(1st_generation)" title="IPhone (1st generation)">original iPhone</a> and <a href="/wiki/IPhone_3G" title="IPhone 3G">iPhone 3G</a> came with the iPhone Stereo Headset, a push-button and microphone on the right side of the headphones (there is no volume control, and only limited control of calls). Apple EarPods are compatible with <a href="/wiki/IPhone_3GS" title="IPhone 3GS">iPhone 3GS</a> and onwards, <a href="/wiki/IPod_Touch#2nd_generation" title="IPod Touch">iPod Touch (2nd generation)</a> and onwards and all models of the <a href="/wiki/IPad" title="IPad">iPad</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IPad_mini" title="IPad mini">iPad mini</a>. They require <a href="/wiki/IOS" title="IOS">iOS</a> 2.2 and onwards for iOS devices but are not compatible with the iPhone 3G or the original iPhone and the <a href="/wiki/IPod_Touch#1st_generation" title="IPod Touch">iPod Touch (1st generation)</a> due to sound problems. They are also compatible with most <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">Android</a> devices and computers. The EarPods shipped with the <a href="/wiki/IPhone_5" title="IPhone 5">iPhone 5</a> and later, along with those individually sold, come with a carrying case, whilst the <a href="/wiki/IPod_Touch_(5th_generation)" title="IPod Touch (5th generation)">iPod Touch (5th generation)</a>, <a href="/wiki/IPod_Touch_(6th_generation)" title="IPod Touch (6th generation)">iPod Touch (6th generation)</a> and iPod Nano (7th generation) only have basic biodegradable packaging, due to their lack of a remote and mic.</p>
<p><b>EarPods with Lightning Connector</b> ship with the <a href="/wiki/IPhone_7" title="IPhone 7">iPhone 7</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IPhone_7_Plus" title="IPhone 7 Plus">iPhone 7 Plus</a> and work with all devices that have a lightning connector and support <a href="/wiki/IOS_10" title="IOS 10">iOS 10</a> or later. They also ship with a headphone adapter for 3.5mm headphones. These adapters work with all devices that have a lightning connector.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Apple_AirPods">Apple AirPods</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apple_earbuds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Apple AirPods">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/AirPods" title="AirPods">AirPods</a></div>
<p>Introduced at the "See you on the 7th" event in September and initially supposed to launch before the end of October 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> After being delayed from its original launch date,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> AirPods became available for online purchase on December 13, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The AirPods are wireless earbud-style headphones with microphone, dual accelerometers, IR sensors used to pause the music if they are not in a user's ears, and motion touch sensors that are used to summon Siri. With the lack of an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Audio_jack" title="Audio jack">audio jack</a> in the iPhone 7, a wireless connection is one of only two remaining means to connect earphones to the device, the other being <a href="/wiki/Digital_audio" title="Digital audio">digital audio</a> through the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lightning_connector" title="Lightning connector">Lightning connector</a>. They have a battery life of five hours, and come with a charging case that gives them a total of 24 hours of battery life. The case is charged via an included <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lightning_connector" title="Lightning connector">Lightning</a> to USB cable. AirPods are compatible with iPhone, iPad, Apple Watch, Macs, and the 6th generation iPod Touch, but it requires <a href="/wiki/MacOS" title="MacOS">macOS</a> Sierra, <a href="/wiki/IOS" title="IOS">iOS</a> 10 and <a href="/wiki/WatchOS" title="WatchOS">watchOS</a>. They are also compatible with <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">Android</a>, but the setup process is more complicated and limits the Airpod's functionality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
