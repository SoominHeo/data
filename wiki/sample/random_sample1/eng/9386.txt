<p><b>Aehobak</b> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">애호박</span>; <small>Korean pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Korean" title="Help:IPA for Korean">[ɛ.ɦo.bak]</a></span>), also called <b>Korean zucchini</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is the edible, green to yellow-green <a href="/wiki/Summer_squash" title="Summer squash">summer squash</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Cucurbita_moschata" title="Cucurbita moschata">Cucurbita moschata</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Aehobag1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Aehobag1-2">[2]</a></sup> commonly used in <a href="/wiki/Korean_cuisine" title="Korean cuisine">Korean cuisine</a>. An aehobak has the shape of <a href="/wiki/Zucchini" title="Zucchini">zucchini</a>, but has thinner, smoother skin and more delicate flesh.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> It is usually sold in shrink-wrapped plastic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Common varieties of Korean zucchini include <a href="/wiki/Seoulmadi_squash" title="Seoulmadi squash">Seoulmadi aehobak</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bulam-sacheol_aehobak&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bulam-sacheol aehobak (page does not exist)">bulam-sacheol aehobak</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Miso_aehobak&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Miso aehobak (page does not exist)">miso aehobak</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p><b>Ingredients</b></p>
<ul>
<li><i><b>hobakgoji</b></i> – dried aehobak, prepared by slicing an aehobak thinly and <a href="/wiki/Food_drying" title="Food drying">sun-dry</a> the slices</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Dishes</b></p>
<ul>
<li><i><b>aehobakbuchimgae</b></i> – a type of <a href="/wiki/Buchimgae" title="Buchimgae">buchimgae</a>, made by seasoning julienned aehobak and mixing them with wheat flour and beaten egg, then pan-frying them in oil</li>
<li><i><b>aehobaknamul</b></i> – a type of <a href="/wiki/Namul" title="Namul">namul</a>, made by slicing aehobak and stir-frying the slices with <a href="/wiki/Saeujeot" title="Saeujeot">saeujeot</a>, ground meat, vegetable oil, toasted <a href="/wiki/Sesame" title="Sesame">sesame seeds</a>, and chopped <a href="/wiki/Allium_fistulosum" title="Allium fistulosum">spring onions</a></li>
<li><i><b>hobakgojibokkeum</b></i> – a type of <a href="/wiki/Bokkeum" title="Bokkeum">bokkeum</a>, made by stir-frying soaked <i>hobkgoji</i>(dried aehobak)</li>
<li><i><b>hobakjeon</b></i> – a type of <a href="/wiki/Jeon_(food)" title="Jeon (food)">jeon</a>, made by slicing aehobak thinly, egg-washing the slices, and pan-frying them in oil</li>
<li><i><b>hobakjijimi</b></i> – a type of <a href="/wiki/Jijimi" title="Jijimi">jijimi</a>, made by boiling thinly sliced aehobak with chopped <a href="/wiki/Allium_fistulosum" title="Allium fistulosum">spring onions</a> with <a href="/wiki/Doenjang" title="Doenjang">doenjang</a> or <a href="/wiki/Gochujang" title="Gochujang">gochujang</a></li>
<li><i><b>hobakmureum</b></i> – a type of <a href="/wiki/Jjim" title="Jjim">jjim</a>, made by splitting an aehobak vertically into three pieces, putting seasoned meat filling inside and sprinkling <a href="/wiki/Mushroom" title="Mushroom">mushrooms</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Egg_garnish&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Egg garnish (page does not exist)">egg garnish</a> on it, and steaming it</li>
</ul>
