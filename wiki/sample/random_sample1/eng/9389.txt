<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Action-adventure game</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="hatnote" role="note">"Action-adventure" redirects here. For other uses, see <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Action-adventure_(disambiguation)" title="Action-adventure (disambiguation)">Action-adventure (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p>The <b>action-adventure</b> <a href="/wiki/Video_game_genre" title="Video game genre">video game genre</a> includes video games that combine core elements from the <a href="/wiki/Action_game" title="Action game">action</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adventure_game" title="Adventure game">adventure</a> genres.</p>
<p>With the decline of the adventure game genre from mainstream popularity, the use of the term (and the hybrid term "action-adventure") has been more liberal. It is not uncommon for gamers to apply the term "adventure" or "action adventure" to describe the genre of fiction to which a game belongs, and not the gameplay itself.</p>
<p>Action-adventure is a hybrid genre, and thus the definition is very inclusive, leading it to be perhaps the broadest genre of video games, and can include many games which might better be categorized under narrow genres. Typically, pure adventure games have situational problems for the player to solve, with very little or no action. If there is action, it is generally confined to isolated minigames. Pure action games have gameplay based on real-time interactions that challenge the reflexes. Therefore, action-adventure games engage both reflexes and problem-solving, in both violent and non-violent situations.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definition">Definition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Action-adventure_game&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Definition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An action-adventure game can be defined as a game with a mix of elements from an <a href="/wiki/Action_game" title="Action game">action game</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Adventure_game" title="Adventure game">adventure game</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> especially crucial elements like puzzles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Puzzle1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Puzzle1-2">[2]</a></sup> Action-adventures require many of the same physical skills as action games, but also offer a storyline, numerous characters, an inventory system, dialogue, and other features of adventure games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fundamentals_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-fundamentals-3">[3]</a></sup> They are faster-paced than pure adventure games, because they include both physical and conceptual challenges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fundamentals_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-fundamentals-3">[3]</a></sup> Action-adventure games normally include a combination of complex story elements, which are displayed for players using audio and video. The story is heavily reliant upon the <a href="/wiki/Player_character" title="Player character">player character</a>'s movement, which triggers story events and thus affects the flow of the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Puzzle2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Puzzle2-4">[4]</a></sup> Some examples of action-adventure games include <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda_(series)" title="The Legend of Zelda (series)">The Legend of Zelda</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/God_of_War_(series)" title="God of War (series)">God of War</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fundamentals_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-fundamentals-3">[3]</a></sup> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tomb_Raider_(series)" title="Tomb Raider (series)">Tomb Raider</a></i> series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gal_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gal-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Exactly when a game stops being an adventure game and becomes an action game is a matter of interpretation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rollings_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rollings-6">[6]</a></sup> There are quite a few disagreements in the community and in the media over what actually constitutes an action-adventure game. One definition of the term "action adventure" may be '"An action/adventure game is a game that has enough action in it not to be called an adventure game, but not enough action to be called an action game."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-justadventure_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-justadventure-7">[7]</a></sup> In some cases an action game with puzzles will be classified as an action-adventure game, but if these puzzles are quite simple they might be classified as an action game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fundamentals_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-fundamentals-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NGen15_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-NGen15-8">[8]</a></sup> Others see action games as a pure genre, while an action-adventure is an action game that includes situational problem-solving.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-justadventure_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-justadventure-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NGen15_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-NGen15-8">[8]</a></sup> Adventure gamers may also be purists, rejecting any game that makes use of physical challenges or time pressure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fundamentals_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-fundamentals-3">[3]</a></sup> Regardless, the action-adventure label is prominent in articles over the internet and media. The term "action-adventure" is usually substituted for a particular subgenre due to its wide scope.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subgenres">Subgenres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Action-adventure_game&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subgenres">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although action-adventure games are diverse and difficult to classify, there are some distinct subgenres. Popular subgenres include:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>First-person action-adventure</b>, which make use of <a href="/wiki/First-person_shooter" title="First-person shooter">first-person shooter</a> gameplay, forgoing constant action in favor of important adventure game elements such as environmental problem-solving and a complex plot. These are sometimes called Real-Time Adventure games or RTAs for short. Notable examples of this include <i><a href="/wiki/Metroid_Prime" title="Metroid Prime">Metroid Prime</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Half-Life_2" title="Half-Life 2">Half-Life 2</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dishonored" title="Dishonored">Dishonored</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Far_Cry_3" title="Far Cry 3">Far Cry 3</a></i>.</li>
<li><b>Third-person action-adventure</b>, in which gameplay is in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_person_(video_games)" title="Third person (video games)">third-person</a>. Notable examples include games like the <i><a href="/wiki/Tomb_Raider" title="Tomb Raider">Tomb Raider</a></i> series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda" title="The Legend of Zelda">The Legend of Zelda</a></i> series, <i>Rune</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Severance:_Blade_of_Darkness" title="Severance: Blade of Darkness">Severance: Blade of Darkness</a></i>, the <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto" title="Grand Theft Auto">Grand Theft Auto</a></i> series, the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hitman_(video_game_series)" title="Hitman (video game series)">Hitman</a></i> series and the <i><a href="/wiki/Uncharted" title="Uncharted">Uncharted</a></i> series.</li>
<li><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isometric_adventure_game" title="Isometric adventure game">Isometric platform games</a></b>, which feature freely explorable environments with three-dimensional gameplay and two-dimensional graphics using an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isometric_graphics_in_video_games" title="Isometric graphics in video games">isometric projection</a>.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Open_world" title="Open world">Sandbox/Open world</a> action-adventure</b>, which focus on <a href="/wiki/Nonlinear_gameplay" title="Nonlinear gameplay">nonlinear gameplay</a> in an open world. Notable examples include the <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto" title="Grand Theft Auto">Grand Theft Auto</a></i> series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda" title="The Legend of Zelda">The Legend of Zelda series,</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Assassin%27s_Creed" title="Assassin's Creed">Assassin's Creed</a></i> series.</li>
<li><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Platform_Game#Platform-adventure_game" title="Platform Game">Platform-adventure games</a></b>, which emphasize both exploration and puzzle solving, but also feature traditional platform game conventions. Examples of games of this type are <i><a href="/wiki/Tomb_Raider" title="Tomb Raider">Tomb Raider</a></i> series, the <i><a href="/wiki/Banjo-Kazooie_(series)" title="Banjo-Kazooie (series)">Banjo-Kazooie</a></i> series, <a href="/wiki/Super_Mario" title="Super Mario">Super Mario</a> series and the <i><a href="/wiki/Metroid" title="Metroid">Metroid</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Castlevania" title="Castlevania">Castlevania</a></i> games; the term "<a href="/wiki/Metroidvania" title="Metroidvania">Metroidvania</a>" is derived from these latter two and used to describe games in this genre that generally are based on two-dimensional platformers.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Stealth_game" title="Stealth game">Stealth games</a></b>, which emphasize avoiding detection by enemies rather than engaging them in direct combat, leading to a greater emphasis on exploration and puzzle-solving than other types of action games. Notable examples include the <i><a href="/wiki/Metal_Gear" title="Metal Gear">Metal Gear</a></i> series, the <i><a href="/wiki/Assassin%27s_Creed" title="Assassin's Creed">Assassin's Creed</a></i> series, and the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hitman_(video_game_series)" title="Hitman (video game series)">Hitman</a></i> series.</li>
<li><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Survival_horror_game" title="Survival horror game">Survival horror games</a></b>, which emphasize "inventory management" and making sure the player has enough ammunition and recovery items to "survive" the horror setting. Survival-horror is a thematic genre with diverse gameplay, however, so not all survival horror games share these features. The <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil" title="Resident Evil">Resident Evil</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Silent_Hill" title="Silent Hill">Silent Hill</a></i> franchises popularized this subgenre and stand to date as the most popular franchises of its kind.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Action-adventure_game&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Adventure_game#Common_objectives_and_gameplay" title="Adventure game">Common objectives and gameplay of adventure games</a> and <a href="/wiki/Action_game#Defining_elements" title="Action game">defining elements of action games</a></div>
<p>Action-adventure games are faster paced than pure adventure games, and include physical as well as conceptual challenges<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rollings_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rollings-6">[6]</a></sup> where the story is enacted rather than narrated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> While motion-based, often reflexive, actions are required,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Puzzle2_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Puzzle2-4">[4]</a></sup> the gameplay still follows a number of adventure game genre tropes (gathering <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Item_(game)" title="Item (game)">items</a>, exploration of and interaction with one's environment, often including an <a href="/wiki/Overworld" title="Overworld">overworld</a> connecting areas of importance, and puzzle-solving).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Puzzle1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Puzzle1-2">[2]</a></sup> While the controls are arcade-style (character movement, few action commands) there is an ultimate goal beyond a high score.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Puzzle1_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Puzzle1-2">[2]</a></sup> In most action-adventure games, the player controls a single <a href="/wiki/Avatar_(computing)" title="Avatar (computing)">avatar</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Protagonist" title="Protagonist">protagonist</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fundamentals_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-fundamentals-3">[3]</a></sup> This type of game is often quite similar to <a href="/wiki/Role-playing_video_game" title="Role-playing video game">role-playing video games</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meg_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-meg-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>They are distinct from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graphic_adventure_game" title="Graphic adventure game">graphic adventures</a>, which sometimes have free-moving central characters, but also wider variety of commands and fewer or no action game elements and are distinct too from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Text_adventures" title="Text adventures">text adventures</a>, characterized by many different commands introduced by the user via a complex <a href="/wiki/Text_parser" title="Text parser">text parser</a> and no free-moving character. While they share general gameplay dynamics, action-adventures vary widely in the design of their viewpoints, including <a href="/wiki/Bird%27s-eye_view" title="Bird's-eye view">bird's eye</a>, side scrolling, first-person, <a href="/wiki/Third-person_shooter" title="Third-person shooter">third-person</a>, over-the-shoulder, or even a 3/4 isometric view.</p>
<p>Many action-adventure games simulate a conversation through a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conversation_tree" title="Conversation tree">conversation tree</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> When the player encounters a <a href="/wiki/Non-player_character" title="Non-player character">non-player character</a>, they are allowed to select a choice of what to say. The NPC gives a scripted response to the player, and the game offers the player several new ways to respond.</p>
<p>Due to the action-adventure subgenre's broad inclusive nature it causes some players to having difficulty finishing a particular game. To compensate for this lack of the player's ability, companies have devised ways to give the player help, such as helpful clues, or allowing them to skip puzzles outright.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Puzzle3_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Puzzle3-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Action-adventure_game&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Legend_of_Zelda_NES.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="224" data-file-width="256" height="193" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/3a/Legend_of_Zelda_NES.PNG/220px-Legend_of_Zelda_NES.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/3/3a/Legend_of_Zelda_NES.PNG 1.5x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Legend_of_Zelda_NES.PNG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda_(1986_video_game)" title="The Legend of Zelda (1986 video game)">The Legend of Zelda</a></i> (1986), a seminal title that established the action-adventure game genre.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The history of the action-adventure genre began with the <a href="/wiki/Text-based_game" title="Text-based game">text-based</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Colossal_Cave_Adventure" title="Colossal Cave Adventure">Colossal Cave Adventure</a></i> (1976), which was the first <a href="/wiki/Adventure_game" title="Adventure game">adventure game</a> ever created. First written for the <a href="/wiki/PDP-10" title="PDP-10">PDP-10</a>, it was later ported to countless other platforms. A programmer for <a href="/wiki/Atari" title="Atari">Atari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Warren_Robinett" title="Warren Robinett">Warren Robinett</a>, was given the task of adapting <i>Colossal Cave Adventure</i> to the <a href="/wiki/Atari_2600" title="Atari 2600">Atari 2600</a>. With the new iteration, Atari planned to allow the player to fight in combat like in an <a href="/wiki/Action_game" title="Action game">action game</a>, thus leading to the first action-adventure game, <i><a href="/wiki/Adventure_(Atari_2600)" title="Adventure (Atari 2600)">Adventure</a></i> (1979).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-justadventure_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-justadventure-7">[7]</a></sup> It is considered the first known game in this genre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Later, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silas_Warner" title="Silas Warner">Silas Warner</a> arrived at <a href="/wiki/Muse_Software" title="Muse Software">Muse Software</a>, where he developed <i><a href="/wiki/Castle_Wolfenstein" title="Castle Wolfenstein">Castle Wolfenstein</a></i>, an action-adventure game that would inspire <a href="/wiki/Id_Software" title="Id Software">id Software</a> to create the <a href="/wiki/First-person_shooter" title="First-person shooter">first-person shooter</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Wolfenstein_3D" title="Wolfenstein 3D">Wolfenstein 3D</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> According to Roe R. Adams (who worked on the <i><a href="/wiki/Wizardry" title="Wizardry">Wizardry</a></i> series of <a href="/wiki/Role-playing_video_game" title="Role-playing video game">RPGs</a>), early action-adventures "were basically <a href="/wiki/Arcade_game" title="Arcade game">arcade games</a> done in a <a href="/wiki/Fantasy" title="Fantasy">fantasy</a> setting," pointing to <i><a href="/wiki/Castlevania" title="Castlevania">Castlevania</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Trojan_(video_game)" title="Trojan (video game)">Trojan</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Wizards_%26_Warriors" title="Wizards &amp; Warriors">Wizards &amp; Warriors</a></i> as examples.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The action-adventure later became an established genre with <a href="/wiki/Shigeru_Miyamoto" title="Shigeru Miyamoto">Shigeru Miyamoto</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda_(1986_video_game)" title="The Legend of Zelda (1986 video game)">The Legend of Zelda</a></i> (1986), which became a success due to how it combined elements from different genres to create a compelling hybrid, including exploration, <a href="/wiki/Transport_puzzle" title="Transport puzzle">transport puzzles</a>, adventure-style inventory <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puzzle_game" title="Puzzle game">puzzles</a>, an action component, a <a href="/wiki/Monetary_system" title="Monetary system">monetary system</a>, and simplified RPG-style <a href="/wiki/Experience_point#Level-based_progression" title="Experience point">level building</a> without the <a href="/wiki/Experience_point" title="Experience point">experience points</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The game was also an early example of <a href="/wiki/Open_world" title="Open world">open world</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nonlinear_gameplay" title="Nonlinear gameplay">nonlinear gameplay</a>, and introduced innovations like <a href="/wiki/Saved_game" title="Saved game">saving</a> on battery backup. It became one of the most influential games of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_of_video_game_consoles_(third_generation)" title="History of video game consoles (third generation)">8-bit era</a>, inspiring action-adventures like <i><a href="/wiki/Metroid" title="Metroid">Metroid</a></i> and RPGs like <i><a href="/wiki/Final_Fantasy" title="Final Fantasy">Final Fantasy</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <i>Zelda</i> has since remained the most prolific action-adventure game series through to the present day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Games like <i><a href="/wiki/Xanadu_(video_game)" title="Xanadu (video game)">Xanadu</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Metroid_(video_game)" title="Metroid (video game)">Metroid</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Vampire_Killer" title="Vampire Killer">Vampire Killer</a></i> combined action-adventure exploration with a <a href="/wiki/Side-scrolling_video_game" title="Side-scrolling video game">side-scrolling</a> <a href="/wiki/Platform_game" title="Platform game">platformer</a> format, creating the <a href="/wiki/Metroidvania" title="Metroidvania">Metroidvania</a> platform-adventure subgenre. Similarly, games like <i><a href="/wiki/Castle_Wolfenstein" title="Castle Wolfenstein">Castle Wolfenstein</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Metal_Gear_(video_game)" title="Metal Gear (video game)">Metal Gear</a></i> combined action-adventure exploration with stealth mechanics, laying the foundations for the <a href="/wiki/Stealth_game" title="Stealth game">stealth game</a> subgenre, which would later be popularized in 1998 by <i><a href="/wiki/Metal_Gear_Solid" title="Metal Gear Solid">Metal Gear Solid</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Tenchu:_Stealth_Assassins" title="Tenchu: Stealth Assassins">Tenchu: Stealth Assassins</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Thief:_The_Dark_Project" title="Thief: The Dark Project">Thief: The Dark Project</a></i>.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cinematic_platformer" title="Cinematic platformer">cinematic platformer</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Persia_(1989_video_game)" title="Prince of Persia (1989 video game)">Prince of Persia</a></i> (1989) featured action-adventure elements, inspiring games such as <i><a href="/wiki/Another_World_(video_game)" title="Another World (video game)">Another World</a></i> (1991) and <i><a href="/wiki/Flashback_(1992_video_game)" title="Flashback (1992 video game)">Flashback</a></i> (1992). <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alone_in_the_Dark_(video_game)" title="Alone in the Dark (video game)">Alone in the Dark</a></i> (1992) featured the use of <a href="/wiki/3D_computer_graphics" title="3D computer graphics">3D graphics</a>, which would later be popularized by <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil_(1996_video_game)" title="Resident Evil (1996 video game)">Resident Evil</a></i> (1996) and <i><a href="/wiki/Tomb_Raider_(1996_video_game)" title="Tomb Raider (1996 video game)">Tomb Raider</a></i> (1996). <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil" title="Resident Evil">Resident Evil</a></i> in particular created the <a href="/wiki/Survival_horror" title="Survival horror">survival horror</a> subgenre, inspiring titles such as <i><a href="/wiki/Silent_Hill_(video_game)" title="Silent Hill (video game)">Silent Hill</a></i> (1999) and <i><a href="/wiki/Fatal_Frame_(video_game)" title="Fatal Frame (video game)">Fatal Frame</a></i> (2001).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-justadventure_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-justadventure-7">[7]</a></sup> Action-adventure games have gone on to become more popular than the pure <a href="/wiki/Adventure_game" title="Adventure game">adventure games</a> and pure <a href="/wiki/Platform_game" title="Platform game">platform</a> <a href="/wiki/Action_game" title="Action game">action games</a> that influenced them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
