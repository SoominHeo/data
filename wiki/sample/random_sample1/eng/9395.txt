<p><b>Anne Louise Dudek</b> (born March 22, 1975) is an American actress. She is known for portraying Tiffany Wilson in the 2004 film <i><a href="/wiki/White_Chicks" title="White Chicks">White Chicks</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_House_characters#Amber_Volakis" title="List of House characters">Dr. Amber Volakis</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/House_(TV_series)" title="House (TV series)">House</a></i>, Lura Grant on <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Love" title="Big Love">Big Love</a></i>, and Francine Hanson on the <a href="/wiki/AMC_(TV_channel)" title="AMC (TV channel)">AMC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Mad_Men" title="Mad Men">Mad Men</a></i>. She has also starred in the British television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Book_Group" title="The Book Group">The Book Group</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anne_Dudek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dudek was born in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Newton,_Massachusetts" title="Newton, Massachusetts">Newton, Massachusetts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Her father was an architect.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> She attended <a href="/wiki/Northwestern_University" title="Northwestern University">Northwestern University</a>. She is of <a href="/wiki/Polish_Americans" title="Polish Americans">Polish descent</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anne_Dudek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From the mid-1990s (while at Northwestern University) through 2001, Dudek appeared in various theater productions and on <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a>. She made her Broadway debut in <i>Wrong Mountain</i> in 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She won the Connecticut Critics Circle Award for outstanding performance in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Glass_Menagerie" title="The Glass Menagerie">The Glass Menagerie</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>After success on Broadway, she made the jump to television. Her first starring role on television came when she was cast as the lead in the British <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comedy_drama" title="Comedy drama">comedy drama</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Book_Group" title="The Book Group">The Book Group</a></i>. The show aired on <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> in the United Kingdom to rave reviews. In her native United States, Dudek has since guest starred on other TV shows, including <i><a href="/wiki/Desperate_Housewives" title="Desperate Housewives">Desperate Housewives</a></i> (as <a href="/wiki/Karl_Mayer" title="Karl Mayer">Karl Mayer</a>'s girlfriend in Season 1), <i><a href="/wiki/How_I_Met_Your_Mother" title="How I Met Your Mother">How I Met Your Mother</a></i> (as Ted's <a href="/wiki/Krav_Maga" title="Krav Maga">Krav Maga</a>-trained ex-girlfriend), <i><a href="/wiki/Friends" title="Friends">Friends</a></i> (as 'Precious', <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mike_Hannigan" title="Mike Hannigan">Mike Hannigan</a>'s girlfriend), <i><a href="/wiki/ER_(TV_series)" title="ER (TV series)">ER</a></i> (as a mother who accidentally shot her son), <i><a href="/wiki/Charmed" title="Charmed">Charmed</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Bones_(TV_series)" title="Bones (TV series)">Bones</a></i> (as <a href="/wiki/Seeley_Booth" title="Seeley Booth">Seeley Booth</a>'s lawyer girlfriend), <i><a href="/wiki/Numbers_(TV_series)" title="Numbers (TV series)">Numb3rs</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Six_Feet_Under_(TV_series)" title="Six Feet Under (TV series)">Six Feet Under</a></i>. She also played Lucinda Barry in the pilot episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Psych" title="Psych">Psych</a></i>, and a teacher who has a sexual relationship with one of her students (based on the case of <a href="/wiki/Debra_Lafave" title="Debra Lafave">Debra Lafave</a>) on <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Criminal_Intent" title="Law &amp; Order: Criminal Intent">Law &amp; Order: Criminal Intent</a></i>. In both "Friends" and "How I Met Your Mother", her character was dumped on her birthday.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dudek had a recurring role on <i><a href="/wiki/House_(TV_series)" title="House (TV series)">House</a></i> (from season 4 onwards) as <a href="/wiki/List_of_House_characters#Recurring_characters" title="List of House characters">Dr. Amber Volakis</a>, one of 40 physicians under consideration by <a href="/wiki/Gregory_House" title="Gregory House">Dr. Gregory House</a> for permanent positions on his team. Her character, nicknamed "Cutthroat Bitch" by House, continued to the end of season 4 but was killed off in the two-part season finale, "<a href="/wiki/House%27s_Head" title="House's Head">House's Head</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Wilson%27s_Heart" title="Wilson's Heart">Wilson's Heart</a>". She would later reappear as a hallucination by House in the season 5 episode "<a href="/wiki/Saviors_(House)" title="Saviors (House)">Saviors</a>", appearing in this form for the remainder of the season.</p>
<p>Dudek later appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Mad_Men" title="Mad Men">Mad Men</a></i> as next-door neighbor Francine Hanson, and on <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Love" title="Big Love">Big Love</a></i> as one of antagonist <a href="/wiki/Alby_Grant" title="Alby Grant">Alby Grant</a>'s wives, both recurring roles. In 2010, she joined the cast of <i><a href="/wiki/Covert_Affairs" title="Covert Affairs">Covert Affairs</a></i>. She played <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Hopkins" title="Anthony Hopkins">Anthony Hopkins</a>' character's daughter in the 2003 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Human_Stain_(film)" title="The Human Stain (film)">The Human Stain</a></i>, and cruise line heir Tiffany Wilson in the 2004 <a href="/wiki/Comedy_film" title="Comedy film">comedy film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/White_Chicks" title="White Chicks">White Chicks</a></i>. In 2012, she played an <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">ISS</a> astronaut in one episode of the TV show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Touch_(2012_TV_series)" title="Touch (2012 TV series)">Touch</a></i>. She also reprised her role as Amber Volakis for the <i>House</i> series finale "<a href="/wiki/Everybody_Dies_(House)" title="Everybody Dies (House)">Everybody Dies</a>".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anne_Dudek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dudek was previously married to artist Matthew Heller. They have a son, Akiva, born in December 2008,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and a daughter, Saskia, born in February 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The pair divorced in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
