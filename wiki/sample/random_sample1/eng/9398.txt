<p><b>Andrew John Ridgeley</b> (born 26 January 1963) is an English singer, songwriter and record producer. He was best known in the 1980s as a member of the music <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Musical_duo" title="Musical duo">duo</a> <a href="/wiki/Wham!" title="Wham!">Wham!</a> (with <a href="/wiki/George_Michael" title="George Michael">George Michael</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Ridgeley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andrew John Ridgeley was born in <a href="/wiki/Windlesham" title="Windlesham">Windlesham</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Surrey,_England" title="Surrey, England">Surrey</a>, England. Ridgeley's parents were Jennifer and Albert Ridgeley. His mother is English and his father is of Italian and <a href="/wiki/Egyptians" title="Egyptians">Egyptian</a> descent. Ridgeley grew up in <a href="/wiki/Bushey" title="Bushey">Bushey</a>, Hertfordshire, and attended <a href="/wiki/Bushey_Meads_School" title="Bushey Meads School">Bushey Meads School</a>. His mother was a schoolteacher at Bushey Heath Primary School and his father worked for Canon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rttnews1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-rttnews1-1">[1]</a></sup> When <a href="/wiki/George_Michael" title="George Michael">George Michael</a> was enrolled at the school, Ridgeley volunteered to take him under his wing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rttnews1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-rttnews1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Ridgeley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After years of playing in various music groups, the most notable, being The Executive, Michael and Ridgeley formed Wham!. They then approached various record labels with a homemade <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compact_cassette" title="Compact cassette">tape</a> (which took ten minutes to <a href="/wiki/Sound_recording_and_reproduction" title="Sound recording and reproduction">record</a> in Ridgeley's living room), and signed with <a href="/wiki/Innervision_Records" title="Innervision Records">Innervision Records</a>. According to <i>I'm Coming to Take You to Lunch</i>, a book written by Wham's manager, <a href="/wiki/Simon_Napier-Bell" title="Simon Napier-Bell">Simon Napier-Bell</a>, the band left Innervision after the first album and signed with CBS.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wham.21">Wham!</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Ridgeley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Wham!">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Wham!" title="Wham!">Wham!</a></div>
<p>Wham! enjoyed worldwide success from 1982 to 1986, selling more than 28 million records worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Wham! made their U.S. debut appearance on <a href="/wiki/Dick_Clark" title="Dick Clark">Dick Clark</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/American_Bandstand" title="American Bandstand">American Bandstand</a></i> and went on to become the only British act in the 1980s to secure three No. 1 singles in both the UK and the US.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Simon Napier-Bell has admitted that he fabricated a story in 1984 that Ridgeley had been smashed on the nose by somebody in a nightclub, in order to get publicity for Wham! on the front page of British tabloid newspapers. After days of tabloid headlines, it was later revealed that the bandages on Ridgeley's face were because he had plastic surgery on his nose.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wham! had two UK No. 1 singles in 1984 and were competing that year with pop rivals <a href="/wiki/Duran_Duran" title="Duran Duran">Duran Duran</a> to be Britain's biggest pop act. Napier-Bell devised a publicity scheme which he believed would turn Wham! into major international stars. In April 1985, he took Wham! to China for a 10-day visit. This gained huge worldwide media attention when Wham! became the first Western pop group to play in China, in front of 15,000 people at the Worker's Gymnasium in Beijing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1985, Ridgeley performed at the Live Aid charity concert next to backing singers like Kiki Lee, while his bandmate Michael, performed with Elton John.</p>
<p>In 1986, "<a href="/wiki/The_Edge_of_Heaven" title="The Edge of Heaven">The Edge of Heaven</a>" became Wham!'s fourth and final UK No. 1 single. With George Michael keen to move into a more adult market, Wham! broke up after a farewell concert entitled "The Final" in front of 72,000 people at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium_(1923)" title="Wembley Stadium (1923)">Wembley Stadium</a> on Saturday 28 June 1986.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yogsbackyard1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-yogsbackyard1-7">[7]</a></sup> Shortly afterwards, Ridgeley moved to <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a>, and tried his hand at <a href="/wiki/Formula_Three" title="Formula Three">Formula Three</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motor_racing" title="Motor racing">motor racing</a>. Meeting with little success, Ridgeley moved to Los Angeles in pursuit of a career in acting. He returned to Britain permanently in 1990.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">CBS Records</a> (later <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment" title="Sony Music Entertainment">Sony Music</a>), having taken up the option on Wham!'s contract that specified solo albums from Michael and Ridgeley, released a guitar and drum driven solo recording from Ridgeley, <i><a href="/wiki/Son_of_Albert" title="Son of Albert">Son of Albert</a></i>, in 1990. His brother Paul, an occasional percussionist for <a href="/wiki/Bananarama" title="Bananarama">Bananarama</a>, played drums on the album. <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">Singles</a> included "Shake" and "Red Dress".</p>
<p>"Shake", which was the first single from his solo album, had only moderate success, reaching No. 16 on the Australian charts<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aus_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-aus-8">[8]</a></sup> and No. 58 in the UK charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uk_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-uk-9">[9]</a></sup> "Shake" was the 81st highest-selling single of 1990 in Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> "Red Dress" only charted in Australia, where it peaked outside the top 100.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aus_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-aus-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ridgeley's record company, CBS, passed up the option of a second album from him after unsuccessful sales of the album <i>Son of Albert</i>, which was panned by critics and failed to make the top 75 in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dailymail1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-dailymail1-11">[11]</a></sup> <i>Son of Albert</i> was one of the worst received albums of 1990, achieving only half a star in a savage <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> magazine review.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1998_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1998-12">[12]</a></sup> Ridgeley later said: "It was disappointing and depressing to receive quite such a beating over that album."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1998_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1998-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 27 January 1991, Ridgeley joined George Michael on stage for a few songs at the encore of his performance at the <a href="/wiki/Rock_in_Rio" title="Rock in Rio">Rock in Rio</a> event at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Est%C3%A1dio_do_Maracan%C3%A3" title="Estádio do Maracanã">Maracanã Stadium</a> in Rio de Janeiro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yogsbackyard1_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-yogsbackyard1-7">[7]</a></sup> Since 1991, Ridgeley has generally shunned the limelight,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-13">[13]</a></sup> but he did agree to give an interview on camera for a 2005 documentary <i>A Different Story</i>, about the life of George Michael.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Ridgeley also appeared as a studio guest on the first series of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBC_2" title="BBC 2">BBC 2</a> programme <i><a href="/wiki/Fantasy_Football_League" title="Fantasy Football League">Fantasy Football League</a></i> in 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-Wham.21">Post-Wham!</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Ridgeley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-Wham!">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since 1982, he has reportedly amassed £10 million from sales and royalties of records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dailymail1_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-dailymail1-11">[11]</a></sup> Although the single "<a href="/wiki/Careless_Whisper" title="Careless Whisper">Careless Whisper</a>" was issued as a George Michael solo piece, it was credited as being co-written by Ridgeley.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> It has sold six million copies worldwide<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and, to date, is the 34th best-selling single of all time in the United Kingdom, having sold over 1.3 million copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Ridgeley still benefits financially from receiving thousands of pounds per annum from his share of "Careless Whisper" royalties alone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1998_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1998-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2005, George Michael and Andrew Ridgeley made plans to reunite as Wham! for <a href="/wiki/Live_8" title="Live 8">Live 8</a>, but Ridgeley reportedly pulled out at the last minute. In 2012, George Michael dismissed rumours that he and Ridgeley were set for a Wham! reunion to mark the 30th anniversary of the group's first record. Michael said that there was no truth in speculation the group would reform for a one-off concert.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Upon hearing of Michael's death on 25 December 2016, Ridgeley paid his respects on Twitter, saying, "Heartbroken at the loss of my beloved friend Yog."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Ridgeley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ridgeley lives near <a href="/wiki/Wadebridge" title="Wadebridge">Wadebridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, England, United Kingdom, in a restored 15th-century farm property with his partner <a href="/wiki/Keren_Woodward" title="Keren Woodward">Keren Woodward</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> a member of the group <a href="/wiki/Bananarama" title="Bananarama">Bananarama</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Albums">Albums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Ridgeley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Albums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
