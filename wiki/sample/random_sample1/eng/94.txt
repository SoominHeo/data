<p><b>1592 (<a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MDXCII</a>)</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year" title="Leap year">leap year</a> <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Wednesday" title="Leap year starting on Wednesday">starting on Wednesday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> ED) of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Saturday" title="Leap year starting on Saturday">leap year starting on Saturday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> BA) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>, the 1592nd year of the <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">Common Era</a> (CE) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a></i> (AD) designations, the 592nd year of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium" title="2nd millennium">2nd millennium</a>, the 92nd year of the <a href="/wiki/16th_century" title="16th century">16th century</a>, and the 3rd year of the <a href="/wiki/1590s" title="1590s">1590s</a> decade. As of the start of 1592, the Gregorian calendar was 10 days ahead of the Julian calendar, which remained in localized use until 1923.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1592&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93June">January–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1592&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: January–June">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_30" title="January 30">January 30</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pope_Clement_VIII" title="Pope Clement VIII">Pope Clement VIII</a> (born Ippolito Aldobrandini) succeeds <a href="/wiki/Pope_Innocent_IX" title="Pope Innocent IX">Pope Innocent IX</a>, who died one month earlier, as the 231st <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">pope</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_7" title="February 7">February 7</a> – <a href="/wiki/George_Gordon,_1st_Marquess_of_Huntly" title="George Gordon, 1st Marquess of Huntly">George Gordon, 1st Marquess of Huntly</a>, sets fire to <a href="/wiki/Donibristle" title="Donibristle">Donibristle</a> Castle in Scotland and murders <a href="/wiki/James_Stewart,_2nd_Earl_of_Moray" title="James Stewart, 2nd Earl of Moray">James Stewart, 2nd Earl of Moray</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_3" title="March 3">March 3</a> – <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Dublin" title="Trinity College, Dublin">Trinity College, Dublin</a>, Ireland's oldest university, is founded.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_14" title="March 14">March 14</a> – Ultimate <a href="/wiki/Pi_Day" title="Pi Day">Pi Day</a>: the largest correspondence between calendar dates and significant digits of <a href="/wiki/Pi" title="Pi">pi</a> since the introduction of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_4" title="April 4">April 4</a> – The future <a href="/wiki/Henry_IV_of_France" title="Henry IV of France">Henry IV of France</a>, King designate of <a href="/wiki/Henry_III_of_France" title="Henry III of France">Henry III of France</a>, announces in a declaration, so-called "Expedient," his intention to take instruction in and convert to the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic religion</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_13" title="April 13">April 13</a> – <a href="/wiki/Japanese_invasions_of_Korea_(1592%E2%80%9398)" title="Japanese invasions of Korea (1592–98)">Japanese invasions of Korea (1592–98)</a> open with beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Busanjin" title="Siege of Busanjin">Siege of Busanjin</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_24" title="April 24">April 24</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Sangju_(1592)" title="Battle of Sangju (1592)">Battle of Sangju</a>: The Japanese are victorious over the Koreans (<a href="/wiki/Joseon" title="Joseon">Joseon</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_28" title="April 28">April 28</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ch%27ungju" title="Battle of Ch'ungju">Battle of Ch'ungju</a>: Japan inflicts a decisive defeat on Korea.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_7" title="May 7">May 7</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Okpo" title="Battle of Okpo">Battle of Okpo</a> is another naval victory for Korea over Japan.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_29" title="May 29">May 29</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Sacheon_(1592)" title="Battle of Sacheon (1592)">Battle of Sacheon</a>: Korean admiral <a href="/wiki/Yi_Sun-sin" title="Yi Sun-sin">Yi Sun-sin</a> destroys all 13 Japanese ships taking part, using his improved <a href="/wiki/Turtle_ship" title="Turtle ship">turtle ship</a> for the first time in battle.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_2" title="June 2">June 2</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Dangpo" title="Battle of Dangpo">Battle of Dangpo</a> is another decisive naval victory for Korea over Japan.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93December">July–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1592&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: July–December">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_8" title="July 8">July 8</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hansan_Island" title="Battle of Hansan Island">Battle of Hansan Island</a>: Korean admiral <a href="/wiki/Yi_Sun-sin" title="Yi Sun-sin">Yi Sun-sin</a> destroys or captures around 60 Japanese ships without loss in a battle in which around 190 ships take part.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_20" title="July 20">July 20</a> – The Japanese <a href="/wiki/Japanese_invasions_of_Korea_(1592%E2%80%9398)#Capture_of_Pyongyang" title="Japanese invasions of Korea (1592–98)">capture the Korean capital</a> <a href="/wiki/Pyongyang" title="Pyongyang">Pyongyang</a>, causing <a href="/wiki/Seonjo_of_Joseon" title="Seonjo of Joseon">Seonjo</a> to request the assistance of <a href="/wiki/Ming_dynasty" title="Ming dynasty">Ming dynasty</a> Chinese forces, who recapture the city a year later.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_30" title="July 30">July 30</a> – <a href="/wiki/Alonso_de_Sotomayor" title="Alonso de Sotomayor">Alonso de Sotomayor</a> petitions the viceroy of <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> for more troops to help resist attacks by Indians and English pirates.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_9" title="August 9">August 9</a> – English explorer <a href="/wiki/John_Davis_(English_explorer)" title="John Davis (English explorer)">John Davis</a>, commander of the <i><a href="/wiki/Desire_(ship)" title="Desire (ship)">Desire</a></i>, probably discovers the <a href="/wiki/Falkland_Islands" title="Falkland Islands">Falkland Islands</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_14" title="August 14">August 14</a> – The Koreans are victorious over the Japanese in the naval <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hansan_Island" title="Battle of Hansan Island">Battle of Hansan Island</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_15" title="August 15">August 15</a> (or 19) – The Portuguese galleon <i><a href="/wiki/Madre_de_Deus" title="Madre de Deus">Madre de Deus</a></i>, laden with treasure, is captured by English privateers in the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_1" title="September 1">September 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Busan_(1592)" title="Battle of Busan (1592)">Battle of Busan</a>: The Korean fleet makes a surprise attack on the Japanese but fails to break their supply lines to Busan.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_5" title="October 5">October 5</a> – The Koreans are victorious over the Japanese in the naval <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Jinju_(1592)" title="Siege of Jinju (1592)">Siege of Jinju</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_17" title="November 17">November 17</a> – <a href="/wiki/John_III_of_Sweden" title="John III of Sweden">John III</a> is succeeded by his son <a href="/wiki/Sigismund_III_Vasa" title="Sigismund III Vasa">Sigismund</a> as <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_Sweden" title="Monarchy of Sweden">King of Sweden</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Date_unknown">Date unknown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1592&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Date unknown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>The <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Collegium_Melitense" title="Collegium Melitense">Collegium Melitense</a></i> is founded by Bishop Garagallo.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/William_Cecil,_1st_Baron_Burghley" title="William Cecil, 1st Baron Burghley">William Cecil, 1st Baron Burghley</a>, chief adviser of Queen <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_I_of_England" title="Elizabeth I of England">Elizabeth I of England</a>, is taken seriously ill.</li>
<li>Negotiations begin for the dissolution of the childless marriage of <a href="/wiki/Henry_IV_of_France" title="Henry IV of France">Henry IV of France</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marguerite_of_Valois" title="Marguerite of Valois">Marguerite of Valois</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Confucius" title="Confucius">Confucian</a> shrine of <a href="/wiki/Munmyo" title="Munmyo">Munmyo</a> is destroyed by fire.</li>
</ul>
