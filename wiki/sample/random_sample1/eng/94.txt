<p><b>.be</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a> <a href="/wiki/Country_code" title="Country code">country code</a> <a href="/wiki/Top-level_domain" title="Top-level domain">top-level domain</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CcTLD" title="CcTLD">ccTLD</a>) for <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. The domain became active in 1989 and was administrated by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pierre_Verbaeten&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pierre Verbaeten (page does not exist)">Pierre Verbaeten</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Katholieke_Universiteit_Leuven" title="Katholieke Universiteit Leuven">Katholieke Universiteit Leuven</a>. In 2000, the control of the TLD was transferred to <a href="/wiki/DNS_Belgium" title="DNS Belgium">DNS Belgium</a>. As of June 2013 there are 1,392,477 registered domains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was announced in November 2005 that the initial registration of domains would be free until the beginning of 2006, though with some limits on the number any individual was allowed to register. This was remarkably popular, with some 17,000 registrations coming in on the first day of the promotion.</p>
<p>Domain names are registered directly at second level. Some of Belgian's main academic institutions, such as the <a href="/wiki/Vrije_Universiteit_Brussel" title="Vrije Universiteit Brussel">Vrije Universiteit Brussel</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universit%C3%A9_Libre_de_Bruxelles" title="Université Libre de Bruxelles">Université Libre de Bruxelles</a>, use third-level names under ac.be, but others have abandoned its use (<a href="/wiki/Katholieke_Universiteit_Leuven" title="Katholieke Universiteit Leuven">Katholieke Universiteit Leuven</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universiteit_Gent" title="Universiteit Gent">Universiteit Gent</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universit%C3%A9_Catholique_de_Louvain" title="Université Catholique de Louvain">Université Catholique de Louvain</a>).</p>
<p>Any .be registration has to be ordered via a registered agent.</p>
<p>The domain has also been in use as a logo for the federal government since 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regional_domains">Regional domains</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.be&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Regional domains">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The far right party <a href="/wiki/Vlaams_Belang" title="Vlaams Belang">Vlaams Belang</a> introduced a draft resolution in the <a href="/wiki/Flemish_Parliament" title="Flemish Parliament">Parliament</a> of the region of <a href="/wiki/Flanders" title="Flanders">Flanders</a> demanding the creation of a Flemish top level domain .vl (for "Vlaanderen", Flanders in Dutch) but this resolution failed to receive support of the region's other parties. Only recognized UN member states are eligible for a two-letter domain extension.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rfc1591_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-rfc1591-3">[3]</a></sup> In October 2008, the Flemish government expressed its intention to obtain a three-letter domain code for Flanders, like .vla, .vln or .fla. In 2014 <a href="/wiki/.vlaanderen" title=".vlaanderen">.vlaanderen</a> and <a href="/wiki/.brussels" title=".brussels">.brussels</a> were added, also administered by DNSBelgium.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Domain_hacks">Domain hacks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=.be&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Domain hacks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On the website <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, a shortener for YouTube videos is <i>youtu.be</i>, for example <i><code>www.youtube.com/watch?v=</code></i> becomes <i><code>youtu.be/</code></i>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>’s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chrome_experiment" title="Chrome experiment">chrome experiment</a>, for the 40th anniversary of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rubik%27s_cube" title="Rubik's cube">Rubik's cube</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rubik%27s_cube#Chrome_Cube_Lab" title="Rubik's cube">Chrome cube lab</a>, features many domain hacks like Image Cube (<a class="external text" href="http://imagecu.be" rel="nofollow">imagecu.be</a>), and I Am The Cube (<a class="external text" href="http://iamthecu.be" rel="nofollow">iamthecu.be</a>).</p>
