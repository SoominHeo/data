<p><b>Andrew Eben Strominger</b> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Theoretical_physics" title="Theoretical physics">theoretical physicist</a> who is the Director of Harvard's Center for the Fundamental Laws of Nature. He has made significant contributions to <a href="/wiki/Quantum_gravity" title="Quantum gravity">quantum gravity</a> and <a href="/wiki/String_theory" title="String theory">string theory</a>. These include his work on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Calabi-Yau_manifold" title="Calabi-Yau manifold">Calabi-Yau compactification</a> and topology change in string theory, and on the stringy origin of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_hole_entropy" title="Black hole entropy">black hole entropy</a>. He is a senior fellow at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Society_of_Fellows" title="Society of Fellows">Society of Fellows</a>, and is the Gwill E. York Professor of Physics.</p>
<p>Strominger completed his undergraduate studies at Harvard in 1977 before attending the University of California, Berkeley for his Masters. He received his PhD from MIT in 1982 under the supervision of <a href="/wiki/Roman_Jackiw" title="Roman Jackiw">Roman Jackiw</a>. Prior to joining Harvard as a professor in 1997, he held a faculty position at the University of California, Santa Barbara. He is the author of over 200 publications.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Strominger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_contributions">Notable contributions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Strominger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Notable contributions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>a paper with <a href="/wiki/Cumrun_Vafa" title="Cumrun Vafa">Cumrun Vafa</a> that explains the microscopic origin of the <a href="/wiki/Black_hole_thermodynamics" title="Black hole thermodynamics">black hole entropy</a>, originally calculated thermodynamically by <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Hawking" title="Stephen Hawking">Stephen Hawking</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Bekenstein" title="Jacob Bekenstein">Jacob Bekenstein</a>, from string theory</li>
<li>a paper with Philip Candelas, Gary Horowitz, and <a href="/wiki/Edward_Witten" title="Edward Witten">Edward Witten</a> in the 1980s about the relevance of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Calabi-Yau" title="Calabi-Yau">Calabi-Yau</a> manifolds for obtaining the <a href="/wiki/Standard_Model" title="Standard Model">Standard Model</a> from <a href="/wiki/String_theory" title="String theory">string theory</a></li>
<li>other articles discussing the <a href="/wiki/DS/CFT_correspondence" title="DS/CFT correspondence">dS/CFT correspondence</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Kerr/CFT_correspondence" title="Kerr/CFT correspondence">Kerr/CFT correspondence</a> (variations of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AdS/CFT" title="AdS/CFT">AdS/CFT</a> correspondence)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/S-brane" title="S-brane">S-branes</a> (a variation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D-branes" title="D-branes">D-branes</a>)</li>
<li>OM-theory (with <a href="/wiki/Shiraz_Minwalla" title="Shiraz Minwalla">Shiraz Minwalla</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Seiberg" title="Nathan Seiberg">Nathan Seiberg</a>)</li>
<li>noncommutative <a href="/wiki/Soliton" title="Soliton">solitons</a> (with <a href="/wiki/Shiraz_Minwalla" title="Shiraz Minwalla">Shiraz Minwalla</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rajesh_Gopakumar" title="Rajesh Gopakumar">Rajesh Gopakumar</a>)</li>
<li>massless black holes in the form of wrapped <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D-branes" title="D-branes">D3-branes</a> that regulate the physics of a <a href="/wiki/Conifold" title="Conifold">conifold</a> and allow <a href="/wiki/Topology" title="Topology">topology</a> change</li>
<li>the <a href="/wiki/SYZ_conjecture" title="SYZ conjecture">SYZ conjecture</a>, an interpretation of <a href="/wiki/Homological_mirror_symmetry" title="Homological mirror symmetry">mirror symmetry</a> as a special case of <a href="/wiki/T-duality" title="T-duality">T-duality</a> (with <a href="/wiki/Eric_Zaslow" title="Eric Zaslow">Eric Zaslow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shing-Tung_Yau" title="Shing-Tung Yau">Shing-Tung Yau</a>)</li>
<li>purely cubic action for <a href="/wiki/String_field_theory" title="String field theory">string field theory</a></li>
<li>superstrings with <a href="/wiki/Torsion_tensor" title="Torsion tensor">torsion</a></li>
<li>a study of the relationship between asymptotic symmetries in asymptotically flat space-times, soft theorems and memory effects</li>
<li>an analytic calculation of the exact spectrum of gravitational wave emission from <a href="/wiki/Extreme_mass_ratio_inspiral" title="Extreme mass ratio inspiral">extreme mass ratio inspirals (EMRIs)</a> into rapidly rotating black holes (these gravitational waves are expected to be detected with future space-based <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gravitational_wave_detector" title="Gravitational wave detector">gravitational wave detectors</a> such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evolved_Laser_Interferometer_Space_Antenna" title="Evolved Laser Interferometer Space Antenna">eLISA</a></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Strominger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In recognition of his accomplishments, Strominger has been awarded numerous prizes, fellowships, and honorary professorships. These include the <a href="/wiki/Oskar_Klein_Memorial_Lecture" title="Oskar Klein Memorial Lecture">Klein Medal</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Swedish_Academy_of_Sciences" title="Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences">Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences</a> and the 2014 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dirac_Prize" title="Dirac Prize">Dirac Medal</a> from the <a href="/wiki/International_Centre_for_Theoretical_Physics" title="International Centre for Theoretical Physics">Abdus Salam International Centre for Theoretical Physics</a>, which he received for his contributions to the origin, development, and further understanding of string theory. Furthermore, he received the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fundamental_Physics_Prize" title="Fundamental Physics Prize">Physics Frontiers Breakthrough Prize</a> from the Milner Foundation along with colleague <a href="/wiki/Cumrun_Vafa" title="Cumrun Vafa">Cumrun Vafa</a> in 2014. This award was bestowed upon the pair in honor of their "numerous deep and groundbreaking contributions to quantum field theory, quantum gravity, string theory and geometry." The Foundation also recognized their "joint statistical derivation of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bekenstein-Hawking_Formula" title="Bekenstein-Hawking Formula">Bekenstein-Hawking area-entropy relation</a> unified the laws of thermodynamics with the laws of black hole dynamics and revealed the holographic nature of quantum spacetime."</p>
