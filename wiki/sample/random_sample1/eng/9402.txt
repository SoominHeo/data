<p><b>Andrews Nakahara</b> (born March 12, 1983) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese-Brazilian" title="Japanese-Brazilian">Japanese-Brazilian</a> professional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_artist" title="Mixed martial artist">mixed martial artist</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kickboxer" title="Kickboxer">kickboxer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kyokushin" title="Kyokushin">kyokushin</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karateka" title="Karateka">karateka</a> currently signed with <a href="/wiki/K-1" title="K-1">K-1</a>. He made his MMA pro debut against <a href="/wiki/Kazushi_Sakuraba" title="Kazushi Sakuraba">Kazushi Sakuraba</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Dream_2" title="Dream 2">Dream 2: Middleweight Grand Prix 2008 First Round</a>. Andrews and <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Filho_(martial_artist)" title="Francisco Filho (martial artist)">Francisco Filho</a> were recently awarded a Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu Purple Belt under former <a href="/wiki/Strikeforce_(mixed_martial_arts)" title="Strikeforce (mixed martial arts)">Strikeforce</a> contender Magno Almeida.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-graciemag_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-graciemag-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrews_Nakahara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nakahara was born in <a href="/wiki/Mogi_das_Cruzes" title="Mogi das Cruzes">Mogi das Cruzes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> on March 12, 1983 to <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a> parents who arrived in Brazil during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Brazilian" title="Japanese Brazilian">Japanese immigration</a> boom in the early 60s and 70s. Even though his parents are Japanese, Nakahara cannot speak Japanese and was raised only speaking <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>. Nakahara first begun to study <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kyokushin_kaikan" title="Kyokushin kaikan">Kyokushin kaikan</a> a strong <a href="/wiki/Full_contact_karate" title="Full contact karate">full contact karate</a> style developed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Masutatsu_Oyama" title="Masutatsu Oyama">Masutatsu Oyama</a>. Nakahara went on to join the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Karate_Organization" title="International Karate Organization">International Karate Organization</a> (IKO) team. Since then he has been successful in many competitions both in Brazil and America including championship fights with rival karatekas <a href="/wiki/Ewerton_Teixeira" title="Ewerton Teixeira">Ewerton Teixeira</a> and Eduardo Tanaka.</p>
