<p><b>Andrea Baker</b> (née <b>Taylor</b>) is an American actress and voice actress, best known for voicing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_Totally_Spies!#Clover" title="List of characters in Totally Spies!">Clover</a> in the English dub of the French and Canadian animated television series <i><a href="/wiki/Totally_Spies!" title="Totally Spies!">Totally Spies!</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrea_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baker was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_D.C." title="Washington D.C.">Washington D.C.</a>. After graduating from <a href="/wiki/Georgetown_University" title="Georgetown University">Georgetown University</a>, she and a friend drove for more than six weeks across the US and eventually ended up in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_me_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-about_me-2">[2]</a></sup> Baker took a six-week "Acting for Commercials" course at Tepper Gallegos Casting, where she was introduced to her future agent, Steve Simon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_me_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-about_me-2">[2]</a></sup> Taylor also studied scene study in class mentored by Bobby Shaw Chance. She was signed on by Arlene Thornton's voice-over agency and then started voice acting for commercials and television.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_me_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-about_me-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her first on-screen appearance was a small role on <i><a href="/wiki/General_Hospital" title="General Hospital">General Hospital</a></i> in 1997.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_me_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-about_me-2">[2]</a></sup> After graduating from DW Brown's studio, she auditioned for a role in the 1998 feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Pleasantville_(film)" title="Pleasantville (film)">Pleasantville</a></i>. Barbara Harris introduced Baker to Nancy Meyers, who hired Baker as an "inner voice" actress on <i><a href="/wiki/What_Women_Want" title="What Women Want">What Women Want</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_me_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-about_me-2">[2]</a></sup> Baker has been working exclusively as a voice actress since 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_me_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-about_me-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Besides from film and television, Baker has appeared in several plays for Afternoon Theatre, Basement Productions, and Joanne Baron Studios.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-resume_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-resume-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrea_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baker met her husband during production of <i><a href="/wiki/What_Women_Want" title="What Women Want">What Women Want</a></i>. They married in October 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interview_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-interview-4">[4]</a></sup> They have two children together.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_me_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-about_me-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
