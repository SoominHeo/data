<p>The <b>Andreanof Islands</b> (<a href="/wiki/Aleut_language" title="Aleut language">Aleut</a>: <span lang="ale" xml:lang="ale"><i>Niiĝuĝin tanangis</i></span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>) are a group of islands in the <a href="/wiki/Aleutian_Islands" title="Aleutian Islands">Aleutian Islands</a>, in southwestern <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a>. They are located at about 52° North and 172°57' to 179°09' West.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andreanof_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Andreanof Islands are located between <a href="/wiki/Amchitka_Pass" title="Amchitka Pass">Amchitka Pass</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Rat_Islands" title="Rat Islands">Rat Islands</a> group to the west, and <a href="/wiki/Amukta_Pass" title="Amukta Pass">Amukta Pass</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Islands_of_Four_Mountains" title="Islands of Four Mountains">Islands of Four Mountains</a> group to the east. The islands extend about 275 miles (440 km). The total land area of all islands (including the Delarof Islands) is 1,515.349 sq mi (3,924.737 km²).</p>
<p>The total population was 412 persons as of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Census,_2000" title="United States Census, 2000">2000 census</a>, the vast majority in the city of <a href="/wiki/Adak,_Alaska" title="Adak, Alaska">Adak</a> on <a href="/wiki/Adak_Island" title="Adak Island">Adak Island</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Islands">Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andreanof_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Delarof_Islands" title="Delarof Islands">Delarof Islands</a> are a subgroup of the Andreanof Islands group as well as the westernmost islands in the group.</p>
<p>The largest islands in the group are, from west to east, <a href="/wiki/Gareloi_Island" title="Gareloi Island">Gareloi Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tanaga_Island" title="Tanaga Island">Tanaga Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kanaga_Island" title="Kanaga Island">Kanaga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adak_Island" title="Adak Island">Adak Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kagalaska_Island" title="Kagalaska Island">Kagalaska Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Great_Sitkin_Island" title="Great Sitkin Island">Great Sitkin Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atka_Island" title="Atka Island">Atka Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amlia" title="Amlia">Amlia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Seguam_Island" title="Seguam Island">Seguam Island</a>.</p>
<p>The islands are usually foggy and are treeless because of the almost constant wind.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andreanof_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Andreanof Islands were named for the Russian navigator, Andreyan Tolstykh, who was the first to explore the islands in 1761.</p>
<p>There were several United States military bases on the islands during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. The bases on Adak were enlarged and made permanent after the war but were closed in 1995.</p>
