<p><b>Andrew Ivan "Andy" Bell</b> (born 25 April 1964) is the lead singer of the English <a href="/wiki/Synthpop" title="Synthpop">synthpop</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duet_(music)" title="Duet (music)">duo</a> <a href="/wiki/Erasure" title="Erasure">Erasure</a>. His solo career includes the albums <i><a href="/wiki/Non-Stop_(Andy_Bell_album)" title="Non-Stop (Andy Bell album)">Non-Stop</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Electric_Blue_(album)" title="Electric Blue (album)">Electric Blue</a></i> &amp; "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=IPop(album)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="IPop(album) (page does not exist)">iPop</a>".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Bell_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andrew Ivan Bell grew up in the <a href="/wiki/Dogsthorpe,_Peterborough" title="Dogsthorpe, Peterborough">Dogsthorpe</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> area in <a href="/wiki/Peterborough" title="Peterborough">Peterborough</a>. His family still resides in the city and surrounding areas such as <a href="/wiki/Market_Deeping" title="Market Deeping">Market Deeping</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Bell was educated at the <a href="/wiki/The_King%27s_(The_Cathedral)_School" title="The King's (The Cathedral) School">King's School</a> in the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Bell_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1985, while selling women's shoes and performing in a band called the Void, he responded to a newspaper advert that was looking for a singer. <a href="/wiki/Vince_Clarke" title="Vince Clarke">Vince Clarke</a> had been Bell's "hero"; Bell was successful in his audition and together they formed the group <a href="/wiki/Erasure" title="Erasure">Erasure</a>. The pair have sold over 25 million albums worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bell has also donated time and made <a href="/wiki/Sound_recording_and_reproduction" title="Sound recording and reproduction">recordings</a> for charities. These included Ferry Aid's <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">cover</a> of "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Let_It_Be_(Ferry_Aid_song)" title="Let It Be (Ferry Aid song)">Let It Be</a>" (1987); a cover of <a href="/wiki/Cole_Porter" title="Cole Porter">Cole Porter</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Too_Darn_Hot" title="Too Darn Hot">Too Darn Hot</a>", that was included in 1990s <i>Red Hot + Blue</i> album to raise funds for AIDS and HIV research; re-making <a href="/wiki/Lene_Lovich" title="Lene Lovich">Lene Lovich</a>'s "Rage" alongside her to be included in <i><a href="/wiki/People_for_the_Ethical_Treatment_of_Animals" title="People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals">PETA</a>'</i>s album (1991) in favour of a wildlife campaign; and performing twice on Big Spender's Red Hot and Dance events to support various AIDS projects (in both December 1994 and November 2004). Bell also performed on the <a href="/wiki/True_Colors_(concert_tour)" title="True Colors (concert tour)">True Colours Tour 2008</a>.</p>
<p>Bell sang the role of Montresor in the opera <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fall_of_the_House_of_Usher_(opera)" title="The Fall of the House of Usher (opera)">The Fall of the House of Usher</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Hammill" title="Peter Hammill">Peter Hammill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Judge_Smith" title="Judge Smith">Judge Smith</a>, released in 1991 and reissued with a new recording in 1999.</p>
<p>Bell's first solo album was announced in July 2005. He signed a worldwide solo <a href="/wiki/Recording_contract" title="Recording contract">recording contract</a> with <a href="/wiki/Sanctuary_Records" title="Sanctuary Records">Sanctuary Records</a>, and announced details of his debut album, which was released on 3 October 2005 and entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Electric_Blue_(album)" title="Electric Blue (album)">Electric Blue</a></i>. The album featured fourteen tracks, including three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duet_(music)" title="Duet (music)">duets</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Claudia_Br%C3%BCcken" title="Claudia BrÃ¼cken">Claudia BrÃ¼cken</a> of <a href="/wiki/Propaganda_(band)" title="Propaganda (band)">Propaganda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jake_Shears" title="Jake Shears">Jake Shears</a> of <a href="/wiki/Scissor_Sisters" title="Scissor Sisters">Scissor Sisters</a>.</p>
<p>The first single, "Crazy", released on 26 September 2005, included club <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remixes</a> from his Erasure partner <a href="/wiki/Vince_Clarke" title="Vince Clarke">Vince Clarke</a>, plus <a href="/wiki/Cicada_(band)" title="Cicada (band)">Cicada</a>, MHC and King Roc. <i>Electric Blue</i> was co-written and recorded throughout 2004 and 2005 with <a href="/wiki/Manhattan_Clique" title="Manhattan Clique">Manhattan Clique</a> (Philip Larsen and Chris Smith) who have worked with Erasure, <a href="/wiki/Moby" title="Moby">Moby</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_B-52%27s" title="The B-52's">the B-52s</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stereophonics" title="Stereophonics">Stereophonics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Goldfrapp" title="Goldfrapp">Goldfrapp</a>.</p>
<p>Bell released his second solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Non-Stop_(Andy_Bell_album)" title="Non-Stop (Andy Bell album)">Non-Stop</a></i>, on 7 June 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-andybell_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-andybell-4">[4]</a></sup> It was co-written and co-produced by Bell and <a href="/wiki/Pascal_Gabriel" title="Pascal Gabriel">Pascal Gabriel</a>, who previously remixed "<a href="/wiki/It_Doesn%27t_Have_to_Be" title="It Doesn't Have to Be">It Doesn't Have to Be</a>" for Erasure. It also features a collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Perry_Farrell" title="Perry Farrell">Perry Farrell</a>.</p>
<p>Using the pseudonym <b>MimÃ³</b>, Bell released two Pascal Gabriel-produced singles on <a href="/wiki/Mute_Records" title="Mute Records">Mute Records</a>: "Running Out" (2009)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and "Will You Be There?" (2010)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> prior to his second solo album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The name, used as a "tribute to good friend Tomeau MimÃ³â, was not used on any subsequent solo releases, as a legal block was issued against Bell by another artist already using the MimÃ³ name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Both songs will appear on <i>Non-Stop</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-andybell_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-andybell-4">[4]</a></sup> Both of these singles have since been re-branded as Andy Bell.</p>
<p>In June 2011, he appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Popstar_to_Operastar_(series_2)" title="Popstar to Operastar (series 2)">second season</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ITV_Network" title="ITV Network">ITV</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Popstar_to_Operastar" title="Popstar to Operastar">Popstar to Operastar</a></i> and finished in fifth place.</p>
<p>In August 2016 he appeared on the <a class="external text" href="http://atlantisevents.com/" rel="nofollow">Atlantis cruise</a> line from Venice to Barcelona.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Bell_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bell is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Openly_gay" title="Openly gay">openly gay</a>, and had a longtime partner in Paul M. Hickey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Bell told <i><a href="/wiki/Melody_Maker" title="Melody Maker">Melody Maker</a></i> in 1986, "I don't want to go out of my way to talk about it but I'm not going to pretend I'm not [gay]. I won't portray a heterosexual in videos and we're consciously doing lyrics that could apply to either sex".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He told Barry Walters of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seventeen_(magazine)" title="Seventeen (magazine)">Seventeen</a></i>, "I want to be known as a good performer but it's important to me to take a stance. If you're doing music, you should use it for something and have substance. Being gay and open about it is my substance".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 17 December 2004, Bell publicly announced that he has been <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HIV_positive_people" title="HIV positive people">HIV-positive</a> since 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In a 2007 interview, he remarked that there is a complacency among gay men about <a href="/wiki/HIV" title="HIV">HIV</a>:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>There are definitely HIV-positive people who meet other HIV-positive men, and like still having unprotected sex. I think it must be quite hard for young gay men because there is so much for them now to doâthere are so many saunas and stuff like that for them to go to, and it's so easy to get it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Bell suffers from <a href="/wiki/Avascular_necrosis" title="Avascular necrosis">avascular necrosis</a>, which is unrelated to the HIV infection. He has had both hips replaced, which keeps him from "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pogoing" title="Pogoing">pogoing</a> around" in more recent performances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
