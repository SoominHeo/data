<p><b>Andrew Alexander</b> "<b>Andy</b>" <b>Cole</b> (born 15 October 1971) is an English former professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a>. As a player, he was a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a> from 1988 to 2008. He is most notably remembered for his time in the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>, where he spent six years of his career and winning numerous trophies in the process.</p>
<p>He also played in the top division of English football for <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a>, as well as in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League" title="Football League">Football League</a> for <a href="/wiki/Bristol_City_F.C." title="Bristol City F.C.">Bristol City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C." title="Birmingham City F.C.">Birmingham City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nottingham_Forest_F.C." title="Nottingham Forest F.C.">Nottingham Forest</a>. He is the third highest goalscorer in <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> history, with 187 goals.</p>
<p>Cole has the distinction of being one of the few players in England to have swept all possible honours in the English game, including the <a href="/wiki/PFA_Young_Player_of_the_Year" title="PFA Young Player of the Year">PFA Young Player of the Year</a> award, as well as the coveted <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> title. Cole was also <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cap_(football)" title="Cap (football)">capped</a> fifteen times for England between 1995 and 2001, scoring once against Albania in a World Cup qualifier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blackburn-deal_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-blackburn-deal-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Cole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Cole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cole started his career as a youth player for <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> on leaving school in 1988, signing professional in 1989. He made his only league appearance for Arsenal, aged 19, as a <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> against <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_United_F.C." title="Sheffield United F.C.">Sheffield United</a> at <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_Stadium" title="Arsenal Stadium">Highbury</a> during a <a href="/wiki/Football_League_First_Division" title="Football League First Division">First Division</a> game on 29 December 1990; Arsenal won 4â1 but Cole did not score. He also made a substitute appearance against <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> in the <a href="/wiki/FA_Community_Shield" title="FA Community Shield">Charity Shield</a> in <a href="/wiki/1991_FA_Charity_Shield" title="1991 FA Charity Shield">1991</a> and almost made an immediate impact, hitting the side netting from outside the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The following season he was loaned to <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Third_Division" title="Football League Third Division">Third Division</a>, where he scored three goals in 13 games.</p>
<p>Cole was sold to <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Second_Division" title="Football League Second Division">Second Division</a> <a href="/wiki/Bristol_City_F.C." title="Bristol City F.C.">Bristol City</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">Â£</a>500,000 deal â at the time he was their most expensive player. Having proved himself as a competent young goalscorer with Bristol City (who began the <a href="/wiki/1992%E2%80%9393_in_English_football" title="1992â93 in English football">1992â93 season</a> in the new <a href="/wiki/Football_League_First_Division" title="Football League First Division">Football League Division One</a> following the creation of the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>), Cole was quickly one of the hottest prospects in England and his name was regularly linked with <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> clubs throughout the 1992â93 season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Newcastle_United">Newcastle United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Cole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Newcastle United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 1993, Division One leaders <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> broke their club record club by paying Â£1.75Â million to sign Cole. He then scored 12 goals in as many league games as the Magpies cruised to the Division One title and won promotion to the Premier League. His 12 goals included two hat-tricks, the first against <a href="/wiki/Barnsley_F.C." title="Barnsley F.C.">Barnsley</a> on 7 April, the second on the final day of the season in a 7â1 hammering of <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He also scored the first of the club's two goals in their 2â0 promotion clinching win over <a href="/wiki/Grimsby_Town_F.C." title="Grimsby Town F.C.">Grimsby Town</a> at <a href="/wiki/Blundell_Park" title="Blundell Park">Blundell Park</a> on 4 May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/David_Kelly_(association_footballer)" title="David Kelly (association footballer)">David Kelly</a> was sold to <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a>, manager <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Keegan" title="Kevin Keegan">Kevin Keegan</a> brought in <a href="/wiki/Peter_Beardsley" title="Peter Beardsley">Peter Beardsley</a> as Cole's strike partner for the <a href="/wiki/1993%E2%80%9394_FA_Premier_League" title="1993â94 FA Premier League">1993â94 Premier League campaign</a>.</p>
<p>Cole scored 34 goals in 40 games during Newcastle's first Premier League season as they finished third and qualified for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a>. Cole scored a total of 41 goals in all competitions â breaking the club's goalscoring record which had been set by <a href="/wiki/Hughie_Gallacher" title="Hughie Gallacher">Hughie Gallacher</a> nearly 70 years earlier (Gallacher still holds the record for the highest number of league goals in a season with 36). His first top division goal was in a 1â1 draw against defending league champions Manchester United (who went on to win the double) at Old Trafford on 21 August. This was Newcastle's first goal in the Premier League.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Exactly three months later, he scored all three goals as the Magpies crushed <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> 3â0 at home. Another hat-trick followed against <a href="/wiki/Coventry_City_F.C." title="Coventry City F.C.">Coventry City</a> in late February and with <a href="/wiki/Peter_Beardsley" title="Peter Beardsley">Peter Beardsley</a> almost as lethal as his strike partner, the Magpies finished third in the league and qualified for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> for the first time since the 1970s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Cole was subsequently voted <a href="/wiki/PFA_Young_Player_of_the_Year" title="PFA Young Player of the Year">PFA Young Player of the Year</a> for that season.</p>
<p>Cole then scored nine Premier League goals in 18 games for Newcastle after the start of the <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_in_English_football" title="1994â95 in English football">1994â95 season</a>, and also scored a <a href="/wiki/Hat-trick" title="Hat-trick">hat-trick</a> against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Antwerp_FC" title="Royal Antwerp FC">Royal Antwerp</a> in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-euro_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-euro-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In all, Cole hit 68 goals in 84 games for Newcastle giving him a strike rate of 81%. In terms of goals per game for Newcastle, only Hughie Gallacher has a better record.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> His last goal for the Magpies came in the 1â1 home draw with <a href="/wiki/Ipswich_Town_F.C." title="Ipswich Town F.C.">Ipswich Town</a> on 26 November 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manchester_United">Manchester United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Cole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Manchester United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 10 January 1995, Cole was suddenly sold in a shock deal to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> for a deal worth Â£7Â million â Â£6Â million cash plus Â£1Â million-rated <a href="/wiki/Keith_Gillespie" title="Keith Gillespie">Keith Gillespie</a> going in the opposite direction, setting a new record for the most expensive British transfer.</p>
<p>Despite joining halfway through the <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_in_English_football" title="1994â95 in English football">1994â95 season</a>, Cole still managed to score 12 goals in just 18 Premier League games for United. This included his first, the winner in a 1â0 victory over <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a> on 4 February at <a href="/wiki/Old_Trafford" title="Old Trafford">Old Trafford</a> and five in the <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C._9%E2%80%930_Ipswich_Town_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C. 9â0 Ipswich Town F.C.">9â0 rout</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ipswich_Town_F.C." title="Ipswich Town F.C.">Ipswich Town</a>; making him the first player to score five goals in a Premier League game.</p>
<p>However, he missed two goal chances in the final minutes against <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a> on the final day of the season as they could only manage a 1â1 draw and the league title went to <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> instead. He was <a href="/wiki/Cup-tied" title="Cup-tied">cup-tied</a> for the <a href="/wiki/1995_FA_Cup_Final" title="1995 FA Cup Final">FA Cup Final</a> a week later. Without him, United lost to <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> 1â0. United were also without the banned <a href="/wiki/Eric_Cantona" title="Eric Cantona">Eric Cantona</a> and the injured <a href="/wiki/Andrei_Kanchelskis" title="Andrei Kanchelskis">Andrei Kanchelskis</a>, the club's two other highest scoring players that season.</p>
<p>His first full season in <a href="/wiki/1995%E2%80%9396_in_English_football" title="1995â96 in English football">1995â96</a> with Manchester United proved to be difficult, as Cole struggled to find his trademark form in a side now built around the much heralded return of <a href="/wiki/Eric_Cantona" title="Eric Cantona">Eric Cantona</a>. Though Cole scored in four successive games during the winter, including an important opening goal in United's 2â0 defeat of title rivals Newcastle United on 27 December, Cole was badgered by fans and critics alike across much of the season for only scoring 14 times and missing many chances. However, Cole picked up his form in the business end of the season and scored critical goals including the equaliser in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1995-96_FA_Cup" title="1995-96 FA Cup">FA Cup</a> semi-final against <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> to help send United to <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium_(1923)" title="Wembley Stadium (1923)">Wembley</a> again. He then collected his first Premier League title winners medal and scored the second goal in United's 3â0 defeat of Middlesbrough on the final day of the season to help United win the Premier League title for the third time in four years. He also played in their FA Cup final victory to become part of England's first ever side to win the double twice.</p>
<p>Before the <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%9397_in_English_football" title="1996â97 in English football">1996â97 season</a> began, Cole had to deal with being offered to Blackburn Rovers as part-exchange in a Â£12Â million deal that would have brought <a href="/wiki/Alan_Shearer" title="Alan Shearer">Alan Shearer</a> to <a href="/wiki/Old_Trafford" title="Old Trafford">Old Trafford</a> but the offer was turned down and Shearer opted for Newcastle instead. Despite <a href="/wiki/Alex_Ferguson" title="Alex Ferguson">Alex Ferguson</a>'s clear indication to Cole that he was looking for another striker, after the Shearer deal fell through, Cole fought to stay at the club and was handed the <a href="/wiki/Squad_number_(association_football)" title="Squad number (association football)">number 9 shirt</a> (having previously worn 17). The arrival of <a href="/wiki/Ole_Gunnar_Solskj%C3%A6r" title="Ole Gunnar SolskjÃ¦r">Ole Gunnar SolskjÃ¦r</a> â and being the victim of two broken legs suffered after a tackle by <a href="/wiki/Neil_Ruddock" title="Neil Ruddock">Neil Ruddock</a> in a reserve game against <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> restricted Cole's first-team chances further. However, Cole managed to recover by December that year, and still play in 20 Premier League games (10 as a substitute) for the season. Cole then ended the season strongly with several crucial goals in both the league (such as away at title rivals Arsenal), and in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> (where he scored a goal voted the season's best European goal against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Porto" title="F.C. Porto">Porto</a>) to complete his comeback from injury. Cole then scored the title sealing goal in a landmark 3â1 win for United at <a href="/wiki/Anfield" title="Anfield">Anfield</a> â the scene of his broken legs just half a season earlier â against <a href="/wiki/Roy_Evans" title="Roy Evans">Roy Evans</a>' "<a href="/wiki/Spice_Boys_(footballers)" title="Spice Boys (footballers)">Spice Boys</a>" Liverpool team and thereby aiding United in winning their fourth title in five years, with Cole winning another Premier League title medal.</p>
<p>For the <a href="/wiki/1997%E2%80%9398_in_English_football" title="1997â98 in English football">1997â98 season</a>, the retirement of <a href="/wiki/Eric_Cantona" title="Eric Cantona">Eric Cantona</a> saw Cole emerge as first choice striker once again, and he discovered his best form ever for the club, becoming the joint top goalscorer in the Premier League during the course of the season with 18 goals, including a slew of spectacular goals â one of which, a chip against <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a>, had the fans' vote as the Manchester United goal of the season. Cole also developed a strong partnership with <a href="/wiki/Teddy_Sheringham" title="Teddy Sheringham">Teddy Sheringham</a> (despite considerable personal friction between the two), but United finished trophyless for only the second time in nine seasons as they lost to <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> in the end. Cole achieved several personal landmarks in this campaign, scoring his first <a href="/wiki/List_of_UEFA_Champions_League_hat-tricks" title="List of UEFA Champions League hat-tricks">European hat-trick</a> for the club in an away match at <a href="/wiki/Feyenoord" title="Feyenoord">Feyenoord</a>, as well as ending the season as runner-up in the <a href="/wiki/PFA_Players%27_Player_of_the_Year" title="PFA Players' Player of the Year">PFA Players' Player of the Year</a> award to Arsenal's <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Bergkamp" title="Dennis Bergkamp">Dennis Bergkamp</a>. Despite this accreditation, and being the leading goalscorer in all competitions that season with 25, Cole was omitted from England's 1998 World Cup squad by then-manager <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Hoddle" title="Glenn Hoddle">Glenn Hoddle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-euro_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-euro-8">[8]</a></sup> Cole remained upbeat when interviewed and when asked about his new-found return to success, Cole claimed that he had found freedom in his life after the injuries of the previous season, saying he had great joy with his newborn son, and lived for him and his family in his faith as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Born_again_(Christianity)" title="Born again (Christianity)">Born again</a> <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He also claimed the friendship of <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Giggs" title="Ryan Giggs">Ryan Giggs</a>, his room-mate on away games, was a major motivating factor through the tough times when fans doubted him at United.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Cole faced competition from new signing <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Yorke" title="Dwight Yorke">Dwight Yorke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Teddy_Sheringham" title="Teddy Sheringham">Teddy Sheringham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ole_Gunnar_Solskj%C3%A6r" title="Ole Gunnar SolskjÃ¦r">Ole Gunnar SolskjÃ¦r</a> during the <a href="/wiki/1998%E2%80%9399_in_English_football" title="1998â99 in English football">1998â99 season</a> but ended up developing an immensely successful partnership with Yorke. Cole and Yorke contributed 53 goals between them and were rated as one of the most feared attacking partnerships in Europe, with the pair scoring against sides like <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a> away at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nou_Camp" title="Nou Camp">Nou Camp</a>, and repeating the form all season with incredible one touch passes and assists that at times seemed to demonstrate a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telepathic" title="Telepathic">telepathic</a> understanding. Cole played a key role in the side's unique treble of the Premier League title, FA Cup and UEFA Champions League. Cole scored the winning goal in United's final Premier League game of the season against <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a>, a result which meant United finished one point ahead of rivals Arsenal to win the Premier League title. He also scored United's third and winning goal in their <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> semi-final second leg against <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a>, sealing their place in the final for the first time in over 30 years. Also in this season, Cole scored his 100th Premier League goal in a top of the table clash against Arsenal at Old Trafford on 17 February; the match ended 1â1.</p>
<p>During United's pre-season tour of Australia in July 1999, Cole was involved in a horror tackle which left 19-year-old Australian defender <a href="/wiki/Simon_Colosimo" title="Simon Colosimo">Simon Colosimo</a> sidelined for six months and requiring a complete knee reconstruction. Before the injury, Colosimo was one of Australia's best young players and was about to make a big money move to Europe, and was never able to complete a career in Europe, despite a handful of appearances for <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a>.</p>
<p>Cole was United's top scorer again in <a href="/wiki/1999%E2%80%932000_in_English_football" title="1999â2000 in English football">1999â2000</a> with 19 goals in 28 Premier League games. He collected his fourth Premier League title medal in five seasons, and scored over 20 goals in all competitions for the third successive season. Cole scored many goals for United including the only goal of the game in their top of the table clash against their closest rivals <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a>. He also joined an elite group during this season by scoring his 100th goal for the club in a 2â2 draw against <a href="/wiki/Wimbledon_F.C." title="Wimbledon F.C.">Wimbledon</a>.</p>
<p>Another title followed in <a href="/wiki/2000%E2%80%9301_in_English_football" title="2000â01 in English football">2000â01</a> when, despite suffering from an injury that restricted his appearances, Cole scored 13 goals in all competitions, including four in the European Cup allowing him at the time to become Manchester United's record goal scorer in European competition of all time.</p>
<p>The following <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_in_English_football" title="2001â02 in English football">2001â02 season</a> saw Cole face competition from new signing <a href="/wiki/Ruud_van_Nistelrooy" title="Ruud van Nistelrooy">Ruud van Nistelrooy</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Yorke" title="Dwight Yorke">Dwight Yorke</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ole_Gunnar_Solskjaer" title="Ole Gunnar Solskjaer">Ole Gunnar Solskjaer</a> and also <a href="/wiki/Paul_Scholes" title="Paul Scholes">Paul Scholes</a> for places up front, with Ferguson adopting a more conservative approach especially in European games by playing Scholes behind Van Nistelrooy with <a href="/wiki/Roy_Keane" title="Roy Keane">Roy Keane</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juan_Sebastian_Veron" title="Juan Sebastian Veron">Juan Sebastian Veron</a> in a 3-man midfield. Despite this, Cole did manage to score 7 goals before leaving for Blackburn after falling behind to the formidable partnership of Van Nistelrooy and Solskjaer, forcing him into a "super sub" role.</p>
<p>Cole made one last appearance for Manchester United in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Celebration_Match" title="UEFA Celebration Match">UEFA Celebration Match</a> six years later, on 13 March 2007, coming on at half time for a friendly game between Manchester United and a European XI, in celebration of the 50th anniversary of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Community" title="European Community">European Community</a> and 50 years of Manchester United in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">European Cup</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Blackburn_Rovers">Blackburn Rovers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Cole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Blackburn Rovers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The arrival of <a href="/wiki/Ruud_van_Nistelrooy" title="Ruud van Nistelrooy">Ruud van Nistelrooy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Ver%C3%B3n" title="Juan SebastiÃ¡n VerÃ³n">Juan SebastiÃ¡n VerÃ³n</a> counted against Cole's first-team chances in the <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_in_English_football" title="2001â02 in English football">2001â02 season</a>, and on 29 December 2001 Cole was sold to Blackburn Rovers for Â£8Â million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blackburn-deal_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-blackburn-deal-2">[2]</a></sup> Within two months of arriving he had collected a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> winners medal, scoring the winning goal for Blackburn in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup_Final_2002" title="Football League Cup Final 2002">final</a> against Tottenham Hotspur who were managed by the former England manager and open critic of Cole, Glenn Hoddle. This victory meant that, in the space of seven seasons, Cole had won all three domestic trophies plus a European trophy. Cole ended the season with a total of 18 goals in all competitions, 5 for Manchester United and 13 in just 20 games for Blackburn.</p>
<p>Rovers finished sixth the following season and qualified for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a>. That campaign saw Cole reunited with <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Yorke" title="Dwight Yorke">Dwight Yorke</a>, who had signed for Blackburn from Manchester United for Â£2Â million in July 2002.</p>
<p>Cole had a frustrating season in <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_in_English_football" title="2003â04 in English football">2003â04</a> as Rovers slid into the bottom half of the Premier League finishing 15th. Cole netted 11 goals but his relationship with manager Graeme Souness hit rock bottom after Cole reported him to the PFA accusing him of unfair treatment. Cole scored 37 goals in 100 appearances in all competitions for Blackburn<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_career_and_retirement">Later career and retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Cole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Later career and retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Thirteen years after spending a month on loan at Fulham, Cole returned to <a href="/wiki/Craven_Cottage" title="Craven Cottage">Craven Cottage</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_in_English_football" title="2004â05 in English football">2004â05 season</a> joining them on a one-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He was the club's top scorer and scored one of the goals of the season against Liverpool. Despite this successful period at Fulham he decided to leave the club after only one season as his family wanted to return to the <a href="/wiki/North_West_England" title="North West England">North West</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Cole signed for <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a> on a free transfer at the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_in_English_football" title="2005â06 in English football">2005â06 season</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and enjoyed a good start to his career at <a href="/wiki/City_of_Manchester_Stadium" title="City of Manchester Stadium">Eastlands</a>. <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Pearce" title="Stuart Pearce">Stuart Pearce</a>'s side's spent most of the season in the top half of the table but Cole's season was ended by injury in March.</p>

<p>Despite signing a new contract with Manchester City only months earlier<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and leaving Fulham in 2005 to return to the <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">north</a>, Cole signed for south coast club <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a> on transfer deadline day (31 August 2006) for an undisclosed fee, reported as Â£500,000 with the potential to rise to Â£1Â million depending on appearances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He scored his first league goal for his new club in the 2â0 win at home to West Ham on 14 October 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> However, Cole struggled to break into <a href="/wiki/Harry_Redknapp" title="Harry Redknapp">Harry Redknapp</a>'s side and in March 2007 he signed on loan for <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C." title="Birmingham City F.C.">Birmingham City</a> of the Championship until the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Birmingham_sign_Cole_in_loan_deal_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Birmingham_sign_Cole_in_loan_deal-16">[16]</a></sup> Cole returned to Portsmouth after five appearances and one goal (against Wolverhampton Wanderers)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> for Birmingham. He was released on 3 August 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>After being released by Portsmouth at the end of the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_in_English_football" title="2006â07 in English football">2006â07 season</a>, Cole signed a one-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a> on a free transfer, reuniting him once more with former <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers" title="Blackburn Rovers">Blackburn Rovers</a> strike partner <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Yorke" title="Dwight Yorke">Dwight Yorke</a>, and under the management of former United team-mate <a href="/wiki/Roy_Keane" title="Roy Keane">Roy Keane</a>. After seven games for the club he was loaned out to <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a> and was released at the end of the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Premier_League" title="2007â08 Premier League">2007â08 Premier League season</a>.</p>
<p>On 4 July 2008, Cole signed a 12-month deal with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nottingham_Forest" title="Nottingham Forest">Nottingham Forest</a>, his 12th club, and hometown club from childhood. However, on 31 October 2008, Forest confirmed that Cole's contract had been cancelled by mutual consent, after eleven appearances and no goals.</p>
<p>On 11 November 2008, Cole announced his retirement from football, bringing an end to a 19-year career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Cole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Despite first being capped for <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> in 1995, he had earned only 15 <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">caps</a> by the time he announced his retirement from international football after failing to be selected for the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 World Cup</a> squad. He scored one goal for England, in a World Cup qualifying match against <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_football_team" title="Albania national football team">Albania</a> in March 2001. He also scored in his single appearance for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/England_B_national_football_team" title="England B national football team">England B</a> team.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Glenn_Hoddle" title="Glenn Hoddle">Glenn Hoddle</a>, in defence of his decision not to select Cole for the World Cup in 1998, accused Cole of needing six or seven chances to score one goal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Injury just prior to <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2000" title="UEFA Euro 2000">Euro 2000</a> led to Cole missing out on another major competition for his country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Cole earned his first four caps under four different managers. He made his debut against <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_football_team" title="Uruguay national football team">Uruguay</a> under <a href="/wiki/Terry_Venables" title="Terry Venables">Terry Venables</a> in 1995, appeared next against <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> under <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Hoddle" title="Glenn Hoddle">Glenn Hoddle</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tournoi_de_France" title="Tournoi de France">Tournoi de France</a> in 1997, made his third appearance against <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> under caretaker <a href="/wiki/Howard_Wilkinson" title="Howard Wilkinson">Howard Wilkinson</a> in 1999 and finally earned his fourth cap against <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_football_team" title="Poland national football team">Poland</a> under new manager <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Keegan" title="Kevin Keegan">Kevin Keegan</a> in his first starting appearance a few weeks later.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">Coaching career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Cole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Coaching career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In August 2009, Cole was hired by his former <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> and <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> team-mate, <a href="/wiki/Milton_Keynes_Dons_F.C." title="Milton Keynes Dons F.C.">Milton Keynes Dons</a> manager <a href="/wiki/Paul_Ince" title="Paul Ince">Paul Ince</a>, to coach the club's forwards on an initially temporary basis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> However, a week later, Cole agreed to spend at least two days a week working on finishing with the forwards at <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a>, under his former <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a> team-mate <a href="/wiki/Lee_Clark_(footballer)" title="Lee Clark (footballer)">Lee Clark</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In December 2010, Cole was back at Manchester United, working at the Carrington training ground while finishing his coaching badges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Cole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cole's father, Lincoln, emigrated to the UK from <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a> in 1957 and worked as a coal miner in <a href="/wiki/Gedling,_Nottinghamshire" title="Gedling, Nottinghamshire">Gedling, Nottinghamshire</a>, from 1965 to 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Cole married his long-time girlfriend Shirley Dewar in July 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shirley_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shirley-26">[26]</a></sup> Their son, <a href="/wiki/Devante_Cole" title="Devante Cole">Devante</a>, is also a professional football forward; he joined <a href="/wiki/Fleetwood_Town_F.C." title="Fleetwood Town F.C.">Fleetwood Town</a> in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2008, Cole was questioned by police after an alleged assault on his wife in their <a href="/wiki/Alderley_Edge" title="Alderley Edge">Alderley Edge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cheshire" title="Cheshire">Cheshire</a>, home before being released on <a href="/wiki/Bail" title="Bail">bail</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Six months later, Cole, through his representative law firm, <a href="/wiki/Schillings" title="Schillings">Schillings</a>, won <a href="/wiki/Damages" title="Damages">damages</a> in an action against the owners of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daily_Star_(United_Kingdom)" title="Daily Star (United Kingdom)">Daily Star</a>, for <a href="/wiki/Defamation" title="Defamation">defamation</a> regarding the publication of material concerning the assault allegations and for harms caused against his family by sensationalist reports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shirley_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-Shirley-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2016, Cole was named in the <a href="/wiki/Panama_Papers" title="Panama Papers">Panama Papers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Charity_work">Charity work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Cole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Charity work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2000, Cole visited <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a> and returned to set up his own charitable foundation, called the "Andy Cole Children's Foundation", which helped <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AIDS" title="AIDS">AIDS</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orphans" title="Orphans">orphans</a> in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships_with_other_players">Relationships with other players</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Cole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Relationships with other players">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Neil_Ruddock" title="Neil Ruddock">Neil Ruddock</a> considered Cole to be the player he most enjoyed playing against. In a candid interview with <a href="/wiki/Talksport" title="Talksport">Talksport</a>, he jokingly referred to the incident that resulted in Cole suffering two broken legs in 1997 as "not big, and not clever", adding "but it was great", and that "I didn't mean to break both of his legs if I'm honest, I only meant to break one".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010, Cole wrote in his column in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Independent" title="The Independent">The Independent</a></i> that he had "loathed" and "pretty much detested" former Manchester United and England team-mate <a href="/wiki/Teddy_Sheringham" title="Teddy Sheringham">Teddy Sheringham</a> "for 15 years", after Sheringham did not offer to shake Cole's hand as Cole was substituted on for Sheringham to make his England debut in a match against <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_football_team" title="Uruguay national football team">Uruguay</a> at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium_(1923)" title="Wembley Stadium (1923)">Wembley</a> in 1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
