<p><b>Anthony Allen Lerew</b> (born October 28, 1982) is an American professional baseball pitcher who is a free agent. He has played in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB) for the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Braves" title="Atlanta Braves">Atlanta Braves</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Royals" title="Kansas City Royals">Kansas City Royals</a>; in <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Professional_Baseball" title="Nippon Professional Baseball">Nippon Professional Baseball</a> (NPB) for the <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka_SoftBank_Hawks" title="Fukuoka SoftBank Hawks">Fukuoka SoftBank Hawks</a>, and in the <a href="/wiki/KBO_League" title="KBO League">KBO League</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Kia_Tigers" title="Kia Tigers">Kia Tigers</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthony_Lerew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lerew, a graduate of <a href="/wiki/Northern_High_School_(Dillsburg,_Pennsylvania)" title="Northern High School (Dillsburg, Pennsylvania)">Northern York High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dillsburg,_Pennsylvania" title="Dillsburg, Pennsylvania">Dillsburg, Pennsylvania</a>, was drafted by the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Braves" title="Atlanta Braves">Atlanta Braves</a> in the 11th round of the <a href="/wiki/2001_Major_League_Baseball_draft" title="2001 Major League Baseball draft">2001 Major League Baseball draft</a>. After four seasons in the Braves' minor league system, he debuted on September 4, <a href="/wiki/2005_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2005 Major League Baseball season">2005</a>, in a home game against the <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Reds" title="Cincinnati Reds">Cincinnati Reds</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In the 2005 season, Lerew recorded no <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Win_(baseball)" title="Win (baseball)">wins</a>, no <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loss_(baseball)" title="Loss (baseball)">losses</a>, 5 <a href="/wiki/Strikeout" title="Strikeout">strikeouts</a>, and a 5.62 <a href="/wiki/Earned_run_average" title="Earned run average">ERA</a> in 7 games, all of which were <a href="/wiki/Relief_pitcher" title="Relief pitcher">relief</a> outings.</p>
<p>On Feb. 23, <a href="/wiki/2006_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2006 Major League Baseball season">2006</a>, the Braves signed Lerew to a one-year deal. A month later, on Febr. 23, he was optioned to the Triple-A <a href="/wiki/Richmond_Braves" title="Richmond Braves">Richmond Braves</a>. With Richmond, Lerew compiled a 3-5 record with a 7.48 ERA and 69 strikeouts in 16 games, 15 of which he <a href="/wiki/Starting_pitcher" title="Starting pitcher">started</a>.</p>
<p>The Braves called up Lerew from Richmond on September 1, 2006. He made his '06 debut the next day, making a relief appearance in the fifth inning. Lerew pitched 2 innings, allowing 5 <a href="/wiki/Run_(baseball)" title="Run (baseball)">runs</a> and striking out 1 batter. He was sent back down to the Richmond Braves on September 4, having appeared in only 1 game.</p>
<p>On May 8, <a href="/wiki/2007_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2007 Major League Baseball season">2007</a>, Lerew made his first big-league start for Atlanta when he was called up from Richmond yet again, this time to replace <a href="/wiki/Mark_Redman" title="Mark Redman">Mark Redman</a> in a game against the <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Padres" title="San Diego Padres">San Diego Padres</a>. He was later sent down to Richmond again.</p>
<p>On June 20, 2007, Lerew underwent season-ending <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tommy_John_surgery" title="Tommy John surgery">Tommy John surgery</a> and was placed on the 60-day <a href="/wiki/Disabled_list" title="Disabled list">disabled list</a>. He recovered from surgery in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_Florida" title="Southern Florida">Southern Florida</a>, at the Braves extended <a href="/wiki/Spring_training" title="Spring training">spring training</a> site. Once recovered, he spent the rest of <a href="/wiki/2008_in_baseball" title="2008 in baseball">2008</a> pitching for the Gulf Coast Braves and with Triple-A Richmond.</p>
<p>On March 5, <a href="/wiki/2009_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2009 Major League Baseball season">2009</a>, Lerew was outrighted to Triple-A Gwinnett to make room on the roster for <a href="/wiki/Tom_Glavine" title="Tom Glavine">Tom Glavine</a>, and was released 5 days later. On March 18, Lerew signed with the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Royals" title="Kansas City Royals">Kansas City Royals</a>.</p>
<p>Lerew spent the 2009 minor league season with the Double-A <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Arkansas_Naturals" title="Northwest Arkansas Naturals">Northwest Arkansas Naturals</a>. He was called up to the Royals as a September callup, making his Royals debut on September 24, 2009. He re-signed with them in February.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On June 16, 2010, Lerew was called up from the Triple-A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Omaha_Royals" title="Omaha Royals">Omaha Royals</a> to the Kansas City Royals to replace <a href="/wiki/Luke_Hochevar" title="Luke Hochevar">Luke Hochevar</a>, who went on the DL with a sprained elbow. He earned his first career major league win on June 28 of that year over the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_White_Sox" title="Chicago White Sox">Chicago White Sox</a>.</p>
<p>On Jan 16, 2012, Lerew signed a bonus of $50,000 with <a href="/wiki/Kia_Tigers" title="Kia Tigers">Kia Tigers</a> of the Korea Baseball Organization with <a href="/wiki/Alex_Graman" title="Alex Graman">Alex Graman</a>. As a starter, he went 11-13 in his debut season with 94 strikeouts and an ERA of 3.83. He was resigned with Kia Tigers for 2013 season, but was released on July,2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 11, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/York_Revolution" title="York Revolution">York Revolution</a> signed Lerew.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lerew signed a minor league deal with the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Angels_of_Anaheim" title="Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim">Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim</a> on May 20, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lerew signed with the <a href="/wiki/York_Revolution" title="York Revolution">York Revolution</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_League_of_Professional_Baseball" title="Atlantic League of Professional Baseball">Atlantic League of Professional Baseball</a> for the 2015 season. He became a free agent after the 2015 season.</p>
