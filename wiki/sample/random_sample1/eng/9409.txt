<p><b>Anthony Ricketts</b> (born 12 March 1979 in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a>) is a former professional <a href="/wiki/Squash_(sport)" title="Squash (sport)">squash</a> player from <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.</p>
<p>Ricketts won the <a href="/wiki/British_Open_Squash_Championships" title="British Open Squash Championships">British Open</a> in 2005, beating <a href="/wiki/James_Willstrop" title="James Willstrop">James Willstrop</a> in the final 11-7, 11-9, 11-7. He also won the 2005 <a href="/wiki/Tournament_of_Champions_(squash)" title="Tournament of Champions (squash)">Tournament of Champions</a> title, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PSA_Super_Series" title="PSA Super Series">Super Series</a> Finals in 2006.</p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2002_Commonwealth_Games" title="2002 Commonwealth Games">2002 Commonwealth Games</a>, Ricketts won a Silver Medal in the men's doubles partnering <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Boswell" title="Stewart Boswell">Stewart Boswell</a>. Ricketts and Boswell again won a men's doubles Silver Medal at the 2006 Commonwealth Games. The pair won the men's doubles title at the 2006 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Doubles_Squash_Championships" title="World Doubles Squash Championships">World Doubles Squash Championships</a>.</p>
<p>Ricketts was a member of the Australian team which won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Team_Squash_Championships" title="World Team Squash Championships">World Team Squash Championships</a> in 2003.</p>
<p>Following a knee injury, Ricketts announced his retirement from squash in December 2007.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthony_Ricketts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After a hugely successful 2002 – in which he leapt from 18 to 7 in the Dunlop PSA World Rankings over the year – Anthony Ricketts consolidated his success the following year by rising to World No. 6 in October 2003.</p>
<p>But a knee injury following his appearance in the Apawamis Open in January 2004 caused the Australian to return to Sydney from his UK base in Reading for treatment. He returned to the PSA Tour seven months later at the English Open in Sheffield – and just over a year later clinched the British Open title for the first time and rose to a career-high world No. 3.</p>
<p>Ricketts first came to international notice in the 2000 Hong Kong Open when he came through the qualifiers to reach his first Super Series event semi-finals. Later in the month, he beat top seed Paul Price in the final of the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Open_(squash)" title="Australian Open (squash)">Australian Open</a> to claim his domestic Open title for the first time.</p>
<p>It was in February 2005 in New York, where seeded ten in the <a href="/wiki/Tournament_of_Champions_(squash)" title="Tournament of Champions (squash)">Tournament of Champions</a>, he beat third seed <a href="/wiki/Peter_Nicol" title="Peter Nicol">Peter Nicol</a>, then <a href="/wiki/Amr_Shabana" title="Amr Shabana">Amr Shabana</a> in the semi-finals, before defeating world champion and World No 1 <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Lincou" title="Thierry Lincou">Thierry Lincou</a> in the final. "This is a big moment for me, I've been waiting for this for a long time," said the Ricketts after beating the Frenchman 11-10 7-11 11-9 6-11 11-7 in 89 minutes to take the what was then the biggest title of his career.</p>
<p>But better was yet to come. In the 2005 British Open in Manchester, the sixth-seeded Ricketts beat Peter Nicol in the semi-finals, then another Englishman <a href="/wiki/James_Willstrop" title="James Willstrop">James Willstrop</a> in straight games in the final to add his name to those already on this prestigious trophy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The success took Ricketts to No. 3 in the world rankings – making him the top-ranked Australian for the first time – and he maintained his momentum through to November's Qatar Classic where he reached the semi-finals as seventh seed. In December, Ricketts beat both Peter Nicol and top seed Thierry Lincou en route to the final of the maiden Saudi International to celebrate his fourth PSA final appearance of the year.</p>
<p>The UK-based Aussie's success continued into 2006, when he reached the final of the Canary Wharf Classic in London in February, then gained the silver medal (with <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Boswell" title="Stewart Boswell">Stewart Boswell</a>) in the Commonwealth Games doubles in Melbourne.</p>
<p>In his third appearance in the Super Series Finals in May, Ricketts recovered from losing the first qualifying round match against Lee Beachill to beat the Englishman in the final to win the title for the first time.</p>
<p>Following a knee injury, Ricketts announced his retirement from squash in December 2007.</p>
<p>He is now the Senior Coach at the Australian Institute of Sport Squash Unit in Brisbane, Australia.</p>
