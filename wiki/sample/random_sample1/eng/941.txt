<p><b>ESPNU</b> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Digital_cable" title="Digital cable">digital cable</a> and <a href="/wiki/Satellite_television" title="Satellite television">satellite</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_sports_television_channels" title="List of sports television channels">sports television channel</a> that is owned by <a href="/wiki/ESPN_Inc." title="ESPN Inc.">ESPN Inc.</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Joint_venture" title="Joint venture">joint venture</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Disney%E2%80%93ABC_Television_Group" title="Disney–ABC Television Group">Disney–ABC Television Group</a> division of <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">The Walt Disney Company</a> (which owns a controlling 80% stake) and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hearst_Corporation" title="Hearst Corporation">Hearst Corporation</a> (which owns the remaining 20%). The channel is primarily dedicated to coverage of <a href="/wiki/College_athletics" title="College athletics">college athletics</a>, and is also used as an additional outlet for general ESPN programming. ESPNU is based alongside <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ESPN_Plus" title="ESPN Plus">ESPN Plus</a> at its headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Charlotte,_North_Carolina" title="Charlotte, North Carolina">Charlotte, North Carolina</a>.</p>
<p>As of February 2015, ESPNU is available to approximately 73,594,000 pay television households (63.2% of households with at least one television set) in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ESPNU&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The network was launched on March 4, 2005, with its first broadcast originating from the site of <a href="/wiki/Gallagher-Iba_Arena" title="Gallagher-Iba Arena">Gallagher-Iba Arena</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oklahoma_State_University_-_Stillwater" title="Oklahoma State University - Stillwater">Oklahoma State University</a> campus in <a href="/wiki/Stillwater,_Oklahoma" title="Stillwater, Oklahoma">Stillwater, Oklahoma</a>. The network's first live event was a semifinal game of the <a href="/wiki/Ohio_Valley_Conference" title="Ohio Valley Conference">Ohio Valley Conference</a> men's basketball tournament between <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Missouri_State_University" title="Southeast Missouri State University">Southeast Missouri State University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Kentucky_University" title="Eastern Kentucky University">Eastern Kentucky University</a>. The network was launched as a response to rival College Sports Television (CSTV) (now <a href="/wiki/CBS_Sports_Network" title="CBS Sports Network">CBS Sports Network</a>). ESPN was also being investigated by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Justice_Department" title="United States Justice Department">U.S. Justice Department</a> on allegations of "warehousing" collegiate sporting events from certain conferences, or signing a deal with a conference for all their games, but only televising a small number and not allowing the conference to make other arrangements for television broadcasts.</p>
<p>ESPN and XOS Technologies entered into a partnership for college athletics websites to compete directly with CSTV's growing internet presence. On August 28, 2006, ESPNU launched a new <i><a href="/wiki/SportsCenter" title="SportsCenter">SportsCenter</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Spin-off_(media)" title="Spin-off (media)">spin-off</a> focusing entirely on college sports. The program, <i><a href="/wiki/SportsCenterU" title="SportsCenterU">SportsCenterU</a></i>, was originally scheduled to be broadcast from ESPN's headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Bristol,_Connecticut" title="Bristol, Connecticut">Bristol, Connecticut</a>, however ESPN instead chose to originate the show from Charlotte. <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hall_(sportscaster)" title="Mike Hall (sportscaster)">Mike Hall</a> was the program's lead anchor until August 2007, when he left for the new <a href="/wiki/Big_Ten_Network" title="Big Ten Network">Big Ten Network</a>. He was replaced by Mike Gleason and Lowell Galindo. The two are joined on-set by <a href="/wiki/Color_commentator" title="Color commentator">color commentators</a> that vary depending on the sports season.</p>
<p>The same day as <i>SportsCenterU</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s debut, ESPNU launched the website ESPNU.com. The site included live streaming of college sports events, a multi-media player dedicated to college sports, podcasts and ESPN Motion clips of studio programming from the ESPNU television network.</p>
<p>ESPNU expanded its live programming to <a href="/wiki/Water_polo" title="Water polo">water polo</a> by broadcasting its first-ever water polo match between the women's teams of <a href="/wiki/Princeton_University" title="Princeton University">Princeton University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bucknell_University" title="Bucknell University">Bucknell University</a> on March 28, 2009, from DeNunzio Pool in <a href="/wiki/Princeton,_New_Jersey" title="Princeton, New Jersey">Princeton, New Jersey</a>.</p>
<p>In addition to its collegiate sports coverage, ESPNU has simulcast <a href="/wiki/ESPN_Radio" title="ESPN Radio">ESPN Radio</a>'s midday program over its airwaves since 2008, with the exception of a brief period between 2011 and 2012. The program airing for the majority of that time was <i><a href="/wiki/The_Herd_with_Colin_Cowherd" title="The Herd with Colin Cowherd">The Herd with Colin Cowherd</a></i>, which has since moved to <a href="/wiki/Fox_Sports_Radio" title="Fox Sports Radio">Fox Sports Radio</a> and is simulcast on <a href="/wiki/Fox_Sports_1" title="Fox Sports 1">Fox Sports 1</a>. Following Cowherd's departure and several weeks of guest hosts taking over the timeslot, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dan_Le_Batard_Show_with_Stugotz" title="The Dan Le Batard Show with Stugotz">The Dan Le Batard Show with Stugotz</a></i> became the permanent replacement for <i>The Herd</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Carriage">Carriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ESPNU&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Carriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On May 19, 2009, ESPN announced it had reached a carriage agreement with <a href="/wiki/Comcast" title="Comcast">Comcast</a>, which allows the cable provider to carry ESPNU on a widely distributed digital cable tier, instead of a less popular sports tier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> ESPNU was added to most Comcast systems in time for the start of the 2009–10 <a href="/wiki/College_football" title="College football">college football</a> season. This ended several years of negotiations and somewhat of a feud between Comcast and ESPN over carriage of ESPNU.</p>
<p>On that same date, ESPNU reached a new carriage agreement with <a href="/wiki/DirecTV" title="DirecTV">DirecTV</a>, which moved the channel from the satellite provider's add-on "Sports Pack" to its basic "Choice" package on July 1, 2009, swapping channels and packages with ESPN Classic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-directv_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-directv-3">[3]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cablevision" title="Cablevision">Cablevision</a> added ESPNU to its systems on March 23, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cablevision_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-cablevision-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the month of November 2015, both ESPNU and <a href="/wiki/SEC_Network" title="SEC Network">SEC Network</a> were available on the Brazilian version of <a href="/wiki/WatchESPN" title="WatchESPN">WatchESPN</a>, carrying their live feeds alongside the local channels as a trial to test the market's reception of American college games. ESPN announced that the trial was a success and, although the channels are no longer available to Brazilians, an especific product for this public would be created soon.</p>
<p>ESPNU is carried on <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Vue" title="PlayStation Vue">PlayStation Vue</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Sling_TV" title="Sling TV">Sling TV</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dish_Network_lawsuit">Dish Network lawsuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ESPNU&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Dish Network lawsuit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On August 4, 2009 <a href="/wiki/Dish_Network" title="Dish Network">Dish Network</a> sued ESPN for $1 million in a federal lawsuit, alleging that ESPN breached its contract by not extending the same carriage terms that the programmer provided to Comcast and DirecTV for ESPNU and ESPN Classic. The lawsuit claims ESPN violated the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Most_Favored_Nations" title="Most Favored Nations">Most Favored Nations</a>" clause.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The next day, ESPN announced it would fight the lawsuit and said in a press release: "We have repeatedly advised Dish that we are in full compliance with our agreement and have offered them a distribution opportunity with respect to ESPNU and ESPN Classic consistent with the rest of the industry. We will not renegotiate settled contracts and will vigorously defend this legal action, the apparent sole purpose of which is to get a better deal."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dish Network moved the channel from its "Classic Gold 250" package to its "Classic Bronze 100" package on September 30, 2009. However, it claimed that the move had nothing to do with the lawsuit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
