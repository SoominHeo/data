<p><b>Anjelica Huston</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/Ë/ primary stress follows">Ë</span><span title="'h' in 'hi'">h</span><span title="/juË/ long 'u' in 'cute'">juË</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/Én/ 'on' in 'button'">Én</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born July 8, 1951) is an American actress, director and former fashion model. Huston became the third generation of her family to win an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a>, when she won <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress">Best Supporting Actress</a> for her performance in 1985's <i><a href="/wiki/Prizzi%27s_Honor" title="Prizzi's Honor">Prizzi's Honor</a></i>, joining her father, director <a href="/wiki/John_Huston" title="John Huston">John Huston</a>, and grandfather, actor <a href="/wiki/Walter_Huston" title="Walter Huston">Walter Huston</a>. She also received Academy Award nominations for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enemies,_a_Love_Story_(film)" title="Enemies, a Love Story (film)">Enemies, a Love Story</a></i> (1989) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Grifters_(film)" title="The Grifters (film)">The Grifters</a></i> (1990).</p>
<p>Huston received <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_Film_Awards" title="British Academy Film Awards">British Academy Award</a> nominations for her work in the <a href="/wiki/Woody_Allen" title="Woody Allen">Woody Allen</a> films <i><a href="/wiki/Crimes_and_Misdemeanors" title="Crimes and Misdemeanors">Crimes and Misdemeanors</a></i> (1989) and <i><a href="/wiki/Manhattan_Murder_Mystery" title="Manhattan Murder Mystery">Manhattan Murder Mystery</a></i> (1993). Among her other roles, she starred as <a href="/wiki/Morticia_Addams" title="Morticia Addams">Morticia Addams</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Addams_Family_(film)" title="The Addams Family (film)">The Addams Family</a></i> (1991) and <i><a href="/wiki/Addams_Family_Values" title="Addams Family Values">Addams Family Values</a></i> (1993), receiving Golden Globe nominations for both, and played the Grand High Witch in the children's movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Witches_(1990_film)" title="The Witches (1990 film)">The Witches</a></i> (1990). She has frequently collaborated with director <a href="/wiki/Wes_Anderson" title="Wes Anderson">Wes Anderson</a>, including <i><a href="/wiki/The_Royal_Tenenbaums" title="The Royal Tenenbaums">The Royal Tenenbaums</a></i> (2001) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Life_Aquatic_with_Steve_Zissou" title="The Life Aquatic with Steve Zissou">The Life Aquatic with Steve Zissou</a></i> (2004). She is also the author of two memoirs; <i>A Story Lately Told</i> and <i>Watch Me</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anjelica_Huston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Anjelica Huston was born in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Monica,_California" title="Santa Monica, California">Santa Monica, California</a>, and is the daughter of director and actor <a href="/wiki/John_Huston" title="John Huston">John Huston</a> and ItalianâAmerican prima ballerina and model <a href="/wiki/Enrica_Soma" title="Enrica Soma">Enrica Soma</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Huston's paternal grandfather was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian" title="Canadian">Canadian</a>-born actor <a href="/wiki/Walter_Huston" title="Walter Huston">Walter Huston</a>. Huston has Scots-Irish, English and Welsh ancestry from her father.</p>
<p>She spent much of her childhood in Ireland, particularly near <a href="/wiki/Craughwell" title="Craughwell">Craughwell</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_Galway" title="County Galway">County Galway</a>, and England, where she attended <a href="/wiki/Holland_Park_School" title="Holland Park School">Holland Park School</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In the late 1960s, she began taking a few small roles in her father's movies.</p>
<p>She started very small, substituting her hands for <a href="/wiki/Deborah_Kerr" title="Deborah Kerr">Deborah Kerr</a>'s in the British <i><a href="/wiki/Casino_Royale_(1967_film)" title="Casino Royale (1967 film)">Casino Royale</a></i> and advanced to bigger roles in 1969, starring, for example, in <i><a href="/wiki/A_Walk_with_Love_and_Death" title="A Walk with Love and Death">A Walk with Love and Death</a></i>, where she played the 16-year-old French noblewoman Claudia. In the same year, her mother, who was 39 years old, died in a car accident, and Anjelica relocated to the U.S., where she modeled for several years. While she modeled, she worked with photographers such as <a href="/wiki/Richard_Avedon" title="Richard Avedon">Richard Avedon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bob_Richardson_(photographer)" title="Bob Richardson (photographer)">Bob Richardson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Huston has an older brother, Tony, a younger maternal half-sister named <a href="/wiki/Allegra_Huston" title="Allegra Huston">Allegra</a>, whom she called "Legs", a younger paternal half-brother, actor <a href="/wiki/Danny_Huston" title="Danny Huston">Danny Huston</a>, and an adopted older brother, Pablo. She is the aunt of <i><a href="/wiki/Boardwalk_Empire" title="Boardwalk Empire">Boardwalk Empire</a></i> actor <a href="/wiki/Jack_Huston" title="Jack Huston">Jack Huston</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anjelica_Huston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anjelica_Huston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Deciding to focus more on movies, in the early 1980s she studied acting. Her first notable role was in <a href="/wiki/Bob_Rafelson" title="Bob Rafelson">Bob Rafelson</a>'s remake of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Postman_Always_Rings_Twice_(1981_film)" title="The Postman Always Rings Twice (1981 film)">The Postman Always Rings Twice</a></i> (1981). Later, her father cast her as Maerose, daughter of a Mafia don whose love is scorned by a hit man (Jack Nicholson) in the film adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Richard_Condon" title="Richard Condon">Richard Condon</a>'s Mafia-satire novel <i><a href="/wiki/Prizzi%27s_Honor" title="Prizzi's Honor">Prizzi's Honor</a></i> (1985). Huston won the Best Supporting Actress Oscar for her performance, making her the first person in Academy Award history to win an Oscar when a parent and a grandparent had also won one.</p>
<p>Huston earned a Best Actress Oscar nomination for her portrayal of a con artist in <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Frears" title="Stephen Frears">Stephen Frears</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/The_Grifters_(film)" title="The Grifters (film)">The Grifters</a></i> (1990). She also starred as the lead in her father's final directorial film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dead_(1987_film)" title="The Dead (1987 film)">The Dead</a></i> (1987), an adaptation of a <a href="/wiki/James_Joyce" title="James Joyce">James Joyce</a> story.</p>
<p>She was then cast as Morticia Addams, in the hugely successful 1991 movie adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Addams_Family_(film)" title="The Addams Family (film)">The Addams Family</a>.</i> In 1993, she reprised the role for the sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Addams_Family_Values" title="Addams Family Values">Addams Family Values</a></i>. Anjelica also starred in the 1998 Hollywood blockbuster, <i><a href="/wiki/Ever_After" title="Ever After">Ever After: A Cinderella Story</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Drew_Barrymore" title="Drew Barrymore">Drew Barrymore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Melanie_Lynskey" title="Melanie Lynskey">Melanie Lynskey</a> as the Baroness Rodmilla De Ghent. She starred in two <a href="/wiki/Wes_Anderson" title="Wes Anderson">Wes Anderson</a> films, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Royal_Tenenbaums" title="The Royal Tenenbaums">The Royal Tenenbaums</a></i> (2001) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Life_Aquatic_with_Steve_Zissou" title="The Life Aquatic with Steve Zissou">The Life Aquatic with Steve Zissou</a></i> (2004), as well as appearing in a minor role in 2007's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Darjeeling_Limited" title="The Darjeeling Limited">The Darjeeling Limited</a></i>. She voiced the role of Queen Clarion in the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Fairies" title="Disney Fairies">Disney Fairies</a> film series starring <a href="/wiki/Tinker_Bell" title="Tinker Bell">Tinker Bell</a>. On January 22, 2010, Huston was honored on the Hollywood Walk of Fame. In 2011, Huston was in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Horrid_Henry:_The_Movie" title="Horrid Henry: The Movie">Horrid Henry: The Movie</a></i>. Huston later appeared on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Broadcasting_Company" title="National Broadcasting Company">NBC</a> television series, <i><a href="/wiki/Smash_(TV_series)" title="Smash (TV series)">Smash</a></i>, portraying Broadway producer Eileen Rand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2015 and 2016 Huston appeared in the second and third seasons of the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Video" title="Amazon Video">Amazon Video</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Transparent_(TV_series)" title="Transparent (TV series)">Transparent</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Directing_career">Directing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anjelica_Huston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Directing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Huston has recently expanded her horizons, following in her father's footsteps in the director's chair. Her first directorial credit was <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bastard_Out_of_Carolina_(film)" title="Bastard Out of Carolina (film)">Bastard Out of Carolina</a></i> (1996), followed by <i><a href="/wiki/Agnes_Browne" title="Agnes Browne">Agnes Browne</a></i> (1999), in which she both directed and starred, and then <i><a href="/wiki/Riding_the_Bus_with_My_Sister" title="Riding the Bus with My Sister">Riding the Bus with My Sister</a></i> (2005).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_activism">Political activism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anjelica_Huston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Political activism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>In November 2007, Huston led a letter campaign organized by the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Campaign_for_Burma" title="U.S. Campaign for Burma">U.S. Campaign for Burma</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Human_Rights_Action_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Human Rights Action Center (page does not exist)">Human Rights Action Center</a>. The letter, signed by over twenty five high-profile individuals from the entertainment business, was addressed to the United Nations Secretary General <a href="/wiki/Ban_Ki-moon" title="Ban Ki-moon">Ban Ki-moon</a> and urged him to "personally intervene" to secure the release of <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Peace_Prize" title="Nobel Peace Prize">Nobel Peace Prize</a> recipient <a href="/wiki/Aung_San_Suu_Kyi" title="Aung San Suu Kyi">Aung San Suu Kyi</a> of Burma.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2012, Huston recorded a public service announcement for <a href="/wiki/People_for_the_Ethical_Treatment_of_Animals" title="People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals">PETA</a>, urging her colleagues in Hollywood to refrain from using <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_apes" title="Great apes">great apes</a> in television, movies and advertisements.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Later, the animal rights organization named her their <i>Person of the Year 2012</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anjelica_Huston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1969, at age 18, Huston began dating photographer <a href="/wiki/Bob_Richardson_(photographer)" title="Bob Richardson (photographer)">Bob Richardson</a>, who was 23 years her senior. Their relationship lasted almost four years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-9">[9]</a></sup> She met <a href="/wiki/Jack_Nicholson" title="Jack Nicholson">Jack Nicholson</a> in 1973 and they lived together, on and off,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CharlieRose-StoryLatelyTold-2013_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-CharlieRose-StoryLatelyTold-2013-10">[10]</a></sup> from that year until 1990, when the media reported he had fathered a child with <a href="/wiki/Rebecca_Broussard" title="Rebecca Broussard">Rebecca Broussard</a>. She was briefly involved in a relationship with <a href="/wiki/Ryan_O%27Neal" title="Ryan O'Neal">Ryan O'Neal</a> during one of her separations from Nicholson in the late 1970s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 23, 1992, Huston married sculptor <a href="/wiki/Robert_Graham_(sculptor)" title="Robert Graham (sculptor)">Robert Graham</a>. The couple lived in a five-story house designed by Graham at 69 Windward Avenue in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Venice,_California" title="Venice, California">Venice, California</a>, until his death on December 27, 2008. They did not have any children.</p>
<p>Huston's home went on the market for $18 million in 2010, but initially failed to sell. In September 2012, the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Post" title="New York Post">New York Post</a></i> reported that Huston was planning to transform her house into a private social club;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> the actress was said to have accepted $12 million for the property and to serve on the advisory board for a new private club to be based there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In April 2014, Huston sold the house for $11.15 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Huston was close friends with actor <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Peck" title="Gregory Peck">Gregory Peck</a>, whom <a href="/wiki/John_Huston" title="John Huston">her father</a> directed in <i><a href="/wiki/Moby_Dick_(1956_film)" title="Moby Dick (1956 film)">Moby Dick</a></i> (1956). The two of them first met on the set of the film when she was four years old as Peck was in costume as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Captain_Ahab_(Moby-Dick)" title="Captain Ahab (Moby-Dick)">Captain Ahab</a>. Decades later, after her father's death, Huston reunited with Peck and maintained a friendship that lasted until his death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Huston wrote her memoirs as one 900-page book; she split it into two books at her publisher's urging.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
