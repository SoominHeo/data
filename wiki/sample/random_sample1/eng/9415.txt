<p><b>Angela Michelle</b> "<b>Angie</b>" <b>Harmon</b> (born August 10, 1972) is an American actress and model. She became a well-known model before gaining international fame for her roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Baywatch_Nights" title="Baywatch Nights">Baywatch Nights</a></i> and as New York A.D.A. <a href="/wiki/Abbie_Carmichael" title="Abbie Carmichael">Abbie Carmichael</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order" title="Law &amp; Order">Law &amp; Order</a></i>. She also starred as Detective <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jane_Rizzoli" title="Jane Rizzoli">Jane Rizzoli</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TNT_(TV_network)" title="TNT (TV network)">TNT</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Rizzoli_%26_Isles" title="Rizzoli &amp; Isles">Rizzoli &amp; Isles</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angie_Harmon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Harmon was born in <a href="/wiki/Highland_Park,_Texas" title="Highland Park, Texas">Highland Park, Texas</a>, the daughter of Daphne Demar (<i>née</i> Caravageli) and Lawrence Paul "Larry" Harmon, a hospital information network executive in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dallas,_Texas" title="Dallas, Texas">Dallas, Texas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmref_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-filmref-2">[2]</a></sup> Her father has <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a> and <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a> ancestry,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> while her mother is of <a href="/wiki/Greeks" title="Greeks">Greek</a> descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Who_Do_You_Think_You_Are%3F_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Who Do You Think You Are? (U.S. TV series)">Who Do You Think You Are?</a></i>, Harmon found that she had a German ancestor who served in <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a>'s army during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Revolutionary_War" title="Revolutionary War">Revolutionary War</a>, and that ancestor later acquired land in <a href="/wiki/Mercer_County,_Kentucky" title="Mercer County, Kentucky">Mercer County, Kentucky</a>. The land is still owned by a distant branch of her family.</p>
<p>Harmon worked as a <a href="/wiki/Child_model" title="Child model">child model</a>, and in 1987 won a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seventeen_(magazine)" title="Seventeen (magazine)">Seventeen</a></i> modeling contest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She attended <a href="/wiki/Highland_Park_High_School_(University_Park,_Texas)" title="Highland Park High School (University Park, Texas)">Highland Park High School</a> until 1990, where she was a member of the Highland Belles. She won a Spectrum Model Search contest shortly afterward, subsequently pursued a successful modeling career, and became well known in the early 1990s. She worked as a runway model for <a href="/wiki/Calvin_Klein" title="Calvin Klein">Calvin Klein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giorgio_Armani" title="Giorgio Armani">Giorgio Armani</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Donna_Karan" title="Donna Karan">Donna Karan</a> and appeared on the covers of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ELLE" title="ELLE">ELLE</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cosmopolitan_(magazine)" title="Cosmopolitan (magazine)">Cosmopolitan</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Esquire_(magazine)" title="Esquire (magazine)">Esquire</a></i>. She is signed with <a href="/wiki/IMG_Models" title="IMG Models">IMG Models</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angie_Harmon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Harmon began acting in 1995 after being discovered on a plane by <a href="/wiki/David_Hasselhoff" title="David Hasselhoff">David Hasselhoff</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> She then starred in several television series, including <i><a href="/wiki/Baywatch_Nights" title="Baywatch Nights">Baywatch Nights</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Baywatch" title="Baywatch">Baywatch</a></i> and <i>C-16: FBI</i>. She also appeared in the 1998 film, <i><a href="/wiki/Lawn_Dogs" title="Lawn Dogs">Lawn Dogs</a></i>, which received only a limited theatrical release. In the late 1990s, Harmon became better known by joining the long-running <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order" title="Law &amp; Order">Law &amp; Order</a></i>, playing ADA <a href="/wiki/Abbie_Carmichael" title="Abbie Carmichael">Abbie Carmichael</a> from 1998 to 2001. During this time, she also voiced <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Gordon" title="Barbara Gordon">Barbara Gordon</a> in the animated film <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_Beyond:_Return_of_the_Joker" title="Batman Beyond: Return of the Joker">Batman Beyond: Return of the Joker</a></i>, succeeding <a href="/wiki/Stockard_Channing" title="Stockard Channing">Stockard Channing</a> in that role.</p>
<p>Harmon left <i>Law &amp; Order</i> to concentrate on her film career, saying that she preferred working in film to television.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tv_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-tv-7">[7]</a></sup> After appearing in the 2001 <a href="/wiki/Direct-to-video" title="Direct-to-video">direct-to-video</a> film <i>Good Advice</i>, she had a supporting role in <i><a href="/wiki/Agent_Cody_Banks" title="Agent Cody Banks">Agent Cody Banks</a></i> (2003), playing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CIA" title="CIA">CIA</a> handler of a teenage agent (<a href="/wiki/Frankie_Muniz" title="Frankie Muniz">Frankie Muniz</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2006, Harmon co-starred with <a href="/wiki/Cuba_Gooding_Jr." title="Cuba Gooding Jr.">Cuba Gooding Jr.</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Woods" title="James Woods">James Woods</a> in the direct-to-DVD political suspense drama <i><a href="/wiki/End_Game_(2006_film)" title="End Game (2006 film)">End Game</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2006, Harmon starred in an ABC <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pilot_episode" title="Pilot episode">pilot</a> <i>Secrets of a Small Town</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> which did not go to series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> She also starred in another failed television show on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Inconceivable" title="Inconceivable">Inconceivable</a></i>, which was canceled after two episodes. The following year, she starred in another ABC pilot, <i><a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Murder_Club_(TV_series)" title="Women's Murder Club (TV series)">Women's Murder Club</a></i>. The police drama was picked up, but only ten of the thirteen episodes aired.</p>
<p>Harmon was one of five actresses who posed discreetly nude for the May 2008 issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Allure_(magazine)" title="Allure (magazine)">Allure</a></i> magazine, alongside <a href="/wiki/Gabrielle_Union" title="Gabrielle Union">Gabrielle Union</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zoe_McLellan" title="Zoe McLellan">Zoe McLellan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jill_Scott" title="Jill Scott">Jill Scott</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ana_Ortiz" title="Ana Ortiz">Ana Ortiz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010 Harmon hosted an <a href="/wiki/Infomercial" title="Infomercial">infomercial</a> for "UpLiv", a stress management program and also appeared in an infomercial for Olay "Pro-X" wrinkle cream.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 2010 to 2016, she co-starred with <a href="/wiki/Sasha_Alexander" title="Sasha Alexander">Sasha Alexander</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TNT_(TV_network)" title="TNT (TV network)">TNT</a> crime-drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Rizzoli_%26_Isles" title="Rizzoli &amp; Isles">Rizzoli &amp; Isles</a></i>, playing <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> police detective Jane Rizzoli. The show premiered July 12, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> She directed the series' 100th episode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Harmon appears in the music video for <a href="/wiki/Craig_Morgan" title="Craig Morgan">Craig Morgan</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/This_Ole_Boy_(song)" title="This Ole Boy (song)">This Ole Boy</a>", released in January 2012.</p>
<p>In July 2012, during promotions for a new season of <i>Rizzoli and Isles</i>, Harmon revealed on <a href="/wiki/Conan_O%27Brien" title="Conan O'Brien">Conan O'Brien</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Conan_(talk_show)" title="Conan (talk show)">late night series</a> that she was a fan of and very interested in playing a movie version of the <a href="/wiki/She-Hulk" title="She-Hulk">She-Hulk</a> from <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a> should such a movie become available.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Harmon won Outstanding Female Actor in a Leading Role in a Drama Series for <i>Rizzoli &amp; Isles</i> at the 2012 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gracie_Allen_Awards" title="Gracie Allen Awards">Gracie Allen Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angie_Harmon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On March 13, 2000, Harmon became engaged to former <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">NFL</a> player <a href="/wiki/Jason_Sehorn" title="Jason Sehorn">Jason Sehorn</a>. She was appearing as a guest on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_with_Jay_Leno" title="The Tonight Show with Jay Leno">The Tonight Show with Jay Leno</a></i> when Leno called Sehorn out as a surprise guest. Sehorn immediately walked up to her, knelt down on one knee and proposed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hollywood.com_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hollywood.com-18">[18]</a></sup> They were married on June 9, 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> They have three daughters:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> born October 2003,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> born June 2005, and born December 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people12182008_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-people12182008-22">[22]</a></sup> In November 2014, Harmon and Sehorn announced their separation after 13 years of marriage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Harmon is a <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a>. She and Sehorn supported President <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> and delivered a speech at the <a href="/wiki/2004_Republican_National_Convention" title="2004 Republican National Convention">2004 Republican National Convention</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> She supported <a href="/wiki/John_McCain" title="John McCain">John McCain</a> for president in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Harmon is a devout <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a> and has stressed the importance of <a href="/wiki/Prayer" title="Prayer">prayer</a> in her daily life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
