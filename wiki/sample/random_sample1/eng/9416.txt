<p><b>Antoine Fuqua</b> (born January 19, 1966) is an American <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">film director</a> and <a href="/wiki/Film_producer" title="Film producer">producer</a>. Initially active as a <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> director, he has worked primarily in the <a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thriller_films" title="Thriller films">thriller film</a> subgenres, and is best known for his 2001 <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Actor" title="Academy Award for Best Actor">Academy Award-winning</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Training_Day" title="Training Day">Training Day</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antoine_Fuqua&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fuqua was born in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, the son of Carlos and Mary Fuqua. He is the nephew of record producer and executive <a href="/wiki/Harvey_Fuqua" title="Harvey Fuqua">Harvey Fuqua</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Moonglows" title="The Moonglows">The Moonglows</a>. After going to school for electrical engineering, with the hope of going on to fly jets in the military,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Fuqua began his career directing music videos for popular artists like <a href="/wiki/Toni_Braxton" title="Toni Braxton">Toni Braxton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coolio" title="Coolio">Coolio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Wonder" title="Stevie Wonder">Stevie Wonder</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Prince_(musician)" title="Prince (musician)">Prince</a>. From 1998 onwards, he began directing feature films, although he has worked on a few music videos since then.</p>
<p>His first feature film was the <a href="/wiki/John_Woo" title="John Woo">John Woo</a>-produced action film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Replacement_Killers" title="The Replacement Killers">The Replacement Killers</a></i> (1998), starring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chow_Yun_Fat" title="Chow Yun Fat">Chow Yun Fat</a>. He then directed the crime thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Training_Day" title="Training Day">Training Day</a></i> (2001), for which star <a href="/wiki/Denzel_Washington" title="Denzel Washington">Denzel Washington</a> won an <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Actor" title="Academy Award for Best Actor">Academy Award for Best Actor</a>. His next films were the action war drama <i><a href="/wiki/Tears_of_the_Sun" title="Tears of the Sun">Tears of the Sun</a></i> (2003), the Arthurian legend film <i><a href="/wiki/King_Arthur_(2004_film)" title="King Arthur (2004 film)">King Arthur</a></i> (2004), the conspiracy action thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Shooter_(2007_film)" title="Shooter (2007 film)">Shooter</a></i> (2007), the crime film <i><a href="/wiki/Brooklyn%27s_Finest" title="Brooklyn's Finest">Brooklyn's Finest</a></i> (2010), and the action thrillers <i><a href="/wiki/Olympus_Has_Fallen" title="Olympus Has Fallen">Olympus Has Fallen</a></i> (2013) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Equalizer_(film)" title="The Equalizer (film)">The Equalizer</a></i> (2014), the latter of which pairs Fuqua with Denzel Washington again. His most recent film is the action western <a href="/wiki/The_Magnificent_Seven_(2016_film)" title="The Magnificent Seven (2016 film)"><i>The Magnificent Seven</i></a> (2016).</p>

<p>He co-created the comic book miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/After_Dark_(comics)" title="After Dark (comics)">After Dark</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Wesley_Snipes" title="Wesley Snipes">Wesley Snipes</a>, which was written by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Milligan" title="Peter Milligan">Peter Milligan</a> and illustrated by Jeff Nentrup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Fuqua was scheduled to direct <i><a href="/wiki/Prisoners_(2013_film)" title="Prisoners (2013 film)">Prisoners</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> based on a storybook from <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Guzikowski" title="Aaron Guzikowski">Aaron Guzikowski</a>, but left the project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He was slated to direct <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a>'s official biopic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tupacfilm_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-tupacfilm-5">[5]</a></sup> The project was postponed to allow Fuqua to direct rapper <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s second feature film, <i><a href="/wiki/Southpaw_(film)" title="Southpaw (film)">Southpaw</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> However, Eminem put <i>Southpaw</i> on hold to focus on music,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and was replaced with Jake Gyllenhaal.</p>
<p>In 2010, <a href="/wiki/CBS_Films" title="CBS Films">CBS Films</a> hired Fuqua to direct a new movie based on a <a href="/wiki/Vince_Flynn" title="Vince Flynn">Vince Flynn</a> novel, <i><a href="/wiki/Consent_to_Kill" title="Consent to Kill">Consent to Kill</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In May 2014, <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox" title="20th Century Fox">20th Century Fox</a> set Fuqua to direct a drug smuggling thriller film <i>Narco Sub</i> which is scripted by David Guggenheim.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2011, Fuqua signed on to direct a film featuring the romantic love story between <a href="/wiki/Yang_Guifei" title="Yang Guifei">Consort Yang Yuhuan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Xuanzong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Xuanzong of Tang">Emperor Xuanzong</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Tang_dynasty" title="Tang dynasty">Tang dynasty</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> As reported in March 2013, he has stated that he's awaiting for the film project to go through the appropriate Chinese government channels before directing the historical <a href="/wiki/Epic_film" title="Epic film">epic film</a>, currently known as <i>The Tang Dynasty</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, Fuqua directed <i><a href="/wiki/The_Magnificent_Seven_(2016_film)" title="The Magnificent Seven (2016 film)">The Magnificent Seven</a></i>, a remake of the <a href="/wiki/The_Magnificent_Seven" title="The Magnificent Seven">1960 western of the same name</a> and <a href="/wiki/Akira_Kurosawa" title="Akira Kurosawa">Akira Kurosawa</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Seven_Samurai" title="Seven Samurai">Seven Samurai</a></i>, on which the western was based. This was the third of Fuqua's films starring <a href="/wiki/Denzel_Washington" title="Denzel Washington">Denzel Washington</a>, who plays the lead role of Sam Chisolm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He was also the executive producer of the 2016 television remake of <i><a href="/wiki/Roots_(2016_miniseries)" title="Roots (2016 miniseries)">Roots</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antoine_Fuqua&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1998, he and actress <a href="/wiki/Lela_Rochon" title="Lela Rochon">Lela Rochon</a> were engaged and they married on April 9, 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hol_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-hol-14">[14]</a></sup> Daughter Asia Rochon Fuqua was born on July 28, 2002, followed by son Brando in May 2004. Fuqua has a son Zachary, from a previous relationship.</p>
