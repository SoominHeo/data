<p>The <b>Geneva County massacre</b> occurred in southern <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a> on March 10, 2009. In the incident, 10 people were killed in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shooting_spree" title="Shooting spree">shooting spree</a> by Michael Kenneth McLendon, 28, in three small communities in two counties: <a href="/wiki/Kinston,_Alabama" title="Kinston, Alabama">Kinston</a> in <a href="/wiki/Coffee_County,_Alabama" title="Coffee County, Alabama">Coffee County</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Samson,_Alabama" title="Samson, Alabama">Samson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Geneva,_Alabama" title="Geneva, Alabama">Geneva</a> in <a href="/wiki/Geneva_County,_Alabama" title="Geneva County, Alabama">Geneva County</a>.</p>
<p>Five of McLendon's victims were family members, starting with his mother, and two were children. After engaging in an exchange of fire with police, McLendon committed suicide, bringing the total number of dead to eleven in the related incidents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-3">[3]</a></sup> Officials at the time said this was the worst shooting event in Alabama history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-5">[5]</a></sup> McLendon was said to have been depressed about lack of work; in a note he said that his mother's family was not giving her enough support.</p>
<p>McLendon first killed his mother and burned down her house in the town of Kinston. Traveling to Samson, he killed his maternal grandmother, uncle, two cousins, and others; and wounded six.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> When law enforcement reached him, McLendon was dead from a gunshot; initially, it was unclear whether the shot was self-inflicted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Later reports said he committed <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">suicide</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The incident was called the worst mass shooting in <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>'s history in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Shootings">Shootings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geneva_County_massacre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Shootings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McLendon began his attacks about 3:30 p.m. on March 10, 2009 at his mother's house where they lived in Kinston, a town of 540 residents. McLendon shot and killed his mother and their three dogs, and set the interior of the house on fire. He drove to his uncle's house in the small city of Samson, population about 2,000. There he shot his uncle, two cousins, and a neighbor and her infant daughter, all of whom were sitting on the porch of his uncle and aunt's house. His aunt escaped the shooting uninjured because she was inside the house, while the neighbor's four-month-old daughter survived her gunshot wounds. McLendon went next door and shot and killed his maternal grandmother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cops_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cops-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>McLendon left his grandmother's home and started driving, shooting at people from his car. The first to die was 43-year-old Sonya Smith, a gas station attendant. McLendon next shot and killed Bruce Malloy, a 51-year-old motorist who drove past him. The last fatality was 24-year-old James Starling, whom McLendon shot in the back as he tried to get away. McLendon also shot and wounded four other persons trying to escape his attack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cops_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cops-10">[10]</a></sup> He drove along <a href="/wiki/Alabama_State_Route_52" title="Alabama State Route 52">Highway 52</a> toward Geneva, continuing to shoot from his car, and eventually leading police on a 24-mile chase.</p>
<p>Law enforcement officers at one point used the <a href="/wiki/PIT_maneuver" title="PIT maneuver">PIT maneuver</a> on the suspect's car, but he shot at them with an assault rifle, wounding Geneva police chief Frankie Lindsey in the arm, and kept going. McLendon reached Reliable Building Products, owned by <a href="/wiki/Ruskin_Company" title="Ruskin Company">Ruskin Company</a>, where he had worked in 2003. After engaging in a shootout with police, he committed <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">suicide</a> inside the building. He had so much ammunition in his car that he appeared to have intended to kill many more people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ayres_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ayres-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The shooting spree lasted about an hour before McLendon was found dead at 4:17 p.m.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnnbriefpolice_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-cnnbriefpolice-1">[1]</a></sup> He was found to have been armed with a Soviet-made SKS and a Bushmaster. He also had at least one .38 caliber pistol, police said. He fired more than 200 rounds, police said at a news conference."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cops_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-Cops-10">[10]</a></sup> A large amount of ammunition was found in his car.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cops_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-Cops-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Victims">Victims</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geneva_County_massacre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Victims">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Among the victims were the suspect's mother, and relatives from his maternal side: grandmother, uncle, and two cousins, as well as Andrea and Corinne Myers, the neighbor wife and daughter of the deputy sheriff, Josh Myers. Only the four-month-old Ella Myers, who was wounded, survived McLendon's shooting of the people sitting on the porch. Myers' four-year-old son was in the house, but survived unharmed. His uncle and aunt had largely raised McLendon. His victims ranged in age from 18 months to 74 years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hitlist_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-hitlist-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Perpetrator">Perpetrator</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geneva_County_massacre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Perpetrator">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Michael_McLendon.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="300" data-file-width="231" height="260" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/12/Michael_McLendon.jpg/200px-Michael_McLendon.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/1/12/Michael_McLendon.jpg 1.5x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Michael_McLendon.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Michael McLendon</div>
</div>

<p><b>Michael Kenneth McLendon</b> (September 19, 1980 – March 10, 2009) was born in southern Alabama to Lisa McLendon (née White) and her husband. After his parents divorced, he was largely raised by his aunt and maternal uncle, Phyllis and James White, of <a href="/wiki/Samson,_Alabama" title="Samson, Alabama">Samson, Alabama</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Off_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Off-15">[15]</a></sup> He attended local schools. An "A" student in high school, he was known for being quiet; he graduated in 1999.</p>
<p>Unmarried, McLendon lived with his mother at her home in Kinston. This area of southern Alabama has had a depressed economy following the relocation of textile jobs overseas several years before. McLendon's work history showed a pattern of short tenure at jobs. He worked briefly with the police department in Samson, but failed to complete basic training at the state academy, washing out after a "week and a half."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnnbriefpolice_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-cnnbriefpolice-1">[1]</a></sup> He had worked at the Reliable Products warehouse in Geneva, where he was asked to leave in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABC_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-ABC-16">[16]</a></sup> He and his mother had worked at Pilgrim Foods, a poultry plant, and had filed suit with other workers when they were suspended in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cops_10-4"><a href="#cite_note-Cops-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Most recently, McLendon worked at Kelley Foods, a sausage factory, but quit the job abruptly the Wednesday before the shootings. Supervisors there said that he was a team leader and well-liked.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABC_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-ABC-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Motive">Motive</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geneva_County_massacre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Motive">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Witnesses said that McLendon was disturbed by his parents' divorce years ago, and had been depressed about his failure to start a career, disappointed that he had failed to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">U.S. Marines</a> or law enforcement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnnbriefpolice_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-cnnbriefpolice-1">[1]</a></sup> He had recently complained that his mother was not getting enough support from her family. At the time of the shooting, officials did not know where his father was.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Off_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Off-15">[15]</a></sup> Detectives discovered a handwritten list by McLendon in his home which identified several people from previous jobs, with notations about their actions or comments against him, described as people who "had either disciplined him or had reported him to supervisors for work related infractions".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABC_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-ABC-16">[16]</a></sup> The Alabama Bureau of Investigation noted that none of the people named in the list was among those he killed,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cops_10-5"><a href="#cite_note-Cops-10">[10]</a></sup> but police were trying to determine if he had intended to attack them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-apGunmenHadList_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-apGunmenHadList-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Investigators found a letter in which McLendon said he had killed his mother and planned to commit suicide. The letter also noted he was having a dispute over a legal issue with his mother's family, as they held a family Bible that he wanted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> He said that he and his mother had "suffered enough".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>McLendon was described as very familiar with guns. The investigators "found dozens of ammunition boxes, military and survival gear and medical supplies at McLendon's Kinston home".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnnbriefpolice_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-cnnbriefpolice-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Federal_troops">Federal troops</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geneva_County_massacre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Federal troops">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In response to a request for assistance from the Geneva County Sheriff's Office and Samson Police, Army officers ordered federal troops from nearby <a href="/wiki/Fort_Rucker" title="Fort Rucker">Fort Rucker</a> to be deployed to the streets of Samson. They manned traffic stops and guarded a makeshift morgue. An Army investigation later determined this action to be in violation of the <a href="/wiki/Posse_Comitatus_Act" title="Posse Comitatus Act">Posse Comitatus Act</a>, which prohibits federal troops from performing civil law enforcement actions. The Army took administrative action against at least one officer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">Reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geneva_County_massacre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Andalusia radio station <a href="/wiki/WAAO-FM" title="WAAO-FM">WAAO-FM</a> organized a fund-raising event to benefit the victims' families. Their goal was $10,000 with the event; more than $47,000 in cash, plus donations of caskets and concrete vaults for each of the victims, brought the total value of donations to more than $100,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wtok090314_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-wtok090314-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
