<p><b>Alan Erwin Ball</b> (born May 13, 1957)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Curr_Bio_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Curr_Bio-1">[1]</a></sup> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> writer, <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">director</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Film_producer" title="Film producer">producer</a> for <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a>, <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">film</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Theatre" title="Theatre">theatre</a>.</p>
<p>He is particularly notable for writing <i><a href="/wiki/American_Beauty_(1999_film)" title="American Beauty (1999 film)">American Beauty</a></i> and for creating <i><a href="/wiki/Six_Feet_Under_(TV_series)" title="Six Feet Under (TV series)">Six Feet Under</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/True_Blood" title="True Blood">True Blood</a></i>, work which earned him an Academy Award, an Emmy, and awards from the <a href="/wiki/Writers_Guild_of_America_Award" title="Writers Guild of America Award">Writers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Directors_Guild_of_America_Award" title="Directors Guild of America Award">Directors</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Producers_Guild_of_America_Award" title="Producers Guild of America Award">Producers</a> Guilds.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Ball_(screenwriter)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ball was born in <a href="/wiki/Marietta,_Georgia" title="Marietta, Georgia">Marietta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>, to Frank and Mary Ball, an aircraft inspector and a homemaker. His older sister, Mary Ann, was killed in a car accident when Ball was 13; he was in the passenger seat at the time. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He attended high school in <a href="/wiki/Marietta,_Georgia" title="Marietta, Georgia">Marietta</a>, and went on to attend the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Georgia" title="University of Georgia">University of Georgia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Florida_State_University" title="Florida State University">Florida State University</a>, from which he graduated in 1980 with a degree in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theater_arts" title="Theater arts">theater arts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> After college, he began work as a playwright at the General Nonsense Theater Company in <a href="/wiki/Sarasota,_Florida" title="Sarasota, Florida">Sarasota, Florida</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_and_television_career">Film and television career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Ball_(screenwriter)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Film and television career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ball broke into television as a writer and story editor on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Situation_comedy" title="Situation comedy">situation comedies</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Grace_Under_Fire" title="Grace Under Fire">Grace Under Fire</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Cybill" title="Cybill">Cybill</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ball has written two films, <i><a href="/wiki/American_Beauty_(1999_film)" title="American Beauty (1999 film)">American Beauty</a></i> (1999) and <i><a href="/wiki/Towelhead_(film)" title="Towelhead (film)">Towelhead</a></i> (2007), the latter of which he also produced and directed. He is also the creator, writer and executive producer of the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Six_Feet_Under_(TV_series)" title="Six Feet Under (TV series)">Six Feet Under</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/True_Blood" title="True Blood">True Blood</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He was <a href="/wiki/Showrunner" title="Showrunner">showrunner</a> for <i>True Blood</i> for its first five seasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010 Ball began work on a television adaptation of the crime noir novel <i>The Mystic Arts of Erasing All Signs of Death</i> by <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Huston" title="Charlie Huston">Charlie Huston</a>, to be titled <i>All Signs of Death</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In December 2010, after several months of pre-production, HBO cancelled production on <i>All Signs of Death</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>He is also one of the executive producers of the <a href="/wiki/Cinemax" title="Cinemax">Cinemax</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Banshee_(TV_series)" title="Banshee (TV series)">Banshee</a></i>.</p>
<p>In January 2015, it was announced that Ball's period musical drama <i>Virtuoso</i> had had a pilot ordered by <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a>. The pilot will be executively produced by <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>. Confirmed actors to be starring in the show include <a href="/wiki/Peter_Macdissi" title="Peter Macdissi">Peter Macdissi</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Iva_Babic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Iva Babic (page does not exist)">Iva Babic</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Francois_Civil" title="Francois Civil">Francois Civil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lindsay_Farris" title="Lindsay Farris">Lindsay Farris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Mirallegro" title="Nico Mirallegro">Nico Mirallegro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alex_Lawther" title="Alex Lawther">Alex Lawther</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Ball_(screenwriter)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ball has discussed his Buddhist faith in numerous interviews, noting how it has influenced his film making. In an interview with Amazon.com, Ball commented on the iconic scene in <i><a href="/wiki/American_Beauty_(1999_film)" title="American Beauty (1999 film)">American Beauty</a></i> with the plastic bag, stating, "I had an encounter with a plastic bag! And I didn't have a video camera, like Ricky does... There's a Buddhist notion of the miraculous within the mundane, and I think we certainly live in a culture that encourages us not to look for that."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Ball has also discussed how his Buddhism has shaped themes in <i><a href="/wiki/Six_Feet_Under_(TV_series)" title="Six Feet Under (TV series)">Six Feet Under</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/True_Blood" title="True Blood">True Blood</a></i> which he has substantially contributed to.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ball is <a href="/wiki/Homosexuality" title="Homosexuality">gay</a> and has been called "a strong voice for [the] <a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">LGBT</a> community".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In 2008 he made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Out_Magazine" title="Out Magazine"><i>Out</i> magazine</a>'s annual list of the 100 most impressive gay men and women.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> He lives in Los Angeles with his partner, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Macdissi" title="Peter Macdissi">Peter Macdissi</a>, who has starred in several of Ball's works.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Ball_(screenwriter)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For his work in television and film, Ball has received critical acclaim and numerous awards and nominations, including an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy</a> a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe</a>, and awards from the <a href="/wiki/Writers_Guild_of_America_Award" title="Writers Guild of America Award">Writers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Directors_Guild_of_America_Award" title="Directors Guild of America Award">Directors</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Producers_Guild_of_America_Award" title="Producers Guild of America Award">Producers</a> Guilds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
