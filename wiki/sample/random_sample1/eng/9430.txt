<p><b>Alan Wray Tudyk</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/uː/ long 'oo' in 'food'">uː</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span></span>/</a></span></span> <span title="English pronunciation respelling"><a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i><b><span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">TOO</span></span></b>-dik</i></a></span>; born March 16, 1971) is an American actor and voice actor known for his roles as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_the_Firefly_universe#Hoban_Washburne" title="List of characters in the Firefly universe">Hoban "Wash" Washburne</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_western" title="Space western">space western</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Firefly_(TV_series)" title="Firefly (TV series)">Firefly</a></i> and movie <i><a href="/wiki/Serenity_(film)" title="Serenity (film)">Serenity</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Dollhouse_characters#Alpha" title="List of Dollhouse characters">Alpha</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a> TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Dollhouse_(TV_series)" title="Dollhouse (TV series)">Dollhouse</a></i>, Tucker McGee in <i><a href="/wiki/Tucker_%26_Dale_vs._Evil" title="Tucker &amp; Dale vs. Evil">Tucker &amp; Dale vs. Evil</a></i>, Steve the Pirate in <i><a href="/wiki/DodgeBall:_A_True_Underdog_Story" title="DodgeBall: A True Underdog Story">DodgeBall: A True Underdog Story</a></i>, and Wat in <i><a href="/wiki/A_Knight%27s_Tale" title="A Knight's Tale">A Knight's Tale</a></i>. He also co-starred on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Suburgatory" title="Suburgatory">Suburgatory</a></i>. Tudyk had a supporting role in the film <i><a href="/wiki/42_(film)" title="42 (film)">42</a></i> as baseball manager <a href="/wiki/Ben_Chapman_(baseball)" title="Ben Chapman (baseball)">Ben Chapman</a> and voiced roles in <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Animation_Studios" title="Walt Disney Animation Studios">Walt Disney Animation Studios</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Wreck-It_Ralph" title="Wreck-It Ralph">Wreck-It Ralph</a>,</i> <i><a href="/wiki/Frozen_(2013_film)" title="Frozen (2013 film)">Frozen</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Hero_6_(film)" title="Big Hero 6 (film)">Big Hero 6</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Zootopia" title="Zootopia">Zootopia</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Moana_(2016_film)" title="Moana (2016 film)">Moana</a></i>. He has also featured in the web series <i><a href="/wiki/Con_Man_(web_series)" title="Con Man (web series)">Con Man</a></i> and the films <i><a href="/wiki/Maze_Runner:_The_Scorch_Trials" title="Maze Runner: The Scorch Trials">Maze Runner: The Scorch Trials</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Rogue_One" title="Rogue One">Rogue One: A Star Wars Story</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Tudyk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tudyk was born on March 16, 1971, in <a href="/wiki/El_Paso,_Texas" title="El Paso, Texas">El Paso</a>, Texas, the son of Betty Loyce (née Wiley) and Timothy Nicholas Tudyk. His father is of <a href="/wiki/Poles" title="Poles">Polish</a> descent<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RobertsS_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-RobertsS-1">[1]</a></sup> and his mother has German, English, and Scottish ancestry. He was raised in <a href="/wiki/Plano,_Texas" title="Plano, Texas">Plano, Texas</a>, a suburb of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dallas,_Texas" title="Dallas, Texas">Dallas</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interav_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-interav-2">[2]</a></sup> where he attended <a href="/wiki/Plano_Senior_High_School" title="Plano Senior High School">Plano Senior High School</a>. He had a brief experience as a stand-up comic, before quitting after an angry audience member threatened to kill him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Tudyk studied drama at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Methodist" title="Methodist">Methodist</a>-affiliated <a href="/wiki/Lon_Morris_College" title="Lon Morris College">Lon Morris College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville,_Texas" title="Jacksonville, Texas">Jacksonville, Texas</a> where he won the Academic Excellence award for drama. While in college, he played Beaver Smith in an eastern <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a> summer stock theater production of <i>Billy the Kid</i>. Tudyk entered <a href="/wiki/Juilliard_School" title="Juilliard School">Juilliard</a> but left in 1996 before earning a degree.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Tudyk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2000, Tudyk played Gerhardt, a gay German drug addict, alongside <a href="/wiki/Sandra_Bullock" title="Sandra Bullock">Sandra Bullock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Viggo_Mortensen" title="Viggo Mortensen">Viggo Mortensen</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/28_Days_(film)" title="28 Days (film)">28 Days</a></i>. He played Wat in 2001's <i><a href="/wiki/A_Knight%27s_Tale" title="A Knight's Tale">A Knight's Tale</a></i>, Steve the Pirate in the 2004 comedy <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dodgeball:_A_True_Underdog_Story" title="Dodgeball: A True Underdog Story">Dodgeball: A True Underdog Story</a></i> and the emotional robot <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sonny_(robot)" title="Sonny (robot)">Sonny</a> in <a href="/wiki/I,_Robot_(film)" title="I, Robot (film)"><i>I, Robot</i></a>. In 2005, he reprised his role as the playful, easy-going <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_the_Firefly_universe#Hoban_Washburne" title="List of characters in the Firefly universe">Hoban "Wash" Washburne</a> in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Serenity_(film)" title="Serenity (film)">Serenity</a></i>, derived from the cult television series <i><a href="/wiki/Firefly_(TV_series)" title="Firefly (TV series)">Firefly</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Joss_Whedon" title="Joss Whedon">Joss Whedon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dreadcentral1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-dreadcentral1-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2007, Tudyk had a supporting role as a strong-willed doctor in the western film <i><a href="/wiki/3:10_to_Yuma_(2007_film)" title="3:10 to Yuma (2007 film)">3:10 to Yuma</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Christian_Bale" title="Christian Bale">Christian Bale</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russell_Crowe" title="Russell Crowe">Russell Crowe</a> along with a brief but memorable role in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Knocked_Up" title="Knocked Up">Knocked Up</a></i> and a highly physical comedic performance in the British film <i><a href="/wiki/Death_at_a_Funeral_(2007_film)" title="Death at a Funeral (2007 film)">Death at a Funeral</a></i>. Tudyk starred as Tucker in the <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">indie</a> horror comedy <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tucker_%26_Dale_vs_Evil" title="Tucker &amp; Dale vs Evil">Tucker &amp; Dale vs Evil</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tucker_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-tucker-5">[5]</a></sup> He also appears in <i><a href="/wiki/Transformers:_Dark_of_the_Moon" title="Transformers: Dark of the Moon">Transformers: Dark of the Moon</a></i> as Dutch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thewrap_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-thewrap-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <i><a href="/wiki/Alvin_and_the_Chipmunks:_Chipwrecked" title="Alvin and the Chipmunks: Chipwrecked">Alvin and the Chipmunks: Chipwrecked</a></i>, he voiced Simone (a reckless <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_accent" title="French accent">French accent</a> speaking Chipmunk, the result after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Simon_Seville" title="Simon Seville">Simon</a> was bitten by a spider). In 2012, Tudyk appeared in <a href="/wiki/Timur_Bekmambetov" title="Timur Bekmambetov">Timur Bekmambetov</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln:_Vampire_Hunter_(film)" title="Abraham Lincoln: Vampire Hunter (film)">Abraham Lincoln: Vampire Hunter</a></i>, in the role of <a href="/wiki/Stephen_A._Douglas" title="Stephen A. Douglas">Stephen A. Douglas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alvh_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-alvh-7">[7]</a></sup> Tudyk also voiced King Candy in <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Animation_Studios" title="Walt Disney Animation Studios">Walt Disney Animation Studios</a>' 2012 film <i><a href="/wiki/Wreck-It_Ralph" title="Wreck-It Ralph">Wreck-It Ralph</a></i>, a performance for which he won the Annie Award for Voice Acting. He played <a href="/wiki/Ben_Chapman_(baseball)" title="Ben Chapman (baseball)">Ben Chapman</a> in the 2013 movie <i>42</i> about Jackie Robinson.</p>
<p>Tudyk voiced the Duke of Weselton in Disney's 2013 film <i><a href="/wiki/Frozen_(2013_film)" title="Frozen (2013 film)">Frozen</a></i>. In 2014, he voiced Alistair Krei in Disney's <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Hero_6_(film)" title="Big Hero 6 (film)">Big Hero 6</a></i>, and in 2016 he voiced Duke Weaselton and Hei Hei in Disney's <i><a href="/wiki/Zootopia" title="Zootopia">Zootopia</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Moana_(2016_film)" title="Moana (2016 film)">Moana</a></i>, respectively. That same year, he played the droid <a href="/wiki/K-2SO" title="K-2SO">K-2SO</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Rogue_One" title="Rogue One">Rogue One: A Star Wars Story</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadway">Broadway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Tudyk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Broadway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1999, Tudyk made his Broadway debut in <i><a href="/wiki/Epic_Proportions" title="Epic Proportions">Epic Proportions</a></i>. He was also in <i>Wonder of the World</i>, <i>The Most Fabulous Story Ever Told</i>, <i>Misalliance</i>, <i>Oedipus</i> and <i>Bunny Bunny</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Butler.2C_Tudyk.2C_Kind.2C_Golden.2C_Huffman_cast_in_reading_of_musical_Xanadu_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Butler.2C_Tudyk.2C_Kind.2C_Golden.2C_Huffman_cast_in_reading_of_musical_Xanadu-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2005, Tudyk filled in for <a href="/wiki/Hank_Azaria" title="Hank Azaria">Hank Azaria</a>'s roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Spamalot" title="Spamalot">Spamalot</a></i> from June to December and starred in a limited run of <i><a href="/wiki/Prelude_to_a_Kiss_(play)" title="Prelude to a Kiss (play)">Prelude to a Kiss</a></i> on Broadway.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Tudyk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>One of Tudyk's better-known roles is the playful, easy-going <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_the_Firefly_universe#Hoban_Washburne" title="List of characters in the Firefly universe">Hoban "Wash" Washburne</a> in the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Firefly_(TV_series)" title="Firefly (TV series)">Firefly</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Joss_Whedon" title="Joss Whedon">Joss Whedon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dreadcentral1_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-dreadcentral1-4">[4]</a></sup> Although the series ran for only one season, <a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios" title="Universal Studios">Universal Studios</a> purchased the rights to the show and turned it into a film, <i><a href="/wiki/Serenity_(film)" title="Serenity (film)">Serenity</a></i>, in which he reprised the role. He did a few voices from <i><a href="/wiki/Make_Way_for_Noddy" title="Make Way for Noddy">Make Way for Noddy</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dreadcentral_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-dreadcentral-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2014, Tudyk took over the role of lead anchor on the live action [adult swim] comedy, <i>Newsreaders</i>. Tudyk appeared as the cult leader, Father, in a two-part episode of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Strangers_With_Candy" title="Strangers With Candy">Strangers With Candy</a></i> entitled "Blank Stare." Among several guest spots on popular shows such as <i><a href="/wiki/Arrested_Development_(TV_series)" title="Arrested Development (TV series)">Arrested Development</a></i>, he played a convicted pedophile on a popular episode of <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI: Crime Scene Investigation</a></i>. Tudyk was cast as a special guest star in Joss Whedon's <i><a href="/wiki/Dollhouse_(TV_series)" title="Dollhouse (TV series)">Dollhouse</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interav_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-interav-2">[2]</a></sup> The show featured people whose personalities had been erased, with Tudyk portraying <a href="/wiki/List_of_Dollhouse_characters#Alpha" title="List of Dollhouse characters">Alpha</a>, a former "active" who accidentally downloaded 48 separate personalities. Alpha served as the main antagonist of the series' first season, with guest appearances in the show's second season. Tudyk also guest-starred in three episodes of ABC's modern remake of the television miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/V_(2009_TV_series)" title="V (2009 TV series)">V</a></i>. Tudyk provided the voice of superhero <a href="/wiki/Green_Arrow" title="Green Arrow">Green Arrow</a> and villain <a href="/wiki/Psimon" title="Psimon">Psimon</a> in the animated series <i><a href="/wiki/Young_Justice_(TV_series)" title="Young Justice (TV series)">Young Justice</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Comic-Con_2010:_Young_Justice_Goes_Under_Cover_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Comic-Con_2010:_Young_Justice_Goes_Under_Cover-10">[10]</a></sup> He also co-starred in the ABC comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Suburgatory" title="Suburgatory">Suburgatory</a></i> as Noah Werner, a dentist from the city, who moves to the suburbs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-seriesorder_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-seriesorder-11">[11]</a></sup> Tudyk voiced Debbie the prostitute in season 3 of <i>The Life and Times of Tim</i>.</p>
<p>He is currently working on his own TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Con_Man_(web_series)" title="Con Man (web series)">Con Man</a></i> based loosely upon his experiences touring the convention circuit after the cancellation of <i><a href="/wiki/Firefly_(TV_series)" title="Firefly (TV series)">Firefly</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He will be a main cast member of the DC Comics-based show, <i><a href="/wiki/Powerless_(TV_series)" title="Powerless (TV series)">Powerless</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_games">Video games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Tudyk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Video games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tudyk plays the voice of a Marine in the Xbox 360 game <i><a href="/wiki/Halo_3" title="Halo 3">Halo 3</a></i>. He is joined by his <i>Firefly</i> co-stars <a href="/wiki/Adam_Baldwin" title="Adam Baldwin">Adam Baldwin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Fillion" title="Nathan Fillion">Nathan Fillion</a> who both voice Marines. All three actors are given personalities in the game resembling those of their characters from <i><a href="/wiki/Firefly_(TV_series)" title="Firefly (TV series)">Firefly</a></i>. Tudyk made an appearance in <i><a href="/wiki/Halo_3:_ODST" title="Halo 3: ODST">Halo 3: ODST</a></i> as the character "Mickey", an elite ODST (Orbital Drop Shock Trooper) who is part of the player character's squad as they try to escape a city invaded by the Covenant. He also reprised the voice of the DC Comics character <a href="/wiki/Green_Arrow" title="Green Arrow">Green Arrow</a> in the game <i><a href="/wiki/Injustice:_Gods_Among_Us" title="Injustice: Gods Among Us">Injustice: Gods Among Us</a></i>.</p>
