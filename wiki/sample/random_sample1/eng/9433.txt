<p><b>Alimi Ballard</b> (born October 17, 1977) is an American television actor. He is best known for his role as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FBI" title="FBI">FBI</a> agent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/David_Sinclair_(Numb3rs)" title="David Sinclair (Numb3rs)">David Sinclair</a> on the CBS police procedural, <i><a href="/wiki/Numbers_(TV_series)" title="Numbers (TV series)">Numb3rs</a></i>. Ballard recently stars in the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> legal thriller, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Catch_(TV_series)" title="The Catch (TV series)">The Catch</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alimi_Ballard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 1993 to 1996, Ballard portrayed <a href="/wiki/Frankie_Hubbard" title="Frankie Hubbard">Frankie Hubbard</a> (son of <i><a href="/wiki/All_My_Children" title="All My Children">All My Children</a></i> supercouple <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Hubbard_and_Angie_Baxter" title="Jesse Hubbard and Angie Baxter">Jesse and Angie</a>) on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> daytime soap opera, <i><a href="/wiki/Loving_(TV_series)" title="Loving (TV series)">Loving</a></i> and its successor series <i><a href="/wiki/The_City_(1995_TV_series)" title="The City (1995 TV series)">The City</a></i>. In 1997, he was a regular cast member on the short-lived ABC sitcom <i>Arsenio</i>, which starred <a href="/wiki/Arsenio_Hall" title="Arsenio Hall">Arsenio Hall</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vivica_A._Fox" title="Vivica A. Fox">Vivica A. Fox</a>. He also portrayed Quizmaster Albert on the ABC/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_WB_Television_Network" title="The WB Television Network">WB</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Sabrina,_the_Teenage_Witch_(TV_series)" title="Sabrina, the Teenage Witch (TV series)">Sabrina, the Teenage Witch</a></i> (1997—99) and Herbal on the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_Angel_(TV_series)" title="Dark Angel (TV series)">Dark Angel</a></i> (2000—01).</p>
<p>From 2005 to 2010, Ballard co-starred in the CBS police procedural, <i><a href="/wiki/Numbers_(TV_series)" title="Numbers (TV series)">Numb3rs</a></i> as David Sinclair. In 2011, Ballard guest-starred as Special Agent Gayne Levin on another CBS procedural, <a href="/wiki/NCIS_(TV_series)" title="NCIS (TV series)">NCIS</a>, reuniting him with former Dark Angel star, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Weatherly" title="Michael Weatherly">Michael Weatherly</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He also had the recurring role as Officer Crawford on <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI: Crime Scene Investigation</a></i> from 2012 to 2015 as a newly promoted detective after appearing in Season 3 as a music producer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He also guest starred on <i><a href="/wiki/NYPD_Blue" title="NYPD Blue">NYPD Blue</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/American_Dreams" title="American Dreams">American Dreams</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Rizzoli_%26_Isles" title="Rizzoli &amp; Isles">Rizzoli &amp; Isles</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Drop_Dead_Diva" title="Drop Dead Diva">Drop Dead Diva</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Castle_(TV_series)" title="Castle (TV series)">Castle</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2015, Ballard was cast opposite <a href="/wiki/Mireille_Enos" title="Mireille Enos">Mireille Enos</a> in the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> legal thriller, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Catch_(TV_series)" title="The Catch (TV series)">The Catch</a></i> produced by <a href="/wiki/Shonda_Rhimes" title="Shonda Rhimes">Shonda Rhimes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alimi_Ballard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ballard was born in The Bronx, New York, and began acting in high school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-5">[5]</a></sup> He is married to Dahn Ballard. They have two children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
