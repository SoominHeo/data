<p><b>Alyssa Jayne Milano</b> (born December 19, 1972) is an American actress, producer and former singer. She is best known for her roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Who%27s_the_Boss%3F" title="Who's the Boss?">Who's the Boss?</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Melrose_Place" title="Melrose Place">Melrose Place</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Charmed" title="Charmed">Charmed</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Mistresses_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Mistresses (U.S. TV series)">Mistresses</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alyssa_Milano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Milano was born on December 19, 1972<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fr-bio_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-fr-bio-1">[1]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Bensonhurst,_Brooklyn" title="Bensonhurst, Brooklyn">Bensonhurst, Brooklyn</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fr-bio_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-fr-bio-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocean_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocean-2">[2]</a></sup> the daughter of fashion designer and talent manager Lin Milano and film-music editor Thomas M. Milano,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fr-bio_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-fr-bio-1">[1]</a></sup> (who had been an early member of the band <i>Sleepy Hollow</i> but left in 1972).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billay_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-billay-3">[3]</a></sup> She and her brother, Cory,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio._4-0"><a href="#cite_note-bio.-4">[4]</a></sup> who is seven years her junior,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYDailyNews_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYDailyNews-5">[5]</a></sup> are of Italian ancestry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Her family remained in Bensonhurst until a neighborhood shooting prompted them to relocate to <a href="/wiki/Great_Kills,_Staten_Island" title="Great Kills, Staten Island">Great Kills, Staten Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocean_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ocean-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alyssa_Milano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1984.E2.80.931996">1984–1996</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alyssa_Milano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1984–1996">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Milano began her career at age seven, when her babysitter, without notifying her parents, took her to an audition for one of the four principal parts in a national touring company of <i><a href="/wiki/Annie_(musical)" title="Annie (musical)">Annie</a></i>. Milano was one of four selected from more than 1,500 girls. During the course of her work in the play, Milano and her mother were on the road for 18 months. After returning to New York, she appeared in television commercials<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and did several roles in <a href="/wiki/Off-Broadway" title="Off-Broadway">off-Broadway</a> productions, including the first American musical adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Jane_Eyre" title="Jane Eyre">Jane Eyre</a></i>. When accompanying a friend from the <i>Annie</i> production to the office of a New York agent, Milano was introduced to the agent, who began representing her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes-8">[8]</a></sup> She does not feel that growing up in front of the camera harmed her childhood,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYDailyNews_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYDailyNews-5">[5]</a></sup> and has said: "I love my family very much – they've really backed my career. I consider myself to be normal: I've got to clean my room, and help in the kitchen."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-teenbeat_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-teenbeat-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 1984, Milano made her film debut in the coming of age drama <i><a href="/wiki/Old_Enough" title="Old Enough">Old Enough</a></i>, which she recalled as a "great" way for "starting out".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocean_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-ocean-2">[2]</a></sup> The film was screened at the <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Film Festival</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> where it won First Prize.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Milano auditioned as <a href="/wiki/Tony_Danza" title="Tony Danza">Tony Danza</a>'s daughter on the sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Who%27s_the_Boss%3F" title="Who's the Boss?">Who's the Boss?</a></i> After winning the part, she and her family moved to Los Angeles, where the show was produced. It premiered on <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> on September 20, 1984.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes-8">[8]</a></sup> Throughout <i>Who's the Boss?</i>, Milano developed a close relationship with co-star Danza.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-qa_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-qa-12">[12]</a></sup> Commenting on their early years together, "She was just the sweetest little girl of all time...She became much like my daughter."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes-8">[8]</a></sup> The series established Milano as a teen idol,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes12.29.91_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes12.29.91-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBSNews_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBSNews-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and provided her opportunities for other roles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBSNews_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-CBSNews-14">[14]</a></sup> Her education was split between school and an on-set tutor with whom Milano would work for three hours a day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RyanWhite_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-RyanWhite-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>At age twelve, Milano co-starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Commando_(1985_film)" title="Commando (1985 film)">Commando</a></i> as Jenny Matrix, the daughter of John Matrix (<a href="/wiki/Arnold_Schwarzenegger" title="Arnold Schwarzenegger">Arnold Schwarzenegger</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBSNews_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-CBSNews-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The body type and personality of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ariel_(The_Little_Mermaid)" title="Ariel (The Little Mermaid)">Ariel</a>, the main character in the 1989 animated film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Little_Mermaid_(1989_film)" title="The Little Mermaid (1989 film)">The Little Mermaid</a></i>, were based upon Milano.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exam_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-exam-18">[18]</a></sup> On stage, she starred in <i>Tender Offer</i>, a <a href="/wiki/One-act_play" title="One-act play">one-act play</a> written by <a href="/wiki/Wendy_Wasserstein" title="Wendy Wasserstein">Wendy Wasserstein</a>, <i>All Night Long</i> by American <a href="/wiki/Playwright" title="Playwright">playwright</a> <a href="/wiki/John_O%27Keefe_(playwright)" title="John O'Keefe (playwright)">John O'Keefe</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and the first American musical adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Jane_Eyre" title="Jane Eyre">Jane Eyre</a></i>. She returned to the theater in 1991, producing and starring in a <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> production of <i><a href="/wiki/Butterflies_Are_Free" title="Butterflies Are Free">Butterflies Are Free</a></i> from December 26, 1991 to January 19, 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>A few years later this film was shown in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, prompting a producer to offer Milano a five-album record deal. Milano's albums, which she described as "<a href="/wiki/Bubblegum_pop" title="Bubblegum pop">bubblegum pop</a>", scored platinum in the country, though she later showed her discontent in their musical quality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocean_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-ocean-2">[2]</a></sup> Subsequently, she starred in the children's film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Canterville_Ghost_(1986_film)" title="The Canterville Ghost (1986 film)">The Canterville Ghost</a></i>, which did not achieve much praise or attention and <i>Variety</i> magazine noted in its review: "Milano as the catalyzing daughter Jennifer adapts to the ghostly Sir Simon without a qualm; that, of course, is the true charm of the story, but Milano doesn't exhibit enough presence to match the droll, charming Gielgud".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Milano starred in two 1988 television films, <i><a href="/wiki/Crash_Course_(film)" title="Crash Course (film)">Crash Course</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Dance_%27til_Dawn" title="Dance 'til Dawn">Dance 'til Dawn</a></i>. Both projects allowed her to work alongside close personal friend <a href="/wiki/Brian_Bloom" title="Brian Bloom">Brian Bloom</a> who worked with his brother <a href="/wiki/Scott_Bloom" title="Scott Bloom">Scott</a> with her in episodes of <i>Who's the Boss</i>; this working camaraderie would later expand in 1993 when Milano made a <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">cameo appearance</a> in Bloom's film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Webbers" title="The Webbers">The Webbers</a></i>. She produced a teen workout video, <i>Teen Steam</i>, and achieved some fame outside the USA with her music career, which lasted until the early 1990s. Even though she scored platinum in Japan, Milano had no interest to pursue a music career in the United States: "I'm not interested in crossing over. I'd much rather have it released where it's appreciated than laughed at."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes12.29.91_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes12.29.91-13">[13]</a></sup> Simultaneously, she wrote a weekly column called "From Alyssa, with love" for the teen magazine <i>Teen Machine</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-premiere_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-premiere-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Milano played a teenage prostitute in the 1992 independent film <i><a href="/wiki/Where_the_Day_Takes_You" title="Where the Day Takes You">Where the Day Takes You</a></i>. The film, which focuses on a group of young runaway and homeless teenagers,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> was shot on and around <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Boulevard" title="Hollywood Boulevard">Hollywood Boulevard</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and was met with positive critical reception.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> It was nominated for the Critics Award at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deauville_Film_Festival" title="Deauville Film Festival">Deauville Film Festival</a>, and won the Golden Space Needle Award at the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_International_Film_Festival" title="Seattle International Film Festival">Seattle International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although Milano feared that viewers would only recognize her as "the girl from <i>Who's the Boss?</i>",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes12.29.91_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes12.29.91-13">[13]</a></sup> she was noticed by the media, which helped her land the role of <a href="/wiki/Amy_Fisher" title="Amy Fisher">Amy Fisher</a> in the high-profile TV movie <i>Casualties of Love: The Long Island Lolita Story</i>, one of three TV films based on Fisher's shooting of <a href="/wiki/Mary_Jo_Buttafuoco" title="Mary Jo Buttafuoco">Mary Jo Buttafuoco</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EntertainmentWeekly_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-EntertainmentWeekly-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Milano said that her portrayal of Fisher in the film, which was based on the Buttafuoco's point of view,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EntertainmentWeekly_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-EntertainmentWeekly-27">[27]</a></sup> "was the least 'Alyssa' of anything [she had] done."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-qa_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-qa-12">[12]</a></sup> The film was shot from November - December 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> She welcomed the cancellation of the series, as she was ready to move on to other roles and enthusiastic to "showcase" what she was able to do. Looking back on eight years of playing the same role, Milano commented, "Creatively, it's been very frustrating. I gave her more of a personality. I changed her wardrobe, cut her hair, anything to give her new life."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes12.29.91_13-3"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes12.29.91-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the early 1990s, Milano auditioned for nearly every film role in her age bracket, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B_movies" title="B movies">B movies</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes12.29.91_13-4"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes12.29.91-13">[13]</a></sup> and finally tried to shed her "nice girl" image by appearing nude in several erotic films targeted at adults, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Embrace_of_the_Vampire" title="Embrace of the Vampire">Embrace of the Vampire</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Deadly_Sins_(film)" title="Deadly Sins (film)">Deadly Sins</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Poison_Ivy_II:_Lily" title="Poison Ivy II: Lily">Poison Ivy II: Lily</a></i>. She said the nude appearances taught her to begin requiring a <a href="/wiki/Nudity_clause" title="Nudity clause">nudity clause</a> in her contracts giving her "full control" over all her nude scenes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-qa_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-qa-12">[12]</a></sup> In a 1995 interview, she explained her motivation for some explicit scenes in <i>Embrace of the Vampire</i>: "I'm not going to say that I was manipulated into doing things that I didn't want to do. I did it because it was a woman director and I felt protected. And I learned a lot as far as knowing where the camera is and what coverage they need so that it's not all explicit."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-premiere_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-premiere-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>She starred in other roles, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Candles_in_the_Dark" title="Candles in the Dark">Candles in the Dark</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Confessions_of_a_Sorority_Girl" title="Confessions of a Sorority Girl">Confessions of a Sorority Girl</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Surrogate_(1995_film)" title="The Surrogate (1995 film)">The Surrogate</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/To_Brave_Alaska" title="To Brave Alaska">To Brave Alaska</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Fear_(1996_film)" title="Fear (1996 film)">Fear</a></i>, which did not receive very positive reviews, although the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> called Milano "very good" in the production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>

<p>Milano starred in the lead role in <i><a href="/wiki/Hugo_Pool" title="Hugo Pool">Hugo Pool</a></i> (1997).</p>
<p>In late 1996, Milano was offered a role of <a href="/wiki/List_of_Melrose_Place_characters#Jennifer_Mancini" title="List of Melrose Place characters">Jennifer Mancini</a> on the drama <i><a href="/wiki/Melrose_Place" title="Melrose Place">Melrose Place</a></i> by producer <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Spelling" title="Aaron Spelling">Aaron Spelling</a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> "We were looking for someone with sparkle. Alyssa was the perfect choice."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-completewoman_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-completewoman-32">[32]</a></sup> She left early in season seven. Since Milano is from the same hometown as NFL Network's <a href="/wiki/Rich_Eisen" title="Rich Eisen">Rich Eisen</a>, she revealed some of her family's connections with the Giants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> In 2013, Milano expanded "Touch" into <a href="/wiki/NASCAR" title="NASCAR">NASCAR</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>

<p>Milano starred in two comedy films, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hall_Pass_(2011_film)" title="Hall Pass (2011 film)">Hall Pass</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Eve_(2011_film)" title="New Year's Eve (2011 film)">New Year's Eve</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, Milano created the comic book series <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hacktivist_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hacktivist (comics) (page does not exist)">Hacktivist</a></i>, which was written by Jackson Lanzing and Collin Kelly, drawn by <a href="/wiki/Marcus_To" title="Marcus To">Marcus To</a>, and published by <a href="/wiki/Archaia_Entertainment" title="Archaia Entertainment">Archaia Entertainment</a>. The book, which explores the modern world of hacking and global activism, is described as "a fast-paced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cyber-thriller" title="Cyber-thriller">cyber-thriller</a> about friendship and freedom in a time of war." The publication was released digitally in late 2013, while the first print edition issue of the four-issue miniseries was published in January 2014. A hardcover edition collecting all four issues was released in July 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> The series received positive reviews, as it currently holds a score of 8.1 out of 10 at the review aggregator website Comic Book Roundup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Humanitarian_and_political_causes">Humanitarian and political causes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alyssa_Milano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Humanitarian and political causes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the late 1980s, Milano reached out to <a href="/wiki/Ryan_White" title="Ryan White">Ryan White</a>, a schoolboy ostracized for having AIDS, and a fan of Milano's.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RyanWhite_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-RyanWhite-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> She attended a big party for him, where she sat with him for six hours, making <a href="/wiki/Friendship_bracelet" title="Friendship bracelet">friendship bracelets</a> with him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> They appeared together on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Phil_Donahue_Show" title="The Phil Donahue Show">The Phil Donahue Show</a></i>, where Milano kissed White, in order to show that she could not catch the disease through casual contact with him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2004, Milano participated in UNICEF's "<a href="/wiki/Trick-or-Treat_for_UNICEF" title="Trick-or-Treat for UNICEF">Trick or Treat</a>" campaign as the national spokesperson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> She raised approximately $50,000 for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Africans" title="South Africans">South African</a> women and children with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AIDS" title="AIDS">AIDS</a> by selling her own and schools' photo work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BankRate_66-0"><a href="#cite_note-BankRate-66">[66]</a></sup></p>
<p>In support of <a href="/wiki/People_for_the_Ethical_Treatment_of_Animals" title="People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals">PETA</a>, she appeared in a 2007 advertisement for them, advocating <a href="/wiki/Vegetarianism" title="Vegetarianism">vegetarianism</a>, in a dress made entirely of vegetables.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2007, The Sabin Vaccine Institute, named Milano a Founding Ambassador for the <a href="/wiki/Global_Network_for_Neglected_Tropical_Diseases" title="Global Network for Neglected Tropical Diseases">Global Network for Neglected Tropical Diseases</a>, an alliance formed to advocate and mobilize resources in the fight to control neglected tropical diseases (NTDs), to which Milano donated $250,000. She is also a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Children%27s_Fund" title="United Nations Children's Fund">UNICEF</a> Goodwill Ambassador for the United States of America,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ItalianMamma_69-0"><a href="#cite_note-ItalianMamma-69">[69]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Details_70-0"><a href="#cite_note-Details-70">[70]</a></sup> Her field work for the organization has included a 2004 trip to <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a> to speak with HIV-positive women and people disfigured by land mines during the country's civil war; a trip to <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> to meet displaced mothers living in squalor following a tsunami; and a 2010 trip to the settlement of Kolonia in western <a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Kosovo</a> to witness impoverished living conditions. Milano wrote on her blog that the latter trip was "the hardest experience I've had on a field visit," and described a waste dump close to the settlement where children spent time looking for metal to sell or scavenging for food.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup></p>
<p>For her 37th birthday, which occurred on December 19, 2009, Milano ran an online fundraising campaign for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charity:Water" title="Charity:Water">Charity:Water</a>. Her original goal was to raise $25,000, but a donation from her husband put her over the $75,000 mark on December 18. The fundraiser ran until December 26.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> In September 2013, Milano released a viral tape on <a href="/wiki/Funny_or_Die" title="Funny or Die">Funny or Die</a> that drew attention to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Syrian_civil_war" title="Syrian civil war">Syrian civil war</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, she expressed her support for presidential candidate <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Clinton" title="Hillary Clinton">Hillary Clinton</a> on her <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> account.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alyssa_Milano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Milano has <a href="/wiki/Dyslexia" title="Dyslexia">dyslexia</a>. In a 2004 interview, she explained how she deals with the disorder:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>I've stumbled over words while reading from teleprompters. Sir John Gielgud, whom I worked with on The Canterville Ghost years ago, gave me great advice. When I asked how he memorized his monologues, he said, "I write them down." I use that method to this day. It not only familiarizes me with the words, it makes them my own.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dyslexia_76-0"><a href="#cite_note-dyslexia-76">[76]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Milano was involved with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lost_Boys" title="The Lost Boys">The Lost Boys</a></i> actor <a href="/wiki/Corey_Haim" title="Corey Haim">Corey Haim</a> from 1987 to 1990. Milano and her parents, together with his manager at the time, unsuccessfully tried to get Haim help for his addiction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1993, Milano became engaged to actor <a href="/wiki/Scott_Wolf" title="Scott Wolf">Scott Wolf</a>, but they broke off their engagement the following year. On January 1, 1999, Milano married singer Cinjun Tate; they divorced in early 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio._4-1"><a href="#cite_note-bio.-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>After one year of dating, Milano became engaged to <a href="/wiki/Creative_Artists_Agency" title="Creative Artists Agency">Creative Artists Agency</a> agent David Bugliari in December 2008,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> and they married on August 15, 2009 at Bugliari's family home in New Jersey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> They have a son and a daughter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup></p>
