<p><b>Alyson Rae Stoner</b> (born August 11, 1993)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TV_Guide_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-TV_Guide-1">[1]</a></sup> is an American actress, singer-songwriter, choreographer, voice actress, singer, dancer and model. Stoner is best known for her roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Cheaper_by_the_Dozen_(2003_film)" title="Cheaper by the Dozen (2003 film)">Cheaper by the Dozen</a></i> (2003), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Suite_Life_of_Zack_%26_Cody" title="The Suite Life of Zack &amp; Cody">The Suite Life of Zack &amp; Cody</a></i> (2005–2007) and the <i><a href="/wiki/Step_Up_(film)" title="Step Up (film)">Step Up</a></i> series (2006, 2010, 2014).</p>
<p>She has been a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Background_dancer" title="Background dancer">dancer</a> for several artists, such as <a href="/wiki/Missy_Elliott" title="Missy Elliott">Missy Elliott</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kumbia_Kings" title="Kumbia Kings">Kumbia Kings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Outkast" title="Outkast">Outkast</a> and <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">Will Smith</a>. Stoner is also known for hosting <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Mike%27s_Super_Short_Show" title="Mike's Super Short Show">Mike's Super Short Show</a></i> (2001–07) and for voicing <a href="/wiki/List_of_Phineas_and_Ferb_characters#Isabella_Garcia-Shapiro" title="List of Phineas and Ferb characters">Isabella Garcia-Shapiro</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Phineas_and_Ferb" title="Phineas and Ferb">Phineas and Ferb</a></i> (2007–2015).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alyson_Stoner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stoner was born in <a href="/wiki/Toledo,_Ohio" title="Toledo, Ohio">Toledo, Ohio</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Daughter" title="Daughter">daughter</a> of LuAnne Hodges (née Adams), a former executive secretary at <a href="/wiki/Owens-Illinois" title="Owens-Illinois">Owens-Illinois</a>, and Charlie Stoner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> While growing up in Toledo, Stoner attended <a href="/wiki/Maumee_Valley_Country_Day_School" title="Maumee Valley Country Day School">Maumee Valley Country Day School</a> and studied <a href="/wiki/Ballet" title="Ballet">ballet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tap_dance" title="Tap dance">tap dance</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jazz_dance" title="Jazz dance">jazz dance</a> at the O'Connell's Dance Studio. She also modeled and trained at the <a href="/wiki/Margaret_O%27Brien" title="Margaret O'Brien">Margaret O'Brien</a> Modeling Studio. She won Best Model of the Year at the <a href="/wiki/International_Modeling_and_Talent_Association" title="International Modeling and Talent Association">International Modeling and Talent Association</a> Convention in New York City under O'Brien's studio in 2000.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alyson_Stoner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2001, Stoner became the co-host of the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Mike%27s_Super_Short_Show" title="Mike's Super Short Show">Mike's Super Short Show</a></i> along with Michael Alan Johnson, an infomercial segment regarding forthcoming <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Pictures" title="Walt Disney Pictures">Disney</a> releases. In 2003 and 2005, she appeared as Sarah, one of the twelve Baker children, in the successful comedies <i><a href="/wiki/Cheaper_by_the_Dozen_(2003_film)" title="Cheaper by the Dozen (2003 film)">Cheaper by the Dozen</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Cheaper_by_the_Dozen_2" title="Cheaper by the Dozen 2">Cheaper by the Dozen 2</a></i>. She has also appeared in several television shows aimed at the preteen market, including <i><a href="/wiki/The_Suite_Life_of_Zack_%26_Cody" title="The Suite Life of Zack &amp; Cody">The Suite Life of Zack &amp; Cody</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/That%27s_So_Raven" title="That's So Raven">That's So Raven</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Drake_%26_Josh" title="Drake &amp; Josh">Drake &amp; Josh</a></i>. She later worked for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/4kids_Entertainment" title="4kids Entertainment">4kids Entertainment</a> and voiced Mrs. Hanson from <i><a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Mew_Mew" title="Tokyo Mew Mew">Mew Mew Power</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2006, she had a small film role in <i><a href="/wiki/Step_Up_(film)" title="Step Up (film)">Step Up</a></i> as Camille, <a href="/wiki/Channing_Tatum" title="Channing Tatum">Channing Tatum</a>'s character's younger sister. She currently provides the voice of Isabella Garcia-Shapiro and Jenny (until Season 4) in the Disney Channel animated series <i><a href="/wiki/Phineas_and_Ferb" title="Phineas and Ferb">Phineas and Ferb</a></i> and personally came up with Isabella's catchphrase "Whatcha doin'?". In 2008, she starred in the Disney Channel Original movie <i><a href="/wiki/Camp_Rock" title="Camp Rock">Camp Rock</a></i> as Caitlyn Gellar, an aspiring music producer. Her reception in <i>Camp Rock</i> was particularly strong, with various media outlets suggesting it was about time she played a main character role having backed up for other big names such as Will Smith and Eminem. Stoner starred as Alice McKinley in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Alice_Upside_Down" title="Alice Upside Down">Alice Upside Down</a></i>, based loosely on the Alice series, particularly <i>The Agony of Alice</i> by <a href="/wiki/Phyllis_Reynolds_Naylor" title="Phyllis Reynolds Naylor">Phyllis Reynolds Naylor</a>. This was her second starring role and, according to an interview that appears on the direct-to-DVD release, she states that this was the first time she has had to appear in every scene in a movie.</p>
<p>In 2008, she sang two songs, "Lost and Found" and "Free Spirit", from the soundtrack of the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Alice_Upside_Down" title="Alice Upside Down">Alice Upside Down</a>,</i> in which she plays the lead role. She has also recorded a cover version of the song <i><a href="/wiki/Dancing_in_the_Moonlight" title="Dancing in the Moonlight">Dancing in the Moonlight</a></i> for the 2009 Disney DVD/Blu-ray movie, <i><a href="/wiki/Space_Buddies" title="Space Buddies">Space Buddies</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2010, Stoner appeared in the third <i>Step Up</i> film <i><a href="/wiki/Step_Up_3D" title="Step Up 3D">Step Up 3D</a></i> opposite <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adam_Sevani" title="Adam Sevani">Adam Sevani</a> where she reprised the role. Stoner uploaded a video onto her official YouTube page of her final dance rehearsal which introduced other cast members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-etonline.com_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-etonline.com-4">[4]</a></sup> The same year, Stoner returned to reprise her role as Caitlyn Gellar in <i><a href="/wiki/Camp_Rock_2:_The_Final_Jam" title="Camp Rock 2: The Final Jam">Camp Rock 2: The Final Jam</a></i>, a sequel to <i>Camp Rock</i>. Stoner also guest-starred in the television drama series <i><a href="/wiki/House_(TV_series)" title="House (TV series)">House</a></i> as a skater.</p>
<p>In 2011 Stoner started working with <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriters</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Vocal_coach" title="Vocal coach">vocal coach</a> to change her vocal style from "kiddie-pop" to a more soulful pop vibe with a little alternative edge for her first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-youtube-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (August 2015)">not in citation given</span></a></i>]</sup> On April 4, 2010 Stoner posted a video to her <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> Channel announcing the release of her debut single, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flying_Forward" title="Flying Forward">Flying Forward</a>" on April 20, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-youtube-5">[5]</a></sup> Stoner released her debut EP entitled <i>Beat the System</i> in 2011. She also appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Wii_Fit" title="Wii Fit">Wii Fit</a></i> related videos on the Nintendo Channel on the <a href="/wiki/Wii" title="Wii">Wii</a> video game console demonstrating balance exercises. She also replaced <a href="/wiki/Hayden_Panettiere" title="Hayden Panettiere">Hayden Panettiere</a> as the voice of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kairi_(Kingdom_Hearts)" title="Kairi (Kingdom Hearts)">Kairi</a> in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_Hearts_Re:Chain_of_Memories" title="Kingdom Hearts Re:Chain of Memories">Kingdom Hearts Re:Chain of Memories</a></i> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xion_(Kingdom_Hearts)" title="Xion (Kingdom Hearts)">Xion</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Kingdom_Hearts_358/2_Days" title="Kingdom Hearts 358/2 Days">Kingdom Hearts 358/2 Days</a></i>. She voiced the character Opal in seasons three and four of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Korra" title="The Legend of Korra">The Legend of Korra</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ToledoFreePress_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ToledoFreePress-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_ventures">Other ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alyson_Stoner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dancing">Dancing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alyson_Stoner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Dancing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After choreography training in Los Angeles, Stoner appeared as a background dancer in several music videos, notably <a href="/wiki/Missy_Elliott" title="Missy Elliott">Missy Elliott</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Work_It_(Missy_Elliott_song)" title="Work It (Missy Elliott song)">Work It</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Gossip_Folks" title="Gossip Folks">Gossip Folks</a>", "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_Really_Hot" title="I'm Really Hot">I'm Really Hot</a>", <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Just_Lose_It" title="Just Lose It">Just Lose It</a>", and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/No_Tengo_Dinero_(Kumbia_Kings_song)" title="No Tengo Dinero (Kumbia Kings song)">No Tengo Dinero</a>" by the <a href="/wiki/Kumbia_Kings" title="Kumbia Kings">Kumbia Kings</a>. She has also been a back-up dancer for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OutKast" title="OutKast">OutKast</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2004_Kids%27_Choice_Awards" title="2004 Kids' Choice Awards">2004 Kids' Choice Awards</a> and for <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">Will Smith</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2005_Kids%27_Choice_Awards" title="2005 Kids' Choice Awards">2005 show</a>. Stoner danced with a dancing group called the JammXKids between 2003 and 2006. She quit the group in early 2006 due to scheduling conflicts, but continues to work with the individual kids on different jobs. She was also one of the dancers in the special features for the <i><a href="/wiki/Shark_Tale" title="Shark Tale">Shark Tale</a></i> DVD.</p>
<p>Stoner teaches <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> classes at <a href="/wiki/The_Millennium_Dance_Complex" title="The Millennium Dance Complex">the Millennium Dance Complex</a>, and is credited as the youngest person to teach a master class there. She also recently appeared in <a href="/wiki/Debby_Ryan" title="Debby Ryan">Debby Ryan</a>'s "We Got the Beat" for Disney Channel's <i>Radio Rebel</i>. In February 2015, she released a dance tribute video to Missy Elliot featuring a <a href="/wiki/Mashup_(music)" title="Mashup (music)">mash up</a> medley of Missy Elliot videos she had danced in when she was younger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube.com_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Within the first week, it received more than 12 million views.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube.com_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Publishing">Publishing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alyson_Stoner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Publishing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Stoner is the "dance editor" for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KEWL_Magazine" title="KEWL Magazine">KEWL Magazine</a></i>. In February 2009, Stoner released the <i>Alyson Stoner Project</i> described as "a dance video hybrid — melding many styles of entertainment into one package".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-myspace_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-myspace-9">[9]</a></sup> It was directed by <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Schmidt" title="Kevin Schmidt">Kevin Schmidt</a>, her co-star from <i><a href="/wiki/Cheaper_by_the_Dozen_(2003_film)" title="Cheaper by the Dozen (2003 film)">Cheaper by the Dozen</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-myspace_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-myspace-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
