<p><b>Allison Iraheta</b> (born April 27, 1992) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Singing" title="Singing">singer</a> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a>, who was the fourth place finalist on the <a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_8)" title="American Idol (season 8)">eighth season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scandal_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-scandal-2">[2]</a></sup> Prior to <i>Idol</i>, Iraheta won the <a href="/wiki/Telemundo" title="Telemundo">Telemundo</a> competition <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quincea%C3%B1era:_Mama_Quiero_Ser_Artista_(singing_competition)" title="Quinceañera: Mama Quiero Ser Artista (singing competition)">Quinceañera: Mamá Quiero Ser Artista</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-3">[3]</a></sup> Following the conclusion of <i>Idol</i>, Iraheta was signed to a record deal with <a href="/wiki/19_Entertainment" title="19 Entertainment">19 Entertainment</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew_jive_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ew_jive-4">[4]</a></sup> Her debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Just_Like_You_(Allison_Iraheta_album)" title="Just Like You (Allison Iraheta album)">Just Like You</a></i> was released on December 1, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv4_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtv4-5">[5]</a></sup> She is currently the lead singer in the band <a href="/wiki/Halo_Circus" title="Halo Circus">Halo Circus</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allison_Iraheta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Iraheta was born in <a href="/wiki/Glendale,_California" title="Glendale, California">Glendale, California</a>, to <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">Salvadoran</a> immigrants who moved to the United States before she was born.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-3">[3]</a></sup> She is the youngest in the family, with two older siblings, Jacki and Carlos Iraheta.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lat_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-lat-6">[6]</a></sup> According to her family, she has been singing since she was a toddler.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rsw_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-rsw-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2001, she began taking voice lessons with Raphael Enriquez at the Los Angeles Music and Art School, a non-profit community school of the arts in East Los Angeles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lamusart_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-lamusart-8">[8]</a></sup> Before rising to prominence, she was a frequent performer at the local Latin Electronics Store <i>La Curaçao</i> and the Los Angeles Music and Art School's annual benefit concert, <i>Stars for the Arts</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lat_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-lat-6">[6]</a></sup> Iraheta attended Los Angeles' Animo Ralph Bunche Charter High School.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv2_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtv2-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Quincea.C3.B1era:_Mam.C3.A1_Quiero_Ser_Artista_.282006-07.29"><i>Quinceañera: Mamá Quiero Ser Artista</i> (2006-07)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allison_Iraheta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Quinceañera: Mamá Quiero Ser Artista (2006-07)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2007, Iraheta won the reality show, <i>"<a href="/wiki/Quincea%C3%B1era" title="Quinceañera">Quinceañera</a>: Mama Quiero Ser Artista" (Sweet Fifteen: Mom, I Want to be an Artist)</i>, a reality TV singing competition that was produced and broadcast in 2006-2007 on <a href="/wiki/Telemundo" title="Telemundo">Telemundo</a>, where she sang in both <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the show, a group of teenage girls such as <a href="/wiki/Olivia_Bonilla" title="Olivia Bonilla">Olivia Bonilla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brissia_Mayagoitia" title="Brissia Mayagoitia">Brissia Mayagoitia</a>, and others of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_and_Latino_American" title="Hispanic and Latino American">Hispanic and Latino American</a> background competed in a singing competition for the main prize of $50,000 and a record deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv3_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtv3-10">[10]</a></sup> Iraheta was the first (and only) winner of the competition and received the grand prize, but due to legal problems of the show the recording contract was never finalized and the production of the show was closed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rickey_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-rickey-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-laprensagrafica_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-laprensagrafica-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="American_Idol_.282009.29"><i>American Idol</i> (2009)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allison_Iraheta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: American Idol (2009)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_8)" title="American Idol (season 8)">American Idol (season 8)</a></div>
<p>Iraheta auditioned for the <a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_8)" title="American Idol (season 8)">eighth season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a> in 2009. She sang "<a href="/wiki/(You_Make_Me_Feel_Like)_A_Natural_Woman" title="(You Make Me Feel Like) A Natural Woman">(You Make Me Feel Like) A Natural Woman</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Aretha_Franklin" title="Aretha Franklin">Aretha Franklin</a> and made it to the Hollywood audition rounds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-13">[13]</a></sup> The judges unanimously agreed to put her through to the semifinals, with <a href="/wiki/Simon_Cowell" title="Simon Cowell">Simon Cowell</a> saying "keep your eye on that one" and <a href="/wiki/Paula_Abdul" title="Paula Abdul">Paula Abdul</a> predicting that Iraheta would be a "dark horse" in the competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mb_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-mb-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Judges <a href="/wiki/Simon_Cowell" title="Simon Cowell">Simon Cowell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Randy_Jackson" title="Randy Jackson">Randy Jackson</a> declared Iraheta to be "one to watch in this competition"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-idol1_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-idol1-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-16">[16]</a></sup> after her rendition of Heart's "<a href="/wiki/Alone_(Heart_song)" title="Alone (Heart song)">Alone</a>" during the semifinals. For her performance, Iraheta earned unanimous praise from the judges, with Abdul praising her for being able to "sing the telephone book".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvn_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtvn-17">[17]</a></sup> Iraheta was the top vote-getting female of Group 2 and, along with <a href="/wiki/Kris_Allen" title="Kris Allen">Kris Allen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adam_Lambert" title="Adam Lambert">Adam Lambert</a>, advanced directly from the semifinals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zap_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-zap-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Iraheta kicked off her run in the finals with a well-received performance of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Give_In_to_Me" title="Give In to Me">Give In to Me</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv1_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtv1-19">[19]</a></sup> Once again, Abdul praised Iraheta for being a "rock star," with the rest of the panel in agreement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv1_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-mtv1-19">[19]</a></sup> The following week, Iraheta performed "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blame_It_On_Your_Heart" title="Blame It On Your Heart">Blame It On Your Heart</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Patty_Loveless" title="Patty Loveless">Patty Loveless</a>. Iraheta received rave reviews from the judges, but landed in the Bottom 3 for the first time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jv_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-jv-20">[20]</a></sup> On Motown Night, Iraheta performed a cover of the Temptations' "<a href="/wiki/Papa_Was_a_Rollin%27_Stone" title="Papa Was a Rollin' Stone">Papa Was a Rollin' Stone</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo-21">[21]</a></sup> The performance earned a standing ovation from mentor <a href="/wiki/Smokey_Robinson" title="Smokey Robinson">Smokey Robinson</a>, as well as further praise from the judges. DioGuardi enthusiastically lauded Iraheta for having a voice that "comes from God...you can't teach that! You sing like you've been singing for 400 years!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo-21">[21]</a></sup></p>

<p>On Top Downloads night, Iraheta performed No Doubt's "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Speak" title="Don't Speak">Don't Speak</a>" while accompanying herself on the guitar and ended up in the Bottom 3 again.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo1_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo1-22">[22]</a></sup> For her performance during Top 8 week, Iraheta sang Bonnie Raitt's "<a href="/wiki/I_Can%27t_Make_You_Love_Me" title="I Can't Make You Love Me">I Can't Make You Love Me</a>", for which she received much acclaim.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-et_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-et-23">[23]</a></sup> Jackson compared her to first season winner <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Clarkson" title="Kelly Clarkson">Kelly Clarkson</a>, and DioGuardi commented, "You know what? Let's go make a record!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-et_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-et-23">[23]</a></sup> After covering "<a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Want_to_Miss_a_Thing" title="I Don't Want to Miss a Thing">I Don't Want to Miss a Thing</a>" during Movie Night, Cowell boldly stated "We can see you all the way through the end now. You are the girls' only hope [of winning this competition.]"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-et1_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-et1-24">[24]</a></sup> Abdul compared her to fellow contestant <a href="/wiki/Adam_Lambert" title="Adam Lambert">Adam Lambert</a>, saying that the two possessed the same "special sauce."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-et1_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-et1-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the second Top 7 Night (due to the judges' save of <a href="/wiki/Matt_Giraud" title="Matt Giraud">Matt Giraud</a>), Iraheta performed a rock-infused version of Donna Summer's "<a href="/wiki/Hot_Stuff_(Donna_Summer_song)" title="Hot Stuff (Donna Summer song)">Hot Stuff</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sea_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sea-25">[25]</a></sup> Emerging on a glowing, red staircase, Iraheta was praised by Cowell for delivering "a brilliant performance".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sea_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sea-25">[25]</a></sup> DioGuardi also praised Iraheta by saying, "That was like a 9–10 for me!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sea_25-2"><a href="#cite_note-Sea-25">[25]</a></sup> The following night, Iraheta again landed in the bottom 3 for the third time, and <a href="/wiki/Lil_Rounds" title="Lil Rounds">Lil Rounds</a> was eliminated from the competition, leaving Iraheta as the last remaining female contestant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo2_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo2-26">[26]</a></sup> The next week, Iraheta performed a widely acclaimed cover of "<a href="/wiki/Someone_to_Watch_Over_Me_(song)" title="Someone to Watch Over Me (song)">Someone to Watch Over Me</a>" as part of the Rat Pack theme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usat1_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-usat1-27">[27]</a></sup> Jackson compared her vocal ability to <a href="/wiki/Pink_(singer)" title="Pink (singer)">Pink</a>, "but with 9,000 more octaves", while DioGuardi remarked, "you converted some fans tonight... If that doesn't land you in the finals, I don't know what will!".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usat1_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-usat1-27">[27]</a></sup> During the subsequent results show, it was revealed that Iraheta was among the Top 2 vote-getters (along with <a href="/wiki/Danny_Gokey" title="Danny Gokey">Danny Gokey</a>) for the night.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew1_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-ew1-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>The following week, Iraheta performed a solo version of "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cry_Baby_(song)" title="Cry Baby (song)">Cry Baby</a>" and closed the show with "<a href="/wiki/Slow_Ride" title="Slow Ride">Slow Ride</a>", a duet with <a href="/wiki/Adam_Lambert" title="Adam Lambert">Adam Lambert</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-et3_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-et3-29">[29]</a></sup> Regarding her performance of "Cry Baby", Cowell remarked that her growth throughout the competition was "staggering", and praised her for her "complete confidence and terrific vocals".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-et3_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-et3-29">[29]</a></sup> Her duet of "Slow Ride" with Lambert received further praise from the judges, with DioGuardi describing her as a "rock goddess".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-et3_29-2"><a href="#cite_note-et3-29">[29]</a></sup> Mentor <a href="/wiki/Slash_(musician)" title="Slash (musician)">Slash</a> commented that Iraheta possessed a better voice than most women "twice her age".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-av_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-av-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 6, 2009, Iraheta was eliminated from the competition in 4th place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scandal_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-scandal-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-travesty_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-travesty-31">[31]</a></sup> Slash tweeted that "Alison [sic] didn't deserve to be voted off AI so soon, she has amazing potential."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rs3_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-rs3-32">[32]</a></sup> Similarly, American Idol music director <a href="/wiki/Rickey_Minor" title="Rickey Minor">Rickey Minor</a> was "absolutely shocked" at her departure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-minor_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-minor-33">[33]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Just_Like_You_.282009-2012.29"><i>Just Like You</i> (2009-2012)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allison_Iraheta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Just Like You (2009-2012)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Just_Like_You_(Allison_Iraheta_album)" title="Just Like You (Allison Iraheta album)">Just Like You (Allison Iraheta album)</a></div>
<p>After her elimination, Iraheta made guest appearances on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live_with_Regis_and_Kelly" title="Live with Regis and Kelly">Live with Regis and Kelly</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zap2it_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-zap2it-34">[34]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ellen_DeGeneres_Show" title="The Ellen DeGeneres Show">The Ellen DeGeneres Show</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ellen1_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-ellen1-35">[35]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_with_Conan_O%27Brien" title="The Tonight Show with Conan O'Brien">The Tonight Show with Conan O'Brien</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tv.com_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-tv.com-36">[36]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Larry_King_Live" title="Larry King Live">Larry King Live</a></i>, as well as local Fox television affiliates<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kttv_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-kttv-37">[37]</a></sup> and numerous radio stations across the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filipinonline_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-filipinonline-38">[38]</a></sup> She performed on the Grand Finale of <a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a> <a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_8)" title="American Idol (season 8)">season 8</a>, including a group medley of "<a href="/wiki/So_What_(Pink_song)" title="So What (Pink song)">So What</a>" and a duet of "<a href="/wiki/Time_After_Time_(Cyndi_Lauper_song)" title="Time After Time (Cyndi Lauper song)">Time After Time</a>" with original artist <a href="/wiki/Cyndi_Lauper" title="Cyndi Lauper">Cyndi Lauper</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-ew-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>On June 9, 2009, following weeks of speculation, it was officially confirmed that Iraheta had signed a record deal with <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/19_Entertainment" title="19 Entertainment">19 Recordings</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew_jive_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-ew_jive-4">[4]</a></sup> Since Iraheta finished 4th place in <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> <a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_8)" title="American Idol (season 8)">season 8</a>, she participated in <a href="/wiki/American_Idols_LIVE!_Tour_2009" title="American Idols LIVE! Tour 2009">American Idols LIVE! Tour 2009</a>, performing "<a href="/wiki/So_What_(Pink_song)" title="So What (Pink song)">So What</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Pink_(singer)" title="Pink (singer)">Pink</a>, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cry_Baby_(song)" title="Cry Baby (song)">Cry Baby</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Janis_Joplin" title="Janis Joplin">Janis Joplin</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Barracuda_(song)" title="Barracuda (song)">Barracuda</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Heart_(band)" title="Heart (band)">Heart</a>, and "<a href="/wiki/Slow_Ride" title="Slow Ride">Slow Ride</a>" (with <a href="/wiki/Adam_Lambert" title="Adam Lambert">Adam Lambert</a>) by <a href="/wiki/Foghat" title="Foghat">Foghat</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-azcentral_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-azcentral-40">[40]</a></sup> Iraheta also performed "<a href="/wiki/Barracuda_(song)" title="Barracuda (song)">Barracuda</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Heart_(band)" title="Heart (band)">Heart</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_with_Conan_O%27Brien" title="The Tonight Show with Conan O'Brien">The Tonight Show with Conan O'Brien</a></i> on July 15, 2009 to promote the tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tv.com_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-tv.com-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo3_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo3-41">[41]</a></sup> In September 2009, Allison concluded the 50 city tour with her fellow Idol contestants, during which she began recording her debut album, <i>Just Like You</i>, under Jive Records, working with <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Rudolf" title="Kevin Rudolf">Kevin Rudolf</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Hodges" title="David Hodges">David Hodges</a> , Tommy Henriksen and <a href="/wiki/Max_Martin" title="Max Martin">Max Martin</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bialczak_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bialczak-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>Iraheta's debut album was released December 1, 2009. The lead single "Friday I'll Be Over U", produced by <a href="/wiki/Max_Martin" title="Max Martin">Max Martin</a>, debuted on October 5, 2009 and was made available through digital distribution on November 3, 2009. According to <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a>,</i> Jive expected Allison to sell 75,000 copies of her album in the first week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-citylinkmix_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-citylinkmix-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rockstarweekly_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-rockstarweekly-44">[44]</a></sup> The album sold approximately 32,000 copies in its first week, debuting at number 35 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200 album chart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboard_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-billboard-45">[45]</a></sup> Approximately 7,000 of the copies sold in the first week were sold digitally,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-46">[46]</a></sup> resulting in a debut at number 14 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">Billboard Digital Albums Chart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboard_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-billboard-45">[45]</a></sup> Allison's song "Pieces" was featured on the January 13th episode of American Idol.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-atlanta_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-atlanta-47">[47]</a></sup> Allison performed her second single "Scars" on American Idol's Season 9 Top 24 results show on February 25, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew2_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-ew2-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2010, it was announced Iraheta would be opening for <a href="/wiki/Adam_Lambert" title="Adam Lambert">Adam Lambert</a> on his 2010 <a href="/wiki/Glam_Nation_Tour" title="Glam Nation Tour">"GlamNation Tour"</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bialczak_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bialczak-42">[42]</a></sup> Allison and Australian guitarist <a href="/wiki/Orianthi" title="Orianthi">Orianthi</a> shared opening act duties on the initial North American leg of the tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zap2it2_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-zap2it2-49">[49]</a></sup> She performed songs from "Just Like You" for 71 of the 74 dates from June through September 2010. On the dates that included Orianthi, her typical set included "Holiday," "Scars/Don't Waste the Pretty" (with Orianthi usually playing lead guitar for the latter, as she does on the album), "Robot Love," a cover of <a href="/wiki/Pat_Benatar" title="Pat Benatar">Pat Benatar</a>'s "Heartbreaker" and "Friday I'll Be Over U." She would usually add three songs on the nights where she was the only opening act.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-setlist_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-setlist-50">[50]</a></sup> On Orianthi's final appearance on the tour, Iraheta and her band came onstage, playing air guitar and sweeping up with brooms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube1_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-youtube1-51">[51]</a></sup> For Iraheta's final date on the tour, September 21, 2010 in Puyallup, Washington, Adam Lambert and his dancers covered her with silly string and carried her offstage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube2_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-youtube2-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a July 2010 interview with Beatweek Magazine Iraheta stated that she would be writing more material for her second album and that it would be much more 'personal'. She stated, "So I think I'm gonna get more personal when I start writing."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digitalspy_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-digitalspy-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beatweek_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-beatweek-54">[54]</a></sup> Iraheta was dropped by record label <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a> in September 2010, due to her debut album underperforming. She was also dropped from 19 Entertainment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19_Entertainment_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-19_Entertainment-55">[55]</a></sup> She then began writing music for her second album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-about-56">[56]</a></sup> On March 1, 2012, one of Iraheta's new songs, titled "Self-Control", was featured in a video for Skaist-Taylor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube3_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-youtube3-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Halo_Circus_-_Formation_and_Debut_Album_.282013-Present.29"><i>Halo Circus - Formation and Debut Album</i> (2013-Present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allison_Iraheta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Halo Circus - Formation and Debut Album (2013-Present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 3, 2013, Iraheta announced on her Twitter account that she had formed a new band called <a href="/wiki/Halo_Circus" title="Halo Circus">Halo Circus</a>. The band consisted of Iraheta (vocals), Matthew Hager (bass, keyboards), David Immerman (guitars) and Valerie Franco (drums). The band marks a change of musical direction for Iraheta following a hiatus in 2012, a time which she used to "regroup, find myself and rediscover my voice." Halo Circus played their first gig at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Troubadour_(Los_Angeles)" title="The Troubadour (Los Angeles)">The Troubadour</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Hollywood,_California" title="West Hollywood, California">West Hollywood, California</a> on January 26, 2013. Iraheta eloped with bass player, <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Hager" title="Matthew Hager">Matthew Hager</a> the next day, January 27, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Hollywood_Reporter_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Hollywood_Reporter-58">[58]</a></sup> They played their second gig at <a href="/wiki/Whisky_a_Go_Go" title="Whisky a Go Go">Whisky a Go Go</a> on April 17<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alliholics1_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-Alliholics1-59">[59]</a></sup> and their third gig at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hotel_Cafe" title="Hotel Cafe">Hotel Cafe</a> on May 21, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alliholics2_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-Alliholics2-60">[60]</a></sup> On May 30, 2013 footage from their Hotel Cafe performance, along with an interview with Iraheta and Hager was featured on the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> Latino website. In the interview, Hager said "Allison happens to be one of the finest vocalists I've ever heard on every level".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NBC_Latino_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-NBC_Latino-61">[61]</a></sup> In June 2013 drummer Valerie Franco left the band and was replaced by Veronica Bellino.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-articlebio_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-articlebio-62">[62]</a></sup> In July 2013 the band signed a deal with the <a href="/wiki/Manimal_Vinyl" title="Manimal Vinyl">Manimal Vinyl</a> label,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rs_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-rs-63">[63]</a></sup> and released their first single, "Gone", via iTunes on October 8, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rs2_64-0"><a href="#cite_note-rs2-64">[64]</a></sup> On July 15, 2014, Halo Circus released their second single, a cover of Duran Duran's "Do You Believe in Shame?". This release was met to very positive reviews from critics, and was included in a Duran Duran tribute album, "Making Patterns Rhyme: A Tribute to Duran Duran."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-itunes_65-0"><a href="#cite_note-itunes-65">[65]</a></sup> This was available digitally on October 24, 2014 and released on vinyl by Manimal Records on October 28, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo4_66-0"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo4-66">[66]</a></sup> Their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Bunny_(album)" title="Bunny (album)">Bunny</a></i>, was released on June 23, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-muzoic_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-muzoic-67">[67]</a></sup> For the summer and fall of 2016, the band toured small clubs in the US.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tour_68-0"><a href="#cite_note-tour-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
