<p><b>Kevin Robert Kiley Jr.</b> (born April 28, 1981) is an American <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sports_commentator" title="Sports commentator">commentator</a>, best known for his tenure with <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a> under the ring name <b>Alex Riley</b>. He was a participant in the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_NXT_(TV_series)" title="WWE NXT (TV series)">NXT</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_wrestling_career">Professional wrestling career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Riley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Professional wrestling career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Wrestling_Entertainment.2FWWE">World Wrestling Entertainment/WWE</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Riley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: World Wrestling Entertainment/WWE">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Florida_Championship_Wrestling_.282007.E2.80.932010.29">Florida Championship Wrestling (2007â2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Riley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Florida Championship Wrestling (2007â2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>In 2007, Kiley signed a developmental contract with <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">World Wrestling Entertainment</a> (WWE) and was assigned to their <a href="/wiki/Farm_team#Professional_wrestling" title="Farm team">developmental territory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida_Championship_Wrestling" title="Florida Championship Wrestling">Florida Championship Wrestling</a> (FCW). On October 30, 2007, he debuted in a losing effort to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shawn_McGrath" title="Shawn McGrath">Shawn Osbourne</a> under his real name Kevin Kiley, and later faced off with wrestlers like <a href="/wiki/Heath_Slater" title="Heath Slater">Sebastian Slater</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steven_Lewington" title="Steven Lewington">Jack Gabriel</a>.</p>
<p>In September 2008, he changed his <a href="/wiki/Ring_name" title="Ring name">ring name</a> to <b>Carson Oakley</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> As Oakley, he began teaming with <a href="/wiki/Colt_Cabana" title="Colt Cabana">Scotty Goldman</a>, and the pairing unsuccessfully challenged <a href="/wiki/Tyson_Kidd" title="Tyson Kidd">TJ Wilson</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Hart_Smith" title="David Hart Smith">DH Smith</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Florida_Tag_Team_Championship" title="Florida Tag Team Championship">Florida Tag Team Championship</a> in November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In early December, he underwent another name change, calling himself <b>Alex Riley</b> and developing the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Gimmick" title="Glossary of professional wrestling terms">gimmick</a> of a university <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jock_(athlete)" title="Jock (athlete)">jock</a>, complete with a <a href="/wiki/Letterman_(sports)#Letterman_jacket" title="Letterman (sports)">letterman's jacket</a> as a part of his attire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marrero_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Marrero-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hedgepeth_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hedgepeth-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mullins_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mullins-10">[10]</a></sup> He gained Beverly Mullins as a <a href="/wiki/Manager_(professional_wrestling)" title="Manager (professional wrestling)">manager</a>, and to fit with his jock gimmick, Mullins adopted the gimmick of a <a href="/wiki/Prom" title="Prom">prom queen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mullins_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mullins-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, he joined FCW General Manager <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brian_Jossie" title="Brian Jossie">Abraham Washington</a>'s cabinet, being called Washington's "top draft pick", and as a result, was named the number one contender to <a href="/wiki/Tyler_Reks" title="Tyler Reks">Tyler Reks</a>' <a href="/wiki/FCW_Florida_Heavyweight_Championship" title="FCW Florida Heavyweight Championship">FCW Florida Heavyweight Championship</a> in July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cabinet_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-cabinet-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In August, he lost in a triple threat match including Reks and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Johnny_Curtis" title="Johnny Curtis">Johnny Curtis</a>. In mid-2009, he competed in several <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Dark_match" title="Glossary of professional wrestling terms">dark matches</a> prior to <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown" title="WWE SmackDown">SmackDown</a></i>, wrestling against <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Noble" title="Jamie Noble">Jamie Noble</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montel_Vontavious_Porter" title="Montel Vontavious Porter">Montel Vontavious Porter</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Yun" title="James Yun">Jimmy Wang Yang</a>, and also appeared at <a href="/wiki/House_show" title="House show">house shows</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWW_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-OWW-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hedgepeth_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hedgepeth-9">[9]</a></sup> On the August 30 episode of <i>FCW</i>, Riley dropped Mullins as his manager, calling her "dead weight".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cabinet_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-cabinet-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 18, 2010, Riley defeated <a href="/wiki/Justin_Gabriel" title="Justin Gabriel">Justin Gabriel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wade_Barrett" title="Wade Barrett">Wade Barrett</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Basic_non-elimination_matches" title="Professional wrestling match types">triple threat match</a> to win the FCW Florida Heavyweight Championship. After his title win he called out Barrett and paid him for his services as a hired help.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FCWchampionships_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-FCWchampionships-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> At the July 22 FCW tapings, Riley lost the title to <a href="/wiki/Mason_Ryan" title="Mason Ryan">Mason Ryan</a> in a triple threat match, also involving Johnny Curtis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FCWchampionships_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-FCWchampionships-13">[13]</a></sup> At the television tapings on September 22, Riley faced Ryan in a rematch, but lost by <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling#Submission" title="Professional wrestling">submission</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FCW10_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-FCW10-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="NXT_and_alliance_with_The_Miz_.282010.E2.80.932011.29">NXT and alliance with The Miz (2010â2011)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Riley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: NXT and alliance with The Miz (2010â2011)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On June 1, 2010, <a href="/wiki/The_Miz" title="The Miz">The Miz</a> announced that he would mentor Riley for the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_NXT_(TV_series)" title="WWE NXT (TV series)">NXT</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He made his <i>NXT</i> debut on the June 8 episode, but did not compete in a match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> He made his in-ring debut on the following edition of <i>NXT</i>, defeating <a href="/wiki/Low_Ki" title="Low Ki">Kaval</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NXT061510_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-NXT061510-19">[19]</a></sup> Kaval returned the favor two weeks later, by pinning Riley in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_tag_team_match_types#Multiple_man_teamed_matches" title="Professional wrestling tag team match types">six-man tag team match</a>. Later that night, Riley was ranked fourth in the first poll.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In the second poll on the July 27 episode of <i>NXT</i>, Riley moved up to third place, behind <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michael_McGillicutty" title="Michael McGillicutty">Michael McGillicutty</a> and Kaval.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On August 9 the rookies appeared in a six-man tag team match on <i>Raw</i>, in which Riley teamed with <a href="/wiki/Bray_Wyatt" title="Bray Wyatt">Husky Harris</a> and Michael McGillicutty to defeat <a href="/wiki/Lucky_Cannon" title="Lucky Cannon">Lucky Cannon</a>, Kaval and <a href="/wiki/Percy_Watson" title="Percy Watson">Percy Watson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The following night however, Riley's team lost a rematch, and he slipped to fifth place in the poll, narrowly avoiding elimination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Riley was eliminated from <i>NXT</i> in the season finale on August 31, ending up in third place overall. After Kaval was announced as the winner, Riley and the other eliminated rookies attacked him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>

<p>He appeared on the September 6 episode of <i>Raw</i>, when he tried to help his mentor The Miz, who had been placed in the LeBell Lock <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_holds" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> He appeared again the following week, replacing The Miz in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Variations_of_singles_matches" title="Professional wrestling match types">submission match</a> against Bryan, but lost.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> On the September 20 episode of <i>Raw</i> The Miz announced that he had signed Riley to a "personal services contract", allowing Riley to accompany The Miz to ringside and continue to appear on <i>Raw</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> As The Miz's protÃ©gÃ©, Riley interfered in his matches, including at the <a href="/wiki/Bragging_Rights_(2010)" title="Bragging Rights (2010)">Bragging Rights</a> <a href="/wiki/Pay-per-view" title="Pay-per-view">pay-per-view</a>, where Riley unsuccessfully attempted to help The Miz's team win.<sup class="reference" qualifying match on November 22 as a result.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The following week, Riley interfered in the <a href="/wiki/Tables,_Ladders,_and_Chairs_match" title="Tables, Ladders, and Chairs match">Tables, Ladders, and Chairs match</a> for the <a <p>On the February 28 episode of <i>Raw</i>, John Cena challenged Riley to a match which, if Cena won, Riley would no longer be The Miz's apprentice. The Miz accepted the match on Riley's behalf, and later that night, Cena and Riley faced off in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steel_cage_match" title="Steel cage match">steel cage match</a>. Riley lost the match despite interference from The Miz.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The angle was used to write Riley off television as he was scheduled to be sent back to Florida Championship Wrestling to be repackaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Despite this, Riley appeared on the March 14 episode of <i>Raw</i>, interfering in The Miz's match against <a href="/wiki/The_Great_Khali" title="The Great Khali">The Great Khali</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The following week, Riley announced that The Miz had re-hired him, this time as The Miz's <a href="/wiki/Vice_president" title="Vice president">VP</a> of Corporate Communications.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> He accompanied The Miz to his match at <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_XXVII" title="WrestleMania XXVII">WrestleMania XXVII</a> against Cena where he interfered several times, throwing Cena into an exposed turnbuckle, attacking him with his briefcase, and distracting the <a href="/wiki/Referee_(professional_wrestling)" title="Referee (professional wrestling)">referee</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles_competition_.282011.E2.80.932013.29">Singles competition (2011â2013)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Riley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Singles competition (2011â2013)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>As part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011_WWE_Draft#Supplemental_draft" title="2011 WWE Draft">2011 Supplemental Draft</a> on April 26, Riley was moved to the <a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown" title="WWE SmackDown">SmackDown</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_Brand_Extension" title="WWE Brand Extension">brand</a>, separating him from The Miz.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Riley debuted on the April 29 episode of <i>SmackDown</i> by interrupting Randy Orton, but had an <i><a href="/wiki/Cutter_(professional_wrestling)#Jumping_cutter" title="Cutter (professional wrestling)">RKO</a></i> performed on him before he could speak.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Despite being drafted, Riley continued to appear on <i>Raw</i> alongside The Miz. On the May 2 episode of <i>Raw</i>, The Miz chastised Riley not being at ringside at <a href="/wiki/Extreme_Rules_(2011)" title="Extreme Rules (2011)">Extreme Rules</a> the previous night when The Miz had lost the WWE Championship to Cena.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Riley appeared alongside The Miz at the <a href="/wiki/Over_the_Limit_(2011)" title="Over the Limit (2011)">Over the Limit</a> pay-per-view and attempted to help The Miz win his <a href="/wiki/%22I_Quit%22_match" title='"I Quit" match'>"I Quit" match</a> against Cena, but The Miz was unsuccessful and failed to regain the WWE Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> The following night on the May 23 episode of <i>Raw</i>, The Miz's request for another WWE Championship match was immediately denied and The Miz blamed him. Riley proceeded to attack The Miz, and turning <a href="/wiki/Face_(professional_wrestling)" title="Face (professional wrestling)">face</a> in the process.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MizFire_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-MizFire-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MizFire_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-MizFire-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> The following week, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Cole_(wrestling)" title="Michael Cole (wrestling)">Michael Cole</a> announced that Riley had been rehired by the <a href="/wiki/Anonymous_Raw_General_Manager" title="Anonymous Raw General Manager">Anonymous Raw General Manager</a> and he returned to Raw, as in storyline he had not been eligible to be drafted as he was under contract to The Miz, not WWE. Following an argument Riley attacked Cole, before chasing away The Miz who had tried to attack him from behind.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the June 6 episode of <i>Raw</i>, Riley teamed with John Cena to face <a href="/wiki/Ron_Killings" title="Ron Killings">R-Truth</a> and The Miz, with <a href="/wiki/Stone_Cold_Steve_Austin" title="Stone Cold Steve Austin">Steve Austin</a> as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Special_guest_referee" title="Special guest referee">special guest referee</a> but were defeated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> On the same show, it was confirmed that Riley would face The Miz at <a href="/wiki/WWE_Capitol_Punishment" title="WWE Capitol Punishment">Capitol Punishment</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> At the pay-per-view, Riley defeated The Miz.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cappun_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-cappun-47">[47]</a></sup> The following night on <i>Raw</i>, Riley teamed with John Cena and <a href="/wiki/Randy_Orton" title="Randy Orton">Randy Orton</a> to take on The Miz, R-Truth and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_(wrestler)" title="Christian (wrestler)">Christian</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_tag_team_match_types#Elimination_tag_team_matches" title="Professional wrestling tag team match types">six-man tag team elimination</a> match. Riley was the first person eliminated, but his team went on to win the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Money_in_the_Bank_(2011)" title="Money in the Bank (2011)">Money in the Bank</a>, Riley competed in a <a href="/wiki/Money_in_the_Bank_ladder_match" title="Money in the Bank ladder match">Money in the Bank ladder match</a> which was won by last year's winner and his former pro The Miz .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> The following night on <i>Raw</i>, Riley was one of the eight men placed in the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Championship" title="WWE Championship">WWE Championship</a> tournament but lost to The Miz in the first round, ending their feud.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>

<p>On the August 1 episode of <i>Raw</i>, Riley began a feud with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_United_States_Champion" title="WWE United States Champion">United States Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Dolph_Ziggler" title="Dolph Ziggler">Dolph Ziggler</a>, beginning with a verbal confrontation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Riley and Ziggler faced off the following week, with Riley winning by disqualification after <a href="/wiki/Vickie_Guerrero" title="Vickie Guerrero">Vickie Guerrero</a>, Ziggler's manager, slapped him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> On the August 15 episode of <i>Raw</i>, Riley lost to <a href="/wiki/Jack_Swagger" title="Jack Swagger">Jack Swagger</a> due to interference by Guerrero, who also managed Swagger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> Riley defeated Swagger in a rematch the following week after Swagger was distracted by Guerrero and Ziggler arguing at ringside.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Night_of_Champions_(2011)" title="Night of Champions (2011)">Night of Champions</a>, Riley competed in a fatal four-way match for the United States Championship which also involved Ziggler, Swagger and <a href="/wiki/John_Morrison_(wrestler)" title="John Morrison (wrestler)">John Morrison</a>, in which Ziggler retained his title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> In October, Riley suffered a legitimate hip injury that left him inactive for several weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> For the remainder of 2011 after his return, Riley was largely relegated to appearing on <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Superstars" title="WWE Superstars">Superstars</a></i>, defeating the likes of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drew_McIntyre" title="Drew McIntyre">Drew McIntyre</a> and <a href="/wiki/JTG" title="JTG">JTG</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, Riley had a 14-match losing streak, losing to the likes of <a href="/wiki/Brodus_Clay" title="Brodus Clay">Brodus Clay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Matt_Bloom" title="Matt Bloom">Lord Tensai</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heath_Slater" title="Heath Slater">Heath Slater</a>. Riley entered the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Rumble_(2012)" title="Royal Rumble (2012)">2012 Royal Rumble</a> as the second entrant, and was eliminated first by The Miz.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> Riley claimed a rare victory on the May 2 episode of <i>NXT</i> when he teamed with <a href="/wiki/Tyson_Kidd" title="Tyson Kidd">Tyson Kidd</a> to beat JTG and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Johnny_Curtis" title="Johnny Curtis">Johnny Curtis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> After several months absence from television, he made his return on the July 3 episode of <i>SmackDown</i> in a <a href="/wiki/World_Heavyweight_Championship_(WWE)" title="World Heavyweight Championship (WWE)">World Heavyweight Championship</a> <a href="/wiki/Money_in_the_Bank_(2012)" title="Money in the Bank (2012)">Money in the Bank</a> qualifying match against Dolph Ziggler, in a losing effort. Riley ended his singles losing streak on August 6 episode of <i>Raw</i> by defeating Ziggler with a <a href="/wiki/Pin_(professional_wrestling)#Roll-up" title="Pin (professional wrestling)">roll-up</a> after a distraction from <a href="/wiki/Chris_Jericho" title="Chris Jericho">Chris Jericho</a> on commentary. On September 21, Riley announced that he was undergoing elbow and knee surgery. In January 2013, Riley teamed up with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Derrick_Bateman" title="Derrick Bateman">Derrick Bateman</a> to take part in the <a href="/wiki/NXT_Tag_Team_Championship#Tournament" title="NXT Tag Team Championship">NXT Tag Team Championship Tournament</a>, and the two were defeated by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Hero" title="Chris Hero">Kassius Ohno</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leo_Kruger" title="Leo Kruger">Leo Kruger</a> in the first round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> On the January 21, 2013 episode of <i>Raw</i> (the final episode before the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Rumble_(2013)" title="Royal Rumble (2013)">Royal Rumble</a>), Riley made his first televised appearance in 5 months as part of a show-closing brawl.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> Riley next appeared on the April 17 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Main_Event" title="WWE Main Event">Main Event</a></i>, competing in an 11-man battle royal to determine the number 1 contender for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intercontinental_Championship" title="Intercontinental Championship">Intercontinental Championship</a> and surviving until the final four when he was eliminated by <a href="/wiki/Primo_(wrestler)" title="Primo (wrestler)">Primo</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Color_commentary_.282013.E2.80.932015.29">Color commentary (2013â2015)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Riley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Color commentary (2013â2015)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Following his lack of in-ring success, Riley transitioned to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Color_commentary" title="Color commentary">color commentary</a> role. At the June 24 <i>Raw</i> taping, Riley debuted as the new color commentator of <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Superstars" title="WWE Superstars">Superstars</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> Riley became a regular commentator for <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_NXT" title="WWE NXT">NXT</a></i> starting at the July 11 tapings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> He also frequently served as color commentator on <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Main_Event" title="WWE Main Event">Main Event</a></i>.</p>
<p>Following the launch of the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Network" title="WWE Network">WWE Network</a>, Riley became known as "The Analyst" Alex Riley and adopted a persona similar to a sports commentator or agent. Riley was a regular member of the expert panel on both the <i>Raw</i> and <i>SmackDown</i> pre-shows on the WWE Network, and also made several appearances on pay-per-view in this role.</p>
<p>In 2014, Riley made several in-ring appearances at WWE <a href="/wiki/House_show" title="House show">live events</a>, picking up untelevised wins over the likes of <a href="/wiki/Tyson_Kidd" title="Tyson Kidd">Tyson Kidd</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heath_Slater" title="Heath Slater">Heath Slater</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_in-ring_competition_.282015.E2.80.932016.29">Return to in-ring competition (2015â2016)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Riley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Return to in-ring competition (2015â2016)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Throughout late 2014 and early 2015, Riley started a <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> campaign with the <a href="/wiki/Hashtag" title="Hashtag">hashtag</a> #FreeRiley, apparently in an attempt to get management to notice him and book him as an in-ring competitor, given that his last televised match was in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> After a storyline which involved him being harassed on commentary by <a href="/wiki/NXT_Championship" title="NXT Championship">NXT Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Owens" title="Kevin Owens">Kevin Owens</a>, Riley returned to televised competition on the March 11 edition of <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_NXT" title="WWE NXT">NXT</a></i>, defeating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CJ_Parker_(wrestler)" title="CJ Parker (wrestler)">CJ Parker</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> After the match, Owens called Riley "the dumbest man in NXT" for returning to the ring and threatened to destroy his career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> On March 29 at <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_31" title="WrestleMania 31">WrestleMania 31</a>, Riley competed in the AndrÃ© The Giant Memorial Battle Royal but was eliminated by <a href="/wiki/Damien_Sandow" title="Damien Sandow">Damien Mizdow</a>. Kevin Owens attacked Riley during an NXT taping in April 2015, and on May 2, 2015 it was announced that Riley would undergo surgery, to fix the degenerative arthrosis in his knee.</p>
<p>Riley made his return from injury on the January 27, 2016 episode of <i>NXT</i> after eight months of inactivity, sporting longer hair and beard, in a winning effort against <a href="/wiki/Bull_Dempsey" title="Bull Dempsey">Bull Dempsey</a>, turning <a href="/wiki/Heel_(professional_wrestling)" title="Heel (professional wrestling)">heel</a> for the first time since 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> Next 3 weeks, he was defeated by <a href="/wiki/Tye_Dillinger" title="Tye Dillinger">Tye Dillinger</a>. On March 31 episode of NXT, he was defeated by <a href="/wiki/Apollo_Crews" title="Apollo Crews">Apollo Crews</a>. On May 11 episode of NXT taping, Riley worked his last WWE match where he was defeated by <a href="/wiki/Shinsuke_Nakamura" title="Shinsuke Nakamura">Shinsuke Nakamura</a>.</p>
<p>Riley was released from his WWE contract along with several other wrestlers on May 6, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-futureendeavors_68-0"><a href="#cite_note-futureendeavors-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Riley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Riley's father, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Kiley_(sportscaster)" title="Kevin Kiley (sportscaster)">Kevin Kiley, Sr.</a>, is a sports reporter and commentator. His mother, Lauren (nÃ©e Riley), is a former <a href="/wiki/Miss_Virginia" title="Miss Virginia">Miss Virginia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WWE_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-WWE-5">[5]</a></sup> He has a younger brother named Kristopher.</p>
<p>Riley attended <a href="/wiki/Robinson_Secondary_School" title="Robinson Secondary School">Robinson Secondary School</a> in Fairfax, VA, where he played both <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">football</a> and <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-boston_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-boston-2">[2]</a></sup> He then went to <a href="/wiki/Boston_College" title="Boston College">Boston College</a>, where he majored in communications.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-boston_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-boston-2">[2]</a></sup> He played for the <a href="/wiki/Boston_College_Eagles_football" title="Boston College Eagles football">Boston College Eagles football</a> team, initially as a <a href="/wiki/Quarterback" title="Quarterback">quarterback</a>, before becoming a <a href="/wiki/Linebacker" title="Linebacker">linebacker</a> during the 2001 season he has also played for the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Football_League" title="Canadian Football League">Canadian Football League</a> <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Alouettes" title="Montreal Alouettes">Montreal Alouettes</a>.</p>
<p>Riley once had a job selling medical equipment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> He is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catholic" title="Catholic">Catholic</a> and has a tattoo of a Celtic cross on his back.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-myspace.com_70-0"><a href="#cite_note-myspace.com-70">[70]</a></sup> He was arrested for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DUI" title="DUI">DUI</a> in Florida in November 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> yet the charges were dismissed in July 2011 after <a href="/wiki/Hillsborough_County,_Florida" title="Hillsborough County, Florida">Hillsborough County</a> Circuit Judge Dick Greco Jr. ruled that law enforcement did not have probable cause to stop Riley's vehicle and thus violated his <a href="/wiki/Fourth_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution" title="Fourth Amendment to the United States Constitution">fourth amendment</a> rights against unreasonable searches and seizures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
