<p><b>Alexander Max "Alex" Band</b> (born June 8, 1981) is an American <a href="/wiki/Musician" title="Musician">musician</a> and <a href="/wiki/Singer-songwriter" title="Singer-songwriter">singer-songwriter</a>, best known for his work in his former band <a href="/wiki/The_Calling" title="The Calling">The Calling</a> and their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hit_song" title="Hit song">hit song</a> "<a href="/wiki/Wherever_You_Will_Go" title="Wherever You Will Go">Wherever You Will Go</a>", which topped the <a href="/wiki/Adult_Top_40" title="Adult Top 40">Adult Top 40</a> for 23 weeks.</p>
<p>Along with bandmate and fellow songwriter <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aaron_Kamin" title="Aaron Kamin">Aaron Kamin</a> as part of The Calling, the pair achieved success with the release of their two studio albums; <i><a href="/wiki/Camino_Palmero" title="Camino Palmero">Camino Palmero</a></i> (2001) and <i><a href="/wiki/Two_(The_Calling_album)" title="Two (The Calling album)">Two</a></i> (2004). They also achieved top 40 chartings with singles "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adrienne_(song)" title="Adrienne (song)">Adrienne</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Our_Lives" title="Our Lives">Our Lives</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Things_Will_Go_My_Way" title="Things Will Go My Way">Things Will Go My Way</a>". As a solo artist he is well known for providing the vocals on the top 10 hit, "<a href="/wiki/Why_Don%27t_You_%26_I" title="Why Don't You &amp; I">Why Don't You &amp; I</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Santana" title="Carlos Santana">Carlos Santana</a> in 2004. In 2008 he created his own <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a>, AMB Records and released a 5-song EP entitled, <i>Alex Band EP</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Band later released his debut full-length album, <i><a href="/wiki/We%27ve_All_Been_There" title="We've All Been There">We've All Been There</a></i> in June 2010. He currently resides in Los Angeles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Band&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alex Band was born in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, into an <a href="/wiki/Entertainment" title="Entertainment">entertainment</a> family. His mother is Meda Band, and his father, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Band" title="Charles Band">Charles Band</a>, is a well known <a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">horror film</a> <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">director</a>. His grandfather <a href="/wiki/Albert_Band" title="Albert Band">Albert Band</a> was also another well known <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">director</a>. Band's father is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jewish_people" title="Jewish people">Jewish</a> and his mother is <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-3">[3]</a></sup> Both his parents were raised in Italy and they owned a castle in <a href="/wiki/Giove,_Umbria" title="Giove, Umbria">Giove</a>, <a href="/wiki/Umbria" title="Umbria">Umbria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Band explores spiritual themes in several of his songs though claims to be of no particular faith himself. Of the song "Please" Band said, "...I grew up with a Jewish Father who didn't practice and a Christian Mother whose only tie to Jesus was celebrating Christmas. Needless to say I had to find my own religion. And in this song I'm asking God what that should be...what should I believe? In a world so full of different religions and so many people fighting over whose belief is the correct one, it frustrates me to think that they all really boil down to the same thing."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>At a young age, Band's parents divorced and his father remarried. He grew up with two of his half brothers Harlan and Zalman, and his sister <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Taryn_Band&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Taryn Band (page does not exist)">Taryn</a>. After his parents divorce, his mother moved to Germany where she remarried and still resides. Band was about 8 at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She had 3 sons after her move, who Band eventually met when he toured Germany. The single "<a href="/wiki/Could_It_Be_Any_Harder" title="Could It Be Any Harder">Could It Be Any Harder</a>" explains some of his feelings towards his mother's move.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Growing up Band made small appearances in his father's films. He grew up on Camino Palmero street, which would later inspire his <a href="/wiki/Camino_Palmero" title="Camino Palmero">first album of the same name</a>. He began playing guitar and song writing at the age of 8.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AB-Facts_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-AB-Facts-6">[6]</a></sup> Musically, Band was influenced by <a href="/wiki/Pearl_Jam" title="Pearl Jam">Pearl Jam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bon_Jovi" title="Bon Jovi">Bon Jovi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Live_(band)" title="Live (band)">Live</a>, <a href="/wiki/Train_(band)" title="Train (band)">Train</a>, and <a href="/wiki/U2" title="U2">U2</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>He soon formed his first band with friend and filmmaker, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jethro_Rothe-Kushel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jethro Rothe-Kushel (page does not exist)">Jethro Rothe-Kushel</a>, called "Maybe Solitude". Rothe-Kushel also directed his first music video for a song called "Dormant Prayer". Several bands followed including one named "Generation Gap" that consisted of him, Aaron Kamin, a musician he met when Kamin began dating his sister, and a few much older musicians. The band has a brief cameo in <a href="/wiki/Coyote_Ugly_(film)" title="Coyote Ugly (film)">Coyote Ugly</a>. At the age of 14 he had a <a href="/wiki/Kokopelli" title="Kokopelli">kokopelli</a> tattooed on his wrist. This would later become a symbol for <a href="/wiki/The_Calling" title="The Calling">The Calling</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AB-Facts_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-AB-Facts-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Band&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Calling">The Calling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Band&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Calling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the age of 15 Band signed with <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a> with the band he had created with Kamin now named <a href="/wiki/The_Calling" title="The Calling">The Calling</a>. At this time he dropped out of school though he later received a diploma through home schooling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AB-Facts_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-AB-Facts-6">[6]</a></sup> The Calling released their first single "<a href="/wiki/Wherever_You_Will_Go" title="Wherever You Will Go">Wherever You Will Go</a>" to much acclaim. The song soon reached number 1 on the Billboard Hot AC charts for 23 weeks and peaked at number 5 on the Hot 100 charts, as well as becoming an international hit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The band's first album <i><a href="/wiki/Camino_Palmero" title="Camino Palmero">Camino Palmero</a></i> soon followed peaking at number 36 on the Billboard Sales charts and eventually went Platinum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The band's next singles, "Adrienne" and "Could It Be Any Harder" soon followed charting in the AC top 40. However, by 2004 the second album titled, <i><a href="/wiki/Two_(The_Calling_album)" title="Two (The Calling album)">Two</a></i> failed to live up to expectations charting at number 54 on Billboard Top 200.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The album suffered from a lack of label support though the singles fared slightly better. The first single, "Our Lives" was featured in the 2004 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympics" title="Olympics">Olympics</a> ceremony, and charted at number 34 in the Billboard top 40.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-11">[11]</a></sup> A final single, "Anything" performed decently with little promotion, charting at number 23 in the Adult Top 40.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the lack of label support with "Two", the band announced a hiatus. On June 4, 2005 they performed their last concert to date in Temecula, CA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Songwriting">Songwriting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Band&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Songwriting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During and after his time in The Calling, Band and Kamin co-wrote all of the band's songs. They also wrote songs for several other artists as well as for several movie soundtracks, most of which Band would perform on. One of the first such tracks was the single, "For You" which appeared on the <i><a href="/wiki/Daredevil_(film)" title="Daredevil (film)">Daredevil</a></i> soundtrack. The OST peaked at number 9 on the Billboard Top 200.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>A cover of "<a href="/wiki/Keep_Your_Hands_to_Yourself" title="Keep Your Hands to Yourself">Keep Your Hands to Yourself</a>" was recorded for the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Sweet_Home_Alabama_(film)" title="Sweet Home Alabama (film)">Sweet Home Alabama</a></i>. Band recorded a song "Take Hold of Me" for use in his wife's movie <i>Fish Without a Bicycle</i> in which he has a small part.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He also Appeared on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bratz:_Motion_Picture_Soundtrack" title="Bratz: Motion Picture Soundtrack">Bratz: Motion Picture Soundtrack</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_career">Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Band&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Band's solo career started long before the hiatus of The Calling. He was personally chosen by <a href="/wiki/Chad_Kroeger" title="Chad Kroeger">Chad Kroeger</a> to perform on the <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Santana" title="Carlos Santana">Carlos Santana</a> single "<a href="/wiki/Why_Don%27t_You_%26_I" title="Why Don't You &amp; I">Why Don't You &amp; I</a>" in 2003. Originally, Kroeger <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">wrote</a> and sang on "Why Don't You &amp; I" for <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Santana" title="Carlos Santana">Santana</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Shaman_(album)" title="Shaman (album)">Shaman</a></i>. However, when <a href="/wiki/Arista_Records" title="Arista Records">Arista Records</a> decided to release the song as a single, Kroeger's <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a> refused permission, citing concerns that Kroeger appearing on a "high-profile single" that could conflict with his own newly released comeback album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Band's version became extremely successful charting at number 3 on the Billboard Top 40, and number 8 on the Billboard Top 100. It was also recently named the number 5 song of the decade on the Adult Pop charts by Billboard. It reached number 1 on the AC charts and continues to be played on AC stations to this day. Band's version appears on Santana's special release album, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ceremony_(Santana)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ceremony (Santana) (page does not exist)">Ceremony: remixes &amp; rarities</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Santana" title="Ultimate Santana">Ultimate Santana</a></i>. Band also contributed the song "The Truth" to the soundtrack of the 2005 movie "<a href="/wiki/Doll_Graveyard" title="Doll Graveyard">Doll Graveyard</a>" which was directed by his father.</p>
<p>In November 2005 it was announced Band had signed as a solo artist with <a href="/wiki/Ron_Fair" title="Ron Fair">Ron Fair</a> as part of <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Geffen Records</a>. A new album had been recorded and was said to be released in late 2007, but was delayed several times. Band left the Geffen label in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>During this time Band had a few releases. One of his songs titled, "It Doesn't Get Better Than This" for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bratz:_The_Movie" title="Bratz: The Movie">Bratz: The Movie</a></i> soundtrack. Band performed and co-wrote the song, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coming_Home_(Alex_Band_song)" title="Coming Home (Alex Band song)">Coming Home</a>" which was featured in the film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Final_Season" title="The Final Season">The Final Season</a></i> in late 2007.</p>
<p>In October 2007 Band performed at the first Alex Band's Donate Life Rocks charity concert for "Donate Life America" in Los Angeles, CA. The concert was taped and will air on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DISH_Network" title="DISH Network">DISH Network</a>. Band also taped an appearance on <a href="/wiki/The_Real_World:_Hollywood" title="The Real World: Hollywood">The Real World: Hollywood</a> which filmed at the concert. The second Alex Band Donate Life concert was held in Richmond, VA on August 21, 2008. The first music video in his solo career was again directed by lifelong friend and director / producer, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jethro_Rothe-Kushel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jethro Rothe-Kushel (page does not exist)">Jethro Rothe-Kushel</a>, for a song called "Only One".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Indie_Label">Indie Label</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Band&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Indie Label">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Band expressed displeasure with the continued delayed release of his debut album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On April 16, 2008 Band announced via his official fan site that he had left Geffen and created his own label called AMB Records under which the album in the US will be released. He will work with EMI for the international release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-19">[19]</a></sup> <i>"I've been working on leaving my label for a while now, and moving to somewhere else to put this record out finally and give it the push it deserves...and I'm happy to announce that I will be putting the record out ON MY OWN! With help from good friends in the business and an amazing team, I'm putting together my own Label. This gives me the freedom to make my own decisions, to invest in myself, and to do what I want, when I want. This is the way to go these days in the music business and I am sooooooo lucky to be able to do it. There are no firm details as to when exactly the record will be released, but I am doing everything in my power to make the process as speedy as possible. I will let you all know of course as soon as I find out more."</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 25, 2008 Band released a 5-song EP titled, "Alex Band EP". The EP contained 4 new songs in addition to the song "Coming Home". The EP is sold via his official fan site. A new version of the EP was created for his tour in Brazil. On May 13 it was announced that the track, "Only One" would be the official single, for Brazil only, with both single and video available through Band's official fan site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> His debut solo album "<a href="/wiki/We%27ve_All_Been_There" title="We've All Been There">We've All Been There</a>" was released on June 29, 2010. The first single "Tonight" was released on February 15, 2010.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Band&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Band appeared in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Coyote_Ugly_(film)" title="Coyote Ugly (film)">Coyote Ugly</a></i> as a musician on the stage of the club.</p>
<p>Band also appeared on the <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_NY" title="CSI: NY">CSI: NY</a></i> episode "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Help_(CSI:_NY_episode)" title="Help (CSI: NY episode)">Help</a>", which aired January 14, 2009. Band played a drugged-out musician who is the suspect in a murder case. The songs he performed were his own (as did fellow guest star <a href="/wiki/Bonnie_McKee" title="Bonnie McKee">Bonnie McKee</a>). He has said there will be more acting in the future.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Band&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Band married girlfriend, actress <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Sky" title="Jennifer Sky">Jennifer Sky</a>, on July 25, 2004. The couple split in 2009. In February 2010, he became engaged to Kristin Blanford. She appeared in music videos for his songs "Tonight" (2010)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> and "Euphoria" (2011). They married on May 1, 2011 and divorced in April 2012. Band married girlfriend, Shayna Weber, in October 2013 which was confirmed on the Twitter account of each. Via Twitter, Alex confirmed in early 2016 that he and Shayna are expecting their first child in the summer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Their son, Max Bowie Band, was born on September 3rd, 2016. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Band was reportedly abducted on August 18, 2013 by two men that robbed, beat him severely, and dumped him on train tracks in <a href="/wiki/Lapeer,_Michigan" title="Lapeer, Michigan">Lapeer, Michigan</a>. He was taken to an emergency room at a nearby hospital where he was treated and released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Charity_work">Charity work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Band&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Charity work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Band has worked with several charities. After an illness his ex-wife dealt with, Band became a supporter of <a href="/wiki/Organ_donation" title="Organ donation">organ donation</a>, working closely with the charity <a href="/wiki/Donate_Life_America" title="Donate Life America">Donate Life America</a>. Annually he performs at "Alex Band's Donate Life Rocks Concert" to raise money and awareness for the cause.</p>
<p>Each year Debbie Hagerman, who ran his official fan site, organized a birthday charity project in Band's name, asking that, in lieu of gifts, fans donate to a charity instead. In addition a calendar was produced each year showcasing Band in concert. All proceeds went to The Alex Band Education Fund for <a href="/wiki/Donate_Life_America" title="Donate Life America">Donate Life America</a>. The projects have raised over $30,000 in honor of Band.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Band is also active in promoting awareness and involvement in <a href="/wiki/The_Michael_J._Fox_Foundation" title="The Michael J. Fox Foundation">The Michael J Fox Foundation for Parkinson's Disease</a>. He himself was diagnosed in 2012 with young onset Parkinson's, which he announced during a benefit show performed on Stageit from Ryan Cabrera's home on March 23, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
