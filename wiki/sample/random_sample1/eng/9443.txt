<p><b>Alexzandra Spencer "Alexz" Johnson</b> (born November 4, 1986<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Official_Website_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Official_Website-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-All_Music_Biography_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-All_Music_Biography-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TV_Guide_Profile_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-TV_Guide_Profile-3">[3]</a></sup>) is a Canadian singer-songwriter, record producer, actress and philanthropist. Her debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Voodoo_(Alexz_Johnson_album)" title="Voodoo (Alexz Johnson album)">Voodoo</a></i> was independently released in 2010, followed by the demo release of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Basement_Recordings" title="The Basement Recordings">The Basement Recordings</a></i> in 2011, with its sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Basement_Recordings_II" title="The Basement Recordings II">The Basement Recordings II</a></i>, and EP <i><a href="/wiki/Skipping_Stone" title="Skipping Stone">Skipping Stone</a></i> in 2012. A follow-up extended play <i>Heart</i> followed in 2014, with her sophomore effort <i>Let 'Em Eat Cake</i> being released October of that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Interview_with_Alexz_Johnson_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Interview_with_Alexz_Johnson-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>She is best known for her roles as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jude_Harrison" title="Jude Harrison">Jude Harrison</a> in the <a href="/wiki/CTV_Television_Network" title="CTV Television Network">CTV</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Instant_Star" title="Instant Star">Instant Star</a></i>, as Annie Thelen in the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/So_Weird" title="So Weird">So Weird</a></i>, and as Erin Ulmer in the 2006 horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Final_Destination_3" title="Final Destination 3">Final Destination 3</a></i>. She was nominated three times for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gemini_Award" title="Gemini Award">Gemini Award</a> for Best Performance in a Children's or Youth Program or Series (<i>Instant Star</i>), winning the award in 2008.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexz_Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Johnson was born in <a href="/wiki/New_Westminster" title="New Westminster">New Westminster</a>, British Columbia and raised in nearby <a href="/wiki/Coquitlam" title="Coquitlam">Coquitlam</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The sixth of ten children, Johnson says her family embraced the arts and being creative. One brother is an actor and screenwriter, a sister is a stage actress, and her brother Brendan is a singer-songwriter and music producer with whom she often collaborates.</p>
<p>From age three, Johnson often sang for her large extended family. By age seven, she was receiving vocal training, participating in festivals with her local youth choir, and giving solo performances at school and community functions. She entered talent contests and festivals, winning a National Anthem Contest at the age of 11, with her rendition of the Canadian National Anthem, "<a href="/wiki/O_Canada" title="O Canada">O Canada</a>", heard on national television. This resulted in radio and television interviews, as well as interest from management. That year, she was voted Best Anthem singer of the season by the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vancouver_Sun" title="Vancouver Sun">Vancouver Sun</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timescolonist.com_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-timescolonist.com-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Locally, Johnson was often referred to as the "<a href="/wiki/British_Columbia_Coast" title="British Columbia Coast">West Coast</a> <a href="/wiki/Celine_Dion" title="Celine Dion">Celine</a>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timescolonist1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-timescolonist1-7">[7]</a></sup> and sang the <a href="/wiki/O_Canada" title="O Canada">Canadian National Anthem</a> at games for the NHL's <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_Canucks" title="Vancouver Canucks">Vancouver Canucks</a> and the NBA's <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_Grizzlies" title="Vancouver Grizzlies">Vancouver Grizzlies</a>. She performed at charity events and fairs around the province, in addition to the <a href="/wiki/BC_Summer_Games" title="BC Summer Games">BC Summer Games</a>. She opened the Variety Club show singing a duet with <a href="/wiki/Bob_McGrath" title="Bob McGrath">Bob McGrath</a>, sang the first set of songs at the <a href="/wiki/Molson_Indy_Vancouver" title="Molson Indy Vancouver">Molson Indy</a> (a major car race), and entertained on New Year's Eve at <a href="/wiki/Planet_Hollywood" title="Planet Hollywood">Planet Hollywood</a> at the age of 12.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timescolonist.com_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-timescolonist.com-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexz_Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1999.E2.80.932003:_Beginnings_and_So_Weird">1999–2003: Beginnings and <i>So Weird</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexz_Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1999–2003: Beginnings and So Weird">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although Johnson was only focused on a music career at the time, she decided to pursue a career in acting at the age of 11, where she "walked into an acting agency and told them [she] wanted to sing". She landed auditions for commercials and a television pilot entitled "Most Talented Kids".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alexz_Johnson_Interview_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Alexz_Johnson_Interview-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Johnson auditioned for the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> original series <i><a href="/wiki/So_Weird" title="So Weird">So Weird</a></i>, which was casting for a lead actress who could also sing for the final season of the show. At the age of 13, Johnson replaced the lead character in the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/So_Weird" title="So Weird">So Weird</a></i> for the show's final season. Johnson portrayed the lead role of Annie Thelen, which combined her acting and musical talents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The role featured her singing alongside <a href="/wiki/Mackenzie_Phillips" title="Mackenzie Phillips">Mackenzie Phillips</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> She showed an interest in songwriting, co-writing one of the songs for the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 22, 2000, Johnson was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> for the first time, she went on to be featured in sixteen different issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Her highest ranking was number two in "<i>The Top 10 Favorite Artist Picks</i>" for "<i>The Most Popular New Talent on Broadband Talent Net</i>" as of June 2, 2001 after 33 weeks on. Her song "Dream About You" charted six different times and came in at number one in "<i>Radio Play Favorite Song Requests</i>" for "<i>The Most Listened-to New Tracks On Brandband Talent Net</i>" on November 11, 2000. Her song, "Wishing On A Star", came in at number five in the same issue. Also "Sunshine Reigns" charted at number three on July 22, 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the year 2000, a song performed by Johnson entitled "Everything (Feels Like New)" was featured in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney_Channel_Original_Movie" title="Disney Channel Original Movie">Disney Channel Original Movie</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Other_Me" title="The Other Me">The Other Me</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Lawrence_(actor)" title="Andrew Lawrence (actor)">Andrew Lawrence</a>. A full studio version of the song was never released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the cancellation of <i>So Weird</i>, Johnson continued her interest in songwriting, co-writing with her brother Brendan Johnson. Several labels offered her contracts, but she held off, wanting more creative control and a hand in the songwriting. In 2001 Johnson teamed up with the production/songwriting team of Johnny Elkins and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CJ_Vanston" title="CJ Vanston">CJ Vanston</a>. A song performed by Johnson entitled "Transparent Lies", was recorded for a compilation album titled 'Fill Your Head'. The album was compiled in order to help students with their SAT scores.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Her work with Vanston and Elkins featured a more bubblegum pop sound than what she preferred and ended around 2003. Johnson continued to work with her brother Brendan on demos. These were the types of songs included on the demo CD she sent to the executives of <i><a href="/wiki/Instant_Star" title="Instant Star">Instant Star</a></i> with her audition tape.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2002, Johnson shared the stage with <a href="/wiki/David_Foster" title="David Foster">David Foster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Bubl%C3%A9" title="Michael Bublé">Michael Bublé</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ed_McMahon" title="Ed McMahon">Ed McMahon</a> at a David Foster Foundation fundraiser.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timescolonist1_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-timescolonist1-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<p>Throughout 2004, Johnson made various appearances on Canadian television series including <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chris_Isaak_Show" title="The Chris Isaak Show">The Chris Isaak Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Collector" title="The Collector">The Collector</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Cold_Squad" title="Cold Squad">Cold Squad</a></i>. In 2005 she made guest appearances in the made-for-TV films including <i><a href="/wiki/Reefer_Madness_(2005_film)" title="Reefer Madness (2005 film)">Reefer Madness: The Movie Musical</a></i>, as well as the role of Angel DeSousa in the <a href="/wiki/Lifetime_(TV_network)" title="Lifetime (TV network)">Lifetime</a> original movie <i><a href="/wiki/Selling_Innocence" title="Selling Innocence">Selling Innocence</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2004 Johnson sent in an audition tape to the producers of <i><a href="/wiki/Degrassi:_The_Next_Generation" title="Degrassi: The Next Generation">Degrassi: The Next Generation</a></i> for their upcoming show <i><a href="/wiki/Instant_Star" title="Instant Star">Instant Star</a></i>. They were specifically looking for a Canadian teen actress/vocalist to play the lead role, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jude_Harrison" title="Jude Harrison">Jude Harrison</a>. The first audition tape Executive Producer <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Stohn" title="Stephen Stohn">Stephen Stohn</a> received was Johnson's, and he was convinced she was perfect for the role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> After its first season, <i>Instant Star</i> was nominated for three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gemini_Award" title="Gemini Award">Gemini Awards</a> (the Canadian equivalent of the American <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a>) in the category of Best Children's or Youth Fiction Program or Series, including a nomination for Best Performance (Alexz Johnson). On August 28, 2007, the show received three more Gemini Award nominations, in the category of Best Children's or Youth Program or Series, including another for Best Performance (Alexz Johnson, in the episode, "I Fought the Law").</p>
<p>Johnson sang all of the songs that her character Jude, on <i>Instant Star</i> performed on the show. She recorded soundtracks for each of the four seasons of <i>Instant Star</i>: <i><a href="/wiki/Songs_from_Instant_Star" title="Songs from Instant Star">Songs from Instant Star</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Songs_from_Instant_Star_Two" title="Songs from Instant Star Two">Songs from Instant Star Two</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Songs_from_Instant_Star_Three" title="Songs from Instant Star Three">Songs from Instant Star Three</a></i>, and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Songs_From_Instant_Star_Four" title="Songs From Instant Star Four">Songs From Instant Star Four</a></i>. She wrote or co-wrote five of the songs on the first season's soundtrack, including "24 Hours", "Let Me Fall", "Criminal, "Skin", and "That Girl", which Johnson had written before when she was fourteen.</p>
<p>Most of the original recordings for the originally planned debut album were written by Johnson in early 2005, but she had yet to find a major label to help create and release the songs. In early 2006, Johnson announced she was leaving the indie Canadian label <a href="/wiki/Orange_Records" title="Orange Records">Orange Records</a> that released the first two soundtracks to <i>Instant Star</i>, in search of something to showcase her as an artist and not her fictional counterpart, Jude Harrison.</p>
<p>Johnson auditioned for the role of Julie Christensen in the thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Final_Destination_3" title="Final Destination 3">Final Destination 3</a></i>. She showed up at the audition in an all-black, punk rockish outfit, later claiming that she was in a bad mood. The casting directors concluded she was a better fit for the role of Erin Ulmer, a character with a bad attitude. Her performance attracted praise from some critics, including one (writing for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Philadelphia_Inquirer" title="The Philadelphia Inquirer">The Philadelphia Inquirer</a></i>) who said: "The characters are so loathsome, you're glad to see them go. Except for two: the goth couple Ian (<a href="/wiki/Kris_Lemche" title="Kris Lemche">Kris Lemche</a>) and Erin (Alexz Johnson). He's a cynical know-it-all, like <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Miller" title="Dennis Miller">Dennis Miller</a> with black fingernails; she's like <a href="/wiki/Parker_Posey" title="Parker Posey">Parker Posey</a> with raccoon mascara.."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In 2007, Johnson starred in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lifetime_Television" title="Lifetime Television">Lifetime</a> movie, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Devil%27s_Diary_(2007_film)" title="Devil's Diary (2007 film)">Devil's Diary</a></i>, for which she received a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leo_Award" title="Leo Award">Leo Award</a> nomination, in the category of Best Lead Performance by a Female in a Feature-Length Drama.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008.E2.80.932009:_Starting_her_music_career">2008–2009: Starting her music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexz_Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2008–2009: Starting her music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On August 26, 2008, Johnson was nominated a third time for a Gemini award. On October 21, 2008, she won the Gemini Award for Best Performance in a Children's or Youth Program or Series, for the <i>Instant Star</i> episode "Let It Be." Johnson continued with <i>Instant Star</i> for four seasons, and expected to return for a fifth. This was interrupted when <a href="/wiki/CTV_Television_Network" title="CTV Television Network">CTV</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_N" title="The N">The N</a> both pulled funding following the fourth season, which led to the cancellation of the series. This allowed Johnson to go back to focusing on her music.</p>
<p>After a showcase in New York City, Johnson met with several labels and producers and chose <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol Records</a>, which allowed her to work with Paul Buckmaster, though her brother was the main producer of the material at the time. In writing songs for the album, she worked with UK writers Martin Terefe and Sacha Skarbek as well as producer/songwriter Brio Taliaferro.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> On June 25, 2007, Johnson appeared on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MTV_Canada" title="MTV Canada">MTV Canada</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vagueness" title="Wikipedia:Vagueness"><span title="This information is too vague. (September 2011)">vague</span></a></i>]</sup> She confirmed that all of her songs had been written and recorded. She planned on shopping for a label over the next few months and hoped to release the album in the winter.</p>
<p>On February 15, 2008, she announced in her MySpace blog that she had signed a record deal with label <a href="/wiki/Epic_Records" title="Epic Records">Epic Records</a> (part of the <a href="/wiki/Sony_BMG" title="Sony BMG">Sony BMG</a> group), expecting the album to be released in the fall. Having heard the first six songs, <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Stohn" title="Stephen Stohn">Stephen Stohn</a>, in his MySpace blog of February 16, 2008, commented that, "they are just incredibly good – very different from the songs you've come to know through <i>Instant Star</i>, much more rhythmic, with almost a world beat."</p>
<p>In an interview in 2008,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> she described her upcoming album as "pop with eclectic world rhythms and influences", citing influences from "older artists" such as <a href="/wiki/Paul_Simon" title="Paul Simon">Paul Simon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kate_Bush" title="Kate Bush">Kate Bush</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Annie_Lennox" title="Annie Lennox">Annie Lennox</a>, and saying that she was "trying to find a way to make those great older songs younger."</p>
<p>The producer for the album became <a href="/wiki/Greg_Wells" title="Greg Wells">Greg Wells</a> after Johnson was introduced to him through his brother <a href="/wiki/Rob_Wells" title="Rob Wells">Rob Wells</a>, one of the songwriters for <i>Instant Star</i>. The new arrangement with <a href="/wiki/Epic_Records" title="Epic Records">Epic Records</a> also affected the line-up of artists for the <i>Instant Star</i> 4 soundtrack, as the record label allowed her a maximum of four songs on <i>Instant Star</i> 4. In her MySpace blog of February 15, 2008, Johnson indicated that she was planning an extensive tour around her new album, saying of her songs that, "I can't wait to perform them live! Looking forward to seeing all of your faces!" Johnson also began working with multi instrumentalist <a href="/wiki/Luis_Conte" title="Luis Conte">Luis Conte</a> and legendary strings expert <a href="/wiki/Paul_Buckmaster" title="Paul Buckmaster">Paul Buckmaster</a>. Five songs were posted on her MySpace page by her record company on June 11, 2008. These songs were supposed to appear in her upcoming album and were entitled: "Easy", "Swallowed", "Chicago", "Golden", and "Running With the Devil".</p>
<p>Johnson appeared in the hit <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_CW_Television_Network" title="The CW Television Network">CW</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Smallville" title="Smallville">Smallville</a></i> episode "Legion", as the super heroine <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Girl" title="Saturn Girl">Saturn Girl</a>. The episode aired on January 15, 2009. She was the second actress from <a href="/wiki/Instant_Star" title="Instant Star">Instant Star</a> to appear in the show, the first one being <a href="/wiki/Laura_Vandervoort" title="Laura Vandervoort">Laura Vandervoort</a>. She also appeared in the TV-movie version of the <a href="/wiki/Lois_Duncan" title="Lois Duncan">Lois Duncan</a> novel <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Stranger_With_My_Face_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Stranger With My Face (2009 film) (page does not exist)">Stranger with My Face</a></i>, in which she plays twin sisters who were separated at birth. She stated that the filming—which included a dramatic scene in which her two characters conflict through astral projection, leading to a fall off a cliff and into a waterfall—was so mentally and physically demanding, that she felt like she could never take on another role like it again. It premiered on Lifetime Movie network on August 29, 2009. The director of <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Stranger_With_My_Face_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Stranger With My Face (2009 film) (page does not exist)">Stranger with My Face</a></i>, Jeff Renfroe, greatly praised what he saw when working with Johnson and her costar <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Francis" title="Andrew Francis">Andrew Francis</a>. "They're both young actors who came from the school of working from the gut and not trying to intellectualize everything."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In a February 2009 interview with the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Times-Colonist" title="Times-Colonist">Times-Colonist</a></i>, a newspaper in <a href="/wiki/Victoria,_British_Columbia" title="Victoria, British Columbia">Victoria</a>, British Columbia, Johnson said that her album was completely finished, noting that she was simply waiting for changes to be finalized with the new head of her label, <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Ghost" title="Amanda Ghost">Amanda Ghost</a>, who had worked with Johnson on the album. She hoped to have the album out by September 2009, followed by tours through parts of Europe and North America.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Before the album's release date, Epic Records removed Johnson from their Artist Lists. Johnson later gave clarification on her website that launched August 11, 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> saying "Epic has released over a third of their artists from their contracts and, la dee da... I'm one of those artists..." She also later clarified in an interview that the "head of the label who signed [her] ended up shifting to a different company. It led a lot of artists on Epic astray."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alexz_Johnson_Interview_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Alexz_Johnson_Interview-8">[8]</a></sup> She confirmed that due to losing her deal with Epic, she would be releasing a different album independently with her brother Brendan producing. She said she hopes that her unreleased debut album with Epic would see the light one day, noting that for now, the songs are still tied up in the arrangement with Epic.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.932011:_Voodoo.2C_Reloaded_and_internet_promotion">2009–2011: <i>Voodoo</i>, <i>Reloaded</i> and internet promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexz_Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2009–2011: Voodoo, Reloaded and internet promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The independent release will be by the company which also runs her website – Laydee Spencer Music, Inc. On August 27, 2009, Johnson released a list of songs that are going to be on her album in alphabetical order, and later requested fans to go through the clips of five upcoming songs on her site, and to recommend which should be the lead-off single. The tracks posted were: "L.A. Made Me", "Hurricane Girl", "Voodoo", "A Little Bit", and "Trip Around the World". On December 1, 2009 it was announced that her debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Voodoo_(Alexz_Johnson_album)" title="Voodoo (Alexz Johnson album)">Voodoo</a></i> would be released on March 30, 2010. The debut single, Trip Around The World, was released on February 2, 2010 along with a music video shot by Michael Maxxis. Johnson shared in an interview that her favourite songs of <i><a href="/wiki/Voodoo_(Alexz_Johnson_album)" title="Voodoo (Alexz Johnson album)">Voodoo</a></i> are "Look at Those Eyes", "Voodoo", and "Gonna Get It".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheSoundAlarm.com_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-TheSoundAlarm.com-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Later in 2010, Johnson informed her fans through her Twitter account and her official website that all the songs from <i>Voodoo</i> would be remixed by The Demolition Crew due to lack of airplay. The Demolition Crew undertook to make the songs more radio-friendly. <i><a href="/wiki/Reloaded_(Alexz_Johnson_album)" title="Reloaded (Alexz Johnson album)">Reloaded</a></i>, as the remix album's name is, was released on April 26, 2011. The first single, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boogie_Love" title="Boogie Love">Boogie Love (The Demolition Crew Remix)</a>" was released on January 10, 2011 while the second one, "Look at Those Eyes (The Demolition Crew Remix)", was released on March 29, 2011.</p>
<p>While awaiting the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Reloaded_(Alexz_Johnson_album)" title="Reloaded (Alexz Johnson album)">Reloaded</a></i>, Johnson became a trending topic on Twitter for four days. This was due to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABC_Family" title="ABC Family">ABC Family</a> television series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pretty_Little_Liars_(TV_series)" title="Pretty Little Liars (TV series)">Pretty Little Liars</a></i>, playing "Time to Be Your 21", a song performed by Johnson in the series Instant Star, in the season finale. While trending on Twitter, she garnered attention from <a href="/wiki/Demi_Lovato" title="Demi Lovato">Demi Lovato</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nelly_Furtado" title="Nelly Furtado">Nelly Furtado</a>. Lovato has spoken several times about Johnson being her inspiration to start a career in music, while Furtado spoke about her single "Look at Those Eyes" calling it "an event". With the help of the Twitter trend, Johnson made the homepage of several online sites, including L.A. Times Ministry of Gossip Blog.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Johnson created a YouTube account where she posts videos of herself performing along with her brother Brendan James Johnson, and <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Robbins" title="Jimmy Robbins">Jimmy Robbins</a>. On June 22, 2011 Johnson held two live concerts on Stage It for fans around the world. The concerts were performed online via webcam along with her band "The Bed". On June 24, 2011 it was announced that Johnson was the new voice of television series <i><a href="/wiki/Degrassi:_The_Next_Generation" title="Degrassi: The Next Generation">Degrassi: The Next Generation</a></i> Season 11 new opening theme song. The idea came naturally as Demacio "Demo" Castellón was producing the track and asked her if she could contribute to it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TeenNick.com_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-TeenNick.com-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.932013:_The_Basement_Recordings.2C_Skipping_Stone_and_Live_From_The_Skipping_Stone_Tour">2011–2013: <i>The Basement Recordings</i>, <i>Skipping Stone</i> and <i>Live From The Skipping Stone Tour</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexz_Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2011–2013: The Basement Recordings, Skipping Stone and Live From The Skipping Stone Tour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Johnson has revealed that she is writing for her next album and hoping to enter the studio by the end of the Summer to start recording. Johnson and her brother, Brendan Johnson, released a digital album of demos entitled, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Basement_Recordings" title="The Basement Recordings">The Basement Recordings</a></i>, on August 26, 2011.</p>
<p>In October, via Twitter, Johnson announced her plans to release an EP featuring new material, and confirmed on October 29, that she was in New York filming the music video for her new single <i>Skipping Stone</i>. Johnson confirmed that her EP, <i>Skipping Stone</i> would be out on January 24, 2012. She also confirmed that it would originally be a digital release, and following her Kickstarters Campaign, a physical release would follow. The video for <i>Skipping Stone</i> was released in December.</p>
<p>Beginning on January 24, 2012, Johnson launched a <a href="/wiki/Kickstarter" title="Kickstarter">Kickstarter Campaign</a> in order to raise funds for her upcoming U.S tour. She set her goal for $30,000 in 60 days. For different money denominations, Johnson is offering prizes. Examples include, digital downloads of her upcoming EP, Skipping Stone, signed T-shirts and CDs, signed scripts and clothing from Instant Star, concert tickets, personalized emails and phone calls, live performances, dinner and a broadway show, etc. Johnson unexpectedly reached her goal within 24 hours, confirming her ability to tour during the summer of 2012. Johnson reached $50,000 on day three, allowing her the opportunity to expand the tour and travel overseas. All money donated goes toward touring, recording, merchandise, and anything music related.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>The sequel to the 2011 demo album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Basement_Recordings" title="The Basement Recordings">The Basement Recordings</a></i>, named <i><a href="/wiki/The_Basement_Recordings_II" title="The Basement Recordings II">The Basement Recordings II</a></i> was released on May 1, 2012. On February 22, 2013, Johnson released the third installment of the series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Basement_Recordings_III" title="The Basement Recordings III">The Basement Recordings III</a>.</i></p>
<p>On October 26, 2012, Johnson released a trailer for <i>The Skipping Stone Tour Documentary</i> which chronicles her summer on tour. Johnson released the documentary, a live album, <i>Live From The Skipping Stone Tour</i>, and a photo book containing photos from <i>The Skipping Stone Tour</i> on December 4, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.932015:_Further_touring.2C_Heart_and_Let_.27Em_Eat_Cake">2013–2015: Further touring, <i>Heart</i> and <i>Let 'Em Eat Cake</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexz_Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2013–2015: Further touring, Heart and Let 'Em Eat Cake">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Johnson launched a joint North American tour with Charlene Kaye, Misty Boyce, and Jay Stolar in the spring of 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> On August 22, 2012, Johnson posted a video from a recording session for her upcoming album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Alexz has worked with <a href="/wiki/Bleu_(musician)" title="Bleu (musician)">Bleu</a>, Boots Ottestad, <a href="/wiki/Wax_Ltd" title="Wax Ltd">Wax Ltd</a>, Jamie Hartman, Theo Katzman, and her brother Brendan James Johnson on writing the album. Johnson later announced that she would collaborate with her fans in funding her next studio project through <a href="/wiki/PledgeMusic" title="PledgeMusic">PledgeMusic.com</a>. According to the site, Johnson has managed to gather 156% of the money needed for the production and release of her second album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> On Christmas Day of 2013, Johnson released the lead single from the album, "American Dreamer", exclusively to pledgers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The single was released publicly on January 10, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 29, 2014, Johnson released a 4-track EP, <i>Heart</i> exclusively to pledgers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> It includes "American Dreamer" as well as three other tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> While the EP was originally intended to be an album sampler, Johnson clarified in an interview that the EP will stand alone as its own work and that the album will feature all new tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Johnson was part of a European tour in support of the EP in early 2014, opening for <a href="/wiki/Ron_Pope" title="Ron Pope">Ron Pope</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wakey!Wakey!" title="Wakey!Wakey!">Wakey!Wakey!</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Johnson was also the opening act for <a href="/wiki/Max_Schneider" title="Max Schneider">MAX</a> on a North American tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pledgers received digital copies of Johnson's sophomore album <i>Let 'Em Eat Cake</i> one week before the official release date of October 14, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Alexz embarked on a tour in support of the album from October until December of the same year. <a href="/wiki/Jared_%26_The_Mill" title="Jared &amp; The Mill">Jared &amp; The Mill</a> and Patrick Droney joined her as special guests.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>From February to March 2015, Johnson had a five-week residency at The Hotel Cafe in Hollywood, CA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>Johnson also collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Bleu_(musician)" title="Bleu (musician)">Bleu</a> on the project Johnson &amp; McAuley. The duo released a self-titled EP in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016:_Third_studio_album">2016: Third studio album</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexz_Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2016: Third studio album">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Johnson began working on her third studio album in November 2016, with <a href="/wiki/Mark_Howard_(producer)" title="Mark Howard (producer)">Mark Howard</a> producing the record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences_and_musical_style">Influences and musical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexz_Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Influences and musical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: left; clear: left; margin: 0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0; width:50%; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>I love interesting voices. I love <a href="/wiki/Rickie_Lee_Jones" title="Rickie Lee Jones">Rickie Lee Jones</a>, I think she's an amazing lyricist and song writer. Annie Lennox is a strong woman and she wasn't afraid to be really eccentric and who she was. It's nice to see singers that find sensuality and sexuality and femininity and all those key words, I feel like it's nice to have that, not so obvious, it's nice when it comes from a different place, and I feel like it's important that that is out there as well in the music industry.</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;">Johnson, In an interview with Fearless Radio, dated November 4, 2014. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>Johnson was quoted saying, "I love pop music, but kind of eclectic, creative pop." She has said her influences include <a href="/wiki/Kate_Bush" title="Kate Bush">Kate Bush</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Gabriel" title="Peter Gabriel">Peter Gabriel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Simon" title="Paul Simon">Paul Simon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Annie_Lennox" title="Annie Lennox">Annie Lennox</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> She is also an admirer of <a href="/wiki/Jason_Mraz" title="Jason Mraz">Jason Mraz</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Civil_Wars" title="The Civil Wars">The Civil Wars</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sara_Bareilles" title="Sara Bareilles">Sara Bareilles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Whitney_Houston" title="Whitney Houston">Whitney Houston</a> has also influenced Johnson vocally, while <a href="/wiki/Roy_Orbison" title="Roy Orbison">Roy Orbison</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis Presley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Billy_Joel" title="Billy Joel">Billy Joel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carole_King" title="Carole King">Carole King</a> influenced her writing growing up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>

<p>During 2001, Johnson worked with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Vision" title="World Vision">World Vision</a>, and had a section on her official site where fans could order shirts with a design done by Johnson and featuring her autograph. There were production problems and the shirts were never sent out. Johnson made an apology on her site and provided refunds to everyone who ordered one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> In 2002, she performed as the opening act at <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Gretzky" title="Wayne Gretzky">Wayne Gretzky</a>'s celebrity dinner and auction, held in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edmonton,_Alberta" title="Edmonton, Alberta">Edmonton, Alberta</a>, to raise funds for the <a href="/wiki/Ronald_McDonald" title="Ronald McDonald">Ronald McDonald</a> children's charity.</p>
<p>During the peak of her run on <i><a href="/wiki/So_Weird" title="So Weird">So Weird</a></i>, Johnson ended up performing and visiting the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nashville" title="Nashville">Nashville</a> Kids Fair where she also hosted the event. During her time in Nashville, Johnson visited ill children in a local hospital. The trip was made as a part of an outreach by <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a>, and Johnson visited the children with the dog who starred in the Disney original film <i><a href="/wiki/Air_Bud" title="Air Bud">Air Bud</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>During 2002 and 2003, Johnson focused on her music, and decided to perform with several charitable organizations. She worked with <a href="/wiki/David_Foster" title="David Foster">David Foster</a>, opening for his charity events for the David Foster Foundation. After the season premiere of the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/Instant_Star" title="Instant Star">Instant Star</a></i> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_N" title="The N">The N</a> she also did a public service announcement for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_Harvest" title="Second Harvest">Second Harvest</a>, which had been assisting victims of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">Hurricane Katrina</a>.</p>
<p>On November 21, 2008, Johnson was a special guest performer for a Women in Leadership (a Vancouver organization promoting women's role in the media) foundation called SuperWomen &amp; Friends—A Red Carpet Gala.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
