<p><b>Albert Joseph Scanlon</b> (10 October 1935 – 22 December 2009) was an English <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> player. He began his career with <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> and was one of the "<a href="/wiki/Busby_Babes" title="Busby Babes">Busby Babes</a>" who survived the <a href="/wiki/Munich_air_disaster" title="Munich air disaster">Munich air disaster</a> of 1958. Although he sustained severe injuries, he recovered and continued to play league football for <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_City_F.C." title="Lincoln City F.C.">Lincoln City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mansfield_Town_F.C." title="Mansfield Town F.C.">Mansfield Town</a>. He then went on to play non-league football until his retirement.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albert_Scanlon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Hulme" title="Hulme">Hulme</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>, Scanlon was a nephew of former <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> winger <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Mitten" title="Charlie Mitten">Charlie Mitten</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-encyclopedia_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-encyclopedia-1">[1]</a></sup> He attended St Wilfred's School in Hulme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obituary_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-obituary-2">[2]</a></sup> His talent for football was noticed early on, and he was selected to play for the Manchester Boys side. He joined the Manchester United groundstaff in 1950, before signing a professional contract in December 1952.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obituary_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-obituary-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Manchester_United">Manchester United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albert_Scanlon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Manchester United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Scanlon made his way through the Manchester United youth system, winning two <a href="/wiki/FA_Youth_Cup" title="FA Youth Cup">FA Youth Cups</a> in 1953 and 1954 before making his first-team debut against <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> on 20 November 1954.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obituary_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-obituary-2">[2]</a></sup> Scanlon was involved in two league title wins – in 1956 and 1957 – but he failed to make enough appearances to earn a winners' medal on either occasion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obituary_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-obituary-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 5 February 1958, Scanlon started on the left wing in United's European Cup quarter-final second leg away to <a href="/wiki/Red_Star_Belgrade" title="Red Star Belgrade">Red Star Belgrade</a>; for five of the players who started the game, it was to be their last ever match for the club. The next day, on the return journey to Manchester, the team's plane crashed while attempting to take off from Munich airport, in an incident that later became known as the <a href="/wiki/Munich_air_disaster" title="Munich air disaster">Munich air disaster</a>. The crash claimed the lives of 23 of the 44 passengers on board the plane, so Scanlon was relatively fortunate to escape with just a fractured skull, a broken leg and kidney damage. He made a full recovery and was back in action at the start of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1958-59_in_English_football" title="1958-59 in English football">following season</a>, going on to appear in every game that season, scoring 16 goals in the process.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obituary_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-obituary-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_career">Later career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albert_Scanlon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Scanlon was sold to <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> for <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>18,000 in November 1960,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-encyclopedia_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-encyclopedia-1">[1]</a></sup> but his time on <a href="/wiki/Tyneside" title="Tyneside">Tyneside</a> was not a success and he then dropped down to a lower level to play for <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_City_F.C." title="Lincoln City F.C.">Lincoln City</a> in February 1962.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obituary_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-obituary-2">[2]</a></sup> He was on the move again just over a year later, joining <a href="/wiki/Mansfield_Town_F.C." title="Mansfield Town F.C.">Mansfield Town</a> in April 1963, just in time to participate in the club's celebrations at being promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Third_Division" title="Football League Third Division">Third Division</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obituary_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-obituary-2">[2]</a></sup> Mansfield narrowly missed out on promotion to the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Second_Division" title="Football League Second Division">Second Division</a> two years later, finishing third in Division Three, but they dropped to 19th the following season and Scanlon took the opportunity to drop out of league football. He joined <a href="/wiki/Belper_Town_F.C." title="Belper Town F.C.">Belper Town</a> in 1966,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-encyclopedia_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-encyclopedia-1">[1]</a></sup> and retired not long afterwards.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_years_and_death">Later years and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albert_Scanlon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Later years and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following his retirement from football, Scanlon's employments included a job as a security worker at a <a href="/wiki/Colgate-Palmolive" title="Colgate-Palmolive">Colgate-Palmolive</a> factory not far from <a href="/wiki/Old_Trafford" title="Old Trafford">Old Trafford</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obituary_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-obituary-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 13 May 2007, Scanlon presented the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> trophy to Manchester United along with former team-mate <a href="/wiki/Bill_Foulkes" title="Bill Foulkes">Bill Foulkes</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>He attended United's victorious <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Cup" title="European Cup">European Cup</a> final appearance in May 2008 in Moscow, being flown to the game in Russia on a private jet hired by United along with fellow Munich survivors <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Charlton" title="Bobby Charlton">Sir Bobby Charlton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Gregg" title="Harry Gregg">Harry Gregg</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Scanlon was admitted to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salford_Royal" title="Salford Royal">Salford Royal</a> hospital in <a href="/wiki/Salford,_Greater_Manchester" title="Salford, Greater Manchester">Salford</a> with kidney problems and pneumonia on 21 October 2009. He was in intensive care for more than a month, before dying on 22 December 2009, at the age of 74.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> His funeral was held on 13 January 2010, and more than 300 people attended the service at All Souls Church in <a href="/wiki/Weaste" title="Weaste">Weaste</a>, including fellow Munich survivors <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Charlton" title="Bobby Charlton">Bobby Charlton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Gregg" title="Harry Gregg">Harry Gregg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bill_Foulkes" title="Bill Foulkes">Bill Foulkes</a>, and former Manchester United players <a href="/wiki/Denis_Law" title="Denis Law">Denis Law</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nobby_Stiles" title="Nobby Stiles">Nobby Stiles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
