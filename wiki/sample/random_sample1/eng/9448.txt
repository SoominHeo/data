<p><b>Albert Wesker</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">アルバート・ウェスカー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Arubāto Wesukā</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is one of the central characters in <a href="/wiki/Capcom" title="Capcom">Capcom</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil" title="Resident Evil">Resident Evil</a></i> series (<i>Biohazard</i> in Japan). He is first introduced in the original <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil_(1996_video_game)" title="Resident Evil (1996 video game)">Resident Evil</a></i> game, in which he is the captain of U.S. police team <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Special_Tactics_And_Rescue_Squad" title="Special Tactics And Rescue Squad">STARS</a>. His character is further explored in subsequent installments, which feature Wesker as a recurring character within the series' extensive narrative.</p>
<p>Initially introduced as a supporting character in the first game, he has been one of the primary <a href="/wiki/Antagonist" title="Antagonist">antagonists</a> throughout the series, where he usually manipulates story events behind-the-scenes. Power-hungry, knowledgeable and infinitely cunning, he was a man who sought to replace the entire human race through mass extinction and forced evolution, believing humanity to be an evolutionary dead-end. To this end, he was affiliated with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Umbrella_Corporation" title="Umbrella Corporation">Umbrella Corporation</a> as one of its most promising researchers, and at the same time participated in illicit activities by going undercover as S.T.A.R.S. captain in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Raccoon_City" title="Raccoon City">Raccoon City</a>. Through the course of much betrayal of his allies to further his own plans, Wesker fakes his death, gains superhuman strength, speed &amp; regeneration from an experimental variant of the Progenitor virus, and works alongside both Umbrella's mysterious rival company and their successors in the field of biological weapons development, TRICELL, until his ultimate defeat in <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil_5" title="Resident Evil 5">Resident Evil 5</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearances">Appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albert_Wesker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Resident_Evil_games">In <i>Resident Evil</i> games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albert_Wesker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In Resident Evil games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Wesker made his first appearance as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-playable_character" title="Non-playable character">non-playable character</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil_(1996_video_game)" title="Resident Evil (1996 video game)">original <i>Resident Evil</i> game</a>, <a href="/wiki/Supporting_character" title="Supporting character">assisting</a> the player character against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lisa_Trevor" title="Lisa Trevor">Lisa Trevor</a> in <a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil_(2002_video_game)" title="Resident Evil (2002 video game)">the remake</a> and the hidden main antagonist in first part of series. In that game, he is the <a href="/wiki/Commanding_officer" title="Commanding officer">commanding officer</a> of Alpha team of the Special Tactics And Rescue Service (STARS). It is eventually revealed that Wesker is a <a href="/wiki/Double_agent" title="Double agent">double agent</a> working for the Umbrella Corporation; ordered by his superiors to lure the STARS into the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Resident_Evil_characters#Oswell_E._Spencer" title="List of Resident Evil characters">Spencer</a> mansion to be used as test subjects against the mutated creatures to gather battle data. <a href="/wiki/Chris_Redfield" title="Chris Redfield">Chris Redfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jill_Valentine" title="Jill Valentine">Jill Valentine</a> and the others come to learn of Wesker's true motive, but after releasing the <a href="/wiki/Tyrant_(Resident_Evil)" title="Tyrant (Resident Evil)">Tyrant</a> monster, Wesker is seemingly killed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-psu_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-psu-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spot_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-spot-7">[7]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Sega_Saturn" title="Sega Saturn">Sega Saturn</a> port of <i>Resident Evil</i> features a Battle Mode minigame where the player can fight a zombified version of Wesker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>However, this is part of an even bigger plan. In the special giveaway <a href="/wiki/Mockumentary" title="Mockumentary">fictional documentary</a> titled <i>Wesker's Report</i> (rewritten in 2003 to include details from <i>Resident Evil 0</i> and the <i>Resident Evil</i> remake<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>), Wesker reveals that he planned to sell the Tyrant to Umbrella's rival company in the B.O.W. field as a means to buy his way into a high position, and he deployed the elite S.T.A.R.S. against it in order to obtain combat data to prove the Tyrant's effectiveness to the company. However, his plan was ruined after the Tyrant was destroyed by his former subordinates, and he survived his apparent death in the first game by injecting himself beforehand with an experimental variant of the t-virus, provided by his former Umbrella colleague William Birkin, that revived and augmented him with superhuman strength, speed and <a href="/wiki/Regeneration_(biology)" title="Regeneration (biology)">regeneration</a>, but <a href="/wiki/Posthuman" title="Posthuman">at the expense of his humanity</a>. Wesker returned in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Resident_Evil_Code:_Veronica" title="Resident Evil Code: Veronica">Resident Evil Code: Veronica</a></i>, augmented with <a href="/wiki/Superhuman" title="Superhuman">superhuman</a> powers and working as an agent for "H.C.F." (Hive/Host Capture Force), a Special Forces unit of the rival company opposed to Umbrella. He orchestrates a raid on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rockfort_Island" title="Rockfort Island">Rockfort Island</a> in order to capture Alexia Ashford, creator of the t-Veronica virus who now holds the only remaining sample of the virus within her body. When Chris Redfield knocks his sunglasses off during a brief struggle, Wesker's orange, almost reptilian eyes are seen for the first time. He confronts both Chris and Alexia and he took the corpse of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steve_Burnside" title="Steve Burnside">Steve Burnside</a> in order to extract the t-Veronica virus. In the expanded release, <i>Code: Veronica X</i>, he briefly confronts Chris' younger sister <a href="/wiki/Claire_Redfield" title="Claire Redfield">Claire</a> and nearly kills her in order to torment Chris, but is called away by his associates and thus chooses to spare her life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-psu_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-psu-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spot_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-spot-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wesker then appeared alongside William Birkin in <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil_Zero" title="Resident Evil Zero">Resident Evil Zero</a></i>, a prequel to the original game where it is revealed that he attended the Umbrella Executive Training School as a prospective executive, and later in <i>Resident Evil 4</i> as a conspirator manipulating events from behind the scenes, where he would issue orders to <a href="/wiki/Ada_Wong" title="Ada Wong">Ada Wong</a>. He is also unlockable in "The Mercenaries" scenario. He is also the protagonist of <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil:_The_Umbrella_Chronicles" title="Resident Evil: The Umbrella Chronicles">Resident Evil: The Umbrella Chronicles</a></i> and a playable character in several of the game's scenarios, expanding upon events alluded to in early games, as well as his involvement in Umbrella's downfall after the events of <i>Code: Veronica</i> and his development in <i>Resident Evil 4</i>. In the end he kills <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sergei_Vladimir" title="Sergei Vladimir">Sergei Vladimir</a>, a loyal Umbrella executive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-psu_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-psu-6">[6]</a></sup> Wesker also very briefly appears in <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil:_The_Darkside_Chronicles" title="Resident Evil: The Darkside Chronicles">Resident Evil: The Darkside Chronicles</a></i>, as he at one point is seen by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jack_Krauser" title="Jack Krauser">Jack Krauser</a>. He also gave Javier a sample of the virus within Steve Burnside.</p>
<p>Wesker returns in <i>Resident Evil 5</i> as the main antagonist conspiring with the pharmaceutical division of the TRICELL conglomerate as a means to create the "Uroboros virus", an enhanced virus derived from the Progenitor virus, with which he ultimately plans to release into the Earth's atmosphere. Before being killed by Wesker, Oswell Spencer reveals that Wesker was the survivor of a Progenitor virus variant administration experiment - the "Wesker Project." (It is stated in the <i>RE5</i> <a href="/wiki/Downloadable_content" title="Downloadable content">downloadable content</a> "Lost in Nightmares" that another Wesker, Alex, survived as well). He would eventually enact a plot to transform the human race into powerful superhumans like himself, and be a god to rule over them in a new age. Chris and <a href="/wiki/Sheva_Alomar" title="Sheva Alomar">Sheva Alomar</a> prevent the worldwide infection and ultimately kill Wesker inside a volcano at the end of the game. Masachika Kawata, the game's producer, confirmed that Wesker perished in the game's finale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Some concept artwork for <i>Resident Evil 5</i> also indicated that Wesker was to have been in his human form while in lava and then blasted with missiles from a F-16 Fighting Falcon, before eventually being shot in the head on the landing platform by Chris and Sheva.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wesker appears in the spin-off title <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil:_The_Mercenaries_3D" title="Resident Evil: The Mercenaries 3D">Resident Evil: The Mercenaries 3D</a></i> as a playable character along with various other <i>Resident Evil</i> characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> His voice is heard on the 15th anniversary special video narrating the events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He has a son named <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jake_Muller" title="Jake Muller">Jake Muller</a> who is introduced in <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil_6" title="Resident Evil 6">Resident Evil 6</a></i>.</p>
<p>Wesker is also featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil:_Revelations_2" title="Resident Evil: Revelations 2">Resident Evil: Revelations 2</a></i> as a playable character in Raid Mode, presented in-universe as a <a href="/wiki/Simulation" title="Simulation">virtual simulator</a>. The primary antagonist of the main game is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alex_Wesker" title="Alex Wesker">Alex Wesker</a>, the only other survivor out of the Wesker children. Albert is briefly shown in the main story in a portrait with Alex. He also received an indirect mention in <i>Resident Evil 7: Biohazard</i>, where it was revealed that his H.C.F. group was involved in the creation of Eveline during the 2000s.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Resident_Evil_films">In <i>Resident Evil</i> films</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albert_Wesker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In Resident Evil films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Wesker's character was adapted for the 2007 live-action film <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil:_Extinction" title="Resident Evil: Extinction">Resident Evil: Extinction</a></i>. Played by <a href="/wiki/Jason_O%27Mara" title="Jason O'Mara">Jason O'Mara</a>, this version of Wesker is the head of the Umbrella Corporation, as opposed to Wesker's role in the games as a renegade high-ranking Umbrella researcher. He runs Umbrella's operations from behind the scenes, holding meetings via hologram with his underground board of directors in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>. Originally, Wesker's character was not intended to be in the film, with his role and lines in the script being given by Commander Okamoto.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Shawn_Roberts" title="Shawn Roberts">Shawn Roberts</a> took over the role of Wesker in 2010's <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil:_Afterlife" title="Resident Evil: Afterlife">Resident Evil: Afterlife</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> where the character (who was the main antagonist throughout the film) is closer to his <i>Code Veronica</i> and especially his <i>Resident Evil 5</i> incarnations, complete with superhuman abilities and glowing red eyes; he even wears the same outfit as in the latter game. Wesker says some of his lines from the game as well, and the fight between Wesker and Chris and Claire Redfield in the film is an almost shot-for-shot remake of a fight between Wesker and Chris and Sheva in <i>Resident Evil 5</i>.</p>
<p>He later appears in <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil:_Retribution" title="Resident Evil: Retribution">Resident Evil: Retribution</a></i> as a defector from Umbrella, who sends <a href="/wiki/Ada_Wong" title="Ada Wong">Ada Wong</a> to rescue Alice from an underground Russian Umbrella outpost run by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Queen_and_White_Queen" title="Red Queen and White Queen">Red Queen</a> who has taken over the rest of Umbrella. He returns as the secondary antagonist in the sixth film, <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil:_The_Final_Chapter" title="Resident Evil: The Final Chapter">Resident Evil: The Final Chapter</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_appearances"><span id="Other_appearances"></span>Other appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albert_Wesker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Albert Wesker appears in the crossover fighting game <i><a href="/wiki/Marvel_vs._Capcom_3:_Fate_of_Two_Worlds" title="Marvel vs. Capcom 3: Fate of Two Worlds">Marvel vs. Capcom 3: Fate of Two Worlds</a></i> as a playable character and a key character in the game's plot, in which he joins forces with <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Doom" title="Doctor Doom">Doctor Doom</a> as the two build an army of supervillains, attempting to merge the two dimensions in the hopes of conquering both.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spider-ManWesker_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spider-ManWesker-18">[18]</a></sup> During the final battle, if Wesker is not one of the playable characters, he will also be revealed to be one of <a href="/wiki/Galactus" title="Galactus">Galactus</a>' heralds along with Doom, the <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel</a> villain <a href="/wiki/Dormammu" title="Dormammu">Dormammu</a>, and a fellow Capcom villain <a href="/wiki/Akuma_(Street_Fighter)" title="Akuma (Street Fighter)">Akuma</a>. He also makes a guest appearance as an unlockable character skin in <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_Planet_2" title="Lost Planet 2">Lost Planet 2</a></i>. An <a href="/wiki/Action_figure" title="Action figure">action figure</a> of Wesker was released by <a href="/wiki/Palisades_Toys" title="Palisades Toys">Palisades Toys</a> in 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Two more were released by <a href="/wiki/Hot_Toys" title="Hot Toys">Hot Toys</a> in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In 2016, <a href="/wiki/D._C._Douglas" title="D. C. Douglas">D. C. Douglas</a> reprised the role in <a href="/wiki/Jim_Sterling" title="Jim Sterling">Jim Sterling</a>'s short comedy horror film <i>JimSAW</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albert_Wesker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The character of Albert Wesker has been well received by video game publications. In 2006, <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a> ranked Wesker number three on their list of most memorable villains, claiming that "Res Evil fans know that whenever Wesker shows up, trouble isn't far behind,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and also ranked him as the 14th best video game villain in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In 2008, <i><a href="/wiki/GamePro" title="GamePro">GamePro</a></i> ranked Wesker as the 40th most diabolical video game villain of all time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> An IGN article from March 2010 titled "Big Boss of the Day: Resident Evil's Albert Wesker" discussed his appearances across the franchise, comparing him with other video game villains including <a href="/wiki/Bowser_(character)" title="Bowser (character)">Bowser</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sephiroth_(Final_Fantasy)" title="Sephiroth (Final Fantasy)">Sephiroth</a> as well commenting it is strange to have a <i>Resident Evil</i> without him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/GameSpot" title="GameSpot">GameSpot</a> featured him in the "All Time Greatest Game Villains" poll, where Wesker lost to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ganondorf" title="Ganondorf">Ganondorf</a> from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda" title="The Legend of Zelda">The Legend of Zelda</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In 2009, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GamesRadar" title="GamesRadar">GamesRadar</a> listed Wesker as one of the top seven characters who never stay dead, describing him as the "proverbial man behind the curtain" in the <i>Resident Evil</i> series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Similarly, <a href="/wiki/1UP.com" title="1UP.com">1UP.com</a> also placed him number eight in their article "They is Risen" in response to his apparent resurrection in the <i>Resident Evil</i> series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> GamesRadar gave praise to Wesker's death in <i>Resident Evil 5</i> as it showed the character's strength due to the requirements to defeat him while <a href="/wiki/D._C._Douglas" title="D. C. Douglas">D. C. Douglas</a>'s performance as the character also received positive comments during the self-bloopers from the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> According to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PlayStation_Universe" title="PlayStation Universe">PlayStation Universe</a>, "From his perpetual shades, stoic persona and swanky haircut, Wesker has all the makings of an iconic – albeit somewhat stereotypical – videogame villain."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spot_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-spot-7">[7]</a></sup> In the January 2010 issue, <i><a href="/wiki/Nintendo_Power" title="Nintendo Power">Nintendo Power</a></i> named Wesker the ninth best villain in Nintendo history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> GamesRadar also praised Wesker's role as an antagonist, putting him in their 2013 list of the best villains in video game history at number 13.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In 2013, GamesRadar staff included him among the 30 best characters in the three decades of Capcom's history and wrote that with his appearance as a playable character in <i>Marvel vs. Capcom 3</i>, "even after his death in <i>RE5</i>, Albert Wesker shows no signs of stopping, which is just the way we like it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the other hand, <i><a href="/wiki/Play_(UK_magazine)" title="Play (UK magazine)">Play</a></i> listed such fight as the third worst boss fight in the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a> games, emphasizing how many times the player had to fight him until he is defeated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> 1UP.com also ranked him as the top thing in the series that <i>Resident Evil 5</i> "could do without" prior to its release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PlayStation_Official_Magazine_-_UK" title="PlayStation Official Magazine - UK">PlayStation Official Magazine</a></i> shared this opinion and ranked Wesker as the ninth worst PlayStation boss fight ever.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> In 2012, <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a></i> ranked him as the second "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Douchebaggery" title="Douchebaggery">douchiest</a>" video game character, stating, "Wesker has a following, yes, but there’s something very <a href="/wiki/Agent_Smith" title="Agent Smith">Agent Smith</a> about him that makes us shake our heads."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
