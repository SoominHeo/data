<p><b>Alvin Warren Ceccoli</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/æ/ short 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="'v' in 'vie'">v</span><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span></span> <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/tʃ/ 'ch' in 'china'">tʃ</span><span title="/ɛ/ short 'e' in 'bed'">ɛ</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/oʊ/ or /ə/ 'o' in 'omit'">oʊ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/i/ 'y' in 'happy'">i</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born 5 August 1974 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sydney,_Australia" title="Sydney, Australia">Sydney, Australia</a>) is an Australian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">footballer</a> who played for three <a href="/wiki/A-League" title="A-League">A-League</a> clubs (<a href="/wiki/Sydney_FC" title="Sydney FC">Sydney FC</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_Coast_Mariners" title="Central Coast Mariners">Central Coast Mariners</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adelaide_United" title="Adelaide United">Adelaide United</a>) and was capped internationally for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australia_national_football_(soccer)_team" title="Australia national football (soccer) team">Australia</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alvin_Ceccoli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alvin Ceccoli is of <a href="/wiki/San_Marino" title="San Marino">Sammarinese</a> heritage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Alvin started his <a href="/wiki/National_Soccer_League" title="National Soccer League">National Soccer League</a> (NSL) career at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wollongong_Wolves" title="Wollongong Wolves">Wollongong Wolves</a> in 1995 as a 21-year-old. He played 156 matches in the <a href="/wiki/National_Soccer_League" title="National Soccer League">National Soccer League</a>, punctuated by a short stint in <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AEK_Athens_FC" title="AEK Athens FC">AEK Athens FC</a>. He won successive NSL Championships with Wollongong in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Soccer_League_1998-99" title="National Soccer League 1998-99">1998/99</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Soccer_League_1999-00" title="National Soccer League 1999-00">1999/2000</a> before joining <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parramatta_Power" title="Parramatta Power">Parramatta Power</a>, becoming part of the losing grand final side in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Soccer_League_2003-04" title="National Soccer League 2003-04">2003/04</a>.</p>
<p>He was one of seven players signed by <a href="/wiki/Sydney_FC" title="Sydney FC">Sydney FC</a> from the defunct NSL. Ceccoli's first season at the glamour club was a rebirth for his career. Alvin not only became one of the strongest defenders in the league, but also earned his second national team cap against <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_national_football_team" title="Bahrain national football team">Bahrain</a> in a qualifying match for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_Cup" title="Asian Cup">Asian Cup</a> in 2007, as a reward for an excellent season with Sydney FC. During the second season with Sydney however, he had some disagreements with new coach <a href="/wiki/Terry_Butcher" title="Terry Butcher">Terry Butcher</a> which soured his relationship with the club, and he sought a move elsewhere.</p>
<p>A transfer to <a href="/wiki/J2_League" title="J2 League">Japanese second division</a> team <a href="/wiki/Avispa_Fukuoka" title="Avispa Fukuoka">Avispa Fukuoka</a> allowed him to reunite with former Sydney coach <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Littbarski" title="Pierre Littbarski">Pierre Littbarski</a>, and was formally released by Sydney on 16 January 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He spent close to a year at Avispa and was released at the end of the season.</p>
<p>On 3 January 2008 Ceccoli signed a contract for the remainder of the <a href="/wiki/A-League" title="A-League">A-League</a> season with <a href="/wiki/Central_Coast_Mariners_FC" title="Central Coast Mariners FC">Central Coast Mariners FC</a> as cover for <a href="/wiki/Dean_Heffernan" title="Dean Heffernan">Dean Heffernan</a>, his contract running until after the 2008 Grand Final on 25 February.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Ceccoli agreed on 20 January 2008 to sign with rival <a href="/wiki/A-League" title="A-League">A-League</a> club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adelaide_United" title="Adelaide United">Adelaide United</a> for their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AFC_Champions_League_2008" title="AFC Champions League 2008">2008 AFC Champions League</a> campaign beginning in March. It would be his third <a href="/wiki/A-League" title="A-League">A-League</a> club in as many years, but was notably absent from Adelaide's first two ACL matches. On 20 March 2008 it was revealed he had left the club to return home to <a href="/wiki/Wollongong" title="Wollongong">Wollongong</a> to be close to his young family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Staying locally, he has joined <a href="/wiki/Illawarra_Premier_League" title="Illawarra Premier League">Illawarra Premier League</a> club Dandaloo FC for 2008. In 2015 he was selected in Sydney FC's team of the decade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
