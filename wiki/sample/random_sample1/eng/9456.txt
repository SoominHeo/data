<p><b>Angus Macfadyen</b> (born 21 September 1963)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a Scottish actor known for his roles as <a href="/wiki/Robert_the_Bruce" title="Robert the Bruce">Robert the Bruce</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Braveheart" title="Braveheart">Braveheart</a></i>, Vice-Counsel Dupont in <i><a href="/wiki/Equilibrium_(film)" title="Equilibrium (film)">Equilibrium</a></i>, Jeff Denlon in the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saw_(film_series)" title="Saw (film series)">Saw</a></i> franchise and McCreedy in <a href="/wiki/Cameron_Crowe" title="Cameron Crowe">Cameron Crowe</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/We_Bought_a_Zoo" title="We Bought a Zoo">We Bought a Zoo</a></i>. He has made appearances on several television series such as <i><a href="/wiki/Californication_(TV_series)" title="Californication (TV series)">Californication</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Criminal_Minds" title="Criminal Minds">Criminal Minds</a></i> and the final season of <i><a href="/wiki/Chuck_(TV_series)" title="Chuck (TV series)">Chuck</a></i>. He currently stars as <a href="/wiki/Robert_Rogers_(soldier)" title="Robert Rogers (soldier)">Robert Rogers</a> in the <a href="/wiki/AMC_(TV_channel)" title="AMC (TV channel)">AMC</a> historical drama <i><a href="/wiki/Turn:_Washington%27s_Spies" title="Turn: Washington's Spies">Turn: Washington's Spies</a></i> and as <a href="/wiki/Allan_Pinkerton" title="Allan Pinkerton">Allan Pinkerton</a> on the first-run syndicated series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pinkertons" title="The Pinkertons">The Pinkertons</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-imdb.com-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angus_Macfadyen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Macfadyen was born in <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> and lived a colourful childhood, being brought up in such places as the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> and <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. His father was a doctor in the <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">World Health Organization</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Macfadyen attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Edinburgh" title="University of Edinburgh">University of Edinburgh</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Central_School_of_Speech_and_Drama" title="Central School of Speech and Drama">Central School of Speech and Drama</a> in London.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angus_Macfadyen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s">1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angus_Macfadyen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1990s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Macfadyen's first role was Philip in the film made for television <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lost_Language_of_Cranes_(film)" title="The Lost Language of Cranes (film)">The Lost Language of Cranes</a></i>. He portrayed a young man who must tell his parents that he is gay. Playing the role of his father was fellow Scottish actor <a href="/wiki/Brian_Cox_(actor)" title="Brian Cox (actor)">Brian Cox</a>. Macfadyen then acted in the television film <i>15: The Life and Death of Philip Knight</i> (1993) and also on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Takin%27_Over_the_Asylum" title="Takin' Over the Asylum">Takin' Over the Asylum</a></i> (1994) about a salesman who runs a radio station in a mental institution. That role led to his role as <a href="/wiki/Robert_the_Bruce" title="Robert the Bruce">Robert the Bruce</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mel_Gibson" title="Mel Gibson">Mel Gibson</a>'s film <i><a href="/wiki/Braveheart" title="Braveheart">Braveheart</a></i> (1995).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb.com_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-imdb.com-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>After <i>Braveheart</i> won Best Picture at the Academy Awards, Macfadyen acted in the independent film <i><a href="/wiki/Nevada_(1997_film)" title="Nevada (1997 film)">Nevada</a></i> (1997), before giving the most over-the-top performance of his career in the action/fantasy <i><a href="/wiki/Warriors_of_Virtue" title="Warriors of Virtue">Warriors of Virtue</a></i> (1997). He also co-starred alongside <a href="/wiki/Don_Cheadle" title="Don Cheadle">Don Cheadle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ray_Liotta" title="Ray Liotta">Ray Liotta</a> when he played Peter Lawford in the HBO film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Rat_Pack" title="The Rat Pack">The Rat Pack</a></i> (1998).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb.com_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-imdb.com-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Macfadyen played <a href="/wiki/Orson_Welles" title="Orson Welles">Orson Welles</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tim_Robbins" title="Tim Robbins">Tim Robbins</a>'s third directorial film <i><a href="/wiki/Cradle_Will_Rock" title="Cradle Will Rock">Cradle Will Rock</a></i> (1999). Although the film received a nomination for the Palme d'Or at <a href="/wiki/Cannes" title="Cannes">Cannes</a>, it was a financial flop. Macfadyen next made the Shakespearan film <i><a href="/wiki/Titus_(film)" title="Titus (film)">Titus</a></i> (1999), alongside Anthony Hopkins, where he played Lucius, eldest son of Titus Andronicus. Directed by <a href="/wiki/Julie_Taymor" title="Julie Taymor">Julie Taymor</a>, the film was a critical triumph but with mediocre box office results.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s">2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angus_Macfadyen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the noir film <i>Second Skin</i> (2000), Macfadyen acted in a number of poorly received films. One such film was the action film <i>Styx</i> (2001) which starred <a href="/wiki/Peter_Weller" title="Peter Weller">Peter Weller</a>. A year later, he acted in the comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Divine_Secrets_of_the_Ya-Ya_Sisterhood_(film)" title="Divine Secrets of the Ya-Ya Sisterhood (film)">Divine Secrets of the Ya-Ya Sisterhood</a></i> (2002) alongside a number of famous names: <a href="/wiki/Sandra_Bullock" title="Sandra Bullock">Sandra Bullock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ellen_Burstyn" title="Ellen Burstyn">Ellen Burstyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maggie_Smith" title="Maggie Smith">Maggie Smith</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Judd" title="Ashley Judd">Ashley Judd</a>. That same year, Macfadyen took the role of Vice-Counsel Dupont in <i><a href="/wiki/Equilibrium_(film)" title="Equilibrium (film)">Equilibrium</a></i>. The film, also starring <a href="/wiki/Christian_Bale" title="Christian Bale">Christian Bale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sean_Bean" title="Sean Bean">Sean Bean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taye_Diggs" title="Taye Diggs">Taye Diggs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emily_Watson" title="Emily Watson">Emily Watson</a>, is set in the future, where a fascist regime forbids all emotions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb.com_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-imdb.com-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Macfadyen acted as Marcus Crassus in the TV version of <i><a href="/wiki/Spartacus" title="Spartacus">Spartacus</a></i>, the character of Bill in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pleasure_Drivers" title="The Pleasure Drivers">The Pleasure Drivers</a></i>, and the lead in the dramatic film <i>The Virgin of Juarez</i> (2006). He then played the pirate Blackbeard in <a href="/wiki/Blackbeard_(2006_film)" title="Blackbeard (2006 film)">a television film of the same name</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb.com_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-imdb.com-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>His next big role was that of <a href="/wiki/List_of_Saw_characters#Jeff_Denlon" title="List of Saw characters">Jeff Denlon</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Saw_III" title="Saw III">Saw III</a></i>. Jeff is a man obsessed with revenge, and he is led into a series of traps that test his ability to forgive. The film was a smash hit for its 10 million dollar budget, earning almost 200 million dollars worldwide. Macfadyen then co-starred in the <a href="/wiki/Box_office_bomb" title="Box office bomb">box office bomb</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Redline_(2007_film)" title="Redline (2007 film)">Redline</a></i> (2007); the same year he appeared in <i>Saw IV</i>, which was also a box office success.</p>
<p>Macfadyen continued making films, starring as the outlaw Will Tunney in his western film <i>Shadowheart</i> (2009). He has appeared on television on the series <i><a href="/wiki/Californication_(TV_series)" title="Californication (TV series)">Californication</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Killer_Wave" title="Killer Wave">Killer Wave</a></i>. He has also acted in the mystery film <i><a href="/wiki/San_Saba_(film)" title="San Saba (film)">San Saba</a></i> and the thriller film <i><a href="/wiki/Clean_Break_(film)" title="Clean Break (film)">Clean Break</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s">2010s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Angus_Macfadyen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Macfadyen has appeared in two thrillers: <i>Pound of Flesh</i> (2010) alongside <a href="/wiki/Malcolm_McDowell" title="Malcolm McDowell">Malcolm McDowell</a> which revolves around a corrupt college professor, and the crime thriller <i>Shadows of the White Nights</i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Christian_Slater" title="Christian Slater">Christian Slater</a>. He starred as Lucas Blackstone in the Christian film <i>Taken by Grace</i>. Additionally, Macfadyen was part of the cast of the <a href="/wiki/USA_Network" title="USA Network">USA Network</a>'s television series <i><a href="/wiki/Psych" title="Psych">Psych</a></i>, the <a href="/wiki/Cameron_Crowe" title="Cameron Crowe">Cameron Crowe</a> feature film <i><a href="/wiki/We_Bought_a_Zoo" title="We Bought a Zoo">We Bought a Zoo</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Matt_Damon" title="Matt Damon">Matt Damon</a>, and the final season of <i><a href="/wiki/Chuck_(TV_series)" title="Chuck (TV series)">Chuck</a></i> as villain Nicholas Quinn.</p>
<p>Currently Macfadyen stars as Robert Rogers in <a href="/wiki/AMC_(TV_channel)" title="AMC (TV channel)">AMC's</a> historical drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Turn:_Washington%27s_Spies" title="Turn: Washington's Spies">Turn: Washington's Spies</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Allan_Pinkerton" title="Allan Pinkerton">Allan Pinkerton</a> on the first-run syndicated series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pinkertons" title="The Pinkertons">The Pinkertons</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb.com_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-imdb.com-2">[2]</a></sup> In late 2014, he began principal photography on <i>Macbeth</i>, a feature-length film adaptation of the Shakespearean play in which he stars and directs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
