<p><b>Yana Oleksandrivna Klochkova</b> (<a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>: <span lang="uk" xml:lang="uk">Яна Олександрівна Клочкова</span>; born 7 August 1982 in <a href="/wiki/Simferopol" title="Simferopol">Simferopol</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukrainian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swimmer" title="Swimmer">swimmer</a>, who has won five Olympic medals in her career, with four of them being gold. She is Master of Sports<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, World Class and the only multi gold medal holder (more than two)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> in Ukraine.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yana_Klochkova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Her <a href="/wiki/Gold_medal" title="Gold medal">gold medals</a> came in the 200 meter <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Individual_medley" title="Individual medley">individual medley</a> and the 400 meter individual medley at the <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">2000</a> and <a href="/wiki/2004_Summer_Olympics" title="2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Summer Olympics</a>; her <a href="/wiki/Silver_medal" title="Silver medal">silver medal</a> came in the 800 meter freestyle at the <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">2000 Summer Olympics</a>. She has also won ten titles at swimming's world championships, nineteen European championship titles. She currently holds the short-course world record in the 400 meter individual medley. Her 400 m individual medley world record was broken by American Katie Hoff at the 2007 World Championships in Melbourne. In 2003 she won four golds at the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2003_Summer_Universiade" title="Swimming at the 2003 Summer Universiade">Summer Universiade</a> in South Korea. She was also awarded the <a href="/wiki/Hero_of_Ukraine" title="Hero of Ukraine">Hero of Ukraine</a> medal. In 2004, she was named by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swimming_World_magazine" title="Swimming World magazine">Swimming World magazine</a> as the <i>World Female Swimmer of the Year</i>.</p>
