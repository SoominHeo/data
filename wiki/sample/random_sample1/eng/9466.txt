<p><b>Takashi Yanase</b> (February 6, 1919 – October 13, 2013) was a Japanese writer, poet, illustrator and lyricist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He was best known as the creator of the picture book and anime series <i><a href="/wiki/Anpanman" title="Anpanman">Anpanman</a></i>. Yanase was chairman of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Japan_Cartoonists_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Japan Cartoonists Association (page does not exist)">Japan Cartoonists Association</a> from May 2000 to 2012.</p>

Three books were published concurrently with the anime of the same name, which was developed in tandem.</dd>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Comics">Comics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Takashi_Yanase&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Comics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt><i>Little Bō</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">リトル・ボオ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ritoru Bō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd>The heroic adventures of a small person wearing a hat that comes down over his eyes, run in <a href="/wiki/Takashimaya" title="Takashimaya">Takashimaya</a> department store's mail-order catalog.　A prototype for the <i>Anpanman</i> character Butterko appears here with the same name, though the name pun is different (from <i>dotabata</i>, running around noisily).</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt><i>Hot-Blooded Fairytale Masterpiece Anpanman</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">熱血メルヘン傑作アンパンマン</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd>Published in the January 1975 to May 1976 issues of <i>Monthly Poetry and Fairytales</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">月刊詩とメルヘン</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. This manga was a series of illustrated stories aimed at adults, and included the supporting characters "Nakasu Yaruse" (a cartoonist) and "Miruka Mite" (a female reporter). Never reprinted.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt><i>Anpanman</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">あんぱんまん</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd>Serialized in the September 1976 to July 1982 issues of Monthly Ichigo Ehon <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">月刊いちごえほん</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, published by <a href="/wiki/Sanrio" title="Sanrio">Sanrio</a>. The series consisted of 71 two-page chapters of six or eight panels each. In January 1981, partway through the series, the title was changed to <i>Anpanman</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">アンパンマン</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (the same name, written in <a href="/wiki/Katakana" title="Katakana">katakana</a> instead of <a href="/wiki/Hiragana" title="Hiragana">hiragana</a>). Though never republished in collected form, it included several important elaborations of the <i>Anpanman</i> mythos, detailing the character's origin story and introducing the characters Butterko, Cheese, Shokupanman, Currypanman, and Baikinman.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt><i>Fly! Anpanman</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">とべ！アンパンマン</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd>A newspaper comic that ran in the Sunday issue of the <i><a href="/wiki/Yomiuri_Shimbun" title="Yomiuri Shimbun">Yomiuri Shimbun</a></i> from 1 January 1990 to 29 May 1994, with a total of 227 strips. The series is partially reprinted in a three-volume collection that covers the first year and a half, with English translation　(by Yuriko Tamaki) in the margins. Apart from its bilingual printing, it is also known for having Baikinman as its focus character, with a number strips in which Anpanman does not appear at all.</dd>
</dl>
