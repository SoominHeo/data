<p><b>Giannitsa</b> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el" xml:lang="el">Γιαννιτσά</span> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Greek" title="Help:IPA for Greek">[ʝaniˈt͡sa]</a></span>, in English also <b>Yannitsa</b>, <b>Yenitsa</b>) is the largest city in the <a href="/wiki/Regional_units_of_Greece" title="Regional units of Greece">regional unit</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pella_(regional_unit)" title="Pella (regional unit)">Pella</a> and the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Pella_(municipality)" title="Pella (municipality)">Pella municipality</a>, in the region of <a href="/wiki/Central_Macedonia" title="Central Macedonia">Central Macedonia</a> in northern <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kallikratis_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kallikratis-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>The municipal unit Giannitsa has an area of 208.105 km<sup>2</sup>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stat01_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-stat01-3">[3]</a></sup> Its population is 31,983 people (2011 census). It includes a few outlying villages (Mesiano, Melissi, Pentaplatanos, Archontiko, Ampelies and <a href="/wiki/Damiano,_Pella" title="Damiano, Pella">Damiano</a>). The municipality Pella as a whole includes many villages and has 63,122 inhabitants. The city is located in the center of Macedonia between <a href="/wiki/Mount_Paiko" title="Mount Paiko">Mount Paiko</a> and the plain of Giannitsa, and is the economic, commercial and industrial center of the <a href="/wiki/Pella_(regional_unit)" title="Pella (regional unit)">Pella regional unit</a>. <a href="/wiki/European_route_E86" title="European route E86">European route E86</a> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_National_Road_2" title="Greek National Road 2">Greek National Road 2</a>) runs along the south of the city.</p>
<p>The former shallow, swampy, and variable-sized <a href="/wiki/Giannitsa_Lake" title="Giannitsa Lake">Giannitsa Lake</a> or Loudias Lake, fed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loudias_River" title="Loudias River">Loudias River</a> and south of the city, was drained in 1928-1932 by the New York Foundation Company.</p>
<p>About 7 km (4 mi) from Giannitsa are the ruins of ancient <a href="/wiki/Pella" title="Pella">Pella</a>, birthplace of <a href="/wiki/Alexander_the_Great" title="Alexander the Great">Alexander the Great</a> and capital of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient_Macedonia" title="Ancient Macedonia">ancient Macedonia</a>. The city is 48 km (30 mi) from <a href="/wiki/Thessaloniki" title="Thessaloniki">Thessaloniki</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Giannitsa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city was founded as <i>Yenice-i Vardar</i> 'new (town) of Vardar' in around 1372. It was sometimes called <i>Evrenos Beg yöresi</i> 'Evrenos Bey's town'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Turkish name, in the form Γενιτσά, was used until February 1926 when its name was Hellenized as Yannitsa/Γιαννιτσά.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In other languages, the city is called: <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Turkish_language" title="Ottoman Turkish language">Ottoman Turkish</a> <i>Yenice-i Vardar</i> ('new-town of Vardar', as opposed to <i>Yenice-i Karasu</i>, modern <a href="/wiki/Genisea" title="Genisea">Genisea</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Xanthi" title="Xanthi">Xanthi</a> and known for tobacco), <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a> <i>Yenice</i> or <i>Vardar Yenicesi</i>, and <a href="/wiki/Macedonian_language" title="Macedonian language">Macedonian</a>: <span lang="mk" xml:lang="mk">Ениџе Вардар</span>, <i>Enidže Vardar</i> or Пазар <i>Pazar</i> 'market'.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Giannitsa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Prehistoric</b></p>
<p>In the area of "Old Market", on the southern hill of the city, there was an Early Neolithic settlement (late 7th to early 6th millennium BC). Giannitsa was also inhabited through the Bronze and Iron Ages. Incidental findings, such as coins, inscriptions, and sculptures indicate that the area was inhabited during the Hellenistic period (323-30BC). In ancient times, the area was called <a href="/wiki/Bottiaea" title="Bottiaea">Bottiaea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Ottoman</b></p>
<p>Though there was probably a pre-existing Byzantine castle in the vicinity, the importance of the city of Yenije begins with its foundation by <a href="/wiki/Gazi_Evrenos" title="Gazi Evrenos">Gazi Evrenos</a> in around 1372. Yenije became the base of the <a href="/wiki/Ghazi_(warrior)" title="Ghazi (warrior)">ghazi</a> followers of Evrenos who took Macedonia and later Albania. The city was an important Ottoman cultural center and sacred area in the 15th and 16th centuries. Starting in the mid-15th century, Yenidje became a center of literature and the arts. Under Ahmet Bey, a descendant of <a href="/wiki/Gazi_Evrenos" title="Gazi Evrenos">Gazi Evrenos</a>, many mosques, schools, workhouses and charitable projects were founded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the early 20th century, Yenije was a battleground between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macedonian_Bulgarians" title="Macedonian Bulgarians">Bulgarian</a> and Greek-Macedonian partisans in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macedonian_struggle" title="Macedonian struggle">Macedonian struggle</a>. <a href="/wiki/Penelope_Delta" title="Penelope Delta">Penelope Delta</a>'s novel <i>Secrets of the Swamp</i> (referring to the shores of Giannitsa Lake) is a romanticised account of this from the Greek point of view.</p>

<p><b>Balkan wars</b></p>
<p>Yenidje "retained its emphatically Turkish character up to 1912" and members of the Evrenos family lived in the city in a large palace in the center of town until then.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hist_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-hist-10">[10]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/First_Balkan_War" title="First Balkan War">First Balkan War</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Yenidje" title="Battle of Yenidje">Battle of Yenidje</a> (20 October 1912) was one of the most important battles the Greek army fought.</p>
<p><b>German occupation</b></p>
<p>The German army invaded Giannitsa on April 11, 1941 . On April 20, 1941, some Austrian forces arrived. The municipal registry of Giannitsa confirms four random killings in various parts of the city. On 16 September 1943, the Municipality of Giannitsa, headed by the Mayor, Thomas Magriotis and the help of local soccer teams organized a demonstration in the city and indulge<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (August 2014)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> in German commandant a text against the intention of the Germans to surrender <a href="/wiki/Central_Macedonia" title="Central Macedonia">Central Macedonia</a> to the Bulgarians. According to oral testimony on November 13, 1943, the Germans arrested around 50 people, whom they transferred to the camp of Pavlos Melas at <a href="/wiki/Thessaloniki" title="Thessaloniki">Thessaloniki</a> and they killed thirteen. At the same time, the Germans invaded for the first time the village Eleftherohori 7 km (4 mi) away from the city, steal and destroy. In this attack there were no casualties. On 23 March 1944, the village was burned, and the place deserted. Eleftherohori lost 19 lives. On 5 August 1944, the Austrian soldier Otmar Dorne left the German occupation army and joined the 30th Constitution of the <a href="/wiki/Greek_People%27s_Liberation_Army" title="Greek People's Liberation Army">E.L.A.S</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Mount_Paiko" title="Mount Paiko">Mount Paiko</a>. The defection of Dorne, and the presence of the SS sergeant <a href="/wiki/Friedrich_Schubert" title="Friedrich Schubert">Schubert</a>, led to mass reprisals on 14 September 1944 in Giannitsa: about 120 residents of Giannitsa were executed by forces of the Jagdkommando Schubert with the collaboration of Greek units under the command of <a href="/wiki/Georgios_Poulos" title="Georgios Poulos">G. Poulos</a>. Among those executed was the Mayor, Thomas Mangriotis. The Swedish ambassador Timberg indicated that one third of the city was destroyed by fire. The citizens left the city. Emile Wenger visited Giannitsa few days after the mass execution, as a representative of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Red_Cross" title="International Red Cross">International Red Cross</a> and wrote "Giannitsa is already a dead city". On 20 September 1944, a citizens' committee sent a message to the National Government stating the facts and asking for weapons. The Germans left Giannitsa on November 3, 1944.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Landmarks">Landmarks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Giannitsa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Landmarks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Monuments</b></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tomb_of_Evrenos_in_Giannitsa.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Tomb_of_Evrenos_in_Giannitsa.jpg/220px-Tomb_of_Evrenos_in_Giannitsa.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Tomb_of_Evrenos_in_Giannitsa.jpg/330px-Tomb_of_Evrenos_in_Giannitsa.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Tomb_of_Evrenos_in_Giannitsa.jpg/440px-Tomb_of_Evrenos_in_Giannitsa.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Yenidje was an important center in the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Greece" title="Ottoman Greece">Ottoman</a> period, and several important monuments survive: <i>Tombs of <a href="/wiki/Gazi_Evrenos" title="Gazi Evrenos">Gazi Evrenos</a></i> (Built in 1417) and <i>Gazi Ahmed Bey</i>, <i>Kaifoun Baths,</i> the <i>Great Mosque</i>, the <i>Army Mosque</i>, the <i><a href="/wiki/Turkish_bath" title="Turkish bath">hammam</a> of Evrenos</i>, and the <i>Clock Tower</i> (It was built from 1667 to 1668 by Ottomans. The choice of location was made with military criteria because they wanted to control the commercial activity that was taken place across the city throughout central Macedonia). These monuments have been declared historical monuments by the Greek Archaeological Service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hist_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-hist-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Points of interest include also the Cathedral Church of Giannitsa (1860), the Neoclassical Multicenter, the Filippeio tourist center, the Macedonian tombs, and the prehistoric settlement of Archontiko.</p>
<p><b>Museums</b></p>
<ul>
<li>The <i>Military museum of Giannitsa</i>, (opened 24/2/2012) displays photographs, texts, weapons, uniforms, medals and other materials, with a particular emphasis on the Battle of the Swamp and the Balkan Wars 1912-1913.</li>
<li>The <i><a href="/wiki/Folklore_Museum_of_Giannitsa" title="Folklore Museum of Giannitsa">Folklore Museum of Giannitsa</a></i> (opened 10/1977) by the "Philippos" History and Folklore Association to promote local history and traditions.</li>
</ul>

<p><b>Statues</b></p>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Alexander_the_Great" title="Alexander the Great">Alexander the Great</a></i>, near the Cultural Center, unveiled on 20 October 2009, the anniversary of the liberation of the city and the Day of the <a href="/wiki/Macedonian_Struggle" title="Macedonian Struggle">Macedonian Struggle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Macedon" title="Philip II of Macedon">Philip II of Macedon</a></i>, located in the park of the "Filippio" tourist center. Near the statue, there is a relief depicting the Macedonian phalanx.</li>
<li><i>Black Statue - The Memorial of Giannitsà</i>, erected at the eastern entrance to the town in 1926 in honor and remembrance of the battle of Giannitsà. (Sculptor: Gregory Zevgolis).</li>
<li><i>Mass Grave</i>, a list of the residents who were executed at the 1st Primary School on 14 September 1944 by German troops.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Aravyssos</b></p>
<ul>
<li>About 10 km (6 mi) northwest of the city is the spring of Aravyssos, which produces drinking water. The surrounding grove and creek include a popular park.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Giannitsa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Giannitsa is predominantly a rural area. The draining of the Lake Giannitsà left fertile soil for agriculture, leading to population growth in the region.</p>

<li><a href="/wiki/Gazi_Evrenos" title="Gazi Evrenos">Gazi Evrenos</a> (died 1417), founder of the Ottoman city, whose mausoleum is in the center of town</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Entertainment">Entertainment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Giannitsa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Entertainment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pezodromos_Giannitsa.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1600" data-file-width="1200" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Pezodromos_Giannitsa.jpg/220px-Pezodromos_Giannitsa.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Pezodromos_Giannitsa.jpg/330px-Pezodromos_Giannitsa.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Pezodromos_Giannitsa.jpg/440px-Pezodromos_Giannitsa.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Pezodromos_Giannitsa.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Central Pedestrian street</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The focus of the social life of the city is at the central pedestrian street, where people gather to eat and drink or to take a walk. Giannitsa was one of the first cities that founded Open Theatre (3000 seats) and became an institution for cultural events and big names in theater and music, every year, the last month of summer giving a culture festival character . Also various theatrical and musical events take place in a closed theater located internally of the Cultural Centre which has a modern architecture. In the first days of September there is a big market for about a week. Also DI.K.E.P.A.P. (ΔΗ.Κ.Ε.Π.Α.Π) is a charitable non -profit cultural organization founded in 1996 that develops music, visual arts, dance, film and other arts.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Giannitsa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The most popular team is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anagennisi_Giannitsa" title="Anagennisi Giannitsa">Anagennisi Giannitsa</a> football team which plays at the Municipal Stadium.</p>
<p>There is a <a href="/wiki/Motocross" title="Motocross">motocross</a> track northwest of the city, in the foothills of Mount Paiko, where local, Greek, and European races are run.</p>
<p>At the river Loudias, there are rowing races in which the Nautical Club participates.</p>
