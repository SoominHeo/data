<p><b>Motohiro Yamaguchi</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">山口 素弘</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yamaguchi Motohiro</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born January 29, 1969)</span> is a former <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> player. He played for <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_football_team" title="Japan national football team">Japan national team</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Motohiro_Yamaguchi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yamaguchi was born in <a href="/wiki/Takasaki,_Gunma" title="Takasaki, Gunma">Takasaki, Gunma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, and played as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a>. He was capped 58 times and scored 4 goals for the <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_football_team" title="Japan national football team">Japanese national team</a> between 1995 and 1998, including three games at the <a href="/wiki/1998_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1998 FIFA World Cup">1998 FIFA World Cup</a>.</p>
