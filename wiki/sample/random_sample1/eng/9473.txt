<p><b>Yamanashishi Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">山梨市駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yamanashishi-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_station" title="Railway station">railway station</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%AB%C5%8D_Main_Line" title="Chūō Main Line">Chūō Main Line</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">East Japan Railway Company</a> (JR East), in Kamikanogawa, in the city of <a href="/wiki/Yamanashi,_Yamanashi" title="Yamanashi, Yamanashi">Yamanashi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yamanashi_Prefecture" title="Yamanashi Prefecture">Yamanashi Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It is located 122.2 rail kilometers from <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Station" title="Tokyo Station">Tokyo Station</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Layout">Layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yamanashishi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yamanashishi Station has one <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platform</a> and one <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platform</a> serving three tracks. The platforms are connected by an overpass.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yamanashishi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yamanashishi Station opened on 11 June 1903 as <b>Kusakabe Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">日下部駅</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> on the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Government_Railways" title="Japanese Government Railways">Japanese Government Railways</a> (JGR) Chūō Main Line. The JGR became the JNR (<a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a>) after the end of World War II. The station was renamed to its present name on January 15, 1962. Scheduled freight services were discontinued from November 15, 1982. With the dissolution and <a href="/wiki/Privatization" title="Privatization">privatization</a> of the JNR on April 1, 1987, the station came under the control of the East Japan Railway Company. Automated <a href="/wiki/Turnstile" title="Turnstile">turnstiles</a> using the <a href="/wiki/Suica" title="Suica">Suica</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IC_Card" title="IC Card">IC Card</a> system came into operation from October 16, 2004.</p>
