<p>Count <b>Yamada Akiyoshi</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">山田顕義</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, November 18, 1844 – November 11, 1892)</span>, was a Japanese statesman, a <a href="/wiki/Samurai" title="Samurai">samurai</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ch%C5%8Dsh%C5%AB_Province" title="Chōshū Province">Chōshū Province</a>, and one of the early leaders of the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yamada_Akiyoshi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career_as_a_samurai">Early career as a samurai</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yamada_Akiyoshi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career as a samurai">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yamada was born in what is now the city of <a href="/wiki/Hagi,_Yamaguchi" title="Hagi, Yamaguchi">Hagi, Yamaguchi</a>, as the son of a 102-<i><a href="/wiki/Koku" title="Koku">koku</a></i> samurai official of the Chōshū Navy. After studies at the domain's <i><a href="/wiki/Meirinkan" title="Meirinkan">Meirinkan</a></i> academy (where he studied the <i><a href="/wiki/Yagy%C5%AB_Shinkage-ry%C5%AB" title="Yagyū Shinkage-ryū">Yagyū Shinkage-ryū</a></i> school of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_swordsmanship" title="Japanese swordsmanship">Japanese swordsmanship</a>), he joined the <i>Shoka Sōnjuku</i> run by <a href="/wiki/Yoshida_Sh%C5%8Din" title="Yoshida Shōin">Yoshida Shōin</a> in June 1857. He was in the retinue of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=M%C5%8Dri_Motonori&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mōri Motonori (page does not exist)">Mōri Motonori</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%AF%9B%E5%88%A9%E5%85%83%E5%BE%B3" title="ja:毛利元徳">ja</a>)</span> in <a href="/wiki/Kyoto" title="Kyoto">Kyoto</a> in the autumn of 1862. A strong supporter of the <i><a href="/wiki/Sonn%C5%8D_j%C5%8Di" title="Sonnō jōi">sonnō jōi</a></i> movement, he signed his name in blood (together with <a href="/wiki/Takasugi_Shinsaku" title="Takasugi Shinsaku">Takasugi Shinsaku</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kusaka_Genzui" title="Kusaka Genzui">Kusaka Genzui</a>, <a href="/wiki/It%C5%8D_Hirobumi" title="Itō Hirobumi">Itō Hirobumi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inoue_Kaoru" title="Inoue Kaoru">Inoue Kaoru</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shinagawa_Yajir%C5%8D" title="Shinagawa Yajirō">Shinagawa Yajirō</a>) to a petition to rid Japan of the foreigners. After the Chōshū forces were driven from Kyoto by supporters of the <i><a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dbu_gattai" title="Kōbu gattai">kōbu gattai</a></i> movement, he went into exile with <a href="/wiki/Sanj%C5%8D_Sanetomi" title="Sanjō Sanetomi">Sanjō Sanetomi</a>. During this period, he studied western military science under <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cmura_Masujir%C5%8D" title="Ōmura Masujirō">Ōmura Masujirō</a>. He soon had the opportunity to put his training to practical use during the <a href="/wiki/Kinmon_incident" title="Kinmon incident">Kinmon incident</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shimonoseki_Campaign" title="Shimonoseki Campaign">Shimonoseki Campaign</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Second_Ch%C5%8Dsh%C5%AB_expedition" title="Second Chōshū expedition">Second Chōshū expedition</a>. He subsequently played a major role in the <a href="/wiki/Boshin_War" title="Boshin War">Boshin War</a>, commanding a group of 700 men under the authority of Chōshū <i><a href="/wiki/Daimy%C5%8D" title="Daimyō">daimyō</a></i> <a href="/wiki/M%C5%8Dri_Takachika" title="Mōri Takachika">Mōri Takachika</a>, starting with the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Toba%E2%80%93Fushimi" title="Battle of Toba–Fushimi">Battle of Toba–Fushimi</a>, and also commanding <a href="/wiki/Satch%C5%8D_Alliance" title="Satchō Alliance">Satchō Alliance</a> naval forces in <a href="/wiki/Mutsu_Bay" title="Mutsu Bay">Mutsu Bay</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Meiji_restoration">Meiji restoration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yamada_Akiyoshi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Meiji restoration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 1869, Yamada was received in an audience (together with <a href="/wiki/Kuroda_Kiyotaka" title="Kuroda Kiyotaka">Kuroda Kiyotaka</a>) and appointed <i>Hyōbu no dai-jō</i> (senior staff officer in the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_the_Military" title="Ministry of the Military">Ministry of the Military</a>). This rank became that of <a href="/wiki/Major_general" title="Major general">major general</a> in the fledgling <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Army" title="Imperial Japanese Army">Imperial Japanese Army</a> in July 1871. On 22 October 1871, he set sail for the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Iwakura_Mission" title="Iwakura Mission">Iwakura Mission</a>. Visiting <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_City" title="Salt Lake City">Salt Lake City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_DC" title="Washington DC">Washington DC</a>, he also went to <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> to view naval shipyards. He then returned to Japan via Paris, Berlin, the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lausanne" title="Lausanne">Lausanne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. He also visited the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weltausstellung_1873_Wien" title="Weltausstellung 1873 Wien">1873 Vienna World Exposition</a>, returning to Japan 2 June 1873. On his return, he was named <a href="/wiki/Envoy_(title)" title="Envoy (title)">Envoy Extraordinary and Minister Plenipotentiary</a> to <a href="/wiki/Qing_dynasty" title="Qing dynasty">Qing China</a> to negotiate diplomatic relations, however, <a href="/wiki/Kido_Takayoshi" title="Kido Takayoshi">Kido Takayoshi</a> went in his place, as Yamada was called upon to use military force to suppress the <a href="/wiki/Saga_Rebellion" title="Saga Rebellion">Saga Rebellion</a> and subsequently the <a href="/wiki/Satsuma_Rebellion" title="Satsuma Rebellion">Satsuma Rebellion</a> by disgruntled ex-samurai.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> On 5 July 1874 he was appointed Justice Lord under the <i><a href="/wiki/Daij%C5%8D-kan" title="Daijō-kan">daijō-kan</a></i> system, which he held to 10 September 1879. He was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Rising_Sun" title="Order of the Rising Sun">Order of the Rising Sun</a>, 2nd class in 1875, and was promoted to lieutenant general in November 1878.</p>
<p>The following year, Yamada was appointed a <i>sangi</i> (councillor), and served as head of the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Industry_(Japan)" title="Ministry of Industry (Japan)">Minister of Industry</a> (1879-1880), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Home_Ministry_(Japan)" title="Home Ministry (Japan)">Home Minister</a> (1881–1883) and <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_Justice_(Japan)" title="Minister of Justice (Japan)">Minister of Justice</a> under the first <a href="/wiki/It%C5%8D_Hirobumi" title="Itō Hirobumi">Itō</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kuroda_Kiyotaka" title="Kuroda Kiyotaka">Kuroda</a>, first <a href="/wiki/Yamagata_Aritomo" title="Yamagata Aritomo">Yamagata</a> and <a href="/wiki/Matsukata_Masayoshi" title="Matsukata Masayoshi">Matsukata</a> cabinets (1883–1891). In addition, he helped develop the modern Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legal_code" title="Legal code">legal code</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and helped establish both the <i>Koten Kokyusho</i> (present-day <a href="/wiki/Kokugakuin_University" title="Kokugakuin University">Kokugakuin University</a>) and the <i>Nihon Horitsu Gakko</i>)(present-day <a href="/wiki/Nihon_University" title="Nihon University">Nihon University</a>).</p>
<p>Yamada was elevated to <a href="/wiki/Count" title="Count">count</a> (<i>hakushaku</i>) in the <i><a href="/wiki/Kazoku" title="Kazoku">kazoku</a></i> peerage on July 7, 1884, and served in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Peers_(Japan)" title="House of Peers (Japan)">House of Peers (Japan)</a> from its establishment in 1890. On January 28, 1892, he was appointed to a seat in the <a href="/wiki/Privy_Council_of_Japan" title="Privy Council of Japan">Privy Council of Japan</a> but died in November of the same year at the age of 49, while inspecting the Ikuno Silver Mine in <a href="/wiki/Asago,_Hy%C5%8Dgo" title="Asago, Hyōgo">Asago, Hyōgo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He was posthumously awarded the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Paulownia_Flowers" title="Order of the Paulownia Flowers">Order of the Paulownia Flowers</a>. His grave is at the Buddhist temple of <a href="/wiki/Gokoku-ji" title="Gokoku-ji">Gokoku-ji</a> in Tokyo.</p>
