<p><b>Yamashina</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">山科区</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yamashina-ku</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is one of the eleven <a href="/wiki/Wards_of_Kyoto" title="Wards of Kyoto">wards</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Japan" title="Municipalities of Japan">city</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kyoto,_Kyoto" title="Kyoto, Kyoto">Kyoto</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Prefecture" title="Kyoto Prefecture">Kyoto Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It lies in the southeastern part of the city, and <a href="/wiki/Yamashina_Station" title="Yamashina Station">Yamashina Station</a> is one stop away from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kyoto_Station" title="Kyoto Station">Kyoto Station</a> on the <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dkaid%C5%8D_Main_Line" title="Tōkaidō Main Line">Tōkaidō Main Line</a> (Biwako Line).</p>
<p>The area of Yamashina-ku is 28.70 km² and the population is 135,091 as of October 1, 2016.</p>
<p>It used to be a farming village located between Kyoto and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shiga" title="Shiga">Shiga</a>, but Yamashina has now become a bedroom town for those commuting to urban area of Kyoto and <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a> because of its convenient location.</p>
<p>Historically, Yamashina was an important point for traffic connecting Kyoto and east part of Japan. In the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>, it flourished as a post town of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=T%C5%8Dkaido&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tōkaido (page does not exist)">Tōkaido</a> road (東海道 Eastern Ocean Road). There used to be many Kaido roads such as <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nara_Kaido&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nara Kaido (page does not exist)">Nara Kaido</a>, Shibutani Kaido, and Oiwa Kaido.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="sightseeing_spots">sightseeing spots</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yamashina-ku,_Kyoto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: sightseeing spots">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yamashina-ku is the location of the tomb of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Tenji" title="Emperor Tenji">Emperor Tenji</a>, the oldest Imperial tomb in Kyoto. The grave of <a href="/wiki/Sakanoue_no_Tamuramaro" title="Sakanoue no Tamuramaro">Sakanoue no Tamuramaro</a> is also here. The <a href="/wiki/Lake_Biwa_Canal" title="Lake Biwa Canal">Lake Biwa Canal</a> passes through the ward. Yamashina is home to the Ōishi Shrine and several noteworthy temples.</p>
