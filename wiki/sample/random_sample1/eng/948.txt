<p><i><b>Epic Rap Battles of History</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> video series created by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Shukoff" title="Peter Shukoff">Peter Shukoff</a> (a.k.a. Nice Peter) and <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Ahlquist" title="Lloyd Ahlquist">Lloyd Ahlquist</a> (a.k.a. EpicLLOYD). The series puts famous historical and pop culture figures, real and fictional, against one another in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rap_battle" title="Rap battle">rap battle</a> format.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forbes_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes-1">[1]</a></sup> As of January 2017, it is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_most_subscribed_users_on_YouTube" title="List of most subscribed users on YouTube">30th most subscribed channel on YouTube</a> with over 14.1 million subscribers and over 3.3 billion total video views.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although early episodes of the series featured only Nice Peter and EpicLLOYD, later episodes have regularly featured guest appearances, and other internet celebrities such as <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Donovan" title="Lisa Donovan">Lisa Donovan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Traphik" title="Traphik">Timothy DeLaGhetto</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Watsky" title="George Watsky">George Watsky</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DeStorm" title="DeStorm">DeStorm Power</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jesse_Wellens" title="Jesse Wellens">Jesse Wellens</a>, <a href="/wiki/PewDiePie" title="PewDiePie">PewDiePie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jenna_Marbles" title="Jenna Marbles">Jenna Marbles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lilly_Singh" title="Lilly Singh">Lilly Singh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ray_William_Johnson" title="Ray William Johnson">Ray William Johnson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhett_and_Link" title="Rhett and Link">Rhett and Link</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Smosh" title="Smosh">Smosh</a>. Media celebrities like rappers <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chali_2na" title="Chali 2na">Chali 2na</a> and <a href="/wiki/T-Pain" title="T-Pain">T-Pain</a>, parody artist <a href="/wiki/%22Weird_Al%22_Yankovic" title='"Weird Al" Yankovic'>"Weird Al" Yankovic</a>, and the comedian duo <a href="/wiki/Keegan-Michael_Key" title="Keegan-Michael Key">Keegan-Michael Key</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Peele" title="Jordan Peele">Jordan Peele</a> have also appeared in the series.</p>
<p>On July 3, 2013, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barack_Obama_vs._Mitt_Romney_(music_video)" title="Barack Obama vs. Mitt Romney (music video)">Barack Obama vs. Mitt Romney</a></i>, became the first of its singles to be <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">certified Gold</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) in the United States. Ten other singles, including <i>Adolf Hitler vs Darth Vader</i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Einstein_vs._Stephen_Hawking_(music_video)" title="Einstein vs. Stephen Hawking (music video)">Albert Einstein vs Stephen Hawking</a></i>, <i>Dr. Seuss vs William Shakespeare</i>, <i>Master Chief vs Leonidas</i>, <i>Steve Jobs vs Bill Gates</i>, <i>Justin Bieber vs Ludwig van Beethoven</i>, <i>Mario Brothers vs Wright Brothers</i>, <i>Mr. T vs Mr. Rogers</i>, <i>Abraham Lincoln vs Chuck Norris</i> and <i>Hitler vs Vader 2</i>, were subsequently also certified Gold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_development">History and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epic_Rap_Battles_of_History&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:NicePeter_Paris-2014_2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="617" data-file-width="1000" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/NicePeter_Paris-2014_2.jpg/220px-NicePeter_Paris-2014_2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/NicePeter_Paris-2014_2.jpg/330px-NicePeter_Paris-2014_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/NicePeter_Paris-2014_2.jpg/440px-NicePeter_Paris-2014_2.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

Dante Cimadamore (left) and Peter Shukoff (right)</div>

<p>The idea for historical figures rapping against each other was conceived by Ahlquist, who gave the idea to Shukoff as an improvised show, which was titled "<i>Check OneTwo</i>"; as the two worked on improvisation skits, along with <a href="/wiki/Zach_Sherwin" title="Zach Sherwin">Zach Sherwin</a>, who has appeared on the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forbes_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes-1">[1]</a></sup> They were met with lots of difficulty at first and decided that it would fare much better as a YouTube series than an improv show. The first three rap battles were each shot on a $50 budget.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Peter_states_that_first_3_battles_cost_approx_.2450_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Peter_states_that_first_3_battles_cost_approx_.2450-4">[4]</a></sup> On September 26, 2010, the first rap battle was uploaded to YouTube, featuring Shukoff as <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon" title="John Lennon">John Lennon</a> and Ahlquist as <a href="/wiki/Bill_O%27Reilly_(political_commentator)" title="Bill O'Reilly (political commentator)">Bill O'Reilly</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYtimes2013_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYtimes2013-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Before the web series even existed on YouTube, Nice Peter and EpicLLOYD recorded the very first song, which was a battle between <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Child%27s_Play_(film_series)" title="Child's Play (film series)">Child's Play</a></i> antagonist <a href="/wiki/Chucky_(Child%27s_Play)" title="Chucky (Child's Play)">Chucky</a> and actor <a href="/wiki/Michael_J._Fox" title="Michael J. Fox">Michael J. Fox</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The song was freestyle and poorly made; as a result, it was not released to the public. Instead, Shukoff and Ahlquist recorded their first official song for the first episode <i>John Lennon vs Bill O'Reilly.</i></p>
<p>The 15th episode, called "<i>The Final Battle"</i>, marked the end of the first season. This battle pitted EpicLLOYD and NicePeter against each other with cameos from all of the characters they had played over the fifteen episodes. <i>Epic Rap Battles of History</i> later returned on December 8, 2011, hosted on their new channel (moving from NicePeter's personal channel), <i>ERB</i>, with behind-the-scenes videos on their second channel, <i>ERB2</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Nice Peter and Maker Studios also created the official website for the series,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> where fans could vote on winners for each video, and read short comical first-person biographies by each character.</p>
<p>On September 27, 2012, following a four-month pause after the <i>Steve Jobs vs. Bill Gates</i> episode, a video was released on the series' YouTube channel where EpicLLOYD (voicing to an animation of <a href="/wiki/Theodore_Roosevelt" title="Theodore Roosevelt">Theodore Roosevelt</a>) announced that new episodes would be released every two weeks until the Christmas holidays,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> starting with the 22nd episode <i>Frank Sinatra vs. Freddie Mercury</i> (aired on October 1, 2012).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IDT_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-IDT-10">[10]</a></sup> <i>Moses vs. Santa Claus</i>, aired on December 10, 2012, was the last battle before taking a break for the holidays.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 17, 2013, <i>Epic Rap Battles of History</i> was nominated for five <a href="/wiki/Streamy_Awards" title="Streamy Awards">Streamy Awards</a>, <a href="/wiki/3rd_Streamy_Awards" title="3rd Streamy Awards">winning</a> four of them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Shukoff and Ahlquist also performed part of the <i>Steve Jobs vs. Bill Gates</i> battle live.</p>
<p>On September 9, 2013, <i>ERB</i> uploaded a trailer video on their channel announcing that <i>Epic Rap Battles of History</i> would return on October 7, 2013 for a third season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-season_3_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-season_3-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-season_3-_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-season_3--14">[14]</a></sup> On October 14, <i>ERB</i> released a second video with EpicLLOYD voicing Theodore Roosevelt, who announced the upcoming season's schedule.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The third season went on hiatus after the release of <i>Donald Trump vs. Ebenezer Scrooge</i> on December 19, 2013. In March 2014, an episode of Nice Peter's weekly show <i>The Monday Show</i>, published on March 11, and a third announcement video of <i>Epic Rap Battles of History News</i>, published on March 18, confirmed that Season 3 would continue on May 5, 2014. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%22Weird%22_Al_Yankovic" title='"Weird" Al Yankovic'>"Weird" Al Yankovic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Smosh" title="Smosh">Smosh</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rhett_and_Link" title="Rhett and Link">Rhett and Link</a> were also confirmed as guest appearances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The third season concluded with the release of <i>Artists vs. TMNT</i> on July 14, 2014.</p>
<p>On September 30, 2014, a teaser video for the fourth season of <i>Epic Rap Battles of History</i>, featuring the <i><a href="/wiki/Ghostbusters_(franchise)" title="Ghostbusters (franchise)">Ghostbusters</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/MythBusters" title="MythBusters">MythBusters</a></i>, was released; the series returned on November 10, 2014 with its 46th episode, <i>Ghostbusters vs. Mythbusters</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On December 15, 2014, when <i>Steven Spielberg vs. Alfred Hitchcock</i> was released, the fourth season went on hiatus. The season continued on May 25, 2015 and concluded with the release of the season finale, <i>Jim Henson vs. Stan Lee</i>, on August 3, 2015.</p>
<p>It was confirmed in a podcast by NicePeter that there would be a Season 5, with pre-production starting in November 2015 once they came back from the world tour.</p>
<p>On December 16, the first ever off-season battle was released, entitled, "Deadpool vs. Boba Fett".</p>
<p>On February 26, 2016, it was announced that Season 5 would begin on May 2, 2016,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and on March 22 it was announced that production had begun.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Season 5 officially began on May 2, 2016, with the release of the first battle of the season, George R.R. Martin vs. J.R.R. Tolkien. <i>ERB</i> releases battles every other Monday as of the first half of Season 5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On July 12, 2016, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_the_Great" title="Alexander the Great">Alexander the Great</a> vs. <a href="/wiki/Ivan_the_Terrible" title="Ivan the Terrible">Ivan the Terrible</a> was released. This marked the midseason break.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> EpicLloyd later confirmed they were planning to return mid to late October. Filming began for the second half of Season 5 on September 23rd. On October 26, Season 5 resumed with <i>Donald Trump vs. Hillary Clinton</i>. The final episode of season 5 was released on January 9, 2017, where EpicLloyd and NicePeter battle against each other once again, as it is a sequel to the Season 1 finale. It is unconfirmed as to whether or not ERB will be renewed for Season 6 or if Season 5 is the final season.</p>
<p>In 2016, Epic Rap Battles of History was nominated for an <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> in the newly initiated category <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Short_Form_Variety_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Short Form Variety Series">Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Short Form Variety Series</a> at the <a href="/wiki/68th_Primetime_Emmy_Awards" title="68th Primetime Emmy Awards">68th Primetime Emmy Awards</a>.</p>
