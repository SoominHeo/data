<p><b>Tatsurō Yamashita</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">山下 達郎</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yamashita Tatsurō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (occasionally credited as <b>Tatsu Yamashita</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> or <b>Tats Yamashita</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> born February 4, 1953) is a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Singer-songwriter" title="Singer-songwriter">singer-songwriter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a>.</p>
<p>Yamashita is best known for the song "Christmas Eve", which initially appeared on his album <i><a href="/wiki/Melodies_(Tatsuro_Yamashita_album)" title="Melodies (Tatsuro Yamashita album)">Melodies</a></i> in 1983 and later became a hit throughout the decades.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-christmaseve_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-christmaseve-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JP1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-JP1-4">[4]</a></sup> It is considered one of the standard Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christmas_song" title="Christmas song">Christmas songs</a>, winning the Special Prize at the <a href="/wiki/45th_Japan_Record_Awards" title="45th Japan Record Awards">45th</a> <a href="/wiki/Japan_Record_Award" title="Japan Record Award">Japan Record Awards</a> 20 years after its release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45rt_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-45rt-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tatsuro_Yamashita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1973, Yamashita formed the band Sugar Babe with musicians including <a href="/wiki/Taeko_Onuki" title="Taeko Onuki">Taeko Onuki</a> and Kunio Muramatsu, and released their sole album <i>Songs</i> two years later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sugarbabebio_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-sugarbabebio-6">[6]</a></sup> After the group disbanded in 1976, Yamashita signed to RCA and launched his own career, with release of the solo album <i>Circus Town</i>. That same year he teamed up with <a href="/wiki/Eiichi_Ohtaki" title="Eiichi Ohtaki">Eiichi Ohtaki</a>, who was the producer of Sugar Babe,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and brief Sugar Babe member Ginji Ito to release an album titled <i>Niagara Triangle Vol. 1</i>. The collaboration was cited by <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> as one of the six Japanese <a href="/wiki/Supergroup_(music)" title="Supergroup (music)">supergroups</a> that changed the history of Japanese music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> When his 1979 <a href="/wiki/Japan_Record_Award" title="Japan Record Award">award-winning</a> album <i>Moonglow</i> gained moderate success, Yamashita rose to fame.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22rt_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-22rt-9">[9]</a></sup> In 1980, his song "Ride on Time" peaked at #3 on the Japanese Oricon, and the same-titled album topped the chart subsequently.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-goodluck_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-goodluck-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jpno1album80s_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-jpno1album80s-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a solo artist, Yamashita has released 17 studio albums, 2 live albums, multiple compilations and over 40 singles. He has been one of the most commercially successful Japanese male solo recording artists in the history of the Japanese album chart, selling approximately 9 million albums in total.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-makihara_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-makihara-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon_album_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-oricon_album-13">[13]</a></sup> He has also composed for films and television commercials, and worked on records by other artists. He is particularly successful as a musical collaborator for <a href="/wiki/Mariya_Takeuchi" title="Mariya Takeuchi">Mariya Takeuchi</a>, who married him in 1982. They have one daughter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-takeuchibio_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-takeuchibio-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
