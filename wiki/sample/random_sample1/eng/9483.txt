<p><b>Yamazaki Susumu</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">山崎 烝</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, 1843? - February 6, 1868)</span> was a <a href="/wiki/Shinsengumi" title="Shinsengumi">Shinsengumi</a> officer and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spy" title="Spy">spy</a>, otherwise known as a <i>kansatsu</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">監察</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>inspector</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.</p>
<p>He was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ronin" title="Ronin">ronin</a> from <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a> and an expert in <i>Katori Ryu</i>. In 1863, he officially joined the Shinsengumi and in 1864, Yamazaki and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shimada_Kai&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shimada Kai (page does not exist)">Shimada Kai</a> were assigned by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kondo_Isami" title="Kondo Isami">Kondo Isami</a> to investigate the situation which led to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ikedaya_Jiken" title="Ikedaya Jiken">Ikedaya Jiken</a> on July 8. However, there is a theory that Yamazaki's participation in the Ikedaya Jiken is merely fiction by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shimozawa_Kan" title="Shimozawa Kan">Shimozawa Kan</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shiba_Ryotaro" title="Shiba Ryotaro">Shiba Ryotaro</a>.</p>
<p>His occupation whilst in <a href="/wiki/Kyoto" title="Kyoto">Kyoto</a> was as a doctor, and the Shinsengumi had originally used his pharmacy as their base before moving on to its later headquarters at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nishihonganji&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nishihonganji (page does not exist)">Nishihonganji</a> (See <a href="/wiki/Yamanami_Keisuke" title="Yamanami Keisuke">Yamanami Keisuke</a>).</p>
<p>Yamazaki was said to be excellent in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Literary" title="Literary">literary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Military_art" title="Military art">military arts</a>, having been taught by his uncle. His scholastic talent was necessary when discussing politics with a court noble.</p>
<p>In 1865, he accompanied Kondo to <a href="/wiki/Hiroshima" title="Hiroshima">Hiroshima</a>. It is believed that Yamazaki did not return to <a href="/wiki/Kyoto" title="Kyoto">Kyoto</a> with Kondo until the next year in order to further investigate <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%8Dsh%C5%AB_Domain" title="Chōshū Domain">Chōshū</a>'s status.</p>
<p>Yamazaki studied medicine under <a href="/wiki/Matsumoto_Jun_(physician)" title="Matsumoto Jun (physician)">Matsumoto Ryōjun</a>. According to Matsumoto, Yamazaki was a gentle and taciturn man.</p>
<p>In 1867, Yamazaki became a <a href="/wiki/Hatamoto" title="Hatamoto">hatamoto</a> along with the rest of the Shinsengumi.</p>
<p>During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Toba-Fushimi" title="Battle of Toba-Fushimi">Battle of Toba-Fushimi</a> in 1868, Yamazaki was severely injured and died on February 6. The exact location of his death is debatable, though it is believed he had drowned whilst escaping. He had Kondo and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hijikata_Toshizo" title="Hijikata Toshizo">Hijikata Toshizo</a>'s full confidence and was one of the most faithful members of the Shinsengumi till the very end of his life.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Yamazaki_in_fiction">Yamazaki in fiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yamazaki_Susumu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Yamazaki in fiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yamazaki is featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Peacemaker_Kurogane" title="Peacemaker Kurogane">Peacemaker Kurogane</a></i> (anime/manga), <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Soshite,_Haru_no_Tsuki&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Soshite, Haru no Tsuki (page does not exist)">Soshite, Haru no Tsuki</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Kaze_Hikaru" title="Kaze Hikaru">Kaze Hikaru</a></i> (manga), <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Getsumei_Seiki&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Getsumei Seiki (page does not exist)">Getsumei Seiki</a></i> (manga), <i><a href="/wiki/Shinsengumi!" title="Shinsengumi!">Shinsengumi!</a></i> portrayed by Jun Hashimoto, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bakumatsu_Renka_Shinsengumi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bakumatsu Renka Shinsengumi (page does not exist)">Bakumatsu Renka Shinsengumi</a></i> (video game series) and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hakuouki:_Shinsengumi_Kitan" title="Hakuouki: Shinsengumi Kitan">Hakuouki: Shinsengumi Kitan</a></i> (anime/<a href="/wiki/Otome_game" title="Otome game">otome game</a>). He is also depicted in the 1999 film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taboo_(1999_film)" title="Taboo (1999 film)">Gohatto</a></i>.</p>
<p>Even though the real Yamazaki was not a <a href="/wiki/Ninja" title="Ninja">ninja</a>, his anime, manga, and TV depictions tend to be a well-trained ninja including Han'nya (a character loosely based on Yamazaki) from the anime/manga <i><a href="/wiki/Rurouni_Kenshin" title="Rurouni Kenshin">Rurouni Kenshin</a></i>. Additionally, the character Yamazaki Sagaru in the anime and manga <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gintama" title="Gintama">Gintama</a></i> is roughly based on Yamazaki. Haruka Takagi, the teacher of Isami from the anime series <i><a href="/wiki/Soar_High!_Isami" title="Soar High! Isami">Soar High! Isami</a></i> is also loosely based on Yamazaki.</p>
