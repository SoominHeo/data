<p><b>Yamato</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">大和村</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yamato-son</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_villages_in_Japan" title="List of villages in Japan">village</a> located on <a href="/wiki/Amami_%C5%8Cshima" title="Amami Ōshima">Amami Ōshima</a>, in <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cshima_District,_Kagoshima" title="Ōshima District, Kagoshima">Ōshima District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kagoshima_Prefecture" title="Kagoshima Prefecture">Kagoshima Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>As of June 2013, the village had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 1,638 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 18.6 persons per km². The total area was 88.15 km².</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yamato,_Kagoshima&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yamato occupies the central portion of the northeast coast of Amami Ōshima, facing the <a href="/wiki/East_China_Sea" title="East China Sea">East China Sea</a>. The climate is classified as <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> <i>Cfa</i>) with very warm summers and mild winters. Precipitation is high throughout the year, but is highest in the months of May, June and September. The area is subject to frequent <a href="/wiki/Typhoon" title="Typhoon">typhoons</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yamato,_Kagoshima&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yamato Village was established on April 1, 1908. As with all of the Amami Islands, the village came under the administration of the United States from July 1, 1946 to December 25, 1953.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yamato,_Kagoshima&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The village economy is primarily agricultural, with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sugar_cane" title="Sugar cane">sugar cane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Horticulture" title="Horticulture">horticulture</a> as the main activities.</p>
