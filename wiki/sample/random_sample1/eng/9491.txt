<p><b>Yasuhiko Yabuta</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">薮田 安彦</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yabuta Yasuhiko</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born June 19, 1973 in <a href="/wiki/Kishiwada,_Osaka" title="Kishiwada, Osaka">Kishiwada, Osaka</a>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Pitcher" title="Pitcher">baseball pitcher</a>. He currently plays for the <a href="/wiki/Chiba_Lotte_Marines" title="Chiba Lotte Marines">Chiba Lotte Marines</a> in Japan's Nippon Professional Baseball. He competed in the <a href="/wiki/2006_World_Baseball_Classic" title="2006 World Baseball Classic">2006 World Baseball Classic</a> and struck out <a href="/wiki/Alex_Rodriguez" title="Alex Rodriguez">Alex Rodriguez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Damon" title="Johnny Damon">Johnny Damon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Derrek_Lee" title="Derrek Lee">Derrek Lee</a>.</p>
<p>Yabuta made his professional debut with the Marines in 1996, and spent 12 years with the organization. He served as a setup man for the Marines in 2007 recording a 4–6 record with a 2.73 <a href="/wiki/Earned_run_average" title="Earned run average">ERA</a> in 58 games. After the season, he declared his <a href="/wiki/Free_agent" title="Free agent">free agency</a> and came to America to pursue a Major League Baseball career. On November 22, 2007, the Kansas City Star reported that the Kansas City Royals agreed with Yabuta on a two-year contract with a club option for 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yabuta officially signed with the Royals on November 28, 2007. He signed a two-year, $6,000,000 deal with the Kansas City Royals. The deal included a $4m club option for a third year. Yabuta was sent down to Triple-A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Omaha_Royals" title="Omaha Royals">Omaha Royals</a> on June 25, 2008. He was designated for assignment on August 2 and was eventually sent outright to the minors. He was brought back to the Royals when the rosters expanded on September 1.</p>
<p>His teammates on the Royals have given him the nickname "Shake."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On November 24, 2009, he signed a one-year deal with his old team Chiba Lotte, and he has remained with the team since.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
