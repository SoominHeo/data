<p><b>Yasuj</b> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="fa" xml:lang="fa">ياسوج</span>‎‎; also <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romanize" title="Romanize">Romanized</a> as <b>Yāsūj</b>, <b>Yasooj</b>, and <b>Yesūj</b>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lurish_language" title="Lurish language">Lurish</a>: یاسووج or یاسیچ - <b>Jasuc</b> and <b>Jasyç</b>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a city in and the capital of <a href="/wiki/Kohgiluyeh_and_Boyer-Ahmad_Province" title="Kohgiluyeh and Boyer-Ahmad Province">Kohgiluyeh and Boyer-Ahmad Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>. At the 2006 census, its population was 96,786, in 20,297 families.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yasuj is an industrial city in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zagros" title="Zagros">Zagros Mountains</a> of southwestern Iran. The term "Yasuj" is also used to refer to the entire region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yasuj has both a sugar processing plant<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and a coal-burning <a href="/wiki/Power_station" title="Power station">powerplant</a> that generates electricity for the area.</p>
<p>The people of Yasuj speak <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luri" title="Luri">Luri</a> Language.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yasuj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of Yasuj has been settled since as early as the <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a>. Findings include the Martyrs Hills (dating from 3rd millennium BC), the Khosravi Hill from the <a href="/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire" title="Achaemenid Empire">Achaemenian period</a>, the ancient site of Gerd, the Pataveh bridge, and the Pay-e Chol cemetery. Yasuj is the place where <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexander_III_of_Macedon" title="Alexander III of Macedon">Alexander III of Macedon</a> and his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macedon" title="Macedon">Macedonian</a> forces stormed the <i><a href="/wiki/Persian_Gates" title="Persian Gates">Persian Gates</a></i> ("Darvazeh-ye Fars"), and fought themselves a way into the <a href="/wiki/Fars_Province" title="Fars Province">Persian heartland</a> (331 BC).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Yasuj Museum, which opened in 2002, displays coins, statues, pottery, and bronze vessels recovered from surrounding archaeological sites.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Yasuj was called Tal-e Khosrow (Khosrow Hill) in the last century.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yasuj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yasuj has the typical continentally-influenced <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_climate" title="Mediterranean climate">Mediterranean climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen</a> <i>Csa</i>) of western Iran, though because of its location in the direct line of rain-bearing winds from the Persian Gulf it is the wettest Iranian city south of the Elburz Mountains with an annual rainfall nine times that of <a href="/wiki/Isfahan" title="Isfahan">Isfahan</a> and twice that of <a href="/wiki/Kermanshah" title="Kermanshah">Kermanshah</a>. The heavy precipitation allows the existence of small <a href="/wiki/Glacier" title="Glacier">glaciers</a> on the highest Zagros peaks – in contrast the Kuhrud Mountains to the east have no glaciers despite being of the same height due to aridity.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yasuj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The economy of Yasuj is based on the following local activities:</p>
<ul>
<li>baskets</li>
<li>carpets/rugs</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mosaic" title="Mosaic">mosaic</a> tiles</li>
<li>bricks</li>
<li>livestock feed</li>
</ul>
<p>By 2014 a new refinery will be constructed by the private sector, at a cost of $2.2 billion. It will produce petrol, gasoil, kerosene, furnace oil, liquefied gas, asphalt, and sulfur.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
