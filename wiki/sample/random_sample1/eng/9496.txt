<p>The <b>Yas Marina Circuit</b> (Arabic: حلبة مرسى ياس) is the venue for the <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a>. The circuit was designed by <a href="/wiki/Hermann_Tilke" title="Hermann Tilke">Hermann Tilke</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tilke_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-tilke-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and is situated on <a href="/wiki/Yas_Island" title="Yas Island">Yas Island</a>, about 30 minutes from the capital of the UAE, <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a>. Yas Marina is the second Formula One track in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, with the first being in <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_International_Circuit" title="Bahrain International Circuit">Bahrain</a>. A two-day <a href="/wiki/GP2_Asia_Series" title="GP2 Asia Series">GP2 Asia Series</a> test was held to officially open the circuit, which was held a week before the <a href="/wiki/2009_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2009 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It also hosted the opening event for the Australian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/V8_Supercars" title="V8 Supercars">V8 Supercars</a> series, the <a href="/wiki/Yas_V8_400" title="Yas V8 400">Yas V8 400</a>, in February 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Outside motorsport the circuit was used for the final stage of the inaugural <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi_Tour" title="Abu Dhabi Tour">Abu Dhabi Tour</a> cycle race in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The circuit has <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIA" title="FIA">FIA</a> Grade 1 license.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yas_Marina_Circuit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The twenty-one corners twist through Yas Island off the Abu Dhabi coast,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> passing by the marina and through the <a href="/wiki/Yas_Viceroy_Abu_Dhabi_Hotel" title="Yas Viceroy Abu Dhabi Hotel">Yas Viceroy Abu Dhabi Hotel</a> designed and conceived by <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>-based architects Hani Rashid and <a href="/wiki/Lise_Anne_Couture" title="Lise Anne Couture">Lise Anne Couture</a> of <a href="/wiki/Asymptote_Architecture" title="Asymptote Architecture">Asymptote Architecture</a>, and winding its way through sand dunes, with several long straights and tight corners.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Marina" title="Marina">marina</a>-based development includes a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theme_park" title="Theme park">theme park</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Water_park" title="Water park">water park</a>, as well as residential areas, hotels and beaches.</p>
<p>The circuit has five grandstand areas (Main Grandstand, West Grandstand, North Grandstand, South Grandstand and Marina Grandstand (aka Support)) and part of its pit lane exit runs underneath the track. It also houses a team building behind the pit building, Media Center, Dragster Track, VIP Tower and <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_World" title="Ferrari World">Ferrari World Theme Park</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yas_Marina_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yas_Marina-11">[11]</a></sup> Additionally, one of the gravel traps runs underneath the West grandstand.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Construction">Construction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yas_Marina_Circuit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Construction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The circuit was built by main contractor <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cebarco-WCT_WLL&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cebarco-WCT WLL (page does not exist)">Cebarco-WCT WLL</a>, under contract from developer <a href="/wiki/Aldar_Properties" title="Aldar Properties">Aldar Properties</a>. Among the sub-contractors involved were <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=KOH_AH_HING&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="KOH AH HING (page does not exist)">KOH AH HING</a> from Malaysia (structural contractor), as well as specialised subcontractors like <a href="/wiki/Voltas" title="Voltas">Voltas</a> (MEP), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=PKE-Siemens&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="PKE-Siemens (page does not exist)">PKE-Siemens</a> (MEP), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Able-Middle_East&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Able-Middle East (page does not exist)">Able-Middle East</a> (earthworks), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hamilton_International&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hamilton International (page does not exist)">Hamilton International</a> (interior) and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bau_Bickhardt&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bau Bickhardt (page does not exist)">Bau Bickhardt</a> (track) to name a few.</p>
<p>The circuit was constructed with a permanent lighting system provided by <a href="/wiki/Musco_Lighting" title="Musco Lighting">Musco Lighting</a>, similar to that also installed at the <a href="/wiki/Losail_International_Circuit" title="Losail International Circuit">Losail Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Qatar" title="Qatar">Qatar</a>. Yas Marina Circuit is the largest permanent sports venue lighting project in the world; previously that title was held by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Losail" title="Losail">Losail</a>.</p>
<p>The surface of the track is made of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graywacke" title="Graywacke">graywacke</a> <a href="/wiki/Construction_aggregate" title="Construction aggregate">aggregate</a>, shipped to Abu Dhabi from a <a href="/wiki/Bayston_Hill" title="Bayston Hill">Bayston Hill</a> quarry in <a href="/wiki/Shropshire" title="Shropshire">Shropshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. The surface material is highly acclaimed by circuit bosses and Formula One drivers for the high level of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grip_(autoracing)" title="Grip (autoracing)">grip</a> it offers. The same aggregate material is used at the <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_International_Circuit" title="Bahrain International Circuit">Bahrain International Circuit</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="Bahrain Grand Prix">Bahrain Grand Prix</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-surface_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-surface-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 7 October 2009, the circuit was granted final approval to hold Formula One races by the FIA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> was the first driver to complete a test run on the circuit.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/GP3_Series" title="GP3 Series">GP3</a> visited Abu Dhabi for the first time at the end of the 2013 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yas_Marina_Circuit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the first practice sessions at the <a href="/wiki/2009_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2009 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a>, the circuit was welcomed by the drivers, with <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> commenting that every corner was 'unique',<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Drivers_praise_Abu_Dhabi_circuit_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Drivers_praise_Abu_Dhabi_circuit-15">[15]</a></sup> while double World Champion <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> echoed his sentiments, stating that it was enjoyable because there was always something to do. <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India's</a> <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> rated the circuit as being better than Formula One's other night race in <a href="/wiki/Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit" title="Marina Bay Street Circuit">Singapore</a> as he felt there was too much light at Marina Bay.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Not all of the drivers were complimentary, with <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> expressing a particular dislike of the pit exit, which dips under the main circuit by way of a tunnel. Although the pit exit remained free of incidents for the early practice sessions, Fisichella claimed that it was both very difficult and dangerous.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Drivers_praise_Abu_Dhabi_circuit_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Drivers_praise_Abu_Dhabi_circuit-15">[15]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> notoriously gave his thoughts on the circuit, stating "the first few turns are quite good, but the rest of it is shit".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
