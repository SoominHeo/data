<p><b>Yasugi</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">安来市</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yasugi-shi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Shimane_Prefecture" title="Shimane Prefecture">Shimane Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>As of April 1, 2011, the city has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 42,324, with 13,996 of <a href="/wiki/Household" title="Household">households</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 100.54 persons per km². The total area is 420.97 km<sup>2</sup> (162.54 sq mi).</p>
<p>Yasugi was elevated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_status" title="City status">city status</a> on April 4, 1954.</p>
<p>The ruins of <a href="/wiki/Gassantoda_Castle" title="Gassantoda Castle">Gassantoda Castle</a> are in Yasugi.</p>
<p>Yasugi steel is the brand name made by Hitachi Metals, Ltd. The city was an ancient steel-making (tatara) center.</p>
<p>According to legend, the goddess Izanami was buried here. In <a href="/wiki/Shinto" title="Shinto">Shinto</a>, both holy and negative concept exists.</p>
<p><b>Yasugibushi</b>, which is as known famous for folk song (<b>Minyo</b>) in Japan.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On October 1, 2004, the towns of <a href="/wiki/Hakuta,_Shimane" title="Hakuta, Shimane">Hakuta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hirose,_Shimane" title="Hirose, Shimane">Hirose</a> (both from <a href="/wiki/Nogi_District,_Shimane" title="Nogi District, Shimane">Nogi District</a>) were merged into Yasugi.</p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Adachi_Museum_of_Art" title="Adachi Museum of Art">Adachi Museum of Art</a> has <a href="/wiki/Japanese_garden" title="Japanese garden">Japanese gardens</a> and a collection of contemporary <a href="/wiki/Japanese_painting" title="Japanese painting">Japanese paintings</a>, comprising approximately 1,300 of the country's most highly regarded paintings produced after the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji period</a> and centering on the works of <a href="/wiki/Yokoyama_Taikan" title="Yokoyama Taikan">Yokoyama Taikan</a>. There is a free shuttle between JR Yasugi Station and the museum (20 minutes).</p>
