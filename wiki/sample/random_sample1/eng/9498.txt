<p><b>Kanjani Eight</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">関ジャニ∞（エイト）</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kanjani Eito</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, stylized as <b>Kanjani∞</b>)</span> is a seven-member Japanese <a href="/wiki/Boy_band" title="Boy band">boy band</a> from Japan's <a href="/wiki/Kansai_region" title="Kansai region">Kansai region</a>. They are managed by the multimedia talent agency, <a href="/wiki/Johnny_%26_Associates" title="Johnny &amp; Associates">Johnny &amp; Associates</a>, and signed to <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Records" title="Imperial Records">Imperial Records</a>. The group was formed in 2002 and made their CD debut in 2004 as "Johnny's modern <a href="/wiki/Enka" title="Enka">enka</a> group", though after the year 2006, their sound and style has become a mix of <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a>. Like the rest of the acts managed under Johnny &amp; Associates, Kanjani Eight also perform in various other areas of the Japanese entertainment industry such as variety show hosting, television, movie, and stage acting, and radio talk show hosting.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kanjani_Eight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Combination_of_two_groups:_2002.E2.80.932003">Combination of two groups: 2002–2003</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kanjani_Eight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Combination of two groups: 2002–2003">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kanjani Eight was the combination of the four top leading Kansai Juniors of the "Junior golden age" and the newly popular V. West (Five West), a rock band Kansai Junior unit. Prior to the creation of the group, <a href="/wiki/Subaru_Shibutani" title="Subaru Shibutani">Subaru Shibutani</a>, <a href="/wiki/You_Yokoyama" title="You Yokoyama">Yuu Yokoyama</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shingo_Murakami" title="Shingo Murakami">Shingo Murakami</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ryo_Nishikido" title="Ryo Nishikido">Ryo Nishikido</a> were one of the top leading juniors of the time, headlining many Junior hosted programs and acting in dramas. After the debuts of <a href="/wiki/Arashi" title="Arashi">Arashi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tackey_%26_Tsubasa" title="Tackey &amp; Tsubasa">Tackey &amp; Tsubasa</a>, the golden era was suddenly coming to an end and so was their popularity. By the year 2001 all their activity had slowed down to magazine photoshoots.</p>
<p>On the contrary, a new Kansai Junior unit named V. West was taking off in popularity, more so than their eastern counterpart FiVe. The group consisted of <a href="/wiki/Ryuhei_Maruyama" title="Ryuhei Maruyama">Ryuhei Maruyama</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shota_Yasuda" title="Shota Yasuda">Shota Yasuda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hiroki_Uchi" title="Hiroki Uchi">Hiroki Uchi</a>, Kiyohito Mizuno, and Tooru Imayama.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The group became so popular that by 2001 they had their own television show, Weekly V. West.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> But, when Mizuno and Imayama left Johnny's &amp; Associates, worry had begun to creep within unit as to their fate within the talent agency.</p>
<p>In 2002, after the end of Weekly V. West, a new program was created to replace it on Kansai TV Channel 8 entitled J3Kansai (pronounced as J Cube Kansai). This show brought together the top leading Kansai Juniors of the golden age and V. West creating the new Junior group, Kanjani, a <a href="/wiki/Portmanteau" title="Portmanteau">portmanteau</a> of the words Kansai Johnny's.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> With the success of the stage play, <i>Another</i>, the group became official garnering the name Kanjani Eight. The "8" stood for the channel that the show J3Kansai aired on. <a href="/wiki/Tadayoshi_Okura" title="Tadayoshi Okura">Tadayoshi Ohkura</a> had been added to the group as a drummer and then as an official member in the 12 episode finalizing the group as an eight-member group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kanjani Eight, as a newly formed group, held their first concert in December 2002, titled " Kanjani Eight Xmas Party 2002 ". This concert ended up becoming a yearly tradition before ending in 2005. It was also the birthplace of the group's signature act, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kanjani_Sentai_Eight_Ranger" title="Kanjani Sentai Eight Ranger">Kanjani Sentai Eight Ranger</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2003, Kanjani Eight members Nishikido and Uchi were called to go to Tokyo to become a part of <a href="/wiki/News_(band)" title="News (band)">NEWS</a>, another Johnny &amp; Associates group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Debut:_2004.E2.80.932005">Debut: 2004–2005</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kanjani_Eight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Debut: 2004–2005">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although Nishikido and Uchi were already members of NEWS, they went on to also debut as members of Kanjani Eight when the group released their debut single titled "Naniwa Iroha Bushi" in the Kansai area on August 25, 2004. The single became a local hit, capturing the traditional feel of their hometown region through the pop-enka tune. Shibutani had said, in regards to the feel of the song, " The song has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kawachinagano" title="Kawachinagano">Kawachi</a> Workman's bass, so it's entirely something new. It'd make me happy to see others dancing when they hear it." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Despite the release of the CD, their debut was lackluster, leaving many to question if the debut actually was real. The members themselves had found out of the debut through a newspaper article; Shibutani had said that he was "very surprised" when he found out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Even though there was questions about the group's debut, "Naniwa Iroha Bushi" had risen to the number-one spot on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a>'s Enka charts and reaching number-eight on the Oricon's generic music charts. When the single made its nationwide debut on September 22, it had hit number-one on both the Oricon Enka and General Music charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Much of 2004 activities were Kansai region focused in comparison their fellow acts who had the luxuries of nationwide exposure. Kanjani Eight had become hometown idols with majority of their popularity within the region but outside the area they were almost unknown. The release of their second single, "<a href="/wiki/Osaka_Rainy_Blues" title="Osaka Rainy Blues">Osaka Rainy Blues</a>", further exemplified the fact with the CD single debuting number-nine on the Oricon. To this date, "Osaka Rainy Blues" is the group's lowest single in sales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>2005 had marked the launch of Kanjani Eight's popularity even though it had opened up with the lackluster sales of their second single, "Osaka Rainy Blues". The group had been starring in three regular programs, now being aired nationwide, and were starting to get recognition as a debut Johnny's act.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> But with growing success came a scandal and in 2005 <a href="/wiki/Hiroki_Uchi" title="Hiroki Uchi">Hiroki Uchi</a> was arrested for disorderly conduct and underage drinking, being 18 at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yomiuri_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-yomiuri-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The scandal had a significant impact on Kanjani Eight as well as NEWS, the other group he was active in. Johnny's &amp; Associates removed Uchi from both units and put him under an indefinite hiatus from all activities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yomiuri_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-yomiuri-15">[15]</a></sup> Also, the drama special about the World War II <a href="/wiki/Kamikaze" title="Kamikaze">Kamikaze</a> pilots starring the group, <i>Yakusoku</i>, was canceled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Kanjani Eight continued on that year to do their first <a href="/wiki/Osaka-j%C5%8D_Hall" title="Osaka-jō Hall">Osaka-jō Hall</a> performance with one member less in the summer of 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="KJ1_F.T.O:_2006"><i>KJ1 F.T.O</i>: 2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kanjani_Eight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: KJ1 F.T.O: 2006">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the nationwide exposure and scandal in 2005, Kanjani Eight were pushing forward without looking back. Their late 2005 single, "<a href="/wiki/Sukiyanen,_Osaka/Oh!_Enka/Mugendai" title="Sukiyanen, Osaka/Oh! Enka/Mugendai">Sukiyanen, Osaka/Oh! Enka/Mugendai</a>" had shown signs of the group's growing success opening at number-two on the Weekly Oricon charts and in March 2006, the group had released their first nationwide debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/KJ1_F.T.O" title="KJ1 F.T.O">KJ1 F.T.O</a></i>, the title standing for Kanjani 1: Funky Town Osaka. The album had hit number-two on the Oricon on its debut.</p>
<p>Now known nationally and having an official album release under their belts, Kanjani Eight had begun their first tour. Named after the album's title, the F.T.O.N, Funky Tokyo Osaka Nagoya, concert tour spanned the three cities for six days and twenty performances in total. Soon after the small spring concert tour, the band launched their first nationwide tour, entitled Nationwide 1-St Tour 2006 followed up with a part two winter concert which was appropriately named after the release of their first number-one single, "<a href="/wiki/Kan_Fu_Fighting" title="Kan Fu Fighting">Kan Fu Fighting</a>". Both tours were a total of 46 performances.</p>
<p>As with all Johnny's, music and television appearances were not the only thing on the group's active schedule. 2006 had also marked the acting debut leads for Ohkura, Maruyama, and Yasuda. By the end of 2006, the group had been in more than four dramas including the Kansai TV drama special showcasing the entire band in three separate stories.</p>
<p>Kanjani Eight have also been active in several stage plays, returning once again for the third year in a row to headline the popular Johnny's &amp; Associates produced play, <i>Dream Boys</i>, with <a href="/wiki/KAT-TUN" title="KAT-TUN">KAT-TUN</a>. This year also marked the end of their summer special stage play series with the return of <i>Another</i> as <i>Another's Another</i>. Their final summer special stage play had a total of 58 performances and marked the last time Kanjani Eight would ever perform at Osaka Shochiku-za Theater.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="KJ2_Zukkoke_Dai_Dass.C5.8D:_2007"><i>KJ2 Zukkoke Dai Dassō</i>: 2007</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kanjani_Eight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: KJ2 Zukkoke Dai Dassō: 2007">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>2007 would be marked as the year of Kanjani Eight's success as a group and as a Johnny. The year had opened up with the chart-topping single, "<a href="/wiki/Kan_Fu_Fighting" title="Kan Fu Fighting">Kan Fu Fighting</a>", which was then followed by the release of their second single, "<a href="/wiki/Zukkoke_Otoko_Michi" title="Zukkoke Otoko Michi">Zukkoke Otoko Michi</a>". "Zukkoke Otoko Michi" debuted at number-one on the Weekly Charts<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and number-three for the month of April. The single went later on to be the number-one8 top selling single of 2007. Despite the popularity of the song, it still wasn't as substantial as the success of "Kanfuu Fighting" which had been number-nine on the 2007 yearly charts.</p>
<p>The release of "Zukkoke Otoko Michi" also marked the change of labels from Teichiku Records to its subsidiary, Imperial Records partly due to the change of sound that the band had. Teichiku is an Enka label and the change to Imperial allowed the band to be on a label with a broader sound as Imperial is Teichiku Entertainment's pop/rock label.</p>
<p>June 2007 was the release of their second full-length album, <i><a href="/wiki/KJ2_Zukkoke_Dai_Dass%C5%8D" title="KJ2 Zukkoke Dai Dassō">KJ2 Zukkoke Dai Dassō</a></i>. The album was a significant departure in sound from their first release, focusing the funk and rock side of things, but still didn't stop record sales as it had debut number-one on the Oricon<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and number-two overall for the month of June.</p>
<p>Majority of 2007 was spent on the road as the group had opened up 2007 holding a concert series as <a href="/wiki/Kyocera_Dome" title="Kyocera Dome">Kyocera Dome</a> entitled Eh?! Honma?! Bikkuri!! Dome Concert in Osaka (Eh? Really?! Shocking!!). The concert tour was extended to a full-length nationwide tour of the same name, breaking records of being the first pop group ever to perform in all forty-seven <a href="/wiki/Prefectures_of_Japan" title="Prefectures of Japan">prefectures</a> in the same concert tour. The <i>Eh! Honma! Bikkuri!! Tour 2007</i> had lasted five months and was a total of 113 performances.</p>
<p>In between all the concert activities, many members in Kanjani Eight were involved in other side projects. Yokoyama had starred in the Fuji TV drama, <i>Haikei, Chihiue-sama</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">拝啓、父上様</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Dear, Father</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> as well as the drama adaptation of the manga, <i><a href="/wiki/Y%C5%ABkan_Club" title="Yūkan Club">Yūkan Club</a></i>. Nishikido had starred in the Attention Please drama special and in 1 Liter of Tears. Ohkura had a role in the franchise revival of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hissatsu_Shigotonin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hissatsu Shigotonin (page does not exist)">Hissatsu Shigotonin</a> as well as a supporting role in the TBS drama, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Utahime&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Utahime (page does not exist)">Utahime</a></i>. Maruyama also held a role in the TBS special, <i>Wachigaiya Itosato</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">輪違屋糸里</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Directly following up the concert was the release of their seventh single, "<a href="/wiki/It%27s_My_Soul" title="It's My Soul">It's My Soul</a>". The song had followed the style of its preceding single and album, and managed to hit number-one on the Oricon in its debut week<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> and number-two overall for the month of October. The single managed to hit twenty-second most sold single of 2007.</p>
<p>2007 had ended with the release of the documentary photobook and the concert DVD, <i>47</i>, which was from the Tokyo Dome performance of their nationwide 2007 tour. <i>47</i> ended up becoming the top selling music DVD of 2008 on the Oricon charts, staying on for a solid 57 weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Puzzle:_2008.E2.80.932009"><i>Puzzle</i>: 2008–2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kanjani_Eight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Puzzle: 2008–2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>2008 continued the flow of the band's high-speed activity for the first half of the year. The release of their eighth single, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wahaha_(Kanjani_Eight_song)" title="Wahaha (Kanjani Eight song)">Wahaha!!</a>", met with success reaching number-one on the Weekly Oricon charts,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_Hot_100" title="Japan Hot 100">Japan Hot 100</a>, Billboard Japan Hot Single Sales, and reaching number-two7 on the top singles of 2008 (Oricon).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Right after their 4-city, 24-performance spring tour, Kanjani Eight hosted the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nippon_Television" title="Nippon Television">NTV</a> special, <i>Touch! Eco 2008: Ashita no Tame ni... 55 no Chōsen? Special</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">Touch! eco 2008 明日のために…55の挑戦?スペシャル</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Touch! Eco: For the Sake of Tomorrow... 55 Challenges? Special</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, a 13-hour television special spreading the awareness of ecology and the impact of energy waste and littering to the planet. Soon after, they hosted the sequel to their variety special, <i>Sore Yuke! Daidaman, Daidaman Z</i>.</p>
<p>Much of 2008 after June was spent with mild activities from the group. After the successful summer tour, the group released their ninth single, "<a href="/wiki/Musekinin_Hero" title="Musekinin Hero">Musekinin Hero</a>". The single marked a milestone in the group's rising stardom by breaking over 300,000 units sold. The single had reached number-one on the charts and number-eleven on the 2008 yearly charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, Kanjani Eight had released their third album <i>Puzzle</i> after nearly two years since the release of <i>Zukkoke Dai Dassō</i>. <i>Puzzle</i> was a complete departure in sound compared to their previous two albums, containing more of mixture of R&amp;B, rock, and pop songs. The album took only three months to complete and was released in April instantly garnering the number-one spot on the Oricon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The album ended up the number-one1 best-selling album in the first half 2009. After a very successful concert tour, a DVD was released from their Tokyo Dome performance. The DVD had reached number-one on the General and Music DVD Oricon charts.</p>
<p>Their tenth single, "<a href="/wiki/Ky%C5%AB_J%C5%8D_Show!!" title="Kyū Jō Show!!">Kyū Jō Show!!</a>", was released in November after nearly a year since their last single. The CD single hit number-one on the Oricon weekly charts and number-two overall for the month of November, despite the number-one spot, it did not beat the success of "Musekinin Hero".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2009, Kanjani Eight released their eleventh, twelfth, and thirteenth single. "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gift_(Kanjani_Eight_song)" title="Gift (Kanjani Eight song)">Gift</a>" was released as three separate singles, white, red and green, with different songs on each one. The single release is the band's first Christmas themed song and is said by the group to be a "Gift to the fans". It was released on the day before Christmas Eve (White), Christmas Eve (Red), and Christmas Day (Green). With these releases, Kanjani Eight became the first (and so far only) group to occupy the top three spots in the Oricon singles chart at once.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="8Uppers:_2010"><i>8Uppers</i>: 2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kanjani_Eight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 8Uppers: 2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In a news article concerning <a href="/wiki/Hiroki_Uchi" title="Hiroki Uchi">Hiroki Uchi</a> becoming involved with the Japanese production of <i><a href="/wiki/Guys_and_Dolls" title="Guys and Dolls">Guys and Dolls</a></i>, the president of Johnny &amp; Associates, <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Kitagawa" title="Johnny Kitagawa">Johnny Kitagawa</a>, stated that Uchi has been permanently removed from both Kanjani Eight and NEWS and will instead focus on solo work and acting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was announced on July 6, 2010 that the group's fourteenth single "Wonderful World!!" debuted at number-one on the Oricon weekly singles chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Kanjani Eight released their fifteenth single "Life (Me no Mae no Mukō e)" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">LIFE 〜目の前の向こうへ〜</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> on August 25, 2010, and it went on to top the Oricon weekly singles chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fight:_2011"><i>Fight</i>: 2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kanjani_Eight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Fight: 2011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>2011 opened up with a triple single release from the group. <a href="/wiki/T.W.L/Yellow_Pansy_Street" title="T.W.L/Yellow Pansy Street">T.W.L/Yellow Pansy Street</a> was the first; a double-A side single that was released on April 20, 2011. Yellow Pansy Street served as the theme song to the 19th Shin-chan movie, <a href="/wiki/Crayon_Shin-chan:_The_Storm_Called:_Operation_Golden_Spy" title="Crayon Shin-chan: The Storm Called: Operation Golden Spy">The Storm Called: Operation Golden Spy</a> and T.W.L was the 14th theme song for the <a href="/wiki/Crayon_Shin-chan" title="Crayon Shin-chan">animated show</a>. The single immediately shot to no. 1 on the Oricon charts. Ohkura and Murakami both guest starred in the theatrical movie as two bumbling anti-heroes whom Shin-chan unexpectedly gets in their way.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Following that release, in March 2011, Kanjani Eight released the second single in the series of releases with <a href="/wiki/My_Home_(song)" title="My Home (song)">My Home</a>. This single was the theme song to Nishikido's <a href="/wiki/TV_Asahi" title="TV Asahi">TV Asahi</a> drama, <i>Inu wo Kau to Iu koto ~Sky to Waga ga Uchi no 180 Nichi ~</i>. The triple release project was concluded with the single titled, <a href="/wiki/365_Nichi_Kazoku" title="365 Nichi Kazoku">365 Nichi Kazoku</a>, which was the theme song to Ohkura's <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Broadcasting_System" title="Tokyo Broadcasting System">TBS</a> drama, <i>Umareru.</i> Both singles earned the top spot on the Oricon charts and remained on the charts for 13 weeks respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 6, 2011, Kanjani Eight was announced to be the hosts of the 34th annual 24 Hour Television, a televised charity event produced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nippon_Television" title="Nippon Television">NTV</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> After the devastating <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">March 11th Touhoku tsunami and earthquake</a>, Kanjani Eight were selected because the members themselves were affected by the <a href="/wiki/Great_Hanshin_earthquake" title="Great Hanshin earthquake">1995 Hanshin earthquake</a>, nearly 16 years earlier. Murakami commented, in regards to being selected to host the fund raising event, " Now it's our turn to return the favor ", in regards to the help given to them during the 1995 quake. Yokoyama stated, " I can still remember the screams and the parents protecting us. We, Kanjani8, have experienced a devastating earthquake, but today Kobe and Osaka are doing great. I want to convey to the people of Touhoku how if we become one we can overcome (anything)!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> The theme for that year's 24 Hour Television was, " Strength ", in response to the perseverance of the victims of the 2011 earthquake.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2011, all the members of Kanjani Eight were announced to appear in the 24 Hour Television drama special titled, <i>Ikiteru dake de nankurunai sa</i>. It had already been announced that Murakami Shingo had the leading role and Shibutani Subaru had the supporting role as his friend since childhood, but it was revealed on July 19 that everyone else in Kanjani Eight would make an appearance in the drama. This was a historical first for the charity event as only one member of the hosting group would star in the special.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> The drama aired the night of August 20, 2011 during the 24 Hour Television broadcast. It was later released on DVD on November 26, 2011. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>

<p>Their fifth album, Fight, was released on November 17, 2011 and reached no. 1 on the Oricon charts with a total of 253,524 units sold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> <i>Monjai Beat</i> served as the supporting track for the album and promotion began in October and continued throughout the rest of the year. The album closed out the year as the 21st top sold album of 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> Fight was later certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="8EST:_The_8th_Anniversary:_2012.E2.80.93present"><i>8EST: The 8th Anniversary</i>: 2012–present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kanjani_Eight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 8EST: The 8th Anniversary: 2012–present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 1, 2012, during Kanjani8's yearly countdown concert at Kyocera dome, the group announced their 8th anniversary after the New Year's celebrations. A large sign was displayed on the overhead LED screens congratulating the group on 8 years. Because of how significant the number 8 is to the group's image and namesake, the 8th anniversary was a milemarker celebration for their career. The concert also kicked off the return of the Eight Rangers, the group appearing on stage in their suits during the final encore of the performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p>To mark the beginning of the 8th anniversary celebrations, in February 2012 it was announced that Ryo Nishikido would star in a drama titled, <i>Papa wa Idol</i> or, officially in English, <i>My Papa is an Idol</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> It was also announced that Kanjani Eight themselves would appear in the drama to add to the 8th anniversary celebrations. The goal of the drama was to blur the lines between reality and fiction, so TBS dedicated some of their prime time programming to coincide with the events going on within the drama.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> To even further blur the lines, the entire cast performed a real wedding at a chapel which was broadcast on live television prior to the premiere of the drama.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Golden_Week_(Japan)" title="Golden Week (Japan)">Golden Week</a> special aired in May, tying into the <i>Papadoru</i> theme, in which the members of Kanjani Eight split into teams of two to assume the roles as fathers and take care of children. Yasuda Shota was the only one not in a team and filmed his segment in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Papau_New_Guinea" title="Papau New Guinea">Papau New Guinea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>In early March, a movie adaptation of the fan-favorite skit, <a href="/wiki/Kanjani_Sentai_Eightranger" title="Kanjani Sentai Eightranger">Eight Ranger</a>, was announced. The movie was to be directed by <a href="/wiki/Yukihiko_Tsutsumi" title="Yukihiko Tsutsumi">Tsutsumi Yukihiko</a>, known for his <i><a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Boys" title="20th Century Boys">20th Century Boys</a></i> movie series and drama work on Ikebukuro West Gate Park and SPEC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> The movie would take a more grim and darker approach to the otherwise childish skits, introducing characters with alcoholism and gambling addiction. Shortly after the movie announcement, it was later revealed that the movie production would collaborate with the Papadoru production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> Various other tie ins and collaborations followed with different companies such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/7-11" title="7-11">7-11</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sanrio" title="Sanrio">Sanrio</a>, Scalabo, and others. The movie was released in theaters on July 28, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first of Kanjani Eight's anniversary singles were announced on April 3, 2012. Titled <i>Ai Deshita</i>, it served as the theme song for the TBS drama <i>Papadoru</i>. Filming for the promotional video as well as CD jacket art and promotional materials were incorporated into the actual drama. The group performed the song, live, in the final episode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> The June release date was announced on April 17 and the CD track listing was announced in May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> <i>Ai Deshita.</i> was the number one top selling single on the Oricon charts upon release and stayed on the charts for a total of 15 weeks making it their 7th consecutive single to reach number one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<p>The second of Kanjani Eight's anniversary singles was announced shortly after the release of "Ai Deshita", on June 21, 2012 titled "ER". It would serve as the theme song to movie, <i>Eight Rangers</i>. What made this single unique from the rest was that it was released under the pseudonym, Eight Rangers, rather than the group name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> It was revealed that the single would go on sale on July 25, 2012; three days before the actual movie would have its nationwide release. The single had three versions, two of the versions including the music video for the A-side, directed by Tsutsumi Yukihiko. Track listing also included a longer, studio version of the song, "∞Rangers", which was used as theme song for the concert skits. The single, upon release, reached number one on the Oricon and remained on the charts for a total of 20 weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> "ER" was the group's 8th consecutive number one hit and also managed to become the group's second highest grossing single to date, putting "Kanfu Fighting", in third place after four years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup></p>

<p>In June, a fan appreciation event was announced. On August 8, 2012, Kanjani Eight performed "Matsubara Shinichi Presents: Sugohachi" at Kyocera Dome Osaka, a free live to winners of a lottery raffle. The raffle tickets were included in the releases for "Ai Deshita" and "ER". Roughly 40,000 winning tickets were available in that raffle. Also, during that same month, Kanjani Eight produced and performed at <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_Kanjani_Eight_concerts" title="List of Kanjani Eight concerts">Hasai Bokura mo Hacchai, Hacchake Matsuri</a></i> (8sai), pay-for fan appreciation event that took place in Tokyo on the weekend of August 5 and in Osaka on the weekend of August 10 respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> The "<a href="/wiki/List_of_Kanjani_Eight_concerts" title="List of Kanjani Eight concerts">Sugohachi</a>" performance involved Kanjani Eight playing a game where they moved around a game board, performing tasks related to the group's history. At the end of the event, they had a countdown of the top 5 best songs selected by fans in a survey taken at the Tokyo 8sai event.</p>
<p>The Hasai Bokura mo Hacchai, Hacchake Matsuri was a festival with various activities for fans to take part of spanning an entire day. A website was launched with information shortly after the announcement. Tickets were sold, initially as a lottery, to the general public online or through phone via Ticket PiA. After the results were release, a general sale happened as a first-come, first-served. Attendees were allowed to select the day and group that they wanted to go in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> Open to the general population, the event split up attendees into five different groups, each assigned a different talk and live time. The talks involved Kanjani Eight taking part in games in order to win center position on their next release while the lives had the group perform a mini concert, one half being dance tracks and the other half being acoustic. The title, Hasai Bokura mo Hacchai, Hacchake Matsuri (∞祭　ボクらも８っちゃい、８っちゃけ祭) is a pun that does not directly translate into English. Hasai can be read as Eight Festival or taken as a double meaning for Eight Years. Hacchai/Hacchake is more of an expression to explain someone who's really over the top or has really gone beyond the norm.</p>
<p>On August 6, Kanjani Eight's third anniversary single, "Aoppana", was announced. "Aoppana" was the theme song to the drama, <i>Boys on the Run</i>, which starred member <a href="/wiki/Ryuhei_Maruyama" title="Ryuhei Maruyama">Maruyama Ryuhei</a> in the lead. The single was released on September 9, 2012, and had two editions—a regular and limited edition. The first press of the regular edition of the song contained a photo album that featured the childhood photos of each member while the limited edition single contained the DVD of the promotional video as well as the behind the scenes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> The cover art was also different, with the regular edition featuring Kanjani Eight as children and the limited edition featuring the group as their adult selves doing the same poses. The single shot to number one upon release with a total of 280,000 units sold. It stayed on the charts for 21 weeks and is the group's tenth highest grossing single to date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
<p>In early September, Kanjani Eight's first <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">compilation album</a> was announced. Titled, <i>8EST: Eight x Eighter = Eightest!</i>, three versions of the album was released. Two limited editions along with a normal edition. Limited edition A contained the live performances from 8sai as well as a documentary about the production of the festival. It also contained a digest of "Matsubara Shinichi Presents: Sugohachi". Limited edition B contained all five days of the 8sai live talk segments, the "Hohoemi Date", a punishment game that the two worst performing members in the 8sai games had to do, and a postcard set featuring the promotional artwork of all their singles up to Crouton, the only original song from <i>8EST</i>. The album contained all of the group's A-side releases up to "ER", except for the normal edition which contained all the songs from <a href="/wiki/Gift_(Kanjani_Eight_EP)" title="Gift (Kanjani Eight EP)">GIFT</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> <i>8EST</i> reached number one on the Oricon charts with 298,000 units sold, the first compilation album of theirs to do so. It was also their 5th consecutive single since their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/KJ2_Zukkoke_Dai_Dass%C5%8D" title="KJ2 Zukkoke Dai Dassō">KJ2 Zukkoke Dai Dassou</a></i>, to reach number one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></p>

Bokura no omoi wa mugendai!</a></i>, it was scheduled for ten cities with a total of 35 performances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> The tour kicked off with the group's first stadium performance at <a href="/wiki/Ajinomoto_Stadium" title="Ajinomoto Stadium">Ajinomoto Stadium</a> in Tokyo on September 15, 2012. During those performances, it was then announced that the tour would be extended to include two dome performances at Tokyo and Osaka. <p>The concert tour continued on with the arena and dome performances. In support of their compilation album, the group only performed their most popular songs and unit/solos. The arena performances had a different setlist than the dome performances, with the arena being a condensed version of the stadium performances (containing shorter songs and a new Eight Rangers skit) while the dome performances axed the skit for longer songs and solos. Tickets for the concert were sold through the fanclub while the Tokyo and Osaka dome extensions were opened up to the general public through playguide and sold via Lawson Ticket. Also, different this year was the absence of a countdown live. This was because Kanjani Eight was to perform for the first time at the NHK Kouhaku Uta Gassen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup></p>
<p>The group was the 5th artist by total sales revenue in Japan in 2012 and 2013, with ¥4.916 billion and ¥4.738 billion, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014:_Launching_of_the_independent_record_label_.27Infinity_Records.27">2014: Launching of the independent record label 'Infinity Records'</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kanjani_Eight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2014: Launching of the independent record label 'Infinity Records'">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Right after their 10th anniversary celebrations(十祭, Jussai), Kanjani Eight announced launching of the independent record label 'Infinity Record' that was named by members. Their contract with record company Teichiku Entertainment has expired on the 24th and they have since launched own independent label, to be under the umbrella of J-storm. With the independent label, there will be an increased responsibility as members seek to pursue Kanjani Eight-ness. They will be responsible for production, from song arrangement and recording, release time, and concert planning. According to Murakami Shingo, this has been decided so that "we can show our colors and ideas more deeply" and promises growth as a group. Additionally, launching of the independent record label is first time as a boy group of Johnny &amp; Associations.</p>
<p>The group was the 3rd artist by total sales revenue in Japan in 2014, with <a href="/wiki/Japanese_yen" title="Japanese yen">¥</a>5.862 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sub-units">Sub-units</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kanjani_Eight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Sub-units">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Within Kanjani Eight are several subunits created by the members for various reasons, either for a creative outlet or for other special reasons such as concerts. Out of all the units created between them, only <b>SanKyōDai</b> and <b>SubaruBand</b> have made it onto actual albums.</p>
<p><b>SubaruBand</b> – A rock band that is composed of Shibutani (vo), Maruyama (ba), Yasuda (g), and Ohkura (dr). The group made its first appearance in 2002 at the Kanjani Eight Xmas Party. The songs are composed by Yasuda, lyrics written by Shibutani, and arranged by the band. Notable songs are "One", "High Position", and "Down up ↑"</p>
<p><b>SanKyōDai</b> – An acoustic band as well as a full sounding band with Maruyama and Ohkura on support. Its main members are Yuu (Yokoyama), Chipa (Yasuda), and Baru (Shibutani) and their first appearance was at the 2003 Kanjani8 Xmas Party. Up until the Christmas 2005 show, SanKyoudai had released new song, these songs being, "Purin" (Pudding), "Amechan" (Candy), "Mikan" (Oranges), and "Onigishi" (Onigiri). Lyrics are written by Yuu and songs composed by Chipa.</p>
<p><b>YamaDa</b> – A <a href="/wiki/Manzai" title="Manzai">manzai</a> duo created by Maruyama and Yasuda in which Maruyama plays the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_owarai_terms#boke" title="Glossary of owarai terms">boke</a> and Yasuda plays the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tsukkomi" title="Tsukkomi">tsukkomi</a>.</p>
<p>There are also other lesser known subunits that the group has created such as OhYamaDa (band), SanBaka (Shibutani, Yokoyama, Murakami), Yasuba (Yasuda and Shibutani), and others.</p>
