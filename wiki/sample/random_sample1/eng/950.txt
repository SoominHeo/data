<p>"<b>Everytime</b>" is a song by American recording artist <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a>, taken from her fourth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Zone" title="In the Zone">In the Zone</a></i> (2003). It was released on May 10, 2004 by <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a> as the third <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a> from the album. After her relationship with American singer <a href="/wiki/Justin_Timberlake" title="Justin Timberlake">Justin Timberlake</a> ended in 2002, she made friends with her backing vocalist <a href="/wiki/Annet_Artani" title="Annet Artani">Annet Artani</a>. They started writing songs together at Spears's house in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, and then traveled to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lombardy,_Italy" title="Lombardy, Italy">Lombardy, Italy</a>, where "Everytime" was written. Spears composed the music and she developed the lyrics with Artani. According to Artani, the song was written as a response to Timberlake's 2002 song "<a href="/wiki/Cry_Me_a_River_(Justin_Timberlake_song)" title="Cry Me a River (Justin Timberlake song)">Cry Me a River</a>". Spears has neither confirmed nor denied these allegations.</p>
<p>"Everytime" is considered a pop <a href="/wiki/Sentimental_ballad" title="Sentimental ballad">ballad</a> with breathy vocals. Its lyrics are a plea for forgiveness for inadvertently hurting a former lover. Music critics mostly complimented its lyrical content, deeming the song "organic" in comparison with most of the tracks on its host album. Spears' maturity in the production and her songwriting abilities were also complimented. "Everytime" peaked inside the top five in several countries, while reaching the top of the charts in Australia, Hungary, Ireland and the United Kingdom. In the U.S. it was a top 20 single, peaking at number 15 on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. Spears performed "Everytime" in a series of live appearances such as television shows <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Top_of_the_Pops" title="Top of the Pops">Top of the Pops</a></i>. She has also performed the song on piano in a flowered-themed setting at <a href="/wiki/The_Onyx_Hotel_Tour" title="The Onyx Hotel Tour">The Onyx Hotel Tour</a> (2004), while suspended on a giant umbrella at <a href="/wiki/The_Circus_Starring_Britney_Spears" title="The Circus Starring Britney Spears">The Circus Starring Britney Spears</a> (2009) and in an angel costume at <a href="/wiki/Britney:_Piece_of_Me" title="Britney: Piece of Me">Britney: Piece of Me</a> (2013). "Everytime" has been covered by artists such as <a href="/wiki/Glen_Hansard" title="Glen Hansard">Glen Hansard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Clarkson" title="Kelly Clarkson">Kelly Clarkson</a>, and was used in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Spring_Breakers" title="Spring Breakers">Spring Breakers</a></i> (2013).</p>
<p>Inspired by the cinematography of <i><a href="/wiki/Leaving_Las_Vegas" title="Leaving Las Vegas">Leaving Las Vegas</a></i>, the music video for "Everytime" portrays Spears as a star hounded by paparazzi, who drowns in her bathtub when she starts bleeding from a wound in her head. In the hospital, doctors fail to resuscitate her while a child is born in the next room, implying she has been <a href="/wiki/Reincarnation" title="Reincarnation">reincarnated</a>. The original treatment would have had Spears killing herself from a drug overdose, but the plot was removed after it received criticism by organizations such as <a href="/wiki/Kidscape" title="Kidscape">Kidscape</a>, who perceived it as a glamorization of suicide. Critics noted the video for its religious references to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Passion_of_the_Christ" title="The Passion of the Christ">The Passion of the Christ</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Kabbalah" title="Kabbalah">Kabbalah</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stigmata" title="Stigmata">stigmata</a>, and for predicting Spears's future struggles with fame.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_writing">Background and writing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everytime&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and writing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>Spears's relationship of three years with pop singer <a href="/wiki/Justin_Timberlake" title="Justin Timberlake">Justin Timberlake</a> ended in 2002 after months of speculation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2legchanges_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-2legchanges-2">[2]</a></sup> In November 2002, Timberlake released the song "<a href="/wiki/Cry_Me_a_River_(Justin_Timberlake_song)" title="Cry Me a River (Justin Timberlake song)">Cry Me a River</a>" as the second single from his <a href="/wiki/Justified_(album)" title="Justified (album)">solo debut album</a>. The music video featured a Spears look-alike and fueled the rumors that she had been unfaithful to him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-itself_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-itself-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sawyer_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-sawyer-4">[4]</a></sup> "Cry Me a River" is often credited as being the song that propelled the album into the charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-justinrs_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-justinrs-5">[5]</a></sup> In September 2001, <a href="/wiki/Annet_Artani" title="Annet Artani">Annet Artani</a> accepted to become a <a href="/wiki/Backing_vocalist" title="Backing vocalist">backing vocalist</a> for Spears's 2001–2002 <a href="/wiki/Dream_Within_a_Dream_Tour" title="Dream Within a Dream Tour">Dream Within a Dream Tour</a>. Her interactions with Spears during most of the tour were limited to small conversations at the gym and vocal warm ups. Artani had begun a relationship with the show's musical director during 2002; however, it was not working out well by the end of the tour. Before the last date in <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a>, Spears called her and asked about the relationship. Artani told her they were going to break up, to which Spears responded, "Don't worry about it, you're going to hang out with me".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-muu_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-muu-6">[6]</a></sup> Following the end of the tour, Spears and Artani began to forge a friendship. Spears invited Artani to her house in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a>. According to Artani, their relationship grew out of their shared romantic experiences at the time. She explained, "Basically, we commiserated because she, at that time, had broken up with Justin [Timberlake]. Maybe like nine months before, but of course it was really fresh in the media. I was just breaking up with this guy, so we kind of like—I think we kind of needed each other." Artani stayed at Spears's house for a few weeks, in which they started writing songs at the piano. Shortly after, they traveled to <a href="/wiki/Lake_Como" title="Lake Como">Lake Como</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lombardy,_Italy" title="Lombardy, Italy">Lombardy, Italy</a>. Artani added, "It was me and her, her stylist and Felicia, and we had this humongous house to ourselves, and they had a piano there as well".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-muu_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-muu-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to Artani, "Everytime" was written in large part as a response to "Cry Me a River" as well as various radio interviews. Artani explained, "He was getting personal. Here, she had a different type of image, and he was really exposing some stuff that she probably didn't want out there, and in front of <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Lynn_Spears" title="Jamie Lynn Spears">her little sister</a> ... I remember her sister being mortified and her being mortified. I'm sure that that really hurt her."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-muu_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-muu-6">[6]</a></sup> The song was also speculated to be a reply to Timberlake's "Never Again", a ballad which appeared on his debut solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Justified_(album)" title="Justified (album)">Justified</a></i>. "Everytime" was recorded at Conway Studios in Los Angeles and mixed at Frou Frou Central in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London,_United_Kingdom" title="London, United Kingdom">London, United Kingdom</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-liner_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-liner-7">[7]</a></sup> During an interview with Hip Online, Spears commented about the recording sessions, saying,</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"... Like with 'Everytime' I wrote the whole thing from scratch on the piano. Musically there was no track or anything. I was just at my house and I did the whole thing by myself. And then I went and I played it for [Guy Sigsworth] and I just basically told him exactly how I wanted the song to sound. And he was so amazing because there's a lot of producers you tell them things and they don't get it. And you're like oh, that's not the right way. He got it just right. He was amazing. And so that song specifically, you know, I did everything."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hiponline_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-hiponline-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>"Everytime" was one of the first songs finished for <i>In the Zone</i>, previewed on May 30, 2003, to Quddus Philippe of <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> at Battery Studios in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-preview_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-preview-9">[9]</a></sup> It was registered with the U.S. Copyright Office on April 26, 2003 under the title of "Everytime I Try" and SRU000530591 registration number with a given recording year of 2002. Spears named it the most personal song of the album along with "Touch of My Hand", explaining, "It's one of the songs that when you hear, it's like the kind of song when you go to heaven. It kind of takes you away. You know, it takes you in to a very cool consciousness I think."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hiponline_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-hiponline-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>"Everytime" is a <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ballad_(music)" title="Ballad (music)">ballad</a>. It begins with a music box introduction accompanying Spears's breathy vocals, which build from soft to strong through the song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-preview_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-preview-9">[9]</a></sup> According to the sheet music published at Musicnotes.com by <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Publishing_Group" title="Universal Music Publishing Group">Universal Music Publishing Group</a>, "Everytime" is composed in the key of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/E%E2%99%AD_major" title="E♭ major">E♭ major</a>, with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tempo_(music)" title="Tempo (music)">tempo</a> of 110 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beats_per_minute" title="Beats per minute">beats per minute</a>. Spears's vocal range spans from the low-tone of A♭<sub>3</sub> to the high-tone of E♭<sub>5</sub>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sheet_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-sheet-10">[10]</a></sup> "Everytime" lyrics are a plea for forgiveness for inadvertently hurting a former lover. In the song, the singer explains she feels unable to continue in lines such as "Everytime I try to fly I fall / Without my wings I feel so small". Jennifer Vineyard of MTV compared the song lyrically to another ballad from <i>In the Zone</i>, "Shadow", since they both speak "about how reminders of a lover can still linger after he's gone."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-albumpreview_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-albumpreview-11">[11]</a></sup> During an interview with Jennifer Vineyard of MTV, Spears said about "Everytime", "It's about heartbreak, it's about your first love, your first true love. That's something all people can relate to, because you all have that first love that you think you're going to be with the rest of your life."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rubber_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-rubber-12">[12]</a></sup> When asked if "Everytime" was about Timberlake during an interview with <a href="/wiki/Diane_Sawyer" title="Diane Sawyer">Diane Sawyer</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Primetime_(TV_series)" title="Primetime (TV series)">PrimeTime</a></i>, she responded "I'll let the song speak for itself."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-itself_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-itself-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everytime&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gavin Mueller of <i><a href="/wiki/Stylus_Magazine" title="Stylus Magazine">Stylus Magazine</a></i> deemed "Everytime" as the best track of <i>In the Zone</i>, explaining "it is just a spare piano ballad, simple yet effectively fragile".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stylus2_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-stylus2-13">[13]</a></sup> Ali Fenwick of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Johns_Hopkins_News-Letter" title="The Johns Hopkins News-Letter">The Johns Hopkins News-Letter</a></i> complimented Spears's songwriting and added the song "shows a glimmer of the talent that hides behind the robotic, synthed-out vocals on the rest of the album."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-johnhopkins_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-johnhopkins-14">[14]</a></sup> Christy Lemire of <a href="/wiki/MSNBC" title="MSNBC">MSNBC.com</a> called it "actually a pretty tune" and named it the best ballad in <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits:_My_Prerogative" title="Greatest Hits: My Prerogative">Greatest Hits: My Prerogative</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msnbc_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-msnbc-15">[15]</a></sup> Jason Shawhan of <a href="/wiki/About.com" title="About.com">About.com</a> said "Everytime" "screams Single!".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-about-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Spence D. of <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a> said the song "continues to mine the <i>Zone</i> turf and unleashes what is ostensibly Britney's first mature ballad, at least in terms of being musically staid and stripped of any danceteria sweat and gloss."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ign_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-ign-17">[17]</a></sup> Linda McGee of <a href="/wiki/RT%C3%89.ie" title="RTÉ.ie">RTÉ.ie</a> said that along with <i>In the Zone</i>'s "Brave New Girl", they were "individually impressive", but disrupted the direction of the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rte_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-rte-18">[18]</a></sup> David Browne of <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> commented, "With its dainty piano, 'Everytime' plays like a forlorn postmortem on her Justin Timberlake era."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-ew-19">[19]</a></sup> Sterling Clover of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Village_Voice" title="The Village Voice">The Village Voice</a></i> called it "a weeper in the best '<a href="/wiki/Time_After_Time_(Cyndi_Lauper_song)" title="Time After Time (Cyndi Lauper song)">Time After Time</a>' (1984) tradition."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vv_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-vv-20">[20]</a></sup> William Shaw of <i><a href="/wiki/Blender_(magazine)" title="Blender (magazine)">Blender</a></i> said that while "Everytime" was not her greatest ballad, the lyrics were "certainly heartfelt".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blenderint_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-blenderint-21">[21]</a></sup> A reviewer from the <i><a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Daily_Examiner" title="Huddersfield Daily Examiner">Huddersfield Daily Examiner</a></i> stated, "[the] breathy ballad [has] got a stage musical feel to it, but Britney's no <a href="/wiki/Elaine_Paige" title="Elaine Paige">Elaine Paige</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hudder_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-hudder-22">[22]</a></sup> Sal Cinquemani of <i><a href="/wiki/Slant_Magazine" title="Slant Magazine">Slant Magazine</a></i> named it along with "Shadow" "two sappy ballads".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-slant_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-slant-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everytime&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On May 22, 2004, "Everytime" debuted at number 61 on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, becoming the "Highest Debut" of the week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> On July 3, 2004, it peaked at number 15 and stayed in the position for four weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The song also peaked at number four on <i>Billboard</i>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mainstream_Top_40_(Pop_Songs)" title="Mainstream Top 40 (Pop Songs)">Pop Songs</a> and at number 17 and number 25 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Club_Songs" title="Hot Dance Club Songs">Hot Dance Club Songs</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Adult_Top_40_Tracks" title="Hot Adult Top 40 Tracks">Adult Pop Songs</a> charts, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcharts_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbcharts-26">[26]</a></sup> On November 18, 2004, "Everytime" was certified <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">gold</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) selling 500,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riaacerti_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-riaacerti-27">[27]</a></sup> As of July 2010, "Everytime" has sold 469,000 paid <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital downloads</a> in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upsales_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-upsales-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Canada, the song peaked at number two on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Singles_Chart" title="Canadian Singles Chart">Canadian Singles Chart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Australia, "Everytime" debuted at the top of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ARIA_Singles_Chart" title="ARIA Singles Chart">ARIA Singles Chart</a> on the week of June 28, 2004, becoming the "Highest Debut".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> It received a gold certification by the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Recording_Industry_Association" title="Australian Recording Industry Association">Australian Recording Industry Association</a> (ARIA) for shipments over 35,000 units.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ariacertification_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-ariacertification-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the United Kingdom, the single debuted at the top of the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> on June 20, 2004 ― for the week ending date June 26, 2004 ― becoming her second consecutive number one single in Britain from <i>In the Zone</i>, following "<a href="/wiki/Toxic_(song)" title="Toxic (song)">Toxic</a>" in March 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ukbb_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-ukbb-32">[32]</a></sup> According to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Official_Charts_Company" title="The Official Charts Company">The Official Charts Company</a>, the song has sold 270,000 copies in Britain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>"Everytime" was also successful elsewhere in Europe, reaching the top of the charts in Hungary and Ireland, peaking at number two in France, number three in Sweden and reaching top five positions in Austria, Belgium (Flanders and Wallonia), Czech Republic, Denmark, Germany, Norway and the Netherlands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hungary_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-hungary-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ultratop_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-ultratop-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-acharts_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-acharts-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everytime&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_and_release">Development and release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everytime&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Development and release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 9, 2004, the treatment of the music video for "Everytime" was released online. It portrayed Spears as a star hounded by photographers, who eventually kills herself by taking prescription drugs and drowning in a bathtub. The suicide scene was perceived to be Spears's response to the rumors that suggested she suffered from a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Psychological_disorder" title="Psychological disorder">mental disorder</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-puzzling_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-puzzling-37">[37]</a></sup> After news of the concept broke, it was criticized by a number of organizations in the United Kingdom and the United States. Director of child protection charity <a href="/wiki/Kidscape" title="Kidscape">Kidscape</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michele_Elliott" title="Michele Elliott">Michele Elliott</a>, said, "This is absolutely outrageous, totally irresponsible, completely stupid. If even one child follows her lead she will surely be held responsible. What is she thinking of?". She also commented the release of the music video could cause an increase in suicide rates, comparing it to the <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Marilyn_Monroe" title="Death of Marilyn Monroe">death of actress Marilyn Monroe</a> in August 1962.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thesun_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-thesun-38">[38]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/MTV_News" title="MTV News">MTV News</a>' "You Tell Us" also received numerous letters from upset viewers, who criticized Spears saying they perceived the ending was a glamorization of suicide. On March 12, 2004, Spears announced through <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a> she had removed the concept, "due to the potential for a fictional accidental occurrence to be misinterpreted as a suicide". She also clarified it was not her intent to present suicide in any sort of positive light.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-remove_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-remove-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>The video was directed by <a href="/wiki/David_LaChapelle" title="David LaChapelle">David LaChapelle</a> and shot on March 13 and 14, 2004, in Los Angeles. The lighting was described as "saturated, but low and naturalistic" to give the video a cinematic feel, referencing the 1995 film <i><a href="/wiki/Leaving_Las_Vegas" title="Leaving Las Vegas">Leaving Las Vegas</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-puzzling_37-1"><a href="#cite_note-puzzling-37">[37]</a></sup> It premiered on <i><a href="/wiki/Total_Request_Live" title="Total Request Live">TRL</a></i> on April 12, 2004. Spears called the show and explained the video explored <a href="/wiki/Reincarnation" title="Reincarnation">reincarnation</a>. She added, "It's more like a movie. It's different than anything I've ever done. It's dark, and it shows me in a different light. Of course, I'm going to go back and do dance videos, but I wanted to be inspired and challenged."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reincarnation_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-reincarnation-40">[40]</a></sup> An alternate version of the video which features Spears singing in the white hallway scenes was released on the 2004 DVD <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits:_My_Prerogative_(video)" title="Greatest Hits: My Prerogative (video)">Greatest Hits: My Prerogative</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dvd_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-dvd-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everytime&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">

Spears looking at the blood in her hand.</div>

<p>The video begins with an aerial shot of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Las_Vegas,_Nevada" title="Las Vegas, Nevada">Las Vegas, Nevada</a>, showing the <a href="/wiki/Palms_Casino_Resort" title="Palms Casino Resort">Palms Casino Resort</a> and continues with a shot of a marquee hotel that reads "<i>Britney Spears Live From Miami</i> <a href="/wiki/The_Onyx_Hotel_Tour" title="The Onyx Hotel Tour">The Onyx Hotel Tour</a> Las Vegas", with a picture of Spears holding a leather strap and referencing her <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a> concert special from Miami. Christian author Eva Marie Everson compared the image to "<a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a> doing her own impersonation of Marilyn Monroe."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-everson_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-everson-42">[42]</a></sup> Spears and her boyfriend (played by <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Dorff" title="Stephen Dorff">Stephen Dorff</a>) are arriving to the hotel inside a limousine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-everson_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-everson-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vegas_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-vegas-43">[43]</a></sup> They sit apart from each other, staring out separate windows.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-everson_42-2"><a href="#cite_note-everson-42">[42]</a></sup> Spears wears a <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_Barons" title="Birmingham Barons">Birmingham Barons</a> cap in these scenes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-barons_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-barons-44">[44]</a></sup> The boyfriend is talking on his cell phone, and when she attempts to touch him, he pushes her away. The entrance is full of fans and <a href="/wiki/Paparazzi" title="Paparazzi">paparazzi</a> taking pictures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-everson_42-3"><a href="#cite_note-everson-42">[42]</a></sup> When they leave the car, fans and paparazzi alike act in an extreme way and fights break in the crowd. While her bodyguards try to protect her, her boyfriend throws magazines at the paparazzi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-puzzling_37-2"><a href="#cite_note-puzzling-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thesun_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-thesun-38">[38]</a></sup> Stephanie Zacharek of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> compared the shots of the paparazzi with the Jews in the 2004 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Passion_of_the_Christ" title="The Passion of the Christ">The Passion of the Christ</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytmv_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-nytmv-45">[45]</a></sup> During this scene, she gets hit in the head with a camera, and unknowingly gets a wound on her head, but keeps walking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thesun_38-2"><a href="#cite_note-thesun-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>Inside their hotel room, Spears and her boyfriend start screaming at each other. He throws a vase at the wall while Spears goes into the bathroom, hurling something at the mirror. She begins to fill the bathtub and remove her clothes. After this, the video includes intercut scenes of a close-up of Spears singing in a white shirt in front of a bright white light.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-puzzling_37-3"><a href="#cite_note-puzzling-37">[37]</a></sup> As she lies in the bathtub, a <a href="/wiki/Red_string_(Kabbalah)" title="Red string (Kabbalah)">red string</a>, a custom associated with <a href="/wiki/Kabbalah" title="Kabbalah">Kabbalah</a> is seen in her wrist. She touches her head and looks at her hand, realizing she is bleeding from the wound. According to Jennifer Vineyard of MTV, the blood in her hand is <a href="/wiki/Stigmata" title="Stigmata">stigmata</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reincarnation_40-1"><a href="#cite_note-reincarnation-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vegas_43-1"><a href="#cite_note-vegas-43">[43]</a></sup> She loses consciousness in the bathtub and drowns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reincarnation_40-2"><a href="#cite_note-reincarnation-40">[40]</a></sup> Shortly after, her boyfriend finds her and tries to resuscitate her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-puzzling_37-4"><a href="#cite_note-puzzling-37">[37]</a></sup> Meanwhile, it is revealed that in the close-up scenes Spears is actually inside a hospital hallway. The video continues with scenes of her being carried into an ambulance and surrounded by photographers, as well as scenes of her being resuscitated by doctors in a hospital bed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-everson_42-4"><a href="#cite_note-everson-42">[42]</a></sup> The ghost of Spears in a white shirt, watches herself in the bed and walks into the next room, where a baby girl is born. Spears is then seen running away from the camera into the light. The music video ends with her rising from under the water, resting her head and smiling, suggesting the whole scene of her death was a dream or a morbid fantasy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thesun_38-3"><a href="#cite_note-thesun-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reincarnation_40-3"><a href="#cite_note-reincarnation-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everytime&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Eva Marie Everson wrote that the music video showed the reality "behind the glitz and the glamour".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-everson_42-5"><a href="#cite_note-everson-42">[42]</a></sup> Dominic Fox commented, "Even in its bowdlerised form, the 'Everytime' video presents a moment of existential indecision, a fugue of suicidal ideation in which the singer fantasises about her own death".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fox_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-fox-46">[46]</a></sup> While reviewing the music video for her 2009 single "<a href="/wiki/If_U_Seek_Amy" title="If U Seek Amy">If U Seek Amy</a>", James Montgomery of MTV called the music video for "Everytime" "underrated".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bestmtv_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-bestmtv-47">[47]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> in their 2009 article "Britney Spears: The Complete Video Guide", called it "horribly prophetic and depressing" and added that the clip foreshadowed Spears's struggles with fame and mental instability during 2007 and 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rstone_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-rstone-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everytime&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

Spears performing "Everytime" at her Las Vegas residency show, <a href="/wiki/Britney:_Piece_of_Me" title="Britney: Piece of Me">Britney: Piece of Me</a>.</div>

<p>On October 18, 2003, "Everytime" was performed by Spears during the <a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live_(season_29)" title="Saturday Night Live (season 29)">twenty-ninth</a> season of the American comedy show <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-albumpreview_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-albumpreview-11">[11]</a></sup> She also performed it at <i>Britney Spears: In the Zone</i>, a concert special that aired in <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> on November 17, 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dispatch_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-dispatch-49">[49]</a></sup> "Everytime" was also performed by Spears at 2004's <a href="/wiki/The_Onyx_Hotel_Tour" title="The Onyx Hotel Tour">The Onyx Hotel Tour</a>. Before the tour began, Spears said that it was one of the songs she was looking most forward to perform, explaining, "I really think I'm talking to everyone when I perform 'Everytime'".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rubber_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-rubber-12">[12]</a></sup> It was the first song of the third act, titled "Mystic Garden". It began with a video interlude in which Spears walked into a garden wearing a rainbow-colored dress and sat in a flower-covered piano. As the video ended, it was revealed that she was sitting onstage in a similar setting. She started the performance talking to the audience about the media coverage of her personal life. She played the piano and sang until the second verse, where she stood up and walked to the center of the stage to continue the performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvkickoffreview_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtvkickoffreview-50">[50]</a></sup> Neil Strauss of <i>The New York Times</i> commented, "It was the only song that she appeared to sing unaccompanied by backing tapes".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyttr_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyttr-51">[51]</a></sup> Kelefa Sanneh of <i>Blender</i> called it the best performance of the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blenderonyx_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-blenderonyx-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>"Everytime" was also performed by Spears at the British music chart show <i><a href="/wiki/Top_of_the_Pops" title="Top of the Pops">Top of the Pops</a></i> on August 5, 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-totp_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-totp-53">[53]</a></sup> Spears also performed the song at 2009's <a href="/wiki/The_Circus_Starring_Britney_Spears" title="The Circus Starring Britney Spears">The Circus Starring Britney Spears</a>. "Everytime" was the only song that was not included in the released setlist, and was added as a surprise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msnbcp_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-msnbcp-54">[54]</a></sup> It was the sixth and last song of the second act, titled "House of Fun (Anything Goes)". After a Bollywood-inspired performance of "<a href="/wiki/Me_Against_the_Music" title="Me Against the Music">Me Against the Music</a>" from <i>In the Zone</i>, Spears sat on a giant umbrella in the middle of the stage and briefly talked to the audience. She performed "Everytime" while the umbrella was lifted into the air.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usat_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-usat-55">[55]</a></sup> Spears included "Everytime" on the setlist for her Las Vegas residency, <a href="/wiki/Britney:_Piece_of_Me" title="Britney: Piece of Me">Britney: Piece of Me</a>. After a brief interlude, descended from the ceilling as a "giant, white-winged angel". After a snow shower of confetti, the song transitioned into "<a href="/wiki/...Baby_One_More_Time_(song)" title="...Baby One More Time (song)">...Baby One More Time</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BillboardReview_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-BillboardReview-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cover_versions">Cover versions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everytime&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Cover versions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:James_Franco_2007_Spiderman_3_premiere.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="742" data-file-width="537" height="207" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/James_Franco_2007_Spiderman_3_premiere.jpg/150px-James_Franco_2007_Spiderman_3_premiere.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/James_Franco_2007_Spiderman_3_premiere.jpg/225px-James_Franco_2007_Spiderman_3_premiere.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/James_Franco_2007_Spiderman_3_premiere.jpg/300px-James_Franco_2007_Spiderman_3_premiere.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

In the 2013 film <i><a href="/wiki/Spring_Breakers" title="Spring Breakers">Spring Breakers</a></i>, the character of Alien (<a href="/wiki/James_Franco" title="James Franco">James Franco</a>) sings "Everytime" on a white grand piano.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hw1_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-hw1-57">[57]</a></sup></div>

<p>"Everytime" was covered by <a href="/wiki/Glen_Hansard" title="Glen Hansard">Glen Hansard</a> of Irish band <a href="/wiki/The_Frames" title="The Frames">The Frames</a>. It was recorded during a live show at <a href="/wiki/Today_FM" title="Today FM">Today FM</a> and released in the 2004 covers compilation, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Even_Better_than_the_Real_Thing_Vol._2" title="Even Better than the Real Thing Vol. 2">Even Better than the Real Thing Vol. 2</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-frames_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-frames-58">[58]</a></sup> It was also covered in Mandarin by Taiwanese girl group <a href="/wiki/S.H.E" title="S.H.E">S.H.E</a> and released on their studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Encore_(S.H.E_album)" title="Encore (S.H.E album)">Encore</a></i> (2004). Their version was retitled "Bié Shuō Duìbùqǐ" (<span lang="zh">別說對不起</span> "Don't Say Sorry").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-she_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-she-59">[59]</a></sup> American <a href="/wiki/Pop_rock" title="Pop rock">pop rock</a> singer Sally Maer also covered "Everytime" to release it on her studio album <i>Bed of Roses</i> (2008).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> "Everytime" was used during the 2009 Irish production of the 1896 play <i><a href="/wiki/The_Seagull" title="The Seagull">The Seagull</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-seagull_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-seagull-61">[61]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Evancho" title="Jackie Evancho">Jackie Evancho</a> covered the song for her debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Prelude_to_a_Dream" title="Prelude to a Dream">Prelude to a Dream</a></i> (2009). On August 19, 2010, her version debuted at number three on <i>Billboard'</i>s Classical Digital Songs chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> British singer <a href="/wiki/Cher_Lloyd" title="Cher Lloyd">Cher Lloyd</a> covered the song live on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK)" title="The X Factor (UK)">The X Factor</a></i> in the United Kingdom in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> On July 27, 2012, <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Clarkson" title="Kelly Clarkson">Kelly Clarkson</a> covered the song during the Las Vegas stop of her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Tour_(Kelly_Clarkson_and_The_Fray)" title="2012 Summer Tour (Kelly Clarkson and The Fray)">summer tour</a>, as an audience request. Clarkson had a harpist accompany her during the performance, and told the audience, "This song is one of my favourite songs. [...] I actually prefer [Spears's] version better, because it just sounds really sad, but I'm going to try and do it." Spears approved of Clarkson's cover via her <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> account, calling it "beautiful".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-omg_64-0"><a href="#cite_note-omg-64">[64]</a></sup> "Everytime" was also covered on the American series <i><a href="/wiki/Glee_(TV_series)" title="Glee (TV series)">Glee</a></i> episode "<a href="/wiki/Britney_2.0" title="Britney 2.0">Britney 2.0</a>" by the character <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marley_Rose" title="Marley Rose">Marley Rose</a> (played by <a href="/wiki/Melissa_Benoist" title="Melissa Benoist">Melissa Benoist</a>); her rendition was rated a B by <a href="/wiki/TVLine" title="TVLine">TVLine</a>'s Michael Slezak.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> In July 2016, the song was covered by <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> personality <a href="/wiki/Trisha_Paytas" title="Trisha Paytas">Trisha Paytas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup></p>
<p>"Everytime" was also used in the 2013 American film <i><a href="/wiki/Spring_Breakers" title="Spring Breakers">Spring Breakers</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Harmony_Korine" title="Harmony Korine">Harmony Korine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hw1_57-1"><a href="#cite_note-hw1-57">[57]</a></sup> The scene begins with the characters of Candy (<a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Hudgens" title="Vanessa Hudgens">Vanessa Hudgens</a>), Brittany (<a href="/wiki/Ashley_Benson" title="Ashley Benson">Ashley Benson</a>) and Cotty (<a href="/wiki/Rachel_Korine" title="Rachel Korine">Rachel Korine</a>) standing in the backyard of the character of Alien (<a href="/wiki/James_Franco" title="James Franco">James Franco</a>), who sits at a white grand piano. The girls are wearing <a href="/wiki/My_Little_Pony" title="My Little Pony">My Little Pony</a> ski masks, sparkly pink tiger bathing suits, sweatpants with "DTF" on the rear and shotguns in their hands. They ask Alien to "Play us something sweet. Something uplifting, Something fucking inspiring," to which he responds "One of the greatest singers of all time, and an angel if there ever was one on this earth", before starting to sing "Everytime". As the song transitions from Alien's vocals to Spears's original version we are shown a montage of the girls and Alien robbing other spring breakers at gunpoint.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hw1_57-2"><a href="#cite_note-hw1-57">[57]</a></sup> Harmony Korine said about the song,</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"I like her music! I think it’s like perfect pop music. That song "Everytime" — I was always taken by that song. It has this real kind of beauty and airlessness and this haunting kind of morose lullaby effect and underneath it there’s some type of hardcore aggression and menace that was really connected to the film in the way that the film deals with the culture of surfaces, this candy-coated neon haze reality. Underneath it, there’s this subtext and feeling of this kind of darkness. It was a connection I felt."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
