<p><b>Yacine Nasr Eddine Brahimi</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">ياسين إبراهيمي</span>‎‎; born 8 February 1990) is a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a>-born <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Algeria" title="Demographics of Algeria">Algerian</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">Porto</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Algeria_national_football_team" title="Algeria national football team">Algeria national team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He plays as an <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">attacking midfielder</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Winger" title="Midfielder">winger</a> and is described as a "technically gifted player". His coach, <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Antonetti" title="Frédéric Antonetti">Frédéric Antonetti</a>, declared that Brahimi would be the "future play-maker of Stade Rennais".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Yacine is also known for his "Brahimi Moments" (in Portuguese, "Momentos Brahimi")<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="I follow the football Portuguese league and I've never heard this expression (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<p>Brahimi started his career throughout various clubs in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%8Ele-de-France_(region)" title="Île-de-France (region)">Île-de-France</a> <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_France" title="Regions of France">region</a>, having trained at ASB Montreuil and CO Vincennois. In 2003, he was selected to attend the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clairefontaine" title="Clairefontaine">Clairefontaine</a> academy. Brahimi spent three years at the academy and, upon leaving, signed with <a href="/wiki/Stade_Rennais_F.C." title="Stade Rennais F.C.">Stade Rennais F.C.</a>. While in the club's youth academy, he achieved several club honours. After turning professional, Brahimi was loaned out to <a href="/wiki/Ligue_2" title="Ligue 2">second division</a> club <a href="/wiki/Clermont_Foot" title="Clermont Foot">Clermont Foot</a>. While at Clermont, he had a successful individual <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_in_French_football" title="2009–10 in French football">2009–10 season</a>. After spending the previous season there on loan, he moved to the <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> club <a href="/wiki/Granada_CF" title="Granada CF">Granada CF</a> in 2013, and then to Porto for €6.5 million a year later.</p>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algerian</a> international, Brahimi is a former <a href="/wiki/France_national_youth_football_team" title="France national youth football team">France youth international</a> having represented the country at all youth levels. In 2009, he played on the <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-19_football_team" title="France national under-19 football team">under-19 team</a> that reached the semi-finals at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-19_Football_Championship" title="2009 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship">2009 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship</a>. In February 2013, Brahimi switched his international allegiance to Algeria and made his debut for them a month later, also playing at the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2015_Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="2015 Africa Cup of Nations">2015 Africa Cup of Nations</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yacine_Brahimi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yacine_Brahimi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Brahimi was born in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> to <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algerian</a> parents and grew up in the eastern suburbs of Paris in <a href="/wiki/Montreuil,_Seine-Saint-Denis" title="Montreuil, Seine-Saint-Denis">Montreuil</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seine-Saint-Denis" title="Seine-Saint-Denis">Seine-Saint-Denis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brahimi_123_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-brahimi_123-6">[6]</a></sup> While growing up, he often emulated <a href="/wiki/Zinedine_Zidane" title="Zinedine Zidane">Zinedine Zidane</a> while playing <a href="/wiki/Street_football" title="Street football">street football</a> with friends.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Brahimi began his football career playing for his local club ASB Montreuil. After a four-year stint there, he joined CO Vincennois, known for producing <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> player <a href="/wiki/Blaise_Matuidi" title="Blaise Matuidi">Blaise Matuidi</a>, in nearby <a href="/wiki/Vincennes" title="Vincennes">Vincennes</a>. Two years later, Brahimi was selected to attend the renowned <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clairefontaine" title="Clairefontaine">Clairefontaine academy</a> in order to further his development. While training at Clairefontaine during the week, he regularly played for Vincennois on the weekends. In his final year at Clairefontaine, Brahimi spent a year at the <a href="/wiki/Camp_des_Loges" title="Camp des Loges">Camp des Loges</a>, the youth training center of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain_FC" title="Paris Saint-Germain FC">Paris Saint-Germain</a>, training alongside youth international teammate <a href="/wiki/Mamadou_Sakho" title="Mamadou Sakho">Mamadou Sakho</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brahimi_youth_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-brahimi_youth-8">[8]</a></sup> Despite being courted by several French and <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> clubs, following his stint at Clairefontaine, he signed an <i>aspirant</i> (youth) contract with <a href="/wiki/Stade_Rennais_F.C." title="Stade Rennais F.C.">Rennes</a>. In October 2010, Brahimi cited choosing Rennes as his destination because of the club's educational value stating "In Rennes, the academy gives much importance to the schoolwork" and "It was a warranty for my parents. They advised me to choose Stade Rennes. That way, I could continue my football training while preparing my <a href="/wiki/Baccalaur%C3%A9at" title="Baccalauréat">Baccalauréat</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brahimi_youth_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-brahimi_youth-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rennes">Rennes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yacine_Brahimi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rennes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Brahimi joined Rennes and quickly became a part of a slew of talent in the club's youth academy. He joined <a href="/wiki/Damien_Le_Tallec" title="Damien Le Tallec">Damien Le Tallec</a>, the younger brother of <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Le_Tallec" title="Anthony Le Tallec">Anthony Le Tallec</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yann_M%27Vila" title="Yann M'Vila">Yann M'Vila</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yohann_Lasimant" title="Yohann Lasimant">Yohann Lasimant</a>, <a href="/wiki/K%C3%A9vin_Th%C3%A9ophile-Catherine" title="Kévin Théophile-Catherine">Kévin Théophile-Catherine</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Souprayen" title="Samuel Souprayen">Samuel Souprayen</a> as the club's most sought after prospects. The combination of M'Vila, Brahimi, Camara, and Le Tallec, all four being members of the class of 1990, were particularly instrumental in their youth team's successes. With the under-16 team, the foursome won the Tournoi Carisport, a national tournament that regularly pits the top academies in France against each other. Two seasons later with the under-18 team, Brahimi won the under-18 league championship for the 2006–07 season. In 2008, the youth academy achieved its biggest honour after winning the <a href="/wiki/Coupe_Gambardella" title="Coupe Gambardella">Coupe Gambardella</a>. The title was Rennes' third Gambardella Cup and its first since 2003 when the likes of <a href="/wiki/Yoann_Gourcuff" title="Yoann Gourcuff">Yoann Gourcuff</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sylvain_Marveaux" title="Sylvain Marveaux">Sylvain Marveaux</a> were playing in the competition. Following the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ligue_1_2007%E2%80%9308" title="Ligue 1 2007–08">2007–08 season</a>, on 23 June, Brahimi signed his first professional contract agreeing to a three-year deal until June 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Though on a professional contract, Brahimi was not assigned a number on the senior team and instead played on the club's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Championnat_de_France_amateur" title="Championnat de France amateur">Championnat de France amateur</a> team in the fourth division appearing in 22 matches and scoring three goals helping the side finish 1st among professional clubs in their group, thus qualifying for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Championnat_de_France_Amateurs_2008-09#Playoffs" title="Championnat de France Amateurs 2008-09">competition's playoffs</a>, where they lost to <a href="/wiki/Olympique_Lyonnais_Reserves_and_Academy" title="Olympique Lyonnais Reserves and Academy">Lyon</a> in the semi-finals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Clermont_.28loan.29">Clermont (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yacine_Brahimi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Clermont (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Following a successful <a href="/wiki/France_national_youth_football_team" title="France national youth football team">international season</a> with <a href="/wiki/France_national_youth_football_team" title="France national youth football team">France</a>, Rennes decided it would be best to send the young player on loan to receive some much needed playing time. On 3 July 2009, the club announced that Brahimi would join Ligue 2 club <a href="/wiki/Clermont_Foot" title="Clermont Foot">Clermont Foot</a> on loan for the entire <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Ligue_2" title="2009–10 Ligue 2">2009–10 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Brahimi arrived at the club following the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-19_Football_Championship" title="2009 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship">2009 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship</a>, alongside fellow loanee from <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Vecchione" title="Carlo Vecchione">Carlo Vecchione</a>, and was assigned the number 28 shirt. He made his professional debut on the opening match day of the season appearing as a substitute in a 2–1 defeat to <a href="/wiki/AC_Arles-Avignon" title="AC Arles-Avignon">Arles-Avignon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He scored his first goal for the club on 25 September converting a <a href="/wiki/Penalty_kick_(association_football)" title="Penalty kick (association football)">penalty</a> in the club's 3–2 win over <a href="/wiki/Angers_SCO" title="Angers SCO">Angers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Brahimi's first goal in open play occurred on 4 December in Clermont's 3–1 win away to <a href="/wiki/SC_Bastia" title="SC Bastia">Bastia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Two weeks later, he scored again, converting another penalty in a 1–1 draw with <a href="/wiki/En_Avant_de_Guingamp" title="En Avant de Guingamp">Guingamp</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 23 March 2010, Brahimi scored the opening goal in Clermont's 3–0 win over <a href="/wiki/AC_Ajaccio" title="AC Ajaccio">Ajaccio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Three days later, he scored a goal and provided an assist in a 3–1 away victory over <a href="/wiki/FC_Istres" title="FC Istres">Istres</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> A month later, Brahimi converted his third penalty of the season in a 3–2 victory over <a href="/wiki/Dijon_FCO" title="Dijon FCO">Dijon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The win moved Clermont up to 4th position in the league standings and just three points off a promotion place. The following week, Brahimi continued to display his offensive prowess scoring against <a href="/wiki/Le_Havre_AC" title="Le Havre AC">Le Havre</a>. The match, however, ended negatively for Clermont as the club lost 2–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> It was the club's first defeat in a match where Brahimi had scored a goal. Three days later, Brahimi scored his eight goal of the campaign in a 3–1 victory against <a href="/wiki/En_Avant_de_Guingamp" title="En Avant de Guingamp">Guingamp</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The victory established a <i>promotion playoff</i> match on the final match day of the season as 4th placed Clermont faced 3rd-placed Arles-Avignon with the victory earning promotion to Ligue 1. Unfortunately for Clermont, the club failed to earn promotion to Ligue 1 for the first time in club history as it lost the match 1–0. Brahimi played 89 minutes in the match receiving a yellow card in the process.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Rennes">Return to Rennes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yacine_Brahimi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Return to Rennes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Following the season, on 15 May 2010, Rennes manager <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Antonetti" title="Frédéric Antonetti">Frédéric Antonetti</a> confirmed that Brahimi would return to the team, despite interest from <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> club <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> club <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a>, and that he would be heavily relied upon for the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Ligue_1" title="2010–11 Ligue 1">2010–11 season</a> possibly serving as the <a href="/wiki/Playmaker" title="Playmaker">playmaker</a> replacing the aging <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_Leroy_(footballer)" title="Jérôme Leroy (footballer)">Jérôme Leroy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> On 7 August 2010, Brahimi made his club debut for Rennes in the club's opening league match against <a href="/wiki/Lille_OSC" title="Lille OSC">Lille</a>. He started the match and played 71 minutes in a 1–1 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The following day, Rennes announced that Brahimi had signed a four-year contract extension with the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The new deal will keep him at the club until June 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 14 August 2010, Brahimi scored his first career goal for the club in a 3–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/AS_Nancy" title="AS Nancy">Nancy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> For a portion of the fall season, the play-maker underwent a dry spell not scoring a goal or providing an assist. As a result, Brahimi began rotating in and out of the starting lineup. After the winter break, Brahimi returned to form scoring and providing an assist in the team's 7–0 hammering of <a href="/wiki/Championnat_National" title="Championnat National">Championnat National</a> club <a href="/wiki/AS_Cannes" title="AS Cannes">Cannes</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Coupe_de_France" title="Coupe de France">Coupe de France</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> A week later, he scored a double in a 4–0 league win over <a href="/wiki/AC_Arles-Avignon" title="AC Arles-Avignon">Arles-Avignon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> On 5 February 2011, Brahimi scored a goal against his former youth club <a href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain_F.C." title="Paris Saint-Germain F.C.">Paris Saint-Germain</a>. The long-range driven goal was the only goal of the match as the victory moved Rennes equal on points with Paris Saint-Germain for second place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Granada">Granada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yacine_Brahimi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Granada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 30 August 2012, Brahimi signed on loan for <a href="/wiki/Granada_CF" title="Granada CF">Granada CF</a> of <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> for €700,000, with the option to sign for €4 million afterwards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> This option was taken on 4 June 2013, when he signed a four-year deal at the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> At the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LFP_Awards" title="LFP Awards">LFP Awards</a>, he was voted the best African player of the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_La_Liga" title="2013–14 La Liga">2013–14 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Porto">Porto</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yacine_Brahimi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Porto">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 22 July 2014, <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">FC Porto</a> announced the signing of Brahimi from <a href="/wiki/Granada_CF" title="Granada CF">Granada CF</a> for a fee of <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>6.5 million in 5-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> On 24 July 2014 Porto sold 80% of his economic rights to Doyen Sports for €5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> The deals effectively made Porto acquired 20% economic rights of the player for €1.5 million.</p>
<p>His first goal for the club came in the second leg of their <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2014–15 UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> play-off against <a href="/wiki/Lille_OSC" title="Lille OSC">Lille</a>, netting directly from a free-kick as Porto advanced to the group stage with a 3–0 aggregate victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> He scored his first <a href="/wiki/List_of_UEFA_Champions_League_hat-tricks" title="List of UEFA Champions League hat-tricks">hat-trick</a> for the club in Porto's first match of the Champions League group stage, a 6–0 home win over <a href="/wiki/FC_BATE_Borisov" title="FC BATE Borisov">BATE Borisov</a> on 17 September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> In their next European fixture, he had his penalty saved by <a href="/wiki/Andriy_Pyatov" title="Andriy Pyatov">Andriy Pyatov</a> with the game goalless at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shakhtar_Donetsk" title="Shakhtar Donetsk">Shakhtar Donetsk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> He scored his first league goal against <a href="/wiki/C.D._Nacional" title="C.D. Nacional">C.D. Nacional</a> in a 2–0 win on 1 November. On 5 November, Brahimi scored one goal and assisted another as Porto defeated <a href="/wiki/Athletic_Bilbao" title="Athletic Bilbao">Athletic</a> 2–0 to secure qualification to the knock-out stage of the Champions League.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 26 June 2015 Porto bought back 30% economic rights from Doyen for €3.8 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>Brahimi scored one goal as Porto defeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maccabi_Tel-Aviv" title="Maccabi Tel-Aviv">Maccabi Tel-Aviv</a> 2-0 in the group stage of <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2015–16 UEFA Champions League">2015–16 UEFA Champions League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yacine_Brahimi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="France">France</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yacine_Brahimi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: France">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As early as the age of 13, Brahimi began featuring with regional youth teams playing for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%8Ele-de-France_(region)" title="Île-de-France (region)">Île-de-France</a> alongside future youth international teammates <a href="/wiki/Mamadou_Sakho" title="Mamadou Sakho">Mamadou Sakho</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tripy_Makonda" title="Tripy Makonda">Tripy Makonda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maxime_Partouche" title="Maxime Partouche">Maxime Partouche</a>, and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Corchia" title="Sébastien Corchia">Sébastien Corchia</a>. Brahimi made his debut for the under-16 team on 21 March 2006 converting a penalty in a 3–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_youth_football_team" title="Germany national youth football team">Germany</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> He played with the under-16 team at the 2006 edition of the <a href="/wiki/Montaigu_Tournament" title="Montaigu Tournament">Montaigu Tournament</a> playing in all four matches the team contested as France were crowned champions defeating <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> 2–1 in the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> With the under-17 team, Brahimi made his debut on 25 September in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-17_Football_Championship_qualifying_round" title="2007 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship qualifying round">qualification match</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-17_Football_Championship" title="2007 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship">2007 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship</a> against <a href="/wiki/Lithuania_national_football_team" title="Lithuania national football team">Lithuania</a>. France won the match 4–2 with Brahimi making a substitute appearance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Despite appearing in the first round of qualification, Brahimi was absent from the tournament itself, due to the player struggling to adapt at his new club domestically.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brahimi_youth_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-brahimi_youth-8">[8]</a></sup> He later failed to make the team that qualified for the <a href="/wiki/2007_FIFA_U-17_World_Cup" title="2007 FIFA U-17 World Cup">2007 FIFA U-17 World Cup</a>, which was achieved by virtue of their semi-final appearance in the UEFA-sanctioned tournament. Brahimi made his debut with the under-18 team on 18 December 2007 in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exhibition_match" title="Exhibition match">friendly match</a> against <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a> in a 1–0 loss and later featured in two friendly matches against Germany, one of which was played at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GAZi-Stadion_auf_der_Waldau" title="GAZi-Stadion auf der Waldau">GAZi-Stadion auf der Waldau</a> in <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart" title="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>With the under-19 team, Brahimi appeared in all 19 matches scoring 12 goals. He was immense during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-19_Football_Championship_qualification" title="2009 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship qualification">2009 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship qualification</a> scoring a team-leading five goals against <a href="/wiki/Liechtenstein_national_football_team" title="Liechtenstein national football team">Liechtenstein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malta_national_football_team" title="Malta national football team">Malta</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Republic of Ireland</a> netting two goals each against the Liechtenstein and the hosts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> Brahimi also scored several goals in friendly matches. On 30 March 2009, he scored a goal against the <a href="/wiki/Ukraine_national_under-19_football_team" title="Ukraine national under-19 football team">Ukraine</a> and, the following month, scored a brace against <a href="/wiki/Finland_national_under-19_football_team" title="Finland national under-19 football team">Finland</a> in a 7–0 rout.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> At the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-19_Football_Championship" title="2009 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship">2009 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship</a>, Brahimi scored two goals; one against <a href="/wiki/Serbia_national_under-19_football_team" title="Serbia national under-19 football team">Serbia</a> and another against <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-19_football_team" title="Spain national under-19 football team">Spain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> The game-winning goal against Spain assured France's progression to the semi-finals where they lost to <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-19_football_team" title="England national under-19 football team">England</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>Brahimi is also eligible to represent <a href="/wiki/Algeria_national_football_team" title="Algeria national football team">Algeria</a> at international level. Following a successful season with Clermont, he was contacted by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Alg%C3%A9rienne_de_Football" title="Fédération Algérienne de Football">Fédération Algérienne de Football</a> who offered the player an opportunity to play on the team at the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brahimi_123_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-brahimi_123-6">[6]</a></sup> Brahimi was set to be named to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coach_(sports)" title="Coach (sports)">manager</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rabah_Saadane" title="Rabah Saadane">Rabah Saadane</a>'s preliminary squad list for the competition, but turned down the opportunity with his preference being to continue his international career with <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brahimi_123_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-brahimi_123-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TSA_Algerie_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-TSA_Algerie-60">[60]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Football_360_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-Football_360-61">[61]</a></sup> On 12 May 2010, after almost a year not representing France internationally, Brahimi was called up to the <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-20_football_team" title="France national under-20 football team">under-20 team</a> by <a href="/wiki/Coach_(sport)" title="Coach (sport)">coach</a> Patrick Gonfalone to participate in the <a href="/wiki/2010_Toulon_Tournament" title="2010 Toulon Tournament">2010 Toulon Tournament</a>. In the team's second group stage match against <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_football_team" title="Japan national football team">Japan</a>, Brahimi scored the final goal in the team's 4–1 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> He played in all five of the team's matches as France finished in third place.</p>
<p>On 26 August 2010, Brahimi was called up to the under-21 team by coach <a href="/wiki/Erick_Mombaerts" title="Erick Mombaerts">Erick Mombaerts</a> for the first time for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship_qualification" title="2011 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship qualification">2011 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship qualification</a> matches against the <a href="/wiki/Ukraine_national_under-21_football_team" title="Ukraine national under-21 football team">Ukraine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Malta_national_under-21_football_team" title="Malta national under-21 football team">Malta</a>. He made his under-21 debut in the match against Malta. Brahimi played the entire match in a 2–0 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> He, subsequently, featured in the team's three matches before dropping out of the selection for the team's friendly match against <a href="/wiki/Slovakia_national_under-21_football_team" title="Slovakia national under-21 football team">Slovakia</a> in February 2011 due to injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p>

<p>In February 2013, it was announced that Brahimi had decided to switch his FIFA national allegiance to represent <a href="/wiki/Algeria_national_football_team" title="Algeria national football team">Algeria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> He made his debut a month later as a starter in the 3–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Benin_national_football_team" title="Benin national football team">Benin</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(CAF)" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification (CAF)">2014 FIFA World Cup qualifiers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 22 June 2014, Brahimi scored his first international goal, the fourth for Algeria as they won 4–2 against <a href="/wiki/South_Korea_national_football_team" title="South Korea national football team">South Korea</a> in the group stage of the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-67">[67]</a></sup> He started all of Algeria's group matches, and was a substitute in the Last 16 as they lost in extra time to eventual champions <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<p>Brahimi was also in Algeria's squad for the <a href="/wiki/2015_Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="2015 Africa Cup of Nations">2015 Africa Cup of Nations</a>. In their first group game, against <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_football_team" title="South Africa national football team">South Africa</a>, his cross was headed in for an equaliser as an <a href="/wiki/Own_goal" title="Own goal">own goal</a> for <a href="/wiki/Thulani_Hlatshwayo" title="Thulani Hlatshwayo">Thulani Hlatshwayo</a> as Algeria came from behind to win 3–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> In their next game, he was chosen as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Man_of_the_Match" title="Man of the Match">Man of the Match</a>, although the team lost 0–1 to <a href="/wiki/Ghana_national_football_team" title="Ghana national football team">Ghana</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> Brahimi featured in their next two matches as well, ending with defeat in the quarter-finals against eventual champions the <a href="/wiki/Ivory_Coast_national_football_team" title="Ivory Coast national football team">Ivory Coast</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yacine_Brahimi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Described as "Technically gifted player", Brahimi is known for his great dribbling abilities and ability to take on players. Described by many coaches and journalists as a "Versatile winger that is unpredictable". Brahimi has earned himself the name as a great dribbler and a winger with pace and skill, earning himself the 2014 BBC African player of the year.</p>
