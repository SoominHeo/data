<p><b>Yael Stone</b> (born 6 March 1985) is an Australian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a>. She has worked extensively in Australian theatre and has won two Sydney Theatre Awards. Outside of Australian theatre, she is best known for her portrayal of <a href="/wiki/Lorna_Morello" title="Lorna Morello">Lorna Morello</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Orange_Is_the_New_Black" title="Orange Is the New Black">Orange Is the New Black</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yael_Stone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stone was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, the daughter of Judy, a nurse, and Harry Stone, an architect.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Her father was born in Czechoslovakia, to Holocaust survivor parents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Yael's father is from a Jewish family, and her mother <a href="/wiki/Conversion_to_Judaism" title="Conversion to Judaism">converted</a> to Judaism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Her brother, Jake Stone, was the lead singer of the band <a href="/wiki/Bluejuice" title="Bluejuice">Bluejuice</a>. Her sister, <a href="/wiki/Elana_Stone" title="Elana Stone">Elana Stone</a>, is also a musician.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vogue_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-vogue-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Stone attended the <a href="/wiki/Newtown_High_School_of_the_Performing_Arts" title="Newtown High School of the Performing Arts">Newtown High School of the Performing Arts</a> and the <a href="/wiki/National_Institute_of_Dramatic_Art" title="National Institute of Dramatic Art">National Institute of Dramatic Art</a> (NIDA).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chron_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-chron-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yael_Stone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stone began acting as a child, with roles in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Me_Myself_I_(film)" title="Me Myself I (film)">Me Myself I</a></i> and the miniseries <i>The Farm</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vogue_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-vogue-4">[4]</a></sup> She worked primarily in theatre; at the 2008 Sydney Theatre Awards, she won the awards for Best Newcomer and Best Supporting Actress for her performance in <i>The Kid</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smh_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-smh-6">[6]</a></sup> She also worked in television, including supporting roles in <i><a href="/wiki/All_Saints_(TV_series)" title="All Saints (TV series)">All Saints</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Spirited" title="Spirited">Spirited</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vogue_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-vogue-4">[4]</a></sup> From 2010 to 2011, she appeared in <i>The Diary of a Madman</i>, a role for which she was again nominated for Best Supporting Actress at the Sydney Theatre Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smh_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-smh-6">[6]</a></sup> In February 2011, she traveled to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> to perform in the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Academy_of_Music" title="Brooklyn Academy of Music">Brooklyn Academy of Music</a>'s production of <i>The Diary of a Madman</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smh_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-smh-6">[6]</a></sup> before returning to lead roles in <i>A Golem Story</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Summer_of_the_Seventeenth_Doll" title="Summer of the Seventeenth Doll">Summer of the Seventeenth Doll</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/As_You_Like_It" title="As You Like It">As You Like It</a></i> in Sydney.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vogue_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-vogue-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Stone moved to New York permanently in December 2011 and co-founded an experimental theatre company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vogue_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-vogue-4">[4]</a></sup> After four months in New York, she was cast in the <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Orange_Is_the_New_Black" title="Orange Is the New Black">Orange Is the New Black</a></i>, a show set in a women's prison.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chron_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-chron-5">[5]</a></sup> Stone plays <a href="/wiki/Lorna_Morello" title="Lorna Morello">Lorna Morello</a>, a prisoner from <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>; her accent, a mixture of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brooklyn_accent" title="Brooklyn accent">Brooklyn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boston_accent" title="Boston accent">Boston</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stone_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stone-7">[7]</a></sup> was called "the most amazing accent on television" by a journalist for <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Republic" title="The New Republic">The New Republic</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> while another reviewer deemed the role to be Stone's "breakout turn".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-if_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-if-9">[9]</a></sup> She reprised her role in the show's second season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and was billed as a series regular in the third season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>She has also appeared on the web series "High Maintenance".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yael_Stone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2012, Stone married Australian actor <a href="/wiki/Dan_Spielman" title="Dan Spielman">Dan Spielman</a>, with whom she lives in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-if_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-if-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
