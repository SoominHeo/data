<p><b>Yao</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">八尾市</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yao-shi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Osaka_Prefecture" title="Osaka Prefecture">Osaka Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. The city was founded on April 1, 1948. As of 2009, the city has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 271,454 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 6,510 persons per km². The total area is 41.71 km². Yao is home to a <a href="/wiki/General_aviation" title="General aviation">general aviation</a> airport, <a href="/wiki/Yao_Airport" title="Yao Airport">Yao Airport</a>.</p>
<p>It is perhaps best known for its <a href="/wiki/Kawachi_ondo" title="Kawachi ondo">Kawachi ondo</a> festival. The first day features dancers in colorful costumes parading along the street. The second day is a huge <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bon_Odori#Bon_Odori" title="Bon Odori">bon odori</a> on the grounds of Yao high school where hundreds of people gather to dance and celebrate their historic song and dance.</p>
