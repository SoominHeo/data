<p><b>Public sex</b> is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_sexual_behavior" title="Human sexual behavior">sexual activity</a> that takes place in a public context. It refers to one or more persons performing a sex act in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_place" title="Public place">public place</a>, or in a <a href="/wiki/Private_place" title="Private place">private place</a> which can be viewed from a public place.</p>
<p>Such a private place may be a back yard or a bedroom with the curtains open. Public sex also includes sexual acts in semi-public places where the general public is free to enter, such as shopping malls. Public sex acts can be performed in cars (colloquially called "parking"), on beaches, in forests, theatres, buses, streets, <a href="/wiki/Toilet" title="Toilet">toilet</a> <a href="/wiki/Cubicle" title="Cubicle">cubicles</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Cemetery" title="Cemetery">cemeteries</a>.</p>
<p>In September 2003, the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> reported on a "dogging" craze fueled by Internet publicity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Dogging is a <a href="/wiki/British_English" title="British English">British English</a> slang term for engaging in public sex, usually in a car park or country park, while others watch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pub_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-pub-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wired_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-wired-4">[4]</a></sup> Dogging has aspects of <a href="/wiki/Exhibitionism" title="Exhibitionism">exhibitionism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Voyeurism" title="Voyeurism">voyeurism</a>.</p>
<p>There is some evidence on the Internet that "dogging" has begun to spread to other countries, such as the United States, Canada, Ireland, Australia, Barbados, Brazil, the Netherlands, Denmark,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Norway,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Poland,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and Sweden.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Outdoor public sex purportedly takes place often in parks and beaches in Vancouver and San Francisco.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> According to the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_(magazine)" title="New York (magazine)">New York</a></i> magazine, public sex occurs frequently in New York City, and is a fantasy common to many people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYmag_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYmag-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legality">Legality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_sex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Legality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Social views related to public sex and sexuality vary greatly between different cultures and different times. There are many and varied laws which apply to sex in public, which use a variety of terms such as <a href="/wiki/Indecent_exposure" title="Indecent exposure">indecent exposure</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_lewdness" title="Public lewdness">public lewdness</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gross_indecency_between_men" title="Gross indecency between men">gross indecency</a>, and others. In some jurisdictions, an offense is committed only if the participants are seen by others, so that a sex act may occur in a closed toilet cubicle without an offense being committed.</p>
<p>That is the situation, for example, in the United Kingdom as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Sexual_Offences_Act_2003" title="Sexual Offences Act 2003">Sexual Offences Act 2003</a>. Section 71 of the act makes it an offence to engage in sexual activity in a public lavatory. In the United Kingdom, public sex comes under laws related to <a href="/wiki/Voyeurism" title="Voyeurism">voyeurism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Exhibitionism" title="Exhibitionism">exhibitionism</a> or public displays of sexual behaviour, but public sex laws are ambiguous. Prosecution is possible for a number of offences under section 5 of the <a href="/wiki/Public_Order_Act_1986" title="Public Order Act 1986">Public Order Act 1986</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indecent_exposure" title="Indecent exposure">exposure</a> under section 66 of the Sexual Offences Act 2003, or under the common law offence of <a href="/wiki/Outraging_public_decency" title="Outraging public decency">outraging public decency</a>. The policy of the Association of Chief Police Officers (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ACPO" title="ACPO">ACPO</a>) is that arrests are a last resort and a more gradual approach should be taken in such circumstances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the UK, there has been a rise in <a href="/wiki/Sex_on_premises_venue" title="Sex on premises venue">public sex venues</a>, possibly due to a more relaxed approach to the enforcement of laws relating to public sex since the early 1990s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Finlayson1998_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Finlayson1998-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
