<p><b>Yauhen Mikalaevich Tsurkin</b> (<a href="/wiki/Belarusian_language" title="Belarusian language">Belarusian</a>: <span lang="be" xml:lang="be">Яўген Мікалаевіч Цуркін</span>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C5%81acinka" title="Łacinka">Łacinka</a>: Jaŭhien Mikałajevič Curkin; born 9 November 1990) is a Belarusian <a href="/wiki/Swimming_(sport)" title="Swimming (sport)">swimmer</a>, who specialized in sprint freestyle and butterfly events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-london2012_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-london2012-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yauhen_Tsurkin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He won a bronze medal in the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2012_European_Aquatics_Championships_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_50_metre_butterfly" title="Swimming at the 2012 European Aquatics Championships – Men's 50 metre butterfly">50 m butterfly</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2012_European_Aquatics_Championships" title="2012 European Aquatics Championships">2012 European Aquatics Championships</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Debrecen,_Hungary" title="Debrecen, Hungary">Debrecen, Hungary</a>, breaking a new Belarusian record time of 23.37 seconds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tsurkin qualified for the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_100_metre_freestyle" title="Swimming at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's 100 metre freestyle">men's 100 m freestyle</a>, as a member of the Belarusian swimming team, at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, by attaining a B-standard entry time of 49.62 seconds from the European Championships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He challenged seven other swimmers on the fourth heat, including British-born Paraguayan swimmer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benjamin_Hockin" title="Benjamin Hockin">Benjamin Hockin</a>. Tsurkin edged out sixth-place finisher <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Melconian_Alvez" title="Gabriel Melconian Alvez">Gabriel Melconian Alvez</a> of Uruguay by fifteen hundredths of a second (0.15), outside his qualifying entry time of 50.53 seconds. Tsurkin failed to advance into the semifinals, as he placed thirty-fourth out of 60 swimmers in the preliminaries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2012_European_Short_Course_Swimming_Championships" title="2012 European Short Course Swimming Championships">2012 European Short Course Swimming Championships</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chartres,_France" title="Chartres, France">Chartres, France</a>, Tsurkin lowered his national record time to 22.73 seconds, but was barely out touched by France's <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9rick_Bousquet" title="Frédérick Bousquet">Frédérick Bousquet</a> for the final top seeding in the semifinals of the men's 50 m butterfly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
