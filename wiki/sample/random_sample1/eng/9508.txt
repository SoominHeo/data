<p><b>Oil wrestling</b> (<a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <span lang="tr" xml:lang="tr"><i>Yağlı güreş</i></span>), also called <b>grease wrestling</b>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> is the <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkish</a> <a href="/wiki/National_sport" title="National sport">national sport</a>. It is so called because the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wrestler" title="Wrestler">wrestlers</a> douse themselves with <a href="/wiki/Olive_oil" title="Olive oil">olive oil</a>. The term "Güreş" is shared with other forms of wrestling practiced by <a href="/wiki/Turkic_peoples" title="Turkic peoples">Turkic-speakers</a> across Europe and Central Asia, such as the <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbek</a> <a href="/wiki/Kurash" title="Kurash">kurash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tuvans" title="Tuvans">Tuvan</a> <a href="/wiki/Khuresh" title="Khuresh">khuresh</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tatar" title="Tatar">Tatar</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%C3%B6r%C3%A4%C5%9F" title="Köräş">köräş</a>. The wrestlers, known as pehlivan meaning "hero" or "champion" wear a type of hand-stitched <a href="/wiki/Leather" title="Leather">leather</a> <a href="/wiki/Trousers" title="Trousers">trousers</a> called a <i>kisbet</i> (sometimes <i>kispet</i>), which is traditionally made of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Domestic_buffalo" title="Domestic buffalo">water buffalo</a> hide, and most recently has been made of <a href="/wiki/Calfskin" title="Calfskin">calfskin</a>.</p>
<p>Unlike <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sport_wrestling" title="Sport wrestling">Olympic wrestling</a>, oil wrestling matches may be won by achieving an effective hold of the kisbet. Thus, the pehlivan aims to control his opponent by putting his arm through the latter's kisbet. To win by this move is called <i>paça kazık</i>. Originally, matches had no set duration and could go on for one or two days until one man was able to establish his superiority, but in 1975 the duration was capped at 40 minutes for the baspehlivan<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (September 2011)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and 30 minutes for the pehlivan category. If there is no winner, play continues for another 15 minutes—10 minutes for the pehlivan category, wherein scores are kept to determine the victor.</p>
<p>The annual <a href="/wiki/K%C4%B1rkp%C4%B1nar" title="Kırkpınar">Kırkpınar</a> tournament, held in <a href="/wiki/Edirne" title="Edirne">Edirne</a> in Turkish Thrace since 1362, is the oldest continuously running, sanctioned sporting competition in the world.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Oil wrestling festivals also take place in northern Greece in the Eastern Macedonia (<a href="/wiki/Serres" title="Serres">Serres</a> region) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_Thrace" title="West Thrace">West Thrace</a> (<a href="/wiki/Rhodope_Mountains" title="Rhodope Mountains">Rhodope Mountains</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xronos_26471id_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-xronos_26471id-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xronos_36187id_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-xronos_36187id-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-na_serron_laikos_politismos_palh_ladi_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-na_serron_laikos_politismos_palh_ladi-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In recent years, this style of wrestling has also become popular in other countries, particularly the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oil_wrestling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cognate forms of <a href="/wiki/Folk_wrestling" title="Folk wrestling">folk wrestling</a> practiced by <a href="/wiki/Turkic_peoples" title="Turkic peoples">Turkic-speakers</a> are found throughout Western Eurasia (i.e. <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>) under the names <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ra%C5%9F" title="Köraş">Köraş</a>, <a href="/wiki/Khuresh" title="Khuresh">Khuresh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kurash" title="Kurash">Kurash</a>, etc.</p>

<p>Oil wrestling can be traced back to the ancient Sumer and Babylon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Greco-Roman traditions also point to the practice of oil wrestling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Turkish wrestlers started covering themselves according to Islamic law (between the navel and the knees) after the 10th century.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The Turkish word for wrestling can be traced back to the old <a href="/wiki/Oghuz_Turks" title="Oghuz Turks">Oghuz Turkic</a> languages, which originate from the Eurasian steppes, where wrestling has also been practiced. After the conquest of <a href="/wiki/Anatolia" title="Anatolia">Anatolia</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Seljuq_Empire" title="Great Seljuq Empire">Seljuk</a> Turks, a form of traditional freestyle wrestling called <a href="/wiki/Karakucak" title="Karakucak"><i>Karakucak Güreşi</i></a> (literally "Ground hug") was popularized, where special leather clothing was sanctioned and wrestlers commenced the activity by pouring olive oil on their bodies in order to make it harder for the wrestler grip one's opponent. This form continued to what is today known as Yağlı Güreş or Turkish oil wrestling. In the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>, wrestlers learned the art in special schools called tekke (<span lang="cta" xml:lang="cta">تکیه</span>), which were not merely athletic centres, but also spiritual centres.</p>

<p>Wrestlers oil one another prior to matches as a demonstration of balance and mutual respect. If a man defeats an older opponent, he kisses the latter's hand (a sign of respect for elders in Turkey).</p>
<p>Matches are held all over Turkey throughout the year, but in early summer, around 1000 competitors gather in <a href="/wiki/K%C4%B1rkp%C4%B1nar" title="Kırkpınar">Kırkpınar</a> for an annual three-day wrestling tournament to determine who will be the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baspehlivan" title="Baspehlivan">baspehlivan</a> ("chief wrestler") of Turkey. Ottoman chroniclers and writers<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (June 2011)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> attest that the <a href="/wiki/K%C4%B1rkp%C4%B1nar" title="Kırkpınar">Kırkpınar Games</a> have been held every year since 1362, making them the world's oldest continually sanctioned sporting competition.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute#Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (June 2011)">dubious</span></a> <span class="metadata">– <a href="/wiki/Talk:Oil_wrestling#Dubious" title="Talk:Oil wrestling">discuss</a></span></i>]</sup> The games have been cancelled only about 70 times. The original site was some 35 kilometres (22 mi) distant. In 1924, they were moved to the present location after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Balkan_War" title="Balkan War">Balkan War</a>.</p>
<p>There are some organized oil wrestling competitions outside Turkey, particularly by the Royal Dutch Power Sport Federation (<i>KNKF Koninklijke Nederlandse Krachtsport en Fitnessfederatie</i>) in the Netherlands.</p>
