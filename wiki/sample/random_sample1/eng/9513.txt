<p><b>Yacouba Isaac Zida</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina Faso">Burkinabé</a> <a href="/wiki/Military_of_Burkina_Faso" title="Military of Burkina Faso">military officer</a> who briefly served as Burkina Faso's acting head of state in November 2014. He took power in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Burkinab%C3%A9_uprising" title="2014 Burkinabé uprising">2014 Burkinabé uprising</a>, sidelining a more senior officer, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honor%C3%A9_Nab%C3%A9r%C3%A9_Traor%C3%A9" title="Honoré Nabéré Traoré">Honoré Nabéré Traoré</a>. A few weeks later, a civilian, <a href="/wiki/Michel_Kafando" title="Michel Kafando">Michel Kafando</a>, was chosen to replace Zida as transitional head of state; Kafando then appointed Zida as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_heads_of_government_of_Burkina_Faso" title="List of heads of government of Burkina Faso">Prime Minister</a> on 19 November 2014.</p>
<p>Zida was briefly removed from office by the <a href="/wiki/Regiment_of_Presidential_Security" title="Regiment of Presidential Security">Regiment of Presidential Security</a> in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2015_Burkinabe_coup_d%27etat" title="2015 Burkinabe coup d'etat">September 2015 coup</a>, but he was restored as Prime Minister within a week.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yacouba_Isaac_Zida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Zida obtained a master's degree in International Management from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Lyon" title="University of Lyon">University of Lyon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-2">[2]</a></sup> He also received military training from the American army.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Under President <a href="/wiki/Blaise_Compaor%C3%A9" title="Blaise Compaoré">Blaise Compaoré</a>, he served as deputy commander of the <a href="/wiki/Regiment_of_Presidential_Security" title="Regiment of Presidential Security">Regiment of Presidential Security</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He was a UN peacekeeper in <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a> from 2008 to 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_Burkinab.C3.A9_uprising">2014 Burkinabé uprising</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yacouba_Isaac_Zida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2014 Burkinabé uprising">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Palais_Kossyam_2013.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2057" data-file-width="3656" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Palais_Kossyam_2013.jpg/220px-Palais_Kossyam_2013.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Palais_Kossyam_2013.jpg/330px-Palais_Kossyam_2013.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Palais_Kossyam_2013.jpg/440px-Palais_Kossyam_2013.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Palais_Kossyam_2013.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Kossyam palace, where Zida aligned himself with the protesters</div>

<p>Amidst the <a href="/wiki/2014_Burkinab%C3%A9_uprising" title="2014 Burkinabé uprising">2014 Burkinabé uprising</a>, President Compaoré resigned on 31 October 2014 and army chief <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honor%C3%A9_Nab%C3%A9r%C3%A9_Traor%C3%A9" title="Honoré Nabéré Traoré">Honoré Nabéré Traoré</a> announced that he was taking over as head of state, but his claim to power was immediately contested by a group of junior officers headed by Zida, who aligned himself with the protesters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On 1 November 2014, the armed forces unanimously backed Zida to lead the country in an interim capacity towards the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burkinab%C3%A9_presidential_election,_2015" title="Burkinabé presidential election, 2015">2015 presidential election</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 17 November 2014, a civilian, <a href="/wiki/Michel_Kafando" title="Michel Kafando">Michel Kafando</a>, was chosen to replace Zida as transitional head of state, and he was sworn in on 18 November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Kafando then appointed Zida as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_heads_of_government_of_Burkina_Faso" title="List of heads of government of Burkina Faso">Prime Minister of Burkina Faso</a> on 19 November 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In the transitional government, appointed on 23 November, Zida held the defense portfolio in addition to his role as Prime Minister.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In mid-2015 there was a dispute between Zida and the Regiment of Presidential Security. In early July it was reported that he had resigned, but he refuted the reports and criticized the press for careless reporting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Nevertheless, he faced opposition from the military as well as Compaoré supporters, leaving him in a precarious position, although he maintained the support of Sankarists led by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B%C3%A9n%C3%A9wend%C3%A9_Sankara" title="Bénéwendé Sankara">Bénéwendé Sankara</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On 19 July 2015, President Kafando stripped Zida of the defense portfolio and took over the portfolio himself. He also dismissed <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Auguste_Denise_Barry&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Auguste Denise Barry (page does not exist)">Auguste Denise Barry</a>, who was closely associated with Zida, from his key post as Minister of Territorial Administration and Security.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 16 September 2015, two days after a recommendation from the National Reconciliation and Reforms Commission to disband the <a href="/wiki/Regiment_of_Presidential_Security" title="Regiment of Presidential Security">Regiment of Presidential Security</a> (RSP), members of the RSP <a href="/wiki/2015_Burkinab%C3%A9_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="2015 Burkinabé coup d'état">detained</a> President Kafando and Prime Minister Zida.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On 23 September, after the coup failed, Zida was reinstated as Prime Minister.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2015, near the end of the transitional period, Zida was promoted from the rank of lieutenant-colonel to <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G%C3%A9n%C3%A9ral_de_division" title="Général de division">général de division</a></i> in line with a law passed during the transition that provided for promotions to be granted "for services rendered to the nation".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Roch_Marc_Christian_Kabor%C3%A9" title="Roch Marc Christian Kaboré">Roch Marc Christian Kaboré</a>, who was elected as President, was sworn in on 29 December 2015<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and subsequently appointed <a href="/wiki/Paul_Kaba_Thieba" title="Paul Kaba Thieba">Paul Kaba Thieba</a>, an economist, as Prime Minister.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>The law providing for Zida's promotion was scrapped by the newly elected National Assembly. With permission from President Kaboré, Zida traveled to Canada in January 2016 to visit his family. He was due to return in February, but he failed to do so despite orders from Kaboré. In December 2016, Kaboré said that Zida would be expelled from the army for desertion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
