<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>In <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a>, the <b>Jacobi–Anger expansion</b> (or <b>Jacobi–Anger identity</b>) is an expansion of exponentials of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trigonometric_function" title="Trigonometric function">trigonometric functions</a> in the basis of their harmonics. It is useful in physics (for example, to <a href="/wiki/Plane_wave_expansion" title="Plane wave expansion">convert</a> between <a href="/wiki/Plane_wave" title="Plane wave">plane waves</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cylindrical_wave&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cylindrical wave (page does not exist)">cylindrical waves</a>), and in <a href="/wiki/Signal_processing" title="Signal processing">signal processing</a> (to describe <a href="/wiki/Frequency_modulation" title="Frequency modulation">FM</a> signals). This identity is named after the 19th-century mathematicians <a href="/wiki/Carl_Gustav_Jacob_Jacobi" title="Carl Gustav Jacob Jacobi">Carl Jacobi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carl_Theodor_Anger" title="Carl Theodor Anger">Carl Theodor Anger</a>.</p>
