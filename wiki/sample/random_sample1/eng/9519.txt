<p><b>Yakov Mikhailovich Sverdlov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Я́ков Миха́йлович Свердло́в</span>; <small>IPA: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Russian" title="Help:IPA for Russian">[ˈjakəf mʲɪˈxajləvʲɪtɕ svʲɪrdˈlof]</a></span>; known by pseudonyms "Andrei", "Mikhalych", "Max", "Smirnov", "Permyakov"; 3 June [<a href="/wiki/Old_Style_and_New_Style_dates" title="Old Style and New Style dates">O.S.</a> 22 May] 1885 – 16 March 1919) was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolshevik" title="Bolshevik">Bolshevik</a> party administrator and chairman of the <a href="/wiki/All-Russian_Central_Executive_Committee" title="All-Russian Central Executive Committee">All-Russian Central Executive Committee</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakov_Sverdlov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sverdlov was born in <a href="/wiki/Nizhny_Novgorod" title="Nizhny Novgorod">Nizhny Novgorod</a> as Yakov Mikhailovich Sverdlov to Jewish parents Mikhail Izrailevich Sverdlov and Elizaveta Solomonova. His father was a politically active engraver who produced <a href="/wiki/Forgery" title="Forgery">forged</a> documents and stored arms for the revolutionary underground. The Sverdlov family had six children: two daughters (Sophia and Sara) and four sons (Zinovy, Yakov, Veniamin, and Lev). After his wife's death in 1900, Mikhail converted with his family to the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Orthodox_Church" title="Russian Orthodox Church">Russian Orthodox Church</a>, married Maria Aleksandrovna Kormiltseva, and had two more sons, Herman and Alexander. Yakov's eldest brother <a href="/wiki/Zinovy_Peshkov" title="Zinovy Peshkov">Zinovy</a> was adopted by <a href="/wiki/Maxim_Gorky" title="Maxim Gorky">Maxim Gorky</a>, who was a frequent guest at the house.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Yakov Sverdlov joined the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Social_Democratic_Labour_Party" title="Russian Social Democratic Labour Party">Russian Social Democratic Labour Party</a> in 1902, and then the Bolshevik faction, supporting <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Lenin" title="Vladimir Lenin">Vladimir Lenin</a>. He was involved in the <a href="/wiki/1905_Russian_Revolution" title="1905 Russian Revolution">1905 revolution</a> while living in the Ural Mountains.</p>
<p>After four years of high school, he became a major activist and speaker in <a href="/wiki/Nizhny_Novgorod" title="Nizhny Novgorod">Nizhny Novgorod</a>. For most of the time from his arrest in June 1906 until 1917 he was either imprisoned or exiled. During the period 1914–1916 he was in internal exile in <a href="/wiki/Turukhansk" title="Turukhansk">Turukhansk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Stalin" title="Joseph Stalin">Joseph Stalin</a>. Both had been betrayed by the <a href="/wiki/Okhrana" title="Okhrana">Okhrana</a> agent <a href="/wiki/Roman_Malinovsky" title="Roman Malinovsky">Roman Malinovsky</a>. Like Stalin, he was co-opted <i>in absentia</i> to the 1912 <a href="/wiki/Prague_Conference" title="Prague Conference">Prague Conference</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKotkin2014154_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKotkin2014154-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Party_leader">Party leader</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakov_Sverdlov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Party leader">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the 1917 <a href="/wiki/February_Revolution" title="February Revolution">February Revolution</a> Sverdlov returned to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Petrograd" title="Petrograd">Petrograd</a> from exile and was re-elected to the <a href="/wiki/Central_Committee_of_the_Communist_Party_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Central Committee of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union">Central Committee</a> of the Communist Party. He played an important role in planning the <a href="/wiki/October_Revolution" title="October Revolution">October Revolution</a>.</p>
<p>He first met Lenin in April 1917 and was subsequently trusted as the chairman of the Central Committee <a href="/wiki/Secretariat_(administrative_office)" title="Secretariat (administrative office)">Secretariat</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKotkin2014193.E2.80.93194_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKotkin2014193.E2.80.93194-2">[2]</a></sup> Sverdlov was elected chairman of the <a href="/wiki/All-Russian_Central_Executive_Committee" title="All-Russian Central Executive Committee">All-Russian Central Executive Committee</a> in November, becoming thereby <i>de jure</i> head of state of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_SFSR" title="Russian SFSR">Russian SFSR</a> until his death. He played an important role in the controversial decision to end the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Constituent_Assembly" title="Russian Constituent Assembly">Russian Constituent Assembly</a> and the signing of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Brest-Litovsk" title="Treaty of Brest-Litovsk">Treaty of Brest-Litovsk</a>.</p>
<p>Sverdlov had a prodigious memory and was able to retain the names and details of fellow revolutionaries while in exile. His organizational capability was well-regarded and during his chairmanship thousands of local party committees were initiated. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKotkin2014193.E2.80.93194_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKotkin2014193.E2.80.93194-2">[2]</a></sup> Sverdlov is sometimes regarded as the first <a href="/wiki/List_of_heads_of_state_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="List of heads of state of the Soviet Union">head of state of the Soviet Union</a>, although the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> wasn't established until 1922, three years after his death.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Romanov_family">Romanov family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakov_Sverdlov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Romanov family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shooting_of_the_Romanov_family" title="Shooting of the Romanov family">Shooting of the Romanov family</a></div>
<p>A number of sources claim that Sverdlov played a major role in the execution of Tsar Nicholas II and his family.</p>
<p>A book written in 1990 by the Moscow playwright <a href="/wiki/Edvard_Radzinsky" title="Edvard Radzinsky">Edvard Radzinsky</a> claims that Sverdlov ordered their execution on 16 July 1918. This book as well as other Radzinsky's books were characterized as "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Folk_history" title="Folk history">folk history</a>" (Russian term for <a href="/wiki/Pseudohistory" title="Pseudohistory">pseudohistory</a>) by journalists and academic historians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-knife_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-knife-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-novyymir_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-novyymir-7">[7]</a></sup> However <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Slezkine" title="Yuri Slezkine">Yuri Slezkine</a> in his book <i>The Jewish Century</i> expressed the same opinion: "Early in the Civil War, in June 1918, Lenin ordered the killing of Nicholas II and his family. Among the men entrusted with carrying out the orders were Sverdlov, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filipp_Goloshchekin" title="Filipp Goloshchekin">Filipp Goloshchekin</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ru.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D0%93%D0%BE%D0%BB%D0%BE%D1%89%D1%91%D0%BA%D0%B8%D0%BD,_%D0%A4%D0%B8%D0%BB%D0%B8%D0%BF%D0%BF_%D0%98%D1%81%D0%B0%D0%B5%D0%B2%D0%B8%D1%87" title="ru:Голощёкин, Филипп Исаевич">ru</a>)</span> and <a href="/wiki/Yakov_Yurovsky" title="Yakov Yurovsky">Yakov Yurovsky</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESlezkine2011178_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESlezkine2011178-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 1922 book by a <a href="/wiki/White_movement" title="White movement">White Army</a> general, <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Diterikhs" title="Mikhail Diterikhs">Mikhail Diterikhs</a>, <i>The Murder of the Tsar’s Family and members of the House of Romanov in the Urals,</i> sought to portray the murder of the royal family as a Jewish plot against Russia. It referred to Sverdlov by his Jewish nickname "Yankel" and to Goloshchekin as "Isaac". This book in turn was based on an account by one <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Nikolai_Sokolov" title="Nikolai Sokolov">Nikolai Sokolov</a>, special investigator for the <a href="/wiki/Omsk" title="Omsk">Omsk</a> regional court, whom Diterikhs assigned with the task of investigating the disappearance of the Romanovs while serving as regional governor under the White regime during the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Civil_War" title="Russian Civil War">Russian Civil War</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to <a href="/wiki/Leon_Trotsky" title="Leon Trotsky">Leon Trotsky</a>'s diaries, after returning from the front (of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Civil_War" title="Russian Civil War">Russian Civil War</a>) he had the following dialogue with Sverdlov:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<dl>
<dd>-By the way, where is the tsar?</dd>
<dd>-Executed, of course.</dd>
<dd>-And where is the family?</dd>
<dd>-And the family, together with him.</dd>
<dd>-The whole?</dd>
<dd>-The whole. What do you have in mind?</dd>
<dd>-Who made the decision?</dd>
<dd>-We here made the decision. <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Lenin" title="Vladimir Lenin">Ilyich</a> reckoned there should be no live banner left for "them".</dd>
</dl>
</blockquote>
<p>The investigating magistrate in Ekaterinburg in 1918 saw the signed telegraphic instructions to murder the Imperial Family came from Sverdlov. These details have been published for decades. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakov_Sverdlov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:227px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ekaterinbourg.jpeg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="482" data-file-width="737" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Ekaterinbourg.jpeg/225px-Ekaterinbourg.jpeg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Ekaterinbourg.jpeg/338px-Ekaterinbourg.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Ekaterinbourg.jpeg/450px-Ekaterinbourg.jpeg 2x" width="225"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ekaterinbourg.jpeg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Snow-covered statue of Sverdlov in <a href="/wiki/Yekaterinburg" title="Yekaterinburg">Yekaterinburg</a>, formerly Sverdlovsk.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Sverdlov is commonly believed to have died of either <a href="/wiki/Typhus" title="Typhus">typhus</a> or most likely <a href="/wiki/Influenza" title="Influenza">influenza</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/1918_flu_pandemic" title="1918 flu pandemic">1918 flu pandemic</a>, after a political visit to <a href="/wiki/Oryol" title="Oryol">Oryol</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKotkin2014318.E2.80.93319_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKotkin2014318.E2.80.93319-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKhrushchev2006_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKhrushchev2006-13">[13]</a></sup> He is buried in the <a href="/wiki/Kremlin_Wall_Necropolis" title="Kremlin Wall Necropolis">Kremlin Wall Necropolis</a>, in Moscow.</p>
<p>Historian <a href="/wiki/Arkady_Vaksberg" title="Arkady Vaksberg">Arkady Vaksberg</a> claimed that there were reliable rumours that Sverdlov was beaten to death by workers in <a href="/wiki/Oryol" title="Oryol">Oryol</a>. Another speculation is that he was eliminated due to his supposed involvement in an attempt to assassinate Lenin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Waksberg_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Waksberg-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1924, <a href="/wiki/Yekaterinburg" title="Yekaterinburg">Yekaterinburg</a> was renamed Sverdlovsk in his honor. In 1991, Sverdlovsk was changed back to Yekaterinburg.</p>
<p>His son <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Andrei_Sverdlov&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Andrei Sverdlov (page does not exist)">Andrei</a> had a long career as an officer for the Soviet security organs (<a href="/wiki/NKVD" title="NKVD">NKVD</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OGPU" title="OGPU">OGPU</a>). His niece Ida married NKVD chief <a href="/wiki/Genrikh_Yagoda" title="Genrikh Yagoda">Genrikh Yagoda</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakov_Sverdlov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Russian_Navy" title="Imperial Russian Navy">Imperial Russian Navy</a> destroyer leader <a href="/wiki/Russian_destroyer_Novik_(1911)" title="Russian destroyer Novik (1911)"><i>Novik</i></a> (commissioned during 1913) was renamed <a href="/wiki/Soviet_destroyer_Yakov_Sverdlov" title="Soviet destroyer Yakov Sverdlov"><i>Yakov Sverdlov</i></a> during 1923.</li>
<li>The first ship of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sverdlov_class_cruiser" title="Sverdlov class cruiser"><i>Sverdlov</i> class cruisers</a> was also named after him.</li>
<li>The city of <a href="/wiki/Yekaterinburg" title="Yekaterinburg">Yekaterinburg</a> had the name of Sverdlovsk in the USSR (1924–1991).</li>
<li>A few <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Sverdlovsky_District_(disambiguation)" title="Sverdlovsky District (disambiguation)">locations in the former Soviet Union</a> still bear Sverdlovsk's name, in the Russian Federation, in Ukraine, and in Kyrgyzstan. Others have been renamed.</li>
</ul>
