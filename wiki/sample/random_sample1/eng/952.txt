<p><b>Felix Gary Gray</b> (born July 17, 1969) is an American music video director, film director, film producer, and actor<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Gray directed <i><a href="/wiki/Friday_(1995_film)" title="Friday (1995 film)">Friday</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Set_It_Off_(film)" title="Set It Off (film)">Set It Off</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Negotiator" title="The Negotiator">The Negotiator</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Straight_Outta_Compton_(film)" title="Straight Outta Compton (film)">Straight Outta Compton</a></i>, as well as the remake of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Italian_Job_(2003_film)" title="The Italian Job (2003 film)">The Italian Job</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F._Gary_Gray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Gray was born in New York. He began his career in 1989 when he appeared uncredited in the satire comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Major_League_(film)" title="Major League (film)">Major League</a></i>. Three years later, he directed the music video for <a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/It_Was_a_Good_Day" title="It Was a Good Day">It Was a Good Day</a>". The video is a literal adaptation of the lyrics. He would go on to direct subsequent videos for Ice Cube, as well as artists such as <a href="/wiki/Cypress_Hill" title="Cypress Hill">Cypress Hill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Outkast" title="Outkast">Outkast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Queen_Latifah" title="Queen Latifah">Queen Latifah</a>.</p>
<p>At age 24, Gray directed his first film, the urban comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Friday_(1995_film)" title="Friday (1995 film)">Friday</a></i> with rapper-producer Ice Cube and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Tucker" title="Chris Tucker">Chris Tucker</a>. Next, he directed the film <i><a href="/wiki/Set_It_Off_(film)" title="Set It Off (film)">Set It Off</a></i>, with <a href="/wiki/Jada_Pinkett_Smith" title="Jada Pinkett Smith">Jada Pinkett</a> and Queen Latifah. He then directed <i><a href="/wiki/The_Negotiator" title="The Negotiator">The Negotiator</a></i>, which starred Academy Award-winner <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Spacey" title="Kevin Spacey">Kevin Spacey</a> and Academy Award-nominee <a href="/wiki/Samuel_L._Jackson" title="Samuel L. Jackson">Samuel L. Jackson</a>, and earned Gray both Best Film and Best Director awards at the 1998 Acapulco Film Festival.</p>
<p>Gray also directed <i><a href="/wiki/The_Italian_Job_(2003_film)" title="The Italian Job (2003 film)">The Italian Job</a></i>, an action-thriller starring Academy Award winner <a href="/wiki/Charlize_Theron" title="Charlize Theron">Charlize Theron</a> and Oscar nominee <a href="/wiki/Mark_Wahlberg" title="Mark Wahlberg">Mark Wahlberg</a>. Gray garnered the Best Director award at the 2004 Black American Film Festival for his work on the film, which surpassed the $100 million mark in the domestic box office.</p>
<p>His next film was <i><a href="/wiki/A_Man_Apart" title="A Man Apart">A Man Apart</a></i>, a violent action thriller starring Vin Diesel. He would later direct <i><a href="/wiki/Be_Cool" title="Be Cool">Be Cool</a></i>, an adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Elmore_Leonard" title="Elmore Leonard">Elmore Leonard</a>'s novel of the same name. The <a href="/wiki/John_Travolta" title="John Travolta">John Travolta</a> vehicle was a critical flop, but made $95.2 million worldwide.</p>
<p>His next film was the thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Law_Abiding_Citizen" title="Law Abiding Citizen">Law Abiding Citizen</a></i>, starring Academy Award winner <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Foxx" title="Jamie Foxx">Jamie Foxx</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Butler" title="Gerard Butler">Gerard Butler</a>. Scripted by <a href="/wiki/Kurt_Wimmer" title="Kurt Wimmer">Kurt Wimmer</a>, it grossed over $100 million worldwide.</p>
<p>Gray is also responsible for directing many music videos. He has worked with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TLC_(band)" title="TLC (band)">TLC</a>, Dr. Dre, <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Wonder" title="Stevie Wonder">Stevie Wonder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Babyface_(musician)" title="Babyface (musician)">Babyface</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, among others.</p>
<p>Gray received The Ivan Dixon Award of Achievement from the Black Hollywood Education and Resource Center and was named one of the “50 Best and Brightest African Americans Under 40” by <i>Black Enterprise</i> magazine. He was honored by the African American Film Critics Association with their 2004 Special Achievement Award and was recognized by the Artist Empowerment Coalition with the Artist Empowerment Award that same year. He also received the Pioneer Director award from the Pan-African Film and Arts Festival in 2010.</p>
<p>He also directed the critically acclaimed drama <i><a href="/wiki/Straight_Outta_Compton_(film)" title="Straight Outta Compton (film)">Straight Outta Compton</a></i>, which is a <a href="/wiki/Biographical_film" title="Biographical film">biographical film</a> about the rap group, <a href="/wiki/N.W.A" title="N.W.A">N.W.A</a>. The film made $200 million worldwide. Gray himself announced this film as his best work ever<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<p>Gray was recently selected as the director for the <a href="/wiki/The_Fate_of_the_Furious" title="The Fate of the Furious">eighth installment</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fast_and_the_Furious" title="The Fast and the Furious">The Fast and the Furious</a></i> franchise.</p>
