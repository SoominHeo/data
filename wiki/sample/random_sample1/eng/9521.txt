<p><b>Yakumo</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">八雲町</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yakumo-chō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_towns_in_Japan" title="List of towns in Japan">town</a> in <a href="/wiki/Futami_District,_Hokkaido" title="Futami District, Hokkaido">Futami District</a> (formerly located in <a href="/wiki/Yamakoshi_District,_Hokkaido" title="Yamakoshi District, Hokkaido">Yamakoshi District</a>), <a href="/wiki/Oshima_Subprefecture" title="Oshima Subprefecture">Oshima Subprefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hokkaido" title="Hokkaido">Hokkaido</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>As of 2008, the town's <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> was estimated as 19,543, with a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">density</a> of 23.08 persons per km<sup>2</sup>. The total area is 955.98 km<sup>2</sup>.</p>
<p>On October 1, 2005, the town of <a href="/wiki/Kumaishi,_Hokkaido" title="Kumaishi, Hokkaido">Kumaishi</a> (from <a href="/wiki/Nishi_District,_Hokkaido" title="Nishi District, Hokkaido">Nishi District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hiyama_Subprefecture" title="Hiyama Subprefecture">Hiyama Subprefecture</a>) was merged into Yakumo (formerly part of <a href="/wiki/Yamakoshi_District,_Hokkaido" title="Yamakoshi District, Hokkaido">Yamakoshi District</a>); now in the newly created <a href="/wiki/Futami_District,_Hokkaido" title="Futami District, Hokkaido">Futami District</a>. The former town of Kumaishi joined Oshima Subprefecture at the same time.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakumo,_Hokkaido&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yakumo is the only municipality which faces both the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Japan" title="Sea of Japan">Sea of Japan</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. The Yakumo area and the Kumaishi area are separated by mountains.</p>
<p>The name comes from the word "Yakumo" in the <a href="/wiki/Waka_(poetry)" title="Waka (poetry)">Waka</a> composed by <a href="/wiki/Susanoo-no-Mikoto" title="Susanoo-no-Mikoto">Susanoo-no-Mikoto</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakumo,_Hokkaido&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>1881 The village of Yakumo was founded in <a href="/wiki/Yamakoshi_District,_Hokkaido" title="Yamakoshi District, Hokkaido">Yamakosi District</a>.</li>
<li>1902
<ul>
<li>Yakumo village and Yamakoshinai village was merged to form the new village of Yakumo.</li>
<li>Kumaishi village was founded in <a href="/wiki/Nishi_District,_Hokkaido" title="Nishi District, Hokkaido">Nishi District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hiyama_Subprefecture" title="Hiyama Subprefecture">Hiyama Subprefecture</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>1919 Yakumo village became Yakumo town.</li>
<li>1957 Otoshibe village in <a href="/wiki/Kayabe_District,_Hokkaido" title="Kayabe District, Hokkaido">Kayabe District</a> was merged into Yakumo town.</li>
<li>1962 Kumaishi village became Kumaishi town.</li>
<li>2005 Yakumo town and Kumaishi town was merged to form new town of Yakumo in <a href="/wiki/Futami_District,_Hokkaido" title="Futami District, Hokkaido">Futami District</a>. (Because of it, Hiyama Subprefecture was divided between north and south.)</li>
</ul>
