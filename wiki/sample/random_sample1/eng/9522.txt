<p><b>Yakubu Aiyegbeni</b> (born 22 November 1982), known as <b>Yakubu</b>, is a Nigerian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a>. His nickname is "The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yak" title="Yak">Yak</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-efcprofile_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-efcprofile-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>His performances in the <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2002–03 UEFA Champions League">2002–03 UEFA Champions League</a> for <a href="/wiki/Maccabi_Haifa_F.C." title="Maccabi Haifa F.C.">Maccabi Haifa</a> earned a loan move to English club <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a> which became permanent as they were promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> in 2003. He has since appeared in over 250 Premier League matches for Portsmouth, <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a>, and is the second highest African goalscorer in Premier League history with 96 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He is the third highest scorer in the history of the <a href="/wiki/Nigeria_national_football_team" title="Nigeria national football team">Nigeria national football team</a>, and represented them at four <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_Cup_of_Nations" title="African Cup of Nations">African Cup of Nations</a> finals, the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 2000 Summer Olympics">football at the 2000 Summer Olympics</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Aiyegbeni Yakubu was born in <a href="/wiki/Benin_City" title="Benin City">Benin City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edo_State" title="Edo State">Edo State</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hugman2010-11_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Hugman2010-11-1">[1]</a></sup> A powerful central striker, he started his career with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Julius_Berger_FC" title="Julius Berger FC">Julius Berger FC</a> in Lagos as a teenager in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nigerian_Premier_League" title="Nigerian Premier League">Nigerian Premier League</a>. He was later loaned to the Portuguese club <a href="/wiki/Gil_Vicente_F.C." title="Gil Vicente F.C.">Gil Vicente</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Maccabi_Haifa">Maccabi Haifa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Maccabi Haifa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israeli</a> team <a href="/wiki/Maccabi_Haifa_F.C." title="Maccabi Haifa F.C.">Maccabi Haifa</a> paid $300,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Yakubu's highest scoring season at Haifa was 2001–02, when he scored 13 times in 22 games (League season was 33 matches). He came to England and trained with <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a> but could not obtain a work permit at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He scored seven goals in eight European Cup appearances in the 2002–03 season <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>, (including <a href="/wiki/List_of_UEFA_Champions_League_hat-tricks" title="List of UEFA Champions League hat-tricks">a hat-trick</a> against Olympiakos and a penalty in the 3–0 win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchester_United" title="Manchester United">Manchester United</a>).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Portsmouth">Portsmouth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Portsmouth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yakubu moved to English club <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a> of the First Division (now the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_Championship" title="English Championship">English Championship</a>) on loan on 6 January 2003. Signed by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Redknapp" title="Harry Redknapp">Harry Redknapp</a>, Yakubu helped Portsmouth win the <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_in_English_football" title="2002–03 in English football">2002–03 Division 1</a> title, scoring at a rate of one goal every two games (7 in 14 matches). He made his debut as a 57th-minute substitute for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vincent_Pericard" title="Vincent Pericard">Vincent Pericard</a> in a 1–1 away match versus <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brighton_%26_Hove_Albion" title="Brighton &amp; Hove Albion">Brighton &amp; Hove Albion</a> on 18 January. On 1 February he started for the first time and scored after 4 minutes at home to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grimsby_Town" title="Grimsby Town">Grimsby Town</a>. The match ended 3–0 with Yakubu substituted for Pericard after 89 minutes. Seven days later, Yakubu scored a goal in each half of a 6–2 victory over <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a> for his first brace in English football. He gained his second brace of the season with two first-half goals in a 5–0 away victory at <a href="/wiki/Millwall" title="Millwall">Millwall</a> on 1 March.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yakubu made his loan spell at Portsmouth permanent in May 2003<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> for a fee of £4 million. He played 37 of Portsmouth's 38 Premier League matches in 2003–04, starting all but two. Yakubu's 16 goals made him the club's leading goal-scorer, and joint-sixth highest scorer in the entire league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yakubu scored his first Premier League goal in his second match, opening a 1–1 draw versus <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchester_City" title="Manchester City">Manchester City</a> on 23 August 2003. After eight consecutive league matches without a goal, he scored a brace in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> match away to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nottingham_Forest" title="Nottingham Forest">Nottingham Forest</a> on 29 October, one of which was in extra time of the 4–2 win. Yakubu scored the final goal of a 6–1 home win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leeds_United" title="Leeds United">Leeds United</a> on 8 November. On 3 January 2004, he scored an 89th-minute winner in the FA Cup third round match at home to <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_F.C." title="Blackpool F.C.">Blackpool</a>. Three days later he scored an equaliser in the Premier League as Portsmouth lost 2–1 away to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aston_Villa" title="Aston Villa">Aston Villa</a>. On 10 January he scored his first Premier League brace with two goals in the second half of a 4–2 home win over Manchester City. He played at the <a href="/wiki/2004_African_Cup_of_Nations" title="2004 African Cup of Nations">2004 African Cup of Nations</a>.</p>
<p>Yakubu ended the season with 11 goals in his last 10 Premier League matches, starting with one each in four straight games, the first being the only goal in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Coast_Derby" title="South Coast Derby">South Coast Derby</a> versus Southampton on 21 March. This was followed by a late winner at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers" title="Blackburn Rovers">Blackburn Rovers</a> in a 2–1 away victory, and an equaliser away to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charlton_Athletic" title="Charlton Athletic">Charlton Athletic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On the final day of the season, he scored four goals as Portsmouth defeated <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a> 5–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 2004–05 season he scored 13 goals. His final goal for Portsmouth in May 2005 before he left the club – the equaliser in a 1–1 home draw to <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a> in the penulitimate league game of the season – was the goal that ensured Portsmouth would not be relegated from Premiership in the 2004–05 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yakubu's 28 Premier League goals between his two seasons make him Portsmouth's highest ever scorer in the Premier League, with nine more than their second-highest scorer <a href="/wiki/Benjani_Mwaruwari" title="Benjani Mwaruwari">Benjani Mwaruwari</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Identifying_reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Identifying reliable sources"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on an unreliable source. (February 2015)">unreliable source?</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Middlesbrough">Middlesbrough</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Middlesbrough">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:115px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Yakubu_aiyegbeni.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="275" data-file-width="113" height="275" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c5/Yakubu_aiyegbeni.jpg" width="113"/></a>

<p>After the 2004–05 season, he transferred from Portsmouth to <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a> for a £7.5 million record fee, the highest for a Nigerian ever.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Yakubu scored the only goal of the game with a penalty in the first leg of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> last 16 round at home against <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Roma</a>, giving Middlesbrough a 1–0 victory, who later went through on away goals. His performances were rewarded, as he helped Middlesbrough reach the <a href="/wiki/2006_UEFA_Cup_Final" title="2006 UEFA Cup Final">2006 UEFA Cup Final</a> against <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla</a>, in which they lost 4–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>His transfer from Portsmouth to Middlesbrough in July 2005 is one of those about which the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006_allegations_of_corruption_in_English_football#Stevens_report" title="2006 allegations of corruption in English football">Stevens inquiry</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> report in June 2007 expressed concerns because of the lack of co-operation from agents <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pinhas_Zahavi" title="Pinhas Zahavi">Pinhas Zahavi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barry_Silkman" title="Barry Silkman">Barry Silkman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Everton">Everton</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Everton">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yakubu was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> in August 2007 for a fee of £11.25 million on a five-year deal,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-efcprofile_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-efcprofile-2">[2]</a></sup> which was at the time the most the club had ever paid for a player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Shunning the traditional centre-forwards' number 9, Yakubu asked to be assigned number 22, setting this number as a goal-scoring target for his first season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> a feat he fell one goal short of achieving.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He scored 11 minutes into his debut for the club, the first goal in a 2–1 win against <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reebok_Stadium" title="Reebok Stadium">Reebok Stadium</a> in September 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> His first hat-trick for the club came against <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a> in a 3–0 home league win in December 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Yakubu travelled to <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a> in early 2008 to compete in the <a href="/wiki/2008_Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="2008 Africa Cup of Nations">Cup of Nations</a>. Following Nigeria's exit from the competition, he was late in returning to his club, and as a result was dropped by manager <a href="/wiki/David_Moyes" title="David Moyes">David Moyes</a> once back in England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-efcprofile_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-efcprofile-2">[2]</a></sup> His double against <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> in a 3–1 win at <a href="/wiki/Goodison_Park" title="Goodison Park">Goodison Park</a> on the last day of the 2007–08 season helped the Toffees clinch fifth place and a place in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008-09_UEFA_Cup" title="2008-09 UEFA Cup">2008-09 UEFA Cup</a> competition. He finished his first season with 15 Premier League goals to his name, and 21 goals in all competitions for Everton.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sb_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-sb-22">[22]</a></sup> Yakubu achieved his highest goal tally for a single season, and also became the first Everton player since <a href="/wiki/Peter_Beardsley" title="Peter Beardsley">Peter Beardsley</a> to net 20 goals in a season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yakubu scored his 100th goal in English football in a league game at <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a> early in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Premier_League_2008%E2%80%9309" title="Premier League 2008–09">Premier League 2008–09</a> season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> He scored a goal against <a href="/wiki/Standard_Li%C3%A8ge" title="Standard Liège">Standard Liège</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> and got back on the scoresheet with a goal against Middlesbrough, his first goal in 10 games. He ruptured his <a href="/wiki/Achilles_tendon" title="Achilles tendon">achilles tendon</a> in the 1–0 away win at <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> on 30 November, ruling him out for the rest of the season. Yakubu played his first competitive game after the Achilles tendon injury against <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley Reserves</a>, and in his first start for the first team in almost a year, he scored within 11 minutes against <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a> in the League Cup. He came on for <a href="/wiki/J%C3%B4" title="Jô">Jô</a> in first-half injury time and scored an equaliser with his first touch to bring Everton level at <a href="/wiki/Stamford_Bridge_(stadium)" title="Stamford Bridge (stadium)">Stamford Bridge</a> against <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a>. He later scored the winning goal against Stoke.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leicester_City">Leicester City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Leicester City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Yakubu.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="830" data-file-width="420" height="296" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Yakubu.jpg/150px-Yakubu.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Yakubu.jpg/225px-Yakubu.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Yakubu.jpg/300px-Yakubu.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Yakubu.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Yakubu playing for <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a> in 2011</div>

<p>Yakubu joined <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a> on loan until the end of the season on 13 January 2011, with the possibility of a permanent deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> He made his debut and scored in a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Preston_North_End_F.C." title="Preston North End F.C.">Preston North End</a> on 15 January 2011,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Preston_1-1_Leicester_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Preston_1-1_Leicester-27">[27]</a></sup> followed by another goal in a 4–2 win over <a href="/wiki/Millwall_F.C." title="Millwall F.C.">Millwall</a> on 22 January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leicester_4_-_2_Millwall_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Leicester_4_-_2_Millwall-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 12 February 2011 he scored a left footed volley in their 2–0 win over local rivals <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a>. On 2 April 2011, he scored his first hat-trick against his former club <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a>. He then scored a double in Leicester's 4–2 win over <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Blackburn_Rovers">Blackburn Rovers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Blackburn Rovers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 31 August 2011, Yakubu signed a three-year deal with <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> for an undisclosed fee and was assigned the number 24 shirt by manager <a href="/wiki/Steve_Kean" title="Steve Kean">Steve Kean</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yakubu_signs_for_Rovers_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yakubu_signs_for_Rovers-29">[29]</a></sup> He scored his first and second goals for Blackburn Rovers on 17 September on his Premier League debut against <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> in which Blackburn won 4–3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> He was voted Man of the Match as he helped Blackburn to their first win of the season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> with Blackburn fans chanting "Feed the Yak and he will score!" by the time the final whistle blew.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> He scored his third goal in the league in the 3–3 away draw against <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City</a>. Yakubu's fourth and fifth goals of the league season came in an away 3–3 draw at <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a> on 19 November 2011. He opened the scoring from long range after 65 seconds and equalised with an injury-time penalty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> He had earlier been behind an incident when Blackburn were 2–1 down, by dummying a short corner to allow team-mate <a href="/wiki/Morten_Gamst_Pedersen" title="Morten Gamst Pedersen">Morten Gamst Pedersen</a> to dribble the ball from the corner and cross for <a href="/wiki/Junior_Hoilett" title="Junior Hoilett">Junior Hoilett</a> to equalise the game 2–2. At the end of the game, Yakubu admitted that he did not touch the ball and that the goal should not have stood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 3 December 2011, Yakubu <a href="/wiki/List_of_Premier_League_hat-tricks" title="List of Premier League hat-tricks">scored all four goals</a> for Blackburn in a 4–2 home win over <a href="/wiki/Swansea_City_A.F.C." title="Swansea City A.F.C.">Swansea City</a>. He scored two in each half, finishing with a penalty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> It was his first Blackburn hat-trick and his first four-goal game since the last day of the 2003–04 season, for Portsmouth against Middlesbrough. He scored 2 goals, one being a penalty, in his side's shock 2–3 victory against Manchester United at <a href="/wiki/Old_Trafford" title="Old Trafford">Old Trafford</a> on 31 December 2011 which left him on 13 goals for the season in all competitions. On 15 January 2012, in a match against <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a>, Yakubu was sent off for the first time in the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> for a straight red.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> On his return from suspension, he scored against QPR. On 10 April 2012, Yakubu scored twice, while missing one penalty and scoring a second, in Blackburn's 2–3 loss at home to Liverpool. In his first season at Blackburn, he scored 18 goals, but the club were relegated to the Championship by the end of the season after a 1–0 home loss to in-form <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic" title="Wigan Athletic">Wigan Athletic</a>. After relegation was confirmed, Yakubu stated that he did not want to play Championship football and wanted to leave Blackburn.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guangzhou_R.26F">Guangzhou R&amp;F</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Guangzhou R&amp;F">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yakubu confirmed on 28 June 2012 that he had left Blackburn and had signed for Chinese side <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou_R%26F_F.C." title="Guangzhou R&amp;F F.C.">Guangzhou R&amp;F</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> on an initial three-year deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> On 15 July, Yakubu made his Chinese Super League debut in a Guangzhou Derby match which Guangzhou R&amp;F played against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guangzhou_Evergrande" title="Guangzhou Evergrande">Guangzhou Evergrande</a> at <a href="/wiki/Tianhe_Stadium" title="Tianhe Stadium">Tianhe Stadium</a>. He scored the winning goal of the match in the 87th minute, ending Guangzhou Evergrande's 34-league-home-match unbeaten run.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reading">Reading</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Reading">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 2 February 2015, <a href="/wiki/Reading_F.C." title="Reading F.C.">Reading F.C.</a> signed free agent Yakubu on a contract until the end of the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Reading_F.C._season" title="2014–15 Reading F.C. season">2014–15</a> season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Royals_sign_Yakubu_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-Royals_sign_Yakubu-40">[40]</a></sup> On 14 February, Yakubu scored his first goal for the Royals with the winner in a 2–1 defeat of <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a> at <a href="/wiki/Pride_Park" title="Pride Park">Pride Park</a> to put the club into the quarter-finals of the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_FA_Cup" title="2014–15 FA Cup">FA Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Yakubu was released by Reading on 21 May 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Released_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-Released-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kayserispor">Kayserispor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Kayserispor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 31 August 2015, Yakubu signed a one-year contract, with the option of an additional year, with Turkish <a href="/wiki/S%C3%BCper_Lig" title="Süper Lig">Süper Lig</a> side <a href="/wiki/Kayserispor" title="Kayserispor">Kayserispor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Yakubu has scored 21 goals in his 57 appearances for <a href="/wiki/Nigeria_national_football_team" title="Nigeria national football team">Nigeria</a> since his 2000 debut. He is currently the third highest ever international goalscorer for <a href="/wiki/Nigeria_national_football_team" title="Nigeria national football team">Nigeria</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000_Olympic_Games">2000 Olympic Games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2000 Olympic Games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In September 2000, Yakubu featured in Nigeria's squad for <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 2000 Summer Olympics">football at the 2000 Summer Olympics</a> in Australia. Nigeria were the holders for the tournament. Then of Maccabi Haifa, the 17-year-old Yakubu wore the number 9 shirt. He scored the injury-time equaliser in Nigeria's first match, a 3–3 draw with <a href="/wiki/Honduras_national_football_team" title="Honduras national football team">Honduras</a> on 13 September in <a href="/wiki/Adelaide" title="Adelaide">Adelaide</a>. He also played against the hosts Australia on 16 September in Sydney as his side won 3–2 but did not play on 19 September as they drew with <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> in Adelaide. He featured in the quarter-finals as Nigeria lost 4–1 to <a href="/wiki/Chile_national_football_team" title="Chile national football team">Chile</a> in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a> on 23 September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fifa.com_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-fifa.com-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2002_African_Cup_of_Nations_and_2002_FIFA_World_Cup">2002 African Cup of Nations and 2002 FIFA World Cup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2002 African Cup of Nations and 2002 FIFA World Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yakubu made his full international debut on 22 April 2000 in a 4–0 home win in <a href="/wiki/Lagos" title="Lagos">Lagos</a> against <a href="/wiki/Eritrea_national_football_team" title="Eritrea national football team">Eritrea</a> in qualification for the 2002 African Cup of Nations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fifa.com_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-fifa.com-44">[44]</a></sup> He scored his first international goal in an away win against Namibia on 16 June 2001 in the same qualification campaign. On 1 July 2001, he scored his first international brace, with the second and fourth goals in a 4–0 away win over <a href="/wiki/Sudan_national_football_team" title="Sudan national football team">Sudan</a> in <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 FIFA World Cup</a> qualification. He was named in the squad for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2002_Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="2002 Africa Cup of Nations">2002 Africa Cup of Nations</a> in Mali and was given the number 8 shirt by manager <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shaibu_Amodu" title="Shaibu Amodu">Shaibu Amodu</a>. On 9 February 2002, Yakubu scored the only goal of the third-place play-off against <a href="/wiki/Mali_national_football_team" title="Mali national football team">Mali</a> after 29 minutes. However, Yakubu was not named later in the year in the squad selected by new manager <a href="/wiki/Festus_Onigbinde" title="Festus Onigbinde">Festus Onigbinde</a> for the 2002 World Cup in Japan and <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004_African_Cup_of_Nations">2004 African Cup of Nations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 2004 African Cup of Nations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 7 June 2003, Yakubu scored Nigeria's first two goals as they came from behind to beat <a href="/wiki/Malawi_national_football_team" title="Malawi national football team">Malawi</a> 4–1 in a qualifier for the 2004 African Cup of Nations. He retained the number 8 shirt in the squad named for the finals in <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a> by manager <a href="/wiki/Christian_Chukwu" title="Christian Chukwu">Christian Chukwu</a> but did not score. Alongside Celestine Babayaro and Victor Agali, he was sent home from the tournament for indiscipline. His then manager at Portsmouth, Harry Redknapp said he cared less about the tournament and was glad to have Yakubu back.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification">2006 FIFA World Cup qualification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: 2006 FIFA World Cup qualification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yakubu scored the final goal in an away 3–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe_national_football_team" title="Zimbabwe national football team">Zimbabwe</a> and an equaliser in an away 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Gabon_national_football_team" title="Gabon national football team">Gabon</a> as Nigeria were beaten to the qualification place by <a href="/wiki/Angola_national_football_team" title="Angola national football team">Angola</a> and missed the finals for the first time since 1994. Despite having a goal difference superior by +14 to +8, Nigeria did not qualify as the tie-break was on head-to-head between Nigeria and Angola.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Decision_against_2006_African_Cup_of_Nations">Decision against 2006 African Cup of Nations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Decision against 2006 African Cup of Nations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although Nigeria gained a place at the tournament due to their performance in World Cup qualification, Yakubu decided not to play at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006_African_Cup_of_Nations" title="2006 African Cup of Nations">2006 African Cup of Nations</a> to concentrate on his form at Middlesbrough instead. He was praised for his decision as the club defeated defending champions and league leaders <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> 3–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_FIFA_World_Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: 2010 FIFA World Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> in South Africa, Yakubu notably missed an open goal from three metres against <a href="/wiki/South_Korea_national_football_team" title="South Korea national football team">South Korea</a> in the last Group game<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> but later went on to score a penalty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Yakubu's penalty made sure that Nigeria earned a 2–2 draw, but this was not enough for his side to progress out of the group stages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakubu_Aiyegbeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2008 Yakubu married Yvonne Lameen Ikhana, the daughter of former Nigerian international football player <a href="/wiki/Kadiri_Ikhana" title="Kadiri Ikhana">Kadiri Ikhana</a>, who is also the father-in-law to former Nigerian international football player <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Ovie" title="Patrick Ovie">Patrick Ovie</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Yakubu and Ikhana have 2 daughters named Kayla and Katriel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> He used to play barefoot on the streets of Benin, Nigeria, until his brother gave him a pair of boots aged 12.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
