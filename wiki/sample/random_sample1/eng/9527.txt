<p><b>Yahya Golmohammadi</b> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="fa" xml:lang="fa">ÙØ­ÙÙ Ú¯Ù ÙØ­ÙØ¯Ù</span>ââ, born 19 March 1971) is an Iranian former <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> and coach who is the former <a href="/wiki/Manager_(association_football)" title="Manager (association football)">head coach</a> of <a href="/wiki/Oxin_Alborz_F.C." title="Oxin Alborz F.C.">Oxin</a>. He is one of only six Iranians to score in a <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cup</a>. His position was usually the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_defender" title="Central defender">central defender</a>. He was a member of <a href="/wiki/Iran_national_football_team" title="Iran national football team">Iranian national football team</a> and played most of his career in <a href="/wiki/Persepolis_F.C." title="Persepolis F.C.">Persepolis</a>. He previously managed <a href="/wiki/Saba_Qom_F.C." title="Saba Qom F.C.">Saba Qom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tarbiat_Yazd_F.C." title="Tarbiat Yazd F.C.">Tarbiat Yazd</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nassaji_Mazandaran_F.C." title="Nassaji Mazandaran F.C.">Nassaji Mazandaran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Persepolis_F.C." title="Persepolis F.C.">Persepolis</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Naft_Tehran_F.C." title="Naft Tehran F.C.">Naft Tehran</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahya_Golmohammadi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was born on 19 March 1971 in <a href="/wiki/Minabad,_Ardabil" title="Minabad, Ardabil">Minabad</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ardabil_Province" title="Ardabil Province">Ardabil Province</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> where, as a youngster was recognized for his footballing talent.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_career">Playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahya_Golmohammadi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Club">Club</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahya_Golmohammadi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Club">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yahya Golmohammadi played most of his professional career for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Persepolis_FC" title="Persepolis FC">Persepolis</a>. He made his debut as a football player in 1989 with <a href="/wiki/Tractor_Sazi_F.C." title="Tractor Sazi F.C.">Tractor Sazi</a>. Two years later, he signed with <a href="/wiki/Poora_F.C." title="Poora F.C.">Poora</a>. He played in Poora for four years and later signed with <a href="/wiki/Persepolis_F.C." title="Persepolis F.C.">Persepolis</a> in 1995 and began his work with <a href="/wiki/Stanko_Poklepovi%C4%87" title="Stanko PoklepoviÄ">Stanko PoklepoviÄ</a> and won the league in his first season. In 1999 he moved to <a href="/wiki/Ahvaz" title="Ahvaz">Ahvaz</a> based club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foolad" title="Foolad">Foolad</a>, but returned to Persepolis after only three seasons. In 2005, Yahya left <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Persepolis_FC" title="Persepolis FC">Persepolis</a> for a lucrative contract with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saba_Battery_FC" title="Saba Battery FC">Saba Battery</a>, many critics were skeptical about his success in this newly formed team, but Saba Battery, with the help of Golmohammadi and Iranian national teammate <a href="/wiki/Ali_Daei" title="Ali Daei">Ali Daei</a>, surprised everyone by winning the <a href="/wiki/Hazfi_Cup" title="Hazfi Cup">Hazfi Cup</a>, and also did extremely well in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_Champions_League" title="Asian Champions League">Asian Champions League</a>, beating more experienced and seasoned campaigners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He played for Saba until 2008 when he announced his retirement as a football player.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahya_Golmohammadi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Golmohammadi was a reliable defender with a cool approach to the game and slide tackles that became his hallmark. He has been immensely popular with Iranian fans after he scored the last minute winner in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup_(qualification)" title="2002 FIFA World Cup (qualification)">2002 World Cup qualifying</a> game against <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Republic of Ireland</a>, coming deep to head the ball home though Iran lost the game in aggregate and failed to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">Korea/Japan World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> His emotions after the game were a dramatic scene that very few die-hard fans will forget.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 11 June 2006, Golmohammadi scored the equaliser in a 3â1 loss to <a href="/wiki/Mexico_national_football_team" title="Mexico national football team">Mexico</a> in the opening round of the <a href="/wiki/Iran_at_the_2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="Iran at the 2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a>. His tackle against <a href="/wiki/Lu%C3%ADs_Figo" title="LuÃ­s Figo">LuÃ­s Figo</a> in the next game against <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a> cost Iran a penalty. Golmohammadi was injured in the same game and was not available for Iran's final World Cup game versus <a href="/wiki/Angola_national_football_team" title="Angola national football team">Angola</a>. He retired from international football immediately after World Cup 2006. He has 74 caps for the senior team and scored five times during his international duty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was also a member of <a href="/wiki/Iran_national_under-23_football_team" title="Iran national under-23 football team">Iran national under-23 football team</a> in <a href="/wiki/2002_Asian_Games" title="2002 Asian Games">2002 Asian Games</a> which Iran won the football tournament under <a href="/wiki/Branko_Ivankovi%C4%87" title="Branko IvankoviÄ">Branko IvankoviÄ</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">Coaching career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahya_Golmohammadi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Coaching career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Saba">Saba</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahya_Golmohammadi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Saba">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the resignation of <a href="/wiki/Mohammad_Hossein_Ziaei" title="Mohammad Hossein Ziaei">Mohammad Hossein Ziaei</a> as the head coach of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saba_Battery_F.C." title="Saba Battery F.C.">Saba Battery</a>, Golmohammadi was named as his successor to lead the team for the ensuing fixtures. Golmohammadi led Saba in the remaining ten matches and led them to a third-place finish behind <a href="/wiki/Persepolis_F.C." title="Persepolis F.C.">Persepolis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sepahan_F.C." title="Sepahan F.C.">Sepahan</a> which is the best result for Saba in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iran_Pro_League" title="Iran Pro League">Iran Pro League</a> in the club's history. There were many rumors that Golmohammadi would continue to lead Saba for the following season but he refused to extend his contract. <a href="/wiki/Firouz_Karimi" title="Firouz Karimi">Firouz Karimi</a> succeeded him as head coach of Saba in June 2008.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tarbiat_Yazd">Tarbiat Yazd</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahya_Golmohammadi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Tarbiat Yazd">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2008, Golmohammadi took over the <a href="/wiki/Azadegan_League" title="Azadegan League">Azadegan League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Tarbiat_Yazd_F.C." title="Tarbiat Yazd F.C.">Tarbiat Yazd</a>. His team started the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Azadegan_League" title="2008â09 Azadegan League">2008â09 season</a> with three consecutive wins but at the end of the season, Tarbiat finished at 5th place of Group B and were not promoted to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iran_Pro_League" title="Iran Pro League">Iran Pro League</a>. He continued his work with Tarbiat Yazd the following season and led the team to a third-place finish but were shy one point of qualifying for a promotion play-off. At the end of the season, he resigned as head coach of Tarbiat Yazd in order to become head coach of another Azadegan league side, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nassaji_Mazandaran_F.C." title="Nassaji Mazandaran F.C.">Nassaji Mazandaran</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nassaji_Mazandaran">Nassaji Mazandaran</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahya_Golmohammadi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Nassaji Mazandaran">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He became head coach of his native town team, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nassaji_Mazandaran_F.C." title="Nassaji Mazandaran F.C.">Nassaji Mazandaran</a> in July 2010. He was also unable to promote Nassaji to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iran_Pro_League" title="Iran Pro League">Iran Pro League</a> as they finished at 4th place in the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Azadegan_League" title="2010â11 Azadegan League">2010â11</a> season. He was sacked by the club in September 2011.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rah_Ahan">Rah Ahan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahya_Golmohammadi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Rah Ahan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/Ali_Daei" title="Ali Daei">Ali Daei</a> became head coach of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rah_Ahan_F.C." title="Rah Ahan F.C.">Rah Ahan</a>, he was chosen by his former teammate as first team coach. Yahya worked with Daei for one season as his assistant along with <a href="/wiki/%C5%BDeljko_Mija%C4%8D" title="Å½eljko MijaÄ">Å½eljko MijaÄ</a> but separated from the Rah Ahan coaching staff at the end of the season in order to take over the reins of <a href="/wiki/Saba_Qom_F.C." title="Saba Qom F.C.">Saba Qom</a> for the second time.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Saba">Return to Saba</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahya_Golmohammadi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Return to Saba">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/Abdollah_Veisi" title="Abdollah Veisi">Abdollah Veisi</a> left the team for <a href="/wiki/Paykan_F.C." title="Paykan F.C.">Paykan</a>, Golmohammadi was named as head coach of Saba for a second time and returned to the club where he began his coaching career after four years. Golmohammadi signed a one-year contract with the club. His first match was a 4â0 win over <a href="/wiki/Paykan_F.C." title="Paykan F.C.">Paykan</a> which placed Saba at the top of the league. They stayed at the top the following week as well. They also defeated <a href="/wiki/Persepolis_F.C." title="Persepolis F.C.">Persepolis</a> 2â0 at the <a href="/wiki/Azadi_Stadium" title="Azadi Stadium">Azadi Stadium</a>. The day after, Saba defeated <a href="/wiki/Gahar_Zagros_F.C." title="Gahar Zagros F.C.">Gahar Zagros</a> 1â0. However, Golmohammadi was transferred to a hospital due to heart complications. He resigned on 24 September 2012 as head coach of Saba Qom due to health problems. His team was in 7th place before his departure.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Persepolis">Persepolis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahya_Golmohammadi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Persepolis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 25 September 2012, <a href="/wiki/Persepolis_F.C." title="Persepolis F.C.">Persepolis</a> officials announced that Golmohammadi would become the assistant coach of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manuel_Jos%C3%A9" title="Manuel JosÃ©">Manuel JosÃ©</a> instead of <a href="/wiki/Mohsen_Ashouri" title="Mohsen Ashouri">Mohsen Ashouri</a>. He officially began his work on 26 September. On 7 December 2012, Golmohammadi was promoted to the first team as the caretaker manager after Jose was banned from training camp by club president <a href="/wiki/Mohammad_Rouyanian" title="Mohammad Rouyanian">Mohammad Rouyanian</a>. Three days later, he was confirmed as the club's head coach and signed a contract with the club until the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He appointed <a href="/wiki/Vinko_Begovi%C4%87" title="Vinko BegoviÄ">Vinko BegoviÄ</a> and <a href="/wiki/Karim_Bagheri" title="Karim Bagheri">Karim Bagheri</a> as his new assistant managers at Persepolis.</p>
<p>His first game in charge of Persepolis came in a <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Hazfi_Cup" title="2012â13 Hazfi Cup">Hazfi Cup</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Malavan_F.C." title="Malavan F.C.">Malavan</a> which his team won 6â0 with a hat-trick from <a href="/wiki/Karim_Ansarifard" title="Karim Ansarifard">Karim Ansarifard</a>, two goals from <a href="/wiki/Hadi_Norouzi" title="Hadi Norouzi">Hadi Norouzi</a> and a final goal from <a href="/wiki/Gholamreza_Rezaei" title="Gholamreza Rezaei">Gholamreza Rezaei</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> His side won their other three matches in Hazfi Cup to qualified to the final, when they lose in penalties to <a href="/wiki/Sepahan_F.C." title="Sepahan F.C.">Sepahan</a>. His first game for Persepolis in <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Iran_Pro_League" title="2012â13 Iran Pro League">Iran Pro League</a> was ended 2â2 against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sanat_Naft_F.C." title="Sanat Naft F.C.">Sanat Naft</a> with two early goals from <a href="/wiki/Ali_Karimi" title="Ali Karimi">Ali Karimi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mohammad_Nouri_(footballer)" title="Mohammad Nouri (footballer)">Mohammad Nouri</a>. With a 2â0 victory against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fajr_Sepasi_F.C." title="Fajr Sepasi F.C.">Fajr Sepasi</a>, Golmohammadi presiding over his first league win as manager of Persepolis. He then led Persepolis to an eleventh undefeated streak until 1â0 lose to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rah_Ahan_F.C." title="Rah Ahan F.C.">Rah Ahan</a> on 10 March 2013. When he became head coach, Persepolis was in 12th place and he moved to sixth place in Iran Pro League table. Persepolis finished the season in the seventh place. He was offered by club officials a two years extension which was close to sign but after Hazfi Cup final, he refused to sign and his managerial rule at Persepolis was finished on 11 May 2013.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Naft_Tehran">Naft Tehran</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahya_Golmohammadi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Naft Tehran">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 20 May 2013, Yahya signed a one-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Naft_Tehran_F.C." title="Naft Tehran F.C.">Naft Tehran</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Mansour_Ebrahimzadeh" title="Mansour Ebrahimzadeh">Mansour Ebrahimzadeh</a>. His side began the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Iran_Pro_League" title="2013â14 Iran Pro League">season</a> with a 1â0 away win against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fajr_Sepasi_F.C." title="Fajr Sepasi F.C.">Fajr Sepasi</a>. Naft Tehran finished the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Iran_Pro_League" title="2013â14 Iran Pro League">2013â14 Iran Pro League</a> in 3rd place, qualified to the <a href="/wiki/2015_AFC_Champions_League" title="2015 AFC Champions League">AFC Champions League</a> play-offs that marked the club's best position in its history. Golmohammadi left Naft Tehran to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zob_Ahan_F.C." title="Zob Ahan F.C.">Zob Ahan</a> by mutual consent on 10 May 2014, after only one season in charge of the team.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Zob_Ahan">Zob Ahan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahya_Golmohammadi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Zob Ahan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 10 May 2014, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zob_Ahan_F.C." title="Zob Ahan F.C.">Zob Ahan</a> announced on their official website that Yahya Golmohammadi signed a two-year deal with the Isfahani outfit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Despite a disappointing start, Zob Ahan began their successful run from the January 2015. They finished the league in the 4th place, securing <a href="/wiki/AFC_Champions_League" title="AFC Champions League">AFC Champions League</a> spot. They also won <a href="/wiki/Hazfi_Cup" title="Hazfi Cup">Hazfi Cup</a> after defeating <a href="/wiki/Naft_Tehran_F.C." title="Naft Tehran F.C.">Naft Tehran</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2015_Hazfi_Cup_Final" title="2015 Hazfi Cup Final">final</a>. The title was the first major trophy that Golmohammadi won since he started his managerial career. Zob Ahan also won the title in next year for the second season in row, by defeating <a href="/wiki/Esteghlal_F.C." title="Esteghlal F.C.">Esteghlal</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2016_Hazfi_Cup_Final" title="2016 Hazfi Cup Final">final</a>.</p>
<p>On 24 September 2016, Golmohammadi parted away with Zob Ahan after a poor start of the season, which placed Zob Ahan in the 14th position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
