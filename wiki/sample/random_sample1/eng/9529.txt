<p><i><b>Yakgwa</b></i> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_traditional_dish" title="Korean traditional dish">Korean traditional dish</a>. It was originally considered as a <a href="/wiki/Dessert" title="Dessert">dessert</a> and more recently as a <a href="/wiki/Confectionery" title="Confectionery">confectionery</a> (<a href="/wiki/Hangwa" title="Hangwa">hangwa</a>), because of its sweet taste and flower biscuit shape. <i>Yakgwa</i> is made mainly from <a href="/wiki/Honey" title="Honey">honey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sesame_oil" title="Sesame oil">sesame oil</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wheat_flour" title="Wheat flour">wheat flour</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin">Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakgwa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The origin of <i>yakgwa</i> is not clear since this food had many different names. However, it seems like <a href="/wiki/Koreans" title="Koreans">Korean</a> people started to eat it during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unified_Silla" title="Unified Silla">Unified Shilla</a> era. During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goryeo_Dynasty" title="Goryeo Dynasty">Goryeo Dynasty</a>, <i>yakgwa</i> was known even in China under the name <a href="/wiki/Goryeo" title="Goryeo">Goryeo</a> <a href="/wiki/Mandu_(dumpling)" title="Mandu (dumpling)">mandu</a>. This is from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseon_Dynasty" title="Joseon Dynasty">Joseon Dynasty</a> era that <i>yakgwa</i> was being called <i>yakgwa</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It was an essential dessert in the Goryeo royal court.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Literally, <i>yakgwa</i> means the medicinal (<a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E8%97%A5" title="wikt:è¥">è¥</a>) confectionery (<a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E8%8F%93" title="wikt:è">è</a>). This name came from the main ingredients it is made of. In Korea, during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseon_Dynasty" title="Joseon Dynasty">Joseon Dynasty</a> era, <a href="/wiki/Honey" title="Honey">honey</a> was considered as a medicine good for health. That is why it was called the medicinal confectionery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ingredients">Ingredients</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakgwa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ingredients">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Yakgwa</i> is traditionally made of <a href="/wiki/Honey" title="Honey">honey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sesame_oil" title="Sesame oil">sesame oil</a>, and wheat flour. Recently, Yakgwa has been manufactured by corporations that add more ingredients in order to improve the taste of the biscuit.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yakgwa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Yakgwa gets it shape by being pressed into wooden molds or flattened on decorative tiles. Both the pistol and molds must be greased with sesame oil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
