<p><b>Jan Werich</b> (<small>Czech:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Czech" title="Help:IPA for Czech">[ˈjan ˈvɛrɪx]</a></span>; 6 February 1905 – 31 October 1980) was a <a href="/wiki/Czechs" title="Czechs">Czech</a> actor, playwright and writer.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Werich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Between 1916 and 1924, Werich attended "reálné gymnasium" (equivalent to high school) in Křemencová Street in Prague (where his future partner, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ji%C5%99%C3%AD_Voskovec" title="Jiří Voskovec">Jiří Voskovec</a>, also studied). He studied law at the Charles University Law School from 1924 to 1927, from which he made an early departure to begin his artistic career and forge one of the most important partnerships of his life.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Werich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Theater">Theater</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Werich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Theater">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His collaboration with Jiří Voskovec and <a href="/wiki/Jaroslav_Je%C5%BEek_(composer)" title="Jaroslav Ježek (composer)">Jaroslav Ježek</a> lasted for more than 10 years. Their partnership was a platform for their numerous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Left-wing" title="Left-wing">left-wing</a> political satires. The trio's work took inspiration from <a href="/wiki/Dada" title="Dada">Dada</a>, with its love of the absurd, a reaction against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bourgeois" title="Bourgeois">bourgeois</a> values and the horrors of <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>.</p>
<p>In the years leading up to <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> and the closure of Czechoslovak theatres, Werich, Voskovec and Ježek were forced into exile in the United States in 1938, where Voskovec and Ježek remained for the rest of their lives. Werich returned to his homeland five years later, and started a partnership with <a href="/wiki/Miroslav_Horn%C3%AD%C4%8Dek" title="Miroslav Horníček">Miroslav Horníček</a>. He also worked with famous puppeteer <a href="/wiki/Ji%C5%99%C3%AD_Trnka" title="Jiří Trnka">Jiří Trnka</a> to write modern fairy tales. With his new partner Miroslav Horníček at his side, he re-staged many of the plays he had created with Voskovec in the 1930s but set them in the political content specific to the time.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Werich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

Werich's screentest as Blofeld.</div>

<p>Jan Werich appeared in such films as <i>Byl jednou jeden král</i> (English: <i>Once Upon a Time There Was a King</i>), which was released in 1955, and made many political compromises to appease the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communist" title="Communist">Communist</a> censors. The 1960s were a peak in Werich's career. From the creation of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=ABC_Theatre&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="ABC Theatre (page does not exist)">ABC Theatre</a> he moved to the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=City_Theatres_of_Prague&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="City Theatres of Prague (page does not exist)">City Theatres of Prague</a> and then to the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Musical_Theatre_of_Karlin_and_Nusle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Musical Theatre of Karlin and Nusle (page does not exist)">Musical Theatre of Karlin and Nusle</a>.</p>
<p>Werich was originally cast by producer <a href="/wiki/Harry_Saltzman" title="Harry Saltzman">Harry Saltzman</a> to play <a href="/wiki/Ernst_Stavro_Blofeld" title="Ernst Stavro Blofeld">Ernst Stavro Blofeld</a> in the 1967 <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/You_Only_Live_Twice_(film)" title="You Only Live Twice (film)">You Only Live Twice</a></i>. Upon his arrival at the Pinewood set, both producer <a href="/wiki/Albert_R._Broccoli" title="Albert R. Broccoli">Albert R. Broccoli</a> and director <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Gilbert" title="Lewis Gilbert">Lewis Gilbert</a> felt that he was a poor choice, resembling a "poor, benevolent Santa Claus". Nonetheless, in an attempt to make the casting work, Gilbert continued filming. After several days, both Gilbert and Broccoli determined that Werich was not menacing enough, and recast Blofeld with <a href="/wiki/Donald_Pleasence" title="Donald Pleasence">Donald Pleasence</a> in the role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MGM_documentary_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-MGM_documentary-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Werich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jan Werich signed the pro-reform manifesto <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Words&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="2000 Words (page does not exist)">2000 Words</a></i> in 1968. After the <a href="/wiki/Prague_Spring" title="Prague Spring">Soviet invasion</a> of 1968, he and his wife immediately fled to <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> along with many other Czechs at the time. After long deliberations he realized he had to go back, saying that his "home was his castle" and realizing that if he stayed abroad he would never work again. He returned to Czechoslovakia in early 1969. Werich met with Voskovec for the very last time in Vienna, Austria in 1974. He had limited opportunities to perform in public until his death in 1980. His last public appearance was in Prague's Lucerna in 1977.</p>
<p>He did not sign the <a href="/wiki/Charter_77" title="Charter 77">Charter 77</a> proclamation, organized by <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A1clav_Havel" title="Václav Havel">Václav Havel</a>, but in the end he succumbed to pressure from the Communist authorities and in order to be able to work again, publicly proclaimed his full loyalty to the regime, by attending "Anti-charta77" meetings and even signing a petition denouncing Charter 77. He later claimed he was tricked into signing and that he did not really know what his signature was for, as the original attendance document signed open entry was potentially substituted for a later "declaration of faith".</p>

<li>Slaměný klobouk (The Straw Hat) - 1933; based upon Labiche's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Un_chapeau_de_paille_d%27Italie_(play)" title="Un chapeau de paille d'Italie (play)">The Italian Straw Hat</a></li>
<li>Kat a blázen (Executioner and Fool) - 1934; an anti-fascist work and critique (the German Embassy protested that this play insulted their head of state, and as a result, Werich and Voskovec were expelled from the theatre "U Nováků")</li>
<li>Vždy s úsměvem (Keep Smiling) - 1934</li>
<li>Panoptikum (The Wax Museum) - 1935</li>
<li>Balada z hadrů – (Rag Ballad) - 1935; draws from the works of <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Villon" title="François Villon">François Villon</a>.</li>
<li>Nebe na Zemi (Heaven on Earth) - 1936; based on Fletcher's <a href="/wiki/The_Spanish_Curate" title="The Spanish Curate">The Spanish Curate</a></li>
<li>Rub a líc (Heads or Tails) - 1936; later made into the <a href="/wiki/The_World_Is_Ours_(film)" title="The World Is Ours (film)">Svět patří nám</a> film</li>
<li>Panorama - 1937</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/T%C4%9B%C5%BEk%C3%A1_Barbora" title="Těžká Barbora">Těžká Barbora</a> – (Heavy Barbora) - 1937; depicts a conflict between two fictional nations, Eidamští ("<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edam_(cheese)" title="Edam (cheese)">Edams</a>") and Yberlandští ("<a href="/wiki/%C3%9Cber" title="Über">Yberlanders</a>"), the latter attacking the former, the former then employ two soldiers (Werich and Voskovec), that build a cheese canon (Těžká Barbora)</li>
<li>Pěst na oko – (A Fist in the Eye) - 1938; humans refuse to take a lesson from history and constantly repeat the same mistake</li>
<li>Divotvorný hrnec - (The Wondermaking Pot) - 1946; a musical comedy based on <a href="/wiki/Finian%27s_Rainbow" title="Finian's Rainbow">Finian's Rainbow</a> by 'Yip' Harburg and Fred Saidy</li>
</ul>
