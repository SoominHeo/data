<p><b>Jan Blokhuijsen</b> (born 1 April 1989) is Dutch long track <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Speed_skater" title="Speed skater">speed skater</a> who until 2013 skated for the commercial <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=TVM_ploeg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="TVM ploeg (page does not exist)">TVM ploeg</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://nl.wikipedia.org/wiki/TVM_(schaatsploeg)" title="nl:TVM (schaatsploeg)">nl</a>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He currently holds the <a href="/wiki/World_record_progression_team_pursuit_speed_skating_men" title="World record progression team pursuit speed skating men">world record</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Long_track_speed_skating#Team_pursuit" title="Long track speed skating">team pursuit</a> discipline along with teammates <a href="/wiki/Sven_Kramer" title="Sven Kramer">Sven Kramer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Koen_Verweij" title="Koen Verweij">Koen Verweij</a>. At the <a href="/wiki/2014_Winter_Olympics" title="2014 Winter Olympics">2014 Winter Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sochi" title="Sochi">Sochi</a> he won a silver medal in the <a href="/wiki/Speed_skating_at_the_2014_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_5000_metres" title="Speed skating at the 2014 Winter Olympics – Men's 5000 metres">5000 m</a> competition.</p>
<p>In 2007, he got his VWO degree at school. After a successful period in skating and <a href="/wiki/Inline_skating" title="Inline skating">inline skating</a> (in which he won several Dutch and European titles at the junior categories and a second place at the world championships in Korea) he decided to focus only on the speedskating. In February 2008, he won the Dutch Allround Speed Skating Championships junior title at the ice rink of Groningen. Later in the same month he became world champion at the junior world championships speedskating, where he finished in front of his teammates <a href="/wiki/Koen_Verweij" title="Koen Verweij">Koen Verweij</a> and <a href="/wiki/Berden_de_Vries" title="Berden de Vries">Berden de Vries</a>. Together with them he also won the world title at the team pursuit.</p>
<p>He is currently at the 8th position in the <a href="/wiki/Adelskalender_(skating)" title="Adelskalender (skating)">adelskalender</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
