<p><b>Yann Gérard M'Vila</b> (<small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[əm.vi.la]</a></span>; born 29 June 1990) is a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who currently plays for <a href="/wiki/FC_Rubin_Kazan" title="FC Rubin Kazan">FC Rubin Kazan</a>. He operates primarily as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Defensive_midfielder" title="Midfielder">defensive midfielder</a>, and is described by his former club as a player who possesses "excellent defensive abilities" and "impressive physical strength", but can also play as a box-to-box midfielder, due to his impressive work rates and stamina.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He is the younger brother of <a href="/wiki/Yohan_M%27Vila" title="Yohan M'Vila">Yohan M'Vila</a>.</p>
<p>M'Vila is a <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">French international</a> having played at all levels for which he was eligible. He has <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captained</a> several of his age groups including the <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-19_football_team" title="France national under-19 football team">under-19 team</a> that reached the semi-finals of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-19_Football_Championship" title="2009 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship">2009 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship</a>. M'Vila also played on France teams that participated in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-17_Football_Championship" title="2007 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship">2007 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2007_FIFA_U-17_World_Cup" title="2007 FIFA U-17 World Cup">2007 FIFA U-17 World Cup</a>. He is currently playing for the senior team. M'Vila made his senior debut on 11 August 2010 in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exhibition_match" title="Exhibition match">friendly match</a> against <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_football_team" title="Norway national football team">Norway</a>, and was part of the French squad which reached the quarter-finals of <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">UEFA Euro 2012</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yann_M%27Vila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yann_M%27Vila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>M'Vila began his football career at the age of six for ES Sains-Saint-Fussien. He spent three years at the club and, in July 1999, joined the biggest club in the region <a href="/wiki/Amiens_SC" title="Amiens SC">Amiens SC</a>. M'Vila joined the club as a youth player and practiced in the club's soccer school, which was established by his father, who had previously played on the club's reserve team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-papa_mvila_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-papa_mvila-3">[3]</a></sup> After spending five seasons at the club, M'Vila left the club after developing friction and ventured to Paris to join <a href="/wiki/FC_Mantes" title="FC Mantes">FC Mantes</a>. During his short stint at Mantes, M'Vila lived with his grandparents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-life_mvila_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-life_mvila-4">[4]</a></sup> M'Vila admitted that, during his time at Mantes, he had "forgot his dream" of becoming a professional footballer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-life_mvila_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-life_mvila-4">[4]</a></sup> The desire was brought back after he was discovered by a <a href="/wiki/Stade_Rennais_F.C." title="Stade Rennais F.C.">Stade Rennais</a> scout in October 2004. The following month, M'Vila departed Mantes to join the prestigious <a href="/wiki/Youth_system" title="Youth system">youth academy</a> of Rennes after signing an <i>aspirant</i> (youth) contract with the club.</p>
<p>Among M'Vila's teammates in the youth system included fellow prospects <a href="/wiki/Damien_Le_Tallec" title="Damien Le Tallec">Damien Le Tallec</a>, the younger brother of <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Le_Tallec" title="Anthony Le Tallec">Anthony Le Tallec</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yohann_Lasimant" title="Yohann Lasimant">Yohann Lasimant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Abdoul_Camara" title="Abdoul Camara">Abdoul Camara</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Yacine_Brahimi" title="Yacine Brahimi">Yacine Brahimi</a>. The combination of M'Vila, Brahimi, Camara, and Le Tallec, all four being members of the class of 1990, were particularly instrumental in their youth team's successes. With the under-16 team, the foursome won the Tournoi Carisport, a national tournament that regularly pits the top academies in France against each other. Two seasons later with the under-18 team, M'Vila won the under-18 league championship for the 2006–07 season. In 2008, the youth academy achieved its biggest honour after winning the <a href="/wiki/Coupe_Gambardella" title="Coupe Gambardella">Coupe Gambardella</a>. The title was Rennes' third Gambardella Cup and its first since 2003 when the likes of <a href="/wiki/Yoann_Gourcuff" title="Yoann Gourcuff">Yoann Gourcuff</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sylvain_Marveaux" title="Sylvain Marveaux">Sylvain Marveaux</a> were playing in the competition. In the final, Rennes faced <a href="/wiki/FC_Girondins_de_Bordeaux" title="FC Girondins de Bordeaux">Bordeaux</a> and M'Vila was tasked with the objective of containing fellow French starlet <a href="/wiki/Gr%C3%A9gory_Sertic" title="Grégory Sertic">Grégory Sertic</a>. He accomplished the task and also scored a goal on a left-footed strike from 25 metres (27 yd) out leading the team to a 3–0 victory.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rennes">Rennes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yann_M%27Vila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rennes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Yann_Mvila_2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="599" data-file-width="442" height="298" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Yann_Mvila_2.jpg/220px-Yann_Mvila_2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Yann_Mvila_2.jpg/330px-Yann_Mvila_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Yann_Mvila_2.jpg/440px-Yann_Mvila_2.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>Following the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ligue_1_2006%E2%80%9307" title="Ligue 1 2006–07">2006–07 season</a>, on 11 August 2007, M'Vila, alongside teammates Le Tallec and Camara, signed his first professional contract after agreeing to a three-year deal with Rennes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He spent the entire season playing on the club's reserve team in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Championnat_de_France_amateur" title="Championnat de France amateur">Championnat de France amateur</a> in the fourth division before being officially promoted to the senior squad and assigned the number 15 shirt for the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Ligue_1" title="2008–09 Ligue 1">2008–09 season</a>. Due to having minor surgery on his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toenail" title="Toenail">toenail</a> and his attitude being questioned by manager <a href="/wiki/Guy_Lacombe" title="Guy Lacombe">Guy Lacombe</a>, M'Vila missed significant practice time with the senior team and began the season with the club's Championnat de France amateur team for the second consecutive year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Despite Lacombe questioning the player's attitude, M'Vila has declared that he bears no ill feelings towards Lacombe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mvila_lequipe_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-mvila_lequipe-8">[8]</a></sup> He, subsequently, appeared in 20 matches with the reserve team and scored no goals as the team finished 1st among professional clubs in its group, thus qualifying for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Championnat_de_France_Amateurs_2008-09#Playoffs" title="Championnat de France Amateurs 2008-09">playoffs</a>, where the club lost to <a href="/wiki/Olympique_Lyonnais_Reserves_and_Academy" title="Olympique Lyonnais Reserves and Academy">Lyon</a> in the semi-finals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following a successful international campaign with the <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-19_football_team" title="France national under-19 football team">France under-19 team</a>, on 13 August 2009, new manager <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Antonetti" title="Frédéric Antonetti">Frédéric Antonetti</a> announced that M'Vila would be earning some significant playing time with the club for the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Ligue_1" title="2009–10 Ligue 1">2009–10 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Two days later, after appearing with the CFA team in the first week of the season, he was named to the 19-man squad to face <a href="/wiki/OGC_Nice" title="OGC Nice">Nice</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> M'Vila made his professional debut in that match appearing as a substitute in the 80th minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Due to the suspension of Japanese <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> <a href="/wiki/Junichi_Inamoto" title="Junichi Inamoto">Junichi Inamoto</a>, M'Vila earned his first start the following week against <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Marseille</a>. He played the entire match, which ended 1–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The following week against <a href="/wiki/RC_Lens" title="RC Lens">Lens</a>, M'Vila earned his first professional <a href="/wiki/Penalty_card" title="Penalty card">red card</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> After returning from suspension, M'Vila never lost his place within the team breaking the starting eleven in all the remaining league matches, save for one.</p>
<p>On 15 September 2009, M'Vila signed a contract extension with Rennes until June 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stade-rennais-online.com_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-stade-rennais-online.com-15">[15]</a></sup> On 27 August 2010, he agreed to another extension with the club. The new deal extends until 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stade-rennais-online.com_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-stade-rennais-online.com-15">[15]</a></sup> On 18 December, in a 1–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Valenciennes_FC" title="Valenciennes FC">Valenciennes</a>, M'Vila played the entire match completing 100 of his 112 attempted passes, the former statistical output being a league-high for the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Due to his performances domestically and internationally, M'Vila was linked to Spanish club <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a> and English club <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> during the fall season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In December 2010, the midfielder quelled the interest by declaring his commitment to Rennes and also jokingly stating that he would only depart Rennes after scoring his first goal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Coincidentally, he scored his first professional goal on 9 January 2011 in a 7–0 hammering of <a href="/wiki/Championnat_National" title="Championnat National">Championnat National</a> club <a href="/wiki/AS_Cannes" title="AS Cannes">Cannes</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Coupe_de_France" title="Coupe de France">Coupe de France</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> A week later, M'Vila scored his first career league goal in a 4–0 win over <a href="/wiki/AC_Arles-Avignon" title="AC Arles-Avignon">Arles-Avignon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On 20 February, M'Vila scored the opening goal for Rennes, converting a free kick, in a 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Toulouse_FC" title="Toulouse FC">Toulouse</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> M'Vila was rewarded for his performances during the season with a nomination for the UNFP Ligue 1 Young Player of the Year. The midfielder lost out on the award to national team teammate <a href="/wiki/Mamadou_Sakho" title="Mamadou Sakho">Mamadou Sakho</a>, but was given consolation with an appearance on the organization's Team of the Year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> A day later, Rennes confirmed on its website that M'Vila had signed a one-year contract extension with the club. The new deal will keep him with Rennes until 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>M'Vila opened the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_in_French_football" title="2011–12 in French football">2011–12 season</a> making his <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">European</a> debut in a <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2011–12 UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> third qualifying round first leg tie against <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgian</a> club <a href="/wiki/FC_Metalurgi_Rustavi" title="FC Metalurgi Rustavi">Metalurgi Rustavi</a> on 28 July 2011. M'Vila played the entire match in a 5–2 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> On 25 August, he scored his first and only goal of the campaign in a 4–0 second leg win over <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbian</a> club <a href="/wiki/Red_Star_Belgrade" title="Red Star Belgrade">Red Star Belgrade</a> in the Europa League playoff round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> On 15 September, M'Vila <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captained</a> Rennes for the first time in a 1–1 league draw with Nancy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> He finished the campaign appearing in a career-high 50 matches, which included all 38 league contests, as Rennes finished in sixth place. Despite consistently appearing with the team, as the season progressed, M'Vila was subjected to criticism from the club's supporters due to his under-performance as perceived by them. The criticism culminated on 11 April 2012 following the midfielder's performance in Rennes' shock 2–1 defeat to semi-professional club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Quevilly" title="US Quevilly">Quevilly</a> in the semi-finals of the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Coupe_de_France" title="2011–12 Coupe de France">Coupe de France</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> In the team's ensuing match against Nice, M'Vila was jeered by the club's support. The midfielder was defended by his coach Antonetti, who declared, "We demand a lot from M'Vila. But, we forget that he has already given so much to the club," while also joking that M'Vila "touched the ball so much that they [supporters] couldn't keep up. It made me smile a little."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rubin_Kazan">Rubin Kazan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yann_M%27Vila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Rubin Kazan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 22 January 2013, it was announced that Rennes had accepted an offer in the region of 12 million euros from <a href="/wiki/FC_Rubin_Kazan" title="FC Rubin Kazan">Rubin Kazan</a> for M'Vila.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>M'Vila went AWOL during the 2013–14 winter break, failing to join Rubin Kazan's training camp in Turkey and instead returning to France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> This caused a dispute with coach <a href="/wiki/Rinat_Bilyaletdinov" title="Rinat Bilyaletdinov">Rinat Bilyaletdinov</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Internazionale_.28loan.29">Internazionale (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yann_M%27Vila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Internazionale (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>On 15 July 2014, M'Vila joined <a href="/wiki/Inter_Milan" title="Inter Milan">Internazionale</a> on loan from Rubin Kazan until the end of the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Serie_A" title="2014–15 Serie A">2014–15 season</a> with an option to purchase the player on a permanent basis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> He made his <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> debut on 31 August as they began the season with a goalless draw at <a href="/wiki/Torino_F.C." title="Torino F.C.">Torino</a>; he was substituted after 56 minutes for fellow debutant <a href="/wiki/Dani_Osvaldo" title="Dani Osvaldo">Dani Osvaldo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> On January 25, it was announced that the loan had been terminated and therefore Yann M'vila returned to Kazan following a disagreement with new Inter manager <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Mancini" title="Roberto Mancini">Roberto Mancini</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibtimes.co.uk_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-ibtimes.co.uk-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>M'Vila spent a period training with <a href="/wiki/FC_Dynamo_Moscow" title="FC Dynamo Moscow">Dynamo Moscow</a>, but did not officially sign for the Russian club after a dispute resulting in the trashing of his Moscow home.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibtimes.co.uk_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-ibtimes.co.uk-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Sunderland_.28loan.29">Sunderland (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yann_M%27Vila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sunderland (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>On 6 August 2015, M'Vila joined <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a> on a season-long loan with a view to being made a permanent deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> He made his first appearance for the club in an under-21 match against <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City</a>, but was sent off after 69 minutes for a head-butt on <a href="/wiki/Jamar_Loza" title="Jamar Loza">Jamar Loza</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> On 29 August, M'Vila scored his first Sunderland goal, a long range free-kick against <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> On 25 October 2015, M'Vila was named the Premier League man of the match in Sunderland's 3-0 home victory over <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tyne-wear_derby" title="Tyne-wear derby">Tyne-wear derby</a>. He subsequently described the atmosphere of the match as the best he had played in, eclipsing that of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Milan_Derby" title="Milan Derby">Milan Derby</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> After another man of the match performance in a 3-1 defeat at Arsenal on 5 December 2015, M'Vila was praised by team-mate <a href="/wiki/Ola_Toivonen" title="Ola Toivonen">Ola Toivonen</a>, who described him as "a top European player. As he plays more games he’s going to show that he’s the top player we know he is.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2016, M'Vila expressed his desire to stay at Sunderland on a permanent basis, provided the Black Cats avoided relegation. He also hoped to reach an agreement with Rubin Kazan to terminate his contract, which runs until December 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>M'Vila finished the season with 37 league appearances for Sunderland, as they ultimately secured survival with a 3-0 home victory over Everton on 11 May 2016. Manager <a href="/wiki/Sam_Allardyce" title="Sam Allardyce">Sam Allardyce</a> confirmed both club and player were keen to make the move permanent. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 September Yann M'Vila took his frustrations to Instagram after the club rejected to sign the player by not picking up the phone. M'Vila was a fan favourite during his time in the North East which caused a massive angering response to Ellis Short the Sunderland owner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Rubin">Return to Rubin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yann_M%27Vila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Return to Rubin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>M'Vila played his first game for Rubin in 2 years upon the return from loans on 22 September 2016 when he came on as a substitute in a <a href="/wiki/Russian_Cup_(football)" title="Russian Cup (football)">Russian Cup</a> matchup against <a href="/wiki/FC_Chita" title="FC Chita">FC Chita</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Rubin's manager <a href="/wiki/Javi_Gracia" title="Javi Gracia">Javi Gracia</a> chose to play <a href="/wiki/Alex_Song" title="Alex Song">Alex Song</a> over M'Vila at the defensive midfield position in all the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Premier_League" title="Russian Premier League">league</a> games up to that point. He returned to league action for Rubin on 2 October 2016, when he was a late substitute in a game against <a href="/wiki/FC_Krasnodar" title="FC Krasnodar">FC Krasnodar</a>. In Rubin's next match against <a href="/wiki/FC_Krylia_Sovetov_Samara" title="FC Krylia Sovetov Samara">FC Krylia Sovetov Samara</a> on 15 October 2016, M'Vila returned to the starting line-up, pushing Song to the bench.</p>
<p>On 26 December 2016, he signed a new 3.5-year contract with Rubin that will run until the summer of 2020.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yann_M%27Vila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth">Youth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yann_M%27Vila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Youth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>M'Vila has featured for all of <a href="/wiki/France_national_youth_football_team" title="France national youth football team">France's national youth teams</a> for which he was eligible beginning with the <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-16_football_team" title="France national under-16 football team">under-16 team</a>. He made his international debut with the team on 23 May 2006 in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exhibition_match" title="Exhibition match">friendly match</a> against <a href="/wiki/Romania_national_football_team" title="Romania national football team">Romania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> M'Vila made his only other appearance in the team in the return leg against Romania. M'Vila began featuring as a regular international with the <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-17_football_team" title="France national under-17 football team">under-17 team</a> making his debut on 5 December 2006 in a friendly match against the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic_national_football_team" title="Czech Republic national football team">Czech Republic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> M'Vila scored his first international goal on 20 February 2007 against <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_under-17_football_team" title="Portugal national under-17 football team">Portugal</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Algarve_Cup" title="Algarve Cup">Algarve Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> He was a member of the team that reached the semi-finals at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-17_Football_Championship" title="2007 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship">2007 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship</a> where they lost to <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-17_football_team" title="England national under-17 football team">England</a>. In the competition, M'Vila scored a goal against group stage opponents <a href="/wiki/Ukraine_national_football_team" title="Ukraine national football team">Ukraine</a> in a 2–2 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> The draw resulted in France advancing to the <a href="/wiki/2007_FIFA_U-17_World_Cup" title="2007 FIFA U-17 World Cup">2007 FIFA U-17 World Cup</a> held in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> M'Vila appeared in all five matches helping the team reach the quarter-finals where they were defeated by <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-17_football_team" title="Spain national under-17 football team">Spain</a> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Penalty_shootout_(association_football)" title="Penalty shootout (association football)">penalties</a>.</p>
<p>With the <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-18_football_team" title="France national under-18 football team">under-18 team</a>, M'Vila appeared in six of the ten matches the team contested. He made his debut with the team in its opening match against the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_national_under-17_football_team" title="United States national under-17 football team">United States</a> in the Tournoi de Limoges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> M'Vila appeared in all 18 matches scoring 3 goals with the <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-19_football_team" title="France national under-19 football team">under-19 team</a>. He was given the honor of <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captaining</a> the team by coach <a href="/wiki/Jean_Gallice" title="Jean Gallice">Jean Gallice</a>. He scored one goal during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-19_Football_Championship_qualification" title="2009 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship qualification">qualification process</a> against <a href="/wiki/Romania_national_football_team" title="Romania national football team">Romania</a> in a 3–0 victory. The victory assured qualification to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-19_Football_Championship" title="2009 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship">the tournament</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> At the tournament, held in the <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, M'Vila appeared in all three group stage matches helping France reach the semi-finals. Due to picking up a <a href="/wiki/Penalty_card" title="Penalty card">yellow card</a> in back-to-back matches, M'Vila missed the semi-final defeat to <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-19_football_team" title="England national under-19 football team">England</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> Upon receiving the <a href="/wiki/Penalty_card" title="Penalty card">yellow card</a> in the final group stage match against <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-19_football_team" title="Spain national under-19 football team">Spain</a>, a very distraught M'Vila palmed his face into his knees as he knew he would miss the important semi-final match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>Just days after the under-19 elimination, M'Vila was called up to the <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-21_football_team" title="France national under-21 football team">under-21 team</a>, the only one of the under-19 squad, to participate in the team's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exhibition_match" title="Exhibition match">friendly match</a> against <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_under-21_football_team" title="Poland national under-21 football team">Poland</a> on 12 August.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> He made his under-21 debut in that match appearing as a substitute in the 74th minute of a 2–2 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> On 25 February 2010, M'Vila was reportedly named to the <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">senior team</a> for their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exhibition_match" title="Exhibition match">friendly</a> against <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a> on 3 March. The news was based on the preliminary list sent to news agencies minutes before coach <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Domenech" title="Raymond Domenech">Raymond Domenech</a> was to announce the squad in a press conference.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> However, following the formalization of the list at the start of the press conference, his name was replaced with <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Marseille</a> <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> <a href="/wiki/Beno%C3%AEt_Cheyrou" title="Benoît Cheyrou">Benoît Cheyrou</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> M'Vila was later called up to the under-21 team for their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exhibition_match" title="Exhibition match">friendly</a> against <a href="/wiki/Croatia_national_under-21_football_team" title="Croatia national under-21 football team">Croatia</a>, but withdrew from the team due to injury. On 11 May, M'Vila was named to the 30-man preliminary list by Domenech to play in the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> but failed to make the final 23.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Senior">Senior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yann_M%27Vila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Senior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 5 August 2010, M'Vila was officially called up to the <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">senior team</a> for the first time by new manager <a href="/wiki/Laurent_Blanc" title="Laurent Blanc">Laurent Blanc</a> for the team's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exhibition_match" title="Exhibition match">friendly match</a> against <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_football_team" title="Norway national football team">Norway</a> on 11 August 2010. He earned his first senior cap in the match starting in the defensive midfielder role. Despite France losing the match 2–1, M'Vila earned rave reviews for his performance, with many media outlets in France naming him the team's player of the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> Following his debut, M'Vila appeared in five consecutive matches for France. After starting in the team's <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2012 qualifying">UEFA Euro 2012 qualifying</a> victory over <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina_national_football_team" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina national football team">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a> in September 2010, he became the only player to feature as a starter in Blanc's first three matches as manager. On 25 March 2011, M'Vila completed 92.5 percent of his 134 attempted passes in a 2–0 UEFA Euro 2012 qualifying win over <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg_national_football_team" title="Luxembourg national football team">Luxembourg</a>. The feat was the best by any France player in an international match since 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> On 2 September 2011, M'Vila scored his first international goal in a 2–1 Euro qualifying victory over <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_football_team" title="Albania national football team">Albania</a>.</p>
<p>After appearing regularly in qualifying for UEFA Euro 2012, on 29 May 2012, M'Vila was named to the squad to participate in the competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> After missing the team's opening match against <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> due to an ankle injury, on 15 June, he made his debut at a senior international competition in the team's second group stage match against the <a href="/wiki/Ukraine_national_football_team" title="Ukraine national football team">Ukraine</a>. M'Vila appeared as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Alou_Diarra" title="Alou Diarra">Alou Diarra</a> in the second half as France won the match 2–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 8 November 2012, M'Vila was banned from national team matches until June 2014 after an unauthorised night out while on under-21 duty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yann_M%27Vila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>M'Vila primarily plays as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Defensive_midfielder" title="Midfielder">defensive midfielder</a> and is described as a player who "boasts a fantastic work-rate" and is "robust in the challenge".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mvila_espn_70-0"><a href="#cite_note-mvila_espn-70">[70]</a></sup> He is also known for his passing ability and "superb vision".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mvila_espn_70-1"><a href="#cite_note-mvila_espn-70">[70]</a></sup> M'Vila has earned critical acclaim for his playing style, which has led to coaches and players drawing comparisons to fellow <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">French internationals</a> <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Vieira" title="Patrick Vieira">Patrick Vieira</a> and <a href="/wiki/Claude_Mak%C3%A9l%C3%A9l%C3%A9" title="Claude Makélélé">Claude Makélélé</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mvila_espn_70-2"><a href="#cite_note-mvila_espn-70">[70]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mvila_fifa_71-0"><a href="#cite_note-mvila_fifa-71">[71]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> Under manager <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Antonetti" title="Frédéric Antonetti">Frédéric Antonetti</a>, despite playing as a defensive midfielder, M'Vila acts as the team's deep-lying play-maker and is often instrumental in building-up attacks from the back.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mvila_espn_70-3"><a href="#cite_note-mvila_espn-70">[70]</a></sup> His importance in the team was shown in Rennes' 1–0 league defeat to <a href="/wiki/Toulouse_FC" title="Toulouse FC">Toulouse</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Ligue_1" title="2011–12 Ligue 1">2011–12 season</a>, in which the <i>Bretagne</i> side failed to produce anything offensively. M'Vila, himself, declared that Rennes struggled because he was forced to play in a withdrawn role due to "the opponents being organized to close me down" and, as a result, he was "not in the drivers' seat".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> Though, he plays as a defensive midfielder, M'Vila can also play as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Box-to-box_midfielder" title="Midfielder">box-to-box midfielder</a> as he has done at times under <a href="/wiki/Laurent_Blanc" title="Laurent Blanc">Laurent Blanc</a> at international level.</p>
<p>M'Vila's passing ability and ability to retain possession consistently has been described as "precise" and "imaginative", respectively. His manager Antonetti has declared that M'Vila "reads the game like Makélélé, has the presence of Vieira and can pass the ball like <a href="/wiki/Yaya_Tour%C3%A9" title="Yaya Touré">Yaya Touré</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mvila_fifa_71-1"><a href="#cite_note-mvila_fifa-71">[71]</a></sup> In the past two Ligue 1 seasons (<a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Ligue_1" title="2010–11 Ligue 1">2010–11</a> and <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Ligue_1" title="2011–12 Ligue 1">2011–12</a>), M'Vila led the league each season in passes attempted and passes completed finishing the campaigns with a combined passing percentage of 84 percent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mvila_lfpstats_75-0"><a href="#cite_note-mvila_lfpstats-75">[75]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Ligue_1" title="2010–11 Ligue 1">2010–11 season</a>, he finished third behind attacking midfielders <a href="/wiki/Yohan_Cabaye" title="Yohan Cabaye">Yohan Cabaye</a> and <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Amalfitano" title="Morgan Amalfitano">Morgan Amalfitano</a> in balls played.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mvila_lfpstats_75-1"><a href="#cite_note-mvila_lfpstats-75">[75]</a></sup> Despite his position, M'Vila is also known for his disciplined style of play. So far in his career as a professional, in which he has accumulated over 120 appearances, M'Vila has collected only 16 yellow cards and suffered only one expulsion, which came in his third professional appearance; he was also expelled from the team's <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2011–12 UEFA Europa League">2011–12 UEFA Europa League</a> group stage match against <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scottish</a> club <a href="/wiki/Celtic_F.C." title="Celtic F.C.">Celtic</a> in November 2011, but this was after incurring a second bookable offence.</p>
<p>Despite the positive opinions, M'Vila has endured criticism, most notably from Rennes' former manager <a href="/wiki/Guy_Lacombe" title="Guy Lacombe">Guy Lacombe</a>, who viewed the player as a "loose cannon" while also questioning M'Vila's attitude and dedication.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> While M'Vila, initially, voiced his frustration at his lack of playing time under Lacombe, he has since stated he bears no ill feelings towards his former manager.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mvila_lequipe_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-mvila_lequipe-8">[8]</a></sup> After hearing M'Vila's initial frustrations in May 2010, Lacombe informed French publication <i><a href="/wiki/France_Football" title="France Football">France Football</a></i> that, "If he [M'Vila] had a black season, it's more his fault than that of the coaches," referring to M'Vila's 2008–09 season after signing his first professional contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yann_M%27Vila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>M'Vila was born in <a href="/wiki/Amiens" title="Amiens">Amiens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Somme_(department)" title="Somme (department)">Somme</a>. His father, Jean-Elvis M'Vila, was a former football player who is originally from the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a>. He moved to France in 1983 and played football for 13 years regularly turning out for the reserves of local club <a href="/wiki/Amiens_SC" title="Amiens SC">Amiens SC</a>. His father currently resides in <a href="/wiki/Cholet" title="Cholet">Cholet</a>. He was a police officer and now works in <a href="/wiki/Metallurgy" title="Metallurgy">metallurgy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-life_mvila_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-life_mvila-4">[4]</a></sup> Yann also has two younger sisters and an older brother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-papa_mvila_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-papa_mvila-3">[3]</a></sup> His brother, <a href="/wiki/Yohan_M%27Vila" title="Yohan M'Vila">Yohan M'Vila</a>, also plays professional football. Yann, himself, is married and has a son who was born when he was 18 years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-life_mvila_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-life_mvila-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2010, his wife gave birth to another child.</p>
