<p><b>Ján Kozák</b> (born 17 April 1954) is a former <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovak</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> and later <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manager_(football)" title="Manager (football)">football manager</a>. He is the current manager of the <a href="/wiki/Slovakia_national_football_team" title="Slovakia national football team">Slovakia national football team</a>, a position he assumed in July 2013. His son <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J%C3%A1n_Koz%C3%A1k_(footballer_born_1980)" title="Ján Kozák (footballer born 1980)">Ján Kozák</a> is also a footballer. As the current manager of Slovakia, his team qualified for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2016_UEFA_European_Football_Championship" title="2016 UEFA European Football Championship">2016 UEFA Euro</a>, thus becoming Slovakia's first time to appear in the tournament.</p>
<p>In 1981, Kozák became footballer of the year in Czechoslovakia. He played 254 matches in <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovak_First_League" title="Czechoslovak First League">Czechoslovak First League</a> and scored 57 goals. He also participated in <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1980" title="UEFA Euro 1980">UEFA Euro 1980</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1982_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1982 FIFA World Cup">1982 FIFA World Cup</a>.</p>
