<p><b>Jan Anna Gumaar Ceulemans</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[ˈjɑn ˈkøːləˌmɑns]</a></span>; born 28 February 1957 in <a href="/wiki/Lier,_Belgium" title="Lier, Belgium">Lier, Belgium</a>) is a former <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Attacking_midfielder" title="Attacking midfielder">attacking midfielder</a>. A prolific goalscorer Ceulemans was well known for his stamina, aerial ability and technique.</p>
<p>He is his country's most capped player with 96 international appearances. Most of his time with <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_football_team" title="Belgium national football team">Belgium</a> took place under the guidance of <a href="/wiki/Guy_Thys" title="Guy Thys">Guy Thys</a>. This period saw the Belgium squad record some of their finest results, which include reaching the final of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euro_80" title="Euro 80">Euro 80</a> and fourth place at the <a href="/wiki/1986_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1986 FIFA World Cup">1986 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ceulemans_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ceulemans-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Ceulemans&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ceulemans' first of three World Cup appearances was at the <a href="/wiki/1982_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1982 FIFA World Cup">1982 FIFA World Cup</a>, where Belgium beat the defending champions Argentina 1–0 in the opening game of the tournament at <a href="/wiki/Camp_Nou" title="Camp Nou">Camp Nou</a> en route to reaching the second round. Among one of Ceulemans' finest achievements was captaining his national side to fourth place in the <a href="/wiki/1986_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1986 FIFA World Cup">1986 FIFA World Cup</a>, scoring three goals in the tournament including a spectacular diving header against Spain in the quarter-finals. His performance earned the nickname "Captain Courageous".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He retired from international competition after the <a href="/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1990 FIFA World Cup">1990 FIFA World Cup</a>; Jan scored the 3rd goal in a 3–1 win over Uruguay but Belgium was eliminated by England in the round of sixteen, with the winning goal being scored by <a href="/wiki/David_Platt_(footballer)" title="David Platt (footballer)">David Platt</a> in the 119th minute in extra time. Jan had struck the post during the game.</p>
<p>Professionally, he stayed at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Brugge" title="Club Brugge">Club Brugge</a> for 13 years, endearing himself to his country when he turned down an offer from Italian giant <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">A.C. Milan</a>. He remains the only football player to have posed with fhe AC Milan board for the press that never actually became an AC Milan player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>After retiring as a player due to knee injury, he became a manager at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K.S.C._Eendracht_Aalst" title="K.S.C. Eendracht Aalst">KSC Eendracht Aalst</a> in 1992. He won promotion to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_First_Division" title="Belgian First Division">Belgian First Division</a> and even a qualification for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a>. He moved in 1998 to <a href="/wiki/K.V.C._Westerlo" title="K.V.C. Westerlo">K.V.C. Westerlo</a> where he also qualified for UEFA Cup. In 2005 he is back at 'his' <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Brugge" title="Club Brugge">Club Brugge</a> where he would be manager for 3 years but after several bad results he was fired in April 2006. For the 2007–2008 season, he returned to <a href="/wiki/K.V.C._Westerlo" title="K.V.C. Westerlo">KVC Westerlo</a>. He currently lives in <a href="/wiki/Kessel,_Belgium" title="Kessel, Belgium">Kessel</a>.</p>
<p>He was named by <a href="/wiki/Pel%C3%A9" title="Pelé">Pelé</a> as one of the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_100" title="FIFA 100">top 125 greatest living footballers</a> in March 2004.</p>
