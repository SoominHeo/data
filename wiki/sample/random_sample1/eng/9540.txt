<p><b>Johannes "Jan" Vennegoor of Hesselink</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[ˈjɑn ˈvɛ.nə.ˌɣoːr ɔf.ˈɦɛ.sə.lɪŋk]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:Nl-Jan_Vennegoor_of_Hesselink.ogg" title="File:Nl-Jan Vennegoor of Hesselink.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/58/Nl-Jan_Vennegoor_of_Hesselink.ogg" title="Nl-Jan Vennegoor of Hesselink.ogg">listen</a>)</small>; born 7 November 1978) is a Dutch former <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who last played for <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</a>. He formerly played for the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands national team</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>. He played for clubs such as the Dutch <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a>'s <a href="/wiki/FC_Twente" title="FC Twente">FC Twente</a> and <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Premier_League" title="Scottish Premier League">Scottish Premier League</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Celtic_F.C." title="Celtic F.C.">Celtic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a> of the English <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> and <a href="/wiki/SK_Rapid_Wien" title="SK Rapid Wien">Rapid Vienna</a> of <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Football_Bundesliga" title="Austrian Football Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Vennegoor_of_Hesselink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Twente_and_PSV">Twente and PSV</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Vennegoor_of_Hesselink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Twente and PSV">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Oldenzaal" title="Oldenzaal">Oldenzaal</a>, Vennegoor of Hesselink scored 59 league goals for <a href="/wiki/FC_Twente" title="FC Twente">FC Twente</a> over the course of five seasons. In 2001, he was signed by <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a>-rivals <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</a>. Vennegoor of Hesselink scored 22 times for PSV during his first season with the club. His second and third seasons at PSV proved less fruitful, with eight and twelve goals, respectively. At the end of the 2003–04 season, he was told he could leave the club, but stayed on and scored 19 goals in 28 matches during the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_in_Dutch_football" title="2004–05 in Dutch football">2004–05 season</a>. He also earned a recall to the national team in March 2005 after an absence of almost five years.</p>

<p>On 24 August 2006, Vennegoor of Hesselink left PSV and signed a three-year deal with <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Premier_League" title="Scottish Premier League">Scottish Premier League</a> champions <a href="/wiki/Celtic_F.C." title="Celtic F.C.">Celtic</a>, with the option of a further year, for a fee of around £3.4 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He made his debut on 26 August against <a href="/wiki/Hibernian_F.C." title="Hibernian F.C.">Hibernian</a> and scored the winning goal after coming on as a substitute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In his next game against <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen_F.C." title="Aberdeen F.C.">Aberdeen</a> on 9 September, he scored the winner from outside the box in a 1–0 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He then scored his first European goal for Celtic in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> four days later, scoring the opening goal as United won 3–2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Injuries subsequently curtailed his appearances but he found his form late on, scoring his first hat-trick for <i>the Bhoys</i> against <a href="/wiki/St_Mirren_F.C." title="St Mirren F.C.">St Mirren</a> on 20 January 2007<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and a last minute winner against <a href="/wiki/Inverness_Caledonian_Thistle_F.C." title="Inverness Caledonian Thistle F.C.">Inverness</a> the following week, before being <a href="/wiki/Penalty_card#Red_card" title="Penalty card">sent off</a> with a second <a href="/wiki/Penalty_card#Yellow_card" title="Penalty card">yellow card</a> after leaving the field of play to celebrate with the Celtic fans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He ended the season with 18 goals to his name and SPL and <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Cup" title="Scottish Cup">Scottish Cup</a> winners medals as well.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.9308">2007–08</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Vennegoor_of_Hesselink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2007–08">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Vennegoor of Hesselink started <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Scottish_Premier_League" title="2007–08 Scottish Premier League">the new season</a> well, scoring five goals in his first six league matches. After shaking off injury, he started to find some form and by January his performances improved immensely, delivering a Man of the Match performance and scoring against in a 5–1 thrashing of <a href="/wiki/Kilmarnock_F.C." title="Kilmarnock F.C.">Kilmarnock</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Cup" title="Scottish Cup">Scottish Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He followed that up with a goal against <a href="/wiki/Heart_of_Midlothian_F.C." title="Heart of Midlothian F.C.">Hearts</a> on 16 February and then a stunning diving header against <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a> four days later to give Celtic a 1–0 lead in the Champions League Round of 16 first-leg at <a href="/wiki/Celtic_Park" title="Celtic Park">Celtic Park</a>, although Barcelona won the match 3–2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On 16 April, Vennegoor of Hesselink scored his first <a href="/wiki/Old_Firm" title="Old Firm">Old Firm</a> goal against <a href="/wiki/Rangers_F.C." title="Rangers F.C.">Rangers</a> at Celtic Park in injury-time to give Celtic a 2–1 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Many saw that goal as the major turning point in Celtic's incredible comeback to win the league. On 22 May, the last day of the season, he scored what proved to be the league-winning goal with a powerful header off a <a href="/wiki/Corner_kick" title="Corner kick">corner kick</a> against <a href="/wiki/Dundee_United_F.C." title="Dundee United F.C.">Dundee United</a> at <a href="/wiki/Tannadice_Park" title="Tannadice Park">Tannadice Park</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> It was Vennegoor of Hesselink's 20th goal of the season and rounded off another successful year for both player and club, as the big striker and teammate <a href="/wiki/Scott_McDonald" title="Scott McDonald">Scott McDonald</a> struck up an extremely productive partnership, leading most strike pairings in the UK for almost the entire season, a total of 40 scored by the pair combined in the league alone and 51 in total.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2008.E2.80.9309">2008–09</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Vennegoor_of_Hesselink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2008–09">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The 2008–09 season started slowly for Vennegoor of Hesselink. On 31 August, just three minutes after coming on as a substitute, he was sent off against <a href="/wiki/Rangers_F.C." title="Rangers F.C.">Rangers</a> at Celtic Park for kicking out at Rangers' <a href="/wiki/Kirk_Broadfoot" title="Kirk Broadfoot">Kirk Broadfoot</a>. Rangers won the match 4–2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He scored his first goals of the season against Aberdeen in a 3–2 win at Celtic Park on 27 September, with his second being a last-minute winner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Vennegoor of Hesselink then went on a long goal drought (mainly due to the fact that he was out injured for over three months) until 4 April 2009, when he scored the final goal of Celtic's 4–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Hamilton_Academical_F.C." title="Hamilton Academical F.C.">Hamilton Academical</a> with a simple tap-in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He scored again four days later in another 4–0 victory with another simple tap-in, this time the second against <a href="/wiki/Falkirk_F.C." title="Falkirk F.C.">Falkirk</a>. The big striker made it three goals in three games on 11 April when he scored the opening goal against <a href="/wiki/Heart_of_Midlothian_F.C." title="Heart of Midlothian F.C.">Hearts</a> at <a href="/wiki/Tynecastle_Stadium" title="Tynecastle Stadium">Tynecastle</a> with less than 25 seconds on the clock. The game ended 1–1 with <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Aguiar" title="Bruno Aguiar">Bruno Aguiar</a> scoring the equaliser for Hearts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> It was then four in four when he scored a 43rd-minute opener in a 2–0 win at home to Aberdeen on 18 April.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He ended the season with a <a href="/wiki/Scottish_League_Cup" title="Scottish League Cup">League Cup</a> winners' medal, after Celtic beat Rangers 2–0 in <a href="/wiki/2009_Scottish_League_Cup_Final" title="2009 Scottish League Cup Final">the final</a> on 15 March.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Although Celtic did not lose any games in which Vennegoor of Hesselink scored in the 2008–09 season, he was released at the end of his contract in July 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hull_City">Hull City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Vennegoor_of_Hesselink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Hull City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 3 September 2009, Vennegoor of Hesselink signed a two-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He made his debut for the club against <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a> on 12 September when he came on as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Craig_Fagan" title="Craig Fagan">Craig Fagan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sunderland_4_-_1_Hull_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sunderland_4_-_1_Hull-20">[20]</a></sup> He scored his first goal for the club with the opening goal in the 2–1 home win against <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a> on 3 October 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hull_2_-_1_Wigan_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hull_2_-_1_Wigan-21">[21]</a></sup> He also gained another winner with a last minute strike against <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke</a> after coming on as a substitute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hull_2_-_1_Stoke_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hull_2_-_1_Stoke-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>His contract with Hull City expired at the end of the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_in_English_football" title="2009–10 in English football">2009–10 season</a>, and as of 1 July 2010 was confirmed as a free agent by officials at Hull City, along with fellow former Dutch national team player, <a href="/wiki/George_Boateng" title="George Boateng">George Boateng</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wage_Bill_Tumbles_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wage_Bill_Tumbles-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rapid_Vienna">Rapid Vienna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Vennegoor_of_Hesselink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Rapid Vienna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 30 August 2010, Vennegoor of Hesselink joined <a href="/wiki/SK_Rapid_Wien" title="SK Rapid Wien">Rapid Vienna</a> as a free agent on a two-year deal after his contract expired with Hull City. Vennegoor of Hesselink said on the club's official website "I received several interesting offers from European clubs. However, Rapid's offer gave me and my family the best feeling. This club and this city made it an easy decision for me to join Rapid. I'm looking forward to the next chapter in my career".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 21 October 2010, he scored the first goal to help his side to a 2–0 away win against <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgarian</a> outfit <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CSKA_Sofia" title="CSKA Sofia">CSKA Sofia</a> in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> match, snapping a two-game losing streak for the team from <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> in the tournament in question.</p>
<p>Vennegoor of Hesselink did not play many games in the 2010–11 season, due to several injuries. After a muscle rupture in May, his season was therefore over. In June, Vennegoor of Hesselink and <a href="/wiki/SK_Rapid_Wien" title="SK Rapid Wien">Rapid Vienna</a> mutually agreed to terminate his contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_PSV">Return to PSV</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Vennegoor_of_Hesselink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Return to PSV">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After his departure from <a href="/wiki/SK_Rapid_Wien" title="SK Rapid Wien">Rapid Vienna</a>, Vennegoor of Hesselink trained with the <a href="/wiki/FC_Twente" title="FC Twente">FC Twente</a> youth team. On 4 October 2011, it was announced that Vennegoor of Hesselink had joined <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</a> to train with the first team squad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Early November, it was rumoured that Vennegoor of Hesselink would return to the PSV squad on a second spell.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> On 3 November 2011, it was confirmed that he had signed with <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</a> until the end of the season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Vennegoor_of_Hesselink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 15 May 2012, Vennegoor of Hesselink announced his retirement from professional football.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Vennegoor_of_Hesselink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Vennegoor of Hesselink was a squad regular for the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a>. He made his international debut on 11 October 2000 in <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a> in the Netherlands' 2–0 World Cup qualifier defeat against <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a>, but did not score his first international goal until nearly seven years later, in a friendly match against <a href="/wiki/Thailand_national_football_team" title="Thailand national football team">Thailand</a> on 6 June 2007. Despite his fabulous form in his first season at <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</a>, Vennegoor of Hesselink missed <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2004" title="UEFA Euro 2004">UEFA Euro 2004</a>, but was included in the Dutch squads to play at the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 World Cup</a>, where he made a late appearance as a substitute in the second-round game against <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a>, and at <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008" title="UEFA Euro 2008">UEFA Euro 2008</a>, where he appeared against <a href="/wiki/Romania_national_football_team" title="Romania national football team">Romania</a>. He was not, however, called up for the <i>Oranje'</i>s <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 World Cup</a> campaign, where the Netherlands finished runners-up to <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Vennegoor_of_Hesselink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>British newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> researched his unusual-sounding name and found that he was thus named because:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>...way back in the 17th century, two farming families in the Enschede area of Holland intermarried. Both the Vennegoor and Hesselink names carried equal social weight, and so – rather than choose between them – they chose to use both. 'Of' in Dutch actually translates to 'or', which would mean that a strict translation of his name would read Jan Vennegoor or Hesselink.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
