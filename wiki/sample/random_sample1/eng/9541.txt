<p><b>Yandex N.V.</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/j/ 'y' in 'yes'">j</span><span title="/ʌ/ short 'u' in 'bud'">ʌ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/ɛ/ short 'e' in 'bed'">ɛ</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Яндекс</span>) is a Russian <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> <a href="/wiki/Technology_company" title="Technology company">technology company</a> specializing in <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>-related services and products. Yandex operates the largest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Search_engine" title="Search engine">search engine</a> in Russia with about 60% market share in that country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marketshare_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-marketshare-5">[5]</a></sup> It also develops a number of Internet-based services and products. Yandex ranked as the 4th largest search engine worldwide, based on information from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comscore.com" title="Comscore.com">Comscore.com</a>, with more than 150 million searches per day as of April 2012, and more than 50.5 million visitors (all company's services) daily as of February 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The company's mission is to provide answers to any questions users have or think about (explicit or implicit).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Yandex also has a very large presence in <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>, providing nearly a third of all search results in those markets and 43% of all search results in <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Yandex.ru home page has been rated the most popular website in Russia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The web site also operates in Belarus, Kazakhstan, Ukraine and <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yandex_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-yandex-10">[10]</a></sup> Yandex Labs is a wholly owned division of Yandex located in the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay_Area" title="San Francisco Bay Area">San Francisco Bay Area</a>. In 2014, Yandex announced plans to open a <a href="/wiki/Research_and_development" title="Research and development">research and development</a> office in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters_2014-02-06_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters_2014-02-06-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>It opened the first sales office outside the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CIS_countries" title="CIS countries">CIS countries</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lucerne" title="Lucerne">Lucerne</a>, Switzerland in 2012 for its European advertising clients,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and the second one in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, China in 2015 for Chinese companies that work on the Russian language market.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Market_share">Market share</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yandex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Market share">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to research studies conducted by <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Nelson_Sofres" title="Taylor Nelson Sofres">TNS</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VTSIOM#History" title="VTSIOM">FOM</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Comcon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Comcon (page does not exist)">Comcon</a>, Yandex<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> is the largest resource and largest search engine in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internet_in_Russian_language" title="Internet in Russian language">Russian Internet</a> market, based on audience reach. Yandex currently has a market share of over 55%<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marketshare_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-marketshare-5">[5]</a></sup> in Russia's search engine market by traffic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yandex' main competitors on the Russian market are <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mail.ru" title="Mail.ru">Mail.ru</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rambler_(portal)" title="Rambler (portal)">Rambler</a>. Yandex is therefore one of the national non-English-language search engines (with, among others, <a href="/wiki/Naver" title="Naver">Naver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seznam.cz" title="Seznam.cz">Seznam.cz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Baidu" title="Baidu">Baidu</a>) that pose as significant competitors for Google in their respective countries.</p>
<p>According to Yandex marketing, one of its biggest advantages for Russian-language users is the ability to recognize Russian <a href="/wiki/Inflection" title="Inflection">inflection</a> in search queries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yandex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yandex's roots trace back to 1990, when <a href="/wiki/Arkady_Volozh" title="Arkady Volozh">Arkady Volozh</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Arkady_Borkovsky&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Arkady Borkovsky (page does not exist)">Arkady Borkovsky</a> founded the company Arkadia, which developed MS-DOS software for use in patents and goods classification. Their software featured a full-text search with Russian <a href="/wiki/Morphology_(linguistics)" title="Morphology (linguistics)">morphology</a> support. In 1993 Arkadia became a subdivision of Comptek International, another company founded by Volozh in 1989. In 1993–1996 the company continued developing its search technologies and released software for searching through the Bible and Russian classical literature.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1993 <a href="/wiki/Arkady_Volozh" title="Arkady Volozh">Arkady Volozh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ilya_Segalovich" title="Ilya Segalovich">Ilya Segalovich</a>, friends since their school days and by then working together to develop search software,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> invented the word "Yandex" to describe their search technologies. The name initially stood for "Yet Another iNDEXer".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yandexcomhistory_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-yandexcomhistory-19">[19]</a></sup> The Russian word "Я" ("Ya") corresponds to the English <a href="/wiki/Personal_pronoun" title="Personal pronoun">personal pronoun</a> "I", making "Яndex" a <a href="/wiki/Bilingual_pun" title="Bilingual pun">bilingual pun</a> on "index". Another pun is based on the <i><a href="/wiki/Yin_and_yang" title="Yin and yang">yin and yang</a></i> contrast (Russian: инь – индекс, ян – яндекс).</p>
<p>The search engine yandex.ru was launched on September 23, 1997 and was publicly presented at the Softool exhibition in Moscow. Initially the search engine was developed by Comptek. In 2000 Yandex was incorporated as a standalone company by Arkady Volozh.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yandexcomhistory_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-yandexcomhistory-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yandex's revenue comes primarily from online advertisement. In 1998 Yandex launched <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Contextual_advertisement" title="Contextual advertisement">contextual advertisement</a> on its search engine. In 2001 it launched the <a href="/wiki/Yandex.Direct" title="Yandex.Direct">Yandex.Direct</a> advertisement network.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yandexcomhistory_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-yandexcomhistory-19">[19]</a></sup> Yandex LLC became profitable in November 2002. In 2004, Yandex sales increased to $17M, which was 10 times greater than the company's revenue just two years earlier. The net income of the company in 2004 constituted $7M. In June 2006, the weekly revenue of <a href="/wiki/Yandex.Direct" title="Yandex.Direct">Yandex.Direct</a> context ads system exceeded $1M. All of Yandex's accounting measures have been audited by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deloitte_Touche_Tohmatsu" title="Deloitte Touche Tohmatsu">Deloitte Touche Tohmatsu</a> since 1999.</p>
<p>In September 2005 Yandex opened an office in Ukraine<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and presented the Ukrainian portal, www.yandex.ua.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In 2008 Yandex extended its presence in Ukraine by increasing bandwidth between Moscow datacenters and UA-IX in Ukraine five times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In 2007 Yandex introduced a customized search engine for Ukrainian users;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Yandex also opened its development center in Kiev in May 2007. In 2009, all services of www.yandex.ua were localized for the Ukrainian market.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In 2010, Yandex launched its "Poltava" search engine algorithm for Ukrainian users, based on Yandex's <a href="/wiki/MatrixNet" title="MatrixNet">MatrixNet</a> technology and ranking local resources higher for location-based queries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2007 Yandex acquired moikrug.ru,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> a Russian social network, to search and support professional and personal contacts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In June 2008 Yandex acquired SMI Link, a Russian road traffic monitoring agency, to merge it with Yandex.Maps services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2008 Yandex further acquired the rights to the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Punto_Switcher&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Punto Switcher (page does not exist)">Punto Switcher</a> software program, an automatic Russian to English keyboard layout switcher.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yandex also founded Yandex Labs in 2008. The main objective of the company, located in the San Francisco Bay area, is to foster "innovation in search and advertising technology".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2009 Yandex introduced a player of free legal music in its search results. A little over a year later Yandex launched the Yandex.Music service and significantly extended its music catalogue to 800,000 tracks from 58,000 performers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mozilla_Firefox_3.5" title="Mozilla Firefox 3.5">Mozilla Firefox 3.5</a>, and subsequent versions, Yandex is the default search provider for Russian-language builds rather than the previous default (<a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yandexfox_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-yandexfox-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 19, 2010, Yandex launched an English-only web search engine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010 Yandex launched the Yandex.Start program to find startups and to work with them systematically. As a result of the program, Yandex purchased WebVisor's behavior analysis technology in December 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> In January 2011 the next startup, single sign-in service Loginza, was acquired by Yandex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2011 Yandex introduced premium placement opportunity in its Business directory; advertisers' local small businesses will be highlighted on a map for relevant queries. It was announced that the potential audience of the product includes over 25 million users of Yandex's search engine and over 11.5 million of Yandex.Maps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>In spring 2011 Yandex raised $1.3 billion in an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> on <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a>. It was the biggest U.S. IPO for a <a href="/wiki/Dot-com_company" title="Dot-com company">dotcom</a> since <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Google_Inc." title="Google Inc.">Google Inc.</a> went public in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Among the largest investors in Yandex were <a href="/wiki/Baring_Vostok_Capital_Partners" title="Baring Vostok Capital Partners">Baring Vostok Capital Partners</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Management" title="Tiger Management">Tiger Global Management</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2011 Yandex acquired <i>The Tweeted Times</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> a news delivery startup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2011 Yandex launched a search engine and a range of other services in Turkey, at yandex.com.tr. The company also opened an office in Istanbul.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yandex_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-yandex-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2011 Yandex acquired developer <a href="/wiki/SPB_Software" title="SPB Software">SPB Software</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2012 Yandex acquired share in Seismotech, Ltd. that provides services in the area of interpretative processing of seismic data and software development (Prime package), and provided its equipment and distributed computing technologies for processing of geological-geophysical data.</p>
<p>In March 2013 it was revealed that a motion movie production about the history of Yandex is in the works.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> In July 2013, <a href="/wiki/Mail.Ru" title="Mail.Ru">Mail.Ru</a> started placing Yandex.Direct ads on its search result pages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> October 2013 Yandex acquired <a href="/wiki/KinoPoisk" title="KinoPoisk">KinoPoisk</a>, the biggest Russian movie search engine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> Also in 2013, Yandex became the largest media property in Russia by generated revenue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2014 Yandex acquired Israeli <a href="/wiki/Geolocation" title="Geolocation">geolocation</a> startup <a href="/wiki/KitLocate" title="KitLocate">KitLocate</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2014 Yandex acquired the online auto classifieds portal Auto.ru<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2015 Yandex acquired the <a href="/wiki/Internet_security" title="Internet security">internet security</a> company <a href="/wiki/Agnitum" title="Agnitum">Agnitum</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
