<p><b>Yancy Victoria Butler</b> (born July 2, 1970) is an American actress. She is known for her roles as Natasha Binder in the 1993 film <i><a href="/wiki/Hard_Target" title="Hard Target">Hard Target</a></i>, Jess Crossman in <i><a href="/wiki/Drop_Zone_(film)" title="Drop Zone (film)">Drop Zone</a></i>, and Detective <a href="/wiki/Sara_Pezzini" title="Sara Pezzini">Sara Pezzini</a> on the <a href="/wiki/TNT_(TV_channel)" title="TNT (TV channel)">TNT</a> supernatural drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Witchblade_(TV_series)" title="Witchblade (TV series)">Witchblade</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yancy_Butler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Butler was born in <a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Village" title="Greenwich Village">Greenwich Village</a> in Manhattan, the daughter of Leslie Vega, a theatre company manager, and <a href="/wiki/Joe_Butler" title="Joe Butler">Joe Butler</a>, the drummer and vocalist for the 1960s rock group <a href="/wiki/The_Lovin%27_Spoonful" title="The Lovin' Spoonful">The Lovin' Spoonful</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sno_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-sno-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yancy_Butler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Butler's first major role was in the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Mann_%26_Machine" title="Mann &amp; Machine">Mann &amp; Machine</a></i> in 1992, in which she played an android police officer partnered with a human detective. The series was set in Los Angeles in the near future. The series was co-created by <a href="/wiki/Dick_Wolf" title="Dick Wolf">Dick Wolf</a>. A year later, she starred in her second series, <i><a href="/wiki/South_Beach_(1993_TV_series)" title="South Beach (1993 TV series)">South Beach</a></i>, in which she played a con artist and thief who made a deal with the federal government; in exchange for their wiping of her criminal record, she performs certain tasks for them.</p>
<p>Butler's first film appearance was a small supporting role in the 1979 slasher film <i><a href="/wiki/Savage_Weekend" title="Savage Weekend">Savage Weekend</a></i>. Her next film appearance was a starring role in <a href="/wiki/John_Woo" title="John Woo">John Woo</a>'s 1993 film, <i><a href="/wiki/Hard_Target" title="Hard Target">Hard Target</a></i>. She then starred in the 1994 film <i><a href="/wiki/Drop_Zone_(film)" title="Drop Zone (film)">Drop Zone</a></i>. In 1995, she starred as Corinne the dancer in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Let_It_Be_Me_(film)" title="Let It Be Me (film)">Let It Be Me</a></i>. Butler was cast as a series regular in the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_South" title="Brooklyn South">Brooklyn South</a></i> playing Anne-Marie Kersey, a Brooklyn policewoman, which ran for one season from 1997 to 1998.</p>
<p>Butler starred in the television film <i><a href="/wiki/Witchblade_(2000_film)" title="Witchblade (2000 film)">Witchblade</a></i> (2000) and the <a href="/wiki/Witchblade_(TV_series)" title="Witchblade (TV series)">subsequent television series</a>, adapted from the ongoing comic book <a href="/wiki/Witchblade" title="Witchblade">of the same title</a>; the series ran for two seasons on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turner_Network_Television" title="Turner Network Television">Turner Network Television</a> network for a total of 23 episodes. The series has a cult following, and was ranked seventh in the Top 10 Basic Cable Dramas for 2002. The show's cancellation was announced after the second season in September 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EOnline-WitchbladeCancelled_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-EOnline-WitchbladeCancelled-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2005, Yancy starred in the film <i>Bloodlines</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2006, she starred in the film <i>Striking Range</i>. In 2007, she appeared in 13 episodes of the television soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/As_the_World_Turns" title="As the World Turns">As the World Turns</a></i>, as Ava Jenkins.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> She was confirmed to appear in <i><a href="/wiki/Tales_of_an_Ancient_Empire" title="Tales of an Ancient Empire">Tales of an Ancient Empire</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> the follow-up to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sword_and_the_Sorcerer" title="The Sword and the Sorcerer">The Sword and the Sorcerer</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On January 24, 2012, <i>Tales of an Ancient Empire</i> was released on DVD. Butler, however, did not appear in the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She appeared in a small role in another comic-book adaptation, the <a href="/wiki/Kick-Ass_(film)" title="Kick-Ass (film)">film version</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Kick-Ass_(comic_book)" title="Kick-Ass (comic book)">Kick-Ass</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yancy_Butler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Substance_abuse_and_legal_issues">Substance abuse and legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yancy_Butler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Substance abuse and legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 2, 2003, Butler was arrested after getting into a fight with her father, Joe Butler, at her uncle's Long Island home. She was charged with criminal contempt for violating an order of protection and two counts of harassment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> She subsequently completed a stint in rehab and attended counseling sessions. L Fourteen months after the cancellation of the <i>Witchblade</i> series, in November 2003, Butler was taken into custody in Delray Beach, Florida, for disorderly intoxication and sentenced to a substance-abuse treatment program after police there found her stumbling in and out of traffic. Butler told police that she was an alcoholic who was on medication, according to a Delray Beach Police report. At first, she said she'd been kicked out of a halfway house, but later said she was still in one, the report said.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EOnline-ButlerRehab_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-EOnline-ButlerRehab-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 13, 2007, Butler was charged with driving under the influence and failure to drive in the established lane after she crashed her Saab 900 into a wire-guard rail in Sharon, Connecticut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The actress was released on a $500 bail and ordered to appear in Bantam, Connecticut Superior Court on March 26, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Actress_Yancy_Butler_Arrested_for_DUI_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Actress_Yancy_Butler_Arrested_for_DUI-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Actress_finds_legal_trouble_of_her_own_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Actress_finds_legal_trouble_of_her_own-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SHARON:_Actress_charged_in_domestic_violence_case_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-SHARON:_Actress_charged_in_domestic_violence_case-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 28, Bantam Superior Court Judge Richard Marano issued a rearrest order for Butler because she had twice not appeared at scheduled court dates. The judge directed that the warrant not be served until April 2 to give Butler a chance to explain why she was not in court.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A_no-show_in_court.2C_.27Witchblade.27_star_facing_another_arrest_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-A_no-show_in_court.2C_.27Witchblade.27_star_facing_another_arrest-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Two days prior to the DUI offense, Butler allegedly phoned 911 after a fight with her boyfriend, 53-year-old Earl Ward Jr. Responding officers reported witnessing a drunk Butler complaining about Ward, who refused to hand over her car keys. The argument resulted in her throwing several items at him. Ward later told police that Butler had been smoking crack cocaine and drinking liquor over the course of the weekend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stalking_complaint">Stalking complaint</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yancy_Butler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Stalking complaint">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On February 2, 2007, a former boyfriend of Butler's was arraigned in Litchfield, Connecticut on charges stemming from a stalking complaint she had filed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On September 27, 2007, the former boyfriend pleaded no contest in Bantam Superior Court to one count of creating a public disturbance and was ordered to pay a $50 fine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
