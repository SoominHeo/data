<p><b>Yangon Region</b> (<a href="/wiki/Burmese_language" title="Burmese language">Burmese</a>: <span style="font-family:'Myanmar Text','Noto Sans Myanmar', Myanmar3, Myanmar2, Myanmar1, Padauk, mm3web, TharLon, 'Masterpiece Uni Sans', Parabaik, Yunghkio, Thanlwin, 'Win Uni Innwa', 'MyMyanmar Unicode', 'WinUni Innwa';">ရန်ကုန်တိုင်းဒေသကြီး</span>; <a href="/wiki/MLC_Transcription_System" title="MLC Transcription System">MLCTS</a>: <i><span title="Transcription in Myanmar Language Commission Transcription System">rankun tuing desa. kri</span></i>, <small>pronounced: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Burmese" title="Help:IPA for Burmese">[jàɴɡòʊɴ táɪɴ dèθa̰ dʑí]</a></span>; formerly <b>Rangoon Division</b> and <b>Yangon Division</b>) is an administrative region of <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">Myanmar</a>. Located in the heart of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lower_Myanmar" title="Lower Myanmar">Lower Myanmar</a>, the division is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Bago_Region" title="Bago Region">Bago Region</a> to the north and east, the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Martaban" title="Gulf of Martaban">Gulf of Martaban</a> to the south, and <a href="/wiki/Ayeyarwady_Region" title="Ayeyarwady Region">Ayeyarwady Region</a> to the west. Yangon Region is dominated by its capital city of <a href="/wiki/Yangon" title="Yangon">Yangon</a>, the former national capital and the largest city in the country. Other important cities are <a href="/wiki/Thanlyin" title="Thanlyin">Thanlyin</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twante" title="Twante">Twante</a>. The division is the most developed region of the country and the main international gateway. The division measures 10,170 km<sup>2</sup> (3,930 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-br_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-br-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yangon_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The region was historically populated by the <a href="/wiki/Mon_people" title="Mon people">Mon</a>. Politically, the area was controlled by <a href="/wiki/Mon_kingdoms" title="Mon kingdoms">Mon kingdoms</a> prior to 1057, and after 1057, with few exceptions, by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bamar" title="Bamar">Burman</a> kingdoms from the north. The control of the region reverted to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bago,_Burma" title="Bago, Burma">Bago</a>-based Mon kingdoms in the 13th to 16th centuries (1287–1539) and briefly in the 18th century (1740–1757). The <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Empire" title="Portuguese Empire">Portuguese</a> were in control of Thanlyin (Syriam) and the surrounding area from 1599 to 1613.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mha_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-mha-4">[4]</a></sup> For centuries, Thanlyin was the most important port city in Lower Myanmar until the mid-18th century when King <a href="/wiki/Alaungpaya" title="Alaungpaya">Alaungpaya</a> chose to enlarge a small village across the river near the great <a href="/wiki/Shwedagon_Pagoda" title="Shwedagon Pagoda">Shwedagon Pagoda</a> named Dagon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mn_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-mn-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British</a> first captured Yangon in the <a href="/wiki/First_Anglo-Burmese_War" title="First Anglo-Burmese War">First Anglo-Burmese War</a> (1824–1826) but returned it Burmese administration after the war. The British seized Yangon and all of Lower Burma in the <a href="/wiki/Second_Anglo-Burmese_War" title="Second Anglo-Burmese War">Second Anglo-Burmese War</a> of 1852, and subsequently transformed Yangon into the commercial and political hub of British Burma. Yangon was the capital of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Burma" title="British Burma">British Burma</a> and Hanthawaddy Province, which covered today's Yangon and Bago divisions. The British brought in many <a href="/wiki/Non-resident_Indian_and_person_of_Indian_origin" title="Non-resident Indian and person of Indian origin">Indians</a> to serve as workers and civil servants. By the 1930s, the Indians made up half of Yangon city, and only one third was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bamar" title="Bamar">Bamar</a> (Burman). Between <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, Yangon was the center of Burmese nationalist movement. Many future Burmese political figures such as <a href="/wiki/Aung_San" title="Aung San">Aung San</a>, <a href="/wiki/U_Nu" title="U Nu">U Nu</a>, <a href="/wiki/U_Thant" title="U Thant">U Thant</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ne_Win" title="Ne Win">Ne Win</a> were all one-time Rangoon University students.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tmu_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-tmu-6">[6]</a></sup> Yangon Region was under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Empire" title="Japanese Empire">Japanese</a> occupation between April 1942 and May 1945.</p>
<p>After Myanmar gained independence from the United Kingdom in January 1948, the Hanthawaddy Province was renamed Pegu (Bago) Division, with Yangon as its capital. In 1964, Yangon Division was split from Bago Division. The capital of Bago Division was changed from Yangon to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bago,_Burma" title="Bago, Burma">Bago</a>. In June 1974, Hanthawaddy and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hmawbi_township&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hmawbi township (page does not exist)">Hmawbi</a> townships were transferred from Bago Division to Yangon Division.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-md_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-md-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Post-war Yangon grew tremendously. Successive Burmese governments built satellite towns near Yangon. Today, Yangon Region is essentially the Greater Yangon metropolitan area surrounded by a hollow rural hinterland.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yangon_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yangon Region consists of 4 districts and 44 townships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Of the 44, the city of Yangon now encompasses 33 townships.</p>

<p>The indigenous Burmese (mainly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bamar" title="Bamar">Bamar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rakhine_people" title="Rakhine people">Rakhine,</a> <a href="/wiki/Karen_people" title="Karen people">Karen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mon_people" title="Mon people">Mon</a>) make up the majority of the population. The division is also home to a large number of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burmese_Chinese" title="Burmese Chinese">Chinese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Burmese_Indians" title="Burmese Indians">South Asians</a>. The majority of the inhabitants are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theravada_Buddhist" title="Theravada Buddhist">Theravada Buddhists</a>, followed by <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslims</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christians</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hindu" title="Hindu">Hindus</a>.According to 2014 census report, the population of Yangon region is 7.36 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><a href="/wiki/Burmese_language" title="Burmese language">Burmese</a> is the primary language used by Burmese of all ethnic backgrounds. <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> is the main <a href="/wiki/Second_language" title="Second language">second language</a> among the urban elite of Yangon.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yangon_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Yangon#Economy" title="Yangon">Yangon § Economy</a></div>
<p>Yangon Region is the most developed region in the country. According to the government's official statistics for FY 2010–2011, the size of the economy of Yangon Region was 8.93 trillion kyat, or 23% of the <a href="/wiki/Gross_domestic_product" title="Gross domestic product">national GDP</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mt-_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-mt--10">[10]</a></sup> Greater Yangon is Lower Myanmar’s main trading hub for all kinds of merchandise – from basic food stuffs to used cars. <a href="/wiki/Bayinnaung_Market" title="Bayinnaung Market">Bayinnaung Market</a> is the largest wholesale center in the country for <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beans" title="Beans">beans</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pulses" title="Pulses">pulses</a>, and other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agricultural" title="Agricultural">agricultural</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commodities" title="Commodities">commodities</a>. Much of the country’s legal imports and exports go through Thanlyin's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thilawa_port" title="Thilawa port">Thilawa port</a>, the largest and busiest port in Myanmar. At least 14 light <a href="/wiki/Industrial_park" title="Industrial park">industrial zones</a> ring Yangon,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iz_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-iz-11">[11]</a></sup> employing thousands of workers. Outside Greater Yangon, rice farming remains predominant. Other important crops include jute, pulses, rubber, sugarcane, and groundnut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-br_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-br-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yangon_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Yangon#Transport" title="Yangon">Yangon § Transport</a></div>
<p>Yangon Region has the best transportation infrastructure in the country. All transport to and from the rest of the country (and the world) goes through Yangon. Five "highways" link Yangon to the rest of the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ngamoeyeik_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-ngamoeyeik-12">[12]</a></sup> (To be sure, the definition of highway is loosely used. Most highways are no more than two lane roads with pot-hole prone pavement.) <a href="/wiki/Yangon_International_Airport" title="Yangon International Airport">Yangon International Airport</a> is the main international gateway of the country. <a href="/wiki/Yangon_Central_Railway_Station" title="Yangon Central Railway Station">Yangon Central Railway Station</a> is a major hub of the 5,068-kilometre (3,149 mi) <a href="/wiki/Myanmar_Railways" title="Myanmar Railways">Myanmar Railways</a> system. <a href="/wiki/Twante_Canal" title="Twante Canal">Twante Canal</a>, which links Yangon to Ayeyarwaddy Region, is also widely used for both transport and commerce.</p>
<p>As motor transportation is highly expensive for most people, buses are the main mode of transportation within the division or regions nearby. In January 2008, Yangon Region had nearly 182,000 motor vehicles, 17.7% of the country's total.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uncrd_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-uncrd-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yangon_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_universities_in_Yangon_Division" title="List of universities in Yangon Division">List of universities in Yangon Division</a></div>
<p>Although the city of Yangon has the best education facilities in the country, the educational facilities and opportunities available in the rest of Yangon Region are extremely poor. Many students in rural and poor districts do not finish middle school. According to official statistics, only about 23% of primary school students make it to high school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cso-edu_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-cso-edu-14">[14]</a></sup> Most students are enrolled in the public school system. Private schools, which cost at least $8000 a year in tuition per student, are strictly the preserve of the elite.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-st_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-st-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<p>Nearly all of the division's universities are in Greater Yangon. <a href="/wiki/Dagon_University" title="Dagon University">Dagon University</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Dagon_Township" title="North Dagon Township">North Dagon</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_East_Yangon" title="University of East Yangon">University of East Yangon</a> in Thanlyin are among the largest undergraduate universities in the country. Yangon's <a href="/wiki/University_of_Medicine_1,_Yangon" title="University of Medicine 1, Yangon">University of Medicine 1, Yangon</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Medicine_2,_Yangon" title="University of Medicine 2, Yangon">University of Medicine 2, Yangon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yangon_Technological_University" title="Yangon Technological University">Yangon Technological University</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Computer_Studies,_Yangon" title="University of Computer Studies, Yangon">University of Computer Studies, Yangon</a> and Thanlyin's <a href="/wiki/Myanmar_Maritime_University" title="Myanmar Maritime University">Myanmar Maritime University</a> are among the most selective universities in Myanmar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/University_of_West_Yangon" title="University of West Yangon">University of West Yangon</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Htantabin_township" title="Htantabin township">Htantabin</a> and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Officers_Training_School_(Myanmar)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Officers Training School (Myanmar) (page does not exist)">Officers Training School</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hmawbi" title="Hmawbi">Hmawbi</a> are two major institutions of higher education outside Greater Yangon.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_care">Health care</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yangon_Region&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Health care">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_hospitals_in_Yangon" title="List of hospitals in Yangon">List of hospitals in Yangon</a></div>
<p>The general state of health care in Myanmar is poor. The military government spends anywhere from 0.5% to 3% of the country's GDP on health care, consistently ranking among the lowest in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Although health care is nominally free, in reality, patients have to pay for medicine and treatment, even in public clinics and hospitals. Public hospitals lack many of the basic facilities and equipment. Still, Yangon Region has the best medical facilities and personnel available in the country.
