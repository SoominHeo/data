<p><b>F4</b> or <b>JVKV</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Boy_band" title="Boy band">boy band</a> consisting of <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Yan" title="Jerry Yan">Jerry Yan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vanness_Wu" title="Vanness Wu">Vanness Wu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ken_Chu" title="Ken Chu">Ken Chu</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vic_Chou" title="Vic Chou">Vic Chou</a>. The group was formed in 2001 after the <a href="/wiki/Taiwanese_drama" title="Taiwanese drama">Taiwanese drama</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Meteor_Garden" title="Meteor Garden">Meteor Garden</a></i> that they starred in was widely successful. Together they have starred in several Taiwanese drama series and released three albums, <i><a href="/wiki/Meteor_Rain_(album)" title="Meteor Rain (album)">Meteor Rain</a></i> (2001), <i><a href="/wiki/Fantasy_4ever" title="Fantasy 4ever">Fantasy 4ever</a></i> (2002), and <i><a href="/wiki/Waiting_for_You_(F4_album)" title="Waiting for You (F4 album)">Waiting for You</a></i> (2007).</p>
<p>In 2007, due to copyright issues, the group changed its name to JVKV, using the initials of its members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nameinitials_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nameinitials-1">[1]</a></sup> Other than their home country, Taiwan, they have also attained popularity in other parts of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Philippines" title="The Philippines">The Philippines</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F4_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation_and_Meteor_Garden:_2001">Formation and <i>Meteor Garden</i>: 2001</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F4_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation and Meteor Garden: 2001">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The group formed as a result of the success of a Taiwanese drama series, <i><a href="/wiki/Meteor_Garden" title="Meteor Garden">Meteor Garden</a></i>. The series is an adaptation of the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Djo" title="Shōjo">shōjo</a> <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series, '(<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hana_Yori_Dango" title="Hana Yori Dango">Hana Yori Dango</a></i>). Yan plays the lead role of Dao Ming Si, an extremely arrogant and rich university student who meets his match in the poor but proud girl, Shan Cai. Chou, Zhou, Wu and Zhu play as his friends Hua Ze Lei, Mei Zuo, and Xi Men, respectively. In the series, the four guys identify themselves as a group called "Flower Four", or often shortened as F4.</p>
<p><i>Meteor Garden</i> was released in <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> in 2001 and turned the leads into stars virtually overnight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2002 <i>Meteor Garden</i> was released in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a> and in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> in 2003. It was also released in some place of Europe. The series was aired on Xing Kong channel, and was later remade in China. A sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Meteor_Garden_II" title="Meteor Garden II">Meteor Garden II</a>,</i> was shot in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> later and released in 2002.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_venture.2C_Meteor_Rain.2C_and_Fantasy_4ever:_2001-2003">Musical venture, <i>Meteor Rain</i>, and <i>Fantasy 4ever</i>: 2001-2003</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F4_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Musical venture, Meteor Rain, and Fantasy 4ever: 2001-2003">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2001, F4 became a boy band and signed a record deal with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment" title="Sony Music Entertainment">Sony Music Taiwan</a>. In the same year they released their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Meteor_Rain_(album)" title="Meteor Rain (album)">Meteor Rain</a></i>, and in the following year they released <i><a href="/wiki/Fantasy_4ever" title="Fantasy 4ever">Fantasy 4ever</a></i>. The album includes the <a href="/wiki/Standard_Chinese" title="Standard Chinese">Mandarin</a> version of "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Help_Falling_in_Love" title="Can't Help Falling in Love">Can't Help Falling in Love</a>" that they recorded for the Asian version of <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney" title="Walt Disney">Walt Disney</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Lilo_%26_Stitch" title="Lilo &amp; Stitch">Lilo &amp; Stitch</a></i> soundtrack,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> a Mandarin version of "Yo Te Amo" by <a href="/wiki/Chayanne" title="Chayanne">Chayanne</a>, and a Pepsi advertisement theme song "Ask For More".</p>
<p>In 2003, they embarked on an Asian tour to support <i>Fantasy 4ever</i>, and went on an unannounced hiatus afterwards as the members began to focus on their solo careers.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Waiting_for_You_and_name_change_to_JVKV:_2007-2008"><i>Waiting for You</i> and name change to JVKV: 2007-2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F4_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Waiting for You and name change to JVKV: 2007-2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 29, 2007, <i>Meteor Garden</i> producer Angie Chai announced that the group would no longer be known as F4. The change in name stemmed from copyright-related issues. Japanese publisher <a href="/wiki/Shueisha" title="Shueisha">Shueisha</a>, who owns the copyright to <i>Hana Yori Dango</i> allowed the group to use F4 as its band name in 2001. However, as Japan had filmed and aired its own <a href="/wiki/Live_action" title="Live action">live action</a> versions of the popular manga, with its own cast of four, the publisher felt that there may be confusion over the name in different countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Due to this conflict, the group was renamed JVKV using the first initials of each of the group's members. According to the producer, the members are listed in the new name by the order of their birth dates from oldest to youngest, thus Vanness Wu's name appears before Vic Chou's.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nameinitials_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nameinitials-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite rumours of disbanding,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> JVKV released their third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Waiting_for_You_(F4_album)" title="Waiting for You (F4 album)">Waiting For You</a></i>, on December 28, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Their third album still bore the name F4, not JVKV. F4 were the first foreign artists to hold 7 consecutive concerts in Japan.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jiangsu_TV.E2.80.99s_Spring_Festival:_2013">Jiangsu TV’s Spring Festival: 2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F4_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Jiangsu TV’s Spring Festival: 2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2013, the group was confirmed to perform at Jiangsu TV’s Spring Festival in China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The performance was taped in Beijing on January 30, 2013,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and was aired on February 10, 2013. It was their first performance together in four years. During the show, they hinted that they were going to have a concert tour in China.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_projects">Solo projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F4_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Solo projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>JVKV are also individually concentrating on their solo careers in both acting and music. Three of them appeared in <i>Wish To See You Again</i>, starring Chou and Chu, while Wu made a few guest appearances in a few episodes. Due to scheduling conflicts, Yan did not star in the series; his part was played by <a href="/wiki/Kingone_Wang" title="Kingone Wang">Kingone Wang</a>. Yan filmed a series in Taiwan alongside <a href="/wiki/Wu_Chun" title="Wu Chun">Wu Chun</a> of <a href="/wiki/Fahrenheit_(Taiwanese_band)" title="Fahrenheit (Taiwanese band)">Fahrenheit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Show_Luo" title="Show Luo">Show Luo</a>, called <i>Hot Shot</i> otherwise known as 篮球火 which was released in the Summer 2008.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jerry_Yan">Jerry Yan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F4_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Jerry Yan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Yan" title="Jerry Yan">Jerry Yan</a></div>
<p>In August 2004, Yan came out with his first solo album titled, <i>The First Time</i> (第一次 Jerry for You). He's acted in Taiwanese drama series such as <i>Spicy Teacher</i>, <i>Meteor Garden</i>, its sequel <i>Meteor Garden 2</i>, <i>Meteor Rain</i>, <i>Come To My place</i>, <i>Love Scar</i>, <i>The Hospital</i>, and made a movie, <i>Magic Kitchen</i> 魔幻廚房 (2004). <i>Hot Shot</i> (2008) was broadcast in Taiwan, and <i>Starlit</i> aired in Taiwan in 2009; and <i>Down With Love</i>, with <a href="/wiki/Ella_Chen" title="Ella Chen">Ella Chen</a> of <a href="/wiki/S.H.E" title="S.H.E">S.H.E</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vanness_Wu">Vanness Wu</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F4_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Vanness Wu">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Vanness_Wu" title="Vanness Wu">Vanness Wu</a></div>
<p>Wu focused his attention on both movies and music. He first released a solo album titled <i>Body Will Sing</i> in 2002, and has since released several more albums. He also has been featured in several music videos, such as "Ai Cuo" with <a href="/wiki/Wang_Leehom" title="Wang Leehom">Wang Leehom</a>, the Chinese version of "<a href="/wiki/Crazy_in_Love" title="Crazy in Love">Crazy in Love</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Coco_Lee" title="Coco Lee">Coco Lee</a>'s "Hip Hop Tonight". Besides music, he also has been acting as well, notably in <i>Autumn's Concerto</i> (下一站，幸福) with Ady An. He also has been involved with several big-screen movies such as <i>Star Runner</i>, <i>Kung Fu Fighter</i>, <i>Three Kingdoms: Resurrection of the Dragon</i>, and <i>Dragon Squad</i>, among others. He also took part in China's Jiangsu TV's "Stars in Danger: The High Dive" (星跳水立方). As of 2013, he has released two Japanese albums, <i>Reflections</i> and <i>Reason</i>. He has also collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Kangta" title="Kangta">Kangta</a> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/H.O.T" title="H.O.T">H.O.T</a> on the album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scandal_(album)" title="Scandal (album)">Scandal</a></i>, which was produced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SM_Entertainment" title="SM Entertainment">SM Entertainment</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vic_Chou">Vic Chou</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F4_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Vic Chou">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Vic_Chou" title="Vic Chou">Vic Chou</a></div>
<p>Chou was the first JVKV member to release a solo album. He has since came out with 3 solo albums, <i>Make a Wish</i> (2002), <i>Remember I Love You</i> (2004), and <i>I'm not F4</i> (2007).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ken_Chu">Ken Chu</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F4_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Ken Chu">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Ken_Chu" title="Ken Chu">Ken Chu</a></div>
<p>Chu,released his own cookbook, <i>Delicious Relations</i> (美味關係) in 2006, after starring in a string of idol dramas. He has also starred in at least one drama per year since 2001, except 2005, where he came out with his own solo album, <i>On Ken's Time</i>. He has released his 2nd solo album "Getting Real" in January 2009.</p>
