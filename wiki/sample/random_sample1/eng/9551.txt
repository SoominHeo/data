<p><b>Yang Lei</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">阳蕾</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">陽蕾</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Yáng Lěi</span></i>; born October 12, 1984 in <a href="/wiki/Chongqing" title="Chongqing">Chongqing</a>), also known as <b>Shinee Lee</b>, is a Chinese <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a> and actress who achieved instant fame when she won the nationwide singing contest <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Girl_(TV_series)" title="Super Girl (TV series)">Super Girl</a></i> in 2006.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Lei&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born into a middle-class family, Yang Lei was not encouraged to pursue a career in entertainment. Before 2006, she was a music teacher at the Heping primary school.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Super_Girl_competition"><i>Super Girl</i> competition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Lei&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Super Girl competition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While still a 22-year-old teacher at the Heping primary school, Yang participated in the <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Girl_(TV_series)" title="Super Girl (TV series)">Super Girl</a></i> singing contest (similar to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Idol_series" title="Idol series">Idol series</a> of contests around the world) in 2006. She won the qualifying competition in Chengdu, then went on to win the nationwide contest among 80,000 applicants. Subsequently, she won the tenth in the nationwide contest.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Lei&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Her first single was "Learn" (Chinese: 学会), recorded during the time when she was on Super Girl. Her second single was "1234 Dance" (Chinese: 1234舞). Her third single was "Love me, don't be troublesome" (Chinese: 爱我不罗嗦), which is an interlude of the TV play named "He Was Cool" (Chinese: 那小子真帅). Her fourth single was "Love and Bravery" (Chinese: 爱，勇气).</p>
<p>Her debut album, titled <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Panda_is_coming&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Panda is coming (page does not exist)">The Panda is coming</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">熊猫来了</span>), was released on May 5, 2009. It presold 10,000 copies and sold more than 4,000 copies in the first month.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Lei&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Her first TV play was "He Was Cool" (Chinese: 那小子真帅), in which she played the leading lady named "Han Qiansui" (Chinese: 韩千穗). Her second TV play was "Diary of the Boys" (Chinese: 男生日记), in which she played the leading lady named "Mo Xiner" (Chinese: 莫欣儿).</p>
