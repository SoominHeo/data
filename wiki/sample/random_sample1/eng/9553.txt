<p>A <b>sock</b> is an item of <a href="/wiki/Clothing" title="Clothing">clothing</a> worn on the feet and often covering the <a href="/wiki/Ankle" title="Ankle">ankle</a> and some part of the calf. Some type of <a href="/wiki/Shoe" title="Shoe">shoe</a> or <a href="/wiki/Boot" title="Boot">boot</a> is typically worn over socks. In ancient times, socks were made from leather or matted animal hair.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In the late 16th century, machine-knit socks were first produced. Until 1800 both hand knitting and machine knitting were used to produce socks, but after 1800, machine knitting became the predominant method.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>One of the roles of socks is absorbing perspiration. The <a href="/wiki/Foot" title="Foot">foot</a> is among the heaviest producers of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sweat" title="Sweat">sweat</a> in the body, as it can produce over 0.25 US pints (0.12 l) of perspiration per day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Socks help to absorb this sweat and draw it to areas where air can evaporate the perspiration. In cold environments, socks made from wool insulate the foot and decrease the risk of <a href="/wiki/Frostbite" title="Frostbite">frostbite</a>. Socks are worn with sport shoes (typically white-coloured socks) and dress shoes (typically dark-coloured socks). In addition to the numerous practical roles played by socks, they are also a <a href="/wiki/Fashion" title="Fashion">fashion</a> item, and they are available in myriad colours and patterns.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span></h2>
<p>The modern English word <i>sock</i> is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> word <i>socc</i>, meaning "light slipper". This comes from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>soccus</i>, a term to describe a "light, low-heeled <a href="/wiki/Shoe" title="Shoe">shoe</a>" worn by Roman comic actors,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and deriving from the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> word <i>sykchos</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-online_etymology_dictionary_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-online_etymology_dictionary-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>

<p>Socks have evolved over the centuries from the earliest models, which were made from animal skins gathered up and tied around the ankles. In the 8th century BC, the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">Ancient Greeks</a> wore socks from matted animal hair for warmth. The <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Romans</a> also wrapped their feet with leather or woven fabrics. By the 5th century AD, socks called "<a href="/wiki/Puttee" title="Puttee">puttees</a>" were worn by holy people in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> to symbolize purity. By 1000 AD, socks became a symbol of wealth among the nobility. From the 16th century onwards, an ornamental design on the ankle or side of a sock has been called a clock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The invention of a <a href="/wiki/Knitting_machine" title="Knitting machine">knitting machine</a> in 1589 meant that socks could be knitted six times faster than by hand. Nonetheless, knitting machines and hand knitters worked side by side until 1800.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The next revolution in sock production was the introduction of <a href="/wiki/Nylon" title="Nylon">nylon</a> in 1938. Until then socks were commonly made from <a href="/wiki/Silk" title="Silk">silk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wool" title="Wool">wool</a>. Nylon was the start of blending two or more yarns in the production of socks, a process that still continues.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fabrication">Fabrication</span></h2>
<p>Socks can be created from a wide variety of materials. Some of these materials are <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wool" title="Wool">wool</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nylon" title="Nylon">nylon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Acrylic_fiber" title="Acrylic fiber">acrylic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Polyester" title="Polyester">polyester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olefin_fiber" title="Olefin fiber">olefins</a>, (such as <a href="/wiki/Polypropylene" title="Polypropylene">polypropylene</a>), or <a href="/wiki/Spandex" title="Spandex">spandex</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-9">[9]</a></sup> To get an increased level of softness other materials that might be used during the process can be <a href="/wiki/Silk" title="Silk">silk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bamboo" title="Bamboo">bamboo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Linen" title="Linen">linen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cashmere_wool" title="Cashmere wool">cashmere</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Mohair" title="Mohair">mohair</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-9">[9]</a></sup> The color variety of sock choices can be any color that the designers intend to make the sock upon its creation. Sock 'coloring' can come in a wide range of colors. Sometimes art is also put onto socks to increase their appearance. Colored socks may be a key part of the uniforms for sports, allowing players teams to be distinguished when only their legs are clearly visible.</p>
<p>The township-level district of <a href="/wiki/Datang,_Zhuji" title="Datang, Zhuji">Datang</a> in the city of <a href="/wiki/Zhuji" title="Zhuji">Zhuji</a> in <a href="/wiki/Zhejiang" title="Zhejiang">Zhejiang</a> Province, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>, has become known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sock_City" title="Sock City">Sock City</a>. The town currently produce 8 billion pairs of socks each year,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> a third of the world's sock production, effectively creating two pairs of socks for every person on the planet in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<p>Socks are manufactured in a variety of lengths. Bare or ankle socks extend to the ankle or lower and are often worn casually or for athletic use. Bare socks are designed to create the look of "bare feet" when worn with shoes. <a href="/wiki/Knee_highs" title="Knee highs">Knee-high socks</a> are sometimes associated with formal dress or as being part of a uniform, such as in sports (like football and baseball) or as part of a school's <a href="/wiki/Dress_code" title="Dress code">dress code</a> or youth group's uniform. Over-the-knee socks or socks that extend higher (thigh-high socks) are today considered female garments. They were widely worn by children, both boys and girls, during the late-19th and early-20th centuries., although the popularity varied widely from country to country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> When worn by adult females, knee-high or thigh-high socks can become the object of <a href="/wiki/Sexual_attraction" title="Sexual attraction">sexual attraction</a> and <a href="/wiki/Underwear_fetishism" title="Underwear fetishism">fetishism</a> by some men.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Toe_socks" title="Toe socks">toe sock</a> encases each toe individually the same way a finger is encased in a <a href="/wiki/Glove" title="Glove">glove</a>, while other socks have one compartment for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hallux" title="Hallux">big toe</a> and one for the rest, like a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mitten" title="Mitten">mitten</a>; most notably Japanese <a href="/wiki/Tabi" title="Tabi">tabi</a>. Both of these allow one to wear <a href="/wiki/Flip-flops" title="Flip-flops">flip-flops</a> with the socks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Leg_warmer" title="Leg warmer">Leg warmers</a>, which are not typically socks, may be replaced with socks in cold climates.</p>
<p>A business sock is a term for a colored sock for conservative appearance and casual footwear. The term is often used loosely to indicate a term for a conservative office setting. For instance, business socks, business shirts and business shoes are used for office and job. These socks usually have patterns and are known to be a cause for bleach stains in laundry machines due to their colored manufacturing process and dyed attributes.</p>
<p>Crew socks are short, thick everyday socks usually ribbed at the top of the <a href="/wiki/Ankle" title="Ankle">ankles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> They can be used in a way to warm legs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (September 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The first familiar practice of crew sock<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (September 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> was in 1948.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Crew socks are short, thick everyday socks usually ribbed top of the <a href="/wiki/Ankle" title="Ankle">ankles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Crew socks are usually unisex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Low cut sock is a kind of sock that describe in a way to be cut below <a href="/wiki/Ankle" title="Ankle">ankle</a>. Low cut socks are formed to cover the contours of feet. Low cut socks are unisex wearing but women and girls commonly use them. Low cut socks are normally worn with shoes such as <a href="/wiki/Boat_shoe" title="Boat shoe">boat shoes</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oxford_(shoe)" title="Oxford (shoe)">Oxfords</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moccasin" title="Moccasin">moccasins</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loafer" title="Loafer">loafers</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The Ancient Egyptian style of sock is a blend between modern Western socks and Japanese tabi, both of which it predates. Like tabi, Egyptian socks have one compartment for the big toe and another for the rest, permitting their use with sandals; like Western socks, they fit snugly to the foot and do not use fasteners like tabi.</p>

<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, a sock's size is similar to the person's shoe size; for example, a foot that has a shoe size of 9 would require a sock sized 8–10. In some other parts of the world socks are sized differently from shoes. In the U.S. numerical sock size is the length of the foot in inches, whereas shoe size is not. Sock lengths vary, from ankle-high to thigh level.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">For the baseball uniform, see <a href="/wiki/Baseball_uniform#Stockings_and_pants" title="Baseball uniform">Baseball uniform § Stockings and pants</a>.</div>
<p>Most sports will require some sort of sock, usually a tube sock to protect one's legs from being scraped while participating in sport activities. In basketball, tube socks are worn, and in lacrosse, mid-calf socks are required.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In football, knee socks are used. They are mostly to stop grass burns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_uses_of_the_word">Other uses of the word</span></h2>
<p>The layer of leather or other material covering the insole of a shoe is also referred to as a sock. When only part of the insole is covered, leaving the forepart visible, this is known as a half-sock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>

<p>A sock is also used as a holiday item during <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a>. Children hang a large ceremonial sock called a <a href="/wiki/Christmas_stocking" title="Christmas stocking">Christmas stocking</a> by a nail or hook on Christmas Eve, and then their parents fill it with small presents while the recipients are asleep. According to tradition, <a href="/wiki/Santa_Claus" title="Santa Claus">Santa Claus</a> brings these presents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span></h3>
<p>Among Muslims, socks have initiated a discussion about the intricacies of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wudhu" title="Wudhu">wudhu</a>, the formal washing carried out before prayer. Some <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> clerics, mindful of possible hardship among Muslims in inhospitable circumstances, have issued Muslim edicts permitting practicing Muslims to wipe water over their sock or sprinkle their sock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> This would allow prayer where there are no seating facilities, or if there is a queue. This is the stated opinion especially of <a href="/wiki/Maliki" title="Maliki">Maliki</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sunni" title="Sunni">Sunnis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
