<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Yangshan Port</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><span style="font-size: small;"><span id="coordinates"><a href="/wiki/Geographic_coordinate_system" title="Geographic coordinate system">Coordinates</a>: <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Yangshan_Port&amp;params=30_37_N_122_04_E_scale:10000"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">30°37′N</span> <span class="longitude">122°04′E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">30.617°N 122.067°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">30.617; 122.067</span></span></span></a></span></span></span> <b>Yangshan Port</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%B4%8B%E5%B1%B1" title="wikt:洋山">洋山</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%B8%AF" title="wikt:港">港</a></span>, <small><a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">p</a></small> <i>Yángshān Gǎng</i>, <small><a href="/wiki/Wu_Chinese" title="Wu Chinese">Wu</a></small> <i>Yan-se Kaon</i>), formally the <b>Yangshan Deep-Water Port</b> (<span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%B4%8B%E5%B1%B1" title="wikt:洋山">洋山</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%B7%B1" title="wikt:深">深</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%B0%B4" title="wikt:水">水</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%B8%AF" title="wikt:港">港</a></span>, <small><a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">p</a></small> <i>Yángshān Shēnshuǐ Gǎng</i>, <small><a href="/wiki/Wu_Chinese" title="Wu Chinese">Wu</a></small> <i>Yan-se Sen-sy Kaon</i>), is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deep_water_port" title="Deep water port">deep water port</a> for <a href="/wiki/Containerization" title="Containerization">container ships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hangzhou_Bay" title="Hangzhou Bay">Hangzhou Bay</a> south of <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>. Connected to <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pudong_New_Area" title="Pudong New Area">Pudong New Area</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Donghai_Bridge" title="Donghai Bridge">Donghai Bridge</a> and forming part of the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Shanghai" title="Port of Shanghai">Port of Shanghai</a>, the islands of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_Yangshan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Greater Yangshan (page does not exist)">Greater</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_Yangshan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lesser Yangshan (page does not exist)">Lesser Yangshan</a> are administered separately as part of <a href="/wiki/Zhejiang" title="Zhejiang">Zhejiang</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Shengsi_County" title="Shengsi County">Shengsi County</a>.</p>
<p>Built to allow the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Shanghai" title="Port of Shanghai">Port of Shanghai</a> to grow despite shallow waters near the shore, it allows <a href="/wiki/Berth_(moorings)" title="Berth (moorings)">berths</a> with depths of up to 15 metres (49 ft) to be built, and can handle today's largest container ships. The port is built on the islands of Greater and Lesser Yangshan, part of the <a href="/wiki/Zhoushan" title="Zhoushan">Zhoushan</a> <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a>, with fill from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Land_reclamations_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Land reclamations of the People's Republic of China">land reclamation</a>.</p>
<p>It is connected to the mainland via the 32.5 km (20.2 mi) <a href="/wiki/Donghai_Bridge" title="Donghai Bridge">Donghai Bridge</a>, opened on 1 December 2005 as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_bridges_by_length" title="List of bridges by length">world's longest sea bridge</a>. The six-lane highway bridge took 6,000 workers two and half years to construct.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, the port handled 36.54 million TEU up 3.5% over 2014. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2013 the volume was 33.6 million TEU. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>In mid-2011, port officials said the port was on track to move 12.3 million <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twenty_foot_equivalent_unit" title="Twenty foot equivalent unit">TEUs</a> during the year, up from 10.1 million TEUs in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marine_News_China_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Marine_News_China-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Construction_phases">Construction phases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yangshan_Port&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Construction phases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2000 and 2001, the decision was made to commence construction on the first of four phases. The first two phases have nine berths in total along a 3 km (1.9 mi) <a href="/wiki/Wharf" title="Wharf">quayside</a>. The first phase, which opened in 2004, can accommodate 2.2 million containers annually and includes 10 quay cranes. The second phase was opened in December 2006, and comprises 72 hectares (180 acres) with 15 quay cranes. The third phase, opened in stages, was completed in 2010 with seven berths.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The fourth phase, expected to open in 2015, will add 4 million <a href="/wiki/Twenty-foot_equivalent_unit" title="Twenty-foot equivalent unit">TEUs</a> to the port's annual capacity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marine_News_China_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Marine_News_China-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The total cost of building the port may reach US$12 billion over 20 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> When complete, the port will have 30 berths capable of handling 15 million TEUs annually.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chinadaily_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-chinadaily-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
