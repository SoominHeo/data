<p><b>Yang Young-Ja</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: 양영자, <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: 梁英子; born July 6, 1964) is a retired female <a href="/wiki/Table_tennis" title="Table tennis">table tennis</a> player from <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> who won gold medals in women's doubles at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_World_Table_Tennis_champions" title="List of World Table Tennis champions">1987 ITTF World Championships</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1988_Summer_Olympics" title="1988 Summer Olympics">1988 Summer Olympics</a>, together with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hyun_Jung-Hwa" title="Hyun Jung-Hwa">Hyun Jung-Hwa</a>. She is also a two-time women's singles world champion runner-up in 1983 and 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>She retired right after the <a href="/wiki/1988_Summer_Olympics" title="1988 Summer Olympics">1988 Summer Olympics</a>, and currently resides in Seoul working as a table tennis coach responsible for the junior team of South Korea.</p>
