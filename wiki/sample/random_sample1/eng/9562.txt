<p><b>L 1159-16</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Red_dwarf" title="Red dwarf">red dwarf</a> in the northern <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Aries_(constellation)" title="Aries (constellation)">Aries</a>. With an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apparent_visual_magnitude" title="Apparent visual magnitude">apparent visual magnitude</a> of 12.298,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aj104_1_340_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-aj104_1_340-2">[2]</a></sup> it is too faint to be seen by the <a href="/wiki/Naked_eye" title="Naked eye">naked eye</a>, although it lies relatively close to the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a> at a distance of about 14.5 <a href="/wiki/Light-year" title="Light-year">light-years</a> (4.4 <a href="/wiki/Parsec" title="Parsec">parsecs</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-van_Altena1995_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-van_Altena1995-5">[5]</a></sup> It is a <a href="/wiki/Flare_star" title="Flare star">flare star</a>, which means it can suddenly increase in brightness for short periods of time.</p>
