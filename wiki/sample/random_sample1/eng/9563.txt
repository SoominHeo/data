<p><b>Delta Arietis</b> (<b>δ Arietis</b>, abbreviated <b>Delta Ari</b>, <b>δ Ari</b>), also named <b>Botein</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAU-CSN_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-IAU-CSN-8">[8]</a></sup> is a star in the northern <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Aries_(constellation)" title="Aries (constellation)">Aries</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apparent_visual_magnitude" title="Apparent visual magnitude">apparent visual magnitude</a> of this star is 4.35,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mnras172_667_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-mnras172_667-2">[2]</a></sup> which is bright enough to be viewed with the naked eye. It has an annual <a href="/wiki/Parallax" title="Parallax">parallax</a> shift of 19.22 <a href="/wiki/Minute_and_second_of_arc#Symbols_and_abbreviations" title="Minute and second of arc">mas</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa474_2_653_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-aaa474_2_653-1">[1]</a></sup> corresponding to a physical distance of approximately 170 <a href="/wiki/Light-year" title="Light-year">light-years</a> (52 <a href="/wiki/Parsec" title="Parsec">parsecs</a>) from the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nomenclature">Nomenclature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Delta_Arietis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Nomenclature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>δ Arietis</i> (<a href="/wiki/Latinisation_of_names" title="Latinisation of names">Latinised</a> to <i>Delta Arietis</i>) is the star's <a href="/wiki/Bayer_designation" title="Bayer designation">Bayer designation</a>.</p>
<p>It bore the traditional name <i>Botein</i> which is derived from Al Bīrūnī's <i>Al Buṭayn</i> (<a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a> <i>ألبطين</i>), the dual of <i>Al Baṭn</i>, "the Belly". This is the name of a star association consisting of this star, <a href="/wiki/Epsilon_Arietis" title="Epsilon Arietis">Epsilon Arietis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zeta_Arietis" title="Zeta Arietis">Zeta Arietis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pi_Arietis" title="Pi Arietis">Pi Arietis</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rho3_Arietis" title="Rho3 Arietis">Rho<sup>3</sup> Arietis</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allen1963_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-allen1963-9">[9]</a></sup> According to a 1971 <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> catalogue of stars, <i>Al Buṭain</i> was the title for five stars :Delta Arietis (listed as <i>Botein</i>), Pi Arietis (as <i>Al Buṭain I</i>), Rho3 Arietis (<i>Al Buṭain II</i>), Epsilon Arietis (<i>Al Buṭain III</i>) and Zeta Arietis (<i>Al Buṭain IV</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2016, the <a href="/wiki/International_Astronomical_Union" title="International Astronomical Union">International Astronomical Union</a> organized a <a href="/wiki/IAU_Working_Group_on_Star_Names" title="IAU Working Group on Star Names">Working Group on Star Names</a> (WGSN)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WGSN_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-WGSN-11">[11]</a></sup> to catalogue and standardize proper names for stars. The WGSN approved the name <i>Botein</i> for this star on 12 September 2016 and it is now so entered in the IAU Catalog of Star Names.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAU-CSN_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-IAU-CSN-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the catalogue of stars in the <i>Calendarium of Al Achsasi Al Mouakket</i>, this star was designated <i>Nir al Botain</i>, which was translated into <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> as <i>Lucida Ventris</i>, meaning "the brightest of the belly".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>, <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">天陰</span> (<span lang="zh-Latn" xml:lang="zh-Latn">Tiān Yīn</span>), meaning <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hairy_Head_(Chinese_constellation)" title="Hairy Head (Chinese constellation)">Yin Force</a></i>, refers to an <a href="/wiki/Asterism_(astronomy)" title="Asterism (astronomy)">asterism</a> consisting of Delta Arietis, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/63_Arietis" title="63 Arietis">63 Arietis</a>, Zeta Arietis, <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Tau_Arietis_(disambiguation)" title="Tau Arietis (disambiguation)">Tau Arietis</a> and <a href="/wiki/65_Arietis" title="65 Arietis">65 Arietis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Consequently, Delta Arietis itself is known as <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">天陰四</span> (<span lang="zh-Latn" xml:lang="zh-Latn">Lóu Su sì</span>, English: the Fourth Star of Yin Force.)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Properties">Properties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Delta_Arietis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Properties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Delta Arietis is an <a href="/wiki/Stellar_evolution" title="Stellar evolution">evolved</a> <a href="/wiki/Giant_star" title="Giant star">giant star</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Stellar_classification" title="Stellar classification">stellar classification</a> of K2 III.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa454_3_943_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-aaa454_3_943-3">[3]</a></sup> It belongs to a population known as <a href="/wiki/Red_clump" title="Red clump">red clump</a> giants, which means it is generating energy through the fusion of helium at its core.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mnras408_2_1225_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-mnras408_2_1225-15">[15]</a></sup> With close to twice the mass of the Sun,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa454_3_943_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-aaa454_3_943-3">[3]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Stellar_atmosphere" title="Stellar atmosphere">outer envelope</a> has expanded until it is around ten<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa526_A100_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-aaa526_A100-5">[5]</a></sup> times the Sun's radius. It shines with 45<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa526_A100_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-aaa526_A100-5">[5]</a></sup> times the Sun's luminosity at an <a href="/wiki/Effective_temperature" title="Effective temperature">effective temperature</a> of 4,810 K,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa526_A100_5-7"><a href="#cite_note-aaa526_A100-5">[5]</a></sup> giving it the orange-hued glow of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K-type_star" title="K-type star">K-type star</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-csiro_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-csiro-16">[16]</a></sup> It is a suspected <a href="/wiki/Variable_star" title="Variable star">variable star</a> that ranges in magnitude from 4.33 to 4.37.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gcvs_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-gcvs-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
