<p><b>Yang Tinghe</b> (1459–1529), style name Jiefu, was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Secretary" title="Grand Secretary">Grand Secretary</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ming_Dynasty" title="Ming Dynasty">Ming Dynasty</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Zhengde_Emperor" title="Zhengde Emperor">Zhengde</a> (Wuzong) and <a href="/wiki/Jiajing_Emperor" title="Jiajing Emperor">Jiajing</a> (Shizong) emperors. Yang was born and died in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xindu" title="Xindu">Xindu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sichuan" title="Sichuan">Sichuan</a> province, China.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Tinghe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yang Tinghe earned the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jinshi_(imperial_examination)" title="Jinshi (imperial examination)">Jinshi</a> degree in the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_examination" title="Imperial examination">imperial examination</a> in 1478 at the age of 19.</p>
<p>After the death of the Zhengde Emperor in 1521, Yang became the <i>de facto</i> policy maker of the imperial government for 37 days. He conducted a series of reforms in these 37 days, abolished many unpopular legacies of Wuzong, including the arrest of his favorite, general <a href="/wiki/Jiang_Bin" title="Jiang Bin">Jiang Bin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Yang played an important role in choosing the young Zhu Houcong (then Prince Xing and a cousin of the late Zhengde) as the next emperor.</p>
<p>After Zhu Houcong was brought to Beijing from his parents' estate in the <a href="/wiki/Hubei" title="Hubei">Hubei</a> <a href="/wiki/Zhongxiang" title="Zhongxiang">countryside</a> and enthroned as the Jiajing Emperor, Yang Tinghe tried to continue his influence in the court, given the young age of the new emperor. However, the Grand Secretary disagreed with the emperor as to whom the latter should <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancestor_veneration" title="Ancestor veneration">venerate</a> as his dead father. Yang Tinghe was forced to retire after his political failure during this so-called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_rites_controversy" title="Great rites controversy">Great rites controversy</a> in 1524.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Tinghe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The poets <a href="/wiki/Yang_Shen" title="Yang Shen">Yang Shen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Huang_E" title="Huang E">Huang E</a> were Yang Tinghe's son and daughter-in-law.</p>
