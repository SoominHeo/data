<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Eight Eccentrics of Yangzhou</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Eight Eccentrics of Yangzhou</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">扬州八怪</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">揚州八怪</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Yángzhoū Bā Guài</span></i>) is the name for a group of eight <a href="/wiki/Chinese_painting" title="Chinese painting">Chinese painters</a> known in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" title="Qing Dynasty">Qing</a> for rejecting the orthodox ideas about painting in favor of a style deemed expressive and individualist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CiHai_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-CiHai-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The term was also used because they each had strong personalities at variance with the conventions of their own time. Most of them were from impoverished or troubled backgrounds. Still the term is, generally, more a statement about their artistic style than any social <a href="/wiki/Eccentricity_(behavior)" title="Eccentricity (behavior)">eccentricities</a>.</p>
<p>The eight had an influence and association with painters like <a href="/wiki/Gao_Fenghan" title="Gao Fenghan">Gao Fenghan</a>, as well as several others.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Eight">The Eight</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eight_Eccentrics_of_Yangzhou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The Eight">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The generally accepted list is:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CiHai2_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-CiHai2-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Wang_Shishen" title="Wang Shishen">Wāng ShìShèn (汪士慎)</a> (1686-1759)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Huang_Shen" title="Huang Shen">Huáng Shèn (黄慎)</a> (1687-1768)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Li_Shan_(painter)" title="Li Shan (painter)">Lĭ Shàn (李鱓/李鳝)</a> (1686?-1756)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jin_Nong" title="Jin Nong">Jīn Nóng (金农)</a> (1687-1764)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luo_Pin" title="Luo Pin">Luō Pìn (罗聘)</a> (1733-1799)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gao_Xiang" title="Gao Xiang">Gāo Xiáng (高翔)</a> (1688-1753)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Zheng_Xie" title="Zheng Xie">Zhèng Xiè (郑燮)</a> also known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zheng_Banqiao" title="Zheng Banqiao">Zhèng BănQiáo (郑板桥)</a> (1693-1765)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Li_Fangying" title="Li Fangying">Lĭ FāngYīng (李方膺)</a> (1696 - 1755)</li>
</ul>
<p>Alternate lists include:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CiHai3_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-CiHai3-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<ol>
<li>Huang Shen, Li Shan, Jin Nong, Zheng Xie,Li Fangying, <a href="/wiki/Gao_Fenghan" title="Gao Fenghan">Gao Fenghan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bian_Shoumin" title="Bian Shoumin">Bian Shoumin</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Fa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yang Fa (page does not exist)">Yang Fa</a></li>
<li>Wang Shishen, Huang Shen, Li Shan, Jin Nong, Luo Pin, Zheng Xie, <a href="/wiki/Min_Zhen" title="Min Zhen">Min Zhen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gao_Fenghan" title="Gao Fenghan">Gao Fenghan</a></li>
</ol>
