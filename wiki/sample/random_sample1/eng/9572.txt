<div class="hatnote" role="note">This is a <a href="/wiki/Chinese_name" title="Chinese name">Chinese name</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_surname" title="Chinese surname">family name</a> is <a href="/wiki/Yang_(surname)" title="Yang (surname)">Yang</a>.</div>
<p><b>Yang Zhi</b> is a fictional character in <i><a href="/wiki/Water_Margin" title="Water Margin">Water Margin</a></i>, one of the <a href="/wiki/Four_Great_Classical_Novels" title="Four Great Classical Novels">Four Great Classical Novels</a> of <a href="/wiki/Chinese_literature" title="Chinese literature">Chinese literature</a>. He ranks 17th of the 36 Heavenly Spirits of the 108 Liangshan heroes and is nicknamed "Blue Faced Beast".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Zhi_(Water_Margin)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The novel describes Yang Zhi as about seven <i><a href="/wiki/Chi_(unit)" title="Chi (unit)">chi</a></i> tall, with red whiskers on the sides of his face, and a blue birthmark on his face, which earns him the nickname "Blue Faced Beast". He is highly skilled in martial arts and uses either a <a href="/wiki/Dao_(sword)" title="Dao (sword)">saber</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Qiang_(spear)" title="Qiang (spear)">spear</a> in battle. He is also an excellent archer as well. He is a descendant of <a href="/wiki/Yang_Ye" title="Yang Ye">Yang Ye</a>, the progenitor of the <a href="/wiki/Generals_of_the_Yang_Family" title="Generals of the Yang Family">Generals of the Yang Family</a>. He works as a minor officer in the office of <a href="/wiki/Gao_Qiu" title="Gao Qiu">Gao Qiu</a>, a Grand Marshal, in Dongjing (東京; present-day <a href="/wiki/Kaifeng" title="Kaifeng">Kaifeng</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a>).</p>
<p>Once, he is tasked with escorting a convoy of rare minerals and plants obtained from the common people, known as a <i>huashigang</i> (花石綱) tribute, for <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Huizong_of_Song" title="Emperor Huizong of Song">Emperor Huizong</a>, but loses the goods by accident while crossing the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_River" title="Yellow River">Yellow River</a> during a storm. He is afraid of returning to report to his superior after failing his mission. Yang Zhi manages to scrape some money as compensation for the lost goods and travels back to Dongjing, but overhears news that the newly enthroned emperor is granting amnesty to certain people who have violated the law, to celebrate his ascension. On the way back to the capital, Yang Zhi passes by <a href="/wiki/Mount_Liang" title="Mount Liang">Liangshan Marsh</a> and encounters <a href="/wiki/Lin_Chong" title="Lin Chong">Lin Chong</a>. Lin Chong wants to kill him and take his head to prove his loyalty to Liangshan's chief, Wang Lun. After several rounds of fighting, neither of them emerges as the victor. Just then, Wang Lun appears and stops the fight. He is impressed by Yang Zhi's skill and wants to recruit Yang to join Liangshan, although he is not sincere about it. Yang Zhi declines and leaves.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Selling_his_precious_saber">Selling his precious saber</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Zhi_(Water_Margin)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Selling his precious saber">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yang Zhi returns to Dongjing to report to Gao Qiu about his failed mission, expecting to be pardoned per the emperor's decision to grant amnesty to offenders. However, Gao Qiu does not forgive Yang Zhi and has Yang stripped off his post. Yang Zhi becomes penniless and wanders around the city aimlessly. In desperation, he decides to sell his family heirloom – a precious saber he values as much as his own life. He is harassed by the hooligan Niu Er in the marketplace and he kills Niu accidentally. Yang Zhi then goes to the county office to surrender himself and is placed on trial for murder. The local residents are grateful to Yang Zhi for killing Niu Er, who has been terrorising them, and they plead with the magistrate for leniency on Yang's behalf. The magistrate sentences Yang Zhi to face tattooing and exile to Daming Prefecture (大名府; in present-day <a href="/wiki/Handan" title="Handan">Handan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hebei" title="Hebei">Hebei</a>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Escorting_the_convoy_of_birthday_gifts">Escorting the convoy of birthday gifts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Zhi_(Water_Margin)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Escorting the convoy of birthday gifts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Daming Prefecture, Yang Zhi meets Grand Secretary Liang Shijie. Liang Shijie has heard about Yang Zhi's skill and recruit him as a military officer. The next day, Yang Zhi engages some of Liang Shijie's officers in jousts and defeats them with ease. Liang Shijie is impressed with Yang Zhi's skill and intends to promote him to a high ranking position. However, <a href="/wiki/Suo_Chao" title="Suo Chao">Suo Chao</a> objects to Liang Shijie's decision and he challenges Yang Zhi to a contest. Yang Zhi and Suo Chao fight for several rounds and neither emerges the winner. Eventually, Liang Shijie decides to promote both Yang Zhi and Suo Chao to the same rank. Yang Zhi develops a lifelong friendship with Suo Chao, who also joined Liangshan in a later chapter after being persuaded by Yang.</p>
<p>Liang Shijie treats Yang Zhi well and assigns him to lead some men to escort a convoy of birthday gifts to Dongjing for Liang's father-in-law, the Imperial Tutor <a href="/wiki/Cai_Jing" title="Cai Jing">Cai Jing</a>. However, Yang Zhi sees the convoy as a reflection of corruption in the Song government, since lower-ranked officials often extorted money from the common folk and used them to purchase gifts for their higher-ranked colleagues in the hope that they will be promoted faster. Yang Zhi and his men disguise themselves as ordinary traders so as not to arouse any suspicion from potential robbers. However, they still fall for <a href="/wiki/Wu_Yong" title="Wu Yong">Wu Yong</a>'s scheme at Yellow Soil Ridge (黃泥崗). They are tricked into drinking drugged wine and all of them become unconscious. <a href="/wiki/Chao_Gai" title="Chao Gai">Chao Gai</a> and his six companions then make off with the birthday gifts while Yang Zhi and his men are knocked out.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Becoming_an_outlaw">Becoming an outlaw</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Zhi_(Water_Margin)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Becoming an outlaw">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since Yang Zhi took only sips of the wine, he is the first to recover consciousness and his men are still out cold. He is afraid to return to Daming Prefecture after failing his mission. He feels depressed and decides to abandon his men. Yang Zhi has been very strict with his men earlier during the journey by pushing them to their limits, depriving them of sleep and rest. As such, when his men come to, they see that Yang Zhi had left them and decide to frame him. They return to Daming Prefecture and lie to Liang Shijie that Yang Zhi has collaborated with robbers to steal the birthday gifts. Liang Shijie is furious and he issues an order for Yang Zhi's arrest.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, Yang Zhi arrives at <a href="/wiki/Cao_Zheng" title="Cao Zheng">Cao Zheng</a>'s restaurant and tries to leave after his meal without paying because he has no money with him. He gets into a fight with Cao Zheng but they become friends after they introduce themselves and express admiration for each other's skill. They meet <a href="/wiki/Lu_Zhishen" title="Lu Zhishen">Lu Zhishen</a> and the three of them travel to Mount Twin Dragons (二龍山), where they kill the bandit leader Deng Long and seize control of the stronghold.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Zhi_(Water_Margin)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yang Zhi stays with some other outlaws on Mount Twin Dragons, participates in the battle of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qingzhou_(ancient_China)" title="Qingzhou (ancient China)">Qingzhou</a> (青州; in present-day <a href="/wiki/Shandong" title="Shandong">Shandong</a>) in a later chapter, and follows the outlaws from Liangshan Marsh back to their base. After the Grand Assembly of the <a href="/wiki/108_Stars_of_Destiny" title="108 Stars of Destiny">108 Stars of Destiny</a>, Yang Zhi becomes one of the Eight Tiger Cub Vanguard Generals of the Liangshan cavalry. He follows the heroes on their campaigns against the <a href="/wiki/Liao_dynasty" title="Liao dynasty">Liao</a> invaders and rebel forces after the heroes have been granted amnesty by <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Huizong_of_Song" title="Emperor Huizong of Song">Emperor Huizong</a>. He dies of illness during the campaign against the rebel leader <a href="/wiki/Fang_La" title="Fang La">Fang La</a> after the Liangshan forces captured Dantu County (丹徒縣; in present-day <a href="/wiki/Zhenjiang" title="Zhenjiang">Zhenjiang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jiangsu" title="Jiangsu">Jiangsu</a>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Yang_Zhi.27s_precious_saber">Yang Zhi's precious saber</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Zhi_(Water_Margin)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Yang Zhi's precious saber">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yang Zhi has a precious saber as his family heirloom, which has been passed down from generation to generation since the time of his ancestor, <a href="/wiki/Yang_Ye" title="Yang Ye">Yang Ye</a>. The saber is said to possess three extraordinary properties: it can slice through metal as though it were mud and soil; it can cut a single strand of hair placed on the blade; it is resistant to blood stains. When the hooligan Niu Er challenges Yang Zhi to demonstrate the saber's properties, Yang cuts through a stack of metal coins with the saber. Following that, when Yang Zhi places a strand of hair on the blade and blows, the hair is sliced into two. Niu Er then insists that Yang Zhi kills a man with the saber to prove that the blade will not have any blood stains. Yang Zhi refuses and eventually kills Niu Er by accident during the ensuing scuffle.</p>
