<p><b>Yang Jing</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">杨晶</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Yáng Jīng</span></i>; born December 1953) is a Chinese politician of <a href="/wiki/Mongols_in_China" title="Mongols in China">Mongol ethnicity</a>. He currently serves as a Secretary of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secretariat_of_the_CPC_Central_Committee" title="Secretariat of the CPC Central Committee">Secretariat of the Communist Party of China</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/State_Councilor" title="State Councilor">State Councilor</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secretary_General_of_the_State_Council" title="Secretary General of the State Council">Secretary General of the State Council</a>, and Chair of the Work Committee of Central Government Departments,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cvx_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-cvx-1">[1]</a></sup> and the President of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Academy_of_Governance" title="Chinese Academy of Governance">Chinese Academy of Governance</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Prior to his ascendance to leading roles at the State Council, he served as the Director of the <a href="/wiki/State_Ethnic_Affairs_Commission" title="State Ethnic Affairs Commission">State Ethnic Affairs Commission</a> (2008-2013), and the <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_Inner_Mongolia" title="Politics of Inner Mongolia">Chairman of the Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region</a> (2003-2008). Yang is the highest-ranking non-<a href="/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Han</a> official in the Chinese government.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Jing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yang was born in <a href="/wiki/Jungar_Banner" title="Jungar Banner">Jungar Banner</a> in what was Ih Ju League of Inner Mongolia near the modern city of <a href="/wiki/Ordos_(city)" title="Ordos (city)">Ordos</a>, and is of ethnic Mongol ancestry. He worked as a teenager in a farming equipment factory. In September 1973 Yang was recommended to obtain higher education at the <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia_University_of_Technology" title="Inner Mongolia University of Technology">Inner Mongolia Industry College</a>. He then returned to his hometown to serve in the local Communist Party organization. In 1982 Yang earned a degree in Chinese language from <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia_University" title="Inner Mongolia University">Inner Mongolia University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cvx_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-cvx-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>After graduating, Yang worked in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communist_Youth_League" title="Communist Youth League">Communist Youth League</a> as a local organizer in Ih Ju League, then chief administrator of <a href="/wiki/Dalad_Banner" title="Dalad Banner">Dalad Banner</a>. He then went on to work in the Inner Mongolia regional bureau of statistics, then headed the Regional Bureau of Tourism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-duow_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-duow-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Between 1993 and 1996, Yang served as the Inner Mongolia regional chief of the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Youth_League_of_China" title="Communist Youth League of China">Communist Youth League of China</a> under the League's first secretary <a href="/wiki/Li_Keqiang" title="Li Keqiang">Li Keqiang</a>, who later became Premier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCMP_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-SCMP-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1998 Yang became party chief of the regional capital, <a href="/wiki/Hohhot" title="Hohhot">Hohhot</a>, an office he occupied until 2003. Between 2003 and 2008 he served as the Chairman of <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia" title="Inner Mongolia">Inner Mongolia</a> and concurrently the region's Deputy <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">party secretary</a>, alongside Party Secretary <a href="/wiki/Chu_Bo" title="Chu Bo">Chu Bo</a>. Yang shouldered major responsibility as Inner Mongolia Chairman when a turbine factory in Ulanqab League collapsed in July 2005, killing six workers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-duow_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-duow-3">[3]</a></sup> He left the office in 2008 to take up his new appointment in Beijing as the head of the <a href="/wiki/State_Ethnic_Affairs_Commission" title="State Ethnic Affairs Commission">State Ethnic Affairs Commission</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCMP_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-SCMP-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yang Jing earned a seat on the party's Central Secretariat in November 2012, becoming the first ethnic-minority official to sit on the body in the party's history. Several months later at the <a href="/wiki/12th_National_People%27s_Congress" title="12th National People's Congress">12th National People's Congress</a>, Yang was appointed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secretary_General_of_the_State_Council" title="Secretary General of the State Council">Secretary General of the State Council</a> in <a href="/wiki/State_Council_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China#Principal_officers" title="State Council of the People's Republic of China">Li Keqiang's cabinet</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Yang's position was also unique in that State Council Secretaries-General did not usually hold concurrent seats on the party secretariat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-duow_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-duow-3">[3]</a></sup> He was also the first ethnic-minority official to hold the State Council Secretary-General post.</p>
<p>Yang was a member of the <a href="/wiki/17th_Central_Committee_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="17th Central Committee of the Communist Party of China">17th</a> and <a href="/wiki/18th_Central_Committee_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="18th Central Committee of the Communist Party of China">18th</a> Central Committees, and an alternate member of the <a href="/wiki/16th_Central_Committee_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="16th Central Committee of the Communist Party of China">16th Central Committee</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He has been named as a member of the <i><a href="/wiki/Tuanpai" title="Tuanpai">tuanpai</a></i>, an informal designation given to politicians with background in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communist_Youth_League" title="Communist Youth League">Communist Youth League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-duow_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-duow-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
