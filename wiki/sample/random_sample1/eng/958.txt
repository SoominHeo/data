<p>The <b>Korean FA Cup</b> is a national cup knockout competition involving <a href="/wiki/K_League_Classic" title="K League Classic">K League Classic</a>, <a href="/wiki/K_League_Challenge" title="K League Challenge">K League Challenge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Korea_National_League" title="Korea National League">National League</a>, and various amateur and university-level clubs, which is held annually by the <a href="/wiki/Korea_Football_Association" title="Korea Football Association">Korea Football Association</a> (KFA). Its previous format began in 1921, as the All Joseon Football Tournament (1921–1940) and became the <a href="/wiki/Korean_National_Football_Championship" title="Korean National Football Championship">National Football Championship</a> (1946–2000), but the FA Cup in it present format began in 1996. The <a href="/wiki/Korea_Football_Association" title="Korea Football Association">KFA</a> merged its cup competition with the FA Cup in 2000. The winner gains entry to the <a href="/wiki/AFC_Champions_League" title="AFC Champions League">Asian Champions League</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korean_FA_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <b>All Korea Football Tournament</b> was founded by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korea_Sports_Council" title="Korea Sports Council">Korea Sports Council</a> in 1921, during Japanese rule in <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>. Youth, student and adult football clubs from various provinces participated. After 1934, it became a part of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_National_Sports_Festival" title="Korean National Sports Festival">Korean National Sports Festival</a>, which was the championship for various sports games and matched Koreans against other sports championships operated by Japanese who lived in Korea. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korea_Sports_Council" title="Korea Sports Council">Korea Sports Council</a> was disbanded in 1937, due to the Japanese government's oppression. The <a href="/wiki/Korea_Football_Association" title="Korea Football Association">KFA</a> succeeded it after 1938, but was cancelled after 1940 for the same reason during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the liberation of Korea, the <a href="/wiki/Korea_Football_Association" title="Korea Football Association">KFA</a> founded the <b><a href="/wiki/Korean_National_Football_Championship" title="Korean National Football Championship">National Football Championship</a></b> in 1946. Many clubs from all over Korea participated. It opened in late autumn, like its predecessor, the All Korea Football Tournament.</p>
<p>It declined after the founding of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K-League" title="K-League">The Korean Professional Football League (K-League)</a> in 1983, because professional clubs and famous players didn’t take part in it. There were several efforts to make professional clubs join the tournament,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and it became so successful that many top-rank clubs joined in and the <a href="/wiki/Korea_Football_Association" title="Korea Football Association">KFA</a> renamed it <b>FA Cup</b> for the 1988 and 1989 seasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> However, it soon returned to an amateur tournament, because of discord between the <a href="/wiki/Korea_Football_Association" title="Korea Football Association">KFA</a> and professional football clubs. <a href="/wiki/Korea_Football_Association" title="Korea Football Association">KFA</a>'s <b>FA Cup</b> separated from the <a href="/wiki/Korean_National_Football_Championship" title="Korean National Football Championship">National Football Championship</a> in 1996. The two competitions merged again in 2000.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Format">Format</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korean_FA_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Format">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>1996–2005</b></p>
<p>The Korean FA Cup took place after the end of the regular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K-League" title="K-League">K-League</a> season, and was usually completed over a short period. Games were played in a single-elimination format, with extra time and penalties if required. K-League sides were seeded in the 1st round of the tournament proper, but all matches were played at neutral venues, such as <a href="/wiki/Gimcheon" title="Gimcheon">Gimcheon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Namhae_County" title="Namhae County">Namhae</a>.</p>
<p><b>2006–present</b></p>
<p>To elevate the status of the tournament, matches were spread throughout the year. The 2006 edition, for example, started in early March, with rounds also held in April, July, August and November. The final was played in December.</p>
<p>As in previous years, the competition was contested in a straight knockout format.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Winners_.28only_FA_Cup_after_1996.29">Winners (only FA Cup after 1996)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korean_FA_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Winners (only FA Cup after 1996)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Pohang_Steelers" title="Pohang Steelers">Pohang Steelers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Suwon_Samsung_Bluewings" title="Suwon Samsung Bluewings">Suwon Samsung Bluewings</a> are the most successful clubs in the Korean FA Cup with four titles each. Five clubs, including Pohang Steelers, <a href="/wiki/Jeonnam_Dragons" title="Jeonnam Dragons">Jeonnam Dragons</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeonbuk_Hyundai_Motors" title="Jeonbuk Hyundai Motors">Jeonbuk Hyundai Motors</a>, Suwon Samsung Bluewings and <a href="/wiki/Seongnam_FC" title="Seongnam FC">Seongnam FC</a>, have won the Korean FA Cup more than twice.</p>
