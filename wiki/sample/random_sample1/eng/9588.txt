<p><i><b>A State of Mind</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/2004_in_film" title="2004 in film">2004</a> <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">documentary film</a> <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">directed</a> by <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Gordon_(film_director)" title="Daniel Gordon (film director)">Daniel Gordon</a> and produced by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Bonner&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nicholas Bonner (page does not exist)">Nicholas Bonner</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It follows two <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korean</a> child <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gymnast" title="Gymnast">gymnasts</a> and their families for over eight months during training for the 2003 <a href="/wiki/Pyongyang" title="Pyongyang">Pyongyang</a> <a href="/wiki/Mass_games" title="Mass games">mass games</a>. The film won two awards at the North Korean <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pyongyang_Film_Festival" title="Pyongyang Film Festival">Pyongyang International Film Festival</a> in 2004 and was shown at 11 other film festivals worldwide before being released in a theatrical run in 2005.</p>
<p>UK-based dance band <a href="/wiki/Faithless" title="Faithless">Faithless</a> used clips from the documentary for the video to the single "<a href="/wiki/I_Want_More_(Faithless_song)" title="I Want More (Faithless song)">I Want More</a>."</p>
