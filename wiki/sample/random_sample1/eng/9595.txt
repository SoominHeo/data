<p><b>Amanda Jeannette Detmer</b> (born September 27, 1971) is an American actress. She has made her big screen debut playing Miss Minneapolis in 1999 comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Drop_Dead_Gorgeous_(film)" title="Drop Dead Gorgeous (film)">Drop Dead Gorgeous</a></i>, and later had supporting role in <i><a href="/wiki/Final_Destination_(film)" title="Final Destination (film)">Final Destination</a></i>.</p>
<p>In early 2000s, Detmer had starring roles in comedy films <i><a href="/wiki/Boys_and_Girls_(2000_film)" title="Boys and Girls (2000 film)">Boys and Girls</a></i> (2000), <i><a href="/wiki/Saving_Silverman" title="Saving Silverman">Saving Silverman</a></i> (2001), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Majestic_(film)" title="The Majestic (film)">The Majestic</a></i> (2001), and <i><a href="/wiki/Kiss_the_Bride_(2002_film)" title="Kiss the Bride (2002 film)">Kiss the Bride</a></i> (2002). Later, she has had starring roles in short-lived television series include <i><a href="/wiki/A.U.S.A." title="A.U.S.A.">A.U.S.A.</a></i> (2003), and <i><a href="/wiki/What_About_Brian" title="What About Brian">What About Brian</a></i> (2006-07).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amanda_Detmer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Detmer was born in <a href="/wiki/Contra_Costa_County,_California" title="Contra Costa County, California">Contra Costa County, California</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and raised in <a href="/wiki/Chico,_California" title="Chico, California">Chico</a>. Her mother, Susan (née Thurmon), is a teacher, and her father, Melvin Lester Detmer, is a singing cowboy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> She attended <a href="/wiki/California_State_University,_Chico" title="California State University, Chico">California State University, Chico</a> for her undergraduate education; deciding to complete her Master of Fine Arts (MFA) from <a href="/wiki/New_York_University" title="New York University">New York University</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tisch_School_of_the_Arts" title="Tisch School of the Arts">Tisch School of the Arts</a> before seriously embarking on a career in show business.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amanda_Detmer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Detmer made her onscreen debut in 1995 with the television movie <i>Stolen Innocence</i>, and her big screen debut playing Miss <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis</a> in the beauty pageant-set comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Drop_Dead_Gorgeous_(film)" title="Drop Dead Gorgeous (film)">Drop Dead Gorgeous</a></i> (1999). In 2000, she has appeared as Terry Chaney in the supernatural horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Final_Destination_(film)" title="Final Destination (film)">Final Destination</a></i>, and in the romantic comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Boys_and_Girls_(2000_film)" title="Boys and Girls (2000 film)">Boys and Girls</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Prinze_Jr." title="Freddie Prinze Jr.">Freddie Prinze Jr.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Biggs" title="Jason Biggs">Jason Biggs</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Claire_Forlani" title="Claire Forlani">Claire Forlani</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The following year, Detmer had major roles in comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Saving_Silverman" title="Saving Silverman">Saving Silverman</a></i> with Jason Biggs, and the romantic comedy-drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Majestic_(film)" title="The Majestic (film)">The Majestic</a></i> directed and produced by <a href="/wiki/Frank_Darabont" title="Frank Darabont">Frank Darabont</a> starring opposite <a href="/wiki/Jim_Carrey" title="Jim Carrey">Jim Carrey</a>. In 2002, she had her first star-billed role in the <a href="/wiki/Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer" title="Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer">Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer</a> romantic comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Kiss_the_Bride_(2002_film)" title="Kiss the Bride (2002 film)">Kiss the Bride</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She also had supporting roles in films include <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Fat_Liar" title="Big Fat Liar">Big Fat Liar</a></i> (2002) and <i><a href="/wiki/You,_Me_and_Dupree" title="You, Me and Dupree">You, Me and Dupree</a></i> (2006).</p>
<p>On television, Detmer was regular cast member on the two short-lived NBC sitcoms, <i><a href="/wiki/M.Y.O.B._(TV_series)" title="M.Y.O.B. (TV series)">M.Y.O.B.</a></i> (2000) starring <a href="/wiki/Lauren_Graham" title="Lauren Graham">Lauren Graham</a>, and <i><a href="/wiki/A.U.S.A." title="A.U.S.A.">A.U.S.A.</a></i> (2003) opposite <a href="/wiki/Scott_Foley" title="Scott Foley">Scott Foley</a>. From 2006 to 2007, she starred in the ABC comedy-drama <i><a href="/wiki/What_About_Brian" title="What About Brian">What About Brian</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Barry_Watson_(actor)" title="Barry Watson (actor)">Barry Watson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Lancaster" title="Sarah Lancaster">Sarah Lancaster</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rosanna_Arquette" title="Rosanna Arquette">Rosanna Arquette</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2009, she had a recurring role in the ABC medical drama <i><a href="/wiki/Private_Practice_(TV_series)" title="Private Practice (TV series)">Private Practice</a></i>. From 2011 to 2012, she co-starred in the another ABC series, short-lived sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Man_Up!" title="Man Up!">Man Up!</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> From 2011 to 2013, Detmer also had a recurring role in the USA Network drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Necessary_Roughness_(TV_series)" title="Necessary Roughness (TV series)">Necessary Roughness</a></i>. She also guest starred on <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Criminal_Intent" title="Law &amp; Order: Criminal Intent">Law &amp; Order: Criminal Intent</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Vampire_Diaries" title="The Vampire Diaries">The Vampire Diaries</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mentalist" title="The Mentalist">The Mentalist</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Two_and_a_Half_Men" title="Two and a Half Men">Two and a Half Men</a></i>. In 2017, Detmer was cast in the untitled drama starring <a href="/wiki/Reba_McEntire" title="Reba McEntire">Reba McEntire</a> for ABC, created by <a href="/wiki/Marc_Cherry" title="Marc Cherry">Marc Cherry</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
