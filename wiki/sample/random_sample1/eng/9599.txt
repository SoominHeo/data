<p><b>Avaya Inc.</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'v' in 'vie'">v</span><span title="/aɪ/ long 'i' in 'tide'">aɪ</span><span title="/./ syllable break">.</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>/</a></span></span>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> <a href="/wiki/Technology_company" title="Technology company">technology company</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Clara,_California" title="Santa Clara, California">Santa Clara, California</a> that specializes in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internet_telephony" title="Internet telephony">Internet telephony</a>, wireless data communications, and <a href="/wiki/Customer_relationship_management" title="Customer relationship management">Customer relationship management</a> (CRM) software.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The company also provides business communication solutions for customers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Avaya currently provides services to organizations at over 1 million customer locations worldwide with operations divided globally in 5 regions<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and is ranked at #101 on Forbes America's Largest Private Companies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin_of_the_name">Origin of the name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin of the name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many anecdotes and myths surround the origin of this name. The official statement at the time of announcement of the independent entity stated: <i>“Avaya sounds open and fluid – reflecting a company that’s open-minded and one that provides seamless, effortless interconnections among people and businesses."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></i></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years_.28until_2000.29">Early years (until 2000)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early years (until 2000)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1995, the company that would become Avaya was a part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lucent_Technologies" title="Lucent Technologies">Lucent Technologies</a>. Before that it had been a part of <a href="/wiki/AT%26T" title="AT&amp;T">AT&amp;T</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Avaya-Jan_-2015-21_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Avaya-Jan_-2015-21-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2000, Lucent's management decided to <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spin</a> off this business, which was then known as its enterprise communications group for flexibility, accelerated innovation, and improved operations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Avaya-Jan_-2015-21_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Avaya-Jan_-2015-21-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Avaya Inc., with the stock symbol, AV, became an independent company and started trading on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> on October 2, 2000.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Growth_.282001.E2.80.932006.29">Growth (2001–2006)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Growth (2001–2006)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>2001 – Avaya Interaction Center for customer relationship management (CRM) debuted which enabled businesses to evolve multi-platform call centers to multimedia, multi-site contact centers.</li>
<li>2002 – Avaya IP Office, Avaya Unified Communication Center launched
<ul>
<li>– Proposed "Converged Communications" roadmap focused on the crucial role applications would play in making communications an enabler of better business performance</li>
<li>– Ran the communications networks for the FIFA World Cup games in Japan and Korea</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>2003 – Introduction of Avaya MultiVantage Communications Applications, integrated with Avaya Communications Manager</li>
<li>2004 – Avaya's Contact Center Express was launched as the first complete multimedia contact center solution designed solely for medium-sized businesses</li>
<li>2005 – Avaya Voice Portal 3.0 (later Avaya Aura Experience Portal) was launched as the first software only/VoIP Application Platform (IVR plus other automated channels) for Avaya.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Avaya-SpeechTek-2005_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Avaya-SpeechTek-2005-10">[10]</a></sup> Voice Portal was the successor to the Conversant IVR and Avaya Interactive Response (AvayaIR) IVR and became the core automation edge to the Avaya Aura Call Center Solution set.</li>
<li>2006 – Louis D'Ambrosio took over as the CEO and president of Avaya
<ul>
<li>– Avaya one-X Quick Edition was launched based on the emerging SIP-based communications</li>
<li>– FIFA World Cup games in Germany ran on Avaya communications Network</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>2007 – Avaya Customer Interaction Suite based on End-to-end Session Initiation Protocol (SIP) support was launched
<ul>
<li>– Introduction of Avaya Communication Manager 4.0</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Going_private_.282007.E2.80.932012.29">Going private (2007–2012)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Going private (2007–2012)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2007">2007</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2007">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In October 2007, Avaya was acquired by two private equity firms, <a href="/wiki/TPG_Capital" title="TPG Capital">TPG Capital</a> and <a href="/wiki/Silver_Lake_Partners" title="Silver Lake Partners">Silver Lake Partners</a>, for $8.2 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Avaya-Nov-2007-8-K_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Avaya-Nov-2007-8-K-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The company was delisted from the New York Stock Exchange after the acquisition was completed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2008">2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Avaya Speech to Text let people read their voicemail messages on their mobile devices or computers</li>
<li>Avaya Unified Communications focused on role-based communications needs for teleworkers, home agents, enterprise and small business mobile workers, branch office integration, retail stores and branch banking</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kevin_J._Kennedy" title="Kevin J. Kennedy">Kevin Kennedy</a> took over as the CEO and president of Avaya.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Avaya-Jan-2015-23_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Avaya-Jan-2015-23-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2009">2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The integrated communication offering Avaya Aura was launched. In December 2009 Avaya acquired Nortel Enterprise assets for $900 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2010">2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Official converged network equipment supplier for the Vancouver 2010 Olympic and Paralympic Winter Games</li>
<li>Avaya Aura Contact Center was launched</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2011">2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>In June 2011, Avaya filed with the SEC to raise up to $1 billion in an initial public offering.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ipo_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-ipo-16">[16]</a></sup> On October 4, 2011, Avaya reported that it was acquiring Sipera Systems for its session border controller (SBC) functionality and UC security applications.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Avaya-Oct-2011-8-K_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Avaya-Oct-2011-8-K-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On October 19, 2011 it was reported that Avaya would buy Aurix.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012">2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2012">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On April 30, 2012 the shareholders approved the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Radvision" title="Radvision">Radvision</a> by Avaya for about $230 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Avaya-Jun-2012-8-K_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Avaya-Jun-2012-8-K-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The deal closed in June 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013-2015">2013-2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2013-2015">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Avaya Fabric Connect supported the network backbone at InteropNet 2013</li>
<li>Official Supplier of Network Equipment for Sochi 2014 Olympic Winter Games<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Launch of <a href="/wiki/Avaya_Stadium" title="Avaya Stadium">Avaya Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Jose,_California" title="San Jose, California">San Jose</a>, California in 2015</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Explored_sale_and_bankruptcy_.282016-2017.29">Explored sale and bankruptcy (2016-2017)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Explored sale and bankruptcy (2016-2017)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to news articles<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> citing "internal sources" in May 2016, Avaya's <a href="/wiki/Private_equity_firm" title="Private equity firm">Private Equity</a> owners <a href="/wiki/Silver_Lake_Partners" title="Silver Lake Partners">Silver Lake Partners</a> and <a href="/wiki/TPG_Capital" title="TPG Capital">TPG Capital</a> are considering a sale of telecommunications equipment company Avaya Inc that could value it at between $6 billion and $10 billion, including debt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> CEO <a href="/wiki/Kevin_J._Kennedy" title="Kevin J. Kennedy">Kevin Kennedy</a> on the company's earnings call in May 2016 had earlier confirmed that <a href="/wiki/Goldman_Sachs" title="Goldman Sachs">Goldman Sachs</a> was helping Avaya evaluate expressions of interest that have been received relative to specific assets, as well as in exploring other potential strategic opportunities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> As of November 2016, Avaya was weighing chapter 11 bankruptcy filing while trying to sell off its call center business.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> On January 19, 2017 Avaya filed for bankruptcy protection under <a href="/wiki/Chapter_11,_Title_11,_United_States_Code" title="Chapter 11, Title 11, United States Code">Chapter 11</a>, however stated that its foreign operations would remain unaffected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> On January 19, 2017, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Avaya_Inc." title="Avaya Inc.">Avaya Inc.</a> filed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chapter_11_bankruptcy_protection" title="Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection">chapter 11 bankruptcy protection</a>. In its petition, Avaya listed $5.5 billion in assets and $6.3 billion in debts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Acquisitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 2001, the company has sold and acquired several companies to support its current product set, including VPNet Technologies, Inc., VISTA Information Technologies Inc., Quintus, RouteScience, Tenovis, Spectel, NimCat Networks, Traverse Networks, Ubiquity Software Corporation, Agile Software NZ Limited, Konftel, Sipera, Aurix, <a href="/wiki/Radvision" title="Radvision">Radvision</a> and <a href="/wiki/Esnatech" title="Esnatech">Esnatech</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Avaya-Jan-2015-22_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-Avaya-Jan-2015-22-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Purchase_of_Nortel.27s_Enterprise_assets">Purchase of Nortel's Enterprise assets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Purchase of Nortel's Enterprise assets">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Through <a href="/wiki/Nortel" title="Nortel">Nortel's</a> bankruptcy proceedings, certain assets related to their Enterprise Voice and Data business units were put up for auction. Avaya placed a $900 million bid and was formally announced as the winner of these assets on September 14, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nortel-Networks-Sep-2009-8-K_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nortel-Networks-Sep-2009-8-K-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_information">Corporate information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Corporate information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Avaya's headquarters are located at 4655 Great America Parkway, <a href="/wiki/Santa_Clara,_California" title="Santa Clara, California">Santa Clara</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. The company had offices located in over 145 countries worldwide in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The company also sponsors a user's group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Training">Training</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Training">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Avaya sponsors training programs for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Professional_certification_(Computer_technology)" title="Professional certification (Computer technology)">IT professional certifications</a> and training for use of Avaya's products.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Avaya_Learning_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Avaya_Learning-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_business">Government business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Government business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1985, Performance Engineering Corporation (later PEC Solutions Inc.) was formed to offer technology services to government customers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> On June 6, 2005, <a href="/wiki/Nortel" title="Nortel">Nortel Networks Inc.</a> completed the acquisition of PEC Solutions, Inc. to form Nortel PEC Solutions Inc.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> On January 18, 2006, Nortel PEC Solutions was renamed Nortel Government Solutions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> On December 21, 2009, Avaya acquired Nortel's government business as part of Nortel's asset sale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_sponsorships">Sports sponsorships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avaya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Sports sponsorships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

The contract includes the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 FIFA World Cup</a> in South Korea &amp; Japan and <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a> in Germany.</p>
