<p>The <b>Abomination</b> (<b>Emil Blonsky</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Character_(arts)" title="Character (arts)">fictional</a> <a href="/wiki/Supervillain" title="Supervillain">supervillain</a> appearing in <a href="/wiki/American_comic_book" title="American comic book">American comic books</a> published by <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a>. The character first appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Tales_to_Astonish" title="Tales to Astonish">Tales to Astonish</a></i> #90 (April 1967), and was created by writer <a href="/wiki/Stan_Lee" title="Stan Lee">Stan Lee</a> and artist <a href="/wiki/Gil_Kane" title="Gil Kane">Gil Kane</a>, to be the rival of the Hulk.</p>
<p>Debuting in the <a href="/wiki/Silver_Age_of_Comic_Books" title="Silver Age of Comic Books">Silver Age of Comic Books</a>, the Abomination has been featured in other Marvel-endorsed products such as arcade and <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video games</a>, <a href="/wiki/Animation" title="Animation">animated</a> <a href="/wiki/Television_program" title="Television program">television series</a>, merchandise such as <a href="/wiki/Action_figure" title="Action figure">action figures</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trading_card" title="Trading card">trading cards</a>, and the 2008 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Incredible_Hulk_(film)" title="The Incredible Hulk (film)">The Incredible Hulk</a></i> where he was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Tim_Roth" title="Tim Roth">Tim Roth</a>.</p>
<p>In 2009, the Abomination was ranked as <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a>'s 54th Greatest Comic Book Villain of All Time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publication_history">Publication history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abomination_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Publication history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Stan_Lee" title="Stan Lee">Stan Lee</a> chose the name "the Abomination", which he realized belonged to no other character, before conceiving the character's background and appearance. Lee recalled that he simply told the artist <a href="/wiki/Gil_Kane" title="Gil Kane">Gil Kane</a> to "make him bigger and stronger than the <a href="/wiki/Hulk_(comics)" title="Hulk (comics)">Hulk</a> and we'll have a lot of fun with him."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Emil Blonsky first appeared in the title <i><a href="/wiki/Tales_to_Astonish" title="Tales to Astonish">Tales to Astonish</a></i>, and was introduced as a <a href="/wiki/KGB" title="KGB">KGB</a> agent and spy<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> who became the Abomination after deliberately exposing himself to a greater quantity of the same <a href="/wiki/Gamma_ray" title="Gamma ray">gamma radiation</a> that transformed Bruce Banner into his <a href="/wiki/Alter_ego" title="Alter ego">alter ego</a> the Hulk, using a machine Banner was planning on using to commit suicide. In his first appearance, Blonsky became a large scaly humanoid even stronger than the Hulk. In accordance with Lee's wishes, the character defeated the Hulk in their first battle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Astonish.27_1967_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Astonish.27_1967-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The character has been featured in a number of Marvel titles, gradually shifting from unthinking savage brute,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Astonish.27_1967_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Astonish.27_1967-4">[4]</a></sup> to master schemer,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Thor_.23178_July_1970_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Thor_.23178_July_1970-5">[5]</a></sup> to tortured soul,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hulk.27_1991_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hulk.27_1991-6">[6]</a></sup> and finally repentant villain and occasional defender of the weak<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> before being killed in battle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fictional_character_biography">Fictional character biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abomination_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Fictional character biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Emil Blonsky was born in <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a> (then part of Yugoslavia) and became a KGB agent who was sent to an Air Force Base in New Mexico to photograph equipment when he bombarded himself with a much higher dose of gamma radiation and transforms for the first time when Bruce Banner walks in and is nearly killed. The Hulk is revived by <a href="/wiki/General_officer" title="General officer">General</a> <a href="/wiki/Thunderbolt_Ross" title="Thunderbolt Ross">Thunderbolt Ross</a> using radioactive rays. The Hulk eventually reverts to Banner, who lures the Abomination into a trap and drains off the Abomination's excess power, allowing the Hulk to defeat him. The entire encounter is observed by the cosmic entity the <a href="/wiki/Stranger_(comics)" title="Stranger (comics)">Stranger</a>, who encountered the Hulk in the previous storyline when he planned to control his mind and use him to wipe out most of humanity so the survivors could build a better civilization, but was dissuaded from destroying the Earth by Banner's noble actions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He takes the Abomination - judged to be evil - off world for further study.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Astonish.27_1967_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Astonish.27_1967-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Abomination reappears when summoned by a <a href="/wiki/Coven" title="Coven">coven</a> of <a href="/wiki/Witchcraft" title="Witchcraft">witches</a> to briefly battle the cosmic hero the <a href="/wiki/Silver_Surfer" title="Silver Surfer">Silver Surfer</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and summons <a href="/wiki/Thor_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Thor (Marvel Comics)">Thor</a> (via an absent Stranger's technology) to aid him in escaping the Stranger's laboratory world. Thor frees the Abomination and the other captives, but, on discovering they are all evil, uses his mystic hammer <a href="/wiki/Mjolnir_(comics)" title="Mjolnir (comics)">Mjolnir</a> to <a href="/wiki/Time_travel" title="Time travel">time travel</a> several hours into the past to undo his mistake. After defeating the Abomination and placing the Abomination in prison, Thor departs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Thor_.23178_July_1970_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Thor_.23178_July_1970-5">[5]</a></sup> When the Hulk is defeated by the alien Xeron the Star Slayer (who is in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> hunting a giant creature called <a href="/wiki/Klaatu_(comics)" title="Klaatu (comics)">Klaatu</a>) and brought aboard a space vessel, the Abomination is revealed to be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chief_Mate" title="Chief Mate">first mate</a> of the alien crew. When the captain of the vessel directs Xeron and the crew to battle Klaatu in space, the Hulk and the Abomination are thrown from the vessel and battle until the pair are drawn into Earth's <a href="/wiki/Orbit" title="Orbit">orbit</a> and separated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>It is revealed in <a href="/wiki/Flashback_(narrative)" title="Flashback (narrative)">flashback</a> that the Abomination entered into a <a href="/wiki/Coma" title="Coma">coma</a> on impacting with the Earth and is buried for two years. Revived by an off-course <a href="/wiki/Missile" title="Missile">missile</a> fired from <a href="/wiki/Hulkbusters" title="Hulkbusters">Hulkbuster</a> Base (under Ross' jurisdiction), the Abomination joins forces with General Ross to defeat the Hulk, but is battered into submission by an angered Hulk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The Abomination reappears with fellow Hulk foe the <a href="/wiki/Rhino_(comics)" title="Rhino (comics)">Rhino</a>, and the pair activates a gamma bomb at the Hulkbuster base in an attempt to destroy the Hulk. The Hulk's companion of the time, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Wilson_(comics)" title="Jim Wilson (comics)">Jim Wilson</a>, deactivates the bomb and the Hulk tricks the villains during combat, forcing them to collide and knock each other <a href="/wiki/Unconsciousness" title="Unconsciousness">unconscious</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> A comatose Abomination is eventually found by soldiers at Ross' direction and has a miniature bomb implanted in his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_skull" title="Human skull">skull</a>, being told to fight and defeat the Hulk or be killed. The Abomination tricks the Hulk into an alliance and betrays Ross by attempting to <a href="/wiki/Ransom" title="Ransom">ransom</a> the captured <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Space_Center" title="Kennedy Space Center">Kennedy Space Center</a>. The plan fails when the Hulk turns on the Abomination and the pair fight, with the Abomination being caught on a <a href="/wiki/Rocket" title="Rocket">rocket</a> when it explodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/Illusion" title="Illusion">illusion</a> of the Abomination also appears with other Hulk foes when the Hulk (at the time possessing the intelligence of Banner) enters the <a href="/wiki/Brain" title="Brain">brain</a> of <a href="/wiki/Colonel" title="Colonel">Colonel</a> <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Talbot" title="Glenn Talbot">Glenn Talbot</a> at <a href="/wiki/Microscopic_scale" title="Microscopic scale">microscopic</a> size to <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/excision" title="wikt:excision">excise</a> a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tumor" title="Tumor">tumor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Abomination eventually reappears as a servant of the entity the <a href="/wiki/Galaxy_Master" title="Galaxy Master">Galaxy Master</a>, having been empowered with even greater strength. After another extended battle with the Hulk, the Hulk attacks and destroys the Galaxy Master, causing the villain to weaken and apparently become lost in <a href="/wiki/Outer_space" title="Outer space">space</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> When Hulk foe <a href="/wiki/MODOK" title="MODOK">MODOK</a> invades the Hulkbuster base, he colludes with General Ross to revive the Abomination, who was found in a block of ice above Earth and kept in <a href="/wiki/Cryogenics" title="Cryogenics">cryogenic</a> storage for further study. MODOK intends to use the Abomination against his superiors at <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Idea_Mechanics" title="Advanced Idea Mechanics">Advanced Idea Mechanics (AIM)</a>, while Ross hopes the villain will destroy the Hulk. The Abomination, however, has become afraid of the Hulk and has to be mentally forced by MODOK to fight. MODOK, however, is ousted by AIM, and a hesitant Abomination is beaten by the Hulk when he intervenes to save Banner's laboratory assistant. The Abomination refuses to rejoin the fight, and is <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/disintegrate" title="wikt:disintegrate">disintegrated</a> by MODOK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Abomination is restored by the demonic entity <a href="/wiki/Mephisto_(comics)" title="Mephisto (comics)">Mephisto</a>, who directs the Abomination and other villains against the <a href="/wiki/Cosmic_entity_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Cosmic entity (Marvel Comics)">cosmic entity</a> the <a href="/wiki/Beyonder" title="Beyonder">Beyonder</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The restoration is temporary as a still disembodied Abomination's particles mingle with the mind of the villain <a href="/wiki/Tyrannus_(comics)" title="Tyrannus (comics)">Tyrannus</a>, who reintegrates the Abomination under his control. Tyrannus as the Abomination then comes into conflict with the Gray Hulk, and quickly defeats the weaker version of the Hulk. When the Hulk reverts to Banner, Tyrannus forces him to create a procedure that will separate Blonsky, who is mentally fighting Tyrannus for control of the Abomination. The process is successful and Blonksy is restored to a human form, free of Tyrannus, still occupying the form of the Abomination. An enraged Gray Hulk defeats Tyrannus, who is placed into custody by the organization <a href="/wiki/S.H.I.E.L.D." title="S.H.I.E.L.D.">S.H.I.E.L.D.</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>After encounters against <a href="/wiki/Avengers_(comics)" title="Avengers (comics)">Avengers</a> <a href="/wiki/Wonder_Man" title="Wonder Man">Wonder Man</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Hawkeye_(comics)" title="Hawkeye (comics)">Hawkeye</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> the Tyrannus-controlled Abomination reappears during the "<a href="/wiki/Atlantis_Attacks" title="Atlantis Attacks">Atlantis Attacks</a>" storyline with <a href="/wiki/Deviant_(comics)" title="Deviant (comics)">Deviant</a> <a href="/wiki/Ghaur" title="Ghaur">Ghaur</a> freeing Tyrannus from the body of the Abomination and placing Blonsky's mind within the creature once more. The process drives Blonksy insane, and he battles heroes <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man" title="Spider-Man">Spider-Man</a> and <a href="/wiki/She-Hulk" title="She-Hulk">She-Hulk</a>, managing to knock both unconscious. The Abomination is eventually driven off when placed on fire by She-Hulk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The Abomination's mental faculties eventually return and the Abomination reappears in the "Countdown" storyline as a pawn of another Hulk foe, the <a href="/wiki/Leader_(comics)" title="Leader (comics)">Leader</a>. The Abomination is sent to a <a href="/wiki/Toxic_waste" title="Toxic waste">toxic waste</a> site to collect samples, and encounters the gray version of the Hulk, who is outmatched and also weak due to being <a href="/wiki/Poison" title="Poison">poisoned</a>. The Hulk, however, throws the Abomination into toxic waste that partially dissolves the Abomination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Abomination temporarily teams with villains <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mary_MacPherran" title="Mary MacPherran">Titania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gargantua_(comics)" title="Gargantua (comics)">Gargantua</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and finds and stalks his former wife, Nadia (a famous <a href="/wiki/Ballet" title="Ballet">ballet</a> dancer). The Abomination captures her and after taking her into the New York City sewers, reveals his true identity. After a brief skirmish, the Hulk persuades the Abomination to free his wife.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hulk.27_1991_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hulk.27_1991-6">[6]</a></sup> The Abomination is also captured by the <a href="/wiki/Robot" title="Robot">robot</a> <a href="/wiki/Sentinel_(comics)" title="Sentinel (comics)">Sentinels</a> but eventually freed by the <a href="/wiki/Mutant_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Mutant (Marvel Comics)">mutant</a> <a href="/wiki/X-Men" title="X-Men">X-Men</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>After befriending a woman who finds her way into the sewers,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> the Abomination battles <a href="/wiki/Namor" title="Namor">Namor</a> during an attempt to save his kidnapped former wife.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The Abomination retaliates against the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Police_Department" title="New York City Police Department">NYPD</a> when the <a href="/wiki/Police_commissioner" title="Police commissioner">police commissioner</a> orders the sewers be cleared of all homeless. After killing several police officers, he is eventually driven away when confronted by the Hulk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The Abomination battles the mutant <a href="/wiki/Nate_Grey" title="Nate Grey">Nate Grey</a> (who is searching the sewers for a colleague), who uses his mental abilities to trick the Abomination in thinking that he defeated Grey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> He battles a <a href="/wiki/Delusion" title="Delusion">delusional</a> Hulk,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> before encountering the <a href="/wiki/Warren_Worthington_III" title="Warren Worthington III">Angel</a> when the mutant visits the sewers in which he was once captured and maimed during the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fall_of_the_Mutants" title="Fall of the Mutants">Fall of the Mutants</a>" storyline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>When <a href="/wiki/Betty_Ross" title="Betty Ross">Betty Ross</a> dies in the title <i>Hulk</i>, Banner mistakenly thinks her proximity to the Hulk has induced a fatal case of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radiation_poisoning" title="Radiation poisoning">radiation poisoning</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Using a gamma device, a vindictive General Ross tracks what he believes to be the Hulk to a destroyed town, where the Abomination reveals he was the true culprit. Despite baiting a newly arrived Hulk, the Abomination is unable to force the Hulk to fight and departs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>The circumstances of Betty's death are eventually revealed: Blonksy's transformation into the Abomination apparently alienates his former wife Nadia, driving his hatred of Banner; Blonsky, deciding to deprive Banner of Betty in return, secretly <a href="/wiki/Poison" title="Poison">poisons</a> her with his radioactive blood. After hearing the Abomination's admission, Banner eventually discovers the truth and the Hulk defeats the Abomination in combat. Taken into custody by the military, Blonsky is forced to watch old <a href="/wiki/Home_movies" title="Home movies">home movies</a> of him and his wife together (prior to his transformation) as punishment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Operatives from a secret organization "Home Base" eventually release the Abomination to battle the Hulk, and although able to taunt the Hulk about Betty's murder, he is defeated once again.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> This encounter is revealed to be a <a href="/wiki/Dream" title="Dream">dream</a> generated by longtime <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Strange" title="Doctor Strange">Doctor Strange</a> foe <a href="/wiki/Nightmare_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Nightmare (Marvel Comics)">Nightmare</a> in an effort to torture the Hulk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>After a humorous encounter with the <a href="/wiki/Demigod" title="Demigod">demigod</a> <a href="/wiki/Hercules_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Hercules (Marvel Comics)">Hercules</a>, in which the Abomination is chosen as an adversary for the hero while he completes the modern version of the twelve <a href="/wiki/Labours_of_Hercules" title="Labours of Hercules">Labours of Hercules</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> the Abomination is pardoned and employed by the U.S government as a <a href="/wiki/Contract_killing" title="Contract killing">hitman</a> against hostile foreign powers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> The Abomination is also a conflicted opponent for the heroine <a href="/wiki/She-Hulk" title="She-Hulk">She-Hulk</a> (currently employed by <a href="/wiki/Espionage" title="Espionage">spy</a> organization <a href="/wiki/S.H.I.E.L.D." title="S.H.I.E.L.D.">S.H.I.E.L.D.</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> The Abomination has a subtle but significant role in the <i><a href="/wiki/World_War_Hulk" title="World War Hulk">World War Hulk</a></i> storyline,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> being the source of gamma-irradiated <a href="/wiki/DNA" title="DNA">DNA</a> that allows the creation of an anti-Hulk response team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Abomination reappears after the events of <i>World War Hulk</i>, encountering a new foe called the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hulk" title="Red Hulk">Red Hulk</a>. This new opponent savagely beats, shoots, and kills the Abomination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> The Abomination reappears in the court of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Death_deity" title="Death deity">death god</a> <a href="/wiki/Pluto_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Pluto (Marvel Comics)">Pluto</a>, attacking the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympus_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Olympus (Marvel Comics)">Olympian</a> god when he loses power over the dead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Later on, it is revealed that the Abomination's killer, the Red Hulk, is actually General Ross's gamma-powered alter ego; he killed Blonsky as an act of revenge for his deliberate gamma poisoning (and later death) of Ross's daughter Betty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the "<a href="/wiki/Dark_Reign_(comics)" title="Dark Reign (comics)">Dark Reign</a>" storyline, Abomination is among the dead characters present at <a href="/wiki/Zeus_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Zeus (Marvel Comics)">Zeus</a>' trial.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chaos_War_(comics)" title="Chaos War (comics)">Chaos War</a>" storyline, Abomination is among the dead characters in the Underworld that Pluto liberated in order to help defend the Underworld from <a href="/wiki/Amatsu-Mikaboshi_(comics)" title="Amatsu-Mikaboshi (comics)">Amatsu-Mikaboshi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Abomination fights the Hulk and his allies until <a href="/wiki/Marlo_Chandler" title="Marlo Chandler">Marlo Chandler</a> taps into the <a href="/wiki/Death_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Death (Marvel Comics)">Death</a> essence within her to destroy his undead body.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>A shadow organization (later revealed to be the Ancient Order of the Shield<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup>) bent on gaining control of the Hulk harvests biological material from a mortally-wounded Bruce Banner. After Banner regains consciousness following the experiment and escapes as the Hulk, the organization uses this material to resurrect the Abomination under their control, leaving him "free of a mind or a conscience" and with an ability to seek out Banner/Hulk for retrieval.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> Hulk defeats the Abomination yet again with the aid of the Avengers. <a href="/wiki/Iron_Man" title="Iron Man">Iron Man</a> uses technology taken from Order of the Shield agents to teleport the Abomination into interplanetary space estimated to be "somewhere in the vicinity of <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">Jupiter</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Powers_and_abilities">Powers and abilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abomination_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Powers and abilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Abomination is similar to the Hulk in terms of strength, stamina, speed and durability, and the ability to <a href="/wiki/Regeneration_(biology)" title="Regeneration (biology)">regenerate</a>. In contrast with the Hulk, he retains his intellect after transforming, and cannot change back into human form. He also possesses <a href="/wiki/Gill" title="Gill">gills</a>, enabling underwater breathing; and, he can enter a state of <a href="/wiki/Suspended_animation" title="Suspended animation">suspended animation</a> when bereft of <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a> for long periods. Originally, the Abomination is twice as strong as the Hulk, but unlike the Hulk his strength does not increase/decrease in proportion to level of anger, with the result that the Hulk is able to gain an advantage over Blonsky if the fight takes a sufficient amount of time for his anger to increase.</p>
<p>After being further empowered by Amatsu-Mikaboshi, the Abomination is considerably larger than before, can project hellfire and is strong enough to rip off the shell of his successor <a href="/wiki/Rick_Jones_(comics)" title="Rick Jones (comics)">A-Bomb</a> (something which the Red Hulk had previously been unable to do).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
