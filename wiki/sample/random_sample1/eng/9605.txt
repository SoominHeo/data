<p><b>Upington</b> is a town founded in 1884 and located in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Cape" title="Northern Cape">Northern Cape</a> province of <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, on the banks of the <a href="/wiki/Orange_River" title="Orange River">Orange River</a>. The town was originally called Olyfenhoudtsdrif ('Olive wood drift'), due to the abundance of olive wood trees in the area, but later renamed after Sir <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Upington" title="Thomas Upington">Thomas Upington</a>, Attorney-General and then Prime Minister of the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Colony" title="Cape Colony">Cape</a>. It originated as a mission station established in 1875 and run by Reverend Christiaan Schröder. The mission station now houses the town museum, known as the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kalahari_Orange_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kalahari Orange Museum (page does not exist)">Kalahari Orange Museum</a>. The museum is also the home of a <a href="/wiki/Donkey" title="Donkey">donkey</a> <a href="/wiki/Statue" title="Statue">statue</a>, which recognises the enormous contribution that this animal made to the development of the region during the pioneering days of the 19th century.</p>
<p>The elevation of Upington is 2742 feet (835 metres). It is the closest large centre to the <a href="/wiki/Augrabies_Falls" title="Augrabies Falls">Augrabies Falls</a> (arguably the greatest of South African waterfalls) and the <a href="/wiki/Kgalagadi_Transfrontier_Park" title="Kgalagadi Transfrontier Park">Kgalagadi Transfrontier Park</a>. The landscape is very arid but the soil is fertile and crops such as fruit are grown in irrigated fields. The area is best known for its export-quality grapes, raisins and wines, which are cultivated on the rich flood plains of the Orange river.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wine_Industry">Wine Industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Upington&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Wine Industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Upington's most famous wines are produced by an organisation generally known as <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Orange_River_Cellars&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Orange River Cellars (page does not exist)">Orange River Cellars</a> (ORC). The organisation has six depots in the area (all of them on the banks of the Orange River) at Upington, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kanoneiland&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kanoneiland (page does not exist)">Kanoneiland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grootdrink" title="Grootdrink">Grootdrink</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kakamas" title="Kakamas">Kakamas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keimoes" title="Keimoes">Keimoes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Groblershoop" title="Groblershoop">Groblershoop</a>. The wines from ORC are exported, inter alia, to Europe and the USA.</p>

<p>Upington has a modern <a href="/wiki/Upington_Airport" title="Upington Airport">airport</a> with scheduled flights provided by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SA_Airlink" title="SA Airlink">SA Airlink</a> (an affiliate of <a href="/wiki/South_African_Airways" title="South African Airways">South African Airways</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> One of the airport's runways is 4900m in length.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>In the first half of the 20th century there was a passenger train service from Cape Town to Upington. Until the 1950s a narrow-gauge railway line (two-foot gauge) ran from Upington to <a href="/wiki/Kakamas" title="Kakamas">Kakamas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Keimoes" title="Keimoes">Keimoes</a>, two nearby towns. The trains carried passengers and freight, being mainly <a href="/wiki/Dried_fruit" title="Dried fruit">dried fruit</a>. The line to Kakamas was still in use during the November 2010 grape harvest season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Shopping">Shopping</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Upington&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Shopping">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shopping in Upington is characterised by High Street shopping, with most of stores located along the Scott, Schroder, <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Mandela" title="Nelson Mandela">Nelson Mandela</a> and Mark streets. Upington have a mall, which is the area's first super regional mall. The mall is anchored by <a href="/wiki/Game" title="Game">Game</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Checkers" title="Checkers">Checkers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spar_(retailer)" title="Spar (retailer)">Spar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Woolworths_(South_Africa)" title="Woolworths (South Africa)">Woolworths</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tiger_Wheel_%26_Tyre&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tiger Wheel &amp; Tyre (page does not exist)">Tiger Wheel &amp; Tyre</a> and other clothing stores, banks and restaurants. It is located at the corner of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Random_van_Riebeck&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Random van Riebeck (page does not exist)">Random van Riebeck</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Malharbe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Malharbe (page does not exist)">Malharbe</a> streets.</p>

<p>Upington is located on the northern banks of the Orange River and is surrounded by irrigated vineyards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PinchuckMcCrea2002_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-PinchuckMcCrea2002-8">[8]</a></sup> The hottest month of the year is January, with average daily temperatures swinging from 36 <a href="/wiki/Celsius" title="Celsius">°C</a> (97 <a href="/wiki/Fahrenheit" title="Fahrenheit">°F</a>) to 20 °C (68 °F), while in the coolest month of July temperatures range between 21 °C (70 °F) and 4 °C (39 °F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-saws-climstats_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-saws-climstats-7">[7]</a></sup> Also the driest month of the year, July sees an average rainfall of only 2 millimetres (0.079 in), while the wettest month of March sees 37 millimetres (1.5 in). Upington has been noted as being the sunniest location on the planet for three months of the year, from November through January. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
