<p><i><b>Miscanthus sinensis</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of <a href="/wiki/Flowering_plant" title="Flowering plant">flowering plant</a> in the grass <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> Poaceae, <a href="/wiki/Native_plant" title="Native plant">native</a> to eastern Asia throughout most of China, Japan, Taiwan and Korea. It is an <a href="/wiki/Herbaceous_plant" title="Herbaceous plant">herbaceous</a> <a href="/wiki/Perennial_plant" title="Perennial plant">perennial</a> grass, growing to 0.8–2 m (3–7 ft) tall, rarely 4 m (13 ft), forming dense clumps from an underground <a href="/wiki/Rhizome" title="Rhizome">rhizome</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Leaf" title="Leaf">leaves</a> are 18–75 cm (7–30 in) tall and 0.3–2 cm broad. The <a href="/wiki/Flower" title="Flower">flowers</a> are purplish, held above the foliage. This plant is the preferred structure for the nesting of some species of paper wasps, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Ropalidia_fasciata" title="Ropalidia fasciata">Ropalidia fasciata</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ito_1992_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ito_1992-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nomenclature">Nomenclature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miscanthus_sinensis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Nomenclature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Common names include <b>Chinese silver grass</b>, <b>Eulalia grass</b>, <b>maiden grass</b>, <b>zebra grass</b>, <b>Susuki grass</b>, and <b>porcupine grass</b>. The Latin <i>Miscanthus</i> comes from the Greek for "stalk" and "flower".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AZPLA_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-AZPLA-2">[2]</a></sup> The qualifier <i>sinensis</i> means "from China",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RHSLG_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-RHSLG-3">[3]</a></sup> though the plant is found elsewhere in eastern Asia.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Forms_and_varieties">Forms and varieties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miscanthus_sinensis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Forms and varieties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><i>M. sinensis</i> <a href="/wiki/Form_(botany)" title="Form (botany)">f.</a> <i>glaber</i> Honda</li>
<li><i>M. sinensis</i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Var." title="Var.">var.</a> <i>gracillimus</i> Hitchc.</li>
<li><i>M. sinensis</i> var. <i>variegatus</i> Beal</li>
<li><i>M. sinensis</i> var. <i>zebrinus</i> Beal</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultivation">Cultivation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miscanthus_sinensis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cultivation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is widely cultivated as an <a href="/wiki/Ornamental_plant" title="Ornamental plant">ornamental plant</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temperateness" title="Temperateness">temperate</a> regions around the world.</p>
<p>It has become an <a href="/wiki/Invasive_species" title="Invasive species">invasive species</a> in parts of North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> However, it is possible to reduce the likelihood of escape or <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(biology)" title="Hybrid (biology)">hybridization</a> with extant wild <i>M. sinensis</i> populations with breeding and proper management.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultivars">Cultivars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miscanthus_sinensis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cultivars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Several <a href="/wiki/Cultivar" title="Cultivar">cultivars</a> have been selected, including 'Strictus' with narrow growth habit, 'Variegata' with white margins, and 'Zebrina' with horizontal yellow and green stripes across the leaves. Those marked <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant:small-caps;">agm</span> have gained the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Horticultural_Society" title="Royal Horticultural Society">Royal Horticultural Society</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Award_of_Garden_Merit" title="Award of Garden Merit">Award of Garden Merit</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miscanthus_sinensis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>M. sinensis</i> is a candidate for <a href="/wiki/Bioenergy" title="Bioenergy">bioenergy</a> production due to its high yield, even in high stress environments, easy propagation, effective nutrient cycling, and high genetic variation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synonyms">Synonyms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miscanthus_sinensis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Synonyms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><i>Eulalia japonica</i> Trin.</li>
<li><i>Saccharum japonicum</i> Thunb.</li>
</ul>
