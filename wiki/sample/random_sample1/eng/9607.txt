<p><i><b>Undercover Boss</b></i> is a television franchise series created by <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Lambert_(media_executive)" title="Stephen Lambert (media executive)">Stephen Lambert</a> and produced in many countries. It originated in 2009 on the British <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The show’s format features the experiences of senior executives working undercover in their own companies to investigate how their firms really work and to identify how they can be improved, as well as to reward hard-working employees.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Format">Format</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Undercover_Boss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Format">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Each episode features a high-ranking executive or the owner of a corporation going <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Undercover" title="Undercover">undercover</a> as an entry-level employee in his or her own company. The executives alter their appearance and assume an <a href="/wiki/Pseudonym" title="Pseudonym">alias</a> and fictional back-story. The fictitious explanation given for the accompanying camera crew is that the executives are being filmed as part of a documentary about entry-level workers in a particular industry, or a competition with another individual with the winner getting a job with the company. They spend approximately one to two weeks undercover (one week being the norm in some editions, such as the U.S. version, and two weeks in some other versions, such as the Australian edition), working in various areas of their company's operations, with different parts and in most cases a different location each day. They are exposed to a series of predicaments with amusing results, and invariably spend time getting to know the people who work in the company, learning about their professional and personal challenges.</p>
<p>At the end of their time undercover, the executives return to their true identity and request the employees they worked with individually to travel to a central location—often corporate headquarters. The bosses reveal their identity, and reward hard-working employees through promotion, or financial rewards; while other employees are given training, better working conditions, or, in extreme cases, termination.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Undercover_Boss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Undercover_Boss_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Undercover Boss (U.S. TV series)">Undercover Boss (U.S. TV series)</a></div>
<p>The first episode of the U.S. series, also produced by <a href="/wiki/Studio_Lambert" title="Studio Lambert">Studio Lambert</a>, premiered on 7 February 2010 after <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLIV" title="Super Bowl XLIV">Super Bowl XLIV</a> and featured Larry O'Donnell, then-<a href="/wiki/Board_of_directors" title="Board of directors">President</a> and Chief Operating Officer of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waste_Management,_Inc" title="Waste Management, Inc">Waste Management, Inc</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 9 March 2010, CBS announced it had commissioned <i>Undercover Boss</i> for a second season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On 28 July, <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> announced four company executives from <a href="/wiki/NASCAR" title="NASCAR">NASCAR</a>, <a href="/wiki/DirecTV" title="DirecTV">DirecTV</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chiquita_Brands_International" title="Chiquita Brands International">Chiquita Brands International</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Wolf_Lodge,_Inc." title="Great Wolf Lodge, Inc.">Great Wolf Lodge, Inc.</a> had signed up for the second season of <i>Undercover Boss</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The second season opener was the <a href="/wiki/Choice_Hotels" title="Choice Hotels">Choice Hotels</a> CEO, Steve Joyce on 26 September 2010.</p>
<p>The series was a ratings success for the network, with its premiere episode receiving 38.6 million viewers and a share of 32%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ep1r2_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ep1r2-11">[11]</a></sup> The first season was the most popular new show in any genre in the 2009–10 television season with an average audience of 17.7 million viewers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>CBS's premiere of <i>Undercover Boss</i> on 7 February 2010, immediately following the network's coverage of Super Bowl XLIV, delivered 38.6 million viewers—the largest audience ever for a new series following the Super Bowl since the advent of <a href="/wiki/People_meter" title="People meter">people meters</a> in 1987, the largest audience ever to watch the premiere episode of a reality series, and the most watched new series premiere overall on television since <i><a href="/wiki/Dolly_(TV_series)" title="Dolly (TV series)">Dolly</a></i> on 27 September 1987 (39.47 million). It was also the third largest post-Super Bowl audience behind <a href="/wiki/The_One_After_the_Superbowl" title="The One After the Superbowl"><i>Friends</i> Special</a> on 28 January 1996 and <i><a href="/wiki/Survivor:_The_Australian_Outback" title="Survivor: The Australian Outback">Survivor: The Australian Outback</a></i> on 28 January 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Repeats of <i>Undercover Boss</i> have been picked up by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TLC_(TV_channel)" title="TLC (TV channel)">TLC</a>, beginning 25 July 2011; and <a href="/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey_Network_(U.S._TV_channel)" title="Oprah Winfrey Network (U.S. TV channel)">OWN: Oprah Winfrey Network</a>, beginning Fall 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> TLC has also picked up selected episodes of the UK, Canadian and Australian versions, which air as <i><b>Undercover Boss: Abroad</b></i>; this series debuted on Monday, 13 February 2012, with TLC broadcasting the series most Monday nights, and OWN slated to show the series on Tuesday nights beginning 13 March 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Undercover Boss</i> won the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Reality Program in 2013 and 2012 having also been nominated in the same category in 2011 and 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Undercover Boss</i> has been criticised as a reality show for presenting scenarios and situations which rarely happen to employees in real workplaces and are in fact dramatic tricks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Others point out that CEOs in real-life workplaces do not typically go out of their way to provide gifts and other extra benefits to front-line employees at the expense of the bottom line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>The fourth season premiered on 2 November 2012 on CBS. On 13 March 2014, <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> announced the sixth season renewal of <i>Undercover Boss</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
