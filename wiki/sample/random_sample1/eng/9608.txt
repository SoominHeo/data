<p><i><b>The Unborn</b></i> is a 2009 American <a href="/wiki/Supernatural_fiction" title="Supernatural fiction">supernatural</a> <a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">horror film</a> written and directed by <a href="/wiki/David_S._Goyer" title="David S. Goyer">David S. Goyer</a>. The film stars <a href="/wiki/Odette_Annable" title="Odette Annable">Odette Yustman</a> as a young woman who is tormented by a <a href="/wiki/Dybbuk" title="Dybbuk">dybbuk</a> and seeks help from a rabbi (<a href="/wiki/Gary_Oldman" title="Gary Oldman">Gary Oldman</a>). The dybbuk seeks to use her death as a gateway to physical existence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The film is produced by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Bay" title="Michael Bay">Michael Bay</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Production_company" title="Production company">production company</a> <a href="/wiki/Platinum_Dunes" title="Platinum Dunes">Platinum Dunes</a>. It was released in American theaters on January 9, 2009, by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rogue_Pictures" title="Rogue Pictures">Rogue Pictures</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Unborn_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Casey Beldon has nightmarish hallucinations of strange-looking dogs in the neighbourhood and an evil child with bright blue eyes following her around. While <a href="/wiki/Babysitting" title="Babysitting">babysitting</a> Matty, her neighbor's son, she finds him showing his infant sibling its reflection in a mirror. Matty attacks Casey, smashing the mirror on her head, and tells her: "Jumby wants to be born now". She puts him to bed and leaves in shock.</p>
<p>Casey's friend Romy tells her of a <a href="/wiki/Folklore" title="Folklore">superstition</a> that newborns should not see their reflections in the mirror for at least a year because otherwise they will die soon. Casey's eyes begin to change color; a doctor asks if she is a twin, and explains the change as <a href="/wiki/Chimera_(genetics)#Tetragametic_chimerism" title="Chimera (genetics)">tetragametic chimerism</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heterochromia" title="Heterochromia">heterochromia</a>, and that is completely normal. Her neighbor's infant dies, supporting the superstition.</p>
<p>Casey's father admits that she had a twin brother years ago who died while he was in the womb when <a href="/wiki/Nuchal_cord" title="Nuchal cord">her umbilical cord strangled him</a>, and whom he and Casey's mother had nicknamed "Jumby". She begins to suspect that the spirit is haunting her and that is the soul of her dead twin wanting to be born so it can enter the world of the living as evil.</p>
<p>Casey meets Sofi Kozma—whom she later learns is her grandmother—who explains that as a child she had a twin brother who died during <a href="/wiki/Nazi_human_experimentation#Experiments_on_twins" title="Nazi human experimentation">Nazi experiments</a> in <a href="/wiki/Auschwitz_concentration_camp" title="Auschwitz concentration camp">Auschwitz</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Dybbuk" title="Dybbuk">dybbuk</a> brought the brother back to life to use as a portal into the world of the living. Kozma killed her twin to stop the spirit, and now it haunts her family for revenge, which is why Casey's mother became insane and committed suicide.</p>
<p>Kozma gives Casey a <a href="/wiki/Hamsa" title="Hamsa">hamsa</a> amulet for protection; instructs her to destroy all mirrors and burn the shards; and refers her to <a href="/wiki/Rabbi" title="Rabbi">Rabbi</a> Joseph Sendak, who can perform a Jewish <a href="/wiki/Exorcism" title="Exorcism">exorcism</a> to remove the dybbuk out of her soul. Sendak does not believe Casey's story until he sees a dog with its head twisted upside down in his <a href="/wiki/Synagogue" title="Synagogue">synagogue</a>. The dybbuk kills Kozma and, soon after, Romy. Casey and her boyfriend Mark—who both see the spirit after it kills Romy—realize that it is getting stronger.</p>
<p>Sendak, Mark, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Episcopal_priest" title="Episcopal priest">Episcopal priest</a> Arthur Wyndham, and other volunteers begin the exorcism, but the dybbuk attacks them and several are wounded or killed. The spirit, having possessed the priest, chases Casey and Mark. Mark knocks Wyndham unconscious but gets possessed. Casey stabs Mark in the neck with the amulet; Sendak arrives and he and Casey complete the exorcism. The rite draws the dybbuk out of the human world, but Mark falls and dies during the separation.</p>
<p>Casey mourns her boyfriend but still wonders why the dybbuk became suddenly active in her life now, and why it didn't attack her earlier. She takes a pregnancy test, and learns that she is pregnant by Mark, with twins.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cast">Cast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Unborn_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Odette_Annable" title="Odette Annable">Odette Yustman</a> as Casey Beldon: The film's protagonist; a young woman who is paranoid after becoming the centerpiece to a series of supernatural and strange events; eye color, hallucinations, dreams, and seeing strange things. Casey later learns she had a twin that died in the womb, this is all happening to her because a dybbuk (a spirit trapped between worlds) sees her as a doorway to return to the mortal world.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Meagan_Good" title="Meagan Good">Meagan Good</a> as Romy Marshall: Casey's superstitious and loyal best friend. Romy is amongst those targeted by the dybbuk to effectively weaken Casey to make her vulnerable.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gary_Oldman" title="Gary Oldman">Gary Oldman</a> as Rabbi Joseph Sendak: A rabbi who Casey confides in to stop the spirit.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cam_Gigandet" title="Cam Gigandet">Cam Gigandet</a> as Mark Hardigan: Casey's skeptical but supportive boyfriend. Initially he is doubtful of all the transpiring events, but he later becomes helpful for Casey.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/James_Remar" title="James Remar">James Remar</a> as Gordon Beldon: Casey's father.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jane_Alexander" title="Jane Alexander">Jane Alexander</a> as Sofi Kozma: A survivor of the Nazi concentration camp Auschwitz, who is revealed to be Janet's biological mother and Casey's grandmother. Following the traumatizing possession and target by the dybbuk in the 1940s, Sofi becomes knowledgeable to avoid the spirit, and becomes a guide for Casey.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Idris_Elba" title="Idris Elba">Idris Elba</a> as Arthur Wyndham: A priest who assists in Casey's exorcism.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Carla_Gugino" title="Carla Gugino">Carla Gugino</a> as Janet Beldon: Casey's deceased mother, who was clinically depressed and committed suicide when Casey was a young girl. Janet committed suicide after becoming a target by the dybbuk attempting to gain access to the mortal realm, and the mental effects drove her to commit suicide.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Atticus_Shaffer" title="Atticus Shaffer">Atticus Shaffer</a> as Matty Newton: A neighborhood boy whom Casey babysits and begins exhibiting odd behavior over the course of the film.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ethan_Cutkosky" title="Ethan Cutkosky">Ethan Cutkosky</a> as Barto: Sofi's twin brother who died in Auschwitz during a Nazi experiment on twins. A dybbuk saw him as a chance to get in the mortal realm, and resurrected him to get into the mortal world. To stop it, Sofi killed "Barto" out of intention to defeat the spirit. The dybbuk continued to appear in the form of Barto throughout the film, ultimately having merged with his soul.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rhys_Coiro" title="Rhys Coiro">Rhys Coiro</a> as Mr. Shields: Casey's college professor.</li>
<li>Michael Sassone as Eli Walker: An old man in the senior citizen's home Sofi lives at.</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Unborn_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Unborn_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the United States, <i>The Unborn</i> opened at the third position, grossing $19,810,585 averaging $8,405 at 2,357 sites.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It spent only eight weeks in release, and had a final gross of $42,670,410.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BOM_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-BOM-2">[2]</a></sup> Worldwide, the film grossed $76,710,644.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Unborn_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film received largely negative reviews from critics. Review aggregation website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> gives the film a rating of 10% based on 115 reviews, with the site's consensus stating: "David Goyer's <i>Unborn</i> is a tame genre effort with cheap thrills and scares that border on silliness."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> gave the film a 30 out of 100, based on reviews from 16 critics, indicating "generally unfavorable reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Unborn_(2009_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>The Unborn</i> was released on <a href="/wiki/DVD_region_code" title="DVD region code">region 1</a> <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray</a> July 7, 2009<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and on June 22, 2009 in Region 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The DVD includes both the theatrical version (88 minutes) and the unrated cut (89 minutes), as well as <a href="/wiki/Deleted_scene" title="Deleted scene">deleted scenes</a>. The Blu-ray release contains the DVD features plus two exclusive <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc#BD-Live" title="Blu-ray Disc">BD Live</a> features.</p>
