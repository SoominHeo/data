<p><b>Andrássy Avenue</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>: <span lang="hu" xml:lang="hu"><i>Andrássy út</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Boulevard" title="Boulevard">boulevard</a> in <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>, dating back to 1872. It links Erzsébet Square with the <a href="/wiki/City_Park_(Budapest)" title="City Park (Budapest)">Városliget</a>. Lined with spectacular Neo-renaissance mansions and townhouses featuring fine facades and interiors, it was recognised as a <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a> in 2002. It is also one of Budapest's main shopping streets, with fine cafes, restaurants, theatres, Embassies and luxury boutiques.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Among the most noticeable buildings are the State Opera House, the former Ballet School (under reconstruction since several years), the Zoltán Kodály Memorial Museum and Archives, the Hungarian University of Fine Arts and the Ferenc Hopp Museum of East Asian Arts.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A1ssy_%C3%BAt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Budapest_andrassy_ut_1875.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="768" data-file-width="589" height="261" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Budapest_andrassy_ut_1875.jpg/200px-Budapest_andrassy_ut_1875.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Budapest_andrassy_ut_1875.jpg/300px-Budapest_andrassy_ut_1875.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Budapest_andrassy_ut_1875.jpg/400px-Budapest_andrassy_ut_1875.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Budapest_andrassy_ut_1875.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
1875, Andrássy Avenue at Váczy körút (<i>now Bajcsy-Zsilinszky út</i>)</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>It was decreed to be built in 1870, to discharge the parallel Király utca from heavy traffic and to connect the inner city parts with the <a href="/wiki/City_Park_(Budapest)" title="City Park (Budapest)">City Park</a>. Its construction began in 1872 and the avenue was inaugurated on August 20 (a national holiday), 1876. Its realization was a blend of the plans proposed by the top 3 competitors Lajos Lechner, <a href="/wiki/Frigyes_Feszl" title="Frigyes Feszl">Frigyes Feszl</a> and Klein &amp; Fraser. Its palaces were built by the most distinguished architects (led by <a href="/wiki/Mikl%C3%B3s_Ybl" title="Miklós Ybl">Miklós Ybl</a>) of the time, financed by Hungarian and other banking houses. These were mostly finished by 1884 and mostly aristocrats, bankers, landowners and historical families moved in. It was named in 1885 after the main supporter of the plan, Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Gyula_Andr%C3%A1ssy" title="Gyula Andrássy">Gyula Andrássy</a>.</p>
<p>The construction of the <a href="/wiki/Budapest_Metro" title="Budapest Metro">Budapest Metro</a>, the first underground railway in Continental Europe, was proposed in 1870, since the capital had always been opposed to surface transport on this road. Construction began in 1894 and was finished in 1896, so this new metro line could facilitate the transport to Városliget, the main venue of the <a href="/wiki/Millennium" title="Millennium">millennium</a> celebrations of Hungary.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Foldalatti_Andrassy.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="491" height="196" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Foldalatti_Andrassy.png/200px-Foldalatti_Andrassy.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Foldalatti_Andrassy.png/300px-Foldalatti_Andrassy.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Foldalatti_Andrassy.png/400px-Foldalatti_Andrassy.png 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Foldalatti_Andrassy.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Cross-section of the first metro line under Andrássy út, 1890s</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The boulevard was renamed three times in the 1950s; a testament to the rapid political changes of the period. It became <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Stalin" title="Joseph Stalin">Sztálin</a> út ("Stalin Street") in 1950 during the Soviet occupation. During the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Revolution_of_1956" title="Hungarian Revolution of 1956">1956 uprising</a> it was renamed to Magyar Ifjúság útja ("Avenue of Hungarian Youth"). The following year the governing communists changed the name to Népköztársaság út ("<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_Hungary" title="People's Republic of Hungary">People's Republic</a> Street"). The former name of Andrássy was restored in 1990, after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/End_of_Communism_in_Hungary_(1989)" title="End of Communism in Hungary (1989)">end of the communist era.</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2011, Secretary of State for Culture <a href="/wiki/G%C3%A9za_Sz%C5%91cs" title="Géza Szőcs">Géza Szőcs</a> officially announced plans to build a new structure along Andrássy út close to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/V%C3%A1rosliget" title="Városliget">City Park</a> and near the existing <a href="/wiki/Museum_of_Fine_Arts_(Budapest)" title="Museum of Fine Arts (Budapest)">Budapest Museum of Fine Arts</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kunsthalle_Budapest" title="Kunsthalle Budapest">Budapest Art Hall</a> (Műcsarnok). This building would house the collections of the current <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_National_Gallery" title="Hungarian National Gallery">Hungarian National Gallery</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTI_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-MTI-3">[3]</a></sup> This expanded plan, which would utilize the entire boulevard, is referred to as the <a href="/wiki/Budapest_Museum_Quarter" title="Budapest Museum Quarter">Budapest Museum Quarter</a> or Andrássy Quarter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Foldes_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Foldes-4">[4]</a></sup> However, since that announcement the plans changed several times and have now been reduced to three new museum buildings in and around the city park.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sections">Sections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A1ssy_%C3%BAt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andrássy út consists of four main parts, from inside to outside as follows:</p>
<ol>
<li>From Erzsébet tér to <a href="/wiki/Oktogon_(intersection)" title="Oktogon (intersection)">Oktogon</a>: an urban-like part mostly for commercial purposes.</li>
<li>From Oktogon to Kodály körönd: widened with an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All%C3%A9e" title="Allée">allée</a>, including residential areas and universities.</li>
<li>From Kodály körönd to Bajza utca: it is even more widened, and residential palaces are fronted by small gardens.
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Andr%C3%A1ssy_94_-_1895.tif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4076" data-file-width="5675" height="158" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Andr%C3%A1ssy_94_-_1895.tif/lossy-page1-220px-Andr%C3%A1ssy_94_-_1895.tif.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Andr%C3%A1ssy_94_-_1895.tif/lossy-page1-330px-Andr%C3%A1ssy_94_-_1895.tif.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Andr%C3%A1ssy_94_-_1895.tif/lossy-page1-440px-Andr%C3%A1ssy_94_-_1895.tif.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Andr%C3%A1ssy_94_-_1895.tif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Kodály körönd 4 - 1895</div>
</div>
</div>
</li>
<li>From Bajza utca to Városliget: the same width; <a href="/wiki/Villa" title="Villa">villas</a> encompassed by gardens, including a couple of embassies.</li>
</ol>
