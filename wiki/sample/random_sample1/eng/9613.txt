<p><i><b>Unbroken</b></i> is a 2014 American <a href="/wiki/War_film" title="War film">war film</a> produced and directed by <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Jolie" title="Angelina Jolie">Angelina Jolie</a>, written by <a href="/wiki/Coen_brothers" title="Coen brothers">Coen brothers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_LaGravenese" title="Richard LaGravenese">Richard LaGravenese</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_Nicholson_(writer)" title="William Nicholson (writer)">William Nicholson</a> and based on the 2010 non-fiction book by <a href="/wiki/Laura_Hillenbrand" title="Laura Hillenbrand">Laura Hillenbrand</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Unbroken:_A_World_War_II_Story_of_Survival,_Resilience,_and_Redemption" title="Unbroken: A World War II Story of Survival, Resilience, and Redemption">Unbroken: A World War II Story of Survival, Resilience, and Redemption</a></i>. The film revolves around the life of <a href="/wiki/United_States_at_the_Olympics" title="United States at the Olympics">USA Olympian</a> and athlete <a href="/wiki/Louis_Zamperini" title="Louis Zamperini">Louis "Louie" Zamperini</a>, portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Jack_O%27Connell_(actor)" title="Jack O'Connell (actor)">Jack O'Connell</a>. Zamperini survived in a raft for 47 days after his bomber crash landed in the ocean during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, then was sent to a series of <a href="/wiki/Prisoner_of_war" title="Prisoner of war">prisoner of war</a> camps.</p>
<p>The film had its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_premiere" title="Film premiere">world premiere</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a> on November 17, 2014, and received a wide release in the United States on December 25, 2014. The film grossed $115.6 million in North America, with a worldwide total of over $163 million.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unbroken_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film opens with <a href="/wiki/Louis_Zamperini" title="Louis Zamperini">Louis "Louie" Zamperini</a> flying as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bombardier_(air_force)" title="Bombardier (air force)">bombardier</a> of a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">United States Army Air Forces</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B-24_Liberator" title="B-24 Liberator">B-24 Liberator</a> bomber, during an April 1943 bombing mission against the <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Japanese</a>-held island of <a href="/wiki/Nauru" title="Nauru">Nauru</a>. The plane is badly damaged in combat, with a number of the crew injured. The pilot, Phil, manages to bring it to a stop at the end of the runway thanks to an exploded tire.</p>
<p>The story flashes back to Louie's childhood as a young Italian-American boy in <a href="/wiki/Torrance,_California" title="Torrance, California">Torrance</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. Louie is a troublemaker, who steals, drinks liquor and smokes, to the disappointment of his parents. He is often picked on by other kids for being of Italian ethnicity. One day, Louie is caught looking up women's dresses from under the <a href="/wiki/Bleacher" title="Bleacher">bleachers</a> during a track meet. His brother Peter sees how fast Louie runs away and decides to train him to be a runner. As he grows, Louie becomes more disciplined and also becomes an accomplished distance runner, earning him the nickname "The Torrance Tornado". He qualifies for the <a href="/wiki/1936_Summer_Olympics" title="1936 Summer Olympics">1936 Summer Olympics</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Berlin,_Germany" title="Berlin, Germany">Berlin, Germany</a>. Louie comes in 8th and sets a record for speed in the final lap in the <a href="/wiki/5000_metres" title="5000 metres">5,000 meters</a> race.</p>
<p>Returning to 1943, Louie, the surviving crew and several replacements are sent on a search-and-rescue mission with a plane previously used for spare parts. During the mission, the plane's two left engines fail and the plane crashes in the ocean. Louie, Mac and Phil survive and live on two inflatable rafts. After three days, a search plane flies over but they are unable to get its attention. They weather a storm and fend off a shark attack while subsisting on rations, rainwater, birds and fish. On the 27th day, they get the attention of a Japanese plane, which strafes and damages the rafts but misses them. On the 33rd day Mac dies.</p>
<p>On the 47th day, Japanese sailors capture Louie and Phil and they become <a href="/wiki/Japanese_war_crimes#Forced_labor" title="Japanese war crimes">prisoners of war</a>. Louie and Phil are then taken to <a href="/wiki/Kwajalein_Atoll" title="Kwajalein Atoll">Kwajalein Atoll</a>, where they are kept in a dungeon. Louie sees the names of nine <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._marines" title="U.S. marines">U.S. marines</a>, that were held captive on the island, scratched into the wall. Louie later finds out that they were beheaded. One day, Japanese ask Louie and Phil to tell them about B-class bombers. Louie tells they flew D-class and draws a picture of a radio. The film then fast forwards to when the two are separated and sent to different POW camps.</p>
<p>Louie's camp, Ōmori, in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, is headed by a Japanese corporal, <a href="/wiki/Mutsuhiro_Watanabe" title="Mutsuhiro Watanabe">Mutsuhiro Watanabe</a>, who treats him very cruelly, in part because of Louie's status as a former Olympian and an American soldier. Watanabe is especially tough on Louie, beating him often. Louie is given an escape from Watanabe's torture when two Japanese newsmen give him the opportunity to broadcast a message home saying that he is alive after they learn the <a href="/wiki/Federal_government_of_the_United_States" title="Federal government of the United States">US government</a> classified him as <a href="/wiki/Killed_in_action" title="Killed in action">KIA</a>. When he refuses to broadcast a second message with anti-American propaganda, he is sent back to camp where Watanabe has each of the other prisoners punch him to teach him respect.</p>
<p>After two years, Watanabe gets a promotion and leaves the camp. The camp is damaged when <a href="/wiki/Bombing_of_Tokyo" title="Bombing of Tokyo">Tokyo is bombed</a> by American forces. Louie and the other internees are forced to move to Naoetsu prison camp, where Louie discovers, to his horror, that Watanabe is in charge. The prisoners are now put to work loading coal barges. Louie, exhausted, pauses during work, and Watanabe tells him to lift a giant piece of wood and orders a guard to shoot Louie if he drops it. Louie successfully lifts and holds up the wood despite being thoroughly worn out and enrages Watanabe by staring straight at him in the eyes, who proceeded to beat him severely.</p>
<p>At the end of the war, Louie and the other prisoners in the camp are set free to return to their homes. In the meantime, Louie tried to find Watanabe in his quarters but realises he had fled. Back home in America, he kisses the ground and hugs his family.</p>
<p>At the end of the film, there is a slideshow showing what happened after the war. Louis was married and had two children. Phil survived and eventually married his sweetheart. Mutsuhiro "Bird" Watanabe went into hiding for several years and successfully evaded prosecution in spite of being listed in the top 40 most-wanted Japanese <a href="/wiki/War_crime" title="War crime">war criminals</a> by <a href="/wiki/Douglas_MacArthur" title="Douglas MacArthur">General Douglas MacArthur</a>. Louie lived out his promise to devote his life to God; eventually believing in Jesus Christ and forgave his war-time captors, meeting with many of them. Watanabe, however, refused to meet with Louie.</p>
<p>In January 1998, Louie had an opportunity to revisit his time as an Olympian when he ran a leg of the Olympic Torch relay for the <a href="/wiki/1998_Winter_Olympics" title="1998 Winter Olympics">Winter Olympic Games in Nagano</a>, Japan. He was four days short of his 81st birthday. The site for his leg of the relay was not far from one of the POW camps where he was held during the war. The closing titles reveal that Louie Zamperini died on July 2, 2014, at age 97.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unbroken_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unbroken_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios" title="Universal Studios">Universal Pictures</a> purchased the rights to the book in January 2011, having already acquired the film rights to Zamperini's life story towards the end of the 1950s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Early drafts for the film were written by <a href="/wiki/William_Nicholson_(writer)" title="William Nicholson (writer)">William Nicholson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Richard_LaGravenese" title="Richard LaGravenese">Richard LaGravenese</a> while <a href="/wiki/Francis_Lawrence" title="Francis Lawrence">Francis Lawrence</a> was scheduled to direct. <a href="/wiki/Coen_brothers" title="Coen brothers">Joel and Ethan Coen</a> were then tapped to rewrite the script after Jolie was named director.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hollywoodreporter2-25-2013_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-hollywoodreporter2-25-2013-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 30, 2013, Jolie was confirmed to direct the film in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Jolie was paid a $1 million salary for directing the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Salaries_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Salaries-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Walden_Media" title="Walden Media">Walden Media</a> was originally set as Universal's co-financer,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> but withdrew from the project prior to filming and were subsequently replaced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legendary_Pictures" title="Legendary Pictures">Legendary Pictures</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes.com_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-latimes.com-2">[2]</a></sup> The filming was based in <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a>, with scenes also shot in <a href="/wiki/Fox_Studios_Australia" title="Fox Studios Australia">Fox Studios Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Village_Roadshow_Studios" title="Village Roadshow Studios">Village Roadshow Studios</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unbroken_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Principal_photography" title="Principal photography">Principal photography</a> began on October 16, 2013 and ended on February 4, 2014,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> with post-production also being done in Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some of the scenes were shot at sea in <a href="/wiki/Moreton_Bay" title="Moreton Bay">Moreton Bay</a> on October 16, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On December 14, four days of filming were completed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Werris_Creek,_New_South_Wales" title="Werris Creek, New South Wales">Werris Creek</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Other scenes were shot at <a href="/wiki/Cockatoo_Island_(New_South_Wales)" title="Cockatoo Island (New South Wales)">Cockatoo Island (New South Wales)</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unbroken_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The official film soundtrack was released on December 15, 2014, through <a href="/wiki/Parlophone" title="Parlophone">Parlophone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>. The film score was composed by <a href="/wiki/Alexandre_Desplat" title="Alexandre Desplat">Alexandre Desplat</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The album also features "<a href="/wiki/Miracles_(Coldplay_song)" title="Miracles (Coldplay song)">Miracles</a>", a song written and recorded by British alternative rock band <a href="/wiki/Coldplay" title="Coldplay">Coldplay</a>, which was released digitally as a single on December 15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Coldplay.com_single_unveiled_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Coldplay.com_single_unveiled-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unbroken_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unbroken_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Unbroken</i> grossed $115.6 million in the U.S. and Canada and $47.6 million in other territories for a worldwide total of $163 million, against a budget of $65 million.</p>
<p>The film opened in the North America on December 25, 2014 across 3,131 theaters and grossed $15.6 million on its opening day (including Christmas Eve previews) which is the third-biggest Christmas Day debut ever, behind <i><a href="/wiki/Les_Mis%C3%A9rables_(2012_film)" title="Les Misérables (2012 film)">Les Misérables</a></i> ($18 million), and <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes_(2009_film)" title="Sherlock Holmes (2009 film)">Sherlock Holmes</a></i> ($24 million) and the fifth-biggest Christmas Day gross ever.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forbes_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The film was among one of the four <a href="/wiki/Wide_release" title="Wide release">widely released</a> film on December 25, 2014, the other three being <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Studios_Motion_Pictures" title="Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures">Walt Disney</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Into_the_Woods_(film)" title="Into the Woods (film)">Into the Woods</a></i> (2,478 theaters), <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Pictures" title="Paramount Pictures">Paramount Pictures</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/The_Gambler_(2014_film)" title="The Gambler (2014 film)">The Gambler</a></i> (2,478 theaters) and <a href="/wiki/The_Weinstein_Company" title="The Weinstein Company">TWC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Eyes" title="Big Eyes">Big Eyes</a></i> (1,307 theaters).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> It earned $31,748,000 in its traditional three-day opening weekend (including its revenue from Christmas Day it earned $47.3 million) debuting at #2 at the box office behind <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit:_The_Battle_of_the_Five_Armies" title="The Hobbit: The Battle of the Five Armies">The Hobbit: The Battle of the Five Armies</a></i> setting a record for the third-biggest Christmas debut behind <i>Sherlock Holmes</i> ($62 million) and <i><a href="/wiki/Marley_%26_Me_(film)" title="Marley &amp; Me (film)">Marley &amp; Me</a></i> ($36 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and fourth biggest among World War II theme movies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OW_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-OW-21">[21]</a></sup> It was the eighth film that earned $25 million plus in its debut weekend for Universal Pictures and the fifth $30 million plus debut for an "original" movie following <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lone_Survivor_(film)" title="Lone Survivor (film)">Lone Survivor</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Ride_Along_(film)" title="Ride Along (film)">Ride Along</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Neighbors_(2014_film)" title="Neighbors (2014 film)">Neighbors</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Lucy_(2014_film)" title="Lucy (2014 film)">Lucy</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OW_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-OW-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unbroken_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Miyavi,_Angelina_Jolie,_Jack_O%27Connell,_Matthew_Baer.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2816" data-file-width="1880" height="330" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Miyavi%2C_Angelina_Jolie%2C_Jack_O%27Connell%2C_Matthew_Baer.jpg/220px-Miyavi%2C_Angelina_Jolie%2C_Jack_O%27Connell%2C_Matthew_Baer.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Miyavi%2C_Angelina_Jolie%2C_Jack_O%27Connell%2C_Matthew_Baer.jpg/330px-Miyavi%2C_Angelina_Jolie%2C_Jack_O%27Connell%2C_Matthew_Baer.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Miyavi%2C_Angelina_Jolie%2C_Jack_O%27Connell%2C_Matthew_Baer.jpg/440px-Miyavi%2C_Angelina_Jolie%2C_Jack_O%27Connell%2C_Matthew_Baer.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>On <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, the film holds a rating of 51% based on 194 reviews; the average rating is 6/10. The site's consensus reads, "<i>Unbroken</i> is undoubtedly well-intentioned, but it hits a few too many of the expected prestige-pic beats to register as strongly as it should."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, the film has a score of 59 out of 100 based on 48 reviews, indicating "mixed or average reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/CinemaScore" title="CinemaScore">CinemaScore</a>, audiences gave the film an average grade of "A-" on an A+ to F scale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OW_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-OW-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>The SAG Nominating Committee gave it a standing ovation after a screening.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>The score received a mixed critical reaction. Callum Hofler of Entertainment Junkie stated, "At its finest, <i>Unbroken</i> is perhaps Desplat's strongest and most resonant emotional work since <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tree_of_Life_(film)" title="The Tree of Life (film)">The Tree of Life</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows_%E2%80%93_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 2</a></i>, both from 2011. It comes off as bold, ambitious, yet intimate and sentimental all the same. It can be an elegant and harmonious exploration of human determination, drive and spirit." He also criticised numerous components, claiming that, "In most cases though, the primary issue with the album is its lack of energy and vitality. There is many a time where the music seems to just sit in place, lacking major progression in character, motive or mindset." He awarded the score a final rating of 6 out of 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Jorn Tillnes of Soundtrack Geek acclaimed the album, stating, "This score is pretty great. It's been a really good year for Desplat. <i><a href="/wiki/Godzilla_(2014_film)" title="Godzilla (2014 film)">Godzilla</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Monuments_Men" title="The Monuments Men">The Monuments Men</a></i> at the top of the pile, but this is not far behind." He summarized with, "It is a turning point though for those who think Desplat is about boring bass rhythms and motifs. This might even get the haters to respect him as a composer." He awarded the score an 87.8 out of 100.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unbroken_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Prior to the film's release, some Japanese nationalists asked for the film and the director to be banned from their country, largely because of a part in Hillenbrand's book, which was not depicted in the film, where she describes "POWs were beaten, burned, stabbed, or clubbed to death, shot, beheaded, killed during medical experiments, or eaten alive in ritual acts of cannibalism" by the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Army" title="Imperial Japanese Army">Japanese Army</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> A petition on <a href="/wiki/Change.org" title="Change.org">Change.org</a> calling for a ban attracted more than 10,000 signatures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ishikawa_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-ishikawa-29">[29]</a></sup> In response, it triggered a Change.org petition by <a href="/wiki/Indo_people" title="Indo people">Dutch Indonesian</a> group The Indo Project voicing support for the movie, as they saw it as a reflection of what their family members in the former Dutch East Indies experienced in <a href="/wiki/Japanese_war_crimes#Torture_of_prisoners_of_war" title="Japanese war crimes">Japanese camps</a>. Several prominent <a href="/wiki/Indo_people" title="Indo people">Dutch Indos</a>, including author <a href="/wiki/Adriaan_van_Dis" title="Adriaan van Dis">Adriaan van Dis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doe_Maar" title="Doe Maar">Doe Maar</a> frontman <a href="/wiki/Ernst_Jansz" title="Ernst Jansz">Ernst Jansz</a>, and actress <a href="/wiki/Wieteke_van_Dort" title="Wieteke van Dort">Wieteke van Dort</a>, signed the petition in support of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Another petition on Change.org calling for a release of the film in Japan, this time written in Japanese, gathered more than 1,200 signatures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ishikawa_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-ishikawa-29">[29]</a></sup> The film was eventually released in Japan on February 6, 2016 by independent distributor Bitters End on a much smaller scale than originally intended, while <a href="/wiki/Toho" title="Toho">Toho-Towa</a>, the usual distributor of Universal titles, had passed on releasing the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film received some criticism for omitting Zamperini's fight against alcoholism and PTSD, as well as his <a href="/wiki/Billy_Graham" title="Billy Graham">Billy Graham</a>-inspired religious conversion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-test_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-test-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
