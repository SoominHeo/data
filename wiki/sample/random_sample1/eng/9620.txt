<p><b>Earl Boen</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'b' in 'buy'">b</span><span title="/oʊ/ long 'o' in 'code'">oʊ</span><span title="/ɪ/ or /ə/ 'e' in 'roses'">ᵻ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born November 7, 1945) is an American actor and voice actor. He has starred in films, television series, animated series, radio programs and video games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>He is perhaps best known as criminal psychologist Dr. Peter Silberman in the <a href="/wiki/Terminator_(franchise)" title="Terminator (franchise)"><i>Terminator</i> series</a>. Boen reprised the role in both <i><a href="/wiki/Terminator_2:_Judgment_Day" title="Terminator 2: Judgment Day">Terminator 2</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Terminator_3:_Rise_of_the_Machines" title="Terminator 3: Rise of the Machines">3</a></i>, making him the only actor besides <a href="/wiki/Arnold_Schwarzenegger" title="Arnold Schwarzenegger">Arnold Schwarzenegger</a> to appear in the first three <i>Terminator</i> films as the same character.</p>
<p>Earl Boen is also very active in the voice-over field. He has played Mr. Bleakman in <i><a href="/wiki/Clifford_the_Big_Red_Dog_(TV_series)" title="Clifford the Big Red Dog (TV series)">Clifford the Big Red Dog</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Simon_Stagg" title="Simon Stagg">Simon Stagg</a> on several episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Justice_League_(TV_series)" title="Justice League (TV series)">Justice League</a></i>, Police Chief Kanifky in <i><a href="/wiki/Bonkers_(TV_series)" title="Bonkers (TV series)">Bonkers</a></i>, and many characters on multiple other animated shows and films.</p>

<p>Boen was born in New York City. Having started acting in the mid-1950s, he gained roles on the stage in several leading repertory companies between 1965 and 1976 before moving to Hollywood.</p>
<p>Boen is known to many TV viewers as the Harper family's pastor, Rev. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lloyd_Meechum" title="Lloyd Meechum">Lloyd Meechum</a> on the 1980s sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Mama%27s_Family" title="Mama's Family">Mama's Family</a></i>. His character was featured in several of the most memorable episodes (on both the network and syndicated versions). He also appeared as a clergyman in episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Golden_Girls" title="The Golden Girls">The Golden Girls</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Golden_Palace" title="The Golden Palace">The Golden Palace</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wonder_Years" title="The Wonder Years">The Wonder Years</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Boy_Meets_World" title="Boy Meets World">Boy Meets World</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Seinfeld" title="Seinfeld">Seinfeld</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Three%27s_Company" title="Three's Company">Three's Company</a></i>. He also appeared as Willie's boss in <i><a href="/wiki/ALF_(TV_series)" title="ALF (TV series)">ALF</a></i>, and as Patrick Morrison in the <i><a href="/wiki/Matlock_(TV_series)" title="Matlock (TV series)">Matlock</a></i> episode "The Cult".</p>
<p>Other films in which he appeared include <i><a href="/wiki/The_Main_Event_(1979_film)" title="The Main Event (1979 film)">The Main Event</a></i> (1979), <i><a href="/wiki/Battle_Beyond_the_Stars" title="Battle Beyond the Stars">Battle Beyond the Stars</a></i> (1980), <i><a href="/wiki/9_to_5_(film)" title="9 to 5 (film)">9 to 5</a></i> (1980), <i>Soggy Bottom, U.S.A.</i> (1981), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Man_with_Two_Brains" title="The Man with Two Brains">The Man with Two Brains</a></i> (1983), <i><a href="/wiki/To_Be_or_Not_to_Be_(1983_film)" title="To Be or Not to Be (1983 film)">To Be or Not to Be</a></i> (1983), <i><a href="/wiki/Alien_Nation_(film)" title="Alien Nation (film)">Alien Nation</a></i> (1988), <i><a href="/wiki/Marked_for_Death" title="Marked for Death">Marked for Death</a></i> (1990), <i><a href="/wiki/Naked_Gun_33%E2%85%93:_The_Final_Insult" title="Naked Gun 33⅓: The Final Insult">Naked Gun 33⅓: The Final Insult</a></i> (1994), and <i><a href="/wiki/Nutty_Professor_II:_The_Klumps" title="Nutty Professor II: The Klumps">Nutty Professor II: The Klumps</a></i> (2000).</p>
<p>Boen commonly plays doctors in movie and television roles. He may also be noted for his role as Jim Petersen, Angela Bower's boss on the show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Who%27s_The_Boss%3F" title="Who's The Boss?">Who's The Boss?</a></i>. Earl had a recurring role as Dr. Kramer in the 1990s Fox network cult classic comedy TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Get_a_Life_(TV_series)" title="Get a Life (TV series)">Get a Life</a></i>. In 1987, came his first voice role as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taurus_(G.I._Joe)" title="Taurus (G.I. Joe)">Taurus</a> in the direct to video <i><a href="/wiki/G.I._Joe:_The_Movie" title="G.I. Joe: The Movie">G.I. Joe: The Movie</a></i>.</p>
<p>Some of his most well known voice roles have been villainous pirate <a href="/wiki/LeChuck" title="LeChuck">LeChuck</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Monkey_Island_(series)" title="Monkey Island (series)"><i>Monkey Island</i></a> series of adventure games. He has also provided the introductions for <i><a href="/wiki/World_of_Warcraft" title="World of Warcraft">World of Warcraft</a></i> and its expansions, voiced Magtheridon in <i><a href="/wiki/World_of_Warcraft:_The_Burning_Crusade" title="World of Warcraft: The Burning Crusade">World of Warcraft: The Burning Crusade</a></i> and King Terenas Menethil II in <i><a href="/wiki/World_of_Warcraft:_Wrath_of_the_Lich_King" title="World of Warcraft: Wrath of the Lich King">World of Warcraft: Wrath of the Lich King</a></i>, including the ending cinematic for that expansion.</p>
<p>Boen retired from screen acting in 2003, but continues his work as a voice actor in radio, television cartoons and video games.</p>
