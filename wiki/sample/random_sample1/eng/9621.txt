<p><b>Alana de la Garza</b> (born June 18, 1976) is an American actress. She is best known for her roles as <a href="/wiki/Connie_Rubirosa" title="Connie Rubirosa">Connie Rubirosa</a> on the NBC television series <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order" title="Law &amp; Order">Law &amp; Order</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_LA" title="Law &amp; Order: LA">Law &amp; Order: LA</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i>, and Marisol Delko-Caine on <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Miami" title="CSI: Miami">CSI: Miami</a></i>. In 2014 and 2015, she starred as Detective Jo Martinez in the ABC series <i><a href="/wiki/Forever_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Forever (U.S. TV series)">Forever</a></i>. Since 2016, she has starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Criminal_Minds:_Beyond_Borders" title="Criminal Minds: Beyond Borders">Criminal Minds: Beyond Borders</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/Clara_Seger" title="Clara Seger">Special Agent Clara Seger</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alana_de_la_Garza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>De la Garza was born in <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">Columbus, Ohio</a>, to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican-American" title="Mexican-American">Mexican-American</a> father and an Irish-American mother. She was raised in <a href="/wiki/El_Paso,_Texas" title="El Paso, Texas">El Paso</a>, Texas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She won the "Miss Photogenic" title in the <a href="/wiki/Miss_Texas_Teen_USA" title="Miss Texas Teen USA">Miss El Paso Teen USA</a> local beauty pageant. She then became a special-needs teacher and enrolled at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Texas_at_El_Paso" title="University of Texas at El Paso">University of Texas at El Paso</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> studying <a href="/wiki/Physical_therapy" title="Physical therapy">physical therapy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Social_work" title="Social work">social work</a>. Upon graduating, she landed some small roles while living in <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando, Florida</a>, then soon after moved to New York City.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alana_de_la_Garza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2001, she was featured in the music video for Brooks &amp; Dunn's song "<a href="/wiki/Ain%27t_Nothing_%27bout_You" title="Ain't Nothing 'bout You">Ain't Nothing 'bout You</a>". Garza also appeared in the music video "<a href="/wiki/All_or_Nothing_(O-Town_song)" title="All or Nothing (O-Town song)">All or Nothing</a>" released by the boy band O-Town in 2001. She obtained the role of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rosa_Santos" title="Rosa Santos">Rosa Santos</a> on the soap opera, <i><a href="/wiki/All_My_Children" title="All My Children">All My Children</a></i>, guest-starring roles in <i><a href="/wiki/JAG_(TV_series)" title="JAG (TV series)">JAG</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Charmed" title="Charmed">Charmed</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Two_and_a_Half_Men" title="Two and a Half Men">Two and a Half Men</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_(TV_series)" title="Las Vegas (TV series)">Las Vegas</a></i>. She starred as Maria in the short-lived television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mountain_(TV_series)" title="The Mountain (TV series)">The Mountain</a></i>. In 2006, she starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Fix_It_(2006_film)" title="Mr. Fix It (2006 film)">Mr. Fix-It</a></i> co-starring <a href="/wiki/David_Boreanaz" title="David Boreanaz">David Boreanaz</a>. She also guest-starred as an evil <a href="/wiki/Krypton_(comics)" title="Krypton (comics)">Kryptonian</a> (named "Aethyr" in promotional materials) in the <a href="/wiki/Smallville_(season_5)" title="Smallville (season 5)">Season 5</a> premiere episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Smallville" title="Smallville">Smallville</a></i> and played the recurring role of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marisol_Delko_Caine" title="Marisol Delko Caine">Marisol Delko Caine</a> on the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Miami" title="CSI: Miami">CSI: Miami</a></i>. She has twice been featured in the "Girls of <i><a href="/wiki/Maxim_(magazine)" title="Maxim (magazine)">Maxim</a></i><span style="padding-left:.2em;">"</span> online gallery.</p>
<p>In 2006, she joined the cast of <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order" title="Law &amp; Order">Law &amp; Order</a></i> during the premiere of Season 17, portraying <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Assistant_District_Attorney" title="Assistant District Attorney">Assistant District Attorney</a> <a href="/wiki/Connie_Rubirosa" title="Connie Rubirosa">Connie Rubirosa</a>. Her performance as ADA Rubirosa has been widely praised by critics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2007, she earned a nomination for "Best Supporting Actress—Television" by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imagen_Foundation_Awards" title="Imagen Foundation Awards">Imagen Foundation Awards</a>. Following in 2008, she was nominated for an <a href="/wiki/ALMA_Award" title="ALMA Award">ALMA Award</a> as "Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Television Series".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Later that year, she garnered an "Impact Award" for "Outstanding Performance in a Dramatic Television Series" by the National Hispanic Media Coalition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>De la Garza played Assistant District Attorney (later DDA) Rubirosa for the final four seasons of <i>Law &amp; Order</i>, a role she also played on the short-lived spin-off <i>Law &amp; Order: LA</i> (2011). On January 22, 2014 she guest starred in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i> as Connie Rubirosa. Rubirosa, who had recently departed the Los Angeles District Attorney's Office, is now a federal prosecutor, heading up a joint task force on underage sex trafficking. Her guest appearance originally aired on January 22, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> This is, to date, her final appearance in the franchise.</p>
<p>In July 2011, de la Garza was reported to reprise her role as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marisol_Delko_Caine" title="Marisol Delko Caine">Marisol Delko Caine</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> on <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Miami" title="CSI: Miami">CSI: Miami</a>'</i>s season 10 premiere.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2012, she was cast as a lead in the NBC medical drama pilot <a href="/wiki/Do_No_Harm_(TV_series)" title="Do No Harm (TV series)"><i>Do No Harm</i></a>. She played Dr. Lena Solis, a neurosurgeon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In June 2012, De La Garza was added to the cast of <i><a href="/wiki/Are_You_Here" title="Are You Here">Are You Here</a></i>, the feature film debut of <i><a href="/wiki/Mad_Men" title="Mad Men">Mad Men</a></i> creator Matthew Weiner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014–15 she co-starred as police detective Jo Martinez in the ABC television series <a href="/wiki/Forever_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Forever (U.S. TV series)"><i>Forever</i></a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In September 2015, she began a recurring role as the new head of Homeland Security on <i><a href="/wiki/Scorpion_(TV_series)" title="Scorpion (TV series)">Scorpion</a></i>. She has recently joined the main cast of <i><a href="/wiki/Criminal_Minds:_Beyond_Borders" title="Criminal Minds: Beyond Borders">Criminal Minds: Beyond Borders</a></i>, starring as the female-lead opposite <a href="/wiki/Gary_Sinise" title="Gary Sinise">Gary Sinise</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alana_de_la_Garza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She married her long-time boyfriend, Michael Roberts, a writer, on May 31, 2008, in <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orange_County,_Florida" title="Orange County, Florida">Orange County</a>, Florida. They have a son<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Early.2F_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Early.2F-19">[19]</a></sup> and a daughter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
