<p>The <b>zebra moray</b>, <i>Gymnomuraena zebra</i>, is a species of marine <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muraenidae" title="Muraenidae">Muraenidae</a>. It is the only member of the genus <i><b>Gymnomuraena</b></i>, though it sometimes has been included in <i><a href="/wiki/Echidna_(genus)" title="Echidna (genus)">Echidna</a></i> instead.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zebra_moray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The zebra moray is considered as a medium-sized fish even if it can reach a maximum length of 150 cm. However, the average size commonly observed is more of the order of 50 cm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is densely banded dark and whitish, giving rise to its common name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Its snout is round and short.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_.26_habitat">Distribution &amp; habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zebra_moray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution &amp; habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The zebra moray is widespread throughout the <a href="/wiki/Indo-Pacific" title="Indo-Pacific">Indo-Pacific</a> area from eastern coast of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> until western coast of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_continent" title="American continent">America</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gal%C3%A1pagos" title="Galápagos">Galápagos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The zebra moray is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benthic" title="Benthic">benthic</a> fish, its favorite habitat corresponds to the rocky or coral reef on coastal shallow water between 1 and 39 meters deep.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biology">Biology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zebra_moray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Biology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Gymnomuraena zebra</i> has a nocturnal activity and actively hunt its prey. Unlike most other moray eels, it feeds almost exclusively on <a href="/wiki/Crustacean" title="Crustacean">crustaceans</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_urchins" title="Sea urchins">sea urchins</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mollusk" title="Mollusk">mollusks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
