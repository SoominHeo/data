<p><i><b>Hydrophis cyanocinctus</b></i>, commonly called the <b>annulated sea snake</b> or the <b>blue-banded sea snake</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Venomous" title="Venomous">venomous</a> <a href="/wiki/Hydrophiinae" title="Hydrophiinae">sea snake</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Elapidae" title="Elapidae">Elapidae</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ITIS_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ITIS-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hydrophis_cyanocinctus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Head moderate. Diameter of eye less than its distance from the mouth in the adult. Rostral slightly more broad than deep. Nasals shorter than the frontal, more than twice as long as the suture between the prefrontals. Prefrontals usually in contact with the second upper labial. Frontal more long than broad, as long as its distance from the rostral or the tip of the snout. One preocular and two postoculars. Two superposed anterior temporals. Seven or eight upper labials; third, fourth, and usually fifth entering the eye. Both pairs of chin-shields in contact, or posterior pair separated by one scale.</p>
<p>Body long. Dorsal scales subimbricate, keeled or with two or three tubercles, in 39-45 rows (27-33 anteriorly). Ventrals 281-385, smooth or with two or more tubercles.</p>
<p>Greenish-olive above, with blackish or olive transverse bars or annuli, broadest on the back, sometimes connected by a black band along the belly; or yellowish, with a black vertebral band and a few black bars on the neck.</p>
<p>Length of head and body 1360Â mm; tail 140Â mm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hydrophis_cyanocinctus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Found in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> (From the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">Myanmar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>: <a href="/wiki/Visayan_Sea" title="Visayan Sea">Visayan Sea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Panay" title="Panay">Panay</a>, etc.) and the marine waters around <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">Solomon Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a> (including <a href="/wiki/Hainan" title="Hainan">Hainan</a>), <a href="/wiki/East_China_Sea" title="East China Sea">East China Sea</a> (including <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>), coastal regions of <a href="/wiki/Shandong" title="Shandong">Shandong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liaoning" title="Liaoning">Liaoning</a> (<a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>) coasts of <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a> (<a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>), east through <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a> until <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea" title="New Guinea">New Guinea</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behavior">Behavior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hydrophis_cyanocinctus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behavior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Hydrophis cyanocinctus</i> inhabits shallow coastal waters. It is often accidentally caught by <a href="/wiki/Prawn" title="Prawn">prawn</a> <a href="/wiki/Fishing_trawler" title="Fishing trawler">trawlers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diet">Diet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hydrophis_cyanocinctus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Diet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It feeds on <a href="/wiki/Marine_invertebrates" title="Marine invertebrates">marine invertebrates</a> and also on various groups of <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a> such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eels" title="Eels">eels</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gobies" title="Gobies">gobies</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Das.2C_2006_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Das.2C_2006-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>This <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ovoviviparous" title="Ovoviviparous">ovoviviparous</a>. The young are born alive in broods of 3-16.
