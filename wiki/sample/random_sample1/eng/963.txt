<p><b>Football Club Verbroedering Dender Eendracht Hekelgem</b>, also simply known as Dender or Verbroedering Dender, is a <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgian</a> association football club based in <a href="/wiki/Denderleeuw" title="Denderleeuw">Denderleeuw</a>. The club is named after former clubs <a href="/wiki/K.F.C._Denderleeuw_Eendracht_Hekelgem" title="K.F.C. Denderleeuw Eendracht Hekelgem">KFC Denderleeuw Eendracht Hekelgem</a> and <b>Verbroedering Denderhoutem</b>, which merged at the end of the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_in_Belgian_football" title="2004–05 in Belgian football">2004–05 season</a>, and after the river <a href="/wiki/Dender" title="Dender">Dender</a>, which crosses the town of Denderleeuw. The home stadium of the club is <a href="/wiki/Florent_Beeckmanstadion" title="Florent Beeckmanstadion">Florent Beeckmanstadion</a> in Denderleeuw. Their best league ranking was the 15th place in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_Pro_League" title="Belgian Pro League">first division</a> (in <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Belgian_First_Division" title="2007–08 Belgian First Division">2007–08</a> and <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Belgian_First_Division" title="2008–09 Belgian First Division">2008–09</a>). The club colours are red, blue and black.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F.C._Verbroedering_Dender_Eendracht_Hekelgem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Verbroedering Denderhoutem was founded in 1935<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and registered to the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Belgian_Football_Association" title="Royal Belgian Football Association">Royal Belgian Football Association</a> in 1943. They first reached the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_Promotion" title="Belgian Promotion">Promotion</a> (4th-highest level in Belgian football) in the 1980s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> After several relegations and promotions in the provincial leagues and Promotion, the club finally reached the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Third_Division" title="Belgian Third Division">third division</a> in <a href="/wiki/1998%E2%80%9399_in_Belgian_football" title="1998–99 in Belgian football">1998–99</a>. They remained at that level except for one season in <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_in_Belgian_football" title="2003–04 in Belgian football">2003–04</a> when they played in the Promotion. After one season back at the third level, Denderhoutem merged with fellow club KFC Denderleeuw EH from the third division.</p>
<p>Verbroedering Dender won consecutively the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Third_Division_A" title="Belgian Third Division A">third division A</a> title (in <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_in_Belgian_football" title="2005–06 in Belgian football">2005–06</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Second_Division" title="Belgian Second Division">second division</a> title (in <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_in_Belgian_football" title="2006–07 in Belgian football">2006–07</a>), to reach the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_Pro_League" title="Belgian Pro League">first division</a> in <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Belgian_First_Division" title="2007–08 Belgian First Division">2007–08</a> for the first time in their history. They achieved promotion after beating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dessel_Sport" title="Dessel Sport">Dessel</a> 0–2 on the second division's 33rd match day on 13 May 2007. They finished at the 15th place in 2007–08 and again in <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Belgian_First_Division" title="2008–09 Belgian First Division">2008–09</a>, though the club was relegated at the end of the 2008–09 season because the number of clubs in the first division was reduced from 18 to 16.</p>
