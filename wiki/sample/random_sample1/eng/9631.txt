<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Uhm_Ji-won&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Uhm Ji-won made her acting debut in the late 1990s, and after an early role in the Korean <a href="/wiki/Tokusatsu" title="Tokusatsu">tokusatsu</a> series <i>Vectorman</i>, went on to appear in a number of films and <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">television drama series</a>. In 2004, she appeared alongside <a href="/wiki/Han_Suk-kyu" title="Han Suk-kyu">Han Suk-kyu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lee_Eun-ju" title="Lee Eun-ju">Lee Eun-ju</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Scarlet_Letter_(2004_film)" title="The Scarlet Letter (2004 film)">The Scarlet Letter</a></i>, receiving a nomination for Best Supporting Actress at the <a href="/wiki/Blue_Dragon_Film_Awards" title="Blue Dragon Film Awards">Blue Dragon Film Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cinemaise_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cinemaise-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2005, Uhm played a leading role in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Sang-soo" title="Hong Sang-soo">Hong Sang-soo</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Tale_of_Cinema" title="Tale of Cinema">Tale of Cinema</a></i>, and was praised for giving an "engaging, emotionally nuanced dual performance" as fictional actress Choi Young-shil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The film required her to perform her first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nude_scene" title="Nude scene">nude scene</a>, and she later remarked that, "After stripping in front of the camera, I felt that I could now take any role."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Uhm starred alongside <a href="/wiki/Yoo_Ji-tae" title="Yoo Ji-tae">Yoo Ji-tae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kim_Ji-soo_(actress)" title="Kim Ji-soo (actress)">Kim Ji-soo</a> in the 2006 film <i><a href="/wiki/Traces_of_Love" title="Traces of Love">Traces of Love</a></i>, portraying a survivor of the 1995 <a href="/wiki/Sampoong_Department_Store_collapse" title="Sampoong Department Store collapse">Sampoong Department Store collapse</a> who still suffers from <a href="/wiki/Psychological_trauma" title="Psychological trauma">psychological trauma</a> years later. She prepared for the role by studying news and documentaries of the event, as well as reading through various <a href="/wiki/Psychology" title="Psychology">psychology</a> texts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Uhm was once again nominated for Best Supporting Actress at the Blue Dragon Film Awards,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Korea_Society_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Korea_Society-5">[5]</a></sup> and later won the same category at the <a href="/wiki/Chunsa_Film_Art_Awards" title="Chunsa Film Art Awards">Chunsa Film Art Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Naver_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Naver-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>After a <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">cameo</a> in director <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jee-woon" title="Kim Jee-woon">Kim Jee-woon</a>'s 2008 epic western <i><a href="/wiki/The_Good,_the_Bad,_the_Weird" title="The Good, the Bad, the Weird">The Good, the Bad, the Weird</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and a leading role in another Hong Sang-soo <a href="/wiki/Art_film" title="Art film">art film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Like_You_Know_It_All" title="Like You Know It All">Like You Know It All</a></i>, she returned to lighter fare in 2010 with the romantic comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Woman_Who_Still_Wants_to_Marry" title="The Woman Who Still Wants to Marry">The Woman Who Still Wants to Marry</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Uhm appeared an episode of <a href="/wiki/BBC_World_News" title="BBC World News">BBC World</a>'s <i>The Third Eye</i>, a documentary series that spotlights up-and-coming countries. The installment of the eight-part segment on Korea is interspersed with interviews with Uhm giving her opinions on Korean culture, domestic movies and the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Wave" title="Korean Wave">Korean Wave</a>. It also featured footage from her 2010 sex comedy movie <i><a href="/wiki/Foxy_Festival" title="Foxy Festival">Foxy Festival</a></i>. Uhm, who was interviewed in Korea, was chosen in light of her impressive acting skills, natural charm and English fluency, producers said.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 2012 to 2013, she starred in family dramas by renowned TV writer <a href="/wiki/Kim_Soo-hyun_(writer)" title="Kim Soo-hyun (writer)">Kim Soo-hyun</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Childless_Comfort" title="Childless Comfort">Childless Comfort</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Thrice_Married_Woman" title="Thrice Married Woman">Thrice Married Woman</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> This was followed by a well-received supporting performance in the gangster comedy <i>Man on the Edge</i>.</p>
<p>Uhm then played the mother of a <a href="/wiki/Rape" title="Rape">sexually assaulted</a> child in <i><a href="/wiki/Hope_(2013_film)" title="Hope (2013 film)">Hope</a></i>; she said it was the first movie where she let go of everything, calling it one of her best works.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Uhm won Best Actress at the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Association_of_Film_Critics_Awards" title="Korean Association of Film Critics Awards">Korean Association of Film Critics Awards</a>, and received nominations at the Blue Dragon Film Awards and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baeksang_Arts_Awards" title="Baeksang Arts Awards">Baeksang Arts Awards</a>. This was followed by period mystery-thriller <i><a href="/wiki/The_Silenced" title="The Silenced">The Silenced</a></i> and kidnapping drama <i>Missing Child</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Uhm_Ji-won&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Uhm began dating architect Oh Young-wook in 2013; Oh is the founder of well-known architectural firm ogisadesign d'espacio architects (oddaa), and has also published several books on travel and art.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> They married on May 27, 2014 at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shilla_Hotel" title="Shilla Hotel">Shilla Hotel</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
