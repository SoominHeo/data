<p><i><b>Behind Enemy Lines: Colombia</b></i> is a 2009 <a href="/wiki/War_film" title="War film">war film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Tim_Matheson" title="Tim Matheson">Tim Matheson</a> and starring <a href="/wiki/Joe_Manganiello" title="Joe Manganiello">Joe Manganiello</a>, former <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a> and <a href="/wiki/Total_Nonstop_Action_Wrestling" title="Total Nonstop Action Wrestling">TNA</a> wrestler <a href="/wiki/Ken_Anderson_(wrestler)" title="Ken Anderson (wrestler)">Ken Anderson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keith_David" title="Keith David">Keith David</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tim_Matheson" title="Tim Matheson">Tim Matheson</a>. It is the third installment in the series, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Sequel" title="Sequel">sequel</a> to <i><a href="/wiki/Behind_Enemy_Lines_(2001_film)" title="Behind Enemy Lines (2001 film)">Behind Enemy Lines</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Behind_Enemy_Lines_II:_Axis_of_Evil" title="Behind Enemy Lines II: Axis of Evil">Behind Enemy Lines II: Axis of Evil</a></i>. The film was co-produced by <a href="/wiki/WWE_Studios" title="WWE Studios">WWE Studios</a>, written by <a href="/wiki/Tobias_Iaconis" title="Tobias Iaconis">Tobias Iaconis</a> and released <a href="/wiki/Direct-to-video" title="Direct-to-video">direct-to-video</a> on January 6, 2009.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Behind_Enemy_Lines:_Colombia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a> is in chaos, as a five-man team of US <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy_SEALs" title="United States Navy SEALs">Navy SEALS</a> embark on a secret mission to ensure that peace talks between the country's government and insurgent guerrillas don't erupt into violence, an unforeseen complication threatens all out war. Out of nowhere, the meeting falls under attack and the leaders from both sides are killed. The SEALS have been framed for the crime, leaving them to fight for their lives from behind enemy lines against the <a href="/wiki/Agrupaci%C3%B3n_de_Fuerzas_Especiales_Antiterroristas_Urbanas" title="Agrupación de Fuerzas Especiales Antiterroristas Urbanas">Colombian Special Forces (AFEUR)</a>. Abandoned by their government and left for dead, the weary soldiers race to uncover the evidence that will prove their innocence while ensuring that the violence is contained. Should the fighting spill over the border, the entire region or both sides could be plunged into a nightmarish inferno of war and death.</p>
