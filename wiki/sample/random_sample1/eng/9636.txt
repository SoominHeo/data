<p><i><b>Enemy of the State</b></i> is a 1998 American spy-<a href="/wiki/Conspiracy_fiction" title="Conspiracy fiction">thriller</a> about a group of U.S. <a href="/wiki/National_Security_Agency" title="National Security Agency">National Security Agency</a> agents conspiring to kill a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">U.S. Congressman</a> and the cover up that ensues after a tape of the murder is discovered. It was written by <a href="/wiki/David_Marconi" title="David Marconi">David Marconi</a>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Tony_Scott" title="Tony Scott">Tony Scott</a>, and produced by <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Bruckheimer" title="Jerry Bruckheimer">Jerry Bruckheimer</a>. It stars <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">Will Smith</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gene_Hackman" title="Gene Hackman">Gene Hackman</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Jon_Voight" title="Jon Voight">Jon Voight</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Bonet" title="Lisa Bonet">Lisa Bonet</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Regina_King" title="Regina King">Regina King</a> in supporting roles.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enemy_of_the_State_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>NSA official Thomas Bryan Reynolds meets with U.S. Congressman Phil Hammersley in a public park to discuss support for a new <a href="/wiki/Counter-terrorism" title="Counter-terrorism">counter-terrorism</a> legislation the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Congress" title="U.S. Congress">U.S. Congress</a> is pushing that dramatically expands the <a href="/wiki/Surveillance" title="Surveillance">surveillance</a> powers of intelligence agencies over individuals and groups. Hammersley remains committed to blocking its passage, since he believes it would almost totally destroy the privacy of American citizens. Reynolds, determined to have the bill pass so as to gain a long-delayed and anticipated promotion within the NSA, has his team murder Hammersley, spread heart pills over his car, place him in the car and push it in a lake to make it look like he had a heart attack. In the aftermath, they discover that wildlife researcher Daniel Zavits had a camera aimed in the woods at their location. Zavits inspects the footage and, realizing he has captured the congressman's murder, calls a journalist he knows. The call is monitored by Reynolds's team who attempt to break into Zavits' apartment to retrieve the tape. Realising he is in danger, Zavits transfers the video to a disc before fleeing the apartment ahead of Reynolds's men.</p>
<p>Zavits bumps into an old college friend, labour lawyer Robert Clayton Dean, and slips the disc into his shopping bag without his knowledge. Shortly after, Zavits is killed when he runs into the street in front of a fire truck. When the NSA discovers that Dean might have the video, Reynolds's team raids his house and plants surveillance devices. They then disseminate false evidence to implicate Dean of working with the mob family of Paulie Pintero and having an affair with ex-girlfriend Rachel Banks. The subterfuge destroys Dean's life: he is dismissed from his job, his bank accounts are frozen, and his wife Carla throws him out of the house.</p>
<p>Dean believes Pintero is behind the <a href="/wiki/Smear_campaign" title="Smear campaign">smear campaign</a> as revenge for a prior case, with help from Banks' secretive contact Brill. Dean sets up a meet with Brill, to which the NSA sends an impostor, but the real Brill rescues Dean. Brill explains that his pursuers are NSA agents and rids him of tracking devices hidden in his clothing. With Dean and Brill in hiding, the NSA agents kill Banks and frame Dean for the murder.</p>
<p>Dean obtains the disc and Brill identifies Reynolds in the recovered video, but the disc is destroyed during an escape from an NSA raid. Brill, whose real name is Edward Lyle, tells Dean of his past as a communications expert; he was stationed in Iran during the <a href="/wiki/Iranian_Revolution" title="Iranian Revolution">Iranian Revolution</a>; his partner, Rachel's father, was killed, but Lyle made it out and has been in hiding since. Lyle tries to coax Dean into running away, but Dean is adamant about clearing his name.</p>
<p>Dean and Lyle trail another supporter of the surveillance bill, U.S. Congressman Sam Albert, videotaping him having an affair with his aide. Dean and Lyle "hide" one of the NSA's bugs in Albert's room so Albert will find it and have the NSA start an investigation into Albert's tapping. Lyle also deposits money into Reynolds's bank account to make it appear that he is taking bribes, putting pressure on Reynolds.</p>
<p>Lyle contacts Reynolds to set up a meeting to exchange the video and get Reynolds to incriminate himself. Reynolds' men instead ambush the meeting and hold Lyle and Dean at gunpoint, demanding the tape. Dean tells them that the Hammersley murder footage is in the hands of Pintero, knowing Pintero's restaurant is under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FBI" title="FBI">FBI</a> surveillance. Dean, Reynolds, and the NSA team head into Pintero's restaurant. Using ambiguous language, Dean convinces Pintero that Reynolds is after the incriminating video Dean blackmailed him with and the encounter turns into a massive gunfight that kills the mobsters, Reynolds, and several of his NSA team. Lyle escapes while the FBI rescues Dean and uncovers the entire conspiracy.</p>
<p>The U.S. Congress is forced to abandon the bill to avoid a national scandal, though they cover up the NSA's involvement to preserve the agency's reputation. Dean is cleared of all charges and is reunited with his wife. Lyle leaves Dean a "goodbye" message via his TV as he's watching, showing himself relaxing in a tropical location.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enemy_of_the_State_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The story is set in both <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore</a>, and most of the filming was done in Baltimore. Location shooting began on a ferry in <a href="/wiki/Fell%27s_Point,_Baltimore" title="Fell's Point, Baltimore">Fell's Point</a>. In mid-January, the company moved to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> to complete production in April 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mel_Gibson" title="Mel Gibson">Mel Gibson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Cruise" title="Tom Cruise">Tom Cruise</a> were considered for the part that went to Will Smith, who took the role largely because he wanted to work with Gene Hackman, and had previously enjoyed working with producer <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Bruckheimer" title="Jerry Bruckheimer">Jerry Bruckheimer</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Bad_Boys_(1995_film)" title="Bad Boys (1995 film)">Bad Boys</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/George_Clooney" title="George Clooney">George Clooney</a> was also considered for a role in the film. <a href="/wiki/Sean_Connery" title="Sean Connery">Sean Connery</a> was considered for the role that went to Hackman. The film's crew included a <a href="/wiki/Technical_surveillance_counter-measures" title="Technical surveillance counter-measures">technical surveillance counter-measures</a> consultant who also had a minor role as a spy shop merchant. Hackman had previously acted in a similar thriller about spying and surveillance, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Conversation" title="The Conversation">The Conversation</a></i> (1974).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enemy_of_the_State_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Enemy of the State</i> received 71% positive reviews on the film-critics aggregator <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, with 84 critics surveyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> displayed a normalized ranking of 67 out of 100 on the basis of 22 critics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Turan" title="Kenneth Turan">Kenneth Turan</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> expressed enjoyment in the movie, noting how its "pizazz [overcame] occasional lapses in moment-to-moment plausibility;"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Janet_Maslin" title="Janet Maslin">Janet Maslin</a> of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Times" title="New York Times">New York Times</a></i> approved of the film's action-packed sequences, but cited how it was similar in manner to the rest of the members of "Simpson's and Bruckheimer's school of empty but sensation-packed filming."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In a combination of the two's views, Edvins Beitiks of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Examiner" title="San Francisco Examiner">San Francisco Examiner</a></i> praised many of the movie's development aspects, but criticized the overall concept that drove the film from the beginning—the efficiency of government intelligence—as unrealistic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kim_Newman" title="Kim Newman">Kim Newman</a> considered <i>Enemy of the State</i> a "continuation of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Conversation" title="The Conversation">The Conversation</a></i>", the 1974 psychological thriller that starred Hackman as a paranoid, isolated surveillance expert.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enemy_of_the_State_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film opened at #2, behind <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rugrats_Movie" title="The Rugrats Movie">The Rugrats Movie</a></i>, grossing $20,038,573 over its first weekend in 2,393 theatres and averaging about $8,374 per venue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Real_life">Real life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enemy_of_the_State_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Real life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>An episode of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_Broadcasting_Service" title="Public Broadcasting Service">PBS</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Nova_(TV_series)" title="Nova (TV series)">Nova</a></i> titled "Spy Factory" reports that the film's portrayal of the NSA's capabilities are fiction: although the agency can intercept transmissions, connecting the dots is difficult.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> However, in 2001, then-NSA director Gen. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Hayden_(general)" title="Michael Hayden (general)">Michael Hayden</a>, who was appointed to the position during the release of the film, told <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">CNN</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Kyra_Phillips" title="Kyra Phillips">Kyra Phillips</a> that "I made the judgment that we couldn't survive with the popular impression of this agency being formed by the last Will Smith movie."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/James_Risen" title="James Risen">James Risen</a> wrote in his 2006 book <i><a href="/wiki/State_of_War:_The_Secret_History_of_the_CIA_and_the_Bush_Administration" title="State of War: The Secret History of the CIA and the Bush Administration">State of War: The Secret History of the CIA and the Bush Administration</a></i> that Hayden "was appalled" by the film's depiction of the NSA, and sought to counter it with a PR campaign on behalf of the agency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2013 the NSA's <a href="/wiki/PRISM_(surveillance_program)" title="PRISM (surveillance program)">PRISM</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boundless_Informant" title="Boundless Informant">Boundless Informant</a> programs for domestic and international surveillance were uncovered by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_Post" title="Washington Post">Washington Post</a></i> as the result of information provided by whistleblower <a href="/wiki/Edward_Snowden" title="Edward Snowden">Edward Snowden</a>. This information revealed capabilities such as collection of Internet browsing, email and telephone data of not only many Americans, but citizens of other nations as well. <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a>'</i>s John Patterson opined that Hollywood depictions of NSA surveillance, including <i>Enemy of the State</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Echelon_Conspiracy" title="Echelon Conspiracy">Echelon Conspiracy</a></i>, had "softened" up the American public to "the notion that our spending habits, our location, our every movement and conversation, are visible to others whose motives we cannot know."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="TV_series">TV series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enemy_of_the_State_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: TV series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
