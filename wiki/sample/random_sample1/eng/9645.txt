<p><i><b>East of Eden</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">에덴의 동쪽</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Eden-ui Dong-jjok</span></i>) is a 2008 <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> television series, starring <a href="/wiki/Song_Seung-heon" title="Song Seung-heon">Song Seung-heon</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Yeon_Jung-hoon" title="Yeon Jung-hoon">Yeon Jung-hoon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Park_Hae-jin" title="Park Hae-jin">Park Hae-jin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Da-hae" title="Lee Da-hae">Lee Da-hae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Han_Ji-hye" title="Han Ji-hye">Han Ji-hye</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Lee_Yeon-hee" title="Lee Yeon-hee">Lee Yeon-hee</a>. It was produced by Chorokbaem Media as a 47th Anniversary Special Project Drama for <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">MBC</a>, on which it aired from August 25, 2008 to March 10, 2009 on Mondays and Tuesdays at 21:55 for 56 episodes. The ₩25 billion drama tells the story of the brothers Dong-chul (Song) and Dong-wook (Yeon). Their fates diverge after the murder of their coal miner father, with one joining the mob and the other becoming a successful lawyer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East_of_Eden_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A period epic that spans the years 1960 to 2000, <i>East of Eden</i> tells a saga of the bitter rivalry between two men who are eternally bound by fate.</p>
<p>Shin Tae-hwan (<a href="/wiki/Jo_Min-ki" title="Jo Min-ki">Jo Min-ki</a>) is the managing director of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coal_mine" title="Coal mine">coal mine</a> in <a href="/wiki/Taebaek" title="Taebaek">Taebaek</a>. The true embodiment of ruthless ambition, he strives to inherit the coal mining company Taesung Group. In the process, he kills Lee Ki-chul (<a href="/wiki/Lee_Jong-won" title="Lee Jong-won">Lee Jong-won</a>), a miner crusading for worker's rights who has been blocking his path to success. He also seduces Yoo Mi-ae (Shin Eun-jung), a nurse at Taebaek Hospital, only to coldly brush her away after he's tired of her.</p>
<p>At the same moment that Yang Chun-hee (<a href="/wiki/Lee_Mi-sook" title="Lee Mi-sook">Lee Mi-sook</a>), wife of the deceased Ki-chul, is giving birth to a son at Taebaek Hospital, Shin Tae-hwan's wife also gives birth to a baby boy. Filled with rage at Shin Tae-hwan's betrayal, the nurse Mi-ae comes up with a scheme to fulfill her own revenge. She switches the two babies. By doing so, she viciously transforms the fates of not just two lives, but those around them.</p>
<p>Years later, Chun-hee's sons Lee Dong-chul (<a href="/wiki/Song_Seung-heon" title="Song Seung-heon">Song Seung-heon</a>) and Lee Dong-wook (<a href="/wiki/Yeon_Jung-hoon" title="Yeon Jung-hoon">Yeon Jung-hoon</a>) are the closest of brothers. To alleviate their poverty, Dong-chul becomes a gangster, while the younger Dong-wook dreams of becoming a prosecutor to help the family seek revenge on Shin Tae-hwan. Two women enter Dong-chul's life: the smart and sensible Min Hye-rin (<a href="/wiki/Lee_Da-hae" title="Lee Da-hae">Lee Da-hae</a>), and Gook Young-ran (<a href="/wiki/Lee_Yeon-hee" title="Lee Yeon-hee">Lee Yeon-hee</a>), the willful daughter of his gang boss (<a href="/wiki/Yoo_Dong-geun" title="Yoo Dong-geun">Yoo Dong-geun</a>). Meanwhile, Shin Myung-hoon (<a href="/wiki/Park_Hae-jin" title="Park Hae-jin">Park Hae-jin</a>), who's been molded in his father Tae-hwan's spiteful, selfish image, sets his eye on Kim Ji-hyun (<a href="/wiki/Han_Ji-hye" title="Han Ji-hye">Han Ji-hye</a>), Dong-wook's sweetheart.</p>
<p>Until a revelation shakes Dong-chul to his core: that his real brother was raised by Shin Tae-hwan and that the beloved brother that had been beside him all these years is actually the son of his enemy.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_broadcast">International broadcast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East_of_Eden_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International broadcast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The series aired in Japan in 2009 on <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Broadcasting_System" title="Tokyo Broadcasting System">TBS</a> and cable channel DATV.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Selected scenes were also screened in theaters, free of charge, in eight major Japanese cities including Osaka, Sapporo, Fukuoka and Hiroshima over 23 sessions in October 2008. These screenings were sponsored by Hanbang Cosmetics Company to mark lead actor <a href="/wiki/Song_Seung-heon" title="Song Seung-heon">Song Seung-heon</a>'s 32nd birthday; Song was the model of the company's ad campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li>It aired in Vietnam on <a href="/wiki/VTV3" title="VTV3">VTV3</a> from July 23, 2009, under the title <b>Phía đông vườn địa đàng</b>.</li>
<li>It aired in the Philippines on <a href="/wiki/GMA_Network" title="GMA Network">GMA Network</a> from August 16, 2010 until January 13, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li>It aired in Malaysia on <a href="/wiki/TV2_(Malaysia)" title="TV2 (Malaysia)">TV2</a> in 2014.</li>
<li>It aired in Thailand on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/True_Asian_Series" title="True Asian Series">True Asian Series</a> starting May 29, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
