<p><b>Jimmy Kudo</b>, known originally as <b>Shinichi Kudo</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">工藤 新一</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kudō Shin'ichi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, is the <a href="/wiki/Protagonist" title="Protagonist">protagonist</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gosho_Aoyama" title="Gosho Aoyama">Gosho Aoyama</a>'s series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Case_Closed_(manga)" title="Case Closed (manga)">Case Closed</a></i>, which is known in Japan as <i>Meitantei Conan</i> <span style="font-weight:normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">名探偵コナン</span><span class="t_nihongo_help"><sup><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span style="color:#00e; font:bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration:none; padding:0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup></span>, literally Great Detective Conan, officially translated as <i>Detective Conan</i>)</span>. A high school detective, he is forced to ingest the lethal poison <span class="nowraplinks"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/APTX_4869" title="APTX 4869">APTX 4869</a></span> after his encounter with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gin_(Case_Closed)" title="Gin (Case Closed)">Gin</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vodka_(Case_Closed)" title="Vodka (Case Closed)">Vodka</a>. Due to a rare side effect, the poison shrinks him into a child and he adopts the pseudonym <b>Conan Edogawa</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">江戸川 コナン</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Edogawa Konan</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> to hide from those who poisoned him. He moves in with his childhood friend <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rachel_Moore_(Case_Closed)" title="Rachel Moore (Case Closed)">Rachel Moore</a> and her father <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Richard_Moore_(Case_Closed)" title="Richard Moore (Case Closed)">Richard Moore</a> as he awaits the day he can take down the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Organization_(Case_Closed)" title="Black Organization (Case Closed)">Black Organization</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Creation_and_conception">Creation and conception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jimmy_Kudo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Creation and conception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The idea that Jimmy Kudo would be turned into a child stemmed from the idea of a <a href="/wiki/Tortoiseshell_cat" title="Tortoiseshell cat">Tortoiseshell cat</a> <a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes" title="Sherlock Holmes">Sherlock Holmes</a> as a protagonist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fami_2011_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fami_2011-3">[3]</a></sup> Gosho Aoyama's idea was the cat would indicate the crucial evidence needed to solve the case; a performance the child-turned Jimmy does to help those around with the investigation. Jimmy was inspired by the fictional private eye Shunsaku Kudo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Gosho revealed that his editor was against the name Conan due to the protagonist from the anime series <i><a href="/wiki/Future_Boy_Conan" title="Future Boy Conan">Future Boy Conan</a></i> sharing the same name and had suggested to name him Doyle instead. Gosho stuck with the name Conan believing it would overtake the <i>Future Boy</i> series. According to Gosho, Jimmy's catch phrase <i>Shinjitsu wa Itsumo Hitotsu!</i> <span style="font-weight:normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">真実はいつもひとつ!</span><span class="t_nihongo_help"><sup><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span style="color:#00e; font:bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration:none; padding:0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup></span>, literally There is only one truth!)</span> was thought of by the <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> series script writer and drawn into chapter 96 of the <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> at the same time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fami_2011_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Fami_2011-3">[3]</a></sup> In the English localization, the catch phrase was translated as "One Truth Prevails".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CC_48_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-CC_48-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearances">Appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jimmy_Kudo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_Case_Closed_manga_and_anime_series">In the <i>Case Closed</i> manga and anime series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jimmy_Kudo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In the Case Closed manga and anime series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jimmy appears at the beginning of the series as a gifted 16-year-old student at Teitan High School in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He was already well known as a brilliant young detective who had closed many difficult cases that the professionals could not.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> After a date with his childhood friend <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rachel_Moore_(Case_Closed)" title="Rachel Moore (Case Closed)">Rachel Moore</a> to a theme park, Jimmy leaves Rachel and follows two suspicious characters, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gin_(Case_Closed)" title="Gin (Case Closed)">Gin</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vodka_(Case_Closed)" title="Vodka (Case Closed)">Vodka</a>, suspecting they are criminals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Gin ends up attacking Jimmy and forces him to drink an experimental poison APTX (Apotoxin) 4869 <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">四八六九</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yon Hachi Roku Kyū</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> which is supposed to kill him, and then leaves him for dead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-feedingofaptx_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-feedingofaptx-10">[10]</a></sup> However, instead of killing Jimmy, a rare side-effect occurs, causing his age to regress, and he shrinks into the form of a young child.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> With advice from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hiroshi_Agasa" title="Hiroshi Agasa">Dr. Herschel Agasa</a>, Jimmy adopts the identity of Conan Edogawa, devised from the names of <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Conan_Doyle" title="Arthur Conan Doyle">Arthur <i><b>Conan</b></i> Doyle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Edogawa_Ranpo" title="Edogawa Ranpo"><i><b>Edogawa</b></i> Ranpo</a> to prevent Gin, Vodka, and their syndicate, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Organization_(Case_Closed)" title="Black Organization (Case Closed)">Black Organization</a>, from returning to kill him and those around him. For this reason, he chooses not to reveal his true identity to anyone else, to keep them safe.</p>
<p>Jimmy pretends to be a distant relative of Dr. Agasa's and is placed in the care of Rachel and her father, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Richard_Moore_(Case_Closed)" title="Richard Moore (Case Closed)">Richard Moore</a>, who owns a detective agency. Jimmy and Dr. Agasa feel that some clues regarding the Black Organization may possibly filter through the agency, allowing Jimmy to learn more about them to ultimately bring them down.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> As for Jimmy Kudo's disappearance, Conan has to constantly find creative ways to fool Rachel into believing he is off solving a very difficult case and will return once the investigation is concluded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> This becomes increasingly difficult as she begins to suspect who he really is.</p>
<p>As a child, Conan must attend Teitan Elementary, where he inadvertently forms a detective club called the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Case_Closed_characters#Junior_Detective_League" title="List of Case Closed characters">Junior Detective League</a> with friends he makes at the school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Conan is forced to adapt to his new daily life and becomes accustomed to attending elementary school while secretly helping Richard solve crimes with the use of his gadgets invented by Dr. Agasa. The most prominent of these are: his voice-changing bowtie, allowing him to impersonate anyone's voice;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> his customized glasses which allow him to track and listen through his <a href="/wiki/Covert_listening_device" title="Covert listening device">covert listening devices</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> his super sneakers which multiply his kicking force;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> his wrist watch stun gun, which allows him to tranquilize Richard or a criminal;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> his Solar Powered skateboard<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> as well as a soccer ball dispenser belt, super strong/elastic suspenders, among others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ch_380_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ch_380-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Conan eventually meets <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harley_Hartwell_(Case_Closed)" title="Harley Hartwell (Case Closed)">Harley Hartwell</a>, a detective from <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a> who is seeking to challenge Jimmy in a deductive battle. After drinking <a href="/wiki/Baijiu" title="Baijiu">baijiu</a> while sick with a cold, Conan reverts to a teenager and rectifies a deduction made by Harley.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ch_95_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ch_95-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ch_96_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ch_96-22">[22]</a></sup> In their second encounter, Harley is able to deduce Conan's identity as Jimmy Kudo and confronts him about it. The two later become good friends.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ch_121_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ch_121-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>As the series progresses, Conan befriends biochemist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shiho_Miyano_(Case_Closed)" title="Shiho Miyano (Case Closed)">Shiho Miyano</a>, a former member of the Black Organization and inventor of the APTX 4869 poison. She and her sister had grown up within the organization, as their scientist parents had been members too, before their deaths. After her sister was killed by Gin, she took the poison to commit suicide. However, like Jimmy, her age regressed and she shrank back to a small child's size. She then escapes and adopts the pseudonym <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anita_Hailey_(Case_Closed)" title="Anita Hailey (Case Closed)">Anita Hailey</a>. Dr. Agasa ends up taking her in, and she vows to help Conan take down the Black Organization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> She later invents a prototype antidote to the APTX 4869 allowing Conan to temporarily become Jimmy again, which Conan uses on occasion to fool Rachel when she suspects his true identity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ch_258_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ch_258-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ch_260_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ch_260-26">[26]</a></sup> During the events of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holmes%27_no_Mokushiroku" title="Holmes' no Mokushiroku">Holmes' no Mokushiroku</a>, Jimmy, while in his original form, confesses his feelings to Rachel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ch_752_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ch_752-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Conan's investigation of the Black Organization leads him to discover the American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FBI_(Case_Closed)" title="FBI (Case Closed)">FBI</a>'s existence in Japan, as they are also investigating the syndicate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ch_435_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ch_435-28">[28]</a></sup> His collaboration with FBI agents <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jodie_Starling" title="Jodie Starling">Jodie Starling</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shuichi_Akai" title="Shuichi Akai">Shuichi Akai</a> allows them to capture organization member <a href="/wiki/List_of_Case_Closed_characters#Hidemi_Hondo" title="List of Case Closed characters">Kir</a>. They discover she is really an undercover CIA agent and return her to the organization to continue to spy from within.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ch_604_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ch_604-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_other_media">In other media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jimmy_Kudo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In other media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jimmy Kudo has appeared in all of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Case_Closed_films" title="List of Case Closed films">feature films of the series</a>, both the <a href="/wiki/Original_video_animation" title="Original video animation">original video animation</a> series (<a href="/wiki/List_of_Case_Closed_OVAs#Sh.C5.8Dnen_Sunday_Original_animation" title="List of Case Closed OVAs">Shōnen Sunday Original animations</a> and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Case_Closed_OVAs#Magic_File" title="List of Case Closed OVAs">Magic Files</a>), the two-hour cross-over <a href="/wiki/Television_special" title="Television special">television special</a> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lupin_the_3rd_vs_Detective_Conan" title="Lupin the 3rd vs Detective Conan">Lupin the 3rd vs Detective Conan</a></i>, and is the protagonist in all <a href="/wiki/List_of_Case_Closed_video_games" title="List of Case Closed video games"><i>Case Closed</i>-related video games</a>. He is the protagonist in the <a href="/wiki/Novel" title="Novel">novels</a> of the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Novel_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Novel-30">[30]</a></sup> In the 2006–2007 live-action series, he is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Shun_Oguri" title="Shun Oguri">Shun Oguri</a> as a teenager and Nao Fujisaki in child form.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Drama_info_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-Drama_info-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Drama_2_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Drama_2-32">[32]</a></sup> In the 2011 live-action movie and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Kudo_Shinichi_he_no_Chosenjo_episodes" title="List of Kudo Shinichi he no Chosenjo episodes">TV drama series</a>, <a href="/wiki/Junpei_Mizobata" title="Junpei Mizobata">Junpei Mizobata</a> portrays the teenage Jimmy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006, the Japanese government used Conan in campaigns to help promote crime awareness among children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Targeting the same audience, <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_(Japan)" title="Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Japan)">Japan's Ministry of Foreign Affairs</a> used Conan and his friends in two pamphlets: one to promote the ministry's mission, the other to introduce the <a href="/wiki/34th_G8_summit" title="34th G8 summit">34th G8 summit</a> held in the country in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Conan and his friends were also featured in the sixth installment of the Anime, Heroes and Heroines commemorative stamp series issued by <a href="/wiki/Japan_Post" title="Japan Post">Japan Post</a> in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jimmy_Kudo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the survey "friendship" developed by rankingjapan.com in which people had to choose what anime character they would like to have as a friend, Jimmy ranked third.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> In <i><a href="/wiki/Newtype" title="Newtype">Newtype</a></i> magazine Jimmy ranked fourth and ninth in the 2001 and 2010 polls for most popular male anime character, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> In the <i><a href="/wiki/Animage" title="Animage">Animage</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anime_Grand_Prix" title="Anime Grand Prix">Anime Grand Prix</a> awards from 1998, Jimmy was voted as the tenth most popular male anime character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Mania Entertainment rated Conan as the third greatest anime detective.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Jimmy Kudo and Conan Edogawa were the second and third most popular characters in the series defined by a poll on ebooksjapan.jp.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Jian DeLeon of <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a></i> magazine named him eighteenth on a list of "The 25 Most Stylish Anime Characters."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
