<p><b>Edobashi Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">江戸橋駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Edobashi-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station on the <a href="/wiki/Nagoya_Line_(Kintetsu)" title="Nagoya Line (Kintetsu)">Nagoya Line</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tsu,_Mie" title="Tsu, Mie">Tsu, Mie</a>, Japan, operated by the private railway operator <a href="/wiki/Kintetsu_Railway" title="Kintetsu Railway">Kintetsu Railway</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edobashi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Edobashi Station is served by the Kintetsu Nagoya Line, and is 65.3 rail kilometers from the terminus of the line at <a href="/wiki/Kintetsu_Nagoya_Station" title="Kintetsu Nagoya Station">Kintetsu Nagoya Station</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-terada2002_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-terada2002-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edobashi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Edobashi Station has two <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platforms</a> serving four tracks.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edobashi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Edobashi Station opened on January 1, 1917 as a station on the Ise Railway. The Ise Railway became the Ise Electric Railway on September 12, 1926, which merged with the Sangu Express Electric Railway on September 15, 1936. On March 15, 1941, the Sangu Express Electric Railway merged with Osaka Electric Railway to become a station on Kansai Express Railway's Nagoya Line. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kintetsu_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kintetsu-2">[2]</a></sup> This line in turn was merged with the Nankai Electric Railway on June 1, 1944 to form Kintetsu.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kintetsu_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Kintetsu-2">[2]</a></sup> The station was relocated 100 meters north of its former location in June 1959.</p>
