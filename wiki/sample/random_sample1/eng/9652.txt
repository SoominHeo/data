<p><b>Édouard Marie Herriot</b> (<small>French: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[edwaʁ ɛʁjo]</a></span>; 5 July 1872 – 26 March 1957) was a French <a href="/wiki/Radical_Party_(France)" title="Radical Party (France)">Radical</a> politician of the <a href="/wiki/French_Third_Republic" title="French Third Republic">Third Republic</a> who served three times as <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_France" title="Prime Minister of France">Prime Minister</a> and for many years as President of the Chamber of Deputies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-obit-1">[1]</a></sup> He was leader of the first <a href="/wiki/Cartel_des_Gauches" title="Cartel des Gauches">Cartel des Gauches</a>.</p>
<p>Hérriot was born at <a href="/wiki/Troyes" title="Troyes">Troyes</a>, France on 5 July 1872. He served as <a href="/wiki/Mayor_(France)" title="Mayor (France)">Mayor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lyon" title="Lyon">Lyon</a> from 1905 until his death, except for a brief period from 1942 to 1945, when he was exiled to Germany for opposing <a href="/wiki/Vichy_France" title="Vichy France">the Vichy regime</a>. As mayor, Herriot improved relations between municipal government and local unions, increased public assistance funds, and launched an urban renewal programme,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> amongst other measures. He died in <a href="/wiki/Lyon" title="Lyon">Lyon</a> on 26 March 1957.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-obit-1">[1]</a></sup> He is buried at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cimeti%C3%A8re_de_Loyasse" title="Cimetière de Loyasse">Cimetière de Loyasse</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Denial_of_the_Holodomor">Denial of the Holodomor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%89douard_Herriot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Denial of the Holodomor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The height of denial of the <a href="/wiki/Holodomor" title="Holodomor">Holodomor</a> was reached during a visit to Ukraine carried out between 26 August and 9 September 1933 by Herriot, who had recently left the French Prime Ministry. Herriot denied accounts of the famine and said that Soviet Ukraine was "like a garden in full bloom".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Black_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Black-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Furthermore, he announced to the press that there was no famine in Ukraine, that he did not see any trace of hunger, and that the allegations of starving millions were being spread by adversaries of the Soviet Union. "When one believes that the Ukraine is devastated by famine, allow me to shrug my shoulders", he declared. The 13 September 1933 issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Pravda" title="Pravda">Pravda</a></i> was able to write that Herriot "categorically contradicted the lies of the bourgeoisie press in connection with a famine in the USSR."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%89douard_Herriot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Governmental functions</b></p>
<p>Président of the Council of Ministers : 1924–1925 / 19–21 July 1926 / June–December 1932.</p>
<p>Minister of Transport, Public Works and Supply : 1916–1917.</p>
<p>Minister of Education and Fine Arts : 1926–1928.</p>
<p>Minister of Foreign Affairs : 1924–1925 / 19–21 July 1926 / June–December 1932.</p>
<p>Minister of State : 1934–1936.</p>
<p><b>Electoral mandates</b></p>
<p><i><b>National Assembly of France</b></i></p>
<p>President of the National Assembly of France : 1947–1954.</p>
<p>Member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_France" title="National Assembly of France">National Assembly of France</a> for <a href="/wiki/Rh%C3%B4ne_(department)" title="Rhône (department)">Rhône (department)</a> : 1946–1957 (He died in 1957). Elected in 1946, reelected in 1951, 1956.</p>
<p><i><b>Constitutional Assembly</b></i></p>
<p>Member of the Constitutional Assembly for <a href="/wiki/Rh%C3%B4ne_(department)" title="Rhône (department)">Rhône (department)</a> : 1945–1946. Elected in 1945, reelected in June 1946.</p>
<p><i><b>Chamber of Deputies of France</b></i></p>
<p>President of the Chamber of Deputies of France : 1925–1926 / 1936–1940.</p>
<p>Member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_France" title="Chamber of Deputies of France">Chamber of Deputies of France</a> for <a href="/wiki/Rh%C3%B4ne_(department)" title="Rhône (department)">Rhône (department)</a> : 1919–1942 (Dissolution of Parliament by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philippe_Petain" title="Philippe Petain">Philippe Petain</a> in 1942). Elected in 1919, reelected in 1924, 1928, 1932, 1936.</p>
<p><i><b>Senate of France</b></i></p>
<p>Senator of <a href="/wiki/Rh%C3%B4ne" title="Rhône">Rhône</a> : 1912–1919. Elected in 1911.</p>
<p><i><b>General council</b></i></p>
<p>General councillor of <a href="/wiki/Rh%C3%B4ne_(department)" title="Rhône (department)">Rhône (department)</a> : 1945–1951.</p>
<p><i><b><a href="/wiki/City_council_(France)" title="City council (France)">Municipal Council</a></b></i></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mayor_(France)" title="Mayor (France)">Mayor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lyon" title="Lyon">Lyon</a> : 1905–1942 (Deposition by <a href="/wiki/Philippe_P%C3%A9tain" title="Philippe Pétain">Philippe Pétain</a> in 1942) / 1945–1957 (He died in 1957). Reelected in 1908, 1912, 1919, 1925, 1929, 1935, 1945, 1947, 1953.</p>
<p>Municipal councillor of <a href="/wiki/Lyon" title="Lyon">Lyon</a> : 1904–1942 (Deposition by <a href="/wiki/Philippe_P%C3%A9tain" title="Philippe Pétain">Philippe Pétain</a> in 1942) / 1945–1957 (He died in 1957). Reelected in 1908, 1912, 1919, 1925, 1929, 1935, 1945, 1947, 1953.</p>
<p><b>Political functions</b></p>
<p>President of the <a href="/wiki/Radical_Party_(France)" title="Radical Party (France)">Radical Party (France)</a> : 1919–1926 / 1931–1936 / 1948–1953 / 1955–1957.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trivia">Trivia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%89douard_Herriot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Trivia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
