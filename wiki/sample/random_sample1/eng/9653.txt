<p><b>Eduardo dos Reis Carvalho</b> (<small>Portuguese pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">[iˈðwaɾðu kɐɾˈvaʎu]</a></span>; born 19 September 1982), known simply as <b>Eduardo</b>, is a Portuguese professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">goalkeeper</a> for <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal national team</a>.</p>
<p>He appeared in 134 <a href="/wiki/Primeira_Liga" title="Primeira Liga">Primeira Liga</a> games over ten seasons, almost always representing <a href="/wiki/S.C._Braga" title="S.C. Braga">Braga</a>. He also played professionally in Italy, Turkey, Croatia and England.</p>
<p>After gaining the first of his 36 <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">caps</a> for <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a> in 2009, Eduardo was part of the squads in two <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cups</a> and as many <a href="/wiki/UEFA_European_Championship" title="UEFA European Championship">European Championships</a>, starting in the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 edition of the former tournament</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eduardo_Carvalho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Carvalho was born in <a href="/wiki/Mirandela" title="Mirandela">Mirandela</a>. A product of <a href="/wiki/S.C._Braga" title="S.C. Braga">S.C. Braga</a>'s youth system, he first appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Primeira_Liga" title="Primeira Liga">Primeira Liga</a> during <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Primeira_Liga" title="2006–07 Primeira Liga">2006–07</a> when he served a six-month loan at <a href="/wiki/S.C._Beira-Mar" title="S.C. Beira-Mar">S.C. Beira-Mar</a>. In <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Primeira_Liga" title="2007–08 Primeira Liga">the following season</a> he was also loaned, this time to <a href="/wiki/Vit%C3%B3ria_F.C." title="Vitória F.C.">Vitória de Setúbal</a>, and was as a key player in a <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Carvalhal" title="Carlos Carvalhal">Carlos Carvalhal</a>-led side that <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Ta%C3%A7a_da_Liga" title="2007–08 Taça da Liga">won</a> the inaugural <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%A7a_da_Liga" title="Taça da Liga">Portuguese League Cup</a> and thus <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_UEFA_Cup" title="2008–09 UEFA Cup">qualified</a> for the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a>, with a <i><a href="/wiki/Man_of_the_match" title="Man of the match">Man of the match</a></i> display in the final against <a href="/wiki/Sporting_Clube_de_Portugal" title="Sporting Clube de Portugal">Sporting Clube de Portugal</a> where he saved three attempts in the <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shoot-out_(association_football)" title="Penalty shoot-out (association football)">penalty shootout</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Eduardo returned to Braga for the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Primeira_Liga" title="2008–09 Primeira Liga">2008–09 campaign</a>, playing in all the league matches and also in the <a href="/wiki/Minho_Province" title="Minho Province">Minho</a> team's <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_UEFA_Cup" title="2008–09 UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup round-of-16 run</a>. The same happened in <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Primeira_Liga" title="2009–10 Primeira Liga">the following year</a> – with the club finishing in its best-ever position, second – as he only conceded 20 goals, joint-best in the competition.</p>
<p>On 7 July 2010, Eduardo signed a four-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa C.F.C.</a> for €4.8 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> replacing departed <a href="/wiki/Marco_Amelia" title="Marco Amelia">Marco Amelia</a>. Braga would also receive a 25% future transfer fee if the former sold the player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Eduardo started all the games but one for the Italians in <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Serie_A" title="2010–11 Serie A">his first and only season</a>, in a final midtable position. In July 2011 he returned to his country and joined <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">S.L. Benfica</a> on loan, with the <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a> club having an option to buy at the end of the campaign.</p>
<p>On 26 June 2012, Eduardo signed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C4%B0stanbul_B%C3%BCy%C3%BCk%C5%9Fehir_Belediyespor_(football)" title="İstanbul Büyükşehir Belediyespor (football)">İstanbul Büyükşehir Belediyespor</a> in a temporary deal, joining countryman <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Carvalhal" title="Carlos Carvalhal">Carlos Carvalhal</a> (coach) at the <a href="/wiki/S%C3%BCper_Lig" title="Süper Lig">Süper Lig</a> team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The following summer he returned to both Portugal and Braga, while still owned by Genoa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 27 June 2014, Eduardo joined several compatriots at Croatian side <a href="/wiki/GNK_Dinamo_Zagreb" title="GNK Dinamo Zagreb">GNK Dinamo Zagreb</a> after agreeing to a three-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On 25 August 2016, after 95 competitive appearances, he signed with <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> on a one-year deal, being bought in to provide experienced cover and competition to <a href="/wiki/Thibaut_Courtois" title="Thibaut Courtois">Thibaut Courtois</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asmir_Begovi%C4%87" title="Asmir Begović">Asmir Begović</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eduardo_Carvalho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008" title="UEFA Euro 2008">UEFA Euro 2008</a>, Eduardo was called for new <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal national team</a> coach <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Queiroz" title="Carlos Queiroz">Carlos Queiroz</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">qualifiers</a> against <a href="/wiki/Malta_national_football_team" title="Malta national football team">Malta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Denmark_national_football_team" title="Denmark national football team">Denmark</a>, when he was understudy to <a href="/wiki/Quim_(footballer,_born_1975)" title="Quim (footballer, born 1975)">Quim</a>.</p>
<p>He made his full debut on 11 February 2009 in a <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_game" title="Exhibition game">friendly match</a> with <a href="/wiki/Finland_national_football_team" title="Finland national football team">Finland</a>, playing the first 60 minutes in a 1–0 home win, and remained the starter throughout the remainder of the qualifying campaign. He also started in the final stages in South Africa, keeping <a href="/wiki/Shutout" title="Shutout">clean sheets</a> against <a href="/wiki/Ivory_Coast_national_football_team" title="Ivory Coast national football team">Ivory Coast</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/North_Korea_national_football_team" title="North Korea national football team">North Korea</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and only conceding in the <a href="/wiki/Single-elimination_tournament" title="Single-elimination tournament">round-of-16</a> loss against eventual champions <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a> (0–1), where he saved many shots from the opposition, mostly by <a href="/wiki/David_Villa" title="David Villa">David Villa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>After being relegated to the bench at his club, Eduardo met the same fate in the national team, to <a href="/wiki/Rui_Patr%C3%ADcio" title="Rui Patrício">Rui Patrício</a>, and both finished the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">UEFA Euro 2012</a> <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2012 qualifying">qualifying campaign</a> with five games as Portugal qualified for the final stages. He was also named in the squad of 23 for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 World Cup</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> but only actually played five minutes in the last group stage match against <a href="/wiki/Ghana_national_football_team" title="Ghana national football team">Ghana</a>, coming on for injured <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beto_(Portuguese_footballer,_born_1982)" title="Beto (Portuguese footballer, born 1982)">Beto</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
