<p><b>Eduard Pütsep</b> (21 October 1898 – 22 August 1960) was an <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonian</a> wrestler. He competed in Greco-Roman wrestling in the 1920, 1924 and 1928 Olympics and won a gold medal in the bantamweight division in 1924, becoming the first Olympic champion in wrestling from Estonia. In 1928 he placed sixth in Greco-Roman and ninth in <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_wrestling" title="Freestyle wrestling">freestyle wrestling</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-sr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pütsep took up wrestling during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> and in 1917 placed third at the Russian championships. At his first international competition, the 1920 Olympics, he lost in a semifinal to the eventual silver medalist <a href="/wiki/Heikki_K%C3%A4hk%C3%B6nen" title="Heikki Kähkönen">Heikki Kähkönen</a>. Next year he finished fourth at the world championships, and in 1922 won a silver medal. He retired from competitions in 1933 and attended the <a href="/wiki/1936_Summer_Olympics" title="1936 Summer Olympics">1936 Summer Olympics</a> as the head coach of the Latvian wrestling team. During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> he moved to Finland and continued to coach wrestlers there. Since 1977 an annual international wrestling tournament in his honor has been held in <a href="/wiki/V%C3%B5ru" title="Võru">Võru</a>, Estonia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-sr-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eok_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-eok-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-esbl_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-esbl-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pütsep could communicate in eight languages. Yet during his wrestling years he acted in silent films, and was nicknamed "Estonian Chaplin". In 1924, he starred in <i>Õnnelik korterikriisi lahendus</i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Konstantin_M%C3%A4rska" title="Konstantin Märska">Konstantin Märska</a>, and in 1925 played in <i>Tšeka komissar Miroštšenko</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eok_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-eok-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-esbl_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-esbl-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
