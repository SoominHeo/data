<p><b>Edward Jovanovski</b> (born June 26, 1976) is a retired <a href="/wiki/Canadians" title="Canadians">Canadian</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> <a href="/wiki/Defenceman" title="Defenceman">defenceman</a>. He was born in <a href="/wiki/Windsor,_Ontario" title="Windsor, Ontario">Windsor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, and is of <a href="/wiki/Macedonians_(ethnic_group)" title="Macedonians (ethnic group)">Macedonian</a> descent.</p>
<p>Jovanovski played <a href="/wiki/Junior_ice_hockey#Major_junior" title="Junior ice hockey">major junior ice hockey</a> for two seasons with the <a href="/wiki/Windsor_Spitfires" title="Windsor Spitfires">Windsor Spitfires</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Ontario_Hockey_League" title="Ontario Hockey League">Ontario Hockey League</a> (OHL), earning First All-Star, Second All-Star and All-Rookie Team honours. He was then selected first overall in the <a href="/wiki/1994_NHL_Entry_Draft" title="1994 NHL Entry Draft">1994 NHL Entry Draft</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Panthers" title="Florida Panthers">Florida Panthers</a>. During his rookie NHL season, he earned <a href="/wiki/NHL_All-Rookie_Team" title="NHL All-Rookie Team">All-Rookie Team</a> honours and helped the Panthers advance to the <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Cup_Finals" title="Stanley Cup Finals">Stanley Cup Finals</a>, where they lost to the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Avalanche" title="Colorado Avalanche">Colorado Avalanche</a>. After three-and-a-half seasons in Florida, Jovanovski was traded to the <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_Canucks" title="Vancouver Canucks">Vancouver Canucks</a> in a seven-player deal involving <a href="/wiki/Pavel_Bure" title="Pavel Bure">Pavel Bure</a>. During his tenure with Vancouver, he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Babe_Pratt_Trophy" title="Babe Pratt Trophy">Babe Pratt Trophy</a> as the team's best defenceman three consecutive years. He also led the club's defencemen in scoring four consecutive years. In July 2006, Jovanovski became an unrestricted free agent and signed with the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Coyotes" title="Arizona Coyotes">Phoenix Coyotes</a>. He led the team's defencemen in scoring during his first three years with the club. Known as a two-way defenceman, he has recorded three 40-point and one 50-point season in the NHL.</p>
<p>Internationally, Jovanovski plays for the <a href="/wiki/Canada_men%27s_national_ice_hockey_team" title="Canada men's national ice hockey team">Canadian national team</a>. A one-time <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winter_Olympics" title="Winter Olympics">Winter Olympian</a>, he won a gold medal at the <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey_at_the_2002_Winter_Olympics" title="Ice hockey at the 2002 Winter Olympics">2002 Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_City" title="Salt Lake City">Salt Lake City</a>. At the under-20 level, he won gold at the <a href="/wiki/1995_World_Junior_Ice_Hockey_Championships" title="1995 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships">1995 World Junior Championship</a>. He went on to play in four <a href="/wiki/Ice_Hockey_World_Championships" title="Ice Hockey World Championships">Men's World Championships</a>, winning silver in 2005 and 2008. He also represented Canada at the <a href="/wiki/2004_World_Cup_of_Hockey" title="2004 World Cup of Hockey">2004 World Cup</a>, playing in one game due to injury in the championship-winning tournament.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_career">Playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ed_Jovanovski&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_junior_.281993.E2.80.9395.29">Major junior (1993–95)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ed_Jovanovski&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Major junior (1993–95)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After playing bantam and Junior B in his hometown of Windsor, Jovanovski joined the <a href="/wiki/Ontario_Hockey_League" title="Ontario Hockey League">Ontario Hockey League</a> (OHL) with the <a href="/wiki/Windsor_Spitfires" title="Windsor Spitfires">Windsor Spitfires</a>. In his rookie season with the Spitfires, Jovanovski led all team defencemen in scoring, with 50 points in 62 games. In addition to being voted as the Emms Division's top bodychecker by League coaches,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stunner_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-stunner-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-equals_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-equals-2">[2]</a></sup> he was named to the OHL's All-Rookie and Second All-Star Teams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhl_notes_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhl_notes-3">[3]</a></sup> That off-season, Jovanovski was selected first overall by the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Panthers" title="Florida Panthers">Florida Panthers</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1994_NHL_Entry_Draft" title="1994 NHL Entry Draft">1994 NHL Entry Draft</a>. He admitted to being surprised at the top selection, as he had not been ranked first at any point in his draft-eligible season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stunner_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-stunner-1">[1]</a></sup> Czech prospect <a href="/wiki/Radek_Bonk" title="Radek Bonk">Radek Bonk</a> was ranked first overall by the <a href="/wiki/NHL_Central_Scouting_Bureau" title="NHL Central Scouting Bureau">NHL Central Scouting Bureau</a>, but Panthers President <a href="/wiki/Bill_Torrey" title="Bill Torrey">Bill Torrey</a> preferred to select a defenceman over a forward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-equals_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-equals-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the Draft, the <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_NHL_season" title="1994–95 NHL season">1994–95 NHL season</a> was suspended for three-and-a-half months due to a <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_NHL_lockout" title="1994–95 NHL lockout">labour dispute between NHL players and owners</a>. Consequently, Jovanovski remained in junior for the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_OHL_season" title="1994–95 OHL season">1994–95 OHL season</a>. When NHL was set to resume play in January 1995, it was speculated that the Panthers might sign Jovanovski and call him up to the NHL.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-patient_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-patient-4">[4]</a></sup> However, then-Panthers General Manager <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Murray_(ice_hockey)" title="Bryan Murray (ice hockey)">Bryan Murray</a> announced that Jovanovski would remain with Windsor, citing that he would likely not receive much playing time with the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-patient_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-patient-4">[4]</a></sup> He averaged approximately 40 minutes per game with Windsor that season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-patient_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-patient-4">[4]</a></sup> scoring 23 goals and 65 points over 50 contests. He also added nine points in nine playoff games before Windsor was eliminated. Jovanovski was named to the OHL First All-Star Team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhl_notes_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-nhl_notes-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>While still with the Spitfires, Jovanovski was charged with sexual assault, along with two of his teammates. The charges were eventually dropped after his lawyer threatened the alleged victim with a lawsuit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rape1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-rape1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Florida_Panthers_.281995.E2.80.931999.29">Florida Panthers (1995–1999)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ed_Jovanovski&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Florida Panthers (1995–1999)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following his second major junior season, Jovanovski signed a four-year, $5.7 million contract with the Panthers in June 1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Playing with the club during the subsequent pre-season, he broke his right hand during a fight with <a href="/wiki/Hartford_Whalers" title="Hartford Whalers">Hartford Whalers</a> forward <a href="/wiki/Brendan_Shanahan" title="Brendan Shanahan">Brendan Shanahan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-coming_of_age_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-coming_of_age-7">[7]</a></sup> The injury caused him to miss the first 11 games of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-coming_of_age_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-coming_of_age-7">[7]</a></sup> After recovering, he scored his first career NHL goal against the Whalers on December 2, 1995, a game-winner in a 5–3 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He finished his rookie season with 10 goals and 21 points over 70 games. During the regular season, he was encouraged by Panthers management to play more conservatively than he was used to in the OHL, focusing on defensive positioning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ranking fourth in the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Conference_(NHL)" title="Eastern Conference (NHL)">Eastern Conference</a> going into the <a href="/wiki/1996_Stanley_Cup_playoffs" title="1996 Stanley Cup playoffs">1996 playoffs</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Jovanovski helped the Panthers advance to the <a href="/wiki/1996_Stanley_Cup_Finals" title="1996 Stanley Cup Finals">Stanley Cup Finals</a>. He scored his first career <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Cup_playoffs" title="Stanley Cup playoffs">Stanley Cup playoff</a> goal in Game 2 of the first round against <a href="/wiki/Boston_Bruins" title="Boston Bruins">Boston Bruins</a> goaltender <a href="/wiki/Craig_Billington" title="Craig Billington">Craig Billington</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Facing the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Avalanche" title="Colorado Avalanche">Colorado Avalanche</a> in the Finals, the Panthers were defeated in four-straight games. Jovanovski contributed a goal and nine points in 22 post-season games. It would be his only Stanley Cup Finals appearance. In the off-season, he was named to the <a href="/wiki/NHL_All-Rookie_Team" title="NHL All-Rookie Team">NHL All-Rookie Team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tsn_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-tsn-12">[12]</a></sup> He was also nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Calder_Memorial_Trophy" title="Calder Memorial Trophy">Calder Memorial Trophy</a> as the League's rookie of the year, alongside <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Blackhawks" title="Chicago Blackhawks">Chicago Blackhawks</a> forward <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Daz%C3%A9" title="Éric Dazé">Éric Dazé</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ottawa_Senators" title="Ottawa Senators">Ottawa Senators</a> forward <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Alfredsson" title="Daniel Alfredsson">Daniel Alfredsson</a>, who ultimately won the award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On November 23, 1996, Jovanovski received a three-game suspension from the NHL without pay. In a game against the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Stars" title="Dallas Stars">Dallas Stars</a> the previous night, he illegally left the penalty box ready to fight any opposing players during a break in play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Later in the season, he suffered a knee injury during a game against the <a href="/wiki/Edmonton_Oilers" title="Edmonton Oilers">Edmonton Oilers</a> in January 1997, sidelining him for several weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The following month, he was involved in an on-ice altercation with <a href="/wiki/San_Jose_Sharks" title="San Jose Sharks">San Jose Sharks</a> forward <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Nicholls" title="Bernie Nicholls">Bernie Nicholls</a>, resulting in a two-game suspension and the maximum $1,000 fine for Nicholls for intent to injure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The accumulated injuries and suspensions caused Jovanovski to miss 21 games in his second NHL season. He scored seven goals and 23 points over 61 contests.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1997%E2%80%9398_NHL_season" title="1997–98 NHL season">1997–98</a>, Jovanovski appeared in 81 games, recording nine goals and 23 points. With his contract set to expire following the <a href="/wiki/1998%E2%80%9399_NHL_season" title="1998–99 NHL season">1998–99 season</a>, Jovanovski was beginning to be seen as a disappointment in Florida, failing to improve on his successful rookie year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bure_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-bure-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vancouver_Canucks_.281999.E2.80.932006.29">Vancouver Canucks (1999–2006)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ed_Jovanovski&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Vancouver Canucks (1999–2006)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 17, 1999, after three-and-a-half seasons with the Panthers, Jovanovski was traded in a seven-player deal to the <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_Canucks" title="Vancouver Canucks">Vancouver Canucks</a>. He was sent with <a href="/wiki/Dave_Gagner" title="Dave Gagner">Dave Gagner</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mike_Brown_(winger,_born_1979)" title="Mike Brown (winger, born 1979)">Mike Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Weekes" title="Kevin Weekes">Kevin Weekes</a> and a first-round draft pick in <a href="/wiki/2000_NHL_Entry_Draft" title="2000 NHL Entry Draft">2000</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nathan_Smith_(ice_hockey)" title="Nathan Smith (ice hockey)">Nathan Smith</a>) in exchange for <a href="/wiki/Pavel_Bure" title="Pavel Bure">Pavel Bure</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bret_Hedican" title="Bret Hedican">Bret Hedican</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brad_Ference" title="Brad Ference">Brad Ference</a> and a third-round pick in 2000 (Robert Fried).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bure_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-bure-17">[17]</a></sup> Joining the Canucks in the midst of a rebuilding period for the franchise,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Jovanovski quickly established himself as a top defenceman in Vancouver's lineup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> His ability to join the rush as a defenceman complemented the Canucks' up-tempo style of play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>A month following his trade, Jovanovski suffered a broken foot while blocking a shot in a game against the <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey_Devils" title="New Jersey Devils">New Jersey Devils</a> on February 9, 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Later in the season, he was involved in an altercation with <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Canadiens" title="Montreal Canadiens">Montreal Canadiens</a> forward <a href="/wiki/Shayne_Corson" title="Shayne Corson">Shayne Corson</a>. After being high-sticked in the face by Corson, the two players were sent off the ice, at which point Corson entered the Canucks' dressing room to verbally confront Jovanovski.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-corson_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-corson-22">[22]</a></sup> According to Corson, the feud stemmed from comments Jovanovski had said about his family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-corson_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-corson-22">[22]</a></sup> As a result of entering the Canucks' dressing room, the Canadiens forward was later suspended five games by the NHL, in addition to one game for the high-sticking infraction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-corson_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-corson-22">[22]</a></sup> In 31 games with the Canucks that season, Jovanovski recorded two goals and 11 points. Combined with his games played with the Panthers, he totalled 27 points in 72 games.</p>

<p>In his first full season with Vancouver, Jovanovski tallied five goals and 26 points (first among team defencemen) over 75 games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> He also ranked second among team defencemen in average ice time per game, behind only <a href="/wiki/Mattias_%C3%96hlund" title="Mattias Öhlund">Mattias Öhlund</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The following season, in <a href="/wiki/2000%E2%80%9301_NHL_season" title="2000–01 NHL season">2000–01</a>, he led all team defencemen with 12 goals and 47 points over 79 games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> He was then named to his first of three consecutive <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League_All-Star_Game" title="National Hockey League All-Star Game">NHL All-Star Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/51st_National_Hockey_League_All-Star_Game" title="51st National Hockey League All-Star Game">2001</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhl_notes_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-nhl_notes-3">[3]</a></sup> At the end of the season, he also earned his first of three consecutive <a href="/wiki/Babe_Pratt_Trophy" title="Babe Pratt Trophy">Babe Pratt Trophies</a>, awarded annually to the Canucks' fan-voted best defenceman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Jovanovski's offensive emergence helped the Canucks return to the playoffs after a six-year absence. It also marked Jovanovski's first playoff season since 1997.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_NHL_season" title="2001–02 NHL season">2001–02</a>, Jovanovski scored a career-high 17 goals, ranking second amongst all NHL defencemen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> His 48 points ranked sixth among NHL defencemen and was his highest total as a Canuck.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The Canucks finished as the eighth seed in the Western Conference for the second consecutive season, and were eliminated by the <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Red_Wings" title="Detroit Red Wings">Detroit Red Wings</a> in the opening round of the <a href="/wiki/2002_Stanley_Cup_playoffs" title="2002 Stanley Cup playoffs">2002 playoffs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Jovanovski contributed a goal and five points in six post-season games.</p>
<p>Midway through the following season, he was re-signed by the Canucks to a three-year contract extension on January 27, 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tsn_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-tsn-12">[12]</a></sup> The day after signing, he injured his foot, sidelining him for 14 games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tsn_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-tsn-12">[12]</a></sup> Despite his season being shortened by injury, he recorded a career-high 40 assists to go with six goals in <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_NHL_season" title="2002–03 NHL season">2002–03</a>. His 46 points ranked tenth overall in the League amongst defencemen,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> and it marked the fourth consecutive year he led Canucks defencemen in scoring.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Jovanovski added eight points in 14 <a href="/wiki/2003_Stanley_Cup_playoffs" title="2003 Stanley Cup playoffs">post-season</a> games before the Canucks were eliminated in the second round by the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Wild" title="Minnesota Wild">Minnesota Wild</a>. His seven goals led all League defencemen in playoff scoring.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>Midway through the <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_NHL_season" title="2003–04 NHL season">2003–04 season</a>, Jovanovski suffered a third-degree shoulder separation during a game against the <a href="/wiki/Nashville_Predators" title="Nashville Predators">Nashville Predators</a> on January 25, 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shoulder_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-shoulder-34">[34]</a></sup> Colliding with opposing forward <a href="/wiki/Martin_Erat" title="Martin Erat">Martin Erat</a>, he fell to ice and slid into the rink boards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shoulder_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-shoulder-34">[34]</a></sup> He returned late in the season to help the Canucks secure the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Division_(NHL)" title="Northwest Division (NHL)">Northwest Division</a> title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> In 56 games, he scored seven goals and 23 points. Adding four assists in seven <a href="/wiki/2004_Stanley_Cup_playoffs" title="2004 Stanley Cup playoffs">2004 playoff</a> games, the Canucks were defeated in the first round by the <a href="/wiki/Calgary_Flames" title="Calgary Flames">Calgary Flames</a>.</p>

<p>Due to the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_NHL_lockout" title="2004–05 NHL lockout">NHL lockout</a>, as well as rib and knee injuries sustained during the <a href="/wiki/2004_World_Cup_of_Hockey" title="2004 World Cup of Hockey">2004 World Cup</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worldcup_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-worldcup-36">[36]</a></sup> Jovanovski was inactive during the 2004–05 season. When the NHL resumed play in <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_NHL_season" title="2005–06 NHL season">2005–06</a>, Jovanovski was on pace for a career year, but his season was interrupted by groin, foot and abdominal injuries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tsn_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-tsn-12">[12]</a></sup> He finished with 33 points in 44 games for a career-high 0.75 points-per-game average.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhl_stats_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhl_stats-37">[37]</a></sup> The Canucks suffered from Jovanovski missing the final 27 games of the season and did not qualify for the <a href="/wiki/2006_Stanley_Cup_playoffs" title="2006 Stanley Cup playoffs">2006 playoffs</a>. Following the 2005–06 season, Jovanovski did not receive a contract offer from the Canucks, subsequently becoming an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unrestricted_free_agent" title="Unrestricted free agent">unrestricted free agent</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-contract06_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-contract06-38">[38]</a></sup> The decision to let him go was influenced by the re-signings of <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Sedin" title="Daniel Sedin">Daniel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Henrik_Sedin" title="Henrik Sedin">Henrik Sedin</a>, as well as the acquisition of goaltender <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Luongo" title="Roberto Luongo">Roberto Luongo</a>, leaving no room on the team's <a href="/wiki/Salary_cap" title="Salary cap">salary cap</a> to retain Jovanovski.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-contract06_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-contract06-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Phoenix_Coyotes_.282006.E2.80.932011.29">Phoenix Coyotes (2006–2011)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ed_Jovanovski&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Phoenix Coyotes (2006–2011)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 1, 2006, Jovanovski joined the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Coyotes" title="Arizona Coyotes">Phoenix Coyotes</a>, signing a five-year, $32.5 million contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-contract06_38-2"><a href="#cite_note-contract06-38">[38]</a></sup> Having received a contract offer from the Panthers, he was speculated to return to Florida, where he spent his summers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-contract06_38-3"><a href="#cite_note-contract06-38">[38]</a></sup> He cited being coached by <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Gretzky" title="Wayne Gretzky">Wayne Gretzky</a> as a strong factor for choosing Phoenix.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-contract06_38-4"><a href="#cite_note-contract06-38">[38]</a></sup> Jovanovski was named to his fourth NHL All-Star Game in the subsequent season, but continued to be plagued with injuries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tsn_12-4"><a href="#cite_note-tsn-12">[12]</a></sup> He missed the last 22 games of the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_NHL_season" title="2006–07 NHL season">2006–07 regular season</a> with an abdominal injury,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tsn_12-5"><a href="#cite_note-tsn-12">[12]</a></sup> limiting him to 29 points (first among Coyotes defencemen) in 54 games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> The Coyotes finished last in the Western Conference with 31 wins and 67 points.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_NHL_season" title="2007–08 NHL season">2007–08</a>, Jovanovski recorded a career-high 51 points (12 goals and 39 assists), which tied for tenth among League defencemen and led all Coyotes defencemen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> It was also the second-highest total for a defenceman in Coyotes history<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[notes 1]</a></sup> after <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Tverdovsky" title="Oleg Tverdovsky">Oleg Tverdovsky</a>'s 55-point year in <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%9397_NHL_season" title="1996–97 NHL season">1996–97</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[42]</a></sup> Jovanovski's career season included a one-game suspension on December 1, 2007, for a hit to the head of Minnesota Wild forward <a href="/wiki/Mari%C3%A1n_G%C3%A1bor%C3%ADk" title="Marián Gáborík">Marián Gáborík</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[43]</a></sup> Jovanovski also appeared in his second consecutive <a href="/wiki/56th_National_Hockey_League_All-Star_Game" title="56th National Hockey League All-Star Game">All-Star Game in 2008</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tsn_12-6"><a href="#cite_note-tsn-12">[12]</a></sup> The team continued to struggle, however, as they ranked 12th in the West, eight points out of a <a href="/wiki/2008_Stanley_Cup_playoffs" title="2008 Stanley Cup playoffs">2008 playoff</a> spot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[44]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_NHL_season" title="2008–09 NHL season">following season</a>, Jovanovski's offensive production dipped to 36 points in 82 games. Although it was his lowest total since his third season with the Panthers in 1997–98 (not including seasons with major injuries), he still led all Coyotes defencemen in scoring for the third consecutive year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[45]</a></sup> On a team basis, he missed the <a href="/wiki/2009_Stanley_Cup_playoffs" title="2009 Stanley Cup playoffs">playoffs</a> with Phoenix for the third-straight year, as they finished 13th in the West.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>Early in the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_NHL_season" title="2009–10 NHL season">2009–10 season</a>, Jovanovski missed ten games with a lower-body injury in November and December 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tsn_12-7"><a href="#cite_note-tsn-12">[12]</a></sup> Shortly after returning to the Coyotes lineup, he was suspended for two games by the NHL for a hit to the head of Minnesota Wild forward <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Ebbett" title="Andrew Ebbett">Andrew Ebbett</a> with his forearm on December 7, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[47]</a></sup> The following month, he received another two-game suspension for elbowing <a href="/wiki/New_York_Islanders" title="New York Islanders">New York Islanders</a> rookie forward <a href="/wiki/John_Tavares_(ice_hockey)" title="John Tavares (ice hockey)">John Tavares</a> in the head.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[48]</a></sup> Over 66 games during the season, he notched 10 goals and 34 points. He was overtaken as the team's top-scoring defenceman by <a href="/wiki/Keith_Yandle" title="Keith Yandle">Keith Yandle</a>, who had scored 41 points.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[49]</a></sup> While the season was marked with an uncertain future for the Coyotes from a business perspective, as the franchise had filed for bankruptcy in May 2009 and was taken over by the NHL,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[50]</a></sup> the team performed well and recorded their best season in history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[51]</a></sup> With 50 wins and 107 points (a 28-point improval from the previous season), they finished with the third best record in the West and came within five points of a <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Division_(NHL)" title="Pacific Division (NHL)">Pacific Division</a> and Conference title, trailing only the <a href="/wiki/San_Jose_Sharks" title="San Jose Sharks">San Jose Sharks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[52]</a></sup> Facing the Detroit Red Wings in the opening round, the Coyotes were eliminated in seven games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[53]</a></sup> Jovanovski had one goal and no assists in his first NHL playoffs since 2004.</p>

<p>In the first month of the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_NHL_season" title="2010–11 NHL season">2010–11 season</a>, Jovanovski was chosen to serve in place of the suspended <a href="/wiki/Shane_Doan" title="Shane Doan">Shane Doan</a> as team captain from October 21 to 25, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhl_notes_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-nhl_notes-3">[3]</a></sup> Over a week later, he recorded his first career NHL <a href="/wiki/Hat-trick" title="Hat-trick">hat-trick</a> in a 4–3 win against the Nashville Predators on November 3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hattrick_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-hattrick-55">[54]</a></sup> He scored all three goals against goaltender <a href="/wiki/Pekka_Rinne" title="Pekka Rinne">Pekka Rinne</a>, becoming the first Coyotes defenceman to score a hat-trick in team history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hattrick_55-1"><a href="#cite_note-hattrick-55">[54]</a></sup> On December 26, he became the 256th player in NHL history to play <a href="/wiki/List_of_NHL_players_with_1000_games_played" title="List of NHL players with 1000 games played">1,000 career games</a>, reaching the feat against the Dallas Stars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[55]</a></sup> Jovanovski struggled with injuries throughout the season, missing time on six different occasions. His most serious injury was an orbital bone fracture after his face collided with an opposing player's helmet in a game against the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Thrashers" title="Atlanta Thrashers">Atlanta Thrashers</a> on February 17, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[56]</a></sup> After missing 20 games with the injury, he returned in April for the last three games of the regular season and the playoffs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[57]</a></sup> In Jovanovski's limited time in the season, he recorded 14 points over 50 games. The Coyotes ranked sixth in the West,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[58]</a></sup> drawing the Red Wings in the first round for the second consecutive year. Jovanovski recorded one assist in the post-season as the Coyotes were eliminated in four games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[59]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Florida_.282011.E2.80.932014.29">Return to Florida (2011–2014)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ed_Jovanovski&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Return to Florida (2011–2014)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jovanovski signed a four-year, $16.5 million contract to return to the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Panthers" title="Florida Panthers">Florida Panthers</a> on July 1, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[60]</a></sup> He played a large role in helping reshape the franchise's rebuilding and drive to make the playoffs. Beginning in his second year after rejoining the Panthers, he served as team captain for the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_NHL_season" title="2012–13 NHL season">2012–13</a> and <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_NHL_season" title="2013–14 NHL season">2013–14 seasons</a>. On June 29, 2014, Jovanovski was placed on <a href="/wiki/Waivers_(NHL)" title="Waivers (NHL)">unconditional waivers</a> for the purpose of a buyout.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[61]</a></sup> Jovanovski remained inactive in the NHL during the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_NHL_season" title="2014–15 NHL season">2014–15 season</a>. On December 28, 2015 Jovanovski announced his retirement from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NHL" title="NHL">NHL</a>.</p>

<p>Jovanovski competed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canada_men%27s_national_junior_hockey_team" title="Canada men's national junior hockey team">Canada's under-20 team</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1995_World_Junior_Ice_Hockey_Championships" title="1995 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships">1995 World Junior Championships</a>, held in <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>. Scoring two goals in seven games, he helped Canada go undefeated to win gold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[62]</a></sup> Following his NHL rookie season, he was named as a reserve to the <a href="/wiki/Canada_men%27s_national_ice_hockey_team" title="Canada men's national ice hockey team">Canadian men's team</a> for the <a href="/wiki/1996_World_Cup_of_Hockey" title="1996 World Cup of Hockey">1996 World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-legends_64-0"><a href="#cite_note-legends-64">[63]</a></sup> The youngest player on the roster,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[64]</a></sup> he played in one exhibition game against <a href="/wiki/Russia_men%27s_national_ice_hockey_team" title="Russia men's national ice hockey team">Russia</a>, a 4–4 tie, but did not appear in any main tournament games as Canada lost in the final to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_ice_hockey_team" title="United States men's national ice hockey team">United States</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[65]</a></sup></p>
<p>Two years later, Jovanovski competed at the <a href="/wiki/1998_Men%27s_World_Ice_Hockey_Championships" title="1998 Men's World Ice Hockey Championships">1998 World Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>; he was the second-youngest named to the Canadian squad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[66]</a></sup> He scored two goals and an assist over six games as Canada failed to qualify past the crossover round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[67]</a></sup> He made his second World Championships appearance at the <a href="/wiki/2000_Men%27s_World_Ice_Hockey_Championships" title="2000 Men's World Ice Hockey Championships">2000 tournament</a> in Russia. He scored a goal and an assist over nine games. Canada lost the semifinal, however, 2–1 to the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Czech_men%27s_national_ice_hockey_team&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Czech men's national ice hockey team (page does not exist)">Czech Republic</a>, then lost the bronze medal game 2–1 to <a href="/wiki/Finland_men%27s_national_ice_hockey_team" title="Finland men's national ice hockey team">Finland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-00wc_69-0"><a href="#cite_note-00wc-69">[68]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2001, Jovanovski was chosen to Canada's Olympic team for the <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey_at_the_2002_Winter_Olympics" title="Ice hockey at the 2002 Winter Olympics">2002 Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_City" title="Salt Lake City">Salt Lake City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[69]</a></sup> Playing the United States in the gold medal game, Jovanovski earned an assist on the game-winning goal, backhanding a saucer pass from the opposition's blueline to <a href="/wiki/Joe_Sakic" title="Joe Sakic">Joe Sakic</a> on a five-on-three power play advantage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oly_71-0"><a href="#cite_note-oly-71">[70]</a></sup> Canada went on to win the gold medal, 5–2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oly_71-1"><a href="#cite_note-oly-71">[70]</a></sup> Jovanovski had three points, all assists, in total.</p>
<p>Prior to the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_NHL_lockout" title="2004–05 NHL lockout">2004–05 NHL lockout</a>, Jovanovski played for Canada at the <a href="/wiki/2004_World_Cup_of_Hockey" title="2004 World Cup of Hockey">2004 World Cup</a>. He suffered a cracked rib and a second degree sprain on his <a href="/wiki/Medial_collateral_ligament" title="Medial collateral ligament">medial collateral ligament</a> (MCL) during the first game against the United States, sidelining him for the rest of the tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worldcup_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-worldcup-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-legends_64-1"><a href="#cite_note-legends-64">[63]</a></sup> He was replaced by San Jose Sharks defenceman <a href="/wiki/Scott_Hannan" title="Scott Hannan">Scott Hannan</a> in the lineup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worldcup_36-2"><a href="#cite_note-worldcup-36">[36]</a></sup> Canada went on to win the championship over Finland in the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[71]</a></sup></p>
<p>The following year, he competed at the <a href="/wiki/2005_Men%27s_World_Ice_Hockey_Championships" title="2005 Men's World Ice Hockey Championships">2005 World Championships</a> in Austria. In the semi-final, Jovanovski scored the game-winning goal against Russia in a 4–3 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[72]</a></sup> Advancing to the gold medal game, Canada lost 3–0 to the Czech Republic, thus earning silver.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[73]</a></sup> He finished the tournament with a goal and two assists over nine games. Later that year, he was named to his second Canadian Olympic team for the <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey_at_the_2006_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Ice hockey at the 2006 Winter Olympics – Men's tournament">2006 Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a>, but was not able to play due to a lower abdominal injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[74]</a></sup></p>
<p>Making his fourth World Championships appearance in <a href="/wiki/2008_Men%27s_World_Ice_Hockey_Championships" title="2008 Men's World Ice Hockey Championships">2008</a>, Jovanovski earned a second-straight silver medal. He recorded one assist over nine games as Canada lost in the gold medal game by a 5–4 score in overtime to Russia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[75]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_style">Playing style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ed_Jovanovski&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Playing style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jovanovski is known as a two-way defenceman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-coming_of_age_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-coming_of_age-7">[7]</a></sup> Able to contribute offensively, he is an adept puck-carrier and joins plays deep in the opposing team's zone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-coming_of_age_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-coming_of_age-7">[7]</a></sup> He plays defence physically, bodychecking opponents primarily with his shoulder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-coming_of_age_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-coming_of_age-7">[7]</a></sup> In both aspects of his game, he is considered to play with a high level of risk, taking the chance of being out of position in favour of a good scoring chance or a hit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-coming_of_age_7-5"><a href="#cite_note-coming_of_age-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emotion_77-0"><a href="#cite_note-emotion-77">[76]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ed_Jovanovski&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jovanovski was born in <a href="/wiki/Windsor,_Ontario" title="Windsor, Ontario">Windsor, Ontario</a>, to Kostadin and Lilja Jovanovski.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-equals_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-equals-2">[2]</a></sup> His parents immigrated to Canada in 1973 from <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Republic_of_Macedonia" title="Socialist Republic of Macedonia">Macedonia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-equals_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-equals-2">[2]</a></sup> Coming from an athletic family, Kostadin was a semi-professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">soccer</a> player in <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" title="Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-equals_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-equals-2">[2]</a></sup> Jovanovski followed after his father and played organized soccer growing up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stunner_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-stunner-1">[1]</a></sup> He did not start playing hockey until age 11, when his older brother, Denny, joined a team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stunner_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-stunner-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>During his junior career, Jovanovski and two other Windsor Spitfire teammates were charged with sexually assaulting a 24-year-old woman in February 1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-coming_of_age_7-6"><a href="#cite_note-coming_of_age-7">[7]</a></sup> After a pre-trial hearing in June, the Crown attorney dropped the charges in August due to a lack of convincing evidence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-coming_of_age_7-7"><a href="#cite_note-coming_of_age-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Beginning his NHL career with the Florida Panthers, he owned a condominium in <a href="/wiki/Boca_Raton,_Florida" title="Boca Raton, Florida">Boca Raton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-coming_of_age_7-8"><a href="#cite_note-coming_of_age-7">[7]</a></sup> He met his wife, Kristin, in Florida and retained a residence in Boca Raton, where he spent his summers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-contract06_38-5"><a href="#cite_note-contract06-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-highstakes_78-0"><a href="#cite_note-highstakes-78">[77]</a></sup> Jovanovski and Kristin had their first child, daughter Kylie Everett, on August 22, 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[78]</a></sup> Three years later, Kyra was born, while her twin sister died in utero.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-double_80-0"><a href="#cite_note-double-80">[79]</a></sup> Kristin was later pregnant with twins a second time and gave birth to son Cole and daughter Coco on May 25, 2006, in Florida.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-highstakes_78-1"><a href="#cite_note-highstakes-78">[77]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-double_80-1"><a href="#cite_note-double-80">[79]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2005, Jovanovski was featured in a documentary aired on multicultural network <a href="/wiki/Omni_Television" title="Omni Television">Omni Television</a>. Entitled <i>The Late Bloomer: Ed Jovanovski</i>, it explored his career, family tragedy and attachments to his Macedonian heritage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[80]</a></sup></p>
