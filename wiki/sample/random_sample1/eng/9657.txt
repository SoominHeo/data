<p><b>Edmund Joseph "Cotton" Minahan</b> (December 10, 1882 – May 20, 1958) was a professional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baseball_player" title="Baseball player">baseball player</a>, and <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Track_and_field" title="Track and field">track and field athlete</a> who competed at the <a href="/wiki/1900_Summer_Olympics" title="1900 Summer Olympics">1900 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cotton_Minahan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Minahan was born in <a href="/wiki/Springfield,_Ohio" title="Springfield, Ohio">Springfield, Ohio</a> and educated at <a href="/wiki/Georgetown_University" title="Georgetown University">Georgetown University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manhattan_College" title="Manhattan College">Manhattan College</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Baseball">Baseball</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cotton_Minahan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Baseball">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was a right-handed <a href="/wiki/Pitcher" title="Pitcher">pitcher</a> for one season (1907) with the <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Reds" title="Cincinnati Reds">Cincinnati Reds</a>. For his career, he compiled a 0–2 record, with a 1.29 <a href="/wiki/Earned_run_average" title="Earned run average">earned run average</a>, and 4 <a href="/wiki/Strikeout" title="Strikeout">strikeouts</a> in 14 innings pitched.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Athletics">Athletics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cotton_Minahan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Athletics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Minahan competed in the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_60_metres" title="Athletics at the 1900 Summer Olympics – Men's 60 metres">60 metres event</a>, placing fourth overall. He placed second in his initial heat with an unknown time (though the winner ran it in 7.0 seconds) before coming in fourth of four in the final with an estimated time of 7.2 seconds.</p>
<p>He also ran in the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1900_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_100_metres" title="Athletics at the 1900 Summer Olympics – Men's 100 metres">100 metres</a>, finishing in 12th or 13th place overall. He took second in his quarterfinal heat to advance to the semifinals but placed fourth in his heat there to be eliminated.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cotton_Minahan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Minahan died on May 20, 1958 in <a href="/wiki/East_Orange,_New_Jersey" title="East Orange, New Jersey">East Orange, New Jersey</a> at the age of 75.</p>
