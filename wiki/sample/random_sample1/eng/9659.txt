<p><b>Edvard Vilhelm "Eetu" Westerlund</b> (1 February 1901 – 7 December 1982) was a Greco-Roman wrestler from <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>. He competed at the 1924, 1928 and 1936 Olympics and won a gold medal in 1924 and a bronze in 1928. He collected the same pair of medals at the world championships in 1921–22.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iat_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-iat-1">[1]</a></sup> Domestically Westerlund competed both in Greco-Roman and freestyle wrestling and won three titles: in 1927 in Greco-Roman lightweight, in 1929 in freestyle lightweight and in 1936 in Greco-Roman light-heavyweight. His brothers <a href="/wiki/Kalle_Westerlund" title="Kalle Westerlund">Kalle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emil_Westerlund" title="Emil Westerlund">Emil</a> were also Olympic wrestlers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-sr-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
