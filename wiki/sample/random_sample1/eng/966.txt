<p><b>FC Daugava</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvian</a> football club, based at the <a href="/wiki/Daugava_Stadium_(Daugavpils)" title="Daugava Stadium (Daugavpils)">Daugava Stadium</a>, in the city of <a href="/wiki/Daugavpils" title="Daugavpils">Daugavpils</a>. They play in the <a href="/wiki/Latvian_Second_League" title="Latvian Second League">Latvian Second League</a> in 2015 and are one of two clubs representing the city. They are also one of two clubs with the name <i>Daugava</i> and should not be confused with <a href="/wiki/FK_Daugava_(2003)" title="FK Daugava (2003)">FK Daugava Rīga</a>.</p>
<p>In 2008 they won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latvian_Cup" title="Latvian Cup">Latvian Cup</a> and in 2012 won the <a href="/wiki/Latvian_Higher_League" title="Latvian Higher League">Latvian Higher League</a> championship for the first time in the club's history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The current manager of the team is <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Tabanov" title="Ivan Tabanov">Ivan Tabanov</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Daugava&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The club was founded in 1944.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>They started the 1992 season with a young team, but later some experienced players were added to the squad and Ditton managed to regain a place in the higher league. In 2006 Ditton were taken over by new investors. Ukrainian manager <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Yuran" title="Sergei Yuran">Sergei Yuran</a> was invited to join the team staff, but he was soon replaced by <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Kiriakov" title="Sergei Kiriakov">Sergei Kiriakov</a>. That season the club achieved their highest position to that point, finishing 5th in the championship.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>At the end of 2006, Russian businessman Igor Malishkov became the main shareholder in the club. It was his decision to change the club's name to <i>FC Daugava</i>. Meanwhile, he also created a plan to develop the infrastructure of the club and build a new stadium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Malishkov invited <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Petrenko_(footballer)" title="Sergei Petrenko (footballer)">Sergei Petrenko</a>, who was then famous for his success with <a href="/wiki/FC_Torpedo_Moscow" title="FC Torpedo Moscow">Torpedo Moscow</a>, to become the manager of the team. Petrenko left in May 2007 for family reasons. In June 2007 <a href="/wiki/Igor_Gamula" title="Igor Gamula">Igor Gamula</a> was appointed, but he only started managing the team in August 2008. Till then the incumbent manager was <a href="/wiki/Mihails_Zem%C4%BCinskis" title="Mihails Zemļinskis">Mihails Zemļinskis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 19 July 2008 the Daugava Stadium was officially opened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> That year the club won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latvian_Cup" title="Latvian Cup">Latvian Cup</a> for the first time in its history. On 8 February 2009, due to financial problems, FC Daugava merged with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FC_Dinaburg" title="FC Dinaburg">Dinaburg</a> and formed one team under the name of <i>Dinaburg</i> for the 2009 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> That season Dinaburg were relegated from the <a href="/wiki/Latvian_Higher_League" title="Latvian Higher League">Latvian Higher League</a> due to suspicions of participation in match fixing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Daugava then decided to create a team that would play in the Latvian First League the following season, meanwhile Dinaburg ended its existence, stating that the club's image was spoiled. As <a href="/wiki/Daugavpils" title="Daugavpils">Daugavpils</a> is one of the biggest cities in Latvia, the <a href="/wiki/Latvian_Football_Federation" title="Latvian Football Federation">LFF</a> came up with a proposal to offer Daugava a place in the Latvian Higher League. The offer was accepted and Daugava were given a chance to represent Daugavpils in the higher league in the 2010 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011 the club achieved its best result until then, finishing third in the championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2012 Daugava became the champions of the Latvian Higher League for the first time in the club's history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Match-fixing_scandal">Match-fixing scandal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Daugava&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Match-fixing scandal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In July 2013 <a href="/wiki/Federbet" title="Federbet">Federbet</a> organization expressed its concerns in regards to possible match fixing attempts concerning Daugava's <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> game against <a href="/wiki/IF_Elfsborg" title="IF Elfsborg">IF Elfsborg</a>, which the club lost 7–1, with 7 goals being scored in the second half.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Prior to the 2014 Latvian Higher League season Daugava was initially denied the participation license due to non-existing youth academy but it was later obtained in exchange of promises to develop one in the near future.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In October 2014, during a spot-check at the club, several of its members were interrogated, with the club's former president Oleg Gavrilov being arrested for money laundering, legalization of illegal earnings and other crimes, including ignorance of the previously imposed disqualification upon him from any activates affiliated with Latvian football. Four other people, including the club's technical director, manager and two players were disqualified for an indeterminate period of time till the end of the investigation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The investigation is currently still in progress and is lead in cooperation of the <a href="/wiki/Latvian_Football_Federation" title="Latvian Football Federation">Latvian Football Federation</a>, <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a> and the State Police of Latvia.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Logo">Logo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Daugava&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Logo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>FC Daugava are currently using a logo based on the design of the legendary Latvian football club FK <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daugava_R%C4%ABga" title="Daugava Rīga">Daugava Rīga</a> that was founded in 1944 and played in the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Top_League" title="Soviet Top League">Soviet Top League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
