<p><b>Edward Battell</b> was a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">British</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Racing_cyclist" title="Racing cyclist">racing cyclist</a>. He competed at the <a href="/wiki/1896_Summer_Olympics" title="1896 Summer Olympics">1896 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>.</p>
<p>Battell competed in the 333 metres, 100 kilometres, and road races. He came third in the road race,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sports-reference_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-sports-reference-1">[1]</a></sup> 87 km from Athens to <a href="/wiki/Marathon,_Greece" title="Marathon, Greece">Marathon</a> and back. He came fourth in the 333m in 26.2 seconds. He was among the seven of the nine that started not to finish the 100 km.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Amateurism_protests">Amateurism protests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_Battell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Amateurism protests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Battell worked as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Servant" title="Servant">servant</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Embassy" title="British Embassy">British Embassy</a> in <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>. There were objections that he and another rider, <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Keeping" title="Frederick Keeping">Frederick Keeping</a>, worked for a living. That made them "not gentlemen so they could not possibly be amateurs." The historian Mike Price said: "Gentlemen were not paid so were true amateurs. Servants had to be paid and so lost any claims to being amateur. Such was the attitude of the day. Protests were overruled."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Olympic_road_race">Olympic road race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_Battell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Olympic road race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The course for the 87 km race was poorly surfaced and often rutted. It ran over the marathon course between Athens and Marathon and then back again. Most riders fell off at least once. Battell was among them and he finished bleeding and coated in dust. His crash cost him the lead and he was passed by the home rider, Aristidis Konstantinides. He had also fallen, his bike beyond repair. He borrowed another, only to ride it into a wall in Athens after avoiding a spectator.</p>
