<p><b>Edward England</b>, born <b>Edward Seegar</b> (c.1685–1721)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> was a famous African coast and Indian Ocean <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pirate" title="Pirate">pirate</a> captain from 1717 to 1720. The ships he sailed on included the <i>Pearl</i> (which he renamed <i>The Royal James</i>) and later the <i>Fancy</i>, for which England exchanged the <i>Pearl</i> in 1720. His flag was the classic <a href="/wiki/Jolly_Roger" title="Jolly Roger">Jolly Roger</a> — almost exactly as <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Bellamy" title="Samuel Bellamy">"Black Sam" Bellamy</a> used — with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_skull" title="Human skull">skull</a> above two crossed bones on a black background.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_England&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in Ireland as Edward Seegar sometime around 1685<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He was probably raised as a Catholic <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and was said to be an educated man.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Seegar changed his surname to England when he turned pirate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> England made his way to Jamaica and during the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Spanish_Succession" title="War of the Spanish Succession">War of Spanish Succession</a> he served as a <a href="/wiki/Privateer" title="Privateer">privateer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He was captured by the pirate captain Christopher Winter and forced to join the crew.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Winter most likely took England to the pirate base on <a href="/wiki/Nassau,_Bahamas" title="Nassau, Bahamas">Nassau, Bahamas</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> England took part in <a href="/wiki/Henry_Jennings" title="Henry Jennings">Henry Jennings</a> assault on the Spanish salvage camp at Palma de Ayz, Florida, stealing £87,000 in gold and silver.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> England is next reported as <a href="/wiki/Charles_Vane" title="Charles Vane">Charles Vane</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Quartermaster" title="Quartermaster">quartermaster</a>, in March 1718. Vane's sloop, the <i>Lark</i> was captured by the Royal Navy, but England and the rest of the crew were released to induce the other pirates of Nassau to accept the King's pardon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Captaincy-Coast_of_Africa">Captaincy-Coast of Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_England&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Captaincy-Coast of Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vane granted England command of a captured vessel in mid-1718. England decided not to accept the King's pardon. He sailed to the Coast of Africa, after the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Woodes_Rogers" title="Woodes Rogers">Woodes Rogers</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_Providence" title="New Providence">New Providence</a>. During the voyage to the Coast of Africa he took several ships, particularly the <i>Cadogan</i> captained by a man called Skinner. Some of England's crew murdered Skinner who they had previously served under because he had failed to pay their wages. As soon as Skinner came on board, he saw his old Boatswain, who said, ″Ah! Captain Skinner is it you, I am much in your debt, and now I shall pay you in your own coin″. They immediately seized Captain Skinner, bound him to the windlass and pelted him with glass bottles. Afterwards they said, because he had been a good Master he should have an easy death, and so shot him through the head. England also encountered <a href="/wiki/Howell_Davis" title="Howell Davis">Howell Davis</a> on that ship. Davis was a likeable man and easily gained favour with England, however Davis refused to sign articles with the pirates saying he would rather die. This impressed England who saw a lot of potential in Davis and he gave the command of the <i>Cadogan</i> to him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> England eventually exchanged his sloop for a larger ship called, the <i>Pearl</i> renaming it the <i>Royal James</i> and returned to Africa in the spring of 1719. Between the River Gambia and Cape Coast, the pirates took ten ships. After which England took two more ships, one the pirates named her <i>Victory</i> and made <a href="/wiki/John_Taylor_(pirate)" title="John Taylor (pirate)">John Taylor</a> the captain. England and his crew stayed for some time in an African town, but a conflict arose over the pirates' treatment of the local women. Fighting broke out, the pirates burned the town, and set sail.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Indian_Ocean">Indian Ocean</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_England&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Indian Ocean">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>By 1720, England had reached the Indian Ocean, where he met with fellow pirate Captain <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Levasseur" title="Olivier Levasseur">Oliver la Buse</a>. England captured a thirty-four gun Dutch ship, which he named <i>Fancy</i> in honour of <a href="/wiki/Henry_Every" title="Henry Every">Henry Every</a>, and he made the <i>Fancy</i> his new flagship. England decided to attack an East Indiaman under the command of <a href="/wiki/James_Macrae" title="James Macrae">James Macrae</a>, the <i>Cassandra</i>, near the island of <a href="/wiki/Comoros" title="Comoros">Comoros</a>. A long and bloody battle ensued, and both ships ran aground. Macrae and his surviving crew escaped and hid on the island for ten days. Finally Macrae, who was injured, tired, and hungry, went aboard England's consort the <i>Victory</i>, hoping for mercy, and surrendered to the pirates. The cargo of the <i>Cassandra</i> was valued at £75,000. Ninety of the <i>Fancy's</i> crew died in the attack on the <i>Cassandra</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> England had a good nature and unusual kindness, in that he didn't believe in torturing captives unless they couldn't otherwise be persuaded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> England ordered Macrae's life spared, and the pirates kept the <i>Cassandra</i> and gave the heavily damaged <i>Fancy</i> to Macrae. England's quartermaster, John Taylor, who was now captain of the <i>Victory</i>, resented this choice, and only agreed after England got him drunk. England then captured a small English ship near <a href="/wiki/Kochi" title="Kochi">Cochin</a>, the drunk captain reporting a false rumour that Macrae was preparing a fleet to chase the pirates. This enraged the pirates, particularly Taylor, who had wanted to kill Macrae. Around this time, Taylor organised a vote to remove England from command.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marooning_in_Mauritius">Marooning in Mauritius</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_England&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Marooning in Mauritius">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>England was subsequently <a href="/wiki/Marooning" title="Marooning">marooned</a> on <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a> with three other crew members without adequate provisions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> After about four months they built a small boat and managed to sail across the Indian Ocean and made it to St. Augustine's Bay in <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> One of the men marooned with England who had also defended Macrae was described as "a man with a terrible pair of whiskers and a wooden leg, being stuck round with pistols," and is said to have been the model for Robert Louis Stevenson's character <a href="/wiki/Long_John_Silver" title="Long John Silver">Long John Silver</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Identifying_reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Identifying reliable sources"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on an unreliable source. this book was published in 1724, 160 years before Robert Louis Stevenson's Treasure Island (August 2015)">unreliable source?</span></a></i>]</sup> England survived for a while on the charity of other pirates,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> possibly some of <a href="/wiki/Henry_Every" title="Henry Every">Henry Every</a>'s old crew.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> He died in 1721,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> from ″severe Strings of his Conscience″ according to the unreliable account of a sailor named Downing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_England&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Edward England is known today as one of the more humane pirate captains of the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Age_of_Piracy" title="Golden Age of Piracy">Golden Age of Piracy</a>.</p>
<p><i>He had a great deal of good Nature, and did not want for Courage ; he was not avaritious, and always averse to the ill Usage of Prisoners received : He would have been contented with moderate Plunder, and less mischievous Pranks, could his Companions have been brought to the same Temper, but he was generally over-rul'd, and as he was engaged in that abominable Society, he was obliged to be a Partner in all their vile Actions.</i>—Captain Charles Johnson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
