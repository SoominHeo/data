<p><b>Edward Caird</b> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FRSE" title="FRSE">FRSE</a> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/ɛr/ 'err' in 'merry'">ɛr</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span></span>/</a></span></span>; 23 March 1835 – 1 November 1908) was a Scottish <a href="/wiki/Philosopher" title="Philosopher">philosopher</a>, born in <a href="/wiki/Greenock" title="Greenock">Greenock</a>. He was a holder of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LL.D." title="LL.D.">LL.D.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Civil_Law" title="Doctor of Civil Law">DCL</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DLitt" title="DLitt">DLitt</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_Caird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The younger brother of the theologian <a href="/wiki/John_Caird_(theologian)" title="John Caird (theologian)">John Caird</a>, he was the son of engineer John Caird, the proprietor of <a href="/wiki/Caird_%26_Company" title="Caird &amp; Company">Caird &amp; Company</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> born at <a href="/wiki/Greenock" title="Greenock">Greenock</a> in <a href="/wiki/Renfrewshire_(historic)" title="Renfrewshire (historic)">Renfrewshire</a>, and educated at <a href="/wiki/Greenock_Academy" title="Greenock Academy">Greenock Academy</a> and the Universities of <a href="/wiki/University_of_Glasgow" title="University of Glasgow">Glasgow</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oxford_University" title="Oxford University">Oxford</a> (B.A. 1863). He became Fellow and Tutor of <a href="/wiki/Merton_College,_Oxford" title="Merton College, Oxford">Merton College</a>.</p>
<p>In 1866, he was appointed to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Professor_of_Moral_Philosophy,_Glasgow" title="Professor of Moral Philosophy, Glasgow">Chair of Moral Philosophy</a> at <a href="/wiki/University_of_Glasgow" title="University of Glasgow">Glasgow</a>, which he held until 1893. In that year he became <a href="/wiki/Master_(college)" title="Master (college)">Master</a> of <a href="/wiki/Balliol_College,_Oxford" title="Balliol College, Oxford">Balliol College</a>, from which he retired in 1907.</p>
<p>He was elected a Honorary Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_of_Edinburgh" title="Royal Society of Edinburgh">Royal Society of Edinburgh</a> in 1900.</p>
<p>In May 1902 he was at <a href="/wiki/Caernarfon" title="Caernarfon">Carnavon</a> to receive the honorary degree <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D.Litt." title="D.Litt.">D.Litt.</a> (Doctor of Letters) from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Wales" title="University of Wales">University of Wales</a> during the ceremony to install the Prince of Wales (later King <a href="/wiki/George_V" title="George V">George V</a>) as Chancellor of that university.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The philosopher <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(philosopher)" title="John Watson (philosopher)">John Watson</a> was among his students at the University of Glasgow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>He died in <a href="/wiki/Oxford" title="Oxford">Oxford</a> on 1 November 1908 and was buried there in St Sepulchres Cemetery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_Caird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He married Caroline Frances Wylie in 1867. They had no children.</p>
