<p><b>Edward Charles Morice Fox</b>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Officer_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Officer of the Order of the British Empire">OBE</a> (born 13 April 1937) is an English stage, film and television actor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>He played the part of the professional assassin, known only as the <a href="/wiki/Jackal_(The_Day_of_the_Jackal)" title="Jackal (The Day of the Jackal)">"Jackal"</a>, who is hired to assassinate the French president <a href="/wiki/Charles_de_Gaulle" title="Charles de Gaulle">Charles de Gaulle</a> in the summer of 1963, in the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Day_of_the_Jackal_(film)" title="The Day of the Jackal (film)">The Day of the Jackal</a></i> (1973).</p>
<p>He portrayed <a href="/wiki/Edward_VIII" title="Edward VIII">King Edward VIII</a> in the British <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_drama_series" title="Television drama series">television drama series</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Edward_%26_Mrs._Simpson" title="Edward &amp; Mrs. Simpson">Edward &amp; Mrs. Simpson</a></i> (1978).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_Fox_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fox was born in <a href="/wiki/Chelsea,_London" title="Chelsea, London">Chelsea</a>, London, the son of <a href="/wiki/Robin_Fox_(theatrical_agent)" title="Robin Fox (theatrical agent)">Robin Fox</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theatrical_agent" title="Theatrical agent">theatrical agent</a>, and Angela Muriel Darita Worthington, an actress and writer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He is the elder brother of actor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Fox_(actor)" title="James Fox (actor)">James Fox</a> and film producer <a href="/wiki/Robert_Fox_(producer)" title="Robert Fox (producer)">Robert Fox</a>, and an uncle of actor <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Fox" title="Laurence Fox">Laurence Fox</a>. His paternal great-grandfather was industrialist and inventor <a href="/wiki/Samson_Fox" title="Samson Fox">Samson Fox</a>, and his paternal grandmother was Hilda Hanbury, sister of stage performer <a href="/wiki/Lily_Hanbury" title="Lily Hanbury">Lily Hanbury</a>. His maternal grandfather was <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Playright&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Playright (page does not exist)">dramatist</a> <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Lonsdale" title="Frederick Lonsdale">Frederick Lonsdale</a>, and his maternal grandmother was the daughter of football player and stockbroker <a href="/wiki/Charles_Morice" title="Charles Morice">Charles Morice</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Fox was educated at <a href="/wiki/Harrow_School" title="Harrow School">Harrow School</a> in northwest London and served as a lieutenant in the <a href="/wiki/Coldstream_Guards" title="Coldstream Guards">Coldstream Guards</a>, a regiment of the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_Fox_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fox made his theatrical début in 1958<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="in what? (June 2010)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, and his first film appearance was as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra_(actor)" title="Extra (actor)">extra</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Loneliness_of_the_Long_Distance_Runner_(film)" title="The Loneliness of the Long Distance Runner (film)">The Loneliness of the Long Distance Runner</a></i> (1962). He also had a non-speaking part as a waiter in <i><a href="/wiki/This_Sporting_Life" title="This Sporting Life">This Sporting Life</a></i> (1963). Throughout the 1960s he worked mostly on stage, including a turn as <i><a href="/wiki/Hamlet" title="Hamlet">Hamlet</a></i>. In the late 1960s and early 1970s he established himself with roles in major British films including <i><a href="/wiki/Oh!_What_a_Lovely_War" title="Oh! What a Lovely War">Oh! What a Lovely War</a></i> (1969), <i><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Britain_(film)" title="Battle of Britain (film)">Battle of Britain</a></i> (1969) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Go-Between_(1971_film)" title="The Go-Between (1971 film)">The Go-Between</a></i> (1971). In <i>The Go-Between</i>, he played the part of Lord Hugh Trimingham, for which he won a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAFTA" title="BAFTA">BAFTA</a> award for Best Supporting Actor. His acting ability also brought him to the attention of director <a href="/wiki/Fred_Zinnemann" title="Fred Zinnemann">Fred Zinnemann</a>, who was looking for an actor who wasn't well-known and could be believable as the assassin in the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Day_of_the_Jackal_(film)" title="The Day of the Jackal (film)">The Day of the Jackal</a></i> (1973). Fox won the role, beating out other contenders such as <a href="/wiki/Roger_Moore" title="Roger Moore">Roger Moore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Caine" title="Michael Caine">Michael Caine</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>From then onwards, he was much sought after, appearing in such films as <i><a href="/wiki/A_Bridge_Too_Far_(film)" title="A Bridge Too Far (film)">A Bridge Too Far</a></i> (1977) as <a href="/wiki/Brian_Horrocks" title="Brian Horrocks">Lieutenant General Horrocks</a> – a role he has cited as a personal favourite<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> – and for which he won yet another <a href="/wiki/BAFTA_Award_for_Best_Actor_in_a_Supporting_Role" title="BAFTA Award for Best Actor in a Supporting Role">Best Supporting Actor award</a> at the <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_Film_Awards" title="British Academy Film Awards">British Academy Film Awards</a>. He also starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Force_10_from_Navarone_(film)" title="Force 10 from Navarone (film)">Force 10 from Navarone</a></i> (1978), with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Shaw_(actor)" title="Robert Shaw (actor)">Robert Shaw</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harrison_Ford" title="Harrison Ford">Harrison Ford</a>.</p>
<p>He portrayed King Edward VIII in the television drama <i><a href="/wiki/Edward_%26_Mrs._Simpson" title="Edward &amp; Mrs. Simpson">Edward &amp; Mrs. Simpson</a></i> (1978). In the film <i><a href="/wiki/Gandhi_(film)" title="Gandhi (film)">Gandhi</a></i> (1982), Fox portrayed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brigadier-General" title="Brigadier-General">Brigadier-General</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reginald_Dyer" title="Reginald Dyer">Reginald Dyer</a>, responsible for the <a href="/wiki/Jallianwala_Bagh_massacre" title="Jallianwala Bagh massacre">Amritsar massacre</a> in India. He then appeared as <a href="/wiki/M_(James_Bond)" title="M (James Bond)">M</a> in the unofficial <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">Bond</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Never_Say_Never_Again" title="Never Say Never Again">Never Say Never Again</a></i> (1983), a <a href="/wiki/Remake" title="Remake">remake</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Thunderball_(film)" title="Thunderball (film)">Thunderball</a></i> (1965). He also appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bounty" title="The Bounty">The Bounty</a></i> (1984) and <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_Geese_II" title="Wild Geese II">Wild Geese II</a></i> (1985) both opposite <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Olivier" title="Laurence Olivier">Laurence Olivier</a>, and in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Importance_of_Being_Earnest_(2002_film)" title="The Importance of Being Earnest (2002 film)">The Importance of Being Earnest</a></i> (2002), <i><a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Nickleby_(2002_film)" title="Nicholas Nickleby (2002 film)">Nicholas Nickleby</a></i> (2002), and <i><a href="/wiki/Stage_Beauty" title="Stage Beauty">Stage Beauty</a></i> (2004).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_stage_work">Later stage work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_Fox_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later stage work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fox has consolidated his reputation with regular appearances on stage in London's West End. He was seen in <i><a href="/wiki/Four_Quartets" title="Four Quartets">Four Quartets</a></i>, a set of four poems by <a href="/wiki/T._S._Eliot" title="T. S. Eliot">T. S. Eliot</a> accompanied by the <a href="/wiki/Musical_keyboard" title="Musical keyboard">keyboard music</a> of <a href="/wiki/Johann_Sebastian_Bach" title="Johann Sebastian Bach">Johann Sebastian Bach</a> performed by <a href="/wiki/Christine_Croshaw" title="Christine Croshaw">Christine Croshaw</a>. In 2010, Fox performed a one-man show, <i>An Evening with <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Trollope" title="Anthony Trollope">Anthony Trollope</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Richard_Digby_Day" title="Richard Digby Day">Richard Digby Day</a>. In 2013 he replaced <a href="/wiki/Robert_Hardy" title="Robert Hardy">Robert Hardy</a> in the role of <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a> in the premiere of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Audience_(2013_play)" title="The Audience (2013 play)">The Audience</a></i>, after Hardy had to withdraw for health reasons.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_Fox_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For his role as Viscount Trimingham in <i>The Go-Between</i> (1971), he won Best Supporting Actor award at the following year's British Academy Film Awards.</p>
<p>For his role as Lieutenant General Horrocks in <i>A Bridge Too Far</i> (1977), he won the Best Supporting Actor award at the British Academy Film Awards.</p>
<p>In 2003, he was made an Officer of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Order of the British Empire">Order of the British Empire</a> for his services to theatre and British cinema.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_Fox_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fox has been married twice, to actresses <a href="/wiki/Tracy_Reed_(English_actress)" title="Tracy Reed (English actress)">Tracy Reed</a> (1958–1961) and <a href="/wiki/Joanna_David" title="Joanna David">Joanna David</a> (from July 2004, after a long-standing relationship). He has a daughter, Lucy, Viscountess <a href="/wiki/Jenico_Preston,_17th_Viscount_Gormanston" title="Jenico Preston, 17th Viscount Gormanston">Gormanston</a>, by Reed, and two children, <a href="/wiki/Emilia_Fox" title="Emilia Fox">Emilia Fox</a> and <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Fox_(actor)" title="Freddie Fox (actor)">Freddie Fox</a>, with David.</p>
<p>He is the elder brother and uncle, respectively, of actors <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Fox_(actor)" title="James Fox (actor)">James Fox</a> and <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Fox" title="Laurence Fox">Laurence Fox</a>.</p>
<p>Fox joined the Countryside March to support hunting rights in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He also supported the restoration of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Hall,_Harrogate" title="Royal Hall, Harrogate">Royal Hall, Harrogate</a>, funded by his great-grandfather <a href="/wiki/Samson_Fox" title="Samson Fox">Samson Fox</a>.</p>
