<p><b>Edi Mue Gathegi</b> (born March 10, 1979) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kenyan_American" title="Kenyan American">Kenyan American</a> film, stage and television actor. He appeared as recurring character Dr. Jeffrey Cole (aka "Big Love") in the television series <i><a href="/wiki/House_(TV_series)" title="House (TV series)">House</a></i>, as Cheese in the 2007 film <i><a href="/wiki/Gone_Baby_Gone" title="Gone Baby Gone">Gone Baby Gone</a></i> and as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Twilight_characters#Laurent" title="List of Twilight characters">Laurent</a> in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Twilight_(2008_film)" title="Twilight (2008 film)">Twilight</a></i>, its sequel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Twilight_Saga:_New_Moon" title="The Twilight Saga: New Moon">The Twilight Saga: New Moon</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Darwin_(comics)" title="Darwin (comics)">Darwin</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/X-Men:_First_Class" title="X-Men: First Class">X-Men: First Class</a></i>. He also featured in a short television series, Into the Badlands as Baron Jacobee. He's has also been a recurring character in NBC's television series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Blacklist_(TV_series)" title="The Blacklist (TV series)">The Blacklist</a></i> as Matias Solomon, an operative for The Cabal, a covert organization. Gathegi is scheduled to star in the 2016–2017 season <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crime_thriller" title="Crime thriller">crime thriller</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Blacklist:_Redemption" title="The Blacklist: Redemption">The Blacklist: Redemption</a></i>, on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> as Matias Solomon, a mercenary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spinoff_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spinoff-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edi_Gathegi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in Eastlands, <a href="/wiki/Nairobi" title="Nairobi">Nairobi</a>, Kenya, Gathegi grew up in <a href="/wiki/Albany,_California" title="Albany, California">Albany</a>, California.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vf_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-vf-3">[3]</a></sup> As an undeclared undergraduate at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Santa_Barbara" title="University of California, Santa Barbara">University of California, Santa Barbara</a>, he was more interested in playing basketball and was good at it, until he injured his knee; this plunged him into depression, so he took up an acting class as an "easy course". This is how he discovered his love for acting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vf_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-vf-3">[3]</a></sup> Afterwards, studied at <a href="/wiki/New_York_University" title="New York University">New York University</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tisch_School_of_the_Arts#Graduate_Acting_Program" title="Tisch School of the Arts">Graduate Acting Program</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Tisch_School_of_the_Arts" title="Tisch School of the Arts">Tisch School of the Arts</a>, graduating in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Gathegi's career began in theatre,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tl1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-tl1-5">[5]</a></sup> and his stage credits include <i><a href="/wiki/Two_Trains_Running" title="Two Trains Running">Two Trains Running</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Old_Globe_Theatre" title="Old Globe Theatre">Old Globe Theatre</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/As_You_Like_It" title="As You Like It">As You Like It</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Twelfth_Night" title="Twelfth Night">Twelfth Night</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Othello" title="Othello">Othello</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream" title="A Midsummer Night's Dream">A Midsummer Night's Dream</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Cyrano_de_Bergerac_(play)" title="Cyrano de Bergerac (play)">Cyrano de Bergerac</a></i>, among others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mue_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-mue-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edi_Gathegi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gathegi's first professional role was the Haitian Cabbie in the 2006 film <i><a href="/wiki/Crank_(film)" title="Crank (film)">Crank</a></i>. Though he had originally auditioned for the role of Kaylo, the producers gave the role to <a href="/wiki/Efren_Ramirez" title="Efren Ramirez">Efren Ramirez</a> and instead offered Gathegi an appearance as the Haitian Cabbie.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tl1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-tl1-5">[5]</a></sup> He was dubious at first about performing a <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haitian</a> accent, but was coached by a Haitian friend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tl1_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-tl1-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2007, after guest-starring on <i><a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Heights_(TV_series)" title="Lincoln Heights (TV series)">Lincoln Heights</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Veronica_Mars" title="Veronica Mars">Veronica Mars</a></i>, Gathegi went on to star as Bodie in <i><a href="/wiki/Death_Sentence_(2007_film)" title="Death Sentence (2007 film)">Death Sentence</a></i>, Darudi in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fifth_Patient" title="The Fifth Patient">The Fifth Patient</a></i> and Cheese in <i><a href="/wiki/Gone_Baby_Gone" title="Gone Baby Gone">Gone Baby Gone</a></i>. He later had a recurring role as <a href="/wiki/Mormonism" title="Mormonism">Mormon</a> intern Dr. Jeffrey Cole on the television medical drama <i><a href="/wiki/House_(TV_series)" title="House (TV series)">House</a></i>, his character was often mocked for his religious beliefs by <a href="/wiki/Gregory_House" title="Gregory House">Dr. House</a>, who himself is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atheist" title="Atheist">atheist</a>. He also guest-starred on <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Miami" title="CSI: Miami">CSI: Miami</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI: Crime Scene Investigation</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Life_on_Mars_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Life on Mars (U.S. TV series)">Life on Mars</a></i> in 2008 before being cast as Laurent in <i><a href="/wiki/Twilight_(2008_film)" title="Twilight (2008 film)">Twilight</a></i>. When Gathegi first auditioned for the 2008 film, adapted from the same-titled <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twilight_(novel)" title="Twilight (novel)">first book</a> in <a href="/wiki/Stephenie_Meyer" title="Stephenie Meyer">Stephenie Meyer</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Twilight_(novel_series)" title="Twilight (novel series)">Twilight series</a>, he had not heard of the series and was not aware that his character was a vampire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lat_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-lat-6">[6]</a></sup> He now has read the whole series and calls himself a hardcore fan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lat_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-lat-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He portrays <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eddie_Willers" title="Eddie Willers">Eddie Willers</a> in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlas_Shrugged_(film)" title="Atlas Shrugged (film)">Atlas Shrugged</a></i> (2011), based on <a href="/wiki/Ayn_Rand" title="Ayn Rand">Ayn Rand</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Atlas_Shrugged" title="Atlas Shrugged">novel of the same name</a>. He later portrayed another Haitian character, Jean Baptiste, in the fifth season of <i><a href="/wiki/Justified_(TV_series)" title="Justified (TV series)">Justified</a></i>, but was unhappy with the role and asked to be written off the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
