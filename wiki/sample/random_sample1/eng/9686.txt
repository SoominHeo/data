<p><b>Hernández</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[erˈnandeθ]</a></span>) is a widespread Spanish surname that became common around the 15th century. Originally a <a href="/wiki/Patronymic" title="Patronymic">patronymic</a>, it means <i>son of Hernando</i>, Hernando or Fernando being the Spanish version of Germanic <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand" title="Ferdinand">Ferdinand</a>. <a href="/wiki/Fern%C3%A1ndez" title="Fernández">Fernández</a> is a very common variant of the name. Hernandes and Fernandes are their Portuguese equivalents.</p>
<p>The fact that the Hernández family of Spain bears a version of the French royal arms leads many to speculate that they descend from the French royal <a href="/wiki/House_of_Valois" title="House of Valois">house of Valois</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
