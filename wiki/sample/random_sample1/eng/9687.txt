<p><b>Hernández-Camacho's night monkey</b> (<i>Aotus jorgehernandezi</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Night_monkey" title="Night monkey">night monkey</a> of the family <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aotidae" title="Aotidae">Aotidae</a>. It was first described in 2007 by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Defler" title="Thomas Defler">Thomas Defler</a> and Marta Bueno. It has a gray neck and a white patch over each <a href="/wiki/Eye" title="Eye">eye</a>, separated by a black band. The fur on the chest, belly, lower arms and lower wrists is thick and white. It differs from other gray-necked night monkey species other than <a href="/wiki/Brumback%27s_night_monkey" title="Brumback's night monkey">Brumback's night monkey</a> in having 50 <a href="/wiki/Chromosome" title="Chromosome">chromosomes</a>.</p>
<p>It is believed to live in <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a> on the western slopes and foothills of the <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a>, between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quind%C3%ADo" title="Quindío">Quindío</a> and <a href="/wiki/Risaralda_Department" title="Risaralda Department">Risaralda</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-defler_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-defler-1">[1]</a></sup> It is possible that it occurs in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tatam%C3%A1_Natural_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tatamá Natural National Park (page does not exist)">Tatamá Natural National Park</a>. This range is currently also considered part of the <a href="/wiki/Range_(biology)" title="Range (biology)">range</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Panamanian_night_monkey" title="Panamanian night monkey">Panamanian night monkey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-2">[2]</a></sup> The Latin name honors the late Colombian <a href="/wiki/Biologist" title="Biologist">biologist</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jorge_Hern%C3%A1ndez-Camacho&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jorge Hernández-Camacho (page does not exist)">Jorge Hernández-Camacho</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-defler_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-defler-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
