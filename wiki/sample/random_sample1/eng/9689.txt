<p><b>Erhard Schmidt</b> (13 January 1876 – 6 December 1959) was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baltic_German" title="Baltic German">Baltic German</a> <a href="/wiki/Mathematician" title="Mathematician">mathematician</a> whose work significantly influenced the direction of mathematics in the twentieth century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mactutor_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-mactutor-1">[1]</a></sup> Schmidt was born in <a href="/wiki/Tartu" title="Tartu">Tartu</a> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Dorpat</i></span>), in the <a href="/wiki/Governorate_of_Livonia" title="Governorate of Livonia">Governorate of Livonia</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mathematics">Mathematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Erhard_Schmidt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Mathematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His advisor was <a href="/wiki/David_Hilbert" title="David Hilbert">David Hilbert</a> and he was awarded his doctorate from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Georg-August_University_of_G%C3%B6ttingen" title="Georg-August University of Göttingen">Georg-August University of Göttingen</a> in 1905. His doctoral dissertation was entitled <i>Entwickelung willkürlicher Functionen nach Systemen vorgeschriebener</i> and was a work on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Integral_equations" title="Integral equations">integral equations</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Together with <a href="/wiki/David_Hilbert" title="David Hilbert">David Hilbert</a> he made important contributions to <a href="/wiki/Functional_analysis" title="Functional analysis">functional analysis</a>. <a href="/wiki/Ernst_Zermelo" title="Ernst Zermelo">Ernst Zermelo</a> credited conversations with Schmidt for the idea and method for his classic 1904 proof of the <a href="/wiki/Well-ordering_theorem" title="Well-ordering theorem">Well-ordering theorem</a> from an "<a href="/wiki/Axiom_of_choice" title="Axiom of choice">Axiom of choice</a>", which has become an integral part of modern <a href="/wiki/Zermelo%E2%80%93Fraenkel_set_theory" title="Zermelo–Fraenkel set theory">set theory</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the war, in 1948, Schmidt founded and became the first editor-in-chief of the journal <i><a href="/wiki/Mathematische_Nachrichten" title="Mathematische Nachrichten">Mathematische Nachrichten</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mactutor_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-mactutor-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Socialism">National Socialism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Erhard_Schmidt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: National Socialism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> Schmidt held positions of authority at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Berlin" title="University of Berlin">University of Berlin</a> and had to carry out various <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nazi" title="Nazi">Nazi</a> resolutions against the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jew" title="Jew">Jews</a>—a job that he apparently did not do well, since he was criticized at one point for not understanding the "<a href="/wiki/Jewish_question" title="Jewish question">Jewish question</a>." At the celebration of Schmidt's 75th birthday in 1951 a prominent Jewish mathematician, <a href="/wiki/Hans_Freudenthal" title="Hans Freudenthal">Hans Freudenthal</a>, who had survived the Nazi years, spoke of the difficulties that Schmidt faced during that period without criticism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mactutor_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-mactutor-1">[1]</a></sup> He was, however, a conservative and a nationalist, and defended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hitler" title="Hitler">Hitler</a> after <a href="/wiki/Kristallnacht" title="Kristallnacht">Kristallnacht</a>, telling <a href="/wiki/Issai_Schur" title="Issai Schur">Issai Schur</a> that "Suppose we had to fight a war to rearm Germany, unite with Austria, liberate the Saar and the German part of Czechoslovakia. Such a war would have cost us half a million young men. But everybody would have admired our victorious leader. Now, Hitler has sacrificed half a million Jews and has achieved great things for Germany. I hope some day you will be recompensed but I am still grateful to Hitler".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
