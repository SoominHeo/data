<p><b>Ernst Conrad Rudolf Freiherr von Freyberg-Eisenberg</b> (born 26 October 1958 in <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a>) is a German manager and was President of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Istituto_per_le_Opere_di_Religione" title="Istituto per le Opere di Religione">Istituto per le Opere di Religione</a> between February 2013 and July 2014<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VIS_2014_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-VIS_2014-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-news.va_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-news.va-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ernst_von_Freyberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Freyberg is the eldest of three sons of Ulrich von Freyberg-Eisenberg and Brita Gertrud Blohm. He is married<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and a member of the <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta" title="Sovereign Military Order of Malta">Sovereign Military Order of Malta</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ernst_von_Freyberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He studied law at the universities of <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_Maximilian_University_of_Munich" title="Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich">Munich</a> and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bonn" title="University of Bonn">Bonn</a> from 1978 to 1985 and at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_University_of_Administrative_Sciences_Speyer" title="German University of Administrative Sciences Speyer">German University of Administrative Sciences Speyer</a> in 1986/87. He was admitted to the bar in Ulm in 1988.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ernst_von_Freyberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Founding_of_Finance_House_von_Freyberg">Founding of Finance House von Freyberg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ernst_von_Freyberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Founding of Finance House von Freyberg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 1988 to 1991, Freyberg worked as an analyst for <i>TCR Europe Limited</i> and <i>Three Cities Research, Inc</i>. In 1991, he co-founded <i>finance house von Freyberg</i>. In 1998, <i>Close Brothers Corporate Finance Ltd</i>, the corporate finance arm of <i>Close Brothers</i>, replaced finance house <a class="external text" href="http://www.melcofin.com/" rel="nofollow"><i>von Mellenthin</i></a> with finance house von Freyberg as its German associate, purchasing 51%. In 2005, <i>Close Brothers Corporate Finance</i> began consolidating its European network and finance house Freyberg was renamed <i>Freyberg Close Brothers</i>. In 2010, <i>Close Brothers Corporate Finance</i> became loss-making and <a href="/wiki/Close_Brothers_Group" title="Close Brothers Group">Close Brothers Group</a> put it up for sale. It was purchased by the Japanese investment bank <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daiwa_Securities_SMBC_Co.,_Ltd." title="Daiwa Securities SMBC Co., Ltd."><i>Daiwa Securities SMBC</i></a> and is now called <a class="external text" href="http://de.dcadvisory.com/" rel="nofollow"><i>Daiwa Corporate Advisory GmbH</i></a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2012, Ernst von Freyberg was replaced as CEO by Stefan Jaecker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chairmanship_and_supervisory_board_activities">Chairmanship and supervisory board activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ernst_von_Freyberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Chairmanship and supervisory board activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since 2012, he has been chairman of the <a href="/wiki/Supervisory_board" title="Supervisory board">supervisory board</a> of <a href="/wiki/Blohm_%2B_Voss" title="Blohm + Voss">Blohm + Voss</a>, and was/is member of other supervisory and advisory boards, e.g. of <a class="external text" href="http://www.malteser.de/ueber-die-malteser/malteser-deutschland-gemeinnuetzige-gmbh.html" rel="nofollow"><i>Malteser Deutschland GmbH</i></a>, the <i>Flossbach von Storch AG</i> trust administration and the IT service provider <i>Magirus AG</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Istituto_per_le_Opere_di_Religione_.28IOR.29">Istituto per le Opere di Religione (IOR)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ernst_von_Freyberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Istituto per le Opere di Religione (IOR)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ernst von Freyberg was appointed President of the Board of Superintendence of the <a class="external text" href="http://www.ior.va/" rel="nofollow">Istituto per le Opere di Religione</a> (IOR) by the Commission of Cardinals of the IOR on 15 February 2013 replacing <a href="/wiki/Ettore_Gotti_Tedeschi" title="Ettore Gotti Tedeschi">Ettore Gotti Tedeschi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Freyberg was named ad interim Director of the IOR on 1 July 2013, following the resignations of Director Paolo Cipriani and Deputy Director Massimo Tulli <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-news.va_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-news.va-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On 30 November 2013, Rolando Marranci was appointed new General Director.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>During his tenure at the IOR, von Freyberg has identified two particular tasks as critically important. Firstly, he wants to open up the Institute and make it more transparent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Secondly, he will investigate and, where necessary, eliminate any cases of money laundering and tax evasion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Von Freyberg has also espoused a “zero tolerance” approach when it comes to suspicious transactions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2014 it was announced that Freyberg had been replaced by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jean-Baptiste_de_Franssu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jean-Baptiste de Franssu (page does not exist)">Jean-Baptiste de Franssu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VIS_2014_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-VIS_2014-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Non-occupational_activities">Non-occupational activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ernst_von_Freyberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Non-occupational activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ernst von Freyberg is Knight of the <a class="external text" href="http://www.orderofmalta.int/?lang=en" rel="nofollow">Sovereign Military Order of Malta</a>. His voluntary work at the Order of Malta particularly focuses on accompanying pilgrimages to Lourdes and acting as honorary treasurer for the order's German association. In 2009, he also founded the <i>Freyberg Stiftung</i>. The foundation’s main goals lie in supporting teenagers through education.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
