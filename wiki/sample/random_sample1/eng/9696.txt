<p><b>Eric Anthony Duncan</b> (born December 7, 1984, in <a href="/wiki/Florham_Park,_New_Jersey" title="Florham Park, New Jersey">Florham Park, New Jersey</a>) is a retired American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baseball_player" title="Baseball player">baseball player</a>. Considered an excellent high school baseball player, Duncan was chosen by the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> in the first round of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2003_Major_League_Baseball_Draft" title="2003 Major League Baseball Draft">2003 Major League Baseball (MLB) Draft</a>, and became one of the best prospects in baseball. However, injuries and ineffectiveness in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minor_league_baseball" title="Minor league baseball">minor league baseball</a> prevented Duncan from reaching MLB.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Amateur_career">Amateur career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eric_Duncan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Amateur career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Duncan attended <a href="/wiki/Seton_Hall_Preparatory_School" title="Seton Hall Preparatory School">Seton Hall Preparatory School</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Orange,_New_Jersey" title="West Orange, New Jersey">West Orange, New Jersey</a>, which is also the alma mater of the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a> starting pitcher <a href="/wiki/Rick_Porcello" title="Rick Porcello">Rick Porcello</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eric_Duncan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Duncan was selected by the Yankees in the first round (27th overall) of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2003_Major_League_Baseball_Draft" title="2003 Major League Baseball Draft">2003 Major League Baseball Draft</a>. Prior to the 2005 season, <i><a href="/wiki/Baseball_America" title="Baseball America">Baseball America</a></i> named Duncan the Yankees' 2005 top prospect, and the 36th best prospect in baseball. Prior to the 2006 season, <i>Baseball America</i> ranked him the 86th best prospect in baseball.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Duncan was drafted as a third baseman, but was converted into a first baseman while in the Yankee organization, due to the long-term contract of Yankee <a href="/wiki/Alex_Rodriguez" title="Alex Rodriguez">Alex Rodriguez</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> However, Duncan began to struggle when he reached <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_(baseball)" title="Triple-A (baseball)">Triple-A</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the 2009 season, the Yankees released Duncan. He spent the 2010 season in the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Braves" title="Atlanta Braves">Atlanta Braves</a> organization, playing for the Double-A <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_Braves" title="Mississippi Braves">Mississippi Braves</a>. Duncan signed a minor league contract with an invitation to 2011 spring training with the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rockies" title="Colorado Rockies">Colorado Rockies</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He was released on April 4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On April 19, Duncan was signed to a minor league contract by the St. Louis Cardinals and assigned to Double-A <a href="/wiki/Springfield_Cardinals" title="Springfield Cardinals">Springfield</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He signed a minor league contract with the Kansas City Royals on November 16, 2011 and was assigned to the Double A <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Arkansas_Naturals" title="Northwest Arkansas Naturals">Northwest Arkansas Naturals</a> (Texas League). He suffered a torn quadriceps during spring training in 2012 and returned to the field on May 5. In July 2012, he announced his voluntary retirement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eric_Duncan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>2003 - 1st Team High School All-American IF</li>
<li>2004 - Midwest League All-Star 3B</li>
<li>2006 - Arizona Fall League All-Star 3B</li>
<li>2006 - Arizona Fall League MVP</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">Coaching career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eric_Duncan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Coaching career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following his retirement, Duncan became a volunteer coach for the <a href="/wiki/Seton_Hall_Pirates" title="Seton Hall Pirates">Seton Hall Pirates</a> of <a href="/wiki/Seton_Hall_University" title="Seton Hall University">Seton Hall University</a>. He is also a student at Seton Hall, majoring in <a href="/wiki/Political_science" title="Political science">political science</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, the <a href="/wiki/Staten_Island_Yankees" title="Staten Island Yankees">Staten Island Yankees</a> named Duncan as their hitting coach, he returned to the same position in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
