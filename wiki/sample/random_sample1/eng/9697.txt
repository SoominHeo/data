<p><b>Eric Lawrence Lively</b> (born August 31, 1981), better known as <b>Eric Lively</b>, is an American actor. He played the role Nick Larson in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Butterfly_Effect_2" title="The Butterfly Effect 2">The Butterfly Effect 2</a></i>. He is also known for his role as <a href="/wiki/List_of_The_L_Word_characters#W" title="List of The L Word characters">Mark Wayland</a> in <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a>'s hit drama series <i><a href="/wiki/The_L_Word" title="The L Word">The L Word</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eric_Lively&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Eric Lawrence Brown was born in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>. He has a sister, actress <a href="/wiki/Blake_Lively" title="Blake Lively">Blake Lively</a>, half-sisters <a href="/wiki/Lori_Lively" title="Lori Lively">Lori</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robyn_Lively" title="Robyn Lively">Robyn Lively</a>, and a half-brother, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Lively" title="Jason Lively">Jason Lively</a>, who played Rusty in <i><a href="/wiki/National_Lampoon%27s_European_Vacation" title="National Lampoon's European Vacation">National Lampoon's European Vacation</a></i>. He is the son of actor <a href="/wiki/Ernie_Lively" title="Ernie Lively">Ernie Lively</a> (né Ernest Wilson Brown, Jr.) and talent manager Elaine Lively (née McAlpin).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> All four siblings/half-siblings are, or have been, in the entertainment industry.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eric_Lively&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lively's first film was as a baby in the 1983 film <i><a href="/wiki/Brainstorm_(1983_film)" title="Brainstorm (1983 film)">Brainstorm</a></i>. After graduating high school, Lively moved to New York City to study <a href="/wiki/Photography" title="Photography">photography</a>, his first passion. He studied photography at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parsons_The_New_School_for_Design" title="Parsons The New School for Design">Parsons The New School for Design</a> in New York City. At one time, he was an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abercrombie_and_Fitch" title="Abercrombie and Fitch">Abercrombie and Fitch</a> model.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>He guest starred on an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Full_House" title="Full House">Full House</a></i> at age 13, where he played <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stephanie_Tanner" title="Stephanie Tanner">Stephanie Tanner</a>'s first boyfriend. He had a role in <i><a href="/wiki/American_Pie_(film)" title="American Pie (film)">American Pie</a></i> (1999). He followed this up on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a> <i><a href="/wiki/So_Weird" title="So Weird">So Weird</a></i>, on which he played Carey Bell from 2000 to 2001. He learned to play the guitar for his character on <i><a href="/wiki/So_Weird" title="So Weird">So Weird</a></i>. Lively then became a cast member of the short-lived 2003 <a href="/wiki/Norm_Macdonald" title="Norm Macdonald">Norm Macdonald</a> series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Minute_With_Stan_Hooper" title="A Minute With Stan Hooper">A Minute With Stan Hooper</a></i>. In 2004 he acted in <i><a href="/wiki/Speak_(film)" title="Speak (film)">Speak</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Kristen_Stewart" title="Kristen Stewart">Kristen Stewart</a>. In 2005, Lively was offered two different television roles - on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/24_(television)" title="24 (television)">24</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_L_Word" title="The L Word">The L Word</a></i>. He took the role on <i>The L Word</i> as <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">documentary</a> filmmaker Mark Wayland. He chose a recurring role on <i>The L Word</i> over a regular role on <i>24</i> because he felt the subject matter was more important. He was part of the cast of <a href="/wiki/The_WB" title="The WB">the WB</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Modern_Men" title="Modern Men">Modern Men</a></i> from March until May 2006. He was the main character in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Butterfly_Effect_2" title="The Butterfly Effect 2">The Butterfly Effect 2</a></i> in 2006.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Lively played the President's son, Roger Taylor, in <i><a href="/wiki/24:_Redemption" title="24: Redemption">24: Redemption</a></i>, a television film prequel to the <a href="/wiki/24_(season_7)" title="24 (season 7)">seventh season</a> of <i>24</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lively appeared as <a href="/wiki/Pink_(singer)" title="Pink (singer)">Pink</a>'s on-screen boyfriend in her music video "<a href="/wiki/Please_Don%27t_Leave_Me" title="Please Don't Leave Me">Please Don't Leave Me</a>" in 2009, with stuntman Justin Sundquist serving as his stunt double. In 2012, he guest starred in the <a href="/wiki/Lifetime_(TV_network)" title="Lifetime (TV network)">Lifetime</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Client_List_(TV_series)" title="The Client List (TV series)">The Client List</a></i>.</p>
