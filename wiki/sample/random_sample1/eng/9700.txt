<p><b>Eric Johann Johnson</b> (born August 7, 1979) is a Canadian actor known for playing <a href="/wiki/Flash_Gordon" title="Flash Gordon">Flash Gordon</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Flash_Gordon_(2007_TV_series)" title="Flash Gordon (2007 TV series)">eponymous 2007-2008 TV series</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Whitney_Fordman" title="Whitney Fordman">Whitney Fordman</a> on the likewise <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Science-fiction" title="Science-fiction">science-fiction</a> TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Smallville" title="Smallville">Smallville</a></i>, and for portraying <a href="/wiki/List_of_Rookie_Blue_characters#Luke_Callaghan" title="List of Rookie Blue characters">Detective Luke Callaghan</a> on the police drama <i><a href="/wiki/Rookie_Blue" title="Rookie Blue">Rookie Blue</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eric_Johnson_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Johnson began his career acting at age nine, when his parents enrolled him in <i>Stage Polaris</i>, a theater company in Edmonton.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Johnson played the younger version of <a href="/wiki/Brad_Pitt" title="Brad Pitt">Brad Pitt</a>'s character in <i><a href="/wiki/Legends_of_the_Fall" title="Legends of the Fall">Legends of the Fall</a></i>. In Canada, Johnson was the lead in an award-winning film titled <i>Scorn</i>. He got his breakout the next year in 2001 as football jock and Clark Kent rival Whitney Fordman in <a href="/wiki/Smallville" title="Smallville">Smallville</a>.</p>
<p>Johnson played the lead role in a new <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flash_Gordon_(2007_TV_Series)" title="Flash Gordon (2007 TV Series)">Flash Gordon</a></i> TV series that debuted on <a href="/wiki/Syfy" title="Syfy">Sci Fi</a> in August 2007. The show was canceled after one season, in April 2008. On November 27, 2010 he starred as Jake Finley in the Hallmark movie, <a href="/wiki/Debbie_Macomber" title="Debbie Macomber">Debbie Macomber</a>'s <i>Call Me Mrs. Miracle</i>. He portrays Luke Callahan on the Canadian police drama <i><a href="/wiki/Rookie_Blue" title="Rookie Blue">Rookie Blue</a></i> and inmate Cal Sweeney, a bank robber, on the Fox series <i><a href="/wiki/Alcatraz_(TV_series)" title="Alcatraz (TV series)">Alcatraz</a></i>. In 2012, Johnson was cast as the voice actor and physical actor of <a href="/wiki/Sam_Fisher_(Splinter_Cell)" title="Sam Fisher (Splinter Cell)">Sam Fisher</a> in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Splinter_Cell:_Blacklist" title="Splinter Cell: Blacklist">Splinter Cell: Blacklist</a></i>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Michael_Ironside" title="Michael Ironside">Michael Ironside</a>, who voiced the character in previous installments.</p>
<p>In 2013, Johnson was cast in <a href="/wiki/Cinemax" title="Cinemax">Cinemax</a>'s original medical drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Knick" title="The Knick">The Knick</a></i> as a racist, but talented, young medic who is <a href="/wiki/Clive_Owen" title="Clive Owen">Clive Owen</a>'s protege; the series is being executive produced by <a href="/wiki/Steven_Soderbergh" title="Steven Soderbergh">Steven Soderbergh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 12, 2016, Johnson was cast in <i><a href="/wiki/Fifty_Shades_Darker_(film)" title="Fifty Shades Darker (film)">Fifty Shades Darker</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Fifty_Shades_Freed_(film)" title="Fifty Shades Freed (film)">Fifty Shades Freed</a></i> as Jack Hyde.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eric_Johnson_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2004, Johnson married writer-producer Adria Budd, whom he met on the set of <i><a href="/wiki/Smallville" title="Smallville">Smallville</a></i>, as she was <a href="/wiki/Annette_O%27Toole" title="Annette O'Toole">Annette O'Toole</a>'s and <a href="/wiki/John_Glover_(actor)" title="John Glover (actor)">John Golver</a>'s personal assistant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> They have a daughter, Calla Prairie Johnson, who was born in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
