<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eric_Heisserer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Heisserer's professional screenwriting career was launched with the sale of <i>The Dionaea House</i> to Warner Bros. in 2005,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> based on an online <a href="/wiki/Epistolary_novel" title="Epistolary novel">epistolary</a> story of the same name that he wrote in October 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He then developed an original television pilot for <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Pictures" title="Paramount Pictures">Paramount Pictures</a> and <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a>, and wrote feature projects for <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Bruckheimer" title="Jerry Bruckheimer">Jerry Bruckheimer</a> Films and <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Bros.</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2007, he sold a pitch to <a href="/wiki/Regency_Enterprises" title="Regency Enterprises">Regency Enterprises</a> and Fox called <i>Inhuman</i>, a supernatural thriller set in Tokyo that combines live action and anime sequences.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In December 2008, Heisserer was hired to re-envision and rewrite the script for the franchise reboot of <i><a href="/wiki/A_Nightmare_on_Elm_Street_(2010_film)" title="A Nightmare on Elm Street (2010 film)">A Nightmare on Elm Street</a></i>, produced by <a href="/wiki/Platinum_Dunes" title="Platinum Dunes">Platinum Dunes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> An early draft had been written by <a href="/wiki/Wesley_Strick" title="Wesley Strick">Wesley Strick</a>. The script went on to land director <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Bayer" title="Samuel Bayer">Samuel Bayer</a>, actor <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Earle_Haley" title="Jackie Earle Haley">Jackie Earle Haley</a>, and began filming in May 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>He rewrote the <a href="/wiki/Prequel" title="Prequel">prequel</a> to director <a href="/wiki/John_Carpenter" title="John Carpenter">John Carpenter</a>'s 1982 remake <i><a href="/wiki/The_Thing_(1982_film)" title="The Thing (1982 film)">The Thing</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In April 2010, Heisserer signed on to write <i><a href="/wiki/Final_Destination_5" title="Final Destination 5">Final Destination 5</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> the fifth film of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Final_Destination_(film_series)" title="Final Destination (film series)">horror film franchise</a>.</p>
<p>Heisserer made his directorial debut with the film <i><a href="/wiki/Hours_(2013_film)" title="Hours (2013 film)">Hours</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Paul_Walker" title="Paul Walker">Paul Walker</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2013 Heisserer was set to write the script for the then upcoming thriller book <i><a href="/wiki/Bird_Box" title="Bird Box">Bird Box</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Josh_Malerman" title="Josh Malerman">Josh Malerman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Pictures" title="Universal Pictures">Universal Pictures</a> acquired the rights in February 2013 and now set the script for <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Muschietti" title="Andrés Muschietti">Andy Muschietti</a> to direct the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Heisserer co-wrote <i><a href="/wiki/The_Conjuring_2" title="The Conjuring 2">The Conjuring 2</a></i> along with the brothers <a href="/wiki/Chad_Hayes_(writer)" title="Chad Hayes (writer)">Chad Hayes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carey_Hayes" title="Carey Hayes">Carey Hayes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Heisserer wrote along with artist <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Felipe_Massafera&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Felipe Massafera (page does not exist)">Felipe Massafera</a> and Colorist <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Wes_Dzioba&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Wes Dzioba (page does not exist)">Wes Dzioba</a>, the comic book series <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shaper_(comic)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shaper (comic) (page does not exist)">Shaper</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eric_Heisserer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Heisserer is the son of Margaret L., a senior editor for a publishing company, and Andrew J. Heisserer, a professor of ancient history, of <a href="/wiki/Norman,_Oklahoma" title="Norman, Oklahoma">Norman, Oklahoma</a>. Since 2010, Heisserer has been married to television producer/writer <a href="/wiki/Christine_Boylan" title="Christine Boylan">Christine Boylan</a>. His previous marriage ended in divorce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
