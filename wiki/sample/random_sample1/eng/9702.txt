<p><b>Eriksson</b> is a common <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a> <a href="/wiki/Patronymic" title="Patronymic">patronymic</a> <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> meaning "son of Erik", itself an <a href="/wiki/Old_Norse" title="Old Norse">Old Norse</a> <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> There are other spelling variations of this surname such as Erikson, Ericson, Ericsson and Erixon. Erikson is uncommon as a <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a>.</p>
<p>Notable people with the surname include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Charlotta_Eriksson" title="Charlotta Eriksson">Charlotta Eriksson</a> (1794–1862), Swedish actress</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Erik_Homburger_Erikson" title="Erik Homburger Erikson">Erik Homburger Erikson</a> (born 1902), <a href="/wiki/Developmental_psychology" title="Developmental psychology">developmental psychologist</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Psychoanalyst" title="Psychoanalyst">psychoanalyst</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Felix_Eriksson" title="Felix Eriksson">Felix Eriksson</a> (born 1992), Swedish professional ice hockey player</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hans-Erik_Eriksson" title="Hans-Erik Eriksson">Hans-Erik Eriksson</a> (born 1961), Swedish computer scientist</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Helena_Eriksson" title="Helena Eriksson">Helena Eriksson</a> (born 1962), Swedish poet</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Eriksson" title="Kenneth Eriksson">Kenneth Eriksson</a> (born 1956), Swedish rally driver</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leif_Eriksson" title="Leif Eriksson">Leif Eriksson</a> (<i>Leifr Eiríksson</i>) (c. 970 – c. 1020), Icelandic-born Norse explorer, son of Erik the Red</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lena_Eriksson" title="Lena Eriksson">Lena Eriksson</a> (born 1972), Swedish breaststroke swimmer</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lennart_Eriksson_(musician)" title="Lennart Eriksson (musician)">Lennart Eriksson</a> (born 1956), Swedish musician (<a href="/wiki/Ebba_Gr%C3%B6n" title="Ebba Grön">Ebba Grön</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Matz_Robert_Eriksson" title="Matz Robert Eriksson">Matz Robert Eriksson</a> (born 1972), Swedish drummer</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Eriksson" title="Sebastian Eriksson">Sebastian Eriksson</a> (born 1989), Swedish footballer</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sven-G%C3%B6ran_Eriksson" title="Sven-Göran Eriksson">Sven-Göran Eriksson</a> (born 1948), Swedish football manager</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sven-Olov_Eriksson" title="Sven-Olov Eriksson">Sven-Olov Eriksson</a> (1929–1999), Swedish runner</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ursula_and_Sabina_Eriksson" title="Ursula and Sabina Eriksson">Ursula and Sabina Eriksson</a>, born in <a href="/wiki/Sunne,_Sweden" title="Sunne, Sweden">Sunne, Sweden</a>, are Swedish identical twins, who survived suicide attempts by being run over by motorway traffic in front of the Police, with Sabina committing manslaughter the following day, in the UK in 2008</li>
</ul>
