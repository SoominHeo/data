<p><b>Erin Daniels</b> (born October 9, 1973) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a>. She is best known for her role as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dana_Fairbanks" title="Dana Fairbanks">Dana Fairbanks</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/The_L_Word" title="The L Word">The L Word</a></i> (2004–2007). Her feature film work includes <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Single_Man_(film)" title="A Single Man (film)">A Single Man</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/One_Hour_Photo" title="One Hour Photo">One Hour Photo</a></i>, and <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Joshua_Tree,_1951:_A_Portrait_of_James_Dean&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Joshua Tree, 1951: A Portrait of James Dean (page does not exist)">Joshua Tree, 1951: A Portrait of James Dean</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Erin_Daniels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Daniels was born <b>Erin Cohen</b> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Louis,_Missouri" title="St. Louis, Missouri">St. Louis, Missouri</a>, where she grew up. Her father was an <a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">architect</a> and her mother was a clinical social worker. She grew up in a <a href="/wiki/American_Jews" title="American Jews">Jewish</a> family; her mother co-founded the Central Reform Congregation in St. Louis and her grandmother received an award from the <a href="/wiki/National_Conference_for_Community_and_Justice" title="National Conference for Community and Justice">National Conference of Christians and Jews</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jw_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-jw-1">[1]</a></sup> She attended <a href="/wiki/Clayton_High_School_(Missouri)" title="Clayton High School (Missouri)">Clayton High School</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vassar_College" title="Vassar College">Vassar College</a>, graduating from the latter in 1995<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vassar_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-vassar-2">[2]</a></sup> after majoring in art.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jw_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-jw-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Erin_Daniels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After Vassar, she moved to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, where she studied with William Esper, a protégé of <a href="/wiki/Sanford_Meisner" title="Sanford Meisner">Sanford Meisner</a>, and worked <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Off_Broadway" title="Off Broadway">Off Broadway</a>. She then moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles</a> for a part on the short-lived TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Action_(TV_series)" title="Action (TV series)">Action</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jw_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-jw-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2002, the St. Louis Film Festival awarded Daniels the Emerging Star Award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/One_Hour_Photo" title="One Hour Photo">One Hour Photo</a></i>, <i>Wheelmen</i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/House_of_1000_Corpses" title="House of 1000 Corpses">House of 1000 Corpses</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> before becoming a major cast member of <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_L_Word" title="The L Word">The L Word</a></i>. Her depiction of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dana_Fairbanks" title="Dana Fairbanks">Dana Fairbanks</a> on that show prompted an anonymous donation of a million dollars to the Dr. Susan Love Research Foundation,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> an organization that promotes and funds <a href="/wiki/Breast_cancer" title="Breast cancer">breast cancer</a> <a href="/wiki/Lactiferous_duct" title="Lactiferous duct">intraductal</a> research.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Daniels has also appeared in the TV shows <i><a href="/wiki/Action_(TV_series)" title="Action (TV series)">Action</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Jericho_(2006_TV_series)" title="Jericho (2006 TV series)">Jericho</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dexter_(TV_series)" title="Dexter (TV series)">Dexter</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Saving_Grace_(TV_series)" title="Saving Grace (TV series)">Saving Grace</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Shots_(TV_series)" title="Big Shots (TV series)">Big Shots</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Justice_(2006_TV_series)" title="Justice (2006 TV series)">Justice</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Swingtown" title="Swingtown">Swingtown</a></i>.</p>
<p>She played the role of The Mother in director Matthew Mishory's debut feature, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Joshua_Tree,_1951:_A_Portrait_of_James_Dean&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Joshua Tree, 1951: A Portrait of James Dean (page does not exist)">Joshua Tree, 1951: A Portrait of James Dean</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Erin_Daniels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Daniels married producer Chris Uettwiller in 2008. They are currently living in Los Angeles with their two children; a son born 2009 and a daughter born 2012.</p>
