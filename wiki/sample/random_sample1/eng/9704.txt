<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Football_career">Football career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emanuele_Calai%C3%B2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Football career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Calaiò started his football career at <a href="/wiki/Torino_F.C." title="Torino F.C.">Torino</a> making his league debut against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reggina_Calcio" title="Reggina Calcio">Reggina</a> on 6 January 2000, he scored a goal for Torino three minutes into his debut. He made 20 league appearances in total for Torino.</p>
<p>In January 2002 Calaiò was exchanged with <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Cibocchi" title="Alessandro Cibocchi">Alessandro Cibocchi</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ternana_Calcio" title="Ternana Calcio">Ternana</a> with inflated price tag to create paper profit on both clubs (which Ternana was fined in 2010, 8 years later<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>) and then a short spell with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Messina_Peloro" title="F.C. Messina Peloro">Messina</a> followed. In January 2003, was loaned by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pescara_Calcio" title="Pescara Calcio">Pescara</a> and turned permanently in June 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He also returned to Torino Calcio in 2003 in exchange with Cibocchi again.</p>
<p>With Pescara, Calaiò solidified himself as an impressive striker, the club won promotion back into <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> in 2003, with Calaiò scoring 20 times in his second season. In the third at Serie B, he scored 6 in the first half of the season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Napoli">Napoli</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emanuele_Calai%C3%B2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Napoli">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2005, <a href="/wiki/S.S.C._Napoli" title="S.S.C. Napoli">Napoli Soccer</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C1" title="Serie C1">Serie C1</a> signed him for €2.85 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> he scored just 6 in the first season, but in the second he scored 18, won the champion and promotion back to Serie B with club. He scored 14 in his third season with Napoli, won promotion back to Serie A. With the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Marcelo_Zalayeta" title="Marcelo Zalayeta">Marcelo Zalayeta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ezequiel_Lavezzi" title="Ezequiel Lavezzi">Ezequiel Lavezzi</a>, he had limited chances to play.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Siena">Siena</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emanuele_Calai%C3%B2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Siena">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2008, he joined fellow Serie A team, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Siena" title="A.C. Siena">Siena</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Co-ownership_(football)" title="Co-ownership (football)">co-ownership</a> bid, for €2.3 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> in June 2009 Siena signed him outright for €1.25 million (which made Napoli register a financial loss of €1.05 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He followed Siena relegated to Serie B in 2010 and finished as the runner-up and promoted back to top division. On 10 June 2011 he signed a new 3-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Calaiò was one of the two starting strikers along with <a href="/wiki/Mattia_Destro" title="Mattia Destro">Mattia Destro</a> in <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Serie_A" title="2011–12 Serie A">2011–12 Serie A</a> season, scoring 11 goals in 25 league matches until he broken his leg in March 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Napoli">Return to Napoli</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emanuele_Calai%C3%B2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Return to Napoli">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 11 January 2013, his transfer to former team <a href="/wiki/S.S.C._Napoli" title="S.S.C. Napoli">Napoli</a> was officially announced. He arrived on-loan with the option to make the switch permanent if Napoli qualified for the Champions League during the 2013 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Catania">Catania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emanuele_Calai%C3%B2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Catania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 11 July 2014, his transfer to <a href="/wiki/Calcio_Catania" title="Calcio Catania">Catania</a> was officially announced. He signed a two-year contract with an option for a third.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spezia">Spezia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emanuele_Calai%C3%B2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Spezia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 3 August 2015 Calaiò was signed by <a href="/wiki/Spezia_Calcio" title="Spezia Calcio">Spezia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Parma">Parma</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emanuele_Calai%C3%B2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Parma">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 4 August 2016 Calaiò was sold to <a href="/wiki/Lega_Pro" title="Lega Pro">Lega Pro</a> newcomer <a href="/wiki/Parma_Calcio_1913" title="Parma Calcio 1913">Parma</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emanuele_Calai%C3%B2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Calaiò capped for both <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">Italy U21</a> and Serie B U21 selection which played the latter for the match 3–2 won <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_under-21_football_team" title="Belgium national under-21 football team">Belgium U21</a> on 30 March 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He also received the Serie B U21 call-up for the match against <a href="/wiki/A.C._Legnano" title="A.C. Legnano">Legnano</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>He also call-up to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2000_UEFA_European_Under-18_Football_Championship" title="2000 UEFA European Under-18 Football Championship">2000 UEFA European Under-18 Football Championship</a> qualification and played in qualifying match in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2001_UEFA_European_Under-18_Football_Championship" title="2001 UEFA European Under-18 Football Championship">2001 edition</a>. Calaiò capped once for U17 team, at that time the feeder team of U18. (now called <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-18_football_team" title="Italy national under-18 football team">Italy national under-18 football team</a>)</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emanuele_Calai%C3%B2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Calaiò is married to Federica, sister of <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Mora" title="Nicola Mora">Nicola Mora</a>'s wife.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Both players played for Torino during the 2000–01 season and Napoli in 2004–05.</p>
