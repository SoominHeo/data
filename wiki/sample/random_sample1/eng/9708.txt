<p><b>Emily Jordan Osment</b> (born March 10, 1992)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TV_Guide_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-TV_Guide-1">[1]</a></sup> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> actress, singer, and songwriter born in Los Angeles, California.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TV_Guide_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-TV_Guide-1">[1]</a></sup> After working in several television films in her childhood, she gained fame for co-starring as the character Gerti Giggles in <i><a href="/wiki/Spy_Kids_2:_The_Island_of_Lost_Dreams" title="Spy Kids 2: The Island of Lost Dreams">Spy Kids 2: The Island of Lost Dreams</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Spy_Kids_3-D:_Game_Over" title="Spy Kids 3-D: Game Over">Spy Kids 3-D: Game Over</a></i>. She went on to co-star in the <a href="/wiki/59th_Primetime_Emmy_Awards" title="59th Primetime Emmy Awards">Emmy Award-nominated</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">sitcom</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Hannah_Montana" title="Hannah Montana">Hannah Montana</a></i> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lilly_Truscott" title="Lilly Truscott">Lilly Truscott</a> as well as the series movie, <i><a href="/wiki/Hannah_Montana:_The_Movie" title="Hannah Montana: The Movie">Hannah Montana: The Movie</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She also starred as Cassie in <a href="/wiki/R._L._Stine" title="R. L. Stine">R. L. Stine</a>'s: <i><a href="/wiki/The_Haunting_Hour:_Don%27t_Think_About_It" title="The Haunting Hour: Don't Think About It">The Haunting Hour Volume One: Don't Think About It</a></i> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney_Channel_Original_Movie" title="Disney Channel Original Movie">Disney Channel Original Movie</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dadnapped" title="Dadnapped">Dadnapped</a></i> as Melissa Morris. Osment expanded her repertoire into pop music and <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">alternative</a>/<a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a> where she has recorded teen pop hits like "<a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Think_About_It" title="I Don't Think About It">I Don't Think About It</a>", "<a href="/wiki/If_I_Didn%27t_Have_You" title="If I Didn't Have You">If I Didn't Have You</a>" alongside her <i><a href="/wiki/Hannah_Montana" title="Hannah Montana">Hannah Montana</a></i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Mitchel_Musso" title="Mitchel Musso">Mitchel Musso</a>, and "<a href="/wiki/Once_Upon_a_Dream_(Sleeping_Beauty_song)" title="Once Upon a Dream (Sleeping Beauty song)">Once Upon a Dream</a>".</p>
<p>Osment entered the world of <a href="/wiki/Music" title="Music">music</a> recording soundtracks for movie singles and <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney</a> albums. According to an interview at the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">2008 Grammys</a>, she was writing some songs together with <a href="/wiki/Eve_6" title="Eve 6">Eve 6</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2009 she signed with the <a href="/wiki/Wind-up_Records" title="Wind-up Records">Wind-up Records</a> label and released "<a href="/wiki/All_the_Way_Up_(Emily_Osment_song)" title="All the Way Up (Emily Osment song)">All the Way Up</a>", the first single from her <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> debut, <i><a href="/wiki/All_the_Right_Wrongs" title="All the Right Wrongs">All the Right Wrongs</a></i>, released on October 26, 2009. The EP debuted at the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and her songs reached the top positions on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a> and <a href="/wiki/Radio_Disney" title="Radio Disney">Radio Disney</a>. The EP was promoted within the United States by the 2010 Clap Your Hands Tour.</p>
<p>Osment's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Fight_or_Flight_(Emily_Osment_album)" title="Fight or Flight (Emily Osment album)">Fight or Flight</a></i> was released on October 5, 2010 via <a href="/wiki/Wind-up_Records" title="Wind-up Records">Wind-up Records</a>. The lead single "<a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Be_Friends_(song)" title="Let's Be Friends (song)">Let's Be Friends</a>" was released on June 8, 2010<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and the second single "<a href="/wiki/Lovesick_(Emily_Osment_song)" title="Lovesick (Emily Osment song)">Lovesick</a>" was released on October 19, 2010. In October 2010 she began her Fight or Flight Tour, whose first performance took place in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>.</p>
<p>She starred in the 2011 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_ABC_Family_Original_Movies" title="List of ABC Family Original Movies">ABC Family Original Movie</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cyberbully_(2011_film)" title="Cyberbully (2011 film)">Cyberbully</a></i>, the 2013/2014 series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cleaners_(2013_TV_series)" title="Cleaners (2013 TV series)">Cleaners</a></i>, produced for the online service <a href="/wiki/Crackle_(company)" title="Crackle (company)">Crackle</a> and the 2014 <a href="/wiki/Lifetime_(TV_network)" title="Lifetime (TV network)">Lifetime</a> movie <i><a href="/wiki/A_Daughter%27s_Nightmare" title="A Daughter's Nightmare">A Daughter's Nightmare</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Studio_Universal" title="Studio Universal">Studio Universal</a> movie <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kiss_Me_(film_2014)" title="Kiss Me (film 2014)">Kiss Me</a></i>. She currently stars in the <a href="/wiki/Freeform_(TV_channel)" title="Freeform (TV channel)">Freeform</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Young_%26_Hungry" title="Young &amp; Hungry">Young &amp; Hungry</a></i> as Gabi Diamond.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emily_Osment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Osment was born in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, where her family still resides, to actor Michael Eugene Osment,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and Theresa Osment (<i>née</i> Seifert), an English teacher. She was raised as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_(Los_Angeles_archdiocese,_California,_USA)" title="Roman Catholic (Los Angeles archdiocese, California, USA)">Roman Catholic</a>. Her father has appeared in various films, including her movie <i><a href="/wiki/Soccer_Mom_(film)" title="Soccer Mom (film)">Soccer Mom</a></i>, and her older brother is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a>-nominated actor <a href="/wiki/Haley_Joel_Osment" title="Haley Joel Osment">Haley Joel Osment</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emily_Osment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1998.E2.80.932005:_Career_beginning">1998–2005: Career beginning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emily_Osment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1998–2005: Career beginning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Osment's entry into the entertainment industry began in 1998, when she was cast in a commercial for flower delivery company <a href="/wiki/Florists%27_Transworld_Delivery" title="Florists' Transworld Delivery">FTD</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emilybio_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-emilybio-7">[7]</a></sup> From there she went on to feature in many commercials, after which she made her acting debut in the 1999 film <i>The Secret Life of Girls</i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Eugene_Levy" title="Eugene Levy">Eugene Levy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Linda_Hamilton" title="Linda Hamilton">Linda Hamilton</a>. The same year, she co-starred with <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Close" title="Glenn Close">Glenn Close</a> in the Hallmark Classic television film <i><a href="/wiki/Sarah,_Plain_and_Tall:_Winter%27s_End" title="Sarah, Plain and Tall: Winter's End">Sarah, Plain and Tall: Winter's End</a></i>, a role that would lead a <a href="/wiki/Young_Artist_Award" title="Young Artist Award">Young Artist Award</a> nomination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emilybio_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-emilybio-7">[7]</a></sup> Since then, she has gone on to do a number of roles, such as appearances on television shows like <i><a href="/wiki/Touched_by_an_Angel" title="Touched by an Angel">Touched by an Angel</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Friends" title="Friends">Friends</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/3rd_Rock_from_the_Sun" title="3rd Rock from the Sun">3rd Rock from the Sun</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2002, Osment made her film debut as Gerti Giggles in <i><a href="/wiki/Spy_Kids_2:_The_Island_of_Lost_Dreams" title="Spy Kids 2: The Island of Lost Dreams">Spy Kids 2: The Island of Lost Dreams</a></i> which grossed $229 million globally and for which she won the "Young Artist Award" for best performance in a feature film. In 2003, she reprised her role in the third film of the <i>Spy Kids</i> series, <i><a href="/wiki/Spy_Kids_3-D:_Game_Over" title="Spy Kids 3-D: Game Over">Spy Kids 3-D: Game Over</a></i>, which grossed $197 million worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Osment did a voice-over role in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Disney_Channel_Original_Movies" title="List of Disney Channel Original Movies">Disney movie</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Lilo_%26_Stitch_2:_Stitch_Has_a_Glitch" title="Lilo &amp; Stitch 2: Stitch Has a Glitch">Lilo &amp; Stitch 2: Stitch Has a Glitch</a></i> and also a voice role in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edward_Fubbwupper_Fibbed_Big" title="Edward Fubbwupper Fibbed Big">Edward Fubbwupper Fibbed Big</a></i>.</p>

<p>In 2006, Osment got the role of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lilly_Truscott" title="Lilly Truscott">Lilly Truscott</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Hannah_Montana" title="Hannah Montana">Hannah Montana</a></i>, the best friend of Miley Stewart and girlfriend to Oliver Oken on seasons 3 and 4. When Miley was Hannah, Lilly was under the <a href="/wiki/Pseudonym" title="Pseudonym">alias</a> Lola Luftnagle. The series premiere scored record ratings for the Disney Channel with 5.4 million viewers, a response "beyond our wildest expectations", according to the president of Disney Channel Entertainment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tweens_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-tweens-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The show earned her a nomination for <a href="/wiki/Young_Artist_Award" title="Young Artist Award">Young Artist Award</a> in 2007 for best performance in a TV Series (Comedy or Drama). Osment also appeared in promoting <i><a href="/wiki/Hannah_Montana" title="Hannah Montana">Hannah Montana</a></i>, Hannah Montana 's Backstage Secrets, which plays Lola in a fictional interview in the series canon . In addition, Osment helped to design some of the pieces from the collection of clothing Hannah Montana Disney, which released the last part in the summer of 2006. She also had a voice over role in a movie called <i><a href="/wiki/Holidaze:_The_Christmas_That_Almost_Didn%27t_Happen" title="Holidaze: The Christmas That Almost Didn't Happen">Holidaze: The Christmas That Almost Didn't Happen</a></i>. She performed several songs for the movie, including "Don't Ya Just Love Christmas" and "One Day". Emily sings the chorus of the song <a href="/wiki/Billy_Ray_Cyrus" title="Billy Ray Cyrus">Billy Ray Cyrus</a> "You've Got a Friend" , from his album 2007 <i>Home at Last</i>. Osment has also recorded a song titled "<a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Think_About_It" title="I Don't Think About It">I Don't Think About It</a>" in <a href="/wiki/The_Haunting_Hour:_Don%27t_Think_About_It" title="The Haunting Hour: Don't Think About It">The Haunting Hour: Don't Think About It</a>, for which they made a music video. The video was released in Haunting Hour DVD and premiered at <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a>.</p>
<p>On December 25, Osment hosted the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_World_Christmas_Day_Parade" title="Walt Disney World Christmas Day Parade">Walt Disney World Christmas Day Parade</a> with <i>Hannah Montana</i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Mitchel_Musso" title="Mitchel Musso">Mitchel Musso</a>. The parade featured acts like <a href="/wiki/Miley_Cyrus" title="Miley Cyrus">Miley Cyrus</a> and the <i><a href="/wiki/High_School_Musical" title="High School Musical">High School Musical</a></i> cast. She and Musso performed a sketch based on a <i>Hannah Montana</i> episode. Osment also had a voice over role in a movie called Holidaze: The Christmas That Almost Didn't Happen, which also featured the voices of fellow Disney Channel stars <a href="/wiki/Brenda_Song" title="Brenda Song">Brenda Song</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dylan_and_Cole_Sprouse" title="Dylan and Cole Sprouse">Dylan and Cole Sprouse</a>. Emily performed several songs for the film, including "Don't Ya Just Love Christmas" and "One Day". She also starred as a <a href="/wiki/Goth_subculture" title="Goth subculture">goth</a> in <a href="/wiki/R._L._Stine" title="R. L. Stine">R. L. Stine</a>'s: <i><a href="/wiki/The_Haunting_Hour:_Don%27t_Think_About_It" title="The Haunting Hour: Don't Think About It">The Haunting Hour: Don't Think About It</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> It earned her a <a href="/wiki/Young_Artist_Award" title="Young Artist Award">Young Artist Award</a> nomination in 2008 for "Best Performance in a TV Movie, Miniseries or Special Leading Young Actress". She also recorded the song "I Don't Think About It" for the movie's soundtrack.</p>
<p>Osment also recorded a remake version of the song "<a href="/wiki/If_I_Didn%27t_Have_You" title="If I Didn't Have You">If I Didn't Have You</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Mitchel_Musso" title="Mitchel Musso">Mitchel Musso</a> for the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DisneyMania_6" title="DisneyMania 6">DisneyMania 6</a></i> CD. The song was produced by <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Todd_(record_producer)" title="Bryan Todd (record producer)">Bryan Todd</a>. The two also filmed a music video for their remake.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In addition, she also recorded a song, released in 2008, with "The Disney Channel Circle of Stars". She recorded a remake of "<a href="/wiki/Once_Upon_a_Dream_(Sleeping_Beauty_song)#Emily_Osment_version" title="Once Upon a Dream (Sleeping Beauty song)">Once Upon A Dream</a>", released on September 12, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emstudalbum_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-emstudalbum-14">[14]</a></sup> She recorded a song called "Hero in Me" for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney_Channel_Original_Movie" title="Disney Channel Original Movie">Disney Channel Original Movie</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dadnapped" title="Dadnapped">Dadnapped</a></i> and also filmed a music video to accompany it. In 2008 she starred in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Soccer_Mom_(film)" title="Soccer Mom (film)">Soccer Mom</a></i>, and was ranked on number 1 on <i><a href="/wiki/Parade_(magazine)" title="Parade (magazine)">Parade</a></i> magazine and <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> to <i>Hot Kid Stars To Watch</i> list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> She also starred in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney_Channel_Original_Movie" title="Disney Channel Original Movie">Disney Channel Original Movie</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dadnapped" title="Dadnapped">Dadnapped</a></i> which premiered in February 2009.</p>

<p>In June, 2009 Osment signed with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wind-Up_Records" title="Wind-Up Records">Wind-Up Records</a> and announced that was writing and recording tracks with <a href="/wiki/Eve_6" title="Eve 6">Eve 6</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wind-up_Records_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wind-up_Records-16">[16]</a></sup> She has collaborated on tunes with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tom_Higgenson" title="Tom Higgenson">Tom Higgenson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Max_Collins_(musician)" title="Max Collins (musician)">Max Collins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Fagenson" title="Tony Fagenson">Tony Fagenson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toby_Gad" title="Toby Gad">Toby Gad</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mandi_Perkins" title="Mandi Perkins">Mandi Perkins</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_TODAY_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-USA_TODAY-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/All_the_Way_Up_(Emily_Osment_song)" title="All the Way Up (Emily Osment song)">All the Way Up</a>" was released as the lead single on August 25, 2009. The song debuted at number 77 in the Canadian Hot 100 then a week later peaked at number 76.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On October 26, 2009, Osment release her debut extended play, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_The_Right_Wrongs" title="All The Right Wrongs">All The Right Wrongs</a></i>, with six songs in the standart edition and eight songs in the deluxe. The EP peaked at number 117 at <i>Billboard</i> Top 200 and number 1 at <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" title="Top Heatseekers">Top Heatseekers</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bb200_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-bb200-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heatseekers_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-heatseekers-21">[21]</a></sup> Abouy the album, Osment said: "The songs on the album are from four different people, I wrote with four different people, and they all had to fit on the album together…I kind of just did my own thing. I listen to a lot of Alanis Morissette, I kind of got inspired from her and her song “Jagged Little Pill” …There’s a lot of edge to (Emily’s album)…I love hearing the guitars and the drums in the songs".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/You_Are_the_Only_One_(Emily_Osment_song)" title="You Are the Only One (Emily Osment song)">You Are the Only One</a>" was released as second single on February 27, 2010. It failed to make an impact on any charts, but enjoyed some success on Radio Disney. Originally, "You Are the Only One" was intended to be released as the lead single from the album, but was dropped due to Osment wanting to release "All the Way Up" instead.</p>

<p>Osment confirmed through her <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> account that she would be on tour: the Clap Your Hands Tour included nine dates in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. On March 24, 2010, Osment confirmed on Good Day NY that her debut album would be released in the summer of 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MY_FOX_NY_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-MY_FOX_NY-23">[23]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Be_Friends_(song)" title="Let's Be Friends (song)">Let's Be Friends</a>" was released as the album's lead single on June 8, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The single debuted and peaked at number twenty-four on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_Hot_100" title="Japan Hot 100">Japan Hot 100</a> chart and number 67 at Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In addition, the single became her first to chart on an official US singles chart, when it peaked at number 31 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Club_Play" title="Hot Dance Club Play">Hot Dance Club Play</a> chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> In October 5 she released her fist album, <i><a href="/wiki/Fight_or_Flight_(Emily_Osment_album)" title="Fight or Flight (Emily Osment album)">Fight or Flight</a></i>, focused on <a href="/wiki/Electropop" title="Electropop">electropop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Synthpop" title="Synthpop">synthpop</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-seventeen.com_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-seventeen.com-27">[27]</a></sup> The album peaked at number 170 at <i>Billboard</i> Top 200 and number 2 at <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" title="Top Heatseekers">Top Heatseekers</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bb200_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-bb200-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heatseekers_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-heatseekers-21">[21]</a></sup> The second single, "<a href="/wiki/Lovesick_(Emily_Osment_song)" title="Lovesick (Emily Osment song)">Lovesick</a>", was released on February 4, 2011. Due to the strong digital downloads the song received, the song debuted at number 165 on the UK Singles Chart. During the week of April 17, 2011, the single entered the Top 100 in the UK, reaching a peak of 67 on the chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The single also became her second to achieve success on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a>, where it debuted at number 71, and later rose to a peak of 66.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October, Osment made her second tour <i>Fight or Flight Tour</i> to promote her first album <a href="/wiki/Fight_or_Flight_(Emily_Osment_album)" title="Fight or Flight (Emily Osment album)">Fight or Flight</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> It included concerts in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a> . It began on October 30, 2010 in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, and ended on December 8, 2010 in <a href="/wiki/Appleton,_Wisconsin" title="Appleton, Wisconsin">Appleton</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. Osment presented at the <a href="/wiki/2010_MTV_Europe_Music_Awards" title="2010 MTV Europe Music Awards">2010 MTV Europe Music Awards</a> on November 7 in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, presenting an award to <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a> for best male artist. In March 2010, Osment lent her voice to the secondary character, Celeste Kendall Perkins for the animated series <a href="/wiki/Kick_Buttowski:_Suburban_Daredevil" title="Kick Buttowski: Suburban Daredevil">Kick Buttowski: Suburban Daredevil</a> of <a href="/wiki/Disney_XD" title="Disney XD">Disney XD</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In May 10, 2011, she released the duet single "<a href="/wiki/Hush_(Emily_Osment_and_Josh_Ramsay_song)" title="Hush (Emily Osment and Josh Ramsay song)">Hush</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Josh_Ramsay" title="Josh Ramsay">Josh Ramsay</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marianas_Trench" title="Marianas Trench">Marianas Trench</a>, and the song peaked at number 90 in Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> In 2011, Osment starred in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABC_Family" title="ABC Family">ABC Family</a> movie about a young woman who suffers cyberbullying, called <i><a href="/wiki/Cyberbully_(2011_film)" title="Cyberbully (2011 film)">Cyberbully</a></i>, released on July 17, 2011. ABC worked with <i>Seventeen Magazine</i> to make the film with the hope that help eliminate Internet harassment. It was filmed in Montreal and was released on DVD on February 7, 2012. The film received generally positive reviews and had 3.4 million viewers on its premiere. To promote the film, she released her single, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drift_(Emily_Osment_song)" title="Drift (Emily Osment song)">Drift</a>".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.93present:_Cleaners_and_Young_.26_Hungry">2013–present: <i>Cleaners</i> and <i>Young &amp; Hungry</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emily_Osment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2013–present: Cleaners and Young &amp; Hungry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, Osment starred in the TV series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cleaners_(2013_series)" title="Cleaners (2013 series)">Cleaners</a></i>, which in 2014 was announced for a second season. Osment had a cameo in <i><a href="/wiki/Life_with_Boys" title="Life with Boys">Life with Boys</a></i>, where she has to take a picture with the main character, Tess, and her boyfriend. She does not appear again and four episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Family_Guy" title="Family Guy">Family Guy</a></i>. She reprised her voice role as Pep for the third <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills_Chihuahua_3" title="Beverly Hills Chihuahua 3">Beverly Hills Chihuahua</a> film, which was released on DVD September 18, 2012, and was one of her last works at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney" title="Disney">Disney</a>. Emily Osment confirmed through her official <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> account that she had left her label <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wind-Up_Records" title="Wind-Up Records">Wind-Up Records</a> and has launched an independent project with airs folk, indie and country in a band called Ramshackle, consisting of Osment and her friend Dan Schechter, performing in bars such as the Troubadour and at the Spiedie Fest 2013, performing covers of <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">The Beatles</a>, <a href="/wiki/R._Kelly" title="R. Kelly">R. Kelly</a> and "Lullaby". In 2013, Emily made a special participation in the episode "Bazinga! That's From a TV Show" for the comedy series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Two_And_A_Half_Men" title="Two And A Half Men">Two And A Half Men</a></i> where she played the role of Ashley. Osment starred in the short film called <i><a href="/wiki/Seasick_Sailor" title="Seasick Sailor">Seasick Sailor</a></i> led by <i>Devon Bostick</i> and <i>Torre Catalano</i>. The film has been presented in some film festivals and won the award for <i>Best Narrative Short</i> at the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Film_Festival" title="Los Angeles Film Festival">Los Angeles Film Festival</a>.</p>
<p>Osment also participated in the drama, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kiss_Me_(film_2014)" title="Kiss Me (film 2014)">Kiss Me</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Probst" title="Jeff Probst">Jeff Probst</a> in which she starred opposite Irish actress <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Bolger" title="Sarah Bolger">Sarah Bolger</a>. In October, Emily filmed a new movie <i><a href="/wiki/No_Way_Jose" title="No Way Jose">No Way Jose</a></i>. In October 2013, the third movie in the Nightmare franchise started filming, called <i><a href="/wiki/A_Daughter%27s_Nightmare" title="A Daughter's Nightmare">A Daughter's Nightmare</a></i>, which premiered on <a href="/wiki/Lifetime_(TV_network)" title="Lifetime (TV network)">Lifetime</a> in 2014. Osment recorded the song <i>In Case Of Fire</i> for the soundtrack of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Osment also finished recording the second season of the web series <i>Cleaners</i>.</p>
<p>In 2014, she debuted as the lead role in the new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABC_Family" title="ABC Family">ABC Family</a> TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Young_%26_Hungry" title="Young &amp; Hungry">Young &amp; Hungry</a></i>, produced by <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Tisdale" title="Ashley Tisdale">Ashley Tisdale</a>. Emily plays Gabi, a feisty young blogger hired as the personal chef to a young San Francisco tech entrepreneur. Not the typical polished chef, Gabi is blunt, klutzy and has an outrageous sense of style.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The pilot episode, which aired on June 25, 2014, garnered 1.08 million views in the US.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The show is currently at its fourth season.</p>
<p>In 2015, one of the projects Osment is involved in is the movie <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Love_Is_All_You_Need%3F_(2015_film)" title="Love Is All You Need? (2015 film)">Love Is All You Need?</a></i> The film, which also stars <a href="/wiki/Briana_Evigan" title="Briana Evigan">Briana Evigan</a>, is an independent drama set in a world where being gay is normal and heterosexual people are intimidated. In August 2015, Osment was booked for a recurring role on the third season of the CBS series <i><a href="/wiki/Mom_(TV_series)" title="Mom (TV series)">Mom</a></i>. She played Jodi, a girl living on the street who was a drug addict; Christy (<a href="/wiki/Anna_Faris" title="Anna Faris">Anna Faris</a>) and Bonnie (<a href="/wiki/Allison_Janney" title="Allison Janney">Allison Janney</a>) helped her stay clean and find a job. The third season premiered on November 5, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> But Jodi died from a drug overdose in midseason, a decision made with the creation of the character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2007, Osment signed a deal to appear in a nationwide campaign called "Smart Girls Rock" by the New York-based denim brand Vanilla Star. "It's a new line for girls, the clothes are really denim and girly with an edge of punk."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-b-38">[38]</a></sup> She promoted her song "I Don't Think About It" with the release of the campaign and commercial. Late that summer, Disney released a <i>Hannah Montana</i> clothing collection. Osment helped to design some of the pieces from the collection. Her image was used in some of these pieces, as well as other <i>Hannah Montana</i> merchandise (such as dolls and DVDs).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Philanthropy">Philanthropy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emily_Osment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Philanthropy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>She agreed to sign with <a href="/wiki/Cosmogirl" title="Cosmogirl">CosmoGIRL!</a> shirts for the president of an online charity auction. In 2007, Osment designed a line of shirts for 2lovecollection.com, whose proceeds went to the Make-a-Wish Foundation and St. Jude Research Hospital.</p>
<p>In early 2008, Osment participated in the "Disney Channel Earth Day", where she and other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney" title="Disney">Disney</a> stars met with TreePeople to teach children how to care for the environment with small changes in their homes and neighborhoods. Osment was also part of the green project, <a href="/wiki/Disney%27s_Friends_for_Change" title="Disney's Friends for Change">Disney's Friends for Change</a>, an initiative for the environment created in 2009. The campaign wants young people aware of the current environmental situation and engage with it in around them. Children had the opportunity to choose how Disney will invest one million dollars in various environmental programs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disneychannelpress_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-disneychannelpress-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>Osment is a Global Ambassador for STOMP Out Bullying, the leading national Anti-Bullying/Cyberbullying organization in the country for kids and teens. She for was voted the U.G.L.Y celebrity of 2011 at the seventh annual Celebrity of the Year Award, thanks to the votes of young Americans impressed with the performance of the actress in her TV movie <a href="/wiki/Cyberbully_(2011_film)" title="Cyberbully (2011 film)">Cyberbully</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Preventing bullying is the fundamental mission of Hey UGLY international nonprofit organization that sponsors the annual award.</p>
