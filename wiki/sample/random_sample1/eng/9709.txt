<p><b>Emily Jean Perkins</b> (born May 4, 1977)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NorthernStars.ca_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-NorthernStars.ca-1">[1]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Canadians" title="Canadians">Canadian</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actress</a>, best known for her roles as Crystal Braywood in the TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Hiccups_(TV_series)" title="Hiccups (TV series)">Hiccups</a></i>, and Brigitte Fitzgerald in <i><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Snaps_(film)" title="Ginger Snaps (film)">Ginger Snaps</a></i>. Since the late 1980s she has appeared in various <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">films</a> and <a href="/wiki/Television_program" title="Television program">television series</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emily_Perkins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Perkins was born in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NorthernStars.ca_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-NorthernStars.ca-1">[1]</a></sup> As a child actress she was in three made-for-television films: <i><a href="/wiki/Small_Sacrifices" title="Small Sacrifices">Small Sacrifices</a></i> (1989), <i><a href="/wiki/Anything_to_Survive" title="Anything to Survive">Anything to Survive</a></i> (1990), and <a href="/wiki/Stephen_King" title="Stephen King">Stephen King</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/It_(1990_film)" title="It (1990 film)">It</a></i> (1990).</p>
<p>In 1998, Perkins took a supporting role in the Canadian TV crime-drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Da_Vinci%27s_Inquest" title="Da Vinci's Inquest">Da Vinci's Inquest</a></i> as Carmen in the episode "The Most Dangerous Time." She returned to the series in 2001 to take a recurring, supporting role as the prostitute Sue Lewis for a further 34 episodes, a role which earned her a <a href="/wiki/Leo_Awards" title="Leo Awards">Leo Award</a> for Best Supporting Performance in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2000, Perkins starred in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Snaps_(film)" title="Ginger Snaps (film)">Ginger Snaps</a></i> as Brigitte Fitzgerald. In 2004 she starred in two back-to-back films, a sequel titled <i><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Snaps_2:_Unleashed" title="Ginger Snaps 2: Unleashed">Ginger Snaps 2: Unleashed</a></i>, and a prequel titled <i><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Snaps_Back:_The_Beginning" title="Ginger Snaps Back: The Beginning">Ginger Snaps Back: The Beginning</a></i>. Her role in <i>Ginger Snaps</i> gained her Best Actress award at the Málaga International Week of Fantastic Cinema in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <i>Ginger Snaps 2: Unleashed</i> is Perkins' largest role in a film. In 2004 the horror magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Fangoria" title="Fangoria">Fangoria</a></i> awarded Perkins their Chainsaw Award for her work in <i>GS2: Unleashed</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Perkins had supporting roles in <i><a href="/wiki/She%27s_the_Man" title="She's the Man">She's the Man</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Another_Cinderella_Story" title="Another Cinderella Story">Another Cinderella Story</a></i>, and a small role as a receptionist in <i><a href="/wiki/Juno_(film)" title="Juno (film)">Juno</a></i>. She stars in the thriller <i>Blood: A Butcher's Tale.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2008, she appeared as "superfan" Becky Rosen in the CW TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Supernatural_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Supernatural (U.S. TV series)">Supernatural</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Starting March 1, 2010, Perkins played the regular role of Crystal Braywood in the Canadian TV comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Hiccups_(TV_series)" title="Hiccups (TV series)">Hiccups</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> After two seasons the show was cancelled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>She is married to Canadian academic <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Mathijs" title="Ernest Mathijs">Ernest Mathijs</a>, who has written about <a href="/wiki/Cult_film" title="Cult film">cult films</a>, including her roles in the <i>Ginger Snaps</i> series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
