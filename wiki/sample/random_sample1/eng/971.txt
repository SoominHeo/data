<p><b>FC Viikingit</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finnish</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Vuosaari" title="Vuosaari">Vuosaari</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Helsinki" title="East Helsinki">East Helsinki</a>, and currently playing in the <i><a href="/wiki/Kakkonen" title="Kakkonen">Kakkonen</a></i>, the second tier of <a href="/wiki/Football_in_Finland" title="Football in Finland">Finnish football</a>. They play their home matches at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vuosaaren_urheilukentt%C3%A4" title="Vuosaaren urheilukenttä">Vuosaaren urheilukenttä</a>, also called Heteniitty. The club's name and crest refer to <a href="/wiki/Vikings" title="Vikings">Vikings</a>, and they are known for their red colour.</p>
<p>FC Viikingit played the 2007 season in the <a href="/wiki/Veikkausliiga" title="Veikkausliiga">Finnish premier division</a> (<i>Veikkausliiga</i>), but were relegated to the <i>Ykkönen</i> and competed there until 2014, when they were relegated to <a href="/wiki/Kakkonen" title="Kakkonen">Kakkonen</a>. The club's reserve team, <a href="/wiki/FC_Viikkarit" title="FC Viikkarit">FC Viikkarit</a>, were promoted to the <i><a href="/wiki/Kakkonen" title="Kakkonen">Kakkonen</a></i> for the 2014 season but they had to give it up, because they were not able to play on the same division as the main team.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history_.281965.E2.80.931997.29">Early history (1965–1997)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Viikingit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early history (1965–1997)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The club was first founded as <i>Vuosaaren Viikingit</i> ('Vuosaari Vikings') in 1965. The new club started at the very bottom of the Finnish football pyramid, spending their first years in the fifth and sixth divisions. For the 1973 season, they were promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Nelonen" title="Nelonen">IV division</a> and played there for the next ten seasons. In 1983, the team were promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Kolmonen" title="Kolmonen">III division</a>, but this visit was cut short, as they were relegated again the following season. They were promoted again for the 1993 season, and this time managed to stay up. They even made a year-long visit to the <i><a href="/wiki/Kakkonen" title="Kakkonen">Kakkonen</a></i> in 1996, but were relegated after finishing last in their group. In 1997, the footballing section of the club was split into its own organisation with the name FC Viikingit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-historia_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-historia-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climb_to_the_Veikkausliiga_.281997.E2.80.932007.29">Climb to the Veikkausliiga (1997–2007)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Viikingit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climb to the Veikkausliiga (1997–2007)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With the new organisation in place, the football club started to enjoy some success in the third division, and were promoted back to the Kakkonen for the 2001 season. The newly promoted team did well in the second division, finishing at first place in their group and winning direct promotion to the <i><a href="/wiki/Ykk%C3%B6nen" title="Ykkönen">Ykkönen</a></i> for the 2002 season. In their first season in the first division, FC Viikingit finished second to last and had to compete in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Play-off" title="Play-off">play-off</a> against <a href="/wiki/Warkauden_Jalkapalloklubi" title="Warkauden Jalkapalloklubi">WJK</a> from <a href="/wiki/Varkaus" title="Varkaus">Varkaus</a>. The Reds won the play-off and remained in the first division. For the 2003 season, <a href="/wiki/Markku_Kanerva" title="Markku Kanerva">Markku Kanerva</a> was appointed the club's manager. He led the club to a successful season, finishing third in a 14-team league, only one point from a promotion play-off spot. In 2004, the club were managed by <a href="/wiki/Steven_Polack" title="Steven Polack">Steven Polack</a> and finished fourth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-historia_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-historia-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jari_Europaeus" title="Jari Europaeus">Jari Europaeus</a> was appointed manager of the Reds in 2005. In the following season, he led the club to first place in the Ykkönen, winning promotion to the <i><a href="/wiki/Veikkausliiga" title="Veikkausliiga">Veikkausliiga</a></i>, the highest level of Finnish football. In the first ever premier division match played at Heteniitty, Viikingit beat <a href="/wiki/FC_Honka" title="FC Honka">FC Honka</a> with a 3–2 scoreline and an attendance of over 3,000. The aim for the <a href="/wiki/2007_Veikkausliiga" title="2007 Veikkausliiga">2007 Veikkausliiga</a> season was to stay up in the top flight, and with the performances the team gave in the first half of the season, this looked likely. The attendance record, which is still in force, was created in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Local_derby" title="Local derby">local derby</a> against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HJK_Helsinki" title="HJK Helsinki">HJK Helsinki</a>, which drew 4,255 viewers to Vuosaaren urheilukenttä. In the second half of the season, the team's performances began to drop. However, they managed to finish 13th and avoid direct relegation, but still had to compete in a relegation play-off against <a href="/wiki/RoPS" title="RoPS">RoPS</a>. The first leg in <a href="/wiki/Rovaniemi" title="Rovaniemi">Rovaniemi</a> finished 1–0 in favour of the home side, and therefore the Reds needed a win in the second leg, which was played at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T%C3%B6%C3%B6l%C3%B6n_pallokentt%C3%A4" title="Töölön pallokenttä">Töölön pallokenttä</a> due to heavy snowfall at Heteniitty. However, the match finished 1–1, and Viikingit were relegated to the first division.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-historia_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-historia-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_the_Ykk.C3.B6nen_.282008.E2.80.93.29">Return to the Ykkönen (2008–)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Viikingit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Return to the Ykkönen (2008–)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After relegation from the top flight in the 2007 season, Viikingit have often reached the top three in the Ykkönen, but have fallen short of promotion every time. In 2008, the new manager <a href="/wiki/Toni_Korkeakunnas" title="Toni Korkeakunnas">Toni Korkeakunnas</a> led the team to second place, but <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KuPS" title="KuPS">KuPS</a> took the promotion play-off with an aggregate scoreline of 2–1. History repeated itself in 2010, when the Reds played <a href="/wiki/JJK_Jyv%C3%A4skyl%C3%A4" title="JJK Jyväskylä">JJK Jyväskylä</a> in the play-off, but the <a href="/wiki/Jyv%C3%A4skyl%C3%A4" title="Jyväskylä">Jyväskylä</a> side were marginally better (3–2 on aggregate). From the 2011 season, Viikingit have been managed by long-time Reds player <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ilkka_J%C3%A4ntti&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ilkka Jäntti (page does not exist)">Ilkka Jäntti</a>, finishing fifth and third respectively in the 2011 and 2012 seasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-historia_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-historia-1">[1]</a></sup> The club's attendance record in the Ykkönen, 1,670 viewers, was created on 21 July 2011 against local rivals <a href="/wiki/HIFK_Fotboll" title="HIFK Fotboll">HIFK</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-soccerway1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-soccerway1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, the club were in danger of relegation to the <a href="/wiki/Kakkonen" title="Kakkonen">Kakkonen</a>, but played well in the closing stage of the season, finishing seventh in the end.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-season13_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-season13-3">[3]</a></sup> This was the club's worst finish since 2002. The season's bright spot was the fact that the reserve team, <a href="/wiki/FC_Viikkarit" title="FC Viikkarit">FC Viikkarit</a>, won their group in the <a href="/wiki/Kolmonen" title="Kolmonen">Kolmonen</a> and were promoted to the second division for the 2014 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kolmonen13_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-kolmonen13-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ground">Ground</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Viikingit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Ground">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>FC Viikingit play their home matches at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vuosaaren_urheilukentt%C3%A4" title="Vuosaaren urheilukenttä">Vuosaaren urheilukenttä</a>, often called <i>Heteniitty</i> or <i>Hettari</i>. The ground is located in <a href="/wiki/Keski-Vuosaari" title="Keski-Vuosaari">Central Vuosaari</a> in a former <a href="/wiki/Gravel_pit" title="Gravel pit">gravel pit</a>, and for this reason is sometimes also called <i>Monttu</i> ('the Pit'). The ground currently has a capacity of 1,500, but has opportunities for expansion. For the <a href="/wiki/2007_Veikkausliiga" title="2007 Veikkausliiga">2007 Veikkausliiga</a> season, the ground was expanded to 3,500 seats, but these expansions were removed after the club were relegated.</p>
<p>The reserve side <a href="/wiki/FC_Viikkarit" title="FC Viikkarit">FC Viikkarit</a> play their home matches at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kartanon_kentt%C3%A4&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kartanon kenttä (page does not exist)">Kartanon kenttä</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nordsj%C3%B6n_kartano" title="Nordsjön kartano">Nordsjön kartano</a>. This is likely to change for the 2014 season, however, as the ground currently does not fulfil the stadium requirements for the <a href="/wiki/Kakkonen" title="Kakkonen">Kakkonen</a>. The women's first team, as well as youth teams, also play at Kartanon kenttä.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation_and_other_teams">Organisation and other teams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Viikingit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Organisation and other teams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The football club split into two separate organisations in the end of the 2008 season. The first team now operates under its own registered association, <i>FC Viikingit edustus ry</i>, while all other teams continue under <i>FC Viikingit ry</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="FC_Viikkarit">FC Viikkarit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Viikingit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: FC Viikkarit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/FC_Viikkarit" title="FC Viikkarit">FC Viikkarit</a></div>
<p>The reserve side FC Viikingit/2 was founded in 2007 and played its first seasons in the <a href="/wiki/Kolmonen" title="Kolmonen">Kolmonen</a>. In the 2013 season, the team won its group and achieved promotion to the <a href="/wiki/Kakkonen" title="Kakkonen">Kakkonen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kolmonen13_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-kolmonen13-4">[4]</a></sup> Upon reaching promotion, the team was renamed FC Viikkarit. The reserve team manager in the 2014 season is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexei_Zhukov" title="Alexei Zhukov">Alexei Zhukov</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-viikkarit_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-viikkarit-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_teams">Other teams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Viikingit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Other teams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The women's first team was extablished in 2001, and it was promoted to the women's first division (<i><a href="/wiki/Naisten_Ykk%C3%B6nen" title="Naisten Ykkönen">Naisten Ykkönen</a></i>) for the 2014 season. The manager of the women's first team is Akseli Grasutis, who is assisted by coach Kari Simula.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-naiset_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-naiset-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>FC Viikingit have a total of twenty youth teams, thirteen for boys and seven for girls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-joukkueet_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-joukkueet-8">[8]</a></sup> The youth teams have a total of 602 registered players, making Viikingit the seventh largest club in Helsinki in terms of youth football.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In addition, the club runs two amateur teams in lower leagues, <i>FC Viikingit 05</i> and <i>FC Viikingit/KoMa</i>. As of 2014<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=FC_Viikingit&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, both of these teams play in the <a href="/wiki/Kutonen" title="Kutonen">Kutonen</a>.</p>
