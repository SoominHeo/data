<p><b>Emmit King</b> (born March 24, 1959 in <a href="/wiki/Bessemer,_Alabama" title="Bessemer, Alabama">Bessemer, Alabama</a>) is a retired <a href="/wiki/Track_and_field" title="Track and field">track and field</a> <a href="/wiki/Sprint_(running)" title="Sprint (running)">sprinter</a> from the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, who twice was a member of the American Relay Team for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Summer_Olympics" title="Summer Olympics">Summer Olympics</a> (1984 and 1988) but he didn't compete. He is best known for winning the bronze medal at the inaugural <a href="/wiki/1983_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1983 World Championships in Athletics">1983 World Championships</a> in the men's 100 metres. He set his personal best (10.04) in the 100 metres on 1988-06-17 at a meet in <a href="/wiki/Tampa,_Florida" title="Tampa, Florida">Tampa, Florida</a>.</p>
<p>While at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Alabama" title="University of Alabama">University of Alabama</a>, King became the 1983 NCAA 100 meters National Champion (10.15 seconds). In 1986, he became a member of <a href="/wiki/Phi_Beta_Sigma" title="Phi Beta Sigma">Phi Beta Sigma</a> fraternity through the Theta Delta chapter at the University.</p>
